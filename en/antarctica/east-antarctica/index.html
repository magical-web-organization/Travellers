Introduction to East Antarctica

East Antarctica is the largest region of Antarctica, covering almost 2/3 of its landmass. It is the coldest and driest place on Earth, with temperatures dropping to -128.6°F at the Soviet Union’s Vostok Station in 1983. The region is known for its vast expanse of ice sheets, spectacular glaciers, and unique wildlife.

If you’re planning a trip to East Antarctica, you’ll find plenty of attractions that will leave you mesmerized. Here are some places you may want to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Russian Progress II and Molodezhnaya Research Stations

Where to Stay in East Antarctica: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Since East Antarctica is not a tourist destination, all accommodations are exclusively for researchers, and basic in nature. Options include:

  • Australian Antarctic Division’s Casey Station
  • Chinese Antarctic Station

What to Buy in East Antarctica: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

With limited tourist infrastructure, there are no shopping opportunities within East Antarctica. However, you can buy souvenirs from gift shops located at the research station.

Local Culture and Customs of East Antarctica

East Antarctica is home to a small number of scientists and researchers, so you will not find any local culture or customs. Visitors are expected to be respectful and adhere to research station rules and regulations.

Best Time to Visit East Antarctica

The best time to visit East Antarctica is in December and January, when the sun never sets, and the region is least likely to experience extreme weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Antarctica

Do:

  • Follow all research station rules and regulations.
  • Dress appropriately, in layers, and bring along wind and waterproof gear.
  • Be respectful of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or remove any natural resources.
  • Leave any trash or waste behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Antarctica

East Antarctica is not considered a safe destination for tourists without a specific purpose. All visitors need to follow strict rules and regulations set by the research stations.

Health in East Antarctica

Medical facilities within East Antarctica are limited to the research stations, which is why visitors must ensure they have adequate insurance coverage.

Getting to East Antarctica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Since East Antarctica is not considered a tourist destination, there are limited transportation options available. The most common way to access East Antarctica is through chartered flights offered by research stations.

Airports Near East Antarctica

  • Casey Aerodrome

Other Options

There are no other transport options available, other than by chartered flights.

How to Get Around in East Antarctica

All visitors must follow the rules and regulations set by the research centers. As such, visitors will be transported by vehicles supplied by the research centers.

Where to Go Next from East Antarctica: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are few nearby destinations to visit after East Antarctica. You could consider visiting other regions of Antarctica, such as the Antarctic Peninsula or Ross Island.

Conclusion

East Antarctica is a unique destination that is not for everyone. It is not a tourist destination, but rather a place dedicated to scientific research. If you have a specific reason to visit, ensure you follow all rules and regulations set by the research stations, dress appropriately, and carry adequate insurance coverage.
