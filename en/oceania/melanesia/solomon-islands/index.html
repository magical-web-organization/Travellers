If you’re looking for a unique travel experience, you should definitely consider visiting the Solomon Islands. Located in Melanesia, in Oceania, this archipelago consists of six major islands and over 900 smaller islands. The Solomon Islands offer a combination of culturally rich attractions and naturally beautiful landscapes, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure and relaxation.

Introduction to Solomon Islands

The Solomon Islands were historically inhabited by Melanesian tribes, with a rich and diverse culture. In recent times, this nation has been influenced by both the British and Japanese colonialism. Today, it’s a peaceful, democratic country that’s gaining popularity among tourists for its diverse culture, friendly locals, and stunning scenery. The capital city, Honiara, is the hub of the Solomon Islands, where the majority of tourism infrastructure can be found.

List of Places to Visit in Solomon Islands

There is so much to see and do in the Solomon Islands. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum and Cultural Centre: This museum is located in Honiara and represents the rich history and cultural diversity of the Solomon Islands.
  • Mataniko Falls: These spectacular falls are located just outside Honiara and have great cultural significance to local people.
  • Tenaru Falls and World War II Battle Sites: These falls have great historical significance as they were the site of a World War II battle between the Japanese and American forces.
  • Nusa Tupe Cultural Village: Located on the island of Guadalcanal, this village is an excellent opportunity to learn about Melanesian culture and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Solomon Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Marovo Lagoon: This lagoon is one of the most incredible natural sites in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Munda: This is the gateway to the West New Georgia Sound, known for its crystal clear waters and World War II history.
  • Diving at Tulagi: Discover the incredible underwater world in the clear waters around Tulagi.
  • Vona Vona Lagoon: This lagoon is known for its rich wildlife and offers visitors the opportunity to swim with dolphins and have beach picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Solomon Islands

The Solomon Islands have excellent hiking and biking trails. Here are a few of the best options:

  • Mount Austen: This hike may be challenging, but the views from Mt. Austen are breathtaking and make it worthwhile.
  • Kokoda Trail: This trail is part of the island’s World War II history and offers some incredible views.
  • Mataniko Falls: This is a gentle hike that takes you to the stunning Mataniko Falls.

Best Things to Do in Solomon Islands: Activities and Tours

The Solomon Islands is a paradise for those seeking adventure. Here are some activities and tours you don’t want to miss:

  • Diving and Snorkeling: With its vibrant underwater life, the Solomon Islands are a popular destination for diving and snorkeling.
  • Fishing: The Solomon Islands are known for their excellent fishing spots, and visitors can join a fishing tour to catch their own big fish.
  • Cultural Tours: The Solomon Islands offer a range of cultural tours that provide visitors with an opportunity to learn about local customs and traditions.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to see the unique and stunning scenery of the Solomon Islands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Solomon Islands

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to the Solomon Islands:

Day Activity
1 Visit the National Museum and Cultural Centre in Honiara
2 Go diving or snorkeling in the clear waters of Tulagi
3 Take a boat tour of Vona Vona Lagoon
4 Take a cultural tour of Nusa Tupe Village
5 Hike Mount Austen and enjoy the breathtaking views
6 Explore the Marovo Lagoon and its beautiful surroundings
7 Relax on the beach at Munda

Food and Drink Scene of Solomon Islands

The food culture of the Solomon Islands is diverse, and visitors get to taste a mix of traditional and modern dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoda: This is a fresh fish dish that’s marinated in lime juice, coconut cream, chili, and vegetables.
  • Sago Pudding: A sweet dish made from sago flour and coconut cream.
  • Island-style BBQ: Seafood, pork, or chicken barbecued the Solomon Islands style are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: This is a traditional drink made from the roots of the Kava plant, is central to many ceremonies and rituals.
  • Coconut Milk: The locals drink coconut milk, the juice is creamy and refreshing, and is a good replacement for water.

Where to Stay in Solomon Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options available in the Solomon Islands, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Heritage Park Hotel: This hotel is located in Honiara and is the perfect base to explore the island.
  • Uepi Resort: This resort is located in Marovo Lagoon; it offers visitors the chance to stay in eco-friendly bungalows and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.
  • Sanbis Resort: This resort is situated on the island of Gizo and is perfect for families and couples looking for a secluded getaway.

What to Buy in Solomon Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade crafts to local produce, the Solomon Islands offer a plethora of souvenirs and local products. Here are a few things you might want to bring back:

  • Shell necklaces and jewelry: These make for great souvenirs and are a popular handicraft in the Solomon Islands.
  • Sago: This flour is a staple food in the Solomon Islands and could be an excellent purchase.
  • Wooden carvings: The locals are skilled in wood carvings, and the island offers an impressive range of products.

Local Culture and Customs of Solomon Islands

The Solomon Islands have a rich and diverse culture with its traditions, dances, and customary rituals. Two of the important ceremonies that visitors should witness are the Custom dance ceremony and The Shark Calling Ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Solomon Islands

The Solomon Islands has a tropical climate with average temperatures ranging from 26 to 32 degrees Celsius throughout the year. The weather follows a wet and dry season. The best time to visit is between October and April during the dry season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Solomon Islands

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts for visitors to the Solomon Islands:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs: The locals are traditionally minded and respect their culture.
  • Carry cash: Most places in the Solomon Islands do not accept cards for payment, so carry cash.
  • Protect the environment: The Solomon Islands are an eco-friendly nation; take care of the environment.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes: The locals dress conservatively, and it’s respectful to follow the same. Avoid wearing snug clothes and revealing the skin.
  • Take photographs of people without permission: Always ask permission before taking pictures.

Safety and Health

It’s essential to take precautions while traveling to the Solomon Islands.

Safety in Solomon Islands

  • Natural disasters: Earthquakes, tsunamis, and cyclones can occur in the Solomon Islands from time-to-time. It is important always to keep up to date with the latest information from local authorities as well as staying in contact with your embassy if necessary.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings: Tourists have been targeted in the past, so avoid wearing expensive items or money belts that attract attention.

Health in Solomon Island

  • Water safety: It’s important to boil water before drinking or purchase bottled water.
  • Malaria: The Solomon Islands are a high-risk country for malaria; take precautions and take an antimalarial regimen.

Getting to Solomon Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

It’s easy to reach the Solomon Islands via air routes. Here are a few travel options:

Airports Near Solomon Islands

  • Honiara International Airport: It the principal entry point to the Solomon Islands with regular flights from major international airports.

Other Options

  • Cruise ships: It’s possible to reach Solomon Island via Cruise ships from Australia and New Zealand.
  • Yachts/ Ferries: Some yachts travel the pacific, and they can anchor in Gizo harbor and disembark.

How to Get Around Solomon Islands

The island has a well-connected network of roads, and sea-links make traveling between islands comfortable and affordable. Taxis and buses are inexpensive and can be booked. Though the roads have to share space with locals and wildlife, road accidents are rare in the Solomon Islands.

Where to Go Next from Solomon Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Papua New Guinea: Visit Papua New Guinea with its rich wildlife and unique culture.
  • Vanuatu: Vanuatu offers visitors a stunning landscape with volcanic peaks, waterfalls, and pristine sandy beaches.

Conclusion

Solomon Islands is a fantastic destination to explore the natural beauty and diverse culture of the South Pacific. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip, enjoy your stay, and immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions. So pack your bags and start planning your trip to this incredible island paradise.
