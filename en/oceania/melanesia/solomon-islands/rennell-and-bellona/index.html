Introduction to Rennell and Bellona

Rennell and Bellona, located in the Solomon Islands, is a hidden gem in the Pacific Ocean. With lush forests, vibrant coral reefs, and a rich cultural heritage, it offers raw and unspoiled beauty. The islands are named after English explorer Lord Bellona and Admiral Rennell. It has a population of about 3,000 people and is mostly inhabited by Polynesians and Micronesians.

List of Places to Visit in Rennell and Bellona

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

With a history that dates back thousands of years, Rennell and Bellona have a rich culture that continues to be maintained through museums and historical buildings. Some notable museums and historical landmarks include the Grave of the first Missionary John Williams, the Kapu Parish, and the Arnon Atoll, which served as a military outpost during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Rennell and Bellona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The islands’ natural beauty is breathtaking, with natural lakes, lagoons, mangroves, and lush forests. To witness the abundance of flora and fauna, visit Lake Tegano, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, or take a boat ride to explore the surrounding coral reefs and spot colorful fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rennell and Bellona

For the adventurous traveler, hiking through forests and biking around the islands allows you to experience the beauty of Rennell and Bellona up close. Popular hiking and biking routes include the Rennell Trails and the Nusa Baruku Trail.

Best Things to Do in Rennell and Bellona: Activities and Tours

Engage in island activities like traditional canoe tours or try your hand at fishing with local fishermen. Or spend time with Polynesian community and learn about their unique way of life by visiting the Bellona Island Cultural Centre. Be part of cultural activities such as basket weaving, traditional cooking, and sit under coconut trees while drinking local beverages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rennell and Bellona

A week is ample time to explore the beauty of Rennell and Bellona. This itinerary will ensure you have an unforgettable experience:

  • Day 1: Arrive at the Honiara International Airport, travel to Bellona Island. Relax at the beach and explore surroundings.
  • Day 2: Visit the Bellona Island Cultural Centre and learn about the Polynesian and Micronesian culture.
  • Day 3: Go on a guided tour of the island’s mangroves and explore natural caves and lagoons.
  • Day 4: Transfer to Rennell Island and visit Arnon Atoll.
  • Day 5: Go on a guided hike through the forest and witness the magnificent view at Mount Levevi.
  • Day 6: Cool off at Lake Tegano, take a swim, and go for a boat ride.
  • Day 7: Do a traditional canoe tour and close the island experience with souvenirs of Rennell and Bellona.

Food and Drink Scene of Rennell and Bellona

Island cuisine is mouth-watering, rich in coconut, fish, taro, and yams. Seafood is a staple food in Rennell and Bellona, with specialties like baked mackerel and sea salt-roasted fish.

Must-Try Dishes

Ota, a dish made from fresh coconut, red onions, and tuna, is a Polynesian specialty worth trying. Another delicacy worth tasting is Wabubuli, a dish made with taro, coconut milk, and sugar.

Local Beverages

Several local beverages can be found in Rennel and Bellona. One favorite is Lea, a coconut wine, which you can savor while watching the sunset.

Where to Stay in Rennell and Bellona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The islands have limited accommodation options, but there are a few places to stay that offer comfortable lodgings. Popular options include the Lake Tegano Research Station and the Rennell Island Lodge.

What to Buy in Rennell and Bellona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You will find many fantastic souvenirs and local products to take home from Rennell and Bellona. Handcrafted baskets and traditional clothing are excellent souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Rennell and Bellona

Rennell and Bellona have unique customs and traditions, and it is essential to respect their culture. Local people welcome visitors and will be happy to share their customs with you.

Best Time to Visit Rennell and Bellona

The best time to visit Rennell and Bellona is from March to September when the weather is dry. However, the islands’ beauty is an all-year-round attraction.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rennell and Bellona

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Rennell and Bellona:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Seek permission before taking pictures of the locals or their property
  • Try the local Polynesian and Micronesian dishes
  • Carry light, breathable clothing

Don’t:

  • Take anything from archaeological sites or historical places
  • Wear revealing clothing that might offend locals
  • Use your camera’s flash while taking pictures of local people

Safety and Health

Safety in Rennell and Bellona

Rennell and Bellona are generally safe for tourists, but it is always essential to take necessary precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding walking around late at night.

Health in Rennell and Bellona

Consulting a doctor is highly advisable before visiting Rennell and Bellona.

Getting to Rennell and Bellona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rennell and Bellona

The nearest international airport is the Honiara International Airport, and from there, you can take a domestic flight to Rennell and Bellona.

Bus Stations Near Rennell and Bellona

Buses are not a common mode of transportation as there are none on the island.

Other Options

Other transportation options include renting a car or finding a private driver to take you around the islands.

How to Get Around in Rennell and Bellona

The easiest way to get around the island is by hiring a bike or a private driver.

Where to Go Next from Rennell and Bellona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a boat ride to nearby islands, such as the Florida Islands. Divers can embark on an excursion to the Marovo Lagoon, the world’s largest double-barrier lagoon and UNESCO World Heritage site.

Conclusion

Visiting Rennell and Bellona is like stepping back in time - an untouched paradise. With the assistance of this comprehensive guide, plan an unforgettable journey and form memories that will last a lifetime.
