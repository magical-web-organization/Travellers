Introduction to Santa Cruz Islands

Located in the Solomon Islands, Santa Cruz Islands offer an exotic and pristine destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. With its lush rainforests, turquoise waters, and abundant wildlife, Santa Cruz Islands is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Cruz Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nena Sika Cultural Centre: This museum showcases the traditions, history, and culture of the people of Temotu Province.

  2. Santa Cruz Heritage Centre: This museum displays various artifacts and images that depict the lives and traditions of the Santa Cruz Islanders.

  3. Lomlom Airport WWII Trenches: A historical site that showcases the World War II trenches, which are deeply embedded in the volcanic rocks.

Natural Attractions of Santa Cruz Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jacques Cousteau Marine Reserve: This marine reserve is home to a range of marine life, including various types of fish, sharks, and dolphins. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling and scuba diving in the pristine waters.

  2. Santa Cruz Island War Relics: A collection of war relics scattered throughout the island.

  3. Graciosa Bay: One of the most beautiful and peaceful beaches in Santa Cruz Islands. The beach has crystal clear water and is great for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Cruz Islands

  1. The Coral Trail: This trail runs along the rugged coastline of Santa Cruz Islands and is perfect for hiking and biking.

  2. The Central Ridge Trail: This trail runs along the ridge of the island, offering panoramic views of the island’s landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Santa Cruz Islands: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: With its warm waters and abundant marine life, Santa Cruz Islands is a great destination for scuba diving and snorkeling.

  2. Bird Watching: Santa Cruz Islands has a diverse range of bird species, including the endemic Santa Cruz ground dove.

  3. Cultural Tours: Santa Cruz Islands offers a range of cultural tours where visitors can experience the traditional way of life of the local people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Cruz Islands

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at hotel

Day 2: Visit Jacques Cousteau Marine Reserve for snorkeling and scuba diving.

Day 3: Explore Santa Cruz Heritage Centre and Nena Sika Cultural Centre.

Day 4: Hike or bike along the Coral Trail.

Day 5: Visit Graciosa Bay for a day of swimming and sunbathing.

Day 6: Explore the Lomlom Airport WWII Trenches and the War Relics of Santa Cruz Islands.

Day 7: Departure from Santa Cruz Islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Cruz Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kokoda: This is a traditional dish that features diced raw fish marinated with lemon juice, salt, coconut cream, and a variety of vegetables.

  2. Tuluk: A local dish made of taro, coconut cream, and various other ingredients that can include coconut meat, fish, pork, or chicken.

Local Beverages

  1. Kava: A traditional drink made from the root of the kava plant, which has a tranquilizing effect.

  2. Palm Wine: Also known as “naonao,” this is a locally made beverage that is fermented from the sap of the coconut palm.

Where to Stay in Santa Cruz Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Santa Cruz Beach Bungalows: A beachfront property with uniquely designed bungalows

  2. Ferafalu Eco-lodge: A small eco-friendly lodge offering a range of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Santa Cruz Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Shell Necklaces: Made from finely crafted shells.

  2. Hand-carved Wooden Canoes: Made from wooden logs in a traditional fashion.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Cruz Islands

The people of Santa Cruz Islands are friendly and hospitable. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and traditions. The locals speak Pijin, which is a blend of English and local dialects.

Best Time to Visit Santa Cruz Islands

The best time to visit Santa Cruz Islands is between April and October, when the weather is dry and pleasant, with average temperatures around 27°C (81°F).

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Cruz Islands

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Dress modestly when visiting the villages or cultural sites.

  3. Carry enough cash, as most places do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  1. Swim or snorkel within 100 meters of the shore to avoid getting caught in the strong currents and sudden drop-offs.

  2. Touch or disturb the wildlife.

  3. Litter on the beaches or in the ocean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Cruz Islands

Although Santa Cruz Islands is considered safe, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep valuables in a safe place.

Health in Santa Cruz Islands

Visitors should take necessary precautions against malaria, yellow fever, and other tropical diseases. It is advisable to consult a doctor before traveling to Santa Cruz Islands.

Getting to Santa Cruz Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Cruz Islands

  1. Santa Cruz Island Airport: Located on Santa Cruz Island, this airport serves as the main gateway to Santa Cruz Islands.

  2. Nendo Island Airport: Located on Nendo Island, this airport offers flights to Santa Cruz Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Santa Cruz Islands by boat from Honiara, the capital of the Solomon Islands.

How to Get Around in Santa Cruz Islands

The best way to get around Santa Cruz Islands is by boat, as most of the attractions can only be accessed by water. Visitors can rent a boat or hire a local guide to take them around the islands.

Where to Go Next from Santa Cruz Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Savo Island: Located northeast of Santa Cruz Islands, Savo Island is known for its active volcano, hot springs, and pristine beaches.

  2. Guadalcanal Island: This large island is the capital of the Solomon Islands and offers a range of attractions, including the National Museum of Solomon Islands and the Central Market.

Conclusion

Santa Cruz Islands offer a unique and exciting travel destination, with its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and adventurous activities. Plan a visit to this idyllic island paradise and experience all that it has to offer!
