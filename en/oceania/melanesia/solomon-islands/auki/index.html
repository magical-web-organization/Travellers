Introduction to Auki

Auki is a picturesque town located in Malaita, one of the largest islands in the Solomon Islands, in Melanesia, in Oceania. The town boasts scenic views of the Pacific Ocean and lush tropical forests, making it an excellent destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Beyond the natural beauty, Auki is also steeped in culture and history, with numerous museums and sites of cultural significance that celebrate the traditions and customs of the Malaitan people. This guide will explore the best things to do and see in Auki, including natural attractions, cultural landmarks, hiking and biking trails, local food and drink, transportation options, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Auki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Auki is home to several museums and historical sites that provide insights into the traditions and customs of the Malaitan people. The most notable of these is the Langa Langa Lagoon Cultural Centre, which showcases local dance and music, handicrafts, and traditional ceremonies. Another essential cultural attraction is the Auki Market, where visitors can find an array of locally made crafts, produce, and food.

Natural Attractions of Auki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Auki offers a variety of outdoor activities and scenic views, including hiking and biking trails, water sports, and wildlife watching opportunities. One must-visit destination is the Nafinua Inland Lake, a serene freshwater lake nestled amidst lush forests, where visitors can swim, fish, and picnic. For hiking enthusiasts, the Mount Maetabe Trail offers stunning panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding hills. Alternatively, visitors can opt for a scenic biking trail along the coast, which offers an up-close encounter with the local villages and their daily lives.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auki

Auki has many picturesque hiking and biking routes that showcase its stunning natural beauty and cultural landmarks. The Mount Maetabe Trail is a popular hiking destination, which is a challenging 5-kilometer trail that takes visitors through dense forests and steep hills, providing stunning views of the surrounding hills and the Pacific Ocean. For biking enthusiasts, the coastal road from Auki to Langalanga offers a scenic ride with picturesque views of the ocean, the nearby islands, and the local villages.

Best Things to Do in Auki: Activities and Tours

A trip to Auki is incomplete without experiencing the local culture and taking part in outdoor activities. One activity that visitors should consider is island hopping, which allows them to explore the nearby islands and learn about their unique cultures and traditions. Visitors can also opt for a guided tour of the Langa Langa Lagoon Cultural Centre to learn about the local music, dance, and handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auki

Day 1: Explore the Auki Market and try some of the local food and drinks. Day 2: Visit the Nafinua Inland Lake and enjoy a picnic along its banks. Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Langa Langa Lagoon Cultural Centre. Day 4: Go Island hopping and explore the nearby islands. Day 5: Hike the Mt. Maetabe Trail and soak in the stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding hills. Day 6: Bike along the coastal road and visit the nearby villages to learn about the local customs and traditions. Day 7: Relax on the beach and soak up the tropical sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Auki

Must-Try Dishes

Auki’s food scene is a unique blend of local and western influences, with seafood and coconut milk being two primary ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes include fish curry, coconut rice, cassava pops, and sweet potato fries. Visitors can also try the local delicacy ‘Kakalu,’ which is made from grated taro root, mixed with coconut milk, and baked in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

One of the most popular drinks in Auki is coconut water, served straight from the tree with the husk removed. Visitors can also try local varieties of beer, such as Solbrew and Belikin, and indulge themselves with tropical fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Auki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auki offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to upscale hotels. Some of the top-rated hotels in Auki include Auki Lodge, Westwind Bungalow and Atkinsons Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Auki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Auki’s markets are well-known for their handmade crafts, shell jewelry, carvings, and woven baskets. Visitors can also buy sarongs, traditional dresses and shirts, and handwoven mats as souvenirs to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Auki

The Malaitan people have a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in their music, handicrafts, and traditional ceremonies. The Langa Langa Lagoon Cultural Centre is a must-visit destination to get an insight into their cultural art forms. Visitors should be mindful to respect local customs and traditions, dress modestly, and seek permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Auki

The best time to visit Auki is from May to October, when the weather is dry and sunny, and temperatures range between 24°C and 32°C. This period is also ideal for hiking and outdoor activities, as the trails are dry, and there are fewer rainfall disruptions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auki

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Learn some local phrases to communicate better with the locals

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior
  • Litter or harm the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Auki

Auki is a relatively safe place for visitors, but the usual precautionary measures should be followed, such as keeping valuables safe, staying in well-lit areas, and taking care while hiking on pathways.

Health in Auki

Visitors to Auki should be up to date with their vaccinations and take necessary precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It is advisable to bring mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Auki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Auki

The nearest airport to Auki is Gwaunaru’u Airport, which is situated 7 kilometers from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Auki

From the airport/mainland, visitors can take a bus or private taxi to Auki, which offers picturesque views of the island and local villages.

Other Options

Visitors can opt for a boat ride to explore the nearby islands or take a plane ride to other destinations in the Solomon Islands.

How to Get Around in Auki

Auki is a small town, and most places can be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire a private taxi for longer non-walking distances.

Where to Go Next from Auki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other destinations in the Solomon Islands such as Honiara, Guadalcanal, and Marovo Lagoon from Auki.

Conclusion

Auki is a beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventure. Visitors can immerse themselves in the rich culture and traditions of the Malaitan people, explore the lush forests, hike the challenging trails, and indulge in the local cuisine. Combined with its proximity to other unique destinations in the Solomon Islands, Auki is a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic, off-the-beaten-track adventure.
