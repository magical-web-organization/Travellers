Introduction to Guadalcanal

Guadalcanal is an Island situated in the Solomon Islands in Melanesia, Oceania. It is known for its tranquil atmosphere, secluded beaches, and great climate. Guadalcanal is a perfect destination for travelers who seek serenity and adventure.

In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, food, and drink scene, transportation options, and much more. So, get ready for your next exciting adventure in Guadalcanal.

List of Places to Visit in Guadalcanal

Guadalcanal has a rich cultural and historic background, with a range of exciting places to visit. Here are some must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Solomon Islands National Museum The National Museum is the perfect place to learn about the country’s history, culture, and people. The museum has a vast collection of Solomon Island artifacts, including traditional crafts, tools, canoes, and more.

  • Japanese War Memorial The Japanese War Memorial is a site of great historical significance. It honors the Japanese soldiers who died during the battle for Guadalcanal in World War II. There is a beautiful garden and a commemorative wall with names of the soldiers.

  • Tenaru Falls Tenaru Falls is a beautiful waterfall located in the southeastern part of the island. The fall is surrounded by lush green forests and mountains, providing an excellent place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Guadalcanal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kokoda Trail The Kokoda Trail is a challenging 96-kilometer hike that provides visitors with an opportunity to explore the rugged mountains and dense forests of Guadalcanal. The trail is known for its beautiful views and historical significance.

  • Mataniko Falls Mataniko Falls is located about 20 minutes from the Honiara Central Business District and is a popular spot to cool off in the hot summer months. Visitors can picnic alongside the river or take a refreshing swim in the waterfall pools.

  • Tetepare Island Tetepare Island is a pristine island located west of Guadalcanal. It is known for its marine life, including dolphins, turtles, and a variety of fish. It is the perfect location for diving, snorkeling, and beachcombing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guadalcanal

Guadalcanal offers a range of beautiful hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails that will give you a unique experience along with scenic views:

  • Tenaru Falls Trek The Tenaru Falls trek is one of the island’s most popular trails and takes approximately two hours to complete. The trek follows the Tenaru River and leads to the breathtaking Tenaru Falls.

  • Mt. Austen, The Galloping Horse, and Bloody Ridge Trail This challenging trail gives you a glimpse of the battle that took place during the World War II. It is a 3-hour hike that takes you through the dense jungle of Guadalcanal to the top of Mount Austen, where you can see tremendous views of the island.

  • Vavaya Ridge Trail This is a relatively easy hiking trail that offers stunning views of Guadalcanal’s north coast.

Best Things to Do in Guadalcanal: Activities and Tours

Guadalcanal is a destination that provides unique experiences packed with exciting activities and tours. Here are some must-do activities during your visit to Guadalcanal:

  • Dolphin Watching and Snorkeling Dolphins are one of the most joyful sea creatures. You can take a boat ride along the north coast of Guadalcanal island, where you may see dolphins swimming and playing in the water. The boat ride usually includes snorkeling in the clear blue waters, where you can see a wide variety of marine life.

  • Visiting Local Villages Guadalcanal island has several local villages, where you can experience local customs and culture, learn about traditional crafts, and engage with the locals. There are also opportunities for homestays, where you can experience local lifestyles and interact with the hospitable locals.

  • Bird Watching Guadalcanal has a wide variety of bird species, including the Palm Cockatoo, Yellow-bibbed Fruit-dove, and the White-rumped Shama. The island has several forests and wetlands that provide perfect habitats for these species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Guadalcanal

Day 1: Arrival and orientation in Honiara.
Day 2: Day visit to Japanese War Memorial and the National Museum.
Day 3: Hiking the Kokoda Trail.
Day 4: Dolphin Watching and snorkeling along the northern coast.
Day 5: Cultural Village Visit and Homestay.
Day 6: Beachcombing and swimming at Tetepare Island.
Day 7: Last day for shopping before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Guadalcanal

Guadalcanal has a diverse food and drink scene, which is heavily influenced by traditional cooking styles and ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Curry Curry is a popular dish in the Solomon Islands, and Fish Curry is one of the most delicious variations. It is typically prepared using fresh seafood, coconut milk, and spices.

  • Sipi Sipi is a traditional Solomon Island dish that includes boiled meat, vegetables, and coconut milk.

  • Chocolate Cake Chocolate Cake is a popular dessert in Guadalcanal Island, and it’s typically made using local cocoa powder.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water Fresh coconut water is widely available in Guadalcanal and is a refreshing drink to cool off after a long hike.

  • Kava Kava is a traditional drink that has been used for decades in the Solomon Islands. It is prepared by pounding the roots of the Kava plant and then mixing it with water.

Where to Stay in Guadalcanal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guadalcanal offers a range of accommodations for travelers of all kinds. From luxury hotels to backpacker hostels, there is something to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Heritage Park Hotel The Heritage Park Hotel is a high-end hotel with stunning ocean views. It has an outdoor pool and bar, an onsite restaurant, and fitness facilities.

  • YHA Guadalcanal YHA Guadalcanal is a budget-friendly hostel located in Honiara. It provides comfortable dormitory-style rooms for backpackers and budget travelers.

  • Toa Guesthouse Toa Guesthouse is a family-owned and operated guesthouse in the heart of Honiara. It has six rooms that provide a cozy and comforting stay with a unique cultural experience.

What to Buy in Guadalcanal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Guadalcanal is known for its exceptional handicrafts and traditional products. Here are some of the best souvenirs that you can buy in Guadalcanal:

  • Basket Weaving Locals weave beautiful baskets and bags, which make for practical and aesthetic souvenirs.

  • Carvings Guadalcanal Island is known for producing unique and intricate carvings, including mask, sculptures and figures that make a great art collection.

Local Culture and Customs of Guadalcanal

Guadalcanal Island has a unique and vibrant culture, heavily influenced by Melanesian and Polynesian traditions. Here are some customs that you should respect during your stay:

  • Respect the Elders In the Solomon Islands, respecting the elders is essential, and you should always address them with respect and courtesy.

  • Dress Appropriately The Solomon Islanders are conservative, and it is important to dress modestly to show respect. It is best to avoid wearing revealing clothes when visiting villages, markets, or other public areas.

Best Time to Visit Guadalcanal

The best time to visit Guadalcanal is during the dry season, from May to October. The weather is warm and dry, and the water is perfect for swimming and water sports. The wet season, which runs from November to April, can make hiking trails difficult to navigate and cause flash flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guadalcanal

Do:

  • Engage with the Locals The people of Guadalcanal are friendly and hospitable; engaging with them can make your trip even more memorable.

  • Respect the Environment Guadalcanal’s environment is fragile, and travelers should be mindful of their impact and take responsibility by not littering or damaging natural resources.

Don’t:

  • Wander Around Unsafe Areas Some areas of Guadalcanal can be unsafe, especially at night. It is best to stay in well-lit and populated areas and avoid venturing into unfamiliar areas.

  • Disrespect Local Customs The Solomon Islanders have their unique customs, and it’s essential to respect them. Always ask for permission before taking pictures or engaging in any activities in their villages or during traditional ceremonies.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guadalcanal

Guadalcanal is relatively safe, but travelers should take precautions and be mindful of their surroundings. Here are a few safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay Alert Always be aware of your surroundings and any potential dangers in crowded areas, marketplaces, and public areas.

  • Transportation Avoid hitchhiking, and opt for registered taxis or other modes of transportation to ensure safety.

Health in Guadalcanal

Travelers should take necessary precautions and be up-to-date with their vaccinations. They should also avoid raw or undercooked foods, and drink only bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Guadalcanal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Guadalcanal can be reached through its international airport (Henderson International Airport). The airport is located about 10 kilometers east of Honiara, the capital city. Here are some transportation options for getting around in Guadalcanal:

  • Taxis Taxis are readily available at Henderson airport and throughout Honiara. Taxis are usually shared, and fares are typically negotiated with the driver.

  • Buses Public buses are available from Honiara, and they are an affordable way to travel within the island. However, buses can be overcrowded, and schedules can be unreliable.

  • Car Rentals Car rentals are available in Honiara, and it is an excellent option for travelers who prefer private transportation. Drivers will need a valid international driver’s license.

How to Get Around in Guadalcanal

Getting around Guadalcanal is relatively easy, with several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and car rentals. For short distances, you can also rent bicycles or hitchhike in rural areas.

Where to Go Next from Guadalcanal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guadalcanal is a good starting point to explore other islands in the Solomon Islands. Here are some popular destinations:

  • Munda Munda is an island in the Western Province of the Solomon Islands. It is known for its World War II wreck-diving sites, beautiful beaches, and coral reefs.

  • Honiara Honiara is the capital city of the Solomon Islands and is home to the National Museum, markets, restaurants, and a vibrant nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Guadalcanal Island is a destination that provides unique experiences and a relaxing atmosphere to travelers. The island’s scenic beauty, cultural and historical attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local food make it an excellent place for adventure and relaxation. Plan your visit to Guadalcanal, and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
