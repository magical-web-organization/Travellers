Introduction to Lenakel

Located in the southern part of Tanna Island in Vanuatu, Lenakel is a charming town that offers a unique glimpse into the Melanesian culture. It is famous for its breathtaking volcanic landscapes, fascinating cultural attractions, and warm hospitality of its locals. Lenakel is a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the natural beauty and traditions of Vanuatu.

Food and Drink Scene of Lenakel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lap-lap (baked pudding made with yam or taro)
  • Tuluk (smoked meat and vegetable wrapped in banana leaves)
  • Naka-baur (steamed breadfruit pudding)
  • Fish dishes (tuna, marlin, and mahi-mahi)

Local Beverages

  • Kava (traditional drink made from the root of the kava plant)
  • Coconut water

Local Culture and Customs of Lenakel

  • Respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.
  • Greet locals with a smile and learn a few phrases in the local language.
  • Do not touch anyone’s head as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Attend a kastom dance show and experience the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Lenakel

The best time to visit Lenakel is during the dry season from April to October when the weather is sunny and comfortable for outdoor activities. The wet season from November to March brings heavy rains but also offers a lush green landscape, making it a great time for nature lovers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lenakel

Do:

  • Support the local economy by purchasing handicrafts and locally made products.
  • Ask for permission before taking a photo of someone or something
  • Wear comfortable and appropriate clothing when visiting cultural sites and villages.

Don’t:

  • Swim or snorkel near hot springs and volcanic vents.
  • Take anything from the land or ocean without permission from the local chiefs.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lenakel

  • Travel with a local guide or join a reputable tour company.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and other insect-borne diseases.
  • Be cautious when swimming or diving as strong currents and rough waves can be dangerous.

Health in Lenakel

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Visit the local health center if you feel unwell or need medical attention.

Getting to Lenakel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lenakel

  • Tanna Airport (code:TAH)

Bus Stations Near Lenakel

  • Bus service is limited and mostly used by locals.

Other Options

  • Private taxis or shared minivans

How to Get Around in Lenakel

  • Walking or hiking is the best way to explore Lenakel and its surroundings.
  • Taxis and minivans are available for hire.
