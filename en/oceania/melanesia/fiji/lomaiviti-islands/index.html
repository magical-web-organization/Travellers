Introduction to Lomaiviti Islands

Located in Fiji, in Melanesia, in Oceania, the Lomaiviti Islands are a hidden paradise waiting to be explored. This group of islands is known for its crystal-clear waters, stunning coral reefs, and verdant mountain ranges. The archipelago comprises seven inhabited and numerous uninhabited islands, each with unique cultural, historical, and natural attractions. Lomaiviti Islands promises not only relaxation but also outdoor adventures and cultural experiences.

Whether you’re seeking a serene beach getaway or an adrenaline-pumping escapade, Lomaiviti Islands has it all. This travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in Fiji.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lomaiviti Islands

The islands offer several trails for nature enthusiasts who love to hike or bike.

  • Mount Uluigalau Trek: This formidable mountain ranges to 2753 feet, rising high above the sea. The trek to the summit offers stunning views across the Pacific Ocean.
  • Across the Cross Island Walk: A beautiful stroll over to the other side of the island and down through the rainforest to the beautiful Honeymoon Beach on the opposite side of Ovalau

Week-Long Itinerary for Lomaiviti Islands

Day 1-2: Arrival in Levuka and Cultural Tours

Check-in at your hotel and explore the iconic Levuka Historical Port Town. Visit Ovalau Island Cultural Centre and Lomaiviti Heritage Centre to learn about the island’s rich history and culture.

Day 3-4: Outdoor Adventures

Explore the Great Astrolabe Reef, one of the largest barrier reefs in the world, through snorkeling or diving tours. Take a guided hike to Mount Uluigalau or go for a bike ride on the island trails.

Day 5-6: Day Trips to Yasawa Islands and Naviti Island

Sail to the remote Yasawa Islands and visit the famous Sawa-i-Lau Caves. Stop by the picturesque Naviti Island and enjoy a day of water activities or secluded beach relaxation.

Day 7: Departure

Spend your last day exploring the local villages, shopping for souvenirs, and relaxing at the beach before your departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Lomaiviti Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoda: a local Fijian ceviche made from fresh fish, coconut milk and lime juice.
  • Rourou: Spinach cooked in coconut milk similar to creamed or buttered spinach.
  • Coconut rice pudding: A dessert-like rice pudding made with coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Tanoa: Traditional Fijian Kava drink made from the root of the kava plant
  • Coconut Water: Refreshing and loaded with electrolytes

Where to Stay in Lomaiviti Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Royal Hotel - This iconic historical hotel has been around since the 1800s, housing some of Fiji’s most famous visitors.
  • Levuka Homestays: Discover the local culture with homestays that offer comfortable and affordable accommodation.
  • Oarsmans Bay Lodge - A luxurious eco-friendly resort on the Yasawa Islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Lomaiviti Islands

Fijians are known for their friendly and hospitable nature, so it’s essential to respect the local customs and culture. The culture is a mix of Melanesian and Polynesian traditions, and it’s customary to remove your shoes when visiting local homes or temple.

Best Time to Visit Lomaiviti Islands

Fiji’s wet season lasts from November to April, and it’s best to visit the Lomaiviti Islands during the dry season from May to October. The weather is pleasant during this period, with temperatures ranging from the mid-70s to the mid-80s.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lomaiviti Islands

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting local villages or religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Leave your shoes on when entering a local home or temple
  • Touch people’s heads or feet as they are considered sacred

Safety and Health

Safety in Lomaiviti Islands

Fiji is a relatively safe country to visit, but tourists should take basic safety precautions and avoid unlit areas or remote trails during the night.

Health in Lomaiviti Islands

It’s a good idea to carry a medical kit with you during your trip. Bring supplies for minor injuries, insect bites, and stomach troubles. Make sure to use insect repellent and cover up to avoid mosquito bites that can transmit diseases.

Getting to Lomaiviti Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lomaiviti Islands

The nearest airport to the Lomaiviti Islands is Suva’s Nausori Airport, a 45-minute drive to Levuka. Other nearby airports are Nadi International Airport and Lautoka Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lomaiviti Islands

Fiji has an extensive bus network that connects major cities and towns to rural areas. Buses are affordable and comfortable, but expect longer travel times due to the road conditions.

Other Options

Ferry services are available from Suva to Levuka and other islands in the Lomaiviti group. Private boat charters are also a popular option for tourists who want to explore the islands at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Lomaiviti Islands

Most tourist attractions are within walking distance from town centers, but taxis and minibusses are available for longer destination travel.
