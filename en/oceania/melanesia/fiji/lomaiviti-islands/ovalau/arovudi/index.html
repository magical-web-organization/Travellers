Looking for a remote destination to unwind and relax? Arovudi, located on the Ovalau Island in Fiji, is undoubtedly the perfect spot for you! This mesmerizing island offers numerous cultural and natural attractions, stunning scenic views, and plenty of adventurous activities. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll show you everything Arovudi has to offer, from the best places to visit to the must-try local dishes. Let’s begin!

Introduction to Arovudi

Nestled along the pristine waters of the Pacific Ocean, Arovudi is located on the eastern side of the Ovalau Island in the Lomaiviti Island group in Fiji. This quiet fishing village boasts stunning views of the ocean and the greenery on the island. Its exotic culture, friendly people, and endless recreational activities make Arovudi a perfect destination for travelers who seek to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Arovudi

When it comes to places to visit in Arovudi, there is no shortage of options. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Arovudi divided into two categories, culturally important attractions, natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Levuka Historical Port Town: Levuka was once the capital of Fiji and has a rich history. The Levuka Historical Port Town showcases a glimpse of the past through numerous restaurants, housing, majestic architecture, and an abundance of natural beauty.

  2. Arovudi and Levuka Parish Church: The Arovudi and Levuka Parish Church is Fiji’s oldest church, built-in 1852. The church’s beauty is simply breathtaking, and visitors are intrigued by its unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Arovudi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Nasau Park: Nasau Park is a paradise for nature lovers. This huge park has dozens of beautiful walking tracks with an abundance of flora and fauna. The waterfall inside the park is a major attraction, where visitors can take a refreshing bath in the crystal-clear water.

  2. Ghost Village: The Ghost Village, located in the interior of the island, is a hidden treasure. It is said that the village is untouched since the last inhabitants were expelled in the 1800s. Numerous myths and legends surround the Ghost Village, making it a must-visit spot for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arovudi

Arovudi is a perfect location for hikers and bike enthusiasts. The hilly terrain and lush vegetation make it a haven for enthusiasts. The following are some of the most popular hiking/biking routes in Arovudi.

  1. The Cross Island Walk: The Cross Island Walk, a 7-hour trek through the interior of the island, reveals beautiful landscapes and the natural beauty of Fijian rainforests. Waterfalls and natural swimming pools on the way make it a perfect spot for a dip.

  2. Mountain Biking: The rugged countryside of Arovudi offers a unique mountain biking experience. The journey through the narrow pathways surrounded by lush greenery is nothing short of invigorating.

Best Things to Do in Arovudi: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: The crystal clear waters around Arovudi make it a fantastic spot for snorkeling and scuba diving. The colorful marine life and pristine coral reefs can be seen while participating in these activities.

  2. Jet Skiing and Kayaking: The aquamarine water around Arovudi also provides visitors with the opportunity to participate in jet skiing and kayaking, making it a memorable experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Arovudi

Food is an essential part of any culture, and Arovudi’s culture is rich in flavors. The following are the must-try dishes and local beverages during your visit to Arovudi.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kokoda: Kokoda is a raw fish salad marinated in lemon or lime juice and served with coconut cream.

  2. Lovo: Lovo is Fiji’s popular dish that involves slow-cooking meat and vegetables in an earth oven steam pit, giving it a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  1. Kava: Kava is Fiji’s national drink. It is made by pounding the root of the kava plant, mixing it with water, and serving it in a coconut bowl.

  2. Fiji Bitter: Fiji Bitter is a locally brewed beer with a refreshing taste, ideal for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Arovudi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arovudi offers visitors a range of accommodation options. From luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay in Arovudi.

  1. Ovalau Holiday Resort: Ovalau Holiday Resort offers guests a luxurious stay and a range of activities, including kayaking, diving, and hiking.

  2. Arovudi Guesthouse: Arovudi Guesthouse offers budget-friendly accommodations, a warm and friendly staff, and a gateway to discover the Arovudi culture.

What to Buy in Arovudi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arovudi has a vast collection of local products that can make the perfect souvenirs or gifts for loved ones. Here are some of the things travelers can buy:

  1. Tapa Cloth: Tapa cloth is a traditional Fijian cloth made from plant fibers and decorated with tribal designs. It is a popular souvenir among travelers.

  2. Handmade Crafts: The local craftsmen in Arovudi make handicrafts such as mats, baskets, and fans, which can serve as an excellent souvenir to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Arovudi

The Fijian culture is rich and diverse, and Arovudi is no exception. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are always impressed by the Fijian way of life. Here are some aspects of the Fijian culture that visitors should know:

  1. Traditional Ceremonies: Fijians are proud of their traditions, and visitors are welcome to attend the cultural ceremonies, such as Meke and Lovo.

  2. Respect: Visitors are requested to respect the local customs. For instance, it is inappropriate to wear revealing clothing in public.

Best Time to Visit Arovudi

The best time to visit Arovudi is from May to October when the weather is dry and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arovudi

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs.

  2. Carry mosquito repellent while going outdoors.

Don’t:

  1. Photograph locals without permission.

  2. Wear revealing clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arovudi

Arovudi is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take the usual precautions like locking their hotel rooms and keeping a watchful eye on their belongings.

Health in Arovudi

Tourists visiting Arovudi should carry mosquito repellent and protect themselves against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Arovudi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arovudi Island is accessible from the main island of Viti Levu by air or sea.

Airports Near Arovudi

The nearest airport to Arovudi is Nausori International Airport on Viti Levu. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight or water taxi to Ovalau Island.

Bus Stations Near Arovudi

Buses run daily from Viti Levu to Suva and then take a ferry to Ovalau Island, where Arovudi is located.

Other Options

Water taxis are available from Suva, which takes around three hours to reach Arovudi Island.

How to Get Around in Arovudi

The best way to get around in Arovudi is on foot or by taxi. Visitors can also hire a bike or scooter to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Arovudi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Arovudi’s natural beauty and cultural attractions, visitors can venture out to the nearby destinations:

  1. Taveuni Island: Taveuni Island is a natural paradise with waterfalls, diving spots, and hiking trails.

  2. Yanuca Island: Yanuca Island is a fantastic place to relax with luxurious resorts and white sandy beaches.

Conclusion

Whether you love nature, adventure, culture, or just relaxation, Arovudi has something for you. This beautiful Fijian island offers countless activities and attractions to visitors, making it an ideal destination to escape the daily grind of city life. We hope our comprehensive guide to Arovudi helps you plan a memorable trip filled with adventure and relaxation. Bon voyage!
