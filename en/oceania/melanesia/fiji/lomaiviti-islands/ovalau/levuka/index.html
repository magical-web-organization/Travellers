Introduction to Levuka

Levuka is a town located on the island of Ovalau in the Lomaiviti Islands of Fiji. It is considered one of the most charming and historical towns in Fiji, attracting travelers who love nature, history, and culture. The town was once the capital city of Fiji and served as the center of trade, culture, and politics in the 19th century. Today, it is known for its scenic views, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures.

Food and Drink Scene of Levuka

Levuka offers a unique culinary experience that combines traditional Fijian cuisine and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Levuka.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoda (Fijian ceviche)
  • Palusami (taro leaves baked in coconut milk)
  • Lovo (a traditional Fijian feast cooked in an underground oven)
  • Kava (a traditional Fijian drink made from a pepper plant)

Local Beverages

  • Fiji Bitter (a local beer brand)
  • Bounty Rum (a local rum brand)
  • Mango smoothie (made with fresh Fiji mangoes)

Local Culture and Customs of Levuka

Fiji is known for its friendly and hospitable locals, and Levuka is no exception. When visiting Levuka, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting villages and temples
  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home
  • Avoid wearing a hat when entering a Fijian village
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of locals

Best Time to Visit Levuka

The best time to visit Levuka is during the dry season, which is from May to October. The weather is cooler and less humid during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, prices may be higher during peak season, so budget accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Levuka

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Levuka:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the natural attractions of Levuka
  • Take a cultural tour of a nearby village

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals or their beliefs
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting villages or temples
  • Touch or damage the coral reef when snorkeling or diving
  • Litter or leave trash on the beach

Safety and Health

Levuka is generally safe for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions and follow local safety guidelines. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Levuka

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place

Health in Levuka
