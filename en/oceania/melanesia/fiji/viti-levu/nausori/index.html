Introduction to Nausori

Nausori is a town located in the province of Rewa on the island of Viti Levu, Fiji. It is situated on the banks of the Rewa River and is a hub for local trade and commerce. Nausori is known for its cultural landmarks and natural beauty. This travel guide will take you on a tour of the best places to visit in Nausori, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Nausori

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Nausori can explore the town’s rich cultural heritage through its museums and historical places. The Fiji Museum is one of the top attractions in the area, where you can learn about Fiji’s past, its people, and its culture. Another must-visit location is the Colo-i-Suva Forest Park, which is home to breathtaking waterfalls, hiking trails and some of Fiji’s most diverse wildlife. Those interested in religion should definitely visit the Mariamma Temple, which is dedicated to the Hindu goddess of rain.

Natural Attractions of Nausori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There are plenty of natural attractions in Nausori that will take your breath away. The Orchid Island Cultural Centre & Waterfall Walk is famous for its rare orchids, calming waterfalls and fresh air, and the Waisila Rainforest Reserve is a fantastic nature trail that dazzles visitors with stunning views of Nausori while delivering a rewarding hiking experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nausori

Nausori is also home to some beautiful hiking/biking trails that are perfect for anyone looking for an outdoor adventure. Some of the most famous routes include the Mount Tomanivi hike, which is Fiji’s highest peak and offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys, and the Koroyanitu National Heritage Park, which has a network of well-maintained hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Nausori: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Nausori, there are plenty of options. Lighthouse Dive Centre is a popular spot for scuba diving, while The Pearl Championship Golf Course is perfect for golf enthusiasts. Nausori is also a great destination for white-water rafting, and handy travel agents can set you up with a tour by boat, plane, bike or on foot.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nausori

If you’re planning a trip to Nausori, we recommend spending at least a week here to really experience everything that this region has to offer. Here’s a great itinerary to get you started:

Day 1: Arrive and check in to your hotel. Day 2: Visit the Fiji Museum. Day 3: Go hiking at Mount Tomanivi. Day 4: Explore the Colo-i-Suva Forest Park. Day 5: Take a white-water rafting tour. Day 6: Visit the Orchid Island Cultural Centre & Waterfall Walk. Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Suva.

Food and Drink Scene of Nausori

Must-Try Dishes

Fijian cuisine is a fusion of Indian, Chinese, and local ingredients. Each dish is infused with aromatic spices that tantalize the taste buds. Some dishes that you absolutely must try while in Nausori include Kokoda, which consists of raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut milk, and Lovo, which is various meats cooked in an underground oven using hot stones.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Fijian kava is a must-try. This drink comes from the root of the kava plant and is known for its calming effects. For those looking for something less potent, coconut water is a great thirst quencher.

Where to Stay in Nausori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For those looking for accommodations in Nausori, the options are plenty. From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts, there are plenty of options for a comfortable stay. Some popular choices include The Pearl Resort, which is a beautiful resort located on a white sandy beach, and the Novotel Suva Lami Bay, which is situated on the edge of the South Pacific Ocean.

What to Buy in Nausori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists can purchase a range of souvenirs and local products in Nausori. Some popular items include shell jewelry, woven baskets, and traditional wooden carvings. You’ll find them all at the local outdoor markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Nausori

The locals in Nausori are friendly and welcoming to visitors. It is important to dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites. Fiji has a mostly Christian and Hindu population, and visitors should be respectful of their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Nausori

Nausori can be visited year-round. The peak season is from July to September, which is the driest time of the year. However, if you’re looking for a good deal and fewer crowds, consider visiting during the low season from November to April when hotel rates are low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nausori

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and cultural sites.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Don’t carry large sums of money or valuables with you.
  • Don’t disrespect the local traditions, such as leaving your hat on inside a temple.
  • Don’t take pictures of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nausori

Nausori is relatively safe for tourists. However, visitors should always exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when travelling alone at night or carrying valuable possessions. For transportation, we recommend private cars and taxis.

Health in Nausori

No special vaccinations are required when visiting Nausori, but visitors should make sure they are up-to-date on their routine vaccinations. It’s also important to protect against mosquito bites, as dengue fever and malaria are prevalent in the region.

Getting to Nausori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nausori

The closest airport is the Nausori International Airport, which is a 10-minute drive from the town center. Alternatively, visitors can fly into the larger Nadi International Airport and take a domestic flight from there.

Bus Stations Near Nausori

Several bus stations are located in Nausori and connect the town to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars from rental companies or hire a private driver.

How to Get Around in Nausori

The best way to get around Nausori is by taxi or rental car. There are also buses available that run between different towns and cities in the region.

Where to Go Next from Nausori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to Suva, the capital city of Fiji, which is just a short drive away. There are several other nearby islands like Lautoka and Yasawa Islands that offer breathtaking scenery for a relaxing beach trip.

Conclusion

Nausori is a destination that is worth exploring. With its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, it is a place that tourists can truly fall in love with. Plan your next trip to the Melbourne of Fiji, and immerse yourself in everything this charming town has to offer.
