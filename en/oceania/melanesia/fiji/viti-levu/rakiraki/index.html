Introduction to Rakiraki

Located in the northern part of Viti Levu, the largest island in Fiji, Rakiraki is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by travel enthusiasts. This beautiful town is known for its rich cultural heritage, outdoor recreational activities, scenic landscapes, and delicious cuisine. Rakiraki is a perfect destination for travelers who love history, adventure, and relaxation. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of Rakiraki, Fiji.

List of Places to Visit in Rakiraki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Make sure to stop by the impressive historical landmarks and museums in Rakiraki:

  • Nananu-i-Ra Island: Nananu-i-Ra Island is a popular destination for adventure-seekers, with hiking trails and scenic views overlooking the glittering blue waters.
  • Navatu Rock Fort: Navatu Rock Fort is the cultural heritage site where Chief Ravu conquered and defended himself against raiding tribes.
  • Wairiki Missionary Station: Wairiki Missionary Station is a historical site where Reverend William Cross established the first ever church in Fiji.

Natural Attractions of Rakiraki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rakiraki is a hub for natural adventure and scenic views.

  • The Secret Island Beach: The Secret Island Beach is an isolated spot ideal for a peaceful and serene day lying on the sand, coral reef, and swift oceanic currents.
  • Rafting on the Navua River: Take on the river rapids of the Navua River in Fiji.
  • Rakiraki Trail: Get a glimpse of the traditional villages of Rakiraki while hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rakiraki

If nature hiking or mountain biking is your thing, then Rakiraki is the destination for you! Although the trails have different degrees of difficulties and length.

  • Venture to the Wainunu Waterfall: One of the most beautiful waterfalls in the area and a joy to hike too.
  • Get involved in the Coral Coast Cycle Race: Held twice a year with individual or group categories suitable for all skill levels

Best Things to Do in Rakiraki: Activities and Tours

Rakiraki offers endless possibilities for activities suitable for all age groups and interests.

  • Go fishing: Rakiraki has some of the best fishing spots in Fiji, with plenty of different species of fish to catch.
  • [Take advantage of the town beach] (https://ohanabeachfrontresort.com/fiji-surfing-spots/): The town beach is always free and the perfect spot to catch some waves. Best surfing is between November and April
  • Watch Shark feeding at Bligh Waters: Taking a boat tours is the best way to see wild sharks without too much effort for a memorable sight.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rakiraki

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Rakiraki, Fiji, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day 1: Arrive and settle in, and take a refreshing swim in the waterfalls or at the town beach.

Day 2: Visit the Rakiraki Museum and the culturally significant buildings and forts, like Navatu Rock Fort and Wairiki Missionary Station.

Day 3: Go biking or hiking on the various trails around town.

Day 4: Experience the thrill of rafting down the Navua River.

Day 5: Take a trip to The Secret Island Beach to relax and soak up the sun.

Day 6: Go shark watching at Bligh Waters for unforgettable boat tours and stunning scenes.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the local markets in town and enjoy the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Rakiraki

Must-Try Dishes

One of the top must-try dishes in Rakiraki is Kokoda, a signature Fijian dish made of raw fish marinated in citrus juices. Additionally, the town’s local cuisine is also famous for its delectable seafood like prawns and lobsters, plus fresh tropical fruits.

Local Beverages

Rakiraki provides plenty of options in the drinks department. The perfect way to wind down or start the evening is always with one of the famous Fijian beers or tropical cocktails specially concocted with local fruits and spices.

Where to Stay in Rakiraki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rakiraki offers a myriad of affordable accommodation options, from backpacker hostels, bed and breakfasts, fancy hotels and resorts. Some popular options include:

  • Tanoa Rakiraki Hotel: a moderate-level hotel/resort offering an array of amenities such as rejuvenation spa treatment and outdoor activities like tennis and volleyball.

  • Wananavu Beach Resort: A luxurious beach resort that aims to provide holidaymakers with authentic Fijian hospitality and distinguished faclities.

What to Buy in Rakiraki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some traditional local and ethnic handicrafts in Rakiraki trade include pottery, woven mats, baskets, fans, shell jewellery, wood carvings and Tapa cloth.

Local Culture and Customs of Rakiraki

Fijians cherish their traditions and customs. When traveling through Rakiraki, it is always best to respect their culture and interact with locals through formalities of “Bula!” (greeting) and “Vinaka!” (thank you).

Best Time to Visit Rakiraki

Rakiraki enjoys a tropical climate that’s typically warm across the year. The dry season runs from May to October, and the wet season is from January to April. Before scheduling a trip to Rakiraki, research about the weather forecast.

############ Do:’s and Don’ts of Rakiraki

  • Do: Respect local culture and traditions, spend quality time with locals, and explore Rakiraki’s natural treasures.
  • Don’t: Dress provocatively, carry or bring illegal drugs, litter around, or touch any cultural or sacred spots without guidance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rakiraki

Rakiraki is generally safe, but as in any other place, take ordinary precautions, like walking in well-lit areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Rakiraki

It is essential to take sufficient precautions and stay hygienic to avoid any possible infections or diseases. Vaccines and medication can be suggested by your doctor or the medical staff of the hotel or resort.

Getting to Rakiraki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rakiraki

Closest airports include:

  • Nadi International Airport: Located 120km south of Rakiraki, with direct international flights from the U.S., Asia, Australia, and New Zealand.
  • Nausori International Airport: Located 67km southeast of Rakiraki, offering domestic flights from Fiji’s other islands.

Bus Stations Near Rakiraki

Many buses connect Rakiraki with other parts of Viti Levu and the rest of Fiji, with frequent and cheap trip schedules.

Other Options

  • Taxis

  • Car rentals

How to Get Around in Rakiraki

While traveling in Rakiraki, many tourists choose rental cars to take them around town, but two buses go through Rakiraki daily, which are cheaper.

Where to Go Next from Rakiraki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and side trips to explore to make the most of your visit to Rakiraki include:

  • Suva City: Explore the site of Fiji’s capital where visitors can relish mouth-watering food and colonial architecture buildings.
  • Lautoka City: Nicknamed the sugar city in recognition of its local sugar cane plantations, Lautoka offers a colorful cultural experience.
  • The Coral Coast: Packed with lush green forests and sun-drenched beaches, The Coral Coast is easily accessible from Rakiraki and stuffed with unusual sights and adventures.

Conclusion

With its rich history, fantastic outdoor activities, and delicious food, Rakiraki is undoubtedly a hidden gem of Fiji. This guide provides you with all the information you need to make the most of your trip to Rakiraki, Fiji. Get your bags packed and discover the beauty of this idyllic destination.
