Introduction to Viti Levu

Viti Levu is the largest island in Fiji and is known for its stunning natural beauty and cultural landmarks. The island is home to the capital city of Suva and the major towns of Nadi, Lautoka, and Sigatoka.

With a diverse range of activities and attractions, Viti Levu offers something for every type of traveler. From scenic hiking trails and breathtaking beaches to culturally significant museums and historical landmarks, Viti Levu is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Fiji.

List of Places to Visit in Viti Levu

Here is a list of must-see places to visit on your trip to Viti Levu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Fiji Museum - Discover the rich history and culture of Fiji at the Fiji Museum, located in Suva. The museum features exhibits on traditional Fijian art, culture, and history.

  2. Sri Siva Subramaniya Temple - The largest Hindu temple in the Southern Hemisphere, Sri Siva Subramaniya Temple is a beautiful example of Dravidian architecture. The temple is located in Nadi and is a must-see destination for anyone interested in Hindu culture and spirituality.

  3. Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park - This national park is home to 2,600 acres of sand dunes, archaeological sites, and picturesque beaches. Learn about the ancient Fijian culture at the park’s museum and take a scenic nature hike along the sand dunes.

Natural Attractions of Viti Levu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Coral Coast - The Coral Coast is a beautiful stretch of coastline between Nadi and Suva. The area is known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and abundance of water activities including snorkeling, diving, and fishing.

  2. Colo-I-Suva Forest Park - Located just outside Suva, the Colo-I-Suva Forest Park is a popular destination for hiking and enjoying nature. The park features hiking trails through lush rainforests, waterfalls, and swimming holes.

  3. Navua River - Take a scenic river cruise along the stunning Navua River, located just south of Suva. The river is surrounded by lush jungle and provides opportunities for swimming, kayaking, and river rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Viti Levu

Viti Levu is a hiker’s paradise with a range of trails suitable for all skill levels. Here are a few popular hiking and biking routes:

  1. Mount Korobaba - This moderately difficult hike near Suva offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and the Pacific Ocean. The hike takes about three hours and is suitable for experienced hikers.

  2. Navilawa Falls - The Navilawa Falls in the Namosi Highlands are a stunning natural wonder. The hike to the falls takes about two hours and is suitable for intermediate hikers.

  3. Toga Nature Trail - Located near the Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park, this scenic nature trail is suitable for all skill levels and provides stunning views of the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Viti Levu: Activities and Tours

  1. River Rafting - Experience the thrill of white water rafting on the Navua River with a guided tour. The tour includes a scenic river cruise and a thrilling river rafting adventure.

  2. Island Hopping - Take a guided tour of the nearby Mamanuca and Yasawa Islands, known for their stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters. Explore the top sights including the famous Cloud 9 floating bar and the Sawa-I-Lau Caves.

  3. Village Tour - Learn about the local Fijian culture and traditions with a guided village tour. Meet the locals, participate in traditional ceremonies and enjoy traditional Fijian feasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Viti Levu

Day 1: Arrival in Suva, check into hotel, visit the Fiji Museum.

Day 2: Take a morning hike up Mount Korobaba, explore Suva and its cultural landmarks in the afternoon.

Day 3: Visit the Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park and take a scenic nature hike.

Day 4: Take a river rafting tour on the Navua River.

Day 5: Explore the Coral Coast, visit beaches, and take a snorkeling tour.

Day 6: Take a guided village tour and learn about traditional Fijian culture.

Day 7: Departure from Suva.

Food and Drink Scene of Viti Levu

Fijian cuisine is a delightful blend of Indian, Chinese and Melanesian influences. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kokoda - A Fijian dish made with raw fish marinated in coconut milk and lime juice.

  2. Lovo - A traditional Fijian feast made with meat, fish, and vegetables cooked on hot stones.

  3. Cassava Cake - A sweet dessert made with cassava, coconut cream, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  1. Kava - A traditional Fijian drink made from crushed kava root. It has a mildly sedative effect and is enjoyed at social gatherings.

  2. Coconut Water - A refreshing and healthy drink made from fresh coconut water.

  3. Fiji Bitter Beer - A locally brewed beer, perfect for enjoying while relaxing on the beach.

Where to Stay in Viti Levu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Viti Levu offers a range of accommodation options for every budget from luxury hotels and resorts to hostels and guesthouses. Here are a few top picks:

  1. InterContinental Fiji Golf Resort & Spa - A luxurious beachfront resort located on Natadola Bay, offering modern accommodations and numerous amenities including a golf course, spa, and multiple restaurants.

  2. Uprising Beach Resort - A budget-friendly accommodation option located on the Coral Coast, offering comfortable guest rooms, a bar and restaurant, and a range of water activities.

  3. Tanoa Plaza Hotel - A centrally located hotel in Suva, offering affordable accommodations and numerous amenities including a fitness center, pool, and multiple dining options.

What to Buy in Viti Levu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are a few local products and souvenirs to bring home from Viti Levu:

  1. Bula Shirt - A colorful and traditional Fijian shirt.

  2. Handicrafts - From woven baskets and mats to carved wooden statues, there are numerous handicrafts made by local artisans that make for beautiful souvenirs.

  3. Pure Fijian Coconut Oil - Locally produced coconut oil is famed for its moisturizing and healing properties and makes for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Viti Levu

Fiji’s culture is a colorful blend of Melanesian, Polynesian, Indian, and European influences. Here are a few customs and traditions to be aware of while visiting Viti Levu:

  1. Kava Ceremonies - Kava is an important part of Fijian culture and is used in ceremonial events and social gatherings.

  2. Respect for Elders - Fijians have great respect for elders and it is customary to address them with the title “Kaivata”.

  3. Modest Dress - Fijians have a conservative dress sense and it is respectful to dress modestly, particularly when visiting temples and other cultural landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Viti Levu

The best time to visit Viti Levu is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. This is the peak tourist season, and the weather is perfect for enjoying outdoor activities and exploring the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Viti Levu

Do:

  1. Respect Local Customs - Fiji’s culture is a vibrant and colorful blend of traditions, customs, and rituals. Respect local customs and traditions, and ask for permission before taking photographs of locals or participating in religious ceremonies.

  2. Learn Some Fijian - Fijians are friendly, and a few simple phrases in Fijian can go a long way towards making connections with locals.

  3. Take a Boat Tour - The nearby islands of Mamanuca and Yasawa are stunning, and taking a boat tour is the perfect way to explore them.

Don’t:

  1. Wear Shoes Inside - It is customary to remove your shoes before entering local’s houses and some public places.

  2. Drink Tap Water - Tap water in Fiji is not safe to drink. Only drink bottled water or water that has been boiled and cooled.

  3. Take Coral - Coral reefs are protected, and it is illegal to take any coral or sea creatures from the ocean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Viti Levu

Viti Levu is a safe destination, but like any traveler, it is best to use common sense and take precautions to ensure a trouble-free vacation.

Health in Viti Levu

Fiji has a high rate of mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever, Zika virus, and Chikungunya. It is important to use mosquito repellent and netting to avoid being bitten. Also, ensure that you are up to date with your vaccinations before traveling to Fiji.

Getting to Viti Levu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Viti Levu

The major international airport in Fiji is Nadi International Airport, located on the western side of Viti Levu. There is a smaller airport, Nausori International Airport, located near Suva.

Bus Stations Near Viti Levu

Viti Levu has a well-connected bus network that runs between major towns and cities. The bus terminals are typically located in the town centers.

Other Options

Taxis and private car hires are readily available in Viti Levu.

How to Get Around in Viti Levu

The best way to get around Viti Levu is by car or taxi. Buses are also a convenient and inexpensive mode of transportation, with regular services between the major towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Viti Levu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider after Viti Levu:

  1. Mamanuca and Yasawa Islands - These island chains are renowned for their stunning scenery and crystal-clear waters, making them perfect for diving and snorkeling.

  2. Taveuni Island - Known as the “Garden Island” of Fiji, Taveuni is a lush and tropical destination with stunning waterfalls, hikes, and diving opportunities.

  3. Lautoka - A bustling city and the second-largest in Fiji after Suva. Lautoka is known for its Indian-influenced architecture and the famous Sugar Festival.

Conclusion

Viti Levu is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Fiji. From its stunning beaches and outdoor adventures to its cultural landmarks and delicious cuisine, Viti Levu offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore the rich culture and history, Viti Levu is sure to exceed your expectations.
