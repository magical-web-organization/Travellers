Suva, the vibrant capital of Fiji, is a city that offers a perfect blend of urban lifestyle, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty. Suva is located on the southeast coast of Viti Levu, the largest island in Fiji, and is often considered the gateway to the rest of the island nation. From visiting museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic vistas, Suva has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the must-see attractions, best things to do, local cuisine, cultural customs, safety concerns, transportation options, and nearby destinations to help you plan a memorable trip to Suva.

Introduction to Suva

Suva is a bustling city of over 100,000 people and is home to many ethnic groups, including indigenous Fijians, Indo-Fijians, Chinese, and Europeans. The city’s diverse population has resulted in a rich and vibrant culture that is evident in its architecture, cuisine, festivals, and traditions. As the political, administrative, and economic center of Fiji, Suva is a city that never sleeps and offers a plethora of activities for tourists to enjoy.

Food and Drink Scene of Suva

Fijian cuisine is a blend of cultural influences, combining flavors from Polynesian, Melanesian, and Indian cuisines. Fijian food is known for its fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and vegetables, and the use of coconut milk in many dishes. Here are some must-try dishes in Suva:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoda: raw fish marinated in coconut milk and citrus juice.
  • Lovo: a traditional Fijian dish similar to a barbecue, where meat and vegetables are cooked underground.
  • Cassava: a starchy root vegetable, similar to potato.
  • Palusami: taro leaves stuffed with corned beef and cooked in coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: a traditional Fijian drink made from the roots of the kava plant.
  • Fiji Bitter: the most popular beer in Fiji.
  • Coconut water: a refreshing and hydrating drink that is widely available in Suva.

What to Buy in Suva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Suva is a paradise for shopping enthusiasts, with an abundance of local markets and shops that offer traditional Fijian handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. Here are some things to buy in Suva:

  • Handicrafts: Fijian handicrafts are made from natural materials such as coconut shells, pandanus leaves, and woven fibers.

  • Kava bowls: a traditional Fijian bowl used for drinking kava.

Local Culture and Customs of Suva

Fijians are known for their friendly and hospitable nature, and visitors to Suva can expect a warm welcome. It is important to respect Fijian culture and customs while visiting Suva, including removing your shoes before entering someone’s home and dressing conservatively when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Suva

The best time to visit Suva is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The temperature during this time is milder, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suva

Do:

  • Learn a few Fijian phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Take kava with strangers without asking for permission first.
  • Discuss politics and religion in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suva

Suva is a safe city, but tourists should exercise caution when walking alone at night, and avoid carrying large sums of money and valuables. It is also recommended to use licensed taxis or transportation services.

Health in Suva

Travelers to Suva should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations, and take precautions against mosquitoes, as they can carry diseases such as dengue fever. It is also recommended to drink bottled water and avoid eating uncooked food.

Getting to Suva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Suva

Suva is served by Nausori International Airport, which is located 30 minutes from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Suva

InterCity Coachlines operates daily services to Suva from cities across Fiji, including Nadi, Lautoka, and Sigatoka.

Other Options

Car rentals are available from Nausori International Airport or in Suva city center. Additionally, taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber are widely available.

How to Get Around in Suva

The easiest way to get around Suva is by taxi, which is inexpensive and readily available. Buses also run frequently throughout the city, offering a more affordable transportation option.

Conclusion

Suva is a city that offers a perfect blend of urban lifestyle, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, outdoor adventures, or indulging in local cuisine, Suva has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready to make some unforgettable memories in Suva, Fiji!
