Introduction to Nananu-i-Ra

Nananu-i-Ra is a stunning island located in the northern part of Viti Levu, the main island of Fiji. It is an unforgettable destination for travelers who seek relaxation, tranquility, and adventure. Its pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters are ideal for all kinds of water activities such as surfing, diving, snorkeling, and fishing. This hidden gem of Fiji offers visitors a chance to connect with nature and experience the local culture. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and local customs of Nananu-i-Ra.

List of Places to Visit in Nananu-i-Ra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cannibal Cave- A limestone cave used by the ancient Fijian tribes for cannibalistic rituals.
  2. Vatia Beach- An abandoned Dutch settlement that was once a thriving coconut plantation and a significant trading center in the 18th century.
  3. Vatudamu Fort- A historical site built by the Fijian warriors during the 19th century, used for defense against foreign invasions.

Natural Attractions of Nananu-i-Ra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Vunikura Hill- A 1,000-foot hill with breathtaking panoramic views of the island and the ocean.
  2. Nananu-i-Ra Beach- A beautiful, palm-lined beach with white sands and crystal clear waters suitable for swimming and snorkeling.
  3. Arched Rock- A natural rock formation shaped like an arch located on the east coast of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nananu-i-Ra

  1. Vunikura Hill Trail- A moderate 2-hour trek that starts from the village of Navuniivi and leads to the summit of Vunikura Hill.
  2. Nananu-i-Ra Beach Trail- A leisurely walk that takes approximately 30 minutes along the Nananu-i-Ra beach.

Best Things to Do in Nananu-i-Ra: Activities and Tours

  1. Surfing- Nananu-i-Ra is known for its perfect surfing waves, especially during the winter months (May-October). The best surf spots are Natadola Beach and Cloudbreak.
  2. Diving and Snorkeling- Nananu-i-Ra has a rich marine life, including colorful corals, tropical fish, and sharks. The best diving and snorkeling spots are Dolphin Bay, Navini Island, and Mamanuca Islands.
  3. Fishing- Nananu-i-Ra is a popular spot for game fishing. Visitors can hire local fishing boats to go deep-sea fishing or fishing by the reef.
  4. Island Hopping- Visitors can explore nearby islands such as Malolo Island, Tavarua Island, and Naviti Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Nananu-i-Ra

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kokoda- A traditional Fijian appetizer made with raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut milk.
  2. Lovo- A traditional Fijian dish that involves cooking meat, fish, or vegetables in an underground oven for several hours.
  3. Palusami- A Fijian dish made with meat or fish wrapped in taro leaves and cooked in coconut cream.

Local Beverages

  1. Kava- A traditional Fijian drink made from the kava plant’s roots. It has a mildly sedative effect and is used in social and ceremonial occasions.
  2. Coconut Juice- A refreshing drink made from freshly harvested coconuts.

Where to Stay in Nananu-i-Ra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Wananavu Beach Resort- A luxurious beachfront resort with stunning ocean views, swimming pool, and restaurants.
  2. Palmlea Farms Lodge- A budget-friendly guesthouse with sea-facing rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Nananu-i-Ra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handcrafts- Visitors can purchase handcrafted souvenirs, including baskets, mats, items made from coconut shells or woodcarvings, and traditional Fijian clothes.
  2. Fijian Tapa Cloth- A traditional Fijian cloth made from the inner bark of trees that is used for clothing and decoration.

Local Culture and Customs of Nananu-i-Ra

  1. The Fijian culture is centered around respect for elders and communal living. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, dress modestly, and avoid revealing clothing.
  2. Visitors should remove their shoes when entering a Fijian home or temple.
  3. Fiji has a rich music and dance culture. Visitors can attend a traditional Fijian dance and music performance, including the Meke dance and the Lali drum.

Best Time to Visit Nananu-i-Ra

The best time to visit Nananu-i-Ra is between May to October when the weather is dry and the ocean waves are perfect for surfing. The temperature ranges from 22°C to 30°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nananu-i-Ra

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Carry insect repellent and sunscreen.
  3. Rent a boat and explore nearby islands.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  2. Remove any coral or shells from the beach or ocean.
  3. Take pictures of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nananu-i-Ra

  1. Visitors should be aware of strong ocean currents and powerful waves while swimming and surfing.
  2. Visitors should not leave their valuables unattended on the beach or near tourist areas.

Health in Nananu-i-Ra

  1. Visitors should carry mosquito repellent and use it regularly because of the risk of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus.
  2. It is recommended to drink bottled water or boiled water as the tap water could cause stomach problems.

Getting to Nananu-i-Ra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nananu-i-Ra

  1. Nadi International Airport- Approximately a three-hour drive from Nananu-i-Ra.
  2. Suva Airport- Approximately a two-hour drive from Nananu-i-Ra.

Bus Stations Near Nananu-i-Ra

The nearest bus station is located in Raki Raki, approximately a one-hour drive from Nananu-i-Ra.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a private taxi or rental car to reach Nananu-i-Ra from airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Nananu-i-Ra

Visitors can explore the island on foot, by bicycle, or hire a taxi or rental car.

Where to Go Next from Nananu-i-Ra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Natadola Beach- A beautiful beach with white sands and clear waters, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  2. Yasawa Islands- An archipelago of 20 volcanic islands with scenic beauty, pristine beaches, and diverse marine life.

Conclusion

Nananu-i-Ra is a paradise for travelers who seek adventure and relaxation, with stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. As you plan your next holiday trip, use this comprehensive guide to Nananu-i-Ra to explore the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and local customs of Fiji. Bon Voyage!
