Introduction to Coral Coast

Coral Coast is a stunning region located on the southern coast of Viti Levu, Fiji’s largest island. It is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and world-renowned coral reefs. The area is perfect for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and a glimpse into the local Fijian life.

List of Places to Visit in Coral Coast

If you are planning a visit to the Coral Coast region, make sure to add these top attractions to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fiji Museum: located in Suva, just a short drive from Coral Coast, this museum showcases Fiji’s history and cultural heritage

  • Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park: a protected site containing ancient Fijian burial sites and traces of human habitation dating back 3,000 years

  • Vatukarasa Village: an authentic Fijian village offering a glimpse into the traditional Fijian culture

Natural Attractions of Coral Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sigatoka River Safari: a thrilling 18 km jet boat ride through lush rainforests and local Fijian villages

  • Kula Eco Park: a wildlife park featuring endangered Fijian wildlife, like the Fiji crested iguana

  • Navua River Eco Adventures: offers bamboo rafting, zip lining, waterfall hikes, and cultural tours

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coral Coast

The Coral Coast is surrounded by lush vegetation and stunning landscapes, making it the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some top-rated routes:

  • Sigatoka Valley: a scenic valley featuring breathtaking vistas and natural hot spring waterfalls

  • Pacific Harbour: a coastal trail with views of the beach and mangrove forests

Best Things to Do in Coral Coast: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling: experience the colorful underwater world of Fiji’s coral reefs

  • White water rafting: navigate the rapids of the Navua River for an adrenaline-pumping adventure

  • Beach activities: enjoy swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding, and more on the golden shores of the Coral Coast

Week-Long Itinerary for Coral Coast

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to the Coral Coast:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Fiji Museum in Suva
2 Go scuba diving in the coral reef
3 Take the Sigatoka River Safari
4 Spend a day at the beach and try kayaking or paddleboarding
5 Trek through the Sigatoka Valley
6 Tour the Kula Eco Park
7 Go white water rafting on the Navua River

Food and Drink Scene of Coral Coast

Fijian cuisine is a tasty mix of Indian, Chinese, and Pacific Island flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoda: raw fish marinated in coconut and lime juice

  • Lovo: traditional Fijian feast cooked in an underground oven

  • Palusami: taro leaves stuffed with corned beef or mutton

Local Beverages

  • Fiji Bitter: a popular local beer brewed on the island

  • Kava: a traditional Fijian drink made from the root of the kava plant, known for its relaxing properties

Where to Stay in Coral Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Coral Coast for budget travelers and luxury seekers. Here are some top-rated places to stay:

  • Warwick Fiji: a beachfront resort featuring multiple dining options, a spa, and sports activities

  • Mango Bay Resort: a budget-friendly option with comfortable accommodations and views of the Pacific Ocean

  • Bedarra Beach Inn: a quaint guesthouse with a private beach, restaurant, and bar on the Coral Coast

What to Buy in Coral Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some Fijian souvenirs, here are some items to consider:

  • Handwoven mats: crafted by Fijian women from pandanus leaves, these mats feature intricate designs

  • Tapa cloth: traditional Fijian fabric made from bark cloth, usually featuring bold patterns and colors

  • Black pearls: Fiji is known for its exquisite black pearls, which are harvested from the waters of the Pacific Ocean

Local Culture and Customs of Coral Coast

Fijian culture is deeply ingrained in the daily lives of the local residents. It’s important to respect their culture and customs while visiting Coral Coast. Here are some tips to help you navigate Fijian culture:

  • Dress modestly in public places, especially in traditional Fijian villages

  • Remove your shoes before entering a Fijian home or place of worship

  • Ask for permission before taking photographs

Best Time to Visit Coral Coast

The best time to visit Coral Coast is from late October to early November or from late March to early April, when crowds are thinner, and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coral Coast

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Coral Coast:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Pack plenty of sunscreen and bug spray

  • Try traditional Fijian dishes and beverages

Don’t:

  • Wear camouflage or military attire

  • Touch or damage coral reefs or other natural habitats

  • Take photos of locals without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Coral Coast

For the most part, Coral Coast is a safe region to visit. However, it’s important to take precautions like locking your accommodation and not leaving valuables unattended. Additionally, be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Health in Coral Coast

While tap water in Coral Coast is safe to drink, it’s still recommended to drink bottled water to avoid stomach upset. There are medical facilities in the area, but it’s important to have travel insurance and be prepared in case of a medical emergency.

Getting to Coral Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coral Coast

The nearest international airport to Coral Coast is Nadi International Airport (NAN), located about 64km away. From here, you can take a taxi or shuttle service to Coral Coast.

Bus Stations Near Coral Coast

Buses are a common mode of transportation in Fiji, and there are several bus stations in the Coral Coast region. The most popular bus routes run from Nadi and Suva.

Other Options

Many visitors also opt for rental cars or private transfers to get around Coral Coast and its surrounding regions.

How to Get Around in Coral Coast

Taxis and buses are widely available in Coral Coast and can take you to most destinations in the area. Alternatively, many visitors opt to rent a car to explore the region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Coral Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Fiji’s stunning scenery, consider checking out these nearby destinations:

  • Yasawa Islands: a group of 20 volcanic islands known for their white sandy beaches and vibrant coral reefs

  • Suva: Fiji’s bustling capital city, with plenty of museums, restaurants, and nightlife

Conclusion

Coral Coast is a wonderful destination that offers a mix of adventure, relaxation, and cultural experiences. With breathtaking landscapes, a fascinating history, and friendly locals, Coral Coast should definitely be on your travel bucket list. With this guide, you’re well-equipped to plan your perfect trip to this beautiful and vibrant region.
