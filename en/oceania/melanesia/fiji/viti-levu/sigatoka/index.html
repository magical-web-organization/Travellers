Sigatoka is a charming town located on the Coral Coast of Viti Levu, the largest island in Fiji. Boasting a plethora of cultural and natural attractions, Sigatoka draws visitors from all over the world who flock to see its pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and fascinating archaeological sites. Whether you’re an adventure-seeker, a history enthusiast or a culture lover, Sigatoka has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on an adventure through Sigatoka’s best attractions, food and drink scene, accommodations, and much more.

Introduction to Sigatoka

Sigatoka, also known as the “Salad Bowl of Fiji,” is a town located at the mouth of Sigatoka River. With a population of approximately 20,000 people, Sigatoka is a bustling hub of activity with an array of restaurants, bars, shops, and markets. The town is surrounded by rugged cliffs that separate two pristine beaches, the Natadola Beach and the Coral Coast. Sigatoka is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a mix of relaxation and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Sigatoka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sigatoka offers plenty of rich cultural experiences to its visitors, and you can start your journey by visiting its museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park: This national park is home to one of the largest sand dunes in the South Pacific. The Sigatoka Sand Dunes have been inhabited by humans for over 3,000 years and offer a glimpse into the area’s fascinating archaeology.

  • Tavuni Hill Fort: This ancient fortress dates back to the 16th century and was once used by the Fijian warriors as a lookout point when defending their land against the Tongans. Today visitors can hike to the top of the hill and admire the stunning panoramic views of the town and the surrounding landscape.

  • Sigatoka Pottery Village: The pottery village is home to a group of local artisans who make and sell traditional Fijian pottery products. Visitors can watch the artists at work and purchase pottery items as souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Sigatoka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sigatoka is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of natural wonders to explore. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Kula Eco Park: If you’re traveling with kids, Kula Eco Park is a must-visit attraction. The park is home to a variety of endangered Fijian species, including the crested iguana, the Fiji banded iguana, and the Fiji ground frog. Visitors can take a guided tour and enjoy interactive experiences with the animals.

  • Sigatoka River Safari: A river safari is an excellent way to explore the town’s natural beauty and wildlife. During the tour, you will travel up the Sigatoka River and witness the stunning landscapes, marine life, and wildlife.

  • Naihehe Cave: Naihehe Cave is a popular spot for adventure seekers who love to explore underground caves. The cave has a rich history and was once used by the Fijian people as a fortress during times of warfare.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sigatoka

Sigatoka is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Here are some excellent spots to traverse:

  • Korotogo Back Road: This scenic road is a popular spot for biking and hiking, with stunning views of the Coral Coast and surrounding mountain ranges.

  • Navua River: The Navua River is a beautiful place to go on a biking or hiking tour. Visitors can see waterfalls, caves and visit a Fijian village along the way.

Best Things to Do in Sigatoka: Activities and Tours

Sigatoka offers an array of activities and tours to suit every traveler’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Sigatoka:

  • Surfing: The Coral Coast is one of Fiji’s most famous surfing spots, attracting surfers from all over the world. Sigatoka has several surf schools and rentals that offer lessons to beginners and board rentals to experienced surfers.

  • Fiji Day Cruise: A Fiji day cruise is a great way to explore the Sigatoka and its surrounding islands. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling, swimming and beach activities while enjoying the scenic views of the islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Sigatoka

Fijian cuisine is a fusion of Indian, Chinese, and Polynesian flavors, and Sigatoka is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Sigatoka:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoda: Kokoda is a Fijian ceviche made with fish, coconut cream, lime, and chili. It is a refreshing and delicious dish that is a must-try in Sigatoka.

  • Lovo: Lovo is a traditional Fijian dish prepared by cooking meat, fish, and vegetables in an underground oven with hot stones and banana leaves. It is a unique and flavorful dish that is not to be missed.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: Kava is the national drink of Fiji, made from the roots of the kava plant. It has a mild sedative effect and is often consumed during social gatherings.

  • Fiji Bitter: Fiji Bitter is a popular local beer, known for its smooth taste and refreshing flavor. It is a perfect drink to enjoy while watching the sunset over the Coral Coast.

Where to Stay in Sigatoka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sigatoka has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a budget-friendly hostel, you can find it in Sigatoka. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sigatoka:

  • Shangri-La’s Fijian Resort & Spa: This luxurious resort is set on a private island and features three swimming pools, a spa, and several on-site restaurants. It is an excellent choice for couples and families.

  • Bedarra Beach Inn: Bedarra Beach Inn is a small, family-run hotel located on the Coral Coast. It offers comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and a beachfront bar.

  • Mango Bay Resort: Mango Bay Resort is a budget-friendly option located on the Coral Coast. It features basic rooms, a swimming pool, and a beachfront bar.

What to Buy in Sigatoka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sigatoka offers plenty of souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as a memory of your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy in Sigatoka:

  • Traditional Fijian Crafts: The pottery village is an excellent place to buy traditional Fijian pottery products, including bowls, vases, and figurines.

  • Pearls: Fiji is home to some of the world’s most beautiful pearls, and Sigatoka has several stores that offer high-quality pearls at affordable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Sigatoka

Fijians are known for their warm hospitality and laid-back demeanor. It is important to respect their customs and traditions when visiting Sigatoka. Here are some key things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: Fijians are conservative when it comes to clothing, so it is essential to dress moderately when visiting villages and other areas outside of resorts.

  • Remove Shoes When Entering Homes and Temples: Removing shoes when entering homes and temples is a sign of respect in Fijian culture.

Best Time to Visit Sigatoka

The best time to visit Sigatoka is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the temperatures are comfortable, and the rainfall is minimal, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and beach days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sigatoka

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts when visiting Sigatoka:

Do:

  • Respect Fijian Culture and Customs

  • Try Fijian cuisine and local beverages

  • Visit cultural and historical sites

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting villages and other areas outside of resorts

  • Touch or remove natural items from reefs and beaches

  • Disrespect Fijian culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Sigatoka

Sigatoka is a relatively safe town, but it is important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Keep valuables locked in your hotel safe

  • Walk with a group, especially at night

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash

Health in Sigatoka

Fiji is not a high-risk area for diseases, but it is recommended to be up to date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), and hepatitis A and B. It is also advisable to bring insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Sigatoka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sigatoka is approximately a two-hour drive from Fiji’s international airport, Nadi International Airport. Visitors can reach Sigatoka by taxi, bus, or rental car. Here are some transportation options for getting around Sigatoka:

Airports Near Sigatoka

  • Nadi International Airport: Nadi International Airport is Fiji’s main international airport and is located approximately a two-hour drive from Sigatoka.

  • Nausori International Airport: Nausori International Airport is Fiji’s second international airport and is located on the eastern side of Viti Levu.

Bus Stations Near Sigatoka

  • Sigatoka Bus Station: Sigatoka Bus Station is the town’s primary bus station and is located in the center of town.

  • Nadi Bus Station: Nadi Bus Station is Fiji’s main bus station and offers buses to several locations throughout the country.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at both Nadi and Nausori airports and are an excellent option for travelers who want to explore Sigatoka and the surrounding areas independently.

How to Get Around in Sigatoka

Sigatoka is a small town and is easy to get around on foot or via local taxis. Buses also run frequently along the main road, making it easy to get to other parts of the Coral Coast.

Where to Go Next from Sigatoka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sigatoka is a great place to base yourself while exploring the Coral Coast and its surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider:

  • Natadola Beach: Natadola Beach is a pristine, white-sand beach located approximately 30 minutes from Sigatoka. It is a beautiful place to spend a relaxing day in the sun.

  • Suva: Suva is the capital city of Fiji and is located approximately two hours from Sigatoka. It offers a wide range of cultural and historical attractions, including museums and art galleries.

Conclusion

Sigatoka is a hidden gem in Fiji that offers a mix of adventure, relaxation, and cultural experiences to its visitors. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality, Sigatoka is the perfect destination for anyone seeking an authentic Fijian experience. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to explore Sigatoka and all that it has to offer.
