Tokelau is a group of three atolls in Polynesia, situated in the South Pacific Ocean. It is one of the world’s smallest territories, with a total land area of just 10 km² and a population of around 1,500 people. This destination may be off the beaten path, but it offers visitors a unique and authentic Pacific Island experience. In this travel guide to Tokelau, we’ll cover all aspects of traveling to this secret paradise - from where to stay and what to see and do, to important travel tips that every traveler should know.

Introduction to Tokelau

Tokelau is made up of three atolls - Atafu, Nukunonu, and Fakaofo - located north of Samoa and east of Tuvalu. It is a territory of New Zealand, and Tokelauan people are considered New Zealand citizens. The islands are notable for their pink beaches, crystal-clear lagoons, and coral reefs that make it an excellent destination for diving enthusiasts. The culture is another major draw, and visitors can expect a warm welcome from locals.

List of Places to Visit in Tokelau

Tokelau may be small, but there are plenty of places to see and explore. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Te Fale-o-Atafu: At Afafu, visitors can explore the traditional Tokelauan Fale, an example of the island’s unique architecture.
  • Motus: Tokelau is made up of coral atolls and their small associated islets, known as motus. These motus offer visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty of the islands.
  • Cultural Tour: Tokelau offers visitors a chance to experience the local culture by taking part in a cultural tour. Visitors can learn about the history and customs of the Tokelauan people.

Natural Attractions of Tokelau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fale Fono: The Fale Fono, situated in Fakaofo, is a traditional meeting house that serves as the island’s parliament. It is worth a visit to appreciate the building’s intricacies.
  • Fakaofo Lagoon: The lagoon in Fakaofo is known for its diversity of marine life. Visitors can go snorkeling and scuba diving to explore the lagoon’s vibrant underwater ecosystem.
  • Nukunonu Beach: Nukunonu boasts a beautiful long beach with clear waters. Visitors can swim, relax, and enjoy the island ambiance.
  • Atafu Beach: This pink beach is one of the main attractions in Atafu. It is truly a unique and stunning natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tokelau

There are no marked hiking trails or biking routes on Tokelau. However, visitors are welcome to explore the islands on foot or by bike. Local guides can offer suggestions of areas to visit. It’s important to wear comfortable, sturdy shoes and bring plenty of water.

Best Things to Do in Tokelau: Activities and Tours

Though small, Tokelau offers visitors many exciting things to do. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Scuba Diving: Tokelau’s coral reefs and lagoons offer some of the best diving in the world. Visitors can explore the underwater world of Tokelau by taking part in a scuba diving tour.
  • Fishing: The waters around Tokelau are teeming with marine life, making it a prime destination for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Surfing: The waves off the Tokelauan coasts are a surfer’s dream. Visitors can try their hand at surfing or watch the talented locals show off their skills on the waves.
  • Island Hopping: A tour of all three atolls is a must-do to explore Tokelau to its fullest. Visitors can experience the different cultures, scenery, and attractions of each island.

Food and Drink Scene of Tokelau

The cuisine of Tokelau is heavily influenced by the island’s geography and culture. Visitors can expect a mix of Polynesian, Pacific Island, and New Zealand dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kakaka: Consisting of coconut milk and breadfruit, kakaka is a staple dish among Tokelauans.
  • Pulaka: Pulaka is a vegetable that is commonly eaten in Tokelau. It is usually served boiled or baked.
  • Pandanus Fruit: The pandanus fruit can be eaten by itself or used to flavor and color dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: This traditional drink is made from the roots of a kava plant. It is used in ceremonies and social gatherings.
  • Coconut Milk: Coconut milk is a refreshing drink that can be served on its own or mixed with other flavors.

Where to Stay in Tokelau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Due to its small size and remote location, there are very few options for accommodations on Tokelau. Visitors can stay at a couple of guesthouses on each atoll or opt for a homestay with a local family. The facilities are basic, but the warmth of the Tokelauan people will more than make up for it.

What to Buy in Tokelau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There aren’t many souvenir shops in Tokelau, but visitors can purchase locally made crafts and products from markets. Here are some great souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Tapas: These beautiful works of art are made from the bark of a mulberry tree. They are dyed and rubbed until a soft material is created, then patterned with traditional designs.
  • Paddles: Carved from local woods, paddles are often used in traditional dances and ceremonies. They make for a unique and practical souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tokelau

Tokelauans are proud of their rich culture and customs. Visitors should respect them and learn about them by attending cultural events, visiting cultural attractions, and reading up on the local history.

Best Time to Visit Tokelau

Between May and October is the best time to visit Tokelau, as the weather is dry and pleasant. The wet season runs from November to April and is characterized by heavy rainfall and the occasional typhoon.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tokelau

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Tokelau:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try to learn a few phrases in Tokelauan to show your appreciation.
  • Take your shoes off before entering someone’s home or holy sites.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people or their belongings without permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tokelau is not a significant concern. The islands are small, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take general precautions, such as locking up valuables. Healthwise, travelers should be up to date with their routine vaccines, including measles, and hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Tokelau: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are two ways to get to Tokelau: via Apia, the capital city of Samoa, or through Fiji. Visitors can fly to Samoa and then take a charter flight to Tokelau. Alternatively, visitors can fly to Fiji and take a cargo ship to Tokelau. It’s important to note that there are no public transportation options on the islands.

Airports Near Tokelau

  • Fakaofo Airport (FKA): The airport on the Fakaofo Atoll is the main international gateway for Tokelau.

Other Options

  • Cargo Ship: Visitors can opt to take the inter-island cargo ship from Samoa or Fiji. The ship runs on a monthly schedule.

How to Get Around in Tokelau

There are no cars on Tokelau, and visitors must explore the islands on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Tokelau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tokelau is a remote destination, and visitors may want to continue their Pacific Island adventure with a trip to nearby destinations such as Samoa, Fiji, or Tuvalu.

Conclusion

Tokelau may be small and relatively unknown, but it offers visitors an authentic Pacific Island experience. With a rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality, it’s no wonder that this destination is becoming increasingly popular.
