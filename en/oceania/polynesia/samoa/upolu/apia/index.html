Introduction to Apia

Apia, located in Upolu, Samoa, in Polynesia, is a vibrant city along the Pacific Ocean. It is the capital of Samoa and has a rich cultural heritage that dates back centuries. In this guide, you will learn about the best places to visit and things to do while in Apia, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Apia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Robert Louis Stevenson Museum: This beautiful museum is located in the former residence of the famous writer, Robert Louis Stevenson. It highlights the life and works of the author, giving visitors a glimpse into his illustrious career and personal life.

  • Museum of Samoa: This museum highlights the long and rich history of Samoa, featuring artifacts and exhibits from the nation’s past. Visitors can explore the many facets of Samoan life, from ancient traditions to contemporary customs.

  • Immaculate Conception of Mary Cathedral: This stunning cathedral is a masterpiece of colonial architecture and is considered to be one of the most beautiful buildings in Samoa. Visitors can marvel at the intricate details, take in the peaceful atmosphere, and attend services.

  • Mulinu’u Peninsula: This historic site was once the political and social center of Samoa. Visitors can explore ruins, cemeteries, and traditional architecture, including the Parliament House of Samoa.

Natural Attractions of Apia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Papapapaitai Falls: This stunning waterfall is located high up in the mountains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain to take in the awe-inspiring scenery and swim in the cool waters of the falls.

  • Piula Cave Pools: These natural pools are located near a scenic beach and provide visitors with a refreshing place to cool off. The pools are inside a cave, with sunlight filtering through cracks in the rocks, creating a mesmerizing display.

  • Lalomanu Beach: This beautiful beach is surrounded by bright blue waters and white sand. Visitors can relax on the beach, take a dip in the ocean, or participate in water sports activities.

  • Mount Vaea: This mountain offers stunning panoramic views of Apia and the surrounding areas. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain to see the beautiful landscape and explore the jungle trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apia

Mount Vaea Trail

The Mount Vaea Trail is a moderate hike that takes about three hours to complete. It starts at the base of the mountain and winds its way up to the peak, offering spectacular views of the city and the surrounding area. The trail is well-marked, and hikers should bring plenty of water and wear sturdy shoes.

Lalomanu Coastal Walk

This walk is a scenic coastal route that runs along the shoreline of Lalomanu Beach. It is an easy hike that takes around one hour to complete, and visitors can enjoy stunning views of the ocean, coral reefs, and lush vegetation.

Best Things to Do in Apia: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking and Snorkeling: Visitors can rent sea kayaks and explore the beautiful coastline of Apia, including secluded beaches and rocky coves. Snorkeling gear is also available for rent, allowing visitors to discover the vibrant undersea life of the Pacific Ocean.

  • Surfing: Samoa is quickly becoming a world-renowned surfing destination, with a variety of waves suitable for all levels of surfers. Visitors can take classes to learn how to surf, or rent a board and hit the waves on their own.

  • Island Tours: Visitors can take guided tours of the island and learn about the culture, traditions, and history of Samoa. Tours are available by jeep, bus, or boat, and can include stops at local villages, markets, and historical sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Apia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oka: This dish is a traditional Samoan dish made with raw fish, lemon juice, coconut cream, and vegetables.

  • Palusami: This is a dish made with taro leaves wrapped around coconut cream and onions, and then baked in an oven.

  • Curried Crab: This dish is made with crab meat, coconut cream, and various spices.

Local Beverages

  • Vailima Beer: This is the most popular beer in Samoa, and is known for its crisp and refreshing taste.

  • Koko Samoa: This is a hot chocolate drink made with cacao beans and coconut cream.

Where to Stay in Apia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

  • Tanoa Tusitala: This beautiful hotel is located in the heart of Apia and features a swimming pool, restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.

  • Sheraton Samoa Aggie Grey’s Resort: This upscale resort is located on the coast of Apia and features a private beach, spa, and multiple restaurants.

Hostels

  • Samoa Backpackers: This affordable hostel is located in the heart of Apia and features dormitory-style rooms, a shared kitchen, and a communal lounge.

  • Travellers Point Hotel: This small hotel offers basic rooms and dormitories, and is located close to local attractions and restaurants.

Guesthouses

  • Tailua Beach Fales: This beautiful guesthouse offers traditional Samoan-style beachfront bungalows and is located on a pristine white sandy beach in Lalomanu village.

  • Taufua Beach Fales: This budget guesthouse offers traditional Samoan-style beachfront bungalows and is located on a popular beach in Lalomanu village.

What to Buy in Apia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional Crafts

Visitors can buy a range of traditional crafts and souvenirs in Apia, including:

  • Lava-Lava: Traditional Samoan garment that can be used as a wrap, skirt, dress or headscarf.

  • Carvings: Intricately crafted wooden figures and masks, including tribal art.

  • Jewelry: Handmade jewelry, including shell necklaces, bracelets, earrings, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Apia

Samoan culture is rich and vibrant and is evident through their indigenous music, dance, and art. Visitors should be respectful of Samoan customs and traditions, which include:

  • Covering up: Visitors should wear modest clothing when in public, and not expose too much skin.

  • Removing shoes before entering homes or sacred sites: Samoans often remove their shoes before entering homes, so visitors should do the same.

  • Showing respect: Visitors should show respect when visiting churches, temples, or other sacred sites by not touching anything or taking photographs without permission.

Best Time to Visit Apia

The best time to visit Apia is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, temperatures are cooler, and there is less humidity. It is also an ideal time to participate in outdoor activities and enjoy the beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apia

Do:

  • Try traditional Samoan food: Apia has a range of traditional dishes that visitors can try, including oka, palusami, and curried crab.

  • Learn about Samoan customs and traditions: Visitors should take the time to learn about Samoan culture and customs.

  • Respect the environment: Visitors should respect the environment by not littering or damaging natural habitats.

Don’t:

  • Touch sacred artifacts or enter designated areas without permission: Visitors should always ask permission before touching or entering designated sacred sites.

  • Take photos without asking: Visitors should not take photographs of people, buildings, or cultural artifacts without obtaining permission first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apia

Apia is generally a safe city for visitors, but it is always essential to take precautions, such as:

  • Avoiding walking alone at night: Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and stick to well-lit areas.

  • Staying aware of your surroundings: Visitors should be aware of their surroundings at all times and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Apia

Visitors should take the necessary health precautions when visiting Apia, including:

  • Drinking bottled water: Visitors should drink bottled water to avoid water-borne diseases.

  • Wearing sunscreen: Visitors should wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and other skin conditions.

Getting to Apia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apia

The nearest airport to Apia is Faleolo International Airport, located 25 kilometers west of the city center. Visitors can take a taxi or a shuttle bus to reach the city.

Bus Stations Near Apia

The Apia Bus Terminal is located in the city center and provides regular bus services around Apia and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or motorbikes to explore the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Apia

Visitors can use taxis, buses, or rental cars and motorbikes to get around Apia and the surrounding areas. Taxis and buses are the most affordable options, while rental cars or motorbikes offer greater flexibility and independence.

Where to Go Next from Apia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savai’i Island

Savai’i Island is a nearby destination that is accessible by ferry or plane. The island is home to stunning natural attractions and traditional Samoan villages.

Samoa Cultural Village

This cultural village is located just outside Apia and provides visitors with an excellent opportunity to learn about Samoan culture and traditions.

Conclusion

Apia is an exciting and beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural attractions. Visitors can explore museums, temples, and historic sites, hike or bike in the mountains, or relax on the beach. They can try traditional Samoan food and learn about the customs and traditions of the local people. With its warm weather and friendly people, Apia is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
