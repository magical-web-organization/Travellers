Introduction to Savaii

Savaii is the largest island in Samoa, located in the South Pacific Ocean. It is covered by lush tropical forests, pristine blue lagoons, and home to some of the most unique plant and animal species in the world. The island is known for its spectacular natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Savaii

If you are planning a trip to Savaii, here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saleaula Lava Fields
  • Sapapali’i Village
  • Paia Dwarfs Cave
  • Tia Seu Ancient Mound
  • Samoa Cultural Village

Natural Attractions of Savaii: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alofaaga Blowholes
  • Afu Aau Waterfall
  • Taga Blowholes
  • Mount Matavanu Crater
  • Saleaula Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savaii

Savaii also offers some of the best hiking and biking routes, and here are a few you must explore:

  • Falealupo Canopy Walkway
  • O Le Pupu Pu’e National Park
  • Mount Silisili Hike
  • Cape Mulinuu Hike

Best Things to Do in Savaii: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling
  • Surfing
  • Deep sea fishing
  • Kayaking and canoeing
  • Island tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Savaii

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Savaii:

Day Activity
1 Arrival, check-in, and relax at the hotel

Food and Drink Scene of Savaii

Savaii’s cuisine is all about fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and vegetables. Here are a few must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oka (raw fish in coconut cream)
  • Palusami (taro leaves cooked in coconut cream)
  • Fa’alifu Lu’a’i (corned beef and coconut cream)
  • Pani Popo (coconut buns)

Local Beverages

  • Koko Samoa (hot chocolate made from cocoa)
  • Vaifala (syrupy drink made from pandanus juice)
  • Samoa Ava (traditional ceremonial drink made from kava root)

Where to Stay in Savaii: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some popular accommodations for tourists in Savaii:

  • Le Lagoto Resort and Spa
  • Stevensons at Manase
  • Lusia’s Lagoon Chalets
  • Bayview Resort

What to Buy in Savaii: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs to bring back home with you include traditional Samoan carvings, shells, and baskets. You can also buy kava root and cocoa to make traditional Samoan beverages at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Savaii

Savaii is known for its friendly and welcoming locals. It’s important to be respectful of their culture and customs, and here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Always remove your shoes before entering a home or a church
  • Cover your arms and legs when visiting a village or a religious site
  • Offer to buy kava before participating in any traditional ceremonies

Best Time to Visit Savaii

The best time to visit Savaii is from April to October when the weather is dry and less humid. However, if you want to experience the island’s lush greenery and blooming flowers, plan your trip between November and March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savaii

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the island’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Swim or go near the water during a storm
  • Leave your trash on the beach or in public areas
  • Take coral or shells from the beach as souvenirs

Safety and Health

Safety in Savaii

Savaii is considered a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s important to watch out for rough waters and high tides when swimming or snorkeling. Don’t leave your valuables unattended on the beach or in public areas.

Health in Savaii

The island has limited healthcare facilities, so it’s important to have travel insurance. Make sure you have all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Samoa and take appropriate precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Savaii: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Savaii

The closest airport to Savaii is Fagali’i Airport, which is serviced by Samoa Airways and Virgin Australia Airlines. You can also take a ferry from Apia, the capital of Samoa, to get to Savaii.

Bus Stations Near Savaii

Savaii has a few bus stations spread across the island that connect visitors to different tourist attractions.

Other Options

Renting a car, scooter, or bicycle is also a great way to explore the island. Taxis are also available in the main tourist areas.

How to Get Around in Savaii

The best way to get around Savaii is to rent a car or a scooter. You can also take a taxi, hire a guide, or use the local buses to travel around the island.

Where to Go Next from Savaii: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve explored all of Savaii and want to check out other nearby destinations, here are a few suggestions:

  • Upolu Island
  • Manono Island
  • Apia, Samoa’s capital city

Conclusion

A trip to Savaii is a unique experience that offers both breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip, experience the island’s diverse attractions, savor its delicious food and drinks, and immerse yourself in its fascinating local culture.
