Introduction to Johnston Atoll

Located in Polynesia, Oceania, Johnston Atoll is a small island known for its pristine natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Established in the late 1800s as a fishing base and later used for military purposes, the island now attracts tourists from all over the world who come to explore its unspoiled coral reefs, scenic trails, and cultural landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Johnston Atoll

If you’re planning a trip to Johnston Atoll, make sure to include these places in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Johnston Atoll National Wildlife Refuge: one of the few remaining natural habitats that provide nesting, foraging, and resting sites for seabirds and other migratory species.
  • Johnston Island Museum: a museum that tells the history of the island, its role in World War II and beyond.
  • Coral Head National Park: an area dedicated to the preservation of Johnston Atoll’s coral reef ecosystem.

Natural Attractions of Johnston Atoll: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kingman Reef: a group of three submerged coral reefs that offer a stunning underwater experience.
  • Sand Island: one of the most beautiful beaches on the island, where you can enjoy sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling.
  • Johnston Island Lagoon: a favorite spot for kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Johnston Atoll

Johnston Atoll is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails that allow you to explore the island’s natural beauty up close. Some popular routes include:

  • Sand Island Trail: a 4-mile trek that takes you around Sand Island and offers spectacular ocean views.
  • Reef Trail: a 3-mile route that takes you through the island’s coral reef ecosystem.
  • East Island Trail: a 5-mile route that takes you across the island’s eastern tip and offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Johnston Atoll: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a nature lover, Johnston Atoll has something to offer to everyone. Some of the best things to do on the island include:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the vibrant underwater world of Johnston Atoll with a guided snorkeling or diving trip.
  • Kayaking and Fishing: Rent a kayak or fishing boat and explore the stunning lagoons and waterways of the island.
  • Bird Watching: Observe the island’s diverse bird species up close at the Johnston Atoll National Wildlife Refuge.
  • Sunset Cruise: Take a relaxing sunset cruise and enjoy the stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Johnston Atoll

When it comes to food, Johnston Atoll offers a unique blend of Polynesian, Asian, and Western cuisines to its visitors. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poke Bowl: a traditional Hawaiian dish made with raw fish, rice, and vegetables.
  • Spam Musubi: a popular Japanese snack made with Spam, rice, and nori.
  • BBQ Chicken Plate Lunch: a classic Hawaiian lunch consisting of grilled chicken, rice, and macaroni salad.

Local Beverages

  • Kona Coffee: a unique coffee grown on the volcanic slopes of the Big Island of Hawaii.
  • POG Juice: a refreshing blend of passion fruit, orange, and guava juice.
  • Mai Tai: a classic Polynesian cocktail made with rum, lime juice, and orange curacao.

Where to Stay in Johnston Atoll: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation options, Johnston Atoll offers several choices to its visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay on the island:

  • Dillingham Hotel: a luxurious hotel located on Sand Island with breathtaking ocean views.
  • Lazy Lagoon Guesthouse: a budget-friendly guesthouse situated near Johnston Island Lagoon.
  • Barking Sands Beach Cottages: a beachfront resort located on the beautiful Barking Sands Beach.

What to Buy in Johnston Atoll: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Johnston Atoll, here are some of the best options:

  • Handmade Shell Jewelry: beautifully crafted jewelry made from shells found on the island.
  • Koa Wood Carvings: intricately carved wooden sculptures made from native Koa wood.
  • Local Artwork: paintings and sculptures created by local artists that depict the island’s natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Johnston Atoll

The culture and customs of Johnston Atoll are deeply rooted in Polynesian traditions and beliefs. Visitors are expected to respect the island’s cultural heritage and observe local customs, such as:

  • Removing shoes before entering homes and places of worship.
  • Greeting locals with a smile and a friendly “Aloha”.
  • Respecting the island’s natural environment by not leaving trash or litter behind.

Best Time to Visit Johnston Atoll

The best time to visit Johnston Atoll is between December and April when the weather is dry and mild, and sea conditions are generally calm. However, visitors can also enjoy the island’s natural beauty during the off-season months, from May to November when there are fewer crowds and lower accommodation prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Johnston Atoll

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Johnston Atoll:

Do:

  • Respect the island’s cultural heritage and traditions.
  • Take care of the island’s natural environment by not leaving trash or litter behind.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid areas that are off-limits or dangerous to visitors.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb the island’s wildlife and marine animals.
  • Ignore local customs or beliefs.
  • Trespass on private properties or military zones.

Safety and Health

Safety in Johnston Atoll

Johnston Atoll is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions and hold themselves accountable for their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure while in public places or crowded areas.
  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings at all times.
  • Follow local regulations and safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities or water sports.

Health in Johnston Atoll

Visitors to Johnston Atoll are advised to take the following health precautions:

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid water-borne infections.
  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites by wearing long-sleeved clothing and using insect repellent.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any signs of illness or infection.

Getting to Johnston Atoll: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Johnston Atoll is relatively easy, and visitors can choose from the following transportation options:

Airports Near Johnston Atoll

  • Honolulu International Airport (HNL): the closest international airport to Johnston Atoll with direct flights to and from major cities in Oceania and North America.

Bus Stations Near Johnston Atoll

There are no direct bus routes to Johnston Atoll as the island is not connected to mainland Polynesia.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Johnston Atoll by sea, either through private yacht or cruise ship.

How to Get Around in Johnston Atoll

The island of Johnston Atoll is only 2.8 square miles, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car, scooter, or golf cart to get around the island and its various attractions.

Where to Go Next from Johnston Atoll: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Polynesia, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Hawaii: just a short flight away from Johnston Atoll, Hawaii offers visitors a diverse range of attractions, from tropical beaches to volcanic landscapes.
  • Samoa: another popular Polynesian destination known for its pristine waters, lush landscapes, and cultural heritage.
  • Tahiti: the largest island in French Polynesia, Tahiti is famous for its stunning coral reefs, water sports, and overwater bungalows.

Conclusion

Johnston Atoll is a hidden gem of Polynesia that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventure, and cultural heritage. Whether you’re exploring the island’s scenic trails, snorkeling in its vibrant reefs, or savoring its local cuisine, Johnston Atoll promises to be an unforgettable experience for everyone.
