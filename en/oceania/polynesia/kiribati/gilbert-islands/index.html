Introduction to Gilbert Islands

Located in Kiribati, a tropical island nation in the Pacific Ocean, Gilbert Islands offer an idyllic destination for any traveler looking to experience Polynesian culture and breathtaking scenery. The archipelago consists of 16 coral atolls, each offering its unique features in terms of landscapes, marine life, and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Gilbert Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kiribati National Library and Archives: Discover the history of Kiribati and Gilbert Islands at this public library which features an extensive collection of books, newspapers, and archives.
  • Betio War Memorial: A memorial dedicated to the fallen soldiers during the Battle of Tarawa in WWII, this is an essential visit for history buffs.
  • King George V and Queen Victoria Residences: Known as “The Big House,” the two-story wooden house is a stunning architectural landmark and considered a national treasure among the locals.

Natural Attractions of Gilbert Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Butaritari Island: This island is known for its turquoise waters and coral reefs, making it a popular site for snorkeling and diving.
  • Abaiang Island: The island is famous for its dense mangrove forests, which are a UNESCO-designated natural reserve.
  • Maiana Island: This Island is perfect for those who want to roam around in nature and explore the beautiful scenery and experience traditional island life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gilbert Islands

  • Aiaki-Tabiteuea Trail: This trail runs between two islands southeast of Kiribati. It covers 18 kilometers and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Betio Causeway: This trail runs through many military installations and cemeteries. It covers 8 kilometers and provides an excellent biking experience.

Best Things to Do in Gilbert Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Gilbert Islands are known for their rich marine life, so snorkeling and diving are must-do activities. Most hotels and resorts provide scuba diving trips, but for a more authentic experience, consider hiring a local guide.
  • Fishing: Take a fishing trip and catch some fresh fish in the turquoise waters of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Attend a Local Festivals and Events: Gilbert Islands are known for their vibrant festivals, including the Te Maiea Equinox Festival, which celebrates the start of the planting season, and the Kiribati Independence Day Festival, which is held every year on July 12.

Food and Drink Scene of Gilbert Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kakamora: A traditional dish made from a coconut with its top cut off and hollowed out, filled with fish or meat and steamed.
  • Palusami: A dish made from taro leaves wrapped around coconut cream and then steamed.
  • Ika Mata: Raw fish dish cooked in lime juice, onions, and coconut cream.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: A traditional drink made from the root of the kava plant and used for ceremonial purposes and social gatherings.
  • Coconut Water: A refreshing, natural drink, as coconuts are abundant in the region.

Where to Stay in Gilbert Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some of the popular accommodations in Gilbert Islands are:

  • Tabiteuea Sunrise: A beautiful, budget-friendly property right on the beach.
  • Otintaai Hotel and Restaurant: A mid-range property that offers amenities such as a restaurant and bar and bicycle rental.
  • King’s Holiday Motel: A luxury hotel with excellent facilities, such as an outdoor swimming pool, restaurant, and a gym.

What to Buy in Gilbert Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shell and Pearl Jewelry: Gilbert Islands are known for their beautiful shells and pearls that are crafted into unique pieces of jewelry.
  • Tapa: A traditional cloth artwork made from the inner bark of the mulberry tree that’s made into clothing, bed sheets, and wall hangings.
  • Kiritimati Christmas Island artwork: Take home unique artwork made exclusively on Kiritimati Island.

Local Culture and Customs of Gilbert Islands

Gilbert Islands have a rich Polynesian culture, and locals are known to be friendly and hospitable. Some common cultural customs and traditions include:

  • Dance: Traditional dances are often performed during ceremonies and celebrations. Look out for the lively and colorful performances.
  • Etiquette: It is essential to show proper respect to elders and women by using formal titles such as “ma’am” and “sir.”
  • Religion: Most locals are Christian, and it is customary to attend church services on Sundays.

Best Time to Visit Gilbert Islands

Gilbert Islands have a tropical climate, with warm weather year-round. The best time to visit is from April to October, during the dry season when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities and sight-seeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gilbert Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as wearing conservative clothing and using formal titles.
  • Try the local cuisine, which features fresh seafood and coconut-based dishes.
  • Take the time to learn some phrases in the local language, Gilbertese.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and help protect the fragile ecosystem.
  • Don’t take sand or shells from the beaches as souvenirs as it contributes to the erosion of the shorelines.
  • Avoid talking about sensitive subjects such as politics, religion, and land ownership.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gilbert Islands

Gilbert Islands are relatively safe for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and secure, avoiding walking alone in deserted areas at night, and following local rules and regulations.

Health in Gilbert Islands

Travelers should take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid diseases such as dengue fever, malaria, and chikungunya. It is also recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to Gilbert Islands.

Getting to Gilbert Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gilbert Islands

Bonriki International Airport is the main airport in Kiribati and serves flights from countries such as Australia and Fiji.

Bus Stations Near Gilbert Islands

There are no bus services on the islands, but taxis or rental cars are readily available for getting around.

Other Options

Ferries are available to travel between the islands, but they can be unreliable.

How to Get Around in Gilbert Islands

Walking or biking is the best way to explore the islands, but rental cars and taxis are also available for convenience.

Where to Go Next from Gilbert Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those who want to explore more of Kiribati’s neighboring islands and regions, these destinations are close by:

  • Christmas Island: The largest coral atoll in the world, known for its crystal-clear waters, and exotic marine life.
  • Republic of Nauru: A small island nation known for its phosphate mining operations and stunning beaches.

Conclusion

Gilbert Islands offer a unique travel experience that includes natural beauty, unique cultural heritage, and lots of outdoor activities. We hope our comprehensive travel guide helps in planning your dream trip to this idyllic destination in Polynesia.
