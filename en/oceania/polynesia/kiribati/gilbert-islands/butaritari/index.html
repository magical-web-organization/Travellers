Introduction to Butaritari

Butaritari, also known as Makin, is a coral island situated in the remote Kiribati archipelago in Polynesia, Oceania. The island is located close to the equator and is a popular location for tourists who wish to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and explore the picturesque countryside. With its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, rich history, and unique cultural heritage, Butaritari is perfect for those who want to experience an unforgettable tropical vacation that is off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Butaritari

Here are some of the top destinations and attractions that you must visit in Butaritari:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Makin Island War Memorial: This war memorial was built to honor the soldiers who fought and died in the battles of World War II. The memorial features a monument and a plaque that lists the names of the soldiers who gave their lives for their country.
  • Te mauri: Visit the sacred stone known as Te mauri that is the focal point of the island’s cultural and spiritual beliefs.
  • Butaritari Church: This church is a popular place of worship in the island.

Natural Attractions of Butaritari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Makoba Beach: This beautiful and secluded beach is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing under the warm sun.
  • Makin Island Lagoon: The breathtaking turquoise waters of the lagoon are perfect for snorkeling, diving, and exploring the coral reefs and marine life.
  • Butaritari Wildlife Sanctuary: Visit the island’s sanctuary to see the rare coconut crabs and native bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Butaritari

  • Tearinibai to Ukiangang: A scenic 2.8-mile hike that passes through villages and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Makin Atoll to the Buariki Church: A strenuous 6.5-mile bike ride that takes you through different terrain types and village areas.

Best Things to Do in Butaritari: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Trips: Take a fishing trip and catch a variety of fish species like barracuda, tuna, and marlin.
  • Traditional Dance Show: Attend a traditional dance show, which is a popular cultural activity.
  • Sightseeing Tour: Explore the island’s main attractions with a guided tour that takes you to the most beautiful and photogenic locations.

Food and Drink Scene of Butaritari

Kiribati is known for its delightful Pacific and Asian-flavoured dishes, and Butaritari has its own local delicacies as well.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kakai: A coconut shell filled with cooked sago and in-between layers have coconut cream.
  • Ika Mata: A popular dish made with fresh raw fish marinated in lime and coconut cream.
  • Chicken Curry: A widely enjoyed dish that contains chicken, fresh vegetables, and ample spices.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: A popular alcoholic drink that is made from the roots of the kava plant.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is available throughout the island.

Where to Stay in Butaritari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Butaritari has a variety of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that offer affordable accommodation options. Some of the recommended places to stay include:

  • Kingfisher Lodge: A beachside eco-resort that offers a variety of accommodation options, including chalets, bungalows, and tents.
  • Villa Lodge: Situated in the heart of Butaritari, this guesthouse offers spacious and comfortable rooms, and a restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • Paradise Motel: Located between the airport and the local villages, this hotel offers comfortable and affordable rooms with fantastic views.

What to Buy in Butaritari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Butaritari with you, here are some of the top souvenirs and local products that you can buy:

  • Shell Jewelry: Handmade jewelry made with seashells is a popular souvenir on the island.
  • Copra Products: Coconut oil, soaps, lotions, and souvenirs made from the island’s coconut harvest are popular local products.
  • Woven Items: Handwoven baskets, hats, and mats, made by local artisans, are great souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Butaritari

Kiribati culture is deeply rooted in traditions, and Butaritari is no exception. The island’s vibrant culture includes traditional dances, music, and celebrations such as the Maneaba Festival, which is celebrated every year on the island. The local customs include showing respect to the elders and asking for permission before entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Butaritari

The best time to visit Butaritari is between April and October, when the weather is warm and dry, and the sea is calm and ideal for swimming and exploring the lagoons and reefs. The peak tourist season is from December to February, and this is when the island’s main festivals and celebrations take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Butaritari

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be a responsible tourist and avoid littering.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when out in the sun.

Don’t:

  • Swim in restricted areas as currents can be dangerous
  • Take pictures without permission from locals.
  • Walk over the sacred rocks known as Te mauri.

Safety and Health

Safety in Butaritari

Butaritari is relatively safe for tourists, and there are no reports of major crime. However, tourists should take necessary precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Butaritari

Tourists must be up to date with their routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, rubella, and polio. It is also advisable to carry a small medical kit containing first aid supplies and any necessary medications.

Getting to Butaritari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Butaritari

Butaritari Airport is serviced by Air Kiribati, which flies to/from Tarawa International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Butaritari

There are no bus services available on the island.

Other Options

Tourists can also choose to take a ferry to Butaritari from other nearby islands, although this can be an expensive and time-consuming option.

How to Get Around in Butaritari

Tourists can choose to rent bicycles as there are few cars or taxis on the island. This is also an environmentally friendly way to navigate the island.

Where to Go Next from Butaritari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby islands that offer day trips from Butaritari include:

  • Abaiang Island: Known for its traditional dances and wonderful beaches.
  • Marakei Island: A quiet and small island with a rich cultural heritage and history.
  • Tarawa Atoll: The capital of Kiribati, Tarawa Atoll is a bustling city that offers cultural and historic attractions, as well as opportunities for water sports and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Butaritari is a hidden gem in the Pacific region that offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. Plan a visit to this beautiful island and immerse yourself in its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality.
