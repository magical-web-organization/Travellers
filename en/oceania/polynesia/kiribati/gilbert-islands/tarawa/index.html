Introduction to Tarawa

Located in the heart of the South Pacific, Tarawa is a picturesque island in Kiribati, an archipelago of 33 islands and atolls. Tarawa features miles of pristine beaches, crystal-clear water, lush green forests, and a rich cultural heritage. It is a perfect destination for those seeking both outdoor adventure and cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Tarawa

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Tarawa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kiribati National Museum: This museum houses a rich collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Kiribati. Explore the traditional houses to see how people used to live on the islands.

  • Japanese Guns: Located on the south end of the island, these guns are relics from World War II and were left behind by the occupying Japanese army.

  • Bairiki National Government Center: The seat of government for Kiribati, Bairiki Island is home to many important governmental buildings.

Natural Attractions of Tarawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bonriki International Airport: The airport is located on a small island just off the coast of Tarawa. The view from above while landing or taking off is breathtaking.

  • Ambo Island: A small island located on the northwest side of the atoll, Ambo Island is home to a beautiful beach and a diverse array of marine life, making it perfect for snorkeling and scuba diving.

  • Te Umanibong: A beautiful lookout point from where you can enjoy views of the surrounding turquoise waters and white sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarawa

If you’re a fan of hiking or biking, then you’re in luck because Tarawa has some excellent trails to explore. Here are a few to consider:

  • Abatao Trail: A scenic trail leading through the forests and coconut plantations of the island.

  • Buota Trail: A rewarding hike that offers stunning views of the coastline and the interior of Tarawa.

Best Things to Do in Tarawa: Activities and Tours

Looking for things to do in Tarawa? Here are a few of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Snorkeling: Tarawa is home to some of the most pristine coral reefs in the world. Take a snorkeling tour and explore the underwater world.

  • Fishing: The waters around Tarawa are teeming with fish. Take a fishing tour and try your luck catching some of the local fish.

  • Cultural Tours: Immerse yourself in the traditional Kiribati culture by taking a guided tour to the local villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarawa

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Tarawa, here is a sample itinerary that you can use:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Tarawa and relax on the beach
Day 2 Take a cultural tour to the local villages
Day 3 Go snorkeling or scuba diving on Ambo Island

Day 5 Go fishing in the waters around Tarawa

Day 7 Relax on the beach before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Tarawa

No trip to Tarawa is complete without trying some of the local dishes and beverages. Here are a few must-try options:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ika Mata: A traditional fish salad made with fresh fish, lime juice, coconut cream, and onions.

  • Palusami: Taro leaves stuffed with a mixture of coconut cream and corned beef, wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.

  • Kakae: A type of taro that is cooked and served with coconut cream.

Local Beverages

  • Te Botebote: A refreshing drink made with coconut milk, sugar, and various other ingredients.

  • Kava: A traditional Polynesian beverage made from the roots of the kava plant. It is known for its calming effects.

Where to Stay in Tarawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarawa offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and travel styles. Here are a few options:

  • Betio Lodge: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in Betio, Tarawa’s main town.

  • Sunrise Inn: A mid-range hotel located on the lagoon side of Tarawa.

  • Quarantine Bay Beachfront: A luxury beachfront resort with private bungalows and outdoor showers.

What to Buy in Tarawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, here are a few options:

  • Tivaevae: Beautifully embroidered bed covers or wall hangings that are unique to Kiribati.

  • Woven baskets: Made from pandanus or coconut leaves, these baskets are perfect for carrying beach gear or as decorative items in your home.

  • Local handicrafts: Handmade jewelry, clothes, and bags are great souvenirs to remind you of your trip to Tarawa.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarawa

Kiribati has a rich cultural heritage that is steeped in tradition. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Etiquette: Kiribati culture places a strong emphasis on respect and hospitality. Always ask permission before taking photos, and avoid touching people or objects without permission.

  • Dress Code: As Kiribati is a conservative country, it is recommended to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Swimwear should be reserved for the beach.

Best Time to Visit Tarawa

The best time to visit Tarawa is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. Temperatures during this time range from 25°C to 30°C, with little to no rainfall. If you’re planning to visit during the wet season, which runs from November to April, be prepared for high humidity and occasional heavy rainfalls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarawa

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Tarawa:

Do:

  • Respect the culture: Kiribati culture places a strong emphasis on respect and hospitality. Always be courteous and respectful to locals and their customs.

  • Take your shoes off: It is customary to take your shoes off when entering a traditional Kiribati house or government building.

Don’t:

  • Touch or step on traditional objects: Traditional objects, such as mats or carvings, are considered sacred, and it is considered disrespectful to touch or step on them.

  • Take photos without permission: Always ask permission before taking photos of people or objects, as it is considered rude to do otherwise.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Tarawa, it’s important to be aware of any safety or health concerns.

Safety in Tarawa

  • Petty crime: Petty crime can be an issue, especially in the more crowded areas of Betio and Bairiki. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or expensive items on you, and keep valuables in a safe place.

  • Reef Safety: Be aware of the dangers of swimming too close to the reef, as it can be sharp and cause injury.

Health in Tarawa

  • Medical facilities: Medical facilities are limited in Tarawa, and it’s important to have comprehensive travel insurance.

  • Mosquito-borne illnesses: Tarawa is located in a tropical climate, and mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus can be a concern. Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing when outdoors, especially during the rainy season.

Getting to Tarawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarawa is served by Bonriki International Airport (TRW), which offers flights to various destinations in the region. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Tarawa

  • Bonriki International Airport: The airport is located on a small island just off the coast of Tarawa.

Bus Stations Near Tarawa

  • Betio Bus Station: Betio is the main town on the island and is serviced by a bus station.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Tarawa and can be ordered by phone or found at taxi stands.

  • Bicycle: Bicycles can be rented from various locations in Tarawa and are a popular mode of transportation for locals and tourists alike.

How to Get Around in Tarawa

Here are a few ways to get around Tarawa:

  • Bus: Buses operate on a set route around the island and are a cheap and convenient way to get around.

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Tarawa and can be ordered by phone or found at taxi stands.

  • Bicycle: Bicycles can be rented from various locations in Tarawa and are a popular mode of transportation for locals and tourists alike.

Where to Go Next from Tarawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, consider visiting some of the nearby islands and destinations around Tarawa:

  • Kiritimati Island: Located in the Line Islands, Kiritimati is the largest coral atoll in the world with a wealth of underwater treasures.

  • Phoenix Islands Protected Area: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this is the largest marine protected area in the world. It is home to some of the world’s most pristine and untouched coral reefs.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip to Tarawa. With its beautiful beaches, rich cultural heritage, and range of activities, Tarawa is a must-visit destination in the Pacific. Enjoy your trip!
