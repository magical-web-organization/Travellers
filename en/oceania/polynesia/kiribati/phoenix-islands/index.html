Introduction to Phoenix Islands

Located in the heart of the Pacific Ocean, Phoenix Islands is a group of 8 atolls and 2 submerged coral reefs that belong to Kiribati in Polynesia. This remote and largely untouched paradise is a true haven for adventurers and nature lovers, offering a wealth of outdoor activities, scenic views, and indigenous culture.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Phoenix Islands, we will give you an overview of the best things to see and do, where to stay, what to eat, and how to make the most out of your trip to this stunning destination.

List of Places to Visit in Phoenix Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kiritimati Atoll. This is the largest coral atoll in the world and home to a unique cultural heritage from the ancient village of Ronton. Visit the nearby museum or take a guided tour to learn about the local culture and way of life.

  • Tabuaeran Island. This island in the northernmost part of Phoenix Islands is known for its beautiful beaches and fascinating WWII history. You can visit St. Luke’s Chapel, built by American soldiers during the war, or explore the abandoned Japanese military buildings and bunkers.

Natural Attractions of Phoenix Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fanning Island. Known for its pristine beaches and vibrant marine life, Fanning Island is an ideal spot for snorkeling and swimming. You can also take a hike to discover the lush interior of the island, or rent a bike to explore the local villages.

  • Orona Atoll. This uninhabited atoll offers a breathtaking landscape of coral reefs, crystal-clear lagoons, and exotic birds. You can take a boat tour to explore the different islands in the atoll, or go fishing and camping on the idyllic beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phoenix Islands

  • Kiritimati Island. Explore the rugged terrain and stunning coastline of Kiritimati Island with a guided hiking or biking tour. You can also visit the nearby wildlife sanctuaries and bird colonies, or take a boat trip to explore the surrounding coral reefs.

Best Things to Do in Phoenix Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Local Villages. Visit the different islands and atolls in Phoenix Islands to experience the local way of life and traditions. You can try your hand at weaving, fishing, or cooking traditional dishes, or simply relax and soak in the laid-back atmosphere.

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling. Discover the underwater world of Phoenix Islands with a scuba diving or snorkeling tour. You can explore the coral reefs, swim with colorful fish and sea turtles, or even encounter dolphins and whales.

Food and Drink Scene of Phoenix Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Palusami. This is a traditional Polynesian dish made with taro leaves, coconut cream, and onions, cooked in an underground oven.

  • Ika Mata. Freshly caught raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut milk, served with taro or breadfruit.

Local Beverages

  • Kava. This is a traditional drink made from the roots of the kava plant, known for its relaxing and calming effects.

Where to Stay in Phoenix Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kiritimati Island. There are several accommodation options on Kiritimati Island, ranging from luxury resorts to guesthouses and homestays. Popular choices include Amokura Lodge, Tabakea Inn, and The Villages.

  • Fanning Island. There are a few guesthouses and homestays on Fanning Island, as well as a campsite for outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Phoenix Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tivaevae. These are intricately hand-stitched quilts made by local women, depicting traditional patterns and motifs.

  • Turtle Shell Jewelry. You can find unique jewelry made from turtle shells, decorated with colorful beads and shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Phoenix Islands

Phoenix Islands is home to a rich and diverse culture, influenced by Polynesian, Micronesian, and Melanesian traditions. The locals strongly value family, community, and respect for nature, and maintain their customs through music, dance, and art.

Best Time to Visit Phoenix Islands

The best time to visit Phoenix Islands is from November to April, during the dry season. This is the peak tourist season, with warm and sunny weather, calm seas, and excellent visibility for snorkeling and diving. However, it’s important to note that the weather can be unpredictable and storms can occur any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phoenix Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Protect the environment and wildlife.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in local villages.

  • Remove shells or coral from the beaches.

  • Disrespect the local culture or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phoenix Islands

Phoenix Islands is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s important to take necessary precautions and avoid walking alone at night or in deserted areas.

Health in Phoenix Islands

There are no major health risks in Phoenix Islands, but it’s recommended to stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and protect yourself from mosquito bites. It’s also advisable to check with your doctor regarding any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Phoenix Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phoenix Islands

The nearest airport to Phoenix Islands is in Kiritimati Island (also known as Christmas Island), which has direct flights from Honolulu and Fiji. From there, you can take local boats or flights to other islands and atolls.

How to Get Around in Phoenix Islands

The best way to get around Phoenix Islands is by local boats or ferries, which are widely available for inter-island transportation. You can also rent bikes or cars on some islands.

Where to Go Next from Phoenix Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, you can visit other islands and destinations in Kiribati, such as the capital city of Tarawa or the neighboring Line Islands. You can also take a boat trip to explore the nearby French Polynesia or Fiji.

Conclusion

Phoenix Islands is a true gem of the Pacific, offering an unparalleled blend of nature, culture, and adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect trip to this stunning destination and create memories that will last a lifetime.
