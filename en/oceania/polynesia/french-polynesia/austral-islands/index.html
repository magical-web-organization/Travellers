Introduction to Austral Islands

Austral Islands, located in French Polynesia, is an idyllic destination for travelers seeking a mix of adventure and relaxation. The archipelago is formed by five main islands and 11 smaller islets, each with its own unique charm.

While not as well-known as Tahiti or Bora Bora, Austral Islands offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the rich Polynesian culture and discover breathtaking natural landscapes. In this guide, we will provide you with an extensive list of places to visit, things to do, food to indulge in, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Austral Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tubuai Museum - Displays a collection of artifacts, including traditional musical instruments, weaponry, and pottery.
  • Tama’a Sacred Stone - A revered archaeological site believed to have once been a temple or royal palace.
  • Taputapuatea Marae - The ancient temple complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an important place of worship for Polynesian people.

Natural Attractions of Austral Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maria Cave - A stunning underground cave system filled with stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Tubuai Lagoon - The clear blue waters are perfect for snorkeling, diving and stand-up paddleboarding.
  • Ile de Raivavae - Known for its serene beaches, hiking trails, and stunning coral reefs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Austral Islands

  • Mt. Taitaa - At 1,388 ft, this is the highest peak in Austral Islands. The rugged terrain provides a challenging hike with breathtaking views at the summit.
  • Tubuai Coastal Trail - Follow the coastline on this 30-mile trail that takes you past pristine beaches, forests, and sweeping vistas.
  • Raivavae Trail - A 3-mile hike that takes you through a lush rainforest to the top of a hill overlooking the island.

Best Things to Do in Austral Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching - From July to November, humpback whales migrate to the warm waters of Austral Islands. Take a guided tour to see these majestic creatures up close.
  • Cultural Day Tour - Immerse yourself in the local Polynesian culture by visiting a traditional village, learning to make handicrafts, and watching a dance performance.
  • Snorkeling Adventure - Explore the vibrant coral reefs and colorful marine life that thrive in the crystal-clear waters of Austral Islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Austral Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poisson Cru - A traditional Polynesian dish made with raw fish, coconut milk, and lime juice.
  • Firi Firi - A sweet doughnut served with coffee for breakfast.
  • Uru - A starchy fruit commonly used as a substitute for rice or potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Alcohol - There are a variety of locally produced beers, wines, and spirits in Austral Islands, including Hinano Beer and Mahina Surf Spirit.
  • Fruit Juices - Try the tropical flavors of pineapple, guava, and mango in fresh juices served throughout the islands.

Where to Stay in Austral Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pension de La Plage - A beachfront guesthouse on Tubuai Island with cozy rooms and ocean views.
  • Raivavae Motu Lodge - A rustic retreat with comfortable bungalows nestled in a tropical garden on Ile de Raivavae.
  • Fare Gaina - A charming bed and breakfast with spacious rooms in the heart of the main village on Rurutu Island.

What to Buy in Austral Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tapa Cloth - Handcrafted from the bark of mulberry trees, tapa cloth is a traditional textile used in clothing and home décor.
  • Mother of Pearl Jewelry - Exquisite jewelry made from the iridescent shells of mollusks found in the region.
  • Black Pearls - Known for their lustrous shine and rich color, black pearls are a prized souvenir from French Polynesia.

Local Culture and Customs of Austral Islands

  • Etiquette - It is important to show respect for local customs, which may include removing shoes before entering a home or dressing modestly in public spaces.
  • Dances - Polynesian dances are an important part of the local culture, with performances often featuring traditional costumes and music.
  • Language - While French and Tahitian are the official languages of French Polynesia, many locals in Austral Islands speak Paumotu, a Polynesian language specific to the islands.

Best Time to Visit Austral Islands

The best time to visit Austral Islands is during the months of June to September. This is the dry season, with less humidity and moderate temperatures perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Austral Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the Polynesian culture.

Don’t:

  • Take coral or other natural resources from the islands.
  • Swim in restricted areas or disturb the marine life.
  • Disrespect sacred sites or landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Austral Islands

While Austral Islands is considered a safe destination, it is important to take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Use common sense when walking alone or at night, and lock your valuables in a safe or locker.

Health in Austral Islands

It is recommended to receive a hepatitis A and B vaccination before traveling to Austral Islands. Remember to drink plenty of bottled water, use sunscreen, and protect yourself from mosquito bites.

Getting to Austral Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Austral Islands

The main airport for Austral Islands is on Tupuai Island, which is served by Air Tahiti. Flights from Tahiti, the main hub in French Polynesia, arrive several times a week.

Bus Stations Near Austral Islands

There are no bus or railway services in Austral Islands.

Other Options

Ferry services are available from Tahiti to the neighboring islands of Rurutu and Tubuai.

How to Get Around in Austral Islands

The best way to get around Austral Islands is by car or scooter rental, which can be arranged through your accommodations. There are also taxi services available on most of the islands.

Where to Go Next from Austral Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tahiti - The bustling capital of French Polynesia is just a short flight away and offers a vibrant culture scene and stunning beaches.
  • Moorea - This nearby island is known for its crystal-clear waters, world-class diving, and luxurious resorts.
  • Raiatea and Taha’a - Known as the “Vanilla Islands,” these neighboring islands offer a chance to explore lush rainforests, snorkel in coral gardens, and visit vanilla plantations.

Conclusion

Austral Islands is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to explore the Polynesian culture and stunning natural landscapes. From the fascinating museums and historical landmarks to the outdoor adventures and scenic trails, there is something for every traveler. Plan your trip with our comprehensive guide and get ready to immerse yourself in the beauty of Austral Islands.
