Introduction to Rapa

Rapa is a hidden gem in French Polynesia, located in the Austral Islands and the southernmost inhabited island in the country. Its rich culture and natural beauty make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. The island has a poplulation of around 500 people who are mostly Polynesian.

List of Places to Visit in Rapa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rapa Museum - Discover the fascinating history of Rapa and its people at the Rapa Museum, located in Ahurei.

Marae Tapioi and Utuautu - Explore one of the oldest archaeological sites in French Polynesia. These ancient stone temples date back more than 1,000 years.

Natural Attractions of Rapa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aura Cave - Descend into the subterranean darkness and discover the unique rock formations in Aura Cave. This attraction is not for the faint of heart as it requires climbing ropes and descending over slick rocks. It’s a great half day activity.

Mont Perahu - Hike to the top of the highest peak on Rapa and marvel at the breathtaking views of the surrounding ocean.

Lake Ahurei - Rent a kayak and explore the serene waters of Lake Ahurei. Watch the birds, the fishes and enjoy the calm.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rapa

Mont Perahu Hike - The Mont Perahu hike is a challenging adventure that takes you to the highest peak on Rapa. The hike takes around 8 hours round trip, but the views are worth the effort.

Te Pukatea Hike - The Te Pukatea hike is a more relaxed, intermediate trek that takes you through Rapa’s lush vegetation to a beautiful waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Rapa: Activities and Tours

Kayaking - Rent a kayak and explore the calm waters of Lake Ahurei.

Snorkeling - Discover the vibrant coral reefs and underwater life of Rapa by snorkeling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rapa

Day 1-2: Discover Rapa’s museums and historical places, including the Rapa Museum and Marae Tapioi and Utuautu.

Day 3-4: Hike or bike to Rapa’s natural wonders, including Mont Perahu and Te Pukatea waterfall.

Day 5-6: Enjoy outdoor activities such as kayaking in Lake Ahurei or snorkeling to discover the colourful corals.

Day 7: Take a day to relax and enjoy the island’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Rapa

Must-Try Dishes

Roho - Roho is a traditional Polynesian dish made with fish and coconut milk. Try it at one of the local restaurants on the island.

Poisson Cru - Poisson Cru is a raw tuna dish marinated in lime, coconut milk, and onion.

Local Beverages

Rouau - Made from fermented oranges, Rouau is a drink that is unique to Rapa. It is a slightly sour and sweet refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Rapa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pension Tepuarahi - This family-run guesthouse features five bungalows and is located on a hillside with stunning views of the ocean.

Fare Manuia - Fare Manuia features six bungalows and is located near the village of Ahurei.

What to Buy in Rapa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tifaifai - Tifaifai is a traditional Polynesian quilt that is handmade and features vibrant colors and patterns.

Tahitian Pearls - Tahitian pearls are famous throughout the world for their unique beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Rapa

Rapa’s culture is deeply rooted in its Polynesian heritage. Traditions such as dance, music, and weaving are still an essential part of island life. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, including the wearing of modest clothing when visiting sacred sites.

Best Time to Visit Rapa

The best time to visit Rapa is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is mild, and rainfall is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rapa

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting sacred sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take anything from archaeological sites.
  • Be disrespectful towards locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rapa

Rapa is a safe place for travelers. Keep in mind that the terrain can be challenging, so it’s essential to be careful when hiking or biking.

Health in Rapa

Medical facilities on the island are limited, so it’s essential to bring any necessary medications with you. Remember to drink plenty of water and protect yourself from the sun to avoid sunburn or sunstroke.

Getting to Rapa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rapa

The closest airport to Rapa is at Rurutu island. You can take a local flight from Rurutu to the airport at Rapa.

Bus Stations Near Rapa

There are no buses on Rapa. It’s best to rent a bike, hire a taxi or rent a car with a driver for tours.

Other Options

The only way to get to Rapa is by taking a local flight from Rurutu.

How to Get Around in Rapa

The best way to get around Rapa is on foot or by bike. Rental cars are also available, but they can be expensive. Taxis are not readily available on the island and should be booked in advance.

Where to Go Next from Rapa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearest island to Rapa is Rurutu. If you’re looking to stay in French Polynesia, you can also visit Tahiti or Bora Bora.

Conclusion

Rapa offers an unforgettable travel experience for those seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. From hiking and biking to exploring archaeological sites and enjoying local cuisine, the island has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for relaxation or adventure, Rapa is a destination that should not be missed.
