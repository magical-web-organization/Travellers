Introduction to Gambier Islands

Nestled in the far southeastern corner of French Polynesia, Gambier Islands is a destination that’s off the beaten track. While it may not be as popular as Bora Bora or Tahiti, the country’s more famous destinations, this island group is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be explored. Gambier Islands is made up of four main islands: Mangareva, Aukena, Akamaru, and Taravai, and several smaller ones.

Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Gambier Islands is a paradise for travelers who are looking for an authentic Polynesian experience. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, a foodie, or an adventure seeker, there’s something for you in this beautiful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Gambier Islands

Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places in Gambier Islands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Notre-Dame de Rapa Church

This beautiful church is the centerpiece of Gambier Islands. It was built by French missionaries in the mid-19th century and is an impressive example of Western architecture blended with Polynesian styles. The church is adorned with intricate carvings, and the interiors are decorated with murals and frescoes.

Musée de Mangareva

The Musée de Mangareva is a small museum that showcases the history and culture of the islands. It houses a collection of artifacts, including traditional crafts, carvings, and ancient stone tools. The museum also displays exhibits on the islands’ flora and fauna.

Ruins of Old Mangareva

The ruins of Old Mangareva are the remains of an ancient Polynesian settlement that dates back to the 14th century. The site is located on an islet called Taravai, and you can only reach it by boat. The ruins include stone platforms, terraces, and tombs.

Natural Attractions of Gambier Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mt. Duff

Mt. Duff is the highest point in Gambier Islands, standing 441 meters high. It’s a challenging but rewarding climb, with breathtaking views of the surrounding islands and the ocean.

Lake Vaihiria

Lake Vaihiria is a beautiful crater lake that’s surrounded by lush vegetation and steep cliffs. You can swim, kayak, or simply relax on the shore and take in the peaceful scenery.

Akamaru Atoll

Akamaru Atoll is a stunning coral atoll that’s home to numerous species of marine life. You can go snorkeling or diving to explore the colorful coral reefs and spot tropical fish, rays, and turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gambier Islands

Gambier Islands is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of trails that lead to stunning vistas and natural wonders. Here are some of the best hiking routes in the islands:

Mt. Mokoto

Mt. Mokoto is a relatively easy hike, winding through lush vegetation and scenic views of the islands. The trail starts from the village of Rikitea and takes about 2-3 hours to complete.

Aukena Loop

The Aukena Loop is a popular hiking and biking trail that circles the island of Aukena. The trail passes through stunning beaches, cliffs, and forests, with plenty of opportunities for wildlife viewing.

Taravai Ridge Walk

The Taravai Ridge Walk is a challenging hike that rewards with stunning views of the islands and the ocean. The trail starts from the village of Agonoko and takes about 5-6 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Gambier Islands: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there are plenty of things to do in Gambier Islands. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

Snorkeling and Diving

Gambier Islands has some of the best diving and snorkeling spots in French Polynesia. You can explore the stunning coral reefs and encounter a variety of marine life, including rays, sharks, and turtles.

Island Tours

Island tours are a great way to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Gambier Islands. You can visit historical sites, ancient ruins, and local villages, and learn about the islands’ unique traditions and customs.

Sunset Cruise

A sunset cruise is the perfect way to end a day in Gambier Islands. You can enjoy the tranquil scenery, spot wildlife, and soak in the breathtaking views as the sun sets over the horizon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gambier Islands

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for exploring Gambier Islands:

Day 1: Arrive in Rikitea and explore the village.

Day 2: Hike to Mt. Duff for panoramic views of the islands.

Day 3: Take a boat tour to Akamaru Atoll for snorkeling or diving.

Day 4: Visit the Musée de Mangareva and learn about the islands’ history and culture.

Day 5: Explore the ruins of Old Mangareva on Taravai Island.

Day 6: Relax on the beaches of Aukena Island or go on a biking tour.

Day 7: Enjoy a sunset cruise and farewell dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Gambier Islands

Gambier Islands has a rich culinary heritage, with a mix of Polynesian, French, and other international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Poisson Cru

Poisson cru is a popular Polynesian raw fish salad that’s made with coconut milk and lime juice. It’s a refreshing and flavorful dish that’s perfect for a hot day.

Oyster Soup

Oyster soup is a traditional Gambier Islands dish that’s made with fresh oysters, coconut milk, and a variety of local spices and herbs. It’s a flavorful and warming soup that’s perfect for a cool evening.

Breadfruit Salad

Breadfruit salad is a salad made from boiled breadfruit, tomatoes, onions, and other vegetables. It’s a simple but tasty salad that’s perfect for a light lunch.

Local Beverages

Hinano Beer

Hinano beer is a popular beer in French Polynesia that’s brewed in Tahiti. It’s a refreshing lager that’s perfect for hot days.

Coconut Milk

Coconut milk is a staple in Polynesian cuisine, and it’s used in many dishes and beverages. It’s a creamy and refreshing drink that’s perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Gambier Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gambier Islands has a limited but cozy selection of accommodation options. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the islands:

Chez Yvonne

Chez Yvonne is a cozy guesthouse located in Rikitea. It offers comfortable rooms, a shared lounge, and a garden. It’s a great option for budget-conscious travelers.

Pension Papara

Pension Papara is a family-run guesthouse located in Mangareva. It offers simple but comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and a terrace. It’s a great option for travelers who want to experience local hospitality.

Hôtel Akama

Hôtel Akama is the only hotel in Gambier Islands. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar. It’s a great option for travelers who want a more upscale experience.

What to Buy in Gambier Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gambier Islands is known for its handicrafts, including intricate carvings, woven baskets, and shell jewelry. Here are some local products that you can bring home as souvenirs:

Black Pearls

Gambier Islands is home to some of the best black pearls in French Polynesia. You can buy them directly from local pearl farms or from gift shops in Rikitea.

Handwoven Baskets

Handwoven baskets are a staple in Gambier Islands. They’re made from pandanus leaves and come in a variety of sizes and designs.

Carvings

Gambier Islands is famous for its intricate carvings, which include wooden figures, masks, and other decorative objects. You can find them in gift shops and artisan markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Gambier Islands

Gambier Islands has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique blend of Polynesian and French influences. Here are some customs and traditions that you should be aware of:

Tifaifai

Tifaifai is a traditional quilt-making technique that’s practiced by women in Gambier Islands. The quilts are made by stitching together colorful cloth squares, and they’re often given as gifts or used in ceremonial events.

Tattoos

Tattoos are an important part of Polynesian culture, and many locals in Gambier Islands have intricate tattoos that symbolize their heritage and identity. If you’re interested in getting a tattoo, make sure to do your research and find a reputable artist.

Traditional Dancing

Traditional dancing is a popular form of entertainment in Gambier Islands. The dances often tell stories about the islands’ history and culture, and they’re performed at festivals and other events.

Best Time to Visit Gambier Islands

The best time to visit Gambier Islands is from May to October when the weather is dry and the temperatures are cooler. December to April is the rainy season, so it’s best to avoid this time if you want to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gambier Islands

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Gambier Islands:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Learn a few words of the local language, such as “hello” (ia orana) and “thank you” (mauruuru).
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Respect the environment and avoid littering and damaging coral reefs.

Don’t:

  • Touch or take anything from historical sites or cultural landmarks.
  • Ignore local rules and regulations.
  • Swim in areas where the water is not safe or designated for swimming.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gambier Islands

Gambier Islands is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always best to take standard safety precautions, such as:

  • Keeping your valuables secure.
  • Staying alert and aware of your surroundings.
  • Avoiding isolated areas.

Health in Gambier Islands

Gambier Islands is a relatively healthy destination, with no major health risks. However, it’s always best to take basic health precautions, such as:

  • Drinking bottled water or boiled water.
  • Protecting yourself from mosquito bites.
  • Bringing sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Gambier Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gambier Islands is a remote destination, but it’s still accessible by air and sea. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Gambier Islands

Gambier Islands has a small airport located on Mangareva Island that serves flights from Tahiti. Air Tahiti is the main airline that operates flights to Gambier Islands.

Boat Services

Boat services are also available, connecting Gambier Islands to nearby destinations such as Rapa and Tahiti. However, boat schedules are less frequent than flights, and the journey can take several hours or days.

How to Get Around in Gambier Islands

The best way to get around Gambier Islands is by foot, bike, or boat. Bikes can be rented from guesthouses or hotels, and they’re a great way to explore the islands at a leisurely pace. Boat tours and rentals are also available for exploring the surrounding waters.

Where to Go Next from Gambier Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are plenty of nearby destinations that you can explore from Gambier Islands. Here are some ideas:

Rapa

Rapa is a small island located about 112 kilometers south of Gambier Islands. It’s home to stunning volcanic landscapes, ancient ruins, and a traditional culture that’s unique to the island.

Tubuai

Tubuai is a group of islands located about 1,000 kilometers west of Gambier Islands. It’s known for its stunning beaches, coral reefs, and abundant marine life. You can take a flight or boat from Gambier Islands to reach Tubuai.

Conclusion

Gambier Islands is a paradise for travelers who are looking for an authentic Polynesian experience. From stunning natural beauty to rich cultural heritage, this hidden gem is a destination that’s worth exploring. Plan your trip to Gambier Islands today, and discover the beauty of the islands for yourself.
