Introduction to Maupiti

Located in the Society Islands of French Polynesia, Maupiti is a hidden gem that offers a more laid-back and authentic experience compared to its popular neighboring islands like Bora Bora. Despite being small in size, Maupiti boasts of stunning beaches, jagged peaks, fascinating cultural sites, and exciting outdoor activities that will keep any traveler entertained. In this guide, we’ll detail everything you need to know to plan and experience the best of Maupiti.

List of Places to Visit in Maupiti

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Maupiti’s Mairie: The administrative center of the island has a colorful, friendly main building, and is worth seeing.

  2. The Ruins of Maiau Village: These ancient ruins provide insight into Maupiti’s rich past and history. The ruins include many important sites like temples and marae.

  3. Tereia Hill: Hike up to the highest point of the island to visit this important archeological site and take in the breathtaking views of the lagoon below.

Natural Attractions of Maupiti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Teurufaatiu: Hike up to the summit this mountain for an unrivaled 360-degree view of the island, lagoon and surrounding ocean.

  2. Coco Beach: This picturesque beach boasts of crystal-clear waters and beautiful palm trees. Relax with a book, swim in the lagoon, or try stand-up paddleboarding.

  3. Maupiti Lagoon: Kayak or take a boat tour of the mesmerizing lagoon and enjoy swimming with the sharks and manta rays. The lagoon is also known for diving and snorkeling among colorful coral gardens and exotic fish species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maupiti

  1. Hike up to Mount Teurufaatiu through dense rainforests and limestone cliffs to reach the summit. The views from the top are breathtaking.

  2. Take a scenic bike ride around the island and visit some of Maupiti’s hidden beaches, ancient marae, and rustic villages. It is the perfect way to explore this tiny island at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Maupiti: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a boat tour around the lagoon and explore Maupiti’s natural wonders.

  2. Try snorkeling, diving or kayaking in the lagoon, among vibrant coral gardens and marine life.

  3. Relax on one of Maupiti’s stunning beaches and soak in the tropical scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maupiti

Day 1: Arrival and leisure time on the beach
Day 2: Visit the Ruins of Maiau Village and experience the local culture and history
Day 3: Hike up Tereia Hill and enjoy the views
Day 4: Take a boat tour of the lagoon
Day 5: Explore Mount Teurufaatiu
Day 6: Unwind and enjoy Coco Beach
Day 7: Departure from Maupiti

Food and Drink Scene of Maupiti

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Poisson cru: A raw fish dish that is marinated in vinegar and coconut milk, making this a refreshing and light meal.

  2. Maupiti style crepes: Made with coconut cream, bananas, and caster sugar, this is a perfect dessert option for food lovers.

Local Beverages

  1. Hinano Beer: A refreshing lager that you’ll find everywhere in French Polynesia.

  2. Local Cocktails: Try Maupiti’s signature cocktails, made with local rum, fresh fruits, and juices.

Where to Stay in Maupiti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Ninamu Resort: This upscale resort features beach access and full-service amenities.

  2. Papahani Guesthouse: A traditional Polynesian-style guesthouse located on a private beach.

  3. Vaimoana Bungalows: An intimate bungalow resort located on the lagoon and ideal for any traveler looking to have a low-key and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Maupiti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Monoi Oil: A fragrant, scented oil made from coconut oil and Tahitian Gardenia that is commonly used in cosmetics and perfumes.

  2. Fresh vanilla: Take home some of Maupiti’s fresh vanilla beans, which make for a perfect culinary souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Maupiti

  1. Keep it casual and comfortable: This is a laid-back island, so there is no need for dressy attire. It is best to dress in comfortable, casual clothing.

  2. Respect the culture and traditions of the locals: Maupiti is known for its traditional dance and chants, so it is important to respect the local cultural practices.

Best Time to Visit Maupiti

The best time to visit Maupiti is from May to October when the climate is dry and cooler. This is also the peak tourist season, so it is important to book accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maupiti

Do:

  1. Do respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Do try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t leave trash on beaches or other public areas.

  2. Don’t forget to pack your sunscreen, mosquito repellant, and other sun-protection gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maupiti

  1. As with any island, it is important to pay attention to currents when swimming or snorkeling in the lagoon.

  2. Be cautious of sunburn and try to avoid intense UV rays during peak sun hours.

Health in Maupiti

  1. Drink bottled water, as the tap water in Maupiti is not safe for consumption.

  2. Watch what you eat and try to stick to hygienic food options.

Getting to Maupiti: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maupiti

The Maupiti airport is located on a tiny landmass off the northwest edge of the island and all flights are connected through Tahiti.

Bus Stations Near Maupiti

There are no public bus services available on the island.

Other Options

  1. Boat services: Boats operate between the islands, and between Tahiti and Maupiti.

  2. Helicopter tours: Helicopter tours of French Polynesia are available.

How to Get Around in Maupiti

Bicycle

Bicycles are the primary mode of transport on the island, and it is easy to rent one from local vendors.

Taxi

Taxis are not readily available, but transportation can be arranged through hotels or guesthouses.

Where to Go Next from Maupiti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bora Bora: Known for its overwater bungalows, Bora Bora is only 30 minutes by air and 6 hours by boat from Maupiti.

  2. Huahine: Just a quick 45-minute flight from Maupiti, Huahine is known for its natural beauty and cultural sites, including ancient marae and historic plantations.

Conclusion

Maupiti is a hidden treasure in the South Pacific that offers natural beauty, rich culture and history, and a relaxing atmosphere. With its stunning beaches, outdoor activities and friendly locals, it is no surprise that this little island has become a popular destination for tourists.
