Introduction to Papeete

Papeete is a vibrant city and the capital of French Polynesia. Located on Tahiti Island, Papeete is a bustling metropolis surrounded by stunning natural landscapes. The city offers a unique blend of French and Polynesian cultures, making it one of the most popular destinations among tourists. In this guide, we will take you on a virtual tour to discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Papeete

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Papeete offers a rich history and cultural heritage that can be experienced through its museums and historical sites.

  • Papeete Market: This market displays vibrant local culture with a range of handicrafts, local food items, and flowers.
  • Musee de la Perle Robert Wan: Learn about the fascinating history of Tahitian pearls and the legacy of Robert Wan, whose company is now a world-renowned pearl brand.
  • Black Pearl Museum: Discover the story behind the world’s most famous Tahitian black pearls, from their formation to their cultivation and perfection.
  • James Norman Hall Home and Museum: Explore the life and works of James Norman Hall, an American author who lived in Tahiti during the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Papeete: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Papeete is known for its magnificent natural beauty and plenty of opportunities for outdoor exploration.

  • Faatama Valley: Get close to nature by trekking through this beautiful valley, where you can spot waterfalls and lush greenery.
  • Point Venus: A famous lighthouse stands as a monument on Point Venus, which is a perfect site for a romantic sunset.
  • Botanical Garden of Papeete: Catch a glimpse of the local flora and fauna through an extensive collection of trees and flowers visited by butterflies.
  • Place Toata: This cultural center hosts a wide range of events throughout the year, from cultural festivals to musical performances.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Papeete

Take a break from the city bustle and explore the scenic beauty of Papeete by hiking or biking through its trails.

  • Three Coconut Trees Walk: Take a peaceful coastal walk that runs from Matavai Bay to Venus Point, with picturesque views of the beachside coconut trees.
  • Mount Aorai: Located close to the city, Mount Aorai offers some of the best hiking trails with scenic views of Papeete.
  • Belvedere Lookout: Hike up to this lookout point to witness stunning views of the city and the island surrounding it.

Best Things to Do in Papeete: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring natural beauty and cultural heritage, Papeete offers a wide range of activities for adventurers and explorers, including hiking, cycling, sailing, and more.

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Discover the underwater world of Tahiti by snorkeling or diving in places like Fakarava Atoll, Moorea Island, and Tahaa Island.
  • Surfing: Catch some waves and try your hand at surfing in Papenoo Beach, one of the best surfing spots in Tahiti.
  • Lagoon Tour: Take a tour on a glass-bottom boat and explore the lagoons of Polynesia, watch sharks and rays slide beneath you.
  • Tahitian Cultural Experience: Experience local culture and traditions, including dance performances, musical shows, and traditional feasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Papeete

As a cultural melting pot, Papeete boasts a diverse range of food and drinks to tantalize your taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poisson Cru: A Polynesian specialty, thinly sliced raw tuna mixed with coconut milk and lime juice served with vegetables.
  • Firi Firi: Fried doughnut-shaped pastries made from flour and coconut milk, often served as a snack or dessert.
  • Brochettes: Grilled meat skewers marinated in local spices, served with rice and vegetables.
  • Vanilla Flavors: Tahiti is famous for its vanilla, used in many desserts and dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Mai Tai: A classic cocktail made from rum and tropical fruits, sometimes spiced with cinnamon or nutmeg.
  • Local Beers: Hinano and Tabu are popular beer choices among tourists and locals alike.
  • Fruit juices: Freshly squeezed fruit juices like pineapple, mango, and passion fruit are popular refreshments in Tahiti Island.

Where to Stay in Papeete: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Papeete offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget.

  • Le Meridien: A luxury beach resort with picturesque sea views and tropical gardens.
  • Fare Suisse: A guesthouse popular among backpackers located near the city centre.
  • Tahiti Pearl Beach Resort: A seaside getaway with a private beach, infinity pool, and spa.

What to Buy in Papeete: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tahitian Pearl: Tahitian pearls are well known around the world for their luster and unique black color.
  • Pareus: A traditional Polynesian cloth used as a wrap or skirt, with various designs and colors.
  • Woodcarvings: Handmade wood souvenirs featuring symbolic Polynesian motifs, such as Tiki.
  • Monoi oil: Made from macerated Tiare flowers and coconut oil, used for hair care and skin moisturizing.

Local Culture and Customs of Papeete

  • Respect for elders: Tahitian culture places significant importance on respect for elders and their knowledge.
  • Beachside etiquette: Attire appropriate to wear, meeting places set aside for families, and recreational games observed in Polynesian culture.
  • Tattoos and Body Art: Body art, including traditional tattoos, is an integral part of Polynesian culture.

Best Time to Visit Papeete

The period from May to October is the best time to visit Papeete, with cooler temperatures and lesser rainfall. Also, considered the peak season, the weather is perfect for outdoor activities, sightseeing, and enjoying the beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Papeete

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Use Polynesian phrases like “Ia Orana” and “Maururu” to greet locals.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat while outdoors to protect your skin from the sun.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Swim or sunbathe naked on public beaches.
  • Touch archaeological sites and artifacts without clearance.
  • Give money or gifts to beggars or wander solo at night in deserted areas.
  • Disrespect the Polynesian way of life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Papeete

Papeete is generally a safe city to visit. However, like any other big city, travelers should watch out for pickpockets and avoid areas with high crime rates.

Health in Papeete

Tahiti Island is relatively a safe destination health-wise. As a tropical island, it is advisable to use mosquito repellent to avoid dengue fever virus, among others. Also, visitors are advised to get travel health insurance and check their need for vaccines before their trip.

Getting to Papeete: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Papeete

Papeete International Airport is the main gateway to Tahiti Island from other international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Papeete

Papeete is served by Tahiti’s public transportation, which includes two types of buses – the “Le Truck” for touring around the city center and “Rai” for farther distances within the island.

Other Options

Taxis, car rentals, and private tours are easily accessible in Papeete for tourists who wish to travel at their pace and destinations.

How to Get Around in Papeete

Papeete can be explored via public transportation, car rentals, or bike or foot tours. Since the city is relatively small, you can choose to rent a bicycle or motorbike to explore the city at your pace or take a guided walking tour.

Where to Go Next from Papeete: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Moorea Island: A picturesque island known for its turquoise lagoons, lush landscapes, and pristine beaches.
  • Bora Bora Island: A romantic island getaway with luxurious resorts and stunning beaches, often visited by honeymooners.
  • Huahine Island: An island with an abundance of archaeological sites, rich cultural heritage, and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Papeete is a city with a vibrant culture, rich history, and natural beauty. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just downtime on a perfect beach, Papeete provides the perfect escape. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your itinerary and make the most of your trip to Papeete, Tahiti.
