Fa’a’ā Travel Guide: Discovering the Hidden Gems of French Polynesia

Fa’a’ā is a picturesque town located in French Polynesia, a collection of islands nestled in the South Pacific Ocean. It is situated on the island of Tahiti and is often considered the gateway to French Polynesia. Fa’a’ā may be small, but it’s filled with hidden gems that are waiting to be discovered. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best sights and activities that Fa’a’ā has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Fa’a’ā

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Tahiti and the Islands: This museum showcases the culture and history of French Polynesia through its impressive collection of exhibits and artifacts.
  • James Norman Hall Home: This historical site was once the residence of James Norman Hall, a famous American author who wrote Mutiny on the Bounty.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral: This Gothic-style cathedral is a must-see for its stunning architecture and beautiful stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Fa’a’ā: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pointe Venus: This scenic spot boasts a lighthouse and beautiful black sand beach. It’s a great spot for swimming and surfing.
  • Fautaua Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking. The hike to reach the waterfall takes approximately 2 hours.
  • Vaipahi Gardens: These botanical gardens are home to an array of tropical flowers and plants and provide a peaceful escape from the bustling town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fa’a’ā

  • Mount Aorai: This hiking trail offers stunning views of the island and its surrounding waters. It’s a challenging hike that takes approximately 6-8 hours to complete.
  • Fataua Valley: This lesser-known hiking trail offers a more secluded, off-the-beaten-path experience. The trail takes approximately 2 hours to complete.
  • Bike Ride Along the Coast: For a more leisurely option, rent a bike and ride along the coastline. The views are breathtaking, and it’s a great way to explore the town at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Fa’a’ā: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: Fa’a’ā is known for its great surf, and taking a surfing lesson is a must-do activity. There are plenty of surf schools in the area that offer lessons for all skill levels.
  • Snorkeling Tour: Take a snorkeling tour and explore the vibrant marine life that surrounds the island.
  • Helicopter Tour: For a bird’s eye view of Fa’a’ā and its surrounding islands, take a helicopter tour. It’s a once-in-a-lifetime experience that you won’t forget.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fa’a’ā

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Tahiti and the Islands and Notre-Dame Cathedral. Day 2: Take a surfing lesson and spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach. Day 3: Hike to Fautaua Waterfall in the morning, and spend the afternoon at Vaipahi Gardens. Day 4: Rent a bike and ride along the coastline. In the afternoon, take a snorkeling tour. Day 5: Take a helicopter tour over the island in the morning. Spend the afternoon exploring the James Norman Hall Home. Day 6: Hike Mount Aorai and enjoy the stunning views. Day 7: Spend the day at Pointe Venus, swimming and surfing.

Food and Drink Scene of Fa’a’ā

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poisson Cru: A local dish made with raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut milk. It’s a delicious and refreshing dish.
  • Pain Coco: A sweet coconut bread that’s often served for breakfast or as a snack.
  • Tahitian Vanilla Ice Cream: Tahiti is known for its vanilla, and the ice cream is no exception. It’s creamy, rich, and a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Hinano Beer: A popular beer in French Polynesia that’s perfect for sipping on the beach.
  • Pineapple Juice: The pineapples in French Polynesia are some of the sweetest in the world, and the juice is a refreshing treat.
  • Mai Tai: This iconic cocktail is made with rum, orange curaçao, lime juice, and orgeat syrup. It’s a delicious and tropical way to unwind.

Where to Stay in Fa’a’ā: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tahiti Ia Ora Beach Resort: This luxurious resort is located on a beautiful stretch of beach and offers stunning views of the surrounding islands.
  • Fare Suisse: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Fa’a’ā and is ideal for travelers on a budget.
  • Intercontinental Resort Tahiti: This upscale resort offers a variety of activities and amenities, including a private beach, large swimming pool, and on-site restaurants.

What to Buy in Fa’a’ā: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Monoī Oil: This fragrant oil is made from coconut oil and the tiaré flower, which is native to French Polynesia. It’s perfect for hydrating skin and hair.
  • Black Pearls: French Polynesia is famous for its black pearls, and they make a beautiful and unique souvenir.
  • Tifaifai Quilts: These vibrant, handmade quilts are a traditional Polynesian craft and make a lovely gift or keepsake.

Local Culture and Customs of Fa’a’ā

Fa’a’ā is known for its laid-back lifestyle, warm hospitality, and strong connection to Polynesian culture. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the island’s relaxed pace of life. It’s important to respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites or public places.

Best Time to Visit Fa’a’ā

The best time to visit Fa’a’ā is between May and October when the weather is dry and sunny. December through March is the rainy season, and visitors may encounter more precipitation and high humidity. However, even during the rainy season, the temperature remains warm, and the island is still beautiful to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fa’a’ā

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and indulge in some tropical cocktails.
  • Do respect the locals and their customs.
  • Do take a snorkeling tour and explore the vibrant marine life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the environment.
  • Don’t wander into private property or restricted areas.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fa’a’ā

Fa’a’ā is generally a safe place for tourists, but it’s important to practice common sense and take precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid walking alone at night or in poorly lit areas, and keep your valuables in a safe place. It’s also a good idea to check the weather conditions before engaging in any outdoor activities.

Health in Fa’a’ā

French Polynesia is a relatively healthy country, and there are no major health concerns for travelers. It’s recommended to drink bottled water and to use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites. Visitors should also be aware of the strong sun and take measures to protect their skin.

Getting to Fa’a’ā: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fa’a’ā

The Faa’a International Airport is the main airport in French Polynesia and is located just a few kilometers from Fa’a’ā. Many international airlines offer service to this airport.

Bus Stations Near Fa’a’ā

There are public buses that run throughout Tahiti, including Fa’a’ā. However, the buses can be unreliable, and it’s recommended to rent a car or take a taxi.

Other Options

There are also several ferry companies that provide service between the different islands in French Polynesia. Taking a ferry is a great way to explore the surrounding islands and to get a different perspective on the beauty of the region.

How to Get Around in Fa’a’ā

The easiest way to get around Fa’a’ā is by rental car, scooter or bicycle. Taxis are also available but can be expensive. Many of the attractions in Fa’a’ā are within walking distance, but having transportation gives you the ability to explore other areas of Tahiti and surrounding islands.

Where to Go Next from Fa’a’ā: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore once you’ve experienced Fa’a’ā. Moorea, Bora Bora, and Huahine are all nearby islands with their own unique charm and beauty. Papeete, the capital city of Tahiti, is also worth a visit for its cosmopolitan atmosphere.

Conclusion

Fa’a’ā may be a small town, but it’s filled with beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore Tahiti’s great outdoors, Fa’a’ā has plenty to offer. From hiking to snorkeling, our travel guide has everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to French Polynesia. Bon voyage!
