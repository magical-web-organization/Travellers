Introduction to Bora Bora

Bora Bora is a tropical paradise located in the Society Islands of French Polynesia. With its clear blue lagoon, white sandy beaches, and lush greenery, Bora Bora is a popular destination for honeymooners, families, and adventure seekers alike. The island is surrounded by a barrier reef, making it an ideal place for snorkeling, scuba diving, and other water activities. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors will find plenty of opportunities for cultural and historical exploration.

List of Places to Visit in Bora Bora

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bora Bora offers several places to explore the island’s rich history and cultural heritage. The following are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Bora Bora:

  • Mount Otemanu: This towering extinct volcano is one of the most iconic landmarks in Bora Bora. The peak offers breathtaking views of the lagoon and the surrounding islands.
  • Bora Bora Lagoonarium: This open-air aquarium is a popular tourist attraction that offers visitors the chance to see and swim with marine life in their natural habitat.
  • Matira Beach: Known as one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, Matira Beach is a favorite spot for both locals and tourists. The crystal-clear water and stunning sunsets make this beach a must-visit attraction.
  • Marae Fare Opu: This ancient temple is an important reminder of Bora Bora’s cultural heritage. The temple is believed to have been built around 1000 AD and was used for religious ceremonies.

Natural Attractions of Bora Bora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bora Bora’s natural attractions are awe-inspiring and offer a variety of outdoor activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Bora Bora:

  • Coral Gardens: The Coral Gardens are a must-visit spot for snorkeling enthusiasts. The diversity of marine life here is incredible.
  • Mount Pahia: Located on the east side of the island, Mount Pahia offers a challenging hike with stunning views of the island. The hike can take several hours, so make sure to bring plenty of water and snacks.
  • Shark and Ray Snorkeling: Bora Bora’s lagoon is home to several shark and ray species, and visitors can get up close and personal with them on a guided tour.
  • Motu Tapu: This small island is located just off the coast of Bora Bora and is untouched by modern development. It offers a serene setting for visitors to relax and soak up the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bora Bora

Bora Bora offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Mount Otemanu Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of the island’s most iconic landmark. The hike can take between 6-8 hours, but the stunning views at the summit are worth the effort.
  • Faanui Bay to Anau Hike: Starting at the Faanui Bay, this easy to moderate hike takes you through lush vegetation, past waterfalls, and offers stunning views of the lagoon.
  • Vaitape to Matira Point Bike Ride: This easy bike ride takes you through the charming town of Vaitape, past the famous Matira Beach, and continues to the southernmost point of the island.

Best Things to Do in Bora Bora: Activities and Tours

Bora Bora offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy during their stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Bora Bora:

  • Bora Bora Jet Ski Tour: Explore the lagoon on a thrilling jet ski tour that takes you to some of the island’s most beautiful spots.
  • Bora Bora Island ATV Tour: Discover the lush interior of Bora Bora on an ATV tour that takes you through pineapple fields, plantations, and past ancient temples.
  • Bora Bora Sunset Cruise: Relax as you watch the sun set over the lagoon on a romantic catamaran cruise.
  • Bora Bora Helicopter Tour: Take in breathtaking views of the island from above on a helicopter tour.

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Bora Bora:

Food and Drink Scene of Bora Bora

Must-Try Dishes

Bora Bora’s cuisine is influenced by its Polynesian heritage, and seafood plays a significant role in the island’s dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Bora Bora:

  • Poisson Cru: This raw fish salad is a staple dish in traditional Polynesian cuisine.
  • Coconut Bread: This soft and sweet bread is made with coconut and is often served as a dessert.
  • Grilled Lobster: Freshly caught lobster grilled with garlic and butter is a popular dish among seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

Bora Bora offers several refreshing beverages made from local ingredients. Here are some of the popular local beverages to try:

  • Hinano Beer: This locally brewed beer is one of the most popular on the island.
  • Mai Tai: This fruity cocktail made with rum, lime juice, and orange liqueur is a popular choice among visitors.
  • Coconut Water: Bora Bora’s coconut water is packed with electrolytes and is a refreshing drink to enjoy on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Bora Bora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bora Bora offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Four Seasons Resort Bora Bora: This luxury resort offers overwater bungalows, beachfront villas, and stunning views of the lagoon.
  • Intercontinental Bora Bora Resort & Thalasso Spa: This resort features overwater bungalows with private pools, a stunning coral garden, and an award-winning spa.
  • Bora Bora Pearl Beach Resort & Spa: This resort offers a range of accommodations, including overwater bungalows, beach villas, and garden suites.

What to Buy in Bora Bora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bora Bora offers several unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Black Pearls: Bora Bora is famous for its black pearls, which are exported worldwide.
  • Monoi Oil: This fragrant oil made from coconut oil and tiare flower is a popular beauty product in Bora Bora.
  • Tifaifai: This traditional Polynesian quilt is made from colorful fabrics and is a popular souvenir among visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Bora Bora

Bora Bora’s culture is deeply rooted in Polynesian traditions, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs. Some of the local customs in Bora Bora include:

  • Removing shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Dressing modestly when visiting cultural sites or attending events.
  • Bringing a small gift when visiting someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Bora Bora

The best time to visit Bora Bora is between May and October, when the weather is dry and sunny. This is also the peak tourist season, and visitors are advised to book their accommodations and activities well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bora Bora

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Enjoy the island’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Swim or sunbathe topless, as it is considered inappropriate in Bora Bora.
  • Ignore the sunscreen, as the tropical sun can be intense.
  • Take any coral or shells from the beach, as it is illegal and harmful to the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bora Bora

Bora Bora is generally a safe destination for tourists. Visitors are advised to take the following precautions:

  • Keep valuables in a safe or secure location.
  • Watch out for strong currents when swimming or snorkeling.
  • Stay aware of weather conditions during hurricane season (November to April).

Health in Bora Bora

Bora Bora is a low-risk destination for tropical diseases. Visitors should keep the following in mind:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach illnesses.
  • Wear insect repellent to protect against mosquito bites.
  • Bring any necessary medication or prescriptions with you.

Getting to Bora Bora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bora Bora is accessible by air and sea. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Air Tahiti: This airline offers daily flights from Papeete, Tahiti to Bora Bora.
  • Cruise Ships: Several cruise lines make stops in Bora Bora as part of their South Pacific itineraries.

Airports Near Bora Bora

  • Motu Mute Airport: This is the main airport in Bora Bora and is located on the island of Motu Mute.

Other Options

  • Private Yachts: Visitors can also arrive in Bora Bora by private yacht or charter.
  • Ferry: There is a passenger ferry that runs between Bora Bora and Raiatea.

How to Get Around in Bora Bora

Bora Bora is a small island, and most visitors get around by bicycle, moped, or taxi. It is important to note that there are no rental cars on the island, and visitors are not allowed to drive their own vehicles.

Where to Go Next from Bora Bora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bora Bora is surrounded by several other beautiful islands and destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Tahiti: Bora Bora’s neighbor island is home to the capital city of Papeete and offers a range of cultural and outdoor activities.
  • Moorea: This island is located just a short ferry ride from Tahiti and is an ideal destination for hiking and cultural exploration.
  • Huahine: This lesser-known island is another great option for outdoor activities, including snorkeling, hiking, and island tours.

Conclusion

Bora Bora is a paradise on earth, with its breathtaking natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive guide provides visitors with everything they need to know to plan their trip to Bora Bora, from must-visit attractions to logistics and transportation. With all this information at hand, visitors can relax and enjoy the island’s laid-back atmosphere and tropical charm.
