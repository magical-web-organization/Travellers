Introduction to Tikehau

Tikehau is a picturesque coral atoll located in the eastern part of the Tuamotu Islands. It is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, abundant marine life, and pristine beaches backed by coconut palms. The island is also famous for its pink sand beaches, which are a result of crushed coral mixed with white sand. Tikehau is situated 340 km northeast of Tahiti, making it easily accessible by air. The main settlement on the island is Tuherahera, which is where most of the accommodation, shops, and restaurants are located.

List of Places to Visit in Tikehau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Tikehau does not have any significant museums, it is home to several cultural sites that are worth exploring. Here are a few top ones:

  • The Tikehau Church: Built in 1874, this church is a magnificent example of French Polynesian architecture. The striking white church is set against a backdrop of turquoise waters and vibrant coral reefs.

  • The Tikehau Cemetery: The island’s cemetery is a peaceful and serene spot surrounded by palm trees and tropical vegetation.

  • The Tikehau Pearl Farm: Tikehau is known for its pearls, and a visit to the pearl farm is a must-do on the island. Take a tour of the farm to learn about the pearl-making process and shop for exquisite pearl jewelry.

Natural Attractions of Tikehau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tikehau boasts a wealth of natural beauty, both above and below the water. Here are some of the best things to do on the island that will help you appreciate its beauty:

  • Snorkeling: Tikehau is a snorkeler’s paradise, with a diverse range of coral reefs and sea life. Take a tour or rent your own equipment and explore the lagoon’s marine life, including colorful fish, rays, and even the occasional shark.

  • Diving: Tikehau has world-renowned dive sites, including the famous Tuheiava Pass, where you can see schools of sharks, manta rays, and turtles.

  • Bird Watching: Tikehau is home to a variety of bird species. Take a bird watching tour to spot birds such as the Pacific Golden Plover and the Ruddy Turnstone.

  • Sunset Cruise: Take an evening boat ride to witness the spectacular sunset views over the lagoon, an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tikehau

Tikehau is a flat atoll, and there are no significant hiking trails. However, biking is a popular way to explore the island’s beautiful scenery. Rent a bike from one of the rental shops on the island, and cycle through the small villages to see how the locals live.

Best Things to Do in Tikehau: Activities and Tours

Tikehau offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit everyone’s interests. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Deep-Sea Fishing: Tikehau is known for its abundant sea life, including tuna, mahi-mahi, and marlin. Charter a fishing boat and try your luck at catching the big one.

  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and paddle through the atoll’s clear waters, enjoying breathtaking views of the coral reefs and marine life.

  • Shark and Ray Feeding: Take a boat tour to the shallow waters around the atoll and watch as the guides hand-feed the friendly sharks and rays.

  • Traditional Dance and Music Shows: Experience the vibrant culture of Tahiti by attending a traditional dance and music show, performed by the locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tikehau

Day 1: Arrive in Tikehau and check into your accommodation. Spend the day exploring the local village of Tuherahera and relax on the beach.

Day 2: Take a snorkeling tour in the morning, followed by a bike ride through the island in the afternoon.

Day 3: Go diving in some of the best dive sites on the atoll, including the famous Tuheiava Pass.

Day 4: Enjoy a sunset cruise and witness the stunning sunset views over the lagoon.

Day 5: Immerse yourself in the local culture by attending a traditional dance and music show.

Day 6: Take a picnic on one of the deserted motus (islets) and spend the day enjoying the pristine beaches.

Day 7: Say goodbye to Tikehau and catch your return flight.

Food and Drink Scene of Tikehau

Must-Try Dishes

Tikehau is famous for its fresh seafood and Polynesian cuisine. Here are some dishes to try:

  • Poisson Cru: This Tahitian specialty is a raw fish salad seasoned with coconut milk and lime juice.

  • I’a Ota: Another raw fish dish, this one is marinated in lime juice and mixed with diced vegetables.

  • Coconut Bread: This delicious bread is made using coconuts and is a perfect accompaniment to any meal.

Local Beverages

Tikehau has a range of local drinks, including:

  • Hinano Beer: A popular Tahitian beer.

  • Mai Tai: A tropical cocktail made with rum, orange curacao, and lime juice.

  • Coconut Water: A refreshing and healthy drink, found in abundance on the island.

Where to Stay in Tikehau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tikehau offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the top options are:

  • Tikehau Pearl Beach Resort: A luxurious resort located on a private islet, offering stunning views and world-class facilities.

  • Pension Chez Louise: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of the village, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to the island’s attractions.

  • Havaiki Lodge: A mid-range option located on the lagoon’s edge, offering easy access to the water and stunning views.

What to Buy in Tikehau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tikehau offers a range of locally made products that are perfect to bring back home as souvenirs. Here are some items to consider:

  • Black Pearls: Tikehau is famous for its pearls, and it is a perfect place to buy high-quality black pearls, whether it is a necklace or pair of earrings.

  • Tahitian Sarongs (Pareos): These versatile pieces of cloth make for excellent souvenirs, and it is perfect for use as a beach cover-up, skirt, or dress.

  • Monoi Oil: A locally made oil made from coconut oil infused with Tiare flowers, it is a popular souvenir for its excellent skin and hair benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Tikehau

The local culture of Tikehau is heavily influenced by Polynesian traditions. The locals speak Tahitian, and the Tahitian way of life is evident in their hospitality and welcoming nature.

Best Time to Visit Tikehau

The best time to visit Tikehau is from May to October when the weather is dry and pleasant, and the temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tikehau

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Explore the natural beauty of the island.

Don’t:

  • Touch the coral reefs, as this can damage them and harm the marine life.

  • Litter on the island.

  • Ignore the locals’ advice regarding safety and weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tikehau

Tikehau is a safe island with a low crime rate. However, it is recommended to take regular precautions such as:

  • Avoid walking alone in secluded areas.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings.

  • Listen to the locals’ advice, especially regarding weather conditions and marine life.

Health in Tikehau

Visitors to Tikehau do not need any specific vaccinations or health precautions. However, it is recommended to drink bottled water and take precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Tikehau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tikehau

The Tikehau atoll has a small airport that receives flights from Tahiti and other nearby islands.

Bus Stations Near Tikehau

Tikehau does not have any bus or public transportation services.

Other Options

The most common way to get to Tikehau is by plane. Regular flights are available from Tahiti, Huahine, and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Tikehau

The best way to get around Tikehau is by bicycle, which can be rented from one of the rental shops on the island. Another excellent option is to rent a scooter or a jeep for exploring the island’s more remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Tikehau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tikehau is an excellent starting point for exploring the Tuamotus archipelago. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Rangiroa: A popular dive destination, Rangiroa is an hour’s flight from Tikehau.

  • Fakarava: Another excellent dive destination, Fakarava is a two-hour boat ride from Tikehau.

  • Apataki: This atoll is known for its stunning beaches and picturesque lagoon, and it is a three-hour boat ride from Tikehau.

Conclusion

Tikehau is undoubtedly a paradise on earth, with breathtaking natural beauty, pristine beaches, and a vibrant local culture. A trip here is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that will leave you with lasting memories. Follow our comprehensive travel guide to make the most of your visit to this idyllic island.
