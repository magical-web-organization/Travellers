Introduction to Manihi

Manihi is a small atoll island located in the Tuamotu Islands in French Polynesia, making it one of the lesser-known destinations in Oceania. With its crystal-clear waters, idyllic beaches, and rich cultural heritage, Manihi is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. In this travel guide, we’ll give you all the information you need to plan your dream vacation to this South Pacific paradise.

List of Places to Visit in Manihi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manihi has a deep cultural history that is worth exploring. The Tukuhora Marae is one such site that is of great historical significance to locals. It is an ancient ritual and ceremonial center where local Polynesians come to connect with their cultural traditions and history. Another must-visit attraction is the Church of Saint Joseph, which is located in the heart of the main village on the island.

Natural Attractions of Manihi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Manihi is primarily known for its beautiful lagoon and coral reef, which offer diverse marine life perfect for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. The Hiraanae Pass, located on the west side of the island, is a great spot for snorkeling and diving. For those who prefer a more laid-back experience, the beaches on the northwest side of the island offer opportunities to relax and soak in the sun. The scenic views from the island’s famous Pink Sand Beach are a must-see for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manihi

Manihi is only 4.4 square miles in size, so it is relatively easy to explore on foot. The island has a few marked hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the island’s scenery and diverse plant and animal life. Another popular option is to rent a bike and ride around the island, and seeing it in this way provides a unique perspective of the Te Rairoa Lagoon.

Best Things to Do in Manihi: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do on the island include snorkeling and diving, pearl farming experiences, cultural tours of the island’s historical sites, and walking or biking tours of the island’s natural beauty. Island tours can be arranged through local tour operators or your place of accommodation on the island. With many other activities and tours to choose from, a visit to Manihi is sure to be packed with unforgettable experiences and moments.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manihi

With so much to explore, here is a suggested one-week itinerary for your Manihi trip:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the island’s natural beauty by taking a snorkeling or diving tour
  • Day 3-4: Take a pearl farm tour and learn about the history of Manihi’s pearl industry
  • Day 5: Explore the island’s historical and cultural sites, including the Tukuhora Marae and Church of Saint Joseph
  • Day 6: Rent a bike and ride around the island, stopping at scenic spots and beaches along the way
  • Day 7: Take a relaxing day on one of the island’s beaches, soaking in the sun and relaxing with picturesque views

Food and Drink Scene of Manihi

One of the highlights of a trip to Manihi is indulging in some of the island’s delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poisson Cru: A traditional Tahitian dish that is made from raw fish, coconut milk, and lime juice.
  • Ika Mata: A similar dish to Poisson Cru made with fish, tomato, and onion.
  • Grilled Seafood: Sample freshly caught seafood that is grilled to perfection and served with a variety of sauces and dips.

Local Beverages

  • Hinano Beer: A staple beer in French Polynesia, Hinano has a light, crisp flavor that is perfect for a hot day in paradise.
  • Noni Juice: Made from the tropical noni fruit, this juice is often used for medicinal purposes and is known to have several health benefits.

Where to Stay in Manihi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manihi offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular options include the Manihi Pearl Beach Resort, which is a beautiful and luxurious resort located by the beach and lagoon. Another great option is the Pension Poerani Nui, which offers budget-friendly rooms for travelers on a tighter budget, complete with exceptional views over the lagoon.

What to Buy in Manihi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Manihi is known for its pearl farms, and you can purchase pearls and related products as souvenirs to take home. As well as pearls, you can also buy tapa cloth, which is a traditional Polynesian fabric made from the bark of trees and is often decorated with intricate patterns and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Manihi

Polynesian culture is prevalent on Manihi, and visitors should respect and take an interest in local customs. Many of the island’s cultural sites require visitors to dress modestly and remove shoes before entering. Visitors should also be mindful of their behavior on the island, especially around sacred cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Manihi

The best time to visit Manihi is between May and October when the weather is generally dry and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 24°C to 28°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manihi

Do:

  • Participate in cultural activities and respect local customs.
  • Take a pearl farm tour and learn about Manihi’s distinctive pearl industry.
  • Indulge in the delicious local cuisine, including fresh seafood and local beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear immodest clothing when visiting cultural and historical sites.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manihi

Manihi is generally considered a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise common sense when out and about on the island, especially when it comes to walking or biking on the roads. Visitors should also be cautious when swimming or participating in water activities and follow all proper safety protocols.

Health in Manihi

There are no specific health risks associated with Manihi. Visitors are advised to take appropriate precautions to protect themselves from the sun, including wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated with the island climate. Visitors should also bring a well-stocked first aid kit, including basic healthcare essentials like pain relievers and antihistamines.

Getting to Manihi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manihi

The nearest airport to Manihi is the Fakarava Airport, which is about 40 miles away from the island. From there, visitors can take a charter flight or transfer to a local flight on one of the local airlines to reach Manihi.

Bus Stations Near Manihi

Manihi is a small island and doesn’t have any bus stations. Visitors can explore the island on foot or rent a bike to get around.

Other Options

There is no direct ferry service to Manihi, and visitors cannot bring their cars to the island. Visitors can choose to charter a boat or take a small plane to get to the island.

How to Get Around Manihi

Manihi is a very small island, and visitors can explore it on foot or rent a bike to get around. Visitors can also rent a boat to explore the lagoon, which offers a unique perspective of the island’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Manihi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Tuamotu Islands offer a range of destinations for visitors to explore. Some of the popular nearby destinations include Fakarava Island, Tikehau Atoll, and Rangiroa Atoll, all of which offer their unique attractions and experiences that travelers should take advantage of. Visitors who crave a more ‘big city’ experience can take a flight to Tahiti, the capital city of French Polynesia, which offers a range of urban activities and attractions, including museums, restaurants, and shops.

Conclusion

Manihi is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique views into French Polynesia’s learning and lifestyles, the island offers a unique experience that will leave visitors with memories and experiences that are one-of-a-kind. With this comprehensive travel guide, trip planning is easy, and visitors can make their dream vacation a reality.
