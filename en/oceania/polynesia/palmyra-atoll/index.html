Introduction to Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll, located in Polynesia, is one of the most remote and beautiful destinations in the world. The atoll is owned by The Nature Conservancy and managed jointly with the US government. It is a designated National Wildlife Refuge and open exclusively for research and outdoor recreational activities. Its crystal-clear lagoon, coral reefs, and expansive sandy beaches offer visitors a rare opportunity to explore untouched natural landscapes and marine life. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Palmyra Atoll.

List of Places to Visit in Palmyra Atoll

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palmyra Atoll is a wildlife sanctuary with no permanent residents and, therefore, no museums or historical places. The atoll’s rich history, from its discovery by Americans to its use as a military base during World War II, can be explored through books, online resources, and anecdotes shared by the staff on-site.

Natural Attractions of Palmyra Atoll: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Palmyra Atoll is a paradise for nature and outdoor adventure lovers. The following are the top natural attractions on the atoll:

  • Lagoon and Beaches: The atoll’s lagoon is home to over 400 fish species, including clownfish, parrotfish, and eagle rays. The coral reefs are a snorkeler’s and diver’s paradise, offering breathtaking views of colorful corals, sponges, and sea anemones. You can also take a stroll on any of the atoll’s three beautiful beaches.
  • Bird Watching: Palmyra Atoll is a haven for seabirds, with over 16 species nesting on the atoll or visiting during their migration. You can spot red-footed boobies, frigatebirds, terns, and other beautiful birds during your stay.
  • Hiking and Biking Trails: Palmyra Atoll has several nature trails that offer scenic views of the atoll’s wildlife and plants. You can walk or bike on these trails with the appropriate permits and safety gear.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll has designated hiking and biking trails that are open to visitors. The following are some of the top trails that you can explore with appropriate permits and safety gear:

  • South Trail: This is a 1.5-mile trail that takes you from the southern beaches to the airstrip. You will enjoy scenic views of the lagoon and bird watching opportunities along the way.
  • North Trail: This is a 2.5-mile trail that takes you to the northern beaches. During the hike, you will see the atoll’s plant life and wildlife and enjoy stunning ocean views.
  • Coconut Grove Trail: This is a short trail that leads you through a dense grove of palm trees and offers a great opportunity to see land hermit crabs in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Palmyra Atoll: Activities and Tours

Palmyra Atoll offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. The following are some of the best things to do on the atoll:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The atoll’s coral reefs offer some of the best snorkeling and diving opportunities in the world. You can see a wide variety of colorful fish, sea turtles, and other marine life.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: You can explore the atoll’s lagoon on a kayak or paddleboard and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding islands and wildlife.
  • Fishing: You can fish for yellowfin and skipjack tuna, wahoo, and mahi-mahi in the atoll’s waters.
  • Sunset Cruise: You can enjoy a romantic sunset cruise around the atoll and experience the beauty of the setting sun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palmyra Atoll

Day 1-2: Arrival and Orientation

  • Meet with staff and receive orientation and permits.
  • Explore the beaches and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Snorkeling and Diving

  • Go snorkeling or diving and explore the coral reefs.
  • Participate in a scientific research project.

Day 4: Bird Watching and Hiking

  • Take a morning bird watching walk and see species such as red-footed boobies and frigatebirds.
  • Hike on the atoll’s trails and see unique plant species.

Day 5: Fishing and Kayaking

  • Go fishing in the atoll’s waters and catch yellowfin or skipjack tuna.
  • Explore the lagoon on a kayak or paddleboard.

Day 6: Preservation of Nature and Sunset Cruise

  • Participate in the atoll’s conservation project.
  • Enjoy a sunset cruise and champagne.

Day 7: Departure

  • Say farewell to the island and complete exit procedures.

Food and Drink Scene of Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll does not have restaurants or bars, but the atoll’s well-stocked kitchen can provide freshly prepared healthy meals to visitors staying in the island’s accommodations.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banana Pancakes: A delicious breakfast made of fresh bananas, honey, and cinnamon.
  • Sashimi: Fresh fish sashimi is a local favorite and available on occasion.
  • Coconut Curry: Made from locally grown coconuts and other fresh ingredients, this flavorful dish is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is available on the island and is the perfect drink to beat the heat.
  • Fruit Smoothies: Made from locally grown tropical fruits, these smoothies are a refreshing drink alternative.

Where to Stay in Palmyra Atoll: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palmyra Atoll has limited accommodation options and they are reserved for researchers, government officials, and visitors engaged in outdoor recreational activities. Accommodations include tents and a limited number of cottages and guesthouses. You can reserve accommodation through The Nature Conservancy.

What to Buy in Palmyra Atoll: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palmyra Atoll is a wildlife sanctuary with no stores or commercial activity. However, you can bring back souvenirs and local products such as hand-made ornaments made from Seashells, bird feather art by indigenous artists and a variety of local handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll is a wildlife sanctuary with no permanent residents, but the local culture is centered around conservation and preservation of nature. The island’s staff and visitors are expected to abide by strict environmental guidelines and respect the natural wildlife. As guests of the atoll, everyone should pitch in to keep the isle safe, pristine, and accessible for future generations.

Best Time to Visit Palmyra Atoll

The best time to visit Palmyra Atoll is during the dry season from November to April. During this time, you can enjoy beautiful weather that allows for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, snorkeling, and bird watching without any interruption.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palmyra Atoll

Do:

  • Abide by the environmental guidelines provided at the orientation.
  • Respect the wildlife and preserve the island’s natural resources.
  • Participate in volunteer conservation projects.

Don’t:

  • Take away any animals, plants or artifacts from the atoll.
  • Litter the beaches and other places on the island.
  • Damage coral reefs or abuse the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll is generally safe for visitors and crime-free. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as locking their tents or guesthouses, and keeping an eye out for waves or currents that can be dangerous in tropical climates.

Health in Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll has no medical facilities or pharmacies. Visitors should obtain appropriate health and travel insurance coverage before arrival.

Getting to Palmyra Atoll: Transportation Options and Logistics

Palmyra Atoll is only accessible by private boat or charter flights arranged through The Nature Conservancy. The atoll is about 1,000 miles south of Hawaii, and flights or boats are only available for authorized purposes.

Airports Near Palmyra Atoll

The closest airports to Palmyra Atoll are:

  • Honolulu International Airport, Hawaii
  • Lihue Airport, Kauai, Hawaii
  • Kona International Airport, Hawaii

Bus Stations Near Palmyra Atoll

As Palmyra Atoll can be accessed only via the sea, bus stations are irrelevant.

Other Options

Private yachts are also permitted to visit the atoll, but they must have the necessary permits and approval from authorities in advance.

How to Get Around in Palmyra Atoll

Palmyra Atoll is a small island, and walking is the easiest way to get around. Bicycles are also available to rent by visitors or for local staff purposes.

Where to Go Next from Palmyra Atoll: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palmyra Atoll is a secluded island in the Pacific Ocean, and its location allows visitors to explore other islands in the region via boats or air transport. Some of the best nearby destinations include Papeete, the capital of French Polynesia, and Honolulu, the capital of Hawaii.

Conclusion

Palmyra Atoll is a slice of paradise in the middle of the Pacific Ocean. Its natural beauty and outdoor recreational activities are unmatched, and visitors can enjoy experiencing it all in a conservation-driven environment. This comprehensive tour guide provides visitors to the atoll with all the information needed to plan their trip of a lifetime.
