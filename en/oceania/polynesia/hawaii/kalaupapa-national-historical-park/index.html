Introduction to Kalaupapa National Historical Park

Located on the island of Molokai in Hawaii, Kalaupapa National Historical Park is a significant cultural and natural attraction that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the island’s history and natural beauty. The park is home to significant historical sites, including the world’s most extensive leprosy settlement, churches, cemeteries, museums, and scenic landscapes. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know about Kalaupapa National Historical Park, from where to stay to what to do and see, to help you plan your visit to this mesmerizing destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kalaupapa National Historical Park

Kalaupapa National Historical Park is rich in cultural and natural attractions, and there is so much to see and explore in the park. Below is a list of must-visit places in the park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kalaupapa Peninsula: The most renowned areas of the park, Kalaupapa Peninsula, was once a leprosy colony in the 19th century and the home of St. Damien and Marianne Cope.
  • Kalaupapa Overlook: A stunning view of the Kalaupapa peninsula and the surrounding sea cliffs.
  • St. Philomena Catholic Church: Constructed in 1872, it is the oldest Catholic Church in Hawaii and was a prominent feature of the leprosy settlement.
  • Kalaupapa Memorial: The memorial is dedicated to the many people who lived at the leper colony and perished from the disease.
  • Father Damien’s Grave: Father Damien, also known as Saint Damien of Molokai, is the most prominent person associated with the leper colony. He cared for the people living with the disease and eventually contracted leprosy himself, passing away in 1889. The grave is a testament to his unwavering dedication to helping the afflicted.
  • Molokai Museum & Cultural Center: This museum contains various exhibits that showcase the history, culture, and traditions of the Hawaiian people.

Natural Attractions of Kalaupapa National Historical Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kalaupapa Trail: The most popular hiking choice in the park, the trail offers stunning views and is a moderately challenging hike that runs 3.5 miles down the cliffs to the peninsula.
  • Makanalua Peninsula: A lush green peninsula on the Kalawao side of the park, the Makanalua Peninsula is home to amazing wildlife and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Phallic Rock: A unique natural attraction on the island of Molokai, this phallic-shaped rock is said to promotes fertility.
  • Pelekunu Valley: Located on the Kalawao side of the park, Pelekunu Valley features another picturesque valley with waterfalls and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalaupapa National Historical Park

Kalaupapa National Historical Park is a hiker’s paradise, with many trails and hikes of varying difficulties to choose from. Some of the more popular hikes include:

  • Kalaupapa Trail: A moderate hike down the cliffs to the peninsula, the trail is 3.5 miles long and is a popular option for visitors.
  • Palaau State Park: This state park has several hiking trails, including the Kalaupapa Lookout Trail, which offers stunning views of the peninsula.
  • Halawa Valley: The Valley has several hikes, each with different difficulty levels, with the most popular hike being the Halawa Falls Trail, a 2.5-mile round trip hike to the waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Kalaupapa National Historical Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, there are other activities and tours to engage in in Kalaupapa National Historical Park. Some of them include:

  • Mule Ride: A popular way to explore the park, the mule ride begins at the top of the cliffs and descends to the peninsula, giving riders the opportunity to enjoy breathtaking views and get a taste of the old way of life for the leper patients who were once transported this way.
  • Whale Watching: Kalaupapa National Historical Park’s proximity to the ocean puts visitors in an ideal position to spot humpback whales and enjoy their playful antics.
  • Photography: The park’s stunning natural beauty and rich history make it an ideal location for photography enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Kalaupapa National Historical Park

Discovering the local food and drinks is an integral part of any travel experience, and Kalaupapa National Historical Park in Hawaii is no exception. Below is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy on your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poke: A traditional Hawaiian dish consisting of raw fish, usually tuna or shrimp combined with fresh vegetables such as onion and cucumber served with seaweed and rice.
  • Kalua Pork: A slow-cooked, shredded, and seasoned pork dish that has been the staple of Hawaiian cuisine for generations.
  • Laulau: A dish made from pork, fish, or chicken with a mix of vegetables then wrapped in a taro leaf and steamed.

Local Beverages

  • Mai Tais: A traditional Polynesian drink that features rum, curacao, lime juice, and orgeat, garnished with tropical fruit and served over ice.
  • Hawaiian Sun: A locally produced juice made from tropical fruits such as guava, passion fruit, mango, and pineapple.
  • Kona Coffee: A famous Hawaiian coffee that is rich, aromatic, and highly prized the world over.

Where to Stay in Kalaupapa National Historical Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kalaupapa National Historical Park has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets, from luxurious resorts to affordable guesthouses and hostels. Below is a list of some of the most popular and recommended accommodations in the park.

  • Molokai Shores Condominiums: A luxurious accommodation option located right on the ocean, boasting spectacular views, and offering an abundance of amenities.
  • Paniolo Hale: A family-friendly, affordable accommodation option located just a 15-minute drive from the park.
  • Aloha Vacation Cottages: A unique accommodation option offering standalone cottages complete with full kitchens and other modern amenities.

What to Buy in Kalaupapa National Historical Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

A visit to Kalaupapa National Historical Park isn’t complete without bringing home some souvenirs and local products. Here are some ideas:

  • Koa Wood Products: Koa Wood is native to Hawaii and is a prized timber used to make various products such as bowls, lamps, furniture, and other decorative items.
  • Shell Jewelry: Hawaii has some of the world’s most beautiful shells, and local artisans use them to create stunning, one-of-a-kind jewelry pieces.
  • Local Artwork: Hawaii is home to a thriving artist community, and visitors can find unique pieces of artwork that celebrate the island’s vibrant culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalaupapa National Historical Park

The culture and customs of Hawaii are different from the rest of the United States, and it’s important to respect and uphold them during your visit to Kalaupapa National Historical Park. Below are some cultural customs to be mindful of:

  • Aloha Spirit: A renowned Hawaiian custom that emphasizes treating one another with respect and kindness and living in harmony with the people and the land.
  • Mālama ʻĀina: Mālama ʻĀina, which means “to care for the land,” is a phrase that embodies the Hawaiian spirit of environmental consciousness and preservation.
  • Island Time: Locals in Hawaii don’t rush, and it’s customary to take life at a slower pace than in other parts of the world.

Best Time to Visit Kalaupapa National Historical Park

The best time to visit Kalaupapa National Historical Park is between May and September when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the island is bustling with activity. Visiting during this time also means you’ll avoid the heavy crowds that come with the winter and summer peak tourism season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalaupapa National Historical Park

To ensure that your visit to Kalaupapa National Historical Park goes smoothly and respect the local culture and environment, it’s important to adhere to the do’s and don’ts of the park.

Do:

  • Be Respectful: Always treat the locals with respect and kindness, and be mindful of cultural customs and traditions.
  • Pack Properly: Pack light, breathable clothing and comfortable shoes for outdoor activities such as hiking.
  • Stay Hydrated: Due to the park’s warm climate, staying hydrated is essential for health.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Littering is not only disrespectful to the park’s environment, but it is also illegal.
  • Disturb the Wildlife: Hawaii is home to several unique plant and animal species, and it is essential to respect their home and avoid any disturbance.
  • Take Anything from the Beaches: Hawaii’s beaches are protected, and the park’s conservation efforts prohibit visitors from taking anything from the beaches.

Safety and Health

Visiting Kalaupapa National Historical Park requires some preparation and adherence to safety guidelines to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Kalaupapa National Historical Park:

  • Hiking Safety: Always stay on designated hiking trails, and carry a map and plenty of water.
  • Ocean Safety: Avoid swimming in the ocean during storms, and always obey lifeguard instructions.
  • Water Safety: The park’s water sources are prone to contamination, so it’s essential to pack enough bottled water.

Health in Kalaupapa National Historical Park:

  • Insect Repellent: Hawaii is home to several insects that can cause illness, so it’s essential to pack insect repellent.
  • Sunscreen: Protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays by packing and regularly applying sunscreen.

Getting to Kalaupapa National Historical Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kalaupapa National Historical Park is located on the Kalaupapa Peninsula, and transportation to the park is somewhat limited.

Airports Near Kalaupapa National Historical Park:

  • Molokai Airport: Located approximately 8 miles away, catching a flight to Molokai is the most common way of getting to Kalaupapa National Historical Park.

Bus Stations Near Kalaupapa National Historical Park:

  • Molokai Bus Service: The Molokai bus service only serves the main towns on the island and does not service the park itself.

Other Options:

  • Charter Flights: Visitors can also book a private charter flight to access the park.

How to Get Around in Kalaupapa National Historical Park

Getting around Kalaupapa National Historical Park requires some effort as there are no rental car agencies or public transportation available in the park. Visitors can explore the park on foot or rent a bike, with the latter being the most popular option.

Where to Go Next from Kalaupapa National Historical Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kalaupapa National Historical Park is not just a destination in itself. It’s also an excellent base for exploring the nearby attractions and destinations, including:

  • Halawa Valley: A historic valley that is part of Molokai’s cultural heritage, famous for its stunning waterfalls and lush greenery.
  • Kamakou Preserve: Featuring the highest peak on the island of Molokai, the preserve is home to several rare and endangered plants and animals.
  • Papohaku Beach: Located on the west side of the island, Papohaku is one of the longest and widest white sand beaches in Hawaii.

Conclusion

Kalaupapa National Historical Park in Hawaii is an excellent destination for anyone looking for an immersive cultural and natural experience. The park offers visitors an opportunity to explore Hawaii’s rich history, hike breathtaking trails, and enjoy unique food and drinks. By following this guide, you’ll be well prepared to explore Kalaupapa National Historical Park and take in all it has to offer.
