Paia is a picturesque town located in Maui, Hawaii. Its charm lies in its laid-back vibe, vibrant arts scene, and stunning natural landscapes. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this travel guide will help you explore all that Paia has to offer. From culturally significant museums and mesmerizing outdoor adventures to local cuisine and accommodations, read on for a comprehensive tour guide to Paia.

Introduction to Paia

Paia is a town situated on Maui’s north coast and is known for its eclectic mix of shops, restaurants, and art galleries. Once a bustling plantation town, it’s now a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the world. Surrounded by lush vegetation and stunning views of the ocean, Paia is a great destination for travelers who love outdoor adventures and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Paia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alexandra & Baldwin Sugar Museum: This museum chronicles the history of sugar production in Hawaii and its impact on the island’s economy and culture. It’s a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of Maui.
  • Paia Contemporary Gallery: Featuring contemporary art from local and international artists, this gallery is a great place to explore the vibrant arts scene of Paia.
  • Paia Town Association: This is a community-based non-profit organization that promotes the preservation of Paia’s historic architecture and culture.

Natural Attractions of Paia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ho’okipa Beach Park: A famous windsurfing destination, Ho’okipa Beach Park is also great for swimming, surfing, and watching sea turtles.
  • Baldwin Beach Park: With two miles of white sand beaches, Baldwin Beach Park is a popular spot for sunbathing and swimming.
  • Paia Bay: A secluded beach north of Paia town, Paia Bay is great for surfing, kiteboarding, and windsurfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paia

  • Makawao Forest Reserve: Hike or bike through the forest reserve’s trails, which feature stunning views of the island.
  • Haleakala National Park: A star-filled night sky and breathtaking sunrise await at Haleakala National Park. Hiking trails include the Pipiwai Trail and Pali Trail.

Best Things to Do in Paia: Activities and Tours

  • Road to Hana: A famous road trip adventure in Maui, the Road to Hana winds through the island’s lush rainforests, hidden waterfalls, and scenic coastal views.
  • Paia Fish Market: A popular seafood restaurant in Paia that’s known for its fresh catch of the day.
  • Maui Pineapple Tours: Take a tour of the Maui Gold Pineapple farm and learn about the history of pineapple farming in Maui.

Food and Drink Scene of Paia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mahi-mahi: A popular dish in Maui, mahi-mahi is a type of fish that’s often grilled or served raw in poke bowls.
  • Kalua pork: Made by slow-roasting a whole pig in an underground oven, kalua pork is a traditional Hawaiian dish that’s rich in flavor.
  • Ahi poke: A popular raw fish salad that’s typically made with fresh ahi tuna, soy sauce, and sesame oil.

Local Beverages

  • Maui Brewing Company: A local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers, including its flagship Maui Wheat ale.
  • Maui Wine: Located in the Ulupalakua Ranch, Maui Wine is known for its award-winning pineapple wine and scenic vineyards.

Where to Stay in Paia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Paia Inn: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Paia town, The Paia Inn offers stylish rooms and exceptional service.
  • Maka Kai Oceanfront Cottage: This oceanfront cottage is perfect for couples or small groups, and it offers stunning views of the ocean.
  • Northshore Hostel Maui: If you’re a budget traveler, Northshore Hostel Maui offers affordable accommodations and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Paia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maui Gold Pineapple: Maui Gold Pineapple is known for its juicy and sweet pineapple, which you can buy fresh from the farm or as a gift pack.
  • Maui-made art and jewelry: Paia is home to a thriving arts scene, so be sure to check out the local galleries and shops for unique art and jewelry pieces.
  • Macadamia nuts: Another Hawaiian specialty, macadamia nuts can be found in various flavors and are great for bringing home as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Paia

Paia is a town that values sustainability and preservation of its natural resources. When visiting Paia, be sure to respect the environment and the cultural traditions of the community.

Best Time to Visit Paia

The best time to visit Paia is from April to June and September to November when the weather is mild, and crowds are smaller. The busiest time of year is from December to March when the weather is cooler, and whales can be seen from the shore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paia

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the local community.
  • Take care of the environment and practice sustainability.
  • Explore the natural scenery and outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter on the beach or in the town.
  • Ignore local signs and warnings.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paia

Paia is a safe town, but it’s always a good idea to practice caution and be aware of your surroundings. Be sure to lock your car and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Paia

Paia has a limited medical facility, so it’s advisable to bring any necessary medications and take precautions to prevent common tropical diseases.

Getting to Paia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paia

  • Kahului Airport: The closest airport to Paia, Kahului Airport is located about 15 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Paia

  • Queen Ka’ahumanu Center: A shopping center in Kahului that offers various bus connections to different parts of Maui.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Maui, and there are multiple car rental companies available at Kahului Airport. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available but be aware that prices can be high.

How to Get Around in Paia

Paia is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Biking is another great option for exploring the town and its nearby attractions. Car rentals and taxi services are also available for those who want more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Paia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kahului: A vibrant city located only 15 minutes away by car, Kahului offers plenty of shopping and dining options.
  • Wailuku: A historic town that’s home to many ancient Hawaiian sites and the historic Ka’ahumanu Church.
  • Lahaina: A popular tourist destination located on the west coast of Maui, Lahaina is known for its picturesque beaches and rich history.

Conclusion

Paia is a charming town that offers an authentic Hawaiian experience. Whether you’re a culture lover or an outdoor enthusiast, Paia has something to offer everyone. From its breathtaking natural landscapes to its vibrant arts scene and local cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in Paia, Maui. Plan your perfect trip with this comprehensive guide and get ready to fall in love with this magical destination.
