Introduction to Makawao

Makawao is a beautiful town located in central Maui, Hawaii. The town is known for its charming rural atmosphere and is often referred to as the “cowboy town” of Maui. Makawao is a favorite destination for visitors seeking to experience the authentic Hawaiian culture. Visitors will find a warm community with lots of interesting cultural landmarks, stunning natural attractions, and outdoor activities. In this comprehensive guide, we will look at all the must-visit places in Makawao, where to stay, where to eat, and several other insider tips to make your trip to Makawao unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Makawao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haleakala National Park

Haleakala National Park is one of the top things to do in Makawao. Located about 10 miles from the town, the park offers breathtaking views of the island’s highest peak, Haleakala, which rises over 10,000 feet above sea level. The park has several hiking trails that take visitors through a range of landscapes from mountainous terrain to desert-like land.

Makawao History Museum

The Makawao History Museum is another must-visit destination for visitors who are interested in the town’s rich history. The museum has a collection of photos, artifacts, and historical documents that offer insight into the lives of the early settlers and their lifestyle.

Natural Attractions of Makawao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Makawao Forest Reserve

The Makawao Forest Reserve is a stunning 2,500-acre rainforest home to several exotic species of birds, flowers, and trees. Visitors can hike through the forest trails, past streams and waterfalls, and enjoy the unspoiled beauty of Maui’s nature.

Ho’okipa Beach Park

Ho’okipa Beach Park is famed for its world-class surfing, windsurfing, and kiteboarding. Visitors can watch surfers carve the giant waves and enjoy panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean from its cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Makawao

Hiking and biking are two of the most popular activities in Makawao. The town has several excellent trails that offer outstanding views of Hawaiian landscapes.

Polipoli Springs State Recreation Area

The Polipoli Springs State Recreation Area has some of Maui’s most beautiful hiking trails. Visitors can access the park’s various trails from Kula. The park has trails of varying lengths and difficulty levels, offering something for everyone.

Best Things to Do in Makawao: Activities and Tours

Helicopter Tours

Visitors can take a helicopter tour of Makawao for a bird’s eye view of the stunning natural attractions. The helicopter tours take visitors over the island’s highest peak and show the spectacular waterfalls that are hidden in the lush rainforest.

Horseback Riding

Horseback riding is a popular activity in Makawao and offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the Hawaiian countryside. There are several tour operators who offer guided tours through the trails surrounding the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Makawao

Day 1: Arrive in Makawao and explore the town’s vibrant atmosphere and shops.

Day 2: Hike the trails in Polipoli Springs State Recreation Area.

Day 3: Take a helicopter tour of Makawao and marvel at the stunning natural attractions.

Day 4: Go horseback riding and experience a slower pace while exploring the Hawaiian countryside.

Day 5: Enjoy some beach time at nearby Ho’okipa Beach Park.

Day 6: Visit the Haleakala National Park and hike the trails through its diverse natural attractions.

Day 7: Experience the town’s nightlife and sample some of its local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Makawao

Must-Try Dishes

Maui-Style Poke

Maui-style poke is a traditional Hawaiian dish made of raw fish seasoned with soy sauce, sea salt, sesame oil, and seaweed. Visitors can find the best Maui-style poke at the town’s local markets and specialty food stores.

Malasadas

Malasadas are a type of Portuguese donut, and Makawao is home to several bakeries that offer the best malasadas on Maui.

Local Beverages

Maui Brewing Company

The Maui Brewing Company is a celebrated local brewery that supplies craft beer across the island. Visitors can take a brewery tour or enjoy their drinks in one of Maui’s bars or restaurants.

Where to Stay in Makawao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Makawao has several options for visitors who want to spend a night or two in town.

Maui Sunseeker LGBT Resort

The Maui Sunseeker LGBT Resort is a luxurious hotel with modern amenities. The hotel has spacious rooms with private balconies and patios offering breathtaking views.

Hale Ho’okipa Inn Makawao

Hale Ho’okipa Inn Makawao is a guesthouse that blends Hawaiian charm with contemporary luxury. The inn has elegantly decorated rooms with cozy fireplaces and spacious private patios.

What to Buy in Makawao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Polynesia at Home

Polynesia at Home is a local store that offers authentic Polynesian crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and clothing. Visitors can take home unique souvenirs that showcase Hawaii’s rich culture and heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Makawao

Visitors to Makawao should be respectful of the local customs, particularly in religious and cultural contexts. Visitors should also be aware of the importance of the Hawaiian language, which is an official language in the state.

Best Time to Visit Makawao

The best time to visit Makawao is from September to November and from March to May when the weather is mild and prices are low. The winter months are generally considered the high season, but visitors can still find great deals on accommodations and flights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Makawao

Do:

  • Do learn some Hawaiian phrases and words like “mahalo” (thank you) and “aloha” (hello/goodbye) as locals appreciate it.
  • Do respect the local customs and culture of Makawao.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash at the public beaches or parks in Makawao.
  • Don’t touch the marine life when snorkeling or diving in Makawao’s waters.

Safety and Health

Safety in Makawao

Makawao is a generally safe town, but visitors should take precautions by following the standard safety measures like locking doors and windows when staying in their accommodation and being cautious when handling valuables in public areas.

Health in Makawao

Visitors should carry adequate sun protection when exploring the outdoors in Makawao. They should also have mosquito repellent lotion to prevent mosquito bites. Visitors should also consider carrying medications for motion sickness if they take helicopter tours.

Getting to Makawao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Makawao

Visitors to Makawao can arrive at Kahului Airport, located around 10 miles away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Makawao

Public buses are available to take visitors from Kahului Bus Station to Makawao Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange private transportation services to take them to Makawao or rent cars locally.

How to Get Around in Makawao

Visitors can rent cars or bikes locally to explore Makawao and the surrounding areas. Guests can also use taxis or shuttle services to navigate the town.

Where to Go Next from Makawao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several popular destinations near Makawao that visitors can explore. Maui’s beaches are often a favorite, and visitors can try traveling to nearby towns such as Lahaina and Kihei.

Conclusion

Makawao has a lot to offer visitors, from stunning natural attractions to rich Hawaiian history and culture. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip to Makawao, Maui, and explore the beautiful island’s diverse landscape and unique culture.
