Introduction to Kihei

Located in the beautiful island of Maui, Kihei is a small beach town that offers some of the most breathtaking views of Hawaii. With its crystal-clear waters, long stretches of white sand, and tropical landscapes, Kihei attracts both adventurous travelers and sun seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Kihei

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bailey House Museum

Originally built in 1833 and once the home of a missionary family, this museum showcases Maui’s history through a diverse range of exhibits.

Pi’ilanihale Heiau

A significant cultural site of ancient Hawaii, this place is a mysterious and awe-inspiring place that’s been reverently preserved by Maui’s people.

Natural Attractions of Kihei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Haleakala National Park

Haleakala National Park is one of Maui’s must-visit attractions, known for its otherworldly sunrises and sunsets, scenic walking and hiking trails, stunning natural formations and fauna.

Maui Ocean Center

This popular attraction showcases the marine life of Hawaii in a way that’s educational and engaging. Visitors can learn about Hawaiian sea creatures such as green sea turtles, sharks and tropical fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kihei

Hoapili Trail

This challenging route takes you to South Maui’s coast, through ancient Hawaiian settlements, lava fields, and gorgeous bird breeding sites.

Kealia Pond National Wildlife Refuge

This flat route is perfect for both biking and hiking and offers an opportunity to enjoy Maui’s natural beauty. It’s an ideal spot for bird watching, with thousands of waterbirds visiting the pond each year.

Best Things to Do in Kihei: Activities and Tours

Snorkeling and Scuba Diving

Maui is renowned for its marine life and there’s no better way to experience this than by snorkeling. You can see many species of fish, turtles, eels, dolphins, and even whales.

Road to Hana Tour

Experience the natural beauty of Maui by taking the famous Road to Hana drive, which is a 52-mile drive through rainforests, waterfalls, and stunning sceneries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kihei

Day Activity
1 Visit Haleakala National Park and take in a sunrise
2 Take a Waterfall and Rainforest hike in East Maui
3 Relax on the beach in Kihei and go snorkeling
4 Drive to Lahaina and experience the historic whaling town
5 Rent a bike and cycle along the Hoapili Trail
6 Take a Road to Hana Tour
7 Experience the marine life at the Maui Ocean Center and enjoy night paddleboarding

Food and Drink Scene of Kihei

Must-Try Dishes

Shave Ice

A Hawaiian classic, this shaved ice treat is a must-try for anyone visiting Kihei. It comes in a variety of flavors, from fruit to sweet and savory.

Poke Bowl

A delicious traditional dish made of marinated ahi tuna, onions, seaweed, and soy sauce, the poke bowl is perfect for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

Mai Tai

The Mai Tai is a classic Hawaii drink that contains rum, Curaçao liqueur, lime juice and orgeat almond syrup. Enjoy this refreshing drink on the beach.

Kona Coffee

Hawaii is home to some of the world’s finest coffee, and Kihei is not an exception. Enjoy a cup of Kona coffee as you relax on a beautiful Maui morning.

Where to Stay in Kihei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maui Coast Hotel

The Maui Coast Hotel offers the perfect balance of luxury, affordability and convenience. Their facilities include a pool and an on-site restaurant.

Wailea Beach Villas

The Wailea Beach Villas offer spacious and luxurious villas with great ocean views and classy amenities, such as a private pool, spa, and fitness center.

What to Buy in Kihei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hawaiian Shirts

Colorful and stylish, Hawaiian shirts are a perfect souvenir to take back home. Whether you’re looking for patterns displaying hibiscus flowers or pineapple patterns, you’ll find lots of options in Kihei.

Macadamia Nuts

One of Hawaii’s most famous exports, macadamia nuts are used in many local dishes and baked goods. Buy them raw, roasted or chocolate-coated to take home for your friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Kihei

Maui is known for its laid-back atmosphere and friendly people. Respect the local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering someone’s home, being mindful of where you sit while attending a cultural event, and avoid littering.

Best Time to Visit Kihei

The best time to visit Kihei is during the low season, which is from April to May or September to November. This time of year offers low prices and warm temperatures without the crowds that can be in the high season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kihei

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment.
  • Learn a few Hawaiian words and phrases.
  • Take the scenic routes and drives around Kihei, as there’s so much to see!

Don’t:

  • Swim in the ocean without paying attention to posted signage and warning flags.
  • Forget to apply sunscreen frequently.
  • Ignore the culture and traditions of the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kihei

Although Kihei is generally a safe town, it’s important to take certain safety precautions while traveling. Avoid leaving valuables in parked cars, and never leave windows or doors unlocked.

Health in Kihei

While the tap water in Kihei is safe to drink, it’s important to observe proper food hygiene and to avoid overexposure to UV rays.

Getting to Kihei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kihei

The closest airport to Kihei is the Kahului Airport, which is about 20 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kihei

Kihei is located on the south shore of Maui and is served by the Maui County Bus which runs from Kahului to Kihei.

Other Options

Many visitors rent cars and drive around the island to reach different destinations, as this offers the most flexibility and freedom.

How to Get Around Kihei

A car rental remains the most convenient option to really experience Kihei, but biking or walking is also an option for those who want a more adventurous trip.

Where to Go Next from Kihei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lahaina

Located just 18 miles from Kihei, Lahaina is a historic whaling town that’s now a popular tourist attraction, offering museums, shops, galleries and restaurants.

Hana

Hana is a small town located on the other side of Maui and can only be reached via the famous Road to Hana drive. It is known for its beautiful waterfalls and black sand beaches.

Conclusion

Kihei, Maui offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, watersports, outdoor activities and more. This travel guide has covered every aspect you need to know before booking your next flight, making sure you can make the most of your visit to Kihei.
