Introduction to Honokaa

Honokaa is a small town located on the northern coast of Big Island, Hawaii, popularly known for its plantation-era charm and lush surroundings. This quaint town with a population of around 3,800 people attracts travelers from all over the world with its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking beauty. Honokaa’s undulating landscapes dotted with verdant fields, dense rainforests, cascading waterfalls, and sprawling beaches provide visitors with endless opportunities for relaxation, adventure, and exploration.

List of Places to Visit in Honokaa

Culturally Important Attractions

Akiko’s Buddhist Bed and Breakfast

Akiko’s Buddhist Bed and Breakfast is more than just a place to stay; it is a refuge for the mind and soul. This charming retreat is nestled amidst the lush vegetation of Honokaa, providing an ideal setting for a peaceful and rejuvenating getaway. The B&B is adorned with handcrafted Buddhas, offering an ambiance of tranquility and serenity.

Waipio Valley Lookout

Waipio Valley Lookout is a spectacular scenic point that offers an awe-inspiring view of the verdant valley and the sapphire-blue sea beyond. Located at the end of a winding road, the lookout provides a perfect place to witness the sunrise and sunset.

Natural Attractions of Honokaa

Akaka Falls State Park

Akaka Falls is one of the most mesmerizing waterfalls in Hawaii, located just a few miles from Honokaa. The waterfall plunges from a height of 422 feet and cascades into a pool beneath, creating a magnificent sight. Visitors can take a hike through a lush rainforest park that surrounds the falls, featuring a range of endemic flora and fauna.

Honokaa-Waipio Road Scenic Drive

The Honokaa-Waipio Road Scenic Drive is a 10.5-mile picturesque route that leads to one of the island’s most stunning valleys, Waipio Valley. The scenic drive offers spectacular views of rolling hills, lush vegetation, and stunning waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Honokaa

Waipio Valley

The Waipio Valley hike is a treasured adventure for nature lovers. With its lush greenery and a waterfall at the end, the hike is an exciting opportunity to explore Hawaii’s natural beauty. Adventurers also get to see rare plant and bird species in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Honokaa: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding at Na’alapa

Na’alapa is an authentic, family-operated ranch offering a unique horseback riding experience for all levels of riders. The guided tour takes you through the striking and diverse Hawaii terrains, showcasing beautiful beaches, lush forests, and historical landmarks.

Mauna Kea Observatories Tour

Mauna Kea Observatories are the world’s most significant observing site for astronomical discovery. With its towering telescopes, the observatory offers a fascinating, informative tour that includes the history of astronomy, engineering, and astronomy fundamentals.

Food and Drink Scene of Honokaa

Must-Try Dishes

Loco Moco

Loco Moco is a popular Hawaiian dish that features a combination of steamy rice, gravy, and a meat patty served with fried eggs. This dish is often served in the local diners and is perfect for breakfast.

Poke

Poke is a Hawaiian cuisine that features raw fish, marinated and served with different vegetable toppings and sauces. This dish is widely available and can be found in many restaurants.

Local Beverages

Kona Coffee

Kona coffee is world-renowned coffee grown on the slopes of Mauna Kea, featuring a unique taste compared to the other coffees around the world. Visitors can sample freshly roasted Kona coffee in many stores around Honokaa.

Where to Stay in Honokaa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Honoka’a Backpackers Hostel

Honoka’a Backpackers Hostel is a budget-friendly accommodation option and is ideal for backpackers and travelers looking for a simple and straightforward base to explore Honokaa and surrounding areas.

Hawaii Island Retreat

Hawaii Island Retreat is a luxury spa and wellness resort that offers an unrivaled experience of comfort and relaxation. The retreat offers guests an all-inclusive experience, including rooms, meals, therapies, and yoga classes.

What to Buy in Honokaa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hamakua Honey

Hamakua Honey is an aromatic and all-natural local honey that is a favorite among locals and visitors alike. The honey is harvested atop the lush mountains of Honokaa and is available in many stores and local farmers’ markets in the area.

Koa Wood Products

Koa Wood Products are delicate art pieces made from the local timber, Koa. The wood is known for its natural beauty and has a myriad of colors and patterns, making it a standout souvenir to take home from Honokaa.

Local Culture and Customs of Honokaa

Honokaa is a melting pot of Polynesian and western cultures, which has influenced the town’s customs and traditions. Respect for the culture and lifestyle of the locals is crucial, and visitors should be mindful of their actions. Respect the local customs such as removing your shoes before entering a house and the traditional language, Hawaiian.

Best Time to Visit Honokaa

Honokaa has a tropical climate, and the best time to visit is during the winter season, from November to April, when there are fewer crowds, and the weather is pleasant and mild. Summer months can be sweltering and humid, with more crowds as schools break for summer vacations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Honokaa

Do:

  • Respect the culture and language of the locals.
  • Carry sunscreen and mosquito repellent while venturing outside.
  • Take time to explore the history-rich museums and cultural centers while in the town.

Don’t:

  • Harm or remove the natural flora and fauna.
  • Drive on private property, even for scenic off-road adventures.
  • Disrespect or ignore the “No Trespassing” signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Honokaa

Honokaa is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. Visitors should practice the standard safety protocols and secure their valuable items. Inexperienced hikers should avoid venturing alone, and surfing or swimming should be done under the supervision of trained professionals.

Health in Honokaa

Hawaii has a tropical climate, and visitors should take adequate precautions against mosquito bites and Dengue fever. Visitors should carry sunscreen and keep themselves hydrated to avoid any heat-related illnesses.

Getting to Honokaa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Honokaa

The nearest airport to Honokaa is Hilo International Airport, located approximately 38 miles away. Visitors can fly domestically and internationally from this airport and rent a car or hire a shuttle service to reach Honokaa.

Bus Stations Near Honokaa

Island of Hawaii’s public transportation system is limited, and there are no bus services that connect Honokaa to the rest of the towns on the island. Visitors can take a shuttle service or rent a car from the airport to get to Honokaa.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to get around Honokaa and explore the nearby towns and attractions. Visitors can also opt for private taxis or ride-sharing services available from Hilo International Airport.

How to Get Around in Honokaa

The best way to get around Honokaa is by renting a car or hiring a taxi. Walking and cycling are also great options for exploring the town’s streets at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Honokaa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hilo

Hilo is a vibrant town that offers visitors with a mix of outdoor adventures and relaxed island vibes. The town features a range of museums, galleries, and delicious local cuisines.

Kona

Kona is one of the most popular towns located on the west coast of Big Island, offering scenic beach views, world-class accommodations, and adventures for the whole family.

Conclusion

Honokaa is a town that is worth exploring, the natural and cultural beauty of the place is awe-inspiring. With its scenic wonders, exciting adventures, cultural attractions, and stunning views, Honokaa has something for everyone. Visitors will have unforgettable experiences that will make them feel captivated and in awe of the town’s elegance for years to come.
