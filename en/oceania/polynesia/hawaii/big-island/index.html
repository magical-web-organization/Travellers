Introduction

Welcome to Big Island, Hawaii! As the largest island in the Hawaii archipelago, Big Island is home to diverse landscapes and cultural attractions that attract visitors from all over the world. When you visit Big Island, Hawaii, you can expect to experience the warm tropical climate, stunning beaches, powerful volcanoes, and pristine ecosystems.

List of Places to Visit in Big Island

Big Island is a fantastic destination that offers plenty of opportunities for adventure, historical exploration, food, and culture.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, you will want to explore Big Island’s important cultural sites, including ‘Imiloa Astronomy Center and the Big Island Museum. Also, don’t miss the Hawaii Volcanoes National Park, home to some of the most active volcanoes on Earth.

Natural Attractions of Big Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Big Island is an absolute paradise. Take a trek through the pristine landscapes of Waipio Valley, have a relaxing soak in the natural hot springs in Ahalanui Park, or go for a swim at Hapuna Beach State Park. For a taste of adventure, brave the zipline courses, rappelling off cliffs or take a helicopter tour of the entire island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Island

With so many fantastic natural attractions, Big Island is a prime location for hiking and biking. With opportunities ranging from the barren landscapes of Kohala Mountain to the meandering historical trails of Kealakekua Bay, there is no end to the hiking and biking routes in Big Island, Hawaii.

Best Things to Do in Big Island: Activities and Tours

As a tropical gem, Big Island offers a whole world of amazing activities and tours. You can visit the Kahua Ranch, shooting range, sunset tours, guided snorkelling trips, Zodiac boat tours, stand-up paddleboarding, or make friends with the very friendly dolphins at the Hilton Dolphin Quest.

Week-Long Itinerary for Big Island

To ensure you maximise your stay, follow our carefully crafted week-long itinerary for Big Island.

Day Activity
Day 1 Discover the rich history of Hilo Town.
Day 2 Explore the volcanoes at Hawaii Volcanoes National Park.
Day 3 Head to the beaches on the island’s western coast.
Day 4 Visit the beaches on the eastern coast of the island.
Day 5 Take a tour of the beautiful Kohala Coast.
Day 6 Go snorkelling and scuba diving with the sea creatures in Kailua-Kona.
Day 7 Hike in the beautiful Waipio Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Big Island

As part of Hawaii, Big Island is known for its food and drink scene, characterised by tropical flavours and refreshing drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

For a truly authentic Hawaiian experience, try the poke bowls, Kalua Pig, Lomi-Lomi (salmon salad), or Spam musubi–rice with Spam fried in teriyaki sauce all wrapped up in seaweed.

Local Beverages

The coffee produced on the island’s farms is world-renowned. You should also taste the handcrafted beers by the Kona Brewing Company or the offerings from Hawaii Island’s Distillery.

Where to Stay in Big Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Big Island has an array of accommodation options, offering everything from rustic, eco-friendly lodges to luxurious beachside resorts. You can find the perfect accommodation for your taste, budget and adventure inclination.

What to Buy in Big Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Big Island offers visitors a wide range of great souvenirs to take back home. From handcrafted jewellery originating from the island’s black sand beaches to the famous Kona Coffee, you won’t lack for a unique and memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Island

Hawaii has blessed Big Island with a unique array of customs, traditions and languages that give it a rich culture. The residents of Hawaii have a deep respect for the land, the sea and their ancestors. Your visit is the perfect opportunity to explore the unique culture of Big Island and create unforgettable memories.

Best Time to Visit Big Island

Big Island is a year-round destination, with a warm tropical climate. However, the best time to visit is between December and April; these months offer the lowest precipitation and crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Island

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Protect the environment and natural resources.
  • Stay safe and follow local safety protocols.
  • Explore the island and try new experiences.

Don’t:

  • Do not congregate or attempt to touch the native wildlife.
  • Do not remove native plants or animals from protected areas.
  • Do not litter or leave trash on the beaches or in the streets.

Safety and Health

When visiting Big Island, your safety is of utmost importance. You should be aware of the following tips:

Safety in Big Island

  • Listen to weather warnings, and follow evacuation orders in case of natural disasters.
  • Be alert to ocean conditions and only swim at beaches with lifeguards.
  • Drive carefully on narrow and winding roads with steep drop-offs.

Health in Big Island

  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated while spending time outdoors.
  • Be careful while hiking or spending time outside to avoid insect bites and stings.
  • Consult a physician to get updated on malaria and other tropical disease vaccinations.

Getting to Big Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Big Island is accessible through several means of transportation, including air, sea, and land.

Airports Near Big Island

The Kona International Airport is the largest and most well-known airport in Big Island, serving both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Big Island

Big Island’s bus station is located in Hilo and offers transportation via lorry to other towns and attractions on the island.

Other Options

The island is also accessible through the state’s ferry system, which offers an affordable and picturesque option for visitors who want to avoid air and road transportation.

How to Get Around in Big Island

The primary modes of transportation on the island include car rentals and buses. Taxis and Ubers are also available, but fares can be a bit pricey.

Where to Go Next from Big Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

As a part of the Hawaii archipelago, Big Island offers visitors the opportunity to explore other fantastic destinations nearby, including Maui, Oahu, and Kauai.

Conclusion

Plan your trip carefully, so you can experience all the wonder and adventure that Big Island has to offer. With stunning landscapes, a rich culture, and plenty of exciting activities, you are sure to have an unforgettable visit to this tropical paradise.
