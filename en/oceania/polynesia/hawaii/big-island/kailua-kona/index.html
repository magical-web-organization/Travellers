Introduction to Kailua-Kona

Located on the Big Island in Hawaii, Kailua-Kona is a charming seaside town with a rich history and stunning natural surroundings. The town is situated on the west coast of the island, which is known for its sunny weather, beautiful beaches, and world-class snorkeling and diving opportunities. If you’re looking for a relaxing vacation in Hawaii, Kailua-Kona should definitely be on your list!

List of Places to Visit in Kailua-Kona

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hulihe‘e Palace: This historic royal residence is a must-visit for anyone interested in Hawaiian history and culture. The palace was built in 1838 and was once the summer home of Hawaiian royalty.
  • Mokuaikaua Church: This beautiful church was built in 1837 and is considered the oldest Christian church in Hawaii.
  • Kailua Village Artists Galleries: This local art gallery showcases the work of talented artists from around the island, including contemporary and traditional Hawaiian art.

Natural Attractions of Kailua-Kona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kona Coffee Living History Farm: This working coffee farm and museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the history of coffee production in Hawaii.
  • Kaloko-Honokohau National Historical Park: This stunning natural area comprises 1,160 acres of pristine coastline, rugged lava fields, and ancient Hawaiian archaeological sites.
  • Manta Ray Night Snorkel: Take an unforgettable night snorkeling tour to see the majestic manta rays in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kailua-Kona

  • Kahalu’u Bay: This popular beach is the perfect spot for a leisurely hike or bike ride along the scenic coastline.
  • Kealakekua Bay: This historic bay is home to Captain Cook’s Monument, and a hike to the top offers sweeping views of the coast and surrounding countryside.
  • Greenwell Farms Coffee Plantation: Take a guided tour of this working coffee farm, and enjoy a hike through the lush tropical forest that surrounds it.

Best Things to Do in Kailua-Kona: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Kailua-Kona is known for its crystal-clear waters and abundance of marine life, making it a popular destination for snorkeling and diving.
  • Helicopter Tour: Take a thrilling helicopter tour over the island of Hawaii, and see the stunning landscape from a whole new perspective.
  • Sunset Sail: Watch the sunset from the deck of a sailboat, and enjoy a romantic evening on the water with your loved one.

Food and Drink Scene of Kailua-Kona

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poke: This traditional Hawaiian dish features chunks of raw fish marinated in soy sauce, sesame oil, and other flavorful ingredients.
  • Kona Coffee: The coffee grown in Kona is some of the best in the world, and you can sample it at one of the many local coffee shops and cafes.
  • Kalua Pork: This slow-roasted pork dish is a staple of Hawaiian cuisine, and it’s a must-try when visiting Kailua-Kona.

Local Beverages

  • Mai Tai: This classic tropical cocktail features rum, lime juice, orange curacao, and almond syrup, and it’s the perfect drink to enjoy on a hot day.
  • Kona Brewing Company Beer: This local brewery offers a variety of delicious craft beers, including their signature Longboard Lager and Koko Brown Ale.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is the perfect refreshing beverage on a hot day, and you can find it at many local markets and roadside stands.

Where to Stay in Kailua-Kona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Royal Kona Resort: This oceanfront resort features stunning views of the coastline, spacious rooms, and an on-site restaurant and spa.
  • Kona Seaside Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is just steps from the beach and features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and an outdoor pool.
  • Kona Bayview Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Kailua-Kona and offers cozy rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Kailua-Kona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kona Coffee: Bring home a bag of freshly-roasted Kona coffee to savor the taste of Hawaii long after your trip is over.
  • Hawaiian Shirts and Dresses: The vibrant colors and traditional prints of Hawaiian clothing make for a perfect souvenir.
  • Macadamia Nuts: Hawaii is known for its delicious macadamia nuts, and you can find them in a variety of flavors and packages at local markets and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Kailua-Kona

Kailua-Kona is a welcoming and friendly town that celebrates its rich cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to learn about Hawaiian culture and history, and to respect the customs and beliefs of the local community.

Best Time to Visit Kailua-Kona

The best time to visit Kailua-Kona is in the months of April to June and September to December when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. During the peak summer months of July and August, the town can get quite crowded, and hotel prices tend to be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kailua-Kona

Do:

  • Respect Hawaiian culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Take time to explore the stunning natural surroundings

Don’t:

  • Remove rocks, shells, or other natural objects from the beaches or other natural areas
  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs
  • Litter or damage the natural environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Kailua-Kona

Kailua-Kona is a safe and friendly town, but like any tourist destination, visitors should take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. It’s a good idea to avoid leaving valuables in your car or unattended on the beach, and to be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Kailua-Kona

Kailua-Kona has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. If you need medical attention during your stay in Kailua-Kona, you can rest assured that you’ll be in good hands.

Getting to Kailua-Kona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kailua-Kona

  • Kona International Airport: This airport is located just 7 miles from Kailua-Kona, and it offers regular flights to and from destinations across Hawaii and the mainland United States.

Bus Stations Near Kailua-Kona

  • Kona Bayview Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is just a short walk from the Kona bus station, which offers regular service to destinations across the island.

Other Options

  • Renting a Car: Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to Kailua-Kona, as it allows for maximum flexibility and exploration of the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Kailua-Kona

Kailua-Kona is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who want to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from Kailua-Kona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hawaii Volcanoes National Park: This stunning national park is just a short drive from Kailua-Kona, and it offers visitors the chance to see some of the most breathtaking natural wonders in Hawaii.
  • Mauna Kea Observatory: Located on the summit of Mauna Kea, this world-renowned observatory offers visitors the chance to explore the stars like never before.
  • Waimea: This charming town is known for its rolling green hills, stunning waterfalls, and world-class hiking and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Kailua-Kona is a charming and welcoming town that’s perfect for anyone looking to experience the beauty and tradition of Hawaiian culture. With its stunning natural surroundings, delicious cuisine, and rich history, there’s something for everyone in Kailua-Kona. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your next vacation to this wonderful destination!
