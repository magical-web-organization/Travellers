Introduction to Kohala

Located in the Big Island of Hawaii, Kohala is one of the most beautiful and culturally rich destinations in Polynesia. Kohala is renowned for its stunning beaches, diverse flora and fauna, historical sites, and pristine landscapes. Home to several small towns and villages, Kohala offers a unique blend of indigenous and modern cultures, the essence of Polynesian hospitality, and a plethora of thrilling activities and experiences. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation, a romantic getaway, or an outdoor adventure, Kohala is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Kohala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kohala has several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the unique Polynesian culture and history. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Puukohola Heiau National Historic Site: One of the most significant historical sites in Kohala, Puukohola Heiau is a sacred temple built by King Kamehameha I in 1790. The temple is a testament to the power and influence of the Hawaiian monarchy.

  • Lapakahi State Historical Park: Lapakahi is a restored ancient Hawaiian fishing village that dates back to the 15th century. Visitors can explore the ruins, enjoy the stunning coastal views, and learn about the traditional Hawaiian fishing practices.

  • Kohala Historical Sites State Monument: This monument is home to several historical sites, including the Mo’okini Heiau (an ancient temple), the birthplace of King Kamehameha I, and the Kamehameha Akahi Aina Hanau monument.

Natural Attractions of Kohala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kohala is blessed with breathtaking natural beauty, from its pristine beaches and lush green forests to its stunning waterfalls and rocky cliffs. Some top natural attractions include:

  • Pololu Valley Lookout: Situated at the end of Highway 270, the Pololu Valley Lookout offers panoramic views of the dramatic sea cliffs, black sand beach, and the lush green valley.

  • Akaka Falls State Park: This park is home to two magnificent waterfalls, the 100-foot Kahuna Falls and the 442-foot Akaka Falls. Visitors can stroll through the lush rainforest and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Mauna Kea Summit: Mauna Kea is the highest peak in Hawaii, and visitors can drive up to the summit to witness some of the most spectacular views of the night sky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kohala

Kohala offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that cater to different difficulty levels and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • Waipio Valley: This stunning hiking trail takes you through a lush tropical forest to the beautiful black sand beach at the bottom of the valley.

  • Puako Petroglyphs: This easy hike takes you through a rugged terrain filled with ancient petroglyphs that depict the stories of the ancient Hawaiians.

  • Kohala Waterfall Adventure: This thrilling ATV tour takes you through the lush forest to several hidden waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Kohala: Activities and Tours

Kohala offers a plethora of activities and tours that are perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers. Some top picks include:

  • Dolphin and Whale Watching: Kohala is home to several species of dolphins and whales, and visitors can enjoy a magical experience of watching these fascinating creatures in their natural habitat.

  • Snorkeling: Kohala has several pristine beaches and reefs that offer great snorkeling opportunities.

  • Cultural Tours: Kohala is rich in Polynesian culture, and visitors can enjoy several traditional cultural tours that include food, music, dance, and storytelling.

Food and Drink Scene of Kohala

Must-Try Dishes

Kohala is home to several mouth-watering cuisines that blend the rich flavors of Polynesia with modern culinary techniques. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Kalua Pork: Slow-roasted pork that is cooked with salt and water and wrapped in banana or ti leaves.

  • Poke: A traditional Hawaiian dish made from diced raw fish, soy sauce, sesame oil, and chili peppers.

  • Lomi Lomi Salmon: A salad made from diced salmon, tomatoes, onions, and scallions.

Local Beverages

Kohala has several local beverages that are perfect for a hot day or a relaxing evening. Some top picks include:

  • Kona Coffee: This coffee is made from locally grown coffee beans, and it has a rich aroma and taste.

  • Mai Tai: A refreshing cocktail made from rum, lime juice, orange liqueur, and pineapple juice.

  • Lilikoi Juice: A sweet and tangy juice made from the passion fruit, which is grown locally in Hawaii.

Where to Stay in Kohala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kohala has several options for accommodation, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Some top picks include:

  • Mauna Kea Beach Hotel: A luxury hotel that offers stunning ocean views, spacious rooms, and world-class amenities.

  • Kohala Village Inn: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable rooms and a central location in the heart of Kohala.

  • Hale Mana Mauna Kea: A cozy guesthouse that offers modern amenities and a peaceful environment in the middle of a lush forest.

What to Buy in Kohala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kohala offers several unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home as a reminder of your trip. Some top picks include:

  • Hawaiian Shirts: These shirts feature vibrant tropical prints and are a staple of Polynesian culture.

  • Macadamia Nuts: These nuts are grown locally in Hawaii and are famous for their sweet and nutty flavor.

  • Kona Coffee: This coffee is grown locally in Hawaii and is available in several varieties and strengths.

Local Culture and Customs of Kohala

Kohala has a rich and vibrant Polynesian culture that is reflected in its art, music, dance, and cuisine. Visitors can learn more about the local culture and customs by attending traditional cultural events, visiting museums, and interacting with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Kohala

The best time to visit Kohala is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Kohala is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy a variety of activities and experiences regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kohala

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Be mindful of your environment and avoid littering.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb the turtles and other wildlife.

  • Trespass on private property without permission.

  • Overlook the importance of sunscreen and protective clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kohala

Kohala is generally a safe destination, and visitors can enjoy a worry-free vacation. However, it is essential to take safety precautions such as locking your doors, not carrying large sums of money, and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kohala

Visitors to Kohala should ensure they have adequate medical insurance, and they take essential health precautions such as getting vaccinated, staying hydrated, and wearing protective clothing and sunscreen.

Getting to Kohala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kohala

Kohala is served by two major airports: Hilo International Airport and Kona International Airport. Both airports have several direct flights from major cities in the United States and other countries.

Bus Stations Near Kohala

Kohala does not have a comprehensive public transportation system, but visitors can opt for private bus services that offer tours and transports to various destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, bike, or scooter to explore Kohala at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Kohala

Kohala offers several options for getting around, including car rentals, taxi services, and private tours.

Where to Go Next from Kohala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kohala is surrounded by several stunning destinations that are perfect for a day trip or an extended vacation. Some top picks include:

  • Waimea: This charming town is home to several art galleries, boutiques, and restaurants, and offers breathtaking views of the Mauna Kea mountain.

  • Kailua-Kona: Kailua-Kona is the largest town on the Big Island and offers a myriad of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

  • Hawaii Volcanoes National Park: This national park is home to two of the most active volcanoes in the world, and visitors can witness the power of nature in action.

Conclusion

Kohala is a destination that offers something for everyone, from stunning natural landscapes and thrilling outdoor activities to rich cultural heritage and delightful cuisine. Exploring Kohala is an experience that will leave you with lasting memories and a newfound appreciation for the beauty of Hawaii.
