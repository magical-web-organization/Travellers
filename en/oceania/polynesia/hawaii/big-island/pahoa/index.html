Introduction to Pahoa

Located on the Big Island of Hawaii, Pahoa is a charming town with a rich cultural history and stunning natural beauty. This eclectic community has a small-town feel and is full of unique shops, restaurants, and galleries. Pahoa is a great destination for anyone looking to experience Hawaii off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Pahoa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about Hawaiian history and culture, there are several important attractions to visit in Pahoa:

  • Kalapana-Kapoho Road: This scenic drive takes you past ancient Hawaiian villages, lava fields, and black sand beaches.
  • Lava Tree State Park: This park contains unique lava formations that were created during a volcanic eruption in 1790.
  • Pu’uhonua O Hōnaunau National Historical Park: Explore ancient Hawaiian temples, royal grounds, and a sacred place of refuge in this historic park.

Natural Attractions of Pahoa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pahoa is known for its stunning natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy:

  • Hawaiian Paradise Park: This park has miles of walking paths, picnic areas, and ocean views.
  • Hawaii Volcanoes National Park: This park features two active volcanoes and a range of hiking trails.
  • Kapoho Tide Pools: These natural pools offer excellent snorkeling and swimming opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pahoa

Pahoa has plenty of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Kilauea Iki Trail: This 4-mile trail takes you through the heart of Hawaii Volcanoes National Park.
  • Mauna Loa Trail: This 11-mile trail takes you to the summit of Mauna Loa, one of the tallest mountains in the world.
  • Kalapana Trail: This 5-mile trail takes you to the site of a former village that was buried by lava in 1990.

Best Things to Do in Pahoa: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Pahoa:

  • Manta Ray Night Snorkel: Snorkel with the majestic manta rays at night.
  • Hawaii Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic countryside on horseback.
  • Lava Boat Tour: See the lava flowing into the ocean up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pahoa

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Pahoa, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Pahoa town and visit local shops and galleries. Day 2: Drive down Kalapana-Kapoho Road and visit Lava Tree State Park. Day 3: Visit Hawaii Volcanoes National Park and hike the Kilauea Iki Trail. Day 4: Take a Manta Ray Night Snorkel tour. Day 5: Visit Pu’uhonua O Hōnaunau National Historical Park. Day 6: Go on a Lava Boat Tour. Day 7: Relax on the Kapoho Tide Pools and enjoy the natural beauty of Hawaii.

Food and Drink Scene of Pahoa

Must-Try Dishes in Pahoa

Pahoa has a range of delicious local cuisine:

  • Poke: Raw fish served with soy sauce and other seasonings.
  • Lomi-Lomi Salmon: A traditional Hawaiian dish with salted salmon and diced tomato and onion.
  • Kalua Pork: Slow-roasted pork that is often served at luaus.

Local Beverages

Don’t miss out on these refreshing local beverages in Pahoa:

  • Kona Coffee: Grown on the Big Island, this premium coffee is world-renowned for its rich flavor.
  • Hawaiian Sun: A popular fruit juice made with locally grown tropical fruits.
  • Mai Tai: This classic cocktail is a must-try when visiting Hawaii.

Where to Stay in Pahoa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pahoa has a range of accommodation options to fit any budget:

  • Pahoa Hostel: A budget-friendly option with shared dorms and private rooms.
  • The Bali House: A luxurious guesthouse with tropical gardens and ocean views.
  • The Inn at Kulaniapia Falls: A peaceful retreat with stunning waterfall views and eco-friendly accommodations.

What to Buy in Pahoa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Pahoa with these local products:

  • Kona Coffee: Bring home a bag of premium coffee grown on the Big Island.
  • Hawaiian Shirts: These colorful and breezy shirts are a staple of Hawaiian fashion.
  • Leis: These traditional Hawaiian garlands are made with fresh flowers and make a beautiful souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Pahoa

Respect these customs and traditions when visiting Pahoa:

  • Aloha Spirit: The Hawaiian people are known for their warm and welcoming nature. Embrace the spirit of aloha and treat others with respect.
  • Hula Dance: This traditional dance is an important part of Hawaiian culture. Attend a hula performance to experience the beauty of this art form.
  • Beach Etiquette: When visiting Hawaii’s beautiful beaches, respect the environment and leave no trace behind.

Best Time to Visit Pahoa

The best time to visit Pahoa is during the winter months (December to February) when the weather is mild and the crowds are lighter. However, Pahoa has a mild climate year-round, making it a great destination any time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pahoa

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Treat the Hawaiian culture with respect and learn about local customs and traditions.
  • Pack Sunscreen: The Hawaiian sun is strong year-round, so pack plenty of sunscreen.
  • Take a Lei: It’s customary to take a lei when arriving or leaving Hawaii.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep Pahoa’s natural beauty intact by taking care of the environment and not littering.
  • Remove Lava Rocks: It’s considered bad luck and disrespectful to remove lava rocks from their natural setting.
  • Forget to Tip: Tipping is customary in Hawaii, so don’t forget to tip your server or tour guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pahoa

Pahoa is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions:

  • Hiking Safety: Stick to marked trails and always carry a map and plenty of water.
  • Water Safety: Never turn your back on the ocean, and follow posted signs for safety precautions.

Health in Pahoa

Pahoa doesn’t have any major health concerns, but it’s important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun. If you’re planning on hiking or participating in any outdoor activities, be sure to bring plenty of water and sunscreen.

Getting to Pahoa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pahoa

The closest airport to Pahoa is Hilo International Airport, which is about a 30-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Pahoa

There are several bus stations in Pahoa, including the Pahoa Transfer Station and the Keaau Station. Buses are a convenient and low-cost way to get around the area.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great way to explore Pahoa and the surrounding area at your own pace. There are several car rental agencies at Hilo International Airport.

How to Get Around in Pahoa

The best way to get around Pahoa is by car or bike. If you don’t have your own vehicle, there are several bike rental shops in town.

Where to Go Next from Pahoa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hilo: This charming town is located just 30 minutes from Pahoa and is home to historic architecture, beautiful parks, and a bustling downtown area.
  • Keaau: Located just a few miles from Pahoa, Keaau is home to several unique shops and restaurants and is a great destination to explore for an afternoon.
  • Waipio Valley: This scenic valley is located about 1.5 hours from Pahoa and features stunning views, hiking trails, and a black sand beach.

Conclusion

Pahoa is a unique and charming destination in Hawaii that offers a range of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to relax on a beach or explore the area’s rich history and culture, Pahoa has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Pahoa.
