Volcano Village is a small community in the Big Island of Hawaii. It is located in Polynesia, Oceania, and is known for its proximity to the Kilauea volcano. This guide will provide you with the information you need to explore this unique destination in Hawaii, including places to visit, hiking/biking routes, things to do, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Volcano Village

Volcano Village is a small town located in the southeastern part of the Big Island of Hawaii. It is situated at an altitude of around 3,700 feet, and is known for its cool and misty climate. The town is surrounded by lush forests and beautiful landscapes, and is famous for its proximity to the Kilauea volcano, which is one of the most active volcanoes in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Volcano Village

The people in Volcano Village are friendly and welcoming. Here are some customs and cultural norms to keep in mind when visiting:

  1. Pay respect to the Hawaiian language and culture.
  2. Remove shoes when entering someone’s home.
  3. Respect sacred sites and don’t take anything from them.
  4. Talk to the locals and learn about their culture and lifestyle.
  5. Follow the Leave No Trace principles when hiking.

Best Time to Visit Volcano Village

The best time to visit Volcano Village is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is warm and dry. However, the town can be crowded during this time, so if you prefer a quieter experience, visit during the fall or winter.

Safety and Health

It’s important to be aware of safety and health concerns when visiting Volcano Village.

Safety in Volcano Village

  1. Follow all posted safety warnings at the national park.
  2. Stay away from restricted areas around the volcano.
  3. Watch out for falling rocks on hiking trails.
  4. Don’t go hiking alone.
