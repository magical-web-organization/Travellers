Introduction to Hilo

Hilo is a beautiful city located in the northeastern coast of the Big Island in Hawaii. Known for its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and spectacular outdoor activities, Hilo is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Hawaiian experience.

With its tropical rainforests, majestic waterfalls, and black sand beaches, Hilo offers a diverse range of attractions that elevate it from the typical tourist destination. From the culturally significant museums and historical landmarks to the adventurous hiking and biking trails, Hilo has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through all the must-visit places, best things to do, food scene, where to stay, and more in Hilo.

List of Places to Visit in Hilo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lyman Museum and Mission House: A must-visit for history enthusiasts, the Lyman Museum and Mission House is a fascinating repository of Hawaiian and Polynesian history and culture. The museum houses a diverse collection of artifacts ranging from fossils and minerals to artwork and textiles.

  • ‘Imiloa Astronomy Center: The ‘Imiloa Astronomy Center is a unique blend of astronomy, cultural heritage, and science education. Visitors can witness the wonders of the universe through the center’s world-class planetarium and explore the history of Hawaiian astronomy through interactive exhibits.

  • Pacific Tsunami Museum: Hilo has a long history of facing natural disasters, especially earthquakes and tsunamis. The Pacific Tsunami Museum is dedicated to remembering the victims of the 1946 and 1960 tsunamis that devastated the city and educating visitors about tsunamis and their impact.

Natural Attractions of Hilo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hawaii Volcanoes National Park: The Hawaii Volcanoes National Park lies just south of Hilo and is home to two active volcanoes, Kilauea and Mauna Loa. Visitors can witness the raw power of nature through the volcanic landscapes, lava tubes, and steam vents.

  • Akaka Falls State Park: Akaka Falls State Park is a lush rainforest located near Hilo that boasts breathtaking views of some of Hawaii’s tallest waterfalls. The 0.4-mile hike through the park rewards visitors with stunning views of the 442-feet-tall Akaka Falls and the 100-feet-tall Kahuna Falls.

  • Rainbow Falls: Located just a few minutes away from downtown Hilo, Rainbow Falls is a picturesque waterfall that cascades down a volcanic cliff into a stunning pool. The falls are named for the rainbows that appear on sunny days, making it a perfect spot for a scenic photo op.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hilo

  • Waipi’o Valley: This scenic hike takes visitors through lush rainforests to the stunning Waipi’o Valley lookout. The valley is known for its spectacular waterfall and taro fields, providing a peek into the traditional Hawaiian way of life.

  • Mauna Kea: The Mauna Kea Summit hike is an adventurous way to explore the highest peak in Hawaii. Visitors can hike or bike up to the summit, which stands at 13,796 feet, to witness the incredible panoramic views of the Big Island from above the clouds.

Best Things to Do in Hilo: Activities and Tours

  • Helicopter Tours: For a unique perspective on Hilo’s natural beauty, helicopter tours offer a bird’s-eye view of the city’s stunning landscapes, including the volcanoes, waterfalls, and lush rainforests.

  • Snorkeling/Scuba Diving: The Big Island’s clear waters are ideal for snorkeling and scuba diving. Visitors can discover the underwater world of colorful reefs, tropical fish, and even manta rays.

  • Nighttime Lava Viewing: Witnessing the fiery red lava spewing from the Kilauea volcano is an unforgettable experience. The nighttime lava viewing tours offer a chance to get up close and personal with the lava flows under the stars.

Food and Drink Scene of Hilo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lau Lau: This traditional Hawaiian dish is a must-try for foodies. It consists of pork, fish, or chicken wrapped in taro leaves and cooked over an open flame.

  • Poke: Poke is a classic Hawaiian dish made with cubed raw fish marinated in soy sauce and sesame oil. It can be mixed with a variety of ingredients, such as onions, seaweed, and avocado, to create a delicious meal.

Local Beverages

  • Kona Coffee: Grown on the slopes of the Big Island’s Mauna Loa volcano, Kona coffee is renowned for its rich, robust flavor and smooth finish.

  • Mai Tai: This classic Hawaiian cocktail is made with rum, orange curaçao, lime juice, and a splash of pineapple juice. It’s the perfect drink to accompany a Hawaiian sunset.

Where to Stay in Hilo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Royal Kona Resort: With its oceanfront location, the Royal Kona Resort offers stunning views of the Pacific and easy access to some of Hilo’s top attractions.

  • Hilo Bay Hostel: This affordable hostel is a perfect option for budget-minded travelers. It offers clean and comfortable accommodations in the heart of Hilo’s downtown area.

  • Hale Kawehi: Hale Kawehi is a cozy guesthouse located in a historic Hilo home that offers a unique Hawaiian experience with its tropical gardens and traditional décor.

What to Buy in Hilo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Macadamia Nuts: Hawaii is famous for its macadamia nuts. Whether it’s in a flavored spread, chocolate-covered, or straight from the farmer’s market, these nuts are a delicious souvenir to bring home.

  • Koa Wood Products: Koa wood is a rare and beautiful wood native to Hawaii. Visitors can find a variety of products, such as bowls, boxes, and even guitars, made from this special wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Hilo

Hilo has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs, traditions, and values. The Hawaiian culture values respect, hospitality, and sharing, which visitors will find evident in the warm and welcoming locals.

Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the Hawaiian culture by observing the customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering a home, asking permission before taking photos, and not disturbing the delicate ecosystems of the natural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Hilo

The best time to visit Hilo is between March and May when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. However, Hilo’s weather is mild year-round, with temperatures ranging from the mid-60s to the low-80s, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking a tropical getaway.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hilo

Do:

  • Respect the Hawaiian culture and customs.

  • Take a rain jacket or umbrella with you, as Hilo is known for its rainy weather.

  • Explore the local food scene by trying traditional Hawaiian dishes.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended in your car or on the beach.

  • Disturb the natural ecosystems and wildlife of the attractions by littering or touching the wildlife.

  • Disrespect other visitors by being too loud or intrusive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hilo

Hilo is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors are advised to take precautions against theft and natural hazards, such as flash floods, high surf, and volcanic eruptions. It’s always wise to carry a map, extra water, and snacks while exploring the natural attractions.

Health in Hilo

Hilo is a relatively healthy destination, with no major health risks, but visitors are advised to take precautions against sunburn, dehydration, and mosquito bites. It’s essential to use sunscreen, wear protective clothing, and stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Hilo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hilo

Hilo International Airport (ITO) is the closest airport to Hilo and is served by several airlines, including Hawaiian Airlines, United Airlines, and Delta Air Lines. From the airport, visitors can rent a car, take a taxi or use ride-share services to get to their hotel.

Bus Stations Near Hilo

The Hele-On Bus System operates in Hilo and offers affordable transportation to the city’s main attractions. The bus system is a great way for budget-conscious travelers to explore the city.

Other Options

Travelers can also use rental cars, bikes, or scooters to explore the city on their own. Additionally, guided tours are available for those who prefer a more structured travel experience.

How to Get Around in Hilo

Hilo is a relatively small city, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike. However, for those who prefer public transportation, the Hele-On Bus System is a cheap and convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Hilo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mauna Kea Summit: The Mauna Kea Summit is only a short drive away from Hilo and provides breathtaking views of the Big Island from above the clouds.

  • Kona: Kona, located on the west coast of the Big Island, is a popular destination known for its sunny weather, beautiful beaches, and excellent snorkeling and scuba diving opportunities.

  • Waipio Valley: Waipio Valley is a lush rainforest valley located north of Hilo that offers some of the best hiking and waterfall tours on the Big Island.

Conclusion

Hilo is a unique destination that combines the rich history and culture of Hawaii with the stunning natural beauty of its tropical landscapes. From hiking through lush rainforests to snorkeling in clear waters and witnessing the fiery lava flows of Kilauea, Hilo offers an array of attractions that cater to every traveler’s taste.
