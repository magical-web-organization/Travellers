Introduction to Lanai

Picture crystal-clear waters, panoramic ocean views, and serene landscapes – all this and more awaits you in Lanai, Hawaii. From an undiscovered paradise to a vacationer’s destination, this island embodies untouched beauty and tranquillity.

Found west of Maui, Lanai offers a unique escape from the bustling crowds of other Hawaiian islands. With its rich history and picturesque landscapes, it comes as no surprise that Lanai is gaining unprecedented popularity and becoming a must-visit destination in Hawaii.

List of Places to Visit in Lanai

If you are looking for beautiful scenery, adventurous hikes, and rich culture, here are some places you must visit while in Lanai.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kaunolu Village: One of the most significant archaeological sites in Lanai, Kaunolu is a must-visit attraction to learn more about the culture and history of the island.
  • Lanai Culture and Heritage Center: Visit the center to get a glimpse at the historical archives and learn more about Lanai’s past.

Natural Attractions of Lanai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Garden of the Gods: Marvel at the whimsical rock formations and picturesque views at Garden of the Gods. It is a must-visit spot for photographers and nature enthusiasts alike.
  • Pu’u Pehe: A beautiful sea stack on the southern shore of Lanai, Pu’u Pehe is an ideal spot to watch the sunrise or sunset.
  • Shipwreck Beach: Enjoy the stunning views of the wrecked tanker ships while taking a relaxing swim or trying your luck at fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lanai

Lanai offers many hiking and biking trails that cater to different fitness levels and preferences. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Koloiki Ridge: A challenging hike that rewards you with stunning views of the neighboring islands, Molokai and Maui.
  • Hulopoe Bay Trail: An easy-to-moderate hike that leads to a picturesque beach, perfect for swimming and playing in the waves.

Best Things to Do in Lanai: Activities and Tours

Lanai caters to different interests and ages, making the island perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers. Here are some activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Fishing: With miles of coastline and plenty of fish species, Lanai offers fantastic opportunities for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Golfing: Tee off on some of Hawaii’s most beautiful golf courses, including the Jack Nicklaus-designed Manele Golf Course.
  • Jeep Tours: Ideal for adventure enthusiasts, you can rent a Jeep and explore the island’s rugged terrain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lanai

To give you an idea of how you can make the most of your week in Lanai, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the beaches at Hulopoe Bay
Day 2 Visit the Lanai Culture and Heritage Center and go on a Jeep tour
Day 3 Hike the Koloiki Trail in the morning and have a round of golf in the afternoon
Day 4 Take a fishing charter for half a day
Day 5 Spend the day at the Garden of the Gods and Pu’u Pehe
Day 6 Take a helicopter tour over Lanai and the neighboring islands
Day 7 Relax and rejuvenate at the spa, followed by dinner at one of the island’s top restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Lanai

Lanai’s food and drink scene offer a fusion of traditional Hawaiian, Japanese, and American cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poke: A famous Hawaiian dish, poke consists of diced raw fish, onions, and seasonings. Enjoy it with some rice and veggies for a complete meal.
  • Hawaiian Plate Lunch: A lunch dish that consists of a protein of your choice (usually chicken or fish), macaroni salad, and two scoops of rice.
  • Shave Ice: A refreshing dessert made with shaved ice, flavored syrups, and sweet toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Mai Tai: A popular tiki cocktail made with rum, orange curacao, and lime juice. It is a perfect drink to enjoy while watching the sunset.
  • Kona Coffee: Known worldwide for its rich, nutty flavor, Kona coffee is a must-try beverage in Lanai.

Where to Stay in Lanai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lanai offers a range of accommodation options that suit different budgets and preferences. Here are the top recommendations:

  • Four Seasons Resort Lanai: An ultimate luxury resort that offers stunning ocean views, world-class dining, and a range of recreational activities.
  • Sensei Lanai, A Four Seasons Resort: A wellness resort that combines luxury with mindfulness and offers customized fitness programs and spa treatments.
  • Hotel Lanai: A boutique hotel that provides affordable and comfortable accommodations, perfect for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Lanai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted jewelry to locally made snacks, Lanai offers a range of souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some top picks:

  • Koa Wood Products: Made from Hawaii’s native Koa wood, these products include photo frames, furniture, and jewelry.
  • Lanai Coffee: Lanai’s coffee is said to have a unique, full-bodied flavor, making it a perfect souvenir or gift for coffee lovers.
  • Taro Chips: Made from taro root, a local staple food, these chips are a perfect snack to munch on while exploring the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Lanai

Lanai has a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to respect its customs and beliefs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Aloha Spirit: Hawaiian culture promotes kindness, hospitality, and friendliness. Embrace the Aloha spirit while visiting Lanai.
  • Respect for Nature: Lanai’s natural landscapes are fragile and sensitive. Observe the “Leave No Trace” principles by not littering and taking care of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Lanai

Lanai has a pleasant climate throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is during the shoulder seasons, from April to May and September to November, when crowds are sparse, and you can enjoy decent weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lanai

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Lanai:

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of Lanai.
  • Dress modestly when visiting sacred sites.
  • Take care of the environment and follow the “Leave No Trace” principles.

Don’t:

  • Take any artifacts or natural materials from the island.
  • Ignore public signs and laws.
  • Disturb the natural habitats of wildlife.

Safety and Health

Lanai is a safe place to visit. However, here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Lanai

  • Stay away from dangerous areas, especially beaches with strong currents.
  • Be cautious of sudden weather changes.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially when visiting crowded areas.

Health in Lanai

  • Make sure you have travel health insurance before visiting Lanai.
  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.
  • Bring a first-aid kit with you, in case of any emergency.

Getting to Lanai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lanai can be accessed by air or sea. Here are some ways to get to Lanai and transportation options on the island:

  • Air: You can fly to Lanai from Honolulu or Maui via Mokulele Airlines.
  • Sea: You can take a ferry from Lahaina in Maui to Lanai via Expeditions Ferry.

How to Get Around in Lanai

The best way to get around Lanai is by renting a car or a Jeep. You can also opt for taxis or shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Lanai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some time in your hands, here are some nearby destinations you can explore after visiting Lanai:

  • Maui: Just a ferry ride away, Maui offers beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and cultural sites.
  • Molokai: A lesser-known island in Hawaii, Molokai offers natural beauty and an authentic Hawaiian experience.

Conclusion

Though small, Lanai has a lot to offer to tourists and travelers looking to explore Hawaii’s hidden gems. From its stunning beaches, adventurous hikes, and rich culture, you’ll find something unique at every corner of the island. Remember to follow local customs and to take care of the environment while enjoying the Aloha spirit in Lanai.
