Introduction to Manoa-Makiki

Manoa-Makiki is a neighborhood located in Honolulu, on the island of Oahu, in the state of Hawaii, a group of volcanic islands in the central Pacific Ocean. This residential area is known for its lush greenery, serene atmosphere, and diverse community. Manoa-Makiki is surrounded by the majestic Koolau Mountains and is home to the University of Hawaii at Manoa, a prestigious academic institution.

List of Places to Visit in Manoa-Makiki

There are plenty of things to see and do in Manoa-Makiki, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking trails, and more. Here are some of the must-visit places in Manoa-Makiki:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bishop Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Hawaii and the Pacific region through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Hawaii State Art Museum: The museum features a collection of contemporary artworks by local and international artists.
  • Washington Place: This historic house served as the residence of the governor of Hawaii and is now a museum that provides a glimpse into the state’s political and social history.

Natural Attractions of Manoa-Makiki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manoa Falls: This waterfall is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers. The trail to the falls is about 1.6 miles long and winds through a tropical rainforest.
  • Tantalus Lookout: This scenic spot offers panoramic views of Honolulu, Diamond Head, and the Pacific Ocean. You can reach the summit by car, bike, or foot.
  • Lyon Arboretum: This botanical garden boasts over 5,000 plant species from Hawaii and other tropical regions. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore the trails on their own.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manoa-Makiki

Manoa-Makiki is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails that offer breathtaking views and challenging terrain. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Manoa Falls Trail: This 1.6-mile trail leads to the 150-foot Manoa Falls and passes through a lush rainforest.
  • Makiki Valley Loop Trail: This 2.7-mile loop trail offers stunning views of the city and the mountains and takes hikers through native Hawaiian plants and trees.
  • Round Top Drive: This scenic drive is a popular route for bikers and offers stunning views of the city, the ocean, and the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Manoa-Makiki: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the natural wonders of the area, here are some other activities to consider:

  • Visit the Saturday farmers’ market: The market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts by local artisans.
  • Attend a concert at the University of Hawaii: The university’s music program hosts a variety of performances, from classical to jazz.
  • Take a cooking class: Learn how to make traditional Hawaiian dishes like poke, kalua pig, and haupia from local chefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manoa-Makiki

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Manoa-Makiki:

  • Day 1: Visit the Bishop Museum and explore the nearby Kalihi-Palama neighborhood.
  • Day 2: Hike the Manoa Falls Trail in the morning and attend a concert at the University of Hawaii in the evening.
  • Day 3: Take a guided tour of Lyon Arboretum and learn about Hawaii’s flora and fauna.
  • Day 4: Visit the Hawaii State Art Museum and check out the nearby Chinatown district.
  • Day 5: Drive up to Tantalus Lookout and enjoy panoramic views of the city and the mountains. Attend the Saturday farmers’ market in the afternoon.
  • Day 6: Take a cooking class and learn to make traditional Hawaiian dishes. Visit the Ala Moana Center for some shopping and dining.
  • Day 7: Relax at a nearby beach or take a day trip to a neighboring island like Maui or Kauai.

Food and Drink Scene of Manoa-Makiki

Manoa-Makiki offers a diverse food and drink scene, ranging from street food vendors to upscale restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poke: This raw fish salad is a staple of Hawaiian cuisine and comes in a variety of flavors and combinations.
  • Plate lunch: This filling meal features a protein, rice, and macaroni salad and can be found at many local eateries.
  • Shave ice: This refreshing dessert features shaved ice and flavored syrup and is perfect for hot days.

Local Beverages

  • Mai tai: This tropical cocktail features rum, orange curaçao, lime juice, and orgeat syrup and is a must-try for any visitor to Hawaii.
  • Kona coffee: This gourmet coffee is grown on the slopes of the Kona district on the Big Island of Hawaii.
  • POG juice: This popular juice blend features passionfruit, orange, and guava flavors and is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Manoa-Makiki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manoa-Makiki offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • The Surfjack Hotel & Swim Club: This stylish boutique hotel features a vintage Hawaiian aesthetic and an outdoor pool.
  • Hostelling International Honolulu: This hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms and is within walking distance of Waikiki Beach.
  • Manoa Valley Inn: This historic bed and breakfast features elegant rooms and a tranquil garden setting.

What to Buy in Manoa-Makiki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, here are some popular items to consider:

  • Hawaiian shirts: These colorful shirts are a popular souvenir and can be found at many local shops.
  • Macadamia nuts: These delicious nuts are a staple of Hawaiian cuisine and make for a tasty treat.
  • Kona coffee: Bring home some of this gourmet coffee as a gift for your coffee-loving friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Manoa-Makiki

Hawaiian culture is rich and diverse, and visitors should take time to learn about its history and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the culture: Hawaiians have a deep connection to their land and traditions, so visitors should be mindful of their behavior and actions.
  • Learn some Hawaiian words: Knowing a few words in the indigenous Hawaiian language, like “aloha” (hello) and “mahalo” (thank you), can show respect and appreciation for the local culture.
  • Attend a cultural event: Many festivals and events celebrate Hawaiian culture throughout the year and offer an opportunity to learn and experience the traditions.

Best Time to Visit Manoa-Makiki

Manoa-Makiki is a year-round destination, with warm temperatures and sunny skies throughout most of the year. However, visitors should be aware of the rainy season, which runs from November to March and can bring heavy rainfall and flash floods. The best time to visit is from April to October, when the weather is dry, and the days are long.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manoa-Makiki

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Manoa-Makiki:

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Take your shoes off before entering someone’s home.
  • Explore the natural wonders and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect sacred places or objects.
  • Play loud music or disturb the peace.

Safety and Health

Manoa-Makiki is a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to ensure a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in Manoa-Makiki

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid risky behavior.
  • Watch out for traffic when walking or biking.
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun.

Health in Manoa-Makiki

  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing.
  • Be aware of the risks of mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus.
  • If you have any medical concerns, seek medical attention immediately.

Getting to Manoa-Makiki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manoa-Makiki is easily accessible by various transportation options. Here are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Manoa-Makiki

  • Honolulu International Airport (HNL) is the main gateway to Hawaii and is located about 9 miles from Manoa-Makiki.

Bus Stations Near Manoa-Makiki

  • The nearest bus station is the University of Hawaii Bus Terminal, which is served by several bus routes.

Other Options

  • Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in Honolulu.
  • Rental cars are also an option but are not necessary for getting around Manoa-Makiki.

How to Get Around in Manoa-Makiki

Manoa-Makiki is a relatively small neighborhood that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. However, the area is hilly, so visitors should be prepared for some uphill climbs. Buses and taxis can also be used to get around.

Where to Go Next from Manoa-Makiki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Manoa-Makiki is a great base for exploring other parts of Oahu and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Waikiki Beach: This famous beach is located about 3 miles from Manoa-Makiki and is known for its golden sand and clear waters.
  • Diamond Head: This volcanic crater is a popular hiking destination and offers stunning views of the coast and the city.
  • Pearl Harbor: This historic site is located about 10 miles from Manoa-Makiki and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Conclusion

Manoa-Makiki is a hidden gem in Honolulu, offering a mix of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a laid-back atmosphere. With its lush greenery, scenic vistas, and diverse community, Manoa-Makiki is a unique destination that will leave a lasting impression on visitors.
