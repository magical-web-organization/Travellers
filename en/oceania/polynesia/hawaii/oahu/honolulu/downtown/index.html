Introduction to Downtown Honolulu

Downtown Honolulu is the bustling commercial center of Hawaii’s capital city and situated in Oahu, one of Hawaii’s most popular islands for tourists. The city lies in Polynesia and is part of the Oceania region. This vibrant neighborhood in Hawaii offers a unique mix of modernity and traditional Hawaiian culture.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take a look at the best things to do in Downtown Honolulu, where to stay, local culture, and safety tips to help make your visit a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown Honolulu

If you’re looking to explore Downtown Honolulu, here is a list of places to visit for an unforgettable experience:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Downtown Honolulu is rich in history and has several museums and historical places to explore:

1. ‘Iolani Palace

The ‘Iolani Palace is the only royal palace in the United States and is a significant landmark in Honolulu. It served as the residence of Hawaiian royalty until the overthrow in 1893. The palace has since been restored and opened to the public in 1978. The palace grounds are open every day except Sunday.

2. Bishop Museum

The Bishop Museum is the state museum of Hawaii and is home to one of the largest collections of Hawaiian cultural artifacts. The museum has several exhibits that feature the history, culture, and natural science of Hawaii.

3. Washington Place

Washington Place is another significant historical landmark in Honolulu. It served as the residence of Hawaii’s last monarch, Queen Liliuokalani. Today it is the official residence of the governor of Hawaii, but it is open for public tours.

Natural Attractions of Downtown Honolulu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Downtown Honolulu might be known for its commercial centers but it does have stunning outdoor attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

1. Hawaii State Capitol Building

The Hawaii State Capitol Building is an architectural masterpiece designed by John Carl Warnecke in the 60s. The building houses the chambers of the governor and state legislature. The palace grounds cover an area of two square blocks and feature beautiful water fountains and gardens.

2. Aloha Tower Marketplace

The Aloha Tower Marketplace is a popular spot to take in the breathtaking views of the ocean and city. The tower is a 10-story structure built-in 1926 that has a beautiful art deco architectural style. In the marketplace, you’ll find shops, cafes, and restaurants, as well as, the impressive 87-year-old clock.

3. Chinatown

One other natural attraction in downtown Honolulu is the colorful and vibrant, Chinatown. The neighborhood is famous for its narrow streets, lively street markets, and shops.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown Honolulu

Downtown Honolulu is a great place to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Listed below are some of the routes that are highly recommended:

###1. Diamond Head

Diamond Head is one of the most famous hikes in Oahu, and it provides a breathtaking view of Downtown Honolulu and beyond. The hike is about 1.6 kilometers long, which takes around two hours minimum to complete. The trail is steep in parts, and the difficulty is moderate.

###2. Ala Moana Beach Park

The Ala Moana Beach Park is a famous park, and it has great bike lanes. You can go coastal cruising and catch breathtaking views of Diamond Head, Downtown Honolulu, Waikiki skyline, and more. You can also rent bikes and have a pleasant ride with the entire family.

3. Makapu‘u Point

Makapu‘u Point is ideal for hiking, boasting panoramic views of the island’s southeast side. The hike is around 3 kilometers long, and the difficulty is moderate. Once there, you cannot afford to miss seeing the famous lighthouse, which marks out the point.

Best Things to Do in Downtown Honolulu: Activities and Tours

Downtown Honolulu has many things to offer to everyone, from family-friendly activities to cultural and historical tours:

1. Honolulu Museum of Art

The Honolulu Museum of Art is the ideal place to experience the art, history, and culture of Hawaii. You’ll encounter an impressive collection of artworks by various artists from Oceania and Asia.

2. Hanauma Bay Snorkeling Tour

The Hanauma Bay Snorkeling Tour is an excellent way to explore the rich underwater world of Hawaii. You’ll be able to swim with colorful tropical fish, sea turtles, and other marine life that thrive in the bay.

3. Pearl Harbor and USS Arizona Memorial

The Pearl Harbor and USS Arizona Memorial Tour is a must-do activity while in Downtown Honolulu. You’ll experience the historical event that took place on December 7, 1941. The tour includes films, photographs, exhibits, and a visit to the USS Arizona Memorial.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown Honolulu

Downtown Honolulu has a rich food and drink scene with an array of traditional and fusion cuisine:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Poke

Poke is a classic Hawaiian dish and is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike. The dish typically consists of raw ahi tuna seasoned with soy sauce, sesame oil or avocado, and served with green onions or avocado.

2. Shave Ice

Shave Ice is a refreshing Hawaiian dessert that is perfect after a long day of sunshine and beach. It is a mix of shaved ice and various flavored syrups that range from coconut cream to acai or passionfruit.

3. Spam Musubi

Spam Musubi is a Japanese-inspired treat that is made of a slice of cooked Spam sandwiched between two pads of rice seaweed, held together by a strip of nori around the middle.

Local Beverages

1. Mai Tai

Mai Tai is an iconic rum-based cocktail that originated in the Polynesian-themed bars of California in the 1950s.

2. Kona Coffee

Kona coffee is one of the most popular coffee blends globally, and Hawaii is known for cultivating one of the finest batches. It is a unique blend with a full-bodied, smooth taste produced from coffee grown in the slopes of Kona, Hawaii.

3. Pineapple Juice

Pineapple juice is a refreshing juice made from fresh pineapple. Some farmers’ markets have stands where farmers showcase their homegrown pineapple and other fruits, making the juice extra flavorful.

Where to Stay in Downtown Honolulu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Honolulu has an array of accommodation options that cater to every budget and preference. Here are some of the options you should know:

Budget-friendly stays

1. The Beach Waikiki Boutique Hostel

The Beach Waikiki Boutique Hostel offers a comfortable and friendly environment for budget-conscious travelers. You will get a wide range of private and shared dorm rooms.

2. The Plumeria Hostel

The Plumeria Hostel is a bed and breakfast-style accommodation located in Chinatown. It is a comfortable and affordable option that offers a unique cultural experience.

Mid-tier stays

1. Aston at the Executive Centre Hotel

This hotel is located in the heart of the financial district, and the rooms are spacious, comfortable, and ideally suited to business travelers.

2. The Equus Hotel

The Equus is a beautiful hotel that offers comfortable and cozy rooms complete with exceptional amenities.

Luxury stays

1. The Royal Hawaiian

The Royal Hawaiian is a luxurious resort that provides its guests with world-class amenities and services.

2. Prince Waikiki

Prince Waikiki is a top-of-the-line luxury hotel that provides luxury accommodation, excellent dining options, and stunning views of the ocean and nearby Diamond Head.

What to Buy in Downtown Honolulu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Downtown Honolulu is known for its local products and souvenirs that make perfect keepsakes to take back home:

1. Hawaiian Jewelry

Hawaiian jewelry is one of the most popular souvenirs in Downtown Honolulu. The traditional Hawaiian designs, including hibiscus flowers and plumerias, are easily recognizable, and they come in various styles with different price ranges.

2. Hawaiian Shirts

Hawaiian shirts are iconic, and it is almost impossible to leave Hawaii without purchasing one. They are colorful and comfortable, and they are perfect for beach days.

3. Macadamia Nuts

Macadamia nuts, a product of Hawaii, make an excellent souvenir to take back home. The nuts come in different varieties and flavors, and they’re an excellent snack that is both delicious and healthy.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown Honolulu

Downtown Honolulu has a rich and diverse culture, and visitors are encouraged to respect these customs and cultural traditions:

Traditional Hawaiian customs:

  • Hawaiian greetings
  • Hawaiian naming practices
  • Respect for elders and family values

Best Time to Visit Downtown Honolulu

The best time to visit Downtown Honolulu is from September to November when the weather is mild, and beach crowds are relatively less. The summer months (June to August) are high season in Hawaii, and the winter months (December to February) experience surf swells that attract numerous tourists, resulting in higher costs and more crowded beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown Honolulu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated
  • Take your shoes off before entering a home or temple

Don’t:

  • Touch turtles and other marine creatures
  • Litter on the beaches
  • Disrespect sacred sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Honolulu is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Avoid flashing expensive jewelry or carrying large amounts of cash
  • Keep valuable possessions in a safe in your hotel

Health in Downtown Honolulu

Downtown Honolulu like all other regions of Hawaii, has its health risks. Visitors should take precautions and necessary measures to stay healthy:

  • Wear insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites that can potentially cause illnesses
  • Refrain from drinking water from streams or irrigation ditches due to the possibility of bacteria or pollution contamination

Getting to Downtown Honolulu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Downtown Honolulu is easily accessible, and visitors can get there with a variety of transportation options:

Airports Near Downtown Honolulu

The Daniel K. Inouye International Airport is the largest airport that serves the Honolulu area. It is located approximately 15 minutes from Downtown Honolulu.

Bus Stations Near Downtown Honolulu

Visitors can take the bus to get to Downtown Honolulu. The Oahu Transit Services offer several bus routes within downtown Honolulu.

Other Options

Visitors can hire taxis or rent a car through rentals that are based all over the Downtown area.

How to Get Around in Downtown Honolulu

Downtown Honolulu has several ways to get around the town, including public transportation and bike rentals:

  • Bus - Oahu Transit Service offer bus services around town.
  • Bike - Biki (bikeshare) is a popular mode of transportation in Downtown Honolulu.
  • Car rentals - Visitors can hire cars from several car rental companies in Downtown Honolulu.

Where to Go Next from Downtown Honolulu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Downtown Honolulu is an excellent base for exploring other areas in Oahu. Here are some places to go and things to do within your reach:

Waikiki

Waikiki is home to a vibrant nightlife scene, a stunning beach, and great shops and restaurants. The combination of culture, luxury, and nature makes it an excellent destination to visit close to Downtown.

Ala Moana

Ala Moana is a unique and exciting upscale destination that combines luxury shopping, dining, and nightlife. The area is famous for the Ala Moana Center, one of the largest shopping centers in the United States.

Conclusion

Downtown Honolulu is a vibrant and fascinating destination that offers an exciting blend of modernity and tradition. Its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions, and beautiful weather make it an excellent destination for unforgettable vacations. Armed with this guide, you will have all the necessary information to enjoy everything Downtown Honolulu has to offer. So, pack your bags, and head out for your next adventure in Hawaii.
