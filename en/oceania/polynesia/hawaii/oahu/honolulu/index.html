Introduction to Honolulu

Honolulu, the capital of Hawaii, is located on the island of Oahu. Known for its beautiful beaches, friendly locals, and rich culture, Honolulu is a popular vacation spot for travelers from around the world. Whether you’re looking to relax on a pristine beach or explore historical sites and landmarks, Honolulu has something to offer for everyone. In this travel guide, we will provide you with an in-depth look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more in Honolulu.

List of Places to Visit in Honolulu

If you’re visiting Honolulu, there is no shortage of places to visit. Here are some of the must-see sites in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iolani Palace: The only royal palace in the United States, Iolani Palace offers visitors a glimpse into Hawaii’s monarchy era.
  • USS Arizona Memorial: This memorial honors the men who lost their lives during the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941.
  • Bishop Museum: Home to the largest collection of Hawaiian artifacts in the world, the Bishop Museum is a great place to learn about Hawaii’s history and culture.
  • Honolulu Museum of Art: This museum features an impressive collection of Asian and Hawaiian art, as well as contemporary art from around the world.
  • Hawaiian Mission Houses Historic Site and Archives: These houses and archives offer a look into the lives of early Christian missionaries who arrived in Hawaii in the early 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Honolulu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waikiki Beach: This world-famous beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and long stretches of white sand.
  • Diamond Head State Monument: A popular hiking spot, Diamond Head offers stunning panoramic views of the coastline.
  • Hanauma Bay Nature Preserve: Snorkelers and scuba divers flock to this protected bay to admire the colorful marine life.
  • Manoa Falls: This picturesque waterfall is nestled in the lush Manoa Valley and is one of the most popular hikes in Honolulu.
  • Nuuanu Pali Lookout: From this lookout, you can see breathtaking views of the Koolau Mountain Range, the Windward Coast, and the town of Kaneohe.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Honolulu

Honolulu is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the city:

  • Ka’ena Point Trail: This 5.5-mile trail winds along the coast and leads to Ka’ena Point, where you can spot humpback whales during the winter months.
  • Manoa Falls Trail: This moderate 1.6-mile hike takes you through a lush rainforest to a picturesque waterfall.
  • Koko Head Trail: This challenging and steep 1,048-step trail offers stunning views of the coastline at the top.
  • Makapuu Lighthouse Trail: This paved 2-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the ocean and the Makapuu Lighthouse.
  • Ala Moana Beach Park Path: This 3.5-mile bike path runs along the coast and is a great way to see the city’s beaches and landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Honolulu: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or with friends, there is no shortage of things to do in Honolulu. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Sunset Catamaran Cruise: Enjoy a relaxing cruise while admiring the beautiful sunset over the Pacific Ocean.
  • Pearl Harbor Tour: Visit the USS Arizona Memorial, Battleship Missouri, and other significant landmarks in this tour.
  • Polynesian Cultural Center: Learn about the culture and traditions of the Polynesians with interactive exhibits, performances, and hands-on activities.
  • Wet ‘n’ Wild Hawaii: Beat the heat and enjoy the waterpark’s slides and attractions.
  • Oahu Atlantis Submarine Adventure: Take a ride on a submarine and explore the marine life and shipwrecks in Oahu’s waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Honolulu

Honolulu’s food and drink scene is a unique mix of different cuisines and influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poke: This raw fish salad is a staple in Hawaiian cuisine and can be found in many restaurants and food stalls.
  • Kalua Pig: This slow-roasted pork dish is a Hawaiian classic and is often served at luau celebrations.
  • Lomi-Lomi Salmon: This traditional Hawaiian side dish features salted salmon, tomatoes, and onions.
  • Malasadas: This Portuguese dessert is a popular treat in Honolulu and is best enjoyed fresh and hot.
  • Shave Ice: This refreshing treat is a Hawaiian specialty and is perfect for cooling down on a hot day.

Local Beverages

  • Mai Tai: This classic Hawaiian cocktail features rum, lime juice, orange curacao, and orgeat syrup.
  • Hawaiian Sun: This locally produced fruit juice is available in a variety of flavors, including passionfruit, guava, and pineapple.
  • Kona Coffee: Hawaii is famous for its coffee, and Kona coffee is one of the best-known varieties.

Where to Stay in Honolulu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Honolulu offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers on different budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • The Royal Hawaiian: This historic hotel, also known as the “Pink Palace of the Pacific,” offers luxurious rooms and is located right on Waikiki Beach.
  • Aqua Palms Waikiki: This budget-friendly hotel is located just a few blocks from Waikiki Beach and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Polynesian Hostel Beach Club: This hostel is located right on the beach and offers affordable shared and private rooms.
  • Waikiki Beachside Hostel: This hostel is located just steps away from Waikiki Beach and offers both dormitory and private rooms at a reasonable price.
  • Airbnb: For a more authentic experience, you can also rent a room or an entire apartment through Airbnb.

What to Buy in Honolulu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Honolulu, here are some of your best options:

  • Koa Wood Products: Koa is a native Hawaiian wood, and you can find various products made from it, such as jewelry, bowls, and pens.
  • Hawaiian Shirts: These colorful and comfortable shirts are a classic souvenir from Hawaii.
  • Macadamia Nuts: These crunchy and delicious nuts are a Hawaii specialty and can be found in many stores and markets.
  • Pineapple: Hawaiian pineapple is among the sweetest in the world and is a must-try while in Honolulu.
  • Ukulele: The ukulele is a traditional Hawaiian instrument and a unique souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Honolulu

Hawaiian culture and customs are very different from those of the mainland United States. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Honolulu:

  • Respect Hawaiian Culture: Hawaiian culture and traditions are an essential part of the locals’ identity, and it is essential to show respect while visiting Hawaii.
  • Learn Basic Hawaiian Phrases: Learning some basic Hawaiian phrases, such as mahalo (thank you) and aloha (hello/goodbye), can go a long way in showing respect for the culture.
  • Remove Your Shoes: It is considered impolite to wear shoes inside someone’s home, and it is also customary to remove your shoes before entering a temple or shrine.
  • Bring a Host Gift: If you’re invited to someone’s home, it is customary to bring a small gift, such as flowers or a box of chocolates.
  • Keep the Beaches Clean: Hawaiians take great pride in their beaches, and it is essential to dispose of any trash properly.

Best Time to Visit Honolulu

The best time to visit Honolulu is between mid-April and June and between September and mid-December. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. However, it is important to note that Hawaii’s weather is generally mild and sunny all year round, so you can plan a trip at any time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Honolulu

When visiting Honolulu, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the Locals: Hawaiians are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and it is important to show respect towards them while visiting the islands.
  • Explore the Local Culture and Traditions: Immerse yourself in Hawaiian culture by attending a luau, trying local foods, or exploring the island’s history.
  • Try Water Sports: Honolulu offers a wide range of water sports, such as surfing, snorkeling, and paddleboarding, so don’t be afraid to give them a try.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the Beaches: Hawaiians take great pride in their beaches, and it is impolite to leave trash behind.
  • Wear Shoes Inside Homes or Places of Worship: Remember to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Ignore Local Traditions: Be mindful of the island’s customs and traditions and show respect towards them.

Safety and Health

Honolulu is generally considered a safe and healthy destination. However, it is important to be aware of potential hazards and take necessary precautions.

Safety in Honolulu

  • Water Safety: The ocean currents in Hawaii can be strong, and it’s important to follow safety rules while swimming or engaging in water activities.
  • Sun Safety: Protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays by applying sunscreen and wearing a hat and sunglasses.
  • Street Safety: Honolulu is generally safe, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

Health in Honolulu

  • Drink Water: Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, especially if you’re engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Mosquito Safety: Mosquitoes can be present in Honolulu and can transmit diseases. Use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to protect yourself.
  • Vaccinations: Check with your doctor regarding recommended vaccinations, especially if you’re traveling from another country.

Getting to Honolulu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Honolulu is easily accessible by air and sea. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Honolulu

  • Honolulu International Airport: Located just a few miles from Waikiki, this is the main airport serving Honolulu.

Bus Stations Near Honolulu

  • Ala Moana Center: This shopping center is a hub for island-wide bus transportation.

Other Options

  • Cruise Ships: Honolulu is a popular stop for many cruise lines.
  • Ferries: Ferries operate between Oahu and nearby islands, such as Maui and Kauai.

How to Get Around in Honolulu

While renting a car is an option, it is not necessary to get around Honolulu. Here are some other transportation options:

  • Public Transportation: The island’s bus system, called TheBus, is very affordable and covers most major tourist destinations.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are also available in the city.
  • Biking or Walking: Honolulu is a very walkable city, and renting a bike is a great way to explore the island’s coastline and neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Honolulu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Honolulu, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Maui: Just a short flight away, Maui offers beautiful beaches, water activities, and stunning views of the island’s landscape.
  • Kauai: Known as the “Garden Isle,” Kauai offers beautiful hiking trails, waterfalls, and dramatic coastline views.
  • Big Island: The largest of the Hawaiian islands, Big Island is known for its volcanoes, black sand beaches, and diverse terrain.

Conclusion

Honolulu is a beautiful and unique vacation spot that offers a range of activities, attractions, and experiences. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore historical landmarks, or engage in outdoor activities, Honolulu has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and get the most out of your Hawaiian vacation.
