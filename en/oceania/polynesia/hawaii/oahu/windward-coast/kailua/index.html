Introduction to Kailua

Kailua is a picturesque town located on the Windward Coast of Oahu, Hawaii. Known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush green surroundings, it is a hidden jewel waiting to be explored. Kailua is surrounded by mountains and the ocean, offering a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, fishing, and snorkeling. This travel guide will provide insider tips on where to go, what to do, where to stay, and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in Kailua

Kailua offers a range of attractions to tourists of all ages and interests. Below is a list of must-visit places in Kailua:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bishop Museum
  • Pearl Harbor
  • Hawaiian Mission Houses Historic Site and Archives
  • Iolani Palace

Natural Attractions of Kailua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lanikai Beach
  • Kailua Beach
  • Kaiwa Ridge Trail
  • Maunawili Falls Trail

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kailua

Kailua is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. The town has several trails suitable for different skill levels. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Kailua are:

  • Ka’au Crater Trail
  • Olomana Three Peaks Trail
  • Makapuu Point Lighthouse Trail
  • Kealia Trail

Best Things to Do in Kailua: Activities and Tours

Kailua offers a range of activities and tours for tourists of all ages. Below is a list of must-do activities in Kailua:

  • Kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding
  • Snorkeling and diving
  • Fishing and whale watching
  • Horseback riding and ATV tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Kailua

To make the most of your visit to Kailua, we have created a week-long itinerary featuring a mix of cultural and outdoor activities.

Day 1: Visit Pearl Harbor and the USS Arizona Memorial.

Day 2: Explore the Bishop Museum and nearby attractions.

Day 3: Hike the Ka’au Crater Trail.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Hawaiian Mission Houses Historic Site and Archives.

Day 5: Visit Lanikai Beach and go kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding.

Day 6: Take a horseback riding tour on the beach.

Day 7: Relax at Kailua Beach and enjoy the sunset.

Local Culture and Customs of Kailua

Hawaii has a unique culture influenced by Polynesian, Asian, and Western traditions. Some customs and manners to keep in mind while in Kailua are:

  • Remove your shoes before entering homes.
  • Respect the Hawaiian language and pronunciation.
  • Don’t touch or remove anything from sacred sites.

Best Time to Visit Kailua

The best time to visit Kailua is from April to October when the weather is warm and dry, and the water is calm for swimming and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kailua

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat.
  • Enjoy the beaches and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches.
  • Touch sea turtles and monk seals.
  • Swim in the ocean during high surf.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kailua

Kailua is generally a safe town, but tourists should take precautions such as:

  • Locking car doors and securing valuables.
  • Staying aware of their surroundings and avoiding isolated areas at night.
  • Using certified tour operators for outdoor activities.

Health in Kailua

Kailua does not have any major health risks, but tourists should take precautions such as:

  • Drinking bottled water or boiled tap water.
  • Using mosquito repellent to avoid dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Kailua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tourists can reach Kailua via Honolulu International Airport, which is about 30 minutes away by car. The town has several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Kailua

  • Honolulu International Airport

Bus Stations Near Kailua

  • TheBus

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services
  • Rental cars and bicycles

How to Get Around in Kailua

The best way to get around Kailua is by car, bicycle, or on foot. The town has several rental car and bicycle shops, and most attractions are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Kailua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kailua is located near several other popular destinations in Hawaii. Some options for excursions are:

  • Honolulu
  • Waikiki
  • North Shore Oahu
  • Hanauma Bay

Conclusion

Kailua is a town filled with natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Whether you are looking for pure relaxation or adventure, Kailua has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Kailua and experience the best it has to offer.
