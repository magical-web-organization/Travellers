Introduction to Aiea

Aiea is a town in central Oahu, Hawaii, located northwest of Honolulu. The town’s name originates from the Hawaiian word “Aiea,” meaning “noisy” or “screaming.” Aiea is a small town with a population of approximately 9,000 people, but it’s a great place for visitors seeking adventure, history, and breathtaking natural beauty. With its proximity to Pearl Harbor and surrounding military installations, Aiea boasts an intriguing history.

List of Places to Visit in Aiea

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Aiea offers plenty of attractions to explore. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Aiea:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • USS Arizona Memorial and Pearl Harbor Visitor Center: A must-visit for any history buff, this world-famous site commemorates the Pearl Harbor attack on December 7, 1941.
  • Pacific Historic Parks: Located at the Pearl Harbor Visitor Center, the Pacific Historic Parks includes guided tours, exhibits, and a museum that tells the story of the Pacific War and the events surrounding Pearl Harbor.
  • Aiea Loop Trail: A 4.8-mile hiking trail that overlooks the beautiful Halawa Valley on Oahu. The trail is known for its lush foliage and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Aiea Bay State Recreation Area: A beautiful park that’s home to a variety of water activities, from snorkeling to kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding.
  • Aloha Stadium Swap Meet & Marketplace: A must-visit for shopping enthusiasts, the Aloha Stadium Swap Meet is a large flea market that offers local handicrafts, souvenirs, and fresh produce.

Natural Attractions of Aiea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keaiwa Heiau State Recreation Area: A beautiful park that’s home to several hiking trails, including the Aiea Loop Trail, which offers scenic vistas of the Halawa Valley and the Koolau Mountains. The park also features several picnic areas, a botanical garden, and a historic heiau (Hawaiian temple).
  • Pearl Country Club: A beautiful golf club surrounded by picturesque views of Pearl Harbor, the golf course provides stunning mountain and ocean views while golfing.
  • Halawa Valley Falls Trail: A 1.8-mile hiking trail leading to a beautiful 20-foot waterfall.
  • Pearl Harbor Aviation Museum: Located on Ford Island, this museum features a collection of aircraft, engines, and aviation-related exhibits that tell the story of aviation in Hawaii and World War II.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aiea

Aiea is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a wealth of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Aiea:

  • Aiea Loop Trail: A 4.8-mile hiking trail that winds through lush foliage and scenic vistas of the Halawa Valley and the Koolau Mountains. It’s a moderate to challenging hike that takes around 3-4 hours.
  • Halawa Ridge Trail: A challenging 6.5-mile hiking trail that offers stunning views of Pearl Harbor and Diamond Head from the top of Halawa Ridge. It’s an advanced hike that takes around 6-8 hours.
  • Keaiwa Heiau State Recreation Area Trails: The park features several hiking trails ranging from easy to challenging, including the Aiea Loop Trail and the Keaiwa Ridge Trail.
  • Pearl Harbor Historic Trail: A scenic 10.5-mile biking and hiking path that follows the coastline and leads visitors to historic sites around Pearl Harbor.

Best Things to Do in Aiea: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and exploring the town’s historical sites, Aiea is chock-full of adventurous activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some best things to do in Aiea:

  • Go on a USS Arizona Memorial Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of the USS Arizona Memorial to learn more about the history of Pearl Harbor and pay their respects.
  • Take a Surf Lesson: Hawaii is known for its water sports, and visitors can take a surfing lesson in the nearby waters to catch some waves.
  • Go on a Helicopter Tour: Visitors can take a helicopter tour to see Aiea’s breathtaking natural beauty, including waterfalls, mountain ranges, and the coastlines.
  • Take a Scuba Diving Lesson: Visitors can go scuba diving in Aiea’s crystal-clear waters to explore the depths and see the marine life.
  • Attend a Hawaiian Luau: Attending a Hawaiian luau is a must-do when visiting Hawaii. Visitors can enjoy traditional Hawaiian food, drink, and entertainment at the Aulani Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Aiea

Aiea has plenty of local gems and restaurants that offer visitors a chance to taste the town’s food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garlic Shrimp Plate: This is a popular dish in Aiea, consisting of succulent shrimp seasoned with garlic served with white rice and salad.
  • Hawaiian Poke Bowl: A traditional Hawaiian dish that’s deliciously fresh and healthy. It’s made with diced raw fish served over rice and topped with vegetables such as onion or cucumber.
  • Kalua Pork: A traditional Hawaiian dish made from slow-roasted pork cooked in an underground oven, known as an imu. It’s shredded and served with rice and salad.

Local Beverages

  • Mai Tai: A classic Hawaiian cocktail made with rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, and grenadine.
  • Kona Coffee: Hawaii is famous for its Kona coffee, which has a rich flavor and aroma. Visitors can try it at several local coffee shops in Aiea.
  • Pineapple Juice: Fresh pineapple juice is a popular drink in Hawaii, perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Aiea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can choose to stay in budget-friendly accommodations in Aiea, including hostels and guesthouses or upscale hotels. Here are some options:

  • Aulani, a Disney Resort & Spa: An upscale resort that features a water park, a lazy river, and several restaurants and bars.
  • Airport Honolulu Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located five minutes away from the airport.
  • The Cabins at Camp Mokuleia: A guesthouse located in Waialua, around 30 minutes away from Aiea, making it an ideal option for visitors seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Aiea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aiea is home to several souvenir shops and stores that offer visitors a chance to take home a piece of Hawaii. Here are some local products and souvenirs to buy:

  • Hawaiian shirts: A vintage Hawaiian shirt is a staple item of any Hawaiian souvenir.
  • Pineapple products: Visitors can take home local pineapple products, such as jams, sauces, and dried pineapple.
  • Hawaiian jewelry: Local jewelry shops in Aiea sell handcrafted Hawaiian-themed necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.
  • Hawaiian music: Visitors can take home Hawaiian music CDs or vinyl records as a memento of their trip to Aiea.

Local Culture and Customs of Aiea

Hawaii’s native culture is a blend of Polynesian, European, and Asian influences. Visitors should respect it and the customs and traditions of the locals while visiting Aiea. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Aloha Spirit: Hawaii has a unique warmth and friendliness that locals call “The Aloha Spirit.” Visitors should embrace this spirit and show kindness and respect.
  • Removal of Shoes: Many homes, temples, and other establishments in Hawaii have a strict “no shoes” policy.
  • Hawaiian Language: Locals speak both English and the Hawaiian language. Visitors are encouraged to learn a few phrases such as “Mahalo” (thank you) or “Aloha” (hello/goodbye) when in Aiea.

Best Time to Visit Aiea

The best time to visit Aiea is during the dry months from April to October when the weather is warm but not too humid. The peak season is December to March, when many visitors flock to the island to escape colder climates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aiea

Visitors should be mindful of the local customs and traditions while visiting Aiea. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Embrace the Aloha Spirit: Show kindness, respect, and warmth to locals and fellow visitors alike.
  • Respect the Local Culture: Be mindful of Hawaii’s indigenous culture, including language, customs, and traditions.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Aiea has plenty of hiking trails and outdoor adventures that visitors should explore.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Hawaii is known for its pristine beaches and lush forests. Visitors should not litter or leave trash behind.
  • Leave valuables in cars: Car robberies in Hawaii are common, and visitors should avoid leaving valuables in their cars while parked.
  • Over-Indulge in Alcohol or Drugs: Visitors should respect the island’s etiquette and avoid any harmful behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aiea

Hawaii is a relatively safe place but visitors should exercise caution while exploring Aiea and other parts of the island. Visitors should take the right precautions, such as:

  • Avoiding hiking alone: Visitors should always hike with a partner or a group.
  • Watching for rip tides: Visitors should be cautious when swimming in the ocean and be aware of any sudden rip tides.
  • Staying hydrated and sunscreen: Visitors should wear sunscreen and hydrate themselves frequently due to the hot and humid weather.
  • Locking vehicles and valuables: Visitors should be careful and lock valuable items in safes or inside their rooms when not in use.

Health in Aiea

Hawaii is relatively safe, health-wise, but visitors should take note of some health concerns while visiting Aiea. These include:

  • Mosquitos and Tropical Diseases: Visitors should avoid getting bitten by mosquitos, especially in the wet months from November to March, as they can cause diseases like Dengue fever, Zika, and Chikungunya.
  • Sun Safety: Visitors should take precautions to prevent sunburn by wearing sunscreen and protective clothing.
  • COVID-19: Visitors should follow Hawaii state health guidelines regarding travel and COVID-19 to keep themselves and others safe.

Getting to Aiea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aiea

Honolulu International Airport is the closest airport to Aiea, located about 10 miles southeast of Aiea. Major airlines operate numerous flights to Honolulu from cities across North America and Asia.

Bus Stations Near Aiea

The Bus is the public transportation system on Oahu, including Aiea. Several bus routes service Aiea and other parts of the island.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or taxi to get around Aiea and other parts of Oahu. Renting a car can be cost-efficient and offers visitors the freedom to explore at their pace.

How to Get Around in Aiea

Getting around Aiea is easy, with several transportation options available:

  • The Bus: The public transportation system is the most cost-efficient way to get around Aiea. Several bus routes serve the town.
  • Taxis/Uber/Lyft: Visitors can choose to take a taxi, Uber, or Lyft for convenience and comfort.
  • Rent a Car: Renting a car is a cost-efficient way to get around Aiea and other parts of Oahu for visitors that prefer the flexibility of going where they want when they want.

Where to Go Next from Aiea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aiea is an excellent base to explore other parts of Oahu, and some nearby destinations to consider include:

  • Waikiki Beach: Hawaii’s most famous beach area is located just south of Aiea and features high-end shopping, dining, and world-famous Waikiki Beach.
  • North Shore: Just an hour’s drive north of Aiea, visitors can experience the laid-back lifestyle of the North Shore and its world-renowned surf spots.
  • Kailua: This small town is located on the east side of Oahu, a short drive from Aiea. Kailua offers excellent beaches, water sports, and an authentic Hawaiian experience.

Conclusion

Aiea, Oahu, is a beautiful town with a rich history and natural beauty that makes it an idyllic destination for visitors seeking adventure, breathtaking scenery, and a unique Hawaiian experience. With plenty of hiking trails, water activities, and historical sites to explore, visitors can create a perfect itinerary and enjoy their trip to Aiea, Hawaii.
