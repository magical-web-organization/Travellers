Introduction to Howland Island

Located in the Polynesian region of Oceania, Howland Island is an uninhabited coral island that covers an area of just 1.6 square miles. Despite its small size, the island offers a unique and unforgettable experience to travelers who are looking for adventure and solitude in a remote and pristine setting.

List of Places to Visit in Howland Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As Howland Island is uninhabited, there are no museums or historical places to visit on the island. However, it is believed to have been visited by Polynesian navigators and was later discovered by American navigator, Captain George Washington from Hawaii in 1842. The island was also the site of Amelia Earhart’s disappearance in 1937.

Natural Attractions of Howland Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The island’s crystal clear waters, coral reefs, and deserted beaches offer a great opportunity for snorkeling, diving, swimming, and sunbathing. The island doesn’t have any tourist infrastructure, so visitors need to bring their snorkeling or diving equipment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Howland Island

Howland Island provides visitors with the opportunity to hike or bike around the island and relish the tranquil and untouched nature of its surroundings. Hiking or biking the island only requires basic fitness and equipment. The trails are not marked, so it is recommended to bring a GPS or maps while exploring the island on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Howland Island: Activities and Tours

Howland Island attracts travelers who are looking for solitude, adventure, and a nature-filled experience. Some of the best activities to do in the island are its water-based activities such as snorkeling and diving. Visitors can also hike or bike the island and take in the stunning views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Howland Island

For travelers who want to extend their stay, here’s a sample itinerary for a week on the island:

Day 1-3: Snorkeling, diving, and hiking the island.

Day 4-5: Relaxing on the beach and taking in the sunset and sunrise views.

Day 6-7: Fishing and exploring other nearby uninhabited islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Howland Island

As there are no permanent residents on the island, visitors need to bring their own food and water, and there is no local cuisine to try. Be sure to pack all necessary food and supplies before arriving.

Where to Stay in Howland Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

As Howland Island is uninhabited, there are no hotels, hostels, or guesthouses on the island. Visitors need to come prepared with their own camping equipment or makeshift shelter.

What to Buy in Howland Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

As the island is uninhabited, there is no local market to buy souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Howland Island

As the island is uninhabited, there are no customs, traditions, or cultures to experience on the island. Visitors should respect the environment and leave the island as pristine as they found it.

Best Time to Visit Howland Island

The best time to visit Howland Island is between December and May when the weather is dry and sunny. However, tourists should check if the island is open to visitors as conditions can change despite these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Howland Island

Do:

  • Bring all necessary food and supplies before arriving.
  • Respect the environment and leave the island as you found it.
  • Bring a GPS or maps while exploring the island on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Swim or snorkel alone without a partner.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Howland Island

Howland Island is considered safe for visitors as long as precautions are taken in the water and when exploring the surroundings. Visitors should be mindful of the island’s remote and uninhabited nature, and shouldn’t venture too far from the shore by themselves.

Health in Howland Island

Visitors should ensure that they bring all necessary medical supplies and medicines from their home countries. Healthcare on the island is non-existent.

Getting to Howland Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Howland Island is only accessible by charter flights, and there is no regular service schedule available.

The nearest airports to Howland Island are the Pohnpei International Airport and the Honolulu International Airport. From these airports, visitors can charter a plane to Howland Island. There are no buses or trains to take on the island as it is uninhabited.

How to Get Around in Howland Island

The island’s terrain is mostly flat. Visitors can explore the island either on foot or by biking the island. Visitors should bring their own equipment for either activity.

Where to Go Next from Howland Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby beautiful and popular tourist destinations around Howland Island includes:

  • Honolulu, Hawaii
  • Pohnpei, Micronesia
  • Easter Island, Chile

All of these destinations can be reach by charter plane. Travelers should make sure to plan ahead and verify availabilities.

Conclusion

Howland Island has a raw and untouched feel to it. Visitors who are looking for solitude and adventure in a remote setting will find Howland Island the perfect travel destination. However, visitors should remember to bring all necessary supplies and pack out everything they brought with them to leave the island as it was.
