Introduction to Tari Basin

Tari Basin is a remote and picturesque region located in the Highlands of Papua New Guinea, in Oceania. Known for its rugged natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse wildlife, Tari Basin offers a unique experience to adventurous travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path destination.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit places, sights, and activities to help you plan your perfect trip to Tari Basin.

List of Places to Visit in Tari Basin

Tari Basin offers a variety of attractions for travelers of all interests. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Tari Basin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tari Basin Cultural Centre
  • Huli Wigmen Village
  • Paiya Cave
  • Mendi Museum

Natural Attractions of Tari Basin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ambua Lodge and its surrounding waterfalls
  • Bosavi Crater
  • Mount Giluwe
  • Karawari Cave

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tari Basin

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Tari Basin, owing to its rugged terrain and breathtaking natural beauty. The following are some popular trails that hikers and bikers can explore:

  • Mount Hagen Trail
  • Trans Highlands Trail
  • Bosavi Crater Trail
  • Olga Pass Trail

Best Things to Do in Tari Basin: Activities and Tours

Tari Basin offers various activities and tours to satisfy all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do while in Tari Basin:

  • Attend a cultural performance by the Huli Wigmen
  • Trek to the waterfalls surrounding Ambua Lodge
  • Visit the Paiya Cave
  • Take a birdwatching tour

Food and Drink Scene of Tari Basin

Tari Basin has a unique culinary scene, influenced by its diverse ethnic groups. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mumu (traditional Highland-style roasting)
  • Laplap (staple dish made with yam or taro root)
  • Smoked pork belly
  • Sweet potato pudding

Local Beverages

  • Kava (a mildly psychoactive drink)
  • Coconut milk

Where to Stay in Tari Basin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tari Basin has a limited number of accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to more luxurious lodges. Here are some of the recommended places to stay:

  • Rondon Ridge
  • Ambua Lodge
  • Biamu Guesthouse
  • Tari Gap Guest House

What to Buy in Tari Basin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tari Basin offers an array of unique souvenirs and local products for tourists. You must take back some items that represent the local culture and traditions, such as:

  • Huli Wigmen handcrafted masks and carvings
  • Hand-woven baskets
  • Bilums (traditional woven bags)
  • Shell jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Tari Basin

Tari Basin is home to several ethnic groups, each with its unique traditions and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Tari Basin:

  • Always ask for permission before taking photographs
  • Dress modestly
  • Show respect for the local culture and traditions
  • Ask for permission before entering private property or homes

Best Time to Visit Tari Basin

The best time to visit Tari Basin is during the dry season, which runs from May to October, where outdoor activities are in full swing. The region can, however, be visited throughout the year, even during the wet season when the landscape is lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tari Basin

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures or entering private property
  • Be cautious when exploring the rugged terrain

Don’t:

  • Remove any cultural artifacts or souvenirs from the Tari Basin
  • Refuse to try local dishes
  • Leave litter or waste in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Tari Basin

Tari Basin is relatively safe for travelers. However, it’s essential to take precautions like:

  • Avoid traveling at night
  • Stick to the recommended trails
  • Avoid wandering off into isolated areas

Health in Tari Basin

Travelers to Tari Basin should get vaccinations for common diseases like hepatitis A and B, typhoid fever, and influenza. Malaria is also prevalent in the area and one must take the necessary medical precautions to prevent infection.

Getting to Tari Basin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tari Basin is a remote location with limited means of transportation. Here are some of the recommended modes of transport:

Airports Near Tari Basin

  • Tari Airport (TIZ)
  • Mount Hagen Airport (HGU)

Bus Stations Near Tari Basin

  • No bus stations located in or near Tari Basin

Other Options

  • Private car hire services
  • Taxi services

How to Get Around in Tari Basin

Tari Basin doesn’t have a public transport system, and visitors can get around by hiring a private car, taxi, or walking.

Where to Go Next from Tari Basin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After visiting Tari Basin, visitors have several options for nearby destinations, including:

  • Mount Wilhelm
  • Mount Giluwe
  • Karawari Cave
  • Lake Kutubu

Conclusion

Tari Basin is a unique and intriguing destination that deserves a spot on any traveler’s adventure list. This hidden gem of Papua New Guinea offers an array of experiences, from culturally significant sites to breathtaking natural beauty and wildlife.
