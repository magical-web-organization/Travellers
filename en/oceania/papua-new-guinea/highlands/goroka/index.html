Introduction to Goroka

Goroka is a town located in the Eastern Highlands Province of Papua New Guinea. It is the capital of the province and is home to approximately 20,000 people. Goroka is known for its vibrant culture, unique natural attractions, and excellent hiking and biking routes.

List of Places to Visit in Goroka

When visiting Goroka, there are several must-see attractions that you should add to your itinerary. The following list includes some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Goroka Cultural Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the rich cultural heritage of the Eastern Highlands Province. It features a range of exhibits on the region’s unique traditions, including music, dance, art, and crafts.
  • J.K. McCarthy Museum: This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits on the colonial history of Papua New Guinea and is named after one of the country’s most prominent colonial administrators.

Natural Attractions of Goroka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Gahavisuka Provincial Park: This park is home to one of Papua New Guinea’s tallest peaks, Mount Gahavisuka, and is a popular destination for hiking and trekking.
  • Simbu Caves: These limestone caves are located just outside of Goroka and offer a unique outdoor adventure experience with beautiful views and geological formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goroka

Goroka boasts several excellent hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular include:

  • Goroka Bike Park: This bike park offers challenging terrain and a variety of trails for riders of all skill levels.
  • Simbu Trek: This multi-day trek takes you through the scenic Eastern Highlands Province and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Goroka: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and outdoor activities, there are several other things to do in Goroka:

  • Participate in traditional dances: Visitors can watch or participate in traditional dances that occur throughout the year in Goroka.
  • Join a cultural tour: Guided tours are available to visitors who want to learn more about the local culture and history while visiting Goroka.

Food and Drink Scene of Goroka

Papua New Guinea is a melting pot of cultures, and the same can be said of the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mumu: This is a traditional method of cooking in Papua New Guinea, where food is cooked in an underground oven.
  • Kokoda: This dish consists of raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut cream and is a popular dish across the country.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: This is a traditional drink made from the root of the kava plant and is said to have relaxing properties.
  • Coconut milk: This refreshing drink is made from the meat and juice of coconuts and is a staple across Papua New Guinea.

Where to Stay in Goroka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Goroka to suit all budgets:

  • Pacific Gardens Hotel: This hotel is the most luxurious option in Goroka and offers a range of facilities, including a restaurant, bar, and pool.
  • Goroka Lodge Hotel: This is a mid-range option that offers basic amenities and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Pimaga Lodge: This guesthouse offers budget accommodation in a peaceful setting on the outskirts of Goroka.

What to Buy in Goroka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Goroka is an excellent place to buy locally made handicrafts and souvenirs. Some of the best items to bring home include:

  • Wood carvings and baskets: Basket weaving and wood carving are traditional arts in Papua New Guinea, and visitors can find unique pieces to take home as souvenirs.
  • Coffee: Papua New Guinea is known for its high-quality coffee, and Goroka is home to several coffee plantations.

Local Culture and Customs of Goroka

Papua New Guinea has a diverse cultural heritage, and there are several customs and traditions specific to Goroka:

  • Sing-sing: This is a traditional dance performed by locals that tells the stories of their culture and history.
  • Bilas: This is a traditional style of dress that involves intricate body painting, necklaces, and headdresses made from natural materials.

Best Time to Visit Goroka

The best time to visit Goroka is between June and August when the weather is dry and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goroka

Here are a few tips to help you navigate the local customs and culture while visiting Goroka:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites or attending traditional dances.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without asking permission, especially of locals.
  • Remove or damage natural objects, including plants and rocks.

Safety and Health

Goroka is generally a safe destination for visitors, and the locals are very friendly. However, as with any destination, there are a few precautions to take:

Safety in Goroka

  • Avoid walking alone after dark.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Goroka

  • Make sure you get the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Papua New Guinea.
  • Be cautious about what you eat and drink, and always choose bottled water.

Getting to Goroka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Goroka is located in the Eastern Highlands Province of Papua New Guinea and is accessible by various means of transportation:

Airports Near Goroka

  • Goroka Airport: This is the closest airport to Goroka and is located just a few kilometers from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Goroka

  • Goroka Bus Station: Buses run to and from Goroka regularly, connecting it to other destinations in Papua New Guinea.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Goroka, and they are a convenient way to get around the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Goroka

The most popular way to get around Goroka is by taxi, which is affordable and readily available. You can also opt to rent a bike or car if you prefer more independence.

Where to Go Next from Goroka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several exciting destinations to visit near Goroka, including:

  • Mount Wilhelm: This is the highest mountain in Papua New Guinea and offers excellent hiking and trekking opportunities.
  • Madang: This coastal town is known for its incredible beaches and diving spots.

Conclusion

Goroka is an excellent destination for visitors who want to immerse themselves in Papua New Guinea’s culture and natural beauty. From traditional dances to outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in this vibrant town.
