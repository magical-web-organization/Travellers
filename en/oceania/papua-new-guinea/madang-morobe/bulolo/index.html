Introduction to Bulolo

Bulolo is a town in the Madang-Morobe province of Papua New Guinea, Oceania. It is situated in the middle of the beautiful mountain range of Lake Bulolo and the Markham Valley.

Bulolo is one of the most attractive tourist destinations globally. It is filled with adventures that nature lovers and history buffs will love. If you’re looking for a new off-beat destination to explore, Bulolo is a must-visit place that should be on your bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Bulolo

There is no shortage of interesting and exciting attractions in Bulolo, which offer a taste of the local culture and stunning natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bulolo has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this by visiting some of the historical attractions in the area. Here are some of the places worth visiting:

  • Bulolo District Museum: This museum features natural and cultural artifacts from the region’s history, as well as a collection of photographs, and textiles.

  • Wau Ecology Institute: This museum showcases a wide variety of Papua New Guinean wildlife and covers ecology topics.

Natural Attractions of Bulolo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Bulolo offers a wide range of activities to keep you engaged. Surrounded by mountains, forests, and rivers, Bulolo boasts some of the country’s most beautiful natural landscapes.

  • Lake Bulolo: The majestic Lake Bulolo offers a tranquil paradise for swimming and boating.

  • Mount Wilhelm: Mount Wilhelm is the highest mountain peak in Papua New Guinea, and it’s a popular hiking spot among adventure seekers.

  • Markham Valley: The lush green valleys of Markham offer scenic views of the landscape, and the Markham River is great for a peaceful boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bulolo

Bulolo’s natural beauty makes it an ideal destination for hiking and biking activities. Here are some popular routes you can try:

  • Mount Wilhelm trail: This challenging yet rewarding trail is 4,509 meters high and takes up to five days to complete.

  • Lake Bulolo circumnavigation: A scenic and moderate three-day trail around the volcanic lake.

  • Chambri Lakes community trail: A moderate trail through the charming Chambri Lakes villages.

Best Things to Do in Bulolo: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to outdoor adventures, here are some of the enjoyable activities to engage in during your Bulolo visit:

  • Cultural Village Tour: Get a taste of the traditional way of life in Bulolo, visit a local village, and participate in cultural activities such as dance.

  • Rafting: Thrilling white water river rafting in the Markham River, one of the country’s most spectacular rivers.

  • Fishing: Lake Bulolo is stock full of rainbow trout, making it an excellent destination for fishing enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bulolo

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you experience the best Bulolo has to offer:

Day Itinerary
1 Arrival in Bulolo, check-in at the hotel, rest, and relax.
2 Visit the Bulolo District Museum, The Wau Ecology Institute, and Lake Bulolo.
3 Go hiking on the Mount Wilhelm trail.
4 Go rafting on the Markham River or fishing at Lake Bulolo.
5 Take a cultural village tour in Bulolo to learn about traditional life.
6 Do the Lake Bulolo Circumnavigation hiking trail.
7 Depart Bulolo for the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Bulolo

Papua New Guinea boasts a diverse range of local cuisine that features fresh vegetables, exotic fruits, and fish. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages while in Bulolo:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lap Lap: a Papuan dish made from a coconut base with a variety of roots, vegetables, and meat wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.

  • Kokoda: This fish dish is cured in lemon juice, mixed with vegetables, and served raw with coconut cream.

  • Mumu: A traditional Papua New Guinean feast that involves roasting meat in an underground oven, otherwise called the Mumu, for several hours.

Local Beverages

  • Morobe Gold: This beer is locally brewed and has a unique flavor that makes it a local favorite.

  • Kona: A coffee beverage made from medium-roast coffee beans that are grown in the highlands of Papua New Guinea.

Where to Stay in Bulolo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Bulolo to fit all budget levels. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Crossroads Transit Hotel: A high-quality hotel located near Bulolo Airport.

  • Wau Ecology Institute & Lodge: A comfortable lodge located close to the rainforest and the Wau Ecology Institute.

  • Lani Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse that features good living spaces.

What to Buy in Bulolo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to take home something unique, Bulolo offers a range of souvenirs such as handmade jewelry, fabrics, and carvings. Craft markets and stalls can be found in the town center.

Local Culture and Customs of Bulolo

The Bulolo people observe traditional customs and beliefs influenced by their history and culture. Visitors should show respect for the locals’ customs and be mindful of traditional practices.

Best Time to Visit Bulolo

The best time to visit Bulolo is between May and October when humidity levels are low, and the weather is favorable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bulolo

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ customs and beliefs.

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.

  • Bring sturdy hiking shoes and waterproof clothing.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs or videos of the locals without their permission.

  • Litter the natural environment.

  • Buy artifacts that are made from endangered animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bulolo

Bulolo is generally a safe place for tourists. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions:

  • Don’t walk alone at night in isolated areas.

  • Avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables.

Health in Bulolo

Visitors to Bulolo must be current on their vaccinations and take preventative measures such as insect repellent to lower the risk of malaria and other diseases. It’s recommended to seek medical advice before traveling.

Getting to Bulolo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bulolo

  • Bulolo Airport is located a few kilometers from the town center and hosts flights from different locations in Papua New Guinea.

  • Lae Nadzab Airport offers an alternative option for visitors who can take a domestic flight into Bulolo.

Bus Stations Near Bulolo

Bulolo’s road infrastructure is still improving and is not extensive. Therefore there are no direct buses to Bulolo from other destinations where cars and trucks are the primary options. The few bus services in the region connect to Lae, the capital of Morobe.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a driver is another option to get to Bulolo. However, the roads are not as robust, and driving can be challenging, hence opting for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Bulolo

Taxis, buses, and private cars are the most common modes of transportation in Bulolo. Taxis and buses impose fixed rates, while private cars can be hired at hourly or daily rates.

Where to Go Next from Bulolo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for another destination near Bulolo, some nearby places worth visiting include:

  • Mount Hagen: A beautiful mountainous region located northwest of Bulolo.

  • Lae: Known as the garden city because of its beautiful gardens, Lae is the second-largest city in Papua New Guinea, and it’s the capital of the Morobe province.

  • Sepik River: located on the northern coast of Papua New Guinea, the Sepik River is famous for its unique art and culture.

Conclusion

Bulolo has everything that a traveler could want, from rich culture and history to stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities. Make sure to visit this hidden gem in Papua New Guinea and experience the unique charm and beauty for yourself.
