Madang, located in Madang-Morobe on the north coast of Papua New Guinea in Oceania, is a beautiful city surrounded by lush forests, pristine beaches, and crystal-clear waters. The city is known for being the hub of both business and tourism in the region, offering visitors a unique blend of culture and adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the must-see attractions, best places to stay, delicious foods to try, and everything else you need to know about Madang, Papua New Guinea.

Introduction to Madang

Madang is a city of contrasts - a blend of ancient traditions and modern innovation. It is known for its stunning natural scenery, diverse culture, and friendly locals. The city is located on the north coast of Papua New Guinea, and it is home to a rich cultural heritage that dates back thousands of years. Madang is the perfect destination for travelers who are looking to experience the vibrant culture and natural beauty of Papua New Guinea.

List of Places to Visit in Madang

Madang offers an array of activities for visitors to explore. Here are some places that should be on your must-visit list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Madang Cultural Centre: A great place to learn about the diverse and unique cultures of Papua New Guinea. There are regular performances and exhibitions showcasing traditional music, dance, and art.
  • Binnen Island: This picturesque island is home to some of the oldest and most unique artifacts in Papua New Guinea.
  • Kalibobo Light House: This iconic lighthouse was constructed in 1911 and has been an important landmark for the city ever since.
  • Madang Historical Society: A place to learn about the history of the region and the impact of European colonialism in Papua New Guinea.

Natural Attractions of Madang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dunk Island: Often referred to as the ‘jewel in the crown’ of Madang, this breathtaking island is a must-visit for nature lovers and beach-goers. It features crystal-clear waters, untouched rainforests, and stunning coral reefs.
  • Nubia and Baung Beaches: These pristine beaches are perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling.
  • Lighthouse Point: This is an ideal location to enjoy the stunning sunsets and panoramic views of the coastline.
  • Madang Resort Dive Center: This is an exceptional place to explore the underwater world of Papua New Guinea. There are a variety of dives available ranging from beginner to advanced.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Madang

Madang has a wide range of hiking and biking trails that cater to adventurers of all levels. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Coastal Track: This popular trail goes around the Madang coastline and takes about 5 to 7 hours to complete.
  • Amele Village: A beautiful village that is accessible by a scenic trek through the rainforest.
  • Mauganui Trek: This trek takes you through the rainforest, offering stunning views of the coastline and surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Madang: Activities and Tours

Madang offers numerous activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Madang:

  • Madang Market Tour: Explore the colorful and vibrant Madang market.
  • Madang River Cruise: This is an excellent way to see the beauty of Madang from a different perspective.
  • Bogia Coconut Farm Tour: Discover how coconuts are grown, harvested, and processed.
  • Paddle-boarding and Kayaking: Rent a paddle-board or kayak and explore the beautiful bays of Madang.

Week-Long Itinerary for Madang

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Madang:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Madang cultural center, visit the Madang historical society, and take a stroll around the city center
Day 2 Take a day trip to Nubia and Baung beaches.
Day 3 Go hiking along the Amele village trek.
Day 4 Take a Madang river cruise and visit the Kalibobo lighthouse.
Day 5 Rent a paddle-board or kayak for the day.
Day 6 Visit Dunk Island and go for a snorkel.
Day 7 Take a Bogia coconut farm tour and relax at Lighthouse Point for sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Madang

Madang’s food scene offers a unique blend of traditional and modern cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaukau: This is a type of sweet potato that is a staple in the Papua New Guinean diet.
  • Rakam Cay: A type of lime that is used in many traditional dishes.
  • Sago Pudding: A dessert made from sago, a type of starch extracted from the trunk of sago palm trees.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink, served straight from the coconut.
  • PNG Coffee: Papua New Guinea produces some of the best coffee in the world. Try a cup at a local café or restaurant.
  • Sago Wine: A traditional drink made from the sago plant.

Where to Stay in Madang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Madang has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some top options:

  • Madang Lodge Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Madang Resort Hotel: A luxurious resort that features scenic views of the Madang harbor.
  • Jais Aben Resort: This beautiful resort is located only 15-minutes drive from the city and offer its visitors easy accessibility to the beautiful Madang coastline.

What to Buy in Madang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can buy as a memory of your trip:

  • Traditional carvings and handicrafts: These are available at the Madang cultural center and Madang market.
  • PNG Coffee: Take home some of the best coffee in the world.
  • Coconut products: From coconut oil to soap, there are a variety of products made from coconuts available in Madang.

Local Culture and Customs of Madang

Madang is a city that values its traditions and cultural heritage. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respectful Dress: Papua New Guineans value modesty. It is recommended to dress modestly when out in public places.
  • Greetings: It is customary to greet everyone you meet, especially elders, with a handshake and a smile.
  • Respect for Elders: In the local culture of Madang, elders are held in high regard and should be treated with respect.

Best Time to Visit Madang

The best time to visit Madang is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is sunny, and the humidity is lower, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Madang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Madang:

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture: Take the time to learn about the local customs and traditions.
  • Be Patient: It’s important to be patient when visiting Madang, as things can move at a slower pace.
  • Be Aware of Your Surroundings: As with any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: It’s recommended to drink bottled water to avoid any potential health risks.
  • Waste: Madang is a coastal city with a fragile ecosystem. Take care to not litter, and dispose of waste appropriately.

Safety and Health

It’s important to be aware of safety and health issues while traveling to Madang.

Safety in Madang

  • Petty Crime: Petty crime is prevalent in Papua New Guinea. Keep an eye on your personal belongings when out in public places.
  • Transportation Safety: It’s important to practice caution when using public transportation or taxis. Avoid driving at night if possible.

Health in Madang

  • Malaria: Malaria is prevalent in Papua New Guinea. Consult with your doctor on recommended vaccines and malaria medication.
  • Food and Water Safety: It’s important to practice good food and water hygiene to avoid any potential gastrointestinal issues.

Getting to Madang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Madang is easily accessible by air, land, and sea. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Madang

  • Madang Airport: Located only 10 km from the city center, this is the main airport serving Madang.

Bus Stations Near Madang

  • Madang Bus Terminal: This bus terminal serves the local area and is located in the city center.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rentals are available at Madang airport and in the city center.
  • Boat: There are regular boat services available to Madang from other cities along the coast.

How to Get Around in Madang

Madang is a small city, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by local taxi. Taxi prices are negotiable, and it’s recommended to agree on the fare before getting in.

Where to Go Next from Madang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider after exploring Madang:

  • Lae: Located only 210 km from Madang, Lae offers visitors a unique blend of history and culture.
  • Wewak: This small town is located in the East Sepik Province and is known for its beautiful beaches and traditional handicrafts.
  • Rabaul: This town is located on the island of New Britain and is known for its stunning volcanic landscapes and rich colonial history.

Conclusion

Madang is a unique and vibrant city that offers visitors an array of activities and attractions. The city’s rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural scenery, and friendly locals make it a must-visit destination in Papua New Guinea. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to plan a trip to Madang and enjoy an unforgettable adventure.
