Introduction to Madang-Morobe

Madang-Morobe is a region in Papua New Guinea located in the northern coast of the island country in Oceania. This region is known for its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and historical landmarks. The region is home to many tribes with over 800 different languages spoken, making Papua New Guinea one of the most linguistically diverse countries in the world. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the best of Madang-Morobe and help you plan your perfect trip to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Madang-Morobe

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Madang-Morobe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Madang Museum: The Madang Museum showcases the rich history and culture of the region. Visitors can view displays of traditional artifacts, weapons, pottery, and more.
  2. Haus Tambaran: This is a traditional meeting house of the coastal tribes and is famous for its unique architecture and intricate carvings. Visitors can observe traditional dances and ceremonies on special occasions.

Natural Attractions of Madang-Morobe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sepik river: This is one of the longest rivers in Papua New Guinea and is home to diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the river by boat or can take a guided tour for a more immersive experience.
  2. Karkar Island: This is an active volcanic island and is home to many rare and unique species of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Madang-Morobe

Madang-Morobe offers some of the most beautiful and scenic hiking and biking routes in Papua New Guinea. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Trek to Mount Wilhelm: This is Papua New Guinea’s highest peak and is located in the Madang-Morobe region. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the landscape and indulge in some bird-watching on the trek.
  2. Morobe rail trail: This is 200 kilometers long and was originally built as a rail line. Today it is used as a hiking and biking route and passes through beautiful sceneries, including forests, waterfalls, and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Madang-Morobe: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving: The Madang-Morobe region has one of the most diverse marine ecosystems in the world. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the underwater world and see beautiful coral reefs, colorful fish, and sea creatures.
  2. Traditional dance and ceremony: Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending traditional dances and ceremonies organized by the various tribes in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Madang-Morobe

Food and Drink Scene of Madang-Morobe

The food and drink scene of Madang-Morobe is diverse and rich in flavor. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Mumu: This is a traditional dish made of vegetables, pork, and sweet potato cooked underground for hours.
  2. Sago: This is a staple food of the region and is made from the sago palm. It is usually served with fish or pork.

Local Beverages

  1. Coconut water: This is a refreshing drink that is widely available in the region.
  2. Betel nut: This is a popular stimulant, and the nut is traditionally chewed with lime and mustard.

Where to Stay in Madang-Morobe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Madang-Morobe has many options for accommodation. Visitors can choose from hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Some of the best options are:

  1. Madang Resort Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Madang and offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the harbor.
  2. Beurit Guest House: This guesthouse is located in Lae and provides a friendly and welcoming atmosphere for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Madang-Morobe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Papua New Guinea is famous for its traditional handicrafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. Bilum: This is a hand-woven bag made from bush string and comes in a variety of colors and designs.
  2. Carvings: The region is famous for its intricate carvings made from wood and stone.

Local Culture and Customs of Madang-Morobe

Madang-Morobe is rich in culture and tradition. Visitors should be mindful and respectful of local customs. It is customary to shake hands and exchange pleasantries when meeting someone new. Visitors should dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering a traditional meeting house.

Best Time to Visit Madang-Morobe

The best time to visit Madang-Morobe is from May to October when the weather is dry and sunny. The wet season is from November to April and can make transportation and outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Madang-Morobe

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local food and beverages.
  3. Be prepared for the weather.

Don’t:

  1. Take photographs without permission.
  2. Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  3. Wear revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Madang-Morobe

Visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings at all times. It is not advisable to travel alone, especially at night. Visitors should avoid displaying valuables, and it is recommended to use a reputable tour company for transportation and activities.

Health in Madang-Morobe

Visitors should take necessary precautions to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as malaria and dengue fever. It is recommended to bring insect repellent and use mosquito nets when sleeping.

Getting to Madang-Morobe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Madang-Morobe

Madang Airport is the main airport in the region and receives daily flights from Port Moresby.

Bus Stations Near Madang-Morobe

There are no bus stations in Madang-Morobe, but visitors can take public transportation or hire a private car with a driver.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Madang-Morobe by boat or plane from other parts of the country.

How to Get Around in Madang-Morobe

The most common mode of transportation in Madang-Morobe is by car or hiring a private car with a driver. Visitors can also take public transportation, but it can be challenging to navigate for first-time visitors.

Where to Go Next from Madang-Morobe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Lae: This is the second-largest city in Papua New Guinea and is located in the neighboring Morobe region. Visitors can explore the bustling markets and visit the Morobe Mining Joint Venture Cultural Centre.
  2. Goroka: This is an inland town with spectacular views of the highlands and is known for its annual Goroka Show, where traditional dances and performances are showcased by tribes from all over Papua New Guinea.

Conclusion

Madang-Morobe is a must-visit destination for those seeking adventure, cultural experiences, and stunning natural beauty. With its rich history, diverse culture, and breathtaking landscapes, Madang-Morobe is a hidden gem that should not be missed. Plan your trip today and start exploring this beautiful region of Papua New Guinea.
