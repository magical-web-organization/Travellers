If you’re looking for a remote escape filled with natural beauty, cultural immersion, and outdoor adventures, look no further than Kerema. Located on the western side of Papua New Guinea, Kerema is a hidden gem that boasts breathtaking vistas, traditional communities, and an endless array of activities to experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Kerema’s best sights, sounds, and flavors while equipping you with all the necessary information to plan your next trip to this stunning destination.

Introduction to Kerema

Kerema is a small coastal town that is the capital of the Gulf Province of Papua New Guinea. It is situated on the southern coast of the Gulf of Papua and is accessible by land, sea, and air. The town is home to approximately 5,000 residents who primarily make a living from agriculture, fishing, and forestry. Kerema is known for its beautiful beaches, lush forests, and unique traditional culture, which has remained relatively untouched by modernity.

List of Places to Visit in Kerema

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kerema has a rich cultural heritage that can be experienced through its museums and historical sites. The Kerema Cultural Centre is a must-visit for those interested in the region’s history and traditions. Here, you can explore various artifacts, photographs, artwork, and other items that reflect the Gulf Province’s cultural diversity and identity. Another significant cultural site in Kerema is the Keravat National Historic Site, which features burial caves, pottery, and carvings from the Papuan Gulf culture.

Natural Attractions of Kerema: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kerema’s natural beauty is awe-inspiring, with lush rainforests, stunning beaches, and marine life that is said to be some of the most diverse in the world. The Bibiko Beach and Village is a great place to start your journey and is famous for its white sand beach and clear blue water. You can also explore the Napatana Mangroves, which is a protected reserve of mangroves, and home to exotic bird and plant species. For adrenaline junkies, the Kapuas River offers opportunities for rafting and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerema

Kerema offers various hiking and biking routes that showcase the region’s natural beauty and wildlife. The Tabubil Trail is a popular route that takes you through the beautiful rainforest, where you can spot a wide range of plant and animal species. Another excellent option is the Laloki River Trail, which follows the Laloki River and allows you to experience jungle life while passing by some traditional villages and ancient settlements.

Best Things to Do in Kerema: Activities and Tours

Kerema offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors, catering to different interests. If you’re interested in water activities, such as snorkeling and diving, the Kerema Dive Club is a great place to start. For those interested in cultural immersion, you can visit the Mawase Village, where you can learn about the region’s traditional handicrafts, music, and dance. To explore the region’s wildlife, an adventurous bushwalk in the nearby forest or a visit to the nearby Kumuls sanctuary can be a life-changing experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kerema

Kerema is a place where a week could quickly pass by with numerous activities to engage in, whether it be cultural, outdoor, or just relaxation.

Day 1: Explore the Kerema Cultural Center and learn about the cultural history and traditions of the region. Day 2: Go beach hopping at Bibiko Beach and Village, Taikone Beach, and Kikori Beach. Day 3: Take a hike through the rainforest and visit the traditional village at Kaniu. Day 4: Explore the Napatana Mangroves and visit traditional village settlements. Day 5: Take a river tour on the Kapuas River and visit the Kumuls sanctuary. Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at the Kerema Bay and try island hopping or kayaking. Day 7: Wrap up the trip with souvenir shopping at local markets and spend the evening attending a cultural dance performance at one of the nearby village communities.

Food and Drink Scene of Kerema

Kerema’s cuisine is known for its unique blend of traditional and modern flavors. The cuisine heavily features seafood, given the region’s coastal location, and traditional ways of cooking such as smoking or drying are still being used. Some of the must-try dishes include fresh fish, mud crabs, and coconut-based dishes like Kokoda. When it comes to beverages, coconut water, fresh fruit juices, and locally brewed beer like SP or Niugini Ice are popular choices.

Where to Stay in Kerema: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kerema offers limited tourist accommodation options, with no major hotels and resorts in the town. Guests can find budget-friendly guesthouses and motels offering basic amenities such as hot water, fans, and clean beds. If guests are looking for privacy and and quality facilities, then the nearby Hela Lodge or Gulf Inn Hotel in nearby Port Moresby will be viable options.

What to Buy in Kerema: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To take home a piece of Kerema’s unique culture, your best bet would be to visit local markets or street vendors to purchase handicrafts such as woven bags, baskets, carvings, or fresh produce like tropical fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Kerema

The Kerema people have rich traditional values and live a mostly agrarian lifestyle. They have preserved their cultural identity and stories through dance, music (such as panpipers), and art. Their welcoming attitude towards visitors and strong family values are two of their prominent traits. Respect for elders, communal practices, and adherence to one’s moral values are widely respected.

Best Time to Visit Kerema

Kerema has a tropical climate, with temperatures ranging from 27°C to 32°C. The town experiences a wet season from November to April and a dry season from May to October. The best time to visit Kerema is during the dry season, from May to October, when the weather is more stable, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without any restrictions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerema

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect the local culture.
  • Ask before taking photographs of people or sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, such as the seafood and coconut-based dishes.

Don’t:

  • Break any traditional customs or offend locals.
  • Take any artifacts or items that are part of the local heritage or culture.
  • Wear revealing clothing while in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerema

Kerema is relatively safe for tourists, but visitors should always take precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night, not carrying large amounts of cash, and being aware of their surroundings. Natural hazards like flooding or river crossing can also pose a risk to visitors.

Health in Kerema

Visitors should take standard precautions such as getting vaccinated against common diseases, drinking bottled water, and avoiding raw or undercooked food. Health services and facilities in Kerema are limited and may not meet western standards, so it’s a good idea to bring necessary medicines or ask for a local medical professional’s assistance.

Getting to Kerema: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kerema

The nearest airport to Kerema is the Kerema Airport, which serves domestic flights. Visitors can fly to Port Moresby and then transfer to a domestic flight to Kerema.

Bus Stations Near Kerema

Kerema has no major bus terminals or stations. Visitors can access the town by car, taxi or local public transport vehicles.

Other Options

Travelers may also reach Kerema via boat or ferry from nearby ports or towns.

How to Get Around in Kerema

The most convenient way to get around in Kerema is by local transport, such as taxis and public vehicles which can be easily found at designated stops. Visitors may also opt for renting a car or motorbike through the help of a local guide or agency.

Where to Go Next from Kerema: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors looking to explore more of Papua New Guinea can consider visiting nearby destinations like Rabaul, Lihir Island, or the Sepik River region. These locations offer unique experiences, such as trekking active volcanoes, exploring ancient cave systems, or discovering traditional cultural sites.

Conclusion

Kerema is a beautiful destination for travelers seeking a unique, off-the-beaten-path experience. With its incredible natural beauty, vibrant culture, and a plethora of outdoor activities, Kerema has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan a fantastic trip to this stunning location.
