Introduction to Wewak

Situated in the Sepik region of Papua New Guinea, Wewak is a charming town that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. Wewak is known for its pristine beaches, stunning waterfalls, and historical landmarks that showcase the town’s rich history.

In this ultimate travel guide to Wewak, we will take you through the top places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, and the best time to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Wewak

Wewak has a lot to offer visitors, from cultural attractions to stunning natural landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit places in Wewak:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sepik Hill War Memorial - The memorial is a tribute to the Australian soldiers who fought in the battle of Wewak during World War II.
  • Wewak Cultural Centre - The cultural center showcases the diverse cultures and traditions of the Sepik region through art and objects.
  • Sepik River Bridge - The bridge, which was built during the 1930s, is a historical landmark that connects Wewak to other parts of the Sepik region.

Natural Attractions of Wewak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Korogoro Creek - The creek is home to some of the most beautiful waterfalls in the region and is ideal for swimming and picnicking.
  • Cape Wom Beach - The beach is famous for its sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and relaxing atmosphere.
  • Lorengau Market - The market is a great place to shop for locally made handicrafts, fresh produce, and seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wewak

The best way to truly experience the natural beauty of Wewak is through hiking or biking. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Wewak:

  • Manam Island - The island offers a challenging hike with breathtaking views of the sea, mountains, and surrounding areas.
  • The Sepik River - A bike ride along the river’s banks is ideal for exploring the region’s lush rainforest and picturesque villages.

Best Things to Do in Wewak: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, here are some other fun things to do in Wewak:

  • Fishing - The region is home to some of the best fishing spots in Papua New Guinea, with plenty of opportunities for barramundi and other freshwater species.
  • Island Hopping - The coastal region of Wewak has several islands surrounded by clear waters, making it an excellent destination for island hopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Wewak

Wewak’s food scene is heavily influenced by its diverse cultures. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast pork - A popular dish made with tender pork cooked to perfection.
  • Sago - A starchy dish made from sago palm that is commonly served with fish or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water - A refreshing drink that is perfect for hot tropical days.
  • Puro Super - A locally brewed beer that is a great accompaniment to any meal.

Where to Stay in Wewak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wewak offers plenty of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Wewak:

  • Wewak Boutique Hotel - A luxurious hotel that offers excellent amenities and stunning views of the ocean and Sepik River.
  • In Wewak Boutique Hotel - A budget-friendly accommodation option that offers comfortable rooms and a great location.

What to Buy in Wewak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wewak is known for its local handicrafts and unique products, making it an excellent place to shop for souvenirs. Here are some of the top products you should consider:

  • Sepik masks - A traditional mask that represents various spirits and motifs in Sepik culture.
  • Woven baskets - Beautifully woven baskets that come in various designs and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Wewak

Wewak is home to several tribes, each with their unique culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions. Engaging with locals is an excellent way to learn more about the region’s rich history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Wewak

Wewak has a tropical climate with moderate temperatures throughout the year. The best time to visit is between May and October, which is the driest season with plenty of sunshine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wewak

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts for visitors to Wewak:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take plenty of water and insect repellent for outdoor activities
  • Try the local cuisine and products

Don’t:

  • Touch or take items from cultural sites
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Cause damage to the environment or its wildlife

Safety and Health

Wewak is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions to avoid any health and safety-related issues.

Safety in Wewak

  • Always have a copy of your passport with you
  • Use a money belt to keep your valuables safe
  • Avoid traveling at night

Health in Wewak

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites
  • Get vaccinated before traveling to Wewak

Getting to Wewak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wewak is accessible by air and sea. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Wewak

  • Wewak International Airport - The airport has regular flights to Port Moresby and other local destinations.

Bus Stations Near Wewak

  • Wewak Bus Depot - The depot offers bus services to local destinations.

Other Options

  • Taxis - Taxis are available for hire in Wewak.

How to Get Around in Wewak

The best way to get around Wewak is by hiring a car or a taxi. Cycling is also an option for those who prefer to explore the town’s attractions at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Wewak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Wewak, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Manus Island - A beautiful island that offers some of the best snorkeling and diving experiences in Papua New Guinea.
  • Madang - A coastal town that is famous for its underwater reefs, pristine beaches, and colorful flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Wewak is a hidden gem in Papua New Guinea that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. If you are looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination for your next vacation, Wewak should be at the top of your list. With this comprehensive guide, you will have all the information you need to plan an unforgettable adventure in Wewak.
