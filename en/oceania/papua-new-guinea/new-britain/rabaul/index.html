Introduction to Rabaul

Rabaul is a picturesque town located on the eastern end of New Britain Island in Papua New Guinea, Oceania. Known for its stunning natural scenery, vibrant culture, and rich history, Rabaul is a popular destination for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and cultural exploration.

The town was built on the rim of a massive caldera, and evidence of volcanic activity is visible all around. A series of eruptions in the 1990s devastated much of the town, but it has since been rebuilt, making it an interesting blend of the old and the new.

List of Places to Visit in Rabaul

There are countless attractions to explore in and around Rabaul, catering to a variety of interests. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Discover the rich cultural heritage of the region by visiting some of Rabaul’s museums and historical sites. Be sure to check out:

  • The Rabaul Museum: A wealth of information about the town’s history and culture, including a tribute to the war veterans.
  • The Yamamoto Peace Memorial: A tribute to the Japanese admiral Isoroku Yamamoto who lost his life during World War II.
  • The Baining Cultural Center: A fascinating glimpse into the unique culture and traditions of the Baining people.

Natural Attractions of Rabaul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rabaul is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, providing ample opportunities for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Don’t miss these natural attractions:

  • Mount Tavurvur: Hike to the summit of this still-active volcano and experience the stunning views of the surrounding islands and ocean.
  • Hot Springs: Relax in the therapeutic waters of the geothermal hot springs located in the nearby jungle.
  • Simpson Harbour: Take a boat tour around the harbour to see the remnants of sunken ships from World War II and experience the beauty of the region from the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rabaul

For those who love to hike or bike, Rabaul offers some of the best routes in Papua New Guinea. Here are some of the top trails:

  • The Kokopo War and Reconstruction Trail: A 10km route that takes you through the city’s war and reconstruction history and provides stunning views.
  • Mount Kapnarun Trail: A challenging hike to the summit of Mount Kapnarun, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • The Rabaul Volcano Tour: A popular tour that takes hikers to the peaks of the local volcanoes, providing jaw-dropping views of the town and the harbor.

Best Things to Do in Rabaul: Activities and Tours

There is never a shortage of things to do in Rabaul. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Scuba Diving: Explore the rich marine life and coral reefs surrounding the islands.
  • Snorkeling: If you aren’t a scuba diver, try snorkeling to see the underwater world of the region.
  • Cultural Tours: Discover local customs and traditions by going on an organized tour and seeing how locals live.
  • Bird Watching: Papua New Guinea is known for its diverse and unique bird species, and Rabaul is no exception.

Food and Drink Scene of Rabaul

No trip to Rabaul is complete without trying some local food and drink. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mumu: A traditional dish made by cooking meat, vegetables, and fruits in an underground pit.
  • Kokoda: Fresh fish marinated in coconut cream and lime juice, a local delicacy.
  • Kaukau: A type of sweet potato that is a staple in Papua New Guinea.

Local Beverages

  • SP Beer: This beer is made locally and is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.
  • Coconut water: This refreshing drink is available everywhere and is an excellent way to quench your thirst in the hot climate.

Where to Stay in Rabaul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rabaul offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Gazelle International Hotel: One of the most luxurious hotels in Rabaul, with a stunning ocean view and excellent facilities.
  • Rabaul Hotel: A budget-friendly option, offering clean, comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • Kabaira Beach Hideaway: A unique eco-resort set on a tranquil beach, providing a serene and relaxing environment.

What to Buy in Rabaul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some of the best options:

  • Tapa cloth: A type of bark cloth used in traditional clothing and decorative items.
  • Carvings: Wood carvings are a popular souvenir in Papua New Guinea, and you can find some beautiful and intricate pieces in Rabaul.
  • Traditional jewelry: Made from shells and other natural materials, these pieces are beautiful and unique.

Local Culture and Customs of Rabaul

Rabaul has a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly: Papua New Guineans take modesty seriously, and it is best to dress conservatively while in Rabaul.
  • Remove footwear: It is customary to remove shoes before entering a home or a place of worship.
  • Show respect: Papua New Guineans value politeness and essential social norms such as greeting people when you meet them.

Best Time to Visit Rabaul

The best time to visit Rabaul is between May and October, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the region is humid and hot year-round, so be prepared to cope with the heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rabaul

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Rabaul:

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Travel with a guide or a group if exploring outside the town.
  • Try local food and beverages for an authentic experience.

Don’t:

  • Make unwanted physical contact or take photographs without permission.
  • Leave your belongings unattended or carry large amounts of cash.
  • Waste natural resources, such as water and electricity.

Safety and Health

Rabaul is a safe destination for travelers, but it is always good to take precautions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Rabaul

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight. If you’re out and about, bring only what you need and leave the rest of your belongings in a safe location.
  • Be cautious around dogs and other animals.
  • Take necessary precautions when hiking or biking, including wearing sturdy shoes and carrying a first-aid kit.

Health in Rabaul

  • Bring insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites, which can carry diseases like malaria and dengue fever.
  • Drink only bottled or purified water to avoid waterborne illnesses.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect against the harsh sun.

Getting to Rabaul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Rabaul requires a bit of planning, as transportation options can be limited. Here are some options:

  • Air travel: The Rabaul Airport is serviced by major airlines, including Air Niugini, and connects to other cities within Papua New Guinea and beyond.
  • Boat travel: Several boats depart from the mainland to Rabaul weekly, but be prepared for a longer journey.

Airports Near Rabaul

The primary airport near Rabaul is the Rabaul Airport, located about 10 kilometers from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Rabaul

Buses and taxis are available for hire in Rabaul, but there isn’t a dedicated bus station.

Other Options

Shared taxis or Minibuses are common for travel in Papua New Guinea, but they aren’t always reliable. Another option is to hire a private car or taxi.

How to Get Around in Rabaul

Traveling around Rabaul is easiest by foot or hiring a car or taxi. Buses and minibusses are also available but may not always follow a strict schedule.

Where to Go Next from Rabaul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rabaul is an excellent starting point for exploring the rest of Papua New Guinea. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Kokopo: A nearby town offering stunning beaches and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Kimbe Bay: A once hidden, but now popular, diving spot with a stunning underwater world.
  • Buka Island: An island with a rich history and culture, along with beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Rabaul offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and adventure activities, making it an excellent destination for any traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan a trip to Rabaul that includes the best places to visit, things to do, and food to try, while ensuring your stay remains safe, comfortable, and filled with memories.
