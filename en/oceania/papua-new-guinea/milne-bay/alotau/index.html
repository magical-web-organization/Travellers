Introduction to Alotau

Alotau is a picturesque town nestled on the south-eastern coast of Papua New Guinea, in the province of Milne Bay, Oceania. The town is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and wartime history. Alotau is the gateway to the surrounding islands and adventure activities making it a great place to start your journey.

List of Places to Visit in Alotau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alotau Cultural Festival: Held every year in November, this festival showcases the traditional dances, music, and crafts of the Milne Bay Region.

  • Milne Bay War Memorial: This memorial honors the brave soldiers who fought against the Japanese during World War II.

  • Esa’ala Caves: These sacred limestone caves are of great cultural significance to the local people and invite visitors to explore every corner.

Natural Attractions of Alotau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kapwala Island: This island offers stunning snorkeling and scuba diving sites with colorful coral reefs and schools of fish.

  • Trobriand Islands: Famous for its yam harvest festival, these pristine islands present a unique cultural experience.

  • Losuia Island: This charming island presents an opportunity to swim in crystal clear water, hike to stunning viewpoints, and encounter captivating birdlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alotau

  • Milne Bay Provincial Hill View offers fantastic views of the surrounding area and is a great spot for a picnic.

  • Kwato Church and Grotto Trail offers a historical and spiritual experience with a one-hour easy walk.

  • Blanche Bay to Saidor Track offers an adventurous trek with picturesque views of the coastline and forest.

Best Things to Do in Alotau: Activities and Tours

  • Milne Bay Canoe and Snorkel Tour: Head off from Samarai Island on a guided canoe trip and snorkel and discover the pristine coral reefs.

  • Trobriand Islands Cultural Tour: Experience a unique local and cultural perspective with this guided tour.

  • Fishing Trip with Local Villagers: Discover fishing techniques and skills with the local guides and enjoy the catch of the day.

Food and Drink Scene of Alotau

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaukau and Sago: A traditional dish made of yam and sago, served with fresh fish and vegetables.

  • Papua New Guinean Curry: A unique twist on traditional curry that uses ingredients native to the area, including homemade coconut milk and shrimp.

  • Lap Lap: A must-try local dish made with taro or banana, meat, and coconut milk, which is wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Kokoda- A refreshing drink made from coconut cream and pineapple juice.

  • Sago Palm Wine - A traditional local wine made in the region from fermented sago palm sap. It’s a unique taste that can be found in the local market.

  • SP Beer - The local beer made from Papua New Guinea offers a classic beer taste that is a must-try when in the area.

Where to Stay in Alotau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Masurina Lodge: Located in the center of Alotau, this affordable lodge offers comfortable stays including breakfast and basic amenities.

  • Alotau Beach Resort: Overlooking the Milne Bay, this resort offers beautiful views, delicious meals, and great hospitality.

  • Napatana Lodge: Located on the beach, this family-owned hotel offers authentic experiences of local dining, culture, and adventure.

What to Buy in Alotau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wood Carvings and Masks: Alotau’s famous for its wood carvings and masks featured in cultural festivals and unique artwork.

  • Traditional Weaves: The unique fabrics made by local artisans are popular souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Alotau

The local culture of Alotau is diverse and rich in history. Each tribe has their customs and beliefs, making it a fascinating destination to understand and experience the many different traditions. Visitors should respect local customs, such as covering up and removing their shoes before entering homes, and not taking photos without permission.

Best Time to Visit Alotau

The best time to visit Alotau is from June to September when there are fewer rainfall and the climate is cooler, making it easier to explore the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alotau

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress conservatively when visiting villages.

  • Give gifts to your host family as a show of goodwill.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without asking for permission.

  • Disrupt the religious ceremonies.

  • Touch the head of a local person, as it is considered a sign of disrespect.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alotau

While Alotau is generally safe, visitors should take precautions against theft and robbery. It’s recommended to avoid carrying large amounts of money or valuables and stay vigilant when walking alone at night.

Health in Alotau

Malaria is prevalent in Papua New Guinea, therefore it is best to take precautions and consult with a healthcare provider before visiting.

Getting to Alotau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alotau

Gurney Airport is the closest airport to Alotau and is located around 10 kilometers from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Alotau

There are buses available for travel within Alotau, plus it is worth considering private transport options or taxi services for other areas.

Other Options

The ferry crossing from Port Moresby is another option, with once a week departure. It takes about 30 hours to reach Alotau from the capital.

How to Get Around in Alotau

For a visit to Alotau, travelers should consider hiring a local taxi, using public transportation, or renting a car to explore further afield. Boats can also be hired to explore the surrounding islands while a bicycle is another great option for exploring nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Alotau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kimbe Bay: A vibrant location with an exotic underwater environment for scuba diving and snorkeling.

  • Port Moresby: The capital city is quite busy, with significant historical and cultural sites of interest.

  • Rabaul: A beautiful tropical paradise, offering picturesque sceneries with a dense mix of history, adventure, and relaxation.

Conclusion

Alotau is an amazing destination, with its diverse culture, rich history, stunning natural scenery, and outdoor activities. It offers a unique blend of nature and culture that visitors will forever admire. Ensure to use this guide for your upcoming visit to make the most of your time and have a safe and enjoyable trip.
