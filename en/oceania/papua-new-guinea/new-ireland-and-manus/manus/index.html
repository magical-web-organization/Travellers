Introduction to Manus

Manus is an idyllic island nestled off the coast of Papua New Guinea. The island is home to tropical rainforests, pristine beaches, and colorful coral reefs. It’s a travel destination sought after by adventurers, divers, and nature enthusiasts.

Manus is known for its warm hospitality, rich culture, and captivating wildlife. If you’re planning to visit this untouched paradise, we’ve got you covered. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover all the best places to stay, things to do, where to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Manus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visiting Manus is an opportunity to experience Papua New Guinea’s diverse culture. The island boasts a significant history, and there are several cultural landmarks worth exploring.

One of these historical sites is the Lorengau WWII Museum built on the remains of an airfield used by the Japanese during WWII. You’ll get a chance to see the aircraft that showed the ruthlessness demonstrated on Manus Island during WWII.

Another popular historical site is the Catholic Church in Lorengau Town, which dates back to the 1950s. There’s also the Manus Provincial Government Building, built in the 1970s, which features intricate wood carvings and traditional artwork by local artists.

Natural Attractions of Manus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Manus is also a haven for nature enthusiasts. From the lush rainforests to the crystal clear waters, the island offers plenty of natural attractions to explore.

The Hap Island Marine Park is one of the best places to experience the island’s marine life. You’ll be blown away by the kaleidoscope of colors displayed by the corals and thousands of marine species.

There’s also the Bipi Island Bird Sanctuary, where you’ll come across exotic species of birds like the hornbills and cockatoos. If you’re lucky, you might spot the elusive roosting bats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manus

Hiking is one of the best ways to experience the island’s intriguing terrain. There are a variety of hiking trails you can explore, ranging from easy to moderate.

One of the popular hiking destinations is Mount Dremsel, an extinct volcano that provides a breathtaking view of the island from the top. You can also opt for a hike through the local rainforests.

Biking routes are also available, and there’s no better way to tour the island while experiencing the cool coastal breeze.

Best Things to Do in Manus: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to indulge in while on the island of Manus. You can opt for kayaking or paddleboarding at the beaches while exploring the island’s serene surroundings.

If you want to engage with the locals and immerse yourself in their culture, there’s the Melanesian Feast, which offers traditional food, songs, and dance performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Manus

Must-Try Dishes

Manus Island boasts a rich culinary experience, with fresh seafood, exotic fruits, and locally produced vegetables. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Boumba: A traditional Manus dish made with taro leaves, coconut cream, and smoked fish.
  • Pota: A sweet potato pudding spiced with exotic flavors like vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  • Roasted Sago Grubs: A protein-rich dish prepared from sago grubs roasted to perfection.

Local Beverages

There are plenty of tropical drinks to try on Manus, with coconut milk being a primary ingredient in most of their drinks. You can also opt for fresh fruit juices, refreshing smoothies, and brewed coffee.

Where to Stay in Manus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manus offers a variety of hotels and guesthouses catering to different budgets. Some of the recommended accommodations include Seeadler Bay Hotel, Manus Paradise Lodge, and Tree Top Lodge. For backpackers, Comfort Inn Manus and Jais Aben Resort offer excellent budget-friendly options.

What to Buy in Manus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Manus offers plenty of souvenir options, including PNG carvings, woven bags, traditional jewelry, and pottery items. You can find these at local markets or souvenir shops in town.

Local Culture and Customs of Manus

The locals on Manus are warm and welcoming. They take pride in their culture and enjoy showcasing their traditions to visitors.

It’s essential to show respect for their culture by dressing modestly and avoiding revealing clothing. It’s also a sign of respect to ask for permission before taking photos of the locals or their habitat.

Best Time to Visit Manus

The best time to visit Manus is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The climate during this period is comfortable, with sunny skies and occasional drizzles. You’ll also get to witness the Manus Day celebrations, which takes place every year on the 15th of August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manus

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Pidgin or Tok Pisin, the most common language spoken by locals.

Don’t:

  • Touch anything on an altar within a church.
  • Intrude into any local cultural event or take photos without permission.
  • Wander alone in darker, quieter streets at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manus

Manus is a relatively safe travel destination with friendly locals always willing to help visitors. However, it’s important to remain vigilant, especially in public areas like markets and busy streets. Avoid carrying large amounts of money or valuables around, and opt for a money belt or purse that displays some level of waterproofing or firm, durable materials.

Health in Manus

It’s always best to receive vaccines for diseases like hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and polio before traveling to Manus. Also, pack an adequate supply of essential medication or consult with a travel clinic advisor before leaving.

Getting to Manus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manus

Manus Airport – An airport on the north side of Manus Island is the main transport hub for visitors traveling to Manus Island.

Bus Stations Near Manus

There are no bus stations near Manus. Most visitors will explore the island on foot or by bike.

Other Options

Ferries from the main island of Papua New Guinea are also an option, but there is no regular schedule, meaning it may take an unspecified amount of time to arrive.

How to Get Around in Manus

Most visitors to the island rely on walking, biking, or hiring a canoe. Motorbikes are also available for hire at budget prices.

Where to Go Next from Manus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the neighboring islands less than 100 km from Manus include Wuvulu Island, Rambutyo Island, and the Hermit Islands. Visitors can join guided boat tours to explore these destinations.

Conclusion

Manus is a beautiful travel destination that offers a magical experience to its visitors. Boasting a rich culture, diverse wildlife, and picturesque views, there’s something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your dream holiday and explore everything that Manus has to offer.
