Introduction to New Ireland and Manus

New Ireland and Manus are two of the most stunning provinces in Papua New Guinea, located in the Oceania region. These two provinces are known for their untouched natural beauty, rich marine life, and fascinating cultural heritage. There’s something for everyone here, whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about traveling to New Ireland and Manus, including places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in New Ireland and Manus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the cultural history of New Ireland and Manus, you won’t be disappointed. These provinces have a rich history dating back centuries, and there are many museums and historical places to explore. Here are some of the most notable ones:

  • Malagan Beach Warwagira
  • Namatanai Museum
  • Manus Provincial Cultural Centre
  • Lihir Island Historic Heritage Park
  • Kavieng War Memorial

Natural Attractions of New Ireland and Manus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main attractions of New Ireland and Manus is the stunning natural landscape. From rainforests to coral reefs and waterfalls, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the natural attractions you shouldn’t miss:

  • Nusa Lik Island
  • Emirau Island
  • Tsoi Island
  • Lissenung Island
  • Lavongai (New Hanover) Island

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Ireland and Manus

New Ireland and Manus are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a leisurely stroll or a challenging hike, there are many trails to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  • Lihir Island
  • Cocobeach
  • Kavieng Lagoon Nature Park
  • Nusa Lik Island
  • Tinetalgo Waterfall

Best Things to Do in New Ireland and Manus: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in New Ireland and Manus. Whether you want to explore the cultural heritage, go on an outdoor adventure, or relax on the beach, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Snorkeling and diving
  • Surfing
  • Fishing
  • Kayaking
  • Bird watching

Week-Long Itinerary for New Ireland and Manus

If you’re planning a week-long trip to New Ireland and Manus, here’s an itinerary you can follow:

Day 1 - Arrive in Port Moresby and take a domestic flight to Kavieng. Day 2 - Explore Kavieng town and the War Memorial. Day 3 - Take a boat tour to Nusa Lik Island and enjoy the beautiful scenery. Day 4 - Go on a snorkeling or diving tour in Lissenung Island. Day 5 - Visit Namatanai Museum and Manus Provincial Cultural Centre. Day 6 - Hike to Tinetalgo Waterfall and Cocobeach. Day 7 - Depart for Port Moresby.

Local Culture and Customs of New Ireland and Manus

New Ireland and Manus have a rich and fascinating cultural heritage. The locals here are friendly and welcoming, but it’s important to respect their customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting villages.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos.
  • Don’t touch or step over things that are considered sacred.
  • Greet people with a handshake or by saying “Hello.”
  • Don’t point with your finger. Use your chin instead.

Best Time to Visit New Ireland and Manus

The best time to visit New Ireland and Manus is between May and October, when the weather is dry and sunny. This is also the peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodations and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Ireland and Manus

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Stay hydrated.
  • Protect yourself from the sun.
  • Bring insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Eat street food.
  • Drink tap water.
  • Forget to pack sunscreen.
  • Wander alone at night.
  • Touch or step over things that are considered sacred.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Ireland and Manus

New Ireland and Manus are generally safe places to visit, but it’s always important to stay alert and aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips to stay safe:

  • Avoid carrying large sums of money with you.
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended.
  • Be cautious when walking alone at night.
  • Use reputable tour operators.
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in New Ireland and Manus

It’s important to take precautions to protect your health in New Ireland and Manus. Here are some tips:

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Wash your hands regularly.
  • Use insect repellent.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to New Ireland and Manus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Ireland and Manus

The main airport serving New Ireland and Manus is Kavieng Airport.

Bus Stations Near New Ireland and Manus

There are no bus stations in New Ireland and Manus. However, taxis are readily available.

Other Options

You can also get to New Ireland and Manus by boat, but this is less common.

How to Get Around in New Ireland and Manus

The best way to get around New Ireland and Manus is by taxi or rental car. However, if you’re feeling adventurous, you can also hire a bike or take a boat tour.
