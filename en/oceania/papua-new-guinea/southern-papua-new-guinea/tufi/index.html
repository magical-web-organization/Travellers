Introduction to Tufi

Tufi is a picturesque town located on the coast of Oro Province in Papua New Guinea’s Southern region. The town is built on a steep coastal hillside, offering visitors stunning views of the clear blue waters of the Tufi fjords. With its serene and tranquil environment, Tufi is an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Tufi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tufi Cultural Centre: The Centre offers visitors a glimpse into the traditional way of life of Papua New Guinea’s Sirovai people.
  2. Tufi War Memorial: The Memorial honors the fallen heroes who fought in the World War II battles of Tufi.
  3. Tufi Lookout: The Lookout provides stunning panoramic views of the fjords, the mountains, and the town.

Natural Attractions of Tufi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tufi Dive Resort: The Dive Resort offers water adventures such as diving, snorkeling, and kayaking.
  2. Tufi Hilltop Lookout: The Lookout is an excellent spot for sunrise and sunset views and birdwatching.
  3. Fjord Tour: The Fjord Tour takes visitors on a boat ride through the fjords, where you can swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tufi

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Tufi offers several trails, including:

  1. Tufi Village Trek: This trek takes you through the lush forest, past rivers and waterfalls, and traditional villages.
  2. Tufi-Koropi Trek: This trek takes between four to six hours and offers stunning views of the fjords from the top of the mountain.

Best Things to Do in Tufi: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do and see in Tufi, including:

  1. Scuba Diving: Tufi is known for its exciting underwater world, with a rich marine life, and several wrecks to explore.
  2. Snorkeling: The waters of the fjords are crystal clear and are teeming with colorful coral reefs and a range of marine animals.
  3. Kayaking: Rent a kayak or a paddleboard and explore the calm fjord waters.
  4. Cultural Tours: Visit a traditional Sirovai village and experience the lifestyle of the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Tufi

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kokoda: This is a traditional dish of raw fish marinated in lime juice and mixed with tomatoes and onions.
  2. Pota: This is a dish made from cooked taro leaves, mixed with coconut cream and seasoned with salt, and served with fish or meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Coconut water: Fresh coconut water is a favorite local beverage, and it’s widely available throughout Tufi.
  2. Coffee: Papua New Guinea is known worldwide for its coffee, and Tufi offers excellent locally brewed coffee.

Where to Stay in Tufi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Tufi, including:

  1. Tufi Dive Resort: The Resort offers comfortable guest rooms and excellent dive packages.
  2. Tufi Retreat: The Retreat offers rustic accommodation and stunning views of the fjords.
  3. Tufi Guest House: The Guest House offers budget-friendly accommodation and a warm welcoming environment.

What to Buy in Tufi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Carvings: Papua New Guinea is known for its intricate wood carvings that depict local legends, animals, and traditional tales.
  2. Weavings: Locally woven baskets and mats are fantastic souvenirs to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tufi

The people of Tufi are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the customs, traditions, and beliefs of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Tufi

The best time to visit Tufi is between April to November. This time is considered the dry season, with less rainfall and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tufi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Use ecological sound practices and preserve the natural environment.
  • Carry cash with you, as ATMs are not widely available.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes, as it may offend some locals.
  • Take photographs without seeking permission.
  • Purchase illegal souvenirs made from protected wildlife.

## Safety and Health

Safety in Tufi

Tufi is a relatively safe destination, but visitors are advised to take a few precautions, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep valuables safely stored in the hotel.
  • Follow the instructions of the tour guide carefully.

Health in Tufi

Visitors should take malaria prophylaxis before traveling to Tufi. It’s recommended to carry a mosquito net and insect repellent, as mosquitoes are prevalent in the area.

Getting to Tufi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tufi

Tufi airport (TFI) is the nearest airport to Tufi, located at a distance of 16 kilometers from the town. It’s serviced by Air Niugini and PNG Air.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Tufi via boat from Oro Bay or by road via Popondetta.

How to Get Around in Tufi

Visitors can get around Tufi on foot or use local transport, which includes buses, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Tufi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Milne Bay: Milne Bay is another beautiful coastal destination known for its stunning coral reefs and pristine beaches.
  2. Kokoda Track: Experienced hikers can take on the challenging Kokoda Track, which passes through dense forests and rugged terrain.

Conclusion

Tufi is an idyllic destination that offers travelers a blend of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. From scuba diving to cultural tours, there’s something to suit every visitor’s interest. With its friendly locals and tranquil surroundings, Tufi is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
