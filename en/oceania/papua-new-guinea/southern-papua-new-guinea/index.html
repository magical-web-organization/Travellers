Introduction to Southern Papua New Guinea

Located in Oceania, Southern Papua New Guinea is a stunning region that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Known for its rugged terrain, pristine beaches, and diverse flora and fauna, this region is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With the majority of the population belonging to indigenous tribes, Southern Papua New Guinea is also rich in ancient history and cultural traditions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Papua New Guinea

Southern Papua New Guinea is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The region has several trekking and cycling trails that offer breathtaking views of lush tropical forests, mountain ranges, and pristine rivers. The most popular hiking and biking trails in Southern Papua New Guinea include:

  • The Kokoda Track
  • The Black Cat Track
  • The Lakekamu Basin Trail
  • The Highlands Highway

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Papua New Guinea

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Southern Papua New Guinea is diverse and has a blend of cultures. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Mumu (a traditional steamed dish consisting of pork, chicken, and vegetables cooked in an underground oven)
  • Sago (a starchy staple made from an extract of the sago palm)
  • Lap Lap (a dish made of grated taro and sometimes mixed with chicken, beef, pork or fish with coconut cream)

Local Beverages

Southern Papua New Guinea also has some unique and refreshing drinks that are worth trying:

  • Coconut Water
  • Banana Beer or Wine
  • Kava

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Papua New Guinea

Southern Papua New Guinea is home to several indigenous tribes that have preserved their ancient cultures and customs for centuries. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Sing-sing (music and dance festivals)
  • Bride Price (a custom where the groom’s family pays a bride price to the bride’s family)
  • Sepik River Crocodile Ceremony (a ritual that marks the importance of crocodiles in the local culture)

Best Time to Visit Southern Papua New Guinea

The best time to visit Southern Papua New Guinea is from May to October when the weather is dry and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Papua New Guinea

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Southern Papua New Guinea:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Wear modest clothing when visiting religious or cultural sites
  • Learn a few local words and phrases

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission
  • Wear flashy jewelry or expensive watches
  • Wander off the designated hiking trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Papua New Guinea

Southern Papua New Guinea is generally a safe place for tourists, but it is advised to take necessary precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Do not display valuable items or cash in public places
  • Hire a reputable local guide for hikes and tours

Health in Southern Papua New Guinea

Southern Papua New Guinea is in a malaria zone, so it’s advised to take anti-malaria medication before and during your trip.

Getting to Southern Papua New Guinea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Papua New Guinea

The main international airports in Southern Papua New Guinea are located in Port Moresby and Mount Hagen.

Bus Stations Near Southern Papua New Guinea

There are no bus services that connect Southern Papua New Guinea to other regions or countries.

Other Options

The only way to enter Southern Papua New Guinea is by air or water.

How to Get Around in Southern Papua New Guinea

The best way to get around Southern Papua New Guinea is by hiring a car with a driver or booking tours with local operators.
