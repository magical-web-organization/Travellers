A Comprehensive Guide to Port Moresby - Southern Papua New Guinea

If you’re planning a trip to Port Moresby in Southern Papua New Guinea, you’ve come to the right place. This comprehensive guide will provide you with everything you need to know about the city, including the top things to do, where to stay, what to eat, safety tips, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Port Moresby

Port Moresby is the capital city of Papua New Guinea, a country situated in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. The city is located on the southeastern coast of Papua New Guinea and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Port Moresby

There are many places to visit in Port Moresby. Whether you’re interested in museums and historical sites or outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The National Museum and Art Gallery: A must-visit for those interested in Papua New Guinea’s history and culture. It features a collection of traditional artifacts, contemporary art, and natural history exhibits.
  2. Bomana War Cemetery: A solemn tribute to the soldiers who lost their lives during World War II.
  3. Parliament House: A visually stunning modern structure built in the shape of a traditional Sepik Haus Tambaran.

Natural Attractions of Port Moresby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Adventure Park PNG: A fantastic place for families with kids. This park features a variety of outdoor activities, including zip-lining, go-karting, and miniature golf.
  2. Varirata National Park: A scenic nature reserve known for its abundant birdlife and breathtaking panoramic views of the city.
  3. Port Moresby Nature Park: A wildlife preserve featuring an exotic collection of birds, reptiles, and marsupials.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Moresby

Port Moresby is home to some amazing hiking and biking trails, and are must-dos for nature enthusiasts. Some of the best include but are not limited to:

  1. Kokoda Track: A challenging, multi-day trek through some of Papua New Guinea’s most rugged and beautiful terrain.
  2. Mount Victoria: A popular hiking destination with panoramic views of the city.
  3. Varirata National Park Trails: A range of hiking trails through the park’s lush rainforest and grasslands.

Best Things to Do in Port Moresby: Activities and Tours

  1. Go snorkeling at Loloata Island.
  2. Experience the local culture and cuisine with a guided food tour.
  3. Take a sunset cruise on the pristine waters of the Pacific Ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Moresby

For a week-long itinerary for Port Moresby, we suggest the following:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Port Moresby

The food scene in Port Moresby is diverse and vibrant, with plenty of local and international options. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Mumu: Traditional slow-cooked meat and vegetables cooked in an underground pit.
  2. Kokoda: A delicious seafood dish made with coconut milk and lime juice.
  3. Chicken Curry: A spicy curry made with local spices, chicken, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. SP Lager: Papua New Guinea’s best-known beer.
  2. Hot Mocha: A popular coffee beverage made with sweetened chocolate and milk.
  3. Coconut Water: Refreshing, hydrating, and full of electrolytes, coconut water is a staple beverage in Papua New Guinea.

Where to Stay in Port Moresby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options available in Port Moresby, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some popular choices include:

  1. Holiday Inn Express Port Moresby
  2. Airways Hotel
  3. The Stanley Hotel and Suites

What to Buy in Port Moresby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, some popular options include:

  1. Bilum Bags: A colorful and sturdy traditional woven bag.
  2. Carvings and Artwork: Beautiful and intricate locally made artworks.
  3. Pukpuk: A wooden carved statue symbolizing adventure and bravery.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Moresby

Papua New Guinea has over 800 different tribal cultures, each with their unique customs and traditions. It’s essential to respect these customs and traditions when visiting Port Moresby.

Best Time to Visit Port Moresby

The best time to visit Port Moresby is from May to October when the weather is dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Moresby

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress modestly.
  3. Be prepared for the city’s high crime rate.

Don’t:

  1. Take photos without permission.
  2. Walk alone at night.
  3. Carry large amounts of cash or valuables.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Moresby

Port Moresby has a high crime rate, and visitors must take appropriate safety precautions.

Health in Port Moresby

Visitors should take malaria prophylaxis and be up to date on all routine vaccinations.

Getting to Port Moresby: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to Port Moresby:

Airports Near Port Moresby

Jacksons International Airport is the primary airport serving Port Moresby.

Bus Stations Near Port Moresby

Some international buses operate in and out of the city, but most locals use minibusses or taxis.

Other Options

Many people arrive by boat or by private vehicle.

How to Get Around in Port Moresby

Public transportation options are generally unpredictable and unsafe, and visitors should use taxis or hire a private car and driver.

Where to Go Next from Port Moresby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, some nearby destinations to check out include:

  1. Milne Bay: A beautiful and remote coastal province with pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  2. Rabaul: A historic town located on the eastern side of Papua New Guinea, known for its volcanoes, hot springs, and diving spots.
  3. Mount Hagen: The second-largest city in Papua New Guinea, home to unique cultural experiences and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Port Moresby is an exciting and beautiful destination that’s worth the journey. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have a safe, fun, and memorable experience exploring the city’s unique culture, traditions, and outdoor wonders.
