Introduction to Chatham Islands

Located in New Zealand, Chatham Islands is an archipelago made up of ten islands, the two largest of which are Chatham Island and Pitt Island. It is one of the most remote and isolated regions in the world, with a unique culture and ecosystem. Here’s our comprehensive tour guide to help you explore the best of Chatham Islands.

List of Places to Visit in Chatham Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chatham Islands Museum: The museum showcases the social and natural history of the islands, chronicling the lives of the Moriori people and European settlers.
  • Te One School Museum: The Te One School Museum offers visitors insights into the island’s education system through the ages and the country’s communication system.
  • Owenga Rd Historic Reserve: The site holds special cultural significance as it is an area that depicts the stories of the Maori and Moriori people, who have inhabited the lands for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Chatham Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waitangi Wharf: This wharf overlooks the Pacific Ocean, and offers you the chance to spot seabirds such as the black petrel and many more.
  • Tarakohe Beach: The golden sand beach offers swimming, shellfish gathering and a peaceful place to soak up the island’s temperate climate.
  • Grey’s Beach: At low tide, you can step on the reef and see the spectacular marine life underneath, including rock lobsters, starfish, and paua or abalone.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chatham Islands

Explore Chatham Island’s scenic beauty by following one of its many hiking or biking trails. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Heritage Trail: The gentle 2.5km (1.5 miles) trail meanders through the Waitangi West Reserve, following the coast and agricultural lands.
  • Hapupu Reserve Track: This moderate 5km (3 miles) trail takes you to the largest remaining native forest on the island, where you can see a variety of plant and birdlife.
  • Maunganui Stone Cottage Walk: This leisurely guided walk takes you to the oldest house in the region, which was built by the island’s first missionary, and offers stunning views of the coast and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Chatham Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Chatham Island is renowned for fishing spots such as the Tuku estuary and Pitt Island, where visitors can catch a variety of fish such as snapper, crayfish, and kahawai.
  • Whale watching: The seas around Chatham Islands are home to populations of southern right whale and humpback whale, making it a popular destination for whale watchers.
  • Chatham Island Tours: Join island locals on a fascinating guided tour and learn about the island’s history, geography, agriculture, and the unique way of life of its people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chatham Islands

Day 1: Visit the Chatham Islands Museum and the Te One School Museum

Day 2: Take a hike along the Heritage Trail and relax at Waitangi Wharf

Day 3: Travel to Pitt Island and explore the older forest of the Hapupu Reserve Track

Day 4: Experience rock lobster and crayfish fishing

Day 5: Go whale watching, followed by a visit to Grey’s Beach

Day 6: Join a tour to experience local highlights such as the local winery, among others.

Day 7: End your trip with souvenir shopping and a meal featuring local cuisines.

Food and Drink Scene of Chatham Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paua Fritters: Soft and juicy, paua or abalone fritters are available at most of the local restaurants and cafes.
  • Crayfish Soup: The dish is made with fresh crayfish flavoured with locally grown herbs amongst others.
  • Fried crab rolls: They are usually made with locally sourced crabs, seasoned with shredded ginger and spring onions, served with soy sauce and vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Moa Brewery Kawa Kawa Pale Ale - Brewed with the local kawa kawa plant, providing a unique and earthy taste.
  • Chatham Island Wine: Wine-lovers will delight in this region’s range of award-winning vintages.

Where to Stay in Chatham Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Chathams: The Hotel Chathams, located close to the airport, places visitors within easy reach of shops and cafes.
  • Chatham Islands Motel - Quiet and cosy motel where you can relax and rejuvenate while enjoying the island’s serenity.
  • Torere B&B Guesthouse - Combines local authenticity with luxurious lodgings with a beach view.

What to Buy in Chatham Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kari kari: Small decorative carved objects crafted from bone, shells, and greenstone.
  • Muka: Traditional cords made from harakeke or flax, that are used to make mats, baskets and other crafts.
  • Zebra Stone: A unique stone that is only found on the island, showcasing the unique geological features of Chatham Islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Chatham Islands

The indigenous people of the Chatham Islands are the Moriori and Maori people, and their cultures continue to be an integral and revered part of the island’s unique identity.

Best Time to Visit Chatham Islands

The best time to visit Chatham Islands is from October to May, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 20°C. The period is the peak season due to favourable weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chatham Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take precautions to protect the fragile ecosystem
  • Be grateful for the unique isolation experience

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or litter the island
  • Trespass in any prohibited areas, either public or private
  • Bring non-indigenous plants and animals to the island

Safety and Health

Safety in Chatham Islands

In emergency situations, call the police in case of any emergency. More tourist information can be acquired from the Visitor Information Centre. However, like other places, it is important to practice basic safety precautions such as securing your valuables, knowing the terrain before hikes, etc.

Health in Chatham Islands

Medical care for the locals is rather basic, with only one hospital on the island. However, most medicine stores can handle minor illnesses and injuries.

Getting to Chatham Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chatham Islands

  • Chatham Islands / Tuuta Airport: This airport is close to the town of Waitangi and offers flights from Wellington and Auckland.

Bus Stations Near Chatham Islands

There are no bus services on the islands.

Other Options

There are no ferry connections to Chatham Islands, hence you either need to take a flight or a chartered boat.

How to Get Around in Chatham Islands

The best way to explore the islands is by car hire, though guided tours, bike rentals, and hiking are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from Chatham Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pitt Island: The second-largest island in the group, with pristine beaches and untouched wilderness.
  • Taumata Island: Home to rare and endemic birds, plant species, and spectacular scenery.

Conclusion

Chatham Islands boasts a unique culture and environment that’s unmatched anywhere else globally. Our comprehensive travel guide provides you with detailed information on different activities, cuisine, transport options, and the local culture to ensure you have a once in a lifetime experience here. Be sure to plan your trip and explore the islands at your pace!
