Introduction to Piha

Piha is a small laid back coastal village, located in the Auckland Region of New Zealand’s North Island. The location is nestled amidst natural surroundings and has a magnificent black sand beach that attracts travelers from across the world. The scenery is beautiful, dotted with rocky cliffs, black sand beaches, and green lush forests. Due to its untouched beauty, Piha is a perfect location for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Piha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, Piha has an array of cultural attractions that you must explore. The Arataki Visitor Centre, located in the Waitakere Ranges Regional Park, is an excellent place to start your cultural journey. The museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the local Maori culture, arts, and tradition. You can also explore the Whatipu Beach Historic Reserve, a heritage-rich area, home to archaeological sites, watchtowers, and shipwrecks.

Natural Attractions of Piha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Piha is a perfect gateway to explore the natural beauty of New Zealand. There are several places to explore here, including the Kitekite Falls, Mercer Bay Loop, and Piha Beach. The famous Lion Rock, a large rock formation, dominating the beach, is a must-visit attraction. Adventurers must hike to the top of the rock to experience stunning views of the ocean and surrounding cliffs. Additionally, Karekare Falls, located in the nearby Karekare Regional Park, is a picture-perfect spot if you are looking for a refreshing dip in the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Piha

Piha is known for its extensive trail and hiking routes, providing you with a chance to explore its natural beauty. The Waitakere Ranges Regional Park has a network of hiking trails, including the Hillary Trail, which is a great option for seasoned hikers. If you are looking for an easier trail, head to the Mercer Bay Loop or the Fairy Falls, both located close to the coast.

Best Things to Do in Piha: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural beauty and hiking trails, Piha offers several action-packed activities too. The local surf school provides visitors with the opportunity to learn and experience surfing. If you are not a fan of surfing, you may opt for abseiling or canyoning. Piha’s horse riding tours, which include guided rides both on the beach and trails, are also something to look forward to.

Week-Long Itinerary for Piha

Day 1: Visit the Arataki Visitor Centre and Whatipu Beach Historic Reserve. Day 2: Explore the nearby Karekare Regional Park and hike up the Karekare Falls. Day 3: Head to the beach and take surfing lessons from the local surf school. Day 4: Hike up to the top of the picturesque Lion Rock and enjoy the views. Day 5: Experience horse riding on the beach and surrounding local trails. Day 6: Go canyoning or abseiling in Piha’s rocky gorges. Day 7: Take a leisurely stroll on Piha beach, soak up the sun, and relax.

Food and Drink Scene of Piha

Must-Try Dishes

Piha has a thriving food scene, and you should make sure to try the local delicacies during your visit. While at Piha’s black sand beach, don’t forget to taste some famous fish and chips. If you are in the mood for Mexican food, Piha offers a great option in El Pescador, which serves the best tacos and margaritas in town. One of the most unique places to eat is the Piha Cafe, where you can enjoy a fresh morning coffee, homemade scones, or some of their baked goods.

Local Beverages

Piha has a few local breweries and wine taverns that serve some of the best local beverages in the region. Piha Beer, which is brewed locally, is a must-try for beer enthusiasts. If you are a wine lover, stop at the West Brook winery for its unique blend of organic wines.

Where to Stay in Piha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Piha offers several options when it comes to staying. If you are looking for a luxury stay, the Piha Beachstay Luxury Accommodation is an excellent choice, with its prime location, comfortable rooms, and beautiful ocean view. For a more affordable option, the Piha Eco Sanctuary is a great option with its eco-friendly cabins, immersed in nature’s beauty.

What to Buy in Piha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Piha is a perfect spot to take some souvenirs home. Stop by the Piha Store for local crafts, souvenirs, and gifts. Wild Coast Perfumery is where you can purchase some of the best organic fragrances made from local ingredients. ZeaYou Gallery is an excellent place to browse and buy some locally handcrafted jewelry and art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Piha

The local culture of Piha is deeply rooted in its Maori heritage and culture. Many residents of Piha are of Maori descent, and you can observe it in the lifestyle, tradition, art, and literature. The Maori people have a deep connection with nature, and you can experience it through their traditional dances, fire walking, and storytelling.

Best Time to Visit Piha

The best time to visit Piha is during the summer months, from December to February, when the weather is warm, and beach activities like surfing and swimming are in full swing. The shoulder season of March to May offers a quieter ambiance, with spring vibes and mild temperatures. If you are looking for discounted deals, plan your visit during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Piha

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Piha people.
  • Take precautions while hiking and exploring the natural reserves.
  • Leave the beach clean and tidy after use.

Don’t:

  • Break any environmental or cultural laws.
  • Litter or pollute the water bodies or natural reserves.
  • Enter restricted or private areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Piha

Piha is a safe and friendly place to visit. However, it is essential to take precautions while exploring nature reserves or hiking trails. Be mindful of the warning signs, don’t overestimate your capabilities and always stay on the designated paths.

Health in Piha

Piha has adequate health facilities, and there are accessible medical clinics and hospitals nearby. However, due to the natural surroundings and outdoor activities, visitors should be aware of risks associated with exposure to the sun and ocean waves. Be sure to bring enough water, sunscreen, hats, and protective gear for your outdoor activities.

Getting to Piha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Piha

Auckland International Airport is the closest airport, located 45 km away from Piha.

Bus Stations Near Piha

Bus services are available from Auckland to Piha, with a bus departing every hour during peak season.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and private shuttles are also available for hire from Auckland to Piha.

How to Get Around in Piha

Piha is a small town, and most of the attractions and activities are accessible by walking. If you are tired of walking, you can rent bicycles or use taxis and shuttles for ease of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Piha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Auckland is the closest city and is home to numerous attractions such as the Sky Tower, the Auckland Museum, and the volcanic island of Rangitoto. A short drive will take you to the nearby Waitakere Ranges, the Muriwai Beach, or the Bethells Beach. The Coromandel Peninsula and Bay of Islands are also great destinations to explore further North.

Conclusion

With the perfect combination of natural beauty, outdoor activities, culture, and excellent food, Piha is a must-visit location on your trip to New Zealand. Plan your itinerary, pack your bags, and immerse yourself in the magical surroundings of Piha.
