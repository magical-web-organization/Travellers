Introduction to Long Bay

Long Bay is a beautiful coastal suburb located in North Harbour, Auckland, New Zealand, known for its stunning beaches and natural beauty. The area has become popular with tourists due to its diverse range of attractions, including its cultural heritage, scenic views, and outdoor activities. In this guide, we will explore all that Long Bay has to offer, from the best places to visit to where to stay and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Long Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Long Bay has several museums and historical sites that are a must-visit for anyone interested in New Zealand’s rich cultural heritage. The most popular attractions include:

  • Auckland War Memorial Museum: Located in nearby Parnell, this museum offers a comprehensive look at New Zealand’s history, including the Maori culture and artifacts. It also houses a large collection of natural science exhibits and is an excellent place to spend a day learning about the country’s past.
  • The New Zealand Maritime Museum: Located in the heart of Auckland’s waterfront, this museum offers a fantastic overview of the country’s maritime history. Visitors can experience the nation’s seafaring traditions through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Michael Joseph Savage Memorial Park: This beautiful park is located in nearby Bastion Point and is dedicated to the life and achievements of the country’s first Labour Prime Minister, Michael Joseph Savage. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the city skyline and Waitemata Harbour.

Natural Attractions of Long Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Long Bay is renowned for its natural beauty, including its pristine beaches, lush forests, and rolling hills. Some of the best natural attractions in the area include:

  • Long Bay Regional Park: This stunning park is located on the coast and offers a range of outdoor activities, including swimming, hiking, picnicking, and camping. The park’s walking trails also offer spectacular views of the surrounding sea and coastline.
  • Tiritiri Matangi Island Reserve: This wildlife sanctuary is located just off the coast of Long Bay and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the island’s native forests, see rare birds such as the takahē and kōkako, and learn about the island’s conservation efforts.
  • Shakespear Regional Park: This 500-hectare park is located near Long Bay and features a pristine beach, rolling hills, and coastal forests. Visitors can enjoy hiking, wildlife viewing, camping, and fishing, among other activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Bay

Long Bay offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the area’s natural beauty. Some of the best routes include:

  • Coastal Cliff Walk: This easy to moderate trail takes visitors along the coastline from Long Bay Regional Park to Browns Bay Beach. It offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding countryside.
  • Tiri Tiri Loop Track: This intermediate level trail takes hikers through the Tiritiri Matangi Island Reserve and offers views of the island’s rare bird species and native flora.
  • Takapuna to Milford Walkway: This easy, flat trail takes visitors along the coastline from Takapuna Beach to Milford Beach and offers fantastic views of Rangitoto Island and the Hauraki Gulf.

Best Things to Do in Long Bay: Activities and Tours

Long Bay offers a range of fun activities and tours to explore the area’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Some of the best things to do in Long Bay include:

  • Swimming: Long Bay is known for its pristine beaches and clear blue waters, making it the perfect place to spend a day in the sun.
  • Kayaking and Stand Up Paddle Boarding: Visitors can explore the coastline and surrounding islands by renting a kayak or SUP.
  • Tiritiri Matangi Island Tour: This guided tour takes visitors to the wildlife sanctuary on Tiritiri Matangi Island and offers the chance to see rare birds and flora.
  • Wine Tours: New Zealand is known for its excellent wines, and Long Bay offers several wine tours that take visitors to the country’s top vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Bay

Day 1: Explore Long Bay Regional Park and hike the Coastal Cliff Walk.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of Tiritiri Matangi Island Reserve.

Day 3: Visit Auckland War Memorial Museum and learn about New Zealand’s rich cultural history.

Day 4: Spend the day exploring the Shakespear Regional Park, swimming at Army Bay, and hiking the Tawharanui Regional Park.

Day 5: Take a wine tour of New Zealand’s world-renowned vineyards, located just a short drive from Long Bay.

Day 6: Rent a kayak or SUP and explore the Long Bay coastline.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at the beach, enjoying the sun, sand, and surf.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Bay

Must-Try Dishes

New Zealand is known for its fresh seafood, and Long Bay offers several local dishes that are a must-try. Some of the best dishes to try in Long Bay include:

  • Fish and Chips: A classic Kiwi dish, Long Bay’s fresh fish and chips are a must-try. Local favorites include the crispy battered snapper and hot chips.
  • Paua Fritters: Paua, or abalone, is a popular seafood found in the area, and locals love it battered and fried into fritters.
  • Kiwi Burger: A twist on the classic burger, the Kiwi Burger features a beef patty, fried egg, beetroot, and cheese.

Local Beverages

New Zealand is known for its excellent wines, and Long Bay offers several local wines that are worth trying. Some of the best local wines include:

  • Sauvignon Blanc: Marlborough, located on the South Island, is known for its world-renowned Sauvignon Blanc wines.
  • Pinot Noir: Central Otago, located on the South Island, is known for its excellent Pinot Noir wines.
  • Craft Beer: New Zealand is home to several excellent craft breweries, and Long Bay offers several local microbrews that are worth trying.

Where to Stay in Long Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Bay offers several excellent accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Long Bay include:

  • Long Bay Villas: These luxury villas offer stunning ocean views, private balconies, and spacious living areas.
  • Long Bay Spacious House: A beautiful holiday home perfect for families or groups, located just a short walk from the beach.
  • Long Bay Motor Camp: A budget-friendly option, this motor camp offers cozy cabins and campsites located just steps from Long Bay Regional Park.

What to Buy in Long Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Long Bay offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or keepsakes. Some of the best products to buy in Long Bay include:

  • Paua Jewelry: Paua, or abalone, is a popular seafood in the area and is also used to make beautiful jewelry.
  • Manuka Honey: New Zealand is known for its high-quality honey, and the local Manuka honey is particularly prized for its antibacterial and medicinal properties.
  • Maori Carvings: Long Bay is located in an area with a rich Maori cultural heritage, and visitors can find some beautiful Maori carvings for sale in local markets and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Bay

New Zealand has a rich cultural heritage, and Long Bay offers several opportunities to learn about the country’s traditions and customs. Some of the local customs to be aware of include:

  • Maori Culture: New Zealand’s indigenous people, the Maori, have a rich culture and history that is celebrated throughout the country.
  • Kia Ora: A common Maori greeting that means “hello” or “welcome.”
  • Haka: The Maori war dance is a powerful expression of cultural identity and is often performed on special occasions.

Best Time to Visit Long Bay

The best time to visit Long Bay is during the summer months of December to February when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, Long Bay is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy the area’s outdoor activities and cultural attractions any time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Bay

Do:

  • Do try local seafood dishes, including the famous fish and chips.
  • Do learn about Maori culture and traditions.
  • Do explore the many outdoor activities offered in the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to protect yourself from the sun, as New Zealand has a high UV index.
  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Don’t ignore cultural customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Bay

Long Bay is a safe destination for visitors, but as with any tourist destination, visitors should take common sense precautions. Some safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Always lock rental cars and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Swim in designated areas and always supervise children near water.

Health in Long Bay

New Zealand has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect quality medical care if needed. It is recommended that visitors take out travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical costs.

Getting to Long Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Bay

The nearest international airport to Long Bay is Auckland International Airport, located approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Long Bay

Long Bay is well-served by local buses, with several bus stops located throughout the area. The most convenient bus route is the NX bus, which runs from Auckland City to Albany.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Long Bay from Auckland City. For visitors looking for a more environmentally friendly option, electric scooters are available for rent throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Long Bay

Long Bay is a small area and is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s excellent public transportation system, with local buses and trains available throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Long Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Long Bay’s proximity to Auckland City makes it the perfect base for exploring the city’s cultural attractions, dining scene, and nightlife. Visitors can also take advantage of the area’s excellent transportation links to explore the rest of New Zealand, including the North Island’s stunning natural scenery and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Long Bay is a beautiful coastal destination that offers a diverse range of attractions, from cultural heritage sites to outdoor adventures and beautiful beaches. Whether you’re interested in hiking through the nearby parks or learning about Maori culture, Long Bay has something for everyone.
