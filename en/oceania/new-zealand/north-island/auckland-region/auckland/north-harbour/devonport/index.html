Introduction to Devonport

Located in North Harbour, Devonport is a charming coastal village, just 15 minutes away from Auckland’s bustling central business district via a ferry ride. Devonport is a popular destination known for its stunning beaches, verdant parks, and rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Devonport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • North Head Historic Reserve: A former military fort with tunnels and bunkers, offering panoramic views of the Hauraki Gulf and the Waitemata Harbour.
  • Torpedo Bay Navy Museum: The museum is an ode to the contribution of the Royal New Zealand Navy to the country’s history, with its naval artifacts, interactive displays, and immersive exhibitions.
  • Devonport Museum: The museum showcases the fascinating maritime history of the village, as well as its colonial past through photographs, maps, and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Devonport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Victoria: The extinct volcano in the heart of the village offers magnificent views of the Auckland skyline and the Hauraki Gulf.
  • Cheltenham Beach: A serene and sandy beach with clear blue waters, offering picturesque views of Rangitoto Island.
  • North Shore Coastal Walk: A scenic walk that stretches for six kilometers along the coastline, offering breathtaking views of the Hauraki Gulf and the Waitemata Harbour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Devonport

  • Takarunga-Hill Historic Loop Track: This 5.3-kilometer loop track takes hikers and bikers through the historic military defense installations and the forest reserve of Mount Victoria, rewarding them with sweeping views of the Hauraki Gulf.
  • North Shore Coastal Walk: The continuous walkway that stretches from Takapuna to Devonport offers hikers and bikers a chance to savor the natural beauty of the Auckland coast.

Best Things to Do in Devonport: Activities and Tours

  • Devonport Artisan Market: A weekly market that showcases local crafts, food, and music.
  • Devonport Ferry: A 12-minute ferry ride from Auckland to Devonport, offering a chance to admire the Auckland skyline and immerse oneself in the village’s relaxed and laid-back atmosphere.
  • Devonport Food and Wine Tour: A guided tour that takes visitors through some of the village’s best culinary offerings, along with a deep dive into the local wine and beer scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Devonport

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips: Aucklanders consider Devonport Fish and Chips as some of the best Fish and Chips in town.
  • Kumara Chips: Deep-fried sweet potato chips that are a healthier alternative to regular fries.
  • Custard Square: A creamy sweet pastry slice that has been a favorite local dessert since the 1960s.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Devonport is home to some of the best craft breweries, such as Hector’s Brewery and Tutukaka Coast Brewing Company.
  • Coffee: Devonport boasts of several coffee shops, including Corelli’s Cafe, that serve some of the freshest and most flavorful coffee in Auckland.

Where to Stay in Devonport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Esplanade Hotel: A boutique hotel situated across the street from Devonport’s Wharf, featuring modern yet elegant rooms with spectacular harbor views.
  • Peace and Plenty Inn: A Victorian-style B&B, in proximity to Devonport village center.
  • Bunkdown Lodge Backpackers: An affordable backpacker lodge located a few minutes’ walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Devonport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maori Artifacts: Devonport boasts of several artisan shops and galleries that sell authentic Maori artifacts, such as carved wooden taonga and traditional jewelry.
  • Devonport Chocolates: Established in 1987, Devonport Chocolates is known for its rich and decadent range of chocolates and fudges.

Local Culture and Customs of Devonport

Devonport is known for its relaxed and friendly culture, where people often greet each other on the streets. The village is home to several Maori tribes who have lived here for generations. Visitors are expected to respect the cultural traditions and heritage of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Devonport

Auckland has a temperate climate, making it a year-round destination. However, the best time to visit Devonport is between December and February when the weather is warm and bright, and the days are long.

Do’s and Don’ts of Devonport

Do:

  • Greet people warmly.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and the occasion.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Remove or disturb Maori artifacts.
  • Engage in inappropriate behavior or language in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Devonport

Devonport is a safe and peaceful village with low levels of crime. However, visitors should take general precautions such as keeping valuable possessions in the hotel safe and being cautious while walking in poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Devonport

New Zealand has a public health care system that offers quality care to all. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers accidents, medical emergencies, and evacuation.

Getting to Devonport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Devonport is easily accessible from Auckland’s central business district via a 12-minute ferry ride from Auckland’s Ferry Terminal.

Airports Near Devonport

  • Auckland Airport (AKL) - 25 kilometers from Devonport.

Bus Stations Near Devonport

  • Victoria Road, Devonport – 5-minute walk from Wharf.
  • Devonport Bus Terminal – Located in the heart of the village.

Other Options

  • Taxis and Ridesharing Services: Uber, Zoomy, and Ola are popular ridesharing services in Auckland.
  • Rental Cars: Several car rental companies operate at Auckland International Airport.

How to Get Around in Devonport

Devonport is a small village that is easily walkable. Alternatively, visitors can rent bicycles or use the local public transport system.

Where to Go Next from Devonport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Auckland Central Business District: Just a 12-minute ferry ride from Devonport, Auckland offers visitors several cultural experiences, such as museums, galleries, and shopping.
  • Waiheke Island: A ferry ride away, Waiheke Island is known for its idyllic beaches, vineyards, and olive groves.
  • Rangitoto Island: Located in the Hauraki Gulf, Rangitoto Island is an extinct volcanic island that offers visitors a chance to hike to the summit and enjoy spectacular views of the Gulf.

Conclusion

Devonport is a charming and peaceful village with a rich cultural and historical heritage. Visitors can savor the stunning natural beauty, indulge in the culinary delights, and soak in the relaxed and friendly culture of the locals. A trip to Devonport is an experience that they will cherish forever.
