Introduction to Muriwai

Muriwai is a small coastal town located in the Auckland Region of New Zealand’s North Island, about 42 km northwest of Auckland city. This stunning beach town is famous for its black sand beaches, rugged cliffs, and stunning sunsets, making it a popular destination among tourists and locals alike.

Once a quiet fishing village, Muriwai has become a popular destination for surfers, hikers, bird-watchers, and those looking for a peaceful escape from the buzz of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Muriwai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Muriwai Surf School and Board Rentals: Learn to surf or rent a board here. The instructors are qualified and experienced in teaching both beginners and advanced surfers.

  • The Kauri Museum: Located just 30 minutes from Muriwai, the Kauri Museum is home to the largest collection of kauri gum artwork in the world, along with interesting exhibits on the history of the kauri tree and its gum.

  • Auckland Museum: Learn more about the fascinating history and culture of New Zealand in this excellent museum. It showcases a wide range of artifacts, from Maori artifacts to ancient fossils.

Natural Attractions of Muriwai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muriwai Beach: The highlight of Muriwai is undoubtedly its stunning black-sand beach, stretching over 60 km. The surf’s normally quite good, making it popular among surfers.

  • Takapu Refuge: The cliff-top refuge is home to a colony of about 1,200 gannets between August and February. Watch these majestic birds soar and dive into the sea.

  • The Muriwai Gannet Colony: Take in the breathtaking views of the Tasman Sea from the viewing platform, while taking in the sight of about 1,200 gannets from August to March.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Muriwai

  • The Hillary Trail: This 120 km coastal trek is stunning and a must-do for any hiking enthusiast. Taking an average of four days, the trail offers stunning views of Muriwai and its surrounding areas.

  • Muriwai Beach to Bethells Beach: This stunning coastal walk traverses towering sand dunes and a raging river. The walk is about 10 km long with scenic stops and activities along the way.

Best Things to Do in Muriwai: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Take surf lessons at the beach or rent a surfboard from one of the local providers to take advantage of the great surfing conditions.

  • Horseback Riding: Take in the beauty of Muriwai Beach on horseback. There are several horse trek companies in the area.

  • Skydiving: If you’re looking for an adrenaline rush, go skydiving over Muriwai beach for spectacular aerial views.

Food and Drink Scene of Muriwai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Stop by the local fish and chip shops and savor the fresh seafood.

  • Kauri Cliffs Honey: The kauri tree is also home to delicious honey. Try the honey made from the kauri tree that can also be bought at local stores.

  • Pavlova: Enjoy New Zealand’s national dessert made from meringue with whipped cream and fresh fruits.

Local Beverages

  • Waiwera Water: Try the refreshing mineral water sourced locally.

  • Wine: Muriwai is located in the heart of New Zealand’s wine region, and there are many vineyards in the surrounding area. Try some of the delicious local wines while in town.

Where to Stay in Muriwai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Muriwai Beach Private Retreat: Offers a luxurious and private stay in Muriwai, with stunning views of the beach and mountains.

  • Muriwai Surf School and Accommodation: This hostel offers budget-friendly accommodation combined with surf lessons.

  • Takapu Lodge: Offers luxury accommodation just a minute away from the gannet colony.

What to Buy in Muriwai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kauri Gum: Take home a piece of New Zealand’s history with this tree resin.

  • Kiwi Socks: Keep your feet warm during winter with this unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Muriwai

The Maori culture has a deep connection with Muriwai, and you’ll find the culture prevalent throughout the town. Visitors are encouraged to learn more about the Maori culture and the traditions of the land.

Best Time to Visit Muriwai

The best time to visit Muriwai is during the summer months of December to February when the weather is warm and the attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muriwai

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and keep the environment clean.

  • Visit the local cultural sites and learn more about the Maori culture.

  • Take pictures of the stunning views and memories.

Don’t:

  • Remove any artifacts from the environment.

  • Disturb the wildlife and their natural habitats.

  • Engage in any dangerous activities without proper safety gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Muriwai

Muriwai is a generally safe place to visit. However, visitors should be cautious of the strong surf and rips that can occur. Follow the warnings and instructions provided by the beach patrol.

Health in Muriwai

Muriwai has a medical center and pharmacy in the town center for minor medical issues. For serious illnesses or injuries, visitors should seek medical attention in Auckland city.

Getting to Muriwai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Muriwai

The closest airport to Muriwai is the Auckland International Airport, which is about 55km away. Visitors can hire a rental car, catch a taxi, or take public transport to get to Muriwai.

Bus Stations Near Muriwai

The nearest bus stations are in Helensville and Kumeu, which are approximately 12 km away from Muriwai.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from Auckland city centre.

How to Get Around in Muriwai

Muriwai is relatively small, so walking or biking is the best option for getting around. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Muriwai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Auckland: Explore New Zealand’s largest city and its many attractions.

  • The Bay of Islands: Experience some of the most beautiful beaches and scenery in New Zealand located about 180 km north of Muriwai.

  • Waitakere Ranges: Explore the hiking trails and stunning views of this natural reserve located just a few kilometers away from Muriwai.

Conclusion

Muriwai offers a peaceful getaway from city life, with its stunning black sand beaches, harbor wildlife, and lush hinterland. Whether you’re looking to surf, hike, or simply relax, Muriwai is a must-visit destination on your next trip to New Zealand.
