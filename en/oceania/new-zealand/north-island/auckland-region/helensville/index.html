Introduction to Helensville

Helensville is a small town situated in the Auckland region of North Island in New Zealand. It boasts numerous cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and scenic views, making it a great place to visit for travelers of all kinds. In this guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, cultural and customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Helensville

There are plenty of must-see places in Helensville. Here is a list of the top ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Helensville District Historical Society Museum: Showcase the history of the town and surrounding settlements.
  • Kaipara Coast Sculpture Gardens: A garden filled with varieties of sculptures and art.
  • Parry Kauri Park: A park to walk around and see the majestic Kauri trees.

Natural Attractions of Helensville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Matakohe-Limestone Island Scenic Reserve: A scenic reserve teeming with wildlife
  • South Head Lighthouse: Provides amazing views of the Tasman Sea and the Kaipara Harbour
  • Muriwai Beach: A black sand beach with breathtaking views and great for surfing
  • Woodhill Forest: A forest ideal for hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Helensville

Helensville provides many hiking and biking trails ideal for outdoor lovers. Here is a list of the top ones:

  • Mt. Auckland Walk: A walking track offering stunning vistas over the Kaipara Harbour.
  • Moggill Creek Dam Track: A cycling track that offers beautiful scenery and is ideal for mountain biking enthusiasts.
  • Muriwai to Bethells Beach: A hike offering dramatic ocean views and rolling sand dunes.

Best Things to Do in Helensville: Activities and Tours

Helensville provides visitors with many activities and tours. Here are some worth trying:

  • Horse Riding Tours: A horseback tour through Woodhill Forest for nature enthusiasts.
  • Surf Lessons at Muriwai Beach: Learn to surf in the waters of Muriwai beach with experienced instructors around.
  • Tasting Tours: Tours around wineries, micro-breweries, and cheese and chocolate factories.
  • Fishing Trip: A fishing trip to catch various species of fish in the Kaipara Harbour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Helensville

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you make the most out of your trip to Helensville:

Day Activity
1 Check into a hotel or guesthouse and Visit Parry Kauri Park
2 Go on a surfing lesson or climb South Head Lighthouse to have a view of Tasman Sea
3 Grab a horseback ride through the beautiful Woodhill Forest
4 Take a tasting tour around breweries, wineries, or cheese factories
5 Go for a cycling tour in Moggill Creek Dam Track
6 Visit Matakohe-Limestone Island Scenic Reserve
7 Enjoy a fishing trip to catch various species in the Kaipara Harbour

Food and Drink Scene of Helensville

Helensville’s food and drink scene offers visitors with taste experiences that incorporate the neighborhood’s homesteading customs. Here are some amazing things to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kumara Chips with Aioli: A dish made from locally grown sweet potatoes.
  • Hangi: Traditional Maori food cooked underground, involving meat, veg, and potatoes.
  • Crispy Pork Belly: A tasty pork dish commonly found in many restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Kumeu Wine Country is famous for award-winning wines.
  • Craft Beer: Many local brewers in Helensville provide excellent beers.

Where to Stay in Helensville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Helensville has many lodging options for visitors, ranging from hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are the top lodging options:

  • Horseman’s Inn: A historic hotel situated in the heart of Helensville.
  • Kauri Villas: Provides a peaceful stay with quirky cottage decor.
  • Nature’s Retreat: A cozy bed and breakfast providing warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Helensville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Helensville is famous for its crafts, local products, and souvenirs. Here are a few things to bring back:

  • Pounamu (Greenstone): A local Maori pendant and earrings made from greenstone.
  • Merino Wool: Can purchase merino wool to keep you warm
  • Local Art and Pottery: Helensville houses many independent potters and artists who create unique pieces of art with their pottery equipment.

Local Culture and Customs of Helensville

The locals of Helensville welcome visitors warmly and are proud of their neighborhood and customs. The Maori individuals are the indigenous occupants of New Zealand and should be welcomed with respect. They acknowledge the significance of these people and their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Helensville

The best time to visit Helensville is between December and March when the weather is pleasant and warm. June, July, and August are the winter months, which can be chilly, but you can enjoy an off-season trip with low rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Helensville

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the indigenous people.
  • Seek authorized information and alerts when you are willing to go for outdoor activities.
  • Carry suitable gear and clothes while going for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Do not dump waste whenever you are visiting any natural area.
  • Do not leave marked tracks while going for a hike in the forest.
  • Do not smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Helensville

The town is tranquil and safe to visit. But, always take care of your valuables and make sure to lock your car and hotel room correctly.

Health in Helensville

Helensville offers good quality medical facilities with a local hospital, dentist, and several clinics in the region. In case of an emergency, dial 111.

Getting to Helensville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Helensville

  • Auckland Airport: Around 40 kilometers from Helensville. It provides a variety of international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Helensville

  • Auckland InterCity SkyCity Terminal: To arrive by bus in Helensville, catch a bus to Auckland. You can get off at SkyCity Terminal and then take a bus or train to Helensville.

Other Options

  • Car Hire is also a decent alternative if you’re inclined to get around yourself, and there are plenty of car rental choices available.

How to Get Around in Helensville

Helensville is a modest area with numerous attractions nearby, so strolling around is a comfortable choice or bicycles can be rented for the more adventurous. You can also rent a car or hire taxis for your visit.

Where to Go Next from Helensville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kumeu Wine Country

Visited for the award-winning wines and vineyards, Kumeu Wine Country is just a 15-minute drive from Helensville.

Bethells Beach

Bethells Beach, commonly known as Te Henga, is a rugged west coast beach located just 30 kilometers from town.

Conclusion

Helensville provides a unique charm to visitors that captivates them with natural beauty, attractions, and experiences. The town’s cultural scene is exceptional, and the locals are accommodating. We hope this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the necessary information you will require on your visit to Helensville.
