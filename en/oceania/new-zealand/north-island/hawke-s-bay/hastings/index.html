Introduction to Hastings

Located on New Zealand’s North Island, Hastings is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. It is situated in the heart of the Hawke’s Bay region, known for its award-winning wineries, stunning coastlines, and scenic landscapes. Hastings is a perfect destination for travelers who are interested in exploring the rich history and culture of New Zealand while enjoying outdoor activities and relaxing in charming accommodations.

List of Places to Visit in Hastings

Hastings offers a wide range of attractions for visitors, from cultural landmarks to scenic wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit in Hastings:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hastings City Art Gallery - showcasing contemporary and historical art pieces from local and international artists.
  • Te Mata Peak - a cultural landmark with stunning panoramic views of the region.
  • Hawke’s Bay Museum & Art Gallery - a must-visit museum featuring the Maori culture and the history of the Hawke’s Bay region.
  • Cranford Hospice and Palliative Care Trust - a historical building and garden offering guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Hastings: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Kidnappers - a scenic headland with a gannet colony.
  • Waimarama Beach - a stunning beach with crystal clear waters and white sand.
  • Maraetotara Falls - a spectacular waterfall and swimming spot.
  • Tuki Tuki River - perfect for kayaking, fishing, or a leisurely scenic drive.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hastings

Hastings has several hiking and biking trails, allowing visitors to explore the natural beauty of the region. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Te Mata Park - offering several hiking trails through native bush and stunning views from the top.
  • Peka Peka Wetlands - a bird-watching haven in the heart of Hastings.
  • Hawke’s Bay Trails - a multi-use trail system that connects Hastings, Napier and Havelock North.

Best Things to Do in Hastings: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tours - Hastings is renowned for its wineries, and visitors can take wine tours and tastings to experience the local wine culture.
  • Farm Tours - visitors can take farm tours to see how the local produce and wine are grown and processed.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides - a unique and stunning way to experience the beauty of the Hawke’s Bay region from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hastings

Here is a suggested one-week itinerary for a trip to Hastings:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival and check-in at accommodation. Explore local surroundings.
Day 2 Visit Te Mata Peak and the City Art Gallery.
Day 3 Take a wine tour and visit local wineries.
Day 4 Hike or bike the local trails.
Day 5 Relax on the stunning Waimarama beach and enjoy water activities.
Day 6 Visit the Hawke’s Bay Museum and Art Gallery and the Cranford Hospice and Palliative Care Trust.
Day 7 Explore the local farm tours and take a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Hastings

As a region renowned for its wine production, Hastings offers a wide range of dining experiences, blending local produce and flavors with international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb - Hastings produces some of the finest lamb in the world, and it is a staple in many local dishes.
  • Seafood - being located near the coast, the seafood in Hastings is fresh and delicious.
  • Pies - a traditional Kiwi comfort food - Hastings boasts delicious pies in many varieties.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Hastings is known for its award-winning wines, particularly Chardonnay, Merlot and Syrah.
  • Beer - Hastings has several craft beer breweries producing some excellent local beers.
  • Coffee - New Zealand is famous for its coffee culture, and Hastings has several specialty coffee shops that cannot be missed.

Where to Stay in Hastings: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hastings offers a variety of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some suggestions for places to stay:

Luxury Hotels

  • The Farm at Cape Kidnappers - a 5-star luxury lodge situated in a picturesque setting overlooking the ocean.
  • Black Barn Retreats - offering luxurious villas and suites surrounded by vineyards and orchards.

Boutique Hotels

  • Porters Boutique Hotel - an elegant and charming hotel located in the heart of Hastings.
  • Havelock House - a beautifully restored historic home offering a unique experience.

Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Hastings Backpackers - a budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Hastings.
  • Sundowner Bed and Breakfast - offering affordable ensuite rooms with stunning views of the countryside.

What to Buy in Hastings: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hastings is a great place to buy unique local products and souvenirs. Some of the popular items to bring home include:

  • Wine - a bottle of one of the local award-winning wines is an excellent souvenir.
  • Artwork - Hastings has several galleries showcasing local artists.
  • Honey and cheese - Hastings produces some of the best honey and dairy products in New Zealand.

Local Culture and Customs of Hastings

The Maori culture is an essential part of Hastings, and visitors can experience it through cultural performances, food, and arts. Visitors should respect the local customs and be polite and courteous to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Hastings

The best time to visit Hastings is during the summer months (December to February), when the weather is warm and sunny, and many festivals and events are held. Autumn and spring are also perfect times to visit, with mild temperatures and stunning colors in the vineyards and parks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hastings

To make the most of your trip to Hastings, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try local wine and cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Hastings

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Hastings

Hastings is generally a safe city, and visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking doors and being aware of their surroundings. The beaches can have strong currents and rips, and visitors should always pay attention to the warning signs and safety guidelines.

Health in Hastings

New Zealand has excellent healthcare services, and visitors can access medical facilities in Hastings and the surrounding areas. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Hastings: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hastings can be easily accessed by air, road or bus.

Airports Near Hastings

The nearest airport to Hastings is Hawkes Bay Airport, located 19km northeast of the city center. Several airlines offer flights to Hawke’s Bay, including Air New Zealand and Jetstar.

Bus Stations Near Hastings

Hastings is well-connected by bus to other major cities, including Napier, Wellington, and Auckland. The Green Bus and Intercity Bus services operate several daily trips from Hastings to these major cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Hastings from other parts of the country. The scenic drive offers stunning views of the region’s natural beauty.

How to Get Around in Hastings

Hastings is a small city, and most attractions can be easily accessed on foot, by bike or by car. Rental cars are available at the airport, and taxis and Uber services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Hastings: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hastings is situated in the heart of the Hawke’s Bay region, and several other destinations can easily be visited as a day trip from Hastings. Here are some of the nearby destinations to visit:

  • Napier - a charming Art Deco city with a beautiful waterfront and stunning architecture.
  • Cape Kidnappers - a stunning and famous headland with a gannet colony and breathtaking views.
  • Wairarapa - a wine-producing region with charming towns and beautiful countryside.

Conclusion

Hastings is a charming and beautiful city with a lot to offer visitors. From cultural landmarks to natural wonders, Hastings has something for everyone. With this travel guide, visitors can experience the best of what Hastings has to offer and create unforgettable memories.
