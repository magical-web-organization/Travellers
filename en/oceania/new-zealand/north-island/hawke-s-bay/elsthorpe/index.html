Introduction to Elsthorpe

Elsthorpe is a small settlement situated on the coast of Hawke’s Bay in the North Island of New Zealand. This hidden gem is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails, alluring cultural and historical landmarks, natural wonders, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re traveling solo, with family or friends, this guide will help you make the most out of your trip to Elsthorpe.

List of Places to Visit in Elsthorpe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Hawke’s Bay region is steeped in history, and Elsthorpe is no exception. Here are some of the landmarks you shouldn’t miss out on:

  • Elsthorpe War Memorial Hall: This historical hall located in the heart of the town commemorates the sacrifices and contributions of the soldiers in World War I.
  • Te Ana Maori Rock Art: This unique site is home to stunning examples of Maori rock art, some of which date back to over a century.
  • The Elsthorpe Cemetery: An old cemetery located just outside the town, it is the resting place of some of the earliest settlers in the region.

Natural Attractions of Elsthorpe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, then Elsthorpe has lots to offer. Here are some places you must visit:

  • Elsthorpe Beach: A beautiful sandy beach, perfect for relaxing or going for a swim in the ocean. You can also go fishing or try your hand at surfing.
  • Tukituki River: The river that runs through the town is perfect for kayaking, rafting, or just taking a leisurely raft ride.
  • Elsthorpe Scenic Reserve: Home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, this reserve offers several hiking and biking trails of varying difficulty, with incredible views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elsthorpe

Elsthorpe boasts some of the most scenic trails in New Zealand. For hikers and bikers, here are the must-visit routes:

  • Elsthorpe Scenic Reserve Track: This track winds through the reserve and takes you through lush green forests to scenic outlooks from where you can enjoy some fantastic views.
  • Tukituki River Track: This track runs alongside the river and offers easy to moderate level hiking options. You can also cycle this track and enjoy the picturesque river views.

Best Things to Do in Elsthorpe: Activities and Tours

There are lots of things to do in Elsthorpe, whether you’re a history buff or an adrenaline junkie. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Wine tasting tours: Several vineyards near Elsthorpe offer wine tours where you can sample some of New Zealand’s finest wines.
  • Kayaking and canoeing: Take a leisurely paddle down the Tukituki River for a peaceful and scenic adventure.
  • Hunting and fishing: Go on a guided hunting or fishing excursion to catch some of the region’s most prized gamefish.

Food and Drink Scene of Elsthorpe

Elsthorpe has an excellent food and drink scene, with plenty of local gems nestled amid fantastic scenery and abundant produce. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New Zealand lamb: Try this internationally famous dish in the form of roasted every day at most local restaurants.
  • Fish and Chips: This classic British dish is served everywhere in Elsthorpe, and you can check out Elijah’s Kitchen for the best authentic fish and chips experience.

Local Beverages

  • New Zealand Wine: Try some of the finest wines from the vineyards near Elsthorpe. New Zealand’s unique climate produces some excellent flavors worth tasting.
  • Craft Beers: Elsthorpe has some fantastic craft beer breweries, and you can try some of their creations at most local pubs and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Elsthorpe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elsthorpe caters to every budget, from luxury lodges to budget-friendly guesthouses and holiday parks. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Grange Homestay: A historic homestay offers a warm welcome, comfortable accommodation, and a delicious breakfast.
  • Elsthorpe Lodge: A luxury lodge offers spectacular views of the countryside, and it’s a great option for those who want to enjoy some peace and quiet.
  • Elsthorpe Holiday Park: If you’re traveling on a budget, then this holiday park is an excellent option, with a range of accommodation choices from campsites to basic cabins.

What to Buy in Elsthorpe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Elsthorpe has many fantastic local products that you can take home as souvenirs, such as:

  • Wine: Pack some bottles of New Zealand’s renowned Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Noir as a reminder of your trip.
  • Maori Art and Craft: The Te Ana Maori Rock Art center has a gallery selling authentic Maori crafts such as woven baskets, carvings, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Elsthorpe

Kiwis are friendly and welcoming people, and Elsthorpe is no exception. However, there are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: It’s customary to shake hands, give a nod, or a smile while greeting somebody for the first time.
  • Tipping: Tipping isn’t expected but appreciated.
  • Etiquette: It’s essential to respect the local customs and practices, especially when visiting Marae or other cultural areas.

Best Time to Visit Elsthorpe

Elsthorpe’s weather is temperate with mild winters and warm summers, and you can visit all year round. The most popular time for tourists is during summer (December-February) when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elsthorpe

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace when hiking and biking.
  • Tip, if you receive excellent service

Don’t:

  • Litter; Elsthorpe has designated waste disposal areas, and it’s essential to keep the town tidy.
  • Reveal Clothing: Although New Zealand is very liberal, it’s recommended to dress modestly in sacred spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elsthorpe

Elsthorpe is generally a safe place to visit, but like any destination, it’s essential to take certain precautions:

  • When hiking, always carry sufficient food, water, and warm clothing.
  • Check the weather conditions before starting your hike, and never hike alone.

Health in Elsthorpe

Elsthorpe has easy access to medical facilities, and visitors can contact any of the local clinics or urgent medical care services in case of any health-related emergencies.

Getting to Elsthorpe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Elsthorpe is accessible by several modes of transportation, especially from the larger cities in New Zealand.

  • By Air: The closest airport is in Napier, which is about 45 minutes drive to Elsthorpe.
  • By Bus: InterCity and Kiwi Experience operate daily buses to Elsthorpe from cities across New Zealand.
  • By Car: You can rent a car from the major cities in New Zealand and drive to Elsthorpe along State Highway 2.

How to Get Around in Elsthorpe

Exploring Elsthorpe is best done via a car, bike, or even on foot.

Where to Go Next from Elsthorpe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hastings: A 30-minute drive from Elsthorpe, Hastings is known for its farmers’ market and vineyards.
  • Napier: The Art Deco city of Napier, 45 minutes drive from Elsthorpe, is known for its architecture and wine region.
  • Waipatiki Beach: A secluded beach famous for its crystal clear waters, located an hour’s drive from Elsthorpe.

Conclusion

Elsthorpe is a unique blend of stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and a vibrant food and drink scene, making it the perfect destination for anyone who wants to experience the best of what New Zealand has to offer. Plan your trip following this guide and get ready to make some amazing memories in Elsthorpe.
