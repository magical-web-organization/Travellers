Introduction to Morere

Morere is a hidden gem in the Hawke’s Bay region of New Zealand’s North Island. Known for its natural geothermal springs and beautiful surrounding forests, Morere offers a unique experience for travelers seeking tranquility, relaxation, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Morere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Morere Settlers Museum: This museum tells the story of the early European settlers who arrived in the Morere area in the late 19th century.
  • Te Papawai Marae: An important cultural center for the local Maori community. Visitors can experience a traditional Maori welcome and learn about Maori culture.

Natural Attractions of Morere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Morere Hot Springs: This natural geothermal spring is the main attraction in Morere. Visitors can relax in the warm waters surrounded by lush forest and listen to the sounds of native birdlife.
  • Morere Scenic Reserve: This reserve offers a variety of hiking trails through native forest and is home to a large population of kahikatea trees, some of the oldest in the country.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morere

  • The Mohaka River Trail: This trail follows the Mohaka River, which is known for its excellent trout fishing. The trail takes hikers and bikers through native forest and past stunning waterfalls.
  • The Te Urewera Great Walk: This multi-day hike takes travelers through remote wilderness areas, offering stunning views of mountains, lakes, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Morere: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Trekking: Experience the beauty of the Morere area on horseback. Guided tours are available for all skill levels.
  • Kayaking on the Mohaka River: Rent a kayak or book a guided tour to explore the stunning Mohaka River and its surrounding scenery.
  • Birdwatching: With its lush vegetation and varied habitats, Morere is a haven for native birdlife. Birdwatchers can spot a wide variety of species, including the Kereru, Tui, and Fantail.

Food and Drink Scene of Morere

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Roast: New Zealand lamb is world-famous, and Morere is no exception. Be sure to try a lamb roast during your visit.
  • Hangi: A traditional Maori feast cooked in an underground oven, usually consisting of meat, vegetables, and sweet potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Hawke’s Bay Wine: The Hawke’s Bay region is known for its award-winning wine, especially Chardonnay and Merlot.
  • Craft Beer: The craft beer scene in Hawke’s Bay has been growing in recent years, with breweries such as Zeelandt and Brave Brewing.

Where to Stay in Morere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Morere Hot Springs Lodge: This lodge is located right next to the hot springs and offers a variety of accommodation options, including private cabins and shared dormitory-style rooms.
  • Morere Bed and Breakfast: Located in a charming historic home, this bed and breakfast offers a quiet and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Morere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maori carvings: Local Maori artists create beautiful carvings in a variety of styles, from traditional to modern.
  • Manuka Honey: Manuka honey is produced in New Zealand and is known for its health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Morere

The Maori people have a significant presence in the Morere area, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect their culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Morere

The best time to visit Morere is during the summer months (December - February) when the weather is warm and dry. However, the hot springs are open year-round, and the surrounding forests are beautiful during the autumn months (March - May).

Do’s and Don’ts of Morere

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take your time and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Ignore safety precautions around hot springs or other natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the local Maori culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morere

  • Be cautious when using hot springs as the water can be extremely hot and dangerous.
  • Stay on designated hiking trails and follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions and any potential hazards such as flooding or landslides.

Health in Morere

  • Access to medical facilities may be limited in Morere, so visitors should bring any necessary medication with them.
  • It is recommended that travelers have travel insurance in case of an emergency.

Getting to Morere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morere

The closest airports to Morere are:

  • Napier Airport: (88 km/55 miles)
  • Gisborne Airport: (100 km/62 miles)

Bus Stations Near Morere

Intercity buses run from Napier and Gisborne to the nearby settlements of Wairoa and Nuhaka, which are both within driving distance of Morere.

Other Options

Car rental is available from both Napier and Gisborne airports, giving visitors the flexibility to explore the area on their own.

How to Get Around in Morere

The best way to get around Morere is by car. However, bikes and e-bikes can also be rented from the Morere Hot Springs Lodge.

Where to Go Next from Morere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gisborne: Known as the “sunshine city,” Gisborne is a popular destination for beach lovers and surfers.
  • Lake Waikaremoana: A stunning lake located in the Te Urewera National Park, offering hiking trails, fishing, and boating.
  • Hawkes Bay Wine Region: Visit nearby vineyards and wineries to taste award-winning wines.

Conclusion

Morere is a tranquil and beautiful destination that offers visitors the opportunity to relax, explore the natural beauty of the area, and learn about the local culture. A visit to Morere is a must for anyone seeking a unique and authentic New Zealand experience.
