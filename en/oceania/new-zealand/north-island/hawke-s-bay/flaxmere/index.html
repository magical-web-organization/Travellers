Introduction to Flaxmere

Located in the Hawke’s Bay region of New Zealand’s North Island, Flaxmere is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. This multi-ethnic suburb is home to a vibrant Maori community, and visitors can get a taste of their rich culture through the many museums, galleries, and cultural events in the area. In addition, Flaxmere is surrounded by beautiful countryside, with plenty of hiking and biking routes, parks, and nature reserves to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Flaxmere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Flaxmere can learn about the rich cultural heritage of the area by visiting some of its iconic museums and historical places:

  • Flaxmere Library and Community Centre: A hub of community activities and events, this center hosts free talks, workshops, and exhibitions that showcase the unique multi-cultural character of the area.

  • Arataki Cultural Centre: This museum houses a rich collection of Maori artifacts, including traditional tools, weapons, and clothing. Visitors can learn about the unique customs and traditions of the indigenous Maori people, and take part in cultural activities such as weaving and carving lessons.

  • Otane Heritage Trail: This scenic hiking trail takes visitors through some of the most historically significant sites in Flaxmere, including the site of the Battle of Te Ranga and the birthplace of famous Maori leader Te Kooti Arikirangi Te Turuki.

Natural Attractions of Flaxmere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Flaxmere is surrounded by natural beauty, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Gannet Colony at Cape Kidnappers: Located just a short drive from Flaxmere, this nature reserve is home to a wide range of seabirds, including the iconic gannet. Visitors can take a guided tour of the colony and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding coastline.

  • Te Mata Peak: Rising 400 meters above the surrounding countryside, Te Mata Peak offers stunning views of Hawke’s Bay and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike to the summit or take a scenic drive up to the lookout point.

  • Black Barn Vineyards: This picturesque vineyard offers wine-tasting tours and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the vineyard and surrounding countryside, and take home a few bottles of their favorite vintage as a souvenir.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Flaxmere

Flaxmere is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Flaxmere include:

  • Cape Kidnappers Coastal Trail: This scenic trail takes visitors along the dramatic coastline of Cape Kidnappers, offering breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding countryside.

  • Te Mata Peak Trail: This challenging hike takes visitors to the summit of Te Mata Peak, where they can enjoy panoramic views of Hawke’s Bay and the surrounding landscape.

  • Tuki Tuki River Trail: This scenic biking trail follows the Tuki Tuki River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, with plenty of opportunities to stop and take in the scenery along the way.

Best Things to Do in Flaxmere: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Flaxmere offers a range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests:

  • Art Gallery Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of the many art galleries and studios in Flaxmere, and meet some of the talented artists and craftsmen who call this area home.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Hawke’s Bay is renowned for its wineries, and visitors can take a tour of some of the region’s best vineyards, including Black Barn Vineyards, Elephant Hill, and Craggy Range.

  • Outdoor Adventure Tour: Adrenaline junkies can get their fix with a range of outdoor adventure tours, including kayaking, bungee jumping, and surfing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Flaxmere

For visitors who want to make the most of their time in Flaxmere, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Flaxmere and check into your accommodation. Take a stroll around town and visit the Flaxmere Library and Community Centre.

  • Day 2: Visit the Otane Heritage Trail and learn about the rich cultural history of the area.

  • Day 3: Hike to the summit of Te Mata Peak and enjoy panoramic views of Hawke’s Bay.

  • Day 4: Take a wine tasting tour of some of Hawke’s Bay’s best vineyards.

  • Day 5: Explore the Gannet Colony at Cape Kidnappers and take a guided tour of the seabirds’ nesting grounds.

  • Day 6: Take an outdoor adventure tour, such as kayaking or bungee jumping.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy some time in Flaxmere before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Flaxmere

No visit to Flaxmere would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Flaxmere include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kumara: A traditional Maori dish made from sweet potato, kumara is a flavorful and nutritious staple of New Zealand cuisine.

  • Fish and Chips: A classic Kiwi dish, fish and chips are available at many local restaurants and takeaways.

  • Lamb: New Zealand is renowned for its high-quality lamb, and visitors to Flaxmere can sample some of the region’s best at local restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Hawke’s Bay Wines: With over 200 vineyards in the region, Hawke’s Bay is famous for its fine wines, including Chardonnay, Merlot, and Syrah.

  • Emerson’s Beer: This award-winning craft brewery produces a range of unique and flavorful beers, including the popular Pilsner and London Porter.

What to Buy in Flaxmere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Flaxmere

Flaxmere is a multi-ethnic suburb with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions. Some of the key aspects of Maori culture to be aware of include:

  • Hongi: This is the traditional Maori greeting, where two people press their noses together.

  • Tapu: This concept refers to anything that is sacred or restricted, and visitors should be mindful of areas or objects that are considered tapu.

  • Whanau: This is the Maori word for family, and visitors to Flaxmere will find a warm and welcoming community that values family and community connections.

Best Time to Visit Flaxmere

Flaxmere enjoys a mild, temperate climate year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months from December to February. This is when the weather is at its best, with long sunny days and warm temperatures. Visitors should be aware that this is also peak tourist season, so be sure to book accommodation and tours in advance.
