Whakatane, a charming coastal town located in the Bay of Plenty region of New Zealand’s North Island, is a gem just waiting to be discovered. With its stunning scenery, rich culture, and endless outdoor adventure opportunities, Whakatane is a must-visit destination for any traveler to New Zealand. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore all of the top attractions, activities, and sights to see in Whakatane.

Introduction to Whakatane

Whakatane is located on the eastern coast of the North Island and is known for its stunning beaches, pristine forested landscapes, and rich Maori culture. It lies in the shadow of the active White Island volcano, which can be seen from the town’s shoreline on clear days. Despite its small size, Whakatane boasts a vibrant arts community, fantastic cafes and restaurants, and plenty of attractions to keep visitors busy for days.

List of Places to Visit in Whakatane

Whakatane is home to a diverse range of culturally important attractions, natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, tours, and food and drink scenes. Here’s a list of our top picks for places to visit in Whakatane.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mataatua Wharenui Cultural Centre
  • Te Koputu a te Whanga a Toi Museum
  • Ohope Beach Historical Walk
  • Whakatane Kiwi Trust

Best Things to Do in Whakatane: Activities and Tours

  • Dolphin and Whale Watching Tours
  • Guided Kayak Tours of Ohiwa Harbour
  • Jet Boat Tour of the Whakatane River
  • Scenic Helicopter Flights over White Island

Week-Long Itinerary for Whakatane

If you’re looking to spend a whole week exploring Whakatane, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activities
1 Visit Mataatua Wharenui Cultural Centre, walk the Ohope Beach Historical Walk, and try some local cuisine for dinner.
2 Hike the Tarawera River Trail, followed by an afternoon dolphin watching tour.
3 Take a guided kayaking tour of Ohiwa Harbour and visit the Whakatane Kiwi Trust.
4 Explore the Mokoroa Falls Track before taking a scenic helicopter flight over White Island.
5 Enjoy a leisurely day at Ohiwa Beach, followed by a sunset dinner overlooking the harbor.
6 Take a jet boat tour of the Whakatane River, followed by dinner at one of the town’s many great restaurants.
7 Visit Moutohorā Island for a day trip filled with hiking, birdwatching, and beach time.

Food and Drink Scene of Whakatane

Whakatane boasts plenty of restaurants and cafes serving up delicious local cuisine, made with fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. To get the full food and drink experience of Whakatane, we recommend trying the following:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips from any of the local shops along the Whakatane River.
  • Kai moana (seafood) from any of the area’s celebrated seafood restaurants.
  • Sustainable produce and fresh-caught game cooked on an open fire at Patutahi Plateau Winery.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beers from the Mata brewery on the outskirts of town.
  • Award-winning wines at the Ohope-based Toi Toi winery.
  • Local coffee from Tohora Coffee Roastery, which sources beans from the volcanic soils of the Bay of Plenty.

What to Buy in Whakatane: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Whakatane with you, we suggest picking up a few of the following souvenirs and products:

Local Culture and Customs of Whakatane

Whakatane has a rich Maori cultural heritage that is still very much alive in the town’s daily life. Visitors should keep in mind the following local customs and traditions:

  • Remove your shoes before entering a marae (Maori meeting place).
  • Always ask before taking photos of people, places, or objects of cultural significance.
  • Respect tikanga Maori (Maori customs and protocols) when attending any cultural event.

Best Time to Visit Whakatane

Whakatane has a subtropical climate, with warm, humid summers and mild winters. The best time to visit is during the summer months (December to February), when temperatures are warm, rain is minimal, and outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whakatane

To have the best possible experience in Whakatane, we recommend following these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, especially when visiting Maori sites.
  • Try as many local dishes and ingredients as possible.
  • Take advantage of the region’s outdoor adventure opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Swim outside of designated areas or in unsafe waters.
  • Rub the noses of the bronze statues in town - it’s not a local custom.
  • Ignore warnings or advice from local authorities regarding weather or outdoor conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Whakatane

Whakatane is generally a very safe place to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should still exercise common sense and caution, especially when swimming or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Whakatane

Whakatane has a modern hospital and several medical clinics, as well as pharmacies and other healthcare services. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise.

Getting to Whakatane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whakatane is located about 90 minutes’ drive from Tauranga, Bay of Plenty’s main city, and about two and a half hours’ drive from Auckland. Here are your options for getting to Whakatane:

Airports Near Whakatane

The closest airport to Whakatane is Rotorua Regional Airport, which is about 85km away.

Bus Stations Near Whakatane

Intercity and ManaBus both run regular bus services to Whakatane from Tauranga and other nearby cities.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies located in Tauranga and Auckland, giving visitors the freedom to explore Whakatane and its surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Whakatane

While Whakatane is a small town, there are several transportation options available for visitors:

  • Walking or bicycling is the best way to explore the town and its nearby attractions.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing apps like Uber are available for those who need transportation.
  • Rental cars and campervans are an excellent choice for those looking to explore the region’s surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Whakatane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There’s no shortage of nearby destinations and excursions to enjoy after exploring Whakatane. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Visit the geothermal wonderland of Rotorua.
  • Explore the rugged terrain of the Urewera National Park.
  • Take a scenic drive along the Coromandel Peninsula.

Conclusion

Whakatane is a hidden gem that offers something for every traveler. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor adventure opportunities, Whakatane is the perfect destination to add to your New Zealand itinerary. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Whakatane.
