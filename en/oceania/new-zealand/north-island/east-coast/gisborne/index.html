Introduction to Gisborne

Nestled on the East Coast of New Zealand’s North Island, Gisborne is a city known for its rich Maori culture, stunning beaches, and scenic vineyards. Fondly called the “sunshine capital” of New Zealand, Gisborne is the first city in the world to see the sun each day.

Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker, or culture enthusiast, Gisborne has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Gisborne

Here are some of the must-visit places in Gisborne:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tairawhiti Museum - Explore the fascinating history and culture of Gisborne and the East Coast through the museum’s extensive collections and exhibitions.

  2. Eastwoodhill Arboretum - Discover New Zealand’s largest and most comprehensive collection of exotic and indigenous trees and shrubs at this stunning garden.

  3. Te Poho-o-Rawiri Meeting House - Immerse yourself in Maori culture and history at this beautifully restored meeting house.

Natural Attractions of Gisborne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Waikanae Beach - Soak up the sun, go surfing, or take a leisurely stroll on this popular and picturesque beach.

  2. Titirangi Reserve - Enjoy stunning views of Poverty Bay and the city from the top of Titirangi Hill, and explore the park’s walking tracks and historical landmarks.

  3. Rere Rockslide - Brave the exhilarating 60-meter rockslide, one of the most popular adventure activities in Gisborne.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gisborne

Gisborne offers a wealth of hiking and biking routes, each with its unique landscapes and challenges. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Mount Hikurangi - This challenging hike to the summit of Mount Hikurangi offers stunning views of the East Coast and is a significant cultural site for Maori.

  2. Motu Trails - Comprising three unique trails, the Motu Trails offer a range of mountain biking and walking options, from relatively easy to challenging, through stunning scenery.

  3. Te Araroa Trail - Follow the footsteps of the country’s longest and most comprehensive walking trail, which runs through Gisborne and provides stunning views of the northern coastline.

Best Things to Do in Gisborne: Activities and Tours

  1. Wine Tasting - Gisborne is known for its wine production, and there are several vineyards to explore and taste local wines.

  2. Surfing - With shallow, east-facing beaches, coastal Gisborne makes an ideal surf location, even for novices.

  3. Fishing - Get up close to nature and go fishing in the East Coast’s abundance of famous locations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gisborne

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Gisborne that can help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Soak up the sunshine at Waikanae Beach

Day 2: Explore the local arts and crafts scene in the city centre.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Mount Hikurangi.

Day 4: Visit the Tairawhiti Museum and learn about Gisborne’s history and cultural heritage.

Day 5: Go wine tasting in the local vineyards.

Day 6: Bike the Motu Trails through stunning scenery.

Day 7: Take a tour of the Rere Rockslide.

Food and Drink Scene of Gisborne

Gisborne is famous for its fresh seafood, locally produced wines, and superb coffee. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips - Enjoy fresh fish and chips from the harbor.

  2. Hāngi – A traditional Maori dish that is cooked underground.

  3. Bluff Oysters - On the east coast enjoy some of the most delicate and delicious oysters you will ever taste.

Local Beverages

  1. Wines - Try Gisborne’s pocket full of sunshine and explore the vineyards of the region.

  2. Coffee - Gisborne has a fantastic coffee scene with a lot of famous coffee shops.

Where to Stay in Gisborne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There’s no shortage of affordable and comfortable accommodation options in Gisborne. From backpacker hostels to luxury hotels, here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Flamingo Motel - Experience a comfortable stay at Flamingo Motel while also being a hop skip and jump to the city.

  2. YHA Gisborne - Stay in budget-friendly accommodation while experiencing the beauty of the city.

  3. Beachcomber Motel - Enjoy the ocean views and relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Gisborne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gisborne offers a fascinating array of souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Woodcarvings and Carved Jade - Purchase authentic Maori woodcarvings and carved jade pieces to take home.

  2. Wine and Beer – Because of the region’s reputation as a wine destination, Gisborne is an excellent place to purchase wine and beer souvenirs.

  3. Local Handicrafts - Buy locally produced textiles, jewelry, and pottery pieces from the local arts and crafts scene.

Local Culture and Customs of Gisborne

The Maori culture plays a significant role in Gisborne’s identity and heritage. Visitors should be respectful and mindful while experiencing New Zealand’s culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Gisborne

Gisborne can be visited throughout the year, with unique things to do in different seasons. The warmest months are December and January, making it a great time to enjoy the beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gisborne

Do:

  1. Respect the Maori culture and heritage.

  2. Try the local wine and seafood.

  3. Take plenty of photos and explore the beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  1. Litter the beaches or natural areas.

  2. Ignore traffic signals and speed limits in the city.

  3. Ignore local customs and etiquette.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gisborne

Gisborne is generally a safe city, with a relatively low crime rate. Visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as keeping personal belongings secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Gisborne

Gisborne has a well-equipped hospital and medical clinics, though visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before travelling.

Getting to Gisborne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gisborne

Gisborne Airport is an easy point of entry into the city. Flights arrive and depart daily from the airport with a range of airlines.

Bus Stations Near Gisborne

There are many bus services that connect Gisborne to other cities and towns of New Zealand.

Other Transportation Options

Renting a car is also an option for getting to and travelling around Gisborne.

How to Get Around in Gisborne

Walking is a great way to explore the city centre, but to get around with ease and reach further destinations, renting a car/campervan or using a bike is best.

Where to Go Next from Gisborne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Napier - Explore the Art Deco architecture, wine country, and Hawkes Bay coastline.

  2. Auckland - Take the 6-hour drive from Gisborne to Auckland and experience the buzz of New Zealand’s largest city.

  3. Rotorua – Experience the cultural heart of New Zealand, volcanic activity, and beautiful natural scenery.

Conclusion

Gisborne offers a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, history, and culture. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan a perfect trip to this beautiful city, with something for everyone.
