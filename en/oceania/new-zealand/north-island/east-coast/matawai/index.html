A Comprehensive Guide to Matawai

Located in the East Coast region of North Island, New Zealand, Matawai is a small settlement with a population of just over 100 people. However, what it lacks in size, it makes up for in its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a quiet retreat or an adventure-packed holiday, Matawai is the perfect destination, and this comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Matawai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Matawai Marae - This is the local Maori meeting house where visitors can learn about the culture and traditions of the local people.
  • Tolaga Bay Wharf - This historic wharf is the longest in New Zealand and is a popular spot for fishing and sightseeing.
  • Gisborne Museum - Just a short drive from Matawai, this museum showcases the history and culture of the Gisborne region.

Natural Attractions of Matawai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rere Rockslide - This natural rockslide is a popular attraction for locals and visitors alike, offering a thrilling ride down the smooth rock face.
  • Piper’s Lodge - This scenic spot offers hiking trails, horse riding, fishing, and hunting.
  • Mt Hikurangi - This sacred mountain is important to local Maori culture and offers stunning views from its summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matawai

  • Te Hau White Track - This hiking track takes visitors through native forests and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Motu Trails - This network of mountain biking trails offers a range of options for all skill levels, including easy family rides and more challenging routes.

Best Things to Do in Matawai: Activities and Tours

  • Tubing on the Mata River - A fun and relaxing way to see the local countryside.
  • Fishing and Hunting - There are plenty of opportunities for hunting and fishing in the surrounding area.
  • Guided Tours - Local guides offer a range of tours, including cultural tours, hiking trips, and wildlife watching.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matawai

Day Activity
1 Explore Matawai and visit local attractions
2 Hike the Te Hau White Track or mountain bike the Motu Trails
3 Visit the Tolaga Bay Wharf and go fishing or sightseeing
4 Explore the Rere Rockslide and nearby waterfalls
5 Visit the Gisborne Museum and learn about the history and culture of the region
6 Go tubing on the Mata River or try horse riding at Piper’s Lodge
7 Hike Mt Hikurangi and enjoy the stunning views from the summit

Food and Drink Scene of Matawai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangi - A traditional Maori meal cooked in an underground oven.
  • Fish and Chips - Freshly caught fish served with crispy fries.
  • Kumara Pie - A savoury pie made with sweet potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Gisborne Wine - Known for its Chardonnay, Gisborne produces some of the best wine in New Zealand.
  • Lemon and Paeroa - A refreshing soft drink made with lemon and mineral water.

Where to Stay in Matawai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Piper’s Lodge - This lodge offers a range of accommodation options, including cabins and camping.
  • Matawai Hotel - A classic country hotel offering comfortable rooms and a bar and restaurant.
  • Airbnb - There are several Airbnb options in the area, including houses and cottages.

What to Buy in Matawai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pounamu - Also known as greenstone or jade, this precious stone holds great cultural significance for Maori people.
  • Honey - The local honey is renowned for its unique flavour and health benefits.
  • Wood Carvings - Beautifully crafted carvings of animals, people, and cultural symbols.

Local Culture and Customs of Matawai

Matawai is heavily influenced by Maori culture, with many residents proudly practicing their traditional customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect these customs and learn about the local culture during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Matawai

The best time to visit Matawai is in the summer months (December to February), when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) also offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matawai

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Pack warm and waterproof clothing for outdoor activities
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Throw litter or disturb wildlife
  • Take precious stones or artefacts from cultural sites
  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines

Safety and Health

Safety in Matawai

Matawai is a safe and peaceful place, but visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety. This includes being aware of their surroundings when hiking or exploring the outdoors and following safety guidelines for activities such as fishing and hunting.

Health in Matawai

Visitors to Matawai should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and are up to date on their vaccinations. It is also important to bring any necessary medication and to take precautions against sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Matawai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matawai

The nearest airport to Matawai is in Gisborne, which is approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Matawai

There are no major bus stations in Matawai, but regular services run between Gisborne and other major centres in the North Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Matawai by car or camper van, with several major highways passing through the region.

How to Get Around in Matawai

The best way to get around Matawai is by car or mountain bike, as the area is quite spread out and there are many scenic routes to explore. Alternatively, visitors can take guided tours or hire a local driver.

Where to Go Next from Matawai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gisborne - Just an hour’s drive away, Gisborne offers a range of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Bay of Plenty - This scenic region is just a few hours’ drive from Matawai and offers beautiful beaches, hot pools, and outdoor activities.
  • Rotorua - Known for its geothermal attractions and Maori culture, Rotorua is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the North Island.

Conclusion

Matawai may be a small settlement, but it offers a wealth of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed holiday, Matawai is the perfect destination. By following the advice and tips in this guide, you can ensure that your trip to Matawai is a memorable one.
