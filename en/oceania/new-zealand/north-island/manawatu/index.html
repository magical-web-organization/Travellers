Introduction to Manawatu

Located in the North Island of New Zealand, Manawatu has a diverse range of attractions and enchanting landscapes, making it a must-visit place for nature lovers, adventurers, and cultural enthusiasts. This region encompasses forests, mountains, rivers, and most of the Tararua and Ruahine ranges, providing a perfect outdoor playground full of unforgettable experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Manawatu

Here are some of the most popular tourist destinations in Manawatu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Te Manawa Museum: This museum is a perfect place to learn about the history and culture of the Manawatu region through interactive exhibits, art galleries, and historical collections.
  • New Zealand Rugby Museum: This museum offers an impressive experience for rugby fans, showcasing the rich history and accomplishments of New Zealand’s national rugby team, the All Blacks.
  • Feilding Farmers’ Market: Visiting this farmers’ market is an excellent way to experience the local produce and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Manawatu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manawatu Gorge: Located on the edge of the Tararua Range, this spectacular natural wonder is perfect for hiking, biking, rock climbing, and enjoying panoramic views.
  • Pukaha Mount Bruce Wildlife Centre: This wildlife center offers visitors an opportunity to see New Zealand’s unique and endangered birds, such as the Kiwi, Takahe, and Kakariki, in their natural habitat.
  • Palmerston North Esplanade Scenic Railway: Take a scenic railway ride through the beautiful scenery on Palmerston North’s Esplanade, observing the park’s wildlife and splendor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manawatu

Manawatu has a vast network of tracks for hiking, mountain biking, and trail running, such as:

  • Manawatu River Pathway: An easy 8.7 km trail that follows the Manawatu River through the city of Palmerston North.
  • The Ashhurst Domain Walk: A gentle 35-minute walk with scenic viewpoints starting from Ashhurst village.
  • Manawatu Gorge Track: A moderate track that takes 3-4 hours to traverse the mountainous terrain with stunning views of the Manawatu Gorge.

Best Things to Do in Manawatu: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for some exciting and adventurous experiences, Manawatu offers a range of activities and tours, such as:

  • Bungy Jumping: Face your fear of heights with a 50-meter bungy jump at the Manawatu Gorge.
  • Jet Boating: Enjoy a thrilling ride on the rapids of the Rangitikei River on this one-hour jet boating tour.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Soar above the Manawatu landscapes in a hot air balloon tour and enjoy breathtaking panoramic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manawatu

Here’s an itinerary for a perfect week in Manawatu:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Te Manawa Museum and Victoria Esplanade Gardens.
2 Explore the Manawatu Gorge and walk through the Sculpture Gardens in Palmerston North.
3 Go for a mountain biking adventure in the Tararua Ranges.
4 Visit the Pukaha Mount Bruce Wildlife Centre.
5 Enjoy a scenic railway ride through the Palmerston North’s Esplanade.
6 Experience bungy jumping at the Manawatu Gorge.
7 Take a hot air balloon ride for an unforgettable aerial view of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Manawatu

Manawatu’s food and drink scene is home to fresh, locally sourced produce, and innovative cuisine. Here’s some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Manawatu Beef and Lamb: This region is well known for its high-quality beef and lamb, and there’s no better place to experience it than in Manawatu. Head to the local restaurants for the best of this region’s culinary delights.
  • Cattle Flat White Cheese: A soft, creamy cheese perfect for food enthusiasts who like to explore local tastes.

Local Beverages

  • Palmerston North Speight’s Ale House: A craft brewery that serves a wide selection of beers and wine.
  • Coffee Supreme: Palmerston North is famous for some of the best coffee regions in the country. Try this coffee from the Coffee Supreme cafe chain to experience its unique taste!

Where to Stay in Manawatu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

With a variety of accommodation options, you will be spoiled for choice. Here are some excellent options:

  • Hotel Coachman: Located in Palmerston North, this hotel offers a choice of rooms, a restaurant, and a spa for a comfortable stay.
  • Feilding Motel: A boutique motel located near Manawatu Gorge and the Feilding Friday Farmers’ Market.

What to Buy in Manawatu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring the memories of Manawatu home with you with these local souvenirs:

  • Manawatu Honey: Take a jar of honey as a well-deserved sweet treat.
  • Jams and preserves: Pick up some homemade organic jams and preserves as a gift for a loved one.

Local Culture and Customs of Manawatu

Since the majority of the population of Manawatu is Maori, their language, customs, and culture should be respected and valued. It’s essential to learn some basic phrases like ‘kia ora’ meaning hello or ‘tena koe’ meaning thank you and acknowledge mana whenua, which signifies spiritual authority.

Best Time to Visit Manawatu

Manawatu can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is from November to March during the summer season when the weather is warm and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manawatu

Do:

  • Respect locals and educate yourself about the Maori culture.
  • Visit the Puanga Festival, which celebrates the Maori New Year.
  • Explore the nature, take in the scenery and leave it undamaged.

Don’t:

  • Assume that all Maori customs and views are the same.
  • Litter or damage any part of nature and the environment.
  • Trespass off public land without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manawatu

New Zealand is a safe country to visit, and Manawatu is no exception. However, it’s always essential to be careful on the trails and track routes and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Manawatu

It’s essential to get travel insurance before visiting Manawatu. Also, make sure you have all necessary vaccinations, inform the accommodation provider if you have any health concerns.

Getting to Manawatu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manawatu

The closest airport to Manawatu is Palmerston North International Airport, with domestic and international flight options.

Bus Stations Near Manawatu

There are regular bus services from Wellington to Palmerston North, Feilding, and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

You can rent a car from the airport or use taxi or shuttle services. Alternatively, a scenic train ride from Wellington will allow you to enjoy the panoramic views of the Manawatu region.

How to Get Around in Manawatu

Getting around Manawatu is easy, thanks to an extensive network of roads, trails, and tracks. You can either use your vehicle or rent one, or take public transport.

Where to Go Next from Manawatu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After visiting Manawatu, you can explore nearby areas, such as:

  • Wellington: the capital city of New Zealand, famous for its museum, galleries, and the Wellington Cable Car.
  • Wairarapa: A scenic region that offers gourmet food, wine, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Manawatu offers visitors a wide range of attractions, breathtaking natural vistas, and a strong cultural identity. This comprehensive tour guide will surely make your visit to Manawatu an outstanding one, from hiking and biking routes to cultural attractions, activities, and local cuisine. Discover the beauty and rich history of Manawatu for an unforgettable travel experience.
