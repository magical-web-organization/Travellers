Introduction to Shannon

Shannon is a small town located in the Manawatu region of the North Island of New Zealand. Known for its stunning natural scenery and rich cultural history, Shannon is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience the beauty of New Zealand. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Shannon.

List of Places to Visit in Shannon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shannon Railway Heritage Trust Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the railway history of Shannon and the wider Manawatu region. It features a number of exhibits showcasing the railway technology from the 19th century to the present day.
  • Owlcatraz Native Bird and Wildlife Park: This park is home to over 50 different species of birds, including the rare North Island Brown Kiwi. Visitors can get up close and personal with the birds and learn about their native habitats and conservation efforts.
  • Te Ahu a Turanga Marae: This is a traditional meeting place for the Ngati Raukawa iwi (tribe). Visitors can learn about Maori culture and history through tours and interactive experiences.

Natural Attractions of Shannon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Te Apiti - Manawatu Gorge: This stunning gorge is home to a variety of walking and hiking trails, as well as majestic waterfalls and breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.
  • Palmerston North Esplanade: Located just a short drive from Shannon, this park features a large lake, walking trails, and a variety of outdoor activities, including fishing and boating.
  • Foxton Beach: This beautiful beach is a great spot for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing. Visitors can also check out the nearby Foxton Windmill, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shannon

  • Te Apiti - Manawatu Gorge Track: This 11-kilometer track takes hikers through the heart of the Te Apiti - Manawatu Gorge, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and lush vegetation.
  • Owlcatraz Kiwi Track: This 2-kilometer track takes visitors through the Owlcatraz Native Bird and Wildlife Park, offering the chance to see a variety of native birds up close.
  • Palmerston North City Walkway: This 16-kilometer walkway takes visitors through the heart of Palmerston North, with stops at a variety of cultural and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Shannon: Activities and Tours

  • JetBoat Tours: Take a thrilling ride on the Manawatu River, with stops at a variety of scenic locations along the way.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the lush green hills of the Manawatu region on horseback.
  • Wine Tours: Visit some of the region’s best wineries and sample a variety of local wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Shannon

Must-Try Dishes

  • New Zealand Fish and Chips: This classic Kiwi dish features locally caught fish, served with a side of crispy fries and tangy tartar sauce.
  • Lamb Roast: New Zealand is known for its high-quality lamb, and a traditional lamb roast is a must-try dish for any visitor.
  • Kiwi Burger: This classic New Zealand burger is made with locally sourced beef, topped with beetroot, fried egg, and pineapple.

Local Beverages

  • New Zealand Wine: The Manawatu region is home to a number of excellent wineries, producing high-quality Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Noir, and Chardonnay.
  • Craft Beer: New Zealand has a growing craft beer scene, with a number of small breweries producing unique and delicious brews.
  • Kombucha: This fermented tea beverage has become increasingly popular in New Zealand, with a number of locally produced brands available in the Shannon area.

Where to Stay in Shannon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shannon Motor Lodge: This family-owned and operated motel offers comfortable rooms with free Wi-Fi and on-site parking, as well as a communal BBQ area.
  • The Jolly Stockman: This historic hotel features a restaurant, bar, and comfortable guestrooms, and is located just a short drive from Shannon.
  • Kowhai Grove: This charming guesthouse offers a unique and authentic New Zealand experience, with friendly hosts and a variety of amenities, including a hot tub and outdoor seating area.

What to Buy in Shannon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kiwiana Gifts: Visit one of the many gift shops in Shannon to pick up a variety of Kiwiana souvenirs, including t-shirts, keychains, and other memorabilia.
  • Locally Made Crafts: The Manawatu region is known for its high-quality handcrafted goods, including pottery, textiles, and woodwork.
  • New Zealand Wine: Bring home a bottle of your favorite local wine to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Shannon

New Zealanders are known for their friendly and welcoming attitude towards visitors, and Shannon is no exception. Visitors should be respectful of Maori culture and customs, and be open to learning about the rich history and traditions of the region.

Best Time to Visit Shannon

The best time to visit Shannon is during the summer months (December-February), when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the region is beautiful year-round, with stunning scenery and a variety of outdoor activities available no matter the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shannon

Do:

  • Be respectful of Maori culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in the region.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind when exploring the natural attractions of Shannon.
  • Ignore any warning signs or safety guidelines when hiking or exploring outdoors.
  • Drive under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shannon

Shannon is a relatively safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and a friendly local population. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions when exploring the region, particularly when hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities.

Health in Shannon

New Zealand has a high standard of healthcare, with top-quality hospitals and medical facilities available throughout the country, including in Shannon. However, visitors should always take appropriate health and safety precautions, including wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated when outdoors for extended periods of time.

Getting to Shannon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shannon

  • Palmerston North Airport: This small regional airport is located just a short drive from Shannon, and offers flights to and from a variety of destinations throughout New Zealand.

Bus Stations Near Shannon

  • Palmerston North Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the heart of Palmerston North, just a short drive from Shannon. It offers bus services to a variety of destinations throughout New Zealand.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from a number of rental agencies located at Palmerston North Airport and in nearby Palmerston North.

How to Get Around in Shannon

The best way to get around Shannon is by car, either by renting a vehicle or using your own. However, there are also a variety of taxi and shuttle services available in the region, as well as bus services to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Shannon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wellington: Located just a short drive from Shannon, Wellington is the capital city of New Zealand and is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, as well as its restaurants, bars, and nightlife.
  • Taranaki: Located to the west of Shannon, Taranaki is known for its stunning natural scenery, including the mighty Mount Taranaki and the rugged Taranaki coastline.
  • Napier/Hawke’s Bay: Located to the east of Shannon, Napier/Hawke’s Bay is a popular destination for wine lovers, as well as those interested in Art Deco architecture and history.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventure, cultural landmarks, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, Shannon offers something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly local population, Shannon is truly a must-visit destination for travelers exploring the North Island of New Zealand.
