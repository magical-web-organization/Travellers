Introduction to Levin

Levin is a picturesque town located in Manawatu, North Island, New Zealand. It offers a range of attractions, including museums, natural landscapes, adventure activities, delicious food, and friendly culture. Whether you’re traveling solo, with your partner, friends, or family, Levin has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Levin, we’ll cover all the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs and culture. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Levin

Here are some top places to visit in Levin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Te Takere – A public library that includes a museum, art gallery, cafe, and community center. Te Takere is a unique place that brings together the diverse cultural arts and history of Levin.

  • Levin Adventure Park – A recreational park that offers exciting activities such as ziplining, paintball, archery, and mini-golf.

Natural Attractions of Levin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Horowhenua – A beautiful lake located just 5 km from Levin. Here you can go fishing, swimming, or simply enjoy the serene view.

  • Tararua Range – A must-visit for hikers. Tararua Range has amazing trails and provides breathtaking views from the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Levin

Levin is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  • South Hokio Beach Track – A 4.2 km track that offers stunning views of the beach while walking through a peaceful wooded area.

  • Makahika Outdoor Pursuits Centre Circuit – A 10 km track with beautiful scenery and challenging terrain, perfect for intermediate hikers.

Best Things to Do in Levin: Activities and Tours

  • Garden Tour – Levin is famous for its beautiful gardens. Join the tour and explore the gardens throughout the city.

  • Fishing – Levin has a great fishing culture. Hire a boat or fish from the bank, and enjoy a peaceful day outdoors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Levin

Day 1: Visit Te Takere and Levin Adventure Park.

Day 2: Hiking day to Tararua Range.

Day 3: Bicycle ride through South Hokio Beach Track.

Day 4: Visit Lake Horowhenua for a swim and picnic.

Day 5: Take a garden tour and explore the beauty of Levin.

Day 6: Go on a fishing trip on Lake Horowhenua.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy local food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Levin

Levin has a vibrant food and drink culture. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meat pie – A New Zealand classic that you can easily find in Levin.

  • Feijoa – A local fruit that has a unique flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Wine – Levin has some excellent vineyards, producing exceptional wines. Don’t miss the opportunity to taste locally produced wine.

  • Craft beer – There are various local breweries in Levin that offer great craft beer.

Where to Stay in Levin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Levin has plenty of accommodation options to suit your budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Levin Motor Lodge – A spacious and comfortable motel located in downtown Levin.

  • Zenbu Motel – A budget-friendly option that doesn’t compromise on quality.

  • The Woolshed at Strathnaver Park – A charming guesthouse that offers a unique experience in the beautiful countryside.

What to Buy in Levin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Manuka Honey – A honey produced by bees that feed on the Manuka plant, a New Zealand native.

  • Possum Merino – A wool blend that is durable and warm, often used in clothing or accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Levin

Levin is a friendly and welcoming community that takes pride in its diverse cultural heritage. Always greet people with “Kia ora” (hello) or “Haere mai” (welcome).

Best Time to Visit Levin

Levin is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between December and February when the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Levin

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Explore the natural surroundings and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Disrespect people, places, or property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Levin

Levin is a safe and friendly town, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Levin

New Zealand has a great health care system. If you need medical assistance, there are plenty of medical centers and hospitals in Levin.

Getting to Levin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Levin

Levin doesn’t have an airport, but the closest airports are:

  • Wellington Airport – Located 90 km south of Levin.

  • Palmerston North Airport – Located 30 km north of Levin.

Bus Stations Near Levin

Levin has a bus stop with regular services to Palmerston North and Wellington.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a taxi to travel to Levin.

How to Get Around in Levin

The best way to get around in Levin is by car or bike. You can also explore the city center by foot.

Where to Go Next from Levin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palmerston North – A vibrant urban center located just 30 km north of Levin.

  • Whanganui National Park – A beautiful and rugged park located just 80 km northwest of Levin.

Conclusion

Levin is a delightful town with plenty of activities and attractions that cater to a variety of interests. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to explore all that Levin has to offer. Plan your trip, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in Levin, New Zealand.
