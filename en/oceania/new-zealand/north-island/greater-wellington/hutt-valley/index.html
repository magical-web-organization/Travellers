Introduction to Hutt Valley

Located in Greater Wellington, in the North Island of New Zealand, lies the stunning Hutt Valley. The valley is situated on the eastern side of Wellington Harbour and is surrounded by the Rimutaka Ranges – a mountain range that offers breathtaking scenic views.

The valley is home to over 142,000 people and boasts a rich cultural and historical heritage, with many museums and historical places to visit. The natural beauty of the valley is also unparalleled, with a range of outdoor adventures and scenic views.

This comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Hutt Valley. Learn about the local culture and customs, the food and drink scene, and the natural and cultural attractions of this stunning destination.

List of Places to Visit in Hutt Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dowse Art Museum: A contemporary art museum in Lower Hutt, featuring both local and international works of art.
  • Petone Settlers Museum: A museum that highlights the colonial history of Petone and the Hutt Valley region.
  • Matiu/Somes Island: A predator-free island that was used as a quarantine station and internment camp.
  • Staglands Wildlife Reserve: A wildlife park where visitors can see both native and exotic animals, including deer, pigs, and birds.

Natural Attractions of Hutt Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belmont Regional Park: A park that offers stunning scenic views of the surrounding landscape, as well as hiking and mountain biking trails.
  • Rimutaka Rail Trail: A 115-kilometer trail that follows the path of an abandoned rail line, offering stunning views and a range of outdoor activities.
  • Kaitoke Regional Park: A park that boasts a beautiful landscape of bush-clad hills and a picturesque river, as well as walking and camping facilities.
  • Wainuiomata Coast: A rugged coastline with stunning views of the ocean and mountains, as well as opportunities for surfing and beachcombing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hutt Valley

  • Pakuratahi Forest: A nature reserve that boasts over 1800 hectares of land, with a range of hiking and mountain biking trails.
  • Eastern Bays Walkway: A scenic walkway that follows the coastline, providing stunning ocean views along the way.
  • Rimutaka Cycle Trail: A 115-kilometer trail that takes visitors through some of the most scenic parts of the valley, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and landscape.

Best Things to Do in Hutt Valley: Activities and Tours

  • Hutt River Trail: A trail that follows the Hutt River, providing visitors with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Upper Hutt City Library: A library that offers a range of activities and events for visitors of all ages.
  • Fishing and boating: The Hutt River is a popular spot for fishing and boating enthusiasts, with a variety of fish to catch and scenic views to enjoy.
  • Laser Strike: A fun and exciting activity where visitors can play laser tag in a futuristic setting.

Food and Drink Scene of Hutt Valley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paua fritters: A New Zealand delicacy made with paua (abalone) and batter, fried until golden.
  • Whitebait patties: Another New Zealand specialty, made with whitebait, flour, and seasonings, then fried.
  • Lamingtons: A classic Australian and New Zealand dessert, consisting of sponge cake, chocolate icing, and coconut flakes.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Wellington is known for its bustling craft beer scene, with a wide variety of local breweries to choose from.
  • Wine: The Hutt Valley region is home to a growing number of vineyards, producing high-quality local wines.

Where to Stay in Hutt Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Boulcott Lodge: A luxurious lodge with spacious rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Silverstream Retreat: A peaceful retreat with comfortable rooms and beautiful surroundings.
  • Garden View Bed & Breakfast: A cozy and charming bed & breakfast with a beautiful garden.
  • Central Hutt Apartment: A self-contained apartment in the heart of the city, with excellent amenities and a central location.

What to Buy in Hutt Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: The area around Hutt Valley produces some of the finest honey in New Zealand.
  • Manuka oil: A natural oil extracted from the manuka plant, known for its antibacterial properties.
  • Feathers and furs: Hutt Valley is home to a range of native birds and animals, making it an excellent place to buy feathers and furs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hutt Valley

Hutt Valley is home to a range of different cultures and communities, each with its unique customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to learn about these customs and show respect for local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hutt Valley

The best time to visit Hutt Valley is during the summer months, from December to February when the weather is warm and dry. However, the valley can be enjoyed year-round, with each season offering its unique beauty and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hutt Valley

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy the stunning scenic views and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the parks or on the hiking trails.
  • Disturb the local wildlife or habitats.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hutt Valley

Hutt Valley is generally considered a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables in the car and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Hutt Valley

Medical facilities in Hutt Valley are generally of a high standard, and visitors should have no problem finding adequate medical care if needed. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Hutt Valley.

Getting to Hutt Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hutt Valley

The nearest airport to Hutt Valley is Wellington International Airport, located about 30 minutes’ drive from the valley.

Bus Stations Near Hutt Valley

There are several bus services that connect Hutt Valley to Wellington and other nearby destinations, including the Metlink bus service and the Intercity bus service.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Hutt Valley, with the nearest train station being in Lower Hutt.

How to Get Around in Hutt Valley

There are several options for getting around Hutt Valley, including renting a car, taking public transportation, or using a taxi or rideshare service.

Where to Go Next from Hutt Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wairarapa: A beautiful region known for its stunning vineyards, located just to the east of Hutt Valley.
  • Lower Hutt: A nearby city known for its rich history and beautiful parks.
