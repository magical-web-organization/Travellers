Introduction to Masterton

Welcome to Masterton, a charming town located in the Wairarapa region of New Zealand’s North Island. Known for its picturesque scenery, outdoor adventures, and vibrant culture, Masterton offers an unforgettable travel experience for visitors of all ages.

From hiking and biking on scenic trails to exploring cultural attractions, museums, and historical sites, there’s something for everyone in Masterton. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip by highlighting the best things to do, places to visit, food and drink scene, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Masterton

Make the most out of your trip to Masterton by exploring its top attractions and landmarks. From natural wonders to cultural landmarks, here is a list of places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aratoi Wairarapa Museum of Art and History: Explore the rich history and culture of Masterton and Wairarapa through a collection of artworks, sculptures, and historical artifacts.

  • Queen Elizabeth Park: Located on the banks of the Waipoua River, the park features stunning gardens, a mini-golf course, and a playground for kids. It’s a perfect spot for a relaxing picnic or romantic stroll.

  • The Wool Shed Museum: Discover the history of the New Zealand wool industry at this unique museum, which is home to an impressive collection of shearing equipment, sheepskin products, and wool-related exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Masterton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pukaha Mount Bruce Wildlife Centre: Home to some of New Zealand’s most endangered birds and animals, the wildlife center offers a unique opportunity to learn about the conservation efforts of the region.

  • Castlepoint Scenic Reserve: A must-visit for nature enthusiasts, the reserve is known for its beautiful lighthouse, stunning rock formations, and sandy beaches.

  • Rere Falls Trail: Take a scenic hike along the Rere Falls Trail, which offers breathtaking views of the nearby Rere Rockslide and Wharekopae River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Masterton

Masterton is a paradise for outdoor lovers, boasting a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Here is a list of popular routes to add to your itinerary:

  • Castlepoint Walkway: Take a scenic hike along the Castlepoint Walkway, which offers stunning views of the lighthouse, the Castle Rock, and the rugged coastline.

  • The Tararua Range: For more experienced hikers, the Tararua Range offers challenging but rewarding trails that lead to scenic mountain vistas and hidden waterfalls.

  • Waiohine Gorge Track: This track offers an easy, family-friendly hike that leads to the stunning Waiohine Gorge, which features crystal-clear waterfalls and beautiful rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Masterton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or solo, Masterton has plenty of exciting activities and tours to suit your interests. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the breathtaking views of Masterton and the surrounding region from the sky in a hot air balloon, a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

  • Cycling Tour: Explore the town’s beautiful countryside by bike on a guided cycling tour, which takes you to some of the most scenic spots in the area.

  • Horseback Riding: Saddle up and take a leisurely horseback ride through the beautiful Wairarapa countryside, complete with stunning views and plenty of photo opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Masterton

To make the most out of your trip to Masterton, here’s a sample week-long itinerary that includes some of the town’s top attractions, activities, and dining options:

Day 1: Explore Masterton’s museums and historic sites, including Aratoi Wairarapa Museum of Art and History and The Wool Shed Museum. Enjoy a casual dinner at Cafe Strada or Ten O’Clock Cookie Bakery Cafe.

Day 2: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Castlepoint Walkway and enjoy a picnic lunch at Castlepoint Lighthouse. Spend the afternoon at Queen Elizabeth Park.

Day 3: Embark on an exhilarating hot air balloon ride over Masterton and the surrounding region and enjoy a champagne breakfast. In the afternoon, take a tour of the Pukaha Mount Bruce Wildlife Centre.

Day 4: Hike the Waiohine Gorge Track and have a picnic lunch by the beautiful waterfall. Afterward, rest up at your accommodation or take a short trip to the town of Carterton for dinner.

Day 5: Discover the Tararua Range on a guided hike and enjoy the stunning views. In the evening, go for a night walk at Mt. Holdsworth to see glowworms in the natural habitat.

Day 6: Spend the day adventuring at Rere Falls Trail and Wharekopae River. In the evening, grab dinner at one of the town’s local eateries, like Masterton’s own Lone Star.

Day 7: Relax and unwind with a massage or body treatment at a Masterton spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Masterton

Masterton is a hub of gourmet cuisine, offering a wide range of dishes and beverages to satisfy foodies of all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A New Zealand classic, fish and chips is a perfect meal after a long day of exploring. You can find great fish and chips shop all over Masterton, with most having plenty of outdoor seating areas.

  • Honey Toast: A local dessert treat made with honey, New Zealand-made ice cream, and toasted bread or cake.

  • Feijoas: Known in New Zeland as the pineapple guava they are used in a variety of ways from eating raw to making chutneys, jams, jellies and juice.

Local Beverages

  • Martinborough Wine: Masterton is located near Martinborough, a world-renowned wine region that produces some of New Zealand’s finest wines. Be sure to try a bottle of the area’s pinot noir or chardonnay.

  • Craft Beer: Masterton has a growing craft beer scene, with several local breweries, such as RedHare Brewing Company and Regent 58, offering delicious, unique brews.

Where to Stay in Masterton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Masterton offers a variety of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs and budget. From luxury hotels to backpacker hostels, here are some options to consider:

  • Copthorne Hotel and Resort Masterton: This upscale hotel offers spacious rooms and suites, an indoor pool, and an on-site restaurant and bar.

  • Gateway Motor Inn Masterton: Featuring clean, comfortable rooms and affordable rates, this accommodation is perfect for budget travelers and families.

  • The Summit Lodge: This cozy guesthouse offers beautiful views, a tranquil garden, and personalized service.

What to Buy in Masterton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Masterton is a treasure trove of unique, locally-made crafts and products. Here are some souvenirs and local products to add to your shopping list:

  • Wool Products: Masterton is well-known for its wool products, such as sheepskin rugs, merino wool gloves, and cozy blankets.

  • Honey: New Zealand’s pristine environment makes it an ideal place for beekeeping, and Masterton produces some of the country’s best honey.

  • Handmade Crafts: From pottery and glassware to textiles and jewelry, Masterton has a vibrant artisan scene that showcases the region’s beautiful natural surroundings.

Local Culture and Customs of Masterton

Masterton is a welcoming and friendly town with a rich cultural heritage. To respect the local culture, here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Greeting People: When meeting someone, it is customary to shake hands and say hello.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in Masterton or New Zealand in general as it is factored into the wages.

  • Respect for Nature: New Zealand is known for its pristine environment, and it’s customary to dispose of trash properly and respect the heritage sites in the areas.

Best Time to Visit Masterton

Masterton is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on what you’re interested in seeing or doing. Here are some tips to help you plan your trip:

  • Summer (December-February) and Autumn (March-May) are the best seasons for outdoor activities and hiking.

  • Winter (June-August) offers a chance to explore the town’s cultural attractions, museums, and galleries.

  • Spring(September-November) is ideal for enjoying the countryside’s scenery and gardens.

Do’s and Don’ts of Masterton

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind during your visit to Masterton:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Carry a water bottle especially if you plan to go on an extended hike or bike ride

  • Take a guided tour of the area, or have a car full of gasoline, as the distances between towns in the Wairarapa region can be quite long.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter- dispose of your trash properly.

  • Don’t pick any flowers or plants without permission.

Safety and Health

Here are some essential safety and health tips to consider during your visit:

Safety in Masterton

  • Keep an eye out for animals while walking or hiking outside marked tracks.

  • Always adhere to provided information about weather and fire safety.

  • Closely supervise kids, particularly in the waves or around rivers or beaches during tides.

Health in Masterton

  • New Zealand has very strict border policies to protect the country from pests and diseases. Be aware of what food products you can and can’t bring into the country.

  • In case of an emergency requiring an ambulance, please dial 111.

Getting to Masterton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to help you get to Masterton easily:

Airports Near Masterton

The closest major airport to Masterton is Wellington International Airport. From there, you can rent a car or take a shuttle to Masterton.

Bus Stations Near Masterton

The main bus station in Masterton is the Masterton i-SITE Visitor Information Center. You can find local bus services available to this station from around Wairarapa including train connections from Wellington.

Other Options

If you’re looking for a more adventurous way of getting to Masterton, consider taking the train or renting a car.

How to Get Around Masterton

Masterton is a compact and easy-to-navigate town, and you can explore most of the area on foot. Alternatively, renting a car, bike or hiring a taxi is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Masterton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Masterton is surrounded by other lovely towns and cities, including Featherston, Carterton, and Greytown. If seeking for more adventures, Taupo, Wellington and Napier are all within three hour driving distance

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a cultural buff, Masterton offers an abundance of attractions, activities, and sights to explore. Make sure to add this charming town to your itinerary for a truly unforgettable New Zealand adventure.
