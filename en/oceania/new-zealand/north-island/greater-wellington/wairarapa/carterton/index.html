Introduction to Carterton

Located in the heart of Wairarapa, Carterton is a charming town that offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this quaint town.

Carterton is surrounded by rolling hills, vineyards, and forests, making it a popular base for those who want to explore the nearby attractions. From hiking and biking to wine tasting and hot air balloon rides, the town has plenty of activities to keep you busy.

List of Places to Visit in Carterton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carterton has a rich cultural heritage and is home to some of the most fascinating museums and historical places in New Zealand. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Carterton District Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Carterton and its surrounding areas. From photographs and documents to antique items and tools, the museum offers a glimpse into the town’s past.

  • Stonehenge Aotearoa: This unique attraction is a modern-day interpretation of the original Stonehenge in England. The site features a series of standing stones that align with the stars and planets, making it perfect for stargazing enthusiasts.

  • Alwin Gallery: This art gallery features the works of local artists and is a great place to discover the town’s vibrant art scene.

Natural Attractions of Carterton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carterton’s location makes it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. From scenic drives to hiking trails, the town has plenty of natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Pukaha National Wildlife Centre: Located just a short drive from Carterton, this wildlife sanctuary is home to some of New Zealand’s rarest birds, including the kiwi, takahe, and kaka.

  • Queen Elizabeth Park: This beautiful park is perfect for picnics, bike rides, and walks. The park also has a miniature train that takes visitors on a fun ride through the park.

  • Gladstone Vineyard: Take a tour of this beautiful vineyard to learn about the wine-making process and taste some of the finest wines in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carterton

Carterton is a popular destination for hikers and bikers, thanks to its picturesque landscapes and numerous trails. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Rimutaka Rail Trail: This 115km trail follows the route of an old railway line and offers stunning views of the countryside.

  • Waiohine Gorge Track: This challenging track takes you through a gorge, past waterfalls and steep cliffs, and offers breathtaking views of the valley.

  • Mitre Flats Walk: This easy walk takes you through lush forest and past a stunning waterfall. The track is perfect for families with kids.

Best Things to Do in Carterton: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Carterton offers a range of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take to the skies and enjoy stunning views of the town and the surrounding country-side.

  • Climbing Mount Holdsworth: This challenging climb takes you to the summit of Mount Holdsworth, which offers panoramic views of Wairarapa.

  • Carterton Farmers’ Market: Held every Sunday, this market is a popular destination for foodies looking to sample local produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carterton

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary that covers some of the best attractions in Carterton:

  • Day 1: Visit the Carterton District Historical Society Museum and take a stroll through Queen Elizabeth Park.

  • Day 2: Take a tour of Stonehenge Aotearoa and visit the Alwin Gallery.

  • Day 3: Hike the Rimutaka Rail Trail or Mitre Flats Walk.

  • Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride over the town.

  • Day 5: Visit Pukaha National Wildlife Centre and do some wine tasting at Gladstone Vineyard.

  • Day 6: Go on a farm tour and learn about the region’s farming industry.

  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at a spa or take a scenic drive through the town’s countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Carterton

Carterton is a small town, but it has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb: Wairarapa is known for its high-quality lamb, and you can find it on the menus of many local restaurants.

  • Cheese: The region is also home to many artisan cheese makers, and you can sample some of the best cheeses at the local markets and restaurants.

  • Feijoa: This delicious fruit is a local specialty and is used in many dishes and drinks.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Wairarapa is one of New Zealand’s premier wine regions, and you can find some of the best wines in the region at Gladstone Vineyard and other local wineries.

  • Beer: There are several craft breweries in the area that offer a range of delicious brews.

Where to Stay in Carterton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carterton offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top picks:

  • The Claremont: This boutique hotel offers luxury accommodation and fine dining in a stunning setting.

  • Highwayman Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of town.

  • Gladstone Vineyard Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located on a beautiful vineyard and offers stunning views and comfortable accommodation.

What to Buy in Carterton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Carterton is known for its artisanal products, and there are several local shops where you can find unique souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • Carterton Craft Cottage: This shop showcases the work of local artisans and offers a range of unique and handmade products.

  • Wairarapa Farmers’ Market: This market is a great place to find fresh produce, artisanal products, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Carterton

Carterton has a strong community spirit and celebrates its culture through a range of festivals and events. The town is also known for its vibrant art scene and outdoor adventures. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Carterton

The best time to visit Carterton is during the summer months (December-February) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also beautiful during the autumn and winter months, when the countryside is painted in hues of gold and orange.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carterton

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take your time to explore the town’s natural and cultural attractions.

  • Support the local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Leave rubbish or litter in public places.

  • Ignore safety precautions while hiking or biking.

  • Disrespect the local people or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carterton

Carterton is a safe town for tourists, but visitors are advised to take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in Carterton

There are several medical clinics and pharmacies in Carterton, and visitors are advised to carry any necessary prescription medications with them.

Getting to Carterton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carterton

The nearest international airport to Carterton is Wellington International Airport, which is located about 80km away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Carterton

The InterCity bus service connects Carterton to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to get around Carterton and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Carterton

The best way to get around Carterton is by car, bike, or on foot. There are several car rental companies in town, and most of the attractions are within walking or biking distance.

Where to Go Next from Carterton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Carterton is located in the heart of the Wairarapa region, which offers plenty of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Martinborough: This picturesque wine town is just a short drive from Carterton and is known for its world-class Pinot Noir.

  • Castlepoint: This stunning beach is located on the east coast of Wairarapa and is perfect for swimming, surfing, and fishing.

  • Wellington: New Zealand’s capital city is just an hour’s drive from Carterton and offers a range of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining.

Conclusion

Carterton is a hidden gem in Wairarapa and offers something for everyone. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, the town has plenty of things to see and do. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing holiday or an action-packed adventure, Carterton is the perfect destination to explore the best of New Zealand’s North Island.
