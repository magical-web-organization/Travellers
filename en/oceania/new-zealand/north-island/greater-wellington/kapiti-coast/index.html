Introduction to Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast is a scenic region located in Greater Wellington, in the North Island of New Zealand. Kapiti Coast is popular among tourists for its stunning beaches, lush parks, vibrant wildlife, rich culture and history, and outdoor adventures. The region is named after Kapiti Island, a nature reserve and bird sanctuary off the coast.

Kapiti Coast is home to the Māori tribe of Ngāti Toa Rangatira, who arrived in the region in the 1820s. The tribe has a rich cultural heritage and has contributed significantly to New Zealand’s history and economy. Kapiti Coast is also known for its vibrant food and drink scene, which features local delicacies and beverages.

In this comprehensive tour guide for Kapiti Coast, you will discover the best attractions, activities, food, accommodation, and local culture of the region, ensuring you have an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kapiti Coast

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Kapiti Coast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nga Purapura: A cultural center showcasing Māori heritage, arts, and crafts, and hosting cultural events and workshops.
  2. Kapiti Coast Museum: A museum exhibiting the history and culture of Kapiti Coast, from the first settlers to modern-day developments.
  3. Wharemauku Stream Historic Walk: A trail showcasing the history and significance of Wharemauku Stream to the Māori tribe of Ngāti Toa Rangatira.
  4. Otaki Maori Racecourse: A historic racecourse established by the Māori tribe of Ngāti Toa Rangatira in 1886, hosting horse races and cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Kapiti Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kapiti Island: A nature reserve and bird sanctuary home to unique New Zealand wildlife, such as the Little Spotted Kiwi and Takahe. Visits are by permit only.
  2. Queen Elizabeth Park: A park offering a range of activities, such as walking trails, bike paths, wildlife watching, camping, and bird feeding.
  3. Paekakariki Escarpment Track: A scenic hiking trail offering breathtaking views of Kapiti Coast and the South Island.
  4. Otaki Forks: A recreational area offering hiking trails, camping facilities, and a natural swimming pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast is a paradise for adventure seekers and outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking routes catering to all levels of fitness and experience. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kapiti Coast:

  1. Kapiti Coastal Cycle Route: A popular 18-kilometer bike trail offering stunning ocean views from Paekakariki to Peka Peka.
  2. Te Araroa Trail: A 4,000-kilometer hiking trail that passes through Kapiti Coast, offering spectacular natural scenery and challenging terrain.
  3. Kapiti Nature Walks: A network of short walks and hikes through the native forest and coastal areas of Kapiti Coast.
  4. Paekakariki Escarpment Track: A 10-kilometer hiking trail offering stunning views of Kapiti Coast and the Tasman Sea.

Best Things to Do in Kapiti Coast: Activities and Tours

Kapiti Coast offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and budgets. Here are some of the best things to do in Kapiti Coast:

  1. Wildlife Watching: Visit Kapiti Island or Queen Elizabeth Park to witness unique New Zealand wildlife, such as the Little Spotted Kiwi, Takahe, and Kaka.
  2. Cultural Tours: Take a cultural tour of Nga Purapura and other important cultural sites to learn about the Māori heritage and customs.
  3. Food and Drink Tours: Explore the vibrant food and drink scene of Kapiti Coast by taking a tour of local cafes, breweries, and wineries.
  4. Water Sports: Enjoy a range of water sports, such as kayaking, paddleboarding, and surfing, on the stunning beaches of Kapiti Coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast is renowned for its vibrant food and drink scene, which showcases local delicacies and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages of Kapiti Coast:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried Whitebait: A local delicacy made from freshwater fish caught in the nearby rivers.
  2. Pork Belly: Succulent pork belly slow-cooked to perfection, served with seasonal vegetables and Kapiti Coast honey.
  3. Kapiti Coast Cheese Board: A selection of artisanal cheeses produced in the region, accompanied by local wines and craft beers.

Local Beverages

  1. Kapiti Coast Craft Beer: Several breweries in the region produce unique craft beers, such as Tuatara Brewing, ParrotDog Brewery, and Tinker Tailor.
  2. Kapiti Coast Wine: Some of the best wines in New Zealand are produced in Kapiti Coast, such as Schubert Wines, Johner Estate, and Te Hera Estate.

Where to Stay in Kapiti Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kapiti Coast offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Kapiti Coast:

  1. Raumati Sands Resort: A stunning beachfront resort offering luxurious rooms and suites, a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.
  2. Kapiti Gateway Motel: A family-friendly motel offering comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a BBQ area.
  3. Kapiti Lindale Motel & Conference Centre: A pet-friendly motel offering modern rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  4. Nomads Capital Backpackers Wellington: A budget-friendly hostel in nearby Wellington, offering comfortable dorms and private rooms, a kitchen, and lounge area.

What to Buy in Kapiti Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kapiti Coast is known for its unique local products and souvenirs that make for great gifts and mementos of your trip. Here are some items to consider buying in Kapiti Coast:

Local Culture and Customs of Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast has a rich cultural heritage and customs that are deeply rooted in the Māori heritage of the region. Visitors are advised to be respectful of the local culture and traditions and learn about them through cultural tours and events.

Best Time to Visit Kapiti Coast

The best time to visit Kapiti Coast is in the summer months of December to February when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the beaches are ideal for swimming and water sports. However, Kapiti Coast can be visited year-round for its stunning natural scenery and unique New Zealand wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kapiti Coast

Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow when visiting Kapiti Coast:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of Kapiti Coast, and learn about them through cultural tours and events.
  • Explore the natural scenery of Kapiti Coast through hiking and biking routes, and wildlife watching.
  • Enjoy the vibrant food and drink scene of Kapiti Coast by sampling the local delicacies and beverages.
  • Follow safety guidelines and instructions when engaging in outdoor adventures and water sports.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harm the unique New Zealand wildlife found in Kapiti Coast, such as the Little Spotted Kiwi and Takahe.
  • Litter and damage the natural environment of Kapiti Coast.
  • Ignore safety guidelines and warnings when engaging in outdoor adventures and water sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast is a relatively safe region to visit. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards and follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor adventures and water sports. It is also advisable to take precautions against sunburn and insect bites.

Health in Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast has a well-established healthcare system, with several medical facilities and pharmacies available in the region. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before their trip to cover any medical emergencies or accidents.

Getting to Kapiti Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kapiti Coast can be easily accessed by air, road, and rail. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip to Kapiti Coast:

Airports Near Kapiti Coast

The nearest airport to Kapiti Coast is Wellington International Airport, which is approximately 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kapiti Coast

Bus services connect Kapiti Coast with nearby cities, such as Wellington and Palmerston North.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Kapiti Coast or take the train from Wellington to Paraparaumu.

How to Get Around in Kapiti Coast

Kapiti Coast can be easily explored by car, bike, or on foot. Several rental car and bike companies are available in the region. Visitors can also take public transport, such as buses and trains, to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kapiti Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kapiti Coast is located near several exciting destinations and excursions that are worth visiting. Here are some of the top nearby destinations and excursions:
