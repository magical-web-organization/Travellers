Located in the stunning region of Taranaki, in the North Island of New Zealand, lies the charming town of Hawera. Known for its friendly locals, beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and unique attractions, Hawera is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, historical sites, or simply a relaxing getaway, Hawera has it all. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best of Hawera, including the must-see sights, top activities, best places to eat and stay, and much more. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore the beauty of Taranaki in Hawera!

Introduction to Hawera

Hawera is a peaceful town located in South Taranaki, in New Zealand’s North Island. It is surrounded by lush green hills, stunning parks, and farms. This small town has a rich Maori and European history, dating back to the 19th century. It’s a great destination for those looking for a relaxing break from their daily routine. Visitors can choose from a broad range of activities, exploring the local museums and galleries or relaxing at the parks and gardens. You can visit the farmers’ market, indulge in some of the region’s local delicacies, or take an art trail of the region’s street art. With something for every kind of traveler, Hawera is the perfect destination for a memorable getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Hawera

Hawera offers a wide range of attractions to explore, from natural marvels to historical sites. Here is our list of the top places to visit in Hawera:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hawera Water Tower

One of the landmarks of the town, Hawera Water Tower, is also known as the “Lighthouse of Taranaki”. Built in the early 1900s to provide the town with water for fire protection, it now acts as an observation tower, offering panoramic views of the town and towards Mount Taranaki.

Tawhiti Museum

The Tawhiti Museum is a unique collection of theater-like displays, showcasing the colonial history of the region. Visitors can expect to see the life-size figures in the form of wax models depicting life in Hawera from the 1850s onwards.

Hawera Historical Museum

The Hawera Historical Museum preserves and showcases the local history and heritage of the region with interactive exhibits, artefacts, and archival materials on display, dating back to the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Hawera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Egmont National Park

The stunning Egmont National Park is a nature lover’s paradise, located just a short drive from Hawera. The park features a snow-capped Mount Taranaki, natural pools, hiking trails, cycling, and bird-watching opportunities.

Tawhiti Bush Reserve

The Tawhiti Bush Reserve is another nearby attraction that visitors can explore. It features dense forest trails, native flora and fauna, and beautiful waterfalls.

Lake Rotokare

Located just a few minutes from the town, Lake Rotokare is a beautiful natural lake that offers fishing, boating, and kayaking opportunities. It is also home to some of the rarest birds and plants in New Zealand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hawera

Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore many scenic trails around Hawera such as the Hawera Coastal Walkway, which is suitable for all fitness levels. For something more challenging, the Pouakai Circuit In Egmont National Park is a three-day journey that provides unparalleled views of Mount Taranaki.

Best Things to Do in Hawera: Activities and Tours

Hawera is full of unique and exciting activities that visitors can enjoy. Here are some that we recommend:

Surfing

Taranaki is renowned for its consistent surf, and Hawera is an ideal location to learn how to surf or rent the boards. Both Opunake and Waihi Beach are just a short drive away.

Hawera Farmers’ Market

The Hawera Farmers’ Market is the perfect place to get your hands on fresh produce, artisanal products, and sample some of the local delicacies like Slow cooked Pork Belly and Rakautatahi Premium Black Angus Beef.

Live Performances

The town has a long history as an arts and cultural hub in the area, with live music performances, theatrical shows, film festivals, and more hosted throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hawera

Visitors with a week-long stay in Hawera can follow this comprehensive itinerary to make the most of their trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Hawera

Hawera is home to some of the best cuisine in the Taranaki region. Visitors can taste some of the regional delicacies, including:

Must-Try Dishes

Slow Cooked Pork Belly

One of the local favourites, Slow Cooked Pork Belly is served with apple sauce and a side of creamy garlic potatoes.

Rakautatahi Premium Black Angus Beef

Rakautatahi Premium Black Angus Beef is raised locally in the town and is known for its tenderness and taste.

Local Beverages

Hawera Cider

The Hawera Cider is a refreshing drink made with locally sourced apples, and it’s perfect for a hot summer day.

Taranaki Craft Beer

Taranaki craft beer has become increasingly popular, and the wide selection of beers at the bars and local breweries is worth a taste.

Where to Stay in Hawera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Hawera can choose from a range of accommodation options. From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, there is something for every budget. Some of the best places to stay in Hawera include:

Hawera Central Motor Lodge

The Hawera Central Motor Lodge is a highly recommended motel with modern amenities and is conveniently located in the heart of the town.

Tairoa Lodge

Tairoa Lodge is a luxury bed and breakfast accommodation option, set on a beautiful rural property just 10 minutes’ drive from the town.

What to Buy in Hawera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take a piece of the culture and heritage of Hawera back home. Some of the best things to buy in Hawera include handmade jewellery, locally made pieces of art, and artisanal products such as Hawera Cider, Taranaki honey, and other preserves.

Local Culture and Customs of Hawera

Visitors to Hawera can experience the rich culture and heritage of the local Maori communities, admiring traditional artworks and visiting historical sites showcasing the stories of the area’s cultural significance.

Best Time to Visit Hawera

The best time to visit Hawera is during the summer months, from December to February. The warm weather and long daylight hours offer the ideal conditions for outdoor adventures, beach days, and other activities. However, the town’s calm surroundings offer the chance to relax and rejuvenate, regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hawera

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Sample some of the local delicacies and experiences
  • Enjoy the natural surroundings and explore the many scenic spots in the area

Don’t:

  • Leave rubbish or litter in public areas
  • Disrupt wildlife in their natural habitats
  • Show intolerance or disrespect towards the local people

Safety and Health

Safety in Hawera

Hawera is a safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should still take normal precautions such as locking their accommodation and keeping an eye on their belongings in public areas.

Health in Hawera

Travelers to the region do not need to take any specific vaccinations or medications. Still, as in any New Zealand destination, visitors should wear appropriate clothing, protect themselves from the sun, and stay hydrated.

Getting to Hawera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hawera is easily accessible by road, air, and bus. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Hawera

The nearest international airports are Wellington International Airport and Auckland International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Hawera

Hawera is a significant stop on the Wellington to New Plymouth route, with multiple local and intercity bus services operating in the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car from the regional airports or take a rental car from New Zealand’s major cities, as the drive to Hawera is a beautiful and scenic journey.

How to Get Around in Hawera

Visitors to the town can use taxis, local buses, or rental cars to explore the surrounding areas. Still, in the town, visitors can walk or cycle, around most areas and the town is very pedestrian-friendly.

Where to Go Next from Hawera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby town of Opunake, just 30 minutes away from Hawera, for more surf beaches, local farmers markets and quaint shops.

Conclusion

Hawera is a beautiful and charming destination, offering a wide range of experiences and activities for visitors to enjoy. From natural marvels to cultural sites and culinary delights, the town has something for everyone. So, why not pack your bags and head to Hawera to explore this hidden gem of Taranaki today!
