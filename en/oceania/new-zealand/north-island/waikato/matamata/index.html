Introduction to Matamata

Nestled in the heart of Waikato, in the North Island of New Zealand, Matamata is a charming rural town famous for its picturesque farmlands, rolling green hills, and a vibrant Maori culture. The town is most well-known as the location of the Hobbiton Movie Set – a major tourist attraction that draws visitors from all over the world, making it a must-visit destination for any Lord of the Rings fan. But beyond Hobbiton, Matamata has so much more to offer. From culturally-rich museums to scenic nature reserves, there’s something for everyone in this idyllic town.

List of Places to Visit in Matamata

Here are some of the top attractions to add to your Matamata itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wairere Falls

A hike to the top of Wairere Falls is a must-do excursion if you’re visiting Matamata. The waterfall is one of the highest in the North Island, standing at 153 meters tall. The walking track, which takes around two to three hours to complete, offers stunning views of the falls and surrounding forest.

Firth Tower Museum

Explore the rich history of Matamata and the wider Waikato region at the Firth Tower Museum. The museum features a stunning collection of artifacts, photos, and exhibits that showcase the area’s past, including a visit to an authentic 19th-century blacksmith’s shop and a restored schoolhouse.

Te Puna Quarry Park

Boasting unmatched views of the Kaimai Range and the Western Bay of Plenty, the Te Puna Quarry Park offers visitors a unique insight into the local flora and fauna. Explore the park’s extensive walking tracks that wind through native bush, past ponds, and up to its historic quarry.

Natural Attractions of Matamata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hobbiton Movie Set

No visit to Matamata is complete without a trip to the famous Hobbiton Movie Set, used as the filming location for the Lord of the Rings and Hobbit trilogies. Visitors can book a guided tour of the set, see the hobbit holes, and even enjoy a pint at the ‘Green Dragon Inn.’

Opal Hot Springs

Just a 15-minute drive from Matamata, the Opal Hot Springs offer a relaxing retreat for visitors looking to take a break from the town’s hustle and bustle. The hot pools are set amid lush tropical gardens and provide the perfect spot to unwind with family and friends.

Kaimai Mamaku Conservation Park

Experience the best of Matamata’s natural beauty at the Kaimai Mamaku Conservation Park. Explore the park’s stunning hiking trails, scenic waterfalls, and verdant beech forests, which offer a haven for native birds and wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Matamata

Matamata offers a variety of dining options to suit every taste and budget. With an emphasis on fresh, locally-sourced produce and unique New Zealand dishes, you’ll be spoilt for choice.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangi: A traditional Maori meal cooked using heated rocks and underground ovens. The meat and vegetables are slow-cooked for several hours to create tender, flavorful dishes.

  • Kiwi Burger: A popular fast-food item, featuring a beef patty, fried egg, beetroot, and pineapple, served on a bun.

  • Whitebait Fritters: A Kiwi delicacy made using freshly-caught whitebait, mixed with egg and flour, and pan-fried until golden brown.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Waikato region is famous for its wines, especially Chardonnay, Merlot, and Sauvignon Blanc.

  • Beer: New Zealand is home to some of the best craft breweries in the world. Try local favorites like Steinlager, Monteith’s, and Lion Red.

What to Buy in Matamata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or local gift to bring home? Here are some of the best things to buy in Matamata:

  • Locally-made honey and jams, especially the famous Manuka honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Matamata

The Maori culture is an integral part of Matamata’s heritage. Visitors to Matamata are encouraged to learn about the Maori way of life, including their traditions, customs, and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Matamata

Matamata is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in all seasons. The best time to visit depends on your interests and what you want to do while you’re in town. The spring and summer months (September to February) offer mild temperatures and long days, perfect for outdoor activities. The autumn and winter months (March to August) are cooler but less crowded, making it the ideal time to explore the town’s museums and cultural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matamata

Matamata is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking doors and windows, not leaving valuables unattended, and staying vigilant in crowded areas.

Health in Matamata

New Zealand is generally a safe place to travel, and there are no major health issues to be aware of in Matamata. However, visitors should take standard precautions like drinking bottled water and wearing sun protection when outdoors.

How to Get Around in Matamata

Matamata is a small town, and most attractions and amenities are within walking distance. However, visitors who want to explore further afield should rent a car or arrange transportation beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Matamata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rotorua: A world-famous geothermal wonderland, just a 45-minute drive from Matamata.
  • Taupo: Known for its stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and fishing.
  • Tauranga: A beautiful coastal city with pristine beaches, excellent seafood, and a relaxed atmosphere.

Conclusion

Matamata is a truly unique destination, offering a perfect blend of stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor activities. Whether you’re a Lord of the Rings fan or simply looking to explore the best of New Zealand, Matamata is a must-visit destination. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and charm of this incredible town.
