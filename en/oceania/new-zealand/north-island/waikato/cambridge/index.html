Introduction to Cambridge

Located in Waikato on the North Island of New Zealand, Cambridge is a quaint town known for its picturesque natural scenery, historic landmarks, thrilling outdoor activities, and vibrant cultural scene. The town, which is named after the prestigious University of Cambridge in England, offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern-day fun that attracts visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Cambridge

If you’re planning a trip to Cambridge, here are some top-rated places that you should consider adding to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cambridge Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of the town with exhibits that feature artifacts, photographs, and stories of the town’s past.
  • St. Andrew’s Anglican Church: This century-old church is a stunning example of neo-Gothic architecture and features beautiful stained glass windows.
  • Mighty River Domain: This popular park is a great place to relax, have a picnic or take a stroll around the lake. It also hosts many events throughout the year including the annual Balloons over Waikato Festival.

Natural Attractions of Cambridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cambridge Green Belt: This picturesque area is a nature lover’s paradise. With over 20km of walking and cycling trails, it offers a great opportunity to explore the beautiful countryside.
  • Maungatautari Ecological Reserve: This ecological paradise is home to many rare and endangered species of plants and animals.
  • Lake Karapiro: This stunning lake is known for its water sports including kayaking, rowing, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambridge

Cambridge offers many scenic hiking and biking routes that travelers of all experiences and skill levels can enjoy. Here are some of the best trails to consider:

  • Karapiro Loop Track: This 10km track offers picturesque views of the lake and is suitable for both hiking and biking.
  • Waikato River Trail: This 100km trail offers a range of experiences from easy walks to advanced mountain biking.
  • Maungatautari Summit Track: This 3.5km track rewards hikers with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is suitable for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Cambridge: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Ballooning - Balloons over Waikato: This annual event attracts visitors from all over the world for an unforgettable hot air balloon experience.
  • Avantidrome - Track Cycling Centre: Visitors can experience the thrill of track cycling at the Avantidrome, which hosted the 2020 UCI Track Cycling World Championships.
  • The Sanctuary Mountain Maungatautari: This sanctuary is home to many kiwis and other rare birds in their natural habitat. Guided tours are available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cambridge

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Cambridge, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Visit the Cambridge Museum and stroll through the town’s quaint streets.
  • Day 2: Explore the outdoors with a bike ride on the Karapiro Loop Track and a visit to Lake Karapiro.
  • Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride during the Balloons over Waikato Festival.
  • Day 4: Visit St. Andrew’s Anglican Church and the Mighty River Domain.
  • Day 5: Hike the Waikato River Trail for a full day adventure.
  • Day 6: Visit the Maungatautari Ecological Reserve for a guided tour.
  • Day 7: Relax with a picnic at the Cambridge Green Belt.

Food and Drink Scene of Cambridge

Cambridge’s food and drink scene is a blend of traditional New Zealand and modern international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to look out for:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangi - This traditional Maori dish is cooked in an earth oven and typically consists of meat and vegetables.
  • Pavlova - A classic New Zealand dessert made with meringue, whipped cream, and topped with fresh fruit.
  • Fish and Chips - A popular staple of New Zealand’s coastal cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Cambridge has several vineyards and wineries that produce world-class wine.
  • Beer - The town has its own brewery called Good George, which produces a range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Cambridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cambridge has several options for accommodations ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the top-rated options to consider:

  • Cambridge Boutique Accommodation: This luxury bed and breakfast is located in a tranquil area of the town and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Cambridge Coach House: This charming guesthouse is situated in the heart of the town and offers comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.
  • Karapiro Willows: This family-friendly accommodation is located right on the lake and offers stunning views and a range of facilities.

What to Buy in Cambridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Cambridge, here are some local souvenirs and products to consider:

  • Good George Brewery Products - Beer and merchandise from the local Cambridge brewery.
  • Maori Arts and Crafts - Traditional Maori arts and crafts including jewelry and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Cambridge

New Zealanders are known for their friendliness and laid-back attitudes, and Cambridge is no exception. Here are some local customs and cultural norms to be aware of:

  • Māori culture is an important part of New Zealand culture. Take the time to learn about the culture and show respect for traditions and customs.
  • Tipping is not expected in New Zealand, but it is appreciated for exceptional service.
  • Kiwis are known for their casual attire, so feel free to leave your formal wear at home.

Best Time to Visit Cambridge

The best time to visit Cambridge is during the summer months of December to February when the weather is warm, and the town is bustling with activities and events. Autumn and spring are also good times to visit for milder weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambridge

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Cambridge:

Do:

  • Try new foods and drinks, including local delicacies like hangi and wine.
  • Respect the natural environment and follow the “leave no trace” principle when exploring the outdoors.
  • Learn about Maori culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Swim in areas that are not designated for swimming.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior or cultural appropriation.

Safety and Health

While Cambridge is generally a safe destination for travelers, it’s still important to take precautions to ensure your safety and wellbeing. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Cambridge

  • Always lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation.
  • Take care when driving on the winding roads and watch for pedestrians and cyclists.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Cambridge

  • New Zealand has a high UV index, so be sure to wear sunscreen and a hat to protect your skin from sun damage.
  • Make sure you have adequate travel insurance to cover your medical needs while in New Zealand.
  • Remember to stay hydrated and pack any necessary medications.

Getting to Cambridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cambridge

  • Hamilton International Airport: Located about 15 minutes away from Cambridge, Hamilton International offers flights to and from major cities such as Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch.

Bus Stations Near Cambridge

  • Cambridge Coach Stop on Queen Street: A number of coach companies such as InterCity and ManaBus stop in Cambridge.

Other Options

  • Driving: Cambridge is located along State Highway 1 and is easily accessible by car.
  • Cycling: If you’re feeling adventurous, you can cycle to Cambridge along the Waikato River Trail.

How to Get Around in Cambridge

Cambridge is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or bike. Car rental options are also available if you prefer to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Cambridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding areas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Waitomo Caves: Just a short drive from Cambridge, the Waitomo Caves are a series of underground caves known for their unique glowworms.
  • Hobbiton Movie Set: Lord of the Rings fans will enjoy a visit to the Hobbiton Movie Set located in nearby Matamata.
  • Rotorua: This geothermal wonderland is known for its mud pools, hot springs, and geysers.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or thrill-seeker, Cambridge offers something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide has provided insider tips for exploring the North Island gem of New Zealand, including top-rated places to visit, must-try dishes, and local customs. So pack your bags and get ready to experience the best that Cambridge has to offer.
