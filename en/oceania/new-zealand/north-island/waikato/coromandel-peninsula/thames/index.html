Thames is a charming town situated in the Coromandel Peninsula, in the Waikato region of New Zealand’s North Island. Founded during the gold rush era, this quaint town boasts a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and exciting outdoor activities. This travel guide to Thames will help you plan your trip, from the best things to do and see to practical advice on where to stay and how to get around.

Introduction to Thames

Thames is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. The town is located on the southeastern coast of the Coromandel Peninsula and is known for its picturesque landscapes, rugged coastline, and lush forests. It is a popular destination for tourists seeking adventure, relaxation, and a taste of New Zealand’s unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Thames

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thames is home to several museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into its rich past. The Thames School of Mines and Mineralogical Museum is one of the most popular tourist attractions in town. It showcases the history of gold mining in the region, as well as a vast mineral collection. Visit the Thames Historical Museum to learn more about the town’s origins, and the Goldmine Experience to see how miners toiled for fortunes in the late 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Thames: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thames is a nature lover’s paradise, offering spectacular hiking trails, stunning waterfalls, and picturesque beaches. The Kauaeranga Valley is a popular spot for hiking and camping, with several trails ranging in difficulty levels. You can also go kayaking, paddleboarding, or fishing in the waters surrounding Thames.

Take a scenic drive along the Thames Coast Road to discover hidden beaches and dramatic coastal cliffs. The Coromandel Forest Park is perfect for a day trip, with its lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and diverse flora and fauna. Don’t miss the stunning view from the top of the Thames War Memorial Lookout.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thames

Thames is renowned for its stunning hiking trails and mountain biking routes. The Pinnacles Walk is an 8km trek that takes you through dense forests and steep mountain climbs. The Kauaeranga Kauri Trail offers a picturesque view of the Kauaeranga River and ends in a stunning waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Thames: Activities and Tours

In Thames, there are many exciting things to do for travellers of all ages and interests. Take a boat tour to explore the rugged coastline and spot marine life, including dolphins and whales. The Goldfield’s railway offers a nostalgic train ride through the stunning landscapes of Thames.

Spend a day indulging in the town’s thriving arts and crafts scene, which features unique local artists and galleries. You can also visit the Thames Market, where you can sample local delicacies, crafts, and produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thames

Day 1: Arrive in Thames and explore the town’s rich history, including the Thames School of Mines and Mineralogical Museum and the Thames Historical Museum.

Day 2: Go hiking in the Kauaeranga Valley, followed by a dip in the nearby swimming holes.

Day 3: Take a boat tour along the Coromandel coast, exploring hidden bays and coves and soaking in the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Spend a day exploring the town’s art scene, visiting local galleries and shops.

Day 5: Spend a day on the water, kayaking or paddleboarding in the Thames estuary.

Day 6: Take a train ride through the stunning landscapes of Thames with Goldfield’s railway.

Day 7: Venture out of town for a day trip to the nearby Coromandel Forest Park, where you can go hiking, fishing, and exploring the wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Thames

Thames offers a diverse food scene, featuring everything from traditional New Zealand fare to international flavours. For a taste of the local cuisine, try fish and chips or meat pies. Don’t miss the chance to sample fresh local seafood, such as crayfish or scallops.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips from a local takeout shop
  • Pies from the local bakeries
  • Fresh seafood, including crayfish and scallops

Local Beverages

Thames has a thriving coffee culture, with several cafes offering a range of specialty coffee blends. You can also sample local wines and craft beers at the town’s many bars and pubs.

Where to Stay in Thames: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thames offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. You can choose from charming bed and breakfasts, luxury hotels, backpacker hostels, and campgrounds. Some of the most popular options include the Nightingale Falls Farmstay Retreat and the Rolleston Motel.

What to Buy in Thames: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thames is known for its unique arts and crafts scene, featuring many local artists and artisans. You can find everything from handmade pottery, jewelry and woven textiles to traditional Maori crafts. Don’t forget to pick up some local honey or jams to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Thames

Thames is a melting pot of cultures, reflecting the diverse heritage of New Zealand. The town has a strong indigenous Maori influence and is known for its strong arts and crafts community. Respect for the environment and nature is an essential part of the local culture, and visitors are encouraged to be mindful of their impact on the environment.

Best Time to Visit Thames

The best time to visit Thames is during the summer months, from December to February, when the weather is warm, and the days are longer. However, the shoulder seasons of spring and autumn offer a more relaxed atmosphere and are perfect for those looking to avoid the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thames

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take care of the environment
  • Try local food and drinks
  • Explore the outdoors

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in public places
  • Enter private property without permission
  • Ignore the local road rules
  • Disrespect the local culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Thames

Thames is renowned for its friendly and helpful locals, but like any town, visitors should be vigilant, particularly when walking alone at night. Also, ensure your car and belongings are locked and secure.

Health in Thames

As with any travel destination, visitors should take precautions to safeguard their health. Sunscreen and hat are definite must-haves, particularly during the summer months. Be mindful of the local wildlife, such as venomous spiders and snakes in parks and forests.

Getting to Thames: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thames

The closest airport to Thames is Auckland International Airport, located approximately two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Thames

Thames has a reliable bus service to and from Auckland, Hamilton, Whitianga, and Coromandel Town.

Other Options

Self-driving or hiring a car is the most popular way to reach Thames and explore its surrounding regions.

How to Get Around in Thames

Thames town is compact and easy to explore on foot. However, to explore beyond the town centre, hiring a car or bicycle is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Thames: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thames is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding towns, attractions, and natural wonders. Some popular nearby destinations include Coromandel Town, Hahei, and Whitianga. Visitors can also explore the nearby Hauraki Gulf Marine Park or visit the breathtaking Mount Maunganui in Tauranga.

Conclusion

Thames is a charming town with something for everyone, offering a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and exciting outdoor adventures. This travel guide provided you with the best things to do and see, where to stay, and useful tips on getting around. Make the most of your visit to Thames by exploring this beautiful town and the nearby regions.
