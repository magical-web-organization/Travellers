Introduction to Tairua

Tairua is a small coastal town on the eastern coast of the Coromandel Peninsula in Waikato, North Island, New Zealand. It is a popular holiday destination known for its stunning beaches, scenic views, and laid-back atmosphere. Tairua is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, swimming, surfing, and fishing. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, local customs and culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Tairua

  • Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places - Visit the Tairua Museum to learn about the town’s history, including the gold rush era, wartime history and the early development of the town.
  • Natural Attractions of Tairua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views - Head to Mt. Paku for panoramic views of Tairua and the surrounding area. Take a scenic drive or hike to the Broken Hills, where you’ll find steep cliffs, hidden waterfalls, and an abandoned mine.
  • Hiking/Biking Routes of Tairua - The Pauanui Tairua Trail (10km) is a great beginner/intermediate biking and hiking track with impressive views of Tairua Harbour and Pauanui Beach.
  • Best Things to Do in Tairua: Activities and Tours - Some of the most popular activities in Tairua include kayaking or paddleboarding up the river, fishing charters, surfing, and swimming.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tairua Museum is located in the old Schoolhouse (1895) and is renowned for its exhibits highlighting local history and the gold rush era. They provide a great deal of information on Tairua’s development such as early trades such as farming and logging, and the shipping of goods overseas. The museum’s collection highlights the development of the town, including its people, schooling, and primary industries such as timber, agriculture, and extracting gold.

Natural Attractions of Tairua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Tairua offers a variety of scenic spots to explore. Mt. Paku is a favourite of most tourists. The short but steep trail up Mt. Paku rewards hikers with breathtaking views of Tairua and the surrounding area. For spectacular waterfalls and hiking trails, we recommend visiting Broken Hills. The hike provides an adventurous retreat with breathtaking views of the ocean, the town, and the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tairua

The Pauanui Tairua Trail is 10km and beginner-friendly, offering some of the most scenic views of Tairua Harbour and Pauanui Beach. It’s perfect for families with children who want to explore the local area on foot or bike.

Best Things to Do in Tairua: Activities and Tours

Tairua offers plenty of activities for visitors of all ages. If you’re looking for an adventure, try kayaking or paddleboarding up the river, surfing or fishing charters. The hot water beach, located around 20 minutes away at Cathedral Cove, is also a beautiful spot to explore, famous for its natural hot water jets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tairua

Day 1: Arrive in Tairua and explore the town centre Day 2: Hike up Mt. Paku in the morning and visit the Tairua Museum in the afternoon Day 3: Drive out to Broken Hills for a day of hiking, sightseeing, and exploring the abandoned mine Day 4: Take a fishing charter or kayak up the river Day 5: Relax at the beach and soak up the sun Day 6: Go on a day trip to Cathedral Cove and explore the hot water beach Day 7: Depart from Tairua

Food and Drink Scene of Tairua

Tairua offers a good variety of dining options, from fine dining with a view to more casual options like fish and chips. If you’re a wine lover, Tairua has wineries such as Mercury Bay Estate and Kuaotunu Bay Vineyards. You can also taste craft beers at the Pour House.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Tairua include fish and chips, fresh seafood, and the local favourite Hokey Pokey ice cream.

Local Beverages

If you’re a wine lover, make sure to taste some of Tairua’s great wines. For beer enthusiasts, the Pour House provides a variety of locally crafted beers.

Where to Stay in Tairua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tairua offers a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and villas. Popular picks include Pacific Harbour Lodge, Harbour View Lodge or the Boatshed.

What to Buy in Tairua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking for a local souvenir, pop into the Blue Ginger Gallery and the Little Gallery of Fine Arts, which offer a beautiful selection of local art. You can also find a wide range of art and craft stores in the town offering items such as pottery, paintings, and scrapbooking materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Tairua

The local Māori community maintains a deep respect for their traditions and culture, which is infused into the local way of life. Visitors should be respectful of the community’s customs and traditions, such as removing shoes when entering someone’s home or a marae (meeting place).

Best Time to Visit Tairua

The best time to visit Tairua is from December through March when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is open year-round, and each season has its charm. If you’re looking to avoid the crowds and experience the serenity of Tairua, visit during the off-season months of May through September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tairua

Do:

  • Respect the Māori culture and traditions
  • Explore the town’s scenic views
  • Try the fresh seafood

Don’t:

  • Leave rubbish on the beach or local environs
  • Exceed the speed limits on public roads or private property

Safety and Health

Safety in Tairua

Tairua is relatively safe, and the town has a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking doors and windows and not leaving valuables within visible reach of others.

Health in Tairua

Tairua has several medical centres and pharmacies for minor injuries or illnesses. In case of an emergency, dial 111.

Getting to Tairua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tairua is located 2.5 hours drive from Auckland, and one hour’s drive from Thames. The best way to get to Tairua is by car. Alternatively, you can take a bus or shuttle service from Auckland or Hamilton.

Airports Near Tairua

The nearest airport is Auckland International Airport. You can hire a car from there or take a shuttle service or bus to Tairua.

Bus Stations Near Tairua

There are various bus options running from Auckland or Hamilton to Tairua.

Other Options

The best way to explore Tairua is on foot, by bike, or by car. Car rentals are available at Auckland and Hamilton airports if needed.

How to Get Around in Tairua

Most visitors choose to explore Tairua on foot, as the town is relatively small and easy to navigate. You can also hire a bike or car to get around, or take a taxi or shuttle service.

Where to Go Next from Tairua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions include Whitianga, Hahei Beach, Hot Water Beach, and Cathedral Cove. All are accessible within an hour or two’s drive from Tairua.

Conclusion

Tairua offers visitors a peaceful retreat alongside pristine beaches and stunning views. It has everything from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, making it an ideal destination for travellers looking to unwind and explore.
