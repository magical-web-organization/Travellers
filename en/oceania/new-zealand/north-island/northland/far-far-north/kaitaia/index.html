Introduction to Kaitaia

Kaitaia is a charming small town located in the Far Far North region of Northland, North Island, New Zealand. It is the last major town before reaching the northern tip of the country, Cape Reinga. Kaitaia is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, with golden beaches, lush forests, and scenic mountains. This town is also a gateway to some of the most incredible cultural experiences in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Kaitaia

There are various places to visit in Kaitaia and its surroundings. Some of the top attractions include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Te Ahu Centre: This cultural hub offers a range of exhibitions, art galleries, and interactive displays that showcase the history, culture, and people of the Far North region.

  • Kauri Museum: Just a short drive from Kaitaia, this museum showcases the history of kauri logging, one of the most significant industries of the region.

Natural Attractions of Kaitaia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ninety Mile Beach: This stunning beach goes on for miles and is a popular spot for swimming, surfing, and fishing.

  • Cape Reinga: This iconic spot is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Kaitaia. The northernmost point of New Zealand offers stunning views of the Tasman Sea and the Pacific Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaitaia

  • Mt. Karioi: A hiking trail offering stunning views of the surrounding area, including the ocean view and the town below.

  • Puketi Forest: This majestic forest is home to Kauri trees and offers various hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Kaitaia: Activities and Tours

  • Sandboarding: Go sandboarding down the giant sand dunes on 90 Mile Beach for an unforgettable adventure.

  • Kaitaia Farmers Market: This weekly market is a fantastic way to experience the local culture and taste delicious produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kaitaia

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kaitaia, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town and visit Te Ahu Centre
  • Day 2: Visit Cape Reinga and learn about Maori culture and folklore
  • Day 3: Kayaking tour through the Mangrove Forests
  • Day 4: Hiking through the majestic Kauri forests
  • Day 5: Surfing or swimming in 90 Mile Beach
  • Day 6: Visit the Kauri Museum and learn about the history of the logging industry
  • Day 7: Try sandboarding on the dunes or go shopping at the local markets

Food and Drink Scene of Kaitaia

Kaitaia’s food scene reflects its diverse culture and history. Here are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangi: A traditional Maori dish made with root vegetables and meat cooked in an earth oven.

  • Kauri Clams: An iconic seafood dish in Kaitaia, featuring locally harvested Kauri clams.

Local Beverages

  • Kaitaia Fire Honey Liqueur: This unique drink is made with honey, herbs, and spices, providing a spicy kick.

  • Manuka Honey Beer: A unique beer made with Manuka honey, known for its health benefits.

Where to Stay in Kaitaia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kaitaia has various accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Kauri Lodge: A boutique lodge set in a beautiful garden, offering a comfortable stay with a touch of luxury.

  • Ahipara Bay Motel: Located just a few minutes from 90 Mile Beach, this motel offers wonderful ocean views and is ideal for families or couples.

What to Buy in Kaitaia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kaitaia is known for its unique souvenirs and locally-made products. Some items to consider bringing home include:

  • Kauri gum: A unique resin from Kauri trees that is prized for its beauty and historical significance

  • Handmade jewelry and crafts: The region is home to many skilled artisans who create beautiful jewelry and crafts

Local Culture and Customs of Kaitaia

Kaitaia has strong ties to Maori culture, and visitors should show respect for local customs and traditions. The locals embrace visitors and encourage sharing their unique culture. It’s important to understand that the indigenous culture holds a special place in the community. There are various opportunities to learn about Maori culture through museums and tours.

Best Time to Visit Kaitaia

Kaitaia is an all-year-round destination, with pleasant weather throughout the year. The summer (December to February) is the peak season, when the weather is warmer and the days are longer. However, spring and autumn are great times to visit as it’s quieter at the popular tourist spots.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaitaia

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take your litter with you, to keep the environment clean.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities while being mindful of safety precautions, like surfing, walking, or swimming.

Don’t:

  • Take anything belonging to the wildlife, plants, and environment.
  • Swim on the eastern side of 90 Mile Beach because of the currents
  • Smoke weed or do drugs in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaitaia

Kaitaia is a relatively safe destination but keep a careful eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night. There is plenty of wildlife in the area, so be cautious while taking walking tracks and approaching beaches or bush areas.

Health in Kaitaia

It’s essential to have travel insurance for medical emergencies while in Kaitaia. Be aware of the sun, especially in summer, and wear sunscreen and a hat.

Getting to Kaitaia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaitaia

The nearest airport is the Kaitaia Airport, which is approximately 7 kilometers from the town center. Most flights are connected to Mount Cook Airline or Air New Zealand.

Bus Stations Near Kaitaia

There are no specific bus stations in Kaitaia, but travelers can reach the town via bus services operating from cities like Auckland, Paihia, and Kerikeri.

Other Options

Some of the other ways to reach Kaitaia include driving a rental car or taking a taxi from major cities like Auckland or Whangarei.

How to Get Around in Kaitaia

Renting a car is the best way to get around Kaitaia and its surroundings. There are also cycling tours and equipment rental options. There are taxi and bus services available, but the best way to explore the area is to drive.

Where to Go Next from Kaitaia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kaitaia is a great base for exploring the Far North regions and beyond. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Ahipara: Known for its long beaches and stunning sunsets, Ahipara offers a unique beach experience for tourists.
  • Bay of Islands: A beautiful location filled with history and culture, perfect for watersports activities.
  • Whangarei: Explore the beautiful wildlife and native flora and fauna varieties that can found in Whangarei.

Conclusion

Kaitaia is a beautiful and culturally-rich destination in New Zealand. Whether you’re here for adventure, culture, or relaxation, there’s something for everyone in this town.
