Introduction to Far Far North

Located in Northland, in North Island, in New Zealand, Far Far North is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique combination of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The region is home to several important historical sites, as well as outdoor adventure opportunities that are sure to delight even the most seasoned of travelers. From hiking and biking routes to museums and cultural attractions, Far Far North has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at what makes this region so special and provide you with insider tips on the best things to see and do during your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Far Far North

Far Far North is home to a diverse range of attractions, including natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and exciting outdoor adventures. Here are just a few of the must-see destinations to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Far Far North is steeped in heritage and culture, and you can learn more about the region’s fascinating history by visiting some of its most important cultural landmarks. Here are some of the top museums and historical places to visit:

  • Te Ahu Centre, Kaitaia
  • Waitangi Treaty Grounds, Paihia
  • Kemp House and Stone Store, Kerikeri
  • Rewa’s Village, Awanui
  • Taipa Area School, Taipa
  • Waka Landing, Houhora

Natural Attractions of Far Far North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll feel right at home in Far Far North. The region is home to a wide range of natural attractions, from stunning beaches and rugged coastline to lush forests and towering mountains. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Cape Reinga
  • Ninety Mile Beach
  • Ahipara Gumfields Historic Reserve
  • Waipoua Forest
  • Tane Mahuta
  • Houhora Head

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far Far North

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Far Far North, thanks in part to the region’s diverse landscapes and beautiful scenery. Here are some of the top routes for hiking and biking in Far Far North:

  • Te Paki Coastal Track
  • Tirohanga Track
  • Hukatere Coastal Trail
  • Waitangi Mountain Bike Park
  • Kerikeri River Track
  • Waipoua Forest Walks

Best Things to Do in Far Far North: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there’s plenty to keep you busy in Far Far North. Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider during your visit:

  • Dolphin and whale watching tours
  • Eco-tourism experiences
  • Adventure activities like skydiving, kayaking, and surfing
  • Fishing trips
  • Cape Reinga tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Far Far North

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Far Far North, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 - Kaitaia

  • Visit Te Ahu Centre in Kaitaia
  • Check out the ancient kauri trees in nearby Awanui

Day 2 - Paihia

  • Explore the Waitangi Treaty Grounds
  • Take a ferry to Russell for scenic views and charming streets

Day 3 - Kerikeri

  • Visit the Kemp House and Stone Store
  • Walk the Kerikeri River Track
  • Take a dip in Rainbow Falls

Day 4 - Ahipara

  • Walk along Ninety Mile Beach
  • Visit Ahipara Gumfields Historic Reserve
  • Surfing and sandboarding opportunities

Day 5 - Cape Reinga

  • Take a tour of Cape Reinga
  • Hike the Te Paki Coastal Track
  • Watch the sun set at Houhora Head

Day 6 - Waipoua Forest

  • Visit Tane Mahuta
  • Take a guided walk through the forest

Day 7 - Hokianga

  • Take a boat tour of Hokianga Harbour
  • Visit the ancient Maori settlement at Opononi

What to Buy in Far Far North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Far Far North is home to a variety of locally produced goods that make for great souvenirs. Here are some of the top products to look for during your visit:

  • Organic tea from Keri Keri Tea
  • Honey from Kauri Coast Honey
  • Handmade pottery from Waiheke Island
  • Artwork and crafts from local markets

Local Culture and Customs of Far Far North

Far Far North has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the Maori, British, and European settlers who have called the region home over the centuries. Visitors can learn more about the region’s culture and customs by attending cultural events and visiting cultural landmarks such as Rewa’s Village and the Waitangi Treaty Grounds.

Best Time to Visit Far Far North

The best time to visit Far Far North is during the summer months of December to February, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can still enjoy the region’s attractions during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall, when the crowds are smaller and the landscapes are ablaze with color.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far Far North

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Far Far North:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take care of the natural environment
  • Try new foods and drinks

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or natural habitats
  • Ignore signage or safety warnings
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior or activities

Safety and Health

Visitors to Far Far North should take appropriate measures to ensure their safety and health during their trip. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Far Far North

  • Be aware of unstable cliffs and rock formations
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean or hiking near waterfalls
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife

Health in Far Far North

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Stay hydrated and avoid over-exerting yourself in the heat
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health concerns

Getting to Far Far North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Far Far North is accessible via several transportation options, including air, bus, and private car. Here’s what you need to know about getting to and around the region:

Airports Near Far Far North

  • Kaitaia Airport
  • Bay of Islands Airport

Bus Stations Near Far Far North

  • Intercity Bus Station, Kaitaia

Other Options

  • Rental car
  • Private shuttle service

How to Get Around in Far Far North

Public transportation in Far Far North is limited, so it’s recommended that visitors rent a car or use a private shuttle service to get around. Alternatively, taxis and ride-sharing services are available in some areas.

Conclusion

With this comprehensive travel guide as your guide, you’re well on your way to an unforgettable trip to one of New Zealand’s most hidden and enchanting gems.
