Introduction to Ninety Mile Beach

Ninety Mile Beach is a beautiful stretch of shoreline located in Far Far North, in Northland, in North Island, in New Zealand, in Oceania. Despite its name, the beach is actually only 55 miles long, making it one of the longest beaches in the world. It is a popular tourist destination, famous for its stunning natural beauty, adventurous activities, and cultural significance.

List of Places to Visit in Ninety Mile Beach

Ninety Mile Beach offers a wide range of attractions to visit, including culturally significant museums and historical sites, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, and exciting activities and tours.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ninety Mile Beach has a rich cultural history, and there are several museums and historical places that visitors can explore to learn more about the area’s heritage. Some of the most notable places to visit include:

  • Te Ahu Museum and Cultural Centre
  • Waitangi Treaty Grounds
  • The Ancient Kauri Kingdom
  • Kauri Museum

Natural Attractions of Ninety Mile Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There are several natural wonders to explore in Ninety Mile Beach, including unique sand dunes, stunning sunsets, and picturesque views. Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy a variety of activities such as:

  • Sand dune surfing
  • Fishing
  • Beachcombing
  • Horse riding

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ninety Mile Beach

For those who enjoy hiking and biking, there are several trails to explore in Ninety Mile Beach. Some of the best routes include:

  • Te Paki Coastal Track
  • Kokota Track
  • Ahipara-Tarangaia-Maunganui Bluff Track

Best Things to Do in Ninety Mile Beach: Activities and Tours

Ninety Mile Beach offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Cape Reinga Day Tour
  • Twilight Safari
  • Hokianga Electric Tuk-Tuk Tours
  • Kayaking Around the Mangrove Forests

Week-Long Itinerary for Ninety Mile Beach

For those who want to spend more time in Ninety Mile Beach, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that will help visitors make the most of their trip. Here’s an overview of what you can expect:

Day Activity
1 Visit Te Paki Nature Reserve and sand dunes

Food and Drink Scene of Ninety Mile Beach

Ninety Mile Beach has a thriving food and drink scene, with several local dishes and beverages that visitors should try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood chowder
  • Green-lipped mussels
  • Kiwi-style fish and chips
  • Hangi (traditional Maori feast)

Local Beverages

  • Sauvignon Blanc from the Marlborough region
  • Pinot Noir from Central Otago
  • Monteith’s beer

  • Ahipara Bay Motel

  • The Kauri Nest Guesthouse

What to Buy in Ninety Mile Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Ninety Mile Beach can purchase a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the best items to buy include:

  • Handmade jewelry and crafts
  • Locally-sourced honey and jams
  • Kauri wood carvings and sculptures
  • Maori artwork

Local Culture and Customs of Ninety Mile Beach

Ninety Mile Beach is home to several cultural and historical landmarks that visitors should be aware of. Maori culture is an important part of the area’s heritage, and visitors should show respect by:

  • Asking permission before taking photographs of Maori people, artwork or cultural events
  • Removing their shoes when entering a Maori Marae or meeting house
  • Following cultural customs as advised by the local guides or hosts

Best Time to Visit Ninety Mile Beach

The best time to visit Ninety Mile Beach is between December and March when the weather is warmer, and outdoor activities and tours are available. However, visitors should be aware that this is peak tourist season, and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ninety Mile Beach

To ensure an enjoyable trip to Ninety Mile Beach, visitors should follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect Maori culture and customs
  • Take proper safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities
  • Enjoy the pristine natural beauty of the beach

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Drive on the beach without a permit or in areas where cars are not allowed
  • Disturb the wildlife

Safety and Health

Visitors to Ninety Mile Beach should be aware of safety and health concerns when planning their trip.

Safety in Ninety Mile Beach

  • Always follow safety instructions when participating in outdoor activities
  • Wear proper sun protection and hydration when spending time outdoors
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes, especially during the winter months

Health in Ninety Mile Beach

  • Visitors should take precautions against sunstroke, sunburn, and dehydration.
  • The area has a high UV rating, so visitors should wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Ninety Mile Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Ninety Mile Beach can travel by air, bus, or car.

Airports Near Ninety Mile Beach

The closest airports to Ninety Mile Beach are in Kaitaia and Whangarei. Visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle from the airport to their accommodations.

Bus Stations Near Ninety Mile Beach

Intercity and Naked Bus operate daily services to Northland and Far North, with stops in Kaitaia and Awanui.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Ninety Mile Beach from Auckland via rental car, shuttle service or organized tour. The drive takes approximately 5-6 hours, depending on traffic and weather conditions.

How to Get Around in Ninety Mile Beach

The best way to get around Ninety Mile Beach is by car, although visitors should be aware that some areas are only accessible via foot or quad bike.

Where to Go Next from Ninety Mile Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Ninety Mile Beach can explore nearby destinations such as Cape Reinga, the Bay of Islands, and Kerikeri, or take a day trip to the Hokianga Harbor and Waipoua Forest.

Conclusion

Ninety Mile Beach is a beautiful and culturally rich destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor activities to cultural landmarks and delicious local cuisine. With proper planning and consideration, visitors can enjoy an unforgettable trip to this stunning part of New Zealand.
