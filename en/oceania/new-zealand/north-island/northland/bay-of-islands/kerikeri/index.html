Introduction to Kerikeri

Kerikeri is a charming town located in the Bay of Islands region of North Island, New Zealand. Known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene, Kerikeri attracts visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, embarking on outdoor adventures, or indulging in local cuisine, Kerikeri has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kerikeri

Here are some of the top places to visit in Kerikeri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kemp House and the Stone Store: These two iconic buildings are the oldest standing European structures in New Zealand and offer a glimpse into the country’s early colonial history.
  • Waitangi Treaty Grounds: This historic site marks the signing of the treaty between the Maori and the British Crown in 1840. It’s an important cultural landmark that offers insight into the country’s past.
  • Rewa’s Village: A recreated Maori village that offers visitors the chance to learn about Maori culture and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Kerikeri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rainbow Falls: A stunning waterfall that drops 27 meters into a swimming hole. The perfect place to cool off on a hot day.
  • Puketi Forest: This ancient forest is home to majestic kauri trees and offers visitors the chance to go hiking, bird watching, and learn about the area’s ecology.
  • Haruru Falls: This unique horseshoe-shaped waterfall is a must-see attraction. Visitors can take a scenic walk to the falls and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerikeri

For active travelers, Kerikeri offers a wealth of hiking and biking opportunities. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  • The Kerikeri River Track: This 8km track offers stunning views of the river and passes through scenic farmland.
  • Opua Forest Paihia Mountain Bike Park: A network of trails that wind through the forest and offer a range of difficulty levels.
  • The Bay of Islands Coastal Walkway: This 55km walkway offers spectacular views of the coastline and passes through some of the region’s most beautiful beaches and bays.

Best Things to Do in Kerikeri: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tours: Kerikeri is located in the heart of New Zealand’s wine country, and there are several wine tours available that offer visitors the chance to sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Dolphin Watching Tours: The Bay of Islands is home to several dolphin species, and visitors can take boat tours to see them up close.
  • Fishing Charters: Kerikeri is also a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts, and there are several fishing charters available that offer the chance to catch a variety of fish.

Food and Drink Scene of Kerikeri

Kerikeri is known for its vibrant food and drink scene, which showcases the region’s bounty of fresh, seasonable produce and locally-sourced ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangi: A traditional Maori method of cooking food underground, which results in tender, flavorful dishes.
  • Kiwi Burger: A classic burger with a New Zealand twist, featuring a beef patty, egg, beetroot, cheese, lettuce, tomato, and onion.
  • Fish and Chips: Fresh-caught fish served with crispy chips is a Kiwi favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Marlborough wines: New Zealand is famous for its wine, and Marlborough is one of the country’s most renowned wine regions. Be sure to try their Sauvignon Blanc and Pinot Noir.
  • Craft Beer: The country’s craft beer scene is booming, and there are several local breweries in Kerikeri that serve up delicious brews.
  • Manuka Honey Mead: Mead is a honey-based alcohol that’s been enjoyed for centuries. Manuka honey is a New Zealand specialty, and the local mead is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Kerikeri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kerikeri offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Kerikeri Homestead Motel and Apartments: A cozy, family-run motel that offers spacious rooms, modern amenities, and a beautiful garden setting.
  • Woodlands Motel and Conference Venue: This stylish motel features comfortable rooms and is conveniently located close to Kerikeri’s attractions and amenities.
  • The Ridge Country Lodge: A luxurious guesthouse that boasts stunning panoramic views of the Bay of Islands and offers a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, spa, and tennis court.

What to Buy in Kerikeri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kerikeri is home to several specialty shops and markets where visitors can purchase unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some popular options:

  • Maori Carvings and Artwork: The Maori culture is an integral part of New Zealand’s history, and there are several shops and galleries in Kerikeri that sell high-quality Maori art and carvings.
  • Manuka Honey: Produced by bees who collect nectar from the manuka tree, this honey variety is renowned for its antibacterial properties and unique flavor.
  • Kiwi Fruit Products: New Zealand is home to the kiwi fruit, and there are several local products such as jams, chutneys, and dried fruit that feature this delicious ingredient.

Local Culture and Customs of Kerikeri

New Zealand’s culture is a unique blend of Maori and European influences, and visitors to Kerikeri can discover the country’s rich history and traditions through its cultural attractions and experiences. It’s important to respect the Maori culture, which includes adhering to certain customs, such as removing your shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Kerikeri

Kerikeri enjoys a mild climate year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months from December to February when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also the busiest time of year, so be sure to book your accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerikeri

Do:

  • Respect the Maori culture and traditions
  • Take advantage of the region’s incredible outdoor opportunities
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the environment or wildlife
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Litter

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerikeri

Kerikeri is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take the same precautions as you would when traveling anywhere else. Avoid walking alone at night, lock up your belongings, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kerikeri

New Zealand has a high standard of healthcare, and Kerikeri has several medical facilities and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving in the country.

Getting to Kerikeri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kerikeri

  • Kerikeri Airport: This small regional airport services domestic flights from several New Zealand cities, including Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch.

Bus Stations Near Kerikeri

  • Kerikeri Bus Depot: This bus depot is located in the town center and offers intercity bus services to several other North Island destinations.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to New Zealand, as it allows for greater flexibility and convenience when exploring the region.

How to Get Around Kerikeri

Kerikeri is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of the town center. However, renting a car is recommended for visitors who want to explore the wider region. There are also several taxi and shuttle services available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Kerikeri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kerikeri is located in the heart of the Bay of Islands, which is home to numerous other towns, beaches, and attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Paihia: A picturesque seaside town that offers a range of outdoor activities and sightseeing opportunities.
  • Russell: The country’s first capital, Russell is a charming town that features historic buildings and beautiful waterfront views.
  • Hole in the Rock: A unique rock formation located in the Bay of Islands that’s only accessible by boat.

Conclusion

Kerikeri is a fascinating destination that offers visitors a wealth of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drink options. Whether you’re looking for a week-long vacation or a short weekend trip, Kerikeri is the perfect place to experience the best of New Zealand.
