Introduction to Lower Northland

Located in the northern region of New Zealand’s North Island, Lower Northland is a paradise for travelers seeking to explore the natural beauty, cultural heritage, and rich history of the region. The area is characterized by stunning coastlines, pristine beaches, picturesque harbors, rolling hills, dense forests, and vibrant cultural centers. This comprehensive travel guide provides everything you need to know about the best places to visit, eat, stay and explore in Lower Northland.

List of Places to Visit in Lower Northland

Lower Northland is a vast region with a bounty of attractions that beg to be explored. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Lower Northland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Waitangi Treaty Grounds
  • Whangarei Art Museum
  • Kemp House and Stone Store (Kerikeri)
  • Waka Cruise (Hokianga)

Natural Attractions of Lower Northland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bay of Islands
  • Whangarei Falls
  • Poor Knights Islands
  • Cape Reinga and Ninety Mile Beach
  • Waipoua Forest
  • Hokianga Harbour

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lower Northland

Lower Northland is a hiker’s paradise, offering a variety of trails that range from easy walks to challenging multi-day hikes. The best hiking and biking routes in the region include:

  • Te Araroa Trail
  • Cape Brett Walkway
  • Kauri Coast Walk
  • Mount Manaia Track
  • Whangarei Heads Coastal Walkway

Best Things to Do in Lower Northland: Activities and Tours

Lower Northland offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours suitable for all ages. Some of the best things to do in Lower Northland include:

  • Water activities: dolphin and whale watching, sailing, kayaking, and snorkeling
  • Adventure sports: bungy jumping, skydiving, and jet boating
  • Fishing and hunting
  • Cultural experiences: guided tours of Maori marae (meeting houses) and cultural events

Week-Long Itinerary for Lower Northland

With so many incredible attractions, it can be challenging to plan the perfect trip to Lower Northland. Here is a week-long itinerary that covers the region’s top attractions:

Day 1: Arrival in Whangarei and visit the Whangarei Falls Day 2: Explore the Bay of Islands Day 3: Hike the Cape Brett Walkway Day 4: Visit Waitangi Treaty Grounds and Kerikeri Day 5: Drive to the Hokianga Harbour and enjoy a Waka Cruise Day 6: Experience the Kauri Coast Walk and Waipoua Forest Day 7: Visit Cape Reinga and Ninety Mile Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Lower Northland

Lower Northland offers a fantastic food and drink scene that showcases the region’s natural bounty. It is a place where culinary traditions meet modern innovation. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages of Lower Northland:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood, such as snapper and crayfish
  • Maori Hangi, a traditional feast cooked in an earth oven
  • Meat pies and sausage rolls
  • Lemon and Paeroa (L&P), a refreshing soda made from lemon and carbonated mineral water

Local Beverages

  • New Zealand wines, especially from Marlborough and Hawke’s Bay
  • Craft beer from boutique local breweries
  • Manuka honey and herbal teas

Where to Stay in Lower Northland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lower Northland is home to a diverse range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Lower Northland:

  • Hotel: The Duke of Marlborough (Russell)
  • Hostel: Base Backpackers (Bay of Islands)
  • Guesthouse: Stone Store Lodge (Kerikeri)

What to Buy in Lower Northland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lower Northland is a great place for buying souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the top things to buy in Lower Northland:

  • Kauri wood carvings and jewelry
  • Manuka honey and herbal remedies
  • Local arts and crafts
  • Wines and craft beer
  • Kiwiana (New Zealand-themed gifts)

Local Culture and Customs of Lower Northland

Lower Northland is a region with a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the Maori and European settlers who made it their home. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the key cultural practices of Lower Northland:

  • Maori protocols when visiting Marae (meeting houses)
  • Respect for the environment and conservation efforts
  • Observance of traditional ceremonies and customs

Best Time to Visit Lower Northland

Lower Northland can be visited year-round. The best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Summer (December to February) is the most popular season for outdoor activities, while autumn (March to May) is perfect for relaxing and enjoying the beautiful fall colors. Winter (June to August) offers a quieter and more peaceful experience, with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lower Northland

Do:

  • Respect the Maori culture and customs
  • Embrace the natural environment and conservation efforts
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Be friendly and open to meeting new people

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Disrespect the Maori culture
  • Ignore safety warnings when participating in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Lower Northland

Lower Northland is a safe region for travelers. However, it is always best to take necessary precautions and follow safety guidelines to avoid any unpleasant incidents.

  • Pay attention to safety instructions when participating in adventure sports
  • Stay informed of weather and environmental conditions
  • Avoid swimming in unpatrolled areas

Health in Lower Northland

Lower Northland has a high standard of healthcare facilities for visitors. However, it is best to have travel insurance and be prepared for any health concerns that may arise during your trip.

  • Drink bottled water or boiled water
  • Be aware of sun exposure and use sunscreen

Getting to Lower Northland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lower Northland is easily accessible by various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Lower Northland

  • Whangarei Airport (WRE) is the main airport of the region, with daily flights from Auckland and Wellington.

Bus Stations Near Lower Northland

  • The Intercity Bus Service offers daily buses to various destinations in Lower Northland from Auckland and other major cities in New Zealand.

Other Options

  • Car rental is available at major airports in Auckland, Wellington and within the region.
  • The Interislander Ferry connects New Zealand’s North and South Island, with trains and buses connecting ferry terminals to Whangarei in Northland.

How to Get Around in Lower Northland

Lower Northland is best toured by rental car. However, taxis, buses and guided tours are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lower Northland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lower Northland is ideally situated for exploring other nearby destinations, including:

  • Auckland
  • Rotorua and Taupo
  • Coromandel Peninsula
  • The Far North and the Bay of Islands

Conclusion

Lower Northland is a magical destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure. With our comprehensive guide, you will have all the information you need to make your trip to Lower Northland unforgettable.
