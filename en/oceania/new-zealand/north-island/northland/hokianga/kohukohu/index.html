Introduction to Kohukohu

Kohukohu is a charming little town located in the Hokianga region of Northland, New Zealand. Tucked away on the western coast of North Island, Kohukohu offers visitors a chance to experience the untamed beauty of New Zealand’s natural landscapes, as well as the rich culture and history of the indigenous Maori people who have lived here for centuries.

Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, learn about Maori culture, or simply enjoy the relaxed pace of life in a small New Zealand town, Kohukohu has something for you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, food to try, and things to do in Kohukohu, as well as offer some helpful tips to make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Kohukohu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kohukohu is a town steeped in history and culture, and there are several important museums and historical places to visit in the area.

  • Kohukohu Heritage Trail: Take a walking tour of the town’s historic buildings and landmarks, including the picturesque St. Mary’s Church and the Old Post Office, which has been beautifully restored and now houses a museum.

  • The Founders’ Heritage Park: Learn about the settlers who established Kohukohu in the 19th century, as well as the rich history of the Maori people who have lived in the area for centuries.

  • Te Rarawa Anganui Hapu: This cultural center offers visitors an opportunity to learn about Maori history, culture, and traditions through art exhibits, performances, and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Kohukohu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kohukohu’s stunning natural landscapes offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

  • Hokianga Harbor: Take a leisurely boat ride or kayak tour on this stunning harbor, known for its calm waters and stunning sunsets.

  • Waipoua Forest: This ancient kauri forest is home to some of the largest and oldest trees in New Zealand, including Tane Mahuta, a giant kauri tree that is estimated to be over 2,000 years old.

  • Rawene Beach: Relax on the sandy shores of this scenic beach, which offers breathtaking views of the Hokianga Harbor and surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kohukohu

Kohukohu and its surrounding area are also great for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking.

  • Kauri Coast Trail: This 90-kilometer trail runs from Dargaville to Kai Iwi and offers stunning views of the New Zealand coastline, as well as easy access to several Maori cultural sites.

  • Kai Iwi Lakes Walk: This easy, family-friendly walk takes you through lush forests and past three beautiful lakes, where you can swim, fish, or simply enjoy a picnic.

  • Hokianga Summit Track: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Kohukohu’s highest peak, offering stunning views of the harbor and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kohukohu: Activities and Tours

Kohukohu offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors, whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply exploring the town’s charming streets and historic buildings.

  • Paddle Boarding and Kayaking: Explore the calm waters of the Hokianga Harbor on a paddle board or kayak tour.

  • Fish and Chips on the Beach: Grab some fish and chips from one of the local takeaways and head down to Rawene Beach for a relaxing afternoon picnic.

  • Maori Cultural Experiences: Learn about Maori culture and traditions through artwork, performances, and workshops at Te Rarawa Anganui Hapu.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kohukohu

For a complete and unforgettable experience, we recommend spending a week exploring all that Kohukohu and its surrounding area have to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Founders’ Heritage Park and the Kohukohu Heritage Trail.

  • Day 2: Take a boat ride or kayak tour on the Hokianga Harbor.

  • Day 3: Explore the Waipoua Forest and see the giant kauri trees.

  • Day 4: Relax on the beach or go for a swim at Rawene Beach.

  • Day 5: Take a guided Maori cultural tour at Te Rarawa Anganui Hapu.

  • Day 6: Hike to the top of Hokianga Summit and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the town’s friendly atmosphere, quaint cafes, and local shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Kohukohu

Kohukohu offers visitors a unique taste of New Zealand’s local cuisine, as well as some international favorites.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kumara: This sweet potato dish is a Maori favorite and can be served mashed, roasted, or in a pie.

  • Whitebait Fritters: A local specialty, these fritters are made with tiny fish and served with lemon and a side salad.

  • Hokey Pokey Ice Cream: A classic New Zealand dessert, this caramel-flavored ice cream is studded with chunks of crunchy toffee.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Kohukohu is home to several craft breweries, offering visitors a chance to sample some of New Zealand’s best artisanal beers.

  • Wine: Northland’s warm climate is perfect for growing grapes, and there are several local wineries that offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Kohukohu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kohukohu offers visitors a range of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference.

  • Kohouse: This charming guesthouse offers guests affordable, spacious rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

  • Kohukohu Hotel: This historic hotel has been beautifully restored and offers guests comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Kiwi cuisine.

  • Kohukohu Backpackers: This friendly hostel is the perfect choice for budget-minded travelers, with shared dorms and private rooms available.

What to Buy in Kohukohu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kohukohu is home to several local shops and markets, offering visitors a chance to take home some unique souvenirs and local products.

  • Kauri Gum: This fossilized tree sap is a local specialty and can be found in many shops throughout the town.

  • Maori Artwork: From intricate carvings to vibrant paintings, Maori artwork is a beautiful and meaningful souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kohukohu

Kohukohu is home to a vibrant Maori community, and visitors should be respectful of their culture and customs. It is also important to be mindful of the town’s relaxed pace of life and friendly atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Kohukohu

The best time to visit Kohukohu is during the summer months of December through February, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring and autumn can also be great times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kohukohu

Do:

  • Do take a cultural tour to learn about Maori history and traditions.

  • Do respect the town’s relaxed pace of life and friendly atmosphere.

  • Do try the local cuisine and sample some of the region’s craft beers and wines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

  • Don’t take any artifacts or fossils from the area.

  • Don’t disrespect the local Maori community or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kohukohu

Kohukohu is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and securing valuables.

Health in Kohukohu

Make sure to bring any necessary medications with you, as Kohukohu has limited medical facilities. It is also important to take basic precautions against sunburn and insect bites when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Kohukohu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kohukohu

The nearest airport to Kohukohu is Whangarei Airport, which is located approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kohukohu

InterCity buses run daily between Auckland and Kohukohu, with several stops along the way.

Other Options

Kohukohu is also accessible by car, with State Highway 12 running through the town.

How to Get Around in Kohukohu

The best way to get around Kohukohu is on foot, with everything in the town easily accessible by walking. Alternatively, renting a bike is a great way to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Kohukohu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kohukohu is located in the heart of Northland, offering visitors plenty of exciting nearby destinations and excursions.

  • Cape Reinga: Located at the northern tip of New Zealand, Cape Reinga offers breathtaking views and an important cultural landmark for the Maori people.

  • Bay of Islands: Just a short drive from Kohukohu, the Bay of Islands is home to stunning beaches, historic towns, and an abundance of marine life.

  • Kauri Cliffs: This luxury golf resort offers stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding countryside, as well as world-class spa treatments and dining.

Conclusion

Kohukohu is a hidden gem in New Zealand, offering visitors a chance to explore the stunning natural landscapes of Northland, as well as the rich culture and history of the Maori people. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life, Kohukohu has something for everyone. So why not start planning your trip today and experience all that Kohukohu has to offer?
