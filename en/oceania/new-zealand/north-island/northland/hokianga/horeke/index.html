Horeke is a charming rural village located in the historic Hokianga region of Northland, New Zealand, offering breathtaking views of the Hokianga Harbour and its surrounding lush countryside. While it may not be on most tourists’ radars, Horeke is a hidden gem worth exploring. In this comprehensive guide, we cover everything you need to know about this offbeat destination, including where to stay, what to see and do, and where to eat.

Introduction to Horeke

Horeke is nestled in the heart of Northland, New Zealand’s north island, and is around a three-hour drive from Auckland. The village boasts a rich history, dating back to the early 1800s when it was established as a busy timber milling town. Today, visitors can learn about its past by visiting the Horeke Tavern or taking a walking tour of the old timber mills. The village is an excellent place to explore New Zealand’s rural beauty and learn about Maori culture.

List of Places to Visit in Horeke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Horeke’s history is on full display throughout the village with several places to visit, including:

  • Horeke Tavern: With its traditional decor, this tavern is a must-visit spot for history buffs. It is the oldest operating pub in New Zealand and serves up traditional pub fare and local beers.

  • Clendon House: This historic house dates back to 1865 and was home to several prominent figures in New Zealand’s history. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the region’s early colonial period and capture a fantastic view of the Hokianga Harbour.

  • Horeke War Memorial: The memorial stands tall in honour of those soldiers from the region who lost their lives in various wars and conflicts.

Natural Attractions of Horeke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Horeke’s natural beauty is stunning, with several outdoor activities and scenic spots to explore, including:

  • Wairere Boulders: This outdoor attraction features natural lava formations that have been sculpted by the elements. Visitors can take a walk/hike through the boulders or take a guided tour.

  • Horeke Beach: This is one of the best hidden beaches in Northland, with stunning views of the harbour and lush green surroundings.

  • Mangungu Mission House: This historical site offers stunning views and a rich history to learn from.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Horeke

Hiking and biking are excellent ways to explore Horeke’s natural beauty. The best trails/routes to explore include:

  • Omahuta Kauri Sanctuary Track: This walking trail takes you through a beautiful Kauri forest.

  • Puketi Forest: This is a popular track leading to the Puketi Kauri forest with stunning waterfalls along the way.

Best Things to Do in Horeke: Activities and Tours

Horeke offers several activities that are perfect for travellers seeking adventure. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Fishing: Hokianga Harbour offers an opportunity to go fishing for snapper, kahawai, and more.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take an incredible journey to uncover some of the beautiful landscapes and sceneries on a canoe or kayak.

  • Blacksmith Experience: Experience the art of forging and smithing iron as it was done in the olden days.

Week-Long Itinerary for Horeke

Horeke is a small village, but a week-long trip can be relaxing and fulfilling. Our detailed itinerary includes:

Day Activities
1 A tour of Horeke’s historic sites and museums.
2 A hike up the Omahuta Kauri Sanctuary Track, followed by a visit to Wairere Boulders.
3 A kayak trip through Hokianga Harbour.
4 A visit to Puketi Kauri Forest.
5 A day of fishing in the harbour.
6 A visit to the charming Opononi and Omapere seaside towns.
7 Relax at Horeke Beach and enjoy the natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Horeke

Horeke is full of traditional and authentic food and drinks. The best things to try, include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangi: A traditional Maori cuisine of meat preserved with wood smoke.

  • Pavlova: A dessert made of a meringue base and topped with whipped cream and fruits.

Local Beverages

  • Kauri Wine: A wine made from the kauri fruit, found in few regions around the world.

  • Beer: The local beer scene offers a range of beer options guaranteed to suit the preferences of different tastes.

Where to Stay in Horeke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Horeke offers several accommodation options that will suit your preferences and budget. The best places to stay include:

  • Horeke Tavern: A historic pub that also offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining.

  • Kohukohu Hotel: A charming, restored historic hotel with comfortable rooms and stunning views.

What to Buy in Horeke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home souvenirs and trinkets that will serve as a reminder of your incredible trip to Horeke. Some of the best products to buy include:

  • Kauri Wood Crafts: Horeke is known for its kauri wood sculptures, bowls, and carvings.

  • Handmade Jewellery: Unique pieces of jewellery made by local artisans using traditional methods and natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Horeke

The village has deep roots in indigenous culture and proudly shows off the North’s Maori past. Visitors should respect local customs and try to learn more about local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Horeke

The best time to visit Horeke is between November and April, summer season in New Zealand.

Do’s and Don’ts of Horeke

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Explore the village’s history and cultural richness.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings. Leave every area better than how you found it.

  • Ignore the local culture and people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Horeke

Horeke is a relatively safe village, but visitors should still exercise normal safety measures such as locking cars, avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on personal belongings.

Health in Horeke

Visitors should always carry a basic first aid kit, and always attend to any cuts, scratches or insect bites immediately when they happen. Visitors should also carry sunscreen, as the sun can get pretty harsh during the summer months.

Getting to Horeke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Horeke

The closest airport to Horeke is Auckland Airport, which is about a three-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Horeke

Buses depart from Auckland and drive towards Horeke. Daily bus tours can also be arranged.

Other Options

Travellers can take a car or motorbike ride on the scenic State Highway 1 and then venture into Horeke from there.

How to Get Around in Horeke

Walking is perfectly fine within the village, while cycling is an excellent option for visitors who want to venture out further.

Where to Go Next from Horeke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations to Horeke include:

  • Opononi and Omapere: Along the beautiful Hokianga Harbour offers a variety of water sports activities.

  • KeriKeri: a popular destination and one of Northland’s cultural and organic hubs.

Conclusion

Horeke is a hidden gem that offers a mix of history, Maori culture, and scenic beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you can have an unforgettable trip to this rural village filled with authentic food, scenic views, and adventure.
