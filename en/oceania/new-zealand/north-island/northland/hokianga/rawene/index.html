Introduction to Rawene

Located in the Hokianga region of Northland, New Zealand, Rawene is a small town with a rich history and plenty of natural beauty. With its Maori and European history, you’ll find a fusion of culture everywhere you go. Rawene offers a variety of activities, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, making it an excellent destination for everyone’s travel bucket list. Continue reading to discover the best things to do in Rawene.

List of Places to Visit in Rawene

There are plenty of attractions to explore around Rawene. To help you plan your itinerary, here’s a list of places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Opononi and Omapere Beaches - These beaches are among the most popular in the Northland.
  2. Wairere Boulders - For those looking for an outdoor adventure, hike the boulder-strewn path.
  3. The Hokianga Harbour - Take a leisurely boat ride or a scenic walk overlooking the beautiful harbor.
  4. Rawene Cemetery - This historic cemetery provides a unique insight into the town’s rich history.
  5. Mangungu Mission House - This historic building is worth a visit for its history and stunning architecture.
  6. Clendon House - Dive into the early colonial history of New Zealand by visiting this well-preserved home.

Natural Attractions of Rawene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Signal Station Lookout - The best way to take in the stunning views of the Hokianga Harbor is from this lookout.
  2. Twin Coast Cycle Trail - Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore the flora and fauna of New Zealand while enjoying this stunning trail.
  3. Tane Mahuta - This famed kauri tree is known for its age and size.
  4. Koutu Boulders - Witness one of the few places in the world where large spherical boulders are readily accessible and visible.
  5. Mitimiti Beach - Relax like a local and savor the beautiful sand beaches and calm waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rawene

Rawene has plenty of outdoor activities to satisfy the adventurous spirit. The Kauri Coast Track takes you deep into the forest, while the Twin Coast Cycle Trail offers a leisurely ride. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes around Rawene:

  1. Omahuta-Puketi Forest - Check out the impressive kauri forest.
  2. Waipoua Forest - Discover New Zealand’s oldest and most significant trees with the help of a local guide.
  3. Hokianga Harbour to Mangungu Mission House - Follow the route used by early settlers crossing the harbor to get to the mission house.

Best Things to Do in Rawene: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to make your visit to Rawene memorable. Here are a few recommendations:

  1. Kohu Ra Tākiri Marae - Embrace Maori culture with a visit to this historic meeting house.
  2. Wine Tours - Tasting award-winning wine and enjoying a picnic with stunning views.
  3. Boat Tours - Check out Rawene from a different perspective by taking a boat tour of the harbor.
  4. Fishing Charters - Experience the fishing in New Zealand on a charter boat with advanced fishing tools.
  5. Horse Treks - Embark on a beautiful journey to the heart of the Hokianga forest on horseback.

Food and Drink Scene of Rawene

Rawene has excellent dining and drinking options to enjoy with friends and family. Below is a list of dishes and drink varieties that you have to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips - New Zealand’s specialty dish can be found at various eateries around the city.
  2. Whitebait Fritters - Rawene’s famous dish made from whitebait, an iconic New Zealand dish.
  3. Kina - A locally sourced delicacy that is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer - Find various local craft beer breweries around town.
  2. Wine - Rawene’s vineyards produce a variety of award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Rawene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rawene is home to elaborate accommodations ranging from lodges to backpackers’ hostels. Choose from the comprehensive list of some recommended lodging options below:

  1. Jetty House - A popular bed and breakfast offering stunning views from every room of the house.
  2. The Boat Shed - An opulent luxury hotel set in a resort estate with a day spa and restaurant.
  3. Rawene Holiday Park - A park with modern facilities and extensive views of the Hokianga Harbor.

What to Buy in Rawene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Rawene back home with you by purchasing these unique souvenirs and local products:

  1. Local Art - Pieces inspired by New Zealand’s natural landscape and local artists are popular gifts.
  2. Kauri Products - From carved wood to polished souvenirs, kauri is a popular local product.
  3. Paua Jewelry - Unique jewelry made from locally sourced paua, one of New Zealand’s best-known shellfish.

Local Culture and Customs of Rawene

Rawene is a cultural melting pot, rich with Maori and European cultures. Embrace the local culture while taking part in activities like a Maori Cultural performance, Marae visit, or a local wine tour with a knowledgeable guide for insight into local customs.

Best Time to Visit Rawene

The best time to visit Rawene is during the summer from December to February. Expect warm weather that is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rawene

Do:

  1. Do respect local Maori customs and traditions.
  2. Do try out the local food and beverages.
  3. Wander and enjoy the scenic views and architecture of Rawene.

Don’t:

  1. Do not trespass on private land.
  2. Do not litter or damage the environment.
  3. Don’t leave the town without experiencing all that Rawene has on offer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rawene

Rawene is safe to travel with general precautions on public transport, in crowded areas, and after dark necessary.

Health in Rawene

Rawene has primary medical facilities. For any medical emergency, dial 111, and request for an ambulance. It is also advisable to purchase travel health insurance in advance.

Getting to Rawene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rawene

The nearest domestic airport to Rawene is Kaitaia Airport, which is a 1.5-2-hour drive away. The international airport nearest to Rawene is Auckland International Airport, providing more significant travel options for overseas visitors.

Bus Stations Near Rawene

Intercity and other local bus services operate regularly to and from Rawene. The primary coach operators visiting Rawene include InterCity and ManaBus.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most flexible way to get to Rawene as it provides greater freedom to explore the region and visit neighboring attractions like Bay of Islands or Cape Reinga.

How to Get Around in Rawene

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around Rawene as it is a small town and provides a stunning, easy-to-navigate environment. To explore the surrounding areas, use rental cars or take group guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Rawene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While in Rawene, take an opportunity to explore nearby destinations, including:

  1. The Bay of Islands - Take a ferry ride to this iconic Northland destination.
  2. Ahipara - Drive up the West Coast and explore sandy waters of Ahipara.
  3. Cape Reinga - Take a scenic drive to the Cape and stand on the tip of New Zealand.

Conclusion

Rawene is a fantastic destination in New Zealand that caters to all travelers’ types. The town offers natural beauty, cultural experiences, and adventurous activities that make for a memorable trip. Plan your getaway and engage in a unique blend of Maori and European culture, explore the scenic harbor, local art, wines, and more. Explore Rawene and creating memories that will last a lifetime.
