Introduction to Turangi

Nestled in the central part of North Island in New Zealand, Turangi is an idyllic town known for its world-renowned trout fishing, stunning scenery, and great outdoors. It is home to the Tongariro River, which is rated as one of the best trout fishing rivers in the world. Turangi is also a great base for exploring the Tongariro National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site. In this comprehensive Turangi travel guide, we will cover everything from the best hiking and biking routes to where to stay, eat, and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Turangi

Turangi is a small town but has several attractions worth exploring. We have categorized it into culturally important attractions, museums, and historical places and natural attractions for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Trout Centre

The National Trout Centre is a popular attraction for families and fishers alike. It features aquariums of live trout, static and interactive displays, and hatchery tours. It’s a great way to learn about the history and importance of trout fishing in Turangi and New Zealand.

Opepe Memorial

The Opepe Memorial is a war memorial located near Turangi. It commemorates the significant military engagement that took place between British troops and Maori fighters in 1869. It is an important site in New Zealand’s colonial history.

Natural Attractions of Turangi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tongariro National Park

Tongariro National Park is one of the most popular natural attractions in New Zealand. It is home to three active volcanoes (Mount Tongariro, Mount Ruapehu, and Mount Ngauruhoe) and offers stunning landscapes perfect for hiking and trekking.

Tongariro River

The Tongariro River is the lifeblood of Turangi, offering some of the finest trout fishing opportunities in the world. It’s also a great spot for recreational kayaking and rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Turangi

Turangi offers some of the most beautiful and challenging hiking and biking routes in New Zealand. Here are some of our top picks:

Tongariro Alpine Crossing

This 19.4 km one-way hike is one of the most popular in New Zealand. It offers stunning views of the volcanic landscape, including Mount Ngauruhoe (Mount Doom in the Lord of the Rings movies). The hike takes around 6-8 hours, and hikers need to be prepared for changes in weather.

Huka Falls Trail

The Huka Falls Trail is an easy 1-hour walk that takes you to the famous Huka Falls. It’s a great choice for beginners, families, and those who love stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Turangi: Activities and Tours

Turangi is the perfect place for adventure enthusiasts, families, and solo travelers. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Turangi.

Fishing

Fishing is undoubtedly the most popular activity in Turangi. The town is famous for its trout fishing, and there are plenty of licensed guides who can take you on a fishing trip.

Tongariro Alpine Crossing Guided Tour

If you want to experience the Tongariro Alpine Crossing but don’t feel confident enough to tackle it on your own, a guided tour is a great option. Guides take care of everything from transport, gear, and safety.

Week-Long Itinerary for Turangi

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week spent in Turangi.

Day 1: Arrive in Turangi, settle in, and explore the town center.

Day 2: Hike the Tongariro Alpine Crossing.

Day 3: Go fishing on the Tongariro River.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of Tongariro National Park.

Day 5: Relax and enjoy the scenery or go kayaking or rafting in the Tongariro River.

Day 6: Explore the National Trout Centre or visit the Opepe Memorial.

Day 7: Depart from Turangi.

Food and Drink Scene of Turangi

Turangi offers several dining options, ranging from cafes, restaurants, takeaways, and pubs. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Trout

If you love fish, try the trout, which Turangi is famous for. You can find it in most restaurants and cafes in the town.

Kumara Chips

Kumara chips are a must-try for vegetarians and vegans. They are a healthier alternative to potato chips and are locally grown.

Local Beverages

Manuka Beer

Manuka beer is a unique beverage native to New Zealand, brewed using manuka honey.

Schnapps

Turangi Schnapps is a popular local drink, made using New Zealand apple juice.

Where to Stay in Turangi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Turangi has several accommodation options catering to all budgets and preferences.

Luxury Hotels

For those who want a luxury stay, there are several resorts and hotels, such as the Tongariro Lodge, which is famous for its trout fishing.

Backpacker Hostels

For budget travelers, there are several backpacker hostels and camping options, such as Turangi Bridge Backpackers and Turangi Kiwi Holiday Park.

What to Buy in Turangi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Before leaving Turangi, don’t forget to check out the local shops and pick up some souvenirs.

Trout-Related Products

Trout-related products, such as trout flies, fishing gear, and fishing books, are perfect for fishing enthusiasts.

Maori Carvings

Maori carvings are an intrinsic part of New Zealand’s culture. They make great decorative pieces or souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Turangi

New Zealanders are friendly and welcoming people. In Turangi, the culture is deeply rooted in fishing, hunting, and farming.

Best Time to Visit Turangi

Turangi can be visited year-round; however, the best time to visit is during the summer months (December to February), when the weather is warm and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turangi

Do:

  • Respect the environment and keep it clean.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and hiking conditions.
  • Be prepared for changes in weather

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Harm wildlife
  • Ignore safety rules

Safety and Health

Safety in Turangi

Turangi is a safe place to visit, but it’s essential to take the necessary precautions while hiking or fishing in the area.

Health in Turangi

Medical facilities are available in Turangi. However, it’s always wise to have travel insurance before commencing any activities.

Getting to Turangi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Turangi is located approximately 4 hours south of Auckland and 1 and a half hours south of Taupo. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Turangi

The closest airports to Turangi are Taupo Airport and Rotorua Airport.

Bus Stations Near Turangi

Intercity buses run daily services to Turangi from Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch.

Other Options

Car rental is also an option for those who want to drive in New Zealand.

How to Get Around in Turangi

To explore the town center, walking or cycling is the best way. However, to visit nearby attractions, such as the Tongariro National Park, a rental car or taxi is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Turangi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue your journey after Turangi, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

Taupo

Taupo is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning lakes, waterfalls, hot springs, and geothermal activity.

Rotorua

Rotorua is another popular destination in New Zealand known for geothermal activity, Maori culture, and adventure sports.

Conclusion

Turangi is a hidden gem in New Zealand, offering breathtaking views, outdoor adventures, and a chance to experience New Zealand’s rich culture. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Turangi with ease and explore everything this beautiful town has to offer.
