Introduction to Taupō

Nestled in the heart of New Zealand’s Central North Island is the picturesque and scenic town of Taupō. Known for its natural beauty and spectacular landscapes, Taupō is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts, thrill-seekers, and those looking for a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. From glistening lakes to rugged mountains, Taupō offers a diverse range of experiences for visitors to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Taupō

Taupō is filled with must-see sights and attractions for visitors. Here are just a few to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Taupō Museum and Art Gallery - showcasing Taupō’s history and art scene with interactive exhibits and displays.

  2. Maori Rock Carvings - a unique cultural attraction accessible only by boat or kayak, depicting Māori legends and stories.

  3. Wairakei Terraces and Thermal Health Spa - a stunning geothermal attraction featuring terraced gardens and a thermal health spa.

Natural Attractions of Taupō: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tongariro Alpine Crossing - a world-famous hike offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape, including Mount Ngauruhoe (a.k.a. Mount Doom of The Lord of the Rings fame).

  2. Huka Falls - a popular attraction with visitors, featuring a powerful waterfall and crystal clear blue water.

  3. Great Lake Taupō - a sprawling lake offering activities such as swimming, fishing, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taupō

Taupō is a great destination for avid hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  1. Mount Tauhara - a challenging climb up to a lookout point with breathtaking views of Lake Taupō.

  2. Great Lake Walkway - an easy walking and biking trail offering spectacular lake and mountain views.

  3. Craters of the Moon - a volcanic landscape characterized by steaming vents and craters, with a variety of walking trails to explore.

Best Things to Do in Taupō: Activities and Tours

Taupō offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are just a few worth checking out:

  1. Skydiving - jump out of a plane with Skydive Taupō for a thrilling and unforgettable experience.

  2. Geothermal Hot Springs - relax and unwind in one of Taupō’s numerous hot springs and geothermal pools.

  3. Jet Boating - take a thrilling ride down the Waikato River with Hukafalls Jet and experience the power of Huka Falls up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taupō

To make the most of your time in Taupō, consider the following week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Taupō, explore the town center and surrounding area.
  • Day 2: Hike to the summit of Mount Tauhara for panoramic views of Lake Taupō.
  • Day 3: Explore the Wairakei Terraces and Thermal Health Spa.
  • Day 4: Take a boat or kayak to the Māori Rock Carvings.
  • Day 5: Spend the day at Great Lake Taupō with swimming, fishing and water sports.
  • Day 6: Visit the Tongariro Alpine Crossing and hike the trail.
  • Day 7: Take a jet boat ride down the Waikato River and visit Huka Falls.

Food and Drink Scene of Taupō

Taupō has an impressive food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local food and beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pumice Chicken - a local specialty made with free-range chicken, served with a variety of sauces and sides.

  2. New Zealand lamb - a Kiwi classic, cooked to perfection and served with fresh seasonal vegetables.

  3. Whitebait fritters - a popular seafood dish made with fresh whitebait, eggs, and seasoning.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft beers - Taupō has a thriving craft beer industry, with numerous breweries producing excellent beer from locally sourced ingredients.

  2. Wine - the nearby Hawke’s Bay region is one of New Zealand’s premier wine producing areas, with a range of wines available to try in Taupō.

Where to Stay in Taupō: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taupō offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes:

  1. The Hilton Lake Taupō - a luxurious hotel offering stunning views of the lake and mountains.

  2. Base Backpackers Taupō - a budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable dormitories and private rooms.

  3. Lake Taupō Lodge - a charming bed and breakfast featuring beautifully designed rooms and a tranquil garden.

What to Buy in Taupō: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those seeking unique souvenirs and local products, Taupō has plenty to offer. Here are some ideas:

  1. Handmade art and crafts - visit the Taupō Art Connection gallery for a range of locally made paintings, sculptures, and pottery.

  2. New Zealand Manuka honey - renowned for its antibacterial properties, Manuka honey makes for a delicious and healthy souvenir.

  3. Lavender products - Taupō has numerous lavender farms and shops selling a range of lavender-infused products.

Local Culture and Customs of Taupō

Taupō has a rich cultural history and heritage, with many Māori traditions and customs still practiced today. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and etiquette, such as removing shoes before entering someone’s home and asking permission before taking photographs of Māori cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Taupō

The best time to visit Taupō is during the summer months (December - February), when temperatures are warm and the weather is generally dry. However, autumn (March - May) and spring (September - November) also offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taupō

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Explore the great outdoors and natural beauty of Taupō.

  3. Try some of the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave garbage in public spaces.

  2. Take anything from the natural environment or harm local wildlife.

  3. Disrespect local people or cultural traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taupō

Taupō is generally a safe and welcoming destination, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking doors and windows at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Taupō

Taupō has a modern and well-equipped medical center, as well as several pharmacies and clinics. Visitors are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance that includes coverage for medical emergencies.

Getting to Taupō: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taupō is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Taupō

The nearest international airport to Taupō is located in Auckland, approximately 280km away. Taupō also has a small domestic airport serving flights to other parts of the country.

Bus Stations Near Taupō

Taupō is well-connected to other parts of New Zealand by bus, with numerous services departing from the town center.

Other Options

For those traveling from Auckland or Wellington, a scenic train ride with KiwiRail is a memorable way to reach Taupō.

How to Get Around in Taupō

Taupō is relatively small and easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Car rentals are also available for those who wish to explore the surrounding area in greater depth.

Where to Go Next from Taupō: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taupō is ideally situated for exploring other areas of the North Island. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Rotorua - a geothermal wonderland with hot springs, mud pools, and Māori cultural experiences.

  • Waiheke Island - a beautiful island located near Auckland, known for its vineyards, beaches, and scenic walks.

  • Waitomo Caves - a network of caves and underground rivers featuring bioluminescent glowworms.

Conclusion

Taupō is an incredible destination for those seeking natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. With so much to see and do, Taupō is sure to be a highlight of any trip to New Zealand.
