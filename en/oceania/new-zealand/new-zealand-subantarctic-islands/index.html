Introduction to New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

New Zealand Subantarctic Islands is a group of islands located in the Southern Ocean, south of New Zealand. Comprising five main islands - Auckland Island, Antipodes Island, Campbell Island, Snares Island, and Bounty Island - the Subantarctic Islands are a nature lover’s paradise. These islands are home to a unique variety of flora and fauna, including penguins, albatross, fur seals, and sea lions. The islands also have a rich cultural history, dating back to the 18th century when they were first discovered by European explorers.

List of Places to Visit in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Enderby Island: This island is named after the Enderby Brothers, who established the first shore-whaling station in New Zealand in the early 1800s. Visit the ruins of the station and the graves of the whalers.
  • Campbell Island: The island has a rich cultural history, ranging from the prehistoric Maori to the European accents on the flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of New Zealand Subantarctic Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Auckland Island: Take a scenic boat tour along the rugged coastline of Auckland Island and marvel at the impressive landscape and unique wildlife.
  • Antipodes Island: Explore the rugged beauty of the island’s mountains, cliffs, and rocky shorelines, home to a vast array of birdlife and seals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

  • Dent Island: Hike up to Mount Dick and take in the breathtaking views of Auckland Island and the surrounding islands.
  • Campbell Island: Take a walk around the island on the recently opened loop track and discover the island’s diverse flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Watching: Take a guided tour to see the unique wildlife of the islands, including penguins, fur seals, and albatross.
  • Fishing: Join a fishing expedition and catch a variety of fish, including cod, haddock, and snapper.

Food and Drink Scene of New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty soup made with fresh seafood such as mussels, clams, and fish.
  • Venison Stew: A delicious stew made with locally hunted venison, potatoes, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Wines: New Zealand is known for its delicious wines, including Sauvignon Blanc and Pinot Noir.
  • Beer: Enjoy a cold local beer, such as Speight’s or Monteith’s.

Where to Stay in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The New Zealand Subantarctic Islands are a remote location where visitors can stay in camping tents or lodges. There are no hotels, hostels, or guesthouses on these islands.

What to Buy in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antarctic krill oil: This oil is extracted from a small shrimp-like crustacean that lives in the Southern Ocean.
  • Penguin-themed souvenirs: Bring home a penguin-shaped plush toy or a t-shirt with a picture of the adorable birds.

Local Culture and Customs of New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

  • The Maori people were the first inhabitants of New Zealand, and their culture and traditions still influence the country today.
  • The islands have a rich whaling history, and visitors can learn more about the whaling industry and see the remains of shore-whaling stations.

Best Time to Visit New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

The best time to visit the New Zealand Subantarctic Islands is during the summer months from November to March when the weather is mild, and the wildlife is abundant. The winter months from April to October are not suitable for tourism due to harsh weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and do not disturb their natural habitats.
  • Dress in warm layers and appropriate outdoor gear.

Don’t:

  • Leave any trash or litter behind.
  • Disturb any historical sites or artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

The New Zealand Subantarctic Islands can be hazardous due to rough weather conditions and rugged terrain. It is essential to follow the guidelines of the park authorities and be properly equipped for outdoor activities and tours.

Health in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

Visitors are advised to be up to date on their routine vaccinations and to get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies before traveling to the islands. It is also recommended to carry a basic first aid kit and any necessary prescription medications.

Getting to New Zealand Subantarctic Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

There are no airports on the New Zealand Subantarctic Islands.

Bus Stations Near New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

There are no bus stations on the New Zealand Subantarctic Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can charter a boat or join a wildlife watching tour to reach the New Zealand Subantarctic Islands.

How to Get Around in New Zealand Subantarctic Islands

The best way to get around the New Zealand Subantarctic Islands is on foot or by boat. There are no roads or public transportation on the islands.

Where to Go Next from New Zealand Subantarctic Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stewart Island: Visit the third-largest island in New Zealand and explore its beautiful beaches and hiking trails.
  • South Island: Explore the rugged and breathtakingly beautiful South Island, home to majestic mountains, fjords, and glaciers.

Conclusion

The New Zealand Subantarctic Islands offer a unique and unforgettable adventure for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With its awe-inspiring scenic views, diverse flora and fauna, rich cultural history, and exciting outdoor activities, the islands are a perfect destination for those looking for an adventure of a lifetime. Follow this guide and plan your trip to the New Zealand Subantarctic Islands to experience the most unique and breathtaking parts of New Zealand.
