Introduction to Gore

Gore is a charming town located in Southland, South Island, New Zealand. With beautiful scenery, a rich cultural heritage, and plenty of outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Gore. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, this town has something to offer. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a deep dive into the top attractions, best things to do, and local tips for exploring Gore like a pro.

List of Places to Visit in Gore

If you’re planning a trip to Gore, you won’t want to miss these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gore Historical Museum: Explore the rich history of Gore and Southland at this excellent museum. You’ll find artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that tell the story of the town and its residents.
  • ** Hokonui Moonshine Museum**: Discover the infamous history of illegal moonshine-making in Gore and Southland. This interesting museum tells the story of the moonshine trade and its impact on the region.
  • Eastern Southland Gallery: If you’re an art lover, don’t miss the opportunity to visit this excellent gallery. The exhibitions are always changing, so there’s always something new to see.

Natural Attractions of Gore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • ** Mataura River**: The Mataura River is a fly fisherman’s paradise. The clear waters are home to an abundance of brown trout and attract anglers from all over the world.
  • ** Hokonui Hills**: Hiking in the Hokonui Hills is a must-do if you’re visiting Gore. The stunning views from the top are well worth the effort.
  • ** Dolamore Park**: This beautiful park is the perfect place for a picnic or a leisurely stroll. There are walking trails, playgrounds, and plenty of open spaces for relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gore

Gore has plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, including hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes for exploring the beautiful nature around Gore:

  • ** Mataura River Trail**: This 13 km trail follows the Mataura River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It’s an easy ride and suitable for all ages and abilities.
  • ** Hokonui Hills Track**: This challenging hike takes you through the stunning Hokonui Hills and rewards you with incredible views from the top.
  • ** Waikaka River Track**: This 2-hour hike takes you along the picturesque Waikaka River and through native bush. It’s an easy hike suitable for all ages and abilities.

Best Things to Do in Gore: Activities and Tours

Aside from the attractions and hiking/biking routes listed above, here are some other fun things to do in Gore:

  • Fishing: Whether you’re an experienced angler or a first-timer, Gore is a great place to fish. You can hire a guide or explore the waters on your own.
  • Golf: Gore has several excellent golf courses, including the Gore Golf Club and the Hokonui Golf Club. Tee off with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Farm Stay: If you want to experience rural life in New Zealand, consider a farm stay. You’ll get to meet animals, learn about farming practices, and enjoy country hospitality.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gore

If you have a week to spend in Gore, here’s a sample itinerary for making the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Gore Historical Museum and have lunch at a local cafe
2 Fly fishing on the Mataura River
3 Hike the Hokonui Hills Track and have a picnic in Dolamore Park
4 Visit the Eastern Southland Gallery and try some local wines at a nearby winery

Food and Drink Scene of Gore

Gore has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of cafes, restaurants, and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb: New Zealand is known for its excellent lamb, and Gore is no exception. Try it grilled, roasted, or in a hearty stew.
  • Cheese: Southland is home to some excellent cheese makers, and you can sample their wares at local markets and specialty stores.
  • Blue Cod: This local fish is a must-try for seafood lovers. It’s often served battered and fried, and goes well with a cold beer.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: New Zealand has a thriving craft beer scene, and Gore is no exception. Check out the local breweries and try some unique brews.
  • Wine: The nearby Otago region is known for its excellent wines, including pinot noir and chardonnay. Take a wine tour and sample some of the best.

Where to Stay in Gore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gore has a variety of accommodations to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Heartland Hotel Croydon: This 4-star hotel is located in the heart of Gore and offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Riverlea Motel: This budget-friendly motel is located next to the Mataura River and has everything you need for a comfortable stay.
  • The Brown Trout Motel: This family-owned motel is located in a quiet neighborhood and offers spacious rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Gore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Gore, consider these local products:

  • Merino Wool: New Zealand is known for its excellent merino wool, and you can find it in many shops in Gore.
  • Greenstone: Also known as pounamu, this beautiful green stone is a traditional Maori symbol of strength and connection to the land.
  • Local Cheese: Southland is home to some excellent cheesemakers, and their products make great gifts for foodie friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Gore

Gore is a proud and welcoming community with a strong connection to the land and its history. Maori culture is an important part of the town’s identity, and you’ll find plenty of opportunities to engage with local traditions and customs. Respect for the environment is also a key value in Gore, and visitors are encouraged to practice sustainable travel habits.

Best Time to Visit Gore

Gore has a temperate climate with mild temperatures year-round. However, the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and natural scenery, visit in the summer months (December-February). If you’re interested in winter sports and snow activities, visit in the winter months (June-August).

Do’s and Don’ts of Gore

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Gore:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and practice sustainable travel habits.
  • Engage with local traditions and customs, and be respectful of Maori culture.
  • Try the local food and drink - it’s delicious!

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Enter private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Gore is generally a safe and healthy town to visit. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions and practice good health habits.

Safety in Gore

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid unsafe areas at night.
  • Wear appropriate gear and follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Gore

  • New Zealand has a public healthcare system, and visitors are usually covered under reciprocity agreements. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance.
  • Protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen and a hat.
  • Take basic precautions to avoid food and waterborne illnesses.

Getting to Gore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gore is easily accessible by several transportation options. Here are some of the best ways to get to Gore:

  • By plane: The closest airport is Invercargill airport, located about an hour’s drive from Gore. You can take a taxi or shuttle to reach Gore.
  • By bus: Gore is well-connected by bus to other cities in New Zealand. InterCity and other bus companies operate regular services.
  • By car: If you’re driving, Gore is located on State Highway 1 and is easily accessible from other parts of Southland and Otago.

How to Get Around in Gore

Once you’re in Gore, the best way to get around is by car or bike. There are several car rental companies in town, and bikes can be rented from local shops as well. If you prefer public transportation, there is a limited bus service within Gore and to nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Gore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more places to explore after Gore, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Invercargill: This nearby city is known for its excellent museums and galleries, as well as its beautiful parks and gardens.
  • Queenstown: Known as the adventure capital of New Zealand, Queenstown is just a few hours’ drive from Gore and offers a wide range of outdoor activities and adventures.
  • Fiordland National Park: This stunning national park is home to Milford Sound, one of the most beautiful natural wonders in New Zealand. It’s just a few hours’ drive from Gore and makes for an excellent day trip.

Conclusion

Gore is a hidden gem in Southland, New Zealand. With a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and plenty of outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. By following the tips and recommendations in this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be well on your way to exploring the best of Gore and experiencing the local culture like a pro.
