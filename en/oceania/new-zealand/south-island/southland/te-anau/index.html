Introduction to Te Anau

Te Anau is located in Southland, on the South Island of New Zealand, in the stunning region of Fiordland. Named after the lake on its doorstep, Te Anau is a charming small town that’s known for its picturesque landscapes, magnificent national parks, and outdoorsy vibe. It’s the perfect destination for nature lovers, hikers, campers, kayakers, and adventurers looking to get off the beaten path and experience a more authentic side of New Zealand.

List of Places to Visit in Te Anau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fiordland National Park Visitor Centre: Located in the heart of Te Anau, this visitor center is your one-stop-shop for all things Fiordland. From exhibits and interactive displays to helpful staff who can answer any questions you might have about the park, this is an essential stop for anyone visiting Te Anau.
  • Te Anau Museum: This quaint little museum is home to a collection of artifacts, photos, and memorabilia that tell the story of Te Anau’s past. It’s a great place to learn about the town’s history and the early explorers who first settled here.
  • Ivon Wilson Park: This stunning park is home to a replica of a traditional Maori Waka, which is a large canoe used by the indigenous people of New Zealand. It’s a great way to learn about the culture and history of the Maori people.

Natural Attractions of Te Anau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Milford Sound: Located about two hours away from Te Anau, Milford Sound is one of the most beautiful natural wonders in New Zealand. This stunning fjord is surrounded by towering mountains, cascading waterfalls, and crystal clear waters that are home to a variety of marine life. Take a scenic boat tour or kayak excursion to fully experience this incredible destination.
  • Te Anau Glowworm Caves: These magical caves are home to thousands of tiny glowworms that light up the underground caverns like stars in the night sky. Take a guided tour to learn about the fascinating geology and biology of the caves and get up close and personal with these unique creatures.
  • Kepler Track: This 60 km hiking trail is one of the best in New Zealand, and it starts right in Te Anau. Take a day hike or a multi-day trek to experience some of the most stunning landscapes in the region, including forests, alpine meadows, and snow-capped mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Te Anau

Te Anau has plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, including the Kepler Track, which we mentioned earlier. Here are a few more routes to consider:

  • Luxmore Track: This challenging hike takes you up to the summit of Mount Luxmore, where you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Mavora Lakes Park: This beautiful park is home to several hiking and biking trails that wind through forests, pastures, and around the stunning Mavora Lakes. It’s a great place to spot wildlife and enjoy a peaceful picnic in nature.

Best Things to Do in Te Anau: Activities and Tours

  • Scenic Flight Over Fiordland: Take to the skies for a thrilling aerial tour of Fiordland, including sights like Milford Sound, the glaciers of the Southern Alps, and the rugged coastline of the Tasman Sea.
  • Kayaking on Lake Te Anau: Rent a kayak or join a guided tour to explore the stunning waters of Lake Te Anau up close. You’ll paddle past waterfalls, forests, and may even spot some friendly dolphins along the way.
  • Horseback Riding: Saddle up and explore the scenic countryside surrounding Te Anau. Guided tours are available for riders of all skill levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Te Anau

If you’re planning to spend a week in Te Anau, here’s a sample itinerary to give you an idea of what you can see and do.

Day 1: Visit the Fiordland National Park Visitor Centre and explore the town of Te Anau.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive to Milford Sound and spend the day exploring the fjord on a boat tour or kayak excursion.

Day 3: Hike the Kepler Track and enjoy a picnic lunch in the stunning alpine meadows.

Day 4: Relax and take it easy, maybe spending the day at the Te Anau Glowworm Caves.

Day 5: Take a scenic flight over Fiordland and enjoy a bird’s eye view of this incredible region.

Day 6: Rent a bike and explore the Mavora Lakes Park, maybe even spending the night camping under the stars.

Day 7: Enjoy some horseback riding and explore some of the quieter areas around Te Anau before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Te Anau

Must-Try Dishes

  • Venison: New Zealand is known for its delicious venison, and Te Anau is no exception. Be sure to try a hearty venison dish while you’re in town.
  • Whitebait: Whitebait is a type of fish that’s popular throughout New Zealand. Try it pan-fried with a squeeze of lemon for a tasty treat.
  • Kumara: This sweet potato-like vegetable is a staple in Maori cuisine. Try it roasted or mashed for a unique and flavorful side dish.

Local Beverages

  • New Zealand Wine: New Zealand has emerged as a major producer of high-quality wines in recent years, so be sure to sample some of the local vintages while you’re in Te Anau.
  • L&P: This fizzy lemonade is a Kiwi classic and a must-try for visitors to New Zealand.
  • Monteith’s Beer: Monteith’s is a popular beer brand in New Zealand, and you’ll find it on tap at many of Te Anau’s local pubs.

Where to Stay in Te Anau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Te Anau has plenty of options for lodging, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Distinction Te Anau Hotel & Villas: This modern four-star hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of Lake Te Anau.
  • Te Anau Lakeview Kiwi Holiday Park & Motels: This budget-friendly option offers a range of accommodations, including cabins, campsites, and cozy motel rooms.
  • Fiordland Lodge: This upscale guesthouse is located just outside of town and offers breathtaking views of Fiordland National Park.

What to Buy in Te Anau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Merino Wool Products: New Zealand is famous for its high-quality merino wool, so be sure to pick up a cozy scarf, sweater, or hat to bring home.
  • Greenstone Jewelry: Greenstone, also known as jade, is a traditional Maori stone that is often used to make beautiful jewelry. Look for unique pendants or earrings while you’re in town.
  • Manuka Honey: This honey is made from the nectar of the Manuka bush and is known for its antibacterial properties. It’s a delicious and healthy souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Te Anau

New Zealand has a rich and fascinating indigenous culture, and Te Anau is a great place to learn more about it. Be sure to be respectful of Maori customs and traditions, and take the time to learn about the history and culture of the people who call this beautiful land home.

Best Time to Visit Te Anau

The best time to visit Te Anau is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer (December to February) is the high season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices during this time. Winter (June to August) can be cold and rainy, but it’s a great time to take advantage of the region’s ski opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Te Anau

Do:

  • Take the time to explore Fiordland National Park and the spectacular landscapes that surround Te Anau.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs, and take the time to learn about the history and traditions of the Maori people.
  • Try some of the local dishes, including venison and whitebait.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather. Bring warm clothing and rain gear, even in the summer.
  • Damage the environment or leave trash behind. Practice Leave No Trace principles and help keep the area pristine for future visitors.
  • Disrespect the local culture or treat it as a tourist attraction. Take the time to learn and understand the rich history and traditions of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Te Anau

Te Anau is generally a safe destination, but as with any outdoor activity, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of the weather and take appropriate precautions when hiking, boating, or engaging in other outdoor activities.
  • Watch out for insect bites and wear appropriate clothing and bug spray to avoid getting bitten by sandflies and mosquitoes.
  • Stay on marked trails and be aware of the potential for land slips and rockfalls in some areas.

Health in Te Anau

Te Anau has a small medical clinic and pharmacy, but for serious medical issues, you’ll need to travel to the nearby city of Invercargill or Queenstown. If you have any underlying health conditions, be sure to consult with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Te Anau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Te Anau is located about two hours away from the nearest airport (Queenstown), so you’ll need to plan accordingly when it comes to transportation. Here are a few options to consider:

Airports Near Te Anau

  • Queenstown Airport: This is the nearest airport to Te Anau, located about two hours away by car. Several airlines offer daily flights to Queenstown from major cities in New Zealand and Australia.

Bus Stations Near Te Anau

  • Te Anau Coach & Bus Services: This local bus company operates regular services to and from Queenstown, as well as other destinations on the South Island.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the area at your own pace. Several major rental car companies operate out of Queenstown Airport.
  • Private Shuttle: There are several private shuttle companies in the area that offer door-to-door service from Queenstown to Te Anau.

How to Get Around in Te Anau

Te Anau is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to visit some of the more remote areas outside of town, you’ll need a car or bike. There are also several local taxi companies that operate in the area.

Where to Go Next from Te Anau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Te Anau is located in the heart of Fiordland, so there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few ideas:

  • Milford Sound: This stunning fjord is located about two hours away from Te Anau and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in New Zealand.
  • Queenstown: This vibrant city is located about two hours away and is known for its charming downtown area, outdoor adventures, and world-class restaurants and bars.
  • The Routeburn Track: This famous hiking trail is located about an hour away from Te Anau and is known for its stunning landscapes and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Te Anau is a small town that packs a big punch when it comes to natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to hike through majestic mountains, explore crystal-clear lakes, or simply relax in a cozy guesthouse, there’s something here for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and get ready to experience one of the most breathtaking destinations in all of New Zealand.
