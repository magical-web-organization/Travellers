Introduction to Southland

Southland, located in the southern region of New Zealand’s South Island, offers an incredible mix of natural beauty, rich history, and stunning landscapes. With its vast and untouched wilderness, breathtaking coastal views, and unique attractions, Southland is a traveler’s paradise.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and the do’s and don’ts of Southland. Whether you’re keen on outdoor adventures, rich indigenous culture, or simply admiring stunning scenery, Southland is a destination that has much to offer. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Southland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Te Hikoi Southern Journey Museum

This Museums is located in Riverton and well-known for its exhibitions that showcase the history and culture of the Maori People, pioneers, and other settlers. You can learn about the traditional ways of life, tools of survival, and tales of colonialization.

Clifton Suspension Bridge

This bridge is the oldest suspension bridge in New Zealand. You’ll find it a short walk from Clifton Beach, near Invercargill. The location boasts stunning views of the surrounding coastline and the bridge is an incredible feat of engineering.

Bill Richardson Transport World

Bill Richardson Transport World is a unique museum that car enthusiasts will love. The museum showcases a collection of over 400 vehicles, including vintage cars, trucks, and other transportation vehicles. It also features a working workshop, where visitors can see vehicle restoration work in progress.

Natural Attractions of Southland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Milford Sound

Milford Sound, located in Fiordland National Park, is one of the most visited natural attractions in New Zealand. The stunning scenery includes steep cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and wildlife sightings, making it an unforgettable experience.

Stewart Island

Stewart Island is a great destination for wildlife lovers. Visitors can spot kiwis, tui birds, and parakeets, as well as take part in wildlife tours and fishing trips.

Catlins Coast

If you’re looking for stunning coastal views, Catlins Coast is the place to go. Here, you’ll find rugged cliffs, crashing waves, and wildlife sightings that will leave you breathless.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southland

Hiking Trails

South Island is famous for overland tracks that offer trekkers extensive landscapes. Some of the best hiking destinations in Southland include:

Kepler Track

Kepler Track is located in Fiordland National Park and is one of the most popular overnight hikes, due to its unique and diverse range of landscapes. It’s also great for day treks.

Routeburn Track

Routeburn Track is a challenging hike that takes you through the beautiful alpine region of Southern Alps. The trailhead is about an hour’s drive from Queenstown.

Hollyford Track

The Hollyford Track offers a great mix of river valleys, native forests, and beaches. This multi-day hike is perfect for those who enjoy a little challenge.

Biking Trails

Southland also offers great biking trails with picturesque scenery.

Around the Mountains Trail

Around the Mountains Trail is a family-friendly biking trail that traces the edge of the Eyre Mountains and the bottom of the Hollyford Valley. It features stunning views and ties in with other trails.

Clutha Gold Trail

The Clutha Gold Trail takes bikers through the historic gold mining region of Central Otago. The trail covers approximately 73km and is considered beginner-friendly.

Best Things to Do in Southland: Activities and Tours

Visit the Catlins Wildlife Park

Catlins Wildlife Park is not your ordinary zoo. It features interactive exhibits and experiences with wildlife. Visitors can hold a kiwi bird, feed a possum, and get up close with other animals in their natural habitats.

Gore Art Gallery

The Gore Art Gallery is a unique attraction that showcases a range of contemporary artworks. The gallery also hosts several art festivals throughout the year, which you can take advantage of.

Hump Ridge Track

If you’re looking for some adventure, consider taking the Hump Ridge Track. This 61km hike will take you through some of Southland’s stunning landscapes and unique tracks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southland

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Southland, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day 1 - Invercargill City Tour and Bill Richardson Transport World

Day 2 - Milford Sound Boat Cruise

Day 3 - Te Hikoi Southern Journey Museum and Clifton Suspension Bridge

Day 4 - Stewart Island Day Tour

Day 5 - Catlins Coast Road Trip

Day 6 - Kepler Track

Day 7 - Gore Art Gallery and Hiking or Biking in Around the Mountains Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Southland

Southland’s food scene comprises of fresh seafood, lamb, and farm-to-table produce.

Must-Try Dishes

Whitebait Fritters

Whitebait fritters are made from eggs, vegetable oil, and whitebait. You can find them at many restaurants in Southland.

Bluff Oysters

Bluff Oysters are a must-try for seafood lovers. They’re in season from March to August and are served raw or lightly cooked.

Bacon & Egg Pie

This New Zealand classic dish is made from bacon, eggs, cheese, and pastry. It’s perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Local Beverages

Speight’s Beer

Speight’s Beer is a Southland favorite, offering a crisp taste with hints of malt and caramel.

Pinot Noir

Pinot Noir is a must-try wine when in Southland. The region’s cool climate and soil make it one of the best places for this wine’s full-bodied taste.

Where to Stay in Southland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

Some of the best hotels in Southland include The Kelvin Hotel, Invercargill and Queens Park Motel, Invercargill-both offer spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a convenient location.

Hostels

Invercargill Top 10 Holiday Park is a clean, quiet, and affordable hostel with great facilities and an excellent location.

Guesthouses

The Lodges B&B, Fiordland is a great choice for visitors seeking a comfortable guesthouse with pristine views.

What to Buy in Southland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southland is known for its locally produced crafts and goods. Some of the best souvenirs to take home include:

Possum Merino Clothing

Possum Merino Clothing is a warm and cozy type of clothing made from Possum and Merino Wool. It’s perfect for cold weather or for anyone who loves woolen garments.

Greenstone (Pounamu)

Greenstone is a type of durable rock that has cultural significance to the Maori people. You can find Greenstone jewelry and other products in Southland’s many souvenir shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Southland

Kiwi people are known for their friendly nature, and most locals in Southland are welcoming to travelers. Southland’s Maori culture is celebrated and integral to the region’s history and contemporary life.

Best Time to Visit Southland

The best time to visit Southland is between December and February. The weather during this time is mild, with temperatures averaging 20°C and less rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southland

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes
  • Take your rubbish when on outdoor trips

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings
  • Remove or take samples of any plant or animal life
  • Show disrespect towards the locals’ way of life

Safety and Health

Safety in Southland

Southland is generally a safe place for travelers. Watch out for rough waters, as well as sudden weather changes during outdoor activities.

Health in Southland

Medical facilities are limited in Southland, so make sure to have adequate medical insurance before traveling.

Getting to Southland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southland

Southland’s main airport is Invercargill Airport, which is served by all major Australian airlines.

Bus Stations Near Southland

InterCity Bus lines connect Southland from other major cities in New Zealand.

Other Options

The TranzAlpine train line is a scenic option for those traveling from Christchurch to Southland. Car rental services and personalized tour companies are also available in the region.

How to Get Around in Southland

The most convenient mode of transport in Southland is a car. Otherwise, bus lines could also be a suitable option for travelers.

Where to Go Next from Southland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southland is centrally located to some of New Zealand’s top destinations. Some of the nearby destinations include Queenstown, Milford Sound, and Dunedin.

Conclusion

Southland, New Zealand, is a fantastic destination for travelers looking for a mix of adventure and leisure. From hiking and biking trails to delicious food and unique products, Southland has it all. Follow our guide, and you’ll be left with nothing to regret on your trip to Southland.
