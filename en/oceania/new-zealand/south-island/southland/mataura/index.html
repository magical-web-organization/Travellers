Introduction to Mataura

Mataura is a charming town located in the Southland region of New Zealand’s South Island. It is situated on the banks of the Mataura River and is known for its rich history, picturesque scenery, and warm hospitality. Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or an adventure seeker, Mataura has something to offer to all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Mataura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mataura Museum - Explore the town’s rich heritage through fascinating exhibits on local industries, people, and events.
  2. Mataura Heritage Trail - Take a walk along this trail to discover historic landmarks and sites that showcase the town’s past.
  3. Mataura Marae - Learn about Maori culture and traditions at this spiritual and cultural center.

Natural Attractions of Mataura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mataura River - Go fishing, kayaking, or simply enjoy the serene beauty of the river.
  2. Eyre Mountains/Taka Ra Haka Conservation Park - Take a hike, go bird watching or camping amidst the stunning landscapes of this park.
  3. Waituna Lagoon - This Ramsar-listed wetland is home to numerous bird species and offers great opportunities for wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mataura

  1. Heritage Trail - Explore the town’s history along this easy and scenic walking trail.
  2. Mataura River Trail - This four-kilometer trail is perfect for biking, running, or walking alongside the picturesque river.
  3. Eyre Mountains/Taka Ra Haka Conservation Park Trail - Challenge yourself with a hike on this trail, which offers spectacular views of the Eyre Mountains and the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Mataura: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing Tours - Enjoy a guided fishing tour on the Mataura River, which is famous for its brown trout.
  2. Kayaking and Canoeing - Rent a kayak or a canoe and explore the beautiful waterways of Mataura.
  3. Wildlife Tours - Take a wildlife tour to discover the diverse birdlife and flora of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mataura

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Mataura Museum, Mataura Heritage Trail, and the town center
Day 2 Go fishing or kayaking on the Mataura River
Day 3 Visit Mataura Marae and learn about Maori culture
Day 4 Take a hike in Eyre Mountains/Taka Ra Haka Conservation Park
Day 5 Go bird watching at Waituna Lagoon
Day 6 Try local food and wine at Mataura’s cafes and restaurants
Day 7 Relax and unwind at your accommodation or go souvenir shopping in the town center

Food and Drink Scene of Mataura

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Whitebait Fritters - A local delicacy, these fritters are made with fresh whitebait and seasoned with herbs and spices.
  2. Muttonbird - A traditional Maori food, this seasonal delicacy tastes similar to duck meat.
  3. New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc - Mataura is located in one of the country’s premier wine regions, so be sure to try some of the local wine.

Local Beverages

  1. Speight’s Brewery - This historic brewery in nearby Invercargill produces a range of award-winning beers.
  2. Mac’s Brewery - Located in Nelson, this brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are popular throughout the country.

Where to Stay in Mataura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Mataura Lodge - This luxurious lodge offers guests a relaxing retreat surrounded by beautiful gardens and views of the Mataura River.
  2. Gore Motels - Located just a few kilometers from Mataura, these modern and functional accommodations offer a range of options for all budgets.
  3. Airbnb - Visitors to Mataura can also find a variety of unique and affordable accommodations through Airbnb.

What to Buy in Mataura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Local Wines - Mataura is home to several vineyards that produce award-winning wines, which make for great gifts or souvenirs.
  2. Paua Shell Jewelry - Paua shells are a popular decorative item in New Zealand, and you can find a variety of jewelry and souvenirs made from them in Mataura.
  3. Merino Wool Products - New Zealand is known for its high-quality merino wool, and visitors can find a variety of woolen products such as hats, scarves, and mittens in the town’s shops and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Mataura

Mataura and its surrounding areas are rich in Maori culture and traditions, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect these customs during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Mataura

The best time to visit Mataura is during the summer season (December-February) when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, visitors can enjoy Mataura’s scenic beauty and cultural attractions year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mataura

Do:

  • Respect Maori culture and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and wildlife.
  • Try local food and wine.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety advice when outdoors.
  • Disrespect local customs and etiquette.
  • Bring non-biodegradable materials to the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mataura

Mataura is a safe destination, but visitors are advised to take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Wear suitable clothing and footwear, carry water and snacks, and let someone know your plans and expected return time.

Health in Mataura

Medical facilities are available in nearby Gore and Invercargill. Visitors are advised to carry their travel insurance and any necessary medications and vaccinations.

Getting to Mataura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mataura

The nearest airports to Mataura are Dunedin International Airport (166 km) and Queenstown International Airport (211 km).

Bus Stations Near Mataura

The closest bus station is in Gore, which is 13 km away from Mataura. Buses are also available from Invercargill and Dunedin.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or camper van to explore Mataura and its surrounding areas. Taxi and shuttle services are also available.

How to Get Around in Mataura

Mataura is a small town, and most attractions can be reached on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mataura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Invercargill - Explore this charming city and its attractions such as the Burt Munro Museum and the Southland Museum and Art Gallery.
  2. Fiordland National Park - This stunning national park is located just a few hours’ drive from Mataura and offers breathtaking scenery, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing.
  3. Catlins Coast - Spend a day exploring the rugged coastline, waterfalls and wildlife encounters of The Catlins.

Conclusion

Mataura welcomes visitors with open arms and offers them a memorable experience. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning scenery, and vibrant food and drink scene, Mataura is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore New Zealand’s South Island.
