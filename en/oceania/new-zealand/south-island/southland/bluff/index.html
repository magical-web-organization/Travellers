Introduction to Bluff

Bluff is a small coastal town located at the southernmost point of New Zealand’s South Island. With a population of just over 1,800, this charming town is known for its picturesque scenery, fresh seafood, and friendly locals. Bluff is a must-visit destination for those who are looking for a peaceful escape from the city life.

List of Places to Visit in Bluff

Bluff has a lot to offer for tourists. Here is a list of must-visit places in Bluff:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bluff Maritime Museum: Explore exhibits on the town’s maritime history, including whale hunting and the famous Bluff oyster.
  • Stirling Point: Take a picture with the iconic signpost that marks the southernmost point of the South Island.

Natural Attractions of Bluff: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bluff Hill: Take a stroll or bike ride to the top of Bluff Hill for breathtaking views of the ocean and the surrounding islands.
  • Ulva Island: Take a ferry to Ulva Island, a wildlife sanctuary with native birdlife and lush forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bluff

Bluff offers an array of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best spots:

  • Bluff Hill: Explore this 265-meter hill with a 2-hour loop track walk.
  • Omaui Track: This 10km hike through the Omaui Scenic Reserve is a must for birdwatchers.
  • Bluff to Invercargill cycleway: Take a bike ride through the scenic coastal route, starting from Bluff and ending in Invercargill.

Best Things to Do in Bluff: Activities and Tours

  • Oyster Festival: Visit Bluff in May to attend the annual Oyster Festival, celebrating the town’s famous Bluff oyster.
  • Fishing and Boating: Go fishing or take a boat cruise, and enjoy the scenic views of the Foveaux Strait.
  • Bluff Hill Lookout Point: Take a picnic and watch the sunset from the lookout point atop Bluff Hill.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bluff

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Bluff, here is our recommended itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore and hike in Bluff Hill, Stirling Point, and the Maritime Museum.
  • Day 3-4: Take a ferry to Ulva Island and explore the native forests and birdlife there.
  • Day 5-6: Relax and enjoy the coastal scenery with a fishing or boat cruise and a sunset picnic on Bluff Hill.
  • Day 7: Visit nearby Invercargill and explore the Southland Museum and Art Gallery and Queens Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Bluff

Bluff is known for its fresh seafood, especially the famous Bluff oyster. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bluff Oysters: Taste the famous delicacy at one of the many seafood restaurants in town.
  • Blue Cod: Enjoy a classic fish and chips with a fresh Blue Cod fillet.
  • Paua Fritters: Try this delicious Maori dish, fried patties made of Paua, or Abalone.

Local Beverages

  • As Bluff is a small town, some local bars may not be available. But you should try some of the local wines of Otago area while you’re in Bluff.

Where to Stay in Bluff: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bluff has an array of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Bluff Ocean Vista Motel: A modern motel offering amazing views of the ocean.
  • Bluff Lodge: A comfortable guesthouse with a cozy fireplace and beautiful garden.
  • The Lighthouse Guesthouse: Heritage-listed guesthouse with stunning views of the Foveaux Strait.

What to Buy in Bluff: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products you should bring back home from Bluff:

  • Bluff Oyster: Bring home a box of Bluff oyster, either canned or fresh, to share with your loved ones.
  • Paua Shell Jewelry: Buy some jewelry made of Paua shells as a beautiful and unique souvenir.
  • Manuka Honey: Purchase some delicious Manuka honey, known for its medicinal properties, before you leave Bluff.

Local Culture and Customs of Bluff

Bluff has a rich history and culture, with a strong connection to the ocean and nature. The locals are friendly and welcoming to tourists, and visitors are encouraged to respect their cultural values and practices.

Best Time to Visit Bluff

The best time to visit Bluff is during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, Bluff is a beautiful destination all year round, with different outdoor activities and festivals throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bluff

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local seafood, especially Bluff oysters.
  • Take part in outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Litter anywhere in town.
  • Enter private properties without permission.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bluff

Bluff is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Bluff

Bluff has adequate medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Bluff: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bluff

The nearest airport to Bluff is Invercargill Airport, which is located about 30 minutes from Bluff by car.

Bus Stations Near Bluff

InterCity and Newmans Coach Lines operate bus services from Invercargill to Bluff.

Other Options

A ferry service is also available to Stewart Island, which connects to Bluff.

How to Get Around in Bluff

Bluff is a small town with limited transportation options. Visitors can rent a car or bike to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bluff: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to visit from Bluff:

  • Stewart Island: Take a ferry to Stewart Island, an untouched haven for nature enthusiasts.
  • The Catlins: Explore the stunning coastal scenery and waterfalls of the Catlins, located about 2-hours from Bluff.
  • Invercargill: Visit the nearby city of Invercargill, known for its art and culture scene.

Conclusion

Bluff is a small town with huge character and charm. With stunning coastal vistas, fresh seafood, and rich cultural heritage, Bluff is a destination not to be missed on your New Zealand itinerary.
