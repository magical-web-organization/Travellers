Introduction to Ward

Located in the Marlborough region of South Island, New Zealand, Ward is a small town with a big personality. Boasting stunning natural landscapes, exciting outdoor activities, and a rich Maori culture, Ward is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic Kiwi experience.

In this guide, we will take you on a journey through Ward’s must-visit attractions, including museums, hiking and biking trails, and local food and drink options. We’ll also cover where to stay, the best time to visit, and provide tips on how to get there and get around.

So strap on your hiking boots and pack your sense of adventure – let’s explore the enchanting town of Ward!

List of Places to Visit in Ward

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Maori Point Road

Located on the edge of town, Maori Point Road is home to a significant Maori archaeological site. Here, visitors can learn about the history and culture of the indigenous people of New Zealand.

2. Kekerengu Store

Kekerengu Store, on State Highway 1, is a historic building that dates back to the early 1900s. This quaint general store offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s rural history and unique way of life.

3. Ward Beach

Ward Beach is not only a stunning natural attraction, but also an important historical site. It was here that the Amokura, a Maori trading schooner, ran aground in 1932. Today, visitors can explore the wreckage and learn more about New Zealand’s maritime history.

Natural Attractions of Ward: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Mt Fishtail

Attracting hikers and trail runners from around the world, Mt Fishtail is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts. The summit, which offers breathtaking 360-degree views of the surrounding countryside, is accessible via several different trails.

2. Queen Charlotte Track

One of New Zealand’s Great Walks, the Queen Charlotte Track is a 70km trail that passes through Ward’s beautiful Marlborough Sounds. Hikers can enjoy stunning sea views, lush green forests, and spot the native wildlife, including dolphins and seals.

3. Robin Hood Bay

A hidden gem just outside of Ward, Robin Hood Bay boasts pristine white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and some of the best kayaking and paddleboarding opportunities in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ward

1. Mt Robertson

This challenging hike rewards visitors with stunning views of Cook Strait and the surrounding countryside.

2. Tantragee Track

An intermediate-level biking trail, the Tantragee Track takes riders through dense forests and along ridge lines, offering stunning views.

3. Northbank Road

Northbank Road is a popular mountain biking trail, offering challenging terrain and beautiful views of the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Ward: Activities and Tours

1. Whale Watching Tour

The Marlborough Sounds are home to several whale species, including the elusive and rare Hector’s Dolphin. Local tour operators offer impressive whale watching tours, allowing visitors to see these majestic creatures up close.

2. Wine Tasting Tour

Marlborough is known for its excellent wine, and many of the region’s best vineyards are just a short drive from Ward. Visitors can take wine-tasting tours, learn about the winemaking process, and sample world-class wines.

3. Fishing

Ward is renowned for its excellent fishing opportunities. Visitors can charter fishing trips with local guides, who will take them to the best spots for catching snapper, kingfish, and other species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ward

Day 1 Arrive in Ward, check into accommodation, and explore Ward Beach.
Day 2 Hike Mt Fishtail, taking in the stunning views from the summit.
Day 3 Take a wine-tasting tour of the Marlborough region’s vineyards.
Day 4 Go on a whale watching tour and see the majestic creatures up close.
Day 5 Try your hand at fishing, booking a charter boat with a local guide.
Day 6 Relax on the beautiful beaches of Robin Hood Bay.
Day 7 Depart Ward, either for home or your next adventure!

Food and Drink Scene of Ward

Must-Try Dishes

1. Bluff Oysters

This local delicacy is considered some of the best seafood in the world. Try them freshly shucked at one of the region’s many seafood restaurants.

2. Whitebait Fritters

A must-eat for visitors to New Zealand, whitebait fritters are a local favorite. These small, crispy patties are made from tiny freshwater fish and are often served with lemon and aioli.

3. Venison

Thanks to the region’s ample forest and open fields, Ward is renowned for its venison dishes. Hunters can bring their own or sample it at local restaurants.

Local Beverages

1. Sauvignon Blanc

Marlborough is world-famous for its excellent Sauvignon Blanc, with the region’s particular climate and soil conditions producing some of the best in the world.

2. Craft Beer

Ward has a booming craft beer industry, with several local breweries producing unique and flavorful beers.

3. Kiwi Cocktails

Visitors should try kiwi cocktails, made with fresh local fruit and herbs, and garnished with some of the region’s unique flora and fauna.

Where to Stay in Ward: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Ward Beach Holiday Park

This amenity-rich caravan park offers guests the option to stay in cabins, campsites, or motels. There is a playground for children and barbecue facilities for guests.

2. Tiki Wine Country Lodge

Located just a short drive from Ward, this luxury lodge offers guests stunning views of the surrounding countryside and easy access to the region’s best vineyards.

3. Matua Lodge

This charming bed and breakfast provides guests with a cozy, comfortable stay, complete with a homemade breakfast and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Ward: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Manuka Honey

New Zealand is famous for its Manuka honey, which is known for its antibacterial properties and health benefits. Bring some home for your pantry or as the perfect gift for the foodie in your life.

2. Greenstone Jewelry

Greenstone, known as pounamu to the Maori people, is an important cultural symbol in New Zealand. Beautiful greenstone jewelry, often in the form of intricate necklaces and earrings, makes for a thoughtful and unique gift.

3. Local Artworks

Ward is home to many talented artists, whose work is inspired by the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can purchase unique and beautiful artworks to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ward

The Maori people have a strong presence in the Marlborough region, and visitors are advised to show respect for their culture and traditions. Kiwis are known for their laid-back and friendly nature, so visitors should feel free to chat with locals and ask for recommendations.

Best Time to Visit Ward

The best time to visit Ward is during the summer months, from December to February, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall can also be good times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ward

Do:

  • Respect the local Maori culture and traditions.
  • Try the local seafood – it’s some of the best in the world!
  • Explore the beautiful natural surroundings and partake in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect wildlife or their habitats.
  • Drive under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ward

Ward is a very safe destination for travelers. Visitors should take basic safety precautions, like locking their cars and not leaving valuables visible.

Health in Ward

Visitors to New Zealand should have comprehensive travel insurance, including medical coverage. The country has an excellent public health system, but medical treatment can be expensive for those without insurance.

Getting to Ward: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ward

The nearest airports to Ward are Blenheim (approx. 50 min drive) and Picton (approx. 1.5-hour drive). International visitors may also choose to fly into Christchurch or Wellington, which offer more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Ward

There are several bus companies that operate in the Marlborough region, including InterCity and Newmans Coach Lines. Visitors can take buses from major cities like Christchurch and Wellington to Blenheim, then transfer to local buses to get to Ward.

Other Options

Many visitors to Ward choose to rent a car or camper van, which allows them the freedom to explore the countryside at their own pace. Taxi services are also available, though visitors should expect to pay more for the convenience.

How to Get Around in Ward

Ward is easily navigable on foot or by bike, though those who want to explore more of the region may choose to rent cars or camper vans. Taxis and buses are available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Ward: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Picton

The picturesque town of Picton is located just a short drive away from Ward and is the gateway to the beautiful Marlborough Sounds. Visitors can take ferries to the North Island from here or explore the town’s museums, art galleries, and restaurants.

Nelson

Located just across the Cook Strait in the South Island, Nelson is a vibrant hub of arts, culture, and food. Visitors can check out the local craft beer scene, explore the region’s best vineyards, or spend a day shopping in the town’s many boutiques.

Conclusion

Ward, New Zealand is a destination not to be missed by travelers looking for authentic Kiwi experiences. With its stunning natural landscapes, exciting outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage, Ward is a unique and unforgettable destination that will capture the hearts of visitors from around the world.
