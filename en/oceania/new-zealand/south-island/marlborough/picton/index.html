Introduction to Picton

Nestled in Marlborough, South Island, New Zealand, is the picturesque town of Picton. Surrounded by spectacular scenery, this charming coastal town is the gateway to the Marlborough Sounds and a popular stopover for those traveling between the North and South Islands. Known for its stunning views, outdoor adventures, and premium vineyards, Picton has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Picton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Edwin Fox Maritime Museum: One of the oldest surviving historic ships in the world, the Edwin Fox is now a museum that showcases the history of the ship and the town.

  • Picton Museum: The museum highlights the local history of Picton and Marlborough with its collection of artifacts and displays.

  • Omaka Aviation Heritage Center: An internationally acclaimed museum with an impressive collection of World War I and II aircraft and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Picton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Queen Charlotte Track: This 70 km track offers breathtaking views of the Marlborough Sounds and is a popular spot for trekking, mountain biking, and kayaking.

  • Marlborough Sounds: This series of waterways, hills, and bays are perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming.

  • Picton Foreshore: A relaxing spot to stroll around, relax, and take in the beauty of the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Picton

  • Waikawa Track: A scenic route that offers stunning views of Picton Harbor and the surrounding landscape.

  • Bob’s Bay Track: A short and easy track that leads to a secluded and pristine beach.

  • Esson’s Valley Track: A challenging and rewarding route that winds through the picturesque forest.

Best Things to Do in Picton: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tours: Visit the many local vineyards and taste some of the premium wines that Marlborough is famous for.

  • Dolphin Watching: Take a tour and enjoy the sights of playful dolphins in their natural habitat.

  • Scenic Flights: Get a bird’s eye view of Marlborough Sounds and the surrounding area on a scenic flight tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Picton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green-Lipped Mussels: This local delicacy is a must-try for seafood lovers.

  • Lamb Dish: New Zealand is known for its delicious lamb dishes, so don’t miss out.

  • Roast Beef: A classic Kiwi dish that you can find in most restaurants in Picton.

Local Beverages

  • Sauvignon Blanc: Marlborough is famous for its world-class Sauvignon Blanc, and you can find several wineries in the area.

  • Craft Beers: Picton has some excellent local craft breweries, so make sure to try a few of them.

  • Flat White: The national coffee of New Zealand, a flat white is a must-try for coffee lovers.

Where to Stay in Picton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Picton Yacht Club Hotel: A luxurious hotel situated in a prime location with stunning views of Picton Harbor.

  • The Gables: A charming and elegant guesthouse with beautiful gardens and spectacular views.

  • Picton Top 10 Holiday Park: A budget-friendly option, this holiday park offers cabins, campsites, and many amenities.

What to Buy in Picton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Greenstone Jewelry: New Zealand is known for its beautiful greenstone carvings and jewelry, which can be found in many stores in Picton.

  • Sauvignon Blanc Wine: Don’t forget to bring back a bottle of Marlborough’s world-famous Sauvignon Blanc wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Picton

The Maori culture has a strong influence on the local culture and customs. It is essential to respect their customs and traditions while visiting. Kiwis are generally friendly and laidback.

Best Time to Visit Picton

The best time to visit Picton is during the summer months from December to February when the weather is warm and sunny. Winter months from June to August are also popular among tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Picton

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take care of the outdoor environment and leave no trace.

  • Wear sunscreen and appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.

  • Leave rubbish in public places.

  • Drive under the influence of alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Picton

Picton is a safe place to visit, but it is essential to take basic precautions when traveling, such as keeping your belongings safe and not leaving valuables unattended. Be aware of the weather hazards, especially when hiking.

Health in Picton

Picton has excellent healthcare facilities. Don’t forget to bring any necessary medication with you as it might not be readily available.

Getting to Picton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Picton

  • Nelson Airport (NSN): 115 km away.

  • Wellington International Airport (WLG): 85 km away.

Bus Stations Near Picton

  • Picton i-SITE Visitor Center: Buses and coaches depart from here to major destinations.

Other Options

  • Interislander Ferry: An iconic ferry service that connects Picton to Wellington.

How to Get Around in Picton

Picton is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot. Taxis, rental cars, and bicycles are also available.

Where to Go Next from Picton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Queen Charlotte Sound: A picturesque waterway with plenty of hiking and biking trails.

  • Marlborough Wine Country: A must-visit for wine lovers, this region boasts some of the best vineyards in the country.

  • Nelson: A charming town with a vibrant arts and music scene, located 115 km from Picton.

Conclusion

Picton is a beautiful and charming coastal town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and premium vineyards. With plenty of attractions and activities to keep you busy, as well as fantastic food, accommodation, and transportation options, Picton is a must-visit destination in New Zealand.
