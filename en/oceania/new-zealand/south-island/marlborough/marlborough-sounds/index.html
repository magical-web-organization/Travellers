Introduction to Marlborough Sounds

Marlborough Sounds, located in the northeastern part of the South Island of New Zealand, is a stunning coastal region famous for its breathtaking scenery, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage. Comprising of 1,500 kilometers of coastline, Marlborough Sounds is a collection of ancient sunken river valleys which create a maze of picturesque waterways, secluded bays, and small coves.

The best way to explore the region’s natural beauty is by boat, kayak or by foot, as hiking trails wind through native forests, across rugged ridges and expansive valleys. Marlborough Sounds is also home to a range of unique bird species, including the kiwi and native bush parrot, the Kakariki.

List of Places to Visit in Marlborough Sounds

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Edwin Fox Maritime Museum - an impressive museum displaying the history of the world’s oldest surviving ship, the Edwin Fox.
  2. Omaka Aviation Heritage Centre - a museum with a world-renowned collection of aviation artifacts.
  3. Marlborough Museum - A museum dedicated to the natural and cultural history of Marlborough Sounds.

Natural Attractions of Marlborough Sounds: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Queen Charlotte Track - A 72 km track winding through tranquil native forests, along the rugged coastline and with stunning views over the sounds.
  2. Dolphin and Whale Watching - Marlborough Sounds is home to many species of dolphins, orcas, and whales that can be spotted on guided tours.
  3. Mountain Biking - Marlborough Sounds has a network of trails for mountain biking enthusiasts.
  4. Scenic Drives - Explore the region’s scenic highlights by driving along the Sounds’ scenic road, a stunning drive winding along the coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marlborough Sounds

  1. Queen Charlotte Track - A 3-5 day walking track, offering jaw-dropping views over Queen Charlotte and Kenepuru Sounds.
  2. Link Pathway - A 44 km trail connecting Picton and Havelock. The track is gentle and suitable for all ages.
  3. Madsen’s Camp Track - found in the Pelorus Sound, is a must-do hiking route for adventurers.

Best Things to Do in Marlborough Sounds: Activities and Tours

  1. Seafood Tour - Enjoy local specialties of the region, including fresh seafood, on a guided tour.
  2. Wine tours - Go on a winery tour and enjoy some of the world’s best Sauvignon Blancs.
  3. Birdwatching - Marlborough Sounds hosts a variety of unique bird species, including the native bush parrot, the Kakariki.
  4. Kayaking or boat tours - Explore the indigenous flora and fauna of the Marlborough Sounds’ aquamarine waters and secluded bays.

Food and Drink Scene of Marlborough Sounds

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Green-lipped mussels - the delicacy of Marlborough region.
  2. New Zealand lamb - enjoy a variety of magnificent lamb dishes in Marlborough.
  3. Crayfish - fresh and locally caught crayfish are a must-have experience.

Local Beverages

  1. Sauvignon Blanc - Marlborough is famous for its world-class Sauvignon Blancs.
  2. Pinot Noir - the region produces excellent Pinot Noir, with crisp acidity and fruity flavors.

Where to Stay in Marlborough Sounds: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Bay of Many Coves - luxury lodge with stunning views over the Queen Charlotte Sound
  2. Furneaux Lodge - a historic lodge located in Endeavour Inlet.
  3. Picton Yacht Club Hotel - a comfortable hotel located by Picton Marina.

What to Buy in Marlborough Sounds: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Greenstone (Pounamu) - New Zealand’s national stone is found exclusively on the west coast of the South Island.
  2. Sauvignon Blanc - A bottle of Marlborough’s renowned wine will always remind you of your fantastic Marlborough experience.
  3. Kiwi art and craft - visit local artisans and pick up a piece of unique kiwi art and craft.

Local Culture and Customs of Marlborough Sounds

Marlborough Sounds is a region with a rich cultural heritage, and its local Maori community still plays an important role in shaping its culture and customs. Visitors should be respectful of the region’s cultural heritage and seek out opportunities to learn from the local community.

Best Time to Visit Marlborough Sounds

Marlborough Sounds can be visited year-round, with warm summers and mild winters. The best time of year to experience the region’s beauty is from November to April when the weather is at its warmest, and the water is calm for kayaking and boat tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marlborough Sounds

Do:

  1. Respect the cultural heritage of the region and learn about its Maori history.
  2. Take a boat tour or go kayaking to explore the stunning bays and secluded coves.
  3. Drive along the scenic road to experience the breathtaking views of the Marlborough Sound.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the pristine waters or on the trails.
  2. Over-exert yourself on hikes without ensuring that you are adequately prepared for the weather and terrain.
  3. Ignore the rich cultural heritage of the region. Always respect the customs and culture of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marlborough Sounds

While Marlborough Sounds is a safe destination for travelers, visitors should take appropriate precautions to ensure their safety, especially when engaging in outdoor activities. It’s essential to pack adequate equipment and supplies for outdoor activities and pay attention to weather forecasts and warnings.

Health in Marlborough Sounds

Marlborough Sounds has a good healthcare system and provides excellent medical care for visitors in case of emergencies. Nevertheless, tourists should always make sure they have adequate medical cover before traveling.

Getting to Marlborough Sounds: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marlborough Sounds

The closest airport to Marlborough Sounds is Blenheim Airport, which is serviced by daily flights from major cities like Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch.

Bus Stations Near Marlborough Sounds

Bus companies like InterCity and Nelson Coachlines operate daily services to Blenheim and Picton.

Other Options

Water taxis or chartered flights are additional options for travel to and around Marlborough Sounds. Additionally, visitors can rent cars or hire taxi services to get around the area.

How to Get Around in Marlborough Sounds

The best way to get around Marlborough Sounds is by walking, hiking, or taking a boat tour. Rental cars are available, and visitors can use taxis or rental bikes.

Where to Go Next from Marlborough Sounds: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Christchurch - A vibrant city with lots of history, museums, and galleries.
  2. Kaikoura - A charming coastal town known for Whale Watching and swimming with dolphins.
  3. Abel Tasman National Park - A beautiful park with stunning beaches, perfect for kayaking and hiking.

Conclusion

Marlborough Sounds is an extraordinary destination for those seeking a perfect blend of adventure, natural beauty, and cultural exploration. With its stunning scenery, delicious food and drink scene, and exciting outdoor activities, visitors can expect an unforgettable Marlborough Sounds experience.
