Introduction to Havelock

Nestled in the heart of Marlborough, South Island, is the picturesque town of Havelock. This small-but-vibrant community is known for its stunning natural beauty, making it a popular choice for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Havelock is an ideal destination for those who enjoy hiking and biking, as well as those who love to explore the great outdoors. The town also boasts a rich culture and history, providing visitors with plenty of opportunities to learn and explore.

List of Places to Visit in Havelock

Whether you’re an avid cyclist, a history buff, or a foodie, Havelock has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Havelock:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Edwin Fox Maritime Museum: Located on the waterfront, this museum is home to the Edwin Fox, the world’s second-oldest wooden-hulled ship. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the fascinating history of this iconic vessel.

  • The Captain Cook Monument: A towering statue of Captain James Cook, this monument pays tribute to the famous explorer’s visit to the area in the 1700s.

  • The Pelorus Mail Boat: Experience the authentic working life of New Zealand’s only water-accessed post delivery as you navigate your way around the stunning Marlborough Sounds.

Natural Attractions of Havelock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Queen Charlotte Track: This popular hiking and biking route, which stretches for 70 kilometers through the Marlborough Sounds, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Davies Bay: Located just a short drive from Havelock, Davies Bay is a secluded beach with crystal-clear water and stunning views.

  • Pelorus River: Known for its turquoise water and lush greenery, the Pelorus River is a popular spot for kayaking and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Havelock

Havelock is known for its beautiful hiking and biking routes. The Queen Charlotte Track is one of the most popular trails in the area, but there are plenty of other options for those who prefer a shorter or less demanding hike. Some of the other popular trails in Havelock include:

  • The Havelock Lookout Track: This 2.5-kilometer trail offers stunning views of Havelock and the surrounding area.

  • The Snout Track: This 8-kilometer trail takes you up to the Snout headland, where you’ll enjoy panoramic views of the Marlborough Sounds.

  • Link Pathway: Follow this popular pathway and explore the scenic coastline, native forest, and idyllic waterways.

Best Things to Do in Havelock: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Havelock has something to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Havelock:

  • Kayaking: Explore the beautiful Marlborough Sounds by sea kayak and enjoy the chance to get up close and personal with the stunning local wildlife.

  • Fishing: The waters around Havelock are home to some of the best fishing in New Zealand. Hire a charter and spend the day reeling in a big catch.

  • Wine Tasting: Marlborough is famous for its wine, and Havelock is the perfect place to indulge in a bit of wine tasting. Visit one of the local vineyards and sample some of the region’s finest wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Havelock

There’s so much to see and do in Havelock that you could easily spend a week exploring the town and its surroundings. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Havelock:

Day 1: Visit the Edwin Fox Maritime Museum and learn about the fascinating history of the world’s second-oldest wooden-hulled ship.

Day 2: Take a hike on the Queen Charlotte Track and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the Marlborough Sounds.

Day 3: Spend a relaxing day on the beach at Davies Bay.

Day 4: Visit a local vineyard and indulge in a bit of wine tasting.

Day 5: Take a kayak tour of the Marlborough Sounds and enjoy the chance to get up close and personal with the local wildlife.

Day 6: Rent a bike and explore the picturesque town of Havelock.

Day 7: Visit the Pelorus Bridge Scenic Reserve and take a refreshing dip in the Pelorus River.

Food and Drink Scene of Havelock

Havelock has a great food and drink scene, with plenty of options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green-Lipped Mussels: Havelock is known for its delicious green-lipped mussels, which are served in restaurants throughout the town.

  • Fish and Chips: Enjoy a classic kiwi meal of fish and chips while taking in the stunning views of the Marlborough Sounds.

  • Lamb: New Zealand is famous for its lamb, and Havelock is no exception. Enjoy a delicious lamb dish at one of the local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Sauvignon Blanc: Marlborough is famous for its Sauvignon Blanc, and Havelock is the perfect place to sample some of the region’s finest wines.

  • Craft Beer: Havelock is home to several craft breweries, offering a wide selection of delicious local beers.

Where to Stay in Havelock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Havelock has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Havelock:

  • Havelock Motel and Motor Lodge: This motel offers comfortable and affordable accommodation just a short walk from the waterfront.

  • Blue Moon Lodge: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Havelock and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

  • Mahakipawa Hilltop Accommodation: This peaceful retreat is located just a short drive from Havelock and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Havelock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a special souvenir to remember your trip to Havelock, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the best local products and souvenirs to bring home:

  • Green-Lipped Mussel Products: Havelock is famous for its green-lipped mussels, and you can find a wide range of mussel-related products in the local shops.

  • Wine: Marlborough is known for its delicious wine, so why not bring home a bottle or two as a souvenir?

  • Handmade Crafts: Havelock is home to several local artists and craftspeople, offering a range of handmade souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Havelock

Havelock has a rich culture and history, and visitors can learn more about the town’s heritage through its museums, historical sites, and cultural events. The town is also known for its friendly locals and laid-back atmosphere, making it a popular choice for travelers looking to experience New Zealand’s famous hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Havelock

The best time to visit Havelock is during the summer months (December-February), when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is a popular destination year-round, thanks to its beautiful natural scenery and range of outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Havelock

Havelock is a safe town, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when travelling, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Havelock

Havelock has a small medical center that can provide basic medical care for visitors. However, visitors with complex medical needs may need to travel to a larger town or city for treatment.

Getting to Havelock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Havelock is located about 25 kilometers from the town of Picton, which is the main gateway to the Marlborough Sounds. Here are some transportation options for getting to Havelock:

  • Airports Near Havelock: The nearest airport to Havelock is the Blenheim Airport, located about 35 kilometers away.

  • Bus Stations Near Havelock: There are several bus companies that operate services between Picton and Havelock.

  • Other Options: Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Picton to Havelock.

How to Get Around in Havelock

Havelock is a small town and is easily navigated on foot or by bike. There is also a local bus service that operates in and around the town.

Where to Go Next from Havelock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Havelock is located in the heart of the Marlborough Sounds and is the perfect base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Picton: Just a short drive from Havelock, Picton is the gateway to the Marlborough Sounds and is home to a range of restaurants, shops, and attractions.

  • Marlborough Wine Region: Visit the nearby Marlborough wine region and sample some of New Zealand’s finest wines.

  • Abel Tasman National Park: This beautiful national park, located about 90 kilometers from Havelock, offers stunning natural scenery and a range of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Havelock is a beautiful and vibrant town that offers something for everyone. From hiking and biking to wine tasting and cultural attractions, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this stunning part of New Zealand. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a longer trip, Havelock is the perfect destination for anyone looking to explore the natural wonders of Marlborough.
