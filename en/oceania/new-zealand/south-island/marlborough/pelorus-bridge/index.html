Introduction to Pelorus Bridge

Pelorus Bridge is a stunning location in South Island, New Zealand, where travelers can experience the beauty of the Marlborough region. The area offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions, as well as opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Pelorus Bridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pelorus Museum: Learn about the history of the Pelorus Bridge area from early Maori settlements to present-day life.
  • Edwin Fox Maritime Museum: Discover the story of this preserved ship and its journey from England to New Zealand.

Natural Attractions of Pelorus Bridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pelorus Bridge Scenic Reserve: Take a walk in the native forest to see the sprawling trees and fresh water streams.
  • Pelorus River: Go swimming, kayaking, or fishing in the stunning, clear river.
  • Mount Richmond Forest Park: Hike or mountain bike in the beautiful, mountainous park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pelorus Bridge

  • Pelorus Bridge to Havelock Track: A beautiful 36-km track one way, passing through scenic views, and interesting historic places on the way.
  • Pelorus River Track: A gentle 2-hour hike that takes visitors through the forest, over bridges, and along the river.
  • Mount Richmond Forest Park: A range of hiking and biking trails that take visitors through diverse terrains and beautiful scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Pelorus Bridge: Activities and Tours

  • Jet boat tours: Take a high-speed tour on the river.
  • Fishing tours: Head out onto Pelorus River for a fishing adventure.
  • Kayaking Tours: Book a guided kayaking trip on the river to see the sights from the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pelorus Bridge

Day 1: Pelorus Bridge and Scenic Reserve Day 2: Edwin Fox Maritime Museum and Havelock Day 3: Pelorus River Track and River Adventures Day 4: Mount Richmond Forest Park Day 5: Wine Tasting and Marlborough Sounds Day 6: Blenheim and Omaka Aviation Heritage Centre Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Pelorus Bridge

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Lipped Mussels: A delicious seafood specialty that can be found in many nearby restaurants.
  • New Zealand Lamb: A must-try dish for meat lovers, often prepared with locally-sourced herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc: A signature wine of the region known for its bright citrus flavors.
  • Craft Beer: Try some of the local brews produced by nearby breweries.

Where to Stay in Pelorus Bridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pelorus Bridge Accommodation: Affordable hostel-style accommodations nestled in the scenic reserve.
  • Pelorus Bridge Lodge: A charming lodge with an on-site restaurant and activities desk.
  • River Haven: A tranquil retreat featuring a private riverfront deck and gardens.

What to Buy in Pelorus Bridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey and Honeycomb Products: Grab a jar of local honey to take home or try some of the unique honeycomb products.
  • Artisan Crafts: Visit the markets or local galleries to pick up handcrafted wooden items, pottery, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Pelorus Bridge

The people of Marlborough have a strong connection to the land and sea. Many locals are involved in fishing and wine-making, and celebrate the bounty of the region all year round.

Best Time to Visit Pelorus Bridge

The best time to visit Pelorus Bridge is during the summer months, from December to February. This is the time for blue skies and warm weather. However, it is important to note that accommodations and activities may be busier during this season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pelorus Bridge

Do:

  • Respect the environment and take care not to damage it.
  • Take appropriate safety measures when participating in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Participate in activities that may harm the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pelorus Bridge

The Pelorus Bridge area is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take measures to avoid accidents when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, or kayaking.

Health in Pelorus Bridge

Visitors to Pelorus Bridge should be aware of the risk of sunburn and should take appropriate measures to protect their skin. It is also recommended that visitors bring plenty of insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Pelorus Bridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pelorus Bridge

  • Hokitika Airport: Distance of 100 km (approximately)
  • Nelson Airport: Distance of 60 km (approximately)

Bus Stations Near Pelorus Bridge

  • Nelson: The Bus Station - Approximately 44.2 km away, with a travel time of 40-50 minutes.
  • Havelock: The Foreshore - Approximately 16.9 km away, with a travel time of 20-25 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors to Pelorus Bridge can also reach the area by car or by organized tour.

How to Get Around in Pelorus Bridge

The best way to get around Pelorus Bridge is by car, but visitors can also take guided tours or rent bicycles to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Pelorus Bridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Marlborough Wine Region: Experience some of the world-famous wine of New Zealand just a short drive away.
  • The Marlborough Sounds: Explore the stunning Sounds, go kayaking or simply relax and enjoy the view.

Conclusion

Pelorus Bridge is an awe-inspiring experience with the perfect concoction of natural attractions, cultural significance, and fun activities. With so many activities, beautiful experiences, and scenic views to choose from, Pelorus Bridge is an ideal spot for tourists and adventure seekers.
