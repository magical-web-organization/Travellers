Omarama Travel Guide – Discovering the Hidden Gems of Mackenzie Country

Looking for a picturesque, laid-back town to spend your vacation in New Zealand? Look no further than Omarama. Nestled in the heart of Mackenzie Country, South Island, Omarama is a popular destination for travelers seeking tranquility, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures.

In this comprehensive Omarama travel guide, we’ll help you plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town. From the list of places to visit, outdoor adventures, epic hiking/biking routes to food and drinks scene, and safety tips - get ready to discover the hidden gems of Omarama.

List of Places to Visit in Omarama

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or thrill-seeker, Omarama has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit places in Omarama:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wrinkly Ram is a unique woolen clothes store and café where you can sip coffee while browsing through the art and handicraft displays.
  • The Sir Edmund Hillary Alpine Centre showcases the life and achievements of the renowned mountaineer Sir Edmund Hillary.
  • St Thomas’ Anglican Church is a 150-year-old historic church that boasts stunning architecture and serene atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Omarama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clay Cliffs are natural clay formations of ancient glacier activity that have been sculpted by the weather. It is a picturesque sight that can be explored on foot.
  • Omarama Hot Tubs is a popular spot for relaxation, featuring wooden hot tubs that provide fantastic views of the surrounding mountain range.
  • Sailplane flights are a popular adventure here, where you can have a unique experience viewing the beautiful mountains of South Island from high above.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Omarama

Omarama is famed for its hiking and biking routes, all recording the mesmerizing natural beauty, which lures trekkers, bikers, and hikers to visit this scenic place.

  • Omarama to Sailors Cutting is an epic biking route, which takes you through some of the stunning sceneries, including Lake Benmore and sailwater canal.
  • Omarama to Quailburn is another exhilarating biking route that takes you through mountain tracks, forested valleys, and rugged terrain. It is perfect for those seeking thrilling adventures.
  • Chain Hill Walkway in Omarama takes you through a gentle uphill route with stunning views of the hill valleys.

Best Things to Do in Omarama: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of exciting activities and tours to experience while sightseeing in Omarama. Here are some of the best:

  • Gliding to the Cliffs is a popular tour that takes you above the clay cliffs in a glider plane, providing stunning views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes.
  • Omarama Station Horse Trekking allows you to witness the natural beauty of the Lower Waitaki Basin through horse trekking tours that offer a stunning visual experience.
  • Omarama Clay Target Shooting provides a thrilling experience for those who love shooting and adventure sports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Omarama

If you’re looking to explore Omarama for a week-long vacation, here’s a perfect itinerary for you:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Omarama, explore the town center, and visit the Wrinkly Ram, Sir Edmund Hillary Alpine Centre, and St. Thomas’ Anglican Church.
  • Day 2: Visit the Omarama Hot Tubs and go for a sailplane flight.
  • Day 3: Take the Omarama to Quailburn biking route and experience the spectacular mountains, valleys, and rugged terrain.
  • Day 4: Visit Clay Cliffs and hike on the Chain Hill Walkway.
  • Day 5: Go for Omarama Station Horse Trekking and explore the natural beauty at the Lower Waitaki Basin.
  • Day 6: Opt for Gliding to the Cliffs to enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes.
  • Day 7: Enjoy your last day in Omarama by trying some delicious local dishes, shopping for souvenirs, and relaxing in the hot tubs.

Food and Drink Scene of Omarama

Omarama is a culinary delight for those who love to indulge in local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pies: Omarama is home to some of the best pies in New Zealand, including venison pies, pork, and blueberry pies.
  • Seafood dishes: Freshwater salmon and trout dishes are local favorites, served with a side of fresh vegetables.
  • Speight’s beer: Speight’s is a popular beer brand in New Zealand, and you’ll find it in most restaurants and bars in Omarama.

Local Beverages

  • Robinson’s Fruit Juice: A popular local beverage, made from fresh fruits, which is sure to refresh you on a hot day.
  • Central Otago Pinot Noir: This local wine is a must-have if you’re a wine lover, as it is one of the premium wines of New Zealand.

Where to Stay in Omarama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Omarama offers great accommodation options, from affordable hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some popular places to stay:

  • Omarama Top10 Holiday Park: An affordable option that offers a range of accommodation types, from campsites to cabins.
  • Ahuriri Motels: A modern motel that is convenient and centrally located, offering luxury studios and apartments.
  • Heritage Gateway Hotel: A luxurious hotel in Omarama that boasts 98 well-appointed rooms with all modern amenities.

What to Buy in Omarama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for a memento to remind you of your Omarama adventure or a gift for someone special, Omarama has plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some popular choices:

  • Possum Merino: Omarama is famous for its woolen products including possum merino gloves and hats, featuring intricate designs and elegant styles.
  • Lavender oil: Omarama is home to the Highland Lavender Farm, which produces high-quality lavender oil that can be an ideal gift for someone special.
  • Omarama Honey: The local honey is a must-try for those who love natural, healthy products. The local honey is produced from natural nectar collected in areas surrounding Omarama.

Local Culture and Customs of Omarama

Omarama has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Maori and European traditions. If you visit Omarama, ensure you show respect towards the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Maori greetings: The Maori people have their unique form of greeting, which is a handshake and a kiss on the cheek. Don’t be shy, try it out.
  • Respect religious buildings: Churches are essential for the locals, and they are often places of peace and meditation. If you visit a church, make sure to be quiet and respectful.
  • Environmental Awareness: Omarama locals take deep pride in environmental sustainability and preservation. While exploring this beautiful place, pay attention and cooperate regarding waste disposal and overall environmental awareness.

Best Time to Visit Omarama

The best time to visit Omarama depends on your interests, as the town offers different charm every season:

  • Summer (December – February): Summer is ideal for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking and outdoor sports. The average temperature ranges between 18° C – 22° C.
  • Autumn (March – May): The town is bathed in golden hues, and the climate is mostly mild and pleasant with crisp mornings and balmy afternoons, which makes it perfect for sightseeing and adventure.
  • Winter (June – August): Winter is chilly, but it is a great time to visit if you are keen on enjoying some seasonal activities, such as skiing, snowboarding or glacier tours.
  • Spring (September – November): Spring is perfect for those who love nature and blooming flowers. The alps are covered with snow, and there are new lambs and calves in the paddocks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Omarama

When traveling to Omarama, it is essential to respect the local customs, avoid hazards, and take care of your safety. Here are some things to do and don’t:

Do:

  • Obey traffic laws, seat belts and speed limits, especially when driving around Mackenzie Country.
  • Take your trash with you and dispose of it properly.
  • Respect the environment and wildlife of Omarama.

Don’t:

  • Don’t go off-trail while hiking or biking.
  • Don’t litter the natural areas and don’t bring food into wildlife areas.
  • Don’t climb fences for pictures and videos of farmland.

Safety and Health

In Omarama, safety and health are crucial issues to consider to have an enjoyable vacation. Here are some tips on how to keep yourself safe and healthy:

Safety in Omarama

  • Be aware of the changeable weather and check local conditions.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and shoes for outdoor activities like hiking and biking.
  • Don’t touch or collect natural relics because they may hold ecological significance.

Health in Omarama

  • Use sun protection cream, keep hydrated, and carry water with you during outdoor activities in the hot weather.
  • If you’re unwell, seek medical assistance immediately.
  • Take some insect repellent suitable for the area in your packing.

Getting to Omarama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Omarama is accessible from major cities and towns of New Zealand, and there are several transportation options available:

Airports Near Omarama

  • Christchurch (241 km away) is the closest international airport and has connecting flights to major cities worldwide.
  • Dunedin (207 km away) is a domestic airport with flights to Auckland and other major New Zealand destinations.

Bus Stations Near Omarama

  • Intercity operates bus services to Omarama from major destinations in New Zealand.

Other Options

  • Rental cars and campervans are the most popular mode of transport for tourists in New Zealand.
  • Self-drive on the scenic route takes you through some of the breathtaking landscapes and sights that New Zealand offers.

How to Get Around in Omarama

Discover the beauty of Omarama on foot, bike, or car as they are the best modes of traveling within the town. Renting a car or bicycle is easy to get around the town and nearby regions.

Where to Go Next from Omarama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Omarama is surrounded by stunning destinations, and some of the nearest and recommended locations to visit when in Omarama are:

  • Tekapo (80 km away): Famous for its turquoise lake and the Church of the Good Shepherd, Starry Skies Reserve, and Hot Springs.
  • Twizel (29 km away): Known for its natural beauty, including Lake Pukaki, the Clay Cliffs, and the Mount Cook National Park.
  • Otago Region (55 km away): A region famous for its wine and vineyards, the Otago Coast, and the Moeraki Boulders.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an adventure seeker or simply looking for a relaxing escape in nature, Omarama will not disappoint. With its stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, rich culture, and culinary delights, Omarama is sure to leave you with lasting memories. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to plan your perfect trip to Omarama.
