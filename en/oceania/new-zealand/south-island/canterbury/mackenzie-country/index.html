Introduction to Mackenzie Country

Mackenzie Country is a stunning destination located in the Canterbury region of South Island, New Zealand. Characterized by vast open spaces, dramatic landscapes, and charming towns, Mackenzie Country is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The region is named after a legendary sheep thief, James MacKenzie, who once roamed these lands in the mid-19th century.

List of Places to Visit in Mackenzie Country

There are many places to visit in Mackenzie Country. Here are some of the best cultural and natural attractions that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fairlie Heritage Museum - This museum showcases the history of Mackenzie Country, including the stories of early settlers and the region’s unique flora and fauna.
  • Mount John Observatory - Discover the secrets of the universe and marvel at the breathtaking views of Mackenzie Basin from this astronomical observatory.
  • Church of the Good Shepherd - This charming church is one of the most photographed landmarks in New Zealand, located on the shores of Lake Tekapo.
  • High Country Salmon Farm - Learn how salmon is farmed in New Zealand and feast on fresh seafood at this farm located in Twizel.

Natural Attractions of Mackenzie Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Tekapo - This turquoise lake is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and is a popular spot for kayaking, swimming, and picnicking.
  • Mount Cook National Park - Hike in the shadow of New Zealand’s highest mountain, Mount Cook. This national park is home to glaciers, alpine lakes, and stunning views.
  • Aoraki Dark Sky Reserve - Stargaze under some of the clearest skies in the world. This preserve is dedicated to protecting the night sky from light pollution.
  • Braemar Station - Take a scenic flight over the Southern Alps and land on a high-country sheep farm, where you can explore the landscape on horseback or by 4WD.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mackenzie Country

Mackenzie Country is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Some popular trails include:

  • Hooker Valley Track - This 10-kilometer hike leads to the stunning Hooker Glacier, offering beautiful views of Mount Cook and the surrounding alpine scenery.
  • Round the Mountain Track - This 4-day trek offers stunning views of the Mackenzie Basin and the Southern Alps.
  • Alps2Ocean Cycle Trail - This 301-kilometer trail connects Mount Cook National Park with the Pacific Ocean, passing through scenic valleys, lakes, and small towns.

Best Things to Do in Mackenzie Country: Activities and Tours

There are a plethora of things to do in Mackenzie Country. Here are some of the best:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Soar over the Mackenzie Basin and take in the stunning panoramic views from high above.
  • Farm Tours - Discover the rural lifestyle of New Zealand farmers on a tour of one of the area’s many local farms.
  • Glacier Kayaking - Paddle through the tranquil turquoise waters of Tasman Glacier terminal lake, surrounded by towering mountains and icebergs.
  • Skydiving - Experience the ultimate adrenaline rush by skydiving over stunning Mackenzie Country scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Mackenzie Country

Mackenzie Country offers a diverse range of food and drink options, from traditional New Zealand cuisine to world-class seafood. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Roast - This is New Zealand’s national dish, and for a good reason: the lamb is grass-fed and locally sourced, resulting in a rich and delicious flavor.
  • Pavlova - This traditional dessert is made from whipped egg whites, sugar, and fruit and is a staple of Kiwi cuisine. It is also a hotly debated topic between New Zealand and Australia, with both countries claiming its origin.

Local Beverages

  • Central Otago Wine - This region is known for its world-class Pinot Noir, which grows in the cool climate of the area.
  • Speights Beer - This beer is a classic Kiwi brew that is brewed in Dunedin, on the east coast of the South Island.

Where to Stay in Mackenzie Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mackenzie Country offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the best places to stay in the region:

  • Peppers Bluewater Resort - This luxurious resort offers stunning lake views and all the amenities you could desire, including a spa, pool, and on-site restaurant.
  • Tailor Made Tekapo Backpackers - This cozy hostel is located in the heart of Lake Tekapo village and offers affordable dorms and private rooms.
  • The Godley Hotel - This historic hotel in Lake Tekapo has been recently renovated and offers affordable rooms with stunning mountain views.

What to Buy in Mackenzie Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mackenzie Country offers some unique souvenirs and local products that you should consider bringing home:

  • Merino Wool Products - Merino wool is renowned for its softness and warmth, and Mackenzie Country is one of the best places to find high-quality merino wool products.
  • Local Honey - Mackenzie Country boasts some of the best honey producers in New Zealand, with a variety of different types of honey available to taste and purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Mackenzie Country

Mackenzie Country has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the Maori and European settlers who have lived in the region. The rural lifestyle and farming traditions are also a significant part of the Mackenzie Country culture.

Best Time to Visit Mackenzie Country

The best time to visit Mackenzie Country is from November to February, which is the New Zealand summer. The weather is warm and sunny, and the landscapes are at their greenest and lushest during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mackenzie Country

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mackenzie Country:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and pick up your litter.
  • Take appropriate clothing and gear for the weather, as it can change quickly.
  • Be mindful of the rural lifestyle and farming traditions of the region.

Don’t:

  • Go off-trail while hiking and biking.
  • Feed the wildlife.
  • Ignore warning signs and potential dangers.

Safety and Health

Mackenzie Country is a relatively safe and healthy destination. However, it is essential to take precautions when exploring the outdoors and remote areas of the region.

Safety in Mackenzie Country

  • Always carry a map and emergency supplies when hiking or biking.
  • Be aware of changes in the weather and keep an eye on weather forecasts.
  • Be careful of slippery rocks and steep cliffs near waterfalls and glaciers.

Health in Mackenzie Country

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Drink plenty of water and avoid dehydration.
  • Be mindful of food hygiene and wash your hands regularly.

Getting to Mackenzie Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Mackenzie Country is easy with several transportation options available. Here’s what you need to know before you go:

Airports Near Mackenzie Country

The nearest airports to Mackenzie Country are in Christchurch, which is approximately a 3-hour drive from the region.

Bus Stations Near Mackenzie Country

Several bus companies run services to Mackenzie Country from major cities like Christchurch and Queenstown.

Other Options

Some tourists choose to rent a car or campervan to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Mackenzie Country

The best way to get around Mackenzie Country is by car or bike. Many of the attractions and destinations are spread out, but the roads are well-maintained and easy to navigate.

Where to Go Next from Mackenzie Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mackenzie Country is well-positioned for exploring the rest of the South Island. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Queenstown - This bustling tourist town is three hours drive away and offers endless activities, from bungee jumping to skiing.
  • Franz Josef Glacier - This stunning glacier is located on the west coast of the South Island and is a popular destination for day trips and overnight stays.
  • Kaikoura - This coastal town is known for its world-class seafood, whale watching, and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Mackenzie Country is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to New Zealand. With stunning natural beauty, a rich cultural heritage, and a wide range of activities and experiences, Mackenzie Country is the ideal location for anyone who loves the outdoors and adventure. So what are you waiting for? Book your trip to Mackenzie Country today and get ready to experience the trip of a lifetime!
