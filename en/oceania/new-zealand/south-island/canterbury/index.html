Introduction to Canterbury

Canterbury is a region of South Island in New Zealand and is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. With the Southern Alps to the west and the Pacific Ocean to the east, Canterbury offers a plethora of activities for adventurous travelers and outdoor enthusiasts. Its cities and towns are dotted with charming cafes, restaurants, and shops, and its museums and historical sites offer a glimpse into the country’s fascinating history.

List of Places to Visit in Canterbury

Here are some of the must-visit places in Canterbury:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Canterbury Museum - A fascinating museum that showcases the history and culture of Canterbury, with exhibits ranging from Maori artifacts to Antarctic expeditions.

  2. Ko Tane - A cultural experience that provides an insight into the Maori way of life, including traditional Maori performances and a guided tour of a Maori village.

  3. Air Force Museum of New Zealand - A unique museum that celebrates the country’s aviation history, with displays of historic aircraft, uniforms, and other memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Canterbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Hutt - A popular ski resort in the Southern Alps with stunning views and a range of slopes for skiers and snowboarders of all levels.

  2. Arthur’s Pass National Park - A UNESCO World Heritage site that offers breathtaking views of the mountains and a range of hiking trails and outdoor activities.

  3. Banks Peninsula - A scenic area near Christchurch known for its rugged coastline, charming villages, and diverse wildlife, including dolphins, penguins, and seals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canterbury

Canterbury is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a network of trails that wind through picturesque mountains, forests, and valleys. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Hump Ridge Track - A challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views of the Fiordland region and takes you past historic sites and waterfalls.

  2. Queen Charlotte Track - A popular trail that winds through lush forests and along breathtaking coastline, with options for hiking or biking.

  3. Alps 2 Ocean Cycle Trail - A 301-kilometer ride that takes you from the Southern Alps to the Pacific Ocean, past picturesque lakes, rivers, and charming towns.

Best Things to Do in Canterbury: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or a culture vulture, Canterbury has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  1. Skydiving - Take a leap of faith and experience the rush of freefalling over the stunning landscapes of Canterbury.

  2. Whale Watching - Get up close and personal with some of the world’s largest creatures on a whale watching tour off the coast of Kaikoura.

  3. Christchurch Gondola - Take a scenic ride up to the top of the Port Hills for stunning panoramic views of the city and the Southern Alps.

Food and Drink Scene of Canterbury

When it comes to food and drink, Canterbury is a foodie’s paradise. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Paua Fritters - A local delicacy of battered and deep-fried paua, a type of shellfish unique to New Zealand.

  2. Whitebait Fritters - A popular dish made with the tiny fish species found in the region’s rivers and streams.

  3. Lamb - New Zealand is famous for its lamb, and Canterbury produces some of the best in the country.

Local Beverages

  1. Wine - Canterbury is home to several vineyards and wineries that produce some of New Zealand’s finest wines.

  2. Beer - The region has several craft breweries that produce a range of unique and delicious beers, from lagers to IPAs.

Where to Stay in Canterbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Canterbury has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some options:

  1. Heritage Christchurch - A luxurious hotel housed in a restored historic building in the heart of Christchurch.

  2. YHA Hostels - Affordable and comfortable hostels located throughout the region, perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

  3. Farmstays - Experience rural life in Canterbury by staying at one of the many farmstays in the region.

What to Buy in Canterbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider bringing back home:

  1. Possum Merino Clothing - Made from the fur of possums that are considered pests in New Zealand, possum merino clothing is soft, warm, and eco-friendly.

  2. Manuka Honey - Produced from the nectar of manuka trees that grow only in New Zealand, manuka honey is renowned for its unique flavor and health benefits.

  3. Jade (Pounamu) - A highly prized stone in Maori culture, jade is found in several regions of New Zealand, including Canterbury.

Local Culture and Customs of Canterbury

The Maori people have a rich cultural heritage in Canterbury, and their traditions and customs are celebrated throughout the region. It is important to be respectful of their customs and to seek permission before entering sacred sites or participating in cultural activities.

Best Time to Visit Canterbury

The best time to visit Canterbury depends on your interests and the activities you want to do. For skiing and snow sports, the winter months (June-August) are the best time to visit. For outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, the summer months (December-February) are ideal. Autumn (March-May) and spring (September-November) are also great times to visit, with milder temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canterbury

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Canterbury:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, and seek permission before entering sacred sites or participating in cultural activities.

  • Stay on designated trails and respect the natural environment.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • litter or damage the environment.

  • Ignore signs or warnings, especially in areas where there may be wildlife or difficult terrain.

  • Drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canterbury

Canterbury is generally a safe region for travelers, but it is always important to take basic precautions, such as locking your car and securing your valuables. It is also important to be aware of the risks when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or skiing, and to be prepared with appropriate gear and clothing.

Health in Canterbury

New Zealand has a high standard of medical care, and Canterbury has several hospitals and medical centers. Travelers should ensure that they have adequate insurance coverage before traveling to New Zealand. It is also important to be aware of the risks of sunburn and dehydration, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Canterbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Canterbury

The main airport serving Canterbury is Christchurch International Airport, which is located in the city of Christchurch and is served by several international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Canterbury

Canterbury is served by several local and national bus companies, with stops in major cities and towns throughout the region.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, Canterbury is easily accessible by car and has a network of well-maintained highways and roads.

How to Get Around in Canterbury

Canterbury is a large region, and the best way to get around depends on your itinerary and the activities you want to do. Renting a car is a popular option, as it gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace. Alternatively, there are several local and national bus companies that serve the region, as well as private tour companies that offer guided tours. Bikes and hiking are also great ways to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Canterbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Canterbury is an excellent starting point for exploring other parts of New Zealand. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Lake Tekapo - A picturesque lake surrounded by mountains, with hot springs and stunning starry skies.

  2. Aoraki/Mount Cook - New Zealand’s tallest mountain and a stunning natural wonder, located in the neighboring region of Canterbury.

  3. Christchurch - Known as the “Garden City,” Christchurch is the largest city in the South Island and is famous for its gardens, parks, and historic buildings.

Conclusion

Canterbury is a region of breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, with a range of activities and experiences that cater to all kinds of travelers. From adrenaline-fueled outdoor adventures to fascinating museums and historical sites, there is something for everyone in Canterbury. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this stunning corner of New Zealand.
