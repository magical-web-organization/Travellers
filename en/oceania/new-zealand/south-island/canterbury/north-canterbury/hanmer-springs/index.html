Introduction to Hanmer Springs

Located in the beautiful region of North Canterbury, Hanmer Springs is a must-visit destination in New Zealand’s South Island. Famous for its picturesque natural landscapes and hot spring spa resorts, Hanmer Springs has something for every traveler. Whether it’s outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, or simply relaxation, Hanmer Springs delivers.

List of Places to Visit in Hanmer Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amuri Museum of Workers’ Housing showcases the lives of early workers in the Amuri Basin, with original houses and artifacts from the late 1800s to early 1900s.
  • Hanmer Springs Heritage Forest features the history of forestry in the area with interpretive signs along the walking trails.
  • St. James Chapel is a beautiful historic stone church dating back to the 1920s.

Natural Attractions of Hanmer Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hanmer Springs Thermal Pools and Spa is the main attraction with a wide variety of pools, from hot and relaxing to cool and invigorating.
  • Conical Hill Track is a popular hiking trail with stunning views of Hanmer Springs and the surrounding mountains.
  • Hanmer Springs Ski Area is a perfect winter ski destination, with a variety of trails for skiers and snowboarders of all levels.
  • Waiau River offers a thrilling jet boat ride through the awe-inspiring Waiau Gorge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanmer Springs

  • Conical Hill Track mentioned above is a great option for hikers, with a well-maintained trail and beautiful views of the town.
  • Hanmer Forest Park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels, from easy walks to technical mountain bike tracks.
  • St. James Walkway is a multi-day backcountry trail for experienced hikers, with breathtaking views of the Southern Alps and vast valleys.

Best Things to Do in Hanmer Springs: Activities and Tours

  • Adrenaline Forest is a high ropes course with various challenging levels to suit all abilities.
  • Quad Adventures is a thrilling way to explore the backcountry, with guided tours for both beginners and experienced riders.
  • Hanmer Springs Animal Park is a family-friendly attraction with plenty of animals to feed and pet, including alpacas, sheep, and llamas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hanmer Springs

Food and Drink Scene of Hanmer Springs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb rack served with roasted vegetables and mint sauce, a classic New Zealand dish.
  • Whitebait fritters made from small freshwater fish mixed with eggs and flour, pan-fried to perfection.
  • Manuka smoked salmon served with a creamy sauce and a slice of lemon, a delicious Kiwi twist on a favorite seafood dish.

Local Beverages

  • Sauvignon Blanc is a popular white wine produced in the nearby Marlborough region, with vibrant acidity and notes of passionfruit and citrus.
  • Speight’s Ale is a classic New Zealand beer with a rich malt flavor and a smooth finish.
  • Apple cider from local farmers is a refreshing and crisp option, perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Hanmer Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Heritage Hanmer Springs is a luxury hotel with stunning views of the Southern Alps and a range of room options to suit any traveler.
  • YHA Hanmer Springs is a budget-friendly hostel with shared and private rooms and a fully-equipped kitchen for self-catering.
  • The St James is a charming guesthouse with original decor and a cozy atmosphere, just a short walk from the town center.

What to Buy in Hanmer Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Greenstone jewelry is a beautiful and valuable souvenir, hand-carved from the precious Pounamu stone by local craftsmen.
  • Manuka honey is a unique and delicious honey produced from the Manuka tree, famous for its medicinal properties.
  • Local wines from the nearby Waipara Valley are a great option for wine lovers, with a variety of reds and whites to choose from.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanmer Springs

  • New Zealand has a strong Maori heritage and culture, and visitors should respect and appreciate the traditions and customs of the local people.
  • Hanmer Springs is a small town with a tight-knit community, and visitors should be friendly and courteous to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Hanmer Springs

The best time to visit Hanmer Springs is from December to February, during the summer months when temperatures are warm and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the region’s natural beauty is on display year-round, and winter is a wonderful time to visit for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanmer Springs

Do:

  • Bring comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for outdoor activities.
  • Respect the natural environment and take care not to damage or litter in the parks and hiking trails.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages, and support the local economy by shopping for souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the hiking trails and other outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the Maori culture or be insensitive to its traditions.
  • Leave your garbage or litter behind in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanmer Springs

Hanmer Springs is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take necessary precautions and be aware of the following:

  • Take care when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and skiing, and follow safety guidelines.
  • Take note of weather warnings, particularly during winter months, and be prepared for changing conditions.

Health in Hanmer Springs

There are no major health concerns in Hanmer Springs, but visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Always drink bottled water to avoid possible stomach upsets.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn during outdoor activities.

Getting to Hanmer Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hanmer Springs

The closest airport to Hanmer Springs is in Christchurch, approximately a 90-minutes drive south of the town.

Bus Stations Near Hanmer Springs

The local bus company is Hanmer Springs Connection, which provides regular services to and from Christchurch and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

The most convenient way to get around Hanmer Springs is by rental car, especially for exploring the surrounding natural attractions and hiking trails.

How to Get Around in Hanmer Springs

Walking is the best way to get around the town center and enjoy the local attractions. Rental bikes are also available for more extensive exploration of the nearby parks and forests.

Where to Go Next from Hanmer Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kaikoura is an easy day trip from Hanmer Springs, with its stunning coastline, whale watching tours, and delicious seafood restaurants.
  • Nelson Lakes National Park offers breathtaking scenery, hiking trails, and water activities such as kayaking and fishing.
  • Christchurch is the largest city in the South Island, with a thriving arts and culture scene and a range of tourist attractions, including the famous Christchurch Botanic Gardens.

Conclusion

Hanmer Springs is a perfect destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone looking for a relaxing holiday in a beautiful natural setting. With a variety of activities, places to stay, and local culture to explore, Hanmer Springs is an excellent choice for your next adventure in New Zealand’s stunning South Island.
