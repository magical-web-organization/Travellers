Introduction to Fairlie

Nestled in Mid and South Canterbury in the South Island of New Zealand, Fairlie is a charming town with a thriving tourism industry. Known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink scene, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Fairlie.

List of Places to Visit in Fairlie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the top cultural attractions in Fairlie is the Fairlie Heritage Museum, which is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum showcases the town’s rich cultural heritage, including the stories of early settlers, the establishment of the railway and telegraph, and the development of agriculture in the region. Another notable historical site is the Fairlie Cemetery, which dates back to the 1860s and features the gravesites of early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Fairlie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fairlie is surrounded by awe-inspiring natural beauty and outdoor adventures suitable for everyone from novice to expert-level hikers. Tekapo Springs is an ideal place to find relaxation after a long outdoor day with family-friendly hot pools, sauna and ice skating facilities, and thrilling snow tubing. The Mackenzie Basin is another must-see attraction, where visitors can hike or bike through the stunning landscapes of glacial lakes, snow-capped mountains, and alpine flowers. The scenic drives from Mackenzie Basin to Omarama offer breathtaking views of the region’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fairlie

The beautiful landscape, various terrains and staggering view of Fairlie makes it a perfect destination for hikers and bikers. Some must-see hiking/biking routes are the Fairlie Heath Walkway, Godley Peaks Track, and The Grand Traverse. Each of these routes covers different aspects of Fairlie’s natural beauty and offers the opportunity to get to know the town first-hand.

Best Things to Do in Fairlie: Activities and Tours

Fairlie’s rich natural resources have no limit to the various activities and adventure tours available for visitors. The Astro Cafe at Mount John Observatory is an ideal place for stargazers where they can stargaze up close, using the observatory’s state-of-the-art telescopes. Other must-see activities include the Lord of the Rings tours, helicopter tours over the glaciers, calming hot pool experiences, and horse trekking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fairlie

Day 1: Visit the Fairlie Heritage Museum and the Fairlie Cemetery.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking around Fairlie’s natural attractions, such as Godley Peaks Track.

Day 3: Take a Lord of the Rings tour and visit Mackenzie Basin to experience the breathtaking alpine landscapes.

Day 4: Indulge in the local food and drink scenes, with a focus on the Cheese Shop, where visitors can sample some of the finest cheese in the region.

Day 5: Join a hot pool experience after a day of skiing or snowshoeing

Day 6: Take a helicopter tour over the glaciers or go on a horse trekking adventure.

Day 7: Return home, taking in all the great experiences that Fairlie has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Fairlie

Must-Try Dishes

Fairlie is known for its landmark dishes and delicacies such as the natural ice-cream made from locally grown ingredients of  Hokey Pokey, Rakautara Raspberry, and Strawberry Rhubarb. Visitors should take the opportunity to sample these delicacies at the Fairlie Bakehouse Café’s fine dining and delicious food. It’s also a must-try for visitors to sample the town’s incredible selection of cheese at the Cheese Shop, which boasts an impressive range of gourmet cheeses.

Local Beverages

One of the excellent local beverages to try when in Fairlie is Pinot Noir, which is a local wine variety produced from grapes grown in the region. Fairlie is also home to the Fairlie Bakehouse Brewery, which produces a variety of local beers crafted with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Fairlie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fairlie is home to an array of charming accommodations to suit every budget and taste. The Mackenzie Country Inn is a great place for budget-conscious travelers, while the Fairlie Lodge Bed and Breakfast provides visitors with a more personal touch. Holiday parks are another option in Fairlie, with the Aspen Grove and Fairlie Holiday Park providing exceptional camping and caravan/motorhome parking facilities.

What to Buy in Fairlie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Fairlie can find a wide range of souvenirs and local products to take home as a piece of Fairlie’s memory. Some of the best products to buy include locally made handcrafts, jewelry, artisanal cheeses, and unique New Zealand wines. It’s also worth considering purchasing natural artisan crafts made by the local community.

Local Culture and Customs of Fairlie

The town’s rich history and cultural heritage are still evident in many of the town’s customs, festivals, and beliefs. Farming has been at the heart of Fairlie’s economy, and the annual A&P Show is an excellent example of this local enthusiasm. Visitors are recommended to check for updates on local dates of events in the area, where they can immerse themselves in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Fairlie

The best time to visit Fairlie is in the summer months, between December and February, when the weather is mild and most outdoor activities are available. During the winter months, Fairlie is a popular skiing destination, with high visitor volumes from July to September each year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fairlie

Do:

  • Engage with locals - Fairlie is known to be friendly and hospitable.
  • Leave the natural environment as pristine as visitors find it.
  • Dress in layers to be prepared for any weather.

Don’t:

  • Litter, carve graffiti, or damage native flora and fauna.
  • Disturb the natural habitats of animals and birds.
  • Drive recklessly or over the speed limit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fairlie

Fairlie is virtually crime-free, and a peaceful place to visit. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions when traveling and to be aware of weather and traffic conditions when hiking outdoors.

Health in Fairlie

New Zealand is known internationally for its pure, clean water and stable environment. Visitors will find high standards of health and hygiene to be the norm in Fairlie. Visitors should keep to sunscreen/a hat during sunny days, as it’s easy to get sunburned in New Zealand.

Getting to Fairlie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fairlie is easily accessible by air, train, and bus, as well as by car or camper van.

Airports Near Fairlie

The nearest airport to Fairlie is Christchurch International Airport, with numerous daily domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Fairlie

Intercity and Kaikoura Shuttles both have bus services stopping in Fairlie. The bus timetables are available online for visitors to plan their journeys.

Other Options

Car rental and camper van hire provide another option for visitors looking to travel on their schedule, whereby they can discover the surrounding towns and regions at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Fairlie

The best way to get around Fairlie is on foot, as most of the town is compact and easy to navigate. Visitors can also hire bicycles or scooters, but, hiking and biking are the most popular options. Taxis are available, and car rental/camper van offers flexible travel as visitors can escape the town and wander in the surrounding towns.

Where to Go Next from Fairlie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous attractions and sightseeing opportunities within a few hours’ drive of Fairlie. Tekapo has stunning alpine landscapes, night skies, and Lake Tekapo (just 30 minutes away). Christchurch, the South Island’s largest city, is a few hours away on the east coast. Porters Pass (the intersection of SH73 and SH77) provides an excellent opportunity to peer into the Castle Hill Basin.

Conclusion

Fairlie is an ideal destination for travelers looking to enjoy nature’s beauty and outdoor adventures, explore the local culture and food scene, and relax in warm hospitality. By following this comprehensive tour guide, visitors will have an unforgettable first-hand experience of what Fairlie has to offer. So, plan your visit and join us on a journey through one of New Zealand’s most charming towns.
