Introduction to Methven

Methven is a small town nestled in the heart of the Canterbury region in New Zealand. It is located in the Mid and South Canterbury and is a popular destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers. The town is situated on the foot of Mount Hutt and features some of the most stunning landscapes in the country.

Methven is known for its picturesque surroundings, ski resorts, and outdoor adventures, but it also has a rich cultural history and a vibrant food and drinks scene. This guide will give you everything you need to know to have a memorable trip to Methven.

List of Places to Visit in Methven

Methven is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit places in Methven:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Methven Heritage Centre - A museum located in the heart of the town, showcasing the rich cultural history of Methven and the Canterbury region.
  • St Patrick’s Church - A beautiful church located in the town center, built-in 1890.

Natural Attractions of Methven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hutt Ski Resort - A popular ski resort located on the slopes of Mount Hutt, offering world-class skiing and snowboarding.
  • Rakaia Gorge - A scenic gorge located on the Rakaia River, popular for jet boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • Lake Coleridge - A beautiful lake offering stunning views, fishing opportunities, and hiking trails.
  • Mount Somers Conservation Area - A nature reserve offering hiking and biking trails, bird watching, and stunning views of the mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Methven

Methven is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails traversing through some of the most picturesque landscapes in the country. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Methven:

  • Mount Hutt Summit Track - A challenging hike to the summit of Mount Hutt, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Rakaia Gorge Walkway - A moderate hike through the scenic Rakaia Gorge, offering stunning views of the river and the surrounding mountains.
  • Methven Walkway - A flat and easy walk that takes you through the town, past the Methven Heritage Center, and along the river.

Best Things to Do in Methven: Activities and Tours

Methven offers an array of adventures and activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Methven:

  • Skiing and Snowboarding - Methven is home to the popular Mount Hutt Ski Resort, offering world-class skiing and snowboarding.
  • Jet Boating and Fishing - Rakaia Gorge is a popular destination for jet boating, fishing, and scenic tours.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides - Take a hot-air balloon ride over the stunning landscapes of Methven.
  • Horse Riding - Explore the Methven countryside on horseback with guided tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Methven

Methven offers an exciting food and drinks scene, with an array of cafes, restaurants, and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meat Pie - A classic New Zealand savory pie, filled with minced meat and vegetables.
  • Lamb Ribs - Slow-cooked lamb ribs, marinated in herbs and spices.
  • Pavlova - A dessert made of meringue and topped with whipped cream and fresh fruits.

Local Beverages

  • Sauvignon Blanc - A popular white wine produced in the Marlborough region of New Zealand.
  • Craft Beer - Methven is home to some excellent craft breweries, with the best-known being Brown Pub Brewery.

Where to Stay in Methven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Methven has a range of accommodations, catering to all budgets and needs. Here are some options:

  • Hotel: Methven Resort - A four-star hotel in the heart of Methven, offering excellent amenities and services.
  • Hostel: Ski Time Lodge - A budget-friendly hostel located near Mount Hutt Ski Resort.
  • Guesthouse: Abisko Lodge - A boutique guesthouse offering stunning views of Mount Hutt.

What to Buy in Methven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Methven offers an array of local products and souvenirs that make for perfect gifts and souvenirs. Here are some options:

  • Merino Wool - New Zealand produces some of the best-quality merino wool in the world, and Methven is an excellent place to buy it.
  • Manuka Honey - A health honey produced in New Zealand, known for its antibacterial properties.
  • Possum Fur - Possums are considered pests in New Zealand, and their fur is used to make warm and soft clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Methven

Methven has a rich cultural history, with the Maori people being the first settlers in the region. The town also has a strong farming culture, with sheep and dairy farming being the primary sources of livelihood. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Methven

The best time to visit Methven depends on your preferences and interests. Winter is best for skiing and snowboarding, while summer is perfect for outdoor adventures and scenic tours. March to May and September to November are shoulder seasons, offering mild temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Methven

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry warm clothing, especially if you are visiting during the winter months.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Leave no trace while hiking and camping.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the Maori culture.
  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in Methven

Methven is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors are advised to take some precautions, including:

  • Carry a first-aid kit and medication.
  • Always wear a helmet while biking or skiing.
  • Practice safe hiking and camping practices.
  • Be wary of steep slopes and unstable terrain while hiking.

Health in Methven

Methven has excellent healthcare facilities, with a hospital and several clinics in the town. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Methven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Methven is easily accessible via road and air. Here are some options:

Airports Near Methven

  • Christchurch International Airport - The nearest international airport to Methven, located about 90 minutes’ drive from the town.
  • Ashburton Aerodrome - A small airport located about 45 minutes’ drive from Methven, offering domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Methven

  • Intercity Bus Station - A bus station located in the center of Methven, offering daily services to Christchurch and other towns in the region.

Other Options:

  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car from Christchurch International Airport or in Methven town itself.

How to Get Around in Methven

Methven is a small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi for destinations further away.

Where to Go Next from Methven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Methven is an excellent base for exploring the Canterbury region of New Zealand. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Christchurch - The largest city in the region, located about 90 minutes’ drive from Methven.
  • Tekapo - A stunning lake and alpine village, located about 2 hours’ drive from Methven.
  • Arthur’s Pass National Park - A nature reserve offering hiking trails and stunning views, located about 2.5 hours’ drive from Methven.

Conclusion

Methven is an excellent town to experience the natural beauty and cultural richness of New Zealand. Whether you are into skiing or snowboarding, hiking or biking, or just want to relax and enjoy the local food and drinks scene, Methven has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you are sure to have an unforgettable trip to this lovely town.
