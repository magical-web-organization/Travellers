Introduction to Milton

Nestled in the heart of Otago region in South Island of New Zealand, Milton is a charming small town that is often overlooked by travellers. However, this lesser-known destination offers a plethora of natural and cultural attractions, making it a perfect spot for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and curious visitors.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a deep dive into the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, cultural customs to learn, and everything you need to know to plan your trip to Milton.

List of Places to Visit in Milton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Milton Heritage Precinct – a beautifully restored Victorian-era streetscape that includes the museum, courthouse, and post office buildings.
  • Tokomairiro Presbyterian Church – a historic church built in 1860s with stained-glass windows and intricate carpentry.
  • Waihola Presbyterian Church – another historic church with beautiful architecture and views of the lake.

Natural Attractions of Milton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Waihola – a stunning lake surrounded by rolling hills, perfect for picnics, swimming, and kayaking.
  • Silverstream Reserve – a vast reserve with walking tracks, picnic areas, and a playground in a natural setting.
  • Hinahina Lookout – a lookout point that offers breathtaking views of the Tokomairiro Plains and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milton

  • Silverstream Walkway – a 4 km track that winds through the Silverstream Reserve, offering views of the stream and natural flora and fauna.
  • Hinahina Walkway – a 2.5 km track that leads to the Hinahina Lookout, passing through native bush and grasslands.
  • Lake Waihola Walkway – a 2.8 km track that follows the shoreline of Lake Waihola and offers panoramic views of the lake and surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Milton: Activities and Tours

  • Horse trekking – explore the countryside on horseback and soak in the stunning scenery.
  • Fishing – indulge in fishing at Lake Waihola and catch some rainbow trout.
  • Scenic flights – take a flight over Otago region and see the breathtaking landscape from the air.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milton

Food and Drink Scene of Milton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Whitebait Fritters – a local delicacy made with New Zealand’s native tiny fish, whitebait.
  • Green-Lipped Mussels – the fresh and sweet mussels are caught from the nearby sea and cooked in various delicious ways.
  • Braised lamb shank – tender lamb shank slow-cooked to perfection, and an essential part of New Zealand cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Wine – Otago region is famous for its award-winning pinot noir and other wine varieties.
  • Beer – numerous local breweries offer quality craft beers that are sure to quench your thirst.
  • Coffee – New Zealand is known for its exceptional coffee, and Milton has some great cafes that serve brewed-to-perfection coffee.

Where to Stay in Milton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Highwayman Motel – a top-rated motel with self-contained units, a pool, spa, and stunning views of the countryside.
  • The Muddy Creek Cafe and Accommodation – a cozy and affordable accommodation option with an on-site cafe, perfect for budget travellers.
  • Milton Bed & Breakfast – a charming B&B with lovely hosts, delicious breakfast, and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Milton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kiwiana souvenirs – buy “kiwi” shaped key chains, t-shirts, and other souvenirs to remember your trip to New Zealand.
  • Greenstone and Pounamu – a type of precious jade stone that is found in New Zealand, and is often carved into intricate designs and jewellery.

Local Culture and Customs of Milton

  • Marae – a sacred and central place in Maori culture where gatherings, ceremonies, and cultural events take place.
  • Te Reo Maori – the native language of New Zealand, and an integral part of the country’s culture and identity.
  • Haka – a traditional Maori dance that is often performed to showcase strength, power, and emotion.

Best Time to Visit Milton

Milton experiences mild temperatures throughout the year, making it a perfect destination to visit all year round. However, the best time to visit is during the summer months (December-February), when the weather is sunny and warm, and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milton

Do:

  • Respect the local Maori culture and traditions.
  • Take your garbage with you and keep the environment clean.
  • Try the local food and wine, as it’s delicious.

Don’t:

  • Ignore warning signs when swimming or exploring outdoors.
  • Litter on the streets or public places.
  • Ignore locals’ advice, as they know the best spots to visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milton

  • Milton is generally a safe place to visit.
  • However, always be cautious while swimming in natural water bodies and follow the safety guidelines.

Health in Milton

  • Milton has several medical facilities that are well-equipped.
  • It’s recommended to get travel insurance before visiting Milton.

Getting to Milton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Milton

  • Dunedin International Airport – about 50 km from Milton.

Bus Stations Near Milton

  • Invercargill Bus Services – provide daily bus services from Invercargill to Milton.
  • Ritchies Transport – offer coach services connecting Milton to other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Driving – it’s recommended to rent a car or drive your own car while visiting Milton, as it’s the most convenient way to explore the surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Milton

  • Walking – most of the attractions in Milton are within walking distance.
  • Cycling – hire a bike and explore the town and surrounding countryside on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Milton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dunedin – a bustling university town that is only a short drive from Milton, known for its Victorian architecture, cafes, and art galleries.
  • Catlins Coast – a scenic coastal drive that offers stunning views of rugged cliffs, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  • Queenstown – a picturesque alpine resort town that offers endless adventure and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Milton may be a small town, but it has a lot to offer for travel enthusiasts of all interests and budgets. From natural attractions and adventure activities to cultural experiences and delicious cuisine, Milton should definitely be on your New Zealand bucket list. Plan your trip accordingly, follow the local customs and safety guidelines, and immerse yourself in the beauty and charm of Milton.
