Introduction to Mapua

Mapua is a charming village located in Nelson Bays, South Island, New Zealand. The village is situated on the Waimea Estuary, offering stunning views of the water, boats, and nearby hills. Mapua is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, thanks to its laid-back atmosphere, beautiful surroundings, and variety of activities.

List of Places to Visit in Mapua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mapua has a rich history that is reflected in its museums and historical places. Some of the top cultural attractions to visit in Mapua include:

  • Gardens of the World: A peaceful garden with a collection of plants from all over the world, including Japanese maples, South African proteas, and Australian eucalyptus trees.
  • Mapua Museum: The museum offers a fascinating insight into the history of Mapua, Nelson Bays, and wider New Zealand, featuring exhibits on Maori culture, early settlers, and local industries.
  • Bird’s Eye View: This art gallery features works from local artists, as well as a large collection of vintage maps and prints.

Natural Attractions of Mapua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mapua is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top natural attractions in Mapua include:

  • Abel Tasman National Park: This nearby national park is famous for its golden beaches, clear waters, and native forests. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities here, including hiking, kayaking, and wildlife spotting.
  • Kaiteriteri Beach: Located just a short drive from Mapua, this beautiful beach is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Waimea Inlet: A picturesque inlet where visitors can go for a scenic walk, bike ride, or take a boat tour to see the area’s diverse birdlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mapua

Mapua is a fantastic destination for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails of varying difficulty levels to choose from. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Mapua include:

  • Great Taste Trail: This 175-kilometer trail connects Nelson to Mapua, Richmond, and Motueka, passing through vineyards, orchards, beaches, and charming villages along the way.
  • Dellside Reserve Track: This easy hike takes visitors on a loop through a beautiful reserve with picnic areas, playgrounds, and stunning views of the Richmond Ranges.
  • Dun Mountain Trail: A challenging 35-kilometer trail that offers panoramic views of the Nelson region, including the Tasman Bay, Richmond Range, and Abel Tasman National Park.

Best Things to Do in Mapua: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Mapua offers a variety of activities and tours to suit different interests and preferences. Some of the top things to do in Mapua include:

  • Wine Tasting: Mapua is home to several excellent wineries, including the award-winning Neudorf Vineyards, which offers tastings and tours.
  • Farmers Market: The weekly Mapua Farmers Market is a great place to sample local produce, handicrafts, and more.
  • Boat Tour: Visitors can take a scenic boat tour of the Waimea Inlet and the Abel Tasman Coastline, where they can spot dolphins, seals, and native birds.
  • Shopping and Dining: Mapua has a great selection of boutiques, galleries, and restaurants, including the famous Jellyfish Cafe, which serves up delicious seafood dishes and coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mapua

To make the most of your time in Mapua, we recommend this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Mapua Wharf, visit Gardens of the World, and have lunch at the Smokehouse Cafe.
Day 2 Hike or bike the Great Taste Trail and enjoy a wine tasting at a local vineyard.
Day 3 Take a boat tour of the Waimea Inlet and enjoy a seafood dinner at Jellyfish Cafe.
Day 4 Visit the Mapua Museum and have lunch at the charming Boatshed Cafe.
Day 5 Go swimming at Kaiteriteri Beach and have a picnic lunch at Nelson Lakes National Park.
Day 6 Bike the Dun Mountain Trail and have a wine tasting at Neudorf Vineyards.
Day 7 Shop at the Mapua Farmers Market and have a farewell lunch at Rimu Wine Bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Mapua

Mapua has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a variety of cafes, restaurants, and wineries to choose from. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Mapua include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nelson Bay Greenshell Mussels: A regional specialty, these mussels are large and sweet, and can be prepared in a variety of ways, including steamed, fried, or baked.
  • Crayfish: Another local seafood delicacy, crayfish is best enjoyed grilled or in a chowder.
  • Venison: Mapua is located in a region famous for its deer farming, so trying venison is a must. Venison dishes in Mapua are often served with local fruits and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Nelson region is known for its excellent wines, particularly Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, and Pinot Noir. Visitors can sample wines from a variety of vineyards in and around Mapua.
  • Beer: Nelson is home to numerous craft breweries, including the nationally renowned Emerson’s Brewery and Garage Project. Visitors can enjoy a variety of unique and flavorful brews in Mapua’s many pubs and bars.

Where to Stay in Mapua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mapua offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Mapua include:

  • Mapua Chalets: Located just a short walk from the wharf, these cozy chalets offer stunning views of the Waimea Inlet and all the comforts of home.
  • The Appleby House and Rabbit Island Huts: These rustic huts and guestrooms are situated on an organic farm near Rabbit Island, a short drive from Mapua. They offer guests a chance to experience farm life and enjoy the great outdoors.
  • Nautilus Lodge: This modern, stylish lodge is located in nearby Motueka, offering guests easy access to both Mapua and Abel Tasman National Park.

What to Buy in Mapua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mapua has a variety of unique and locally made products that make great souvenirs or gifts. Some of the top things to buy in Mapua include:

  • Art and Crafts: Mapua has many galleries and boutiques where visitors can purchase locally made art and handicrafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Local Food and Produce: Visitors can purchase fresh seafood, fruit, vegetables, and other local products at the Mapua Farmers Market.
  • Wine and Beer: Visitors can bring home a variety of excellent wines and craft beers from Mapua and the wider Nelson region.

Local Culture and Customs of Mapua

New Zealand has a rich and unique culture that is reflected in its art, music, and traditions. Visitors to Mapua can experience the local culture by attending cultural events, visiting art galleries and exhibits, and trying the local food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Mapua

Mapua can be visited year-round, but the best time to go depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months (December-February) offer the warmest weather and the most outdoor activities, while the winter months (June-August) offer quieter streets and cheaper accommodation rates. Overall, the shoulder seasons (September-November and March-May) are a great time to visit, with mild weather, smaller crowds, and cheaper rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mapua

Do:

  • Do explore Mapua Wharf and its many galleries, cafes, and restaurants.
  • Do try the local seafood and produce.
  • Do go wine tasting at a local vineyard.
  • Do bring warm clothing and waterproof gear, as weather can be unpredictable.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t engage in disruptive or illegal behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mapua

Mapua is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low levels of crime and a friendly, welcoming community. However, visitors should still take common sense precautions, such as not leaving valuables in plain sight and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Mapua

New Zealand has a high standard of healthcare, with both public and private hospitals available. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and accidents. Tap water is generally safe to drink in New Zealand, but visitors with sensitive stomachs may prefer to stick to bottled water.

Getting to Mapua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mapua is located in South Island, approximately a 30-minute drive from Nelson Airport. Visitors can also reach Mapua by bus from Nelson or other nearby towns. Rental cars, bikes, and taxis are also available for hire in Mapua and Nelson.

Airports Near Mapua

  • Nelson Airport: Located approximately 22 kilometers from Mapua, Nelson Airport is the closest airport to Mapua.

Bus Stations Near Mapua

  • Richmond Bus Station: Located approximately 14 kilometers from Mapua, Richmond Bus Station offers services to areas throughout Nelson Bays.
  • Nelson Bus Station: Located approximately 20 kilometers from Mapua, Nelson Bus Station offers services to areas throughout South Island.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars/Bikes: Rental cars and bikes are available for hire in Nelson or Mapua.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Nelson or Mapua.

How to Get Around in Mapua

Mapua is a small village that is easily explored on foot or by bike. Rental cars, taxis, and public transportation are also available for visitors who want to explore further afield or need transportation to and from their accommodation.

Where to Go Next from Mapua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mapua is located in a beautiful part of New Zealand with many nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring. Some of the top places to visit near Mapua include:

  • Abel Tasman National Park: This nearby national park is famous for its beautiful beaches, clear waters, and native forests.
  • Nelson Lakes National Park: This stunning alpine park offers visitors hiking, skiing, and boating opportunities.
  • Kaikoura: A popular destination for whale watching, Kaikoura also offers stunning coastal scenery and a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a foodie, a nature lover, or a history buff, Mapua has something to offer. From its charming cafes and wineries to its stunning natural scenery and cultural attractions, there is plenty to see and do in this quaint New Zealand village.
