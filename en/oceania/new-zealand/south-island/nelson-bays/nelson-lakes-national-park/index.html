Introduction to Nelson Lakes National Park

Located in Nelson Bays, South Island, New Zealand, Nelson Lakes National Park is a pristine wilderness area with awe-inspiring natural beauty. Covering 102,000 hectares, the park comprises two large lakes, Rotoiti and Rotoroa, surrounded by snow-capped mountains, native forests, and alpine meadows.

List of Places to Visit in Nelson Lakes National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Nelson Lakes National Park can explore the rich Maori culture and history of the region through art and artifacts at:

  • Te Papa o Ngāti Tūmatakokiri marae
  • The World of Wearable Art Museum in nearby Nelson
  • The Bead Gallery in St. Arnaud, which showcases locally made jewelry

Natural Attractions of Nelson Lakes National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nelson Lakes National Park offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including:

  • Hiking the alpine trails to marvel at the majestic mountain views
  • Biking the scenic tracks through the heart of the park
  • Fishing in the clear waters of Rotoiti and Rotoroa
  • Kayaking and paddleboarding on the pristine lakes
  • Horseback riding through the lush alpine meadows

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nelson Lakes National Park

Nelson Lakes National Park boasts over twenty different hiking and biking trails, from easy to challenging, catering to all levels of fitness and experience. The tracks include:

  • The Travers-Sabine Circuit, a multi-day hiking track for experienced hikers who wish to cross the entire park.
  • The Mt. Robert Circuit, a one-day trek that affords stunning views over Lake Rotoiti and the surrounding forests.
  • The St. Arnaud Range Track, an impressive 3 to 4-hour hike that leads up to the summit of the mountain range with incredible mountain views.

Best Things to Do in Nelson Lakes National Park: Activities and Tours

Nelson Lakes National Park offers visitors a variety of outdoor adventures and guided tours, including:

  • Eco-tours that explore local flora and fauna, led by experienced naturalists
  • Boat tours that allow visitors to explore the lakes without hiking the rugged terrain
  • Overnight camping tours where visitors can experience the park’s beauty day and night, with guided star gazing.

Food and Drink Scene of Nelson Lakes National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Nelson Lakes National Park is known for its farm-fresh produce and artisanal food, with must-try dishes including:

  • Venison sausages served with local herbs and spices
  • Trout, smoked or grilled
  • Local cheese served with honey and fruit

Local Beverages

Visitors can sample some of New Zealand’s best wines and beers, including:

  • Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc
  • Green Man beer, an infused cider

Where to Stay in Nelson Lakes National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nelson Lakes National Park has a variety of accommodations, ranging from rustic cabins to luxurious lodges. Options include:

  • Alpine Lodge
  • Lake Rotoiti Lodge
  • Nelson Lakes Shuttles and Lodge
  • Nelson Lakes Motels

What to Buy in Nelson Lakes National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nelson Lakes National Park has a variety of locally made items for visitors to bring home, including:

  • Artwork, including ceramics and glassware
  • Souvenirs, including adorned T-shirts and magnets from the gift shop at the World of Wearable Art Museum.
  • Local honey, cheese, and native spices

Local Culture and Customs of Nelson Lakes National Park

Nelson Lakes National Park is home to many Maori communities with a rich cultural heritage that visitors are encouraged to explore and learn from. Visitors are expected to respect their traditions and cultures.

Best Time to Visit Nelson Lakes National Park

The best time to visit Nelson Lakes National Park is from November to April, during summer in New Zealand. During the winter months, many hiking trails are closed due to snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nelson Lakes National Park

Do:

  • Bring sturdy hiking boots and appropriate clothing for the outdoors
  • Respect the local Maori communities and their cultures
  • Follow the “Leave No Trace” principles while exploring the park

Don’t:

  • Smoke in non-designated areas of the park
  • Litter or damage any part of the park
  • Feed or approach any of the park’s wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Nelson Lakes National Park

It is essential to stay on marked trails while hiking and biking in the park and follow all warning signs in areas of high avalanche or animal activity. Visitors should carry sufficient water, food, and first-aid supplies for any outdoor activity, and check the weather conditions before embarking on any hike or other outdoor adventure.

Health in Nelson Lakes National Park

Visitors should be aware that there is limited medical help in the park. It is essential to pack all necessary medications and carry a first-aid kit for any minor injuries.

Getting to Nelson Lakes National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nelson Lakes National Park

The closest airport to Nelson Lakes National Park is Nelson Airport, located approximately 100 kilometers from the park. A shuttle or car hire is required to reach the park.

Bus Stations Near Nelson Lakes National Park

The nearest bus station to Nelson Lakes National Park is in Nelson, from where visitors can take a shuttle or taxi to the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Nelson Lakes National Park, although it is recommended that visitors use a 4x4 vehicle for some of the more remote parts of the park.

How to Get Around in Nelson Lakes National Park

The best way to get around Nelson Lakes National Park is on foot or bike. Alternatively, visitors can book a guided tour that provides transportation.

Where to Go Next from Nelson Lakes National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Nelson Lakes National Park can explore other nearby destinations, including the city of Nelson, the Abel Tasman National Park, and the Marlborough Sounds.

Conclusion

Nelson Lakes National Park is a fantastic destination for visitors to explore and enjoy the natural beauty of New Zealand. With breathtaking landscapes, a rich Maori culture, and outdoor activities for all tastes and levels of fitness, Nelson Lakes National Park is a must-visit destination for any traveler to New Zealand.
