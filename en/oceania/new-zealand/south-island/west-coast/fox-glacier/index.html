Introduction to Fox Glacier

Located in the West Coast region of South Island in New Zealand, Fox Glacier is one of the most beautiful natural wonders of the Oceania region. The glacier is nestled in a remote valley surrounded by snow-capped mountains and lush rainforest, making it a popular destination for adventure and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Fox Glacier

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Coast Wildlife Centre: This world-class facility offers visitors a chance to encounter rare native wildlife, including kiwis and tuataras. The center also has interactive displays and educational exhibits.

  • Te Runanga o Makaawhio: This Maori cultural center provides insights into the indigenous history of the region and the culture of the Maori people. Visitors can enjoy traditional performances, art exhibits, and guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Fox Glacier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fox Glacier: A guided glacier hike can help visitors experience the majesty of the glacier up close. Visitors can also explore the ice caves and crevasses formed by the glacier.

  • Lake Matheson: Known as the “Mirror Lake,” this picturesque spot provides stunning reflections of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Franz Josef Glacier: Located a short distance from Fox Glacier, this glacier is another popular destination for visitors to the region. Guided hikes and helicopter tours are available to explore the glacier.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fox Glacier

  • Fox Glacier Valley Walk: A leisurely 1-hour walk that passes through the rainforest and offers views of Fox Glacier.

  • Chalet Lookout Track: This 90-minutes hike leads to an elevated lookout point that provides panoramic views of the Fox Glacier Valley.

  • Moraine Walk: This 1-hour walk is an easy, accessible track that passes through moraine formations, ending at a lookout point over Fox Glacier Valley.

Best Things to Do in Fox Glacier: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Glacier Hiking: Explore the beauty of the glacier with a guided hike that takes you deep into the ice formations.

  • Skydiving: For thrill-seekers, skydiving over Fox Glacier is an unforgettable experience.

  • Heli-Hiking: Take a helicopter ride to a remote area of the glacier for a guided hike and picnic lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Fox Glacier

Must-Try Dishes

  • Whitebait Fritters: A local delicacy made from tiny shrimps battered and fried, served with lemon wedges.

  • Paua Fritters: Paua is a type of abalone commonly found in the waters around New Zealand. Enjoy it in fritter form with a side of tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Monteith’s Brewery: Located in nearby Greymouth, this brewery offers locally brewed beers in a historic setting.

  • Central Otago Wines: Many of the vineyards in Central Otago, just a short drive from Fox Glacier, produce internationally acclaimed Pinot Noir.

Where to Stay in Fox Glacier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Heartland Hotel Fox Glacier: Located just a few minutes’ drive from Fox Glacier, this comfortable hotel offers modern amenities and easy access to the region’s attractions.

  • YHA Franz Josef Glacier: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorm and private rooms and is a short drive from Fox Glacier.

  • The Westhaven Motel: A boutique motel located in Fox Glacier Village, this is a great option for those looking for spacious rooms and beautiful mountain views.

What to Buy in Fox Glacier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pounamu: Also known as greenstone or jade, pounamu is a traditional Maori stone found in the rivers and mountains of South Island. Local artisans create beautiful sculptures, jewelry, and home decor items from it.

  • Manuka Honey: Produced by bees that feed on the manuka plant, this honey has antibacterial properties and is said to have health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Fox Glacier

The Fox Glacier region has a rich history and cultural significance for the Maori people. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the traditional practices and customs of the Maori and to respect their cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Fox Glacier

The best time to visit Fox Glacier is during the summer months of November to February when the weather is mild, and the skies are clear. However, the area can be visited year-round, with unique experiences and stunning views available in all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fox Glacier

Do:

  • Wear warm, waterproof clothes and sturdy shoes when exploring the glacier and surrounding areas.
  • Respect the natural environment and cultural heritage of the region.
  • Hire a local guide when venturing onto the glacier.

Don’t:

  • Attempt to explore the glacier or surrounding mountains without a guide.
  • Damage or remove natural resources, including plants and wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fox Glacier

Visitors should be aware of the dangers of exploring a glacier and its surrounding areas. Hazardous conditions can present themselves suddenly, and visitors should follow the advice of their guides and adhere to safety protocols at all times.

Health in Fox Glacier

Mosquitoes and sandflies can be an annoyance during the summer months. Visitors should bring insect repellent and cover up to minimize bites. The region is free from major health risks, but visitors should have travel insurance that covers emergency medical attention in case of accidents.

Getting to Fox Glacier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fox Glacier

Greymouth Airport (GMN) and Hokitika Airport (HKK) are the nearest airports, both approximately 2 hours drive from Fox Glacier. International visitors will need to fly into Christchurch or Queenstown before driving or transferring to Fox Glacier.

Bus Stations Near Fox Glacier

Intercity and Nakedbus provide bus services to Fox Glacier from nearby locations such as Wanaka, Queenstown, Christchurch, and Nelson.

Other Options

Car hire is a popular option for visitors to the region, allowing them to explore at their own pace. Taxis and shuttle services are also available for hire to explore Fox Glacier and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Fox Glacier

Within Fox Glacier Village and the nearby attractions, visitors can easily explore the area on foot or by bike. For those venturing further afield, car hires are available from multiple providers in the region.

Where to Go Next from Fox Glacier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Franz Josef Glacier: Located just a short drive from Fox Glacier, Franz Josef is another stunning glacier that offers a range of outdoor activities and scenic views.

  • Queenstown: This famous destination is known for its adventure sports, lake, and soaring mountains, located about a 5-hour drive from Fox Glacier.

  • Milford Sound: Located about 4 hours from Fox Glacier, this fjord in Fiordland National Park is one of the most popular attractions in New Zealand.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking for adventure, breathtaking scenery, cultural experiences, or just relaxation, Fox Glacier is an exceptional destination to explore. With a range of activities and attractions, this remote corner of the world is a must-visit for any travel enthusiast.
