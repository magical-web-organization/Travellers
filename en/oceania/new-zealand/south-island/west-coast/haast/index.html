Haast is a hidden gem located on the West Coast of the South Island of New Zealand. Visitors to Haast will enjoy scenic vistas, outdoor adventures, and a charming small-town atmosphere. This comprehensive travel guide will provide an overview of the must-visit attractions, the best things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs of Haast.

Introduction to Haast

Haast is a remote and scenic township situated on the edge of the Te Wahipounamu World Heritage area. The town is named after explorer Julius Von Haast, who discovered the Haast Pass in 1863. Haast is the gateway to the stunning Fiordland National Park and the Southern Alps. It offers a range of accommodations, restaurants, and activities, making it the perfect destination for a relaxing holiday or an outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Haast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haast has a rich history, with several museums and historical places of interest.

  • Haast Heritage Museum: The museum tells the story of the region’s history and culture, with displays focusing on local Maori history, natural history, and early European settlement history.
  • Jackson Bay: A historic fishing village located at the end of a scenic drive, this small settlement is a great place to explore the local fishing culture and enjoy seafood at the local restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Haast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Haast is famous for its natural beauty, with several natural attractions that highlight its rugged landscape.

  • Haast Beach: This 15-kilometer-long beach is a great place to enjoy a picnic or a walk, with stunning views of the Tasman Sea.
  • Haast Pass: This scenic drive takes visitors through the Haast Pass, and has amazing views of the Southern Alps, waterfalls, glaciers, and beech forests.
  • Gates of Haast: The Gates of Haast are a natural wonder located near Haast Pass, featuring towering rock formations, waterfalls, and stunning river scenery.
  • Lake Moeraki: A pristine alpine lake surrounded by rainforest, this area is known for its hiking trails, fishing, and kayaking opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haast

Haast has many hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Here are some of the most popular routes.

  • Alex Knob Track: A challenging 10-hour return trek to the summit of Alex Knob that rewards hikers with stunning panoramic views of the region.
  • Haast Ridge Track: This popular 5-hour trek takes visitors through a mix of beech forest and tussock fields, with panoramic views of the mountains.
  • Blue Pools Track: An easy 30-minute walk that leads visitors to a stunning glacial-fed pool, with crystal clear waters and great swimming spots.

Best Things to Do in Haast: Activities and Tours

Haast offers visitors a great range of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests.

  • Jet Boating: Experience the thrill of jet boating on the Haast River, taking visitors on a scenic tour through the stunning Gates of Haast, and past waterfalls and rapids.
  • Heli-Hiking: Enjoy a unique and unforgettable experience with a heli-hiking tour, taking visitors to remote mountain locations for guided hiking tours.
  • Fishing: Haast is renowned for its excellent fishing opportunities, with the chance to catch freshwater trout or saltwater salmon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haast

Day 1: Arrival in Haast, check into accommodation, explore Haast Beach. Day 2: Hike the Blue Pools Track and then head to Jackson Bay for lunch and fishing village exploration. Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the Haast Pass, stopping to explore waterfalls and wildlife sightings. Day 4: Jet boat tour down the Haast River, explore the Gates of Haast, and then head to Lake Moeraki for kayaking and hiking. Day 5: Heli-hiking excursion via local operator, taking visitors to remote mountain ranges for guided hiking and wildlife sightseeing. Day 6: Fishing expedition on the Haast River or Charter Boat trip. Day 7: Departure from Haast.

Food and Drink Scene of Haast

Must-Try Dishes

Haast offers a range of dining options, with several must-try dishes that highlight the local cuisine.

  • Whitebait Fritters: A Haast specialty, whitebait fritters are small fish that are fried with egg and flour to make a tasty snack or meal.
  • Venison: Haast is renowned for its wild deer population, providing venison dishes that are both delicious and sustainable.

Local Beverages

With several wineries and craft breweries in the area, visitors to Haast have the opportunity to sample local beverages.

  • Makarora Brewery: A small-batch brewery that offers a range of craft beers, brewed on-site.
  • Haast Heli Wine Tours: A unique winery tour experience that takes visitors by helicopter to remote vineyards.

Where to Stay in Haast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haast offers a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses.

  • Heartland World Heritage Hotel: Located in the center of town, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations, with an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Haast River Motels and Holiday Park: Budget-friendly accommodations that offer self-contained units, cabins, and camping facilities.

What to Buy in Haast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Haast offers several souvenir shops with local products that make for great mementos of your trip.

  • Pounamu (Greenstone): A Maori cultural treasure, greenstone jewelry and carvings are available for purchase at several shops in Haast.
  • Merino Wool Products: Haast is home to several sheep farms that produce high-quality merino wool products, including clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Haast

The local culture in Haast is a blend of Maori and European influences, with a strong emphasis on outdoor activities and sustainability.

Best Time to Visit Haast

The best time to visit Haast is during the summer months, from November to March, when the weather is warm and sunny. Winter months, from June to August, can be cold and rainy, but they offer a unique opportunity to enjoy winter sports and outdoor activities like skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haast

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Take advantage of the local outdoor activities and guided tours.
  • Experience the local cuisine and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Undertake outdoor activities beyond your ability or experience level.
  • Disrespect local customs and cultural practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haast

Haast is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. Visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing, and be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Haast

Visitors should be aware of the potential health risks associated with outdoor activities, such as insect bites and exposure to the sun. It is recommended to carry insect repellent and sunscreen and to be adequately dressed when outside.

Getting to Haast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haast

The nearest airport to Haast is Hokitika Airport, located approximately 150 kilometers away. The airport offers domestic flights to Christchurch, Wellington, and other major cities in New Zealand.

Bus Stations Near Haast

Haast has a bus service that connects to major cities and towns in the South Island, including Queenstown, Wanaka, and Christchurch.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car in nearby towns and drive to Haast, taking advantage of the scenic drives and stopping to explore the natural attractions along the way.

How to Get Around in Haast

Haast is a small town, easily navigable on foot or by car. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take guided tours to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Haast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Haast is the perfect starting point for a range of excursions and nearby destinations, such as:

  • Franz Josef Glacier: A stunning glacier located just 100 kilometers from Haast, offering visitors the opportunity to hike on the glacier, take scenic flights, and soak in hot springs.
  • Glenorchy: A picturesque town located near Queenstown, offering hiking, fishing, and horseback riding opportunities.
  • Milford Sound: A world-renowned fjord located in Fiordland National Park, offering boat tours and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Haast is a unique and beautiful destination, offering visitors the opportunity to explore New Zealand’s rugged natural beauty, experience the local culture, and engage in outdoor activities. Plan your trip to Haast today for an unforgettable experience.
