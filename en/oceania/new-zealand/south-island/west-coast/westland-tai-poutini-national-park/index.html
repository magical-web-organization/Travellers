Introduction to Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Westland Tai Poutini National Park is a natural wonderland located on the rugged west coast of New Zealand’s South Island. The park spans 1.2 million acres and is home to four of the country’s 10 highest peaks, glaciers, forests, beaches, rivers, and lakes. Visitors can explore wild landscapes, pristine nature, and iconic sites.

List of Places to Visit in Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Here are the must-visit destinations when exploring Westland Tai Poutini National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The West Coast Historical Museum in Hokitika which showcases the history of gold mining on the West Coast, as well as Maori artifacts.
  • The Pounamu Pathway in Hokitika which takes you on a fascinating journey through the art and culture of New Zealand’s indigenous Maori people.

Natural Attractions of Westland Tai Poutini National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Franz Josef Glacier, one of the world’s most accessible glaciers, where visitors can take guided hikes or scenic helicopter flights.
  • Fox Glacier, a beautiful glacier that offers similar hiking and scenic flight options.
  • Lake Matheson, a stunning lake famous for its reflections of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Arthur’s Pass National Park, a must-visit destination for hikers and nature lovers with its rugged mountain ranges, alpine flora, and fauna such as the cheeky Kea bird.
  • Okarito Lagoon, a serene lagoon offering bird watching tours to spot the White Heron, the Kotuku, and other rare avian species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Westland Tai Poutini National Park has plenty of trails for hikers and bikers of all levels. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Copland Track: This challenging 18-kilometer hike starts from the Welcome Flat hut and leads you past waterfalls, hot springs, and glaciers
  • Alex Knob Track: This 11-kilometer hike offers sweeping views of the Franz Josef glacier and the surrounding mountains.
  • Hokitika Gorge Track: A short and easy 15-minute walk takes you to a stunning turquoise blue gorge that will take your breath away.

Best Things to Do in Westland Tai Poutini National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Glacier hiking: Explore the Franz Josef or Fox Glacier in all their amazing glory by taking a guided hiking or ice-climbing tour of the glaciers.
  • Scenic helicopter tours: Get a birds-eye view of the West Coast’s stunning scenery by taking a scenic flight over the glaciers, mountains, forests, and beaches.
  • Kayaking: Paddle your way through the serene waters of Lake Mapourika and take in the stunning views of the Southern Alps.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Day 1:

  • Arrive in Hokitika and explore the town’s rich history
  • Visit the West Coast Historical Museum

Day 2:

  • Drive to Franz Josef Glacier and take a guided tour of the glacier
  • Relax in one of the nearby hot pools

Day 3:

  • Indulge in a scenic helicopter ride over Franz Josef and Fox Glaciers
  • Explore the nearby beaches

Day 4:

  • Drive to Okarito Lagoon and take a bird watching tour
  • Visit a local Maori village to learn about the culture of New Zealand’s indigenous people.

Day 5:

  • Explore Lake Matheson and take the nature walk around the lake
  • Drive to Arthur’s Pass National Park and go on a hike

Day 6:

  • Explore the stunning beaches of the West Coast
  • Take a sunset or scenic cruise to spot the local wildlife, including seals, dolphins, penguins, and whales.

Day 7:

  • Relax in Hokitika and shop for local souvenirs
  • Enjoy the vibrant nightlife of the town

Food and Drink Scene of Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Whitebait Fritters: A West Coast specialty of tiny freshwater fish caught in spring that are battered and fried.
  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty soup that’s made with seafood such as mussels, prawns, and squid, potatoes, and cream.
  • Venison: A lean and low-fat meat that’s sustainably grown on the West Coast.

Local Beverages

  • West Coast Brewing: A local brewery that produces craft beers, including the Hokitika Blond and the West Coast IPA.
  • Monteith’s Brewing Company: A well-known brewery that produces award-winning beers such as the Monteith’s Black and the Radler.

Where to Stay in Westland Tai Poutini National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There’s accommodation to suit every budget in Westland Tai Poutini National Park. Here are some recommendations:

  • Scenic Hotel Franz Josef Glacier: A deluxe hotel that overlooks the mountains and glaciers.
  • YHA Franz Josef Glacier: A budget-friendly option perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Glenfern Villas: A charming bed and breakfast surrounded by lush gardens.

What to Buy in Westland Tai Poutini National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pounamu (greenstone): The West Coast is famous for its Pounamu; the traditional Maori treasure is believed to bring strength, peace, prosperity, and good luck.
  • Tiki carvings: Carved wooden Tiki sculptures are a popular souvenir from the West Coast.
  • New Zealand honey: The local honey harvested from the West Coast’s native flora is delicious and healthy.

Local Culture and Customs of Westland Tai Poutini National Park

The Maori culture plays a significant role in the West Coast’s history and traditions. Visitors should respect the local culture, take part in Maori cultural tours, and learn about the traditions, songs, and dances of Maori culture.

Best Time to Visit Westland Tai Poutini National Park

The best time to visit Westland Tai Poutini National Park is during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is warmer and the days are longer; however, the park is accessible year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Do:

  • Take only photographs, leave only footprints.
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles and dispose of your waste properly.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Keep your distance from wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife.
  • Remove or damage any plants or animals.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Light fires outside designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westland Tai Poutini National Park

When exploring the West Coast, visitors should keep these safety tips in mind:

  • Inform someone of your intended plans and itinerary.
  • Dress in warm, waterproof, and windproof clothing.
  • Check the weather and track conditions before embarking on any outdoor activities.
  • Bring plenty of drinking water and food.

Health in Westland Tai Poutini National Park

Visitors should adhere to these health tips when visiting Westland Tai Poutini National Park:

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Wear appropriate footwear and insect repellent.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid waterborne diseases.

Getting to Westland Tai Poutini National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westland Tai Poutini National Park

The nearest airports are:

  • Hokitika Airport (HKK)
  • Christchurch International Airport (CHC)

Bus Stations Near Westland Tai Poutini National Park

There are several bus services that operate to Westland Tai Poutini National Park:

  • Intercity Bus
  • Magic Bus
  • Kiwi Experience

Other Options

  • Self-drive: Rent a car or a campervan from Christchurch or Hokitika.
  • Private transfers: Arrange private transfers from the airport or your hotel in Hokitika.

How to Get Around in Westland Tai Poutini National Park

The best way to explore Westland Tai Poutini National Park is by car or campervan. Visitors can also take guided tours, shared shuttle buses, or bike rentals in some locations.

Where to Go Next from Westland Tai Poutini National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations to consider after visiting Westland Tai Poutini National Park:

  • Nelson Lakes National Park: A beautiful mountainous region that offers hiking, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Abel Tasman National Park: A coastal paradise that boasts golden beaches, turquoise waters, and hiking trails.
  • Punakaiki: A small town famous for its unique rock formations and blowholes.

Conclusion

Westland Tai Poutini National Park is a dream destination for nature and adventure lovers. With its stunning glaciers, mountains, forests, and beaches, there is something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of the West Coast.
