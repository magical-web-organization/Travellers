Introduction to Hokitika

Hokitika is a small town located on the West Coast of New Zealand’s South Island, in the region of Westland. Despite its size, Hokitika has a lot to offer visitors, including natural beauty, outdoor activities, and a rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Hokitika

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hokitika has a number of museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions in this category include:

  • Hokitika Museum: The Hokitika Museum is a great place to learn about the town’s history, including its gold rush past and Maori culture. The museum’s collection includes artifacts and displays from the early 1900s, as well as more recent exhibits.
  • West Coast Treetop Walk: The West Coast Treetop Walk is a unique attraction that offers visitors the chance to explore the region’s stunning rainforest canopy from above. The walkway is suspended 20 meters above the forest floor and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lake Kaniere Scenic Reserve: The Lake Kaniere Scenic Reserve is a beautiful natural area that is home to a variety of wildlife, including native birds and fish. Visitors can hike around the lake or take a guided tour to learn more about the area’s flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Hokitika: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hokitika is known for its stunning natural beauty, which includes snow-capped mountains, pristine beaches, and lush rainforests. Some of the most popular natural attractions in Hokitika include:

  • Hokitika Gorge: The Hokitika Gorge is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Its turquoise-colored waters are a result of glacial runoff and make for a stunning sight.
  • Lake Mahinapua: Lake Mahinapua is a peaceful lake that is home to a variety of birdlife, including the rare White Heron. Visitors can take a guided kayaking tour of the lake to experience its tranquility up close.
  • West Coast Wilderness Trail: The West Coast Wilderness Trail is a 139km-long cycle trail that takes visitors through the stunning landscape of the West Coast. The trail begins in Hokitika and ends in Greymouth.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hokitika

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Hokitika, and there are plenty of trails to choose from. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Hokitika Gorge Walk: This 30-minute walk takes visitors through stunning native forest to a viewing platform overlooking the turquoise waters of the Hokitika Gorge.
  • Mahinapua Walkway: The Mahinapua Walkway is a 30km-long track that takes visitors through a variety of habitats, from wetlands to conifer forests. The walkway is home to a range of birdlife, including the White Heron.
  • Hokitika beach: The beach in Hokitika is a popular spot for walking, jogging, and cycling. It’s also a great place to watch the sunset over the Tasman Sea.

Best Things to Do in Hokitika: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Hokitika. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Jade carving: Hokitika is famous for its jade carving, and visitors can take a guided tour of a local jade factory to see how the stone is transformed into beautiful pieces of art.
  • Gold panning: Hokitika’s gold rush past is still evident today, and visitors can try their hand at gold panning on the beach or at a local tour.
  • Horse trekking: Horse trekking is a great way to experience the rugged beauty of the West Coast. There are a number of guided tours available that take visitors through native forests and along the beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hokitika

Day 1: Arrive in Hokitika and explore the town’s museums, including the Hokitika Museum and the National Kiwi Centre.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of a local jade factory and try your hand at carving your own piece of jade.

Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Hokitika on a guided hiking tour of the Hokitika Gorge or Lake Kaniere Scenic Reserve.

Day 4: Spend the day relaxing on Hokitika beach or take a guided horse trek through the local countryside.

Day 5: Indulge in some local cuisine at one of Hokitika’s many restaurants or cafes.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Franz Josef Glacier to experience the beauty of this natural wonder.

Day 7: Depart Hokitika and head to your next destination, taking in the stunning scenery of New Zealand’s West Coast along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Hokitika

Must-Try Dishes

Hokitika is known for its fresh seafood, as well as its locally grown produce. Some of the must-try dishes in Hokitika include:

  • Whitebait fritters: Whitebait fritters are a Hokitika specialty, made from a type of small fish that is found in the local rivers and streams.
  • West Coast whitebait: Another delicious dish featuring the local whitebait is the classic West Coast whitebait patty, often served in sandwich form.
  • Venison: Venison is a popular meat in Hokitika, with many local restaurants serving up this gamey meat in a variety of dishes.

Local Beverages

Hokitika has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries producing a range of tasty brews. Some of the must-try beverages in Hokitika include:

  • Monteith’s Brewing Company: Monteith’s is one of New Zealand’s largest beer brands, with a brewery located in Hokitika. Visitors can take a guided tour of the brewery to learn more about how beer is made.
  • West Coast Brewing Co: The West Coast Brewing Co is a small craft brewery that produces a range of delicious beers, including a popular IPA.
  • Hokitika Wildfoods Festival Brew: This beer is produced by a local brewery and is only available during the annual Hokitika Wildfoods Festival, which takes place each March.

Where to Stay in Hokitika: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hokitika has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Hokitika include:

  • Hokitika Heritage Lodge: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building in the heart of Hokitika. The rooms are beautifully decorated and the staff are friendly and welcoming.
  • Beachfront Hotel Hokitika: This hotel is located right on the beach and offers stunning views of the Tasman Sea. The rooms are clean and comfortable, and the hotel has a range of amenities, including a restaurant and bar.
  • Teichelmann’s Bed & Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in a charming historic building and offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Hokitika: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hokitika is known for its local products, including jade jewelry, artisanal chocolates, and handcrafted wooden items. Some of the best places to shop for souvenirs in Hokitika include:

  • Hokitika Craft Gallery: This gallery showcases the work of local artisans and features a range of handcrafted items, including jewelry, pottery, and artwork.
  • The Jade Studio: The Jade Studio is a local jade workshop that produces a range of beautiful jewelry and sculptures.
  • Auntie’s Cottage: This charming gift shop features a range of locally made products, including handmade chocolates and soaps.

Local Culture and Customs of Hokitika

Hokitika has a rich cultural heritage, which includes Maori traditions and its gold rush past. Visitors to Hokitika should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and take the time to learn about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Hokitika

The best time to visit Hokitika depends on what you want to do. Summer (December-February) is the peak tourist season, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities on offer. Winter (June-August) can be cold and wet, but is a great time to visit for winter sports and snow-related activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hokitika

Do:

  • Try local delicacies, including whitebait fritters and venison.
  • Explore the town’s museums and galleries to learn about Hokitika’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Take a guided tour of a local jade factory to learn about the process of carving jade.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore safety warnings and advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hokitika

Hokitika is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions, such as locking their car and keeping an eye on their valuables. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and kayaking, and take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Hokitika

Hokitika has good quality medical facilities and visitors can access medical care if needed. However, visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical costs.

Getting to Hokitika: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hokitika

Hokitika Airport is a small regional airport that is serviced by several regional airlines, including Air New Zealand.

Bus Services Near Hokitika

Several bus companies operate services to Hokitika, including Intercity and Naked Bus. Buses stop in the center of town, making it easy to access accommodation and attractions.

Other Transportation Options

Visitors can also hire a car to explore the region independently, or take a guided tour to see the sights in and around Hokitika.

How to Get Around in Hokitika

Hokitika is a small town and is easily navigated on foot. Visitors can also hire a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Hokitika: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hokitika is a great base from which to explore New Zealand’s West Coast. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions worth considering include:

  • Franz Josef Glacier: Located just a few hours south of Hokitika, Franz Josef Glacier is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in natural beauty and outdoor adventures.
  • Punakaiki: Punakaiki is home to the famous Pancake Rocks and Blowholes, and is a great place to experience the rugged beauty of the West Coast.
  • Greymouth: Greymouth is a larger town located to the north of Hokitika, and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions, including the Brunner Mine Site and the Monteith’s Brewery.

Conclusion

Hokitika is a small town with a lot to offer visitors. From its stunning natural beauty to its rich cultural heritage, Hokitika is the perfect destination for those looking to experience the best of New Zealand’s West Coast.
