Introduction to Mount Aspiring National Park

Located in the West Coast region of the South Island of New Zealand, Mount Aspiring National Park is a pristine wilderness area that offers breathtaking vistas, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. The park is named after its highest peak, Mount Aspiring, which stands tall at 3,033 meters and dominates the skyline. The park boasts over 300 species of plants, numerous glaciers, alpine lakes, waterfalls, and rivers that carve their way through the rugged terrain.

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast or nature lover, you’ll find plenty of things to do in Mount Aspiring National Park. From hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding, there’s something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you plan your visit and discover the best places to visit, food to try, things to do, where to stay, and more in Mount Aspiring National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Aspiring National Park

Here’s a list of the top attractions to visit in Mount Aspiring National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wanaka Transport and Toy Museum: This museum has a large collection of vintage cars, trucks, tractors, and toys that will delight visitors of all ages.
  • Lake Wanaka: Located at the foot of the Southern Alps, Lake Wanaka is the fourth-largest lake in New Zealand and a stunning natural wonder.
  • Otago Central Rail Trail: Follow in the footsteps of early pioneers and gold miners as you cycle along this scenic rail trail.

Natural Attractions of Mount Aspiring National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rob Roy Glacier Track: This hiking trail takes you through beech forests, over streams, and along the Rob Roy Stream to the impressive Rob Roy Glacier.
  • Blue Pools Track: This easy hike takes you through a native forest to a series of crystal-clear pools that are a beautiful shade of blue.
  • Blue Pools Jetboat Tour: For a unique perspective of the Blue Pools, take a thrilling jet boat ride to explore this stunning natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Aspiring National Park

With over 500 km of hiking and biking trails, Mount Aspiring National Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Routeburn Track: This popular 32-km trail takes you through diverse landscapes, from alpine meadows to deep valleys.
  • Diamond Lake and Rocky Mountain Track: This 8-km loop takes you through beech forests and alpine meadows, with stunning views of the Southern Alps and Lake Wanaka.
  • Queenstown Bike Trail: This 125-km trail takes you from Queenstown to the historic gold-mining town of Arrowtown, passing through scenic valleys and across suspension bridges.

Best Things to Do in Mount Aspiring National Park: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to explore in Mount Aspiring National Park:

  • Skiing and snowboarding at Treble Cone or Cardrona Alpine Resort.
  • Horseback riding in the stunning Matukituki Valley.
  • Lord of the Rings scenic tours that take you to the movie locations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Aspiring National Park

With so much to see and do, it can be challenging to plan your visit to Mount Aspiring National Park. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Wanaka and explore the town’s attractions.
  • Day 2: Hike the Roy Roy Glacier Track.
  • Day 3: Take a jet boat tour of the Blue Pools.
  • Day 4: Explore the Diamond Lake and Rocky Mountain Track.
  • Day 5: Go skiing or snowboarding at Treble Cone or Cardrona Alpine Resort.
  • Day 6: Take a Lord of the Rings tour to explore the movie locations.
  • Day 7: Depart from Wanaka.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Aspiring National Park

New Zealand is famous for its fresh and delicious food, and Mount Aspiring National Park is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb roast: New Zealand is famous for its flavorful lamb.
  • Fish and chips: A classic Kiwi dish that is always a crowd-pleaser.
  • Hokey Pokey ice cream: A unique blend of vanilla ice cream with bits of crunchy honeycomb.

Local Beverages

  • Central Otago Pinot Noir: This award-winning wine is made from grapes that grow in the nearby vineyards.
  • Speights beer: A favorite beer among Kiwis that is brewed in Dunedin.

Where to Stay in Mount Aspiring National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wanaka is the primary gateway to Mount Aspiring National Park, and there are plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget. From luxury lodges to budget-friendly hostels, here are some recommendations:

  • Edgewater: A luxurious hotel with stunning lake views.
  • YHA Wanaka: A budget-friendly hostel that offers shared and private rooms.
  • Minaret Station: A high-end lodge that offers unique glamping experiences.

What to Buy in Mount Aspiring National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Mount Aspiring National Park with these souvenirs and local products:

  • Merino wool products: New Zealand is known for its high-quality merino wool, which is used to make everything from sweaters to socks.
  • Pounamu: Also known as greenstone, this beautiful stone is a traditional Maori carving material that is highly valued.
  • Kiwi products: From kiwifruit to kiwi-themed t-shirts, you’ll find plenty of options to commemorate your visit to New Zealand.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Aspiring National Park

The indigenous people of New Zealand are the Maori, and they have a rich culture and history that is still celebrated today. When visiting Mount Aspiring National Park, respect the local customs and traditions by:

  • Learning some Maori phrases and greeting locals with “Kia ora”
  • Removing your shoes before entering a Maori meeting house (marae)
  • Asking for permission before taking photos of people

Best Time to Visit Mount Aspiring National Park

The best time to visit Mount Aspiring National Park is during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is warmer and the days are longer. However, the park is open year-round, and each season offers unique opportunities to explore the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Aspiring National Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mount Aspiring National Park:

Do:

  • Plan ahead and book accommodations and tours in advance.
  • Stay on the designated trails to protect the fragile ecosystem.
  • Pack layers of clothing, as the weather can change quickly.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any trace of your visit.
  • Disturb the wildlife or plants.
  • Use drones within the park without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Aspiring National Park

Mount Aspiring National Park is a wilderness area, and visitors should be aware of the potential risks. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Check the weather forecast before heading out on a hike.
  • Bring a map and compass, and know how to use them.
  • Tell someone your itinerary and expected return time.

Health in Mount Aspiring National Park

New Zealand has a high level of healthcare, and there are medical facilities available in the nearby town of Wanaka if needed. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications and travel insurance.

Getting to Mount Aspiring National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wanaka is the primary gateway to Mount Aspiring National Park, and there are several transportation options to get there:

Airports Near Mount Aspiring National Park

  • Queenstown Airport: Located about an hour’s drive from Wanaka, this airport is the closest major airport.
  • Dunedin Airport: About a three-hour drive from Wanaka.

Bus Stations Near Mount Aspiring National Park

  • Wanaka Bus Station: Serves local and regional destinations.
  • Dunedin Bus Station: Serves long-distance destinations.

Other Options

  • Rental car: A popular option for exploring the park and surrounding areas.
  • Hitchhiking: New Zealand is known for its hitchhiking culture, and it’s a safe and common way to get around.

How to Get Around in Mount Aspiring National Park

While hiking and biking are popular ways to explore the park, there are also other transportation options available:

  • Guided tours: Many tour companies offer guided tours of the park and surrounding areas.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the nearby town of Wanaka.
  • Rental cars: Rental cars are available in Wanaka and Queenstown.

Where to Go Next from Mount Aspiring National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mount Aspiring National Park is just one of many stunning destinations in New Zealand. Here are some nearby attractions and excursions to consider:

  • Fiordland National Park: About a three-hour drive from Wanaka, this park is home to stunning fjords and awe-inspiring scenery.
  • Milford Sound: A popular day trip from Wanaka, this fjord is known for its stunning waterfalls and wildlife.
  • Queenstown: Known as the adventure capital of New Zealand, Queenstown offers everything from bungy jumping to skydiving.

Conclusion

Mount Aspiring National Park is a stunning destination that offers something for everyone. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in this pristine wilderness area. Plan your visit ahead of time and make the most of your time in this beautiful corner of the world.
