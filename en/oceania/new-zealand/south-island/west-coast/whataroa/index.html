Introduction to Whataroa

Whataroa is a small town located on the West Coast of the South Island in New Zealand, known for its stunning natural beauty, wildlife reserves, and outdoor adventure activities. It’s a great destination for travelers looking to explore the untouched wilderness of New Zealand and immerse themselves in Maori culture.

List of Places to Visit in Whataroa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maori Heritage Center: Explore the rich history and traditions of the Maori people at this cultural center, which features traditional carving displays, artwork, and live performances.
  • Ross Goldfields Heritage Trail: Take a historical walk through the Ross Goldfields, a site of significance during the New Zealand gold rush of the 1860s.
  • Westland Heritage Trail: Learn about the early European settlers of Westland and their struggles to adapt to this wild and remote region.

Natural Attractions of Whataroa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Whataroa Glacier: Take a scenic helicopter ride from Whataroa to the Franz Josef Glacier, one of the most stunning natural wonders of New Zealand.
  • White Heron Sanctuary: Observe the endangered white heron, the only breeding colony of the species in New Zealand, in their natural habitat at this protected sanctuary.
  • Okarito Lagoon: Take a guided kayak tour through the Okarito Lagoon, a beautiful wetland area that’s home to over 70 bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whataroa

  • Whataroa River Track: A scenic and easy hike along the river, with stunning views of New Zealand’s Southern Alps and the Tasman Sea.
  • Tatare Tunnels Walkway: Explore the historic railway tunnels and enjoy the beautiful forest scenery on this easy walk.
  • Robinsons Ridge Track: A challenging hike that rewards you with panoramic views of the glacier and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Whataroa: Activities and Tours

  • Glacier Hiking and Climbing: Experience the thrill of exploring a glacier with guided tours and climbing expeditions.
  • Jet Boating: Feel the thrill of speeding through scenic rivers and gorges on a jet boat tour.
  • Maori Cultural Experiences: Learn about the traditions and customs of the Maori people through cultural performances, workshops, and tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Whataroa

Day 1: Explore the Maori Heritage Center and take a scenic helicopter ride over Franz Josef Glacier, followed by a relaxing soak in the nearby thermal pools.

Day 2: Take a guided kayak tour through the Okarito Lagoon, followed by a visit to the White Heron Sanctuary.

Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking adventure on one of the many scenic trails around Whataroa, followed by a delicious seafood dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the historic Ross Goldfields and Westland Heritage Trails, followed by a visit to the local craft market.

Day 5: Go on a thrilling jet boat ride on the Whataroa River, followed by a wine tasting at a local vineyard.

Day 6: Take a guided glacier hike or climbing expedition, followed by a relaxing evening at a local pub.

Day 7: Finish your trip with a Maori cultural experience, which may include a traditional hangi feast, storytelling, dance performance, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Whataroa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kai Moana: Fresh seafood like green-lipped mussels, crayfish, and whitebait are a must-try in Whataroa.
  • Hāngi: A traditional Maori feast where meat and vegetables are cooked slowly in an earth oven for several hours, resulting in a delicious and smoky flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sauvignon Blanc: New Zealand is famous for its Marlborough region Sauvignon Blanc, which pairs perfectly with seafood.
  • Pinot Noir: The cooler climate of the South Island produces excellent Pinot Noir wines, which can be enjoyed with meat dishes and local cheese.

Where to Stay in Whataroa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Whataroa Hotel: A historic hotel with comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Glacier Desert Motel: A cozy motel with a garden, BBQ facilities, and free parking.
  • Waiho Motel: A fully-equipped motel with stunning mountain views and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Whataroa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pounamu: Known as New Zealand jade or greenstone, pounamu is a precious stone that has cultural significance for the Maori people, who believe that it brings strength, protection, and good luck.
  • Possum Merino: A luxurious blend of possum fur and merino wool, possum merino products are warm, soft, and eco-friendly.

Local Culture and Customs of Whataroa

The Maori people of the region have a rich and diverse culture that is celebrated through traditional songs, dances, storytelling, and carving artwork. Visitors to Whataroa can participate in workshops and performances, as well as visit cultural centers to learn more about the history and traditions of the Maori people.

Best Time to Visit Whataroa

The best time to visit Whataroa is during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the daylight lasts longer. This is also the peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and book in advance. Alternatively, the shoulder seasons of spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) can also be a great time to visit, as the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whataroa

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Maori people.
  • Follow the designated hiking and biking trails to protect the fragile ecosystem.
  • Support local businesses and buy locally-made products.

Don’t:

  • Remove or damage any vegetation or wildlife.
  • Ignore warning signs or wander off the designated trails.
  • Take any souvenirs or artifacts from historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Whataroa

Whataroa is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s important to be aware of the risks of outdoor activities, such as hiking, climbing, and jet boating, and take necessary precautions to avoid accidents.

Health in Whataroa

New Zealand has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors are encouraged to purchase travel insurance before they arrive. There are medical centers and hospitals located in nearby towns, such as Hokitika and Greymouth.

Getting to Whataroa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Whataroa

The nearest airport to Whataroa is the Hokitika Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive away. Alternatively, visitors can fly into Christchurch International Airport or Queenstown International Airport, both of which are about a 4-5 hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Whataroa

There are no bus stations in Whataroa, but visitors can take a bus to nearby towns like Hokitika and Greymouth, and then rent a car or take a taxi to Whataroa.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or campervan and drive from other parts of New Zealand to Whataroa. The scenic drive along the West Coast is particularly stunning.

How to Get Around in Whataroa

The best way to get around Whataroa is by car or bike. There are no public transportation options apart from a few taxis, so it’s important to rent a car or bring your own.

Where to Go Next from Whataroa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Franz Josef Glacier: A stunning glacier located about 25 km south of Whataroa, known for its scenic hiking trails, helicopter tours, and hot pools.
  • Westport: A charming coastal town located about 2 hours north of Whataroa, with beautiful beaches, historic sites, and outdoor activities.
  • Lake Tekapo: A popular tourist destination located about 4-5 hours east of Whataroa, known for its mesmerizing turquoise lake, hot springs, and stargazing tours.

Conclusion

Whataroa is a hidden gem of New Zealand, where visitors can experience the wild and natural beauty of the West Coast, immerse themselves in Maori culture, and enjoy a variety of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adrenaline-fueled adventure, Whataroa has something to offer for everyone. So book your trip today and discover the wonders of this beautiful region.
