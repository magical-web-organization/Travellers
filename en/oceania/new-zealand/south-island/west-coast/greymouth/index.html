Introduction to Greymouth

Greymouth is a beautiful coastal town located on the West Coast of New Zealand’s South Island. It is known for its untamed nature, rugged landscapes, rich history, and adventurous spirit. The town itself is small, boasting a population of just over 10,000 people, but it serves as a gateway to some of the most breathtaking natural wonders in the country.

List of Places to Visit in Greymouth

Visitors to Greymouth will find no shortage of things to see and do. Below are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shantytown Heritage Park: This open-air museum tells the story of Greymouth’s gold rush history. Visitors can take a train ride, pan for gold, and explore the replica buildings.
  • Monteith’s Brewing Company: Take a guided tour of the brewery and learn about the history of beer-making in Greymouth. Finish off with some tastings.
  • Left Bank Art Gallery: Located in an 1870s heritage building, this gallery showcases works from local artists.

Natural Attractions of Greymouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paparoa National Park: A stunning coastal park featuring the famous Pancake Rocks and Blowholes. Great for hiking, camping, and scenic drives.
  • Lake Brunner: A picturesque lake perfect for fishing, swimming, and boating.
  • Taramakau Bridge: A unique bridge that offers views of the wild Grey River and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greymouth

  • The West Coast Wilderness Trail: A 132km-long trail that can be done by bike or on foot. It takes you through beautiful scenery and charming West Coast towns.
  • Truman Track: A 90-minute walk through lush rainforest that ends at the breathtaking Truman Beach.
  • Point Elizabeth Walkway: A scenic hike that offers views of the Tasman Sea and the Grey River.

Best Things to Do in Greymouth: Activities and Tours

  • Blackwater Rafting: Take a guided tour to explore the underground caves and witness the glowworms.
  • Heli-Hiking: Get flown to remote areas of the Southern Alps for a day of guided hiking and stunning views.
  • Kayaking: Paddle down the scenic Grey River with a guided kayaking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greymouth

  • Day 1: Visit Shantytown Heritage Park and Monteith’s Brewing Company.
  • Day 2: Take a scenic drive to Paparoa National Park, including a stop at Pancake Rocks and Blowholes.
  • Day 3: Go fishing or boating in Lake Brunner.
  • Day 4: Hike the West Coast Wilderness Trail or the Point Elizabeth Walkway.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the underground caves and the glowworms.
  • Day 6: Heli-hike in the Southern Alps.
  • Day 7: Explore the Left Bank Art Gallery and the Taramakau Bridge.

Food and Drink Scene of Greymouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Whitebait: A seasonal delicacy in New Zealand, this small fish is usually served in omelettes or fritters.
  • Pies: Kiwis love their pies, and the West Coast is known for having some of the best.
  • Seafood: Greymouth is located on the coast, so fresh seafood is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Monteith’s Beer: A local brewery that has been around since 1868. Try their famous Radler beer.
  • Esk Valley Wine: A winery located in the nearby Hawke’s Bay region, producing award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Greymouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greymouth has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some top picks include:

  • The Ashley Hotel Greymouth: A 3.5-star hotel located near the town center.
  • Duke Hostel Greymouth: A budget-friendly hostel with shared and private rooms.
  • Gables Motor Lodge: A 4-star motel located in a quiet area, with modern and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Greymouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Greenstone (Pounamu): A type of jade that is revered by the Maori people. It is often carved into jewelry and other decorative items.
  • Possum Merino Wool: A soft and warm type of wool made from a blend of possum fur and merino wool.
  • Local Honey: Greymouth produces some of the best honey in New Zealand, thanks to its abundant wildflowers and bush.

Local Culture and Customs of Greymouth

The people of Greymouth are known for their laid-back and friendly nature. When visiting, it is important to respect the local culture and customs, including the Maori culture. During a formal Maori powhiri (welcome ceremony), visitors should follow the protocol and respect the elders.

Best Time to Visit Greymouth

The best time to visit Greymouth is during the summer months (December to February), when the weather is warm and dry. However, it is also the busiest time, so expect larger crowds and higher prices. The shoulder seasons of spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) offer mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greymouth

Do:

  • Do explore the natural attractions, including Paparoa National Park and Lake Brunner.
  • Do try the local delicacies, such as whitebait and pies.
  • Do interact with locals and learn about their culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather, as it can change quickly on the West Coast.
  • Don’t ignore safety warnings or advice when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greymouth

When visiting Greymouth, it is important to be aware of potential hazards and follow safety precautions. The West Coast can experience heavy rainfall and flooding, so always check the weather forecast before embarking on outdoor activities. Swimming in the ocean can also be dangerous, with strong currents and undertows. Always swim at patrolled beaches and follow lifeguards’ instructions.

Health in Greymouth

Greymouth has all the necessary medical facilities and services. Visitors should have travel health insurance to cover any medical emergencies. Mosquitoes can be present in some areas, so bring insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Greymouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greymouth

The closest major airport to Greymouth is Christchurch International Airport, located about 220km to the east. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight to Hokitika Airport, which is just 30km north of Greymouth.

Bus Stations Near Greymouth

Intercity and other bus companies offer services to Greymouth from major cities in both the North and South Islands.

Other Options

For those who prefer a more scenic journey, the TranzAlpine train connects Greymouth with Christchurch, passing through stunning alpine scenery. It is considered one of the most beautiful train journeys in the world.

How to Get Around in Greymouth

The best way to explore Greymouth and the surrounding areas is by car. Rental cars are available from major cities, including Christchurch and Auckland. Taxis and shuttles are also available for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Greymouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Franz Josef Glacier: A stunning glacier located about 175km south of Greymouth, offering guided tours and scenic flights.
  • Hokitika: A small town located 35km north of Greymouth, known for its jade jewelry and driftwood art.
  • Westport: A charming coastal town located 100km north of Greymouth, offering natural attractions and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Greymouth is a unique and beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the untamed nature and rich history of the West Coast. From the scenic drives to the outdoor adventures and cultural experiences, there is something for everyone. Plan your trip today to experience the best of Greymouth.
