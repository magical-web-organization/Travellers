Introduction to Southern Lakes

Located in the Southern Alps of South Island, New Zealand, Southern Lakes is a breathtaking region with jaw-dropping scenery. This area is famous for its crystal-clear glacial lakes, majestic mountains, and dramatic landscapes. It is an all-year-round destination that offers a variety of outdoor activities, from hiking and mountain biking in the summer to skiing and snowboarding in the winter.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Lakes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical sites in Southern Lakes that give visitors a glimpse into the region’s rich history and culture. Here are a few places you might want to visit:

  • Lakes District Museum and Gallery: This museum in Arrowtown showcases the gold-mining history of the region.

  • Fiordland National Park: As one of New Zealand’s oldest national parks, Fiordland National Park is home to several historical sites, including the historic mining town of Te Anau.

  • Walter Peak High Country Farm: Located on the shores of Lake Wakatipu, this farm provides visitors the opportunity to immerse themselves in New Zealand’s rural lifestyle and heritage.

Natural Attractions of Southern Lakes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Lakes is a paradise for nature lovers. From stunning lakes and rivers to snow-capped mountains and glaciers, the region offers a wide range of natural attractions. Here are some of the top outdoor and scenic spots to explore:

  • Milford Sound: This iconic fjord is one of New Zealand’s most popular tourist destinations, known for its dramatic cliffs, waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.

  • Mount Aspiring National Park: This park is home to Mount Aspiring, one of the country’s tallest peaks, as well as numerous glaciers, lakes, and forests.

  • Queenstown: Widely considered the “adventure capital of the world,” Queenstown is a hub for adrenaline-pumping activities like bungee jumping, skydiving, and jet boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Lakes

Southern Lakes has abundant hiking routes and cycling trails that showcase the stunning scenery of the region. Here are a few popular routes to consider:

  • Routeburn Track: A 32-kilometer track that traverses the Southern Alps, the Routeburn Track is one of New Zealand’s Great Walks.

  • Queenstown Trail: This 130-kilometer cycle trail takes you on a scenic journey through the Wakatipu Basin, Central Otago, and Fiordland.

  • Mount Cook National Park: The park boasts beautiful hikes that take visitors past glaciers, lakes, and alpine fields.

Best Things to Do in Southern Lakes: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and cycling, Southern Lakes has plenty of activities to offer. Here are some of the best things to do and tours to take:

  • Lake Cruise: Take a scenic cruise around Lake Wakatipu, and soak in the stunning scenery while enjoying a delicious meal.

  • Skydiving: Queenstown is one of the best places in the world to go skydiving, with spectacular views of the mountains and lakes below.

  • Wine Tour: Southern Lakes is renowned for its wine, and a guided wine tour is a great way to explore some of the region’s best vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Lakes

Day 1: Explore Queenstown and the surrounding area, including Skyline Gondola and the Kiwi Birdlife Park.

Day 2: Take a drive to Glenorchy and explore the area on foot.

Day 3: Embark on a Milford Sound day trip, and take in the stunning views of the fjord.

Day 4: Head to Wanaka for some hiking and outdoor activities.

Day 5: Explore the Cardrona Valley, and stop at the historic Cardrona Hotel.

Day 6: Take a wine tour in the Gibbston Valley, and enjoy some delicious wine and food.

Day 7: Spend a day at Fiordland National Park, exploring the stunning natural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Lakes

Must-Try Dishes

Southern Lakes is home to some delicious culinary offerings that you shouldn’t miss out on. Here are a few dishes to look out for:

  • Muttonbird: A traditional Maori dish, muttonbird is a delicacy that is best enjoyed roasted with kumara (sweet potato).

  • Pavlova: A dessert made with a crispy meringue base, topped with whipped cream and fresh fruit, pavlova is a New Zealand classic.

  • Kiwi Burger: A burger that features a beef patty, egg, beetroot, and pineapple, the Kiwi Burger is a delicious treat.

Local Beverages

Southern Lakes is home to several wineries and breweries, making it an ideal destination for wine and beer lovers. Here are a few local beverages you should try:

  • Central Otago Pinot Noir: Renowned for its fruity and complex flavor profile, Central Otago Pinot Noir is a must-try for any wine enthusiast.

  • Speights: A classic New Zealand beer, Speights is a popular option among locals.

Where to Stay in Southern Lakes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Lakes has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Sofitel Queenstown Hotel and Spa: A luxurious hotel in the heart of Queenstown, offering stunning mountain and lake views.

  • YHA Queenstown Central: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Queenstown, offering comfortable dorm and private rooms.

  • Blanket Bay Lodge: A secluded high-end lodge located near Glenorchy, offering guest rooms and suites with stunning views.

What to Buy in Southern Lakes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Lakes has a range of unique local products that make for great souvenirs and gifts. Here are a few items to consider:

  • Merino Wool: New Zealand is famous for its high-quality merino wool, which is used to make clothes, blankets, and other products.

  • Greenstone (Pounamu): A type of jade found only in New Zealand, Greenstone is used to make jewelry, sculptures, and other decorative items.

  • Manuka Honey: Produced from the nectar of the Manuka tree, Manuka honey is a natural sweetener with health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Lakes

The Maori people have a rich history and culture in New Zealand, and many of their customs and traditions are still celebrated today. Visitors to Southern Lakes should be respectful of Maori culture and customs, including the hongi (traditional Maori greeting) and respect for tapu (sacred sites).

Best Time to Visit Southern Lakes

Southern Lakes is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. For outdoor activities like hiking and cycling, the summer months (December to February) are ideal. The winter months (June to August) are popular for skiing and snowboarding. Spring and autumn offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Lakes

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife by practicing Leave No Trace principles.

  • Try the local food and drink, including the wines and craft beers.

  • Take part in outdoor activities, like hiking, cycling, and skiing.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local culture and customs, particularly with regards to Maori customs.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Underestimate the weather and always be prepared for changes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Lakes

Southern Lakes is a safe destination for travelers, although visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities. Always follow safety guidelines and be prepared for changes in weather and conditions.

Health in Southern Lakes

Southern Lakes has good medical facilities, but visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. The region is also free from major infectious diseases.

Getting to Southern Lakes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Lakes

Queenstown Airport (ZQN) is the main airport in the region, with direct flights to major cities in New Zealand, Australia, and Fiji.

Bus Stations Near Southern Lakes

InterCity and GreatSights operate buses that connect Southern Lakes with other parts of the country, including Dunedin, Christchurch, and Auckland.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for getting around Southern Lakes, with several car rental companies operating at Queenstown Airport and in other towns.

How to Get Around in Southern Lakes

Public transportation in Southern Lakes is limited, so renting a car is the easiest way to get around. Taxis, shuttles, and tour companies also operate in the region.

Where to Go Next from Southern Lakes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Lakes is often part of a longer trip to New Zealand, with nearby destinations like Fiordland National Park, Mount Cook National Park, and the West Coast offering spectacular natural attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, stunning landscapes, or cultural experiences, Southern Lakes in New Zealand has everything you need for an unforgettable trip. Plan your visit with this comprehensive travel guide, and get ready to explore one of the country’s most captivating regions.
