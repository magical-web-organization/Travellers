Introduction to Kingston

Kingston is a hidden gem located in the Queenstown-Lakes district of Otago, South Island, New Zealand, and is often overlooked by travelers visiting the country. Despite its small size, Kingston is a town packed with natural beauty, outdoor adventure opportunities, and historical significance. It’s a perfect destination for travelers looking for a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Kingston

While Kingston may be small, there are plenty of attractions throughout the town to keep travelers busy. Whether you’re seeking culture, history, or outdoor adventure, Kingston has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those looking to explore the history and culture of the region, Kingston offers several notable attractions worth visiting. Here are some must-see cultural attractions:

  • Kingston Flyer Steam Train: Ride in style on the Kingston Flyer Steam Train, a heritage railway service that runs from Kingston to Fairlight. This scenic ride is an iconic attraction in Kingston that takes you back to New Zealand’s golden age of steam railways.
  • Kingston Historical Society Museum: Learn about the rich history of the town and the surrounding area at the Kingston Historical Society Museum. Here, you will find exhibits showcasing the early settlers, gold mining history, and the famous Kingston Flyer Steam Train.

Natural Attractions of Kingston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kingston is located just a stone’s throw away from the breathtaking Southern Alps, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors will be blown away by the range of activities and scenic views offered in the area. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in Kingston:

  • Lake Wakatipu: Take a cruise or paddle on the glistening, crystal-clear waters of Lake Wakatipu. Surrounded by majestic mountains, Lake Wakatipu offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kingston Beach: Situated on the northern shore of Lake Wakatipu, Kingston Beach is the ideal spot to admire a picture-perfect sunrise or sunset while enjoying a peaceful walk.
  • The Remarkables: This iconic mountain range towers over the town of Kingston and offers a range of outdoor activities from hiking to skiing, as well as breathtaking views over Lake Wakatipu.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingston

One of the best things about visiting Kingston is the variety of hiking and biking trails available. Here are some of the top trails worth checking out:

  • Kingston Flyer Trail: This 14km track runs alongside the Kingston Flyer Steam Train route. It’s a relatively easy walk or bike ride that offers stunning views of the nearby countryside.
  • Round The Mountain (RTM) Track: This multi-day track takes hikers through some of the best scenery the Southern Alps have to offer. It’s a great way to experience the alpine wilderness and escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life.
  • Queenstown Trail: This 100km trail network is perfect for both biking and hiking. The trail passes through Kingston and offers picturesque views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kingston: Activities and Tours

Kingston offers a variety of outdoor adventure activities for travelers of all ages. From water sports to skiing, Kingston has something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Kingston:

  • Jet Boating: Experience the thrill of high-speed jet boating on Lake Wakatipu and the Kawarau River.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Kingston is a stone’s throw away from some of the best ski resorts and snowboarding parks in New Zealand. Visitors can take a short trip to the nearby ski resorts of Coronet Peak and The Remarkables.
  • Fishing: Treat yourself to a relaxing day of fishing on the pristine Lake Wakatipu.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kingston

For those looking to make the most out of their visit to Kingston, we’ve created a week-long itinerary of activities and attractions:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Kingston Flyer Steam Train and the Kingston Historical Society
2 Go cycling or hiking on the Round The Mountain Track
3 Visit the Remarkables Ski Resort and go skiing or snowboarding

Food and Drink Scene of Kingston

Kingston may be small, but it offers a range of dining options to suit all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try while in Kingston:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Neck Chop: This tender lamb chop is a staple of New Zealand cuisine and is a must-try for foodies visiting Kingston.
  • Venison: Venison is another New Zealand specialty and can be enjoyed in a variety of dishes such as stews, pies, and grilled options.

Local Beverages

  • Central Otago Wine: The region of Central Otago is famous for its world-class wines, particularly its Pinot Noir. Visitors can visit local vineyards and wineries to sample the best wines the region has to offer.
  • Kawarau Ale: This locally brewed beer has a refreshing taste that is perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Kingston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kingston has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Kingston:

  • The Kingston Flyer: This historic hotel was once a train station and has been beautifully restored to offer modern amenities to guests. It’s a great option for those looking for a unique experience.
  • Kingston Holiday Park: This holiday park offers a range of accommodation options from campsites to cabins. It’s perfect for budget-conscious travelers or groups.
  • The Kingston Homestead: This charming guesthouse is situated in the heart of Kingston and offers comfortable rooms, stunning views, and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Kingston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kingston may be a small town, but it offers plenty of opportunities for unique shopping experiences. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Kingston:

  • Greenstone Jewelry: Greenstone, also known as Pounamu, is a type of jade that is found in New Zealand. It has cultural significance for the Maori people and is often carved into beautiful jewelry pieces.
  • Central Otago Wines: Treat yourself to a bottle or two of the world-class wines produced in the Central Otago region.
  • Sheepskin Products: New Zealand is famous for the quality of its sheepskin products, from cozy blankets to comfortable slippers.

Local Culture and Customs of Kingston

New Zealand is a country rich in culture and history, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions while visiting Kingston. Here are some things to keep in mind while in the town:

  • Maori Culture: The Maori people are the indigenous people of New Zealand, and their culture is deeply ingrained in the country’s history and traditions. Visitors should take the time to learn about Maori culture and show respect for their customs and traditions.
  • Environmental Protection: New Zealand is known for its natural beauty, and it’s important to protect the environment while enjoying outdoor activities in Kingston. Visitors should follow designated trails, pack out all their rubbish, and leave the natural environment undisturbed.

Best Time to Visit Kingston

Kingston is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferred activities and preferences. Here’s what you need to know about visiting Kingston throughout the year:

  • Summer (December to February): This is the best time to visit Kingston if you’re interested in outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. The weather is warm, and the days are long.
  • Autumn (March to May): Autumn in Kingston offers stunning foliage and milder temperatures, making it perfect for outdoor excursions.
  • Winter (June to August): While it may be cold, winter in Kingston offers a prime opportunity for skiing and snowboarding in the nearby ski resorts.
  • Spring (September to November): The weather starts to warm up in the spring, and visitors can take part in outdoor activities before the summer crowds arrive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingston

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Kingston:

Do:

  • Show Respect for Maori Culture: Take the time to learn about the Maori culture and show respect for their customs and traditions
  • Protect the Environment: Take care to preserve the environment while enjoying outdoor activities in Kingston by following designated trails, packing out your rubbish, and being mindful of your impact on the natural environment.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Always pack out your rubbish and leave the natural environment undisturbed.
  • Ignore Safety Warnings: Pay attention to posted signs and warnings, especially when hiking or engaging in water activities.

Safety and Health

Kingston is a safe destination, but visitors should take common-sense precautions while traveling. Here’s what you need to know about staying safe and healthy in Kingston:

Safety in Kingston

  • Water Safety: Always follow the posted safety signs and warnings when engaging in water activities on Lake Wakatipu.
  • Hiking Safety: Always follow designated trails, and let someone know about your intended route and expected return time.
  • Vehicle Safety: Be cautious when driving on the winding mountain roads in the area and follow posted speed limits and traffic signs.

Health in Kingston

  • Sun Protection: New Zealand experiences high levels of UV radiation, so it’s important to protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays while enjoying outdoor activities in Kingston.
  • Drinking Water: While tap water in New Zealand is safe to drink, it may have a different taste due to the mineral content. If you prefer, you can purchase bottled water.

Getting to Kingston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kingston is easily accessible by both car and public transportation. Here’s what you need to know about getting to Kingston:

  • Airports Near Kingston: The closest airport to Kingston is Queenstown International Airport, which is about a 45-minute drive away.
  • Bus Stations Near Kingston: Buses run regularly between Queenstown and Kingston and take about an hour to get there.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Queenstown to Kingston.

How to Get Around in Kingston

Once in Kingston, visitors can easily get around the town by foot or by bike. Bikes can be rented from local shops, and many of the attractions and activities in town are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Kingston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kingston is the perfect jumping-off point for exploring the best destinations in the Otago region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Queenstown: Known as the adventure capital of New Zealand, Queenstown is a bustling town with plenty to see and do, from adrenaline-pumping activities to visiting wineries and museums.
  • Arrowtown: This charming town is famous for its gold mining history and offers eclectic shopping and dining options.
  • Milford Sound: Take a day trip to Milford Sound, a world-renowned natural wonder that offers breathtaking views of towering waterfalls, stunning alpine scenery, and abundant wildlife.

Conclusion

Kingston is a stunning destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventure to cultural significance. Its proximity to the Southern Alps and Lake Wakatipu make it an ideal destination for nature lovers, while its charming town and rich history offer something for those seeking a more laid-back experience. So, what are you waiting for? Plan your trip to Kingston today!
