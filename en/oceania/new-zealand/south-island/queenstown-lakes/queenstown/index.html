Introduction to Queenstown

Queenstown is a beautiful resort town located in the southwestern region of New Zealand’s South Island. Surrounded by towering peaks and picturesque lakes, it is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. The town’s natural beauty, combined with its vibrant nightlife, exceptional dining scene and array of attractions, make it a must-visit destination for any travel enthusiast.

List of Places to Visit in Queenstown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Queenstown is not only famous for its scenic beauty but also for its rich cultural heritage. For a glimpse into the town’s history, visit the following places:

  • Lake Wakatipu: The longest lake in New Zealand and third-largest in Oceania. It is considered sacred by the Maori, the Indigenous Polynesian people of New Zealand.

  • Arrowtown: A quaint little town located 20 minutes away from Queenstown that boasts a strong gold-mining heritage from the 1860s.

  • Queenstown Arts Centre: Operated by the Queenstown Centre for Creative Arts, this centre features exhibitions of contemporary art, local crafts, and more.

  • Toi o Tahuna Gallery: An art gallery dedicated to showcasing local Maori and Kiwi artists.

Natural Attractions of Queenstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Queenstown is renowned as the adventure capital of New Zealand, with a huge range of activities to suit every level of thrill-seeker:

  • Skyline Gondola and Luge: Take the scenic gondola ride up to enjoy panoramic views of Lake Wakatipu and the Southern Alps, a remarkable sight to behold.

  • Milford Sound: Take a 4-hour journey to the west to enjoy the remarkable fjords of Milford Sound.

  • Queenstown Hill: Enjoy breathtaking views of Queenstown from the top of this famed hill.

  • Wanaka: Head 1-hour northwest from Queenstown to enjoy the pristine Lake Wanaka, a popular destination for watersports and hikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Queenstown

Queenstown offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, from easy walks to more challenging hikes. Here are some great options:

  • Ben Lomond Track: Take a 6-hour journey and enjoy picturesque views of Lake Wakatipu, more of Queenstown’s range of mountains and the historic mining township of Arrowtown.

  • Moke Lake Loop Track: A relatively easy 2-hour hike, usually preferred by tourists who are taking their first ever hiking experience.

  • Queenstown Trail: A great option for exploring the beautiful scenery surrounding Queenstown by bike or foot. The trail goes through the beautiful wine country and scenic farms.

Best Things to Do in Queenstown: Activities and Tours

Queenstown is known for its adventure activities, but it’s not only for thrill-seekers as there are plenty of laid back activities as well. Here are some recommendations:

  • Bungee Jumping: Try the famous Nevis Bungee, suitable for adventure enthusiasts or the less adventurous Kawarau Bridge Bungee overlooking the beautiful Kawarau River.

  • Jet Boat Ride on Shotover River: Operating for more than 60 years, this is jet boating activity uses aqua thrust jet engines to navigate rugged terrains, and rapids and streams.

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Queenstown is known for hosting world-class mountains such as The Remarkables, Coronet Peak, and Cardrona Mountain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Queenstown

Here’s a 7-day itinerary that will allow you to make the most of your stay in Queenstown:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Queenstown and explore the city.
Day 2 Visit Lake Wakatipu and take the Skyline Gondola and Luge.
Day 3 Go on a Milford Sound day trip.
Day 4 Take a hike on the famous Ben Lomond Track and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
Day 5 Rent a bike and explore the romantic vineyards in Gibbston Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Queenstown

Must-Try Dishes

Queenstown offers a variety of food experiences in its many restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Fergburger: It is dubbed by many as the world’s tastiest burger and a staple of the city’s fast-food scene.

  • Greenstone Kitchen: Offers a modern take on New Zealand cuisine with delicious dishes such as beef cheek and pumpkin in thyme sauce.

  • The Cow: This restaurant has is a favourite for both locals and foreigners, known for their thin-crust pizza and pasta dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Central Otago Wine: Queenstown is situated in the Central Otago wine region, famous for its Pinot Noir. Enjoy the wine from the local vineyards.

  • Gibbston Valley Cheese: Be sure to try the cheese specialities, such as blue cheese, fondue, and artisanal cheese varieties in Gibbston Valley.

Where to Stay in Queenstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Queenstown offers a range of accommodation options from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some options:

  • Millbrook Resort: A luxurious spa and golf hotel located in Arrowtown, 20-minutes away from Queenstown.

  • Hotel St. Moritz Queenstown – MGallery Collection: This 5-star hotel is located in the centre of town, on the waterfront.

  • Bumbles Backpackers: This popular hostel is located in the heart of Queenstown and offers great budget-friendly options.

What to Buy in Queenstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Queenstown offers a variety of unique and locally made souvenirs, such as:

  • Pounamu: A type of greenstone or jade found only in New Zealand. It is a significant part of Maori culture and craftsmanship.

  • Manuka Honey: Delicious honey produced from the Manuka Tree, famous for its healing properties

  • Fleece jackets: Queenstown has a pretty cold climate, so tourists could consider buying stylish fleece jackets from the local market.

Local Culture and Customs of Queenstown

The Maori culture is an essential part of New Zealand’s identity, and Queenstown is no exception. Be sure to respect their culture and customs, such as the traditional greeting “Kia Ora” and listening to narrative song and dance performances.

Best Time to Visit Queenstown

Queenstown is a popular destination year-round, but the best time to visit depends on what type of holiday you are looking for. The summer months (December - February) are great for outdoor adventures and water sports. The winter months (June - August) offer some of the best skiing and snowboarding conditions in the world.

Do’s and Don’ts of Queenstown

Do:

  • Be respectful of the locals and their customs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take a ride on the Skyline Gondola.
  • Explore Queenstown’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture.
  • Ignore safety warnings.
  • Underestimate the power of nature.

Safety and Health

Queenstown is known for its adrenaline-pumping experiences, but safety always comes first. Always follow instructions and guidelines for any activity or tour you are taking part in.

Safety in Queenstown

For emergency services, dial 111. Queenstown has low crime rates, so tourists should feel safe walking around the city at night. However, it’s still important to be aware of your surroundings and use common sense.

Health in Queenstown

Queenstown has excellent healthcare facilities, including a large public hospital and a medical centre that’s open 24 hours a day in the city centre.

Getting to Queenstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Queenstown

Queenstown Airport is located just a 10-15 minute drive from the town centre and has flights to major cities in Australia and New Zealand.

Bus Stations Near Queenstown

Intercity buses run between major cities in New Zealand, and Nakedbus offers a more budget-friendly option. Local buses are also available to get around Queenstown and its neighbouring towns.

Other Options

Queenstown is well-connected to other major New Zealand destinations by road. The best way to experience the city’s natural beauty is by renting a car or motorhome.

How to Get Around in Queenstown

The best way to get around Queenstown is by car. Alternatively, taxis, Uber, and local buses are also available to get around the town outskirts and neighbouring towns.

Where to Go Next from Queenstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Queenstown is located in a prime location for a range of destinations and excursions. Here are some options:

  • Wanaka: Head 1-hour northwest from Queenstown to enjoy the pristine Lake Wanaka, a popular destination for watersports and hikes.

  • Te Anau: Visit the famous Fiordland National Park, located a 3-hour drive from Queenstown.

Conclusion

Queenstown is a must-visit destination for those looking for natural wonders and outdoor adventure. The town’s scenic beauty, adventure activities, vibrant nightlife, and exceptional dining scene make it a paradise for travellers. We hope this guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Queenstown.
