Introduction to Civic

Civic is Canberra’s central business district located in the Australian Capital Territory in Oceania. It is a bustling area with many attractions, including museums, galleries, parks, sophisticated restaurants, and vibrant nightlife. Planning a trip to Civic can be overwhelming, but this guide will help you explore this part of Canberra with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Civic

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Australia: This museum offers an insight into Australia’s cultural and historical heritage. It houses a wide range of exhibits, including the “First Australians” gallery, a unique showcase of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander culture and their stories.
  • Australian War Memorial: It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Canberra, with exhibitions displaying the stories of Australian soldiers and their contributions to major wars and peacekeeping missions.
  • Questacon: Questacon is the National Science and Technology Centre, which offers interactive exhibits and displays to engage visitors of all ages in science and technology principles.

Natural Attractions of Civic: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Burley Griffin: It is a man-made lake located in the heart of Canberra, offering stunning views of surrounding hills and parks. You can take a leisurely walk or bike ride along the lake’s shorelines or take a boat ride to see the sights from the water.
  • Mt. Ainslie: Mt. Ainslie is a prominent peak located right behind the Parliament House, offering incredible panoramic views of Canberra city from the summit. You can hike the trail to the top of the mountain, and it is best done at sunrise or sunset for picture-perfect views.
  • Australian National Botanic Gardens: The botanic gardens are spread over 90 hectares, offering a unique opportunity to explore diverse plant species from all over Australia. The gardens have different walking paths and trails that take you through different ecosystems, such as the rainforest gully and the rock garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Civic

Civic has a vast network of bike and hiking trails crisscrossing throughout the city. Some of the most popular trails include

  • Centenary Trail: The Centenary trail is a 145km loop trail that runs through the heart of Canberra and its suburbs. It is designed for trail runners, mountain bikers, and bushwalkers and offers a unique opportunity to explore the city’s natural beauty.
  • Mount Majura Walking Trails: Mt. Majura offers several hiking trails for all levels of fitness, ranging from easy strolls to challenging walks. The walking trails are well-marked, and you can even take your furry friends along.
  • Lake Burley Griffin Circuit Trail: The Lake Burley Griffin Circuit Trail is one of the most popular cycling and walking trails in Canberra. The trail is approximately 28km long and takes you around Lake Burley Griffin, offering stunning views of the city’s landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Civic: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a Hot Air Balloon ride over Canberra to get a bird’s-eye view of the city’s landmarks, including Parliament House, Lake Burley Griffin, and Mt. Ainslie. The views from the hot air balloon are breathtaking, especially at sunrise or sunset.
  • Segway Tour: Segway tours offer a unique and innovative way to explore Civic. You can glide effortlessly around the city, covering more ground than on foot, while listening to interesting commentary about the city’s history and landmarks.
  • Nightlife: Civic is famous for its vibrant nightlife. There are numerous pubs, bars, and clubs in the city that offer live music, stand-up comedy, and other entertainment options.

Week-Long Itinerary for Civic

Day 1: Visit the National Museum of Australia and Questacon

Day 2: Explore the National Gallery of Australia and the Australian War Memorial

Day 3: Hike to the top of Mt. Ainslie for panoramic views of the city

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride or a segway tour

Day 5: Explore the Australian National Botanic Gardens or the Royal Australian Mint

Day 6: Take a leisurely stroll around Lake Burley Griffin or rent a bike and take a ride on the cycling trails

Day 7: Indulge in the vibrant nightlife of Civic

Food and Drink Scene of Civic

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peking Duck: Peking Duck is an authentic Chinese dish cooked until crispy, served with pancakes or steamed buns, and sauces. You can try this dish at the Asian restaurants in Civic.
  • Fish and Chips: Australia is famous for its fish and chips, and you can try this dish at the many cafes scattered across the city.
  • Canberra Truffle: Canberra is renowned for its truffle, and you can try this delicacy at the various restaurants across the city.

Local Beverages

  • Microbreweries beer: Canberra has an excellent microbrewery scene, offering a wide range of local beers made by unique brewers.
  • Wine: Canberra is a cool-climate wine region, and some of Australia’s best wines are made right in Civic. There are several vineyards offering wine-tasting tours.

Where to Stay in Civic: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Civic has an array of accommodation options to suit all budgets.

  • Hotels: Some of the premium hotels in Civic are Hyatt Hotel Canberra, Crowne Plaza Canberra, and Novotel Canberra
  • Hostels: Some popular hostels in Civic include YHA, Canberra City, and the Civic Hotel.
  • Guesthouses: Travellers can also choose to stay in guesthouses like the Astra Apartments, the Avenue Hotel, and the Vibe Hotel Canberra

What to Buy in Civic: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Canberra Wines: Canberra is famous for its cool-climate wines, and you can bring some of them home as souvenirs.
  • Aboriginal Art: Civic has several galleries where you can find authentic Aboriginal art made by the local artists.
  • Canberra Glass Works: Canberra has a budding glass-making scene, and you can purchase unique glassware from local glass shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Civic

Canberra is a cosmopolitan city, and people are quite friendly and welcoming. The local lifestyle reflects the city’s multiculturalism, where people from various backgrounds come together and celebrate their diversity.

Best Time to Visit Civic

The best time to visit Civic is during autumn (March to May) and spring (September to November) when the weather is pleasant. Summertime (December to February) can be quite hot, with temperatures reaching up to 30 degrees Celsius, while winters (June to August) can be chilly, with temperatures dropping up to 0 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Civic

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try local delicacies
  • Take a walk around the city and explore all the attractions in Civic

Don’t:

  • Litter the streets or parks
  • Smoke in public places
  • Disrespect national monuments or parks

Safety and Health

Safety in Civic

Civic has a low crime rate, and it is considered a safe city for tourists. However, it’s always best to take basic safety precautions when travelling in any foreign destination.

Health in Civic

Australians are health-conscious, and you can find several health food stores, organic shops, and vegetarian restaurants in Civic. It’s also advisable to carry a first-aid kit with you just in case.

Getting to Civic: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Civic

Canberra International Airport is located approximately 7.5 km from Civic, and you can take a taxi or a bus to reach Civic.

Bus Stations Near Civic

Civic has a central bus interchange where all the bus services operate to and from other parts of Canberra.

Other Options

You can also book a private car or take a shared shuttle bus service from the airport to reach Civic.

How to Get Around in Civic

Civic is a compact city, and you can explore most of it by walking. However, you can rent a bike or take a bus if you need to travel further.

Where to Go Next from Civic: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations to visit near Civic are:

  • Tidbinbilla Nature Reserve: It’s located approximately 45 minutes from Civic. The nature reserve offers nature walks, wildlife encounters, and even a Space Tracking Station which is part of NASA’s global deep space network.
  • Snowy Mountains: The Snowy Mountains are located approximately three hours away from Civic, off-trail mountaineering activities, and skiing.
  • Blue Mountains: The Blue Mountains are located approximately four hours away from Civic. They offer majestic views, hiking trails, and natural escapes.

Conclusion

Civic is a vibrant and bustling city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty.
