Introduction to Gondwana Rainforests of Australia

Gondwana Rainforests of Australia is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that covers over 366,000 hectares across the states of New South Wales and Queensland. The region is home to some of the oldest and most diverse subtropical rainforests in the world with a history that dates back to over 200 million years ago. In this travel guide, we will provide a comprehensive tour of the Gondwana Rainforests of Australia, including attractions, activities, food, and accommodation options, as well as travel tips and safety advice.

Best Time to Visit Gondwana Rainforests of Australia

The best time to visit Gondwana Rainforests of Australia is during the autumn months from March to May or during the spring between September and November when the weather is mild and the rainwater makes the waterfalls even more spectacular.
