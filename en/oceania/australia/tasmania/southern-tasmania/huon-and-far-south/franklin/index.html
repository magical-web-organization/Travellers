Introduction to Franklin

Located in Southern Tasmania, Franklin is a charming town with a rich history and unbeatable natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or looking to explore Tasmania’s cultural side, Franklin has something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ve gathered the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Franklin.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Franklin Museum: Learn about the early settlers and the history of the town at the Franklin Museum. The museum has a collection of photographs, documents, and artifacts from the past.
  • Palais Theatre: This historic theatre was built in 1914 and still hosts movie screenings, live music performances, and events.
  • St. John the Evangelist Roman Catholic Church: A beautiful church with stunning stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Franklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Franklin River Cruise: Take a scenic river cruise along the Franklin River and enjoy breathtaking views of the waterfalls, gorges, and wilderness.
  • Mount Wellington: A perfect spot for hiking and mountain biking, Mount Wellington offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding areas.
  • Hartz Mountains National Park: A great spot for a day trip, Hartz Mountains National Park features scenic hikes, waterfalls, and a diverse range of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin

  • Huonville to Franklin Trail: A 14km bike trail that takes you along the Huon River to the town of Franklin.
  • Franklin Foreshore Walk: A scenic walk along the Franklin waterfront that offers beautiful views of the Huon River.
  • Hartz Mountains Summit Walk: A challenging hike that rewards you with stunning panoramic views from the summit.

Best Things to Do in Franklin: Activities and Tours

  • Franklin Market: Held every Sunday, the Franklin Market is a great place to shop for local produce, crafts, and souvenirs.
  • Huon Valley Scenic Drive: A picturesque drive that takes you past apple orchards, vineyards, and rolling hills.
  • Taste of the Huon: An annual festival that showcases the best of the Huon Valley’s produce, wine, and cider.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Franklin Museum, Palais Theatre, and St. John the Evangelist Roman Catholic Church. Enjoy a scenic river cruise along the Franklin River.
Day 2 Hike the Hartz Mountains Summit Walk.
Day 3 Take the Huon Valley Scenic Drive to the Tahune Airwalk and enjoy stunning views from the treetop walkway.
Day 4 Explore the Huon River on a kayaking or fishing tour.
Day 5 Visit local wineries and taste some of the finest wines in Tasmania.
Day 6 Take a day trip to the Bruny Island and explore the island’s natural beauty.
Day 7 Shop at the Franklin Market and try some local produce at the Taste of the Huon festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huon Valley Mushrooms: The Huon Valley is known for its variety of mushrooms, which are often featured in local dishes.
  • Tasmanian Seafood: Try some of Tasmania’s freshest seafood, including oysters, abalone, and salmon.
  • Apple Cider: The Huon Valley is Australia’s apple capital, and the cider produced here is exceptional.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Wines: Tasmania’s cool climate produces some of the finest wines in Australia, including Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Riesling.
  • Tasmanian Whisky: Tasmania has a growing reputation for its whisky, with several distilleries producing award-winning spirits.

Where to Stay in Franklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Franklin Manor: A beautifully restored historic mansion that features elegant rooms with modern amenities and a restaurant that serves delicious local cuisine.
  • Huon Valley Eco-Wilderness Retreat: A stunning eco retreat that offers visitors a unique experience in a peaceful and secluded location.
  • Riverfront Motel and Villas: A family-friendly accommodation that offers comfortable rooms with river views and amenities such as a pool and BBQ area.

What to Buy in Franklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huon Pine Products: The Huon Pine is a rare and valuable timber that is only found in Tasmania. You can find a variety of Huon Pine products, including bowls, cutting boards, and furniture.
  • Franklin Apple Products: The Franklin area is known for its high-quality apples, and you can find apple cider, apple juice, and apple products such as jams and chutneys.
  • Local Crafts: The Franklin Market is a great place to find handmade crafts such as knitted items, jewelry, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin

The local culture in Franklin is deeply rooted in its history and natural beauty. The town has a thriving arts community, with several galleries, concert venues, and events that showcase local talent. The Huon Valley is also known for its friendly locals who are always happy to share their knowledge and experiences with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Franklin

The best time to visit Franklin is from December to February when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also peak season, so be sure to book your accommodation and tours in advance. March to May and September to November are also great times to visit, with milder weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin

Do:

  • Do take your time to explore the natural beauty of the Huon Valley.
  • Do visit the Franklin Market to sample local produce and crafts.
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, especially if you plan on hiking or outdoor activities.
  • Don’t expect big-city nightlife. Franklin is a small town known for its quiet and peaceful atmosphere.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin

Franklin is a relatively safe town, but be aware of weather conditions and potential hazards in outdoor activities. Follow safety guidelines and be prepared for emergencies while hiking, cycling, or kayaking. Also, make sure to lock your doors and windows at night.

Health in Franklin

There are no major health risks in Franklin, but it’s always a good idea to carry a small first aid kit and any necessary medications. The nearest hospital is in Huonville, about a 10-minute drive from Franklin.

Getting to Franklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Franklin is approximately a 40-minute drive from Hobart, Tasmania’s capital city.

Airports Near Franklin

The nearest airport to Franklin is Hobart International Airport, which is approximately 47 km away.

Bus Stations Near Franklin

There are no bus stations in Franklin, but regular bus services run from Hobart to nearby towns such as Huonville.

Other Options

Car rentals are available at Hobart International Airport and in Hobart city center. Taxis and shuttle services are also available for transportation to and from Franklin.

How to Get Around in Franklin

The best way to get around Franklin is by walking or cycling. The town is small and easy to navigate, and there are several bike rental companies in the area. Car rentals are also available for exploring the surrounding areas and day trips.

Where to Go Next from Franklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hobart: Tasmania’s capital city is a must-visit destination that offers a blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures.
  • Bruny Island: A beautiful island that features stunning beaches, wildlife, and fresh seafood.
  • Port Arthur: A historical site that offers a glimpse into Tasmania’s convict past.

Conclusion

Franklin is a hidden gem in Tasmania that offers a unique blend of history and natural beauty. From scenic river cruises to challenging hikes, there are plenty of activities to keep you busy. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or seeking adventure, Franklin won’t disappoint.
