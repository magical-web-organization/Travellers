Southport, a small town located in Southern Tasmania, is a hidden gem nestled within the Huon and Far South region. Known for its stunning landscapes, cultural landmarks, and warm hospitality, Southport has much to offer for tourists seeking an authentic Tasmanian experience. In this article, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, restaurants to try, and where to stay in Southport, as well as important travel information to help you plan your trip.

Introduction to Southport

Southport is a picturesque seaside town located at the southern end of the Huon Valley. Surrounded by lush green forests, rolling hills, and pristine beaches, this small town boasts a rich cultural history and natural wonders that attract visitors from all over the world.

Southport’s history dates back to the early 1800s when the area was home to the first European settlers in Tasmania. Today, Southport has become an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those interested in experiencing Tasmania’s unique heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Southport

From historical landmarks to natural wonders, Southport has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the best places to visit in Southport.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Southport Convict Station: A historical site that dates back to the 1830s, the Southport Convict Station was once a bustling hub for convicts and workers who worked on the construction of the nearby road and bridge. Today, it’s a popular tourist attraction where visitors can learn about Tasmania’s convict history.
  • Ida Bay Railway: A quaint and charming railway station that has been transporting passengers through Tasmania’s lush wilderness since 1922. The Ida Bay Railway offers visitors a unique experience that combines scenic views with a dose of history.
  • Tahune AirWalk: A breathtaking elevated walkway built among the treetops that offers stunning views of the surrounding forest. The Tahune AirWalk is a highlight of any trip to Southport and a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Natural Attractions of Southport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hasting Caves and Thermal Springs: Located just a short drive from Southport, the Hasting Caves and Thermal Springs are a natural wonder that can only be found in Tasmania. Visitors can explore the caves, swim in the thermal pool, and take a guided tour of the area.
  • Southport Lagoon Conservation Area: A vast area of unspoiled wilderness, the Southport Lagoon Conservation Area is perfect for hiking, camping, and fishing. The area is also home to a variety of rare birds and wildlife, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.
  • Cockle Creek and Recherche Bay: A scenic coastal drive that offers stunning views of the ocean and Tasmanian wilderness, the Cockle Creek and Recherche Bay route is a must-see destination for photographers and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southport

Southport has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape and wilderness. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Hartz Mountains National Park: A popular hiking destination that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness and landscape. The park has a variety of trails that are suitable for hikers of all levels.
  • Southport Bluff Walk: A scenic coastal walk that offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape. The Southport Bluff Walk is perfect for hikers who want to explore the area’s natural beauty.
  • Southport to Dover Rail Trail: A popular biking route that follows the path of an old railway line. The Southport to Dover Rail Trail is perfect for cyclists of all levels and offers scenic views of the surrounding wilderness and landscape.

Best Things to Do in Southport: Activities and Tours

Southport has an array of activities and tours to suit every traveler’s interest. Here are some of the best things to do in Southport:

  • Tasting Local Wines: Southport is home to several vineyards, and many offer wine tasting tours that allow visitors to sample local wines and learn about the winemaking process.
  • Fishing Charters: Fishing charters are popular activities in Southport, with many operators offering tours that take visitors to the best fishing spots in the region.
  • Wildlife Tours: Southport and its surrounding area are home to several unique species of wildlife, including the Tasmanian Devil. Wildlife tours are an excellent way to experience the area’s natural beauty and learn about the local fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Southport

Southport offers a range of dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Southport is situated along the coastline, making it an excellent destination for seafood lovers. Local classics include fish and chips, oysters, and abalone.
  • Local Produce: Tasmania is known for its fresh produce, and local restaurants take advantage of the abundance of locally-sourced ingredients. Be sure to try local favorites such as the Tasmanian cheese platter and the slow-roasted lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Tasmania has become known for its wines, with several vineyards located in the region. Be sure to try local wines such as Pinot Noir and Chardonnay.
  • Beer: Tasmania has a growing craft beer scene, with several breweries located in the region. Be sure to sample local favorites such as the Moo Brew Pilsner and Cascade Premium Lager.

Where to Stay in Southport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southport has several accommodation options to suit every traveler’s needs and budget. Here are some recommended places to stay in Southport:

  • Far South Wilderness Camp: A luxury camping experience that offers guests the opportunity to stay in eco-friendly tents situated in the heart of the Southport wilderness.
  • Southport Hotel: A charming historic hotel located in the heart of the town, offering guests comfortable rooms and a warm Tasmanian welcome.
  • Dover Beachside Tourist Park: A picturesque holiday park located just a short drive from Southport, offering guests a range of accommodation options and great facilities.

What to Buy in Southport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Southport can purchase a range of locally made products and souvenirs, including:

  • Wooden Crafts: Southport is home to several talented woodworkers, and visitors can purchase locally made wooden bowls, spoons, and other crafts.
  • Local Produce: Visitors can purchase locally sourced fruit, vegetables, and other food products from the nearby markets and stores. Be sure to try locally made cheeses and jams.
  • Tasmanian Whiskey: Tasmania has a growing whiskey industry, with several distilleries located in the region. Whiskey lovers can purchase locally made whiskey as a souvenir or gift to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Southport

Southport, like the rest of Tasmania, has a unique culture and heritage that is reflected in its people and traditions. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Sustainable Living: Southport is home to several eco-friendly initiatives and is committed to sustainable living, with many locals adopting eco-conscious practices in their daily lives.
  • Cultural Heritage: Southport has a rich cultural heritage, with many locals proud of their Celtic heritage and traditions. This is reflected in the town’s festivals and celebrations throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Southport

The best time to visit Southport is during the summer months (December to February), when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities available. However, Southport can be visited year-round, with each season offering unique experiences and activities for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southport

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Southport:

  • Take advantage of the stunning natural landscape and participate in outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Safety and Health

Southport is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling in unfamiliar areas, such as keeping valuables out of sight and staying alert.

Safety in Southport

  • Be cautious when swimming at remote beaches or near rocks and reefs.
  • Stay on designated hiking trails and wear appropriate footwear when hiking.
  • Be mindful of wildlife and keep a safe distance.

Health in Southport

  • Tasmania has a clean water supply, and tap water is safe to drink.
  • Make sure to bring any necessary medications and travel insurance.
  • Be sure to apply sunscreen and wear appropriate clothing to protect against the sun.

Getting to Southport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southport is located approximately 100 km south of Hobart, Tasmania’s capital. Here are some transportation options for getting to Southport:

Airports Near Southport

The nearest airport to Southport is Hobart International Airport, which offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Southport

There are several local bus services that connect Southport to nearby towns and cities, including Hobart.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from Hobart to Southport. It’s important to note that some of the roads in Southport and the surrounding area are narrow and winding, so drivers should take caution when driving in the area.

How to Get Around in Southport

Southport is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, some of the surrounding areas and attractions may require a car or other transportation options. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi or join a guided tour to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Southport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southport is conveniently located near several popular destinations in Tasmania, making it an ideal starting point or stopping point for travelers. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Bruny Island: Just a short boat ride from Southport, Bruny Island offers stunning scenery, wildlife, and fresh produce. Visit local vineyards for wine tastings, explore the coastline, or go on a wildlife tour.
  • Port Arthur: A significant historical site located on the Tasman Peninsula, Port Arthur was once a convict settlement and is now a popular tourist destination. Take a guided tour to learn about Tasmania’s convict history and explore the area’s stunning natural beauty.
  • Hobart: Tasmania’s capital city, Hobart, is a bustling metropolis that offers visitors a range of attractions and activities. From art galleries and museums to fine dining and shopping, Hobart has something for everyone.

Conclusion

Southport is a unique destination that offers visitors a chance to experience Tasmania’s rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality. From historical landmarks to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in this charming seaside town. With this ultimate travel guide to Southport, you’ll be sure to have an unforgettable trip and create memories that will last a lifetime.
