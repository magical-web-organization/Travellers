Introduction to Geeveston

Geeveston, located in Southern Tasmania, is a charming town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. It is home to some of the best hiking trails, biking routes, and outdoor adventures in Tasmania, making it a popular tourist destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts. In addition to its outdoor attractions, Geeveston features several museums and historical landmarks, making it a perfect destination for culture buffs as well. In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Geeveston, to help you plan your ultimate getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Geeveston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tahune Forest AirWalk: This is a must-visit attraction in Geeveston, which features a 600-meter steel walkway suspended 50 meters above the forest floor. It offers panoramic views of the Huon River and the forest canopy.

  • Forest and Heritage Centre: This museum explores the history of the timber industry in Tasmania. It features interactive exhibits, videos, and artifacts from the early logging days to present.

  • Geeveston Heritage Museum: This small museum showcases some of the town’s most significant historical artifacts, including photographs, documents, and machinery.

  • Hartzview Vineyard: This winery is renowned for producing high-quality wines from locally grown grapes. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyard and enjoy wine tastings and pairings.

Natural Attractions of Geeveston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hastings Caves and Thermal Springs: This reserve features a range of outdoor activities, including guided cave tours, walking trails, picnic areas, and hot thermal pools.

  • Hartz Mountains National Park: This park offers some of the best hiking trails in Tasmania, including the iconic Hartz Peak Summit and Lakes Track. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the mountains, valleys, and forests.

  • Ida Bay Railway: This train is one of the last operating bush railways in Tasmania, and it takes passengers on a scenic tour through the South West Wilderness.

  • Arve River Picnic Area: This beautiful picnic area is located alongside the Arve River and provides perfect picnic spots and fishing facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Geeveston

Geeveston is a popular destination for hikers and bikers, thanks to its vast wilderness and numerous trails. Below are some of the notable routes:

  • The Tahune Airwalk: This is a 1.6-kilometer walking trail that takes visitors through the towering eucalyptus trees of the forest.

  • Hartz Mountains National Park: This park contains a network of hiking trails of varying lengths and difficulty, including the Hartz Peak Summit, Lake Esperance Track, and Waratah Lookout Track.

  • Ida Bay Railway: The railway’s tracks, which go through some stunning forest and coastal scenery, make it an ideal spot for mountain biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Geeveston: Activities and Tours

  • Farm and Winery Tours: Visitors can enjoy wine tastings, vineyard tours, and sampling local produce such as honey and cheeses at Geeveston farms and vineyards.

  • The Huon Trail: This scenic drive takes visitors through the best of Huon and Far South, such as the historic towns of Franklin, Cygnet, and the Huon River.

  • Huon Jet Boat: This is an exhilarating boat ride through the Huon River rapids, allowing visitors to see and experience the area’s powerful natural forces.

Week-Long Itinerary for Geeveston

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Geeveston and check-in at the hotel. Relax at the hotel pool or spa.
2 Explore the Tahune AirWalk and experience panoramic views of the Huon River and the surrounding forest.
3 Spend a day at Hastings Caves and Thermal Springs, go underground on a guided tour to the Newdegate Cave, and unwind in the hot thermal pool.
4 Hike the Hartz Mountains National Park and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the mountains and valleys of Tasmania.
5 Take a guided wine tour of local vineyards and farms.
6 Go on a thrilling jet boat ride on the Huon River rapids.
7 Check-out of the hotel and head to nearby destinations and excursions such as Bruny Island, located just 50 km south of Geeveston.

Food and Drink Scene of Geeveston

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scallop pies: This is a famous Tasmanian dish that features fresh scallops baked into a meat pie.

  • Bruny Island Cheese: This artisan cheese is made in nearby Bruny Island and is known for its unique flavors and textures.

  • Huon Salmon: This is a local delicacy that is famous for its freshness and flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Wine: Tasmanian wineries are famous for producing high-quality cool-climate wine, including Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Riesling.

  • Tasmanian Whisky: Tasmania has several distilleries that produce award-winning whiskies, including Lark Distillery and Sullivans Cove Distillery.

Where to Stay in Geeveston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Kermandie Hotel: This historic hotel features 10 comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar with regular live music.

  • Forestry House: This modern and stylish hostel offers both private and shared accommodation and is located within walking distance of Geeveston’s town center.

  • The Wooden Boat Centre: This guesthouse is unique and rustic and offers a beautiful waterfront location and traditional wooden boat building classes.

What to Buy in Geeveston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Geeveston Fruit and Veggie Market: This local market offers a variety of fresh and local produce, including fruits and vegetables, honey, and cheese.

  • Geeveston Woodcrafts: This store features handcrafted wooden items, including bowls, plates, and other homeware.

Local Culture and Customs of Geeveston

Geeveston is a small and friendly community, and visitors will find the locals to be warm and welcoming. Some of the local customs include:

  • Saying hello to passersby, even if they are strangers.

  • Showing respect for the natural environment and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Geeveston

The best time to visit Geeveston is during the summer months, from December to February when the weather is warm, and the days are longer. However, outdoor activities can be enjoyed all year round, and Geeveston is beautiful in spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) when the foliage changes color.

Do’s and Don’ts of Geeveston

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Keep the environment clean and tidy.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.

  • Disturb local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Geeveston

Geeveston is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take common-sense precautions such as orienting themselves to the area and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Geeveston

Geeveston has a medical center and pharmacy, and visitors should bring any necessary medications and consult with their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Geeveston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Geeveston

The nearest airports to Geeveston are located in Hobart, which is approximately 60 kilometers away. There are regular flights to Hobart from major Australian cities such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.

Bus Stations Near Geeveston

There are regular bus services to Geeveston from Hobart and other towns in Southern Tasmania. Visitors can book bus tickets online or at the bus stations.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors who want to explore the area at their own pace. Visitors can rent a car at Hobart airport or the city center.

How to Get Around in Geeveston

Geeveston is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bicycle. Tourists can rent bicycles from several local tour operators.

Where to Go Next from Geeveston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bruny Island: This beautiful nature reserve is located south of Geeveston and offers stunning landscapes, beaches, and wildlife.

  • Port Arthur Historic Site: This historic site is located on the Tasman Peninsula and features several historic structures, ruins, and artifacts.

  • Huon Valley: This valley is a beautiful region full of vineyards, farms, and forests.

Conclusion

Geeveston, with its rich and diverse culture, natural beauty, and warm hospitality, is a perfect getaway destination for nature lovers and culture buffs alike. With its numerous attractions, activities, and accommodation options, Geeveston is a must-visit destination for a memorable vacation.
