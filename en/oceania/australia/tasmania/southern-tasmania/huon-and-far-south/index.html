Introduction to Huon and Far South

Huon and Far South is a picturesque region located in Southern Tasmania, Australia. Renowned for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, this area is a must-visit destination for travelers. From breathtaking natural wonders to delicious local cuisine and exciting outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in Huon and Far South.

List of Places to Visit in Huon and Far South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to experience the rich culture and heritage of Huon and Far South is by visiting its museums and historical sites. Some of the most popular attractions in this category include:

  • The Tahune Airwalk
  • The Hastings Caves and Thermal Springs
  • The Huon Valley Apple and Heritage Museum
  • The Ida Bay Railway

Natural Attractions of Huon and Far South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts and outdoor adventurers, there is no shortage of things to do and see in Huon and Far South. Here are some of the top natural attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Mount Wellington
  • Recherche Bay Nature Reserve
  • Hartz Mountain National Park
  • South Bruny National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huon and Far South

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Huon and Far South, given its diverse landscapes and natural ecosystems. With well-maintained trails and scenic routes, there are options for all skill levels. Here are some top recommendations:

  • The Huon Trail
  • The Tahune Forest AirWalk
  • The South Cape Bay Track
  • The Bruny Island Lighthouse Walk

Best Things to Do in Huon and Far South: Activities and Tours

Looking for exciting activities and tours to add to your itinerary? Here are some of the best things to do in Huon and Far South:

  • Kayaking in the Huon River
  • Visiting local wineries and indulging in wine tasting
  • Horseback riding in the Huon Valley
  • Joining a guided cultural tour of local Aboriginal heritage sites

Week-Long Itinerary for Huon and Far South

If you only have a week to explore Huon and Far South, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1-2: Cycle the Huon Trail and visit the Huon Valley Apple and Heritage Museum. Day 3-4: Go underground at the Hastings Caves and Thermal Springs and take a dip in the warm natural pools. Day 5-6: Explore the wilderness of South Bruny National Park and visit local wineries or join a cultural tour. Day 7: Visit the Tahune Airwalk and enjoy panoramic views of the Huon River and southern forests.

Food and Drink Scene of Huon and Far South

No visit to Huon and Far South would be complete without indulging in the local food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Freshly caught seafood, such as abalone and crayfish
  • Huon Valley apples, in all their forms: apple cider, apple juice, and more
  • Tasmanian scallops, prepared with a variety of sauces and seasonings
  • The famous Bruny Island Cheese

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian wine, including the pinot noir, riesling, and chardonnay varietals
  • Tasmanian whiskey, made with the island’s high-quality barley and water

Where to Stay in Huon and Far South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, there are plenty of options for accommodation in Huon and Far South. Here are a few top choices:

  • The Kermandie Hotel and Marina
  • Driftwood Cottages
  • The Tahune Airwalk Lodge
  • The Bruny Island Beach House

What to Buy in Huon and Far South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and local products to take home with you? Here are a few top picks:

  • Handmade jewelry and crafts from local artisans
  • Local honey and preserves, made with Huon Valley apples and berries
  • Handcrafted wooden items, such as bowls and utensils
  • Tasmanian wool products, including blankets and clothing

Local Culture and Customs of Huon and Far South

When visiting Huon and Far South, it’s important to understand and respect the local culture and customs. Here are a few tips:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and heritage areas.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of local people or their homes.
  • Respect the wildlife and natural environment by following park rules and leaving no trace.

Best Time to Visit Huon and Far South

The best time to visit Huon and Far South is from December to February, when temperatures are warm and rainfall is low. However, the region’s natural beauty and cultural attractions are worth exploring year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huon and Far South

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by following park rules and leaving no trace.
  • Explore local culture and heritage through museums and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including seafood and Tasmanian wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm wildlife in any way.
  • Ignore cultural customs, especially when visiting religious sites or heritage areas.
  • Take unnecessary risks when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huon and Far South

Huon and Far South is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to take precautions when participating in outdoor activities and following park rules. In case of emergency, dial 000, Australia’s national emergency hotline.

Health in Huon and Far South

No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Huon and Far South. However, it’s important to take precautions against insect bites, especially during warmer months. Always carry sunscreen, wear a hat, and protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Huon and Far South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huon and Far South

The closest major airport to Huon and Far South is Hobart International Airport, which is approximately a 1.5-hour drive away. Car rental services are available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Huon and Far South

Huon and Far South is serviced by regular bus routes that connect to Hobart and other nearby destinations. Redline Coaches and Tassielink Transit operate in the region.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by ferry, Bruny Island Ferry connects the mainland to Bruny Island. Alternatively, you can hire a car in Hobart and drive to Huon and Far South.

How to Get Around in Huon and Far South

Car rentals and guided tours are the easiest ways to get around Huon and Far South, especially if you plan to visit multiple attractions. Cycling is also a popular way to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Huon and Far South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to expand your itinerary beyond Huon and Far South, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Launceston, Tasmania’s second-largest city
  • The Tasman Peninsula, known for its stunning coastline and historic sites
  • The Bay of Fires, a scenic coastal area renowned for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters

Conclusion

Huon and Far South is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking stunning natural scenery, rich history and culture, and exciting outdoor activities. With so much to see and do in this region, start planning your trip now to experience everything that Huon and Far South has to offer.
