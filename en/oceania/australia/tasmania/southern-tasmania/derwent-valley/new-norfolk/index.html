Introduction to New Norfolk

New Norfolk is a small charming town situated in the Derwent Valley, in Southern Tasmania. The town, founded in 1807, has a rich history with well-preserved colonial architecture that showcases its past as a major agricultural and trading center. Its location between the stunning Mount Field National Park and Hobart, the capital of Tasmania, makes it a popular stop for tourists looking to experience the best of Tasmania.

List of Places to Visit in New Norfolk

New Norfolk, although a small town, has a plethora of attractions that cater to different interests. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Shingle Shed - a museum that preserves the town’s agricultural and industrial history.
  • The National Trust’s Willow Court Historic Site - a former asylum that has now become an important heritage site.
  • The Anglican Church of St. Matthews - a 19th-century masterpiece that has been restored to its original grandeur.
  • The Derwent Valley Museum - a community-run museum that showcases the region’s heritage.

Natural Attractions of New Norfolk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt Field National Park - a stunning park that boasts several beautiful waterfalls, walking tracks, and wildlife.
  • The Styx Valley - a forest of giant eucalyptus trees that are among the tallest in the world.
  • The New Norfolk Esplanade - a picturesque riverside that offers stunning views and a perfect spot for picnics and barbeques.
  • The Derwent River - a beautiful river popular for kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Norfolk

New Norfolk has several scenic and exciting hiking and biking trails that provide visitors with an opportunity to discover the town’s diverse landscapes. Some amazing trails include:

  • The Waterfalls Circuit in Mt Field National Park.
  • The Styx Big Trees Walk that takes you through the beautiful forested areas.
  • The Derwent River Trail that takes you along the river and passes through lovely small towns.

Best Things to Do in New Norfolk: Activities and Tours

New Norfolk offers several activities that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best:

  • Take a tour of the historic Willow Court Asylum.
  • Visit the local markets that showcase local produce, crafts, and traditional foods.
  • Take a helicopter tour of the Derwent Valley and experience the stunning views from high up in the sky.
  • Go kayaking or fishing in the beautiful Derwent River.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Norfolk

Here’s a suggested itinerary for your week-long trip to New Norfolk:

Day 1 Visit the New Norfolk Esplanade and picnic along the river.

Food and Drink Scene of New Norfolk

New Norfolk has a fantastic food and drink scene that is sure to impress foodies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scallop pies - a traditional Tasmanian pastry filled with scallops.
  • Trout - a delicious freshwater fish cooked in various styles.
  • Grilled lamb chops - a popular dish that features tender and succulent lamb chops grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian wine - Tasmania’s wine region has been gaining international recognition for producing some of the world’s best wines.
  • Tasmanian whisky - Tasmania’s whiskies are known for their unique flavors and are produced using the pure water that flows from the island’s mountains.
  • Tasmanian beers - Tasmania’s microbreweries produce a range of beers that are slowly gaining popularity in Australia.

Where to Stay in New Norfolk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Norfolk has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best:

  • The Junction Motel - a charming motel situated close to the town center.
  • The Oast House - a cozy bed and breakfast that offers stunning views of the Derwent Valley.
  • The New Norfolk Hotel - a century-old hotel with well-appointed rooms and a popular on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in New Norfolk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

New Norfolk has several local products and souvenirs that will make for excellent gifts or mementos. Some of the best include:

  • Traditional Tasmanian handicrafts and arts.
  • Tasmanian honey and jams.

Local Culture and Customs of New Norfolk

New Norfolk has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and festivals. Some important aspects of the town’s culture and customs include:

  • The town’s colonial architecture.
  • The town’s appreciation of art and craftsmanship.
  • The town’s love for the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit New Norfolk

New Norfolk is beautiful throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during late spring and early autumn. During these periods, the weather is mild, and the foliage is stunning, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Norfolk

Do:

  • Be mindful of the town’s wildlife and natural beauty by using designated areas for smoking and disposal of trash.
  • Respect the town’s cultural and historical landmarks.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Feed the native animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Norfolk

New Norfolk is generally a safe town, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions like locking their doors, carrying valuables safely, and using designated parking areas.

Health in New Norfolk

New Norfolk has a good healthcare system, and visitors can expect good quality medical care if needed. However, it is always advisable to carry personal medical kits with essential medications.

Getting to New Norfolk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Norfolk

The closest airport to New Norfolk is the Hobart airport, which is a 40-minute drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near New Norfolk

There are no bus stations in New Norfolk, but regular bus services run from Hobart to New Norfolk.

Other Options

The best way to explore New Norfolk is by car, and there are several car rental services available in Hobart.

How to Get Around in New Norfolk

New Norfolk is a small town that can be explored on foot. However, renting a car or bicycle is a good idea if you want to visit the places that are outside the town center.

Where to Go Next from New Norfolk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Norfolk is ideally located between Mount Field National Park and Hobart, making it an excellent stop for visitors planning to explore the region. Some other nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting include:

  • The Tasman Peninsula - home to several stunning coastal trails and the Port Arthur Historic site.
  • The Mount Wellington Park - a beautiful park with stunning views of Hobart.
  • The Huon Valley - a scenic area famous for its vineyards and orchards.

Conclusion

New Norfolk is a charming town that offers a range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions that cater to different interests. By following our comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful town and experience the best Tasmania has to offer.
