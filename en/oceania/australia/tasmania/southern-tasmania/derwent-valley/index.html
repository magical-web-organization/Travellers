Derwent Valley is a picturesque region located in Southern Tasmania, Australia. Nestled between the majestic Mount Field National Park and the bustling city of Hobart, this valley boasts breath-taking natural beauty, rich history and culture, and an array of outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, hiking trails, cultural landmarks, food and drink scene, accommodation options, and more.

Introduction to Derwent Valley

Derwent Valley is a popular destination for visitors seeking to explore Tasmania’s natural beauty, heritage and culture. Located just over an hour’s drive from Tasmania’s capital city of Hobart, the valley is known for its stunning landscapes, ancient forests, pristine waterways, and charming towns. The region is also steeped in a rich history, with a wealth of cultural and historical attractions that make it a must-visit destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Derwent Valley

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Derwent Valley:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Agrarian Kitchen Eatery & Store: A popular restaurant, cooking school, and market that showcases local produce and ingredients.
  • Willow Court Historic Site: A collection of historic buildings that were once a mental institution, now transformed into a cultural and heritage destination.
  • New Norfolk Heritage Walk: A self-guided walking tour that takes you through the historic buildings and landmarks in the town of New Norfolk.

Natural Attractions of Derwent Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Field National Park: Home to some of Tasmania’s most beautiful waterfalls, snow-capped peaks, and alpine vegetation, as well as the iconic Russell Falls.
  • Derwent River: A picturesque river that offers kayaking, canoeing, and fishing opportunities.
  • Styx Valley: Home to some of the tallest trees in the world, including the famous Swamp Gum.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Derwent Valley

Derwent Valley is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with many trails that cater to all experience levels. From easy walks to more challenging hikes, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Three Falls Track: A 5 km walk that takes you through lush forests and past three stunning waterfalls.
  • Mount Field East: A 16 km hike that offers stunning views of the valley and the Tasmanian wilderness.
  • Maydena Bike Park: A popular destination for mountain biking enthusiasts, with world-class trails and stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Derwent Valley: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Derwent Valley Half-Day Tour: A guided tour that takes you through the valley’s natural and cultural attractions.
  • Fly Fishing Tour: Experience fly fishing in the pristine rivers and streams of the Derwent Valley with a local guide.
  • Hot-Air Balloon Adventure: Take a hot-air balloon ride over the beautiful Derwent Valley and enjoy 360-degree views of the landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Derwent Valley

If you have a week to explore Derwent Valley, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore Mount Field National Park and the Three Falls Track
2 Visit Willow Court Historic Site and New Norfolk Heritage Walk
3 Take a fly fishing tour in the Derwent River
4 Go on a hot-air balloon adventure over Derwent Valley
5 Hike Mount Field East and enjoy scenic views
6 Discover local eateries and markets in New Norfolk and Agrarian Kitchen
7 Explore the Styx Valley and visit the famous Swamp Gum

Food and Drink Scene of Derwent Valley

Derwent Valley is known for its local produce, particularly fresh berries, apples, truffles, and honey. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Cider: Made from locally grown apples, Derwent Valley’s cider is a refreshing and crisp drink that is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Truffle Risotto: Tasmanian truffles are some of the best in the world, and the Truffle Risotto at Agrarian Kitchen Eatery & Store is a must-try.
  • Berry Picking: During the summer months, you can visit local farms and orchards to pick your own fresh berries.

Local Beverages

  • Cascade Brewery: One of Australia’s oldest breweries, Cascade is located in Hobart and offers a variety of beers, ciders, and ginger beers.
  • Lark Distillery: Tasmania’s first distillery, Lark is located in Hobart and produces premium whisky, gin, and vodka.
  • The Agrarian Kitchen Eatery & Store: If you’re looking for locally produced and organic food in Derwent Valley, this is the place to go.

Where to Stay in Derwent Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Derwent Valley offers a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels and guesthouses. Here are some suggestions:

  • The National Park Hotel: A historic hotel located near Mount Field National Park that is known for its friendly staff and comfortable rooms.
  • The New Norfolk Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of New Norfolk that offers stylish rooms and modern amenities.
  • Maydena Mountain Cabins: A range of cabins and chalets in a picturesque setting, perfect for a peaceful getaway.

What to Buy in Derwent Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Derwent Valley:

  • Tasmanian Honey: Derwent Valley is home to many beekeepers who produce honey that is known for its unique flavour and aroma.
  • Tasmanian Truffles: Known for their quality and flavour, Tasmanian truffles make a great gift for foodies and gourmet enthusiasts.
  • Handmade Glassware: Tasmania is well known for its skilled glassblowers, and you can find a range of handcrafted glassware in Derwent Valley.

Local Culture and Customs of Derwent Valley

The people of Derwent Valley are known for their hospitality and friendliness. The region has a rich history and culture, and locals are proud to share their traditions and heritage with visitors. Be sure to respect the local customs and traditions, and ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Best Time to Visit Derwent Valley

Derwent Valley can be visited at any time of the year, but the best time to visit is from December to March during the summer months. This is when the weather is warm and dry, and outdoor activities and tours are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Derwent Valley

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take your rubbish with you and dispose of it properly.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural environment by littering or damaging plants and wildlife.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Derwent Valley

Derwent Valley is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions such as locking their car doors and securing valuables. Visitors should also take caution when venturing into the wilderness, as weather conditions can change rapidly and mobile phone coverage may be limited.

Health in Derwent Valley

Visitors should ensure they have travel insurance and are up-to-date with their vaccinations before visiting Tasmania. The region has excellent medical facilities and emergency services, but visitors should bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Derwent Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Derwent Valley can be reached by car or public transport. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Derwent Valley

The nearest airport is Hobart International Airport, located around an hour’s drive from Derwent Valley.

Bus Stations Near Derwent Valley

The main bus station in Derwent Valley is located in New Norfolk.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or join a guided tour to explore Derwent Valley.

How to Get Around in Derwent Valley

Derwent Valley is best explored by car or bike. Visitors can also join guided tours or use public transport to get around.

Where to Go Next from Derwent Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit:

  • Bruny Island: A beautiful island located off the coast of Tasmania, known for its unspoiled beaches and wildlife.
  • Port Arthur: A historic site and former penal colony that offers a glimpse into Australia’s early colonial history.
  • Hobart: Tasmania’s capital city, known for its historic buildings, art galleries, and food scene.

Conclusion

Derwent Valley offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in this picturesque region of Tasmania. We hope our comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Derwent Valley and that you enjoy your visit.
