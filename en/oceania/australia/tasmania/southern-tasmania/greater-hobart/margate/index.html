Margate, a small town located in the Greater Hobart region of Southern Tasmania, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Its stunning natural landscapes, cultural diversity, and vibrant food scene make it a must-visit destination for travelers. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top things to do in Margate, where to stay, and how to get around. Keep reading to start planning your trip!

Introduction to Margate

Margate is located in the southernmost state of Australia - Tasmania. It is situated 20km south of Hobart and is home to approximately 2,000 residents. The town is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, unspoiled beaches, and rich culture. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical sites, Margate offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Margate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Margate has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical sites that provide insight into the town’s past. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit:

  • Margate Train is an iconic site offering visitors a glimpse into Tasmania’s past with the picturesque figurines dotting the steam train lines.
  • Margate Heritage Museum is home to a vast collection of artefacts and photographs that showcase Margate’s history.
  • Bruny Island’s heritage listed lighthouse offers panoramic views of the rugged coastline and is well worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Margate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the outdoors, Margate has a lot to offer. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, or just taking in the scenery, there is something for all adventurers.

  • Mount Wellington is a popular hiking spot that offers stunning views of Hobart city and the Derwent estuary.
  • The Margate Train offers not only a cultural experience but also a scenic route. Sit in any of the figurines on a steam train journey, as you take in Margate’s natural beauty.
  • The Peter Murrell Reserve is ideal for bushwalking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Margate

One of the best things to do in Margate is exploring the scenery while hiking or biking. Here are some top routes to check out:

  • The Shot Tower Heritage Circuit Trail is a 3.5 km circuit that takes you through the Peter Murrell Reserve while giving you panoramic views of Hobart.
  • The Snug Falls Track is a 1.7 km return journey where you’ll discover a picturesque waterfall.
  • Bruny Island’s Lighthouse Trail is a 2km moderate-level hike that provides a breathtaking outlook of the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Margate: Activities and Tours

There are numerous activities and tours to enjoy in Margate. Here are some of the best:

  • Visit Nutpatch Nougat and Chocolate and indulge in savory chocolate and gourmet treats.
  • Take a scenic drive around the Huon Valley, famous for its orchards and vineyards, and enjoy wine and cheese tasting.
  • Go horse riding through the lush natural wilderness and connect with the natural landscape.
  • Take the Pennicott Wilderness Journey and experience the scenery of Bruny Island while cruising Bruny Island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Margate

Day 1: Take a scenic drive around the Huon Valley and explore the natural orchards and vineyards.

Day 2: Take a steam train journey on the Margate Train, then head over to the Margate Heritage Museum for a glimpse into Margate’s history.

Day 3: Head to Mount Wellington and take a guided hiking trail.

Day 4: Take a wildlife tour and discover the natural beauty of Bruny Island.

Day 5: Go horse riding through Margate’s natural wilderness.

Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at the beach.

Day 7: Take a gourmet tour of the town’s popular chocolatiers and wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Margate

Margate’s food and drink scene is diverse and infused with unique local flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian oysters
  • Scallop pies
  • Grilled Tasmanian salmon
  • Battered flathead
  • Wallaby steak
  • Chicken parmigiana

Local Beverages

  • Cascade Beer, the oldest continually operating brewery in Australia
  • Local Wines, including Sauvignon Blanc and Pinot Noir.

Where to Stay in Margate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Margate offers an array of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Villa Howden is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Margate. It is perfect for travelers seeking a lavish stay with breathtaking views of North West Bay.
  • The Driftwood Shack Margate is a cozy yet elegant cottage that is perfect for couples looking for a romantic getaway.
  • Kookaburra Ridge Quad B&B is the perfect place for travelers looking to stay in a bed and breakfast setting.

What to Buy in Margate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Margate has a unique culture that is reflected in its local products. Here are some souvenirs and local products you can take home:

  • Huon Pine products
  • Leatherwood honey
  • Tasmanian wool products
  • Tasmanian pepperberry salts

Local Culture and Customs of Margate

Margate has a rich culture that is unique to Tasmania. The people of Margate are hospitable and welcoming to visitors. It is common to see festivals, art shows, and cultural events throughout the year. Visitors should respect the town’s customs and traditions while enjoying their stay.

Best Time to Visit Margate

The best time to visit Margate is between November and February. The weather is favorable, and there are numerous festivals and events during this time. However, visitors can still enjoy great weather during autumn, with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Margate

There are some cultural norms and customs to observe while visiting Margate:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and keep it clean.
  • Engage with the locals and learn more about their culture.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural surroundings.
  • Refer to the locals as Australian, rather than Tasmanian.
  • Overlook the town’s historical sites and museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Margate

Margate is generally a safe town, but visitors should be careful while hiking or exploring the outdoors. It is recommended to bring a map and take paths that are well-marked.

Health in Margate

Margate has several medical centers and hospitals where visitors can access medical care if they need it. It is always best to have travel insurance in case of any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Margate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Margate is well-connected to major towns and cities in Tasmania. Some transportation options include:

Airports Near Margate

  • Hobart International Airport (15km away)

Bus Stations Near Margate

  • Metro Tasmania offers bus services to Margate from Hobart’s city center.

Other Options

  • Car rental services provide another transportation option that allows visitors to explore more of the town’s surroundings at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Margate

Margate is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, renting a car or bike is also an option for travelers looking for more independence.

Where to Go Next from Margate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Margate provides an excellent base for exploring Tasmania’s southern regions in Hobart and the Huon Valley. Here are some nearby destinations to consider visiting:

  • Mount Field National Park
  • The Tahune AirWalk
  • Hobart City
  • Port Arthur Historic Site

Conclusion

Margate offers a unique experience for travelers who are looking for a chance to explore Tasmania’s stunning natural landscape while immersing themselves in the town’s rich culture. With numerous places to visit, activities, and events, there is no doubt that Margate is well worth a visit. Plan your trip today and get ready to fall in love with this charming town.
