Introduction to Bruny Island

Located in Southern Tasmania, Bruny Island is known for its stunning natural scenery, rich heritage, and acclaimed food and drink scene. The island is home to a welcoming community, with locals always eager to share their island paradise with visitors. If you’re planning a trip to Tasmania, make sure to add Bruny Island to your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Bruny Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bruny Island Lighthouse: This historical lighthouse was built in 1836 and provides visitors with stunning views of the island’s coastline.
  • Bruny Island History Room: This museum showcases the island’s history through artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  • Quarantine Station: This site was used to isolate new arrivals to Tasmania during the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Bruny Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Neck: A narrow strip of land that connects North and South Bruny Island, this area is famous for its stunning views and is home to a colony of little penguins.
  • Adventure Bay: A picturesque bay popular for swimming, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Cloudy Bay: A beautiful white sandy beach that provides a secluded spot for swimming and surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bruny Island

  • Cape Queen Elizabeth: This scenic hike offers breathtaking views of the rugged coastline and is home to diverse wildlife including eastern grey kangaroos and Australian fur seals.
  • South Bruny National Park: This expansive park offers a range of hiking trails, including the popular Labillardiere Peninsula and Fluted Cape hikes.
  • Bruny Island Cycleway: A scenic route that takes cyclists on a 35-kilometer loop of the island.

Best Things to Do in Bruny Island: Activities and Tours

  • Bruny Island Cruises: A top-rated wildlife and wilderness tour that takes visitors to see the island’s stunning scenery and wildlife including seals, dolphins, and sea eagles.
  • Bruny Island Berry Farm: A family-friendly farm that offers strawberry picking and other activities.
  • Bruny Island Premium Wines: A vineyard that produces award-winning wines, which visitors can taste in the cellar door.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bruny Island

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore North Bruny Island and visit the Neck lookout
Day 2 Hike to Cape Queen Elizabeth and catch the stunning views of the coastline
Day 3 Enjoy a Bruny Island Cruise to see seals, dolphins, and eagles
Day 4 Visit the Bruny Island Berry Farm or join a fishing tour
Day 5 Discover the South Bruny National Park and the Labillardiere Peninsula hike
Day 6 Indulge in the island’s food and drink scene and try some local delicacies
Day 7 Relax on a beautiful white sandy beach like Cloudy Bay before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Bruny Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: Bruny Island is home to some of Australia’s best oysters.
  • Cheese and cider: The island’s renowned cheese producers and cider makers offer some of the best tasting experiences to visitors.
  • Whisky: Moo Brew Distillery is famous for its single malt whisky that is made with local barley and water.

Local Beverages

  • Bruny Island Beer Co.: A craft brewery that produces a range of artisan beers.
  • Bruny Island Premium Wines: This vineyard produces award-winning wines, which visitors can taste in the cellar door.
  • House of Whisky: An extensive collection of Tasmania’s famous single malt whiskies are on offer at this rustic tasting room.

Where to Stay in Bruny Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Discovery Holiday Parks: A family-friendly option located in Adventure Bay.
  • The Bruny Island Lodge: Boasting stunning views, this lodge offers a luxury stay.
  • The Jetty House: A quaint guesthouse with rustic charm located in Alonnah.

What to Buy in Bruny Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bruny Island Cheese: A range of artisan cheeses made on the island.
  • Oysters: Freshly caught oysters from local farms.
  • Wooden crafts: Beautifully crafted wooden items made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Bruny Island

The island has a tight-knit community that is proud of its heritage and natural environment. Visitors are encouraged to be mindful of the island’s resources, including the unique wildlife and scenic landscapes. The locals are friendly and welcoming, making for an unforgettable experience.

Best Time to Visit Bruny Island

The best time to visit Bruny Island is during the summer months between December through to February. During this time, the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are abundant. However, visitors who prefer fewer crowds and cooler temperatures can visit Bruny Island during the shoulder season of March to April or October to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bruny Island

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Take the time to sample the island’s locally produced food and drink
  • Make sure to book ahead during peak season

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm wildlife
  • Light any fires in prohibited areas
  • Take any plants, animals or geological features from the island

Safety and Health

Safety in Bruny Island

Bruny Island is considered a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and few hazards. However, visitors should be mindful of the island’s rugged terrain and coastal areas, where rogue waves can occur.

Health in Bruny Island

There are limited medical facilities on the island, with the nearest hospital located in Kingston, close to Hobart. Visitors should ensure that they have ample medical insurance coverage before visiting the island.

Getting to Bruny Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bruny Island

The nearest airport to Bruny Island is Hobart International Airport, which is around 70 km from the island.

Bus Stations Near Bruny Island

The Redline coach service operates daily between Hobart and Kettering where visitors can catch a ferry to Bruny Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Kettering and take a car ferry to North Bruny Island or take a private boat charter service.

How to Get Around in Bruny Island

The island is best explored by car, with rental options available in Hobart and at the ferry terminal in Kettering. Visitors can also explore the island by bike or on foot, with many trails and routes available.

Where to Go Next from Bruny Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hobart: Tasmania’s capital city is just a short drive from Bruny Island and offers visitors a wealth of cultural attractions, food, and nightlife.
  • Mount Field National Park: A stunning park that offers visitors the chance to experience Tasmanian wilderness.
  • Port Arthur Historic Site: A UNESCO World Heritage-listed site that showcases Tasmania’s convict history.

Conclusion

Bruny Island is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Tasmania. Its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and acclaimed food and drink scene will make for an unforgettable experience. Visitors are encouraged to explore the island’s trails, enjoy the local cuisine, and take the time to immerse themselves in the island’s unique culture and customs.
