Introduction to Richmond

Nestled in the heart of Southern Tasmania, Richmond is a charming town that boasts of historical landmarks, natural wonders, and a burgeoning food and drink scene. The town’s strategic location makes it an easy stopover point when traveling from Hobart to other destinations in Tasmania. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, places to stay, and delicacies to taste in Richmond.

List of Places to Visit in Richmond

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Richmond is steeped in history, with many well-maintained colonial buildings, most of which date back to the early 1800s. Visitors interested in history will appreciate a visit to the Richmond Gaol, a historic prison that offers an immersive experience into the life of prisoners in the past. If you want to indulge in history and arts, visit the Old Hobart Town Model Village, a unique attraction that features a miniature replica of an 1800s Hobart town. For a peek into the Anglican Church in Tasmania, visit St. Luke’s Church, a beautifully preserved national heritage site.

Natural Attractions of Richmond: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For travelers who appreciate nature and scenic views, Richmond does not disappoint. Get panoramic views of the city and beyond by taking a climb up to the top of the Richmond Bridge, Australia’s oldest Roman arch bridge. A visit to the Coal River Valley Vineyards lets you enjoy a glass of wine, take in the rolling hills, and breathe in fresh air. Enjoy birdwatching and the serene atmosphere at the Orielton Lagoon, a Ramsar-listed wetland area that’s home to over 88 bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Richmond

Richmond is a perfect place for an active holiday. The town boasts of several trails that allow for biking and hiking tours. The Coal River Farm located in Cambridge is an excellent place for a family-friendly bike ride. The Richmond Village Loop Bike Ride provides a scenic view of the Richmond Bridge. For a relaxing hike, explore the five-kilometer long Coal Valley Trail that spans across the southern Tasmania wine region.

Best Things to Do in Richmond: Activities and Tours

Besides admiring the town’s beauty, Richmond offers plenty of activities and tours. Indulge in a spirit-tasting tour at the Old Kempton Distillery to taste some of Tasmania’s finest whiskey. The Richmond Bridge Climb provides an unforgettable view from atop the historical Richmond bridge. Take a stroll at the Richmond Botanical Gardens, botanical gardens filled with rich plant life endemic to Tasmania.

Week-Long Itinerary for Richmond

Day 1: Visit the Richmond Gaol, and discover the stories of Tasmanian convicts. Day 2: Explore the Coal River Valley Vineyards, and sample local Tasmanian wines. Day 3: Take a bike ride at the Coal River Farm in Cambridge, and experience a farmstay. Day 4: Indulge in some pancakes and scones at the Salamanca Market in Hobart, if it’s open on that day. Day 5: Take a guided tour of the historic town center of Richmond, and learn about the town’s unique history. Day 6: Visit the Richmond Bridge Climb, and take in the scenic view from atop Australia’s oldest Roman arch bridge. Day 7: Go on a Tasmanian devils feeding tour at the Bonorong Wildlife Sanctuary and Endangered Species Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Richmond

Must-Try Dishes

Richmond has a burgeoning food scene, with many restaurants and cafes that serve farm-to-table dishes. Try out the Van Bone’s Bakery’s famous Scallop Pie, a savory dish featuring Tasmanian scallops. Sample local cheese from the Wicked Cheese Company and the Bruny Island Cheese Company. For a quick bite, grab some of the Sorell Fruit Farm’s hand-picked farm fresh berries and ice cream.

Local Beverages

Richmond has an inclusive food scene with several local drinks perfect for every palate. Visit the Puddleduck Vineyard to taste a range of Tasmanian wines. Stop by Richmond Tasting House to try some local craft beers. Try the Tasmanian-made Gin at the last remaining distillery from the 1800s at the Lark Distillery.

Where to Stay in Richmond: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Richmond is filled with options for accommodation that suits your style and budget. If you’re looking for a unique experience, stay at the Mill House Cottage, a historic cottage that dates back to the mid-1800s. Alternatively, the Richmond Barracks is conveniently located near the town center and is perfect for budget-friendly accommodation. The richmond bread stall bed and breakfast, located in a historic bakery is a unique and luxurious experience.

What to Buy in Richmond: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Richmond, don’t forget to take a piece of it with you by exploring the local markets and shops. The Richmond Fruit and Veg Market offer farm-fresh fruits and vegetables. Pick up some genuinely Tasmanian souvenirs from the Richmond Village Stores. The Wicked Cheese Company’s shop offers perfect souvenir cheese selections.

Local Culture and Customs of Richmond

While in Richmond, respect and appreciate the local culture. Take note of the history that has shaped this southern Tasmanian town to what it is now. Be sure to visit the local markets to meet and engage with the residents and take part in the town’s community engagements.

Best Time to Visit Richmond

The best times to visit Richmond are between December - February and June - September. December - February is the Australian summer season, perfect for outdoor activities, sunny days, and a warm climate. June - September is the Australian winter season, perfect for indoor activities and comfortable, cool temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Richmond

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Visit the local markets and shops

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Disrespect historic landmarks and churches
  • Engage in activities that harm the local environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Richmond

Richmond is generally a safe town to explore. However, always exercise caution and keep valuables safe. Be aware of your surroundings, especially in dimly lit areas at night, such as parking lots.

Health in Richmond

Richmond has adequate medical facilities for minor health issues, including a hospital and a pharmacy. Medical insurance is advisable for visitors to Richmond.

Getting to Richmond: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Richmond

Richmond is about 30 km north of Hobart International Airport, which provides connections to other major Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Richmond

Several bus stations located in and around Hobart connect travelers to Richmond through various transport providers.

Other Options

Richmond is 25 minutes away from Hobart by car. In addition, there are several car rental services within Hobart.

How to Get Around in Richmond

You can walk around Richmond town, but it might not always be the most convenient option. Getting around by car is often the best option.

Where to Go Next from Richmond: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Richmond is near Hobart, the Tasman Peninsula, and the East Coast of Tasmania. Drive to Hobart and explore the city, or visit the Tasman Peninsula’s natural landscapes and wildlife. Take a trip to the east coast of Tasmania, hit the beaches or explore dormant volcanoes at Freycinet National Park.

Conclusion

Richmond is a town with a rich history, a beautiful natural landscape, and is home to a burgeoning food and drink scene. Whether you’re a lover of history or outdoor activities, Richmond, with its cultural and natural attractions, has something for you. We hope our comprehensive tour guide has been informative and helpful in planning your trip to Richmond, Tasmania, Australia.
