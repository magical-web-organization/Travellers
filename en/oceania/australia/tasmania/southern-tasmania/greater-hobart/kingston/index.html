Introduction to Kingston

Kingston, located in Greater Hobart in Southern Tasmania, is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. This picturesque town is situated just a few minutes’ drive from Hobart and is known for its stunning landscapes, exquisite food, and friendly locals.

Kingston offers a plethora of experiences for travelers, whether you’re looking to enjoy outdoor adventures, explore cultural attractions, or simply relax in a peaceful setting. Here’s a detailed guide to help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in Kingston.

List of Places to Visit in Kingston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kingborough Heritage Trail: Follow the heritage trail to explore the historical and cultural significance of Kingborough, from historic settlements and churches to picturesque landscapes and parks.
  • Kingston Beach: Stroll along the scenic beach and enjoy the breathtaking views of the ocean and the mountains.
  • Bruny Island: Take a day trip to the stunning Bruny Island, located just off the coast from Kingston, and discover its unspoiled natural beauty.
  • Bonorong Wildlife Sanctuary: Visit the wildlife sanctuary and get up close with many of Australia’s iconic animals, from kangaroos and koalas to Tasmanian devils.

Natural Attractions of Kingston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hobart Rivulet Walking Track: Take a leisurely walk along the Hobart Rivulet and enjoy the serene views of the rainforest.
  • Mount Wellington: Head to Mount Wellington and experience the scenic view of Hobart from the summit.
  • Waterfall Bay: Explore the picturesque Waterfall Bay, with its stunning waterfalls and beautiful scenery.
  • Tahune Forest Airwalk: Get an adrenaline rush while walking along the airwalk suspended over the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingston

  • Wellington Park: Hike along the walking trails of Wellington Park and experience the diverse flora and fauna of Tasmania.
  • Johnston’s Gorge: Take a day trip to Johnston’s Gorge and enjoy the stony path along the creek.
  • The Tasman Trail: Cycle along the remarkable Tasman Trail, which links numerous attractions throughout Tasmania.

Best Things to Do in Kingston: Activities and Tours

  • Whiskey Tours: Take a tour of the local whiskey distilleries in Tasmania and experience the rich history and taste of the local beverages.
  • Salmon Ponds: Visit the historic salmon ponds and fly-fish in one of the most picturesque places in Tasmania.
  • Cocktail Making Class: Enroll in a cocktail-making class and learn some new skills in a fun and relaxed atmosphere.
  • Farm-To-Table Experience: Enjoy a farm-to-table experience and taste the freshness of local produce and cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Kingston

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Tasmania is known for its fresh seafood, including oysters, salmon, and lobster.
  • Apple Cider: Try the locally produced apple cider and enjoy the fruity flavors.
  • Tasmanian Whiskey: Experience the unique taste of Tasmanian whiskey, which has a rich history and cultural significance.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Try the local beers which are made from Tasmania’s fresh water source.
  • Wine: Tasmania is famous for its vineyards, so try the Pinot Noir to experience the taste of Tasmania.

What to Buy in Kingston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tasmanian Honey: Tasmania’s unique environment produces flavorsome honey, so try the locally produced honey.
  • Handcrafted Goods: Tasmania is known for its exquisite handcrafted goods; thus, purchase some local handcrafted souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kingston

Kingston is a laid-back town that reflects the rich cultural heritage of Tasmania. The locals here are friendly and welcoming, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation. Observe the following customs while traveling in Kingston:

  • Dress warmly as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Bring a coat or jacket, even if traveling during the summers, as it can get cold at night.
  • Always carry cash as some places may not accept cards.
  • It’s customary to tip in a hotel or restaurant.
  • Smoking is prohibited in public transport and indoor public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Kingston

The best time to visit Kingston is during the summers, from December to February when the weather is favorable for outdoor activities. The temperatures are mild, and there’s less chance of rain. However, the fall season, which is from March to May, also offers a pleasant climate and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingston

Do:

  • Keep your phone charged as there may be limited charging stations available in remote areas.
  • Take care while swimming as the water can be cold.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring mosquito repellent, especially during the summers.
  • Make sure to carry water, especially during hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Always remember to dispose of your trash properly.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t engage in any outdoor activity without adequate training or precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kingston

Kingston is a safe place to visit, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions, such as:

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Walk-in well-lit areas, particularly at night.
  • Consider traveling with a group or using a guide service if you plan to hike or bike in remote areas.

Health in Kingston

Healthcare services in Kingston are of good quality, and visitors can access hospitals and clinics if necessary. However, visitors are advised to take the following precautions:

  • Always carry your prescribed medicine.
  • Wear appropriate clothing to protect yourself from the sun and cold.
  • Insect repellent is recommended to prevent insect bites.

Getting to Kingston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kingston

The nearest major airport to Kingston is the Hobart International Airport, located about 20 kilometers from Kingston. From there, you can either hire a car or use the public transport service to reach your accommodation.

Bus Stations Near Kingston

Kingston is connected to the public transport system of Tasmania, and there are multiple bus services available to explore the town and its neighboring areas.

Other Options

  • Car Hire: Hiring a car is an excellent option as it provides the flexibility to explore the town and its surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Kingston

Kingston is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. However, renting a car or taking a bus or taxi is the best option if you want to explore the surrounding areas or venture into the city.

Where to Go Next from Kingston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kingston is an excellent base for exploring Southwest Tasmania, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Some of the popular destinations are:

  • Richmond: Known for its beautiful colonial buildings and galleries showcasing local artwork.
  • Hobart: The capital city of Tasmania, renowned for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and iconic attractions such as Mount Wellington and Salamanca Market.
  • Port Arthur: A well-known destination that showcases the convict history of Tasmania, with its haunting ruins and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

Kingston is a picturesque town that showcases the natural beauty and rich cultural heritage of Tasmania. From hiking trails and scenic views to iconic landmarks and local cuisine, Kingston offers an abundance of attractions that cater to every traveler’s interest. By following this comprehensive guide, you’ll undoubtedly make the most of your time exploring Kingston and its nearby destinations.
