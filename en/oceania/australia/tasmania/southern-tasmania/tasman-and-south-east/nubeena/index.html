Introduction to Nubeena

Nestled in the scenic Tasman Peninsula in Southern Tasmania, Nubeena is a picturesque town that offers a blend of natural, cultural, and historical attractions. The town is home to various outdoor adventures, including hiking and biking trails, a thriving food and drink scene, and iconic landmarks that reflect the region’s rich history.

Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Nubeena has plenty to offer. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to do, see, and experience during your visit to this beautiful Tasmanian town.

List of Places to Visit in Nubeena

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nubeena is rich in history, and there are several must-visit cultural attractions in the area.

  • Port Arthur Historic Site: Explore the ruins of the old Port Arthur convict settlement, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Eaglehawk Neck: Marvel at the colorful sandstone formations on the Tasman Arch, and visit the Tasmanian Devil Conservation Park.
  • The Convict Trail: Take a walk along the Convict Trail and discover the hidden stories of Australia’s past.
  • The Wooden Boat Centre: Learn about traditional wooden boat-building techniques and see skilled craftsmen at work.

Natural Attractions of Nubeena: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nubeena is surrounded by some of Australia’s most breathtaking natural landscapes.

  • Cape Raoul: Hike the picturesque trail along Cape Raoul, where you’ll be rewarded with stunning views of the coast.
  • Tasman National Park: Visit the Tasman National Park and witness the natural beauty of the Tasmanian wilderness.
  • Remarkable Cave: Explore the sea caves along the Tasmanian coastline and dip your toes in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Three Capes Track: Take a multi-day hike along the Three Capes Track and experience the beauty of Tasmania’s wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Nubeena

If you’re a hiking or biking enthusiast, Nubeena has plenty of trails for you to explore.

  • The Cape Raoul Trail: a scenic hike that offers stunning views of the coast.
  • The convict trail: A hike through history, retracing the footsteps of the region’s convicts
  • The Three Capes Track: A multi-day hike through beautiful scenery with accommodation provided along the way.
  • The Tasman Arch: A short hike that ends with a view of the stunning sandstone formation.

Best Things to Do in Nubeena: Activities and Tours

Nubeena offers a range of activities and tours for all tastes and ages.

  • Tasman Island Cruise: Take a boat tour around the Tasmanian coastline and witness the beauty of the region’s marine life
  • Tasmanian Devil Conservation Park: Learn about Tasmania’s native wildlife, including the iconic Tasmanian devil
  • Coal Mines Historic Site: Explore the remnants of Tasmania’s first operational mine with a guided tour.
  • Sea Kayaking: rent a kayak and explore the beautiful shoreline from a different perspective

Week-Long Itinerary for Nubeena

Day 1: Arrive and check-in at your accommodation Day 2: Explore the Port Arthur Historic Site Day 3: Go on a hike along the Three Capes Track or Cape Raoul Day 4: Visit the Tasmanian Devil Conservation Park Day 5: Take a boat tour and explore the Tasmanian coastline Day 6: Discover the Wooden Boat Center and the Eagles Nest Day 7: Relax and soak up the picturesque Tasmanian scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Nubeena

Must-Try Dishes

Nubeena is a food lover’s paradise, and the town is known for its farm-to-table cuisine.

  • Fresh seafood at the Tasmanian Wild Seafood Adventures
  • Local cheese and wine tasting at the Bream Creek Farmers Market
  • Mescal inspired cocktails at the Habanero Mexican Bar
  • Wood-fired pizza at the Port Arthur Lavender Farm.

Where to Stay in Nubeena: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options in Nubeena to suit all budgets and lifestyles.

  • Lufra Hotel and Apartments: A historic hotel with ocean views
  • Norfolk Bay Convict Station: A restored convict station-turned boutique accommodation
  • Four Seasons Waterfront Villas: Self-contained villas with ocean views
  • The Mussel Boys: Tiny homes that cater to those who want a unique and eco-friendly experience.

What to Buy in Nubeena: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Nubeena with you by visiting these local shops and markets.

  • Bream Creek Farmers Market: Buy local produce including cheese, wine, chocolate, and honey.
  • Port Arthur Lavender Farm: Purchase handmade lavender soaps, candles, and skincare
  • Bangor Vineyard Shed: Get a bottle of wine and a stunning view of the vineyards
  • Dunalley Waterfront Cafe & Yacht Club: Pick up some locally made pies and pastries

Local Culture and Customs of Nubeena

Nubeena is a town steeped in history and culture. The area has a strong connection to its convict past. When visiting Nubeena, respect the local customs and honor the town’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Nubeena

The best time to visit Nubeena is during the summer months, December to February, when the weather is mild, and there is plenty of sunshine. The autumn months of March to May offer stunning foliage, while winter brings the chance to see snow in the mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nubeena

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace.
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities and bring adequate equipment.
  • Be respectful of the region’s convict history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Go off the beaten track without a guide in the Tasmanian wilderness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nubeena

Nubeena is a safe town, but visitors should exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities. Always follow the instructions of local guides and stay on designated trails when hiking.

Health in Nubeena

Tasmania has excellent medical facilities, but visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling to Nubeena.

Getting to Nubeena: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nubeena

The closest airport to Nubeena is Hobart International Airport, which is approximately a 75-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Nubeena

There is no direct bus service to Nubeena. Still, visitors can take a bus to the nearby town of Port Arthur and then use a taxi, rental car, or shuttle service to reach their destination.

Other Options

Visitors can rent cars or bikes at Hobart International Airport or take taxis from Hobart directly to Nubeena.

How to Get Around Nubeena

The best way to get around Nubeena is by car or bike. Taxis and shuttle services are available, but rentals are limited and must be reserved in advance.

Where to Go Next from Nubeena: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more Tasmanian adventures, Nubeena is an excellent base for exploring the rest of the Tasman Peninsula.

  • Fortescue Bay: A secluded coastline further along the track.
  • Bruny Island: An island accessible by a ferry that offers fantastic wildlife and food
  • Richmond: A historic town filled with excellent cafes and antique shops.

Conclusion

Nubeena is an idyllic Tasmania town that offers something for every kind of traveler. The town’s natural beauty, strong cultural heritage, and thriving culinary scene make it a must-visit destination in Tasmania. So, pack your bags, plan your itinerary, and start discovering the best of Nubeena today!
