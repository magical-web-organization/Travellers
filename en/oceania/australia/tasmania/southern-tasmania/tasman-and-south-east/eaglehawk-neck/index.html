Introduction to Eaglehawk Neck

Eaglehawk Neck is a small town located on the Tasman Peninsula, in the southeastern part of Tasmania. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, dramatic coastline, and fascinating history. The town was once a notorious penal settlement, and its rugged landscape was used as a natural barrier to keep prisoners from escaping.

Today, visitors come to Eaglehawk Neck to explore its beautiful beaches, go hiking or biking in the rugged wilderness, and learn more about its rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a quiet getaway, Eaglehawk Neck has something to appeal to every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Eaglehawk Neck

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. The Eaglehawk Neck Historic Site

The Eaglehawk Neck Historic Site is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Tasmanian history. The site includes a number of important landmarks, including the Dogline, a series of wooden barriers that were used to keep prisoners from escaping, and the Officers Quarters, which provide a glimpse into the daily lives of the soldiers who guarded the prisoners.

2. The Tasmanian Devil Conservation Park

The Tasmanian Devil Conservation Park is a great destination for animal lovers and families. Here, you’ll have the opportunity to see Tasmanian devils up close, as well as a range of other native wildlife, including kangaroos, wallabies, and quolls. The park also offers educational talks and guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Eaglehawk Neck: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. The Tessellated Pavement

The Tessellated Pavement is a natural geological formation that is truly awe-inspiring. Here, you’ll find a series of flat, rectangular rocks that have been naturally carved over time by the ocean’s waves. It’s a great place for photography, especially at sunrise or sunset.

2. The Cape Raoul Walk

The Cape Raoul Walk is a challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views of the Tasman Peninsula coastline. The 14-kilometer trail takes you through rugged wilderness, past towering cliffs, and down to a secluded beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eaglehawk Neck

The Three Capes Track

The Three Capes Track is a multi-day hike that takes you through some of the most spectacular scenery in Tasmania. The 48-kilometer trail includes sections of the Tasman National Park, and offers breathtaking views of the Tasman Peninsula coastline.

Biking Trails in Eaglehawk Neck

Eaglehawk Neck is also a great destination for cycling enthusiasts. The town’s quiet roads and rugged terrain make it ideal for mountain biking, and there are a number of trails and routes to explore.

Best Things to Do in Eaglehawk Neck: Activities and Tours

1. Sea Kayaking

Sea kayaking is a fantastic way to explore the rugged coastline of Eaglehawk Neck. There are a number of tour operators in the town that offer sea kayaking adventures, ranging from short excursions to multiday trips.

2. Wildlife Tours

Eaglehawk Neck is home to a range of native wildlife, including Tasmanian devils, echidnas, and wallabies. There are a number of wildlife tours available in the town, offering the chance to see these amazing animals up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Eaglehawk Neck

Must-Try Dishes

Eaglehawk Neck is known for its fresh seafood, including lobster, oysters, and abalone. Other local specialties include Tasmanian salmon and wallaby.

Local Beverages

Tasmania is known for its world-class wine, and there are a number of vineyards and wineries in the Eaglehawk Neck area. The town also has a number of microbreweries and distilleries, offering a range of craft beers, ciders, and spirits.

Where to Stay in Eaglehawk Neck: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eaglehawk Neck has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. There are luxury hotels and boutique guesthouses, as well as hostels and self-catering apartments.

What to Buy in Eaglehawk Neck: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eaglehawk Neck is known for its high-quality local handicrafts, including pottery, leather goods, and textiles. Other popular souvenirs include Tasmanian devil plush toys and locally-made jams and preserves.

Local Culture and Customs of Eaglehawk Neck

Eaglehawk Neck has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its history as a penal settlement and its unique geographical location. The town’s residents are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Eaglehawk Neck

The best time to visit Eaglehawk Neck is during the summer months, from December to February, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town’s natural beauty can be appreciated year-round, and there are plenty of activities and attractions to enjoy in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eaglehawk Neck

Do:

  • Take care of the environment and natural beauty
  • Explore the town’s history and heritage

Don’t:

  • Swim in dangerous areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Eaglehawk Neck

Eaglehawk Neck is generally a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take reasonable precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Keep valuables out of sight, avoid unlit areas at night, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Eaglehawk Neck

Eaglehawk Neck has a range of medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Eaglehawk Neck: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eaglehawk Neck

The closest major airport to Eaglehawk Neck is Hobart International Airport, which is approximately 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Eaglehawk Neck

Tasmania has a reliable bus network, with regular services to and from Hobart and other major towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire cars or arrange private transfers to Eaglehawk Neck.

How to Get Around in Eaglehawk Neck

Eaglehawk Neck is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, visitors may wish to hire a car or bike to explore the surrounding wilderness and coastline.

Where to Go Next from Eaglehawk Neck: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations include Port Arthur, a historic town located just 20 kilometers away, and Fortescue Bay, a beautiful beach about 10 kilometers from Eaglehawk Neck. The town of Hobart, Tasmania’s capital city, is also a must-visit destination and is easily accessible by bus or car.

Conclusion

Eaglehawk Neck is a fantastic destination for anyone interested in history, nature, and outdoor adventure. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, it’s a must-visit destination on any trip to Tasmania.
