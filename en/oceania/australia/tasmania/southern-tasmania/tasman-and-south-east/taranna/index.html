Taranna, located in the Tasman and South East region of Tasmania in Australia, is a hidden gem that boasts of breathtaking natural landscapes, fascinating historical sites, and exciting outdoor adventures. This guide will take you on a journey through Taranna and help you discover the best places to visit, the hiking routes, the local cuisine, and how to get around.

Introduction to Taranna

Taranna, once a busy logging town, is now an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventurous souls. The town is located on the stunning Tasman Peninsula and is surrounded by lush forests, crystal-clear waterways, and spectacular ocean views.

List of Places to Visit in Taranna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tasmanian Devil Unzoo - This award-winning wildlife park is home to Tasmanian devils and other native animals. It offers various tours, including a hands-on experience with the animals.

  • Port Arthur Historic Site - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Port Arthur Historic Site tells the history of the early convict settlements in Tasmania. This outdoor museum is full of fascinating stories and stunning architecture.

  • Convict Coal Mines Historic Site - This historic site is a well-preserved relic of the region’s convict history. Explore the underground tunnels and mine shafts to learn about the lives of the convicts who worked there.

  • Eaglehawk Neck Historic Site - This historic site is home to the remains of several convict-built buildings, including a guardhouse, a hotel, and a few cottages.

Natural Attractions of Taranna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tasman National Park - One of the most prominent natural attractions in Taranna, the Tasman National Park boasts of stunning cliffs, pristine beaches, and unique rock formations like Port Arthur’s Remarkable Cave.

  • Three Capes Track - This four-day hiking trail offers stunning views of the coast, cliffs, and the rugged terrain. It is one of the most popular hiking routes in Australia and requires pre-booking.

  • Tessellated Pavement - This natural wonder is a rare occurrence that forms when the rock surface is divided into polygon shapes, resembling a tiled pavement. It is a must-see attraction for nature lovers.

  • Tasman Arch and Devil’s Kitchen - These geological features will leave you in awe with their natural beauty. The Tasman Arch is a massive rock formation that collapsed into the sea, creating a unique arch-like shape. Devil’s Kitchen is another geological formation, where the sea has eroded the rocks, creating an impressive landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taranna

  • Waterfall Bay and Shipstern Bluff Walk - This scenic walk takes you through the Waterfall Bay lookout, past the dramatic sea cliffs of Shipstern Bluff and the Cape Raoul lookout.

  • Cape Pillar and Cape Hauy Walk - This challenging yet rewarding hike takes you through some of the most dramatic and picturesque sceneries in Tasmania. You will be rewarded with stunning views of the sea cliffs, beaches, and unique rock formations.

  • Tasman Island Cruise - For those looking for a unique way to explore Taranna, this cruise stops at some of the most spectacular coastal cliffs and provides an opportunity to spot local wildlife like seals, dolphins, and whales.

Best Things to Do in Taranna: Activities and Tours

  • Kayak Tours - Discover the magnificent coastline and its hidden coves on a guided kayak tour.

  • Scuba Diving - Explore the underwater world and the rich marine life it offers with a scuba diving tour.

  • Fishing Charters - Spend a day out on the water catching some of Tasmania’s best seafood.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides - Admire the stunning natural landscapes of Taranna from the skies on a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Taranna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood - Taranna offers some of the freshest seafood in Tasmania. Try the local catch like Tasmanian salmon, abalone, mussels, and oysters.

  • Tasmanian Cheese and Wine - Tasmania is known for its excellent wine and cheese. Try some local cheese and wine at the local restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Beer - Tasmania is home to some of the best craft breweries in Australia. Try some of the local beers like Moo Brew and Cascade.

  • Tasmanian Wine - Tasmania produces some of the best cool-climate wines in Australia. Try some of the local wines like Pinot Noir and Chardonnay.

Where to Stay in Taranna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Stewarts Bay Lodge - A luxury lodge offering stunning views of the bay and nearby ocean. Rooms include spa baths, private balconies, and fully-equipped kitchens.

  • Mason’s Cottages - Located in the heart of Taranna, Mason’s Cottages offers self-contained accommodations with fully-equipped kitchens and private BBQ areas.

  • Port Arthur Villas - Budget-friendly villas located close to the Port Arthur Historic Site. Rooms include kitchenettes, private balconies, and BBQ areas.

What to Buy in Taranna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery - Taranna is known for its handmade pottery. You can find unique pieces like vases, bowls, and plates.

  • Local Beer and Wine - Take some of the local beer and wine home with you as a souvenir or as a gift for family and friends.

  • Tasmanian Devil Souvenirs - The Tasmanian devil is an iconic animal in Tasmania. You can find various souvenirs like t-shirts, keychains, and plush toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Taranna

Taranna has a strong connection with nature and the environment. The local community places a high emphasis on preserving and protecting the natural environment. When visiting Taranna, it is important to respect the local cultural customs and follow the Leave No Trace principles.

Best Time to Visit Taranna

The best time to visit Taranna is from November to April when the weather is mild and the days are longer. However, if you want to skip the crowds and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region in peace, visit in the winter months of June to August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taranna

Do:

  • Respect the local cultural customs and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Prepare for the weather and bring appropriate clothing and gear.
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife and follow guidelines for interacting with them.
  • Take a guided tour and learn about the local history and environment.

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause any harm to the environment.
  • Approach animals without permission or respect their natural habitat.
  • Enter restricted areas or private property without permission.
  • Ignore the local rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taranna

Taranna is a relatively safe destination. However, it is essential to follow basic safety guidelines like keeping valuables safe and avoiding walking alone at night. Additionally, it’s important to be aware of the signs of hypothermia and prepare for sudden weather changes, especially when hiking.

Health in Taranna

Taranna has several hospitals and medical centers that provide emergency and non-emergency services. However, it is important to bring any necessary medication with you and be aware of basic first aid procedures when in the outdoors.

Getting to Taranna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taranna

The nearest airport to Taranna is Hobart International Airport, located 89 km away. The airport offers several rental car agencies and shuttle services to get to the town.

Bus Stations Near Taranna

There are several bus services that operate between Hobart and various destinations in Taranna, including Port Arthur and Eaglehawk Neck.

Other Options

Driving is one of the easiest ways to get to Taranna. The town is located on the Arthur Highway, which connects Hobart to Port Arthur.

How to Get Around in Taranna

The best way to get around Taranna is by car or bike. Car rental agencies are available at the airports and in nearby cities. You can also rent bikes and explore Taranna at a slower pace.

Where to Go Next from Taranna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Arthur - A popular tourist destination known for its rich history and stunning landscapes.

  • Hobart - The capital city of Tasmania and a cultural hub filled with museums, galleries and restaurants.

  • Freycinet National Park - Located north-east of Taranna, Freycinet National Park offers hiking trails, beaches, and stunning views of the coastline.

Conclusion

Taranna is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or a hiker, Taranna has something to offer everyone. Explore the natural scenery, indulge in the local cuisine, and immerse yourself in the rich history and culture of Taranna.
