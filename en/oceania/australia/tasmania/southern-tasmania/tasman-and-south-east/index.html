Tasman and South East: A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Tasman and South East is located in Southern Tasmania, in Australia, in the continent of Oceania. It is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies. This comprehensive tour guide provides information on the best places to visit, natural and cultural attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tasman and South East is home to several museums and historical places that give visitors a glimpse of its rich culture and heritage. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in the region include:

  • Port Arthur Historic Site: This historic site is one of the most popular attractions in Tasmania, offering visitors a chance to learn about the convict past of Australia.

  • Coal Mines Historic Site: This historic site was once a penal settlement and coal mine, and now offers visitors an opportunity to see the remains of the mines and the convicts’ living quarters.

Natural Attractions of Tasman and South East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Tasman and South East has a lot to offer. From scenic views to exciting outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone. Some of the top natural attractions in the region include:

  • Cape Raoul: This coastal walk offers visitors incredible views of the Tasman Peninsula, rugged cliffs, and the ocean.

  • Tasman Arch: This natural wonder is a great place to see the ocean’s power as it creates waves that crash into the arch.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tasman and South East

Tasman and South East is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. With its scenic views and rugged terrain, there are several excellent hiking and biking routes in the region. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Three Capes Track: This 4-day-long hike covers 48 km of stunning coastline, cliffs, and forests.

  • Tasman Peninsula Circuit: This 3-day hike covers 47 km of rugged terrain and offers visitors views of some of the most famous attractions in the region.

Best Things to Do in Tasman and South East: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other things to do in Tasman and South East. Some of the best activities and tours in the region include:

  • Wildlife cruises: Visitors can take a wildlife cruise to see seals, dolphins, and whales in their natural habitat.

  • Fishing: Tasman and South East is a great place for fishing, with plenty of charters available for those keen on reeling in a catch.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tasman and South East

For those who plan on spending a week in Tasman and South East, the following itinerary will help visitors make the most of their trip:

Day 1: Visit the Port Arthur Historic Site and take a ghost tour in the evening.

Day 2: Hike the Three Capes Track and camp at Cape Raoul.

Day 3: Explore the Tasman Peninsula Circuit and visit the Tessellated Pavement.

Day 4: Take a wildlife cruise from Pennicott Wilderness Journeys and visit Eaglehawk Neck.

Day 5: Go fishing.

Day 6: Hike to Fortescue Bay and spend the day at the beach.

Day 7: Visit the Coal Mines Historic Site and Tasman Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Tasman and South East

Tasman and South East is known for its farm-to-table food scene, with several excellent restaurants and cafes offering unique and delicious dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in the region include:

  • Oysters: Tasman and South East is famous for its fresh oysters, which can be enjoyed raw or cooked.

  • Scallops: Another local delicacy, scallops are often served in restaurants around the region.

  • Local wine: Tasman and South East is home to several vineyards, producing some excellent wines that visitors can try.

Where to Stay in Tasman and South East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Tasman and South East, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top options include:
