Introduction to Mole Creek Karst National Park

Mole Creek Karst National Park, situated in Western Tasmania, is a nature lover’s paradise. It’s an ideal destination for those who love to explore caves, rock formations, stunning waterfalls, and the natural beauty surrounding the park. Visiting Mole Creek Karst National Park is a unique opportunity to experience Tasmania’s diverse wildlife, scenic trails, and local culture and cuisine. In this guide, we’ll go through all the detailed information you need to plan your visit to Mole Creek Karst National Park - from the best places to visit, activities to try, accommodation options, transportation, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Mole Creek Karst National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

King Solomon Cave

King Solomon Cave is a beautifully carved labyrinth of underground rock formations, stalactites, and stalagmites, which is more than 300 million years old. It is located within the Mole Creek Karst National Park on the eastern edge of the Mersey-Forth Valley. Guided tours are available throughout the year, which takes approximately an hour, covering a distance of 300 meters. The caves are open to visitors between 9:00 am to 5:00 pm daily.

Marakoopa Cave

Marakoopa Cave is another fascinating cave system located within the Mole Creek Karst National Park. It is home to some of the magnificent glow-worms found in the southern hemisphere. The cave hosts two underground rivers and an array of limestone structures, and is over 500,000 years old. Access to the caves is only through guided tours, which lasts around an hour. Tours are available throughout the day.

Trowunna Wildlife Sanctuary

Trowunna Wildlife Sanctuary is a wildlife hospital and rehabilitation centre, protecting and conserving Tasmania’s native wildlife and providing sanctuary to unique Tasmanian animal species like Tasmanian devils, echidna, and wombats. This sanctuary offers visitors a chance to learn about the native animals of Tasmania, and about the conservation work carried out by the centre. Tours are offered daily.

Natural Attractions of Mole Creek Karst National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alum Cliffs Track

Alum Cliffs Track is a picturesque hiking trail, which is easy to moderate and has a duration of less than two hours. The trail takes you through natural bushland, tall eucalyptus forests, and tall rocky cliffs overlooking the Alum Cliffs Gorge. The area has incredible views of the Mersey and Forth River valleys, wildlife, and vegetation.

Westmorland Falls

The Westmorland Falls is Tasmania’s highest waterfall, standing at 275 meters high. It’s an area of rugged beauty, ideal for nature lovers and hikers. Experienced hikers can take the challenging Westmorland Falls trail and get rewarded with the stunning views of the waterfall.

Christmas Hills Raspberry Farm

Christmas Hills Raspberry Farm is a delightful working farm located in a picturesque setting in the Mole Creek Karst National Park. The farm is known for its delicious raspberries, and visitors can pick their own berries during the harvesting season (November to May). The farm also offers a range of jams, jellies, sauces, and desserts for visitors to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mole Creek Karst National Park

Mole Creek Karst National Park has numerous trails that cater to different levels of difficulty, and all offer unique perspectives on the park. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the park:

Chudleigh Park

Chudleigh Park is an easy day hike, ideal for beginners or families with children. The trail takes you through Chudleigh Park, a lush green forest with a variety of wildlife and mountain views.

Ferndene

Ferndene is a 10 km hike, which takes approximately six hours to complete, and is suitable for experienced hikers. Ferndene trail takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the park, including the Ferndene waterfall, river, and alpine heathland.

Tumbling Waters

Tumbling Waters is a short hike, around 20 minutes, and is perfect for visitors wishing to experience the park’s unique waterfalls and rock formations without go-for long hikes.

Best Things to Do in Mole Creek Karst National Park: Activities and Tours

Caving

Mole Creek Karst National Park boasts an incredible array of caves and underground rivers. Visitors can explore the cave systems and observe the prehistoric formations up close. Guided tours have been available year round and are an excellent way to experience the caves and underground rivers.

Wildlife Watching

The Mole Creek Karst National Park area is home to unique Tasmanian animal species like Tasmanian devils, echidna, and wombats. Visitors can see these animals during guided tours by qualified wildlife experts.

Wine tasting

Tasmania’s wine industry continues to rise, and Mole Creek Karst National Park is an excellent place to learn about the local wine scene. Several wineries offer guided wine tours, where visitors can taste the region’s finest wines, accompanied by locally sourced cheese, chocolates, and other delicacies.

Food and Drink Scene of Mole Creek Karst National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Wallaby and kangaroo meat

Mole Creek Karst National Park is a hub for bush food and offers an excellent selection of native delicacies. Visitors can try wallaby and kangaroo meat. Wallaby meat is rich in protein and low in fat.

Tasmanian Oysters

Tasmanian oysters are some of the country’s best oysters. Fresh, plump, and juicy, they are perfect for anyone who enjoys seafood.

Local Beverages

Tasmanian wine

Tasmania has steadily gained a reputation for its excellent quality wines. Several wineries in Mole Creek Karst National Park offer wine tasting tours.

Where to Stay in Mole Creek Karst National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mole Creek Guesthouse

Mole Creek Guesthouse is a luxurious 4-star accommodation located in the heart of Mole Creek Karst National Park. It boasts 14 beautifully decorated rooms, a restaurant, spa, and a range of outdoor activities, like horse riding, fishing, and tennis.

Marakoopa Cafe and Guesthouse

Marakoopa Cafe and Guesthouse is located in Mayberry, and is a perfect place to stay for budget travellers. The accommodation offers cosy and comfortable rooms, excellent food, and a friendly ambiance.

What to Buy in Mole Creek Karst National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tasmanian wool products

Tasmania is known for its unique wool products, and Mole Creek Karst National Park is home to several local wool producers. Visitors can buy scarves, sweaters and bags made from Tasmanian wool as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mole Creek Karst National Park

Tasmanian culture is proud of its unique heritage, history, and Indigenous tradition. Visitors should respect cultural customs and values while participating in cultural activities.

Best Time to Visit Mole Creek Karst National Park

The best time to visit Mole Creek Karst National Park is between September and May, which is springtime in Tasmania. During this time, the weather is mild, and the flora and fauna are at their most vibrant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mole Creek Karst National Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment and help preserve natural habitats.
  • Carry essential equipment for hiking and other outdoor activities.
  • Follow the park’s regulations and rules

Don’t:

  • Harm or harass the local wildlife.
  • Smoke within the park’s boundaries.
  • Take or damage plants or other natural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mole Creek Karst National Park

The park can be dangerous if visitors don’t take proper precautions. It’s essential to carry necessary equipment like water, food, proper clothing, and footwear while participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Mole Creek Karst National Park

It’s advisable to carry a first-aid kit for minor emergencies. Visitors with chronic health issues should carry their medication and inform their doctor about the travel plans.

Getting to Mole Creek Karst National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mole Creek Karst National Park

The closest airport to Mole Creek Karst National Park is Launceston Airport, which is just over an hour’s drive from the park.

Bus Stations Near Mole Creek Karst National Park

Metro Tasmania operates several buses between Launceston and nearby localities in Mole Creek.

Other Options

Car rentals, shuttle services, and guided tours are also available for visitors who prefer a more organized transport service.

How to Get Around in Mole Creek Karst National Park

There are plenty of hiking trails around the park, so walking, hiking or biking is an excellent way to explore the park. Several parks offer shuttle buses, taxi services, or bike rentals around the park.

Where to Go Next from Mole Creek Karst National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park

Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park is a stunning World Heritage site located in central Tasmania, approximately 77 km from Mole Creek Karst National Park. Cradle Mountain is one of Australia’s most popular tourist destinations, famous for its scenic wilderness, pristine lakes, and many walking trails.

Launceston

Launceston is Tasmania’s second-largest city, just over an hour’s drive from Mole Creek Karst National Park. Visitors can walk around the city and explore its vibrant local food scene, street art, museums, and galleries.
