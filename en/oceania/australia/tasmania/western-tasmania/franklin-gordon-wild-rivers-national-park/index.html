Introduction to Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park is a must-visit for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Located in Western Tasmania, Australia, the park covers over 447,000 hectares of diverse wilderness that includes ancient rainforests, wild rivers, stunning waterfalls, and rugged mountain ranges. This park is renowned for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, adventurous activities, and stunning scenery that cannot be found anywhere else in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park is a hiking and biking paradise with numerous trails that cater to all skill levels. Some popular routes include the Franklin River Nature Trail, the Frenchmans Cap Track, and the Walls of Jerusalem Circuit.

Best Things to Do in Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park: Activities and Tours

No matter what your interests are, Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park offers a wide range of activities that cater to all ages and adventure levels. Popular activities include:

  • Rafting on the Franklin River
  • Canoeing and kayaking
  • Fishing
  • Birdwatching
  • Abseiling and rock climbing
  • Fly fishing
  • Helicopter tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

To make the most out of your time in Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park, here’s a week-long itinerary that incorporates the best sights and activities:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Hobart, drive to Strahan, and explore the town
2 Spend a day on the Gordon River Cruise or take a helicopter tour
3 Hike on the Franklin River Nature Trail or to Donaghys Hill Lookout
4 Explore Sarah Island and Heritage Landing
5 Take a scenic drive to Lake St. Clair, hike the Overland Track, and relax at Pumphouse Point
6 Visit Nelson Falls and do some fly fishing or canyoning
7 Hike in the Walls of Jerusalem National Park or tackle the Frenchmans Cap and enjoy a farewell dinner in Strahan before heading back to Hobart

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park is home to the palawa people, the Aboriginal people of the region. Their heritage and traditions are an integral part of the park’s culture and history. Visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

The best time to visit Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park is in the summer months of December to February when the weather is warm and dry. Autumn (March to May) is also a great time to visit when the leaves change color, and the crowds have dispersed. It’s important to note that Tasmania’s weather is unpredictable, and visitors need to be prepared for rain and colder temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

Do:

  • Respect the cultural heritage of the palawa people
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear
  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations

Don’t:

  • Leave your rubbish behind
  • Enter prohibited areas
  • Take native plants or animals
  • Start a fire except in designated areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

Ensure you follow all safety rules and regulations set by the park authorities. Take necessary precautions while outdoors.

Health in Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

Tick-borne diseases, like lyme disease, can be prevalent in the park. Stay informed by checking for current health-related warnings ahead of your visit, and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park is located in remote western Tasmania. It’s best to fly into Hobart or Launceston and hire a car to drive to the national park. You can also take the shuttle bus from Hobart to Strahan or hire a taxi to get around the park.

Airports Near Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

  • Hobart International Airport
  • Launceston Airport

Bus Stations Near Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

  • Strahan Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Hire a car from Hobart or Launceston
  • Take a shuttle bus from Hobart to Strahan

How to Get Around in Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park

The best way to get around Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park is by hiring a car or taking a taxi. Some areas are only accessible by foot, and some roads may not be suitable for ordinary vehicles.
