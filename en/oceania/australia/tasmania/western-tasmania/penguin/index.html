Introduction to Penguin

Located in the beautiful Western Tasmania region of Australia, Penguin is a charming coastal town with plenty to offer visitors. From natural wonders to cultural attractions, Penguin has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to unwind on the beach, explore local history and culture, or embark on an outdoor adventure, Penguin is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Penguin

Here’s a list of must-visit places in and around Penguin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Penguin History Room: A museum featuring local history and artifacts.
  • Penguin Market: A weekly farmer’s market offering local produce and handmade crafts.
  • Burnie Regional Art Gallery: An art gallery showcasing works from local and national artists.
  • Burnie Makers Workshop: A cultural center featuring exhibits, workshops, and local artisan crafts.

Natural Attractions of Penguin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Penguin Beach: A beautiful beach with clear blue water and white sand.
  • Penguin Reserve and Coastal Trail: A scenic trail that winds along the coast, offering stunning views of the ocean and Penguin Island.
  • Dial Range: A mountain range located just outside of Penguin that offers hiking and scenic views.
  • Gunns Plains Caves: A fascinating cave system with underground streams and unique limestone formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Penguin

Penguin offers several hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Penguin to Ulverstone Coastal Path: A 11-kilometer trail offering scenic views and moderate hiking difficulty.
  • Dial Range Circuit: A challenging 14-kilometer trek through the Dial Range that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Forth to Cradle Mountain Rail Trail: A 45-kilometer paved trail that runs from Forth to Cradle Mountain, perfect for biking or hiking.

Best Things to Do in Penguin: Activities and Tours

  • Seal Watching Tour: Take a tour to Penguin Island to see seals in their natural habitat.
  • Penguin Paddle and Peddle: Rent a bike or kayak and explore the beautiful scenery surrounding Penguin.
  • Penguin Paranormal: A ghost tour of the historic Penguin Cemetery.
  • Burnie Arts and Industrial Tour: A guided tour of the Burnie Arts and Industrial District.

Week-Long Itinerary for Penguin

Day 1: Arrive in Penguin and explore the town’s beaches and local attractions.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Dial Range Circuit and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 3: Take a tour to Penguin Island to see seals in their natural habitat.

Day 4: Explore the local history and culture with visits to the Penguin History Room and Burnie Makers Workshop.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive to nearby attractions like Gunns Plains Caves or Mount Roland.

Day 6: Relax and unwind on Penguin Beach or a local spa.

Day 7: Shop for local souvenirs and handmade crafts at the Penguin Market before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Penguin

Penguin is known for its delicious seafood and local produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian seafood: Freshly caught seafood served at local restaurants.
  • Beef and lamb: Locally sourced meat dishes served with seasonal vegetables.
  • Tasmanian oysters: Freshly shucked oysters served at local markets and restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian wine: Visit local wineries for a taste of Tasmania’s finest wines.
  • Cascade Brewery: Visit Australia’s oldest operating brewery to enjoy a locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Penguin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Penguin offers a variety of accommodations for any budget:

  • The Madsen Boutique Hotel: A luxury hotel with ocean views and modern amenities.
  • Penguin Backpackers: A budget-friendly hostel popular with backpackers.
  • Mount Gnomon Farm: A charming bed and breakfast located on a working farm.

What to Buy in Penguin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Penguin Market: Pick up handmade crafts and souvenirs at this weekly farmer’s market.
  • Local art: Visit Burnie Regional Art Gallery to purchase works from local artists.
  • Tasmanian honey: Find locally produced honey at the Penguin Market or neighboring farms.

Local Culture and Customs of Penguin

Penguin is a town with a strong sense of community. Visitors will notice a laid-back, friendly atmosphere and a deep appreciation for nature and local traditions. When visiting, be respectful of the town’s history and customs and take part in local events when possible.

Best Time to Visit Penguin

Penguin is a great destination any time of year, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (December to February) when temperatures are warm and the beaches are ideal for swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Penguin

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Take part in local events and festivals.
  • Explore the town’s beautiful natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect local heritage and traditions.
  • Ignore safety warnings when hiking or exploring the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Penguin

Penguin is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions when hiking or exploring the local area to ensure their safety. Always stay on marked trails and obey any warning signs.

Health in Penguin

Penguin has several medical centers and hospitals for any health needs. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should take necessary precautions and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Penguin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Penguin

The closest airports to Penguin are Burnie/Wynyard Regional Airport and Devonport Airport. Both airports offer domestic flights to major Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Penguin

Tasmania’s public transportation system, Metro Tasmania, operates several bus routes throughout the state. Visitors can take the Launceston to Burnie bus, with stops in Penguin.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get to Penguin from neighboring cities like Devonport or Burnie.

How to Get Around in Penguin

Penguin is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Rental cars and taxis are also available for visitors who wish to travel outside of the town.

Where to Go Next from Penguin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cradle Mountain: A national park with stunning mountain scenery and hiking trails.
  • Tamar Valley Wine Route: A beautiful wine route with scenic views and vineyards.
  • Devonport: A seaside city with beautiful beaches and museums.

Conclusion

Penguin is a beautiful coastal town with a rich history and culture that’s waiting to be explored. From its beautiful beaches to its stunning natural scenery, Penguin has something to offer every visitor. Plan your perfect trip to Penguin today and make unforgettable memories!
