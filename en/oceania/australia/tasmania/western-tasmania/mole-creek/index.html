Introduction to Mole Creek

Nestled in Western Tasmania, Mole Creek is a serene town that boasts natural wonders and cultural attractions. This charming town is located in the heart of a picturesque valley surrounded by mountains, caves, and forests.

List of Places to Visit in Mole Creek

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marakoopa Cave

Marakoopa Cave is among the many limestone caves that are in Mole Creek. It is known for its natural beauty, featuring incredible reflections, stunning rock formations, and an underground stream. The guided tour is informative and makes it easy for visitors to explore the cave safely.

Mole Creek Museum

Get to know more about the history of the town by visiting the Mole Creek Museum. The museum houses exhibits about the early settlers, mining history, and natural history. The collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents will take you back in time to the early days of Mole Creek.

Natural Attractions of Mole Creek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cradle Mountain National Park

Witness the breathtaking beauty of Tasmania’s landscape by visiting the Cradle Mountain National Park. Located nearby Mole Creek, the park features a diverse range of flora and fauna, towering mountain peaks, and sparkling mountain lakes. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and wildlife watching.

Trowunna Wildlife Sanctuary

Get up close and personal with Tasmania’s native animals by visiting Trowunna Wildlife Sanctuary. Visitors can see Tasmanian devils, wallabies, and other endangered species. The sanctuary also offers tours and educational demonstrations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mole Creek

The Overland Track

The Overland Track offers a challenging but rewarding hiking trail showcasing Tasmania’s stunning wilderness. The path spans 65 kilometers, and it takes around six days to complete the journey. Hikers will be able to see Cradle Mountain, waterfalls, forests, and more.

Mole Creek Karst National Park

The Mole Creek Karst National Park is perfect for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers. The park has various trails from easy walks to challenging tracks. The hike showcases the intricate karst landforms, including cave networks, sinkholes, and underground streams.

Best Things to Do in Mole Creek: Activities and Tours

Nature and Wildlife Tours

Discover the incredible flora and fauna of Mole Creek on a nature tour. Join a local guide as they take you through the forest, showing you native wildlife, waterfalls, and other natural wonders.

Hot Air Balloon Rides

Take to the skies and enjoy a bird’s eye view of Mole Creek’s stunning landscape. A hot air balloon ride is a unique experience that allows you to see Tasmania’s wilderness from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mole Creek

Day 1: Arrive in Mole Creek and visit Trowunna Wildlife Sanctuary.

Day 2: Explore Mole Creek Karst National Park and visit Marakoopa and King Solomon Caves.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to Cradle Mountain National Park and hike along Dove Lake Trail.

Day 4: Cycle through Mole Creek and the surrounding countryside.

Day 5: Take a balloon ride over Mole Creek Valley.

Day 6: Walk along the Liffey Falls and enjoy the scenic waterfalls.

Day 7: Visit the Mole Creek Museum and learn about the town’s rich history.

Food and Drink Scene of Mole Creek

Must-Try Dishes

Steak and Ale Pie

A classic Australian dish, the Steak and Ale Pie is a hearty meal that features tender chunks of steak, vegetables, and a savory gravy.

Tasmanian Lobster

Savor the fresh and succulent Tasmanian Lobster, locally known as ‘crayfish.’ Served with butter sauce or barbecued, it is a must-try seafood delicacy.

Local Beverages

Tasmanian Wines

Tasmania produces some of the world’s best wines. A tour of Mole Creek’s wineries is a must-do activity when visiting the town.

Cascade Premium Beer

One of Australia’s oldest breweries, Cascade, produces the refreshing Cascade Premium Beer. It is a popular beverage throughout Tasmania.

Where to Stay in Mole Creek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Motel Mayfair on Cavell

Located in the heart of Mole Creek, the Motel Mayfair on Cavell features spacious rooms, a delightful restaurant, and picturesque views of the valley.

Windmill Hill Lodge

Windmill Hill Lodge is a charming, rustic lodge set on a lush countryside. It features an outdoor pool, a restaurant, and a lounge.

What to Buy in Mole Creek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tasmanian Honey

Known for its unique flavor, the Tasmanian Honey is a premium honey that is in Mole Creek.

Handmade Crafts and Pottery

Mole Creek is home to many artisans and crafters who produce handmade merchandise. Visitors can find beautiful pottery, jewelry, and other locally-made items.

Local Culture and Customs of Mole Creek

The custodians of Mole Creek’s land are the Tasmanian Aboriginal people. Respect their customs and history through your interactions with the land and the people.

Best Time to Visit Mole Creek

The best time to visit Mole Creek is between December and February, during the summer season. The weather is favorable for hiking, outdoor activities, and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mole Creek

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow all safety guidelines and instructions when exploring the caves.
  • Carry your food and snacks as there are limited food options available.

Don’t:

  • Do not remove or damage any of the natural formations in the caves.
  • Do not feed or interact with the wildlife without expert supervision.
  • Do not litter in the national parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mole Creek

Tasmania has a low crime rate, making Mole Creek a relatively safe town. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring the national parks and follow all safety guidelines and instructions.

Health in Mole Creek

Mole Creek has no hospitals, but there is a pharmacy in the town. Visitors should carry necessary medications and have travel insurance before traveling to the town.

Getting to Mole Creek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mole Creek

The nearest airport to Mole Creek is the Devonport Airport, which is about 60 km away. The Launceston Airport is also a viable option, located 113 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mole Creek

Tasmanian Redline Coaches provide regular service to and from Mole Creek.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to get to Mole Creek.

How to Get Around in Mole Creek

The best way to get around Mole Creek and the surrounding areas is by car. Bikes are also an excellent option for exploring the town center.

Where to Go Next from Mole Creek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Launceston

Launceston city is located 1.5 hours away from Mole Creek. It is known for its culture, art, and shopping.

Devonport

Devonport city is located 45 minutes away from Mole Creek. It is a beautiful coastal city, famous for its beaches and water activities.

Conclusion

Mole Creek is a hidden gem in Tasmania that offers breathtaking natural wonders and rich cultural history. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to the town and explore everything it has to offer.
