Introduction to Ulverstone

Ulverstone is a charming coastal town on the banks of Leven River in Western Tasmania. With a population of just over 10,000, it is an ideal destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers. The town is known for its lush greenery, scenic landscapes, and friendly locals. Here’s everything you need to know about Ulverstone:

List of Places to Visit in Ulverstone

There are plenty of amazing places to explore in Ulverstone. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ulverstone History Museum: Explore the rich history and cultural heritage of the town through a vast collection of artefacts, photographs and memorabilia from the past.
  • Leven Regional Art Gallery: Discover an exhibition of local and national art, including paintings, sculptures and installations in a modern and contemporary gallery setting.

Natural Attractions of Ulverstone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Roland: Take a scenic hike up the towering mountain to experience panoramic views of the surrounding forests, valleys and town.
  • Leven Canyon: Experience stunning natural views of the river canyon surrounded by the lush greenery of the Tasmanian wilderness.
  • Preston Falls: Hike through the forest to see the impressive waterfall cascading down the rocks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ulverstone

Ulverstone is also known for its exciting hiking and biking routes, catering to both novices and experienced adventurers. Here are some of the popular trails:

  • Rail Trail: This 23 km trail follows the historic railway route and takes you along the picturesque Leven River.
  • Wild Mersey Mountain Bike Trails: A network of thrilling mountain bike tracks takes you through the stunning natural landscapes of the Wild Mersey area.

Best Things to Do in Ulverstone: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the town’s natural and cultural attractions, there is plenty to do in Ulverstone.

  • Visiting Markets: Attend local markets that sell fresh produce, handmade crafts, and interesting antiques.
  • Visit The Penguin Viewing Platform: Head out further along the coast to witness the fascinating penguin colonies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ulverstone

Here’s a plan for a week-long visit to Ulverstone:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Ulverstone History Museum
2 Take a scenic drive along the coast to see the Penguin Viewing Platform
3 Hike the Leven Canyon
4 Explore the Mount Roland summit

6 Discover the local art and artists at the Leven Regional Art Gallery

Food and Drink Scene in Ulverstone

Ulverstone has a robust food and drink scene with various options that are worth exploring for anyone who’s a foodie at heart.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian scallops: Locally sourced scallops, served in restaurants all around the town, they are definitely a must-try.
  • Fish and chips: Try some fresh fish and chips, straight off the boat at one of Ulverstone’s seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian wine: Taste some of Tasmania’s best wines at the vineyards around Ulverstone.
  • International Beers: For the beer enthusiasts, visit the Two Meter Tall Brewery for a taste of international brews.

Where to Stay in Ulverstone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ulverstone has plenty of options when it comes to accommodation:

  • The Lighthouse Hotel: A modern boutique hotel centrally located near all of the popular tourist destinations.
  • The Seabreeze Hotel: A comfortable and affordable hotel option near the beach.
  • The Ulverstone Guesthouse: A cosy guesthouse run by friendly hosts that feels like a home away from home.

What to Buy in Ulverstone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of interesting local products to take home as souvenirs of your trip to Ulverstone.

  • Tasmanian honey: Take home a jar or two of locally produced honey, straight from Tasmanian bees.
  • Arts and Crafts: Visit the markets or local gift shops to find unique handcrafted items, like pottery and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Ulverstone

Ulverstone is a town with a friendly, relaxed, and welcoming atmosphere. The locals take pride in preserving the town’s heritage and cultural traditions. Be prepared to be pleasantly surprised by the genuine warmth that Ulverstone has to offer.

Best Time to Visit Ulverstone

The best time to visit Ulverstone is between December and March when the weather is warm and sunny. During this season, the town comes alive with various festivals and events like the Taste of Tasmania food festival and the Australian Masters Games.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ulverstone

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment: Follow appropriate guidelines when exploring natural attractions like the Leven Canyon and Mount Roland.
  • Support local businesses: Take the time to explore local markets, cafes, and restaurants to get a true sense of Ulverstone.

Don’t:

  • Littering: Please be mindful of the environment. Littering is not tolerated in Ulverstone.
  • Ignoring local customs and traditions: Be considerate of the town’s heritage and cultural traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ulverstone

Ulverstone is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s still essential to take some precautions:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: Be cautious when exploring new places, and keep your valuables with you at all times.

Health in Ulverstone

Tasmania has a high-quality healthcare system, including a public hospital in Ulverstone should you need medical assistance during your stay.

Getting to Ulverstone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ulverstone

The closest airport to Ulverstone is Devonport Airport, which is a 20-minute drive away. Other airports in the surrounding area include Launceston Airport and Hobart Airport.

Bus Stations Near Ulverstone

The most prominent bus station near Ulverstone is the East Devonport Bus Station. Taxis are also cheap, and Uber is available here.

Other Options

  • Self-Driving: Exploring Ulverstone by car can feel very therapeutic and allow you to take your time to savour the sights and natural beauty of the area.

How to Get Around in Ulverstone

One of the best ways to get around Ulverstone is via rental car or bike, both of which are available to rent in town. Additionally, with everything close together, Ulverstone is a highly walkable town.

Where to Go Next from Ulverstone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ulverstone is ideally located to explore other destinations in Western Tasmania. Here are some of the popular places worth visiting:

  • Cradle Mountain: Explore the stunning natural beauty of Cradle Mountain, situated just a few hours’ drive from Ulverstone.
  • Launceston: A bustling city with a rich history, located about an hour and a half drive away from Ulverstone.

Conclusion

Ulverstone is a hidden gem of a town in Western Tasmania, with so much to offer visitors. With beautiful natural landscapes, deep cultural heritage, and a robust food and drink scene, it’s a great destination to explore for a week, a weekend, or even longer. Come see what Ulverstone has to offer for yourself!
