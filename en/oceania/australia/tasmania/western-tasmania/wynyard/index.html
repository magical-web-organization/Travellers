Introduction to Wynyard

Located in Western Tasmania, Wynyard is a charming coastal town that offers visitors a wide range of experiences and attractions. From its beautiful beaches to its rugged wilderness areas, Wynyard is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and nature lovers. This travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Wynyard.

List of Places to Visit in Wynyard

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wonders of Wynyard Exhibition - This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of Wynyard. It showcases the town’s evolution from a small settlement to a bustling seaside destination.

  • Fossil Bluff - This geological attraction is home to some of the oldest and most significant fossils in Australia. Visitors can explore the fossil beds and learn about the area’s history.

  • Table Cape Lighthouse - Built in 1888, this lighthouse is a prominent landmark in Wynyard. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the area’s maritime history.

Natural Attractions of Wynyard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wynyard Beach - This stunning beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing. The warm water and soft sand make it a popular spot for families and beachgoers.

  • Fossil Bluff Marine Conservation Area - This coastal reserve offers visitors a unique look at Tasmania’s marine life. It’s an excellent spot for snorkeling, diving, and kayaking.

  • Table Cape Tulip Farm - This working farm is known for its stunning display of over 5,000 tulips every spring. Visitors can take guided tours of the fields and learn about the history of tulips in Australia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wynyard

  • Table Cape Coastal Walk - This hike offers stunning views of the coastline and Table Cape. It’s a relatively easy walk that’s suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

  • Inglis River Track - This 3-kilometer hike follows the Inglis River and offers views of the town and surrounding countryside. It’s a popular spot for birdwatching and picnicking.

  • Wynyard Foreshore Trail - This 2-kilometer walk follows the shoreline and offers beautiful views of the ocean. It’s a great spot for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Wynyard: Activities and Tours

  • Wynyard Golf Club - Located just outside of town, this golf course is the perfect spot for a round of golf. It offers stunning views of the ocean and is suitable for players of all skill levels.

  • Wynyard Aviation Museum - This museum showcases the town’s aviation history and is home to a collection of vintage aircraft. It’s a must-visit for aviation enthusiasts.

  • Wynyard Maritime Museum - This museum is dedicated to the town’s maritime history and is home to a collection of historical boats and ships. Visitors can take guided tours and learn about the area’s maritime heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Wynyard

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian Seafood - Wynyard is known for its fresh seafood, which includes everything from lobster and oysters to scallops and fish.

  • Beef and Lamb - Tasmania is known for producing high-quality beef and lamb, and many restaurants in Wynyard feature these meats on their menus.

  • Tasmanian Wine - The cool climate of Tasmania is perfect for producing high-quality wine. Wynyard is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours.

Local Beverages

  • Boags Beer - This iconic beer is brewed in nearby Launceston and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

  • Hardy’s Wines - Another excellent winery in the area, Hardy’s produces some of the best wines in Tasmania.

  • Tasmanian Whiskey - Tasmania is known for its whiskey, and many distilleries in the area offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Wynyard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Burnie Airport Motel - This motel offers affordable accommodations close to the airport and the town center.

  • Waterfront Wynyard Hotel - Located in the heart of town, this hotel offers stunning views of the ocean and comfortable rooms.

  • Stanley Seaview Inn - This cozy inn is located just a short drive from Wynyard and offers stunning views of Stanley and the surrounding area.

What to Buy in Wynyard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tasmanian Whiskey - Tasmania is known for its whiskey, and many distilleries in the area offer tastings and tours.

  • Handmade Crafts and Artwork - The area is home to many talented artists and craftsmen, and there are several markets and shops where visitors can buy handmade items.

  • Tasmanian Honey - Tasmania produces some of the best honey in the world, and there are several local honey producers in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Wynyard

Wynyard is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors should feel free to explore and ask locals for recommendations. It’s important to respect the area’s natural resources and wildlife and to leave everything as you found it.

Best Time to Visit Wynyard

The best time to visit Wynyard is during the spring or summer, when the weather is warm and the flowers are in bloom. September to November is the best time to see the tulips at the Table Cape Tulip Farm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wynyard

Do:

  • Respect the area’s natural resources and wildlife.

  • Explore the town and ask locals for recommendations.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and attractions.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter behind.

  • Disturb the local wildlife.

  • Ignore warning signs and safety warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wynyard

Wynyard is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should always lock their cars and take basic safety precautions when exploring the area.

Health in Wynyard

There are several medical centers and hospitals in the area, and visitors should take appropriate health precautions when traveling to the area.

Getting to Wynyard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wynyard

  • Burnie Airport - This airport is located just a few kilometers from Wynyard and offers flights to several major cities in Australia.

  • Devonport Airport - Located about an hour’s drive from Wynyard, Devonport Airport offers flights to Melbourne and several other Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Wynyard

  • Burnie Bus Station - This bus station offers regular services to and from Wynyard and other nearby towns and cities.

  • Devonport Bus Station - Located about an hour’s drive from Wynyard, Devonport Bus Station offers services to Melbourne and other Australian cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in town and explore the area at their own pace.

  • Taxi - There are several taxi services in the area, and visitors can easily hail a taxi on the street or call for a pickup.

How to Get Around in Wynyard

The best way to get around in Wynyard is by car or bicycle. There are several rental companies in town that offer both cars and bicycles for rent. There is also a bus service that operates in and around town.

Where to Go Next from Wynyard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stanley - This charming seaside town is just a short drive from Wynyard and is known for its historic buildings and stunning scenery.

  • Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park - This national park is one of Tasmania’s most popular attractions, known for its stunning mountain views and outdoor activities.

  • Launceston - Tasmania’s second-largest city is just a few hours’ drive from Wynyard and offers a wide range of attractions and activities.

Conclusion

Wynyard is a hidden gem on Tasmania’s west coast. With its stunning beaches, breathtaking scenery, and wide range of attractions and activities, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking for a unique and memorable vacation. This comprehensive guide has everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Wynyard, Tasmania.
