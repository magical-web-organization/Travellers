Introduction to Rocky Cape National Park

Rocky Cape National Park is a place of extraordinary beauty, located on the northwest coast of Tasmania. The national park is named after the prominent rocky headland, Rocky Cape, that offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding ocean.

Rocky Cape National Park is a place of significant cultural importance to Tasmania’s Indigenous people, the Palawa. The park is home to many historic sites that tell the stories of Palawa culture, including rock shelters and shell middens.

List of Places to Visit in Rocky Cape National Park

There are many wonderful places to visit in Rocky Cape National Park, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rocky Cape Lighthouse: At the northern end of the park, the lighthouse was built in 1890 and is still operational today. It offers stunning views of the coast and surrounding landscape.
  • Aboriginal Cultural Centre: Located on the western side of the park, this centre has many displays and exhibits that tell the story of the Palawa people and their culture.
  • Shell Cove: An extensive shell midden site that is the testament to the thousands of years of occupation by the Palawa people.

Natural Attractions of Rocky Cape National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boat Harbour Beach: A beautiful and secluded beach that’s perfect for swimming, fishing, or just relaxing.
  • The Highfield Historic Site: This historic site is located near the town of Stanley. Visitors can explore the restored buildings, including the mansion and gardens.
  • Sisters Beach: With its white sandy beach and crystal clear water, Sisters Beach is a favourite with families.
  • Rocky Cape Lookout: Located at the northern end of the park, the lookout offers panoramic views of the coastline, sapphire blue water and surrounding national parks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rocky Cape National Park

Rocky Cape National Park has several well-maintained and marked hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an up-close experience of the park.

  • The Cape Forestier Circuit: This trail is located near the town of Boat Harbour and takes visitors on a journey through a unique coastal vegetation type.
  • The East Ridge Track: The trail begins at the Rocky Cape Lighthouse and continues up the ridge and through the coastal scrub, offering stunning views of the coastline.
  • The Coastal Circuit: This trail offers visitors a chance to explore the beautiful coastline of the park, offering various opportunities to swim, rock climb and explore the shoreline.

Best Things to Do in Rocky Cape National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Aboriginal Cultural Centre Tour: Join a tour and learn about the rich cultural significance of the Palawa people in the Rocky Cape National Park region.
  • Rocky Cape Lighthouse Tour: Visitors can climb up the steep spiral staircase to the lookout that offers stunning views of Tasmania’s north coast.
  • Fishing Charters: Local fishing charters offer visitors an unforgettable experience of fishing on the Tasmanian coast.
  • Wine and Cheese Tasting near Rocky Cape National Park: Visit the local cheese and wine makers near Rocky Cape National Park and indulge in scrumptious local produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rocky Cape National Park

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Rocky Cape National Park:

Day 1: Visit the Aboriginal Cultural Centre and Shell Cove. Day 2: Explore the Coastal Circuit and swim at Sisters Beach. Day 3: Take a tour of Rocky Cape Lighthouse and enjoy the scenic views. Day 4: Boat Harbour Beach and lunch at a local restaurant. Day 5: Hike the East Ridge Track. Day 6: Highfield Historic Site and Stanley. Day 7: Depart Rocky Cape National Park and explore Tasmania’s north coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Rocky Cape National Park

The food and drink scene in Rocky Cape National Park is abundant in delicious local cuisine, traditional dishes and local wines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crayfish: The crayfish found in Tasmanian waters are famous around the world for their sweet and delicate flavour.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian wine: Tasmania is known for its various vineyards and wineries, producing some of Australia’s best wines. Try a pinot noir from the northern coast vineyards.

Where to Stay in Rocky Cape National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation near Rocky Cape National Park, including guesthouses, hotels and hostels.

  • Rocky Cape Tavern: Offering budget-friendly accommodation to travellers, owning a convince store and restaurant.
  • Three Chimneys Farmstay: A perfect option for those seeking private rooms with serene views and farm-to-table meals.
  • Boat Harbour Beach Holiday Park: Offering cabin stays, campsites with internal amenities and access to Boat Harbour Beach.
  • Stanley Hotel and Suites: Located in the nearby town of Stanley, the hotel offers luxury accommodation, ocean views and fine dining.

What to Buy in Rocky Cape National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rocky Cape National Park has many souvenir shops near on-site, offering a chance to buy local products such as:

  • Aboriginal Art: From paintings to carvings, visitors can take home local artwork.
  • Handmade Jewellery: Featuring local stones, shell, and pearls, creates a perfect treasured souvenir.
  • Tasmanian Wines: Indigenous wines reflecting Tasmania’s climate and soil, available from nearby Cellar Doors.

Local Culture and Customs of Rocky Cape National Park

Rocky Cape National Park is significant to Tasmania’s Indigenous people, the Palawa. Visitors must respect the culture and follow some basic rules to ensure the park’s significance is upheld for future generations.

Best Time to Visit Rocky Cape National Park

The best time to visit Rocky Cape National Park is from September to May. During this time, the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rocky Cape National Park

Do:

  • Respect the Palawa people and their culture.
  • Take only photos, leave only footprints.
  • Stay on designated trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park.
  • Remove anything from the park.
  • Disturb any native wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rocky Cape National Park

Rocky Cape National Park is a safe place to visit. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions while hiking.

Health in Rocky Cape National Park

There are no significant health risks in Rocky Cape National Park. Be sure to carry enough water and sunscreen during outdoor activities.

Getting to Rocky Cape National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rocky Cape National Park is accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Rocky Cape National Park

The nearest airport to Rocky Cape National Park is Burnie Airport located only 30km south of the park.

Bus Stations Near Rocky Cape National Park

Public transport offers buses to Rocky Cape National Park various time during the day. Passengers enter at the bus station in Burnie.

Other Options

Rental cars, private taxi services, and tourist buses can be hired at nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Rocky Cape National Park

The best way to get around Rocky Cape National Park is by car or private charter service. There are minimum public services and foot/bus access to some hiking trails is limited.
