Introduction to Smithton

Located in the Western part of Tasmania, Smithton is a quaint town that offers a plethora of outdoor activities, scenic beauty, and cultural landmarks. It is situated on the banks of the Duck River and is known for its rich cultural heritage and friendly locals. Smithton is a great place to explore, whether you’re a solo traveler, a family, or traveling with a group of friends.

List of Places to Visit in Smithton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Circular Head Heritage Centre - The circular head heritage center is where you can learn about the history and culture of Smithton. It houses a collection of artifacts that reflect the town’s past and showcases the cultural heritage of the region.
  • Allendale Gardens - Allendale gardens has a collection of rare and exotic plants that are worth exploring. It also features an art gallery, cafe, and a plant nursery.
  • Emily Bay - Emily Bay is a beachside suburb located near Smithton. It is known for its stunning beach views and pristine waters.

Natural Attractions of Smithton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tessellated Pavement - Tessellated pavement is a natural rock formation located on the south-east coast of Tasmania, near Eaglehawk Neck. This rock formation resembles a tiled pavement and is worth the visit.
  • Tarkine Forest Adventures - Tarkine Forest Adventures offer a range of outdoor experiences such as guided walks, mountain biking, kayaking, and much more. It is a great place to explore nature.
  • Arthur River Cruises - Arthur River Cruises offer a unique experience to explore the Tarkine wilderness and Arthur River. You can take a boat ride to explore the rainforest, birdlife, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smithton

  • Woolnorth Wind Farm - Cape Grim Lookout - This is a great hiking trail that offers amazing views of Cape Grim. It is an 11 km circular route that takes you through the Woolnorth wind farm and Cape Grim lookout.
  • West Point Beach - West Point beach is a great option for a leisurely walk or bike ride, and it is located near Smithton. You can explore the beach and enjoy stunning views of the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Smithton: Activities and Tours

  • Waterfall Bay Tour - This tour takes you to the Waterfall Bay region, where you can explore the stunning coastline and the Rock Lobster Fishing grounds.
  • Tarkine Drive Tour - This tour takes you through the Tarkine forest and enables you to see some of the most stunning natural landscapes that Tasmania has to offer.
  • Flower Field Tour - If you’re in Smithton during the spring season, then you should definitely take the Flower Field Tour. It takes you through the scenic route of Table Cape to view tulips, daffodils, and other wildflowers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Smithton

Day 1: Arrive in Smithton, explore the town, and visit Circular Head Heritage Centre. Day 2: Go on a hiking or biking trail to the Woolnorth Wind Farm - Cape Grim Lookout. Day 3: Take a tour of the Waterfall Bay region and explore the Rock Lobster Fishing grounds. Day 4: Visit the Tarkine Forest Adventures and indulge in outdoor activities. Day 5: Take the Tarkine Drive Tour and witness the stunning natural landscapes and wildlife. Day 6: Visit Allendale Gardens, explore the collection of plants and art gallery. Day 7: Take a boat ride on Arthur River Cruises and explore the Tarkine rainforest.

Food and Drink Scene of Smithton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian Salmon - The Tasmanian salmon is known worldwide as some of the freshest and most flavorful salmon.
  • Wallaby Pie - A wallaby pie is an iconic dish in Tasmania that is made with wallaby meat and potatoes.
  • Tasmanian Scallops - The Tasmanian scallops are usually served with lemon, garlic, and a little bit of butter. They are known for their unique taste.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Whiskey - Tasmania is famous for its whiskey, with several distilleries located nearby. You can take a tour of the distilleries and enjoy the rich taste of whiskey.
  • Tasmanian Beer - There are several microbreweries located in Tasmania, and you can try some of the local beers ranging from lagers to stouts.

Where to Stay in Smithton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Stanley Hotel and Apartments - Stanley Hotel and Apartments offer a range of accommodation options including rooms, suites, and apartments.
  • Seaview Inn - Seaview Inn is a great option for budget travelers. They offer clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • The Bridge - The Bridge is a guesthouse that offers unique and cozy rooms that are traditional and modern at the same time.

What to Buy in Smithton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tasmanian Devil Souvenirs - Tasmanian devil souvenirs are a must-have. You can find them in most of the souvenir shops in Smithton.
  • Tasmanian Honey - Tasmania is famous for its leatherwood honey, which is unique to the region.
  • Tasmanian Licorice - Tasmanian licorice is known for its unique taste and is a great souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Smithton

The locals in Smithton are friendly and welcoming. The town has a rich cultural heritage, and you can explore this by visiting the various museums and cultural landmarks. The locals are also proud of their town’s natural beauty and work to preserve it for future generations.

Best Time to Visit Smithton

The best time to visit Smithton is during the summer months from December to February. The weather is mild, and you can explore the town and surrounding areas without any problems. However, if you want to witness the stunning colors of autumn, then the months of March to May are a good time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smithton

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Say “hello” and “thank you” to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the town.
  • Ignore the road safety measures and guidelines.
  • Be a nuisance to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Smithton

Smithton is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, it is important to take standard safety precautions, such as locking your car and valuables, especially if you are visiting the more remote areas.

Health in Smithton

The health facilities in Smithton are excellent, with a hospital, pharmacy, and several medical clinics.

Getting to Smithton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Smithton

The nearest airport to Smithton is Burnie Airport, which is located approximately 62km east of the town. There are also two other airports nearby, Devonport Airport and Launceston Airport, which are located approximately 140 and 170km away respectively.

Bus Stations Near Smithton

There is no bus station in Smithton. However, there are several bus stations nearby in Burnie and Devonport, which offer daily bus services to Smithton.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a taxi from the nearby airports to reach Smithton.

How to Get Around in Smithton

The best way to get around in Smithton is by car. There are several car rental companies located nearby, and you can also hire a taxi if required. Buses are also available, but they are not very frequent.

Where to Go Next from Smithton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stanley - Stanley is a historic town located approximately 22km south of Smithton. It is known for its stunning views of the coastline and its historic buildings.
  • Cradle Mountain - Cradle Mountain is a popular tourist destination and is located approximately 250km east of Smithton. It is known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Launceston - Launceston is Tasmania’s second-largest city and is located approximately 170km east of Smithton. It is known for its cultural landmarks, art galleries, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Smithton is a great place to visit and explore. It offers a range of activities, outdoor adventures, stunning natural beauty, and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re a solo traveler, traveling with a group of friends or with family, Smithton has something for everyone. So pack your bags and explore the amazing town of Smithton.
