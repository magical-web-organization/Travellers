Introduction to Beauty Point

Beauty Point is a picturesque coastal town located in Western Tasmania, known for its rich history, natural beauty, and abundant wildlife. Situated on the banks of the Tamar River, this quaint town has a lot to offer visitors, from stunning views of the ocean and forested hillsides to cultural experiences and outdoor adventures.

Whether you’re looking to soak up the local culture, enjoy some outdoor recreation, or simply relax and unwind in a beautiful setting, Beauty Point is a must-visit destination. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at what this town has to offer, including the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Beauty Point

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Platypus House: This unique attraction gives visitors the opportunity to see platypuses up close in their natural habitat. There’s also a Tasmanian Echidna exhibit and a guided tour of the seahorse aquarium.

  • Beaconsfield Mine & Heritage Centre: Learn about the history of mining in Tasmania, including the famous rescue of two miners in 2006, and explore the interactive exhibits and displays housed in the restored heritage building.

  • Grubb Shaft Gold and Heritage Museum: See a range of mining machinery, artifacts, and gold specimens from the early days of mining in the region. You can also pan for gold and learn about the history of Chinese miners in Tasmania.

Natural Attractions of Beauty Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Narawntapu National Park: This coastal reserve is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including kangaroos, wallabies, and wombats, making it ideal for wildlife viewing. Visitors can also explore the bushwalking trails and enjoy scenic views of the sea and surrounding countryside.

  • Lighthouse Point: This area provides a scenic coastal view of the Tamar River and surrounding countryside. The historic lighthouse has been converted into self-contained accommodation for overnight stays.

  • Beauty Point Waterfront Park: Take a stroll along the waterfront, have a picnic, or enjoy a spot of fishing at this picturesque park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beauty Point

  • Narawntapu National Park: With a variety of walking trails ranging from easy to difficult, you can choose the length and level of your hike. Don’t forget to bring a camera to capture the beautiful natural scenery along the way.

  • Tamar River Conservation Area: This area offers a range of leisurely walks along the banks of the Tamar River, with opportunities for bird watching along the way.

  • Mountain Bike Trails: Explore the natural environment of Beauty Point via mountain biking. Trails such as Greens Beach, Kelso, and Hollybank offer exciting and challenging terrains.

Best Things to Do in Beauty Point: Activities and Tours

  • Tamar Valley Wine Tours: Beauty Point is situated in the heart of the Tamar Valley wine region, which means there’s a lot of local wine to sample. Join a guided tour to experience all the area has to offer.

  • Fishing Tour: Take a guided fishing tour to catch a variety of fish in the Tamar River or the nearby ocean. You’ll also get the chance to relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

  • Tamar Island Wetlands Centre: Explore the wetlands and see a range of birdlife in their natural habitats. The centre also offers guided walks and bird watching tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Beauty Point

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scallop Pie: This iconic Tasmanian dish is a flaky pastry filled with scallops, potatoes, and peas.

  • Abalone: Another local favorite, abalone is often served pan-fried or in a creamy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Tamar Valley Wine: Sample the region’s award-winning wines, including Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Riesling.

  • Boag’s Beer: Tasmania’s favorite beer is made using pure Tasmanian water and high-quality hops.

Where to Stay in Beauty Point: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Peppers Silo Hotel: This luxury hotel is situated in an old grain silo and offers stunning views of the Tamar River.

  • Beauty Point Waterfront Hotel: Located right next to the water, this hotel is perfect for those who want to be close to the action.

  • Beauty Point Tourist Park: A wide range of accommodations such as camping sites, cabins, and motel-style rooms are available here.

What to Buy in Beauty Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tasmanian Saffron: Grown right in the Tamar Valley, Tasmanian saffron is considered some of the world’s best, and it can be the perfect gift.

  • Timber Crafts: Local artisans create beautiful and unique wooden crafts, including cutting boards, bowls, and platters.

Local Culture and Customs of Beauty Point

  • Aboriginal Culture: The land on which Beauty Point is situated has been inhabited by the Tasmanian Aboriginal people for thousands of years. Visitors can learn about their culture and visit cultural sites throughout the region.

  • Fishing Culture: Fishing is a big part of life in Beauty Point, and visitors can watch the local fishermen bring in their catches at the wharf or take a guided fishing tour themselves.

Best Time to Visit Beauty Point

The best time to visit Beauty Point is in the summer months, from December to February when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is beautiful year-round, with each season offering its own unique attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beauty Point

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and the local wildlife

  • Take a guided tour to learn about the local history and culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

  • Fish without obtaining the necessary permits

Safety and Health

Safety in Beauty Point

Beauty Point is a very safe town to visit, but visitors should exercise caution when swimming at the beach or engaging in outdoor activities in more isolated areas.

Health in Beauty Point

The town has an excellent public health system, and visitors can access medical care at the local hospital or one of the many medical clinics in the area.

Getting to Beauty Point: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beauty Point

The nearest major airport to Beauty Point is Launceston Airport, which is located approximately 60 kilometers away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Beauty Point

Several bus companies operate services to Beauty Point, including Tassie Link and Redline Coaches. Visitors can catch a bus from Launceston, which takes around 45 minutes.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to reach Beauty Point, and visitors can rent a car from Launceston Airport or in the town itself.

How to Get Around in Beauty Point

Walking is the easiest way to get around Beauty Point, with most attractions being located within walking distance of each other. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from Beauty Point: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Launceston: Tasmania’s second-largest city is just a 40-minute drive from Beauty Point and offers a range of cultural attractions, restaurants, and shopping.

  • Cradle Mountain: For those looking for some adventure, Cradle Mountain offers hiking trails, wildlife sightings, and stunning views of the Tasmanian wilderness.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Western Tasmania or learn about the local culture and history, Beauty Point has something for everyone. With its range of attractions, activities, and accommodations, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this charming coastal town.
