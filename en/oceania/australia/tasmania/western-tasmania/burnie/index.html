Introduction to Burnie

Welcome to Burnie, a charming coastal city located in Western Tasmania, Australia. Burnie is known for its rugged natural beauty, historical sites, and unique cultural experiences. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, history buff, or just seeking a relaxing beach vacation, Burnie has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Burnie.

List of Places to Visit in Burnie

Here are some of the top places to visit in Burnie:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Burnie Regional Museum
  • Maker’s Workshop
  • Emu Valley Rhododendron Garden
  • Lactos Cheese Factory
  • Fernglade Reserve

Natural Attractions of Burnie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Burnie Park
  • Hellyer Gorge
  • Ridgley Beach
  • Guide Falls Reserve

Burnie offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Tasmania. Here are some of the top routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Coastal Walk
  • Fern Glade Track
  • Hellyer Gorge Track
  • Ridgley Highway
  • Blackwood River Track

Best Things to Do in Burnie: Activities and Tours

From relaxing beach vacations to thrilling outdoor adventures, Burnie offers plenty of activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Burnie:

  • Visit the Regional Museum and learn about Burnie’s history
  • Go on a scenic drive along the North West Coast
  • Take a tour of the Lactos Cheese Factory
  • Go surfing or swimming at Burnie Beach
  • Participate in a guided rainforest walk

Week-Long Itinerary for Burnie

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Burnie:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Burnie Regional Museum, Walk along Burnie Beach
Day 2 Take a scenic drive along the North West Coast, visit Emu Valley Rhododendron Garden
Day 3 Visit Maker’s Workshop, Enjoy a guided rainforest walk at Hellyer Gorge
Day 4 Visit Lactos Cheese Factory, Go swimming at Ridgley Beach
Day 5 Visit Fernglade Reserve, Go on a scenic drive along the Ridgley Highway
Day 6 Explore Savage River National Park
Day 7 Visit Guide Falls Reserve, Go on a coastal walk

Local Culture and Customs of Burnie

Burnie is a city rich in culture and heritage. The friendly locals and unique experiences make it a must-visit destination in Tasmania. While visiting Burnie, remember to be respectful of local customs and traditions, and embrace the relaxed vibe of the city.

Best Time to Visit Burnie

The best time to visit Burnie is during the spring and summer months (September through February), when temperatures are mild and ideal for outdoor activities and beach vacations. However, Burnie is a great destination to visit year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burnie

Do:

  • Try Stopping at Ridgley Beach to watch the sunset
  • Take a guided tour of the Lactos Cheese Factory , which offers fantastic dairy products
  • Enjoy the beautiful natural scenery and wildlife

Don’t:

  • Litter to maintain the natural beauty of Burnie
  • Disturb the wildlife by throwing food.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burnie

Burnie is a safe city to visit, but it is always important to exercise caution. It is recommended that you avoid walking alone at night and always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Burnie

There are no health concerns specific to Burnie, but it is always best to travel with proper insurance and consult with a doctor before traveling. Maintain a healthy lifestyle by drinking plenty of water, wearing sunscreen, and staying active.

Getting to Burnie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burnie

The Burnie Airport is located approximately 20 minutes from the city center. It is serviced by airlines including Regional Express, Virgin Australia, and Qantas Airways.

Bus Stations Near Burnie

Burnie is serviced by several bus companies, including Tassielink Transit, Redline Coaches, and Merseylink.

Other Options

Renting a car or driving to Burnie is also a great option and gives you the flexibility to stop at surrounding areas along the way.

How to Get Around in Burnie

Burnie is a small city and easy to navigate. Public transportation options include taxis and buses. However, the best way to properly explore Burnie’s many attractions is by renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Burnie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Burnie is located in close proximity to several other popular Tasmanian destinations, including Launceston and Devonport. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Visit Cradle Mountain National Park
  • Explore the stunning beaches in Devonport
  • Visit Launceston’s historical landmarks and attractions

Conclusion

Burnie is a charming and unique destination that offers something for every kind of traveler. From exploring the natural landscape to enjoying the city’s rich culture and history, Burnie is a must-visit destination in Western Tasmania. We hope this travel guide helps you plan your visit to Burnie and experience all this beautiful city has to offer.
