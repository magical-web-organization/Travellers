Introduction to Walls of Jerusalem National Park

Nestled in the highlands of Western Tasmania, Walls of Jerusalem National Park offers a pristine wilderness experience with its alpine lakes, bare summits, and wildlife. The park is renowned for its hiking trails, which provide access to some of the most spectacular alpine scenery on the island. In this travel guide, we will explore everything you need to know about planning a trip to Walls of Jerusalem National Park and experiencing its remarkable natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Walls of Jerusalem National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Walls of Jerusalem National Park is primarily known for its natural attractions, it also has deep cultural and historical significance. The Aboriginal people have lived in this region for tens of thousands of years, and the area has been a site of pilgrimage for thousands of years. A visit to Walls of Jerusalem National Park will not be complete without taking the time to understand the cultural significance of the region. Here are some must-see culturally important attractions in Walls of Jerusalem National Park:

  • Mawson’s Hut Replica Museum
  • Queen Victoria Museum & Art Gallery
  • National Automobile Museum of Tasmania

Natural Attractions of Walls of Jerusalem National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Walls of Jerusalem National Park is best known for its stunning natural scenery, which offers diverse opportunities for adventure and exploration. The park’s trail system is extensive, with over 22 kilometers of well-marked and maintained tracks suitable for all levels of fitness and hiking experience. Here are some must-see natural attractions in Walls of Jerusalem National Park:

  • Lake Adelaide
  • Lake Ball
  • Dixons Kingdom

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walls of Jerusalem National Park

Walls of Jerusalem National Park is a hiker’s paradise, featuring a network of over 100 kilometers of hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the park’s rugged peaks, alpine lakes, and rivers. Some of the most popular hiking trails in the park include:

  • Walls of Jerusalem Circuit
  • Lake Ball Track
  • Herods Gate to Dixons Kingdom

Biking is also an excellent way to explore Walls of Jerusalem National Park. Mountain biking is permitted on all the park’s trails except for the Overland Track, which is reserved for hikers only.

Best Things to Do in Walls of Jerusalem National Park: Activities and Tours

Walls of Jerusalem National Park offers a range of activities and tours to suit all interests and skill levels. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or a first-time visitor, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this beautiful national park. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Summit Mt. Jerusalem - The highest peak in the park
  • Explore the Walls of Jerusalem Circuit - A diverse and beautiful walking track that takes you to stunning alpine landscapes
  • Experience the wildlife - Keep an eye out for Tasmanian devils, wallabies, echidnas and a variety of bird life

Food and Drink Scene of Walls of Jerusalem National Park

The Walls of Jerusalem National Park does not have many dining options within the park boundaries. However, there are plenty of places to eat in the surrounding towns and villages. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to sample in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian cheese platters

Where to Stay in Walls of Jerusalem National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Walls of Jerusalem National Park offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. There are a number of camping sites scattered throughout the park, and many visitors choose to go bushwalking and camping. However, if you are looking for more comfort, there are several hotels, guesthouses, and hostels nearby. Here are some recommended choices:

  • Arthurs Lake Chalet
  • The Wilderness Gallery
  • Central Highlands Lodge

Local Culture and Customs of Walls of Jerusalem National Park

The Walls of Jerusalem National Park is a special place in Tasmanian Aboriginal history. It is a place known for traditional stories, dance, and song which reflects deeply upon the importance of this sacred site. Please be considerate when walking around the park as sacred sites are thought to be found in certain parts of the park. Respect local culture and follow local customs while on your journey.

Best Time to Visit Walls of Jerusalem National Park

The best time to visit Walls of Jerusalem National Park is from November to March, which is the peak season due to the mild temperatures and ideal hiking conditions. However, the park is open all year round and visitors can still enjoy snowshoeing and skiing tracks in the winter months of June to August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walls of Jerusalem National Park

Do:

  • Pack insect repellent and sunscreen
  • Dress in layers as weather can change suddenly
  • Take ample water supplies with you and appropriate snack
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles to protect the environment

Don’t:

  • Climb on walls and monuments that may be considered sacred
  • Remove any objects from the land, such as stones or anything with cultural significance
  • Use wild woods or plant life for fires

Safety and Health

Safety in Walls of Jerusalem National Park

Walls of Jerusalem National Park is a remote and wild area, where visitors may experience dangers that include sharp cliffs, strong wind and chilly water. The conditions can be extremely harsh at times and it is important to be prepared when out. Some safety tips to keep in mind are:

  • Check weather forecasts and be prepared to adjust plans accordingly
  • Register at the Parks and Wildlife office prior to commencing your visit
  • Stick to maintained tracks and avoid off-track travel

Health in Walls of Jerusalem National Park

In general, Tasmania is a low-risk region in terms of infection. However, it is important to take proper precautions to avoid food and waterborne illness, insect bites and heat exhaustion. Some tips for staying healthy during a visit are:

  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration
  • Cover yourself with long-sleeved clothing and insect repellent
  • Learn basic wilderness first aid
  • Bring any medications you may need, along with a first aid kit

Getting to Walls of Jerusalem National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Walls of Jerusalem National Park is located about two hours’ drive from Launceston in Tasmania’s north. There are a few different transportation options to consider when planning your visit:

Airports Near Walls of Jerusalem National Park

  • Launceston Airport: 140 km / 2 hrs drive
  • Hobart Airport: 184 km / 2 hrs, 30 min drive

Bus Stations Near Walls of Jerusalem National Park

  • Launceston Bus Station to Derwent Bridge: 129 km
  • Hobart Bus Station to Derwent Bridge: 175 km

Other Options

  • Public Transport mobile app provide information on transport via buses, shuttles and taxis from a variety of locations.
  • Car hire or private taxi services: inquire with local tourist information centers or transport companies.

How to Get Around in Walls of Jerusalem National Park

Visitors may park their cars at the park’s entry point, and then proceed to explore the park on foot, bicycles or water-crafts.

Where to Go Next from Walls of Jerusalem National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Once you have finished exploring Walls of Jerusalem National Park, there are still plenty of other places to discover in Tasmania. Some nearby destinations to consider visiting include:

  • Lake St Clair National Park
  • Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park
  • Bay of Fires
  • Wineglass Bay

Conclusion

Walls of Jerusalem National Park is a unique, beautiful and culturally significant destination. The park attracts visitors from around the world with its stunning natural scenery, rugged wilderness, alpine hiking trails, and unique culture. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or a first-time visitor, Walls of Jerusalem National Park is sure to leave you feeling inspired with its remarkable splendor.
