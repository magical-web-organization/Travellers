Introduction to Latrobe

Latrobe is a small town located in Western Tasmania, Australia. It is situated in the Mersey Valley, a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Visitors to Latrobe can explore the town’s rich history, enjoy scenic views of the surroundings, and participate in outdoor activities like hiking and biking. In this travel guide, we will help you plan your trip to Latrobe, highlighting the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Latrobe

Latrobe has a range of attractions to suit every interest. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • House of Anvers Chocolate Factory and Museum: This museum showcases the history and production of chocolate. It also sells handmade chocolates and fudges made on-site.
  • Latrobe Regional Gallery: The gallery features local and international exhibitions of contemporary visual arts and artefacts.
  • Latrobe Courthouse: A historic courthouse where you can learn about the history of the area and its legal system.
  • Australian Axeman’s Hall of Fame: This museum celebrates timber sports and features historical and modern-day events, equipment, and personalities.

Natural Attractions of Latrobe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mersey Bluff: This bluff offers stunning views of the town, the valley, and nearby beaches and is perfect for picnics and sunsets.
  • Mersey River: Kayak or raft along the river for an enjoyable experience with nature at its finest.
  • Don River Railway: Ride on the restored steam train for a scenic tour and see the historical mining towns and old fields along the way.
  • Warrawee Reserve: A bushland reserve offering several walking trails suitable for all levels of hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Latrobe

Latrobe has many beautiful trails and walking tracks to enjoy. Here are some popular options:

  • Warrawee Reserve Walking Trails: Multiple trails that range from easy to challenging, offering diverse scenery.
  • Tasmanian Trail: Goes through Latrobe as part of 480 km (300 miles) of mixed-use trails that connect Devonport to Dover and beyond.
  • Wild Mersey Mountain Bike Trails: A system of trails that includes beginner, intermediate and advanced level rides.

Best Things to Do in Latrobe: Activities and Tours

  • House of Anvers Chocolate Factory Tour: Take the self-guided tour to learn about the chocolate-making process and sample the handmade chocolates.
  • Mersey Bluff Lighthouse: Visit the Mersey Bluff Lighthouse for stunning coastal views and a heritage museum.
  • Giant Tasmanian Freshwater Lobster: The largest of its kind in the world, it can be seen in the Don River and is a rare species.
  • Golfing: The Latrobe Golf Club has a challenging course and beautiful views of the Mersey River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Latrobe

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Latrobe:

  • Day 1: Explore the town and visit the attractions, such as the House of Anvers and Courthouse.
  • Day 2: Take a tour at the mining town of Beaconsfield or visit the nearby Narawntapu National Park.
  • Day 3-5: Explore the countryside and discover hiking trails, such as Warrawee Reserve Walking Trails.
  • Day 6: Golf at the Latrobe Golf Course and visit other local attractions.
  • Day 7: Take a leisurely canoe trip down the Mersey River and enjoy the natural surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Latrobe

Latrobe has a range of dining options for every taste bud. Here are some must-have dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scallop Pie: A famous savory dish made with Tasmanian scallops.
  • Wild Mushroom Risotto: A delicious vegetarian option that uses local and wild ingredients.
  • Atlantic Salmon: A local classic that is served fresh.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Cider: The area has some of the best ciders, and a visit to Red Brick Road Cider House is a must-do for any cidery lover.
  • Coffee: Local coffee shops serve delicious brews made with Tasmanian coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Latrobe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Latrobe has many accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Lucinda B&B: An award-winning, heritage-listed B&B with comfortable and beautiful rooms.
  • Latrobe Art Apartments: Modern and convenient apartments in the town center.
  • Huntsman Lake Retreat: A luxury retreat nestled in the Tasmanian wilderness, perfect for couples.

What to Buy in Latrobe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Apart from chocolates and fudges from House of Anvers, here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Visit the heritage-listed Studio and Gallery of Les Blakebrough for the best ceramic pieces.
  • Tasmanian Timber Products: The Timberworks Gallery sells unique wooden pieces, including furniture and sculptures.
  • Tasmanian Wines: The region produces some of the best wines in the country, such as those from Wines for Joanie Vineyard.

Local Culture and Customs of Latrobe

Being a small town, Latrobe has a friendly culture where people are welcoming and hospitable. Like most Australian towns, there is a laid-back attitude towards life, and the locals value the area’s wildlife and natural scenery. Respectful behavior and manners are appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Latrobe

The best time to visit Latrobe is during the months of November to April, which are the warmest months of the year. This time also coincides with the tourist season, and most attractions and activities are open to the public. However, if you prefer a quieter time, you can visit during the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Latrobe

Here are some helpful tips for visiting Latrobe:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Carry a picnic or stop for lunch at one of the town’s cafes.
  • Learn about local history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter, or leave trash behind.
  • Disturb the peaceful environment by making loud noises.
  • Forget to carry — and drink — plenty of water when hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety and health in Latrobe are generally not a concern, and the town has a low crime rate. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as wearing appropriate clothing, carrying a map, and staying hydrated. If you have pre-existing medical conditions, it is essential to bring medication and consult with your doctor before travelling.

Getting to Latrobe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Latrobe is easily accessible through different means of transport. Here are some popular options:

Airports Near Latrobe

  • Devonport Airport: 14 km (9 miles) from Latrobe
  • Launceston Airport: 95 km (59 miles) from Latrobe

Bus Stations Near Latrobe

  • Merseylink Bus Station, Devonport: 10 km (6 miles) from Latrobe
  • Burnie Bus Station: 45 km (28 miles) from Latrobe

Other Options

  • Hire a car and drive to Latrobe by taking the Bass Highway.

How to Get Around in Latrobe

Latrobe is a small town, and most attractions and places to visit are easily accessible. Walking and cycling are the most popular modes of transportation within the town. If you plan to explore further, hiring a car or taking public transport is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Latrobe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Cradle Mountain: A national park famous for its striking landscape and diverse wildlife, just 80 km (50 miles) away.
  • Launceston: A vibrant city known for its cultural scene and beautiful parks, 100 km (62 miles) away.
  • Stanley: A historic town filled with heritage buildings and stunning coastline views, around 110 km (68 miles) away.

Conclusion

Latrobe is a hidden gem, offering many attractions and activities that make it a must-visit destination in Western Tasmania. From enjoying chocolate at the House of Anvers to hiking the Warrawee Reserve trails, the town caters to different interests. With this travel guide, your trip to Latrobe is sure to be a memorable one.
