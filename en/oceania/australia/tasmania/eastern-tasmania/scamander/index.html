Introduction to Scamander

Scamander is a small coastal town located in Eastern Tasmania, Australia. Situated on the banks of the Scamander River, the town is home to about 700 residents. It is a popular destination for beachgoers, nature lovers, and outdoor enthusiasts, offering breathtaking landscapes, thrilling adventures, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Scamander

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Scamander Museum and Heritage Centre: See displays and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Scamander and its surrounding areas.
  • St. Mary’s by the Sea: A restored church that dates back to the 19th century and has become a popular venue for weddings and other events.

Natural Attractions of Scamander: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scamander Beach: Relax on the soft sand and swim in the clear waters of this pristine beach.
  • Scamander Reserve: Go for a hike or a bike ride in this beautiful reserve, which offers scenic views of the coastline and wildlife sightings.
  • Douglas-Apsley National Park: A protected wilderness area with amazing waterfalls, walking tracks, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scamander

  • Scamander River Trail: A scenic 10 km trail that follows the banks of the Scamander River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • St. Helens Point Conservation Area: This 3.3 km trail takes you to the top of St. Helens Point, where you can see panoramic views of Tasmania’s north-east coast.

Best Things to Do in Scamander: Activities and Tours

  • Beachcombing: Collect seashells, driftwood, and other treasures while strolling along Scamander Beach.
  • Fishing: Go fishing in Scamander River or on the beach, and try your luck catching species such as bream, salmon, and flathead.
  • Kayaking: Explore the tranquil waters of Scamander River by kayak, and see the natural beauty of the area up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Scamander

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Platter: Sample the freshest seafood caught and prepared locally.
  • Steak and Ale Pie: A meat pie made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Craft Beer: Choose from a variety of locally brewed beers that reflect Tasmania’s unique culture.
  • Tasmanian Wine: Explore Tasmania’s vineyards and taste their flavorful wines, grown in the cool-climate regions of the state.

Where to Stay in Scamander: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pelican Sands Scamander: Located directly on Scamander Beach, this resort offers waterfront accommodation and easy access to the beach.
  • Scamander Sanctuary Holiday Park: A family-friendly camping and caravan park that offers comfortable and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Scamander: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork: Discover the unique artwork created by local artists, ranging from paintings to sculptures.
  • Fudge: Sample and take home some high-quality fudge made with Tasmanian ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Scamander

Scamander is a small town that prides itself on its relaxed atmosphere and friendly locals. The area has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its museums, galleries, and festivals. Visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local community by attending events, talking to locals, and learning about the area’s traditions.

Best Time to Visit Scamander

The best time to visit Scamander is between December and February, during the warm summer season. This is the peak tourist season, and visitors can enjoy the beach, outdoor activities, and festivals. However, the shoulder seasons between September and November and March to May offer milder weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scamander

Do:

  • Respect the local environment.
  • Try the local seafood.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore warning signs at beaches or wildlife reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scamander

Scamander is generally a safe town. However, it is always advisable to take basic safety precautions such as locking your car and not leaving valuables unattended. When hiking or exploring the local area, it is important to follow the designated trails, pack adequate supplies, and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Scamander

Medical facilities are limited in Scamander. Visitors with specific medical needs should check with their doctor before travelling and ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance.

Getting to Scamander: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scamander

The closest major airport to Scamander is Launceston Airport, which is about a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Scamander

Tassielink operates regular bus services connecting Scamander to Hobart and other major cities in Tasmania.

Other Options

Driving yourself is the most convenient way to reach Scamander, and is recommended if you are planning to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Scamander

The best way to get around Scamander is by car, as it will give you the freedom to explore the area at your own pace. However, there are also local taxi and bus services available, as well as bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Scamander: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. Helens: A scenic coastal town that is known for its fishing and beautiful beaches.
  • Bay of Fires: A stunning coastline filled with white sand beaches, turquoise waters, and unique geological formations.
  • Freycinet National Park: A protected wilderness area with world-renowned hiking trails, secluded beaches, and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation, a thrilling outdoor adventure, or a taste of Tasmania’s unique culture and history, Scamander has something to offer. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of the world.
