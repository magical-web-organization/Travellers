A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Perth, Tasmania

Introduction to Perth

Perth is a charming coastal town located in Eastern Tasmania, Australia. This scenic town is known for its beautiful beaches, historical landmarks, and rich culture. With numerous outdoor activities and exciting tours, Perth, Tasmania is a perfect tourist destination for all kinds of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Perth

Perth is home to several culturally important and natural attractions. Here are some of the most popular tourist spots in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Port Arthur Historic Site: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this former prison settlement is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Tasmanian Museum and Art Gallery: This museum displays exhibits on Tasmania’s natural history, indigenous culture, and contemporary art.
  • St. John’s Anglican Church: This historic church is one of the oldest stone buildings in Australia.

Natural Attractions of Perth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Wellington: One of the highest peaks in Tasmania, Mount Wellington offers breathtaking views of Hobart and the surrounding areas.
  • Tasman Arch: This natural geological formation is a top destination for sightseeing.
  • Maria Island National Park: A protected island, this national park is a haven for wildlife, nature lovers, and hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perth

Perth has several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore its gorgeous landscapes. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Three Capes Track: A challenging but rewarding walking trail, this track takes you through stunning coastal views and pristine wilderness.
  • Waterfall Bay to Fortescue Bay Track: This hike is popular for its scenic beauty and wildlife sightings.
  • Mount Field National Park: This park offers several hiking trails with varying levels of difficulty, making it a favorite destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Perth: Activities and Tours

Perth offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Perth:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Tasmania is known for its award-winning wines, and a wine tour is a refreshing way to explore the local vineyards and savour the diverse flavors.
  • Caving Tours: The Mole Creek Caves are a natural wonder, and a caving tour is a thrilling adventure for those looking for something more challenging.
  • Whitewater Rafting: The rapids of the rivers in Tasmania offer an exciting adventure for adrenaline junkies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perth

A week in Perth can be a perfect opportunity to explore the town’s culture and natural beauty. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Hobart, the capital city of Tasmania, and explore the local attractions such as the Salamanca Market, Battery Point and Constitution Dock.

Day 2: Visit the Port Arthur Historic Site and take a guided tour of the prison settlement.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to Wineglass Bay via Freycinet National Park.

Day 4: Visit the Museum of Old and New Art for a contemporary art experience or the Tasmanian Museum and Art Gallery for a historical perspective.

Day 5: Explore the natural wonders of Mount Wellington and the Tasman Arch.

Day 6: Experience the thrill of a whitewater rafting adventure.

Day 7: Bid farewell to Perth and Tasmanian as you depart from Hobart airport.

Food and Drink Scene of Perth

Perth offers several dining options that let you sample the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood: Perth is renowned for its fresh and flavorful seafood dishes, with the fish and chips at the Dunalley Fish Market being a particular favorite.
  • Hearty pies: Get a taste of local flavor with the savory pies sold in bakeries around the town
  • Chocolate: Hand-crafted chocolates are a particular specialty of the area, with notable shops like Federation Chocolate offering a range of delicacies.

Local Beverages

  • Wines: The wine regions of Tasmania produce some of the finest wines in Australia, and Wineglass Bay is a great spot for wine tastings.
  • Beer: Check out the Iron House Brewery in Port Arthur for a range of delicious artisan beers.

Where to Stay in Perth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perth has several accommodation options for visitors, from hotels to hostels to guesthouses. Some recommended places to stay include:

  • Lufra Hotel and Apartments: This beautiful waterfront hotel offers stunning views and comfortable rooms.
  • Port Arthur Motor Inn: A cozy and welcoming inn within walking distance of the historic site.
  • Eaglehawk Neck Beach House: A beachfront guesthouse with beautiful views of the ocean.

What to Buy in Perth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perth is home to several boutiques and specialty stores that sell unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best places to shop include:

  • Federation Chocolate: This hand-crafted chocolate shop offers unique and tasty souvenirs to take home.
  • Tasmanian Devil Unzoo: The cultural center at the Unzoo is a great place to shop for souvenirs, including local honey products and hand-woven scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Perth

Perth has a distinct cultural heritage, reflected in the town’s arts, festivals, and traditions. Visitors can explore the history and customs through guided tours, museums, and cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Perth

Perth has a temperate climate with mild summers and cool winters. The best time to visit Perth is from December to February when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities, although this is also peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perth

Do:

  • Be respectful of the town’s cultural heritage and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor adventures and scenic views the town offers.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt the natural environment by littering or damaging the ecosystems.
  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.
  • Disrespect other visitors or locals in the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perth

Perth is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard precautions and avoid leaving valuables unattended or isolated areas at night.

Health in Perth

Tasmania has a high-quality health care system. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance and check to ensure their existing medical insurance provides coverage in Tasmania.

Getting to Perth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perth is located about 1.5 hours drive from Hobart, the capital of Tasmania. Other transportation options include:

  • Air: Hobart International Airport is the nearest airport, which has direct flights from Melbourne, Brisbane, and Sydney.
  • Road: Visitors can drive to Perth from Hobart and other nearby towns using local transportation or rental cars.

Airports Near Perth

  • Hobart International Airport, 50km away

Bus Stations Near Perth

  • Tassie Link, Swansea (30 minutes away)

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to travel around the town.

How to Get Around Perth

Perth is a small town, and walking and cycling are popular ways to get around. Local travel agencies also provide transportation to popular attractions.

Where to Go Next from Perth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perth is an ideal starting point for exploring Tasmania’s scenic landscapes and beautiful towns. Nearby destinations include:

  • Hobart: The capital city of Tasmania, Hobart is home to several historical landmarks, museums, and art galleries.
  • Freycinet National Park: Known for its beautiful white sand beaches, spectacular birdlife and rich Aboriginal history.
  • Bruny Island, named one of the top experiences in Australia by Lonely Planet, is located south of Hobart and offers remarkable beaches, wildlife, and gourmet food.

Conclusion

Perth, Tasmania, is a charming coastal town that offers a mix of nature, culture, and adventure. Visitors can explore its rich history and natural beauty through tours and outdoor activities, sample the local cuisine and wine, and take home unique souvenirs. With its scenic landscapes and friendly locals, Perth is a must-visit destination for those traveling to Tasmania.
