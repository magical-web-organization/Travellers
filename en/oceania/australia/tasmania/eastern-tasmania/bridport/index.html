Introduction to Bridport

Welcome to Bridport, a charming town on the north-east coast of Tasmania. Known for its pristine beaches, rugged coastline, and exceptional natural beauty, Bridport is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, this town has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all the best things to see, do, and eat in Bridport. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Bridport

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Bridport:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bridport Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Bridport in this fascinating museum, which features exhibits about the town’s maritime and agricultural heritage.
  • Bridestowe Lavender Estate: Explore the beautiful lavender fields and learn about the production process at this stunning estate, which is home to one of the largest lavender farms in the southern hemisphere.

Natural Attractions of Bridport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount William National Park: Hike one of the many trails in this stunning national park, which features rugged coastlines, secluded beaches, and abundant wildlife.
  • Billycock Hill: Enjoy panoramic views of Bridport and the surrounding countryside from this popular lookout point.
  • Barnbougle Dunes: Tee off at one of the world’s top golf courses, located in the heart of Bridport and overlooking the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridport

Bridport is home to several outstanding hiking and biking trails that take you through its breathtaking natural landscapes. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Bridport Walking Track: This 5 km trail takes you through bushland, along the beach, and up rocky cliffs to stunning views of the ocean and the town.
  • The Blue Tier Trail: A challenging 42 km ride through the rugged Blue Tier mountains, this trail offers incredible views of the surrounding forests and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Bridport: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Bridport:

  • Fishing: Bridport is famous for its excellent fishing opportunities, with a variety of game fish such as trout, salmon, and bream found in the town’s rivers and bays.
  • Surfing and swimming: With several beaches to choose from, Bridport offers some of the best surfing and swimming in Tasmania.
  • Wine tasting: Sample some of the region’s best wines at Pipers Brook Vineyard, which offers tastings, tours, and a picturesque picnic area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bridport

If you are planning a week-long trip to Bridport, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Bridport

Bridport is home to several excellent restaurants, cafes, and vineyards that showcase the best of local Tasmanian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian seafood: Freshly caught fish, oysters, and crayfish are highlights of the town’s seafood scene.
  • Bridestowe lavender ice cream: A unique and delicious dessert made with lavender grown on the Bridestowe Lavender Estate.
  • Wallaby burger: A local specialty that features a patty made from wallaby meat, a lean and flavorful alternative to beef.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian whisky: The town’s many distilleries produce some of the best whiskies in Australia.
  • Pipers River wine: This cooler climate region is known for its exceptional Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and sparkling wines.
  • Bridestowe lavender tea: A soothing and aromatic tea made from the town’s famous lavender flowers.

Where to Stay in Bridport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels, Bridport offers a range of accommodation options for every traveler. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Barnbougle: A world-class golf resort with luxury rooms and suites overlooking the ocean.
  • Bridport Bay Inn: A charming historic hotel with comfortable rooms and a popular restaurant.
  • Bridport Holiday House: A spacious and well-equipped vacation rental that can accommodate families and large groups.

What to Buy in Bridport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to remind you of your trip to Bridport? Here are some local products worth bringing home:

  • Bridestowe lavender products: From essential oils to soaps and sachets, there are plenty of ways to enjoy the town’s signature scent.
  • Tasmanian honey: The island’s many types of honey are prized for their unique flavors and health benefits.
  • Handcrafted wood pieces: Browse the local craft markets for beautiful and functional items made from Tasmanian timber.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridport

Bridport has a rich culture and heritage that is reflected in its buildings, museums, and festivals. Here are some local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Agriculture: Many locals work in farming and agriculture, producing crops like potatoes, lavender, and grapes.
  • Fishing: Fishing is an important industry for the town, and many locals enjoy fishing as a recreational activity as well.
  • Music festivals: Bridport and the surrounding region host several popular music festivals each year, including the Taste of Tasmania festival and the Boho Festival.

Best Time to Visit Bridport

The best time to visit Bridport is between December and February, when the weather is warm and sunny and the beaches are at their best. However, the town is beautiful year-round and has plenty to offer visitors in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bridport

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bridport:

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and wine.
  • Respect the natural environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or damage natural features.
  • Leave rubbish or litter behind.
  • Ignore safety warnings or ocean conditions when swimming or surfing.

Safety and Health

Bridport is a safe and healthy place to visit, but it’s important to be aware of potential hazards and risks. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Bridport

  • Take care when swimming or surfing, and always check ocean conditions and safety warnings before entering the water.
  • Be aware of the risk of bushfires during the summer months, and follow local advice and warnings.
  • Take care when driving on the town’s rural roads, which can be narrow and winding.

Health in Bridport

  • Australia has a comprehensive healthcare system, and Bridport has a medical center and pharmacy for basic healthcare needs.
  • It’s a good idea to bring insect repellent and sunscreen to protect against mosquitoes and sunburn.
  • Tap water is safe to drink in Bridport and throughout Tasmania.

Getting to Bridport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bridport is located about 85 km north-east of Launceston, the nearest major city. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around Bridport:

Airports Near Bridport

  • Launceston Airport: Located about 90 minutes by car from Bridport, Launceston Airport offers flights to major Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Bridport

  • Scottsdale Bus Station: Regular bus services run from Launceston to Scottsdale, with connections to Bridport available from there.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a popular option for exploring the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Bridport and the surrounding area, but can be expensive for longer journeys.

How to Get Around in Bridport

Most of Bridport’s attractions and amenities are within walking distance of the town center. However, if you want to explore further afield, here are some transportation options:

  • Bike rental: Several bike rental companies operate in Bridport, allowing you to explore the town and its surroundings at your own pace.
  • Local buses: There is a local bus service that runs between Bridport and nearby towns and attractions.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the surrounding countryside and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bridport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bridport is located in a beautiful region of Tasmania that is home to many other attractions and destinations. Here are some nearby places worth visiting:

  • Launceston: Tasmania’s second-largest city is home to historic buildings, art galleries, and gourmet food markets.
  • Bay of Fires: Just a short drive north of Bridport, this stunning coastline features white sand beaches, orange lichen-covered rocks, and crystal-clear waters.
  • The Blue Derby Trails: Mountain bike enthusiasts will love the world-class mountain bike trails of the Blue Derby, located just over an hour’s drive from Bridport.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Bridport. From natural attractions and outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and local cuisine, this charming Tasmanian town has something for everyone. We can’t wait for you to explore it for yourself!
