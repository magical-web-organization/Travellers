Introduction to Bicheno

Located on the east coast of Tasmania, Bicheno is a small town with pristine beaches, a rich cultural heritage, and abundant nature reserves. The town is named after James Ebenezer Bicheno, a colonial secretary and botanist who contributed significantly to the development of Tasmania.

Bicheno offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, such as snorkelling, scuba diving, hiking, and wildlife spotting. The town also has a notable food and drink scene, with fresh seafood and local produce that are sure to delight your taste buds.

This travel guide aims to help you plan your visit to Bicheno by providing insights into the top attractions, activities, and local culture of the town.

List of Places to Visit in Bicheno

Bicheno has a diverse range of places to explore. From historical sites and museums to natural reserves and outdoor activities, there is something to suit every taste. Here are some of the best places to visit in Bicheno.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bicheno History Room: Discover the rich colonial history of Bicheno at this small museum, which showcases artefacts and exhibits related to the town’s European and Aboriginal past.

  • Gulch Harbour: This historic harbour is home to a fleet of fishing vessels and was once the centre of the abalone industry in Tasmania. You can also find a picturesque boardwalk here.

  • Whalers Lookout: Overlooking the town and the ocean, this lookout offers panoramic views of the coast and the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Bicheno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bicheno Blowhole: Witness the raw power of nature at this popular attraction, where waves crash against the rocks and shoot up through a hole in the ground.

  • Douglas-Apsley National Park: Covering over 13,000 hectares, this reserve is a haven for wildlife and offers stunning walks and waterfalls.

  • Redbill Beach: This long, sandy beach is perfect for a leisurely walk or a dip in the ocean. Located just a few minutes drive from Bicheno town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bicheno

Bicheno has some of the most spectacular hiking and biking trails in Tasmania. From short and easy walks to challenging treks, there is something for every level of fitness and ability. Here are some hiking and biking trails to explore:

  • Bicheno Coastal Walk: This relaxing and scenic walk takes you along the town’s stunning coastline, taking in some of the best views that Bicheno has to offer.

  • Whalebone Point/Hovell’s Rocks: This trail is a must-do for rock climbers and adventure seekers who enjoy a challenge. It is also a great place to witness shorebirds and other coastal wildlife.

  • Douglas-Apsley National Park: Covering over 13,000 hectares, this reserve is a haven for wildlife and offers stunning waterfalls and a range of walking trails for all abilities.

Best Things to Do in Bicheno: Activities and Tours

Bicheno offers a range of activities and tour options for every kind of visitor. Whether you’re a nature lover, thrill-seeker or a foodie, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in Bicheno:

  • Snorkelling and scuba diving: Discover the ocean’s hidden treasures at Governor Island Marine Reserve or along the shoreline of Diamond Island.

  • Penguin Tours: Bicheno is home to a colony of Little Penguins, and there are guided tours available that offer a chance to see them up close and personal.

  • Wine Tours: Take a tour of the local wineries and sample some of the finest wines from the cool-climate vineyards of Tasmania.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bicheno

Here is a recommended itinerary for spending a week in Bicheno:

Day Activities
1 Take a walking tour of the town, visit the Bicheno History Room and watch the sunset at Whalers Lookout.
2 Explore the Douglas-Apsley National Park, relax on the beach and try some local cuisine at the restaurants in the town.
3 Go snorkelling or scuba diving at the Governor Island Marine Reserve and book an evening tour to see the Little Penguins.
4 Take a wine tour of the local vineyards or visit the Devil’s Corner Cellar Door for some wine tasting.
5 Hike the Bicheno Coastal Walk and admire the scenic views of the coast.
6 Join a tour to see the Bicheno Blowhole and take a picnic lunch at Redbill Beach.
7 Take a day trip to nearby Freycinet National Park and explore Wineglass Bay and Cape Tourville Lighthouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Bicheno

Bicheno has a thriving food and drink scene that serves up locally-sourced and sustainable produce. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood: Indulge in locally caught scallops, abalone, and lobster. Many restaurants offer a “catch of the day” option on their menus.

  • Tasmanian cheese platter: Tasmania produces some of the finest cheeses in the world, and you can sample a variety of them at any of the local cafes or restaurants.

  • Tasmanian Beef and lamb: Tasmania is also known for its premium beef and lamb, so be sure to try it while you are here.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian Wine: Tasmania is renowned for its premium cool-climate wines. Try the sparkling wines from Jansz or the Pinot Noirs from Devil’s Corner Cellar Door.

  • Beer: The local beer scene is thriving in Tasmania, and you can sample a range of artisanal brews at any of the local breweries.

  • Coffee: Tasmania has some of the best coffee in the country, and you can grab a cup at any of the small cafes in the town.

Where to Stay in Bicheno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options available in Bicheno to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you prefer a comfortable hotel room or a traditional bed and breakfast, Bicheno has something for everyone. Here are some of the top recommended places to stay:

  • Beachfront at Bicheno: This modern and luxurious hotel is located right on the beachfront and offers stunning ocean views. Rooms come equipped with a private balcony, spa bath and satellite TV.

  • Bicheno Hideaway: This self-contained accommodation has its own private entrance and is surrounded by lush gardens. Each unit comes equipped with a kitchenette and stunning mountain views.

  • Wintersun Gardens Motel: This affordable motel offers comfortable and clean rooms with a pool, spa and sauna. It is within walking distance of the town centre and nearby beaches.

What to Buy in Bicheno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bicheno offers a range of local products and souvenirs, perfect for bringing home a piece of Tasmania with you. Here are some of the best things to buy in Bicheno:

  • Tasmanian Whisky: Tasmania is renowned for its premium single malt whisky. Take a tour of the local distilleries and sample some of the best whiskies in the world.

  • Local honey: Tasmania has some of the purest honey in the world, and you can find a range of honey products at the local markets.

  • Local art and crafts: Bicheno has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you can find unique handmade items at the local galleries and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Bicheno

Bicheno has a rich cultural heritage, and it is important to understand and respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the local customs and traditions of Bicheno:

  • Bicheno was once home to the Paredarerme people, and their history and culture is still celebrated today.

  • The town has a strong fishing heritage, and many locals still make a living from the sea.

  • The locals are warm and welcoming, and it is not uncommon to strike up a conversation with a stranger.

Best Time to Visit Bicheno

Bicheno is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. The peak tourist season is from December to February, when the weather is warm and the beaches are busy. The shoulder seasons, from March to May and September to November, offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds. Winter from June to August is the quietest season, and you might be lucky enough to spot whales off the coast.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bicheno

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bicheno:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Take care of the environment and dispose of your rubbish properly.

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Pick or disturb the wildlife.

  • Go off-trail or venture into restricted areas.

  • Leave valuables unattended in your car or accommodation.

Safety and Health

When travelling to Bicheno, it is important to take precautions for your safety and health. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Bicheno

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

  • Take precautions when hiking, such as carrying plenty of water and letting someone know your route.

  • Drive carefully on the roads and watch out for wildlife.

Health in Bicheno

  • Tasmania has excellent healthcare, but it is recommended to have travel insurance.

  • Bring any necessary medication with you.

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Bicheno: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bicheno is easily accessible by car or bus, and there are several transportation options available.

Airports Near Bicheno

The nearest airport is Launceston Airport, which is approximately 2 hours drive from Bicheno. Other options include Hobart Airport, which is 2.5 hours drive away, and Devonport Airport, which is 3 hours drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bicheno

There are regular bus services that operate from both Hobart and Launceston. Redline and Tassielink Transit are two major bus companies that offer services to Bicheno.

Other Options

  • Driving: Bicheno is roughly 2.5 hours drive from both Launceston and Hobart.

  • Cycling: Bicheno is a bike-friendly town, and there are plenty of bike rental options available.

How to Get Around in Bicheno

Bicheno is a small town, and many of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, it is recommended to hire a car or bike. There are also local taxi services available to take you around town.

Where to Go Next from Bicheno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bicheno is an excellent base for exploring some of Tasmania’s top attractions. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can visit:

  • Freycinet National Park: This national park is home to the world-famous Wineglass Bay, as well as a range of hiking trails and stunning coastal scenery. It is just a 30-minute drive from Bicheno.

  • St Helens: This small town is known for its pristine beaches, fishing, and breathtaking views. It is just over an hour’s drive from Bicheno.

  • Maria Island National Park: This incredible island is home to an abundance of wildlife, including kangaroos, wombats, and wallabies. It is accessible by ferry from the nearby town of Triabunna.

Conclusion

Bicheno is a true gem of Tasmania, offering visitors a diverse range of attractions, activities, and local culture. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or a foodie, there is something for every taste in this charming coastal town. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your visit to Bicheno and that you enjoy your time here.
