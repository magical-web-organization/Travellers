Introduction to Ben Lomond National Park

Ben Lomond National Park is situated in Eastern Tasmania, Australia, and is a place of remarkable natural beauty. Sprawling over an area of more than 160 square kilometers, the national park is a home to several endemic species of flora and fauna, making it a prime location for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

The park is named after the Scottish mountain, Ben Lomond, which means “beacon hill”. Its unique landscape, which comprises a mix of rugged mountain ranges, alpine windswept plateau, and lush green forests, has made it a must-visit destination on the island.

List of Places to Visit in Ben Lomond National Park

Whether you’re interested in exploring culture, wildlife, or the great outdoors, Ben Lomond National Park has a lot to offer. Here’s a list of some of the top places to visit in the park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ben Lomond Historical Society Museum: A stunning museum that showcases the history and heritage of the region. You can explore the unique collections and displays, including period furniture, clothing, and intricate weaving looms.

Natural Attractions of Ben Lomond National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jacobs Ladder: This scenic mountain pass, located near the summit of Ben Lomond, provides stunning views of the region and is a prime destination for hikers and sightseers.
  • Ben Lomond Ski Resort: Known for its snowy slopes, the resort is perfect for snowboarding, skiing, and other outdoor recreational activities.
  • Legges Tor: The highest peak in the national park with an altitude of over 1500 meters. It offers a phenomenal panoramic view of the surrounding alpine landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ben Lomond National Park

Ben Lomond National Park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to advanced. The park has 14 official trails of varying difficulty levels, but if you are up for a challenge, there is a multitude of off-trail routes that you can explore. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Legges Tor Summit Track: A challenging hike to the highest peak in the national park, providing beautiful views of the surrounding area.
  • Jacobs Ladder Track: A moderate hike to the picturesque Jacobs Ladder mountain pass that provides sweeping views of the alpine landscape.
  • Ben Lomond Descent: A bike ride that takes you downhill from the summit of Ben Lomond to the nearby town of Blessington.

Best Things to Do in Ben Lomond National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and sightseeing, here are some must-do activities in the national park:

  • Snowboarding and skiing at the Ben Lomond Ski Resort during winter.
  • Camping amidst nature in the popular camping areas like Jacobs Ladder and Dago Point.
  • Wildlife spotting, including kangaroos, wallabies, wombats, and bird watching.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ben Lomond National Park

A week-long itinerary for Ben Lomond National Park could include the following:

Day 1: Explore the region around the Ben Lomond Historical Society Museum and Ben Lomond Village.

Day 2: Visit the Jacob’s Ladder, and take a moderate hike on Jacob’s Ladder Track.

Day 3: Set out to explore the national park, indulge in wildlife spotting, and visit the various alpine ecosystems.

Day 4-6: Hike different trails in the national park, such as the Legges Tor Summit Track, the Carr Villa Circuit, and the Stretcher Track.

Day 7: Finish your trip with a visit to the Ben Lomond Ski Resort for some skiing, snowboarding or other activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Ben Lomond National Park

Ben Lomond may be a national park with no towns or major communities, but it still offers lots of choices for tasty and unique food and drink.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasmanian salmon: freshly caught and cooked salmon is a must-try dish here. You can find it served up at various restaurants throughout the region.

Local Beverages

  • Tasmanian cider: Tasmania is known for its sparkling ciders, and you can find several local brands around the park.

Where to Stay in Ben Lomond National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The best option for staying in Ben Lomond National Park is camping. The park offers multiple designated camping areas, including Jacobs Ladder and Dago Point. If camping isn’t your thing, there are also several bed and breakfasts, guesthouses, and holiday homes in nearby towns like Blessington and Evandale.

What to Buy in Ben Lomond National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While there are few shopping options in Ben Lomond National Park, you can still take home some unique souvenirs like indigenous handicrafts and homemade jams and chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Ben Lomond National Park

The park has a significant cultural history, being home to the Aboriginal Tasmanians for over 35,000 years. Visitors are advised to respect the Aboriginal culture and traditions and ensure that they leave no traces of litter behind.

Best Time to Visit Ben Lomond National Park

The best time to visit the national park is during the summer months (December to February), which boasts balmy temperatures and clear skies. Winter is also an excellent time to visit if you want to experience winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ben Lomond National Park

Here’re some ongoing sustainability measures and tips to follow to protect the park and its natural environment:

Do:

  • Hike on designated trails only
  • Carry your trash and litter with you until you reach designated garbage areas.
  • Respect the park’s quiet hours and noise restrictions

Don’t:

  • Light campfires outside of designated areas.
  • Bring in prohibited items such as weapons or illegal substances

Safety and Health

Safety in Ben Lomond National Park

Hiking can be dangerous, and visitors are advised to be careful while venturing out into the park. It’s good to take precautions such as carrying a map, informing someone of your travel plans, and bringing adequate food and water.

Health in Ben Lomond National Park

Visitors to the national park enjoy good health, and vaccination requirements do not exist. Nevertheless, visitors with medical conditions are advised to carry their medication with them.

Getting to Ben Lomond National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The national park is situated approximately 60 km southeast of Launceston, and can be accessed via car or bus.

Airports Near Ben Lomond National Park

The closest airport is the Launceston airport, which is about an hour’s drive away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Ben Lomond National Park

Numerous bus companies operate along the Launceston-Blessington route, which is near the park.

Other Options

The best way to experience the park’s natural beauty is by driving a car and exploring the region at your convenience.

How to Get Around in Ben Lomond National Park

The national park is best explored on foot, bike, or car. Hiking and biking trails make it easy for visitors to explore while enjoying the stunning views.

Where to Go Next from Ben Lomond National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous destinations and attractions in Tasmania that visitors can explore after their trip to Ben Lomond National Park. Here are some of them:

  • MONA, the Museum of Old and New Art in Hobart.
  • Maria Island, a national park located in Tasmania’s east coast.
  • Port Arthur Historic Site, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its colonial-era relics.

Conclusion

Ben Lomond National Park offers a rare glimpse into Tasmania’s culture and natural beauty. With its stunning landscape, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions, it is undoubtedly a gem in Eastern Tasmania that you do not want to miss. We hope that our guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Ben Lomond National Park.
