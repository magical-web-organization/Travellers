Introduction to George Town

Nestled on the banks of the Tamar River in Eastern Tasmania, George Town is a picturesque town that offers a glimpse into Australia’s history and natural beauty. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and friendly locals, George Town has emerged as a popular tourist destination in recent years.

Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or thrill-seeker, there’s something for everyone in George Town. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, and more. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this charming town in Tasmania.

List of Places to Visit in George Town

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Low Head Pilot Station Maritime Museum

Located in a stunning 19th-century complex, the Low Head Pilot Station Maritime Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. The museum offers a fascinating insight into the lives of the pilots and their families who lived and worked in the station for over 150 years. Visitors can explore the museum’s extensive collection of maritime artifacts, including shipwreck relics and lighthouse lenses.

Bass & Flinders Centre

The Bass & Flinders Centre is another must-visit attraction for history lovers. It’s a modern, interactive museum that celebrates the achievements of explorers George Bass and Matthew Flinders, who arrived in Tasmania in 1798. The museum houses a replica of the ship they sailed, and visitors can learn about the duo’s voyages and discoveries.

Natural Attractions of George Town: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Low Head Penguin Tours

For a unique wildlife experience, head to the Low Head Penguin Tours. Here you can witness the magical sight of fairy penguins returning to their nests after a long day at sea. The tours are conducted by experienced guides who provide fascinating insights into these adorable little creatures’ behavior and habitats.

George Town Coastal Trail

Hiking enthusiasts will love the George Town Coastal Trail, which runs along the stunning Tamar River. The trail offers spectacular views of the river and nearby beaches, and visitors can spot dolphins and seals during the hike. The trail is well marked and suitable for all fitness levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of George Town

The Overland Track

The Overland Track is a world-famous hiking trail that takes visitors through some of Tasmania’s most stunning landscapes. The trek covers over 65 km and takes around four days to complete. During the hike, visitors will cross valleys, mountains, and lakes, and witness some of Australia’s rare wildlife species.

Tamar River Bike Trail

The Tamar River Bike Trail is a popular route for cycling enthusiasts. The 30 km trail follows the scenic Tamar River and offers breathtaking views of vineyards, farmlands, and forests. The trail is suitable for all ages and fitness levels and has several rest stops along the way.

Best Things to Do in George Town: Activities and Tours

Tamar River Cruises

A Tamar River Cruise is a fantastic way to experience the beauty of the Tamar Valley. The cruises offer visitors stunning views of the river, and onboard guides provide fascinating insights into the region’s history and ecology. Visitors can choose from several types of cruises, including wildlife-watching tours, food and wine cruises, and family-friendly trips.

Wine Tasting

With over 30 wineries scattered throughout the region, the Tamar Valley has emerged as a popular wine destination in Australia. Visitors can indulge in wine tasting sessions and sample the region’s award-winning varieties.

Week-Long Itinerary for George Town

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for exploring George Town and its surrounding areas.

Day 1: Visit Low Head Lighthouse and Pilot Station Maritime Museum. Day 2: Explore Bass & Flinders Centre and the Tamar Island Wetlands Reserve. Day 3: Hike along the George Town Coastal Trail and spot dolphins and seals. Day 4: Visit the Beaconsfield Mine & Heritage Centre and the historic town of Launceston. Day 5: Indulge in wine tasting at one of the region’s wineries. Day 6: Take a Tamar River Cruise and spot some wildlife. Day 7: Head to the Hollybank Treetops Adventure and experience thrilling zipline tours.

Food and Drink Scene of George Town

Must-Try Dishes

George Town is home to several restaurants and cafes that offer traditional Tasmanian cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Tasmanian Seafood Platter
  • Scallop Pies
  • Crayfish

Local Beverages

The Tamar Valley is famous for its wine, and visitors can indulge in wine tasting sessions at several wineries. The region is also home to several microbreweries that serve locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in George Town: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

George Town has several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Comfort Hotel Bayside: A modern hotel located on the banks of the Tamar River.
  • The Grove: A boutique-style guesthouse located in the heart of the town.
  • Discovery Parks: A family-friendly holiday park that offers caravan and camping facilities.

What to Buy in George Town: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wines from the Tamar Valley wineries
  • Tasmanian wool products
  • Artisanal chocolates and desserts

Local Culture and Customs of George Town

George Town has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect the town’s customs and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors can learn about the region’s history and culture by interacting with them.

Best Time to Visit George Town

The best time to visit George Town is during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the region’s mild climate means that visitors can explore the town year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of George Town

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Hike and explore the region’s stunning landscapes
  • Indulge in wine-tasting sessions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitats
  • Disturb the local wildlife
  • Ignore safety precautions while hiking or engaging in adventure sports

Safety and Health

Safety in George Town

George Town is a safe town, and visitors need not worry about crime or safety issues. However, it’s always advisable to take necessary safety precautions while hiking or engaging in adventure sports.

Health in George Town

The region has several medical facilities and pharmacies in case of any health emergencies. However, visitors should ensure they have appropriate health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to George Town: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near George Town

The nearest airport to George Town is the Launceston Airport, which is located around 35 km away. Several airlines operate daily flights to Launceston from major Australian cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach George Town by road. The town is located around 310 km from Hobart, and several bus services operate daily between the two towns.

How to Get Around in George Town

George Town is a small town, and visitors can explore most of the attractions on foot or by bike. However, rental cars and bikes are available for those who want to venture out of the town.

Where to Go Next from George Town: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Launceston

The historic town of Launceston is just a short drive away from George Town. Visitors can explore Launceston’s charming cafes, art galleries, and museums.

Devonport

Devonport is a coastal town located around 100 km from George Town. Visitors can explore the stunning beaches or take part in adventure sports such as surfing and fishing.

Conclusion

George Town is an excellent destination for visitors who want to experience Tasmania’s history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning landscapes, exciting outdoor activities, and warm hospitality, this charming town is a must-visit destination in Australia. So pack your bags and get ready to explore George Town’s many attractions.
