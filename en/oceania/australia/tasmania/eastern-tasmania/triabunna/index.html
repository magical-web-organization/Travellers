Introduction to Triabunna

Located in Eastern Tasmania, Triabunna is a picturesque town that boasts a perfect combination of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Whether you are an adventure seeker, a history buff, or a foodie, Triabunna has something in store for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you explore the best things to do and see in Triabunna. From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures, we’ll give you a rundown of the must-visit places in and around the town.

We’ve also included our favorite hiking and biking trails, top activities and tours, and a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip. Read on to discover all the hidden gems of Triabunna.

List of Places to Visit in Triabunna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Maria Island

A quick ferry ride from Triabunna, Maria Island is a National Park that is home to an array of endangered species. The island boasts an abundance of flora and fauna and ancient ruins that date back to the 1800s. Explore the Fossil cliffs, Schouten House, and the Painted Cliffs, which are some of the must-visit places on the island.

Spring Bay Mill

A contemporary arts center and cultural park, Spring Bay Mill, is a former mill that has been beautifully restored and boasts a rich history. The center celebrates design, architecture, contemporary arts, music, and ecology. Explore the center to learn about Tasmania’s rich history and immerse yourself in contemporary art.

Triabunna Wharf

The Triabunna Wharf is a bustling hub of activity, and the perfect location to learn about Tasmania’s fishing industry. Observe the fishermen unloading their catch, and enjoy an up-close look at marine life. The wharf is a great spot to take a quiet walk, listen to the lapping waves and watch the boats sail past.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Maria Island National Park

Maria Island National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The park boasts pristine beaches, lush green forests, and towering cliffs. Hike the Bishop and Clerk trail, which is a challenging 6-kilometer round trip, or explore the park on two wheels with a bike tour.

The Tasmanian Arboretum

Located just outside of Triabunna, the Tasmanian Arboretum is a haven for nature enthusiasts. The arboretum boasts an extensive collection of trees and plants that form a vibrant and colorful mosaic of foliage. Explore the Tam O’Shanter Creek, picnic on the lawns, and enjoy panoramic views of the East Coast of Tasmania.

The Maria Island Walk

The Maria Island Walk is a multi-day hiking tour that takes you through the rugged wilderness of the island. The tour includes comfortable accommodation, gourmet meals, and stunning views of the island’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Triabunna

The Maria Island Walk

The Maria Island Walk takes hikers through varied terrain that includes beaches, forests, and mountains. The 4-day tour covers a distance of 46 kilometers and includes overnight stays in cozy campsites.

The South Coast Track

The South Coast Track is a challenging 7-day hike that takes you through some of the wildest and most remote coastline in Australia. Experience stunning views of bays, inlets, and rivers and witness an abundance of wildlife along the way.

The Three Capes Track

The Three Capes Track is a new and exciting addition to Tasmania’s hiking trails. The track takes hikers through stunning cliff tops, secluded beaches, and breathtaking vistas. The 48-kilometer trail includes accommodation and shuttle services.

Best Things to Do in Triabunna: Activities and Tours

Maria Island Bike Tour

Explore the beauty of Maria Island on two wheels with the Maria Island Bike Tour. This tour is perfect for those who want to explore the island at their own pace.

Furneaux Island Tour

The Furneaux Island Tour is a great way to explore the wonders of Tasmania’s coastal islands. The tour takes you through the rugged beauty of Flinders Island, Cape Barren Island, and Clarke Island.

Fishing Charters

The fishing charters in Triabunna offer an opportunity to experience Tasmania’s fishing culture up close. Take a guided tour to experience catch-and-release fishing or head out for the big catch with experienced fishermen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Triabunna

Day 1: Maria Island

Visit the Fossil cliffs, Schouten House, and the Painted Cliffs on Maria Island.

Day 2: Spring Bay Mill

Explore the Spring Bay Mill to learn about Tasmania’s rich history and immerse yourself in contemporary art.

Day 3: The Tasmanian Arboretum

Explore the Tasmanian Arboretum and picnicking in the beautiful gardens.

Day 4-5: The Maria Island Walk

Embark on the Maria Island Walk to explore the island’s rugged wilderness.

Day 6: Fishing Charters

Take a fishing tour and experience Tasmania’s fishing culture up close.

Day 7: Three Capes Track

Embark on the Three Capes Track, which offers stunning cliff top vistas and secluded beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Triabunna

Must-Try Dishes

Tasmanian Scallop Pie

The Tasmanian Scallop Pie is a local delicacy that is a must-try when visiting Triabunna. The pie is made with fresh Tasmanian scallops and a flaky pastry crust.

Seafood Platter

The Triabunna seafood platter is famous for its fresh seafood. The platter includes oysters, mussels, prawns, and scallops and is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

Tasmanian Wines

Tasmania is famous for its wine, and Triabunna is no exception. Try a local Tasmanian wine to experience the region’s famous flavors.

Tasmanian Beer

Tasmanian beer is brewed with pure Tasmanian water and is a must-try for beer enthusiasts. The region is known for its range of craft beer varieties.

Where to Stay in Triabunna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spring Bay Hotel

The Spring Bay Hotel is a restored historic hotel that offers comfortable accommodation with stunning views of the waterfront. The hotel also boasts a great dining experience.

Gumleaves Bush Holidays

Gumleaves Bush Holidays offers affordable accommodation with stunning views of the surrounding wilderness. This is a great option for those who want to explore the natural beauty of Triabunna.

What to Buy in Triabunna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tasmanian Honey

Tasmanian honey is famous for its unique flavor profile. Visit a local honey farm to purchase fresh honey.

Tasmanian Wine

Bring home a bottle of Tasmanian wine to savor the unique flavors of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Triabunna

Triabunna is known for its warm hospitality and relaxed way of life. Visitors are encouraged to spend time talking to the locals to learn about the region’s customs and history. The residents of Triabunna are proud of their community, and visitors should respect their beliefs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Triabunna

The best time to visit Triabunna is from November to April when the weather is warm and sunny. This period is also ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Triabunna

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Explore the local flora and fauna.
  • Take a guided tour of Maria Island.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural environment.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals.
  • Ignore safety regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Triabunna

Triabunna is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard precautions and be mindful of their belongings.

Health in Triabunna

Visitors to Triabunna should ensure that they have all the required vaccinations and that they have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Triabunna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Triabunna

The nearest airports to Triabunna are Hobart International Airport and Launceston Airport.

Bus Stations Near Triabunna

The closest bus stations to Triabunna are located in Hobart and Launceston.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from Hobart or Launceston to get to Triabunna.

How to Get Around in Triabunna

Triabunna is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent cars or taxis to travel outside the town.

Where to Go Next from Triabunna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coles Bay

Coles Bay is a beautiful coastal town located just an hour from Triabunna. The town offers an array of outdoor activities like hiking and fishing and boasts stunning views of Wineglass Bay.

Freycinet National Park

Freycinet National Park is a picturesque national park located near Coles Bay. The park is home to the world-famous Wineglass Bay and offers an array of outdoor activities like hiking and canoeing.

Conclusion

Triabunna is a hidden gem that offers something for every type of traveler. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, it is a must-visit destination in Tasmania. Plan your trip well, explore the town’s hidden gems, and immerse yourself in the beauty of Triabunna.
