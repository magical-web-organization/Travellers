Introduction to King Island

King Island is a hidden gem located in Tasmania, Australia, and is often overlooked by travelers. Known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural history, the island offers a wide variety of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historical sites, King Island is a destination that is sure to impress.

List of Places to Visit in King Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. King Island Historical Society Museum

The King Island Historical Society Museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents highlighting the history of the island. Visitors can explore exhibits on shipwrecks, mining, and farming, among other topics.

2. Currie Lighthouse

Located on the western side of the island, Currie Lighthouse is a popular attraction that offers breathtaking views of the coastline. Visitors can learn about the history of the lighthouse and its significance to the local community.

Natural Attractions of King Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. King Island Golf and Bowling Club

Known for its stunning views of the surrounding coastline, the King Island Golf and Bowling Club is a must-visit for anyone who loves the outdoors. The club features an 18-hole golf course, a bowling green, and a restaurant.

2. Seal Rocks State Reserve

Seal Rocks State Reserve is home to a population of Australian fur seals and is an excellent spot for wildlife viewing. Visitors can also hike along the park’s many trails and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of King Island

1. Cape Wickham Lighthouse Walk

One of the most popular hikes on the island, the Cape Wickham Lighthouse Walk takes visitors along the northern coast of the island to the Cape Wickham Lighthouse. The trail offers breathtaking views of the coastline and is a great spot for bird watching.

2. Calcified Forest Walk

The Calcified Forest Walk is a short hike that takes visitors through a unique landscape of petrified trees. The trail is relatively flat and is suitable for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in King Island: Activities and Tours

1. Guided Tours

Guided tours are available for visitors who want to learn more about the island’s history and culture. Many tours include visits to museums, historical sites, and local landmarks.

2. Fishing

King Island is known for its excellent fishing, and visitors can book charter boats to take them out on the water in search of the island’s famous crayfish and abalone.

Week-Long Itinerary for King Island

Day 1 Arrive on the island and explore the town of Currie
Day 2 Visit the King Island Historical Society Museum and the Currie Lighthouse
Day 3 Hike the Cape Wickham Lighthouse Walk
Day 4 Enjoy a round of golf at the King Island Golf and Bowling Club
Day 5 Book a fishing charter and spend the day on the water
Day 6 Explore the Calcified Forest Walk and Seal Rocks State Reserve
Day 7 Depart the island

Food and Drink Scene of King Island

Must-Try Dishes

1. King Island Crayfish

King Island Crayfish is considered a delicacy and is a must-try for visitors. The sweet, tender meat is best enjoyed grilled or boiled and served with butter.

2. Wallaby

Wallaby is a local specialty and is a sustainable source of meat on the island. It is often served in stews and curries and is a tasty alternative to beef and lamb.

Local Beverages

1. King Island Dairy Cheese

King Island Dairy is famous for its artisan cheeses, which are made using locally sourced milk. Visitors can sample a variety of cheeses, including blue cheese and cheddar, at the dairy’s tasting room.

2. Tasmanian Wine

Tasmania is known for its excellent wines, and King Island is no exception. Visitors can sample wines from the island’s many vineyards, including the popular Hartzview Vineyard and Bowmore Estate.

Where to Stay in King Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. King Island Escapes

King Island Escapes offers a variety of vacation rentals, including beachfront cottages, apartments, and luxury villas. The properties are all well-appointed and offer stunning views of the surrounding coastline.

2. Island Breeze Motel

The Island Breeze Motel is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town of Currie. The motel also features a restaurant and bar, making it a convenient option for visitors.

What to Buy in King Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. King Island Honey

King Island Honey is famous for its unique flavor, which comes from the island’s native plants and flowers. Visitors can purchase jars of honey as a souvenir or gift for loved ones.

2. King Island Lamb

King Island Lamb is renowned for its flavor and quality. Visitors can purchase lamb products, including chops and sausages, at local markets and butchers.

Local Culture and Customs of King Island

The local culture of King Island is a blend of Australian and Tasmanian traditions, with a focus on sustainable living and conservation. The island is also home to a thriving arts scene, with many local artists showcasing their work in galleries and workshops.

Best Time to Visit King Island

The best time to visit King Island is between November and March, when the weather is warmest and the island’s natural beauty is at its peak. However, visitors can enjoy the island’s many attractions year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of King Island

Do:

  • Do try the local specialties, including King Island Crayfish and Wallaby.

  • Do explore the island’s natural beauty by hiking or biking along the many trails.

  • Do visit local museums and historical sites to learn about the island’s rich cultural history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t feed the wildlife, as it can be harmful to both the animals and humans.

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind, as the island is committed to sustainable living and conservation.

  • Don’t disturb the local ecosystem by taking plants or animals from their natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in King Island

King Island is generally a safe destination for travelers, with a low rate of crime and few safety concerns. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing, and take necessary precautions to avoid injury.

Health in King Island

Medical facilities on King Island are limited, and visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications with them. It is also recommended to bring insect repellent and sunscreen to protect against bites and sunburn.

Getting to King Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near King Island

The King Island Airport is the main airport on the island, with daily flights to and from Melbourne and Launceston.

Other Options

Ferry services are available from the mainland to King Island, but they only run a few times a week. Visitors can also charter boats or planes to reach the island.

How to Get Around in King Island

The best way to get around King Island is by car or bicycle, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car or bike from local providers or join guided tours to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from King Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Tasmania

Tasmania is an excellent destination for visitors who want to explore the natural beauty of Australia. Highlights include Cradle Mountain, Wineglass Bay, and the Tasmanian Devil Conservation Park.

2. Melbourne

Melbourne is a vibrant city that offers a wide variety of cultural attractions, shopping, dining, and nightlife. Visitors can explore iconic landmarks such as the Melbourne Cricket Ground and Federation Square.

Conclusion

King Island is a unique destination that offers a rich cultural history and stunning natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy hiking and biking trails, wildlife watching, fishing, and more, while also sampling local foods and wines. With its warm weather and welcoming community, King Island is a must-visit for travelers to Australia.
