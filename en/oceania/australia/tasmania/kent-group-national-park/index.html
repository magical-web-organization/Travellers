Introduction to Kent Group National Park

Nestled in the northeastern corner of Tasmania, Kent Group National Park is a beautiful and secluded wilderness area that offers visitors an opportunity to experience Tasmania’s rugged beauty. The park is made up of a group of islands, namely Deal, Erith, Dover, and North East.

Kent Group National Park is a wildlife lover’s paradise, and you’ll find many species uniquely adapted to the island environments. Here, you can experience a sense of seclusion, enjoy some of the best outdoor adventures the state has to offer, and create wonderful memories.

List of Places to Visit in Kent Group National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The National Park is home to numerous culturally rich attractions. Some of the best include:

  • Kent Island Lighthouse
  • Kent Group Interpretation Area
  • Reserves Officer’s Hut

Natural Attractions of Kent Group National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The park provides a wide range of natural attractions and outdoor adventure opportunities, including:

  • Snorkeling and diving
  • Wildlife watching and birdwatching
  • Fishing: Take the chance to catch some Australian salmon

The scenic views here are breathtaking, and you shouldn’t miss:

  • The Erith Island walking loop
  • The North East Island circuit walk
  • Deal Island Lighthouse Summit

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kent Group National Park

The Kent Group National Park is famous for its hiking and biking trails. The popular ones include:

  • The Kent Island Lighthouse track
  • The Erith Island walking loop
  • The North East Island circuit walk

These routes provide ample opportunities for wildlife watching, birdwatching, fishing, and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Kent Group National Park: Activities and Tours

For adventurous folks, Kent Group National Park is a haven of fun-filled activities. The best includes:

  • Snorkeling and diving
  • Wildlife and birdwatching
  • Internal circuit flight
  • Fishing expeditions
  • Star gazing

There are guided tours organized around these activities to ensure everyone gets the best experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kent Group National Park

The following is a week-long itinerary to help you explore the park’s best attractions:

Day 1: Arrival Day

Start your first day exploring the cultural and historic attractions on the island.

Day 2: Water Activities

Set off for your first day in the water by snorkeling or diving to experience the rich underwater environment.

Day 3: North East Island circuit walk

Try the North East Island circuit walk that takes about three hours. The route encompasses many points of interest and scenic views.

Day 4: Star gazing

Head to Observation Rock to observe the sky at night with the naked eye or a telescope.

Day 5: Kent Island Lighthouse

Visit the Kent Island Lighthouse after breakfast for the best views of the park.

Day 6: Dover Bay Circuit

Enjoy a hike through one of the park’s most famous tracks, the Dover Bay Circuit.

Day 7: Departure Day

Take the morning to pack and say your goodbyes to Kent Group National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kent Group National Park

Must-Try Dishes

The park is famous for its fresh seafood, so it’s an excellent opportunity to indulge in:

  • Tasmanian salmon
  • Crayfish

Local Beverages

The local beer, wine, and whiskey are all fantastic.

Where to Stay in Kent Group National Park

There are limited accommodation options in the park. However, you can choose from:

  • Kent Island Lighthouse: The park’s only lodge, providing a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in history
  • Campgrounds and backpacker huts are available: You can choose to rough it with a camping or backpacking experience.

What to Buy in Kent Group National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The park is home to a souvenir shop that sells:

  • Kent Island Lighthouse memorabilia
  • Other memorabilia to mark your visit

Local Culture and Customs of Kent Group National Park

The main inhabitants of the park are Tasmanian devils, wallabies, and wombats. Visitors should respect the park’s wildlife and follow the no-trace camping policy.

Best Time to Visit Kent Group National Park

The best time to visit is during the summer months, from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kent Group National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and follow the no-trace camping policy.
  • Take your trash with you.

Don’t:

  • Feed the animals
  • Start fires outside designated areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Kent Group National Park

The park is relatively safe, but visitors should:

  • Carry an adequate supply of water and food
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear
  • Weatherproof to protect yourself from the harsh elements.

Health in Kent Group National Park

Visitors should come with mosquito repellant and sunscreens and be prepared to handle any emergencies.

Getting to Kent Group National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kent Group National Park

The closest airports are in Launceston, Hobart, and Devonport.

Bus Stations Near Kent Group National Park

Woodbridge Hill is the closest bus station to Kent Group National Park.

Other Options

Boat rides are the only other alternative to get to the park.

How to Get Around in Kent Group National Park

Visitors can use a tour guide service or privately explore the park on foot.

Where to Go Next from Kent Group National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

You can continue exploring Tasmania or visit nearby attractions like:

  • Freycinet National Park
  • The Bay of Fires

Conclusion

Kent Group National Park is a stunningly beautiful park anyone visiting Tasmania should explore. With its rich culture and historic villages, beautiful water areas and hiking trails, boating areas, and pristine natural environments, it’s a visitor’s paradise. So pack your bags, and start exploring this hidden treasure.
