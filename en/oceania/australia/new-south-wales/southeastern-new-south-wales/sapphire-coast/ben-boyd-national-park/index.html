Introduction to Ben Boyd National Park

Ben Boyd National Park is one of the most stunning and undisturbed natural habitats in southeastern New South Wales, Australia. Located on the south coast of the state, this national park offers a unique blend of rugged coastline, pristine landscapes, and significant cultural history. The park stretches over 10,000 hectares and features a diverse range of habitats, including rocky headlands, stunning beaches, and eucalyptus forests. So, what makes this park unique and more than just a beautiful natural area?

One of the main highlights of the park is the preservation of ancient Indigenous cultural sites. The coastal cliffs of the park are home to many caves and alcoves, revealing an array of Indigenous rock art – some of which is up to 8,000 years old. In addition, the park was named after Benjamin Boyd, a 19th-century entrepreneur, and pioneer of the area. So, if you’re interested in connecting with nature and exploring a rich cultural heritage, Ben Boyd National Park is an excellent destination choice for you!

List of Places to Visit in Ben Boyd National Park

Here is a complete list of places to explore in Ben Boyd National Park. Let’s dive in!

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Davidson Whaling Station: Explore the ruins of Australia’s first shore-based whaling station and learn about the industry that thrived in the 19th century.

  • Green Cape Lighthouse: Built in the 19th century, Green Cape Lighthouse offers spectacular views of the wild coastline and is a popular spot for whale watching in the winter season.

  • Pinnacles: The Pinnacles are a series of tall, jagged rocks rising out of the ocean located near the southern boundary of the park.

Natural Attractions of Ben Boyd National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boyd Tower: Enjoy panoramic views of Twofold Bay and panoramic views of the park atop Boyd Tower. Take in the saltwater air from the raised tower or explore the nearby walking trails.

  • Saltwater Creek: A tranquil spot suitable for swimming, picnicking, and fishing, this creek is surrounded by rich rainforest.

  • Disaster Bay Lookout: This lookout is perfect for taking in the stunning vistas of Disaster Bay, with a view of the Pinnacles to the south.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ben Boyd National Park

Ben Boyd National Park has a variety of scenic hiking trails that cater to a range of levels, from easy to challenging. These trails are a great way to explore the park’s lush rainforests, charming beaches and stunning coastline. Biking trails lead through the forest of Tower, past the rainforest of Saltwater Creek, and provide an extraordinary view of the entire Disaster Bay.

  • Light to Light Walk: This walking trail stretches 31km, from Boyd Tower to Green Cape Lighthouse, and offers panoramic views of the rugged coastline.

  • Saltwater Creek Loop Walk: Explore the rainforest areas surrounding the Saltwater Creek using a 7km trail.

  • Lennards Island: Meet some of Ben Boyd National Park’s friendly local fur seals on a 12km hike from Bittangabee Bay.

Best Things to Do in Ben Boyd National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, there are other worthwhile things to do within Ben Boyd National Park. Here are some activities you can partake in:

  • Whale Watching Tour: The best time to witness humpback whales is during the migration period between May to November. A guided tour within the park is an excellent way to spot them.

  • Fishing: The park’s pristine coastline provides excellent opportunities for fishing- both rock and beach.

  • Snorkeling & Diving: Take a snorkeling or diving tour with a licensed operator to explore the plethora of curious creatures like octopus and rays that abound within the shores of Ben Boyd National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ben Boyd National Park

Are you planning to spend a week in Ben Boyd National Park? Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 - Check into accommodation within the park and explore Boyd Tower.

Day 2 - Trek along the Light to Light Walk from Boyd Tower to Saltwater Creek and back (~14km).

Day 3 - Take a whale watching tour or fishing trip.

Day 4 - Experience the natural sights of Ben Boyd National Park with a snorkeling or scuba diving excursion.

Day 5 - Follow the Saltwater Creek Loop Walk and grab lunch at the historic Green Cape Lighthouse.

Day 6 - Hike the Lennards Island Trail to observe the playful fur seals.

Day 7 - Enjoy a free day with a day trip to the surrounding towns within the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Ben Boyd National Park

Must-Try Dishes

The dishes that you should try in Ben Boyd National Park vary from local game meats to fresh seafood. Most cafes and restaurants within the park offer local and home-cooked meals with a focus on farm-to-table cuisine.

  • Fresh Oysters: Ben Boyd National Park has several oyster farms that produce fresh oysters rich in flavor.

  • Kangaroo meat: Kangaroo meat is a lean, flavorsome, and nutritious meat and is often served in the cafes and restaurants within Ben Boyd National Park.

Local Beverages

For those wanting to explore the delicious beverages of the region, check out these two popular options:

  • New England Cider: A crisp and refreshing cider derived from apples grown on the farm.

  • Merimbula Craft Beer: Merimbula’s local microbrewery produces a range of ales and lagers, including a unique ginger beer.

Where to Stay in Ben Boyd National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ben Boyd National Park provides plenty of accommodation options for visitors in the park. You can choose from a range of options, including tent camping sites, cabin parks, and caravan sites across multiple locations within the park.

  • Saltwater Creek camping area: This campsite situated alongside the creek, the perfect place to uncover the natural beauty of the park.

  • Green Cape lighthouse cottages: These historic cottages have been fully renovated and provide a unique overnight experience atop the cliffs overlooking the park.

What to Buy in Ben Boyd National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many locally made souvenirs and products to choose from when visiting Ben Boyd National Park, including:

  • Bark Paintings and Carvings: Contemporary or traditional Indigenous bark paintings and carvings that provide a unique reflection of Indigenous culture.

  • Honey produced from local bees.

Local Culture and Customs of Ben Boyd National Park

It is essential to respect the local culture and customs surrounding Ben Boyd National Park, including the preservation of Indigenous rock art sites, local wildlife, and natural habitats.

Best Time to Visit Ben Boyd National Park

The best time to visit Ben Boyd National Park is during the summer season (December to February) when the days are sunny and the water temperature is warm. The park is equally beautiful in the spring and autumn seasons, with fewer crowds than in the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ben Boyd National Park

Do:

  • Explore the park with a licensed tour operator.

  • Respect the park’s rules and regulations.

  • Enjoy local food and the park’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Damage or remove artifacts or plant life within the park.

  • Venture off designated walking paths and trails.

  • Disturb the park’s flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ben Boyd National Park

When exploring Ben Boyd National Park, it is important to be aware of several safety measures, including:

  • Dispose of your trash properly and leave the park free of litter.

  • Protect your skin, and wear protective clothing and hat, as well as sunscreen with a high SPF.

  • Never hike alone, stay on walking trails and paths, and bring plenty of water.

Health in Ben Boyd National Park

It is also important to safeguard your health while exploring Ben Boyd National Park. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Be on the lookout for reptiles, spiders, and venomous snakes.

  • Seek immediate medical attention if bitten or stung by any animal.

Getting to Ben Boyd National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The closest airport is in Merimbula, which is approximately a 30-minute drive from the park.

Bus Stations Near Ben Boyd National Park

How to Get Around in Ben Boyd National Park

The park offers an extensive network of roads that provide easy access to all of the park’s significant attractions. Renting a bike provides an alternative way of getting around and exploring the natural wonders of the park.

Where to Go Next from Ben Boyd National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring more of South Wales, here are some recommended nearby destinations:

  • Bermagui: A charming coastal town with a bustling fishing scene.

  • Pambula: A quaint town known for its thriving arts and crafts community.

  • Merimbula: A popular tourist destination featuring a unique shoreline, a large sparkling lake, and several local food and drink establishments.

Conclusion

Ben Boyd National Park is a nature lover’s paradise boasting an array of natural wonders and cultural artifacts. With an extensive list of places to visit, hiking trails to conquer, and activities to participate in, the park promises not to disappoint. Embark on a journey of discovery within the park and experience one of the best-kept secrets of South Wales.
