Introduction to Bega

Bega is a picturesque town located in the heart of Sapphire Coast, in Southeastern New South Wales, Australia. The region is known for its natural beauty and cultural heritage, with stunning scenery and a range of outdoor activities to enjoy. Bega is a friendly town with a rich history and a lively cultural scene, making it a perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Australian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Bega

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bega Cheese Heritage Centre The Bega Cheese Heritage Centre is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the history of the region and the art of cheese-making. The centre offers educational tours and a range of interactive exhibits, including demonstrations of cheese-making techniques and tastings of the famous Bega Cheese.

  • Bega Valley Regional Gallery The Bega Valley Regional Gallery showcases the works of local and regional artists, with a particular focus on contemporary Australian art. The gallery hosts a range of exhibitions and events throughout the year, making it a popular spot for art-lovers.

  • St John’s Church St John’s Church is the oldest surviving church in the region, dating back to the 19th century. The church is a beautiful example of colonial architecture and is steeped in local history.

Natural Attractions of Bega: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mimosa Rocks National Park Mimosa Rocks National Park is a stunning nature reserve that is home to a range of native wildlife and vegetation. The park offers a range of walking trails, as well as picnic and camping facilities, making it a great destination for families.

  • Bega River The Bega River is the perfect spot for a leisurely stroll or a picnic. The river is home to a range of wildlife, including platypus, and is a popular spot for fishing and canoeing.

  • Tathra Beach Tathra Beach is a picturesque destination that is perfect for swimming and surfing. The beach is surrounded by stunning scenery and is a popular spot for picnics and barbecues.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bega

There are several hiking and biking trails in and around Bega that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Some popular trails include:

  • Bega to Tathra Coastal Walk This 13 km trail offers breathtaking coastal scenery and passes through several charming towns and villages along the way.

  • Jellat Jellat Nature Reserve Jellat Jellat Nature Reserve is a beautiful nature reserve that offers fantastic hiking and biking trails. The reserve is home to a range of native flora and fauna, including kangaroos and koalas.

  • Biamanga National Park Biamanga National Park is a stunning nature reserve that offers a range of hiking and biking trails. The park is home to several waterfalls and scenic lookout points, making it a perfect destination for nature-lovers.

Best Things to Do in Bega: Activities and Tours

  • Cheese-making Workshops The Bega Cheese Heritage Centre offers a range of cheese-making workshops for those wanting to learn the art of cheese-making.

  • Surfing Lessons Tathra Beach is a popular destination for surfing, and there are several surf schools that offer lessons for beginners.

  • Whale Watching Tours Between May and November, the coast around Bega is home to a range of whale species, including humpback whales and southern right whales. Several tour operators offer whale watching tours during the season.
