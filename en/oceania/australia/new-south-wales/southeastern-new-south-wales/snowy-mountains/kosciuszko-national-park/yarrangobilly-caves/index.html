Introduction to Yarrangobilly Caves

Yarrangobilly Caves, located in Snowy Mountains, New South Wales, Australia, is a natural wonder that attracts tourists from all over the world. It is a stunning collection of limestone caves that have been formed over millions of years and is now protected as part of the Kosciuszko National Park. The park spans over 672,000 hectares and offers a range of activities for visitors of all ages. From exploring the natural wonders of the caves, hiking and biking in the surrounding mountains, to soaking in the natural hot springs, Yarrangobilly Caves has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Yarrangobilly Caves

The local culture of Yarrangobilly Caves is heavily influenced by Indigenous Australian culture. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the Aboriginal history and culture of the area and to respect it while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Yarrangobilly Caves

The best time to visit Yarrangobilly Caves is from December to February when the weather is warm and sunny. However, it is also popular during the winter months when the snow is perfect for skiing and snowboarding.
