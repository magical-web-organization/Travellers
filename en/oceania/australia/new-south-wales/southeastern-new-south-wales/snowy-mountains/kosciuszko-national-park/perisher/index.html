Introduction to Perisher

Perisher is a popular ski resort located in the Kosciuszko National Park, in the Snowy Mountains of Southeastern New South Wales, Australia. The resort is the largest in the Southern Hemisphere and boasts an impressive variety of ski terrain, catering to skiers and snowboarders of all skill levels. Perisher is an ideal holiday destination for adventure seekers, families, and couples, as there is something to do for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Perisher

Perisher offers a vast number of attractions and activities that cater to people of all ages and interests. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Perisher.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perisher has several attractions for visitors interested in exploring the cultural side of the area, including:

  • The National Park Visitor Centre: The centre overlooks the beautiful Thredbo River and offers information on the park’s history, facilities, and things to do.
  • Yarrangobilly Caves: These stunning limestone caves and natural pools offer a chance to explore the wonders of nature and Aboriginal history.
  • Snowy Region Visitor Centre: This centre offers self-guided tours and information about the history of the Snowy Mountains Hydro-Electric Scheme.

Natural Attractions of Perisher: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perisher is a paradise for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some natural attractions that you should not miss:

  • Mount Kosciuszko: The highest peak in Australia, Mt Kosciuszko offers breathtaking views of the surrounding ranges.
  • Snowy River: The river offers visitors a beautiful backdrop for canoeing, fishing, and other water activities.
  • Alpine Wildflowers: The region has an abundance of wildflowers that bloom in spring and summer, adding a splash of colour to the already picturesque region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perisher

Perisher offers many hiking and biking trails with varying degrees of difficulty, suitable for all ages and skill levels. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking:

  • Main Range Track: This 22km hike will take you to the summit of Mount Kosciuszko, the highest peak in Australia.
  • Yarrangobilly Caves to the River Walk: This 5km trail will take you through stunning limestone caves and natural pools.
  • Perisher to Blue Cow: This 8.5km trail links Perisher and Blue Cow, offering stunning views of the Snowy Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Perisher: Activities and Tours

Perisher offers a range of activities and tours for people of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Perisher:

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Perisher is known for its extensive ski and snowboarding terrain, catering to all skill levels.
  • Snowshoeing: Explore the winter wonderland by strapping on snowshoes and taking to the trails.
  • Scenic Chairlift Rides: Take a leisurely scenic chairlift ride and soak up the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Horse Riding: Take a guided horse ride through the mountains and experience the Australian wilderness from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perisher

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Perisher:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive and check into your accommodation. Take a walk around the village and explore the town.
Day 2 Ski or snowboard at Perisher’s slopes.
Day 3 Take a guided horse ride or visit Yarrangobilly Caves.
Day 4 Enjoy a scenic chairlift ride and try some snowshoeing.
Day 5 Go skiing or snowboarding at Blue Cow.
Day 6 Take a class at one of the ski schools or explore the local shops and restaurants.
Day 7 Depart perisher and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Perisher

Perisher has many restaurants and cafes that cater to all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meat Pies: This classic Australian dish is a favourite at many Perisher restaurants.
  • Damper: An iconic Aussie bread, traditionally cooked over an open fire.
  • Kangaroo or Emu Steak: Many restaurants serve these meats, providing visitors with a unique culinary experience.

Local Beverages

  • Australian Beer: Check out some of the local breweries in nearby Jindabyne and Thredbo.
  • Hot Chocolate: Warm up with a rich, creamy hot chocolate after a day on the slopes.

Where to Stay in Perisher: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perisher offers a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • The Perisher Manor: A luxurious hotel offering ski-in and ski-out access, spa services, and gourmet dining.
  • The Sundeck Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located close to the lifts, with cosy rooms and mountain views.
  • The Marritz Hotel: A four-star hotel offering ski-in and ski-out access, with stunning mountain views and fine dining.

What to Buy in Perisher: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perisher offers a range of local products and souvenirs that you can take home to remind you of your time in the mountains. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Snow gear: Take home some authentic Australian ski gear.
  • Local art and craft: Check out some of the local artists and buy unique, handmade items.
  • Local wines and beers: Bring home some of the local wines and beers and enjoy them later with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Perisher

Perisher has a laid-back, friendly culture that reflects its Australian roots. Here are some cultural tips to keep in mind:

  • Embrace the Australian spirit of camaraderie and mate-ship.
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles when exploring the wilderness.
  • Be mindful of the indigenous cultures and customs when visiting cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Perisher

The best time to visit Perisher is from June to September, the winter season. During this time, the ski resort is open, and the snow is at its best. The shoulder season of April to May and September to November can also be excellent for hiking, mountain biking, and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perisher

Do:

  • Explore the local area and take in the stunning natural beauty.
  • Try skiing or snowboarding, even if you’re a beginner.
  • Visit nearby cultural sites and museums to learn about the area’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the park or surrounding areas.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local customs and cultures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perisher

Perisher takes safety seriously and has guidelines in place to ensure visitors have a safe and enjoyable experience. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always wear appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Check weather and trail conditions before heading out.
  • Follow all safety guidelines at the ski resort, including wearing helmets when skiing or snowboarding.

Health in Perisher

While in Perisher, it’s essential to pay attention to your health and wellbeing. Here are some tips to stay healthy during your trip:

  • Stay hydrated and avoid alcohol to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Bring sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Stay warm and dry to avoid hypothermia.

Getting to Perisher: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perisher is located in Southeastern New South Wales, making it relatively easy to get to. Here are some transportation options for getting to Perisher:

Airports Near Perisher

  • Canberra International Airport: Located approximately 2.5 hours from Perisher.
  • Sydney Airport: Located approximately 5 hours from Perisher.

Bus Stations Near Perisher

  • Jindabyne Bus Station: Located approximately 33km from Perisher.
  • Thredbo Bus Station: Located approximately 23km from Perisher.

Other Options

  • Driving: Perisher is a drivable distance from many major cities in Australia, with well-signposted roads.

How to Get Around in Perisher

Perisher is easy to navigate, but transport options can be limited during peak season. Here are some ways to get around:

  • Shuttle buses: Perisher offers shuttle services to different parts of the resort.
  • Walking: Many attractions and accommodations are within walking distance.
  • Car rental: If you’re traveling as a group, renting a car can be an excellent option.

Where to Go Next from Perisher: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perisher is a convenient jumping-off point for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some popular nearby destinations:

  • Jindabyne: A picturesque town located approximately 30 minutes from Perisher, with scenic views and water activities.
  • Thredbo: Located approximately 20km from Perisher, this picturesque town is famous for its ski resort and hiking trails.
  • Canberra: Australia’s capital, located approximately 2.5 hours from Perisher, with a range of cultural and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Perisher is a beautiful, scenic destination that offers visitors an abundance of outdoor activities and cultural attractions. From skiing and snowboarding to hiking and horse riding, there’s something to do for everyone in this picturesque region. With this travel guide, you’re now ready to plan an unforgettable trip to one of Australia’s premier ski resorts.
