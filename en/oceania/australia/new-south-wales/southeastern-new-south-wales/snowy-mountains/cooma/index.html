Introduction to Cooma

Cooma is a small and charming town located in the Snowy Mountains of southeastern New South Wales, Australia. Though often overshadowed by the nearby ski resorts, Cooma has plenty to offer on its own. It boasts a rich cultural and natural history and a vibrant outdoor scene.

List of Places to Visit in Cooma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Raglan Gallery and Cultural Centre

Opened in 2010, Raglan Gallery and Cultural Centre is an art gallery that exhibits works by local and regional artists. The gallery also houses a gift shop that offers unique and locally-made souvenirs.

Cooma Court House

Cooma Court House is an early-20th-century courthouse that now functions as a museum. It showcases the town’s legal, social, and political history.

Cooma’s Centennial Park

Cooma’s Centennial Park is a historic park located in the heart of the town. It features a beautiful fountain, a war memorial, a rotunda, and a playground for children.

Natural Attractions of Cooma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yarrangobilly Caves

Yarrangobilly Caves, located approximately one hour from Cooma, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The caves boast crystal-clear pools, stunning limestone formations, and underground streams.

Cooma Creek

Cooma Creek is a pristine and picturesque waterway that runs through the town. It is a perfect spot for picnics, fishing, and leisurely walks.

Murrumbidgee River

The Murrumbidgee River is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts as it offers water sports, such as kayaking and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cooma

Mount Gladstone

Located right on the edge of Cooma, Mount Gladstone is the perfect place for a scenic hike. It offers spectacular views of the town and the surrounding region.

Snowy Mountains

The Snowy Mountains, with its challenging trails and breathtaking scenery, is one of the best hiking spots in Australia. The trek from Thredbo to Mount Kosciuszko - the highest peak in Australia - is a must-do for adventure seekers.

Best Things to Do in Cooma: Activities and Tours

Snowy Mountains Hydro-Electric Scheme Tour

The Snowy Mountains Hydro-Electric Scheme Tour is a unique opportunity to learn about the history and engineering behind one of the most significant engineering projects in Australia. It takes visitors to the construction sites, power stations, and underground tunnels.

Cooma’s Annual Festival

Cooma’s Annual Festival is a vibrant and colourful event that takes place in early March. It features street parades, live performances, and local produce markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Cooma

Must-Try Dishes

Freshly Baked Pies

Cooma is famous for its freshly baked pies that come in different flavours ranging from meat and vegetable to sweet pies made with fruits and berries.

Schnitzels

Schnitzels, a traditional Austrian dish, are also popular in Cooma. They are served hot and crispy, with different toppings and sauces.

Local Beverages

Coffee

Cooma has several independent coffee shops that serve locally roasted coffee. The coffee in Cooma is known for its strong and robust flavour and is perfect to keep you warm during the colder months.

Beer

The local brewery, Cooma Hotel, produces a wide range of brews, including pale ales and lagers. The brewery offers a tasting room that is open for visitors throughout the year.

Where to Stay in Cooma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cooma has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular places to stay include:

The Swiss Motel

The Swiss Motel is an affordable option that offers clean and comfortable rooms. It is located in the heart of the town, with easy access to the museums, galleries, and restaurants.

The Snowy Mountains Resort and Spa

The Snowy Mountains Resort and Spa is a luxurious option that provides beautiful views of the surrounding mountains. It features a heated swimming pool, a sauna, and a spa.

What to Buy in Cooma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Gifts

Cooma has several gift shops that sell handmade jewellery, clothing, and home decor items. These gifts make perfect souvenirs, and they also support the local artisans.

Local Produce

Cooma is surrounded by beautiful farmland that produces some of the freshest and tastiest fruits and vegetables. Visitors can buy fresh produce from the local markets, which makes a healthy and delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Cooma

Cooma has a rich cultural history, shaped by the indigenous Ngarigo people and the European settlers who arrived in the 1800s. The town celebrates this diversity through its art, music, festivals, and food.

Best Time to Visit Cooma

The best time to visit Cooma is between March and May or between September and November. These months offer mild temperatures and fewer crowds, making it the perfect time to explore the town and the surrounding region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cooma

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and wildlife.
  • Visit the local markets and buy locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local people or their property.
  • Ignore safety warnings and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cooma

Cooma is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should be mindful of the natural hazards, such as bushfires and flash floods, that are prevalent in the region.

Health in Cooma

Cooma has several medical clinics and pharmacies that offer a range of services. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, as some speciality medicines may not be available locally.

Getting to Cooma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cooma

The closest airport to Cooma is the Canberra International Airport, located approximately 100 kilometres from the town. Visitors can rent a car or take a bus from the airport to reach Cooma.

Bus Stations Near Cooma

Cooma has a bus station that connects it to major cities in New South Wales. Greyhound and NSW TrainLink operate services to and from the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cooma. The town is easily accessible by car, with several highways connecting it to other major towns and cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Cooma

Cooma is a small town that is easily accessible on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi if they need to travel further distances or transport their luggage.

Where to Go Next from Cooma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jindabyne

Jindabyne, located approximately 70 kilometres from Cooma, is a small town that is famous for its ski resorts and winter sports. During the summer months, visitors can explore the picturesque town and enjoy the hiking trails and the scenic lake.

Canberra

The capital city of Australia, Canberra, is located approximately 100 kilometres from Cooma. It is a vibrant city with a rich cultural history and several attractions, including the National Gallery, the Australian War Memorial, and the Parliament House.

Conclusion

Cooma, with its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural scenery, is a must-visit destination in New South Wales. This comprehensive guide provides all the necessary information to have an unforgettable experience in this charming town.
