Introduction to Kempsey

Kempsey is a charming town situated in the Mid-North Coast of New South Wales, Australia. Off the beaten path, it offers visitors a chance to experience authentic Australian culture and adventure. Kempsey is also known for its rich Aboriginal heritage; the Dunghutti People are the traditional custodians of the land, and their art and cultural events are celebrated throughout the region.

List of Places to Visit in Kempsey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kempsey Museum - Explore exhibitions that showcase Kempsey’s cultural heritage, including Aboriginal artifacts, farming equipment, and local history.
  2. Wigay Aboriginal Cultural Park - This beautiful park is home to the Wigay Aboriginal Cultural Association and offers visitors an insight into the culture and heritage of the Dunghutti People.
  3. Trial Bay Gaol - A historic site that offers stunning views of the ocean and provides an understanding of the early colonial history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Kempsey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hat Head National Park - This stunning park boasts secluded beaches, coastal forests, and excellent walking tracks.
  2. Smoky Cape Lighthouse - The lighthouse offers breathtaking views in all directions and is a great spot for whale-watching during the migratory season.
  3. South West Rocks - A charming seaside town that is home to beautiful beaches, great fishing spots, and the famous horseshoe bay surrounded by rocky outcrops.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kempsey

  1. Macleay Valley Coast Trail - This 10-kilometer walking and cycling trail offers stunning views of the Macleay River, beaches, and surrounding mountains.
  2. Yarriabini National Park - This park offers several walking tracks that pass through forests and lead to stunning lookout points.
  3. Limeburners Creek Nature Reserve - This reserve offers a beautiful 4-kilometer walking trail through stunning rainforest and mangroves.

Best Things to Do in Kempsey: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing Charters - Kempsey is a fishing haven, and several fishing charters offer visitors a chance to catch fish with experienced guides.
  2. Riverboat Cruises - Enjoy a relaxing cruise down the Macleay River and explore the pristine waterways and stunning scenery.
  3. Kempsey Riverside Markets - Held once a month, the markets are a great place to buy local produce, crafts, and art.

Food and Drink Scene of Kempsey

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Smoked Trout Pate - Freshly smoked trout pate is a must-try dish in Kempsey. Several local restaurants serve it as an entrée or as part of a seafood platter.
  2. Macleay Valley Beef - The region is home to some of Australia’s best grass-fed beef, and several cafes and restaurants serve delicious beef dishes.
  3. Local Seafood - Kempsey is a fishing haven, and visitors can try some of the freshest seafood including oysters, prawns, and crabs.

Local Beverages

  1. Macleay Valley Wine - The region has several wineries, and visitors can enjoy a glass of wine while taking in the beautiful scenery.
  2. Kempsey Rum - The region is also home to Australia’s first rum distillery, the Slim Dusty Centre, which produces delicious rum.

Where to Stay in Kempsey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Riverside Guesthouse - A charming guesthouse located in the heart of town, offering comfortable rooms and a relaxing atmosphere.
  2. Kempsey Powerhouse Motel - A modern motel with spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.
  3. Crescent Head Resort and Conference Centre - Located a short drive from Kempsey, this resort offers modern rooms, a spa, and conference facilities.

What to Buy in Kempsey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Slim Dusty Memorabilia - Slim Dusty was one of Australia’s most famous country music singer-songwriters, and The Slim Dusty Centre in Kempsey has an excellent gift shop where visitors can find memorabilia.
  2. Macleay Valley Honey - The region has several honey producers, and visitors can buy delicious honey at local markets.
  3. Dunghutti Artifacts - Visitors can buy authentic Aboriginal artifacts, including boomerangs and didgeridoos, from local galleries and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kempsey

Kempsey has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take time to learn about the Aboriginal and colonial history of the area. The Dunghutti People are the traditional custodians of the land, and their culture and heritage are celebrated throughout the region. Visitors should show respect for the culture and traditions of the local Aboriginal community.

Best Time to Visit Kempsey

The best time to visit Kempsey is between March and May or September and November when the weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists. During these times, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and explore the region without the crowds. The summer months (December to February) can be very hot, and the town can get busy with holidaymakers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kempsey

Do:

  1. Show respect for the local Aboriginal culture and heritage.
  2. Explore the natural beauty of the region, including the parks and reserves.
  3. Try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and grass-fed beef.
  4. Purchase souvenirs from local galleries and markets to support the local community.

Don’t:

  1. Damage or remove any Aboriginal artifacts or cultural sites.
  2. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  3. Ignore safety warnings when swimming or fishing.
  4. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kempsey

Kempsey is a safe town, and visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other Australian town or city. Lock cars and accommodation, safeguard valuables, and avoid flashing cash or expensive jewelry.

Health in Kempsey

Visitors to Kempsey should ensure that their travel insurance covers medical expenses. The town has a hospital and several medical centers that can provide medical care.

Getting to Kempsey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kempsey

The nearest airports to Kempsey are Port Macquarie Airport and Coffs Harbour Airport. Both airports have regular flights from major Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Kempsey

Greyhound Australia provides daily bus services to Kempsey from major cities along the east coast of Australia.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Kempsey, which is approximately a 4-hour drive from Sydney and a 6-hour drive from Brisbane.

How to Get Around in Kempsey

The town is easily accessible by foot or bike. Rental cars are also available, and there are local taxis and buses that run regular services.

Where to Go Next from Kempsey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Port Macquarie - A charming coastal town known for its stunning beaches, outdoor adventures, and koala sanctuary.
  2. Crescent Head - A laid-back surf town with stunning beaches and world-class surfing.
  3. Coffs Harbour - A bustling coastal city with excellent dining and shopping options, outdoor adventure, and the iconic Big Banana.

Conclusion

Kempsey offers visitors a chance to experience authentic Australian culture and adventure. From hiking in national parks to fishing charters and exploring Aboriginal heritage sites, there is something for everyone in Kempsey. Visitors can enjoy the delicious local cuisine and buy souvenirs to support the local community. Ensure to respect local customs and traditions and take necessary safety precautions. Plan your visit to Kempsey and discover what this charming town has to offer.
