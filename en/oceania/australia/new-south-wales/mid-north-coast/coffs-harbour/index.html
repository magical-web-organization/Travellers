Introduction to Coffs Harbour

Located in the heart of Mid-North Coast, Coffs Harbour is a charming coastal town in New South Wales, Australia. From golden beaches and picturesque mountains to lush forests and sprawling national parks, Coffs Harbour has plenty to offer for travellers and adventurers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Coffs Harbour

From cultural landmarks to natural attractions, Coffs Harbour is home to a variety of must-visit sites. Here are some of the top places to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coffs Harbour Regional Museum: This museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of Coffs Harbour and the surrounding area.

  • Yarrawarra Aboriginal Cultural Centre: A must-visit for those interested in learning about Aboriginal culture and history.

  • Bunker Cartoon Gallery: This unique gallery features a diverse collection of cartoon and comic art from Australia and around the world, with a special emphasis on political and social cartoons.

Natural Attractions of Coffs Harbour: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dorrigo National Park: This stunning national park offers hiking trails through lush rainforest, waterfalls, and amazing panoramic views.

  • Solitary Islands Marine Park: An ideal location for snorkelling, diving, kayaking, and whale watching.

  • Muttonbird Island Nature Reserve: This island is home to a variety of wildlife and provides an excellent vantage point for spotting whales and dolphins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coffs Harbour

Coffs Harbour is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, with countless trails and cycling paths to explore.

  • Coffs Creek Walk: A beautiful 10km walk taking you along the Coffs Creek and through lush forest.

  • Bongil Bongil National Park: Offers a range of hiking trails suitable for all experience levels, with stunning views and lots of wildlife.

  • Mangrove Boardwalk: This wheelchair-friendly boardwalk takes you through the beautiful estuary and mangroves.

Best Things to Do in Coffs Harbour: Activities and Tours

Whether you are travelling solo, with friends, or with family, there is something for everyone in Coffs Harbour. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Surfing: Coffs Harbour has some of the best surf breaks in Australia, making it a popular destination for surfing enthusiasts.

  • Jet-Ski Tours: Explore the beautiful coastline with a guided jet-ski tour.

  • Skydiving: Feel the rush of jumping from a plane at 14,000ft and enjoy a bird’s eye view of Coffs Harbour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coffs Harbour

Day 1 - Explore Coffs Harbour: Visit the Butterfly House, explore the Botanic Gardens and enjoy the beaches.

Day 2 - Visit the Coffs Harbour Regional Museum and the Yarrawarra Aboriginal Cultural Centre.

Day 3 - Hike the stunning trails of Dorrigo National Park.

Day 4 - Join a whale watching or dolphin spotting tour from Coffs Harbour Marina.

Day 5 - Enjoy a relaxing day on the beach, swim in crystal clear waters and soak up the sun.

Day 6 - Adventure day - go skydiving, jet-skiing, or horse riding.

Day 7 - Enjoy some shopping at Coffs Harbour’s markets and pick up some souvenirs to take home with you.

Food and Drink Scene of Coffs Harbour

Coffs Harbour is a foodie’s paradise, with a wide range of dining options, from budget-friendly cafes to fine dining restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Coffs Harbour is famous for its fish and chips, with several excellent seafood restaurants in the area.

  • The Big Banana: This famous attraction also has its own gelato bar where guests can try their iconic banana split.

  • Avocado Smash: Australia is famous for its delicious fresh produce, and this dish is a brunch favourite.

Local Beverages

  • The Coastal Brewing Company: Enjoy a cold beer or cider at the Coastal Brewing Company, a local microbrewery.

  • Bellingen Brewery: Another great local brewery that offers a range of unique and delicious craft beers.

Where to Stay in Coffs Harbour: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Coffs Harbour has plenty of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels and resorts. Here are some of the best options:

  • Sheridan Beachside Inn: A stylish and modern boutique hotel located across from Park Beach.

  • Arosa Motel: This motel offers comfortable rooms and is just a short walk from the beach and nearby shops.

  • Hoey Moey Backpackers: A popular choice for budget travellers, this hostel is just 50m from the beach.

What to Buy in Coffs Harbour: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Coffs Harbour has plenty of unique and interesting souvenirs that reflect the culture and natural beauty of the area. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Macadamia Nuts: These nuts are grown in the area and make a delicious and healthy snack.

  • Aboriginal Artwork: Yarrawarra Aboriginal Cultural Centre has a great range of authentic Indigenous art and craftwork.

  • Coffee: Coffs Harbour has several great coffee roasters, providing a range of quality blends you can take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Coffs Harbour

The people of Coffs Harbour are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors. Respect for the environment is highly valued, and it is expected that visitors will do their part to keep the area clean and well-preserved.

Best Time to Visit Coffs Harbour

The best time to visit Coffs Harbour is during the summer months (December - February) when it’s pleasantly warm, and the beach is inviting. The spring months of September to November are also a great time to visit, with mild weather and colourful flora and fauna.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coffs Harbour

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the Australian sun.

  • Do respect the environment and the wildlife, and clean up after yourself.

  • Do try the local food and beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the marine and estuary warning signs.

  • Don’t litter, spit or smoke in public spaces like the beach.

  • Don’t handle or touch the wildlife; if you want to see them, book an organized tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coffs Harbour

Coffs Harbour is a safe place to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Keep your valuables with you at all times, lock your accommodation when you are out and about and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Coffs Harbour

Coffs Harbour has a good standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and medical centres in the area. If you require medical attention, head to Coffs Harbour Health Campus or the Coffs Harbour Medical Centre.

Getting to Coffs Harbour: Transportation Options and Logistics

Coffs Harbour is easy to access, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Coffs Harbour

Coffs Harbour Regional Airport (CFS) is the closest airport to the town centre, with daily direct flights from Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.

Bus Stations Near Coffs Harbour

There are several bus companies that operate from Coffs Harbour, with regular services to and from Sydney and Brisbane.

Other Options

The Pacific Highway runs right through Coffs Harbour, making it easily accessible by car or camper van. If you’re travelling from Sydney, it’s a 6-hour drive; from Brisbane, it’s approximately 5 hours.

How to Get Around in Coffs Harbour

Coffs Harbour is a relatively compact town, and its attractions are within easy reach. You can explore the area on foot, by bike, or by car.

  • Buses: There are several bus operators in Coffs Harbour that provide regular services to the surrounding suburbs.

  • Taxis and Rideshare Services: Taxis and rideshare services are widely available in Coffs Harbour.

  • Rental Car: There are several car rental companies in the area, with plenty of options to suit every budget.

Where to Go Next from Coffs Harbour: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coffs Harbour serves as a gateway to several other beautiful destinations in New South Wales:

  • Byron Bay: This world-famous surfing spot and hippie town is just a 2-hour drive from Coffs Harbour.

  • Port Macquarie: A picturesque coastal town with some of the best coastal walks and beaches in the state is just 2.5 hours away.

  • Hunter Valley: Known for its stunning vineyards and award-winning wines, the Hunter Valley is a 3-hour drive from Coffs Harbour.

Conclusion

Coffs Harbour is a beautiful and diverse destination, with plenty to offer for anyone looking for an adventure downunder. From stunning natural wonders to uniquely Australian cuisine, this charming coastal town has something for everyone. Whether you’re travelling solo, with friends, or with family, Coffs Harbour is sure to impress and make unforgettable memories for your Australian experience.
