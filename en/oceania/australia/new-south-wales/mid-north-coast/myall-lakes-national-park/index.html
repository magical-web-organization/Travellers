Introduction to Myall Lakes National Park

Myall Lakes National Park is a stunning nature reserve located in Mid-North Coast, New South Wales, Australia. Known for its pristine lakes, dense forests, and diverse wildlife, this national park is a paradise for nature lovers, hikers, and outdoor enthusiasts alike. With over 40,000 hectares of protected land, Myall Lakes is one of the largest coastal lake systems in Australia, and is home to several indigenous communities.

List of Places to Visit in Myall Lakes National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of Myall Lakes National Park is its rich cultural history. Visitors to the park can explore several museums and historical sites, including:

  • Wootton Historical Railway Walk: This 2.5km trail takes you through a former railway line and showcases the history of rail transportation in the region.
  • Mungo Brush Rainforest Walk: This scenic walk takes you through a lush rainforest and leads to the historic Mungo Brush settlement.
  • Dark Point Aboriginal Place: Located at the southern end of Myall Lakes National Park, this protected cultural site is of great significance to the Worimi people, who have been living in the region for thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Myall Lakes National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Myall Lakes National Park is a playground for outdoor enthusiasts, with endless opportunities for hiking, camping, swimming, and birdwatching. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Coomba Lookout: This scenic lookout provides panoramic views of the park and its beautiful lakes.
  • Stewart and Lloyds Campgrounds: These campsites offer stunning views of the lakes and provide easy access to hiking trails and water activities.
  • Myall Lakes Wildernis Cruise: Go on a guided tour to explore the park’s waterways and spot local wildlife like dolphins and pelicans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myall Lakes National Park

Myall Lakes National Park is home to several hiking and biking trails that showcase the park’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Mungo Brush Rainforest Walk (2km): This easy walk takes about 30 minutes to complete and leads through a lush rainforest.
  • Yacaaba Headland Walk (6km): This moderate hike takes you to the top of Yacaaba Headland and offers stunning panoramic views of the coastline.
  • Bombah Point Eco Cottages Trail (9km): This trail takes you through dense forests and over the Bombah Point Eco Cottages suspension bridge.

Best Things to Do in Myall Lakes National Park: Activities and Tours

Myall Lakes National Park offers a wide range of fun activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Fishing: Cast a line in one of the park’s lakes and try your luck catching bream or flathead.
  • Swimming: Take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear waters of Myall Lakes.
  • Kayaking: Paddle through the park’s waterways and explore its hidden coves and beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Myall Lakes National Park

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Myall Lakes National Park, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the Wootton Historical Railway Walk and Mungo Brush Rainforest Walk.
  • Day 2: Hike the Yacaaba Headland Walk and take in the stunning panoramic views.
  • Day 3: Relax at Stewart and Lloyds Campgrounds and enjoy a swim in Myall Lakes.
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour on the Myall Lakes Wildernis Cruise.
  • Day 5: Kayak through the park’s waterways and explore its hidden coves and beaches.
  • Day 6: Visit the Dark Point Aboriginal Place and learn about Worimi culture and history.
  • Day 7: Go fishing and try your luck catching bream or flathead.

Food and Drink Scene of Myall Lakes National Park

Must-Try Dishes

When in Myall Lakes National Park, be sure to try some of the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Barramundi: A popular fish dish that can be found in many local restaurants.
  • Kangaroo: If you’re feeling adventurous, try kangaroo meat, which is lean and flavorful.
  • Macadamia Nuts: These native Australian nuts are a delicious snack and can be found in many local markets.

Local Beverages

Myall Lakes National Park is also known for its great drinks. Here are some of the local beverages to try:

  • Hunter Valley Wine: Located just a short drive from Myall Lakes, the Hunter Valley is Australia’s oldest wine region and offers some of the country’s best wine.
  • Newcastle Beer: This local brew is made in nearby Newcastle and is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Myall Lakes National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Myall Lakes National Park offers several options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Bombah Point Eco Cottages: These eco-friendly cottages offer a unique and secluded experience in the heart of the park.
  • Seal Rocks Holiday Park: This beachside campground offers stunning views and easy access to water activities.
  • Ramada Resort by Wyndham Shoal Bay: This luxurious resort is located just a short drive from Myall Lakes and has a private beach and several restaurants.

What to Buy in Myall Lakes National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Myall Lakes National Park, be sure to pick up some souvenirs or local products to bring home with you. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Aboriginal Art: Several local stores sell beautiful handmade Aboriginal art.
  • Macadamia Nuts: These delicious nuts can be found in many local markets and make a great snack.
  • Beachwear: If you’re planning on spending time at the beach, pick up some local swimwear.

Local Culture and Customs of Myall Lakes National Park

Myall Lakes National Park is home to several indigenous communities, including the Worimi people. When visiting the park, it’s important to respect their culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Stay on designated trails: Many areas of the park have significant cultural importance and should only be accessed with a guide.
  • Respect sacred sites: Dark Point Aboriginal Place and other cultural sites should be treated with the utmost respect.
  • Learn about the culture: Take time to learn about the Worimi people and their history in the region.

Best Time to Visit Myall Lakes National Park

The best time to visit Myall Lakes National Park is from September to December and March to May. During these months, the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, the park is open year-round and each season offers its own unique beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myall Lakes National Park

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  • Stay on designated trails and respect park rules.
  • Bring plenty of water and snacks on hikes.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife.
  • Leave trash or litter in the park.
  • Enter areas that are marked as closed or off-limits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Myall Lakes National Park

Myall Lakes National Park is generally a safe destination, but it’s important to take some precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always let someone know where you’re going and when you plan to return.
  • Bring a map and compass, and make sure you know how to use them.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the terrain.

Health in Myall Lakes National Park

Myall Lakes National Park is a remote area and medical facilities may be limited. Here are some health tips:

  • Bring a well-stocked first aid kit and any necessary medication.
  • Protect yourself from the sun and insects.
  • Be prepared for extreme weather conditions.

Getting to Myall Lakes National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Myall Lakes National Park

The closest airport to Myall Lakes National Park is Newcastle Airport, which is about a 1.5-hour drive away. Sydney Airport is also an option, but it’s about a 3.5-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Myall Lakes National Park

There are several bus companies that offer service to the town of Bulahdelah, which is about a 30-minute drive from Myall Lakes National Park. From there, you can take a taxi or rental car to the park.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Myall Lakes National Park is easily accessible by car. From Sydney, take the Pacific Highway and exit at Bulahdelah, and then follow the signs to the park.

How to Get Around in Myall Lakes National Park

The best way to get around Myall Lakes National Park is by car. There are several scenic drives that showcase the park’s beauty, and having a car will also give you the freedom to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Myall Lakes National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Hunter Valley: This nearby wine region is known for its vineyards and cellar doors.
  • Port Stephens: This coastal town offers stunning beaches and water activities like whale watching and dolphin watching.
  • Barrington Tops National Park: This park is located about an hour’s drive from Myall Lakes and offers beautiful rainforests, waterfalls, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Myall Lakes National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the natural beauty of New South Wales. With its stunning lakes, dense forests, and diverse wildlife, this national park offers endless opportunities for adventure and relaxation. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your experience in this beautiful park.
