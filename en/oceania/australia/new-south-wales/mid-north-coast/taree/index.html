Introduction to Taree

Taree is a charming town located in the Mid-North Coast of New South Wales, Australia. Set along the picturesque Manning River, Taree is known for its natural beauty, fascinating history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a culture seeker, Taree has something to offer for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at Taree’s top attractions, best places to stay, best things to do, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Taree

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manning Valley Historical Society Museum: Learn about the Manning Valley’s fascinating history at this museum.
  • Taree Craft Centre: Browse handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  • Taree Heritage Walk: Take a self-guided tour of Taree’s historic buildings and landmarks.
  • Tinonee Museum: Explore the local history of Tinonee, a nearby town.

Natural Attractions of Taree: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Nellie Mountain: Hike to the top of Big Nellie Mountain for stunning views of the Manning Valley.
  • Manning River: Go fishing, kayaking, or stand up paddleboarding on the Manning River.
  • Saltwater National Park: Discover secluded beaches, coastal heathlands, and more at this national park.
  • Taree Recreation Centre: Enjoy a picnic, play a game of frisbee, or hike along the walking tracks at this local recreation centre.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taree

Taree has plenty of excellent hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Bohnock to Cattai Wetlands Walk: Enjoy a scenic hike from Bohnock to Cattai Wetlands, taking in the local flora and fauna along the way.
  • Saltwater Creek Walk: This easy walk follows the banks of Saltwater Creek and leads to a lovely waterfall.
  • Taree to Wingham Rail Trail: Experience scenic views of the local countryside on this rail trail, perfect for both hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Taree: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local farmers market on Saturdays to buy fresh produce and handmade goods.
  • Take a scenic flight over the Manning Valley.
  • Attend one of the many local festivals and events held throughout the year.
  • Go whale watching during the winter months.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taree

Day 1: Explore Taree’s historic buildings and museums. Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride along one of Taree’s scenic trails. Day 3: Relax on one of Taree’s beautiful beaches. Day 4: Go fishing or kayaking on the Manning River. Day 5: Visit a local winery or brewery and sample some delicious drinks. Day 6: Take a scenic flight over the Manning Valley. Day 7: Take a day trip to one of the nearby coastal towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Taree

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coopers Barbecued Chicken: This local eatery is famous for its delicious barbecued chicken.
  • Prawn Linguine: Enjoy fresh prawns tossed with linguine and a creamy sauce at one of Taree’s many Italian restaurants.
  • Manning Valley Beef: Taste some of Australia’s best beef at a local restaurant or butcher shop.
  • Oysters: Try some of Taree’s famous oysters, available at many local seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Manning Valley Brewing Company: This brewery produces a wide range of craft beers.
  • Manning Valley Wines: Sample some of the local wines at one of the many nearby wineries.

Where to Stay in Taree: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • All Seasons Taree: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Taree Motor Inn: Stay in one of the modern and stylish rooms at this motel.
  • Comfort Inn Marco Polo: This hotel is situated in a quiet location, but still within easy reach of Taree’s attractions.

What to Buy in Taree: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts and souvenirs from the Taree Craft Centre.
  • Fresh produce from one of the local farmers markets.
  • A bottle of Manning Valley wine.
  • Local honey from one of the nearby apiaries.

Local Culture and Customs of Taree

Taree is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are always greeted with a warm smile. It’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, and to tread lightly on the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Taree

Taree is a year-round destination, with something to offer no matter when you visit. The summer months (December to February) are popular for beach and outdoor activities, while the winter months (June to August) are perfect for whale watching and other coastal activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taree

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Try some of the local dishes and drinks.
  • Take the time to explore Taree’s unique culture and history.
  • Bring plenty of sunscreen and a hat.

Don’t:

  • Drop litter on the beaches or trails.
  • Swim in unpatrolled areas.
  • Ignore warning signs and advice about local conditions and weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taree

Taree is generally a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid becoming victims of crime. Always lock your car and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.

Health in Taree

Taree has good quality medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors from overseas should ensure they have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Taree: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taree

  • Taree Airport: This small airport offers flights to and from Sydney and other nearby destinations.

Bus Stations Near Taree

  • Taree Bus Depot: This is the main bus station in Taree, with services to and from many nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Wingham, a nearby town, with regular services to and from Sydney.

How to Get Around in Taree

The best way to get around Taree is by car, as public transport is limited. Taxis and car rental services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Taree: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Macquarie: This popular beach destination is just an hour’s drive from Taree.
  • Forster: Explore the stunning beaches and lakes of this coastal town, just a short drive south of Taree.
  • Crowdy Bay National Park: Discover secluded beaches and coastal forests at this nearby national park.

Conclusion

Taree is a hidden gem of the Mid-North Coast, with plenty of unique attractions and experiences to discover. From its fascinating history to its beautiful natural scenery, there’s something for everyone in Taree. Plan your next trip today and experience all that this charming town has to offer.
