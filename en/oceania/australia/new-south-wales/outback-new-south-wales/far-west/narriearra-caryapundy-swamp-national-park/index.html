Introduction to Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park

Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park is located in Far West in Outback New South Wales, Australia and covers over 87,000 hectares of land. The park boasts an abundance of natural and cultural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts.

The park is home to one of the world’s largest wetland complexes, the MacCullock Coongie Lakes, which is ideal for birdwatching and fishing. Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park is also home to a diverse group of wildlife, including kangaroos, emus, and wallabies.

List of Places to Visit in Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yantabulla Heritage Trail

The Yantabulla Heritage Trail is a 2.5-kilometer loop trail that takes visitors through significant historical sites in the park. The trail features interpretive signs that provide information on the history and culture of the area.

Yanda Aboriginal Art Site

The Yanda Aboriginal Art Site is located on the southern boundary of the park and features rock art that is over 5,000 years old. The site also offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

MacCulloch Coongie Lakes

The MacCulloch Coongie Lakes are one of the world’s largest wetland complexes and a major attraction in Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park. The lakes are ideal for birdwatching and camping, and visitors can also enjoy fishing and kayaking.

Junction Waterhole

The Junction Waterhole is located on the Paroo River and provides visitors with a picturesque setting for swimming and fishing. The area is also home to an abundance of wildlife, including kangaroos and emus.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park

Murray-Darling Junction Track

The Murray-Darling Junction Track is a 19-kilometer trail that provides visitors with stunning views of the park’s wetlands and wildlife. The trail can be completed on foot or by bike.

The Lakes Walk

The Lakes Walk is a 2-kilometer walk that takes visitors through the MacCulloch Coongie Lakes. The trail provides visitors with panoramic views of the lakes and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Narriearra Caryapundy Swamp National Park: Activities and Tours

Birdwatching

Fishing
