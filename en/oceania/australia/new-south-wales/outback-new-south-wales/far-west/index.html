Introduction to Far West

Far West is located in the Outback of New South Wales (NSW), Australia. Known for its vast desert landscapes, rich indigenous culture, and unique blend of colonial history, Far West is an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience the true essence of Australia. Far West is a region full of surprises with a range of places to visit including natural wonders, culturally significant museums, and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Far West

Far West has a range of places to visit that are worth exploring. Here are some of the must-see sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Broken Hill Heritage Walk Tour - A walking tour that takes visitors through the city’s colonial history including significant buildings and sites such as Broken Hill Courthouse, Trades Hall, and the Post Office.
  2. White Cliffs Underground Motel and Tourist Park - Explore the opal mines and the unique underground accommodation.
  3. The Albert Kersten Mining and Mineral Museum - Learn about the region’s mining history and see a collection of minerals and rocks.
  4. Royal Flying Doctor Service Visitor Experience - Discover the history and workings of the world’s largest and most comprehensive aeromedical organization.
  5. Milparinka Heritage Precinct - Experience the unique history and culture of the town with a range of walking trails, camping, bird watching, and stargazing options.

Natural Attractions of Far West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mungo National Park - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mungo offers breathtaking scenery, ancient history, and rich indigenous culture.
  2. Cameron Corner - The point where Queensland, South Australia, and New South Wales meet.
  3. Kinchega National Park - A popular spot for nature lovers, with plenty of activities including camping, hiking, boating, and fishing.
  4. Sturt National Park - Home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and a great place for bird watching.
  5. The Living Desert and Sculptures - A unique collection of sandstone sculptures set against the backdrop of the desert landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far West

Far West has numerous hiking and biking opportunities with trails available for all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  1. Mt. Grenfell Historic Site – This is a great spot for hiking or climbing with breathtaking views over the region from the top of Mt. Grenfell.
  2. The Darling River Run – A driving/hiking trail that winds along the Darling River and through the region’s national parks.
  3. The Barrier Ranges – A popular mountain bike route, with challenging terrain and incredible views.

Best Things to Do in Far West: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore Broken Hill – The birthplace of BHP Billiton, have a mining tour to highlight important spots all over the city.
  2. Star-Gazing – Minimal light pollution in Far West makes it the perfect spot for stargazing, either at Mungo or the Milparinka Heritage Precinct.
  3. Explore the Opal Mining Town of White Cliffs – Known as the ‘underground town,’ White Cliffs is the perfect place for a unique accommodation experience and a mining tour.
  4. Go Fishing in the Darling River – The river is well-stocked with several freshwater species including Murray Cod and Yellowbelly.
  5. Experience the Aboriginal culture and history – There are several Aboriginal cultural tours available at places such as Mutawintji National Park or Mungo National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Far West

Day 1: Arrive in Broken Hill and explore the city Day 2: Visit the Albert Kersten Mining and Mineral Museum and White Cliffs Day 3: Explore the Milparinka Heritage Precinct and the fascinating Mutawintji National Park Day 4: Mungo National Park, worth dedicating a full day of exploration Day 5: Visit Sturt National Park and head over to Fort Grey for stunning viewpoints Day 6: Go Fishing or enjoying a river boat trip at Kinchega National Park Day 7: Depart from Far West

Food and Drink Scene of Far West

Far West has several dining options where you can try local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Emu or Kangaroo – Native Australian meats cooked in various styles
  2. Cured Meat – Far West region is known for their artisanal cured meat
  3. Stews – Traditional Australian dish that uses locally available ingredients

Local Beverages

  1. Broken Hill Beer (New Empire) - The local beer that takes inspiration from the region’s mining history.
  2. Outback Wines - Wine with a unique terroir sourced from the region’s local vineyards

Where to Stay in Far West: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Far West has a range of accommodation options available to suit different budgets. Some popular choices include:

  1. ARIA (Broken Hill) - A luxury hotel located in the center of Broken Hill.
  2. The Palace Hotel (Broken Hill) - A heritage-listed hotel with a rich history.
  3. White Cliffs Underground Motel (White Cliffs) - An iconic and unique accommodation experience.
  4. The Albert Hotel Motel (Milparinka) - A historic pub with comfortable accommodation and a great restaurant.
  5. Mt. Grenfell Historic Site Camping Ground (Cobar) – An authentic camping experience with basic facilities.

What to Buy in Far West: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Opals – Far West is famous for its opal mines, and you can find unique souvenirs made from this precious gemstone.
  2. Artisanal Cured Meat – A must-try local treat, these meats make for great gifts and souvenirs.
  3. Aboriginal Art – Far West has a rich heritage of indigenous art, and it makes for a unique souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Far West

Far West is home to a rich Aboriginal culture, and it is essential to show respect while visiting. It is important to be mindful of the traditional values and customs, and visitors should ask permission before taking any photographs in the Aboriginal communities. Additionally, be aware of any cultural events or celebrations taking place during your visit and participate with respect.

Best Time to Visit Far West

The best time to visit Far West is during the cooler months of March to October, as temperatures can reach extreme levels during the summer months (November – February). However, visitors should note that the cooler months can be busier, so plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far West

Do:

  1. Respect the indigenous culture and customs.
  2. Carry plenty of water and sunscreen.
  3. Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the outdoors.

Don’t:

  1. Litter
  2. Enter Aboriginal lands without permission.
  3. Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Far West

Far West is a relatively safe destination. However, visitors are advised to take the usual precautions when traveling to remote areas, including carrying plenty of water, sunscreen, and appropriate clothing for the outdoors. Visitors should also be mindful of the extreme heat and carry adequate supplies for when traveling to more remote areas.

Health in Far West

The region’s heat and remoteness make it important to stay hydrated and well-nourished. Visitors should also make sure their vaccinations are up-to-date and carry a personal medical kit when traveling to remote areas.

Getting to Far West: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Far West

The nearest airport to Far West is in Broken Hill, New South Wales, with daily flights from most major Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Far West

Greyhound Australia operates a daily coach service from Adelaide to Broken Hill.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get to Far West, with the area accessible by sealed road from all major Australian cities.

How to Get Around in Far West

Driving is the best option to get around Far West, as the region is vast and spread out. Rental cars are available in most major towns, and visitors should plan their routes in advance and carry adequate supplies when traveling to more remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Far West: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the surrounding regions or travel to nearby destinations. Here are some popular choices:

  1. Cobar - A historic mining town with a strong indigenous and colonial history.
  2. Broken Hill to Sydney - A scenic route that takes visitors through Outback NSW and the Blue Mountains.
  3. The Flinders Ranges (South Australia) - A stunning natural playground just across the border of South Australia.

Conclusion

Far West is a unique and fascinating destination that is sure to please travelers who want to experience the real Australia. With a range of natural and cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in Far West. Visitors should be mindful of the remoteness, extreme heat, and indigenous culture when planning their trip.
