Introduction to Walgett

Walgett is a charming Outback town that lies in the north-western region of New South Wales, Australia. The town is situated on the junction of the Namoi and Barwon rivers, and it offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor activities.

Walgett was first inhabited by the Indigenous Kamilaroi people, and its name derives from the Aboriginal word “Wollongah” meaning “the meeting of two waters.” The town is now home to a diverse population, including farmers and graziers, Aboriginal communities, and city-dwellers who seek a peaceful retreat in the great Australian Outback.

Visitors to Walgett can look forward to exploring the town’s rich history, admiring its stunning natural surroundings, and experiencing its unique culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Walgett

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Walgett Historical Museum

The Walgett Historical Museum is a must-visit for anyone curious about the town’s past. The museum is housed in the town’s old courthouse, which dates back to 1880. Visitors can explore exhibits that showcase the town’s Indigenous and European history, including photos, artifacts, and memorabilia.

Walgett Aboriginal Medical Service

Walgett is home to a thriving Aboriginal community, and the Walgett Aboriginal Medical Service is a vital part of its healthcare infrastructure. Visitors can take a guided tour of the facility to learn more about the services it provides and gain insight into the challenges that face Indigenous communities in modern-day Australia.

Natural Attractions of Walgett: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Town Common

The Town Common is Walgett’s largest parkland, covering over 145 hectares. Visitors can enjoy scenic walks, spot local birdlife, and take in the stunning views of the Namoi and Barwon rivers. The park also features picnic areas, barbeque facilities, and a children’s playground.

Warrambool Weir

Warrambool Weir is a picturesque spot located on the Namoi River, about six kilometers north of Walgett. The site is perfect for fishing, picnicking, and swimming in the river’s clear waters. Visitors can also take a scenic drive along the river to see more of the region’s stunning natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walgett

The Great Walgett Trek

The Great Walgett Trek is a multi-day hiking and biking trail that takes visitors through the heart of the Outback. The trail covers over 200 kilometers and includes challenging terrain, stunning views, and encounters with local wildlife. Visitors can choose to camp along the route or stay in local guesthouses.

Best Things to Do in Walgett: Activities and Tours

Walgett Races

The Walgett Races are a staple of the town’s social calendar, with the event taking place every year in August. Visitors can soak up the exciting atmosphere and watch as locals and visitors alike compete in horse-racing and other events.

Yanda Aboriginal Art

Yanda Aboriginal Art is an essential stop for anyone interested in Indigenous art. Visitors can discover the rich cultural traditions of the Gamilaroi people and purchase unique artworks, including paintings, sculptures, and didgeridoos.

Food and Drink Scene of Walgett

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Walgett is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Popular dishes include:

  • Drovers pie: A hearty meat pie, often filled with beef or lamb.
  • Damper: A traditional bread made from flour, water, and salt and cooked over a campfire.
  • Balmain bugs: A seafood delicacy, often served grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

The Outback is known for its legendary pubs, and Walgett is no exception. Visitors can sample an array of locally brewed beers, including popular brands like Tooheys and XXXX.

Local Culture and Customs of Walgett

Walgett is home to a vibrant Indigenous community, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including:

  • Refraining from taking photos of Indigenous people without permission
  • Taking off shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Avoiding sensitive or taboo subjects in conversation

Best Time to Visit Walgett

The Outback can be hot and dry, and the best time to visit Walgett is during the cooler months of May to September. During this time, visitors can enjoy comfortable temperatures, clear skies, and plenty of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walgett

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take precautions to protect yourself from the sun and heat
  • Drive safely and be aware of local wildlife on the roads

Don’t:

  • Touch or damage Indigenous artifacts or cultural sites
  • Leave litter or debris in natural areas
  • Ignore signs or warnings regarding safety or local customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Walgett

Walgett is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to protect themselves from the sun and heat, including wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Health in Walgett

The town has a hospital and a number of medical facilities, including the Walgett Aboriginal Medical Service. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as Ross River Virus.

Getting to Walgett: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Walgett

The closest airport to Walgett is Dubbo Regional Airport, which is approximately 3.5 hours drive away. Visitors can also fly into Sydney or Brisbane airports and hire a car to make the journey to Walgett.

Bus Stations Near Walgett

Walgett has a bus stop in the town centre where visitors can catch a bus to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Driving is the most popular mode of transport in the Outback, and visitors can hire a car or bring their vehicle.

How to Get Around in Walgett

Walgett is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Walgett: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lightning Ridge

Lightning Ridge is a small town approximately 150 km north of Walgett. The town is famous for its opal mining industry and is home to a number of underground mines that visitors can explore.

Dubbo

Dubbo is a regional centre located approximately 3.5 hours south-east of Walgett. Visitors can explore the city’s cultural attractions, including the Taronga Western Plains Zoo and the Dubbo Regional Theatre and Convention Centre.

Conclusion

Walgett is a unique and fascinating Outback town that offers visitors an authentic Australian experience. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Walgett has something for everyone.
