Introduction to Cobar

Located in northwestern New South Wales, Cobar is a small mining town with a rich history, beautiful landscapes, and friendly locals. Founded in the 1870s, the area was once a bustling hub for copper and gold mining, but has since evolved into one of the most unique and fascinating destinations in the Outback.

Cobar is a great place to experience the true Australian outback spirit. Visitors will be amazed by the stark contrast of rugged terrain, historic landmarks, and peaceful nature. Whether you’re into history, outdoor activities, or simply looking for a unique and unforgettable adventure, Cobar is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Cobar

Cobar has a wealth of natural beauty and rich history for visitors to explore, with something to cater to every interest. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Great Cobar Heritage Centre: This museum is home to an impressive collection of artifacts, photographs, and displays highlighting the area’s rich history and mining heritage.

  • Fort Bourke Hill Lookout: Located atop Fort Bourke Hill, this lookout offers stunning panoramic views of Cobar and the surrounding landscapes. It’s also a great spot to enjoy a picnic with family and friends.

  • Wilson’s Shaft Mine and Heritage Walk: Take a stroll around the flora and fauna and explore the history of the abandoned shaft mine and viewlets.

  • Cobar Miners Heritage Walk: A path built in 23 years to commemorate the miners that have passed through ages; Scuba Diving in this great, outdoor, rocky landscape, and seeing where early industry thrived, sounds like an awesome adventure, doesn’t it?

Natural Attractions of Cobar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cobar Wetlands: Just a few kilometers from the town center, the wetlands are a sanctuary for various birds and waterbodies. It’s a perfect space for cycling and hiking around the scenic view over the landscape.

  • Day Dream Mine: This is an outstanding tour of the underground life that once was in Cobar. You can see the old mining equipment, tools and historic documents marking the tunnel’s past.

  • Mount Grenfell Historic Site: This site offers a rare opportunity to step back in time and explore the indigenous aboriginal rock engravings dating back around 40,000 years. You’ll also be treated to stunning views of an incredible landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cobar

Cobar is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts thanks to the various hiking and biking trails that criss-cross the region. Hiking and biking offer an amazing way to experience the beautiful surroundings and landscapes.

  • Lerida Hiking Trail: This gentle 4.5km hike is perfect for those looking for a peaceful stroll in nature. Hike over rocky landscapes and scenic views of the sunset.

  • Mount Grenfell Art Site Trail: This trail is easy to moderate but will take your breath away. You’ll encounter fantastic views and a wealth of history along the way.

  • Biking Trails: Cobar is equipped with different biking trails covering over 100kms, with varying degrees of difficulty. One of the best ones is ‘Wentworth Road’, which offers scenic views, and pro-biker friendly challenges.

Best Things to Do in Cobar: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving: The abandoned Copper Shaft provides a unique experience for divers to explore lake-filled down into the shaft’s dark and murky depths.
  • Star Gazing: Cobar offers the perfect starting point for a star gazing adventure. Due to its remote location, you’ll experience an unpolluted view of the starry sky. Try touring the nighttime sky with guidance.
  • Camel Rides: Take a sunset or sunrise camel ride over the outback dunes and get a chance to spot kangaroos, wallabies and birds during the ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Cobar

Food and drink in Cobar are influenced by its mining history mixed in with some multicultural ambience. Here are some dishes and drinks you must try on your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cobar Spicy Beef Burger: Local beef patty,burger sauce, and a unique blending of spicy sriracha & chilli sauce.
  • Lamb Shank Pie: A hearty dish showcasing succulent lamb shanks, Guinness gravy, and a bed of mash, surrounded by crispy pastry.
  • Baked Zucchini Fritters: These are perfect for a light brunch or lunch, featuring fresh zucchini, fetta, and pine nuts.

Local Beverages

Cobar is home to several pubs and bars serving local drinks. Must-try beverages include:

  • Cobar Ale: A unique beer brewed right in Cobar, with a light and refreshing flavour.
  • Cobar Rum: A unique spirit made from local sugar cane, with a rich and complex flavour.
  • Cobar Red Wine: An Australian classic with deep red hue and aged in oak barrels providing a unique taste.

Where to Stay in Cobar: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

Cobar offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences including:

  • Cobar Town and Country Motor Inn: Equipment with all the necessities, this motel offers air-conditioned rooms and a swimming pool.
  • Cobar Caravan Park: This place benefits from a cool environment, lush greenery, and various type of accommodation options that include cabins, campsite and even deluxe villas.
  • Great Western Hotel: Features basic rooms with shared bathrooms but more ideal for backpackers. They also have a famous bar selling local beers and classic dishes.

What to Buy in Cobar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cobar is home to several markets selling a range of local products from souvenirs to unique crafts. Be sure to check out:

  • Cobar Heritage Market: Specialty items for keeping, such as handmade crafts, culturally-inspired wearables, and jewelry.
  • Cobar Farmer’s Market: Sells locally produced fruits and vegetables, meats, and fresh-baked goods.
  • Old Reservoir: Unique crafts taking inspiration from the region’s architecture can also be found here.

Local Culture and Customs of Cobar

One of the charming aspects of Cobar includes its thriving farm culture. Mostly made up of beef, dairy, and sheep farming that create a cherished closeness amongst residents regardless of their origin.

Best Time to Visit Cobar

The best time to visit Cobar would be during the spring and autumn, March through May or September to November, where the temperature is mild. Summers in the outback can be exceedingly hot and winters are very cold, and the weather can be uneven, impacting takes longer to travel between attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cobar

Do:

  • Be respectful of local culture, customs, and traditions.
  • Adventure out and explore the surrounding area for a real Australian outback experience.

Don’t:

  • Never leave your belongings unattended as pickpocketing is rare but can happen.
  • Avoid traveling around the outback alone and always check-in with a friend or family.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cobar

Cobar is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates.

Health in Cobar

Be sure to take appropriate precautions like carrying sunscreen, keeping hydrated, and wearing proper clothing when going out hiking, especially in the hot summer months. Juice up on vitamins to keep common colds and flu at bay.

Getting to Cobar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cobar is reasonably remote and requires some planning.

Airports near Cobar

Cobar is located approximately 6 hours drive from Sydney with Dubbo Airport the closest airport for airline connections.

Bus Stations near Cobar

NSW Trainlink offers daily bus services connecting Dubbo and Cobar.

Other Options

Hired cards, city taxis or even taking a leisurely drive through the outback stretching your adventure even further.

How to Get Around in Cobar

The easiest and most convenient way to get around Cobar is by car, if available to you. Rental cars can be hired from Sydney and Dubbo.

Where to Go Next from Cobar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cobar serves as a great launching point to explore the broader northwestern New South Wales region, with the following destinations and excursions in close proximity:

  • Dubbo: A vibrant regional city known for its world-class zoo and beautiful parks.
  • Broken Hill: A charming, historic mining town with an evocative artistic ambience.
  • Mungo National Park: A surreal wilderness with ancient landscapes and rich indigenous history.

Conclusion

Cobar is a wonderful place full of history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. If you’re looking for a unique and authentic Australian outback experience, Cobar should be at the top of your list. With the above insights and tips, you’re all set for a memorable adventure in Cobar.
