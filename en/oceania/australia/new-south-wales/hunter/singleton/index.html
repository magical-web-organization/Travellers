Introduction to Singleton

Located in the Hunter region of New South Wales, Singleton is a historic town that offers a taste of rural Australia. With a population of just over 22,000, Singleton offers a slower pace of life, magnificent natural attractions, rich history, and friendly locals. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, Singleton has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Singleton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Singleton Historical Society Museum: This museum provides an in-depth look at the history of Singleton, including photographs, artifacts, and documents.
  • Singleton Military Area: As one of the largest Defense training areas in Australia, Singleton Military Area is a must-visit for those interested in military history.
  • Sacred Spaces Singleton: This former convent turned cultural event space offers a unique look into the local religious history.

Natural Attractions of Singleton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Royal National Park: Home to a variety of wildlife and vegetation, Mount Royal National Park is the perfect spot for hiking, birdwatching, and enjoying scenic views.
  • Lake St Clair: This beautiful lake offers a wide range of recreational activities, including fishing, swimming, kayaking, and boating.
  • Wollemi National Park: Wollemi National Park is home to stunning natural landscapes and is the perfect place to explore the great outdoors, with hiking trails, camping grounds, and magnificent rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Singleton

  • Heritage Walk: A 3.5-kilometer trail that takes visitors through the historic heart of Singleton, showcasing the town’s rich history and beautiful architecture.
  • Mount Royal Summit Walk: A 7.8-kilometer trail that ascends Mount Royal, showcasing panoramic views of the national park and the surrounding valley.
  • Lake St Clair Walk: A 2.6-kilometer trail that follows the perimeter of Lake St Clair, providing stunning views of the water and surrounding wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Singleton: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours: With the Hunter Valley just a short day trip away, wine tasting tours are a popular activity for visitors to Singleton.
  • Skydiving: The thrill of freefalling with the beautiful scenery of Singleton is sure to make for an unforgettable experience.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: This is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Singleton with a different perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Singleton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barramundi- Also known as Australian sea bass is a delicious freshwater fish that is grilled or pan-fried and served with a variety of side dishes.
  • Sausage Rolls - A pastry that is filled with seasoned sausage meat and baked for a crispy, flavorful treat.
  • Pumpkin Scones - A traditional Australian pastry that is made with pumpkin puree, is buttery and sweet.

Local Beverages

  • Shiraz - The Hunter Valley’s signature red wine, it’s a medium-bodied, spicy wine variety. The best way to taste this wine is to visit the local vineyards.
  • Australian Pale Ale - A classic beer in Australia that is perfect for summer, with refreshing citrusy and tropical notes.

Where to Stay in Singleton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Singleton offers a variety of accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels, and vacation homes. Some of our top picks include:

  • Quest Singleton - Offers spacious, fully furnished apartments with a range of amenities, including a fitness center and an outdoor pool.
  • Charbonnier Motor Inn - A great mid-range option that offers tastefully decorated rooms, reasonable prices, and room service.
  • Lone Pine Motel - A reasonable, budget-friendly option that is clean, comfortable, and conveniently located.

What to Buy in Singleton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hunter Valley Wines: Bring home a bottle of the region’s signature wine, Shiraz, to enjoy a taste of the Hunter Valley at home.
  • Handmade Soaps and Candles: These locally-made products offer a unique and fragrant souvenir of Singleton.
  • Aboriginal Art - From canvas paintings to carvings, Aboriginal art pieces can be found in a variety of price ranges.

Local Culture and Customs of Singleton

  • A relaxed pace of life - There is no rush in Singleton, and locals often take time to enjoy the little things and take in the beauty around them.
  • A love for the outdoors - Residents of Singleton love to spend time outdoors, whether it’s hiking, swimming, camping, or exploring the national parks.
  • Respect for Indigenous cultures - Singleton’s history is deeply intertwined with Indigenous cultures and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Singleton

The best time to visit Singleton, New South Wales is during spring or autumn. During these seasons, the weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities comfortably. The summer months can be hot and humid, making it harder to enjoy the outdoors, and winter can be too cold for some visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Singleton

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and admire the natural beauty.
  • Be respectful and learn about the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit local attractions and try some of the unique local foods.

Don’t:

  • Littering - Protect and respect our natural surroundings by picking up your litter.
  • Damage local historical and natural sites.
  • Engage in any activities that are disrespectful, hurtful, or illegal.

Safety and Health

Safety in Singleton

Singleton is a safe place to visit with low crime rates. Like any other place, always keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Singleton

Your health and safety are important, and it’s important to be mindful of your physical and mental wellbeing. Stay hydrated and address any injuries immediately. In case of an emergency, dial 000 for assistance immediately.

Getting to Singleton: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Singleton: Newcastle Airport, which is located around 70 kilometers from Singleton.
  • Bus Stations Near Singleton: Singleton is serviced by the Hunter Valley Buses network, with buses that operate throughout the region.
  • Other Options: Singleton is conveniently located just a short drive from the Pacific Highway and the New England Highway, and it is also possible to get to Singleton by train.

How to Get Around in Singleton

The best way to get around in Singleton is by car or bike. Some hotels or accommodations may offer bike rentals for guests. Rental cars are also available if needed.

Where to Go Next from Singleton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newcastle: A vibrant coastal city that allows visitors to explore the beaches and its cultural scene, just over an hour’s drive from Singleton.
  • Barrington Tops National Park: A nature lover paradise with a range of walking trails, camping, and wildlife spotting opportunities.
  • Maitland: A historic city noted for its well-preserved Victorian architecture, historic sites, and local museums.

Conclusion

Singleton, New South Wales, is a hidden gem that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich history and natural beauty of Australia. With its friendly locals, delicious food, and variety of activities, Singleton is a must-visit destination in Australia. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your visit to Singleton and enjoy all that this charming town has to offer.
