Introduction to Scone

Scone is a quaint town nestled in the picturesque Hunter region of New South Wales, Australia. Known for its beautiful natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, Scone is the perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Australian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Scone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Scone is home to numerous museums and historical sites that provide fascinating insights into the town’s past. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Australian Stock Horse Society Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of the Australian Stock Horse breed, which played a vital role in the development of Australia’s pastoral industry.

  • Scone and Upper Hunter Historical Society Museum: The museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the town’s rich heritage.

  • St. Luke’s Anglican Church: A beautiful example of neo-gothic architecture, the church is famous for its stunning stained-glass windows and historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Scone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Scone’s natural beauty is a major draw for visitors, and the town has several outdoor attractions that are worth exploring, including:

  • Barrington Tops National Park: A world heritage site, the national park is home to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in Australia, including the Barrington Tops Plateau, an ancient rainforest, and several scenic waterfalls.

  • Lake Glenbawn State Park: This picturesque park is situated on the banks of Lake Glenbawn and is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking.

  • Scone Regional Livestock Selling Centre: For those interested in agriculture, the selling centre hosts weekly auctions for sheep, cattle, and horses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scone

Scone has several hiking and biking trails that offer an immersive experience of the town’s natural beauty. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Burning Mountain Nature Reserve Walk: A scenic trail through the forested landscape that leads to the Burning Mountain, a naturally occurring underground coal fire that has been burning for thousands of years.

  • White Park Trail: A 4.5 km trail that offers stunning views of the countryside and the Scone Racecourse.

  • Scone Heritage Trail: This historic trail takes visitors on a journey through the town’s history, showcasing the most significant landmarks and sites.

Best Things to Do in Scone: Activities and Tours

From hot air balloon rides to helicopter tours, Scone has plenty of exciting activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Scone:

  • Hunter Valley Wine Tours: A must-do activity for wine enthusiasts, a tour of the Hunter Valley vineyards is a great way to indulge in the town’s rich culture of winemaking.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Taking a hot air balloon ride is a unique way to experience the Hunter Valley’s stunning beauty, and visitors can take in the breathtaking views at sunrise or sunset.

  • Helicopter Tours: For those seeking an adrenaline rush, a helicopter tour of the valley is an exciting way to explore the area from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scone

Here’s a 7-day itinerary for visitors to make the most of their time in Scone:

Day 1: Visit the Australian Stock Horse Society Museum and explore the Scone Racecourse.

Day 2: Take a helicopter tour of the Hunter Valley.

Day 3: Take a hiking trip to the Burning Mountain Nature Reserve.

Day 4: Visit the Scone and Upper Hunter Historical Society Museum in the morning and relax in the afternoon with a visit to the Lake Glenbawn State Park.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride at sunrise followed by a visit to the St. Luke’s Anglican Church

Day 6: Spend a day on a Hunter Valley Wine Tour.

Day 7: Visit the Scone Regional Livestock Selling Centre and explore the Scone Heritage Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Scone

Must-Try Dishes

Scone is known for its quintessentially Australian food and drink offerings, with some of the most popular dishes including:

  • Meat pies: A savory pastry filled with minced meat, vegetables, and gravy.

  • Tim Tams: A chocolate-coated biscuit sandwiched with a creamy filling.

  • Vegemite: A spread made from yeast extract and used primarily as a breakfast spread.

Local Beverages

Hunter Valley is renowned for its wineries, making wine tasting a central part of the town’s culture. For those who prefer other drinks, Scone also has several local breweries and distilleries offering brews and spirits unique to the region.

Where to Stay in Scone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Scone offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Scone:

  • Strathearn Park Lodge: A luxury guesthouse located in a serene and beautiful location, offering stunning views of the valley and a peaceful environment

  • Scone Motor Inn: A comfortable and affordable accommodation option for travelers, situated in the heart of town, close to several local attractions.

  • Segenhoe Inn Luxury Boutique Hotel: This elegant hotel offers stylish, spacious rooms with luxurious amenities, along with sweeping views of the Hunter Valley.

What to Buy in Scone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Scone is known for its locally produced agricultural goods, such as cheese, wine, and exotic flowers. Visitors can also find souvenirs and gifts in the local farmers’ markets and stalls.

Local Culture and Customs of Scone

Scone has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and practices. Australians are generally friendly and hospitable, and visitors can expect a warm welcome in Scone.

Best Time to Visit Scone

Scone is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scone

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and go wine tasting.

  • Respect the local customs and practices.

  • Explore the outdoors and take in the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rules of the land and trespass on private property.

  • Be rude to the locals or litter in public spaces.

  • Forget to carry a hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scone

Scone is a safe and friendly town, and visitors can enjoy exploring the area without worrying about their safety. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should be cautious and vigilant when exploring alone at night.

Health in Scone

Scone has a well-equipped hospital and medical facilities to cater to any health emergencies that visitors may encounter. As always, visitors should carry appropriate health insurance and medications.

Getting to Scone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scone

The nearest airports to Scone are Newcastle Airport and Sydney Kingsford Smith International Airport, both of which have regular flights to major international and domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Scone

Scone has a limited bus service, with the Hunter Valley bus service offering the most comprehensive connections.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a taxi from the nearest airport is the most convenient way to reach Scone. Visitors can also take a train from Sydney or Newcastle to the town.

How to Get Around in Scone

The best way to explore Scone is by car, as most of the town’s attractions and sights are spread out. Taxis and rental cars are readily available for visitors to hire.

Where to Go Next from Scone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations to explore from Scone, including:

  • Newcastle: A bustling coastal city famous for its beaches and vibrant culture.

  • Port Stephens: A stunning coastal town known for its water sports and outdoor adventures.

  • Maitland: A historic town located on the banks of the Hunter River, offering numerous cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Scone’s natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Australian experience. From scenic hikes to wine tasting tours, there’s something for everyone in Scone. Plan your dream trip today and explore this beautiful town in the heart of Australia’s Hunter region.
