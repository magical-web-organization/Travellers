Introduction to Tomaree National Park

Tomaree National Park is a beautiful area in Port Stephens, a coastal town in the Hunter region of New South Wales, Australia. It is known for its breathtaking scenery, diverse wildlife, coastal sand dunes, and historic World War II sites. The park covers an area of approximately 2,224 hectares and offers various activities such as hiking, swimming, surfing, picnicking, and whale watching.

List of Places to Visit in Tomaree National Park

There are several places to visit in Tomaree National Park, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tomaree Head Summit Walk: This popular hike offers stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding mountains. The walk leads to the Tomaree Head summit, which was used as a lookout during World War II.
  • Fort Tomaree: The fort was built during World War II to protect the coast from enemy attacks. It now houses a museum that displays photographs and artifacts from the war.
  • Gun Emplacements: These historic gun emplacements were also built during World War II to protect the coast from Japanese attacks.

Natural Attractions of Tomaree National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Birubi Beach: This is a popular beach for swimming, surfing, and fishing. It is also home to a large sand dune system that stretches for 32 kilometres along the coast.
  • Wreck Beach: Located near the Fingal Bay, this secluded beach offers a perfect escape from the crowds. It is also a popular spot for whale watching during the migration season.
  • Zenith Beach: This is a great spot for surfing and swimming, with its calm waters and clear sands. It offers stunning views of the Tomaree Head and Mount Tomaree.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tomaree National Park

Tomaree National Park offers a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to different skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Tomaree Head Summit Walk: This is a moderate hike that takes about an hour to complete. It offers stunning views of the coast and the surrounding mountains.
  • Wreck Beach Trail: This is a short, easy trail that leads to the Wreck Beach. It passes through a coastal heathland with beautiful wildflowers and birdlife.
  • Yacaaba Headland Walk: This is a challenging hike that takes about four hours to complete. It offers panoramic views of the coastline and the hinterland.

Best Things to Do in Tomaree National Park: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking trails, Tomaree National Park offers a range of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in the park include:

  • Swimming and Surfing: Birubi Beach, Wreck Beach, and Zenith Beach offer great opportunities for swimming and surfing.
  • Fishing: Birubi Beach and Fingal Spit are popular spots for fishing.
  • Whale Watching: Tomaree Head and the Wreck Beach offer great vantage points to watch the annual whale migration.
  • Picnicking: The park has several picnic areas equipped with tables, shelters, and barbecues for visitors.
  • Camping: The park has a campground with basic facilities that can accommodate up to 20 people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tomaree National Park

Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip to Tomaree National Park:

Day 1: Arrive in Port Stephens and check into your accommodation. Day 2: Hike the Tomaree Head Summit Walk and visit Fort Tomaree and the Gun Emplacements. Day 3: Explore the beaches of Fingal Bay and Shoal Bay. Day 4: Go whale watching at Tomaree Head or the Wreck Beach. Day 5: Take a day trip to Newcastle, a vibrant city on the coast. Day 6: Enjoy the sand dunes of Birubi Beach and have a picnic. Day 7: Relax in your accommodation and prepare for departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Tomaree National Park

Tomaree National Park has limited eating options within its boundaries, but there are several great food and drink venues in Port Stephens.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: This classic Australian dish is best experienced at the beachside cafes.
  • Oysters: Port Stephens is known for its fresh oysters, which can be enjoyed at the oyster farms or at local restaurants.
  • Seafood Platter: This delicious platter includes a range of seafood such as prawns, crabs, lobster, and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Hunter Valley is known for its vineyards and produces some of Australia’s best wines.
  • Beer: There are several microbreweries in the region that offer a range of craft beers.
  • Coffee: Australia is known for its quality coffee, and there are several cafes in Port Stephens that serve great coffee.

Where to Stay in Tomaree National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tomaree National Park has a basic campground that can accommodate up to 20 people. However, there are several accommodation options in and around Port Stephens, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts.

Budget Accommodation

  • Samurai Beach Resort: This eco-friendly resort offers cabins and lodges with basic facilities and a swimming pool.

Mid-Range Accommodation

  • Ramada Resort: This resort offers comfortable rooms with great views of the ocean and a swimming pool.
  • Ibis Styles Port Stephens Salamander Shores: This hotel offers modern rooms with a restaurant and a bar.

Luxury Accommodation

  • Anchorage Port Stephens: This 4.5-star resort offers luxurious rooms, a spa, and a waterfront restaurant.
  • The Retreat Port Stephens: This 5-star resort offers villas with private pools, spa treatments, and fine dining.

What to Buy in Tomaree National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several local products and souvenirs that you can buy in Port Stephens, including:

  • Oysters: Freshly shucked oysters make great souvenirs for seafood lovers.
  • Wine: The Hunter Valley produces some of Australia’s best wines, and there are several vineyards in the region where you can purchase wine.
  • Aboriginal Art: Port Stephens has a rich Aboriginal culture, and there are several art galleries that sell authentic Aboriginal art.

Local Culture and Customs of Tomaree National Park

Tomaree National Park is located on the land of the Worimi Aboriginal people. Visitors are encouraged to respect the cultural significance of the area and to learn about Aboriginal culture through guided tours and cultural experiences. Visitors are also reminded to take their litter with them and to respect the natural environment of the park.

Best Time to Visit Tomaree National Park

The best time to visit Tomaree National Park is during the spring and autumn months (September to November and March to May). The weather is mild, and the park is less crowded during these months. The summer months (December to February) are the busiest, and the park can get crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tomaree National Park

Do:

  • Respect the cultural significance of the area and learn about Aboriginal culture.
  • Take your litter with you and keep the park clean.
  • Stay on the marked trails and follow park regulations.
  • Bring enough water and supplies for your trip.

Don’t:

  • Harm or disturb wildlife.
  • Light fires outside designated areas.
  • Leave your belongings unattended.
  • Damage or remove plants or natural features.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tomaree National Park

  • Be aware of the weather conditions and take appropriate precautions.
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Use caution when swimming or surfing in the ocean.
  • Take first aid supplies and know basic first aid techniques.

Health in Tomaree National Park

  • Bring enough water and food for your trip.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid insect bites and stings.
  • Be aware of poisonous plants and animals and avoid contact with them.

How to Get Around in Tomaree National Park

The best way to get around Tomaree National Park is by hiking or biking. The park has several hiking and biking trails that can take you to the different attractions. There are also several tour operators that offer guided tours of the park.

Where to Go Next from Tomaree National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Port Stephens is located near several other attractions and destinations in New South Wales, including:

  • Hunter Valley: This wine region is located approximately 1.5 hours from Port Stephens and offers vineyards and wine tasting tours.
  • Newcastle: This vibrant city is located approximately 1 hour from Port Stephens and offers great cafes, restaurants, and cultural experiences.
  • Sydney: Australia’s largest city is located approximately 2.5 hours from Port Stephens and offers iconic landmarks such as the Sydney Opera House and the Harbour Bridge.

Conclusion

Tomaree National Park is a beautiful destination that offers natural wonders, historic sites, and outdoor adventures.
