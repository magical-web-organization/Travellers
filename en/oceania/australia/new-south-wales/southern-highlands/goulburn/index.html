Food and Drink Scene of Goulburn

Goulburn has a thriving food and drink scene, with a range of local delicacies and beverages to discover. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulburn Sausages: Made from locally-sourced beef and pork, these sausages are a local specialty and can be found at many restaurants and cafes throughout the city.
  • Chicken Parmigiana: A classic Australian pub meal, this hearty dish is served in many of the city’s pubs and restaurants.
  • Berrima Cheddar Cheese: Produced in nearby Berrima, this award-winning cheese is a must-try for foodies.

Local Beverages

  • Goulburn Brewery: This local brewery produces a range of craft beers and is a popular spot for beer lovers to visit.
  • Misty Glen Wines: Located in the nearby village of Gundaroo, Misty Glen produces award-winning wines using locally-sourced grapes.

Where to Stay in Goulburn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options in Goulburn to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Mercure Goulburn: This centrally located hotel offers modern amenities and stylish decor.
  • Lilac City Motor Inn & Steakhouse: This popular motor inn is known for its friendly staff and excellent steakhouse restaurant.
  • Goulburn YHA: For budget-conscious travellers, the YHA offers clean and comfortable accommodation in a central location.
  • Mandelson’s of Goulburn: For those looking for a unique and historic experience, Mandelson’s is a beautifully restored guesthouse located in a historic mansion.

What to Buy in Goulburn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many local products and souvenirs to choose from in Goulburn, including:

  • Goulburn Sausages: These locally-made sausages are a popular souvenir to take home with you.
  • Goulburn Postcards: Featuring iconic landmarks and scenic views, these postcards are a great way to remember your trip.
  • Local Artwork: Goulburn has a thriving arts and culture scene, with many local artists producing unique and beautiful works of art.

Local Culture and Customs of Goulburn

Goulburn has a rich heritage and culture that can be seen in its architecture, landmarks and local traditions. Visitors to Goulburn are encouraged to respect local customs and values, and to take the time to learn about the city’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Goulburn

Goulburn can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and autumn months (September-November and March-May), when the temperatures are mild and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. Summer (December-February) can be quite hot, while winter (June-August) is often cold and can experience snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goulburn

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Goulburn:

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the city’s rich history and architecture
  • Respect local customs and values
  • Take advantage of opportunities to explore the region’s stunning natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local attractions
  • Disrespect local traditions or values
  • Ignore warning signs or disregard safety advice

Safety and Health

Goulburn is a safe city to visit, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety and well-being. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Goulburn

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone after dark
  • Keep your valuables secure and be aware of pickpockets and theft
  • Pay attention to warning signs and heed the advice of local authorities

Health in Goulburn

  • Ensure you have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Goulburn
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn
  • Take precautions to prevent mosquito bites during the summer months

Getting to Goulburn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Goulburn can be easily accessed by a variety of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Goulburn

  • Canberra Airport: approximately 1 hour’s drive from Goulburn.
  • Sydney Airport: approximately 2-3 hours’ drive from Goulburn.

Bus Stations Near Goulburn

  • Goulburn Bus Station: located in the center of the city, with regular services to Sydney, Canberra, and other nearby destinations.
  • Greyhound Bus Station: operates services to Goulburn from Sydney and other major cities.

Other Options

  • Private car hire: car hire is available from both Canberra and Sydney airports and in the Goulburn city center.
  • Train: Goulburn is located on the Sydney to Melbourne train line, with regular services to both cities.

How to Get Around in Goulburn

Goulburn is a compact city that can easily be explored on foot, with many attractions located within walking distance of the city center. There are also several taxi and bus services available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Goulburn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Goulburn is located in the heart of the Southern Highlands, a region known for its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and thriving arts and culture scene. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring:

  • Wombeyan Caves: Located approximately 60 km southwest of Goulburn, the Wombeyan Caves are a series of stunning limestone caves that can be explored on guided tours.
  • Berrima: A historic village located approximately 35 km southeast of Goulburn, Berrima is known for its beautifully preserved architecture and charming atmosphere.
  • Southern Highlands Wineries: The Southern Highlands region is home to a wide range of award-winning wineries, many of which offer wine tastings and cellar door sales.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring Goulburn’s rich history, immersing yourself in the local arts and culture scene, or simply enjoying the stunning natural beauty of the surrounding region, Goulburn is a destination that truly has something for everyone. With this travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to this historic city.
