Introduction to Moss Vale

Located in the Southern Highlands of New South Wales, Australia, Moss Vale is a charming town with a rich history, magnificent scenery, and a vibrant food scene. Founded in the mid-19th century, Moss Vale has been an important agricultural center, with many of its historic buildings preserved to this day.

Moss Vale is known for its fresh air, beautiful landscapes, and plethora of attractions that make it an ideal destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Moss Vale for an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Moss Vale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Moss Vale has a rich cultural heritage, with numerous museums and historical places that provide insight into its past. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Moss Vale include:

  • The Berrima District Museum: located in the nearby town of Berrima, this museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the region, from Aboriginal culture to colonial times.
  • The Old Courthouse Museum: housed in a historic building, this museum showcases exhibits on the region’s legal system and early settlement.
  • Moss Vale and Rural Caminos Museum: this museum celebrates the area’s rural history with displays of farm machinery, antique vehicles, and other artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Moss Vale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Moss Vale is stunningly beautiful, surrounded by lush greenery, rolling hills, and hidden waterfalls and gorges. Explore its natural beauty through these top-rated outdoor attractions:

  • Fitzroy Falls: These falls are an impressive sight, cascading down a 81-meter drop. Explore the walking tracks around the falls or relax at the picnic area and take in the beautiful scenery.
  • Cecil Hoskins Nature Reserve: This reserve is a haven for wildlife and bird watching enthusiasts, featuring over 80 species of birdlife, including the rare Eastern Bristlebird, and other animal species like swamp wallabies, red-necked pademelons, and echidnas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moss Vale

Moss Vale is a paradise for adventurers, offering plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts:

  • Mount Gibraltar Reserve is famous for its stunning views and can be explored on foot through a range of walking tracks. You can also go cycling to explore the park’s natural wilderness.
  • Moss Vale Cycleway: this easy-going cycleway is perfect for those with different fitness levels, thanks to its flat trails and suitable for those with families.

Best Things to Do in Moss Vale: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking to explore the town or the surrounding area, Moss Vale has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Moss Vale:

  • Southern Highlands Food and Wine Trail: this trail is a must-do for food and wine lovers. You’ll get the chance to sample some of Moss Vale’s finest and freshest produce, from locally made chocolates to premium wines.
  • Moss Vale Farmers Market: held every Saturday, the market is the place to go for locally produced fresh food, handmade crafts, and community stalls selling crafts and unique souvenirs.
  • Tour of the Old Dairy: one of the oldest buildings in Moss Vale, the dairy exemplifies some of the best historic architecture in the area and offers visitors a chance to immerse in the historical past of this tiny town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moss Vale

To get the most out of your stay in Moss Vale, consider following this itinerary for a full week of exploration:

Day 1: Visit museums and cultural attractions - Old Courthouse Museum and Berrima District Museum Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Fitzroy Falls and enjoy lunch at Robertson Pie Shop Day 3: Cycle along the scenic Moss Vale Cycleway or hike on any of the Walking Tracks of Mount Gibraltar Reserve. Day 4: Take the Southern Highlands Food and Wine Trail, and enjoy a day exploring the regions gourmet venues. Day 5: Take a scenic drive around Kangaroo Valley- great spot for hiking and wildlife spotting. Day 6: Visit Montrose Berry Farm and sample a range of organic jams, cordials, and baked treats. Day 7: Visit Moss Vale Farmer’s Market for fresh and local produce, and enjoy a relaxing picnic in the beautiful gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Moss Vale

Moss Vale has a thriving food and wine culture, offering visitors a chance to taste some of the freshest and most delicious produce in Australia.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Moss Vale include:

  • Gourmet Pies: You can’t visit Moss Vale without trying its iconic pies. With fillings ranging from beef and mushroom to chicken and leek, you’re sure to find something that tickles your fancy.
  • Fresh Handmade Cheese: Southern Highlands has a variety of artisan cheese maker including the huge range at the Robertson Cheese Factory, making the region well known for their cheese.
  • Local Berries: Berries are a specialty in Moss Vale with juicy raspberries, blueberries, strawberries lining up the shelves in the local market.

Local Beverages

Moss Vale is famous for its wine and beer, with many wineries and breweries offering tours and tastings. Here are a few places to stop during your visit:

  • Southern Highlands Brewery: this local brewery offers a great selection of refreshing beers on tap and some really unique one-off brews that were made on-site.
  • Highland Merchant: specialty coffee roaster with great options for coffee lovers, their coffee can be found in all the great cafes around the region.

Where to Stay in Moss Vale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moss Vale provides visitors with a wide range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • Briars Country Lodge offers a tranquil stay in a heritage-listed, traditional English country house, surrounded by lush gardens and scenic views of the Kangaroo river.
  • The George Boutique Hotel reimagines a historical building into modern accommodations oozing with character and charm.
  • Explorers Motel A popular choice for budget-friendly accommodation, Explorers Motel provides comfortable and contemporary rooms with several room types offered, catering to the needs of different travellers.

What to Buy in Moss Vale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moss Vale offers visitors with some great shopping locations, where they can find a range of unique souvenirs, local artisan crafts, and natural products. Some of the places to check out include:

  • The Book Barn: offering a vast collection of second-hand books, including those on Australian history and topics related to the Southern Highlands.
  • Robertson Cheese Factory: showcasing a variety of artisan cheeses made and sold on-site. Enjoy free samples of many of the different cheeses to find your favorite.
  • Highland Hamper Co: Select from the wide range of locally-made products including jams, pickles, olives cheese to put together your exceptional hamper.

Local Culture and Customs of Moss Vale

Moss Vale celebrates its cultural heritage in several festivals and events held throughout the year, including the Highland Tennessee Man, Robertson Show, Bowral Tulip Festival.

Moss Vale is also famous for its focus on sustainable and locally produced goods. Shopping and eating locally grown and made products are an important part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Moss Vale

The best time to visit Moss Vale is between March and May when the weather is mild and pleasant. This time period is popular with visitors who want to enjoy the beauty of the region without having to battle the extreme temperatures of summer, or the chill of winter which can have views sometimes hidden by mist.

  • Do try locally produced goods like cheese, beer, and wine.

Safety and Health

Moss Vale is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take normal precautions regarding their safety and health while travelling.

Safety in Moss Vale

  • Always keep your valuables secure when exploring the area.
  • Adhere to all local signage, including warning signs and speed limits while driving in the region.

Health in Moss Vale

  • Take appropriate precautions against sunburn, which can be quite severe in summer.
  • Pack insect repellent if you plan on exploring outdoor areas to protect against ticks.

Getting to Moss Vale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moss Vale is located approximately 2.5 hours south of Sydney and 1 hour from Canberra by car, making it a great weekend escape or an easy day trip from these popular locations. Alternatively, there are a few public transportation options you can take:

Airports Near Moss Vale

  • Sydney International Airport (2.5 hours by car)
  • Canberra International Airport (1 hour by car)

Bus Stations Near Moss Vale

  • Moss Vale Railway Station offers direct rail links to Sydney and Canberra with coach transfers from Sydney airport available.

Other Options

Visitors can opt to drive from Sydney or Canberra or take a Uber or Taxi if they do not have their own car.

How to Get Around in Moss Vale

Getting around Moss Vale is a breeze with plenty of transport options like:

  • Bicycle Hire: Several stores offer bike rental options aimed at both relaxed bike riding or on the vast network of mountain Bike trails around the region.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can opt for car rentals with several hire options available.
  • Walking: Exploring on foot is no hassle with plenty of walking tracks and circuit options around the region.

Where to Go Next from Moss Vale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Moss Vale is a destination that offers something for everyone history buffs, food fans, outdoor adventurers, and nature lovers Visitors can spend their days exploring museums and hiking trails or sampling local wines and cheeses. With so many attractions within a short drive of the town, there’s plenty to see and do during your stay. Use this comprehensive travel guide for Moss Vale to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this charming town.
