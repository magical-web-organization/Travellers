Introduction to Bungendore

Bungendore, located in Southern Highlands, New South Wales, is a hidden gem that many tourists overlook. However, this charming and peaceful town is a must-visit for anyone who wants to experience the best of Australian culture, history, nature, and more. With its friendly locals, picturesque landscapes, and unique attractions, Bungendore offers a great escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Bungendore

There are plenty of places to explore and discover in Bungendore, which caters to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the top things to do and see in Bungendore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bungendore Woodworks Gallery and Café - This gallery showcases the finest pieces of furniture, art, and craft made from Australian timbers since 1983. You can also indulge in coffee, cake, or local produce from their café.
  • Lark Hill Winery - This award-winning biodynamic winery, located in the historic gold rush region, offers a unique wine tasting experience and tours. You can discover the vineyards, winery, and cellar door, and learn about sustainable farming and winemaking practices.
  • Braidwood Museum - This museum is a must-visit for those who want to understand the history of Braidwood and the region. You can explore the stories, artifacts, and photos of the Indigenous people, pioneers, gold rush, and more.

Natural Attractions of Bungendore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake George - This natural lake, located 5km west of Bungendore, is a perfect spot for picnics, bushwalking, birdwatching, and sailing. You can also enjoy the panoramic views of the lake and its surroundings.
  • Gibraltar Rocks - This granite rock formation, located in the Gibraltar Range National Park, offers breathtaking views of the Snowy Mountains, Monaro Plains, and the coastal ranges. You can hike, bike, or horse ride to the lookout and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • The Big Hole - This geological wonder, located in Wee Jasper, is a massive limestone sinkhole that is over 100m deep. You can explore the area by bushwalking, fishing, or camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bungendore

Bungendore offers a range of hiking and biking routes for all levels of fitness and experience. You can explore the lush forests, rolling hills, and scenic views by foot or bike. Here are some of the popular routes in Bungendore:

  • Bungendore Park Loop - This easy and flat 3.7km looped walk takes you through the Bungendore Park, where you can see a variety of bird species, kangaroos, and wildflowers.
  • Lake George Bike Ride - This 43km bike ride takes you around Lake George and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Big Hole Walk - This 3km return walk takes you to the bottom of the Big Hole, where you can explore the limestone caves, waterfalls, and unique rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Bungendore: Activities and Tours

Bungendore has something for everyone, no matter their interests or preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Bungendore:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - This serene and magical experience takes you on a hot air balloon ride over the countryside, where you can see the sunrise and panoramic views of the region.
  • Bungendore Farmers Market - This weekly market, held every Saturday, offers a great selection of fresh produce, artisanal products, and local crafts. You can also indulge in delicious food and drinks from the food stalls.
  • Bungendore Fine Art and Design Studio Trail - This self-guided tour takes you through the Bungendore region, where you can discover the art galleries, studios, and workshops of local artists and designers.

Food and Drink Scene of Bungendore

Bungendore offers a great selection of food and drink options, ranging from fine dining to casual cafes and pubs. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Woodfired Pizza from The George Bar and Dining - This award-winning pub offers delicious and authentic woodfired pizzas and local wines, beers, and cocktails.
  • Lamb Shank from The Loaded Dog Hotel - This historic and cozy hotel offers hearty and tasty country-style meals, such as the famous lamb shank and ale pie.
  • Cheese from Crisp Cheese and Deli - This local deli offers a great selection of Australian cheeses, cured meats, and other gourmet produce.

Local Beverages

  • Shiraz from Lerida Estate Winery - This local winery offers rich and full-bodied red wines, including the Shiraz, made from the grapes grown in the Lake George region.
  • Beer from Capital Brewing Co. - This local brewery offers a variety of refreshing and hoppy beers, such as the Coast Ale, Trail Pale Ale, and Rock Hopper IPA.
  • Coffee from The Gathering Café - This local café offers delicious and aromatic coffee, made from the locally roasted beans and served with homemade cakes and pastries.

Where to Stay in Bungendore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bungendore offers a range of accommodation options, from quaint guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bungendore:

  • Bungendore Motel - This family-owned motel offers comfortable and convenient rooms, equipped with air conditioning, Wi-Fi, and parking. It’s located in the heart of Bungendore, close to the shops and restaurants.
  • The Carrington Inn - This historic and elegant guesthouse offers luxurious and spacious suites, decorated with antique furnishings and artworks. It also has a restaurant, bar, and gardens.
  • The George Bar and Dining - This trendy and stylish pub offers boutique accommodation, with six rooms equipped with king-sized beds, flat-screen TVs, and air conditioning. You can also enjoy the delicious food, drinks, and live music at the pub.

What to Buy in Bungendore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Timber Products from Bungendore Woodworks Gallery and Café - You can bring home a piece of Australia’s finest timber furniture, art, and crafts, made in the traditional and modern styles.
  • Wines from Lark Hill Winery - You can bring home a bottle of award-winning biodynamic wine, made from the grapes grown on the stunning hillsides of Lark Hill.
  • Cheese from Crisp Cheese and Deli - You can bring home some of the finest Australian cheese, made from the locally sourced and crafted ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Bungendore

Bungendore has a rich cultural and historical heritage, influenced by the Indigenous people, settlers, and gold rush. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their community and environment. Here are some customs and etiquette to keep in mind when visiting Bungendore:

  • Respect the Nature and Wildlife - Bungendore is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes and wildlife, so be mindful of your impact on the environment and be respectful of the animals’ habitats.
  • Observe the Local Laws and Regulations - Bungendore is a peaceful and safe town, but remember to obey the traffic rules, speed limits, and alcohol restrictions.
  • Engage with the Locals - Bungendore locals are passionate about their culture, history, and traditions. Strike up a conversation with them and learn more about their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Bungendore

Bungendore can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is from September to May, when the climate is mild and pleasant. The summer months (December to February) can get hot and humid, but you can cool off in the lake or winery. The autumn months (March to May) offer a beautiful palette of colors, with the trees changing from green to red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bungendore

Do:

  • Do hike or bike around the town and its surroundings - Bungendore offers a range of scenic and enjoyable hiking and biking trails for all levels of fitness and experience.
  • Do explore the local culture and history - Bungendore has a rich and fascinating cultural and historical heritage, which you can learn more about by visiting the museums, galleries, and landmarks.
  • Do support the local businesses and industries - Bungendore has a thriving local economy, which relies on tourism and agriculture. By supporting the local farmers, boutiques, and restaurants, you contribute to the community’s sustainability and growth.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment - Bungendore values its natural resources and the wildlife, so make sure to keep the town clean and intact.
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs or traditions - Bungendore residents take pride in their cultural and social norms, so respect their values and beliefs.
  • Don’t trespass or enter restricted areas - Bungendore has private properties and protected areas, so make sure to respect the boundaries and follow the rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bungendore

Bungendore is a safe and quiet town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, as with any travel destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Lock your car and valuables in a safe place - Bungendore is a small town, but there are still instances of theft and burglary. Make sure to keep your belongings secure and out of sight when you’re not around.
  • Obey the traffic and driving laws - Bungendore has a few main roads and highways, but they can get busy during peak hours. Make sure to drive safely and follow the speed limits and traffic signs.
  • Watch out for wildlife when driving or hiking - Bungendore is home to a variety of animals, such as kangaroos, wombats, and snakes. Make sure to stay alert, especially when driving at night or hiking in the bushland.

Health in Bungendore

Bungendore has a few medical facilities, including a pharmacy, dentist, and doctor’s surgery. However, if you have complex or urgent medical needs, it’s recommended to seek treatment in the nearby cities, such as Queanbeyan or Canberra. It’s also important to have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to Australia.

Getting to Bungendore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bungendore is located about 30km east of Canberra and 280km south-west of Sydney. Here are some transportation options to get to Bungendore:

Airports Near Bungendore

  • Canberra Airport - This is the closest airport to Bungendore, located about 36km away. There are regular flights from major Australian cities, such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. You can rent a car, take a taxi, or book a shuttle service from the airport to Bungendore.

Bus Stations Near Bungendore

  • Queanbeyan Bus Interchange - This is the main bus station in Queanbeyan, located about 25km away from Bungendore. There are regular bus services from Sydney, Canberra, and other towns in the region.

Other Options

  • Driving - You can drive to Bungendore from Sydney or Canberra, using the Hume Highway and Kings Highway. It takes about 3 hours from Sydney and 30 minutes from Canberra.
  • Cycling - You can cycle to Bungendore from Canberra, using the cycle path through the Molonglo River Corridor. It takes about 2 hours and offers stunning views of the river and hills.

How to Get Around in Bungendore

Bungendore is a small town, and most of the attractions and amenities are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas or have limited mobility, here are some transportation options:

  • Driving - You can rent a car or drive your own vehicle to get around Bungendore and the nearby towns. Make sure to follow the traffic rules and speed limits.
  • Cycling - Bungendore has a few cycling routes and trails, which you can explore at your own pace.
  • Taxi - There are a few taxi companies in Bungendore, which you can book in advance or flag down on the street.

Where to Go Next from Bungendore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bungendore is a great base to explore the Southern Highlands and the nearby towns and cities. Here are some of the top destinations and excursions from Bungendore:

  • Canberra - The capital city of Australia, located about 30km west of Bungendore. You can visit the Parliament House, Australian War Memorial, National Gallery of Australia, and more.
  • Murrumbateman - This peaceful and scenic town, located about 30km north of Bungendore, is known for its wineries and vineyards. You can taste the local wines, visit the art galleries, and enjoy the picturesque landscapes.
  • Batemans Bay - This coastal town, located about 130km east of Bungendore, is a popular spot for swimming, surfing, fishing, and boating. You can also visit the Clyde River National Park and the Mogo Zoo.

Bungendore, with its unique and diverse attractions, offers a great escape from the city and a chance to experience the best of Australian culture, nature, and hospitality. Whether you’re interested in history, art, nature, food, or adventure, Bungendore has something to offer.
