Marulan, a small town situated in Southern Highlands of New South Wales, Australia, is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. This travel guide provides you with all the information you need to plan your visit to Marulan. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, hiking and biking routes to food and drink scene, we’ve covered it all.

Introduction to Marulan

Marulan is a small town located in the Southern Highlands of New South Wales. As you explore this sleepy town, you’ll discover its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Though Marulan is a small town, it offers a wide range of attractions and activities that can keep you engaged for days.

List of Places to Visit in Marulan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marulan Museum - This museum unveils Marulan’s intriguing past with its extensive collection of artifacts and photographs.
  • The Big Merino - This iconic sheep-shaped landmark is a representation of the town’s agricultural heritage.
  • Goulburn Rail Heritage Centre - Located just a short drive from Marulan, this heritage site is home to some of Australia’s oldest steam trains.

Natural Attractions of Marulan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Towrang Valley Lookout - A picturesque site that offers a panoramic view of the beautiful Towrang Valley.
  • Bungonia National Park - Popular among adventure seekers, this national park has several hiking trails, rock climbing opportunities, and stunning views.
  • Wombeyan Caves - This natural wonder, situated in Wombeyan Karst Conservation Reserve, is a network of 40 caves that showcase spectacular geological formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marulan

  • Throsby Trail - A 5.5-kilometer trail that takes you through natural bushland and historical sites.
  • Box Vale Track - A moderate 10-kilometer hike that takes you through a former railway tunnel and lush green vegetation.
  • Fitzroy Falls to Kangaroo Valley - A challenging 24-kilometer trek that rewards you with breathtaking views of Kangaroo Valley and Fitzroy Falls.

Best Things to Do in Marulan: Activities and Tours

  • 4WD Adventure Tours - Explore Marulan’s rugged terrain in a 4WD vehicle.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking - Discover the tranquil waters of Shoalhaven River and Lake Burley Griffin.
  • Marulan Horse Riding Centre - Saddle up and enjoy a peaceful ride through the picturesque countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marulan

Day 1 - Marulan Museum and The Big Merino Day 2 - Bungonia National Park Day 3 - Towrang Valley Lookout and Fitzroy Falls Day 4 - Wombeyan Caves Day 5 - Canoeing and Kayaking Day 6 - Marulan Horse Riding Centre Day 7 - 4WD Adventure Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Marulan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mushroom Burger - Enjoy a delicious vegetarian burger made with locally grown mushrooms.
  • Beef Pie - This meat pie is a classic Australian dish that you can find in most cafés and bakeries.
  • Lamb Shank - A hearty lamb dish that is perfect for chilly nights.

Local Beverages

  • Southern Highlands Wines - Marulan is located in the Southern Highlands, which is known for its world-class wineries.
  • Bundanoon Beer - A craft beer made with locally sourced ingredients from the nearby town of Bundanoon.
  • Café Latte - Marulan has several cozy cafes that serve up delicious coffee.

Where to Stay in Marulan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Goulburn South Caravan Park - A family-friendly park that offers caravan and camping accommodations.
  • Taylors Motel - A budget-friendly motel that is conveniently located near Marulan’s main attractions.
  • The Old Bank Boutique Hotel - A charming hotel that provides a luxury stay experience.

What to Buy in Marulan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Southern Highlands Wine - Marulan is located in the heart of Southern Highlands, which produces some of Australia’s best wines.
  • Merino Wool - Merino wool products are a perfect keepsake or gift for your loved ones.
  • Handmade Leather Goods - Discover unique leather accessories made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Marulan

Marulan is a small town that values community and tradition. The locals are warm and friendly, and they take great pride in their town’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Marulan

The best time to visit Marulan is during the spring (September - November) and fall (March - May) months. The weather is mild, and you can witness the blooming flowers and lush greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marulan

Do:

  • Do respect the town’s natural surroundings.
  • Do take the time to learn about Marulan’s history and heritage.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and a hat during the summer months.
  • Don’t forget to book your accommodations in advance during the holiday season.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marulan

Marulan is a safe town, but it is always recommended to take basic safety precautions. Pay attention to your surroundings and avoid roaming around deserted areas at night.

Health in Marulan

Marulan has several medical centers and pharmacies that can provide basic healthcare needs. If you require emergency medical assistance, call 000 (Australia’s emergency number) or head to the nearest hospital.

Getting to Marulan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marulan

The nearest airports from Marulan are the Canberra International Airport (90 km) and the Sydney International Airport (160 km).

Bus Stations Near Marulan

There are several bus services available that operate between Marulan and other major cities in New South Wales, including Greyhound Australia and Premier Motor Service.

Other Options

If you prefer a more personalized experience, you can hire a car or a taxi service to get to Marulan.

How to Get Around in Marulan

Marulan is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or bicycle. If you prefer to drive, car rentals are available in the town as well.

Where to Go Next from Marulan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Goulburn - Located just 22 km from Marulan, Goulburn is known for its rich colonial architecture and historic landmarks.
  • Kangaroo Valley - A scenic town situated in Shoalhaven region that is known for its bushwalking trails and outdoor activities.
  • Wollongong - A coastal city located 95 km from Marulan that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, art, and culture.

Conclusion

Marulan may be a small town, but it has a lot to offer. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, hiking and biking routes to food and drink scene, Marulan is a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation. We hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your visit to Marulan. Happy travels!
