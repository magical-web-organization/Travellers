Bungonia National Park is a rugged and diverse natural wonder located in the Southern Highlands of New South Wales, Australia. The park is well-known for its stunning natural beauty and offers visitors a wide variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, rock climbing, and caving, to name a few.

Introduction to Bungonia National Park

Bungonia National Park encompasses over 15,000 hectares of stunning landscapes filled with ancient gorges, waterfalls, and wildlife. It is the perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and anyone who enjoys spending time in the great outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Bungonia National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bungonia National Park is rich in cultural significance and is home to several museums and historical sites that offer visitors an insight into the park’s rich history and culture. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Bungonia Settlement School: This heritage-listed site dates back to the early 1900s and tells the story of the early settlers in the area.
  • Palerang Heritage Trail: This trail follows the old coach route from Braidwood to Goulburn and passes through several heritage-listed sites, including Bungonia Courthouse and Police Station.
  • Yerranderie Heritage Village: This unique village offers visitors a glimpse into what life was like in a remote Australian mining town in the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Bungonia National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bungonia National Park is renowned for its natural beauty and offers visitors a wealth of outdoor activities to explore. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Bungonia Lookdown: This stunning lookout offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and is a popular spot for birdwatching and photography.
  • Slot Canyon: This narrow canyon is a popular destination for experienced rock climbers and offers a unique and challenging climbing experience.
  • Bungonia Gorge: One of the park’s most popular attractions, the Bungonia Gorge offers visitors the chance to explore the park’s stunning natural beauty up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bungonia National Park

Bungonia National Park is home to several walking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the park’s stunning natural beauty and wildlife. Here are some of the best hiking routes to try:

  • Green Track: This easy, 2km walking trail takes visitors through the park’s lush forest and offers a great opportunity for wildlife spotting.
  • Bungonia Creek Track: This 9km walking trail follows the Bungonia Creek and takes visitors through some of the park’s most stunning natural landscapes, including the Bungonia Gorge.
  • Lookdown to Jerrara Creek: This challenging 11km hiking trail offers breathtaking views of the park’s stunning natural landscapes and requires a moderate level of fitness to complete.

Best Things to Do in Bungonia National Park: Activities and Tours

Bungonia National Park offers visitors a wide range of activities to explore and enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in the park:

  • Rock Climbing: Bungonia National Park is a popular spot for rock climbing, with several challenging and unique routes to explore.
  • Caving: The park is home to several limestone caves that offer visitors the chance to explore the park’s underground world.
  • Wildlife Spotting: The park is home to several rare and endangered species of wildlife, including wombats and brush-tailed rock wallabies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bungonia National Park

Here is a suggested itinerary for visitors looking to spend a week exploring Bungonia National Park:

  • Day 1: Arrival and orientation
  • Day 2: Explore the Bungonia Gorge and take a guided tour of the limestone caves.
  • Day 3: Hike the Bungonia Creek Track or join a rock climbing tour.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Yerranderie Heritage Village.
  • Day 5: Relax and enjoy the park’s stunning scenery.
  • Day 6: Visit the Bungonia Settlement School and explore the Palerang Heritage Trail.
  • Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Bungonia National Park

Bungonia National Park offers a wide variety of dining and drinking options to suit all tastes and preferences.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kangaroo: A local delicacy cooked in a variety of styles, including grilling and barbequing.
  • Seafood: Fresh, locally sourced seafood is a popular choice in Bungonia National Park, with dishes such as barramundi and prawns being a popular choice.
  • Australian Wine: Bungonia National Park is home to several world-famous wineries, and visitors are encouraged to try the local wines.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Australian beer is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike, with several local breweries located in the area.
  • Coffee: Australia is renowned for its coffee culture, and Bungonia National Park is no exception.

Where to Stay in Bungonia National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bungonia National Park offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Bungonia Gorge Campground: This popular campground offers visitors the chance to stay in the heart of the park and is suitable for both tents and caravans.
  • Bungonia House: This historic guesthouse is located just outside the park’s entrance and offers visitors a comfortable and luxurious stay.
  • Bungonia Lodge: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and offers a range of private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in Bungonia National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bungonia National Park offers a wide variety of souvenirs and local products for visitors to bring home, including:

  • Local Artwork: Bungonia National Park is home to several talented local artists, and visitors can purchase unique and beautiful artwork from the park’s galleries and studios.
  • Local Wines: Bungonia National Park is home to several world-famous wineries and vineyards, and visitors can bring home a bottle of their favorite wine as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Bungonia National Park

Bungonia National Park is home to a rich and diverse culture, with several indigenous communities residing in the area. Visitors are encouraged to respect the park’s cultural heritage and customs and to learn more about the area’s rich history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Bungonia National Park

The best time to visit Bungonia National Park depends on your preferences and the activities you plan to participate in. The park is open year-round, but the best time to visit for hiking and outdoor activities is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bungonia National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s cultural heritage and customs.
  • Follow the park’s safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Take plenty of water and sunscreen when hiking and participate in activities only if you’re physically fit for them.

Don’t:

Getting to Bungonia National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bungonia National Park

The closest airports to Bungonia National Park are Canberra International Airport and Sydney Kingsford Smith International Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus or drive to the park.

Bus Stations Near Bungonia National Park

Several bus stations are located near Bungonia National Park, including Goulburn and Canberra Bus Stations.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to the park, with several highways and main roads leading to the park’s entrance.

How to Get Around in Bungonia National Park

Visitors can explore Bungonia National Park on foot, by bike or by car. It’s essential to follow the park’s regulations and guidelines when driving through the park and to take necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities.
