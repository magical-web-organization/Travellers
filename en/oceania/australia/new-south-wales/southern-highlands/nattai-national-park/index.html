Introduction to Nattai National Park

Tucked in the Southern Highlands of New South Wales, Nattai National Park offers a serene escape into nature. Sprawling over 53,000 hectares, the park is home to a plethora of diverse flora and fauna, providing an excellent opportunity for visitors to immerse themselves in the raw wilderness setting. The park is characterized by steep gorges, clifftops and valleys, and is a famous destination to satisfy the cravings of adventure-thrillers and nature enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Nattai National Park

The park offers a wide array of attractions, ranging from culturally significant museums and historical places to natural wonders, hiking/biking trails, and much more.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Thirlmere Museum of Railway and Transport: Dedicated to preserving the heritage of New South Wales’ rail network, the Thirlmere Museum of Railway and Transport hosts a vast collection of trains, carriages, and related equipment.

  2. Nattai Trig Station: A perfect spot for visitors to catch breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and the park’s rugged terrain.

Natural Attractions of Nattai National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Alexandra Reserve: The park’s most beloved destination provides visitors with an unspoiled haven for swimming, fishing, paddle-boating, and picnicking. The Reserve’s scenic walking trails offer a chance to connect with nature and escape the fast-paced city life.

  2. Wollondilly River Walkway: Perfect for hikers, Wollondilly River Walkway, runs from The Gorge to Thirlmere, along the tranquil river and through the picturesque state forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nattai National Park

Nattai National Park has a vast network of hiking and biking trails, catering to all skill levels. Some of the popular trails include;

  1. Mermaid Pools Walk: This 7km trail loops across the Nattai River and amongst tall forests, ending at a beautiful set of cascading rock pools.

  2. Couridjah Corridor Walk: This 20km trail runs parallel to the Thirlmere Lakes, providing stunning views of lakes and creeks along with an opportunity to spot various wildlife, including swamp wallabies and emus.

Best Things to Do in Nattai National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Camping at The Gorge: Nattai National Park’s The Gorge offers several campsites, surrounded by valleys, and amidst a beautiful natural setting.

  2. Picnic at Lake Alexandra Reserve: Enjoy a relaxing picnic amidst the unspoiled scenery at Lake Alexandra Reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nattai National Park

Here’s a detailed itinerary for a fun-filled and adventurous week exploring Nattai National Park:

Day 1-2: Arrive in Nattai National Park, set up camp, and explore the nearby surroundings, including The Gorge, The Thirlmere Museum of Railway and Transport.

Day 3-4: Take a leisurely walk to Lake Alexandra Reserve, enjoy fishing, swimming and relaxing picnic amidst the scenic surroundings.

Day 5-6: Go for a long hike or a bike ride along the Couridjah Corridor Walk.

Local Culture and Customs of Nattai National Park

The Southern Highlands is rich in Aboriginal culture and heritage, so visitors are encouraged to learn and respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Nattai National Park

The best time to visit Nattai National Park is during the autumn months (March to May) when the weather is mild, and the park is at its verdant best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nattai National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the local wildlife and ecology.
  2. Carry enough water and food.
  3. Respect the environment and follow the Leave No Trace policy.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t disturb the wildlife.
  2. Don’t litter in the park.
  3. Don’t damage trees, plants, or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nattai National Park

  1. Stay on designated trails and paths.
  2. Check for fire bans and warnings and adhere to them.
  3. Be wary of snake encounters during warmer months.

Health in Nattai National Park

  1. Carry enough water and stay hydrated.
  2. Wear proper clothing, including sunscreen and hats to avoid sunburn.
  3. Be mindful of ticks, wear long-sleeved clothing and check yourself before returning.
