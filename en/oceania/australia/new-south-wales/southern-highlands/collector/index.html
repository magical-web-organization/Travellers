Introduction to Collector

Located in the Southern Highlands region of New South Wales, Collector is a charming town with a rich history and stunning natural scenery. The town is known for its cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drink scene. This guide will take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Collector.

List of Places to Visit in Collector

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bushranger Hotel - This historic pub was built in 1860 and is the perfect spot for a cold beer and a delicious meal. The walls of the Bushranger Hotel are covered in memorabilia from the town’s rich history.
  • Collector Heritage Walk - Take a stroll through town and discover the rich history of Collector. The walk takes you to several important heritage buildings and sites, including the historic railway station and the old courthouse.
  • Bushranger Gallery - This art gallery features the works of local artists and is located in the old police station building.

Natural Attractions of Collector: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake George - This stunning natural wonder is located just a few kilometers from Collector. The lake is home to a variety of bird species and is a popular spot for fishing and boating.
  • Wollondilly River - This beautiful river runs through the Southern Highlands region and is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.
  • Mount Ainslie Lookout - Take in stunning views of the surrounding countryside from the top of Mount Ainslie.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Collector

  • Collector to Goulburn Rail Trail - This 27-kilometer trail takes you through beautiful countryside and historic towns. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking.
  • Collector to Lake George - This 12-kilometer trail takes you to the stunning Lake George, with beautiful views along the way.
  • Oolong Sanctuary Loop - This 14-kilometer loop takes you through the Oolong Sanctuary, a beautiful natural area home to a variety of bird species.

Best Things to Do in Collector: Activities and Tours

  • Collector Village Pumpkin Festival - This annual festival celebrates the harvest season and features pumpkin-themed food, drinks, and activities.
  • Collector Hotel Ghost Tour - Take a spooky tour of the historic Collector Hotel and learn about the ghostly residents who still haunt the building.
  • Collector Distillery Tour - Take a tour of the local distillery and sample their delicious spirits.
  • Collector Country Music Festival - This annual festival celebrates country music and features live performances from local and national musicians.

Week-Long Itinerary for Collector

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town and its heritage sites on the Collector Heritage Walk.
Day 2 Take a hike or bike ride on the Collector to Goulburn Rail Trail.
Day 3 Visit the Bushranger Gallery and enjoy lunch at the historic Bushranger Hotel.
Day 4 Take a distillery tour at the local distillery and enjoy a picnic at Wollondilly River.
Day 5 Go fishing or boating at Lake George.

Day 7 Attend the Collector Village Pumpkin Festival and enjoy pumpkin-themed food and activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Collector

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pumpkin Scones - These delicious scones are a local specialty and are often served with clotted cream and jam.
  • Bushranger Burger - This hearty burger is made with locally sourced beef and is topped with cheese, bacon, and a fried egg.
  • Blackberry Pie - This classic Australian dessert is made with locally grown blackberries and a buttery crust.

Local Beverages

  • Collector Distillery Spirits - Sample the local spirits at the distillery, including vodka, gin, and whiskey.
  • Southern Highlands Wines - The Southern Highlands region is known for its cool climate wines, including Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Sauvignon Blanc.

Where to Stay in Collector: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Collector Cosy Corner - This charming guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a great location in the heart of town.
  • Collector Hill Cottage - This cozy cottage is located just outside of town and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Collector Hotel - This historic hotel offers comfortable rooms and a lively pub atmosphere.

What to Buy in Collector: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Collector Distillery Spirits - Bring home a bottle of the local spirits as a delicious souvenir.
  • Local Artwork and Crafts - The Bushranger Gallery and local markets offer a variety of beautiful artwork and crafts made by local artists and craftspeople.

Local Culture and Customs of Collector

Collector is a small town with a strong sense of community. Visitors are welcome to explore the town and its heritage sites, but should be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Collector

The best time to visit Collector is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the countryside is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Collector

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take a hike or bike ride on one of the spectacular trails.
  • Enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local heritage sites.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Collector

Collector is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should still take normal precautions, such as locking their cars and valuables when parking.

Health in Collector

There are no major health risks in Collector. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated during the warmer months.

Getting to Collector: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Collector

The nearest airport to Collector is the Canberra International Airport, located 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Collector

The closest bus station to Collector is in the town of Goulburn, 30 kilometers away.

Other Options

The best way to get to Collector is by car. The town is located just off the Hume Highway and is easily accessible from Sydney and Canberra.

How to Get Around in Collector

The town of Collector is small enough to explore on foot. Visitors will need a car or bike to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Collector: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Canberra - Explore Australia’s capital city, located just over an hour’s drive from Collector.

Conclusion
