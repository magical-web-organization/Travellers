Introduction to Gundaroo

Nestled in the heart of Southern Highlands and surrounded by rolling hills, Gundaroo is a charming town known for its historic sites, wine trails, and outdoor adventures. The town’s name translates to “green place” in the local Ngunnawal language, which is evident by the lush countryside scenery. Whether you want to explore museums and galleries, hike through scenic valleys, or sample artisanal food and wine, Gundaroo has something for everyone. Read on to discover the top places to visit, activities, local customs, and helpful travel tips for your Gundaroo itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Gundaroo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gundaroo has preserved its rich history and heritage through various museums and historical sites. Visit the village’s most prominent landmark, The Royal Hotel, which has stood tall for over 150 years. The impeccably restored pub has beautiful verandas, a cosy nook fireplace, and excellent pub food. You can also visit the historic St. Luke’s Anglican Church, which dates back to 1860 and features classic Gothic Revival architecture. The historic Gundaroo courthouse is another must-see landmark, which is now operational as a museum depicting the history of the region and court system.

Natural Attractions of Gundaroo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, then Gundaroo is the perfect place to explore the great outdoors. The spectacular hills and valleys of Blundell’s Creek offer stunning hiking and biking opportunities. You can also explore the nearby Mulligans Flat Woodland Sanctuary, a wildlife haven and bird-enthusiast paradise with a wide variety of habitats and species to see. Alternatively, for those seeking adventure, hop on a hot air balloon to experience the breathtaking views of Gundaroo.

Hiking/Biking Route of Gundaroo

The region around Gundaroo has several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking scenery. For beginners, the Blundell’s Creek Trail is an excellent option that runs through the village and countryside and is perfect for on-foot and cycling exploration. For experienced hikers, the Uloola Falls loop track offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Gundaroo: Activities and Tours

Gundaroo is an all-in-one destination, and it offers various activities depending on your interests. Explore the Wines of the Canberra District, starting with the exceptional cellar doors and wineries of Gundaroo and its surroundings. Alternatively, visit the local farm gates to discover the best of the Southern Highlands food, from cheese and chocolate to olives and apples. For a truly immersive experience, take a guided tour of Gundaroo and gain insight into the rich history and culture of the village.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gundaroo

Gundaroo is best explored on foot, taking leisurely strolls through the beautiful countryside. Starting with the historic structure of The Royal Hotel, take a tour through the courthouse museum to understand the local history. Take a mesmerizing morning hot air balloon ride with extra treats of champagne, and finishing off by exploring the local wine and cattle farms, cheese factories, and local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Gundaroo

Must-Try Dishes

Gundaroo’s gastro scene offers a wide range of culinary delights, which can be enjoyed in local restaurants and cafes. Be sure to try the Gundaroo Griller at The Royal Hotel, a beefburger with locally made onion jam, mustard mayo, and crispy bacon. Delight your senses with the irresistible pizzas of ‘Crisps Cob Bakery,’ filled with fresh local ingredients. The hot smoked rainbow trout at the Gundaroo Tiller is also a must-try dish.

Local Beverages

When in Gundaroo, sip on the Southern Highland’s finest wine while taking in the enchanting countryside. The local wineries offer an incredible range of flavours, from full-bodied Shiraz to fruity Chardonnay, and various other locally made beverages.

Where to Stay in Gundaroo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite being a small town, Gundaroo has much to offer in terms of accommodation options. The Royal Hotel is a charming and comfortable hotel built-in 1855 and is undoubtedly the town’s gem. There are also guesthouses like Creek House and Gold Creek Station, which offer a range of amenities and a taste of rural life. Alternatively, if you are on a budget, there are hostels and caravan parks to choose from.

What to Buy in Gundaroo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gundaroo is an excellent place to pick up some unique souvenirs. From cheese, salami, and cured meats to local truffles and various other items, there is an abundant collection of delights to bring home with you. If you have a sweet tooth, then be sure to try the local honey, jams, and chocolates that are unique to Gundaroo. Some of the town’s non-food-related souvenirs include pottery, paintings, and souvenirs made from wool, such as jumpers and scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Gundaroo

The Gundaroo region has a rich and diverse culture, with an active population of local landowners and farmers. The locals are welcoming, friendly, and always eager to share their stories. The community is passionate about preserving their cultural heritage, as evidenced by the various cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year. Gundaroo is also a proud indigenous land where visitors can learn about the unique customs and rituals of the Ngunnawal people.

Best Time to Visit Gundaroo

Gundaroo’s climate is suitable for outdoor adventures throughout the year. The best time to visit depends on your preference, with spring and autumn being particularly delightful. Spring is the perfect time to enjoy the wildflowers, while the vibrant autumn colours make for beautiful scenery. The summer months are ideal for warm-weather outdoor activities, while winter is perfect for snuggling under warm blankets by the fireplace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gundaroo

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Try the local food and drinks, and don’t forget to take some home as souvenirs.
  • Engage with locals and learn about their culture and customs.
  • Go for a hot air balloon ride to experience the beauty of Gundaroo from above.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the cultural sensitivity of the local indigenous people.
  • Engage in any aggressive activities with the village wildlife.
  • Disrespect the natural environment of the village.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gundaroo

Gundaroo is a relatively safe destination with friendly locals. However, visitors should still take standard precautions, such as locking up valuables and keeping an eye on your surroundings. Take care when driving around the town, as wildlife such as kangaroos and wallabies are known to cross the roads at night

Health in Gundaroo

There is no major health concern related to Gundaroo. But visitors should take care during the summer as it can get hot, and visitors are advised to stay hydrated.

Getting to Gundaroo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gundaroo

The closest airport to Gundaroo is Canberra Airport, which is around 40 minutes from the town centre. From there, visitors can hire a car, take a taxi or use a shuttle service to get to Gundaroo.

Bus Stations Near Gundaroo

The closest bus stations are at Yass and Murrumbateman. From there, visitors can hire private transport to Gundaroo.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Gundaroo from Sydney, which takes around three and a half hours. Car rental is also available at Canberra Airport.

How to Get Around in Gundaroo

Getting around in Gundaroo is relatively easy, as the town is small, and most attractions are within walking distance. Driving is also a good option as it allows visitors to explore the surrounding countryside and nearby towns. Visitors can also rent bikes or use the regular taxi service available in Gundaroo.

Where to Go Next from Gundaroo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gundaroo is an excellent springboard to explore the surrounding areas of the Southern Highlands. Visitors can plan a day trip to Port Phillip Estate or Lake George, depending on their interests. The nearby town of Yass is also not to be missed, with its charming architecture and unique museums.

Conclusion

Gundaroo is a hidden gem in the Southern Highlands, offering a delightful range of culturally significant landmarks, natural wonders, food, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a food lover, an outdoor enthusiast, or a history buff, Gundaroo is sure to captivate you. Make sure to plan your trip to take in the unique experiences of Gundaroo, which all travellers will remember for years to come.
