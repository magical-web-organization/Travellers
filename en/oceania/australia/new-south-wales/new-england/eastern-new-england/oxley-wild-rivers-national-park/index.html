Introduction to Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Nestled in the eastern part of New England, in the New South Wales state of Australia, lies the majestic Oxley Wild Rivers National Park. This park, covering 211,213 hectares, is home to amazing flora, fauna, and geological features. Two of the country’s wild rivers, the Apsley River and the Macleay River, flow through this scenic park.

List of Places to Visit in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wollomombi Falls

Wollomombi Falls, located in the eastern section of the park, is the highest waterfall in New South Wales, plunging 220 meters down into a gorge. It is one of the park’s most popular attractions.

Cathedral Rock

Cathedral Rock is a beautiful granite rock formation that’s shaped like a cathedral. It’s located in the northern section of the park and offers visitors a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Oxley Wild Rivers National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dangars Gorge

Dangars Gorge is a rugged and spectacular gorge in the heart of Oxley Wild Rivers National Park. Visitors can explore its depths via the Dangarsleigh Walking Track or simply take in the views from the lookout.

Apsley Falls

Apsley Falls is a series of cascading waterfalls located in the eastern section of the park. It’s a great spot for swimming, fishing, and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

The Green Gully Track

The Green Gully Track is a moderate hike that starts at the Green Gully Camping Area and follows the Apsley River through lush rainforest and past cascading waterfalls.

The Chandler River Walking Track

The Chandler River Walking Track is a 9-kilometer hike that follows the Chandler River through pristine wilderness. Hikers will pass by towering eucalyptus forests, rugged gorges, and stunning waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park: Activities and Tours

Wildlife Spotting

Oxley Wild Rivers National Park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including echidnas, kangaroos, and wallabies. Visitors can take a guided tour with a local expert to maximize their chances of spotting these fascinating creatures.

Canoeing

Visitors can rent a canoe and paddle along the Macleay River, taking in the scenic views and spotting wildlife along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Day 1: Explore the eastern section of the park, including Wollomombi Falls and Apsley Falls.

Day 2: Hike the Chandler River Walking Track and enjoy a picnic lunch in the wilderness.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the park to spot some of the area’s unique wildlife.

Day 4: Go canoeing on the Macleay River.

Day 5: Explore the northern section of the park, including Cathedral Rock.

Day 6: Hike the Green Gully Track and enjoy a picnic lunch next to the Apsley River.

Day 7: Relax at the park’s picnic and BBQ areas, or head to a nearby town for some sightseeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Oxley Wild Rivers National Park, you must try the traditional Australian meat pie. These savory pies are filled with beef and gravy and are perfect for a picnic lunch.

Local Beverages

For a refreshing drink, try the local Australian beer. You can find a variety of Australian beers in most local pubs or bars.

Where to Stay in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Oxley Wild Rivers National Park offers a variety of accommodations, from rustic cabins to camping sites. Visitors can also stay in nearby towns, such as Armidale or Kempsey.

What to Buy in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Oxley Wild Rivers National Park, be sure to bring home some Australian honey. It’s known for its high quality and unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Oxley Wild Rivers National Park has a rich Aboriginal culture and visitors can learn about the area’s indigenous history by taking a guided tour or visiting one of the park’s cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

The best time to visit Oxley Wild Rivers National Park is from September to November when the weather is mild, and the park’s flora is in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural environment.
  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  • Bring your own supplies such as food, drinks, and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Take any plants or animals out of the park.
  • Leave any garbage or litter behind.
  • Start any fires in the park without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Visitors should be aware of the park’s natural hazards, such as loose rocks and steep cliffs. It’s important to stay on designated trails, and visitors should avoid hiking alone.

Health in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

Visitors should bring plenty of water and snacks while exploring the park, and also remember to pack sunscreen and insect repellent.

Getting to Oxley Wild Rivers National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

The nearest airports are Armidale and Port Macquarie airports. From the airport, visitors can rent a car and drive to the park.

Bus Stations Near Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

There are no bus stations directly in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park, but visitors can take a bus to Armidale or Kempsey and then rent a car to drive to the park.

How to Get Around in Oxley Wild Rivers National Park

The best way to get around Oxley Wild Rivers National Park is by car. Visitors can rent a car at a nearby airport or in one of the nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Oxley Wild Rivers National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Oxley Wild Rivers National Park, visitors can head to the nearby towns of Armidale or Kempsey to experience more of the local culture and see the sights.

Conclusion

Oxley Wild Rivers National Park offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience Australia’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re exploring the park’s scenic trails or learning about its indigenous history, there’s something for everyone in this amazing part of New South Wales.
