Introduction to Gunnedah

Get ready to experience the beauty and culture of Gunnedah, a charming town located in Southern New England, in New South Wales, Australia. Known for its agricultural heritage, this friendly community boasts a fascinating history, rich arts and culture scene, and incredible outdoor adventures. Surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, enjoy a diverse range of activities, from hiking and biking, to fishing and bird-watching. Dotted with heritage-listed sites, museums and galleries, locals are keen to share the stories and traditions of the region with visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Gunnedah

Whether you’re looking for a laid-back weekend getaway or an action-packed adventure, Gunnedah has something to suit every appetite. Here we’ve put together a list of highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors interested in the region’s rich history will have a plethora of places to explore during their stay in Gunnedah. One of the town’s most popular attractions is the Gunnedah Rural Museum. This outdoor museum is a history lover’s dream, showcasing a range of exhibits that explore the region’s past. From agriculture to transport, learn about the challenges and triumphs faced by pioneers in Southern New England.

For those wanting to learn more about the Indigenous culture of the region, be sure to check out the Anaiwan Culture Centre. Visitors can experience the unique customs and traditions of the Indigenous Australians that called this area home for thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Gunnedah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Home to the stunning natural landscapes of the Liverpool Ranges and the Namoi River, an array of outdoor adventures await in Gunnedah. Take a stroll along the beautiful Namoi River Walk or venture into the nearby Mount Kaputar National Park to soak up breathtaking views and marvel at ancient rock formations. Other outdoor activities include fishing, bird-watching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gunnedah

For those seeking adventure, head to the nearby Mount Kaputar National Park to explore the hiking and biking trails. Take a stroll along the easy 1km scenic walk or hike the challenging 12km Yulludunida Track.

Locals recommend taking on the challenge of the Oxley walking track, an eight-kilometre trail that offers unrivalled views of the Namoi Valley and beyond.

Best Things to Do in Gunnedah: Activities and Tours

Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveller, there are plenty of things to do in Gunnedah. Take a guided tour of the heritage-listed town centre, explore the natural landscapes by kayak, or simply relax poolside at one of the town’s many motels. Visit the ceramic murals, located on the outside of the Gunnedah Visitor Centre. This beautiful, mural project features the regions rich history, culture, and natural environment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gunnedah

Looking to spend a week exploring Gunnedah? Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Gunnedah, spend the afternoon on a guided tour of the town.

Day 2: Take a hike in Mount Kaputar National Park and enjoy panoramic views of the region.

Day 3: Discover the heritage and culture of the area with a visit to Gunnedah Rural Museum and Anaiwan Culture Centre.

Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Gunnedah on the scenic Namoi River Walk.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of nearby farms to learn about regional agriculture.

Day 6: Relax by the pool at one of the town’s many motels or visit the local galleries.

Day 7: Attend one of the town’s regular events or festivals and experience its rich cultural scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Gunnedah

When it comes to dining options, Gunnedah again offers a diversity of choices. For authentic Australian cuisine, visit the Railway Hotel and grab a “beef and barra”: a delightful dish of beef, barramundi fish, and chips. Alternatively, try delicious Chinese or Thai cuisine at Twin Cities Chinese Restaurant or Gunnedah Thai.

Where to Stay in Gunnedah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

No matter what your preference and budget, there is suitable accommodation in Gunnedah for every visitor. From luxurious hotels to budget-welcoming guesthouses, the town offers something for everyone.

For those seeking luxurious options, look to Stay at the Comfort Inn Harvest Lodge, or for backpackers Moonlight Lodge could be a better option.

What to Buy in Gunnedah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and gifts for your friends and family? Gunnedah offers an array of local products, including artisanal jams, honey, and candies.

The town’s main street is filled with retail shops, from clothing and accessories to hand-made pottery and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Gunnedah

While laid-back and friendly, the people of Gunnedah hold firmly to their community’s customs and traditions. Visitors can learn about the unique lifestyle, history, and religious beliefs of the region’s Indigenous people through visits to sites like the Anaiwan Culture Centre.

Best Time to Visit Gunnedah

The best time to visit Gunnedah is during the autumn months of March to May or spring months of September to November - as the weather is still mild and the landscapes are at its greenest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gunnedah

Here are a few do’s and don’ts when visiting Gunnedah:

Do:

  • Respect the Indigenous culture and heritage of the region.
  • Spend time outdoors exploring the stunning natural landscapes in the region.
  • Support local businesses and buy unique souvenirs from Gunnedah.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards the locals or their customs.
  • Engage in disruptive behaviour or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Ignore the regulations and advice given by tour guides and locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gunnedah

Gunnedah is generally a safe place to visit, although visitors should take necessary precautions when hiking, fishing, and camping. Specialists suggest being mindful of the local wildlife, like snakes during hiking, and being safe when swimming in the river.

Health in Gunnedah

There is a hospital in Gunnedah, which can treat minor illnesses and injuries. However, if the situation is more critical, visitors should travel to one of the larger towns for assistance.

Getting to Gunnedah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gunnedah

There are two airports near Gunnedah: the Gunnedah Airport and Tamworth Airport, located about 80 km from the town. Visitors can take a flight from a major Australian city or hire a personal plane.

Bus Stations Near Gunnedah

To reach Gunnedah by bus, visitors can take a scheduled trip from regional towns and cities like Tamworth, Sydney and Moree.

Other Options

Hiring a rental car from the airport or bus station nearest to the town is a cost-effective means to reach and travel around Gunnedah.

How to Get Around in Gunnedah

Visitors to Gunnedah can utilise a local taxi service or hire a car for the duration of their stay. Walking and biking are also popular options for exploring the town centre.

Where to Go Next from Gunnedah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking for areas to visit after your time in Gunnedah? We suggest exploring Tamworth, the largest town in New England, or the equally beautiful, Mount Kaputar National Park.

Conclusion

With a rich history, an abundance of natural beauty and landscapes, as well as a friendly community, Gunnedah promises a trip filled with diverse activities and amazing experiences.
