Introduction to Willow Tree

Are you looking for a peaceful and picturesque getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life? Look no further than Willow Tree, a hidden gem tucked away in Southern New England. Located in New South Wales, Australia, Willow Tree is a small but charming town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or just relaxing amidst stunning landscapes, Willow Tree has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, the most exciting activities, the finest food and drink offerings, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Willow Tree

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to learning about Willow Tree’s rich history and culture, there are several must-visit attractions.

  • Willow Tree Inn - Established in 1847, the Willow Tree Inn is a historic hotel that has retained much of its colonial charm. It offers excellent dining, accommodation, and entertainment options.

  • St. Finbar’s Church - This beautiful church boasts stunning architecture and is an integral part of the town’s religious heritage.

  • Quirindi Rural Heritage Village - Located a short drive away from Willow Tree, this museum complex celebrates the pioneering spirit of the region. It features historic farming equipment, tractors, and preserved buildings.

  • Kamilaroi Heritage Centre - This cultural center honors the heritage of the Kamilaroi people, the traditional owners of the land. It offers a unique insight into Aboriginal culture through exhibits, workshops, and guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Willow Tree: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Willow Tree is situated amidst pristine natural beauty, offering countless opportunities for exploration, wildlife spotting, and outdoor adventures.

  • Pilliga Forest - One of the region’s crown jewels, the Pilliga Forest is a vast expanse of bushland that features walking trails, camping grounds, and an abundance of flora and fauna.

  • Mount Kaputar - This iconic peak boasts rugged landscapes, breathtaking views, and unparalleled hiking opportunities. Prepare for a challenging climb rewarded with mesmerizing vistas.

  • Lake Keepit - This stunning still-water lake is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and boating during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Willow Tree

Willow Tree is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts, featuring miles of scenic trails and challenging routes. Some of the most popular tracks include

  • Pilliga Forest Discovery Centre Trail - This trail leads you through the Pilliga Forest, offering wonderful glimpses of the local flora and fauna along the way.

  • Mount Kaputar Summit Trail - This hike is a must-do for adventure seekers, offering panoramic views of the surrounding peaks and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Willow Tree: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring outdoor attractions, here are some other things to do in Willow Tree:

  • Horseback riding - There are several equestrian centers in the area that offer horse riding tours through the stunning New England countryside.

  • Wine Tasting - Willow Tree is situated in the heart of New England’s wine country, with several vineyards offering wine tastings and cellar door sales.

  • Hot Air Ballooning - Experience the beauty of Willow Tree and its surroundings from a new height aboard a Hot Air Balloon, with scenic flights departing from nearby towns.

Week-Long Itinerary for Willow Tree

Planning a week-long tour of Willow Tree? Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Willow Tree

Must-Try Dishes

Willow Tree boasts an abundance of culinary delights, with several excellent restaurants serving up fresh and locally-sourced ingredients. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Lamb Roast - A hearty and delicious dish that showcases the local farm-raised Lamb

  • Emu Sausages - A traditional Australian snack that’s not to be missed!

  • Wine and Cheese - Take a short scenic drive to the nearby vineyards to sample New England’s finest wines with locally-made cheeses.

Local Beverages

New England is renowned for its wines, with Willow Tree being a notable hub for winemaking. In addition to the local wines, there are breweries and distilleries that offer a wide range of boutique beers and handcrafted liquors.

Where to Stay in Willow Tree: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Willow Tree has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, you’ll find something to suit your needs.

  • Willow Tree Inn - You can’t go past staying at the historic establishment which is the Willow Tree Inn.

  • Bed and Breakfast - The area features several quaint bed and breakfast accommodation, offering a homely stay with warm hospitality.

  • Camping grounds - If you’re looking for a more rugged outdoor experience, there are many options for camping throughout the region.

What to Buy in Willow Tree: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect memento to commemorate your visit to Willow Tree? Here are some ideas:

  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics - Delicate and unique, the handcrafted pottery and ceramics of Willow Tree are ideal for bringing home as souvenirs.

  • Wine and Cheese - Take home a bottle of wine and some local cheese to enjoy the flavors of New England, even after you’ve returned home.

Local Culture and Customs of Willow Tree

As a small regional town, Willow Tree offers visitors the opportunity to experience a genuine slice of rural Australian life. The locals are known for their friendliness, and warm hospitality and visitors should make an effort to respect their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Willow Tree

The best time to visit Willow Tree is between September to November when the weather is mild and the landscapes are at their most picturesque. The summer months of December to February can be hot and humid, making outdoor activities less enjoyable, while Trip to Willow Tree in Winter will be most enjoyable with snow all around and the Chimneys of Willow Tree Inn going on with it’s mild warmth from the fireplace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Willow Tree

Do:

  • Respect the environment and dispose of waste responsibly.

  • Engage with the local community and learn about their heritage and way of life.

  • Try the local delicacies and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Damage cultural or natural heritage sites.

  • Disrupt the peace by making excessive noise or littering.

  • Ignore safety protocols in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Willow Tree

Willow Tree’s crime rate is low, making it a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, particularly when venturing into remote areas.

Health in Willow Tree

There is a well-equipped hospital in nearby Quirindi, ensuring that medical care is never far away.

Getting to Willow Tree: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Willow Tree

The nearest major airport is Tamworth Regional Airport, located approximately an hour’s drive away from Willow Tree. Alternatively, there are flights available to Sydney’s Kingsford Smith Airport.

Bus Stations Near Willow Tree

There is a bus service available that stops at Willow Tree, with connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or take a taxi to access Willow Tree and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Willow Tree

Visitors can explore Willow Tree on foot, or for those who want to venture further afield, hiking and biking are excellent options. Car rentals are also widely available.

Where to Go Next from Willow Tree: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Willow Tree has plenty of attractions to keep visitors occupied, those looking to explore the surrounding area will find several nearby destinations worth a visit. These include:

  • Tamworth - Known as the ‘Country Music Capital of Australia,’ Tamworth offers an exciting mix of culture, history, and entertainment.

  • Armidale - This charming university town boasts a vibrant arts and culture scene, beautiful gardens, and stunning architecture.

  • Port Macquarie - A bustling coastal town known for its beaches, restaurants, and wildlife parks.
