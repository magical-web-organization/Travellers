Introduction to Moree

Located in Western New England, New South Wales, Australia, Moree is a small town known for its natural hot springs and agricultural industry. With a population of just over 8,000 people, Moree offers a peaceful respite for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors can expect warm hospitality from locals and a range of activities to choose from. This guide will serve as your go-to reference to explore all that Moree has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Moree

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, you won’t be disappointed with the range of attractions in Moree that offer a glimpse into Australia’s past.

  • Moree Plains Gallery: This contemporary art gallery showcases local and national artists, including exhibitions, workshops, and education programs.
  • Moree Plains Shire Council Museum: Open seven days a week, the museum features interactive displays that cover the history of the Moree Plains region from pre-colonisation to the present day.
  • Artesian Spa Baths: This is one of Moree’s most popular attractions, offering visitors a chance to relax and unwind in the natural hot springs.

Natural Attractions of Moree: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Moree is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Boolooroo Racecourse: This 1600m track boasts some of the best racing in the New England area.
  • Gwydir River: Fancy a picnic or a spot of fishing? Head to the banks of Gwydir River for a serene experience in nature.
  • Pallamallawa Lake: This picturesque lake is a great spot for bird-watching and taking in stunning views of Moree’s natural surrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moree

If you’re up for some physical activity, consider taking on these hiking and biking routes in Moree.

  • Mt Kaputar National Park: This park offers several hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult. Enjoy stunning views at the summit after a challenging hike.
  • Mehi River Trail: This 8km trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride, taking in the sights and sounds of the Moree countryside.

Best Things to Do in Moree: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions listed above, Moree also offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to experience.

  • Moree Hot Artesian Spa: Enjoy a relaxing soak in the natural hot springs, believed to have therapeutic benefits due to the minerals found in the water.
  • Horse Riding: Explore Moree’s stunning countryside on horseback, with several tour operators offering guided rides.
  • Tour of the Cotton Farming Industry: Moree is at the heart of the New South Wales cotton industry, and visitors can learn about the cotton farming process and its impact on the local economy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moree

Here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most out of your week-long stay in Moree.

Day Activity
1 Visit the Moree Plains Shire Council Museum and learn about the history of the region.
2 Take a dip at the Moree Hot Artesian Spa and enjoy the therapeutic benefits of the natural hot springs.
3 Go on a guided horseback ride to explore the countryside.
4 Hike the Mt Kaputar National Park and enjoy the stunning views.
5 Tour a cotton farm to learn about the local industry.
6 Visit Boolooroo Racecourse and take in some horse racing.

Food and Drink Scene of Moree

No trip is complete without exploring the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks in Moree.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Shank: This Australian favourite is a must-try in Moree, with several restaurants serving up this hearty meal.
  • Pavlova: This iconic dessert is a staple on most Australian menus, made with a crunchy meringue base and topped with fresh fruit and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Sarsaparilla: This traditional Australian soft drink is a unique taste sensation, with its sweet and spicy flavour.
  • Coopers Pale Ale: This brewery is popular across Australia and is best known for its pale ale.

Where to Stay in Moree: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options for visitors to choose from in Moree, catering to all budgets and preferences.

  • Spa Village Travel Inn: This 4-star hotel features a mineral spa and an outdoor swimming pool, making it an ideal choice for those looking to relax and unwind.
  • The Burke and Wills Motor Inn: This 3.5-star motel offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including an outdoor pool and a restaurant.
  • The Moree Hotel: If you’re looking for a more budget-friendly option, this pub-style hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Moree: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir from your trip? Here are some local products to bring home as a reminder of your time in Moree.

  • Cotton Products: From dresses to bed linen, Moree is known for its cotton industry, and there are several local stores that sell cotton products.
  • Aboriginal Art: Moree is a great place to buy authentic Aboriginal art, with several galleries showcasing local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Moree

Moree is a welcoming and friendly town, and visitors should respect the local culture and customs. It’s important to note that Australia has a high level of sun exposure, so make sure to protect yourself from the sun by wearing appropriate clothing, sunscreen, and a hat.

Best Time to Visit Moree

The best time to visit Moree is during the Australian autumn (March to May), when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner, allowing you to enjoy all that Moree has to offer without the crowds. If you’re planning to visit during the Australian summer (December to February), be mindful that it is the peak tourism season, and the temperatures can get quite hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moree

Here’s a list of Do’s and Don’ts to ensure a pleasant and respectful stay in Moree.

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be mindful of the sun exposure and protect yourself accordingly.
  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Forget to tip service providers if you receive good service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moree

Moree is a generally safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and take necessary precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Moree

Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive health insurance when visiting Moree and that they take appropriate measures to protect themselves from sun exposure and insect bites.

Getting to Moree: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moree has several transportation options that make it accessible to visitors from across the country and internationally.

Airports Near Moree

The Moree Airport is located just 5km south of the town centre and offers direct flights from Sydney, Narrabri, and Brisbane.

Bus Stations Near Moree

Greyhound Australia and New England Coaches provide bus services to Moree from major cities including Sydney and Brisbane.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to drive to Moree as it is located on the Newell Highway, which connects Brisbane to Melbourne.

How to Get Around in Moree

The town of Moree is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors can also hire a car or catch a taxi to explore the surrounding attractions.

Where to Go Next from Moree: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the New England region, consider adding these destinations to your itinerary.

  • Narrabri: This town is known for its natural attractions, including Sawn Rocks and Mount Kaputar National Park.
  • Tamworth: Known as the “Country Music Capital of Australia,” Tamworth hosts the Tamworth Country Music Festival every year in January.
  • Barraba: This small town is located in the heart of New England and offers a range of activities, including fishing, camping, and hiking.

Conclusion

Moree is a hidden gem in New South Wales, offering visitors a chance to immerse themselves in Australia’s natural beauty and rich culture. This guide has covered the best places to visit, eat, and stay, ensuring that you have an unforgettable experience in Moree.
