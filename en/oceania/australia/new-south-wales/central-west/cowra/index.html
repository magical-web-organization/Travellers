Introduction to Cowra

Cowra, situated in the Central West of New South Wales, is a charming agricultural town that offers visitors an opportunity to experience Australian rural life. Surrounded by rolling hills, vineyards, and historic attractions, Cowra is a perfect destination for anyone who wants to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy some peace and quiet. The town may be small, but it’s not without things to do and see.

List of Places to Visit in Cowra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cowra Japanese Garden and Cultural Centre

The Cowra Japanese Garden and Cultural Centre is an authentic Japanese garden built to commemorate the Japanese lives lost in a prisoner of war breakout in 1944. The garden features traditional Japanese architecture, a koi pond, and stunning seasonal flora. There’s also a cultural center where you can learn about the garden’s history.

Cowra Prisoner of War Camp

Cowra is home to one of the most notorious POW Camps in Australia. The Cowra Prisoner of War Camp was constructed in 1941 and housed 4,000 Japanese prisoners of war during World War II. You can visit the site and learn about its history in the Cowra Visitors Centre.

Lachlan Valley Railway

If you’re into steam locomotives and railway memorabilia, head over to the Lachlan Valley Railway. It’s a heritage railway that offers visitors the opportunity to experience Australia’s railway history. Take a ride on the vintage train and enjoy the stunning countryside views through the train window.

Natural Attractions of Cowra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wyangala Dam

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure, Wyangala Dam is the place to go. The Wyangala Dam is a beautiful waterway that offers many water sports activities like fishing, swimming, boating, and kayaking.

Mt. McDonald

Mt. McDonald is the highest peak in Cowra. Take a hike or go for a picnic and enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the Cowra Valley from the top.

Belubula River Walk

For something more relaxed, take a stroll along the Belubula River Walk. The River Walk winds through pristine bushland and offers plenty of opportunities to see the local wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cowra

There are several hiking and biking routes in Cowra that offer spectacular views of the surrounding area. Here are a few of the best:

  • Waratah Walk
  • Broula King Walk
  • Railway Heritage Walk
  • Cootamundra Heritage Walk

Best Things to Do in Cowra: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Cowra, from exploring historic sites to getting outdoors and exercising. Here are the best things to do:

  • Take a tour of the historic Cowra Prisoner of War Camp
  • Visit the Cowra Japanese Garden and Cultural Centre
  • Take a hike on one of the many hiking trails in Cowra
  • Go for a picnic or have a BBQ at the top of Mt McDonald

Food and Drink Scene of Cowra

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aussie Meat Pie: A savory pastry filled with meat and gravy, and topped with tomato sauce.
  • Damper: A traditional campfire bread, usually served with butter and jam.
  • Anzac Biscuit: A sweet biscuit made of rolled oats, flour, and coconut.

Local Beverages

  • Cowra Wines: Visit one of the vineyards surrounding Cowra to sample some of the region’s wines. Try the Cabernet, Shiraz or Chardonnay.
  • Cowra Craft Beer: If you prefer beer, try the local craft brew at the Cowra Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Cowra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cowra Motor Inn

The Cowra Motor Inn offers comfortable and spacious rooms with all the necessary amenities. It’s centrally located, providing easy access to all of Cowra’s major attractions.

Cowra Holiday Park

If you’re looking for an affordable and family-friendly accommodation option, the Cowra Holiday Park offers cabins and caravan camping sites. It has facilities for cooking, laundry as well as access to its own swimming pool and playground.

Vineyard Guesthouse

For something a little more tranquil, stay at the Vineyard Guesthouse. Nestled on the banks of the Goobang Creek, the guesthouse is surrounded by vineyards and offers stunning views of the countryside.

What to Buy in Cowra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wine

Buy a bottle of Cowra’s finest red or white wine from one of the many vineyards and cellar doors around Cowra.

Local Honey

The Central West is known for its honey, so be sure to pick up a jar as a souvenir.

Cowra Sapphires

Cowra is known for its sapphires, so if you’re into gemstones, head over to one of the local jewelers and pick up a unique piece of jewelry.

Local Customs and Culture of Cowra

Cowra is a rural town with polite country charm. It is best to greet people with a smile and friendly hello and mind social etiquette. During public events involving playing of National Anthem, always stand to show respect.

Best Time to Visit Cowra

The best time to visit Cowra is between September and November when the flowers of the Japanese Garden are in full bloom and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cowra

Do:

  • Always greet people with a smile and friendly hello
  • Observe local customs and traditions
  • Take your litter with you or dispose of it properly.

Don’t:

  • Litter of any kind
  • Ignore social etiquette
  • To capture photographs of people, it is polite to ask for consent first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cowra

Cowra is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always best to practice normal safety precautions like locking your doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Cowra

There are medical facilities available in Cowra to attend to minor and major injuries. In case of an emergency, dial 000.

Getting to Cowra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cowra

The Canberra International Airport and Sydney International Airport are close to Cowra.

Bus Stations Near Cowra

Cowra is connected to the surrounding towns through bus services offered by both local and international bus services. There are interconnecting services available in the bigger cities for transport to Cowra.

Other Options

Car hire services are available at airports and in major cities. Rideshare services such as Uber, Ola, and Taxify are also available in Cowra.

How to Get Around in Cowra

The best way to get around Cowra is by car or bike. Many of the attractions are spread out across the town and surrounding areas, which makes walking less practical.

Where to Go Next from Cowra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of New South Wales, here are some nearby destinations:

  • Mudgee, a wine region
  • Bathurst, a historic town well-known for its motor racing circuit
  • Forbes, a historic Gold Rush town

Conclusion

Cowra is a charming and pleasant town with something for everyone. From history to nature, you won’t be bored. Take your time and soak in the local culture. Be mindful of the local’s social norms and practices, and you’re in for a great time.
