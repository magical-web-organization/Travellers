Introduction to Coolah Tops National Park

Coolah Tops National Park is one of the most beautiful natural destinations in New South Wales, Australia. This park is located in the Central West region of the state, at an elevation of over 1,000 meters above the sea level. The name of Coolah Tops National Park is derived from the Aboriginal word “Coolah”, which means “wild turkey”. This park offers a range of exciting activities that include hiking, camping, picnicking, mountain biking, birdwatching, and horse riding.

List of Places to Visit in Coolah Tops National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Marlows Lookout is one of the most famous cultural attractions in Coolah Tops National Park. This lookout gives an incredible view over the surrounding region and is named after the Marlow family, who once ran a cattle station in the area.

Natural Attractions of Coolah Tops National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Coolah Tops National Park is home to a range of natural attractions, including the “Breadknife” and “Grand High Tops”. These towering rock formations are popular among hikers and provide panoramas of the surrounding valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coolah Tops National Park

Coolah Tops National Park has many hiking and biking trails that are perfect for those who love the outdoors. The Sassafras Trail, which is a three-kilometer loop track, is a popular hiking trail that provides incredible views of the surrounding landscape. The Corn Trail is another favorite, which is a three-day walk that winds through sub-alpine woodland and stunning panoramic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Coolah Tops National Park: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities in Coolah Tops National Park to keep visitors entertained. Horseback riding, birdwatching, and fishing are popular activities that many visitors enjoy. Additionally, camping is available on-site for those who want to spend more time in the park and experience the wilderness.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coolah Tops National Park

Here’s an example itinerary for a week-long stay in Coolah Tops National Park:

  • Day 1: Arrive and set up camp at the Coolah Tops National Park campground.
  • Day 2: Take on the Sassafras Trail and enjoy the amazing views.
  • Day 3-4: Head onto the Corn Trail and enjoy a three-day trek that winds through stunning landscapes and sub-alpine woodlands.
  • Day 5: Take a horseback ride through the park.
  • Day 6-7: Enjoy some relaxing activities like birdwatching and fishing before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Coolah Tops National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Local food is famous for using quality ingredients and traditional methods of preparation. The Australian meat pie and damper are some must-try dishes in Coolah Tops National Park.

Local Beverages

There are no wineries or breweries located directly within Coolah Tops National Park. However, there is an excellent selection of wines and beers that are produced in the nearby region, which can be found at local markets and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Coolah Tops National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Coolah Tops National Park offers many accommodation options for visitors. Camping and glamping are the most popular options, where visitors can stay in the heart of the national park, while enjoying all the activities it has to offer. Additionally, there are caravan parks, lodges, and cabins available to book.

What to Buy in Coolah Tops National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Coolah Tops National Park include locally produced honey and jams, authentic Australian artifacts, and locally crafted artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Coolah Tops National Park

The indigenous people of Coolah Tops National Park are Wiradjuri. Their culture and traditions are linked to the land and its biodiversity. Visitors to the national park are asked to show respect for the local culture and customs and take back their litter.

Best Time to Visit Coolah Tops National Park

The perfect time to visit Coolah Tops National Park is from March to November, during the dry season. The weather is mild, and the park’s surroundings are lush and green, making it the perfect season for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coolah Tops National Park

Do:

  • Take all your waste back with you.
  • Respect the nature and wildlife of the park.
  • Follow all the rules and regulations of the park.

Don’t:

  • Take your furry friends with you on your trek.
  • Light fires in unapproved areas of the park.
  • Swim in the park’s rivers and streams.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coolah Tops National Park

Visitors should be aware of potential hazards in the park, including snakes and other wildlife, dangerous cliffs, and adverse weather conditions. Visitors should also ensure that they have adequate equipment and clothing needed for the activities they plan to participate in.

Health in Coolah Tops National Park

Visitors must ensure they have access to adequate food, water, and medical supplies when visiting Coolah Tops National Park. Visitors should also be mindful of the high altitude and changing weather conditions and plan their activities accordingly.

Getting to Coolah Tops National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coolah Tops National Park

The nearest airport to Coolah Tops National Park is the Dubbo Regional Airport located 210 km away.

Bus Stations Near Coolah Tops National Park

The Dubbo Bus Station and the Wellington Bus Station are some of the nearest bus stations to Coolah Tops National Park.

Other Options

For visitors who prefer to drive, the national park is located at a two and a half-hour drive from Dubbo and a four-hour drive from Sydney.

How to Get Around in Coolah Tops National Park

The best way to see the park is by walking, mountain biking, or horseback riding. It is also possible to drive through the park into certain areas.

Where to Go Next from Coolah Tops National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Coolah Tops National Park can also visit the nearby Warrumbungle National Park, the Dubbo Zoo, and the Wellington Caves.

Conclusion

Coolah Tops National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves nature, outdoor activities, and stunning landscapes. From hiking and biking trails to cultural attractions and wildlife, Coolah Tops National Park has something for everyone. Whether it’s a day trip or an extended stay, Coolah Tops National Park is an ideal place to explore and enjoy the best of nature.
