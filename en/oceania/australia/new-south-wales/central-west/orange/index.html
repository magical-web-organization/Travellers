Introduction to Orange

Located in Central West, NSW, Orange is a charming city that boasts a rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and a vibrant food and wine scene. Surrounded by rolling hills, verdant vineyards, and historic architecture, Orange is a popular tourist destination that offers something for everyone.

Whether you are an adventure-seeker, history buff, foodie, or simply looking for a peaceful escape from the city, Orange has plenty of attractions and activities to keep you entertained. This guide covers all the essential information you need to plan a memorable trip to Orange.

List of Places to Visit in Orange

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The city of Orange is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into its rich past and cultural heritage. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Orange:

  • Orange Regional Museum: This modern museum showcases the history, culture, and natural wonders of Orange and the surrounding region.
  • Orange Visitor Information Centre: Located in a beautifully restored 1870s building, this centre provides information on the city’s attractions, events, and tours.
  • Cook Park: This beautiful park features a Victorian bandstand, a vintage cannon, and a monument to World War II soldiers.
  • Orange Heritage Trail: This self-guided walking tour takes visitors through the historic sites and landmarks of Orange.

Natural Attractions of Orange: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Orange is a nature lover’s paradise, with spectacular landscapes and abundant wildlife. Here are some natural attractions you shouldn’t miss in Orange:

  • Mount Canobolas: This dormant volcano is the highest peak in the region, offering breathtaking views of Orange and the surrounding countryside.
  • Lake Canobolas: This tranquil lake is a popular spot for fishing, picnicking, and hiking.
  • Orange Botanic Gardens: Spread over 17 hectares, this serene park features a diverse collection of native and exotic plants, as well as picnic and barbecue facilities.
  • Borenore Caves: These limestone caves offer a unique underground adventure with stalagmites, stalactites, and fossils dating back millions of years.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orange

Orange is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the landscape. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  • Mount Canobolas Summit Walk: This challenging hike takes visitors to the peak of Mount Canobolas, offering breathtaking views of the region.
  • The Towac Way: This 18km trail follows the path of Macquarie River and offers stunning views of the valley and hills.
  • Lake Canobolas Loop Trail: This easy 4km loop takes visitors around the scenic Lake Canobolas.

Best Things to Do in Orange: Activities and Tours

Orange offers a variety of activities and tours to suit all tastes, from food and wine tasting to hot air ballooning and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do in Orange:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: The region is famous for its wine, and there are several wineries around Orange that offer wine tasting tours and cellar-door sales.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the stunning views of Orange from the sky with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Food and Drink Tour: Orange is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of cafes, bars, and restaurants offering local specialties and international cuisines.
  • Orange Farmers Market: Held on the second Saturday of each month, this market offers fresh local produce, handmade crafts, and live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Orange

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Orange that allows you to explore the city’s many attractions and landmarks:

Day 1: Arrival and City Orientation

  • Check into your hotel or hostel and freshen up.
  • Visit the Orange Visitor Information Centre, located in the historic railway station.
  • Take a leisurely stroll around Cook Park, located near the city centre.

Day 2: Cultural Attractions

  • Visit the Orange Regional Museum, which showcases the region’s history and culture.
  • Explore the historic landmarks and monuments along the Orange Heritage Trail.

Day 3: Natural Wonders

  • Hike to the summit of Mount Canobolas and enjoy panoramic views of the region.
  • Picnic and relax at Lake Canobolas.

Day 4: Wine and Food Tasting

  • Take a wine tasting tour of the region’s many vineyards and wineries.
  • Explore the city’s food scene with a food and drink tour or visit local restaurants and cafes.

Day 5: Outdoor Adventures

  • Biking along The Towac Way trail.
  • Take a guided hike or explore the Borenore Caves.

Day 6: Relaxation

  • Enjoy a spa day at a local day spa, relax with a massage, or soak in the mineral baths.

Day 7: Departure

  • Head to the Orange Farmers Market for some local produce and handmade crafts.
  • Depart from Orange and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Orange

Orange is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse range of cafes, restaurants, and bars that serve both local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Orange:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb: Orange is famous for its delicious lamb, which is often served with roasted veggies or paired with local wine.
  • Orange Honey: The region’s honey is known for its unique flavour and is used in many local dishes and desserts.
  • Orange Oysters: The oysters along the coast of Orange are known for their rich flavour and are usually served with fresh lime and chili.

Local Beverages in Orange

  • Wine: The region is famous for its wine, particularly shiraz, pinot noir, and chardonnay.
  • Apple Cider: The fertile soil around Orange makes it an ideal location for apple orchards, producing some of the best apple cider in the country.
  • Coffee: Orange has a vibrant coffee scene, with several independent cafes serving locally roasted beans.

Where to Stay in Orange: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Orange offers a range of accommodation options to suit all preferences and budgets, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some places to stay in Orange:

  • De Russie Boutique Hotel: This elegant hotel features luxurious rooms with king-sized beds, spa tubs, and a bar and restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • Ibis Styles Orange: This brightly coloured hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and a swimming pool.
  • The Oriana, Orange: A 4-star hotel with excellent amenities, including a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.

What to Buy in Orange: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Orange is famous for its delicious local produce, handmade crafts, and wines. Here are some products you can bring home as souvenirs:

  • Wine: Orange produces some of Australia’s finest wines, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries around the region that offer cellar-door sales.
  • Handmade Crafts: The city has a vibrant arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans selling handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Orange Marmalade: The region’s navel oranges are famous for their sweetness, making them ideal for making delicious marmalades and jams.

Local Culture and Customs of Orange

Orange has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by its indigenous and colonial history. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including wearing modest clothing when visiting sacred sites and using indigenous names and titles when appropriate.

Best Time to Visit Orange

The best time to visit Orange is during autumn (March to May) when the trees turn golden and the vines change colour. This is also the harvest season, so there are plenty of food and wine festivals to attend.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orange

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Use public transport or carpool to protect the environment.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the indigenous culture and heritage.
  • Get too rowdy or disturb the peace.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orange

Orange is a safe city, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night, keep valuables out of sight, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Orange

Orange has good healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Orange: Transportation Options and Logistics

Orange is easily accessible by plane, bus, and car, and getting around the city is best done by car or bike. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around Orange:

  • Airports Near Orange: The closest major airport is Sydney’s Kingsford Smith International Airport. From there, take a connecting flight to Orange Airport, which is 15 km from the city centre.
  • Bus Stations Near Orange: Greyhound Australia operates bus services to Orange from major cities like Sydney and Canberra.
  • Other Options: Alternatively, you can drive to Orange from Sydney (3.5 hours) or Canberra (2.5 hours).

How to Get Around in Orange

The best way to get around Orange is by car or bike, as the city is spread out and has limited public transport options. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or city centre, or rent a bike from local providers.

Where to Go Next from Orange: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Orange is the gateway to Central West, NSW, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions that make for great day trips. Here are some places to visit near Orange:

  • Bathurst: A historic town with many colonial buildings and landmarks, including Mount Panorama racing circuit.
  • Mudgee: This charming town is famous for its wines, historic buildings, and natural attractions like Wollemi National Park.
  • Dubbo: Located further west, Dubbo is home to Taronga Western Plains Zoo, as well as several museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Orange is a charming city that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and food. With its vibrant wine and food scene, stunning natural beauty, and rich history, Orange is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring regional New South Wales. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your next trip to Orange!
