Introduction to Warrumbungle National Park

Warrumbungle National Park is known for its ancient volcanic landscape, rugged peaks, and stunning natural beauty. The park covers an area of 23,311 hectares and is home to some of the most unique flora and fauna in Australia. The park attracts visitors from across the globe, who come to explore its hiking and biking trails, marvel at its breathtaking vistas, and learn about its rich Aboriginal heritage.
