Introduction to Parkes

Located in the Central West region of New South Wales, Parkes is a gem that often gets overlooked by tourists. The town is famous for its connection to the Apollo 11 moon landing and the annual Parkes Elvis Festival, but there is much more to explore beyond these events. Parkes boasts incredible natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage, making it a must-visit destination in Australia’s Oceania region.

List of Places to Visit in Parkes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Parkes has a rich cultural heritage, and its museums and historical places are a testament to this. The following are must-visit museums and historical places in Parkes:

  • Parkes Observatory Visitor Centre: This iconic attraction played a critical role in the first moon landing, and visitors can learn about the history of the telescope, the science behind black holes and galaxies, and the famous moon landing. The venue includes interactive displays, virtual reality simulations, a theatre, and other exhibits offering insights into the universe.
  • The Dish: A giant telescope is not all that the Parkes Observatory boasts of. In 2000, a movie named “The Dish” featuring Sam Neill and Patrick Warburton was filmed in Parkes. As a result, a 30 meter replica of the original dish was built and has become an iconic landmark.
  • Henry Parkes Centre: Located in a former convent, the Henry Parkes Centre is a cultural masterpiece of Parkes’ history. It includes the King’s Castle, Parkes Motor Museum, Antique Machinery Collection, Australian Railway Monument, and much more.
  • Parkes Antique Motor Club Museum: antique cars, bicycles, radios, and farm machinery curated in this museum shed light on the history of Australian transportation. The collection includes classic and vintage vehicles, and the museum is open for public viewing.

Natural Attractions of Parkes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Parkes is unparalleled. From vast swathes of open plains to lush bushland and rolling hills, visitors will undoubtedly fall in love with the great outdoors in Parkes. The following are some of the best places for outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Goobang National Park: Covering over 44,000 hectares, Goobang National Park is a significant attraction that can be an exciting spot for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking, mountain biking, and camping. The park features diverse wildlife, including the threatened spotted-tailed quoll, koalas, and colorful bird species.
  • Cooke Park: Situated in the heart of town, Cooke Park is an ideal place to relax and enjoy a picnic or barbecue, or kids can splash around in the park’s water play area. Cooke Park honours Sir Joseph Cook, former Prime Minister of Australia, who acted on Parkes’ behalf in Parliament.
  • Bushman’s Hill: A scenic drive from the town centre, Bushman’s Hill offers panoramic views of Parkes and its picturesque landscapes. This spot is perfect for capturing breathtaking sunsets and stargazing at night. It serves as a convenient vantage point for the Great Western Plains and the town’s breathtaking sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parkes

For visitors seeking an energizing outdoor adventure in Parkes, hiking and biking routes abound. The following are some of the most popular routes for hikers and bikers:

  • Heritage Trail: The Heritage Trail is an easy 1.4-kilometre loop trail that winds through Cooke Park and takes hikers and bikers on a guided tour of the town’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Yellow Gum Trail: A 7.3-kilometre trail, the Yellow Gum Trail takes hikers through a dense forest of yellow gums, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Bushrangers Walking Track: One of the most scenic hikes in Parkes, this 3.3-kilometre walk takes visitors through native bushland and past stunning rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Parkes: Activities and Tours

Parkes offers activities and tours to satisfy a range of interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Parkes:

  • Parkes Elvis Festival: The annual Parkes Elvis Festival is a major attraction, drawing Elvis Presley fans from around the world to celebrate the life and music of the King of Rock and Roll. The festival includes live entertainment, a street parade, and much more.
  • Rock climbing and abseiling: The rugged terrain of the nearby mountain ranges offers excellent opportunities for challenging rock climbing and abseiling excursions. Hire a local guide to lead you on an adventure and provide instructions on safety requirements and techniques.
  • Hot air balloon ride: Consider booking a hot air balloon ride over Parkes and experience stunning vistas of countryside, the town, and beyond. Enjoy breathtaking views of the sunrise or a peaceful sunset while gently floating above the majestic scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Parkes

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Parkes:

Day 1: Arrive in Parkes and check into your accommodation. Explore the town centre, Cooke Park and Parkes Visitor Centre.

Day 2: Visit the Parkes Observatory and witness mesmerising planetary wonders and objects through the telescope. Spend your afternoon at the Motor Museum and then visit the Parkes Antique Motor Club Museum.

Day 3: Take a short stroll on the Heritage Trail. Take a leisurely walk along Currajong Creek, and enjoy a picnic lunch in the sun. Afternoon, visit the historic Station (train) and enjoy the antique furniture, estate gardens and railway memorabilia.

Day 4: Rise early and hike the Yellow Gum Trail before renting a bike and hitting the trails. Following this, take some time for some souvenir shopping in town.

Day 5: Participate in a Hot-Air Balloon ride over the rolling hills of Parkes and the surrounding countryside. Spend the afternoon exploring Goobang National Park and enjoying a barbecue or picnic.

Day 6: Head to the Bogan River to fish, swim, or row. In the afternoon, explore Bushman’s Hill, with its commanding views of the surrounding areas. Capture the perfect sunset, then head to Telescope observatory complex for a dinner under the stars.

Day 7: Explore the town’s visitor centre and Down the Rabbit Hole restaurant where you can check out the artworks in the gallery rooms. End the day by enjoying cultural activities and performances of local dance and music.

Food and Drink Scene of Parkes

Parkes offers an excellent range of cuisine and drinks to please any palette. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Parkes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb and barley pie: This Australian treat is prepared with fresh, free-range lamb and house-plucked barley.
  • Bush Tucker Platter: A real Australian foodie experience, this meal showcases traditional bush flavours such as saltbush and emu chorizo.
  • Kangaroo burger: A classic Aussie burger, prepared with locally sourced kangaroo meat and topped with fresh salad ingredients.
  • Apple and rhubarb crumble: An English dessert that has taken root in Australia, this crumble is served with custard or ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Bogan River Gin: This uniquely Australian gin is made with native botanicals and has a refreshing citrus aroma that leaves your palate invigorated.
  • Molong Brewery: A micro-brewery located in the nearby town of Molong, this brewery has crafted local beers such as “Lachlan Lager” and “Bush Brew” that have won numerous awards for their authentic flavor profiles.

Where to Stay in Parkes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Parkes offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. The following are some of the best places to stay in Parkes:

  • Parkes Motor Inn: This modern motor inn offers comfortable, budget-friendly accommodation close to the town centre and major attractions.
  • Clarinda Motel: A cosy and affordable motel with a heated outdoor pool, air conditioning, and a restaurant.
  • Derby Lodge: A luxury guesthouse located in the heart of town, Derby Lodge offers beautifully appointed rooms and suites with all the modern amenities.

What to Buy in Parkes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Parkes has a range of unique souvenir shops offering one-of-a-kind mementos of your visit. Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider bringing home:

  • Australian-made wines: Parkes is located in the heart of the wine country, so sampling the local wines is a must. Buy a bottle of Shiraz, Chardonnay, or Pinot Grigio to take home.
  • Elvis-themed souvenirs: If you’re a fan of Elvis Presley, you’ll find plenty of souvenirs and memorabilia in Parkes. Take a look at the Elvis souvenir shop located in Cooke Park.
  • Artwork and Crafts: Parkes is a rich cultural hub, and local artists and crafters have a lot to offer. Visit the local galleries for a variety of artworks, pottery, and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Parkes

Parkes is a friendly and welcoming town that values its cultural heritage. It is customary to acknowledge the traditional owners of the land on which you are visiting - in this case, the Wiradjuri people. Keep in mind local customs with respect to dress codes in certain places, and always remain courteous to locals.

Best Time to Visit Parkes

Parkes is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. For example, if you are interested in outdoor adventures, summer is the best time to visit, while if you prefer the cooler weather, spring and autumn are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parkes

Do:

  • Acknowledge and respect the traditional landowners.
  • Dress appropriately for cultural and historic places.
  • Take plenty of outdoor activities in Parkes.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any mark or harm on the natural and cultural heritage of Parkes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parkes

Parkes is generally considered safe for tourists, but it is still important to exercise caution while exploring. It is best to avoid walking alone at night and to stay aware of your belongings to prevent theft or loss.

Health in Parkes

Parkes boasts excellent medical facilities and physicians, ensuring you are always safe in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Parkes: Transportation Options and Logistics

It is possible to reach Parkes via direct flights, interstate buses, or even train trips. Once you have reached Parkes, getting around the town is relatively easy.

Airports Near Parkes

The closest airport to Parkes is the Parkes Regional Airport, which is well connected to the country’s major cities, including Sydney, Melbourne and Canberra.

Bus Stations Near Parkes

Bus services are available from major cities such as Sydney and Melbourne to Parkes. The buses are generally comfortable, air-conditioned, and equipped with facilities that make long drives pleasant.

Other Options

Parkes is also accessible by train journeys, with regular services arriving from Sydney and Broken Hill. The scenic train rides are quite enjoyable and a great way to explore the countryside while arriving at your destination.

How to Get Around in Parkes

Parkes is a small town, making it easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Public transport options are limited, but car rentals are available, and taxi services are also an option.

Where to Go Next from Parkes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Parkes serves as a base camp for exploring the surrounding countryside, which is replete with charming towns and natural wonders. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you might want to consider:

  • Forbes: A historic town famous for its stunning heritage architecture, museums and gold mines.
  • Nangar National Park: A natural wonderland with bird-watching, hiking, and camping facilities.
  • Condobolin: An agricultural town with a unique cultural blend of indigenous and rural perspectives.

Conclusion

Parkes is a must-visit destination, offering visitors a range of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and a welcoming environment. Plan a trip today to discover Parkes’ many treasures, from its historic landmarks and natural beauty to its vibrant food and drink scene.
