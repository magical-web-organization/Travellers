Introduction to Hills District

Hills District is an upscale and scenic suburban area located in the north-west of Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. The area is characterized by rolling hills, picturesque countryside, and a great blend of nature and modern facilities. Hills District is famous for its spectacular views, beautiful parks, hiking and biking trails, historical attractions, and top-class dining and shopping options. Whether you are a nature lover, an adventure seeker, a history buff, or a foodie, Hills District has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hills District

If you are planning to explore Hills District, here are some of the top places that you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hills Centre - This local museum showcases the history and culture of the Hills District through art, exhibitions, and historical artifacts.

  • Rouse Hill House and Farm - This historic house and farm is a great place to learn about the early settlers of the Hills District and to experience life as it was in the 1800s.

  • The Pines Historical Aqueduct Trail - This trail features an old aqueduct built in the 1800s, and it is a great place for hikers and history lovers to explore.

Natural Attractions of Hills District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Hills Shire - This beautiful park is ideal for nature walks, picnics, and leisurely bike rides. It features several hiking trails, a lake, and a playground.

  • Baulkham Hills Lookout - This lookout offers a spectacular panoramic view of Sydney, and it is a great place to watch the sunset.

  • Cattai National Park - This park is ideal for hikers, mountain bikers, and kayakers. It features several trails, camping areas, and stunning river views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hills District

Hills District is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, thanks to its beautiful natural scenery and well-maintained trails. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • The Great North Walk - This 250 km walking track stretches from Sydney to Newcastle, passing through scenic hills, forests, and coastal areas.

  • The Old Northern Road - This historic road was once used by pioneers to transport goods from Sydney to the north. It features stunning views, heritage sites, and hiking trails.

  • The Castle Hill Heritage Park - This park offers several walking trails that wind through bushland, wetlands, and heritage sites.

Best Things to Do in Hills District: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling with family, friends, or solo, there are plenty of things to do and explore in Hills District. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • The Hills Food and Wine Trail - This guided tour takes visitors on a culinary journey of the Hills District’s best restaurants and wineries, offering a chance to sample local dishes and wines.

  • The Featherdale Wildlife Park - This park is home to a wide variety of native Australian animals, including kangaroos, wallabies, koalas, and Tasmanian devils.

  • The Blue Mountains Tour - This tour takes visitors to the stunning Blue Mountains, famous for its rugged wilderness, cascading waterfalls, and breathtaking views.

Food and Drink Scene of Hills District

Hills District has a thriving food and drink scene, showcasing a mix of local and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meat pies - These Australian savory pies are a popular snack that you can find in many cafes and bakeries around Hills District.

  • Seafood - Being located by the coast, Hills District is home to some of the freshest and most delicious seafood in Australia.

  • Tim Tams - These iconic Australian biscuits are a must-try for any visitor to Hills District.

Local Beverages

  • Australian beer - There are many local breweries around Hills District that produce top-quality beer, such as the Hills Cider Company, the Modus Operandi Brewing Co., and the 4 Pines Brewing Company.

  • Australian wine - Hills District is famous for its local wineries, such as the Bimbadgen Winery and the Leogate Estate Wines.

Where to Stay in Hills District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hills District has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top picks:

  • The Novotel Sydney Norwest - This four-star hotel offers comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, a gym, and a restaurant.

  • The Castle Hill Inn - This budget-friendly option offers cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and easy access to the Castle Hill Heritage Park.

  • The Grand Mercure The Hills Lodge - This luxury hotel features elegant rooms, a spa, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Hills District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hills District is a great place to shop for unique souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Australian-made fashion and accessories - Many local boutiques and markets offer a wide range of stylish fashion and accessories made by local designers.

  • Handmade crafts and art - Hills District is home to several arts and crafts markets where you can find unique gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hills District

Hills District is a multicultural area, and it is respectful to acknowledge and honor the different cultures and customs of the region. The local people are friendly, hospitable, and proud of their heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hills District

The best time to visit Hills District is during the spring and autumn months (September to November, and March to May), when the weather is mild, and the landscape is beautiful. The summer months (December to February) can be hot and humid, while the winter months (June to August) can be chilly.

Getting to Hills District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hills District is located around 35 km from the Sydney Central Business District, and it is easily accessible via several transportation options.

Airports Near Hills District

The closest airport to Hills District is the Sydney International Airport, located around 40 minutes away by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Hills District

The Hills District is well-connected to the Sydney metropolitan area via several bus stations, including the Hillsbus, the State Transit Authority, and the Sydney Buses.

Where to Go Next from Hills District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore after visiting Hills District, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Blue Mountains - Located around an hour and a half from Hills District, the Blue Mountains offer stunning scenery, top-class hiking trails, and exciting activities such as rock climbing and abseiling.

  • The Hunter Valley - Located around two hours from Hills District, the Hunter Valley is one of Australia’s major wine regions, featuring top-class wineries, gourmet food, and beautiful scenery.

  • Sydney CBD - Located around 35 minutes from Hills District, the Sydney CBD offers an endless array of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, as well as world-famous attractions such as the Opera House and the Harbour Bridge.
