Introduction to Cronulla

Located south of Sydney, Cronulla is a beachside suburb known for its stunning beaches, scenic views, and cultural sites. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply want to relax on the beach, Cronulla has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Cronulla.

List of Places to Visit in Cronulla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cronulla Beach Walk: Take a stroll along the Cronulla Beach Walk, which is filled with historical landmarks and plaques detailing the area’s history.

  • Burraneer Bay: Visit Burraneer Bay, a serene area filled with historic homes and scenic views of the surrounding water.

  • Seaview Terrace: Located on top of a hill, Seaview Terrace is a historic row of houses that provide panoramic views of the area.

  • Hazelhurst Art Gallery: Located nearby in Gymea, the Hazelhurst Art Gallery showcases contemporary Australian art and hosts regular exhibitions and events.

Natural Attractions of Cronulla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Royal National Park: Located just south of Cronulla, the Royal National Park is a protected area filled with hiking trails, scenic drives, and beautiful beaches.

  • Cape Solander Lookout: Head to the Cape Solander Lookout to witness the spectacular views of the Pacific Ocean and watch migrating humpback whales (during whale spotting season).

  • Bundeena: Located on the outskirts of Cronulla, Bundeena is a charming village surrounded by picturesque beaches, hiking trails, and a popular art trail.

  • Wanda Sand Dunes: Explore the Wanda Sand Dunes, which are located behind Wanda Beach, and experience the thrill of sandboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cronulla

The area surrounding Cronulla is renowned for its natural beauty, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • Royal National Park Coastal Walk: Take a walk along the Royal National Park Coastal Walk, which stretches for over 26 km from Bundeena to Otford. Along the way, you’ll be treated to stunning views of the coastline, wildlife, and beautiful beaches.

  • The Esplanade: The Esplanade is a beautiful paved path that stretches along all of Cronulla’s beachfronts and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk.

  • The Boulevarde: For a more challenging ride, tackle The Boulevarde, which is a popular route for local cyclists. The scenic ride takes you past historic homes, scenic lookouts, and through the Royal National Park.

Best Things to Do in Cronulla: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Cronulla is renowned for its great surf breaks, making it a popular destination for surfers.

  • Kayaking: Explore the scenic waterways surrounding Cronulla by kayak and get up close with the local marine life.

  • Fishing: Fishing enthusiasts will be spoilt for choice in Cronulla, with a range of fishing charters available.

  • Cronulla Cinema: Catch a movie at the Cronulla Cinema, which has been a local institution since 1911.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cronulla

Day 1: Start with a morning walk along the Cronulla Beach Walk, visit the Burraneer Bay and then head to the Hazelhurst Art Gallery.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Royal National Park Coastal Walk.

Day 3: Go surfing or take a surf lesson at Cronulla Beach.

Day 4: Hire a kayak and explore the waterways surrounding Cronulla.

Day 5: Take a ferry to Bundeena and explore the picturesque village and the nearby art trail.

Day 6: Spend the day at the beach, enjoying the sun, sand and surf.

Day 7: Take a hike through the Boulevarde and then enjoy the sunset at Cape Solander Lookout.

Food and Drink Scene of Cronulla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Cronulla is known for its delicious fish and chips. Try them at Dunningham Park Fish Shack or Yellowfin Seafood restaurant.

  • Cronulla Beef Burger: A classic Cronulla dish, the beef burgers at Northies or Grind Espresso will satisfy any burger craving.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Cronulla is filled with quality coffee shops serving up a range of specialty coffee blends. Some of our favourites include Grind Espresso, Ham and Jams, and Blackwood Pantry.

  • Cronulla Lager: Enjoy a locally brewed beer at the Cronulla Hotel or Northies Cronulla Hotel.

Where to Stay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rydges Cronulla Beach: Ideally located across the road from Cronulla Beach, the Rydges Cronulla Beach boasts panoramic ocean views and modern amenities.

  • Quest Cronulla Beach: The Quest Cronulla Beach offers self-contained serviced apartments and is located within walking distance of Cronulla’s beaches and restaurants.

  • Cronulla Motor Inn: The Cronulla Motor Inn is a budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and a great location close to Cronulla’s shops and restaurants.

What to Buy in Cronulla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cronulla Beach Towels and Gear: Take a piece of Cronulla’s beach lifestyle home with you by purchasing a beach towel or other beach gear.

  • Local Art and Crafts: Check out the local artisan community and purchase unique handmade products such as pottery, jewellery, and artwork.

Local Culture and Customs in Cronulla

  • Beach Culture: Cronulla’s beach culture is a significant part of the local identity and is evident in the area’s relaxed lifestyle and outdoor activities.

  • Respect the Land: Cronulla’s natural beauty is something to be treasured and visitors are asked to respect the land when exploring the area.

Best Time to Visit Cronulla

The best time to visit Cronulla is from September to November and from March to May. These months provide a nice balance of warm weather and less crowded beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cronulla

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Bring appropriate gear for water activities such as surfing and kayaking.

  • Check the weather conditions before embarking on any outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Ignore the surf lifesavers’ advice when at the beach.

  • Leave valuables unattended at the beach or in your car.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cronulla

Cronulla is a generally safe area, but visitors should take the standard precautions to avoid any incidents. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables safe and secure.

Health in Cronulla

Cronulla has several medical centres and pharmacies, with the nearest hospital located in nearby Miranda.

Getting to Cronulla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cronulla

The nearest airport to Cronulla is Sydney’s Kingsford Smith Airport, which is around 20 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or public transport to Cronulla.

Bus Stations Near Cronulla

Cronulla Station is served by regular train and bus services, making it easy to get around the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the ferry from nearby suburbs such as Bundeena and Sans Souci to Cronulla.

How to Get Around in Cronulla

The best way to get around Cronulla is on foot, with most attractions and beaches within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also hire bicycles or kayaks for a unique way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Cronulla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sydney CBD: Head into Sydney’s CBD for a day of shopping, sightseeing, and dining.

  • Wollongong: Located just north of Cronulla, Wollongong is home to beautiful beaches, a vibrant nightlife, and a range of cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Cronulla is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Sydney, offering scenic views, fantastic outdoor activities, and a relaxed coastal lifestyle. From hiking through the Royal National Park to enjoying the beach culture, there is something for everyone in Cronulla.
