Introduction to Currarong

Nestled in Shoalhaven, in New South Wales, Australia, Currarong is a hidden gem that is perfect for those seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. This small, coastal town offers plenty of things to see and do, making it a great destination for families, solo travelers, and couples alike. Currarong is known for its pristine beaches, picturesque hiking and biking trails, and fresh seafood. Additionally, the town is steeped in history, adding to its cultural allure.

List of Places to Visit in Currarong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aboriginal Heritage Walk and Museum: Visitors can explore the local indigenous culture through the Aboriginal Heritage Walk and Museum. This unique attraction features an interactive museum and guided tour along the walking trail, showcasing the history and cultural significance of the area.

Point Perpendicular Lighthouse: Discover the Point Perpendicular Lighthouse at the northern edge of Jervis Bay. This operating lighthouse provides an insight into the region’s history and affords guests stunning views along the coastline.

Natural Attractions of Currarong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jervis Bay Marine Park: Jervis Bay Marine Park is one of the most beautiful marine parks in the country, known for its crystal clear waters, marine life, and craggy cliffs. Visitors flock to this natural beauty to enjoy diving, surfing, kayaking, and whale watching.

Honeymoon Bay: Located inside Beecroft Weapons Range, Honeymoon Bay is a secluded beach that offers stunning views of the ocean. It is the perfect place for swimming, snorkeling, and taking in breathtaking sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Currarong

Abrahams Bosom Reserve: The Abrahams Bosom Reserve near Currarong is a popular hiking and biking destination. There are several trails to choose from, ranging in difficulty, offering stunning views and showcasing the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Currarong: Activities and Tours

Dolphin and Whale Watching: Don’t miss the chance to see dolphins and whales in their natural habitat. Several local tour companies offer breathtaking experiences out on the waters.

Fishing: Currarong’s abundant seas and river estuaries make it an ideal location for fishing. There are a variety of fishing options, from deep sea fishing to casting lines from the shore.

Food and Drink Scene of Currarong

Must-Try Dishes

Fresh Seafood: Currarong is known for its fresh seafood, including prawns, fish, and crab. Several local restaurants offer these dishes in a variety of preparations, from traditional fish and chips to exotic seafood platters.

Local Beverages

Jervis Bay Brewing Co: Visitors can visit the Jervis Bay Brewing Co and enjoy a wide range of local craft beers. The unique brewery offers a variety of beers that pair perfectly with the local seafood dishes.

Where to Stay in Currarong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beach Houses and Cottages: Currarong offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget, including beach houses and cottages. These properties offer stunning ocean views and are great for families or groups.

What to Buy in Currarong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Art and Handicrafts: Visitors can shop for unique handmade items at several local art studios and galleries, including pottery and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Currarong

Aboriginal Culture: Currarong has a rich indigenous history, and visitors can learn more about it through visiting local museums and taking part in Aboriginal Heritage walks.

Best Time to Visit Currarong

The best time to visit Currarong is between December and February when the weather is warm. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, the best time to visit is during Spring and Autumn, from September to November or March to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Currarong

Do:

  • Try the local seafood dishes
  • Take a Whale Watching tour
  • Visit the local breweries and enjoy the craft beer
  • Take part in the Aboriginal Heritage Walk

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or countryside
  • Disturb the natural habitat or feed the wildlife
  • Exceed the speed limit or display dangerous driving behaviour

Safety and Health

Safety in Currarong

Currarong is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take care while swimming, as the surf can be rough at times. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the dangerous marine creatures that may live in the waters.

Health in Currarong

Visitors should be covered by appropriate travel insurance while in Currarong. Additionally, be sure to pack appropriate sun protection, as sunburn can be a real risk in this climate. Visitors with pre-existing conditions or allergies should bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Currarong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Currarong

The nearest airport to Currarong is Sydney International Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a public bus to the town.

Bus Stations Near Currarong

Although there are no direct bus routes to Currarong, visitors can take a bus to nearby Nowra and then complete the journey by car or taxi.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to reach Currarong, and visitors can easily rent a car from Sydney. The drive to Currarong takes around two and a half hours.

How to Get Around in Currarong

Visitors can easily explore Currarong on foot or by bike. Additionally, renting a car or taking a taxi is an option.

Where to Go Next from Currarong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kangaroo Valley: A stunning valley that is perfect for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy hiking, kayaking, and caving.

Conclusion

Currarong is a beautiful destination that offers a variety of attractions. Whether you are a fan of outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, or delicious food, there is something to suit everyone. We hope you enjoy your visit to this hidden gem!
