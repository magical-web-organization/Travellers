Introduction to Nowra

Nowra is a charming town located in Shoalhaven, New South Wales, Australia. Situated on the southern coast, Nowra offers a perfect combination of natural beauty and cultural richness, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all types. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply looking to relax on a beautiful beach, Nowra has something to offer. In this comprehensive tour guide for Nowra, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip, from top attractions to local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Nowra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nowra Museum: This museum houses a fascinating collection of artifacts that chronicle the rich history of the Shoalhaven area. Here, you can learn about the life of the local Aboriginal people, the arrival of European settlers, and the growth of the town of Nowra over time.

  • Meroogal: This beautifully preserved historic home offers a glimpse into the lives of an upper-class family in the early 20th century. With its intricate woodwork, period furniture, and manicured gardens, Meroogal is a must-visit for anyone interested in architecture and design.

  • Australian Aviation Museum: Aviation enthusiasts won’t want to miss this museum, which features a wide range of aircraft from across the ages. From vintage biplanes to modern jets, there’s something here for everyone.

Natural Attractions of Nowra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seven Mile Beach: This beautiful beach is a perfect spot for swimming, surfing, or simply relaxing in the sun. With its unspoiled natural beauty and calm waters, Seven Mile Beach is a favorite destination for visitors and locals alike.

  • Jervis Bay National Park: Just a short drive from Nowra, Jervis Bay National Park offers a stunning array of natural beauty. With its pristine beaches, rocky coastlines, and diverse wildlife, this park is a nature lover’s paradise.

  • Bomaderry Creek Regional Park: Hikers and birdwatchers will appreciate the beauty of this park, which features a network of trails that wind through lush forests and along sparkling creeks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nowra

  • Nowra to Fitzroy Falls: This challenging hike takes you through some of the most spectacular scenery in the region, including stunning views of the Shoalhaven Gorge and the Fitzroy Falls.

  • Wodi Wodi Track: This easy trail is perfect for families and beginners, winding through beautiful forests and along tranquil creeks.

  • The Bundanon Trust: This scenic bike route takes you through the beautiful grounds of the Bundanon Trust, including the historic homestead of famous Australian artist Arthur Boyd.

Best Things to Do in Nowra: Activities and Tours

  • Shoalhaven River Cruise: Take a leisurely cruise down the picturesque Shoalhaven River, spotting local wildlife and learning about the history of the area.

  • Shoalhaven Zoo: Animal lovers won’t want to miss this small but charming zoo, which features a variety of exotic animals from around the world, including meerkats, lemurs, and lions.

  • Nowra Farmers Market: This weekly market is a great place to sample fresh local produce and artisanal crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Nowra

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oyster and Seafood: Thanks to its location on the coast, Nowra is renowned for its fresh local seafood, including succulent oysters and a variety of fish.

  • Meat Pies: No visit to Australia is complete without sampling the savory meat pies, and Nowra has some of the best around.

  • Bottled Wines: The Shoalhaven area is home to a number of excellent wineries, many of which offer tours and tastings.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Nowra is home to a number of excellent craft breweries, many of which offer tastings and tours.

  • Local Wines: The Shoalhaven area is home to a number of excellent wineries, many of which offer tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Nowra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quest Nowra: This modern hotel offers spacious rooms and all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay, including free Wi-Fi and a fitness center.

  • The Nowra Motor Inn: This affordable option offers clean, comfortable rooms and a convenient location close to many of the town’s top attractions.

  • Greenwell Point Motel: Situated just outside of Nowra, this cozy motel offers a quiet retreat in a picturesque setting.

What to Buy in Nowra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aboriginal Art: Nowra is home to a thriving indigenous art community, and you can find a wide variety of beautiful handmade crafts and artwork.

  • Local Wine: The Shoalhaven area is home to a number of excellent wineries, and you can bring home a bottle of your favorite local vintage.

  • Organic Produce: Nowra is known for its fresh, locally grown produce, and you can bring home a variety of organic fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Nowra

Nowra is a laid-back and friendly town, and locals are always happy to welcome visitors with open arms. The area has a rich history, and the local Aboriginal culture is an important part of the community. Visitors are encouraged to show respect for the area’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Nowra

The best time to visit Nowra is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nowra

Do:

  • Respect the Environment: Nowra is home to some of Australia’s most beautiful natural areas, and visitors are encouraged to take care of these precious resources.

  • Try Local Cuisine: The food and drink scene in Nowra is excellent, and visitors should make a point of sampling some of the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Nowra is a clean and beautiful town, and visitors should dispose of their trash properly to help keep it that way.

  • Show Disrespect for Local Customs: The local Aboriginal culture is an important part of the community, and visitors should avoid anything that might be seen as disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nowra

Nowra is a safe and friendly town, and visitors should feel comfortable exploring on foot or by bike. As with any destination, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions.

Health in Nowra

The health services in Nowra are excellent, with several hospitals and medical clinics located in the area. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance before traveling, just in case.

Getting to Nowra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nowra

The closest airport to Nowra is the Sydney Airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive from Nowra.

Bus Stations Near Nowra

Nowra has a bus station located in the center of town, with regular services to Sydney and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Nowra, which is easily accessible from the nearby Princes Highway.

How to Get Around in Nowra

Nowra is a small town, and many of the top attractions are within easy walking or biking distance. Visitors can also use the local bus service or hire a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Nowra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jervis Bay: This pristine area is just a short drive from Nowra and is known for its crystal-clear waters and unspoiled natural beauty.

  • Kiama: This charming seaside town is famous for its stunning coastline and picturesque lighthouse.

Conclusion

Nowra is a beautiful destination that offers something for every traveler, whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply looking to relax on a beautiful beach. With its rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural landscapes, and friendly locals, Nowra is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Australia. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming town has to offer!
