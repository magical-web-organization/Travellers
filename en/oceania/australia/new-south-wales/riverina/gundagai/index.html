Introduction to Gundagai

Located in the beautiful Riverina region of New South Wales, Gundagai is a charming town that is steeped in history and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the town’s rich heritage through its museums and historical sites, hike or bike along scenic trails, enjoy local food and drink, and take in stunning views of the countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Gundagai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Gundagai Museum and Historical Society: This museum is housed in the former police barracks and contains a collection of artifacts and exhibits related to Gundagai’s history. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the nearby historic gaol.
  • The Prince Alfred Bridge: This historic bridge spans the Murrumbidgee River and is a landmark of Gundagai. The bridge was built in the 1800s and offers visitors stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.
  • The Old Gundagai Courthouse: This historic courthouse was built in the mid-1800s and today houses the Gundagai Visitor Information Centre. Visitors can learn about the region’s history and pick up information about local attractions and events.

Natural Attractions of Gundagai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Dog on the Tuckerbox: This iconic statue is located just outside of Gundagai and is a popular spot for visitors to take photos. The statue depicts a dog guarding the tuckerbox of a swagman and commemorates the region’s early pioneers.
  • Gundagai Lookout: This scenic lookout offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and is a great spot for a picnic lunch or a sunset stroll.
  • The Gundagai Flood Memorial: This memorial pays tribute to the lives lost during the devastating floods that hit the region in 1852.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gundagai

Gundagai has a variety of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Gundagai Historic Railway Trail: This 2km trail follows the path of the former Gundagai railway line and offers visitors a chance to experience the region’s railway history.
  • The Tumut River Trail: This 30km trail follows the beautiful Tumut River and is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.
  • The Kimo Lookout Trail: This 5km trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and is a great spot for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Gundagai: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Town by Foot: Take a leisurely stroll through Gundagai’s historic streets and soak in the town’s unique charm and beauty.
  • Visit Local Wineries: The Riverina region is known for its delicious wines, and Gundagai has several excellent wineries that offer tastings and tours.
  • Go Horseback Riding: Explore the countryside on horseback and experience the beauty of the region in a unique way.
  • Take a Scenic Drive: The back roads around Gundagai offer stunning views of the countryside and are a great way to get a taste of the region’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gundagai

Day 1: Explore Gundagai’s historic sites and museums.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive or hike in the surrounding countryside.

Day 3: Go horseback riding or take a hot air balloon ride over the region.

Day 4: Visit local wineries and enjoy a wine tasting or tour.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Riverina dishes.

Day 6: Go fishing or boating on one of the region’s beautiful rivers.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beauty of Gundagai at a local spa or wellness center.

Food and Drink Scene of Gundagai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Riverina Lamb: This locally sourced and expertly prepared lamb is a must-try for any foodie visiting Gundagai.
  • Damper: This traditional Australian bread is often cooked over an open fire and is a delicious and hearty snack.
  • Pavlova: This light and airy dessert is a local favorite and is often made with fresh fruit and a dollop of cream.

Local Beverages

  • Riverina Wine: The Riverina region is known for its delicious wines, including crisp whites and bold reds.
  • Australian Beer: Australia is known for its excellent beer, and Gundagai has several local breweries that offer excellent craft brews.
  • Bush Tea: This refreshing and caffeine-free tea is an excellent alternative to coffee and is made from native Australian plants.

Where to Stay in Gundagai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Criterion Hotel: This historic hotel offers comfortable, well-appointed rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • The Tuckerbox Motor Inn: This modern and well-equipped motel is a great option for families or those on a budget.

What to Buy in Gundagai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork and Crafts: Visit one of the many local art galleries and shops to find unique and beautiful artwork and crafts to bring home.
  • Wine and Beer: Don’t forget to bring home a bottle or two of the region’s delicious wine and beer as a souvenir of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Gundagai

  • Always greet people with a friendly “hello” or “g’day.”

  • Embrace the outdoors and enjoy the beauty of the region.

Best Time to Visit Gundagai

The best time to visit Gundagai is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and dry, and winter can be cold and rainy.
