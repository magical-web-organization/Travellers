Introduction to Riverina

Riverina is a highly underrated destination located in New South Wales, Australia. This region boasts a spectacular mix of stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Its charming towns and cities, such as Wagga Wagga, Griffith, and Leeton, offer plenty of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages.

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, a foodie, or simply seeking to relax and unwind in nature, Riverina has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local delicacies to try in Riverina.

List of Places to Visit in Riverina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums And Historical Places

Riverina has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit attractions:

1. National Museum of Australian Pottery, Holbrook

Located in the historic town of Holbrook, the National Museum of Australian Pottery offers a fascinating insight into Australian ceramics. The museum has an extensive collection of pottery dating back to the early 19th century.

2. Hermit’s Cave, Glenrowan

Hermit’s Cave is a unique attraction located in Glenrowan. This small cave is home to over 70 murals depicting the life and times of Ned Kelly, an Australian bushranger who is a legendary figure in Australian history.

3. Temora Aviation Museum, Temora

The Temora Aviation Museum is a living museum that houses a remarkable collection of vintage aircraft. Visitors can get up close and personal with iconic planes such as the Spitfire, Vampire, and the F-111.

Natural Attractions of Riverina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Riverina is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts with its breathtaking natural scenery and abundance of outdoor activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Riverina:

1. Murrumbidgee River

The Murrumbidgee River winds its way through Riverina, and it is one of the best spots for a range of outdoor activities such as fishing, kayaking, and hiking. The scenery along the river is simply breathtaking, with plenty of opportunities to see native wildlife.

2. Lake Albert

Lake Albert is a picturesque freshwater lake situated in Wagga Wagga. The lake is an excellent spot for watersports such as sailing, kayaking, and fishing. It is also a popular location for picnics and walking trails.

3. Cocoparra National Park

Cocoparra National Park is a haven for hikers and offers some of the best scenic views in Riverina. Visitors can take a walk along the Mount Bingar Trail or explore the rugged terrain of the park on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Riverina

Riverina is known for its scenic hiking trails and biking routes. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Riverina:

1. Wiradjuri Walking Track, Wagga Wagga

The Wiradjuri Walking Track is a 42km trail that takes hikers through beautiful bushlands and stunning wetlands. The trail also passes by historic sites and cultural landmarks such as the Wiradjuri Reserve.

2. Wagga Rail Trail

The Wagga Rail Trail is a great biking route that follows the former railway line from Wagga Wagga to Ladysmith. The trail passes through charming towns, stunning countryside and offers breath-taking views of the Riverina landscape.

3. Lake Hume Circuit, Albury

The Lake Hume Circuit is a 42km bike ride that takes you around the stunning Lake Hume. The route takes riders through picturesque countryside and charming towns while providing fantastic views of the lake and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Riverina: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adrenaline junkie, Riverina has something to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Riverina:

1. Take a Food and Wine Tour

Riverina is famous for its fresh produce, wine, and food. A food and wine tour is an excellent way to sample the local flavors and learn about the region’s rich wine-making history.

2. Visit the Charles Sturt University Winery

The Charles Sturt University Winery is a must-visit destination for wine lovers. Visitors can learn about the winemaking process while sampling some of the best wines produced in Riverina.

3. Explore the town of Griffith

Griffith is a charming town known for its Italian heritage and vibrant cultural scene. Visitors can explore its rich history, sample local cuisine, and visit local markets for fresh produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Riverina

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Riverina, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 Arrive in Wagga Wagga and take a stroll along the Murrumbidgee River
Day 2 Visit Cocoparra National Park and take a hike along the Mount Bingar Trail
Day 3 Explore the historic town of Holbrook and visit the National Museum of Australian Pottery
Day 4 Take a food and wine tour, visiting local wineries and sampling local produce
Day 5 Spend the day in Griffith, exploring its rich history and cultural heritage
Day 6 Hike the Wiradjuri Walking Track, taking in the stunning natural beauty of the region
Day 7 Relax and unwind at Lake Albert, enjoying some watersports or simply soaking up the scenery

Food and Drink Scene of Riverina

Riverina is known for its fresh produce and wine-making industry. Here are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Wagga Wagga Burger

The Wagga Wagga Burger is a local specialty consisting of a beef patty, beetroot, cheese, lettuce, and tomato. It is a delicious and hearty burger that perfectly blends sweet and savory flavors.

2. Lamb shank pie

The lamb shank pie is a regional delight that is a must-try for foodies visiting Riverina. The pie is made with slow-cooked lamb shanks and is served with mashed potatoes.

3. Orange and almond cake

The orange and almond cake is a popular dessert in Riverina. It is a moist, flavorful cake made with fresh oranges and ground almonds and is usually served with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

1. Riverina wines

Riverina is home to a wide variety of vineyards producing some of the best wines in Australia, including Shiraz, Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, and Semillon.

2. Roger’s Beer

Roger’s Beer is a locally brewed craft beer that is popular in Riverina. It’s a flavorful beer that reflects Riverina’s love for quality ingredients and local produce.

Where to Stay in Riverina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Riverina offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Riverina:

1. Club Motel, Wagga Wagga

Club Motel is a great budget-friendly option for those visiting Wagga Wagga. The motel offers clean and comfortable rooms, on-site parking, and free continental breakfast.

2. The Kidman Wayside Inn, Griffith

The Kidman Wayside Inn is a charming guesthouse located in Griffith. It offers comfortable, well-appointed rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

3. Atura Albury, Albury

Atura Albury is a modern, stylish hotel located in Albury. It offers spacious rooms, on-site parking, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Riverina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home from Riverina, here are some suggestions:

1. Riverina wine

Riverina is famous for its wine-making industry, so why not take home a bottle or two of the region’s best wines as a souvenir?

2. Fresh Produce

Riverina is home to some of the best produce in Australia, from oranges and almonds to beef and lamb. Bring home some fresh local produce to enjoy once you’re back home.

3. Pottery

The National Museum of Australian Pottery in Holbrook is a great place to learn about the history of Australian ceramics. You can also purchase some beautiful locally made pottery as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Riverina

Riverina has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its food, art, music, and festivals. The region is home to a large Indigenous population, so it’s important to respect their customs and traditions when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Riverina

Riverina is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May). The weather is mild during these seasons, and the landscape is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Riverina

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and wine
  • Be mindful of the environment and wildlife

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions
  • Ignore warning signs in natural areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Riverina

Riverina is a safe destination with a low crime rate. It’s always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as locking your car and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Riverina

Riverina has excellent medical facilities, but it’s always advisable to have adequate travel insurance before your trip. It is also essential to protect yourself against the sun and stay hydrated during warmer months.

Getting to Riverina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Riverina is easy, with several transport options available.

Airports Near Riverina

The nearest airports to Riverina are Wagga Wagga Airport, Albury Airport, and Griffith Airport.

Bus Stations Near Riverina

Riverina is easily accessible by bus, with regular services available from most major cities in NSW.

Other Options

Driving is another popular option for getting to Riverina. The region is well-connected by road to major cities such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Canberra.

How to Get Around in Riverina

The best way to get around Riverina is by car. Car rentals are readily available in the region, and it’s an excellent way to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Riverina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Riverina is well-connected to several nearby destinations that are worth exploring:

1. Canberra

Canberra, the capital of Australia, is only a few hours’ drive from Riverina. It’s home to several iconic landmarks such as the Australian War Memorial and Parliament House.

2. Snowy Mountains

The Snowy Mountains offer unparalleled natural beauty. Visitors can explore the stunning alpine landscapes and sample some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the country.

3. Victorian High Country

The Victorian High Country is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore its lush forests, sample its delicious food and wine, and take part in several adventure activities such as hiking, biking, and skiing.

Conclusion

Riverina is a fantastic destination that combines stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re exploring its charming towns and cities, sampling delicious local cuisines, or embracing the great outdoors, Riverina has something to offer everyone.
