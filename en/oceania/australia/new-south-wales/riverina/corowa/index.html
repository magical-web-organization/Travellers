Introduction to Corowa

Corowa, a picturesque town in the Riverina region of New South Wales, Australia, is blessed with natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage. It is located on the Murray River, where New South Wales meets Victoria, and is situated approximately 600 kilometres away from Sydney, the capital city of New South Wales. Corowa is a perfect getaway destination for travelers seeking an off-beat destination.

With its magnificent outdoor vistas, diverse cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor adventures, Corowa has something for everyone. In this guide, we have put together a comprehensive list of places to visit, top attractions, things to do, accommodations, and transportation options. So, sit back, relax, and get ready to explore the hidden gems of Corowa!

List of Places to Visit in Corowa

Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a cultural explorer, Corowa has something to offer. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, a plethora of attractions dotted across the town await you. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Corowa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Corowa Federation Museum: This iconic museum is dedicated to showcasing the history and heritage of Corowa and the Federation of Australia. Visitors can explore the permanent collection of displays featuring photographs, artifacts, and documents that showcase Corowa’s history.

  • John Foord Bridge: This suspension bridge is a popular attraction for photographers looking to capture the stunning views of the Murray River and the surrounding area. The bridge is open to pedestrians, and you can stroll across it to enjoy breathtaking scenery.

  • Pioneer Women’s Memorial: The memorial pays tribute to pioneer women who left their homes and families to settle in new and often challenging environments. The monument features a bronze sculpture of a woman holding a child, symbolizing the strength and spirit of pioneer women.

Natural Attractions of Corowa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Currawa Reserve: This nature reserve is a popular destination for hiking, cycling, picnicking, and birdwatching. Visitors can wander around the walking tracks, enjoy bushland scenery and picturesque views of the Murray River.

  • Murray River: The Murray River offers plenty of activities to indulge in, such as swimming, fishing, boating, water skiing, and kayaking.

  • Tower Hill Reserve: This scenic reserve is one of the region’s hidden treasures, featuring stunning views of the rolling hills in the background. Visitors can explore the well-maintained walking trails, observe birdlife and take in the fresh, clean air.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corowa

Corowa is a great destination for outdoor adventurers. The town is blessed with a abundance of natural beauty, a great network of walking and biking trails presenting an opportunity to explore and enjoy the marvels of Mother Nature. Here are some of the top trails for hiking and biking:

  • Murray River Trail: The Murray River trail is an easy walk, ideal for families and seniors, that takes you right along the Murray River and through the scenic red gum forest.

  • Lagoon Track: The Lagoon track is great for adventurers who want to connect with nature. Here, you can explore the riverine forest and local wildlife such as kangaroos, wallabies and a variety of birds.

Best Things to Do in Corowa: Activities and Tours

Corowa offers plenty of activities to keep visitors entertained. From visiting historic sites and cultural landmarks to indulging in outdoor adventures and culinary experiences. Here are some of the must-do activities in Corowa:

  • Winery Tours: Corowa is home to many award-winning wineries that offer tours and tastings. Try a glass of local wine and get immersed in the flavors of the region.

  • Murray River Cruises: Chartered boat trips on the Murray River offer memorable scenic tours, ranging from sightseeing cruises to sunset dinner cruises.

Food and Drink Scene of Corowa

Corowa is a hidden gem for food lovers, with its collection of local eateries serving fantastic food and wine to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some top recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza at Corowa Pizza Co: Corowa Pizza Co. offers the best pizzas in town, made from locally-sourced and high-quality ingredients.

  • Steak at Soden’s Hotel: Soden’s Hotel serves up juicy succulent steaks grilled to perfection, along with a range of other hearty pub meals.

Local Beverages

  • Corowa Whiskey and Chocolate: Visitors can indulge in a range of local beverages, including Corowa Whiskey and Chocolate. The artisanal whiskey is crafted from local ingredients such as native peppermint.

  • Local Wines: New South Wales produces high-quality wines, and Corowa is no exception. The town has several vineyards producing award-winning wines, including red wines such as shiraz, cabernet sauvignon, and merlot, and white wines such as chardonnay and sauvignon blanc.

  • Best Western Heritage Motor Inn: This motel provides luxury accommodations with spa baths, a licensed restaurant, and a pool. It is perfect for those looking for a luxurious stay.

What to Buy in Corowa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Corowa is known for its collection of souvenirs and local products, ranging from crafts, jewelry, and stylish clothes to local spirits and culinary specialties. Here are the top picks for souvenirs from Corowa:

  • Corowa Whiskey and Chocolate: This unique chocolate and whiskey combination is an absolute must-buy.

  • Gourmet Products: The Riverina region is known for its fresh produce, gourmet meats, cheeses, and chocolates. These products make great souvenirs to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Corowa

Visitors to Corowa will be treated to a truly unique culture and customs. The town has a rich history and cultural heritage that is evident everywhere you go, from its museums and galleries to its local food and drink. Corowa is a friendly and welcoming town, with locals always willing to help and share their stories with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Corowa

The optimal time to visit Corowa is during the Spring and Autumn seasons, from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild, and the nature is at its most vibrant. Summer months (December to February) offer scorching temperatures, but outdoor lovers can still find plenty to do in Corowa’s waterways such as swimming and water sports. Winters are cold and perfect for those who love to explore Corowa’s natural beauty on foot or bike.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corowa

When visiting Corowa, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local wines and spirits
  • Respect the local culture and customs of the region
  • Take scenic drives along the Murray River
  • Sample the local delicacies

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause damage to the natural environment
  • Swim in the Murray River near a boat ramp
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Corowa

Corowa is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions such as:

  • Always lock your car and secure valuables when parking
  • Stay alert when walking alone at night

Health in Corowa

Corowa has a range of medical facilities, including a hospital, doctor’s office, and pharmacy, for any medical needs you might have. Remember to take out travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Corowa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Corowa

There are no airports in Corowa, but Wagga Wagga Airport and Albury Airport are only a short drive away from Corowa and offer flights to major cities in Australia such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.

Bus Stations Near Corowa

There are local bus services that run in Corowa, connecting it to other nearby towns and cities such as Albury, Mulwala, and Wangaratta.

Other Options

Driving is the best way to get around Corowa, with excellent roads and car hire services readily available.

How to Get Around in Corowa

Getting around Corowa is easy, with excellent roads, footpaths, and bike trails. If you don’t have your vehicle, there are rental companies in the town where you can hire bikes, cars, or other vehicles.

Where to Go Next from Corowa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby attractions and destinations to explore once your Corowa adventure is over:

  • Bright: Well-known for its alpine scenery, woodlands, and natural attractions.

  • Rutherglen: Known for its high-quality wines, the town attracts wine lovers from around the world.
