Introduction to Hay

Hay is a charming town situated in the Riverina region of New South Wales in Australia. It lies on the banks of the Murrumbidgee River and is surrounded by picturesque countryside, which provides visitors with ample opportunities to explore the great outdoors. Hay is a destination for those who love history, culture, and nature. In this travel guide, we have compiled a list of things to do, places to visit, and natural wonders to explore in Hay.

List of Places to Visit in Hay

Here are some must-visit places in Hay that shouldn’t be missed.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hay’s rich culture and history can be explored through its museums and historical places. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Hay Gaol Museum: This museum is located in the old Hay Gaol, which was used to house prisoners from 1881 to 1960. Visitors can learn about the harsh conditions of the gaol and see the original cells, exercise yards, and punishment cells.
  • Bishop’s Lodge: A grand Victorian mansion built in 1888 that served as the residence of the Anglican Bishop of Riverina. It now functions as a museum, where visitors can explore the elegant rooms and beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • Shear Outback: A museum dedicated to the wool industry, showcasing the history and importance of shearing in Australian culture.

Natural Attractions of Hay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hay is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, from riverbanks to national parks. Below are some highlights:

  • Paika Creek Conservation Area: A picturesque natural reserve located 17km from Hay town. Visitors can take a scenic bush walk, have a picnic, or go camping.
  • Murrumbidgee River: The Murrumbidgee River runs through Hay and offers a peaceful escape from the town’s hustle and bustle. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the riverbanks or go fishing.
  • Sandy Point Beach: Located on the banks of the Murrumbidgee River 12km from Hay town, this secluded beach is an excellent spot for swimming, kayaking, and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hay

Hay is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with many scenic routes to explore.

  • Hay Nature Walk: A 2.5km nature walk that starts at the Hay Information Centre and takes visitors through the town’s botanic gardens and along the riverbanks.
  • Barren Box Swamp: A 4000-hectare nature reserve located 25km from Hay, offering a variety of walking trails and bird-watching opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Hay: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Hay offers many other exciting activities and tours, such as:

  • Sunset River Cruise: An unforgettable experience of watching the sunset while cruising down the Murrumbidgee River.
  • Hay Ballooning: A once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to witness the stunning surrounds of Hay from above.
  • Hay Golf Club: An 18-hole golf course that provides visitors with a chance to enjoy a round of golf while taking in the beautiful countryside scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hay

Here is a week-long itinerary for Hay, covering some of the most popular attractions and activities:

Day Activities
1 Visit Hay Gaol Museum and take a scenic walk along the riverbanks.
2 Explore Bishop’s Lodge and enjoy a round of golf at Hay Golf Club.
3 Take a sunset river cruise and have dinner at one of Hay’s local restaurants.
4 Visit Shear Outback Museum and take a scenic drive to Paika Creek Conservation Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Hay

Hay is not just known for its history and natural beauty but also for its food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hay duck: A specialty dish made from locally sourced duck, typically served with roasted vegetables.
  • Hay’s own chicken schnitzel: A chicken schnitzel dish topped with tomato sauce and cheese. It can be found at most local pubs and restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Hayfield Wines: A local winery that produces wines from locally grown grapes.
  • Hayter’s Brewery: A microbrewery that produces award-winning beers.

Where to Stay in Hay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hay has plenty of options for every budget. Here are some recommendable accommodations:

  • Hay Plains Holiday Park: A family-friendly holiday park offering cabins, camping, and caravan sites.
  • Nicholas Royal Motel: A luxurious motel located in the heart of the town, offering modern amenities and personalized service.
  • Commercial Hotel Hay: A budget-friendly option that provides comfortable accommodation and an authentic pub experience.

What to Buy in Hay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hay has several stores and markets where visitors can buy souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Hay Showcase: A store that sells locally made arts and crafts, souvenirs, and food products.
  • Hay Farmers Market: A market held every Saturday, selling fresh local produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Hay

Hay has a rich history and a unique culture that is reflected in its architecture, food, and art. The town’s locals are proud of their heritage and are happy to share it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Hay

The best time to visit Hay is during spring (from September to November) and autumn (from March to May) when the weather is pleasant, and the natural surroundings are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hay

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and wildlife.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the local traditions and beliefs.
  • Raise your voice or act rudely in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hay

Hay is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, it is advisable to take precautions and keep an eye on your belongings when in public places.

Health in Hay

Hay has excellent healthcare facilities, including a hospital, medical centers, and pharmacies.

Getting to Hay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hay

The closest airport to Hay is Griffith Airport, which is 133km away.

Bus Stations Near Hay

Greyhound and NSW TrainLink coach services operate services to and from Hay.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car from any of the major car rentals to explore the town or avail of the local taxi service.

How to Get Around in Hay

Hay is a small town that can be explored on foot or bike. However, visitors can rent a car or avail the taxi service to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Hay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hay is surrounded by several scenic destinations that are worth exploring:

  • Mungo National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient Aboriginal history and breathtaking landscapes. It is located 210km from Hay town.
  • Griffith: A vibrant city located 150km away from Hay town, offering many attractions, including wineries, museums, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Hay is a perfect destination for those looking to explore the natural beauty and culture of New South Wales. With its rich history, scenic nature reserves, and exciting activities, Hay has something for every traveler. This must-visit destination in New South Wales is a perfect combination of natural beauty and cultural heritage.
