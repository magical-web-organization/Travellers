Introduction to Lord Howe Island

Lord Howe Island is a small paradise located in the middle of the Tasman Sea, about 700 km northeast of Sydney, Australia. It is a true haven for nature lovers, with its crystal-clear waters, lush forests, and unique wildlife. While getting there requires some effort, the island is definitely worth the trip, offering a serene and magical escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

List of Places to Visit in Lord Howe Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lord Howe Island has a rich history that can be explored through visiting museums and historical sites. A must-visit attraction on the island is the Lord Howe Island Museum, which has displays showcasing the island’s maritime and natural history. Another interesting place to visit is Old Settlement Beach, which was the site of the first settlement on the island, established in 1834. Here, you can see the remains of the original structures, including the first European-style house built in the Pacific Islands.

Natural Attractions of Lord Howe Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main draws of Lord Howe Island is its breathtaking natural scenery. Visitors can go on hikes or bike rides to explore the island’s diverse landscapes, from lush forests and rugged cliffs to secluded beaches. Popular spots include Mount Gower and Mount Lidgbird, as well as the stunning beaches of Ned’s Beach and Lagoon Beach. For those who prefer water activities, snorkeling and scuba diving are great ways to discover the island’s underwater wonders, including vibrant coral reefs and sea turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lord Howe Island

Lord Howe Island is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails that offer breathtaking views of the natural landscape. The most challenging and rewarding hike on the island is the trek to the summit of Mount Gower. This 8-hour hike is only recommended for experienced hikers, but the view from the top is truly spectacular and is definitely worth the effort. For a less strenuous option, try the Malabar Hill Walk or the Transit Hill Walk, both of which offer stunning views of the island’s coastline.

Best Things to Do in Lord Howe Island: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and water activities, there are plenty of other things to see and do on Lord Howe Island. Tours of the island, including nature walks, birdwatching, and glass-bottom boat tours, are a great way to learn more about the island, its history, and its unique wildlife. For a more relaxed experience, visitors can also enjoy a round of golf at the island’s 9-hole course or indulge in a spa treatment.

Food and Drink Scene of Lord Howe Island

Must-Try Dishes

Lord Howe Island has a small but vibrant food scene that highlights fresh local ingredients. Some must-try dishes include freshly caught fish and seafood, locally grown fruits and vegetables, and the island’s famous Kentia Palm Cabbage. For a unique cultural experience, try the Island Fish Fry, where fish is cooked in coconut cream over an open fire.

Local Beverages

The island offers a range of locally produced beverages, including beer and wine. The Lord Howe Island Brewing Co. produces a variety of beers that are perfect for enjoying on a hot day. Visitors can also try the island’s own Capricornia coffee, which is known for its rich and bold flavor.

Where to Stay in Lord Howe Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lord Howe Island offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Popular choices include the Arajilla Retreat, which offers spacious villas surrounded by lush gardens, and the Milky Way Villas, which are located near the beach and offer stunning views of the ocean. For visitors on a budget, the Pinetrees Lodge and Waimarie Apartments offer affordable options with all the basic amenities.

What to Buy in Lord Howe Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lord Howe Island offers a range of unique and locally produced souvenirs and products that make great gifts or keepsakes. Some popular choices include handmade pottery, locally produced honey and preserves, and handcrafted jewelry made from shells and other natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Lord Howe Island

Lord Howe Island has a deep connection to its natural environment, and visitors are encouraged to respect and protect the island’s fragile ecosystem. There are also several cultural events and festivals throughout the year that celebrate the island’s history and traditions, including the Lord Howe Island Homecoming Festival and the Island Feast.

Best Time to Visit Lord Howe Island

The best time to visit Lord Howe Island is between September and May, when the weather is warm and the island is in full bloom. The summer months of December to February are the busiest and most expensive times to visit, so it’s recommended to book in advance if this is the preferred travel period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lord Howe Island

Do:

  • Take care to respect the island’s natural environment by not littering or disturbing wildlife.
  • Explore the island’s diverse landscapes and try different outdoor activities.
  • Learn about the island’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Collect shells or other natural materials from the island, which can disrupt the ecosystem.
  • Bring in any non-native plants or animals.
  • Ignore posted signs regarding safe swimming or other activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lord Howe Island

Lord Howe Island is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. Visitors should be cautious, however, when swimming or surfing as the currents can be strong and dangerous.

Health in Lord Howe Island

There are no major health risks associated with visiting Lord Howe Island, but it is recommended to bring sunscreen and insect repellant. Visitors with specific medical needs should seek advice from a healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Lord Howe Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lord Howe Island

The only way to access Lord Howe Island is by plane, with flights available from Sydney and Brisbane through QantasLink and Regional Express Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Lord Howe Island

There are no bus stations or direct land routes in and out of the island.

Other Options

Charter flights and private boats are also available for those seeking a more personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Lord Howe Island

Most attractions on Lord Howe Island are accessible by foot or bike, with rental bikes available on the island. Visitors can also rent cars or take taxis for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Lord Howe Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to continue their Australian journey, the Great Barrier Reef is a popular destination located only a short flight away. Alternatively, visitors can explore other destinations in the Pacific, such as New Caledonia or Fiji.

Conclusion

Lord Howe Island is a true gem of the Pacific, offering a unique and tranquil escape from the stresses of modern life. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or simply seeking a relaxing getaway, this island paradise has something for everyone to enjoy. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of Lord Howe Island for yourself.
