Introduction to Evans Head

Evans Head is a small costal town located in Northern Rivers, New South Wales, Australia. It is a perfect holiday destination for everyone who loves spending time outdoors, exploring nature, and learning about local history. Besides natural attractions, Evans Head offers an array of thrilling activities, tours, delicious food, and some of the friendliest locals you’ll ever meet. So, pack your bags and come visit this hidden gem on the coast.

List of Places to Visit in Evans Head

Here is a list of top-rated tourist attractions that you can visit during your trip to Evans Head:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Evans Head Memorial Aerodrome: hosting a RAAF museum with military aircrafts and displays
  • Henry Rous Museum: Exhibiting the town’s history, culture, industry, and more
  • Woodburn Museum: Showcasing the history and agriculture of the surrounding Woodburn area.

Natural Attractions of Evans Head: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Broadwater National Park: with picturesque beaches, walking trails, and picnic areas
  • Bundjalung National Park: home to beautiful beaches, headlands, and rainforest
  • Chinamans Beach: A spectacular location for swimming, fishing, and relaxing on clear waters beaches.
  • Frazers Reef: a popular spot with surfers and divers, great for exploring coral ridges
  • Goanna Headland Lookout: Known for its breathtaking views along with an abundance of wildlife in the area like goannas and birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Evans Head

Evans Head is filled with walking, hiking, and biking trails that offer panoramic views of rolling hills and coastline. Here are some popular walking and biking trails to explore during your trip:

  • Yuraygir Coastal Walk: a 65-kilometer scenic hiking track that takes you through secluded beaches, rocky headlands and many wildflower fields.
  • Black Oak Trail: a 9.6-kilometer hiking and biking track that boasts stunning views of the coastal range and Yabbra National Park.

Best Things to Do in Evans Head: Activities and Tours

Evans Head has activities to suit everyone’s taste from adrenaline-pumping activities to educational tours. Here are a few must-do activities:

  • Surfing Lessons: Take surfing lessons from expert instructors to learn surfing in the warm waters of Evans Head
  • Fishing: The area around Evans Head is home to a wide range of fish species, a perfect spot for recreational fishing
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Kayak and paddleboard in the calm waters of the river, a great way to explore the picturesque coastline.
  • Cape Byron Distillery Tour: This informative and unique tour of the distillery highlights the importance of sustainability and the processes of gin-making.

Week-Long Itinerary for Evans Head

Here is a week-long itinerary that covers almost everything you need to experience in Evans Head:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Henry Rous Museum and the Evans Head Memorial Aerodrome
Day 2 Go on a Black Oak Trail hiking tour and experience the outdoor beauty of the region.
Day 3 Explore the Broadwater and Bundjalung National Parks, do some whale watching, or take a dolphin spotting tour.
Day 4 Learn how to surf and take a dip in the water at the Chinamans Beach
Day 5 Take a kayaking and paddleboarding tour
Day 6 Relax on the beautiful beaches or take a tour of the Cape Byron Distillery
Day 7 Go on a fishing trip or take a guided sunset tour by boat along the majestic coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Evans Head

Evans Head is a foodie’s heaven with lots of locally sourced seafood, fine dining restaurants, and delicious eats. Here are some must-try local cuisine and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood: Try some fresh prawns, lobsters, oysters, fish, and crabs from the region, all famous for their quality and taste.
  • Beef Patties: Savor the delicious beef patties and pies that Evans Head is known for.
  • Creamy Gelato: Taste homemade gelato made from local milk and cream, a perfect dessert after a day in the sun.

Local Beverages

  • Australian Wines: Enjoy locally produced wines from the Northern Rivers Region
  • Ginger Beer: Try this homemade, non-alcoholic drink made from natural ginger and cane sugar.

Where to Stay in Evans Head: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Evans Head offers a variety of lodging options from beachfront apartments to family-friendly motels. Here are some recommended places to stay in or around Evans Head:

  • Reflections Holiday Park: A family-friendly park with cabins, campsites, and powered sites
  • Evans Head Motel: Budget-friendly accommodation in the heart of Evans Head.
  • The Australian Hotel: A historic yet modern hotel near the Chinamans Beach with excellent food and drinks
  • Sails Motel: Located just minutes from the beach and in the heart of town and features a pool spa

What to Buy in Evans Head: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are a few souvenirs and local products that you can buy in Evans Head to take home as a memory:

  • Indigenous Art: Get some authentic Australian art from the Bundjalung people
  • Wooden Surfboards: The region is known for locally crafted wooden surfboards
  • Australian Honey and Jams: Buy locally made honey, jams, and marmalades from roadside stalls.

Local Culture and Customs of Evans Head

Evans Head has a laid-back lifestyle, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. The region has rich indigenous heritage and a strong connection to the land and sea. Visitors are expected to respect the land, environment, and culture of the Indigenous people.

Best Time to Visit Evans Head

The best time to visit Evans Head is between September and November when the weather is mild and dry. This is also the peak tourist season, so expect higher prices and crowds during this period. Alternatively, you can visit from December to February to experience summertime and enjoy the beaches and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Evans Head

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Evans Head:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Keep the beaches and national parks clean and free from litter

Don’t:

  • Harm the environment or disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore warning signs during rough ocean conditions or bush-fire season
  • Over-speed or park the car in NO PARKING Areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Evans Head

Evans Head is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions to avoid accidents and mishaps. Be cautious of the rough ocean conditions during high tide warnings or bush-fire season.

Health in Evans Head

The nearest hospital/medical center is located at Ballina which is around 30kms away from Evans Head.

Getting to Evans Head: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Evans Head

The nearest airport to Evans Head is the Gold Coast Airport, which is approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Evans Head

The town connects with regular bus services operated by Transperth to connect with other cities like Brisbane, Lismore, and Ballina.

Other Options

The most popular options for transportation are by car or motorhome. You can also rent one from any major city in Australia and enjoy the scenic drive to Evans Head.

How to Get Around in Evans Head

The town is small, and visitors can explore all the top-rated attractions on foot. However, you can also rent a car or a bike to visit places that may be a little far apart from each other.

Where to Go Next from Evans Head: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Evans Head is located close to other amazing destinations, making it perfect for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursion spots:

  • Byron Bay: A famous surfing destination that offers a laid-back lifestyle and vibrant nightlife.
  • Yamba: A beautiful town located on the Clarence River famous for the magnificent Yamba lighthouse.
  • Ballina: A quaint riverside town where you can visit the Big Prawn and plenty of other attractions.

Conclusion

Evans head offers a mesmerizing experience for tourists who want to explore the beauty of nature and get a glimpse of the rich cultural heritage of the region. With beaches boasting soft sand and turquoise waters, lush national parks, delicious cuisine, and world-class accommodations, Evans Head should be on every traveler’s list.
