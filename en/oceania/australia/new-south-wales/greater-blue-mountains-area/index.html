Introduction to Greater Blue Mountains Area

Located in New South Wales, Australia, Greater Blue Mountains Area is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and culture enthusiasts alike. With its rugged terrain, verdant forests, and ancient rock formations, this region is a paradise for hikers, bikers, and campers. At the same time, its rich history, art, and architecture testify to its cultural significance and diversity.

List of Places to Visit in Greater Blue Mountains Area

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the local history and culture of Greater Blue Mountains Area, make sure to check out the following places:

  • Blue Mountains Cultural Centre - located in Katoomba, this art gallery showcases the works of contemporary and indigenous artists from the Blue Mountains and beyond.
  • Norman Lindsay Gallery and Museum - situated in Faulconbridge, this museum is dedicated to the life and works of Norman Lindsay, a renowned artist, author, and illustrator.
  • Jenolan Caves - these limestone caverns near Oberon are not only a natural wonder but also home to some of the earliest evidence of Aboriginal occupation in the area.
  • Lithgow State Mine Heritage Park - a former coal mine turned heritage park, it offers an industrial heritage tour, displays and exhibitions of mining equipment, and a picnic area.

Natural Attractions of Greater Blue Mountains Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its stunning vistas, flora and fauna, and rugged escarpments, Greater Blue Mountains Area offers endless possibilities for outdoor adventures:

  • Katoomba Scenic World - a popular attraction that’s not to be missed. You can take the cableway, skyway or railway, get a bird’s eye view of the Three Sisters rock formation and the valley below, or enjoy a scenic bushwalk around the surrounding area.
  • Govetts Leap Lookout - a breathtaking lookout with views over Grose Valley, Horseshoe Falls, and Pulpit Rock. The Grand Canyon Track begins at this lookout and is a highly recommended walk.
  • Mount Tomah Botanic Gardens - located in Mount Tomah, this 300-hectare botanic garden boasts over 40,000 plants, including rare and exotic species from around the world. A beautiful spot for a picnic or stroll.
  • Newnes Plateau - an idyllic spot for camping, bushwalking and birdwatching. It has accommodation options ranging from campsites to cabins and cottages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greater Blue Mountains Area

If hiking and biking beckon, then look no further than Greater Blue Mountains Area. There are several trails to choose from, each with its own scenic beauty, difficulty level, and duration:

  • National Pass - located in the Wentworth Falls area, the one-way path offers spectacular views of the Jamison Valley and several waterfalls, including Wentworth Falls.
  • Grand Canyon Track - starts at the base of Evans Lookout and takes you through lush rainforest and past several waterfalls.
  • Mount Hay - a challenging hike that rewards you with sweeping views over the Grose Valley.

Best Things to Do in Greater Blue Mountains Area: Activities and Tours

While there are countless outdoor activities to do in Greater Blue Mountains Area, a few special experiences one shouldn’t miss include:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - gaze over the region’s stunning landscapes while floating over the treetops in a hot air balloon.

  • Abseiling Blue Mountains - see the region from a different perspective by participating in a thrilling abseiling adventure down a steep cliff face.

  • Blue Mountains Food Tour - explore the area’s diverse culinary scene and taste some local delicacies through guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greater Blue Mountains Area

Day 1: Arrive and settle in at your accommodation of choice. Spend the afternoon hiking or biking through the scenic National Park.

Day 2: Enjoy a hot air balloon ride at dawn, followed by brunch at one of the charming cafes of Leura. Then go on the Grand Canyon Track walk.

Day 3: Visit the Jenolan Caves and explore the Lucas and Imperial Caves. On the way back, stop by the Zig Zag Railway for a ride on a heritage steam train.

Day 4: Relax and rejuvenate at the Blue Mountains Sparadise, a day spa located at Leura.

Day 5: Take a food tour and enjoy the local delicacies on offer. End the day with a visit to the Lithgow State Mine Heritage Park.

Day 6: Visit the picturesque Mount Tomah Botanic Garden and enjoy the scenery on a short bushwalk. In the evening, head over to the Mount Victoria Lookout and watch the sunset.

Day 7: Return home or explore some other nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Greater Blue Mountains Area

Must-Try Dishes

While in Greater Blue Mountains Area, make sure you try these local dishes:

  • Katoomba’s famous apple pie - made with locally grown apples, served with whipped cream.
  • Jenolan Caves Cafe’s seafood chowder - a creamy seafood broth that will keep you warm after a cold day of exploring the caves.
  • Birch Restaurant’s slow-cooked lamb shoulder - a hearty main course slow-cooked for hours, falling off the bone with every bite.

Local Beverages

For an authentic Blue Mountains drinking experience, try these:

  • Megalong Valley’s fruit wines - a variety of fruit wines including apple, pear, orange and blackberry and more.
  • Sassafras Creek Wines - producing natural wine using methods free of pesticides and synthetic chemicals.
  • Lithgow Valley Springs’ mineral water - naturally purified water sourced from the Lithgow Valley.

Where to Stay in Greater Blue Mountains Area: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greater Blue Mountains Area provides an array of options for accommodation to suit all budgets. Take your pick from cozy guesthouses, retreat-style lodges, or luxury resorts.

  • Ovolo Woolloomooloo - located in Woolloomooloo Bay, a mere one-hour drive from the Blue Mountains, this hotel is a blend of modern and classic design.

  • Leisure Inn Spires - a peaceful retreat in Leura with beautiful gardens and spa facilities.

  • Echoes Boutique Hotel & Restaurant - situated on the Echo Point cliff edge at Katoomba and offers breathtaking panoramas of the valley below.

What to Buy in Greater Blue Mountains Area: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blue Mountains honey - locally-produced honey with an array of unique flavors like eucalyptus and wildflowers.

  • Handcrafted ceramics and pottery - for a tasteful piece of nature to bring home, ceramics and pottery creations from local artisans are abundant in nearby towns such as Wentworth Falls and Leura.

  • Blue Mountains Botanic Garden products - purchase fine quality organic herbal and garden-based products from the Mount Tomah Botanic Garden.

Local Culture and Customs of Greater Blue Mountains Area

  • Respect the local indigenous communities across Greater Blue Mountains Area by visiting and climbing areas where it is permitted and not where it is prohibited.

  • Leave spaces the way you found them. Leave no trace.

  • Embrace local customs and traditions and thoroughly explore the area’s art, architecture, and history.

Best Time to Visit Greater Blue Mountains Area

The best time to visit Greater Blue Mountains Area is during spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) as the weather is mild, and the vegetation at this time is lush and green. Summer (December to February) has more wide-open spaces, and it is the driest season of the year, although it can also be the busiest time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greater Blue Mountains Area

Do:

  • Do hike, camp and bike in authorized areas in the region.

  • Do respect the local culture and respect the environment at all times.

  • Do stay hydrated. Always bring plenty of water when going on bushwalks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t feed native animals as it can lead them to become dependent on humans for food and harm the environment.

  • Don’t smoke in any National Parks or light any fires without checking first. Bushfires are a risk all year round, especially during summer months, so be extra vigilant.

  • Don’t forget to pack a reusable water bottle, hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, and insect repellent, the ideal essentials for outdoor adventures in Greater Blue Mountains Area.

Safety and Health

It is always a good idea to take some extra safety and health measures when traveling. Remember:

Safety in Greater Blue Mountains Area

  • Always let someone know where you are going and your expected return time.

  • Check local weather conditions and warnings before embarking on outdoor activities.

  • Make sure to pack appropriate clothing and gear for the activity you will be doing and for the weather conditions.

Health in Greater Blue Mountains Area

  • Australia is known for its UV rays. Wear sunscreen of at least SPF 30 and avoid the sun during peak hours when possible.

  • Seek medical attention promptly or contact the emergency services if you get injured while taking part in outdoor activities.

Getting to Greater Blue Mountains Area: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greater Blue Mountains Area

The closest airports to Greater Blue Mountains Area include:

  • Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport - the nearest major airport, located about 100 km (62 mi) away from Katoomba.

  • Bathurst Airport - situated 87 km (54 mi) away from Katoomba.

Bus Stations Near Greater Blue Mountains Area

Greyhound Australia operates long-distance bus services to several towns in Greater Blue Mountains Area, including Katoomba, Leura, and Lithgow. Be sure to check their website for schedules.

Other Options

You can rent a car during your stay or take a shuttle service from Sydney to any location within Greater Blue Mountains Area. Some shuttle service companies offer day trips or tours, which could be an ideal option for first-time visitors.

How to Get Around in Greater Blue Mountains Area

To explore the area conveniently, hop on the local train between Katoomba and Lithgow. Buses and taxis are available in towns and villages throughout the area, and car rental is an option.

Where to Go Next from Greater Blue Mountains Area: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After enjoying the natural beauty and adventure of Greater Blue Mountains Area, consider visiting these areas:

  • Sydney - The cosmopolitan city of Sydney is only a few hours away, with a range of activities to cater to everyone’s needs.

  • Orange - With vineyards, wineries, and vast orchards, the nearby town of Orange is a perfect location for those looking for delectable food and wine tours.

  • Hunter Valley - Hunter Valley is famous for its wineries, scenic countryside, food, and natural beauty.
