Introduction to Lawson

Located in the scenic Blue Mountains region of New South Wales, Australia, Lawson is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, adventure, and relaxation. Home to a vibrant community and plenty of tourist attractions, Lawson is an ideal destination for solo travelers, couples, families, and groups. In this travel guide, we’ll take a look at the top things to do in Lawson, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and many more. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Lawson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Leura Toy and Railway Museum: This family-friendly museum boasts an extensive collection of toys, trains, and other memorabilia that would take you back to the golden age of classic toys and model trains. You can also enjoy a ride on the miniature train that runs through the scenic gardens.
  • Norman Lindsay Gallery and Museum: The former home of the iconic Australian artist and author Norman Lindsay, this museum houses his original artworks, sculptures, furniture, and manuscripts, as well as a lovely garden and café.
  • The Braemar Gallery: A charming heritage-listed stone cottage turned art gallery, The Braemar Gallery features contemporary and traditional artworks by local and national artists, regular exhibitions, workshops, and events.

Natural Attractions of Lawson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Mountains National Park: A world-renowned UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Blue Mountains National Park is home to diverse wildlife, deep gorges, waterfalls, hiking trails, and breathtaking views over the Jamison Valley. You can explore the park on foot, bike, or horseback, and discover the iconic Three Sisters, Katoomba Falls, and Wentworth Falls.
  • Empress Falls: A scenic and challenging hike that leads to a refreshing waterfall and swimming hole, Empress Falls is one of the hidden gems of Lawson and a must-visit for adventure seekers and nature lovers. The trail is well-marked but requires a good level of fitness and steady footing.
  • Braemar Bay: A tranquil and picturesque spot for picnics, barbecues, swimming, and boating, Braemar Bay is nestled on the peaceful Glenbrook Creek in the heart of Blue Mountains. You can hire a canoe or kayak, go fishing or bird-watching, or simply relax by the water’s edge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lawson

  • Lawson Waterfall Loop: A 4km circuit that combines three of Blue Mountains National Park’s most impressive waterfalls - Adelina Falls, Junction Falls, and Cataract Falls - the Lawson Waterfall Loop offers a moderate yet scenic hike that is suitable for all ages and levels. You can enjoy the lush rainforest, rock formations, and occasional wildlife sightings along the way.
  • Lawson to Wentworth Falls: A challenging yet rewarding 10km hike that starts at the Lawson train station and takes you through the Valley of the Waters, Gladstone Pass, Conservation Hut, and Wentworth Falls lookout. You’ll be treated to magnificent views of the surrounding mountains, valleys, and waterfalls, as well as unique flora and fauna.
  • Lawson Mountain Bike Trail: A 5km singletrack that winds through the native bushland and creeks of Lawson, this mountain bike trail features plenty of twists, turns, jumps, and berms that would test your skills and adrenaline. You can ride it solo or with a group, using your own or rented equipment.

Best Things to Do in Lawson: Activities and Tours

  • Scenic Railway and Skyway: A thrilling and scenic ride on the steepest passenger railway and the world’s first cable car, the Scenic Railway and Skyway offers panoramic views of the Blue Mountains, including Katoomba Falls, the Three Sisters, and Jamison Valley. You can choose from different ticket options and experience the attractions during the day, at sunset, or at night.
  • Blue Mountains Explorer Bus Tour: A flexible and informative hop-on, hop-off bus tour that covers the major attractions and villages of the Blue Mountains, including Lawson, Katoomba, Leura, and Wentworth Falls. You can take your time to explore each stop, listen to live commentary, and catch the next bus at your convenience.
  • Abseiling and Rock Climbing Workshop: A challenging and exhilarating adventure that immerses you in the natural surroundings of the Blue Mountains, abseiling and rock climbing workshops are available for beginners and experienced climbers alike. You’ll learn the necessary skills, techniques, and safety procedures from expert instructors and get a chance to conquer your fears and climb to new heights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lawson

Day 1: Visit the museums and galleries of Lawson, including the Leura Toy and Railway Museum, Braemar Gallery, and Norman Lindsay Gallery and Museum. For lunch, try the homemade pies and cakes at the Fairmont Café and Patisserie.

Day 2: Hike the Lawson Waterfall Loop and have a picnic lunch at Adelina Falls. In the afternoon, pamper yourself at the rejuvenating spa of the nearby Hydro Majestic Hotel.

Day 3: Take the Scenic Railway and Skyway to Katoomba and visit the iconic Three Sisters, Echo Point Lookout, and Katoomba Cascades. For lunch, try the vegan-friendly dishes at the Shelter Café.

Day 4: Rent a mountain bike and ride the Lawson Mountain Bike Trail. Follow it up with a cold beer and pub food at the historic Royal Hotel.

Day 5: Go on a full-day bus tour of the Blue Mountains, including stops at Wentworth Falls, Leura, and Scenic World. For dinner, try the Nepalese and Indian cuisine at the Gurkhas Brasserie.

Day 6: Explore the historic village of Glenbrook, including its cafes, boutiques, and parks. For dessert, try the homemade gelato at Emu Plains’ Roma Gifts.

Day 7: Take an abseiling or rock climbing workshop in the Blue Mountains, followed by a celebratory meal at the family-friendly Blue Toro Restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Lawson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pie: A quintessential Aussie dish, pies are a popular comfort food in Lawson and the Blue Mountains. You can find them in different varieties, from classic beef and cheese to vegetarian and vegan options. Try the award-winning pies at the Fairmont Café and Patisserie in Lawson.
  • Fish and Chips: A classic staple of the seaside, fish and chips are also a hit in the Blue Mountains. You can enjoy them fresh and crispy at the Lawson Fish and Chips shop.
  • Roast Beef: A hearty and traditional Sunday roast, roast beef is served with all the trimmings, including roast potatoes, Yorkshire pudding, and vegetables. You can try it at the Oldbrige Inn in nearby Mount Victoria.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Blue Mountains region is known for its cool-climate wines, particularly the Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and Shiraz varieties. You can sample them at the Dryridge Estate Winery in Megalong Valley or the Tizzana Winery in Ebenezer.
  • Beer: Craft breweries and pubs are thriving in the Blue Mountains, with a variety of local and regional beers on tap. Some of the best places to try them include the Lawson Bowling Club and the Mountain Culture Beer Co. in Katoomba.
  • Spirits: Gin distilleries are also on the rise in the region, with brands like the Big River Distilling Co. and the Stone Pine Distillery producing artisanal and award-winning gins using local botanicals.

Where to Stay in Lawson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Blue Mountains Backpacker Hostel: A budget-friendly and social hostel that offers dorm and private rooms, self-catering facilities, free Wi-Fi, and a garden. Located close to the Lawson train station and shops.
  • Lawson Cypress Guesthouse: A cozy and stylish guesthouse that features double and twin rooms, ensuite bathrooms, a guest lounge, and a garden. Breakfast is included in the rate.
  • Danesa Lodge: A spacious and peaceful self-contained apartment that can host up to six guests. Features a fully-equipped kitchen, a laundry, a balcony, and a parking space. Located close to the Blue Mountains National Park and the Hydro Majestic Hotel.

What to Buy in Lawson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blue Mountains Honey: A natural and sweet souvenir from the region, Blue Mountains honey comes in different flavors, including eucalyptus and wildflower. You can buy them at the Honey Gem in Katoomba or the Blue Mountains Honey Co. in Leura.
  • Handmade Soap: A fragrant and sustainable souvenir, handmade soaps are crafted by local artisans using natural and organic ingredients. You can buy them at The Little Candle Company in Lawson or the Love That Bath in Leura.
  • Art and Gifts: A creative and authentic souvenir, artworks and gifts by local artists and designers reflect the beauty and identity of the Blue Mountains. You can buy them at The Garden in Lawson or Gallery One88 Fine Arts in Katoomba.

Local Culture and Customs of Lawson

The Blue Mountains region has a rich indigenous, colonial, and modern history, which is reflected in its art, music, literature, and food. To show respect and appreciation for the local culture and customs of Lawson and its surroundings, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Learn about the traditional owners and custodians of the land, the Gundungurra and Darug people, and their cultural practices and stories.
  • Support local artists, businesses, and products, and engage with the community in a respectful and friendly manner.
  • Follow the environmental and heritage regulations when visiting the National Park, including taking your rubbish with you, keeping to the trails, and not disturbing the wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Lawson

The best time to visit Lawson and the Blue Mountains depends on your preferences and activities. The region experiences four distinct seasons, each offering its charms and challenges.

  • Summer (December to February): The peak season for tourism in the region, summer is ideal for outdoor activities, such as hiking, swimming, and biking. The weather can be hot and humid, and the crowds can be overwhelming, especially during the holidays.
  • Autumn (March to May): A favorite season for many locals and visitors, autumn in Lawson and the Blue Mountains features colorful foliage, crisp weather, and seasonal events, such as the Leura Harvest Festival and the Blackheath Rhododendron Festival.
  • Winter (June to August): Winter is a magical and cozy season in the Blue Mountains, with occasional snowfalls, warm fires, and winter festivals, such as Yulefest. It’s also a great time for indoor activities, such as spa treatments and museum visits.
  • Spring (September to November): Spring in Lawson and the Blue Mountains is a time for renewal, with blooming wildflowers, mild weather, and outdoor events, such as the Leura Garden Festival and the Sculpture at Scenic World exhibition.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lawson

Do:

  • Respect the local culture, customs, and wildlife.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.
  • Carry a map, a phone, and plenty of water on hikes and bike rides.
  • Learn some basic phrases and manners in the local language.
  • Tip your server at restaurants and cafes if you’re happy with the service.
  • Smile and say hello to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Deface or remove any cultural or natural heritage sites and artifacts.
  • Play loud music or disturb the peace and quiet of the surroundings.
  • Feed or touch the wildlife, including birds, snakes, and kangaroos.
  • Walk off-trails or climb on rocks or trees in the National Park.
  • Smoke in the outdoor areas of cafes and restaurants.
  • Break any traffic rules or drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lawson

Overall, Lawson and the Blue Mountains are safe for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, there are some risks and precautions to keep in mind:

  • Stay aware of the weather conditions, particularly during bushfire season (November to March) and flood season (September to May).
  • Respect the wildlife, particularly snakes, spiders, and other venomous creatures.
  • Follow the safety guidelines and regulations when participating in adventure activities, such as abseiling and rock climbing.

Health in Lawson

Australia has a high standard of healthcare, with public and private hospitals and clinics available in all major cities and regions. Some important health tips for traveling to Lawson and the Blue Mountains include:

  • Make sure your travel insurance covers medical emergencies, as private healthcare can be expensive.
  • Protect yourself from the sun and UV rays by wearing sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, and lightweight clothing.
  • Drink plenty of water, particularly during hikes and bike rides.
  • Be aware of the seasonal allergies, particularly during spring.
  • Consult your doctor if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or concerns.

Getting to Lawson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lawson

The nearest major airport to Lawson is Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport (SYD), which is about 90 km east of the town. From the airport, you can take a train, bus, or taxi to Lawson. Alternatively, you can hire a car from the airport and drive to Lawson in about 1.5 hours.

Bus Stations Near Lawson

Lawson has its own train station, which is serviced by the Blue Mountains Line from Sydney. You can catch a train from Sydney Central Station, which takes about 1.5 hours. There are also bus services available from other towns and cities in the region, including Katoomba, Lithgow, and Bathurst.

Other Options

If you prefer a private transfer or tour, you can book a shuttle bus or limousine service from Sydney or the airport to Lawson. You can also hire a bicycle or a car in Lawson or nearby towns to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Lawson

The best way to get around Lawson and the Blue Mountains is by a combination of public transport, walking, and cycling. The Blue Mountains Explorer Bus and the local bus services run frequently between Lawson, Katoomba, and other villages, and offer flexible and affordable options for sightseeing and shopping. For hiking and biking trails, you can rent equipment in Lawson or bring your own, and follow the safety guidelines and regulations.

Where to Go Next from Lawson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions from Lawson and the Blue Mountains that are worth a visit, such as:

  • Sydney: The bustling and diverse capital of New South Wales, Sydney offers a plethora of attractions, activities, and events, such as the Opera House, the Harbour Bridge, the beaches, and the museums. You can reach Sydney by train, bus, or car in about 1.5 hours from Lawson.
  • Jenolan Caves: A mesmerizing and ancient cave system that features underground rivers, stalactites, and chambers, the Jenolan Caves are a natural wonder that is accessible by guided tours and adventure activities. It’s about 1.5 hours’ drive from Lawson.
  • Mt Tomah Botanic Garden: A stunning and educational garden that showcases more than 6,000 species of plants from around the world, the Mt Tomah Botanic Garden is a perfect day trip for families and nature enthusiasts. It’s about 30 minutes’ drive from Lawson.

Conclusion

From its rich history and culture to its stunning nature and adventure, Lawson has something for everyone. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, a family, or a group, you can explore the town and its surroundings at your own pace and style. We hope this ultimate travel guide has given you some inspiration and tips for your next trip to Lawson and the Blue Mountains. Safe travels!
