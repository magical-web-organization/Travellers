Introduction to Christmas Island

Christmas Island is a remote Australian territory in the Indian Ocean, located about 2,600 kilometers northwest of Perth. This stunning island is a true tropical paradise, complete with verdant rainforests, turquoise waters, and diverse flora and fauna. The island is perhaps best known for its annual red crab migration, during which millions of crabs march from the jungle to the ocean to breed.

In addition to its unique wildlife, Christmas Island also boasts a rich cultural heritage and a wealth of outdoor adventures. From hiking through the rainforest to snorkeling in crystal clear waters, there is no shortage of things to see and do on this idyllic island. Keep reading for a comprehensive guide to all the best sights, sounds, and experiences that Christmas Island has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Christmas Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Christmas Island Heritage Trail

The Christmas Island Heritage Trail is a self-guided walking tour that takes visitors through some of the island’s most historically significant sites. This trail includes stops at the former convict settlement, the Japanese Cemetery, and the Grotto, a natural limestone cave system that was once used by early settlers.

The Christmas Island District Museum

The Christmas Island District Museum is a small but fascinating museum that showcases the island’s rich cultural and natural history. The museum’s exhibits feature everything from traditional Malay weapons to rare bird collections, and there is even a section devoted to the island’s many species of crabs.

Natural Attractions of Christmas Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Christmas Island National Park

The Christmas Island National Park covers more than half of the island and is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the park’s many hiking trails, which wind through dense rainforest and lead to secluded beaches and scenic lookouts. Birdwatchers will also find plenty to admire here, as the park plays host to a number of rare and endemic bird species.

The Blowholes

The Blowholes are a series of natural rock formations that shoot spray into the air as waves crash into them. Located on the island’s southern coast, the Blowholes are an impressive sight and a popular spot for taking photos and enjoying the stunning coastal views.

The Dales

The Dales are a series of interconnected swimming holes and cascading waterfalls that are set against a backdrop of lush rainforest. This is an ideal spot for a refreshing swim, and visitors can also explore the many walking trails that wind through the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Christmas Island

The Settlement to Drumsite Trail

This four-kilometer trail winds through the former convict settlement and up to the Drumsite, a scenic lookout that offers sweeping views of the island and the ocean beyond. The trail is not too challenging and is suitable for most fitness levels.

The Greta Beach Trail

This seven-kilometer trail begins near the island’s airport and follows the coast to Greta Beach, one of the most secluded and pristine beaches on the island. The trail offers breathtaking sea views and is an ideal spot for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Christmas Island: Activities and Tours

Snorkeling and Diving

The crystal clear waters surrounding the island are teeming with colorful coral and marine life, making snorkeling and diving a must-do activity on Christmas Island. Several local tour operators offer guided snorkeling and diving trips, and visitors can rent equipment from various rental shops around the island.

Fishing

The waters around Christmas Island are also home to a thriving fishing industry, and visitors can join guided fishing tours to try their luck at catching a variety of fish species. The island’s rich waters are particularly known for their tuna and trevally populations.

Food and Drink Scene of Christmas Island

Must-Try Dishes

Christmas Island is home to a diverse array of dining options, and visitors can sample everything from authentic Malay cuisine to fresh seafood caught locally. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Gado gado: a traditional Indonesian salad made with various vegetables and peanut sauce.
  • Nasi goreng: a ubiquitous fried rice dish found throughout Southeast Asia.
  • Seafood laksa: a spicy noodle soup made with coconut milk and seafood.

Local Beverages

In addition to delicious food, Christmas Island also has a rich tradition of beverage production. Visitors can sample a range of locally made drinks, including:

  • Kopi Luwak: a rare and expensive coffee made from beans that have been eaten and excreted by civet cats.
  • Lemon myrtle tea: a fragrant and flavorful tea made from the leaves of the lemon myrtle tree, a native Australian plant.
  • Christmas Island wine: this unique wine is made from the island’s own Norfolk pine tree and has a distinct flavor unlike any other wine in the world.

Where to Stay in Christmas Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Christmas Island has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Swell Lodge: a luxurious eco-resort located right on the beach.
  • VQ3 Lodge: a comfortable and affordable guesthouse located near the island’s airport.
  • Captain’s Last Resort: a charming hostel nestled in the island’s rainforest.

What to Buy in Christmas Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Christmas Island Red Crab Souvenirs

One of the most iconic symbols of Christmas Island is the red crab, and visitors can find a range of crab-themed souvenirs to bring home as a memento. Look for items like t-shirts, mugs, and plush toys adorned with the adorable crustaceans.

Local Handicrafts

Christmas Island has a rich tradition of handicraft production, and visitors can find a range of unique and beautiful items made by local artisans, like jewelry, wood carvings, and traditional baskets woven from pandanus leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Christmas Island

The people of Christmas Island are a diverse mix of ethnicities and cultures, but the island has a particularly strong Malay influence. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as removing shoes before entering homes or places of worship, and dressing modestly in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Christmas Island

The best time to visit Christmas Island is between April and November, during the dry season. This is when the weather is pleasant and the island’s many outdoor activities are most enjoyable. During the wet season (December to March), the island can experience heavy rainfall and rough seas, which can make some activities difficult or unsafe to undertake.

Do’s and Don’ts of Christmas Island

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care not to damage or disrupt the fragile natural environment.
  • Take advantage of the island’s many outdoor activities and natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Touch or disturb any of the island’s wildlife, including crabs, birds, and other animals.
  • Ignore safety warnings or venture into areas that are unsafe.

Safety and Health

Safety in Christmas Island

Christmas Island is a generally safe place for visitors to travel, with a low crime rate and few safety concerns. However, it is always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take appropriate precautions, such as locking your belongings and being cautious when swimming or engaging in high-risk activities like hiking or diving.

Health in Christmas Island

Christmas Island has a modern healthcare system and a well-equipped hospital that can handle a range of medical issues. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions to avoid common illnesses like sunburn and dehydration, as well as mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Christmas Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Christmas Island

The Christmas Island Airport is the primary point of entry for visitors arriving by air. The airport is well-connected to destinations across Australia and Southeast Asia, with regular flights from Perth, Jakarta, and Kuala Lumpur.

How to Get Around in Christmas Island

While Christmas Island is a relatively small island, getting around can be a challenge due to its rugged terrain and limited public transportation options. Visitors can rent cars, motorbikes, or bicycles to explore the island on their own, or they can opt for guided tours or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Christmas Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to continue their adventures beyond Christmas Island, there are a number of nearby destinations worth exploring. Some popular options include:

  • Cocos Islands: another remote Australian territory located about halfway between Australia and Sri Lanka.
  • Indonesia: the nearby islands of Java and Bali are easily accessible from Christmas Island and offer a wealth of cultural and adventure opportunities.
  • Western Australia: visitors can hop a flight to the nearby city of Perth and explore the many natural wonders of Australia’s western coast.

Conclusion

Christmas Island is a truly unique and unforgettable destination that offers a wealth of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and endless adventure opportunities, this Australian territory deserves a spot on every traveler’s bucket list.
