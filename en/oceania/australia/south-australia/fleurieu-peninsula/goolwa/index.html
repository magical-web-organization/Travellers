Introduction to Goolwa

Goolwa is a charming town nestled on the banks of the Murray River, in the Fleurieu Peninsula region of South Australia. The town is steeped in history, and visitors can find a number of heritage buildings and museums in the town center. Goolwa is also a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, cycling, and water sports. Its natural setting on the banks of the Murray River and the nearby Coorong National Park is a special highlight.

List of Places to Visit in Goolwa

The following are some of the top places to visit in Goolwa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Signal Point Gallery: Located in an old steam-power station, the Signal Point Gallery houses a collection of artworks and artifacts showcasing the town’s history and culture.
  • Steam Exchange Brewery: Set in heritage-listed buildings dating back to the 1850s, the Steam Exchange Brewery is a must-visit for craft beer lovers.
  • Cockle Train: The Cockle Train is a steam-powered heritage railway line that offers scenic rides along the coast. The train has been operating for over 130 years and is a must-do when in Goolwa.

Natural Attractions of Goolwa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coorong National Park: Just a short distance from Goolwa, Coorong National Park is a stunning coastal reserve that offers hiking trails, bird watching, and a chance to learn about the traditional culture of the Ngarrindjeri people.
  • Goolwa Beach: Goolwa Beach is a popular destination for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing. It is a great spot for families with young children because of the protected swimming areas.
  • Murray River: The Murray River is a perfect spot for a peaceful paddle trip, where visitors can take in the picturesque scenery and enjoy the calmness of the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goolwa

Goolwa boasts numerous hiking trails that take you through the town’s beautiful landscapes and natural reserves. Some popular routes include:

  • Murrundi Trail: A scenic walk that takes you along the iconic Goolwa Wharf to the Murray Mouth.
  • Coastal Linear Park Trail: A well-paved bike path that runs parallel to the beach, offering gorgeous views of the ocean.
  • Kaiki Walk: A beautiful trail that leads you through the Coorong National Park, taking you along the shoreline and spotting the local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Goolwa: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities and tours that you can take part in when in Goolwa. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Bird Watching Tour: The Coorong National Park is home to over 230 species of birds, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.
  • Kayak Tour on the Murray River: A relaxing way to explore the river’s tranquil waters is through a kayak tour.
  • Fishing Trip: Goolwa is known for its great fishing spots, and visitors can go on a guided tour to learn the ins and outs of catching the perfect fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Goolwa

Day 1: Visit the Signal Point Gallery, walk along the Goolwa Wharf, and have lunch.

Day 2: Take a hike on the Murrundi Trail and learn about Goolwa’s history.

Day 3: Go on a bird watching tour in the Coorong National Park.

Day 4: Explore the town on a bike tour and take in the beautiful coastline views.

Day 5: Take a fishing trip on the Murray River.

Day 6: Kayak down the Murray River and enjoy the serenity of the river.

Day 7: Visit Goolwa Beach and enjoy some time in the sun before heading out.

Food and Drink Scene of Goolwa

Goolwa has an impressive food and drink scene. Here are some places you should visit to experience the local flavors:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coorong Mullet: A local freshwater fish caught in Coorong’s lagoon waters is an excellent dish made locally.
  • Fleurieu Peninsula Cheese: Goolwa is situated in the Fleurieu Peninsula, which is known for its local cheese, which can be found in local markets and shops.

Local Beverages

  • Steam Exchange Brewery: The Steam Exchange Brewery offers more than 40 handcrafted beers and ciders for those looking to taste the local brewery scene.
  • Wharf Barrel Shed: Wine enthusiasts can explore Wharf Barrel Shed, which produces regional white and red wines.

Where to Stay in Goolwa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can choose from several types of accommodation in Goolwa, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels with waterfront views. Some top options include:

  • Beach Huts Middleton: Located on the beautiful Goolwa Beach. It offers stunning views of the ocean and cozy cabins that provide an unforgettable camping experience.
  • River’s End Retreat: A luxurious bed and breakfast situated on the banks of the Murray River, where you can wake up to beautiful views of the river.

What to Buy in Goolwa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many local, handmade products that you can buy as a souvenir of your visit to Goolwa. Some of the best options include:

  • Ngarrindjeri Weaving: The Ngarrindjeri people’s traditional weaving techniques are unique to the region, and visitors can purchase woven products made by local artists.
  • Fleurieu Peninsula Honey: Fleurieu Peninsula is known for its delicious honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Goolwa

Goolwa has a rich history and a unique culture that visitors can experience. One of the best ways to immerse yourself in the local culture is by visiting the Ngarrindjeri Cultural Centre and learning about their traditions and culture.

Best Time to Visit Goolwa

The best time to visit Goolwa is between September to May when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. The town’s busiest month is during January when the Goolwa Regatta Week takes place, and the town comes alive with vibrant events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goolwa

Do:

  • Respect the local Aboriginal culture, traditions and seek permission before visiting the land of indigenous people.
  • Keep the environment clean via keeping your trash with you and disposing of them in the appropriate waste disposal bins.

Don’t:

  • Do not swim outside the patrolled beaches as it can be dangerous.
  • Do not disturb the local scenery and wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goolwa

Goolwa is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should be cautious around water bodies and be vigilant while using rental bikes and boats at all times.

Health in Goolwa

No specific health risks are present in Goolwa. Hospitals present in nearby cities such as Victor Harbor or Adelaide.

Getting to Goolwa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Goolwa

The Adelaide Airport is the nearest airport to Goolwa, located at a distance of approximately 80 kilometers northwest of the town center. From the airport, visitors can hire a car, or catch a cab to reach Goolwa town, or take a coach bus service.

Bus Stations Near Goolwa

Goolwa is serviced by a local public transport system, the South Link bus service which operates within the Fleurieu Peninsula that can take visitors around the nearby towns and attractions.

Other Options

Hiring a car is the most convenient option to explore Goolwa and nearby attractions independently.

How to Get Around in Goolwa

Public transportation is easily available in Goolwa. Visitors can use public buses or rent vehicles, including bikes to explore the town’s beautiful attractions.

Where to Go Next from Goolwa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Goolwa is located near some of South Australia’s finest attractions, including:

  • Victor Harbor: The township of Victor Harbor is located just 26 kilometers south of Goolwa and is known for its charming cafes, walking tracks, beaches and its famous Southern right whale nursery.
  • McLaren Vale: Famous wine region located east of Adelaide and south of Goolwa; home to almost 100 cellar doors.

Conclusion

Goolwa’s stunning natural scenery, rich history, culture, and abundance of outdoor activities make it an ideal holiday destination for travelers. The town is perfect for a relaxing weekend or a week-long adventure. With a rich town center and a diverse range of accommodation options and food, visitors will have an unforgettable experience in Goolwa, Fleurieu Peninsula, South Australia.
