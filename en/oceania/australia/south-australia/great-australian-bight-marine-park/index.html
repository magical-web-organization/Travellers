The Ultimate Guide to Great Australian Bight Marine Park

Australia’s Great Australian Bight Marine Park is a stunning coastal park located in South Australia, along the country’s southern shoreline. The park is well known for its beautiful natural landscapes, diverse marine life, and rich cultural history. Whether you’re interested in surfing, wildlife watching, or simply soaking up the sun, Great Australian Bight Marine Park is a must-visit destination for all nature lovers. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your trip to this breathtaking park.

Introduction to Great Australian Bight Marine Park

Stretching along the entire southern coast of Australia, Great Australian Bight Marine Park is the third-largest marine protected area in the country, covering over 45,000 square kilometers of pristine coastal waters. The park is home to over 90 species of fish, 36 species of sharks and rays, and a diverse array of marine mammals, including dolphins, seals, and whales. The park’s shoreline is also an important nesting site for several species of sea turtles.

Apart from its rich marine life, Great Australian Bight Marine Park is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several species of endangered plants and animals. The park’s traditional owners, the Mirning people, have been living in the area for thousands of years and have a deep cultural connection to the land and sea.

List of Places to Visit in Great Australian Bight Marine Park

When planning your trip to Great Australian Bight Marine Park, there are many incredible places to explore. Here are some of the park’s top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nullarbor National Park: Located near the Western Australian border, Nullarbor National Park is a stunning natural wonder with a rich cultural history. Here, you can explore ancient Aboriginal rock art and learn about the history of the Mirning people.

  2. Whale Watching Tours: One of the main attractions of Great Australian Bight Marine Park is its incredible whale watching tours. From June to September each year, thousands of southern right whales migrate to the park’s shores to breed and give birth.

Natural Attractions of Great Australian Bight Marine Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Head of Bight Lookout: Located on the Eyre Highway, the Head of Bight Lookout is one of the most spectacular viewpoints in the park. Here, you can enjoy panoramic views of the Bunda Cliffs and spot migratory whales from a distance.

  2. Point Labatt Conservation Park: Home to the largest colony of sea lions in Australia, Point Labatt Conservation Park is a must-visit destination for wildlife lovers. You can take a guided tour to observe these playful creatures up close.

Hiking/Biking routes of Great Australian Bight Marine Park

Great Australian Bight Marine Park is a hiker’s paradise, with several incredible hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the park’s must-visit trails:

  1. Munda Biddi Trail: This 1000km trail is perfect for experienced hikers and bikers looking for a challenging adventure. The trail meanders through several national parks, including Great Australian Bight Marine Park.

  2. Cape Borda Lightstation Walk: Offering breathtaking views of the Southern Ocean, the Cape Borda Lightstation Walk is a moderate hiking trail that takes you through some of the park’s most stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Great Australian Bight Marine Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and sightseeing, Great Australian Bight Marine Park offers several other thrilling activities, including:

  1. Surfing: With its pristine beaches and powerful swells, Great Australian Bight Marine Park is a surfer’s paradise. There are several great surf spots to choose from, including Cactus Beach and Venus Bay.

  2. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Great Australian Bight Marine Park’s crystal clear waters are perfect for snorkeling and scuba diving, with several incredible dive sites to explore.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Australian Bight Marine Park

Great Australian Bight Marine Park’s traditional owners, the Mirning people, have a deep cultural connection to the land and sea. Visitors to the park are encouraged to respect the Mirning people’s culture and traditions, and to leave the park’s natural beauty unspoiled.

Best Time to Visit Great Australian Bight Marine Park

The best time to visit Great Australian Bight Marine Park is from June to September, when the southern right whales migrate to the park’s shores to breed and give birth. The park is also less crowded during this period, making it a great time to enjoy its natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Australian Bight Marine Park

When visiting Great Australian Bight Marine Park, it’s important to follow certain rules and guidelines to protect the park’s natural beauty and wildlife. Here are our top do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  1. Respect the park’s wildlife and leave their habitats undisturbed.

  2. Follow the park’s camping and hiking regulations, and take care not to litter or damage the park’s natural environment.

Don’t:

  1. Touch or feed the park’s wildlife.

  2. Remove any natural or cultural artifacts from the park, including rocks, shells, and Aboriginal rock art.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Australian Bight Marine Park

While Great Australian Bight Marine Park is generally safe, visitors should be aware of potential hazards, including strong currents, dangerous wildlife, and extreme weather conditions. Visitors should always follow the park’s safety guidelines and take appropriate precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Great Australian Bight Marine Park

Visitors to Great Australian Bight Marine Park should take the necessary precautions to protect themselves from the sun and stay hydrated. Visitors should also be aware of potential health risks associated with swimming in seawater, including infections and skin irritations.
