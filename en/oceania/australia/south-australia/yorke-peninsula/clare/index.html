Introduction to Clare

Clare, located in Yorke Peninsula, South Australia, is a small town known for its wineries, natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage. This picturesque town is situated in the heart of the Clare Valley wine region, making it a must-visit destination for wine lovers. Its stunning landscapes, diverse flora and fauna, and rustic charm make it an ideal getaway for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Clare

Here are some of the places you must visit in Clare:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clare Valley Wine, Food & Tourism Centre: This center offers a comprehensive guide to the Clare Valley region, including local wine tastings and gourmet food experiences.
  • Rising Sun Hotel: This historic hotel is over 170 years old and has been carefully restored to its former glory. It is a significant landmark of the town, with its beautiful architecture and antique decor.
  • The Mintaro Maze: This stunning hedge maze has been called South Australia’s “most perplexing experience.” It is a fun activity for the whole family, who can explore the maze and its surrounding gardens.
  • Martindale Hall: This 19th-century Georgian mansion is now a museum showcasing the life of the wealthy back in the day, giving visitors a peek into the luxurious lifestyle of the past.

Natural Attractions of Clare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spring Gully Conservation Park: This park features picturesque walking trails, abundant wildlife, and a tranquil atmosphere.
  • Clare Valley Riesling Trail: This hiking and biking trail is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It passes through the Clare Valley’s stunning landscapes, vineyards, and wineries, offering breathtaking scenery at every turn.
  • Skillogalee Creek Nature Trail: This walking trail is a haven for bird watchers and nature lovers. Visitors can admire the natural beauty of the area, including the native plants and animals.
  • Sevenhill Cellars: This winery, operating since 1851, has a fascinating history and charm, and visitors can learn about its rich past while tasting some of the best wines in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clare

The Clare Valley Riesling Trail and Skillogalee Creek Nature Trail both offer excellent hiking and biking opportunities. The Clare Valley Riesling Trail is a 35 km trail that passes through seven towns, vineyards, and wineries. The Skillogalee Creek Nature Trail, on the other hand, is a short but picturesque walk ideal for birdwatchers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Clare: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours: The Clare Valley is known for its excellent wines, and there are various wine tasting tours available for visitors to this town.
  • Cycling Tours: The Clare Valley Riesling Trail is a popular destination for cycling enthusiasts, and there are several tours available that offer excellent cycling and sightseeing opportunities.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: Visitors can enjoy a breathtaking, scenic view of the Clare Valley’s vineyards and landscape while floating in a hot air balloon.
  • Relax at the Spa: Visitors to Clare can book a relaxing spa experience in one of the many luxury spas in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clare

Here is a week-long itinerary for those visiting Clare:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Clare Valley Wine, Food & Tourism Centre and taste some of the region’s wines.
Day 2 Explore the Skillogalee Creek Nature Trail and visit the iconic Martindale Hall Museum.
Day 3 Take a bicycle tour of the Riesling Trail and visit Sevenhill Cellars.
Day 4 Treat yourself to some relaxation time at a spa in Clare.
Day 5 Go hot air ballooning early in the morning and visit the Mintaro Maze.
Day 6 Spend some time tasting wine and gourmet food in Barossa Valley, less than an hour drive away from Clare.
Day 7 Take a scenic drive to the nearby township of Burra and explore its mining heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Clare

Clare is known for its wines, gourmet cuisine, and farm-to-table cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages when you visit Clare:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Duck breast with a cherry sauce and mashed potatoes.
  • Slow-cooked lamb shanks with root vegetables and wild mushroom sauce.
  • Ploughman’s board featuring local cheese and cured meats.

Local Beverages

  • Clare Valley Wine: This region is famous for its Riesling, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Shiraz wines.
  • Knappstein Beer: This beer is locally brewed and is favored amongst beer enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Clare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Clare Valley Wine: A bottle of wine from the Clare Valley is a must-buy for wine enthusiasts.
  • Otelia Shiraz Range: This award-winning wine is a standout and a must-try.
  • Local cheese and cured meats: These can be found at the Clare Valley Wine, Food & Tourism Centre and are ideal as souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Clare

The people of Clare are welcoming and friendly, and visitors must respect their local customs and culture. One of the unique customs of Clare is their attention to wine making, and visitors must respect the winemakers and appreciate the time and effort that goes into making the wine.

Best Time to Visit Clare

The best time to visit Clare is between March and May or September and November when the weather is milder and the crowds are smaller. During these months, the vineyards are bursting with color, and the scenic beauty of the region is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clare

Do:

  • Do visit some of the lesser-known wineries and vineyards in the region.
  • Do try some of the gourmet cuisine in the region, including cheese and cured meats.
  • Do bring comfortable walking shoes as this region is known for its scenic trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t overindulge in wine.
  • Don’t litter or disturb the fauna and flora.
  • Don’t disrespect the locals and their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clare

Clare is known for being a safe and welcoming town. Visitors need to be aware of the natural hazards when hiking or biking in the countryside, such as snakes and extreme weather conditions.

Health in Clare

Clare is known for its clean air, fresh produce, and pure drinking water. Visitors must take proper precautions to guard against the sun’s harmful rays by wearing protective clothing and applying sunscreen.

Getting to Clare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Clare via air, bus service, and car.

Airports Near Clare

The nearest airports to Clare are:

  • Adelaide Airport: This airport is approximately 1.5 hours drive from Clare.
  • Port Augusta Airport: This airport is approximately 2 hours drive from Clare.

Bus Stations Near Clare

The closest bus station is in Port Pirie and is approximately 30 minutes drive from Clare.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a car and drive to Clare. Driving is an excellent option for those who want the flexibility to explore the town and its surrounding areas without being reliant on public transportation.

How to Get Around in Clare

The best way to get around Clare is by car or bike as public transportation is not easily accessible. Car hire is available in Clare, and visitors can take guided bike tours to explore the trail systems.

Where to Go Next from Clare: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors may want to consider the following destinations while visiting Clare:

  • Barossa Valley: Famous for world-renowned wines, gourmet food, and stunning landscapes, this region is a mere 1-hour drive from Clare.
  • Adelaide: South Australia’s capital city offers endless attractions, including museums, art galleries, and world-class restaurants.

Conclusion

Clare is a charming, picturesque town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and culinary indulgence. With its surrounding vineyards and wineries, scenic vistas, and stunning hiking trails, Clare is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of modern life and connect with nature and history.
