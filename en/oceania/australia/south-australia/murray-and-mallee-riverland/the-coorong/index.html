Discover The Coorong: Your Ultimate Travel Guide

Welcome to The Coorong, a hidden gem of South Australia that boasts stunning natural scenery and rich indigenous culture. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see in The Coorong. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best tourist attractions, hiking routes, accommodations, and local customs of The Coorong. Get ready for an unforgettable adventure!

Introduction to The Coorong

The Coorong is a long, narrow lagoon that stretches for 130 km along the southern coast of South Australia. It’s a designated national park and wetland of international importance due to its diverse ecosystem of saltwater and freshwater habitats, sand dunes, and sea cliffs. The Ngarrindjeri people, who are the traditional owners of the land, have a rich cultural history and spiritual connection to The Coorong. Visitors to the area can experience both the natural beauty and cultural heritage of The Coorong.

List of Places to Visit in The Coorong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ngarrindjeri Cultural Centre: Learn about the traditions, language, and art of the Ngarrindjeri people through interactive exhibits and guided tours.
  2. Poltalloch Station Homestead Museum: Explore the historic homestead, shearers’ quarters, and blacksmith shop that give a glimpse into the early life of European settlers in the region.
  3. The Coorong National Park Visitor Centre: Get information on the park’s history, geography, and wildlife, and pick up brochures and maps for your outdoor adventures.

Natural Attractions of The Coorong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Murray Mouth: See where the Murray River meets the ocean and enjoy the stunning views of the sand dunes, lagoons, and beaches.
  2. Birdwatching: The Coorong is a bird lover’s paradise, with over 200 species of birds living in the area. Bring your binoculars and spot rare birds such as the Hooded Plover and Red-necked Avocet.
  3. Kayaking and Canoeing: Paddle through the tranquil lagoons and channels of The Coorong and get up close to the wildlife, including pelicans, swans, and dolphins.
  4. Fishing: Catch some Coorong Mullet or Mulloway and cook them up for a delicious seafood dinner.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Coorong

  1. The Heysen Trail: This 1200 km hiking trail passes through The Coorong and offers breathtaking views of the coastline, hills, and forests.
  2. The Coorong Beach Walk: Take a leisurely stroll along the beach and enjoy the sea breeze, sand dunes, and stunning sunsets.
  3. The Coorong Loop Cycling Trail: This 200 km trail is perfect for cyclists of all levels and takes you through picturesque landscapes of wetlands, sand hills, and coastal towns.

Best Things to Do in The Coorong: Activities and Tours

  1. The Spirit of the Coorong Cruise: Take a guided boat tour through the lagoons and channels of The Coorong and learn about its unique ecosystem and cultural heritage.
  2. Mundoo Island Station Tour: Visit a working sheep farm on the banks of the Murray River and see the sheep-shearing process, sheepdog demonstrations, and enjoy a BBQ lunch.
  3. Ngarrindjeri Art and Culture Tour: Join a tour led by indigenous guides and learn about the Ngarrindjeri culture, language, and traditional practices, including bush tucker tasting and didgeridoo playing.

Week-Long Itinerary for The Coorong

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Adelaide and drive to The Coorong. Check in to your accommodation and relax.
2 Visit the Ngarrindjeri Cultural Centre and Poltalloch Station Homestead Museum.
3 Take a Spirit of the Coorong Cruise and see the Murray Mouth and birdlife.

6 Visit Mundoo Island Station and experience sheep-shearing and a BBQ lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of The Coorong

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Coorong Mullet: A freshwater fish that’s caught locally and has a delicate, sweet flavor.
  2. Kangaroo: Try this lean, gamey meat in a stew, pie, or grilled.
  3. Marron: A freshwater crayfish that’s found in the Murray River and is delicious grilled or boiled.

Local Beverages

  1. Coorong Brewing Company: Try locally brewed beers, ciders, and wines at this family-owned brewery in Meningie.
  2. Coorong Fishery: Taste a range of Coorong seafood, including smoked mullet, marron pate, and fish cakes, that are sustainably caught and locally sourced.

Where to Stay in The Coorong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Coorong Waterfront Retreat: A luxury holiday home with four bedrooms, a private jetty, and panoramic views of Lake Albert.
  2. Camp Coorong: A camping and glamping site that offers a unique cultural experience with indigenous guides and Ngarrindjeri-inspired activities.
  3. Meningie’s Waterfront Motel: A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in The Coorong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ngarrindjeri Art: Support local artists by purchasing handmade paintings, sculptures, and jewelry that reflect the Ngarrindjeri culture and spirituality.
  2. Coorong Salt Company Products: Take home some natural, hand-harvested salt that’s produced in The Coorong and imbued with a unique mineral flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of The Coorong

Respect the Ngarrindjeri people and their customs by:

  • Seeking permission before entering sacred sites
  • Refraining from taking photographs of ceremonies or cultural practices
  • Using local tour operators and businesses that support indigenous communities
  • Learning about the Ngarrindjeri culture and history through local museums and interpretive signs

Best Time to Visit The Coorong

The best time to visit The Coorong is in the spring and autumn months (September to November and March to May), when the weather is mild and the birdlife is abundant. Avoid visiting in the summer months (December to February) due to hot temperatures and high humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Coorong

Do:

  • Bring sunscreen, a hat, and insect repellent for outdoor activities
  • Respect the natural environment and avoid disturbing wildlife habitats
  • Check the weather forecast and tidal conditions before kayaking or boating
  • Check for fire warnings and restrictions during the summer months

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or cigarette butts in the park
  • Swim in the ocean or lagoons due to dangerous currents and waves
  • Enter private property without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in The Coorong

  • Stay on designated hiking and biking trails to avoid getting lost or injured in the bush
  • Drink plenty of water and carry a first-aid kit during outdoor activities
  • Avoid swimming in the ocean or lagoons due to strong currents, waves, and potential shark sightings

Health in The Coorong

  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases such as Ross River Virus and Murray Valley Encephalitis
  • Drink bottled water or treat water from the tap or underground sources to avoid waterborne illnesses such as Giardia and Cryptosporidium

Getting to The Coorong: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Coorong is located approximately 2 hours southeast of Adelaide.

Airports Near The Coorong

  • Adelaide Airport (ADL)
  • Mount Gambier Airport (MGB)

Bus Stations Near The Coorong

  • Adelaide Central Bus Station (46 Franklin St, Adelaide)
  • Goolwa Bus Stop (20 Cutting Rd, Goolwa)

Other Options

  • Hire a car in Adelaide and drive to The Coorong
  • Join a guided tour or shuttle service from Adelaide

How to Get Around in The Coorong

The best way to get around The Coorong is by car or bicycle. There are several hire car and bike rental options available in Adelaide and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from The Coorong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. The Limestone Coast: Explore the rugged coastline, caves, and vineyards of this diverse region, located 2 hours east of The Coorong.
  2. Kangaroo Island: Visit this wildlife paradise that’s home to kangaroos, seals, and koalas, located 3 hours south of The Coorong.
  3. Adelaide Hills: Relax in this picturesque region that’s dotted with charming towns, wineries, and wildlife parks, located 1 hour west of The Coorong.

Conclusion

We hope this travel guide has inspired you to visit The Coorong and experience its unique natural and cultural attractions. Remember to respect the Ngarrindjeri people and their customs, and enjoy your adventure in this beautiful part of South Australia!
