Introduction to Woomera

Woomera, located in the Outback of South Australia, is a small town with a population of just over 1000 people. Historically known as the site of a British rocket-testing range, Woomera is now a unique destination in the heart of the Australian Outback that offers visitors a glimpse into the cultural and natural wonders of the area. This guide brings you through some of the best places to visit in Woomera, including outdoor adventures, museums, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Woomera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Woomera offers visitors a range of museums and landmarks that document the history of the area and the role it has played in Australia over the years. A visit to Woomera Heritage Centre is a must-do. The museum showcases past and present rocket testing, and Australia’s aviation history. You can also step out of the museum to learn about the Baaninji people and other Indigenous peoples.

Natural Attractions of Woomera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural landscape surrounding Woomera is breathtaking, from the red dirt of the Outback to the unique formations in the desert beyond. A popular outdoor adventure in the area is hiking. Some of the best routes include the Seven Sisters Walking Trail and the Kanku-Breakaways Conservation Park. Another popular activity is stargazing, as the area has been designated as an International Dark Sky Reserve by the International Dark-Sky Association.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Woomera

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Woomera boasts multiple trails that offer plenty of opportunities to explore and enjoy the natural beauty of the area. The Seven Sisters Walking Trail is a 12km hike through spectacular landscapes including mountains and gorges. A leisurely evening drive out from Woomera would take you through the Kanku-Breakaways Conservation Park where you will witness stunning views and colors all around you.

Best Things to Do in Woomera: Activities and Tours

Apart from the museums and outdoor activities, there are plenty of other things to do in Woomera. You can experience a sunset picnic at The Point where the locals recommend to experience the serenity and beauty of the Australian Outback. You could also take a scenic flight over the desert and witness the stark contrast of colors between the red desert and the deep blue sky. Additionally, you can also explore the nearby town of Roxby Downs and the picturesque town of Andamooka.

Week-Long Itinerary for Woomera

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Woomera:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Woomera. Visit the Woomera Heritage Centre and Museum for a comprehensive understanding of the significance of this location.

  • Day 2: Travel to the Kanku-Breakaways Conservation Park to go hiking and exploring the rugged nature and off-grid landscapes. Go star gazing at night.

  • Day 3: Take a scenic flight over the Woomera Prohibited Area and see the different types of airplanes and rockets that frequent Woomera.

  • Day 4: Drive to Andamooka Opal Capital, around 60 kilometers north of Woomera which is famous for opal mining and a unique underground lifestyle.

  • Day 5: Visit the Australian Arid Lands Botanic Garden and enjoy the picturesque landscapes and plants that are unique to the Outback. Take a walk on Ohlsen Beach and experience the stunning sunsets.

  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Roxby Downs, a vibrant mining town famous for copper and uranium deposits.

  • Day 7: Set in the morning to admire the stunning desert sunrise at The Point and soak in the serenity and beauty of the Australian Outback.

Food and Drink Scene of Woomera

Must-Try Dishes

Woomera has its distinctive cuisine with dishes that incorporate regional ingredients and multicultural influences. Some must-try dishes include the Outback Stew, Kangaroo steaks, and milkshakes made with the famous Golden North ice cream, which has been made in South Australia since 1923.

Local Beverages

South Australia is known for wine, and Woomera is no exception. The area is home to some excellent wineries, such as Sevenhill Cellars and Tim Adams Wines, where visitors can enjoy tastings and tours. Additionally, the area is famous for its locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Woomera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Woomera offers various accommodation options for visitors to suit all budgets and preferences. The Woomera Heritage Centre and Museum even offers accommodation in the former officer’s quarters - a unique experience. Other options include a choice of upscale accommodations such as the Eldo Hotel and the luxurious campgrounds at the Breakaway’s Tourist Park. Other accommodation options include apartments, motels, and hostels.

What to Buy in Woomera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Australia is known for its wine and wine-related products. Wine-tasting experiences and tours are popular in the area, making South Australian wine and olive products the perfect gift to bring home. Apart from this, tourists can buy native handicrafts and opal products from Andamooka but be sure to only purchase opals from approved dealers.

Local Culture and Customs of Woomera

Woomera has a rich and diverse culture, drawing on both its Indigenous heritage and its history as a site of rocket testing. The local Baaninji people, who have lived in the area for more than 30,000 years, have a deep connection with the land. Visitors to Woomera should be respectful of the cultural significance of the area and take the opportunity to learn about the Indigenous history and its traditions.

Best Time to Visit Woomera

The best time to visit Woomera is during the winter months, from May to September, when the temperature is milder. In the summer months, temperatures can reach upwards of 45°C. If you are keen to visit the area during the summer months, be sure to stay in air-conditioned accommodations and take precautions to avoid heat exhaustion.

Do’s and Don’ts of Woomera

Do:

  • Respect the local Indigenous communities and their culture.
  • Plan your travel arrangements in advance.
  • Take a guided tour in the prohibited areas and stay on designated trails.
  • Explore the walking trails and the natural landscape around Woomera.

Don’t:

  • Venture outside the designated areas.
  • Swim in the nearby bodies of water, many of which can have toxic algae or currents.
  • Take any images of restricted areas
  • Purchase opals from unapproved dealers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Woomera

Woomera is a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions to avoid accidents, especially when out in the open desert. Always respect the signage and only stick to designated areas to ensure personal safety and minimize environmental destruction.

Health in Woomera

The Outback of Australia is known for its vastness and remoteness and requires medical attention if any health incidents arise. Have adequate medical insurance and a well-prepared first-aid kit while traveling to the area, and take extra precautions to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Woomera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Woomera

The Woomera airport is located in the town center, and you can fly in directly. The nearest metropolitan airport is Adelaide International Airport, which is approximately 550 km away.

Bus Stations Near Woomera

Woomera Hotel offers a shuttle service for visitors who arrive without a vehicle.

Other Options

Visitors might want to rent a vehicle in Adelaide and self-drive to Woomera. The journey is around 7 hours and travels through scenic landscapes.

How to Get Around in Woomera

The best way to get around Woomera is by car or bicycle. Visitors can choose from varying transport options like car rentals and bike hire from the local services providers. Guided tours are popular, especially for first-time visitors to the area, as they offer an opportunity to explore the area’s natural beauty and cultural highlights.

Where to Go Next from Woomera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations include Roxby Downs, which is a vibrant mining town, and the beautiful landscapes of Andamooka. If you’re looking to explore further afar, the Flinders Ranges and the beautiful city of Adelaide are some of the excellent destinations to travel to.

Conclusion

Woomera is a unique destination that provides visitors with the opportunity to explore both the natural beauty of the Outback and the rocket and aviation history of the area. Whether it’s hiking in the mountains, exploring the museums, or simply stargazing, there’s something for everyone in Woomera. Plan ahead, take precautions, and enjoy your trip to this unique slice of the Australian Outback.
