Introduction to Marree

Located in the picturesque Outback region of South Australia, Marree is a town that’s rich in history, culture, and natural wonders. With its remote location, Marree offers a unique experience for those looking to explore the Australian wilderness and immerse themselves in the local culture. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best sights, sounds, and experiences that Marree has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Marree

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marree Railway Station Museum: This museum depicts the history of the Ghan Railway and its impact on Marree. It is one of the few remaining railway stations that still exist and is a great place to explore the town’s history.
  • Lake Eyre Yacht Club: This unique museum offers an insight into the life and livelihoods of the locals. It is dedicated to the history of sailing on the Marree’s Lake Eyre and provides interesting artifacts that paint a picture of the town’s past.
  • Marree Mosque: The architectural beauty of this 19th-century mosque is worth admiring. It is known to be the first mosque built in Australia and is still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Marree: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Eyre: This dry salt lake is the third largest lake in Australia and can delightfully turn into a thriving oasis in times of heavy rains. It is a must-visit for nature and wildlife lovers as it is home to rare bird species and other flora and fauna native to the area.
  • Flinders Ranges: The Flinders Ranges are a range of mountains that offer visitors an excellent opportunity to explore the Australian Outback’s rugged wilderness. The nearby Wilpena Pound scenic lookout provides a panoramic view of the hills.
  • Twin Hills: Twin Hills offers a mesmerizing look into the Australian Outback’s geological wonders. These massive rock formations are a must-visit for scenic views and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marree

  • Marree Walks: The Marree Walks offer fantastic scenic views, and you can explore the unique geological features and wildlife of the Outback. It’s the perfect destination for a day hike, and there are many different trails that vary in length and difficulty.
  • Bike Riding: The surrounding terrain of Marree is ideal for bike riding, and there are many tracks with varying difficulties for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Marree: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Safaris: One of the best ways to explore the Outback is by riding camels through it. Marree offers many camel safaris tours that will take you to remote and unique parts of the Outback.
  • 4WD and ATV Tours: Go off the beaten path and discover the secrets of the Australian desert with a 4WD or ATV tour. These tours provide an adrenaline-pumping adventure and a chance to enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
  • Stargazing: The remote location of Marree makes it the perfect spot for stargazing. The clarity of the sky is fantastic, and you can see myriad stars that are not visible in cities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marree

Day 1: Explore the town’s history on a guided tour of the Marree Railway Station and the Lake Eyre Yacht Club.

Day 2: Take a camel safari and explore the Lake Eyre region.

Day 3: Discover the Flinders Ranges National Park on a 4WD tour.

Day 4: Visit Twin Hills and enjoy a picnic with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 5: Enjoy a scenic flight over the Marree and the surrounding area.

Day 6: Go stargazing at night and take an astronomy class to get a closer look at the stars.

Day 7: Go on a bike riding adventure around the town, and then relax with a cup of tea at one of the local cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Marree

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kangaroo and Emu Meat Dishes: These meats are a typical delicacy in the Outback regions and are available on most menus.
  • Damper Bread: A traditional Australian bread that is cooked over the fire, an experience worth trying.

Local Beverages

  • Coopers Beer: A South Australian beer that is brewed with natural ingredients. It is a refreshing drink after a long day of hiking or biking.

Where to Stay in Marree: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Marree Hotel: A historical hotel that provides basic and comfortable accommodation for visitors.
  • Marree Oasis Hotel Motel: This motel offers clean and budget-friendly accommodation with a variety of rooms.

What to Buy in Marree: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The local markets are great places to find souvenirs, such as handmade Australian jewelry, Aboriginal art, and Australian-made bush tucker products.

Local Culture and Customs of Marree

The local culture of Marree is centered on Outback living and is heavily influenced by Aboriginal culture. It is important to be respectful of the local customs and traditions when visiting the area.

Best Time to Visit Marree

The best time to visit Marree is between May and September when the weather is mild and dry. Avoid visiting the town during summer when temperatures can reach more than 40°C and during Christmas and new year because most businesses are closed on these holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marree

Do:

  • Respect the local Aboriginal cultures and traditions.
  • Be mindful of the environment and take care of the surrounding area.
  • Bring plenty of water on hikes and outdoor excursions.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the local history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property
  • Leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marree

  • It is essential to carry plenty of water and sunscreen while exploring Marree’s countryside.
  • Check the weather forecast before embarking on any outdoor activities, as the weather can change quickly in the Outback.

Health in Marree

  • Healthcare is limited in the town, so it is advisable to bring any necessary medications with you.
  • Ensure that you drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summers.

Getting to Marree: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marree

  • The closest airport to Marree is Port Augusta Airport, which serves flights from cities all over Australia.

Bus Stations Near Marree

  • There are no bus stations near Marree.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is the best option to commute to Marree.

How to Get Around in Marree

The town is small, and walking around Marree is the best way to explore the area. The area outside the town is vast, and taxis or renting a car is advisable to go to places outside the town.

Where to Go Next from Marree: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Coober Pedy: A quirky town known for the underground living and its unique attractions.
  • Alice Springs: A city located at the heart of the Australian Outback, which offers many tourism opportunities and is known for its art and culture.

Conclusion

Marree offers a unique and authentic experience to explore the unspoiled Australian wilderness and immerse yourself in the local culture. The town’s rich history, culture, and natural wonders will make for an unforgettable trip. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re equipped with all the information you need to plan an exciting trip to Marree.
