Introduction to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

Located in South Australia, Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is a remote and awe-inspiring national park that is home to one of the most iconic natural features in Australia. Covering a total area of over 13,000 square kilometers, this park attracts visitors from all over the world who come to witness the stunning salt flats, and rugged landscapes that encapsulate the Outback.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore some of the top places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

Whether you’re interested in exploring the cultural history of the region or you’re an outdoor enthusiast looking to immerse yourself in nature, Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park has something for everyone. Below is a list of some of the top places to visit within the park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Andado Homestead: A historical homestead located on the eastern boundary of the park, this attraction provides an insight into the region’s pioneering history.
  • Muloorina Station: Once a livestock station, this site now acts as a tourist attraction, offering an insight into rural life in the Outback.
  • Marree and William Creek: Two iconic towns located outside the park boundaries, Marree and William Creek were both pivotal in the early pioneering and mining days of the region.

Natural Attractions of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Eyre: The highlight of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is undoubtedly Lake Eyre, the largest lake in Australia. Witness the vast salt flats and the unique wildlife that inhabits the area.
  • Mound Springs: These natural springs can only be found in the arid and semi-arid regions of the world, including Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park. The mound spring scenery is otherworldly and a must-see attraction.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

For those looking to explore the park on foot or bike, there are several hiking and biking routes that you can take, ranging from easy walks to challenging treks. Some of the most popular routes include the Warburton River Walk and the Oodnadatta Track.

Best Things to Do in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park: Activities and Tours

If you prefer to experience the park through guided tours and activities, there are several options available. From scenic flights to scenic drives, you can explore the park in a variety of ways. Below are just some of the activities and tours on offer:

  • Scenic Flights over Lake Eyre: Experience the extraordinary sight of Lake Eyre from above with a scenic flight that is perfect for avid photographers.
  • Scenic Drives: Enjoy scenic drives like the Oodnadatta Track, and Andado Track, that provides a fun and informative trip through the rugged Patom/Patamana land.
  • 4WD Adventure Tours: For a more adrenaline-packed experience, there are several 4WD tours that take you off-road for a unique adventure through the natural landscapes of the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

If you’re planning on spending a week in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park, a sample itinerary you may want to consider is:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Marree, explore the town, the old Ghan Railway, Marree Man and the area around.

  • Day 2: From Marree, head up the Birdsville Track and stop at Mungeranie and Coward Springs for lunch and an evening meal.

  • Day 3: Visit one of the Mound Springs near Coward Springs/Mungeranie. After lunch, drive to William Creek and explore the area around William Creek hotel.

  • Day 4: Scenic Flight over Lake Eyre (2 hours); Return to William Creek Hotel and explore the area, maybe participate in a sunset tour.

  • Day 5: Head to Coober Pedy or Oodnadatta located outside the park and spend the day exploring local mines.

  • Day 6: Return to the park and go on a 4WD tour around Painted Hills somewhere near Muloorina Station.

  • Day 7: Return to Marree.

Food and Drink Scene of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

The culinary scene in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is as unique as the landscape. Visitors can look forward to indulging in local delicacies such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Camel Burgers: Try a delicious camel burger in William Creek Hotel and enjoy the taste of the Outback.
  • Kangaroo steaks: Try a kangaroo steak with a side of bush tucker salad and wash it down with a glass of local wine.

Local Beverages

  • Coopers Ale: A favourite among locals, Coopers Ale is brewed in South Australia and is a must-try for any beer enthusiast.
  • BurraBrewa: A locally crafted beer that is unique to the region, a BurraBrewa is a perfect way to quench your thirst after a long hot day of exploring the park.

Where to Stay in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re planning on staying in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park, there is an array of accommodation options available to suit all budgets and preferences. Below are just a few options that can be considered:

  • William Creek Hotel: One of the only hotels located inside the national park, William Creek is a popular destination for visitors seeking a rustic Outback holiday experience.
  • The Prairie Hotel: Located just outside the national park, the Prairie Hotel is a great option for those looking for a more luxurious experience.
  • Campgrounds: There are several campgrounds throughout the park, such as the Cooinda Campground and Muloorina Station campground, which offer a more affordable and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a unique souvenir from your visit to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park, there are several local products that you may be interested in purchasing:

  • Aboriginal Art: Local Aboriginal artists create unique pieces that reflect the natural landscape of the region. You can get this at most souvenir shops and roadhouses.
  • Kangaroo Leather: Kangaroo leather products, from belts and wallets to boots are readily available throughout the region and make for a great souvenir to bring back home.
  • Local Wines: The region boasts several vineyards. Take home a bottle of the best local wine from the nearby region.

Local Culture and Customs of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

The Outback is home to a rich and diverse culture, and visitors to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local customs and traditions. The Indigenous people of the region, the Arabunna People, have lived in the area for thousands of years, and there are several cultural centers where visitors can learn more about their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

The best time to visit Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is during the cooler months of the year, from May to August. During this time, the temperature is more bearable, and there aren’t as many flies around.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

Do:

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the local Indigenous people
  • Stay on designated hiking and biking trails
  • Bring plenty of water with you when exploring the park

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife
  • Leave any trash or waste behind
  • Ignore the signs and regulations of the park

Safety and Health

Safety in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

Travelling in the Outback can present certain hazards, and visitors are advised to take care when exploring the park. It is essential that you carry sufficient water, and that you inform someone of your travel plans before setting out.

Health in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

It is recommended that you consult a physician before travelling to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of sunburn and heatstroke.

Getting to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park can be a bit of an adventure in itself. There are several transportation options to choose from.

Airports Near Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

The closest airports to Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park are located in Adelaide or Alice Springs. From there, you can hire a car or take a tour bus to the national park.

Bus Stations Near Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

Greyhound Australia has bus services from major cities like Adelaide and Alice Springs that travel to Marree, William Creek, and Oodnadatta.

Other Options

The Outback has a magnificent and diverse network of driving experiences for visitors willing to undertake longer road journeys.

How to Get Around in Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park

The best way to explore Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is by hiring a 4WD and taking a guided tour of the park. Alternatively, if you have your vehicle, you can easily access the park through the Oodnadatta Track and William Creek.

Where to Go Next from Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the South Australian Outback, there are several nearby destinations that you may want to consider, including:

  • Flinders Ranges National Park: Located to the south of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park, Flinders Ranges is a magnificent landscape with rugged mountain ranges and stunning scenic drives.

  • Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park: Located further north of Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park, Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park is home to the iconic Uluru rock formation and is one of the most popular destinations in Australia.

Conclusion

Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is a stunning and unique destination that offers visitors an unforgettable experience that they will cherish for years to come. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural exploration, this park has something for everyone.
