Introduction to Kangaroo Island

Located off the coast of South Australia, Kangaroo Island is a popular destination for visitors seeking adventure, relaxation, and unique wildlife experiences. Endowed with picturesque natural landscapes, cultural landmarks, and a sustainably-cultivated food and wine scene, Kangaroo Island has something for everyone. In this guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, things to do, local culture, food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Kangaroo Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kangaroo Island has a rich history and has preserved it’s cultural heritage with a number of museums and historical landmarks.

Kangaroo Island Museum

The Kangaroo Island museum has a collection of over 7,000 items that showcase the life and times of the island’s pioneers.

Cape Borda Lighthouse

Cape Borda Lighthouse was the third lighthouse built in South Australia and played an important role in guiding ships around Kangaroo Island.

Penneshaw Maritime and Folk Museum

The Penneshaw Maritime and Folk Museum takes visitors on a journey of Kangaroo Island’s maritime history.

Natural Attractions of Kangaroo Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whether you are a nature enthusiast, adrenaline junkie, or just want to soak in some of Australia’s most beautiful scenery, Kangaroo Island has plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore.

Flinders Chase National Park

This national park features some of the most scenic areas of Kangaroo Island, including the Remarkable Rocks, Admirals Arch, and the Hanson Bay Wildlife Sanctuary.

Kelly Hill Caves

If you are looking for unique underground adventures, Kelly Hill Caves are a must-visit. The tour of these limestone caves is educational and breathtaking.

Kangaroo Island Wilderness Trail

This has been named as one of Australia’s Great Walks. It is a five-day trek that takes visitors through spectacular landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kangaroo Island

If you want to explore Kangaroo Island on foot or by bike, there are many trails to choose from.

Trails for Walking

  • Kangaroo Island Wilderness Trail
  • Cape Willoughby Lighthouse Walk
  • Admirals Arch Walking Trail

Trails for Biking

  • The Pack Track
  • Flinders Chase Coastal Trek
  • Cape Borda Lighthouse Highway

Best Things to Do in Kangaroo Island: Activities and Tours

Visit the Seal Bay Conservation Park

The park is home to 1,000 Australian sea lions. Visitors can take guided tours to view these majestic creatures in their natural habitats.

Go Sandboarding at Little Sahara

Little Sahara features sand dunes, and is the ideal place for sandboarding. Visitors can rent sandboards from local shops.

Experience the Southern Ocean Lodge

The Southern Ocean Lodge is a luxury hotel with spectacular views of the ocean. Guests can enjoy guided tours and other activities, as well as gourmet food and wine experiences.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kangaroo Island

Day 1: Visit Cape Willoughby Lighthouse and Scenic Coastal Drive Day 2: Wildlife Encounters and Kangaroo Island Wilderness Trail Day 3: Sandboarding at Little Sahara and Flinders Chase National Park Day 4: Penneshaw Maritime and Folk Museum, and Kingscote Farmers’ Markets Day 5: Admirals Arch and Kangaroo Island Gin Distillery Day 6: Bird Watching, Pelican Feeding, and Snorkeling at Pelican Lagoon Day 7: Final Walk and Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Kangaroo Island

Must-Try Dishes

King George Whiting

A local seafood delicacy, King George whiting is a must-try dish. Try it grilled or battered.

Ligurian Honey

Kangaroo Island is home to a unique strain of bees that produce Ligurian honey. This honey has a distinct flavour and is renowned for its medicinal properties.

Oyster Bay Oysters

Oyster Bay Oysters are farmed in a clean and pristine environment. Taste them fresh from a local oyster farm.

Local Beverages

Kangaroo Island Spirits

Kangaroo Island Spirits is a popular gin distillery that produces high-quality spirits with local botanicals.

Islander Estate Vineyards

This winery produces some of the best red wines in Australia using local Kangaroo Island grapes.

Where to Stay in Kangaroo Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Ocean Lodge

This luxury hotel offers a spectacular view of the ocean. Visitors can enjoy a gourmet food and wine experience, as well as other activities and guided tours.

Kangaroo Island Wilderness Retreat

Located on the edge of Flinders Chase National Park, this accommodation offers a 5-star eco-retreat experience with a focus on sustainability.

Seafront Holiday Park

Seafront Holiday Park offers caravan sites, cabins, and campsites. It is a great option for families and budget-conscious travellers.

What to Buy in Kangaroo Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Honey and Honey Products

Ligurian honey and other honey products are a great souvenir to bring back home from your Kangaroo Island trip.

Kangaroo Island Wines

With more than 30 wine growers and over 12 cellar doors, Kangaroo Island produces some of the best wines in Australia.

Local Art and Craftwork

Kangaroo Island has a thriving arts and crafts community, and you can bring home some unique souvenirs from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Kangaroo Island

Kangaroo Island has a rich Aboriginal culture, and visitors can learn about the island’s indigenous history and customs at the Aboriginal Cultural Center. The island also has a unique farming and fishing culture, which can be experienced firsthand with local food and wine producers and fisherman.

Best Time to Visit Kangaroo Island

Kangaroo Island is a year-round destination. However, the best time to visit is from September to May, when the temperature is mild and rainfall is lower. December to February is the peak tourist season, so book well in advance if you plan to visit during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kangaroo Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife by not littering.
  • Learn about the local Aboriginal culture and history.
  • Try local food and drink experiences, and support local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Collect shells, rocks, or any natural resources.
  • Wear camouflage clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kangaroo Island

Kangaroo Island is safe for tourists. However, visitors should follow basic safety precautions when exploring natural attractions, such as staying on marked trails, not swimming in hazardous water, and carrying plenty of water and other essentials when hiking or biking.

Health in Kangaroo Island

Kangaroo Island has medical services, but visitors should carry their prescription medications and take care to protect themselves from the harsh sun and other environmental factors.

Getting to Kangaroo Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kangaroo Island

Kangaroo Island Airport is the main airport on the island, with regular flights from Adelaide.

Bus Stations Near Kangaroo Island

Public transport is limited on the island, with only a few bus services running on popular routes. Visitors can also hire cars or bikes to explore Kangaroo Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Kangaroo Island via ferries or private boats from Cape Jervis.

How to Get Around in Kangaroo Island

The best way to explore Kangaroo Island is by hiring a car or bike. Visitors can also book guided tours with local tour operators.

Where to Go Next from Kangaroo Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Kangaroo Island, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Adelaide, the wine region of Barossa Valley, and the Flinders Ranges are all within reach by car or plane.

Conclusion

Kangaroo Island has something for everyone, from adventurous wildlife encounters to luxurious food and wine experiences. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip with confidence and truly make the most of your time on this beautiful island.
