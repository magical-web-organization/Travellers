The Suburbs is a neighborhood located in the northeastern region of Perth, the capital city of Western Australia. This suburban area is known for its lush greenery, picturesque views, and laid-back lifestyle. This travel guide aims to provide you with a comprehensive tour of The Suburbs, detailing its top attractions, best activities, food and drink scene, and local customs to help you plan your trip.

Introduction to The Suburbs

The Suburbs is an affluent area that offers a peaceful and serene environment perfect for anyone who wants to take a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. The neighborhood is filled with towering trees, scenic parks, and beautiful gardens, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in The Suburbs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Burswood Park: A beautiful and sprawling park that features stunning views of the Swan River and the city skyline.

  • Kings Park and Botanic Garden: A massive 400-hectare park that includes several gardens, open spaces, and trails. Here you can enjoy stunning views of the city, discover unique flora and fauna, and visit the State War Memorial.

  • Perth Zoo: Located inside South Perth’s parkland, Perth Zoo is a beautiful and interesting place to visit if you love wildlife. The zoo has over 1,200 animals of over 160 species that you can see and interact with.

Natural Attractions of The Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Swan River: A majestic river that flows through Perth, offering breathtaking views and numerous water activities such as kayaking, fishing, and sailing.

  • Matilda Bay Reserve: A peaceful and scenic park located on the banks of the Swan River. Here you can enjoy picnics, barbecues, and stroll along the riverside walking tracks.

  • Lake Monger: A picturesque lake known for its abundant birdlife, where visitors can take a leisurely stroll or jogging along the walkway that surrounds the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Suburbs

  • Bibbulmun Track: This 1000 km hiking trail runs from Albany to Perth CBD and passes through The Suburbs.

  • Munda Biddi Trail: A 1,000 km cycling trail that runs from Mundaring to Albany and passes through The Suburbs.

Best Things to Do in The Suburbs: Activities and Tours

  • Outdoor Cinema: Luna Outdoor Cinema in Leederville showcases the latest blockbusters and classic movies under the stars.

  • The Perth Mint: One of the oldest coin-minting facilities in Australia that offers visitors a glimpse into the minting processes and historical coins of the country.

  • Segway Tours: Glide through The Suburbs on a Segway and enjoy the scenic views while taking in the breeze.

Week-Long Itinerary for The Suburbs

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Kings Park and Botanic Garden and stroll along the parks and gardens.
Day 2 Explore Swan River, take a scenic boat ride or go fishing.
Day 3 Visit Perth Zoo and see the animals up close.
Day 4 Take a cycling tour along the Munda Biddi Trail.
Day 5 Visit the Perth Mint and learn about the history of Australian coins.
Day 6 Watch a movie at the Luna Outdoor Cinema.
Day 7 Enjoy a Segway tour and experience The Suburbs from a different perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of The Suburbs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Try some of the best fish and chips in Perth at the Bluewater Grill in Applecross.

  • Laksa: Laksa is a spicy and sour soup that is popular in Malaysia and Singapore. Laksa Café serves some of the best laksa in The Suburbs.

  • Pies and Pastries: The Three Coins Bakery is famous for its savory pies and pastries, which you can enjoy with coffee or tea.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Swan Valley is home to several wineries where you can savor local wines. Taste local award-winning wines at the Lancaster Winery, Ugly Duckling Wines, and many others.

  • Beer: The Suburbs has several microbreweries producing quality craft beer. Visit the Feral Brewing Company, Mash Brewing, and Perth Billiards Centre for some local brews.

Where to Stay in The Suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rendezvous Hotel Perth Scarborough: This luxurious hotel is located on Scarborough Beach and gives you a stunning view of the beach and the Indian Ocean.

  • Adina Apartment Hotel Perth Barrack Plaza: This sleek hotel is located in the CBD and offers modern furniture and appliances.

  • Sage Hotel West Perth: This hotel features modern and contemporary furnishings and is situated less than 10 minutes away from the CBD.

What to Buy in The Suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Australian Wine: Swan Valley Wine region produces some of Australia’s best wines, including Shiraz and Chardonnay. Take home a bottle or two as a souvenir.

  • Artisanal Foods: Get some jams, chutneys, and preserves made entirely from Western Australian fruits at Kookery Café and Kitchen.

  • Souvenirs: Shops such as The Little Posy Co. offers beautiful and affordable bouquets made from local flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of The Suburbs

The Suburbs is a welcoming and multicultural community. The area was initially a farming community but has now become a residential area. Western Australia culture is known for its love of outdoor activities, and its residents enjoy spending time outdoors, be it cycling, hiking, or barbecuing. The people of The Suburbs pride themselves on their relaxed and laidback lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit The Suburbs

The best time to visit The Suburbs is between March and May or September and November. During these months, the weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities. Additionally, these months also coincide with events such as the Perth International Jazz Festival, the Royal Show, and the Fringe Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Suburbs

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

  • Do observe environmental conservation practices that keep the natural beauty of The Suburbs.

  • Do take out travel insurance before your trip

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the natural environment.

  • Don’t feed the wildlife, as it can upset the delicate ecosystem.

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun.

Safety and Health

Safety in The Suburbs

The Suburbs is generally a safe area, with low crime rates and efficient law enforcement. It is advisable to stay aware of your surroundings and not leave valuables unattended.

Health in The Suburbs

The Suburbs has several medical centers, pharmacies, and hospitals that offer medical assistance. It is advisable to carry your travel insurance and ensure that you have access to medical assistance, if needed.

Getting to The Suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near The Suburbs

The Perth Airport is the nearest airport to the suburb, located about 20 km southwest of the city. From the airport, you can take a taxi or use public transportation.

Bus Stations Near The Suburbs

The suburb is well-connected via public transport, including buses and trains. You can use Transperth Bus Service to reach any destination in the suburb.

Other Options

You can hire a car or book a taxi to travel to The Suburbs from the Perth Airport or the city.

How to Get Around in The Suburbs

The Suburbs is a walkable area, with several pedestrian-friendly pathways. You can also rent bicycles, use public transport or hire a taxi to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from The Suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring The Suburbs, you may want to visit nearby destinations like Perth CBD, Rottnest Island, and Swan Valley Wineries.

Conclusion

The Suburbs in Perth, Western Australia, is a hidden gem that offers stunning views, natural attractions, and exciting activities. This guide aims to help you experience the best that the suburb has to offer and make your visit memorable. Whether you love the outdoors, cultural experiences, or good food, The Suburbs has something for everyone.
