Introducing Fitzgerald River National Park

Fitzgerald River National Park is a nature lover’s paradise in Western Australia. This national park was established in 1973 and is spread over an area of 3,501 square kilometers. The park is known for its stunning landscapes, rare and endangered flora and fauna, and cultural significance. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, surfing, fishing, camping, and many other activities.

Best Time to Visit Fitzgerald River National Park

The best time to visit Fitzgerald River National Park is from March to May and from September to November. During these periods, the temperatures are mild, and the wildflowers bloom, making it the best time for photography.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fitzgerald River National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the cultural values of the Aboriginal people.
  2. Stay on designated trails and roads.
  3. Carry plenty of water and food.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or vandalize.
  2. Venture off-trail, as it may damage the fragile ecosystem.
  3. Feed the wildlife.

How to Get Around in Fitzgerald River National Park

The best way to get around Fitzgerald River National Park is by car. The park’s roads are well maintained and accessible by 2WD vehicles, but some trails require 4WD vehicles. Visitors can also explore on foot or by bicycle.
