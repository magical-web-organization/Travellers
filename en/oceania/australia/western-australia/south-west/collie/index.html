Collie, located in the South West region of Western Australia, is a picturesque town offering a diverse range of tourist attractions. This charming town is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and adventurous outdoor activities. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover or adrenaline junkie, Collie has something exciting in store for everyone.

Introduction to Collie

Collie is situated approximately 200 km south of Perth, Western Australia, and is an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The town is surrounded by scenic hills, deep gorges, and lush forests that make it one of the most beautiful towns in Australia. With a rich tapestry of history and culture, Collie is a must-visit destination for people traveling to Western Australia.

List of Places to Visit in Collie

Home to an array of scenic attractions, Collie offers a vast range of activities and places to visit. Here’s a list of some of the top attractions in Collie:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Collie Art Gallery - Showcasing local art exhibitions and workshops throughout the year.
  • Coalfields Museum - Relive the rich history of Collie’s coalfields through interactive exhibits and guided tours.
  • Roelands Mission Farm - Explore the cultural significance of the Aboriginal community and their way of life.

Natural Attractions of Collie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wellington National Park - Explore the rugged terrain of the South West region, hike to the top of Mount Lennard for stunning panoramic views.
  • Black Diamond Lake - Enjoy water activities like fishing, boating, and swimming in the serene lake.
  • Collie River Valley Tourist Park - Take a leisurely stroll along the river or have fun with the on-site gaming facility.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Collie

Collie offers a range of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the diverse flora and fauna of the region. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Bibbulmun Track - A 1,000-kilometer-long trail that leads through the heart of South-Western Australia, ideal for seasoned hikers and backpackers.
  • Stockton Lake Loop Trail - A short and easy trail that winds its way through the beautiful Stockton Lake.
  • Harris Dam Wall Trail - A peaceful walk along the Harris Dam wall, featuring breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Collie: Activities and Tours

Collie offers a range of activities that will keep you captivated throughout your stay. Here are a few of the top things to do:

  • Horse Riding Adventure - Explore Collie’s picturesque countryside on horseback at Old Surrey Farm.
  • Collie Speedway - Experience the thrill of fast and furious car racing at the Collie Speedway.
  • 4WD Adventures - Take an off-road adventure through Collie’s rugged terrain with Adventurous 4WD Tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Collie

Planning a week-long itinerary for your Collie trip will allow you to explore the best of what this beautiful town has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your visit:

Day 1: Explore Collie’s history and culture at Roelands Mission Farm and Coalfields Museum.

Day 2: Take a leisurely stroll along Black Diamond Lake and enjoy some water activities.

Day 3: Go on a hike on the Bibbulmun Track to explore the stunning natural beauty of the region.

Day 4: Have some fun at the Collie River Valley Tourist Park and enjoy their gaming facility.

Day 5: Take an off-road adventure through Collie’s rugged terrain with Adventurous 4WD Tours.

Day 6: Experience the thrill of fast and furious car racing at the Collie Speedway.

Day 7: End your trip by exploring Collie’s art scene at the Collie Art Gallery.

Food and Drink Scene of Collie

Collie has a vibrant and diverse culinary scene with plenty of exciting places to dine out. Here is our top pick for food and drink:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Brisket at Colliefields Restaurant & Bar - A delicious dish made with local beef and secret spices.
  • Fish and Chips at The Coalfields Café - A must-try dish that features locally caught fish and hand-cut fries.

Local Beverages

  • The Colliefields Wine Range - Locally produced wine that is available at Colliefields Restaurant & Bar.
  • Collie Brewing Company - Delicious craft beer brewed locally in Collie that is available at several pubs and bars in town.

Where to Stay in Collie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Collie offers visitors plenty of accommodation options to suit all sorts of budgets and preferences:

  • Dovers Motel - A comfortable and affordable motel located in the heart of Collie.
  • Collie Ridge Motel - Offers quality and affordable accommodation and is a short drive from the town centre.
  • Kyoti Retreat - A serene and tranquil retreat offering glamping tents and lodging facilities.

What to Buy in Collie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Collie offers a range of locally made products that make for excellent souvenirs. Here’s a list of some of our favourite:

  • Collie Crafts and Collectables - Offers a range of locally handcrafted items that are perfect for souvenirs.
  • Collie River Valley Visitor Centre - Has an array of merchandise and locally made products.

Local Culture and Customs of Collie

Collie has a rich tapestry of history that is embedded in its culture and customs. The town has a strong Aboriginal culture that is celebrated and respected throughout the community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s heritage and its customs.

Best Time to Visit Collie

The best time to visit Collie is between September and November when the weather is warm and dry, allowing visitors to enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the town. Moreover, The Collie Show is held in November, which is a great time to visit Collie.

Do’s and Don’ts of Collie

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of Collie.
  • Take care of the environment and natural beauty of the town.
  • Engage with the local community and learn about their life and cultures.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect or disregard the Aboriginal culture and heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Collie

Collie is generally a safe and friendly town, but visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Keep valuable objects out of sight and locked in your car.
  • Keep your doors and windows locked during your stay.
  • Stay on designated tracks and trails when exploring the natural environment.

Health in Collie

Collie has an excellent health system with a range of medical facilities available. However, visitors should be aware of some potential health hazards, like the risk of snake bites when exploring the natural environment.

Getting to Collie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Collie is located approximately 200 km south of Perth and can be easily accessed by road and air. Here are the best transportation options for getting to Collie:

Airports Near Collie

  • Perth International Airport - The nearest international airport which is approximately 200 km from Collie.

Bus Stations Near Collie

  • TransWA Bus Service - Regular services are available from Perth to Collie.

Other Options

  • Self-drive - Collie is easily accessible by car, with road connections from Perth.

How to Get Around in Collie

Collie is a small town and is easy to navigate on foot, but for those who want some independence, there are several options:

  • Car Rentals - Several car rental companies operate in Collie and offer affordable deals to tourists.
  • Biking - Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the town and its surroundings.
  • Taxi - Taxi services are also available in Collie and can be booked in advance or found at designated taxi stands.

Where to Go Next from Collie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Collie is a fantastic base to explore the rest of the South West region of Western Australia. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Bunbury - A coastal city located approximately 80 km west of Collie that offers a range of cultural and outdoor activities.
  • Balingup - A charming town located approximately 50 km south of Collie that is rich in history and cultural significance.
  • Wellington Dam - A famous dam located approximately 30 km from Collie that offers stunning scenic views and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Collie with its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and adventurous outdoor activities is a must-visit destination in Western Australia. With a range of attractions to choose from, visitors will be spoilt for choice. Plan your trip to Collie today and experience the magic of this beautiful town!
