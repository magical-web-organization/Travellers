Introduction to Dunsborough

Located in the heart of South West, Western Australia, Dunsborough is a charming coastal town renowned for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning scenery. With a laid-back atmosphere and a host of outdoor activities on offer, Dunsborough is the ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers looking for a relaxing getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Dunsborough

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Dunsborough. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich history and culture or getting out into nature, there’s something here for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in history and culture, Dunsborough offers a variety of attractions, including museums, galleries, and historical sites.

  • Cape Naturaliste Lighthouse: One of the most iconic landmarks in the area, the Cape Naturaliste Lighthouse offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding coastline and an insight into the history of the area.
  • Ngilgi Cave: One of the largest show caves in the region, Ngilgi Cave offers an unforgettable underground adventure and a glimpse into the traditional owners of the land, the Wardandi people.
  • Dunsborough Country Club Museum: Explore the history of the town and the surrounding area at the Dunsborough Country Club Museum. Displays range from artifacts from the early settlers to modern-day memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Dunsborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to get out into nature and experience the natural beauty of the area, Dunsborough has plenty to offer. From hiking and biking trails to stunning beaches and coastline, there’s something for everyone.

  • Meelup Beach: One of the most picturesque beaches in the area, Meelup Beach boasts crystal-clear waters, white sands, and stunning views of the surrounding coastline.
  • Geographe Bay: Encompassing 30 kilometers of coastline, Geographe Bay offers a range of activities, including swimming, snorkeling, and fishing.
  • Yallingup Beach: One of the most popular surfing spots in the area, Yallingup Beach offers some of the best waves in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunsborough

With its stunning natural landscapes and beautiful trails, Dunsborough and its surrounding region is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Cape to Cape Track: Stretching for 135 kilometers between Cape Naturaliste and Cape Leeuwin, the Cape to Cape Track is one of Australia’s most iconic hiking trails.
  • Leeuwin-Naturaliste National Park: Home to an extensive network of hiking trails, the Leeuwin-Naturaliste National Park offers spectacular scenery and a chance to see native wildlife.
  • Meelup Reserve Trail: This 5-kilometer trail loops around the stunning Meelup Beach and offers breathtaking views of the coastline along the way.

Best Things to Do in Dunsborough: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some adventure or just want to relax and take in the sights, Dunsborough has something for everyone.

  • Whale Watching Tours: From September to December, humpback and southern right whales can be seen off the coast of Dunsborough. Take a whale-watching tour and witness these majestic creatures up close.
  • Wine Tours: The Margaret River wine region is world-renowned, and Dunsborough is the perfect base to explore it. Take a wine tour and sample some of the region’s finest wines.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: With its crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life, Dunsborough is a fantastic destination for snorkeling and diving. The navy pier is particularly popular with divers, with plenty of colorful fish and other creatures to be seen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunsborough

Here’s a suggested itinerary for exploring Dunsborough and the surrounding region over the course of a week:

Day Activity
1 Explore Dunsborough town and visit the Dunsborough Country Club Museum
2 Take a hike or bike ride in the Leeuwin-Naturaliste National Park
3 Visit Ngilgi Cave and take a whale-watching tour
4 Spend the day relaxing at Meelup Beach or Yallingup Beach
5 Take a wine tour of the Margaret River wine region
6 Go snorkeling or diving at the navy pier
7 Explore the Cape Naturaliste Lighthouse and take a coastal walk

Food and Drink Scene of Dunsborough

Dunsborough has a fantastic food and drink scene, with plenty of local produce and world-class wines on offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Margaret River venison: This lean, tender meat is a local specialty and can be found on menus throughout the region.
  • Western rock lobster: This sweet, delicate seafood is a must-try when visiting Dunsborough.
  • Caramel Slice: A classic Australian dessert made with biscuit, caramel, and chocolate layers, this indulgent treat is perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Margaret River Wines: The region is famous for its wines and, particularly, its cabernet sauvignon and chardonnay varieties.
  • Breweries: Dunsborough has a thriving craft beer scene, with a variety of breweries located in the area.
  • Coffee: Dunsborough has a range of cafes serving up fantastic coffee made with locally roasted beans.

Where to Stay in Dunsborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury or budget accommodation, Dunsborough has plenty of options to choose from.

  • Luxury: Pullman Bunker Bay Resort boasts stunning views of the Indian Ocean and offers top-notch amenities.
  • Mid-Range: Bayshore Beachside Resort offers spacious apartments and easy access to the beach and town center.
  • Budget: Dunsborough Central Motel offers affordable accommodation in the heart of town.

What to Buy in Dunsborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a souvenir or a gift to bring home? Dunsborough has plenty of options, including:

  • Handmade glassware: Dunsborough is home to a number of glassblowers producing stunning handmade pieces.
  • Local honey: The region’s native flora produces some of the finest honey in the world, and it’s easy to find in Dunsborough.
  • Indigenous art: The area has a rich Indigenous history and culture, and you can find a range of traditional art and crafts for sale.

Local Culture and Customs of Dunsborough

The traditional owners of the land around Dunsborough are the Wardandi people, and their culture and customs have played a significant role in shaping the region’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Dunsborough

The best time to visit Dunsborough is during the spring and summer months (September to February), when the weather is warm and the days are long.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunsborough

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Dunsborough, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and pack out your trash.
  • Learn about the local culture and history.
  • Be mindful of other travelers and locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural or cultural attractions.
  • Swim in areas where signs indicate it is unsafe.
  • Take artifacts or souvenirs from natural or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dunsborough

Dunsborough is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always wise to take basic precautions, such as locking your car and keeping an eye on your belongings. Be aware of the hazardous marine life present in the waters around Dunsborough, such as sharks and jellyfish.

Health in Dunsborough

Dunsborough has a modern medical center, and there are also pharmacies, dental clinics, and other healthcare facilities in the area.

Getting to Dunsborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dunsborough

The nearest airport to Dunsborough is Busselton Regional Airport, located approximately 15 minutes away by car. Perth Airport is around a three-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Dunsborough

South West Coach Lines operate daily coach services from Perth to Dunsborough.

Other Options

Car rental is available in Perth if you prefer to drive yourself.

How to Get Around in Dunsborough

Dunsborough is a compact town that’s easily walkable, and many of the area’s attractions are within cycling distance. Buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk or ride.

Where to Go Next from Dunsborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Western Australia, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from, including:

  • Margaret River: Just a short drive from Dunsborough, Margaret River is home to some of the world’s best wineries, fantastic beaches, and beautiful scenery.
  • Cape Leeuwin: Located at the southernmost point of Western Australia, Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse offers stunning views and a glimpse into the area’s maritime history.
  • Perth: Western Australia’s vibrant capital city is a three-hour drive from Dunsborough and offers a range of attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters.

Conclusion

Dunsborough is a hidden gem within Western Australia that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures and natural attractions to a vibrant food and drink scene and rich culture and history. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip and make memories to last a lifetime.
