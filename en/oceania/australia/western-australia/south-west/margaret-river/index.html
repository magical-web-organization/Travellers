Introduction to Margaret River

Located in the South West region of Western Australia, Margaret River is a picturesque town that is known for its premium wine, rugged coastline, and natural beauty. The region is a popular destination for honeymooners, families, and outdoor enthusiasts, and offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Margaret River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Margaret River Cultural Centre

Located in the heart of Margaret River, the Margaret River Cultural Centre is a multi-purpose venue that hosts a variety of cultural events, including art exhibitions, theatre productions, and concerts.

Australian War Memorial

The Australian War Memorial pays tribute to the ANZACs who fought in World War I and World War II. The memorial was built by a group of veterans in 1958 and is located in the town center.

Natural Attractions of Margaret River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cape-to-Cape Track

The 135-kilometre Cape-to-Cape Track stretches from Cape Naturaliste to Cape Leeuwin and offers some of the most breathtaking coastal views in Australia. Hikers can choose to complete the entire trail, which takes about five to six days, or opt for shorter sections.

Lake Cave

Located in the Boranup Forest, Lake Cave is one of the most popular natural attractions in Margaret River. Visitors can take guided tours of the cave and marvel at its stunning stalactites, stalagmites, and crystal formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Margaret River

Meelup Reserve Trail

The Meelup Reserve Trail is a 7-kilometre hiking trail that offers stunning views of Geographe Bay and the surrounding countryside. The trail is suitable for all skill levels and is a great way to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Margaret River: Activities and Tours

Wine Tasting

Margaret River is home to over 120 wineries and is one of the world’s top wine regions. Visitors can explore the various wineries and taste some of Australia’s best wines, including Cabernet Sauvignon, Shiraz, and Chardonnay.

Whale Watching

Between June and December, humpback and southern right whales migrate along Margaret River’s coastline. Visitors can take whale watching tours and observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Margaret River

Must-Try Dishes

Marron

Marron is a freshwater crayfish that is native to Western Australia. It is a delicacy in Margaret River and is usually served grilled or in a creamy sauce.

Kangaroo

Kangaroo is a lean and flavorful meat that is native to Australia. It is served in many restaurants in Margaret River and is often paired with local wines.

Local Beverages

Wine

Margaret River is known for its premium wines, particularly its Cabernet Sauvignon, Shiraz, and Chardonnay. Visitors can taste wines at the various wineries in the region.

Craft Beer

The craft beer scene in Margaret River has been steadily growing in recent years. Visitors can sample local brews at one of the many breweries in the area, such as Colonial Brewing Co. and Cowaramup Brewing Co.

Where to Stay in Margaret River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luxury Hotels

Pullman Bunker Bay Resort

Located in the Margaret River Wine Region, the Pullman Bunker Bay Resort is a beachfront resort that features a pool, spa, and restaurant.

Cape Lodge

Cape Lodge is a luxury country house hotel that is set on a vineyard. It offers a range of activities, including wine tasting, cooking classes, and golf.

Budget Accommodation

Margaret River Backpackers YHA

The Margaret River Backpackers YHA is a budget hostel that is located in the center of town. It offers dormitory-style accommodation and is a great option for backpackers and budget travelers.

Beachside Prevelly Villas

Beachside Prevelly Villas is a self-catering accommodation that offers affordable beachfront villas. It is located just a short drive from Margaret River and offers stunning ocean views.

What to Buy in Margaret River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wine

Margaret River is renowned for its premium wines, which make excellent souvenirs to take home. Visitors can purchase bottles at the various wineries in the region.

Art and Handicrafts

Margaret River has a vibrant arts scene, and visitors can purchase locally made pottery, paintings, and jewelry at the various galleries and markets in town.

Local Culture and Customs of Margaret River

Margaret River has a laid-back and friendly culture, and visitors are encouraged to relax and enjoy the region’s natural beauty. When visiting wineries, it’s important to note that spitting out the wine after tasting is common and not considered rude. Visitors should also dress appropriately for outdoor activities, as the weather can be unpredictable.

Best Time to Visit Margaret River

The best time to visit Margaret River is during the spring (September to November) or autumn (March to May) when the weather is mild and the beaches are less crowded. The summer months (December to February) can be hot and busy, while the winter months (June to August) can be rainy and cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Margaret River

Do:

  • Try the local wine and food
  • Explore the natural attractions and scenic views
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities
  • Respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Swim outside of designated swimming areas
  • Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Margaret River

Margaret River is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables. When swimming at the beach, it’s important to only swim in designated swimming areas and to be aware of rip tides.

Health in Margaret River

Margaret River has modern medical facilities, including a hospital and medical clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance and carry any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Margaret River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Margaret River

The closest airport to Margaret River is Perth Airport, which is located about three hours’ drive away. Visitors can fly into Perth and then hire a car or take a bus to Margaret River.

Bus Stations Near Margaret River

Transwa operates a daily bus service between Perth and Margaret River. The journey takes around four and a half hours and stops at various towns along the way.

Other Options

There are several car hire companies that operate in Margaret River, giving visitors the flexibility to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Margaret River

The best way to get around Margaret River is by car. Visitors can hire a car from Perth or from one of the car hire companies in town. Alternatively, there are several local taxi and shuttle services available.

Where to Go Next from Margaret River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dunsborough

Located just 20 minutes’ drive from Margaret River, Dunsborough is a popular coastal town that offers stunning beaches, great restaurants, and a vibrant nightlife.

Augusta

Augusta is the southernmost town in the Margaret River region and is home to the Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse, which offers spectacular views of the Indian Ocean and the Southern Ocean.

Conclusion

Margaret River is a beautiful and diverse destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a wine lover, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, there’s plenty to see and do in Margaret River. With its stunning natural beauty, friendly culture, and top-notch hospitality, it’s no wonder that Margaret River is one of Australia’s top travel destinations.
