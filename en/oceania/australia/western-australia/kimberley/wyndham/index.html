Introduction to Wyndham

Located in the Kimberley region of Western Australia, Wyndham is a small town that serves as a gateway to some of Australia’s most beautiful landscapes. With a population of just over 800 people, Wyndham is a popular stop for travelers exploring the region’s incredible natural attractions and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Wyndham

Wyndham offers a range of experiences for visitors, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures. Here’s a look at some of the town’s must-visit destinations.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wyndham Museum: This small museum is located in a historic courthouse building and explores the town’s early pioneering history through artifacts and photographs.

  • The Big Crocodile: One of Wyndham’s most iconic landmarks, this giant crocodile statue celebrates the town’s reptilian inhabitants and serves as a popular spot for photos.

Natural Attractions of Wyndham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Bastion: A rocky outcrop overlooking Wyndham, The Bastion offers sweeping views of the town and surrounding countryside.

  • Five Rivers Lookout: Another popular viewpoint, Five Rivers Lookout offers panoramic views of the five rivers that converge near Wyndham.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wyndham

Wyndham is surrounded by rugged terrain, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area.

  • The Grotto: This 2.2km round trip hike takes visitors to a stunning waterfall and swimming hole.

  • The Prison Tree: This 2km hike leads visitors to an ancient boab tree that was once used as a makeshift prison for Aboriginal prisoners.

  • Wyndham to Parry Lagoons Nature Reserve: For more experienced hikers and bikers, this 32km round trip route takes visitors to the picturesque Parry Lagoons Nature Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Wyndham: Activities and Tours

  • Sunset Camel Rides: Take a leisurely camel ride through the countryside and watch the sun set over the rugged landscape.

  • Jet Boat Tour of the Cambridge Gulf: Speed through the Cambridge Gulf on a high-speed jet boat ride and take in the stunning views.

  • Fishing Charters: Enjoy some of Australia’s best fishing with a chartered fishing tour in the waters surrounding Wyndham.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wyndham

Day 1: Explore Wyndham’s cultural attractions, including the Wyndham Museum and The Big Crocodile. Day 2: Hike to The Grotto and cool off in the waterfall and swimming hole. Day 3: Take a camel ride at sunset and enjoy the stunning views. Day 4: Jet boat tour of the Cambridge Gulf. Day 5: Fishing charter to catch some of Australia’s best fish. Day 6: Hike to the Prison Tree and learn about the area’s rich cultural history. Day 7: Spend the day at Parry Lagoons Nature Reserve for some breathtaking nature views.

Food and Drink Scene of Wyndham

While Wyndham is a small town, it offers a range of dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barramundi: One of Australia’s most iconic fish, barramundi is a must-try for seafood lovers.

  • Pearl Meat: This delicacy is harvested from the Pinctada maxima oyster, which is found in the waters surrounding Wyndham.

Local Beverages

  • Matso’s Brewery: Located in nearby Broome, Matso’s Brewery is a must-visit for craft beer lovers and offers a range of unique beers, including their famous ginger beer.

Where to Stay in Wyndham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Wyndham is a small town, it offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area.

  • The Kimberley Klub: This hostel is the perfect budget option for travelers looking to meet new people.

  • The King Sound Resort Hotel: If luxury is what you’re seeking, the King Sound Resort Hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Wyndham Resort: For those looking for a relaxing stay, the Wyndham Resort offers a range of amenities, including a pool, restaurant, and bar.

What to Buy in Wyndham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pearls: Wyndham is known for its pearls, which make for a perfect souvenir or gift.

  • Aboriginal Art: The rich cultural heritage of the area is reflected in the stunning Aboriginal art available for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Wyndham

The traditional owners of the land around Wyndham are the Nyikina-Mangala people. Visitors should show respect for the culture and traditions of the local people, including seeking permission to enter sacred areas and not disturbing cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Wyndham

The best time to visit Wyndham is during the dry season, which runs from April to September. This is when the weather is most comfortable, and the local attractions are fully accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wyndham

Do:

  • Respect the local cultural heritage and traditions.

  • Take precautions against the harsh Australian sun.

  • Explore the natural attractions surrounding Wyndham.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the local rivers and waterways, as they are home to saltwater crocodiles.

  • Litter or damage the fragile ecosystems of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wyndham

As with any remote area, visitors to Wyndham should take precautions when exploring the natural attractions. Be aware of the potential for extreme weather conditions and the presence of dangerous wildlife, such as snakes and crocodiles.

Health in Wyndham

Visitors to Wyndham should be up-to-date with all required vaccinations and be aware of the risks of heatstroke and dehydration during hot weather.

Getting to Wyndham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wyndham

The nearest major airport to Wyndham is in Broome, approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Wyndham

Greyhound Australia offers bus services to Wyndham from Broome and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to drive to Wyndham, although it is recommended that they have a reliable 4WD vehicle to navigate the area’s rugged terrain.

How to Get Around in Wyndham

The best way to explore Wyndham and the surrounding area is by car or 4WD vehicle. For those without a vehicle, local tour companies offer transportation and guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Wyndham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Broome: A popular tourist destination on the coast, Broome offers stunning beaches, historic buildings, and a range of cultural attractions.

  • Derby: Located approximately 2 hours from Wyndham, Derby is another small town with a rich history and stunning natural attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or just a relaxing escape, Wyndham has something to offer. Plan your trip to this beautiful town, and experience all the Kimberley region has to offer.
