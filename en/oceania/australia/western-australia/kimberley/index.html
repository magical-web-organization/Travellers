Introduction to Kimberley

Located in the northernmost region of Western Australia, Kimberley is an untouched wilderness that features breathtaking natural beauty and rich indigenous culture. Spread over a vast expanse of rugged terrain, this remote region has something for every kind of traveler. From cascading waterfalls and pristine beaches to ancient rock formations and fascinating cultural attractions, Kimberley is a treasure trove of travel experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Kimberley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mowanjum Art and Cultural Centre: This center is the perfect place to learn about the art, culture, and history of the Worrora, Ngarinyin, and Wunambal tribes. It houses an impressive collection of indigenous art, artifacts, and storytelling displays.

  • Boab Prison Tree: A giant 1,500-year-old boab tree that was used as a prison cell for Aboriginal prisoners in the 1890s.

  • Purnululu National Park: This park features the famous Bungle Bungle Range and is home to some of the world’s most ancient rock formations. The park has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its significance to the culture and history of the local Aboriginal people.

Natural Attractions of Kimberley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mitchell Falls: One of the most breathtaking natural wonders of northern Western Australia, this stunning four-tiered waterfall is surrounded by rugged cliffs and verdant vegetation.

  • Horizontal Falls: A natural phenomenon where the tides create a series of rapids that run perpendicular to the coast. It is a unique and thrilling place to visit.

  • Cape Leveque: A remote location on the Dampier Peninsula, Cape Leveque boasts stunning, secluded beaches and stunningly clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kimberley

  • The Gibb River Road: One of the most famous four-wheel-drive routes in Australia, Gibb River Road stretches for 660 kilometers through rugged outback terrain.

  • Purnululu National Park Walks: There are plenty of hiking trails available in Purnululu National Park, all of which offer breathtaking views of the park’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Kimberley: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Anglers from around the world come to Kimberley to experience some of the best fishing in Australia. From barramundi to marlin, these waters teem with a diverse array of fish species.

  • Helicopter Tours: A helicopter tour of the region is the perfect way to see the sprawling beauty of Kimberley from above.

  • Camel Rides: A camel ride across the sands of Broome is a classic Kimberley experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Kimberley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barramundi: A popular fish dish in Australia that is often grilled or fried and served with lemon wedges and salad.

  • Kangaroo: It may not be for everyone, but kangaroo is a lean and healthy meat that is used in various savory dishes.

  • Pearl Meat: A delicacy unique to the region, pearl meat is served at high-end dining establishments and tastes like a mixture of scallops and abalone.

Local Beverages

  • Matso’s Brewery: Located in Broome, this brewery produces a range of handcrafted beers using local ingredients like Kimberley watermelons, gingers, and mangoes.

  • Kurrangara Beverages: A local company that produces a range of non-alcoholic artisanal drinks like ginger beer, lemon myrtle iced tea, and rosella cordial.

Where to Stay in Kimberley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kimberley Hotel: A luxury accommodation option in the heart of Broome that offers modern amenities and a central location.

  • El Questro Homestead: A remote, luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding natural beauty.

  • Bungle Bungle Wilderness Lodge: An eco-friendly lodge that allows you to stay in the heart of Purnululu National Park.

What to Buy in Kimberley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pearls and Pearl Jewellery: Kimberley is renowned for its high-quality, natural pearls.

  • Boab Nut Products: Boab nuts are a popular souvenir item, as they can be used to make decorative items like key chains and wall hangings.

Local Culture and Customs of Kimberley

The indigenous culture of the region is deeply ingrained in the daily lives of the people of Kimberley. Visitors are encouraged to engage with the local Aboriginal communities and learn about their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kimberley

The best time to visit Kimberley is between May and September, during the dry season. The temperatures are pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kimberley

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat while exploring the outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Drive off-road without a guide.

  • Take or buy Aboriginal cultural artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kimberley

  • Be mindful of crocodiles and take necessary precautions.

  • Be prepared for rough terrain and isolated areas.

Health in Kimberley

  • Stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.

  • Bring insect repellent and protect yourself from mosquito bites.

Getting to Kimberley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kimberley

  • Broome International Airport

  • Kununurra Airport

Bus Stations Near Kimberley

  • Broome Bus Station

  • Derby Bus Station

Other Options

There are various car rental companies available in Kimberley that offer reliable transportation options for travelers.

How to Get Around in Kimberley

The best way to get around Kimberley is by car or guided tour, as the region is vast and often inaccessible to public transport.

Where to Go Next from Kimberley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kakadu National Park: One of the largest national parks in Australia, Kakadu National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Northern Territory.

  • Darwin: The capital of the Northern Territory and a vibrant city that is famous for its markets, art galleries, and museums.

Conclusion

Kimberley is a remote and rugged destination that offers an unforgettable adventure to all types of travelers. This travel guide covers everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to this stunning region of Western Australia.
