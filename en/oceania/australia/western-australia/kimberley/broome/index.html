Introduction to Broome

Broome is a small, yet exotic coastal town located in the Kimberley region of Western Australia. With its rich cultural heritage, awe-inspiring natural landscapes, and pleasant tropical weather, Broome is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a relaxing and adventurous vacation. The town is renowned for its pearl farming industry, turquoise waters, and pristine beaches, making it a perfect getaway for beach lovers and water sport enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Broome

If you’re planning a visit to Broome, here is a list of top places to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Broome Historical Museum: This museum chronicles the history of Broome, from the early years of pearling to the modern-day lifestyle of the residents.
  • Japanese Cemetery: This cemetery honors the memories of Japanese pearl divers who lost their lives while working in the pearl industry along the coast of Australia.
  • Gantheaume Point: This rock formation displays interesting dinosaur footprints that date back to the Lower Cretaceous era.

Natural Attractions of Broome: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cable Beach: This world-famous beach presents a spectacular sight of white sand and turquoise water with stretches of palm trees lining the shore.
  • Camel rides on the beach: Camel rides on the beach is a popular tourist activity in Broome. It’s a soothing experience watching the sunset while riding on the back of a camel.
  • Staircase to the Moon: This awe-inspiring natural phenomenon occurs during the full moon period, where the moon rises over the beaches at low tide and creates a unique optical illusion that resembles a staircase.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Broome

With its wilderness parks, rugged landscapes, and picturesque scenery, Broome is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Broome:

  • Gantheaume Point: It’s a two-kilometer trail that passes by some fascinating landmarks such as dinosaur footprints, the red rock cliffs, and the lighthouse.
  • Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park: This park offers several hiking trails that will take you around the preserved wetlands, where you can spot saltwater crocodiles in their natural habitat.
  • Broomes Walk Trail: The Broomes Walk Trail is an 8-kilometer trail that takes you along the coastline, where you can witness stunning views of the sun, sand, sea, and sky.

Best Things to Do in Broome: Activities and Tours

Broome is full of fun activities and tours for tourists of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Broome:

  • Pearl Farm Tours: This tour takes you on a journey to discover the history of pearl farming, and you can even try your hand at shucking an oyster.
  • Sunset Sail on Willie Creek: This tour takes you on a scenic sunset sail where you can relax and enjoy the stunning sunset views along the Willie Creek.
  • Swimming with Whale Sharks: Swimming with whale sharks is a unique opportunity that you can’t miss in Broome. Get up close and personal with these gentle giants of the ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Broome

Here is a week-long itinerary for your perfect vacation in Broome:

Food and Drink Scene of Broome

Broome’s food and drink scene is influenced by its multicultural background, producing a unique fusion of flavors and cuisines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Broome:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chilli Mud Crab: This dish consists of fresh, locally caught mud crabs cooked with chili sauce and spices.
  • Barramundi: Barramundi is a type of fish that can be found all around Broome. It’s commonly grilled or BBQ’d and served with a variety of salads and sauces.
  • Prawn Toast: This savoury dish is a popular finger food and appetizer in Broome, consisting of prawn meat, garlic, and lemon toppings on a crispy toast.

Local Beverages

  • Matso’s Ginger Beer: Made from ginger, cane sugar, and natural yeasts, Matso’s Ginger Beer is a refreshing drink that can help you beat Broome’s tropical heat.
  • Broome Brewery Beers: Broome Brewery Beers are a locally brewed beer that has a unique taste and flavor that represents the Broome lifestyle.

Where to Stay in Broome: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Broome offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Broome:

  • Cable Beach Club Resort and Spa: This luxurious resort offers a private beach, a spa, and a variety of restaurants and bars.
  • Roebuck Bay Hotel: Roebuck Bay Hotel is a budget-friendly option that’s located in the heart of Broome and provides easy access to the town’s main attractions.
  • Kimberley Klub YHA: This hostel offers a comfortable stay at an affordable price, and it’s located in an ideal location where you can access all of Broome’s main attractions easily.

What to Buy in Broome: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Broome is famous for its pearl industry, and there are several local products and souvenirs that you can take back home as a memory of your trip. Here are some of the top things to buy in Broome:

  • Pearl Jewelry: Broome is known for its high-quality, locally sourced pearls, and there are several retail stores that sell pearl jewelry in various designs, shapes, and sizes.
  • Boab Nut Carvings: The Boab Tree is an iconic tree that’s found across the Kimberley region, and locals have turned its nuts into exquisite carvings and artworks.
  • Aboriginal Artworks: The Broome region is home to several Aboriginal communities, and their artworks and handicrafts are rich in history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Broome

Broome’s local culture and customs are intriguing and diverse, reflecting the region’s unique blend of history, landscape, and population. Here’s what you need to know about Broome’s local culture and customs:

  • Pearling: Pearling has been an integral part of Broome’s history, and it’s still a significant industry that has ties to the local customs and traditions.
  • Aboriginal Culture: Broome has a rich Aboriginal culture, and visitors can gain a better understanding of it through visiting local museums and galleries, attending cultural events, and engaging with the local communities.
  • Relaxed Environment: Broome is known for its laid-back, easy-going culture, where locals and tourists alike can enjoy a peaceful and relaxed lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Broome

Broome has a tropical climate, with warm temperatures all year round. The dry season, from May to October, is the best time to visit Broome, with sunny days and low rainfall. The wet season, from November to April, can be hot, muggy, and humid, with heavy rainfall and occasional tropical storms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Broome

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while staying in Broome:

Do:

  • Respect the cultural traditions and beliefs of the local communities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, as they represent the region’s unique flavors and influences.
  • Take care of the environment, as Broome’s landscape is sensitive and fragile.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the ocean during the stinger season (November to April), where dangerous jellyfish can be present.
  • Litter or leave rubbish on the beaches or in public areas.
  • Invade the privacy or sacred sites of the Aboriginal communities.

Safety and Health

Broome is a relatively safe and healthy destination, but it’s essential to take some precautions to ensure that your trip is comfortable and enjoyable.

Safety in Broome

  • Be aware of the tides when swimming or engaging in water sports at the beaches.
  • Watch out for crocodiles and stingers in the rivers and ocean.
  • Lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation, and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.

Health in Broome

  • Protect yourself from the sun’s harmful UV rays by wearing sunscreen, hats, and protective clothing.
  • Don’t drink tap water. Instead, opt for bottled water or carry a water filter.
  • Seek medical attention if you’re feeling unwell or experiencing any symptoms during your trip.

Getting to Broome: Transportation Options and Logistics

Broome is located 2,240 kilometers north of Perth, the capital city of Western Australia.

Airports Near Broome

The Broome International Airport is the main airport that serves the town, with flights connecting Broome to major Australian cities.

Bus Stations Near Broome

Broome has regular bus services that connect the town to other towns and cities in the Kimberley region.

Other Options

Driving is another option to get to Broome, but the journey can be long and challenging if you’re not used to driving in remote areas.

How to Get Around in Broome

The best way to get around in Broome is to rent a car or take a taxi, as public transport can be limited. Another option is to rent a bike or walk, as several of Broome’s attractions are within walking or cycling distance.

Where to Go Next from Broome: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Broome’s location offers several nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore on your trip. Here are some of the best destinations and excursions to visit near Broome:

  • The Kimberley Region: The Kimberley region is a vast and rugged landscape featuring canyons, gorges, and waterfalls that are perfect for outdoor adventure activities like hiking and camping.
  • Horizontal Falls: The Horizontal Falls is an exceptional natural phenomenon located in Talbot Bay, where the tides create a horizontal waterfall effect.
  • Derby: Derby is a town located 220 kilometers east of Broome that’s famous for its fishing and pearling industry.

Conclusion

Broome is a fascinating and diverse destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach holiday, an adventurous outdoor escape, or an opportunity to learn about the region’s unique culture and history, Broome is the perfect destination. With its tropical weather, stunning landscapes, and charming local communities, Broome is undoubtedly a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
