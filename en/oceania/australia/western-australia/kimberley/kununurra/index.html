Introduction to Kununurra

Kununurra is a picturesque town located in the Kimberley region of Western Australia and is situated 38 km from the Northern Territory border. This town is bordered by two breathtaking natural attractions, the Ord River and the Purnululu National Park. Kununurra is a popular tourist spot for its rich indigenous culture, dramatic landscapes, and wildlife.

List of Places to Visit in Kununurra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kununurra is renowned for its fascinating Aboriginal heritage. The Warmun Art Centre, located 160 km southeast of Kununurra, is famous for its high-quality contemporary aboriginal art and is worth a visit. Travelers can also learn about the history of the region and the significance of its flora and fauna at the Kununurra Museum.

Natural Attractions of Kununurra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Kununurra is its biggest attraction. The Purnululu National Park is situated around 250 km from the town and is home to the world-famous Bungle Bungle Range. The Ord River, popular for its fishing activities, offers scenic river cruises that give visitors a chance to spot crocodiles, turtles, and water birds. Spring Creek Nature Reserve is a unique wetland that offers a view of a large variety of birdlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kununurra

The Jarnem Tourist Sanctuary and Packsaddle Wildlife Reserve, located 70 km north of Kununurra, offers scenic hiking trails. For biking enthusiasts, the Pumphouse trail follows a 30 km intermediate-level track, where riders can witness the mesmerizing views of Lake Kununurra.

Best Things to Do in Kununurra: Activities and Tours

Kununurra is perfect for activities and tours such as scenic flights above the landscapes, sunset catamaran cruises, and fishing tours. Native animal sightings are frequent occurrences in tours to the Bundarra Birdhide, a birdwatcher’s paradise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kununurra

Day 1: Arrive and explore the town’s local attractions such as the Kununurra Museum, Lions Park, or the Celebrity Tree Park. Day 2: Take a full-day excursion to the Purnululu National Park and explore the Bungle Bungle Range. Day 3: Take a leisurely boat cruise to discover the Ord River’s wildlife and enjoy the sunset from the water. Day 4: Go on the Jarnem Tourist Sanctuary hiking trail, followed by a visit to the Packsaddle Wildlife Reserve, spending the day in the unspoiled outdoors. Day 5: Join the local food tour of Kununurra and taste the culinary delights of the town. Day 6: Take a scenic flight over the lake and enjoy the bird’s eye view of the vast landscape. Day 7: Relax on the shores of Lake Kununurra, and consider a swim or picnic to finish off a perfect trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Kununurra

Kununurra is influenced by the aboriginal cuisine. Locals recommend trying dishes such as kangaroo, barramundi, and crocodile. The town is becoming well-known for its brewery, and visitors can enjoy a refreshing pint of beer at The Hoochery. Local restaurants serve fresh seafood and locally grown tropical fruits, including mangoes and lychees that are must-try.

Where to Stay in Kununurra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kununurra is home to a variety of accommodation options. From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels, the town caters to all types of travelers. The popular options include The Kimberley Grande, Kununurra Country Club Resort, and El Questro Wilderness Park.

What to Buy in Kununurra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aboriginal art pieces, boab nut souvenirs, unique and locally made accessories can be brought back home from the local street markets. The art center in Kununurra sells high-quality indigenous art pieces, including paintings, baskets, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Kununurra

The aboriginal culture and history remain at the forefront of life in Kununurra. It is best to respect their age-old traditions and engage in cultural activities where possible. The town is known for its sense of community, and visitors are asked to uphold a good attitude and respect everyone while in town.

Best Time to Visit Kununurra

The best time to visit Kununurra is between April to August, where the climate remains warm and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities. It is suggested to avoid visiting between November to February when temperatures and humidity can be at their highest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kununurra

Do:

  • Take time to learn about the indigenous culture.
  • Keep water and sunscreen handy during any outdoor activities.
  • Visit the local street markets and take back unique souvenirs.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs.
  • Litter in public spaces or natural landscapes.
  • Ignore the safety rules or tour guides during any outdoor activities.

Safety and Health in Kununurra

Safety in Kununurra

Though Kununurra is a safe town, it is recommended to take basic safety measures, such as locking doors, securing car windows, and keeping valuable items in a safe location. Visitors should also always follow any safety rules or guidelines provided by tour guides during any outdoor activities.

Health in Kununurra

In Kununurra, the health facilities are limited, and it is recommended to carry one’s medication and basic medical supplies. It is advisable to carry adequate sun protection and water during outdoor activities.

Getting to Kununurra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kununurra is accessible via air and roadways.

Airports Near Kununurra

The Kununurra Airport is the nearest airport to the town, offering daily flights to Darwin, Perth, and Brisbane.

Bus Stations Near Kununurra

Bus services operate between Kununurra and nearby towns such as Darwin and Broome.

Other Options

Road tripping is a popular way to explore the Kimberley region, and Kununurra is located on the Great Northern Highway.

How to Get Around in Kununurra

Rental cars and taxis are available in Kununurra. Public buses operate between the town and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Kununurra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Bungle Bungle Range and Purnululu National Park are the popular nearby destinations to Kununurra. Travelers can also explore the historic town of Wyndham, experience the 4WD Kimberly adventure at Mitchell Falls, or take a dip in the natural thermal pools at El Questro Wilderness Park.

Conclusion

Kununurra is a unique and authentic Australian destination, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and those interested in learning more about indigenous culture. With its rich history and spectacular natural attractions, it is high on our list of must-visit places in Western Australia. Follow our guide and have an unforgettable experience in Kununurra.
