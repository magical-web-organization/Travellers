Introduction to Derby

Nestled in the heart of Kimberley, on the northwest coast of Western Australia, lies the charming town of Derby. Known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, Derby offers an unforgettable experience for visitors. With this comprehensive tour guide, discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and local customs of Derby in Western Australia.

List of Places to Visit in Derby

There is no shortage of things to see and do in Derby. From visiting culturally important attractions to exploring the town’s natural beauty, here is a list of must-visit places in Derby:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boab Prison Tree: A fascinating natural wonder and a part of the town’s gruesome history. This 1,500-year-old tree served as a holding cell for indigenous prisoners during the 1890s.

  • Derby Wharf: A historic jetty that once served as a shipping port for pearling luggers and cattle shipments until the late 20th century.

  • Norval Gallery: Dedicated to showcasing contemporary and traditional Aboriginal art, the Norval Gallery is a must-visit spot for anyone interested in indigenous art and culture.

  • Mowanjum Aboriginal Art and Culture Centre: An essential part of Derby’s indigenous heritage, this center provides a glimpse into the art and culture of the Ngarinyin, Worrorra, and Wunambal peoples.

Natural Attractions of Derby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tunnel Creek National Park: A must-visit spot for nature lovers, Tunnel Creek National Park offers scenic views, hiking trails, and a chance to explore the region’s natural underground cave system.

  • Windjana Gorge: A spectacular gorge formed by the mighty Lennard River, Windjana Gorge is home to freshwater crocodiles, wallabies, and a diverse range of bird species.

  • King Leopold Ranges Conservation Park: This vast wilderness park is home to the King Leopold Ranges and offers a range of activities, including hiking, fishing, exploring the region’s waterfalls, and observing the local flora and fauna.

  • Horizontal Falls: A natural phenomenon that occurs as the tide changes, offering an unforgettable experience for visitors. Take a scenic flight or boat tour to observe it from up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Derby

Derby is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in Australia. Here are some of the popular trails you can explore:

  • Gibb River Road: A challenging road trip, this journey takes you through the scenic Australian outback and offers breathtaking views and a chance to take a dip in the region’s freshwater pools.

  • Mornington Wilderness Camp: Located in the King Leopold Ranges Conservation Park, the Mornington Wilderness Camp offers hiking trails that take you through the conservation park, offering a chance to observe the region’s wildlife and stunning natural beauty.

  • Derby Heritage Trail: Wander through Derby’s past with a visit to its heritage trail. A series of well-marked trails, the Derby Heritage Trail offers a glimpse of the region’s cultural history.

Best Things to Do in Derby: Activities and Tours

Looking for a fun-filled day of adventure and excitement? Derby has plenty of activities to offer. From scenic flights to boat tours, here are some of the best things to do in Derby:

  • Boat Tour Along the Kimberley Coast: A boat tour along the picturesque coast of Kimberley allows you to explore the region’s stunning natural beauty and observe its diverse marine life.

  • Scenic Flights Over Horizontal Falls: Experience the wonder of Horizontal Falls from up above. A scenic flight is a perfect way to take in the beauty of this natural phenomenon.

  • Fishing Expedition: Kimberley region is famous for its diverse marine life, and a guided fishing expedition is a fantastic way to test your angling skills and reel in some fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Derby

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Derby:

Day 1: Visit the Boab Prison Tree, explore the Norval Gallery, and take a stroll around Derby Wharf.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking the trails of the King Leopold Ranges Conservation Park and take in the region’s natural beauty.

Day 3: Take a boat tour along the Kimberley Coast and observe the local marine life and stunning scenery.

Day 4: Explore the town’s cultural heritage with a visit to Mowanjum Aboriginal Art and Culture Centre.

Day 5: Spend the day exploring the Tunnel Creek National Park and Windjana Gorge.

Day 6: Take a scenic flight over Horizontal Falls and marvel at the beauty of this natural phenomenon from up above.

Day 7: End your week with a fishing expedition off the coast of Derby, trying to catch some of the region’s diverse seafood.

Food and Drink Scene of Derby

A visit to Derby is incomplete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Derby:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marron: A freshwater crayfish found in the streams and freshwater pools of Kimberley, Marron is a local delicacy and must-try dish for seafood lovers.

  • Barramundi: Found in the coastal waters of Kimberley, Barramundi is a popular fish dish, available at most eateries in Derby.

  • Kangaroo: An Australian staple and often served as steaks or burgers, kangaroo meat is lean, protein-packed, and delicious.

Local Beverages

  • Matso’s Brewing Company: A local brewery known for its unique range of beers and ciders, Matso’s is a must-visit for beer lovers.

  • Kununurra Distillery: The heart of Western Australia’s only rum distillery, Kununurra Distillery produces some of the best rum in the state.

Where to Stay in Derby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to affordable hostels, Derby has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay, depending on your budget and preferences:

  • Kimberley Entrance Caravan Park: A popular budget-friendly option, Kimberley Entrance Caravan Park offers excellent facilities, including camping sites, cabins, and caravans.

  • Spinifex Hotel: A modern hotel with excellent amenities, Spinifex Hotel is ideal for those seeking comfort and luxury.

  • King Sound Resort Hotel: A boutique hotel situated in a tranquil location, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area and excellent facilities.

What to Buy in Derby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Derby’s culture and heritage with these local souvenirs:

  • Boab Nuts: Carved and polished to perfection, Boab nuts are a popular souvenir and make for excellent decorative pieces.

  • Aboriginal Artwork: A beautiful and meaningful souvenir, Aboriginal artwork is a must-buy item for those interested in indigenous culture and art.

  • Kimberley Pearls: Known for their unique hues and textures, Kimberley pearls are a quintessential souvenir and make for perfect gifts and keepsakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Derby

Derby is a town steeped in indigenous culture and history, with a deep connection to the land and their ancestral heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the region’s cultural customs and follow these guidelines:

  • Ask Permission: Always ask permission before taking photos of indigenous people or their artwork as a sign of respect.

  • No Littering: Kimberley region is rich in biodiversity, and visitors are encouraged to keep the area clean and avoid littering.

  • Observe Cultural Protocols: Visitors are encouraged to respect the region’s cultural protocols and observe local customs while exploring the area.

Best Time to Visit Derby

The best time to visit Derby is between April and September, when temperatures are milder and more suitable for outdoor activities. However, if you want to witness the region’s natural phenomenon of Horizontal Falls, the best time to visit is during the full moon period between April and December.

Do’s and Don’t’s of Derby

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Derby:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and cultural protocols

  • Keep the area clean and avoid littering

  • Explore the region’s natural beauty and indulge in outdoor activities

  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the town’s cultural heritage

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission

  • Engage in any activity that may harm the local flora and fauna

  • Disrupt the natural environment or the local ecosystem

  • Disrespect the region’s cultural customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Derby

Derby is a safe town, but visitors are encouraged to take standard safety precautions, including:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night

  • Protect yourself from the sun and stay hydrated

  • Follow all safety guidelines while engaging in outdoor activities

Health in Derby

Kimberley Region is a remote area, and visitors must take preventive measures to protect themselves from diseases and illnesses. Here are some essential health tips for visiting Derby:

  • Always carry insect repellent and apply it as recommended

  • Wear protective clothing and gear to avoid exposure to the sun

  • Stay hydrated and carry enough water with you when exploring the area

  • Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any symptoms of sickness.

Getting to Derby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Derby is a fairly remote town, but getting there is relatively easy. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Derby

Derby has its airport, the Derby Regional Airport, which offers flights to and from Perth, Broome, and other major cities.

Bus Stations Near Derby

Greyhound and Integrity Coach Lines operate services to Derby from major cities and towns across Western Australia.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for self-drive or tour packages offered by various travel agencies.

How to Get Around in Derby

The best way to get around Derby is by car or bicycle. Visitors can rent a car or bicycle from various rental services in the town.

Where to Go Next from Derby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Derby is a great starting point for exploring the Kimberley Region and its nearby destinations. Here are some places to visit after Derby:

  • Broome: A charming coastal town famous for its beaches, pearls, and tropical climate, Broome is an essential stop on any Western Australia itinerary.

  • Fitzroy Crossing: A small town on the banks of the Fitzroy River, Fitzroy Crossing offers an insight into the region’s indigenous history and culture.

  • Kununurra: A picturesque town located near the border of Western Australia and the Northern Territory, Kununurra is known for its stunning landscapes and wildlife experiences.

Conclusion

Derby is a hidden gem of Western Australia, offering visitors an unforgettable experience with its unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With this comprehensive tour guide, explore the region’s must-visit attractions, try out the local cuisine, and immerse yourself in the town’s cultural customs.
