Introduction to Cape Range National Park

Cape Range National Park is a stunning destination located in Gascoyne, Western Australia. The park is known for its spectacular range of wildlife, crystal-clear waters, and warm tropical climate, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park extends over 50,000 acres and boasts a diverse range of landscapes varying from rugged limestone ranges, breathtaking gorges, and stunning white sandy beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Cape Range National Park

Cape Range National Park has a variety of both culturally important attractions as well as breathtaking natural attractions. Below we’ve listed some of the top places to visit while in the park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vlamingh Head Lighthouse: Located on top of Cape Vlamingh, this lighthouse has been standing since 1912 and offers stunning views of the Indian Ocean.
  • Ningaloo Marine Park Discovery Centre: Located in the park’s entrance, this museum showcases the diverse marine life of the Ningaloo Reef.
  • Murujuga National Park: A site with more than a million ancient rock carvings. This is one of the most culturally important areas in Western Australia.

Natural Attractions of Cape Range National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yardie Creek: A deep gorge that cuts through Cape Range and is a great place to spot local wildlife.
  • Turquoise Bay: This beach is famous for its crystal-clear water and exotic marine life, great for snorkeling, diving, and swimming.
  • Cape Range National Park Scenic Drive: A 40-kilometer scenic drive that offers outstanding views of the rugged landscape and beaches of Cape Range National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cape Range National Park

Cape Range National Park offers a variety of walking and biking trails suitable for all skill levels.

  • Mandu Mandu Gorge Trail: A 2.5-kilometer trail that leads you through a stunning gorge with amazing views of Yardie Creek
  • Charles Knife Canyon Trail: A challenging 3-kilometer trek that rewards you with breathtaking views of the park’s highest peak.

Best Things to Do in Cape Range National Park: Activities and Tours

Cape Range National Park offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors interested in exploring the local area.

  • Whale Watching: This park is known for its whale population, and there are plenty of whale watching tours to choose from.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: With its pristine waters, Cape Range National Park is perfect for snorkeling or diving.
  • Kayaking: Take a guided kayak tour and enjoy the beauty of the park’s sea caves, coral reefs, and turquoise waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cape Range National Park

Day 1: Arrive at Cape Range National Park and visit the Ningaloo Marine Park Discovery Centre, to get a feel for the marine life of the park.

Day 2: Take a hike through Cape Range’s rugged landscape, particularly the Charles Knife Canyon Trail with its unique views of the local scenery.

Day 3: Snorkel in the crystal-clear water of Turquoise Bay. Afterwards, try kayaking and explore the local sea caves.

Day 4: Take a guided tour to see the local whale population, or alternatively, if you can bring your own boat.

Day 5: Visit Murujuga National Park to see ancient rock carvings.

Day 6: Relax on the white sandy beaches of Cape Range National Park such as Osprey Beach.

Day 7: Departure from Cape Range National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Cape Range National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Cape Range National Park is known for the beauty of its local scenery, but the local cuisine is equally impressive.

  • Seafood: Western Australia is known for its fresh seafood, especially crayfish, scallops, and prawns.
  • Bush Tucker: Exploring the nearby bush for ingredients makes meals out of local herbs, roots, and berries such as kangaroo meat, crocodile meat, or bush tomato.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: There are local craft breweries situated in the nearby city of Exmouth that you can visit.
  • Wine: Western Australia is recognized as one of the best wine regions in the world and there are many wineries to visit near Cape Range National Park.

Where to Stay in Cape Range National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sal Salis: Nestled in the dunes of Cape Range National Park, Sal Salis is an eco-luxe resort that merges into the surrounding bush.
  • Potshot Hotel Resort: This resort is conveniently located near the park entrance. It has a mix of shared backpacker-hostel rooms and private hotel rooms.
  • Cape Range National Park Campgrounds: This is the perfect accommodation for campers as it offers eleven beautiful, unpowered camping perches, situated along the coast of the park.

What to Buy in Cape Range National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Didgeridoos: these are traditional Australian aboriginal musical instruments made from a variety of woods by skilled craftsmen.
  • Aboriginal Art: Unique artwork that reflects the cultural heritage of Western Australia is something you definitely should consider taking home.

Local Culture and Customs of Cape Range National Park

The local culture of Cape Range National Park is centered around the cultures of the aboriginal people who have lived there for thousands of years. Make an effort to learn about their customs and traditions as they are an integral part of the area’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Cape Range National Park

The best time to visit Cape Range National Park is between late March and early December when the weather is mild and the marine life, including dolphins, dugongs, and humpback whales, is abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape Range National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Stay on designated paths while hiking and biking trails.
  • Learn about the local customs and traditions of the aboriginal peoples who live in the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the environment.
  • Touch or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Ignore the signs and warnings from the Park’s officials.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape Range National Park

Make sure that you bring plenty of water and sunscreen with you when visiting the park. Take care when hiking, and remember to check the weather conditions before your arrival to avoid dangerous situations.

Health in Cape Range National Park

The park offers limited medical facilities, it is suggested that you bring along a first aid kit for any minor emergencies. Note that there is no clean drinking water available in the park, so make sure to bring enough water for your trip.

Getting to Cape Range National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cape Range National Park

The closest airports to Cape Range National Park are located in the nearby city of Exmouth, including the Learmonth Airport, with direct flights from Perth.

Bus Stations Near Cape Range National Park

There are no direct bus services that reach Cape Range National Park, but it is possible to reach the park from nearby towns such as Exmouth, which offers regular daily services to Perth.

Other Options

If you prefer, you can hire a car from the airport or nearby cities and take a scenic drive to the park.

How to Get Around in Cape Range National Park

To explore the park, it is recommended that you hire a car. There are no public buses or shuttles that connect to the different parts of the park.

Where to Go Next from Cape Range National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Karijini National Park: Located in the Pilbara region, Karijini is renowned for its stunning canyons and gorges
  • Ningaloo Reef Marine Park: One of the largest and healthiest coral reef systems on Earth, with many opportunities for snorkeling and diving.

Conclusion

Cape Range National Park offers visitors an unforgettable experience with its stunning natural landscapes, local culture, and wildlife. By reading this guide, you should have a better understanding of how to make the most of your visit, while also respecting the environment and local customs. Enjoy your adventure in Cape Range National Park!
