Exmouth is a popular tourist destination located in the Gascoyne region of Western Australia. Known for its beautiful beaches, World Heritage-listed Ningaloo Reef, and scenic landscapes, Exmouth offers a wide range of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. In this comprehensive tour guide for Exmouth, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Exmouth

Exmouth is a small town with a population of around 2,500 people. It is located on the west coast of Australia, approximately 1,270 km north of Perth and 13 km from the Ningaloo Reef. The town was established in 1964 as a support base for the nearby United States Naval Communication Station. Today, Exmouth is a popular holiday destination for both domestic and international tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Exmouth

Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions or outdoor adventures, Exmouth has something for everyone. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Exmouth Cultural Arts Centre: This centre features a gallery space, workshop rooms, and a gift shop. Visitors can take part in art workshops or enjoy an exhibition.

  2. Navy Pier: This Navy Pier was a former naval communication station but now it is a popular diving spot for those who love to observe marine life.

  3. Vlamingh Head Lighthouse: This lighthouse was constructed in 1912 and it is located on the tip of north-west cape

Natural Attractions of Exmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ningaloo Reef: This world-famous reef attracts thousands of visitors each year. Activities such as snorkelling, diving, and swimming with whale sharks provided in the area.

  2. Cape Range National Park: The park features beautiful gorges, beaches, and walking trails. Visitors can also enjoy various activities such as hiking, wildlife watching, and camping.

  3. Turquoise Bay: Turquoise Bay is a picture-perfect beach that features crystal clear waters and white sand. It’s a perfect spot to enjoy a picnic or go swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Exmouth

Exmouth is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are the top hiking and biking routes:

  1. Yardie Creek Gorge walk: this route is approximately 1.5 km long and from here you can enjoy the stunning views of the gorge and red rocks.

  2. Charles Knife Canyon Loop Trail: This trail features beautiful scenery, with views of the coast and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Exmouth: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkelling with Whale Sharks: Visitors can experience swimming alongside these gentle giants while snorkelling.

  2. Scuba Diving: The region is renowned for its picturesque SCUBA diving spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Exmouth

For those looking to spend a week in Exmouth, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Exmouth and relax at your hotel or guesthouse.

Day 2 – 3: Explore the town and museums. Spend some time at the beach.

Day 4 – 5: Spend time exploring Ningaloo Reef. Go snorkelling or scuba diving

Day 6 – 7: Hike or bike along the beautiful trails in the Cape Range National Park and Turquoise Bay.

Local Culture and Customs of Exmouth

Exmouth is rich in Aboriginal and maritime history. Visitors should always respect the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Exmouth

The best time to visit Exmouth is from March to June or from September to November when the weather is mild and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Exmouth

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.

  2. Carry plenty of sunscreen and water while sightseeing.

Don’t:

  1. Litter on the beaches or in the national park

  2. Touch or take coral from Ningaloo Reef

Safety and Health

Safety in Exmouth

Health in Exmouth

Visitors should always carry plenty of water, sunscreen, and insect repellent while exploring the town and enjoying outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of stingers and other hazards in the water.
