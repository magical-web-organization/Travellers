Stanthorpe, a charming town in Queensland’s Darling Downs region, is a hidden gem for travelers seeking a relaxed and rejuvenating escape. With its breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and world-class culinary scene, Stanthorpe has something to offer every visitor. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, or gastronomic delights, you’ll find something to love in this quaint Australian town. Here’s our guide to the best things to do and see in Stanthorpe, Queensland.

Introduction to Stanthorpe

Located in the Granite Belt, a wine region, Stanthorpe is situated around two and a half hours from Brisbane in Queensland and approximately 15km from the New South Wales border. This charming town is known for producing cool climate wines, which makes it an ideal destination for wine lovers from all over the world. The town has a population of about 5000 and sits on a plateau 1000 meters above sea level.

List of Places to Visit in Stanthorpe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stanthorpe boasts numerous museums and historical sites that reflect the town’s rich cultural heritage. The Stanthorpe Museum is one of the must-visit attractions for anyone interested in the local history of the town. This museum explores the daily life of Stanthorpe’s past inhabitants through exhibits featuring clothing, tools, and furniture, among others.

Another important cultural site is the Heritage Museum, which is home to an extensive collection of photographs and historical artifacts from Stanthorpe and the surrounding region. Visitors can explore the history of the town’s wine industry, railway, and more on their own or with a guide.

Natural Attractions of Stanthorpe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stanthorpe is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes that offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. Some of the most popular natural attractions include Girraween National Park, Bald Rock National Park, and Sundown National Park.

Girraween National Park is a popular destination among hikers and nature lovers, with its rugged terrain, granite outcrops, and wildflowers in springtime. Hikers can savor the unforgettable views of the park’s unique rock formations on the many trails available both short walks and multi-day hikes.

Bald Rock National Park, located nearby, is home to Australia’s largest granite monolith, dominated by granite outcrops. Visitors can climb up the rock to enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside from an elevation of more than 1200m.

Sundown National Park offering stunning views of rocky ridge lines and waterfalls, is a popular destination for adventurous rock climbers and trekkers, mountain bike riders, and kayakers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stanthorpe

The Granite Belt region’s vast array of hiking and bike trails make it the perfect destination for adventure seekers. Some of the most popular trails include Castle Rock Trail, a moderate three-kilometer hike that rewards visitors with breathtaking views from the top. The Mt Marlay Lookout Circuit, which provides an easy walk with superb views of the local vineyards, granite belt, and farmland for visitors.

Cyclists are spoilt for choice with an abundance of marked routes and paths useful for serious cyclists, families, and recreational bike riders alike. Take on one of the trails through the Girraween National Park or Tour de Granite, a 146km cycling tour through the Granite Belt.

Best Things to Do in Stanthorpe: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, visitors can indulge in many fun activities and tours in Stanthorpe. The town is famous for its wine and food scene, where travelers can explore the region’s wineries and indulge in artisanal cheeses, chocolates, and other local delicacies.

Apart from its gastronomic offerings, Stanthorpe is host to several art and cultural events such as Opera in the Vineyard, Art in the Vineyard, the Apple and Grape festival, and many others. Apart from that, a large number of music festivals are held throughout the year, attracting music lovers from near and far.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stanthorpe

A week-long itinerary is perfect for visitors to explore Stanthorpe’s main attractions. Day one can be spent exploring the town’s museums and historical sites. Day two can be set aside for natural attractions like Girraween National Park and Bald Rock National Park.

Day three can be spent cruising the countryside, discovering local wineries and indulging in artisanal cheese, chocolates, some of Queensland’s best craft beer, and other delicacies. Day four can involve hiking Castle Rock Trail, followed by cycling on the granite belt, such as the Tour de Granite, or simply exploring the many natural wonders that surround the area.

Day five can be dedicated to indulging in relaxing wellness activities like a spa or massage session. Day six and seven can be dedicated to discovering art and cultural events, music festivals, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Stanthorpe

Stanthorpe boasts one of Australia’s most sought-after food and wine scenes, with a variety of restaurants, cafes, and wineries that will delight your taste buds. The town has an abundance of local producers and a thriving farmers’ market, offering a wide range of fresh and organic produce.

Must-Try Dishes

Some local dishes that travelers should try include locally sourced meats such as kangaroo, wines, and cheeses. At the top of any list is Stanthorpe apples, which are world-renowned and featured in a wide variety of local dishes and beverages. Another local delicacy is the truffle, which is grown in the surrounding regions and is used to flavor many dishes across the town.

Local Beverages

Stanthorpe’s wine and beer selections are extensive and delicious, thanks to the region’s microclimate and unique soil combination. Visitors can taste an extensive collection of estate wines, beer, and beautifully crafted cocktails from the town’s lively scene.

Where to Stay in Stanthorpe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stanthorpe is teeming with accommodation options to suit every visitor. The town has several luxurious hotels, family-friendly resorts, bed and breakfast accommodation, and backpacker-style hostels.

Best Budget Accommodations

For budget-friendly options, backpacker hostels are an affordable option, including Happy Valley Retreat and Stanthorpe Backpackers.

Best Mid-Range Hotels

Mid-range hotels in Stanthorpe include The Vines Motel and Cottages, which provides affordable accommodation amidst large vineyards. It also includes amenities such as a swimming pool, spa, and hot tub. Donnelly’s Castle Country Inn, another mid-range hotel, offers comfortable accommodation with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Luxury Accommodations

Luxury accommodations in Stanthorpe range from resorts to beautiful motel cum cottages with amenities such as fireplaces, jacuzzis, and spacious balconies offering panoramic views of the countryside. Some standout options include Alure Stanthorpe Country Retreat and Grantham’s Mountain Haven.

What to Buy in Stanthorpe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers cannot leave Stanthorpe without some local Aussie souvenirs or produce to bring back home. Some of the best souvenirs from Stanthorpe include wines, locally crafted beers, and ciders. Cheese, chocolate, and apple products such as jams and honey are also widely available.

Local art and crafts, such as pottery and paintings with indigenous designs, make lovely and unique mementos of your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Stanthorpe

Stanthorpe has a proud heritage and culture, with a fantastic art and music scene and an abundance of homegrown produce, wines, and beer. The region is known for its enriching offerings, cultural diversity, and Aboriginal heritage.

The locals take great pride in their unique heritage and customs. Stanthorpe also has an active community involved in local festivals, events, agricultural fairs, and other traditional ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Stanthorpe

The best time to visit Stanthorpe is during fall in April or May when the leaves of the region’s mature trees turn into various shades of red, yellow, and orange, creating a stunning and unique view. Another time to visit is in November when the annual Apple and Grape Festival is held, featuring wine tastings, farm tours, and live music.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stanthorpe

Do:

  • Try the locally sourced wines, beers, and produce.
  • Respect the local cultural heritage and customs.
  • Enjoy the stunning and unique natural attractions, including Girraween National Park and Bald Rock National Park.
  • Take your time to explore the region’s art and music scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Show disrespect towards the locals’ cultural customs, heritage, or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stanthorpe

Stanthorpe is a safe town to visit. Visitors are, however, reminded to follow standard safety practices, such as being aware of surroundings and keeping personal belongings safe.

Health in Stanthorpe

Stanthorpe is a healthy destination, but visitors are encouraged to carry medication with them for any medical conditions or to consult a doctor before traveling. Insect repellent is essential when exploring natural attractions and is always available in local stores.

Getting to Stanthorpe: Transportation Option and Logistics

Visitors can get to Stanthorpe in several ways, including road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Stanthorpe

The nearest airports are Toowoomba Wellcamp Airport and Brisbane airport, located around 2 hours and three and a half hours away, respectively. Several regional airports are within a few hours’ drive, such as Ballina Byron Gateway Airport, Lismore Airport, and Gold Coast Airport.

Bus Stations Near Stanthorpe

Several bus operators offer bus services to Stanthorpe from Brisbane, including Greyhound Australia, Premier Motor Service, and Crisps Coaches.

Other Options

Driving yourself is the most flexible way to get around the region and explore its many attractions. Rental car options are available in Brisbane and Toowoomba.

How to Get Around in Stanthorpe

Stanthorpe town is dedicated to pedestrians, which provide easy access to the many cafes, restaurants, art galleries, and other attractions in the town’s center. Walking and cycling are the best options to visit the surroundings, and regular taxi services provide transport to selected attractions.

Where to Go Next from Stanthorpe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stanthorpe’s location positions two hours and less to many great destinations including Toowoomba, which is known for its annual Carnival of Flowers. A 45-minute ride forward takes visitors to the border and New South Wales surroundings, featuring the Tenterfield region’s renowned National Parks.

Conclusion

From its stunning natural attractions and outdoor adventures to its rich cultural heritage and thriving culinary scene, Stanthorpe is a must-visit destination for any traveler. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing and rejuvenating escape or an action-packed weekend, Stanthorpe’s got you covered. So come and explore the Granite Belt Region for yourself, and discover why Stanthorpe is one of Australia’s most captivating travel destinations.
