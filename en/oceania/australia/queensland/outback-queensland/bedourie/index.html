Introduction to Bedourie

Often referred to as the “Gateway to the Simpson Desert”, Bedourie is a hidden gem located in the heart of Outback Queensland, Australia. With a population of just over 120 people, Bedourie is a small town known for its natural beauty, unique culture, and rich history. It is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and explore the beautiful Australian outback.

List of Places to Visit in Bedourie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bedourie has a rich history, and visitors can learn all about it at the Bedourie Visitor Information Centre. The centre has a wealth of information about local Aboriginal culture, early explorers, and the region’s mining and pastoral heritage. The Bedourie Town Hall is a historic building that dates back to the early 1900s and is worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Bedourie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the outdoors, Bedourie has plenty to offer. The Diamantina River is a great place to start, offering stunning views and excellent fishing opportunities. You can also explore the Simpson Desert Regional Reserve, which covers over 50,000 hectares of unique natural beauty. The reserve is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including the iconic red kangaroo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bedourie

For those who like to hike or bike, Bedourie has several popular trails, including the Bedourie-Simpson Desert Walking Track. This is a 5-day hike that takes you through some of the region’s most breathtaking landscapes. The Bedourie Loop is a popular bike trail that takes you through the town and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Bedourie: Activities and Tours

For those who love adventure, Bedourie has plenty of activities to choose from. You can take a 4WD tour of the Simpson Desert, go bird-watching along the Diamantina River, or take a scenic flight over the outback. The Bedourie Camel Races, held annually in July, are a unique and quirky event that attracts visitors from all over the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bedourie

Day 1: Explore the Bedourie Visitor Information Centre and the Bedourie Town Hall. Day 2-3: Go hiking in the Simpson Desert Regional Reserve. Day 4: Take a 4WD tour of the Simpson Desert. Day 5: Go bird-watching along the Diamantina River. Day 6: Take a scenic flight over the outback. Day 7: Check out the Bedourie Camel Races (if in July).

Food and Drink Scene of Bedourie

Must-Try Dishes

Outback Queensland is known for its hearty, classic meals, and Bedourie is no exception. One local dish you must try is the Bedourie Camp Oven bread, a simple yet delicious bread cooked in a cast-iron camp oven. You can also try the classic Australian meat pies at the local bakery.

Local Beverages

Bedourie may be a small town, but it has a great selection of drinks to choose from. For non-alcoholic options, try the locally brewed Outback Cola or the refreshing Diamantina Iced Tea. For something stronger, try the iconic XXXX beer or a locally distilled gin.

Where to Stay in Bedourie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bedourie has a small selection of accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The Bedourie Hotel Motel is a popular choice for those looking for clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Bedourie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Bedourie by purchasing some local souvenirs and products. The Bedourie Camp Oven is a staple of the outback and can be purchased at the local tourist centre. You can also purchase local honey and beef jerky, both of which are produced in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Bedourie

The local Aboriginal people have lived in the region for tens of thousands of years and have a rich and vibrant culture. Visitors can learn all about the culture at the Bedourie Visitor Information Centre or by attending one of the many events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Bedourie

The best time to visit Bedourie is between April and October, when the weather is mild and perfect for exploring the outdoors. The Bedourie Camel Races, held annually in July, are a must-see event that attracts visitors from all over the world.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bedourie

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take plenty of water when exploring the outdoors.
  • Buy local products and support the local community.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Litter in the natural areas.
  • Disturb the local wildlife or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bedourie

Bedourie is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Always carry plenty of water and wear appropriate footwear and clothing. Be aware of local wildlife, such as snakes and spiders, and avoid swimming in the Diamantina River during the wet season.

Health in Bedourie

The town has a medical centre that visitors can access if needed. Visitors should also be aware of the harsh sun and take precautions to avoid sunburn and heat stroke.

Getting to Bedourie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bedourie

The closest airport to Bedourie is located in Birdsville, which is approximately 200km away. Visitors can catch a connecting flight from Brisbane or other major Australian cities to reach Birdsville.

Bus Stations Near Bedourie

There is no bus service that travels directly to Bedourie. However, visitors can catch a bus to the nearby town of Birdsville and then take a connecting bus or hire a car to travel to Bedourie.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Bedourie, with the town being located on the popular Outback Highway.

How to Get Around in Bedourie

The town is small and easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a car from one of the local car rental agencies to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bedourie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations to explore from Bedourie, including the nearby towns of Birdsville and Boulia. Visitors can also venture further inland to explore the wonders of the Simpson Desert or head to the coast to experience the beauty of the Great Barrier Reef.

Conclusion

Bedourie is a hidden gem located in the heart of Outback Queensland, offering visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. From its natural beauty to its rich culture and history, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Bedourie. Our travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this amazing part of the world.
