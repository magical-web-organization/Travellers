Introduction to Undara Volcanic National Park

Undara Volcanic National Park is a unique destination situated in Far North Queensland, Australia. This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to the largest and longest lava tube system in the world, which was formed about 190,000 years ago when a series of volcanic eruptions occurred. The park offers visitors an opportunity to witness the transformation of the area’s natural landscape and learn about the geological and cultural history of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Undara Volcanic National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Undara Volcanic National Park has a rich cultural history that dates back thousands of years. Visitors can explore the local Indigenous culture and history by visiting the following attractions:

  • Undara Experience: Learn about the Indigenous culture through storytelling, music, and dance performances. You can also take a guided tour to learn about the geological and cultural history of the area.
  • Archway Explorer: Discover the geological features of the area and explore the lava tubes with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Kalkani Crater Rim Walk: Take a 12.5km round trip hike to the top of Kalkani Crater and learn about the Aboriginal history and culture.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Undara Volcanic National Park offers numerous outdoor adventures and scenic views that captivate the hearts of nature-lovers. Some of the most popular attractions include:

  • Volcano Valley Walk: This 4km round trip hiking trail provides scenic views of the park’s unique geological features. Discover the region’s flora and fauna as you walk through lava tubes, craters and old-growth forest.
  • The Bluff: Located at the edge of the park, the Bluff offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and offers an easy 1.6km walk.
  • Wildlife spotting: The park is home to a wide variety of fauna and flora species, including wallabies, tree kangaroos, echidnas, wedge-tailed eagles, and much more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Undara Volcanic National Park

Undara Volcanic National Park is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The park features a wide range of hiking/biking trails that suit different fitness levels. Here are some popular routes:

  • Cook Lookout: This 6km round trip hiking trail takes you along rocky terrain, which provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Woodfire Circuit: This 6km walk takes you along giant lava tubes, rocky outcrops and past towering pine trees.
  • Kennedy Tree: This 2.5km round trip hike takes you through the rainforest, where you can enjoy birdwatching, wildlife spotting, and breathtaking views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Undara Volcanic National Park: Activities and Tours

Undara Volcanic National Park offers visitors a chance to indulge in various activities and tours. From exploring the lava tubes to wildlife spotting and cultural experiences, there is always something for everyone. Here are some popular activities:

  • Lava Tubes Tour: Explore the park’s underground cave system with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Wildlife Safari: Experience the park’s flora and fauna, including a chance to spot some rare and endangered species.
  • Cultural Experience: Immerse yourself in the Indigenous culture and learn about their history, music, dance, and stories.

Week-Long Itinerary for Undara Volcanic National Park

Here is a sample one-week itinerary to help visitors make the most of their trip to Undara Volcanic National Park:

Day 1: Arrive at the park and check-in at your accommodation. Enjoy the park’s scenic views and indulge in some local dishes.

Day 2: Embark on the Lava Tubes Tour to explore the park’s underground cave system.

Day 3: Discover the park’s flora and fauna on a wildlife safari.

Day 4: Take a cultural experience tour to learn about the Indigenous culture and history of the area.

Day 5: Hike up to Kalkani Crater Rim Walk and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 6: Take a relaxing day to visit the park’s museums and historical sites, including the Undara Experience.

Day 7: Depart the park and make your way to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Undara Volcanic National Park

Undara Volcanic National Park offers visitors a chance to indulge in some amazing food and drinks. The region has a vibrant food and drink scene that incorporates local flavors and ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Shank: Locally sourced lamb shanks slow-cooked in a red wine sauce with roasted garlic and rosemary and served with creamy mashed potatoes.
  • Barramundi: Fresh-caught barramundi fish with mango salsa, locally sourced vegetables, and fried rice.
  • Red Claw: A local freshwater crustacean found in the local volcanic streams, typically served with garlic butter, herb butter or chilli sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Macalister Range Distillery: Offers visitors a unique gin experience, featuring high-quality and locally sourced ingredients.
  • Rainforest Bounty: This craft brewery produces a unique range of beers using locally sourced rainforest fruits such as Davidson Plums and Lilly Pillys.

Where to Stay in Undara Volcanic National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Undara Volcanic National Park offers visitors a variety of accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • Undara Experience: Offers a range of accommodation options, including campsites, cabins, and restored railway carriages.
  • Kofi House: A historic guesthouse offering a luxury stay, with three accommodation choices, premium twin, deluxe king or deluxe queen overlooking the garden.
  • Bedrock Village: A family-owned and operated hotel offering comfortable accommodation with a restaurant and outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Undara Volcanic National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Undara Volcanic National Park is a great place to shop for unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some things you should consider taking home:

  • Indigenous Artifacts: Aboriginal souvenirs and crafts made from the materials found in the natural surroundings of the park.
  • Jewellery: Jewellery made from local gems and minerals.

Local Culture and Customs of Undara Volcanic National Park

Undara Volcanic National Park has a rich cultural history and is influenced by local Aboriginal culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local Indigenous culture and learn about their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Undara Volcanic National Park

The best time to visit Undara Volcanic National Park is during the dry season, which is from May to October. During this time, the weather is dry and mild, making it easy to explore the park’s attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Undara Volcanic National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local Indigenous culture and history.
  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations, especially around hiking and biking trails.
  • Book accommodation and tours in advance.

Don’t:

  • Harm the park’s flora and fauna.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Enter restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Undara Volcanic National Park

Undara Volcanic National Park is generally safe, but visitors should still exercise caution when exploring the park’s attractions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid getting too close to wildlife and follow park rangers’ instructions if you encounter any dangerous animals.
  • Wear appropriate footwear when exploring the park’s hiking and biking trails.
  • Stay hydrated and carry enough water when exploring the park’s attractions.

Health in Undara Volcanic National Park

Visitors to Undara Volcanic National Park should be in good health and have good physical fitness. It is advisable to carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medication when visiting the park.

Getting to Undara Volcanic National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Undara Volcanic National Park is located in Far North Queensland and can be accessed via different modes of transportation. Here are some popular options:

Airports Near Undara Volcanic National Park

  • Cairns International Airport is the nearest international airport, which is about 275 km from the park.
  • Atherton Airport is the nearest regional airport, which is about 76km from the park.

Bus Stations Near Undara Volcanic National Park

  • Bus Queensland operates a daily bus service from Cairns to Mount Surprise, which is the nearest town to the park.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also drive to the park and access it via the Gulf Developmental Road, which connects Cairns and Mount Isa.

How to Get Around in Undara Volcanic National Park

Undara Volcanic National Park is a relatively small park and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take guided tours to explore specific park attractions.

Where to Go Next from Undara Volcanic National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Undara Volcanic National Park is located close to many other popular destinations in Far North Queensland. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore:

  • Cairns: A popular tourist destination that offers visitors a range of attractions, including the Great Barrier Reef, Daintree Rainforest, and Kuranda Scenic Railway.
  • Chillagoe-Mungana Caves National Park: Another popular national park that features limestone caves, hiking trails, and historic mining heritage sites.
