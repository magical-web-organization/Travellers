Introduction to Tully

Tully is a beautiful town located in the Cassowary Coast Region of Queensland, Australia. Famous for its picturesque landscapes, Tully is a heaven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. This town is known for its golden sandy beaches, cascading waterfalls, lush tropical rainforests, and exotic flora and fauna. Whether you want to soak up the sun on the beach, explore the rainforest, or indulge in outdoor activities such as hiking or snorkeling, Tully has something for everyone. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to Tully.

Local Culture and Customs of Tully

The people of Tully are warm and friendly. They take pride in their local customs and traditions, which are influenced by their history as sugar cane farmers. The town also has a strong connection to the local Aboriginal communities. Visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Tully

The best time to visit Tully is from May to October when temperatures are mild, and the weather is dry. This time is perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, water sports, and wildlife viewing. The wet season lasts from November to April and can be humid with heavy rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tully

Do:

  • Respect the local Aboriginal culture and traditions.
  • Pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and protective gear for outdoor activities.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Harm the local wildlife or environment.
  • Swim outside the designated areas on the beach.
  • Ignore safety warnings in the rainforest or park area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tully

Tully is a safe town, but visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as locking doors and windows, not leaving valuables unattended, and avoiding any unnecessary risks. Visitors should also follow the warning signs in national parks or hiking trials.

Health in Tully

Tully has a local hospital and medical facilities. Visitors should have health insurance before traveling to Tully. If planning to participate in outdoor activities, make sure to bring insect repellent, sunscreen, and protective clothes for hiking and water activities.

Getting to Tully: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tully

The nearest airport to Tully is Cairns International Airport, which is about 128km north of Tully. From there, visitors can take a shuttle service or rent a car to reach Tully.

Bus Stations Near Tully

Greyhound Australia runs buses from Cairns to Tully daily.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to explore Tully and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Tully

Bicycles and cars are available on rent in Tully town. Public transport is limited to buses that run to Cairns and nearby areas.
