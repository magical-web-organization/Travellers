A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Cairns, Australia

Cairns, located in Far North Queensland, Australia, is a tropical paradise with stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and the Great Barrier Reef. It is a popular destination for visitors from all over the world, offering a range of activities and attractions to suit all interests and budgets.

Introduction to Cairns

Cairns is a vibrant city with a rich history and a diverse culture. It is the gateway to the Great Barrier Reef, one of the world’s most famous natural wonders, but there’s much more to Cairns than just the reef. The city offers great shopping, dining, and entertainment options, and it’s surrounded by beautiful natural scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Cairns

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cairns has a collection of museums and historical attractions that offer a glimpse into the region’s past and its unique culture. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Cairns Museum: This museum tells the story of Cairns’ cultural and natural history through interactive displays and artifacts.
  • Tjapukai Aboriginal Cultural Park: This attraction showcases the culture and traditions of the local Aboriginal people through storytelling, dance, and music performances.
  • St. Monica’s Cathedral: This beautiful Gothic-style cathedral is a landmark of Cairns, and it’s a must-visit for architecture enthusiasts.
  • Cairns Botanic Gardens: This stunning garden features a diverse range of tropical plants and is perfect for a relaxing afternoon stroll.

Natural Attractions of Cairns: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cairns is surrounded by some of Australia’s most breathtaking natural scenery. From waterfalls to beaches to rainforests, here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Great Barrier Reef: A visit to Cairns wouldn’t be complete without exploring the Great Barrier Reef, one of Australia’s most iconic natural wonders. Visitors can choose from various activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, or taking a glass-bottom boat tour.
  • Kuranda Scenic Railway: This railway journey takes you through the rainforest from Cairns to Kuranda, offering stunning views of waterfalls and mountains.
  • Daintree Rainforest: The world’s oldest continuously surviving tropical rainforest is located in Cairns. The Daintree Rainforest offers hiking, wildlife tours, and authentic indigenous experiences.
  • Cairns Esplanade Lagoon: This man-made lagoon is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing, and it’s free to access.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cairns

From scenic coastal trails to rugged mountain ranges, Cairns has many excellent hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best:

  • Walsh’s Pyramid: This challenging hike takes you up the tallest freestanding pyramid in the world, offering panoramic views of Cairns and the surrounding area.
  • Barron Gorge National Park: This national park has several hiking trails, including the Barron Falls lookout track and the Douglas track.
  • Smithfield Mountain Bike Park: This park has a range of trails for all skill levels, including downhill and cross-country tracks.

Best Things to Do in Cairns: Activities and Tours

Cairns has an abundance of activities and tours to offer, whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation.

  • Skydiving: For adrenaline junkies, skydiving over Cairns is a must-do activity.
  • White Water Rafting: Cairns has several rapids for white water rafting enthusiasts, including the Barron River and Tully River.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: For a peaceful, scenic adventure, consider taking a hot air balloon ride over Cairns.
  • Stand-up Paddle Boarding: The calm waters of the Cairns Esplanade Lagoon make it the perfect spot for stand-up paddleboarding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cairns

For a full week’s itinerary, consider the following:

  • Day 1: Visit the Great Barrier Reef
  • Day 2: Explore the Daintree Rainforest and Cape Tribulation
  • Day 3: Take the Skyrail Rainforest Cableway and explore Kuranda Village
  • Day 4: Visit the Cairns Wildlife Dome and take a jet boat tour
  • Day 5: Go white water rafting or hot air ballooning
  • Day 6: Hike up Walsh’s Pyramid and explore the Atherton Tablelands area
  • Day 7: Relax at the Cairns Esplanade Lagoon and indulge in some retail therapy.

Food and Drink Scene of Cairns

Cairns has a rich food and drink scene, with something to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barramundi: A fish native to Australia, barramundi is often served grilled or fried.
  • Kangaroo: This lean meat is a staple of the Australian diet, and can be found in many Cairns restaurants.
  • Pavlova: This famous dessert is made with meringue and topped with fresh fruit and cream.

Local Beverages

  • XXXX Gold: This famous Australian beer is a Queensland favorite and pairs well with outdoor dining.
  • Bundaberg Rum: A dark rum that is distilled in Queensland and is perfect for enjoying with a mixer or on the rocks.

Where to Stay in Cairns: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cairns offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and needs. Here are some of the top options:

  • Luxury: Pullman Cairns International offers modern accommodations with a pool and spa.
  • Mid-Range: Cairns Colonial Club Resort has resort-style amenities and affordable prices.
  • Budget: Cairns Central YHA is a great option for backpackers and offers both private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in Cairns: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aboriginal Artwork: Cairns has an abundance of authentic Aboriginal artwork made by local artists.
  • Cairns Tea: Daintree Tea Company offers locally sourced and organic tea blends.
  • Opals: Cairns has many shops offering unique opal jewelry and souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Cairns

Cairns is a multicultural city, and visitors should respect local customs and culture. Some tips to keep in mind:

  • Respect Indigenous customs and beliefs.
  • Smoking in public areas is prohibited.
  • Be mindful of local wildlife and do not disturb natural habitats.

Best Time to Visit Cairns

The best time to visit Cairns is between May and September when the weather is mild, and it’s the dry season. However, keep in mind that this is peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cairns

Do:

  • Pack sunscreen and a hat.
  • Respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Touch or stand on coral.
  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Feed wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cairns

Cairns is a safe destination, but visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as:

  • Be aware of jellyfish and crocodiles in the water.
  • Lock your accommodation and car doors.
  • Use caution when swimming in unfamiliar waters.

Health in Cairns

Cairns doesn’t have any specific health risks, but visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Stay hydrated in the tropical climate.
  • Wear insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Seek medical attention if you encounter any health issues.

Getting to Cairns: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cairns has several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Cairns

  • Cairns Airport: Located a few kilometers from the city center, Cairns Airport has both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Cairns

  • Cairns Bus Station: Located on Lake Street, Cairns Bus Station connects Cairns with other destinations in Far North Queensland.

Other Options

  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing: Taxis are readily available in Cairns, and there are several ride-sharing options such as Uber and Didi.
  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available at Cairns Airport and in the city center, and it’s a convenient way to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Cairns

Cairns has several transportation options to get around the city, including:

  • Public Transport: Cairns has a bus system that connects the city with suburbs and nearby destinations.
  • Cycling: Cairns offers bike rental facilities and has several bike trails in and around the city.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing: Taxis are available throughout the city, and ride-sharing services like Uber and Didi also operate here.

Where to Go Next from Cairns: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cairns is a great base for exploring other destinations in the region, including:

  • Port Douglas: A picturesque coastal town known for its natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Palm Cove: A quiet and relaxed beach town located just a short drive from Cairns.
  • Atherton Tablelands: A region known for its scenic beauty and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Cairns is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a tropical getaway with natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and unique cultural experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to Cairns, Australia.
