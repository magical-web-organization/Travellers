Introduction to Currumbin

Located on the southern coast of the Gold Coast in Queensland, Australia, Currumbin is a popular destination for tourists who want to experience the beauty of Australia’s natural landscapes. With its warm weather, beautiful beaches, and scenic views, Currumbin is the perfect place for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Currumbin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary: This award-winning wildlife sanctuary is home to a diverse range of species, including koalas, kangaroos, and crocodiles.
  2. Dust Temple: An art gallery and music venue that showcases the works of local artists.
  3. Currumbin Vikings Surf Club: Founded in 1911, this surf club is a great place to learn about the history of surfing in Australia.

Natural Attractions of Currumbin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Currumbin Beach: This beautiful beach is perfect for swimming, surfing, and relaxing in the sun.
  2. Currumbin Creek: A peaceful waterway that is ideal for kayaking, paddleboarding, and fishing.
  3. Currumbin Rock Pools: A series of natural swimming pools created by a small waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Currumbin

  1. Currumbin Valley Rock Pools Hike: A challenging hike that takes you through stunning rainforest scenery to a series of natural rock pools.
  2. Tomewin Mountain Road Biking Route: A scenic bike ride that takes you through the beautiful hinterland of Currumbin.
  3. Currumbin Valley to Cougal Cascades: A moderately difficult hike that takes you to a beautiful waterfall and swimming hole.

Best Things to Do in Currumbin: Activities and Tours

  1. Surfing Lessons: Take surfing lessons with a local expert and learn how to ride the waves like a pro.
  2. Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary Segway Tour: Take a guided tour of the wildlife sanctuary on a Segway.
  3. Balloon Aloft Gold Coast: Soar above the beautiful landscape of Currumbin in a hot air balloon.

Food and Drink Scene of Currumbin

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips from Currumbin Beach Fish and Chips: This local restaurant serves up some of the best fish and chips in Australia.
  2. Acai Bowl from B-Social: This healthy breakfast option is made with fresh fruits and granola.
  3. Pizzas from Currumbin Alley Surf School Cafe: Located right on Currumbin Beach, this cafe serves up some great pizzas.

Local Beverages

  1. Burleigh Brewing Company: This award-winning local brewery serves up some great craft beers.
  2. Barefoot Barista: This local coffee shop serves up some excellent coffee and other beverages.

Where to Stay in Currumbin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sanctuary Beach Resort: Located right on Currumbin Beach, this resort offers stunning ocean views and comfortable accommodations.
  2. Surf Street Motel: A budget-friendly option that is just a short walk from Currumbin Beach.
  3. Currumbin Sands on the Beach: This beachfront resort offers spacious apartments and stunning ocean views.

What to Buy in Currumbin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Aboriginal Art: There are several art galleries in Currumbin that specialize in Aboriginal art.
  2. Surfing Gear: Currumbin is known for its excellent surfing, so why not take home some gear to remember your trip?
  3. Local Craft Beer: Burleigh Brewing Company is just one of several local breweries that you can buy craft beer from.

Local Culture and Customs of Currumbin

One of the most important things to know about the local culture in Currumbin is that surfing is a way of life here. You’ll see people of all ages out on the waves, and it’s not uncommon to strike up a conversation with a local surfer.

Best Time to Visit Currumbin

The best time to visit Currumbin is from March to May or September to November, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller than during the peak summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Currumbin

Do:

  1. Do respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Do try surfing, even if you’re a beginner.
  3. Do take care of the environment and leave no trace.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter.
  2. Don’t park your car on the beach.
  3. Don’t disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Currumbin

Currumbin is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s still important to take standard safety precautions. Make sure to lock up your valuables and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Currumbin

There are no major health concerns in Currumbin, but it’s always a good idea to bring insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Currumbin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Currumbin

  1. Gold Coast Airport: Located just 5 kilometers away, Gold Coast Airport is the closest airport to Currumbin.
  2. Brisbane Airport: Located about 90 minutes away by car, Brisbane Airport is another option for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Currumbin

  1. Currumbin RSL Bus Stop
  2. Currumbin Beach Bus Stop

Other Options

  1. Taxi: You can easily hail a taxi to take you around Currumbin.
  2. Car Rental: There are several car rental companies in the area that offer affordable rates.

How to Get Around in Currumbin

The best way to get around Currumbin is by foot, bike, or car rental. The area is relatively small, so it’s easy to explore on foot. Biking is also a great way to see the sights.

Where to Go Next from Currumbin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Gold Coast: Just a short drive away, the Gold Coast is packed with attractions, including theme parks, beaches, and nightlife.
  2. Brisbane: About 90 minutes away by car, Brisbane is a bustling city with plenty to see and do.
  3. Byron Bay: Just over the border in New South Wales, Byron Bay is a laid-back beach town with a relaxed vibe.

Conclusion

With its beautiful beaches, natural attractions, and friendly locals, Currumbin is the perfect destination for anyone who wants to experience the best of Australia’s surf culture and natural beauty. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!
