Introduction to Tweed Heads

Tweed Heads is located in Gold Coast, in South East Queensland, in Australia. Nestled on the border of New South Wales and Queensland, Tweed Heads is a charming coastal town that offers visitors an array of cultural, natural, and recreational attractions. It’s a popular destination for those seeking a quieter holiday, without having to sacrifice proximity to the Gold Coast’s exhilarating nightlife and theme parks. In this guide, we’ll explore all Tweed Heads has to offer to help you make the most of your trip to this unique destination.

List of Places to Visit in Tweed Heads

If you’re looking for things to do in Tweed Heads, you’ll find a diverse range of activities that cater to all interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in Tweed Heads.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tweed Regional Gallery & Margaret Olley Art Centre: This art gallery hosts both Australian and international exhibitions and is located near the Tweed Heads Airport. The gallery has a focus on contemporary art and is a must-visit for art lovers.
  • Tweed Heads Historic Site: The Tweed Heads Historic Site showcases the history of the local area with interactive exhibits, displays, and guided tours. Here you can learn more about the indigenous Bundjalung people, as well as early European settlers and their impact on the area.

Natural Attractions of Tweed Heads: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fingal Head Lighthouse: The Fingal Head Lighthouse is located on the southern side of the Tweed River entrance. The historic lighthouse was built in 1878 and is still operating today and you can catch glimpses of whales during their migration season from here.
  • Tweed River: The Tweed River runs along the border between Queensland and New South Wales, and offers excellent opportunities for fishing, boating, and kayaking in scenic surroundings.
  • Mount Warning: Mount Warning is an impressive volcanic formation that is considered to be one of the most ancient landmarks in the world. Visitors can hike to the top and take in panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Coolangatta Beach: One of the most well-known beaches in the area, Coolangatta Beach is a great spot for swimming, surfing, or simply soaking up the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tweed Heads

Tweed Heads is located in an area that is rich in natural beauty, and as such, it offers many hiking and biking routes that showcase the stunning scenery of the area. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Tweed Heads include:

  • The Tweed Link Trail: This well-marked trail stretches from the Tweed Valley to the coast, encompassing ancient rainforest, babbling brooks, and charming rural communities along the way.
  • Mount Warning Summit: This is a challenging hike but it’s worth the effort to reach the summit and gaze over the 360-degree views of the surrounding area. The trek to the summit of Mount Warning is about 4.5 km each way, and it takes around five hours to complete.
  • Cudgen Creek: For those who prefer a less strenuous trek, the path around Cudgen Creek is an easy and picturesque walk suitable for hikers of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Tweed Heads: Activities and Tours

Tweed Heads offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are just a few ideas for things to do during your stay:

  • Surfing Lessons: The stunning beaches in Tweed Heads are ideal for surfing, and you can find surf schools that offer lessons for beginners and experienced surfers alike.
  • Kayaking or Stand-Up Paddle Boarding (SUP): Take an SUP or kayak tour along the Tweed River and take in the many wildlife and bird species in the area.
  • Horse Riding: Enjoy horse riding in the beautiful countryside surrounding Tweed Heads.
  • Tweed Coast Tours: These tours take visitors on a guided tour of the area’s most significant sites, including cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and food and drink hotspots.

Food and Drink Scene of Tweed Heads

Tweed Heads offers a range of dining options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tweed Valley Beef: This is a popular dish in the area, and it’s made using locally raised beef that is grass-fed and free-range.
  • Cameron’s Oysters: The Tweed River is home to some of Australia’s best oysters, which are harvested by the Cameron family and served fresh at local eateries.
  • Moo Moo’s Burger Barn: Enjoy tender, juicy burgers sourced from the famous Byron Bay Hinterland.

Local Beverages

  • Stone & Wood: This local brewery is famous throughout Australia for its craft beer, which you can sample at the brewing facility.
  • Tweed Valley Organic Coffee: The Tweed Valley region produces excellent coffee and has many cafes that offer the best brews in town.
  • Husk Distillery: This boutique distillery offers a unique range of spirits including Ink Gin and spiced rum which are worth trying.

Where to Stay in Tweed Heads: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tweed Heads offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best accommodations options in the area include:

  • Peppers Salt Resort & Spa: This luxury resort offers stunning views of the ocean, superb dining options, and a full range of spa treatments to help you unwind and relax.
  • Best Western Kennedy Drive Motel: This comfortable motel provides friendly service and is conveniently located near the airport and local attractions. It’s an excellent option for budget-conscious travelers.
  • The Hideaway Hostel: This hostel provides clean and affordable dorms and private rooms, perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Tweed Heads: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tweed Heads is home to many local markets offering unique and handmade products. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to take home as gifts include:

  • Cudgen Honey: This locally sourced honey is 100% natural and is produced by the Cudgen Landcare group, which fosters sustainable agriculture practices.
  • Tweed River Pecans: Pecans from the Tweed River region are renowned for their flavor and freshness, and they’re a great souvenir to take home or as a gift.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Tweed Heads has several markets with handmade jewelry vendors that offer pieces of accessories made from local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Tweed Heads

The Bundjalung people are the traditional owners of the Tweed Coast area, and they have a rich cultural heritage that is still evident today through art, language, and spirituality. Visitors should show respect for the land and the local people’s beliefs, so try to learn more about the culture and customs of the area before visiting.

Best Time to Visit Tweed Heads

Tweed Heads is a year-round destination, but visitors can experience the most pleasant weather and avoid the crowds by visiting during the shoulder season of late autumn to early spring (April-June and September-November.)

Do’s and Don’ts of Tweed Heads

Do:

  • Respect the environment and local culture.
  • Try the locally sourced honey and fresh pecans.
  • Participate in outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and horse riding.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces or feed the native wildlife.
  • Assume the climate will always be warm and sunny, as it can rain or become chilly during winter months.
  • Feel limited to the nearby areas, Tweed Heads is a great base to explore both Queensland and New South Wales.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tweed Heads

Tweed Heads is a generally safe place for tourists, but visitors should still take common sense precautions like not leaving valuables in their cars and locking doors and windows when leaving accommodation. There are plenty of first aid centers, and emergency services are available in the region.

Health in Tweed Heads

The sun can be intense in the Tweed Coast area, and visitors should take precautions to avoid sunburns, wear sunscreen, and stay hydrated. There are several medical centers, hospitals, and pharmacies in the area.

Getting to Tweed Heads: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tweed Heads

  • Gold Coast Airport: the nearest airport to Tweed Heads, located 5 km away.
  • Brisbane Airport: located approximately 100 km from Tweed Heads.

Bus Stations Near Tweed Heads

  • Tweed City Shopping Centre Bus Terminal
  • Coolangatta Bus Station
  • Murwillumbah Bus Station

Other Options

  • Self-drive: Tweed Heads is easily accessible via the Pacific Highway, which links Brisbane and the Gold Coast to the north and Byron Bay and Sydney to the south.
  • Taxi Services: Various taxi services are available in the region.
  • Car rental: Car rentals are available from multiple vendors, including the airport and major city centres.

How to Get Around in Tweed Heads

Tweed Heads is a small coastal town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or on rented bicycles. For longer excursions, visitors can take advantage of public transportation or car rentals.

Where to Go Next from Tweed Heads: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Byron Bay: Just a short drive south of Tweed Heads, Byron Bay is a popular destination known for its crystal-clear beaches and chilled-out vibe.
  • Gold Coast: The Gold Coast is famous for its vibrant nightlife, theme parks, and water sports.
  • Ballina: to the south of Tweed Heads, Ballina is a charming coastal town offering a laid-back atmosphere with beautiful parks and beaches.

Conclusion

Tweed Heads has a little something for everyone, whether you’re into outdoor activities, local culture, relaxing beachside, or ocean-side fine dining. With this comprehensive guide, you’re bound to have a memorable experience staying in Tweed Heads.
