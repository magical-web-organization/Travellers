Introduction to Northern suburbs

Located in Gold Coast, the Northern suburbs is a popular travel destination for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. This guide is your go-to resource for planning your trip to Northern suburbs, with detailed information on the best things to see and do, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Northern suburbs

Northern suburbs offer an array of exciting destinations to explore. Here are some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gold Coast Museum: Featuring a wide range of interactive exhibits, this museum is the perfect place to learn about Gold Coast’s rich history.
  • Samford Museum: Visit this museum to see a collection of historical artifacts, including photographs, clothing, and furniture.
  • Caboolture Historical Village: This interactive outdoor museum offers a glimpse into the past with its collection of restored buildings and antique vehicles.

Natural Attractions of Northern suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glass House Mountains National Park: Hike to the top of these striking peaks to experience panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Moreton Island: A popular destination for watersports, with abundant marine life and stunning beaches to explore.
  • Springbrook National Park: Discover the lush rainforests, stunning waterfalls, and unique wildlife of this World Heritage-listed park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern suburbs

For outdoor enthusiasts, Northern suburbs offer an abundance of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Moreton Bay Cycleway: A scenic path that follows the coastline from Sandgate to Redcliffe.
  • Mount Coolum National Park: A moderately challenging climb to the summit of Mount Coolum, offering stunning views of the coast and hinterland.
  • Bunyaville Conservation Park: A popular hiking and mountain biking destination with over 25km of trails to explore.

Best Things to Do in Northern suburbs: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Northern suburbs, here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Whale watching tours: Between July and October, you can witness the spectacle of humpback whales migrating along the Gold Coast.
  • Take a scenic helicopter tour over the Glass House Mountains or Moreton Island for panoramic views.
  • Visit the famous Eumundi Markets for local crafts, clothing, and fresh produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern suburbs

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Northern suburbs, here is an itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit Gold Coast Museum and take a scenic drive to Glass House Mountains National Park.
2-3 Explore Moreton Island and enjoy watersports, snorkelling, and marine wildlife viewing.
4 Hike to the top of Mount Coolum and visit the nearby beach towns of Noosa and Maroochydore.
5-6 Explore Springbrook National Park and take a scenic helicopter tour over the landscape.
7 Visit the Eumundi Markets for local crafts and fresh produce before departing from Northern suburbs.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern suburbs

Northern suburbs offer a wide range of culinary delights to savour. Here are some of the dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Prawn and crab dishes: Freshly caught prawns and crabs are local favourites, often served grilled or in seafood platters.
  • Australian meat pies: A favourite snack to enjoy on the go, these pastries are filled with meat and savoury gravy.
  • Pavlova: A meringue-based dessert topped with fresh fruit and cream and named after famed ballerina Anna Pavlova.

Local Beverages

  • Australian wines: Featuring world-famous wine regions like Barossa Valley and Margaret River, Australian vineyards produce some of the world’s best wines.
  • Local breweries: Sample craft beers from local breweries, such as the Hinterland Brewery in Mount Tamborine.
  • Bundaberg rum: A smooth and silky Australian rum that has become a popular souvenir to bring home.

Where to Stay in Northern suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern suburbs offer a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to consider include:

  • Sebel Brisbane: This luxurious hotel offers spacious rooms with floor-to-ceiling windows and stunning city views.
  • W Brisbane: A chic and modern hotel located on the edge of the Brisbane River with spacious rooms and incredible views.
  • Nomads Backpackers Brisbane: A budget-friendly hostel with a swimming pool, bar, and regular social events.

What to Buy in Northern suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home from your trip to Northern suburbs, consider these local products:

  • Aboriginal artwork: From traditional dot paintings to contemporary designs, Northern suburbs is home to a thriving Aboriginal arts community.
  • Australian opals: Often referred to as the national gemstone, opals are known for their iridescent display and are found in Queensland’s outback mines.
  • Tim Tams: A famous Australian cookie available in a wide range of flavours, these biscuits are perfect for snacking and gifting.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern suburbs

When visiting Northern suburbs, it’s important to respect and appreciate the local culture and customs. Australians value honesty, directness, and a sense of humour. It is also important to be aware of the welfare of Australia’s indigenous peoples.

Best Time to Visit Northern suburbs

The best time to visit Northern suburbs is during the dry season, from June to October, when the weather is mild and cool. The summer months, from December to February, can be hot and humid, with tropical downpours that might spoil your travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern suburbs

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Northern suburbs:

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.
  • Wear sunscreen, a hat, and comfortable footwear when exploring the outdoors.
  • Carry cash when visiting local markets and small shops.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t swim in the ocean outside of designated swimming areas as there may be strong currents.
  • Don’t approach wild animals, including kangaroos and snakes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern suburbs

Northern suburbs is generally very safe for visitors, but it is important to take the usual safety precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Keep valuables in a secure place and avoid unlit areas at night.

Health in Northern suburbs

Australian medical facilities are of a high standard. It is recommended that you purchase comprehensive travel insurance before travelling to Northern suburbs.

Getting to Northern suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northern suburbs are accessible by a range of transportation options. Here are some of the most common ways to get there.

Airports Near Northern suburbs

  • Brisbane International Airport: One of Australia’s busiest airports, located a short drive from Northern suburbs.
  • Gold Coast Airport: This smaller airport is also well-connected by flights and transfers to Northern suburbs.

Bus Stations Near Northern suburbs

  • Roma Street Station: This large transport hub in Brisbane’s CBD services bus and rail connections to Northern suburbs.
  • Maroochydore Bus Station: Located in the nearby town of Maroochydore, this bus station services the Sunshine Coast region.

Other Options

  • Car rental: This is the best option if you plan to explore the area. There are many rental car companies available at the airports and in Brisbane city centre.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and Uber services are available in the area, but they are more expensive than public transport.

How to Get Around in Northern suburbs

Northern suburbs offer a range of transportation options to help visitors to explore. Some of the best ways to get around include:

  • Public transport: The TransLink public transport network is the best option for budget-friendly travel in the area.
  • Car rental: Rent a car to drive around Northern suburbs at your own pace and convenience.
  • Cycling: Cycling is a popular way to get around Northern suburbs, with designated cycle lanes and paths available in many areas.

Where to Go Next from Northern suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Queensland, here are some destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Gold Coast: Famous for its theme parks and stunning beaches, the Gold Coast is just a short drive from Northern suburbs.
  • Sunshine Coast: With its beach towns, wildlife parks, and pristine coastlines, the Sunshine Coast is perfect for a relaxing vacation.
  • Fraser Island: A world heritage-protected sand island with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure and wildlife viewing.

Conclusion

Northern suburbs offer a range of exciting attractions and outdoor adventures for visitors to explore.
