Brisbane, situated in South East Queensland, is the third largest city in Australia, famous for its sunny weather, outdoor activities, and cultural landmarks. This cosmopolitan city has a lot to offer, from the iconic Brisbane River to the scenic views of Moreton Bay, from the cultural landmarks to the adventurous hiking trails. In this ultimate travel guide to Brisbane, we will help you explore the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, hiking routes, food, and drinks scene, along with local customs, safety, and health tips, transportation options, and recommended nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Brisbane

Whether you love exploring cultural landmarks or outdoor adventures, Brisbane has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit places in Brisbane:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Queensland Cultural Centre: Featuring the Queensland Museum, Science Centre, and Art Gallery, this cultural hub is a great place to explore history and culture.
  • Brisbane City Hall: This historic building is home to the Museum of Brisbane and offers a panoramic view of the city from the Clock Tower.
  • Newstead House: A colonial-era mansion that is a window into Brisbane’s past.
  • South Bank Parklands: A cultural precinct with beaches, restaurants, shops, theaters, and the Wheel of Brisbane.

Natural Attractions of Brisbane: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Coot-tha Lookout: This scenic lookout offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding areas.
  • Brisbane Botanic Gardens: Spread over 52 hectares, the Botanic Gardens is a must-visit for nature lovers and botanists.
  • Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary: A wildlife reserve, home to over 130 koalas, as well as kangaroos, Tasmanian devils, and wombats.
  • Moreton Island: A scenic island that is a popular destination for whale watching, dolphin feeding, and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brisbane

Brisbane is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in Australia. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Mount Coot-tha Summit Trail: A challenging 7.5km trail that rewards hikers with panoramic views of wildlife and plants.
  • Enoggera Reservoir Circuit: An 11km hike best enjoyed by experienced hikers.
  • Brisbane Valley Rail Trail: A 162km bike trail that starts in Ipswich and ends in Yarraman.
  • Gold Creek Reservoir Circuit: An easy 5.4km hike with stunning views and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Brisbane: Activities and Tours

Brisbane offers a wide range of tours, activities, and experiences for every type of traveler. Here are some best things to do in Brisbane:

  • Explore the city on a river cruise or the CityCat Ferry.
  • Visit the Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary and feed the kangaroos.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over Brisbane.
  • Go on a food and drink tour and sample Brisbane’s best cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brisbane

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Brisbane:

Day 1 Arrival, exploring South Bank and the City Botanic Gardens, and the Wheel of Brisbane
Day 2 Visit the Queensland Cultural Centre, Museum of Brisbane, and the Brisbane City Hall
Day 3 Explore Mount Coot-tha, Brisbane Botanic Gardens, and the Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary
Day 4 Take a day trip to Moreton Island and go whale watching, sand-dune tobogganing, or snorkeling
Day 5 Take a hot air balloon ride over Brisbane or explore the Gold Creek Reservoir Circuit
Day 6 Enjoy a food and drink tour of Brisbane, sampling local delights
Day 7 Departure day, maybe catch a river cruise or sightseeing tour before heading home

Food and Drink Scene of Brisbane

Brisbane has a vibrant food and drinks scene, with a range of cuisines and local products. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips with tartare sauce
  • Meat pies topped with mushy peas or tomato sauce
  • Pavlova, a meringue dessert topped with fresh fruit
  • Barramundi, a tasty fish with a unique flavor

Local Beverages

  • XXXX Gold, a classic Australian beer
  • Bundaberg ginger beer, an iconic ginger beer that is a must-try in Australia
  • Wines from the Queensland Granite Belt, a wine region in southern Queensland
  • Fresh juice from the Sunshine Coast or the Brisbane Valley

Local Culture and Customs of Brisbane

Brisbane culture is open and welcoming, with a laid-back attitude and a love for outdoors and adventure. When in Brisbane, respect local culture by following these customs:

  • Greet people with a smile and a “G’day!”.
  • Do not litter, and recycle where possible.
  • Respect Aboriginal culture and heritage, including sacred sites.
  • Follow road safety rules, especially when driving on the left side of the road.

Best Time to Visit Brisbane

Brisbane has a sunny subtropical climate, with mild winters and hot summers. The best time to visit Brisbane is from March to May or from September to November, when temperatures are pleasant, and the city is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brisbane

Do:

  • Drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen and a hat, to protect yourself from the intense sun.
  • Take a hat, hiking gear, and insect repellent when exploring outdoor areas.
  • Use public transportation, like buses or trains, to move around the city.
  • Be respectful of local customs, heritage, and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Don’t swim in unpatrolled areas or during strong tide conditions.
  • Don’t touch or interact with wildlife, including koalas, without a guide’s supervision.
  • Don’t jaywalk, cross streets, and intersections only at designated places.

Safety and Health

Brisbane is a safe and healthy city, but like any city, there are some safety and health risks to be aware of.

Safety in Brisbane

  • Watch your belongings, especially in crowded areas or public transportation.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.
  • Be aware of water and fire hazards during outdoor activities.

Health in Brisbane

  • Stay hydrated, wear a hat and sunscreen to protect from sunburn and heatstroke.
  • Be cautious of poisonous animals, like snakes and spiders, and know the steps to take if bitten.
  • Be aware of mosquito-borne illnesses, like dengue fever, and take precautions when outside during dawn and dusk.

Getting to Brisbane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brisbane has several transportation options, making it easy to reach the city and travel around.

Airports Near Brisbane

  • Brisbane Airport, located 15km from the city center, is the primary airport and serves domestic and international flights.
  • Gold Coast Airport, located 100km from Brisbane, is another option for domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Brisbane

  • Brisbane Transit Centre: A major bus terminal that serves several bus companies and routes across Australia.
  • Roma Street Transit Centre: Another bus station, located adjacent to the Roma Street Train Station.

Other Options

  • Catch the train from the Brisbane airport to the city center or other destinations.
  • Rent a car or hire a taxi to reach your destination in Brisbane and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Brisbane

Brisbane has several transportation options to move around the city:

  • CityCat Ferries: A great way to explore the city while enjoying scenic views of the river.
  • Public Transportation: Buses, trains, and trams run regularly through Brisbane’s central business district and surrounding areas.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies are available in Brisbane.
  • Bikes: Brisbane has a bike-sharing system with bike stations across the city.

Where to Go Next from Brisbane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brisbane is surrounded by several exciting destinations, and here are some must-see places you can explore:

  • Gold Coast: Famous for its beaches, nightlife, and amusement parks, Gold Coast is a popular tourist destination just an hour’s drive from Brisbane.
  • Sunshine Coast: Known for its beautiful beaches, charming towns, and scenic views, Sunshine Coast is just a short drive from Brisbane.
  • Fraser Island: The largest sand island in the world, Fraser Island, is a must-visit for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Conclusion

Brisbane is a vibrant and exciting city with something to offer every traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have an enjoyable and memorable trip to Brisbane. Remember to take safety precautions, respect local culture, and enjoy exploring all the best that Brisbane has to offer.
