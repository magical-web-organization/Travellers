Introduction to Redland City

Located in the south-east of Queensland, Redland City is a beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Redland City is primarily known for its picturesque islands, beautiful coastal areas, and stunning mountain ranges.

In this travel guide for Redland City, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip successfully. From top attractions and hiking routes to where to stay and the best time to visit, this guide has everything you need for an unforgettable vacation in Redland City.

List of Places to Visit in Redland City

Redland City is a destination that has something for everyone. Here is our list of top attractions to visit in Redland City:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Redland Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Redland City. Visitors can explore the exhibits to learn about the area’s indigenous people, early explorers, and the development of the region.

  2. The Old Schoolhouse Gallery: Housed in a heritage-listed school building, this gallery showcases the work of local artists and craftspeople.

  3. Cleveland Point Light: This lighthouse has been in operation since 1864 and is an important historical landmark in the region. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for stunning views of Moreton Bay.

Natural Attractions of Redland City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. North Stradbroke Island: This island is a popular destination for outdoor activities such as hiking, surfing, and fishing. Visitors can also enjoy the stunning views from Point Lookout, which offers panoramic views of Moreton Bay and the Pacific Ocean.

  2. Mt Cotton Parklands: This large park offers a range of outdoor activities like picnics, hiking trails, and wildlife watching. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the park to learn about the native flora and fauna.

  3. Moreton Bay Marine Park: This park is located off the coast of Redland City and is home to a range of marine wildlife such as dolphins, sea turtles, and dugongs. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the park’s waterways and see the wildlife up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redland City

Redland City is a fantastic destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes to try:

  1. Bayview Conservation Area: This area has a range of trails that cater to a range of difficulty levels. Visitors can explore the native bushland and see native wildlife such as wallabies and koalas.

  2. Alexandra Hills Reservoir: This reservoir has a walking track around the perimeter that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  3. Flinders-Goolman Conservation Estate: This estate has a range of hiking trails that lead through the bushland and offer panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Redland City: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and visiting popular attractions, Redland City has other exciting things to do. Here are some of our recommendations:

  1. Take a Wine Tour: Redland City is famous for its wineries, and visitors can take guided tours to explore the vineyards, sample a range of wines, and learn about the winemaking process.

  2. Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: There are some fantastic diving and snorkeling spots around Redland City, and visitors can explore the underwater world and see a range of marine life such as tropical fish, turtles, and dolphins.

  3. Visit a Farmers’ Market: Redland City has a range of markets that sell fresh produce, local crafts, and artisanal products. Visitors can sample fresh produce, purchase souvenirs, and learn about the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Redland City

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long stay in Redland City:

Day 1: Arrival and exploring Cleveland Point.

Day 2: Visiting Redland Museum and Old Schoolhouse Gallery.

Day 3: Exploring North Stradbroke Island.

Day 4: Hiking through Bayview Conservation Area and Alexandra Hills Reservoir.

Day 5: Taking a scenic drive through the countryside and visiting some wineries.

Day 6: Exploring Moreton Bay Marine Park and trying scuba diving or snorkelling.

Day 7: Shopping and visiting local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Redland City

Here’s some information about the food and drink scene in Redland City:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Seafood Platter: Redland City is known for its fresh seafood, and the local restaurants serve up some amazing seafood platters.

  2. Australian Meat Pie: A classic dish that’s popular in Redland City.

  3. Fruit Salad: With Redland City being a fruit-growing hub, the local fruit salad is a must-try dish.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Redland City has some fantastic craft breweries that serve up a range of local beers.

  2. Wines: The area’s wineries are also famous for producing some fantastic locally made wines.

Where to Stay in Redland City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Redland City has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Cleveland Motor Inn: An affordable option for travelers looking for a comfortable stay in the heart of Cleveland.

  2. Redland Bay Motel: A clean and affordable motel located just a short drive from major attractions in the area.

  3. The Lighthouse on Straddie: A luxury accommodation option located on North Stradbroke Island. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the ocean and stay in beautifully-appointed rooms.

What to Buy in Redland City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy in Redland City:

  1. Fruit baskets: Redland City is famous for its fresh fruit so take some home.

  2. Locally made cheese: The area has some great cheesemakers who make some fantastic cheese products.

  3. Soaps and body products: There are a range of local artisans making soaps and body products made with natural ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Redland City

The region is culturally diverse, and there is a strong indigenous presence in the area. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs. Many Aboriginal communities run tours to educate visitors on their heritage and culture.

Best Time to Visit Redland City

The best time to visit Redland City is between March and May and between September and November. During these periods, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer months (December to February) can be hot and humid, and the region can be overcrowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redland City

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Be mindful of the environment and follow responsible tourism practices.

  3. Wear sunscreen and a hat when spending time outdoors.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural environment.

  2. Take anything from the natural environment as a souvenir.

  3. Ignore warning signs and swim in dangerous waters.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redland City

Redland City has relatively low crime rates, and it’s considered safe for tourists. Visitors should still exercise caution, especially when travelling alone at night.

Health in Redland City

Australia has a high standard of healthcare. Visitors can access medical services through the public hospitals or through private clinics.

Getting to Redland City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redland City

Brisbane Airport is the closest international airport to Redland City. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to reach Redland City.

Bus Stations Near Redland City

One way to reach Redland City is through bus services operated by Translink. There are also regular bus services to North Stradbroke Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Redland City by ferry services or private car.

How to Get Around in Redland City

Redland City has a reliable public transport system operated by Translink. Visitors can also hire cars or bicycles or join guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Redland City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Redland City is located close to some of the top attractions in South East Queensland. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  1. Brisbane: The state capital has a range of attractions, including the South Bank Parklands and the Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary.

  2. Gold Coast: A popular travel destination for its beautiful beaches and theme parks.

Conclusion

Redland City is a stunning destination that offers a range of activities for visitors. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, history, or culture, Redland City has something for you.
