Introduction to South East Queensland

Located in Queensland, Australia, South East Queensland is a vibrant region that offers a diverse mix of cultural and natural attractions. It encompasses a broad range of destinations, including popular cities such as Brisbane, the Gold Coast, and the Sunshine Coast, as well as smaller towns and picturesque countryside.

South East Queensland is known for its fantastic weather, with a warm and sunny climate that entices outdoor enthusiasts and beach-lovers. The region also offers a unique chance to explore Australia’s indigenous culture and history, as well as its contemporary culinary scene.

Whether you’re here for a short weekend break or an extended holiday, South East Queensland has something for everyone. Let’s begin our journey with a list of the top places to visit in the region.

List of Places to Visit in South East Queensland

When you visit South East Queensland, you will find an extensive range of attractions and destinations. Here are a few highlights to explore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Queensland Museum and Sciencentre, Brisbane
  • The Workshops Rail Museum, Ipswich
  • Abbey Museum of Art and Archaeology, Caboolture
  • Australian Museum of Clothing and Textiles, Caloundra

Natural Attractions of South East Queensland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moreton Island National Park
  • Glass House Mountains National Park
  • Tamborine National Park, Gold Coast Hinterland
  • Lamington National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of South East Queensland

South East Queensland is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to satisfy even the most avid adventurers. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Mt Coot-tha Summit Track, Brisbane
  • The Great Walk, Gold Coast Hinterland
  • Sunshine Coast Hinterland Great Walk, Sunshine Coast
  • Noosa Trail Network, Noosa

Best Things to Do in South East Queensland: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day out or an adrenaline-packed adventure, South East Queensland has plenty to offer. Here are a few of our favorite things to do:

  • Catch a wave at Surfers Paradise, Gold Coast
  • Visit the Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary, Brisbane
  • Explore the underwater world at Sea World, Gold Coast
  • Take a scenic hot air balloon ride over the Gold Coast

Local Culture and Customs of South East Queensland

When traveling in South East Queensland, it’s essential to respect the local customs and culture. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect Australia’s Indigenous culture and heritage, and learn about its history
  • Try the local cuisine, including some traditional ingredients and dishes
  • Take off your shoes when entering a home or sacred space
  • Use “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals

Best Time to Visit South East Queensland

South East Queensland is blessed with a sunny climate, making it a popular destination year-round. However, the best times to visit are between March and May and between September and November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of South East Queensland

When traveling in South East Queensland, keep these tips in mind:

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect against the harsh sun
  • Tip 10% at restaurants if service is exceptional
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months
  • Use public transport where possible

Don’t:

  • Leave your rubbish behind in nature reserves
  • Swim at unpatrolled beaches
  • Remove native plants, animals or heritage items
  • Forget to buy travel insurance

Safety and Health

When traveling to South East Queensland, it is essential to consider safety and health matters.

Safety in South East Queensland

  • Use designated pedestrian crossings when crossing roads
  • Keep valuables and personal items secure
  • Stay aware of your surroundings when traveling alone
  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean

Health in South East Queensland

  • Apply sunscreen liberally and frequently to avoid sunburn
  • Stay well hydrated and carry water with you at all times
  • Seek medical assistance in case of any health issues
  • Be aware of food allergies and avoid consuming unfamiliar foods
