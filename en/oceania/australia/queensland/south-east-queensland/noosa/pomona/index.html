Introduction to Pomona

Pomona is a charming town located in the Noosa Shire, one of the most popular destinations on the Sunshine Coast. It is a perfect place for nature lovers and adventure seekers, as it offers a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic views.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Pomona, we will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan a perfect trip to this beautiful town. From the best places to visit to the local culture and customs, we’ve got you covered. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Pomona

Pomona offers a variety of attractions, from historical monuments and cultural events to breathtaking landscapes. Here are some of the must-visit places in Pomona:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Noosa Museum: A local history museum that showcases the history, culture, and heritage of Noosa region.
  • Cooran Tableland National Park: A scenic park located between Pomona and Cooran, offering magnificent views of the Noosa hinterland.
  • Pomona Railway Station: A historical train station that was built in the early 1900s and still serves as a gateway to the Noosa hinterland.

Natural Attractions of Pomona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pomona Lookout: A lookout that provides stunning views of the hinterland and the surrounding mountains.
  • Mount Cooroora: A popular hiking spot that offers breathtaking views of the Sunshine Coast.
  • Mary River Kayak and Canoe Trails: Explore the Mary River and its surrounding wildlife, including turtles, platypus, and birdlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pomona

Pomona is a heaven for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best trails that you can explore in Pomona:

  • Mount Cooroora Summit Trail: A challenging 3km trail that leads to the summit of Mount Cooroora, offering panoramic views of the hinterland.
  • Noosa Trail Network: A network of trails that covers more than 100km and takes you through some of the most scenic spots in the region.
  • Pomona Rail Trail: A 25km trail that follows the route of the old railway line from Cooroy to Pomona.

Best Things to Do in Pomona: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, there are plenty of things to do in Pomona. Here are some recommendations:

  • Pomona Country Markets: Held on the second and fourth Saturday of each month, this is a perfect place to shop for local produce, crafts, and gifts.
  • Noosa Trail Adventure Company: Offers horse-riding, kayaking, and mountain-biking tours through the beautiful Noosa hinterland.
  • Pomona King of the Mountain Festival: Held every July, this festival is a fun-filled weekend that includes a race up Mt. Cooroora, live music, and local food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pomona

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Pomona, here is a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Noosa Museum and explore Pomona town center
Day 2 Hike up Mount Cooroora and enjoy the views
Day 3 Take a kayak or canoe tour on the Mary River
Day 4 Explore the Noosa Trail Network on a mountain bike tour
Day 5 Attend Pomona Country Markets and shop for local produce
Day 6 Visit Cooran Tableland National Park and enjoy a picnic lunch
Day 7 Attend the Pomona King of the Mountain Festival

Food and Drink Scene of Pomona

Pomona’s food and drink scene offers an array of options for foodies and wine lovers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Noosa Red Tomatoes: These fresh and juicy tomatoes are a popular ingredient in many local dishes.
  • Prawn and Scallop Laksa: A spicy and flavorful noodle soup with prawn and scallop.
  • Macadamia Nut Tart: A delicious dessert made with macadamia nuts, a popular local ingredient.

Local Beverages

  • Noosa Black: A rich and full-bodied coffee that is roasted locally.
  • Kin Kin Ginger Beer: A refreshing and slightly spicy ginger beer that is produced in a nearby town.
  • Flame Hill Vineyard Wines: These award-winning wines are produced on a beautiful vineyard located in the Noosa hinterland.

Where to Stay in Pomona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pomona offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Pomona Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Pomona, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • Noosa Avalon Farm Cottages: These cozy and charming cottages are nestled among the hills of the Noosa hinterland and offer breathtaking views.
  • Cooran Acacia Bed and Breakfast: This beautiful bed and breakfast is located in a 100-year-old house and offers a relaxing and peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Pomona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home as a reminder of your trip to Pomona:

  • Noosa Red Tomatoes: Buy these fresh and juicy tomatoes at the Pomona Country Markets.
  • Kin Kin Natural Skincare: This natural skincare line is made locally and uses organic ingredients.
  • Flame Hill Vineyard Wines: Take home a bottle of award-winning wine from the beautiful Flame Hill Vineyard.

Local Culture and Customs of Pomona

Pomona’s local culture and customs reflect the laid-back and friendly atmosphere of the Sunshine Coast. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the natural environment and the wildlife.
  • Follow the dress code and etiquette when attending local events or visiting cultural attractions.
  • Take your time to explore the region and appreciate its natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Pomona

The best time to visit Pomona is between September and November or from March to May, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, if you’re interested in attending local festivals and events, plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pomona

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Pomona:

Do:

  • Follow the local customs and etiquette.
  • Respect the natural environment and the wildlife.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Leave your rubbish behind.
  • Disturb the wildlife or leave the trails.
  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pomona

Pomona is generally a safe place, but it’s always a good idea to take some precautions:

  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas at night.
  • Lock your doors and windows when you leave your accommodation.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings at all times.

Health in Pomona

Make sure to have travel insurance before your visit to Pomona. Medical facilities are available in nearby towns, but it’s always best to take precautions:

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid overexertion during outdoor activities.
  • Keep insect repellent handy, as mosquitoes and ticks are common in the region.

Getting to Pomona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pomona

The closest airport to Pomona is the Sunshine Coast Airport, which is located approximately 45km away. Alternatively, you can also fly to Brisbane Airport, which is approximately 140km away.

Bus Stations Near Pomona

Pomona has a local bus station that serves the region, and there are also coach services that run to nearby towns, including Noosa and Cooroy.

Other Options

If you want to explore the region at your own pace, it’s best to rent a car or motorcycle. There are many rental companies available in the region.

How to Get Around in Pomona

Pomona is a small town, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot or by bike. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, it’s best to rent a car or motorcycle.

Where to Go Next from Pomona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pomona is located in the heart of the Noosa hinterland, which offers plenty of scenic and cultural attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore:

  • Noosa Heads: A popular beachside town that offers stunning beaches and a vibrant cafe and bar scene.
  • Cooloola Great Walk: A multi-day hiking trail that takes you through the spectacular Great Sandy National Park.
  • Eumundi Markets: One of the most famous markets in Australia, offering art, crafts, and food from local artisans and farmers.

Conclusion

Pomona is a hidden gem in the Noosa Shire, offering a wide range of activities, attractions, and natural beauty. We hope that our comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan a perfect trip to this beautiful town. Happy travels!
