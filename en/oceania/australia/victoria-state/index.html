Introduction to Victoria (state)

Victoria (state) is one of the six states of Australia and is located in the southeastern part of the country. It is bordered by New South Wales to the north, South Australia to the west, and Tasmania to the south. The capital city of Victoria is Melbourne, which is known for its vibrant culture, culinary scene, and iconic landmarks. Victoria is a popular destination for travelers, offering a diverse range of experiences such as outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and scenic views. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit and things to do in Victoria (state).

List of Places to Visit in Victoria (state)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Gallery of Victoria: This is Australia’s oldest and largest public art museum, showcasing an extensive collection of art from around the world.

  • Melbourne Museum: Located in Carlton Gardens, Melbourne Museum is dedicated to the natural and cultural history of Victoria.

  • Old Melbourne Gaol: A former prison turned museum, visitors can experience what life was like for prisoners in the 19th century.

  • Sovereign Hill: An outdoor museum in Ballarat that recreates life during the 1850s gold rush.

Natural Attractions of Victoria (state): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Ocean Road: One of the world’s most scenic coastal drives, the Great Ocean Road stretches for 243 kilometers along Victoria’s south-west coast.

  • Phillip Island: Known for its wildlife and scenic beaches, Phillip Island is home to the famous Penguin Parade, where visitors can watch little penguins march up from the sea to their burrows.

  • Grampians National Park: One of Victoria’s largest national parks, Grampians offers hiking trails, wildlife spotting, and breathtaking views of the mountains, forests, and waterfalls.

  • Yarra Valley: Famous for its wine and food, Yarra Valley is also home to hot air ballooning, scenic drives, and wildlife encounters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Victoria (state)

  • The Coastal Walk: A 35-kilometer trail that takes you along the coast from Cape Otway to Glenample Homestead, passing by the Twelve Apostles and other stunning rock formations along the way.

  • The Grampians Peaks Trail: A 36-kilometer hike in Grampians National Park, offering stunning views of the mountain range, wildlife sightings, and challenging terrain.

  • The Rail Trails: Victoria has a network of 30 Rail Trails that offer scenic cycling routes on disused railway lines through picturesque towns, forests, and valleys.

  • The Great Ocean Walk: A 104-kilometer coastal hike that includes the Twelve Apostles, stunning beaches, and natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Victoria (state): Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Ballooning: Take a hot air balloon ride over Yarra Valley or Melbourne for a beautiful early morning experience.

  • The Otway Fly Treetop Adventures: This zipline and treetop walk adventure allows you to experience the rainforest canopy of the Otways.

  • The Colonial Tramcar Restaurant: A unique dining experience on a moving tramcar, serving up high-class meals while touring Melbourne’s landmarks.

  • Phillip Island Wildlife Tour: A tour that takes you to see penguins, koalas, wallabies, and other native animals on Phillip Island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Victoria (state)

Day 1: Explore Melbourne’s cultural and culinary scene. Day 2-3: Drive the Great Ocean Road, stopping by scenic landmarks such as the Twelve Apostles and Loch Ard Gorge. Day 4-5: Hike in Grampians National Park and enjoy the scenic views and wildlife sightings. Day 6-7: Relax in Yarra Valley, visiting wineries, hot air ballooning, and experiencing the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Victoria (state)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vegemite: A spread made from yeast extract and a popular Australian breakfast item. Not for everyone, but worth a try!

  • Meat Pie: A classic Australian dish that can be found all over the country. Try it with tomato sauce for the true Aussie experience.

  • Lamingtons: A traditional Australian cake made of sponge cake and covered in chocolate and shredded coconut.

  • Pavlova: A meringue-based dessert topped with fresh fruit and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Victoria is known for its wine regions, including Yarra Valley, Mornington Peninsula, and Rutherglen.

  • Beer: Victoria has a thriving craft beer scene, with local breweries such as Stomping Ground, Mountain Goat, and 3 Ravens Brewery.

  • Coffee: Australians take their coffee seriously, and Melbourne is known for its specialty coffee shops and cafes.

## Where to Stay in Victoria (state): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Langham, Melbourne: A luxurious hotel located in the central business district, offering world-class amenities and views of the Yarra River.

  • The Melbourne Connection Travellers Hostel: A budget-friendly option in the heart of Melbourne’s city center, offering dormitory-style accommodation.

  • The Royal Mail Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the Grampians, offering stunning views of the mountain range, gourmet food, and wine experiences.

What to Buy in Victoria (state): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tim Tams: A famous Australian biscuit that can be found in most supermarkets and souvenir shops.

  • Australian Wine: Victoria produces world-class wine, making it a great souvenir to bring home.

  • Aboriginal Art: Victoria has a rich indigenous history, and Aboriginal art can be found in galleries and markets around the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Victoria (state)

  • Australians are generally laid back and friendly, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals.

  • Tipping is not expected in Australia, but it is appreciated if you receive exceptional service.

  • Australia is a multicultural society, with influences from all around the world. This is reflected in the food, music, and arts scene of Victoria.

Best Time to Visit Victoria (state)

The best time to visit Victoria is during the Australian summer months from December to February. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices. Spring (September-November) and Autumn (March-May) are also great times to visit, with milder weather and fewer tourists. Winter (June-August) can be cold and rainy, but also offers the chance to experience winter sports in the mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Victoria (state)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

  • Experience the natural attractions, such as the Great Ocean Road and Grampians National Park.

  • Be respectful of the indigenous history and culture of Victoria.

Don’t:

  • Swim in unpatrolled beaches, as there can be strong currents and dangerous wildlife.

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

  • Smoke in designated non-smoking areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Victoria (state)

Victoria is generally a safe place for travelers, but it’s always good to take precautions to ensure your safety. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid leaving valuables unattended in public places. It’s also important to swim on patrolled beaches and follow the advice of lifeguards.

Health in Victoria (state)

The healthcare system in Victoria is of a high standard, with many hospitals and medical centers available. However, it’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses in case of any emergencies.

Getting to Victoria (state): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Victoria (state)

  • Melbourne Airport (MEL)
  • Avalon Airport (AVV)

Bus Stations Near Victoria (state)

  • Southern Cross Station, Melbourne
  • Ballarat Bus Station, Ballarat

Other Options

  • Train: Victoria is well-connected by train, with services provided by V/Line.
  • Car: Victoria has well-maintained roads, and renting a car is a popular option for exploring the state.

How to Get Around in Victoria (state)

Public transportation in Victoria is reliable and affordable, with train and bus services provided by V/Line. Car rental is also a popular option for exploring the state, but be aware that road rules and traffic laws may differ from your home country.

Where to Go Next from Victoria (state): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tasmania: Located just south of Victoria, Tasmania is an island state known for its wilderness areas, national parks, and outdoor activities.

  • New South Wales: Victoria’s neighboring state to the north, New South Wales is home to iconic landmarks such as the Sydney Opera House and the Blue Mountains.

  • South Australia: Victoria’s neighboring state to the west, South Australia is famous for its wineries, wildlife, and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Victoria (state) is a popular destination for travelers, offering a diverse range of experiences such as outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and scenic views. Whether you’re interested in hiking, food and wine, or cultural history, there’s something for everyone in Victoria. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has given you some ideas for your next trip to this beautiful state.
