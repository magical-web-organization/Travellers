Introduction to Mildura

Nestled in the heart of The Murray region in Victoria, Mildura is a vibrant city filled with culture, history and natural beauty. With its Mediterranean climate and scenic surroundings, Mildura attracts visitors from all over the world. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, accommodations, and dining options that Mildura has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Mildura

Mildura offers a wide range of attractions for visitors. Whether you are interested in history, culture, nature or outdoor adventure, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Mildura:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mildura Arts Centre: This art gallery offers a range of exhibitions, performances and events, featuring local and international artists.

  • The Rio Vista Historic House: This beautiful 19th-century mansion turned museum offers a glimpse into the life of the Chaffey family, who founded Mildura.

  • The Old Aerodrome Museum: This aviation museum features a collection of World War II-era aircraft, engines and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Mildura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Murray River: Take a cruise along the Murray River to enjoy the scenic views and wildlife of this famous waterway.

  • Mungo National Park: This World-Heritage-listed park is home to ancient Aboriginal, and the stunning scenery of the Walls of China.

  • Kings Billabong Wildlife Reserve: This reserve is a wildlife lover’s paradise, with over 100 species of birds, kangaroos, emus, and other animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mildura

Mildura is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails to explore. Discover these scenic routes to get up close to the natural beauty of the city’s surroundings:

  • The Mildura Riverfront Walk: This riverside path runs from the city center to the Lock 11 Parklands, offering stunning river views along the way.

  • The Murray River Trail: This 1500 km trail offers a continuous bike path along the Murray River, with Mildura as one of its highlights.

Best Things to Do in Mildura: Activities and Tours

From wine tasting to hot air balloon rides, there is no shortage of things to do in Mildura. Here are some exciting activities and tours to indulge in:

  • Wine Tasting: Mildura is known for its vineyards, and Chateau Mildura is a must-visit for wine lovers.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience Mildura’s stunning landscapes from above, with a hot air balloon ride over the city.

  • Paddleboat Cruise: Embark on a leisurely paddleboat cruise along the Murray River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mildura

If you have a week to explore Mildura and its surroundings, here’s an itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit the Mildura Arts Centre and take a stroll along the riverfront walk
Day 2 Explore the historic Rio Vista mansion and head to Kings Billabong Wildlife Reserve
Day 3 Soak up the sun at Merbein Beach and discover Murray Sunset National Park
Day 4 Take a day trip to Mungo National Park and experience its wonders
Day 5 Enjoy a hot air balloon ride and indulge in wine-tasting at Chateau Mildura
Day 6 Discover the Old Aerodrome Museum and have a paddleboat cruise along the Murray
Day 7 Spend the day in Orange World and learn about citrus farming in the region

Food and Drink Scene of Mildura

Mildura is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with local produce and wines taking center stage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chaffey Bros Wine Co: This winery offers a range of delicious vintages paired with tasty charcuterie platters.

  • Pizza Cafe: This restaurant serves the best authentic Italian pizza, with fresh, locally sourced ingredients that taste like home.

  • Stefanos: Greek-inspired Mediterranean cuisine with fresh seafood and vegetarian options.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Wine: Try out a new type of wine made with local citrus fruits in Mildura.

  • Australian Shiraz: Come and check out the rich, earthy flavors of this full-bodied wine in Mildura.

  • Coopers Beer: This Australian beer is a local favorite with its pale and amber ales.

Where to Stay in Mildura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mildura offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences, ranging from luxury hotels to quaint guesthouses and hostels. Here are some great options for your stay:

  • Quest Mildura: Perfect for business travelers and families, this hotel offers spacious and modern serviced apartments.

  • Mercure Hotel: This deluxe hotel provides guests with a wide range of amenities, including a heated swimming pool, a tennis court, and a fitness center.

  • Mildura Riverview Motel: Stay at this affordable and comfortable 3-star motel, with views of the Murray River and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Mildura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mildura is known for its fresh produce, citrus fruits, and wines. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should bring home:

  • Dried Fruits: Try some delicious locally dried fruits, including apricots, peaches, and oranges.

  • Local Wines: Pick up a few bottles of Shiraz, white wines, or unique orange wines produced in the region.

  • Citrus Products: Stock up on locally made jams, marmalades and other citrus-based products as gifts for your friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Mildura

Mildura’s diverse community celebrates many different cultures. Visitors are encouraged to respect the customs of the people in the region.

Best Time to Visit Mildura

Mildura can be visited throughout the year, although the region’s Mediterranean climate makes it a popular tourist destination in the warmer months. The best time to visit Mildura is from March to May and from September to November when the temperatures are balmy, and the rainfall is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mildura

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Mildura:

Do:

  • Show respect for the local community and their customs.

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the region and its wildlife.

  • Try out the local produce and wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Ignore safety guidance and regulations for outdoor activities.

  • Forget to drink plenty of water in the hot temperatures.

Safety and Health

Mildura is generally considered a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to follow some basic safety tips to make sure your trip is enjoyable and uneventful:

Safety in Mildura

  • Stick to well-lit areas, especially at night.

  • Keep your valuables safe and don’t leave them unattended.

  • Stay aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking.

Health in Mildura

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated in the hot climate.

  • Beware of potentially harmful wildlife, including snakes and spiders.

  • Protect yourself from the sun with sunscreen, hats, and protective clothing.

Getting to Mildura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mildura is well connected with the rest of Australia, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Mildura

  • Mildura Airport is served by several domestic airlines, including Virgin Australia, Qantas, and regional carriers.

Bus Stations Near Mildura

  • Mildura Central Bus Station is located in the city center, with buses connecting the city to other destinations in Victoria and New South Wales.

Other Options

  • Driving: Mildura is around a six-hour drive from Melbourne, making for a beautiful road trip through the countryside.

How to Get Around in Mildura

The best way to get around Mildura is by car. Taxis, rental cars, and public transport are available, but a car gives you the freedom to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Mildura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have the time, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Mildura:

  • Red Cliffs: This small town is just a 15-minute drive from Mildura and a great spot for hiking, fishing, and exploring the local wineries.

  • Broken Hill: Located just across the border in New South Wales, Broken Hill is a historical mining town full of architecture and art history.

  • Swan Hill: This charming town is home to a range of attractions, including the Pioneer Settlement and the Murray Downs Golf Club.

Conclusion

Mildura is a unique and charming destination that offers a range of experiences for visitors. From its natural beauty to its rich history and culture, there is something here for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you are ready to discover the best that Mildura has to offer.
