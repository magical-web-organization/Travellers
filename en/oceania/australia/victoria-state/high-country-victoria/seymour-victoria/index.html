Introduction to Seymour (Victoria)

Seymour, a small town in Victoria’s High Country, is known for its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Located along the Goulburn River, Seymour offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys and is great for exploring on foot or on a bike.

Whether you’re an adventure enthusiast, history buff, or simply want to soak up the town’s laid-back atmosphere, Seymour has something to offer all types of travelers. This guide provides you with everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Seymour (Victoria).

List of Places to Visit in Seymour (Victoria)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Vietnam Veterans Commemorative Walk - A poignant tribute to the Australian soldiers who fought in the Vietnam War, this 400-meter-long walk features a series of larger-than-life bronze sculptures depicting the soldiers in action.
  • The Old Post Office Seymour - A well-preserved landmark building in Seymour’s main street, the Old Post Office, now serves as a visitor centre as well as a community space with art exhibitions and other events.
  • The Seymour Railway Heritage Centre - The railway heritage centre presents an insight into the history and workings of steam trains and the pioneering rail networks in Victoria.

Natural Attractions of Seymour (Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Tallarook State Forest - Seymour’s surrounding forests offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, from hiking and birdwatching to camping and picnicking. The state forest boasts many tracks and paths through the unique Australian flora and fauna.
  • The Goulburn River High Country Rail Trail - A bike trail that follows the picturesque Goulburn River and passing through the town of Trawool. The rail trail passes through the historic railway station at Nagambie and crosses Australia’s longest rail bridge.
  • The Wine Regions - The nearby Heathcote and Nagambie Lakes regions offer some of Victoria’s most scenic vineyards and wineries, where travelers appreciate the local production of wine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seymour (Victoria)

  • The Great Victorian Rail Trail - This bike path runs for more than 123km between Mansfield and Tallarook, passing through the small towns of Yea and Yarck. The trail passes through tunnels, bridges, and through the scenic backdrop of Victoria.
  • Mount Sugarloaf - One of Seymour’s most popular hiking spots, Mount Sugarloaf is a moderate 5.5km hike. The summit offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and the town’s expanse.
  • Puckapunyal Tank Track - A 7km track that runs along an old WWII tank training ground, offering views of the Australia bush landscape.

Best Things to Do in Seymour (Victoria): Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - A once-in-a-lifetime experience, taking a hot air balloon ride over the top of the Seymour Valley, will offer breathtaking views of the Goulburn River and Victorian countryside.
  • Paddleboat Cruise - Take a leisurely cruise along the lake on the heritage paddleboat PS Emmylou. The tour sets the scene with historical commentary and stories of the Murray River.
  • The Food and Wine Experience - Seymour is known for its rich food and wine scene. A visit to the town’s markets and wineries is not to be missed.

Food and Drink Scene of Seymour (Victoria)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pie: Savory meat pies from ‘Seymour Bakery’ or the ‘Seymour IGA’.
  • Local Cheeses and Olives: Go on a visit to the nearby rolling hills of Kyneton for specialty cheese varieties.
  • Wine: Try new and unique wines at Tallarook Wines and Mitchelton Winery.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The local Brewery in Seymour is ‘Saint Andrews’.
  • Cider: Visit local spots like the ‘Plunkett Fowles Winery and Cellar Door’ for cider.

Where to Stay in Seymour (Victoria): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Seymour Motel - A comfortable motel in the heart of the town.
  • Old Stone House Bed and Breakfast - A historic guesthouse and a great place to stay for couples and families.
  • The Railway Hotel - An affordable accommodation option in Seymour.

What to Buy in Seymour (Victoria): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wines: Bring back your favorite Victorian wine that you tried at the wineries.
  • Local Handicrafts: Pick handmade pottery or woven baskets and wickerwork from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Seymour (Victoria)

Seymour has a rich indigenous history, with plenty of cultural attractions and landmarks celebrating and exploring the region’s Aboriginal heritage. Additionally, Australians typically value and appreciate friendliness, humor, honesty and reserve with acquaintances.

Best Time to Visit Seymour (Victoria)

The best time to visit Seymour (Victoria) is during the spring (September-November) or fall (March-May) when the temperatures are mild, and the surrounding landscapes are in full bloom. In contrast, during the winter (June-August), Oceania’s cold aesthetic comes with the snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seymour (Victoria)

Do:

  • Do visit the local vineyards and wineries.
  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the natural attractions and hiking spots.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss a hot air balloon ride over Seymour Valley.
  • Don’t litter in the natural areas or national parks.
  • Don’t forget to check the opening and closing times of shops, museums, and other attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Seymour (Victoria)

Seymour is a relatively safe town, however, travelers are advised to take general safety precautions while visiting the town, such as securing valuable belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Seymour (Victoria)

Seymour has good healthcare infrastructure, with a hospital, smaller clinics, and general practitioners. With the recent emergence of Covid-19 being advised to check the current virus regulations and plan accordingly.

Getting To Seymour (Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Seymour (Victoria)

The closest airport to Seymour is Melbourne’s Tullamarine International Airport, which is approximately 120km away.

Bus Station Near Seymour (Victoria)

Seymour has a well-equipped bus station, and it is connected to Victoria’s public transport system.

Other Options

Driving is the simplest way to get to Seymour. The town is well-connected to the major highways in Victoria and is around an hour and a half drive to Melbourne. Additionally, hiring a car or using taxi services is another option.

How to Get Around in Seymour (Victoria)

Seymour is a small town, making it easy to get around on foot. However, renting a car or bike is recommended for exploring the surrounding forests and vineyards. Alternatively, taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Seymour (Victoria): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Melbourne - The vibrant capital city of Victoria, Melbourne is just an hour and a half drive from Seymour.
  • Shepparton - Located just about 45 minutes’ drive from Seymour, Shepparton is a great option for day-trips, offering several art exhibits and galleries to explore.
  • Lake Eildon - Backward towards Mansfield, and a large lake which ideal for boating and fishing.

Conclusion

Seymour (Victoria) is a charming little town that allows visitors to immerse themselves in Victoria’s incredible natural beauty, history and culture. From the hiking trails and mountain paths to the cultural landmarks that celebrate the town’s heritage are sure to capture the heart. With a range of accommodation and dining options and a local charm that’s impossible to resist, Seymour is a must-visit destination for all travelers wishing to experiences vibrant rural Victoria.
