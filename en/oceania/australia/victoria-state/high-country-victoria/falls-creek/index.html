Introduction to Falls Creek

Located in the High Country region of Victoria, Australia, Falls Creek is a picturesque alpine resort town with an impressive array of recreational activities for tourists. The town is situated in the northeastern reaches of Victoria and is surrounded by breathtaking vistas of the Australian Alps.

With its natural beauty, upscale resorts, and fantastic skiing terrain, Falls Creek is an attractive destination for adventure seekers, couples seeking a romantic getaway, and families looking for a fun-filled vacation. In this travel guide, we will provide all the essential details about Falls Creek, including its top tourist attractions, food and drink scene, accommodation options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Falls Creek

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Falls Creek Museum: Located in the heart of the village, the Falls Creek Museum is a fascinating repository of the town’s history and heritage. The museum showcases authentic exhibits, including photographs and artifacts, representing the development of the town from its early days to the present.

  • Bogong Power Station: With stunning views of the Bogong Valley, the Bogong Power Station is one of the most important historical landmarks in the area. The power station generated power in the early 20th century and is a fascinating place to visit for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Falls Creek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Falls Creek Alpine Resort: A popular ski destination in Australia, Falls Creek Alpine Resort is home to a vast ski area covering 450 hectares with a range of runs suitable for all abilities. The resort has a number of ski schools, an extensive network of lifts, and plenty of après-ski activities for visitors.

  • Rocky Valley Lake: This picturesque lake is located in the Alpine National Park and is a popular spot for fishing, picnicking, and hiking. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the surrounding mountains and the tranquility of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Falls Creek

  • Rocky Valley Lake Circuit Walk: A moderate 8-kilometer hike with stunning views of the mountains and pristine alpine forests.

  • Wallaces Hut Trail: A challenging 15-kilometer hike that takes visitors through alpine meadows, rocky terrain, and scenic vistas of the area.

Best Things to Do in Falls Creek: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing: With more than ninety trails for skiing and snowboarding, the Falls Creek Alpine Resort is the ideal spot for winter sports enthusiasts.

  • Snowshoeing: A great activity for those looking to explore the winter wonderland of Falls Creek. Snowshoeing around the Rocky Valley Lake is a must-try for visitors.

  • Mountain Biking: Falls Creek offers 40 kilometers of mountain bike trails with something to suit all levels of fitness and skill level.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic beauty of Falls Creek on horseback. Visitors can take a guided horseback tour to enjoy the picturesque surroundings of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Falls Creek

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alpine Fondue: A traditional Alpine dish made from melted cheese and served with crusty bread cubes, potatoes, and pickles.

  • Goulash and Schnitzel: A hearty Central European dish with tender cuts of beef and potato pancakes.

  • Trout: Fresh and locally caught, trout is a great delicacy to try when visiting Falls Creek.

Local Beverages

  • The Victorian High Country is famous for its locally produced craft beers. Grab a pint of some of the best brews of the country at Manfred’s Microbrewery.

  • The region is also home to some of Australia’s best wine regions. Experience the local wines at Bright Brewery.

Where to Stay in Falls Creek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • QT Falls Creek: A luxurious alpine ski resort located on the main ski run, this resort is the perfect spot for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.

  • Falls Creek Country Club: A self-contained ski-in/ski-out apartment complex, the Falls Creek Country Club is your destination for a comfortable and relaxing stay.

  • Attunga Lodge: A fantastic budget-friendly hostel, Attunga Lodge provides a friendly, comfortable atmosphere and an affordable stay.

What to Buy in Falls Creek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-knit woolen beanies and scarves, perfect for keeping warm in the mountains.

  • Local craft beer and wine to be enjoyed back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Falls Creek

  • The town of Falls Creek has a rich history and heritage, and visitors should respect the local cultural values and traditions.

  • Falls Creek’s alpine season typically starts from June through to September. Visitors should wear appropriate clothing and shoes.

Best Time to Visit Falls Creek

The best time to visit Falls Creek depends on your interests and preferences, as the seasons offer different experiences. Winter, from June to September, offers skiing and other cold weather activities. The summer months from December to February offer warmer temperatures for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Falls Creek

Do:

  • Explore the alpine vistas of Falls Creek and enjoy the relaxing outdoor activities.

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the area, as it can harm the natural beauty of the town.

  • Leave valuables in your vehicle, as there are occasional vehicle break-ins in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Falls Creek

  • Always wear protective equipment while skiing or snowboarding.

  • Stay hydrated when participating in outdoor activities and during warm weather.

Health in Falls Creek

  • Falls Creek is above 1600 meters; visitors should take precautions to avoid altitude sickness.

  • Bring any necessary medications and first-aid supplies, as medical facilities in the area are limited.

Getting to Falls Creek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Falls Creek

  • Albury Airport: The closest airport to Falls Creek, located about 110 kilometers away.

  • Melbourne Airport: The major airport in the region, is about 400km away.

Bus Stations Near Falls Creek

  • Falls Creek Coach Services offers daily bus services from Melbourne and other major Australian cities.

Other Options

  • Visitors can take a self-drive to Falls Creek. The resort offers free car parking areas for visitors.

How to Get Around in Falls Creek

  • The easiest way to get around in Falls Creek is by walking.

  • Visitors can also take the free shuttle bus service, which operates between the town and the ski fields.

Where to Go Next from Falls Creek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bright: The nearby picturesque town of Bright offers a range of outdoor activities, including cycling, kayaking, and hiking.

  • Albury-Wodonga: The twin cities of Albury-Wodonga offer a variety of cultural activities, shopping, and dining experiences.

Conclusion
