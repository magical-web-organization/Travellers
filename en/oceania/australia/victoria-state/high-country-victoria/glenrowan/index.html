Glenrowan, located in Victoria’s High Country, is a charming town known for its historical significance and natural beauty. Famous for being the site of the final siege and capture of Australia’s most notorious outlaw, Ned Kelly, Glenrowan draws history buffs, adventure seekers, and nature lovers alike. Whether you are interested in exploring museums, hiking trails, or immersing yourself in local culture and cuisine, Glenrowan has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your ultimate trip to Glenrowan.

List of Places to Visit in Glenrowan

Glenrowan offers a wide variety of attractions, from museums and historical sites to scenic viewpoints and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Glenrowan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ned Kelly Museum and Gift Shop: Discover the story of Australia’s most infamous bushranger, Ned Kelly, and his gang. The museum displays original artefacts and memorabilia related to the siege and capture of Ned Kelly.
  • Siege Precinct: Explore the location of the final siege, now a museum, and the site of the Glenrowan Inn, where Ned Kelly and his gang made their last stand.
  • The Big Ned Kelly: A giant statue of Ned Kelly is a popular spot for photographs.
  • The Kelly Tree: A historic tree at Stringybark Creek, where the Kelly Gang infamously murdered three police officers.

Natural Attractions of Glenrowan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Glenrowan Lookout: Hike to the top of Mount Glenrowan for breathtaking views of the town and surrounding mountains.
  • Warby-Ovens National Park: Explore the rugged beauty of this nearby national park, which offers a wide variety of hiking trails, bird-watching, and picnic areas.
  • Lake Mokoan: A beautiful man-made lake that offers opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.
  • Winton Wetlands: A restored wetland area, once a gold-mining town, that offers opportunities for bird-watching and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glenrowan

Glenrowan is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most notable hiking and biking routes in Glenrowan include:

  • The Ned Kelly Trail: Follow in the footsteps of Ned Kelly and his gang, from Beveridge to Glenrowan, along a 250-kilometre trail that spans through Victoria.
  • Warby-Ovens National Park: Take a leisurely stroll, hike or bike through the many trails and waterfalls of this park that offers varied terrain trails.
  • Mount Glenrowan: Hike to the top for scenic views of the surrounding mountains and town below.
  • Lake Mokoan Circuit Track: Explore an area abundant in wildlife along this beautiful and versatile track.

Best Things to Do in Glenrowan: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring natural attractions, Glenrowan also offers a wide variety of tours and activities that cater to all interests, including:

  • Ned Kelly Walking Tours: Join an extensive walking tour of Glenrowan with an expert guide, who will take you on a journey through Ned Kelly’s life, death and capture in this region.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Discover the incredible wine-making traditions of the High Country with a tour of the local vineyards and award-winning wineries.
  • Adventure Tours: Go whitewater rafting or abseiling in the nearby mountains.
  • Art and Craft Tours: Get artistic with the local galleries that display the works of the High Country’s finest artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glenrowan

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will guide you through the best of Glenrowan:

  • Day 1: Start your journey with a morning hike at Mount Glenrowan Lookout. In the afternoon, visit the Ned Kelly Museum and Siege Precinct.
  • Day 2: Spend your day at Warby-Ovens National Park, enjoy the scenic views and wildlife spotting.
  • Day 3: Go on adventure tours with abseiling, rock climbing, and cave exploration.
  • Day 4: Take an art and craft tour and visit the local galleries.
  • Day 5: Tour the vineyards with wine tasting tours and sample some of the local wines.
  • Day 6: Visit Lake Mokoan in the morning and experience fishing or paddling. In the afternoon, explore the Winton Wetlands.
  • Day 7: Take a Ned Kelly walking tour to learn more about his life and legacy.

Food and Drink Scene of Glenrowan

Glenrowan’s food and drink scene are rich in local ingredients, history and flavour. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ballarat Gold Rush Stout Pie: A traditional pie filled with stout-braised beef.
  • Classic Aussie Hamburger with beetroot: Enjoy juicy beef patties with all the toppings that you can’t deny to like.
  • Return of the Platypus: Grilled cheese and bacon sandwich, served with tomato relish.

Local Beverages

  • Shiraz: the High Country’s warm summers and cold winters create the ideal environment for cultivating intensely flavoured Shiraz grapes.
  • Whisky: The region has some of the renowned whisky makers in Australia.
  • Craft Beer: Regional breweries produce a range of ales, lagers, and stouts.

Where to Stay in Glenrowan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glenrowan offers a wide range of accommodation options for travellers on all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the best accommodation options in the town are:

  • Glenrowan Kelly Country Motel: A comfortable and affordable motel with air-conditioned rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • Renaissance Chateau Mc Ivor Estate: Stay in a castle-style accommodation that comes with private balconies.
  • The Glenrowan Inn: A historical hotel featuring rooms with old-world charm and a full breakfast included. It was the location of the infamous siege itself.

What to Buy in Glenrowan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Glenrowan is a perfect place to buy some souvenirs and gifts that reflect the town’s history, culture, and traditions. Some of the best items to buy in Glenrowan include:

  • Ned Kelly Memorabilia: Purchase mugs, t-shirts, and other items in the gift shops.
  • Artisanal Goods: High-quality handmade crafts and artworks from local artists and craftsmen.
  • Wine: Take home a bottle of exceptional regional wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Glenrowan

Glenrowan has a rich cultural history shaped by the influences of its indigenous people, migrant communities and as a former frontier town. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and ethics, especially when it comes to:

  • Aboriginal culture: There are numerous indigenous sites around the area, and visitors should respect these areas of cultural significance.
  • Rural lifestyle: People in these areas generally live a more laid-back lifestyle, so visitors must respect their peaceful way of life.

Best Time to Visit Glenrowan

Glenrowan is a beautiful destination throughout the year, but the best time to visit is from September to November or from February to April. During these months, the temperatures are mild, making it the ideal time for outdoor activities and exploring the natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glenrowan

Here are some tips for visitors to make the most out of their trip to Glenrowan:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately: Be prepared for the changeable weather and outdoor activities.
  • Respect local wildlife: Observe and enjoy wildlife from a safe distance.
  • Embrace local culture: Learn and appreciate the area’s customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Respect nature and dispose of trash responsibly.
  • Damage or remove historical sites and items.
  • Ignore road safety: Be mindful of road safety, especially when driving on unsealed roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glenrowan

Glenrowan is generally a safe destination, but visitors should be mindful of some general safety tips, including:

  • Inform someone about your travel plans.
  • Carry adequate supplies when exploring the natural attractions.
  • Use sun protection and insect repellent.

Health in Glenrowan

Glenrowan has medical facilities, pharmacies and medical centres, however, it is recommended to bring your medical supplies if you have a pre-existing medical condition, and always have medical insurance to cover any unexpected emergencies.

Getting to Glenrowan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Glenrowan is conveniently located two hours’ drive from Melbourne or six hours’ drive from Sydney. There are also several transportation options available, including:

Airports Near Glenrowan

  • Melbourne Tullamarine Airport (MEL): The nearest major airport, located two hours’ drive away from Glenrowan.

Bus Stations Near Glenrowan

  • Wangaratta Bus Station: A 20-minute drive from Glenrowan.
  • Transit Systems Bus Stop: Located in Glenrowan, provides services to nearby cities like Albury, Bright, Wangaratta, and Wodonga.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: A car is essential for exploring Glenrowan and its surroundings.
  • Private Transfers: Private cars and shuttles are also excellent options for getting around the town.

How to Get Around in Glenrowan

Exploring Glenrowan is easy, as the town is small and easy to navigate by car, bike or foot. Many of the natural attractions are nearby, making it easy to explore them with minimal travel time.

Where to Go Next from Glenrowan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Glenrowan is located in the heart of Victoria’s High Country, and there are many other destinations nearby that are worth visiting, including:

  • Beechworth: A charming gold-rush era town with many historical attractions, located 30 minutes’ drive away from Glenrowan.
  • Bright: A picturesque alpine village nestled in the foothills of the Victorian Alps, located 1.5 hours’ drive away from Glenrowan.
  • Milawa Gourmet Region: Famous for its cheese and wine production and located within 45minutes of drive from Glenrowan.

Conclusion

Glenrowan is a hidden gem in Victoria’s High Country and is an excellent choice for those looking to dive into Victoria’s natural beauty, historical background, and traditional customs. Its abundance of natural and cultural attractions, hiking and biking routes, delicious local food, and welcoming atmosphere make it a must-visit for travellers to Australia. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your Glenrowan adventure with ease and joy.
