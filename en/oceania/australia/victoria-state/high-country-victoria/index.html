Introduction to High Country (Victoria)

High Country is a beautiful region located in the northeastern part of Victoria, Australia. Its scenic beauty, adventurous activities and rich history makes it a popular destination for tourists from around the world.

The region features stunning alpine landscapes, pristine lakes and rivers, numerous charming towns and villages, and a culturally diverse population. It is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and cultural explorers.

List of Places to Visit in High Country (Victoria)

When visiting the High Country region in Victoria, the following places should not be missed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Beechworth Historic Park: It is a great place to explore the rich history of the Victorian Goldfields and learn about its heritage listed sites, views, events, and stories.
  2. Old gaol and courthouse, Beechworth: It is an important historical landmark that served as a prison and courthouse from 1859 to 1866. Now it is a tourist attraction that offers visitors a glimpse into the past.
  3. Bright Art Gallery: It showcases the work of local and regional artists and is the perfect place for art lovers.
  4. Ned Kelly Museum: It is a museum dedicated to the notorious bushranger, Ned Kelly, who is famous for his legendary escape from the Melbourne gaol.
  5. Glenrowan: It has a rich history and a unique place in Australia’s culture. It is the sight of the final siege in 1880 of the Kelly Gang.

Natural Attractions of High Country (Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mt. Buller: It is one of the most popular ski resort destinations in Australia. It offers a wide range of winter sports activities such as skiing, snowboarding, and tobogganing.
  2. Lake Eildon: It is one of the largest artificial lakes in the world and is perfect for watersports activities such as swimming, fishing, and boating.
  3. Alpine National Park: It features some of Australia’s highest mountains, spectacular scenery, and a range of outdoor activities. It is a great place for camping, hiking, cycling, and wildlife spotting.
  4. Bright: This beautiful town is located on the banks of the Ovens River and is known for its outdoor activities, including cycling, hiking, and fishing, and its autumn foliage.
  5. Falls Creek: It is a beautiful alpine resort with ski slopes, cross-country skiing, and snowboarding. It is also an excellent destination for hiking and mountain biking during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of High Country (Victoria)

High Country offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Australia, with breathtaking views and diverse terrains. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in High Country (Victoria) include:

  1. The Alpine National Park: With its alpine lakes, waterfalls, and scenic views, it is a hiker’s paradise. The trails range from easy to strenuous and offer visitors a chance to explore the unique natural beauty of the region.
  2. The Murray to Mountains Rail Trail: It is one of the most popular cycling tracks in Victoria and is a great way to explore the region’s diverse landscape, wineries, and gourmet food.
  3. The Mount Kosciuszko Summit Walk: It is the highest peak in Australia, and the summit walk is a challenging but worthwhile experience that offers stunning views of the High Country.

Best Things to Do in High Country (Victoria): Activities and Tours

High Country is a region filled with unique experiences and exciting activities for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in High Country:

  1. Wine Tasting: High Country is an excellent wine region with many vineyards and wineries to visit and taste the locally produced wines.
  2. Adventure Sports: The region has plenty of opportunities for outdoor and adventure enthusiasts, including all-season skiing, snowboarding, mountain biking, and hiking.
  3. Caving: With over 1000 known caves and sinkholes, High Country is a great destination for spelunking.
  4. Fishing: High Country offers plenty of opportunities to fish for trout, cod, and many other species in its clear streams and lakes.
  5. Whitewater Rafting: The region has several rivers for white water rafting that provide adrenaline-filled experiences for visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of High Country (Victoria)

High Country is not only known for its breathtaking natural beauty, but also for its food and drink scene. The region boasts of many excellent restaurants, wineries, and breweries that offer a variety of dining and drinking experiences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Milawa Cheese: A famous cheese made in the Milawa region.
  2. Brown Brothers Wines: A well-known wine brand in the region.
  3. Alpine Smoked Trout: A local speciality known for its smoky flavour, served with crusty bread.
  4. Beechworth Honey: Beechworth Honey is produced by bees that have access to the unique flora of the High Country.
  5. King Valley Wines: Known for its Italian influence producing wines like the Sangiovese, Barbera, and Nebbiolo varieties.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: The region has several microbreweries producing excellent craft beer, including Bright Brewery and Bridge Road Brewers.
  2. Wine: High Country is well-known for its fine wines, including the pinot noir, shiraz, and chardonnay varieties.
  3. Gin: The region has several distilleries producing high-quality artisanal gin.

Where to Stay in High Country (Victoria): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

High Country has a wide range of accommodations for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Bright Buffalo Motel: An excellent choice for families, this motel offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  2. The George Kerferd Hotel: The hotel is a grand old building with a beautiful garden and a spacious room.
  3. YHA Bright: The perfect hostel for travelers on a budget.
  4. Beechworth on Bridge: A beautiful guesthouse located in the historical town of Beechworth, offering both rooms and self-contained apartments.

What to Buy in High Country (Victoria): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

High Country is a region with unique products and souvenirs that make excellent gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best products to bring home:

  1. Handmade Pottery: The region is home to several potters who create unique and beautiful ceramic pieces.
  2. Local Wine and Cheese: High Country is known for its wine and cheese, and there are many varieties to choose from.
  3. Woolen Clothes: The High Country region is known for its high-quality wool, and there are several shops where you can buy woolen clothes, knitted items, and blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of High Country (Victoria)

The High Country region has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by its indigenous, European, and Chinese history. Today, the region is home to a mix of cultures, and visitors can experience the unique customs and traditions of the area.

Best Time to Visit High Country (Victoria)

The best time to visit High Country is during the summer months of December to February when the weather is warm, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Autumn is also a great time to visit as the region experiences stunning foliage colours that are truly breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of High Country (Victoria)

Do:

  1. Respect the environment and wildlife.
  2. Learn about the local history and customs.
  3. Be respectful to the local communities and culture.
  4. Try the local food and drink.
  5. Make a plan and pack the appropriate gear for outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Use illegal drugs or engage in illegal activities.
  3. Be disrespectful to the local people or their culture.
  4. Go off-trail in the national parks and protected areas.
  5. Leave your adventure plans to chance.

Safety and Health

Safety in High Country (Victoria)

  1. Be aware of wildlife, including snakes and spiders.

  2. Bring appropriate clothing and equipment for outdoor activities, including sun protection, water and weather.

  3. Be aware of any fire risks in the summer months.
  4. Check weather conditions regularly before participating in any outdoor activities.

Health in High Country (Victoria)

  1. The water in the High Country region is safe to drink.
  2. High Country has several medical centers and hospitals for emergencies.
  3. Be aware of the risks of sunburn and skin cancer in Australia.

Getting to High Country (Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

High Country is easily accessible by car, bus, and air. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near High Country (Victoria)

  1. Melbourne Airport: This is the nearest international airport to High Country, located about 3 hours’ drive from the region.
  2. Albury Airport: This is the nearest regional airport to High Country, located 1.5 hours’ drive from the region.

Bus Stations Near High Country (Victoria)

  1. VLine Bus: This is an affordable option for getting to High Country from Melbourne and other regional destinations.
  2. Mitchell Transit: This bus service also operates to several regional destinations in High Country.

Other Options

  1. Rail: The V/Line train services run from Melbourne to Wangaratta and the regional coaches can take you to other destinations.
  2. Car Rental: If you are looking to explore High Country on your own, car rentals are available at all major airports and in Melbourne.

How to Get Around in High Country (Victoria)

The best way to get around High Country and explore its beauty is to drive or ride a bike. Public transport options are limited, but there are several agencies offering guided tours and airport transfers in the area.

Where to Go Next from High Country (Victoria): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

High Country is the perfect gateway to explore other destinations in Victoria. Some popular places to visit include:

  1. The Great Ocean Road: A scenic coastal drive with stunning ocean views.
  2. The Yarra Valley: A world-class wine region, perfect for tasting fine wines and exploring the vineyards.
  3. Melbourne: A vibrant city with plenty of cultural attractions, museums, restaurants, and shops.

Conclusion

High Country (Victoria) is an excellent travel destination that offers a blend of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. It is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, cultural explorers, and history buffs. From stunning national parks to world-class wineries, High Country has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now well-equipped to have an unforgettable High Country experience.
