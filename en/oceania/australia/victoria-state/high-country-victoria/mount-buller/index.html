Introduction to Mount Buller

Mount Buller is a picturesque alpine resort located in the heart of the Victorian Alps in Australia. It is one of the most popular destinations in the High Country region, offering a range of outdoor activities and charming local culture. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced skier, hiker, cyclist or sightseer, Mount Buller has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Buller

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Alpine Museum of Australia showcases the history and culture of the Alpine region with exhibits on indigenous history, early exploration, skiing and snowsports, and the environment.
  • Stirling Experience offers a glimpse into the history of the Stirling family, one of Mount Buller’s early pioneers.
  • The Mt Buller Chalet Hotel & Suites is a historic landmark of Mount Buller, built in 1949 and recently renovated to offer modern-day comfort and amenities.

Natural Attractions of Mount Buller: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Buller Ski Resort is one of the most popular ski resorts in Australia and offers a range of winter activities, including skiing, snowboarding and tobogganing.
  • During the summer months, Mount Buller transforms into a haven for hikers and cyclists. The trails range from easy walks to challenging hikes, offering stunning views of the surrounding alpine landscape.
  • The Mount Buller Summit Walk takes you to the top of the mountain for panoramic views of Victoria’s High Country.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Buller

  • The Mount Buller Village Circuit is a popular trail that takes you around the village, with plenty of scenic views and photo opportunities.
  • The Razorback Trail is a challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Mount Stirling, offering incredible views along the way.
  • The Mt Buller to Craig’s Hut Trail takes you through beautiful alpine scenery and the iconic Craig’s Hut, used as a location in the movie “The Man from Snowy River”.

Best Things to Do in Mount Buller: Activities and Tours

  • The Mt Buller Snowshoe Tour lets you experience the peace and beauty of the snowy alpine landscape during winter.
  • The Village Bike Park features over 30km of purpose-built singletrack for mountain biking enthusiasts of all levels.
  • The Mount Buller Helicopter Tour offers an unforgettable bird’s eye view of the stunning alpine scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Buller

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alpine-style cheese, such as the Milawa Cheese Company’s King River Gold, made from cow’s milk with a distinct nutty flavor.
  • Kelly’s Italian Restaurant serves authentic Italian cuisine, including homemade pasta and wood-fired pizza.
  • The Koflers Hutte offers traditional Austrian dishes, such as Wiener schnitzel and apple strudel, in a cozy alpine setting.

Local Beverages

  • The Mansfield Coffee Merchant roasts coffee beans sourced from around the world, with a focus on ethical and sustainable production.
  • The Delatite Winery produces a range of award-winning wines, including Shiraz, Riesling, and Pinot Grigio.
  • The Grandview Hotel offers craft beers from local breweries, such as Black Dog Brewery and Bridge Road Brewers.

Where to Stay in Mount Buller: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Mt Buller Chalet Hotel & Suites offers a luxurious stay in a historic alpine setting, with modern amenities and excellent dining options.
  • The ABOM Hotel & Apartments offer a range of accommodations, including self-contained apartments and cozy hotel rooms.
  • The Enzian Hotel offers comfortable and affordable lodge-style accommodations, with stunning views of Mount Buller.

What to Buy in Mount Buller: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade candles from The Candle Shop in the Village Center, featuring unique scents such as alpine air and mulled wine.
  • Local honey from High Country Apiaries, produced from bees in the surrounding alpine landscape.
  • Elegant and stylish scarves made from Australian merino wool at Mountain Kitchen and Homewares.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Buller

  • The indigenous people of the region are the Taungurung, who have lived in the area for thousands of years. Visitors can learn more about their culture and history at the National Alpine Museum of Australia.
  • Mount Buller has a vibrant arts scene, with its own arts festival held every year.
  • The local language and accent differ slightly from other parts of Australia, with a more distinct Alpine influence.

Best Time to Visit Mount Buller

The best time to visit Mount Buller depends on your preferred activities. Winter is the best time for skiing and snowboarding, but summer offers exceptional hiking, biking, and sightseeing opportunities. Spring and autumn offer milder weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Buller

Do:

  • Dress in layers, as the weather can change quickly.
  • Follow the ski resort’s safety guidelines for skiing and snowboarding.
  • Respect the environment and keep trails clean.

Don’t:

  • Go off-trail in the Alpine region, as this can damage the fragile alpine ecosystem.
  • Ignore signs and safety warnings, especially during skiing and snowboarding activities.
  • Litter or damage the historic landmarks and cultural attractions of Mount Buller.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Buller

  • Skiers and snowboarders should wear a helmet and follow all resort safety guidelines.
  • Hikers and bikers should carry plenty of water and stay on designated trails.
  • Be aware of the risks of bushfires during hot and dry weather, and follow all safety guidelines.

Health in Mount Buller

  • The higher elevation of Mount Buller can cause altitude sickness in some people. Be sure to drink plenty of water and minimize alcohol and caffeine consumption.
  • Bring sunscreen and wear protective clothing to prevent sunburn during outdoor activities.
  • In case of an emergency, there is a medical center located in the village center.

Getting to Mount Buller: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Buller

  • Melbourne Tullamarine Airport is the closest international airport to Mount Buller, approximately three hours away by car.
  • Albury Airport is approximately 2.5 hours away by car, and offers domestic flights from Sydney and Melbourne.

Bus Stations Near Mount Buller

  • The Mount Buller Bus Line offers daily direct services from Melbourne, with stops in Mansfield and Merrijig.
  • V/Line offers regular bus services from Melbourne to Mansfield, with connecting shuttles to Mount Buller.

Other Options

  • Driving from Melbourne to Mount Buller takes approximately three hours, and offers stunning views of the Alpine region.
  • Private transfers are available from Melbourne and nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Mount Buller

  • The Mount Buller Village Shuttle Bus provides a convenient and free mode of transportation around the village.
  • Hikers and bikers can explore the trails on foot or by bike.
  • Skiers and snowboarders can rely on the resort’s ski lifts, gondolas and chairlifts.

Where to Go Next from Mount Buller: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The charming town of Mansfield offers a range of dining and shopping options, as well as access to the picturesque Lake Eildon.
  • The King Valley is a scenic wine region with a range of award-winning wineries.
  • The Great Alpine Road is one of Australia’s most scenic drives, offering stunning views of the Alpine region.

Conclusion

Mount Buller is an exceptional destination in the High Country region of Victoria, offering a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local food and wine. Whether you are visiting for a day or a week, there is always something new to discover in this breathtaking alpine resort.
