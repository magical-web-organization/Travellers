Introduction to Ararat (Victoria)

Ararat is a charming town situated in the heart of the Wimmera region of Victoria, Australia. Founded in the mid-19th century during the Gold Rush, Ararat has a rich cultural and historical heritage, as well as a stunning natural landscape. Visitors to Ararat can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking, as well as exploring museums and historic sites. With its friendly locals, delicious food, and welcoming atmosphere, Ararat is the perfect destination for a relaxing getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Ararat (Victoria)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Langi Morgala Museum – This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Ararat and the surrounding region. Visitors can see exhibits on the town’s gold-mining past, as well as artifacts from local Aboriginal communities.
  • J Ward Museum Complex – Originally built as a treatment center for the criminally insane, J Ward now serves as a museum and historic site. Visitors can see the original cells, hear stories of the inmates, and learn about the history of mental health care in Victoria.
  • Ararat Railway Heritage Museum – This museum is housed in a restored railway station and showcases the history of the railway in Victoria. Visitors can see historic trains and other railway artifacts, as well as learn about the important role of the railway in the development of the state.

Natural Attractions of Ararat (Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Langi Ghiran – This stunning mountain range offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region, as well as panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can also explore the local vineyards and taste the delicious wines produced in the area.
  • Gum San Chinese Heritage Centre – This center celebrates the contribution of the Chinese community to the history and culture of Ararat. Visitors can see exhibits on Chinese immigration, herbal medicine, and other aspects of Chinese culture.
  • Grampians National Park – Located just a short distance from Ararat, this national park is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts. With its rugged mountain ranges, beautiful waterfalls and lakes, and diverse wildlife, the Grampians offers endless opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ararat (Victoria)

  • Ararat Hills Regional Park – This park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails which wind through the scenic landscape of the region. Visitors can see kangaroos, wallabies, and other wildlife, as well as enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Mount Buangor State Park – This park offers several challenging hiking trails that lead to stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can also see rare plant and animal species, including the powerful owl and the brush-tailed phascogale.

Best Things to Do in Ararat (Victoria): Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours – Ararat is home to several world-renowned wineries, including Mount Langi Ghiran and Best’s Wines. Visitors can take guided tours of the vineyards and taste the delicious wines produced in the area.
  • Food Tours – Ararat has a vibrant food scene, with many restaurants serving delicious local cuisine. Foodies can take guided tours of the town’s best eateries, sampling local foods and meeting the passionate chefs and restaurateurs who create them.
  • Hot Air Balloon Tours – What better way to see the stunning beauty of Ararat than from the sky? Visitors can take hot air balloon tours that offer panoramic views of the town, the surrounding mountains, and the beautiful countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Ararat (Victoria)

Ararat has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from the town’s Gold Rush past, as well as its Aboriginal and Chinese communities. Visitors to Ararat should take time to learn about the town’s history and culture, as well as respecting the customs and traditions of the local people.

Getting to Ararat (Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

Ararat is located in the Wimmera region of Victoria, approximately 200 km northwest of Melbourne. Visitors can reach Ararat by car, bus, or train.
