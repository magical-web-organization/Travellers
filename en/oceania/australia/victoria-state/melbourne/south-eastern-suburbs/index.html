Introduction to South-Eastern suburbs

Melbourne is a bustling city with much to see and do. The South-Eastern suburbs are particularly noteworthy, offering visitors popular attractions as well as a chance to explore less-known areas of Melbourne. In this guide, we’ll explore some of the best things to do in the South-Eastern suburbs, from culturally important museums and landmarks to natural attractions like hiking and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in South-Eastern suburbs

The South-Eastern suburbs have a plethora of sights and attractions to experience. Here are some places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The South-Eastern suburbs are steeped in rich history and culture. Here are some museums and historical places to add to your list:

  • Old Cheese Factory: A historic cheese factory that is now home to a museum, arts centre and Sunday market. The Cheese Factory is located at 34 Homestead Road, Berwick.

  • Cranbourne Botanic Gardens: Cranbourne Botanic Gardens is home to over 11,000 Australian native plants across 363 hectares including walking tracks, picnic areas and playgrounds. The venue hosts exhibitions, talks and workshops throughout the year.

  • Panny’s Chocolate Factory: Located in Phillip Island, Panny’s Chocolate Factory is a popular family-friendly destination that features fun chocolate-themed attractions, including a chocolate waterfall and samples.

Natural Attractions of South-Eastern suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The South-Eastern suburbs have a wide range of natural attractions. Here are some outdoor adventures to consider:

  • Wilsons Promontory National Park: Wilsons Promontory is a stunning coastal national park with diverse ecosystems including rugged mountains, rainforests and pristine beaches.

  • Dandenong Ranges: Located on the eastern outskirts of Melbourne, the Dandenong Ranges is known for its scenic drives, lush forests, and stunning waterfalls.

  • Phillip Island: Phillip Island is renowned for its wildlife including the world’s smallest penguins, Koalas and seals. Beautiful coastal scenery and a range of outdoor activities make this destination a hit with tourists from around the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South-Eastern suburbs

The South-Eastern suburbs offer several great hiking and biking routes:

  • The Main Yarra Trail: This 33-kilometer trail starts in Heidelberg and takes you through some of the most beautiful areas of the Yarra River. Enjoy stunning views of the city skyline along the way.

  • The Bay Trail: This 28-kilometer trail hugs the coastline from Brighton all the way to Seaford. The flat and easy route makes this track great for beginners.

Best Things to Do in South-Eastern suburbs: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in the South-Eastern suburbs. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Visit the Moonlit Sanctuary Wildlife Conservation Park: This award-winning wildlife park in Pearcedale gives visitors the opportunity to get up close and personal with Australian native animals including wallabies, kangaroos, and koalas.

  • Go Wine Tasting: The Mornington Peninsula is known for its exceptional wineries. Be sure to stop by some cellar doors and try the local produce.

  • Take a Balloon Ride: See the South-Eastern suburbs from a new angle. A hot air balloon ride is a peaceful way to take in the stunning rural and coastal landscapes of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for South-Eastern suburbs

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in the South-Eastern suburbs:

Day 1: Get Acquainted with Melbourne

Head into Melbourne CBD, explore the vibrant city centre’s boutique laneways, enjoy a coffee and familiarize yourself with Victoria’s historic capital.

Day 2: Embrace the Great Outdoors

Take a drive through the Dandenong Ranges, explore stunning gardens and nip into intriguing stores for some retail therapy. Also enjoy nature around the region.

Day 3: Phillip Island Adventure

Take a trip to Phillip Island to see the penguin parade and spot seals at the Seal Rocks. If you can, time your visit so you can take in the stunning coastal scenery on your way there and back.

Day 4: The Mornington Peninsula Wine Region

Tour the Mornington Peninsula Wine Region sampling premium wines and enjoying the stunning views.

Day 5: Emerald and Puffing Billy

Take a trip to the township of Emerald, where you can board the Puffing Billy steam train and experience the scenic Dandenong Ranges.

Day 6: Phillip Island Wildlife Park & Panny’s Chocolate Factory

Visit Moonlit Sanctuary Wildlife Conservation Park in Pearcedale, and then head to Phillip island for a tour of Panny’s Chocolate Factory.

Day 7: Wilsons Promontory National Park

Take a day trip to Wilsons Promontory National Park to explore the many hiking trails and take in the stunning scenery of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of South-Eastern suburbs

The South-Eastern suburbs are renowned for their diverse food and drink offerings. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spaghetti Carbonara: This classic pasta dish is a favorite on many menus, but I suggest trying it at Trattoria Emilia where they use pancetta, parmesan and fresh pasta.

  • Lamb Shawarma: For a healthy and delicious lunch option, try lamb shawarma at Oasis Bakery in Murrumbeena.

  • Fried Chicken: For amazing fried chicken, look no further than Belle’s Hot Chicken in Windsor. The southern-style recipe has a cult following.

Local Beverages

  • Mornington Peninsula Wine: The region’s hot-tempered wines have been making their mark on the world stage, be sure to try them while you’re in the area.

  • Ginger Beer: Melbourne company Strangelove makes a ginger beer that is sweet, spicy and quite refreshing.

  • Cider: Sample one of the local ciders available on tap at Mornington Peninsula Brewery in Mornington.

Where to Stay in South-Eastern suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

In the South-Eastern suburbs, there are many great accommodation options to choose from. Here are a few:

Hotels

  • The Langham Melbourne: This luxury hotel is located right in the heart of Southbank with stunning city views.

  • Park Hyatt Melbourne: A 5-star hotel, located in East Melbourne and right next to Fitzroy Gardens.

  • The Blackman Hotel: Signature Suites inspired by artist Charles Blackman, this hotel is in one of Melbourne’s trendiest hubs, St Kilda.

Hostels

  • Melbourne Metro YHA: A budget-friendly option located in trendy North Melbourne within walking distance of the CBD.

  • The Nunnery: This historic hostel is located in Fitzroy and caters to budget travellers with private and dorm rooms available.

Guesthouses

  • Adeline Guest House: This guest house is located in the heart of St Kilda and provides the ultimate sanctuary in this buzzing Melbourne neighbourhood.

  • The Gatehouse: A well-appointed guesthouse located in Mentone and catering to couples looking for a luxurious stay.

What to Buy in South-Eastern suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When looking for the perfect souvenir or local product to take home from South-Eastern suburbs, consider the following:

  • Art: South-Eastern suburbs are known for their arts and crafts. Visit markets and stores in the area to discover unique and one-of-a-kind pieces.

  • Wine: The Mornington Peninsula is one of Australia’s most prosperous wine regions. Bring home a bottle or two to enjoy.

  • Chocolates: Visitors can indulge in a variety of chocolates at Panny’s Chocolate Factory or local shops like The Chocolate Shop in Prahran. If you’re looking for a gift, consider boxed chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of South-Eastern suburbs

Melbourne is a highly diverse city that values cultural exchange and understands its rich history can include many voices and experiences. It’s essential that you take an open-minded and respectful approach to the area’s customs and culture.

Best Time to Visit South-Eastern suburbs

The best time to visit the South-Eastern suburbs is between March to May and September to November when the weather is milder, and rainfall is limited. In summer, temperatures can soar, making outdoor activities less comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of South-Eastern suburbs

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to remember while visiting South-Eastern suburbs:

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the local people.

  • Learn a few phrases in the local language; it shows that you are interested in their culture.

  • Dress casually and appropriately for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave rubbish behind while enjoying outdoor activities.

  • Don’t make loud or disruptive public comments that could be considered offensive.

  • Don’t take photos or film in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in South-Eastern suburbs

The South-Eastern suburbs are generally safe, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions such as carrying photocopies of their passport and valuables protection.

Health in South-Eastern suburbs

The South-Eastern suburbs have a health system, with medical facilities located throughout the area. Tourists should consider purchasing medical insurance before travelling to Australia.

Getting to South-Eastern suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to and around the South-Eastern suburbs is easy to do. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near South-Eastern suburbs

  • Melbourne Airport: This primary airport is about 25 kilometers northwest of the South-Eastern suburbs.

  • Moorabbin Airport: This airport is about 20 kilometers southeast of Melbourne’s city center.

Bus Stations Near South-Eastern suburbs

  • Melbourne Bus Terminal caters to many bus companies arriving and leaving Melbourne, including Grayhound, Fire Fly, and VLine buses.

  • Cranbourne Bus Station: The bus station operates services around the city.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car in Melbourne is a great option if you plan on seeing the surrounding areas.

  • Train Travel: Melbourne city has exceptional train services for day trips out of the city.

How to Get Around South-Eastern suburbs

The easiest way to explore the South-Eastern suburbs is by car. Alternatively, public transportation such as buses and trains is also available.

Where to Go Next from South-Eastern suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South-Eastern suburbs are the perfect starting point for exploring Melbourne’s outskirts and surrounding regions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Great Ocean Road: This scenic coastal drive is home to stunning beaches and natural wonders.

  • Yarra Valley Wine Region: This world-famous wine region is only an hour east of Melbourne.

Conclusion

The South-Eastern suburbs are an often-overlooked gem in the Melbourne region. With vibrant cultural experiences, incredible natural scenery and unlimited opportunities to indulge in great food and wine, South-Eastern suburbs should be on every traveler’s itinerary. Plan your trip accordingly and you will fall in love with the South-Eastern suburbs – we guarantee it!
