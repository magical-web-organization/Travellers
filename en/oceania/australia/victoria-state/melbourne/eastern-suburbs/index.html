Introduction to Eastern Suburbs

Located in the eastern part of Melbourne, Eastern suburbs has become a popular destination for travelers due to its abundance of natural attractions, cultural sites, and outdoor activities. The region is known for its scenic views, lush forests, beautiful parks, and cultural landmarks. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historical sites, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Eastern suburbs.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Suburbs

If you’re planning to visit Eastern suburbs, make sure to check out these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heide Museum of Modern Art
  • Montsalvat Arts Centre
  • Box Hill Historic Cemetery
  • Ringwood Clocktower
  • Heritage Hill Museum and Historic Gardens

Natural Attractions of Eastern Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dandenong Ranges National Park
  • Yarra Valley Wine Region
  • Blackburn Lake Sanctuary
  • Sherbrooke Forest
  • Lysterfield Lake Park

  • 1000 Steps Walk

Best Things to Do in Eastern Suburbs: Activities and Tours

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Suburbs

The eastern suburbs of Melbourne is home to a diverse range of cultures, including Chinese, Vietnamese, and Indian. Visitors to the region should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Suburbs

The best time to visit Eastern suburbs is during the spring (September to November) or autumn (March to May) when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are at their most colorful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Suburbs

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Visit the cultural and historical attractions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Underestimate the distance between attractions and plan your trip accordingly

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Suburbs

Eastern suburbs is generally safe for visitors, but it’s still important to take precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in areas with little foot traffic, and keep your belongings secure.

Health in Eastern Suburbs

Australia has a universal healthcare system, so visitors to Eastern suburbs can access medical treatment if necessary. It’s recommended to take out adequate travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.
