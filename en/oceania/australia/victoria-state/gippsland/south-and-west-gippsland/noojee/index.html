Introduction to Noojee

Located in South and West Gippsland in Victoria, Australia, Noojee is a nature lover’s paradise. It’s a small town surrounded by the stunning Strzelecki Ranges, lush forests, and pristine rivers. It’s the perfect destination for those seeking an adventure in nature, away from the crowds.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Noojee.

List of Places to Visit in Noojee

Noojee is a rich cultural and historical town, offering a range of fascinating sights and experiences. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Noojee.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Noojee Trestle Bridge - This iconic wooden railway trestle bridge was built in 1919 and stands at 102 feet high. It offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and is an iconic landmark of Noojee.
  • Noojee Heritage Centre - This museum showcases the rich heritage and history of Noojee. It includes displays of local artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia of the region.
  • Neerim South Heritage Walk - This self-guided walk takes visitors through the historic streets of Neerim South and showcases the town’s rich heritage.

Natural Attractions of Noojee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Toorongo Falls Reserve - This reserve is home to two stunning waterfalls, Toorongo and Amphitheatre falls, as well as scenic walking tracks and picnic areas.
  • Mount Baw Baw - Located just an hour away from Noojee, Mount Baw Baw is a popular ski resort in winter and an adventure playground in the summer. It offers a range of activities such as hiking, mountain biking, sightseeing, and much more.
  • Ada Tree - This giant Mountain Ash tree is over 300 years old and stands tall at 76 meters. It’s a popular spot among nature lovers and hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noojee

Noojee has some of the most stunning walking and biking trails in Victoria. Here are some of the best routes to explore.

  • Noon Hill Trail - This hiking trail is a 7km loop track that offers stunning views of the valley and the Trestle Bridge.
  • Ada Tree to Amble Inn - This 14km hike takes visitors through some of the most stunning natural landscapes of the region, such as the Ada Tree and Toorongo Falls Reserve.
  • Latrobe River Walk - This easy 3.4km track is suitable for all ages and takes visitors along the Latrobe River and through the stunning Noojee Trestle Bridge.

Best Things to Do in Noojee: Activities and Tours

  • Noojee Horse Riding Centre - Experience the awe-inspiring scenery of Noojee on horseback. The horse riding center offers guided tours through some of the most scenic spots in the area.
  • Mount Baw Baw Alpine Resort - Offering a range of activities such as skiing, snowboarding, and hiking, Mount Baw Baw is a popular destination for adventure seekers.
  • Toorongo River Sanctuary - This sanctuary offers visitors the opportunity to get up close and personal with some of Australia’s unique wildlife species such as kangaroos, wallabies, and platypus.

Food and Drink Scene of Noojee

Noojee offers a range of dining options, from quaint cafes to pubs serving traditional Australian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks to experience in Noojee.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef and Guinness Pie - This hearty pie is a classic dish in Australian cuisine and is served in many of the local pubs.
  • Lamb Shank - This slow-cooked lamb shank is another Australian classic, offering a hearty and flavorsome meal.
  • Fish and Chips - Freshly caught fish and thick chips, served with a side of tartar sauce, is another must-try dish in Noojee.

Local Beverages

  • Beechworth Brewery - This local brewery offers a variety of beers, including their popular “Pale Ale.”
  • Yarra Valley Cider - Made from the fresh apples and pears of the Yarra Valley, this cider is a refreshing local drink perfect for the Australian climate.

Where to Stay in Noojee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Noojee offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to backpacker hostels. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in Noojee.

  • Noojee Hotel - This historic hotel offers comfortable accommodation with a traditional Australian pub experience.
  • Toorongo River Sanctuary - This eco-friendly sanctuary offers visitors a unique glamping experience, surrounded by stunning natural landscapes.
  • Noojee Dairy - This beautiful guesthouse is set on a working dairy farm and offers rustic charm and a peaceful retreat from the city.

What to Buy in Noojee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Noojee

Noojee has a rich and diverse culture, with many local customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Noojee.

  • Respect for nature - Noojee is home to many unique and endangered species of flora and fauna. Visitors are asked to be mindful of their impact on the environment and minimize waste.
  • Friendliness and hospitality - The locals in Noojee are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Visitors are welcomed with open arms and encouraged to interact with the locals.
  • Love for Aussie Rules Football - Australian Rules Football is a popular sport in the region, and visitors are encouraged to join the locals and support the local team during the season.

Best Time to Visit Noojee

Noojee is a year-round destination, offering something for visitors in every season. The peak season runs from December to February, and visitors can expect warm temperatures and a bustling tourist scene. The winter season (June to August) offers a range of winter activities such as skiing and snowboarding. For those seeking a quieter and more peaceful visit, the shoulder seasons of March to May and September to November are an excellent choice.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noojee

When visiting Noojee, there are several things to keep in mind to ensure a pleasant and enjoyable stay.

  • Experience the local culture - Interacting with the locals will provide a unique and authentic experience of Noojee.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks - There are many delicious foods and drinks to try, take advantage of the local produce.

Safety and Health

When visiting Noojee, there are a few safety and health considerations to keep in mind.

Safety in Noojee

  • Natural hazards - Noojee is home to many natural hazards, such as fast-flowing rivers and steep cliffs. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and follow safety guidelines.
  • Hiking and outdoor activities - Visitors should ensure they have appropriate clothing and equipment when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Wildlife - Noojee is home to many unique and endangered species of flora and fauna. Visitors should be mindful of their impact on the environment and respect the animals.

Health in Noojee

  • Sun exposure - Australia has a high UV index, and visitors should ensure they wear sunscreen and appropriate clothing to prevent sunburn.
  • Emergency Services - In case of an emergency, visitors should dial 000 to contact emergency services.

Bus Stations Near Noojee

Other Options

How to Get Around in Noojee

Noojee is a small town, and most of the attractions and sights are within walking distance. For those who want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car or taking a tour is the most convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Noojee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Noojee is surrounded by many other stunning attractions and destinations. Here are some nearby places to consider visiting.

Conclusion
