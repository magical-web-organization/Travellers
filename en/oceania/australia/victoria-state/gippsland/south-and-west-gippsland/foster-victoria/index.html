Foster (Victoria) Travel Guide: Attractions, Activities, and Logistics

Nestled in the heart of South and West Gippsland, Foster (Victoria) is a charming town in Victoria (state) that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures or prefer to immerse yourself in the rich local culture, there’s something for everyone in Foster. Plan your trip with our comprehensive travel guide featuring top attractions, things to do, dining and accommodation options, transportation logistics, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Foster (Victoria)

Foster (Victoria) has a diverse range of attractions to suit every traveler, from cultural and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor activities. Here is a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Port Julia Art Gallery: This impressive gallery hosts an extensive collection of artwork by renowned local artists.
  • Tarwin Lower Historic Town – Great Southern Rail Trail: This once-thriving port town offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s pioneering history.
  • Toora Heritage Pear Orchard: Experience a different kind of agriculture in the region’s only surviving pear orchard.
  • Foster Museum: Explore the area’s history and artifacts, including Aboriginal relics and a collection of vintage cars.
  • Woorarra Cemetery: Visit the gravesite of the notorious criminal Ned Kelly’s father and other notable pioneers.

Natural Attractions of Foster (Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shallow Inlet Coastal Reserve: Went fishing, or swimming in the pristine waters of Shallow Inlet Coastal Reserve.
  • Agnes Falls Scenic Reserve: Experience the beauty of the region’s highest single span waterfall, an incredible 59 meters high.
  • Yarram Heritage Walking Trail: A picturesque walk giving visitors an insight into the historical, religious, and cultural landmarks significant to the Yarram and district community.
  • Wilsons Promontory National Park: Go camping, hiking, or fishing in this stunning park.
  • Tarra-Bulga National Park: Explore magnificent rainforests, picturesque beauty spots, and fascinating plants and animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Foster (Victoria)

Foster has a range of scenic hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top trails include:

  • Great Southern Rail Trail - Foster to Fish Creek: A flat and easy 18 km ride which takes in scenic countryside and small towns.
  • Prom Coastal Walk Follows: The aptly named walk takes in majestic coastal views and finishes at Tidal River camping area.
  • Mount Nicoll Circuit Walk: A 8.6km moderate track that takes hikers along the slopes of Mount Nicoll that’s known for its abundant birdlife.
  • Agnes Falls Scenic Reserve Walking Track: A short and easy walk leads hikers past a lush forest and clear streams that culminate in the magnificent waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Foster (Victoria): Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a culture enthusiast, an adrenaline junkie, or a nature lover, Foster offers a range of activities and tours to suit everyone’s preferences. These are some of the best things to do:

  • Foster Farmers Market: A community-run market that opens on the third Saturday of every month, featuring local produce and crafts.
  • Foster Golf Club: Enjoy a round of golf set among an idyllic rural setting.
  • Foster Arts Center: Visit this center to explore the work of local artists or take part in an art class workshop.
  • Foster Pool: Enjoy a refreshing dip in Foster’s community pool.
  • Southern Gippsland Wine Trail: Join a wine tasting tour and visit local wineries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Foster (Victoria)

Foster has a lot to offer, and seven days is an ideal time to explore the most impressive sites and activities. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Itinerary
1 Arrive and get settled in, explore Foster’s downtown
2 Visit local museums and galleries
3 Go hiking in Wilsons Promontory National Park
4 Take an off-road adventure exploring Tarra-Bulga National Park
5 Enjoy wine tasting tours and other local attractions
6 Take a bike ride along the Great Southern Rail Trail
7 Have a relaxing day, explore Foster’s restaurants and cafes, and prepare for departure

Food and Drink Scene of Foster (Victoria)

Foster’s dining scene is as diverse as its attractions, with restaurants, cafes, and diners catering to all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips: Freshly caught seafood is a must-try in this coastal town.
  • Gippsland beef: Sample the region’s world-renowned beef at one of the local steakhouses.
  • Cherry tomatoes: Foster produces some of the best cherry tomatoes you’ll ever taste.
  • Pear wine and cider: In the region’s only surviving pear orchard, The Appleshed, visitors can sample original pear cider and pear wine.
  • Berry farms: Picking one’s fresh berries is an excellent family outing in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Gippsland wine: Raise a glass to the region’s rich winemaking tradition.
  • Breweries: Try some unique ales and lagers at the local microbreweries.

Where to Stay in Foster (Victoria): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Foster has many options for accommodation to meet all budgets and tastes. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Abington Briars Cottage: A beautiful historical cottage offering cozy and private rural accommodation.
  • Foster’s Motel: A budget-friendly motel in the heart of Foster.
  • Prom Central Caravan Park: Located near Wilsons Promontory National Park with excellent facilities, this caravan park offers a range of accommodation options.
  • The Hare and the Hart: A luxury boutique hotel in Yarram that offers guests the ultimate indulgence.

What to Buy in Foster (Victoria): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Foster has many unique souvenirs to take home. Some of the popular options include:

  • Cheese and chocolates: Victoria is famous for its dairy farms, so be sure to sample some chocolate and cheese made from the local milk.
  • Local wines and beers: Gippsland’s wine and beer are in high demand, with many small-scale wineries and breweries producing some outstanding and unique beverages.
  • Art and Crafts: From paintings to sculptures and Pottery, there are many beautiful crafts that make excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Foster (Victoria)

Victoria’s culture is diverse, with unique customs and traditions that have been passed on for generations. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting the region:

  • Respect: Always show respect towards the Indigenous people and their culture.
  • Friendliness: Victoria is known for its warm and friendly atmosphere; don’t be shy to say hello and start a conversation.
  • Community: Victoria’s rural communities and small towns are closely-knit, so it’s essential to be mindful of others’ feelings and attitudes towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Foster (Victoria)

The best time to visit Foster is during the summer months from December to February. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Spring and autumn are also excellent times to visit, with mild temperatures and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Foster (Victoria)

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Foster:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment: Leave no trace and adhere to strict fire safety guidelines.
  • Try local food and drink: Indulge in local delicacies like the iconic Australian meat pie or the refreshing local beverages.
  • Explore the outdoors: Foster’s scenic beauty provides endless opportunities for hiking, biking, and water sports.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local culture: Be polite and respectful when interacting with locals, and try to learn a few words of the local language.
  • Underestimate the weather: Victoria’s weather can be unpredictable, so always be prepared for sudden changes in temperature, wind, and rain.
  • Be rude or aggressive: Victoria’s communities are friendly and hospitable people; it’s critical to treat people with kindness and respect.

Safety and Health

It’s also essential to take care of your safety and health while visiting Foster.

Safety in Foster (Victoria)

Foster is a safe place to visit, but as with any destination, visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as locking car doors, keeping valuables out of sight, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Foster (Victoria)

Visitors should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations before visiting Foster. Tap water is generally safe to drink in Victoria, but it’s advisable to carry a water bottle with you when exploring or hiking.

Getting to Foster (Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Foster is easy, thanks to several transportation options.

Airports Near Foster (Victoria)

  • Melbourne Airport: 175 km (2 hours drive) from Foster.
  • Launceston Airport: 260 km (3 hours drive) from Foster.

Bus Stations Near Foster (Victoria)

  • V/Line buses: Buses operated by V/Line, Victoria’s regional public transport company, run between Melbourne and Foster daily.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available from Melbourne and Launceston airports.

How to Get Around in Foster (Victoria)

The best way to get around Foster is by car, as there are many scenic routes and hidden gems that are only accessible by road. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Foster (Victoria): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Foster, visitors can venture to nearby destinations, including:

  • Wilsons Promontory National Park: Located in the south-east of Victoria, this coastal park is known for its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and rugged mountain ranges.
  • Phillip Island: Just a short drive from Melbourne, Phillip Island is home to a vast array of unique wildlife, including fairy penguins, seals, and pelicans.
  • Mornington Peninsula: Renowned for its wineries, hot springs, and coastal villages, this peninsula is a food and wine lover’s paradise.
