French Island is a picturesque island located in South and West Gippsland, in Victoria, Australia. The lush green forests, tranquil beaches, and diverse wildlife make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore all that French Island has to offer, from its natural and cultural attractions to the best places to stay and eat.

Introduction to French Island

Located in Western Port Bay, French Island is the largest coastal island in Victoria. Known for its natural beauty and diverse wildlife, the island is a popular destination for nature lovers and outdoor adventurers. With no cars, no shops, and only a few settlements, the island offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in French Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fairhaven - A small settlement on the island that was once a bustling township and home to around 700 residents in the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of French Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • French Island National Park - Covering most of the island, the national park is a paradise for nature lovers, with diverse wildlife and scenic walking and cycling trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of French Island

  • Cape Barren Circuit Walk - A 10 km long trail that offers stunning views of Western Port Bay.

Best Things to Do in French Island: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Tours - French Island is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including koalas, wallabies, and echidnas. Take a guided wildlife tour to see them up close.

Food and Drink Scene of French Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb with Potatoes - A traditional dish made with locally sourced lamb and fresh potatoes.
  • Seafood Chowder - A creamy soup made with fresh seafood like mussels, prawns, and fish.

Local Beverages

  • French Island Beer - A locally brewed beer made using the island’s fresh water.

Where to Stay in French Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • French Island Glamping - Offering eco-friendly, luxurious glamping experiences on the island.

What to Buy in French Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery - Made using local clay and glazed with natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of French Island

  • Eco-Friendly Living - The residents of French Island have a deep respect for nature and try to live in harmony with the environment.

Best Time to Visit French Island

The best time to visit French Island is from March to May or September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of French Island

Do:

  • Explore the national park
  • Respect the natural environment
  • Take a wildlife tour

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife
  • Litter
  • Bring your car to the island

Safety and Health

Safety in French Island

French Island is a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions against bushfires during the summer months.

Health in French Island

There are no medical facilities on the island, so visitors should bring any necessary medication when travelling.

Getting to French Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near French Island

The nearest airport is Melbourne International Airport, which is about 2 hours away from the island by car.

Bus Stations Near French Island

There are no bus stations on the island.

Other Options

Visitors can reach the island by taking a ferry from Stony Point.

How to Get Around in French Island

Visitors can explore the island on foot, by bike or by taking a guided tour.

Conclusion

French Island is a hidden gem in Victoria, offering a serene and peaceful getaway from the city’s hustle and bustle. With its diverse wildlife, natural beauty, and eco-friendly lifestyle, the island is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your perfect vacation and experience all that French Island has to offer.
