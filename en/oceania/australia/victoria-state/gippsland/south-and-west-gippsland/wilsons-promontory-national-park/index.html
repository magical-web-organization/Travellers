Introduction to Wilsons Promontory National Park

Located at the southernmost tip of the Australian mainland, Wilsons Promontory National Park is a spectacular display of natural wonders covering over 50,000 hectares of untouched wilderness. Also known as ‘The Prom’, the national park stretches across South and West Gippsland in Victoria, offering visitors a wide range of outdoor recreational activities, cultural attractions, and breathtaking scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Wilsons Promontory National Park

There are numerous must-visit places within Wilsons Promontory National Park that truly capture the essence of Australia’s diverse beauty. Here’s a list of some of the most popular spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tidal River - The heart of the park, Tidal River is a coastal haven for camping, barbeques, and a range of activities.
  • Lighthouse and Marine Discovery Centre - Take a guided tour and learn about the history of the Wilsons Promontory Lighthouse and the surrounding marine life.
  • Stringybark Ridge Historic Walk - A ranger-led tour that covers the history of the park when it was first established.

Natural Attractions of Wilsons Promontory National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Oberon Summit - A moderate hike to the summit provides a spectacular 360-degree panoramic view of the Prom’s coastline and hinterland.
  • Squeaky Beach - Famous for its unique and squeaky sand, this beach is a must-visit spot.
  • Sealers Cove - An idyllic spot for camping, with stunning views of the coastlines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilsons Promontory National Park

Wilsons Promontory National Park offers a range of hiking and biking trails, from easy strolls to challenging treks. Here are some of the best tracks to explore:

  • Great Prom Walk - A 56-kilometre track that takes visitors through some of the most scenic wilderness areas that the park has to offer. This trek is recommended for experienced hikers.
  • Tidal Overlook - A short walk starting from the Tidal River campsite, offering a sweeping view of the Tidal River Beach and beyond.
  • Lilly Pilly Gully Nature Walk - A 4-kilometre trail that takes visitors through the lush rainforest and showcases the park’s diversity.

Best Things to Do in Wilsons Promontory National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours that visitors can enjoy at Wilsons Promontory National Park:

  • Snorkelling and Diving at Refuge Cove - Home to an abundance of marine life, this spot is a great place for water enthusiasts.
  • Whale Watching - During the winter months, visitors can witness the Southern Right and Humpback whales as they migrate along the coast.
  • Cultural Tours - Experience the rich cultural heritage of the local Indigenous people with a guided tour from a knowledgeable local Indigenous guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilsons Promontory National Park

When it comes to food and drink, Wilsons Promontory National Park has a wide range of options to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Local Fish and Chips - Try some fresh seafood from the local fish and chip shops in the area.
  • Gippsland Cheese - Sample some of the finest dairy products that Gippsland has to offer.

Local Beverages

  • Gurneys Cider - Made from local apples, this cider is a refreshing drink to enjoy after a long day of hiking.
  • Sailors Grave Brewing - A brewery that prides itself on using native ingredients to create unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Wilsons Promontory National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wilsons Promontory National Park offers a diverse range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences.

  • Tidal River - The only option for accommodation within the park, with a range of camping and cabin options.
  • Sandy Point Beach Resort - A resort located near the park, offering several luxurious and comfortable accommodation options.

What to Buy in Wilsons Promontory National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some of Wilsons Promontory National Park’s unique and local products as a memento of your trip:

  • Handcrafted Pottery - Local artisans create beautiful and unique pottery pieces that you can purchase as a souvenir.
  • Wooden Crafts - Take home a handcrafted wooden item made from the local timber.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilsons Promontory National Park

Wilsons Promontory National Park is rich in culture and heritage, dating back to the times of the local Indigenous people. Visitors should respect the natural and cultural values of the area and ensure they follow the park’s rules and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Wilsons Promontory National Park

The best time to visit Wilsons Promontory National Park is from October to May, avoiding the winter months when the park experiences heavy rainfall and cold temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilsons Promontory National Park

Ensure that you follow the park’s rules and regulations to make your visit an enjoyable and safe one. Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and their habitat.
  • Take your rubbish with you and follow the park’s Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Light fires outside designated areas.
  • Release pets, aquarium animals, or garden plants within the park.

Safety and Health

Visitors should prioritise their safety and health while exploring the park. Here are some tips to ensure you stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Wilsons Promontory National Park

  • Ensure you have enough water and food during your hikes.
  • Check the weather forecast before starting the hike.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear.

Health in Wilsons Promontory National Park

  • Be careful when drinking untreated water.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear insect repellent to avoid sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Wilsons Promontory National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wilsons Promontory National Park is easily accessible by different modes of transportation.

Airports Near Wilsons Promontory National Park

  • Melbourne Airport - 215 kilometres from the park

Bus Stations Near Wilsons Promontory National Park

  • There’s no bus service that goes directly to the park. However, there are bus services that stopover at nearby towns.

Other Options

  • A car rental is the most practical way to get to and around the park.

How to Get Around in Wilsons Promontory National Park

Visitors can use their own vehicles, or walk or cycle to get around the park. Park rangers also run guided tours for visitors who prefer a more comprehensive experience.

Where to Go Next from Wilsons Promontory National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gippsland, the region where Wilsons Promontory National Park is located, has plenty of other charming towns and natural wonders to explore:

  • Phillip Island - A popular destination known for its penguin parade.
  • Foster - A town filled with historical attractions, boutique shops and food and wine experiences.

Conclusion

Wilsons Promontory National Park is a true gem in the Australian wilderness, with its incredible natural landscapes, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities.
