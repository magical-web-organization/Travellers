Introduction to South and West Gippsland

Located in the southeastern region of Victoria, South and West Gippsland is a hidden gem of Australia. This beautiful area is filled with rolling hills, lush farmland, stunning coastlines, and charming small towns, making it a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the true beauty of Australia.

South and West Gippsland also has a fascinating history and culture, with plenty of museums and historical sites to explore, as well as natural attractions that offer outdoor adventures and scenic views. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to this amazing part of Victoria, from where to go and what to do to the best places to eat and stay.

List of Places to Visit in South and West Gippsland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coal Creek Heritage Village: Experience life in rural Victoria from the 1870s to the 1920s at this living history museum.
  • Old Gippstown Heritage Park: Home to a collection of historic buildings, exhibits, and displays that tell the story of Gippsland’s past.
  • Toora Heritage Pear Orchard: Take a walk through this living museum to learn about the history and culture of the region’s pear industry.
  • Foster and District Historical Society: This museum showcases the history of the Foster district, with collections of photographs, artifacts, and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of South and West Gippsland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wilsons Promontory: This breathtaking national park boasts stunning beaches, rugged terrain, and an abundance of wildlife, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers.
  • Agnes Falls: The highest single span falls in Victoria, Agnes Falls is a beautiful natural wonder that is easily accessible from the surrounding area.
  • Mount Worth State Park: Home to some of the best walking trails in the state, this park offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Tarra-Bulga National Park: Featuring ancient rainforests, waterfalls, walking trails, and an abundance of wildlife, Tarra-Bulga is a must-see for anyone visiting the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South and West Gippsland

  • Grand Strzelecki Track: This 80km track takes hikers through some of the most stunning parts of the foothills of the Strzelecki Ranges.
  • Great Southern Rail Trail: Cycle through the beautiful countryside of South Gippsland on this popular 72km cycling trail.
  • Cape Liptrap Coastal Walk: This 7km walking trail offers breathtaking views of the rugged coastline and ocean, showcasing the natural beauty of the region.
  • Five Mile Creek Walking Track: This easy walk takes you through the beautiful Five Mile Creek Gorge, a hidden gem of West Gippsland.

Best Things to Do in South and West Gippsland: Activities and Tours

  • Phillip Island Penguin Parade: Watch the little penguins waddle up the beach at dusk in one of the world’s most popular wildlife attractions.
  • Gippsland Gourmet Country Food and Wine Trail: Visit local farms and wineries to taste the best of Gippsland’s produce, including artisan cheeses, wine, and craft beer.
  • Ride the Rail Trails: Hire a bike and discover the beauty of South and West Gippsland on one of the many rail trails in the area.
  • Fisherman’s Wharf Market: Located in Port Albert on the fourth Sunday of every month, this market offers a variety of local goods and fresh produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for South and West Gippsland

Food and Drink Scene of South and West Gippsland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gippsland Beef: Known for its tender and flavorful taste, Gippsland Beef is a must-try for carnivores.
  • Gippsland Cheese: Gippsland is home to some of the best artisan cheese makers in Australia, with varieties ranging from creamy bries to sharp cheddars.
  • Fish and Chips: With an abundance of fresh seafood available in the region, it’s no wonder that fish and chips is a popular dish in South and West Gippsland.
  • Craft Beer: With an emerging craft beer scene, Gippsland’s breweries are producing some of the most unique and tasty beers in the country.

Local Beverages

  • Gippsland Wine: With a cool climate ideal for growing grapes, Gippsland is home to some exceptional wines.
  • Coffee: With a thriving cafe scene, there is no shortage of excellent coffee in South and West Gippsland, with locally roasted beans being a popular choice.

Where to Stay in South and West Gippsland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Prom Country Lodge: Situated in the heart of Foster, this lodge offers spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Meeniyan Motel: Located in the charming town of Meeniyan, this motel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • The Spud House: A unique stay in the heart of potato country, The Spud House offers a comfortable and quirky experience.
  • Baromi Park Motel: A great choice for families, this motel is located in the center of town and offers spacious rooms and a pool.

What to Buy in South and West Gippsland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gippsland Cheese: Take a piece of Gippsland’s cheese culture home with you, with plenty of local cheese makers offering artisan products.
  • Art and Crafts: With a thriving art scene in South and West Gippsland, there is plenty of locally-made jewelry, pottery, and other crafts to choose from.
  • Wine: Take home a bottle of Victoria’s finest wines from one of the many vineyards in the region.
  • Local Produce: With a focus on farm-to-table dining, there are plenty of local farmers’ markets and specialty stores selling fresh produce, artisanal goods, and preserves.

Local Culture and Customs of South and West Gippsland

South and West Gippsland has a proud agricultural heritage, with a focus on sustainability and environmental responsibility. The region has a strong community spirit, with many small towns hosting festivals and events throughout the year, celebrating local culture and produce.

Best Time to Visit South and West Gippsland

While South and West Gippsland can be visited year-round, the best time to go is during the spring and autumn months. These seasons offer milder weather, fewer crowds, and plenty of opportunities to take in the area’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of South and West Gippsland

Do:

  • Try the local cheese and wine.
  • Explore the natural attractions and go on a hike or bike ride.
  • Visit the local markets and festivals.
  • Take the time to learn about the region’s history and culture.

  • Disrespect local customs or cultures.

Safety and Health

Safety in South and West Gippsland

Overall, South and West Gippsland is a safe destination for travelers. As with any travel destination, it’s a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep your belongings safe. It’s also important to follow safe practices when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or biking, by following local advice and guidelines.

Health in South and West Gippsland

South and West Gippsland has a good standard of healthcare, with plenty of medical clinics and hospitals available in the area. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving and should take appropriate precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated, particularly during the warmer months.

Getting to South and West Gippsland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South and West Gippsland

The nearest airport to South and West Gippsland is Melbourne Tullamarine Airport. From there, you can take a connecting flight to Latrobe Regional Airport, which is located approximately 75km from the region.

Bus Stations Near South and West Gippsland

V/Line offers bus services to various towns and cities in South and West Gippsland, including Leongatha, Foster, and Korumburra.

Other Options

Driving is also an option and will give you the ability to explore some of the smaller towns and scenic routes in the area.

How to Get Around in South and West Gippsland

The best way to get around South and West Gippsland is by car. This will give you the flexibility to explore the surrounding towns and countryside at your own pace. There are also bus services available in the area, but these can be limited and infrequent. Additionally, cycling is a popular way to see the region on the many rail trails in South Gippsland.

Where to Go Next from South and West Gippsland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lakes Entrance: Located on the eastern coast of Victoria, this charming seaside town is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Buchan Caves: Explore the stunning underground world of the Buchan Caves, located approximately 2 hours’ drive from South and West Gippsland.
  • Phillip Island: Just a short drive from South and West Gippsland is Phillip Island, home to stunning beaches, wildlife, and natural attractions.
