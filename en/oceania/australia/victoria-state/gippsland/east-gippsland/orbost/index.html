Are you looking for an offbeat destination to explore in Victoria, Australia? Look no further than Orbost, a charming town located in East Gippsland! Surrounded by natural beauty and rich cultural history, Orbost has plenty to offer visitors seeking adventure, relaxation, or a bit of both. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and helpful tips to plan a memorable trip to Orbost.

Introduction to Orbost

Orbost is a town located in the far east of Victoria, about 375 km east of Melbourne and 235 km south of Canberra. The town is nestled between the Snowy River and the Errinundra National Park, making it a haven for nature lovers. The area is also rich in cultural history, with several important landmarks and museums showcasing the region’s past.

List of Places to Visit in Orbost

If you’re planning a trip to Orbost, you won’t want to miss these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Orbost Post Office Gallery: This beautifully restored heritage building is now a gallery showcasing the work of local artists.
  • Slippery Creek Lookout: A scenic lookout that offers panoramic views of the Snowy River and surrounding mountains.
  • Orbost Exhibition Centre: A cultural center where you can learn about the history and heritage of the area through interactive exhibits.
  • The Grandstand: A historical landmark originally built in 1913, the grandstand offers a glimpse into the town’s sporting heritage.

Natural Attractions of Orbost: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Snowy River National Park: A national park covering over 87000 hectares, Snowy River National Park offers plenty of hiking and camping opportunities, as well as chances to spot kangaroos and other wildlife.
  • Marlo Beach: A popular local beach with calm waters and stunning sunsets.
  • Simpsons Creek: A peaceful picnic area with tranquil waters and scenic views.
  • Cabbage Tree Palms and the Rainforest Walk: A beautiful rainforest walk showcasing the area’s unique wildlife and flora.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orbost

For outdoor enthusiasts, Orbost offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Some popular routes include:

  • Snowy River Wilderness Walk: A multi-day hiking trail that takes you deep into Snowy River National Park and offers stunning views of mountain ranges, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  • Colquhoun State Forest: This forest is home to some of the best mountain biking trails in Victoria, with options for both beginners and experienced riders.
  • The Great Alpine Road: A scenic drive that cuts through Snowy River National Park and offers stunning views of the nearby mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Orbost: Activities and Tours

Looking for something to do in Orbost? Consider these fun activities and tours:

  • Fishing: The Snowy River and surrounding streams are popular destinations for fishing enthusiasts, with opportunities to catch trout, salmon, and more.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take a leisurely paddle down the Snowy River or explore the nearby lakes and waterways by kayak or canoe.
  • Wildlife Tours: Join a guided tour to see kangaroos, wallabies, and other wildlife in their natural habitats.
  • Winery Tours: Visit local wineries to sample some of the best wines in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Orbost

If you’re planning to spend a week in Orbost, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Orbost and explore the town’s cultural landmarks, including the Old Orbost Post Office Gallery and the Orbost Exhibition Centre.
  • Day 2-3: Spend two days exploring Snowy River National Park on foot or by mountain bike.
  • Day 4: Take a leisurely stroll through the Cabbage Tree Palms and the Rainforest Walk before heading to Marlo Beach for a relaxing afternoon.
  • Day 5-6: Enjoy some water activities, such as kayaking or fishing, on the Snowy River or nearby lakes.
  • Day 7: Take a wine-tasting tour of the local wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Orbost

Orbost is a small town, but there are plenty of great dining options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Fresh-caught fish is a local specialty, and you’ll find great fish and chips at several restaurants in town. Try the flake, a type of shark that is popular in the area.
  • Lamb: Victoria produces some of the best lamb in the world, and you can find it on the menu at several restaurants in Orbost.
  • Wattleseed Ice Cream: Wattleseed is a local ingredient that is used to flavor ice cream in several local ice cream shops.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Victoria is known for its wine, and several local wineries offer tastings and tours. Try the shiraz or chardonnay, two popular wine varietals in the area.
  • Beer: Several local breweries offer craft beer tastings, featuring unique flavors such as raspberry ale and ginger beer.

Where to Stay in Orbost: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Orbost offers several types of accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Orbost Motel: This family-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • Orbost Country Roads Motor Inn: A small, cozy motel with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Travellers Rest Motel: A budget-friendly option for travelers seeking basic amenities and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Orbost: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to remember your trip to Orbost? Consider these local products:

  • Local Wine: Take home a bottle of Victoria’s famous wine to share with friends and family.
  • Artisanal Honey: Victoria produces some of the best honey in the world, and you can find it at several local shops.
  • Handmade Crafts: Several local artists sell handmade crafts, such as pottery and jewelry, at the Old Orbost Post Office Gallery and other shops in town.

Local Culture and Customs of Orbost

Orbost is a friendly and welcoming town, and locals are proud of their heritage and natural beauty. Visitors should show respect for the local culture, including the area’s Aboriginal heritage and respect for the environment.

Best Time to Visit Orbost

The best time to visit Orbost is during the spring and autumn months, when temperatures are mild and rainfall is low. However, the town is beautiful year-round and offers plenty of outdoor activities in all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orbost

When visiting Orbost, keep in mind these helpful do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and local wildlife.
  • Show respect for the area’s Aboriginal heritage.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Orbost is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should always take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Orbost

  • Avoid swimming in the Snowy River during periods of heavy rain, as the river can become dangerous and unpredictable.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when hiking or biking. Trail conditions and weather can change quickly.
  • Look both ways before crossing the road, as traffic can be heavy in and around town.

Health in Orbost

  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent, as the area can be prone to sunburn and mosquitoes.
  • Carry plenty of water when hiking or spending time outdoors.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you become ill or injured.

Getting to Orbost: Transportation Options and Logistics

Orbost is located about 375 km east of Melbourne and 235 km south of Canberra. Visitors can get to Orbost by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Orbost

The nearest airports to Orbost include:

  • Melbourne Airport: Located about 350 km west of Orbost, Melbourne Airport offers connections to major cities within Australia and around the world.
  • Canberra Airport: Located about 250 km north of Orbost, Canberra Airport offers connections to major cities within Australia.

Bus Stations Near Orbost

Several bus companies offer connections to Orbost from major cities within Victoria and New South Wales, including:

  • Greyhound Australia: Offers connections from Melbourne and Sydney.
  • V/Line: Offers connections from Melbourne and Bairnsdale.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Orbost by car, as the town is located along the Princes Highway.

How to Get Around in Orbost

The best way to get around Orbost is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi to explore the local area.

Where to Go Next from Orbost: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Victoria, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Bairnsdale: A historic town located about 50 km west of Orbost, Bairnsdale is known for its beautiful architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • Lakes Entrance: A seaside resort town located about 50 km east of Orbost, Lakes Entrance is famous for its stunning beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Croajingolong National Park: A nearby national park that offers plenty of outdoor adventures, including hiking, camping, and fishing.

Conclusion

Orbost may be a small town, but it offers plenty of opportunities for adventure, relaxation, and cultural exploration. Whether you’re hiking through Snowy River National Park, sampling local wines, or exploring the town’s heritage landmarks, Orbost is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next trip to Orbost, and experience all that this hidden gem has to offer!
