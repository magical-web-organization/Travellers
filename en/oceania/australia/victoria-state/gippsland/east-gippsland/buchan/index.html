Introduction to Buchan

Buchan is a hidden gem in Victoria, Australia, known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Located in East Gippsland, Buchan is surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills, and shimmering lakes, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers, hikers, bikers, and photographers.

The town is renowned for its remarkable caves, featuring stunning stalactites and stalagmites, underground rivers, and chambers filled with awe-inspiring formations. Buchan is also home to several cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks, providing visitors with a glimpse into the unique history and traditions of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Buchan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buchan Historical Society Museum: Showcasing the unique history and heritage of Buchan, this museum features fascinating artifacts, photographs, and documents related to the region’s Indigenous culture and European settlement.

  • St. Mary’s Anglican Church: A significant landmark of Buchan, this historic church boasts stunning architecture and intricate designs, providing a glimpse into the region’s religious past.

  • Buchan South School Museum: This museum is housed in a former school building and offers an insight into early education in the region. Visitors can explore the classrooms and learn about the daily life of students and teachers.

Natural Attractions of Buchan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buchan Caves Reserve: This stunning reserve boasts a network of limestone caves, featuring unique formations such as stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors can explore the caves on a guided tour or hike one of the many trails that wind through the surrounding forests and gorges.

  • Snowy River National Park: A paradise for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers, this national park boasts rugged mountains, crystal clear rivers, and diverse wildlife. Visitors can hike through the park, relax by the river, or enjoy a picnic in the lush green valleys.

  • Buchan River: This idyllic river is perfect for fishing, kayaking, and swimming. Surrounded by lush green forests and towering cliffs, the Buchan River provides an oasis of peace and tranquility.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buchan

  • Bennies Loop Walk: This 5.5-kilometer walk winds through the Buchan Caves Reserve, featuring stunning views of the surrounding forests, gorges, and rivers.

  • Snowy Range Walk: This challenging 6-kilometer walk takes visitors through the rugged mountains of the Snowy River National Park, providing breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Buchan South-Stirling Loop: This 11-kilometer loop walk is perfect for adventurous hikers, featuring stunning panoramic views of the Buchan River, Snowy River Valley, and Alpine region.

Best Things to Do in Buchan: Activities and Tours

  • Cave Tours: Explore the stunning limestone caves of Buchan on a guided tour, featuring unique formations, underground rivers, and fascinating geological history.

  • Fishing: The Buchan River is a popular spot for fishing, home to a variety of fish species such as trout, cod, and bass.

  • Trekking: Buchan offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, suitable for all skill levels and providing stunning views of the region’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Buchan

Day 1: Explore the Buchan Caves Reserve, and take a guided tour of the caves.

Day 2: Hike one of the many trails in the Snowy River National Park, featuring stunning views of the surrounding mountains, rivers, and valleys.

Day 3: Go fishing in the Buchan River or relax by the water.

Day 4: Visit the Buchan Historical Society Museum and learn about the unique history and heritage of the region.

Day 5: Take a trip to the nearby town of Bruthen and visit the Bruthen Village Gallery and take a self-guided walk along the Tambo River.

Day 6: Enjoy a picnic in the Buchan South Reserve, or go camping in one of the many camping areas.

Day 7: Detoxify your body and rejuvenate your soul by spending a day at the nearby Buchan Thermal Pools.

Food and Drink Scene of Buchan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buchan Beef: This local delicacy is made from locally raised beef and is a must-try for meat lovers.

  • Buchan Trout: Freshly caught from the Buchan River, this trout dish is a popular choice among seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Buchan Brewery: This local brewery offers a variety of locally brewed beers, perfect for those who love craft beer.

  • High-Country Winery: This winery produces a range of classic wines, including Shiraz, Chardonnay, and Pinot Noir, as well as sparkling wines.

Where to Stay in Buchan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Buchan Caves Hotel: This historic hotel is located near the Buchan Caves Reserve, offering comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Buchan Motel: This family-owned motel offers comfortable rooms, a BBQ area, and easy access to local attractions.

  • Buchan Valley Motel: Located in the heart of Buchan, this motel offers rooms with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

What to Buy in Buchan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: Buchan honey is a must-try, and is available for purchase at several local stores.

  • Handmade crafts: Buchan is home to several talented artisans who sell their handmade crafts at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Buchan

Buchan has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the region’s Indigenous and European history. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions of the region, and learn about its unique history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Buchan

The best time to visit Buchan is between March and May and between September and November, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buchan

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the region.

  • Try the local food and beverages.

  • Explore the local attractions and natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Disrespect the local people or culture.

  • Ignore the safety guidelines and rules at the attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buchan

Buchan is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as securing valuables and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Buchan

Buchan has a medical center and a pharmacy, and visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication or medical supplies with them.

Getting to Buchan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buchan

The nearest airport to Buchan is Melbourne Airport, which is around a 4-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Buchan

V/Line operates daily services to and from Buchan from Bairnsdale, Melbourne, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to reach Buchan.

How to Get Around in Buchan

Buchan is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the local attractions on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Buchan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lakes Entrance: A popular coastal town, known for its stunning beaches and water sports activities.

  • The Snowy River: A scenic river, ideal for fishing and rafting, located in the Snowy River National Park.

  • The Great Alpine Road: A scenic road trip, featuring stunning vistas of the Alpine region, including waterfalls, lakes, and mountains.

Conclusion

Buchan is a perfect destination for those looking for natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. With its stunning caves, breathtaking hiking trails, delicious local food, and rich history, Buchan offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature lover, foodie, culture enthusiast, or adventure seeker, Buchan is waiting to be explored.
