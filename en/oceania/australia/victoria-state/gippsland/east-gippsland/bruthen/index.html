Located in East Gippsland, Victoria, Bruthen is a charming town that offers travelers a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventurous backpacker or a history buff, this town has something for everybody. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Bruthen

Bruthen is a small town that sits along the Tambo River, surrounded by stunning forests and impressive mountains. The town is known for its strong sense of community, friendly locals, and laid-back lifestyle. Although it may be small, Bruthen has a rich and fascinating history that dates back to the gold rush era.

List of Places to Visit in Bruthen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bruthen and District Historical Society Museum: This small museum is dedicated to preserving the town’s history and showcasing local artifacts, including photos, maps, and vintage machinery.

  • The Bruthen Heritage Trail: This self-guided tour takes you through the town’s historic buildings, including the old courthouse, bank, and post office.

  • St. Colman’s Catholic Church: Built-in 1884, this stunning church is a must-visit for its Victorian-era architecture and beautiful stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Bruthen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Buchan Caves: Located just a short drive from Bruthen, the Buchan Caves are a network of limestone caves that offer visitors an awe-inspiring glimpse into the underground world.

  • The Colquhoun State Forest: This vast forest is home to some of the most beautiful and diverse flora and fauna in Victoria. Visitors can enjoy bushwalking, picnicking, and even camping in the forest.

  • The Tambo River: For fishing enthusiasts, head to the Tambo River, which is home to a range of fish species, including the iconic Australian bass and trout.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bruthen

  • The Colquhoun Mountain Bike Trail: This 14km track takes you through some of the most picturesque parts of the Colquhoun State Forest. The trail is suitable for all skill levels and offers some stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • The East Gippsland Rail Trail: A fantastic way to experience the region’s natural beauty, the rail trail is a 94km track that runs through the region’s diverse landscape, including wetlands, forests, and farmland.

Best Things to Do in Bruthen: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Adventures: For those who love fishing, guided tours on the Tambo River are available for anyone looking to catch an Australian bass or trout.

  • Horse Riding Adventures: The Great Dividing Trail is a great place to go horse riding, offering breathtaking views of the forests and mountains.

  • Buchan Caves Adventure Tours: These tours take you on an unforgettable journey through the stunning Buchan Caves, where you can explore and marvel at awe-inspiring rock formations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bruthen

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s historic buildings, including the courthouse, bank, and post office.

  • Day 2-3: Take guided nature walks through the Colquhoun State Forest.

  • Day 4: Spend a day fishing on the Tambo River.

  • Day 5: Visit the Buchan Caves and go on a cave exploration tour.

  • Day 6: Horse riding adventures on the Great Dividing Trail.

  • Day 7: Relax and soak in the tranquil surroundings of Bruthen.

Food and Drink Scene of Bruthen

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Bruthen Bakery: This small bakery offers a range of savory and sweet pies, perfect for a quick bite on the go.

  • The Bullant Brewery: A brewery and restaurant serving up tasty comfort food and beer brewed on-site.

Local Beverages

  • Bullant Brewery Beer: A range of locally brewed beers, including lagers, ales, and stouts, are available at the Bullant Brewery.

Where to Stay in Bruthen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bruthen Inn Hotel: This charming hotel offers comfortable rooms and a great pub menu.

  • the House in Bruthen: A cozy and pet-friendly guesthouse located just a short walk from the town’s shops and restaurants.

What to Buy in Bruthen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bruthen Arts and Crafts Market: Held on the fourth Sunday of every month, the market offers handmade items, including jewelry, pottery, and paintings.

  • Bullant Brewery Beer: Take home a six-pack or two of the locally brewed beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Bruthen

Bruthen is a friendly and welcoming community that values its local heritage and natural environment. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s culture and customs and participate in local events and activities.

Best Time to Visit Bruthen

The best time to visit Bruthen is during the summer months (December to February). The weather is mild and pleasant, perfect for exploring the town and surrounding areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bruthen

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and customs.

  • Participate in local events and activities.

  • Take care of the environment and natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals and their beliefs.

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Ignore safety guidelines when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bruthen

Bruthen is a safe and friendly town. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions when exploring the outdoors, including wearing appropriate clothing and footwear and carrying a map and plenty of drinking water.

Health in Bruthen

Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance and to carry any necessary medications with them. The town has a general practice medical center and a pharmacy.

Getting to Bruthen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bruthen

The closest airport to Bruthen is Bairnsdale Airport, located just under an hour’s drive from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Bruthen

V/Line operates daily bus services to Bruthen from Melbourne and other regional towns.

Other Options

Driving is an excellent option for getting to Bruthen. The town is easily accessible via the Princes Highway, and there is ample parking available.

How to Get Around in Bruthen

The best way to get around Bruthen is on foot or by bike. The town is relatively small and easy to navigate, and there are plenty of cycling tracks and paths in the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bruthen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lakes Entrance: A popular holiday destination located just 30 minutes drive from Bruthen, Lakes Entrance is a beautiful town known for its stunning waterways and beaches.

  • Bairnsdale: A historic town located just under an hour’s drive from Bruthen, Bairnsdale is home to some of the finest colonial architecture in Victoria.

Conclusion

Bruthen may be a small town, but it offers travelers a unique and unforgettable experience. From the stunning nature trails to the rich local culture and food scene, this town is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a solo traveler or traveling with your family or friends, you’re bound to fall in love with Bruthen’s charm and hospitality.
