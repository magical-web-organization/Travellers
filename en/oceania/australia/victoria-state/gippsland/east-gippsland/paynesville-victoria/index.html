Introduction to Paynesville (Victoria)

Nestled in East Gippsland, Paynesville is a charming waterfront town in Victoria that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Known as the boating capital of Victoria, Paynesville overlooks the magnificent Gippsland Lakes and offers access to boating, fishing, hiking, cycling, and golfing opportunities.

List of Places to Visit in Paynesville (Victoria)

There is no shortage of things to see and do in Paynesville. From culturally significant attractions to breathtaking natural wonders, this town has it all. Here is a list of the top places you must visit when in Paynesville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bairnsdale Historical Museum: This museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts, documents, and photos that tell the story of East Gippsland’s history. It offers a glimpse into the lives of the early settlers and their struggles.
  • St Mary’s Catholic Church: Built-in 1920, St Mary’s is a striking example of Gothic-inspired architecture. With its beautiful stained-glass windows and grand bell tower, it is a must-visit for architecture enthusiasts.
  • Paynesville Maritime Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the maritime history of Paynesville. It houses a collection of artifacts, including boats and other items related to boating.

Natural Attractions of Paynesville (Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Raymond Island: A short ferry ride from Paynesville takes you to the Raymond Island, where you can spot koalas in their natural habitat. Walking trails run across the island, offering a chance to explore the unspoiled wilderness.
  • Mitchell River National Park: This majestic national park is located east of Paynesville and offers scenic views of the Mitchell River. With several walking trails, camping sites, and picnic areas, it’s a perfect place to spend a day out in nature.
  • Paynesville Beach: This sandy beach is a perfect place for a relaxing day out with friends and family. It offers beautiful views of the Gippsland Lakes and is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Victoria. Here are some popular ones:

  • Koala Trail: A 1.2-kilometer circuit that takes you through the heart of Paynesville and offers opportunities to spot koalas in their natural habitat.
  • East Gippsland Rail Trail: This 100-kilometer rail trail starts from Bairnsdale and passes through picturesque towns like Lakes Entrance and Orbost before ending in Newmerella.
  • Silt Jetties Trail: This 6-kilometer trail takes you along the Mitchell River and offers scenic views of the Silt Jetties.

Best Things to Do in Paynesville (Victoria): Activities and Tours

There is an abundance of activities and tours to choose from in Paynesville. Here are some must-try ones:

  • Gippsland Lakes Eco Tours: This tour lets you explore the majestic Gippsland Lakes like never before. With an experienced guide, you can learn about the local flora and fauna and even spot dolphins and seals.
  • Fishing Charters: Paynesville is known for its fishing opportunities, and several fishing charters are available to take you out on the water and help you catch some of the freshest fish around.
  • Lakes Entrance Helicopters: Experience the beauty of Paynesville from the sky with a scenic helicopter ride offered by Lakes Entrance Helicopters.

Food and Drink Scene of Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville is not just about natural beauty and outdoor adventures. It also offers a vibrant food and drink scene that will satisfy any foodie. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gippsland Lamb: This succulent lamb dish is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. Served with roasted veggies and a glass of red wine, it’s a meal you won’t forget.
  • Seafood Platter: Paynesville is known for its fresh seafood, and a seafood platter is the perfect way to taste it all. It usually includes prawns, oysters, scallops, and more.
  • Gippsland Cheese: Made from fresh milk from local cows, Gippsland cheese is a must-try for cheese enthusiasts. With a wide variety of flavors, you’re sure to find one you’ll love.

Local Beverages

  • Gippsland Wine: With over 100 vineyards, Gippsland is known for its excellent wine. A glass of red or white is the perfect way to relax after a day out in nature.
  • Boathouse Beer: Brewed locally, Boathouse beer is a favorite among locals. With a rich, malty flavor, it’s the perfect beer to enjoy on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Paynesville (Victoria): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Paynesville offers a wide variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. From luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses, there’s something for everyone. Here are some top choices:

  • The Esplanade Resort & Spa: Located on the waterfront, this luxurious resort offers stunning views of the Gippsland Lakes and is known for its spa services.
  • Captains Cove Waterfront Apartments: These well-equipped apartments offer spectacular views of the marina and are a favorite among families.
  • The Gippsland Lakehouse: A charming guesthouse with cozy rooms and a homey atmosphere, the Gippsland Lakehouse is perfect for those on a budget.

What to Buy in Paynesville (Victoria): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a souvenir to remind you of your trip to Paynesville, here are some things to consider buying:

  • Gippsland Cheese: Made from fresh milk from local cows, Gippsland cheese is a must-try for cheese enthusiasts. Bring home a block or two to share with friends and family.
  • Boathouse Beer: Brewed locally, Boathouse beer is a favorite among locals. Pick up a six-pack to enjoy at home.
  • Handmade Crafts: Paynesville is home to several local artisans who create beautiful handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville is a friendly and welcoming town where locals take pride in their heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture and customs, which include a love of the outdoors and an appreciation for fresh, locally produced food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months of December to February when the weather is warmest, and the Gippsland Lakes are ideal for swimming and boating. Autumn and spring are also good times to visit, with cooler temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paynesville (Victoria)

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the Gippsland Lakes and surrounding areas.
  • Try the fresh, locally produced food and drink.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville is a safe destination for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions when out in nature. Be aware of any potential dangers, such as strong currents in the Gippsland Lakes or bushfires during the summer months.

Health in Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville has a modern medical center and several pharmacies, but it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication with you when traveling. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Paynesville (Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

Paynesville is easily accessible from major cities like Melbourne and Bairnsdale.

Airports Near Paynesville (Victoria)

  • Melbourne Airport: Located 270 kilometers west of Paynesville, Melbourne Airport is a major international airport with connections to cities around the world.
  • Bairnsdale Airport: Located just 11 kilometers from Paynesville, Bairnsdale Airport offers limited flights to Melbourne and regional destinations.

Bus Stations Near Paynesville (Victoria)

  • Bairnsdale Bus Station: Located 10 kilometers from Paynesville, this bus station offers connections to Melbourne and regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option for getting around Paynesville and exploring the surrounding areas.
  • Taxi Service: Several taxi services are available in Paynesville and Bairnsdale, making it easy to get around town.

How to Get Around in Paynesville (Victoria)

Paynesville is a small town that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Paynesville (Victoria): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Paynesville is a perfect base for exploring other destinations in East Gippsland and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Lakes Entrance: A picturesque town located 31 kilometers east of Paynesville, Lakes Entrance is known for its beaches, fishing opportunities, and water sports.
  • Buchan Caves: Located 100 kilometers north of Paynesville, Buchan Caves is home to a spectacular network of underground caves that are over 400 million years old.
  • Wilsons Promontory National Park: Located 230 kilometers southwest of Paynesville, Wilsons Promontory National Park is one of Victoria’s most popular national parks and offers a wide range of hiking and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant food and drink scene, Paynesville is a destination that is sure to delight visitors of all ages.
