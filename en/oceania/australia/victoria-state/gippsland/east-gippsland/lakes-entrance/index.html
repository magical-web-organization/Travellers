If you’re looking for a peaceful retreat to unwind and recharge, look no further than Lakes Entrance. Located in East Gippsland, in Victoria, Australia, this picturesque town is surrounded by pristine lakes, rivers, and beaches that offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know before planning your trip to Lakes Entrance, from top attractions to hiking trails, food, where to stay, travel tips, and more.

Introduction to Lakes Entrance

Lakes Entrance is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in nature. The town’s unique location, where the Gippsland Lakes meet the ocean, offers a wide variety of outdoor activities and water sports, including fishing, boating, kayaking, and surfing. In addition to its natural beauty, Lakes Entrance also has a rich cultural heritage, reflected in its museums, historic sites, and annual festivals and events.

List of Places to Visit in Lakes Entrance

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • ** Griffiths’ Sea Shell Museum & Marine Display:** This museum showcases a vast collection of sea shells and marine life from all over the world, including specimens from Antarctica and the Great Barrier Reef.
  • ** Cunningham Foot Bridge:** Built in 1939, this iconic bridge is the longest and unique footbridge across Australia’s longest lake system, linking towns of Lakes Entrance and Eastern Beach.
  • ** Nyerimilang Heritage Park:** This historic site offers sprawling views over the Gippsland Lakes and features a historic homestead, gardens, and walking trails. An ideal place for picnicking, birdwatching and organising corporate/group events.

Natural Attractions of Lakes Entrance: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • ** Lakes Entrance Beach:** This family-friendly beach is perfect for swimming and lounging in the sun.
  • ** Lakes Entrance Lookout:** This lookout offers breathtaking views of the town, lakes, and ocean.
  • ** Buchan Caves:** A must-see natural attraction just a short drive from Lakes Entrance. This natural wonder is formed from limestone that is over 300 million years old. Guided tours of the caves are available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakes Entrance

  • Lakes Entrance to Lake Tyers Beach: An eight-kilometer scenic trail that offers stunning views of the lakes and beaches along the way. Best time to visit is during sunrise or sunset, or early spring and autumn.
  • ** Colquhoun State Forest MTB Trails**: This is a fun and adventurous mountain bike trail for enthusiasts - it has a mix of intermediate level trails through the forest with natural terrains, jumps and technical features

Best Things to Do in Lakes Entrance: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Dolphin and Whale Watching Cruise: The Gippsland Lakes are home to many species of marine life, including dolphins, whales, and seals. Companies provide organised cruises that are not only a perfect way to experience nature but also informative sessions from experienced tour guides.
  • Fishing charters: Fishing is a favourite local pastime, with various species of fish including luderick, flathead, bream and whiting available around the Lakes and ocean. Fishing charters provide a great opportunity to catch your favourite species under expert guidance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakes Entrance

Day 1: Spend a day exploring the town’s rich cultural heritage, with visits to the Griffiths’ Sea Shell Museum & Marine Display and the Cunningham Foot Bridge.

Day 2: Spend the day soaking up the sun and surf at Lakes Entrance Beach, and take a stroll along the Lakes Entrance Lookout for some stunning views.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the nearby Buchan Caves and explore the stunning limestone formations.

Day 4: Rent a bike or take a hike along the Lakes Entrance to Lake Tyers Beach Trail, and enjoy the scenic views of the lakes and beaches.

Day 5: Get your adrenaline pumping with an action-packed day of surfing, kayaking, or fishing charters on the Gippsland Lakes.

Day 6: Relax and unwind with a day at Nyerimilang Heritage Park. Take a stroll through the historic homestead and gardens, and enjoy a picnic on the lawn.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a sunset cruise on the Gippsland Lakes, and take one last look at the town’s natural beauty from the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakes Entrance

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lakes Entrance fish and chips: Freshly caught fish, battered or crumbed, with chips and lemon, served with a side of homemade tartare sauce, is a classic and must-have dish from most of the local eateries.
  • Prawn and seafood platters: Lakes Entrance is renowned for its deliciously fresh seafood, with mouth-watering platters available at the renowned Lakes Entrance Fisherman’s Co-op.

Local Beverages

  • Wine tasting at Wyanga Park Winery: Take a wine tour and taste delicious local wines, made using grapes grown in the Gippsland region.
  • Local craft beer at The Waterwheel Beach Tavern: A beachfront tavern located in the East Gippsland lakes district, known for serving fresh beer made in-house.

Where to Stay in Lakes Entrance: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Comfort Inn & Suites Emmanuel: Found on the Esplanade, this hotel offers views overlooking Lakes Entrance, and offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool and barbeques, open for all-day meals.
  • BIG4 Whiters Holiday Village: Situated in a picturesque location, big4 holiday village offers a wide variety of accommodation options including cabins, caravans, and camping spots, nestled in nature but still only minutes away from the town centre.

What to Buy in Lakes Entrance: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local seafood: The local co-op sells fresh seafood sourced from the Gippsland Lakes, which you can buy and take home with you.
  • Indigenous art: The East Gippsland Aboriginal Arts Corporation produces and sells indigenous arts and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakes Entrance

Lakes Entrance has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong indigenous presence in the area. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the customs and traditions of the local aboriginal people through various avenues, including the Krowathunkoolong Keeping Place.

Best Time to Visit Lakes Entrance

The best time to visit Lakes Entrance is between February and April and between September and November when tourists are less likely to flock to this popular holiday destination. The weather is warm, and the crowds are more manageable during these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakes Entrance

Do:

  • Respect the environment: Lakes Entrance is a beautiful, natural area, and visitors are expected to take care of it. Be sure to dispose of litter properly and observe any environmental guidelines.
  • Learn about the local indigenous culture: Visit the Krowathunkoolong Keeping Place to learn about the historical and cultural importance of the land to the local Aboriginal people.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash: Be sure to dispose of all waste correctly and do not throw litter in public places or waterways.
  • Feed the wildlife: It might be tempting to feed the local wildlife, but feeding animals can negatively impact their natural behaviour and disrupt the ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakes Entrance

Lakes Entrance is a generally safe place to visit, but like anywhere, visitors should be mindful of their surroundings, belongings and adhere to any safety policies posted. Swim between the designated flags to ensure your safety at the beach and stay aware when driving on unfamiliar roads, especially if there is wildlife around.

Health in Lakes Entrance

It is safe to drink tap water in Lakes Entrance, and the area has adequate medical facilities. However, it is crucial to pack sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from harsh Australian weather.

Getting to Lakes Entrance: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakes Entrance

The nearest airport to Lakes Entrance is the East Sale Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive away. Alternatively, visitors can also fly into Melbourne airport and hire a car or catch a public bus.

Bus Stations Near Lakes Entrance

Visitors arriving by bus can make use of the daily services provided by V-Line, which connects Lakes Entrance to Melbourne, Bairnsdale, and other destinations in the surrounding area.

Other Options

Driving to Lakes Entrance is an option, and the drive is pleasant and scenic, especially from Melbourne and Sydney. You can hire a car from Melbourne, Sydney or Bairnsdale for self-driving experience.

How to Get Around in Lakes Entrance

It is easy to get around in Lakes Entrance, with car and bike hire services, local taxis and public transport (bus) available for visitors. Alternatively, you can choose to explore the town on foot as it is easy to navigate and offers stunning views.

Where to Go Next from Lakes Entrance: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Metung: A small town on the shores of Bancroft Bay, an easy half-hour drive from Lakes Entrance. It’s known for outdoor cafes, restaurants, and people enjoying its calm beach.
  • Orbost: An hour’s drive from Lakes Entrance, Orbost is a historic town, known for its Old Butter Factory and a gateway to Snowy River National Park.

Conclusion

Lakes Entrance is an ideal destination for nature and outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, and history buffs alike. Its unique location, rich cultural heritage, and natural beauty make it a must-visit destination. Pack your bags, plan your trip and get ready for an unforgettable holiday experience.
