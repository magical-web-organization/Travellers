Introduction to Belgrave

Belgrave is a quaint town located in Yarra Valley, Victoria, Australia. This charming town is known for its iconic steam train, Puffing Billy, which draws tourists to the region. But there is much more to this town than just a historic train. Belgrave offers a mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and great food. Whether you want to explore the town’s history or take long hikes with scenic views, Belgrave has something to offer to every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Belgrave

Here is a list of top places to visit in Belgrave:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Puffing Billy Railway: Take a ride on the historic steam train that runs on a 24-kilometer track, weaving through the lush forests and fern gullies of the Dandenong Ranges.

  • The Paper Trail: Take a guided tour of the Australian Paper Mills Company, M3 Stationery and Art Supplies, and discover the history of papermaking in Australia.

  • Grant’s Picnic Ground: A popular picnic spot in Belgrave, the Grant’s Picnic Ground is known for its colorful parrots that flock to the area. You can also hand-feed the birds and explore the walking trails.

Natural Attractions of Belgrave: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dandenong Ranges National Park: Take a hike to explore the stunning beauty of the Dandenong Ranges National Park. The park offers a variety of trails, running through fern-filled gullies, meandering streams, and majestic forests.

  • Olinda Falls: Just a short drive from Belgrave, Olinda Falls is a great spot to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature. The falls are surrounded by lush greenery and beautiful walking trails.

  • William Ricketts Sanctuary: This unique sanctuary is an open-air museum featuring sculptures by William Ricketts in a natural setting. The sculptures depict the cultural heritage of Aboriginal people and their connection to nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belgrave

Belgrave offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes. Some of the popular routes include:

  • 1000 Steps: A challenging hike with steep steps, the 1000 Steps is a popular trail that offers beautiful views of the Dandenong Ranges.

  • Bird Observation Walk: A 1.5 km long walk that takes you through the Sherbrooke Forest, where you can spot a variety of bird species.

  • Trees Adventure: This adventure park is located in Belgrave and offers a range of activities including tree-climbing, zip-lining, and rope courses.

Best Things to Do in Belgrave: Activities and Tours

  • Ride the Puffing Billy: A ride on the historic steam train, Puffing Billy, is a must-do activity in Belgrave.

  • The Paper Trail Tour: Take a guided tour of the Australian Paper Mills Company and learn about the history of papermaking in Australia.

  • Visit the Dandenong Ranges National Park: A beautiful park with walking trails, waterfalls, and breathtaking views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belgrave

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Belgrave:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Belgrave and check into your hotel. Explore the town and its restaurants.
Day 2 Take a ride on the Puffing Billy Railway. Enjoy lunch at the station and explore the historic paper mills.
Day 3 Take a hike in the Dandenong Ranges National Park and enjoy the stunning views.
Day 4 Visit the William Ricketts Sanctuary and learn about the Aboriginal culture.
Day 5 Spend the day exploring the local food and drink scene in Belgrave.
Day 6 Go on a tree-climbing adventure at the Trees Adventure park.
Day 7 Depart from Belgrave after breakfast.

Food and Drink Scene of Belgrave

Belgrave offers a range of restaurants and cafes serving delicious food and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A classic dish served with a side of tartar sauce and lemon wedges.

  • Meat Pie: A savory Australian pie filled with meat and gravy, served with tomato sauce.

  • Lamington: A sponge cake coated in chocolate sauce and coconut flakes, a popular dessert in Australia.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Yarra Valley is known for its wineries, and you must try some of the locally produced wines.

  • Craft Beer: Belgrave has a thriving craft beer scene, and you can find many local breweries and pubs serving delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Belgrave: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belgrave offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Belgrave Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B located in the heart of Belgrave, offering cozy rooms and home-cooked breakfast.

  • Emerald Ridge: A luxurious retreat located in the Dandenong Ranges, offering panoramic views and spa treatments.

  • Belgrave Backpackers: A budget-friendly option, this hostel offers clean rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Belgrave: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belgrave has many unique shops selling souvenirs, handmade crafts, and locally produced products. Here are some recommended places to shop:

  • The Belgrave Book Barn: A charming bookstore selling a wide range of books and collectibles.

  • Emotion Gallery: A gallery selling unique artisanal items, including ceramics, jewelry, and woodcrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Belgrave

Belgrave has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its museums and cultural attractions. The Aboriginal culture and history are an integral part of the town’s identity, and you can see this in the artworks and sculptures at William Ricketts Sanctuary. Belgrave also has a thriving arts scene, with many galleries and exhibition spaces showcasing the works of local artists.

Best Time to Visit Belgrave

The best time to visit Belgrave is from spring to autumn (September to May). The weather is pleasant during this time, and you can enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views. However, if you want to experience the town’s winter charm, you can visit during the winter months (June to August).

  • Explore the local food and drink scene.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belgrave

Belgrave is a safe town, but you should take care of your belongings and avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Belgrave

Belgrave has good medical facilities, and you can easily find a doctor or a hospital in case of an emergency. Make sure to carry your medical insurance documents with you.

Getting to Belgrave: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belgrave

  • Melbourne Airport (MEL) is the closest airport to Belgrave, located 54 km away. You can take a taxi, shuttle bus, or hire a car to reach Belgrave from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Belgrave

  • Belgrave Station is the main bus station in the town, with regular services to Melbourne and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • You can hire a car and drive to Belgrave from Melbourne or other nearby towns.

  • You can take a ride on the historic steam train, Puffing Billy, to reach Belgrave from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Belgrave

Belgrave is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot. However, if you want to visit nearby areas or attractions, you can hire a car, take a taxi, or use public transport.

Where to Go Next from Belgrave: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belgrave is located in Yarra Valley, which offers many nearby destinations to explore, including:

  • Healesville: A charming town located in the heart of the Yarra Valley, known for its wineries and wildlife sanctuary.

  • Olinda: A picturesque village located in the Dandenong Ranges, known for its historic gardens and galleries.

  • Warburton: A tranquil town located on the banks of the Yarra River, known for its outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Conclusion

Belgrave is a charming town located in the Yarra Valley, offering a mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and great food. Whether you want to explore the town’s history or take long hikes with scenic views, Belgrave has something to offer to every traveler.
