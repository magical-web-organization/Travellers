Introduction to Dandenong Ranges

Dandenong Ranges is a mountain range situated in the Eastern Region of Victoria, Australia. It is a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful landscapes, natural attractions, outdoor activities, and food and drink scene.

The region is home to several small towns and villages, each offering unique attractions and experiences to visitors. The Dandenong Ranges is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the city and immerse themselves in nature.

List of Places to Visit in Dandenong Ranges

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Puffing Billy Railway

Puffing Billy Railway is a steam train that runs on a historic narrow-gauge railway from Belgrave to Gembrook. The ride offers stunning views of the natural beauty of Dandenong Ranges and passes through beautiful forests and lush greenery. It is a must-do activity for anyone visiting the area.

2. William Ricketts Sanctuary

The William Ricketts Sanctuary is a magical place that features hand-carved sculptures by the late artist William Ricketts. It is a unique place that showcases the artist’s devotion to Aboriginal culture and spirituality.

3. Tesselaar KaBloom Festival

The Tesselaar KaBloom Festival is an annual event that takes place in Spring showcasing magnificent displays of blooms which marks the beginning of the Spring season. The festival features vibrant flower displays, gardening workshops, and live music performances.

Natural Attractions of Dandenong Ranges: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Sherbrooke Forest

Sherbrooke Forest is a popular spot for bushwalking and hiking. It features several trails that lead visitors through old growth forests, fern gullies, and scenic waterfalls. The forest is home to many native animals and birds, including the elusive lyrebird.

2. Olinda Falls

Olinda Falls is a picturesque waterfall located in the Dandenong Ranges National Park. Visitors can take a short hike and explore the area, which features lush greenery, ferns, and beautiful fauna.

3. SkyHigh Mount Dandenong

SkyHigh Mount Dandenong is a lookout point that offers breathtaking views of Melbourne, the Yarra Valley, and Port Phillip Bay. Visitors can enjoy a meal at the on-site restaurant and take in the stunning views of the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dandenong Ranges

1. Kokoda Memorial Walk

The Kokoda Memorial Walk is a challenging 1000-step climb up a steep hill in the Dandenong Ranges. The walk is dedicated to the Australian soldiers who fought in Papua New Guinea during World War II. The climb is a favourite amongst fitness enthusiasts and avid hikers.

2. The Dandenong Ranges Tourist Track

The Dandenong Ranges Tourist Track offers visitors the opportunity to explore the most scenic sites in the region. The track incorporates the popular attractions and trails mentioned above and is recommended for those who want to experience the best of what the Dandenong Ranges has to offer.

Best Things to Do in Dandenong Ranges: Activities and Tours

1. Hot Air Balloon Ride

A hot air balloon ride over the Dandenong Ranges and Yarra Valley is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that should not be missed. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of the region’s natural beauty from a unique vantage point.

2. Cherry Picking

The cherry picking season in Dandenong Ranges runs from November to January, and visitors can enjoy the experience of picking cherries straight from the trees.

3. Wine Tasting

The cool climate of the Dandenong Ranges provides ideal conditions for growing wine grapes, making it an excellent destination for wine lovers. Visitors can indulge in wine tasting tours of the surrounding vineyards and cellar doors.

Food and Drink Scene of Dandenong Ranges

Must-Try Dishes

1. Beef and Guinness Pie

Beef and Guinness Pie is a classic Australian dish, and several restaurants within the Dandenong Ranges offer their take on the recipe. It is the perfect comfort food for a cool night in the mountains.

2. Scones with Jam and Cream

Scones with Jam and Cream are a traditional British snack and are a favourite amongst visitors to the Dandenong Ranges. Many cafes and restaurants in the area offer their take on the classic recipe.

Local Beverages

1. Yarra Valley Wines

The Yarra Valley is renowned for producing some of the best wines in Australia, and visitors can indulge in wine tasting tours of the surrounding vineyards and cellar doors.

2. Melbourne Coffee

Melbourne is known for its excellent coffee scene, and several cafes in the Dandenong Ranges region offer great coffee and atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Dandenong Ranges: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Cloudhill

Cloudhill is a luxury guesthouse located in Olinda and features stunning gardens, a restaurant, and luxurious accommodations perfect for a romantic getaway or a relaxing escape.

2. Marybrooke Manor

Marybrooke Manor is a historic Tudor-style hotel located in Sherbrooke. It features an on-site restaurant, bar, and gardens and is perfect for those looking for a cosy and charming place to stay.

3. Olinda Guest House

Olinda Guest House is a boutique guesthouse located in the heart of Olinda. It features elegant rooms with fireplaces and a charming on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Dandenong Ranges: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Handmade Crafts

The Dandenong Ranges is home to a thriving community of artists and craftspeople who create unique handmade items that are perfect souvenirs for visitors.

2. Local Honey

The Dandenong Ranges is known for its delicious local honey, which can be purchased at several farmers’ markets and roadside stalls in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Dandenong Ranges

The Dandenong Ranges is home to a diverse community, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and cultures. The region has a rich history and is known for its devotion to preserving native flora and fauna and addressing environmental concerns.

Best Time to Visit Dandenong Ranges

The best time to visit Dandenong Ranges is between September to November and March to May. These months offer the best weather conditions to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dandenong Ranges

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Be mindful of other visitors when exploring trails and attractions.
  • Support local businesses by patronising them.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural features and wildlife.
  • Feed the animals or disturb their habitats.
  • Stray off marked trails and designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dandenong Ranges

Dandenong Ranges is a relatively safe destination for travellers. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring outdoor attractions such as hiking trails and natural features. It is essential to follow instructions and safety advice provided by local authorities and ensure that you are appropriately equipped for outdoor activities.

Health in Dandenong Ranges

The Dandenong Ranges is not known for any particular health hazards. Visitors should ensure that they take adequate precautions to protect themselves from the sun and stay hydrated while exploring the region.

Getting to Dandenong Ranges: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dandenong Ranges

The nearest airport to Dandenong Ranges is Melbourne Tullamarine Airport, which is approximately 63 km away. From the airport, visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi to reach the Dandenong Ranges.

Bus Stations Near Dandenong Ranges

There are several public transport services available in the region, including buses and trains. Visitors can catch the Belgrave Line train from Melbourne City, which stops at several stations in the Dandenong Ranges region.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to explore the Dandenong Ranges. Visitors can rent a car from Melbourne to reach the region, and several car rental companies operate within the area.

How to Get Around in Dandenong Ranges

Getting around the Dandenong Ranges is relatively easy, and visitors can use public transport or rent a car. Several tour operators offer organised tours of the region, which is an excellent way to explore the area without worrying about navigating yourself.

Where to Go Next from Dandenong Ranges: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yarra Valley

The Yarra Valley is a must-visit destination located only an hour away from the Dandenong Ranges. It is famous for its wineries, breweries, and artisanal food producers.

Melbourne City

Melbourne is a bustling city that is only a short distance away from the Dandenong Ranges. Visitors can explore the city’s iconic landmarks, world-class restaurants, and shopping districts.

Conclusion

Dandenong Ranges is a beautiful and diverse destination that offers visitors the opportunity to experience the best of Australian scenery, culture, and lifestyle.
