Introduction to Kinglake (Victoria)

Kinglake is a small town located in the heart of Yarra Valley, in Victoria, Australia. Known for its picturesque landscapes, natural beauty and outdoor activities, Kinglake is a popular destination for those looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Kinglake (Victoria)

From natural attractions to culturally significant landmarks, Kinglake has plenty to offer for tourists. Here are some places you should visit in Kinglake:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kinglake National Park: The park is famous for its scenic beauty, featuring a wide variety of flora and fauna and diverse landscapes. This attraction is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, including hikers, cyclists, and bird watchers.

Natural Attractions of Kinglake (Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Sugarloaf: It is a popular spot for cycling and hiking, offering panoramic views of the region from the top. Mount Sugarloaf is also home to many picnic areas that are perfect for a family day out.
  • Masons Falls Circuit Walk: The 6.4km hiking trail in the Kinglake National Park is one of the most popular activities among tourists. Hike to the Masons Falls and be rewarded by its beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kinglake (Victoria)

Hiking and biking are among the most common activities in Kinglake, with numerous trails for all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes in the area include:

  • The Masons Falls Circuit Walk: 6.4km long hiking trail is one of the most popular activities among tourists. Hike to the Masons Falls and be rewarded by its beauty.
  • Kinglake Forest Adventures: Spanning over 4 hectares, and over 5 different trails, Kinglake Forest Adventures caters to all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Kinglake (Victoria): Activities and Tours

  • Kinglake Pub: A classic country pub, which has recently been modernised. This pub sits under the foothills of the Great Dividing Rides, giving a great country pub feel.
  • Kinglake Tours: Get a personal guide to explore the town. Find out so many great places that only locals would know about!

Week-Long Itinerary for Kinglake (Victoria)

Kinglake has much to offer to keep visitors entertained for a week-long trip. Here’s what your seven-day itinerary could look like:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive, settle in and explore the town
Day 2 Discover Kinglake National Park and its many attractions
Day 3 Hike the Masons Falls Circuit Walk or other hiking trails
Day 4 Visit historical landmarks and museum
Day 5 Take a day trip to nearby towns and vineyards
Day 6 Adventure in Kinglake Forest Adventures
Day 7 Take a guided tour with Kinglake Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Kinglake (Victoria)

With its rural location, the food scene in Kinglake is highly influenced by traditional Australian cuisine. However, Kinglake also features many innovative restaurants, cafes and bars. Here are some food and drink options to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aussie Meat Pie: A traditional snack that is typically packed with beef or pork, vegetables and gravy, and wrapped in a flaky pastry.
  • Pub Classics: The Kinglake Pub serves a great range of pub classics including hamburgers, steak sandwiches and chicken parma.

Where to Stay in Kinglake (Victoria): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Kinglake for every type of traveler including budget-friendly hostels, comfortable guesthouses and luxurious hotels. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Kinglake Forest Adventures: Cabins and lodges in Kinglake Forest, providing a rustic glamping experience.
  • The George at Kinglake: A boutique hotel, offering stylish and comfortable rooms that are perfect for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Kinglake (Victoria): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kinglake has various gift shops popular for souvenirs, local produce and other products. Here are some things you can buy in Kinglake:

  • Kinglake Honey: One of the most popular products to take home from Kinglake. You can find the honey in local shops and gift stores.
  • Truffles: The Yarra Valley is home to numerous vineyards and truffle farms producing the world’s finest black truffles, one of Victoria’s top exports.

Local Culture and Customs of Kinglake (Victoria)

Kinglake is known for its traditional Australian culture and hospitality. Many festivals and events are held throughout the year, such as the Kinglake Homemade and Handmade Market that is held every second Sunday of the month.

Best Time to Visit Kinglake (Victoria)

The best time to visit Kinglake is from December to February, during the summer season, as the weather is pleasant and warm for outdoor activities. The fall season (March-May) is also an excellent time, when the trees are changing colours providing a stunning view.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kinglake (Victoria)

When visiting Kinglake, it is important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife by keeping litter and noise to a minimum.
  • Follow the posted rules and regulations while visiting the Kinglake National Park.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the local flora and fauna.
  • Tresspass in a private property, as most of the land here is private property.

Safety and Health

Kinglake is a safe town and visitors should not encounter any significant safety issues. However, it is important to take precautions just like in any other new location.

Safety in Kinglake (Victoria)

  • Be mindful of the wildlife in the area, such as snakes and spiders, especially during summer.
  • Follow the fire regulations posted in the area during bushfire season.

Health in Kinglake (Victoria)

  • Wear protective clothing like hats and sunscreen while going out for outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated during summer when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Kinglake (Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

Kinglake is located approximately 70kms north of Melbourne and can be easily reached by car, bus or train. The most common transportation options include:

Airports Near Kinglake (Victoria)

  • Melbourne Airport (Tullamarine Airport) is the most convenient airport to reach Kinglake, located about 64km away.

Bus Stations Near Kinglake (Victoria)

  • There are regular buses from nearby towns to Kinglake, with the closest major bus station being in Whittlesea (25km away).
  • Greyhound Buses: Buses from Melbourne to Kinglake are frequent with Greyhound Buses.

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are available for hire from Melbourne to Kinglake.

How to Get Around in Kinglake (Victoria)

Kinglake is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. There are no public transport options, so if you want to explore the nearby towns, it is recommended to rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Kinglake (Victoria): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kinglake is located in the heart of the Yarra Valley and is close to many famous tourist destinations. Here are some nearby excursions:

  • Yarra Valley: Home to many wineries and distilleries, this valley provides an opportunity to taste some of the best wine and craft spirits of Australia.
  • Healesville: Known for its wildlife sanctuary, national park, and boutique wineries. This town is just 30-minutes drive from Kinglake, which is great for a day trip.

Conclusion

Kinglake is a fantastic destination for anyone that wants to explore the natural beauty of Victoria and get a taste of traditional Australian culture. The town offers hiking trails, bike rides, picturesque landmarks and exceptional local cuisine. So, what are you waiting for? Plan your trip now and get ready for an adventure-packed trip.
