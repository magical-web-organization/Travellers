Introduction to Colac

Welcome to Colac, a picturesque town located in South West Coast, Victoria, Australia. With a rich culture, heritage, and a thriving food and wine scene, Colac is a must-visit destination for every traveler. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, sightseeing, or a relaxing vacation, this town has plenty to offer. In this guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Colac.

List of Places to Visit in Colac

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Colac Otway Performing Arts Centre: This arts center has an impressive lineup of theater, dance, and music performances throughout the year.
  • Red Rock Regional Theatre and Gallery: This community-run arts center features exhibitions, films, and events showcasing local and regional artists.
  • Colac and District Historical Society Museum: Explore the history of the region through a range of artifacts and local exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Colac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Colac: A freshwater lake that offers plenty of water-based activities like fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.
  • Mount Elephant: Hike to the top of this volcanic plug and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Beauchamp Falls: A scenic waterfall located in the Great Otway National Park. The falls are accessible via a short hike through a rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colac

Discover Colac’s scenic wonders by hiking or biking the region’s beautiful trails. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Lake Colac Circuit: A moderate 10km circuit that takes you around the lake’s edge.
  • Old Beechy Rail Trail: A 45km trail through the Otway Ranges that passes through forests, farmland, and small towns.
  • Cape Otway Lightstation Walk: A challenging 14km coastal hike that takes you to the Great Ocean Road’s southernmost point.

Best Things to Do in Colac: Activities and Tours

  • Colac Farmers Market: Held on the first Saturday of every month, this market features fresh produce, artisanal foods, and handicrafts.
  • Gellibrand River Blues and Blueberry Festival: Enjoy local blues music and taste delicious blueberry treats at this annual summer festival.
  • Otway Harvest Trail: Take a foodie tour of the Otway Ranges, which features a farm-to-plate experience with a range of local producers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colac

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will allow you to explore Colac and its surroundings thoroughly:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Colac Botanic Gardens and the Colac and District Historical Society Museum
2 Hike the Old Beechy Rail Trail
3 Visit the Great Otway National Park and Beauchamp Falls
4 Explore the local food scene, including the Colac Farmers Market
5 Go on a wine tour in the nearby Otway Ranges
6 Visit Mount Elephant and take the Lake Corangamite Scenic Drive
7 Relax at Lake Colac and enjoy a picnic or water-based activities

Food and Drink Scene of Colac

Must-Try Dishes

  • Timboon Cheesery: Enjoy artisanal cheese varieties produced in the historic Timboon Railway Shed.
  • Birregurra Farm Foods: This farm-to-plate restaurant offers delectable dishes using local ingredients with a focus on sustainability.
  • Otway Artisan Bakery: Satisfy your sweet tooth with delectable pastries and artisan bread from this local bakery.

Local Beverages

  • Otway Estate Winery and Brewery: Sample a range of wines and beers in the estate’s picturesque vineyards and gardens.
  • Camperdown Dairy: Taste delicious ice cream made from milk sourced from local, grass-fed cows.

Where to Stay in Colac: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • The Colac Mid City Motor Inn: A mid-range hotel offering affordable accommodation with amenities like a pool, spa, and restaurant.
  • Otway Escapes Luxury Spa Cottage Accommodation: This self-contained luxury cottage offers a rejuvenating spa and breathtaking views of the Otway Ranges.
  • Colac Backpackers: This hostel is located right in the heart of the town and is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Colac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cakes and Biscuits from Bella’s: This local bakery produces some of the best sweet treats in town.
  • Birregurra Farm Foods Products: Don’t forget to pick up some artisanal food products from this farm-to-table restaurant to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Colac

The region is known for its rich Indigenous and European heritage, and it is essential to show respect for the local customs and traditions. The best way to do this is by learning about the local culture and interacting with the locals mindfully.

Best Time to Visit Colac

The best time to visit Colac is between December and February when the weather is warm and you can enjoy outdoor activities to the fullest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colac

Do:

  • Respect the local Indigenous culture and customs.
  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions of the region.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter the local trails and parks.
  • Disrespect the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colac

Colac is a relatively safe city with little to no violence or crime, but it is advisable to take standard precautions while traveling.

Health in Colac

Colac has adequate medical facilities, including hospitals and medical centers. However, medical costs can be high, and it is essential to carry adequate health insurance or travel insurance while visiting the region.

Getting to Colac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colac

The closest airports to Colac are:

  • Melbourne Airport, 150km away.
  • Avalon Airport, 100km away.

Bus Stations Near Colac

V/Line buses serve Colac, linking it to major cities in Victoria.

Other Options

You can also rent a car and drive or hail a taxi or ride-sharing service to get around the region.

How to Get Around in Colac

The best way to explore the region is by car or bike, and there are several car rental options available in the area. Alternatively, you can take public transport or hire a taxi or ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Colac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Great Ocean Road: Explore the stunning coastline of Victoria’s Great Ocean Road, which is a two-hour drive from Colac.
  • Lorne: This charming seaside town is nestled between the Otway Ranges and the coastline and is located a little over an hour’s drive from Colac.
  • Geelong: Enjoy seaside vibes and the Bellarine Peninsula’s wine region, located around an hour’s drive from Colac.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a culture buff, Colac offers an array of attractions for every traveler. From scenic trails to delicious local food and drink, Colac is worthy of a spot on your bucket list. Plan your next vacation to Colac and explore it all at your own pace.
