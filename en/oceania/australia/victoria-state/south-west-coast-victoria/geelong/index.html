Geelong Travel Guide: An Ultimate Tour Guide to Geelong

Geelong is a charming coastal city known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant food and drink scene. Located in South West Coast, in Victoria, Australia, Geelong is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a mix of adventure, culture, and relaxation. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best things to do and see in Geelong, from natural attractions to cultural landmarks and local food and drinks. We’ll also share tips on where to stay, how to get around, and nearby destinations to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Geelong

Geelong has a plethora of attractions that cater to different tastes and interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in Geelong that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Geelong Gallery - This art museum showcases a range of Australian and international art, featuring pieces from renowned artists such as Frederick McCubbin, Arthur Streeton, and Eugene von Guerard.
  • National Wool Museum - This museum offers a stimulating insight into the history of wool production in Australia and the country’s evolving fashion and textile industry.
  • The Old Geelong Gaol - Built-in 1849, this former prison shows visitors what life was like behind bars for inmates in the 19th and 20th centuries.
  • Queenscliffe Maritime Museum - This museum offers maritime-themed exhibits and interactive experiences that allow visitors to immerse themselves in the rich history of Australia’s seafaring past.

Natural Attractions of Geelong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Ocean Road - One of the most iconic attractions in Australia, Great Ocean Road is a scenic coastal drive that stretches over 240 km along the south-east coast of Australia. Visitors can stop along the way to explore charming towns, stunning beaches and rock formations, and lush rainforests.
  • You Yangs Regional Park - This park offers excellent hiking and mountain biking trails with stunning views of the surrounding valleys and the city of Melbourne.
  • Barwon River - This picturesque river winds its way through the heart of Geelong and offers visitors a range of water-based activities such as kayaking, rowing, and fishing.
  • Bellarine Peninsula - This peninsula is known for its pristine beaches, picturesque vineyards, and charming towns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Geelong

If you love outdoor adventure and physical activity, Geelong has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Great Ocean Walk - This 104 km walking track offers breathtaking vistas of rugged coastline, cliff faces, and stunning beaches.
  • Bellarine Rail Trail - This 32 km trail is perfect for cyclists and hikers, featuring stunning sea views and highlights such as Queenscliff, the historic Bellarine Railway, and the picturesque town of Drysdale.
  • Ted Wilson Trail - This 21 km trail offers beautiful views of the Barwon River and the surrounding wetlands, featuring a range of birdlife and natural flora.
  • Beacon Point Walk - This 3.5 km trail is perfect for hikers who are up for a challenge. The trail offers stunning views of the rugged coastline and some of the most unique rock formations on the planet.

Best Things to Do in Geelong: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling with family, friends, or solo, Geelong has plenty of activities and tours to suit your interests. Here are some of the best things to do and see in Geelong:

  • Adventure Park -This theme park is perfect for families and groups who love water fun. The park offers a range of water-based activities such as water slides, wave pools, and rapids.
  • Little Creatures Brewery - This brewery is a top-rated attraction in Geelong, offering craft beer tastings, brewery tours, and live music events.
  • Eastern Beach Reserve - This charming beach offers visitors a range of activities such as swimming, sunbathing, walking, and cycling. The beach is also home to an iconic swimming complex with a diving tower and an Art Deco pavilion.
  • Sea All Dolphin Swims - This tour company offers visitors the chance to swim with wild dolphins, seals, and other marine life in the stunning waters of Port Phillip Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Geelong

If you are planning to spend a week in Geelong, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your visit:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Geelong Gallery and explore the city center.
Day 2 Take a trip along the Great Ocean Road, stopping at iconic sites like the Twelve Apostles and Loch Ard Gorge.
Day 3 Hike the You Yangs Regional Park and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and the city of Melbourne.
Day 4 Visit the National Wool Museum and explore the historic highlights of the city center.
Day 5 Take a tour of the Little Creatures Brewery, followed by a stroll along Eastern Beach Reserve.
Day 6 Visit the Bellarine Peninsula and enjoy its pristine beaches, vineyards, and charming towns.
Day 7 Swim with wild dolphins with Sea All Dolphin Swims, followed by a sunset picnic at Barwon River.

Food and Drink Scene of Geelong

One of the highlights of visiting Geelong is its vibrant food and drink scene, boasting an assortment of restaurants, cafes, bars, and breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips - Geelong is famous for its seafood cuisine, and one of the most popular dishes is fish and chips, served fresh with a side of tartar sauce and a lemon wedge.
  • Schnitzel - This dish is a German classic that is beloved in Geelong, featuring a pounded and breaded meat cutlet, often served with mashed potatoes and gravy.
  • Meat Pies - A quintessential Australian snack, meat pies are a staple in Geelong, featuring a warm pastry filled with minced meat, veggies, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Geelong has a vibrant craft beer scene, with many of its breweries offering award-winning brews. Some of the best breweries in Geelong include the Little Creatures Brewery, Blackman’s Brewery, and the Geelong Brewery Company.
  • Wine - Geelong is also renown for its wine production, particularly its cool-climate wines, such as Chardonnay and Pinot Noir.

Where to Stay in Geelong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Geelong offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Geelong:

  • Rydges Geelong - This stylish hotel offers comfortable rooms with skyline views and is located in the heart of Geelong’s CBD, close to major attractions and shopping areas.
  • Vue Apartments Geelong - Located in the trendy Waterfront precinct, these self-contained apartments feature stunning bay views and are close to restaurants, bars, and the beach.
  • Nireeda Apartments on Clare - These affordable apartments offer spacious rooms with kitchenettes and are located in the heart of Geelong, close to cafes, shops, and historical landmarks.

What to Buy in Geelong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Geelong, here are some suggestions:

  • Wool Products - As the home of Australia’s wool industry, Geelong is an excellent place to purchase high-quality wool products, such as sweaters, blankets, and scarves.
  • Handmade Crafts - Geelong is home to many talented artists and artisans who produce unique and beautiful handmade crafts, such as pottery, ceramics, and glassware.
  • Local Wine - Geelong’s wineries produce some of the best cool-climate wines in Australia, and many offer cellar door sales, allowing visitors to taste and purchase their wines directly.

Local Culture and Customs of Geelong

Geelong is a diverse community with a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its food, art, and architecture. As with any destination, it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some tips to help you get acquainted with the local culture and customs of Geelong:

  • Australians are generally laid-back and friendly, so don’t be afraid to strike up conversations with locals.
  • Tipping is not expected in Australia, but it’s up to your discretion if you want to tip for exceptional service.
  • Australians drive on the left side of the road, so if you are renting a car, keep this in mind.

Best Time to Visit Geelong

Geelong has a temperate oceanic climate with mild winters and cooling sea breezes during summer. The best time to visit Geelong is during the spring and summer months (September to February), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, if you prefer a quieter time with fewer crowds, consider visiting during the autumn and winter months (March to August).

Do’s and Don’ts of Geelong

To make your visit to Geelong a memorable one, there are some things you should do and some things you should avoid. Here are some do’s and don’ts of Geelong:

Do:

  • Do try to learn some Australian slang, such as “g’day,” “mate,” and “no worries.”
  • Do respect the local culture and customs, such as taking off your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Do try the local cuisine, such as seafood and meat pies.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the strength of the sun in Australia. Always wear sunscreen and a hat and stay hydrated.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash on beaches or parks. Australians take environmental protection seriously.
  • Don’t forget to look both ways before crossing the street, as cars drive on the left-hand side of the road.

Safety and Health

When traveling to any destination, it’s crucial to take precautions to ensure your safety and health. Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Geelong:

Safety in Geelong

Geelong is generally a safe city, but it’s essential to take standard safety precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep your valuables, such as passports, money, and electronics, safe and secure.
  • Observe warning signs at beaches, trails, and parks, as some areas can be hazardous.

Health in Geelong

Australian medical facilities and infrastructure are well-developed and of high quality. However, it helps to take a few precautions to stay healthy:

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the warmer months.
  • Apply sunscreen regularly, even on cloudy days, to avoid sunburn and skin damage.
  • Get immunized before traveling to Australia to protect against common diseases.

Getting to Geelong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Geelong is easily accessible by air, sea, and land, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Geelong

The nearest major airport to Geelong is Melbourne Airport, located approximately 70 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a shuttle bus or hire a taxi or rental car to reach Geelong.

Bus Stations Near Geelong

Geelong has an extensive network of regional buses that connect the city with nearby towns and cities. Visitors can catch a bus from Geelong Station or the Geelong Bus Terminal, both located in central Geelong.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Geelong via train, with regular services operated by V/Line from Melbourne’s Southern Cross Station. Alternatively, visitors can hire a car and drive to Geelong via the Princes Highway.

How to Get Around in Geelong

Getting around Geelong is easy and convenient, with multiple transportation options available:

  • Walking - Geelong’s city center is relatively compact and easy to explore on foot, with many landmarks and attractions located within walking distance of each other.
  • Bike - Visitors can rent a bike from several locations throughout the city. Geelong has many dedicated bike lanes and trails that make cycling a safe and enjoyable way to explore.
  • Bus - Geelong has an extensive network of regional buses that connect the city with nearby towns and cities. Travelers can purchase single-use tickets or add value to a myki card, a reusable travel card that offers discounted fares.
  • Taxi and Ride Sharing Services - Visitors can hire a taxi or use ride-sharing services such as Uber and DiDi to travel around Geelong and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Geelong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Geelong is a perfect base for exploring the wider region and nearby destinations, such as:

  • Torquay - This scenic coastal town is located just a short drive from Geelong and is known for its world-class surf beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Queenscliff - This charming seaside village is located on the Bellarine Peninsula and is home to historic forts, quaint cafes, and stunning beaches.
  • Lorne - This picturesque town is located along the Great Ocean Road and is known for its beautiful beaches, lush rainforests, and unique rock formations.

Conclusion

Geelong is a gem of a city that is sure to surprise and delight visitors of all ages and interests. From its stunning natural landscapes to its rich cultural landmarks and vibrant food and drink scene, Geelong is a destination that truly has something for everyone. Whether you’re hiking along the Great Ocean Walk, swimming with wild dolphins, or sampling craft beer at Little Creatures Brewery, Geelong is a city that is sure to leave a lasting impression on you. So pack your bags, plan your trip, and get ready to explore this amazing destination!
