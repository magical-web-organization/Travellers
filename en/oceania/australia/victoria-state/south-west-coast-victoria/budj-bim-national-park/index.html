Budj Bim National Park: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Located in the South West Coast of Victoria, Australia, Budj Bim National Park is a vast protected area that is home to unique Indigenous cultural heritage sites, breathtaking scenic views, and adventurous outdoor activities. If you’re planning a trip to Victoria, make sure Budj Bim National Park is on your must-visit list.

Introduction to Budj Bim National Park

Budj Bim National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site that is named after the inactive volcano, Mount Budj Bim. The park is approximately 4.5 hours southwest of Melbourne and covers an area of 1,438 hectares. Established in November 2019, the park is jointly managed by the Gunditjmara Traditional Owners and the Victorian Government.

List of Places to Visit in Budj Bim National Park

There are many culturally important attractions and natural wonders to explore in Budj Bim National Park. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Budj Bim Tours: Learn about the Gunditjmara people and their deep connection to the land with an Aboriginal guide through signs of eel traps and stone houses.
  • Budj Bim Cultural Landscape: A UNESCO World Heritage site since July 2019, this site showcases traditional hunting practices, farming techniques and fire management strategies of the Gunditjmara people.

Natural Attractions of Budj Bim National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Eccles: Hike to the summit of this dormant volcano and admire the panoramic views from the top.
  • Lake Surprise: Take a peaceful walk around the picturesque Lake Surprise or go for a swim during the summer months.
  • Tumuli Trail: Explore the Tumuli Trail and discover an ancient stone burial site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Budj Bim National Park

Biking and hiking are two popular activities in Budj Bim National Park. Here are some of our favorite trails to explore:

  • Lake Surprise Circuit: This 2.5-kilometre hiking trail takes you around the Lake Surprise and offers fantastic views of the volcanic landscape.
  • Tumuli Trail Circuit: This hiking trail is approximately 3 km long and takes you through the forest to an ancient burial site.
  • Mount Eccles Summit Walk: This is a challenging hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Budj Bim National Park: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more things to do in Budj Bim National Park, consider these activities and tours:

  • Canoeing: Take a canoe tour and paddle through the park’s many waterways.
  • Wildlife and Nature Tours: Take a guided tour of the park to learn more about the flora and fauna of the area.
  • Cultural Tours: Take an Aboriginal-led tour and discover the deep spiritual connection the Gunditjmara people have with the land.

Week-Long Itinerary for Budj Bim National Park

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Budj Bim National Park, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and settle into your accommodation. Day 2: Begin your exploration of Budj Bim National Park with a hike in the Mount Eccles Summit Walk. Day 3: Visit the Budj Bim Cultural Landscape and take an Aboriginal-led tour. Day 4: Enjoy a day of canoeing and exploring the many waterways of the park. Day 5: Take a nature and wildlife tour and discover more about the flora and fauna of the area. Day 6: Hike the Tumuli Trail Circuit and enjoy a picnic lunch along the way. Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Budj Bim National Park

The food and drink scene of Budj Bim National Park is a mix of local and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Local seafood: The park is home to many lakes and rivers, making local seafood dishes a must-try.
  • Traditional damper: This is a bread made by the Aboriginal people, made from flour, water and fire.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wines: Try the local wines in the region, as wineries invite you to have a taste of their favourites.

Where to Stay in Budj Bim National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budj Bim National Park offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Lake Repose Cottage: This lovely cottage is located on a hilltop and offers fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Winda-Mara: This accommodation is a partnership between members of the local community and provides the Gunditjmara Cultural Heritage Centre

What to Buy in Budj Bim National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring back from Budj Bim National Park:

  • Hand-crafted pottery: Explore the shops in nearby towns like Portland, and pick out a piece of pottery with traditional designs.
  • Artwork: Look out for traditional paintings and artworks that showcase the culture of the local Gunditjmara community.

Local Culture and Customs of Budj Bim National Park

The Gunditjmara people have lived in the area for over 60,000 years and have a deep spiritual connection to the land. It’s important to be respectful of their customs and traditions when visiting the park. When visiting a sacred cultural site, it’s essential to seek permission from the park rangers before visiting.

Best Time to Visit Budj Bim National Park

The best time to visit Budj Bim National Park is from November to March when the weather is warm and sunny. However, keep in mind that during the summer months, the park can get busy with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Budj Bim National Park

Here are some things to do and to avoid doing while visiting Budj Bim National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Seek permission before visiting a sacred cultural site.

Don’t:

  • Take anything from the park or its protected areas.
  • Leave garbage or litter behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Budj Bim National Park

While Budj Bim National Park is generally safe, it’s important to take precautions when exploring the park. Here are some tips:

  • Watch out for snakes
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the conditions.
  • Avoid swimming in areas where signs warn you to.

Health in Budj Bim National Park

The park is located in a serene and healthy environment where you can remove yourself from the stresses of city living.

Getting to Budj Bim National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to reach Budj Bim National Park:

Airports Near Budj Bim National Park

  • Portland Airport, located 17 km away from the park, is the closest airport.

Bus Stations Near Budj Bim National Park

  • V/Line offers bus services to Portland, located approximately 20 minutes from the park.

Other Options

  • Driving: If you’re renting a car, follow the Henty Highway from Portland to Macarthur and then follow the signs to Budj Bim National Park.

How to Get Around in Budj Bim National Park

The best way to get around Budj Bim National Park is by car. Make sure to respect speed limits, park rules and regulations.

Where to Go Next from Budj Bim National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Budj Bim National Park:

  • Great Ocean Road: This iconic coastal drive offers stunning views of the ocean and nearby towns.
  • The Grampians National Park: This park is filled with scenic hiking trails and beautiful waterfalls.

Conclusion

Budj Bim National Park is a fascinating destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in Indigenous culture or outdoor adventures, there’s plenty to see and do. Make sure to add Budj Bim National Park to your Victoria itinerary and make unforgettable memories!
