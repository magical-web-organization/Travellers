Introduction to Apollo Bay

Located on the south-western coast of the picturesque state of Victoria, in Australia, Apollo Bay is a stunning coastal town set in the heart of the Great Otway National Park. Known for its pristine beaches, laid-back atmosphere, and spectacular natural landscapes, Apollo Bay attracts visitors from all around the world who come to relax, explore, and enjoy everything this charming town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Apollo Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apollo Bay Museum: With exhibits that chronicle the town’s rich maritime history and heritage, the Apollo Bay Museum is a must-visit destination for culture buffs and history lovers.

  • Cape Otway Lightstation: The oldest surviving lighthouse in Australia, the Cape Otway Lightstation is an iconic landmark that offers stunning views of the coast and the Great Ocean Road.

  • Great Ocean Road Heritage Centre: This museum showcases the history and heritage of the Great Ocean Road, from its construction to the stories of the people who built it.

Natural Attractions of Apollo Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Otway National Park: Home to a stunning array of flora and fauna, the Great Otway National Park is a nature lover’s paradise, with countless trails, waterfalls, and scenic vistas to explore.

  • Apollo Bay Beach: A long and stunningly beautiful stretch of sand, Apollo Bay Beach is a popular destination for swimming, surfing, and simply relaxing by the sea.

  • Maits Rest Rainforest Walk: A peaceful and immersive experience, the Maits Rest Rainforest Walk is a gentle stroll through ancient rainforest, with boardwalks and viewing platforms that offer a unique perspective on the flora and fauna of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apollo Bay

  • Great Ocean Walk: This stunning multi-day walking trail takes hikers through some of the most spectacular coastal scenery in Australia, with sections that start or end in Apollo Bay.

  • Old Beechy Rail Trail: A family-friendly biking and hiking trail that follows the route of a disused railway line through beautiful bushland and scenery.

  • Mounts Bay Track: This scenic hike offers breathtaking views of the coast and the surrounding mountains, with ample opportunities for picnics and relaxing breaks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Apollo Bay: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tours: From May to October, it’s possible to see whales playing off the coast of Apollo Bay, with several local companies offering boat tours that take visitors close to these magnificent creatures.

  • Surfing Lessons: With an inviting coastline and world-class breaks, Apollo Bay is a great place to learn to surf, with several schools and instructors catering to beginners and advanced surfers alike.

  • Helicopter Tours: For a truly unique perspective on the Great Ocean Road and the coastal landscapes surrounding Apollo Bay, consider taking a scenic helicopter tour, which offers amazing photo opportunities and memories that will last a lifetime.

Food and Drink Scene of Apollo Bay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Platters: With fresh local seafood being a specialty of the region, it’s no surprise that seafood platters are a must-try dish in Apollo Bay.

  • Otway Pork: A local specialty that showcases the quality of the pork produced in the Otway region, Otway Pork is a mouth-watering dish that is sure to satisfy.

  • Kangaroo Fillet: For a uniquely Australian dining experience, consider trying Kangaroo Fillet, a lean and healthy meat that is both delicious and sustainable.

Local Beverages

  • Great Ocean Road Gin: This boutique gin is made with local botanicals and flavors, and has won numerous awards for its unique taste and quality.

  • Otway Estate Wines: With a vineyard located just outside Apollo Bay, Otway Estate produces a range of award-winning wines that showcase the rich flavors and terroir of the region.

  • Forrest Brewery Beers: Based in the nearby town of Forrest, the Forrest Brewery produces a range of delicious, hand-crafted beers that are a favorite of visitors and locals alike.

Where to Stay in Apollo Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Captains at the Bay: A boutique hotel that offers luxurious accommodations and amenities, Captains at the Bay is a perfect choice for couples and travelers seeking upscale accommodations.

  • Apollo Bay Backpackers: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of town, Apollo Bay Backpackers offers comfortable dorm rooms and communal spaces, as well as friendly and knowledgeable staff.

  • Glenoe Cottages and Apartments: For a self-contained, private accommodation experience, consider Glenoe Cottages and Apartments, which offers charming and comfortable cottages and apartments nestled in beautiful gardens and landscapes.

What to Buy in Apollo Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Great Ocean Road Memorabilia: With a plethora of souvenir shops lining the main street of Apollo Bay, it’s easy to find a range of Great Ocean Road-themed merchandise, from t-shirts and hats to mugs and postcards.

  • Local Art and Crafts: With a thriving arts community in the region, Apollo Bay is a great place to buy local art and crafts, from paintings and sculptures to jewelry and pottery.

  • Otway Produce and Products: From locally made jams and preserves to honey and wine, there are plenty of delicious Otway produce and products to take home as souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Apollo Bay

With a laid-back and friendly community, Apollo Bay is a welcoming and inclusive town that celebrates its diverse heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, and to participate in local events and celebrations whenever possible.

Best Time to Visit Apollo Bay

The best time to visit Apollo Bay depends on your interests and preferences. For nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, spring (September-November) and autumn (March-May) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. Summer (December-February) is peak tourist season, with warm weather and plenty of events and activities. Winter (June-August) is the low season, with cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, but also offers the opportunity to see whales and other migratory wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apollo Bay

Apollo Bay is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions against theft and other crimes. It’s also important to be aware of water and beach safety, as there can be strong currents and rips.

Health in Apollo Bay

The nearest hospital to Apollo Bay is located in Colac, approximately an hour’s drive away. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance and take normal precautions against illness and injury.

Getting to Apollo Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apollo Bay

The nearest major airport to Apollo Bay is Melbourne Tullamarine Airport, which is approximately a three-hour drive away. There are also smaller regional airports in the area, including Avalon Airport and Warrnambool Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Apollo Bay

Several bus companies offer regular services to Apollo Bay from Melbourne and other regional destinations. The main bus station in Apollo Bay is located on Nelson Street, in the town center.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Apollo Bay, as it is located on the internationally renowned Great Ocean Road. Car rentals are available at major airports and in the city center of Melbourne.

How to Get Around in Apollo Bay

Walking and cycling are popular ways to get around Apollo Bay, with most attractions and amenities located within easy walking distance of the town center. There are also local taxis and shuttle services available, as well as car rentals for those who want to explore the surrounding region.

Where to Go Next from Apollo Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations and excursions from Apollo Bay include:

  • Port Fairy: A charming seaside town located to the west of Apollo Bay, Port Fairy is known for its historic buildings, excellent fishing, and beautiful beaches.

  • Warrnambool: A bustling city that serves as a gateway to the Great Ocean Road, Warrnambool is a great place to explore local history and culture, as well as to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and whale watching.

  • The Twelve Apostles: One of the most iconic natural attractions in Australia, the Twelve Apostles are a series of limestone stacks located just a short drive from Apollo Bay along the Great Ocean Road.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in hiking, surfing, whale-watching, or simply relaxing by the beach, Apollo Bay offers something for every type of traveler. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly and welcoming community, Apollo Bay is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Victoria, Australia.
