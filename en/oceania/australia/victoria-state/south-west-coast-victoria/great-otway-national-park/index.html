Australia is a beautiful country with endless opportunities for nature lovers to explore. Among the must-visit destinations in Victoria, Great Otway National Park stands out as a popular spot for adventure seekers and enthusiasts alike. Located in the South West Coast of Victoria, the park is famous for its diverse range of landscapes, from lush forests and cascading waterfalls to rugged cliffs and sandy beaches. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Great Otway National Park, covering everything from the best activities and accommodations to local customs and cultural attractions.

Introduction to Great Otway National Park

Established in 2004, Great Otway National Park is a vast protected area spanning across 103,000 hectares between Torquay and Princetown. The park is known for its stunning natural beauty and diverse range of flora and fauna, providing visitors with plenty of opportunities for exploration and adventure. Some of the notable experiences you can expect in the park include hiking and trekking through ancient rainforests to catch a glimpse of native animals, surfing in the wild Southern Ocean, and camping under the stars.

List of Places to Visit in Great Otway National Park

Great Otway National Park is dotted with several fascinating places that you can visit during your trip to the park. Here are some of the top attractions in the park, divided into two categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cape Otway Lightstation: A historical lighthouse dating back to 1848, Cape Otway Lightstation is the oldest operating lighthouse on mainland Australia. You can climb to the top of the lighthouse to enjoy sweeping views of the park and the ocean.

  2. Great Ocean Walk: A 104 km long walking trail along the park’s coast, this scenic walk provides a glimpse of the rugged coastline, Shipwreck Coast and the Twelve Apostles.

  3. Melba Gully State Park: A popular spot for nature photography, Melba Gully is known for its bioluminescent myrtle beech trees that light up in the dark, which is a must-see site at night.

Natural Attractions of Great Otway National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Erskine Falls: One of the most iconic waterfalls in the park, Erskine Falls plunges 30 meters into a lush valley of fern-filled gullies. A viewing platform at the base of the falls offers a perfect spot for taking breathtaking photos or cooling off on hot days.

  2. The Otways Fly Zipline Tour: A thrilling experience that takes you flying through the trees of the Otway Ranges with panoramic views of the rainforest.

  3. Apollo Bay: A charming coastal town located on the Great Ocean Road’s southern side, Apollo Bay is a popular destination for beach enthusiasts, with crystal clear water and golden sands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Otway National Park

Great Otway National Park has several scenic hiking and biking trails that provide stunning natural views and an opportunity to explore the park’s diverse landscapes. Some of the popular trails in the park include:

  1. Great Ocean Walk: One of the most popular scenic hiking trails in Australia, Great Ocean Walk is a 104 km journey that spans from Apollo Bay to the Twelve Apostles.

  2. Old Beechy Rail Trail: A 40-kilometer-long trail that winds through the park’s dense forest, featuring tall trees and tranquil streams.

  3. Triplet Falls Walk: A 2 km round-trip walk to a cascading waterfall complete with boardwalks, stairs and suspension bridges.

Best Things to Do in Great Otway National Park: Activities and Tours

Great Otway National Park is a treasure trove of activities that cater to different interests and skill levels. If you’re an adventure seeker, here are few things that you can do in the park:

  1. Surfing: Great Otway National Park has a few famous surf beaches that attract surfers from around the world, including Marengo Beach, Bells Beach, and Winkipop Beach.

  2. Camping: There are several campsites within the park for visitors to choose from, perfect for those looking to explore the park with their family or friends.

  3. Wildlife spotting: The park is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including kangaroos, koalas, echidnas, and different species of birds. You can explore the park’s many trails and boardwalks, which will take you through their natural habitats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Otway National Park

A week in Great Otway National Park gives you ample time to experience the park’s best attractions, explore its rugged coastline, and immerse yourself in the region’s culture. Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to the park:

Day 1: Explore Apollo Bay and its surroundings. Day 2: Go surfing on one of the park’s many beaches or enjoy a walk through the rainforest at Maits Rest. Day 3: Visit the lighthouse at Cape Otway and experience the charm of the Great Ocean Road. Day 4: Go bird watching and enjoy a wildlife tour at the Great Ocean Ecolodge. Day 5: Embark on the iconic Great Ocean Walk and experience the beauty of the park’s coastal scenery. Day 6: Take a zipline tour through the towering trees of the Otway Ranges. Day 7: Relax at one of the park’s many campsites and spend some quality time with family or friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Otway National Park

Great Otway National Park and the surrounding areas offer several excellent dining options for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to experience:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kangaroo Jerky: A popular snack in the area, kangaroo jerky is a chewy, savory treat you must try during your trip.

  2. Tim Tam: A famous chocolate biscuit that is synonymous with Australia.

  3. Vegemite: A spread made from yeast extract, vegemite is a staple food in Australia and is best enjoyed on toast or crackers.

Local Beverages

  1. Victorian Ale: A beer made with locally sourced ingredients, Victoria Ale is a must-try drink when visiting the park.

  2. Wine: The region is home to several vineyards known for producing excellent Pinot Noirs and Shiraz wines.

  3. Coffee: Australia is known for its coffee culture, and coffee lovers should not miss trying some locally roasted coffee when visiting.

Where to Stay in Great Otway National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Great Otway National Park has several accommodation options to choose from during your stay. From luxury hotels to camping grounds, there’s something for every traveler’s preference.

  1. Beacon Point Ocean View Villas: A luxury accommodation option near Apollo Bay, this lodging comes with private balconies offering sweeping views of the sea.

  2. Apollo Bay Eco YHA: A popular backpacker hostel located in the heart of Apollo Bay.

  3. Great Ocean Road Tourist Park: A family-friendly camping ground providing prime access to Apollo Bay’s beach.

What to Buy in Great Otway National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring back from Great Otway National Park:

  1. Local Honey: The region is known for its locally produced honey, rich in flavor and health benefits.

  2. Handmade Pottery: Several local artisans sell handmade pottery, perfect for adding a unique touch to your home décor.

  3. Surfing Gear: The area has a few surf shops offering a range of gear and attire for surfing enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Otway National Park

When visiting Great Otway National Park, it’s important to respect the local customs and understand the region’s culture. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. The area is home to the Gadubanud indigenous people, and visitors must acknowledge their rich cultural heritage while in the park.

  2. The park is located on the traditional lands of the Eastern Maar and Gulidjan people of the Kulin Nation.

  3. Respect the wildlife, and do not feed the animals.

Best Time to Visit Great Otway National Park

The best time to visit Great Otway National Park is during autumn (March to May) and spring (September to November). The weather is mild, and the park’s vegetation is lush and green during these seasons. However, if you are interested in surfing, visit the park in winter (June to August) when the waves are high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Otway National Park

Do:

  1. Be respectful to the environment and wildlife.

  2. Carry enough water and food when traveling through the park.

  3. Adhere to fire safety regulations and ensure your campsite fire is fully extinguished before leaving it.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter the park, and take your trash with you.

  2. Do not feed the animals, as it is dangerous and could be harmful to their health.

  3. Do not stray from marked trails and risk getting lost in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Otway National Park

When visiting Great Otway National Park, it’s essential to take necessary safety precautions to ensure an enjoyable and accident-free trip.

  1. Always carry a first-aid kit and be prepared in case of an emergency.

  2. Wear appropriate clothing and footwear depending on the outdoor activities you plan to do.

  3. Respect all weather warnings and forecasts to prevent accidents during your stay.

Health in Great Otway National Park

The park is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to be aware of the following health risks:

  1. Mosquitoes can be prevalent during the summer months, so you may need insect repellent.

  2. Sunburn and dehydration can be common, so ensure to stay hydrated and apply sunblock when necessary.

Getting to Great Otway National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Otway National Park

  1. Melbourne Tullamarine International Airport: The main airport in Melbourne, located 200 km from the park.

  2. Avalon Airport: Another airport near Melbourne, located 166 km from the park.

Bus Stations Near Great Otway National Park

  1. Apollo Bay: A bus stop located within the town of Apollo Bay.

  2. Colac: A town approximately 47 km away from the park, offering buses and other means of transportation.

Other Options

  1. Car Rental: Renting a car is an excellent option for visitors who want to explore the park at their own pace.

  2. Taxi/Cabs: There are several taxi and cab services available in the area.

How to Get Around in Great Otway National Park

Great Otway National Park is accessible by car, foot, and bicycle. If you prefer to cycle or walk, there are several trails throughout the park that are easily accessible.

Where to Go Next from Great Otway National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Great Otway National Park is not the only attraction in the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. The Twelve Apostles: One of the most iconic natural wonders in Australia, the Twelve Apostles are located an hour’s drive away from the park.

  2. Warrnambool: A charming coastal town located two hours away from Great Otway National Park.

Conclusion

Great Otway National Park is a fantastic destination for those looking to immerse themselves in Australia’s natural beauty, culture, and adventure. From its stunning waterfalls and pristine beaches to its ancient rainforests, the park has something to offer for everyone. This travel guide has given you a comprehensive overview of the park, including everything you need to know to plan your trip to Great Otway National Park. So go ahead and pack your bags for an unforgettable adventure in Victoria.
