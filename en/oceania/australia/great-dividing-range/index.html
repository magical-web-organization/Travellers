Introduction to Great Dividing Range

Stretching more than 3,500 kilometers along the eastern coast of Australia, the Great Dividing Range is the country’s most extensive mountain range. It is home to breathtaking landscapes, a rich wildlife, and a vibrant culture. With a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and stunning natural scenery, the Great Dividing Range is a must-visit destination for any traveler.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Dividing Range

Australia has a unique and diverse culture, influenced by its Indigenous and colonial history. Australians are known for their laid-back attitude, love of sports, and barbeques. Tipping is not customary, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Great Dividing Range

The best time to visit the Great Dividing Range is between March to May or September to November when the weather is mild and beautiful. Summers (December to February) can be hot and humid, while winters (June to August) can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Dividing Range

Do:

  • Plan ahead and book accommodations and tours in advance.
  • Carry sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat.
  • Take the time to explore the local culture and meet the locals.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the safety signs in the national parks.
  • Feed the wildlife or approach them.
  • Leave your valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Dividing Range

Australia is a very safe country, but it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions. Follow all safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and surfing. Also, be aware of the presence of wildlife such as snakes and spiders.

Health in Great Dividing Range

Australia has excellent medical facilities, but it is always recommended to have travel insurance. It is also important to bring any necessary medications with you, as some may not be available in Australia.

How to Get Around in Great Dividing Range

Getting around the Great Dividing Range can be done by car, bus, or train. However, renting a car is the most convenient way to explore the region, as it allows for flexibility and easy access to remote areas.
