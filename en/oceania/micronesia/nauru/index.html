Introduction to Nauru

Located in Micronesia, Nauru is the third smallest country in the world. It is an island nation, and its capital is Yaren. Nauru may not be known for its tourist attractions, but it has a unique cultural significance, natural beauty, and opportunities for adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Nauru

Nauru has a range of attractions to visit that reflect its history and natural beauty. Here are some of the must-visit places in Nauru:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nauru Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Nauru and explore the exhibits showcasing traditional handicrafts, artifacts, and photographs.
  • World War II Sites: Visit the Japanese Occupation Bunkers or the Air Raid Shelter (O’Beirne’s Cave), which are historical remnants of WWII.
  • Buada Lagoon: Featuring traditional Nauruan architecture with coral and timber posts, it is a popular spot to learn about the indigenous culture of Nauru.

Natural Attractions of Nauru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anibare Bay: A crescent-shaped bay, Anibare is an excellent spot for sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling.
  • Moqua Caves: These limestone caves offer an otherworldly experience for adventure seekers, and you can explore the stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Command Ridge: Offering panoramic views of the island, Command Ridge has some challenging hiking routes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nauru

Experience the natural beauty of Nauru by trying some of the stunning hiking and biking routes available. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Buada Loop Trail: A popular hiking trail in Nauru that takes you through the lush vegetation of the central plateau and features some cultural sites.
  • Anetan Conservation Area: This beautiful reserve offers an exciting experience of hiking and biking. You’ll spot rare flora and fauna, and if you’re lucky, maybe a coconut crab.

Best Things to Do in Nauru: Activities and Tours

While Nauru might not have theme parks or giant malls, it still offers a range of activities and tours worth experiencing.

  • Go fishing, diving or snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of Nauru
  • Attend the Birdman Rally, Nauru’s famous annual event where participants build and fly their own ‘aircraft’
  • Take a cultural tour to learn more about the indigenous culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nauru

Here’s a week-long itinerary to explore Nauru, from sightseeing to trying local cuisine:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Buada Lagoon and learn about the traditional Nauruan lifestyle
2 Explore Nauru Museum, the WWII sites, and appreciate the history of Nauru
3 Discover the Anibare Bay and go snorkeling or swimming
4 Hike to Moqua Caves and witness one of the island’s natural wonders
5 Attend the Birdman Rally and join the judges in selecting a winner
6 Go on a cultural tour of Nauru, learning about its fascinating heritage
7 Sample local cuisine at Menen Hotel Restaurant and relax at the Anibare Bay

Food and Drink Scene of Nauru

Nauru has some mouth-watering dishes and refreshing local beverages that you must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ika (Fish): A national dish that is cooked with coconut milk and is typically served with rice and vegetables.
  • Lu (Taro): A dish made with taro roots boiled in coconut milk, often served with meat or fish.
  • Chicken Curry: Chicken pieces cooked with curry powder that is served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Kava: A traditional drink made from the roots of the pepper plant. It is effective in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.
  • Coconut water: A refreshing and healthy drink that is rich in electrolytes.
  • Banana wine: A vintner must try that’s made from fermented bananas.

Where to Stay in Nauru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

You will find several accommodation options in Nauru. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Menen Hotel: A two-star hotel with pool views, comfortable rooms, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Nasama Resort: A three-star hotel close to the beach, with a bar, spa, and restaurant options.
  • Od’n Aiwo Hotel: A four-star hotel with spa amenities and located centrally, with easy access to the famous attractions.

What to Buy in Nauru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nauru has some fantastic local products that make excellent souvenirs, including handicrafts made from coconut and shells, hand-carved wooden artifacts like spoons and forks, and woven bags made from pandanus and coconut leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Nauru

Grasp the local culture and customs to appreciate Nauru’s indigenous tradition. Some of the customs are:

  • Men and women are expected to dress modestly and cover their shoulders and knees.
  • Nauruans greet each other by waving or nodding rather than shaking hands.
  • Visitors should not touch or handle locals’ belongings without permission.

Best Time to Visit Nauru

The best time to visit Nauru is from December to February as it has warm, sunny weather, which is ideal for outdoor activities. However, be prepared for crowded beaches during the holiday season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nauru

When in Nauru, make sure to follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Show respect for the local culture and tradition.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample the local drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Don’t ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Nauru is safe for travelers. However, you should take necessary precautions to keep yourself safe.

Safety in Nauru

  • Avoid overly exposing your expensive items such as jewelry or expensive gadgets.
  • Lock your hotel room and avoid wandering in unfamiliar places at night.
  • Beware of stray dogs and monkeys.

Health in Nauru

  • Drink only boiled or bottled water.
  • Do not eat raw or uncooked meats and seafood.
  • Wear mosquito repellant while outdoors.

Getting to Nauru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nauru has a few ways to get in and out of the island that you can take to plan your trip conveniently.

Airports Near Nauru

  • Nauru International Airport: It is the only airport in Nauru, and airlines such as Nauru Airlines operate there.

Bus Stations Near Nauru

  • Nauru Island Bus: It is the sole public transportation in Nauru.

Other Options

  • You can ferry to Nauru from Kiribati or take a flight from Fiji, Australia, or Philippines.

How to Get Around in Nauru

Renting a car is the only option for tourists. You can rent one from Menen Hotel or Od’n Aiwo hotel or contact the local tour operators.

Where to Go Next from Nauru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Although Nauru is a small island, there are other exciting nearby destinations to visit, such as Kiribati, Pohnpei, and Yap.

Conclusion

Nauru may be tiny, but don’t let its size fool you. There is so much to explore and experience in this beautiful island nation. From cultural history to natural attractions, adventure sports to the pleasant weather, Nauru offers a unique holiday experience that will stay with you forever.
