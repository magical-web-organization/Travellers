Introduction to Mangilao

Mangilao, located in the western Pacific Ocean in the Micronesia region, is a quaint village located on the island of Guam. This small village may seem like an unlikely travel destination, but it is a must-visit for those who want to experience the local customs, culture, and history of the island. Dotted with ancient historical landmarks, museums, stunning beaches, and natural landscapes, Mangilao invites visitors to explore its wonders and find an escape from the hustle and bustle of their everyday life.

List of Places to Visit in Mangilao

Mangilao is home to several culturally significant attractions and boasts of natural landscapes that are perfect for outdoor adventure activities. Here’s a list of some must-visit places in Mangilao:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • University of Guam Museum: This museum is an ideal spot to start learning about the history, culture, and traditions of Guam. It showcases artifacts that date back to the island’s early Chamorro settlements, Spanish colonial-era, and the significant role Guam played in both World Wars.
  • Guam Premier Outlets: This outlet mall is the go-to spot for fashion enthusiasts. It has some of the finest brands and stores, such as Nike, Adidas, and Michael Kors. It is also a popular spot for local Guam and Micronesia’s residents to hang out.
  • Asan Bay Overlook: This is situated on Asan Beach, a picturesque location with beautiful views and a great deal of Guam’s history. Asan Bay Overlook features thriving flora and fauna, stunning cascading waters, and plenty of World War II remnants.

Natural Attractions of Mangilao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Alifan: This is Guam’s highest point, and it is a perfect spot to witness a magnificent sunrise or sunset. The hike to the peak is moderate, and it offers a breathtaking view of the southern half of Guam.
  • Alupat Island: A perfect rocky spot just off Guam’s shorelines, reachable by paddleboard, offers a marvelous view of Guam’s coastline and its surroundings. More adventurous visitors can climb the volcanic rock formations and explore the island’s crevices.
  • Toguan Bay: For an idyllic seaside adventure, head to Toguan Bay. This hidden gem features crystal clear water, well-maintained beaches, and a plethora of tropical fish. The calm and shallow waters make it a perfect spot for swimming and leisurely snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mangilao

Mangilao is a hiker and biker’s paradise. Mount Alifan provides several hiking trails that are perfect for mountain biking or hiking journey enthusiasts, some of which lead to the peak of the mountain peak. Biking trails and other hiking routes are available nearby, such as towards Yona, Mangilao, and Talofofo caves. It is an excellent way for visitors looking to explore Guam’s natural surroundings and enjoy some fresh air.

Best Things to Do in Mangilao: Activities and Tours

Mangilao is a hotbed of activities and tours for nature and culture enthusiasts alike. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Chamorro Village Night Market: This traditional night market is a familiar gathering place for both locals and visitors. Located in Hagatna, just east of Mangilao, the Chamorro Village Night Market features food stalls, souvenir shops, and live performances from Chamorro and Micronesian artists.
  • Kayaking and Snorkeling in Talofofo River: Explore the lush mangroves and scenic surroundings of Talofofo River through kayaking. Visitors can also see thriving bird populations and explore the underwater floral and faunal diversity as they snorkel in the clear river waters.
  • Visit Inarajan Pools: Just south of Mangilao, visitors can explore Inarajan Pools, another beautiful setting filled with coral swimming pools, which are fantastic spots for snorkeling and swimming.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mangilao

Here’s a one-week itinerary for visitors who want to make the most of their visit to Mangilao:

Food and Drink Scene of Mangilao

Visitors should dive into the island’s cuisines and indulge in traditional Chamorro and Micronesian dishes, along with local contemporary-fusion cuisine. The food scene in Mangilao reflects the city’s diverse and vibrant culture.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kelaguen: Served with a spicy pepper paste, this traditional Chamorro dish is prepared with diced chicken, fish, or beef, fresh lemon juice, and grated coconut.
  • Red Rice: This is a mainstay at the local’s table and is prepared by mixing the staple rice with Annatto seeds, onions, and garlic.
  • Steamed Rice: This simple dish is a must-try in Mangilao, where visitors can sample the different varieties of rice, from the sticky short grained to white rice.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: This refreshing and hydrating drink is available at most local markets and is harvested directly from Guam’s coconut plantations.
  • Fruit Shakes: Perfectly blended with local fruits like papaya, mango, and banana, fruit shakes offer a great option for visitors craving something sweet and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Mangilao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mangilao has plenty of accommodation options, offering easy access to major attractions and locations. Some of the popular options include:

  • Santa Fe Hotel: Situated in downtown Hagatna, Santa Fe Hotel is an ideal location for visitors who want to explore the city’s culture and history.
  • Verona Resort and Spa: Located on pristine Tumon Beach, this resort features private balconies with an ocean view, pools, and spa facilities.
  • Bayview Hotel: A short distance from the Micronesian Mall, this hotel features easy access to one of Guam’s best shopping spots and is a great place to relax.

What to Buy in Mangilao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors looking to take home a piece of Guam can explore local markets and shops situated in and around Mangilao. The Micronesia Mall is an ideal place to find island-inspired souvenirs and bring them home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mangilao

Guam is a beautiful representation of unique marrying cultures with more than one language and religion. The Chamorro, Micronesian, and American cultures are part of their history. Visitors should respect and follow the local etiquettes and customs while exploring the different sites.

Best Time to Visit Mangilao

The best time to visit Mangilao is between December and March, when the weather is at its best, and the humidity is considerably low. It’s an ideal time for visitors to indulge in outdoor activities and explore Guam’s natural surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mangilao

Do:

  • Respect the locals and follow the dress code while visiting Chamorro religious sites.
  • Carry important travel documentation like passport, copy of visa, travel insurance, etc.
  • Be courteous and greet people politely.

Don’t:

  • Photograph the locals or their belongings without permission.
  • Wear disrespectful or revealing attire in sacred religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mangilao

Guam is a generally safe and peaceful destination. Visitors should keep a lookout for their surroundings, especially at night in isolated places.

Health in Mangilao

While Mangilao isn’t considered a high-risk destination regarding health hazards, visitors are advised to take general precautions. It is important to seek medical attention immediately if they experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to Mangilao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mangilao

The Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport is the main entrance to Guam. It is located in the nearby Tumon Bay area and is serviced by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Mangilao

Two of the popular bus routes are the Dededo Mango loop and MTM Batido. They have respective bus schedules and routes that cover Tumon, Hagatna, and Mangilao and significant public transportation on the roads covers most places in Guam.

Other Options

Visitors can also explore the island’s beauty through rental cars, bikes, or guided tours.

How to Get Around in Mangilao

Visitors can explore Mangilao’s heritage and natural surroundings on foot, while for distant places, rental cars and guided tours are the ideal options.

Where to Go Next from Mangilao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can continue their Micronesian adventure by ;

  • Hagatna: An iconic city that is the nation’s capital and boasts historical landmarks and cultural attractions, situated 13km away from Mangilao.
  • Tumon: Situated 11km away from Mangilao, Tumon beach is an iconic destination featuring vibrant local markets, restaurants, and shopping centers.
  • Agana: Situated in the southern part of Guam, Agana is a must-visit destination for those interested in local culture and architecture.

Conclusion

Mangilao, Guam, is an ideal destination for those who love diving into local cultures and experiencing natural serenity. From fine museums to cultural attractions, from natural landscapes to outdoor adventure activities, Mangilao has everything to offer.
