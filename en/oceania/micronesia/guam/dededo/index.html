Introduction to Dededo

Located in the northern part of Guam, Dededo is the most populated village on the island. It is home to various natural and cultural attractions, including the Micronesia Mall, the largest shopping center in the region. Visitors to Dededo can explore museums and historical sites, take outdoor adventures, enjoy local cuisine, and experience the unique Chamorro culture.

List of Places to Visit in Dededo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dededo has several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into Guam’s rich history and culture. The Guam Museum is a must-visit. It features exhibits that showcase the island’s Chamorro heritage, colonial past, and World War II history. Another popular attraction is the Gef Pa’go Cultural Village, where visitors can watch traditional Chamorro dance performances, try weaving a basket, or taste local food.

Natural Attractions of Dededo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy the outdoor adventures and scenic views that Dededo offers. Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore trails at the Tarzan Falls and the Andres P. Sanchez Jr. Nature Preserve. Beachcombers can relax on Ypao Beach, catch some fish at Tanguisson Beach, or dive at Gab Gab Beach. The Masso Reservoir and the Ritidian Point Wildlife Refuge also offer opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife observation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dededo

Dededo offers various hiking and biking trails, including the Marbo Cave Trail and the Chalan Pago-Ordot Trail. The Marbo Cave Trail takes visitors to a cave with unique limestone formations that date back to World War II. The Chalan Pago-Ordot Trail features scenic views of the Hagatna Bay and the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Dededo: Activities and Tours

In addition to museums, historical sites, and outdoor adventures, Dededo also offers other exciting activities and tours. Visitors can try zip lining at the Guam Adventure Park, go horseback riding at the Horses of the Sun Equestrian Center, or explore the jungle on an ATV at the Guam Racing Federation. Another popular activity is the Jungle River Cruise, which takes visitors through the scenic Talofofo and Ugum Rivers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dededo

Day 1: Visit the Guam Museum and take a stroll in the Plaza De Espana. Day 2: Explore the Masso Reservoir and try birdwatching at the Ritidian Point Wildlife Refuge. Day 3: Take the Marbo Cave Trail and explore the unique limestone formations. Day 4: Enjoy water activities at Ypao Beach or Tanguisson Beach. Day 5: Shop at the Micronesia Mall and watch a movie at the theatres. Day 6: Try zip lining, horseback riding, or ATV riding. Day 7: Watch traditional Chamorro dance performances or try weaving at the Gef Pa’go Cultural Village.

Food and Drink Scene of Dededo

Must-Try Dishes

Dededo offers various dishes that show off the unique blend of Chamorro, Spanish, and Asian influences. Some must-try dishes include red rice, chicken kelaguen, shrimp patties, and cabbage soup. Visitors can taste these dishes and more at local restaurants such as Jamaican Grill and King’s Restaurant.

Local Beverages

The traditional Chamorro drink is called the “kafe kashi,” a mixture of coffee, rice, and sugar. Other local beverages include the refreshing coconut water and the potent “tuba,” a coconut wine that is fermented for a couple of days.

Where to Stay in Dededo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dededo offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from hotels such as the Dusit Thani Guam Resort and the Wyndham Garden Guam. Budget-friendly options include hostels such as the Tamuning Plaza Hotel and the Hafa Adai Hostel.

What to Buy in Dededo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Dededo can buy unique souvenirs and local products that showcase the island’s culture and artistry. Some must-buy items include shell jewelry, woven baskets, and wood carvings. The Chamorro Village Night Market is an excellent place to shop for these items and sample local food and drink.

Local Culture and Customs of Dededo

The Chamorro culture is deeply rooted in Dededo and Guam. Visitors can witness this through the traditional dances, the village festivities, and the food. Respect for elders is also an essential part of Chamorro culture, as well as the concept of “inafa’maolek,” which means taking care of each other.

Best Time to Visit Dededo

The best time to visit Dededo is from December to March when the weather is dry and comfortable. However, visitors can enjoy the island’s offerings year-round as the climate is tropical, and temperatures rarely dip below 70°F.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dededo

Do:

  • Respect the island’s culture and customs.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Swim in restricted areas or during hazardous weather.
  • Touch or disturb historical artifacts or sites.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dededo

Dededo is generally a safe and peaceful village. However, visitors should take precautions against theft and pickpocketing in public places. They should also observe safety measures when enjoying water activities or exploring hiking trails and natural sites.

Health in Dededo

Visitors to Dededo should take necessary vaccinations before arriving on the island. They should also protect themselves against mosquito bites, as dengue fever and Zika are prevalent on the island.

Getting to Dededo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dededo

The Guam International Airport is located in Tamuning, which is about a 20-minute drive from Dededo. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Dededo

Dededo has various bus stops that offer transportation to other parts of Guam. The Guam Regional Transit Authority operates public buses that are cost-effective and efficient.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or a motorcycle to explore Dededo and other areas of Guam. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Dededo

The best way to get around Dededo is by rental car or taxi. Visitors can also use the local buses or rent a motorcycle to explore the village and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Dededo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Dededo can explore the nearby destinations such as Tumon, which is known for its beaches, shopping, and nightlife, or Hagatna, which is the capital of Guam and offers a glimpse into the island’s colonial past. Other nearby excursions include a trip to the War in the Pacific National Historical Park or a visit to the Guam National Wildlife Refuge.

Conclusion

Dededo is a vibrant and exciting village that offers visitors a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and local cuisine, visitors to Dededo can experience the beauty and charm of Guam.
