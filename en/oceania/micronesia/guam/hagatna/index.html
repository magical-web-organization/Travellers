Introduction to Hagåtña

Hagåtña, also known as Agana, is the capital city of Guam - an unincorporated territory of the United States in Micronesia. The city is located on the west-central coast of the island, and it’s considered the hub of Guam’s history and culture. Hagåtña is a cultural landmark that offers a mix of rich history, Polynesian traditions, and stunning natural beauty. This guide will explore some of the top places to visit in Hagåtña, things to do, where to stay, and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in Hagåtña

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Hagåtña have an opportunity to delve into Guam’s rich cultural heritage at several museums and historical sites. A must-see destination is the Guam Museum, which was inaugurated in 2017. The museum houses a wide range of exhibits that showcase Guam’s past, present, and future - from traditional arts and crafts to modern military history.

Another must-visit site is the Plaza de España, which is located in the heart of the city. It was built in 1669 by the Spanish, who colonized the island. The park is surrounded by historic buildings such as the Governor’s Palace and the Cathedral of Our Lady of Camarin.

The Latte Stone Park is another culturally significant location in Hagåtña. The park features two Latte stone pillars, an ancient indigenous structure, believed to be over 1,000 years old, and the traditional house used by the Chamorro people centuries ago.

Natural Attractions of Hagåtña: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hagåtña also offers an array of outdoor activities and natural attractions for visitors to enjoy. One of the most popular ones is the Hagåtña Baywalk, which is a popular spot for jogging and strolling. The Baywalk offers a stunning view of the blue waters and traditional Chamorro canoes cruising through it.

The Fort Apugan lookout provides breathtaking views of Hagåtña Bay and the surrounding areas. In colonial times, the fort served as a lookout point for the Spanish soldiers. It is now accessible via a short hike that meanders through the jungle leading to the summit.

The Lina’La’ Park, located near the river mouth of Hagåtña, is a landscaped park that offers a peaceful place to relax and appreciate the natural beauty of Guam. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the river or sit on the benches and enjoy the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hagåtña

Hiking and biking in Hagåtña offer visitors an opportunity to experience Guam’s natural beauty and get a glimpse of its historical sites. Hiking is popular in the Fort Apugan Trail, which takes visitors through the jungle and up to the summit, offering a panoramic view of the city. Another popular biking trail is the Hagåtña Bay Loop, which covers various scenic locations throughout the city and waterfront.

Best Things to Do in Hagåtña: Activities and Tours

Hagåtña is a destination that’s packed with plenty of exciting things to do. One of the must-see events when visiting is the Guam History and Chamorro Heritage Day, which is celebrated annually, usually in March. The event provides visitors with a unique opportunity to experience the traditional Chamorro culture, local cuisine, music, and dance.

Other fun activities include water sports such as snorkeling and kayaking in the Hagåtña’s clear blue waters or embarking on a deep-sea fishing trip. Visitors can also take a Chamorro dance lesson at a traditional dance academy, or attend weekly movie nights held outdoors at the Latte Stone Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hagåtña

Here’s an itinerary to make the most out of a week-long trip to Hagåtña:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Guam Museum and take a walking tour of the Hagåtña Baywalk.
2 Hike to the top of Fort Apugan, then explore the historic sites surrounding Plaza de España, Governor’s Palace, and the Cathedral of Our Lady of Camarin.
3 Take a snorkeling trip to nearby sites, such as the Tumon Bay or the Apra Harbor. In the evening, attend a local outdoor movie night at Latte Stone Park.
4 Join a traditional Chamorro dance class, and learn how to make coconut crab at the Chamorro Village Night Market.
5 Embark on a day tour of the island’s major attractions from Hagåtña, including the Ritidian Beach, the War in the Pacific National Historical Park, and the Talofofo Caves.
6 Take a kayaking tour along Hagåtña River, visit the nearby Paseo Stadium, or watch a baseball game. Later, relax at Lina’la’ Park.
7 Spend a relaxing beach day at the Gab Gab Beach, Jupit Bay, or Taga’chang Beach Park, followed by a farewell dinner at one of the city’s traditional restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Hagåtña

Guam’s food scene is a melting pot of indigenous Chamorro cuisine and global gastronomic influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes to sample in Hagåtña:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kelaguen: A local delicacy comprising thinly sliced meat or seafood that’s cooked in citrus juices and served with chili peppers and onions.
  • Kadon Pika: A traditional Chamorro spicy chicken soup, which is a staple food in the local cuisine.
  • Sashimi: Guam’s proximity to the sea means visitors have access to fresh seafood, making sashimi a must-try dish.
  • Bistek: Guam’s version of the Filipino beef steak, which is marinated in a tangy sauce and served with rice and vegetables.
  • Red rice: A signature dish prepared with the local red rice variety, mixed with onions, garlic, and sofrito sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water: The island’s coconut trees are plentiful, and coconut water is available in many stores across the city.
  • Chamorro beer: Visitors can sample the local beer brands, such as the Pale Ale, Lager or Bock, which are widely available in the restaurants and bars.
  • Latte stone coffee: Guam has several coffee plantations, and visitors can relish the local brew made from the coffee beans grown on the island.

Where to Stay in Hagåtña: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hagåtña offers a range of accommodations for tourists, ranging from high-end resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses and villas. Some of the most popular options include Sheraton Laguna Guam Resort, Leopalace Resort Guam, and Guam Reef Hotel.

Hostels are popular options for travelers seeking budget accommodations, including Guam Capsule Hotel, AOA Harmony Hostel, and Casa Bella Vista Guest House.

What to Buy in Hagåtña: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can collect plenty of souvenirs and local products when holidaying in Hagåtña. Guam’s most popular souvenir items include:

  • Chamorro pottery: Visitors can buy various handmade pottery items, including plates, cups, and bowls, to take home as memorabilia.
  • Traditional art and crafts: Shop for local wood carvings, textiles, and traditional jewelry, created by artisans across the island.
  • Guam chocolate: Visitors can taste the island’s premium chocolate, made with local cacao beans.
  • Personalized souvenirs: Several stores in Hagåtña sell custom-made magnets, key chains, and T-shirts, with the personalized name of the visitor.

Local Culture and Customs of Hagåtña

Chamorro culture is deeply ingrained in the way of life in Hagåtña. Visitors should respect the island’s customs and etiquette when touring the city. A few customs unique to the island include:

  • Saying “Håfa Adai”: Visitors should greet the locals with the traditional greeting, which means “Hello” in Chamorro.
  • Respect the elders: Chamorro culture doesn’t tolerate disrespect toward older people. Visitors should address elders with utmost respect.
  • Leave sandals outside: Chamorro culture expects visitors to remove their sandals before entering people’s homes or other buildings.
  • Be modest: Chamorro culture is quite modest in dress and actions, and visitors should avoid wearing revealing clothing or costumes in public.

Best Time to Visit Hagåtña

The best time to visit Hagåtña is between December and June, which is when the island experiences the dry season with low humidity and stable weather conditions. Temperatures remain around 26°C during the day, providing perfect weather for outdoor adventures and water activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hagåtña

The island of Guam and Hagåtña is generally safe for visitors. However, standard safety precautions apply when traveling. Visitors are advised to be cautious when walking alone at night. Travelers should avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables around but keep them safely locked in their hotels.

Health in Hagåtña

It is essential to take preventative measures to stay healthy while traveling to Hagåtña. Visitors should consider getting vaccinated for MMR (measles, mumps, rubella), tetanus, hepatitis A & B, and Typhoid. During the wet season, visitors should take extra precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever, by wearing mosquito repellent and clothes that cover skin.

Getting to Hagåtña: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hagåtña

Guam International Airport (GUM) is the only international airport on the island. It’s located about 10 km from Hagåtña city center, and visitors can take a taxi or shuttle bus service to reach the city.

Bus Stations Near Hagåtña

Most visitors prefer to rent a car or hail a taxi to move around Hagåtña, as the local bus transportation system is limited. However, visitors can use the “Guam Regional Transit Authority,” which operates within the island and makes stops at select major tourist destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting bikes, or taking transportation services, such as Uber or Lyft, to travel around the city efficiently.

How to Get Around in Hagåtña

Most visitors prefer to rent a car to explore Hagåtña efficiently. Taxis are also readily available in the city, and visitors can use popular ride-hailing services, such as Uber and Lyft.

Biking is becoming popular in Hagåtña, as the city provides a few trails and stunning scenic routes for exploring on a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Hagåtña: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hagåtña is located on the west-central coast of Guam, surrounded by several stunning destinations to explore. Some of the popular options include:

  • Agat: A town on Guam’s southwest coast, known for its beautiful beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Tumon Bay: Located a few kilometers north of Hagåtña, Tumon Bay is the island’s primary tourist hub, offering beautiful beaches, luxury shopping, and nightlife.
  • Ritidian Point: A nature reserve located in the northern part of the island, which offers hiking trails, scenic vistas, and some of Guam’s most secluded beaches.

Conclusion

Hagåtña, the capital city of Guam, offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and exciting outdoor activities. From hiking to snorkeling, exploring traditional Chamorro culture to exploring the historic landmarks, Hagåtña has plenty to offer visitors. The city’s restaurants offer many food and drink options, while its varied accommodations cater to visitors’ diverse budgets and needs. It’s a perfect destination for anyone seeking an exotic island experience coupled with a celebration of tradition and culture.
