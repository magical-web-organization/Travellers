Introduction to Mariana Islands

Mariana Islands is an archipelago located in Micronesia, a region in Oceania. It is comprised of two U.S territories - Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands. Known for its unique culture, rich history, and picturesque landscapes, Mariana Islands is a must-visit for any traveler looking for an off-beat adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Mariana Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Guam Museum and Cultural Center: The museum houses exhibits showcasing the island’s rich history, cultural traditions, and contemporary art.

  • Latte Stone Park: This park features authentic latte stones, a symbol of Chamorro and Carolinian culture.

  • Saipan World War II Museum: This museum chronicles the island’s involvement in World War II.

Natural Attractions of Mariana Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tinian Hot Springs: A natural hot spring that flows into the Pacific Ocean offering an awe-inspiring view.

  • Taga Beach: A popular beach with serene waters, powdery white sand, and scenic surroundings.

  • The Grotto: A natural limestone cavern open to the sky, housing crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life. It’s a perfect location for adventurous divers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mariana Islands

  • Rota Island Hike: A challenging hiking trail offering spectacular views of the cliffs and ocean.

  • Mount Santa Rosa Trail: A 10.8-kilometer hiking trail that leads to the highest peak in Saipan with breathtaking views of the island.

  • Two Lovers Point Hike: This fascinating hike blends Guam’s history, culture, and natural beauty, offering breathtaking views of the island.

Best Things to Do in Mariana Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: This is a perfect way to explore the islands hidden coves, caves, and bays.

  • Snorkeling: Mariana Islands is famous for its crystal-clear waters and coral reefs and it’s a perfect place to go snorkeling.

  • Whale Watching: This is a rare activity in Mariana, however, adventurous travelers can spot dolphins, sea turtles, and flying fish.

Food and Drink Scene of Mariana Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kelaguen: A traditional Chamorro dish made of cooked and chopped chicken, beef, fish, or shrimp, mixed with onions, fresh herbs, and lemon juice.

  • Red Rice: A staple food of the Chamorro people, red rice is cooked with annatto seeds (achote) and flavored with garlic, onions, and soy sauce.

  • Tinala Katne: A smoked beef that is salted and marinated overnight, giving it a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink popular among residents of Mariana Islands for its natural hydrating properties.

  • Fruit Juices: There’s nothing like local fresh fruit juices such as watermelon, pineapple, and papaya that are available in nearly all convenience stores in Mariana!

Where to Stay in Mariana Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Westin Resort Guam: A luxurious resort with amazing amenities, including several pools, great cuisine, and lively entertainment.

  • Hyatt Regency Saipan: This hotel is situated on the stunning white sand beaches of Saipan. It offers top-notch accommodations, recreational activities, and dining options.

  • Marianas Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodations with great amenities and services.

What to Buy in Mariana Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local handicrafts: Travelers can’t go wrong with local handicrafts such as woven hats, shell necklaces, and woven baskets.

  • Trench art: Trench art gift items such as tanks, ships, bullets, and even a condom case with emblems of the military conflict.

Local Culture and Customs of Mariana Islands

Mariana culture is rooted deeply in the Chamorro traditions and customs.

  • Family is important and the most significant institution of society.

  • The leisurely pace of the island’s lifestyle, known as “island time,” is an important part of the culture.

  • Respect your elders and be polite too!

Best Time to Visit Mariana Islands

The dry season, from December to June, is the most popular time to visit Mariana Islands. However, the rainy season is a great option for travelers looking to avoid the crowds and experience the islands differently.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mariana Islands

Do:

  • Observe the local customs and traditions.

  • Respect the local ecology and natural assets.

  • Research and learn about the local areas before visiting.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in beaches or natural sites.

  • Use reef-damaging sunscreens.

  • Harm endangered species.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mariana Islands

Travelers should take precautions against natural calamities such as typhoons, earthquakes, and flooding. Additionally, travelers should be vigilant while exploring the islands at night to avoid potential theft or violence.

Health in Mariana Islands

Travelers should take proper steps to avoid heatstroke or exhaustion, especially during the hot season. Visitors should also research local medical facilities and purchase appropriate travel insurance.

Getting to Mariana Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mariana Islands

  • Guam International Airport

  • Saipan International Airport

Bus Stations Near Mariana Islands

Public transportation options are limited in Mariana, however, there are public buses available on Guam for visitors.

Other Options

Ferries are available for travelers between the islands of Mariana.

How to Get Around in Mariana Islands

The most popular and practical method of transportation around Mariana Islands is renting a car, as public transport is limited. Taxis and bike rentals are also available on Guam and Saipan. Visitors should ensure they have an international driver’s license before renting a car, otherwise you may need to opt for a guided car tour.

Where to Go Next from Mariana Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mariana Islands is situated in an isolated location, and there are no nearby international countries or destinations. However, travelers can explore the nearby islands of Micronesia and discover the charming Federated States of Micronesia (FSM) destinations like Chuuk, Pohnpei, Kosrae, and Yap.

Conclusion

Mariana Islands offers a unique mixture of natural beauty, cultural heritage, outdoor adventure, and history that draws travelers from all over the world. Whether you are a traveler looking for an off-beat adventure to the hot springs or you want to learn about local customs and traditions, Mariana Islands has events and activities for every type of traveler.
