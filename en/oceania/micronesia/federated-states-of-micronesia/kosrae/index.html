Introduction to Kosrae

Kosrae, located in the Micronesia region of Oceania, is a small island that offers tourists a unique experience. It boasts pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, untouched rainforests, and a rich culture. Kosrae is a hidden gem that is worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Kosrae

Before we get into the list of places to visit in Kosrae, it’s important to note that the island is small, and it’s easy to get around. The following are the top attractions to see in Kosrae:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lelu Ruins – These ruins date back to the 14th century, and they are a window into Kosrae’s ancient past. The ruins are situated on a hill overlooking the ocean, which makes it a breathtaking sight to see.

  2. Kosrae Village – This village is nestled within the lush greenery of the island and offers tourists a glimpse of the local culture. Visitors can learn about traditional Kosraean crafts, including weaving and carving.

Natural Attractions of Kosrae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Utwe-Walung Marine Park – Kosrae is an island surrounded by water, so it’s no wonder that its most famous attraction is the Utwe-Walung Marine Park. The park is home to a wide variety of marine life, including sea turtles, reef sharks, and colorful fish.

  2. Mount Finkol – This mountain is one of the highest peaks on Kosrae. It offers a challenging day hike to visitors and rewards them with breathtaking views of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kosrae

Kosrae has plenty of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the most popular routes:

  1. Lelu Trail – The Lelu Trail is a scenic trail that leads through the rainforests of the island. Visitors can enjoy the lush vegetation, the sound of birds, and the occasional encounter with local wildlife.

  2. Mount Oma – Mount Oma is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with panoramic views of the island. The hike is steep, and visitors should be physically fit to make the trek.

Best Things to Do in Kosrae: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving – Kosrae is surrounded by a crystal-clear ocean that’s perfect for snorkeling and diving. Visitors can explore the coral reefs and see a wide variety of marine life.

  2. Kayaking – Visitors can rent a kayak and paddle through the mangroves of the island. It’s a peaceful and relaxing activity that allows visitors to experience the natural beauty of Kosrae.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kosrae

Visitors to Kosrae can optimize their visit by following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and settling in

Day 2: Lelu Ruins and Kosrae Village

Day 3: Snorkeling and Diving in Utwe-Walung Marine Park

Day 4: Mount Finkol Hike

Day 5: Kayaking in the Mangroves

Day 6: Relaxing day on the beach

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Kosrae

Kosrae’s food and drink scene is influenced by its location in the Pacific. Seafood is a staple, and there are also plenty of tropical fruits available. The following are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pwee-pwee – This dish is made with coconut cream, breadfruit, taro, and fish. It’s a traditional Kosraean dish that’s rich in flavor.

  2. Omelet with Papaya – This is a simple yet delicious dish that’s a popular breakfast option on the island.

Local Beverages

  1. Homebrew – Kosraeans make their own beer using local ingredients like breadfruit and taro.

  2. Coconut Juice – This refreshing drink is a fantastic way to beat the heat on the island.

Where to Stay in Kosrae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kosrae has several accommodation options for visitors. The following are some of the best options:

  1. Kosrae Nautilus Resort – This resort is located on the beach and offers guests a restaurant and bar, as well as a pool and spa facilities.

  2. Pacific Treelodge Resort – This eco-friendly resort is located in the middle of the forest and offers guests a unique natural experience.

What to Buy in Kosrae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kosrae is known for its handicrafts, which are made using traditional methods. Visitors can buy a wide range of handicrafts, including baskets, mats, and carvings. Jewelry made of seashells is also a popular souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kosrae

Kosraeans are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Visitors should take the time to learn about the local culture and customs. Visitors should dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering a home or a place of worship.

Best Time to Visit Kosrae

The best time to visit Kosrae is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. The weather during this time is ideal for outdoor activities, and there are plenty of festivals and events to enjoy during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kosrae

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when entering a home or a place of worship.
  • Take off your shoes before entering a home or a place of worship.

Don’t:

  • Take coral, shells, or other marine life from the beaches.
  • Swim in areas that are designated as unsafe or where local people advise against doing so.
  • Leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kosrae

Kosrae is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take normal precautions while traveling, such as locking their doors at night and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Kosrae

Visitors to Kosrae should take precautions against mosquito bites, as there is a risk of dengue fever. Visitors should also ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance and their vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Kosrae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Kosrae by air or sea. The following are the transportation options for getting to Kosrae:

Airports Near Kosrae

  • Kosrae International Airport – Located on the east coast of the island, the airport served by United Airlines and is the main access point for visitors to the island.

How to Get Around in Kosrae

The most convenient way to get around the island is by rental car. Taxis are available, but they can be expensive. Bicycles are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Kosrae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kosrae is relatively isolated, and not many tourists continue to other destinations from here. However, Guam and Palau are popular destinations nearby that visitors can include in their itinerary.

Conclusion

Kosrae is a small but fascinating island that’s worth exploring. From its pristine beaches to its lush rainforest, there’s something for everyone here. The island’s rich culture and friendly locals make it a unique destination that’s worth a visit.
