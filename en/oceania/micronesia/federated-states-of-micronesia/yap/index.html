Introduction to Yap

Located in the Western part of Micronesia, Yap is a stunningly beautiful island filled with a rich culture, nature’s splendor, and unique experiences. With a population of approximately 11,000, Yap has a traditional way of life enriched by customary skills and practices that have been preserved for generations. The island is known for its vibrant history and strong beliefs in navigation techniques, seafaring traditions, and stone money.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore everything Yap has to offer, including the must-visit places, the best food and drink scene, hiking and biking routes, and where to stay. Whether you are an adventure enthusiast or a culture seeker, Yap has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Yap

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yap Living History Museum: This museum displays the traditional ways of life on Yap and how it’s people today still follow the age-old customs and traditions.
  • The Colonia Traditional Navigation Society: This organization provides insight into the importance of navigation and seafaring traditions of Yapese people.
  • Stone Money Bank: The stone money of Yap has long fascinated people the world over, and the stone money bank is an excellent place to learn more about this unique form of currency.

Natural Attractions of Yap: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manta Ray Bay: Known as the home to the world’s most abundant population of manta rays, this bay provides visitors with the incredible opportunity to swim and dive in the company of these graceful creatures.
  • Marine Nature Trail: Enjoy nature’s beauty and learn about Yap’s marine ecology on this scenic hike through a mangrove forest.
  • Waterfall and Cave Adventures: Yap has many secret waterfalls and cave systems that offer an unforgettable outdoor experience to visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yap

  • Yap Island Pathways: This network of walking and biking trails cut through the dense tropical forests of Yap, giving visitors a chance to appreciate the island’s flora and fauna while enjoying a great workout.

Best Things to Do in Yap: Activities and Tours

  • Discover Stone Money: Explore the depths of the ocean and witness firsthand how Yapese locals quarry and transport giant stone slabs used as currency.
  • Explore Yapese Culture: Take part in live local performances and witness age-old customs like weaving, dancing, and tattooing at the Yap Day festival.
  • Dive in Yap: With crystal clear waters and diverse marine life, Yap is considered one of the top dive destinations worldwide.

Food and Drink Scene of Yap

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Rice with Chicken and Vegetables: This staple dish is delicious, nutritious, and easy to find on any restaurant menu in Yap.
  • Taro Soup: A staple in Yapese cuisine, taro soup is prepared during special occasions and served with fish or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Chuuk Pepper Tea: A traditional tea made from peppercorns and citrus fruits. It is spicy and refreshing, served hot or cold.
  • Betel Nut: A popular addiction among Yapese locals, betel nut, is a mild stimulant that is chewed with lime or coconut. Visitors can try them at local bars and small shops all over the island.

Where to Stay in Yap: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Manta Ray Bay Resort and Yap Divers: This luxurious resort offers outstanding accommodations, along with dive packages and excursions.
  • O’Keefe’s Waterfront Inn: Situated in the heart of Colonia, O’Keefe’s provides visitors with a comfortable stay and a fantastic location, surrounded by local shops and restaurants.
  • Yap Pacific Dive Resort and Yap Pacific Sea Sports: This eco-friendly resort is located on the oceanfront and is a perfect choice for visitors who want to get up close with marine life.

What to Buy in Yap: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artifacts: Yapese artwork, including masks and carvings featuring traditional designs, make for beautiful gifts and souvenirs.
  • Sea Urchin Necklaces: Yapese locals are known for using sea urchin shells to make gorgeous and unique necklaces that are a must-bring souvenir.
  • Tapa Cloth: Made of beaten bark from paper mulberry or fig trees, Tapa cloth is a traditional craft in Yap.

Local Culture and Customs of Yap

Yapese culture is a product of its unique location and long history of migration. The people of Yap are known for excellent craftsmanship, seafaring traditions, navigational skills, and the use of large stone disks as currency. Visitors to the island are welcome to experience it all by participating in the annual Yap Day cultural festival.

Best Time to Visit Yap

The best time to visit Yap is between December to April, considered the dry season, when the weather is mild and the ocean is calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yap

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious places or other landmarks.
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of people or houses.

Don’t:

  • Step on any traditional stone paths or disks.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yap

Yap is a safe place to visit but still keep the following in mind:

  • Secure valuables like passports and jewelry.
  • Hire licensed guides instead of exploring on your own.
  • Practice caution while swimming and diving.

Health in Yap

Ensure you have the necessary vaccinations before arriving in Yap. Malaria and dengue can be prevalent in the area. Visitors should carry insect repellent sprays and mosquito nets. It is imperative to consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Yap: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yap

Yap International Airport is the main international gateway to Yap. United Airlines is the most common carrier for Yap.

Bus Stations Near Yap

Yap has no buses or public transportation options for visitors to use. Visitors use taxis or hire private vehicles through rental companies.

Other Options

A convenient option for visitors is to hire a private car or motorbike rental and get around the island’s many attractions conveniently.

How to Get Around in Yap

  • Taxis: Taxis are the most common mode of transportation on Yap, and visitors can hail them on the street or call for a pickup.
  • Car rental: Private car rental is readily available from the airport, and visitors must have a valid International Driver’s Permit (IDP).
  • Biking: Bike rentals are the best way to explore the island while exercising.

Where to Go Next from Yap: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chuuk Islands: Explore the intricately preserved World War II shipwrecks underwater.
  • Palau: A scenic island nation that boasts of wildlife, tropical nature, and rock islands.
  • Guam: A tropical paradise that is affordable and has something for all types of travelers.

Conclusion

Yapese culture, traditions, and breathtaking natural wonders make it a one-of-a-kind travel destination for visitors. In summary, Yap has everything from rugged outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and world-famous activities to make your visit unforgettable. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan the trip of a lifetime to Yap.
