Introduction to Majuro

Majuro is a stunning atoll located in Ratak, one of the two chains of the Marshall Islands in Micronesia, Oceania. Known for its turquoise lagoons, beautiful beaches, and rich history, Majuro is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a unique tropical getaway. Whether you are interested in cultural attractions or outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in Majuro.

List of Places to Visit in Majuro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Majuro has a rich cultural history that provides a glimpse into its past. The following museums and historical places should not be missed:

  • Alele Museum and Public Library: Located in Delap, Alele Museum and Public Library showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Marshall Islands through exhibits on local crafts, fishing, canoe building, navigation, and indigenous plants and animals.
  • Likiep Island: Located northeast of Majuro Atoll, Likiep Island is home to ruins of ancient pre-colonial kingdoms that provide an insight into the Marshallese’s way of life before Western contact.

Natural Attractions of Majuro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Majuro’s natural beauty is unparalleled, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. The following attractions should be on your list:

  • Laura Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches on Majuro Atoll, Laura Beach offers clear turquoise waters, soft white sand beaches, and stunning panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Arno Atoll: A remote, sparsely populated atoll, Arno Atoll offers a pristine natural environment for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. Explore the coral reefs and swim with exotic marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Majuro

Majuro is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Eneko Island Hike: Located on the southern side of Majuro Atoll, Eneko Island offers a beautiful scenic trail that leads to its peaks. The trail is ideal for beginner hikers and offers breath-taking views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Rita Pass Hike: A popular destination for hikers, Rita Pass offers an incredible view of the immense lagoon system and the city infrastructure.

Best Things to Do in Majuro: Activities and Tours

Majuro offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to make the most of their time on the island. Here’s what to add to your itinerary:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Discover the vibrant underwater world of the Marshall Islands and swim with exotic marine life near the coral reefs.
  • Fishing Tours: Enjoy a fishing trip with locals and discover the traditional fishing methods unique to the Marshall Islands.
  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and paddle around the tranquil lagoon of Majuro Atoll for an unforgettable experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Majuro

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Majuro is a mix of traditional Marshallese dishes and Western cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Ijuk: A refreshing local drink made from coconut water and jelly
  • Kakka and Kalo: These root vegetables are staple foods in the Marshallese diet and are usually steamed or boiled.
  • Poke: A traditional dish made with raw fish marinated in coconut milk and vegetables.

Local Beverages

In addition to ijuk, the following are popular local beverages in Majuro:

  • Milk tea: A popular drink made with black tea, milk, sugar, and spices
  • Tamarind juice: A refreshing and tangy drink made from tamarind pulp.

Where to Stay in Majuro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Majuro offers plenty of accommodation options for travelers with various budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  • Marshall Islands Resort: A luxury resort located on the northern shore of Majuro Atoll, offering deluxe rooms and ocean-view villas.
  • Majuro Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse with basic amenities, located near the city center.
  • Hostels: There are several hostels in Majuro that offer comfortable dorm rooms and shared facilities.

What to Buy in Majuro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Majuro can buy traditional Marshallese handicrafts, clothes, and souvenirs at the local markets and shops. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Traditional Marshallese clothes: Handmade clothes with intricate embroidery and designs.
  • Handicrafts: Traditional woven baskets, hats, and fans made from natural fibers.
  • Jewelry: Necklaces and bracelets made with shells, beads, and traditional Marshallese designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Majuro

The Marshallese culture is rich and unique, and visitors to Majuro are encouraged to respect local customs and etiquette. It is recommended to dress modestly in public places and take off shoes before entering homes and sacred places.

Best Time to Visit Majuro

The best time to visit Majuro is during the dry season, which runs from December to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Majuro

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Carry a bottle of water to stay hydrated.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when going outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear swimwear when out and about, unless at the beach or a pool area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Majuro

Majuro is generally a safe place to visit. However, it is recommended to take caution when traveling alone at night, and to avoid wearing expensive jewelry or carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Majuro

Visitors to Majuro should make sure they are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling. It is also recommended to carry mosquito repellent, as mosquitoes in Majuro can carry diseases like dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Majuro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Majuro

Majuro International Airport is the main airport in the Marshall Islands and is served by several airlines, including United Airlines, Nauru Airlines, and Air Marshall Islands.

Bus Stations Near Majuro

There are no bus stations in Majuro, but visitors can hail taxis or rent a car to get around the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange a boat trip to Majuro from nearby islands and atolls.

How to Get Around in Majuro

Visitors to Majuro can get around by taxi, rental car or motorbike, or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Majuro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Discover other amazing Micronesian destinations near Majuro by visiting these locations:

  • Kwajalein Atoll: A world-famous diving destination located west of Majuro, Kwajalein Atoll is known for its coral reefs and rare marine life.
  • Marshall Islands National Park: Located northwest of Majuro Atoll, the Marshall Islands National Park offers a stunning natural setting.

Conclusion

Majuro is a unique tropical paradise that offers a mix of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most of your trip and discover the best places to visit, things to do, and sights to see in this beautiful destination.
