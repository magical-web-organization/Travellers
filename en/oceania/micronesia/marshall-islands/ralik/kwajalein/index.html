Kwajalein is a beautiful island located in Ralik, in Marshall Islands, in Micronesia, in Oceania. This hidden gem offers travelers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural surroundings. With pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and an abundance of outdoor activities, Kwajalein is a must-visit destination for any traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of this beautiful island, from its culturally important attractions to its best hiking and biking routes, and from its food and drink scene to where to stay and what to buy as souvenirs.

Introduction to Kwajalein

Kwajalein is a coral atoll in the Pacific Ocean with a rich history. It was used as a base for the U.S. military during World War II and the Cold War and is still used as a missile testing range and a U.S. military base. However, the island is now open to the public, and visitors can explore its fascinating history and culture, beautiful beaches and lagoons, and enjoy outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Kwajalein

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kwajalein Atoll Museum - showcases the history of the island and has a collection of artifacts and exhibits from World War II and the Cold War era.
  2. Japanese WWII Bunker - a historical site used by the Japanese during the war that is still intact and offers visitors a glimpse into the past.
  3. Emon Beach - a beach that was used as a landing site during World War II and still has the remains of the concrete landing strip.

Natural Attractions of Kwajalein: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sunset Beach - a stunning beach on the west side of the island, perfect for watching the sunset.
  2. Ebeye Island - a small island near Kwajalein, known for its crystal-clear waters and amazing coral reefs.
  3. Kwajalein Lagoon - the lagoon is the largest in the world and is home to an abundance of marine life, including turtles, sharks, and rays.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kwajalein

Kwajalein offers visitors a range of hiking and biking routes that take them through the island’s beautiful nature reserves and landscapes. The most popular routes include:

  1. Barefoot Beach Trail - a scenic trail that runs along the island’s lagoon and offers stunning views of the ocean and beautiful beaches.
  2. Carlos Loop Trail - a hike through the island’s dense jungle that is perfect for birdwatchers and nature lovers.
  3. Piper Loop Trail - a relatively easy hike that takes you to the highest point on the island and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Kwajalein: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving - the island’s crystal-clear waters are perfect for snorkeling and diving, with an abundance of marine life and coral reefs.
  2. Fishing Charters - visitors can charter a fishing boat and try their hand at catching the abundant fish species in the lagoon.
  3. Kayaking and Paddleboarding - the calm waters of the lagoon are perfect for kayaking and paddleboarding, with the opportunity to spot turtles, rays, and other marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kwajalein

Day 1: Visit the Kwajalein Atoll Museum and explore the island’s history.

Day 2: Spend the day on Ebeye Island, swimming and snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters.

Day 3: Take a hiking or biking tour through the island’s stunning jungle trails.

Day 4: Go fishing on a charter boat and try your luck at catching one of the many fish species in the lagoon.

Day 5: Explore the island’s stunning beaches and watch the sunset at Sunset Beach.

Day 6: Spend the day kayaking and paddleboarding in the calm waters of the lagoon.

Day 7: Visit the Japanese WWII Bunker and learn about the island’s wartime history.

Food and Drink Scene of Kwajalein

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Poke - a traditional Hawaiian dish made from fresh fish and vegetables.
  2. Grilled Seafood - with the abundance of fresh seafood in the lagoon, visitors can enjoy a variety of grilled fish and shellfish.
  3. Coconut Bread - a local specialty made from fresh coconut milk and flour.

Local Beverages

  1. Kava - a traditional drink made from the root of the kava plant that has calming properties and is often used in ceremonies.
  2. Coconut Water - the juice of fresh coconuts, a refreshing and healthy drink that is widely consumed throughout the island.

Where to Stay in Kwajalein: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Kwajalein Lodge - a popular hotel located on the island, with a stunning beach and outdoor pool.
  2. Island Guesthouse - a family-owned guesthouse located on the outskirts of the island, perfect for those looking for a more secluded and peaceful stay.
  3. Kwajalein Hospital Guesthouse - a budget-friendly option located near the island hospital, perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Kwajalein: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Jewelry - the island is known for its beautiful handmade jewelry made from local materials like shells and coral.
  2. Coconut Oil - a popular local product, coconut oil is used for cooking, skincare, and hair care.
  3. Local Artwork - visitors can bring home unique artwork and paintings created by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Kwajalein

The Marshallese people are the indigenous people of Kwajalein and have rich cultural traditions. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including dress codes when visiting spiritual sites and asking for permission before taking photos of individuals.

Best Time to Visit Kwajalein

The best time to visit Kwajalein is between December and April when the weather is dry and the island is less prone to storms and typhoons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kwajalein

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid places that are off-limits.
  3. Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Take photos without asking permission.
  2. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting spiritual sites.
  3. Engage in activities that are illegal or unethical.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kwajalein

Kwajalein is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid any areas that are off-limits.

Health in Kwajalein

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccines before traveling to Kwajalein. It’s also recommended that travelers bring insect repellent to protect against mosquitos and other insects.

Getting to Kwajalein: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kwajalein

The nearest airport to Kwajalein is Bucholz Army Airfield, located on the island.

Bus Stations Near Kwajalein

There are no bus stations on Kwajalein as the island is relatively small and visitors typically get around by foot, bike, or car.

Other Options

Visitors can charter a boat or take a ferry to the island from nearby islands like Majuro.

How to Get Around in Kwajalein

The island is relatively small and visitors usually get around by foot, bike, or car. Rental cars are available on the island.

Where to Go Next from Kwajalein: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Majuro - the capital of Marshall Islands, located near Kwajalein, offers visitors a chance to experience the modern side of the country.
  2. Arno Atoll - an atoll located near Kwajalein, known for its stunning beaches and lagoons.

Conclusion

Kwajalein is a beautiful island that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural surroundings. From its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters to its fascinating wartime history and abundance of outdoor activities, Kwajalein is a must-visit destination. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and discover all that Kwajalein has to offer.
