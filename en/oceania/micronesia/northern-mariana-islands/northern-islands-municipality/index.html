Introduction to Northern Islands Municipality

Welcome to the Northern Islands Municipality, a collection of 14 volcanic islands in the Northern Mariana Islands, located in Micronesia in Oceania. The islands are known for their pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and abundant marine life.

The Northern Islands Municipality is a popular destination for tourists who are looking for a unique and untouched tropical paradise. In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to make your trip to the Northern Islands Municipality unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Islands Municipality

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • House of Taga - This archaeological site features massive stone pillars believed to have been erected in honor of a legendary chief named Taga.
  • Chamorro House - A traditional thatched-roof house that provides insight into the local culture.
  • Unai Chulu - A unique cave system that was used as a shelter by ancient Chamorro people.

Natural Attractions of Northern Islands Municipality: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grotto - A natural pool fed by a freshwater waterfall, providing an ideal spot for diving and snorkeling.
  • Bird Island - A sanctuary for seabirds such as frigate birds, white-tailed tropicbirds, and brown boobies.
  • Mañagaha Island - A small tropical islet with picturesque white sandy beaches and clear azure waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Islands Municipality

  • Mount Pagan - An active volcano on the island of Pagan, offering unparalleled views from the summit.
  • Mount Tapochau - The highest peak in the Northern Mariana Islands, with a network of hiking trails leading to the top.
  • Naftan Point Cave Trail - A moderately challenging trail that leads to the Naftan Point cave and a beautiful coral coast.

Best Things to Do in Northern Islands Municipality: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours - Take a boat tour to explore the various islands in the municipality and see the diverse marine life.
  • Diving and Snorkeling - The crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life make the municipality an ideal spot for diving and snorkeling.
  • Fishing - The abundance of fish in the waters of the municipality makes it a great spot for fishing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Islands Municipality

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Islands Municipality

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apigi - Pork wrapped in banana leaves and marinated in vinegar and soy sauce, cooked over charcoal.
  • Kelaguen - A dish made with raw, chopped meat or seafood, mixed with lemon juice, onions, and hot peppers.
  • Lumpia - Filipino-style spring rolls filled with pork, shrimp, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A coconut wine that is sweet and fermented.
  • Fruit Juices - The municipality is known for its fresh fruit juices, including mango, passion fruit, and guava.

Where to Stay in Northern Islands Municipality: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lau Lau Bay Beach Club - A private beach resort with cottages and villas surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Pagan Beach Motel - A budget-friendly beachfront hotel on the island of Pagan.
  • Northern Marianas College Dormitories - A low-cost option for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Northern Islands Municipality: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chamorro arts and crafts - Includes woven hats, baskets, and mats, and Tahitian-style pareos and muumuus.
  • Artifacts and souvenirs - An assortment of seashells, polished stones, and other souvenirs are available in the municipality.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Islands Municipality

The Chamorro culture heavily influences the Northern Islands Municipality. The people of the islands are known for their hospitality and warm welcoming nature. The locals take pride in their traditions, including the “fiesta” celebrations, which honor the patron saint of each village.

Best Time to Visit Northern Islands Municipality

The best time to visit the Northern Islands Municipality is from December to May when the weather is sunny and warm. The rainy season starts in June and lasts until November, and the islands may experience typhoons during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Islands Municipality

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, including dress codes when visiting religious sites.
  • Take your shoes off before entering a Chamorro house.
  • Enjoy the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the ocean.
  • Touch or take anything from the archaeological sites.
  • Underestimate the power of the ocean tides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Islands Municipality

The Northern Islands Municipality is generally safe for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and not leaving valuables unattended on the beach.

Health in Northern Islands Municipality

Visitors to the Northern Islands Municipality should take precautions to avoid waterborne illnesses, such as drinking only bottled water and avoiding raw or undercooked food. It is advisable to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Getting to Northern Islands Municipality: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Islands Municipality

The main airport serving the Northern Islands Municipality is the Saipan International Airport (SPN). There are also several domestic airports on the islands, including Rota International Airport (ROP) and Pagan Airstrip.

Bus Stations Near Northern Islands Municipality

There are no bus stations in the Northern Islands Municipality.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available to rent on the larger islands of Saipan and Rota. Boats and ferries operate between the islands for those who wish to travel between them.

How to Get Around in Northern Islands Municipality

  • Boat Tours - Tour the islands by boat.
  • Rental Car - Rent a car to explore the islands.
  • Taxi - Taxis are available on some islands.

Where to Go Next from Northern Islands Municipality: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saipan - The main island in the Northern Mariana Islands boasts stunning beaches, diverse marine life, and historical sites.
  • Tinian - The peaceful island of Tinian is known for its beautiful beaches, natural sites, and World War II history.

Conclusion

The Northern Islands Municipality is a hidden gem in the Pacific island chain that offers a unique cultural experience and unforgettable natural beauty.
