Introduction to Saipan

Saipan is a tropical paradise located in the Northern Mariana Islands in Micronesia. As the largest island in the group, Saipan has a lot to offer visitors, from historical landmarks and picturesque natural attractions to a lively food and drink scene.

The island’s rich history dates back to World War II, where the Battle of Saipan took place and has since been developed into a bustling tourist destination. Saipan is known for its stunning white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs, making it a popular destination for beach enthusiasts and water sports lovers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Saipan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the biggest draws of Saipan is its historical significance, which is evident in the numerous museums and historical landmarks that dot the island.

One such museum is the Saipan Museum, which offers a glimpse into the island’s rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors can browse through exhibits showcasing the island’s ancient Chamorro culture, Spanish colonial era, and Japanese occupation during World War II.

Another popular attraction is the American Memorial Park, which serves as a tribute to American soldiers who lost their lives in the Battle of Saipan. The park features memorials, museums, and exhibits, as well as a peaceful beachfront park, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely afternoon stroll.

Natural Attractions of Saipan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond the museums and historical landmarks, Saipan is also home to several natural attractions that offer activities and adventures for thrill-seekers.

For instance, the Mariana Trench Dive Site offers divers the chance to explore the deepest part of the ocean - the trench. Those who prefer to stay on the surface can opt for activities like snorkeling, fishing, surfing, and kayaking, which can be enjoyed at most of the island’s beaches.

Visitors can also take in gorgeous views of Saipan by hiking to the peak of Mount Tapochau, the highest point on the island. The hike up might be challenging, but the views of the ocean and surrounding islands are worth it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saipan

Saipan has several trails suitable for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The best time to explore these routes is during the winter months, which have ideal weather conditions.

The Bird Island Trail is one of the most popular trails on the island, leading hikers to a tiny island teeming with birds and surrounded by stunning coral reefs.

The Kingfisher Trail is another top choice, offering hikers enchanting views of Saipan’s pristine beaches and inviting waters.

Best Things to Do in Saipan: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions, Saipan offers several activities and tours that are unique to the island’s culture and history. Here are the best things to do in Saipan:

  • Take an off-road island tour to explore the lush green forests and beautiful beaches of Saipan.
  • Go on a Marianas Trench deep-sea fishing tour to catch an array of fish and even have the opportunity to spot whales and dolphins.
  • Learn more about the island’s indigenous culture by visiting the local villages and engaging with locals.
  • Attend a cultural dance show and feast on traditional cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saipan

  • Day 1: Visit the Saipan Museum and explore the island’s Chamorro culture.
  • Day 2: Take an island tour.
  • Day 3: Hike up to the peak of Mount Tapochau early in the morning and enjoy a panoramic view of the island.
  • Day 4: Enjoy water sports activities such as scuba diving, snorkeling, or kayaking.
  • Day 5: Visit the Marianas Trench Marine National Monument and go deep-sea fishing.
  • Day 6: Visit the American Memorial Park and learn about the island’s history during World War II.
  • Day 7: Attend a cultural dance show and enjoy a traditional Chamorro feast.

Food and Drink Scene of Saipan

Must-Try Dishes

Saipan is known for its blend of indigenous and international cuisine, so visitors can expect to taste unique dishes that are native to the island, as well as international favorites.

Some must-try dishes include:

  • Red rice - a signature Chamorro dish with red rice and BBQ ribs or chicken.
  • Tamales gisu - a Chamorro-style tamale made with grated coconut, cornmeal, and chicken.
  • Fried parrotfish - a local delicacy served in some restaurants.

Local Beverages

The drink scene in Saipan is bustling, with plenty of bars and nightclubs to choose from. As for beverages, visitors can try local drinks such as:

  • Latte stone beer - a locally-brewed beer with a unique flavor profile.
  • Donni Shong - a Chamorro-style coconut milk drink.

Where to Stay in Saipan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saipan has a vibrant accommodation scene, catering for travelers with a range of budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from luxury hotels, cozy guesthouses or backpacker hostels.

Some of the best hotels include the Hyatt Regency Saipan, Fiesta Resort, and Kanoa Resort.

What to Buy in Saipan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors looking for souvenirs and local products can visit various shops and markets across the island, offering everything from traditional handicrafts to locally-made food products.

Some popular items include:

  • Chamorro handmade pottery
  • Coconut oil soaps and lotions
  • Handmade woven baskets

Local Culture and Customs of Saipan

The indigenous Chamorro culture is an integral part of Saipan’s history and identity, and visitors can learn more about it by visiting local villages, engaging with locals, and attending cultural shows.

The Chamorro language is also widely spoken on the island, so learning a few basic phrases can help visitors better understand and interact with locals.

Best Time to Visit Saipan

The best time to visit Saipan is between November and May, where the weather is cool and dry, and there’s less chance of typhoons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saipan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s home.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites or cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Swim in forbidden areas.
  • Take wild animals or plants from the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saipan

Saipan is a safe destination, but visitors should be cautious when exploring the island, especially when engaging in outdoor activities and water sports.

Health in Saipan

Medical services on Saipan are limited, so it’s essential to have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. Visitors should also ensure they take necessary precautions to avoid diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus, which are prevalent on the island.

Getting to Saipan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saipan

Saipan International Airport is the primary airport that services the island, with direct flights from nearby destinations such as Japan, China, and Korea.

Bus Stations Near Saipan

There is no public bus system on Saipan, but visitors can make use of taxis, car rentals, or airport shuttle services.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Saipan via ferry from nearby islands such as Rota and Tinian, although these options are less common.

How to Get Around in Saipan

Taxis and car rentals are the most common modes of transportation on Saipan. Visitors should note that driving is on the right side of the road.

Where to Go Next from Saipan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Saipan can also explore nearby destinations such as:

  • Rota Island
  • Tinian Island
  • Guam

Conclusion

Saipan is a tropical destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From historical landmarks and natural attractions to delicious cuisine and vibrant nightlife, Saipan has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to plan your next adventure and make the most of your trip.
