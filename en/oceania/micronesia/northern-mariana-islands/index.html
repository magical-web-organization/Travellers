Introduction to Northern Mariana Islands

Located in Micronesia, in the heart of the Pacific Ocean, Northern Mariana Islands is a group of 14 islands with a rich history and culture. It is an unincorporated territory of the United States and has a unique blend of American and Chamorro cultures. The islands are home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Mariana Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

American Memorial Park

This park is dedicated to the American soldiers who died during the World War II Battle of Saipan. The park features a museum, a wide range of wildlife, a picnic area, and a theatre.

House of Taga

The House of Taga is an ancient Chamorro monument featuring a stone pillar that is over 15 feet tall and weighs about 50 tons.

The Grotto

The Grotto is a limestone sinkhole filled with crystal-clear water and offers excellent diving and snorkeling opportunities.

Natural Attractions of Northern Mariana Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Saipan’s Best Beaches

Saipan is famous for its beautiful beaches, including Micro Beach, Forbidden Island Beach, and Obyan Beach. These beaches offer breathtakingly beautiful scenery, crystal-clear water, and great opportunities for swimming, surfing, and water sports.

Bird Island

Bird Island is a tiny island located off the western coast of Saipan. It is home to a diverse range of bird species, including frigate birds and the endangered Nihoa Finches.

The Grotto

The Grotto is not only culturally important, but also a natural wonder of Northern Mariana Islands. It is a limestone sinkhole filled with crystal-clear water and offers excellent diving and snorkeling opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Mariana Islands

Mt. Tapochao

Mt. Tapochao is the highest point in Northern Mariana Islands, and the trail to the summit of the mountain offers stunning views of Saipan and surrounding areas.

Bird Island

Bird Island has an easy hiking trail that leads to the summit, offering panoramic views of surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Northern Mariana Islands: Activities and Tours

Scuba-Diving

Northern Mariana Islands is an excellent destination for scuba-diving, with numerous dive sites that offer diverse marine life, clear water, and unique diving experiences.

Snorkeling

Snorkeling in Northern Mariana Islands is an excellent opportunity to see a variety of sea creatures, including whales, dolphins, manta rays, and sea turtles.

Zipline Tours

Zipline tours in Northern Mariana Islands offer a unique way to experience the natural beauty of the islands. The tours allow you to fly through the jungle and enjoy stunning views of the landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Mariana Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Kadon Pika

Kadon Pika is a local dish made with chicken or beef, peppers, onions, and coconut milk. It is served with red rice and is popular on special occasions.

Chicken Kelaguen

Chicken Kelaguen is a local delicacy made with cooked and chopped chicken breast, fresh lemon juice, grated coconut, onions, and hot peppers. It is served with a fresh green salad and rice.

Local Beverages

Tuba

Tuba is a traditional drink made from fermented coconut juice and is popular among locals.

Mwshwi

Mwshwi is a traditional drink made from wild ginger and is known for its medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Northern Mariana Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hyatt Regency Saipan

Hyatt Regency Saipan is a luxury hotel that offers stunning views of the ocean, exceptional service, and top-notch facilities.

Kanoa Resort Saipan

Kanoa Resort Saipan is an affordable, family-friendly resort featuring beach access, a swimming pool, and a range of activities.

What to Buy in Northern Mariana Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shell Jewelry

Shell jewelry is a popular souvenir in Northern Mariana Islands, and you can find it in many markets and stores.

Hand-woven Mats and Baskets

Hand-woven mats and baskets are a practical and authentic souvenir to take home from Northern Mariana Islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Mariana Islands

The culture of Northern Mariana Islands is a unique blend of Chamorro, Carolinian, and American customs and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and their culture is reflected in their music, dance, cuisine, and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Northern Mariana Islands

The best time to visit Northern Mariana Islands is from December to June when the weather is pleasant, and the water is warm. The rainy season starts in July and ends in November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Mariana Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Keep the environment clean and avoid littering.
  • Take part in the local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Take coral or other marine life as a souvenir.
  • Ignore local customs and cultural values.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Mariana Islands

Northern Mariana Islands is a safe destination, and there is no major crime or security concern. However, visitors should take necessary safety precautions and keep valuables in a safe place.

Health in Northern Mariana Islands

The healthcare system in Northern Mariana Islands is limited, and visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance. Visitors should also take necessary precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Northern Mariana Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Mariana Islands

There are three international airports in Northern Mariana Islands:

  • Saipan International Airport
  • Rota International Airport
  • Tinian International Airport

Bus Stations Near Northern Mariana Islands

There is no public bus service in Northern Mariana Islands.

Other Options

The best way to get around Northern Mariana Islands is by renting a car or a scooter.

How to Get Around in Northern Mariana Islands

The best way to get around Northern Mariana Islands is by renting a car or a scooter. Taxis are available but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Northern Mariana Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guam

Guam is a beautiful island located near Northern Mariana Islands. It is home to stunning beaches, historic sites, and natural wonders.

Palau

Palau is an island nation located near Northern Mariana Islands and is famous for its underwater scenery, rock islands, and diverse marine life.
