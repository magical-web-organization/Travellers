Introduction to Gazipur

Gazipur is a district in the Dhaka Division of Bangladesh, located just north of the capital city, Dhaka. The area is filled with lush greenery, scenic nature spots, historical landmarks, and cultural attractions. The district is home to the famous Bhawal National Park, which is a popular tourist spot for nature lovers. Gazipur is also known for its local markets, delicious food, and traditional handicrafts.

List of Places to Visit in Gazipur

Below are some of the top places to visit in Gazipur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhawal Rajbari: Bhawal Rajbari is a historical palace located in Gazipur. It is a great example of traditional Bengali architecture and is surrounded by beautiful gardens.

  • Tongi Bridge: Tongi Bridge is a historical bridge in Gazipur that was built during the British colonial period. It is a popular spot for photographers and history enthusiasts.

  • Shahid Zia Memorial Complex: This complex is a tribute to Ziaur Rahman, a former president of Bangladesh. It includes a museum, library, and research center.

Natural Attractions of Gazipur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bhawal National Park: The Bhawal National Park is a protected area of lush forests, rolling hills, and diverse wildlife. It is perfect for hiking, bird-watching, and nature photography.

  • Joydebpur Lake: Joydebpur Lake is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and picnicking. The lake is surrounded by greenery and has a tranquil atmosphere.

  • Bijoypur River: The Bijoypur River is a picturesque spot to relax and take in the natural scenery. It is also a great place for river rafting and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gazipur

Gazipur offers several scenic routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular trails include:

  • Bhawal National Park Trail: This trail takes visitors through the lush forests of the Bhawal National Park. It is a great spot for bird-watching and wildlife spotting.

  • Sreepur Trail: The Sreepur Trail is a scenic biking route that takes riders through rural areas and traditional villages.

  • Bhawal Garh Trail: This trail takes visitors through the Bhawal Rajbari and its surrounding gardens. It is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a morning jog.

Best Things to Do in Gazipur: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Gazipur, including:

  • Bhawal National Park Safari: The Bhawal National Park offers a guided jeep safari through the park. Visitors can spot various wildlife including tigers, deer, and monkeys.

  • Bhawal Rajbari Tour: The Bhawal Rajbari offers tours of the historic palace and its gardens. Visitors can learn about the history and architecture of the palace.

  • Handicrafts Shopping: Gazipur is famous for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, handloom textiles, and jute products. Visitors can explore the local markets and shops to buy souvenirs and gifts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gazipur

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Gazipur:

Day 1: Visit Bhawal National Park Day 2: Explore Bhawal Rajbari and its surrounding gardens Day 3: Take a guided safari of Bhawal National Park Day 4: Discover local handicrafts at the markets Day 5: Visit Tongi Bridge and Shahid Zia Memorial Complex Day 6: Take a leisurely bike tour of the rural areas Day 7: Relax by Joydebpur Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Gazipur

Gazipur is a foodie’s paradise with plenty of delicious local dishes and beverages to try. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pitha: Pitha is a traditional Bengali savory or sweet snack that is often made with rice or wheat flour.

  • Fuchka: Fuchka is a popular street food in Bangladesh, which is similar to Indian Pani Puri. It is a crispy shell filled with spicy potato and chickpeas.

  • Dhakaiya Biriyani: Dhakaiya Biriyani is a spicy and flavorful rice dish that is popular in Dhaka and other parts of Bangladesh.

Local Beverages

  • Borhani: Borhani is a traditional spiced yogurt drink that is popular in Bangladesh. It is often served with heavy meals to aid digestion.

  • Cha: Cha is a popular tea in Bangladesh, often served with milk and sugar. It is a staple beverage in the country and is served in many local tea stalls.

  • Lassi: Lassi is a sweet and creamy yogurt drink that is often flavored with fruit or spices. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

  • Dhaka Regency Hotel & Resort: Dhaka Regency is a luxury hotel located near the airport. It offers world-class amenities and elegant interiors.

  • Hotel Frankfurt: Hotel Frankfurt is a budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • Green Hill Guest House: Green Hill Guest House is a mid-range guesthouse that offers spacious rooms and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Gazipur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gazipur is famous for its traditional handicrafts, which make great souvenirs and gifts. Some popular items to buy include:

  • Pottery: Gazipur is known for its handcrafted pottery, which comes in various shapes and sizes.

  • Handloom Textiles: Handloom textiles are a popular product in Gazipur, often made with natural fibers and dyes.

  • Jute Products: Jute products, including bags, mats, and baskets, are also popular in Gazipur and can make unique and useful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Gazipur

Bangladesh has a predominantly Muslim population, and Gazipur reflects this cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes and religious practices. Women and men should dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and visitors should avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Gazipur

The best time to visit Gazipur is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer months from March to May can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season from June to October can bring heavy rains and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gazipur

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Try the delicious local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection

  • Ignore local customs and traditions

  • Travel alone at night in remote areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Gazipur

Gazipur is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night. Crime rates are low, but there have been instances of theft and pickpocketing. Visitors should also be cautious when using public transportation, as accidents and traffic can be a hazard.

Health in Gazipur

Visitors should take precautions to avoid illness and disease, including drinking bottled water and practicing good hygiene. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria are prevalent in Bangladesh, so travelers should take precautions to avoid bites.

Getting to Gazipur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gazipur

The nearest airport to Gazipur is Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport, which is located in Dhaka, approximately 34 kilometers south of Gazipur. The airport offers domestic and international flights to major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Gazipur

Gazipur has a central bus station that offers buses to Dhaka and other cities in Bangladesh.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or hire a private car to reach Gazipur from Dhaka.

How to Get Around in Gazipur

The most convenient way to get around Gazipur is by hired car or taxi. There are also public buses and rickshaws available. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to explore the area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Gazipur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gazipur is a great base for exploring other parts of Bangladesh. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Dhaka: Dhaka is the capital city of Bangladesh and is filled with historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and delicious food.

  • Sonargaon: Sonargaon is an ancient city located near Dhaka. It is known for its beautiful architecture and historic sites.

  • Comilla: Comilla is a historic city located east of Dhaka. It is home to several Buddhist archaeological sites and is a great place to learn about ancient Bengali history.

Conclusion

Gazipur is a beautiful and diverse district in Bangladesh, with plenty to offer visitors. From natural attractions and outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and traditional handicrafts, Gazipur has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or discover the rich history and culture of Bangladesh, Gazipur is a must-visit destination.
