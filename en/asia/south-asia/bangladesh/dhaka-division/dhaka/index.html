Dhaka, the capital of Bangladesh, is a bustling metropolis with a rich history and cultural heritage. The city has a lot to offer to travelers, from its vibrant markets and delicious cuisine to its awe-inspiring historical sites and natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through everything that you need to know to plan your trip to Dhaka.

Introduction to Dhaka

Dhaka is located in the Dhaka Division, in the heart of Bangladesh. With a population of over 21 million people, it is one of the most densely populated cities in the world. The city has a rich and vibrant culture, with influences from Mughal, British, and Bengali traditions. The official language is Bengali, although many people also speak English.

List of Places to Visit in Dhaka

When it comes to places to visit in Dhaka, there is something for everyone. From historical sites and museums to scenic parks and cultural attractions, the city has a lot to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit in Dhaka:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ahsan Manzil: Ahsan Manzil is an iconic palace and museum that dates back to the 19th century. It was home to the Nawabs of Dhaka and is now a major cultural and historical site.
  • Bangladesh National Museum: The Bangladesh National Museum is the largest museum in the country and houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the cultural heritage of Bangladesh.
  • Lalbagh Fort: Lalbagh Fort is an impressive Mughal-era fort that was built in the 17th century. The fort is surrounded by a scenic moat and has a beautiful mosque, a tomb, and a hammam inside.

Natural Attractions of Dhaka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baldha Garden: Baldha Garden is a beautiful botanical garden that was established in the early 20th century. It has a stunning collection of exotic plants and trees, including over 600 species of roses.
  • Suhrawardy Udyan: Suhrawardy Udyan is a scenic park that is located near the Dhaka University campus. It has a large lake, walking paths, and beautiful gardens, making it a perfect spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dhaka

Dhaka is not known for its hiking and biking trails. However, there are some scenic routes that you can explore on foot or by bike. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Hatirjheel Lake: Hatirjheel Lake is a man-made lake that is surrounded by scenic walking and biking paths. The lake is located in the heart of the city and is a popular spot for exercise and recreation.
  • Dhaka University Campus: The Dhaka University Campus is a sprawling green space that has several trails and walking paths. It also has some picturesque ponds and lakes that you can explore.

Best Things to Do in Dhaka: Activities and Tours

Dhaka has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Dhaka:

  • Explore Old Dhaka: Old Dhaka is a historic neighborhood that is known for its narrow alleyways and colorful markets. You can take a guided tour of the area and learn more about its rich cultural heritage.
  • Take a River Cruise: Dhaka is located on the banks of the Buriganga River, and a river cruise is a great way to explore the city from a unique perspective. You can enjoy stunning views of the city skyline and take in the sights and sounds of life on the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dhaka

If you are planning a week-long trip to Dhaka, here is a suggested itinerary that you can follow:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Old Dhaka and visit Ahsan Manzil and Lalbagh Fort
Day 2 Visit the Bangladesh National Museum and Baldha Garden
Day 3 Take a river cruise and visit the Botanical Garden

Day 5 Shop at the markets of New Market and Gulshan
Day 6 Go on a food tour of Dhaka and try the local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Dhaka

Dhaka has a vibrant food and drink scene with plenty of local delicacies to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: Biryani is a flavorful rice dish that is made with meat or vegetables and a blend of spices.
  • Kacchi biryani: Kacchi biryani is a type of biryani that is made with marinated meat and fragrant rice.
  • Pitha: Pitha is a type of sweet or savory cake that is made with rice flour and various fillings.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: Lassi is a yogurt-based drink that is flavored with sugar and spices.
  • Cha: Cha is a spiced tea that is served with milk and sugar.
  • Borhani: Borhani is a yogurt-based drink that is flavored with mint, ginger, and other spices.

Where to Stay in Dhaka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dhaka has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. From luxurious hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses, you can find something that fits your needs. Some of the best places to stay in Dhaka include:

  • Pan Pacific Sonargaon Dhaka: This is a luxury hotel that is located in the heart of the city. It has world-class amenities and is perfect for business and leisure travelers.
  • The Westin Dhaka: This is another high-end hotel that has stunning views of the city skyline. It has a range of amenities for business and leisure travelers.
  • Backpacker’s Hostel: This is a budget-friendly hostel that is located in the heart of Old Dhaka. It is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Dhaka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dhaka is known for its handicrafts and textiles. Some of the best things to buy in Dhaka include:

  • Handmade textiles: Dhaka is famous for its handloomed textiles, including saris, scarves, and shawls.
  • Pottery: Pottery is another popular handicraft in Dhaka, with many skilled artisans creating unique pieces.
  • Leather goods: Bangladesh has a thriving leather industry, and you can find high-quality leather products in Dhaka.

Local Culture and Customs of Dhaka

Bangladesh has a rich and diverse culture that is heavily influenced by Islam and Bengali traditions. Some important customs to keep in mind when visiting Dhaka include:

  • Dress Modestly: Bangladesh is a conservative country, and it is recommended that you dress modestly when visiting religious sites and other public places.
  • Remove Your Shoes: It is customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a place of worship.
  • Respect for Elders: Bangladesh places a high value on respect for elders, so it is important to address people who are older than you with the proper titles and respect.

Best Time to Visit Dhaka

The best time to visit Dhaka is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry. The monsoon season runs from June to September, and the city is prone to flooding during this time. The summers can be hot and humid, with temperatures reaching up to 40°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dhaka

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Dhaka:

Do:

  • Carry Plenty of Sunscreen: Dhaka can get very hot and humid during the summers, and it is important to protect your skin from the harsh sunlight.
  • Take Off Your Shoes: It is customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a place of worship.
  • Be Polite and Respectful: Bangladesh places a high value on politeness and respect, so it is important to be mindful of your behavior when interacting with locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: It is not safe to drink tap water in Dhaka, so be sure to stick to bottled water.
  • Ignore Traffic Rules: The traffic in Dhaka can be chaotic, so it is important to be mindful of traffic rules and regulations for your own safety.
  • Say No to Street Food: While Dhaka has a vibrant street food scene, it is important to be careful about what you eat to avoid getting sick.

Safety and Health

When it comes to safety and health, there are some things to keep in mind when visiting Dhaka.

Safety in Dhaka

  • Be Vigilant: Dhaka is known for its high crime rate, so it is important to be vigilant and take precautions when traveling around the city.
  • Avoid Crowded Areas: Avoid crowded areas, as they can be a target for pickpockets and other thieves.
  • Hire a Guide: It is recommended to hire a guide when exploring Dhaka, as they can help you navigate the city safely.

Health in Dhaka

  • Get Vaccinated: It is important to get vaccinated for common diseases such as hepatitis A and typhoid before traveling to Dhaka.
  • Avoid Tap Water: To avoid getting sick, it is important to stick to bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Be Careful About What You Eat: Be careful about what you eat, especially street food, to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Dhaka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dhaka is well-connected to the rest of the world by air, and there are several options for getting around the city.

Airports Near Dhaka

  • Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport: This is the main airport in Dhaka and is located about 20 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Dhaka

  • Kamalapur Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Dhaka and has services to many parts of the country.

Other Options

  • Rickshaws and Taxis: Rickshaws and taxis are readily available in Dhaka and are a popular way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Dhaka

Getting around in Dhaka can be challenging due to the traffic and congestion. Here are some options for getting around the city:

  • Rickshaws: Rickshaws are a popular and affordable way to get around Dhaka, especially in the narrow alleyways of Old Dhaka.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Dhaka, and you can easily find them on the streets or from your hotel.
  • Buses: Local buses are a cheap and efficient way to get around Dhaka, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Dhaka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Dhaka, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Sundarbans National Park: Sundarbans National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is located about 300 kilometers from Dhaka. It is home to the world’s largest mangrove forest and is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.
  • Cox’s Bazar: Cox’s Bazar is a coastal town that is located about 400 kilometers from Dhaka. It has the longest natural beach in the world and is a popular tourist destination.
  • Srimangal: Srimangal is a small town that is located about 180 kilometers from Dhaka. It is known for its lush tea gardens and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Dhaka is a vibrant and exciting city that has a lot to offer to travelers. From its rich culture and history to its stunning natural beauty, there is something for everyone in this bustling metropolis. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to Dhaka and explore everything that this amazing city has to offer.
