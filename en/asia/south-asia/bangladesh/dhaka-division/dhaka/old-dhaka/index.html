Introduction to Old Dhaka

Old Dhaka is a historical and cultural district located in the heart of the bustling city of Dhaka, the capital of Bangladesh. It was founded during the Mughal Empire in the 17th century and has since been an important trade and commerce center, attracting merchants from all over the world. Old Dhaka offers a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture and boasts a rich cultural heritage that has been preserved despite the rapid development of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Old Dhaka

There are many places to visit in the Old Dhaka district, from historical landmarks to natural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ahsan Manzil: The former residence of the Nawab of Dhaka, this stunning white palace reflects the architectural style of the 19th century.
  • Lalbagh Fort: A Mughal-era fort in Old Dhaka. The fort encompasses a mosque, the tomb of Pari Bibi and Audience Hall of the Nawab.
  • Hindu Street: One of the oldest streets in Old Dhaka, it’s a narrow alley packed with shops, stalls and street food. The street is named after the nearby Dhakeshwari Temple, a Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga.

Natural Attractions of Old Dhaka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sadarghat Port: The busiest and largest river port in Bangladesh, located on the banks of the Buriganga River. Take a boat ride from Sadarghat and enjoy the scenic views of the Old Dhaka skyline and river life.
  • Shankharibazar Zamindar Bari: A palatial mansion located in Shankhari Bazaar; it served as the residence of the local zamindar or landlord in the past.
  • Hoseni Dalan Mosque: Located on the northwestern side of Old Dhaka, this is the main Shia mosque of Dhaka.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Dhaka

There are many hiking and biking routes throughout Old Dhaka which allow you to explore the area further.

  • Ramna Green: The Ramna Green is a popular park in the city centre of Dhaka. The park is spread over 69.6 acres of land and has a beautiful lake at the center. You can take a relaxing stroll in the park, enjoy a picnic or take a bike ride around the lake.
  • Bangladesh National Botanic Garden: Located in Mirpur, is the largest garden in Bangladesh. It’s a great place to explore the flora and fauna of the area.

Best Things to Do in Old Dhaka: Activities and Tours

Old Dhaka has many activities to offer visitors. Take a look at the best things to do in Old Dhaka:

  • Take a rickshaw ride through the narrow streets of Old Dhaka, which is an authentic way to experience the district and see the beautiful architecture and vibrant culture.
  • Take a boat ride on the Buriganga River and explore the river life of Old Dhaka.
  • Join a cooking class and learn how to cook some of the traditional dishes of Bangladesh.
  • Visit the local markets and shop for traditional handicrafts, clothing and jewelry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old Dhaka

To fully explore the cultural richness of Old Dhaka, a week-long itinerary is recommended. Here is a proposed itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Old Dhaka

Bangladesh has a rich culinary tradition, and Old Dhaka is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biriyani: A famous dish that originated in the Mughal era. The biriyani of Dhaka is especially famous for its spicy and flavorful taste.
  • Nihari: A meat dish that originates from the Indian subcontinent, Nihari is a popular food in Old Dhaka.
  • Chapli Kabab: A traditional Pashtun meat dish that is a popular snack in Old Dhaka.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A traditional yogurt-based drink, which is common in Bangladesh during the summertime.
  • Tea: Tea is a popular drink in Bangladesh, and tea stalls are found on almost every corner.

Where to Stay in Old Dhaka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old Dhaka has a number of options for accommodations, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Pan Pacific Sonargaon Dhaka: A luxurious hotel situated in the heart of the city.
  • Hotel Asia Pacific: A budget-friendly hotel located in the center of Old Dhaka.
  • Barden Hostel: A hip and trendy hostel located in the heart of the city’s cultural district.

What to Buy in Old Dhaka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many souvenirs and local products that visitors can buy to take home as a memento of their visit. Here are some recommendations:

  • Traditional Bengali handloom sarees and fabrics.
  • Handmade jewelry and accessories made by local artisans.
  • Brass and copper trays, plates and lamps.

Local Culture and Customs of Old Dhaka

Old Dhaka is a predominantly Muslim city, and visitors should respect the local culture and customs during their visit. Dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Old Dhaka

The best time to visit Old Dhaka is during the winter season, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Dhaka

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city on foot and get lost in the narrow alleyways.
  • Interact with locals and learn about their culture.

Don’t:

  • Insult the local culture or religion.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Dhaka

Old Dhaka is a safe place for travelers, but as with any city, visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid theft or crime.

Health in Old Dhaka

Visitors should take precautions with food and water, and should drink bottled water or boiled water. Vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, typhoid fever, and tetanus are also recommended before travelling to Bangladesh.

Getting to Old Dhaka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Old Dhaka

Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport is the main international airport in Dhaka, located approximately 12km north of Old Dhaka.

Bus Stations Near Old Dhaka

There are several bus stations in Dhaka, which offers services to almost all parts of the country. The prominent one in old Dhaka is Kamalapur Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Old Dhaka is well connected to the rest of the city through various modes of transportation, including taxis, rickshaws and buses.

How to Get Around in Old Dhaka

Exploring Old Dhaka on foot is the best way to experience the district’s unique charm and see the beautiful architecture and vibrant culture.

Where to Go Next from Old Dhaka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Old Dhaka. Here are some recommendations:

  • Rangamati, a beautiful hill district in southeastern Bangladesh famous for its natural beauty and indigenous communities.
  • Chittagong, a port city located on the southeastern coast of Bangladesh, famous for its beaches and the ancient ruins of the Buddhist Kingdom of Samatata.
  • Srimangal, a small town known as the tea capital of Bangladesh, located in the north-eastern part of the country. It offers scenic views of tea gardens, forests and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Old Dhaka is a cultural and historical treasure trove that offers visitors a unique window into the rich and diverse heritage of Bangladesh. This comprehensive travel guide should help visitors plan a rewarding and memorable trip to this enchanting district. Be respectful of the local customs and take precautions for your own safety and you will have a great experience in Old Dhaka.
