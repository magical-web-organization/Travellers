Introduction to Dhaka Division

Located in the heart of Bangladesh, Dhaka Division is home to the country’s capital city, Dhaka, and is full of historical, cultural, and natural attractions. The region has a rich cultural heritage showcased through its museums, historical monuments, and festivals. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, scenic views, and local food and drink offerings. In this ultimate travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and cultural experiences in Dhaka Division.

List of Places to Visit in Dhaka Division

Here’s a list of the best places to visit in Dhaka Division:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ahsan Manzil: Also known as the Pink Palace, the museum provides a glimpse into the lives of the Nawabs of Dhaka.

  • Bangladesh National Museum: With artifacts from prehistoric times through the British period, the museum is a must-visit for history buffs.

  • Sonargaon: The ancient capital of Bengal is home to ancient ruins, tombs, and mosques.

  • Lalbagh Fort: Built-in the 17th century, it is a Mughal-era palace complex located in the old part of Dhaka city.

Natural Attractions of Dhaka Division: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Srimangal: A scenic hill district famous for its tea gardens, rainforest, and natural beauty.

  • Sundarbans: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its mangrove forests and wildlife, including the royal Bengal tiger.

  • Madhabpur Lake: One of the most beautiful lakes in the region, renowned for its pristine waters and scenic surroundings.

  • Lawachara National Park: A tropical rainforest with unique flora and fauna, including the critically endangered hoolock gibbon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dhaka Division

Dhaka Division offers a variety of trails for hikers and bikers. Some popular routes include:

  • Hiking in Srimangal: The Lawachara National Park provides an excellent opportunity for a nature hike.

  • Biking in Madhabpur: The tranquil countryside of Madhabpur offers a scenic biking route.

Best Things to Do in Dhaka Division: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or food, Dhaka Division has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Old Dhaka Heritage Walk: Explore the colorful streets of Old Dhaka and experience its rich culture and heritage.

  • Tea Tour in Srimangal: Discover the hidden gems of Srimangal’s tea gardens on this guided tour.

  • Boat Safari in Sundarbans: Take a boat ride through the mangrove forests of Sundarbans and spot exotic wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dhaka Division

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Dhaka Division:

Day Activities
1 Arrival in Dhaka, visit Ahsan Manzil and Lalbagh Fort
2 Explore the National Museum and Old Dhaka Heritage Walk
3 Travel to Srimangal, visit tea gardens and Lawachara National Park
4 Visit Madhabpur Lake and trek in surrounding hills
5 Travel to Sundarbans, take boat safari and visit tiger reserve
6 Hike in Sundarbans or relax on the beaches
7 Departure from Dhaka

Food and Drink Scene of Dhaka Division

Food is an essential part of the Dhaka Division experience. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biriyani: A deliciously spicy and aromatic rice dish with meat or vegetables.

  • Pitha: A traditional Bengali dessert made from rice flour and molasses.

  • Haleem: A hearty stew made of lentils, wheat, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Cha (tea): The famous tea is grown in Srimangal and is characterized by its strong flavor and aroma.

  • Borhani: A savory yogurt drink often served with biriyani.

  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink with a sweet or salty taste.

Where to Stay in Dhaka Division: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dhaka Division has a range of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets. Here are some popular choices:

  • Pan Pacific Sonargaon Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Dhaka city.

  • Hotel Green Hill: A budget guesthouse located in the scenic hills of Srimangal.

  • The Hotel Diamond Park: A popular choice for tourists visiting Sundarbans near Khulna.

What to Buy in Dhaka Division: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to take home from Dhaka Division:

  • Tea: The tea from Srimangal is a popular souvenir item.

  • Handloom textiles: Dhaka is famous for hand-woven saris and lungis.

  • Pottery and ceramics: Local artisans produce beautifully crafted earthenware and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Dhaka Division

Bangladesh is a Muslim-majority country, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Dress modestly, remove shoes before entering religious places and homes, and ask permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Dhaka Division

The best time to visit Dhaka Division is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant and dry. The temperatures are mild, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dhaka Division

Do:

  • Try the local food: Bangladesh is famous for its spicy, flavorful cuisine.

  • Respect local customs: Dress modestly, remove shoes before entering religious places, and ask permission before taking photographs.

  • Learn a few words of Bengali: The locals will appreciate the effort to communicate with them.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection: Public displays of affection are not culturally acceptable.

  • Disrespect religious traditions: It’s essential to be respectful of religious traditions and practices.

  • Drink tap water: Stick to drinking bottled water to avoid any health issues.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dhaka Division

Like any major city, Dhaka has some safety concerns, including petty crimes. Visitors should take precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Dhaka Division

Visitors should have necessary vaccinations and carry a mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as Malaria and Dengue fever. It is also essential to drink bottled water and avoid eating street food.

Getting to Dhaka Division: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dhaka Division

  • Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport (DAC): Located in the capital city, Dhaka

Bus Stations Near Dhaka Division

  • Gabtoli Bus Terminal: The central bus terminal for long-distance buses in Dhaka
  • Kalampur Bus Terminal: The station for buses to Srimangal and other northeastern towns.

Other Options

Travelers can also take trains or hire private cars to reach the different destinations within Dhaka Division.

How to Get Around in Dhaka Division

Public transport options in Dhaka Division include buses, trains, auto-rickshaws, and taxis. For exploring the countryside, rented cars, motorbikes, and bicycles are the best options.

Where to Go Next from Dhaka Division: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dhaka Division is well-connected to other regions in Bangladesh, and visitors can plan excursions to the following places:

  • Chittagong: A coastal city famous for its beautiful beaches and hilltop temples.

  • Cox’s Bazar: Known for its expansive beach and vibrant local culture.

  • Bandarban: A hilly area with beautiful waterfalls and trekking trails.

Conclusion

Dhaka Division is a beautiful region full of historical, cultural, and natural wonders. From exploring the tea gardens in Srimangal to experiencing the wildlife of Sundarbans, there is something for every traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan a fantastic trip to Dhaka Division.
