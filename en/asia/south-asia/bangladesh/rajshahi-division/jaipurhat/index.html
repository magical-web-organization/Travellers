Are you looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Bangladesh? Look no further than Jaipurhat, a small town in the Rajshahi Division that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. From museums and historical places to scenic views and outdoor adventures, Jaipurhat has something for every type of traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do, where to eat and stay, and how to get around.

Introduction to Jaipurhat

Jaipurhat is a small town located in the Rajshahi Division, in the northwest region of Bangladesh. Known for its rich history and cultural heritage, Jaipurhat attracts visitors who want to explore its museums, historical sites, and natural beauty.

The town is situated in the middle of a green valley, surrounded by hills and forests. It has a tranquil atmosphere, with friendly locals and low-key tourism. Jaipurhat is easily accessible by road and offers a convenient stopover between Rajshahi to the south and Dinajpur to the north.

List of Places to Visit in Jaipurhat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jaipurhat has a rich heritage that dates back to the Pala Dynasty period. The town was an important center for trade and commerce during the British colonial era. As a result, there are several historically significant sites to explore.

One of the must-visit places in Jaipurhat is the Kantajew Temple, located about 17 km from the town center. This terracotta temple is one of the finest examples of medieval Hindu architecture in Bangladesh. Built in the late 18th century, the temple has intricate carvings and sculptures that depict ancient myths and legends.

Another place of interest in Jaipurhat is the Paharpur Buddhist Vihara, located about 25 km from the town. It is one of the largest and oldest Buddhist monasteries in South Asia, dating back to the 7th century. The site is a UNESCO World Heritage site and attracts visitors from all over the world.

For those interested in local history, the Jaipurhat Museum is a must-visit attraction. The museum features a collection of artifacts that showcase the region’s cultural and historical significance. It has displays on the Pala Dynasty, Hindu and Buddhist art, and local crafts.

Natural Attractions of Jaipurhat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond its cultural attractions, Jaipurhat also boasts several natural wonders that are worth exploring. The town is surrounded by hills, forests, and rivers, providing plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

One of the most popular natural attractions in Jaipurhat is the Madhabpur Lake, located about 8 km from the town. The lake is a serene spot with crystal clear water, surrounded by lush green hills. Visitors can enjoy boating or fishing, or simply take a relaxing walk along the lake’s shoreline.

Another place to take in the natural beauty of Jaipurhat is the Bhubaneshwari Temple, located atop a hill about 5 km from the town. The temple offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for watching the sunset.

For those looking for more adventurous activities, Jaipurhat has plenty of hiking and biking routes. The hills and forests offer challenging terrain for experienced hikers and bikers, as well as more leisurely trails for beginners.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jaipurhat

If you’re an avid hiker or biker, you’ll love the scenic routes around Jaipurhat. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Bholahat Forest Trail: This trail takes you through the dense forest of Bholahat, and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Nandail Forest Trail: This trail takes you through the Nandail Forest, which is home to several rare bird species, including the Rufous-necked Hornbill.

  • Joypurhat Hill Trek: This trek takes you to the top of Joypurhat Hill, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Basu Bihar Trail: This trail takes you to the Basu Bihar, a Buddhist archaeological site that dates back to the 7th century.

Best Things to Do in Jaipurhat: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Jaipurhat has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a rickshaw tour of the town: This is a great way to explore Jaipurhat’s local markets, temples, and historical sites.

  • Attend a local festival or fair: Jaipurhat is known for its colorful festivals, such as the Baruni Mela and the Poush Mela.

  • Go birdwatching: The forests around Jaipurhat are home to several rare bird species, including the Rufous-necked Hornbill and the Red-breasted Parakeet.

  • Visit a local tea garden: Jaipurhat is surrounded by tea gardens, where you can learn about the process of tea-making and enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jaipurhat

To make the most of your trip to Jaipurhat, we recommend this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Jaipurhat town and visit the Jaipurhat Museum.

Day 2: Take a rickshaw tour of the town’s markets and temples.

Day 3: Visit the Kantajew Temple and the Paharpur Buddhist Vihara.

Day 4: Trek to the top of Joypurhat Hill and watch the sunset.

Day 5: Go birdwatching in the Nandail Forest.

Day 6: Visit the Madhabpur Lake and enjoy a boat ride.

Day 7: Relax in a local tea garden and enjoy a cup of tea before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Jaipurhat

No trip to Jaipurhat is complete without sampling the local cuisine. The town has a rich culinary heritage, with a mix of Bengali, Indian, and Mughlai influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mezban: A traditional Bengali feast, featuring a variety of meat dishes, rice, and dal.

  • Phuchka: A popular street food in Bangladesh, featuring fried dough balls filled with spiced potatoes and tamarind water.

  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink, available in sweet or salty variations.

  • Biriyani: A flavorful rice dish made with chicken, lamb, or beef, and aromatic spices.

Local Beverages

  • Cha: Bengali-style tea made with milk, sugar, and spices.

  • Borhani: A yogurt-based drink, flavored with mint, coriander, and cumin.

  • Sugarcane Juice: A refreshing drink made from freshly pressed sugarcane.

  • Hotel Samara: A luxury hotel with spacious rooms and a rooftop restaurant.

  • Hotel Green Hill: A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms and a central location.

  • Hill View Guest House: A budget guesthouse with basic amenities and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Jaipurhat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back from Jaipurhat, here are some ideas:

  • Terracotta art and pottery: Jaipurhat is known for its intricate terracotta art and pottery, which make for unique souvenirs.

  • Tea: Jaipurhat’s tea gardens produce some of the finest tea in Bangladesh, which is sold in local markets.

  • Hand-woven textiles: The region is known for its colorful hand-woven textiles, such as saris and shawls.

Local Culture and Customs of Jaipurhat

Jaipurhat is a friendly and welcoming town, with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting religious sites. It is customary to remove shoes before entering temples and mosques. When greeting locals, it is appropriate to use the Bengali greeting “Salam” or “As-salamu alaykum”.

Best Time to Visit Jaipurhat

The best time to visit Jaipurhat is from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rains and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jaipurhat

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully when visiting religious sites.

  • Remove shoes before entering temples and mosques.

  • Use the Bengali greeting “Salam” or “As-salamu alaykum” when greeting locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Leave trash behind in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jaipurhat

Jaipurhat is a safe town with low levels of crime. Visitors are advised to keep an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded areas. It is also important to follow safety protocols when hiking or biking in the hills and forests around Jaipurhat.

Health in Jaipurhat

Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever, by wearing long-sleeved shirts and applying mosquito repellent. It is also important to drink bottled or boiled water, and to be cautious about food hygiene when eating street food.

Getting to Jaipurhat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jaipurhat is easily accessible by road, with several bus routes connecting it to nearby towns and cities. The nearest airport is in Rajshahi, about 80 km from Jaipurhat. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Jaipurhat

  • Shah Makhdum Airport (RJH): Located in Rajshahi, about 80 km from Jaipurhat.

Bus Stations Near Jaipurhat

  • Jaipurhat Bus Terminal: Located in the town center, with several buses and minibusses connecting to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest railway station is in Santahar, about 45 km from Jaipurhat.

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies offer vehicles for hire in Rajshahi.

How to Get Around in Jaipurhat

The easiest way to get around Jaipurhat is by rickshaw, which is an inexpensive and convenient mode of transportation. Buses and minibusses also connect the town to nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Jaipurhat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring more of the Rajshahi Division, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Rajshahi: The largest city in the division, known for its vibrant markets and historical sites.

  • Dinajpur: A town with several ancient Hindu and Buddhist temples, including the Kantanagar Temple and the Shiva Temple.

Conclusion

Jaipurhat may be a small town, but it has a lot to offer travelers who want to explore the cultural and natural wonders of Bangladesh. From museums and historical sites to scenic views and outdoor adventures, Jaipurhat is a unique and memorable destination.
