Introduction to Natore

Natore is a district in the Rajshahi Division of Bangladesh. This small town is known for its historical and cultural significance, being the birthplace of many prominent figures in Bangladeshi history. Natore is located on the banks of the Karatoya River, which adds to its natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Natore

If you are planning a trip to Natore, here are some places that you should definitely include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Natore Rajbari: This is the most famous attraction in Natore, a historical palace built in the 19th century, which narrates the stories of the rulers of Natore.
  • Natore Museum: This museum is located inside the Rajbari, exhibiting various tools, ornaments, utensils and other items that have historical significance.
  • Bara Kuthi: Once a residence of the chief official of Natore, Bara Kuthi is now a historical museum that is visited by many tourists.

Natural Attractions of Natore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chalan Beel: This natural freshwater wetland is located in the eastern part of the district, covering an area of approximately 26,000 hectares. It is a popular site for birdwatching, particularly during the winter season.
  • Pakshi Forest: Located in the nearby district of Pabna, this dense forest hosts over 300 species of birds, including migratory birds that come to Bangladesh during the winter season.
  • Natore Dighi: This large pond is surrounded by dense forest and provides scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Natore

  • Sapahar Upazila Bike Trail: This 32 km trail starts from Natore Government College and runs through Sapahar Upazila. The trail passes by a number of mango orchards and scenic villages.
  • Bagatipara Upazila Bike Trail: This 40 km trail starts from Natore Town and passes through Bagatipara Upazila before ending at the famous Jamuna River.

Best Things to Do in Natore: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Natore Rajbari: The Rajbari is a must-visit attraction in Natore, showcasing historical buildings, museums, and gardens.
  • Explore the Old Market: Natore’s Old Market is a great place to sample local food, buy souvenirs or simply stroll around the shops.
  • Attend Festivals: Natore is home to a number of festivals, such as the Raas Festival, Chaitra Shankranti and Pahela Baishakh.

Week-Long Itinerary for Natore

Here is a sample itinerary for exploring Natore for a week:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive at Natore and check into your hotel. Visit Natore Rajbari and Museum.
Day 2 Visit Bara Kuthi Museum and explore the Old Market.
Day 3 Spend a day at Chalan Beel or Pakshir Forest.
Day 4 Embark on the Sapahar Upazila Bike Trail.
Day 5 Visit nearby Pabna to see the Hardinge Bridge and Sardar Bari
Day 6 Take a day trip to the nearby Rajshahi and go exploring the temples and gardens
Day 7 Attend local festivals or take some time to shop and relax before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Natore

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chatpati: A spicy and sour dish that typically consists of chickpeas, potatoes, tomatoes, and onions.
  • Biriyani: Bengali-style rice cooked with meat, vegetables and a blend of spices.
  • Pusta polao: A dessert made from rice, milk, sugar, and cardamom.

Local Beverages

  • Borhani: A traditional savory yogurt-based drink that is often served with spicy food.
  • Doi: A popular Bengali yogurt drink
  • Lassi: A traditional Indian yogurt drink that is sometimes served with fruit or spices.

Where to Stay in Natore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Natore Bungalow: A heritage bungalow turned guesthouse that provides a luxurious stay option.
  • Grace Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a homely atmosphere.
  • Hotel Al- Amin: A mid-range hotel in the center of Natore town that offers modern amenities and facilities.

What to Buy in Natore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handloom Sarees: Natore is famous for its handloom sarees woven out of silk or cotton. They come in different designs and patterns, and are a great souvenir item to take home.
  • Metal Crafts: Decorative pieces made out of brass, copper, or silver metal are a popular item in Natore, with exquisite designs and craftwork.
  • Mangoes: Natore is known for its delicious mangoes, and visitors can take home high-quality mangoes from different markets in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Natore

  • Visitors should dress modestly, covering their legs and shoulders when visiting religious or historical sites, and remove their shoes before entering places of worship.
  • Visitors are expected to show respect and politeness towards the locals.
  • Visitors should carry cash or be prepared to find an ATM, as many shops and markets do not accept credit or debit cards.

Best Time to Visit Natore

The best time to visit Natore is between November to February, during which the weather is dry and comfortable. The temperatures are typically between 15-20 degrees Celsius, making it an ideal time for sightseeing and exploring outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Natore

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious or historical sites.
  • Explore the local markets and street food stalls to experience the local cuisine.
  • Take part in local festivals and cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Engage in unauthorized photography
  • Disrespect the local cultures and customs.
  • Consume or purchase illegal drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Natore

Natore is generally considered a safe place, with low crime rates. It is recommended to take precautions such as keeping valuable items in a secure place and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Natore

Visitors are advised to carry insect repellent and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever or malaria. It is also recommended to carry any required medications and visit a doctor before starting the trip.

Getting to Natore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Natore

The nearest airport to Natore is the Shah Mokhdum Airport (RJH) in Rajshahi, located about 50 km from Natore. For those interested in using domestic flights, Dhaka Domestic Airport and Saidpur Domestic Airport serves Dhaka and Saidpur with frequent domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Natore

Natore can be reached from Rajshahi or Dhaka by bus, with frequent bus services available from these locations.

Other Options

Taxis or rental cars are also an option for those who prefer to travel at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Natore

Auto-rickshaws are the most popular and convenient mode of transportation in Natore, and they can take you to most places in the town.

Where to Go Next from Natore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rajshahi: A city located 45 km away from Natore, known for its rich historical and cultural heritage, such as the Varendra Research Museum and Bagha Mosque.
  • Pabna: Another nearby city that is known for its textile industry and the Hardinge Bridge, an iconic cantilever bridge that crosses the Padma River.

Conclusion

Natore is a perfect destination for those who are interested in exploring historical and cultural sites, and enjoy natural beauty. With a vibrant food and drink scene, friendly locals, and a number of activities, Natore is a must-visit location for anyone visiting Bangladesh.
