Introduction to Rajshahi Division

Located in the northern part of Bangladesh, Rajshahi Division is a culturally and naturally rich region known for its classical roots, historical sites, and scenic beauty. It is home to some of the most extraordinary historical landmarks, temples, and mosques in Bangladesh, chronicling the region’s history since ancient times.

Rajshahi Division is also known for its lush green forests, tranquil rivers, and diverse wildlife, providing ample opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and bird watching. This division has a subtropical monsoon climate, with hot summers and mild winters, making it a year-round destination for visitors.

In this tour guide, we will share comprehensive information on places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and how to get around, so you can make the most of your experience in Rajshahi Division.

List of Places to Visit in Rajshahi Division

Here are our recommended culturally and naturally important attractions in Rajshahi Division:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Puthia Rajbari Complex

Also known as the Palace of Puthia, this complex is one of the most impressive pieces of architecture in Bangladesh. The complex features a cluster of five Hindu temples dating back to the 18th century, built in a unique blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture, making it a must-see for history and architecture lovers.

Mosque City of Bagerhat

Bagerhat is an ancient port city filled with over 50 Islamic monuments, including mosques and shrines that date back to the 15th century. The most impressive of them all is the 60-domed Mosque, which boasts two giant stone elephants at its entrance.

Mahasthangarh

This ancient archaeological site dates back over 2500 years and was once the capital of the ancient Pundra Kingdom. Today, visitors can explore the site’s extensive ruins including those of the city walls, the Citadel, and the residential and administrative areas.

Natural Attractions of Rajshahi Division: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sundarban Mangrove Forest

Sundarban is the world’s largest mangrove forest and is home to the prized Bengal Tiger, as well as many other rare wildlife species. Visitors can explore the forest by boat, spotting tigers, crocodiles, and many other animals along the way.

Padma River

Known as ‘The Mighty Padma,’ this river is the fourth-largest river in the world, and the most important river of Bangladesh’s economy. Visitors can take a breathtaking river cruise with local tour operators, admiring the scenic views of rural Bangladesh, and catching a glimpse of locals going about their daily lives.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rajshahi Division

Chalan Beel- Hasna Bil-Madhurapur Trail

This easy hiking route is perfect for nature lovers looking to explore the beautiful freshwater wetlands of Chalan Beel. Start from Hasna Bil and end at Madhurapur, passing by remote villages, wetlands, and beautiful lakes.

Bike Tour Along the River

Padma

This scenic bike route is perfect for those looking to experience rural Bangladesh from a different perspective. Start from Paksey Ghat and end at Charabari Ghat, taking in the beautiful views of the Padma River along the way.

Best Things to Do in Rajshahi Division: Activities and Tours

Experience a Local Riverboat Journey

Take a traditional Bangladeshi boat ride along the mighty Padma River, experiencing the unique sights and sounds of the local life.

Visit the Village Homesteads

Visit traditional rural homes in the surrounding areas of Rajshahi Division, experiencing the flavour of traditional Bangladeshi village life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rajshahi Division

Here’s a 7-day itinerary for Rajshahi Division:

  1. Day 1: Arrival in Rajshahi Division followed by a visit to the Bagerhat World Heritage site to experience its varied mosque architecture and the historic graveyards.
  2. Day 2: Visit one of the many temples in Puthia Rajbari Complex.
  3. Day 3: Visit the impressive Mahasthangarh ruins of the age-old Citadel, the city walls, and the residential and administrative areas of the ancient city.
  4. Day 4: Enjoy a scenic riverboat cruise along the Padma River or take the Chalan Beel- Hasna Bil-Madhurapur Trail.
  5. Day 5: Explore local village homesteads and cuisine in the surrounding rural areas of Rajshahi Division.
  6. Day 6: Visit the beautiful Sundarban Mangrove Forest and see the mighty Bengal Tiger.
  7. Day 7: Departure from Rajshahi Division

Food and Drink Scene of Rajshahi Division

Must-Try Dishes

Bhapa Chingri

This traditional Bangladeshi dish comprises of tiger prawn mixed with curd, green chili, coriander leaves, and mustard oil, wrapped in a banana leaf and steamed to perfection.

Roshgolla

This famous Bengali dessert comprises of delicately prepared soft sponge cake made from cottage cheese, soaked in light syrup, creating a delectable combination of sweetness and texture.

Local Beverages

Lassi

A traditional drink made from a combination of yogurt, sugar, and fruits like mango or banana, this drink is great for keeping cool on hot summer days.

Cha

A staple drink of Bangladesh, cha or tea, is a must-try while visiting Rajshahi Division. It is typically served in small cups with milk and sugar and sipped during tea-time conversations.

Where to Stay in Rajshahi Division: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rajshahi Division offers a range of staying options for all types of travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

What to Buy in Rajshahi Division: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silk and Cotton Sarees

Rajshahi is renowned for its vibrant silk and cotton sarees, making them a popular souvenir item for tourists.

Traditional Handicrafts

There are many local villages around Rajshahi Division where traditional handicrafts like wood carvings and basketry are created, making them another great souvenir option.

Local Culture and Customs of Rajshahi Division

Bengali culture and customs are a prominent part of Rajshahi Division’s identity. Islam is the predominant religion in the area, and the Bengali language is the most widely spoken. Rajshahi Division is a friendly and hospitable region, so visitors should prepare to be greeted with warmth and hospitality during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Rajshahi Division

October to March is a great time to visit Rajshahi Division, as the weather is mild during these months, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rajshahi Division

Do:

  • Cover up when visiting religious and cultural sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes before entering mosques, temples, or homes.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public.
  • Wear revealing clothing outside of tourist resorts.
  • Disrespect local religious or cultural practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rajshahi Division

It is generally safe for visitors to travel to Rajshahi Division. However, visitors should avoid visiting areas prone to political unrest and should be mindful of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Rajshahi Division

Visitors should take precautions to avoid getting sick while in Rajshahi Division by drinking bottled water, eating at reputable establishments, and carrying bug spray to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Rajshahi Division: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rajshahi Division

Shah Makhdum Airport in Rajshahi is the closest airport to Rajshahi Division.

Bus Stations Near Rajshahi Division

Dhaka is the closest major city with frequent bus service to Rajshahi Division.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Rajshahi Division by train or boat.

How to Get Around in Rajshahi Division

Rajshahi Division is best explored by private car or by hiring a local driver who can guide you throughout your journey. Visitors can also take public transportation like buses or rickshaws, but they may not be as reliable or convenient as other options.

Where to Go Next from Rajshahi Division: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dhaka

The capital city of Bangladesh, Dhaka, is easily accessible from Rajshahi Division and offers visitors a metropolis experience, bustling markets, and the essential local cuisine experience.

Srimangal

Srimangal is a picturesque town known for its spectacular tea plantations, making it an ideal retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Conclusion

Rajshahi Division is a region of Bangladesh filled with rich cultural and natural heritage that visitors can enjoy. From the lush forests, tranquil rivers, and diverse wildlife, to the historic landmarks and local cuisine, there’s always something for everyone. We hope this guide has been helpful and that you enjoy your visit to Rajshahi Division!
