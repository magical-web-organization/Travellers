Introduction to Naogaon District

Naogaon District is located in the Rajshahi Division of Bangladesh, in South Asia. The district is known for its historical and cultural significance, as well as its natural beauty. The district is also home to several universities, making it a hub for education in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Naogaon District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kantaji Temple: a stunning 18th-century Hindu temple
  • Paharpur Buddhist Monastery: an ancient Buddhist monument dating back to the 8th century
  • Naogaon Rajbari: a beautiful palace built during the British Raj era
  • Mahasthangarh: a historical site from the 3rd century BC with ruins of ancient civilizations.

Natural Attractions of Naogaon District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tista Barrage: a scenic spot on the Teesta River
  • Dhopapur Lake: a beautiful lake for boating and fishing
  • Ranibari Forest: a dense forest with beautiful walking trails
  • Bijoypur Bill: a birdwatcher’s paradise with many rare species to spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naogaon District

  • Bhati Falls: a beautiful waterfall with hiking trails
  • Paharpur to Mahasthangarh: a 20 km biking route with historical significance
  • Sareng Naogaon Trek: a 10 km trek in the Ranibari Forest

Best Things to Do in Naogaon District: Activities and Tours

  • Visit local markets: Naogaon is famous for its handmade pottery, textiles, and mangoes
  • Take a rickshaw ride around town: a great way to experience local life
  • Attend a local festival: Naogaon celebrates Pahela Baishakh, Eid-ul-Fitr, and Durga Puja with great fervor.
  • Take a riverboat ride: explore the Teesta River by boat

Week-Long Itinerary for Naogaon District

Day 1: Visit Kantaji Temple and Paharpur Buddhist Monastery Day 2: Go hiking in the Ranibari Forest and visit the Naogaon Rajbari Day 3: Explore the local markets and experience local life Day 4: Take a riverboat ride on the Teesta River Day 5: Visit the ancient ruins of Mahasthangarh Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at Dhopapur Lake Day 7: Attend a local festival and experience the culture firsthand

Food and Drink Scene of Naogaon District

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mango pickle: Naogaon is known for its delicious mangoes, and the pickle is a popular condiment
  • Bhuna khichuri: a local dish made with rice, lentils, and spices
  • Mishti Doi: a sweet yogurt dessert popular in Bangladesh
  • Luchi: a type of fried bread, often enjoyed with curry.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: the local drink of choice, enjoyed multiple times a day
  • Borhani: a savory and spicy drink made with yogurt and herbs
  • Mango lassi: a classic drink made with mango puree and yogurt
  • Sugarcane juice: a refreshing drink made from freshly squeezed sugar cane.

Where to Stay in Naogaon District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Comfort: a modern hotel with excellent amenities
  • Hotel Shahin International: a budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms
  • Hotel Rajmahal: a luxury option with an on-site restaurant and pool.

What to Buy in Naogaon District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: Naogaon has a long tradition of pottery-making, and the local artisans produce beautiful pieces
  • Textiles: the district is known for its woven fabric, including sarees, lungis, and gamchhas
  • Mangoes: Naogaon produces some of the best mangoes in the country, and they make an excellent gift to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Naogaon District

The people of Naogaon are friendly and welcoming, and the local culture is strongly influenced by Hindu and Buddhist traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and remove their shoes before entering. It’s also important to greet people respectfully and ask for permission before taking photos.

Best Time to Visit Naogaon District

The best time to visit Naogaon District is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. The monsoon season, between June and September, sees heavy rains and high humidity, making it difficult to explore the district.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naogaon District

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering religious sites
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Greet people respectfully
  • Explore the local markets

Don’t:

  • Show disrespect towards religion or culture
  • Wear revealing clothing to religious sites
  • Take photos without permission
  • Litter or damage natural sites
  • Ignore safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naogaon District

Naogaon is generally a safe district to visit, however, it’s important to take normal precautions to avoid theft or other crimes. Keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night.

Health in Naogaon District

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Naogaon. It’s also important to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. Travelers should drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food.

Getting to Naogaon District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naogaon District

The nearest airport is in Rajshahi, which is about 53 km away from Naogaon. From Rajshahi, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Naogaon.

Bus Stations Near Naogaon District

Naogaon has a well-connected bus station, with frequent buses to other major cities in Bangladesh, including Dhaka, Chittagong, and Sylhet.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Rajshahi and then take a taxi or bus to Naogaon.

How to Get Around in Naogaon District

The best way to get around Naogaon District is via auto-rickshaw or bicycle. Taxis are also available, but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Naogaon District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rajshahi: known for its silk industry and beautiful architecture
  • Puthia: a small town with several impressive Hindu temples
  • Bogra: home to the ancient ruins of Mahasthangarh
  • Dinajpur: a bustling university town with a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Naogaon District offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its ancient archaeological sites, beautiful hiking and biking trails, and delicious cuisine, Naogaon is a must-visit destination for any traveler interested in Bangladesh.
