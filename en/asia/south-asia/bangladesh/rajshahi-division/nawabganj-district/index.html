Introduction to Nawabganj District

Located in the Rajshahi Division of Bangladesh, Nawabganj District is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This district is known for its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and friendly locals. With a wide range of cultural, natural, and outdoor activities, it is an ideal destination for travelers looking to experience the authentic side of Bangladesh.

List of Places to Visit in Nawabganj District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shrine of Shah Sultan Balkhi Mahisawar - This is a famous shrine of a Sufi saint located in Khaja Hafiz Uddin.
  • Jagannath Temple, Baromilechori - A historic Hindu temple located in Baromilechori, Nawabganj.
  • Mound of Behula, Jhopgari - An ancient site of the Behula-Lakhindar story, situated in Jhopgari of Rohanpur Upazila.
  • Aman Jang Museum - A museum dedicated to the Bengal Renaissance and Nawab Siraj ud-Daulah, situated in Balia of Gomastapur Upazila.

Natural Attractions of Nawabganj District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ramsagar Lake - This man-made lake is a great place for a relaxing boat ride and to enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Mahasthan Garh - A historic archaeological site in Mahasthan of Shibganj Upazila.
  • Bagha Mosque - A beautiful mosque located in the Bagha Upazila.
  • Goddes Haripur Kali Temple - Another historic and famous temple located in Haripur Upazila.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nawabganj District

  • Barind Hill - A moderately challenging hike, this hill offers breathtaking views of the district from the top.
  • Sundarban - This is the world’s largest mangrove forest, located around 250 kilometers south of Nawabganj. It is an ideal place for an adventurous bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Nawabganj District: Activities and Tours

  • River Cruising - Take a boat ride down the Padma river while enjoying stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bird Watching - Nawabganj District is home to a diverse range of bird species, including Bangladesh’s national bird, the Oriental Magpie-Robin.
  • Fishing - The district’s water bodies are the perfect spots for a relaxing fishing trip.
  • Heritage Tours - Explore the rich cultural heritage of Nawabganj by visiting its temples, mosques, and museums.

Food and Drink Scene of Nawabganj District

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhuna Khichuri - A delicious rice and lentil dish, made with a variety of spices and served with meat or fish.
  • Kosha Mangsho - A slow-cooked mutton curry, filled with spices and flavors.
  • Pitha - A sweet dessert made of rice flour, coconut milk and jaggery.

Local Beverages

  • Khejurer Gur - A traditional Bangladeshi beverage made from palm sugar.
  • Lassi - A refreshing yogurt-based drink, often served with fruit.

Where to Stay in Nawabganj District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pan Pacific Sonargaon Hotel - A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Dhaka, with easy access to Nawabganj District.
  • Hotel Momo Inn - A comfortable hotel located in the city center of Nawabganj.
  • Chandrakona Government Guesthouse - A budget accommodation option, with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Nawabganj District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade baskets - Made by local artisans, these baskets are perfect for storing fruits, vegetables or household items.
  • Traditional handicrafts - From embroidered wall hangings to hand-painted pottery, Nawabganj is a treasure trove of local handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Nawabganj District

The people of Nawabganj District are known for their warm hospitality, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms. Respectful behavior and dressing modestly are important to the locals, especially when visiting religious sites. While tipping is not mandatory, it is appreciated and considered a sign of good manners.

Best Time to Visit Nawabganj District

The best time to visit Nawabganj District is during the winter months, from November to February. The weather during this time is cool and pleasant, making it perfect for exploring the district’s many attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nawabganj District

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Carry small denominations of money for small purchases.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Take photos without permission
  • Litter in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Nawabganj District

Nawabganj District is considered a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always a good idea to take standard safety precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on personal belongings.

Health in Nawabganj District

Before traveling to Nawabganj District, it is recommended to consult with a doctor and get necessary vaccinations. Travelers should also take precautions against water-borne illnesses by drinking only filtered or bottled water and avoiding raw vegetables and fruits.

How to Get Around in Nawabganj District

Taxis, rental cars, and motorbikes are all available for hire in Nawabganj District. However, the most common mode of transportation is the local bus network, which is efficient and affordable.

Where to Go Next from Nawabganj District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rajshahi City - The capital of the Rajshahi Division, known for its ancient architecture, museums, and bustling bazaars.
  • Sundarban - The world’s largest mangrove forest, located south of Nawabganj
  • Chittagong Hill Tracts - A mountainous region full of natural beauty, diverse culture and ethnic communities.

Conclusion

Nawabganj District is a destination full of surprises and hidden gems, waiting to be discovered by intrepid travelers. From its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery to its delicious cuisine and friendly locals, this district offers something for everyone. So, pack your bags and head out to explore the best that Nawabganj District has to offer.
