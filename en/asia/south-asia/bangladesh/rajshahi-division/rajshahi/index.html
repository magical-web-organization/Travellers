Introduction to Rajshahi

Welcome to Rajshahi, a city in the northwestern part of Bangladesh that boasts a rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and historical treasures. Rajshahi is the capital city of the Rajshahi Division and is a bustling metropolis filled with exciting activities and enchanting sights.

List of Places to Visit in Rajshahi

Rajshahi offers something for everyone, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts. Here is a list of the top places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Varendra Research Museum: Housing the largest collection of ancient sculptures in Bangladesh, this museum is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  • Puthia Temple Complex: This complex houses a vast collection of historically significant temples, including the Govinda Temple, the Jagannath Temple, and the Shiva Temple.
  • Bagha Mosque: Built in the 16th century, the Bagha Mosque is one of the oldest surviving Islamic structures in Bangladesh.

Natural Attractions of Rajshahi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chalan Beel: This beautiful wetland is home to a multitude of flora and fauna and is the perfect spot for birdwatching and boating.
  • Rajshahi University Campus: The beautiful campus is surrounded by lush greenery and is a serene escape from the bustle of the city.
  • Barind Tract: This unique landscape is dominated by red soil and is home to a variety of rare plant species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rajshahi

If you are seeking an adventurous escape from the city, be sure to explore the following hiking and biking routes in Rajshahi:

  • Tista Barrage Cycling Trail: Enjoy a scenic ride along the Tista River and take in the beautiful views of the lush forests and farmland along the way.
  • Padma River Island Hopping: Take a boat ride along the Padma River and explore the picturesque islands that dot its waters.
  • Bholahat Upazila Trekking: This trek will take you through green hills that serve as one of the primary sources of water in Rajshahi.

Best Things to Do in Rajshahi: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Rajshahi offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do when in the city:

  • Visit a local bazaar: Be sure to wander through Rajshahi’s colorful bazaars, filled with local handicrafts, food, and other goods.
  • Attend a cultural event: Visit during the annual Lalon Mela or Boishakhi Mela festivals for a taste of the local culture and music.
  • Take a river cruise: Enjoy a leisurely boat ride along the Padma River and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Rajshahi

The food scene in Rajshahi is a treat for the senses, with a variety of flavors and spices to explore. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mishti Doi: A sweet, creamy yogurt-based dessert that is a popular treat in Rajshahi.
  • Bhapa Pitha: A steamed rice cake that is often served with spicy chutney.
  • Kalabhuna: A delicious meat-based curry that is best enjoyed with naan or paratha.

Local Beverages

  • Borhani: A salty, tangy beverage made from yogurt, spices, and mint.
  • Lassi: A thick, creamy beverage made from yogurt and sugar.

Where to Stay in Rajshahi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rajshahi offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Parjatan Hotel Rajshahi: A budget-friendly option located near the city center.
  • Hotel Nice International: A mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.
  • Grand Sultan Tea Resort & Golf: A luxurious resort that features a golf course, swimming pool, and spa.

What to Buy in Rajshahi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to take home a piece of Rajshahi, be sure to check out the local bazaars for the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Silk Saris: Rajshahi is known for its high-quality silk saris, which make for a stunning souvenir.
  • Terracotta Pottery: The Puthia Temple Complex is home to some of the best terracotta pottery in Bangladesh.
  • Mangoes: Rajshahi is known for its delicious mangoes and is a must-buy for anyone visiting the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Rajshahi

Rajshahi has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Bengali, Mughal, and British influences. The locals are friendly, hospitable, and welcoming to visitors. It is important to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Rajshahi

The best time to visit Rajshahi is between October and February, when the weather is cool and pleasant. Avoid the monsoon season from June to August, as heavy rain can make travel challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rajshahi

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Rajshahi:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Haggle when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Use your left hand for eating or handling money, as it is considered impolite.
  • Consume tap water, as it may not be safe for drinking.
  • Take photos without permission, especially of locals.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are essential when traveling to Rajshahi. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Rajshahi

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote or poorly lit areas.
  • Keep valuables secured and hidden from plain sight.
  • Use only licensed taxis or public transportation.

Health in Rajshahi

  • Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing to prevent mosquito bites, as dengue fever is a common health risk in Bangladesh.
  • Avoid street food and drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Take precautions when swimming, especially in open water.

Getting to Rajshahi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rajshahi is easily accessible by air, bus or train. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Rajshahi

  • Shah Makhdum Airport, located in the city of Rajshahi, is the closest airport and is serviced by several domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Rajshahi

  • Rajshahi Bus Terminal is located on Natore Road and offers regular bus services to other major cities in Bangladesh.

Other Options

  • Train services run regularly from Dhaka to Rajshahi and take approximately six hours.

How to Get Around Rajshahi

Getting around Rajshahi is relatively easy, with several transportation options available. Some of the best ways to get around include:

  • Rickshaws: These are a common form of transport and are perfect for short distances within the city.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available through various companies and can be booked through smartphone applications.
  • Buses: Local buses run regularly within the city and provide a cheap mode of transport for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Rajshahi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions within a few hours drive from Rajshahi, such as:

  • Natore: A historic city known for its royal palaces and temples.
  • Paharpur: A UNESCO world heritage site and ancient Buddhist monastery.
  • Dighapatia: A picturesque village known for its textile industry.

Conclusion

Rajshahi is a vibrant and exciting destination in Bangladesh, offering a mix of history, culture, and stunning natural landscapes. This comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your visit to this charming city. Whether you are interested in history, food, or outdoor adventures, Rajshahi has something for every traveler.
