A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Chittagong Hill Tracts

Chittagong Hill Tracts is a breath-taking region of Bangladesh, revered for its awe-inspiring landscape of hills, waterfalls, and mesmerizing valleys. Often referred to as “the roof of Bangladesh,” Chittagong Hill Tracts is home to several indigenous tribes, making it one of the culturally and linguistically diverse regions in the country. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through this picturesque paradise, highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, local culture, safety tips, and transportation options.

Introduction

Chittagong Hill Tracts is located in the southeast of Bangladesh, bordering Myanmar and India. The region is divided into three districts: Rangamati, Khagrachhari, and Bandarban. The region offers a unique travel experience, combining outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and boating with culturally enriching activities such as visiting local museums and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Chittagong Hill Tracts

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tribal Cultural Museum in Rangamati is an excellent place to learn about the culture and traditions of the tribal people who inhabit the region.

  2. Chittagong Hill Tracts is also home to several historical attractions such as the Mainimati Ruins and the Rangamati Rajbari, a royal palace.

Natural Attractions of Chittagong Hill Tracts: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Boga Lake is a must-visit destination, highlighted by its crystal-clear water and breathtaking scenery.

  2. Sajek Valley is a popular trekking destination, surrounded by lush green hills and majestic peaks.

  3. Bandarban Hill District is home to several waterfalls, including the majestic Chakma Rajbari, and the Shoilo Propat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chittagong Hill Tracts

  1. Keokradong, the highest peak in Bangladesh, is a popular trekking destination, offering a challenging and exciting trail for avid trekkers and nature lovers.

  2. The Rangamati-Kaptai Lake circuit is an excellent choice for biking enthusiasts, offering panoramic views of the lake’s banks, quaint villages, and lush green hills.

Best Things to Do in Chittagong Hill Tracts: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat tours are a popular way to explore the picturesque waterfalls, lakes, and rivers of Chittagong Hill Tracts.

  2. Paragliding and zip-lining at Nilgiri Hill is a perfect adventure for adrenaline-seekers.

  3. Visit the Buddhist temple in Bandarban Hill District and take part in a spiritual ceremony with the local monks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chittagong Hill Tracts

Day 1: Arrival in Chittagong Hill Tracts, explore nearby attractions.

Day 2: Hiking to Keokradong, the highest peak of Bangladesh.

Day 3: Visiting the Tribal Cultural Museum and Rangamati Rajbari.

Day 4: Exploring the waterfalls and lakes of Bandarban Hill District, including Chakma Rajbari and Shoilo Propat.

Day 5: A boat ride through Kaptai Lake.

Day 6: Biking through the scenic Rangamati-Kaptai Lake circuit.

Day 7: Departure from Chittagong Hill Tracts.

Food and Drink Scene of Chittagong Hill Tracts

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bamboo chicken, a local delicacy, cooked with marinated chicken, onions, garlic, and spices, and slow-cooked in bamboo.

  2. Pitha, a sweet or savory cake made from rice flour.

Local Beverages

  1. Coconut water is a refreshing way to cool off after a day of outdoor activities.

  2. Chaang is a local alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice.

Where to Stay in Chittagong Hill Tracts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Green Hill, Bandarban: A luxurious stay with a stunning view of the hills.

  2. Hotel Sufia, Khagrachhari: A budget-friendly option with excellent hospitality.

  3. Rangamati Water Front Resort: A lakeside resort with a rustic ambiance.

What to Buy in Chittagong Hill Tracts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hand-woven fabrics and textiles.

  2. Traditional handicrafts made by the local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Chittagong Hill Tracts

  1. The region is home to several indigenous tribes with their unique cultural and linguistic heritage.

  2. As a mark of respect, it is customary to remove footwear before entering a temple or holy place in the region.

Best Time to Visit Chittagong Hill Tracts

The winter season, from October to March, is the best time to visit Chittagong Hill Tracts, with moderate temperatures and clear skies.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chittagong Hill Tracts

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions of the indigenous tribes.

  2. Keep the surroundings clean and litter-free.

Don’t:

  1. Do not take photos without permission, especially of the locals.

  2. Do not disturb the natural habitat by littering or defacing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chittagong Hill Tracts

  1. Although the area is generally safe for tourists, visitors should take precautions to avoid theft.

  2. Avoid traveling alone or late at night.

Health in Chittagong Hill Tracts

  1. Visitors to the region must take precautions to avoid water-borne diseases.

  2. Ensure proper medication for any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Chittagong Hill Tracts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chittagong Hill Tracts

The nearest airport to Chittagong Hill Tracts is Shah Amanat International Airport in Chittagong.

Bus Stations Near Chittagong Hill Tracts

Regular bus services connect Chittagong Hill Tracts to major cities like Dhaka and Chittagong.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are available for hire, providing a more comfortable and flexible means of travel.

How to Get Around in Chittagong Hill Tracts

Local buses and shared jeeps are available for community travel. However, hiring a private car is recommended for tourists for a comfortable and safe journey.

Where to Go Next from Chittagong Hill Tracts: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cox’s Bazar, the longest natural sea beach in the world, is just a few hours’ drive from Chittagong Hill Tracts.

  2. Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world, is a short journey from Chittagong Hill Tracts, renowned for its endemic wildlife, especially the Bengal tiger.

Conclusion

Chittagong Hill Tracts is a hidden gem, tucked away in the southeast corner of Bangladesh. The region’s natural beauty, combined with its cultural diversity, makes it a unique travel destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors. This comprehensive travel guide to Chittagong Hill Tracts has provided an overview of what to expect when visiting the region, from places to visit and things to do to local customs and safety tips. It is the perfect guide for anyone planning a trip to this extraordinary region, offering a glimpse into the heart of Bangladesh’s natural and cultural beauty.
