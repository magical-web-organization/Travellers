Introduction to Bandarban District

Bandarban District is one of the three hilly districts of Bangladesh and is located in the southeastern part of the country. Known as the “Queen of the Hills,” it is surrounded by lush green forests and picturesque landscapes. The district is home to diverse ethnic groups, including Marma, Chakma, and Tripura, each with their unique culture and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Bandarban District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buddha Dhatu Jadi - This is the largest Theravada Buddhist temple in Bangladesh and enshrines a tooth relic of Lord Buddha.
  • Golden Temple - A stunning Buddhist temple with an intricate gold-plated facade and a beautiful pagoda structure.
  • Meghla Parjatan Complex - A renowned eco-park that houses a museum, a zoo, and a hanging bridge.

Natural Attractions of Bandarban District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nilgiri - One of the highest peaks in Bangladesh, known for its breathtaking scenery and spectacular sunrise views.
  • Boga Lake - A picturesque lake surrounded by mountains and dense forests, perfect for a peaceful picnic.
  • Sangu River - A stunning river running through the heart of Bandarban district that offers an unforgettable boating experience.
  • Chimbuk Hill - A picturesque hilltop that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding forests and hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bandarban District

  • Thanchi-Bandarban Trek - A popular trekking route that takes you through the dense forests and mountain trails of Bandarban District.
  • Ruma-Bandarban Trek - A challenging mountain trek that rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Nilgiri Trek - A moderate trek that takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Bandarban District, including the famous Nilgiri peak.

Best Things to Do in Bandarban District: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local villages - Take a tour of the nearby villages and experience the unique culture and traditions of the local ethnic groups.
  • Enjoy a river cruise - Take a leisurely boat ride along the Sangu River and soak in the stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Go on a wildlife safari - Explore the lush forests of Bandarban District and spot some of the rare and exotic wildlife that calls it home.

Food and Drink Scene of Bandarban District

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chakma Pitha - A traditional rice cake filled with sweet coconut and jaggery.
  • Marma Bhaja - Fried meat or fish cooked with local spices and herbs.
  • Tripura Chakhwi - A stew made with mixed vegetables, meat, and bamboo shoots.

Local Beverages

  • Chira - A fermented rice drink.
  • Handia - A sweet and sour drink made from rice and lentils.

Where to Stay in Bandarban District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hillside Resort - A luxurious resort with comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Marma Eco Cottage - A cozy guesthouse located in a peaceful and secluded area surrounded by greenery.
  • Hiking Club Guest House - A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Bandarban District, perfect for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Bandarban District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade textiles - Beautiful fabrics and textiles made using traditional techniques by local artisans.
  • Handmade pottery - Unique and intricately designed pottery made from clay by skilled craftsmen.
  • Local spices and herbs - A variety of spices and herbs that are unique to the region and offer a distinct flavor to any dish.

Local Culture and Customs of Bandarban District

Bandarban District is home to diverse ethnic groups, each with their unique culture and traditions. Visitors are advised to be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting any religious or cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Bandarban District

The best time to visit Bandarban District is between October and March, when the weather is cool and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bandarban District

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting any religious or cultural sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and experience the unique flavors of the region.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activities that may harm the environment or wildlife.
  • Take photographs of local people without their permission.
  • Use plastic bags or litter in any natural areas or public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bandarban District

Bandarban District is generally safe, and visitors can move around freely. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding isolated or poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Bandarban District

Visitors to Bandarban District should take basic health precautions, such as drinking only bottled water, using mosquito repellent, and getting all appropriate vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Bandarban District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bandarban District

The nearest airport to Bandarban District is Shah Amanat International Airport in Chittagong, which is about 2.5 hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Bandarban District

There are several bus stations in Chittagong that offer services to Bandarban District. Visitors can take a bus from Chittagong to Bandarban, which takes approximately 4-5 hours, depending on traffic conditions.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a shared taxi or hire a private car from Chittagong to Bandarban District, which takes approximately 3-4 hours.

How to Get Around in Bandarban District

The most convenient way to get around Bandarban District is by hiring a car or a motorbike, which is readily available in most tourist areas.

Where to Go Next from Bandarban District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cox’s Bazar - A popular beach destination in Bangladesh, known for its stunning beaches and seafood cuisine.
  • Rangamati - A beautiful district located in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, known for its scenic beauty and unique culture.
  • Chittagong - The second-largest city in Bangladesh, with historical sites and a vibrant food and culture scene.
