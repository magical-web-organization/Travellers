Introduction to Nijhum Dwip

Nijhum Dwip, also known as the Silent Island, is an untouched paradise tucked away in the Bay of Bengal. The island is located in the southern part of the Noakhali district in Bangladesh, and forms a part of the Hatia Upazila. This remote island is home to unspoiled nature, intriguing wildlife and rare flora, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventurers, and beach enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Nijhum Dwip

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nijhum Dwip doesn’t have many historical sites or museums, but it has a rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the unique lifestyle and customs of the island’s fishing communities. A visit to the fish market can be a fascinating experience. You can also witness fishermen catching fish with traditional fishing nets, and even try your hand at it yourself.

Natural Attractions of Nijhum Dwip: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nijhum Dwip is an ultimate destination for nature lovers. The island is home to many rare species of flora and fauna, and the dense mangrove forest provides a natural habitat for many wildlife species. Take a boat ride to the nearby river to spot dolphins, and explore the large saltwater pond to find the native waterbirds. You can also take a stroll along the sandy beaches and soak in the breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nijhum Dwip

If you’re looking for some adventure, Nijhum Dwip has some great hiking and biking routes. The lush green forests, the long beaches, and the serene rivers offer a perfect backdrop for these activities. A trip to the nearby village can be an enriching experience, as you can interact with the locals and learn about their culture and traditions.

Best Things to Do in Nijhum Dwip: Activities and Tours

Some popular activities and tours in Nijhum Dwip include:

  • Dolphin spotting tours
  • Bird-watching tours
  • Traditional fishing
  • Village tours
  • Beach hopping
  • Picnicking
  • Boat rides

Week-Long Itinerary for Nijhum Dwip

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip to Nijhum Dwip:

Day 1-2: Arrive in Nijhum Dwip and spend the first two days relaxing at the beach, soaking in the sun, and exploring the nearby areas.

Day 3-4: Take a boat ride to the nearby river to spot dolphins, and explore the large saltwater pond to spot the native waterbirds.

Day 5-6: Explore the lush green forests and biking trails, make sure to pack some food and drinks for a picnic.

Day 7: Experience the local lifestyle by visiting the fishing village, witness the fishermen catching fish with traditional fishing nets.

Food and Drink Scene of Nijhum Dwip

Must-Try Dishes

Nijhum Dwip is famous for its delicious seafood. Some must-try dishes are:

  • Prawn Malaikari
  • Fish Curry
  • Fried Fish

Local Beverages

In Nijhum Dwip, the locals drink coconut water and tea. You can also indulge in some fresh fruit juices and exotic cocktails.

Where to Stay in Nijhum Dwip: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nijhum Dwip has limited accommodation options to offer you. However, the Ocean Paradise Hotel and Lounge can be a perfect choice for tourists. The hotel offers excellent services, comfortable rooms, and delicious food.

What to Buy in Nijhum Dwip: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can buy handmade crafts and traditional clothing as souvenirs in Nijhum Dwip. Additionally, you can find exquisite shell and coral jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Nijhum Dwip

Nijhum Dwip is primarily inhabited by fishing communities. They share a unique history and cultural heritage, and speak Chittagonian dialect as their primary language. Visitors are expected to maintain a respectful attitude towards locals and their customs.

Best Time to Visit Nijhum Dwip

The best time for tourism in Nijhum Dwip is from November to February. The weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nijhum Dwip

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the natural environment.
  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen.
  • Try the delicious seafood.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or forests.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nijhum Dwip

Nijhum Dwip is a very safe place for tourists. You should avoid traveling alone to the remote areas of the island after sunset.

Health in Nijhum Dwip

It is important to carry your prescribed medications with you. If you require medical attention, you can visit the health center at Hatia.

Getting to Nijhum Dwip: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nijhum Dwip is not directly connected to any major cities in Bangladesh. Travelers need to take a boat ride from Noakhali to get to the island. A ferry ride from Dhaka to Noakhali is also available.

Airports Near Nijhum Dwip

The nearest airport is Osmani International Airport in Sylhet.

Bus Stations Near Nijhum Dwip

The bus station in Noakhali is the nearest bus station to Nijhum Dwip.

Other Options

You can hire a taxi or private car to reach the boat pier in Noakhali.

How to Get Around in Nijhum Dwip

The best ways to explore Nijhum Dwip is by walking, biking, or using a rented motorcycle.

Where to Go Next from Nijhum Dwip: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Nijhum Dwip is an offbeat travel destination that offers an unspoiled view of nature and an insight into the local lifestyle. From the beautiful mangrove forests to the tranquil beaches and impressive wildlife, there’s a world of adventure waiting to be discovered on this island. Use this exhaustive travel guide to plan your trip and enjoy the best of what Nijhum Dwip has to offer.
