Are you looking to discover the hidden gems of Bangladesh? Look no further than Saidpur. Located in the northern region of the country, Saidpur boasts a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural landscapes, and a burgeoning food scene. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, what to eat, where to stay, and how to make the most of your trip to this beautiful destination.

Introduction to Saidpur

Saidpur is a city in the Rangpur Division of Bangladesh, located in the northern part of the country. Known as the “Gateway to North Bengal,” Saidpur is a historic city that has served as a trading hub for centuries. Today, it is a thriving center of industry, culture, and education. Visitors to Saidpur can discover beautiful temples and mosques, explore lush forests and hills, and enjoy delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Saidpur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tajhat Palace: This beautiful historic palace was built in the early 20th century and boasts intricate architecture and beautiful gardens that are well worth a visit.
  2. Rangpur Museum: The Rangpur Museum is housed in a beautiful colonial-era building and is home to a fascinating collection of artifacts related to the history of the region.
  3. Saidpur Railway Museum: This unique museum is dedicated to the history of the railways in Bangladesh and features a collection of antique locomotives and other railway memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Saidpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Madhabpur Lake: This picturesque lake is surrounded by lush hills and forests and is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and picnicking.
  2. Bakhrabad Hills: These rolling hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and are a popular spot for hiking and camping.
  3. Saidpur Botanical Garden: This beautiful garden is home to a wide variety of plant species and is a great place to relax and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saidpur

  1. Bakhrabad Hill Trail: This moderate hiking trail takes visitors through the rolling hills of Bakhrabad and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. Lighthouse Trail: This scenic biking route takes visitors through the historic streets of Saidpur and past the iconic lighthouse.

Best Things to Do in Saidpur: Activities and Tours

  1. Food Tour: Take a guided tour of the best local restaurants and street food vendors in Saidpur and try some of the delicious local cuisine.
  2. Walking Tour: Explore the historic streets of Saidpur on foot and discover hidden gems and landmarks with the help of a knowledgeable guide.
  3. Boat Ride on Madhabpur Lake: Take a relaxing boat ride on the picturesque Madhabpur Lake and soak up the natural beauty of the surrounding hills and forests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saidpur

Day 1: Explore the historic streets of Saidpur and visit the Rangpur Museum. Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour of the Bakhrabad Hills. Day 3: Visit Tajhat Palace and explore its beautiful gardens. Day 4: Take a guided food tour of the best local restaurants and street food vendors. Day 5: Relax with a boat ride on Madhabpur Lake. Day 6: Visit the Saidpur Railway Museum to learn about the history of the railways in Bangladesh. Day 7: Enjoy a day trip to nearby Rangpur City to discover even more cultural landmarks and natural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Saidpur

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chapli Kabab: A spicy meat kabab made with beef or mutton and a blend of herbs and spices.
  2. Fuchka: A crispy, hollow pastry filled with spicy potato and chickpea mix and tamarind water.
  3. Haleem: A hearty meat stew made with lentils and a variety of spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Lassi: A sweet yogurt drink that is a popular beverage in many parts of South Asia.
  2. Sugarcane Juice: A refreshing and sweet juice made from sugarcane.

Where to Stay in Saidpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Parjatan Motel: A comfortable and affordable option located near many of the city’s top attractions.
  2. Pan Pacific Sonargaon: A luxurious hotel with great amenities and a central location.
  3. Hotel Green Hill: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Saidpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Saidpur is known for its vibrant handicraft scene, and visitors can find beautiful handmade items like pottery, textiles, and jewelry.
  2. Tea: The region around Saidpur is home to many tea plantations, and visitors can purchase high-quality tea to bring home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Saidpur

Saidpur has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting mosques and temples and should remove their shoes before entering. Alcohol is not widely consumed in Saidpur, and visitors should not bring it into public areas or consume it in the presence of locals who may find it offensive.

Best Time to Visit Saidpur

The best time to visit Saidpur is from October to March, when the weather is cooler and dry. The monsoon season from June to September can bring heavy rains and make outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saidpur

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and temples.
  • Try the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public areas.
  • Disrupt religious ceremonies or events.
  • Don’t visit during the monsoon season if you are planning outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saidpur

Saidpur is generally a safe city for visitors, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take normal precautions like keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Saidpur

Visitors to Saidpur should take precautions to protect their health, like drinking bottled water, avoiding uncooked or undercooked food, and using insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue.

Getting to Saidpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saidpur

The nearest major airport to Saidpur is Shah Makhdum Airport in Rajshahi, which is approximately 120 km away. Visitors can also fly into Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka, which is approximately 360 km away.

Bus Stations Near Saidpur

The Saidpur Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the city and offers regular service to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Dhaka to Saidpur, which takes approximately 10 hours. Taxis and private cars can also be hired for transportation.

How to Get Around in Saidpur

Visitors can get around Saidpur using taxis or auto-rickshaws, which are readily available throughout the city. Walking or cycling are also good options for exploring the city on a more leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Saidpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Rangpur City: This nearby city is home to many cultural landmarks and natural attractions, including the Tajhat Palace and Rangpur Museum.
  2. Dinajpur: This historic city is known for its beautiful temples and archaeological sites, including the Kantajew Temple and the ruins of the ancient city of Mahasthangarh.

Conclusion

Saidpur is a northern gem of Bangladesh that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From hiking and biking trails to cultural landmarks and delicious cuisine, Saidpur is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the best of Bangladesh.
