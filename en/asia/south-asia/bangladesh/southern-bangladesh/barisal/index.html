Barisal, situated in southern Bangladesh, is a treasure trove of natural wonders and fascinating cultural sites. From scenic views and outdoor adventures to museums and historical landmarks, there’s plenty to explore in this charming city. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best things to do, see, eat, and experience in Barisal.

Introduction to Barisal

Barisal is a laid-back city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Nestled on the banks of the Kirtankhola River, it’s home to traditional markets, ancient temples, and beautiful colonial architecture. Here, you’ll find the perfect balance between relaxation and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Barisal

There are plenty of sights to explore in Barisal. Below is a list of some of the top attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Durga Sagar - The largest pond in Barisal, located in the heart of the city.

  • Guthia Mosque - A beautiful mosque built in the 17th century.

  • Oxford Mission Church - A stunning colonial church constructed in the early 20th century.

  • Swarupkathi Shrine - A famous Hindu shrine located on the bank of Kirtankhola River

Natural Attractions of Barisal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Guthia Island - A picturesque island in the Kirtankhola River, dotted with scenic villages.

  • Bamboo Forest - A beautiful wooded area featuring intricate bamboo groves.

  • Hijla Island - A serene island known for its lush greenery and crystal clear waters.

  • River Cruise - Take a boat ride through the Kirtankhola River to enjoy the scenic views and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barisal

Barisal is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking trails include:

  • Durga Sagar Trail - This easy, picturesque hike winds around the beautiful Durga Sagar pond.

  • Guthia Island Trail - A moderate bike ride through scenic villages and lush greenery.

  • Hijla Island Hike - A challenging but rewarding trek through dense forests and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Barisal: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Barisal. Here are some of the best:

  • Visiting Traditional Markets - Check out the bustling local markets to experience the authentic Barisal culture.

  • Taking a Boat Tour - Enjoy a lazy afternoon boat ride on the Kirtankhola River to take in the scenic views.

  • Exploring Temples and Mosques - Visit the various historic temples and mosques scattered throughout the city to learn about the rich cultural heritage of Barisal.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barisal

If you’re planning to spend a week in Barisal, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the bustling markets
Day 2 Hike the Durga Sagar Trail and visit Guthia Mosque
Day 3 Take a boat tour on the Kirtankhola River
Day 4 Visit Oxford Mission Church or Swarupkathi Shrine
Day 5 Go on a bike ride across Guthia Island
Day 6 Hike or bike on Hijla Island
Day 7 Relax and unwind at one of Barisal’s beautiful resorts

Food and Drink Scene of Barisal

Barisal is renowned for its delicious cuisine, which draws influences from both Indian and Bangladeshi food traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bamboo Shoot Curry - A flavourful and aromatic curry made with locally sourced bamboo shoots.

  • Pitha - A type of savoury or sweet cake made from rice flour and stuffed with coconut, molasses, or meat.

  • Hilsa Fish Curry - A spicy and tangy curry made with the delicious Hilsa fish, a local delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Sharbat - A refreshing drink made with rose water and sugar syrup.

  • Lemon Tea - A hot beverage made with lemons, crushed ginger, and tea leaves.

Where to Stay in Barisal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barisal offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Whether you’re looking for a luxury resort or a budget-friendly guesthouse, you’ll find something that fits your needs.

  • Hotel Grand Park - A luxurious option with excellent amenities, including an outdoor pool and an on-site restaurant.

  • Peyara Guest House - A budget-friendly guesthouse with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Barisal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take back home, here are some must-visit markets:

  • Guthia Bazaar - A traditional market offering everything from handicrafts to fresh produce.

  • The Floating Market - A unique market held on boats in the Kirtankhola River.

Local Culture and Customs of Barisal

Barisal has a distinct culture and customs. Visitors should be respectful of local beliefs and practices. Make sure to dress modestly and remove shoes when entering temples or mosques.

Best Time to Visit Barisal

The best time to visit Barisal is between October and March, when the weather is comfortable and mild. Summers can be scorching hot, making outdoor activities less favourable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barisal

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Barisal:

Do:

  • Do try out the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Do take off your shoes when entering temples and mosques.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local culture and beliefs.

  • Don’t venture out to isolated areas at night.

Safety and Health

Barisal is a generally safe destination, but it’s always best to exercise caution, especially at night. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Barisal

  • Avoid lingering around dark or isolated areas at night.

  • Be mindful of your belongings and avoid flashing valuables in public.

Health in Barisal

  • Make sure to drink only bottled water to avoid any water-related diseases.

  • Carry a first-aid kit with essential medications and insect repellents to protect against mosquitoes and other insects.

Getting to Barisal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Barisal is well-connected to other parts of Bangladesh. Below are some of the best transportation options:

Airports Near Barisal

  • Barisal Airport - Barisal has a small domestic airport with limited connectivity.

  • Jessore Airport - Jessore Airport is the nearest international airport, located around 100 km from Barisal.

Bus Stations Near Barisal

  • Barisal Bus Terminal - The main bus terminal that connects Barisal to other parts of Bangladesh.

Other Options

  • You can also rent a private car or take a taxi to get to Barisal from other cities.

How to Get Around in Barisal

The best way to get around Barisal is by renting a bike or hiring a taxi. Auto-rickshaws are also a cheap and convenient mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Barisal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time and are looking to explore some nearby destinations, here are some places you can visit:

  • Kuakata - A picturesque seaside town located around 100 km from Barisal.

  • Srimangal - A beautiful tea-growing town located around 250 km from Barisal.

Conclusion

Barisal is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. With this guide, you can explore Barisal’s fascinating landmarks, trendy food scene, and exciting outdoor activities. Plan your Barisal adventure today and create unforgettable memories.
