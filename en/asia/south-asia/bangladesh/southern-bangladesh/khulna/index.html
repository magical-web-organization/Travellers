Introduction to Khulna

Khulna is a bustling city located in southern Bangladesh. With a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, Khulna is a popular destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation. The city is known for its mouth-watering seafood, vibrant markets, and friendly locals. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Khulna.

List of Places to Visit in Khulna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sundarbans: A UNESCO World Heritage site and the largest mangrove forest in the world. The Sundarbans is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including the Bengal tiger, saltwater crocodile, and Indian python.

  • Khan Jahan Ali Mazar: A sacred shrine in honor of Khan Jahan Ali, a renowned Sufi saint and ruler of Khalifatabad.

  • Shat Gombuj Mosque: The largest mosque in Bangladesh, the Sixty Dome Mosque is an impressive example of Islamic architecture with sixty domes.

  • Rabindranath Tagore’s Kuthibari: A home where Rabindranath Tagore, the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize for Literature lived and wrote.

Natural Attractions of Khulna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sundarbans: Not only culturally significant, the region offers great adventure opportunities such as jungle walks, boating, and bird watching.

  • Jessore Road: A scenic route that runs from Khulna to Jessore, dotted with picturesque villages and lush rice paddies.

  • Kuakata: A seaside town renowned for its stunning sunsets and fishing village experience.

  • Bagerhat: An ancient town founded in the 15th century, featuring numerous historic mosques and mausoleums.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khulna

  • The Sundarbans: The largest mangrove forest in the world offers trails for eco-tourists to explore the unique flora and fauna.

  • Sundarban Mangrove Trail: A trek through the jungle to explore the wildlife.

  • Shankarpasha Nature Reserve: A peaceful ecosystem featuring Yak trees, bamboo groves and rare birds, a delight for trekkers, hikers and birdwatchers.

Best Things to Do in Khulna: Activities and Tours

  • Boat tour of the Sundarbans: Take a leisurely cruise and enjoy the stunning scenery, wildlife, and taste of freshly caught seafood.

  • Visit the local markets: immerse yourself in the culture and traditions of the region.

  • Experience local festivals: Local festivals like the Baishakhi festival in April, the Jashn-e-Khulna para festivity in September, and the Kite festival in November are great ways to experience the Khulna way of life.

  • Explore local food: Enjoy sumptuous seafood straight from the river with mouth-watering fish curries and biryanis.

Food and Drink Scene of Khulna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Curry: Khulna’s fish is famous for its delicious and unique taste. Must try the Bharani fish, Rui fish and Hilsha fish curries.

  • Beef Rezala: A specialty from Mughal cuisine, a flavorful curry with beef and herbs.

  • Daal Bora: A favorite snack item made with lentils and batter.

Local Beverages

  • Sugarcane Juice: A refreshing local drink made by pressing sugarcane.

  • Tea: Khulna is renowned for its excellent tea, such as their famous seven-layer tea of Tungipara.

Where to Stay in Khulna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • City Inn Hotel Khulna: A five-star hotel located in the heart of Khulna city.

  • Hotel Royal International: A renowned hotel with scenic river views.

  • Western Inn International Limited: A budget hotel suitable for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Khulna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Khulna Silk: famous silk fabric available in vibrant colors.

  • Wooden Furniture: Handcrafted furniture made from premium quality woods native to the Sundarbans areas.

  • Haribhanga Rice: A locally grown aromatic rice variety of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Khulna

Khulna’s culture is influenced by the locals, Sufi saints, and settlement by the British East India Company. The people are known for their generosity, hospitality, and delicious food. The culture of the region is celebrated through different festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Khulna

The best time to visit Khulna is between November to February when the weather is mild, pleasant, and ideal for exploring the natural beauty of the Sundarbans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khulna

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the seafood and tea.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Learn some Bengali phrases for ease of communication with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately in religious sites.

  • Do not litter or leave the locality dirty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khulna

Khulna is known for being a friendly and safe city for tourists. Petty theft and pickpocketing can be an issue, so it’s best to stay alert and take precautions.

Health in Khulna

Visitors should be careful to stick to bottled water and avoid food from street vendors as it may not be hygienic.

Getting to Khulna: Transportation Options and Logistics

The best way to get to Khulna is by air. The city has an airport with daily flights from Dhaka. Visitors can also reach Khulna by bus or private car from Dhaka, which takes around eight hours.

Airports Near Khulna

  • Jessore Airport (JSR): Located about 30 km from Khulna and has daily flights from Dhaka.

Bus Stations Near Khulna

  • Khan Jahan Ali Bus Terminal: Located in the heart of the city.

Other Options

  • Visitors can opt for an overnight train from Dhaka that connects to Khulna.

How to Get Around in Khulna

Visitors can get around Khulna by taxi or hire a private car.

Where to Go Next from Khulna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Barishal: a district in Bangladesh known for its beautiful beaches, ancient temples, and historical sites.

  • Jessore: A historic town with stunning architecture and outdoor markets.

  • Srimangal: The tea capital of Bangladesh, famous for its picturesque tea gardens.

Conclusion

Khulna is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. A perfect destination for adventurers and relaxation seekers alike, with delicious cuisine, friendly locals, and a wide range of activities to enjoy. Plan your trip and enjoy this unique city in Southern Bangladesh.
