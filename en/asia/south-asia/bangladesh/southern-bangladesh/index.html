Introduction to Southern Bangladesh

Located in South Asia, Southern Bangladesh is a region filled with cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and bustling cities. With rich history, pristine beaches, and flavorful cuisine, Southern Bangladesh offers an authentic travel experience. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie, history buff, or food enthusiast, Southern Bangladesh has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Bangladesh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baitul Mukarram Mosque: Located in Dhaka, the Baitul Mukarram Mosque is one of the largest mosques in Bangladesh and a major cultural landmark. Visitors can witness its stunning architectural features and religious significance.

  • Lalbagh Fort: Built in the 17th century, the Lalbagh Fort is an impressive historical landmark in Dhaka. The fort served as a residence for a Mughal prince before being established as a museum displaying antique artifacts.

  • Ahsan Manzil: Another architectural wonder located in Dhaka, Ahsan Manzil was once the residence of the Nawab (ruler) of Dhaka. Today, it serves as a museum displaying the rich cultural heritage of Bangladesh.

Natural Attractions of Southern Bangladesh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sundarbans Mangrove Forest: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Sundarbans Mangrove Forest spans across Southern Bangladesh and eastern India. The reserve is home to the endangered Bengal Tiger and various species of wildlife.

  • Cox’s Bazar Beach: The longest natural beach in the world, Cox’s Bazar is a scenic and relaxing spot for travelers to unwind. The beach offers stunning views and water sports opportunities.

  • Srimangal Tea Gardens: A perfect destination for eco-tourism, Srimangal Tea Gardens offer breathtaking views of the hill slopes covered in tea plantations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Bangladesh

  • Sundarbans Mangrove Forest Trek: The Sundarbans offers some of the best hiking trails in the region. Visitors can explore the mangrove forests and observe a wide range of wildlife, including tigers, crocodiles, and various bird species.

  • Chittagong Hill Tracts: A popular destination for bikers, the Chittagong Hill Tracts offers stunning views of the hilly terrain and tribal culture.

Best Things to Do in Southern Bangladesh: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of the Sundarbans: A boat tour of the Sundarbans offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the largest mangrove forest in the world and observe wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Visit the Saint Martin’s Island: A pristine island located near Cox’s Bazar, Saint Martin’s Island offers a tranquil atmosphere to relax, swim, and snorkel.

  • Explore Dhaka’s Food Scene: With its rich culinary heritage, Dhaka is a foodie’s paradise. Visitors can explore street food stalls, local markets, and fine dining restaurants to get a taste of authentic Bangladeshi cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Bangladesh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bengali Fish Curry: A spicy and flavorful curry made with a variety of local fish.

  • Beef Tehari: A popular rice dish made with beef and spices.

  • Chotpoti: A street food delicacy made with chickpeas, boiled egg, and tamarind sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing and creamy yogurt drink, perfect to cool down during hot weather.

  • Cha: A traditional cup of tea infused with spices and milk, enjoyed with friends and family.

Where to Stay in Southern Bangladesh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pan Pacific Sonargaon Dhaka: Located in the heart of Dhaka, the Pan Pacific Sonargaon Dhaka offers luxurious amenities and comfortable rooms.

  • BRAC CDM Savar: A tranquil retreat located on the outskirts of Dhaka, BRAC CDM Savar offers a peaceful and cozy stay with beautiful surroundings.

  • Cox’s Bazar Beach Resort: A beachfront resort that offers stunning views and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Southern Bangladesh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-Made Crafts: Southern Bangladesh is home to various handicraft communities that produce beautiful and intricate crafts, including woven baskets, pottery, and textiles.

  • Tea and Spices: Srimangal Tea Gardens is famous for its organic tea leaves and various local spices, perfect to bring home and enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Bangladesh

Bangladesh is a multicultural country with a rich heritage. Islam is the predominant religion, and most people speak Bengali. Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Southern Bangladesh

The best time to visit Southern Bangladesh is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is cool and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Bangladesh

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicate sweets.
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Be reckless in public places.
  • Waste water or resources, as they are scarce in some areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Bangladesh

Bangladesh is a relatively safe destination for travelers. It is advisable to avoid large crowds and be cautious in urban areas, especially at night.

Health in Southern Bangladesh

Southern Bangladesh is a region with a high risk of diseases such as malaria and dengue fever. Visitors should take precautions and consult with a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Southern Bangladesh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Bangladesh

  • Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka
  • Cox’s Bazar Airport

Bus Stations Near Southern Bangladesh

  • Gabtoli Bus Terminal in Dhaka
  • Arambagh Bus Terminal in Dhaka

Other Options

Train transportation is also available and offers a more comfortable and relaxed way of travel.

How to Get Around in Southern Bangladesh

To get around Southern Bangladesh, visitors can use taxis, rickshaws, or rent a car with a driver.

Where to Go Next from Southern Bangladesh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rangamati: Located in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, Rangamati is a scenic and cultural destination that offers breathtaking views, hiking trails, and a glimpse into the tribal culture.

  • Maheshkhali Island: Located near Cox’s Bazar, Maheshkhali Island is a religious and cultural destination, well known for its ancient temples and serene beaches.

Conclusion

Southern Bangladesh is a region filled with natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and authentic experiences. With its rich history, delicious cuisine, and hospitable people, Southern Bangladesh offers an unforgettable travel experience that is both affordable and enjoyable.
