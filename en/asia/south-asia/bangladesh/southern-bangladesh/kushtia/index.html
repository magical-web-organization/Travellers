Introduction to Kushtia

Kushtia is a city situated in the Southwestern part of Bangladesh, known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural landscapes, and famous personalities. It is one of the major tourist destinations in the country, attracting visitors from all over the world. Kushtia is surrounded by the Padma River, which amplifies its beauty and charm. The river provides opportunities for various water sports and boating activities.

List of Places to Visit in Kushtia

If you’re planning a trip to Kushtia, there’s a lot waiting for you to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Rabindranath Tagore’s Kuthibari: It is the ancestral home of Rabindranath Tagore, who is considered one of the most renowned poets and writers in the world. It is a significant landmark and a protected national museum, which displays Tagore’s personal belongings and writings.
  2. Lalon shrine: Lalon Shah is one of the most celebrated mystic poets of Bangladesh, whose philosophy of social harmony and spirituality has attracted people from all faiths around the world. His shibirs or religious meetings are attended by thousands of followers annually.
  3. Kushtia Taj Hat Historical Complex: It is an archaeological site containing an ancient mosque, a palace, tombs and a pond. The Taj Hat Palace was built by the great grandson of Mughal emperor Aurangzeb and is a sight to behold with its intricate carvings and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kushtia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Padma River: The Padma River is the largest distributary of the Ganges River and presents an outstanding example of nature’s beauty. There are ferries available to take you on a scenic tour of the river.
  2. Jhaudia Shahi Mosque: This mosque is situated in the middle of a village, surrounded by lush green paddy fields. It offers a stunning view of the rural landscape of Kushtia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kushtia

Kushtia is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. If you’re a hiking or biking enthusiast, here are some routes you should check out:

  1. Shibganj-Bheramara-Bhed-Bangram: This is an exciting trek that will take you through a series of small villages, farmland, and woodland.
  2. Kajia and Charikata Haor: This biking route starts near the village of Kajia and takes you through the Charikata Haor wetlands, which offer exquisite scenery and wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in Kushtia: Activities and Tours

Kushtia offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are a few options:

  1. Attend a Lalon Shah festival: You can plan your visit to Kushtia around the yearly festivals dedicated to Lalon Shah. These festivals celebrate his teachings, with music and performances taking place over several days.
  2. Explore shops and markets: Kushtia has an excellent collection of handicrafts, handmade items and textiles that visitors can purchase as souvenirs or gifts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kushtia

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long trip to Kushtia:

Day 1: Arrive in Kushtia and check into your accommodation. Day 2: Visit Rabindranath Tagore’s Kuthibari. Day 3: Explore the Padma River by ferry. Day 4: Visit Lalon Shah’s shrine. Day 5: Take a bike trip to Kajia and Charikata Haor. Day 6: Visit the Kushtia Taj Hat Historical Complex. Day 7: Enjoy shopping in the local markets before departing for the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Kushtia

The food of Kushtia is a blend of different Bangladeshi cuisines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bhuna Khichuri: This is a vegetable and beef dish made with Basmati rice, split lentils and spices.
  2. Bhorta: This is a type of mashed vegetable dish that is served with rice and a variety of curries.

Local Beverages

  1. Doi Shorbot: This is a traditional drink made from sweetened yogurt and cold water.
  2. Chaa: This is a sweet tea served with milk.

Where to Stay in Kushtia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kushtia has various accommodation options for visitors. There are different levels of hotels, guesthouses and hostels that offer a range of amenities and prices.

Some of the best options include:

  1. Hotel Uttara: This is a high-end hotel located in the city center.
  2. Hotel Royal Palace: This is a mid-range hotel with excellent facilities and friendly staff.
  3. Kushtia Guest House: This is a budget-friendly option, and it is located near the river.

What to Buy in Kushtia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kushtia is famous for its beautiful textiles and handicrafts. Here are some of the best items to consider buying as souvenirs:

  1. Nakshi Kantha: This is a quilt made from old saris, dhotis and other materials.
  2. Tangail Sari: These are handloom woven saris, famous for their beautiful designs and vivid colors.
  3. Kushtia Special Vegetables: These vegetables are only found in Kushtia, and they are delicious.

Local Culture and Customs of Kushtia

The people of Kushtia are friendly and welcoming, and they have preserved their cultural heritage for centuries. Visitors should be aware of the Islamic customs of the country, and dress conservatively.

Best Time to Visit Kushtia

The best time to visit Kushtia is in the winter months, from November to March, when the weather is mild and dry. The rainy season is from April to September when the weather can be humid and unpleasant for travel.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kushtia

Do:

  1. Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and public places.
  3. Try some of the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of people without their permission.
  2. Show public displays of affection.
  3. Disrespect the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kushtia

It is generally safe for tourists to visit Kushtia. However, as with any location, visitors should take precautions such as not wandering around without a guide, guarding their belongings, and not traveling alone at night.

Health in Kushtia

Visitors need to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, which can cause diseases such as dengue fever or malaria. It is also essential to follow proper hygiene practices when eating or drinking.

Getting to Kushtia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kushtia

The nearest airport is the Jessore Airport, which is approximately 70 kilometers from Kushtia. Other airports that are nearby include Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka and Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Chittagong. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to travel to Kushtia.

Bus Stations Near Kushtia

Kushtia has a central bus station from which visitors can travel around the region. There are also private bus operators who offer transportation services to the city.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Kushtia by train or car as well. The city is connected to the rest of the country via highways and railway networks.

How to Get Around in Kushtia

Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for getting around in Kushtia. There are also bike rentals available for visitors to explore the city independently.

Where to Go Next from Kushtia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kushtia is situated between different notable destinations that are worth visiting. These include:

  1. Jessore: This city is famous for its beautiful temples, mosques and a historic bazar.
  2. Rajshahi: This city is famous for its ancient temples, archaeological sites and silk industry.

Conclusion

Kushtia is a perfect place to experience the rich culture and natural beauty of Bangladesh. This comprehensive guide provides everything visitors need to know when planning a trip to Kushtia. With the top attractions, cultural experiences, food and drink scene, accommodation options, and transportation logistics covered in-depth, visitors can make the most of their time in this beautiful city.
