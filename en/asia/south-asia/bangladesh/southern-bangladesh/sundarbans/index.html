Introduction to Sundarbans

Sundarbans, located in southern Bangladesh, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the largest mangrove forest in the world. The area is home to unique flora and fauna, including the Bengal tiger, and offers visitors a chance to experience the rich culture and history of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Sundarbans

The locals of Sundarbans are predominantly Muslim, and the region is known for its unique folk culture, music, and dance forms, such as Banam and Gambhira.

Best Time to Visit Sundarbans

The best time to visit Sundarbans is between October and March when the weather is cooler and drier, and the vegetation is less dense, making it easier to spot wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sundarbans

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Take appropriate precautions while traveling through the mangrove forest

Don’t:

  • Harm or harass wildlife
  • Litter

Safety and Health

Safety in Sundarbans

Visitors should be cautious while traveling through the mangrove forest and follow the instructions of their guide. Check for updates on political and social conditions before traveling to the region.

Health in Sundarbans

Malaria is prevalent in the region, and visitors are advised to take appropriate precautions such as using mosquito repellents and taking prophylactics.

How to Get Around in Sundarbans

Boats are the primary mode of transportation in Sundarbans, and visitors can rent a boat or take a boat tour to explore the area.
