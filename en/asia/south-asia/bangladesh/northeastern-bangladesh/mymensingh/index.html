Introduction to Mymensingh

Mymensingh, located in the northeast region of Bangladesh, is a culturally rich and historically significant destination. It’s a land of diverse heritage blended with natural beauty, making it an ideal place for cultural exploration and outdoor adventures. The ancient Mymensingh was once ruled by Hindu, Buddhist and Muslim dynasties, from which the city inherits its eclectic architecture and multicultural traditions. Mymensingh is also home to several ethnic and tribal communities like the Garos, Hajongs and Kochs, adding to the city’s rich cultural tapestry.

List of Places to Visit in Mymensingh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bangladesh Agricultural University Museum

This museum is located within the campus of Bangladesh Agricultural University, showcasing the country’s agricultural heritage through preserved specimens of plants and animals, scientific equipment, and farming tools. The museum also exhibits various relics of the British colonial era.

Mymensingh Museum

Also known as Zainul Abedin Sangrahashala, the Mymensingh Museum exhibits a diverse range of objects that showcase the city’s heritage, including collections of coins, manuscripts, pottery, and sculptures, among others. The museum also houses a gallery dedicated to the renowned artist Zainul Abedin’s works.

Shashi Lodge

Constructed in 1904 by Maharaja Shashi Kanta Acharya Choudhury, Shashi Lodge is a prominent Gothic-style mansion in Mymensingh. The lodge is now a protected heritage site and home to the Mymensingh Divisional Library.

Natural Attractions of Mymensingh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bhawal National Park

Bhawal National Park is located in Gazipur, a few miles away from Mymensingh, and is a vast reservation forest area with an abundance of flora and fauna. The park is home to various species of birds, elephants, deer, monkeys, and other wildlife. The park also has several walking trails, rest houses, and picnic spots.

Meghla Parbat

Meghla Parbat is a picturesque hill that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The hill located on the bank of the Brahmaputra River is perfect for hiking, rock climbing, and outdoor picnics.

Madhabpur Lake

Madhabpur Lake, located in Habiganj, is the largest lake in Bangladesh, with an area of 125 square kilometers. The Lake is surrounded by lush forests and is home to numerous migratory birds, making it a popular spot for bird watchers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mymensingh

Mymensingh is a great place for mountain biking or hiking enthusiasts. Several trails offer scenic views of the city or take one through the surrounding countryside.

Bhaluka Upazila

Bhaluka Upazila, which is located 20 kilometers from Mymensingh city, offers an excellent biking route through the natural countryside. The route is around 10 KM long and is mostly flat, making it easy to ride.

Syedpur Upazila

The hiking trail within Syedpur Upazila takes one through the hilly terrain of the region, filled with lush greeneries and pristine landscapes. The trail is around 7KM and requires some level of fitness, but the views are worth it.

Best Things to Do in Mymensingh: Activities and Tours

Mymensingh Heritage Walk

Take a Heritage Walk of Mymensingh to explore the city’s rich cultural and historical past. The walk will take you past many landmarks and buildings that represent ancient and modern architectural styles.

Visit the Rice Fields

Mymensingh is known for its rice fields and farmlands. Take a day trip to explore the rice paddies and learn about the local farming practices.

Attend a Tribal Festival

Mymensingh is home to several ethnic and tribal communities. Attend one of their festivals where the locals showcase their unique cultural practices.

Food and Drink Scene of Mymensingh

Must-Try Dishes

Morog Pulao

Morog Pulao is a signature dish of Mymensingh, made with rice, chicken, yogurt, and a mix of traditional spices. The dish has a unique flavor and is a must-try for visitors.

Shorshe Ilish

Shorshe Ilish is a popular local dish made from Hilsa fish, cooked in a curry of mustard, turmeric, chili, and other spices.

Mutton Rezala

Mutton Rezala is a spicy mutton curry that’s rich in flavors and is typically served with naan or rice.

Local Beverages

Sharbat

Sharbat is a sweet drink made from rose petals, sugar syrup, and water, making it the perfect refreshment on hot days.

Lassi

Lassi is a sweet, yogurt-based drink that is perfect for cooling down on hot days.

Mymensingh has a rich craft industry, offering visitors a variety of local handicrafts and products, including baskets, cane products, brass utensils, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Mymensingh

Mymensingh is a vibrant city and is open to visitors; however, it’s essential to respect the country’s culture and customs. Visitors should dress modestly and seek permission before photographing people or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mymensingh

The best time to visit Mymensingh is during the winter months (November to February) when the climate is suitable for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mymensingh

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of Mymensingh.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn some local phrases to communicate with locals; do not solely rely on English.
  • Try the local cuisines.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or pollute the environment.
  • Do not photograph people or religious sites without permission.
  • Do not display affection in public, as it’s not socially acceptable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mymensingh

Mymensingh is relatively safe for travelers. Visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, like avoiding unfamiliar areas only travel with licensed guides, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or expensive items.

Health in Mymensingh

Visitors should seek vaccination and medication advice from a healthcare professional before traveling. Visitors should drink bottled water and take the necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Mymensingh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mymensingh

The nearest airport to Mymensingh is Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka, around 120 kilometers away. Visitors can hire taxis or take buses from the airport to reach Mymensingh.

Bus Stations Near Mymensingh

Mymensingh has several buses connecting it to other parts of the country. Visitors can take several buses departing from Dhaka’s central bus terminal and reach Mymensingh within 4 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can hire private taxis or take a shared ride to reach Mymensingh. Visitors can also rent cars but must have a valid international driving license and experience driving in the region.

How to Get Around in Mymensingh

Visitors can use taxis, buses or rickshaws to travel around the city. The most convenient transport option is to hire a private car, which is available through car rental agencies.

Where to Go Next from Mymensingh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dhaka

Dhaka, the capital city of Bangladesh, is a bustling metropolis that’s well worth a visit. Visitors can explore its many historic landmarks, museums, and markets.

Srimangal

Srimangal is a picturesque town located in the heart of Bangladesh’s tea country. Visitors can explore the lush tea plantations, wildlife sanctuaries, and local markets.

Conclusion

Mymensingh is a culturally rich and historically significant city in Bangladesh. Visitors can explore the city’s many landmarks and natural attractions, taste the local food, and indulge in outdoor adventures. By using this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their trip with ease, ensuring a memorable and hassle-free stay in Mymensingh.
