Introduction to Sylhet

Nestled in the heart of Northeastern Bangladesh, Sylhet is a beautiful city that offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions. Known for its tea production, mystical shrines, and stunning landscapes, it is a must-visit destination for those seeking an off-the-beaten-track adventure. In this guide, we will take you through the top attractions, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local customs and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Sylhet

Sylhet is a city with a rich history and breathtaking natural landscapes. Here are some of the top places to visit on your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Shrine of Hazrat Shah Jalal: A historical shrine dedicated to Sufi saint Shah Jalal, known for promoting Islam in the region.
  • The Shrine of Hazrat Shah Paran: Another important shrine dedicated to the nephew of Shah Jalal.
  • Tea Gardens: The region is known for its lush tea plantations and you can visit the Madhabpur Lake and Tea Resort for a mesmerizing view of the tea gardens.
  • Lalakhal: Lalakhal is a beautiful river inlet named after the red colour of the water. You can take a boat trip and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding hills.
  • Kulaura Railway Station: This hundred and nineteen-year-old station is a UNESCO heritage site and is known for its architecture.

Natural Attractions of Sylhet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ratargul Swamp Forest: Ratargul Swamp Forest is a hidden gem and the only freshwater swamp forest in Bangladesh. You can explore the forest by boat or hike along the many trails.
  • Lawachara National Park: Located in the northeastern part of Bangladesh, Lawachara National Park is a popular destination for birdwatching and trekking adventures.
  • Sari River: Sari River originates in India and passes through Sylhet for 72km. It is one of the best places for white-water rafting and kayaking in Bangladesh.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sylhet

Sylhet has many picturesque trails for those who love hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Jaflong Hills: Jaflong is a popular spot for trekking and is known for its limestone hills and Khasia tribal villages.
  • Madhabpur Lake and Tea Resort: The Madhabpur Lake has a cycling trail around its perimeter where you can enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding hills and tea gardens.
  • Baikka Beel Wetland Sanctuary: A wildlife sanctuary located near the Baikka Beel wetland, which is home to over 200 species of birds, reptiles and freshwater fish.

Best Things to Do in Sylhet: Activities and Tours

Sylhet offers an array of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the tea gardens and learn about the tea production process.
  • Take a boat ride in Lalakhal.
  • Visit the Ratargul Swamp Forest and explore the ecosystem of the only freshwater swamp forest in Bangladesh.
  • Go trekking and hiking in Lawachara National Park
  • White-water rafting and kayaking in the Sari River

If you have a week to spare, here is a sample itinerary that covers the top attractions in Sylhet:

Food and Drink Scene of Sylhet

Sylhet is known for its unique cuisine and local beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pitha: A traditional rice cake served with coconut and date palm syrup.
  • Borhani: A spicy yoghurt drink made with mint, coriander, and spices.
  • Panshi: A local fish delicacy cooked in mustard oil, herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Srimangal tea: Srimangal produces some of the best tea in Bangladesh. Make sure to try the local brew.
  • Borhani: As mentioned before, it’s a yogurt-based drink with spices, perfect for quenching your thirst during hot and humid weather.

Where to Stay in Sylhet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Sylhet, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include the Hotel Naz Garden, Hotel Hilton City and the Hotel Star Pacific.

What to Buy in Sylhet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sylhet is known for its traditional handicrafts and tea. Here are some of the local products that you might want to bring home:

  • Traditional hand-woven shawls and sarees
  • Brass ornaments and utensils
  • Tea leaves

Local Culture and Customs of Sylhet

Sylhet is home to a rich cultural heritage and has a unique blend of South Asian cultures. The Khasia tribe is indigenous to the area and has its own unique customs and traditions. When visiting Sylhet, make sure to ask for permission before entering the local shrine, and dress appropriately when visiting mosques and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Sylhet

The best time to visit Sylhet is between November and March when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. However, the tea gardens are at their most picturesque during the monsoon season, from May to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sylhet

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and tea.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Make fun of the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit religious sites during prayer times.
  • Wear revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sylhet

Sylhet is generally safe for tourists, but it’s always best to take precautions. Avoid flashing your expensive gadgets and do not leave your valuables unattended.

Health in Sylhet

As with any travel to rural areas, make sure to have up-to-date vaccination shots and carry insect repellents to avoid mosquito bites. It’s also advisable to drink only bottled water and be careful of what you eat.

Getting to Sylhet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sylhet is well connected via road, rail and air. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Sylhet

Sylhet has its own international airport, the Osmani International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Sylhet

There are several bus stations in Sylhet, including:

  • Saidabad Bus Terminal
  • Sylhet Bus Terminal
  • Fakirapool Bus Terminal

Other Options

There are also several private car rental services available in Sylhet.

How to Get Around in Sylhet

The best way to get around in Sylhet is by hiring a private car or a taxi. Public transportation options include local buses and rickshaws.

Where to Go Next from Sylhet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Bangladesh, here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Sylhet that you might want to explore:

  • Dhaka: The capital city of Bangladesh is just a few hours away from Sylhet and is known for its rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Chittagong: Known for its scenic beaches and bustling port, Chittagong is a popular destination among tourists.
  • Cox’s Bazar: This beautiful coastal town is known for its scenic beaches and seafood and is perfect if you want to relax and unwind.

Conclusion

Sylhet is a hidden gem in Bangladesh, offering breathtaking natural landscapes and unique cultural experiences. In this comprehensive guide, we have covered everything you need to know to plan your trip to Sylhet, including the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, local customs, and much more. Don’t forget to pack your camera and your sense of adventure!
