Introduction to Maldives

Maldives is an island nation located in South Asia, in the Indian Ocean. Comprising 26 atolls with over 1,000 coral islands, it is a haven for tourists looking for serene and picturesque beaches, crystal clear waters, and diverse marine life. Maldives is also known as the “Sunny Side of Life” because of its tropical climate and warm temperatures throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Maldives

Maldives offers a plethora of attractions, from historical landmarks and cultural experiences, to natural wonders and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top destinations to visit in Maldives.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum - Located in the capital city of Malé, this museum exhibits a collection of artifacts and historical items that reflect the country’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Hukuru Miskiy Mosque - Also known as the Old Friday Mosque, it is one of the oldest mosques in Maldives and is an important religious site.
  • Malé Fish Market - A bustling market selling fresh seafood and other local produce, a great place to experience the local culture and cuisine.

Natural Attractions of Maldives: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaches - Maldives is home to some of the most pristine and beautiful beaches in the world, such as Veligandu Island Beach and Reethi Beach.
  • Coral Reefs - The Maldives is surrounded by an extensive coral reef system, making it a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling.
  • Sunset Cruise - A romantic and scenic way to enjoy the breathtaking views of the Maldives sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maldives

Maldives is not widely known for its hiking or biking routes, but there are still a few options for those who want to explore the islands on foot or by bike. One of the most popular routes is around the islands of Hulhumalé and Villingili. Another option is exploring the mangrove forests on the island of Addu.

Best Things to Do in Maldives: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving - Maldives is a popular destination for scuba diving, offering an abundance of marine life and colorful coral reefs.
  • Snorkeling - A great alternative to scuba diving, snorkeling is a fantastic way to explore the underwater world of Maldives.
  • Water Sports - Maldives offers a variety of water sports such as kayaking, jet skiing, and windsurfing.
  • Island Hopping - Maldives has countless islands worth exploring, and island hopping is a great way to discover the country’s hidden gems.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maldives

Day 1: Arrive in Malé and check-in at your hotel. Explore the capital city and visit the National Museum and Hukuru Miskiy Mosque.

Day 2-3: Go scuba diving or snorkeling to experience the abundant underwater life of Maldives. Visit Maafushi Island for a taste of local culture and cuisine.

Day 4: Take a sunset cruise and relax on the tranquil beaches of Veligandu Island.

Day 5-6: Go on an island-hopping adventure and explore the many islands of Maldives.

Day 7: Enjoy one last day on the beach before departing Maldives.

Food and Drink Scene of Maldives

Maldives is known for its seafood and coconut-based curries, but also has a variety of international cuisines to choose from. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garudhiya - A fish broth served with rice and chili paste.
  • Mas Riha - A coconut-based fish curry served with rice.
  • Hedhikaa - A variety of traditional Maldivian snacks like Samosas and Gulha.

Local Beverages

  • Raa - A local alcoholic drink made from the sap of the coconut palm tree.
  • Drumstick Juice - A refreshing and healthy drink made from the juice of the Moringa tree.

Where to Stay in Maldives: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maldives offers a wide range of accommodations, from luxury resorts to budget guesthouses. Some of the top options include:

  • The St. Regis Maldives Vommuli Resort - A luxurious resort located on a remote island.
  • Taj Exotica Resort & Spa - A secluded resort offering stunning beach and ocean views.
  • Biyadhoo Island Resort - An affordable resort with a private beach and great snorkeling opportunities.

What to Buy in Maldives: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thundu Kunaa - Locally made mats used for sitting or sleeping.
  • Dhon Riha - A traditional Maldivian dress for women.
  • Local Crafts - Maldives is famous for traditional handicrafts like wooden boxes, coconut-shell artifacts, and lacquer work.

Local Culture and Customs of Maldives

The Maldives is a Muslim country, and it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Dress modestly and remove shoes when entering homes, mosques, or other religious sites. Alcohol is prohibited in public places.

Best Time to Visit Maldives

The best time to visit Maldives is between the months of November and April when the weather is dry and sunny with little rain. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so be prepared for higher prices and more crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maldives

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Swim in the ocean at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maldives

Maldives is generally considered a safe country for tourists. However, take precautions against petty crime like pickpocketing, particularly in crowded areas.

Health in Maldives

Make sure to get the recommended vaccinations before traveling to Maldives, particularly for Hepatitis A and B. Use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.

Getting to Maldives: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maldives

The main airport in Maldives is Velana International Airport, located in the capital city of Malé. There are several major airlines that fly to Maldives, including Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Etihad Airways.

Bus Stations Near Maldives

There are no bus stations in Maldives, but taxis and private transfers are readily available.

Other Options

Ferries and domestic air services are available for inter-island travel in Maldives.

How to Get Around in Maldives

The best way to get around Maldives is by speedboat or seaplane, particularly for traveling between islands. Taxis and buses are also options for getting around in the capital city of Malé.

Where to Go Next from Maldives: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sri Lanka - A short flight from Maldives, it offers a rich cultural and culinary experience.
  • India - The bustling city of Mumbai or the serene beaches of Goa are popular destinations from Maldives.

Conclusion

Maldives is truly a tropical paradise worth visiting at least once in a lifetime. From the beautiful beaches to the diverse marine life, there is something for everyone in Maldives. This comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your trip and experience the best that Maldives has to offer.
