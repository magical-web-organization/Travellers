Introduction to Kuredu

The Maldives — a tropical paradise with turquoise waters, golden sand beaches and palm trees. Kuredu is situated in the Lhaviyani Atoll, at the northern part of the Maldives. It has everything you could want from an island getaway — crystal clear waters, incredible marine life, lush vegetation and luxurious resorts.

Whether you’re looking for an action-packed holiday or a peaceful retreat, there’s something for everyone on this island.

Food and Drink Scene of Kuredu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garudhiya (a fish soup served with rice and chili paste)
  • Mas huni (a breakfast dish made with shredded smoked tuna, coconut, onion and chili)
  • Fihunu mas (grilled fish in a spicy sauce)

Local Beverages

  • Raa (a sweet and mild toddy tapped from palm trees)
  • Kurumba (a coconut milk-based drink)

Local Culture and Customs of Kuredu

The Maldives is predominantly a Muslim country, so it’s important to be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when outside of the resort and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Kuredu

High season runs from December to April, which is the dry season, making it a fantastic time to visit. May to November is considered the low season, with more rains and less sunshine, but it can still be a great time to visit with less crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuredu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Protect the marine life by not touching or removing coral and avoiding sunscreens that contain harmful chemicals.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Drink alcohol outside of the resorts
  • Wear revealing clothing outside of the resort

Safety and Health

Safety in Kuredu

The Maldives is a safe country for tourists, but it’s always best to be vigilant. Avoid walking alone at night outside of the resort and be mindful of your belongings.

Health in Kuredu

Tap water in the Maldives is not drinkable, so stick to bottled water. Sunscreen and insect repellent are must-haves, as well as any medication you might need.

Getting to Kuredu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kuredu

  • Velana International Airport
  • Ifuru Airport

Other Options

  • Speedboat transfers from the airport
  • A sea plane transfer from Velana International Airport

How to Get Around in Kuredu

The island is small enough to walk or cycle around, but you can also take a buggy ride or a boat transfer to get around.
