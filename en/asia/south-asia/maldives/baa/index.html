Introduction to Baa

Nestled in the northern part of the Maldives, Baa is a cluster of 75 islands that makes up the Baa Atoll, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve that boasts pristine beaches, turquoise lagoons, and abundant marine life.

With its outstanding natural beauty, rich culture, and welcoming locals, Baa is a destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature lover, a water sports enthusiast, or simply in search of relaxation and tranquility amidst breathtaking surroundings, Baa is the perfect place for your next vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Baa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Utheemu Ganduvaru: This 500-year-old palace situated in Utheemu Island is a significant historical landmark in Baa. It was the residence of Sultan Mohamed Thakurufaanu, one of the most prominent heroes in Maldivian history, who fought against Portuguese invaders in the 16th century.
  • Baa Atoll Cultural Center: Located in Eydhafushi Island, this center provides a glimpse into the rich and vibrant culture of Baa, featuring exhibitions, workshops, and performances.

Natural Attractions of Baa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hanifaru Bay: This protected marine area is a must-visit for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. It is home to the world’s largest gathering of manta rays and whale sharks, making it a unique and unforgettable experience.
  • Dhigali Haa: The highest point in Baa Atoll, this island offers panoramic views of the surrounding islands and crystal-clear waters.
  • Inguraidhoo Island: Known for its lush greenery and peaceful atmosphere, this island is a great choice for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baa

  • Baa Atoll Biosphere Reserve: This 53,000-hectare area is a treasure trove of natural wonders and biodiversity. It offers several hiking and biking trails that take you through lush forests, coastal mangroves, and sparkling waters.
  • Kihaadhuffaru Island: This island is perfect for cycling, with its flat and well-maintained roads that take you around the island’s scenic spots.

Best Things to Do in Baa: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and diving: Baa Atoll is a paradise for underwater exploration, with its vibrant coral reefs and abundant marine life.
  • Island hopping: Explore the different islands of Baa Atoll and discover their unique charms and hidden gems.
  • Sunset cruise: Experience the breathtaking beauty of Baa’s sunsets as you sail across its tranquil waters.
  • Traditional Bodu Beru dance: Witness the lively and colorful dance performances of locals, showcasing Baa’s rich cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Baa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garudhiya: A traditional fish broth served with rice, lime, chili, and onion.
  • Mas huni: A staple breakfast dish made with shredded smoked tuna, coconut, and onions.
  • Kulhi Boakibaa: A popular snack made with mashed, baked fish, coconut, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Raa: A traditional alcoholic drink made with fermented toddy extracted from coconut palm trees.
  • Sai: A refreshing drink made with cardamom, lime, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Baa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baa Atoll offers a range of accommodation options catering to different travel styles and budgets. From luxurious private island resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, there’s something for everyone in Baa. Some of the top options include:

  • Soneva Fushi: A luxurious resort set on a private island, offering unparalleled comfort and luxury in the heart of the Maldives.
  • Reethi Beach Resort: A budget-friendly resort that offers comfortable rooms, stunning vistas, and a range of activities.
  • Dharavandhoo Stay: A cozy guesthouse that provides a homely atmosphere and convenient location for exploring Baa’s attractions.

What to Buy in Baa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lacquerware: Baa is known for its handcrafted lacquerware, ranging from intricately designed boxes to colorful bowls and plates.
  • Reef fish: Canned reef fish is a popular souvenir item to bring home from the Maldives. It is a tasty and healthy snack and a great way to experience the local cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Baa

Baa’s people are predominantly Muslim, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Women are expected to dress modestly when in public places, and alcohol consumption is restricted to private resorts. Friday is the local weekend, and many businesses and attractions may be closed or have limited hours on this day.

Best Time to Visit Baa

The best time to visit Baa Atoll is between November and April when the monsoon winds bring dry and sunny weather. The water visibility is excellent for snorkeling and diving during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baa

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Protect the environment by avoiding littering and using eco-friendly means of transportation.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local culture or religious practices.
  • Damage coral reefs or disturb marine life.
  • Carry alcohol or pork items in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baa

Baa is a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take necessary precautions and refrain from venturing out after dark or in secluded areas.

Health in Baa

Visitors to Baa should be up to date on routine vaccinations and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases. It is recommended to carry adequate bug spray and use it regularly, especially during the evenings. Tap water in the Maldives is not potable, and visitors should ensure that they consume only bottled water or filtered water from reliable sources.

Getting to Baa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baa

  • Velana International Airport: The main international airport in the Maldives, located in Malé, the capital city. Domestic flights from Malé to Baa Atoll are available from here.

Bus Stations Near Baa

There are no bus stations in the Maldives, as the islands are not connected by land transport.

Other Options

  • Speedboat transfers: Many resorts in Baa Atoll offer speedboat transfers from Malé.
  • Seaplane transfers: For a more scenic arrival, seaplane transfers are available from Velana International Airport to several islands in Baa Atoll.

How to Get Around in Baa

Baa Atoll’s islands are mostly small and can be easily explored by foot or bicycle. Resorts and guesthouses also offer excursions and transportation services, such as boat rides or guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Baa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dharavandhoo Island: A nearby island that is worth visiting for its stunning beaches, cultural attractions, and traditional handicrafts.
  • Alifushi Island: A small island known for its peaceful atmosphere and water sports, such as kiteboarding and windsurfing.

Conclusion

Baa Atoll is a hidden gem of the Maldives, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. With its pristine beaches, colorful reefs, and welcoming locals, Baa is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your dream vacation to Baa and created unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.
