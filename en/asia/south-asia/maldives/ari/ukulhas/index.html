Introduction to Ukulhas

The remote island of Ukulhas can be found in the Ari Atoll of the Maldives. Its location in the midst of the azure Indian Ocean draws tourists to its quiet beaches and warm waters. Visitors to the island can discover its rich culture, go hiking or biking, explore local food scenes, and much more. Our Ukulhas travel guide is designed to help you plan your Maldives vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Ukulhas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to cultural attractions, Ukulhas has two interesting options to offer.

The Ukulhas Cultural Centre, located near the harbor, is an excellent place to gain insight into the island’s culture and history. It showcases exhibits relating to local customs, beliefs, and crafts, and also houses a small library.

Another site worth visiting is the Ukulhas Thundi Mosque. Built over 200 years ago in the traditional Islamic architectural style, it is one of the oldest mosques in the Maldives.

Natural Attractions of Ukulhas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ukulhas is famous for its picturesque beaches and coral reefs. The waters surrounding the island are ideal for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts, with plenty of marine life to spot. Going for a swim in the turquoise lagoon or taking a sunset walk on the beach are perfect ways to relax and unwind.

Those seeking adventure can go for fishing trips, dolphin watching tours, or hiking and biking expeditions around the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ukulhas

Ukulhas has numerous routes ideal for hiking and biking, allowing visitors to explore the island’s natural beauty. One such route is from the beachfront towards the northern end of the island, past the glistening lake filled with marine life, and to a secluded beach. Another worthwhile route is to take the island’s main road from the harbor to the end of the island, where you can enjoy stunning views of the Indian Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Ukulhas: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to engage in while visiting Ukulhas. Some of the popular things to do on the island are:

  • Snorkeling or diving in the surrounding waters to experience the diverse marine life and colorful coral reefs.
  • Fishing in the Indian Ocean with a local fisherman to catch some of the island’s fresh seafood.
  • Going on a dolphin or whale watching tour to see some of the Maldives’ most significant sea creatures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ukulhas

Here’s a Ukulhas itinerary idea for one week:

Day 1 - Arrive in Ukulhas, tour the Cultural Centre, and spend the evening on the beach. Day 2 - Go on a snorkeling trip and enjoy water sports activities. Day 3 - Take a walking tour of the island and visit the Ukulhas Thundi Mosque. Day 4 - Go island hopping and visit some of the nearby islands. Day 5 - Spend the day fishing and cook your catch for dinner. Day 6 - Go on a dolphin or whale watching tour. Day 7 - Relax and unwind with a spa treatment before leaving the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Ukulhas

Must-Try Dishes

While in Ukulhas, you’ll surely want to experience the local Maldivian cuisine. Some of the most popular dishes on the island include:

  • Mas huni: A flavorful dish made with smoked tuna, coconut, onion, and chili, often served for breakfast.

  • Garudhiya: A fish soup made with tuna and served with steamed rice and chili paste.

  • Kulhi boakibaa: A fish cake made with tuna and spices, baked until crispy on the outside.

Local Beverages

Apart from the local coconut water, Ukulhas also offers some alcoholic beverages, including:

  • Rai: A traditional local vodka made with fermented coconut sap. It is served in a shot glass and pairs well with fish dishes.

  • Raa: A traditional distilled alcoholic beverage made from toddy. It tastes similar to rum and is often mixed with coke or coconut water.

What to Buy in Ukulhas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the souvenirs and local products to take home from Ukulhas include:

  • Handmade souvenirs made from coconut shells, wood or sea shells
  • Local snacks, like fish chips and coconut candy
  • Beachwear and other island-themed accessories

Local Culture and Customs of Ukulhas

Ukulhas locals are mostly Muslims and follow traditional values and customs. Visitors are respectfully asked to wear appropriate clothing while visiting the public areas of the island, as well as while at the Cultural Center and the mosque.

Best Time to Visit Ukulhas

Ukulhas is a year-round destination, with temperatures staying warm and pleasant throughout the year between 27 and 30 degrees Celsius. April to December is the ideal time to visit as the weather is dry, and the skies are usually clear, providing the best conditions for snorkeling or diving in the turquoise waters of the Indian Ocean.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ukulhas

Do:

  • Respect local customs, such as dressing modestly while in public areas.
  • Try the local food and beverages to get a taste of the island’s culture.
  • Be mindful of the ocean and any potential hazards.

Don’t:

  • Use or carry illegal drugs.
  • Take any coral, shells or other marine life on the island as souvenirs.
  • Disrespect the inhabitants, locals, or other visitors of the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ukulhas

Ukulhas is generally a safe and peaceful place to visit; however, visitors are encouraged to remain cautious and sensible throughout their stay.

Tourists are advised to maintain respect towards the Maldives’ cultural and religious heritage and preserve the beauty and tranquility of the local environment.

Health in Ukulhas

Although there are no general malaria warnings, visitors are advised to check the latest relevant health information and to take necessary precautions while visiting Ukulhas. Visitors should only drink bottled water while on the island and avoid street food.

Getting to Ukulhas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ukulhas

The nearest airport to Ukulhas is Velana International Airport in Male. Once you arrive there, you will have several options for getting to the island. The journey can be made by speedboat, domestic flight, or seaplane. A round trip can cost anywhere between $200-$600.

Bus Stations Near Ukulhas

As Ukulhas is an island, there are no bus stations. However, after reaching the destination airport, there are ferries available to take visitors from the airport to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can reach the island by taking a private speedboat or a public ferry from the Male International Airport to the island.

How to Get Around in Ukulhas

Ukulhas is a small island, which visitors can explore on foot or bike. Hiking tours can be arranged with local tour guides, which is a great way to explore the island’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Ukulhas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After spending a week in Ukulhas and soaking up its beauty, visitors can go for excursions to nearby islands in the Ari Atoll or explore other famous Maldivian atolls like North Male Atoll or South Male Atoll.

Conclusion

Ukulhas in the Maldives is a haven for those seeking serenity, natural landscapes, and adventure away from the bustling city. Use our comprehensive guide to planning and discovering the island’s beauty while experiencing its unique, rich culture. It’s a vacation you won’t forget.
