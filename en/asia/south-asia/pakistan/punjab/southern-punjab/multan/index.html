Introduction to Multan

Multan, also known as the ‘The City of Sufis’ due to its rich cultural heritage and significant shrine of Sufi mystic Bahauddin Zakariya, is a historic city in Southern Punjab, Pakistan. It is one of the oldest cities in the world and has been continuously inhabited for over 5,000 years. Multan is a blend of old-world charm and modernity, with its ancient landmarks, bazaars, and tombs that coexist alongside modern amenities like cafes and malls.

List of Places to Visit in Multan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Bahauddin Zakariya Tomb Bahauddin Zakariya Tomb is a significant shrine in Multan that attracts thousands of devotees every year. It is the final resting place of Bahauddin Zakariya, the Sufi saint after whom the shrine and university are named.

2. Multan Museum Multan Museum is situated within the Qasim Bagh Fort and showcases a vast array of exhibits related to the history and culture of Multan. The museum’s collections include miniature paintings, coins, stamps, sculptures, and ancient pottery.

3. Shah Rukn-e-Alam Shrine Shah Rukn-e-Alam Shrine is a striking monument built in the 14th century and is one of the most visited Sufi shrines in Pakistan. The shrine stands in the center of the city and is dedicated to the renowned Sufi saint, Syed Jalaluddin Surkh-Posh Bukhari.

Natural Attractions of Multan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Lake View Park Lake View Park is a scenic spot in Multan that offers a wonderful respite from city life. The park is spread across 50 acres and is home to a man-made lake, amusement park rides, and gardens.

2. Sufi Santosh Hill Sufi Santosh Hill is a scenic location near Multan that holds significant spiritual and historical importance. The hill is said to have been a refuge for the Sufi saints and houses ancient shrines and tombs.

3. Chenab River The Chenab River runs through Multan and is a great place to engage in outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Multan

1. Jalalpur Canal Jalalpur Canal is a popular spot for hiking and biking in Multan. The canal runs along the edge of the city and is surrounded by greenery, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

2. Fort Kohna Fort Kohna is a historic fort located on a hill near Multan. The fort is accessible by foot and is a great place to explore for anyone interested in history and architecture.

Best Things to Do in Multan: Activities and Tours

1. The Old City Walk Taking a walk through the narrow lanes and colorful bazaars of the old city is one of the best things to do in Multan. This guided walking tour will help you discover the city’s hidden gems, stunning mosques, and rich cultural heritage.

2. Multan Excursion Plan a day trip from Multan to explore the nearby destinations such as Uch Sharif, the ancient site of Alexander the Great’s battle with King Porus, and the stunning Derawar Fort on the edge of the Cholistan Desert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Multan

Day 1-2: Explore the Old City and visit the famous shrines and mosques. Day 3-4: Discover the cultural heritage of Multan by visiting museums like Multan Museum and old famous tombs and forts such as Fort Kohna. Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Uch Sharif. Day 6: Visit the scenic areas of Multan, including the Lake View Park and Chenab River. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products in the bazaars before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Multan

Must-Try Dishes

1. Sohan Halwa Sohan Halwa is a sweet delicacy that is synonymous with Multan. It is made from wheat, sugar, and ghee and is a delicious treat enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

2. Multani Saji Multani Saji is a popular breakfast item in Multan that consists of roasted chicken, eggs, and local spices. It is usually served with hot bread and tea.

Local Beverages

1. Lassi Lassi is a popular yogurt-based drink that is served with local meals in Multan. It is a refreshing drink that helps to counterbalance the spiciness of local dishes.

2. Sugar Cane Juice Sugar cane juice is a refreshing drink that is made by squeezing the juice out of fresh sugar cane stalks. It is a popular summer drink in Multan and can be found in most local markets and street vendors.

2. Hotel Executive Residency Hotel Executive Residency is a budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and proximity to the city center.

What to Buy in Multan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Handicrafts Multan’s bazaars are full of handicrafts such as pottery, wood-carved items, and traditional textiles. They make excellent souvenirs and gifts to take home.

2. Sohan Halwa Sohan Halwa is not just a sweet delicacy to indulge in but also a popular souvenir for visitors to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Multan

Multan is a culturally diverse city, and the locals are proud of their rich heritage. Visitors should respect the cultural norms and customs of the city, such as dressing modestly, particularly when visiting religious sites, shrines, and mosques, and removing footwear before entering these locations.

Best Time to Visit Multan

The best time to visit Multan is October to March when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers in Multan can be extremely hot and humid, and it’s better to avoid visiting during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Multan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies, especially Sohan Halwa.
  • Respect the cultural norms and customs of the city.
  • Visit the famous shrines, mosques, and tombs of Multan.
  • Explore the bazaars for handicrafts and local products.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting religious sites and shrines.
  • Disrespect local customs or religious beliefs.
  • Drink tap water without boiling or filtering it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Multan

Multan is a relatively safe city for visitors, although it’s advisable to exercise caution and avoid going to isolated areas at night.

Health in Multan

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent any health issues during the trip. It’s also important to carry a first aid kit and medication for any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Multan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Multan

Multan International Airport is the main airport located in the city and is well-connected to major cities in Pakistan and some international destinations like Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Bus Stations Near Multan

Multan is well-connected by bus to the rest of Pakistan. The Daewoo Bus service offers comfortable and affordable travel options to major cities like Lahore and Karachi.

Other Options

Taxis, rickshaws, and private cars are available for hire, making road travel within Multan very convenient.

How to Get Around in Multan

The most popular and convenient way to travel within Multan is by rickshaw, which is widely available and relatively inexpensive. Taxis and private cars can also be hired for easy navigation through the city.

Where to Go Next from Multan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Multan’s central location makes it an excellent base for exploring other destinations in Pakistan, including:

  • Bahawalpur
  • Lahore
  • Karachi
  • Islamabad

Conclusion

Multan is a vibrant city that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and modernity. This comprehensive guide provides all the information needed to plan an unforgettable trip to this ancient city of Southern Punjab, Pakistan. From exploring the old bazaars to discovering cultural wonders and natural beauty, Multan has something to offer for everyone.
