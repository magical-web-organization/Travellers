The Ultimate Travel Guide to Vehari, Pakistan

Vehari is a picturesque city located in Southern Punjab, in the Punjab province of Pakistan. The city is a hub of rich culture and offers a plethora of attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers. Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, local culture or just want to relax and explore the city, Vehari has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will show you what to do, see, and taste in Vehari.

List of Places to Visit in Vehari

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ghazi Khan Museum: Located in the heart of Vehari city, the museum showcases the cultural and historical significance of the region. The museum has several artifacts, including coins, pottery, and weapons.
  • Jamia Masjid: The grand mosque in Vehari city is an excellent example of traditional Islamic architecture, and it stands out for its intricate stonework and ornate decoration.
  • Tomb of Shah Shams Sabzwari: The famous shrine of Sufi saint Shah Shams Sabzwari is a significant site of pilgrimage for the believers in the region. It is a beautiful white marble-domed structure.

Natural Attractions of Vehari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cholistan Desert: The vast desert is a natural reserve that is home to some exotic plants and animals, including the Chinkara deer and the desert cat. The dunes of the desert are perfect for camping, dune bashing, and camel and jeep safaris.
  • Sutlej River: The Sutlej river flowing through Vehari offers breathtaking landscapes and is a fantastic spot for picnics, boating, and fishing. The best time to visit the river is during the monsoon season when the water levels rise, and the river is at its full glory.
  • Kutcha Lake: Located around 30 kilometers south of Vehari, Kutcha Lake is a man-made lake that offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The lake is an excellent spot for boating and fishing, and you can also find plenty of restaurants and cafes around it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vehari

  • Cholistan Jeep Safaris: A jeep safari adventure in the Cholistan desert is an excellent way to explore the natural beauty of the region.
  • Biking through Rural Punjab: The nearby villages of Vehari offer quiet and serene biking trails that take you through the lush green fields, mango orchards, and rural life of Punjab.

Best Things to Do in Vehari: Activities and Tours

  • Attend a Sufi Qawwali Night: Sufi Qawwali music is an integral part of the Punjabi culture. Attending a night of soulful music and poetry, where devotees express their love for the Almighty and their faith, is an experience you must not miss.
  • Explore the Rich Flavors of Vehari: Vehari offers an array of dishes that are full of flavor and unique taste. From the scrumptious paratha rolls to the traditional chicken korma, you must try the local cuisine.
  • Visit the Weekly Bazaars: The weekly bazaars in Vehari are a great place to explore the local culture and its unique products. You can find everything from pottery, spices, and traditional handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vehari

Day 1: Visit the Ghazi Khan Museum and the Jamia Masjid.

Day 2: Go on a Cholistan Jeep Safari and camp overnight in the desert.

Day 3: Explore the Sutlej River and have a picnic by the river.

Day 4: Visit the Kutcha Lake and enjoy boating and fishing.

Day 5: Attend a Sufi Qawwali Night.

Day 6: Try the local cuisine in the weekly bazaars of Vehari.

Day 7: Visit the Tomb of Shah Shams Sabzwari.

Food and Drink Scene of Vehari

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paratha Roll: A flaky paratha stuffed with spicy chicken or beef filling, rolled up, and served with chutney and yogurt.
  • Nihari: A slow-cooked beef or lamb stew that is rich in flavor and spices. It is served with hot naan bread.
  • Siri Paye: A traditional breakfast dish made with goat or lamb feet cooked in a spicy curry.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Sugarcane Juice: A sweet and refreshing juice made by crushing fresh sugarcane and adding lemon juice and mint leaves.

Where to Stay in Vehari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pearl Continental Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Vehari city. It has all the modern amenities and offers a great view of the city.
  • Al-Zara Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house that offers comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • Jinnah Hostel: A youth hostel that provides affordable accommodation and also organizes tours and activities around Vehari.

What to Buy in Vehari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Vehari is known for its beautiful pottery, and you can find a wide range of decorative and functional ceramic items in the local bazaars.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: From embroidered bedspreads to woolen carpets, Vehari has a rich tradition of handicrafts that makes for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Vehari

Vehari is a conservative city, and it is recommended that tourists dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. It is customary to take off your shoes before entering a mosque or any other religious site. Alcohol and drugs are strictly prohibited.

Best Time to Visit Vehari

The best time to visit Vehari is during the winter months (November to March), when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vehari

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Visit the historical and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol or do drugs.
  • Disrespect locals or their religious beliefs.
  • Dress inappropriately.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vehari

Vehari is a relatively safe city, and tourists can move around freely. However, it is recommended to take precautions while camping in the desert and avoid going out alone at night.

Health in Vehari

Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to avoid any foodborne illnesses. It is recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B before traveling to Pakistan.

Getting to Vehari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vehari

The nearest airports to Vehari are Multan International Airport (about 50 kilometers) and Bahawalpur Airport (about 100 kilometers).

Bus Stations Near Vehari

Vehari has well-connected bus stations that are connected to all major cities in Pakistan.

Other Options

You can hire a private car or taxi to travel from Multan or Bahawalpur to Vehari.

How to Get Around in Vehari

Vehari is a small city, and the best way to explore it is on foot. However, you can also hire a taxi or rickshaw to get around the city at a reasonable price.

Where to Go Next from Vehari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Multan: Known as Pakistan’s City of Saints, Multan is a city rich in historical and cultural significance. It is located around 70 kilometers from Vehari.
  • Bahawalpur: Located about 120 kilometers from Vehari, Bahawalpur is known for its beautiful palaces and gardens, and it is a great place to explore the traditional architecture of Punjab.

Conclusion

Vehari is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and historical importance.
