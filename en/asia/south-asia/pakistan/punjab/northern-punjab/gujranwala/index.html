Gujranwala Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tourist Guide

Located in Northern Punjab of Pakistan, Gujranwala is a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage. Gujranwala is often referred to as the “city of wrestlers” due to the traditional sports of Pehlwani and Kushti being deeply ingrained in the culture. The city is famous for its historical sites, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. In this comprehensive guide to Gujranwala, we will explore top attractions, outdoor activities, local culture, best places to stay, and essential travel information.

List of Places to Visit in Gujranwala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gujranwala boasts a rich cultural heritage dating back to the Mughal era, with several historical sites and museums that are a must-visit. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Gujranwala Fort: Built in the 15th century by Emperor Akbar, the fort is a historical landmark in the city. The fort was strategically important and acted as a defensive barrier against Mongol invasions.
  • Lahoria Gate: The main entrance of the historical Gujranwala Fort. This gate is considered to be the main attraction of the fort due to its beautiful architecture and rich history.
  • Jinnah Stadium: If you are a cricket lover, you will definitely want to visit Jinnah Stadium – one of the best cricket stadiums in Pakistan. It is named after the father of the nation, Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah.
  • Gujranwala Clock Tower: The iconic Gujranwala Clock Tower is a landmark in the city and a great spot for a photo op. Made of sandstone and marble, it dates back to the British era.

Natural Attractions of Gujranwala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, Gujranwala has plenty of options for you to explore. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Socho Park: Socho Park is one of the most popular public parks in Gujranwala. With stunning views, beautiful landscapes, and a wide array of recreational activities, the park is an ideal spot for a family day out.
  • Chenab River: The Chenab River is one of the five major rivers of Pakistan and is a great place for a short trip to enjoy nature. You can enjoy a boat ride or simply relax and enjoy the scenery.
  • Sialkot Cantonment: Sialkot Cantonment is a beautiful area that offers a variety of outdoor activities, including golf, cricket, and horse riding. There are also several restaurants in the area where you can enjoy a meal with a view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gujranwala

For adventure enthusiasts, Gujranwala offers several hiking and biking trails in its vicinity. Some of the top routes include:

  • Gujranwala – Sohawa Road: This road stretches up to 8 kilometers, offering a smooth biking experience and some beautiful views.
  • Gujranwala – Sheikhopura Road: This 18-kilometer long road is perfect for a day-long biking tour. The entire route runs parallel to the Chenab River.
  • Gujranwala – Lahore Road: The road that connects Gujranwala to Lahore is a popular route among avid hikers. The total distance is about 70 kilometers, and it passes through several towns and villages.

Best Things to Do in Gujranwala: Activities and Tours

Aside from exploring the historical sites and natural attractions, there are plenty of other things to do in Gujranwala:

  • Visit Local Bazaars: Gujranwala is famous for its bazaars, and visiting these markets is a must-do activity. Head to the main market, known as Shahi Bazaar, for a wide variety of locally made products.
  • Try Traditional Food: Gujranwala is famous for its traditional cuisine, and you must try some of the local dishes such as qudrati karhai, chanay ki daal ka halwa, and laddu pethi.
  • Attend a Wrestling Match: Pehlwani and Kushti are traditional wrestling sports in Gujranwala, and you can witness live matches. The most famous venue is Akhada Stadium.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gujranwala

Day 1: Explore the Historical Sites (Gujranwala Fort, Lahoria Gate, Jinnah Stadium)

Day 2: Hiking or biking on Gujranwala - Lahore Road

Day 3: Visit the Natural Attractions (Socho Park, Chenab River, Sialkot Cantonment)

Day 4: Attend a Pehlwani or Kushti Match at Akhada Stadium

Day 5: Take a day trip to Wazirabad, a nearby town famous for its cutlery

Day 6: Shopping at the local bazaars (Shahi Bazaar)

Day 7: Try traditional food at the local restaurants in Gujranwala.

Food and Drink Scene of Gujranwala

Must-Try Dishes

Gujranwala is famous for its traditional cuisine, and here are some must-try dishes:

  • Qudrati Karhai: A spicy and flavorful local dish made with goat meat.
  • Chanay Ki Daal Ka Halwa: A sweet dessert made with lentils, butter, and cardamom.
  • Laddu Pethi: A pastry made with flour, sugar, and ghee, topped with sesame seeds.

Local Beverages

In Gujranwala, you can find an array of local beverages such as Lassi, a popular drink made with yogurt, milk, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Gujranwala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gujranwala has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Some popular options include:

  • Shelton Hotel: A luxury hotel with all modern amenities.
  • Grand Sitara: A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Mama’s Hotel: A budget-friendly option with basic facilities and clean rooms.

What to Buy in Gujranwala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gujranwala is known for its cutlery products, and you can buy items such as knives, swords, and scissors from the local markets. You can also find traditional clothes and handicrafts in the bazaars.

Local Culture and Customs of Gujranwala

Gujranwala has a unique culture that is deeply rooted in traditions and customs. People are hospitable, and you will find them welcoming and friendly. Local festivals such as Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha are widely celebrated, and you can witness the local traditions and customs during these festivals.

Best Time to Visit Gujranwala

The best time to visit Gujranwala is from November to February when the weather is pleasant and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, whereas winters are mild, cold, and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gujranwala

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Take off your shoes before entering a mosque or religious place
  • Bargain when shopping in the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Photograph locals without their consent
  • Consume alcohol in public places
  • Use foul language or make derogatory comments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gujranwala

Gujranwala is a safe city for tourists, but it’s essential to take standard precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and not going out alone at night.

Health in Gujranwala

Ensure that you drink bottled water and avoid street food that could result in stomach ailments.

Getting to Gujranwala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gujranwala

The nearest airport to Gujranwala is the Sialkot International Airport, located approximately 32 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Gujranwala

The main bus station in Gujranwala is the Butt Bus Service, located in the city center.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or hire a private car to reach Gujranwala.

How to Get Around in Gujranwala

The best way to get around Gujranwala is by taking a rickshaw. They are readily available and cost-effective. You can also hire a private car if you prefer more comfort and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Gujranwala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gujranwala is conveniently located near many popular destinations:

  • Lahore: The cultural capital of Pakistan is just a two-hour drive away from Gujranwala.
  • Wagah Border: The Wagah Border is a famous tourist spot for witnessing the flag ceremony between India and Pakistan.
  • Murree: A popular hill station with enchanting scenery is a four-hour drive from Gujranwala.

Conclusion

Gujranwala is a city rich in culture and history, offering a range of activities and attractions for visitors. Whether you are interested in exploring the natural beauty, indulging in traditional cuisine, or experiencing the local customs, Gujranwala is sure to capture your heart. Follow this comprehensive guide to make the most out of your trip to Gujranwala.
