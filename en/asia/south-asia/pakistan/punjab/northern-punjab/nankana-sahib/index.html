Introduction to Nankana Sahib

Nankana Sahib is a city located in the Northern Punjab province of Pakistan with a population of approximately 80,000 people. The historical and religious value of Nankana Sahib is significant, as this city is the birthplace of Guru Nanak Dev, the founder of Sikhism. This is why Nankana Sahib holds immense importance for Sikhs all around the world, who flock to this city every year to visit the holy sites and pay their respects. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit in Nankana Sahib, where to stay, what to eat, how to get around, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Nankana Sahib

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gurdwara Janamasthan: This is the primary attraction in Nankana Sahib, as it is the birthplace of Guru Nanak Dev. The gurdwara is a stunning white marble structure that houses many relics of Guru Nanak’s life and a well where he used to drink water.
  • Gurdwara Patti Sahib: This gurdwara is located outside the town and is where Guru Nanak is said to have spent most of his childhood. The gurdwara is surrounded by beautiful gardens and has a museum showcasing various items related to Sikh history.
  • Gurdwara Bal Lila Sahib: This gurdwara is said to be the spot where Guru Nanak played as a child. The gurdwara is elevated, with a spacious courtyard, and the architecture is inspired by the Mughal era.

Natural Attractions of Nankana Sahib: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ravi River: The Ravi River runs adjacent to the city and is a perfect spot for a refreshing dip or a relaxing picnic. The river is also known for its stunning sunsets, so make sure to plan a visit during the evening.
  • Nankana Sahib Wildlife Park: Nankana Sahib Wildlife Park is an excellent spot for nature lovers, as it houses various species of flora and fauna. Visitors can observe animals from a safe distance and learn more about the intricate ecosystems of Pakistan.
  • Hiran Minar: Hiran Minar is a unique monument built by Emperor Jehangir in memory of his beloved pet deer. It’s a great picnic spot with stunning views of the lake and the surrounding park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nankana Sahib

  • Anarkali Bazaar: Anarkali Bazaar is a bustling marketplace and is an excellent place for a leisurely walk. This bazaar is famous for traditional textiles, clothes, and jewelry.
  • Chauburji: Chauburji is a Mughal-era monument that is known for its four towers, intricate carvings, and stunning architecture. It is a must-visit for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.
  • Darbar Mahal: Darbar Mahal is a palace built by a Sikh ruler named Maharaja Ranjit Singh. The palace has stunning courtyards and ornate frescoes and is a testimony to the grandeur of the Sikh Empire.

Best Things to Do in Nankana Sahib: Activities and Tours

  • Attend a religious ceremony: One of the best things to do in Nankana Sahib is to attend a religious ceremony at one of the gurdwaras. It’s a unique experience and a fantastic opportunity to immerse oneself in the Sikh culture fully.
  • Visit the Nankana Sahib Market: Nankana Sahib Market is famous for traditional Punjabi handicrafts, clothing, and food. It’s a great place to shop for souvenirs and gifts.
  • Try the local cuisine: Punjabi cuisine is famous all around the world, and Nankana Sahib is no exception. Make sure to try the mouth-watering street food and local specialties that are unique to this region.

Food and Drink Scene of Nankana Sahib

Must-Try Dishes

  • Siri Paye: Siri Paye is a traditional Pakistani breakfast dish that is made by cooking the head and feet of a goat with ginger, garlic, and spices. It is usually served with hot naan bread and is a must-try dish.
  • Samosas: Samosas are triangular-shaped pastry pockets that are stuffed with potatoes, peas, and spices. They are often served as a snack or an appetizer.
  • Nihari: Nihari is a slow-cooked meat stew that is rich in flavor and is often served with naan bread.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: Lassi is a refreshing yogurt-based drink that is famous all around the world. It’s perfect for hot summer days and is often served with a sprinkle of cardamom.
  • Tea: Pakistani tea is famous all around the world for its unique blend of spices and herbs. Make sure to try the local tea at one of the city’s many chai shops.

Where to Stay in Nankana Sahib: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Shahzad: Hotel Shahzad is a budget-friendly hotel with clean and spacious rooms. It’s located in the heart of the city, close to many of the major attractions.
  • Nankana Sarai: Nankana Sarai is a family-owned guesthouse that is known for its warm hospitality and comfortable accommodation. It’s a great option for travelers who are looking for an authentic Pakistani experience.
  • Hotel Divine Valley: Hotel Divine Valley is a luxury hotel with modern amenities and stunning views of the city. It’s the perfect option for travelers who are looking for a more upscale experience.

What to Buy in Nankana Sahib: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Punjabi Jutti: Punjabi Juttis are traditional shoes that are handmade by skilled artisans. They are famous all around the world for their intricate embroidery and beautiful designs.
  • Pashmina Shawls: Pashmina Shawls are made from the wool of the pashmina goat and are known for their softness and warmth. They are a favorite of many travelers who visit Pakistan.
  • Phulkari Stitch Work: Phulkari is a traditional embroidery technique that is famous in Punjab. It involves using colorful threads to create beautiful patterns on fabric. It’s a great souvenir to bring back home and add to your collection.

Local Culture and Customs of Nankana Sahib

The people of Nankana Sahib are warm, friendly, and welcoming to visitors. However, as Nankana Sahib is a holy city with religious significance, visitors are expected to dress modestly and behave respectfully. It’s also essential to remove shoes before entering any gurdwara or other holy places.

Best Time to Visit Nankana Sahib

The best time to visit Nankana Sahib is from November to March when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be scorching, with temperatures hitting over 40°C, making it challenging to explore the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nankana Sahib

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Remove shoes before entering any holy places
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Bargain at local markets

Don’t:

  • Overstay the visa
  • Disrespect religious sites and beliefs
  • Take photos without permission
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Nankana Sahib

Nankana Sahib is a relatively safe city, but visitors should stay vigilant and avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night. It’s also important to keep an eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Nankana Sahib

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent gastrointestinal illnesses. It’s also recommended to carry insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.

Getting to Nankana Sahib: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nankana Sahib

  • Allama Iqbal International Airport: Allama Iqbal International Airport is located in Lahore and is approximately 85 km away from Nankana Sahib.

Bus Stations Near Nankana Sahib

  • Nankana Sahib Bus Station: Nankana Sahib Bus Station is the main bus station in the city and connects it to other major cities in Punjab.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Nankana Sahib and are a great way to get around the city.
  • Private car: Visitors can also hire a personal driver and car to explore the city at their own leisure.

How to Get Around in Nankana Sahib

Visitors can get around Nankana Sahib through:

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available and are a relatively affordable way to get around the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of transportation, especially for shorter distances.
  • Walking: Nankana Sahib is a relatively small city, and many of the major attractions are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Nankana Sahib: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lahore

Lahore is the capital city of Punjab and is located approximately 85 km away from Nankana Sahib. It’s known for its stunning architecture, vibrant culture, and delicious food. Some of the must-visit attractions in Lahore include the Badshahi Mosque, Lahore Fort, and Shalimar Gardens.

Amritsar

Amritsar is a city located in the Indian state of Punjab and is approximately 50 km away from Nankana Sahib. It’s home to the famous Golden Temple, which is a holy site for Sikhs around the world. It’s also known for its delicious food, including the famous Amritsari Kulcha.

Conclusion

Nankana Sahib is a city with immense cultural and historical significance, and a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the rich culture and traditions of Pakistan. From stunning gurdwaras to spectacular natural attractions and mouth-watering cuisine, Nankana Sahib has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and explore the magic of this beautiful city.
