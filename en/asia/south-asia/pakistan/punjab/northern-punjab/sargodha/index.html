Introduction to Sargodha

Sargodha is a city located in Northern Punjab, Pakistan. It is a populous city with a rich cultural history and stunning natural surroundings. Sargodha is known for its agricultural produce, particularly its citrus fruits, and is also home to an airbase of the Pakistan Air Force. It offers plenty of opportunities for travelers to explore its cultural heritage, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Sargodha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sargodha Museum: This museum showcases the cultural heritage of Sargodha and the surrounding areas. It houses a significant collection of ancient artifacts, including pottery, ceramics, coins, inscriptions, and costumes.

  • Shahpur Museum: Located in the nearby town of Shahpur, this museum is home to an extensive collection of archaeological finds, including sculptures, inscriptions, coins, and manuscripts from the Mughal era.

  • Katas Raj Temple: Located approximately 70 km from Sargodha, this is a famous 6th-century temple of the Hindu Lord Shiva. It is considered a sacred site by Hindus and is a popular pilgrimage site.

Natural Attractions of Sargodha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sargodha Hills: Located on the outskirts of the city, Sargodha Hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. It is a popular spot for hiking, trekking, and camping.

  • Jhalar Park: This park is a popular spot for picnicking and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities, including boating, horseback riding, and archery.

  • Khanpur Lake: Located approximately 120 km from Sargodha, this is a stunning man-made lake surrounded by mountains. It offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sargodha

  • Sargodha Hills Trekking Trail: This is a moderate trekking trail that offers stunning views of Sargodha Hills and the surrounding landscapes. It takes approximately 3-4 hours to complete the trail.

  • Kala Chitta Range Trekking Trail: Located approximately 80 km from Sargodha, this is a challenging trekking trail that offers breathtaking views of the Kala Chitta Range. It takes approximately 7-8 hours to complete the trail.

  • Bicycle Route: The streets of Sargodha are bike-friendly, and there are plenty of rental shops where tourists can rent bicycles for a day and explore the city on their own.

Best Things to Do in Sargodha: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning views of Sargodha and the surrounding areas.

  • Local Food Tour: Take a food tour of Sargodha and sample the city’s famous citrus fruits, street food, and traditional dishes.

  • Horseback Riding: Go horseback riding at Jhalar Park and explore the park’s scenic routes.

Food and Drink Scene of Sargodha

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinnow Juice: Sargodha is famous for its Kinnow oranges, and Kinnow juice is a must-try when visiting the city.

  • Murgh Pulao: This is a traditional Pakistani dish that is a must-try when in Sargodha. It is made with rice, chicken, and a blend of spices.

  • Sargodha Special Chat: This is a street food that is popular in Sargodha. It is a savory snack made with chickpeas, potatoes, and a spicy tamarind sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: This is a refreshing yogurt-based drink that is a popular beverage in Sargodha.

  • Tea: Tea is a staple beverage in Pakistan and is served in most restaurants and cafes in Sargodha.

Where to Stay in Sargodha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel One Sargodha: This is a modern hotel located in the heart of the city. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.

  • Nirvana Hotel and Restaurant: This hotel is located on the outskirts of the city and offers stunning views of Sargodha Hills. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

  • The Green Guest House: This is a budget-friendly guest house located in the city center. It offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Sargodha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Citrus Fruits: Sargodha is famous for its Kinnow oranges, and visitors can buy these fresh fruits in the local markets.

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can buy locally made handicrafts, including pottery, textiles, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Sargodha

Sargodha is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. Most of the locals speak Punjabi, and Urdu is also widely spoken. Islam is the dominant religion, and visitors are requested to dress appropriately while visiting mosques or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Sargodha

The best time to visit Sargodha is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and cool. Summers are hot and humid, and temperatures can soar up to 45°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sargodha

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection.
  • Use drugs or alcohol.
  • Engage in any illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sargodha

Sargodha is a relatively safe city, but visitors are advised to take basic precautions and avoid walking alone at night. It is also recommended to keep valuables secure and stay alert in crowded places.

Health in Sargodha

Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled water. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and typhoid before traveling to Pakistan.

Getting to Sargodha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sargodha

  • Sialkot International Airport: This is the nearest international airport, located approximately 150 km from Sargodha.

Bus Stations Near Sargodha

  • Sargodha Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the city and offers regular bus services to Lahore, Islamabad, and other cities in Pakistan.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: Visitors can hire private taxis from Lahore or Islamabad to reach Sargodha.

How to Get Around in Sargodha

Visitors can get around Sargodha using local buses or taxis. Auto-rickshaws are also a convenient and affordable mode of transportation within the city.

Where to Go Next from Sargodha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Islamabad: This is the capital city of Pakistan and is located approximately 250 km from Sargodha. It is a modern cosmopolitan city with plenty of tourist attractions.

  • Lahore: This city is located approximately 170 km from Sargodha and is known for its cultural heritage, Mughal architecture, and delicious food.

Conclusion

Sargodha is a hidden gem in Pakistan and offers plenty of opportunities for travelers to explore its cultural heritage, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. From museums to trekking trails, from local food tours to hot air balloon rides, there is something for everyone in this beautiful city.
