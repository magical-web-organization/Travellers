Rawalpindi Travel Guide: Explore the Gems of Northern Punjab, Pakistan

Rawalpindi is a vibrant city located in Northern Punjab, Pakistan. Being the fourth-largest city in Pakistan, it offers a unique blend of historical landmarks, cultural heritage, natural surroundings, and modern-day amenities. Rawalpindi offers a great deal to explore, making it a must-visit destination for tourists from all over the world.

Introduction to Rawalpindi

Rawalpindi is historically known as “Pindi” and is located near Pakistan’s capital, Islamabad. The city serves as an important transportation hub and offers easy access to Islamabad, the capital city. Rawalpindi is a melting pot of Pakistani culture and traditions. The city is famous for its bustling bazaars, delicious food, and historic landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Rawalpindi

There are many places to visit in Rawalpindi that offer something for everyone’s interests. Following is a list of some of the must-visit places in Rawalpindi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lahore Museum: The Lahore Museum is the oldest and largest museum in Pakistan, which showcases a vast collection of art, history, and culture of Pakistan starting from the Indus Valley Civilization to the present era.

  • Pakistan Army Museum: The Pakistan Army Museum has exhibits from the pre-partition period to the present day, including arms, tanks, helicopters, and other military equipment.

  • Rawalpindi Museum: The Rawalpindi Museum is dedicated to preserving the art, culture, and heritage of Rawalpindi and includes collections of pottery, jewelry, and sculptures from the Mughal period.

Natural Attractions of Rawalpindi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ayub National Park: Ayub National Park is a beautiful park spread over 2,300 acres that offers various outdoor activities like walking, cycling, and horse riding. It also has a zoo, a lake, and a small theme park.

  • Daman-e-Koh: Daman-e-Koh is a beautiful viewpoint located in the hills surrounding Islamabad. It offers a panoramic view of the city and its surroundings.

  • Rawal Lake: Rawal Lake is a scenic artificial lake located in the Margalla Hills. The lake serves as a popular spot for boating, picnicking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rawalpindi

The beautiful Margalla Hills and scenic surroundings offer several hiking and biking opportunities for adventure seekers. Here are some of the most popular routes for hiking and biking in Rawalpindi:

  • Pir Sohawa: Pir Sohawa is a beautiful and popular destination for hikers and bikers alike, located in the Margalla Hills. It offers a breathtaking view of the city from a height of over 5000 feet.

  • Trail 3: Trail 3 is one of the most popular hiking trails in Islamabad and Rawalpindi. It is a challenging trek, offering breathtaking views of the hills and valleys.

  • Monal Restaurant Road: Monal restaurant road is a beautiful road that leads to the Monal Restaurant on the top of the Margalla Hills. It is a popular spot for hikers and bikers and offers stunning views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Rawalpindi: Activities and Tours

There are many fun and exciting things to do in Rawalpindi for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the must-do activities:

  • Visit the Raja Bazaar: Raja Bazaar is a bustling market and one of the oldest bazaars in Rawalpindi. It offers a unique shopping experience and is a great place to buy traditional Pakistani handicrafts and souvenirs.

  • Take a Food Tour: Rawalpindi is famous for its delicious street-food scene. Taking a food tour of the city will give you a chance to explore the wide range of delicious Pakistani cuisine.

  • Explore the Nightlife: Rawalpindi has a vibrant nightlife, with several bars, clubs, and restaurants that offer live music and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rawalpindi

Here is a week-long itinerary that covers the best of Rawalpindi:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Lahore Museum, Rawalpindi Museum, and Pakistan Army Museum
Day 2 Explore the Ayub National Park and enjoy outdoor activities

Food and Drink Scene of Rawalpindi

Rawalpindi is famous for its tasty street food and local Punjabi cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Rawalpindi:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chana Masala: Chana Masala is a spicy chickpea curry, which is served with naan or rice. It is a popular breakfast dish in Rawalpindi.

  • Haleem: Haleem is a slow-cooked meat dish made with wheat, lentils, and spices. It is a popular dish served during Ramadan and other special occasions.

  • Biryani: Biryani is a popular rice dish, made with a blend of spices, meat, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: Lassi is a refreshing yogurt drink that is available at most local eateries in Rawalpindi.

  • Sugarcane Juice: Sugarcane juice is a delicious and refreshing drink that is made from freshly squeezed sugarcane juice.

Where to Stay in Rawalpindi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rawalpindi offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors. The best areas to stay in Rawalpindi are Saddar, Rawal Road, and Shamsabad. These areas offer easy access to all the major tourist attractions in the city. Here are some of the best places to stay in Rawalpindi:

  • Pearl Continental Rawalpindi: Pearl Continental Rawalpindi is a luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Rawalpindi. It offers premium amenities and is located near several tourist attractions.

  • Maisonette Hotels and Resorts: Maisonette Hotels and Resorts offer comfortable and affordable accommodation options in Saddar and Rawal Road.

  • Flashman’s Hotel Rawalpindi: Flashman’s Hotel is a historic hotel located in the heart of Rawalpindi. It offers comfortable accommodation and easy access to all major tourist attractions.

What to Buy in Rawalpindi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rawalpindi is famous for its handicrafts, jewelry, clothing, and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Rawalpindi:

  • Handicrafts: Rawalpindi is famous for its handicrafts, including woodwork, pottery, and brassware. These are great souvenirs to buy and bring back home.

  • Jewelry: The city offers a wide range of traditional Pakistani jewelry, including silver and gold jewelry with precious and semi-precious stones.

  • Clothing: Rawalpindi is famous for its traditional clothing, including shalwar kameez and dupatta. These are great souvenirs to buy and bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Rawalpindi

Rawalpindi has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in Pakistani traditions. Islam is the main religion practiced in Rawalpindi, and visitors are expected to respect religious customs and dress modestly. Pakistani hospitality is renowned, and guests are treated with warmth and respect.

Best Time to Visit Rawalpindi

The best time to visit Rawalpindi is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. The summer months (April to September) can be hot and humid, making it uncomfortable to explore the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rawalpindi

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Rawalpindi:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the delicious street food of Rawalpindi.

  • Bargain with vendors in the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water; always opt for bottled water.

  • Touch religious or cultural artifacts without permission.

  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Rawalpindi is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take common precautions like locking doors and windows, avoiding deserted areas at night, and keeping valuables out of sight.

Safety in Rawalpindi

Rawalpindi is a safe city, but visitors should be cautious while exploring local markets and crowded areas. Pickpocketing and theft can occur in crowded places.

Health in Rawalpindi

Visitors should opt for bottled water and avoid eating raw or undercooked food to avoid getting sick. Visitors should also ensure they have proper travel health insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Rawalpindi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rawalpindi is well connected by air, road, and rail. The city has a domestic airport that operates flights to all major cities in Pakistan. Visitors can also access Rawalpindi via roads and rail transport from major cities in Pakistan like Lahore and Karachi.

Airports Near Rawalpindi

  • Islamabad International Airport: Islamabad International Airport is located in Islamabad and is the closest airport to Rawalpindi. It is well-connected to all major cities in Pakistan and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Rawalpindi

  • Rawalpindi Pir Wadhai Bus Station: Rawalpindi Pir Wadhai Bus Station is the main bus terminal in Rawalpindi that operates buses to other cities in Pakistan.

  • Daewoo Express Terminal: Daewoo Express Terminal is another major bus terminal in Rawalpindi that operates luxury buses to other cities in Pakistan.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Rawalpindi via train from major cities in Pakistan like Lahore, Karachi, and Peshawar.

How to Get Around in Rawalpindi

Rawalpindi offers several transportation options, including taxis, rickshaws, and buses. Visitors can also rent cars to explore the city and its surroundings at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Rawalpindi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rawalpindi is located near several popular tourist destinations in Pakistan. Here are some of the nearby destinations that visitors can explore:

  • Islamabad: Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan and offers a unique blend of modern-day amenities and natural surroundings.

  • Taxila: Taxila is an ancient archaeological site located near Rawalpindi that showcases the rich history of the region.

  • Rohtas Fort: Rohtas Fort is a historic fort located near Rawalpindi that offers a glimpse into the region’s Mughal history.

Conclusion

Rawalpindi offers a unique blend of history, culture, natural beauty, and modern-day amenities. Visitors to Rawalpindi will have a chance to explore the city’s diverse culture, try delicious local food, and indulge in outdoor activities in the surrounding natural surroundings. With so much to see and do, Rawalpindi is a must-visit destination for tourists exploring Northern Punjab in Pakistan.
