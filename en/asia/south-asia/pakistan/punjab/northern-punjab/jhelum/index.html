Jhelum Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Jhelum

Jhelum is a city located in the northern region of Punjab, Pakistan. It is a beautiful and historic city, which attracts tourists from all over the world. Jhelum is famous for its rich culture and traditional heritage, natural beauty, and a range of outdoor activities. From scenic beauty to outdoor adventures and cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in Jhelum.

In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with all the essential information you need to know to plan your trip to Jhelum.

Introduction to Jhelum

Jhelum is situated in the northern region of Punjab. It is one of the oldest cities in Pakistan, dating back to the 16th century. The city is named after the Jhelum River that flows through it, and it has a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Jhelum is also known for its strategic location, as it is close to the border between Pakistan and India.

The city is easily accessible by air, road, and railway. There are many local modes of transport available to get around the city, such as buses, taxis, and rickshaws.

Jhelum is a city that has something for everyone. Whether you are interested in historical landmarks or outdoor adventures, Jhelum has it all. Here is a list of places that you must visit when in Jhelum:

  • Rohtas Fort
  • Akhnoor Fort
  • Sher Shah Suri Masjid
  • Haveli Khushal Singh
  • Jhelum Old City
  • Imran Masjid

  • Mangla Dam

  • Pahar Pur Dam
  • Khabikki Lake

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jhelum

Jhelum is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are many hiking and biking routes in and around the city that you can explore. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Jhelum are:

  • Sohawa - Dakhli - Lilla - Tilla Jogian trek
  • Salt Range Trek
  • Hiking Tilla Jogian to Tilla Hari Singh
  • Hiking to Karwai
  • Jhelum River Track

Best Things to Do in Jhelum: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and outdoor activities, there are many other things to do in Jhelum. Here are some of the best activities and tours that you can enjoy in Jhelum:

  • Visit local markets and bazaars to experience the local culture
  • Visit local food joints, and try out delicious street food
  • Attend cultural festivals and events
  • Take part in adventure sports, such as paragliding and rock climbing
  • Take a boat ride and enjoy the scenic views of the Jhelum River

Local Culture and Customs of Jhelum

Jhelum is a city deeply rooted in Pakistani culture, and it is important to respect their traditions and customs. Here are some cultural practices and customs that you should be aware of:

  • It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a mosque or religious place.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Smoking in public places is not allowed.
  • Be respectful towards locals, and ask for permission before taking their pictures.

Best Time to Visit Jhelum

The best time to visit Jhelum is between October and March when the weather is cool and pleasant. The summer months of May to September can be extremely hot, and it is not advisable to travel during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jhelum

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Jhelum:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and respect local customs and culture
  • Try local street food and beverages
  • Hire a licensed tour guide for better understanding of the sites and culture
  • Keep hydrated and use sunscreen

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of sensitive sites or military installations
  • Wear revealing or provocative clothes
  • Get involved in political discussions or debates

Safety and Health

Jhelum is a relatively safe city, but it is always recommended to take necessary precautions while traveling.

Safety in Jhelum

  • Always carry your identification and travel documents with you
  • Keep your belongings safe, especially in crowded areas
  • Avoid traveling alone at night

Health in Jhelum

  • Drink bottled water and avoid street food in case of sensitive stomachs
  • Carry necessary medication with you
  • Use sunscreen and keep yourselves hydrated during outdoor activities

Getting to Jhelum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jhelum is well connected to major cities in Pakistan by air, road, and railway.

Airports Near Jhelum

The nearest airports to Jhelum are the Islamabad International Airport and the Lahore International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Jhelum

Jhelum is connected to major cities of Pakistan via bus. The Jhelum Bus Terminal is the main bus terminal where all interstate and intrastate buses stop.

Other Options

If you want to travel to Jhelum from other cities in Pakistan, you can also hire a private taxi or rent a car.

How to Get Around in Jhelum

There are many local modes of transportation available in Jhelum, such as buses, taxis, and rickshaws. You can also rent a car or hire a driver for more convenience.

Conclusion

Jhelum is a city that offers unparalleled beauty, adventure, and culture. It is a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to explore the true essence of Pakistan. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan an unforgettable trip to Jhelum.
