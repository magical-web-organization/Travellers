Sheikhan is a beautiful city located in Northern Punjab, Pakistan, known for its rich history, stunning landscapes, and friendly locals. Whether you’re interested in cultural heritage or outdoor adventures, this city has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Sheikhan.

Introduction to Sheikhan

Sheikhan is a city in the Punjab province of Pakistan, situated near the city of Sargodha. The city is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. The area has been inhabited since ancient times, with archaeological evidence suggesting that the region was home to a variety of different cultures and civilizations over the centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Sheikhan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sheikhan Fort: A historical fort built in the early 17th century, by a Mughal ruler, Nawab Muhammad Khan. It is surrounded by beautiful gardens, and its stunning architecture depicts the rich history of Sheikhan city.

  • Shrine of Sage Syed Ahmad Sultan Sheikhanvi: The shrine is the most important Islamic site in the city. The 18th-century shrine is built on the tomb of the famous Sufi saint Syed Ahmad Sultan, who played an influential role in the spread of Islam in the region.

  • Old Bazaar: A lively, bustling market near the Shrine of sage Syed Ahmad Sultan, where visitors can find traditional handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Sheikhan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khanpur Dam: Khanpur Dam is situated on the Haro River about 30 km from Sheikhan. Visitors can enjoy boating, horse riding, hiking, and camping near the dam.

  • Kalabagh Rocks: Kalabagh Rocks are a unique natural formation made up of multi-colored rocks. Visitors can explore the rocky terrain and enjoy the scenic views.

  • Sher Shah Park: Sher Shah Park is a beautiful park built by the Nawab of Sheikhan. Visitors can enjoy picnics and relax in the lush green surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sheikhan

Sheikhan has some of the best hiking and biking routes in Pakistan, with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Sultanpur to Gari Paind Khan: A 12 km long trail, that offers stunning views of the historic Sultanpur Fort.

  • Jeep safari in Khewra Salt Mines: Visitors can explore the world’s second-largest salt mine and enjoy a unique off-road Jeep Safari.

Best Things to Do in Sheikhan: Activities and Tours

  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can join guided tours that explore the rich cultural heritage and history of Sheikhan.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the stunning views of the city and surrounding areas.

  • Local Food Tours: Visitors can sample some of the delicious local dishes and learn about the culinary traditions of Sheikhan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sheikhan

Day 1-2: Visit historic Sheikhan Fort and Shrine of sage Syed Ahmad Sultan, explore the Old Bazaar, and enjoy local cuisine.

Day 3-4: Enjoy outdoor adventures at Khanpur Dam and Kalabagh Rocks.

Day 5-6: Take a hot air balloon ride and explore the neighboring valleys and mountains, go on a Jeep Safari in Khewra Salt Mines.

Day 7: Relax in the beautiful Sher Shah Park or visit nearby cities like Sargodha.

Food and Drink Scene of Sheikhan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Siri Paye: A traditional beef trotter dish cooked with a blend of spices and served with naan bread.

  • Nihari: A rich and flavorful beef curry, slow-cooked with a variety of different spices, and served with naan bread.

  • Khatte Baingan: A delicious eggplant dish cooked with tangy spices and served with roti.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

  • Sugarcane Juice: A sweet and refreshing drink made from freshly squeezed sugarcane.

Where to Stay in Sheikhan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options available in Sheikhan, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Sophiya Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the heart of the city.

  • Shabnam Hotel: A popular mid-range hotel with modern amenities and easy access to local attractions.

  • Nawab’s Inn: A luxury hotel with excellent facilities and a gorgeous garden for guests to relax in.

What to Buy in Sheikhan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can find a variety of different handicrafts in the Old Bazaar, including traditional pottery, textiles, and jewelry.

  • Local Produce: Sheikhan is known for its delicious citrus fruit, including oranges, tangerines, and grapefruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Sheikhan

The locals in Sheikhan are warm, welcoming, and proud of their cultural heritage. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Sheikhan

The best time to visit Sheikhan is from October to February, as the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be extremely hot, and the monsoon season can bring heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sheikhan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly.

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Urdu to communicate with locals.

  • Try the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Haggle aggressively at local markets.

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sheikhan

Sheikhan is a safe city for tourists, but visitors should take basic precautions and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Sheikhan

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, including malaria and dengue fever, by using insect repellant and wearing protective clothing.

Getting to Sheikhan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sheikhan

The nearest airport to Sheikhan is Sargodha International Airport, located about 12 km away.

Bus Stations Near Sheikhan

There are several bus stations in Sheikhan that connect the city to other major cities in Pakistan.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Sheikhan by train or private car.

How to Get Around in Sheikhan

The most convenient way to get around Sheikhan is by rental car, taxi or by bus.

Where to Go Next from Sheikhan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sargodha: A nearby city known for its beautiful parks, historic temples, and delicious local cuisine.

  • Islamabad: The capital city of Pakistan, known for its modern architecture, vibrant culture, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Sheikhan is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes. Visitors can enjoy a variety of different activities, from exploring historic sites to hiking and biking in the scenic surroundings. With its warm and friendly locals, delicious cuisine, and unique attractions, Sheikhan is a must-visit destination in Pakistan.
