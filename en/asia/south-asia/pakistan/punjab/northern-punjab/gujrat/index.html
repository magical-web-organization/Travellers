Introduction to Gujrat

Gujrat is a city in Northern Punjab, located in Pakistan, South Asia’s heart. The city is known for its agricultural resources, and its contribution to the country’s economy is significant. The city is an excellent tourist attraction spot because of its natural beauty, its cultural and historical attractions, and delicious food. With a population of around 2 million, it’s a lively and vibrant city that attracts both national and international tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Gujrat

Here is the list of the top destinations in Gujrat that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pharwala Fort: This ancient fort dates back to the 15th century and is a perfect example of the architectural skills of the ancient Mughal era in Pakistan. The fort is situated on the top of a hill and provides an excellent view of the surrounding areas.

  • Amb Sharif: This historic shrine is located in the village of Amb Sharif, where the famous Sufi Saint Syed Waris Shah is buried. He was the one who authored the famous Punjabi poetry epic, Heer Ranjha.

  • Mangla Dam: This huge dam is situated on the Jhelum River and is one of the largest earth-filled dams in the world. It’s a source of hydroelectricity, irrigation, and is also a popular picnic spot.

Natural Attractions of Gujrat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tilla Jogian: This mountain peak is over 1100 meters high and is located to the east of Gujrat. It’s a perfect spot for hiking, and the mountain range provides breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.

  • Khewra Salt Mines: The Khewra Salt Mines are the world’s second-largest salt mines, which are situated about 160 km south of Islamabad. The mines are over 350 years old and are still functional.

  • Chakwaal River: This river is a popular spot among locals and tourists for its scenic views, clear water, and a perfect spot for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gujrat

Hiking and biking in Gujrat are fantastic outdoor activities because of the favorable weather and scenic views. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Gujrat are:

  • Tilla Jogian: The Tilla Jogian mountain peak has many trekking and hiking routes that take you through the forest, rugged terrain, and some waterfalls along the way.

  • Khewra Salt Mines: The salt mines also offer hiking and biking trails that provide scenic views of the surroundings.

  • Mangla Dam: The Mangla Dam is another popular spot for hiking and biking, and it’s an ideal place to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Gujrat: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the historical and cultural sites: The city is famous for its rich cultural history and heritage, so make sure to visit the historical and cultural sites such as the Pharwala Fort and Amb Sharif.

  • Experience the local food: The street food in Gujrat is famous for its flavor and spices. From mouth-watering Mughlai dishes to the delicious desserts, make sure to try them all.

  • Shop at the local bazaars: Gujrat has many local bazaars, and shopping here will give you a chance to experience the local culture, try the local street food, and buy local handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gujrat

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary for Gujrat:

  • Day 1: Visit Pharwala Fort, Amb Sharif, and have lunch at any local food stall.
  • Day 2: Visit Khewra Salt Mine, and go biking or hiking around the mines.
  • Day 3: Visit Mangla Dam, take a boat ride, and have a picnic with your friends or family.
  • Day 4: Visit Tilla Jogian mountain peak and go hiking.
  • Day 5: Visit the Chakwaal River for fishing or sunbathing.
  • Day 6: Enjoy shopping at the local bazaars and indulge in local street food.
  • Day 7: Spend your last day exploring the city’s landmarks and trying new dishes at various restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Gujrat

Gujrat is a city full of flavor and traditional regional cuisine. Here is the list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lassi: Lassi is a refreshing and healthy drink made with yogurt, milk, ice, and sugar. It’s a perfect summer drink to cool off.

  • Dal Makhani: Dal Makhani is a traditional dish made with black lentils and kidney beans. It’s creamy and rich, and best served with naan or rice.

  • Chana Masala: Chana Masala is a delicious vegetarian dish made with chickpeas, onions, and tomatoes. It’s a popular street food that is spicy, flavorful, and nutritious.

Local Beverages

  • Sugarcane Juice: Sugarcane juice is a refreshing drink that is widely available in Gujrat. The juice is extracted from the sugarcane plant and is full of nutrients and energy.

  • Tea: Tea is the most popular beverage among the locals in Gujrat. It’s a social drink and is best enjoyed with friends and family.

Where to Stay in Gujrat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gujrat has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Gujrat:

  • Hotel One: Hotel One is a luxurious hotel that provides top-notch services and amenities, making it ideal for families and couples.

  • Shelton Hotel: Shelton Hotel is a budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers. The rooms are cozy and comfortable, and the staff is friendly and welcoming.

  • Lalazar Hotel: Lalazar Hotel is a beautiful heritage property that gives you a taste of the traditional Gujrati lifestyle. The rooms are spacious and well-furnished, and the hotel has a beautiful garden that adds to the ambiance.

What to Buy in Gujrat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gujrat is known for its handicrafts, and some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Gujrat include:

  • Handicrafts: Gujrat has a long tradition of exquisite handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and weaving. These handicrafts are beautiful and make for great souvenirs.

  • Pakistani Clothing: You can find some beautiful Pakistani clothing in Gujrat, including shawls, dupattas, and traditional dresses. They make for great souvenirs or gifts for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Gujrat

The people of Gujrat are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their cultural heritage. One of the most respected things in their culture is hospitality, and they take it very seriously. The locals are often seen exchanging greetings and pleasantries, and it’s considered rude to ignore or not reply to someone’s greeting.

Best Time to Visit Gujrat

The best time to visit Gujrat is from November to March when the weather is cooler and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. During the summer months, which are from April to September, the temperature can soar and make it unbearable for tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gujrat

Do:

  • Do try the local food and beverages, which are delicious and authentic.
  • Do respect the local customs, traditions, and culture.
  • Do dress modestly when visiting religious places or cultural destinations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Don’t litter and keep the tourist attractions and public places clean.
  • Don’t drink or smoke in public places, as it’s considered to be disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gujrat

Gujrat is considered to be a safe city for tourists, and the locals are welcoming and helpful. However, it’s always recommended to take the necessary precautions to ensure your safety, such as not carrying large amounts of cash and avoiding unrecognized areas at night.

Health in Gujrat

Gujrat has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where you can seek medical care in case of an emergency. However, it’s advisable to carry your medications with you and check with your doctor regarding any vaccine recommendations or health-related precautions before traveling.

Getting to Gujrat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gujrat

The nearest airport to Gujrat is the Islamabad International Airport, which is located around 160 km away from the city. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to reach Gujrat.

Bus Stations Near Gujrat

Gujrat has a well-connected network of buses that connect it to major cities in Pakistan. Some of the popular bus stations in Gujrat include the GT Road Bus Stand and the Gujrat Bus Stand.

Other Options

You can also reach Gujrat by train or car. The nearest major railway station is the Gujrat Railway Station, which connects it to major cities in Pakistan. If you’re driving, the GT Road is the most accessible route to reach Gujrat.

How to Get Around in Gujrat

Gujrat has several transportation options to get around, including cycle-rickshaws, taxis, and buses. The cycle-rickshaws are the most popular mode of transportation and are cheap and convenient.

Where to Go Next from Gujrat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lahore: Lahore is a popular tourist destination and is situated about 184 km away from Gujrat. The city is known for its famous landmarks, historical sites, and local delicacies.

  • Islamabad: Islamabad is the capital of Pakistan and is located about 160 km away from Gujrat. The city is famous for its modern architecture, beautiful parks, and natural beauty.

  • Sialkot: Sialkot is a historic city located about 80 km away from Gujrat. The city is famous for its sports goods and surgical instruments industries, and it’s an excellent place to buy sports gear.

Conclusion

Gujrat is a beautiful city that offers a blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious food. You can plan your trip to the city with this comprehensive guide and explore the best attractions, things to do, and where to stay. Make sure to follow the local customs and traditions, and you’ll have a fantastic time exploring the city.
