Introduction to Gilgit

Gilgit is a small town located in the northern part of Pakistan. It is the capital of Gilgit-Baltistan, and it is surrounded by beautiful mountains, stunning landscapes, and unique cultural attractions. Gilgit is a popular tourist destination because of its peaceful environment and beautiful natural surroundings. The town is easily accessible from Islamabad, the capital of Pakistan, by road and air.

List of Places to Visit in Gilgit

There are many fascinating places to see in Gilgit. Here are some of the top cultural and natural attractions you should consider exploring during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gilgit Baltistan Museum - This museum showcases the culture and history of Gilgit-Baltistan, including its ancient Buddhist heritage, local traditions, and artistic heritage.
  • Kargah Buddha - A rock carving of Buddha that dates back to the eighth century A.D. It is located near the Gilgit River, and it is a popular attraction for both locals and tourists.
  • Gilgit City Square - This bustling place in the heart of Gilgit is a great place to experience the local culture and meet friendly locals.

Natural Attractions of Gilgit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Naltar Valley - Known for its stunning scenery, Naltar Valley is a popular destination among nature lovers and hikers. The valley is surrounded by snow-capped peaks, lush green forests, and crystal-clear streams.
  • Hunza Valley - Hunza Valley is famous for its picturesque views of majestic mountains, serene lakes, and orchards of apricot and cherry trees. It is located in the upper part of Gilgit, and it is one of the most beautiful places to visit in Pakistan.
  • Rakaposhi Viewpoint - Rakaposhi is one of the highest peaks in Pakistan, and it offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gilgit

Gilgit is a perfect place to experience outdoor adventure. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes you can explore in Gilgit:

  • Danyore Suspension Bridge - A suspensio_bridge that links the town of Gilgit to the village of Danyore. The bridge offers great views of the Gilgit_River and surrounding mountains.
  • Chillinji Pass - The Chillinji Pass trek is a challenging but rewarding trek that takes you through rugged terrain and leads you to stunning views of the surrounding valleys and peaks.
  • Baltoro Glacier Trek - A 10-day trek that takes you through the Karakoram Range, giving you the opportunity to experience the unique landscape and culture of the region.

Best Things to Do in Gilgit: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours you can experience during your visit to Gilgit:

  • Baltit Fort Tour - A tour of the famous Baltit Fort that highlights the rich history and culture of the region.
  • Glacier Tours - Take a tour of some of the local glaciers to experience the icy marvels of nature.
  • Rafting in the Gilgit River - The Gilgit River offers an excellent opportunity for thrilling rafting experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Gilgit

Gilgit is a unique place that offers a distinct cuisine that combines the flavors of Central Asian and Middle Eastern cooking. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you should explore during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chapshoro - A traditional meat-stuffed pastry that is seasoned with local herbs and spices.
  • Yakshini Pulao - A rice dish made with tender lamb chunks and garnished with nuts, raisins, and spices.
  • Khaplu Trout - Freshly caught river trout served with a variety of sauces and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sheer Chai - Tea made with milk, sugar, and local spices.
  • Burusho Snooni - A sour yogurt beverage with a distinctive taste.
  • Apricot Juice - A refreshing and sweet juice made with fresh apricots that are abundant in the region.

Where to Stay in Gilgit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gilgit has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Gilgit:

  • Serena Hotel Gilgit - A luxury hotel that offers superior amenities, including a gym, swimming pool, and on-site restaurants.
  • Gilgit Youth Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that provides comfortable rooms and free breakfast.
  • Sarai Glacier Lodge - A rustic guesthouse that offers a unique and authentic Gilgit experience.

What to Buy in Gilgit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gilgit is a shopping paradise for those who love traditional handicrafts and unique souvenirs. Here are some of the best things you should consider buying during your visit:

  • Handmade Carpets - Gilgit is famous for its handmade carpets that are intricate and unique.
  • Gemstones and Minerals - Gilgit is also known for its precious gems and minerals, including rubies, emeralds, and sapphires.
  • Handicrafts and Traditional Dresses - You can find a wide variety of handicrafts and local dresses at the many local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Gilgit

Gilgit has a rich and diverse culture that is influenced by its unique location and history. Here are some of the customs and traditions that you should be aware of:

  • Shina Language - Shina is the local language of Gilgit, and it is widely spoken across the region.
  • Islam - The majority of people in Gilgit are Muslim, and it is important to respect local customs and dress modestly.
  • Hospitality - People in Gilgit are friendly and welcoming, and it is not uncommon to be invited to someone’s house for a meal or a cup of tea.

Best Time to Visit Gilgit

The best time to visit Gilgit is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is pleasant and the natural scenery is in full bloom. However, Gilgit is also a great place to visit in the winter months for winter activities and spectacular views of snow-capped peaks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gilgit

Here are some do’s and don’ts of Gilgit that you should be aware of:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Try local cuisine and traditional handicrafts
  • Carry a light jacket for evenings, as temperatures can drop significantly

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol or smoke cigarettes in public
  • Touch or remove any artifacts or historical items in museums or archaeological sites
  • Photograph locals without their permission

Safety and Health

Gilgit is generally a safe place to travel. However, it is always advisable to take precautionary measures while traveling. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Gilgit

  • Avoid traveling alone in remote areas
  • Don’t carry too much cash or expensive jewelry
  • Be aware of pickpockets and scams

Health in Gilgit

  • Drink bottled water only
  • Bring insect repellents and sunscreen
  • Be aware of the potential altitude sickness, especially if you are planning to hike to the mountain peaks.

Getting to Gilgit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gilgit is easily accessible by both air and road.

Airports Near Gilgit

The nearest airport to Gilgit is the Gilgit Airport, which is served by several domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Gilgit

Gilgit is also well-connected by road to other major cities in Pakistan. Buses run regularly to and from Islamabad, Rawalpindi, and Skardu.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or take a private taxi to travel to Gilgit.

How to Get Around in Gilgit

Gilgit is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot. However, you can also rent a car or a motorbike to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Gilgit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many places to visit near Gilgit that are worth exploring. Here are some of the popular tourist destinations that you can visit:

  • Skardu - Located a few hours away from Gilgit, this picturesque town offers stunning views of the Karakoram Mountains and the Skardu Valley.
  • Chilas - A small village that is located on the Karakoram Highway and is famous for its unique rock formations and stunning views.
  • Khunjerab Pass - The highest border crossing in the world located on the border between Pakistan and China.

Conclusion

Gilgit is an incredible destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. It is a great place to immerse yourself in the local culture, explore the stunning landscapes, and experience the hospitality of the locals. We hope this comprehensive travel guide to Gilgit helped you plan your next trip to this beautiful part of Pakistan.
