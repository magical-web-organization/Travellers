Introduction to Khunjerab National Park

Khunjerab National Park is located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region in Pakistan, covering an area of over 2,000 square miles. The park contains a range of habitats, including alpine pastures, forests, and glaciers, and is home to a variety of wildlife, including the endangered snow leopard and ibex. Visitors to Khunjerab National Park can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to wildlife watching and photography.

List of Places to Visit in Khunjerab National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khunjerab Pass: Located at an elevation of 15,397 feet, this is the highest paved international border in the world and connects Pakistan and China.
  • Khunjerab National Park Museum: This museum showcases the unique wildlife and culture of the region.
  • Baltit Fort: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back over 700 years, this fort is located in the nearby town of Karimabad and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Khunjerab National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Golden Peak: This majestic peak rises to an elevation of 23,000 feet and is a popular destination for trekking.
  • Attabad Lake: Created as a result of a landslide, this lake is nestled amongst the gorgeous mountains of the region.
  • Passu Cones: A series of stunning, pointed peaks that are recognized as being one of the most beautiful mountain ranges in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khunjerab National Park

  • Khunjerab Pass Trek: A challenging but rewarding trek that takes hikers to the border of Pakistan and China.
  • Shimshal Lake Trek: A 10-day trek that includes stunning views of the Karakoram mountains and alpine passes.
  • Ghujerab Valley Bike Tour: Take a scenic bike ride through the stunning Ghujerab Valley, home to crystal clear lakes, glaciers, and sparkling streams.

Best Things to Do in Khunjerab National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari: Take a jeep tour through the park and try to spot rare animals like the snow leopard and ibex.
  • Mountain Biking: Rent a bike and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the park on two wheels.
  • Cultural Tours: Learn about the unique culture and traditions of the people in the region through a guided cultural tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Khunjerab National Park

  • Day 1: Visit the Khunjerab Pass and the Khunjerab National Park Museum
  • Day 2-4: Try a multi-day trek, like the Shimshal Lake Trek
  • Day 5: Take a wildlife safari through the park
  • Day 6: Rent a bike and go cycling through the park
  • Day 7: Finish the trip with a cultural tour of the region, including a visit to the Baltit Fort.

Food and Drink Scene of Khunjerab National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chapshoro: A traditional meat-filled pastry that is a popular snack in the region.
  • Yak Meat: The meat of the yak is a specialty in the region and can be found in a variety of dishes.
  • Apricots: Khunjerab National Park is famous for its delicious apricots, which can be enjoyed on their own or in a variety of dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Salt Tea: A savory tea that is made with black tea, milk, and salt, and is a popular drink in the region.
  • Apricot Juice: Fresh apricots are used to make a sweet and refreshing juice.

Where to Stay in Khunjerab National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Karakoram Highway Motel: A budget-friendly option located close to the park and also offers stunning views of the mountains.
  • Serena Khunjerab Hotel: A luxurious option that offers all modern amenities and is located within the park boundaries.
  • Hostel Khunjerab National Park: A popular hostel that offers backpackers a comfortable and affordable stay while exploring the park.

What to Buy in Khunjerab National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Embroidery: The women in the region are known for their intricate embroidery work, which can be purchased as a souvenir.
  • Gemstones: Khunjerab National Park is known for its semi-precious stones, like aquamarine, garnet, and topaz, that can be purchased from local vendors.
  • Handicrafts: The local handicrafts of the region, including wooden carvings and utensils, make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Khunjerab National Park

The people of the region are predominantly Muslim and follow a conservative lifestyle, so it is important to dress modestly and respect local customs. Visitors should also avoid alcohol and smoking in public places.

Best Time to Visit Khunjerab National Park

The best time to visit Khunjerab National Park is from May to October, when the weather is favorable for outdoor activities and the roads are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khunjerab National Park

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry warm clothes and comfortable shoes for outdoor activities
  • Carry necessary medications and first-aid kit

Don’t:

  • Drink in public places
  • Smoke in public places
  • Litter or harm the local flora and fauna

Safety and Health

Safety in Khunjerab National Park

  • Visitors should take the necessary precautions while hiking or biking in the park, including carrying enough water, staying on marked trails, and carrying a map.
  • Visitors should also keep a lookout for wild animals and avoid disturbing them.

Health in Khunjerab National Park

  • Visitors should carry sufficient medication and first-aid kits, as medical facilities are limited in the region.
  • Visitors should also be mindful of altitude sickness, especially if planning to trek to high elevations.

Getting to Khunjerab National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khunjerab National Park

The nearest international airport is in Islamabad, Pakistan, which is approximately 1,000 km away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Khunjerab National Park

The Karakoram Highway connects the park to major cities in Pakistan, with regular bus services.

Other Options

Private taxis or cars can also be hired from Islamabad or Gilgit to reach Khunjerab National Park.

How to Get Around in Khunjerab National Park

Private taxis, cars, and jeeps are available for hire to get around the park. Some biking trails also allow visitors to explore the park on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Khunjerab National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fairy Meadows: A gorgeous open meadow surrounded by towering peaks, offering stunning views and great hiking trails.
  • Hunza Valley: Known for its spectacular mountains and glaciers, and for producing some of Pakistan’s best fruits, like apricots and cherries.
  • Skardu: A bustling city that is the gateway to nearby attractions, like the Deosai National Park and the K2 base camp.

Conclusion

Khunjerab National Park is a stunning destination that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. From its impressive mountains to its unique wildlife, there is something for everyone in this beautiful part of the world. With this guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your time in Khunjerab National Park.
