Introduction to Deosai National Park

Deosai National Park is a breathtaking destination located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan, in South Asia. Often referred to as the Land of the Giants, this remote and unspoiled park features a stunning landscape of mountains, valleys, and rolling meadows that stretch out as far as the eye can see. Covering a vast area of more than 3,000 square kilometers, Deosai National Park is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including the Himalayan brown bear and the ibex. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker or simply looking for a remote and peaceful getaway, Deosai National Park is one of the must-visit destinations in Pakistan.

List of Places to Visit in Deosai National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Deosai National Park is a haven for nature lovers, but it also offers visitors plenty of opportunities to explore the rich history and culture of the region. Among the top attractions are the Shigar Fort and Baltit Fort, two stunning fortresses that have been beautifully preserved and offer a glimpse into the region’s ancient past. Another must-visit is the Katpana Village, where you can learn about the local way of life and explore traditional homes made of mud and stone.

Natural Attractions of Deosai National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Deosai National Park offers plenty of breathtaking natural wonders to explore. One of the most popular attractions is the Deosai Plateau, a vast and high-altitude plain that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The park is also home to several breathtaking waterfalls, including the stunning Sheosar Lake, which is known for its crystal-clear waters and surrounding wildflower meadows. For outdoor enthusiasts, there are plenty of opportunities to hike and trek, as well as wildlife watching and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deosai National Park

Deosai National Park offers numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to people of all abilities. With its stunning scenery and varied terrain, you can find endless opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the park on foot or on wheels. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the park include the trek to the Kala Pani lake, the trek to Sheosar Lake, and the Rupal Valley trek.

Best Things to Do in Deosai National Park: Activities and Tours

The national park offers a plethora of activities and tours to suit different desires. You can enjoy horseback riding, bird watching, and jeep safaris around the park. You can also choose to take a guided tour to explore the nearby cultural and historical sites of the region, such as the ancient Burj-e-Thum fort or the Satpara lake. For those seeking a little more adventure, trekking, hiking, and camping in the park are highly recommended.

Week-Long Itinerary for Deosai National Park

If you’re planning to visit Deosai National Park for a week or more, we suggest the following itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Skardu city and relax Day 2: Visit the Shigar Fort and Baltit Fort for a cultural experience and then head to Deosai National Park to see the breathtaking scenery. Day 3: Hike to Sheosar lake and explore wildlife and natural beauty around lake. Day 4: Visit Kala Pani lake, a beautiful site surrounded by glittering wildflowers Day 5: Go for a jeep safari in the park and explore the Upper Kachura lake. Day 6: Visit Katpana village, where you can learn about the local way of life and explore traditional homes made of mud and stone. Day 7: Travel back to Skardu or further on your journey to other destinations in the Gilgit-Baltistan region.

Food and Drink Scene of Deosai National Park

Must-Try Dishes

The food in Deosai National Park is a reflection of the region’s unique cultural heritage, and it is well known for its delicious local dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in the park include apricot chicken, yak meat, and local bread baked in a traditional tandoor oven. The park’s exquisite mountain honey is also a must-try.

Local Beverages

There are not many drinks available in the park, but the best tea that one can have is in the shacks in the park area. The tea they make is from the special plants that grow naturally in the park area.

Where to Stay in Deosai National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation options in Deosai National Park are limited, but there are still a few great options to choose from. You can stay in the Deosai Plains Resort, which offers comfortable accommodations with modern amenities, or choose to stay in one of the traditional guesthouses in the nearby villages. Another option for adventure enthusiasts is camping, which is allowed at designated campsites in the park.

What to Buy in Deosai National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Deosai National Park is known for its unique handicrafts and artisanal products, including traditional textiles, pottery, and wood carvings. Visitors can browse the local markets in the nearby towns and villages to find authentic souvenirs and gifts to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Deosai National Park

The local culture of Deosai National Park is influenced by the region’s diverse history and geography. The park is home to several ethnic groups, including the Balti, Shina, and Burusho people, who have their unique customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the local culture and customs and to respect them during their stay in the park.

Best Time to Visit Deosai National Park

The best time to visit Deosai National Park is between June and September, when the weather is mild, and the wildlife and flora are in full bloom. Winters in the park are harsh and unforgiving, and many of the park’s attractions are closed during this time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deosai National Park

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural beauty.
  • Use designated camping areas and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Be friendly and courteous to locals and other visitors.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park or surrounding areas.
  • Damage or remove any natural or cultural resources from the park.
  • Disturb the wildlife or engage in illegal hunting or poaching.

Safety and Health

Safety in Deosai National Park

Deosai National Park is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. It’s best to avoid traveling alone and to follow any safety guidelines provided by local authorities or park rangers. Visitors should also be mindful of the altitude and take appropriate measures, including staying hydrated and avoiding strenuous activity.

Health in Deosai National Park

Visitors should seek medical advice before traveling to the park, particularly if they have any pre-existing health conditions. It’s also essential to bring any necessary medications, including any prescription or over-the-counter medications, and to take measures to avoid altitude sickness, including gradually acclimatizing yourself to the altitude.

Getting to Deosai National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Deosai National Park by road from Skardu, which is well-connected to other major cities in the region. The park is approximately 89 km away from Skardu, and the journey takes around 2-3 hours. Private vehicles and taxis are among the most popular modes of transportation to the park.

Airports Near Deosai National Park

The nearest airport to Deosai National Park is the Skardu Airport, which is located approximately 20 km from the park. The airport connects to major cities in Pakistan, including Islamabad and Lahore.

Bus Stations Near Deosai National Park

The main bus station in Skardu is the Skardu Bus Stand, which provides regular bus services to other cities in the region, including Gilgit and Islamabad.

Other Options

There are also private tour operators who provide guided tours to Deosai National Park, which can be an excellent option for those who prefer a more organized and hassle-free travel experience.

How to Get Around in Deosai National Park

Traveling around Deosai National Park can be challenging, as much of the park’s terrain is rugged and undeveloped. However, there are several options available, including hiring a private vehicle or taking a guided tour with a local guide.

Where to Go Next from Deosai National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several other destinations worth exploring in the Gilgit-Baltistan region, including the renowned Karakoram Highway and the Skardu Valley. You can also visit the Khaplu Valley, a stunning valley that offers a glimpse into the rich history and culture of the region.

Conclusion

Deosai National Park is a magical destination that offers a unique and unforgettable experience to its visitors. From its breathtaking natural beauty to its rich history and culture, there’s something for everyone in this remote and unspoiled park. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, nature lover, or simply seeking a peaceful escape, Deosai National Park is a must-visit destination in Pakistan.
