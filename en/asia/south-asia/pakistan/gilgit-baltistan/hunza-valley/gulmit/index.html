Introduction to Gulmit

Nestled in the heart of the Hunza Valley in Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan, lies the small town of Gulmit. It is a melting pot of diverse cultures and traditions as it serves as a gateway to the Wakhan Corridor, bordering Afghanistan and China. With its rich history, scenic views, and outdoor adventures, Gulmit is a must-visit for anyone exploring the Hunza Valley.

List of Places to Visit in Gulmit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The rich history and culture of Gulmit can be explored through a variety of museums and historical places. The Gulmit Museum is a great starting point, where you can learn about the history and culture of the Wakhi people. The traditional house of Mir Jamal Khan (Raja of Hunza) is another must-visit attraction. It is a fine example of the architecture and cultural heritage of the Hunza Valley. You can also visit the Borith Lake, which is surrounded by the mountains and considered a sacred place by the locals.

Natural Attractions of Gulmit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gulmit offers a variety of outdoor activities in a serene and natural setting. If you’re looking for some adventure, you can go for a hike in the nearby mountains or opt for a thrilling mountain bike ride. The trek to Passu Glacier is a must-do for a unique outdoors experience. The breathtaking views of the valley from Eagles Nest, a viewpoint at the top of a mountain, are unparalleled. Similarly, Attabad Lake is an emerald-green lake surrounded by mountains offering serene views of the valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gulmit

Gulmit is the base camp for many popular hiking and biking routes in the Hunza Valley. The Tighat Cycling Track leading to Batura Glacier is a popular route among cyclists. The trek to the Ghulkin Glacier and Borith Lake is a moderate-difficulty trek that can be enjoyed by adults and children alike. Be sure to wear appropriate footwear and clothing and take ample water and snacks with you.

Best Things to Do in Gulmit: Activities and Tours

There are several activities in Gulmit for people of all ages and interests. You can sign up for a cultural tour to learn about the music, dance, and dresses of the Wakhi people. You can also book an outdoor adventure tour to explore the natural beauty of the Hunza Valley. The food tour is also famous among tourists, providing a culinary experience of the local dishes of Gulmit. Don’t forget to explore the handicrafts and local souvenirs, including handmade woolen blankets, rugs, and hats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gulmit

Day 1: Explore the Gulmit Museum and cultural sites Day 2-3: Trek to Ghulkin Glacier and Borith Lake Day 4: Take a bike ride or go for a hike Day 5-6: Visit Attabad Lake and Passu Glacier Day 7: Enjoy local food and shop for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Gulmit

Must-Try Dishes

Gulmit has a rich culinary heritage and is known for its flavorful dishes. Some must-try dishes include chapshoro, a type of meat pie; mantu, steamed dumplings filled with spiced meat; and apricot oil tea, a specialty tea of the region. Don’t forget to try the local bread, bakarkhani, and the locally-prepared yogurt.

Local Beverages

The locals of Gulmit love their tea, and you’ll find a variety of tea shops here serving different types of tea. The apricot oil tea, also known as ‘burut chai’, is a unique specialty of the region. You can also try the local beer and spirits, but it’s important to drink responsibly, especially if you’re planning a hike or an outdoor adventure.

Where to Stay in Gulmit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Gulmit, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some popular choices include the Gulmit Continental Hotel, Hunza Inn, and Gulmit Tourist Inn. If you’re on a budget, you can also opt for a homestay with a local family for an authentic cultural experience.

What to Buy in Gulmit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can bring back a piece of Gulmit with you by shopping for local souvenirs such as handmade woolen shawls, traditional Wakhi hats, and embroidered bags. You can also take home locally-made apricot oil, tea, and dry-fruit goodies. Local handicrafts such as embroidery, woodcarvings, and jewellery make for great gifts for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Gulmit

The Wakhi people, who make up a majority of the population of Gulmit, have a rich cultural heritage. They celebrate many festivals, including the Ganod and Navroz festivals. The traditional dresses, music, and dance reflect their unique culture. It is important to respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Gulmit

The best time to visit Gulmit is between April to October when the weather is pleasant, and you can explore the beauty of the valley. November to February usually experiences heavy snowfall, and it’s not advisable to visit during this time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gulmit

Gulmit is a relatively safe town to visit, but it’s important to take necessary precautions when going out for a hike or any outdoor activity. It is recommended to carry a first-aid kit and stay hydrated when going out.

Health in Gulmit

It’s always a good idea to consult with your doctor before traveling to Gulmit, especially if you have any health concerns. Visitors are advised to take necessary vaccinations before traveling to the region. It is important to drink clean water and eat from safe food sources.

Getting to Gulmit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gulmit can be reached by road from the nearby cities of Gilgit and Karimabad. There are regular bus services available from both cities. You can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Gulmit. The nearest airport is the Gilgit airport, and you can hire a taxi from the airport to reach Gulmit.

Airports Near Gulmit

The nearest airport to Gulmit is the Gilgit airport, which is approximately 110 KM away.

Bus Stations Near Gulmit

The Gilgit and Karimabad bus stations are the nearest bus stations to Gulmit.

Other Options

You can also opt for a helicopter ride from Gilgit to Gulmit, which offers stunning views of the valley.

How to Get Around in Gulmit

You can explore Gulmit on foot or hire a taxi to go around the town. For outdoor adventures, you can rent mountain bikes or hire a local guide for hiking trips. It’s always a good idea to check with your hotel or guesthouse for recommendations.

Where to Go Next from Gulmit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Gulmit, you can visit nearby destinations such as Karimabad, the capital of the Hunza valley, and Khunjerab Pass, the highest paved international border crossing in the world. The nearby Sost village is also a great place to visit, especially for its dry-fruit markets and local handicrafts.

Conclusion

Gulmit is a hidden gem in the Hunza Valley, offering a perfect blend of nature, culture, and outdoor adventure. With its rich history, natural beauty, and variety of activities, it’s no wonder why more and more tourists are discovering this magical town. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your trip to Gulmit and explore everything this peaceful town has to offer.
