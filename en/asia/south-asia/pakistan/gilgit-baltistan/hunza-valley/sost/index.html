The Ultimate Travel Guide for Sost, Pakistan

Located in the beautiful Hunza Valley of the Gilgit-Baltistan region in Pakistan, Sost is a hidden gem that is yet to be discovered by many travelers. This small town is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, rich culture, and history, making it an ideal place for adventure seekers and culture enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we will explore the best attractions, activities, and things to do in Sost, as well as provide information about local culture, safety tips, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Sost

Sost is located in the Hunza Valley of Gilgit-Baltistan and is a popular trading point with China due to its proximity to the Khunjerab Pass. Sost is a small town with a population of around 2,300 people. The town is known for its strategic location at the edge of the Karakoram Highway (KKH) and serves as the gateway to China from Pakistan. Sost is a beautiful and unique destination surrounded by stunning mountains and glaciers, making it a popular spot for adventure tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Sost

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sost has a rich cultural history that visitors can explore through its museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Sost Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the region at the Sost Museum. The museum displays art, crafts, and artifacts from the Hunza region.

  • Karakoram Highway: Take a drive on the Karakoram Highway, also known as the KKH, which is the world’s highest international highway. The KKH offers breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Khunjerab Pass: Located at an elevation of 16,000 feet, Khunjerab Pass is a high mountain pass on the border of Pakistan and China. The pass offers spectacular views of the Karakoram Mountains and is a popular spot for photography.

Natural Attractions of Sost: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Sost is unparalleled, and visitors can explore the scenic views and outdoor adventures in the following places:

  • Hunza River: The Hunza River is a famous river in northern Pakistan that’s surrounded by stunning mountains and green valleys. Visitors can enjoy fishing, picnics, and rafting in the river.

  • Gulmit Village: Gulmit is a beautiful village located about 35 kilometers from Sost. The village is popular for trekking and hiking trips to nearby mountains like Passu Peak and Shimshal. The village also has several historical and cultural sites, including the Altit and Baltit Forts.

  • Passu Glacier: The Passu Glacier is a beautiful glacier located about 20 kilometers from Sost. Visitors can take a trek to the glacier, which offers stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sost

Sost is an ideal destination for adventure seekers, and the town offers several hiking and biking routes. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Khunjerab Pass Trek: The Khunjerab Pass Trek is a challenging trek that takes you to the Khunjerab Pass. The trek offers stunning views of the Karakoram Mountains and is best done in the summer months.

  • Gulmit Village Trek: The Gulmit Village Trek is a moderate trek that takes you to Gulmit village. The trek offers breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys and is best done in the summer months.

Best Things to Do in Sost: Activities and Tours

Sost offers several exciting activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best things to do in Sost include:

  • Rafting: Take a rafting trip on the Hunza River and enjoy the stunning views of the Karakoram Mountains and valleys.

  • Cycling Tour: Take a cycling tour of the Karakoram Highway and explore the scenic views and mountain villages.

  • Mountain Climbing: Embark on a mountain climbing trip to Passu Peak or Shimshal Peak, and experience the stunning mountain landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sost

For those planning to spend a week in Sost, the following itinerary can help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Sost and explore the town
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Gulmit Village and explore the historical and cultural sites
  • Day 3: Trek to Khunjerab Pass
  • Day 4: Visit the Sost Museum and explore the local market
  • Day 5: Mountain climbing trip to Passu Peak
  • Day 6: Rafting trip on the Hunza River
  • Day 7: Depart for your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Sost

Sost is a melting pot of cultures and offers a variety of cuisines, including local, Indian, and Chinese. Some of the must-try dishes in Sost include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chapshoro: A dish made of minced meat and vegetables, wrapped in a dough and cooked over a hot stone.

  • Shapik: A noodle soup made with a lot of herbs, vegetables, and meat.

  • Ras Malai: A dessert made of homemade cheese soaked in sweet syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: A traditional tea made with yak butter, salt, and milk.

  • Apricot Juice: A refreshing juice made with fresh apricots.

Where to Stay in Sost: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sost offers several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Sost Silk Route Lodge: Located in the heart of Sost, this lodge offers comfortable rooms and great views of the mountains.

  • Hunza River Lodge: This lodge is located on the banks of the Hunza River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Hunza Embassy Hotel: A hotel that offers clean rooms and great facilities, including a restaurant and a garden.

What to Buy in Sost: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sost is a perfect destination for those who love shopping for unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best things to buy in Sost include:

  • Dry Fruits: Sost is known for its delicious dry fruits such as apricots, almonds, and walnuts, all grown locally.

  • Handicrafts: Sost has a rich culture and is famous for its handmade art and crafts such as embroidery, wood carvings, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Sost

Sost has a rich cultural history, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some of the local customs and traditions in Sost include:

  • Dress Code: Women should dress modestly, and it is recommended to wear a headscarf when entering religious places.

  • Respect for Elders: Elders are held in high regard in local culture, and visitors should show respect and deference towards them.

  • Religious Practices: Sost has a majority Muslim population, and visitors should be respectful of local religious practices.

Best Time to Visit Sost

The best time to visit Sost is from April to October, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The winter months are extremely cold, with heavy snowfall, and roads can be closed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sost

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Carry warm clothes and proper gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection, as it is not culturally accepted in the area.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Use drugs or alcohol, as it is not tolerated in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sost

Sost is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take precautions and be mindful of their surroundings. Some safety tips include:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.
  • Be cautious when hiking or trekking, and always follow the instructions of the guide.

Health in Sost

Visitors should take precautions with food and water in Sost, as it is recommended to stick with bottled or boiled water and cooked food to prevent sickness. Visitors should also carry a basic first aid kit for minor injuries, and it is recommended to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Sost: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sost is located about 45 kilometers from the Gilgit Airport, which offers daily flights to Islamabad. Some of the transportation options to get to Sost include:

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available from Gilgit to Sost and take about two hours.

  • Bus: Buses are available from Gilgit to Sost and take about four to five hours.

  • Shared Jeeps: Shared jeeps are available from Gilgit to Sost and take about three hours.

Airports Near Sost

  • Gilgit Airport: The nearest airport to Sost, located around 45 kilometers away.

  • Islamabad International Airport: This is the closest international airport, located around 800 kilometers from Sost.

Bus Stations Near Sost

  • Gilgit Bus Station: Located in Gilgit, about 125 kilometers away from Sost.

Other Options

  • Private car/Jeep: Visitors can also hire private cars or jeeps to get to Sost from Gilgit.

How to Get Around in Sost

Visitors can easily get around in Sost by foot, but can also use taxis or local buses to get around the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Sost: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sost is a great starting point to explore the Gilgit-Baltistan region, and visitors can easily visit nearby destinations such as:

  • Gilgit: Visit the capital city of the Gilgit-Baltistan region to explore some of the cultural and historical sites.

  • Khunjerab Pass: Take a trip to the highest paved international border crossing in the world and explore the stunning mountain landscapes.

  • Hunza Valley: Explore the beautiful Hunza Valley, which is home to several historical and cultural sites, including the Baltit Fort and Altit Fort.

Conclusion

Sost is an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and history.
