Introduction to Islamgarh

Located in the beautiful Azad Kashmir region of Pakistan, Islamgarh is a small city surrounded by mountains, greenery, and scenic beauty. Islamgarh is renowned for its cultural heritage, serene atmosphere, and outdoor adventures. The city has a rich history, vibrant traditions, and an array of attractions that draw visitors every year.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will highlight the best things to see and do in Islamgarh, including cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture and customs, travel tips, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Islamgarh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Islamgarh has an abundance of historical and cultural landmarks that reflect the rich heritage of the area. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • (1) Aryan Heritage Complex: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Aryan civilization, which existed in the region several thousand years ago.

  • (2) Pir Chinasi Shrine: This beautiful shrine is located atop a hill and provides breathtaking views of the region.

  • (3) Rajpoot Fort: A well-maintained historical fort, built during the Mughal era.

Natural Attractions of Islamgarh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Islamgarh is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best natural attractions of the region:

  • (1) Banjosa Lake: This beautiful lake is situated amid picturesque surroundings, offering unparalleled views of the surrounding mountains.

  • (2) Las Danna Waterfall: For adventure seekers, this waterfall on the outskirts of the city provides an opportunity for trekking and scenic photography.

  • (3) Summair Top: Standing at an elevation of 2,900 meters, this mountain top is perfect for trekking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Islamgarh

Islamgarh is a great place for outdoor adventures such as hiking and biking. Here are some of the top trails worth exploring:

  • (1) Pir Chinasi Trail: This trail is located near the Pir Chinasi Shrine and provides scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • (2) Banjosa Lake Trail: This trail leads you to the beautiful Banjosa Lake, where you can enjoy boating and fishing.

  • (3) Toli Pir Trail: A challenging trek that takes you to the summit of Toli Pir Mountain, offering panoramic views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Islamgarh: Activities and Tours

There are several fun activities to do and tours to take in Islamgarh:

  • (1) Boating on Banjosa Lake: The serene water of the lake provides a perfect setting for boating and relaxing.

  • (2) Camping in the mountains: Get the ultimate camping experience by pitching your tent on the mountainsides of Islamgarh.

  • (3) Visiting the local bazaars: Explore the local markets of Islamgarh to discover unique souvenirs and local handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Islamgarh

To make the most of your trip to this beautiful region, follow this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Islamabad and take a scenic drive to Islamgarh
  • Day 2: Visit the historical and cultural landmarks of the city
  • Day 3: Explore the natural attractions of the region, such as Las Danna Waterfall and Summair Top
  • Day 4: Take a boating tour of Banjosa Lake
  • Day 5: Hike the Toli Pir Trail
  • Day 6: Experience the local culture by shopping at the bazaars and trying local cuisine
  • Day 7: Bid farewell to Islamgarh and leave with unforgettable memories.

Food and Drink Scene of Islamgarh

No trip to Islamgarh is complete without trying the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Kashmiri Chai: A special type of tea, made with a unique blend of spices and milk.

  • (2) Dum Aloo: A famous vegetarian dish, consisting of potatoes cooked in a rich gravy.

  • (3) Goshtaba: A local delicacy made with minced meat and yogurt-based gravy.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Kashmiri Kahwa: A type of green tea that is flavored with cardamom and saffron.

  • (2) Chuski: A local version of shaved ice that is served with different syrups.

  • (3) Shikanjabeen: A popular summer drink, made with lemons and sugar.

Where to Stay in Islamgarh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Islamgarh:

  • (1) Banjosa View Point Guest House: A cozy guest house located near Banjosa Lake, offering stunning views of the surroundings.

  • (2) Hill Top Hotel: A luxurious hotel that offers comfortable rooms, excellent amenities, and scenic views of the surrounding hills.

  • (3) Hill Crest Resort: A budget-friendly accommodation option, offering clean rooms, good service, and easy access to the major attractions.

What to Buy in Islamgarh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Islamgarh is known for producing unique handicrafts and souvenirs that make for great gifts and keepsakes. Some popular items to bring home include:

  • (1) Handmade Shawls: Made from the finest wool and adorned with intricate embroidery.

  • (2) Wooden Carvings: Skillfully crafted wooden products such as boxes, frames, and figurines.

  • (3) Kashmiri Safron: A high-quality spice, often hailed as the best in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Islamgarh

Islamgarh has a rich culture that is deeply rooted in its history and traditions. Here are some customs and practices that you should be aware of:

  • (1) Dress modestly: Respect the local customs and dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques and shrines.

  • (2) Observe prayers: Muslims pray five times a day, and it is customary to observe the prayers and respect the timings.

  • (3) Hospitality: The locals of Islamgarh are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so be prepared to receive a warm welcome wherever you go.

Best Time to Visit Islamgarh

The best time to visit Islamgarh is from March to October. During this time, the weather is mild, the skies are clear, and the natural beauty of the region is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Islamgarh

It is important to observe the local customs and etiquette when visiting Islamgarh. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • (1) Treat locals with respect: The locals of Islamgarh are known for their hospitality and kindness, so be respectful and polite during your interactions.

  • (2) Dress modestly: Be mindful of the local customs and dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • (3) Try local cuisine: Islamgarh has some unique and delicious dishes, so do not hesitate to try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • (1) Consume alcohol in public: Islam is the prevailing religion in Islamgarh, and alcohol consumption is prohibited.

  • (2) Disrespect local customs: Be mindful of the local customs and do not engage in anything that may offend the local population.

  • (3) Litter: Make sure to dispose of your waste properly and keep the surroundings clean.

Safety and Health

Islamgarh is a safe and friendly city for tourists, but it is always best to take precautions and adhere to safety guidelines. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Islamgarh

  • (1) Stay informed: Keep yourself updated about the local weather, and be aware of the potential natural hazards, such as landslides.

  • (2) Avoid public demonstrations: Avoid participating or getting involved in any public demonstrations or protests.

  • (3) Be vigilant: Keep your belongings safe and be cautious while traveling at night or in secluded areas.

Health in Islamgarh

  • (1) Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

  • (2) Carry a first-aid kit: Carry a basic first-aid kit for emergencies.

  • (3) Get vaccinated: Make sure to get vaccinated for common diseases such as hepatitis and typhoid before traveling to the region.

Getting to Islamgarh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Islamgarh is easily accessible from major cities in Pakistan. Here are some of the main transportation options:

Airports Near Islamgarh

  • (1) Islamabad International Airport: Located around 150 kilometers from Islamgarh, this is the nearest international airport.

  • (2) Muzaffarabad Airport: Located around 40 kilometers from Islamgarh, this is a domestic airport that serves the region.

Bus Stations Near Islamgarh

  • (1) Islamabad Bus Station: Several buses operate between Islamabad and Muzaffarabad, the nearest city to Islamgarh.

  • (2) Muzaffarabad Bus Station: Several local buses and taxis run between Muzaffarabad and Islamgarh.

Other Options

  • (1) Private taxi: You can hire a private taxi to reach Islamgarh from Islamabad or Muzaffarabad.

  • (2) Rental Car: Several car rental companies operate in Islamabad and Muzaffarabad, offering comfortable and convenient transportation options.

How to Get Around in Islamgarh

The best way to explore Islamgarh is by hiring a local taxi or renting a car. Alternatively, you can also use public transport such as local buses and shared taxis, but it may not be as convenient as other options.

Where to Go Next from Islamgarh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Islamgarh is surrounded by several other beautiful destinations that are worth visiting. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • (1) Muzaffarabad: The capital city of Azad Kashmir, located around 50 kilometers from Islamgarh.

  • (2) Neelum Valley: A scenic valley surrounded by mountains and waterfalls, located around 80 kilometers from Islamgarh.

  • (3) Kohala: Located around 70 kilometers from Islamgarh, Kohala is a beautiful town famous for its hydroelectric power station and the iconic Kohala Bridge.
