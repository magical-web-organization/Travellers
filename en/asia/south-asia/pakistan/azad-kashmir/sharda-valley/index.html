Introduction to Sharda Valley

Sharda Valley is a stunningly beautiful area in Azad Kashmir, Pakistan, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas. It is a popular destination for adventure-seekers and nature lovers alike, with its breathtaking scenic views, natural wonders, and cultural attractions. Sharda Valley is home to an ancient university and several historic attractions, making it a fascinating place to visit for those interested in history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Sharda Valley

Sharda Valley offers a wide range of attractions to suit every traveler’s interests. Here are the must-visit places in the valley:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sharda University: This ancient university was once a center of learning in South Asia and is now in ruins. It is a must-visit attraction for history and architecture enthusiasts.
  • Sharda Fort: The ruins of this historic fort offer stunning views of the valley and are a popular spot for tourists.
  • Sharda Temple: This ancient Hindu temple is situated on the banks of the Neelum River and attracts pilgrims from across South Asia.

Natural Attractions of Sharda Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kel Valley: This picturesque valley is nestled in the mountains and offers stunning scenic views, making it a popular spot for photography and nature lovers.
  • Taobat: A natural rock formation that resembles a dome and is a popular spot for sightseeing.
  • Dhani Noseri Waterfall: This waterfall is a hidden gem in the valley and requires a short hike to reach. It is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharda Valley

Sharda Valley is the perfect place for trekking, hiking, and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Sharda to Kel Valley Trek: This trek takes hikers through lush forests, mountain streams, and picturesque landscapes, and ends in the breathtaking Kel Valley.
  • Sharda to Taobat Biking Route: This route takes bikers through scenic landscapes and offers challenging uphill climbs and speedy downhill descents.

Best Things to Do in Sharda Valley: Activities and Tours

Sharda Valley offers a wide range of activities to suit every traveler’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Explore the ancient ruins of Sharda University and Sharda Fort.
  • Spend a day relaxing and swimming in the crystal-clear waters of the Neelum River.
  • Take a jeep safari to explore the rugged mountain terrain and stunning natural scenery of the valley.
  • Try your hand at rock climbing or rappelling at Dhani Noseri Waterfall.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sharda Valley

Here is a sample week-long itinerary to explore Sharda Valley’s best attractions:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Sharda University and Sharda Fort.
Day 2 Visit the Sharda Temple and spend the afternoon swimming in the Neelum River.
Day 3 Explore the scenic beauty of the Taobat rock formation and surrounding areas.
Day 4 Embark on the Sharda to Kel Valley trek, stopping at scenic spots along the way.
Day 5 Spend the day relaxing in Kel Valley, enjoying the cool weather and scenic views.
Day 6 Take a jeep safari to explore the rugged mountain terrain around Sharda Valley.
Day 7 Hike to the Dhani Noseri Waterfall for breathtaking views and adventure activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Sharda Valley

The food and drink scene of Sharda Valley is an exciting mix of Pakistani and Kashmiri dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Naan: A traditional bread baked in a tandoor oven and served with curries and stews.
  • Kashmiri Pulao: A fragrant rice dish cooked with spices, saffron, nuts, and dried fruits.
  • Seekh Kebab: Minced meat skewered and grilled over charcoal, usually served with rice or naan.
  • Roghan Josh: A hearty lamb curry cooked with a mix of spices, yogurt, and tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kashmiri Chai: A pink, milky tea infused with cinnamon, cardamom, and saffron.
  • Lassi: A yogurt-based drink, often sweetened and flavored with fruit, herbs, or spices.

Where to Stay in Sharda Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sharda Valley offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Sharda Neelum Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with scenic views and comfortable rooms.
  • Sharda Resorts: A luxury Resort with excellent amenities and beautiful views of the valley.
  • Sharda Guest House: A comfortable guesthouse with friendly staff and a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Sharda Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The local bazaar in Sharda Valley is an excellent place to buy souvenirs and local specialties. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Handicrafts and artwork from the Sharda University market.
  • Kashmiri shawls and pashminas, known for their excellent quality and intricate designs.
  • Handmade soap made with natural ingredients, available at many local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharda Valley

The people of Sharda Valley are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. The area is home to many ethnic groups, including Kashmiris, Punjabis, and Pathans, each with their distinct culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sharda Valley

The best time to visit Sharda Valley is from May to September, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the scenery is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharda Valley

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local food and beverages.
  • Be respectful to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Engage in any drugs or alcohol abuse.
  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharda Valley

Sharda Valley is a relatively safe place to visit, but travelers should take standard precautions, such as keeping their belongings safe, avoiding isolated areas after dark, and being cautious of pickpockets near crowded areas and markets.

Health in Sharda Valley

Travelers are advised to bring their necessary medication and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Sharda Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sharda Valley

The nearest airport to Sharda Valley is Islamabad International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Sharda Valley

Buses are available from Rawalpindi, Islamabad, and Muzaffarabad to Sharda Valley.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire private cars or taxis to travel to the valley.

How to Get Around in Sharda Valley

The best way to get around Sharda Valley is by hiring a taxi or private car, as there is no public transport available.

Where to Go Next from Sharda Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that travelers can visit after exploring Sharda Valley:

  • Muzaffarabad: The capital city of Azad Kashmir, situated on the banks of the Jhelum River.
  • Nanga Parbat: A 26,660-foot-high peak in the Himalayas, popular for trekking and mountaineering activities.
  • Lahore: The vibrant and cultural city of Punjab, known for its landmarks like the Badshahi Mosque and Lahore Fort.

Conclusion

Sharda Valley is a hidden gem in Pakistan, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and adventure activities. With this comprehensive guide, travelers will have all the information they need to plan a memorable and rewarding trip to Sharda Valley.
