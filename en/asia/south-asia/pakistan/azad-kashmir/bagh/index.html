Bagh is a picturesque district located in the Azad Kashmir region of Pakistan. Known for its abundance of natural beauty, historical sites and cultural attractions, it offers a true taste of Pakistani hospitality and culture. Whether you’re a nature lover looking for outdoor adventures, a history buff interested in exploring ancient architecture or an avid shopper looking for unique souvenirs, there is something for everyone in Bagh.

Introduction to Bagh

Once ruled by the Rajputs and later the Mughals, Bagh is a culturally rich district offering a unique blend of history, nature and culture. Known for its mesmerizing landscapes, the district is home to lush green forests, rivers, streams, and snow-capped peaks. With a pleasant climate and hospitable people, Bagh is a popular destination for tourists from all around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Bagh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bagh Fort: An architectural masterpiece built by Sultan Sikandar in the 16th century, Bagh Fort is a symbol of the grandeur of the Mughal era. It was constructed with local red sandstone and features a beautiful mosque and a garden.
  • Ancient Temples: Bagh is home to a number of ancient temples that date back to the 9th century. Some of the prominent temples include the Kala Mai Temple, the Nilnag Temple, and the Shankaracharya Temple.
  • Bagh Museum: Showcasing the history and culture of Bagh, the museum is filled with artifacts, paintings, and sculptures that offer a glimpse of the district’s rich heritage.

Natural Attractions of Bagh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ganga Choti: At 8,000 feet above sea level, this is the highest peak in the region. The view from the top is breathtaking, and the trek to the summit is an adventure in itself.
  • Las Danna: A natural waterfall nestled in the mountains, Las Danna is an idyllic picnic spot and a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.
  • Dhirkot: Known for its panoramic views, Dhirkot is a small hill station located on the Bagh-Muzaffarabad Road that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bagh

  • Pirchanasi: Located about 30 kilometers from Bagh, Pirchanasi is a stunning location offering excellent hiking opportunities.
  • Sudhan Gali: The Sudhan Gali Pass offers a scenic trail for hikers and mountain bikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Bagh: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Bagh Bazaar: Known for its unique local handicrafts, the Bagh Bazaar offers an excellent opportunity to shop for souvenirs and gifts.
  • Explore the Bagh Wildlife Sanctuary: Covering an area of 39,000 hectares, the sanctuary offers a unique opportunity to explore the natural habitat of the local flora and fauna, including the Himalayan black bear, snow leopard, and jungle cat.
  • Attend a local festival: Bagh hosts a range of cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Basant Festival and the Urs of Baba Shadi Shaheed.

Food and Drink Scene of Bagh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: A local favorite, Biryani is a spicy rice dish made with chicken, lamb or vegetables.
  • Harissa: This traditional Kashmiri lamb stew is slow-cooked with pounded wheat and spices and is a must-try dish in Bagh.
  • Kahwa: Served in traditional cups called “Khos”, this Kashmiri tea is an aromatic blend of green tea, cinnamon, cardamom, saffron, and almonds.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A popular Pakistani yogurt drink, Lassi is made with yogurt, water, and sugar, and is best enjoyed chilled.
  • Sugarcane Juice: A refreshing drink that helps beat the heat of Bagh, this juice is extracted from fresh sugarcane and is best enjoyed with ice.
  • Green Tea: Grown in the hilly areas of Bagh, green tea is a popular local beverage, and is loaded with the antioxidants that are essential for health.

Where to Stay in Bagh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • AJ Hotels: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Bagh city, with all in-house amenities.
  • Mir Continental Hotel: A mid-range hotel with clean and comfortable rooms, and a range of amenities for guests.
  • Green Orchard Guest House: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable and clean rooms, located next to a garden.

What to Buy in Bagh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pashmina Shawls: Bagh is famous for its high-quality pashmina shawls, scarves, and blankets made with the finest wool.
  • Walnut Woodcraft: Known for its craftsmanship, walnut woodcrafts are a unique souvenir to take back home.
  • Kehwa: Sourced fresh from the gardens of Bagh, Kehwa is a popular souvenir that is perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Bagh

Bagh is a deeply traditional and conservative region of Pakistan, and visitors are advised to respect the local customs and values. Dress in modest clothing, and avoid alcohol and smoking in public areas. It is always good to learn a few words of the local language to show your respect for the culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Bagh

The best time to visit Bagh is between March and October, when the weather is pleasant, and the natural beauty of the region is at its peak. Be sure to bring warm clothes if you’re visiting within the winter months, November to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bagh

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly and conservatively.
  • Learn a few words of the local language.
  • Enjoy the beautiful natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol or smoke in public areas.
  • Take photos of local women without permission.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions, or sacred sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bagh

Bagh is a relatively safe place to travel to, however always take precautions as it’s part of a politically sensitive region. Avoid unlit areas and be careful of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Bagh

Medical facilities in Bagh are limited, and visitors are advised to take particular care about their health and safety standards. Drink only sealed bottled water, and avoid food from street vendors.

Getting to Bagh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Bagh can travel by plane or bus, with public transport services running regularly from key locations in Pakistan.

Airports Near Bagh

The nearest airport is located in Islamabad, approximately 133 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bagh

The main bus terminal is located in the center of the city, offering transportation to all major towns and cities in the area.

Other Options

Visitors may be able to arrange for private transport via taxis or rental cars. However, ensure you take necessary safety precautions with either of these options.

How to Get Around in Bagh

Local transportation options include auto-rickshaws, taxis, and buses, which are readily available throughout the city. However, hiring a local guide or private car is recommended for trekking and visiting remote or hard to reach places.

Where to Go Next from Bagh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Muzaffarabad: The capital of Azad Kashmir, Muzaffarabad is a beautiful city offering historical sites, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.
  • Neelum Valley: Known for its stunning views of the mountains and natural beauty, Neelum Valley is a scenic paradise that’s perfect for hiking and trekking.

Conclusion

Bagh is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a unique blend of nature, culture, and history. With its breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality, Bagh is a timeless gem that will stick with you for a lifetime.
