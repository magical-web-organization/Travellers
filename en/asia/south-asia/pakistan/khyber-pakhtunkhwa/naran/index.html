Introduction to Naran

Naran is a quaint town located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan. Nestled in the midst of the picturesque Himalayan Mountains, Naran is a popular tourist destination renowned for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural heritage. With its idyllic setting, Naran offers the perfect escape from the chaotic city life.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Naran. From the best tourist attractions to the most popular hiking trails, from the local cuisine to the accommodations, we have got you covered. So, sit back and read on!

List of Places to Visit in Naran

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kaghan Valley Heritage Museum - A must-visit for history buffs, this museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich cultural heritage of the Kaghan Valley.

  • Shogran Valley Archaeological Site - Explore the ruins of an ancient civilization that existed in the Shogran Valley.

  • Siri Paye - A beautiful hill station with green meadows and a stunning view of Malika Parbat.

Natural Attractions of Naran: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saif-ul-Malook Lake - This crystal-clear alpine lake surrounded by snow-capped mountains is a sight to behold. Rent a boat and explore the stunning scenery.

  • Lalazar Meadows - A mesmerizing valley with lush green meadows, crystal-clear streams, and mesmerizing views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Babusar Pass - A high-altitude mountain pass that offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

  • Naran Valley - The main attraction of Naran is its breathtakingly beautiful valley that offers scenic views, trekking, and camping opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naran

  • Borith Lake Trek - A moderate-level trek that takes you to a gorgeous lake surrounded by mountains.

  • Noori Top Trek - A scenic trek that leads to a pass that offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Siri Paye Trek - An easy trek through beautiful meadows that takes you to an amazing viewpoint.

Best Things to Do in Naran: Activities and Tours

  • River Rafting - Experience the thrill of white-water rafting on the Kunhar River.

  • Jeep Safaris - Explore the rugged terrain of Naran and its surroundings on a thrilling jeep safari.

  • Paragliding - Soar through the skies and get a bird’s eye view of the beautiful valley of Naran.

  • Camping - Spend a night under the stars and enjoy the tranquil surroundings of Naran Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Naran

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chapli Kebab - A delicious beef patty seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices served with naan.

  • Sarson Ka Saag - A vegetarian dish made from mustard leaves and spinach, often served with makki ki roti.

  • Kabuli Pulao - A flavorful rice dish cooked with succulent pieces of lamb, carrots, raisins, and nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Kahwa - A fragrant green tea infused with saffron, cardamom, and cinnamon.

  • Lassi - A refreshing yogurt-based drink, perfect to beat the heat.

  • Sugar Cane Juice - A healthy and refreshing drink, extracted from sugar cane.

Where to Stay in Naran: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • PTDC Motel Naran - A government-run motel with basic amenities but a great location.

  • Pine Top Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and scenic views.

  • Fairy Land Hotel - A mid-range hotel with modern amenities and an on-site restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.

  • Kunhar View Guest House - A cozy guest house with a homely atmosphere and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Naran: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kashmiri Shawls - A popular souvenir among tourists, the shawls are made of fine cashmere wool and come in a variety of designs and colors.

  • Dry Fruits - Naran is famous for its high-quality dried fruits, including apricots, figs, and almonds.

  • Stone and Wood Carvings - Shop for intricate carvings made from local stones and wood, a perfect gift for art and craft lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Naran

Naran has a rich cultural heritage and is famous for its unique blend of Hindu and Buddhist influences. The locals are friendly, and their hospitality is exemplary. You will notice that the women wear colorful shawls and embroidered dresses, while the men often sport traditional attires like shalwar kameez and waistcoats. Respecting the local customs and traditions is essential when visiting Naran, and it is courteous to inquire before taking photographs of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Naran

The best time to visit Naran is from May to September, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. During this time, you can witness the beauty of the valleys and indulge in various outdoor activities like trekking, camping, and paragliding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naran

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Dress modestly and carry warm clothes if traveling during winters.

  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any illegal activities or drug usage.

  • Take photographs without permission.

  • Litter in the beautiful valleys of Naran.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naran

Naran is a relatively safe place to visit, but it is always advisable to take precautions to avoid any accidents or mishaps. Please be careful while trekking or indulging in adventure sports and follow the safety guidelines laid down by the instructors. Avoid traveling alone at night, and it is always preferable to travel with a guide or in groups.

Health in Naran

Naran has adequate medical facilities, and the town has a primary health care center. It is advisable to carry any necessary medication along with the prescription. Please take precautions to avoid altitude sickness if traveling to high altitudes.

Getting to Naran: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naran

The nearest airport to Naran is the Islamabad International Airport, which is around 240 km away from Naran. From the airport, you can hire a private taxi or book a shared cab to reach Naran.

Bus Stations Near Naran

The town has a well-connected bus service, and there are several private and public buses plying between Islamabad and Naran. You can also book a minibus or shared taxi from Rawalpindi or Islamabad.

Other Options

If you are traveling with a group, you can also hire a private car to reach Naran. The roads are scenic but can be narrow and bumpy.

How to Get Around in Naran

The best way to explore Naran is on foot or by hiring a local guide. You can also take a jeep safari or rent a car to explore the surroundings of Naran. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Naran: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Babusar Top - A high-altitude mountain pass that offers breathtaking views and is located at the border of Khyber-Pakhtunkhwa and Gilgit Baltistan.

  • Gilgit-Baltistan - Explore the stunning valleys of Gilgit-Baltistan, located north of Naran.

  • Swat Valley - A beautiful valley located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, known for its scenic beauty, rich heritage, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Naran is an idyllic destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. From the stunning valleys to the rugged mountain passes, Naran has something for everyone. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide for Naran will help you plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this beautiful destination in Pakistan.
