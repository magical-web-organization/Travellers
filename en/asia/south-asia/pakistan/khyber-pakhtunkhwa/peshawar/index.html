Introduction to Peshawar

Peshawar is a vibrant city located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan. Known as the “Gateway to the Khyber Pass,” it’s a city with a rich history that dates back to ancient times. With a population of over 3 million people, Peshawar boasts a diverse culture, delicious food, and many interesting landmarks and attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Peshawar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Peshawar Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts, sculptures, and relics from the Gandhara civilization. Visitors can explore the rich history of the region and see rare Buddhist artwork.

  2. Mohabbat Khan Mosque: This stunning mosque was built in the 17th century and features intricate tile work, marble carvings, and a grand courtyard. It’s a must-see landmark for anyone visiting Peshawar.

  3. Bala Hisar Fort: This ancient fort overlooks the city and has been used as a military base for centuries. The fort was built by Afghan King Timur Shah Durrani in the 18th century and has since been renovated multiple times.

Natural Attractions of Peshawar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Khyber Pass: This historic passageway has been used since ancient times and is a popular destination for tourists. The road winds through the rugged mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Shalimar Gardens: This garden complex is located on the outskirts of Peshawar and features lush greenery, fountains, and beautiful walkways. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful stroll or picnic in the park.

  3. Trail 5: This hiking trail is popular among locals and visitors alike. It’s a moderate level trail that winds through the scenic Margalla Hills, offering breathtaking views of the city and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peshawar

  1. Khyber Pass Trek: This trek takes hikers through the rugged terrain of the Khyber Pass, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  2. Galiyat Trek: This trek takes hikers along the lush greenery of the Galiyat region, passing through quaint villages and towns along the way.

  3. Swat Valley Trek: This trek takes hikers through the scenic Swat Valley, passing through beautiful waterfalls, forests, and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Peshawar: Activities and Tours

  1. Food Tour: Peshawar is known for its delicious cuisine, and visitors can take a food tour to sample the local street food and traditional dishes.

  2. Shopping Tour: Peshawar is home to many bustling markets and bazaars selling everything from textiles and jewelry to handicrafts and spices.

  3. Heritage Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the city’s historic landmarks, including the Mohabbat Khan Mosque, Bala Hisar Fort, and Peshawar Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peshawar

Day 1: Explore the city’s historic landmarks, including the Mohabbat Khan Mosque and Peshawar Museum.

Day 2: Take a trip to the Khyber Pass and explore the surrounding mountains.

Day 3: Take a hike along Trail 5 and enjoy the stunning views of the Margalla Hills.

Day 4: Take a food tour of the city and sample the local cuisine.

Day 5: Visit the Shalimar Gardens and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 6: Take a shopping tour of the city’s markets and bazaars.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the city’s vibrant culture and nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Peshawar

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chapli Kebab: This spicy meat dish is a specialty of Peshawar and is made with ground beef, onions, and spices.

  2. Kabuli Pulao: This flavorful rice dish is made with succulent pieces of lamb and topped with raisins and carrots.

  3. Seekh Kebab: This grilled meat skewer is a popular street food in Peshawar and is made with ground beef or lamb.

Local Beverages

  1. Lassi: This sweet, yoghurt-based drink is a local favorite and is perfect for cooling down on a hot day.

  2. Kashmiri Chai: This pink tea is a specialty of the region and is made with a mix of tea leaves, milk, nuts, and spices.

  3. Sugar Cane Juice: This refreshing drink is made by squeezing fresh sugar cane and is a popular drink in Peshawar’s markets.

Where to Stay in Peshawar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pearl Continental Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers guests stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Shelton’s Rezidor: This modern hotel offers comfortable accommodations and is located close to many of Peshawar’s popular landmarks.

  3. Hi Star Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers basic accommodations at an affordable price and is located close to the city’s markets and bazaars.

What to Buy in Peshawar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: The city is known for its beautiful handicrafts, including woven textiles, rugs, and pottery.

  2. Spices: Peshawar is home to many spice markets selling a variety of spices, including cumin, coriander, and turmeric.

  3. Gems and Jewelry: The region is known for its precious stones and jewelry, including rubies, emeralds, and diamonds.

Local Culture and Customs of Peshawar

The people of Peshawar are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Visitors should dress conservatively and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Peshawar

The best time to visit Peshawar is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peshawar

Do:

  1. Dress conservatively and respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Learn a few basic phrases in Urdu, the local language.

  3. try the local cuisine and sample traditional dishes.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water or eat food from street vendors.

  2. Take photographs of people without their permission.

  3. Visit the tribal areas without proper transportation and security.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peshawar

Peshawar is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should take basic precautions like avoiding isolated areas, staying alert in crowded areas, and not flashing expensive belongings.

Health in Peshawar

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccines and consider getting additional vaccines like hepatitis A and typhoid. It’s also important to drink only bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.

Getting to Peshawar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peshawar

  1. Bacha Khan International Airport: This is the main airport serving Peshawar and operates flights to and from many domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Peshawar

  1. Peshawar Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the heart of the city and connects to many destinations throughout Pakistan.

Other Options

  1. Train: The Pakistan Railways operates trains to and from Peshawar, connecting to many destinations throughout the country.

How to Get Around in Peshawar

Visitors can get around Peshawar by using local transportation options like buses, taxis, or auto-rickshaws. Walking is also a great way to explore the city’s many historic landmarks and bazaars.

Where to Go Next from Peshawar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Swat Valley: This scenic valley is located a few hours’ drive from Peshawar and is known for its beautiful meadows, waterfalls, and forests.

  2. Islamabad: This modern and cosmopolitan city is located a few hours’ drive from Peshawar and is home to many interesting landmarks and attractions.

  3. Lahore: This historic city is located in Punjab province and is known for its stunning architecture, delicious cuisine and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Peshawar is a fascinating destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its vibrant bazaars, delicious cuisine, and stunning landmarks, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Pakistan. Use our comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this amazing city.
