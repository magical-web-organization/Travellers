Introduction to Chitral

Chitral, located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan, is a hidden gem in the country’s tourism industry. The area is known for its stunning natural landscapes, including the Hindu Kush mountain range, lush green valleys, and turquoise blue rivers. Apart from the scenic views, Chitral is home to a rich and diverse cultural heritage, stretching back centuries. The friendly locals, who are predominantly of the Kho people, welcome visitors with open arms and always eager to showcase their traditions and customs. Here is a comprehensive guide to help you plan your trip to this hidden gem of Pakistan’s north.

List of Places to Visit in Chitral

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chitral Museum

The Chitral Museum, also known as the Royal Palace of Chitral, is a great place to learn about Chitral’s rich past. The museum displays an array of traditional garments, jewelry, artefacts, and weapons from the past, providing insight into the region’s diverse culture.

Shahi Mosque

Located in the heart of Chitral bazaar, the Shahi mosque is a historical landmark that dates back to the 14th century. This mosque is a prime example of the unique architecture of North Pakistan during the Islamic era.

Kalash Valley

The Kalash Valley is home to the Kalash people, an ethnic group of only around 4000 people that remain in the area, unique to North Pakistan, and believed to date back to the era of Alexander the Great. The valley is famous for its stunning natural beauty and festivals.

Natural Attractions of Chitral: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tirich Mir

Tirich Mir is the highest peak of the Hindu Kush mountain range, standing at an impressive 7,708 meters. The peak provides a stunning backdrop for the valley and the perfect spot for adventure seekers on a trekking expedition.

Garam Chashma

Garam Chashma, located around 40 km from Chitral, is a scenic spot famous for its hot springs. The natural thermal springs are known to have medicinal properties, providing the locals with a source of healing for centuries.

Shandur Pass

The Shandur Pass, situated at 3,740 meters above sea level, is the highest polo ground in the world that hosts an annual mega event, the Shandur Polo Festival, every year between July and August. This festival is a great opportunity to witness the sport of polo and experience the unique cultural traditions of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chitral

The Chitral Gol National Park

The Chitral Gol National Park covers around 7,750 hectares and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including the elusive snow leopard, the Himalayan Ibex, musk deer, and many more. The park is an ideal spot for trekking enthusiasts and a great way to explore the area’s natural beauty.

Bumblebee Bike Trail

The Bumblebee Bike Trail is a relatively new addition to Chitral’s tourism industry, attracting mountain-biking enthusiasts from all over the world with its challenging terrain and stunning mountain views.

Best Things to Do in Chitral: Activities and Tours

Attend a Local Festival

The local festivals of the Kalash Valley are a great way to immerse yourself in the cultural traditions of the area. The three famous festivals are the Chilimjusht, Uchal, and Caumus, held in late spring, mid-summer, and mid-winter, respectively.

Experience the Game of Polo

Polo is a sport that originated in Central Asia and is still played with its traditional rules in Chitral. The Shandur Polo festival, mentioned earlier, is a great opportunity to experience the sport and its unique traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Chitral

Must-Try Dishes

Chapshurro

Chapshurro is a traditional dish consisting of minced meat, onions, and spices wrapped in dough and pan-fried to perfection.

Augahr

Augahr is a popular winter delicacy in Chitral. It is a thick soup made up of wheat flour and shredded lamb, which makes it hearty and delicious.

Local Beverages

Hunza Tea

Hunza tea is a hot beverage made with apricot kernels and almond oil, providing a unique twist on traditional tea.

Sea Buckthorn Juice

Sea Buckthorn is a local fruit that grows in the region and is harvested in the fall. The juice derived from it is said to have health benefits and is a must-try when visiting Chitral.

Where to Stay in Chitral: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hindukush Heights

Hindukush Heights, located in the heart of the Kalash Valley, is a luxury guest house that provides stunning mountain views and traditional furnishings.

PTDC Motel, Chitral

PTDC Motel, located in the centre of Chitral bazaar, is a more affordable option that offers basic amenities and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Chitral: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chitrali Topi

The Chitrali topi, a traditional hat made of wool, is an iconic symbol of Chitral’s culture and a must-have souvenir for all visitors.

Pashmina Shawl

Chitral is famous for producing high-quality pashmina shawls made from the wool of local goats. These shawls are soft, durable and perfect for use in cold weather.

Local Culture and Customs of Chitral

Chitrali people are known for their hospitality, warm-heartedness, and respect for their customs and traditions. The language spoken by the majority of the people is Khowar, and the dress is traditional, colourful and unique to the area. Chitral is an Islamic region, so visitors must respect local customs regarding dress code and modesty.

Best Time to Visit Chitral

The best time to visit Chitral is between April and October as the weather is pleasant, and nature is at its best during these months. The Shandur Polo festival is usually held in the last week of July, making it a great time to visit for those interested in attending the event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chitral

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress code.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Engage with locals, and learn about their traditions and customs.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activity that disrespects Islamic values and traditions.
  • Disrespect or mistreat local culture in any way.
  • Travel alone in remote areas, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chitral

While Chitral is generally safe for visitors, it is advisable to take necessary precautions and stay vigilant at all times. Avoid venturing out alone at night, and always keep your valuables safe.

Health in Chitral

Chitral has basic healthcare facilities, but it is advisable to pack essential medicines and medical supplies if needed. Travellers are also recommended to get vaccinated for measles, mumps, and rubella before visiting the area.

Getting to Chitral: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chitral

The nearest airport to Chitral is the Chitral Airport, located around 1.5 km north-east of the town centre.

Bus Stations Near Chitral

Chitral is well-connected to major cities in Pakistan via bus. The Daewoo Express Bus Service and other services offer comfortable travel to Chitral from major cities in the region.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are other transportation options available for visitors.

How to Get Around in Chitral

Renting a car is the best way to get around Chitral and explore the area at your convenience. However, visitors can also use public transportation options such as taxis, buses, and private vans.

Where to Go Next from Chitral: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gilgit

Gilgit, located around 358 km from Chitral, is another popular destination in Northern Pakistan known for its scenic views and cultural heritage.

Skardu

Skardu is a beautiful town located around 584 km from Chitral and is known for its stunning views and natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Chitral is a fascinating destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure. This comprehensive guide provides useful information to help travellers plan their trip to Chitral and make the most of their experience. From exploring the area’s tourist attractions to trying local cuisine and souvenirs, Chitral has something for everyone. It is a must-visit destination for any avid traveller seeking adventure and a unique cultural experience.
