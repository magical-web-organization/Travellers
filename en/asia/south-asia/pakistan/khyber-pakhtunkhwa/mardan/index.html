Introduction to Mardan

Located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan, Mardan is a city that is steeped in history and culture. The area has been inhabited since ancient times, and the many archaeological sites and museums in the city and surrounding areas testify to this rich heritage. The city itself is bustling and full of life, with many shops, markets, and eateries. The natural beauty of the area is also a draw for visitors, with scenic views and outdoor adventures waiting to be discovered.

List of Places to Visit in Mardan

To fully experience all that Mardan has to offer, make sure to include these must-see attractions on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mardan is home to several important sites that celebrate its rich cultural heritage. These include:

  • Takht-i-Bahi: This UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the most well-preserved ancient Buddhist monasteries in Pakistan, dating back to the 1st century BCE. It was once an important center of learning and is known for its beautiful architecture, including intricate carvings and detailed stonework.

  • Charsadda Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, the Charsadda Museum has an impressive collection of Gandhara, Hindu, and Muslim artifacts, showcasing the rich heritage of the region.

  • Sheikh Maltoon Town: This historic town is named after the famous Pashtun poet Sheikh Maltoon, and is home to many important cultural landmarks, including his shrine and an ancient mosque.

Natural Attractions of Mardan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Mardan and the surrounding areas is truly awe-inspiring, and there are plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some popular options include:

  • Khwaja Ghaltan Baba: A beautiful shrine nestled in the hills outside of Mardan, this is a popular destination for those seeking peace and tranquility. The trek to the top of the hill can be challenging, but the views of the surrounding valley are well worth the effort.

  • Swabi Waterfall: Located in nearby Swabi district, this waterfall is a popular picnic spot and a great place to cool off on a hot day.

  • Sheikh Badin Hill: One of the highest peaks in the area, Sheikh Badin Hill offers breathtaking views of the Mardan valley and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mardan

Mardan and the surrounding areas have some excellent hiking and biking routes, which is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Takht-i-Bahi hiking trail: This trail leads from the Takht-i-Bahi site to the nearby town of Shahbaz Garhi. Along the way, you’ll pass through beautiful countryside and take in stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Swabi biking trail: This trail takes you through the scenic Swabi district, passing by waterfalls, fields of orange trees, and picturesque villages.

Best Things to Do in Mardan: Activities and Tours

There are endless things to see and do in Mardan, but here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Cultural tour: A guided tour of the many museums and historical sites in the area is a great way to learn more about the rich history and culture of Mardan.

  • Food tour: Pakistani cuisine is famous for its spicy flavors and rich aromas, and a food tour of Mardan’s many restaurants and street food vendors is a must-do for foodies.

  • Adventure tour: Mardan and the surrounding areas offer a variety of outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, and rock climbing.

Food and Drink Scene of Mardan

Must-Try Dishes

Mardan is known for its delicious and spicy food. Don’t miss these must-try dishes:

  • Chapli kebab: A famous Pashtun dish made from ground beef or mutton, spices, and herbs.

  • Pulao: A fragrant rice dish cooked with chicken or beef, and a variety of spices.

  • Sajji: A whole lamb roasted over an open fire and seasoned with a blend of spices.

Local Beverages

For a refreshing drink after a meal, try one of these local beverages:

  • Lassi: A traditional yogurt-based drink that’s perfect for cooling off on a hot day.

  • Sugar cane juice: Made from fresh sugar cane, this sweet and tangy juice is a popular thirst-quencher.

Where to Stay in Mardan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mardan has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular choices include:

  • Taj Mahal Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Mardan, the Taj Mahal Hotel boasts spacious rooms, fine dining, and an outdoor pool.

  • Motel Grandeur: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

  • The Backpacker’s Hostel: Perfect for budget travelers, The Backpacker’s Hostel offers dormitory-style rooms, a communal kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views.

What to Buy in Mardan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a truly authentic souvenir of your trip to Mardan, consider buying one of these local products:

  • Traditional Pashtun clothing: Colorful and intricately embroidered, traditional Pashtun clothing can be found in many of the local markets.

  • Handmade crafts: From pottery to baskets to woodcarvings, there are many handmade crafts to choose from in Mardan.

Local Culture and Customs of Mardan

Mardan is a conservative city, and visitors should dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions. The Pashtun culture is known for its hospitality, so expect to be warmly welcomed by the locals.

Best Time to Visit Mardan

The best time to visit Mardan is between October and February, when the weather is cool and comfortable. Avoid visiting during the hot summer months, as temperatures can soar above 100 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mardan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Visit the many museums and historical sites
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Wander around alone at night
  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Eat food from street vendors if you have a sensitive stomach

Safety and Health

Safety in Mardan

While Mardan is generally a safe city, visitors should be aware of the risk of petty crime such as pickpocketing and theft. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Mardan

The tap water in Mardan is not safe to drink, so make sure to stick to bottled water. The food can also be spicy and rich, so those with sensitive stomachs may want to take precautions. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you.

Getting to Mardan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mardan

The closest airport to Mardan is Bacha Khan International Airport in Peshawar, which is approximately 30 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Mardan.

Bus Stations Near Mardan

Mardan has a bus station that connects it to other major cities in Pakistan. Visitors can take a bus from Islamabad or Peshawar to reach Mardan.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a driver is also an option for those who prefer to have more flexibility and independence when traveling. However, be aware that driving in Pakistan can be challenging due to the chaotic traffic and poor road conditions in some areas.

How to Get Around in Mardan

Taxis and rickshaws are the most common mode of transportation in Mardan. Negotiate the fare before getting in the vehicle to avoid any confusion later on. Buses also run throughout the city and are a cheap and convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Mardan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many interesting destinations to explore in the surrounding areas of Mardan. Some popular options include:

  • Peshawar: The capital city of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, Peshawar is known for its rich history, bazaars, and delicious food.

  • Abbottabad: A hill station in the Hazara region, Abbottabad is known for its cool climate, natural beauty, and colonial architecture.

  • Nathiagali: Another popular hill station, Nathiagali is known for its stunning views of the Himalayas and its many hiking trails.

Conclusion

Mardan is a fascinating and unique city, filled with history, culture, and adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring the many museums and historical sites, venturing into the wilderness for a hike, or simply soaking up the local culture and traditions, Mardan has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well on your way to planning an unforgettable trip to this vibrant and exciting city.
