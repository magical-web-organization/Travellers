Introduction to Hangu

Hangu is a district in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan. Located in the north-western part of the country, Hangu is surrounded by mountains and hills, offering breathtaking scenery and a perfect escape for adventure enthusiasts. Hangu has a rich cultural and historical heritage, with significant landmarks and museums.

This travel guide is your ultimate handbook for exploring the best of Hangu. Whether you want to discover the natural beauty of the district, explore its historical landmarks, indulge in local cuisine, or experience its traditional culture, our comprehensive guide has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Hangu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kohat Museum: Kohat Museum is the main museum of the district, housing a valuable collection of archaeological findings from Gandhara civilization, coins, and weapons from the Mughal and British era, as well as a botanical garden.

  • Hangu Fort: Hangu Fort is a historic structure dating back to the 19th century. It served as a military stronghold in the past, and now it’s open to tourists, providing panoramic views of the surroundings.

  • Bab-e-Khyber: Bab-e-Khyber is a strategic mountain pass that connects Pakistan with Afghanistan, located about 30 kilometers from Hangu. The pass was historically significant for invaders and traders, and it’s now a popular tourist spot for its scenic views.

Natural Attractions of Hangu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Orakzai Lake: Orakzai Lake is a natural lake located near Hangu, surrounded by mountainous terrain. The lake is a popular spot for fishing and boating, and it’s an excellent place for a picnic with family and friends.

  • Kohat Tunnel: Kohat Tunnel is a 1.9-kilometer long tunnel that connects Kohat with Hangu. The tunnel is located in the mountains, and it’s a scenic drive with spectacular views on either side.

  • Karrak Lake: Karrak Lake is a high-altitude lake located in the Hangu district, offering mesmerizing views of snow-capped peaks and lush green meadows. The lake is a perfect spot for camping, hiking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hangu

Hangu has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking vistas of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Some popular routes are:

  • Hangu Mountain Trail: The Hangu Mountain Trail is an 8-kilometer long hike through mountains and forests, offering scenic views of the valley and nearby villages.

  • **Kohat Pass **: Kohat Pass is a challenging hike that takes you through rugged terrain and mountains, offering stunning views of the district’s landscape.

  • Hangu-Samana Road: Hangu-Samana Road is a scenic drive that takes you through the mountains and valleys of the district, offering spectacular views of the surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Hangu: Activities and Tours

  • City Tour: Take a guided tour of Hangu’s historical landmarks, museums, and scenic spots, and get an insight into the district’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Hiking and Biking Trails: Hangu has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking vistas of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Boating and Fishing: Orakzai Lake is a popular spot for boating and fishing, with various fish species available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hangu

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Hangu, check-in at the hotel, and explore the local markets and shops.
Day 2 Visit Kohat Museum and Hangu Fort in the morning. In the afternoon, embark on the Hangu Mountain Trail.
Day 3 Take a scenic drive through Kohat Tunnel, visit Bab-e-Khyber, and have lunch at a local restaurant.
Day 4 Spend the day at Orakzai Lake, boating, and fishing, and have a picnic by the lakeside.
Day 5 Visit Karrak Lake, and explore the nearby meadows and valleys.

Food and Drink Scene of Hangu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karahi: Karahi is a popular Pakistani dish made with meat or vegetables, cooked in a cast-iron wok with tomatoes, onions, and aromatic spices.

  • Kabuli Pulao: Kabuli Pulao is a traditional Afghan dish made with basmati rice, lamb, and a blend of spices, topped with raisins and nuts.

  • Kabobs: Hangu is famous for its succulent and juicy kebabs, made with ground beef, lamb, or chicken, marinated in a blend of spices, and barbecued over charcoal.

Local Beverages

  • Kahwa: Kahwa is a traditional green tea infused with almonds, saffron, and cardamom, served with sugar or honey.

  • Sharbat: Sharbat is a refreshing drink made with fruits, such as pomegranate, grape, or apricot, mixed with sugar syrup and served chilled.

Where to Stay in Hangu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Al-Mustafa Guest House: Al-Mustafa Guest House is conveniently located near the city center and offers budget-friendly accommodation with clean and spacious rooms.

  • Shah Hotel: Shah Hotel is a mid-range hotel located near Hangu Fort, offering modern amenities and excellent service.

  • Chinar Guest House: Chinar Guest House is a traditional guest house, offering comfortable rooms and a peaceful atmosphere, surrounded by lush greenery.

What to Buy in Hangu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carpets and Rugs: Hangu is renowned for its hand-woven woolen carpets and rugs, made by local artisans using traditional techniques.

  • Peshawari Chappals: Peshawari Chappals are traditional Pakistani footwear made with leather and unique designs, perfect for casual wear.

  • Silver Jewelry: Hangu’s silver jewelry is famous for its intricate designs and high-quality craftsmanship, making it an ideal souvenir or gift for loved ones back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hangu

Hangu has a rich cultural heritage, with a fusion of Pashtun and Afghan traditions, reflected in their language, dress, and customs. The locals have a warm and hospitable nature, and visitors are welcomed with open arms. Islam is the dominant religion, and the district’s religious landmarks and festivals are an integral part of Hangu’s cultural identity.

Best Time to Visit Hangu

The best time to visit Hangu is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Summers can be scorching, with temperatures soaring above 40°C, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hangu

Do:

  • Dress modestly, and cover your head and arms when visiting religious sites.

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as greeting elders, removing your shoes before entering a mosque, and avoiding public displays of affection.

  • Bargain when shopping in local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Photograph locals without their permission.

  • Eat or drink in public during the holy month of Ramadan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hangu

Hangu is a safe district for tourists, with a relatively low crime rate. However, it’s best to avoid traveling after dark and to take precautions when carrying valuables.

Health in Hangu

Consult your doctor before traveling to Hangu and ensure that your vaccinations are up-to-date. Avoid eating street food and drinking tap water, and carry a first-aid kit and necessary medication.

Getting to Hangu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hangu

  • Islamabad International Airport: Islamabad International Airport is the nearest international airport, located about 190 kilometers from Hangu. It has domestic and international flights.

  • Peshawar International Airport: Peshawar International Airport is also an option, located about 150 kilometers from Hangu, with domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Hangu

  • Daewoo Express: Daewoo Express has services from major cities to Hangu at affordable rates, with a comfortable journey and air-conditioned buses.

  • Faisal Movers: Faisal Movers has services from Lahore and Karachi to Hangu, with experienced drivers and air-conditioned buses.

Other Options

  • Private taxis and cars are available for rent.

  • Traveling by train is also an option, with the nearest train station in Peshawar.

How to Get Around in Hangu

The best way to get around in Hangu is by hiring a local taxi or car, which is relatively cheap and convenient. Motorcycle and bicycle rentals are also available for adventurous travelers, who want to explore the district at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Hangu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Peshawar: Peshawar is the capital city of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, located about 150 kilometers from Hangu. It’s famous for its rich cultural heritage and bazaars, offering a glimpse into the traditional way of living and local cuisine.

  • Islamabad: Islamabad is Pakistan’s capital city, located about 190 kilometers from Hangu. It’s a modern city with green spaces, museums, and monuments, representing the country’s diverse culture and history.
