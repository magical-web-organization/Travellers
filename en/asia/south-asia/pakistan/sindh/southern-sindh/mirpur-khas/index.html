Mirpur Khas is a lesser-known gem situated in Southern Sindh, Pakistan. This beautiful city is a perfect fusion of history, culture, and modernity, with rich heritage and exquisite natural scenery. Whether you are a history enthusiast, culture lover, nature seeker, or adventure enthusiast, Mirpur Khas has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have covered everything you need to know before planning your trip to this enchanting city.

Introduction to Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas is a city located in the southeastern region of Sindh province in Pakistan. It is a historical city known for its rich cultural heritage, historic monuments, and scenic beauty. The city has a population of around 238,000 people and covers an area of approximately 3,000 square miles.

List of Places to Visit in Mirpur Khas

If you’re planning a trip to Mirpur Khas, there are plenty of places worth visiting, from museums and historical places to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of some of our favorite places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mohenjo-daro: This ancient city, located about 4 hours from Mirpur Khas, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, considered one of the world’s most important archaeological sites.
  • Ranikot Fort: Located about 2 hours from Mirpur Khas, it’s a massive fortification system with a circumference of over 26 km, making it the largest fort in the world.
  • The Mirpurkhas Museum: The museum houses a collection of archaeological artifacts, including pottery, coins, manuscripts, and ancient sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Mirpur Khas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gorakh Hill Station: Located in the Khairpur district of Sindh, it’s the highest point in the province, offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys and hills.
  • Lake Keenjhar: Also called Kalri Lake, it’s the largest freshwater lake in Pakistan, located close to Thatta.
  • Umarkot Hill Station: Located in the Tharparkar district, approximately 260 km north-east of Karachi, it’s a popular tourist destination for its desert hills, sand dunes, and lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas offers some of the best hiking and biking trails for adventure enthusiasts. The following are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Sukkur to Rohri Canal: This route runs along the Rohri Canal, taking you past breathtaking scenery and peaceful towns.
  • Umarkot to Nagarparkar: This route starts from the Umarkot Hill Station and goes through the scenic desert hills and sand dunes towards Nagarparkar.

Best Things to Do in Mirpur Khas: Activities and Tours

Mirpur Khas has a lot to offer for visitors, from cultural experiences to thrilling adventures. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit historical monuments: Explore the cultural heritage of Mirpur Khas with visits to local monuments, including the Mohenjo-Daro ruins and the Ranikot Fort.
  • Experience nature: Take a trip to Lake Keenjhar to experience the beauty of the freshwater lake, surrounded by lush green hills and panoramic views.
  • Try local cuisine: Sample the delicious local cuisine, including biryani, dal chawal, and halwa puri. The famous sweet dish of the region is ‘Sohan Halwa,’ a dessert made from milk, sugar, and flour.
  • Shop for souvenirs: Head to the local markets for traditional handicrafts, pottery, and textiles, perfect for taking home as mementos of your trip.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas offers a lot to see and do, so it’s best to plan your trip accordingly. Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Arrive in Mirpur Khas and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit the Mohenjo-Daro ruins. Day 3: Take a trip to the Ranikot Fort. Day 4: Explore the local markets and try traditional cuisine. Day 5: Head to Lake Keenjhar for a day trip. Day 6: Take a trip to Umarkot Hill Station for outdoor adventures. Day 7: Relax and unwind at Gorakh Hill Station.

Food and Drink Scene of Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas has a diverse culinary scene, with options ranging from traditional Pakistani cuisine to international delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: A popular dish made with rice and spiced meat, vegetables, or eggs.
  • Dal Chawal: Rice and lentil soup, a staple dish in Pakistani cuisine.
  • Sohan Halwa: A traditional dessert made with milk, sugar, and flour.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing drink made from yogurt, water, and sugar or salt.
  • Sharbat: A sweet, fruity drink made with rose syrup and water, perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Mirpur Khas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pearl Continental Hotel Mirpur Khas: A luxury hotel with excellent amenities and services.

What to Buy in Mirpur Khas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mirpur Khas offers many unique and traditional souvenirs and products that make for excellent gifts or mementos of your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Handicrafts: Traditional handicrafts, including pottery, woodwork, and textiles, make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Ajrak Fabric: Ajrak is a traditional block-printed cloth, locally made in Sindh, made from natural dyes and worn as clothing or a decorative item.
  • Jewellery: The local markets offer a range of exquisite jewelry, including silver and metalwork and beaded and stone jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas is a city rich in cultural heritage and customs. The local people are friendly and hospitable, making visitors feel welcome. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Islamic traditions: The majority of the population is Muslim, so it’s important to respect Islamic customs and attire.
  • Hospitality: The people of Mirpur Khas are known for their hospitality and kindness towards visitors.
  • Family values: Family values are very important to the people of Mirpur Khas, and visitors should always show respect to family members and elders.

Best Time to Visit Mirpur Khas

The best time to visit Mirpur Khas is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, with temperatures reaching up to 45°C (113°F).

Do’s and Don’ts of Mirpur Khas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mirpur Khas:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially at mosques and other holy places.
  • Try local cuisine and indulge in traditional activities.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol, as it is forbidden in Islam.
  • Criticize Islamic traditions or customs.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Just like any other place, it is important to take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and healthy trip to Mirpur Khas.

Safety in Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas is a safe city for visitors, but it’s always important to take care and be aware of your surroundings. Some tips for staying safe include:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.
  • Only use licensed taxis and transportation.

Health in Mirpur Khas

The local hospitals and medical facilities in Mirpur Khas are reliable, but it’s always advisable to take necessary health precautions, such as:

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Take any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Mirpur Khas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mirpur Khas is easily accessible by road, rail, and air. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Mirpur Khas

  • Jinnah International Airport, Karachi: Located 215 km away from Mirpur Khas, it is the nearest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Mirpur Khas

  • Daewoo Bus Terminal Mirpurkhas: This bus terminal is located in the city center and connects to various parts of the country.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available in major cities and airports.
  • Taxis: Licensed taxis are a popular and safe mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Mirpur Khas

Mirpur Khas is relatively small and easy to navigate, with a local bus system and taxis easily available. It’s also a walking-friendly city, with many attractions located within walking distance of the city center.

Where to Go Next from Mirpur Khas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mirpur Khas is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and attractions. Some popular places to visit nearby include:

  • Thatta: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its historic monuments, architecture, and museums.
  • Kirthar National Park: Located about 2 hours from Mirpur Khas, the park is a wildlife sanctuary, home to a variety of species of animals and birds.
  • Hyderabad: A historic city, located about 2 hours from Mirpur Khas, known for its cultural heritage and civic infrastructure.

Conclusion

Mirpur Khas is an enchanting and lesser-known city with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring history and culture or seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views, Mirpur Khas has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to plan an unforgettable trip to Mirpur Khas, Pakistan.
