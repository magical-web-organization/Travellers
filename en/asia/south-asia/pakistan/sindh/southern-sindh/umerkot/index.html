A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Umerkot, Pakistan

Umerkot is located in Southern Sindh, Pakistan, and is distinguished for having historical significance. It is the town where the founder of the Mughal Empire, Emperor Akbar, was born in 1542. Aside from its rich cultural history, Umerkot has plenty of natural beauty to offer. In this travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Umerkot.

List of Places to Visit in Umerkot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Umerkot Museum

The Umerkot Museum is one of the top cultural attractions in the city. It was built in 1975 and houses exhibits showcasing the region’s cultural and historical heritage. Inside, you can find artifacts from the Indus Valley Civilization, ancient coins of Islamic and Hindu origin, sculptures, and illustrations from the Mughal era.

Akbar’s Birthplace

Akbar’s Birthplace is an essential destination for tourists and history lovers. This palace is where the Mughal Emperor was born and is situated in the center of the city. The palace’s grounds are vast, and the architecture is impressive.

Fort of Umerkot

The Fort of Umerkot, also known as Umarkot Fort, was built by the royal family of the Sodha Rajput clan in the early 17th century. The fort offers a glimpse into the grandeur of the Mughal era’s architecture, incorporating Islamic geometric patterns and local materials.

Natural Attractions of Umerkot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Churrio Jabal

The Churrio Jabal is a ridge of barren hills extending from the east bank of the River Gambhir in the north to the River Kunari in the south. The ridges are sparsely inhabited and are home to rare species of birds, including eagles, buzzards, and magpies. The Churrio Jabal is ideal for hiking, photography, and bird-watching.

Thar Desert

The Thar Desert extends from Umerkot to India’s Rajasthan state and is home to exotic flora and fauna. Tourists can kick-off a desert safari in a 4x4 jeep, providing plenty of adventure backed up by the locals’ hospitality. Camping in the desert and participating in the local culture is an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umerkot

Umerkot has various hiking and biking routes for adventure seekers. The Churrio Jabal, as mentioned above, is ideal for hiking enthusiasts. For mountain bikers, the best experience is gained by visiting the Sadh Belo Temple, which is located on the Tando Bago Road Biswak, approximately 10 km from Umarkot.

Best Things to Do in Umerkot: Activities and Tours

Mela Chiraghan

Mela Chiraghan is a festival of light celebrated by Hindus and Muslims to worship Saint Shah Hussain. The festival is a celebration of light and life, and people take part in various events, including live music, cultural performances, and eating delicious food. The festival is held in the month of March every year.

Faqir Jo Goth Tour

A trip to Faqir Jo Goth, a historic site and village, gives visitors an experience of rural life and their customs. The village is renowned for its handmade pottery, which the skilled artists make using traditional methods. Visitors can also see the locals’ daily lifestyle, including farming fields, traditional houses, and local markets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Umerkot

Here’s a week-long itinerary covering the must-see attractions of Umerkot:

  • Day 1: Visit the Fort of Umerkot early in the morning and explore the palace grounds.
  • Day 2: Visit Churrio Jabal and go hiking, bird-watching, and photography.
  • Day 3: Explore the Chachro Town and its ancient temples, located 70 km from Umerkot.
  • Day 4: Take a desert safari and camp in the Thar Desert.
  • Day 5: Visit the Umerkot Museum and learn about the city’s history and cultural heritage.
  • Day 6: Go on a Faqir Jo Goth tour and experience village life.
  • Day 7: Attend Mela Chiraghan, the festival of lights, and participate in food, music and traditional cultural events.

Food and Drink Scene of Umerkot

Must-Try Dishes

Umerkot’s cuisine has a distinct flavor, incorporating influences from the Mughal era and the surrounding areas. The city’s most popular dishes include:

  • Biryani: a flavorful rice-based dish served with vegetables or meat.
  • Sai Bhaaji: a traditional green vegetable stew made with spinach and mustard greens.
  • Koki: a flatbread baked on a traditional clay oven, served with raita or chutney.
  • Kheer: a sweet dessert made from boiled rice, milk, and sugar.

Local Beverages

Sattu, a drink made from roasted barley flour, provides a healthy drink, replenishing your body with energy and flavor. Kairi Panna, a refreshing summer drink made from raw green mangoes, is popular for its unique flavor and health benefits.

What to Buy in Umerkot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Umerkot has a rich heritage of handicrafts, including pottery, embroideries, and weaving. The city’s most notable souvenirs are handcrafted pottery and ceramic items, including tea sets and vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Umerkot

Umerkot is a traditional city, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. Dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection. Additionally, female travelers should cover their heads when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Umerkot

The best time to visit Umerkot is from October to March, when the temperatures remain mild and pleasant. Visitors are advised to avoid the summer months, where temperatures can rise to as high as 45°C, making it hard to travel and explore the city’s tourist spots.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umerkot

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Cover your head and dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Keep yourself hydrated during your stay in Umerkot.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection.
  • Insult the locals or their culture.
  • Wear revealing clothes, especially when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Umerkot

Umerkot is a safe city with friendly locals. However, visitors should keep their valuables safe and secure, especially in crowded places such as markets and festivals.

Health in Umerkot

Visitors are reminded to drink only bottled water to avoid waterborne diseases. Also, carry insect repellent to protect yourself from mosquito bites that can carry diseases like Dengue fever.

Getting to Umerkot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Umerkot

The nearest airports to Umerkot are in Karachi and Hyderabad. Visitors can either take a chartered flight or drive down to Umerkot.

Bus Stations Near Umerkot

Umerkot is well-connected to the other major cities of Sindh, including Karachi, Hyderabad, and Sukkur. The Pakistan Tourism Development Corporation operates regular bus services from Karachi and Hyderabad.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent taxis, cars, and motorbikes from Hyderabad and Karachi to reach Umerkot.

How to Get Around in Umerkot

Umerkot has a well-connected network of buses, taxis, and rickshaws for getting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Umerkot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hyderabad: Located 280 km from Umerkot, Hyderabad is famous for its historical landmarks, bazaars, and cuisine.
  • Karachi: Karachi is a vibrant and diverse city, located 400 km from Umerkot, and offers several tourist attractions and a variety of cuisines.
  • Tharparkar: Tharparkar is a desert area and is located approximately 200 km from Umerkot. Tourists can explore the unique wildlife and visit the ancient temples in Chachro and surrounding places.

Conclusion

Umerkot is a place to experience Pakistan’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, local cuisine, and customs, there is something for everyone in Umerkot. To make the most of your trip, plan a week-long itinerary and try to experience as much of the city as possible.
