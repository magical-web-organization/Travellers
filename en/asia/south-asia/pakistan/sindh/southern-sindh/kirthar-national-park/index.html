Introduction to Kirthar National Park

Located in Southern Sindh, Pakistan, Kirthar National Park is a protected area spanning across 3,087 square kilometers. Kirthar National Park is a perfect place for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and history lovers. Established in 1974, the park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and is rich in cultural and natural sites to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Kirthar National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, then Kirthar National Park has plenty of places to explore. The park contains several museums and historical sites that are culturally significant.

  1. Sardar Faiz Muhammad Khan Mari’s Tomb: It is the resting place of the famous Mari tribal leader, Sardar Faiz Muhammad Khan. It is located a few kilometers away from the Kirthar Mountains.

  2. Gorakh Hills and Princess of Hope: The Gorakh Hills are a popular tourist spot and offer some of the most stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The Princess of Hope is a natural rock formation near the Gorakh Hills and is a famous landmark of the area.

  3. Manjhand Fort: Built-in the 17th century, Manjhand Fort is a must-visit site for history enthusiasts. The fort is located on the banks of the Indus River and offers scenic views of the surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Kirthar National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kirthar National Park is a place of natural beauty that boasts a range of scenic locations and outdoor activities to enjoy. It’s a perfect place for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

  1. Kirthar Range: The park is named after the Kirthar Range, a mountain range located within the park’s boundaries. The Kirthar Range is also the highest peak in the park, offering breathtaking views from the top.

  2. Lal Suhanra National Park: Lal Suhanra is a stunning national park located near the Kirthar National Park. Visitors can enjoy various outdoor activities such as bird watching, hiking, and camping.

  3. Wildlife Safari: Kirthar National Park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can take a guided wildlife safari to witness the park’s wildlife, including Sindh Ibex, Urial, and Chinkara.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirthar National Park

Kirthar National Park offers several hiking and biking routes that take you through some of the park’s most exotic locations.

  1. Kirthar Loop Trail: It is a 60-kilometer-long trail that circles the Kirthar Mountains. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is perfect for experienced hikers.

  2. Kot Diji Fort to Hingol National Park Trail: This 45-kilometer trail takes you through some of the park’s most beautiful locations, including the Kot Diji Fort and Hingol National Park.

Best Things to Do in Kirthar National Park: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do and must-visit places in Kirthar National Park:

  1. Safari Tour: Enjoy a guided safari tour to witness the park’s wildlife and explore its natural beauty.

  2. Bird Watching: Kirthar National Park is a paradise for bird watchers. Some of the most exotic species of birds can be found here.

  3. Hiking and Biking: Kirthar National Park offers fantastic hiking and biking trails for adventure seekers.

Food and Drink Scene of Kirthar National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Kirthar National Park’s food scene is heavily influenced by Sindhi cuisine, which is rich and flavorful. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  1. Sindhi Biryani: A popular dish of the region, Sindhi Biryani is a savory and spicy rice dish with meat or vegetables.

  2. Sai Bhaji: A vegetable stew made with spinach and meat or lentils.

  3. Lolo: A sweet and savory bread made with cornflour and served with a side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

Sindh is renowned for its refreshing and energizing drinks. Some of the must-try local beverages in Kirthar National Park include:

  1. Shikwaas: A light, refreshing drink made with lemon and herbs.

  2. Sattu: A protein-rich drink made with roasted chickpeas.

Where to Stay in Kirthar National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kirthar National Park has several options for stay, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Gorakh Hill Resort: A luxurious resort located near the Gorakh Hills that offers stunning views and a range of amenities.

  2. Shahbaaz Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located near the Kirthar Mountains that offers comfortable rooms and excellent food.

What to Buy in Kirthar National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kirthar National Park is a place of vibrant culture and exquisite craftsmanship. Here are some of the souvenirs and local products you must bring home:

  1. Sindhi Ajrak: A colorful and intricate fabric that is the hallmark of Sindhi culture.

  2. Sindhi Topi: A traditional cap adorned with intricate embroidery and colorful threads.

Local Culture and Customs of Kirthar National Park

Sindhi culture is deeply rooted in tradition and hospitality. The people of Kirthar National Park are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Some of the customs to keep in mind while visiting Kirthar National Park:

  1. Punctuality: Being on time is an important value in Sindhi culture.

  2. Respect: Show respect to locals and their customs while exploring Kirthar National Park.

Best Time to Visit Kirthar National Park

The best time to visit Kirthar National Park is during the winter months between November to February. The weather during this time is pleasant, and the wildlife is active, making it an excellent time for safari tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kirthar National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the Wildlife: Keep a safe distance while exploring Kirthar National Park to avoid disturbing the wildlife.

  2. Carry Drinking Water: Carry enough drinking water as Kirthar National Park is prone to droughts.

Don’t:

  1. Litter: Keep the park clean and do not litter.

  2. Overstay: Do not overstay in the park or disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kirthar National Park

Kirthar National Park is generally safe for visitors. However, keep in mind the following tips for a safe trip:

  1. Travel with a Guide: Always travel with a guide while exploring Kirthar National Park.

  2. Stay Alert: Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Kirthar National Park

Carry basic medications and first-aid kits while exploring Kirthar National Park. The park is prone to droughts, so carry enough drinking water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Kirthar National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kirthar National Park

Jinnah International Airport in Karachi is the nearest airport to Kirthar National Park, which is around 235 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kirthar National Park

The Sukkur and Larkana bus station are the nearest bus stations to Kirthar National Park.

Other Options

Private taxis and cars are also available for hire to reach Kirthar National Park.

How to Get Around in Kirthar National Park

Exploring Kirthar National Park on foot is one of the best ways to enjoy its natural beauty. However, visitors can also hire a jeep or travel by car or bus to explore the park.

Where to Go Next from Kirthar National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
