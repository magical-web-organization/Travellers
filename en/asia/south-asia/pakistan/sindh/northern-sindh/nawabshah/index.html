Introduction to Nawabshah

Nawabshah is a city located in the Northern part of Sindh in Pakistan. The city is known for its agricultural significance and is home to many cotton ginners and the world-famous National Agriculture Research Center (NARC). The people of Nawabshah are known for their traditional Sindhi culture and hospitality. The best time to visit Nawabshah is during the winter season, from November to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant.

List of Places to Visit in Nawabshah

If you’re visiting Nawabshah, here are some must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Gharib Shah Mausoleum: The mausoleum of Gharib Shah is located in the heart of the city and is a must-visit for those interested in the history and culture of the region.
  • Shaheed Benazir Bhutto Park: Named after the former Prime Minister of Pakistan, this park is a beautiful spot to relax and enjoy the city’s greenery.
  • The Darbar of Sachal Sarmast: A famous sufi saint from Sindh, Sachal Sarmast’s shrine is located in the city of Daraza, just 13km from Nawabshah.

Natural Attractions of Nawabshah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Shahi Bazaar: Located in the old city, the Shahi Bazaar is a vibrant street showcasing the city’s traditional Sindhi crafts, spices, and clothing stores. It’s a great place to immerse yourself in Nawabshah’s local culture.
  • The Lansdowne Bridge: A beautiful bridge over the Indus river, which was built in 1889 and is still in use today.
  • The Sanghar Hills: Located just 35km from Nawabshah, the Sanghar Hills are ideal for those who enjoy trekking and outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nawabshah

Nawabshah is a paradise for nature enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are our top picks:

  • The Tando Adam Hike: The Tando Adam Hike is a challenging hike that takes you through some of the most scenic hills and landscapes of Nawabshah. The hike is a 20km roundtrip and can be completed in approximately 6 hours.
  • The Nawabshah Biking Trail: The Nawabshah Biking Trail is a 20km route that takes you through scenic routes of the city, including the historic landmarks, parks, and beautiful countryside. The biking trail can be completed in approximately 3 hours.

Best Things to Do in Nawabshah: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in Nawabshah:

  • Cultural Tour: Experience the rich cultural heritage of the city by taking a tour of its historical landmarks, museums, and local markets.
  • Agriculture Tour: As an important agricultural hub, Nawabshah offers a unique tour of its flourishing agricultural sector, including visiting cotton fields and touring the National Agriculture Research Center.
  • Sindhi Handicraft Tour: Discover the intricate Sindhi handicrafts, from embroidery and weaving to leatherwork and pottery, by taking a tour of the city’s local markets and workshops.

Food and Drink Scene of Nawabshah

Nawabshah offers a diverse culinary experience, with traditional Sindhi dishes and North Indian cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sindhi Biryani: A spicy and aromatic rice dish made with chicken or beef and mixed with herbs, spices, and potatoes.
  • Saag: A popular Sindhi dish made using spinach and served with rice or bread.
  • Kebabs: Delicious meat skewers made with marinated chicken, beef, or lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A creamy and refreshing drink made with yogurt, milk, and sugar.
  • Sugarcane Juice: A popular drink in Nawabshah made with fresh sugarcane juice and served with a touch of lemon.

  • Hotel Grand Gardenia: A charming hotel with beautiful gardens and comfortable rooms, Hotel Grand Gardenia is a great stay option in Nawabshah.

What to Buy in Nawabshah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Nawabshah home with you by shopping for unique local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to shop for:

  • Sindhi Ajrak: A traditional shawl worn in Sindh, known for its beautiful colors and intricate designs.
  • Tribal Jewelry: Intricate and colorful jewelry pieces made by local artisans using silver, beads, and natural stones.
  • Khaadi Clothes: Handwoven and embroidered clothing made from cotton and silk, often featuring traditional Sindhi designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Nawabshah

Nawabshah is known for its rich cultural heritage, hospitality, and traditional Sindhi culture. It’s important to respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural landmarks. The people of Nawabshah are friendly and welcoming, so don’t hesitate to ask for assistance or directions from locals.

Best Time to Visit Nawabshah

The best time to visit Nawabshah is during the winter season, from November to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summers can be hot and humid, making it less favorable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nawabshah

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural landmarks.
  • Try the traditional Sindhi cuisine and local beverages.
  • Engage with the friendly and welcoming locals.

Don’t:

  • Visit sacred places without permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting religious landmarks.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nawabshah

Nawabshah is a safe city for tourists with low crime rates. It’s important to practice general safety measures such as avoiding walking alone at night in isolated areas and keeping your belongings safe.

Health in Nawabshah

Travelers to Nawabshah should seek medical advice before their trip, especially if they plan for an extended stay. It’s important to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen and protect yourself from mosquito bites.

Getting to Nawabshah: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Nawabshah is the Nawabshah Airport, which serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Nawabshah

There are various bus stations in the city, with regular bus services operating from Nawabshah to other parts of Sindh and Pakistan.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are widely available for traveling within the city and to other destinations in the region.

How to Get Around in Nawabshah

In Nawabshah, you can explore the major attractions on foot or by taking a taxi or rented car. Public transport, including buses and rickshaws, is also available for commuting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Nawabshah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nawabshah is ideally located for exploring other parts of Sindh and Pakistan. Here are some recommended destinations for a day trip or an extended stay:

  • Hyderabad: A city known for its rich Mughal history, traditional bazaars, and stunning landmarks.
  • Karachi: The largest city in Pakistan, offering a mix of modern and traditional cultures, delicious food, and beautiful beaches.
  • Hingol National Park: This national park is located in southwestern Balochistan and is home to diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, and ancient cave paintings.
