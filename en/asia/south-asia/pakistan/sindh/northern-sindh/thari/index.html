Introduction to Thari

Thari is a small town located in Northern Sindh, Pakistan. It is famous for its rich cultural heritage, archaeological sites, natural beauty, and delicious food. If you’re looking for an off-beat destination to explore and seek adventure, Thari is the perfect place to be.

List of Places to Visit in Thari

Thari is a treasure trove of cultural and natural destinations that every traveler should explore. Here are the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Moen Jo Daro Archaeological Site: Moen Jo Daro is one of the most significant archaeological sites in the world, featuring the ruins of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization.
  2. Bhodesar Mosque: This 18th-century mosque is a fine example of the ancient Mughal architecture.
  3. Ranikot Fort: This massive fortification was built in the 17th century and is the largest fort in the world.

Natural Attractions of Thari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Gorakh Hill: The highest point in Sindh offers incredible views of the mountainous landscape and is an ideal hiking spot.
  2. Kirthar National Park: A vast wildlife park featuring diverse flora and fauna, including the Himalayan ibex and the Chinkara deer.
  3. Tando Allahyar Lake: A stunning man-made lake surrounded by lush greenery and natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thari

Thari is a paradise for adventure enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking routes offering breathtaking views of natural landscapes. Here are a few popular options:

  1. Gorakh Hill Trek: A challenging trek leading to the Gorakh Hill summit, offering stunning views of the surrounding area.
  2. Keenjhar Lake Cycle Tour: A peaceful biking tour around Keenjhar Lake, the largest freshwater lake in Pakistan.

Best Things to Do in Thari: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the natural and cultural landmarks, there are various other activities and tours that travelers can enjoy in Thari.

  1. Sindhi Cultural Tour: Experience the unique blend of Sindhi culture by visiting local markets, bazaars and tasting some incredible food.
  2. Nomad Camping Tour: Experience life as a nomad and camp under the stars in the Kirthar Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thari

Here’s a perfect week-long itinerary for exploring Thari:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Moen Jo Daro Archaeological site
2 Visit the Ranikot Fort in the morning, and explore the natural beauty of Kirthar National Park in the afternoon
3 Take the Gorakh Hill trek and camp overnight
4 Explore the Gorakh Hill summit and watch the sunrise from the top
5 Take a cycle tour around Keenjhar Lake
6 Experience the Sindhi culture by visiting local markets and bazaars
7 Relax at Tando Allahyar Lake and enjoy the serene natural beauty

Food and Drink Scene of Thari

Sindhi cuisine is known for its unique blend of spices and flavors. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sai Bhaji: A delectable mix of spinach, lentils, and other vegetables, served with rice or roti.
  2. Biryani: A flavorful rice dish cooked with meat, spices, and vegetables.
  3. Dabal Roti: A meat-filled pastry, considered a local delicacy.

Local Beverages

  1. Sattu: A healthy and refreshing drink made from roasted gram flour.
  2. Thadal: A traditional milk-based drink flavored with almonds, cardamom, and saffron.

Where to Stay in Thari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options available for accommodation in Thari, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

  1. Gorakh Hill Resort: A comfortable and cozy resort located at the top of Gorakh Hill.
  2. Indus Lodge: A luxurious hotel located near Moen Jo Daro.
  3. Al-Musawir Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Thari town.

What to Buy in Thari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thari is famous for its handcrafted pottery, embroidery, and jewelry. Here are a few souvenirs and local products that travelers can bring home:

  1. Thari Handicrafts: Locally-made pottery, embroidery, and rugs.
  2. Sindh Ajrak: A traditional block-printed cloth, considered a symbol of Sindhi culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Thari

Thari is a unique blend of Hindu and Muslim cultures, and travelers should respect the local customs and traditions. The local language is Sindhi, but most people understand Urdu and English.

Best Time to Visit Thari

The best time to visit Thari is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant, and the natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thari

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress modestly while visiting religious sites and cultural landmarks.
  3. Tread lightly and responsibly while hiking or camping.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  2. Wear revealing clothes in public.
  3. Litter or damage natural or cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thari

Thari is a relatively safe place to visit, but travelers should still exercise caution while traveling. Ensure that your valuables are secure and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Thari

Travelers should drink bottled water and avoid street food that may not be hygienic. It’s advisable to carry a first-aid kit and insect repellent when exploring the natural landscapes.

Getting to Thari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thari is well-connected by road, and travelers can use taxis and buses to reach Thari from nearby cities.

Airports Near Thari

  1. Jinnah International Airport, Karachi (360 km)
  2. Multan International Airport, Multan (250 km)

Bus Stations Near Thari

  1. Sukkur Bus Stand (130 km)
  2. Larkana Bus Stand (200 km)

Other Options

  1. Private Cars: Travelers can rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Thari.
  2. Trains: The nearest railway station is in Dadu (120 km), and travelers can take a train to reach there.

How to Get Around in Thari

Travelers can use taxis or rent a car to explore Thari. Bikes are also available for rent for those looking for a more adventurous option.

Where to Go Next from Thari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some more days to explore, here are a few destinations you can visit:

  1. Larkana: A nearby city known for its rich historical and cultural heritage.
  2. Sehwan Sharif: A small town famous for its Sufi shrines and spiritual heritage.

Conclusion

Thari is a hidden gem waiting to be explored, offering a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure. With this travel guide, you can now plan the perfect trip to discover the beauty of Northern Sindh, Pakistan.
