Introduction to Sukkur

Sukkur, a city located in the northern part of Sindh, Pakistan, is one of the most under-the-radar travel destinations in South Asia. Sitting on the west bank of River Indus, Sukkur is surrounded by lush green fields and is home to several beautiful architectural wonders. The city’s rich cultural history is reflected in its temples, shrines, and historical monuments, making it a picturesque place for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Sukkur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sukkur Barrage: A popular tourist spot, Sukkur Barrage is a large dam that was built on River Indus. Visitors can enjoy a boat ride or explore the dam’s vast expanse.
  • Lansdowne Bridge: Built-in 1887, Lansdowne Bridge is a beautiful engineering marvel that was instrumental in connecting Karachi to Punjab. The bridge, considered a historical monument, is a popular attraction for tourists and locals alike.
  • Masum Shah Jo Minaro: A stunning white tower located atop a hill, Masum Shah Jo Minaro, is a functional watchtower in Sukkur.
  • Sukkur City Heritage Museum: Located in the heart of the city, the Sukkur City Heritage Museum features a collection of artifacts that depict the cultural significance of Sindh.

Natural Attractions of Sukkur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sadh Belo: A beautiful and peaceful island located in the middle of River Indus, Sadh Belo is a popular spot for picnics and outdoor activities.
  • Jhimpir Forest: Named after a nearby village, Jhimpir Forest is a lush green area that is home to several species of birds and animals. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, and camping in the forest.
  • Rohri Hills: Located on the outskirts of Sukkur, Rohri Hills is a beautiful hilly area that is perfect for trekking and enjoying scenic views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sukkur

  • Jhimpir to Sadh Belo: This route is perfect for both hiking and biking. The 12 KM trail runs through the Jhimpir forest and ends at Sadh Belo, offering stunning views of the Indus river along the way.
  • Sadh Belo to Bukkur Island: This 14 KM route runs along the banks of the Indus river and takes visitors to Bukkur Island, another beautiful island located just a few miles away from Sadh Belo.

Best Things to Do in Sukkur: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Ride in Sukkur Barrage: Visitors can go on a boat ride in the Sukkur Barrage and enjoy beautiful views of the Indus river and the surrounding landscape.
  • Explore Sadh Belo Island: Sadh Belo Island is a perfect place for picnics, fishing, and swimming in the river. Visitors can explore the island on foot or take a horse cart ride.
  • Take a Tour of the City: Visitors can take a guided tour of the city to explore its rich culture and history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sukkur

Day 1: Arrive in Sukkur and explore the Sukkur City Heritage Museum, Masum Shah Jo Minaro, and Lansdowne Bridge.

Day 2: Visit Sukkur Barrage and enjoy a boat ride in the Indus river.

Day 3: Visit Sadh Belo Island and take part in outdoor activities like swimming, fishing, and hiking.

Day 4: Take a tour of the City and explore the local markets and bazaars.

Day 5: Explore Jhimpir Forest and take part in outdoor activities like hiking, bird watching, and camping.

Day 6: Trek to Rohri Hills and enjoy scenic views of the city.

Day 7: Depart from Sukkur.

Food and Drink Scene of Sukkur

Sukkur has a rich food culture that is deeply tied to its history and geography. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sindhi Biryani: Sindhi Biryani is one of the most famous dishes in Sindh. It is a flavorful rice dish that is served with chicken, beef, or mutton.
  • Sajji: Sajji is a dish made from roasted lamb or chicken that is cooked with a special blend of spices.
  • Sindhi Saag: Sindhi Saag is a traditional Sindhi dish made with spinach and served with bread.

Local Beverages

  • Sharbat: Sharbat is a sweet drink made from fruits, flowers, or herbs.
  • Lassi: Lassi is a yogurt-based drink that is usually served with meals in Sindh.

Local Culture and Customs of Sukkur

Sukkur has a rich cultural history that is deeply tied to Sindh’s cultural heritage. The city is known for its hospitality, music, and art. Respect for elders and guests is a core value in Sindhi culture, and visitors are expected to show the same respect to local people.

Best Time to Visit Sukkur

The best time to visit Sukkur is during the winter months, between November to February when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sukkur

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Bargain when shopping in local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling or using a filter.
  • Take photographs of local women without their consent.
  • Eat food from unhygienic stalls or restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sukkur

Sukkur is generally a safe city for tourists, and incidents of violent crime are rare. Visitors are advised to take general precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night and not carrying large sums of money.

Health in Sukkur

Visitors to Sukkur are advised to drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent illnesses like diarrhea and food poisoning. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and typhoid before traveling to Sukkur.

Getting to Sukkur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sukkur is well-connected to the rest of Pakistan via air, rail, and road.

Airports Near Sukkur

  • Sukkur Airport: The nearest airport to Sukkur is Sukkur Airport, which is located around 5 KM from the city.

Bus Stations Near Sukkur

  • Sukkur Bus Terminal: Sukkur Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the city and is located near the Sukkur Railway Station.

Other Options

  • Railway: Sukkur has a railway station that connects it to other parts of the country, including Karachi, Islamabad, and Lahore.

How to Get Around in Sukkur

The best way to get around Sukkur is by hiring a taxi or taking a bus. Taxis and buses are readily available throughout the city and are a convenient and affordable mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Sukkur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Larkana: Located around 50 KM from Sukkur, Larkana is a historical city that is home to several archaeological sites, museums, and shrines.
  • Moan Jo Daro: Moan Jo Daro, located around 70 KM from Sukkur, is a UNESCO World Heritage site that features the ruins of one of the world’s oldest civilizations.
  • Khairpur: Khairpur, located around 90 KM from Sukkur, is a charming town known for its stunning palaces, gardens, and universities.

Conclusion

Sukkur is a hidden gem in the northern part of Sindh, Pakistan that is well worth a visit.
