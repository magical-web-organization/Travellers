Khuzdar district, located in Balochistan, Pakistan, is a place where people are greeted with a generous hospitality and breathtaking scenery. Historically, the district has been a gateway to many conquerors and traders traveling to and from the Arabian Peninsula to the Indian subcontinent. Today, it is a unique destination that offers diverse cultural and natural attractions. To help you explore the district’s wonders, we have created this comprehensive travel guide.

Introduction to Khuzdar District

Khuzdar district, with its capital city, Khuzdar, is one of the 34 districts of Balochistan province in Pakistan. The district is spread over an area of 36,260 square kilometers and is home to a population of over 1.5 million people. The district is bordered by Kalat in the North, Lasbela in the Southeast, Awaran in the Southwest, and Kech in the West. The district’s varied topography ranges from rugged mountains to fertile plains and hosts some of the most beautiful natural reserves.

List of Places to Visit in Khuzdar District

The district boasts a plethora of tourist attractions that cater to different interests. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or adventure seeker, there’s something here for everyone.

Culturally important attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Khuzdar district is rich in history and culture, and this is evident from the several museums and historical places that the district has to offer.

  1. Khuzdar Fort: Located in the center of the town, Khuzdar Fort is a must-visit place for those interested in history. The fort dates back to the pre-Islamic era and showcases the region’s original architecture, including turrets, gates, and chambers that once housed warriors and horses.
  2. Shrine of Hazrat Mir Ali Baba: This shrine is located in Wadh, around four hours’ drive from Khuzdar. It attracts visitors from all over Pakistan and abroad as it is believed to have healing powers.
  3. Museum of Balochistan: This museum is located in Quetta, two hours’ drive from Khuzdar, and showcases the heritage of Balochistan, including its ancient weapons, tools, and pottery.
  4. Buddhist Stupa of Taunsa Sharif: Located about 35 kilometers from Khuzdar, the Buddhist Stupa of Taunsa Sharif is a historical Buddhist monument.

Natural Attractions of Khuzdar District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khuzdar’s natural beauty is awe-inspiring and offers a range of outdoor activities and scenic views. Here are some of the best destinations for nature enthusiasts:

  1. Kund Malir Beach: Kund Malir Beach is one of the most picturesque beaches in Pakistan, located about 140 kilometers southwest of Khuzdar. The beach has clear blue water, a long sandy coastline, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Hingol National Park: Hingol National Park is Pakistan’s largest protected area, located about 190 kilometers east of Khuzdar. The park boasts diverse landscapes, ranging from rugged mountains to sandy beaches, and is home to several rare and endangered species of flora and fauna.
  3. Moola Chotok Waterfall: Moola Chotok Waterfall is a hidden natural gem located in the Toba Kakar mountain range, about 85 kilometers northeast of Khuzdar. The waterfall offers a refreshing swim in its clear blue waters amidst the scenic views of the mountains.
  4. Gor-e-Subz: Gor-e-Subz is a natural water spring located 30 kilometers from Khuzdar along the RCD highway. The waterfall offers a serene ambiance and a refreshing dip in its crystal clear water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khuzdar District

Khuzdar district offers scenic hiking and biking routes that showcase the natural beauty and serenity of the region. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  1. Quetta-Ziarat Highway: A scenic drive that offers a mesmerizing view of the valley and the surrounding mountains. This route offers a range of activities, including hiking, biking, and off-roading.
  2. Moola Chotok: This route offers a challenging hike through the Toba Kakar mountain range and a visit to the Moola Chotok waterfall.
  3. Kund Malir Beach to Ormara: This route offers a scenic bike ride along the Gulf of Oman and stunning views of the mountains and beaches.

Best Things to Do in Khuzdar District: Activities and Tours

Khuzdar district offers several fun-filled activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Khuzdar District:

  1. Camel Safari: Experience the Arabian Nights in Balochistan with a camel safari in the desert regions of Khuzdar.
  2. White Water Rafting: The Nara River is an ideal spot for white water rafting, located about three hours’ drive from Khuzdar.
  3. Fishing: With abundant freshwater streams and rivers, Khuzdar district is an ideal place for fishing enthusiasts.
  4. Bird Watching: Hingol National Park is home to several rare birds and offers an ideal spot for birdwatchers to catch up with them.

Week-Long Itinerary for Khuzdar District

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will help you make the most out of your visit to Khuzdar District:

Day 1: Visit Khuzdar Fort, Hazrat Mir Ali Baba Shrine, and Museum of Balochistan

Day 2: Visit Kund Malir Beach

Day 3: Visit Hingol National Park

Day 4: Hike to Moola Chotok Waterfall

Day 5: Camel Safari or Off-roading

Day 6: White water rafting or fishing

Day 7: Bird Watching in Hingol National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Khuzdar District

The local cuisine in Khuzdar district reflects its diverse cultural heritage. The district’s cuisine is a mix of Pakistani, Afghani, and Iranian influences. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Saji: Saji is a traditional dish made with lamb or goat meat, cooked in a pit of hot coals and served with creamy naan bread.
  2. Dumpukht: Dumpukht is a dish where meat is cooked on a very low flame and served with rice.
  3. Kabuli Pulao: Kabuli Pulao is a rice dish that has lamb, carrots, raisins, and onions cooked together and served with a side of yogurt sauce.
  4. Balochi Rosh: Balochi Rosh is a unique dish that includes lamb, tomatoes, and potatoes cooked in gravy and served with fluffy naan bread.

Local Beverages

  1. Lassi: Lassi is a popular yogurt-based drink that can be sweet or salty.
  2. Kahwah: Kahwah is a traditional tea made with green tea leaves, cardamom, and saffron.
  3. Sugarcane Juice: Sugarcane Juice is a refreshing drink that is locally made from the sugarcane plant.

Where to Stay in Khuzdar District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Khuzdar district has several options for accommodations that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Khuzdar Continental Hotel: A luxurious hotel that offers a modern lifestyle with its elegant rooms and fine dining options.
  2. Al-Madina Hotel: Al-Madina Hotel is for budget travelers who seek affordable accommodations without compromising on quality.
  3. Mehmood Hotel: Mehmood Hotel offers well-appointed rooms that cater to both leisure and business travelers.
  4. Guest Houses: Guest houses are a great budget accommodation option for budget travelers and offer private rooms and shared spaces.

What to Buy in Khuzdar District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Khuzdar district is famous for its handmade crafts and flavorful spices. Here are some of the best things to buy in Khuzdar district:

  1. Handmade Carpets: Handmade carpets are a specialty of Balochistan and are available in several sizes, patterns, and colors.
  2. Copperware: Copperware includes pots, bowls, vases, and other decorative items that are handcrafted by the local Baloch craftsmen.
  3. Spices: Khuzdar district is famous for its flavorful spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric, and make a great gift or souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Khuzdar District

The people of Khuzdar district are predominantly Baloch, and their culture is rich in hospitality, tradition, and heritage. Balochi is their mother tongue, and Urdu is also spoken widely. Islam is the dominant religion, and people of different faiths live together in harmony. The district celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including Eid, Basant, and Shab-e-Baraat.

Best Time to Visit Khuzdar District

The best time to visit Khuzdar district is from November to February. The weather during these months is pleasant, and the district’s natural beauty is at its peak during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khuzdar District

Do:

  1. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  2. Ask for permission before taking someone’s picture.
  3. Try the local cuisine and experience the local culture.
  4. Bargain when shopping in local markets.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t drink alcohol or smoke in public.
  2. Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  3. Don’t take photographs of people or military installations without permission.
  4. Don’t litter, respect the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khuzdar District

Khuzdar district, like any other destination, has its share of petty crime. It is advisable to avoid traveling alone at night and not to carry large amounts of cash. The district is generally safe and hospitable, and people are always ready to lend a helping hand to tourists.

Health in Khuzdar District

The district has several hospitals and medical facilities that can treat common illnesses and injuries. However, travelers are advised to take precautions such as drinking bottled water and getting vaccinated against common diseases such as hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Khuzdar District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khuzdar District

The nearest airport to Khuzdar district is Quetta International Airport. Several airlines operate flights to Quetta from other major cities in Pakistan.

Bus stations Near Khuzdar District

Khuzdar Bus Station is well-connected with other major cities in Pakistan and offers both air-conditioned and non-air-conditioned bus services.

Other options

Private car rental services are also available in Khuzdar district, and several car rental companies offer a range of vehicles to choose from.

How to Get Around in Khuzdar District

Local transportation options in Khuzdar district include buses, taxis, and rickshaws. Local tour operators and rental car services can also be availed to explore the district’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Khuzdar District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Quetta: Quetta, the capital city of Balochistan, offers an abundance of tourist attractions, including natural reserves and historical sites.
  2. Makran Coast: The Makran Coast, located in Balochistan, offers stunning beaches and seaside resorts.
  3. Ziarat: Ziarat, located about four hours’ drive from Khuzdar, offers an ideal spot for trekking and hiking in the Juniper forest.
  4. Loralai: Loralai, located about three hours northeast of Khuzdar, offers stunning vistas of the Sulaiman mountain range and several trekking and hiking routes.

Conclusion

Khuzdar district, with its diverse cultural and natural marvels, offers a unique travel experience that is both enriching and adventurous. From the rugged mountains to the azure beaches, the district boasts a range of outdoor activities and scenic views. The local culture and heritage add to the district’s charm and allure. Visit Khuzdar district to experience authentic Balochi hospitality and explore the district’s hidden gems.
