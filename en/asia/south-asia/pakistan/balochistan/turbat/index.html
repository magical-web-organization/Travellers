Turbat Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Turbat, located in Balochistan, Pakistan, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This city offers a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. It is perfect for travelers who seek an offbeat destination that is filled with awe-inspiring attractions. This guide will take you on a journey through Turbat, introducing you to the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, and local customs that you need to know before heading here.

Introduction to Turbat

Turbat is a historical city that dates back to 3500 BC. The city is situated in the Kech district of Balochistan province in Pakistan. It is the third-largest city in Balochistan and is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and magnificent natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Turbat

Turbat has a plethora of attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Turbat has something to offer you.

Food and Drink Scene of Turbat

Turbat offers a wide variety of local dishes and beverages that are a must-try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sajji - A dish made from lamb that is marinated in a mixture of spices, cooked on open flames, and served with rice.
  2. Balochi Pulao - A rice dish made with beef or chicken and seasoned with local spices.
  3. Kaak - A type of bread that is made with wheat flour and baked in a clay oven.

Local Beverages

  1. Kahwa - A local tea made with green tea, cinnamon, and cardamom.
  2. Sharbat - A juice made from local fruits such as mangoes, apricots, and pomegranates.

What to Buy in Turbat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Turbat is known for its handicrafts, especially its rugs and carpets. You can also find traditional Balochi clothes and jewelry, which make a great souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Turbat

Turbat is an Islamic city where people follow traditional values and customs. It is important to dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques and other religious sites. Additionally, it is respectful to greet the locals in their native language and avoid physical contact with strangers of the opposite gender.

Best Time to Visit Turbat

The best time to visit Turbat is between November and March when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature is moderate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turbat

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  2. Try the local cuisine.
  3. Learn some phrases in the native Balochi language.

Don’t:

  1. Drink alcohol.
  2. Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  3. Take photographs without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Turbat is a relatively safe city, but travelers should be cautious and avoid venturing out alone at night. Additionally, it is recommended to carry a first-aid kit, insect repellent, and sunscreen when exploring the natural attractions.

Safety in Turbat

  1. Avoid discussing politics and religion with strangers.

Health in Turbat

  1. Drink only bottled water.
  2. Be cautious of what you eat and make sure it is properly cooked.
