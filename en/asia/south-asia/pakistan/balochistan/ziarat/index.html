Introduction to Ziarat

Located in Balochistan, Pakistan, Ziarat is a beautiful and serene mountain town that is a perfect escape from the chaos of city life. Known as the ‘fruit basket of Pakistan,’ Ziarat is famous for its apple orchards and its mesmerizing scenery. Surrounded by beautiful mountains and greenery, Ziarat is also home to several natural attractions, scenic trekking routes, and a rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Ziarat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Quaid-e-Azam Residency

Quaid-e-Azam Residency is a national monument located in Ziarat, which was the residence of the founder of Pakistan, Quaid-e-Azam, during his last days. It is a beautiful colonial-era building that has been preserved as a museum in memory of the great leader.

Juniper Forest

Home to one of the oldest and largest Juniper forests in the world, this natural attraction is an environmental gem that tourists can’t miss. The forest is situated about 30 minutes away from Ziarat.

Kharwari Baba

Located in the Kharwari Baba valley, this is a spiritual place that is revered by locals and visitors alike. The valley is home to a beautiful waterfall and natural pools, making it a refreshing and tranquil spot for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Ziarat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ziarat Residency Park

One of Ziarat’s most popular tourist attractions, this lovely park is situated in the heart of the city, surrounded by breathtaking mountain views. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll while indulging in the beauty of the surroundings.

Sandeman Tangi

A breathtaking natural gorge that is surrounded by picturesque mountains, Sandeman Tangi is a beautiful place for adventure seekers. Visitors can indulge themselves in various activities, including rock climbing, trekking, and camping.

Ziarat Juniper Forest

Home to one of the oldest and largest Juniper forests in the world, this natural attraction is an environmental gem that tourists can’t miss. The forest is situated about 30 minutes away from Ziarat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ziarat

Juniper Forest Trek

A perfect escapade for hikers, the trek to the Juniper forest is the epitome of natural beauty. Visitors can indulge in a day filled with hiking, camping, and enjoying the scenery of the beautiful forest located just outside Ziarat.

Quaid-e-Azam Trek

Visitors can commemorate the life and times of Pakistan’s founder, Quaid-e-Azam, by hiking to the historic site of his residency, an iconic landmark in Pakistan’s history.

Best Things to Do in Ziarat: Activities and Tours

Apple Picking

Ziarat is known as the ‘fruit basket of Pakistan’ and tourists can indulge themselves in traditional apple picking, a fun activity for families that the whole family would enjoy.

Visiting the Local Bazaar

As Ziarat is home to many original Baloch tribes, visitors can witness the bustling marketplace, where local crafters and vendors offer an assortment of products and artisanal crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ziarat

Day 1: Arrival in Ziarat and visiting the Quaid-e-Azam Residency

Day 2: Hike to the Juniper forest

Day 3: Visit Sandeman Tangi

Day 4: Enjoy apple picking and explore the local bazaar

Day 7: Departure from Ziarat

Food and Drink Scene of Ziarat

Must-Try Dishes

Biryani, kababs, and Pakistani-style curries are some of Ziarat’s most famous dishes. Visitors should also try Sajji, Balochistan’s traditional dish, consisting of chicken or lamb, marinated in unique herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

Ziarat’s spectacular range of climate and altitude makes it a perfect place for cultivating tea, which is a favorite beverage of the locals. Be sure to try Chaunsa, a traditional cold beverage prepared with mango pulp, yogurt and sugar.

Where to Stay in Ziarat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ziarat has a variety of budget-friendly accommodations available, ranging from budget hotels to guesthouses. For a more luxurious experience, visitors can opt for mid-tier hotels closer to the city center.

What to Buy in Ziarat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a range of handmade products at Ziarat’s local bazaar to include marble handicraft, traditional Baloch blankets, and pottery. To take a piece of Ziarat’s history home, visitors can also choose to buy Juniper wood products, which are unique to the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Ziarat

Ziarat, being a part of Balochistan, is known for its unique culture and customs. Visitors must respect the traditions of the Baloch tribes, which form an essential part of the local culture. Visitors should dress modestly out of respect for the locals’ customs and culture. Photography is not always welcome, especially when capturing people, and taking permission is necessary.

Best Time to Visit Ziarat

The ideal time to visit Ziarat is during the months of July, August, and September, when the climate is pleasant, and the apple trees are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ziarat

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly
  • Ask before taking photographs of people
  • Pack sunscreen and adequate hydration

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Dress inappropriately
  • Take photographs of locals without permission
  • Litter or vandalize the natural beauty

Safety and Health

Safety in Ziarat

While Ziarat is generally safe for tourists, visitors should take necessary precautions such as carrying copies of important documents and staying in groups during outdoor activities such as hiking and camping.

Health in Ziarat

Visitors should be mindful of the hot weather and humidity during the summer months and carry sunscreen and adequate hydration. Visitors must carry a first-aid kit for emergencies.

Getting to Ziarat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ziarat

The closest airport to Ziarat is Quetta International Airport, which is located approximately 130 kilometers from Ziarat.

Bus Stations Near Ziarat

There are many bus services running to and from Ziarat, primarily operated by Daewoo Express. Visitors can take a bus from Quetta or Karachi bus terminals to reach Ziarat.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi from Quetta, which is an easy and comfortable way of traveling to Ziarat.

How to Get Around in Ziarat

Public transportation in Ziarat is limited, and visitors can opt for private taxis, which are commonly available. Visitors can also hire bicycles or motorcycles to explore the town at their own speed.

Where to Go Next from Ziarat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Quetta

Rich in history and culture, Quetta is a city less than two hours away from Ziarat, home to various museums, bazaars, and natural attractions.

Khuzdar

Khuzdar is a city located approximately six hours away from Ziarat and holds historical and cultural significance. tourists can visit well-known landmarks such as the Pir Ghaib Waterfall to experience the local culture and customs.

Conclusion

Ziarat is a charming town with a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Visitors can immerse themselves in local traditions and activities, including hiking, camping, apple picking, and tea-tasting. Plan a trip to Ziarat for an unforgettable experience of the beauty that Pakistan has to offer.
