Punjab is a land of vibrant colors, rich history, and stunning natural beauty located in South Asia. The state is a perfect mix of modern and ancient, where the old and the new come together to create a unique atmosphere. If you love culture, food, and outdoor adventures, Punjab is a must-visit destination for you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, food and culture scene, travel tips, and much more.

Introduction to Punjab

Punjab is a state in the northern part of India, known for its agriculture, culture, and history. The word ‘Punjab’ means ‘land of five rivers,’ and the state is home to fertile land due to the presence of the five rivers - Sutlej, Beas, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum. Punjab has a rich cultural heritage and is famous for its vibrant festivals, traditional handicrafts, and mouth-watering cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Punjab

Punjab has a lot to offer for every kind of traveler, from historical landmarks to natural vistas. Here are the must-visit places in Punjab:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Golden Temple in Amritsar: The Golden Temple is the holiest shrine of the Sikh religion and one of the most visited places in Punjab. The temple is a symbol of peace, equality, and brotherhood, and its beauty is unmatched.
  • Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar: A public garden which witnessed a tragic incident in 1919 when British troops opened fire on a gathering of unarmed peaceful protesters, killing hundreds of people.
  • Maharaja Ranjit Singh Museum in Amritsar: The museum showcases the life and times of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, who was the founder of the Sikh Empire.
  • Qila Mubarak in Patiala: This fort cum palace is one of the main attractions of Patiala, known for its exquisite Sheesh Mahal and gardens.
  • Sheesh Mahal in Patiala: A stunning palace known for its intricate mirror work and vibrant frescoes.
  • Anandpur Sahib: The birthplace of the Khalsa Panth and one of the most sacred places for the Sikh religion.

Natural Attractions of Punjab: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harike Wetland and Bird Sanctuary: Known as the ‘Harike Pattan,’ this bird sanctuary is home to migratory and resident birds, including the majestic Harriers and Pelicans.
  • Kikar Lodge: A nature resort located near Ropar known for its adventurous activities such as hiking, trekking, and rappelling.
  • Bhakra Nangal Dam: One of the largest and highest gravity dams in the world, this marvel of engineering is located on the Sutlej river and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Ropar Wetland: A Ramsar site and an important wetland located on the Sutlej river, known for its rich biodiversity.
  • Rock Garden in Chandigarh: An artistic garden made entirely of industrial and urban waste and scrap, making it a one-of-a-kind scenic spot.
  • Pinjore Gardens in Panchkula: A Mughal-style garden, also known as Yadavindra Gardens, which is a perfect picnic spot with intricate water fountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Punjab

Punjab is dotted with scenic routes, which are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Kikar Lodge to Shivalik Hills: A scenic route with hills, rivers, and forests, offering the perfect terrain for mountain biking and trekking.
  • Chandigarh to Kasauli: A popular stretch among bikers, this route leads from Chandigarh to Kasauli, offering the perfect combination of scenic beauty and challenging terrain.
  • Ludhiana to Ropar: A flat route that passes through villages and small towns, offering a glimpse into the rustic life of Punjab.

Best Things to Do in Punjab: Activities and Tours

Besides traditional sightseeing, Punjab offers plenty of activities and tours that travelers can indulge in such as:

  • Heritage Walks in Amritsar: A guided tour to explore the rich history and culture of Amritsar, taking you through the ‘gullies’ and bazaars of the city.
  • Food Walks in Amritsar: A culinary tour to explore the mouth-watering dishes of Amritsar such as Amritsari fish, keema naan, lassi and jalebi.
  • Wagah Border Ceremony: A daily flag-lowering ceremony between India and Pakistan, where soldiers from both sides display their patriotism and martial art skills.
  • Night tourism in Patiala: A unique cultural experience of exploring Patiala’s palaces, markets, and food stalls after dark.
  • Nature Walks and Trekking: Explore the mesmerizing natural beauty of Punjab by taking up a nature walk or trekking in the hills of Shivalik, Dhauladhar, and Pir Panjal.
  • Shopping: Explore the vibrant bazaars of Punjab and buy traditional handicrafts such as phulkari, juttis, and Patiala salwar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Punjab

If you are planning to visit Punjab, here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1-2: Amritsar – Visit the Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh, and enjoy a food walk in the old city.
  • Day 3-4: Patiala – Explore the Qila Mubarak, Sheesh Mahal and enjoy the palace’s light and sound show.
  • Day 5-6: Chandigarh – Visit the Rock Garden, Sukhna Lake, and the famous Rose Garden.
  • Day 7: Ropar – Visit the impressive Bhakra Nangal Dam and experience nature at Ropar Wetland.

Food and Drink Scene of Punjab

Punjabi cuisine is famous all over the world, and rightfully so. The rich, spicy, and buttery flavors are sure to tantalize your taste buds. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amritsari Fish: Fried fish marinated in spices and served with chutney and onions.
  • Butter Chicken: Succulent pieces of chicken in creamy tomato gravy.
  • Sarson Ka Saag with Makki Di Roti: A typical Punjabi dish made with mustard greens and corn flour bread.
  • Chole Bhature: Spicy chickpeas curry served with deep-fried bread.
  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink served sweet or salty.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: A refreshing drink made with buttermilk, spices, and herbs.
  • Thandai: A cooling drink made with milk, almonds, and spices such as cardamom, saffron, and nutmeg.
  • Desi Daru: A local alcohol made from fermented sugarcane juice or grains.

Where to Stay in Punjab: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Punjab offers a variety of accommodation options, suitable for different budgets and needs. Here are some of the best options:

  • Luxury hotels: Taj Swarna in Amritsar, JW Marriot in Chandigarh, Patiala Retreat in Patiala.
  • Mid-range hotels: Hotel Lawrence in Amritsar, Hotel Shivalik View in Chandigarh, Hotel Royal Castle in Patiala.

What to Buy in Punjab: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can buy in Punjab:

  • Phulkari Embroidery: Traditional embroidery work that originated in Punjab.
  • Juttis: Hand-crafted leather shoes that are popular in Punjab.
  • Patiala Salwar: A type of baggy trousers that originated in Patiala.
  • Punjab’s famous spices such as Garam Masala, Kasoori Methi, and Dal Makhani mix.
  • Papad and Wadiyan: Dried lentil-based crispy snacks that you can carry home.

Local Culture and Customs of Punjab

Punjabi culture is known for its vibrancy, hospitality, and zestful celebrations. Punjabis love to eat, dance, and sing, and the festive season brings with it a plethora of colorful traditions and celebrations such as Bhangra, Gidda, Lohri, and Basant Panchami. Punjabis are known for their bonhomie and warm-hearted nature, and they welcome guests with delight and affection.

Best Time to Visit Punjab

The best time to visit Punjab is during the winter months, from November to March when the weather is pleasant and mild. The summers can be scorching, and it’s not an ideal time to visit Punjab.

Do’s and Don’ts of Punjab

Do:

  • Wear comfortable cotton clothes, especially during summers.
  • Respect the local culture, traditions, and customs.
  • Try different cuisines and local delicacies.
  • Respect the religious sentiments of the people.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the religious sites or any religious beliefs.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially in the religious places.
  • Show up at someone’s home without an invitation.
  • Waste water or electricity as Punjab is a state water-scare.

Safety and Health

Safety in Punjab

  • Punjab is a safe state, especially for tourists.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in deserted areas.
  • Be careful with your belongings as pickpocketing may happen in crowded areas.

Health in Punjab

  • Ensure that you drink bottled water or have a water purification device with you.
  • Follow good hygiene practices such as washing your hands regularly.
  • Carry necessary medication and first-aid kit with you.

Getting to Punjab: Transportation Options and Logistics

Punjab is well-connected with major cities in India and is easy to reach by different modes of transportation.

Airports Near Punjab

  • Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport in Amritsar
  • Chandigarh International Airport in Chandigarh

Bus Stations Near Punjab

  • Amritsar Bus Stand in Amritsar
  • Ludhiana Bus Stand in Ludhiana
  • Pepsu Road Transport Corporation Bus Stand in Patiala

Other Options

  • You can also travel by train as Punjab is well-connected by rail with major cities in India.
  • Hire a private taxi from the airport or station to your destination.

How to Get Around in Punjab

Punjab has an extensive network of roads and highways, and the best way to explore the state is by hiring a private taxi, renting a car or using public transport such as buses and trains. Auto-rickshaws and cycle-rickshaws are also prevalent in small towns.

Where to Go Next from Punjab: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby places that you can visit after exploring Punjab:

  • Dharamshala and McLeod Ganj in Himachal Pradesh: Known as the abode of Dalai Lama, Dharamshala and McLeod Ganj are famous for their natural beauty and Tibetan culture.
  • Kasauli in Himachal Pradesh: A quiet and serene hill station perfect for a weekend getaway.
  • Rishikesh in Uttarakhand: A popular destination for adventure seekers with its exciting activities such as river rafting, bungee jumping, and trekking.
  • Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh: A spiritual and cultural hub known for its vibrant ghats along the Ganges.
  • Jaipur in Rajasthan: Known as the Pink City, famous for its grand palaces, forts, and vibrant markets.

Conclusion

Punjab is a fascinating state, and there’s so much to explore and experience. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Punjab, from the best places to visit to the must-try dishes and cultural experiences. With its warm hospitality, rich culture, and stunning natural beauty, Punjab is a state that you’ll never forget.
