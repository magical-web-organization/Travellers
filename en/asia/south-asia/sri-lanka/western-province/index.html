Introduction to Western Province

Located in the south-western region of Sri Lanka, Western Province is a bustling hub of commercial, cultural, and historical significance. This beautiful coastal area is home to the famous cities of Colombo and Negombo and is a popular travel destination among locals and tourists alike. It is the most urbanized province of the country and is known for its vibrant nightlife, world-class restaurants, nature reserves, beaches, and cultural heritage.

If you’re planning to visit Western Province, this comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information, tips, and advice to ensure a memorable and safe trip.

List of Places to Visit in Western Province

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Western Province is home to some of Sri Lanka’s most prominent and historically significant sites. These include:

  • Kelaniya Raja Maha Vihara: This famous Buddhist temple dates back to the 3rd century BC and is renowned for its ancient murals and stunning architecture.
  • Colombo National Museum: Established in 1877, this museum showcases Sri Lanka’s cultural heritage and is home to over 100,000 artifacts, including ancient weapons, pottery, and artwork.
  • Gangaramaya Temple: This Buddhist temple is a popular tourist attraction and is home to a museum, art gallery, and library.
  • Dutch Museum: Located in Galle, this museum provides a glimpse into the Dutch colonial past of Sri Lanka.

Natural Attractions of Western Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Western Province is blessed with diverse natural beauty, from pristine beaches to lush forests and wildlife reserves. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Negombo Beach: Located in the heart of Negombo city, this beautiful beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Sinharaja Forest Reserve: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the last remaining rainforests in Sri Lanka and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna.
  • Bellanwila Rajamaha Viharaya: A popular temple with scenic views and a serene atmosphere amid the bustling city of Colombo.
  • Bolgoda Lake: The largest natural lake in Sri Lanka and a great location for fishing, boating, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Province

One of the best ways to explore Western Province is by taking a hike or a bike ride through its scenic landscapes. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Sinharaja Forest Reserve Trek: This scenic trek through the rainforest offers nature lovers a chance to experience beautiful landscapes and wildlife.
  • Bolgoda Lake Cycling Trail: This 15 km trail around the Bolgoda Lake is perfect for cycling enthusiasts and nature lovers.
  • Colombo City Cycling Tour: A thrilling way to explore Colombo, this cycling tour takes you through some of the city’s most historic and cultural sites.

Best Things to Do in Western Province: Activities and Tours

Western Province offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to every type of traveler. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Sunset cruise on the Bolgoda Lake
  • Visit to the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage
  • City tour of Colombo
  • Sri Lankan cooking classes
  • Whale and dolphin watching
  • Hot air balloon ride over cultural sites

Week-Long Itinerary for Western Province

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Western Province, here’s a sample itinerary to help you get started:

Day 1: Arrive in Colombo, city tour of Colombo Day 2: Visit to Kelaniya Raja Maha Viharaya, Gangaramaya Temple, and other cultural attractions Day 3: Safari at Yala National Park Day 4: Trek through Sinharaja Forest Reserve Day 5: Visit the beaches of Negombo and indulge in water sports Day 6: Visit Bolgoda Lake, enjoy a sunset cruise, and experience local village life Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Western Province

Sri Lanka is known for its delicious food, and Western Province is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kottu Roti: A popular street food that is made with shredded roti, vegetables, and meat or seafood.
  • String Hoppers: A staple breakfast dish made with steamed rice flour noodles.
  • Lamprais: A Dutch-influenced dish that consists of rice cooked with curry, meat, and vegetables and wrapped in a banana leaf.

Local Beverages

  • King Coconut: A refreshing and hydrating drink made from the juice of young green coconuts.
  • Arrack: A local alcoholic beverage made from coconut or palm sap.

Local Culture and Customs of Western Province

Sri Lankan culture and customs are deeply rooted in its Buddhist heritage, and Western Province is no exception. Visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and it is customary to remove one’s shoes before entering temples and other holy places. Sri Lankans are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors should reciprocate by being respectful and courteous.

Best Time to Visit Western Province

The best time to visit Western Province is between December and March when the weather is dry and sunny. However, this is also the peak tourist season, and prices may be higher. If you’re looking for a more budget-friendly option, consider traveling between May and September when the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western Province

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Be respectful and courteous to locals
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Carry sunscreen, hat, and insect repellent when visiting outdoor attractions
  • Bargain for prices at local markets and shops

Don’t:

  • Take photos without obtaining permission from locals or at religious sites
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Drink tap water or eat food from unhygienic establishments

Safety and Health

Safety in Western Province

While Western Province is generally a safe place to visit, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large sums of cash
  • Use licensed taxis and transportation options
  • Keep a copy of your passport and other important documents in a safe place

Health in Western Province

Visitors to Western Province should be up to date on their routine vaccinations and should also get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and Typhoid. It is recommended to avoid drinking tap water and eating food from street vendors to prevent gastrointestinal illnesses. Visitors should carry mosquito repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.
