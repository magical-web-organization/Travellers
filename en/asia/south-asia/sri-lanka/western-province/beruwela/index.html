Located in the Western Province of Sri Lanka, Beruwela is a small town that offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From white sand beaches to lush greenery, Beruwela has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide to Beruwela, we’ll take you through top attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, transportation options, and more!

Introduction to Beruwela

Beruwela is home to some of Sri Lanka’s most beautiful beaches, including the Golden Mile Beach, which stretches for approximately five miles. The town is rich in history and culture, as seen in its various historical places and museums. The people of Beruwela are warm and hospitable, and their welcoming nature makes visitors feel at home.

List of Places to Visit in Beruwela

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kechimalai Mosque: Built-in 1000 AD, this mosque is a beautiful example of Islamic architecture. It is said to house a hair of the Prophet Mohammed.

  2. Galapata Viharaya: This temple is known for its intricate statues and beautiful murals. It is also one of the few Buddhist temples in Sri Lanka that has a Vishnu statue.

  3. Beruwela Lighthouse: The lighthouse is a beautiful structure and an iconic landmark of Beruwela. It has been in operation since 1922 and is one of the oldest lighthouses in Sri Lanka.

Natural Attractions of Beruwela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bentota Beach: Bentota Beach is a beautiful beach that stretches for miles. It is an ideal place for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing.

  2. Brief Garden: Brief Garden is a beautiful botanical garden that is home to various flora and fauna. It was once owned by Bevis Bawa, brother of famous Sri Lankan architect Geoffrey Bawa.

  3. Kande Vihare Temple: This temple is located on a hill and offers a beautiful view of the surrounding area. It is also home to a 160-foot Buddha statue, which is the tallest in Sri Lanka.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beruwela

  1. Turtle Conservation Project: This project offers guided tours that take visitors through forests and lagoons. The tour also includes a visit to a turtle hatchery, where visitors can see baby turtles being released into the sea.

  2. Kanneliya Forest Reserve: The Kanneliya forest reserve is home to various species of flora and fauna. It offers various hiking trails that take visitors through lush greenery and beautiful waterfalls.

  3. Sinharaja Forest Reserve: This is one of the few remaining rainforests in Sri Lanka. It offers various hiking trails that take visitors through thick forests and beautiful streams.

Best Things to Do in Beruwela: Activities and Tours

  1. Water Sports: Beruwela is an ideal place for various water sports such as surfing, jet skiing, and diving. There are many resorts and tour operators that offer these activities.

  2. River Safaris: The Bentota River is a beautiful river that flows through Beruwela. Visitors can take a river safari that takes them through mangroves and other beautiful scenery.

  3. Fishing: There are a few tour operators in Beruwela that offer fishing trips. Visitors can catch various species of fish, including tuna, sailfish, and marlin.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beruwela

Day 1: Visit the Kechimalai Mosque, the Galapata Viharaya, and the Beruwela Lighthouse.

Day 2: Spend the day at Bentota Beach, enjoying water activities.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Turtle Conservation Project and visit the turtle hatchery.

Day 4: Visit the Kande Vihare Temple and enjoy the beautiful view of the surrounding area.

Day 5: Take a river safari on the Bentota River.

Day 6: Enjoy hiking at the Kanneliya Forest Reserve and explore beautiful waterfalls.

Day 7: Spend the day at Brief Garden, enjoying the beautiful botanical garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Beruwela

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kottu Roti: Kottu Roti is a popular Sri Lankan dish made with vegetables, eggs, and shredded roti bread.

  2. Seafood: Beruwela is home to some of the freshest seafood, including prawns, lobsters, and crabs.

  3. Rice and Curry: Rice and curry is a staple Sri Lankan dish that is served with various curries and sides.

Local Beverages

  1. King Coconut Water: King coconut water is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot days.

  2. Arrack: This is a popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented sap of coconut flowers.

Where to Stay in Beruwela: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Cinnamon Bey: This is a beautiful five-star resort that offers luxurious accommodations and various amenities.

  2. Centara Ceysands Resort & Spa: This resort is located by the Bentota River and offers beautiful views and luxurious accommodations.

  3. Little Paradise Guesthouse: This guesthouse is a perfect choice for budget travelers. It offers clean and comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Beruwela: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Beruwela is known for its beautiful handicrafts, including wooden carvings and woven items such as bags and mats.

  2. Gems: Sri Lanka is known for its beautiful gems, and Beruwela has many jewelry shops that sell them.

  3. Tea: Sri Lanka is known for its tea, and Beruwela has many tea shops that sell high-quality tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Beruwela

The people of Beruwela are primarily Muslim, and their culture and customs reflect this. Visitors should try to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Beruwela

The best time to visit Beruwela is between November and April when the weather is dry and sunny. Avoid visiting between May and October as it is the monsoon season in Sri Lanka.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beruwela

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  2. Respect local customs and traditions.

  3. Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  2. Wearing revealing clothing in public places.

  3. Engaging in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beruwela

Beruwela is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings, especially when in crowded areas.

Health in Beruwela

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water. It is also advisable to take precautions against mosquito bites as there have been cases of Dengue fever in Sri Lanka.

Getting to Beruwela: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beruwela

The closest airport to Beruwela is Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, which is approximately 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Beruwela

Beruwela has a bus station that connects it to various parts of Sri Lanka.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars and taxis to travel to and from Beruwela.

How to Get Around in Beruwela

The easiest way to get around Beruwela is by tuk-tuk or taxi. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to explore the town at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Beruwela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Galle: Galle is a historic city located approximately 75 kilometers from Beruwela. It is known for its beautiful fort, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  2. Mirissa: Mirissa is a beautiful beach town located approximately 100 kilometers from Beruwela. It is known for its beautiful beaches and whale watching tours.

  3. Yala National Park: Yala National Park is a popular wildlife reserve located approximately 200 kilometers from Beruwela. It is home to various species of wildlife, including leopards, elephants, and crocodiles.

Conclusion

Beruwela has something for everyone - whether you’re looking for a beach holiday, a cultural experience, or an outdoor adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to explore the best of Beruwela in a hassle-free way. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to this beautiful town in Sri Lanka!
