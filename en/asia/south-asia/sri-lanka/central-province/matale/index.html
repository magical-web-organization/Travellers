Introduction to Matale

Matale, located in Sri Lanka’s Central Province, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture, it offers a unique travel experience for those looking to explore outside of the country’s more popular destinations.

Matale’s strategic location in the heart of Sri Lanka makes it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding areas. The city itself is easily accessible by road, making it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Matale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Matale is home to many historically significant sites, including museums, temples, and ancient ruins. You can start your journey by visiting the Aluvihara Temple, which is known for its ancient Buddhist scriptures. The ancient rock fortress of Sigiriya, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site, is also nearby.

Another must-visit site is the Nalanda Gedige Temple, which is believed to be one of the few surviving examples of Sri Lanka’s unique blend of Hindu and Buddhist architecture. The Embekka Devalaya, which features intricate carvings and wooden pillars, is also worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Matale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Matale offers a diverse range of natural attractions, from majestic mountains to lush green tea plantations. The Knuckles Mountain Range, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site, is one of the most popular destinations for hikers and adventure seekers.

The Kandy Lake, surrounded by misty hills, is a serene spot to watch the sunset. The Sembuwatte Lake, which is a man-made lake with crystal clear water, is an excellent spot for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matale

Matale’s stunning landscapes make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Knuckles Mountain Range offers many trekking trails, from easy to challenging. The Pitawala Pathana, located near Riverston in Matale, is a popular destination for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Matale: Activities and Tours

Matale offers an array of activities and tours for travelers, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. You can take a guided tour of the ancient ruins of Sigiriya or climb the Pidurangala rock to witness breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Visit the Matale Heritage Centre to learn about the unique culture and history of the region. For a more adventurous experience, try white water rafting on the Kelani River or go on a wildlife safari in the nearby Minneriya National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matale

Day 1: Visit the Aluvihara Temple and Nalanda Gedige Temple. Day 2: Explore the Knuckles Mountain Range. Day 3: Visit the Embekka Devalaya and the ancient ruins of Sigiriya. Day 4: Take a wildlife safari in Minneriya National Park. Day 5: Learn about the region’s history and culture at the Matale Heritage Centre. Day 6: Take a guided tour of a local tea plantation. Day 7: Relax at the Sembuwatta Lake or Kandy Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Matale

Must-Try Dishes

Matale’s cuisine is influenced by the surrounding regions, offering a fusion of flavors that are unique to the area. Try the Sri Lankan staple dish, Rice and Curry, which is a combination of rice, various curries, and accompaniments such as chutneys and papadums.

Other must-try dishes include Kottu Roti, a popular street food made with shredded flatbread, stir-fried vegetables, and meat, and Pol Roti, a type of Sri Lankan flatbread made with coconut and flour.

Local Beverages

Sri Lanka is known for its world-class tea, and Matale is no exception. Visit a local tea plantation to sample the region’s finest blends, and try other local beverages such as King Coconut water or thambili.

Where to Stay in Matale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matale offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. For those looking for a more upscale experience, the Hunas Falls Hotel is a popular choice, offering stunning views and top-notch amenities. The Misty Hills Hotel is another great option, with spacious rooms and a serene location.

For budget-friendly options, consider the White House Guest House or the Green Hill Residence, both offering comfortable rooms at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Matale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matale is known for its locally made handicrafts, including intricate wood carvings and woven baskets. Visit a local market to pick up unique souvenirs and take a piece of Matale’s vibrant culture home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Matale

Sri Lanka is known for its warm hospitality and friendly locals, and Matale is no exception. When visiting religious sites, it is important to dress conservatively and remove your shoes before entering.

Matale also celebrates a range of festivals throughout the year, including the Sinhala and Tamil New Year, which takes place in April, and the Kandy Esala Perahera, a cultural festival that honors the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha.

Best Time to Visit Matale

The best time to visit Matale is between December and April, when the weather is dry and sunny. The temperature averages around 26°C during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matale

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and learn about the unique flavors of the region.
  • Visit during one of the many festivals to experience the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions of Matale.
  • Photograph locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matale

Matale is a generally safe destination, but it is important to take precautions as you would in any other city. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables secure.

Health in Matale

Matale is a malaria-free zone, but it is recommended to take anti-malaria medication if you plan to visit other areas of Sri Lanka. Tap water is not safe to drink, so it is best to stick to bottled water.

Getting to Matale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matale

The nearest airport to Matale is the Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, which is about 150 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Matale.

Bus Stations Near Matale

Matale has a central bus station that connects it to major cities in Sri Lanka, including Kandy, Colombo, and Nuwara Eliya.

Other Options

You can also hire a private driver or take a train to Kandy and then transfer to Matale.

How to Get Around in Matale

Matale is a small city and is best explored on foot. Taxis and tuk-tuks are readily available for longer journeys or to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Matale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Matale, you can easily explore the surrounding areas of Kandy, Dambulla, and Nuwara Eliya. Take a train to Ella to experience the stunning views from the Nine Arch Bridge, or head to Galle to explore the historic Dutch Fort.

Conclusion

Matale is a vibrant city with a rich culture and history, offering travelers an unforgettable experience. From natural attractions to cultural experiences and adventure activities, there is something for everyone in Matale. Plan your visit today to discover this hidden gem in the heart of Sri Lanka’s Central Province.
