Nuwara Eliya, located in the heart of Sri Lanka’s Central Province, is a charming town that boasts of breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique experiences that you won’t find anywhere else in the country. Known as the “Little England” of Sri Lanka, Nuwara Eliya is a popular tourist destination that attracts travelers from all over the world. In this guide, we’ll take a look at all the must-visit attractions, activities, and tips for a memorable trip to Nuwara Eliya.

Introduction to Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a picturesque hill town located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, at an altitude of 1,868 meters above sea level. The town was founded in the 19th century by the British, who saw the potential of the region for growing tea. The town’s stunning landscapes, mild climate, and colonial architecture quickly made it a popular retreat for British expatriates, who would often escape to the hill station to escape the sweltering heat of Colombo. Today, Nuwara Eliya is a bustling tourist destination that offers a unique blend of Sri Lankan and British influences.

List of Places to Visit in Nuwara Eliya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nuwara Eliya is home to several museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the town’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Hakgala Botanical Gardens: Spread over an area of 28 hectares, the Hakgala Botanical Gardens is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. The gardens were established in 1861 by the British, and today it is one of the most popular attractions in Nuwara Eliya.

  • Seetha Amman Temple: Located in the village of Seetha Eliya, the Seetha Amman Temple is a Hindu temple that is dedicated to the goddess Seetha. According to legend, this temple was built on the spot where Seetha was held captive by Ravana, the demon king of Sri Lanka.

  • Galway’s Land National Park: Galway’s Land National Park is a protected wildlife sanctuary that is home to several endemic species of plants and animals. The park is situated on the slopes of Pidurutalagala, Sri Lanka’s highest mountain peak.

Natural Attractions of Nuwara Eliya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nuwara Eliya is famous for its breathtaking natural landscapes and outdoor activities. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in the town:

  • Adam’s Peak: Adam’s Peak is a sacred mountain peak that is revered by Buddhists, Hindus, Christians, and Muslims. The peak is situated at an altitude of 2,243 meters above sea level, and the climb to the summit offers breathtaking views of the surrounding regions.

  • Lake Gregory: Lake Gregory is a man-made lake that is surrounded by lush green forests and rolling hills. The lake offers several activities such as boat rides, jet skiing, and fishing.

  • Horton Plains National Park: Horton Plains National Park is a protected wildlife sanctuary that is home to several endangered species of plants and animals. The park is famous for its stunning landscapes, including the famous World’s End viewpoint.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a hiker’s paradise, with several scenic trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Some of the popular hiking routes in the town are:

  • Pidurutalagala: Pidurutalagala is the highest mountain peak in Sri Lanka, and the climb to the summit offers magnificent panoramic views of the surrounding regions.

  • Lover’s Leap: Lover’s Leap is a scenic viewpoint that offers a bird’s eye view of Nuwara Eliya town. The viewpoint is located on a cliff that is surrounded by verdant tea plantations.

  • Moon Plains: Moon Plains is a scenic plateau that is situated at an altitude of 2,200 meters above sea level. The plateau offers stunning views of the surrounding regions, and visitors can go on guided hikes and nature walks in the area.

Best Things to Do in Nuwara Eliya: Activities and Tours

Nuwara Eliya offers several unique experiences that you won’t find anywhere else in Sri Lanka. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Tea Factory Visits: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea, and Nuwara Eliya is home to some of the best tea factories in the country. Visitors can go on guided tours of the factory and learn about the tea-making process.

  • Provincial Grand Hotel: The Provincial Grand Hotel is a heritage hotel that was built in the 19th century by the British. The hotel is a popular tourist destination, and visitors can enjoy a traditional English high tea in the hotel’s elegant dining room.

  • Horseback Riding: Nuwara Eliya’s rolling hills and meadows make it the perfect destination for horseback riding. Visitors can go on guided horseback rides through the town’s picturesque countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Nuwara Eliya

No holiday in Nuwara Eliya is complete without trying out the town’s unique cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kothu Roti: Kothu Roti is a popular Sri Lankan street food that is made with shredded roti, vegetables, and chicken or beef.

  • Lamprais: Lamprais is a traditional Sri Lankan dish that consists of rice, meat, vegetables, and spices that are baked in a banana leaf.

  • Hoppers: Hoppers are a type of crispy, bowl-shaped pancakes that are served with a variety of different toppings, such as egg, chicken, and fish curry.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea, and Nuwara Eliya is home to some of the best tea plantations in the country. Visitors can try out different varieties of tea and learn about the tea-making process by going on guided tea plantation tours.

  • Arrack: Arrack is a popular Sri Lankan alcoholic beverage that is made from the sap of coconut flowers.

  • King Coconut: King Coconut is a refreshing and hydrating drink that is made from the juice of young coconuts.

Where to Stay in Nuwara Eliya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nuwara Eliya offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the town are:

  • The Grand Hotel: The Grand Hotel is a luxury heritage hotel that was built in the 19th century by the British. The hotel offers a range of amenities, such as a spa, pool, and restaurants.

  • Jetwing St. Andrew’s: Jetwing St. Andrew’s is a colonial-style hotel that is located in the heart of Nuwara Eliya’s tea country. The hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and is known for its excellent service.

  • The Hill Club: The Hill Club is a members-only club that is open to the public for accommodation. The club offers a range of facilities, such as a bowling alley, golf course, and billiards room.

What to Buy in Nuwara Eliya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nuwara Eliya offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a memento of their trip. Some of the popular items to buy in the town are:

  • Tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea, and Nuwara Eliya is home to some of the best tea plantations in the country. Visitors can buy a variety of different teas from the local stores and supermarkets.

  • Handicrafts: Nuwara Eliya is home to several artisans who produce a range of handicrafts, such as hand-woven carpets, wooden carvings, and pottery.

  • Spices: Sri Lanka is famous for its spices, and Nuwara Eliya offers visitors the chance to buy a range of different spices and spice blends from the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a multi-cultural town that is influenced by both Sri Lankan and British traditions. Visitors are advised to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and temples, and to remove their shoes before entering any sacred spaces. Tipping is a common practice in Sri Lanka, and visitors are advised to tip hotel staff, drivers, and tour guides.

Best Time to Visit Nuwara Eliya

The best time to visit Nuwara Eliya is between the months of December and February, when the weather is cool and dry. The town experiences a monsoon season from April to June, and heavy rain and landslides can occur during this time. To avoid the crowds, visitors can also consider visiting Nuwara Eliya during the shoulder seasons of March and November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nuwara Eliya

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and temples
  • Try out the local cuisine and beverages
  • Tip hotel staff, drivers, and tour guides

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside sacred spaces
  • Drink tap water without boiling it first
  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drug use or gambling

Safety and Health

Safety in Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a safe town for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take basic precautions, such as keeping their belongings secure and not walking alone at night. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for landslides during the monsoon season and should follow any instructions given by the local authorities.

Health in Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya has a well-established healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics that offer high-quality medical care. Visitors are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Nuwara Eliya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nuwara Eliya is located approximately 180 kilometers away from Sri Lanka’s capital city, Colombo. Here are some transportation options for getting to Nuwara Eliya:

Airports Near Nuwara Eliya

  • Bandaranaike International Airport: Bandaranaike International Airport is located in Colombo and is approximately 150 kilometers away from Nuwara Eliya.

Bus Stations Near Nuwara Eliya

  • Nanuoya Bus Station: Nanuoya Bus Station is located approximately 8 kilometers away from Nuwara Eliya town center.

  • Kandy Bus Station: Kandy Bus Station is located approximately 75 kilometers away from Nuwara Eliya town center.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: Visitors can hire a private taxi from Colombo or Kandy to Nuwara Eliya.

How to Get Around in Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and tuk-tuks, to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Nuwara Eliya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nuwara Eliya is surrounded by several other popular tourist destinations that are worth visiting. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Kandy: Kandy is a popular tourist destination that is known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic landscapes.

  • Ella: Ella is a small town that is known for its stunning landscapes and outdoor adventures, such as hiking and rock climbing.

  • Yala National Park: Yala National Park is a wildlife sanctuary that is home to several endangered species of plants and animals, including elephants, leopards, and crocodiles.

Conclusion

Nuwara Eliya is a charming town that offers a unique blend of Sri Lankan and British influences. With its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique experiences, Nuwara Eliya is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the heart of Sri Lanka’s hill country. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker, or history buff, Nuwara Eliya has something to offer everyone.
