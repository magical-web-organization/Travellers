Introduction to Kandy

Kandy, situated in the heart of Sri Lanka, is a vibrant city renowned for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. The city was the last capital of the ancient kings’ era of Sri Lanka and is home to a range of sacred Buddhist sites, including the Temple of the Tooth Relic on the banks of the Kandy Lake. With a wide range of outdoor adventures, picturesque views and cultural landmarks, Kandy is a perfect destination for travelers interested in exploring Sri Lanka’s rich heritage and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Kandy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Temple of the Tooth Relic

The Temple of the Tooth Relic is one of the most significant Buddhist temples and a pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. It is believed to house the tooth relic of Lord Buddha and is situated on the banks of the Kandy Lake.

Bahiravokanda Vihara Buddha Statue

This massive Buddha statue, built-in 1972, stands at 88 feet tall, and getting a glimpse of the Buddha’s serene face is a sight to behold. It’s worth visiting for the breath-taking panoramic views of the city.

The Royal Palace of Kandy

The Royal Palace of Kandy was the residence of the last king of Sri Lanka before the country was colonized. The palace is now a national museum depicting the history of Kandy and Sri Lanka.

National Museum of Kandy

The National Museum of Kandy holds some of Sri Lanka’s most important cultural and anthropological treasures, including collections of traditional Kandyan dress and weapons.

Natural Attractions of Kandy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kandy Lake

The picturesque Kandy Lake, built in 1807, is an essential witness to the city’s history, and its serene surroundings make it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll or picnic.

Udawatta Kele Sanctuary

The pristine forest reserve of Udawatta Kele Sanctuary is perfect for nature lovers and bird enthusiasts. It boasts many stunning wildlife sightings, including monkeys and a variety of exotic birds.

Knuckles Mountain Range

For adventure seekers, the Knuckles Mountain Range, located 30 kilometers from Kandy, offers thrilling hiking and mountaineering opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kandy

If you’re interested in exploring Kandy’s scenic beauty, there are several exciting hiking and biking trails to choose from. These trails will take you through some of the city’s most mesmerizing views and allow you to explore its natural beauty better.

Best Things to Do in Kandy: Activities and Tours

Kandy City Tour

A city tour of Kandy will take you to some of the most historic sites in the area, providing a glimpse of the city’s past and present.

Culinary Tour

If you’re a foodie, take a culinary tour of Kandy and savor the delicious local cuisine.

Cultural Show

Witness a mesmerizing cultural show depicting Kandyan culture and heritage, including traditional dance and music.

Ayurveda Spa Treatment

Relax at one of the many Ayurvedic spas in Kandy and indulge in a rejuvenating massage or treatment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kandy

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kandy, here’s a comprehensive itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Temple of the Tooth Relic, explore the Kandy Lake and Royal Palace.

Day 2: Go hiking or biking in the Knuckles Mountain Range.

Day 3: Visit the National Museum of Kandy, Bahiravokanda Vihara Buddha statue and the Udawatta Kele Sanctuary.

Day 4: Take a culinary tour and indulge in Kandy’s traditional cuisine.

Day 5: Visit the Ceylon Tea Museum, just outside Kandy city, and learn about the history and cultivation of the world-renowned Ceylon tea.

Day 6: Relax at an Ayurvedic spa and enjoy a rejuvenating massage or treatment.

Day 7: Attend a mesmerizing Kandyan cultural show.

Food and Drink Scene of Kandy

Must-Try Dishes

While in Kandy, make sure to try:

Kandyan Curries

A Kandyan curry is a must-try. It is a spicy Sri Lankan curry made with a range of meat or vegetables, served with rice or roti.

Pol Sambol

Pol Sambol is a traditional Sri Lankan condiment made with grated coconut, red onions, and chili powder.

Watalappam

For dessert, try Watalappam, a traditional Sri Lankan dessert made with coconut milk, jaggery, and spices.

Local Beverages

Ceylon Tea

Sri Lanka, formerly known as Ceylon, is renowned for its world-class tea. Enjoy a cup of pure Ceylon tea at any of Kandy’s significant tea estates.

King Coconut

King Coconut water is said to be the best natural electrolyte and thirst quencher. Enjoy a cold king coconut while exploring Kandy’s natural beauty.

Where to Stay in Kandy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

In Kandy, visitors have a range of options for accommodations. Whether you’re looking for luxury hotels, affordable hostels or a traditional Sri Lankan homestay, there is an option for everyone. Some popular accommodations in Kandy include:

Earl’s Regent Hotel

This four-star hotel is located in the heart of Kandy and offers a range of luxurious amenities and views of the surrounding hills.

Clock Inn Kandy

Clock Inn Kandy is a modern hostel in the heart of the city, offering dorms and private rooms for travelers on a budget.

Amma’s Homestay

For a more authentic Sri Lankan experience, stay at Amma’s homestay, where you’ll be welcomed into a local family’s home and have the opportunity to learn about their culture and customs.

What to Buy in Kandy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kandy has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that you can take home as a reminder of your trip, including:

Batik Fabric

Batik fabric is a traditional Sri Lankan craft, using wax to create intricate designs and colors on fabric.

Kandyan Jewellery

Kandyan jewellery is a unique design that has been passed down for centuries in the region.

Spices

Sri Lanka is renowned for its spices; bring home a range of spices including cinnamon, cardamom and curry leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Kandy

Kandy is a deeply traditional and conservative city, and visitors should be aware of the following customs:

Dress Appropriately

When visiting sacred sites or temples, visitors must dress modestly, cover exposed skin and remove footwear.

Greeting

When greeting people, Sri Lankan’s use the phrase “Ayubowan,” which translates to “long life.”

Respect for Buddhism

Buddhism is deeply ingrained in Sri Lankan culture, and visitors must show respect for Buddhist traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Kandy

The best time to visit Kandy is between December and March, when the weather is dry, and the humidity is low. This time of year is peak tourist season and can be busy, but the weather is ideal for exploring the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kandy

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn simple Sinhala phrases like thank you, please and hello.
  • Follow dress codes and cover your skin when visiting temples and sacred sites.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Buddhist traditions and customs.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public places.
  • Take pictures without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kandy

Kandy is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions like anywhere else in the world, including avoiding isolated areas and not carrying large sums of money in public.

Health in Kandy

Travelers to Kandy should ensure they have all required vaccinations before arriving. Visitors should also take sensible precautions such as drinking bottled water, using insect repellents and protecting from the sun.

Getting to Kandy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kandy

Bandaranaike International Airport, Sri Lanka’s primary airport, is located in Colombo, about three-and-a-half-hour drive from Kandy.

Bus Stations Near Kandy

Kandy’s central bus station is located next to the railway station, making it easy to access.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or private vehicle to reach Kandy from the airport or other cities in Sri Lanka.

How to Get Around in Kandy

Taxis, tuk-tuks, and buses are the primary modes of transportation in Kandy. Buses are inexpensive and widely available, while taxis and tuk-tuks are convenient for short trips and for exploring the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Kandy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kandy is a perfect base from which to explore other destinations in Sri Lanka, including:

Sigiriya

Sigiriya is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 90 minutes from Kandy. It is home to Sigiriya Rock Fortress is a must-see for its beautiful wall frescoes and the panoramic view from the summit.

Dambulla

Dambulla is another UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for cave temples and mural paintings. It’s located just over an hour from Kandy.

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, often referred to as “Little England,” is a charming town located about three hours away from Kandy. It’s known for its tea plantations, colonial architecture and cooler temperatures.

Conclusion

Kandy is a beautiful city with a rich culture, historical significance, and stunning natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in learning about Sri Lanka’s cultural heritage, exploring its natural beauty or just relaxing, Kandy has something to offer for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and explore Kandy’s best sites and experiences. Enjoy your stay!
