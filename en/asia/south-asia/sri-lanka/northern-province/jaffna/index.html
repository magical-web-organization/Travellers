Introduction to Jaffna

Jaffna is the largest city in the Northern Province of Sri Lanka, and is considered the cultural capital of the country. It is a fascinating destination for travelers who want to experience a unique mix of South Asian and Tamil culture, with a rich history dating back to the ancient kingdoms that once ruled the region. Despite being devastated by years of conflict, Jaffna has made a remarkable comeback and is now open to visitors who wish to explore its stunning natural beauty, vibrant street life, and world-renowned cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Jaffna

Here are the top attractions and landmarks that you should visit in Jaffna:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jaffna Fort: A 17th-century Dutch fort that served as a military stronghold during the colonial era. It offers sweeping views of the Jaffna Peninsula and is an excellent spot to watch the sunset.
  • Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil: An ancient Hindu temple dating back to the 10th century, renowned for its colorful and elaborate Gopurams (towers) and its annual 25-day festival.
  • Jaffna Library: A symbol of Tamil culture and history, this iconic library was destroyed during the civil war but has been restored to its original grandeur.
  • Point Pedro Lighthouse: A historic lighthouse that has been in operation since 1916 and is a popular spot for picnics and hiking.

Natural Attractions of Jaffna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kayts Causeway: A long and picturesque causeway that connects the Jaffna Peninsula to several small islands, offering breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.
  • Delft Island: A tiny island with wild horses, quaint fishing villages, and scenic natural wonders such as the Baobab tree and the Pigeon Island National Park.
  • Keerimalai Hot Springs: A natural spring that is said to have healing properties, set amidst a pristine natural environment with crystal-clear waters and rugged cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jaffna

Jaffna has several scenic routes that are ideal for hiking, biking, or just exploring on foot.

  • Nagadeepa Island Hike: This is a two-hour hike to the Nagadeepa Temple on the Nagadeepa Island, which is located off the coast of Jaffna. The temple is said to be where Lord Buddha visited in his second visit to Sri Lanka.
  • Mannar Causeway Bike Ride: A scenic 10km bike ride along the causeway to the Mannar Island, offering stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.
  • Chundikkulam Bird Sanctuary Walk: A leisurely walk through the bird sanctuary in Chundikkulam, which is home to a variety of birds and other wildlife such as crocodiles, turtles, and monkeys.

Best Things to Do in Jaffna: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Local Markets: Experience the vibrant street life of Jaffna by visiting the local markets such as the Jaffna Vegetable Market and the Jaffna Fish Market.
  • Attend a Cultural Show: Watch a traditional dance or music performance that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Jaffna.
  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn how to cook authentic Jaffna cuisine, with its unique blend of spices and flavors, by attending a cooking class organized by a local chef.
  • Join a Cycling Tour: Explore the hidden gems of Jaffna on a guided cycling tour that takes you through the city’s most scenic routes and landmarks.
  • Watch a Sunset at Casuarina Beach: Relax on the shores of this beautiful beach and enjoy the stunning views of the setting sun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jaffna

Day 1: Visit the Jaffna Fort, Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil, and the Jaffna Library.

Day 2: Take a scenic bike ride along the Mannar Causeway and explore the Mannar Island, known for its old churches and historic ruins.

Day 3: Explore Delft Island, home to wild horses and natural wonders such as the Baobab tree and the Pigeon Island National Park.

Day 4: Join a walking tour of the Chundikkulam Bird Sanctuary and spot a variety of birds and other wildlife.

Day 5: Visit the Keerimalai Hot Springs and experience the healing properties of its natural waters.

Day 6: Attend a cultural show and learn more about the rich cultural heritage of Jaffna.

Day 7: Take it easy and relax on the shores of Casuarina Beach, and watch a stunning sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Jaffna

Jaffna is famous for its unique and delicious cuisine, which is a blend of South Asian and Tamil flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab curry: A spicy and flavorful curry made with fresh crab and a variety of aromatic spices.
  • Jaffna dosai: A crispy and savory pancake made from fermented rice batter and served with coconut chutney and sambar.
  • Mutton palandy: A rich and creamy curry made with tender mutton meat, coconut milk, and spices.
  • Paniyaram: A sweet or savory fried dumpling that is a popular snack in Jaffna.

Local Beverages

  • Palmyra toddy: A mildly alcoholic drink made from the sap of the Palmyra tree, which is fermented and served fresh.
  • Jaffna mango juice: A refreshing and sweet beverage made from ripe Jaffna mangoes, which are known for their unique flavor and aroma.
  • Ceylon tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea plantations, and Jaffna is no exception. Enjoy a cup of locally grown Ceylon tea to round off your meal.

Where to Stay in Jaffna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jaffna has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses and hostels. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Jetwing Jaffna: A modern and stylish hotel located in the heart of Jaffna, with spacious rooms and a rooftop bar offering stunning views of the city.
  • The Thinnai: A boutique hotel set amidst lush greenery, with a serene atmosphere and comfortable accommodation.
  • Green Grass Hotel: A comfortable and affordable option located close to the Jaffna bus station, with clean rooms and friendly service.
  • Hotel Rolex: A popular choice for budget-conscious travelers, with basic yet comfortable rooms and a central location.

What to Buy in Jaffna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Jaffna include:

  • Palmyra products: Jaffna is famous for its handicrafts made from Palmyra palms, such as baskets, mats, and fans.
  • Spices: Jaffna is a hub for spices, and you can find a variety of high-quality spices such as cinnamon, cardamom, and clove.
  • Jaffna mangoes: These mangoes are renowned for their unique flavor and aroma, and are a popular gift item for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Jaffna

Jaffna has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in Hindu and Tamil traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as taking off your shoes before entering a temple or someone’s home, and dressing modestly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Jaffna

The best time to visit Jaffna is from August to December, as the weather is dry and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season, which can be wet and humid, making it difficult to explore the city and its surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jaffna

Do:

  • Greet locals with a smile and a nod of your head.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Dress modestly in public places.
  • Ask for permission before taking someone’s photo.

Don’t:

  • Enter a temple without taking off your shoes.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Use your left hand for eating or offering something to anyone, as it is considered unclean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jaffna

Jaffna is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it is advisable to take basic precautions such as not venturing out alone at night, avoiding dark and deserted areas, and keeping your valuables in a safe place. It is also recommended to keep your passport and other important documents in a safe place.

Health in Jaffna

Jaffna has excellent healthcare facilities, and there are several government and private hospitals in the city. However, visitors should be cautious about drinking tap water and should stick to bottled water, which is widely available.

Getting to Jaffna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Jaffna by air, train or bus.

Airports Near Jaffna

  • Jaffna International Airport: Located in Palaly, about 20km north of Jaffna city center, with daily flights from major cities in Sri Lanka.

Bus Stations Near Jaffna

  • Jaffna Bus Station: Located in the city center, with frequent buses to major cities in Sri Lanka.

Other Options

  • Train: The train from Colombo to Jaffna is a scenic and comfortable option, with several trains running daily.

How to Get Around in Jaffna

Getting around in Jaffna is easy and affordable, with several options such as:

  • Tuk-tuks: These three-wheeler taxis are a popular mode of transportation in Jaffna, and are ideal for short journeys within the city.
  • Rental bikes: Visitors can rent bikes from several shops and hotels in Jaffna, which is a great way to explore the city and its surroundings.
  • Public buses: Jaffna has a well-connected network of buses that run to all major destinations within the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Jaffna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jaffna is a great base for exploring the Northern Province of Sri Lanka, which offers several exciting destinations and excursions such as:

  • Kilinochchi: A historic town that was the former stronghold of the Tamil rebels, and offers a glimpse into the region’s turbulent history.
  • Anuradhapura: An ancient city of temples and ruins that is a must-visit destination for history buffs.
  • Trincomalee: A beautiful coastal town with stunning beaches, water sports, and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Jaffna is a hidden gem of Sri Lanka that offers visitors a unique mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. It is a destination that is not to be missed, and our comprehensive tourism guide is the perfect tool to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Jaffna. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this fascinating city that has something for everyone!
