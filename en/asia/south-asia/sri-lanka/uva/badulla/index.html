Introduction to Badulla

Badulla is a small town situated in the Uva Province of Sri Lanka, known for its picturesque natural scenery and rich cultural heritage. Badulla town is surrounded by misty mountains and the greenery of tea plantations. It is a popular tourist destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Badulla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Badulla houses many historical and culturally important attractions that will give you an insight into Sri Lanka’s fascinating past. Explore the rich cultural heritage of Badulla by:

Muthiyangana Temple

Muthiyangana Temple is known to be one of the most sacred Buddhist temples in Sri Lanka. It is believed to house one of the hair relics of Buddha. The temple is surrounded by a peaceful, natural environment which makes it a great spot to meditate and relax.

Dunhinda Falls

Dunhinda Falls is a picturesque waterfall located a few kilometers from the town of Badulla. It is about 64 meters high and cascades down in three tiers. This waterfall offers a magnificent view and is a great spot for picnicking and taking refreshing dips in the natural pools formed at the base of the falls.

Badulla Central Market

Take a stroll through the vibrant Badulla Central Market to experience the local lifestyle of the town. Shop for traditional handicrafts, fresh produce, and souvenirs of the region. It is a great place to interact with the friendly locals and taste the authentic Sri Lankan food at affordable prices.

Natural Attractions of Badulla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Badulla is known for its lush green environment, making it an ideal destination for nature-lovers. Explore the best natural attractions of the town by:

Diyaluma Waterfall

Diyaluma Waterfall is one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Sri Lanka, cascading down from a height of approximately 220 meters. The waterfall is surrounded by greenery and walking trails, making it an ideal spot for hiking, picnicking, and taking Instagram-worthy pictures.

Namunukula Mountain Range

Namunukula is a cluster of misty mountains, covered in verdant tea plantations. It is an ideal spot for trekking and it offers stunning views of the town and surrounding mountains.

Rawana Falls

Rawana Falls is a cascading waterfall with a height of approximately 25meters. It is located amidst the Udawalawe Wildlife Sanctuary, which is a home for elephants, leopards, and various bird species. A visit to this waterfall will offer an unforgettable experience blending nature, scenic beauty, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Badulla

Badulla serves as a great base for hiking and biking excursions, thanks to its natural surroundings. Here are the best routes that you can try:

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park is an ideal spot for hikers and nature enthusiasts. This protected area is thriving with biodiversity, including various bird species and mammals, including leopards. The park is home to several stunning hiking trails, including the famous World’s End Trail, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Pilkinton Estate

Pilkinton Estate is an ideal spot for mountain biking enthusiasts. This lush green property is surrounded by tea plantations and is home to a stunning trail that is perfect for an adrenaline-filled ride.

Best Things to Do in Badulla: Activities and Tours

Badulla is a perfect place to enjoy a diverse range of activities and tours. Here are a few must-do things in the town:

Tea Factory Visit

Badulla is surrounded by tea plantations, making it an ideal spot for tea factory visits. A visit to a tea factory will offer an insight into how tea is harvested, processed, and packed. It is also an excellent opportunity to taste various teas and purchase them as souvenirs.

White Water Rafting

Enjoy the thrill of white water rafting in the rapids of the Kelani River, located about an hour’s drive from Badulla. You will be surrounded by verdant forests and the gushing waters of the river, making it an adrenaline-pumping experience.

Wildlife Safari

Travel to Udawalawe Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to various bird species and animals, including elephants and leopards. A wildlife safari in Udawalawe is perfect for those who want to get away from the bustle of Badulla and connect with nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Badulla

Here is a week-long itinerary for those who want to experience the best of Badulla:

Day 1:

Start your day with a visit to Muthiyangana Temple. Next, travel to Kandyan Art Association and Cultural Centre to experience Sri Lanka’s rich culture and art. Finish your day with a culinary adventure at the Badulla Central Market.

Day 2:

Visit Dunhinda Falls, Rawana Falls and take a scenic drive through the Namunukula mountain range.

Day 3:

Explore the nature trails of the Horton Plains National Park and enjoy nearby waterfalls such as Bambarakanda Falls.

Day 4:

Take a break from nature and indulge in a tea factory tour in the tea plantations. Spend the rest of the day experiencing the local lifestyles of Badulla on leisure strolls around town.

Day 5:

Enjoy a white water rafting experience in the nearby Kelani River.

Day 6:

Visit the Udawalawe Wildlife Sanctuary for a wildlife safari.

Day 7:

Rest and relax at your accommodation and reminisce about your experiences in Badulla.

Food and Drink Scene of Badulla

Badulla offers an array of local dishes and beverages that boasts a unique Sri Lankan flavor. Be sure to try these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Kottu Roti

Kottu Roti is a Sri Lankan popular street food made of flatbread, vegetables, and various meats like chicken or beef. It is a must-try dish in Badulla and will surely satisfy your taste buds.

Hoppers

Hoppers is a crispy pancake that is popular for breakfast in Sri Lanka. You can find various types of hoppers like egg hoppers, plain hoppers, and kottu hoppers in almost every local restaurant or street vendor in Badulla.

Local Beverages

Tea

Badulla is surrounded by tea plantations, so you can be sure that the tea served here is fresh and top-notch. Opt for a cup of milk tea, which is popular amongst the locals.

Arrack

Arrack is a popular liquor in Sri Lanka, made from fermented coconut nectar. It is a must-try for those looking to experience the local beverage scene in Badulla.

Where to Stay in Badulla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Badulla has a range of accommodation options, catering to travellers of all budgets. Here are the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to stay in Badulla:

Hotel Oliyana

Hotel Oliyana is one of the best hotels in Badulla, offering luxurious rooms, a pleasant ambience and all the modern amenities you might need during your stay. The hotel is located close to the town center and is ideal for those who prefer to stay in central locations.

Uva Bungalow

For those who prefer a more tranquil environment, Uva Bungalow is a perfect choice. It is located amidst the tea plantations and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Green view Holiday Resort

Green view Holiday Resort is a budget-friendly option situated a few kilometers from the town center. It is surrounded by greenery and is ideal for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the town.

What to Buy in Badulla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Badulla offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts to bring back home. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy in Badulla:

Handmade Crafts and Accessories

Badulla is home to a range of skilled craftsmen who produce intricate items ranging from pottery and batik clothing to handmade soap and intricate jewelry.

Tea and Spices

With Badulla’s close proximity to tea plantations, you can be sure that you will find top-quality tea and spices that you can take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Badulla

In Badulla, culture and traditions are deeply rooted in the Buddhist religion. The locals are friendly and hospitable, making it easy for visitors to blend in. Ensure that you dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites, and respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Badulla

The best time to visit Badulla is from April to September, as it is the dry season. The weather during these months is pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the town’s natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Badulla

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Badulla:

Do:

  • Take your shoes off when entering Buddhist temples and other religious sites.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local religion and customs.
  • Use drugs or consume excessive alcohol.
  • Litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Badulla

Badulla is a relatively safe town for visitors, with friendly and hospitable locals. However, it’s always advisable to take precautionary measures while travelling. Avoid carrying valuables and cash, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Badulla

Health facilities in Badulla are minimal, and it is advisable to carry medications such as painkillers and antiseptics. Ensure that you drink only boiled or bottled water and avoid street food that is not cooked or prepared hygienically.

Getting to Badulla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Badulla

The nearest airport to Badulla is Colombo’s Bandaranaike International Airport, which is about 226 kilometers away. From there, you can travel to Badulla by bus or train.

Bus Stations Near Badulla

Badulla is well-connected with other towns and cities in Sri Lanka by buses. A few major bus stations in the town are Badulla Bus Station, Bandarawela Bus Station, and Hali Ela Bus Station.

Other Options

Tuk-tuk or three-wheeler is a popular mode of transportation around Badulla. You can also rent a bike or car from rental agencies in town.

How to Get Around in Badulla

The best way to explore Badulla is by foot, especially when exploring the natural attractions in the town. Tuk-tuks or three-wheelers are also a popular mode of transportation for short trips around the town.

Where to Go Next from Badulla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Badulla serves as a great base for exploring the nearby towns and cities that offer a unique experience. Here are the best nearby destinations:

Ella

Ella is a small town blessed with natural beauty, known for its hiking trails and stunning views of the mountains. It is located about 16 kilometers from Badulla and is accessible by car or train.

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a beautiful town situated in the central province of Sri Lanka. It is home to many iconic landmarks such as the tea plantations and Lovers Leap waterfall. It is accessible by train or car and is a short drive from Badulla.

Conclusion

Badulla is a small town located in the Uva Province of Sri Lanka, known for its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. Exploring the historical, cultural and natural attractions, trying out diverse activities and tours, enjoying the local food, and respecting the customs and traditions of the locals will offer an unforgettable, immersive experience of this beautiful town.
