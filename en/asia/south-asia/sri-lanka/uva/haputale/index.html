Introduction to Haputale

Nestled in the Uva Province of Sri Lanka, Haputale is a picturesque hill town that boasts breathtaking scenery and a rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by tea plantations and mountains, this idyllic town is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a virtual tour of Haputale, covering everything from the best things to do and places to visit to the local culture and customs. Whether you’re planning to visit for a few days or an extended stay, this guide has everything you need to make the most of your Haputale experience.

List of Places to Visit in Haputale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dambatenne Tea Factory: Located on a picturesque hilltop, this 19th-century tea factory offers a fascinating insight into the history of tea production in Sri Lanka.

  2. Lipton’s Seat: A viewpoint named after the famous tea entrepreneur, Sir Thomas Lipton. It offers a panoramic view of the town and surrounding mountains.

  3. Adisham Bungalow: Built in the early 20th century, this stunning colonial-era mansion now serves as a monastery and museum.

Natural Attractions of Haputale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Horton Plains National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this national park boasts stunning vistas, waterfalls, and endemic flora and fauna. It is a must-visit for hikers and nature lovers.

  2. Diyaluma Falls: Located around 40 km from Haputale, this cascading waterfall is the second-highest in Sri Lanka and boasts breathtaking views.

  3. Kirigalpoththa Mountain: With an altitude of over 2,300m, this mountain offers unmatched views of the landscape surrounding Haputale.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haputale

  1. Haputale to Lipton’s Seat: This 7 km hike takes you through picturesque tea plantations and culminates in stunning views from the famous Lipton’s Seat viewpoint.

  2. Thangamalai Forest Reserve: This 6 km hike takes you through a lush forest reserve and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  3. Ella Rock: This 10 km hike takes you through the stunning Ella Gap to the top of Ella Rock, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Haputale: Activities and Tours

  1. Tea plantation tours: Take a tour of a tea plantation and learn about the history of tea production in Sri Lanka.

  2. White-water rafting: Experience the thrills of white-water rafting on the Kelani River.

  3. Birdwatching: With its diverse flora and fauna, Haputale is a haven for birdwatchers. Take a guided tour to spot some of the region’s rare and exotic bird species.

Food and Drink Scene of Haputale

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kottu Roti: A popular Sri Lankan street food made with shredded roti, vegetables, and meat.

  2. Hoppers: A type of Sri Lankan pancake made with rice flour and coconut milk.

  3. String Hoppers: A type of noodle made with rice flour and shaped into small “nests”.

Local Beverages

  1. Ceylon tea: Haputale, being a part of the tea-producing region, offers some of the best Ceylon tea in Sri Lanka.

  2. Arrack: A popular local liquor made from fermented coconut sap.

  3. King Coconut: A refreshing drink made from the water of young coconuts.

Where to Stay in Haputale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Misty Mountains: A boutique hotel nestled amidst lush greenery, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Colombus Grand Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the town.

  3. Grand Peak Haputale: A guesthouse located in the tea estates, offering a tranquil and serene atmosphere.

What to Buy in Haputale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tea: Take home some of the best Ceylon tea produced in the region.

  2. Handicrafts: Shop for handmade traditional Sri Lankan handicrafts.

  3. Spices: Take home some of the locally grown spices, such as cinnamon and turmeric.

Local Culture and Customs of Haputale

Haputale is a predominantly Buddhist town and visitors are expected to respect the local religious customs and traditions. It is customary to remove shoes and hats when entering temples and other religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Haputale

The best time to visit Haputale is between December and February, when the weather is cool and dry. However, Haputale can be visited throughout the year, as the town enjoys a mild climate throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haputale

Do:

  1. Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.

  2. Respect the local customs and traditions.

  3. Try the local cuisine and sample some of the region’s famed tea.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect religious customs and traditions.

  2. Wear immodest clothing in public areas.

  3. Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haputale

Haputale is a safe and peaceful town. However, visitors are advised to exercise caution when exploring the natural surroundings, as there may be unforeseen hazards such as wild animals or slippery terrain.

Health in Haputale

Visitors are advised to carry mosquito repellent and be up to date with their vaccinations. In case of an emergency, there are medical facilities available in the town.

Getting to Haputale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haputale

The nearest airport to Haputale is Bandaranaike International Airport, located around 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near Haputale

The Haputale bus station is located in the town center and offers connections to other major cities in Sri Lanka.

Other Options

Private taxis can also be hired to reach Haputale.

How to Get Around in Haputale

The best way to explore Haputale is on foot. Tuk-tuks and private taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Haputale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ella: Another hill town located around 15 km from Haputale, famous for its stunning views, tea plantations, and hiking trails.

  2. Yala National Park: A popular national park located around 175 km from Haputale, known for its diverse wildlife and bird species.

  3. Galle: Located around 220 km from Haputale, this historic city boasts stunning colonial-era architecture and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Haputale is a hidden gem in Sri Lanka, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Haputale has something to offer everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your Haputale adventure and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
