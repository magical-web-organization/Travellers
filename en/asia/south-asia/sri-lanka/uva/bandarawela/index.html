Introduction to Bandarawela

Bandarawela is a scenic town located in the Uva Province of Sri Lanka. Its picturesque landscapes and pleasant weather make it a popular destination for travellers looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of Sri Lanka’s major cities. The town is nestled amidst lush green hills and surrounded by tea plantations, which are a prominent feature of the region. Bandarawela offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature, making it an attractive destination for tourists of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Bandarawela

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Adisham Bungalow: This is an English country-style mansion built in the early 1900s by Sir Thomas Villiers. It was later converted into a monastery and is now open to visitors to explore its stunning gardens, chapel and museum.
  • Dowa Rock Temple: This is a unique Buddhist temple built inside a natural cave. It is located in the outskirts of Bandarawela and is home to a number of ancient Buddhist artifacts.
  • Bambaragala Archeological Site: This is an archeological site located on a picturesque hilltop. It offers a panoramic view of the surrounding countryside and features a number of ancient remains, including rock inscriptions and ruins of ancient buildings.

Natural Attractions of Bandarawela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ella Gap: This is a stunning valley located in the nearby town of Ella. It offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding hills and tea plantations. The valley is also home to a number of popular hiking trails and trekking routes.
  • Bogoda Wooden Bridge: This is a unique wooden bridge built in the 16th century, located in the nearby town of Bogoda. It is believed to be the oldest surviving wooden bridge in Sri Lanka and offers a glimpse into the country’s early engineering feats.
  • Lipton’s Seat: This is a viewpoint located at the peak of the Poonagala hill. It offers a panoramic view of the surrounding tea estates and lush green hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bandarawela

Bandarawela is a paradise for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Haputale to Ella: This is a popular hiking trail that spans over 10 km, and takes around 4 to 5 hours to complete. It offers breathtaking views of tea plantations, waterfalls, and lush green hills.
  • Uva-Paranagama Trail: This is a rugged, yet rewarding trek that spans over 15 km, and takes around 8 to 9 hours to complete. It takes hikers through dense forests, waterfalls, and scenic valleys.
  • Bandarawela to Demodara Loop: This is a popular biking route, spanning over 30 km, and takes around 5 to 6 hours to complete. It takes bikers through picturesque tea estates, waterfalls, and breathtaking valleys.

Best Things to Do in Bandarawela: Activities and Tours

  • Tea Plantation Tours: Visit the nearby tea plantations and learn about the process of tea-making. Witness breathtaking landscapes and sample freshly brewed tea straight from the source.
  • River Rafting: Take a thrilling river rafting tour down the nearby Kelani River, which is known for its rapids and scenic views.
  • Bird Watching: Bandarawela is home to a number of exotic bird species, including the Sri Lankan Whistling Thrush and the Grey-headed Canary-flycatcher. Take a guided bird watching tour and witness these feathered beauties in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bandarawela

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Bandarawela:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s cultural attractions, including Adisham Bungalow, Dowa Rock Temple, and Bambaragala Archeological Site.
  • Day 2: Take a hike to Lipton’s Seat and witness the stunning tea plantations and breathtaking views along the way.
  • Day 3: Bike through the scenic tea estates and surrounding valleys.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Ella and witness the stunning Ella Gap and popular hiking trails.
  • Day 5: Take a river rafting tour down the Kelani River and witness the rapids and scenic landscapes.
  • Day 6: Visit the nearby Horton Plains National Park and witness the stunning natural landscapes, including waterfalls and scenic valleys.
  • Day 7: Spend the day leisurely exploring the town’s local markets, sampling local delicacies and picking up souvenirs to take back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Bandarawela

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kottu Roti: This is a popular Sri Lankan street food that consists of shredded roti bread, mixed with vegetables, and meat or fish.
  • Hoppers: This is a traditional Sri Lankan breakfast food made from rice flour and coconut milk. It is served with sambal and other accompaniments.
  • String Hoppers: This is another popular breakfast food made from steamed rice noodles, served with a variety of curries and sambals.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Bandarawela is famous for its tea plantations, and a trip to the town is incomplete without sampling some of the local tea.
  • King Coconut Water: This is a refreshing and healthy drink made from the water of young coconuts.

Where to Stay in Bandarawela: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bandarawela Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of Bandarawela and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and tea plantations.
  • Green View Holiday Resort: This budget friendly resort is located amidst scenic tea estates and offers a peaceful and tranquil environment.
  • Ella Lodge: This cozy guesthouse is located in the nearby town of Ella and offers stunning views of the Ella Gap and surrounding valleys.

What to Buy in Bandarawela: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea plantations, and Bandarawela is no exception. Sample some of the local tea blends and take back home some to enjoy later.
  • Handicrafts: Bandarawela is famous for its handcrafted pottery, woodwork, and handloom textiles. Check out the local markets for some unique finds.

Local Culture and Customs of Bandarawela

Bandarawela is a predominantly Buddhist town, and visitors are advised to dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Shoes and headwear should be removed before entering religious establishments. Tipping is not expected, but it is considered a sign of goodwill.

Best Time to Visit Bandarawela

The best time to visit Bandarawela is between December and March when the weather is cool and pleasant. The town experiences light rainfall throughout the year, but heavy rains are expected between April and June.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bandarawela

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Sample the local tea blends and delicacies.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Use drugs or engage in any illegal activities.
  • Disrespect religious customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bandarawela

Bandarawela is considered safe for tourists, but visitors are advised to be cautious when visiting remote areas, especially at night. Petty theft is not uncommon, and it is advised to keep an eye on your belongings at all times.

Health in Bandarawela

The tap water in Bandarawela is not considered safe for drinking. It is advised to drink bottled water, which is readily available at most stores and supermarkets. Visitors are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever, by wearing protective clothing and using mosquito repellents.

Getting to Bandarawela: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bandarawela

The closest international airport to Bandarawela is the Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Bandarawela, which is around 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bandarawela

Bandarawela is well-connected by bus to all major cities and towns in Sri Lanka. The town’s main bus station is located in the centre of the town and offers regular services to popular destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas. Tuk-tuks are also a popular mode of transportation for getting around the town.

How to Get Around in Bandarawela

Bandarawela is a small town, and most attractions can be explored on foot. Tuk-tuks and taxis are also readily available for getting around the town.

Where to Go Next from Bandarawela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ella: This picturesque town is located just a short drive away from Bandarawela and is known for its stunning landscapes and popular hiking trails.
  • Haputale: This hill town is located just a short distance from Bandarawela and is known for its scenic tea plantations and stunning views.
  • Udawalawe National Park: This wildlife reserve is located in close proximity to Bandarawela and offers a chance to witness the country’s exotic wildlife, including elephants and leopards.

Conclusion

Bandarawela is a hidden gem in Sri Lanka that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to this scenic town and make the most of your time here. So pack your bags and get ready to explore the lush green hills, stunning tea plantations, and exotic wildlife of Bandarawela.
