Introduction to Beragala

Beragala is a tiny rural town tucked away in the highlands of Uva province in Sri Lanka. It is known for its scenic views, lush greenery, and ancient cultural heritage. Surrounded by misty mountains and verdant tea plantations, Beragala is a unique and tranquil destination, and a must-visit for nature and adventure lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Beragala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Beragala boasts an impressive history and culture that date back centuries. Here are some notable heritage sites to visit:

  • Bibilegama Purana Raja Maha Viharaya: This ancient temple, located in the village of Bibilegama, features intricate carvings and murals depicting Buddhist mythology. The main highlight is a massive golden statue of Buddha.

  • Kivulgala Aranya Senasanaya: A serene forest monastery with a cave temple, Kivulgala Aranya Senasanaya is dedicated to Buddhist meditation practice. Visitors can join in the daily activities and learn about the peaceful way of life practiced by the monks.

  • Ravana Ella Falls: This majestic waterfall is named after the mythical demon king Ravana who is believed to have lived in the nearby caves. The waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and offers stunning views.

Natural Attractions of Beragala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beragala is a nature lover’s paradise, with its breathtaking landscapes and rich biodiversity. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Diyaluma Falls: The second-highest waterfall in Sri Lanka, Diyaluma Falls is a natural wonder that cascades down 220 meters into a natural pool. Visitors can swim in the pool and hike to the top of the waterfall for panoramic views.

  • Pahanthuda Ella Falls: The lesser-known Pahanthuda Ella Falls is a hidden gem tucked away in a remote forest. The waterfall is accessed only by a scenic hiking trail and offers a refreshing respite from the heat.

  • Beragalathanna Peak: The highest point in Beragala, Beragalathanna Peak offers awe-inspiring views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is a popular spot for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beragala

Beragala is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with its many hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Here are some popular routes:

  • Beragala to Haputale: This scenic hike takes you through lush tea plantations, forest reserves, and picturesque mountain villages, with stunning views of the Ella Gap.

  • Beragala to Bambarakanda Falls: This challenging hike takes you to the highest waterfall in Sri Lanka, through dense forests, river crossings, and steep climbs. The reward is a spectacular view of the waterfall and its surroundings.

  • Beragala to Nonagama: This easy bike ride takes you through rural villages and paddy fields, with stunning views of the Knuckles Mountain Range and the Victoria Reservoir.

Best Things to Do in Beragala: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, Beragala offers a variety of activities and tours, including:

  • Tea estate tours: Beragala is known for its world-famous Ceylon tea plantations. Visitors can take a tour of a tea factory, learn about the tea-making process, and sample different varieties.

  • Elephant safaris: Nearby Udawalawe National Park is home to a large population of elephants. Visitors can take a jeep safari and observe these gentle giants in their natural habitat.

  • Spiritual retreats: Beragala’s serene and peaceful environment lends itself well to meditation and yoga retreats. Visitors can take part in a retreat led by experienced instructors and learn about a range of mindfulness practices.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beragala

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Beragala:

  • Day 1-2: Arrive in Beragala, acclimatize to the weather, and explore the town.
  • Day 3-4: Hike to Diyaluma Falls and explore the surrounding area.
  • Day 5-6: Take a tea estate tour and visit Bibilegama Purana Raja Maha Viharaya.
  • Day 7: Depart Beragala.

Food and Drink Scene of Beragala

Must-Try Dishes

Beragala’s cuisine is influenced by its cultural and geographical diversity. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Hoppers: Sri Lanka’s most famous breakfast dish, hoppers are thin, crispy rice flour pancakes served with a range of curries and chutneys.

  • Kottu roti: A popular street food, kottu roti is made by stir-frying diced roti bread with vegetables, eggs, and meat.

  • Fish curry: Beragala is close to the coast, and seafood is a staple in the local diet. Try their spicy and flavorful fish curry with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Ceylon tea: Beragala is renowned for its tea plantations, and a cup of freshly brewed Ceylon tea is a must-try.
  • Toddy: This fermented palm tree sap is a popular alcoholic beverage made in Sri Lanka.
  • King coconut water: A refreshing and hydrating drink made from the juice of the tender coconut.

Where to Stay in Beragala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beragala offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Samira Guest House: Offering comfortable rooms and friendly service, Samira Guest House is a favorite among budget travelers.

  • Jetwing Kaduruketha: A luxury eco-resort located in a secluded valley, Jetwing Kaduruketha features tastefully designed villas and offers a range of wellness activities.

  • Greenhill Cottage: A cozy guesthouse with stunning views of the surrounding mountains, Greenhill Cottage is ideal for those seeking a peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Beragala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Beragala’s unique culture and natural beauty offer several souvenir options for visitors to take home. Here are some popular choices:

  • Tea: Beragala’s tea plantations produce some of the finest Ceylon tea in the world. Bring home a bag or two of your favorite variety.

  • Handicrafts: Beragala is famous for its intricate wood carvings and brassware. Look out for traditional masks, sculptures, and potteries.

  • Spices: Sri Lanka is known for its fragrant and flavorful spices. Stock up on cinnamon, cardamom, pepper, and other popular varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Beragala

Beragala’s culture is deeply rooted in Buddhism, and the locals follow traditional customs and practices. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and be respectful of religious sites and customs. Tipping is not customary in Sri Lanka, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Beragala

Beragala’s climate is tropical, with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 30°C throughout the year. The best time to visit is from December to April when the weather is dry and cool. It is advisable to avoid the monsoon season from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beragala

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and soak up the culture.
  • Bargain when shopping at street markets.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Disrespect religious sites.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Buy endangered animal products.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beragala

Beragala is a safe and peaceful destination, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take the usual precautions against pickpocketing and theft. Avoid walking alone at night and do not leave valuables unattended.

Health in Beragala

Beragala has a few basic medical facilities, but serious medical cases might require travel to a larger city. It is advisable to carry basic first aid supplies, insect repellents, and sunscreen. Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or boiled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Beragala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beragala

The nearest airport to Beragala is Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, which is around 200 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach Beragala.

Bus Stations Near Beragala

Beragala is well-connected to major cities in Sri Lanka by bus. The nearest bus station is in the town of Haputale, around 20 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or local bus to reach Beragala.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Beragala. The journey from Colombo takes around 5-6 hours, depending on the traffic and road conditions.

How to Get Around in Beragala

Beragala is a small town, and visitors can explore it on foot or by bicycle. Tuk-tuks are also available for short distances. To visit nearby attractions or go on a hiking trail, visitors can hire a taxi or join a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Beragala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beragala is well-connected to several nearby destinations of tourist interest. Here are some popular choices:

  • Ella: A picturesque town known for its hiking trails and stunning views of Ella Gap.

  • Haputale: A scenic hill town known for its tea plantations and nature trails.

  • Udawalawe National Park: Home to a large population of elephants and other wildlife, Udawalawe National Park is a popular destination for wildlife safaris.

Conclusion

Beragala is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. From its cultural heritage to scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures, Beragala offers a unique and unforgettable experience to visitors. Plan your trip now, and immerse yourself in the tranquility of this charming town.
