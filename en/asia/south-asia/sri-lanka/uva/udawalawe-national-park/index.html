Introduction to Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe National Park is situated in the Uva Province of Sri Lanka, which is located in the southern region of the nation. Covering an area of 308 square kilometers, this park is a famous tourist destination that promises an amazing wildlife experience. Founded in 1972, this park is also a significant animal sanctuary in Sri Lanka. Udawalawe has a rich variety of flora and fauna, which makes it a one-of-its-kind venture.

Local Culture and Customs of Udawalawe National Park

The local people of Udawalawe National Park have a rich cultural heritage that is definitely a sight to behold. The customs and traditions of Udawalawe are heavily influenced by the Sinhalese culture of the region.

Best Time to Visit Udawalawe National Park

The best time to visit the park is between the months of January and March, or from July to September, as the weather is usually dry and mild. This allows visitors to see the wildlife and natural beauty of the park more easily.

Do’s and Don’ts of Udawalawe National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the wildlife and natural environment.
  2. Follow the rules and regulations of the park.

Don’t:

  1. Feed the animals.
  2. Disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe National Park is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors need to be aware of the wild animals in the region, especially the elephants.

Health in Udawalawe National Park

Visitors are suggested to keep their medical kits with them wherever they go. It is also advised to drink bottled water to avoid any kind of water-borne disease.
