Introduction to Unawatuna

Unawatuna is a coastal town located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka. It is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and exciting outdoor adventure opportunities. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore historical sites, or indulge in delicious local cuisine, Unawatuna has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Unawatuna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Unawatuna is home to several museums and historical sites, including the Japanese Peace Pagoda, which offers a breathtaking view of the ocean and the town. Another popular attraction is the Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 16th century.

Natural Attractions of Unawatuna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Unawatuna is a nature lover’s paradise. From snorkeling and scuba diving to hiking and biking, there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Don’t miss out on a visit to the Yatagala Raja Maha Viharaya, an ancient Buddhist temple located in the hills just outside Unawatuna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Unawatuna

For the adventurous traveler, Unawatuna offers many opportunities to explore the surrounding hills and countryside by foot or bicycle. The Rumassala Mountain Hike is a popular trail that offers breathtaking views of the coastline and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Unawatuna: Activities and Tours

From surfing lessons to whale watching tours, Unawatuna has a range of exciting activities to keep visitors entertained. For a unique experience, don’t miss out on a visit to the Turtle Hatchery to see the baby turtles and learn about the conservation efforts in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Unawatuna

Day 1: Visit the Galle Fort and explore the historical sites of the town. Day 2: Spend a relaxing day on the beach and try some local seafood. Day 3: Take a hiking tour of the Rumassala mountain and enjoy the scenic views. Day 4: Learn about Sri Lankan history and culture at the National Museum in Colombo. Day 5: Take a whale watching tour or go snorkeling to see the coral reefs. Day 6: Visit a tea plantation in the hills near Unawatuna and enjoy the scenic views. Day 7: Relax on the beach and soak up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Unawatuna

Must-Try Dishes

Unawatuna is famous for its seafood, with a wide variety of fish and seafood dishes available at local restaurants. Don’t miss out on trying the Sri Lankan rice and curry, a staple dish that is served with a variety of curries and side dishes.

Local Beverages

The local beer, Lion Lager, is a popular choice among tourists and locals alike. Other popular beverages include Ceylon tea and fresh coconut water, which is available at many roadside stands.

Where to Stay in Unawatuna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Unawatuna to suit every budget and travel style. From luxurious beachside resorts to affordable hostels and guesthouses, there is something for every traveler.

What to Buy in Unawatuna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs to bring back from Unawatuna include handmade jewelry, textiles, and wooden carvings. Don’t forget to try the local spices, such as cinnamon and cardamom, which are grown in the nearby hills.

Local Culture and Customs of Unawatuna

The local culture in Unawatuna is heavily influenced by the Buddhist religion, with several ancient temples and monuments located throughout the town. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs when visiting these sites.

Best Time to Visit Unawatuna

The best time to visit Unawatuna is from December to April, when the weather is dry and sunny. The monsoon season runs from May to November, so visitors should plan accordingly if they plan to visit during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Unawatuna

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors.
  • Try the local cuisine and support small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the sea during the monsoon season, when the currents can be dangerous.
  • Litter or disrespect the surrounding natural environment.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Unawatuna

Unawatuna is generally a safe place for travelers, but visitors should take the usual precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. It’s also a good idea to avoid traveling alone at night and to use reputable transportation services.

Health in Unawatuna

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations before traveling to Sri Lanka. Travelers should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and malaria, and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Unawatuna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Unawatuna

The closest airport to Unawatuna is the Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, which is about a three-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Unawatuna

There are several bus stations near Unawatuna, including the Galle Bus Station and the Matara Bus Station.

Other Options

Taxis and tuk-tuks are readily available in Unawatuna, and visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Unawatuna

Walking and biking are popular ways to get around Unawatuna, especially for shorter distances. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also readily available for longer trips and transportation to nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Unawatuna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Unawatuna, visitors can easily explore nearby towns and attractions, such as the tea plantations in the nearby hills or the historical city of Galle. Visitors can also take a day trip to nearby towns such as Mirissa, which is known for its beautiful beaches and whale watching tours.

Conclusion

Unawatuna is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of relaxation and adventure. With its beautiful beaches, rich history, and exciting outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in this charming coastal town.
