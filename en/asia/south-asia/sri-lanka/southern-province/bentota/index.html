Introduction to Bentota

Bentota is a coastal town located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka. With its breathtaking beaches, scenic views, and unique cultural heritage, Bentota is a popular destination for tourists seeking adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion. This comprehensive travel guide highlights the top attractions, activities, accommodations, and local customs in Bentota, helping visitors plan their perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Bentota

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bentota is steeped in cultural and historical significance, with several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich past. Some of the must-visit attractions in Bentota include the following:

  • Brief Garden: a beautifully landscaped garden featuring a collection of antiques, sculptures, and ornaments from around the world.
  • Kande Vihare Temple: a Buddhist temple located in Aluthgama, decorated with vibrant paintings and carvings and featuring a large Buddha statue.
  • Galapata Vihara: another Buddhist temple situated in Induruwa, featuring an intricately carved standing Buddha statue and a beautiful ocean view.
  • Richmond Castle: a colonial mansion built in the early 1900s, featuring ornate architecture and original furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Bentota: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bentota is known for its natural beauty, characterized by lush greenery, rolling hills, and stunning beaches. Here are some of the top natural attractions and outdoor activities in Bentota:

  • Bentota Beach: a beautiful stretch of golden sand, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and watersports.
  • Kanneliya Forest Reserve: a protected rainforest, rich in biodiversity and home to several endemic species of flora and fauna.
  • Madu River: ideal for boat rides and bird watching, this river is teeming with mangrove forests and exotic wildlife.
  • Brief Estate: a stunning plantation featuring coconut groves, rice paddies, and rubber trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bentota

Bentota offers several hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Lunuganga Estate Trail: a beautiful trail that takes you through the gardens of Geoffrey Bawa’s former estate.
  • Dedduwa Lake Trail: a scenic route around Dedduwa Lake, with stunning views of the countryside.
  • Kanneliya Forest Trail: a challenging hike through the rainforest, offering beautiful scenery and an opportunity to spot exotic wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Bentota: Activities and Tours

Aside from the cultural and natural attractions, Bentota offers several activities and guided tours that allow visitors to experience the best of the region. Some of the best things to do in Bentota include:

  • Watersports: Bentota’s beaches are renowned for their watersports facilities, with activities like jet skiing, wakeboarding, and kayaking on offer.
  • River Safari: hop on a boat and explore the Madu River, spotting wildlife and relaxing in the tranquil surroundings.
  • Turtle Hatchery: learn about the conservation efforts to protect Sri Lanka’s sea turtle population and see baby turtles up close.
  • Ayurveda Spa: rejuvenate your mind and body with a traditional Ayurvedic massage or therapy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bentota

A week in Bentota allows visitors to experience everything the region has to offer. Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Bentota:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Bentota, relax on the beach, and explore the town.
  • Day 2: Visit Brief Garden and Kande Vihare Temple in the morning, followed by an afternoon of watersports on Bentota Beach.
  • Day 3: Go on a river safari on Madu River, followed by a visit to a turtle hatchery.
  • Day 4: Explore the Kanneliya Forest Reserve, with a guide, and have lunch at Brief Estate.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to Galle, a charming coastal town known for its Dutch colonial architecture and UNESCO World Heritage sites.
  • Day 6: Indulge in an Ayurvedic massage and relax on the beach.
  • Day 7: Depart from Bentota.

Food and Drink Scene of Bentota

Sri Lankan cuisine is known for its bold flavors, spices, and fresh seafood. Bentota offers several local dishes and beverages that every foodie must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rice and Curry: a staple dish in Sri Lanka, featuring fragrant rice, a variety of curries, pickles, and papadams.
  • Kottu Roti: a popular street food, made of shredded roti, vegetables, and protein of choice, mixed with spices.
  • Seafood: Bentota’s seafood scene is a must-try, with dishes like crab curry, prawn curry, and fish cutlets.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: a fermented drink made from the sap of coconut flowers, with a sweet and sour taste.
  • King Coconut Water: a refreshing drink that is perfect for cooling off on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Bentota: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bentota offers a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the top hotels in Bentota include:

  • Saman Villas: a luxurious boutique hotel with stunning ocean views, private pools, and elegant suites.
  • The River House: a beautiful hotel set on the Madu River, featuring lush gardens, charming villas, and a tranquil atmosphere.
  • AVANI Bentota Resort and Spa: a family-friendly hotel with direct access to Bentota Beach and several amenities, including a pool and spa.

What to Buy in Bentota: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bentota offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or keepsakes. Some of the top items include:

  • Batik Clothing: a traditional Sri Lankan textile, known for its intricate designs and bold colors.
  • Cinnamon: Sri Lanka is known for its high-quality cinnamon, which is a must-buy for any food lover.
  • Spices: Sri Lankan spices are renowned for their unique flavors and aromas, making them an ideal souvenir for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Bentota

Bentota is a predominantly Buddhist community with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples or public places. It is also customary to remove shoes when entering some homes, temples, or mosques.

Best Time to Visit Bentota

Bentota’s weather is warm and humid throughout the year, with two distinct monsoon seasons. The best time to visit Bentota is between December and March when the rainfall is low, and the weather is pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bentota

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting public places.
  • Drink bottled water, especially if you have a sensitive stomach.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Buddhist traditions or customs.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Consume street food without checking its hygiene and preparation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bentota

Bentota is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding secluded areas at night and not carrying large sums of money or valuables.

Health in Bentota

Bentota is a relatively healthy destination, with few health risks for travelers. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and use sunscreen to prevent sunburn.

Getting to Bentota: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bentota is located approximately 60 km south of Sri Lanka’s capital, Colombo. Visitors can reach Bentota by air, land, or sea.

Airports Near Bentota

  • Colombo International Airport (CMB): located approximately 100 km from Bentota, this airport is the closest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Bentota

  • Bentota Bus Station: located in the town center, it offers connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Taxi: visitors can hire a taxi from Colombo, Galle, or other nearby towns and cities.
  • Train: the nearest railway station is Aluthgama, which is an hour south from Colombo.

How to Get Around in Bentota

Bentota is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or bicycle. Visitors can also hire a tuk-tuk or taxi for short trips, or a car or scooter for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Bentota: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bentota is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations in Sri Lanka. Some of the most popular nearby destinations include:

  • Galle: a charming coastal town known for its Dutch colonial architecture and UNESCO World Heritage sites.
  • Unawatuna: a stunning beach town with a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Mirissa: a picturesque coastal town famous for its whale watching tours and beachfront restaurants.

Conclusion

Bentota is a beautiful and vibrant destination that offers something for every type of traveler. From cultural attractions to natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Bentota is a must-visit destination in Sri Lanka. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Bentota and experience the best this region has to offer.
