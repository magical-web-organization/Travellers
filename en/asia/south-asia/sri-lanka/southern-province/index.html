Introduction to Southern Province

Located in the southern part of Sri Lanka, Southern Province is a popular destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, nature, and adventure. The region is home to several top-rated tourist attractions, including ancient temples, historical landmarks, pristine beaches, and lush greenery. Southern Province is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the beauty and diversity of Sri Lanka.

Southern Province is home to a vast array of attractions that cater to every type of traveler. Here are some of the top places to visit in Southern Province:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kataragama Temple
  • Dutch Fort, Matara
  • Yatala Wehera, Tissamaharama
  • Mulgirigala Rock Temple

Best Things to Do in Southern Province: Activities and Tours

Southern Province has no shortage of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Province

Here’s an easy-to-follow itinerary for a one week trip to Southern Province:

Day 1: Explore Galle Fort and then relax on the Unawatuna Beach.

Day 2: Visit the Yala National Park for a wildlife safari experience.

Day 3: Enjoy the sunrise atop the Little Adam’s Peak in Ella.

Day 4: Head to the Udawalawe Elephant Orphanage and then stroll through the Udawalawe National Park.

Day 5: Go surfing at Mirissa Beach and then take a relaxing Ayurveda massage.

Day 6: Visit Bundala National Park for bird watching experience and then relax at Rekawa Beach.

Day 7: Visit Weligama Bay and go whale watching.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Province

Sri Lanka is a predominantly Buddhist country, and it is essential to respect local customs and traditions. Make sure to dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection, especially in rural areas.

Best Time to Visit Southern Province

The best time to visit Southern Province is from December to March when the weather is dry and sunny. This is also the high season for tourism, so expect crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Province

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Haggle at markets and street vendors.
  • Explore the outdoors and take part in adventure activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites and customs.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially in rural areas.
  • Take illegal drugs.
  • Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Province

Southern Province is considered a safe destination for tourists, but it is always best to be cautious and vigilant, especially at night or in crowded areas.

Health in Southern Province

Make sure to have all the necessary vaccinations before you travel to Southern Province, and be cautious with the food and water you consume. It is best to only drink bottled or boiled water and avoid eating uncooked food.

Getting to Southern Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Province

The nearest international airports to Southern Province are Mattala Rajapaksa International Airport and Bandaranaike International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Southern Province

The main intercity bus stations in Southern Province are located in Galle and Matara.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or tuk-tuk, take a train or arrange a private transfer to get around Southern Province.

How to Get Around in Southern Province

The easiest and most affordable way to get around Southern Province is by tuk-tuk or bus. You can also rent a car or motorbike, but make sure you have an international driving license and appropriate insurance.
