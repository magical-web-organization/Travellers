Introduction to Tangalle

Tangalle is a coastal town located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka, which boasts of scenic views, idyllic beaches, and an unparalleled tranquil atmosphere. It is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and history buffs alike. Tangalle is blessed with lush green hills, turquoise blue waters, and pristine beaches, making it one of the top destinations in Sri Lanka.

List of Places to Visit in Tangalle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tangalle is rich in culture and heritage, and you can explore its unique history by visiting museums and historical places. The Mulkirigala Rock Monastery, located on a rock hill, is a sacred Buddhist temple with breathtaking views. The Kalametiya Bird Sanctuary is a wildlife reserve, home to a variety of bird species. The Mulgirigala Raja Maha Vihara, a Buddhist temple that dates back to the third century BC, is a historic landmark that showcases the rich history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Tangalle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tangalle is a nature lover’s paradise, with several natural attractions to explore. The Rekawa Turtle Sanctuary is a protected beach where you can witness turtles laying eggs during the nesting season. The Bundala National Park is a wildlife sanctuary, home to several species of endemic birds, elephants, leopards, and other wildlife. The Hoo-maniya Blow Hole, a natural wonder, is a rock formation that ejects seawater high in the air.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tangalle

Tangalle is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Tangalle nature trail, located in the Tangalle Lagoon, is a scenic route that takes you through vast mangroves, forests, and wetlands. The Mulkirigala Rock, one of the most popular hiking destinations, is a challenging trail that leads you through seven ancient temples that date back to the third century BC. The Sinharaja rainforest, a UNESCO world heritage site, is a must-visit attraction on your hiking or biking tour.

Best Things to Do in Tangalle: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring natural attractions and historical landmarks, there are plenty of things to do in Tangalle. You can take a sunset boat ride on the Tangalle Lagoon, try your hand at fishing, take a surfing lesson, or simply relax on the beach. Several tour operators offer day tours to nearby attractions, such as Yala National Park and the Udawalawe Elephant Sanctuary.

Food and Drink Scene of Tangalle

Must-Try Dishes

Tangalle offers a diverse culinary experience with a mixture of local and international flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include Kottu Roti, rice and curry, hoppers (a type of pancake), and seafood dishes. Don’t miss trying the local fruits, such as mangosteen and rambutan, which are only available in Sri Lanka.

Local Beverages

Tangalle has several local beverages that you must try, such as toddy, a fermented coconut nectar, the legendary Ceylon tea, and king coconut water. Several cafes and bars offer refreshing cocktails made with local produce.

Where to Stay in Tangalle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tangalle has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the top places to stay in Tangalle include the Anantara Peace Haven resort, the Buckingham Place, the Lagoon Paradise Beach Resort, and the Zion Beach house.

What to Buy in Tangalle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tangalle has several local products that you can bring back home as souvenirs, such as cinnamon products, tea, spices, and traditional Sri Lankan handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tangalle

Tangalle is influenced by Sri Lankan culture and Buddhism. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and they follow traditions and customs that date back to ancient times. It’s important to dress appropriately and to respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Tangalle

The best time to visit Tangalle is from December to April when the weather is dry and sunny. Sri Lanka experiences two monsoons, and the region gets affected by the Southwest monsoon, which brings heavy rain and winds from May to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tangalle

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting temples or other sacred places
  • Try new food items and local beverages
  • Take a sunscreen and mosquito repellent when visiting outdoor attractions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the religious norms
  • Engage in illegal activities such as drug abuse
  • Carry firearms or other dangerous objects
  • Litter or pollute the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Tangalle

Tangalle is a safe destination for tourists, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s important to be cautious when traveling alone, especially at night. Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Tangalle

Tangalle is a malaria-prone area, and it’s important to take necessary precautions such as wearing long-sleeved shirts and trousers and using mosquito repellent. It’s advisable to get vaccinated against typhoid, hepatitis A, and tetanus before traveling to Sri Lanka.

Getting to Tangalle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tangalle is well-connected to the rest of the country by buses, trains, and taxis. The nearest airport is the Mattala Rajapaksa International Airport, located about 18 kilometers from Tangalle.

How to Get Around in Tangalle

Tangalle is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot. Tuk-tuks and taxis are also available for short distances, and you can rent a bike or a scooter to explore the nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Tangalle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tangalle is a great starting point for exploring the rest of Sri Lanka. Some of the nearby destinations include Udawalawe National Park, Mirissa, Galle, and Yala National Park.

Conclusion

Tangalle is an undiscovered gem in Sri Lanka, waiting to be explored. With its unique blend of culture, nature, and adventure, it offers an unforgettable experience. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation, an adventure-filled escapade, or a historical tour, Tangalle has something for everyone. So pack your bags, book your tickets, and get ready to experience the enchanting beauty of Tangalle.
