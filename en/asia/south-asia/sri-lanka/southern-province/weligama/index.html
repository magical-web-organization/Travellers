Introduction to Weligama

Nestled in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka, Weligama is known for its stunning beaches, delicious cuisine, and rich cultural heritage. Its name literally means “sandy village”, and it is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the country. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone in Weligama.

Local Culture and Customs of Weligama

Weligama is a predominantly Buddhist community, and visitors should dress conservatively when visiting religious sites. It is also customary to remove your shoes before entering a temple or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Weligama

The best time to visit Weligama is between December and March when the weather is dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Weligama

Do:

  • Respect the local culture
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the beaches and cultural sights

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites
  • Leave your valuables unattended
  • Disrespect the wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Weligama

Weligama is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Weligama

Visitors should drink bottled water and take precautions to protect themselves against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever.

How to Get Around in Weligama

Tuk-tuks and taxis are widely available in Weligama, and visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to explore the area on their own.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach holiday or an adventurous cultural experience, Weligama has something for everyone. Boasting stunning beaches, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, this sleepy coastal village is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Sri Lanka.
