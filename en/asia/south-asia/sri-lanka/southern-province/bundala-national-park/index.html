Introduction to Bundala National Park

Located in Southern Province, Sri Lanka, Bundala National Park is a famous eco-tourism destination, constituting a perfect combination of nature and culture. The park is famous for the array of wildlife that lives there, including elephants, crocodiles, and migratory birds. Bundala is also known for its rich history and cultural heritage, with important historical sites spread over the park.

List of Places to Visit in Bundala National Park

Here is a comprehensive list of the must-visit places in Bundala National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kataragama Temple
  • Dutch Fort, in Tangalle
  • Mulgirigala Rock Temple
  • Tissamaharama Raja Maha Vihara
  • Yatala Wehera

Natural Attractions of Bundala National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bundala Lagoon
  • Maduwanwela Walawwa
  • Kirinda Beach and Temple
  • Mahapelessa Hot Springs

Local Culture and Customs of Bundala National Park

Sri Lankan culture is rich and unique, with many customs and practices. It is important to respect local customs and follow them. Some of the customs specific to Bundala include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Remove footwear before entering temples
  • Do not disturb the wildlife

Best Time to Visit Bundala National Park

The best time to visit Bundala National Park is between November and March, the dry season when wildlife is more visible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bundala National Park

Here are some important do’s and don’ts in Bundala National Park:

Do:

  • Follow the rules and regulations set up by the park authorities
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be environmentally conscious; do not litter the park

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife
  • Take anything from the park as a souvenir
  • Smoke on the park premises

Safety and Health

The safety and health of visitors to Bundala National Park should be taken seriously.

Safety in Bundala National Park

  • Be aware of the surroundings at all times
  • Do not swim in waters where crocodiles are present
  • Do not feed the wildlife

Health in Bundala National Park

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and carry insect repellents to protect from mosquito-borne illnesses.

How to Get Around in Bundala National Park

The best way to explore Bundala National Park is by hiring a jeep or going by foot. Hiking is a great option for those who want to explore the park at a leisurely pace.

Conclusion

Bundala National Park is the perfect travel destination for nature lovers and those with an interest in Sri Lankan culture. From the vast array of wildlife to the rich cultural heritage, there is something for every traveler in Bundala. By following the do’s and don’ts and respecting local culture and customs, you can have a safe and unforgettable experience exploring this gem of Sri Lanka.
