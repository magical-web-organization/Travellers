Introduction to Kumana National Park

Located in Sri Lanka’s Eastern Province, Kumana National Park is a stunning wildlife sanctuary spread across a land area of 35,664 hectares. The park, also known as Yala East, is well known for its rich biodiversity and is home to several migratory and endemic bird species, as well as other wildlife. Kumana National Park offers a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions, making it a popular destination among nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Kumana National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Okanda Kovil: It is a Hindu temple located near the eastern coast of Kumana National Park. The temple has a cultural and mythological significance and is visited by Hindu devotees from across the country.

Natural Attractions of Kumana National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kumana Villu: This is a large seasonal water body in the park that attracts a variety of water birds, including pelicans and painted storks, during the breeding season.

  • Kudumbigala Sanctuary: This sanctuary offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape from the top of its rocky outcrops. It is home to several species of birds, mammals, and insects.

  • Outreach Camps: Visitors can take part in an overnight camping experience in one of the park’s outreach camps, where trained guides will show visitors around and offer information on the park’s wildlife and vegetation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumana National Park

  • Kudumbigala Trail: This trail covers the Kudumbigala Sanctuary, which is popular for bird watching and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Kumana Villu Trail: This trail offers visitors an opportunity to explore the park’s diverse flora and fauna and visit the Kumana Villu water body, which attracts a variety of water birds.

Best Things to Do in Kumana National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Bird Watching: Kumana National Park is home to several endemic and migratory bird species, making it an ideal destination for bird enthusiasts.

  • Wildlife Safari: Visitors can take a guided jeep safari to explore the park’s diverse wildlife accompanied by an experienced guide.

  • Camping: The park offers a unique camping experience where visitors can spend the night under the stars and explore the park’s natural environment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kumana National Park

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Kumana National Park and take a guided jeep safari to explore the park’s wildlife
2 Explore the Kudumbigala Sanctuary and take the Kudumbigala Trail
3 Visit the Okanda Kovil Temple and enjoy the beach and surrounding natural environment
4 Take a guided bird-watching tour at Kumana Villu and enjoy the scenic views of the park
5 Experience an overnight camping adventure in one of the park’s outreach camps
6 Relax and unwind at the park’s beaches and enjoy the seaside view
7 Depart from the park and head to your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Kumana National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kottu Roti: A popular Sri Lankan street food made by stir-frying chopped roti bread with vegetables and meat, often seasoned with spices and curry.

  • Sambol: A spicy condiment made of ground coconut and chili peppers, often served with rice and other dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Sri Lanka is known for its tea, and visitors can enjoy a cup of hot tea or refreshing iced tea while exploring the park.

Where to Stay in Kumana National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jetwing Kaduruketha: A luxurious eco-lifestyle resort situated in the midst of green paddy fields and overlooking the Maduru Oya Reservoir.

  • Safari Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse situated close to the Kumana National Park entrance, offering comfortable accommodation with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Kumana National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase handmade items such as wooden carvings, ceramics, and traditional Sri Lankan textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumana National Park

  • Hospitality: Sri Lankans are known for their hospitality, and visitors can expect to be warmly welcomed by their hosts during their stay in the park.

Best Time to Visit Kumana National Park

The best time to visit Kumana National Park is from March to June, during the park’s dry season, when wildlife sightings are more frequent.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumana National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and do not disturb them.

  • Listen to your guide’s instructions during wildlife safaris.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park.

  • Approach wildlife too closely or feed them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumana National Park

  • Visitors should be cautious during wildlife safaris and follow their guide’s instructions.

  • It is recommended to carry mosquito repellents and wear long sleeves and pants to avoid mosquito bites.

Health in Kumana National Park

  • Visitors should ensure they are updated on their routine vaccinations before visiting Sri Lanka.

  • Malaria is present in some parts of Sri Lanka, and visitors should consult with their health care provider for appropriate preventative measures.

Getting to Kumana National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kumana National Park

  • Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo: This is the main international airport in Sri Lanka, and visitors can take a domestic flight or hire a taxi to reach Kumana National Park.

Bus Stations Near Kumana National Park

  • Ampara Bus Station: This is the main bus station near Kumana National Park, from where visitors can take a bus to reach the park.

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire a private taxi or car to reach Kumana National Park from their chosen location.

How to Get Around in Kumana National Park

The best way to explore the park is through guided jeep safaris, hikes or cycling tours available at the entrance of the park.

Where to Go Next from Kumana National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Arugam Bay: A popular surfing destination located about 20 km from Kumana National Park.

  • Yala National Park: Another popular national park in Sri Lanka, located about 88 km from Kumana National Park.

Conclusion

Kumana National Park offers a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts. Visitors can experience the park’s abundant wildlife, rich culture, and stunning scenic views through a guided safari, hikes, and cultural visits. A trip to Kumana National Park is truly an unforgettable experience.
