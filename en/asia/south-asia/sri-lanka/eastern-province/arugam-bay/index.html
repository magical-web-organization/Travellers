Introduction to Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a famous surfing destination located on the eastern coast of Sri Lanka. This once-sleepy fishing village has become a bustling tourist center due to its sandy beach, clear blue waters, and amazing waves, making it an excellent location for surfing enthusiasts worldwide. The town has plenty of small hotels, cafes, bars, restaurants and shops run by locals that cater to the tourists. Unlike other popular party places in Sri Lanka, Arugam Bay has a laid-back vibe that attracts those who seek quiet and peace.

List of Places to Visit in Arugam Bay

If you’re looking for things to do in Arugam Bay, here are the top places you need to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Muhudu Maha Viharaya - A historical Buddhist temple constructed during the 3rd century B.C.
  • Kudumbigala Monastery - The historic rock cave temple with beautiful frescoes depicting Buddhist history.
  • Lahugala Kitulana National Park - A natural reserve where the ancient temple Lahugala Kitulana is located.

Natural Attractions of Arugam Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Whiskey Point - A renowned surfing spot outside Arugam Bay with an extensive beach area.
  • Peanut Farm Beach - A beautiful secluded beach surrounded by stunning tropical vegetation.
  • Ullei River - A serene river that offers a peaceful ride on boats and canoes with beautiful views of mangroves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a superb location to explore by bike or foot. Here are some popular routes:

  • Lahugala National Park - A short ride or walk from Arugam Bay with a beautiful lake and diverse groups of animals.
  • Kudumbigala Sanctuary - The historical rock cave temple which can be explored through a challenging hike.
  • Yala National Park - A famous wildlife sanctuary located approximately 150 kilometers from Arugam Bay.

Best Things to Do in Arugam Bay: Activities and Tours

Here are some exciting activities to do in Arugam Bay to complete your adventure of this beautiful place:

  • Surfing - Arugam Bay is known for its world-class waves, providing surfers of all levels with an unforgettable experience.
  • Wildlife Safaris - There are many parks and nature reserves for wildlife lovers, including jeep safaris to see the wild animals up close.
  • Boat Trips - Enjoy a boat trip to discover the beautiful mangrove forests and stunning ocean views.
  • Yoga Retreats - Arugam Bay is an exceptional location for Yoga retreats due to its quiet atmosphere, breathtaking natural beauty and peaceful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay offers several unique food and drinks that everyone should try when they visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kottu Roti - A popular Sri Lankan dish made of sliced roti bread, vegetables, eggs, and meat or fish, pounded together on a huge grill with knives.
  • Lamprais - A traditional food consisting of rice cooked in meat broth mixed with vegetables and meat placed in a banana leaf to cook; it’s called “lamp” because of the Dutch word for “lump”.
  • Coconut sambol - A spicy coconut salad that folk on everything, from warm roti bread to fresh fish or curry.

Local Beverages

  • King Coconut - A sweet, refreshing juice best served at room temperature.
  • Arrack - A strong distilled coconut Arrack is as prevalent in Arugam Bay as in the rest of Sri Lanka. It’s a popular drink that tastes like gin and can be consumed neat, with ice or as part of a cocktail.

What to Buy in Arugam Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Arugam Bay can take home some local artefacts to make it a memorable trip.

  • Handmade items - Purchase some hand-made textiles and souvenir items at the local markets of Arugam Bay.
  • Sri Lankan tea - Take home some high-quality tea from the famous tea plantations of Sri Lanka.
  • Spices - Sri Lanka is famous for its spices, and you can find them in abundance in Arugam Bay.

Local Culture and Customs of Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay residents mostly follow Buddhism, one of the oldest religions in the world. Tourists should always respect the local culture and refrain from acting in any way that might be offensive to their customs. Dress modestly when visiting Buddhist temples and cover knees and shoulders while entering.

Best Time to Visit Arugam Bay

The best time to visit Arugam Bay is between May and September when the weather is dry; however, the peak tourist season is from July to August. From November to February, the town experiences heavy rainfall and winds, with many of the hotels and cafes closing down.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arugam Bay

Here are some things to keep in mind while traveling in Arugam Bay:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs - Dress modestly when entering temples.
  • Carry small change - You’ll need it to pay for restrooms and at the local markets.
  • Try to learn some Sinhalese words - It will be appreciated by the locals.

Don’t:

  • Forget to apply sunscreen - Due to close proximity to the Equator, the sun is strong and can lead to sunburn, which can be painful.
  • Wear bikinis away from the beach - Public nudity is not allowed.
  • Lose your temper - Sri Lankans are known for their hospitality; try to maintain a cool and calm demeanor.

Safety and Health

Arugam Bay is considered safe for tourists, with very few incidents of robberies, violence, and harassment reported. Nonetheless, here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Arugam Bay

  • Avoid wearing expensive jewellery and carrying too much cash - Petty theft can be a problem.
  • Stay with a group while on tours - Avoid going on long bike rides or beach walks alone.
  • Watch out for the strong currents while surfing - Always listen to the advice of local surf guides.

Health in Arugam Bay

  • Pack insect repellent - Mosquitoes are prevalent in the town and can cause dengue fever.
  • Drink bottled or filtered water - Avoid drinking from taps if possible to prevent contracting water-borne infections.
  • Always carry your medication - It’s advisable to bring any medication you may need as not all drugs are readily available in Arugam Bay.

Getting to Arugam Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arugam Bay is approximately 320km from Colombo, Sri Lanka’s capital. Here are some alternative modes of transportation from the nearest airports and bus stations:

Airports Near Arugam Bay

  • Batticaloa Airport(CGU) – 41km from Arugam Bay with a journey time of about 1 hour 10 minutes.
  • Mattala Rajapaksa International Airport(HRI) - 195km away from Arugam Bay with a journey time of just over 4 hours.

Bus Stations Near Arugam Bay

  • Ampara - The nearest bus station, with a journey time of 1 hour and 15 minutes.
  • Pottuvil - The next closest bus station, with a journey time of 30 minutes.

Other Options

  • Taxi - If you want to avoid driving in hectic traffic, a taxi is always a better option.
  • Private Transfer - This is the most comfortable option, and private transfers can be arranged in advance to transport we passengers door to door.

How to Get Around in Arugam Bay

Many locations in Arugam Bay can be reached on foot, but here are some other transportation options:

  • Bike - Renting a motorbike is the easiest way to get around the city with daily rates around $5-10.
  • Tuk-Tuk – Tuk-Tuk drivers are always available in town and can take passengers to their destination for a reasonable price.
  • Bus - Local public buses are also available; however, they can be crowded and are not always reliable.

Where to Go Next from Arugam Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arugam Bay is surrounded by some excellent locations to visit nearby. Here are just a few examples:

  • Batticaloa - A city located on the eastern coast of Sri Lanka that possesses a remarkable lagoon and many shops.
  • Trincomalee - Explore the beautiful beaches, the hot water springs or explore the history of the ancient structures, bunker and ruins reflecting the Colonial and Buddhist eras.
  • Kandy - A historical city known for its Temples of the Tooth, which houses the tooth of Buddha.

Conclusion

Arugam Bay is a hidden jewel of Sri Lanka, enveloped with luxury, adventure, and paradise-like beaches. This travel guide will help in planning an unforgettable vacation with your loved ones. Ensure that you have fun and respect the culture and customs of the locals. It is an experience you will remember for a lifetime.
