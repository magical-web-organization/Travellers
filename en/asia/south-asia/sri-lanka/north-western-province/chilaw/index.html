Introduction to Chilaw

Located in the North Western Province of Sri Lanka, Chilaw is a culturally rich and naturally scenic city with a long and fascinating history. Nestled on the west coast of the island, it boasts beautiful beaches, scenic lagoons, lush greenery, ancient temples, and more. This travel guide will help you discover the best attractions, things to do, local cuisine, and more in Chilaw.

List of Places to Visit in Chilaw

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Munneswaram Kovil: Located on the outskirts of Chilaw, this ancient Hindu temple is one of the five major temples dedicated to Lord Shiva in Sri Lanka. It is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus from all over the country.
  • Anawilundawa Wetland Sanctuary: A protected wetland sanctuary inhabited by 150 species of birds, 30 species of reptiles, and numerous other animals.
  • Chilaw Church of Our Lady of Mount Carmel: A colonial-era Catholic church located in the heart of the city, known for its stunning architecture and religious significance.

Natural Attractions of Chilaw: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Negombo Lagoon: A scenic lagoon in Negombo, just south of Chilaw, known for its fishing villages, mangrove forests, and bird-watching opportunities.
  • Munneswaram Beach: A beautiful and tranquil beach perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Muthurajawela Marsh: A wetland ecosystem located between Chilaw and Negombo, home to a diverse range of plant and animal species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chilaw

  • The Coastal Bike Trail: A scenic bike trail that runs from Negombo to Chilaw, offering breathtaking views of the coast and the Indian Ocean.
  • The Northern Lowland Forest Trail: A hiking trail that takes visitors through the lush greenery of the Northern Lowland Forest Reserve, located just north of Chilaw.

Best Things to Do in Chilaw: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the history and culture of the city by visiting its numerous temples, museums, and historical sites.
  • Visit the local markets and shops to discover unique souvenirs and products.
  • Take a boat tour of Munneswaram Lagoon to see the local wildlife and natural beauty up close.
  • Take a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Sri Lankan dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Chilaw

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kottu: A Sri Lankan street food consisting of chopped roti, vegetables, and meat, all stir-fried together on a griddle.
  • Hoppers: A local breakfast dish made from fermented rice flour, coconut milk, and spices, which are then cooked in a special hopper pan.
  • Seafood: As a coastal city, Chilaw is known for its fresh and delicious seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • King Coconut Water: A refreshing and natural drink made from the water of young green coconuts.
  • Arrack: A local liquor made from fermented coconut flower sap.

Where to Stay in Chilaw: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Anantaya Resort and Spa Chilaw: A luxurious resort offering stunning views of the Indian Ocean
  • Green Paradise Resort: A budget-friendly eco-resort offering comfortable rooms and a tranquil atmosphere.
  • Palmyrah Hotel Chilaw: A cozy and affordable hotel located in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Chilaw: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: The local markets are filled with unique handicrafts, such as traditional masks and batik clothing.
  • Spices: Sri Lanka is known for its flavorful spices, which can be found in abundance in Chilaw.
  • Tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its high-quality tea, which can be purchased in Chilaw in a variety of blends and flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Chilaw

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering some temples and homes.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings, especially when taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Chilaw

The best time to visit Chilaw is during the dry season, which runs from December to March. During this time, the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chilaw

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages for a unique culinary experience.
  • Learn about the local customs and traditions to show respect to the locals.
  • Explore the natural scenery and wildlife of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Litter or cause damage to the local environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chilaw

Chilaw is generally a safe city for tourists. However, it is advised to take usual precautions such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Chilaw

Consider getting vaccinations before traveling to Sri Lanka. Take general precautions, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding street food to prevent illness.

Getting to Chilaw: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chilaw

The nearest airport is Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, which is about a 2-hour drive from Chilaw.

Bus Stations Near Chilaw

Chilaw Bus Station is a major hub for buses traveling to and from the city.

Other Options

Taxis and private vehicles are also available to take you to Chilaw or other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Chilaw

Local buses, taxis and tuk-tuks are the most common modes of transportation in Chilaw. Renting a bike or scooter is also a popular option.

Where to Go Next from Chilaw: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Negombo: A coastal city known for its beautiful beaches, seafood, and Dutch colonial architecture.
  • Anuradhapura: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient ruins and ancient temples.
  • Wilpattu National Park: A wildlife sanctuary and national park located about an hour north of Chilaw, known for its leopard population and birdwatching opportunities.

Conclusion

Chilaw, Sri Lanka, is a hidden gem with a rich history, beautiful scenery, and unique culinary experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to explore all the best attractions, outdoor adventures, and local culture that Chilaw has to offer.
