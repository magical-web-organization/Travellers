Introduction to Kurunegala

Kurunegala is a bustling city located in the North Western Province of Sri Lanka. It is the capital of the Kurunegala District and boasts a rich history and incredible beauty. The city has a mix of cultural and natural attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Kurunegala

Kurunegala has many fascinating places to visit. Below is a list of places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Palace of Kurunegala: This historical site was once the palace of King Buwanekabahu IV and is now a popular tourist destination.
  • Ridi Viharaya: An ancient temple dating back over 2,000 years ago, the Ridi Viharaya is adorned with intricate carvings and frescoes.
  • View Point of Rambadagalla Rajamaha Viharaya: A stunning hilltop temple with sweeping views of the city below.
  • Archaeological Museum of Dambadeniya: A museum highlighting the history and civilization of the Dambadeniya Kingdom.

Natural Attractions of Kurunegala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Athagala Rock: A popular hiking spot that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kurunegala Lake: Take a boat ride or stroll along the banks of this tranquil lake in the heart of the city.
  • Diyabeduma Waterfalls: Located about 20 km from Kurunegala, this hidden gem boasts a stunning waterfall and natural swimming pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurunegala

Kurunegala is an excellent destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. A few popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Hike up Athagala Rock for panoramic views of the city.
  • Cycle through the countryside and experience the scenic beauty of Kurunegala.
  • Take a leisurely walk around Kurunegala Lake, enjoying the peace and tranquility it offers.

Best Things to Do in Kurunegala: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Royal Palace of Kurunegala.
  • Go on a safari in the nearby Wilpattu National Park.
  • Explore the historic Ridi Viharaya temple.
  • Take a boat ride around Kurunegala Lake.
  • Hike or bike through the countryside.
  • Try traditional Sri Lankan cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kurunegala

Day 1: Visit the Royal Palace of Kurunegala and the historic Ridi Viharaya temple. Day 2: Take a boat ride around Kurunegala Lake and hike up Athagala Rock. Day 3: Explore the Archaeological Museum of Dambadeniya and go on a safari in Wilpattu National Park. Day 4: Cycle through the countryside and visit the View Point of Rambadagalla Rajamaha Viharaya. Day 5: Relax at Diyabeduma Waterfalls. Day 6: Take a cultural tour of nearby towns and villages. Day 7: Enjoy the delicious and traditional cuisine of Kurunegala.

Food and Drink Scene of Kurunegala

Kurunegala boasts a rich culinary tradition, with an array of dishes and beverages that are well worth sampling.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kottu: A dish of chopped roti, vegetables, and meat, seasoned with a blend of spices.
  • Parippu (dhal curry): A thick lentil curry, often served with rice and other curries.
  • Lamprais: A traditional Sri Lankan dish of rice, meat, and vegetables, wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

  • King Coconut Water: A refreshing drink made from the juice of young green coconuts.
  • Tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea, and Kurunegala has many tea plantations worth visiting.

Where to Stay in Kurunegala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kurunegala has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. A few popular choices include:

  • Kandyan Reach Hotel: Located close to the city center, this hotel is a great choice for those looking for convenience and comfort.
  • The Triangle: A luxury boutique hotel, The Triangle offers stunning views and exceptional service.
  • Backpack Lanka: A popular hostel offering clean, comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Kurunegala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted pottery: Kurunegala is known for its traditional pottery, an ancient craft passed down through generations.
  • Tea: Sri Lanka is famous for its tea, and Kurunegala has many tea plantations worth visiting.
  • Handwoven textiles: The region is also known for its textile industry, producing beautiful fabrics using traditional methods.

Local Culture and Customs of Kurunegala

Kurunegala is proud of its rich cultural heritage and has a strong tradition of hospitality. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kurunegala

The best time to visit Kurunegala is from December to February when the weather is dry and pleasant. However, it’s worth noting that Sri Lanka has a tropical climate and can experience rain at any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurunegala

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try traditional Sri Lankan cuisine.
  • Use the local transportation system to get around.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kurunegala

Kurunegala is a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions to avoid any incidents. Avoid walking alone at night and keep an eye on your belongings when out and about.

Health in Kurunegala

Visitors should ensure they have the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Sri Lanka. The country has a high risk of malaria, dengue fever and other mosquito-borne diseases, so it’s important to take precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Kurunegala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kurunegala is conveniently located and can be accessed by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Kurunegala

  • Bandaranaike International Airport: Located in Colombo, approximately 107 km from Kurunegala.

Bus Stations Near Kurunegala

  • Kurunegala Bus Station: The city has a well-connected bus network with frequent services to other parts of Sri Lanka.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Polgahawela, approximately 15 km from Kurunegala.

How to Get Around in Kurunegala

Kurunegala is a small city, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by using public transportation such as buses or tuk-tuks. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Kurunegala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Anuradhapura: A UNESCO World Heritage site located about 70 km from Kurunegala, Anuradhapura is home to the ruins of an ancient city dating back to the 4th century BC.
  • Sigiriya: A fortress and palace located about 50 km from Kurunegala, Sigiriya is a must-visit destination for history buffs and nature lovers.
  • Kandy: A popular tourist destination located about 80 km from Kurunegala, Kandy is famous for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Kurunegala is a fantastic destination for those seeking a mix of culture and nature. With its rich history, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, there’s plenty to explore and discover in this vibrant Sri Lankan city.
