Introduction to Yapahuwa

Located in the North Western Province of Sri Lanka, Yapahuwa is a small but significant town with an unparalleled cultural and historical wealth. Yapahuwa served as the capital of Sri Lanka during the medieval period, thanks to its strategic location atop a rocky mountain with commanding views of the surrounding countryside. As such, the town boasts an impressive collection of ancient ruins and heritage sites that are a testimony to its glorious past.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Yapahuwa, we’ll take you through all the must-know aspects of visiting this Sri Lankan gem, from the top attractions to the best things to do, where to stay, local food and drink, cultural customs, safety tips, and much more. So, let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Yapahuwa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yapahuwa Rock Fortress: The highlight of Yapahuwa, the Rock Fortress is an ancient palace that dates back to the 13th century. Perched atop a 90-meter high granite rock, the fortress boasts stunning views of the surrounding valleys and features an impressive array of intricate sculptures, carvings, and caves.
  • Yapahuwa Temple: A religious complex situated near the Rock Fortress, Yapahuwa Temple houses a sacred shrine and a stupa that were built during the same time as the fortress. The temple’s architecture and mural paintings are an excellent example of medieval Sinhalese Buddhist art.
  • Yapahuwa Lion Statue: An iconic symbol of Yapahuwa, this statue of a rampant lion with a gaping mouth marks the entrance to the Rock Fortress and stands as a testament to the artistic achievements of the bygone era.
  • Danthapala Vehera: A magnificent stupa located on the outskirts of Yapahuwa, this ancient religious site dates back to 3rd century BC and is believed to hold the remains of a nobleman from that era.

Natural Attractions of Yapahuwa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kurulu Kele Bird Sanctuary: Located close to Yapahuwa, this biodiversity hotspot is a heaven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiast. This protected forest park is home to rare bird species, monkeys, and other wildlife.
  • Ibbagala Rajamaha Viharaya: A serene Buddhist temple, Ibbagala Rajamaha Viharaya is situated amidst verdant rice fields and enjoys stunning views of the mountains. This tranquil place is perfect for a peaceful meditation, especially during sunset time.
  • Yapahuwa Tank: A quaint lake that serves as a natural ecosystem, the Yapahuwa Tank boasts of a picturesque scenery and is an ideal spot for fishing and birdwatching.
  • Waterfalls: The lush jungles surrounding Yapahuwa boast of several waterfalls, including Amanawala Ella Falls and Ala Pothana Falls. Surrounded by green foliage and clear water, these waterfalls offer an idyllic setting for a refreshing dip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yapahuwa

  • Yapahuwa Rock Fortress Hike: The most iconic and challenging hike in Yapahuwa, this trail leading to Yapahuwa Rock Fortress requires climbing hundreds of steep steps but rewards hikers with breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Ibbagala Hiking Trail: A relatively easier trail that starts from the Ibbagala Rajamaha Viharaya and takes hikers through a winding path amidst paddy fields and rural villages.
  • Kurulu Kele Nature Trail: This easy hike is perfect for families and nature enthusiasts who want to explore the rich biodiversity of the area at a leisurely pace. The trail takes visitors through the Kurulu Kele Bird Sanctuary, where they can spot rare birds, butterflies and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Yapahuwa: Activities and Tours

  • Heritage Site Tours: Take a guided tour of the various ancient ruins and cultural landmarks in Yapahuwa to learn about the rich history and architecture of this region.
  • Wildlife Safaris: Go on a safari to explore the diverse flora and fauna of the Yapahuwa region. Kurulu Kele Bird Sanctuary and nearby national parks are great spots for a wildlife adventure.
  • Trekking and Hiking: As mentioned earlier, Yapahuwa has several hiking and trekking trails for both beginners and experienced hikers.
  • Photography: Yapahuwa’s natural beauty and ancient architecture provide ideal photography opportunities. Capture stunning images of the Rock Fortress, Ibbagala Rajamaha Viharaya, and the surrounding landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yapahuwa

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Yapahuwa and check-in at your hotel. Take a stroll around the town and enjoy the local food and drink scene.
2 Tour the Yapahuwa Rock Fortress and the Yapahuwa Temple. Learn about the rich heritage and architecture of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Yapahuwa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yapahuwa Rice and Curry: A staple meal in Yapahuwa and Sri Lanka, rice and curry is a delicacy consisting of rice and several side dishes such as curry, dhal, sambol, and papadam.
  • Kiri Bath: A traditional Sri Lankan festive dish, Kiri Bath is a sweet rice dish cooked with coconut milk and served with curries or sambol.
  • Kiribath with Onion Sambol: A variation of the Kiri Bath where the rice is served with a side dish of onion sambol, which is seasoned with chilli, salt, and lime juice.
  • Hoppers: Similar to pancakes, Hoppers are a crispy, bowl-shaped snack made from rice flour and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • King Coconut Water: A refreshing drink made from young green coconuts that is said to have several health benefits and is perfect to beat the heat.
  • Tea: Sri Lanka is known for its high-quality tea, and Yapahuwa is no exception. Visit a local tea plantation and sample the freshly brewed Ceylon tea.
  • Toddy: A fermented drink made from coconut palm sap, Toddy has a tangy taste and is popular among locals. However, it is an acquired taste and may not appeal to everyone.

Where to Stay in Yapahuwa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yapahuwa has several accommodations ranging from budget guesthouses to luxury hotels. Depending on your preference and budget, here are a few recommendations:

  • Yapahuwa Paradise Resort: A comfortable and affordable hotel that offers a scenic view of Yapahuwa Rock Fortress and Yapahuwa Tank.
  • Yapahuwa Heritage Villa: A boutique hotel that provides a unique experience in a traditional Sri Lankan house with modern amenities.
  • Tree House Yapahuwa: A unique accommodation option offering a stay in a treehouse that blends with the natural surroundings.

What to Buy in Yapahuwa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Yapahuwa has a rich history of art and culture and is known for its handicrafts such as wood carvings and brass sculptures.
  • Tea: As mentioned earlier, Sri Lanka is known for its premium quality tea, and Yapahuwa has several tea plantations that sell fresh tea leaves.
  • Spices: Sri Lanka is famous for its spices, and Yapahuwa is no exception. Bring home aromatic spices such as cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.

Local Culture and Customs of Yapahuwa

It is important to respect the local customs and cultural practices when visiting Yapahuwa. Some of the customs include:

  • Dress: Cosmopolitan attire is acceptable, but visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting religious and cultural sites.
  • Footwear: Some temples and historical sites require visitors to remove their footwear before entering. It is advisable to carry a pair of socks or slippers.
  • Photos: Visitors are not allowed to take photographs in certain areas of historical sites and temples. It is advisable to ask permission before taking someone’s picture.

Best Time to Visit Yapahuwa

The best time to visit Yapahuwa is during the dry season between December to February and July to September when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. However, even during the monsoon season (May to September), the area is lush green, and waterfalls are in full flow, making it a great time for nature lovers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yapahuwa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Carry enough cash as some places may not accept credit cards.
  • Try the local cuisine and tea.
  • Learn some basic Sinhala phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the religious beliefs and customs of the locals.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Take photographs where it is not allowed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yapahuwa

Yapahuwa is generally a safe place, and violent crime against tourists is rare. However, it is important to take standard safety precautions such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night and in remote areas.
  • Be alert and aware of your surroundings.
  • Store your valuables safely and be cautious of pickpockets.

Health in Yapahuwa

Medical facilities in Yapahuwa are basic, but the nearest hospital is less than an hour’s drive away. It is advisable to carry a first-aid kit, mosquito repellent, and bottled water. You should also consult your doctor regarding vaccinations and other precautionary measures before traveling to Sri Lanka.

Getting to Yapahuwa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yapahuwa

The nearest airport to Yapahuwa is Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, which is about 120 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or public bus from Colombo to Yapahuwa.

Bus Stations Near Yapahuwa

Yapahuwa has a well-connected bus network, with regular services to Colombo, Kandy, Anuradhapura, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or taxi from Colombo or other major cities to reach Yapahuwa. Contact your hotel or travel agent for assistance.

How to Get Around in Yapahuwa

Yapahuwa is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Alternatively, you can hire a tuk-tuk or bicycle to get around. Visitors can also opt for a car rental service to explore nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Yapahuwa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yapahuwa’s strategic location makes it a great gateway to nearby destinations, including:

  • Anuradhapura: Ancient capital city famous for its well-preserved ruins and Buddhist temples.
  • Polonnaruwa: A UNESCO world heritage site with impressive ruins such as the Gal Vihara.
  • Wilpattu National Park: A wildlife sanctuary home to leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and other rare animals.

Conclusion

Yapahuwa is a hidden gem in Sri Lanka that offers a unique blend of ancient history, natural beauty, and cultural traditions. From exploring the ruins of the Rock Fortress and the Yapahuwa Temple to trekking in the lush jungles or sipping a refreshing king coconut drink, there is something for everyone in Yapahuwa. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the essential information and inspiration to plan your trip to Yapahuwa. Happy travels!
