Introduction to Pokhara

Pokhara is a picturesque city located in Nepal’s Middle Hills region. Situated at an elevation of 827 meters above sea level, it is surrounded by some of the world’s highest peaks, including Dhaulagiri, Manaslu, and Annapurna. Pokhara serves as a gateway to the Annapurna Circuit, one of the world’s most popular treks for travelers. The city is famous for its breathtaking natural beauty, crystal-clear lakes, and abundance of adventure activities. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at what Pokhara has to offer travelers, including the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink options, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Pokhara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • International Mountain Museum: This museum traces the history of mountaineering in Nepal.
  • Gurkha Memorial Museum: Displays of artifacts and information on Gurkhas.
  • Bindhyabasini Temple: This temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Durga and is one of the oldest temples in the region.
  • The Old Bazaar: The old market area of Pokhara is home to several shops selling traditional Nepali crafts and souvenirs.
  • World Peace Pagoda: This stunning monument has been designed to promote peace and prosperity throughout the world.

Natural Attractions of Pokhara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phewa Lake: The second-largest freshwater lake in Nepal and is a popular spot for boating and fishing.
  • Sarangkot Hill: A popular destination for stunning sunrise and sunset views over the Annapurna Range.
  • Devi’s Fall: A unique waterfall that is fed by the Seti River and flows underground to reappear a few hundred meters downstream.
  • Mahendra Cave: An ancient limestone cave that is filled with stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Pokhara Valley: An area of scenic beauty located in the heart of Pokhara.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pokhara

  • Annapurna Circuit: A 128-mile trek that takes approximately 18 days to complete.
  • Poon Hill trek: A 5-day trek that offers incredible views of the Himalayas.
  • Dhampus Hill: A one-day trek from Pokhara that has stunning views of the Annapurna mountain range.
  • Begnas Lake Trekking Route: A relatively easy trek of 2-3 days that goes through the Begnas Lake region.

Best Things to Do in Pokhara: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding: Go paragliding over the stunning Phewa lake and take in the breathtaking views of the Annapurna Range.
  • Zip-lining: Zip through the stunning forests of Pokhara at Nepal’s longest and tallest zip line.
  • Mountain Biking: Explore Pokhara on a mountain bike with several day trip options.
  • White-water rafting: Raft down the Trishuli River or Seti River, both of which have stunning views and varying levels of difficulty.
  • Sunrise and Sunset View: Wake up early to catch the stunning sunrise from Sarangkot Hill and end your day watching the sunset from Peace Pagoda.

Food and Drink Scene of Pokhara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Daal Bhaat: A traditional Nepali dish consisting of lentil soup, rice, and vegetables.
  • Momos: Nepali-style dumplings that are filled with meat or vegetables and served with a spicy sauce.
  • Chow Mein: A noodle-based dish that is stir-fried with vegetables and sometimes meat.
  • Thukpa: A hearty noodle soup that is popular in the Himalayan region.

Local Beverages

  • Raksi: A traditional Nepali alcoholic drink made from fermented rice.
  • Chang: A millet-based alcoholic beverage that is popular in the Himalayan region.
  • Chiya: Nepal’s version of tea that is often served with milk and sugar.

Where to Stay in Pokhara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shangri-La Village Resort: A luxury resort offering incredible views of the Annapurna Range.
  • Temple Tree Resort and Spa: A boutique hotel situated in the heart of Pokhara’s Lakeside area.
  • Panorama View Hotel: A budget-friendly option offering stunning views of the valley.
  • Holiday Inn Pokhara: This hotel offers top of the line amenities for your stay in Pokhara.
  • Pokhara Eco Resort: A serene resort surrounding by greenery and mountain views.

What to Buy in Pokhara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven textiles: You can find a variety of woolen and silk textiles in the region.
  • Silver and Gold Jewelry: Bracelets, necklaces, and other jewelry made by local artisans.
  • Singing bowls: These handmade metal bowls produce a unique and calming sound when played.
  • Himulayan Salt: This unique salt is sustainably harvested and is believed to be rich in minerals and trace elements.

Local Culture and Customs of Pokhara

  • Nepali language: While Nepali is the country’s official language, English is widely spoken.
  • Greeting customs: Typical greeting custom is pressing one’s palms together and giving a small nod of the head while saying Namaste.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly while visiting any temples, and keep your shoulders and knees covered.

Best Time to Visit Pokhara

The best time to visit Pokhara is between October and April, when the weather is generally mild, and temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C. This also coincides with the dry season, which makes it ideal for trekking and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pokhara

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some words of the Nepali language.
  • Seek permission before photographing locals.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Touch someone’s head without permission.
  • Give money to beggars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pokhara

Pokhara is generally considered a safe place for travelers. However, it is always important to be vigilant about your surroundings and belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Pokhara

Travelers to Nepal are advised to have up-to-date vaccinations for diseases such as hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies. It is also important to be careful about drinking water and eating food from street vendors.

Getting to Pokhara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pokhara

Pokhara Airport is a small domestic airport that connects to Kathmandu and Jomsom.

Bus Stations Near Pokhara

Buses run daily from Kathmandu to Pokhara. There are also buses from Chitwan and other cities in Nepal.

Other Options

Shared taxis or Jeep Safari can be arranged from Kathmandu for a budget option.

How to Get Around in Pokhara

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the city and are an efficient way to get around.
  • Buses: Public buses are a budget-friendly means of transportation.
  • Bicycles: Bikes can be rented from several outlets if you are looking for an alternative way to get around.
  • Walking: The Lakeside area of Pokhara is easy to navigate on foot.

Where to Go Next from Pokhara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Annapurna Base Camp: This is a popular destination for trekkers who want to see the stunning Annapurna Range up close.
  • Chitwan National Park: Located in southern Nepal, this park is home to Bengal tigers, one-horned rhinoceroses, and many other animals.
  • Kathmandu: Nepal’s capital city is a must-visit destination with an abundance of historical and cultural sites to explore.
  • Lumbini: This UNESCO World Heritage site marks the birthplace of Lord Buddha in southern Nepal.

Conclusion

Pokhara offers travelers an abundance of opportunities to explore its natural beauty and rich culture, from trekking and outdoor adventures to exploring historical and cultural sites. Whether you’re traveling alone, with friends or family or as a couple, there’s something in Pokhara to suit everyone. We hope this guide has provided valuable information to make the most of your time in the beautiful city of Pokhara.
