Introduction to Gorkha

Gorkha, located in the Middle Hills of Nepal, has a rich history and is known for its cultural importance and breathtaking natural vistas. The town is a favorite among tourists for its ancient temples and historical places, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, and breathtaking viewpoints. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Gorkha.

List of Places to Visit in Gorkha

Gorkha is brimming with attractions - from museums and historical sites to natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, and outdoor adventures. Here are the must-visit places in Gorkha:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gorkha Palace: This 17th-century palace was home to the ruling Shah dynasty of Nepal and is a cultural and historical emblem of Gorkha.
  • Gorakhnath Temple: A sacred temple dedicated to the patron saint of Gorakhnath. This temple is famous for its distinctive architecture and draws thousands of devotees each year.
  • Manakamana Temple: Located atop a hill overlooking the Trishuli and Marsyangdi River valley, this ancient temple is a revered site of pilgrimage for Hindus from across Nepal and India.
  • Tallo Durbar: This palace was once the residence of the former prime minister of Nepal, and is renowned for its unique architecture and intricate designs.

Natural Attractions of Gorkha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gorkha Trek: A challenging trek through the lush green forests and rugged mountainous terrain that presents panoramic views of the Himalayas and scenic beauty of the natural landscape.
  • Bikhuti Hill: An elevated viewpoint that offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the town and surrounding mountains.
  • Sulikot Hill: An easy trek to Sulikot Hill, which presents envious views of the Manaslu Range and the Marsyangdi Valley.
  • Gorkha Lake: This pristine lake is a perfect spot for picnics and boating amidst the stunning natural beauty of Gorkha.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gorkha

  • Gorkha-Yarsa Trek: This popular trek combines excellent views of the Himalayas with glimpses of rural Nepali life and is an excellent way to combine adventure and culture.
  • Kalika Hill Trek: A moderate level of the trek that passes through a forest of rhododendron and oak trees, followed by scenic landscapes and stunning mountain views.
  • Gorkha-Fika Trek: A leisure trek from Gorkha through amazing terraced rice fields and local villages.
  • Budi Gandaki Valley: A trek through the serene forest, past cascading waterfalls and impressive rock cliffs, and offers stunning views of the Budi Gandaki Valley.

Best Things to Do in Gorkha: Activities and Tours

  • Village Tours: Explore the lifestyle and culture of the Gurung and Magar ethnic groups in Gorkha through a village tour.
  • Bungee Jumping: One of the most popular adventure activities in Gorkha is bungee jumping, a great way to challenge yourself and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Paragliding: Experience the thrill of flying over Gorkha’s rugged terrain and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the Himalayas.
  • Rafting: Hit the white waters of the Trishuli River and soak in the spectacular scenery of Gorkha.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gorkha

Day 1: Arrival in Gorkha, relax, and familiarize yourself with the town Day 2: Visit Tallo Durbar, Gorkha Palace, and the Gorakhnath Temple Day 3: Hike to Bikhuti Hill for a stunning panoramic view of the town and surrounding mountains Day 4: Head to the Manakamana Temple followed by some adventurous bungee jumping Day 5: Trek through the Gorkha-Yarsa Trail Day 6: Visit the Sulikot Hill for serene views of the Manaslu Range and the Marsyangdi Valley followed by paragliding. Day 7: Head back home with a camera full of memories.

Food and Drink Scene of Gorkha

Gorkha offers some of the most delicious cuisine in Nepal, with a range of street food and local dishes that reflect the town’s unique cultural identity.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thakali Set: A traditional Nepali meal served with rice and lentils, accompanied by meat and vegetable curries, pickles, and salad.
  • Sel Roti: A sweet, crispy bread made of rice flour and sugar
  • Bara: A savory lentil pancake served with a spicy tomato sauce
  • Juju Dhau: A sweetened yogurt that originated in Gorkha

Local Beverages

  • Chhyang: A popular local alcoholic beverage made of fermented rice or barley
  • Tea: Gorkha is renowned for its tea plantations, and you can find a variety of tea flavors, including black, green, and oolong.

  • Hotel Laxmi Palace

What to Buy in Gorkha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gorkha offers some unique souvenirs that you can take home as a reminder of your visit, including:

  • Traditional handicrafts: Hand-woven bags, shawls, and scarves.
  • Local tea: Gorkha is renowned for its tea plantations, and you can find a variety of tea flavors to take home.
  • Handmade paper products: Gorkha is famous for its handmade paper products, including notebooks, diaries, and journals.

Local Culture and Customs of Gorkha

Gorkha is primarily a Hindu town and observes all the major Hindu festivals, including Diwali, Holi, and Dashain. The people of Gorkha boast unique cultural traits, music, and dances, such as the traditional Gurung dance and the Yak dance.

Best Time to Visit Gorkha

The best time to visit Gorkha is during the fall season (September - November) and the spring season (March - May). These periods see excellent weather conditions that allow for outdoor activities and offer clear views of the surrounding landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gorkha

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Carry a map, water bottles and other essentials while indulging in outdoor activities like trekking or hiking
  • Bargain while buying souvenirs or shopping from the local markets

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the locals without their consent
  • Wear revealing clothes, especially while visiting religious places
  • Litter the town or mountain trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Gorkha

Like any Himalayan region, Gorkha can be challenging, and visitors should take care when indulging in outdoor activities. Care should be taken while hiking, trekking, or cycling.

Health in Gorkha

Visitors to Gorkha should take care to avoid contracting common illnesses. It is recommended to use bottled or treated water and to take proper precautions with food hygiene.

Getting to Gorkha: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to reach Gorkha is from Kathmandu, via road transportation. There are private taxis and buses that lead to Gorkha. Besides, there are private companies that offer jeep and small car services for Gorkha.

Airports Near Gorkha

The nearest airport to Gorkha is the Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu.

Bus Stations Near Gorkha

There are different bus services from the Budi Gandaki Bus Station to Gorkha.

Other Options

You can opt for a private car or Jeep that can be hired from Kathmandu.

How to Get Around in Gorkha

The best way to explore Gorkha is on foot, as it is a small town with narrow streets to walk around. Some other modes of transportation you can use are taxis, bicycles or rented motorbikes.

Where to Go Next from Gorkha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pokhara: Pokhara is located not much farther from Gorkha and is known for its natural beauty and adventures.
  • Lumbini: Lumbini is the birthplace of Lord Gautama Buddha, making it a pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide.
  • Manaslu Conservation Area: It is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, trekking, and adventure activities.

Conclusion

Gorkha is a beautiful town full of natural wonders and cultural highlights that offer travelers a unique experience in Nepal. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most of your visit to this enchanting town and leave with cherished and long-lasting memories.
