Introduction to Taplejung

Welcome to Taplejung, a hidden gem in the Middle Hills of Nepal. Located in the northeastern part of the country, Taplejung is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking stunning landscapes and unique cultural experiences. With majestic Himalayan peaks, lush green forests, and traditional Nepali villages, Taplejung offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Taplejung

As a visitor to Taplejung, you will be able to explore many culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to visit in Taplejung:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Limbu Museum: Located in Taplejung Bazaar, this museum features exhibits on the Limbu community’s culture and history.
  • Pathibhara Devi Temple: A significant Hindu pilgrimage site in Taplejung, it attracts thousands of devotees from across Nepal and India.
  • Olangchung Gola: A remote village in northeastern Taplejung with a unique Tibetan culture.

Natural Attractions of Taplejung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kanchenjunga Base Camp: A scenic trek that starts in Taplejung and takes you through some of the highest peaks in the Himalayas.
  • Tinjure Milke Jaljale Trail: A trek through lush green forest that leads to breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
  • Ilam Tea Garden: Located about 2.5 hours from Taplejung, this lush garden offers stunning views and some of the best tea in Nepal.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Taplejung

Taplejung is a hiker’s paradise, and there are many scenic routes and trails to explore. Whether you prefer leisurely walks or challenging treks, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to get out and explore nature. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Taplejung:

  • Khabru Trek: A challenging trek that takes you through Tamur Valley, to the Kanchenjunga Base Camp, and along the Nepal-China border.
  • Milke Danda Trek: One of the most beautiful treks in Nepal, it offers stunning views of the Himalayas, lush forests, and traditional villages.
  • Taplejung Loop: A scenic route that takes you through beautiful villages, rolling hills, and lush green forests.

Best Things to Do in Taplejung: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in Taplejung. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Paragliding: Kick your adrenaline into high gear and take to the skies for a bird’s-eye view of the stunning Himalayas.
  • Cultural Tours: As a visitor to Taplejung, you’ll have plenty of opportunities to learn about Nepali culture and history.
  • Wildlife Watching: Go on a safari through the lush forests of Taplejung and see rare and exotic wildlife, including the red panda and snow leopard.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taplejung

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Taplejung, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Taplejung

No trip to Taplejung is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sel Roti: A sweet Nepali bread made with rice flour and served with curry.
  • Dhindo: A traditional Nepali dish made with maize flour and served with gundruk and meat curry.
  • Wai Wai Noodles: Nepali instant noodles prepared with oil, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Chhaang: A traditional Nepali alcoholic drink made from fermented millet or rice.
  • Butter Tea: A popular Nepali drink made with tea, salt, yak butter, and milk.
  • Juju Dhau: A sweet and creamy yogurt that is a specialty of Bhaktapur, served in Taplejung.

Where to Stay in Taplejung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

As a popular tourist destination, Taplejung has many hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • Yatri Niwas: A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms.
  • Hotel Pathibhara: A mid-range hotel with stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • Taplejung Mountain Lodge: A luxury hotel with spacious rooms, a restaurant, and many amenities.

What to Buy in Taplejung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some top items to consider:

  • Handmade Finger Rings: These rings are made by local artisans and are a popular souvenir.
  • Local Tea: Taplejung is known for its tea gardens, and you can buy some of the best tea in Nepal here.
  • Handmade Woolen Items: Taplejung is known for its woolen products, including hats, gloves, and scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Taplejung

As a visitor to Taplejung, it’s important to be respectful of local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Taplejung

The best time to visit Taplejung is from March to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, providing stunning views of the Himalayas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taplejung

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Taplejung:

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Nepali phrases.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash, as many places do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Travel alone after dark.
  • Disrespect local culture and religion.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taplejung

Taplejung is a safe and tourist-friendly destination. However, the following tips can help ensure a safe and enjoyable trip:

  • Avoid walking alone after dark.
  • Be cautious when traveling on mountain roads.
  • Always carry a flashlight and stay on designated hiking trails.

Health in Taplejung

Here are some health tips to keep in mind when traveling to Taplejung:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Be aware of the altitude and take necessary precautions when hiking.

Getting to Taplejung: Transportation Options and Logistics

While Taplejung may seem remote, it is easily accessible by air and road. Here are your transportation options:

Airports Near Taplejung

Taplejung Airport is located in the city, and flights operate regularly from Kathmandu.

Bus Stations Near Taplejung

Regular buses operate from Kathmandu to Taplejung. You can choose from luxury buses to cheap local buses.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or jeep to reach Taplejung from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Taplejung

The best way to explore Taplejung is on foot, but you can also hire a bike or motorcycle. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Taplejung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Ilam: Known for its lush tea gardens and stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • Panchthar: A district in Nepal with many trekking trails and scenic views.
  • Yangma: A village in the Taplejung district with stunning views of the Himalayas and traditional Nepali architecture.

Conclusion

There you have it! A comprehensive guide to Taplejung that covers everything from places to visit and things to do to where to stay and how to get there. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and culture of this hidden gem in the heart of the Himalayas.
