Introduction to Thulo Sailung

Thulo Sailung is a remote region in Ramechhap district, situated 136km east of Kathmandu. The region is named after its highest peak, Thulo Sailung, which stands tall at an altitude of 3,146 meters (10,322 feet). The region is not well-known among tourists, making it an ideal destination for those looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path adventure. The local community follows traditional customs and lifestyles and has an abundance of cultural heritage. The region is also rich in flora and fauna, and visitors can marvel at the pristine natural beauty of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Thulo Sailung

The local community of Thulo Sailung is mainly composed of Gurung and Tamang people, who have a distinct culture and tradition. Visitors are expected to follow specific cultural norms, such as seeking permission before entering someone’s house or village, removing shoes before entering religious sites or houses, and dressing conservatively.

Best Time to Visit Thulo Sailung

The best time to visit Thulo Sailung is from September to November and March to May. During these seasons, the weather is favorable, and the views are stunning. The months from June to August are monsoon season, and it rains heavily, making it difficult to trek or hike. The winter season (December to February) is chilly, and the trails might be closed due to snowfall.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thulo Sailung

Thulo Sailung is a safe destination for tourists, and the local community is cooperative and hospitable. However, it’s important to take necessary precautions, such as carrying enough water, avoiding solo treks, and staying alert during outdoor adventures.

Health in Thulo Sailung

The region has basic medical facilities, but it’s best to carry a first aid kit and necessary medication. Visitors must take necessary precautions, such as drinking boiled or bottled water and following a healthy diet.

Getting to Thulo Sailung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thulo Sailung is accessible via road and air transportation. The nearest airport is Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, and the closest bus terminal is in Hile Bazaar, about 2 hours drive from the region. Visitors can hire taxis or buses to reach Ramechhap from where they can avail of jeeps or buses to reach Thulo Sailung.
