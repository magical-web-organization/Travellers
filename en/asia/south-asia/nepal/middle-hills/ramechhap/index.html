Introduction to Ramechhap

Ramechhap is a beautiful district located in the Middle Hills of Nepal, South Asia. The district is home to lush green forests, scenic views, and rich culture. Ramechhap is a great destination for travelers who are looking to experience the authentic culture, view breathtaking landscapes, and embark on outdoor adventures. Whether you are a nature lover or a history buff, this travel guide to Ramechhap has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ramechhap

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taudaha Lake: Taudaha Lake is a sacred lake for both Hindus and Buddhists. The lake is surrounded by forests and temples. It is believed that this lake was home to a demon called Tarkasur, who was killed by Lord Shiva.

  • Thodung Monastery: Thodung Monastery is a Buddhist monastery located on a hilltop. The monastery is home to statues of Lord Buddha, Guru Rinpoche, and other deities. It offers a panoramic view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Bhairavi Temple: Bhairavi Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Bhairavi. The temple is has intricate carvings and sculptures. It is believed that the temple was built during the reign of King Janak of Mithila.

Natural Attractions of Ramechhap: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gokyo Lake: Gokyo Lake is a group of six lakes located in the Everest region. It is believed that the lakes were formed by the melting of glaciers. The lakes offer a breathtaking view of the Everest range.

  • Namche Bazaar: Namche Bazaar is a town located at an altitude of 3,440 meters. It is the gateway to the Everest region. The town offers a panoramic view of Mount Everest and other peaks of the region.

  • Dolakha Bhimsen Temple: Dolakha Bhimsen Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Bhimsen. The temple is located on a hilltop and offers a stunning view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramechhap

Ramechhap offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Nepal. Here are the top routes that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Gaurishankar Conservation Area: Gaurishankar Conservation Area is a protected area located in the Ramechhap and Dolakha districts. The conservation area is home to the Gaurishankar Himal, which is the 9th highest mountain in Nepal.

  • Pike Peak Trek: Pike Peak Trek is a popular trek that offers a stunning view of Mount Everest, Annapurna, and other mountains. The trek starts from Patale, a small village in Ramechhap.

  • Jiri to Everest Base Camp Trek: Jiri to Everest Base Camp Trek is a classic trek that offers a panoramic view of the Himalayas. The trek starts from Jiri and passes through beautiful villages, forests, and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Ramechhap: Activities and Tours

  • Sunrise and Sunset View: Ramechhap offers some of the best sunrise and sunset views in Nepal. You can enjoy these views from various viewpoints such as Pike Peak, Gokyo Lake, and Thodung Monastery.

  • Mountain Biking: Ramechhap is a great destination for mountain biking. There are various biking routes that pass through villages, forests, and valleys.

  • Cultural Tours: Ramechhap is rich in culture and history. You can take a cultural tour to explore the temples, monasteries, and museums of the district.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramechhap

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kwati: Kwati is a soup made with nine types of sprouted beans. It is a nutritious and healthy dish that is popular in Nepal.

  • Yomari: Yomari is a sweet dish made with rice flour. It is filled with molasses and sesame seeds.

  • Chhang: Chhang is a local beer made from fermented millet. It is a popular drink in Nepal.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: Butter Tea is a traditional drink made with tea leaves, salt, and butter. It is a popular drink in the Himalayan regions of Nepal.

  • Thwon: Thwon is a drink made with fermented millet. It is a popular drink in Ramechhap and other districts of Nepal.

Where to Stay in Ramechhap: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Everest Panorama Resort: Everest Panorama Resort is a beautiful resort located near Pike Peak. It offers a stunning view of the Everest range.

  • Sunbird Lodge: Sunbird Lodge is a budget-friendly lodge located in the heart of Namche Bazaar. It offers clean and comfortable rooms.

  • Guesthouse Tengboche: Guesthouse Tengboche is a guesthouse located in the Tengboche Monastery. It offers a peaceful and serene environment.

What to Buy in Ramechhap: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Khukuri: Khukuri is a traditional Nepali knife. It is a symbol of bravery and courage.

  • Pashmina Shawls: Pashmina Shawls are made from the wool of Himalayan goats. They are warm and luxurious.

  • Handmade Jewellery: Ramechhap is known for its handmade jewellery. You can find beautiful necklaces, bracelets, and earrings in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramechhap

Ramechhap is a district with a diverse culture and rich traditions. Here are some of the customs and traditions of the district:

  • Respect for elders: In Ramechhap, elders are given great respect. It is customary to touch the feet of elders and seek their blessings.

  • Festivals: Ramechhap celebrates many festivals throughout the year. Some of the popular festivals are Dashain, Tihar, and Holi.

  • Attire: The traditional attire of Ramechhap is a long dress called ‘Gunyo Cholo’. Men wear a long shirt called ‘Daura Suruwal’.

Best Time to Visit Ramechhap

The best time to visit Ramechhap is from September to November and from February to May. The weather during these months is clear, and the temperature is moderate. The monsoon season from June to August is not an ideal time to visit as the region receives heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramechhap

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Carry a torch and warm clothes while hiking.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the elders or the culture.
  • Litter in the hills and valleys.
  • Ignore the weather conditions and safety measures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramechhap

Ramechhap is a safe district, but travelers should take certain precautions while traveling. Here are some safety tips:

  • Do not hike alone.
  • Hire a guide from a trusted agency.
  • Carry a map and a first aid kit.

Health in Ramechhap

Travelers should take certain health precautions while visiting Ramechhap. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink bottled water only.
  • Carry mosquito repellent.
  • Get vaccinated for typhoid and hepatitis A.

Getting to Ramechhap: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ramechhap

Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu is the nearest airport to Ramechhap. From Kathmandu, you can take a domestic flight to Ramechhap.

Bus Stations Near Ramechhap

The bus station in Kathmandu is the nearest bus station to Ramechhap. From Kathmandu, you can take a bus to Manthali, which is the district headquarters of Ramechhap.

Other Options

You can hire a private car or taxi from Kathmandu to Ramechhap. The journey takes around 6 hours.

How to Get Around in Ramechhap

Hiking and biking are the best ways to explore Ramechhap. You can also hire a jeep for sightseeing.
