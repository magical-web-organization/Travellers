Introduction to Kakarbhitta

Kakarbhitta is a small town located in the southeastern region of Nepal. It is the main entry point from India and a significant business hub due to its proximity to both India and Bhutan. The town is located at a distance of around 77 kilometers from Darjeeling in West Bengal and around 1,074 kilometers from Kathmandu. Despite being a small town, Kakarbhitta offers a lot to discover, making it an excellent destination for travelers looking to explore the lesser-known destinations of Nepal.

List of Places to Visit in Kakarbhitta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kakarbhitta is known for its multiculturalism, and several museums and historical places are worth exploring. One such attraction is the Jhapa Museum that showcases the historical significance of Jhapa district. The museum houses various artifacts relating to the ancient civilization of Jhapa, including traditional clothing, weapons, jewelry, and musical instruments used by the local tribes. The other significant historical site is The Red Fort, which is a symbol of bravery and resilience during the British occupation of India. This site is not only a cultural attraction but also offers some of the best views of the surrounding area from its ramparts.

Natural Attractions of Kakarbhitta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kakarbhitta has an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor activities that visitors can enjoy. The Kankai Mai Temple, situated at the top of a hill, offers spectacular views of the surrounding hills and plains. A short trek to the temple can also be a thrilling experience. Bird lovers can also visit the Jhapa wetland, which is home to more than 300 species of birds, including migratory birds. The wetland is also a habitat for different plant species, mammals, and amphibians. The wetland is also an ideal place for nature walks and picnics with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kakarbhitta

Kakarbhitta is an excellent place for hikers and bikers as there are many scenic routes to explore. The Kakarvitta to Taplejung Trail is one of the famous hiking trails and is popular for its tea gardens, Himalayan views, adventurous terrain, and hiking opportunities in the Kanchenjunga Conservation Area. The best time to opt for this trail is between April to May and September to November. The town also has several bike rental shops for those who want to explore the town or nearby attractions on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Kakarbhitta: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, there are several fun activities and tours to indulge in. A visit to the Kankai temple by trekking can offer an adrenaline rush to hikers, whereas a traditional cooking class can give a cultural and gastronomic tour to food lovers. One can also take guided bird watching tours, jungle safaris, and visit the nearby monasteries or temples.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kakarbhitta

To help you plan your trip to Kakarbhitta, here’s a week-long itinerary of things to do and explore:

Day 1: Arrive at Kakarbhitta and explore The Red Fort.

Day 2: Visit the Jhapa Museum and explore the Jhapa wetland.

Day 3: Trek to the Kankai Mai Temple and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Try a cooking class and taste traditional Nepalese cuisine.

Day 5: Take a guided jeep safari in the Kanchenjunga Conservation Area.

Day 6: Rent a bike and explore the town at your own pace.

Day 7: Visit nearby monasteries and temples before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Kakarbhitta

The food scene in Kakarbhitta is an amalgamation of Indian, Nepalese, and even Bhutanese flavor. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages in Kakarbhitta:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momo: Delicious and steamed Nepalese dumplings with various fillings.
  • Thukpa: A traditional hot noodle soup with vegetables or meat.
  • Raksi: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice.

Local Beverages

  • Chai: A spicy milk tea that is a staple beverage in Nepal and India.
  • Lassi: A yoghurt-based drink available in sweet or salty flavors.

Where to Stay in Kakarbhitta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kakarbhitta offers various accommodation options that cater to every budget. The town has several budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels catering to backpackers and budget travelers. For those looking for luxury accommodations, there are several high-end hotels with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Kakarbhitta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kakarbhitta has an array of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or keepsakes. One of the famous souvenirs is the tea grown in the tea gardens in the area. Other local products include traditional clothing, handicrafts, and jewelry made by the local tribes.

Local Culture and Customs of Kakarbhitta

Kakarbhitta is a multicultural town, with different communities and tribes living together. The town’s diverse culture and customs are reflected through its cuisine, festivals, and traditions. The town celebrates various Hindu and Buddhist festivals, including Diwali, Dashain, and Buddha Jayanti.

Best Time to Visit Kakarbhitta

The best time to visit Kakarbhitta is between September to November during the autumn season and March to May in the spring season. During these times, the weather is mild and pleasant for exploration and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kakarbhitta

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and traditional beverages.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites and temples.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter the streets and public places.
  • Show affection in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kakarbhitta

Kakarbhitta is a relatively safe town, but visitors should remain vigilant with their belongings in public places. Be cautious of scams when booking transportation and tours. It’s recommended to pre-book transportation and accommodations beforehand.

Health in Kakarbhitta

Visitors should drink bottled or boiled water and bring necessary medications. It’s recommended to consult a doctor before visiting Kakarbhitta and have the necessary vaccinations if needed.

Getting to Kakarbhitta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kakarbhitta

Bhadrapur Airport is the nearest airport to Kakarbhitta, located around 27 kilometers away. The airport has regular flights connecting to Kathmandu and other major airports in Nepal.

Bus Stations Near Kakarbhitta

Kakarbhitta has several bus stations connecting to major destinations in Nepal and India. The town is connected to the nearest city, Biratnagar, through regular bus services.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire taxis or take shared jeeps from the nearest towns and cities to reach Kakarbhitta.

How to Get Around in Kakarbhitta

Visitors can explore the town or nearby attractions on foot, rent a bike or scooter, or hire a taxi or a shared jeep. Shared jeeps are the most popular means of transportation in the area.

Where to Go Next from Kakarbhitta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The following destinations are ideal for a day trip or extended stay:

  • Darjeeling, West Bengal
  • Ilam, Eastern Nepal
  • Bhadrapur, Eastern Nepal

Conclusion

Kakarbhitta offers a unique insight into Nepal’s lesser-known destinations, cultural diversity, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With this guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to Kakarbhitta by exploring the town’s attractions, indulging in local cuisine, and experiencing the local culture and customs.
