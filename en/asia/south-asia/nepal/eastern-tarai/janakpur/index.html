Introduction to Janakpur

Janakpur is one of the oldest and most historically rich cities in Nepal. The city is located in Eastern Tarai, in the south-central region of Nepal, and is also known as the birthplace of the Goddess Sita. Janakpur is a popular pilgrimage destination for Hindus and boasts of various Hindu temples, palaces, museums, and natural attractions. From its rich cultural heritage to its mouthwatering food scene, Janakpur offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the various aspects of Janakpur and help you plan an unforgettable journey to this hidden gem in Nepal.

List of Places to Visit in Janakpur

Janakpur offers a plethora of cultural and natural attractions that will leave you mesmerized. Let’s explore the various places to visit in Janakpur.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Janakpur has a rich cultural heritage and boasts of various historical and religious sites. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Janakpur:

Janaki Mandir

Janaki Mandir is a popular Hindu temple in Nepal and is dedicated to the Goddess Sita. The temple is surrounded by 60 spires and is built in Mughal architectural style. Janaki Mandir attracts thousands of pilgrims every year, especially during the festival of Ram Navami.

Ram Mandir

Ram Mandir is another popular Hindu temple in Janakpur and is dedicated to Lord Ram. The temple is located near the Janaki Mandir and is known for its intricate carvings and beautiful architecture.

Ram Janaki Museum

Ram Janaki Museum is located within the premises of Janaki Mandir and houses various artifacts related to the life of Lord Ram and Goddess Sita. The museum is a must-visit for history buffs and culture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Janakpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its cultural heritage, Janakpur is also home to various natural attractions. Here are some of the outdoor adventures and scenic views that you can explore in Janakpur:

Ganga Sagar Lake

Ganga Sagar Lake is a beautiful natural lake located in Janakpur and is one of the largest lakes in Nepal. The lake is surrounded by lush green forests and is a popular picnic spot for locals and tourists alike.

Dhanusha Dham

Dhanusha Dham is a famous Hindu pilgrimage site in Janakpur and is said to be the spot where Lord Ram broke Lord Shiva’s bow during his swayamvar. The site houses a small pond known as Dhanusha Sagar, where Lord Ram is said to have taken a bath.

Martyr’s Memorial Park

Martyr’s Memorial Park is a beautiful park located in Janakpur, dedicated to the Martyrs who fought for the independence of Nepal. The park offers a serene environment and is a popular spot for walking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Janakpur

Janakpur offers various hiking and biking routes, which provide an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the city. Here are some of the best routes that you can take:

  • Pathibhara: It is a popular hiking spot located on the outskirts of Janakpur, and offers picturesque views of the Himalayan range.
  • Biking Trails: There are various biking trails in and around Janakpur that provide a thrilling experience for adventure enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Janakpur: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the various cultural and natural attractions, Janakpur offers various activities and tours that will make your trip even more memorable. Here are some of the best things to do in Janakpur:

  • Explore the street food scene in Janakpur and try out the typical Nepalese dishes such as momos, chatamari, and sel roti.
  • Take a cultural tour of Janakpur and explore the various Hindu temples, palaces, and museums.
  • Attend the annual Ram Navami festival, which is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm in Janakpur.

Food and Drink Scene of Janakpur

Janakpur offers a unique food and drink scene, which reflects its rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you can enjoy in Janakpur:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chatamari: A Nepalese version of a pizza made with rice flour, minced meat, and eggs.
  • Sel Roti: A typical Nepalese sweet dish made with rice flour, sugar, and milk.
  • Momo: A type of dumpling filled with meat, vegetables, or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Chhang: A popular fermented alcoholic beverage made from rice, barley, or other grains.
  • Raksi: Another popular alcoholic beverage made from distilled rice or barley.

Where to Stay in Janakpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Janakpur has various accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some of the top places to stay in Janakpur:

  • Hotel Manaki International: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Janakpur, offering comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Guest House Nepal: A budget-friendly guesthouse located close to Janaki Mandir.
  • Hotel Ratna: A mid-range hotel offering comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Janakpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Janakpur is known for its unique handicrafts, art, and local products. Here are some of the best souvenirs and products that you can bring home:

  • Handwoven Dhaka Fabric: A type of fabric made from cotton or silk, which is commonly used in traditional Nepalese clothing.
  • Janakpuri Jutti: A type of handmade shoe made from leather that is a popular souvenir item in Janakpur.
  • Pashmina Shawls: High-quality handmade shawls made from the wool of the Himalayan mountain goat.

Local Culture and Customs of Janakpur

Janakpur is a culturally rich city, and it is essential to be aware of its customs and traditions while visiting. Here are some of the local customs and culture of Janakpur:

  • Nepalese people are generally friendly, and it is customary to greet people with a “Namaste” while bowing.
  • When visiting Hindu temples, it is important to remove your shoes before entering the temple.
  • It is customary to dress conservatively, especially while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Janakpur

The best time to visit Janakpur is from September to November and from February to April as the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Janakpur

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind while visiting Janakpur:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Bargain while shopping.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink in public places.
  • Take pictures of people without permission.
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Janakpur is generally a safe destination for tourists. Here are some safety and health tips that you should keep in mind while visiting:

Safety in Janakpur

  • Avoid carrying large sums of cash and valuables while traveling in public transport.
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid isolated areas.

Health in Janakpur

  • It is advisable to carry a mosquito repellent as dengue fever and malaria are prevalent in the region.
  • Drink only boiled or bottled water.
  • Seek medical attention immediately in case of any health issues.

Getting to Janakpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Janakpur is well-connected to various parts of Nepal and India via road, rail, and air. Here are some of the transportation options to reach Janakpur:

Airports Near Janakpur

The nearest airport to Janakpur is the Janakpur Airport, which is about 11 km from the city center. It has regular flights from Kathmandu and other major cities in Nepal.

Bus Stations Near Janakpur

Janakpur is well-connected to various parts of Nepal via bus. The major bus stations in Janakpur are Janakpur Bus Station and Bhanu Chowk Bus Station.

Other Options

Apart from flights and buses, you can also hire a taxi or a private car to reach Janakpur from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Janakpur

Janakpur has various transportation options to get around, including buses, taxis, and rickshaws. It is advisable to negotiate the fare before boarding.

Where to Go Next from Janakpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore while visiting Janakpur:

  • Kathmandu: The capital city of Nepal, which is famous for its cultural heritage and scenic beauty.
  • Chitwan National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site located in the Terai Lowlands, known for its wildlife safaris.
  • Darjeeling: A famous hill station in West Bengal, India, known for its tea gardens and scenic views.

Conclusion

Janakpur is a hidden gem in Nepal and offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. From its rich history to its vibrant food scene, Janakpur is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Nepal. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan an unforgettable journey to Janakpur.
