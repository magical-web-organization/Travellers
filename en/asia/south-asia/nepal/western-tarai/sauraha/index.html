Introduction to Sauraha

Welcome to Sauraha, a beautiful town tucked away in the heart of the Western Tarai region of Nepal, Asia. The town is well-known for its abundant natural beauty, exotic wildlife species, and cultural heritage. Sauraha is situated on the bank of the Rapti River, which serves as a gateway to the famous Chitwan National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Sauraha

Sauraha offers several tourist hotspots that you should not miss while exploring the region. Below are some of the must-visit sites.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tharu Cultural Museum

The Tharu Cultural Museum is the perfect destination to learn about the ancient Tharu culture, traditions, arts, and lifestyle. The museum houses several artifacts, tools, and antiques that are related to the Tharu community.

Balmiki Ashram

Balmiki Ashram is situated on the banks of the Rapti River and is known as the birthplace of the great Indian sage, Maharishi Balmiki. The ashram is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus and is well-known for its serene ambiance.

Devghat Dham

Devghat Dham is a famous Hindu pilgrimage site located about 20 kilometers from Sauraha. The place is a confluence of three rivers – Kali Gandaki, Seti, and Trishuli – and is considered sacred.

Natural Attractions of Sauraha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chitwan National Park

Chitwan National Park is one of the most popular national parks in Nepal and is located just a few kilometers away from Sauraha. The park is home to several rare and endangered animal species such as the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, and the Asian elephant. It is also home to several bird species and offers excellent opportunities for bird watching.

Rapti River

The Rapti River is a major tourist attraction in Sauraha, offering a range of activities such as bird watching, canoeing, and fishing. A relaxing boat ride along the serene river is a must-do activity for anyone visiting Sauraha.

Elephant Breeding Center

The Elephant Breeding Center is a unique attraction in Sauraha that allows visitors to see baby elephants up close. Visitors can watch baby elephants play, feed them, or even ride them.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sauraha

Sauraha’s surroundings offer several hiking and biking trails that lead to some of the most beautiful destinations in the region.

Sauraha to Kasara

The 15-kilometer hike from Sauraha to Kasara is a popular route among hiking enthusiasts. The trail offers stunning views of the forest and a chance to spot several animal species.

Sauraha to Hattisar

The 10-kilometer bike ride from Sauraha to Hattisar is a picturesque route that takes visitors through the beautiful countryside of Nepal. The route offers breathtaking views of the mountains and the lush green jungle.

Best Things to Do in Sauraha: Activities and Tours

Sauraha offers numerous activities and tours that cater to the interests and preferences of every visitor.

Jungle Safari

The jungle safari is a must-do activity in Sauraha. The safari takes visitors into the Chitwan National Park, where they can spot several animal species, including tigers, rhinoceros, and elephants.

Bird Watching

Sauraha is a well-known destination for bird watching enthusiasts. The region is home to several endemic bird species, including the Spiny Babbler, Nepal’s national bird.

Tharu Cultural Show

The Tharu Cultural Show is a traditional dance performance that showcases the unique culture and customs of the Tharu community. Visitors can enjoy the show while relishing authentic Tharu cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Sauraha

Sauraha offers several scrumptious dishes and local beverages that visitors should try while exploring the region.

Must-Try Dishes

Bara

Bara is a traditional pancake-like dish made from lentil paste and served with chutney.

Sel Roti

Sel Roti is a sweet, crispy snack made from rice flour and sugar.

Local Beverages

Raksi

Raksi is a traditional Nepali liquor made from fermented cereals.

Chhang

Chhang is a local beer made by fermenting millet or rice.

Local Culture and Customs of Sauraha

Sauraha is home to a vibrant and diverse culture that dates back several centuries. The Tharu community, which is the region’s major ethnic group, has a rich cultural heritage that visitors can learn about.

Best Time to Visit Sauraha

The best time to visit Sauraha is between October to March, when the weather is pleasant and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sauraha

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people or religious sites without their consent.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sauraha

Sauraha is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and not carrying large sums of money or valuable items while exploring.

Health in Sauraha

Visitors to Sauraha are advised to get vaccinated against diseases such as hepatitis A and B and typhoid before visiting. They should also carry mosquito repellents to protect themselves from mosquito-borne diseases like malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Sauraha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sauraha

The nearest airport to Sauraha is the Bharatpur Airport, located about 20 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Sauraha

Sauraha is connected to major cities and towns in Nepal through a network of buses. The nearest bus station is in Bharatpur, which is about 20 kilometers away.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cabs or taxis to reach Sauraha from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Sauraha

The best way to get around Sauraha is either on foot or by riding a bicycle. Visitors can also hire a taxi or a jeep to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Sauraha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pokhara

Pokhara, the second-largest city in Nepal, is a popular destination located about 135 kilometers west of Sauraha. The city is well-known for its stunning mountain views and adventure sports like trekking and paragliding.

Lumbini

Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, is a popular pilgrimage site located about 220 kilometers west of Sauraha. The region is home to several temples and Buddhist monasteries.

Conclusion

Sauraha is a hidden gem in Nepal, offering tourists a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of the Western Tarai region. The town offers a range of activities, including jungle safaris, hiking, and cultural shows, making it an ideal destination for every tourist.
