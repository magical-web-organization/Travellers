Introduction to Nepalgunj

Located in Western Tarai, Nepalgunj is a multicultural city in Nepal that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. As the gateway to the west, Nepalgunj is the perfect place to start your journey into the Himalayan region, and with its close proximity to the Indian border, it offers a unique cultural experience that you won’t find anywhere else in Nepal. Here is a comprehensive guide to Nepalgunj, including top attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Nepalgunj

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bageshwori Temple

Located in the heart of Nepalgunj city, Bageshwori Temple is a major Hindu temple dedicated to the Goddess Bageshwori. It is one of the most popular pilgrimage sites in Nepal and attracts devotees throughout the year.

Prithvi Narayan Campus

Prithvi Narayan Campus is one of the oldest academic institutions in Nepal and is located in the heart of Nepalgunj. It is a sprawling campus that houses several buildings, including the historic palace of the former rulers of Banke.

Shitalpati

Shitalpati, also known as the “carpet village,” is a unique cultural attraction in Nepalgunj. It is a small village where the Tharu community produces exquisite handwoven mats made from Murta grass.

Natural Attractions of Nepalgunj: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bardia National Park

Bardia National Park is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Nepal. Situated just a few kilometers from Nepalgunj, it is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, including the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, and Asian elephant.

Karnali River

The Karnali River, one of the three major rivers in Nepal, is an ideal destination for rafting and kayaking enthusiasts. The river flows through the pristine wilderness of Western Nepal and offers spectacular views of the Himalayas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nepalgunj

Rara Lake Trek

The Rara Lake Trek is a popular hiking route that takes you through the remote regions of Western Nepal. The trek starts from Nepalgunj and passes through the beautiful Rara Lake, which is the largest freshwater lake in Nepal.

Bardiya Jungle Safari

The Bardiya Jungle Safari is a biking trail that takes you through the stunning forests of Bardia National Park. The trail is perfect for adrenaline junkies and nature lovers alike.

Best Things to Do in Nepalgunj: Activities and Tours

Tharu Village Tour

The Tharu Village Tour is a unique cultural experience that takes you through the traditional villages of the Tharu community. The tour includes a visit to the Shitalpati village and an opportunity to witness the production of handwoven mats.

Bird Watching

Bardia National Park is a paradise for bird watchers. With over 400 species of birds, including the endangered Bengal florican and white-rumped vulture, Bardia National Park is a bird lover’s paradise.

Food and Drink Scene of Nepalgunj

Must-Try Dishes

Dal Bhat

Dal Bhat is the staple food of Nepal, and you must try it when in Nepalgunj. It is a delicious meal that consists of lentil soup, steamed rice, and vegetables.

Chiura

Chiura is a popular snack in Nepalgunj made from beaten rice. It is a quick and healthy snack that is perfect for on-the-go.

Local Beverages

Chhaang

Chhaang is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from millet or rice. It is a popular drink in Nepalgunj and is usually consumed during festivals and special occasions.

Tea

Tea is a popular beverage in Nepalgunj, and you can find tea stalls almost everywhere. Masala tea, lemon tea, and milk tea are some popular varieties.

Where to Stay in Nepalgunj: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Sneha

Hotel Sneha is a top-rated hotel in Nepalgunj that offers comfortable and spacious rooms, a restaurant serving delicious local and international cuisine, and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of the city.

Surkhet Lodge

Surkhet Lodge is a budget-friendly option in Nepalgunj that offers clean and comfortable rooms, a restaurant serving homemade Nepali cuisine, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Nepalgunj: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tharu Handicrafts

Tharu Handicrafts are unique handicrafts made by the Tharu community, such as handwoven mats, baskets, and wooden carvings. These handicrafts are a great souvenir to bring back home.

Pashmina Shawls

Pashmina Shawls, made from the wool of the Himalayan mountain goat, are a luxurious item that you can purchase in Nepalgunj. They are warm, soft, and lightweight, and make a great gift for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Nepalgunj

Festivals

Nepalgunj celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including Bageshwori Jatra, Narakasura Vadha, and Chhath Puja. These festivals are a great opportunity to witness and experience the rich cultural heritage of Nepal.

Clothing

When visiting cultural sites in Nepalgunj, it is important to dress conservatively and cover your shoulders and knees to show respect for the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Nepalgunj

The best time to visit Nepalgunj is from October to March. The weather is cool and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nepalgunj

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting cultural sites
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities such as drug abuse
  • Show public displays of affection
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Nepalgunj

Nepalgunj is a safe and peaceful city, but it is recommended that you take basic safety precautions such as avoiding unlit areas at night and being vigilant of your surroundings.

Health in Nepalgunj

It is important to take necessary precautions to prevent illnesses such as malaria and dengue fever when visiting Nepalgunj. This includes using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved shirts and pants.

Getting to Nepalgunj: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nepalgunj

Nepalgunj Airport is the closest airport to Nepalgunj, and it offers regular flights from Kathmandu.

Bus Stations Near Nepalgunj

Nepalgunj Bus Station is the main bus station in Nepalgunj and offers regular bus services to other major cities in Nepal.

Other Options

You can also reach Nepalgunj by taxi or private car.

How to Get Around in Nepalgunj

The best way to get around Nepalgunj is by taxi or motorbike. You can also rent bicycles and explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Nepalgunj: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dang Valley

Dang Valley, located just a few hours from Nepalgunj, offers stunning natural beauty and opportunities for outdoor adventures such as hiking and rock climbing.

Lucknow

Lucknow, the capital of Uttar Pradesh in India, is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage. It is just a few hours drive from the Nepal-India border and makes for a great day trip from Nepalgunj.

Conclusion

Nepalgunj is a hidden gem in Nepal that offers an authentic cultural experience combined with natural beauty and outdoor adventures. With its unique blend of Hindu and Muslim cultures, Nepalgunj is a melting pot of traditions, religions, and people. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Nepalgunj and make the most of your time in this stunning city.
