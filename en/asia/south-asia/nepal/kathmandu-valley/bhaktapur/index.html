Introduction to Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur is an ancient city located in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal. Known for its well-preserved historical sites, religious monuments, and traditional crafts, Bhaktapur is a popular destination for tourists seeking an authentic cultural experience. With its narrow alleys, artisan workshops, and stunning architecture, Bhaktapur offers a glimpse into the traditional way of life in Nepal.

List of Places to Visit in Bhaktapur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Located in the heart of the city, Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to the 12th century. Home to numerous palaces, temples, and shrines, this square is one of the most cherished historical sites in Nepal. The iconic 55-window Palace, the Lion’s Gate, and the Golden Gate are some of the major attractions within the square.

National Art Museum

Housed in the Taumadhi Square, the National Art Museum is a must-visit for art enthusiasts. It showcases a diverse collection of Nepali art, including sculptures, paintings, textiles, and more.

Pashupatinath Temple

Built in the 17th century, the Pashupatinath Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the holiest places for the Hindus. Located on the banks of the Bagmati River, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Natural Attractions of Bhaktapur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Changu Narayan Temple

Located on a hilltop, the Changu Narayan Temple is considered the oldest Hindu temple in Nepal. The temple offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and the Kathmandu Valley. The intricate wood and stone carvings, dating back to the 5th century, are a testament to the rich artistic heritage of Nepal.

Nagarkot

A scenic hill station located on the outskirts of Bhaktapur, Nagarkot offers panoramic views of the Himalayan mountains. The sunrise and sunset views are particularly breathtaking, with the snow-capped peaks of Mount Everest visible on clear days.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur offers several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure seekers. The trek to Nagarkot is a popular option, with the trail passing through lush forests, terraced fields, and rural villages. The Balthali Village Hike is another option, with the trail taking you through rhododendron forests and offering stunning views of the Himalayan Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Bhaktapur: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Bhaktapur, including pottery making, woodcarving, and traditional thangka painting classes. A guided tour of the city and its attractions will give you a deeper understanding of the history and culture of Bhaktapur.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhaktapur

Must-Try Dishes

Juju Dhau

A traditional Nepali dessert, Juju Dhau or “King of Yoghurt” is a fermented yogurt that is sweet and creamy. The yogurt is served in traditional clay pots and is a must-try dish in Bhaktapur.

Chatamari

A Nepali version of the pizza, Chatamari is a thin crust made of rice flour and is topped with minced meat, vegetables, and eggs. It is a popular snack food in Bhaktapur.

Local Beverages

Chhang

A fermented drink made from rice, Chhang is a popular alcoholic beverage in Nepal. It has a strong flavor and is often flavored with fruits or spices.

Masala Tea

A classic Nepali drink, Masala Tea is made with milk, tea leaves, cinnamon, cloves, and ginger. It is a beloved drink throughout Nepal and is perfect for a chilly day.

Where to Stay in Bhaktapur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bhaktapur has plenty of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels.

Hotel Heritage

Located in the heart of Bhaktapur, Hotel Heritage is a boutique hotel that offers comfortable rooms and personalized service. The hotel’s restaurant serves delicious Nepali cuisine, and the rooftop terrace offers stunning views of the city.

Sunny Guest House

A budget-friendly option, Sunny Guest House is located in a quiet neighborhood and offers clean rooms and basic amenities. The guest house has a garden and rooftop terrace, and the staff is friendly and welcoming.

What to Buy in Bhaktapur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pottery

Bhaktapur is famous for its pottery, with artisans using traditional techniques to create beautiful clay pots, vases, and other decorative items.

Paubha Paintings

A form of traditional Nepali painting, Paubha paintings depict Hindu and Buddhist deities and scenes from mythology. They are usually painted on cotton or silk and make for a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur is a predominantly Hindu city, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and remove your shoes before entering temples and shrines.

Best Time to Visit Bhaktapur

The best time to visit Bhaktapur is during the autumn months (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and there is less rainfall. The spring months (March to May) are also a good time to visit, with the rhododendrons in bloom and the weather beginning to warm up.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhaktapur

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Nepali

Don’t:

  • Touch religious idols or artifacts
  • Eat or drink in public during Ramadan
  • Disrespect local culture or customs
  • Take photographs without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur is a generally safe city, but visitors should be cautious of petty theft and pickpocketing. It is also recommended to avoid political demonstrations and rallies.

Health in Bhaktapur

Visitors to Bhaktapur should take precautions to avoid the risk of altitude sickness, especially if trekking to higher elevations. It is also important to stay hydrated and use insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever.

Getting to Bhaktapur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bhaktapur

The nearest international airport to Bhaktapur is Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu. From there, it is a short drive or bus ride to Bhaktapur.

Bus Stations Near Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur is well-connected by bus to various cities in Nepal, with buses running from the Kathmandu bus station and the Bhaktapur bus station.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire for those who prefer a more comfortable mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur is a small city, and most of the major attractions can be explored on foot. Bicycles and motorbikes are also available for rent for those who prefer to explore at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Bhaktapur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kathmandu

The capital city of Nepal, Kathmandu is a bustling metropolis that is home to several historical sites, temples, and markets.

Patan

Another ancient city in the Kathmandu Valley, Patan is known for its well-preserved palaces, temples, and other historical sites.

Conclusion

Bhaktapur is a city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and tradition. With its stunning architecture, vibrant markets, and natural beauty, Bhaktapur is a must-visit for anyone visiting the Kathmandu Valley. From hiking and biking to pottery making and thangka painting classes, Bhaktapur offers something for everyone. Follow our guide to make the most of your visit to this charming city.
