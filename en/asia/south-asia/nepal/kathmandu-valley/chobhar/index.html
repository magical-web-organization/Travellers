Introduction to Chobhar

Chobhar is a scenic village located in the southwestern part of the Kathmandu Valley in Nepal. It is known for its natural beauty, cultural significance, and religious importance. Chobhar is situated on a hill overlooking the Bagmati River, which adds to its charm. The village is home to a number of attractions that cater to travelers and locals alike, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat or an adventurous vacation. In this travel guide, we will take you through the top things to do in Chobhar, including exploring cultural sites, hiking and cycling, trying local cuisine, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Chobhar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chobhar has a rich cultural and historical heritage that is worth exploring. Some of the must-visit cultural sites in the village include:

  • Chobhar Gorge: This natural landmark is a historical site that is said to have been created when a saint named Manjushree cut the hill to drain the water from the Kathmandu Valley.

  • Kirtipur: Kirtipur is a nearby town that is known for its history and architecture. It was once a powerful kingdom and has preserved many old buildings and temples, including the Bagh Bhairab temple, which is dedicated to the deity of the same name.

  • National Museum: The National Museum of Nepal is located in Chhauni, about 6.8 kilometers from Chobhar. It contains artifacts and exhibits related to the history, culture, and religion of Nepal.

Natural Attractions of Chobhar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chobhar is surrounded by scenic landscapes and natural wonders that offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventurers and nature enthusiasts. Some of the most popular natural attractions in the village include:

  • Chobhar Hill: The hill provides panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and cycling.

  • Bagmati River: The river is a source of irrigation for the Kathmandu Valley and is also considered holy by Hindus. Visitors can go on a rafting or kayaking expedition or take a tranquil boat ride along the river.

  • Dakshinkali Temple: The temple is dedicated to the goddess Kali and is located about 4 kilometers from Chobhar. It is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus and is surrounded by scenic forests and hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chobhar

Chobhar is a popular destination for hiking and cycling enthusiasts due to its varied terrain and scenic landscapes. Here are a few of the most popular routes in the area:

  • Chobhar Hill Hike: The hike to the top of Chobhar Hill is a must-do for visitors to the village. The trail is steep in places but rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valley.

  • Kirtipur Trek: This trek connects Chobhar to the nearby town of Kirtipur and passes through traditional villages, forests, and hills. The trail is suitable for both hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Chobhar: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting cultural sites and exploring natural attractions, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Chobhar. Some of the best things to do in the village include:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Chobhar is famous for its Newari cuisine, which is a traditional Nepalese cuisine that includes dishes such as momos, choila, and bara. Visitors can try these dishes at local restaurants and cafes.

  • Visit Local Markets: The village has many local markets where visitors can buy souvenirs, handicrafts, and locally grown produce.

  • Attend Festivals: Chobhar celebrates many festivals throughout the year, including the Dashain and Tihar festivals. Visitors can witness cultural performances and join in the festivities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chobhar

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Chobhar:

Day 1 - Arrive in Kathmandu and check into your hotel. Rest for the day to get acclimatized.

Day 2 - Explore the cultural sites in Kathmandu, including the Pashupatinath Temple, the Boudhanath Stupa, and the Swayambhunath Temple.

Day 3 - Take a day trip to Chobhar and hike to the top of Chobhar Hill for scenic views of the valley.

Day 4 - Go on a rafting or kayaking trip down the Bagmati River.

Day 5 - Spend the day exploring the town of Kirtipur and its cultural attractions.

Day 6 - Attend a local festival or market in Chobhar.

Day 7 - Depart from Kathmandu.

Food and Drink Scene of Chobhar

Must-Try Dishes

Chobhar is famous for its Newari cuisine, which includes dishes such as:

  • Momos: These are traditional Nepalese dumplings filled with vegetables or meat and served with a spicy dipping sauce.

  • Choila: A dish made from marinated meat that is grilled and served with beaten rice.

  • Bara: A lentil-based snack that is deep-fried and served with a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

Chobhar is known for its local alcoholic beverages, including:

  • Chyang: A traditional Newari alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice.

  • Raksi: A distilled liquor made from fermented grains or fruits.

Where to Stay in Chobhar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Chobhar, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Some of the best options include:

  • Chobhar Resort: This is a luxurious resort located on the outskirts of the village, offering scenic views of the valley and top-notch amenities.

  • Kathmandu Guest House: Located in the nearby city of Kathmandu, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and easy access to cultural attractions.

  • Harvest Moon Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Chobhar, offering clean rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Chobhar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chobhar has many local shops and markets where visitors can buy souvenirs and locally made products. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can buy a range of handmade crafts such as pottery, paintings, and textiles.

  • Spices: Chobhar is known for its flavorful spices, such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric.

  • Musical Instruments: Traditional Nepalese musical instruments such as the madal and the sarangi can be purchased from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Chobhar

The people of Chobhar are predominantly Newaris, who have a distinct culture and language. The Newar community is known for its rich history, music, and art. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as removing their shoes before entering a temple or home.

Best Time to Visit Chobhar

The best time to visit Chobhar is from September to November or from March to May when the weather is mild, and the skies are clear. The monsoon season (June to August) can bring heavy rain, making it difficult to trek or explore the outdoors. The winter season (December to February) can be cold and foggy, but this is also when the air quality in Kathmandu is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chobhar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting temples or religious sites.

  • Try local cuisine and drinks.

  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove anything from religious monuments or temples.

  • Use your left hand to touch or receive things.

  • Take photographs without asking for permission.

  • Disrespect the environment or local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chobhar

Chobhar is a safe and peaceful village with low crime rates. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as not carrying too much cash or valuables and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Chobhar

Visitors to Nepal should take some basic health precautions, such as getting vaccinated for diseases such as typhoid and hepatitis A, drinking only bottled water or treated water, and avoiding street food.

Getting to Chobhar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chobhar

The nearest airport to Chobhar is Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, which is about 15 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Chobhar.

Bus Stations Near Chobhar

There are several bus stations in Kathmandu that offer regular services to Chobhar, including the Gongabu Bus Station and the Kalanki Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or taxi from the city center or Kathmandu to Chobhar, which takes about 30-45 minutes.

How to Get Around in Chobhar

The best way to get around Chobhar is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a taxi or motorcycle to explore the area. It is important to negotiate the price beforehand and avoid overpaying.

Where to Go Next from Chobhar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chobhar is located in the Kathmandu Valley, which is home to several other picturesque towns and villages that are worth exploring. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Patan: A neighboring town that is known for its ancient architecture and heritage.

  • Nagarkot: A scenic hill station located about 32 kilometers from Kathmandu that offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

  • Bhaktapur: An ancient city that is home to traditional Newari architecture and culture.

Conclusion

Chobhar is a hidden gem in the Kathmandu Valley that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are interested in exploring historical sites, hiking, experiencing local cuisine and culture, or simply enjoying the stunning views of the valley, Chobhar has something for everyone. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Chobhar and get the most out of your visit to this beautiful Nepalese village.
