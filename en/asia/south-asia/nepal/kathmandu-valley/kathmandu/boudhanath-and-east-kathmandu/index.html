Introduction to Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Boudhanath and East Kathmandu are two of the most culturally diverse and historically rich destinations in Nepal. East Kathmandu is home to many ancient temples and pagodas, while Boudhanath is known for its stunning Stupa and Tibetan culture. Located in the Kathmandu Valley, Boudhanath and East Kathmandu offer a blend of urban and natural experiences.

This travel guide will help you plan your visit to Boudhanath and East Kathmandu. Here’s everything you need to know about the best places to visit, local food and drink scene, cultural customs, safety tips, transportation options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pashupatinath Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most significant Hindu temples in Nepal.
  • Swayambhunath Temple: A stunning temple also known as the Monkey Temple, located on a hilltop providing panoramic views of the city.
  • Changu Narayan Temple: One of the oldest temples in Nepal, built in the 5th century and dedicated to Lord Vishnu.
  • Patan Durbar Square: A complex of ancient temples, palaces, and courtyards, famous for its intricate carvings and stunning architecture.

Natural Attractions of Boudhanath and East Kathmandu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nagarkot: A scenic hill station located at an altitude of 2,175 meters, known for its stunning sunrise and sunset views over the Himalayas.
  • Shivapuri Nagarjun National Park: A protected forest reserve, home to various flora and fauna, ideal for trekking, hiking, and camping.
  • Dhulikhel: A charming town in the Kavrepalanchok District, known for its scenic views of the Himalayas, peaceful temples, and traditional Newari architecture.
  • Bhaktapur Durbar Square: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its ancient temples, palaces, and intricate wood carvings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

There are numerous hiking and biking routes in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu, which offer an excellent opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Boudhanath - Nagarkot hiking trail
  • Bhaktapur - Nagarkot biking route
  • Shivapuri Nagarjun National Park trek
  • Sundarijal - Chisapani mountain biking trail

Best Things to Do in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu: Activities and Tours

  • Take part in a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Nepalese dishes.
  • Experience a traditional Newari feast, comprising of several courses of delicious food served on banana leaves.
  • Attend a pottery workshop and try your hand at making pottery items.
  • Visit a Tibetan refugee camp and learn about their culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Day 1: Arrival and Boudhanath Stupa visit.
Day 2: Pashupatinath Temple and Patan Durbar Square exploration. Day 3: Bhaktapur Durbar Square visit and Nagarkot hike. Day 4: Explore Swayambhunath Temple and Kathmandu Durbar Square. Day 5: Visit a Tibetan refugee camp and take part in their cultural activities. Day 6: Shivapuri Nagarjun National Park trek or Dhulikhel visit. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momos: Steamed or fried dumplings stuffed with vegetables, meat, or cheese.
  • Dal Bhat: A staple dish in Nepalese cuisine, comprising of lentil soup, rice, curry, and various accompaniments.
  • Sel Roti: A sweetened rice flour bread, traditionally prepared during festivals and occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Tea: A flavorful tea with aromatic spices such as cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger.
  • Chhang: A traditional Nepalese alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet or rice.
  • Lassi: A refreshing yoghurt-based drink, often served with fruit or spices.

Where to Stay in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options available in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu, catering to different budgets and preferences. Some of the popular options are:

  • Hyatt Regency Kathmandu: A luxurious 5-star hotel, located in Boudhanath, offering modern amenities and stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • Shambaling Hotel: A boutique hotel in Boudhanath, adorned with traditional Tibetan decor, offering hospitality and comfort.
  • Backpacker’s Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in Thamel, offering clean rooms and a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thangka Paintings: Traditional Himalayan Buddhist art, depicting deities, mandalas, and other visual themes.
  • Pashmina Shawls: Soft and luxurious woolen shawls, made from the undercoat of the Himalayan mountain goat.
  • Scented Incense Sticks: Aromatic incense sticks made from natural herbs and spices, commonly used in religious ceremonies.

Local Culture and Customs of Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Nepalese culture is a blend of different ethnic groups, languages, and religions. Some of the cultural customs and etiquettes to observe while visiting Boudhanath and East Kathmandu are:

  • Respect the local culture, customs, and religious beliefs
  • Remove shoes before entering temples and holy places
  • Dress modestly, covering shoulders, legs, and midriffs
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places
  • Tipping is not customary, but appreciated in restaurants and hotels

Best Time to Visit Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

The best time to visit Boudhanath and East Kathmandu is from September to November and from February to May. During these months, the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities, and the skies are clear, offering stunning views of the Himalayas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be open to trying new foods and experiences
  • Bargain while shopping in local markets
  • Stay hydrated and carry a water bottle
  • Take adequate precautions for safety and health

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Touch or disrespect religious artifacts and objects
  • Use drugs, consume alcohol or smoke in public places
  • Litter or dispose of waste irresponsibly
  • Participate in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Boudhanath and East Kathmandu are generally safe destinations, but it is always advisable to take adequate safety precautions, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night
  • Keep your valuables secure and hidden
  • Be cautious of pickpockets and tourist scams
  • Use a reputable taxi or transportation service
  • Follow road safety rules

Health in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

It is essential to take adequate health precautions while traveling to Boudhanath and East Kathmandu, such as:

  • Carry a first-aid kit and necessary medications
  • Drink boiled or bottled water
  • Avoid street food and uncooked food
  • Apply mosquito repellent and carry a mosquito net
  • Seek medical attention if feeling unwell

Getting to Boudhanath and East Kathmandu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

The nearest airport to Boudhanath and East Kathmandu is Tribhuvan International Airport, located about 6 km away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Boudhanath and East Kathmandu are well-connected by bus services, and the main bus stations are:

  • Gongabu Bus Station
  • Ratna Park Bus Station

Other Options

Private taxis, shared taxis, and rental cars are also available for transportation within and around Boudhanath and East Kathmandu.

How to Get Around in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu

Boudhanath and East Kathmandu are easily explorable on foot, but other transportation options are also available, such as:

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services
  • Buses and micro-buses
  • Bikes and motorbikes for rent
  • Rickshaws and pedal carts

Where to Go Next from Boudhanath and East Kathmandu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions from Boudhanath and East Kathmandu, such as:

  • Pokhara: A scenic city located about 200 km from Kathmandu, known for its trekking trails, serene lakes, and stunning mountain views.
  • Chitwan National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, located about 150 km from Kathmandu, famous for its wildlife safari and cultural experiences.
  • Lumbini: A sacred Buddhist site, located about 280 km from Kathmandu, known as the birthplace of Lord Buddha.

Conclusion

Boudhanath and East Kathmandu offer a unique blend of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. From exploring ancient temples and palaces to hiking in national parks, indulging in local cuisine, and experiencing the hospitality of the locals, there’s something for everyone in Boudhanath and East Kathmandu. Follow our guide, plan your trip, and make memories that will last a lifetime.
