Introduction to Kirtipur

Located in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal, Kirtipur is a small city steeped in history and culture. Often overlooked by travelers, it offers a unique glimpse into Nepal’s rich past, with its architectural treasures and ancient temples. The city is not as developed as other cities in Nepal, but that only adds to its charm. It is an ideal destination for anyone looking to experience the traditional side of Nepal.

This travel guide will cover all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Kirtipur, Nepal, from the top attractions to the best time to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Kirtipur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bagh Bhairab Temple

Bagh Bhairab Temple is a significant Hindu temple located in Kirtipur. This temple is dedicated to the Hindu deity Bhairab and is known for its unique architecture. The roof of the temple is adorned with a wooden frame, which stands on columns intricately painted with images of gods and goddesses. The temple is especially popular during the annual Bagh Bhairab Jatra festival.

Uma Maheshwar Temple

Uma Maheshwar Temple is one of the oldest temples in Kirtipur. It has a special place in the hearts of the people of Kirtipur as it was built by the local Newar community. The temple is a beautiful example of traditional Newari architecture and is dedicated to the gods Shiva and Parvati.

Tribhuwan University

Tribhuwan University is Nepal’s oldest and largest university located on the outskirts of Kirtipur. The university is spread out over multiple campuses, and its main campus is located within the Kirtipur city limits. While the university is not open to visitors, the campus itself is an attraction in its own right. It is one of the greenest areas in Kathmandu Valley.

Natural Attractions of Kirtipur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Champa Devi Hiking Trail

Champa Devi is the highest hill in the valley surrounding Kirtipur, and it offers a hiking opportunity for adventure seekers. The hike is steep, so it is recommended for experienced hikers. The trail takes you through lush forests and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas and the valley below.

Taudaha Lake

Taudaha Lake is a serene, freshwater lake located just outside Kirtipur. The lake offers a quiet escape from the hustle and bustle of Kathmandu. You can take a boat ride on the lake or relax on its banks and watch the birds.

Taudaha Bazaar

Taudaha Bazaar is a traditional and vibrant market located near Taudaha Lake. It offers an authentic Nepalese market experience, complete with shops selling local handicrafts, fresh produce, and street food.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirtipur

Hiking Trails

Besides the Champa Devi hiking trail, Kirtipur has many other hiking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The hiking trails in Kirtipur offer stunning views of the valley and the Himalayan range.

Biking Routes

Kirtipur provides several opportunities for bikers to explore the valley’s scenic beauty. The hilly terrain of Kirtipur offers challenging yet enjoyable routes for mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Kirtipur: Activities and Tours

Attend a Cultural Festival

Kirtipur hosts several cultural festivals throughout the year, which offer visitors an opportunity to experience Nepalese culture and tradition. The Bagh Bhairab Jatra festival and Indrayani Jatra festival are two festivals that are particularly popular among the locals.

Shop at Taudaha Bazaar

Taudaha Bazaar is the best place to buy authentic Nepalese handicrafts, clothes, and jewelry. The market is colorful, vibrant, and offers a unique flavor of Nepalese culture.

Go Bird Watching

Taudaha Lake is a birdwatcher’s paradise, as it is home to several species of birds, including kingfishers, egrets, and cormorants. You can spend the day bird watching, observing the different species, and listening to their songs.

Food and Drink Scene of Kirtipur

Must-Try Dishes

Yomari

Yomari is a popular Newari delicacy made from rice flour and filled with a sweet mixture of sesame seeds, coconut, and molasses. It is often served during festivals and celebrations.

Bara

Bara is a lentil-based patty that is popular throughout Nepal. It is often served as a snack, and it is delicious when paired with spicy tomato sauce or achar.

Local Beverages

Thwon

Thwon is a traditional Newari rice beer that is commonly consumed in Kirtipur. It is a slightly sour, milky-white brew with a distinct aroma and unique flavor. It is often served during festivals and events.

Tea

Tea is an integral part of Nepalese culture and is consumed throughout the day. The tea in Kirtipur is particularly famous for its unique flavor, which is achieved through the use of milk and spices such as ginger and cardamom.

What to Buy in Kirtipur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kirtipur is known for its unique handicrafts and textiles. Some of the best items to buy as souvenirs include:

  • Khukuri Knives
  • Hand-knitted woolen sweaters, hats, and gloves
  • Traditional Newari mats and rugs

Local Culture and Customs of Kirtipur

The people of Kirtipur are primarily Newars, who have a distinct culture and language. The Newars are known for their traditional architecture and handicrafts, including pottery, weaving, and wood carving.

Best Time to Visit Kirtipur

The best time to visit Kirtipur is from October to November when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. This time is ideal for hiking and outdoor activities as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kirtipur

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Consume drugs and alcohol in public places.
  • Smoke in prohibited areas.
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior towards locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kirtipur

Kirtipur is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, it is recommended that you take basic precautions such as not carrying too much cash and avoiding walking alone in secluded areas at night.

Health in Kirtipur

Due to the city’s elevation, some visitors may experience altitude sickness. It is recommended to hydrate properly and take it easy for the first few days. It is also advisable to get vaccinated against common illnesses such as typhoid and hepatitis A.

Getting to Kirtipur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kirtipur

The closest airport to Kirtipur is the Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, which is approximately 14 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kirtipur

Kirtipur is well connected to neighboring cities and towns by a network of buses. The city has its bus station, which offers both local and long-distance routes.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire and are a convenient option for travelers who prefer privacy or have a tight schedule.

How to Get Around in Kirtipur

Kirtipur is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and rickshaws are also available for hire, and they are an affordable option for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Kirtipur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kirtipur is an excellent base to explore the Kathmandu Valley, which has several attractions of historical, cultural, and natural significance. Some of the top destinations to add to your itinerary include:

  • Bhaktapur
  • Patan
  • Changunarayan Temple
  • Langtang National Park

Conclusion

Kirtipur is a hidden gem in the Kathmandu Valley, offering travelers a unique chance to immerse themselves in Nepal’s history and culture. It is an ideal destination for those who prefer to explore offbeat places and is a must-visit if you are in Nepal. This comprehensive guide should provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Kirtipur.
