Introduction to Periche

Periche is a picturesque village located in the Khumbu region of the Himalayas in Nepal. At an elevation of 4,240 meters above sea level, it is a popular destination for trekkers and mountaineers. The village is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and glaciers, providing breathtaking views to visitors.

Periche is a small village, but it has plenty of things to offer for tourists. The village is home to several hotels, guesthouses, cafes, and shops. The natural beauty of the area and the welcoming spirit of the locals make it an unforgettable experience for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Periche

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tengboche Monastery: Located 20 kilometers from Periche, the Tengboche Monastery is one of the most important Buddhist monasteries in the region. It is also the largest monastery in Khumbu, and it is home to over 50 monks.
  • Khumjung Monastery: Located at an altitude of 3,790 meters, the Khumjung Monastery is another important Buddhist monastery in the region. It is home to the famous Yeti scalp and the skull of a Himalayan black bear.
  • Sagarmatha National Park Visitor Center: The Sagarmatha National Park Visitor Center is a great place to learn about the local flora and fauna. It also showcases the customs and traditions of the Sherpa community.

Natural Attractions of Periche: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ama Dablam: Ama Dablam is a beautiful mountain peak that is located near Periche. It is considered one of the most beautiful mountains in the world, and it is a popular destination for trekkers and mountaineers.
  • Island Peak: Also known as Imja Tse, Island Peak is a popular trekking peak in the Khumbu region. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.
  • Khumbu Glacier: The Khumbu Glacier is the highest glacier in Nepal. It is located at an altitude of 4,900 meters above sea level and is a popular destination for trekkers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Periche

Hiking Routes

  • Periche to Namche Bazaar: This is a popular hiking route that takes around five days to complete. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains, glaciers, and forests.
  • Periche to Lobuche: This is a challenging hiking route that takes around 4-5 hours to complete. It offers breathtaking views of the Khumbu Glacier and the surrounding mountains.
  • Periche to Dingboche: This is an easy hiking route that takes around 2-3 hours to complete. It offers beautiful views of Ama Dablam and other surrounding mountains.

Biking Routes

  • Periche to Gorak Shep: This is a popular biking route that takes around 3-4 hours to complete. It offers stunning views of the Khumbu Glacier and the surrounding mountains.
  • Periche to Tengboche Monastery: This is an easy biking route that takes around 2-3 hours to complete. It offers beautiful views of the Tengboche Monastery and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Periche: Activities and Tours

  • Trekking and Mountaineering: Periche is a popular destination for trekkers and mountaineers. The area is home to several peaks, such as Ama Dablam and Island Peak, which offer challenging and rewarding climbing experiences.
  • Explore Local Villages: Periche is surrounded by several small villages that offer a glimpse into the local way of life. Visitors can explore these villages to learn about the local customs and traditions.
  • Wildlife Watching: The area around Periche is home to several rare species of flora and fauna, including the snow leopard and the Himalayan black bear. Visitors can go on wildlife watching tours to spot these animals.

Food and Drink Scene of Periche

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Bhat: A traditional Nepali dish that consists of rice and lentil soup. It is typically accompanied by vegetables and a spicy sauce.
  • Momos: Dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, served with a spicy dipping sauce.
  • Thukpa: A noodle soup dish that is similar to ramen. It is typically made with vegetables or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: A traditional tea made with tea leaves, yak butter, and salt. It is a popular beverage in Himalayan regions.
  • Chang: A local alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet. It is typically served in pottery mugs and has a slightly sour taste.

What to Buy in Periche: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Prayer Flags: Colorful flags with Buddhist prayers printed on them. They are commonly found in the region and are used to bring good luck and fortune.
  • Yak Wool Products: Warm clothing items, such as hats and gloves, made from the wool of yaks.
  • Khukuri Knives: Traditional Nepali knives that are commonly used for hunting and outdoor activities.

Local Culture and Customs of Periche

The Sherpa people are the indigenous people in the region and have a unique culture and way of life. They are known for their expertise in mountaineering and trekking and have been an integral part of the mountain tourism industry in Nepal.

Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions when visiting the area. Dress modestly, be mindful of local etiquette, and ask for permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Periche

The best time to visit Periche is during the autumn months of September to November or in the spring months of March to May. During these months, the weather is mild, and the skies are clear, providing great views of the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Periche

  • Stay hydrated and acclimatize properly

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Periche

Periche is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should be mindful of the high altitude and take proper precautions. It is recommended to acclimatize properly, stay hydrated, and take necessary safety measures when trekking or mountaineering. Visitors should also purchase travel insurance before visiting Periche.

Health in Periche

Altitude sickness is a common health problem that visitors may experience in Periche. Visitors should be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness and take proper precautions. It is recommended to drink plenty of fluids and avoid alcohol and tobacco.

Getting to Periche: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Periche

The closest airport to Periche is the Tenzing Hillary Airport in Lukla, which is around a 45-minute flight from Kathmandu. From Lukla, visitors can hike or hire a porter to reach Periche.

Bus Stations Near Periche

There are no direct bus services to Periche. Visitors can take a bus from Kathmandu to Jiri, and from there, they can hire a jeep to reach Lukla. From Lukla, visitors can hike or hire a porter to reach Periche.

Other Options

Helicopter services are available from Kathmandu to Lukla, and from Lukla, visitors can hike or hire a porter to reach Periche.

How to Get Around in Periche

Visitors can explore Periche and the surrounding areas on foot. The area offers several hiking and biking trails that cater to varying levels of difficulty. Visitors can also hire a porter to carry their luggage or hire a guide to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Periche: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Namche Bazaar: A bustling market town that is a popular stop for trekkers and mountaineers. Located 13 kilometers from Periche, it offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Gokyo Lakes: A series of six turquoise glacier-fed lakes that are located at an altitude of over 5,000 meters. It offers stunning views of Mount Everest and other surrounding mountains.

Conclusion

Periche is a must-visit destination for trekkers, mountaineers, and nature lovers. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers and provides a glimpse into the unique culture and way of life of the Sherpa people. Visitors can explore the area on foot, take part in outdoor activities, and indulge in local food and beverages. With proper planning and preparation, a visit to Periche can be a once-in-a-lifetime experience.
