Shey-Phoksundo National Park is nestled in the Himalayas of northwest Nepal, offering breathtaking natural beauty and an opportunity to witness the unique culture of the Dolpo ethnic group. With its crystal-clear Phoksundo Lake, snow-capped peaks, and verdant forests, it is home to rare and endangered fauna such as the snow leopard, musk deer, and Himalayan tahr. Travelers to this park can indulge in outdoor adventures like hiking, camping, and trekking. If you’re planning a trip to Shey-Phoksundo National Park, let us guide you through the essential information you’d need to make the most of your travel.

Introduction to Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is one of the largest parks in Nepal, covering an area of 3,555 square kilometers. It is named after the two major attractions of the park, the ancient Shey Monastery and the turquoise Phoksundo Lake. The park is located in the Dolpa region of Nepal, which is known for its pristine landscapes and ancient traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Here are the top attractions to see in Shey-Phoksundo National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shey Monastery: This is a must-visit attraction in the park, which dates back to the 11th century. The monastery is perched on a hilltop and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can explore the ancient murals, sculptures, and artifacts inside.
  • The Ringmo Village: This small village is located in the park and is home to the famous Phoksundo Lake. Ringmo is a typical Tibetan village with a small gompa (monastery) and traditional houses.
  • Tshowa Rangtshen, a Bon-po Stupa: This is a 900-year-old Bon-po stupa located near the Phoksundo Lake. It is a spiritual site for followers of the Bon-po religion, which predates Buddhism in Tibet.

Natural Attractions of Shey-Phoksundo National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phoksundo Lake: One of the most stunning natural attractions of Shey-Phoksundo National Park is Phoksundo Lake. This crystal-clear lake is located at an altitude of 3,600 meters and is surrounded by snow-capped peaks and verdant forests.
  • Himalayan Wildlife: Shey-Phoksundo National Park is home to a variety of rare and endangered flora and fauna. The park is home to the snow leopard, Himalayan musk deer, blue sheep, Himalayan tahr, and other rare species of birds and animals.
  • Waterfalls: The park has many waterfalls, the most famous of which is the Phoksundo Waterfall. This waterfall is 167 meters high and is a sight to behold.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Shey-Phoksundo National Park offers several hiking and biking trails that offer a glimpse into its natural beauty. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Dolpo Trek: This is a 19-day trek that takes you through the remote Dolpo region of Nepal, where you’ll get to witness the unique culture and natural beauty of the area.
  • Phoksundo Lake Trek: This is a 10-day trek that takes you to the turquoise Phoksundo Lake and through the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Kagmara Trek: This is an eight-day trek that takes you through the Kagmara La Pass, with breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Shey-Phoksundo National Park: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Shey-Phoksundo National Park:

  • Camping: The park offers many camping sites that allow you to immerse yourself in nature and enjoy the starry nights.
  • Trekking: As mentioned earlier, there are several trekking trails that you can explore, depending on your fitness level and time constraints.
  • Village Tours: You can visit the nearby villages and interact with the locals to learn about their traditions and way of life.
  • Wildlife Watching: Shey-Phoksundo National Park is an excellent place to observe the rare and endangered fauna of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Shey-Phoksundo National Park:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Kathmandu
  • Day 2: Fly to Nepalgunj and then to Juphal
  • Day 3: Trek to Dunai
  • Day 4: Trek to Chhepka
  • Day 5: Trek to Phoksundo Lake and Ringmo Village
  • Day 6: Explore Phoksundo Lake
  • Day 7: Trek back to Juphal and fly back to Kathmandu

Food and Drink Scene of Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is located in a remote area, and the food scene is primarily limited to traditional Nepali dishes with a Tibetan influence. The local cuisine is simple yet hearty, consisting of staples like dal-bhat-tarkari (lentil soup, rice, and vegetable curry), momos (dumplings), and Tibetan bread. Here are some dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thukpa: A hot and comforting soup made with noodles, vegetables, and/or meat.
  • Khapse: A deep-fried pastry made from flour and sugar.
  • Chhurpi: A hard cheese made from yak milk.

Local Beverages

  • Salt Butter Tea: A traditional drink made with black tea, yak butter, and salt.
  • Chang: A millet-based beer that is a popular drink in the region.

Where to Stay in Shey-Phoksundo National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is a remote area and has limited accommodation options. There are a few guesthouses, lodges, and campsites in the park, with basic amenities. Here are some places to stay:

  • Shey Gompa Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Phoksundo Safari Camp: A luxury campsite that offers comfortable tents with attached toilets and hot showers.
  • Himalayan Yeti Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in Dunai, the gateway to Shey-Phoksundo National Park.

What to Buy in Shey-Phoksundo National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is a rural area and does not have a shopping scene. However, you can buy some souvenirs and local products like:

  • Handmade woolen blankets and shawls
  • Tibetan prayer flags and traditional handicrafts
  • Locally made cheese and dairy products

Local Culture and Customs of Shey-Phoksundo National Park

The local culture of the Dolpo region of Nepal is heavily influenced by Tibetan Buddhism. The locals follow traditional customs and rituals and are known for their warm hospitality. Some cultural customs to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering homes, guesthouses, and temples.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Shey-Phoksundo National Park

The best time to visit Shey-Phoksundo National Park is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, offering unobstructed views of the mountains and valleys. The monsoon season from June to August can make the trails muddy and slippery, making it challenging to trek.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks when trekking.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and terrain.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave behind trash on the trails.
  • Disturb the wildlife or their habitats.
  • Ignore the warnings of your guide or park officials.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shey-Phoksundo National Park

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it is still essential to take some safety measures, like:

  • Hire a licensed trekking guide or porter.
  • Carry a map, compass, and other necessary equipment.
  • Invest in travel insurance before embarking on the trip.

Health in Shey-Phoksundo National Park

As Shey-Phoksundo National Park is located at high altitudes, it is crucial to take precautions to prevent altitude sickness. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Acclimatize gradually to the high altitudes.
  • Drink plenty of water and fluids to stay hydrated.
  • Carry a first aid box with basic medication and supplies.

Getting to Shey-Phoksundo National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is located in the Dolpa region of Nepal and can be accessed by air or road. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Shey-Phoksundo National Park

  • Juphal Airport: This is the closest airport to Shey-Phoksundo National Park, located in the Dolpa region of Nepal.

Bus Stations Near Shey-Phoksundo National Park

  • Nepalgunj Bus Stand: This is the closest bus station to Shey-Phoksundo National Park, located in Nepalgunj city.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: You can also hire a private taxi from Kathmandu or Nepalgunj to reach Shey-Phoksundo National Park.

How to Get Around in Shey-Phoksundo National Park

The only way to get around Shey-Phoksundo National Park is by foot or on horseback. If you’re trekking, you can hire a guide or porter to carry your luggage.

Where to Go Next from Shey-Phoksundo National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Shey-Phoksundo National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers an opportunity to witness the unique culture of the Dolpo ethnic group and observe rare and endangered flora and fauna. It is a remote area, but with proper preparation and planning, you can have a memorable experience that you’ll cherish for a lifetime.
