Introduction to Poonch

Poonch is a small town located in the Poonch district of the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir. It is situated in the Himalayan North region, bordering Pakistan-administered Kashmir. Poonch is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. The town offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped mountains and lush green valleys. It is also famous for its rich history, which dates back to the ancient times of Mahabharata.

List of Places to Visit in Poonch

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Poonch Fort: The fort is an ancient structure that has been preserved over time, and it is a must-visit for history lovers. The fort offers a panoramic view of the entire valley and is an ideal spot for photography.

  • Noor Mahal: The palace was built during the reign of Maharaja Pratap Singh (1885-1925). Noor Mahal has beautiful architecture and is a symbol of the rich cultural heritage of Poonch.

  • Kali Mata Mandir: The temple is located on a hilltop and offers a beautiful view of the valley. It is dedicated to Goddess Kali and is visited by devotees from all over the region.

Natural Attractions of Poonch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shahdra Sharief: Shahdra Sharief is a beautiful and tranquil town, located about 35 km from Poonch. It is famous for its natural beauty and is an ideal spot for a weekend getaway.

  • Bufliaz: Bufliaz is a small town located on the border of Pakistan-administered Kashmir. The town is renowned for its natural beauty and is an ideal spot for outdoor activities such as hiking and trekking.

  • Mandi: Mandi is a beautiful village situated about 6 km from Poonch. It is known for its scenic views and offers a glimpse into the rural life of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poonch

  • Pir Panjal Range: The Pir Panjal range is located on the outskirts of Poonch and is an ideal spot for hiking and trekking. The range offers panoramic views of the entire valley.

  • Surankote: Surankote is a beautiful town located about 60 km from Poonch. It is an ideal spot for a biking trip and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.

  • Arnas: Arnas is a small village located about 20 km from Poonch. It is known for its scenic beauty and is an ideal spot for a hiking trip.

Best Things to Do in Poonch: Activities and Tours

  • Poonch River Rafting: Poonch River rafting is a popular activity among tourists. The rafting starts from the village of Harni and goes up to Potha.

  • Trekking: Poonch offers various trekking options, such as Pir Panjal range, Surankote, and Arnas.

  • Sightseeing: Visitors can explore the historical landmarks and natural attractions of the town, including Poonch Fort, Noor Mahal, Kali Mata Mandir, Shahdra Sharief, Bufliaz, and Mandi.

Food and Drink Scene of Poonch

Must-Try Dishes

  • Harisa: Harisa is a traditional Kashmiri dish made from pounded mutton and rice. It is a popular dish among locals and visitors alike.

  • Rogan Josh: Rogan Josh is an aromatic lamb dish cooked with spices and is a must-try for anyone visiting Poonch.

  • Falooda: Falooda is a popular drink made with milk, rice noodles, and flavoured syrup. It is a refreshing drink and perfect for summers.

Local Beverages

  • Kashmiri Kahwa: Kashmiri Kahwa is a green tea made with a mix of spices such as saffron, cinnamon, and cardamom. It is a popular drink in Poonch, and visitors should try it.

  • Noon Chai: Noon Chai is a pink-coloured tea made with milk, sugar, and tea leaves. It is a traditional beverage of Poonch and is a must-try drink.

What to Buy in Poonch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pashmina Shawls: Pashmina shawls are a must-buy souvenir in Poonch, and visitors can find them in local markets and shops.

  • Kashmiri Carpets: Kashmiri carpets are famous for their intricate designs and are a must-buy if you are in Poonch.

Local Culture and Customs of Poonch

Poonch has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Locals dress modestly, and visitors should dress appropriately.

Best Time to Visit Poonch

The best time to visit Poonch is from April to October when the climate is mild and pleasant. The weather can become harsh during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poonch

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Criticize the local culture and traditions
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Carry firearms or drugs

Safety and Health

Safety in Poonch

Poonch is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take precautions while travelling. It is advised to avoid travelling at night and to follow local customs and traditions.

Health in Poonch

Visitors should be careful of the food and water they consume. It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Poonch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Poonch is well-connected to major cities in Jammu and Kashmir. Visitors can reach Poonch by air, train or road.

Airports Near Poonch

  • Jammu Airport: Jammu airport is the nearest airport to Poonch, and it is located about 250 km from Poonch.

Bus Stations Near Poonch

  • Jammu Bus Stand: Jammu bus stand is a major bus terminal, and visitors can take a bus to Poonch from here.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a cab or taxi from Jammu or Srinagar to reach Poonch.

How to Get Around in Poonch

Visitors can use public transport such as taxis, buses or rickshaws to get around in Poonch. Hiring a private taxi is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Poonch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Muzaffarabad: Muzaffarabad is the capital city of Pakistan-administered Kashmir and is known for its stunning natural beauty.

  • Patnitop: Patnitop is a beautiful hill station situated about 80 km from Poonch. It is known for its snow-capped mountains and picturesque landscapes.

Conclusion

Poonch is a hidden gem in Jammu and Kashmir, offering natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions while exploring the town and trying local cuisine and beverages. A trip to Poonch is sure to be one of the most memorable experiences for anyone travelling to Jammu and Kashmir.
