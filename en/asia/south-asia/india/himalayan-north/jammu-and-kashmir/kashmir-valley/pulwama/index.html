Introduction to Pulwama

Nestled in the Himalayan North, Pulwama is a beautiful district in the Kashmir Valley of Jammu and Kashmir. Known for its stunning natural beauty, the region is surrounded by soaring mountains, lush forests, and glistening lakes. The town is also known for its rich cultural heritage, making it a popular destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Pulwama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aharbal Waterfall: This picturesque waterfall is located in the south-eastern part of Pulwama district. The waterfall offers breathtaking views of the surrounding greenery and is a popular picnic spot for locals.

  • Avantishwar Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, Avantishwar Temple is one of the oldest temples in the Pulwama region. The temple is also known for its impressive architecture and beautiful surroundings.

  • Shrine of Baba Payam-Ud-Din: This holy shrine is located in the village of Tral and is dedicated to the famous Sufi Saint Baba Payam-Ud-Din. The shrine is known for its beautiful architecture and peaceful surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Pulwama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tarsar Lake: This beautiful lake is located in the high-altitude region of the Himalayas and is famous for its crystal clear waters and stunning mountain views.

  • Aripal Nag: Aripal Nag is another beautiful lake located in the Pulwama region. The lake is surrounded by lush green forests and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Kungwattan: Kungwattan is a beautiful meadow located in the Pulwama district. The meadow is surrounded by towering mountains and is a popular destination for trekking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pulwama

  • Aharbal Waterfall Trek: This trek takes you through the scenic forests surrounding the Aharbal Waterfall. The trek is moderately difficult and takes around 4-5 hours to complete.

  • Tarsar Marsar Trek: This 7-day trek takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes of the Himalayas. The trek passes through high-altitude lakes, meadows, and dense forests.

Best Things to Do in Pulwama: Activities and Tours

  • Day Trip to Pahalgam: Pahalgam is a beautiful town located around 30 km from Pulwama. The town is known for its gorgeous scenery and offers plenty of outdoor activities, including trekking, fishing, and horse riding.

  • Saffron Farm Tour: Pulwama is known for its high-quality saffron, and a tour of the saffron farms is a great way to learn more about the region’s culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Pulwama

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rogan Josh: A popular lamb dish cooked in a rich and spicy gravy.

  • Khatti Meat: A sour and spicy mutton curry made with tamarind and tomatoes.

  • Gushtaba: A meatball dish made with minced lamb and yogurt.

Local Beverages

  • Kahwa: A fragrant tea made with saffron and almond.

  • Noon Chai: A traditional pink tea made with salt and baking soda.

Where to Stay in Pulwama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Kesar Resort: A luxurious resort located near Tarsar Lake.

  • Home Away From Home Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Pulwama.

  • Kashmir Houseboat: Experience the unique experience of living on a houseboat on the Dal Lake.

What to Buy in Pulwama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pashmina Shawls: Made from the finest cashmere wool, Pashmina shawls are a staple of Kashmiri handicrafts.

  • Saffron: Pulwama is known for its high-quality saffron, which is in high demand around the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Pulwama

Pulwama is a predominantly Muslim region, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. It is also advisable to be mindful of other religious and cultural sensitivities.

Best Time to Visit Pulwama

The best time to visit Pulwama is between April and October when the weather is pleasant, and the flowers are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pulwama

Do:

  • Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try out the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid unsafe areas.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Do not travel alone at night.

  • Do not indulge in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pulwama

Pulwama is a relatively safe region, but it is advisable to keep a low profile and avoid any political demonstrations and gatherings.

Health in Pulwama

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions against water-borne diseases and altitude sickness when traveling to high-altitude areas.

Getting to Pulwama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pulwama

The closest airport to Pulwama is the Srinagar International Airport, which is located around 30 km away.

Bus Stations Near Pulwama

Pulwama is well-connected to other parts of the Kashmir Valley by bus. Visitors can take a local bus or hire a private cab to reach Pulwama.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Pulwama by train or car.

How to Get Around in Pulwama

The best way to get around Pulwama is by hiring a private cab. Visitors can also use local buses or auto-rickshaws to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Pulwama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Srinagar: The capital city of Jammu and Kashmir is located around 40 km from Pulwama and is known for its beautiful Dal Lake and Mughal gardens.

  • Gulmarg: A popular ski resort located around 70 km from Pulwama, Gulmarg is known for its stunning scenery and adventure sports.

Conclusion

Pulwama is a beautiful destination that offers plenty of natural and cultural attractions for visitors. From picturesque lakes and waterfalls to ancient temples and shrines, the town has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Pulwama and make the most out of their visit.
