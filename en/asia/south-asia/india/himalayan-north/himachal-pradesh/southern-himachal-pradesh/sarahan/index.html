Complete Travel Guide to Sarahan: Places to Visit, Hiking Trails, and Local Culture

Sarahan, a small town in Southern Himachal Pradesh, is a hidden gem situated in the Himalayan North region of India. Surrounded by lush green forests, glistening rivers, and snow-capped mountain ranges, this picturesque town is the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, hiking trails, local culture, food and drink scene, and much more in Sarahan. Let’s start!

Introduction to Sarahan

Situated at an altitude of 2165 meters, Sarahan is a quaint hamlet nestled amidst the lofty Himalayas. It is home to the famous Bhimakali Temple, which is one of the 51 Shaktipeeths in India. Sarahan is also known for its stunning natural beauty, apple orchards, and traditional architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Sarahan

Here is a list of must-visit places in Sarahan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhimakali Temple: A beautiful temple dedicated to the Hindu goddess Bhimakali, which is one of the most revered temples in Himachal Pradesh. The temple’s architecture is a beautiful blend of Hindu and Buddhist styles.

  • Raja Bushahr Palace: A beautiful palace built by the rulers of the Bushahr dynasty in the classical Pahari style. The palace houses a museum that showcases the rich culture and history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Sarahan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shrikhand Mahadev Trek: Sarahan is the starting point of this famous trek, which takes you to the holy Shrikhand Mahadev Temple. This challenging trek takes about 5-6 days to complete and offers stunning views of the Himalayan ranges that will leave you spellbound.

  • Bhabha Pass Trek: Another popular trek that starts from Sarahan, takes you through lush green meadows, glistening rivers, and snow-capped mountains, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarahan

Sarahan is a paradise for adventure enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Bashal Peak Trek: A moderate trek that offers breathtaking views of the lush green valleys and snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas.

  • Tani Jubbar Lake Trek: A beautiful trek that takes you through apple orchards and forests to reach the scenic Tani Jubbar Lake.

Best Things to Do in Sarahan: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting in the Sutlej River: The Sutlej River offers some of the most exciting white water rafting experiences in the region.

  • Visit the Apple Orchards: Sarahan is known for its delicious apples, and a visit to the apple orchards is a must-do activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sarahan

Here is a perfect week-long itinerary for Sarahan:

Day 1: Arrival in Shimla, followed by a scenic drive to Sarahan.

Day 2-3: Visit the Bhimakali Temple, Raja Bushahr Palace, and explore the local markets.

Day 4-5: Trek to the Shrikhand Mahadev Temple or Bhabha Pass.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby villages, such as Taranda or Sangla.

Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely breakfast and depart for Shimla.

Food and Drink Scene of Sarahan

Sarahan’s local cuisine is a mix of Pahari, Tibetan and Himachali flavors, which makes it unique. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Siddu: A steamed bread made of wheat flour and stuffed with herbs and spices, served with local chutneys and butter.

  • Chha Gosht: A delicious mutton curry cooked with yogurt and spices, served with rice or roti.

Local Beverages

  • Chhaang: A local alcoholic drink made from fermented rice and barley.

  • Kanji: A refreshing drink made from fermented carrots, water, and salt.

Where to Stay in Sarahan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sarahan has a variety of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some recommended options:

  • Bhimakali Temple Guest House: A budget-friendly option located close to the temple.

  • Sarahan Heritage Home: A beautiful guest house that offers stunning views of the Himalayas.

What to Buy in Sarahan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to take back home:

  • Apple products: Sarahan is famous for its delicious apples, which are available in various forms like jams, marmalades, and pickles.

  • Handicrafts: You can find beautiful handicrafts made by the locals, such as woolen shawls, caps, and blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarahan

The people of Sarahan follow a unique Pahari culture and predominantly speak Hindi and Pahari. The local festivals are celebrated with much enthusiasm and include the Renuka Fair and the Chadichad festival.

Best Time to Visit Sarahan

The best time to visit Sarahan is between March and June and then again from September to November. The weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views of the Himalayas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sarahan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry warm clothes, especially during winters.
  • Hire a local guide for trekking and other adventure activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter the surroundings.
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious places.
  • Take photographs without seeking permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarahan

Sarahan is a safe destination for travelers. However, always be cautious while trekking or hiking and follow the instructions given by your guide.

Health in Sarahan

Carry sufficient medication and first-aid kit while traveling to Sarahan. Tap water is not safe for drinking, so always carry bottled water.

Getting to Sarahan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sarahan

The nearest airport to Sarahan is the Shimla Airport, located at a distance of 175 kilometers.

Bus Stations Near Sarahan

Sarahan is well-connected by road, and buses run regularly from Shimla, Chandigarh, and Delhi.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or rent a car from Shimla to reach Sarahan.

How to Get Around in Sarahan

The best way to explore Sarahan is on foot. However, you can hire a taxi or rent a car to travel to nearby places.

Where to Go Next from Sarahan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations that you can visit from Sarahan are:

  • Chitkul: A beautiful village located on the Indo-Tibetan border, known for its stunning landscapes and Baspa River.

  • Sangla: A picturesque town located in the Kinnaur district, known for its apple orchards and magnificent views of the Himalayas.

Conclusion

Sarahan is a must-visit destination for those who love nature, adventure, and culture. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan an exciting itinerary and explore the best places, activities, and local traditions of Sarahan. Start packing your bags and get ready to experience the beauty of the Himalayas in this charming town!
