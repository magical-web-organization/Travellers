Sangla is a hidden gem in Eastern Himachal Pradesh, nestled in the Himalayan North region of India. This picturesque valley, also known as Baspa Valley, is dotted with lush green apple orchards, alpine meadows, and snow-capped peaks. Sangla is the perfect destination for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and culture enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we bring you the best that Sangla has to offer, including must-visit places, hiking trails, local cuisine, accommodations, and much more.

Introduction to Sangla

Sangla is located in the Kinnaur district of Himachal Pradesh and is easily accessible by road from Shimla, Kullu, and Chandigarh. It is situated at an altitude of 2,696 meters above sea level and boasts of a pleasant climate throughout the year. The main attraction of Sangla is the Baspa River, which flows through the valley, and the Kamru Fort, which is the oldest fort of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Sangla

Sangla has something for everyone, whether you are looking for historical sites, natural beauty, or cultural experiences. Here are the top places to visit in Sangla:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kamru Fort - The Kamru Fort is a 15th-century fort located in the picturesque village of Kamru, and is situated at an altitude of 2600 meters above sea level. It has a wooden balcony overlooking the Sangla Valley and houses a temple dedicated to the village goddess Kamakhya Devi.
  • Bering Nag Temple - One of the oldest temples in the valley, Bering Nag Temple is situated near Sangla and is dedicated to Lord Jagas, a local deity.
  • Sapni Fort - Sapni Fort is a traditional fort situated on the banks of the Baspa River, and was built by the king of Rampur in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Sangla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chitkul Village - Chitkul Village is the last village on the Indo-Tibetan border, and is situated at an altitude of 3450 meters above sea level. It offers an amazing view of the Kinner Kailash peak and is famous for its meadows and apple orchards.
  • Rakcham Village - Rakcham Village is a small Himalayan village situated near Sangla and offers a breathtaking view of the Baspa River and the Himalayan ranges.
  • Baspa River - The Baspa River is a major source of water for the region and offers opportunities for river rafting and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sangla

Sangla is a perfect destination for hikers and bikers, offering a variety of trekking and biking trails. Here are the best hiking and biking routes to explore in Sangla:

  • Sangla to Rakcham - This trek takes you through lush green forests and offers beautiful views of the mountains and rivers. It is an easy trek and is suitable for beginners.
  • Sangla to Chitkul - The trek from Sangla to Chitkul is a moderate trek, which takes you through scenic meadows and offers a beautiful view of the Kinner Kailash peak.
  • Sangla to Kanda - This is a challenging trek that takes you to the Kanda Peak, offering panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges.

Best Things to Do in Sangla: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, here are some of the best things to do in Sangla:

  • Rafting in the Baspa River - The Baspa River offers exciting river rafting opportunities for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Apple Picking - Sangla is famous for its apple orchards, and during the apple season (August to October), visitors can enjoy picking fresh apples and tasting them right from the tree.
  • Local Cultural Shows - Sangla hosts various cultural shows and festivals throughout the year, showcasing the local culture and tradition of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Sangla

Sangla offers a wide range of local cuisine, including traditional Himachali dishes and Tibetan delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dham - A traditional Himachali meal that includes dal, rajma, and kadhi, served with rice, boor ki kari, and sweet rice.
  • Channa Madra - A popular Himachali dish made with chickpeas, yogurt, and cashews.
  • Momos - Tibetan steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Chang - A traditional Tibetan beverage made from fermented barley.
  • Thukpa - A noodle soup that is popular in the Himalayan region.

Where to Stay in Sangla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sangla offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sangla:

  • Banjara Camps and Retreat - This luxury resort offers beautiful views of the Himalayan ranges and has a variety of activities like hiking and river rafting.
  • Kinner Camps - This resort is located in the picturesque village of Sangla and offers comfortable tents and cottages with beautiful views of the valley.
  • Hotel Rupin River View - A budget-friendly option, Hotel Rupin River View offers comfortable rooms with scenic views of the Baspa River.

What to Buy in Sangla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sangla is famous for its handicrafts and woolen products. Here are some of the souvenirs and local products that you can bring home:

  • Kinnauri Shawls - These handwoven shawls are made of Angora rabbit wool, and are warm and soft.
  • Traditional Himachali Jewellery - The traditional Himachali jewellery, made of silver and semi-precious stones, is beautiful and unique.
  • Wooden Handicrafts - The wooden handicrafts of Sangla, including bowls, trays, and boxes, are handmade and are perfect for gifting.

Local Culture and Customs of Sangla

Sangla has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals celebrate various festivals throughout the year. The Kinnauri Natti, a traditional dance form of the region, is a must-see. Visitors should respect the local customs, dress modestly, and avoid littering.

Best Time to Visit Sangla

Sangla is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from March to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the views of the mountains and valleys are breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sangla

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Sangla:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Take precautions while hiking and biking, and follow the instructions of the guide or instructor.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the valley or disturb the natural habitat.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.
  • Damage the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sangla

Sangla is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take precautions while hiking and biking. Always carry a first-aid kit, water, and snacks while on a trek, and follow the instructions of the guide or instructor.

Health in Sangla

Sangla does not have any major health risks, but visitors should carry insect repellent and sunscreen. Visitors should also carry necessary medicines and consult a doctor in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Sangla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sangla is easily accessible by road from Shimla, Kullu, and Chandigarh. The nearest airport and railway station are in Shimla. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus from Shimla or Rampur to reach Sangla.

Airports Near Sangla

  • Shimla Airport

Bus Stations Near Sangla

  • Rampur Bus Station
  • Shimla Bus Station

Other Options

  • Taxi Hire - Visitors can hire a taxi from Shimla or Rampur to reach Sangla.

How to Get Around in Sangla

Visitors can hire a taxi or take a local bus to explore the valley. Hiking and biking are also popular options for exploring the region.

Where to Go Next from Sangla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can plan from Sangla:

  • Kalpa - A picturesque village located just 40 km from Sangla, Kalpa offers panoramic views of the Kinner Kailash range.
  • Nako - A small village located 75 km from Sangla, Nako offers beautiful views of the Himalayan ranges and has an ancient monastery.
  • Tabo - A historical village located 160 km from Sangla, Tabo has one of the oldest monasteries in the Himalayan region.

Conclusion

Sangla is a hidden gem in the Himalayan region of India and offers a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. So pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable Himalayan adventure in Sangla.
