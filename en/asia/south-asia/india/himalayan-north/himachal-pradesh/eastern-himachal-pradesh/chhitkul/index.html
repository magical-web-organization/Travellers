Chhitkul Travel Guide: Explore the Eastern Himachal Pradesh

Located in the easternmost part of India’s Himachal Pradesh, Chhitkul is a picturesque village nestled in the heart of the Himalayan North. This place is also known as the last inhabited village near the Indo-China border. This remote location, coupled with its natural beauty, makes Chhitkul a perfect destination for a peaceful and relaxing getaway. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve listed all the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Chhitkul

Chhitkul is known for its natural beauty and cultural heritage. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s something for everyone in this beautiful village. Here are some of the top places to visit in Chhitkul:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Kagyupa temple: The Kagyupa Temple is a beautifully designed temple that holds immense importance in the local culture. It has a unique structure and is dedicated to Lord Buddha. A visit to this temple is a must when in Chhitkul.
  • Badrinath Temple: This Temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is believed to be the most sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus. The tranquility and peacefulness one feels in the surroundings of the temple are simply unparalleled.
  • Kamru Fort: This 950-year-old fort is located on the top of the village and offers spectacular views of the surroundings. The architecture and design of the fort are quite impressive, making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Chhitkul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sangla Valley: The Sangla Valley is a breathtakingly beautiful valley famous for its lush green forests, apple orchards, and scenic views. The valley is also known as the ‘Baspa Valley’ as it is situated on the banks of the Baspa River.
  • Chitkul Trek: The Chitkul Trek is a beautiful trek that offers views of snow-capped peaks, lush green meadows, and beautiful villages en route. The trek passes through Rakcham, Sangla, and Chitkul villages, allowing trekkers to explore the beauty of the region.
  • Baspa River: Baspa River is famous for its crystal-clear water and offers numerous opportunities for adventure enthusiasts. One can indulge in activities like rafting, angling, and camping alongside the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chhitkul

Chhitkul is a paradise for those who love to explore nature on foot. From easy treks to difficult hikes, Chhitkul offers something for every type of hiker. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Chhitkul:

  • Chhitkul to Lamkhaga Pass: This is a challenging trek that leads to the Lamkhaga Pass, offering some of the most spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Sangla to Rakcham Trek: This trek starts from Sangla and leads to Rakcham valley, offering stunning views of the Kinnaur Kailash mountain range.
  • Chhitkul to Gangotri Pass Trek: This is a long and strenuous trek that takes one to the Gangotri pass, where one can witness the pristine beauty of the Gangotri glacier.

Best Things to Do in Chhitkul: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and hiking, Chhitkul offers plenty of other activities to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Chhitkul:

  • Camping: Camping alongside the Baspa River or in the beautiful meadows of Chhitkul is a must-do activity when in Chhitkul.
  • Stargazing: Chhitkul is known for its clear skies and offers some of the most stunning views of the night sky.
  • Village Walk: Explore the local culture and lifestyle by taking a walk through the beautiful villages in and around Chhitkul.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chhitkul

If you’re planning to spend a week in Chhitkul, here’s an itinerary that covers all the must-visit places in and around the village:

Food and Drink Scene of Chhitkul

No trip to Chhitkul is complete without trying the local cuisine. The food in Chhitkul is heavily influenced by Tibetan and Himachali cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momos: Momos are steamed dumplings made with various fillings like vegetables, chicken, and mutton.
  • Thupka: Thupka is a soup made with noodles, vegetables, and meat.
  • Siddu: Siddu is a steamed bread made with wheat flour and stuffed with potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: Butter tea is a traditional tea made with butter, salt, and Tibetan tea leaves.
  • Chang: Chang is a local beer made with barley, and it’s quite potent.

Where to Stay in Chhitkul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Though Chhitkul is a remote village, it offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Chhitkul:

  • Hotel Rupin River View: This hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms with views of the Baspa river.
  • The Wanderer’s Nest: The Wanderer’s Nest is a cozy guesthouse situated in the heart of the village, offering beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Camping Sites: Chhitkul offers numerous camping sites where one can spend a night amidst nature.

What to Buy in Chhitkul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chhitkul is famous for its local handicrafts and woolen products. Some of the best things to buy when in Chhitkul are:

  • Shawls and Stoles: The shawls and stoles made by the local artisans are a must-buy when in Chhitkul.
  • Handmade Jewelry: The jewelry made with silver and semi-precious stones is quite popular among tourists.
  • Handicrafts: The local handicrafts made with wood and stone make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Chhitkul

Chhitkul is a predominantly Buddhist village, and the local culture and customs are heavily influenced by Tibetan culture. The locals are warm and welcoming, and most of them speak Hindi, English, and Tibetan. Visitors should respect their customs and traditions by dressing modestly and avoiding the consumption of alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit Chhitkul

The best time to visit Chhitkul is from April to June and from September to November. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and one can indulge in various outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chhitkul

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chhitkul

Chhitkul is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should avoid traveling alone in the dark and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places.

Health in Chhitkul

Chhitkul is a remote village and has limited medical facilities. Visitors are advised to carry basic medication and a first-aid kit.

Getting to Chhitkul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chhitkul is located about 610 kilometers from Delhi and can be reached by road. Here are the different transportation options to reach Chhitkul:

Airports Near Chhitkul

The nearest airport to Chhitkul is the Bhuntar Airport in Kullu, which is around 230 kilometers away from the village.

Bus Stations Near Chhitkul

Buses to Chhitkul are available from major cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and Shimla.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or take a shared taxi from Shimla or Rampur to reach Chhitkul.

How to Get Around in Chhitkul

Chhitkul is a small village, and most of the attractions can be easily explored on foot. However, visitors can also hire a taxi or take a shared taxi to explore the nearby villages and valleys.

Where to Go Next from Chhitkul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after Chhitkul:

  • Kalpa: Kalpa is a beautiful town located about 55 kilometers from Chhitkul and is famous for its apple orchards, stunning views of the Kinner Kailash mountain range, and ancient Hindu temples.
  • Shimla: Shimla is a popular hill station located about 225 kilometers from Chhitkul, offering scenic views, colonial architecture, and various outdoor activities.
  • Nako Lake: Nako Lake is a serene and peaceful lake located about 157 kilometers from Chhitkul, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Conclusion

Chhitkul is a hidden gem in the Eastern Himachal Pradesh that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. From scenic views and outdoor activities to local cuisine and handicrafts, Chhitkul has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip to Chhitkul and explore the unexplored.
