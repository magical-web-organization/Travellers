Introduction to Nako

Nako is a small, remote village situated in the Kinnaur district of Himachal Pradesh, India. This hidden gem is known for its stunning natural beauty, ancient architectural structures, and unique culture that remains untouched by the modern world. Nako is surrounded by high Himalayan peaks, including Mount Kinner Kailash, and is a paradise for trekkers and nature lovers.

The village is home to many age-old temples and monasteries, which are beautifully crafted and serve as a reflection of the local architecture. Founded in the eleventh century, Nako is culturally rich and holds important historical significance. This guide covers all the major attractions, activities, and logistics you need to know before visiting Nako.

List of Places to Visit in Nako

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nako Monastery

Nako Monastery is a unique and magnificent temple complex located in the center of Nako village. The intricate woodwork and murals in the temple are a prime example of traditional architecture and artistry of the region. The monastery was built around the twelfth century and has been carefully preserved by the monks over the centuries.

Nako Lake

Nako Lake is a serene and picturesque lake located at the edge of Nako village. It is a vital source of freshwater for the locals and is considered holy by the villagers. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery and is an excellent spot for a peaceful picnic with family and friends.

Tabo Monastery

Tabo Monastery is a UNESCO World Heritage site located about 60 km from Nako. This ancient monastery was built around 996 AD and is known for its mural paintings depicting the life of Buddha.

Natural Attractions of Nako: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nako Trek

Nako Trek is one of the most popular treks in the region. It is a moderate trek along the Spiti River, which takes you through some of the most mesmerizing landscapes of the Himalayas. The trail starts from Nako and ends in Kaza, covering a total distance of around 62 km.

Gompa Loop Trek

The Gompa Loop Trek is an easy and scenic trek that takes you through some of the most stunning monasteries of the region. The trek starts from Nako and takes you through the Tabo and Dhankar monasteries before ending back in Nako.

Chango Village Trek

The Chango Village Trek is a challenging and adventurous trek that takes you through the remote villages of Chango and Mudh. The trek starts from Nako and takes you through some of the most challenging Himalayan terrains.

Best Things to Do in Nako: Activities and Tours

Yak Safari

Experience the traditional mode of transportation in the Himalayas by taking a Yak safari in Nako. This unique experience takes you through some breathtaking views of the Himalayas, and the majestic animal provides a relaxing ride through the village.

Camping

Camping in Nako is an excellent way to get up close and personal with nature. You can choose to camp either near the lake or in the woods. The night skies in Nako are star-studded and provide a unique camping experience.

Village Walk

Take a stroll through the narrow alleys and winding streets of Nako village. You can experience the local lifestyle and culture of the village by interacting with the locals and experiencing their warm hospitality.

Local Culture and Customs of Nako

The people of Nako village follow traditional customs and beliefs. Visitors are expected to respect local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Nako

The best time to visit Nako is from June to September, as the weather is pleasant, and the village looks lush with greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nako

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately while visiting temples and monasteries
  • Carry woolens and warm clothing as it can get chilly in the evenings

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the local environment
  • Use plastic bottles or bags

Safety and Health

Safety in Nako

Nako is a safe place to visit, but visitors are advised to take general precautions like keeping their valuables safe, avoiding dark alleys at night, and staying alert while trekking.

Health in Nako

Visitors are advised to carry basic medication and stay hydrated while visiting Nako. In case of any medical emergencies, visitors can seek medical assistance from the local dispensaries.

How to Get Around in Nako

The best way to get around in Nako is on foot. The village is small and can be easily explored on foot. You can also hire a taxi or bike to explore nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Nako: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Spiti Valley

Spiti Valley is a popular tourist destination located about 100 km from Nako. This beautiful valley is known for its stunning landscapes, Buddhist monasteries, and adventure activities like trekking and camping.

Kinnaur Valley

Located about 200 km from Nako, Kinnaur Valley is an excellent place for adventure enthusiasts. The valley is surrounded by beautiful Himalayan peaks and is known for trekking, camping, and road trips.

Conclusion

Nako is a beautiful village that should not be missed by anyone visiting Himachal Pradesh. This guide covers everything you need to know about Nako, from cultural attractions to outdoor activities, and prepares you for an unforgettable trip to this magical Himalayan destination.
