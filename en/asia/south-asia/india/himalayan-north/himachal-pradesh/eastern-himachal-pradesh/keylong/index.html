Introduction to Keylong

Nestled in the Eastern Himalayas of Himachal Pradesh, Keylong is a charming little town surrounded by snow-capped mountains and breathtaking landscapes. It is an offbeat destination, often overshadowed by its more popular counterparts like Manali and Shimla, making it a tranquil escape for travelers in search of peace and quiet. The picturesque town offers stunning vistas and a chance to experience the laid-back local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Keylong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khardong Monastery: Built around a thousand years ago, the Khardong Monastery is a significant historical monument with great cultural importance. Visitors can explore the intricate designs, murals, and traditional artifacts inside the monastery.
  • Guru Ghantal Monastery: Guru Ghantal Monastery is another must-visit historically valuable religious site. It was constructed in the 17th century and houses an idol of Guru Padmasambhava, who is considered to be the founder of Tantric Buddhism.
  • Tayul Monastery: Located in the village of Satingri, this monastery dates back to the 17th century and has a rare collection of thangkas, musical instruments, and manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Keylong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bhaga River: The Bhaga river flows through the town and is a great spot to relax and unwind. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along its banks or indulge in some adventure sports, including river rafting or kayaking.
  • Baralacha Pass: Baralacha Pass is a high altitude mountain pass that connects Keylong to Leh. The pass is open in the summer season and offers stunning views of the snow-clad mountain peaks.
  • Suraj Tal Lake: Suraj Tal Lake is located near Baralacha Pass and is the third-highest lake in India. The landscape surrounding the lake is mesmerizing, offering a true glimpse of nature’s beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keylong

  • Manali-Leh Highway: The Manali-Leh Highway is a motorable route and is considered one of the most scenic routes in the world. It provides breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys, and the route is dotted with waterfalls, lakes, and passes.
  • Chandratal-Baralacha Pass Trek: This is a moderately difficult trek that takes you through the beautiful Chandratal Lake and the Baralacha Pass. The trek offers incredible views of glaciers, snow-capped peaks, and an opportunity to experience the local culture.

Best Things to Do in Keylong: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Monasteries: A visit to the various monasteries in Keylong offers an opportunity to learn about the local culture, traditions, and history.
  • Trek to the High Altitude Lakes: Keylong is surrounded by high altitude lakes, including Suraj Tal and Chandratal, which offer incredible views and a chance to connect with nature.
  • River Rafting and Kayaking: Bhaga River provides visitors with a chance to indulge in adventure sports. River rafting and kayaking are popular activities.
  • Explore the Local Markets: The local markets in Keylong offer a glimpse of the local culture and provide an opportunity to try different cuisines and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Keylong

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thukpa: Thukpa is a warming noodle soup with a simple, yet delicious flavor that is often made with yak meat or vegetables, it is perfect for keeping warm on chilly nights.
  • Momos: Momos are a common staple of the region, which are dumplings stuffed with minced or steamed vegetables, or meat served with freshly prepared chutney or soy sauce.
  • Chha Gosht: This is a traditional dish, made with marinated lamb that has been simmered with yogurt, spices, and a local herb.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: A popular beverage, it is made by churning yak butter into hot tea. It is a great way to warm up in the cold weather.
  • Chhang: Chhang is made with fermented rice or barley and is served cold. It is popular among locals and is often consumed at local festivals.

What to Buy in Keylong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pashmina Shawls: Pashmina shawls are made from super-soft wool from the Himalayan goats and are one of the most sought-after products from Keylong.
  • Handcrafted Souvenirs: Keylong is known for its handmade souvenirs such as woolen socks, hats, and mittens, which make for unique gifts and mementos.
  • Local Spices: Keylong is abundant in fresh herbs and spices, which are widely used in local cuisines. Visitors can purchase them to enjoy the authentic flavors of the local cuisine at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Keylong

The local culture in Keylong is heavily influenced by Buddhism and Hinduism. Visitors can witness the cultural fusion during local festivals, which are a blend of both religions. People are friendly and welcoming, and it is customary to greet them with a traditional ‘Juley.’ Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Keylong

The best time to travel to Keylong is from June to September when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. During this period, visitors can explore the scenic beauty of the place, indulge in adventure sports, and participate in local festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keylong

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Keep warm clothing with you as it can get cold.
  • Take your camera to capture the scenic landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or engage in inappropriate behavior.
  • Litter and dispose of waste improperly.
  • Ignore mountain safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keylong

Keylong is generally a safe place, but visitors should exercise caution while trekking, river rafting, and kayaking. Take the necessary safety precautions while indulging in adventure sports and follow the guidelines issued by instructors.

Health in Keylong

Visitors should carry a sufficient supply of medication, along with warm clothing for the cold weather. It is recommended to drink only bottled water and avoid raw food to avoid food poisoning.

Getting to Keylong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keylong

  • Bhuntar Airport: Bhuntar Airport is the nearest airport, located at a distance of 166 km from Keylong.

Bus Stations Near Keylong

  • Manali Bus Station: The Manali Bus station is located at a distance of 115 km from Keylong.

Other Options

  • Taxi or Private Car: Visitors can hire a taxi or private car to reach Keylong comfortably and at their convenience.

How to Get Around in Keylong

The town of Keylong is small, and it is convenient to walk around. However, visitors can also rent a taxi or hire a bike to explore the neighboring areas.

Where to Go Next from Keylong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Leh is an offbeat destination with stunning landscapes and untouched natural beauty, located at a distance of 350 km from Keylong.
  • Manali is a popular hill station situated at a distance of 115 km from Keylong.
  • Spiti Valley is a remote place with a unique landscape and rich cultural heritage situated at a distance of 194 km from Keylong.

Conclusion

Keylong is a lesser-known yet beautiful destination with rich cultural heritage, local charm, and breathtaking landscapes. It is a perfect place for travelers looking for peace, adventure, or both. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Keylong with ease, knowing where to stay, what to eat, and what places to explore.
