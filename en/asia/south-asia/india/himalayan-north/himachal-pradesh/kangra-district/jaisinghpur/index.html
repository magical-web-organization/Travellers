Introduction to Jaisinghpur

Jaisinghpur is a small town located in Kangra district of Himachal Pradesh, India. It is situated amidst lush green valleys and snow-capped mountains of the Himalayan range. Jaisinghpur is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure activities.

List of Places to Visit in Jaisinghpur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bajreshwari Devi Temple: It is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga. The architecture of this temple is a perfect blend of Indian and Tibetan styles. The temple has a beautiful pond and a beautiful garden surrounding it.

  • Kangra Fort: The Kangra Fort is one of the oldest forts in India, dating back to the 4th century. The fort has a beautiful blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture. It is situated at the confluence of the Manjhi and Banganga rivers and offers a mesmerizing view of the surrounding valleys.

  • Kangra Art Museum: The museum is located near the Kangra Fort and is dedicated to preserving the cultural heritage of the Kangra district. It houses rare paintings, sculptures, manuscripts, and ancient coins.

Natural Attractions of Jaisinghpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indrahar Pass: Indrahar Pass is a beautiful trekking route that starts from Jaisinghpur and leads to the Dhauladhar Range of the Himalayas. It is a challenging trek and may take 4-5 days to complete, but the views from the top are breathtaking.

  • Kareri Lake: Kareri Lake is a beautiful high altitude glacial lake situated at an altitude of 2,934 meters above sea level. It is surrounded by lush green forests and snow-capped mountains. It is a moderate trek and can be completed in 1-2 days.

  • Palampur: Palampur is a beautiful town located 35 km from Jaisinghpur. It is famous for its tea gardens, scenic views, and adventure activities such as paragliding, trekking, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jaisinghpur

  • Kareri Lake Trek: The Kareri Lake trek is a beautiful trek that starts from Kareri Village and leads to the Kareri Lake. It is a 14 km long trek that passes through lush green meadows and dense forests.

  • Indrahar Pass Trek: The Indrahar Pass trek starts from Jaisinghpur and leads to the Dhauladhar range. It is a challenging trek that passes through snow-capped mountains and beautiful valleys.

  • Bir Billing: Bir Billing is a famous paragliding spot located near Jaisinghpur. It is also a popular destination for trekking and camping.

Best Things to Do in Jaisinghpur: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding in Bir Billing: Bir Billing is a famous paragliding spot located near Jaisinghpur. It offers an amazing experience of flying over the beautiful valleys and mountains of the Himalayas.

  • Trekking to Indrahar Pass: The Indrahar Pass trek is a challenging trek but offers a mesmerizing view of the Dhauladhar range.

  • Exploring Kangra Fort and Bajreshwari Devi Temple: Explore the rich cultural heritage of Jaisinghpur by visiting Kangra Fort and Bajreshwari Devi Temple.

Food and Drink Scene of Jaisinghpur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sidu: Sidu is a popular dish of Himachal Pradesh made from wheat flour. It is served with ghee and dal.

  • Tudkiya Bhaat: Tudkiya Bhaat is a spicy rice dish made with potatoes and vegetables. It is traditionally served with a dollop of butter on top.

  • Mitha Bhaat: Mitha Bhaat is a sweet rice dish made with sugar, ghee, and saffron.

Local Beverages

  • Chhaang: Chhaang is a local beverage made from barley, millet, or rice.

  • Kangri Dham: Kangri Dham is a special dish of Himachal Pradesh, made with rice, moong dal, curd, and spices. It is served with sweet rice and green chutney.

  • Thukpa: Thukpa is a noodle soup that is popular in the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Tibet.

Where to Stay in Jaisinghpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Taragarh Palace: Taragarh Palace is a heritage hotel situated near Jaisinghpur. It offers luxury accommodation and amenities such as a spa, swimming pool, and restaurant.

  • Jungle Lodge: Jungle Lodge is a budget accommodation option situated in the middle of a forest. It offers basic amenities such as hot water and meals.

  • Nomadic Life Camp: Nomadic Life Camp offers luxury tented accommodation amidst the beautiful surroundings of Jaisinghpur. It offers amenities such as a bar, restaurant, and bonfire.

What to Buy in Jaisinghpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Jaisinghpur is famous for its tea gardens, and you can buy various flavoured tea blends in the local markets.

  • Handicrafts: Himachal Pradesh is famous for its unique handicrafts such as shawls, carpets, and pottery.

  • Pickles: The pickles of Himachal Pradesh are made from local ingredients such as gooseberries, apricots, and plums.

Local Culture and Customs of Jaisinghpur

Jaisinghpur has a rich cultural heritage, and the people of Jaisinghpur are warm and hospitable. The locals celebrate various festivals such as Diwali, Holi, and Baisakhi with great enthusiasm. The traditional attire of Himachal Pradesh is colourful and vibrant, and you can see the local people wearing it during festivals and other special occasions.

Best Time to Visit Jaisinghpur

The best time to visit Jaisinghpur is from March to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities such as trekking and paragliding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jaisinghpur

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Carry warm clothing while visiting in winters.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.

  • Don’t enter temples without taking your shoes off.

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jaisinghpur

Jaisinghpur is a safe place to travel, and the crime rate is low. However, you should always be cautious while travelling alone at night and store your valuables safely.

Health in Jaisinghpur

Jaisinghpur has few medical facilities. It is advisable to carry your basic medication and first-aid kit while travelling to Jaisinghpur.
