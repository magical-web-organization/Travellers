A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Khajjiar, Himachal Pradesh, India

Nestled in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh lies the serene paradise of Khajjiar. Known for its breathtaking vistas, rolling meadows, and pristine lakes, Khajjiar has been dubbed by many as the “Switzerland of India.” This guide highlights the key attractions, activities, accommodations, and insider tips to help you plan your trip to Khajjiar.

Introduction to Khajjiar

Khajjiar is situated in the Dhauladhar range of the Himalayan Mountains and is often referred to as the Mini Switzerland of India for its lush meadows, lakes, and dense forest. It is located at an altitude of 2,000 m above sea level and is an ideal destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Khajjiar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Khajjiar Lake

An idyllic lake surrounded by dense deodar trees that sits amid the rolling grasslands. It is believed that the lake is named after the ancient Khajji Nag temple, located nearby. Boating facilities are offered here.

Khajjinag Temple

This temple is dedicated to the serpent god, Khajinag, and is considered to be 700 years old. The temple is built-in 12th-century architectural style and is mainly visited by Hindu devotees.

Natural Attractions of Khajjiar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in the Khajjiar plateau, this wildlife sanctuary is home to several Himalayan animals like the Black Bear, Himalayan Marten, Leopard, Himalayan Black Marten, Barking Goral, Serow, and Yellow-throated Marten.

Dainkund Peak

A blissful escape for nature lovers, Dainkund Peak offers a spectacular panoramic view of Khajjiar valley. It is also known as the ‘Singing Hill’ due to the echoes produced by the hilltop when the wind blows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khajjiar

Khajjiar to Dalhousie Trek

This is a two-day trek that starts from Khajjiar and ends at Dalhousie. It is a 14 km trek that can take roughly 5-6 hours to complete. En route, hikers can spot diverse flora and fauna and pass through quaint villages.

Khajjiar to Chamba Trek

This is a moderately difficult trek that starts from Khajjiar and ends at Chamba. This 6 km trek takes around 2-3 hours to finish and is ideal for beginners.

Best Things to Do in Khajjiar: Activities and Tours

Zorbing

The rolling meadows of Khajjiar have made it an ideal destination for adventure sports enthusiasts, and zorbing is a must-try. It involves rolling downhill in a giant transparent ball, which can be incredibly thrilling.

Paragliding

Experience the thrill of soaring through the clouds above Khajjiar’s verdant terrains with paragliding. The region boasts some of the best paragliding experiences in the country.

Food and Drink Scene of Khajjiar

While Khajjiar doesn’t have a wide range of food and drink options, the local dishes and beverages are a must-try.

Must-Try Dishes

Madra

A popular dish that is made with chickpeas, yogurt, and a blend of regional spices. It is often served with rice or roti.

Sidu

A steamed bread made with wheat flour, Sidu is a savory treat that is best accompanied by ghee and dal.

Local Beverages

Kangri Dham

This traditional beverage is made up of rice, moong dal, spices, and ghee. It is cooked in a kangri, which is a special kind of pot, and served hot.

A luxurious stay option that offers serene views and comfortable rooms.

Shining Star Resort

A budget guesthouse that is family-friendly and ideal for those traveling in groups.

What to Buy in Khajjiar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shawls and Pashminas

Khajjiar is famous for its shawls and pashminas, which are made from fine wool and are intricately embroidered.

Wooden Handicrafts

Artisans in Khajjiar create beautiful wooden handicrafts like the intricately carved walking sticks, wooden masks, and bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Khajjiar

As a predominantly Hindu region, Khajjiar celebrates several festivals throughout the year, with Shivratri being the most prominent. The people here are known for their hospitality and warmth.

Best Time to Visit Khajjiar

The best time to visit Khajjiar is between April and June or between September and November. The weather is delightful, with temperatures ranging between 15°C to 25°C, and the skies are usually clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khajjiar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Carry sunscreen, sun hats, and other protective items when hiking or biking.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or harm the environment.

  • Do not carry plastic bags into the national parks.

  • Do not purchase souvenirs made from animal products.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khajjiar

Khajjiar is a relatively safe destination, but it is essential to take precautions when traveling on the hilly terrains. Visitors are advised to stay alert and carry basic medicines and first-aid kits.

Health in Khajjiar

Visitors are advised to carry insect repellent, warm clothes, and sunscreen, as well as to stay hydrated throughout their trip.

Getting to Khajjiar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khajjiar

The nearest airport is Gaggal airport in Dharamsala, around 120 km away, followed by Chandigarh airport, around 310 km away.

Bus Stations Near Khajjiar

Khajjiar is well connected to the nearby towns and cities via state-run buses. The nearest bus station is Chamba Bus Station, around 20 km away.

Other Options

Taxis and shared cabs can be hired from Chamba to reach Khajjiar.

How to Get Around in Khajjiar

Local taxis, rental bikes, and bicycles are the easiest ways to get around Khajjiar. Visitors can also choose to hike or trek from their accommodations to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Khajjiar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Travelers can consider visiting nearby destinations like Dalhousie, Chamba, or Dharamshala.

Conclusion

Khajjiar, with its picturesque landscapes, serene environments, and hospitable locals, offers visitors an unforgettable experience. This guide highlights the best places to visit, things to do, places to stay, and cultural customs to help you plan a perfect trip to Khajjiar. Pack your bags and get ready to experience the magic of the Mini-Switzerland of India!
