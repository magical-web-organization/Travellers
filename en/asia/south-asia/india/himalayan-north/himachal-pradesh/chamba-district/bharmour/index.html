Introduction to Bharmour

Nestled in the Himalayan North of India, Bharmour (also known as Bharmaur) is a small town in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh. Bharmour is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventurous opportunities. This town, surrounded by lush green forests and towering mountains, is the perfect place for those seeking peace, solitude, and adventure. The town is located at an altitude of 2195 meters above sea level, and it is famous for its ancient temples.

List of Places to Visit in Bharmour

There are several places to visit in Bharmour, and they can be classified into two categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Chaurasi Temple Complex - This temple complex is located in the center of Bharmour and is a group of 84 ancient temples. These temples were built during the 7th and 10th centuries, and the complex is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

  2. Bharmani Mata Temple - This beautiful temple is located on top of a hill in Bharmour and is dedicated to Goddess Bharmani.

  3. Manimahesh Lake - This beautiful lake is situated at an altitude of 4,080 meters above sea level, and it is surrounded by lofty mountain peaks.

  4. Ganesh Chaturthi Fair - This fair is held in the Chaurasi temple complex every year during the month of August/September. This fair attracts tourists from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Bharmour: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kalicho Pass Trek - This trek is one of the most popular treks in Himachal Pradesh, and it offers an exhilarating experience to trekkers.

  2. Bharmani Mata Temple Trek - This trek is a moderate trek, and it takes you to the beautiful Bharmani Mata Temple.

  3. Manimahesh Kailash Trek - This trek provides mesmerizing views of the beautiful Dhauladhar range and the Manimahesh Lake.

  4. Banni Mata Trek - This trek is a moderate trek and takes you to the beautiful Banni Mata Temple.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bharmour

Bharmour is famous for its trekking and hiking trails. Some of the famous hiking/biking routes in Bharmour are:

  1. Chanderkhani Pass Trek - This trek is a moderate trek, and it takes you through lush green forests, small villages, and meadows.

  2. Hampta Pass Trek - This trek is a popular trek in Himachal Pradesh, and it offers mesmerizing views of the Pir Panjal range.

  3. Bara Bhangal Trek - This trek is a challenging trek and is perfect for experienced trekkers.

Best Things to Do in Bharmour: Activities and Tours

  1. Trekking and Hiking - Bharmour is a paradise for trekkers and hikers.

  2. Camping - Bharmour has several campsites that offer a unique camping experience.

  3. Photography - Bharmour is blessed with natural beauty, and it is perfect for nature photographers.

  4. Adventure sports - Bharmour offers several adventure sports like rock climbing, rappelling, and river crossing.

Day 3: Trek to Manimahesh Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Bharmour

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sidu - This is a traditional dish in Himachal Pradesh, and it is made of wheat flour.

  2. Dhaam - This is a traditional dish that is served during festivals, and it consists of rice, lentils, and vegetables.

  3. Thukpa - This is a famous noodle soup, and it is perfect for cold winter days.

Local Beverages

  1. Chhaang - This is a famous local alcoholic beverage, and it is made of barley.

  2. Masala Chai - This is a famous tea in India, and it is made with spices like ginger, cinnamon, and cardamom.

Where to Stay in Bharmour: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Chanderlok - This hotel is located in the center of Bharmour, and it offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  2. Bharmour Guest House - This guest house is located near the Chaurasi Temple Complex, and it offers affordable accommodation to tourists.

  3. Hotel Mount View - This hotel is located near the bus stand, and it offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Bharmour: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Woolens - Bharmour is famous for its woolens, and you can buy shawls, sweaters, and blankets from the local markets.

  2. Wooden handicrafts - Bharmour is famous for its wooden handicrafts, and you can buy carved wooden figures, boxes, and furniture from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Bharmour

Bharmour is predominantly a Hindu town, and the locals celebrate several festivals throughout the year. Ganesh Chaturthi, Navratri, and Diwali are some of the famous festivals celebrated in Bharmour.

Best Time to Visit Bharmour

The best time to visit Bharmour is from May to October. The weather during this period is pleasant, and the skies are clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bharmour

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Carry warm clothes, as the weather can be unpredictable.

  3. Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the town or the surrounding areas.

  2. Carry plastic bags or bottles.

  3. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bharmour

Bharmour is a safe place for tourists, and the locals are friendly and hospitable.

Health in Bharmour

Tourists should carry a first aid kit and essential medicines. It is advisable to drink only bottled water.

Getting to Bharmour: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bharmour

The nearest airport to Bharmour is Gaggal Airport, located in Kangra, which is approximately 180 km away from Bharmour.

Bus Stations Near Bharmour

The nearest bus stand to Bharmour is in Chamba town, which is approximately 60 km away from Bharmour.

Other Options

The most convenient way to reach Bharmour is by hiring a taxi from Chamba town.

How to Get Around in Bharmour

The best way to explore Bharmour is by hiring a taxi or a local guide.

Where to Go Next from Bharmour: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Dharamshala - This beautiful hill station is located approximately 190 km away from Bharmour.

  2. McLeod Ganj - This town is located approximately 185 km away from Bharmour and is famous for its Tibetan culture.

Conclusion

Bharmour is a wonderful town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The town is perfect for those seeking peace, solitude, and adventure. Bharmour is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Himachal Pradesh.
