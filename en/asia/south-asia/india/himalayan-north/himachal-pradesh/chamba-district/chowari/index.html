Introduction to Chowari

Located in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, Chowari is a hidden gem in the Himalayan North region of India. Known for its breathtaking views of the hills, valleys, and pristine waterfalls, Chowari is the perfect destination for nature lovers seeking a peaceful escape from bustling cities. Its tranquil and unspoiled surroundings make it an ideal spot for hiking, biking, and other outdoor adventures. Chowari also offers a glimpse into the rich history and culture of Himachal Pradesh, making it an all-round delight for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Chowari

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chowari has its fair share of museums and historical places that offer insights into its rich culture and history. Some of the must-visit attractions in Chowari are:

  • Laxmi Narayan Temple: A popular temple among locals, the Laxmi Narayan Temple is known for its beautiful architecture and stunning views of the valley.

  • Bhuri Singh Museum: Located in Chamba district, Bhuri Singh Museum is one of the oldest museums in Himachal Pradesh. It has an impressive collection of traditional Pahari paintings, sculptures, and artifacts related to the region’s culture and history.

  • Champavati Temple: This ancient temple is dedicated to Goddess Champavati, the daughter of King Sahil Varman of Chamba. It is an architectural marvel and reflects the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Natural Attractions of Chowari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chowari is a paradise for adventure-enthusiasts and nature lovers. With its lush forests, rolling hills, and roaring waterfalls, it offers an array of outdoor adventures. Here are some of the places you shouldn’t miss while in Chowari:

  • Chamera Lake: Located just 45 km away from Chowari, Chamera Lake is a serene and picturesque spot surrounded by the breathtaking Dhauladhar mountains. You can indulge in various activities like boating, fishing and enjoy the scenic views around.

  • Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary: For those interested in wildlife watching, the Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary is an ideal spot. It is home to a variety of animals like the Himalayan Black Bear, Himalayan Brown Bear, leopards, and others. Visitors can also enjoy trekking, camping and bird-watching in the sanctuary area.

  • Ganji Pahari: Ganji Pahari, translating to a bald hill, is a perfect spot for hiking and trekking. The spot provides panoramic views of the valley surrounding the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chowari

Chowari has several scenic routes to offer for those seeking a thrilling hike or bike ride. Some of the most popular routes in the region are:

  • Bhalei Mata Temple Trek: The Bhalei Mata Temple Trek is a moderate trail that takes you through oak and deodar forests before ending at the Bhalei Mata Temple. The route offers scenic views of the surrounding valley.

  • Chowari Circuit: The Chowari Circuit is a challenging cycling route that covers a distance of 105 km. The trail takes you over the hills, valleys, and through the forests, providing an adrenaline-filled adventure for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Chowari: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Chowari has a lot to offer to travelers. Here are some of the best things to do while you’re in town:

  • Rafting on Ravi River: For an unforgettable adventure, try river rafting on the Ravi River. The experience is exhilarating and will give you a chance to witness the scenic beauty of the region.

  • Connecting with Locals: Chowari is known for its friendly locals who are eager to converse with visitors and share their culture. You can take part in local festivals, visit markets, and experience the woolen weaving art at a local workshop.

Food and Drink Scene of Chowari

Must-Try Dishes

Chowari is known for its delectable cuisine that reflects the rich history and culture of the region. Some of the must-try dishes in Chowari are:

  • Madra: This is a vegetarian dish made of chickpeas and a variety of spices, including yogurt, ginger, garlic, and other herbs. It is best served with steamed rice.

  • Sepu Vadi: This delicacy is a combination of spinach, gram flour, spices, and a hint of lemon juice. It is best served with rice or roti.

Local Beverages

To quench your thirst or relax after a long day of sightseeing, try some of Chowari’s local beverages like:

  • Chhaang: A traditional Himalayan beverage, Chhaang is a fermetented rice, miled and water beverage which is then mixed with hops that makes it perfect refresher in summers.

  • Arak: Known for its healing properties, Arak is a herbal wine made from the Clematis plant’s roots.

Where to Stay in Chowari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is no shortage of options when it comes to finding accommodation in Chowari. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or backpacking on a budget, you can find hotels, guesthouses, and hostels to suit your needs.

  • The Sunset View Resort: This luxury resort offers stunning views of the valley and provides a comfortable stay with modern amenities.

  • Dhiman’s Homestay: For those seeking a more budget-friendly and authentic experience, you can choose to stay at Dhiman’s Homestay that offers comfortable and clean rooms, and an authentic experience of staying with a local family.

What to Buy in Chowari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chowari is popular for its unique handicrafts, woolen weavings, and wooden carvings. Some of the souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as a reminder of your trip include:

  • Kinnauri Shawls: These shawls are made of soft wool and are known for their vibrant and intricate designs.

  • Wooden Carvings: Chowari is famous for its wooden carvings which can be found in various sizes and designs. They make unforgettable souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Chowari

Chowari’s culture is a blend of traditional Pahari, Punjabi, and Tibetan cultures, and reflects the peaceful and friendly nature of the local people. The community is known for its hospitality, and you can experience this firsthand while interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Chowari

Chowari is beautiful all year round; yet, the ideal time to visit is between March and June when the weather is pleasant, and the hills are covered with blossoming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chowari

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Carry warm clothes, even in summers as the weather can be unpredictable.

  • Drink plenty of water to keep yourself hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places and protect the natural beauty of the place.

  • Don’t purchase endangered species from the local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chowari

Chowari is a relatively safe place for travelers to visit. However, it is essential to be cautious and take necessary precautions while exploring the region.

  • Avoid traveling alone and stay alert while visiting remote areas.

  • Be careful while exploring natural wonders, and follow the rules and regulations set by authorities.

Health in Chowari

Chowari has a clean and healthy environment. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to prevent health issues while planning a trip.

  • Make sure to carry a first-aid kit, and consult a physician before traveling.

  • Stay hydrated and avoid consuming unhygienic food and water.

Getting to Chowari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chowari

The nearest airport to Chowari is Gaggal Airport in Dharamshala, which is approximately 107 km away from Chowari.

Bus Stations Near Chowari

The nearest bus stations to Chowari are Pathankot and Chamba, which are well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and others.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis, hire bikes, or travel by shared cabs to reach Chowari.

How to Get Around in Chowari

The best way to explore Chowari is by trekking, cycling, or walking. There are several routes available to explore the region, and visitors can also hire taxis or use public transport to navigate within the city.

Where to Go Next from Chowari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The region surrounding Chowari has several beautiful and exciting destinations that you can explore. Some of the popular places to visit include:

  • Dalhousie: A beautiful hill station located 30 km away from Chowari, Dalhousie offers panoramic views of the hills and valleys.

  • Khajjiar: Also known as the “Mini Switzerland of India”, Khajjiar is 50 km away from Chowari and offers a picturesque environment surrounded by lush forests and green meadows.

Conclusion

Chowari is an offbeat yet enchanting destination, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and adventure opportunities. With its peaceful surroundings, striking scenic views, and warm hospitality, Chowari is a must-visit for nature lovers and those looking for authentic travel experiences. Use this comprehensive guide to help plan your trip, and make the most of what Chowari has to offer.
