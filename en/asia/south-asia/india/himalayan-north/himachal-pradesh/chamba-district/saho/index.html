Introduction to Saho

Saho, a quaint hamlet located in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, is situated in the lap of the Himalayas in North India. Natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage make it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

The town gets its name from its location at an elevation of 5,200 feet above sea level. It derives its cultural roots from the landscape and its inhabitants, who are predominantly Hindu and speak Pahari dialects.

List of Places to Visit in Saho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Laxmi Narayan Temple - A popular Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Laxmi, adorned with intricate carvings.
  • Brahmaur - An ancient historical town, nearby Saho, known for its intricate architecture of the Bharmani temple.
  • Chamera Dam - A hydroelectricity powerhouse and dam located on the Ravi River.

Natural Attractions of Saho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saho Wildlife Sanctuary - Home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, this sanctuary offers a serene respite from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Manimahesh Lake - A sacred lake that is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus, and is surrounded by stunning Himalayan views.
  • Khajjiar - Known as ‘Mini Switzerland of India’, this charming hill station offers panoramic views of the lush landscapes, scenic meadows, and a serene lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saho

  • Camel’s Hump Trek - A challenging trek through the Saho Wildlife Sanctuary, offers stunning views of the surroundings.
  • Saho to Manimahesh Lake Trek: A moderate trek that takes you through the lush green meadows, serene forests, and dramatic mountain passes that lead to the sacred Manimahesh Lake.

Best Things to Do in Saho: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding - Soar over the scenic landscapes of Saho and get a bird’s eye view of the lush forests, meandering rivers, and snow-capped Himalayan peaks.
  • Saho Bazaar - Shop for souvenirs, local crafts, and organic produce at this bustling marketplace.
  • River Rafting - Enjoy an exhilarating ride through the picturesque Ravi river.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saho

Day Activity
1 Explore the local area and visit Laxmi Narayan Temple
2 Hike to Camel Hump

Food and Drink Scene of Saho

Must-Try Dishes

  • Siddu - A local delicacy made from wheat flour, filled with stuffing made of crushed almonds, walnuts, raisins, and jaggery, and steamed.
  • Dhaam - A traditional, vegetarian meal served on special occasions, consisting of rice, dal, and curries
  • Chha Gosht - A spicy mutton curry made with yogurt, chili, and turmeric.

Local Beverages

  • Tibetian Butter Tea - A salty and frothy tea made with yak butter, salt, and tea leaves, which is a staple beverage for locals.
  • Bhang Lassi - A yogurt-based drink infused with cannabis leaves, known for its medicinal value and recreational purposes.

Where to Stay in Saho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Brindavan Resorts - A luxurious resort offering stunning views of the Himalayan mountains.
  • The Himalaya Darshan Resort - A budget-friendly resort that offers comfortable accommodation and serene surroundings.
  • Zostel Saho - A backpacker’s hostel, perfect for budget-travelers with its comfortable bunk beds, in-house cafe, and common areas to socialize.

What to Buy in Saho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts - Saho is known for its intricate handicrafts made of wood, bamboo, and copper.
  • Organic produce - The town is known for its organic produce, which includes fruits, vegetables, and herbs.
  • Local Apples, considered to be one of the best quality in India.

Local Culture and Customs of Saho

The predominant culture of Saho revolves around Hinduism, which is evident through the many ancient temples that can be found in the town. The recent exposure of Saho to globalization has brought in many Western ideologies; however, traditional customs are still followed enthusiastically by the locals.

Best Time to Visit Saho

The best time to visit Saho is during the summer months(April to June) and the fall months (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the skies are clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saho

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Consult with locals or tour guides for trekking or hiking routes.
  • Keep the surroundings clean and tidy.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public.
  • Litter or destruct the environment.
  • Photograph without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saho

Saho is a safe destination, and the locals are kind and welcoming. However, as with any travels, it’s always best to take basic precautions like not leaving your belongings unattended in public places and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Saho

Avoid tap water while staying in Saho, and only drink boiled or packaged water. Also, carry necessary medicines like altitude sickness pills.

Getting to Saho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saho

The nearest domestic airport to Saho is Kangra Airport, about 170 kilometers away. Alternatively, international flights can be taken to Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is about 700 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Saho

The nearest bus station is Chamba Bus Station, which is well-connected to the nearby towns and other major cities in North India.

Other Options

Cabs/taxis are easily available from major nearby towns that can help get to Saho quickly and efficiently.

How to Get Around in Saho

The best way to explore Saho is by foot, cycle, or motorbike. Cabs and buses are also available for intercity transportation.

Where to Go Next from Saho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dalhousie - Located just 40 kilometers from Saho, this charming hill station is known for its picturesque landscapes and colonial architecture.
  • Kasol - A small village in the Parvati valley known for its natural beauty, and trekking trails.

Conclusion

Saho’s natural beauty and cultural heritage offer an escape to travelers who are looking for a serene and peaceful destination. From stunning treks and outdoor adventures, rich cultural experiences, to incredible local food, and must-visit attractions. Saho has something to offer for everyone.
