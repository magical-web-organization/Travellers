Introduction to Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park is a natural gem located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, situated in the Himalayan North of India. It is a part of the largest wildlife reserve in India - the Corbett Tiger Reserve, which was established in 1936 by the British government to protect the endangered Bengal Tiger. Today, it is one of the most popular tourist destinations in India, drawing nature enthusiasts from all over the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Jim Corbett National Park

The Kumaoni culture is celebrated in Jim Corbett National Park. People in this region follow their ancient traditions and rituals. They wear culturally inspired clothing and perform folk dances at festivals.

Best Time to Visit Jim Corbett National Park

The best time to visit Jim Corbett National Park is from November to June, except for the monsoon season from July to September, which should be avoided.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jim Corbett National Park

Do:

  • Follow Jungle Safari Schedule
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife
  • Carry insect repellent
  • Dress in neutral colors

Don’t:

  • Lit campfires in the park
  • Disturb the animals or their habitat
  • Litter in the park

Safety and Health

Safety in Jim Corbett National Park

  • Follow park rules and regulations
  • Avoid going out at night
  • Carry a first aid kit and medicines.

Health in Jim Corbett National Park

  • Drink only bottled water
  • Avoid uncooked food or street food
