Introduction to Binsar

Binsar is a hidden gem situated in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India. It is an ideal destination for those seeking natural beauty, tranquillity, and off-the-beaten-track adventures. Binsar is known for its rich flora and fauna, including oak, rhododendron, and deodar forests. The region is also home to several endangered species, including the Himalayan Black Bear, barking deer, and wild boar. The region is surrounded by snow-capped peaks, making every view a breathtaking one. In this guide, we will explore all there is to know about Binsar, including the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Binsar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary Museum: Situated in Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary, the museum showcases the flora and fauna of the region.
  • Bineshwar Mahadev Temple: A 16th-century temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is situated in the heart of the sanctuary.

Natural Attractions of Binsar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zero Point: It is a perfect destination for panoramic views of snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas.
  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary: An excursion to Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary, the home of several endangered species, is a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts.
  • Gananath Temple: Situated in a beautiful meadow, this temple is a great place to relax and enjoy the serenity of the natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Binsar

Binsar is a haven for hiking enthusiasts. Here are the best hiking trails to explore:

  • Binsar Sanctuary Trail: A 10-kilometre trail that passes through the sanctuary, showcasing the best of the flora and fauna of the region.
  • Pariyadeva Pashan Trek: A 2-kilometre trek that takes you to a rock formation that resembles a sitting woman.
  • Binsar to Jageshwar Trek: A 20-kilometre trek that takes you through the pristine beauty of the wilderness to the historical Jageshwar temple complex.

Best Things to Do in Binsar: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari: Taking a safari ride through Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary is an incredible experience to witness the broad diversity of wild animals and birds.
  • Nature Walks: Stroll along the natural trails of Binsar and enjoy the scenic beauty of the surroundings, keeping an eye out for the wide range of wildlife that lives in the sanctuary.
  • Trek to Zero Point: An early morning trek to Zero Point offers an opportunity to witness the astonishing sunrise over the Himalayan peaks.
  • Camping: Stay amidst the picturesque surroundings of Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary in tents and spend an unforgettable night under the stars.

Food and Drink Scene of Binsar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhatt ki Churkani: A popular black bean dish with a tangy flavour.
  • Aloo Ke Gutke: A famous Kumaoni potato dish cooked with cumin seeds and other aromatic spices.
  • Gahat Ki Dal: A high protein lentil soup made from a local lentil variety grown in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Buransh Juice: A refreshing juice made from the flowers of Rhododendron tree, a local specialty.
  • Thandai: A cold, spicy milk beverage with almond and saffron.

Where to Stay in Binsar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Binsar. Here are some of the best:

  • The Kumaon: A beautiful wooden lodge situated in a pine forest with views of the snow-capped mountains.
  • Binsar Eco Camp: This camp offers permanent tented accommodation and the perfect way to experience the wilderness of Binsar.
  • KMVN Tourist Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house operated by the Kumaon Mandal Vikas Nigam, situated right beside the Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary gate.

What to Buy in Binsar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shawls and Stoles: Handwoven shawls and stoles made from the local sheep wool are a speciality of the region.
  • Woodcrafts: Binsar is known for its intricate woodcarvings, and visitors can buy beautiful wooden products as souvenirs.
  • Tea: The region produces high-quality tea and is an excellent souvenir to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Binsar

Binsar is a part of the Kumaon region, which has its unique traditions, music, and dance forms. The people of the region are friendly and hospitable, and visitors should respect local customs and cultures.

Best Time to Visit Binsar

The best time to visit Binsar is from October to November and from March to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Binsar

Do:

  • Pack warm clothes, even in the summer months.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry a First Aid kit, especially on treks and outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the wildlife.
  • Pluck flora or fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Binsar

Binsar is a safe destination for travellers, but visitors are advised to take standard precautions while travelling in the region. Avoid travelling alone at night, especially in isolated areas.

Health in Binsar

Binsar has a mountainous terrain and can pose some health risks, especially for visitors with respiratory problems. Visitors are advised to carry enough medication and seek medical attention if required.

Getting to Binsar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Binsar

The nearest airport to Binsar is Pantnagar Airport, which is around 150 kms away from Binsar.

Bus Stations Near Binsar

The nearest bus station to Binsar is Haldwani Bus Station, which is around 119 kms away.

Other Options

The best way to reach Binsar is to take a taxi or private car from Kathgodam railway station, which is around 112 kms away.

How to Get Around in Binsar

The best way to explore Binsar is to go on foot. Trekking and hiking trails are the primary modes of transport in the region. Visitors can also hire taxi services to explore the region’s major attractions.

Where to Go Next from Binsar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Almora: A picturesque hill station situated around 32 kms away from Binsar, known for its beautiful architecture and rich cultural heritage.
  • Mukteshwar: A scenic hill station situated around 68 kms away from Binsar, known for its breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
  • Nainital: A popular hill station situated around 105 kms away from Binsar, known for its beautiful lake and picturesque surroundings.

Conclusion

Binsar is a nature lover’s paradise and has something to offer for everyone who loves adventure, nature and culture. A trip to Binsar is an unforgettable experience and a must-visit destination for anyone who wishes to escape to the mountains.
