Peora is a small charming town located in the Kumaon region in the hills of Uttarakhand, North India. Blessed with breath-taking views of the snow-capped Himalayan Mountains, the picturesque town is a far cry from the hustle and bustle of the city. Peora is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a peaceful getaway. In this comprehensive travel guide to Peora, we will take you through the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, cultural landmarks, food and drink scene, and much more.

Introduction to Peora

Peora is an idyllic village, situated at an altitude of approximately 5900 feet above sea level. It is surrounded by dense forests, apple orchards and terraced fields, making it a nature lover’s paradise. The town is home to friendly locals who welcome visitors with open arms. The town’s tranquility, combined with its natural beauty, make it a perfect base for exploring the surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Peora

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mukteshwar Temple: located around 20 km away from Peora, the temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and boasts of marvelous architecture.

  2. Nanda Devi Temple: located in the heart of the town, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Nanda Devi, and is a reminder of the rich heritage of the region.

Natural Attractions of Peora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kumaon Himalayas: the town is blessed with panoramic views of the Kumaon Himalayas. One can catch a glimpse of several mountain peaks such as Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Panchachuli.

  2. Khali Estate: the estate provides an opportunity for visitors to indulge in outdoor activities such as camping, nature walks and bird watching. Visitors can also enjoy the natural beauty of the region while staying in one of the luxurious cottages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peora

Peora is a haven for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are a number of trails in the surrounding hills that offer breathtaking views and provide an intimate connection with nature. Some popular routes include:

  1. Mukteshwar trek: the trek is approximately 10 km long and takes you through dense forests and apple orchards.

  2. Jungle walk: the trail is ideal for wildlife enthusiasts, taking you through dense forests that are home to several species of birds and animals.

Best Things to Do in Peora: Activities and Tours

  1. Yoga and meditation: Peora’s tranquil surroundings make it an ideal location for yoga and meditation. Several yoga centers and meditation retreats offer day-long as well as week-long courses.

  2. Apple picking: the town is famous for its apples, and visitors can indulge in apple picking at several orchards in the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peora

Day 1: Arrive in Peora and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town.

Day 2: Visit the Nanda Devi Temple and learn about the rich cultural heritage of the region. Go for a jungle walk in the evening.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Mukteshwar and explore the temple and its surroundings. Go for a hike in the hills and indulge in some apple picking.

Day 4: Spend the day at Khali Estate and indulge in some outdoor activities such as camping and bird watching.

Day 5: Take a tour of the town’s apple orchards and learn about the cultivation of apples in the region.

Day 6: Attend a yoga and meditation session and spend the day relaxing in nature.

Day 7: Depart from Peora with beautiful memories of your trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Peora

The food in Peora is a reflection of the region’s rich heritage. The town is known for its delectable dishes made from locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bhang ki Chutney: a type of chutney made from hemp seeds.

  2. Bhatt ke Dubke: a dish made from black beans and served with rice or roti.

Local Beverages

  1. Kumaoni tea: a special tea made in the Kumaon region using locally grown tea leaves.

  2. Rhododendron juice: a refreshing drink made from the flowers of the rhododendron plant.

Where to Stay in Peora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peora offers a variety of accommodation options catering to all budgets. Visitors can choose to stay in a luxury cottage or a budget-friendly guesthouse. Some popular options include:

  1. Himalayan View Cottage: a luxurious cottage located in the heart of the town.

  2. Peora Heritage Homestay: a quaint homestay that offers visitors an opportunity to live with the locals and experience their hospitality.

What to Buy in Peora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Peora is famous for its handicrafts made from wood, wool, and clay.

  2. Apples: visitors can bring home some fresh apples from the town’s orchards.

Local Culture and Customs of Peora

Peora’s culture is deeply rooted in the traditional ways of life in the mountains. The locals still follow traditional customs and rituals, and visitors are encouraged to respect their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Peora

The best time to visit Peora is between the months of March and June, and September to December. The town experiences a pleasant climate during these months and the skies are usually clear, offering an unobstructed view of the Kumaon Himalayas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peora

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Indulge in outdoor activities and explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the town or in the surrounding hills.
  2. Dress inappropriately when visiting temples or other religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peora

Peora is a safe destination for tourists. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions when going for hikes and nature walks, and ensure they are accompanied by a local guide.

Health in Peora

Visitors are advised to carry sufficient warm clothing since the temperature can drop sharply after the sun sets. It is always advisable to carry a first-aid kit when going for hikes.

Getting to Peora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peora

Dehradun Airport is the nearest airport to Peora, located approximately 290 km away. Taxis can be hired from the airport to reach Peora.

Bus Stations Near Peora

Kathgodam Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Peora, located approximately 60 km away.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi from Kathgodam Railway Station or Haldwani Railway Station to reach Peora.

How to Get Around in Peora

The town is small and can be easily covered on foot. Visitors can also hire a taxi or a bicycle to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Peora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Almora: a picturesque town located approximately 50 km away from Peora, known for its quaint bazaars and architecture.

  2. Nainital: a popular tourist destination, located approximately 86 km away from Peora, known for its serene lake and beautiful surroundings.

Conclusion

Peora is a hidden gem in the hills of Uttarakhand. The town’s natural beauty, combined with its rich cultural heritage, make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a peaceful getaway. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope to help you plan your trip to Peora and make the most of your visit.
