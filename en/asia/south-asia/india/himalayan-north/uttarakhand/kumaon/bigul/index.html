Introduction to Bigul

Nestled in the north of Uttarakhand, Bigul is a serene offbeat destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. The quaint hill town is known for its picturesque landscapes, pleasant climate, and peaceful ambiance. Whether you are a history buff, adventure enthusiast, or cultural explorer, Bigul never disappoints with its plethora of tourist attractions, hiking and biking trails, food scenes, and much more. Here is a comprehensive tour guide that will help you plan your trip to Bigul with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Bigul

Bigul offers an extensive range of cultural and natural attractions that cater to the varied interests of the visitors. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bigul:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khali Estate: The historical estate of the Kumaon ruler, Khali estate, is now converted into an elegant heritage hotel. The property boasts of ancient architecture, reminiscent of the traditional Kumaoni style.
  • Bright End Corner: A popular viewpoint that offers a picturesque view of the Himalayan ranges, valley, and cityscape. This viewpoint is also known for its sunset and sunrise views.
  • Gandhi Ashram: Located en route to Ranikhet, Gandhi Ashram is a peaceful abode for all those who seek a tranquil and serene atmosphere. Visitors can explore the ashram and learn about the non-violent philosophy and teachings of Mahatma Gandhi.
  • The Kumaon Regimental Centre Museum: A well-maintained military museum that enlightens visitors about the history of the Kumaon regiment and displays artifacts and weaponry used in wartime.

Natural Attractions of Bigul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rani Jheel: A serene lake hidden away in the heart of the town, it is a perfect spot for birdwatching, picnics or just to soak up some peace and tranquility.
  • Binsar Mahadev Temple: The ancient temple is hidden from the eyes of the casual traveler, located in a remote location on the site of the famous Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary. The place is picturesque and perfect for a nature walk and bird watching.
  • Chaubatia Gardens: The fruit orchards and the botanical gardens of Chaubatia will leave you breathless with their vivid beauty. There are plenty of outdoor activities like trekking and rock climbing you can indulge in around the gardens.
  • Kumaon Language Museum: An initiative by Dr. Kamal K. Mishra, a renowned Kumaoni linguist, the Kumaon Language Museum provides visitors with an insight into the language, culture, and customs of the Kumaon region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bigul

Bigul is heaven for hikers and bikers, with numerous routes to uncover the hilly terrain and alpine surroundings. Here are the top hiking/biking routes in Bigul:

  • Binsar Sanctuary: This route offers a variety of hikes catering to different fitness levels. The trails provide scenic views of the Himalayan peaks, forests, and valleys.
  • Kunjani Trek: Pass through picturesque villages, enjoy the local culture and hospitality through this route while hiking up to Kunjani Pass at a height of over 2500 meters.
  • Mukteshwar-Chauli Ki Jali Trek: A moderately challenging route that culminates at the famous Chauli Ki Jali rock face, where you are greeted by an impressive panoramic view of the Himalayan range.

Best Things to Do in Bigul: Activities and Tours

Bigul is a destination of choice for those seeking a peaceful and offbeat vacation, catering to various interests of visitors. Here are some things you must do while in Bigul:

  • Explore Local Culture: Experience the Kumaoni way of life, up-close with homestays in the village, engage in local cultural and culinary experiences, and participate in festivals and fairs.
  • Pant Nagar University: A visit to this university provides an insight into the agricultural practices in the Himalayan region, cherry orchards, and local produce cultivation.
  • Paragliding: Glide over the Himalayan ranges and get a bird’s eye view of the landscape. The take-off sites are located at Naukuchiatal, Bhimtal, and Mukteshwar.
  • Food Tours: Relish traditional Kumaoni cuisine of Mandua ki Roti, Bhatt ki chudkani, and Sepu Badi in the picturesque settings of the Himalayas.
  • Yoga and Meditation: Soak yourself in the serenity and spirituality of the Himalayan foothills by attending daily yoga and meditation classes.

Food and Drink Scene of Bigul

Bigul has a rich gastronomic heritage that blends Indian and Himalayan cuisine, a must-try for those who come here. Here are some of the signature dishes and beverages of Bigul:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhatt ki Chudkani: A classic dish made of black beans and spices, typically served with mandua ki roti (finger millet bread).
  • Aloo Gutka: A spicy and tangy potato chutney served alongside other Kumaoni delights.
  • Bal Mithai: A sweet, fudgy caramelized sweet made using khoya.
  • Maggie with Soya, Raita of Boondi: The Himalayan take on Maggi, it is garnished with a raita of boondi and soya, giving it an Indian twist.

Local Beverages

  • Kumaoni Chai: A unique blend of tea, cinnamon, and cardamom, Kumaoni chai is perfect to warm you up during the chilly weather.
  • Arsa: A local alcoholic beverage made from fermented leaves, this beverage is a specialty of the Kumaon region.

Where to Stay in Bigul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bigul offers budget-friendly and luxury accommodation options that cater to the varied interests of the visitors. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Bigul:

  • Bungalows LakeView: A luxury resort set amidst apple orchards, with a stunning view of the mountains and the lake.
  • Kumaon Roop Resort: A family-friendly resort surrounded by greenery, it provides a range of activities like bird watching, trekking, and much more.
  • Hotel New Bharat: A budget-friendly option for those traveling to Bigul. The hotel’s location is close to the city’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Bigul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bigul is known for its natural and handicraft products that reflect the local culture and traditions. Here are some of the best souvenirs and products you can bring home from Bigul:

  • Angora and Sheep Woolen products: Shawls, mufflers, sweaters, and stoles made of sheep and angora wool are specialties of the Kumaon region.
  • Kalmuhi: A local handcrafted product made of clay; it is used to store drinking water and is a traditional and natural method of keeping water cool.
  • Organic Products: Bigul is known for its organic and natural products like honey, chutneys, and apricots.

Local Culture and Customs of Bigul

Bigul is home to the Kumaoni culture, which is a blend of Hindu and Buddhist faiths. The people of the region value hospitality and welcome visitors with open arms. A few tips to ensure that you respect the local customs and traditions:

  • Dress Modestly: Ensure that you dress modestly and respectfully while visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs: Follow local customs, respect the culture and traditions, and take prior permission before taking photographs and participating in any cultural activities.
  • Stay Clean: Practice cleanliness and hygiene while exploring the surrounding areas and natural attractions of Bigul.

Best Time to Visit Bigul

The best time to visit Bigul is from March to June, just after the winter season. The climate during this period is pleasant and mild, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. You can also plan a visit during September and October, as the autumn season offers plenty of opportunities for adventure sports and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bigul

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Bigul:

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Be culturally sensitive and dress modestly while visiting religious places
  • Indulge in local cuisine and cultural experiences
  • Carry cash, as there are limited ATMs in the region

Don’t:

  • Refrain from littering and harming the natural surroundings of Bigul
  • Don’t disrespect the local traditions
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife or touch the natural flora/fauna

Safety and Health

Safety in Bigul

Bigul is a safe destination with low crime rates, and the people are friendly and welcoming. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid venturing out alone at night, especially in isolated areas.
  • Make sure to secure your accommodation, rooms and lockers properly; always carry a copy of your ID proof.
  • Don’t leave any valuable items unattended in hotel rooms or in public spaces.

Health in Bigul

Bigul is a clean and hygienic destination, and there are no major health concerns. However, it’s always a good idea to adhere to some basic health rules while traveling:

  • Carry your basic medications and first aid kit if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.
  • Don’t drink tap water, always opt for boiled or packaged bottled water.
  • Carry medical insurance to cover emergency medical expenses.

Getting to Bigul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bigul is accessible by air, rail, and road. Here are some of the transportation options to get to Bigul:

Airports Near Bigul

The Pant Nagar Airport, located at a distance of 115 km from Bigul, is the nearest airport. There are regular flights to Pant Nagar Airport from Delhi, Lucknow, and Dehradun.

Bus Stations Near Bigul

Bigul is well-connected by road from major cities and towns of Uttarakhand. There are frequent buses from Haldwani, Nainital, Almora, and other nearby destinations to Bigul.

Other Options

  • Taxis are available for hire from Haldwani, Nainital, and Almora or one can also book private taxis from Delhi.

How to Get Around in Bigul

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are readily available to get around within the town. For hiking and biking, you can hire a guide or rent a cycle from the local vendors.

Where to Go Next from Bigul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bigul serves as an excellent base for discovering the nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the top destinations to visit:

  • Nainital: Located approximately 70 km from Bigul, Nainital is a serene hill station famous for its Naini Lake and scenic views.
  • Almora: A cultural hub of Kumaon, situated 58 km from Bigul, it’s known for its handicrafts and natural beauty.
  • Bhimtal: Located 38 km from Bigul, Bhimtal is a tranquil town famous for its scenic lake.

Conclusion

Bigul is a hidden gem in the hills, full of natural beauty and cultural richness. With plenty of recreational activities, hiking trails, scenic views, and cultural experiences, Bigul is an ideal destination for those seeking relaxation and adventure.
