Introduction to Almora

Perched high in the mountains of Uttarakhand, Northern India, lies Almora – a charming town that offers a great escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Almora is known for its pristine beauty, rich cultural heritage, and breathtaking views of the Himalayan range. This guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Almora

Almora is full of spectacular attractions that cater to all interests. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Govind Vallabh Pant Public Museum: The museum offers a glimpse into the rich history, culture, and art of Kumaon.
  • Kasar Devi Temple: A revered Hindu temple and a pilgrimage site for locals and tourists alike.
  • Nanda Devi Temple: An ancient temple dedicated to the goddess Nanda Devi.
  • Katarmal Sun Temple: An ancient temple complex that dates back to the 9th century

Natural Attractions of Almora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary: A sanctuary that is home to a variety of flora and fauna, and offers gorgeous views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Zero Point: A viewpoint from where you can get panoramic views of the Himalayan range.
  • Bright End Corner: A scenic spot that offers stunning views of the sunrise and sunset.
  • Chitai Temple: A temple located in a serene setting that offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Almora

Almora is an ideal place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: A trek that takes you through the sanctuary’s serene forests and offers stunning views of the Himalayan range.
  • Kathgodam to Nainital Bike Ride: A scenic bike ride that takes you through the picturesque countryside.
  • Jalori Pass Trek: A challenging trek that offers panoramic views of the Himalayan range.

Best Things to Do in Almora: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of things to see and do in Almora. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Shopping at Lala Bazar: A bustling market that is famous for its handicrafts, fabrics, and souvenirs.
  • Strolling through the old town: A walk through the charming old town of Almora is a must-do activity.
  • Experiencing local festivals and fairs: Some of the popular festivals include Nanda Devi Mela, Uttarayani Mela, and Sharadotsav.

Food and Drink Scene of Almora

Almora is known for its delicious cuisine, and you’ll find plenty of options to tantalize your taste buds. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhatt ki Churkani: A traditional Kumaoni dish made with black beans and served with rice.
  • Madua ki Roti: A nutritious flatbread made with a locally grown grain called ‘madua’.
  • Bal Mithai: A sweet made with khoya and coated with sugar balls.

Local Beverages

  • Buransh Juice: A refreshing drink made with the juice of the Buransh flower.
  • Arsa: A local alcoholic beverage made with rice.

Where to Stay in Almora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Almora offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Kalmatia Sangam: A luxury property that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Heritage Hotel Kesar Devi: A charming heritage property that is perfect for those looking for a tranquil retreat.
  • Khazanchand Mansion: A budget option that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town center.

What to Buy in Almora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Almora is famous for its handicrafts, fabrics, and souvenirs. Here’s what you should consider buying:

  • Kumaoni Shawls: These shawls are made with wool and are warm, soft, and cozy.
  • Angora Wool Products: Almora is known for its angora wool products, which include scarfs, hats, and socks.
  • Handmade Soaps and Herbal Products: These are made with natural ingredients and are good for the skin.

Local Culture and Customs of Almora

Almora has a rich cultural heritage, and it is important to respect local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly: Almora is a conservative town, and it is advisable to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Respect local customs: It is important to respect local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and fairs.
  • Take off your shoes: It is customary to take off your shoes when entering a temple or a home.

Best Time to Visit Almora

The best time to visit Almora is during the summer months (April to June) when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. The winter months (November to February) can be harsh, with snowfall and low temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Almora

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Almora:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry warm clothes during winters
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment
  • Disrespect religious sites and monuments
  • Haggle excessively while shopping

Safety and Health

Safety in Almora

Almora is a safe destination for travellers, but it is important to take necessary precautions:

  • Avoid drinking tap water: Stick to bottled water to avoid any water-borne diseases.
  • Carry warm clothes during winters: The winters in Almora can be harsh, and it is important to carry warm clothes.

Health in Almora

Almora has a few hospitals and medical facilities. However, it is advisable to carry a basic first aid kit with you.

Getting to Almora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Almora

The nearest airport to Almora is Pantnagar Airport (115 kilometers away).

Bus Stations Near Almora

Almora is well connected by road, and you can find buses from major cities like Delhi, Nainital, and Haldwani.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or drive on your own to reach Almora from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Almora

The best way to explore Almora is by walking. If you’re heading to places outside the town, you can hire a taxi or a local bus.

Where to Go Next from Almora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Almora. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Nainital: A popular hill station that is known for its scenic beauty and serene lakes.
  • Kausani: A picturesque town that offers stunning views of the Himalayan range.
  • Ranikhet: A serene town that is known for its lush greenery and scenic hiking trails.

Conclusion

Almora is a charming town that offers a perfect escape for nature lovers, culture seekers, and adventure enthusiasts. With this guide, you can make the most out of your trip to Almora and explore the best places to visit, things to do, and more. So what are you waiting for? Pack your bags, book your tickets, and get ready to experience the magic of Almora!
