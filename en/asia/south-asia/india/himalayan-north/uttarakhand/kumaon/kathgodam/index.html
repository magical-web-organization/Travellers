Introduction to Kathgodam

Nestled in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, Kathgodam perches at an elevation of 554 meters above sea level. Known as the “gateway to Kumaon hills,” this scenic town is a popular stopover for travelers heading towards Nainital, Bhimtal, or Almora. The town, located at the foothills of the Himalayas, serves as a base for treks into the jungles and hills of Kumaon. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a nature lover, Kathgodam has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kathgodam

Kathgodam boasts of an array of tourist attractions that can cater to every traveler’s interest. Here’s a list of the best places to visit in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • G.B. Pant Museum: Dedicated to the first chief minister of Uttarakhand, this museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It displays artifacts and memorabilia from his life and work.

  • Mukteshwar Temple: Located in the nearby town of Mukteshwar, this 350-year-old temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is an ideal stop for those interested in history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Kathgodam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sattal: Sattal, meaning “seven lakes,” is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Surrounded by dense forests, this cluster of seven freshwater lakes offers one of the most scenic views in the area.

  • Bhimtal Lake: Boating, fishing, and photography enthusiasts will enjoy a visit to the tranquil Bhimtal Lake. It is easy to access and offers picturesque views of the hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kathgodam

Kathgodam offers an array of hiking and biking trails that cater to different skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Nainital-Kathgodam Hike: This is a relatively easy hike that passes through dense jungles and scenic hills. It culminates in Nainital, where travelers can enjoy boating, shopping, and dining.

  • Jeolikote-Bhowali-Mukteshwar Bike Trip: This bike trail takes you through some of the most scenic landscapes in the region. The route follows the zig-zag roads of the hills and offers panoramic views of the valley.

Best Things to Do in Kathgodam: Activities and Tours

Kathgodam offers an array of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some suggestions:

  • Mountain Biking Tours: Join a guided bike tour along the picturesque trails of Kumaon. Travelers can enjoy panoramic views and explore the countryside on their own pace.

  • Boating at Naini Lake: Head to Nainital and take a relaxing boat ride on the famous Naini Lake while admiring the scenic beauty of the hills.

Food and Drink Scene of Kathgodam

Kathgodam offers a diverse range of local delicacies that travelers must try during their visit. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhatt Ki Churkani: It is a popular dish made from black beans cooked with a selection of spices and served with steaming rice.

  • Kafuli: This is a vegetarian dish made from spinach and fenugreek leaves, and served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Buransh: A refreshing drink made from rhododendron petals, which is unique to the Kumaon region.

  • Kumaoni Tea: A special tea made by using burnt rhododendron leaves, black tea leaves, and spices. It is served hot with a spoonful of honey.

Where to Stay in Kathgodam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kathgodam offers a range of accommodation options to cater for every budget. Here are some recommended choices:

  • Kumaon Roop Resort: This resort offers a comfortable stay and amazing views of the mountains.

  • The Pine Crest: The Pine Crest is a budget-friendly option offering cozy and clean rooms just a short walk from the train station.

What to Buy in Kathgodam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kathgodam offers some unique and local items perfect for bringing home. Here are some recommendations:

  • Handicrafts and handlooms: The Kumaon region is known for its handicrafts and handlooms, so take the time to browse through the local markets to find some unique items.

  • Local honey: Kumaon produces some of India’s best honey. Head to the local honey shops to pick up some authentic local honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Kathgodam

Kathgodam boasts of a unique and vibrant culture that stems from the region’s history and geography. Here are some interesting facts about the town:

  • Fairs and festivals: The town celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Kautik Mela, the Sharadotsav Mela, and the Nanda Devi Mela.

  • Traditional attire: Kumaoni people still wear their traditional attire on special occasions. Women wear colorful sarees and men wear kurta-pajama with a shawl.

Best Time to Visit Kathgodam

The best time to visit Kathgodam is from March to June and from September to November when the weather is pleasant and most sightseeing places are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kathgodam

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Kathgodam:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Carry enough cash as ATMs are scarce in the rural areas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute the environment.

  • Don’t damage or climb on the historical monuments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kathgodam:

Kathgodam is generally a safe place to visit, but travelers are advised to take the following precautions:

  • Always carry a torch while walking at night.

  • Avoid wearing expensive jewelry and carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Kathgodam

While Kathgodam is relatively safe, travelers must take precautions to maintain personal hygiene, including:

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.

  • Carry medications for common ailments like stomach infections, fever, and cold.

Getting to Kathgodam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kathgodam is well connected with major cities of north India by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Kathgodam

The nearest airport to Kathgodam is Pantnagar Airport, which is about 65 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kathgodam

Kathgodam has a major bus station that connects the town with major destinations in north India.

Other Options

The Kalka-Shimla express is another popular option that takes you on a scenic journey through the hills to Kathgodam.

How to Get Around in Kathgodam

Local buses, shared taxis, and private cabs are the most common modes of transport within Kathgodam and the surrounding region.

Conclusion

Kathgodam is a hidden gem tucked away in the foothills of the Himalayas. Boasting of natural beauty, vibrant culture, and a rich history, this town is a must-visit destination for every traveler heading towards the Kumaon region. With this comprehensive guide, you have all the information you need to plan your trip to Kathgodam today.
