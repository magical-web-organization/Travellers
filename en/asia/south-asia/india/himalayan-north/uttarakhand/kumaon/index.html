Introduction to Kumaon

Kumaon is located in the northern part of the state of Uttarakhand, India. The cities of Nainital, Almora, and Ranikhet are some of the most popular tourist destinations in Kumaon. The history of Kumaon dates back to the ancient period and it boasts of its rich cultural heritage.

Kumaon is known for its religious temples, scenic beauty, wildlife sanctuary, national parks, and mesmerizing beauty that attracts tourists from different parts of the world. The snow-capped Himalayan ranges offer magnificent views attracting adventure enthusiasts as well.

This guide has been designed to help you explore the beauty of Kumaon and make your trip a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Kumaon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nainital Lake: It is the most popular attraction in the region and is located in the heart of the town. It is a freshwater lake surrounded by scenic beauty and is a perfect place for boating and picnics.

  • Kainchi Dham: It is a famous ashram located in the Kumoan region. It is known for its spiritual significance and is a popular tourist attraction.

  • Gobind Vallabh Pant Museum: The museum showcases the rich history, culture, and heritage of Kumoan. It is a perfect destination for history buffs and art lovers.

  • Jageshwar Temple: It is a group of over 100 ancient Hindu temples located in the Jageshwar valley.

Natural Attractions of Kumaon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary: It is a popular nature reserve known for its exotic flora and fauna. It is an ideal place for nature lovers and adventurers.

  • Jim Corbett National Park: One of the most famous national parks in India, it is known for its tigers, elephants, deer, and other wildlife.

  • Nanda Devi National Park: This park is famous for the Nanda Devi peak, which is the second highest peak in India.

  • Kumaon Himalayas: It is a popular tourist attraction, known for its scenic beauty and adventure activities like trekking, mountaineering, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumaon

  • Nainital-Bhimtal-Sattal Trek: The trek passes through several small villages and provides a unique opportunity to explore the beauty of the region.

  • Ranikhet-Chaubatia Trek: This trek offers breathtaking views of the snow-clad Himalayan range and is a perfect destination for adventure enthusiasts.

  • Almora-Jageshwar Trek: The trek passes through some of the most important religious sites of Kumaon and is perfect for those looking to explore the region’s cultural heritage.

Best Things to Do in Kumaon: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Ride at Nainital Lake: Enjoy a boat ride at Nainital Lake and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and greenery.

  • Rappelling at Chauli ki Jali: This adventure activity involves descending a steep rock face using ropes and is perfect for adventure enthusiasts.

  • Jungle Safari at Jim Corbett National Park: Take a wildlife safari to spot the majestic tigers, elephants, and other wildlife.

  • Yoga and Meditation: Kumaon is renowned for its spiritual significance and offers visitors a chance to indulge in yoga and meditation to rejuvenate their mind and soul.

Food and Drink Scene of Kumaon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhatt Ki Churkani: A popular dish made with black soybeans and ghee.

  • Dubuk: A soup-like dish made with black lentils.

  • Aloo Ke Gutke: Fried potatoes tempered with spices and served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Buransh: A refreshing drink made with a local Himalayan flower.

  • Chhang: A fermented beverage made with barley and is a popular local drink in Kumaon.

Where to Stay in Kumaon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nainital: There are plenty of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses available in Nainital, catering to a range of budgets.

  • Almora: Almora offers an excellent choice of accommodations from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Ranikhet: There are a few premium hotels in Ranikhet that offer luxury accommodation, while budget travelers can book guesthouses or budget hotels.

What to Buy in Kumaon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Shawls and Sweaters: Kumaon is known for its hand-woven shawls and sweaters made from wool and other local fabrics.

  • Local Honey: The region is known for its honey, which is considered to be very high in quality and taste.

  • Handicrafts: The region is famous for its handicrafts, including traditional wooden toys and bamboo handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumaon

The people of Kumaon speak Kumaoni, a dialect of the Pahari language. Kumaon is rich in culture and traditions, and visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly. The region is known for its vibrant festivals, including Harela and Bikhauti Mela.

Best Time to Visit Kumaon

The best time to visit Kumaon is between March to June and from September to December. During the summer months, the weather is pleasant, and the mountains are dotted with colorful flowers. The winter months from December to February are ideal for those looking for snowbound adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumaon

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Enjoy the scenic beauty and local culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter the pristine environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife in their natural habitat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumaon

Kumaon is generally a safe region to visit. However, visitors should exercise caution while traveling to remote areas and always take necessary precautions while participating in adventure activities.

Health in Kumaon

Visitors to Kumaon should consult a doctor before visiting and ensure they have the necessary vaccinations. It is recommended to carry a first aid kit and medication as many rural areas may not have access to medical facilities.

Getting to Kumaon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kumaon

The nearest airport is Pantnagar Airport, located around 70 km from Nainital.

Bus Stations Near Kumaon

Nainital, Almora, and Ranikhet are accessible by bus from major cities in India.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can be hired from different parts of Uttarakhand to reach Kumaon. Tourist buses are also available.

How to Get Around in Kumaon

Taxis and private cars are readily available for hire in Kumaon. Buses are also available, but it is recommended to book a taxi for a comfortable journey.

Where to Go Next from Kumaon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mukteshwar: A scenic town known for its stunning views of the Himalayas.

  • Binsar: Known for its wildlife sanctuary and breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

  • Jim Corbett National Park: Famous for its big cats and other wildlife species.

Conclusion

Kumaon is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those wanting to explore the region’s rich culture and heritage. With its scenic beauty, vibrant festivals, and delicious local cuisine, Kumaon has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Kumaon and ensure a memorable experience.
