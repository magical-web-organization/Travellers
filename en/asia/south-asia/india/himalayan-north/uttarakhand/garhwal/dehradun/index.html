Dehradun, also called the “Oxford of India,” is located in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand in the Himalayan North. It is a beautiful city with lush greenery and scenic views that attract tourists from around the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, and local culture of Dehradun.

Introduction to Dehradun

Dehradun is a flourishing city with a rich history and a mix of urban and natural beauty. The city is surrounded by the Himalayas and the Shivalik range with the Ganges and Yamuna rivers flowing on either side of the city. The city is a hub for education, entrepreneurship, and tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Dehradun

Here are some of the top places to visit in Dehradun:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Robber’s Cave: This 600-meter-long cave is an outstanding natural wonder surrounded by hills and is a must-visit destination.
  • Zonal Anthropological Museum: This museum exhibits the culture and heritage of the native tribes, including the personal ornaments, tools, and weapons used by them.
  • Forest Research Institute: This grand establishment is a hub for forestry research and education, and its colonial architecture and vast museum are open to visitors.
  • Tapkeshwar Temple: This 8th-century temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is located inside a natural cave with water dripping from the ceiling.
  • Guru Ram Rai Darbar: This renowned Sikh shrine is a grand establishment and is known for its unique blend of Sikh and Hindu architecture.

Natural Attractions of Dehradun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rajaji National Park: This national park is situated on the outermost fringes of Dehradun and is home to several species of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and bears.
  • Sahastradhara: This place is known for its enchanting waterfalls and medicinal springs, and it is believed that taking a dip in the water here can cleanse your soul.
  • Malsi Deer Park: This park is home to several species of deer, including the spotted deer, and you can take a safari and explore the natural beauty of this place.
  • Lachhiwala: This natural pool is an ideal place for swimming, picnic, and nature walks, and it is located amid dense forests of Sal trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dehradun

There are several hiking and biking trails across Dehradun, and some of the must-try routes are:

  • Tiger Falls Trek: This trek takes you through a dense forest of pine and deodar trees, and the final destination is the mesmerizing Tiger Falls.
  • Nag Tibba Trek: This moderately challenging trek takes you to the Nag Tibba peak, and on the way, you can witness the beauty of the Himalayas and several mountain villages.
  • Kempty Falls Trek: This trek involves a hike to the beautiful Kempty Falls, and on the way, you can witness the beauty of the Himalayas and several mountain villages.

Best Things to Do in Dehradun: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Dehradun:

  • White water rafting: The Ganges river near Dehradun offers an incredible opportunity for a thrilling white water rafting experience.
  • Hot air balloon ride: Witness the beauty of Dehradun from above with a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the panoramic views of the Himalayas and the city.
  • Food walk tour: Experience the local cuisine of Dehradun and explore famous food joints in the city that offer mouth-watering street food.

Food and Drink Scene of Dehradun

Dehradun offers a range of street food and local cuisine that is both delicious and affordable. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kanji Wada: Made with moong dal, Kanji Wada is a popular street food of Dehradun sold at food stalls.
  • Aloo Tamatar Ka Jhol: This is a popular dish made with potatoes and tomato-based curry.
  • Momos: These steamed dumplings are a widespread delicacy across Dehradun and come with both vegetarian and non-vegetarian fillings.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: This is a natural drink found in all roadside eateries and stalls across Dehradun and is made with buttermilk and spices.
  • Lassi: This is a refreshing drink that is made with yoghurt and is served with fruits, dried fruits, or a variety of spices.
  • Kaphal Juice: This juice is made from the berries of the Kaphal tree, which is found in the natural surroundings of Dehradun.

Where to Stay in Dehradun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dehradun has several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses catering to different budgets and traveler preferences. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Vivanta Dehradun by Taj: This 5-star hotel is located in the city center and offers luxurious amenities and services.
  • Hotel Madhuban Dehradun: This 4-star hotel is located on Rajpur Road and offers affordable luxury and comfortable stay.
  • Moustache Dehradun: This hostel is located in the heart of Dehradun, and it is an ideal place for budget travelers who want to experience the local culture of the city.

What to Buy in Dehradun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dehradun is known for its woolen products, handicrafts, and local food products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Pashmina shawls: These are top-quality woolen shawls made with the finest wool fibers and are sold at several outlets across Dehradun.
  • Wooden handicrafts: These handicrafts include wooden statues, furniture, and toys made by local artisans using traditional techniques.
  • Local tea and honey: Dehradun is known for its tea and honey production, and you can buy local tea and honey from several stores and outlets across the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Dehradun

Dehradun has a unique mix of cultures, including Garhwalis, Punjabis, and Tibetans, contributing to various customs and traditions. The local language is Hindi, although many people can speak English. Religion plays a vital role in the culture, and several festivals are celebrated throughout the year, including Holi, Diwali, and Dussehra.

Best Time to Visit Dehradun

The best time to visit Dehradun is between March and June, and September to December. The weather during these months is mild and pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities and sightseeing comfortably.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dehradun

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, and dress modestly while visiting religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine of Dehradun, and explore street food and local bars.
  • Keep the environment clean, and do not litter.

Don’t:

  • Do not consume alcohol in public places or inside temples and religious places.
  • Do not disrespect local beliefs and customs or the national flag or symbols of India.
  • Do not indulge in drugs or any illegal activity, and always carry your identification and travel documents with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dehradun

Dehradun is generally a safe city for tourists, and the crime rates are low. However, it is still recommended to take necessary precautions, such as taking care of your belongings and avoiding venturing out alone late at night.

Health in Dehradun

Dehradun offers high-quality healthcare services, and several private and public hospitals and medical centers cater to different medical emergencies. It is advisable to take proper vaccinations and travel insurance before visiting Dehradun.

Getting to Dehradun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dehradun

The Jolly Grant Airport is the nearest airport to Dehradun, located around 22 kilometers from the city center. It is well-connected with other major Indian cities, including Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Dehradun

Dehradun has several bus stations that connect to different parts of the country, including ISBT Dehradun, Kisan Nagar Bus Stand, and Mussoorie Bus Stand.

Other Options

You can also reach Dehradun by trains, and the Dehradun Railway Station is well-connected to other major cities of India.

How to Get Around in Dehradun

Dehradun offers several transportation options for travelers, including:

  • Auto-rickshaws: Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of transportation in Dehradun for short distances within the city.
  • Taxis: Taxis are easily available in Dehradun, and you can rent them either for a day or for an extended period.
  • Buses: Dehradun has a vast network of local and inter-city buses, and you can travel to different parts of the city and nearby destinations through buses.

Where to Go Next from Dehradun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dehradun is surrounded by several nearby destinations and excursions that offer incredible natural beauty and adventure sports such as:

  • Mussoorie: This is a popular hill station located about 34 kilometers away from Dehradun, and it offers scenic views of the Himalayas, and adventure sports such as trekking and paragliding.
  • Rishikesh: This spiritual city is located about 43 kilometers away from Dehradun and is known for its yoga and meditation retreats, and adventure sports such as white water rafting.
  • Haridwar: This city is located about 54 kilometers away from Dehradun and is a popular Hindu pilgrimage site known for its ghats and the evening Aarti ceremony.

Conclusion

Dehradun is an enchanting city that offers an incredible mix of nature, adventure, and cultural experiences. Whether you are into outdoor adventures, sightseeing, or local cuisine, Dehradun has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide for planning your next trip to Dehradun and experience the best of what the city has to offer.
