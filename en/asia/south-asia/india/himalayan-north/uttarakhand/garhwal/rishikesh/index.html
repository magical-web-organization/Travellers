Introduction to Rishikesh

Located in Garhwal, Uttarakhand, Rishikesh is a picturesque city set in the foothills of the Himalayas. Known as the “Yoga Capital of the World,” Rishikesh is a spiritual hub, attracting travelers from all over the world for its ashrams, yoga centers, and meditation retreats. Rishikesh is also home to the holy Ganges river and streets filled with shops selling souvenirs and local products.

List of Places to Visit in Rishikesh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Triveni Ghat - A place where the three holy rivers - Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati merge. People take a dip in the river here and offer prayers to their ancestors.

  • Rajaji National Park - A wildlife reserve that is home to several species of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and leopards.

  • Lakshman Jhula - A prominent suspension bridge over the Ganges river that is named after Lord Lakshman from the epic Ramayana.

  • Parmarth Niketan - One of the most popular ashrams in Rishikesh, known for its spiritual teachings and daily prayers.

  • Swarg Ashram - A peaceful ashram that offers yoga and meditation classes, with a stunning view of the Ganges river.

Natural Attractions of Rishikesh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Neer Garh Waterfall - A stunning waterfall that offers a great place for a picnic and a refreshing dip in freshwaters.

  • Neer Gaddu Waterfall - A magnificent waterfall that is located near the Laxman Jhula Bridge. Great for trekking and picnics.

  • Bungee Jumping - Experience the thrill of jumping off from a height of 83 meters under the supervision of trained professionals.

  • White Water Rafting - Experience the adrenaline rush of rafting on the Ganges River’s rapids, ranging from Grade II to Grade IV.

  • Trekking - Explore the Himalayas through a range of trekking routes that offer stunning views of the mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rishikesh

  • Shivpuri to Rishikesh trek - An easy trek that takes around 3-4 hours, starting from Shivpuri and ending at the famous Laxman Jhula bridge.

  • Neer Garh to Rishikesh trek - A moderate trek that passes by the Neer Garh waterfalls and offers some unforgettable views of the Himalayas.

  • Kunjapuri Temple Trek - A 5 km trek that takes you to the Kunjapuri temple, which is situated on a hilltop and offers a panoramic view of Rishikesh and the Himalayas.

  • Biking on Rishikesh-Devprayag Route - A scenic ride through the mountainous terrain, passing by villages and lush green forests.

Best Things to Do in Rishikesh: Activities and Tours

  • Ganga Aarti - A daily evening ceremony at Triveni Ghat, where priests offer prayers to the Ganges river in the flickering light of oil lamps.

  • Yoga and Meditation Retreats - Experience authentic yoga and meditation teachings with renowned gurus in the world’s yoga capital.

  • Street Food Tour - Savor the local flavors of Rishikesh by taking a food tour through its bustling streets.

  • Camping - Spend a night under the stars with bonfires, stargazing and BBQs in tranquil settings.

  • Ganges River Safari - Take a boat ride on the Ganges river and witness Rishikesh’s scenic beauty and spiritual allure from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rishikesh

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Rishikesh and explore its vibrant streets and temples.
2 Visit the Rajaji National Park and witness the stunning flora and fauna.
3 Take a hiking trail up to Kunjapuri Temple for a breathtaking sunrise view.
4 Engage in adventurous activities such as bungee jumping or white water rafting.
5 Attend a yoga or meditation retreat and discover Rishikesh’s spiritual side.
6 Take a street food tour and indulge in the local flavors of Rishikesh.
7 Visit the Neer Garh or Neer Gaddu waterfalls and spend your final day in the lap of nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Rishikesh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aloo Poori - A delicious Indian breakfast consisting of spicy potato curry and deep-fried puffy bread.

  • Kachori - Fried dough balls stuffed with lentils and spices.

  • Chaat - A popular street food snack made with crispy fried dough, chickpeas, and a tangy and spicy sauce.

  • Lassi - A sweet and creamy drink made with yogurt and fruit, perfect for cooling off on hot summer days.

Local Beverages

  • Bhang Lassi - A potent drink made with cannabis paste and yogurt that is known to offer a slightly intoxicating effect.

  • Masala Chai - A spiced Indian tea that is perfect for warming up on cold days.

  • Fresh Lime Soda - A refreshing drink made with lime juice and carbonated water.

Local Culture and Customs of Rishikesh

Rishikesh, being the “Yoga Capital of the World,” reflects a rich heritage of spirituality and religion, which is evident in its everyday life. Some customs to follow are:

  • Dress Modestly - Given the spiritual and religious nature of Rishikesh, it is essential to dress modestly, especially when visiting temples or ashrams.

  • Remove Shoes - Before entering a temple or ashram, it is customary to remove shoes as a sign of respect.

  • Do not disrespect the river - The Ganges river is considered holy by the locals. It is essential to respect it by not throwing trash or polluting it with any means.

Best Time to Visit Rishikesh

The best time to visit Rishikesh is from September to November and from February to May. During these months, the weather is pleasant, making it suitable to indulge in outdoor activities and explore the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rishikesh

Do:

  • Carry enough water to stay hydrated.

  • Cover yourself appropriately, especially when visiting a religious place.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter the streets and the river.

  • Take pictures without permission in temples or ashrams.

  • Engage in any disrespectful behavior with the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rishikesh

Overall, Rishikesh is a safe city. However, it’s essential to follow some simple safety measures:

  • Avoid walking alone late at night.

  • Stay away from drugs and other illicit substances.

  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Rishikesh

  • Drink only bottled or purified water.

  • Carry mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

  • Carry a basic first aid kit and any necessary medications.

Getting to Rishikesh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rishikesh

The nearest airport to Rishikesh is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, which is around 35 km away from the city. Taxis and buses are readily available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Rishikesh

Rishikesh Bus Station, located on the main road, is well connected to major cities in Uttarakhand and nearby states.

Other Options

  • You can hire a private taxi from Delhi or Dehradun to reach Rishikesh.

  • Trains are frequent from major cities in India to Haridwar Junction, which is around 25 km away from Rishikesh. You can hire a taxi or take a bus from Haridwar to reach Rishikesh.

How to Get Around in Rishikesh

  • Walking is the best option to explore Rishikesh’s streets and temples.

  • Auto-rickshaws or Tuk-Tuks are available for short distances. Negotiate a rate before getting on.

  • Local buses are the cheapest mode of transportation, but they can be crowded.

Where to Go Next from Rishikesh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Haridwar - A spiritual city situated on the banks of the Ganges river that is known for its temples, ghats, and religious ceremonies.

  • Dehradun - A scenic hill station that is known for its natural beauty, including waterfalls, forests, and mountains.

  • Mussoorie - A popular hill station known for its snow-capped peaks, colonial architecture, and adventurous activities.

Conclusion

Rishikesh is the perfect destination for those seeking spiritual or outdoor experiences. The city is filled with natural attractions, temples, and ashrams that can give you a unique insight into Indian culture and tradition. The blend of modern and traditional lifestyles is evident in the vibrant streets, local markets, food, and more. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan an unforgettable trip to Rishikesh.
