Introduction to Devprayag

Devprayag is a small town in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand, Northern Himalayas, India. It is located at a height of 830 meters above sea level and is surrounded by the majestic Himalayan mountains. The town is known for being the confluence of two of India’s most sacred rivers, the Alaknanda and the Bhagirathi, which together form the holy river Ganges.

Devprayag is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural significance, and adventure activities. The town attracts a diverse range of travelers, from spiritual seekers and history enthusiasts to adventure junkies and nature lovers. In this comprehensive guide to Devprayag, we’ll take a closer look at what makes this town so special and explore the best places to visit, eat, stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Devprayag

Devprayag is a town steeped in history and culture, and there are plenty of places to visit and things to see here. We’ve divided the town’s main attractions into two categories- Culturally Important Attractions and Natural Attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Raghunath Ji Temple: Located on a hilltop overlooking the town, this ancient temple dedicated to Lord Rama is one of the most important religious sites in Devprayag.

  • Chandrabadni Temple: This ancient temple located on the top of a hill is dedicated to Goddess Sati, wife of Lord Shiva. It is said to be one of the most important Shakti Peeths in India.

  • Vashisht Gufa: This ancient cave situated on the banks of the river Ganges is believed to be the meditating place of Sage Vashisht, one of the seven sages of Hinduism.

  • Bhagirathi Shila: This rock located near the confluence of the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers is believed to be the place where King Bhagirath prayed to bring the river Ganges to earth.

Natural Attractions of Devprayag: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Confluence of Alaknanda and Bhagirathi: The confluence of these two rivers is the main attraction of Devprayag. The sight of two mighty rivers merging into one is truly breathtaking.

  • Rudraprayag Dam: Located just a few kilometers from Devprayag, this dam offers incredible views of the river and the surrounding mountains. It is a popular spot for picnics and day trips.

  • Chandrashila Peak: This peak, located about 30 km from Devprayag, offers a challenging yet rewarding trek to the top. The views from the peak are simply spectacular.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Devprayag

Devprayag is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of hiking and biking routes that showcase the natural beauty of the region. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Devprayag-Mandakini Trek: This trek takes you from Devprayag to Mandakini Valley, offering views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks and the Bhagirathi River.

  • Rishi Kund Trek: This trek takes you to the Rishi Kund Lake, believed to be the origin of the river Rishiganga.

  • Bike Ride to Panch Kedar: This bike ride takes you through some of the most scenic routes in the region, passing through the five temples that make up the Panch Kedar circuit.

Best Things to Do in Devprayag: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Devprayag, whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation. Some of the best activities include:

  • River Rafting: The rapids of the Alaknanda river provide the perfect setting for a thrilling river rafting experience.

  • Camping: Camping is a popular activity in Devprayag, with several campgrounds offering scenic views and outdoor activities.

  • Yoga and Meditation: With its serene surroundings and spiritual history, Devprayag is a great place to practice yoga and meditation.

  • Temple Hopping: Devprayag is home to several ancient and revered temples, making it a popular destination for temple-hopping tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Devprayag

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Devprayag:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Devprayag. Visit Raghunath Ji Temple and the confluence of Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers.

  • Day 2: Visit the Chandrabadni Temple and enjoy a scenic trek to the top of the hill.

  • Day 3: Visit Vashisht Gufa and Bhagirathi Shila. Take time to explore Devprayag town.

  • Day 4: Go on a rafting expedition on the Alaknanda river.

  • Day 5: Take a biking trip to the Panch Kedar circuit.

  • Day 6: Trek to the Chandrashila peak for stunning views of the Himalayas.

  • Day 7: Departure from Devprayag.

Food and Drink Scene of Devprayag

Devprayag has a rich culinary scene, with plenty of delicious local dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Singhori: A popular sweet dish made of roasted wheat flour, jaggery and coconut, wrapped in a leaf known as Malu.

  • Gulgula: A sweet snack made from wheat flour, jaggery and spices.

  • Kafuli: A thick, flavorful dish made with spinach, fenugreek leaves, and mashed lentils.

Local Beverages

  • Buraansh Juice: A refreshing drink made from the flowers of the Buraansh tree, found only in the Himalayan region.

  • Kimbhu: A delicious drink made from fermented millet and mixed with herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Devprayag: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Devprayag has several accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Riverside Resort: A luxury resort located near the confluence of the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers, offering stunning views and excellent facilities.

  • Panchvati Inn: A comfortable guesthouse located in the heart of Devprayag town, offering basic amenities and affordable prices.

  • Eco Lodge: An eco-friendly accommodation option located at Rudraprayag, offering a unique experience close to nature.

What to Buy in Devprayag: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Devprayag is known for its local handicrafts, art and textiles. Here are some of the best things to buy as souvenirs:

  • Sarees and Shawls: The town is famous for its handwoven sarees and shawls, made from fine wool, pashmina, and silk.

  • Wooden Handicrafts: The town is home to skilled woodworkers who create intricate wooden handicrafts such as toys, furniture, and decor items.

  • Turquoise Jewelry: Turquoise jewelry is a popular item sold by local artisans in Devprayag.

Local Culture and Customs of Devprayag

Devprayag has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and show respect for local customs and traditions. The town is known for its traditional festivals, such as the annual Chandrabadni Mela, which attracts devotees from all over India.

Best Time to Visit Devprayag

The best time to visit Devprayag is from March to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant with warm days and cool nights, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The monsoon season (July-August) should be avoided as heavy rainfall can disrupt travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Devprayag

Do:

  • Dress modestly, covering your shoulders and knees when visiting religious sites.

  • Take off your footwear before entering a temple or a holy place.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol or non-vegetarian food when visiting religious sites.

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Devprayag

Devprayag is generally a safe place for travelers. However, it is advisable to take precautions such as not venturing out alone at night and avoiding isolated areas.

Health in Devprayag

Travelers to Devprayag should be prepared for the high altitude and the physical exertion required for outdoor activities such as trekking and rafting. It is advisable to carry necessary medication and consult a doctor before undertaking any strenuous activities.

Getting to Devprayag: Transportation Options and Logistics

Devprayag is well connected by roads and can be easily reached from cities like Rishikesh, Haridwar or Delhi. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Devprayag

The nearest airport to Devprayag is Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun, which is about 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Devprayag

Devprayag is well connected by buses from towns like Rishikesh, Haridwar among others.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire from major cities like Delhi, Rishikesh, and Haridwar.

How to Get Around in Devprayag

Devprayag is a small town, and most places of interest are within walking distance from each other. However, taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for hire to explore the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Devprayag: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Devprayag is a great base to explore nearby areas like:

  • Rishikesh: A popular pilgrimage site and adventure destination, located about 77 km from Devprayag.

  • Badrinath: A holy town located about 170 km from Devprayag, famous for the Badrinath Temple.

  • Auli: A scenic hill station located about 100 km from Devprayag, known for its skiing and nature trails.

Conclusion

Devprayag is a hidden gem of Uttarakhand, offering a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural significance, and adventure activities. This comprehensive guide should help you plan your trip to Devprayag and make the most of your time in this beautiful Himalayan town.
