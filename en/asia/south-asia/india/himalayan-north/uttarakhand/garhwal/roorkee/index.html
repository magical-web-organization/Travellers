Introduction to Roorkee

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand, Roorkee is a small city located at the convergence of the Ganges canal and the Solani River. Historically known for its engineering college, Roorkee has now grown to become a center of education, trade, and tourism. The city’s rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and serene environment make it a perfect destination for a relaxing and peaceful vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Roorkee

Whether you’re here for its rich history or its natural beauty, Roorkee has something for everyone. Here is a list of the must-visit places in and around Roorkee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Indian Institute of Technology, Roorkee

Founded in 1847 as the Thomason College of Engineering, the Indian Institute of Technology in Roorkee is one of the oldest technical institutions in Asia. The institute has a rich and illustrious history and is known for producing some of India’s best engineers and architects.

Roorkee Cantonment

Built during the British era, Roorkee Cantonment is a military establishment with a rich history. The cantonment has several old buildings and structures that reflect the colonial architecture of the time. Some of the landmarks at the cantonment include the Victoria Cross Memorial, the War Memorial, and the Gorkha War Memorial.

Roorkee Heritage Walk

Experience the rich cultural heritage of Roorkee through the guided heritage walk. The walk covers some of the most iconic landmarks of Roorkee, such as the Clock Tower, the Solani Aqueduct, and the Central Hall of IIT Roorkee.

Natural Attractions of Roorkee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Solani Aqueduct

One of the most recognizable landmarks of Roorkee, Solani Aqueduct is a marvel of civil engineering built in 1846. The aqueduct carries the waters of Solani River over the Ganges Canal and offers a breathtaking view of the city.

Piran Kaliyar Sharif

Located on the outskirts of Roorkee, Piran Kaliyar Sharif is a 12th-century Sufi shrine. The shrine is built around a natural spring and is believed to have healing properties. The serene and tranquil environment of Piran Kaliyar Sharif attracts visitors from all over the world.

Roorkee Reserve Forest

Nature lovers should head to the Roorkee Reserve Forest to see the rich flora and fauna of Uttarakhand. The forest is home to several species of animals, such as leopards, antelopes, and monkeys. Visitors can also enjoy bird watching and trekking on the forest trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roorkee

Roorkee’s scenic location makes it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Solani Aqueduct to Piran Kaliyar Sharif
  • Roorkee Reserve Forest Trails
  • Roorkee to Dehradun via Rajaji National Park

Best Things to Do in Roorkee: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, there are several other things to do in Roorkee, such as:

  • Boat ride on the Ganges Canal
  • Visit to the local markets to shop for handicrafts and souvenirs
  • Try local cuisine at the popular eateries like Satyam Sweets and Rishabh Restaurant
  • Attend the annual Roorkee Mela which showcases the local culture and traditions

Week-Long Itinerary for Roorkee

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring Roorkee and its surroundings:

Day 1: Arrive in Roorkee and visit IIT Roorkee Day 2: Go on the Roorkee Heritage Walk and visit Solani Aqueduct Day 3: Explore the Roorkee Reserve Forest Day 4: Visit the Piran Kaliyar Sharif Shrine Day 5: Take a boat ride on the Ganges Canal and try local cuisine Day 6: Visit the local markets for shopping Day 7: Attend the Roorkee Mela

Food and Drink Scene of Roorkee

Roorkee’s cuisine is a mix of North Indian and Garhwali flavors. Some of the must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kumaoni Raita
  • Garhwali Dal
  • Jhangore Ki Kheer
  • Aloo ke Gutke
  • Chainsoo

Local Beverages

The local drink of Roorkee is a refreshing buttermilk drink called Chaas. The city is also known for its delicious fruit juices made from locally grown fruits like mangoes and lychees.

Local Culture and Customs of Roorkee

Roorkee has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its traditional dance forms like Langvir Nritya and Barada Nati. The locals are warm and hospitable and take pride in their customs and traditions. Visitors are advised to respect the local culture and dress modestly when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Roorkee

The best time to visit Roorkee is between October and April when the weather is pleasant and mild. Summers (May-June) can be hot and humid, while monsoons (July-September) bring heavy rain and landslides.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roorkee

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places
  • Explore the local markets and try local cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Disturb the peace and tranquility of the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Roorkee

Roorkee is a safe city for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions and not venture out alone at night. Women traveling solo should be extra cautious and avoid isolated areas.

Health in Roorkee

Roorkee has decent medical facilities, but visitors are advised to carry their basic medication and consult a doctor before traveling. Drinking bottled or boiled water is recommended to avoid any waterborne diseases.

Getting to Roorkee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roorkee

The nearest airport to Roorkee is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun (50 km), which is well connected to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Roorkee

Roorkee has a well-connected bus network with regular services to major cities in Uttarakhand and neighboring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for hired cars and taxis from Dehradun or Haridwar to reach Roorkee.

How to Get Around in Roorkee

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are the most convenient and affordable mode of transport within the city. Hiring a bike or scooter is also an option for exploring nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Roorkee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roorkee is a gateway to several tourist destinations in Uttarakhand, such as:

  • Haridwar (33 km)
  • Dehradun (57 km)
  • Mussoorie (91 km)
  • Rishikesh (62 km)

Conclusion

Roorkee is a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. The city’s serene environment, rich cultural heritage, and scenic beauty make it a perfect destination for a relaxing and peaceful vacation. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore all that Roorkee has to offer and make the most of your time in this charming city.
