Introduction to Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary

The Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is a lush green forest situated in Canacona, South Goa. It covers an area of 85 square kilometers and is located on the border of Goa and Karnataka. The sanctuary is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including rare species like the flying squirrel, slender loris, and Indian pangolin. The dense forest, waterfalls, and exotic animals make it a great location for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Local Culture and Customs of Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary

The majority of the people living around the Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary are Hindus. The locals celebrate several festivals with great fervor and enthusiasm. The most popular festival is Goa Carnival, which takes place in February every year. The locals dress up in colorful costumes and participate in street parades.

Best Time to Visit Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is from December to February as the weather is pleasant at this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Carry insect repellent, as there are plenty of mosquitoes in the sanctuary.
  • Dress appropriately in comfortable and light-colored clothes.
  • Seek permission before clicking photographs of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter the sanctuary.
  • Disturb the animals in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary
