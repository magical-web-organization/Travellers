Introduction to Benaulim

Benaulim is a serene and picturesque village located in the Salcete taluka of South Goa. It is situated on the western coast of India and is known for its sun-kissed beaches, tranquil ambiance, and Portuguese heritage. Nestled between the Arabian Sea and the Sal River, Benaulim is a perfect getaway for travelers seeking peace, relaxation, and some fun in the sun. With its ancient churches, cozy bars and restaurants, and a wide range of activities, Benaulim is an ideal destination for people of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Benaulim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Church of St. John the Baptist: The Church of St. John the Baptist is one of the most famous landmarks in Benaulim. It was built in 1576 and boasts remarkable architecture and intricate artwork. The highlight of this church is its annual feast that takes place on 24th June every year.

  2. Sernabatim Beach: Sernabatim Beach is just a few kilometers away from Benaulim and is known for its clear waters, white sands, and stunning sunsets. It is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

  3. Goa Chitra Museum: Goa Chitra Museum is located in the village of Benaulim and is a treasure trove of artifacts that showcase the rich cultural heritage of Goa. From vintage farming tools to antique coins, the museum has something for everyone.

Natural Attractions of Benaulim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Benaulim Beach: Benaulim Beach is undoubtedly the most alluring attraction of the village. With its glittering sand, towering palm trees, and turquoise waters, it offers a perfect setting for a refreshing dip or a calming stroll.

  2. Cabo de Rama: Cabo de Rama is a historical fort located at the southern end of Goa. It is a perfect spot for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike. The fort offers a stunning view of the Arabian Sea, and its ancient architecture never fails to captivate visitors.

  3. Colva Beach: Colva Beach is one of the longest beaches in Goa and is just a few kilometers away from Benaulim. It has a vibrant nightlife with a plethora of bars and restaurants and is a must-visit spot for party enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benaulim

  1. Betul Hill: Betul Hill is a perfect destination for hiking and trekking enthusiasts. It offers a mesmerizing view of the Sal River and is surrounded by dense forests and exotic flora.

  2. Sonsodo Waterfalls: Sonsodo Waterfalls is a hidden gem located in the outskirts of Benaulim. It is a perfect spot for adventure seekers who want to enjoy a refreshing swim under a cascading waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Benaulim: Activities and Tours

  1. Water Sports: Benaulim Beach is an ideal destination for water sports enthusiasts. Visitors can indulge in activities like parasailing, banana boat rides, jet skiing, and many more.

  2. Dolphin Watching: Visitors can take a boat ride off the shore of Benaulim to witness the majestic dolphins up close in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Benaulim

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Prawn Curry: Prawn Curry is a popular seafood delicacy in Goa, and Benaulim is no exception. The dish is made with fresh prawns cooked in a spicy and tangy curry that is sure to tantalize anyone’s taste buds.

  2. Fish Recheado: Fish Recheado is another must-try dish that is a favorite among the locals in Benaulim. It is made with local fish marinated in a spicy mixture of red chilies, vinegar, garlic, and ginger.

Local Beverages

  1. Feni: Feni is a local spirit made from cashews or coconuts and is ubiquitous in Goa. It is a must-try beverage that has a unique flavor and aroma.

  2. Kokum Juice: Kokum Juice is a refreshing drink made from dried kokum fruit, sugar, and water. It is a perfect thirst quencher, especially during the scorching summer heat.

Where to Stay in Benaulim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Fern Beira Mar Resort: The Fern Beira Mar Resort is a luxury hotel located on the beautiful Benaulim Beach. It offers cozy and elegant accommodations, a swimming pool, spa, and numerous other amenities to ensure a comfortable stay.

  2. Maria Paulo Guest House: Maria Paulo Guest House is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of the village. It offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price, making it an ideal choice for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Benaulim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Cashew Nuts: Goa is known for its delicious cashew nuts, and Benaulim is no exception. Visitors can buy a variety of cashew nut products like cashew feni, roasted cashews, and cashew chocolate.

  2. Handicrafts: Benaulim has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can buy a variety of handicrafts like pottery, wooden sculptures, and handwoven textiles as souvenirs to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Benaulim

The locals of Benaulim are warm and welcoming, and the culture is influenced heavily by their Portuguese heritage. Visitors are advised to respect the local culture and customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites and public places. Smoking and drinking in public places are strictly prohibited, and visitors must abide by the local laws and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Benaulim

The best time to visit Benaulim is from November to February when the climate is mild, and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities. The monsoon season from June to September should be avoided as the heavy rains can disrupt travel plans and make outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Benaulim

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Dress modestly while visiting religious sites and public places.

  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke or drink in public places.

  2. Engage in any illegal activities.

  3. Disrespect the locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Benaulim

Benaulim is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors are advised not to walk alone at night in isolated areas and take adequate precautions while indulging in water sports activities.

Health in Benaulim

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions to protect themselves from mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and chikungunya. It is also recommended to carry sunscreen and stay well hydrated while engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Benaulim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Benaulim

The nearest airport to Benaulim is Dabolim International Airport, which is located approximately 25 km away.

Bus Stations Near Benaulim

Margao is the nearest bus station to Benaulim and is located just 7 km away.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis or rent a car to reach Benaulim from nearby cities like Panaji or Vasco da Gama.

How to Get Around in Benaulim

Visitors can rent a bike or a scooter to explore the village at their own pace. Taxis and buses are also readily available and are a convenient option for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Benaulim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Margao: Margao is the largest city in South Goa and is a hub of cultural and historical attractions. Visitors can explore the ancient churches, museums, and bustling markets of this vibrant city.

  2. Palolem Beach: Palolem Beach is located just 40 km away from Benaulim and is known for its crescent-shaped beach and scenic views. It is also an ideal destination for water sports activities.

Conclusion

Benaulim is a hidden gem in South Goa that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature. With its tranquil beaches, historical landmarks, and vibrant culinary scene, it is a perfect destination for travelers seeking serenity and adventure. Plan your trip to Benaulim and experience the magic of this enchanting village.
