Candolim is a beautiful coastal town located in North Goa that boasts of stunning beaches, historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. It is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a mix of relaxation, adventure, and culture all in one place. In this comprehensive tour guide to Candolim, we will walk you through the best things to see, do, and experience in the town, including tips on where to stay, what to buy, and how to get around.

Introduction to Candolim

Candolim is situated in the Bardez taluka of North Goa and is located about 15 km from Panaji, the capital city of Goa. The town is easily accessible by road and has some of the best beaches on the west coast of India. Candolim’s beach is a top attraction and perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. The town’s tropical climate, historical landmarks, lush greenery, and rich culture make it a popular tourist destination in North Goa.

List of Places to Visit in Candolim

There are several places to visit in Candolim. Here are some top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aguada Fort: A 17th-century Portuguese fort located on Sinquerim Beach, built to protect against Dutch and Maratha invasions.
  • Reis Magos Fort: A historical fort located on the northern bank of the Mandovi River in Verem.
  • Sinquerim Beach: One of the best beaches in Candolim and home to the grand Taj Fort Aguada Resort and Spa.
  • Dr. Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary: A mangrove sanctuary located along the Mandovi river.

Natural Attractions of Candolim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dolphin and Sunset Boat Tours: Enjoy a unique experience watching dolphins swim and the most beautiful sunset on a luxurious boat.
  • Candolim Beach: A perfect destination for water sports like parasailing, jet skis, and banana boat rides.
  • Candolim-Calangute-Baga Nature Trail: A beautiful nature trail that connects Candolim, Calangute and Baga beach.
  • Goan Spice Plantations: A refreshing day excursion to the spice plantations to see verdant spice gardens, taste exotic fruits, and experience traditional folk dances.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Candolim

For those who are more adventurous, Candolim offers amazing hiking and biking routes throughout lush green hills, forests and beaches.

  • Fort Aguada to Sinquerim Beach: A short hike with stunning views of the sea.
  • Candolim to Calangute Beach: A scenic hike on the beach with the perfect ambiance of the waves.
  • Candolim to Nerul River: A beautiful biking trail along the river bank, which is considered the best place for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Candolim: Activities and Tours

Candolim offers several activities and tours that promise to keep you entertained and engaged throughout your stay.

  • Water sports: A popular attraction in Candolim is water sports such as Jet Ski, Banana Ride, Parasailing, and Scuba Diving.
  • Dolphin sighting and sunset cruises: Cruises to enjoy dolphin sightings and a beautiful sunset along the Mandovi River.
  • Spice plantation tours: A refreshing day away from the beach to see the verdant spice gardens and experience traditional folk dances.
  • Scuba Diving: A fascinating experience to explore the underwater marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Candolim

Here is a suggested itinerary for spending a week in Candolim:

1 Relax on Candolim Beach
2 Take a trip to Aguada Fort and Sinquerim Beach
3 Try water sports at the Candolim Beach

7 Go for a day trip to nearby destinations like Old Goa or a cruise on the Mandovi River

Food and Drink Scene of Candolim

Candolim is a foodie paradise, offering a blend of Indian and Portuguese cuisine. The town is famous for its seafood – from Goan fish curry to prawn balchao, kingfish, and more. Some top restaurants in Candolim to try include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Prawn Balchao: A spicy Goan dish made with dried shrimp and chili that is perfect with rice or bread.
  • Goan Fish Curry: A sweet and tangy coconut-based curry made with fresh fish.
  • Pork Vindaloo: A fiery pork curry with a blend of Goan spices and vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Feni: An alcoholic beverage made from fermented cashew fruit that is popular in Goa.
  • Kingfisher beer: A widely available beer brand in Goa.

Where to Stay in Candolim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Candolim is a tourist hub, and there are plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Whether you are looking for a luxurious stay at a resort, a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, Candolim has you covered.

Some top hotels in Candolim include:

  • Taj Fort Aguada Resort and Spa
  • Novotel Goa Resort & Spa
  • Lemon Tree Amarante Beach Resort

If you’re on a budget, consider these hostels and guesthouses:

  • Pinto’s Hostel
  • The Highland Heritage

What to Buy in Candolim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Candolim is a shopper’s paradise, and there are several markets and shops in the town selling souvenirs and local products. The best items to buy include:

  • Cashews: Goa is famous for its cashews, roasted and salted or in flavors such as chili, vinegar, and garlic.
  • Handicrafts: Pick up some handicrafts made by the local artisans, including brassware, terracotta, and bamboo products.
  • Clothing: Vibrant Goa-style clothing and jewelry are also popular among tourists

Local Culture and Customs of Candolim

Candolim has a rich culture deeply intertwined with Portuguese influences. Religion plays an important part in the local life of Candolim, and you will notice several beautiful churches in the town. People in Candolim are friendly, and you are likely to bump into friendly locals who will make your visit memorable.

Best Time to Visit Candolim

The best time to visit Candolim is between November and March, when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature is between 20°C and 33°C. During monsoon, Candolim experiences heavy rainfall and is not recommended to plan traveling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Candolim

Do:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting temples and other religious sites
  • Respect local people’s customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Bargain for souvenirs and other items in local markets

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without permission
  • Litter the beaches or other public areas
  • Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Candolim

Candolim is generally a safe place for tourists, and the crime rate is low. However, it is recommended to take precautionary measures such as avoiding secluded areas at night, keeping valuables safely, and taking a licensed taxi or scooter for transportation.

Health in Candolim

There is no need to worry about health issues while traveling to Candolim, but it’s recommended to be cautious and use mosquito repellents as dengue and malaria cases are seen sometimes in this region, especially during monsoon season.

Getting to Candolim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Candolim

  • Dabolim Airport: Located 45 km south of Candolim, it is the nearest airport and provides excellent connectivity to major Indian and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Candolim

The nearest bus stations are Calangute and Mapusa bus stations, from where you can take a taxi, a scooter or a bus service to Candolim.

Other Options

Candolim is well connected by trains that operate from Thivim and Karmali railway stations. There are regular tourist buses from Mumbai and Pune to Goa, and taxis are available almost everywhere in Goa.

How to Get Around in Candolim

The best way to get around Candolim is by scooter or taxi. Scooters are readily available for rent with an average cost of INR 300 - 500 per day. Taxis and auto-rickshaws can be hired for short or long distances. Walking is the best way to explore the beaches and local attractions in and around Candolim.

Where to Go Next from Candolim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Candolim is located in North Goa, and there are several other tourist destinations to explore nearby, including:

  • Panaji: The capital city of Goa, located 15 km from Candolim.
  • Old Goa: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located 25 km from Candolim.
  • Mapusa: A small town located 10 km north of Candolim that is famous for its Friday market and local street food.

Conclusion

Candolim is an ideal destination for tourists looking for a mix of relaxation, adventure, and culture. With stunning beaches, natural beauty, outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, and a vibrant culture, Candolim truly has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you are all set to explore and enjoy your trip to Candolim.
