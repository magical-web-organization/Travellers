Introduction to Anjuna

Located in Bardez, North Goa, Anjuna is a coastal village that is popular for its vibrant culture, scenic surroundings, and hippie vibe. Anjuna has been a go-to destination for backpackers and travelers looking for something off the beaten path. Surrounded by verdant hills and sparkling waters, this coastal village has a unique blend of traditional Indian culture and modern Western influences. Anjuna is also known for its legendary flea markets, bustling nightlife, and beautiful beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Anjuna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Michael’s Church: Built-in 1605, this ancient church holds great cultural and religious significance. The beautiful architecture and serene ambiance make it a must-visit place in Anjuna.
  • Mascarenhas Mansion: Another great attraction for history buffs, this 250-year-old mansion is a beautiful example of the Goan-Portuguese architecture.
  • The Saturday Night Market: A vibrant street market filled with food stalls, music, and vendors selling handmade crafts, jewelry, clothing, and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Anjuna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anjuna Beach: One of the most popular beaches in Goa, Anjuna beach is renowned for its vibrant nightlife, food shacks, and water sports.
  • Chapora Fort: Located atop a hill, this fort offers breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and surrounding hills. It is also famous for its connection to the famous Bollywood movie “Dil Chahta Hai”.
  • Vagator Beach: A scenic and tranquil beach that attracts visitors looking for a quiet escape. The beach is surrounded by palm trees, and the water is perfect for swimming and surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anjuna

  • Anjuna to Chapora Fort: A beautiful trek that takes you through the lush green hills of Anjuna and offers panoramic views of the sea.
  • Anjuna to Vagator Beach: A scenic cycle ride that takes you through the countryside of Anjuna en route to the serene Vagator beach.

Best Things to Do in Anjuna: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Enjoy a variety of water sports such as jet skiing, parasailing, and banana boat rides at Anjuna Beach.
  • Yoga and Meditation: Attend yoga and meditation classes at one of the many yoga centers in Anjuna.
  • Nightlife: Experience the vibrant nightlife of Anjuna by visiting popular bars and clubs like Shiva Valley and Curlies.

Food and Drink Scene of Anjuna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Goa is known for its delicious seafood, so be sure to try some of the local dishes like fish curry and prawn fry.
  • Bebinca: A traditional Goan dessert made with coconut milk, sugar, and ghee.
  • Feni: A popular drink made from fermented cashew fruit or coconut sap.

Local Beverages

  • King’s Beer: A popular local beer known for its crisp taste and refreshing flavor.
  • Chaas: A traditional Indian drink made with yogurt and spices.

Where to Stay in Anjuna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Anjuna Beach Resort: A beautiful beachfront resort that offers modern amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Red Door Hostel: Perfect for budget travelers, this hostel offers comfortable dorm rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Casa Anjuna: A boutique guesthouse that offers a unique blend of traditional Indian architecture and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Anjuna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: The Saturday Night Market is the perfect place to shop for unique handmade crafts and jewelry.
  • Anjuna Flea Market: Shop for clothing, accessories, and souvenirs at one of the many stalls at the famous Anjuna Flea Market.
  • Spices: Goa is known for its delicious spices, so be sure to pick up some popular blends like vindaloo and xacuti.

Local Culture and Customs of Anjuna

Anjuna has a unique blend of Indian and Western culture. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is common to see hippies and travelers mingling with the locals. Respect the local customs and traditions and dress modestly when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Anjuna

The best time to visit Anjuna is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities. The monsoons, which run from June through September, can be unpredictable and may cause disruptions in travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anjuna

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and attend cultural events and festivals.
  • Engage with the locals and immerse yourself in the unique culture and traditions of Anjuna.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the local customs and traditions.
  • Drink and drive, and engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anjuna

Anjuna is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, be cautious when visiting crowded places and markets and keep your belongings safe from pickpockets.

Health in Anjuna

It is advisable to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent any health issues. It is also important to carry necessary medication and have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Anjuna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anjuna

The nearest airport to Anjuna is Goa International Airport, also known as Dabolim Airport, which is located around 43 km away.

Bus Stations Near Anjuna

The Kadamba Bus Stand, located in Panaji, is the closest bus station to Anjuna. It is well-connected to major cities in Goa and surrounding states.

Other Options

Taxis and hired cars are also available for those who prefer a more comfortable and private mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Anjuna

Auto-rickshaws, motorcycles, and bicycles are the most popular modes of transportation in Anjuna. Hiring a taxi or car is also an option, although it can be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Anjuna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Arambol: Another beach town in North Goa, known for its laid-back vibe and scenic surroundings.
  • Panaji: The capital city of Goa, known for its beautiful architecture, bustling markets, and lively nightlife.
  • Dudhsagar Falls: A stunning waterfall located around 90 km away from Anjuna.

Conclusion

Anjuna is a perfect blend of cultural heritage and scenic beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for something different. The unique blend of Indian and Western culture, coupled with the beautiful beaches, lush hills, and vibrant nightlife, makes Anjuna a must-visit destination in India. With this comprehensive guide, you can now plan your trip to Anjuna with ease and enjoy all that this beautiful village has to offer.
