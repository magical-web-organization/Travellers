Introduction to Jaisalmer

Perched on top of a rocky hill amidst the Thar Desert, Jaisalmer stands as a testament to Rajasthan’s rich culture and heritage. Also known as the “Golden City,” this picturesque destination showcases a plethora of sandstone structures that glow with a golden hue in the sunrise and sunset. The city’s imposing forts, ornate havelis, and vibrant bazaars all add to the charm and allure of this magnificent destination.

List of Places to Visit in Jaisalmer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jaisalmer Fort: Also known as the “Sonar Qila” or “Golden Fort,” Jaisalmer Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to 1156 AD. The fort houses several palaces, temples, and havelis that showcase exquisite Rajasthani architecture.

  • Nathmal Ji Ki Haveli: This remarkable haveli was built by two brothers, Hathi and Lulu, who were renowned for their intricate stone carvings. The haveli features impressive miniature paintings and delicate carvings.

  • Salim Singh Ki Haveli: Named after its former owner, this haveli showcases an ethereal blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture, featuring a beautifully-carved peacock-shaped rooftop.

  • Patwon Ki Haveli: One of the largest havelis in Jaisalmer, this building was constructed by a wealthy trader named Guman Chand Patwa. The haveli boasts numerous rooms and ornate balconies, which were built for the trader’s five sons.

  • Thar Heritage Museum: This museum houses an impressive collection of artefacts and exhibits that offer a glimpse into Rajasthan’s rich cultural heritage. The museum features colourful textiles, weapons, family portraits, and much more.

Natural Attractions of Jaisalmer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sam Sand Dunes: Located 42 km from Jaisalmer, Sam Sand Dunes is a vast expanse of spectacular sand dunes that stretch along the Thar Desert. This spot is perfect for camel riding, dune bashing, and watching the sunset.

  • Bada Bagh: A garden complex located 6 km from Jaisalmer, Bada Bagh features a series of cenotaphs or chhatris, which are dedicated to the former Maharajas of Jaisalmer.

  • Gadisar Lake: Situated in the heart of Jaisalmer, Gadisar Lake is a peaceful oasis that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can enjoy boating or take a leisurely stroll along the lakeside promenade.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jaisalmer

  • Jaisalmer Desert Trail: This 3-day trek takes visitors through the scenic Thar Desert, covering a distance of approximately 50 km. The trail features several historical sites and desert villages, offering a unique glimpse into the region’s rugged terrain and cultural heritage.

  • Camel Safari: A quintessential experience in Jaisalmer, a camel safari lets visitors experience the beauty of the Thar Desert from a different perspective.

Best Things to Do in Jaisalmer: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Rajasthani Folk Dance: Visitors can enjoy a mesmerizing performance of Rajasthan’s traditional Kalbelia dance, which is known for its unique and intricate movements.

  • Jeep Safari: An adrenaline-pumping adventure through the desert, a jeep safari offers a thrilling ride over rugged terrain and sand dunes.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: A unique way to experience Jaisalmer’s natural beauty, a hot air balloon ride offers a bird’s eye view of the desert landscape and skyline.

Food and Drink Scene of Jaisalmer

Must-Try Dishes

  • Daal Baati Churma: A staple dish in Rajasthani cuisine, Daal Baati Churma consists of baked wheat balls known as “Baatis,” served with a spicy lentil soup or “Daal,” and a rich dessert made from ground wheat and jaggery known as “Churma.”

  • Laal Maas: A fiery meat dish made with mutton, Laal Maas is a must-try for meat lovers. The dish is cooked with red chilli paste and other local spices, giving it a unique and distinct flavour.

  • Ker Sangri: A popular side dish in Rajasthan, Ker Sangri is made from beans and berries that are found only in the Thar Desert. The dish is typically served with rice or roti and adds a unique flavour to any meal.

Local Beverages

  • Kesar Lassi: A refreshing drink made from yoghurt and saffron, Kesar Lassi is a favourite among locals and tourists alike.

  • Chai: A staple beverage in India, Chai or tea is readily available in Jaisalmer’s tea shops and can be enjoyed hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Jaisalmer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jaisalmer offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

  • Budget: Hostels like the Zostel Jaisalmer offer affordable and comfortable accommodation, with both private and shared rooms available.

  • Mid-range: The Suryagarh Jaisalmer and the Killa Bhawan are both popular mid-range options, offering comfortable and stylish rooms with all essential amenities.

  • Luxury: The Jaisalmer Marriott Resort & Spa and the Suryagarh Jaisalmer Palace are two luxurious properties that promise a truly indulgent stay.

What to Buy in Jaisalmer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antique Items: Jaisalmer is known for its antique shops, offering a wide range of items including vintage cameras, coins, and jewellery.

  • Rajasthani Textiles: Visitors can shop for colourful and intricate textiles, including bandhani (tie-dyed), block-printed, and embroidered fabrics.

  • Camel Leather Items: Jaisalmer is famous for its camel leather items, including wallets, bags, belts, and jackets.

Local Culture and Customs of Jaisalmer

  • Greeting: In Jaisalmer, it is customary to greet people with “Namaste” or “Ram Ram.”

  • Attire: Visitors should be mindful of the region’s cultural sensibilities and avoid wearing revealing or provocative clothing in public places.

  • Cultural Festivals: Jaisalmer hosts several cultural festivals throughout the year, including the famous Desert Festival in February, showcasing the region’s rich cultural heritage through dance, music, and food.

Best Time to Visit Jaisalmer

The best time to visit Jaisalmer is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The temperature during this time ranges from 5°C to 25°C, making it perfect for exploring the city’s cultural and natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jaisalmer

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.

  • Bargain while shopping at the bazaar to get the best prices.

Don’t:

  • Damage any historical sites or monuments.

  • Engage in any illegal activities, especially related to narcotics and illegal gambling.

  • Disrespect any religious or cultural beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer is considered to be a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise general caution and avoid wandering around alone at night.

Health in Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer is located in a desert region, and visitors should take necessary precautions to protect themselves from heat exhaustion and dehydration. Additionally, visitors should be mindful of the region’s hygiene standards and avoid consuming street food or unfiltered water.

Getting to Jaisalmer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jaisalmer

The Jaisalmer Airport is served by regular flights from major Indian cities such as Delhi and Mumbai.

Bus Stations Near Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer is well-connected by road with several major cities in Rajasthan like Jodhpur, Bikaner, and Jaipur.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to take a train to Jaisalmer, with several daily express trains from major cities such as Delhi and Jaipur.

How to Get Around in Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer is a small and compact city, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by hiring a bicycle. Visitors can also take auto-rickshaws or taxis for short journeys or hire a car with a driver to explore nearby attractions outside the city.

Where to Go Next from Jaisalmer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jodhpur: Known as the “Blue City,” Jodhpur is Rajasthan’s second-largest city, famous for its imposing forts, ornate palaces, and colourful bazaars.

  • Udaipur: Known as the “Venice of the East,” Udaipur is considered to be one of the most romantic cities in India, featuring beautiful lakes, ornate palaces and gardens, and vibrant bazaars.

  • Pushkar: Located on the edge of the Thar Desert, Pushkar is a sacred town known for its serene lake, ancient temples, and vibrant annual Camel Fair.

Conclusion

An enchanting blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Jaisalmer promises an unforgettable travel experience. From exploring the majestic forts and ornate havelis to enjoying a camel safari or hot air balloon ride, this golden city of India has something for everyone. So, pack your bags and start planning your trip to Jaisalmer today!
