Have you been itching to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and relax in nature’s lap? Mount Abu in Rajasthan, India’s only hill station in the desert state of Rajasthan, is the perfect getaway. From its breathtaking natural beauty to culturally significant architecture, there’s something for everyone in Mount Abu. Here’s a comprehensive tour guide to help you get the most out of your visit.

Introduction to Mount Abu

Mount Abu is a beautiful hill station nestled in the Aravalli Range, the oldest mountain range in India. It’s often referred to as the “Oasis in the Desert” because of its lush greenery comprising of evergreen forests, orchards, and lakes. Apart from its scenic beauty, Mount Abu is a center of spirituality, culture, and history. It has been home to various communities over the centuries, ranging from sages to royal families, each of whom has left their mark on the city.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Abu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dilwara Jain Temples: Built between the 11th and 13th centuries, the Dilwara Jain Temples are a masterpiece of marble architecture. The intricate carvings and sculptures in the temples are awe-inspiring.
  2. Brahma Kumari World Spiritual University: The Brahma Kumari World Spiritual University is a renowned meditation center attracting thousands of people every year.
  3. Achalgarh Fort: The Achalgarh Fort dates back to the 14th century and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding areas. It is an architectural marvel and a must-visit among history buffs.
  4. Nakki Lake: Nakki Lake is a serene water body surrounded by lush green hills. It’s a popular picnic spot venerated by the locals and tourists alike.
  5. Guru Shikhar: Guru Shikhar is the highest peak in Mount Abu, offering a panoramic view of the surrounding Aravalli range.

Natural Attractions of Mount Abu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Trevor’s Tank: A tranquil spot surrounded by rocky hills, Trevor’s Tank is a breeding ground for several species of birds, including geese and cranes, and is also an excellent spot for rock climbing and rappelling.
  2. Toad Rock: The Toad Rock formation is a must-see landmark in Mount Abu. It is an enormous rock formation that resembles a toad, and tourists often flock to take pictures with it.
  3. Sunset Point: As the name suggests, Sunset Point offers an enchanting view of the sunset against the backdrop of the surrounding hills. It’s also a popular spot for picnicking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Abu

  1. Gurushikhar to Achalgarh: This 10 km trail takes you through rolling hills and scenic routes.
  2. Mount Abu to Trevor’s Tank: This 5 km trail offers a slightly easier hike and great views of the surrounding Aravalli hills.

Best Things to Do in Mount Abu: Activities and Tours

  1. Boating at Nakki Lake: Boating in the pristine water of Nakki Lake surrounded by lush green hills is an unforgettable experience.
  2. Biking and Hiking: Explore the natural beauty of Mount Abu by taking a bike or hiking tour, both of which are readily available in the city.
  3. Wildlife Safari: Mount Abu Wildlife Sanctuary is home to several endangered species like wolf, fox, hyena, and leopard, and taking a wildlife safari is a unique experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Abu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Dal Bati Churma: Rajasthan’s quintessential dish is made up of three components – thick lentil soup, baked dough balls, and a sweet powder made of jaggery and ghee.
  2. Gatte ki Sabzi: Made with gram flour dumplings cooked in a tangy tomato-onion gravy, this dish is a treat for the taste buds.
  3. Laal Maas: A spicy mutton dish cooked with chili paste and other Indian spices, Laal Maas is the perfect meal for spice lovers.

Local Beverages

  1. Masala Tea: Rajasthan’s famous masala tea is made by boiling tea with spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger.
  2. Buttermilk: Buttermilk is a popular drink in Rajasthan and is made by blending yogurt with water and spices like cumin and mint.

What to Buy in Mount Abu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Jewelry: Rajasthan is renowned for its intricate jewelry designs, and Mount Abu is no exception. The city is famous for its silver jewelry embedded with precious and semi-precious stones.
  2. Handicrafts: Mount Abu is home to several shops selling traditional handicrafts like pottery, embroidery, and textiles.
  3. Sweets: Rajasthan is famous for its sweets, and you can bring home delicious mawa barfi, rasgulla, and laddoos from Mount Abu.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Abu

Mount Abu is known for its architecture, culture, and colorful festivals. The locals are friendly and welcoming, but certain cultural practices must be respected.

Best Time to Visit Mount Abu

The best time to visit Mount Abu is between November and June. In winter, the temperature drops to as low as 0°C, and in summer, it can go up to 35°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Abu

Do:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting religious sites.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry sunscreen and water while exploring the city.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Take pictures without permission at religious sites.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Abu

Mount Abu is a safe city, but it’s always advisable to keep your belongings safe and avoid visiting isolated areas late at night.

Health in Mount Abu

Mount Abu is at a high altitude, and visitors should carry adequate sun protection and be prepared for temperature changes.

Getting to Mount Abu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Abu

The nearest airport to Mount Abu is Maharana Pratap Airport in Udaipur, which is about 210 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mount Abu

Mount Abu is connected to major cities in Rajasthan by bus. The Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation operates buses from Jaipur, Udaipur, and other cities.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired to reach Mount Abu.

How to Get Around in Mount Abu

Mount Abu is a small city, and most tourist attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and private cars can also be hired for sightseeing.

Where to Go Next from Mount Abu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Udaipur: Known as the “Venice of the East,” Udaipur, with its serene lakes, magnificent palaces, and lively markets, is a must-visit destination.
  2. Jodhpur: Known for its blue-painted buildings, Jodhpur is a vibrant city rich in culture, history, and architecture.

Conclusion

Mount Abu is an excellent destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and cultural explorers alike. With its stunning natural beauty, culturally significant landmarks, and friendly locals, it’s a place you’ll never forget. Use this tour guide to make the most of your visit to Mount Abu.
