Introduction to Ranakpur

Located in Jodhpur Division, in the Rajasthan region of India, Ranakpur is a small village that’s known for some of the most spectacular Jain temples in the country. Located amidst the Aravallis, the temples here are known for their intricate carvings and stunning architecture. But with Ranakpur, the beauty really lies in the journey, with the region being surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery and opportunities for outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Ranakpur

When it comes to Ranakpur, there are a few key places that you simply cannot miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jain Temple: The Jain temple is the biggest draw for most visitors to the region, and it’s easy to see why. The temple was built in the 15th century and is a perfect example of the intricate architecture and carvings that the area is famous for.

  • Sun Temple: Located in the nearby village of Surya Narayan, the Sun Temple is another must-see landmark in the area. The temple dates back to the 13th century and is known for its impressive carvings of horses and dancers.

  • Sadri: Sadri is a small village located just a few kilometers from Ranakpur, and it’s home to several old temples that are worth checking out, including the Bhimashah Jain Temple and the Varahavtar Temple.

Natural Attractions of Ranakpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary: The Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary is situated a short distance away from Ranakpur and is home to a range of flora and fauna, such as wolves, leopards, and sloth bears. It’s also a great place for hiking and trekking.

  • Ranakpur Dam: The Ranakpur Dam is known for its stunning views of the Aravalli Mountains, and is a great spot to relax and unwind while taking in the natural beauty of the area.

  • Ranakpur Hill Station: Located at an elevation of 3000 feet above sea level, the Ranakpur Hill Station is another popular spot for nature lovers, and offers amazing views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ranakpur

Ranakpur is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. Some of the most popular include:

  • The Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: This trail takes visitors through some of the most scenic parts of the sanctuary, including through forests and past waterfalls, offering the opportunity to see a variety of wildlife along the way.

  • Ranakpur Hill Station Trek: The Ranakpur Hill Station Trek is a popular choice for those who want to experience the countryside and rural life of India. The trail takes visitors through small villages and farms, with plenty of opportunities to learn about local culture and traditions.

Best Things to Do in Ranakpur: Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Ranakpur, there’s a lot on offer, including:

  • Jain Temple Tour: Take a guided tour of the famous Jain Temple and learn about its history, architecture, and religious significance.

  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in the area, offering visitors a chance to explore the countryside and experience the region from a new perspective.

  • Sunset Point: Head to Sunset Point for breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys, particularly during sunset.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ranakpur

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Ranakpur:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Ranakpur, explore local markets and streets
2 Take a guided tour of the Jain Temple and explore nearby museums and historical places
3 Go hiking or biking in the Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary
4 Visit nearby villages and learn about traditional crafts and techniques
5 Spend the day at Ranakpur Hill Station and enjoy a picnic in the countryside
6 Take a horseback riding tour of the area
7 Relax at Ranakpur Dam and take in the stunning views before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Ranakpur

The food and drink scene in Ranakpur is as diverse as it is delicious, with a range of traditional and contemporary options on offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Baati Churma: A traditional Rajasthani dish made of lentils, baked wheat balls, and sweetened crumbled wheat.

  • Laal Maas: A spicy lamb curry made with a blend of local spices and chilies.

  • Gatte ki Sabzi: A vegetarian dish that’s made from gram flour and is typically served with steamed rice.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: A spicy, fragrant tea that’s made with a blend of local spices, milk, and tea leaves.

  • Lassi: A refreshing drink that’s made by blending yogurt, sugar, and ice cubes together.

Where to Stay in Ranakpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Ranakpur to suit all budgets and needs:

  • Ranakpur Hill Resort: Located in a tranquil spot, the Ranakpur Hill Resort is a great option for those looking for a quiet escape.

  • Hotel Fateh Bagh: This Palace hotel is located near the famous Jain temple and is a great option for those looking to experience the rich history and culture of the region.

  • WelcomHeritage Maharani Bagh Orchard Retreat: Offering a more upscale experience, this luxury resort is perfect for those who want to relax and unwind in style.

What to Buy in Ranakpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, then be sure to check out:

  • Traditional Handicrafts: Look out for locally made handicrafts and textiles, including quilted textiles and patchwork quilts.

  • Spices And Tea: The region is known for its spices and tea, so be sure to stock up on local varieties while you’re here.

Local Culture and Customs of Ranakpur

As with most places in India, Ranakpur has its own unique culture and customs to explore. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as taking off shoes before entering temples and dressing conservatively.

Best Time to Visit Ranakpur

The best time to visit Ranakpur is between October and March, when the weather is comfortable and ideal for outdoor exploring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ranakpur

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, particularly when visiting temples or religious sites.

  • Respect local customs, such as taking off shoes before entering certain places.

  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, always stick to bottled water.

  • Get involved in any aggressive behavior, particularly when bargaining for goods at local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ranakpur

Ranakpur itself is a relatively safe destination for travelers, with no major safety issues to be concerned about. As always, visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as keeping an eye on valuables and being cautious when traveling at night.

Health in Ranakpur

Visitors should be sure to seek medical attention in the event of any health issues, particularly if they arise due to mosquitoes or other insects. Visitors should also be careful to watch out for dehydration, particularly in the hot and dry seasons.

Getting to Ranakpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ranakpur

The nearest airport to Ranakpur is Maharana Pratap Airport in Udaipur, which is located around 100 kilometers away from the village.

Bus Stations Near Ranakpur

Ranakpur is well-connected by buses, with regular services to nearby cities such as Udaipur and Jodhpur.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to hire private taxis or take the train to nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Ranakpur

The easiest way to get around in Ranakpur is by private taxi or auto-rickshaw. Visitors can also choose to hire bicycles or motorbikes, particularly if they’re interested in exploring the region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ranakpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve finished exploring Ranakpur and are looking for your next destination, then consider:

  • Udaipur: Located just a few hours away, Udaipur is known for its stunning palaces, bustling local markets, and vibrant cultural scene.

  • Jodhpur: Known as the “Blue City,” Jodhpur is a great destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and architecture, with attractions such as the Mehrangarh Fort and the Umaid Bhawan Palace.

Conclusion

Overall, Ranakpur offers visitors a unique glimpse into the beauty and charm of Rajasthan, with its stunning natural scenery, rich history and culture, and delicious food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or explore the region’s outdoor adventures, there’s plenty to see and do in Ranakpur.
