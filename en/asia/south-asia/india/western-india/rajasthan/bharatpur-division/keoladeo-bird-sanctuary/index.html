Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary Travel Guide

Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary, located in Bharatpur Division in Rajasthan, India, is a natural haven for bird lovers. Spanning over an area of 29 square kilometers, this sanctuary is home to over 370 species of birds, making it one of the most important wetlands in Asia. In addition to boasting diverse avian life, Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary also offers cultural attractions and outdoor adventures. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit and make the most of your experience.

Introduction to Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary was established in 1982 and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1985. Formerly a hunting ground for the royalty of Bharatpur in the 18th and 19th centuries, it was converted into a bird sanctuary by the Maharaja of Bharatpur. Its marshes, woodlands, and freshwater wetlands are important habitat for birds that migrate to India from as far away as Siberia.

List of Places to Visit in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Visitors to Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary can enjoy a range of cultural attractions, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and tours. Here is a list of the most popular places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Government Museum Bharatpur: Located in the center of the city, the Government Museum Bharatpur was established in 1944 and showcases the history of the region.
  • Lohagarh Fort: Constructed in the 18th century, this impregnable fort stands sentinel over the city of Bharatpur and is a fine example of Rajput architecture.
  • Deeg Palace: Located 32 kilometers from Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary, Deeg Palace was a summer retreat for the royal family of Bharatpur and features beautiful gardens and fountains.

Natural Attractions of Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boat rides: Boats are available for hire in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary, taking visitors on a peaceful ride through the sanctuary’s waterways.
  • Nature walks: Visitors can explore the sanctuary’s woodlands and marshes on foot, accompanied by a guide.
  • Cycle rickshaw rides: Cyclists are available for hire to take visitors on tours around the sanctuary.
  • Towers: There are four towers inside the sanctuary that offer panoramic views of the surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary has several trails that run through its woodlands and wetlands, making it ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Keoladeo Park Circular Route: This trail is a 7-kilometer loop that starts and ends at Keoladeo Park, taking visitors around the park’s main attractions.
  • Bharatpur to Deeg: This 32-kilometer trail takes visitors from Bharatpur to Deeg, passing through Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary along the way.
  • Laxman Mandir Trail: This 5-kilometer trail takes visitors to the Laxman Mandir Temple, situated near the sanctuary’s eastern gate.

Best Things to Do in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and bird watching, there are many fun things to do in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Photography tour: Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary offers some of the best bird photography opportunities in India, so consider taking a guided tour to capture memorable shots.
  • Sound and light show: The sound and light show at Lohagarh Fort is a must-see, offering an immersive experience that retells the history of the fort and Bharatpur.
  • Half-day birding tour: A half-day birding tour is the perfect way to experience the sanctuary’s avian life and to learn more about the different bird species from an expert guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Rajasthan is known for its delicious food, and Bharatpur is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulab Jamun: This sweet dessert is made from deep-fried dough balls that are soaked in a syrup made from rosewater and sugar.
  • Lal Maas: This spicy meat dish is made from mutton cooked in a red chili paste with yogurt and other spices.
  • Daal Baati Churma: This is a traditional Rajasthani meal made from lentils, hard wheat rolls called Baati, and Churma, a sweet dish made from wheat flour, ghee, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: This popular drink is made from yogurt that is mixed with water, sugar, and spices.
  • Masala Chai: This spiced tea is made from tea leaves that are boiled with milk, sugar, and spices such as cardamom and ginger.

  • Kadamb Kunj Resort: This eco-friendly resort is located just 1 kilometer from the sanctuary and features luxurious tents and traditional Rajasthani villas.
  • Hotel Sunbird: This budget-friendly hotel is located near the sanctuary and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • Birder’s Inn: This mid-range hotel is located near the sanctuary and caters specifically to bird watchers, offering guided tours and birding equipment rentals.

What to Buy in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary is known for its handicrafts, especially those made by local artisans. Some of the most popular souvenirs and local products include:

  • Blue Pottery: Blue pottery, a traditional craft of Jaipur, is available at many shops in and around Bharatpur.
  • Bandhani: Bandhani, another traditional craft, is a type of tie-dye fabric that is made by artisans in Rajasthan.
  • Brassware: Brassware, including figurines, lamps, and decorative items, is widely available in Bharatpur.

Local Culture and Customs of Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Rajasthan is known for its rich cultural heritage, and visitors to Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary can learn more about the local culture and customs through the region’s architecture, handicrafts, and food. It is important to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering temples and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

The best time to visit Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary is from October to February when the weather is cool and dry, and migratory birds flock to the sanctuary. Avoid visiting during the summer months when temperatures soar above 40°C and the sanctuary is closed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary:

Do:

  • Hire a knowledgeable guide who can help you identify bird species and navigate the sanctuary.
  • Respect the natural environment and avoid littering.
  • Follow designated trails and paths to avoid disturbing bird habitats.

Don’t:

  • Disturb birds or their habitats by making loud noises or getting too close.
  • Feed birds or any other wildlife in the sanctuary.
  • Use flash photography, which can startle birds and other animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary is generally safe for visitors, but it’s a good idea to take some precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.
  • Keep valuables such as cash and passports in a safe place.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and report any suspicious activity to the authorities.

Health in Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary

Keoladeo Bird Sanctuary is a malaria-prone area, so it’s important to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites. Here are some tips:

  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants to cover your skin.
  • Use insect repellent that contains DEET.
  • Consider taking anti-malaria medication before your trip.
