Shekhawati is a region in the north-eastern part of Rajasthan, India. Often referred to as an “open-air art gallery,” Shekhawati is famous for its painted havelis (mansions), which feature intricate frescoes and murals depicting the life and times of the region’s wealthy merchant families. In addition to the havelis, Shekhawati boasts a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide to Shekhawati, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Shekhawati

Shekhawati is located in the semi-arid Shekhawati region of Rajasthan. The region spans over 13,000 square kilometers and includes districts of Jhunjhunu, Sikar, and Churu. It was named after its former rulers, the Shekhawat Rajputs, who established their kingdom here in the 15th century. Today, Shekhawati is known for its stunning havelis, cultural festivals, and vibrant bazaars.

List of Places to Visit in Shekhawati

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mandawa Fort: This magnificent fort was built in the 18th century and is one of the well-preserved forts of the Shekhawati region. The fort features exquisite artwork, mirror work, and wall paintings, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of the region.
  2. Fatehpur Shekhawati: Fatehpur Shekhawati is a small town that is home to some of the most stunning havelis of the Shekhawati region. Some of the must-visit havelis here include the Goenka Chhatri, the Poddar Chhatri, and the Mahavir Prasad Goenka Haveli.
  3. Nawalgarh: Nawalgarh is a colorful town known for its stunning havelis, frescoes, and murals. Some of the must-visit havelis here include the Morarka Haveli, the Khedwal Bhawan, and the Anandi Lal Poddar haveli.

Natural Attractions of Shekhawati: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tal Chhapar Sanctuary: This sanctuary is a haven for birdwatchers, as it is home to a plethora of migratory birds, such as demoiselle cranes, raptors, and larks.
  2. Khetri Mahal Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is located in the Jhunjhunu district of Shekhawati and is home to a variety of animals, including deer, hyenas, and leopards.
  3. Sikar: This town is situated amidst the Aravallis and offers scenic views of the mountain range. Some of the must-visit places in Sikar include the Sikar Fort, the Madho Niwas Kothi, and the Rani Sati Temple.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shekhawati

  1. Mandawa to Fatehpur: This scenic route takes you through the heart of the Shekhawati region and offers breathtaking views of the painted havelis and the rural landscape.
  2. Jhunjhunu to Salasar Balaji: This route takes you to the famous Salasar Balaji Temple and offers stunning views of the Aravalli hills en route.
  3. Sikar to Khetri: This scenic route takes you through the Khetri Mahal Wildlife Sanctuary and offers plenty of opportunities for wildlife sightings and photography.

Best Things to Do in Shekhawati: Activities and Tours

  1. Painted Haveli Tours: One of the best things to do in Shekhawati is to take a tour of the region’s painted havelis. These tours are conducted by local guides who provide fascinating insights about the history, culture, and architecture of the region.
  2. Camel Safaris: Another must-do activity in Shekhawati is to take a camel safari through the desert landscape and explore the region’s rural villages and sand dunes.
  3. Cultural Festivals: The Shekhawati region is known for its vibrant cultural festivals, such as the Teej festival, the Gangaur festival, and the Shekhawati festival. Attending these festivals is a great way to experience the region’s culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Shekhawati

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Gatta Curry: This is a popular Rajasthani dish made with gram flour dumplings and a spicy curry.
  2. Laal Maans: This is a mutton dish cooked in a spicy red gravy, flavored with garlic, red chilies, and other aromatic spices.
  3. Pyaaz Ki Kachori: This is a deep-fried, crispy pastry filled with spiced onion filling.

Local Beverages

  1. Buttermilk: This is a refreshing, yogurt-based drink that is perfect for the hot summer months.
  2. Lassi: This is a thick, creamy yogurt-based drink that is often flavored with fruit pulp or saffron.
  3. Chai: This is a spiced tea that is a staple beverage in the region.

Where to Stay in Shekhawati: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Mandawa Haveli: This is a heritage hotel located in the heart of Mandawa and offers guests an authentic experience of living in a traditional haveli.
  2. Vivaana Culture Hotel: This is a luxury hotel located in Nawalgarh and is known for its stunning architecture, beautiful gardens, and luxurious amenities.
  3. Nawalgarh Plaza: This is a budget-friendly option located in the heart of Nawalgarh and is perfect for travelers who are looking for comfortable accommodation at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Shekhawati: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Paintings and Artwork: Shekhawati is famous for its painted havelis, and you can find miniature replicas of these paintings in local markets and shops.
  2. Handicrafts and Textiles: Shekhawati is home to a variety of talented artisans who create beautiful handicrafts and textiles, such as pottery, brassware, and fabrics.
  3. Jewelry: Shekhawati is known for its intricate jewelry designs, which feature traditional motifs and engraved patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Shekhawati

Shekhawati has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, artwork, and festivals. The region is known for its vibrant music and dance traditions, such as the Ghoomar dance, the Kalbelia dance, and the Chari dance. The region is also known for its colorful bazaars, which are filled with handicrafts, textiles, and local products.

Best Time to Visit Shekhawati

The best time to visit Shekhawati is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the winter festivals are in full swing. The summer months, from April to June, can be scorching, while the monsoon months, from July to September, can disrupt travel plans due to heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shekhawati

Do:

  1. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  2. Respect local customs and traditions.
  3. Bargain when shopping in local markets and bazaars.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  2. Litter in public places.
  3. Take photographs without seeking permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Shekhawati

Shekhawati is a safe place for travelers to visit, but it is always advisable to take precautions, such as carrying a copy of important documents, avoiding crowded places, and ensuring that you have adequate travel insurance.

Health in Shekhawati

Travelers to Shekhawati should ensure that they carry adequate medication and vaccination for common ailments like diarrhea, malaria, and dengue fever. It is also essential to carry sufficient quantities of drinking water and avoid consuming street food to avoid water-borne diseases.
