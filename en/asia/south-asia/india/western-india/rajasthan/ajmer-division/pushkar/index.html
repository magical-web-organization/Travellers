Introduction to Pushkar

Located in the Ajmer division of Rajasthan, Pushkar is a culturally rich city surrounded by beautiful hills and the picturesque Pushkar Lake. The city is known for its rich history, religious importance, and scenic beauty.

Pushkar attracts a lot of tourists from across the world, particularly for the Pushkar Camel Fair that happens during November every year. The fair is a unique cultural experience that exhibits camels and horses, trade, music and dance, and food and crafts.

This travel guide provides helpful information on the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and how to get there in Pushkar.

List of Places to Visit in Pushkar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pushkar Lake: This beautiful lake is one of the most important pilgrimage spots for Hindus. The lake is surrounded by 52 ghats where devotees come to take a holy dip.
  • Brahma Temple: Dedicated to Lord Brahma, the temple is one of the few temples in the world that is dedicated to this deity. The temple’s architecture and intricate carvings are a sight to behold.
  • Varaha Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his boar avatar (Varaha). The temple is known for its beautiful carvings and history.
  • Rangji Temple: This temple is a blend of Dravidian, Rajputana, and Mughal architecture. It is dedicated to Lord Rangji, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu.
  • Man Mahal: It is a beautiful palace located near the Pushkar Lake on the eastern part of the Sarovar. The palace was built by Maharaja Man Singh I of Jaipur, and it now serves as a heritage hotel.

Natural Attractions of Pushkar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Savitri Mata Temple: Situated on top of the Ratnagiri hill, the temple offers a panoramic view of the Pushkar city and the surrounding hills. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Saraswati and provides a peaceful and serene environment.
  • Pushkar Camel Safari: A Camel Safari is an exciting way to explore the Pushkar desert and hills. The safari is a thrilling experience and is a great way to get up close with the desert fauna and flora.
  • Parikrama of Ratnagiri Hill: This trek takes travelers on a journey around Ratnagiri Hill, offering picturesque views of the Pushkar city and the Aravalli hills. The trek requires moderate physical fitness and takes around two hours to complete.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pushkar

  • Pushkar to Ajaypal: This hiking route is a scenic trek that leads from Pushkar to the top of Ajaypal Hill. The trek takes around three hours, and travelers can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and Pushkar city.
  • Pushkar to Kishangarh: This cycling route takes travelers through the Meena tribal hills to the small town of Kishangarh, which is famous for its marble work. The route takes around three hours and offers stunning views of the hills and the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Pushkar: Activities and Tours

  • Pushkar Camel Fair: The camel fair is one of the best things to experience in Pushkar. The fair is held in November and offers an immersive cultural experience with music, dance, crafts, and local food.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride over Pushkar: The Hot Air Balloon ride offers an unparalleled view of Pushkar from above. The ride lasts for approximately one hour and offers a stunning view of the Pushkar city and surrounding hills.
  • Sunset at Pushkar Lake: The Pushkar Lake offers a serene and peaceful environment at sunset. Travelers can sit by the lakeside and enjoy the beautiful sunset while listening to the sounds of traditional Rajasthani music.

Food and Drink Scene of Pushkar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal-baati-churma: A famous Rajasthani dish consisting of lentils, wheat rolls, and sweetened cereal.
  • Laal Maas: A spicy, meat-heavy curry that originates in Rajasthan.
  • Mirchi Bada: A deep-fried fritter stuffed with mashed potatoes and green peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A sweet or savory yoghurt-based drink that is perfect for combating the heat.
  • Masala Chai: A spiced, milky tea that is perfect for cold winter mornings.
  • Thandai: A sweet, refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer afternoons.

Where to Stay in Pushkar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ananta Spa and Resort: A luxurious resort located in the hills surrounding Pushkar. The resort offers breathtaking views of the Aravalli hills and provides guests with luxurious amenities.
  • The Pushkar Bagh Resort: A luxury resort located just outside of Pushkar. The resort offers outdoor activities such as horseback riding, mountain biking, and yoga.
  • Zostel Pushkar: A backpacker’s hostel located in the heart of Pushkar. The hostel offers affordable accommodation and a relaxed, social atmosphere.

What to Buy in Pushkar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pushkar Camel Leather: The local leather industry produces quality leather goods made from camel skin, including bags, shoes, and jackets.
  • Rajasthan Handicrafts: Rajasthan is famous for its handicrafts, including colorful textiles, hand-painted pottery, and intricate metalwork.
  • Famous Rajasthani Jewelry: Rajasthan is famous for its jewelry in areas such as Kundan and Thewa.

Local Culture and Customs of Pushkar

Pushkar is a holy city for Hindus, so visitors are expected to respect the local customs and culture.

It is considered disrespectful to consume non-vegetarian food around the Pushkar Lake or the religious areas. Travelers should dress modestly and remove footwear before entering the temples.

Always seek consent before taking photographs of locals, their homes or businesses. It is also customary to tip service staff after eating out, taking a cab, or any assistance you receive.

Best Time to Visit Pushkar

The best time to visit Pushkar is between October to March, as the weather is mild and pleasant. November is the peak tourist season due to the annual Pushkar Camel Fair.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pushkar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local cuisine and shop locally.

Don’t:

  • Consume non-vegetarian food near religious sites or the Pushkar Lake.
  • Ask locals for their food, drink, or religious belongings.
  • Remove any plants, rocks, or natural items from the area as souvenirs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pushkar

Pushkar is generally considered a low-risk destination for visitors. However, travelers should take general precautions regarding pickpocketing and should avoid traveling alone after dark.

Health in Pushkar

Travelers to Pushkar should take precautions to avoid contracting diseases such as cholera or typhoid by drinking only bottled water, avoiding street food, or any unhygienic item for intake. It is also essential to take sun-protection in consideration and carry mosquito repellents.

Getting to Pushkar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pushkar

The nearest airport to Pushkar is Jaipur International Airport, which is located around 134 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach Pushkar.

Bus Stations Near Pushkar

Ajmer Bus Stand is the nearest major bus station, with buses available from Delhi, Jaipur, and Jodhpur.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car, taxi or take a train to reach Pushkar from other parts of Rajasthan or nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Pushkar

The best way to get around Pushkar is on foot or bicycle, and many of the main attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Auto-rickshaws are cheap and are readily available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Pushkar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ajmer: A nearby city that is a famous pilgrimage site for Muslims, with one of the most famous Dargahs (shrines) of Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti.
  • Jaipur: Known as the “Pink City of India,” Jaipur is a vibrant city that boasts impressive palaces, forts, and an immense historical and cultural significance.

Conclusion

Pushkar is a city with a rich cultural, spiritual, and historical significance. It blends the old-world charm with modern amenities and attracts tourists from across the globe. The above travel guide provides a comprehensive list of things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Pushkar, ensuring that everyone will have a memorable trip to this beautiful city.
