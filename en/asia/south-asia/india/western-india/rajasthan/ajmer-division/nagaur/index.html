If you’re planning a trip to Rajasthan, don’t overlook Nagaur. This small city in the Ajmer division is often overshadowed by Jaipur, Udaipur, and other popular tourist destinations, but it has plenty to offer visitors who want to explore a more authentic side of Rajasthan. From historic landmarks and natural attractions to outdoor adventures and delicious local cuisine, Nagaur has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and tips for making the most of your time in Nagaur.

Introduction to Nagaur

Nagaur is located in the western part of Rajasthan, India. The city has a rich cultural heritage and a fascinating history that dates back to the 4th century. It was an important center of trade and culture during the medieval period and has been ruled by various dynasties, including the Rajputs, Mughals, and Marathas. Today, Nagaur is best known for its historic landmarks, natural beauty, and cultural traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Nagaur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nagaur Fort: Built in the 12th century, this fort is one of the city’s most iconic landmarks. It has a rich history and several temples, palaces, and courtyards within its walls.
  • Tarkeen Dargah: This is a tomb of a famous Muslim saint of the same name. People from all religious backgrounds come to offer their prayers at this site.
  • Jain Temple: Dedicated to Lord Mahavir, this temple is a must-visit for its intricate architecture and intricate carvings on the walls.
  • Rani Mahal: This palace is a prime example of the Mughal architectural style and is a fascinating glimpse into the city’s royal heritage.
  • Cenotaph of Amar Singh: This is a distinctive structure that is notable for its exquisite carvings and intricate work.

Natural Attractions of Nagaur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kuchaman Fort: Nestled within the Aravalli hills, this fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Salt Lake: A large salt lake that is a popular spot for bird-watching and taking in the sunset.
  • Khatu Shyamji: A sacred temple located near the city’s outskirts that is said to have miraculous powers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nagaur

  • Aravalli Range: These hills offer the perfect backdrop for hiking enthusiasts who want to experience the natural beauty of the area.
  • Salt Lake Trail: This trail takes you through the city’s salt lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Nagaur: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Safari: Explore Nagaur’s desert landscape on camelback and experience a truly unique adventure.
  • Village Safari: Get a glimpse into the rural life of Rajasthan by taking a tour of nearby villages to observe their culture and lifestyle.
  • Local Markets: Shop for local handicrafts, jewelry, and textiles at the bustling markets of Nagaur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nagaur

Day Activity
1 Explore Nagaur Fort and Tarkeen Dargah
2 Visit the Jain Temple, Rani Mahal, and Cenotaph of Amar Singh
3 Hike the Aravalli Range or bike the Salt Lake Trail
4 Take a camel safari and explore the desert landscape
5 Visit Kuchaman Fort and take in the scenic views from the top
6 Tour nearby villages and learn about the local culture
7 Shop for souvenirs at the local markets before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Nagaur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Baati Churma: A traditional Rajasthani dish that consists of lentils, baked bread, and a sweet dessert made of wheat and sugar.
  • Laal Maas: This spicy lamb curry is a local favorite and is not to be missed.
  • Gatte Ki Sabzi: A vegetarian delicacy made from gram flour dumplings in a tomato-based curry.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: The traditional Indian tea that is flavored with spices and herbs.
  • Lassi: A refreshing and sweet yogurt-based drink that is perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Nagaur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nagaur has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular ones include:

  • RTDC Hotel Khimsar: This heritage property is located near Kuchaman Fort and has comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • Hotel Meghniwas: This boutique hotel is located within the Nagaur Fort and offers unique royal accommodations with a view.
  • H4U Fort View Hotel: This mid-range hotel is located near many popular attractions and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Nagaur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jewelry: Nagaur is known for its traditional Rajasthani jewelry, made from precious stones and metals.
  • Textiles: Handwoven fabrics, including saris, shawls, and scarves, are popular souvenirs to bring home.
  • Handicrafts: Choose from historic items such as pottery, shoes, and wooden toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Nagaur

Nagaur has a rich cultural heritage that is influenced by its history and surrounding geography. Visitors are advised to dress conservatively and respect local customs, especially when visiting religious sites. Rajasthan is known for its vibrant music and dance traditions, and Nagaur is no exception. Experience the vibrant and colorful festivals such as Jasnath Fair and Marha Baba Ki Mela that are celebrated with great zeal and enthusiasm throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Nagaur

The best time to visit Nagaur is from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant. The summer months can be scorching hot, so it’s best to avoid visiting during this time if possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nagaur

Do:

  • Dress conservatively and respect local customs
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the traditional Rajasthani dishes
  • Take part in cultural and religious festivals

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sentiments or local customs
  • Visit religious sites during times of worship
  • Litter or behave irresponsibly in natural areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Nagaur

Nagaur, like many cities in Rajasthan, is relatively safe for tourists. However, it’s always best to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Nagaur

It’s always advisable to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent food poisoning. Also, it is advised to travel with a first-aid kit which includes necessary medicines and insect repellents.

Getting to Nagaur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nagaur

The nearest airport to Nagaur is Jodhpur Airport, which is approximately 138 km (86 miles) away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Nagaur.

Bus Stations Near Nagaur

Nagaur has a well-connected bus network that connects it to major cities in Rajasthan and other regions of India. Buses are available from Jodhpur, Jaipur, and many other cities.

Other Options

Another way to get to Nagaur is by train. Nagaur has its own railway station that connects it to major cities, including Delhi and Mumbai.

How to Get Around in Nagaur

Nagaur is a small city that can be easily explored on foot. Alternatively, you can hire a taxi, auto rickshaw, or motorcycle to get around more quickly.

Where to Go Next from Nagaur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Khimsar Fort: Located just 32 km from Nagaur, this historic fort is a popular tourist destination and offers unique accommodations, panoramic views, and outdoor adventure activities.
  • Jodhpur: Known as the Blue City, Jodhpur is a must-visit destination that is famous for its historic landmarks, vibrant culture, and picturesque scenery.
  • Pushkar: This holy city, located about 100 km from Nagaur, is famous for its annual camel fair and is a popular destination for travelers interested in Hindu religion and culture.

Conclusion

Nagaur may not be as well-known as some of Rajasthan’s more popular tourist destinations, but it’s a hidden gem that’s worth exploring. From its historic landmarks and natural beauty to its delicious local cuisine and vibrant culture, Nagaur has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this fascinating city.
