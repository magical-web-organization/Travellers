Introduction to Ajmer

Located in the Ajmer Division of Rajasthan, Ajmer is a culturally and historically rich city that has something for everyone. Home to the famous Dargah Sharif, a sufi shrine of great importance, this city is a popular pilgrimage spot for both Hindus and Muslims. Apart from the religious significance, Ajmer is a city that boasts of stunning forts, palaces, lakes, and outdoor natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination in Rajasthan.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Ajmer, we’ll explore the best places to visit, natural wonders, best things to do, where to stay, eat, buy, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Ajmer

Ajmer is a traditional city where locals follow their customs and practices strictly. It is important to dress modestly and respect the religious practices of the city, especially when visiting the Dargah Sharif.

Best Time to Visit Ajmer

The winter months from October to March are the best time to visit Ajmer as the weather is pleasant, and the temperature ranges from 10 – 20 degree Celsius. Summers can be scorching hot, and it is best to avoid visiting Ajmer during April to June.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ajmer

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Cover your head and remove shoes when visiting the Dargah
  • Try local food and beverages
  • Bargain when shopping

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the Dargah Sharif
  • Wear revealing clothes in public places
  • Disrespect religious beliefs
  • Litter the streets

Safety and Health

Safety in Ajmer

Ajmer is relatively safe for tourists, as there is not much reported crime. However, it is always a good idea to take precautions, such as being mindful of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoiding deserted areas after dark.

Health in Ajmer

It is recommended that you drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food, as it may not be hygienic for tourists.

Getting to Ajmer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ajmer

The nearest airport to Ajmer is the Jaipur International Airport, which is approximately 135 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ajmer

Ajmer has a well-connected bus network, with regular buses from Jaipur, Jodhpur, and other major cities.

Other Options

Ajmer is also well-connected by rail and roadways. There are regular trains from Jaipur, Delhi, and Mumbai, and the National Highway 48 connects Ajmer to other parts of Rajasthan and India.

How to Get Around in Ajmer

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are the most convenient transportation options in Ajmer. If you are feeling more adventurous, you can also rent a bike or a car and explore the city on your own.
