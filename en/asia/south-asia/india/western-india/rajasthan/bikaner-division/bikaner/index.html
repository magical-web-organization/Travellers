Introduction to Bikaner

Bikaner is a city located in the northwest of the Rajasthan state of India. It is an ancient city that was once a part of the trade route between Asia and Europe. Bikaner is famous for its forts and palaces, intricate Rajasthani architecture, vibrant markets, and delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive travel guide to Bikaner, we will explore the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, what to buy, local culture, safety and health, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Bikaner

Bikaner has an abundance of cultural and natural attractions that travelers can explore. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bikaner:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Junagarh Fort: This fort dates back to the 15th century and is known for its stunning architecture and museum.
  • Lalgarh Palace: This palace was built in the early 20th century and is famous for its blend of Mughal, Rajput, and European architectural styles.
  • Gajner Palace: This palace is located on the banks of a lake and was once a hunting lodge.
  • Ganga Singh Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Rajasthan.
  • Jain Haveli: This Haveli is a stunning piece of Rajasthani architecture that dates back to the 15th century.

Natural Attractions of Bikaner: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karni Mata Temple: This temple is famous for the thousands of rats that live there and are considered holy.
  • Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife such as chinkara, blackbuck, wild boar, and migratory birds.
  • Devi Kund Sagar: This is a beautiful lake surrounded by temples.
  • Kolayat: This is a peaceful lake with temples and ghats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bikaner

Bikaner is surrounded by desert landscapes that are perfect for hiking and biking. Some popular routes include:

  • Bikaner to Karni Mata Temple: This is a 40 km route that passes through rural villages and sand dunes.
  • Gajner to Deshnokh: This is a 50 km route that takes you through the Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary and past the Karni Mata Temple.

Best Things to Do in Bikaner: Activities and Tours

Bikaner offers a variety of activities and tours to suit any traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Bikaner:

  • Camel Safari: Explore the desert landscapes of Bikaner on a camel safari.
  • Food Tour: Experience the local flavors of Bikaner on a food tour.
  • Market Tour: Explore the vibrant markets of Bikaner.
  • Puppet Show: Watch a traditional Rajasthani puppet show.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bikaner

Here is a week-long itinerary for Bikaner, which includes both cultural and natural attractions:

  • Day 1: Visit Junagarh Fort, Lalgarh Palace, and Ganga Singh Museum.
  • Day 2: Explore Kolayat and the Devi Kund Sagar lake.
  • Day 3: Take a camel safari in the desert.
  • Day 4: Visit Gajner Palace and go on a food tour.
  • Day 5: Visit Karni Mata Temple and the Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Day 6: Go on a market tour and watch a puppet show.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Bikaner

Bikaner is famous for its delicious cuisine, which features a mix of Rajasthani and Mughal flavors. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bikaneri Bhujia: This is a spicy snack made from gram flour and spices.
  • Gatte Ki Sabzi: This is a curry made from gram flour dumplings.
  • Rajasthani Thali: This is a traditional Rajasthani meal consisting of dal, bati, churma, and other dishes.
  • Pyaaz Kachori: This is a deep-fried snack filled with onion and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: This is a sweet or salty yogurt-based drink.
  • Chaas: This is a buttermilk-based drink flavored with spices.

Where to Stay in Bikaner: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bikaner offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget, from luxury hotels to budget guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Narendra Bhawan: This is a luxury hotel with elegant rooms and excellent dining options.
  • Vesta Bikaner Palace: This is a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and good dining options.
  • Hostelavie: This is a budget hostel that offers dormitory-style accommodation and a rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Bikaner: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bikaner is famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and spices. Here are some items to consider:

  • Bikaneri Bhujia: This makes a great gift for foodies.
  • Handmade Textiles: These colorful textiles make beautiful souvenirs.
  • Camel Leather Products: Bags, shoes, and other items made from camel leather are popular in Bikaner.

Local Culture and Customs of Bikaner

Bikaner has a rich culture and history that is reflected in its architecture, food, and traditions. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Removing your shoes before entering temples or homes.
  • Dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respecting local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bikaner

The best time to visit Bikaner is from November to March. The weather during this time is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bikaner

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without permission.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bikaner

Bikaner is a relatively safe city for travelers. However, it is important to take precautions to avoid theft and scams. Make sure to keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bikaner

It is important to take precautions when it comes to food and water in Bikaner. Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid street food if you have a sensitive stomach. It is also recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to India.

Getting to Bikaner: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bikaner is well-connected to other Indian cities by rail, road, and air. Here are some transportation options:

  • Air: The nearest airport is in Jodhpur, about 247 km away from Bikaner.
  • Train: Bikaner Junction is the main railway station, which is well-connected to other cities in India.
  • Bus: There are regular bus services from Jaipur, Jaisalmer, and other cities.

Airports Near Bikaner

  • Jodhpur Airport: This is the nearest airport, about 247 km away from Bikaner.

Bus Stations Near Bikaner

  • Bikaner Bus Stand: This is the main bus stand in Bikaner.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Bikaner.
  • Car Rental: Self-drive rental cars are available, but keep in mind that traffic in India can be chaotic.

How to Get Around in Bikaner

Bikaner is a small city and most attractions are within walking distance. However, you can also hire a taxi or tuk-tuk to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Bikaner: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bikaner is located in a prime location for exploring other cities in Rajasthan. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Jaisalmer: This is a stunning city located in the Thar Desert, famous for its sandstone architecture, camel safaris, and stunning sunsets.
  • Jaipur: This is the capital city of Rajasthan, known for its beautiful palaces, forts, and temples.
  • Pushkar: This is a small town known for its annual camel fair and holy lake.

Conclusion

Bikaner is a city with a rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine.
