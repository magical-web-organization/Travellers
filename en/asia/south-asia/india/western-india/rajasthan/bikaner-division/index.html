Introduction to Bikaner Division

Located in the northern part of Rajasthan, Bikaner Division is a region rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. The division is comprised of three districts - Bikaner, Churu and Sri Ganganagar - and offers visitors a variety of experiences, from exploring ancient forts and museums to hiking scenic trails and indulging in local culinary delights.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Bikaner Division.

List of Places to Visit in Bikaner Division

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Junagarh Fort

Considered one of the most impressive forts in India, Junagarh Fort was constructed in the 16th century and houses several palaces and temples. Visitors can explore the intricately designed halls, courtyards, and museum exhibits. Don’t miss the Anup Mahal and Karan Mahal.

Karni Mata Temple

Karni Mata Temple, also known as the Temple of Rats, is one of the most unique attractions in the world. The temple is home to over 25,000 rats, which are considered sacred by locals. Visitors can witness the surreal sight of rats running around the premises.

Lalgarh Palace

Lalgarh Palace is a stunning palace built in the 19th century for Maharaja Ganga Singh. The palace features a blend of Rajput, Mughal and European architectural styles. It now houses a museum showcasing royal artifacts and works of art.

Natural Attractions of Bikaner Division: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary

Located near Bikaner, Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area for numerous species of wildlife, such as wild boars, antelopes, and blackbucks. The sanctuary is also home to a picturesque lake, offering visitors a chance to witness stunning sunsets.

Tal Chhapar Sanctuary

Tal Chhapar Sanctuary is a perfect destination for birdwatchers and wildlife enthusiasts. The sanctuary is home to various species of birds and animals, like the Bengal fox, desert hare, and blackbuck. Visitors can join guided tours to explore the sanctuary.

Thar Desert

The Thar Desert is an ideal place for an outdoor adventure. Visitors can camp among the sand dunes and enjoy camel rides, jeep safaris, and hot air balloon rides. The desert also hosts vibrant cultural events like the famous Desert Festival.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bikaner Division

Bikaner Cycling Tour

Explore the cultural heritage and natural beauty of Bikaner on a cycling tour. The tour takes you through the streets of Bikaner, showcasing architectural gems like Junagarh Fort and Lalgarh Palace. You also stop at local villages, where you can witness the traditional way of life.

Camel Safari

For a unique hiking experience, try a camel safari. The safari takes you through the Thar desert, offering stunning views of the sand dunes and the sunset. You can also experience the local music and dance performances, and indulge in authentic Rajasthani cuisine.

Best Things to Do in Bikaner Division: Activities and Tours

Culinary Tours

Bikaner Division has a rich culinary tradition. Visitors can explore the local food scene with food tours, which take you to the best street food stalls and restaurants in Bikaner. The tours also provide a chance to learn the techniques and secrets of Rajasthani cooking.

Cultural Tours

Bikaner Division has a unique cultural identity, which is reflected in its historic monuments, festivals, and lifestyle. Visitors can join cultural tours to explore the division’s cultural heritage, including traditional music and dance performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Bikaner Division

Must-Try Dishes

Bikaneri Bhujia

Bikaneri Bhujia is a famous savory snack made of gram flour and spices. The snack is crispy and spicy, and is a perfect accompaniment to your evening tea.

Dal Bati Churma

Dal Bati Churma is a Rajasthani delicacy made of lentil curry, fried bread, and sweetened crushed wheat. The dish is a must-try for foodies visiting Bikaner.

Local Beverages

Chaas

Chaas is a refreshing buttermilk drink, perfect for the hot summer days. It is made of yogurt, water, and spices and is a common beverage in Rajasthan.

Lassi

Lassi is a sweet yogurt drink, made with fruit or spices. It is a popular drink in Bikaner and provides a cooling effect on hot days.

Where to Stay in Bikaner Division: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can choose from a variety of accommodation options in Bikaner Division, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury resorts.

Budget-friendly

The Lost Art Guest House

The Lost Art Guest House is a cozy and affordable guesthouse in Bikaner. The guesthouse provides a homely atmosphere with clean and comfortable rooms.

Madho Niwas

Madho Niwas is a budget hotel located near the Bikaner Railway Station. The hotel offers a range of amenities, including Wi-Fi, room service, and laundry.

Luxury

Narendra Bhawan

Narendra Bhawan is a beautifully designed luxury hotel in Bikaner. The hotel features luxurious rooms, a spa, and a stunning pool. The hotel also boasts an award-winning restaurant, P&C.

Laxmi Niwas Palace

Laxmi Niwas Palace is a grand palace hotel in Bikaner, built in the early 20th century. The hotel features lavish rooms, a spa, and a golf course. It also offers cultural experiences, like traditional music and dance performances.

What to Buy in Bikaner Division: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bikaneri Woollen Shawls

Bikaner is known for its high-quality woolen shawls, made by local artisans. Visitors can buy stylish and warm shawls as souvenirs or gifts.

Kundan Jewelry

Kundan jewelry is a traditional Rajasthani jewelry style, made of gold and precious stones. Visitors can find beautiful and unique pieces of Kundan jewelry in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Bikaner Division

Bikaner Division is steeped in tradition and culture, and visitors can witness and experience various customs and traditions.

Festivals

Bikaner Division hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the famous Desert Festival, which celebrates the local culture with music, dance, and camel races.

Dress Code

Visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Women are advised to cover their head and wear clothes that cover the knees.

Best Time to Visit Bikaner Division

The best time to visit Bikaner Division is during the winter season, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bikaner Division

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and beverages.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bikaner Division

Bikaner Division is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors are advised to take precautions, like keeping their valuables safe and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Bikaner Division

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent any health issues. Check with your doctor for any necessary vaccinations before traveling to Bikaner Division.

Getting to Bikaner Division: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bikaner Division is well-connected to major cities in India by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Bikaner Division

The nearest airport is Sanganer Airport in Jaipur, which is well-connected to major cities in India and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Bikaner Division

Bikaner is connected to major cities in Rajasthan and nearby states by bus. The Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation operates regular buses to Bikaner from nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private taxis or cars to travel to Bikaner Division from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Bikaner Division

Visitors can hire autos, taxis or take local buses to get around in Bikaner Division.

Where to Go Next from Bikaner Division: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer is a medieval town located about 330 km from Bikaner. The town is famous for its forts, temples, and havelis. Visitors can also explore the nearby Sam Sand Dunes.

Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is located about 330 km from Bikaner. The city is known for its forts, palaces, and markets. Visitors can also witness the traditional Rajasthani lifestyle and culture.
