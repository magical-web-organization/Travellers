Introduction to Sariska Reserve and National Park

Sariska Reserve and National Park is situated in the Jaipur Division of Rajasthan, Western India. It spans over an area of 866 km² and is known for its diverse flora and fauna. It is said to be home to India’s largest population of peafowl along with several other species of birds, wild boars, leopards, and tigers.

List of Places to Visit in Sariska Reserve and National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kankwadi Fort - Built by Jai Singh II, Kankwadi Fort is located within the park and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding hills.
  2. Sariska Palace - The palace was built in the early 19th century and is now converted into a hotel.

Natural Attractions of Sariska Reserve and National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Pandupol Temple - Located close to the park, this temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and is considered one of the most sacred places in the area.
  2. Siliserh Lake - Located on the outskirts of the park, this lake is famous for boating and bird watching.
  3. Jai Samand Lake - Another popular lake, located near the park, offers scenic views and boating facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sariska Reserve and National Park

  1. Kankwadi to Pandupol Trail - This trail takes you through the dense forests of the park and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  2. Sariska to Siliserh Lake Trail - This trail offers a chance to witness the rich flora and fauna of the park.

Best Things to Do in Sariska Reserve and National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Jeep Safari - Witness the majestic tigers, leopards, and other wildlife of the park on a jeep safari.
  2. Bird Watching - With over 200 species of birds, Sariska National Park is a bird lover’s paradise.
  3. Night Safari - Experience the thrill of a safari at night and witness the nocturnal animals in their natural habitat.
  4. Trekking - Sariska National Park offers numerous trekking options for adventure enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sariska Reserve and National Park

Day 1 - Arrive in Sariska and check-in to your hotel Day 2 - Morning safari in Sariska National Park, followed by an afternoon visit to Siliserh Lake Day 3 - Trekking in the park followed by a visit to Kankwadi Fort Day 4 - Jeep Safari in the park followed by a visit to Pandupol Temple Day 5 - Visit Jai Samand Lake and explore the surroundings Day 6 - Day trip to nearby temples and historical sites Day 7 - Depart from Sariska

Food and Drink Scene of Sariska Reserve and National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Gatte ki Sabzi - Chickpea flour dumplings cooked in a yogurt-based curry.
  2. Laal Maas - A spicy mutton curry, popular in Rajasthan.

Local Beverages

  1. Lassi - A popular yogurt-based drink, available in both sweet and savory options.
  2. Masala Chai - A spiced tea, popular all over India.

Where to Stay in Sariska Reserve and National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sariska Palace - Built over 200 years ago, this palace has been converted into a hotel offering an old-world charm experience.
  2. Tiger Den Resort - Located close to the park, this is a popular luxury resort for travelers.
  3. The Grand Hira Resort - Located on the outskirts of the park, this is a budget-friendly option for travelers.

What to Buy in Sariska Reserve and National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts - Rajasthan is known for its traditional handicrafts such as pottery, textiles, and jewelry.
  2. Sariska Wildlife T-Shirts - Get a souvenir t-shirt with the park’s logo on it.

Local Culture and Customs of Sariska Reserve and National Park

  1. Visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and historical sites.
  2. Rajasthan is known for its rich cultural heritage, so visitors are advised to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sariska Reserve and National Park

The best time to visit Sariska National Park is from October to June. The park remains closed during the monsoon season from July to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sariska Reserve and National Park

Do:

  1. Obtain a permit before entering the park.
  2. Keep the park clean and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  1. Feed the animals.
  2. Make noise while on a safari tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sariska Reserve and National Park

Visitors are advised to stay within designated areas and not venture out alone after dark. The park houses wild animals, and visitors are expected to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Sariska Reserve and National Park

Visitors are advised to carry necessary medications and insect repellents to avoid any health problems during their visit.

Getting to Sariska Reserve and National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sariska Reserve and National Park

  1. Jaipur International Airport - The nearest airport to Sariska National Park, situated at a distance of around 107 km.

Bus Stations Near Sariska Reserve and National Park

  1. Alwar Bus Stand - Located around 37 km from the park, it is well-connected to major cities in Rajasthan.

Other Options

  1. Private taxis can be hired to reach the park from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Sariska Reserve and National Park

Jeep safaris are the best and most popular way to explore the national park. Visitors can hire a jeep with a driver or book a safari tour in advance.

Where to Go Next from Sariska Reserve and National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Alwar - A historical city in Rajasthan, located around 37 km from Sariska National Park.
  2. Agra - Home to the iconic Taj Mahal, located around 230 km from Sariska National Park.

Conclusion

Sariska Reserve and National Park is a must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts and adventure seekers. With its rich flora and fauna, cultural heritage, and beautiful surroundings, it offers a unique experience for travelers looking to explore the beauty of Rajasthan.
