Introduction to Alwar

Nestled between the Aravalli hills and the Thar desert, Alwar is a hidden gem of Rajasthan, India. With its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty, the city attracts visitors from all over the world. Founded in 1770 CE by the Kachhwaha Rajput king Pratap Singh, Alwar has been ruled by various dynasties, including Mughals and Marathas, which has contributed to its diverse culture and architecture.

In addition to its historical importance, Alwar is an adventurer’s paradise, with many natural attractions and hiking and biking routes. The city is also known for its delicious cuisine, unique shopping experiences, and hospitable people.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, local culture, and travel tips in Alwar, Rajasthan, India.

List of Places to Visit in Alwar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • City Palace - Built-in 1793 by Maharaja Vinay Singh, this magnificent palace is a blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture. The palace houses a museum with a vast collection of historical artifacts, including weapons, paintings, and sculptures.
  • Bala Quila - This impressive fortress stands atop a hill overlooking the city. The fort was built by Hasan Khan Mewati in the 14th century and later captured by the Marathas and the Kachhwaha Rajputs.
  • Moosi Maharani ki Chhatri - This is a breathtaking monument constructed in memory of Maharaja Bakhtawar Singh’s wife. The monument is a fine example of Rajasthani architecture, decorated with intricate carvings and embellishments.
  • Alwar Museum - Situated within the city palace complex, this museum has a vast collection of rare manuscripts, paintings, and sculptures from different periods of Indian history.

Natural Attractions of Alwar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sariska Tiger Reserve - This is one of the premier national parks in India, spread over 800 square kilometers. It is home to a variety of wildlife, including tigers, leopards, wild boars, and antelopes. Visitors can enjoy jeep and elephant safaris, nature walks, and birdwatching.
  • Siliserh Lake - This man-made lake is a popular picnic spot and offers a breathtaking view of the Aravallis. Visitors can also enjoy boating and water sports.
  • Bhangarh Fort - This ruined fort is said to be haunted and is considered one of the most haunted places in India. It is an excellent destination for adventure lovers and history buffs.
  • Purjan Vihar - This is a beautifully landscaped garden spread over 4.5 hectares, featuring lush green lawns, dazzling fountains, and exotic flora.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alwar

Alwar offers many hiking and biking routes, some of which are:

  • Sariska - Thanagazi (15 km) - This scenic route takes visitors through the Sariska Tiger Reserve and the nearby village of Thanagazi, offering stunning views of the Aravallis.
  • Bala Quila - Tijara (25 km) - This rugged trail takes adventurers through the Bala Quila fortress and the rustic town of Tijara.
  • Pandupol - Siliserh Lake (30 km) - This route features challenging uphill climbs and exhilarating downhill descents, offering a unique view of the Siliserh Lake.

Best Things to Do in Alwar: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, history, or culture, Alwar offers something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Alwar:

  • Take a safari in Sariska Tiger Reserve - Discover the wildlife and natural beauty of Rajasthan’s most famous national park.
  • Visit the City Palace Museum - Explore the heritage of Alwar and Rajasthan through a collection of historical artifacts.
  • Shop for souvenirs at the local markets - Alwar’s markets are famous for their handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry.
  • Experience Rajasthani cuisine - Savor the flavors of Rajasthani cuisine, from spicy curries to sweet desserts.
  • Explore the haunted Bhangarh Fort - Venture into the ruins of Bhangarh Fort and experience the eerie atmosphere of one of the most haunted places in India.
  • Take a guided cycling tour - Discover Alwar’s hidden gems and scenic routes with a guided cycling tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alwar

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip to Alwar:

  • Day 1: Visit the City Palace and the Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a safari in Sariska Tiger Reserve.
  • Day 3: Visit Moosi Maharani ki Chhatri and explore the local markets.
  • Day 4: Take a guided cycling tour of the countryside.
  • Day 5: Trek to Bhangarh Fort.
  • Day 6: Visit Siliserh Lake and Purjan Vihar.
  • Day 7: Relax and indulge in Rajasthani cuisine in the city center.

Food and Drink Scene of Alwar

Must-Try Dishes

Alwar offers a variety of delicious and spicy Rajasthani cuisine. Some of the most popular dishes are:

  • Dal Bati Churma - This is a combination of savory dal, crispy Bati, and sweet Churma, a staple dish of Rajasthan.
  • Laal Maas - This fiery lamb curry is made with red chili paste and traditional Rajasthani spices.
  • Pyaz ki kachori - A flaky pastry filled with a spicy onion filling, this is the perfect snack for on-the-go travelers.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi - This sweet, thick, and creamy yogurt drink is a refreshing beverage popular throughout Rajasthan.
  • Chai - Known as “Masala Chai” in Rajasthan, this tea is brewed with milk, spices, and sugar.

  • Hotel Burja Haveli - This is a well-appointed hotel, located in the heart of the city, with easy access to all major attractions.

What to Buy in Alwar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alwar is famous for its handicrafts and traditional textiles. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy:

  • Jaipuri razai - These are traditional handmade quilts made from cotton and wool, famous all over Rajasthan.
  • Bandhani saree - These are beautiful and colorful sarees with traditional Rajasthani designs.
  • Stone sculptures - Alwar is famous for its beautiful stone sculptures, carved from marble and sandstone.

Local Culture and Customs of Alwar

Alwar has a rich and diverse culture, with influences from various dynasties that have ruled the region. The people of Alwar are hospitable and friendly, and visitors are welcomed with open arms. Some of the customs and traditions of Alwar include:

  • Folk Music and Dance - Alwar has a vibrant tradition of music and dance, with performances given at weddings and festivals.
  • Festivals - Alwar celebrates various festivals throughout the year, including Diwali, Holi, and Teej. These festivals are a great way to experience the local culture and customs.
  • Attire - The traditional attire of Alwar reflects the region’s culture and heritage. Men wear dhoti-kurta, while women wear colorful sarees and lehenga-choli.

Best Time to Visit Alwar

The best time to visit Alwar is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable. The temperatures during this period range between 10 to 25 degrees Celsius, making it the ideal time for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alwar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions - Alwar is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to dress and behave appropriately.
  • Try the local cuisine - Alwar is famous for its delicious Rajasthani cuisine, and visitors should sample as many dishes as possible.
  • Engage with the locals - The people of Alwar are friendly and hospitable, and visitors should take the time to interact with them.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes - Visitors should avoid wearing revealing clothes, as it is considered disrespectful and inappropriate.
  • Disrespect religious sites and monuments - Alwar has several religious sites and monuments, and visitors should show respect while visiting them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alwar

Alwar is a safe city, with low crime rates. Visitors should, however, take usual precautions and avoid traveling alone at night to unfamiliar areas.

Health in Alwar

Visitors should take usual precautions while traveling to Alwar, including drinking bottled water, avoiding street food, and carrying a medical kit.

Getting to Alwar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alwar

The nearest airports to Alwar are:

  • Indira Gandhi International Airport, Delhi - located around 136 km from Alwar
  • Jaipur International Airport, Jaipur - situated around 150 km from Alwar

Bus Stations Near Alwar

Alwar is well connected to major cities in Rajasthan by bus. There are regular bus services between Alwar and Jaipur, Delhi, and other cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Alwar by train. Alwar railway station is located on the Delhi-Jaipur rail route, and there are several trains that connect Alwar to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Alwar

Alwar has a well-established public transportation system consisting of buses and auto-rickshaws. Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Alwar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alwar is located in the heart of Rajasthan, and there are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore. Here are some of the nearby destinations:

  • Jaipur - The Pink City of India, famous for its historical sites, architecture, and traditional shopping.
  • Pushkar - This holy city is famous for its Brahma Temple and Pushkar Lake, which is surrounded by 52 ghats.
  • Agra - This city is home to the iconic Taj Mahal, as well as several other historical and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Alwar is a hidden gem of Rajasthan, with something to offer every traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, adventure, or cuisine, Alwar has it all. From exploring the majestic City Palace to trekking to the haunted Bhangarh Fort, Alwar promises to be an unforgettable experience.
