Introduction to Bhandarej

Located in the Jaipur Division in Rajasthan, Bhandarej is a small village with a rich cultural heritage. This serene hamlet is situated at the foot of the Aravalli hills, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Bhandarej is also known for its ancient temples, forts, and palaces, making it a must-visit destination for history buffs, too.

List of Places to Visit in Bhandarej

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhandarej Baori: This magnificent step well was built in the 18th century by the local rulers, and has well-preserved paintings, carvings, and sculptures on its walls.

  • Khatushyam Temple: A famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, Khatushyam Temple is a must-visit destination for those looking to explore the spiritual side of Bhandarej.

  • Bhandarej Fort: Built in the 11th century, Bhandarej Fort is a historic fortification that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Bhandarej: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kalka Mata Temple: Nestled in the panoramic Aravalli Hills, Kalka Mata Temple is a popular hiking destination, offering spectacular views of the valley below.

  • Sariska Tiger Reserve: Just a short drive from Bhandarej, Sariska Tiger Reserve is home to tigers, leopards, and a variety of other wildlife species.

  • Bhandarej Lake: Surrounded by lush green hills and forests, Bhandarej Lake is a peaceful destination for a picnic or a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhandarej

  • Aravalli Hills Trail: This path takes you through the picturesque Aravalli hills, where you can enjoy stunning views of the valley and wildlife of the region.

  • Bhandarej to Kalka Mata Temple Trek: This trek starts from Bhandarej and leads up to the Kalka Mata Temple, offering scenic views of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Bhandarej: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Safari: Explore the beautiful village of Bhandarej atop a camel, and relish the rustic charm of the region on this one-of-a-kind tour.

  • Bird Watching: With an abundance of flora and fauna, Bhandarej is a great destination for bird watching.

  • Village Walk: Take a walk through the village and interact with locals to understand their customs, traditions, and way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhandarej

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Baati Churma: A popular dish of Rajasthan, this is a combination of lentils and wheat bread served with clarified butter.

  • Laal Maas: A fiery, red meat curry made with mutton or lamb and a host of Indian spices.

  • Gatte ki Sabzi: A vegetarian dish made from gram flour, yogurt, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A traditional North Indian drink, Lassi is a yogurt-based beverage that is served in earthen pots.

  • Masala Chai: A spiced tea, Masala Chai is a popular beverage made by simmering tea, milk, and spices together.

Where to Stay in Bhandarej: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Castle Bhandarej: Located in the heart of the village, Castle Bhandarej offers comfortable rooms that give visitors a taste of authentic Rajasthan.

  • The Farm: Spread over a 70-acre farm, this resort is perfect for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

  • Bhandarej Haveli: A restored 200-year-old haveli, Bhandarej Haveli offers visitors a glimpse into the royal past of Rajasthan.

What to Buy in Bhandarej: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blue pottery: A traditional art form of Rajasthan, the blue pottery of Bhandarej is a must-buy for those looking to take home some local handicrafts.

  • Miniature paintings: Rajasthan is famous for its intricate miniature paintings, and Bhandarej is no exception.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhandarej

Bhandarej is a traditional village where people still follow age-old customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting the temples and other religious sites. Taking your shoes off before entering places of worship is a must. Visitors should also obtain permission before photographing locals.

Best Time to Visit Bhandarej

The best time to visit Bhandarej is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhandarej

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting the temples and other religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or vandalize any cultural/historical site.
  • Don’t get involved in drug abuse.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhandarej

  • Bhandarej is a safe village to travel around, but visitors should take standard precautions like avoiding unlit streets and deserted areas.
  • Be wary of touts.

Health in Bhandarej

  • Visitors should drink boiled or bottled water to avoid any water-borne diseases.
  • Consult your doctor before visiting the village.

Getting to Bhandarej: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bhandarej

Jaipur International Airport is the closest airport to Bhandarej, located about 70 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bhandarej

The closest bus station to Bhandarej is in Dausa, located around 30 km away.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi from Jaipur or Dausa to reach Bhandarej.

How to Get Around in Bhandarej

Visitors can easily explore Bhandarej on foot or hire a taxi or a bicycle to move around.

Where to Go Next from Bhandarej: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip from Bhandarej to the following places:

  • Jaipur: The Pink City of Jaipur is located just 70 km from Bhandarej and is home to magnificent palaces, forts, and museums.

  • Agra: The world-famous Taj Mahal is located in Agra, just a 5-hour drive from Bhandarej.

Conclusion

Bhandarej is a charming village in Rajasthan that is worth a visit for history buffs, nature enthusiasts, and anyone who’s looking for an authentic experience of North India.
