Mulshi, a serene and breathtakingly beautiful destination, is situated in South Maharashtra, in Western India. This quaint location is famous for its stunning landscapes, lush flora and fauna, and its rich culture and heritage. It is a perfect getaway for adventure enthusiasts, nature lovers, and those seeking to disconnect from the hustle and bustle of city life. In this comprehensive travel guide to Mulshi, we will provide you with all the necessary information to plan a perfect trip to this scenic destination.

Introduction to Mulshi

Mulshi is located about 40 kilometers from Pune, and about three hours drive from Mumbai, the financial capital of India. Mulshi is known for its serene lakes, breathtaking scenery, and ancient temples. This destination is perfect for peace-seekers who want to escape from the city’s hustle and bustle. Mulshi hosts a pleasant climate throughout the year, making it a year-round getaway destination.

List of Places to Visit in Mulshi

Mulshi has plenty of attractions for visitors to enjoy. Below is a list of the best places to visit in Mulshi.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mahadev Temple

The Mahadev Temple in Mulshi is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is located on the banks of Mulshi Lake, providing a breathtaking view of the lake and hills. The architecture of the temple is alluring, with intricate carvings and designs that reflect the rich heritage of India.

Mulshi Dam

The Mulshi Dam is a well-known tourist spot in Mulshi. This iconic dam is responsible for the formation of the Mulshi Lake, which is one of the largest man-made lakes in Western India. The lake attracts a considerable number of tourists every year because of its stunning surroundings and the mesmerizing view of the hills.

Kailasheshwar Temple

The Kailasheshwar Temple is located near the Mulshi dam, and it is considered one of the holiest places in Maharashtra. This temple is famous for its ancient architecture and amazing sculptures. The temple hosts a grand celebration during Mahashivratri.

Natural Attractions of Mulshi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mulshi Lake

As stated earlier, Mulshi Lake is a reservoir that is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. The lake is surrounded by lush green hills, which makes the view from the lake more beautiful. Visitors can engage themselves in various water sports available in the area, including kayaking, boating, and fishing.

Tamhini Ghat

Tamhini Ghat is a mountain pass located in the Sahyadri range of Maharashtra. This iconic mountain pass is known for its scenic beauty and attracts a large number of visitors. Tamhini Ghat is renowned for its cascading waterfalls and dense forests that make treks more adventurous.

Mulshi Waterfall

Mulshi Waterfall is another stunning attraction located near Mulshi Lake. The waterfall offers a stunning view, especially during monsoon season. It is an ideal site for treks and nature walks, where you can feel the fresh air and witness the awe-inspiring beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mulshi

Mulshi has a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the opportunity to explore the scenic surroundings on foot or on a bike.

  • Mulshi-Tamhini Road

Best Things to Do in Mulshi: Activities and Tours

Bird Watching

Mulshi is home to various species of rare and migratory birds. Visitors can indulge in Bird watching and explore the diverse flora and fauna during their trip. Visiting the Bhira Bird Sanctuary is an experience to never forget as it offers a vast number of bird species.

Water Sports

Water sports are also very popular in Mulshi. Visitors can participate in kayaking, river rafting, paddle boating, or jet skiing. You can also go swimming or fishing in the lake.

Outdoor Activities

Adventure enthusiasts can indulge themselves in a range of outdoor activities in Mulshi, including trekking, camping, mountain climbing, and rock climbing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mulshi

Day 1: Visit Mulshi Dam and Mulshi Lake for dawn and trek to the nearby Tungi fort.

Day 2: Trek to Tamhini Ghat and experience the scenic beauty of waterfalls and dense forests.

Day 3: Explore the rich culture and ancient architecture of Mahadev Temple and Kailasheshwar Temple.

Day 4: Visit Devkund Waterfall and enjoy trekking to the waterfall through the dense forest areas.

Day 5: Visit the Bhira Bird sanctuary and get a chance to view a wide variety of birds.

Day 6: Indulge in kayaking or river rafting in Mulshi Lake.

Day 7: Visit the local market and buy souvenirs and locally made products to bring back home.

What to Buy in Mulshi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can buy a range of locally made items in Mulshi, such as handcrafted pottery, traditional jewelry, and handwoven fabrics. You can also buy local spices and snacks, such as bhakarwadi and chikki, as souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mulshi

The local culture of Mulshi is diverse and colorful, reflecting its natural beauty and historical heritage. The locals are welcoming and gracious, and visitors to Mulshi should respect their customs and traditions of the area. The area has a significant influence of the Marathi culture, which is also reflected in their food, clothing, and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Mulshi

The best time to visit Mulshi is between September and June. During this period, the area experiences pleasant weather, which is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mulshi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and surroundings.
  • Dress appropriately, covering your shoulders and knees while visiting temples and other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the surroundings.
  • Disrespect the local culture or religion.
  • Engage in reckless behavior during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mulshi

Mulshi is a safe and secure destination with low crime rates. Visitors should take general safety precautions while exploring the area.

Health in Mulshi

Visitors should consult their doctor before traveling to Mulshi and take the necessary health precautions. Visitors should also carry mosquito repellent to avoid infections caused by mosquito bites.

Getting to Mulshi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mulshi

The nearest airport to Mulshi is Pune airport, which is about 60 kilometers (37 miles) away. The Mumbai airport is about 147 km (91 miles) away.

Bus Stations Near Mulshi

Mulshi is well connected by bus to cities like Pune and Mumbai. Visitors can take state-run buses or privately operated buses to reach Mulshi.

Other Options

Visitors can also take cabs or drive to Mulshi, which is a scenic and delightful experience.

How to Get Around in Mulshi

Visitors can take a cab, rent a bike or car to explore the local surroundings. it is also a great idea to trek or hike for short distances.
