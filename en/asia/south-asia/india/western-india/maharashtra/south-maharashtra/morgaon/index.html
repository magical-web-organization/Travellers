Introduction to Morgaon

Morgaon is a small village situated in the Baramati Taluka, Pune district of Maharashtra, India. Known for its ancient temples and significant historical structures, Morgaon is a perfect place to visit if you want to learn about India’s rich history and culture. With its scenic beauty, traditional cuisine, and warm hospitality, Morgaon welcomes visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Morgaon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mayureshwar Temple - Dedicated to Lord Ganesha, this ancient temple is one of the Ashtavinayak temples of Maharashtra. The temple is believed to be the first of the eight temples of Lord Ganesha, and it is said to have been constructed during the Peshwa period.

  • The Morgaon Palace - The Morgaon Palace is a significant historical landmark in Morgaon, which now serves as a museum. The palace features unique architectural styles and displays a collection of artifacts, including previously worn royal clothes, gold, silver, and other valuable items.

  • The Chintamani Jain Temple - A magnificent Jain temple located in Morgaon, it is famous for its intricate and mesmerizing architecture. The temple is renowned for its large halls, beautiful murals, and ancient statues of Jain Tirthankars.

Natural Attractions of Morgaon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bhuleshwar Temple - This ancient temple is situated in the Bhuleshwar Hills and is known for its beautiful architecture, attractive sculptures, and intricate carvings. The temple attracts both devotees and tourists looking for scenic views.

  • The Morgaon Lake - A popular attraction among tourists, the Morgaon lake is a picturesque spot with crystal clear water, surrounded by greenery. The lake is perfect for boating, fishing and picnicking with friends and family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morgaon

  • Bhuleshwar Hills Trek - This hike up to the Bhuleshwar Hills provides stunning panoramic views of the surroundings, making it one of the best trekking destinations in Morgaon.

  • Morgaon to Jejuri Cycle Tour - A perfect route for a cycle ride, Morgaon to Jejuri is a popular biking trail that stretches 35kms. The route provides scenic views of the countryside and divine temple views.

Best Things to Do in Morgaon: Activities and Tours

  • Ashtavinayak Darshan Yatra - A popular tour among devotees, Ashtavinayak Yatra covers eight ancient temples of Lord Ganesha, including the Mayureshwar Temple in Morgaon.

  • Jejuri Cultural Tour - A unique and immersive experience, this tour provides visitors an opportunity to witness traditional culture, customs, and rituals of the Jejuri town.

Food and Drink Scene of Morgaon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vada Pav - A crispy potato fritter sandwiched between two slices of bread, Vada Pav is a popular street food in Maharashtra.

  • Misal Pav - Another staple of Maharashtrian cuisine, Misal Pav consists of a spicy gravy made of sprouted beans and a side of bread.

Local Beverages

  • Solkadhi - A refreshing, locally made drink, Solkadhi is made with coconut milk, kokum, and ginger, and it is said to have medicinal properties.

  • Pune Craft Beer - Pune offers a variety of craft beer options, with breweries and pubs offering brews, including wheat beer, pale ale, and IPA.

Where to Stay in Morgaon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mayureshwar Wildlife Sanctuary and Eco-Resort - Located near the Mayureshwar Temple, the resort is an ideal base to explore Morgaon’s surrounding wildlife and eco-tourism attractions.

  • Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation - This state-owned corporation offers a range of accommodation options, including guesthouses and resorts.

What to Buy in Morgaon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Morgaon Dolls - These handmade clay dolls are a unique souvenir and are widely popular among tourists.

  • Paithani Sarees - Available in different styles and colors, Paithani Sarees are a traditional Maharashtrian silk saree that is popular among women.

Local Culture and Customs of Morgaon

Morgaon offers a window into the customs, traditions, and lifestyle of rural Maharashtra. It’s essential to respect the local customs and dress conservatively when visiting religious sites. Hinduism is the prevalent religion in Morgaon, so visitors should avoid eating beef and other pork products.

Best Time to Visit Morgaon

The best time to visit Morgaon is from October to February when the weather is pleasant, making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morgaon

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

  • Try the local food, especially the street food.

Don’t:

  • Consume non-vegetarian food in public places.

  • Drink tap water. Stick to bottled water to avoid water-borne diseases.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morgaon

Morgaon is a safe destination for tourists, with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to keep a close eye on their surroundings and belongings.

Health in Morgaon

Morgaon has a primary healthcare center and a few medical dispensaries. It is recommended that visitors carry their medication and travel health insurance.

Getting to Morgaon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morgaon

The nearest airport to Morgaon is Pune International Airport, about 68 km away.

Bus Stations Near Morgaon

Baramati Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Morgaon, situated at a distance of 15 km.

Other Options

Cabs and cars can be hired from Pune to reach Morgaon.

How to Get Around in Morgaon

Auto-rickshaws, taxis and buses are the most common modes of transportation to get around Morgaon.

Where to Go Next from Morgaon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jejuri - Located 22 km away, Jejuri is known for its ancient temples and traditions.

  • Pune - The cultural capital of Maharashtra, Pune, is located around 70 km away from Morgaon and is famous for educational institutions, shopping, and scenic spots.

Conclusion

Morgaon is a unique destination that offers a diverse blend of scenic beauty, rich culture and traditions, and historical artifacts. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can discover the best places to visit, top activities, local cuisine, where to stay, and much more. Don’t forget to pack your essentials, carry your medication, and get ready for a memorable trip to Morgaon.
