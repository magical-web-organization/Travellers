Introduction to Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar is a small hilly town in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, India. It is famous for its scenic beauty, natural wonders, and ancient temples. Bhimashankar is a perfect weekend getaway destination for people looking for adventure and tranquility.

List of Places to Visit in Bhimashankar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bhimashankar is known for its cultural and historical significance. The town has a number of museums and historical places that give visitors a glimpse into its rich past.

  • Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Hanuman Lake
  • Ahupe Waterfall
  • Nagphani
  • Shiv Jyotirlinga Temple

Natural Attractions of Bhimashankar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bhimashankar is well-known for its natural beauty and breathtaking scenery. Visitors can indulge in many outdoor activities such as trekking, camping, and bird watching.

  • Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Nagphani
  • Bombay Point
  • Hanuman Lake
  • Bhimashankar Trek

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar is a haven for adventure enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. The town has a number of hiking and biking routes that offer a picturesque view of the Western Ghats.

  • Bhimashankar Trek
  • Hanuman Lake Trek
  • Bombay Point Trek
  • Bhimashankar to Khandas Trek
  • Bhimashankar to Rajguru Nagar Trek

Best Things to Do in Bhimashankar: Activities and Tours

Bhimashankar offers an array of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. From adventurous activities to cultural tours, there is something for everyone in Bhimashankar.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar has a rich cultural heritage that visitors can experience. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors can get a sense of the local culture and customs by interacting with them.

Best Time to Visit Bhimashankar

The best time to visit Bhimashankar is between November and March when the weather is pleasant, and the town is alive with festive activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhimashankar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated and carry enough water.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural surroundings.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.
  • Enter the Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary without appropriate permissions.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions while indulging in outdoor activities.

Health in Bhimashankar

Visitors are advised to carry a first-aid kit and necessary medications while visiting Bhimashankar. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water and avoid street food.

Bhimashankar is a hidden gem in Maharashtra, offering a perfect blend of nature and culture. With its scenic beauty and ancient temples, Bhimashankar is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a weekend getaway.
