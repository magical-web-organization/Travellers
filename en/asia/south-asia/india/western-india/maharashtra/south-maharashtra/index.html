Explore the Gems of South Maharashtra: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

South Maharashtra in Western India is a true gem filled with natural wonders, exciting outdoor adventures, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on an exciting excursion to explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in South Maharashtra.

Introduction to South Maharashtra

South Maharashtra is a region located in Western India, bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west and the Western Ghats on the east. The region boasts of a unique blend of cultures, as it has been influenced by the Portuguese, British, and Maratha rulers since ancient times. This stunning destination offers everything from serene beaches to majestic forts, from peaceful hill stations to bustling cities, making it a dream destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in South Maharashtra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Maharashtra is a land of rich cultural heritage, and there are several historical places and museums worth visiting in our travel guide.

  • (1) Shaniwar Wada Fort: This historical fort is located in Pune and was built in the 18th century. It is popular for its architectural excellence and the light and sound show that narrates the fort’s rich history.
  • (2) Ajanta and Ellora Caves: These caves are UNESCO World Heritage Sites, located near Aurangabad. They consist of ancient Buddhist, Jain, and Hindu temples, each with exquisite carvings and paintings, dating back to the 2nd century BC.
  • (3) Bibi ka Maqbara: This monument is located in Aurangabad and is known as the “Taj Mahal of the Deccan.” It was built by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb in memory of his wife and is an excellent example of Mughal architecture.
  • (4) Victoria Memorial: This museum is located in Mumbai and was built in memory of Queen Victoria. It displays several artifacts, paintings, and photographs, shining light on India’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of South Maharashtra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Maharashtra is known for its diverse natural beauty, featuring everything from gorgeous beaches to majestic hills.

  • (1) Lonavala and Khandala: These twin hill stations are located near Mumbai and Pune and are perfect weekend getaways. They are famous for their scenic beauty, lush greenery, and serene atmosphere.
  • (2) Matheran: The smallest hill station in India, Matheran, is located near Mumbai and features a peaceful atmosphere with dense forests and picturesque surroundings.
  • (3) Agonda Beach: This magnificent beach is located in Goa and one of the most picturesque in South Maharashtra, famous for its blue waters, silvery sands, and vibrant nightlife.
  • (4) Tarkarli Beach: This picturesque beach is located in Malvan, and is known for its crystal-clear waters and excellent snorkeling and scuba diving options.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Maharashtra

For adventure seekers, South Maharashtra offers several exciting hiking and biking trails, each offering incredible views of nature’s beauty.

  • (1) Rajmachi Trek: This moderate to difficult trek offers a chance to explore the rugged terrain of Sahyadri hills with lush valleys, breathtaking waterfalls, and green meadows.
  • (2) Prabalgad trek: Prabalgad trek is a popular trek, well known for its hills, scenic walks, dense forests, steep slopes, etc.
  • (3) Tapola: This biking trail in Tapola offers the most scenic bike ride with endless unpaved roads cutting through dense forests, small creeks and crossing some picturesque hamlets.

Best Things to Do in South Maharashtra: Activities and Tours

South Maharashtra has a plethora of fun-filled activities and tours, each offering a unique experience to visitors.

  • (1) Go on a heritage walk to explore the rich history of South Maharashtra with a local guide and understand the intricate details and stories behind the historical places.
  • (2) Indulge in a food tour to taste the local flavors of South Maharashtra, including spicy curries and sweet desserts.
  • (3) Enjoy an adventurous boat ride at Venna Lake in Mahabaleshwar to feel the adrenaline rush.
  • (4) Take a nature walk to explore the lush green valleys, picturesque waterfalls, and serene mountain ranges and experience the beauty of nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Maharashtra

Day 1 - Arrive in Mumbai and explore the city’s famous landmarks like Gateway of India, Elephanta Caves, and Victoria Terminus.

Day 2 - Head to Lonavala/Khandala and explore forts and attractions like Bhaja Caves and Lohagad Fort.

Day 3 - Visit the historic city of Aurangabad and explore the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Day 4 - Visit the iconic Bibi ka Maqbara and explore the mesmerizing beauty of Panchakki and Jama Masjid.

Day 5 - Head to Malvan and enjoy water sports at Tarkarli beach and explore the majestic Sindhudurg Fort.

Day 6 - Take a scenic drive to Mahabaleshwar and explore the breathtaking beauty of Pratapgad Fort, Lingmala Falls, and Venna Lake.

Day 7 - Visit the historical city of Pune and explore the Shaniwar Wada Fort, Dagdhusheth Ganpati Temple, and Aga Khan Palace.

Food and Drink Scene of South Maharashtra

South Maharashtra boasts of an excellent culinary scene, with a mix of traditional and modern cuisines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Vada Pav: one of Mumbai’s most popular street food that consists of a deep-fried potato dumpling served in a bread roll.
  • (2) Misal Pav: another popular street food from Mumbai that consists of sprouted beans cooked in a spicy gravy.
  • (3) Kolhapuri Chicken: a spicy chicken curry from Kolhapur, made with a blend of spices and served with bread or rice.
  • (4) Thali Peeth: a healthy breakfast dish made from a multi-grain flour mix, vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Kokum Sharbat: a refreshing drink made from Kokum fruit, consumed during summers to beat the heat.
  • (2) Sol Kadhi: a traditional drink from the Konkan region, made with coconut milk and Kokum.

Where to Stay in South Maharashtra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Maharashtra offers a range of accommodations, from luxurious hotels to backpacker hostels and homestays.

  • (1) Luxury Hotels: Taj Mahal Palace, Mumbai, The Leela Mumbai, and The Oberoi, Mumbai.
  • (2) Budget Hotels: Ginger Hotel, Pune, Hotel Sai Palace, Aurangabad, and Hotel Ramakrishna, Lonavala.
  • (3) Backpacker Hostels: The Backpacker’s Hive, Mumbai, Zostel Lonavala, and Wanderers Hostel, Pune.
  • (4) Homestays: The Arowana Woods Homestay, Mahabaleshwar, and Cocohut Homestay, Malvan.

What to Buy in South Maharashtra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Maharashtra offers several excellent shopping options, where you can purchase souvenirs and other local products.

  • (1) Kolhapuri Chappals: Kolhapuri leather chappals are famous worldwide and make for an excellent souvenir to bring home.
  • (2) Paithani Sarees: Paithani sarees are made of silk and feature intricate designs, making them one of the most prized possessions of Marathi women.
  • (3) Warli Art: Warli art is a unique art form from Maharashtra that features tribal images and patterns on fabric, making it an excellent art piece to adorn your living space.

Local Culture and Customs of South Maharashtra

South Maharashtra is known for its rich cultural heritage and unique customs. One such custom is the Ganesh festival, celebrated on a grand scale in the region, where idols of Lord Ganesha are installed in households and public places. The state is also famous for its Lavani and Tamasha dance forms, showcasing the region’s rich art and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit South Maharashtra

The best time to visit South Maharashtra is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant and comfortable, ideal for exploring the region’s natural and cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Maharashtra

Do:

  • (1) Try out the local cuisine and street food.
  • (2) Wear comfortable clothing and shoes, suitable for hiking and walking.
  • (3) Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • (4) Carry a camera to capture the region’s natural beauty and cultural treasures.

Don’t:

  • (1) Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • (2) Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • (3) Ignore the safety guidelines while indulging in adventurous activities.
  • (4) Travel alone in secluded areas or during night time.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Maharashtra

South Maharashtra is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions while traveling, such as avoiding secluded areas and keeping your valuables safe.

Health in South Maharashtra

South Maharashtra is a tropical region, and visitors are susceptible to several health-related issues like malaria, dengue, and sunstroke. It is advisable to carry necessary medication and consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to South Maharashtra: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Maharashtra is well-connected to major cities and towns via several transportation options.

Airports Near South Maharashtra

  • (1) Mumbai International Airport
  • (2) Pune International Airport
  • (3) Aurangabad International Airport

Bus Stations Near South Maharashtra

  • (1) Mumbai Central Bus Station
  • (2) Pune Central Bus Station
  • (3) Aurangabad Central Bus Station

Other Options

  • (1) Trains: South Maharashtra is connected to the major Indian cities and towns via trains.
  • (2) Taxis and Car Rentals: Taxis and car rentals are readily available for local transportation.

How to Get Around in South Maharashtra

Local transportation in South Maharashtra includes buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws. Self-drive car rentals and motorbikes are also available from trusted operators.

Where to Go Next from South Maharashtra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Maharashtra is well-connected to several nearby destinations and excursions, where you can extend your stay and explore further.

  • (1) Goa: Famous for its beaches, nightlife, and water sports, Goa is only a few hours away from South Maharashtra.
  • (2) Gujarat: Gujarat is known for its rich cultural heritage, palaces, and temples, only a few hours away from Aurangabad.
  • (3) Karnataka: Karnataka is famous for its historical temples, national parks, and hill stations, only a few hours away from Lonavala/Khandala.

Conclusion

South Maharashtra is a fascinating region of Western India, blessed with natural beauty, rich culture, and awe-inspiring historical places. From exploring picturesque beaches to trekking rugged hills to indulging in traditional cuisine, South Maharashtra has it all. Plan your trip to this beautiful destination, using our comprehensive travel guide, and take home memories that will last forever.
