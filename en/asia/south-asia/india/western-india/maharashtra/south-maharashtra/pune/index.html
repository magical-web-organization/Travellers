Introduction to Pune

Pune is a sprawling city in South Maharashtra, known for its rich cultural and historical heritage, lush greenery, and pleasant weather. It has emerged as a popular tourist destination in recent years, offering a plethora of attractions for travelers of all kinds. Pune is also famous for its educational institutions, leading to a vibrant student culture that permeates the city.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore everything that Pune has to offer, from culturally significant museums and historical sites, to breathtaking natural vistas and outdoor activities. We’ll also take a closer look at the best places to stay, the local cuisine, and provide insight into local customs and culture, making your travel experience in Pune an unforgettable one.

List of Places to Visit in Pune

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pune is home to several museums and historical sites that give visitors a window into the rich cultural history of the region. One must-visit attraction is the majestic Aga Khan Palace, which played a significant role in India’s freedom struggle during the British era. You can also visit the Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, which houses an impressive collection of ancient Indian artifacts and relics, including musical instruments, weapons, and textiles.

Another must-visit landmark is the historic Shaniwar Wada Fort, an 18th-century palace where the Peshwa rulers of the Maratha Empire resided. The fort has been beautifully preserved and restored, and visitors can explore its many intricate halls, courtyards, and gardens.

Natural Attractions of Pune: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pune’s lush green surroundings offer ample opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic vistas. One popular natural attraction is the scenic hill station of Lonavala, which is just a short drive away from Pune. Visitors can trek to places like Tiger’s Leap or enjoy a leisurely afternoon stroll through the many gardens and parks the area has to offer.

If you’re up for it, you can also try your hand at paragliding or zip-lining at the many adventure parks around Pune. Another great option is a visit to the serene Mulshi Lake, surrounded by the Sahyadri mountain range - a perfect spot for a picnic or a quiet afternoon by the water’s edge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pune

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will be spoilt for choice in Pune, thanks to its many scenic and challenging trails. One popular route is the Sinhagad Fort trek, which takes you through a dense forest and up to an ancient fort with breathtaking views of the city. Another great option is the Rajgad Fort trek, which is relatively easy and offers stunning panoramic views.

Mountain biking is also popular in the area, with several biking trails catering to different levels of difficulty. The route from Pune to Lavasa is one of the most scenic and popular trails, passing through dense forests, scenic villages, and offering glimpses of the region’s picturesque countryside.

Best Things to Do in Pune: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Pune also offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to different preferences. You can take a walking tour of the city’s historic areas, explore the vibrant markets for shopping and souvenirs, or even indulge in a spa treatment or two.

If you’re interested in learning more about Pune’s thriving food culture, taking a food tour is an excellent option. You can taste local delicacies, visit bustling street food markets, or even learn to cook traditional dishes at a local culinary school. Similarly, there are plenty of options for those seeking adventure, including hot air balloon rides, skydiving, and scuba diving in nearby coastal areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pune

A week in Pune can allow you to explore the highlights of the city and its surroundings at a leisurely pace. Here’s an itinerary you can follow:

  • Day 1: Explore the city’s historical sites, including Aga Khan Palace and Shaniwar Wada Fort.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Lonavala and explore its many gardens, parks, and viewpoints.
  • Day 3: Trek to Sinhagad Fort or Rajgad Fort.
  • Day 4: Visit the Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum and explore the surrounding area.
  • Day 5: Take a food tour of the city or visit a local culinary school for a cooking class.
  • Day 6: Enjoy outdoor adventures like paragliding or zip-lining at the nearby adventure parks.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind with a spa treatment or take a leisurely stroll around the city at your own pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Pune

No travel experience is complete without indulging in the local cuisine, and Pune’s food culture is a rich and flavorful one. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vada Pav: A popular street food snack made with a deep-fried potato patty served in a bun with chutney and spices.
  • Misal Pav: A spicy and flavorful curry made with sprouted beans, onions, and tomatoes, served with bread.
  • Mastani: A creamy and indulgent milkshake served with toppings like nuts, fruits, and ice cream.
  • Thali: A traditional Indian meal served on a large platter with different dishes, including rice, vegetables, lentils, and bread.

Local Beverages

  • Pune boasts a vibrant and thriving microbrewery scene, and a visit to the many craft breweries in the city is a must-do for beer lovers.
  • Another popular local beverage is sugarcane juice, which is available at street-side stalls in many parts of the city.
  • The city also has a thriving coffee culture, with several specialty coffee shops worth a visit.

Where to Stay in Pune: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pune has a range of accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences. Luxury hotels like the JW Marriott and the Westin Pune Koregaon Park offer opulent rooms and top-notch amenities, while more budget-friendly options like backpacker hostels and guesthouses are also available.

There are several areas to choose from when deciding where to stay; Koregaon Park and Camp are popular for their central location and lively atmosphere, while the nearby hill station of Lonavala offers a serene and peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Pune: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pune has several bustling markets and shopping areas where you can find unique souvenirs and locally made products to bring back home. The Laxmi Road Market is one of the most popular, offering a range of products like clothes, jewelry, handicrafts, and decor items at competitive prices.

Another great option is the Jangli Maharaj Road Market, known for its artisanal products like pottery, sculptures, and paintings. Casey’s Topshop, located in Koregaon Park, offers a selection of handmade clothing and accessories that are both comfortable and stylish.

Local Culture and Customs of Pune

Pune is known for its warm and welcoming locals, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the culture and customs of the region during their stay. One important custom to keep in mind is the act of removing footwear before entering someone’s home or a sacred space, a sign of respect and humility.

It’s also important to dress modestly in public places, especially when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events. When greeting someone, a simple ‘Namaste’ with folded hands is widely accepted and appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Pune

The best time to visit Pune is between October and February, when the weather is cool and pleasant. March to May can be quite hot and humid, while the monsoon season from June to September can make outdoor activities difficult. However, if you want to experience the lush greenery and scenic vistas of the region, the monsoon season can offer spectacular views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pune

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Pune:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take part in cultural events and festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi and Diwali.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a water bottle and sunscreen when exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Worry about language barriers - most locals can communicate in English.
  • Wear revealing or skimpy clothing in public places.
  • Take photographs in sacred or private spaces without permission.
  • Buy products made from protected or endangered species.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pune

Pune is generally a safe city for travelers, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid mishaps. Keep an eye out for pickpockets in crowded places and don’t carry large amounts of cash on you. Stick to well-lit and populated areas at night, and avoid venturing out alone.

Health in Pune

Pune has several quality hospitals and medical facilities, but medical emergencies can be costly for uninsured travelers. It’s always a good idea to carry travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, and be careful with food and water hygiene to avoid any gastrointestinal illnesses.

Getting to Pune: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pune

Pune has its own airport, the Pune International Airport, which offers both domestic and international flights. The airport is well-connected to major Indian cities, making it easy to fly into Pune from other parts of the country.

Bus Stations Near Pune

Pune is well-connected by bus to several major Indian cities. The Pune Bus Terminus offers regular bus services to destinations like Mumbai, Hyderabad, and Bangalore.

Other Options

Train travel is also available to Pune, with regular railway connections to major Indian cities. Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and app-based ride-sharing services like Ola and Uber are widely available in the city, making getting around easy and convenient.

How to Get Around in Pune

Getting around Pune is relatively easy with several transportation options available. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are available at every corner, while app-based ride-sharing services like Ola and Uber are popular and convenient options.

Pune also has a well-developed bus network, with several routes and services catering to different parts of the city. For those interested in cycling, the city also has a bike-sharing service called PEDL, which can be accessed through an app.

Where to Go Next from Pune: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pune’s central location makes it an ideal base for exploring other nearby destinations in Maharashtra. The hill station of Lonavala, the vineyards of Nashik, and the tiger reserve at Tadoba are all within easy reach from Pune.

Conclusion

Pune is a vibrant and engaging city, offering something for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re interested in cultural and historical attractions, outdoor adventures, or world-class cuisine, Pune has it all. The city’s warm and welcoming locals, coupled with its beautiful scenery and mild climate, make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Maharashtra.
