Introduction to Kamshet

Kamshet is a small town located in the Western Ghats of South Maharashtra, India. Nestled between the cities of Mumbai and Pune, Kamshet is renowned for its picturesque landscapes and thrilling adventure activities, attracting tourists and adventure enthusiasts from all over the world. Its pleasant climate, lush greenery, and the sprawling fields of paddy and sunflower make Kamshet an ideal weekend getaway, offering a perfect rejuvenation from the hustle-bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Kamshet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bedsa Caves – These 2000-year-old Buddhist caves are a perfect example of ancient Indian rock-cut architecture, and are located just 10 km from Kamshet.
  2. Tikona Fort – This 17th-century Hill Fort, located in the Mawal region of Maharashtra, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Shinde Wadi Hills – A popular trekking spot, these hills have a number of ancient temples and a lake, and provide panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Kamshet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bhairi Caves – Surrounded by lush greenery, these caves offer a unique trekking experience, with its natural tunnels and waterfalls.
  2. Pawna Dam – The scenic dam has become a popular spot for kayaking, boating, and camping.
  3. Bedse Caves – The ancient Buddhist caves are located amidst scenic vistas, making them a perfect location for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kamshet

  1. Maval Takve – A serene walk through the countryside, this route ends with a beautiful waterfall.
  2. Pavana Lake – A great trek for beginners, this trail offers amazing views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  3. Kondeshwar Waterfall – A 2 km trek through the woods, leading to a waterfall and natural swimming pool.

Best Things to Do in Kamshet: Activities and Tours

  1. Paragliding – Kamshet is one of the favorite paragliding sites in India, offering visitors the opportunity to fly high over the scenic Western Ghats.
  2. Camping – With its breathtaking natural landscape, Kamshet is a popular destination for camping lovers, offering a chance to spend a night under the stars.
  3. Hot Air Balloon Ride – The lush valley and serene countryside of Kamshet are a perfect location for hot air balloon rides.

Food and Drink Scene of Kamshet

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Misal Pav – A spicy curry made from sprouted legumes, served with bread.
  2. Kande Pohe – A breakfast dish made from flattened rice, onion and spices.
  3. Sabudana Khichdi – A popular, healthy breakfast dish made from tapioca pearls.

Local Beverages

  1. Kokam Sharbat – A refreshing drink made from dried kokam fruit, typically served with ice.
  2. Sugarcane Juice – A popular and healthy natural drink.

Where to Stay in Kamshet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Rangoli Retreat – A retreat-style hotel with great mountain views, perfect for a relaxing weekend getaway.
  2. Native Place – A guesthouse and adventure centre managed by India’s top paragliders, perfect for adventure enthusiasts.
  3. Lake View Resort – A resort located by the Pawna Lake, offering luxury camping, tree house rooms, and cottages.

What to Buy in Kamshet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hand-Woven Sarees – Unique handcrafted sarees woven by the local artisans, using traditional techniques.
  2. Chikki – A popular and traditional sweet made from jaggery and nuts.
  3. Kolhapuri Chappals – These handcrafted leather slippers are the perfect souvenir for shoe collectors.

Local Culture and Customs of Kamshet

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Dress appropriately while visiting temples and other religious places
  3. Avoid littering and maintain cleanliness in public places.

Best Time to Visit Kamshet

The best time to visit Kamshet is during the winter season from October to February. The weather is pleasant, and perfect for outdoor activities like trekking and paragliding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kamshet

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine
  2. Take part in adventure activities like paragliding and hiking.
  3. Spend time in nature, meditating and relaxing.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in any disrespectful behavior towards locals or their traditions.
  2. Litter or cause damage to natural habitats.
  3. Ignore safety precautions while taking part in adventure activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kamshet

Kamshet is a relatively safe tourist destination. However, it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions while travelling to new and unknown locations, especially when engaging in adventure activities.

Health in Kamshet

Ensure that you have taken the necessary vaccinations before travelling to Kamshet. Carry mosquito repellent and bottled water while exploring the town.

Getting to Kamshet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kamshet

  1. Pune International Airport – 54 km away from Kamshet
  2. Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport – 100 km away from Kamshet

Bus Stations Near Kamshet

  1. Lonavala Bus Stand – 15 km away from Kamshet
  2. Pune Bus Stand – 50 km away from Kamshet

Other Options

  1. Trains from Mumbai or Pune
  2. Taxis and Rental Cars

How to Get Around in Kamshet

The best way to get around Kamshet is by renting a car or scooter, which will give you the freedom to travel to all the popular places. Taxis are also available on hire.

Where to Go Next from Kamshet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Lonavala – This popular hill station is just 20 km away from Kamshet and is famous for its waterfalls, trekking trails, and dam.
  2. Khandala – A hill station located just 6 km from Lonavala, famous for its scenic views, hiking trails, and caves.

Conclusion

Kamshet is a hidden gem known for its adventure activities, scenic views, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a peaceful escape into nature, Kamshet has something for everyone. Make sure to check out this hidden gem on your next vacation to Maharashtra.
