Introduction to Bhandardara

Located in North Maharashtra, Bhandardara is a picturesque hill station surrounded by the Sahyadri mountain range. This serene destination is perfect for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in nature’s beauty. The cool and pleasant climate of Bhandardara makes it a popular choice for vacationers from nearby cities like Mumbai and Pune.

List of Places to Visit in Bhandardara

From culturally significant sites to natural wonders, Bhandardara has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bhandardara:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amruteshwar Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this ancient temple is a must-visit for history buffs and architecture lovers.
  • Kalsubai Peak: Known for being the highest peak in Maharashtra, Kalsubai is a popular trekking spot and offers breathtaking views of the lush greenery around.
  • Wilson Dam: Located near the Bhandardara lake, this dam is the main source of electricity and water for the surrounding region. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the dam and surrounding areas from the top.

Natural Attractions of Bhandardara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bhandardara Lake: A tranquil lake surrounded by the Sahyadri mountain range, it is perfect for boating and camping.
  • Randha Falls: A stunning waterfall located near Bhandardara, it is among the top natural attractions in the region.
  • Mount Kalsubai: A famous trekking destination with breathtaking views from the top, it is perfect for adventure enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhandardara

Bhandardara has numerous hiking and biking trails available for tourists of all levels of experience. Some of the best routes include:

  • Ratangad Fort Trek: A moderate trek showcasing the fort’s age-old architecture.
  • Mount Kalsubai Trek: A challenging trek for seasoned hikers which rewards with stunning panoramic views of the valley.
  • Wilson Dam to Arthur Lake Trek: An easy trek covering scenic locations such as Randha falls, Amruteshwar temple, and the Wilson Dam.

Best Things to Do in Bhandardara: Activities and Tours

Apart from trekking and enjoying nature, here are a few more things to do in Bhandardara:

  • Camping and stargazing at the lakeside.
  • Boating on Bhandardara Lake.
  • Visiting waterfalls and capturing candid moments like photography enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bhandardara

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Bhandardara:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival in Bhandardara, relax and enjoy the scenic beauty.
Day 2 Trek to Ratangad Fort, visit Amruteshwar Temple.
Day 3 Enjoy the sunset over the lake, boat ride.
Day 4 Visit Randha falls and the nearby villages.
Day 5 Trek to Mount Kalsubai, explore the flora and fauna.
Day 6 Visit the Wilson Dam, take a dip in Arthur Lake.
Day 7 Departure from Bhandardara.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhandardara

Bhandardara is known for its authentic Maharashtrian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vada Pav: a popular fast food-style sandwich filled with mashed potato fritters and chutney.
  • Sabudana Khichdi: a light breakfast dish made with sago, peanuts, and mild spices.
  • Puran Poli: a sweet flatbread stuffed with lentils and jaggery.

Local Beverages

  • Kokum Sharbat: a refreshing drink made from the fruit of the kokum tree, it helps to cool off on hot days.
  • Solkadhi: a drink made with coconut milk and kokum, it has a unique sour flavor.

Where to Stay in Bhandardara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bhandardara has accommodation options suitable for various budgets. Here are some ideal choices for a comfortable stay:

  • Anandvan Resort: A luxurious resort nestled amid the lush green forests of Bhandardara, with premium amenities and excellent customer service.
  • Yash Resort: An eco-friendly resort offering a peaceful retreat, with spacious rooms and stunning views of Mount Kalsubai.
  • MTDC Resort: Run by the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation, this budget-friendly resort provides comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and other basic amenities.

What to Buy in Bhandardara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bhandardara has several souvenirs and local products that make for perfect gift options. Some of the best things to buy in Bhandardara include:

  • Handmade bamboo crafts
  • Warli paintings
  • Organic honey
  • Tribal artisanal products

Local Culture and Customs of Bhandardara

Bhandardara has diverse cultures, with influences from Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. The Warli tribe, native to the region, has a unique culture and is known for its colorful, geometrically designed murals.

Best Time to Visit Bhandardara

The best time to visit Bhandardara is between October and February when the weather is pleasant, and the surroundings are green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhandardara

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry appropriate warm clothing during winters.
  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Venture out into the wilderness without a guide.
  • Harm or disturb wildlife and vegetation.
  • Enter restricted areas without prior permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhandardara

Bhandardara is considered a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and avoid wandering in secluded areas at night.

Health in Bhandardara

Visitors are recommended to get vaccinated against mosquito and insect-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue before traveling to Bhandardara.

Getting to Bhandardara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bhandardara is well-connected by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Bhandardara

The nearest airport to Bhandardara is the Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport in Mumbai, which is approximately 180 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bhandardara

Bhandardara is well connected by bus services from neighboring cities like Pune, Mumbai, and Nashik.

Other Options

One can also hire a taxi, self-drive by car or motorcycle, or take a train that stops at Kasara or Igatpuri, and then proceed to Bhandardara by road.

How to Get Around in Bhandardara

Bhandardara has minimal public transportation, and people usually prefer to explore the destination on foot. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for hire if required.

Where to Go Next from Bhandardara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations one can visit from Bhandardara, including:

  • Igatpuri: Known for its scenic beauty and famed for the Vipassana International Academy.
  • Shirdi: Known for the Shri Sai Baba temple, a significant pilgrimage site for devotees.
  • Nashik: Known for its scenic vineyards, ancient temples, and the Kumbh Mela festival.

Conclusion

Bhandardara is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by travelers. The serene surroundings, the tranquil lake, and the majestic mountains offer a perfect escape from the bustling cities nearby. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you in planning an unforgettable trip to Bhandardara.
