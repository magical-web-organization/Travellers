Introduction to Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary is located in North Maharashtra, India, and covers an area of 341 square kilometers. The sanctuary is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and its unique landscapes include deciduous forests, grasslands, and rocky terrain. The sanctuary is relatively unexplored compared to other sanctuaries in the region, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

When planning your trip to Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary, be sure to visit these places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shivneri Fort: This historic fort is located just outside the sanctuary and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area. The fort is considered one of the birthplaces of the Maratha Empire and was the childhood home of Chhatrapati Shivaji, the founder of the empire.
  • Gharapuri Island and Elephanta Caves: These UNESCO World Heritage sites are located just a short distance from the sanctuary and offer a glimpse into India’s rich cultural heritage. The Elephanta Caves are a series of cave temples dedicated to Lord Shiva and are known for their impressive rock-cut architecture.

Natural Attractions of Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pimpalgaon Joga Dam: The dam is located within the sanctuary and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests. Visitors can also enjoy boating and fishing here.
  • Harishchandragad Hill: This trek is a must-do for adventure enthusiasts as it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the sanctuary and the surrounding landscape. The trek to the top is moderate to difficult and takes approximately 4-5 hours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary offers a range of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Maasana Trek: This 12-kilometer trek takes visitors through dense forests and offers stunning views of the sanctuary and its surroundings.
  • Vallonne Trek: This route is ideal for casual trekkers and takes visitors to the top of the Vallonne Hill, offering panoramic views of the sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary can enjoy a range of activities and tours, such as:

  • Bird Watching: The sanctuary is home to a diverse range of bird species, making it a paradise for bird enthusiasts. Visitors can spot species such as Grey Hornbills, Cuckoos, and Barbets among others.
  • Jungle Safari: Visitors can take a jungle safari to explore the sanctuary and spot wild animals such as Leopards, Sloth Bears, and Indian Gazelles.

Food and Drink Scene of Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary is known for its simple but delicious food, which consists of locally sourced ingredients and spices. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Misal Pav: A popular Maharashtrian breakfast dish, consisting of a spicy sprouts curry served with bread.
  • Vada Pav: A popular street food, consisting of a potato fritter served in a bread bun.
  • Chicken Kolhapuri: A spicy chicken dish from the Kolhapur region, served with rice or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Kokum Sharbat: A refreshing drink made from Kokum fruit, which is native to the region.
  • Solkadi: A savory drink made from coconut milk and Kokum, usually served as an accompaniment to meals.

What to Buy in Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary, be sure to pick up some of these souvenirs as a reminder of your trip:

  • Warli Art: A unique and colorful art form that originated in the Warli tribe of Maharashtra.
  • Kokum Products: Kokum is a fruit that grows abundantly in the region, and products such as Kokum juice and candy make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

The people of Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary are warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Some important customs to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering a temple or shrine.
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary is during the winter months (November to February), when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anerdam Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Take precautions against mosquito bites, as the sanctuary is a breeding ground for mosquitoes.
  • Follow the instructions of the safari guides and station officials.
  • Carry a first aid kit and any necessary medication.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Feed wildlife or disturb their natural habitat.
  • Venture into the sanctuary without a trained guide.
