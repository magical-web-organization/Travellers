Introduction to Karjat

Karjat is a picturesque town located in the Raigad district of North Maharashtra, Western India. The town is situated at the foothills of the Sahyadri Mountain Range, and it is well-known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures and cultural landmarks.

Surrounded by lush greenery, majestic mountains, and cascading waterfalls, Karjat is a popular destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts and history buffs alike. The town’s winding rivers, scenic hiking routes and ancient temples make it an ideal destination for those looking for a peaceful getaway.

Karjat is also home to several Bollywood celebrities, who have built second homes here to escape the hectic city life of Mumbai. Despite being a popular tourist destination, Karjat has managed to preserve its local charm and culture.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Karjat, we will take you through the must-visit attractions, local customs, food and drink scene, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Karjat

Karjat has something for everyone, from nature lovers to history buffs. Here are some of the top places to visit in Karjat.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kondana Caves

The Kondana Caves are a group of 16 Buddhist rock-cut caves dating back to the 2nd century BC. The caves are situated at the base of a waterfall and are accessible via a scenic hike. The caves are also known for their carvings and sculptures of Buddhist deities.

Peth Fort

Peth Fort, also known as Kotligad Fort, is a hill fort situated at an altitude of 3100 feet above sea level. The fort was built during the Maratha Empire and offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Ulhas Valley

The Ulhas Valley is a scenic valley situated on the banks of the Ulhas River. The valley is home to several waterfalls, including the famous Bhivpuri Waterfall. The valley is also popular among hikers and trekkers.

Natural Attractions of Karjat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bhivpuri Waterfall

The Bhivpuri Waterfall is a scenic waterfall situated in the Ulhas Valley. The waterfall is accessible via a short hike and is a popular spot for picnics and swimming.

Matheran Hill Station

Matheran is a popular hill station situated 20 km from Karjat. The hill station is known for its scenic beauty, peaceful ambiance and toy train. The toy train is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers panoramic views of the surroundings.

Kondeshwar Temple

The Kondeshwar Temple is a 13th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is situated in a scenic location, surrounded by lush greenery and a waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Karjat

Karjat is known for its scenic hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the top routes to explore.

Kondana Waterfall Trek

The Kondana Waterfall Trek is a moderate-level trek that takes you through lush greenery, rice fields, and a jungle trail. The trek ends at the Kondana Caves and Waterfall.

Morbe Dam to Matheran Trek

The Morbe Dam to Matheran Trek is a challenging trek that takes you through a dense forest, waterfalls, and steep hills. The trek ends at the Matheran Hill Station.

Bhivpuri Waterfall Trek

The Bhivpuri Waterfall Trek is an easy-level trek that takes you through the Ulhas Valley, rice fields, and a scenic waterfall. The trek ends at the Bhivpuri Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Karjat: Activities and Tours

Karjat offers several activities and tours that are sure to make your stay memorable.

River Rafting

Karjat is home to several rivers and waterfalls, making it an ideal destination for river rafting. The Kundalika River, which is just a short distance from Karjat, is one of the top spots for river rafting.

Camping

Camping is a popular activity in Karjat, with several campsites offering tents, bonfires, and outdoor activities. The Ulhas Valley and the Morbe Dam are popular spots for camping.

Toy Train Ride

The toy train ride in Matheran is a must-do activity when in Karjat. The toy train passes through lush greenery, valleys, tunnels, and waterfalls, offering a unique experience to visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Karjat

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you explore the best of Karjat.

Day 1-2: Kondana Caves Trek and Peth Fort Exploration

On the first day, explore the Kondana Caves via a scenic hike. On the second day, explore the Peth Fort and its surrounding hills and valleys.

Day 3-4: Bhivpuri Waterfall Trek and River Rafting

On the third day, explore the Bhivpuri Waterfall trek via an easy hike. On the fourth day, go river rafting on the Kundalika River.

Day 5-6: Matheran Hill Station and Toy Train Ride

On the fifth day, explore the Matheran Hill Station and experience the toy train ride. On the sixth day, relax at your campsite and enjoy the scenic beauty of Karjat.

Day 7: Cultural Exploration

On the last day, explore the local culture of Karjat by visiting the Kondeshwar Temple and the Ulhas Valley.

Local Culture and Customs of Karjat

Karjat is known for its rich history and traditional customs. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Karjat

The best time to visit Karjat is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Karjat

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing
  • Damage or disturb natural surroundings
  • Interfere with local wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Karjat

Karjat is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors are advised to take precautions and not venture into isolated areas alone at night.

Health in Karjat

Karjat has several clinics and hospitals that offer medical assistance. Visitors are advised to carry their medications and travel insurance to avoid any unforeseen circumstances.

Getting to Karjat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Karjat

The nearest airport to Karjat is Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport in Mumbai, which is approximately 70 km away.

Bus Stations Near Karjat

Karjat is well-connected by bus to major cities in Maharashtra, including Mumbai and Pune.

Other Options

Karjat is also accessible by train, with regular trains from Mumbai and Pune.

How to Get Around in Karjat

Karjat can be explored on foot, but visitors can also hire cabs, autos, or bikes to explore the town.

Conclusion

Karjat is a hidden gem located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, offering scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural landmarks. Plan your trip to Karjat, enjoy the local cuisine, explore the natural beauty and immerse yourself in the local culture.
