Introduction to Kihim

Welcome to Kihim, a beautiful coastal town in North Konkan, Maharashtra, Western India. Located just a few kilometers from Alibaug, Kihim attracts visitors for its stunning beaches, natural beauty, and rich cultural history. Many visitors come for a day-trip from Mumbai, while others choose to stay overnight or even longer. With so much to see and do, Kihim is the perfect destination for a rejuvenating getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Kihim

There are plenty of attractions to visit in Kihim. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kanhoji Angre Samadhi: A historical monument dedicated to Kanhoji Angre, who was considered one of the greatest Maratha naval chiefs.
  • Karmarkar Museum: A collection of beautiful wooden statues and sculptures created by the famous Karmarkar family of artists.
  • Kolaba Fort: A 300-year-old fort situated on an island accessible by boat. It was built by Shivaji Maharaj to fortify the coast.

Natural Attractions of Kihim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kihim Beach: A peaceful beach surrounded by lush green trees that provide natural shade.
  • Phansad Wildlife Sanctuary: A beautiful sanctuary spanning over 163 sq. km., known for its rich flora and fauna.
  • Alibaug Beach: A popular beach located around 9 km away from Kihim, known for its beautiful sunsets and water sports activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kihim

Kihim has several hiking and biking routes for nature enthusiasts. Here are some popular trails:

  • Kihim to Alibaug: A scenic and easy 9 km route that offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Phansad Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: A moderate trek that takes around 4-5 hours and is ideal for bird watching.
  • Kankeshwar Temple Trek: A slightly challenging 8 km trek that offers a panoramic view of the Konkan region.

Best Things to Do in Kihim: Activities and Tours

From water sports to cultural tours, Kihim offers plenty of things to do. Here are some popular activities:

  • Water sports: Enjoy jet skiing, banana boat rides, and parasailing at Kihim Beach.
  • Cultural tours: Take a heritage walk and visit the historical monuments and temples of Kihim.
  • Wellness retreats: Indulge in yoga, meditation, and Ayurvedic spa treatments to rejuvenate your mind and body.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kihim

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your visit to Kihim:

Day 1-2: Relax on the Beach Day 3-4: Explore the Historical Monuments and Museums Day 5: Go for a Trek Day 6: Take a Day Trip to Alibaug Day 7: Indulge in a Wellness Retreat

Food and Drink Scene of Kihim

Food in Kihim is mostly coastal cuisine, with an emphasis on sea-food dishes. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bombil Fry: A crispy fried dish made of Bombay duckfish.
  • Sol Kadhi: A refreshing and tangy drink made of kokum and coconut milk.
  • Prawns Curry: A spicy and flavorful curry made of prawns and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink that you can find almost everywhere in Kihim.
  • Mango Drink: A sweet and tangy mango drink that is a seasonal delight.

Where to Stay in Kihim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kihim has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some popular options:

  • Radisson Blu Resort & Spa, Alibaug: A luxurious resort located near Kihim Beach.
  • Big Splash: A budget hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Kihim Beach House: A guesthouse near the beach, offering cozy rooms and delicious food.

What to Buy in Kihim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring back a piece of Kihim with these unique souvenirs:

  • Sea-Shell Crafts: Decorative products made of seashells, such as jewelry and photo frames.
  • Konkani Sarees: A traditional saree worn by women in the Konkan region.
  • Handmade Chikankari Textiles: Embroidered textiles made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Kihim

The local people of Kihim are welcoming and warm. Konkani is the main language spoken here. Visitors should take care to respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kihim

The best time to visit Kihim is between October and February when the weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities without any discomfort.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kihim

Do:

  • Carry sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Always carry water with you to stay hydrated.
  • Respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter on the beach and other natural areas.
  • Do not disturb the local wildlife.
  • Do not carry any plastic bags as they are not allowed on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kihim

Kihim is a relatively safe place, and there are no major security concerns. Visitors should be cautious while swimming in the sea as the currents can be strong in some areas.

Health in Kihim

It is advisable to carry insect repellent as there are mosquitoes in the area. Take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites as they can cause diseases like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Kihim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kihim

The nearest airport to Kihim is the Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport in Mumbai.

Bus Stations Near Kihim

Buses are available from Mumbai to Alibaug, which is located just 10 km from Kihim.

Other Options

One can also take a ferry from Mumbai to Alibaug and then take a taxi or bus to Kihim.

How to Get Around in Kihim

Getting around Kihim is easy with taxis and auto-rickshaws available for hire. Alternatively, you can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the town at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Kihim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you can visit from Kihim:

  • Murud Janjira: A historic fort located in a coastal village near Kihim.
  • Mandwa: A scenic location with a beautiful beach and an old fort.
  • Kashid: A serene beach town with clean sand and clear waters.

Conclusion

Kihim is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone - stunning beaches, cultural landmarks, and delicious food. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, culture buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Kihim has something to offer. So, pack your bags and head to Kihim for an unforgettable experience!
