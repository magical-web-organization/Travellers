Introduction to Kashid

Kashid is a small village in the North Konkan region of Maharashtra, India. Located just 30 km away from Alibag, this serene destination has become popular among tourists for its natural beauty, white sandy beaches, and lush green mountains. Kashid is an ideal location for those looking to relax and unwind away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Kashid

Kashid has something for everyone. From culturally important attractions to thrilling outdoor adventures, here is a list of places to visit in Kashid.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phansad Wildlife Sanctuary: This UNESCO-listed wildlife sanctuary is home to various species of birds, mammals, and reptiles.
  • Revdanda Beach Fort: A historical fort built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, Revdanda Beach Fort offers a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea and the Revdanda Beach.
  • Korlai Fort: Built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, Korlai Fort remains one of the most prominent historical attractions in Kashid to date.

Natural Attractions of Kashid: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kashid Beach: This 3 km stretch of pristine white sand beach is ideal for long walks, sunbathing or swimming in the sea.
  • Phansad Bird Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to various species of rare and migratory birds, such as the Malabar Trogon, Paradise Flycatcher, and Crested Serpent Eagle, among others.
  • Murud-Janjira Fort: A historic fort built in the 16th century standing tall in the middle of the sea, the Murud-Janjira Fort is a must-visit attraction in Kashid.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kashid

For those looking to explore the lush green mountains of Kashid, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from, such as the Kasid-Creek and the Kundalika River.

Best Things to Do in Kashid: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Kashid, from beach hopping to trekking to fort hopping. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Water Sports: Try out some exciting water sports like banana boat rides, jet skiing, parasailing, and water scootering on Kashid Beach.
  • Camping: Kashid is an excellent destination for camping, and many campgrounds are available for booking.
  • Fishing Trips: Take a fishing excursion and explore the fishing villages in the vicinity of Kashid.

Food and Drink Scene of Kashid

The food culture in Kashid is primarily influenced by the traditional Konkani cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Malvani Fish Curry, a popular seafood dish made with a blend of coconut, spices and fish.
  • Sol Kadhi, a refreshing Konkani soup or drink made with kokum fruit and coconut milk.
  • Vada Pav, a street food snack consisting of a deep-fried potato patty served inside a bread bun.

Local Beverages

  • Cashew Feni, a traditional Goan alcoholic drink made with fermented cashew apple juice.
  • Kokum Sharbat, a homemade non-alcoholic drink made with kokum fruit.

Where to Stay in Kashid: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kashid has a range of accommodation options for all types of travelers, from budget guesthouses to luxury resorts. Here are some of the recommended places to stay in Kashid:

  • Kashid Beach Resort: Located near the beach and offering well-appointed rooms and fantastic sea views.
  • Prakruti Resorts: A top-tier resort located amidst valleys and mountains of the Western Ghats.
  • Palm Beach Resort: An eco-friendly resort with excellent amenities, a private beach, and a stunning view of the Arabian Sea.

What to Buy in Kashid: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kashid is known for several locally made items that you can take home as a souvenir. Here are some of the things you should add to your shopping list:

  • Cashew Nuts: Known for producing high-quality cashew nuts, Kashid has many local shops selling roasted and flavored cashews.
  • Fish Pickles: These delicious pickles, made with fresh fish caught from the Arabian Sea, can make for an excellent edible souvenir.
  • Bamboo Handicrafts: The village of Kashid has many shops selling handcrafted items made from bamboo, such as baskets and mats.

Local Culture and Customs of Kashid

The locals in Kashid have a rich cultural heritage, and their customs are deeply rooted in the traditional Konkani culture. Visitors should be aware of the local customs and practices and respect them.

Best Time to Visit Kashid

The best time to visit Kashid is between October and February when the weather is pleasant, and the sea is calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kashid

To have a hassle-free experience in Kashid, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry light and breathable clothing, sunscreen, and insect repellent.
  • Keep your environment clean by disposing of trash properly.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink in public.
  • Wear excessive jewelry or valuable items while on the beach.
  • Venture out alone at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kashid

Overall, Kashid is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should be cautious while swimming in the sea, and it is always recommended to swim only at the designated areas marked by lifeguards. Travelers should also be vigilant while traveling alone at night and avoiding isolated areas.

Health in Kashid

Visitors should take basic health precautions such as drinking plenty of water, eating hygienic food, and carrying basic medicines such as antacids, painkillers, and mosquito repellent.

Getting to Kashid: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kashid can be accessed by various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Kashid

  • Mumbai International Airport: Located approximately 140 km from Kashid, Mumbai International is the nearest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Kashid

  • Alibag Bus Stand: Located just 30 km from Kashid, Alibag Bus Stand is the nearest bus station.

Other Options

  • Taxis and private cars are readily available for hire from Mumbai and Alibag.

How to Get Around in Kashid

Kashid is a small village, and walking or cycling is the best way to explore the area. Taxis and bike rentals are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Kashid: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations or excursions to visit after Kashid:

  • Alibag: A popular beach destination in Maharashtra, known for its fort and pristine beach.
  • Murud: Home to the Murud-Janjira Fort, Murud is a small town located approximately 45 km from Kashid.

Conclusion

Kashid is an excellent destination that offers serene beaches, lush green forests, and a rich cultural heritage. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are ready to explore Kashid and have an enjoyable and safe experience.
