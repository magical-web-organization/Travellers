Introduction to Bordi

Located in North Konkan on the Western coast of Maharashtra, Bordi is a quaint little village that is a hidden gem of the state. It is known for its scenic beaches, lush greenery, and serene atmosphere, making it the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Bordi is an untouched paradise that has recently gained popularity among travelers who seek solace and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Bordi

Bordi may seem small, but it has a lot to offer to its visitors. Here are the top attractions in Bordi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Asavli Dam
  • Bahrot Caves
  • Devrai Art Village
  • Dahanu Fort

Natural Attractions of Bordi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bordi Beach
  • Kalpataru Botanical Gardens
  • Dahanu Beach
  • Kaldurg Fort

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bordi

Bordi is home to some of the best trekking and cycling trails in the Konkan region. The most popular routes include:

  • Asavli Dam Trek
  • Bordi to Dahanu Beach Cycling Trail
  • Kalpataru Botanical Gardens Trail
  • Bahrot Caves Trek

Best Things to Do in Bordi: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Warli Tribal Village
  • Take a boat ride in Dahanu
  • Go for a beach walk and sunrise/sunset watching
  • Try your hand at harvesting and plucking fruits at fruit farms

Week-Long Itinerary for Bordi

Day 1 - Arrival and relax at the resort. Day 2 - Trek to the Asavli Dam and explore the Bahrot Caves. Day 3 - Cycling to Dahanu Beach and enjoy the sunset. Day 4 - Visit the Warli Tribal Village and witness their customs and traditions. Day 5 - Enjoy the scenic views of Bordi by taking a boat ride in Dahanu. Day 6 - Visit the Devrai Art Village and shop for tribal art and crafts. Day 7 - Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Bordi

Bordi has a unique cuisine that is a blend of Maharashtrian and Gujarati flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thepla, a Gujarati flatbread made with wheat flour, fenugreek leaves, and spices.
  • Kothimbir Vadi, a savory snack made with coriander, gram flour, and spices.
  • Ukadiche Modak, a traditional Maharashtrian sweet dumpling filled with jaggery and coconut.

Local Beverages

  • Kokum Sharbat, a refreshing drink made from Kokum fruit.
  • Solkadi, a tangy and spicy drink made from coconut milk, ginger, and kokum.

What to Buy in Bordi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bordi is famous for its Warli art and crafts. You can shop for these local products at the Devrai Art Village. Other things to buy include:

  • Hand-woven baskets and bags made from natural fibers.
  • Traditional Warli paintings and artifacts.
  • Fruits and vegetables grown in the Dahanu region.

Local Culture and Customs of Bordi

Bordi is predominantly a tribal region that is home to the Warli tribe. They have their own customs and practices deeply rooted in nature, and you can witness their way of life by visiting their villages.

Best Time to Visit Bordi

The weather in Bordi is pleasant throughout the year, but the best time to visit is from November to February when the temperature is at its lowest. The monsoon season (June to September) should be avoided due to heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bordi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and shop for Warli art.
  • Stay hydrated and carry sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Pluck fruits without permission.
  • Litter on the beaches and near natural sites.
  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting tribal villages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bordi

Bordi is a safe destination, but it is always advisable to take precautions while traveling. Keep your valuables safe and secure, and avoid venturing out alone at night.

Health in Bordi

Bordi is relatively clean and hygienic, but it is advisable to carry your own medication for emergencies. Stay hydrated and avoid consuming street food.

Getting to Bordi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bordi

The nearest airport to Bordi is Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport in Mumbai, which is around 140 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bordi

Bordi is connected to major cities in Maharashtra via road. Regular buses operate from Mumbai, Pune, and Nashik to Bordi.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or drive down to Bordi from Mumbai, Pune, or Nashik.

How to Get Around in Bordi

The best way to explore the beauty of Bordi is on foot or by bicycle. You can also hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw to get around.
