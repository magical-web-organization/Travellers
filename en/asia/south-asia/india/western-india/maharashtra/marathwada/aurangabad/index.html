Aurangabad, a city located in the Marathwada region of Maharashtra, is a popular tourist destination among history buffs, adventure seekers, and foodies. It is known for its captivating historical and cultural attractions, jaw-dropping natural vistas, and vibrant local cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect itinerary and fully immerse yourself in the magic of this historic city in Western India.

Introduction to Aurangabad

Aurangabad, also known as the “City of Gates,” is steeped in history dating back to medieval times. Named after the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb, who made it the capital of his kingdom in the 17th century, the city boasts a rich cultural heritage that has been preserved through its numerous monuments, temples, and historical sites. Additionally, the city is also blessed with natural beauty and is surrounded by the Sahyadri Mountains that offer some incredible outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Aurangabad

Aurangabad is home to several culturally, historically, and natural landmarks such as:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Aurangabad Caves: These 12 rock-cut Buddhist shrines, dating back to the 6th and 7th centuries, are renowned for their intricate architecture, carvings, and serene environment.
  2. Bibi Ka Maqbara: Inspired by the Taj Mahal, this magnificent edifice built by Emperor Aurangzeb’s son in memory of his mother is a must-visit site in Aurangabad.
  3. Panchakki: An ancient watermill complex dating back to the 17th century that was used to grind grain for the pilgrims visiting the nearby dargah of Baba Shah Muzaffar.
  4. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Museum: This museum, named after the great Maratha ruler, displays a fascinating collection of artifacts and exhibits relating to the history of Aurangabad and Maharashtra.
  5. Salim Ali Lake and Bird Sanctuary: Located in the heart of Aurangabad city, this nature reserve is home to over 200 species of birds, making it a paradise for bird watchers.

Natural Attractions of Aurangabad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lonar Crater: This unique and awe-inspiring natural crater lake was formed by a meteor impact on Earth some 50,000 years ago and is a popular site for trekking and bird watching.
  2. Khuldabad: A peaceful hill town located around 25 km from Aurangabad, it is famous for its scenic beauty and historical significance as the burial place of several Sufi saints.
  3. Ellora Caves: These 34 monasteries and temples, built between the 6th and 10th centuries, are renowned for their unique architecture, intricate carvings, and exquisite beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aurangabad

Aurangabad is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Some popular routes include:

  • Barapathar Trek: A challenging 12 km trek to Barapathar, the summit of the Sahyadri Mountains, that rewards with stunning views of the western ghats.
  • Ajanta-Ellora Cycling Trail: A 100 km trail that starts from Aurangabad and takes you through the countryside, past small villages, farms, and iconic landmarks such as the Ellora Caves and the Ajanta Caves.
  • Lonar Crater Trek: A 5 km trek around the Lonar crater that is surrounded by thick forests and offers some of the most spectacular views of the Sahyadri Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Aurangabad: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above-listed attractions, here are some other must-try things to do in Aurangabad:

  • Explore the bustling bazaars and markets of Aurangabad and take back some souvenirs.
  • Sample the local cuisine, which is a mix of Mughal and Marathi flavors, at some of the city’s best restaurants.
  • Take a guided tour of the city on foot or on a bicycle, and learn about its fascinating history and rich culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aurangabad

A week-long trip to Aurangabad gives you ample time to explore the city and its surroundings. Here is a recommended itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Aurangabad

Aurangabad’s cuisine is a confluence of Mughlai, Maharashtrian, and Hyderabadi influences. Here are some must-try dishes when you visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Naan Qalia: A Mughal-inspired meat dish made with goat or lamb cooked in a yogurt-based curry and topped with crispy fried onions.
  • Hyderabadi Biryani: A fragrant and flavorful biryani dish made with basmati rice and spiced meat.
  • Sheer Kurma: A special Eid dessert made with milk, vermicelli, and nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A refreshing drink made with milk, almonds, and spices like saffron and cardamom usually had during the Holi festival.
  • Khajoor Shake: A sweet, creamy drink made from dates, milk, and ice cream.

Local Culture and Customs of Aurangabad

Aurangabad is a predominantly Hindu and Muslim city, and its people are welcoming and hospitable. Some local customs to keep in mind include:

  • Wearing respectful clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Removing shoes when entering someone’s home or religious site.
  • Tipping waitstaff and service providers around 10% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Aurangabad

The best time to visit Aurangabad is the winter season, between November and February, when the weather is mild and dry. Summers can be incredibly hot and humid, while monsoons can make traveling difficult due to heavy rains and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aurangabad

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Carry drinking water with you at all times.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people or their homes without asking for permission.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Interfere with local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aurangabad

Aurangabad is a generally safe city, but it is always advisable to take precautions such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe and be mindful of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Use licensed taxis and public transport when traveling.

Health in Aurangabad

It is recommended to take precautions to avoid any health issues when traveling to Aurangabad, such as:

  • Drinking only bottled or filtered water.
  • Avoiding street food or food from unhygienic eateries.
  • Carrying a first aid kit and essential medication.

Getting to Aurangabad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aurangabad

Aurangabad has a domestic airport, with frequent flights from major cities such as Mumbai, Delhi, and Hyderabad. The airport is around 10 km from the city center, and taxis and buses are available for transport.

Bus Stations Near Aurangabad

The city’s main bus station, Aurangabad Central Bus Stand, has excellent connectivity to major cities in Maharashtra and nearby states.

Other Options

Aurangabad is well-connected by train to major cities such as Mumbai, Hyderabad, and Delhi. Several private and state-run taxis and buses are also available for travel within the city.

How to Get Around in Aurangabad

Aurangabad is a small city and easy to navigate on foot or using public transport such as buses or auto-rickshaws. Taxis and rental cars are also available if needed.

Where to Go Next from Aurangabad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aurangabad is an excellent starting point for exploring nearby destinations, including:

  • Shirdi, a pilgrimage site known for the Sai Baba temple.
  • Ajanta caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Nashik, a wine country famous for its vineyards and temples of historical significance.

Conclusion

Aurangabad is a city that offers a perfect mix of history, culture, natural beauty, and adventure. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip and will be able to make the most of your time in this enchanting corner of Western India.
