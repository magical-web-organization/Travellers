Introduction to Nanded

Nanded is a city in the Marathwada region of Maharashtra, Western India. It is a holy city, revered by the Sikh community, as the place where the 10th Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh, spent his last days. Nanded has a rich history, cultural heritage, and scenic beauty that make it a must-visit destination in Maharashtra. This comprehensive travel guide to Nanded will help you plan your trip with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Nanded

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

Gurudwara Shri Hazur Sahib

Gurudwara Shri Hazur Sahib is the most important shrine in Nanded, and one of the holiest places in the Sikh religion. It was built at the spot where Guru Gobind Singh breathed his last. The gurudwara has a serene atmosphere and beautiful architecture that attracts thousands of pilgrims every year.

Kandhar Fort

Kandhar Fort, located 20 kilometers from Nanded city, is a historical site that dates back to the Rashtrakuta dynasty. The fort offers a panoramic view of the surrounding area, and has four entrance gates, a temple, and a mosque.

Nanded Fort

Nanded Fort, built in the 12th century, is a famous historical site that has seen the reign of several dynasties including the Bahamani Sultanate, Mughals, and Marathas. The fort has several bastions, ramparts, and a beautiful gate built in the Mughal style.

Natural Attractions of Nanded: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Godavari River

Godavari River, the second-longest river in India, flows through Nanded, and offers a serene and peaceful environment for tourists to relax and enjoy the view. Visitors can take a boat ride on the river or enjoy a picnic by the banks.

Hingoli Wildlife Sanctuary

Located about 60 kilometers from Nanded, the Hingoli Wildlife Sanctuary is known for its unique animal and bird species such as chinkara, blackbuck, and Indian grey wolf. Tourists can enjoy a jeep safari through the sanctuary and observe the wildlife in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanded

Hiking in Panchakki

Panchakki, a watermill built in the 17th century, is located on the banks of the Godavari River, and provides a scenic and tranquil setting for a hike.

Biking in Shivaji Nagar

Shivaji Nagar, located on the outskirts of Nanded, is a picturesque area with lush green fields on both sides of the road. The area is ideal for a leisurely bike ride and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Nanded: Activities and Tours

Visit the Nanded City Market

The Nanded City Market, located near the Haji Baba Dargah, is a bustling bazaar that offers a variety of goods, from clothes to spices. Tourists can immerse themselves in the local culture and buy souvenirs to take back home.

Take a Heritage Walk

The heritage walk of Nanded, organized by the tourism department, takes visitors on a journey through the city’s rich history and cultural heritage. The walk covers several historical sites such as Kandhar Fort, Nanded Fort, and Gurudwara Shri Hazur Sahib.

Food and Drink Scene of Nanded

Must-Try Dishes

Bhakarwadi

Bhakarwadi is a spicy snack made of gram flour, stuffed with a mixture of sesame, coconut, and poppy seeds, and rolled in spirals. It is a popular snack served with tea or coffee.

Taak

Taak, also known as buttermilk, is a refreshing drink made of yogurt, water, and spices, and is commonly served during hot summer months.

Local Beverages

Solkadhi

Solkadhi, a refreshing drink, is made of coconut milk, kokum, and spices. It is an ideal drink to cool down and refresh yourself after a long day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Nanded: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nanded offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses. Some of the popular hotels and accommodations in Nanded are Hotel Ramakrishna International, Hotel City Pride, and Hotel Anuradha Palace.

What to Buy in Nanded: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Paithani Sarees

Paithani Sarees, known for their intricate zari work and vibrant designs, are a popular traditional garment in Maharashtra. Nanded is known for producing high-quality Paithani sarees, and tourists can purchase them from local markets.

Silverware

Nanded is known for its intricate silverware, including silver jewelry, utensils, and decorative pieces. Visitors can purchase these unique souvenirs from local stores and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Nanded

Nanded has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Hindu, Muslim, and Sikh traditions. It is essential to respect the local culture and customs when visiting the holy city. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites, remove shoes before entering temples or shrines, and follow local customs while interacting with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Nanded

The best time to visit Nanded is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanded

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Shop for souvenirs and local products.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Cause harm to wildlife or natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanded

Nanded is a safe and peaceful city with a low crime rate. However, tourists should always be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Nanded

It is advisable to carry mosquito repellent creams and medicines to protect oneself from mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria. Drink only bottled water and avoid consuming street food to prevent food-related illnesses.

Getting to Nanded: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nanded is well-connected to major cities in India through various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Nanded

The nearest international airport to Nanded is the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport in Mumbai, located approximately 610 kilometers from the city. The nearest domestic airport is the Shri Guru Gobind Singh Ji Airport in Nanded.

Bus Stations Near Nanded

The Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation operates regular bus services to and from Nanded to various cities in Maharashtra and neighboring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Nanded Railway Station from Mumbai, Hyderabad, or Aurangabad.

How to Get Around in Nanded

Nanded has a well-developed public transportation system consisting of buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws. Tourists can rent cars or bikes to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Nanded: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aurangabad

Aurangabad, located 230 kilometers from Nanded, is a historical city known for its famous Ajanta and Ellora caves.

Hyderabad

Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana, is approximately 270 kilometers from Nanded, and is famous for its rich history, cuisine, and culture.

Conclusion

Nanded, the holy city of Maharashtra, is a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. It is a must-visit destination for those interested in exploring new cultures and traditions. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Nanded, and make memories that will last a lifetime.
