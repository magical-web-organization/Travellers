Your Comprehensive Guide to Marathwada, Maharashtra

Located in western India, Marathwada is a region renowned for its rich culture and heritage, historical significance, and natural beauty. This lesser-known tourist destination is truly off the beaten path, offering a unique experience for travelers seeking an authentic taste of India. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Marathwada.

List of Places to Visit in Marathwada

Marathwada boasts several historical, natural, and cultural attractions. Here’s a list of must-visit places in the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ajanta and Ellora Caves: Dating back to the 2nd century BCE to the 10th century CE, these cave temples are a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring intricate carvings and paintings.
  • Chand Minar: A 225-foot tall tower built in 1445, it’s considered the tallest brick minaret in the world.
  • Nizam’s Palace: Also known as the Falaknuma Palace, this stunning palace features a blend of Indian and European architecture and was built in 1893.
  • Aurangabad Caves: Dating back to the 2nd century BCE to the 6th century CE, these rock-cut caves are located in the midst of stunning hills.

Natural Attractions of Marathwada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lonar Crater: A saline soda lake formed in the impact crater of a meteorite that hit the Earth approximately 50,000 years ago.
  • Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve: Home to Royal Bengal Tigers, Indian leopards, and other wildlife species, the reserve offers jungle safaris, nature walks, and birdwatching tours.
  • Daulatabad Fort: A medieval hill fort with magnificent views, dating back to the 12th century.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marathwada

Marathwada is a popular destination for trekking and cycling enthusiasts, thanks to its rolling hills and picturesque landscapes. Here are some must-try routes:

  • Harishchandragad Trek: A scenic trek to the Harishchandragad fort, offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys and waterfalls.
  • Ellora-Aurangabad Cycling Route: A cycling trail that takes you through the ancient city of Aurangabad and the world-famous Ellora Caves.
  • Lonar Crater Trek: A trek to the Lonar Crater, offering breathtaking views of the crater and its surrounding vegetation.

Best Things to Do in Marathwada: Activities and Tours

Apart from marveling at the historical and natural attractions, there are plenty of other activities and tours to indulge in while in Marathwada. Here are some recommendations:

  • Taste local delicacies: Marathwada offers a unique culinary experience, with flavors and spices distinct from other parts of India. Try out various dishes like mutton fry and bhakri while exploring the local food scene.
  • Visit Phalke Smarak: A museum dedicated to the father of Indian cinema, Dadasaheb Phalke. The museum features various exhibits about the Indian film industry, including information about regional cinema in Maharashtra.
  • Explore local markets: Marathwada has many vibrant bazaars that offer a variety of local products like handwoven textiles, jewelry, and curios.

Food and Drink Scene of Marathwada

The food and drink scene of Marathwada is a perfect blend of spicy, sweet, and savory flavors, with influences from various regions. Here are some dishes and beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mutton Fry: A popular spicy mutton dish that’s a staple in Marathwada.
  • Bhakri: A type of flatbread made with jowar or bajra, served with a variety of curries and chutneys.
  • Patodi: A gram flour-based dish that’s steamed and then fried, served with spicy gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Sol Kadhi: A refreshing drink made with coconut milk, kokum, and spices.
  • Sugarcane Juice: A refreshing and healthy drink that is popular in Marathwada.

Where to Stay in Marathwada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marathwada offers plenty of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to guesthouses. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Vivanta Aurangabad: A luxurious hotel located close to the Aurangabad city center.
  • Lemon Tree Hotel: Another great option for luxurious accommodation, located close to the Ellora Caves.
  • Hotel Shivneri Palace: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and great service.

What to Buy in Marathwada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Marathwada is well-known for its handwoven textiles, jewelry, and curios. Here are some recommendations for souvenirs and local products:

  • Paithani Sarees: A type of saree that’s handwoven, featuring intricate designs and patterns.
  • Bidriware: A form of metalwork that involves silver and copper inlay on a blackened zinc alloy.
  • Mashru Fabric: A silk and cotton blend fabric that’s popular in Marathwada.

Local Culture and Customs of Marathwada

Marathwada has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its vibrant festivals and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly, especially while visiting religious places.
  • Remove your footwear before entering a temple or any other religious place.

Best Time to Visit Marathwada

The best time to visit Marathwada is between October and February, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marathwada

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and traditions.
  • Try local dishes and drinks to experience the unique flavors of Marathwada.
  • Carry comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t take photographs of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marathwada

Marathwada is a generally safe destination, with low incidences of crime. However, like any other place, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Avoid visiting isolated places at night.
  • Keep your belongings secure and be wary of pickpockets or thieves.

Health in Marathwada

Marathwada does not pose any unique health risks to travelers. It is advised to carry necessary medications and insect repellents while traveling.

Getting to Marathwada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marathwada is well-connected to various parts of India via air, road, and rail. Here are some ways to reach Marathwada:

Airports Near Marathwada

The Aurangabad Airport is the nearest airport to Marathwada, located approximately 10 kilometers from Aurangabad city center.

Bus Stations Near Marathwada

Several state-owned and private buses operate from Mumbai, Pune, and other cities in Maharashtra to Aurangabad.

Other Options

One can take the train from Mumbai, Pune or Delhi and reach Aurangabad in approx 9 hours.

How to Get Around in Marathwada

Marathwada can be best explored by hiring a private car or taxi. Local buses and auto-rickshaws are also available for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Marathwada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marathwada is located close to several tourist destinations, making it an excellent gateway for many nearby attractions. Here are some recommendations:

  • Paithan: A small town located approximately 50 kilometers from Aurangabad, known for its beautiful Paithani sarees.
  • Shirdi: A holy town located approximately 110 kilometers from Aurangabad, known for the Shirdi Sai Baba temple.
  • Nashik: A city located approximately 200 kilometers from Aurangabad, known for its vineyards and wineries.

Conclusion

Marathwada is a beautiful blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to India. The region offers unique attractions, exciting outdoor activities, and an unforgettable culinary experience. Apply the tips in this guide to plan your Marathwada itinerary, and you’re guaranteed to have an amazing trip.
