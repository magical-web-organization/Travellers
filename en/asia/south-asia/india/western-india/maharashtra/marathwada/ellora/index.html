Introduction to Ellora

Ellora, located in Marathwada, Maharashtra, is a popular travel destination in Western India. It is known for its ancient cave temples, stunning nature, and rich cultural heritage. Home to many UNESCO World Heritage Sites, Ellora attracts visitors from all over the world.

This comprehensive travel guide to Ellora will provide you with everything you need to plan your trip to this beautiful destination. Let’s explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Ellora.

List of Places to Visit in Ellora

Ellora is famous for its ancient cave temples, beautiful nature trails, and historical landmarks. Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Ellora:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ellora Caves: The Ellora Caves is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Ellora. It is a series of 34 rock-cut temples that were built between the 6th and 10th centuries AD. These temples are a mix of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain architecture and are known for their intricate carvings and beautiful artwork.

  • Bibi Ka Maqbara: Bibi Ka Maqbara is a famous mausoleum located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra. It was built in the 17th century by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in memory of his wife. It is also known as the mini Taj Mahal because it resembles the Taj Mahal in Agra.

  • Aurangabad Caves: The Aurangabad Caves located in Aurangabad is a group of 12 Buddhist caves that date back to the 6th and 7th centuries AD. The caves are known for their unique architecture and beautiful sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Ellora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Daulatabad Fort: Daulatabad Fort is a 14th-century fort located near Ellora. It is known for its impressive architecture and unique defense system. The fort offers a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.

  • Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga Temple: Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga Temple is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in India. It is located near Ellora and is known for its beautiful architecture and spiritual significance.

  • Lenyadri Caves: Lenyadri Caves is a group of 30 Buddhist rock-cut caves located near Ellora. The caves date back to the 1st century BC and are known for their beautiful carvings and serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellora

Ellora offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Western India. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Daultabad to Ellora Trail: This is a scenic 20 km trail that takes you through the beautiful countryside of Maharashtra. The trail starts from Daultabad Fort and ends at the Ellora Caves.

  • Aurangabad to Lonar Crater Trail: This is a 110 km trail that takes you through the scenic countryside of Maharashtra. The trail starts from Aurangabad and ends at the Lonar Crater, a famous meteorite impact crater.

Best Things to Do in Ellora: Activities and Tours

Ellora offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Ellora:

  • Ellora Caves Audio Tour: Take an audio tour of the Ellora Caves and learn about the history and significance of these ancient temples.

  • Hot-Air Balloon Ride: Experience the stunning beauty of Ellora from the air with a hot-air balloon ride.

  • Cultural Tours: Take a cultural tour of Ellora and visit museums and art galleries to learn about the rich history and culture of Maharashtra.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ellora

A week-long itinerary for Ellora can include the following:

Day 1: Visit the Ellora Caves, and Bibi Ka Maqbara. Day 2: Hike the Daultabad to Ellora Trail. Day 3: Explore the Aurangabad Caves and visit the Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga Temple. Day 4: Take a hot-air balloon ride and explore the countryside of Maharashtra. Day 5: Visit the Lenyadri Caves and explore the natural beauty of the region. Day 6: Take a cultural tour of Ellora and visit museums and art galleries. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellora

The food and drink scene in Ellora is diverse and delicious. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Khichadi: A popular rice and lentil dish with a mix of spices and herbs.
  • Bhakri: A type of flatbread made from millet or wheat flour.
  • Paan: A local delicacy made from betel leaf, areca nut, and a mixture of spices and sweeteners.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A sweet and refreshing yogurt-based drink.
  • Solkadhi: A refreshing drink made from coconut milk and kokum.
  • Masala Chai: A local spiced tea made with a mix of Indian spices and herbs.

  • Kailas Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located near the Ellora Caves.
  • The Meadows Resort: A luxurious resort located in the scenic countryside of Maharashtra.

What to Buy in Ellora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the souvenirs and local products to bring home from Ellora:

  • Ajanta-Ellora Paintings: Traditional paintings inspired by the ancient art found in the Ellora and Ajanta Caves.
  • Himroo Shawls and Stoles: A local textile made from silk and wool.
  • Paithani Sarees: A traditional Maharashtrian saree known for its intricate designs and beautiful craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellora

The local culture and customs of Ellora are deeply rooted in ancient Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain traditions. Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ellora

The best time to visit Ellora is between October and March when the weather is cool and pleasant. Summers in Ellora can be hot and humid, making it difficult to explore the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellora

Here are some do’s and don’ts of Ellora:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Litter or damage historical monuments.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Ellora is a relatively safe place to visit. However, visitors should be aware of pickpockets and take precautionary measures to keep their belongings safe.

Safety in Ellora

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables locked in a safe in your hotel room.

Health in Ellora

  • Drink bottled water to avoid waterborne diseases.
  • Wear insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria.

Getting to Ellora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ellora is well-connected to major cities in Maharashtra by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Ellora

The nearest airport to Ellora is Aurangabad Airport, which is located around 30 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ellora

Ellora is well-connected to major cities in Maharashtra by bus. The main bus station is located in Aurangabad.

Other Options

  • Hire a taxi or take a private car to reach Ellora from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Ellora

The best way to get around in Ellora is by hiring a taxi, taking local buses, or renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Ellora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to visit after exploring Ellora:

  • Aurangabad: Visit the caves of Ajanta and explore the rich cultural heritage of the city.
  • Sulatanpur Bird Sanctuary: Enjoy birdwatching and explore the natural beauty of the region.

Conclusion

Ellora is a beautiful and richly diverse travel destination. Whether you are a fan of history, culture, or nature, Ellora has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now ready to plan your trip to Ellora, Maharashtra, in India.
