Introduction to Beed

Located in the Marathwada region of Maharashtra, Beed is a lesser-known travel destination in Western India that boasts a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. The city is surrounded by mountains and natural wonders and is home to several cultural landmarks and historic sites.

List of Places to Visit in Beed

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kankaleshwar Temple

The Kankaleshwar Temple is an ancient Hindu temple in Beed, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is believed to have been built during the 9th century and is one of the oldest and most revered temples in the region.

Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, also known as the Beed Mosque, is one of the most prominent Islamic shrines in the city. It was built during the Mughal period and is known for its intricate architecture and intricate calligraphy work.

Khandoba Temple

Khandoba Temple is a popular Hindu temple in Beed, dedicated to Lord Khandoba. It is located on a hilltop and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Dharur Fort

Dharur Fort is a historic fort located in the Beed district. It was built during the 14th century and is known for its unique architectural design and strategic location.

Natural Attractions of Beed: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parli Vaijnath

Parli Vaijnath is a famous pilgrimage site in Beed, known for its beautiful natural surroundings and ancient temples. The area is surrounded by scenic hills and forests, making it an ideal destination for trekking and hiking enthusiasts.

Manjara Dam

Manjara Dam is a popular picnic spot located on the outskirts of Beed. It is built on the Manjara River and is known for its picturesque surroundings and serene atmosphere.

Nalganga Dam

Nalganga Dam is another popular tourist destination in Beed, known for its stunning natural beauty and mesmerizing waterfalls. The area is surrounded by lush green forests and is an ideal spot for birdwatching and wildlife photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beed

Beed offers several exciting hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Some popular routes include the Parli Vaijnath – Khandoba Temple trek, the Dharur Fort trek, and the Parli Vaijnath – Naldurg trek.

Best Things to Do in Beed: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Beed include exploring the city’s historic landmarks and cultural sites, indulging in adventure sports like trekking and hiking, and embarking on a sightseeing tour of the natural wonders around the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beed

Day 1: Visit the Kankaleshwar Temple and Jama Masjid.

Day 2: Trek to Parli Vaijnath and visit the ancient temples in the area.

Day 3: Explore Dharur Fort and the surrounding area.

Day 4: Visit the Manjara Dam and enjoy a picnic in the serene surroundings.

Day 5: Explore the Nalganga Dam and the waterfalls in the area.

Day 6: Visit the Khandoba Temple and enjoy the scenic views from the hilltop.

Day 7: Embark on a sightseeing tour of nearby villages and experience the local culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Beed

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Beed include the traditional Maharashtrian thali, paani puri, bhel puri, and vada pav.

Local Beverages

Local beverages like solkadi, kokam juice, and sugarcane juice are a popular choice among tourists visiting Beed.

Where to Stay in Beed: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beed offers a range of options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxurious hotels and resorts. Some popular options include the Vaidurya Hotel, Krishna Tunga Residency, and Hotel Amarpreet.

What to Buy in Beed: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Beed can take home souvenirs like traditional handloom fabrics, pottery, and wooden toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Beed

Beed’s culture is heavily influenced by its historic background and diverse geography. The locals are warm and welcoming, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Beed

The best time to visit Beed is during the winter months (November to February) when the weather is pleasant and the natural surroundings are at their best.
