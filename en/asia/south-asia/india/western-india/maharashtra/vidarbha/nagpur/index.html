Introduction to Nagpur

Located in Vidarbha, in Maharashtra, in Western India, Nagpur is a city famed for its rich history, culture, and natural wonders. Nicknamed “The Orange City,” Nagpur is best known for its juicy and delicious oranges and is the largest producer of the fruit in India.

Nagpur is a vibrant destination with an abundance of attractions, from museums and historical sites to nature reserves and scenic vistas. In this travel guide, we’ll explore all that Nagpur has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Nagpur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Deekshabhoomi: A significant Buddhist monument and pilgrimage center, Deekshabhoomi is a beautiful complex with a Stupa that stands over 100 feet tall. The monument attracts visitors from all over the world and is one of the biggest Stupas in Asia.
  • Raman Science Centre: An interactive museum featuring numerous exhibits that educate visitors about the marvels of science and technology, the Raman Science Centre is one of the city’s most popular attractions.
  • Nagpur Central Museum: Known for its ancient artifacts, the Nagpur Central Museum is one of the oldest museums in India. It showcases the region’s rich history and art and has an impressive collection of sculptures and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Nagpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seminary Hill: An ideal place for nature lovers, Seminary Hill offers beautiful views of the city and is perfect for a morning or evening walk. It’s also home to a beautiful temple and a beautiful garden.
  • Ramtek Fort Temple: Located on the hills of Ramtek, the ancient fort and temple date back to the 4th century. It is a beautiful spot to visit thanks to the breathtaking views and beautiful architecture.
  • Sita Buldi Fort: A fort popular for its rich history, Sita Buldi offers stunning views of the city and sunset. It is also famous for its museums featuring Indian military equipment and weaponry.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nagpur

  • Gorewada Lake: A pristine destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts alike, Gorewada Lake is spread across 1,900 acres of lush green nature. It offers plenty of biking and hiking trails and is a remarkable spot for bird watching.
  • Waki Woods: Located in Mouda, Waki Woods is a beautiful spot with a vast variety of flora and fauna. The woods are known for their natural beauty, and tourists can hike and bike through the woodland paths to explore the area’s fascinating terrain.

Best Things to Do in Nagpur: Activities and Tours

  • Tiger Safari at Pench Tiger Reserve: Considered a heaven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts, the Pench Tiger Reserve offers an unforgettable tiger safari experience. Visitors can find four species of the big cat at Pench: the Bengal, Indian, White, and Melanistic.
  • Satpura Adventure: A fantastic experience for adventure seekers, Satpura Adventure offers several adrenaline-pumping activities such as rock climbing, trekking, and camping. They also organize customized adventure tours and treks throughout Nagpur.
  • Zero Mile Stone: A historic landmark located in the city center, the Zero Mile Stone is a significant national significance marks the geographical center of India.

Food and Drink Scene of Nagpur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Santra Barfi: A sweet made of condensed milk, sugar and fresh orange juice, Santra Barfi is a must-try snack in Nagpur.
  • Seyal Chicken: A traditional chicken curry cooked with onions and a blend of spices, Seyal Chicken is a Nagpur favorite.
  • Tarri Poha: A breakfast treat made with flattened rice, diced onions, chili, and a mix of refreshing spices, Tarri Poha is a go-to breakfast food in Nagpur.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Known as the “Orange City,” Nagpur is famous for its orange juice. It’s a refreshing drink that can be found at almost every street corner.
  • Santra Sharbat: Santra Sharbat is a local beverage made with oranges, sugar, and water. It’s a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Nagpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Radisson Blu Hotel Nagpur: Located in the heart of the city, Radisson Blu offers luxurious rooms with all modern facilities.
  • Tuli International Hotel: A 4-star hotel, Tuli International Hotel provides comfortable rooms, excellent cuisine, and a lovely ambiance.
  • OYO Rooms: A budget-friendly option, OYO Rooms offers rooms at affordable prices while still providing quality service.

What to Buy in Nagpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Orange Products: Being the Orange City, Nagpur is famous for its oranges, and you can buy several orange products, including jams, jellies, and sweets made with oranges.
  • Handicrafts: Nagpur is famous for handicrafts made by the local tribes, including pottery, traditional paintings, and wickerwork. They make excellent souvenirs and keepsakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Nagpur

Nagpur has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are known for their hospitality and warm nature. Be mindful to dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and to respect the local customs and etiquettes.

Best Time to Visit Nagpur

The best time to visit Nagpur is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and favorable for exploring the outdoors comfortably.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nagpur

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs without asking permission
  • Don’t lick your fingers or slurp your food in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Nagpur

Nagpur is generally safe for tourists. However, it is recommended to carry a copy of personal documents, such as passport and visa, and avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas.

Health in Nagpur

Carry a first-aid kit when visiting Nagpur, and make sure to purchase travel insurance before your trip. It’s recommended to drink packaged water and avoid street food to prevent any health issues.

Where to Go Next from Nagpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pench Tiger Reserve: Just 2 hours away from Nagpur, Pench Tiger Reserve is an excellent destination for nature lovers.
  • Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve: Located approximately 145 km from Nagpur, Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve is known for its breathtaking landscapes, rich flora and fauna, and the diverse range of wildlife species.
  • Maharashtra’s hill stations: Try visiting Maharashtra’s well-known hill stations, including Lonavala, Khandala, and Matheran. All are within driving distance from Nagpur.

Conclusion

Nagpur is a city of contrasts, with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a nature lover or a history buff, Nagpur has something for everyone. Plan a trip to this fantastic destination, explore its ancient monuments, experience its natural beauty and discover its fascinating traditions, food and drink scene, and local customs.
