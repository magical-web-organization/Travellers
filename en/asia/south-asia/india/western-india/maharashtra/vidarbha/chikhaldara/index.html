Chikhaldara, a picturesque hill station located in the Amravati district of Vidarbha in the western Indian state of Maharashtra, is gaining popularity among travellers seeking a break from the traditional tourist destinations of India. With its stunning natural beauty, serene atmosphere, and offbeat charm, Chikhaldara offers an unforgettable experience to its visitors. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the history, culture, activities, food, and accommodations of Chikhaldara.

Introduction to Chikhaldara

Chikhaldara is a small and peaceful hill station situated at an altitude of 1,118 meters above sea level. It is a beautiful weekend getaway for travellers from Mumbai and Pune. Chikhaldara is surrounded by beautiful lakes, dense forests, and cascading waterfalls. It is also known for its serene wildlife sanctuary which is home to many species of flora and fauna. The hill station is named after the ancient fort of Kichakavadha, which is now in ruins. According to the locals, the epic Mahabharata was written here, making it even more special.

Local Culture and Customs of Chikhaldara

The locals of Chikhaldara are predominantly tribal, with a rich cultural heritage. The Warli tribe is one of the prominent tribes in the region. The locals celebrate many festivals such as Diwali and Holi with great enthusiasm.

Best Time to Visit Chikhaldara

The best time to visit Chikhaldara is from October to March when the temperature is pleasant, and the weather is dry. The monsoons from July to September bring life to the waterfalls and greenery surrounding the hill station, but it can be a tricky time due to landslides and flooded roads.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chikhaldara

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry essential medicines and first aid kit.
  • Wear comfortable footwear and clothing for trekking and hiking.
  • Book your accommodation in advance.

Don’t:

  • Carry plastic bags and bottles.
  • Litter in the natural surroundings.
  • Noise-pollute the peaceful hill station.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chikhaldara

Chikhaldara is a peaceful and safe hill station, but travellers need to be cautious during trekking and hiking. Do not venture out alone in the late evenings, especially in the forests. Ensure that you carry a torch and a charged up mobile phone.

Health in Chikhaldara

Carry a first-aid kit and essential medicines for common ailments like cough and cold, fever, and headaches. Mosquito repellents are essential during the monsoon season to prevent diseases like dengue and malaria.

The best way to explore Chikhaldara is on foot, especially for trekking and hiking. Taxis and auto-rickshaws can be hired for local sightseeing.
