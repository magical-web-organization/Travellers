Introduction to Gondia

Gondia is a small district in the Vidarbha region of Maharashtra. It is known for its natural beauty, wildlife, and adventure activities. Gondia has a rich history and culture, with many museums and historical sites to visit. The surrounding forests offer hiking and biking trails, and the rivers and lakes in the area provide ample opportunity for water activities. Gondia’s food and drinks scene is also a must-try for visitors, with delicious local cuisine and refreshing beverages. Now, let’s explore Gondia in detail.

List of Places to Visit in Gondia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gondia Fort: Built during the Maratha Empire, the fort served as a military base and is now a popular tourist spot. The fort offers a panoramic view of the surrounding area and houses a museum showcasing local artifacts.

  2. Nawegaon Bandh Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in the Gondia district, this sanctuary is home to various species of birds, butterflies, and animals like tigers, leopards, and sloth bears.

  3. Shri Gajanan Maharaj Mandir: One of the most famous and visited religious shrines in the area, this temple is dedicated to Saint Gajanan Maharaj.

  4. Deogarh: A historical town famous for its old temples, Deogarh was once the capital of the Nag Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Gondia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary: Spread over an area of 152 sq km, this sanctuary is home to tigers, bison, sambar, and other wildlife species.

  2. Itiadoh Dam: Built on the Pench River, this dam offers breathtaking views and is a popular spot for picnics and water activities.

  3. Suryadeo Mando Devi Temple: Located on a hill, this temple provides a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding area.

  4. Bori Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary has sloth bears, leopards, tigers, and various bird species, and offers hiking and walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gondia

  1. Navegaon National Park: This park is known for its natural beauty and the popular three-hour trail that offers trekking, hiking, and biking.

  2. Pratapgarh: A must-visit place for nature lovers, this place has an elevation of 3,300 feet and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  3. Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary: The sanctuary offers several trekking trails with varying levels of difficulty, and the views are worth the effort.

  4. Itiadoh Dam: The dam area is a great spot for a leisurely bike ride, and there are cycling tours available.

Best Things to Do in Gondia: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Gondia Fort and Nawegaon Bandh Wildlife Sanctuary.

  2. Take a safari tour of the Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary.

  3. Watch the sunrise or sunset at Suryadeo Mando Devi Temple.

  4. Go hiking or biking on the trails in Pratapgarh and Bori Wildlife Sanctuaries.

Food and Drink Scene of Gondia

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Patodi: A local dish made of gram flour, served with hot chutney.

  2. Sabudana Khichdi: A delicious dish made of sago and peanuts and served with curd and spicy chutney.

  3. Parda Biryani: A traditional biryani dish cooked in a sealed vessel.

  4. Saoji Chicken: A spicy chicken curry made with local spices and served with Bhakari, a type of local bread.

Local Beverages

  1. Piyush: A sweet and creamy drink made with buttermilk, sugar, and saffron.

  2. Kokum Sharbat: A refreshing drink made from Kokum fruits.

  3. Santra Sharbat: A tangy and sweet drink made with oranges and sugar.

  4. Sol Kadhi: A cold drink made with coconut milk, spices, and Kokum juice.

Where to Stay in Gondia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Aadhar Hotel: Located in the center of Gondia, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  2. Hotel Radhika Palace: A budget hotel with clean and spacious rooms, this hotel is near Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary.

  3. Kaka Guest House: A homely guest house with clean and comfortable rooms, this guest house is near Suryadeo Mando Devi Temple.

  4. V Resorts Mahua Tola Tadoba: A villa-style resort near the Pench River, this resort offers scenic views and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Gondia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kosa Silk: A type of silk made from the cocoons of an Indian moth, this silk is lightweight and soft.

  2. Warli Art: Paintings and crafts made by the Warli tribe, known for their unique style.

  3. Paithani Sarees: A traditional saree made using pure silk and zari, with intricate designs.

  4. Bamboo Crafts: Handcrafted items like baskets, mats, and utensils made from bamboo.

Local Culture and Customs of Gondia

Gondia has a rich cultural history and is home to different religious communities. The people are welcoming and friendly. It is customary to take off footwear before entering religious places, and you should dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Gondia

The best time to visit Gondia is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gondia

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Hire a guide or join a local tour in wildlife sanctuaries.

  3. Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Litter the environment.

  2. Disturb wildlife in the sanctuaries.

  3. Take photographs in religious places without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gondia

Gondia is a safe place, but you should take the usual precautions while traveling, like avoiding dark and unfamiliar places at night, staying aware of your surroundings, and protecting your valuables.

Health in Gondia

You should be careful with food and drink when traveling in Gondia, as the water and sanitation system may not be up to Western standards. Bring medication for common illnesses like diarrhea and stomach upset.

Getting to Gondia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gondia

The nearest airport to Gondia is Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport in Nagpur, which is about 136 km from Gondia.

Bus Stations Near Gondia

Gondia is well connected by bus to major cities in Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, and Madhya Pradesh.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or car to reach Gondia from other parts of Maharashtra.

How to Get Around in Gondia

Local buses, taxis, and autos are available for transportation in Gondia. You can also rent a bike or car to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Gondia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Pench National Park: A tiger reserve and popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

  2. Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve: A wildlife sanctuary with tigers, leopards, and other animals.

  3. Kanha National Park: A renowned tiger reserve and a popular attraction among travelers.

Conclusion

If you love nature, adventure, and cultural exploration, Gondia is an excellent destination to add to your travel list. This small district in Maharashtra offers a plethora of options for travelers, like wildlife sanctuaries, historical sites, and outdoor activities. Plan your trip well and experience the best that Gondia has to offer.
