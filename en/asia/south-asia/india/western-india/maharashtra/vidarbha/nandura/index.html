Your Comprehensive Travel Guide to Nandura, Maharashtra

Nandura is a small town located in the Vidarbha region of Maharashtra, in Western India. It is a perfect destination for travelers seeking an offbeat experience, away from the hustle and bustle of the big cities. Here, you can explore ancient temples, scenic views, and delicious local cuisine. Plan your trip to Nandura with our comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Nandura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ram Mandir: One of the oldest and most iconic temples in Nandura.
  • Ankleshwar Mandir: A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, visit here for peace and tranquility.
  • Bhosla Rajwada Palace: A palace built by the Bhosla dynasty, it now serves as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Nandura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nandura Hill: Popular for evening walks, treks and sightseeing.
  • Jilpi Lake: A scenic lake located in the outskirts of the town. Perfect for a picnic.
  • Kanha Tiger Reserve: Located 3 hours away from Nandura, this wildlife sanctuary is home to tigers, leopards, and other wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nandura

  • Nandura Hill Trek: A 2-hour trek that takes you to the peak of the Nandura Hill.
  • Jilpi Lake Trail: A 5-kilometer trail that takes you to the Jilpi Lake and through the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Nandura: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Bhosla Rajwada Palace: Explore the ancient architecture and artifacts of the Bhosla dynasty.
  • Attend a Local Festival: Experience the local culture by attending festivals like Diwali, Ganesh Chaturthi, and Holi.
  • Take a Nature Walk: Explore the scenic areas around Nandura on foot.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nandura

Day 1: Visit the Ram Mandir and Ankleshwar Mandir
Day 2: Trek to the Nandura Hill
Day 3: Visit the Bhosla Rajwada Palace and the museum
Day 4: Take a nature walk to explore the lush forests
Day 5: Visit the Jilpi Lake for a picnic
Day 6: Attend a local festival
Day 7: Visit the nearby Kanha Tiger Reserve

Food and Drink Scene of Nandura

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poha: A dish made with flattened rice, onions, and spices.
  • Misal Pav: A spicy curry made from sprouted legumes and served with bread.
  • Dal Khichdi: Rice and lentils cooked together and served hot.

Local Beverages

  • Sol Kadhi: A refreshing drink made from kokum and coconut milk.
  • Sugarcane Juice: Freshly squeezed sugarcane juice is available at local stalls.

Where to Stay in Nandura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Yashwant Palace: A budget hotel located in the heart of the town.
  • Hotel Pritam Palace: A mid-range hotel with modern amenities.
  • Guesthouse Oasis: A cozy guesthouse with garden views.

What to Buy in Nandura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Pick up handcrafted items like hand-woven baskets and pottery.
  • Maharashtrian Sarees: Nandura is famous for its traditional Maharashtrian sarees.
  • Spices: Buy locally grown spices like turmeric and cumin.

Local Culture and Customs of Nandura

Nandura has a predominantly Hindu population and the locals are friendly and welcoming. It is advised to dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering temples.

Best Time to Visit Nandura

The best time to visit Nandura is from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nandura

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Bargain at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local traditions.
  • Wearing revealing clothing in public places.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nandura

Nandura is a safe place for tourists, but it is advised to take precautions while traveling at night.

Health in Nandura

It is recommended to drink only bottled water and carry your own medicaition for any medical emergencies.

Getting to Nandura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nandura

The nearest airport is the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport in Nagpur, located 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Nandura

The nearest bus stop is the Nandura Bus Station, which is well-connected to major cities in Maharashtra.

Other Options

One can also reach Nandura by hiring a taxi or driving down by car.

How to Get Around in Nandura

Nandura is a small town and easily navigable on foot. Autos, taxis and motorcycles are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Nandura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aurangabad: Visit the famous Ajanta and Ellora caves located 240 kilometers from Nandura.
  • Nagpur: Explore the city of Nagpur and its many historical landmarks located 150 kilometers away.

Conclusion

Nandura offers a unique travel experience, with its rich culture, heritage, and picturesque scenery. Plan your trip to Nandura with our comprehensive travel guide and explore the hidden gems of this off-beat destination.
