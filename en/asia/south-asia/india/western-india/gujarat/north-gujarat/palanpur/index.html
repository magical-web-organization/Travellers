Introduction to Palanpur

Palanpur is a small but bustling city located in the northeastern part of Gujarat, India. It is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. The city boasts of a royal past, and its palaces and forts are living examples of its glorious history. Palanpur is also home to a vibrant community that is deeply rooted in its traditions, and the city’s colorful festivals and fairs are a testament to this.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a tour of Palanpur, from the best places to visit to the local culture and customs. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure junkie, or a foodie, there is something here for everyone. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Palanpur

Palanpur offers a variety of attractions for visitors, including museums, historical places, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of some of the most popular places to visit in Palanpur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Balaram Palace - This magnificent palace is a fine example of the regional architecture of Gujarat. It is surrounded by lush green gardens and was the summer residence of the former rulers of Palanpur. The palace has now been converted into a heritage hotel, making it a popular destination for tourists.
  2. Siddhpur - Located just 25 km from Palanpur, Siddhpur is a historic town that is known for its magnificent Jain temples. The town also boasts of beautiful havelis and other architectural treasures.
  3. Keerti Stambh - This impressive tower is a symbol of non-violence and peace. It stands 72 feet tall and has 52 steps, each representing a week in a year.
  4. Palanpur Palace - This beautiful palace is an architectural gem that is now used as a museum. It houses a vast collection of antique furniture, royal costumes, and other artifacts.
  5. Banas Dairy - This is the biggest dairy plant in Asia and provides a fascinating insight into the production of milk and milk products.

Natural Attractions of Palanpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Balaram Ambaji Wildlife Sanctuary - Located just 20 km from Palanpur, this sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife, including leopards, hyenas, and sloth bears. Visitors can take a jeep safari to explore the sanctuary and see the animals up close.
  2. Polo Forest - This forest is located near the Aravalli hills and is known for its scenic beauty and rich biodiversity. Visitors can go trekking or cycling through the forest, and there are also opportunities for camping and bird watching.
  3. Kedarnath Mahadev Temple - This temple is located in the picturesque Kedarnath range and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can go trekking to the temple and enjoy the beautiful scenery along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palanpur

Palanpur offers several hiking and biking routes that take visitors through some of the city’s most beautiful natural landscapes. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  1. Polo Forest Trek - This is a 2-day trek that takes visitors through the beautiful Polo Forest. The trek covers a distance of around 25 km and offers stunning views of the forest and surrounding hills.
  2. Ambaji-Palanpur Cycling Route - This is a scenic cycling route that takes visitors from Ambaji to Palanpur. The route covers a distance of around 50 km and takes cyclists through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the region.

Best Things to Do in Palanpur: Activities and Tours

  1. Heritage Walks - Palanpur offers several heritage walks that take visitors through the city’s historic landmarks and important cultural sites.
  2. Jeep Safaris - Visitors can go on a jeep safari in the Balaram Ambaji Wildlife Sanctuary to see the animals up close and explore the natural beauty of the region.
  3. Food Tours - Palanpur offers several food tours that take visitors on a culinary journey through the city’s vibrant food scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palanpur

Here is a week-long itinerary for Palanpur that includes some of the city’s most popular attractions:

  • Day 1: Balaram Palace and Museum
  • Day 2: Siddhpur and Patan
  • Day 3: Banas Dairy and Keerti Stambh
  • Day 4: Kedarnath Mahadev Temple Trek
  • Day 5: Balaram Ambaji Wildlife Sanctuary Jeep Safari
  • Day 6: Polo Forest Trek
  • Day 7: Relax and Explore Local Cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Palanpur

No trip to Palanpur is complete without experiencing the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Methi na Gota - This is a popular snack that is made with fresh fenugreek leaves and gram flour.
  2. Khaman Dhokla - This is a steamed cake made with gram flour and is a popular breakfast item.
  3. Undhiyu - This is a mixed vegetable dish that is cooked in a clay pot and is a local specialty.
  4. Aamras - This is a sweet dessert made with ripe mangoes and is a must-try during the summer season.

Local Beverages

  1. Buttermilk - This refreshing drink is made with yogurt and is a popular accompaniment to meals in Palanpur.
  2. Sugarcane Juice - This sweet and refreshing juice is a popular local beverage in Palanpur and is available at most street stalls.

Where to Stay in Palanpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palanpur offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and traveler preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Palanpur:

  1. Balaram Palace Resort - This heritage hotel is located in a grand palace and offers luxurious rooms, a swimming pool, and beautiful gardens.
  2. Ginger Palanpur - This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.
  3. Hotel President - This budget hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  4. The Grand Villa Resort - This cozy resort offers comfortable and spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Palanpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palanpur is known for its handicrafts, textiles, and traditional jewelry. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  1. Bandhani Textiles - Palanpur is known for its beautiful Bandhani textiles, which are made by tying and dyeing the fabric.
  2. Silver Jewelry - Palanpur is known for its traditional silver jewelry, which is a popular souvenir among visitors.
  3. Wooden Handicrafts - Palanpur is home to several skilled artisans who create beautiful wooden handicrafts, including toys, furniture, and decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Palanpur

Palanpur is a city that is deeply rooted in its traditions and cultural heritage. Some of the most important festivals and customs in Palanpur include:

  • Navratri - This is a 9-day festival that is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga. It is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm in Palanpur.
  • Tarnetar Fair - This is one of the largest fairs in Gujarat and is held in Palanpur every year in the month of September. It is a celebration of the spirit of love and features colorful decorations and cultural performances.
  • Get-together - Palanpuris are known for their love of get-togethers and gatherings, and these are an important part of the city’s social fabric.

Best Time to Visit Palanpur

The best time to visit Palanpur is between October and March, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season, which lasts from July to September, is also a good time to visit Palanpur as the region looks beautiful after the rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palanpur

Do:

  • Learn some basic Gujarati phrases before traveling to Palanpur.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and street food.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Disrespect the local religious beliefs and customs.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palanpur

Palanpur is generally a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing. It is also advisable to avoid traveling alone at night and to stick to well-lit areas.

Health in Palanpur

Visitors to Palanpur should take the usual precautions to avoid food- and water-borne illnesses. It is also advisable to bring mosquito repellent and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Palanpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Palanpur is well-connected by road, rail, and air. Here are the transportation options to consider when traveling to Palanpur:

Airports Near Palanpur

The nearest airport to Palanpur is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad, which is located around 140 km away. The airport is well-connected to major cities in India and abroad, and visitors can book a taxi from the airport to reach Palanpur.

Bus Stations Near Palanpur

Palanpur has a well-connected bus station that is serviced by state-run and private buses. Visitors can easily book a bus ticket to Palanpur from major cities in Gujarat and Rajasthan.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi to reach Palanpur from neighboring cities such as Ahmedabad and Udaipur. It is also possible to travel to Palanpur by train, and the city has a well-connected railway station that is serviced by several trains.

How to Get Around in Palanpur

Palanpur is a small city, and most of the major attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw to explore the city. Bicycles are also available for hire if you wish to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Palanpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palanpur is located in a region of great scenic beauty, and there are several nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best excursions from Palanpur:

  1. Mt. Abu - This beautiful hill station is located just 70 km from Palanpur and is known for its stunning natural beauty and ancient temples.
  2. Ambaji - This holy town is located just 50 km from Palanpur and is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Amba. It is known for its magnificent temple and beautiful natural scenery.
  3. Patan - This historic town is located just 60 km from Palanpur and is known for its spectacular Rani-ki-Vav stepwell and its beautiful Patola sarees.

Conclusion

Palanpur is a city that is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. It offers a unique blend of old world charm and modern amenities, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all types. Whether you want to explore its rich heritage, indulge in its vibrant food scene, or experience its natural wonders, Palanpur has something for everyone. So why wait? Plan your trip to Palanpur today and discover the magic of this beautiful city!
