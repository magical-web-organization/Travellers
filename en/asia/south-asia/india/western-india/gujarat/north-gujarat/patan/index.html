A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Patan, Gujarat, India

Patan, located in North Gujarat in Western India, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Often overshadowed by its more famous neighbor, Ahmedabad, Patan has a lot to offer to history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers alike.

In this travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and everything else you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Patan, Gujarat, India.

Introduction to Patan

Patan, also known as Anhilwara, was once the capital of Gujarat during the Solanki dynasty. It is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including Rani ki Vav, a stepwell considered to be one of the most beautiful in India.

Patan has a rich history, dating back to the 8th century. The city has also been influenced by Islamic rule, and Mughal and Maratha empires.

List of Places to Visit in Patan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Rani ki Vav - a UNESCO World Heritage Site, considered to be the most intricately designed stepwell in India

  2. Sahastralinga Talav - a beautiful lake surrounded by temples and considered a must-visit for history buffs

  3. Panchasara Paraswani Jain Tirth Dham - a temple complex dedicated to Jainism

  4. Khan Sarovar - a man-made lake with several temples surrounding it

  5. Patan Patola Heritage - a museum showcasing the traditional art of Patan Patola weaving

Natural Attractions of Patan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mahavir Swami Wildlife Sanctuary - home to several endangered species of flora and fauna, including Sloth Bears and Indian Leopards

  2. Saraswati River - a scenic waterbody that flows through the city, perfect for picnic or boating

  3. Sujai river - a beautiful place with an abundance of greenery and is perfect for a leisurely stroll

  4. Taranga Hill - a scenic hill that offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape and is popular with trekkers and hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Patan

  1. Taranga Hill Trek - a challenging trek that takes you through beautiful landscapes and offers stunning views of the region.

  2. Mahavir Swami Wildlife Sanctuary Trek - a moderate trek that takes you through the wilderness of the sanctuary, where you can spot several species of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Patan: Activities and Tours

  1. Patan Heritage Walk - a guided tour of the city that takes you through historical sites and introduces you to the rich history and culture of the region.

  2. Patola Weaving Tours - a tour that takes you through the traditional art of Patola weaving, showcasing the intricate designs and techniques used by local artisans.

Food and Drink Scene of Patan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khaman Dhokla - a savory snack that is popular throughout Gujarat, made from fermented chickpea batter

  2. Sev Khamani - a dish made from Khaman Dhokla, topped with sev, onions, and chutneys

  3. Fafda Jalebi - a popular breakfast dish that consists of a crisp gram flour snack, accompanied by sweet jalebis.

Local Beverages

  1. Buttermilk - a refreshing drink made from yogurt and water, with a pinch of spices

  2. Sodaa - a local soft drink made with soda water, lime juice, and sugar

Where to Stay in Patan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Grand Raveta - a luxury hotel in the heart of the city, with modern amenities and spacious rooms.

  2. Tulsi Motel - a budget-friendly option, located near the historic sites in Patan.

What to Buy in Patan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Patola Silk Sarees - These intricately designed silk sarees are the highlight of the local markets and are loved for their rich history and craftsmanship.

  2. Traditional Indian Jewelry - From silver tribal jewelry to gold and diamond pieces, Patan is home to some of the most exquisite jewelry stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Patan

Patan is known for its rich cultural heritage and customs. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local traditions and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Patan

The best time to visit Patan is from October to March, during the winter season when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without any inconvenience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Patan

Do:

  1. Dress modestly and respect local traditions and customs.

  2. Carry sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water, always opt for bottled water.

  2. Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Patan

Patan is a relatively safe destination, and the locals are known for their hospitality and warmth. Visitors should keep an eye on their belongings, however.

Health in Patan

It is recommended that visitors take necessary vaccines before visiting Patan. Additionally, visitors should carry their own medication and take all necessary precautions to avoid any kind of illness.

Getting to Patan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Patan

The nearest airport to Patan is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad, which is approximately 124 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Patan

The closest bus station to Patan is located in the city of Mehsana, which is approximately 60 km away from Patan.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, book a taxi or take a train to reach Patan.

How to Get Around in Patan

The best way to get around in Patan is by hired taxi, auto-rickshaw, or renting a car. Additionally, visitors can also explore the city on foot.

Where to Go Next from Patan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ahmedabad - A bustling city with a vibrant culture and many historical sites, Ahmedabad is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Patan.

  2. Modhera - Located just 50 km away from Patan, Modhera is famous for the Sun Temple, another UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Patan is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty that makes it a unique and unforgettable destination. We hope this travel guide gives you all the information you need to plan your trip to Patan, Gujarat, India. Enjoy your stay!
