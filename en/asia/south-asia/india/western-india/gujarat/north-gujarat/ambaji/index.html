Introduction to Ambaji

Ambaji is a small town located in the Banaskantha district of the North Gujarat region in the western state of Gujarat in India. This town is known for its stunning temples, rich cultural heritage, scenic views, and pleasant weather. Whether you’re a spiritual person, a nature lover or a history buff, Ambaji has something to offer to everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with a list of places to visit, best things to do, food and drink options, where to stay, and important information about the local culture and customs of Ambaji.

List of Places to Visit in Ambaji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ambaji Temple

Ambaji Temple is the most famous and revered temple in the town of Ambaji, dedicated to Goddess Amba. The temple is located at the foothills of the Aravalli mountain range and is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus. The temple is open all day, and the best time to visit is during Navaratri when the temple is beautifully lit up.

Kumbhariyaji Jain Temple

Kumbhariyaji Jain Temple is a must-visit temple for those interested in Jainism. The temple is located on a hill and is dedicated to Lord Parshvanath. The temple is made of marble and has intricate carvings on the walls and pillars.

Natural Attractions of Ambaji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gabbar Hills

Gabbar Hills is a popular tourist spot and is located around 4 km from Ambaji Temple. It is considered to be the highest point in the Aravali mountain range and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can reach the peak by climbing around 200 steps or by taking a cable car ride.

Mansarovar Lake

Mansarovar Lake is a tranquil lake located around 2 km from Ambaji Temple. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery, and visitors can enjoy boating or take a leisurely walk along the lake’s shore to soak in the stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ambaji

Gabbar Hills Trek

The Gabbar Hills Trek is a popular option for adventure seekers who love hiking. The trek offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and takes around two hours to complete.

Ambaji to Kedarnath Trek

The Ambaji to Kedarnath Trek is a great option for those who love trekking and exploring the wilderness. The trek offers stunning views of the rugged mountains and valleys in the region and takes around 6 to 7 days to complete.

Best Things to Do in Ambaji: Activities and Tours

Experience the Local Culture and Customs

Visitors can experience the local culture and customs of Ambaji by taking a walking tour of the town. You can visit the bustling local markets and interact with the locals to learn more about the region’s unique customs and traditions.

Visit the Ambaji Wildlife Sanctuary

The Ambaji Wildlife Sanctuary is a great place to explore the region’s flora and fauna. The sanctuary has a diverse range of wildlife, including leopards, chitals, sambars, and langurs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ambaji

  • Visitors are expected to dress modestly and cover their heads while visiting the temples.
  • Shoes are not allowed inside the temples.
  • Alcohol consumption and smoking in public places are strictly prohibited.

Best Time to Visit Ambaji

The best time to visit Ambaji is during the winter season from November to February when the weather is pleasant and cool. The monsoon season from July to September is also a great time to visit if you enjoy the rain and lush greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ambaji

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly while visiting the temples.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places.
  • Enter the temples wearing shoes or without covering your head.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ambaji

Ambaji is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions and avoid venturing out alone at night.

Health in Ambaji

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food to avoid any health issues. It is also recommended to carry basic medication and first aid kit while traveling.

Getting to Ambaji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ambaji

The nearest airports to Ambaji are the Ahmedabad Airport and the Vadodara Airport. Both airports are well-connected to major Indian cities.

Bus Stations Near Ambaji

Ambaji is well-connected to major cities in Gujarat by bus. The nearest bus station is located in the Ambaji town, and visitors can hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw to reach their destination.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for private cabs or taxis to reach Ambaji from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Ambaji

Visitors can hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw to get around the town. Bicycles and motorbikes are also available for rent.
