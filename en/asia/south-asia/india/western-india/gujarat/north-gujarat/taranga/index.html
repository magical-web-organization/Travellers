Introduction to Taranga

Taranga is a small village situated in the Mehsana district of Gujarat, India. It is a hidden gem that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of the region. The village is located at the foothills of the Taranga hills, which are home to the famous Taranga Jain temple. This temple is a major attraction in the area, drawing thousands of visitors every year. With its serene atmosphere, stunning landscapes, and numerous tourist opportunities, Taranga is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic rural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Taranga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Taranga is known for its historical and cultural significance. It is home to several museums and historical sites that offer visitors a glimpse into the region’s rich past. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Taranga:

  • Taranga Jain Temple: This 12th-century temple is dedicated to Lord Adinath, the first Tirthankara of Jainism. It is located on the top of the Taranga hills and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Kabirvad: Kabirvad is a small island that lies in the Narmada river. It is considered an important pilgrimage site for Hindus as well as Jains.
  • Bhadra Fort: Bhadra Fort is a historical monument that was built during the reign of Ahmad Shah I in the 14th century. The fort contains a palace, a mosque, and several gardens.
  • Sidi Sayed Mosque: The Sidi Sayed Mosque is a historical mosque located in the old city of Ahmedabad. It is renowned for its intricate stone-carved windows.

Natural Attractions of Taranga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Taranga is surrounded by nature and provides ample opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Taranga:

  • Taranga Hills: The Taranga hills are a popular hiking destination that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can trek up to the Taranga Jain temple at the top of the hill for a breathtaking panorama.
  • Kabirvad Island: Kabirvad Island is a beautiful natural paradise that offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can take a boat ride along the Narmada river and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Kumbharia Jain Temple: The Kumbharia Jain temple is located in the small village of Kumbhariya, close to Taranga. The temple is surrounded by beautiful forests and offers visitors a serene atmosphere to meditate and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Hinglaj Mata Temple: The Hinglaj Mata temple is located on the banks of the Saraswati river and is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery and explore the nearby forests and riverbanks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taranga

Taranga is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Taranga hills offer several trekking routes that are suitable for all levels of hikers. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Taranga include:

  • Taranga Hill Trek: The Taranga Hill trek is a popular hiking route that takes visitors to the top of the Taranga hills. The trek takes around 3-4 hours and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Kabirvad Island Trek: The Kabirvad Island trek is a beautiful hiking route that takes visitors along the banks of the Narmada river to the small island of Kabirvad. The trek takes around 3-4 hours and offers beautiful views of the river and the surrounding forests.
  • Hinglaj Mata Temple Trek: The Hinglaj Mata Temple trek is a popular hiking route that takes visitors through the beautiful forests and riverbanks surrounding the temple.

Best Things to Do in Taranga: Activities and Tours

Taranga offers visitors numerous opportunities for adventure, exploration, and relaxation. Here are some of the best things to do in Taranga:

  • Visit the Taranga Jain Temple: The Taranga Jain temple is a must-visit destination for travelers to Taranga. The temple offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and history of the region.
  • Explore the Surrounding Forests: Taranga is surrounded by beautiful forests that are perfect for nature walks and birdwatching. Visitors can explore the nearby woods and enjoy the fresh air.
  • Take a Boat Ride to Kabirvad Island: Visitors can take a boat ride along the Narmada river to Kabirvad Island and explore the beautiful surroundings.
  • Experience Rural Life: Taranga is an idyllic rural village that offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience the local way of life. Visitors can interact with the local people, enjoy home-cooked meals, and learn about the local culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Taranga

Taranga offers visitors a unique culinary experience that blends the flavors of Gujarat with the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Taranga:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khandvi: Khandvi is a popular snack made from gram flour and yogurt. It is garnished with grated coconut and green chilies and is a must-try dish in Taranga.
  • Undhiyu: Undhiyu is a popular Gujarati dish made from a mixture of vegetables and spices.
  • Puran Poli: Puran Poli is a sweet bread made from wheat flour, jaggery, and lentils. It is a popular dessert in Gujarat.
  • Dhokla: Dhokla is a Gujarati snack made from fermented batter. It is a popular breakfast dish in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Chhaas: Chhaas is a refreshing drink made from buttermilk, cumin, and mint leaves. It is a popular drink in Gujarat.
  • Sugarcane Juice: Sugarcane juice is a popular drink in Taranga and is available at most street vendors.
  • Tea: Tea is a popular drink in Gujarat and is served with milk and sugar.

Where to Stay in Taranga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taranga is a small village and does not have many accommodation options. However, visitors can find several guesthouses and homestays that offer a comfortable stay. Here are some popular accommodation options in Taranga:

  • Taranga Hill Resort: Taranga Hill Resort is a popular hotel located close to the Taranga Jain temple. The hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Bhadra Farm: Bhadra Farm is a homestay located close to the Bhadra Fort. The homestay offers cozy rooms, home-cooked meals, and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Shantivan Farm & Resort: Shantivan Farm & Resort is a popular resort located close to the Taranga hills. The resort offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

What to Buy in Taranga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taranga is known for its handicrafts and local products. Visitors can find several shops in the area that sell handmade goods and souvenirs. Here are some popular products to buy in Taranga:

  • Handicrafts: Taranga is known for its intricate handicrafts such as pottery, embroidery, and beadwork. Visitors can find several shops selling handmade goods and souvenirs in the area.
  • Spices: Gujarat is known for its spices, and visitors to Taranga can buy packets of freshly ground spices to take home with them.
  • Handmade Clothes: Visitors can buy clothes made from locally grown cotton and silk. The clothes are hand-loomed and have intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Taranga

Taranga is a multicultural village that celebrates several festivals and traditions. Visitors can participate in these festivals and learn about the local culture and customs. Here are some popular festivals celebrated in Taranga:

  • Navratri: Navratri is a 9-day festival celebrated in Gujarat. It is a celebration of the Hindu goddess Durga and involves dancing, feasting, and socializing.
  • Diwali: Diwali is the festival of lights and is celebrated throughout India. The festival involves lighting lamps and fireworks and is a celebration of good over evil.
  • Uttarayan: Uttarayan is the kite festival celebrated in Gujarat. It is a social festival that involves flying kites and feasting.

Best Time to Visit Taranga

The best time to visit Taranga is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant and sunny. The temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C and it is a great time to enjoy outdoor activities and explore the natural beauty of the region.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taranga

Taranga is a safe village and visitors are unlikely to encounter any serious safety concerns. However, it is advisable to take the following precautions:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables such as cash, passports, and cameras in a safe place.
  • Be careful when exploring the outdoors and follow all safety precautions.

Health in Taranga

Visitors to Taranga are advised to take the following health precautions:

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Carry mosquito repellent and a first aid kit.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you feel unwell.

Getting to Taranga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taranga is well-connected to the rest of Gujarat by road and rail. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Taranga

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport: The nearest airport to Taranga is the Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad. The airport is around 85 km away from Taranga and is well-connected by road.

Bus Stations Near Taranga

  • Mehsana Bus Station: Mehsana Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Taranga. It is around 40 km away and is well-connected to the rest of Gujarat.
  • Ahmedabad Bus Station: Ahmedabad Bus Station is around 85 km away from Taranga and is a major bus hub in Gujarat.

Other Options

  • Train: Taranga has its own railway station, located on the Mehsana-Ahmedabad railway line. Visitors can take a train from Ahmedabad or Mehsana to reach Taranga.
  • Taxi: Visitors can hire a taxi from Ahmedabad or Mehsana to reach Taranga.

How to Get Around in Taranga

Taranga is a small village and can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a taxi or a local guide to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Taranga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that visitors can explore from Taranga:

  • Ahmedabad: Ahmedabad is a major city in Gujarat and is known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and delicious cuisine. Visitors can explore the old city, visit museums and art galleries, and enjoy the vibrant nightlife.
  • Mehsana: Mehsana is a small city located around 40 km from Taranga. It is known for its beautiful temples, handicrafts, and local cuisine.
  • Modhera: Modhera is a small town located around 70 km from Taranga. It is known for its beautiful Sun Temple, which is an important Hindu pilgrimage site.

Conclusion

Taranga is a beautiful village nestled in the foothills of the Taranga hills. It offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience rural life and explore the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Gujarat. With its serene atmosphere, stunning landscapes, and numerous tourist opportunities, Taranga is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic rural experience.
