Introduction to Mehsana

Mehsana is a city located in the North Gujarat region of Gujarat and is known for its rich history, culture, and natural beauty. It is also famous for its manufacturing industry, particularly in production and distribution of dairy products.

List of Places to Visit in Mehsana

There are various tourist attractions in Mehsana that are worth a visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Modhera Sun Temple
  • Mehsana Jain Derasar
  • Rajmahal Palace
  • Vadnagar Archaeological Site
  • Kadi Sarva Vishwavidyalaya

Natural Attractions of Mehsana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary
  • Shanku’s Water Park
  • GPRS Adventure Club
  • Kankariya Lake
  • Zanzari Waterfall

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mehsana

Mehsana offers great opportunities for hikers and bikers. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Mehsana - Modhera - Patan
  • Mehsana - Vijapur - Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary
  • Mehsana - Kadi - Matar

Best Things to Do in Mehsana: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Mehsana are:

  • Visit the Modhera Sun Temple
  • Explore the Vadnagar Archaeological Site
  • Relax at Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary
  • Go on a trekking expedition to Zanzari Waterfall
  • Take a tour of Shanku’s Water Park
  • Go for village safari tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Mehsana

Day 1: Arrive in Mehsana, visit the Jain Derasar, and Rajmahal Palace. Day 2: Take a trip to Vadnagar and explore the Archaeological Site. Day 3: Visit the Modhera Sun Temple and enjoy the sunset at the temple. Day 4: Go for a hiking trail to Zanzari Waterfall. Day 5: Relax at Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary and enjoy bird watching. Day 6: Take a tour of Shanku’s Water Park and GPRS Adventure Club. Day 7: Visit Kadi Sarva Vishwavidyalaya and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Mehsana

Mehsana is known for its Gujarati cuisine, particularly for their vegetarian delicacies. Some of the must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dhokla
  • Khandvi
  • Khaman
  • Thepla
  • Fafda
  • Jalebi

Local Beverages

  • Chaas
  • Buttermilk
  • Aam pana

What to Buy in Mehsana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, Mehsana is known for its:

  • Traditional handcrafted items like wall hangings, bags, and purses

Local Culture and Customs of Mehsana

The people of Mehsana are predominantly Hindu and celebrate various festivals throughout the year. One of the most popular festivals is Navratri, which is celebrated with great pomp and show.

Best Time to Visit Mehsana

The best time to visit Mehsana is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature ranges from 20 to 30 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mehsana

Do:

  • Dress conservatively
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies

Don’t:

  • Carry non-veg food in Jain temples
  • Take photos of locals without their permission
  • Litter

Safety and Health

Safety in Mehsana

Mehsana is a safe city, but it’s always better to take precautions. Avoid venturing out alone at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Mehsana

It’s advisable to carry sufficient medication if you have a medical condition. Always drink bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Mehsana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mehsana

The nearest airport to Mehsana is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad, which is located 55 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mehsana

Mehsana has a bus station that connects it to major cities and towns in Gujarat.

Other Options

You can also opt for trains that connect Mehsana to various parts of India.

How to Get Around in Mehsana

The best way to get around Mehsana is by hiring local taxis, cabs, or renting two-wheelers or cars.

Where to Go Next from Mehsana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Patan
  • Ahmedabad
  • Gandhinagar
  • Modhera

Conclusion

Mehsana is a charming city that combines history, culture, and natural beauty. It’s a perfect destination for those looking to explore offbeat places and discover the essence of Gujarat. Plan your trip to Mehsana and create memories that last a lifetime.
