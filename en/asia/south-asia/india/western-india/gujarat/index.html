India is a diverse, vibrant country that is home to many different regions, each with its unique characteristics. Gujarat is one such region located in Western India, which is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. With a coastline that stretches over 1,600 km, Gujarat is a must-see destination for any traveler looking to explore India. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know about visiting Gujarat, including the must-visit places, where to stay, and what to do.

Introduction to Gujarat

Gujarat is the sixth-largest state in India, borders Pakistan to the northwest, and is surrounded by other Indian states like Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, and Maharashtra. The state is famous for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking landscapes, and mouth-watering cuisine. The state capital is Gandhinagar, while its largest city is Ahmedabad. The population is composed of diverse communities, making the state a cultural kaleidoscope. Gujarati, Hindi, and English are the primary languages spoken in Gujarat.

List of Places to Visit in Gujarat

Gujarat has an abundance of interesting places to visit, from museums and historical landmarks to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of some must-visit places in Gujarat:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sabarmati Ashram, Ahmedabad
  • Calico Museum of Textiles, Ahmedabad
  • Laxmi Vilas Palace, Vadodara
  • Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, Ahmedabad
  • Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park, Vadodara

Natural Attractions of Gujarat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary, Kutch
  • Sasan Gir National Park, Junagadh
  • Girnar Hill, Junagadh
  • Great Rann of Kutch, Kutch
  • Somnath Temple, Gir Somnath

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gujarat

Gujarat has excellent hiking and biking routes that cut through its scenic landscapes. Whether you’re a hiking enthusiast or a leisure cyclist, Gujarat has something for you. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Gujarat include:

  • Polo Forest, Vijaynagar
  • Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary, Ahmedabad
  • Zarwani Waterfall, Panchmahal
  • Wilson Hills, Valsad
  • Nalsarovar Bird Sanctuary, Ahmedabad

Best Things to Do in Gujarat: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to do in Gujarat, from exploring the state’s history and culture to soaking in the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Gujarat:

  • Visit the White Desert of Kutch during the Rann Utsav Festival
  • Take a wildlife safari in Sasan Gir National Park
  • Attend the International Kite Festival in Ahmedabad
  • Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park
  • Learn about Mahatma Gandhi’s life at his home, Sabarmati Ashram, in Ahmedabad.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gujarat

A week-long trip to Gujarat is the perfect amount of time to explore the region. Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Gujarat:

Day 1-3: Ahmedabad

  • Visit Sabarmati Ashram, Calico Museum of Textiles, and Sidi Saiyyed Mosque
  • Explore Chandola Lake, Kankaria Lake, and Adalaj Stepwell
  • Enjoy the delicious street food of the old city

Day 4-5: Vadodara

  • Visit Laxmi Vilas Palace
  • Explore the UNESCO World Heritage site of Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park
  • Relax in Sayaji Baug

Day 6-7: Kutch

  • Visit the Great Rann of Kutch and enjoy the Rann Utsav Festival
  • Explore Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary

Food and Drink Scene of Gujarat

Gujarat has a distinct cuisine that is unique in India. The food is mainly vegetarian, with a mix of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Gujarat:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dhokla
  • Undhiyu
  • Khandvi
  • Handvo
  • Fafda-Jalebi

Local Beverages

  • Chaas (Buttermilk)
  • Aam Papad Sharbat (Mango Juice)
  • Jal Jeera (Cumin Water)
  • Sattu Drink

Where to Stay in Gujarat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gujarat offers a range of accommodations to cater to everyone’s taste and budget. From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts, you can find accommodation that meets your needs. Here are some top-rated places to stay in Gujarat:

  • The Fern, Ahmedabad
  • Madhubhan Resort and Spa, Anand
  • The Gateway Hotel, Vadodara
  • Hilltop Wellness Retreat, Saputara
  • The Kutch Safari Lodge, Kutch

What to Buy in Gujarat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gujarat is a treasure trove for shopping enthusiasts. The state is famous for its textiles, handicrafts, and spices. Here are some local products to bring home with you:

  • Bandhani (tie-dye) clothing
  • Patola Sarees
  • Handicrafts like wooden toys and embroidered items
  • Spices like jeera and dhaniya

Local Culture and Customs of Gujarat

Gujarat’s culture is as diverse and vibrant as its people. The state celebrates many festivals throughout the year, including Navratri, Diwali, and Uttarayan. The people of Gujarat are known for their hospitality, and you’ll find many locals eager to share their culture with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Gujarat

The best time to visit Gujarat is from November to February when the weather is pleasant. Summers from March to June can be hot and humid, while the monsoon months of July to September can bring heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gujarat

Do:

  • Dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees
  • Remove shoes before entering a religious site
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public
  • Hug or touch someone of the opposite gender in public
  • It is not acceptable to kiss, even on the cheek, in public or private as it is against the culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gujarat

Gujarat is safe for travelers, but you should exercise caution and follow standard safety precautions, like avoiding walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas.

Health in Gujarat

Visitors to Gujarat should take precautions to avoid sickness. These include drinking bottled water only, practicing good hygiene, and being up-to-date on vaccinations.

Getting to Gujarat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gujarat

Gujarat has several airports, including:

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport, Ahmedabad
  • Vadodara Airport, Vadodara
  • Kandla Airport, Kandla

Bus Stations Near Gujarat

Many buses connect Gujarat to other nearby cities and states.

Other Options

You can also reach Gujarat by train as the state has an extensive rail network.

How to Get Around in Gujarat

The easiest way to get around Gujarat is by hiring a taxi, using ride-sharing apps, or using public transportation like buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Gujarat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gujarat is well-connected to other nearby cities and states in India, making it an ideal base for excursions. Here are some destinations you can visit after exploring Gujarat:

  • Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Udaipur, Rajasthan
  • Mumbai, Maharashtra
  • Goa
  • Ahmedabad, Gujarat

Conclusion

Gujarat is a unique and vibrant state in India, filled with fascinating cultural landmarks, scenic landscapes, and delicious food. It’s a must-see destination that offers something for everyone, whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to Gujarat, and we’re sure you’ll enjoy exploring all that this state has to offer.
