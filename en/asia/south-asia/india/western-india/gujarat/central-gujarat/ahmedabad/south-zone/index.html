Introduction to South Zone

South Zone in Ahmedabad, Central Gujarat, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Western India. With its rich cultural heritage and diverse landscape of natural wonders, South Zone is a must-visit destination on any trip to India.

From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, South Zone offers something for everyone. In this tour guide, we will explore the top tourist attractions, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations to visit in South Zone.

List of Places to Visit in South Zone

South Zone is home to several culturally important attractions, natural wonders, hiking/biking routes, and top-rated accommodations. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in South Zone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sabarmati Ashram: A living museum of Mahatma Gandhi’s life and philosophy located on the banks of the Sabarmati River.
  • Calico Museum of Textiles: A world-renowned textile museum that preserves and showcases Indian textile history.
  • Sidi Saiyyed Mosque: A medieval mosque, known for its intricate and beautiful architecture.
  • Jama Masjid: A stunning mosque built in the 15th century by Sultan Ahmed Shah, located in the heart of the city.
  • Adalaj Stepwell: A beautifully crafted stepwell, with five stories and a stunningly complex system of columns and beams.

Natural Attractions of South Zone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kankaria Lake: A man-made lake offering a wide range of boating and other water activities.
  • Vastrapur Lake: A scenic lake offering jogging, boating, and other outdoor activities.
  • Thol Bird Sanctuary: A natural habitat for migratory birds, this sanctuary is home to over 100 species of birds.
  • Kadia Dungar Caves: A cluster of caves located on the banks of the Sabarmati River, with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Nalsarovar Bird Sanctuary: A peaceful sanctuary and home to over 250 species of exotic birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Zone

South Zone offers multiple hiking and biking routes, through which visitors can explore the scenic beauty and lush green forests of Gujarat. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Ahmedabad to Polo Forest: A scenic route covering 180 km through diverse landscapes.
  • Ahmedabad to Mount Abu: A biking route that takes you through the Aravalli Range, covering a distance of 230 km.
  • Sabarmati Riverfront Cycling Tour: An easy biking route along the banks of the Sabarmati River, with stunning views of the city.

Best Things to Do in South Zone: Activities and Tours

South Zone offers a plethora of experiences and activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in South Zone:

  • Visit the Adalaj Stepwell and learn about the ancient architecture of Gujarat.
  • Take a boat ride on the Kankaria Lake.
  • Explore the Calico Museum and learn about the rich textile history of India.
  • Go birdwatching at Nalsarovar Bird Sanctuary.
  • Take a stroll on the Sabarmati Riverfront and soak in the views of the city.
  • Attend the International Kite Festival, one of the biggest festivals in Gujarat.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Zone

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for South Zone:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Sabarmati Ashram and learn about Gandhi’s life and philosophy.
Day 2 Take a day trip to Polo Forest and explore the natural beauty of Gujarat.
Day 3 Explore the Calico Museum of Textiles and learn about the history of Indian textiles.
Day 4 Visit the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque and Jama Masjid, two of the most beautiful mosques in Ahmedabad.
Day 5 Take a cycling tour on the Sabarmati Riverfront and enjoy the views of the city.
Day 6 Go birdwatching at Thol Bird Sanctuary or Nalsarovar Bird Sanctuary.
Day 7 Attend the International Kite Festival or explore the Kadia Dungar Caves.

Food and Drink Scene of South Zone

Gujarat is well-known for its vegetarian food and a wide variety of snacks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in South Zone:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dhokla: A savory snack made of fermented rice and chickpeas.
  • Khandvi: Thin rolls made from a savory batter of gram flour and yogurt.
  • Fafda: A popular Gujarati snack made from gram flour, served with chutney.
  • Undhiyu: A spicy vegetable curry made with winter vegetables and spices.
  • Thepla: A flatbread made with whole wheat flour and various spices.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: A savory yogurt drink seasoned with cumin, salt, and ginger.
  • Lassi: A sweet yogurt drink made with sugar or honey and garnished with cardamom.

Where to Stay in South Zone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Zone offers a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some options:

  • ITC Narmada, Ahmedabad: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and stunning views of the Sabarmati River.
  • The House Of MG, Ahmedabad: A stylish boutique hotel with traditional Gujarati décor.
  • Hyatt Regency, Ahmedabad: A 5-star hotel with a rooftop pool and modern amenities.
  • Backpacker Panda, Ahmedabad: A budget-friendly hostel with clean dorms and private rooms.
  • Hotel Skylon, Ahmedabad: A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms and an in-house restaurant.

What to Buy in South Zone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Zone offers a unique range of souvenirs and local products for tourists to take home. Here are some ideas:

  • Handicrafts: Ahmedabad is known for its rich handicraft culture, from embroidered textiles to pottery and woodworking.
  • Spices and Snacks: Gujarat is known for its unique blend of spices, and these can be found in local markets.
  • Bandhani Sarees: Bandhani is a traditional tie-dye technique used on fabrics, and Gujarat is well-known for its beautiful bandhani sarees.

Local Culture and Customs of South Zone

Gujarat has a rich cultural heritage, with a vibrant art scene, music, dance, and an array of festivals throughout the year. Some customs to keep in mind while traveling in South Zone include:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Always remove your shoes before entering temples, mosques, or anyone’s home.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit South Zone

The best time to visit South Zone in Ahmedabad is between October and March, as the weather is comfortable and pleasant. April and May tend to be very hot and humid, while the monsoon season (June to September) can bring heavy rains and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Zone

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Haggle with vendors in local markets.
  • Keep bottled water handy.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when exploring cultural sites.
  • Eat street food without checking hygiene standards.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Zone

South Zone is a safe and secure destination for tourists. However, always keep safety precautions in mind, especially when walking around at night or in crowded places.

Health in South Zone

Make sure to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summers, and take precautions to avoid mosquitoes, as they can carry diseases like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to South Zone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Zone

The nearest airport to South Zone is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad. From here, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach your accommodation.

Bus Stations Near South Zone

Ahmedabad has several bus stations that connect to other major cities in Gujarat and neighboring states.

Other Options

There are also several trains that connect Ahmedabad to other parts of India, including major cities like Mumbai and Delhi.

How to Get Around in South Zone

South Zone is well-connected by local transportation options like buses and taxis, and Ahmedabad also has a bike-sharing program called “ATBike.” It is best to hire a private taxi for long-distance travels.

Where to Go Next from South Zone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Zone is surrounded by several popular tourist destinations that make for excellent day trips. Here are some nearby destinations:

  • Mount Abu: A hill station located in the Aravalli Range, famous for its tranquil lakes and scenic views.
  • Rann of Kutch: A desert region known for its spectacular natural beauty and colorful handicrafts.
  • Vadodara: A historic city known for its rich architectural heritage, museums, and galleries.

Conclusion

South Zone is a hidden gem in Gujarat, with its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. From exploring the ancient architecture of mosques and visiting living museums of famous personalities like Mahatma Gandhi to indulging in local delicacies and enjoying outdoor activities, South Zone is an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Gujarat.
