Introduction to East Zone

East Zone is a popular destination in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, that offers a blend of the past and the present. It is located in Central Gujarat, which is considered the heart of the state. East Zone has a rich history and culture, and this is evident in the many landmarks, museums, and historical sites in the area.

List of Places to Visit in East Zone

When visiting East Zone, there are several must-visit attractions. These attractions are categorized into two sections: culturally important attractions, and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East Zone has numerous museums and historical places that provide a glimpse into the city’s past. Here are some of the most notable:

  • Sabarmati Ashram: This is the iconic ashram where Mahatma Gandhi spent a considerable amount of time. It is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about India’s history.

  • Sidi Saiyyed Mosque: Constructed in the early 16th century, this mosque is one of the most prominent monuments in Ahmedabad. It is known for its intricate stone lattice work.

  • Calico Museum of Textiles: This is a one-of-a-kind museum that showcases the region’s textile history. It has an exceptional collection of textiles, including fabrics, costumes, and handicrafts.

  • Ahmed Shah’s Mosque: Built-in 1414, this ancient mosque is one of the oldest in Ahmedabad. It is renowned for its intricate carvings and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of East Zone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Zone is an excellent place for nature lovers. Here are some of the natural attractions that you can explore:

  • Kankaria Lake: This man-made lake is a popular spot for boating, jogging, and picnicking. It is surrounded by a beautiful garden and a recreational park.

  • Vastrapur Lake: This is another popular lake that is perfect for a scenic stroll or a jog. The lake is surrounded by lush green lawns and is an excellent place to relax.

  • Sabarmati Riverfront: The Sabarmati Riverfront is a development project that has transformed the Sabarmati River into a beautiful recreational space. It is an excellent place to go for a walk or a jog and enjoy the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Zone

East Zone has several hiking and biking trails that provide an excellent opportunity to explore the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Sabarmati Riverfront Trail: The riverfront trail is a 10 km stretch that is perfect for a leisurely walk, a jog, or a bike ride. It provides panoramic views of the river and the city.

  • Kankaria Lake Trail: This is a 3.5 km trail that goes around the lake. It is ideal for a walk or a bike ride.

Best Things to Do in East Zone: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the attractions mentioned above, there are several other things to do in East Zone. Here are some of the most popular activities and tours:

  • Food Tours: East Zone is known for its delicious food, and a food tour is an excellent way to explore the local cuisine.

  • Heritage Walk: A walk through East Zone is a great way to explore the region’s rich history and culture. You can take a guided heritage walk to get a better understanding of the region’s significance.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Zone

If you’re planning a trip to East Zone and have a week to spare, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Sabarmati Riverfront, Kite Museum, and Sabarmati Ashram

Day 2: Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, Calico Museum of Textiles, and Sanskar Kendra Museum

Day 3: Ahmed Shah’s Mosque, Jama Masjid, and Hathee Singh Jain Temple

Day 4: Kankaria Lake, Nagina Wadi, and Bal Vatika

Day 5: Vastrapur Lake, Prerna Tirth Jain Temple, and Pyramid Crystal Lawns

Day 6: Bhadra Fort, Swaminarayan Temple, and Rani no Hajiro

Day 7: Sabarmati Riverfront Night Walk and Manek Chowk Night Market

Food and Drink Scene of East Zone

East Zone has a vibrant food and drink scene that is rooted in the region’s rich cultural heritage.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the must-try dishes in East Zone:

  • Dhokla: This is a steamed savory cake made from gram flour. It is a staple in Gujarati cuisine, and East Zone is known for its delicious dhoklas.

  • Khandvi: This is a savory snack made from gram flour and yogurt. It is steamed and then rolled into tight cylinders, and is best enjoyed with some chutney.

Local Beverages

Here are some of the local beverages that you can try in East Zone:

  • Chai: You can find chai stalls all over East Zone. It is a popular beverage that is made from black tea, milk, sugar, and spices.

  • Buttermilk: Buttermilk is a refreshing drink that is made from yogurt, water, and spices. It is the perfect way to beat the heat in East Zone.

Where to Stay in East Zone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Zone has several accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best options:

  • The House of MG: This is a beautiful heritage hotel that is perfect for a luxurious stay.

  • Hotel Neelkanth Inn: This is a budget-friendly hotel that provides clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in East Zone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Zone is known for its beautiful handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Bandhani Sarees: Bandhani is a tie-dye technique that is specific to Gujarat. A Bandhani saree is a must-buy for anyone looking for a beautiful and unique textile.

  • Kutch Embroidery: Kutch embroidery is a beautiful type of embroidery that originates from the Kutch region of Gujarat. You can find several beautiful pieces in East Zone.

  • Silver Jewelry: East Zone is known for its intricate silver jewelry. You can find several designs that are unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of East Zone

East Zone has a vibrant culture and rich history that is evident in its architecture, food, and customs. The people of East Zone are friendly and hospitable, and it is essential to respect their customs and traditions when visiting the region.

Best Time to Visit East Zone

The best time to visit East Zone is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and cool. The summer months, from March to June, can be scorching and uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Zone

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the landmarks and historical places

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs
  • Take photographs without permission
  • Drink alcohol in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in East Zone

East Zone is generally safe, but it is advisable to take precautions like avoiding deserted areas at night, protecting your belongings, and being wary of strangers.

Health in East Zone

It is essential to stay hydrated and carry sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun. You should also avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water.

Getting to East Zone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Zone

The nearest airport to East Zone is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport, which is about 11 km away.

Bus Stations Near East Zone

The nearest bus station to East Zone is Kalupur Bus Station, which is about 7 km away.

Other Options

You can also reach East Zone by train, taxi, or auto-rickshaw.

How to Get Around in East Zone

The best way to get around in East Zone is by auto-rickshaw or taxi. You can also explore the region on foot, but it is essential to carry a map or a guidebook.

Where to Go Next from East Zone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from East Zone:

  • Vadodara: This is a beautiful city that is known for its palaces, museums, and parks. It is about 100 km from East Zone.

  • Mount Abu: Mount Abu is a beautiful hill station that is about 220 km from East Zone. It is known for its scenic beauty and is an excellent place for a weekend getaway.

Conclusion

East Zone is a beautiful destination that has something to offer to everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, the region has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to East Zone and explore the best that the region has to offer.
