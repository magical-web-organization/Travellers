Introduction to Anand

Located in Central Gujarat, Anand is a bustling city that boasts a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. Known as the “Milk Capital of India,” Anand is famous for its dairy industry and is home to the famous Amul dairy cooperative.

Aside from its dairy industry, Anand is also a hub of education and trade, with several renowned universities and educational institutions, as well as vibrant markets and bazaars. Whether you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Anand has plenty to offer, from museums and historical landmarks to scenic trails and outdoor adventures.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a virtual tour of Anand and help you discover the best places to visit, food to try, and activities to do in this charming city.

List of Places to Visit in Anand

Here are some of the top attractions in Anand that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amul Dairy Museum: Located in the heart of Anand, the Amul Dairy Museum is a fascinating exhibition that showcases the history and development of the dairy industry in India.
  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel and Veer Vithalbhai Patel Memorial: This grand memorial is dedicated to the memory of two famous brothers who played a key role in the Indian independence movement.
  • Kirti Mandir: Built in honor of the great leader Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, Kirti Mandir is a beautiful temple that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Gujarat.

Natural Attractions of Anand: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sursagar Lake: A picturesque lake located in the center of the city, Sursagar Lake is a popular spot for picnics and boating.
  • Vadtal Swaminarayan Temple: This majestic temple is nestled amidst lush greenery and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Khodiyar Mata Temple: Set in the midst of tranquil surroundings, this temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Khodiyar and is one of the most popular places to visit in Anand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anand

If you’re an adventure enthusiast, Anand has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the landscape. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • Anand - Sojitra - Bakrol - Anand Circuit: This 25-kilometer circuit is perfect for avid bikers who want to explore the scenic countryside and rural villages of Anand.
  • Anand - Borsad - Anand Circuit: This 30-kilometer circuit takes you through the lush greenery of Anand’s countryside and offers sweeping views of the landscape.
  • Anand - Khambhat - Anand Circuit: This 50-kilometer circuit is perfect for experienced bikers who want to challenge themselves and explore the rugged terrain of Anand.

Best Things to Do in Anand: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a culture vulture or an adrenaline junkie, Anand has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a Heritage Walk: Anand is home to several ancient temples and monuments that offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Gujarat. Take a guided heritage walk and explore the city’s magnificent heritage.
  • Visit the Becharaji Bird Sanctuary: Located just a short drive from Anand, the Becharaji Bird Sanctuary is a paradise for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Spot a wide variety of bird species, including spoonbills, egrets, and pelicans.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Get a bird’s eye view of Anand’s spectacular landscape by taking a hot air balloon ride. Watch the sunrise or sunset and enjoy sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Anand

No trip to Anand is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khandvi: A savory snack made from gram flour and buttermilk, Khandvi is a popular snack in Gujarat and an absolute must-try in Anand.
  • Undhiyu: A traditional Gujarati dish made with a mix of vegetables, spices, and herbs, undhiyu is a delicious and flavorful dish that’s perfect for vegetarians.
  • Basundi: A sweet dessert made with milk, sugar, and cardamom, Basundi is a popular dessert in Gujarat and a great way to end a meal on a sweet note.

Local Beverages

  • Chhas: A refreshing drink made with buttermilk, chhas is a popular beverage in Gujarat and a great way to beat the heat on a hot day.
  • Masala Chai: A spiced tea made with milk, tea leaves, and a blend of aromatic spices, masala chai is a staple in every household in India and a must-try in Anand.

What to Buy in Anand: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anand is known for its handicrafts, textiles, and dairy products. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Amul Products: Take home some delicious Amul products, including cheese, butter, and milk chocolates. The Amul Dairy Cooperative also sells a range of handicrafts and textiles.
  • Bandhani: A traditional tie-and-dye textile, Bandhani is a popular souvenir in Gujarat and a great way to add a pop of color to your wardrobe.
  • Embroidered textiles: Gujarat is famous for its intricate embroidery, and Anand is no exception. Take home some embroidered textiles, including sarees and bedspreads, as a memento of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Anand

Anand is a city with a rich cultural heritage and customs that are unique to Gujarat. Here are some customs and traditions that you should be aware of:

  • Gujarati language: Gujarati is the official language of Gujarat, and while most people in Anand can speak Hindi and English, it’s always polite to learn a few words of Gujarati.
  • Traditional dress: Gujarati women often wear sarees or chaniya cholis, while men wear traditional dhotis or kurtas. While it’s not necessary to dress in traditional attire, it’s always respectful to dress modestly.
  • Hospitality: Gujarat is known for its hospitality, and you’ll find that people in Anand are warm and welcoming. Don’t be surprised if you’re invited for a cup of tea or a home-cooked meal!

Best Time to Visit Anand

The best time to visit Anand is from October to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season from June to September brings heavy rains and flooding, so it’s best to avoid visiting during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anand

Here are some do’s and don’ts that will ensure that you have a smooth and enjoyable trip to Anand:

Do:

  • Cover your head and remove your shoes: When visiting a temple or a holy site, it’s customary to cover your head and remove your shoes as a sign of respect.
  • Try the local cuisine: Gujarati cuisine is famous for its vegetarian dishes and delicious snacks, so make sure to try some local delicacies during your visit to Anand.
  • Respect local customs and traditions: Gujarat has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission: It’s important to always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or religious sites.
  • Waste water: Gujarat is a water-scarce state, and it’s important to conserve water whenever possible.
  • Disrespect wildlife: Gujarat has a rich wildlife heritage, and it’s important to respect and protect the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anand

Anand is a relatively safe city, and violent crime is rare. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding deserted areas at night and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Anand

Anand has good medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. It’s important to take basic health precautions, such as washing your hands frequently and drinking bottled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Anand: Transportation Options and Logistics

Anand is well-connected to other cities in Gujarat and India. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Anand

The nearest airport to Anand is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad, which is approximately 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Anand

Anand has a bus station that provides regular services to other cities in Gujarat and India. The nearest major bus station is in Vadodara, which is approximately 35 kilometers away.

Other Options

Anand also has a railway station that is well-connected to other major cities in Gujarat and India. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are also widely available in Anand and can be easily hailed on the street.

How to Get Around in Anand

Anand has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses and auto-rickshaws. Taxis can also be hired for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Anand: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anand is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can take:

  • Vadodara: A bustling city located just 35 kilometers from Anand, Vadodara is known for its palaces, museums, and gardens.
  • Dakor: A small town located 40 kilometers from Anand, Dakor is famous for its ancient temple dedicated to Lord Krishna.
  • Ahmedabad: The largest city in Gujarat, Ahmedabad is approximately 65 kilometers from Anand and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Anand is a city that offers a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. From its ancient temples and monuments to its scenic trails and outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Anand. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or a nature lover, Anand is a destination that should be on your bucket list.
