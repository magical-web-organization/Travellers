Introduction to Dholavira

Dholavira, located in the Kutch district of Gujarat in Western India, is an ancient Harappan civilization site with traces dating back to around 4500 years ago. In 1998, Dholavira was declared as one of India’s six most important archeological sites by the Archaeological Survey of India. The place, which attracts history buffs and cultural enthusiasts alike, is also known for its natural beauty with breathtaking landscapes and a diverse wildlife.

List of Places to Visit in Dholavira

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Archaeological Museum: Situated on the site itself, the museum displays the remains and artifacts excavated from the Harappan civilization. The exhibits include pottery, seals, bangles, beads, and statues that dated back to 2000 BC.
  • The Harappan Civilization Site: Located about 180 km east of Bhuj, the site of Dholavira is one of the five largest Harappan sites in the Indian subcontinent. The ruins of the ancient city, covers about 100 hectares of land, and its walls and gateways give a glimpse of the life of its residents.
  • The Stepwell: Located on the northern periphery of the fortified citadel of Dholavira, it is one of the most impressive structures that denote the advanced water management system of the Harappan Civilization.

Natural Attractions of Dholavira: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Rann of Kutch: The white salt flats of the Rann of Kutch, which covers about 30,000 sq. km, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. During the full moon nights of winter, the salt plains turn into a mesmerizing white zone full of glittering stars, making it an unforgettable experience.
  • Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary: Covering an area of around 7505 sq. km, the wildlife sanctuary is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including the Indian Wild Ass, Chinkara, and various birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dholavira

Dholavira has several biking routes that are perfect for cycling enthusiasts. The White Rann of Kutch is a popular choice among tracking enthusiasts. Apart from this, you can also explore the desert areas nearby the city and cycle through the beautiful towns and villages while experiencing the vibrant local life.

Best Things to Do in Dholavira: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Harappan Civilization site to understand the life of the people who lived here centuries ago.
  • Go on a wildlife safari at the Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary and explore the vast expanse of the wildlife and the desert landscape.
  • Enjoy a camel ride in the neighboring towns to get a feel of the local mode of transportation.
  • Witness the art and culture of the local people through various activities such as folk dance performances, live music, and workshops on traditional crafts such as pottery, weaving, and embroidery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dholavira

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Dholavira:

Day 1: Arrival in Bhuj, and visit Bhuj Fort and the nearby palaces. Day 2: Explore the Harappan Civilization site and Dholavira Fort ruins. Day 3: Visit White Rann of Kutch during the daytime, and experience the night view under the full moon sky. Day 4: Take a wildlife Safari tour in Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary. Day 5-6: Visit the nearby towns such as Sayla and Chotila to get a flavor of the local life, food, and culture Day 7: Departure from Bhuj.

Food and Drink Scene of Dholavira

Must-Try Dishes

Food lovers can indulge in some fiery and flavorful cuisine in Dholavira that mostly revolve around vegetarian cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Kachchhi Dabeli: A popular street food that comprises of a patty made with boiled potatoes and the characteristic sweetness from tamarind chutney and the spiciness from a special blend of masalas.
  • Khandavi: A soft and nutritional roll made out of chickpea flour, yogurt-based curry, and tempered with mustard seeds and cumin.
  • Undhiyu: A heavy preparation made out of various veggies and pulses that are slow-cooked together and flavored with green chilies and jaggery.

Local Beverages

Dholavira has some famous drinks that one may not find anywhere else in India. The drinks are traditional, and locals swear by their health benefits. Here are some beverages to try when in the city:

  • Sattu: It is a local drink made out of various grains and pulses and contains proteins, vitamins, and minerals. It has a nutty taste and is very refreshing.
  • Chhaas: A simple preparation made by blending yogurt with water, salt, and some herbs. It is the perfect drink to beat the heat and offers various digestive medical benefits.

What to Buy in Dholavira: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dholavira is a great place to shop for local handicrafts and textiles. The place is famous for its textiles, silverware, and pottery. Some of the things to shop for when in the city include:

  • Traditional Kutchi Shawls and shawls embellished with embroidery and mirror work
  • Bandhani fabrics
  • Handicrafts like silverware, pottery, and wooden crafts

Local Culture and Customs of Dholavira

Dholavira has a unique and vibrant culture that is deeply rooted in its history and natural surroundings. The Kutchi people are known for their colorful attire, intricate tattoos, and harmonious music. The city celebrates various festivals with zeal and enthusiasm, including Navratri, Bhavnath Mahadev Mela, and Kutch Mahotsav. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dholavira

The best time to visit Dholavira is during the winter season, from November to February, when the climate is mild and pleasant. The days are sunny, and the nights are cold, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dholavira

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry a valid ID proof at all times
  • Secure all your belongings and be on high alert in crowded places
  • Bargain with vendors when shopping in bazaars and markets

Don’t:

  • Take videos or pictures in restricted areas without permission
  • Litter and disturb the natural environment
  • Drink tap water or consume food from unhygienic places

Safety and Health

Safety in Dholavira

Dholavira is a relatively safe city. However, tourists are advised to take necessary precautions while traveling to avoid any kind of mishaps or accidents.

Health in Dholavira

While Dholavira is a safe place to travel, precautions should be taken to maintain good health. It is recommended that tourists bring their own bottled water and avoid consuming street food or drinking from unsecured sources.
