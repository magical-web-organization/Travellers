Introduction to Blackbuck National Park

Located in Saurashtra, Gujarat, Blackbuck National Park is one of the leading wildlife parks in India. The park is spread over an area of 34.08 sq. km and is home to several endangered species like blackbucks, striped hyenas, Indian wolves, and desert foxes. Besides, the park also boasts of several bird species like eagles, falcons, and harriers. The best time to visit the park is between October to June.

List of Places to Visit in Blackbuck National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhavnagar city: Located just 45 km from the park, Bhavnagar is a beautiful city with several tourist attractions like Gaurishankar Lake, Blackbuck Resort, and Takhteshwar Temple.
  • Victoria Park: Established in 1888, this beautiful park is a perfect picnic spot and has a zoo that houses several exotic birds and animals.
  • Nilambag Palace: Built-in 1859, the palace is a fine example of colonial architecture and is now a popular heritage hotel.

Natural Attractions of Blackbuck National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blackbuck Trail: Take a guided walking or biking tour through the park and witness the beautiful blackbucks grazing in their natural habitat.
  • Velavadar Beach: A beautiful beach that’s popular for its black sand and serene waters. Camping options are also available here.
  • Gir National Park: Located just 170 km from Blackbuck National Park, Gir National Park is a great place to spot Asiatic lions, one of the rarest animals in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blackbuck National Park

  • Bhavnagar-Gadhada Trail: A beautiful 13 km hike through fields, small villages, and lush green forests.
  • Blackbuck Trail: A guided hiking or biking tour that takes you through several areas of the park and gives a chance to see beautiful blackbucks grazing in their natural habitat.
  • Kathiawar Peninsula Trail: A scenic biking trail that takes you through several beautiful villages and provides a glimpse into the life of the locals.

Best Things to Do in Blackbuck National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Jeep Safari: A thrilling jeep safari through the park is the best way to witness the exotic wildlife of the region.
  • Bird Watching: The park is home to several bird species like eagles, falcons, and harriers, making it a perfect spot for bird watching enthusiasts.
  • Nature Walks: Take guided nature walks through the park and learn about the flora and fauna of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blackbuck National Park

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive at Bhavnagar. Visit Blackbuck Resort and Gaurishankar Lake.
Day 2 Take a guided jeep safari in Blackbuck National Park.
Day 3 Visit Victoria Park, Nilambag Palace, and Takhteshwar Temple.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Gir National Park.
Day 5 Go on a nature walk and bird watching in Blackbuck National Park.
Day 6 Explore the Bhavnagar-Gadhada trail.
Day 7 Depart for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Blackbuck National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gathiyas: A popular snack made from gram flour and spiced with chili, ginger, and garlic.
  • Thepla: A delicious flatbread made from whole wheat flour, fenugreek leaves, and spices.
  • Dhokla: A savory steamed snack made from a fermented batter of chickpeas and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Solkadhi: A refreshing drink made from kokum fruit, coconut milk, and spices.
  • Chaas: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that helps in beating the heat.

Where to Stay in Blackbuck National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Blackbuck Resort: A luxurious resort located right inside the park with amenities like swimming pool, restaurant, and nature walks.
  • Nilambag Palace: A heritage hotel in Bhavnagar city with beautiful rooms and fine-dining options.
  • Welcomhotel: A modern hotel with beautiful rooms and scenic views of the surrounding areas.

What to Buy in Blackbuck National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bandhani Sarees: Beautiful tie-dyed sarees with intricate designs that are unique to the region.
  • Handicrafts: Buy unique handicrafts made by the local artisans like wall hangings, pottery, and wooden carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Blackbuck National Park

The local culture of the region is heavily influenced by the Hindu religion, and the locals celebrate several festivals like Navratri, Diwali, and Holi. Dress modestly while visiting any religious places and be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Blackbuck National Park

The best time to visit Blackbuck National Park is between October to June when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is easy to spot. Avoid visiting the park during the monsoon season (July to September) as the park remains closed due to heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blackbuck National Park

Do:

  • Hire a local guide before visiting the park to make the most of your visit.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for outdoor activities.
  • Carry a hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disturb the wildlife or get too close to them.
  • Don’t carry plastic bottles or bags as it is prohibited inside the park.
  • Don’t light fires in the park as it is a protected area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blackbuck National Park

  • Always follow the rules and regulations of the park.
  • Avoid wandering off the designated trails.
  • Be aware of the presence of wild animals and maintain a safe distance from them.

Health in Blackbuck National Park

  • Always drink bottled water to stay hydrated.
  • Carry insect repellant to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Seek immediate medical attention in case of an emergency.

Getting to Blackbuck National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blackbuck National Park

The nearest airport to Blackbuck National Park is Bhavnagar airport located about 50 km away. It is well-connected to major cities in India and offers several flights daily.

Bus Stations Near Blackbuck National Park

Several bus services operate between Bhavnagar and nearby cities like Ahmedabad, Rajkot, and Surat.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire from nearby cities like Bhavnagar, Rajkot, and Ahmedabad.

How to Get Around in Blackbuck National Park

Hiring a local guide is the best way to explore the park. Alternatively, you can explore the area on foot, take a jeep safari, or hire a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Blackbuck National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gir National Park: Located just 170 km from Blackbuck National Park, Gir National Park is a great place to spot Asiatic lions, one of the rarest animals in the world.
  • Sasan Gir: Known for its beautiful landscape and wildlife, Sasan Gir is located about 175 km from Blackbuck National Park.
  • Bhuj: Located about 270 km from the park, Bhuj is known for its rich cultural heritage and handicrafts.

Conclusion

Blackbuck National Park is a beautiful and serene place that should be on every nature lover’s bucket list. The park’s pristine beauty, exotic wildlife, and cultural significance make it a must-visit destination. We hope this comprehensive tour guide helps you make the most of your visit to Blackbuck National Park.
