Introduction to Gir Forest National Park

Gir Forest National Park is located in Saurashtra, Gujarat, India. It is a wildlife sanctuary and home to the Asiatic lion. The park is spread over an area of 1,412 km² and was established in 1965. Gir is also a popular birdwatching destination and has over 250 species of birds. The park also houses several temples and historical sites. Gir Forest National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, and anyone looking for a unique and authentic travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Gir Forest National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Gir Interpretation Zone

The Museum at Gir Interpretation Zone offers an insightful look into the flora, fauna, and lifestyle of the region. The museum displays stuffed animals, interactive exhibits, and audiovisual materials.

Somnath Temple

The Somnath Temple is an ancient temple that dates back to 7th century AD. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva and is considered to be an important Hindu pilgrimage site.

Kankai Temple

The Kankai Temple is a spiritual site that is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located on the banks of the Kankai River and attracts a large number of devotees each year.

Natural Attractions of Gir Forest National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gir Interpretation Zone

The Gir Interpretation Zone is located outside the national park and offers wildlife safaris in Jeeps. Visitors can see the Asiatic lion, leopard, deer, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

Crocodile Rearing Centre

The Crocodile Rearing Centre in Sasan Gir is a breeding and research center for crocodiles. Visitors can see the crocodiles and learn about their behavior and breeding habits.

Kamleshwar Dam

The Kamleshwar Dam is a popular picnic spot that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests. The dam is also an excellent spot for birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gir Forest National Park

Sasan-Gir Wildlife Sanctuary Trail

The Sasan-Gir Wildlife Sanctuary Trail is a 5 km hiking trail that takes visitors through the sanctuary. Visitors can see wildlife and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Biking Trails

There are several biking trails around the national park that offer scenic views and an opportunity to explore the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Gir Forest National Park: Activities and Tours

Jeep Safari

The Jeep Safari is the best way to see the wildlife in Gir Forest National Park. The safaris are conducted in the morning and evening, and visitors can see the Asiatic lion, leopard, deer, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

Bird Watching

Gir Forest National Park is home to over 250 species of birds, and is a popular destination for birdwatching enthusiasts.

Cycling Tour

A cycling tour of the national park is a great way to explore the countryside and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gir Forest National Park

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Sasan Gir and check into hotel
2 Morning jeep safari in the national park, visit the Museum at Gir Interpretation Zone
3 Visit the Somnath Temple, explore the Kankai Temple
4 Day trip to Junagadh, visit the Upperkot Fort, the Mahabat Maqbara, and other historical sites
5 Visit the Crocodile Rearing Centre and the Kamleshwar Dam, enjoy a picnic
6 Hike the Sasan-Gir Wildlife Sanctuary Trail or go on a cycling tour
7 Depart from Sasan Gir

Food and Drink Scene of Gir Forest National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Surti Undhiyu

Surti Undhiyu is a traditional Gujarati dish that is made with a variety of vegetables and spices. It is usually served with hot rotis.

Kachori

Kachori is a deep-fried snack that is stuffed with spicy and tangy filling. It is usually served with tamarind chutney.

Local Beverages

Chhaas

Chhaas is a refreshing and hydrating beverage made with yogurt, water, and seasonings. It is an excellent accompaniment to spicy food.

Solkadhi

Solkadhi is a famous beverage of Konkan and Goa. It is made by combining coconut milk with kokum and spices.

Where to Stay in Gir Forest National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget

The Temple View Guest House

The Temple View Guest House offers clean and comfortable accommodation at affordable prices.

The Gir Jungle Lodge

The Gir Jungle Lodge is a basic but comfortable accommodation option that is perfect for budget travelers.

Mid-Range

The Fern Gir Forest Resort

The Fern Gir Forest Resort is a comfortable resort that offers excellent amenities and beautiful views of the surrounding forest.

Amidhara Resort

Amidhara Resort is a budget-friendly resort that offers comfortable and spacious rooms.

Luxury

The Gateway Hotel Gir Forest

The Gateway Hotel Gir Forest is a luxurious resort that offers excellent amenities, world-class facilities, and impeccable service.

Ravla Bhenswara

Ravla Bhenswara is a luxurious heritage hotel that offers old-world charm, comfortable rooms, and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Gir Forest National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

The region is known for its handicrafts, including handwoven textiles, pottery, and jewelry.

Spices

Gujarat is famous for its spices, and visitors can buy various spices and spice mixes to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Gir Forest National Park

The people of Gujarat are known for their hospitality and warmth. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions and be mindful of dress codes when visiting temples and other places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Gir Forest National Park

The best time to visit Gir Forest National Park is from December to March when the weather is pleasant, and wildlife sightings are more frequent.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gir Forest National Park

Do:

  • Take a guided jeep safari to see the Asiatic lion and other wildlife
  • Respect local customs and dress codes when visiting temples and other places of worship
  • Carry sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses to protect against sunburn
  • Stay in a licensed hotel or guesthouse

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park or pollute the environment
  • Disturb or feed the wildlife
  • Enter the park after sundown
  • Carry plastic bags, as they are prohibited inside the national park

Safety and Health

Safety in Gir Forest National Park

Visitors should be cautious when traveling outside the park, as the region is known for occasional lawlessness. It is recommended to hire a licensed guide when exploring the surrounding areas.

Health in Gir Forest National Park

Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent illnesses like food poisoning. It is also recommended to carry insect repellent and a first aid kit.

Getting to Gir Forest National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gir Forest National Park

The nearest airport to Gir Forest National Park is Rajkot Airport, which is about 160 km away.

Bus Stations Near Gir Forest National Park

Junagadh and Veraval are the two main bus stations near Gir Forest National Park. Visitors can also hire a taxi from these cities to get to Sasan Gir.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Junagadh or Veraval and then hire a taxi to get to Sasan Gir.

How to Get Around in Gir Forest National Park

The best way to get around Gir Forest National Park is by hiring a licensed guide and a Jeep.

Where to Go Next from Gir Forest National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Junagadh

Junagadh is a historical city that is known for its ancient forts and palaces.

Somnath

Somnath is a coastal town that is known for its ancient temple and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Gir Forest National Park is a must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts, nature lovers, and anyone looking for an authentic travel experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the park’s natural beauty and cultural heritage, try local cuisine, and engage in outdoor activities.
