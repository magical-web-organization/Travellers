Introduction to Junagadh

Nestled in the heart of Gujarat, Junagadh is a vibrant city that offers a fascinating blend of natural, cultural, and historical attractions. Junagadh is home to a rich history that dates back to 250 BCE and has been ruled by different dynasties over the centuries, including the Mauryans, Guptas, Mughals, and British. This has left an indelible mark on the city’s architecture, culture, and traditions. In this travel guide, we will take you on a comprehensive tour of Junagadh, covering everything from its must-see landmarks to its hidden gems.

List of Places to Visit in Junagadh

Junagadh has many attractions that cater to different interests. Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Uparkot Fort: Built in 319 BCE, this ancient fort is one of the oldest monuments in India and is a testament to Junagadh’s rich history. It is surrounded by a mammoth wall that is 20 meters high.
  • Mahabat Maqbara Palace: This stunning palace is a perfect example of Indo-Islamic architecture and is one of the best-preserved monuments in the city.
  • Sakkarbaug Zoological Garden: Established in 1863, this zoo is one of the oldest and best-maintained zoos in India.
  • The Ashoka Edicts: The famous Ashoka rock edicts are inscribed in Brahmi script on a massive rock in Junagadh. They are believed to be the oldest and most extensive edicts of Emperor Ashoka anywhere in India.

Natural Attractions of Junagadh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gir National Park: Spread over 1412 square km, this national park is home to India’s only population of Asiatic lions. It is also a haven for bird watchers and nature lovers.
  • Girnar Hill: This 1100-meter tall hill is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India, with temples and shrines dedicated to different gods and goddesses.
  • Jama Masjid: This beautiful mosque is one of the oldest Muslim shrines in Gujarat and is known for its intricate carvings and beautiful architecture.
  • Narsinh Mehta Lake: This beautiful lake is surrounded by lush greenery and is perfect for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Junagadh

Junagadh has many trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include:

  • Girnar Hill Trail: This 5 km trail is perfect for avid hikers and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Damodar Kund Trail: This 12 km trail is ideal for bikers and takes you through the beautiful countryside of Junagadh.
  • Sasan Gir National Park Trail: This 20 km trail is perfect for wildlife enthusiasts and takes you through the heart of the national park.

Best Things to Do in Junagadh: Activities and Tours

Junagadh has a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Safari at Gir National Park: Take a jeep safari through the park and spot Asiatic lions, leopards, deer, and antelopes in their natural habitat.
  • Visit the Uparkot Fort: Explore the ancient fort and marvel at its stunning architecture and historical significance.
  • Take a Temple Tour: Visit the many temples and shrines in Junagadh, including the Jain temple, the Jain Derasar temple, and the Bahucharaji Temple.
  • Experience the Local Cuisine: Try the local delicacies such as Khaman, Dhokla, and Fafda, and indulge in the rich flavors of Gujarat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Junagadh

Here’s a week-long itinerary that covers the best of Junagadh:

  • Day 1: Visit the Uparkot Fort and explore the Ashoka Edicts.
  • Day 2: Take a safari at Gir National Park and spot the majestic Asiatic lions.
  • Day 3: Visit the Mahabat Maqbara Palace and the Jama Masjid.
  • Day 4: Take a hike up Girnar Hill and visit the many temples and shrines on the way up.
  • Day 5: Visit the Sakkarbaug Zoological Garden and Narsinh Mehta Lake.
  • Day 6: Take a temple tour and visit the many Jain temples and Bahucharaji Temple.
  • Day 7: Experience the local cuisine and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Junagadh

Junagadh is a food lover’s paradise and offers a wide range of local delicacies that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khaman: This steamed savory snack is made from gram flour and is a popular Gujarati breakfast item.
  • Dhokla: This spongy and fluffy snack is made from fermented gram flour and is a staple food in Gujarat.
  • Fafda: This crispy snack is made from gram flour and is typically served with a spicy green chutney.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: Also known as buttermilk, this refreshing drink is made from curd and is perfect for beating the heat.
  • Masala Chai: This spiced tea is a popular beverage in Gujarat and is served hot and fresh.

Where to Stay in Junagadh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Junagadh has a variety of accommodation options that range from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • The Gateway Hotel: This luxury hotel offers comfortable rooms with all modern amenities and is located close to major attractions.
  • Lotus Hotel: This budget hotel is ideal for those looking for comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.
  • Hill View Guest House: This cozy guesthouse offers a homely atmosphere and is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Junagadh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Junagadh is known for its handicrafts and local products. Some of the best souvenirs to buy include:

  • Bandhani Sarees: These colorful and vibrant sarees are a specialty of Gujarat and make for great souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: Junagadh is known for its intricate brassware, pottery, and handmade jewelry.
  • Spices: Gujarat is famous for its spices, and you can find an extensive range of spices in Junagadh.

Local Culture and Customs of Junagadh

Junagadh has a diverse culture that reflects its rich history and traditions. The people of Junagadh are warm and welcoming and follow a mix of Hindu and Islamic traditions. Some of the important festivals in Junagadh include Navaratri, Diwali, and Eid.

Best Time to Visit Junagadh

The best time to visit Junagadh is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Junagadh

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and shop for souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Junagadh

Junagadh is a safe city for travelers, but it is always best to exercise caution and take necessary precautions. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your belongings secure.

Health in Junagadh

Make sure that you stay hydrated and carry necessary medications with you. Consult a doctor in case of any health concerns.

Getting to Junagadh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Junagadh is well-connected by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near Junagadh

  • Rajkot Airport: Rajkot Airport is the nearest airport to Junagadh and is located 102 km away.

Bus Stations Near Junagadh

  • Junagadh Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Junagadh and is located in the heart of the city.

Other Options

  • Train: Junagadh Railway Station is well-connected to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Junagadh

The best way to get around in Junagadh is by auto-rickshaw or taxi. Public transportation is limited, and it can be challenging to navigate around the city.

Where to Go Next from Junagadh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Junagadh is located in close proximity to many exciting destinations, including:

  • Sasan Gir: Sasan Gir is famous for its wildlife sanctuary and is perfect for an outdoor adventure.
  • Rajkot: Rajkot is a vibrant city that is known for its rich history and cultural landmarks.
  • Somnath: Somnath is a holy city that is home to the famous Somnath Temple.

Conclusion

Junagadh is a city that is steeped in history and culture, and is a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to experience the best of Gujarat. With its rich heritage, natural attractions, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality, Junagadh is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories. So pack your bags and get ready for an adventure of a lifetime!
