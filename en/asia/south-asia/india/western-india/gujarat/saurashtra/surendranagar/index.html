Introduction to Surendranagar

Are you looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Western India? Look no further than Surendranagar, a hidden gem in Gujarat’s Saurashtra region. Founded in the mid-18th century by a Hindu Rajput ruler, Surendranagar boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor adventures for travelers seeking an authentic Indian experience. This travel guide will provide you with everything you need to plan your trip to Surendranagar, including places to visit, hiking/biking routes, activities and tours, food and drink scene, local customs, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Surendranagar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Surendranagar is steeped in history and culture, as evidenced by its numerous museums and historical sites. Highlights include:

  • Sardar Patel Museum: This museum is dedicated to India’s first deputy prime minister, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, who was born in nearby Karamsad. It features memorabilia and artifacts related to Patel’s life and career, as well as exhibits on Indian history and culture.

  • Bhadreshwar Jain Temple: Located on the banks of the Bhadar River, this 200-year-old temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Jains in India. It features magnificent architecture and intricate carvings.

  • Sayla Jain Temple: Another important Jain temple in Surendranagar, this 900-year-old temple is located in the town of Sayla and is renowned for its intricate carvings and architecture.

  • Chamunda Mataji Temple: Located on a hilltop overlooking Surendranagar, this temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Chamunda Devi. It offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for sunset watching.

Natural Attractions of Surendranagar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surendranagar is also home to some of the most spectacular natural attractions in Western India. Whether you’re a nature lover or an adventure seeker, there’s something here for everyone. Here are some of the top sites to explore:

  • Thangadh Wildlife Sanctuary: Covering an area of 65 square kilometers, this sanctuary is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including leopards, hyenas, chinkaras, and various species of birds. Visitors can go on guided safaris and nature walks to explore the park.

  • Dholidhaja Dam: Located on the Bhogavo River, this dam is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and picnicking. It’s also a great place to enjoy scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Balgumari Dargah: Surrounded by lush greenery, this Sufi shrine is a peaceful haven for meditation and contemplation. It’s also a popular spot for nature walks and birdwatching.

  • Hingolgadh Nature Education Sanctuary: Spread over an area of 654 hectares, this sanctuary is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several species of birds, mammals, and reptiles. Visitors can go on guided nature walks and birdwatching tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surendranagar

Surendranagar is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Balgumari Hill Trek: This moderate trek takes you to the top of Balgumari Hill, where you can enjoy panoramic views of Surendranagar and its surrounding hills. The trek takes about 2-3 hours and is suitable for all fitness levels.

  • Hingolgadh Nature Walk: This easy nature walk takes you through the Hingolgadh Nature Education Sanctuary, where you can see a wide variety of flora and fauna. The walk takes about 2 hours and is suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

  • Rajkot Cycling Tour: If you’re interested in exploring the wider Saurashtra region, consider taking a cycling tour from Surendranagar to Rajkot. This tour takes you through rural villages, scenic highways, and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Surendranagar: Activities and Tours

Surendranagar offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best:

  • Horse Riding: Enjoy a horseback ride through the scenic countryside of Surendranagar.

  • Jeep Safari: Explore the rugged terrain of Surendranagar on a jeep safari, where you can see wildlife and visit remote villages.

  • Reiki Healing: Unwind and de-stress with a relaxing Reiki healing session, offered at several wellness centers in Surendranagar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Surendranagar

If you have a week to spare, here’s an itinerary to make the most of your time in Surendranagar:

Day 1: Arrive in Surendranagar and explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, visiting landmarks such as the Sardar Patel Museum and Bhadreshwar Jain Temple.

Day 2: Take a hike to Balgumari Hill and enjoy the stunning panoramic views of Surendranagar and the surrounding hills.

Day 3: Visit the Thangadh Wildlife Sanctuary and go on a guided safari to see wild animals and birds in their natural habitat.

Day 4: Explore the historic town of Sayla and visit the Jain temple there.

Day 5: Take a jeep safari to explore the scenic countryside of Surendranagar and visit remote villages.

Day 6: For a more relaxing day, visit a local wellness center for a Reiki healing session or horseback riding.

Day 7: Departure from Surendranagar or continue your journey to nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Surendranagar

No trip to Surendranagar is complete without indulging in the delicious local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khaman: A popular snack made from chickpea flour and flavored with mustard seeds and green chilies.

  • Fafda: A crispy snack made from gram flour, served with tangy chutney.

  • Jalebi: A sweet and syrupy dessert made from wheat flour and sugar.

  • Undhiyu: A mixed vegetable dish that’s a specialty of Gujarat, made with winter vegetables such as yam, beans, potatoes, and eggplants.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: A refreshing and cooling drink made from spiced buttermilk.

  • Jaljeera: A tangy and spicy drink made from cumin, mint, and tamarind.

  • Aam Panna: A sweet and tangy drink made from raw mango pulp and spices.

Where to Stay in Surendranagar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Surendranagar offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, there’s something here for everyone. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Royal Highness: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Surendranagar, offering spacious rooms and suites with modern amenities.

  • Hotel Shiv Ashish: A budget-friendly hotel located near the Bhadar River, offering comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • The Grand Thakar: A heritage hotel located in a restored 19th-century haveli, featuring traditional architecture and decor.

What to Buy in Surendranagar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Surendranagar is famous for several local products and handicrafts. Here are some of the best things to buy as souvenirs:

  • Bandhani Sarees: Surendranagar is renowned for its vibrant and colorful bandhani (tie-dye) sarees, which make for great gifts.

  • Handicrafts: Surendranagar is home to several local artisans who specialize in making wooden toys, pottery, and embroidered textiles.

  • Coconut Shell Handicrafts: Coconut shell handicrafts, such as bowls and coasters, are a popular souvenir in Surendranagar.

Local Culture and Customs of Surendranagar

Surendranagar has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by the region’s history and geography. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Dress Conservatively: Since Surendranagar is a conservative town, it’s best to dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites. Women should cover their heads and wear long skirts or pants.

  • Respect Local Customs: Surendranagar is a town with strong traditions and customs. Visitors should respect local norms and practices, such as removing shoes before entering temples and not consuming alcohol or non-vegetarian food near temples.

Best Time to Visit Surendranagar

The best time to visit Surendranagar is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are at their best. Summers can be hot and humid, while monsoons can disrupt travel and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surendranagar

Do:

  • Dress conservatively.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit historical and cultural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol or non-vegetarian food near temples.
  • Disrespect local customs or religious sites.
  • Travel alone at night in isolated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surendranagar

Surendranagar is a safe town for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always best to exercise caution and common sense, especially when traveling alone at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Surendranagar

Surendranagar is a relatively healthy town, with no major health concerns for travelers. However, it’s important to take precautions to prevent common illnesses such as dehydration, sunburn, and mosquito bites.

Getting to Surendranagar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Surendranagar

The nearest airport to Surendranagar is Ahmedabad International Airport, which is located about 119 km away. From Ahmedabad, you can take a bus or taxi to Surendranagar.

Bus Stations Near Surendranagar

Surendranagar is well-connected to nearby cities and towns via bus services. Buses run from Ahmedabad, Rajkot, and other cities to Surendranagar.

Other Options

You can also reach Surendranagar by train, as the town is on the Western Railway line. There are several daily trains from Ahmedabad, Rajkot, and other cities to Surendranagar.

How to Get Around in Surendranagar

The best way to get around in Surendranagar is by auto-rickshaw or taxi. These can be hired easily from anywhere in the town. Buses and private cars are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Surendranagar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Surendranagar is a great base to explore nearby destinations in Saurashtra. Here are some recommendations:

  • Rajkot: A vibrant city located about 130 km from Surendranagar, known for its colorful bazaars and historic fort.

  • Gir National Park: Home to the endangered Asiatic lion, Gir National Park is located about 190 km from Surendranagar and is a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

  • Kutch: Known for its vibrant textiles and handicrafts, the Kutch region is located about 380 km from Surendranagar.

Conclusion

Surendranagar is a hidden gem in Western India, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s museums and historical landmarks or venturing into the surrounding countryside for outdoor adventures, Surendranagar has something for everyone.
