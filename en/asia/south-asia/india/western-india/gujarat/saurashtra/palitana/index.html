Introduction to Palitana

Palitana is a city located in the Bhavnagar district of Gujarat in Western India. It is a famous pilgrimage site for Jains due to its 900 temples that are situated on the Shatrunjaya hills. The city boasts a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can marvel at the intricacy of the temples and learn more about Jainism.

List of Places to Visit in Palitana

For those visiting Palitana, there are numerous places to visit. Here’s a quick list of some of the top attractions in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several culturally important attractions in Palitana that visitors can explore to learn more about the culture and heritage of the region. Some of these include:

  • Shatrunjaya Hill: Shatrunjaya Hill is a popular pilgrimage site for Jains and features 900 temples. Visitors can take a 3.5 km trek up the hill to explore these temples.
  • Jain Temples: The Jain temples in Palitana are renowned for their architecture and intricate carvings. Visitors can explore several temples, including the Adinath Temple, Kumarpal Temple, and Vimalshah Temple.
  • The Palitana Temples: The Palitana Temples are a famous Jain pilgrimage site, with over 3,000 temples.
  • Sampriti Raja Museum: The Sampriti Raja Museum is a great place to learn more about the history of Palitana.

Natural Attractions of Palitana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Palitana is also home to many natural attractions that visitors can explore to experience outdoor adventures and stunning scenic views. Some of these include:

  • Bhavnath Mahadev Temple: The Bhavnath Mahadev Temple is situated in the midst of forests and is a great spot for trekking and hiking.
  • Kadia Dungar Caves: The Kadia Dungar Caves are natural caves that offer visitors stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hathisingh Jain Temple: The Hathisingh Jain Temple is one of the largest Jain temples in India, with a beautiful garden surrounding it.
  • Taranga Hill: Taranga Hill is a popular picnic spot for locals and tourists alike who come here to unwind in the lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palitana

Palitana is surrounded by hills and forests, offering plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes around Palitana include:

  • Shetrunjay Hills: The Shetrunjay Hills are a popular destination for hiking, and visitors can explore the several temples that are situated here.
  • Talaja Hills: The Talaja Hills are known for their scenic beauty and are a great spot for hiking and biking.
  • Ghogha Beach: Ghogha Beach is ideal for cycling or walking along the shoreline, and visitors can enjoy stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

Best Things to Do in Palitana: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the temples and natural attractions, there are plenty of activities that visitors can enjoy in Palitana. Some of the best activities and tours in Palitana include:

  • Take a heritage walk through the city to learn more about its culture and heritage.
  • Participate in a cultural tour around the city to learn more about the customs and traditions of Jainism.
  • Join a cooking class to learn more about the local cuisine and food culture.
  • Take a guided safari around the forests and hills surrounding Palitana to spot local wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palitana

If you plan on spending a week in Palitana, here’s an itinerary that you can follow:

Day 1 Arrive in Palitana and explore the city’s heritage sites
Day 2 Visit the Jain temples and explore the natural attractions
Day 3 Trek up to Shatrunjaya Hill and explore the temples
Day 4 Take a guided safari around the surrounding hills and forests
Day 5 Participate in a cultural tour and learn more about Jainism
Day 6 Go cycling along Ghogha Beach and unwind
Day 7 Depart from Palitana and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Palitana

One of the best ways to experience the culture of Palitana is through its delicious food and drink options. Here’s a list of some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Palitana.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khaman Dhokla: Khaman Dhokla is a famous snack in Gujarat made from gram flour and is a must-try.
  • Sabudana Vada: Sabudana Vada is a popular deep-fried snack in Gujarat made from sabudana (sago) and potatoes.
  • Theplas: Theplas are flatbreads made from fenugreek leaves and are a staple in Gujarati cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: Chaas is a buttermilk drink that’s perfect to cool down on hot days.
  • Aam ka Panna: Aam ka Panna is a refreshing drink made from raw mangoes, mint, and spices.

Where to Stay in Palitana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Palitana for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the top accommodation options in Palitana include:

  • Shree Lodging and Boarding House: A budget-friendly guesthouse in the heart of Palitana, perfect for solo travellers and backpackers.
  • Bhavna Park and Resort: A luxury resort in the outskirts of Palitana, perfect for families and couples.
  • House of MG: A heritage hotel in nearby Bhavnagar, with a blend of traditional Indian and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Palitana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palitana is known for its traditional handicrafts, textiles and jewelry, making it a great place to shop for souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy souvenirs in Palitana include:

  • Fine-printed textiles and traditional handicrafts
  • Silverware and jewelry inspired by the city’s heritage

Local Culture and Customs of Palitana

Palitana is a city steeped in tradition and culture, and visitors should be respectful when exploring the city’s various attractions. Jainism is the predominant religion in Palitana, and visitors should avoid consuming meat, alcohol, tobacco or drugs while exploring the city. Visitors should dress modestly while visiting the temples and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Palitana

The best time to visit Palitana is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers are hot and humid and should be avoided by visitors who cannot tolerate high temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palitana

Do:

  • Ensure to dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees while visiting temples or religious sites.
  • Observe hygiene and remove footwear while entering religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before capturing images of the local residents.

Don’t:

  • Do not consume meat, alcohol and tobacco while in the city.
  • Do not touch or disturb the local wildlife while on a safari or trek.
  • Do not bathe, wash your clothes or dispose of garbage in the river.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palitana

Palitana is a safe city but visitors should take necessary precautions like keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding moving about alone at night.

Health in Palitana

Visitors are advised to carry a basic first-aid kit and should take appropriate vaccinations before travelling to Palitana. It is advised that visitors should carry water bottles and avoid street food.

Getting to Palitana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Palitana

The nearest airport to Palitana is the Bhavnagar Airport situated approximately 50 km away. Alternatively, visitors can fly into the Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport situated in Ahmedabad, approximately 210 km away.

Bus Stations Near Palitana

There are several bus stations near Palitana. Some of the major ones are:

  • Palitana Bus Stand
  • Bhavnagar Bus Stand
  • Ahmedabad Central Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or a private car from Bhavnagar or Ahmedabad to reach Palitana.

How to Get Around in Palitana

The best way to get around Palitana is by walking or hiring a bicycle. Visitors can also hire a taxi to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Palitana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several interesting destinations near Palitana for visitors to explore. Some of these include:

  • Bhavnagar: Bhavnagar is a city located approximately 50 km away from Palitana and is known for its stunning sea-view palaces.
  • Ahmedabad: Ahmedabad is the largest city in Gujarat and is famous for its intriguing architecture and cultural heritage.
  • Gir Forest National Park: Gir Forest National Park is located approximately 3.5 hours’ drive from Palitana and is the last surviving habitat of the Asiatic lion.

Conclusion

Palitana is a city that’s steeped in history and culture, offering visitors a unique experience. With its old-world charm, stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Palitana is a must-visit destination for those travelling to Gujarat. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip to Palitana and make the most of your visit.
