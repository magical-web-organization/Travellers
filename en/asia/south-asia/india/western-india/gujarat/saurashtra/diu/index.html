Introduction to Diu

Diu, a tiny island off the coast of Gujarat, is a hidden paradise in Western India. It is a fusion of Portuguese and Indian cultures, blended gracefully into its colonial-style architecture, narrow streets, and colorful festivities.

Diu once served as a major port for the sailors taking the sea route between east and west coasts, attracting various dynasties over time. Today, it is a perfect getaway for beach lovers, history buffs, and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Diu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Diu has a rich history and cultural heritage, showcased in its remarkable museums and historical sites.

  • St. Paul’s Church: Built-in 1601 AD, this church is a stunning example of Baroque architecture.
  • Diu Fort: Built by the Portuguese in 1535 AD, this massive fort offers a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea.
  • Panikotha Fort: Also known as the “Fortim do Mar,” this fort was built on a small island and served as a warehouse for ammunition and arms.
  • Jallandhar Shrine: This ancient temple was constructed to worship the demon king Jallandhar, who was defeated by Lord Shiva.
  • Diu Museum: The museum showcases Diu’s history and culture through its artifacts, including sculptures, inscriptions, and others.

Natural Attractions of Diu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Diu offers more than just its rich cultural heritage. The island city has stunning natural attractions to soothe your soul.

  • Naida Caves: The limestone formation caves will leave you awe-struck with its artistic brickwork and unique architecture.
  • Nagoa Beach: Located just 7 km away from the main town, the crescent-shaped beach offers a picturesque view of the sea.
  • Gangeshwar Temple: The temple is situated on the seashore, where five Shivalingas emerged naturally out of the rocks.
  • Chakratirth Beach: It is famous for its tranquil ambiance and serene surroundings.
  • Kevdi Beach: The beach, located on the western side of the Diu Fort, offers stunning views of the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Diu

Diu has a plethora of hiking and biking routes that offer travelers an off-the-beaten-path experience. The popular routes include:

  • Diu to Simbor: This biking route covers 25 km and offers stunning views of the seashore.
  • Diu to Vanakbara: This 32 km-long route is perfect for hiking and offers captivating views of the Portuguese village on the island.

Best Things to Do in Diu: Activities and Tours

Diu has a plethora of activities and tours to cater to travelers’ varied interests. Some of the best things to do in Diu are:

  • Watersports: Diu has a wide variety of water sports activities such as jet ski, parasailing, banana rides, and speed boating.
  • Heritage Walk: Diu has a rich history, so a guided heritage walk through the city is a must-do activity for history buffs.
  • Sunset Cruise: Enjoy a romantic evening with your loved ones on a sunset cruise, which offers a breathtaking view of the sunset.
  • Bird Watching Tour: Diu is home to many species of birds, so bird-watching tours are a must-do activity for nature lovers.
  • Bicycle Tours: Bicycle tours through the narrow streets of Diu are a great way to explore the island’s hidden gems.

Week-Long Itinerary for Diu

Diu is a small island city, but there’s a lot to experience. Here’s a perfect week-long itinerary to experience the best that Diu has to offer:

  • Day 1: Visit Diu Fort, St. Paul’s Church, and Diu Museum
  • Day 2: Explore the Naida Caves and enjoy water sports on Nagoa Beach.
  • Day 3: Hike to the Gangeshwar Temple and spend an evening on Ahmedpur Mandvi Beach.
  • Day 4: Visit Chakratirth Beach and take a heritage walk through the Diu city.
  • Day 5: Take a sunset cruise and end the day with local seafood delicacies.
  • Day 6: Explore the Vanakbara village and a nearby island called Ghoghla by bicycle.
  • Day 7: Take a bird watching tour to spot the exotic bird species on the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Diu

Diu has a unique culinary culture, blending Portuguese and Indian cuisines into its yummy delicacies. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Diu is a coastal town, and it’s no surprise that seafood is the town’s main attraction. Some of the delicious seafood delicacies include prawn curry, spicy fish curry, and grilled lobsters.
  • Coconut-based Dishes: Coconut-based dishes are a delicacy in Diu. Some of the local favorites include coconut milk with rice, coconut flavored vegetable curry, and coconut thali.
  • Beef Chilly Fry: A hot dish that is made from slow-cooked beef and lots of red chilies.

Local Beverages

  • Feni: A popular alcoholic drink that originates from Goa, Feni is made from cashew fruits and has a strong, smoky flavor.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh, natural, and refreshing, coconut water is a must-try beverage in Diu.

Where to Stay in Diu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Diu has various accommodation options, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels.

Luxurious Hotels

  • Radisson Blu Resort: Located near Nagoa Beach, this resort offers a serene ambiance with all modern amenities.
  • The Fern Residency: The hotel offers elegantly designed rooms and suites, providing an oasis of comfort in the heart of the city.

Budget-Friendly Hostels/Guesthouses

  • Hotel Kohinoor: Located in the heart of Diu, offering basic amenities and warm hospitality.
  • Alanis Villa Guest House: It is a beautiful villa located near the Diu Fort and is an ideal place for backpackers.

What to Buy in Diu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Diu is famous for its handcrafts and local products, making it the perfect place to buy souvenirs as gifts for your loved ones. Here are some of the things to buy in Diu:

  • Shell Products: Diu has a rich collection of sea-shells that are turned into beautiful souvenirs, such as key chains, jewelry, and coasters.
  • Handicrafts: The city is famous for its handcrafted items, such as embroidered cushion covers, decorative pots, and woven wall hangings.
  • Coconut-based Products: As Diu is a hub for coconut trees, it’s not surprising that the city was creating products out of them. Some of the famous coconut-based products include coconut oil, coconut shell craft items, and coconut fiber mats.

Local Culture and Customs of Diu

Diu’s culture and customs are a unique blend of Portuguese and Indian traditions, giving it a distinctive identity. Few things to note:

  • Dress Code: Diu follows a conservative dress code, so it is recommended to dress decently while visiting religious places.
  • Festivals: Diu holds vibrant festivals throughout the year, such as the Festa de Diu, Navratri, and Carnivals, showcasing the city’s cultural diversity.
  • Languages: Gujarati, Hindi, and English are widely spoken in Diu.

Best Time to Visit Diu

The best time to visit Diu is during the winter months, between October to March, when the weather is pleasant and the sea is calm, allowing travelers to enjoy water sports activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Diu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Carry sunscreen and sunglasses while outdoors.
  • Shop at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter on the beaches.
  • Do not disrespect the locals or their beliefs.
  • Do not indulge in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Diu

Diu is a relatively safe Indian town. However, travelers should always be cautious while outdoors and avoid traveling alone on the outskirts of the city, especially at night.

Health in Diu

It’s recommended to carry your medications while visiting Diu, as pharmacies might not be readily available. Travelers should also carry bug repellants and wear appropriate clothing to avoid insect bites.

Getting to Diu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Diu

The nearest airport to Diu is located in the capital city of Gujarat, Ahmedabad, which is connected to major cities of India through regular flights.

Bus Stations Near Diu

Diu is well-connected to other cities in Gujarat and neighboring states through a regular bus service.

Other Options

Travelers can also opt for a taxi or car rental service to reach Diu from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Diu

Taxi Services

Taxis and auto-rickshaws are readily available in Diu and are the most convenient way of getting around for tourists.

Car/Bike Rentals

Bike and car rentals are also available, allowing travelers the freedom to explore the city at their pace.

Where to Go Next from Diu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Diu is a perfect base to explore some of the exciting places nearby, such as:

  • Gir National Park: The park is home to Asiatic lions, making it a major attraction for wildlife lovers.
  • Somnath Temple: One of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva, the temple is an excellent example of cultural and religious significance.
  • Palitana Jain Temple: One of the most magnificent and sacred Jain pilgrimage sites, the temple has over 800 temples and is a must-visit place for spiritual seekers.

Conclusion

Diu is an inviting island city that has something to offer to every traveler. The city’s blend of Portuguese and Indian cultures, stunning natural sights, delicious food, and rich history make it a magnet for visitors looking for an escape from the rigors of everyday life. Whether you’re looking to enjoy a relaxing beach vacation, hike through the stunning natural sights, or immerse yourself in the city’s rich history, Diu offers something for everyone, promising an unforgettable experience.
