Introduction to Silvassa

Silvassa is a beautiful city nestled in the lap of nature, located in the western part of India. It is the capital of the Union Territory of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and is surrounded by lush green forests and hills. Silvassa is a blend of natural and man-made wonders, including historical sites, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and serene landscapes. The city is an ideal destination for those looking for a peaceful holiday away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Silvassa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tribal Cultural Museum: The Tribal Cultural Museum is a must-visit for those interested in tribal culture and tradition. The museum displays tribal artefacts, masks, and musical instruments.

  • Vanganga Lake and Island Garden: It is an artificial lake located in the heart of the city. The island garden is situated in the middle of the lake and can only be reached by boat. The garden has beautiful fountains, sculptures, and a jogging track.

  • Hirwa Van Garden: It is a popular picnic spot and a children’s park. The garden has facilities for boating, a toy train, and a restaurant.

  • Swaminarayan Temple: The Swaminarayan Temple is one of the most sacred places in the city. It is an excellent example of Indian architecture and is surrounded by lush greenery.

Natural Attractions of Silvassa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lion Safari Wildlife Park: The Lion Safari Wildlife Park is a thrilling experience for wildlife enthusiasts. The park has a variety of animals, including tigers, lions, deer, and birds.

  • Dudhni Lake: Dudhni Lake is a serene water body located 20 km away from Silvassa. It is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers.

  • Satmaliya Deer Park: Satmaliya Deer Park is a treat for wildlife enthusiasts. The park has a variety of animals, including spotted deer, Sambhar deer, and peacocks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silvassa

Silvassa is surrounded by lush greenery and hills, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The city has many scenic routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Some of the most popular routes for hiking and biking in Silvassa are:

  • Madhuban Dam – Dadra Vasona: This route is approximately 14 km long and takes approximately 5 hours to complete. It is a level 2 difficulty route and is ideal for beginners.

  • Lion Safari Park – Khanvel: This route is approximately 13 km long and takes approximately 4 hours to complete. The route is a level 3 difficulty route and is ideal for intermediate level hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Silvassa: Activities and Tours

From exploring the cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, Silvassa has plenty to offer. Some of the best things to do in Silvassa are:

  • Boating at Vanganga Lake and Island Garden: Boating in Vanganga Lake and Island Garden is a must-do activity. It is a perfect way to enjoy the lush greenery and scenic views.

  • Lion Safari at Wildlife Park: Lion Safari at Wildlife Park is a unique experience where visitors can spot lions and other wild animals in their natural habitat.

  • Trekking in the Hills: Silvassa is surrounded by hills that offer some fantastic hiking and trekking opportunities. The trekking trails are well marked and offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Silvassa

Day 1: Visit the Tribal Cultural Museum and Vanganga Lake and Island Garden

Day 2: Explore the Lion Safari Wildlife Park and Dudhni Lake

Day 3: Visit the Hirwa Van Garden and Satmaliya Deer Park

Day 4: Enjoy a morning hike in the hills and spend the afternoon at the Swaminarayan Temple

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Daman

Food and Drink Scene of Silvassa

Silvassa has a rich and diverse food culture that reflects the local cuisine of Gujarat. Some must-try dishes when in Silvassa are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dhokla: Dhokla is a Gujarati dish made from fermented batter of gram flour and steamed. It is served with chutney and garnished with coriander and grated coconut. It is a must-try dish when in Gujarat.

  • Undhiyu: Undhiyu is a traditional Gujarati dish made from a variety of vegetables like yam, brinjal, potatoes, and beans. It is served with puri and pickle.

Local Beverages

  • Chhaas: Chhaas is a popular local beverage made from beaten curd, water, and spices. It is a refreshing drink and is an excellent accompaniment to a meal.

  • Kokum Sharbat: Kokum Sharbat is made from the fruit of the kokum tree. It is a tangy and refreshing drink and is an ideal beverage for the hot summer months.

Where to Stay in Silvassa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silvassa has plenty of options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options are:

  • Treat Resort: The Treat Resort is a popular option for tourists. The resort offers a range of facilities like a swimming pool, spa, restaurant, and gym.

  • Hill View Resort: The Hill View Resort is a budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable accommodation and basic amenities.

  • Forest Village Holiday Homes: The Forest Village Holiday Homes are located on the outskirts of Silvassa and offer comfortable accommodation in a serene environment.

What to Buy in Silvassa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silvassa is known for its handicrafts and handloom products. Some popular souvenir items that you can buy in Silvassa are:

  • Warli Paintings: Warli paintings are a traditional form of art that originated in the Warli region of Maharashtra. You can find a variety of Warli paintings in Silvassa, which make for great souvenirs.

  • Terracotta Products: Terracotta products such as pots, lamps, and figurines are popular in Silvassa. They make for great home decor items and are perfect souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Silvassa

Silvassa is a blend of various cultures and traditions. The tribal culture of the region is reflected in its art, music, and dance forms. The Warli tribe is the most prominent tribe in the region and is known for its unique style of painting. The people of Silvassa are warm and hospitable and take pride in their cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Silvassa

The best time to visit Silvassa is between October and April when the weather is pleasant. The summers can be scorching, with temperatures soaring above 40 degrees Celsius. The monsoon season, from June to September, is also a good time to visit Silvassa for those who enjoy the rain and lush greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silvassa

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and tradition.

  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

  • Seek permission before taking photographs of locals, especially in tribal areas.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any form of illegal activities, including drug use.

  • Litter the environment or harm wildlife.

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silvassa

Silvassa is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take the necessary precautions, such as:

  • Avoid travelling alone at night.

  • Keep valuables in a safe place.

  • Be cautious when using public transport.

Health in Silvassa

Silvassa has good healthcare facilities, and there are hospitals and clinics in the city. It is always advisable to carry your prescription medicines and consult a doctor before travelling if you have any underlying medical conditions.

Getting to Silvassa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Silvassa is well-connected to major cities in India by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Silvassa

The nearest airport to Silvassa is Mumbai Airport, which is approximately 167 km away. From Mumbai Airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Silvassa.

Bus Stations Near Silvassa

  • Vapi Bus Station: 13 km away from Silvassa

  • Mumbai Central Bus Station: 173 km away from Silvassa

Other Options

Silvassa is connected to major cities in India by rail. The nearest railway station to Silvassa is Vapi Railway Station, which is approximately 18 km away. From Vapi Railway Station, you can take a taxi or a bus to reach Silvassa.

How to Get Around in Silvassa

The best way to get around in Silvassa is by hiring a taxi or a car. There are also local buses available, but they can be crowded and inconvenient at times. It is advisable to negotiate the fare before hiring a taxi or a car.

Where to Go Next from Silvassa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Daman: Daman is a coastal town located approximately 25 km away from Silvassa. It is known for its beautiful beaches and Portuguese architecture.

  • Vapi: Vapi is a small industrial town located approximately 18 km away from Silvassa. It is known for its textile industry and architectural landmarks.

  • Nashik: Nashik is a city located approximately 145 km away from Silvassa. It is known for its wineries and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Silvassa is a hidden gem in the western part of India. It is a perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful holiday away from the hustle and bustle of city life. The city has something for everyone, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures. With its lush greenery and serene landscapes, Silvassa is a must-visit destination in India.
