Introduction to South Gujarat

Located in the western region of India, South Gujarat is a vibrant destination that offers a blend of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. From ancient temples to modern museums, from serene beaches to thrilling hiking routes, South Gujarat truly has something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in South Gujarat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Laxmi Vilas Palace: This magnificent palace, located in Vadodara, is one of the largest private residences in the world.
  2. Baroda Museum and Picture Gallery: This museum has an impressive collection of art, natural history, and ethnography.
  3. Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site has ruins of a medieval fortress-city and ancient temples.
  4. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Memorial: This museum pays tribute to the life and achievements of India’s first Home Minister, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.

Natural Attractions of South Gujarat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Saputara: This picturesque hill station is surrounded by lush green forests and offers stunning views of the Sahyadri Range.
  2. Dumas Beach: This black sand beach near Surat is perfect for a relaxing evening stroll.
  3. Wilson Hills: This scenic hill station has a cool climate and offers picturesque views of the surrounding landscape.
  4. Shoolpaneshwar Wildlife Sanctuary: This forest reserve is home to various species of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, and crocodiles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Gujarat

  1. Girmal Falls Trek: This trek takes you through the dense forests of the Shoolpaneshwar Wildlife Sanctuary to the picturesque Girmal Falls.
  2. Saputara Monsoon Trek: This trek takes you through the lush green forests of Saputara, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Wilson Hills Trek: This trek takes you through the hilly terrain of Wilson Hills, offering breathtaking views of the nearby villages and forests.

Best Things to Do in South Gujarat: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Sabarmati Ashram: This historic ashram was home to Mahatma Gandhi and played a crucial role in India’s independence movement.
  2. Explore the Old City of Surat: This bustling city has a rich blend of history, culture, and modernity.
  3. Attend the Janmashtami Festival in Dwarka: This festival celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna and is celebrated with much fervor in Dwarka.
  4. Witness the Rann of Kutch Festival: This festival showcases the rich arts, crafts, and culture of the Rann of Kutch and is a must-visit for any traveler to Gujarat.

Food and Drink Scene of South Gujarat

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Undhiyu: This traditional Gujarati dish is a mix of vegetables, lentils, and spices, cooked in an earthen pot.
  2. Khandvi: This popular street-food is made of gram flour and yoghurt, seasoned with mustard and sesame.
  3. Dhokla: This savoury snack is made of fermented rice and lentils, garnished with coriander leaves and grated coconut.
  4. Jalebi-Fafda: This sweet and salty combination of jalebi and fafda is a popular breakfast dish in Gujarat.

Local Beverages

  1. Chaas: This savory drink made of yoghurt, water, and spices is a refreshing beverage.
  2. Aam panna: This refreshing drink is made of raw mango pulp, sugar, and spices.
  3. Kokum Sharbat: This popular drink is made of kokum fruit, sugar, and water and is a favorite during summers.

Where to Stay in South Gujarat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Vadodara: The Gateway Hotel, Hotel Surya, and Hotel Express Towers are some popular options.
  2. Surat: The Grand Bhagwati and Ginger Hotel are popular options in Surat.
  3. Saputara: Some popular options in Saputara include Hotel Aakar Lords Inn, Shilpi Hill Resort, and Hotel Savshanti.

What to Buy in South Gujarat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bandhani Sarees: These colorful tie-and-die sarees from Bhuj are a popular buy in Gujarat.
  2. Embroidered Textiles: The towns of Kutch and Jamnagar are known for their intricate and colorful embroidery work.
  3. Handicrafts and Pottery: The village of Nirona is known for its traditional handicrafts and pottery work.

Local Culture and Customs of South Gujarat

South Gujarat has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of Hindu, Islamic, and Jain influences. The region is known for its vibrant festivals, colorful attire, and intricate handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit South Gujarat

The best time to visit South Gujarat is from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Gujarat

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bargain while shopping for local products.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Wear shoes inside religious places.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Gujarat

South Gujarat is a relatively safe destination, but it is recommended to remain cautious, especially when traveling alone at night. It is also important to be aware of any particular safety risks in the region and take appropriate precautions.

Health in South Gujarat

Travelers to South Gujarat are advised to take indigestion prevention measures and carry necessary medications for common ailments. It is also recommended to take necessary vaccinations before traveling to the region.

Getting to South Gujarat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Gujarat

  1. Vadodara Airport: Located in Vadodara, this airport is well-connected with major cities in India.
  2. Surat Airport: Located in Surat, this airport is well-connected with major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near South Gujarat

  1. Vadodara Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Vadodara and is well-connected with major cities in India.
  2. Surat Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Surat and is well-connected with major cities in India.

Other Options

Trains and taxis are other popular options to travel to and within South Gujarat.

How to Get Around in South Gujarat

Local buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws are popular options to get around in South Gujarat.

Where to Go Next from South Gujarat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ahmedabad: The cultural capital of Gujarat, Ahmedabad is known for its stunning architecture, heritage sites, and a thriving food scene.
  2. Diu: This tranquil town is located near the coast and is a popular destination for its stunning beaches and colonial architecture.
  3. Patan: This ancient town is known for its UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Rani ki Vav.

Conclusion

South Gujarat is a treasure trove of cultural, historical, and natural attractions, making it a must-visit destination in India. From exploring ancient temples to hiking scenic trails, from savoring local delicacies to shopping for traditional handicrafts, South Gujarat offers a plethora of experiences for every type of traveler. Plan your trip to South Gujarat today and get ready to be mesmerized by its charm and beauty!
