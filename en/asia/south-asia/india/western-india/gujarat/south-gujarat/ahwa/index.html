Ahwa is a small town located in the Dang district of South Gujarat, India. Known for its lush green forests, waterfalls, and natural beauty, Ahwa is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. In this travel guide, we will explore what Ahwa has to offer and provide all the necessary information to plan your perfect trip.

Introduction to Ahwa

Ahwa is situated in the Western Ghats range, a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is surrounded by dense forests, which are home to various animals and birds, including tigers, leopards, deer, and peacocks. The town is named after a local deity, Ahwa Devi, and has many temples dedicated to her. The official language of the region is Gujarati, but Hindi and English are also widely spoken.

List of Places to Visit in Ahwa

There are several cultural and natural attractions to explore in and around Ahwa. Here are some of the must-visit places in Ahwa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shabari Dham Temple: Shabari Dham Temple is a popular pilgrimage site located on top of a hill in Ahwa. It is dedicated to Shabari, a character from the Indian epic Ramayana, who is known for her devotion to Lord Rama.
  • Saputara Hill Station: Located around 50 km from Ahwa, Saputara is a picturesque hill station known for its scenic beauty and cool weather. It is also home to several museums and parks that showcase the local culture and history.
  • Gira Waterfall: Gira Waterfall is a popular picnic spot located near Ahwa. It is surrounded by dense forests and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Saputara Tribal Museum: Saputara Tribal Museum is a must-visit for those who want to learn about the culture and traditions of the local tribes. The museum has various exhibits showcasing the traditional dress, music, and crafts of the tribes.

Natural Attractions of Ahwa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Purna Wildlife Sanctuary: Purna Wildlife Sanctuary, located around 30 km from Ahwa, is home to various animals and birds, including tigers, leopards, and bison. The sanctuary also has several trekking trails and waterfalls that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding forests.
  • Don Hill Station: Don Hill Station is a scenic destination located around 15 km from Ahwa. It is a popular spot for trekking and offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Girmal Waterfall: Girmal Waterfall is a majestic waterfall located near Ahwa. It is surrounded by dense forests and offers a serene environment for picnics and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ahwa

Ahwa and the surrounding areas offer several hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Saputara-Dang: This route connects the Saputara hill station to Ahwa via the Dang forests. It is a popular trekking route that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Don Hill Trek: The Don Hill Trek is a challenging and scenic trek that takes you to the top of Don Hill Station.
  • Purna Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: The Purna Wildlife Sanctuary Trek is a must-try for wildlife enthusiasts. It takes you through the dense forests of the sanctuary and offers a chance to spot various animals and birds.

Best Things to Do in Ahwa: Activities and Tours

Ahwa offers a wide range of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some of the best things to do in Ahwa:

  • Tribal Village Tour: Take a tour to the nearby tribal villages to learn about their culture and traditions. You can also buy traditional handicrafts and souvenirs from the village markets.
  • Campfire and Stargazing: Camping in the forests of Ahwa is a unique experience. Spend a night around a campfire and gaze at the stars in the clear sky.
  • Bird Watching Tour: Ahwa and the surrounding areas are home to various bird species, making it a paradise for bird watchers. Take a guided bird watching tour to spot some of the rare and beautiful birds.

Food and Drink Scene of Ahwa

Ahwa is known for its delicious Gujarati cuisine and local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Undhiyu: Undhiyu is a mixed vegetable dish that is a must-try in the region. It is prepared with seasonal vegetables and spices and served with puri or roti.
  • Khandvi: Khandvi is a popular snack in Gujarat made from besan (gram flour) and spices. It is a light and healthy snack that is perfect for tea-time.
  • Dhokla: Dhokla is a traditional Gujarati dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is a light and airy snack that is perfect for breakfast or as a tea-time snack.

Local Beverages

  • Sugarcane Juice: Sugarcane juice is a refreshing drink that is popular in Gujarat. It is sweet and nutritious and is perfect to beat the heat.
  • Chaas: Chaas is a buttermilk-based drink that is popular in Gujarat. It is a cool and refreshing drink that is perfect for warm summer days.

Where to Stay in Ahwa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ahwa has several options for accommodation. Here are some of the best places to stay in Ahwa:

  • Dang Darbar Hotel: Dang Darbar Hotel is located in the heart of Ahwa and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities. It is perfect for those who want to stay in the town center.
  • Saputara Hill Resort: Saputara Hill Resort is located near the Saputara hill station and offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills and valleys. It is perfect for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.
  • Jungle Stay: Jungle Stay offers unique accommodation in the forests of Ahwa. It is perfect for those who want to experience the wilderness and stay close to nature.

What to Buy in Ahwa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ahwa is known for its traditional handicrafts and local products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy:

  • Tribal Handicrafts: The local tribal communities of Ahwa are known for their traditional handicrafts, such as bamboo baskets, mats, and dolls.
  • Dang Durries: Dang Durries are colorful hand-woven rugs that are popular in the region. They are made using traditional techniques and are perfect to decorate your home.
  • Honey: Ahwa is known for its delicious honey, which is collected from the local forests. It is pure and natural and is perfect for health-conscious individuals.

Local Culture and Customs of Ahwa

Ahwa is a diverse town with a rich cultural heritage. The local people are friendly and hospitable, and the town has a unique blend of Hindu and tribal cultures. Here are some of the local customs and traditions:

  • Garba: Garba is a traditional dance form that is popular in Gujarat. It is performed during Navratri, a nine-day festival that celebrates the victory of good over evil.
  • Tribal Festivals: The local tribal communities of Ahwa celebrate several festivals throughout the year, such as Holi, Diwali, and Raksha Bandhan. These festivals are the perfect opportunity to experience the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ahwa

The best time to visit Ahwa is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season, from June to September, is also a good time to visit as the forests and waterfalls are at their best during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ahwa

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ahwa:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious places.
  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen while visiting the forests.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in the forests or waterfalls.
  • Do not disturb the animals or birds in the wildlife sanctuary.
  • Do not take photographs of the local people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Ahwa is a safe destination for tourists, but it is always advisable to take precautions while traveling.

Safety in Ahwa

  • Do not venture into the forests alone, especially after dark.
  • Do not leave your belongings unattended in public places.
  • Always carry a copy of your ID and travel documents.

Health in Ahwa

  • Drink only bottled water or boiled water.
  • Carry a first-aid kit with essential medications.
  • Seek immediate medical attention if you feel unwell or have any health issues.
