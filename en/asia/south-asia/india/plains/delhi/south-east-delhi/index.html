Introduction to South East Delhi

South East Delhi is located in the Plains region of Delhi, the capital city of India. The area is popular among travelers for its cultural and historical attractions, scenic views, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. It’s a perfect destination for those who want to explore the rich history and diverse culture of Delhi and India. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, local food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, and more. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in South East Delhi

From museums and historical sites to natural attractions, there are plenty of places to visit in South East Delhi. Here are the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Humayun’s Tomb: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the best-preserved Mughal monuments in Delhi, and a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Qutub Minar: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, Qutub Minar is a 73-meter tall minaret constructed in 1199 AD, and one of the most famous landmarks of Delhi.
  • Lotus Temple: This unique Bahai House of Worship is shaped like a lotus flower and attracts visitors from all over the world.
  • National Museum: With more than 200,000 artifacts from pre-historic era to modern times, this museum is a treasure trove of Indian history and culture.
  • Raj Ghat: This peaceful and tranquil park is the cremation site of Mahatma Gandhi, a symbol of peace and non-violence in India.

Natural Attractions of South East Delhi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sanjay Van: A sprawling forest area in South Delhi, popular among nature lovers and birdwatchers for its serene atmosphere and diverse wildlife.
  • Okhla Bird Sanctuary: A biodiversity hotspot with over 300 species of birds, located on the banks of Yamuna river.
  • Tughlaqabad Fort: A 14th-century fortress ruins atop a hill, offering panoramic views of Delhi and a perfect spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Nehru Park: A scenic park with beautiful landscapes and fountains, perfect for a relaxing afternoon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South East Delhi

If you’re into hiking and biking, South East Delhi has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Ridge Road Walk: A scenic and easy route that takes you through the lush forests and parks of South Delhi.
  • River Yamuna Trail: Follow the winding River Yamuna and take in the sights and sounds of rural India.
  • Asola Bhatti Wildlife Sanctuary: A challenging route through the wilderness, with plenty of wildlife sightings along the way.

Best Things to Do in South East Delhi: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or solo, South East Delhi has something for everyone. Here are some of the most popular things to do:

  • Heritage Walks: Explore the historical and cultural landmarks of Delhi with a local guide, and learn about the rich history and diverse culture of India.
  • Food Tours: Indulge in some of the best local delicacies and street food of Delhi, and experience the vibrant and colorful local culture.
  • Cycle Tours: Discover the hidden gems of Delhi on a cycle, and experience the city like a local.
  • Sightseeing Tours: Visit the famous landmarks and monuments of Delhi, and get a glimpse of its rich history and diverse culture.

Food and Drink Scene of South East Delhi

South East Delhi is home to some of the best food and drink destinations in Delhi. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chole Bhature: A popular North Indian dish made with spiced chickpeas and deep-fried bread, served with pickles and yogurt.
  • Parantha: A delicious and filling breakfast dish made with flaky bread stuffed with various fillings like potatoes, cheese, and vegetables.
  • Kebabs: Succulent pieces of marinated meat or vegetables, traditionally cooked in a Tandoor oven.
  • Biryani: A fragrant and flavorful rice dish cooked with meat or vegetables, spices, and herbs.
  • Lassi: A refreshing and cooling yogurt-based drink, perfect for beating the Delhi heat.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: A spiced tea served with milk and sugar, a popular and quintessential Indian drink.
  • Thandai: A chilled and sweet milk-based drink flavored with nuts, spices, and saffron, usually served during festivals or special occasions.
  • Nimbu Pani: A refreshing and tangy lemonade, perfect for beating the Delhi heat.

  • Taj Mahal Hotel: A luxurious and iconic hotel with stunning views of the city.
  • The Oberoi: Another luxurious hotel with modern amenities and excellent service.

What to Buy in South East Delhi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without buying some souvenirs, and South East Delhi has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the most popular local products and souvenirs:

  • Handicrafts: Delhi is famous for its intricate handicrafts, like pottery, jewelry, and textiles, all available at various markets and shops.
  • Spices: Delhi is a foodie’s paradise, and buying some local spices like cumin, cardamom, and turmeric is a must.
  • Books: Delhi has some excellent book shops, including the famous Daryaganj Sunday book market, where you can find rare and out-of-print books.
  • Clothes and Accessories: Delhi has a vibrant and colorful fashion scene, and buying some traditional clothes and accessories like sarees, bangles, and scarves is a great idea.

Local Culture and Customs of South East Delhi

India is a diverse and multicultural country, and South East Delhi is no exception. Here’s what you need to know about the local culture and customs:

  • Greeting: Indians usually greet each other with a Namaste, which involves joining both hands and bowing down slightly.
  • Dress Code: India is a conservative country, and it’s advisable to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Religion: India is a secular country with a diverse range of religions, including Hinduism, Islam, Sikhism, and Christianity, among others.
  • Language: Hindi is the official language of Delhi, but English is widely spoken and understood.

Best Time to Visit South East Delhi

The best time to visit South East Delhi is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable, with temperatures ranging from 20-25 degrees Celsius. Summers (April to June) can be hot and humid, with temperatures soaring up to 45 degrees Celsius, while winters (December to February) can be cold and foggy.

Do’s and Don’ts of South East Delhi

To ensure a safe and comfortable trip, here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting South East Delhi:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn some basic Hindi phrases to communicate with the locals.
  • Use public transport or book taxis from reputed companies.
  • Try the local street food.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water. Stick to bottled water or filtered water.
  • Take unsolicited advice and help from strangers.
  • Carry large amounts of cash or valuables with you.
  • Photograph people or local customs without asking.

Safety and Health

Safety and Health are essential aspects of any trip, and South East Delhi is no exception. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in South East Delhi

Like any other major city, South East Delhi has its share of petty crime, and it’s essential to take some precautions to ensure a safe trip. Here are some tips:

  • Stay Alert: Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert, especially when using public transport or walking around crowded markets.
  • Avoid Lonely Places: Avoid traveling alone to secluded areas, especially at night.
  • Use Reputed Taxis: Use taxis from reputed companies or book through hotel receptions to avoid scams or frauds.
  • Avoid Street Dogs: Delhi has a lot of street dogs, and it’s advisable to stay away from them to avoid any mishaps.

Health in South East Delhi

Delhi can be a challenging city to navigate, especially for first-time visitors. Here are some tips to stay healthy:

  • Drink Bottled or Filtered Water: Delhi’s tap water is not safe to drink, so make sure to drink bottled or filtered water.
  • Eat at Hygienic Places: It’s advisable to eat at hygienic places and avoid street food, especially meat dishes or anything that requires water.
  • Take Vaccinations: Make sure to take vaccinations for common diseases like hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus before traveling to Delhi.
  • Carry a First Aid Kit: Carry a basic first aid kit with essential medicines like pain relievers, band-aids, and antacids.

Getting to South East Delhi: Transportation Options and Logistics

South East Delhi is well connected with the rest of Delhi and India, and here are some transportation options:

Airports Near South East Delhi

  • Indira Gandhi International Airport: Located 22 km from South East Delhi, it’s the busiest airport in India, with flights to major international and domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near South East Delhi

  • Sarai Kale Khan Inter-State Bus Terminus: Located in South Delhi, it’s a major bus terminal with buses connecting to major cities and towns in India.

Other Options

  • Metro: Delhi has a well-established metro system with multiple lines that connect to various parts of the city, including South East Delhi.
  • Taxis/Cabs: Taxis and cabs are readily available in Delhi, and you can book them through apps like Uber or Ola.
  • Auto-Rickshaws: Auto-rickshaws are a cheap and convenient transportation option, especially for short distances.

How to Get Around in South East Delhi

South East Delhi is a large and diversified area, and here are the best ways to get around:

  • Metro: Delhi Metro has stations in South East Delhi, and it’s the fastest and most convenient way to get around, especially during peak hours.
  • Taxis/Cabs: Taxis and cabs are widely available, but they can be expensive during peak hours, and there’s a risk of traffic congestion.
  • Auto-Rickshaws: Auto-rickshaws are a popular and affordable mode of transportation, especially for short distances, but make sure to negotiate the fare before getting in.

Where to Go Next from South East Delhi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to add to your itinerary:

  • Agra: Visit the iconic Taj Mahal, and explore the rich history and culture of Agra, located 200 km from Delhi.
  • Jaipur: The Pink City of India, known for its stunning palaces and forts, is located around 280 km from Delhi.
  • Rishikesh: Located around 240 km from Delhi, Rishikesh is a hub for adventure sports and spirituality, and a perfect destination for those seeking a little adventure.

South East Delhi is a vibrant and diverse destination, full of history, culture, and adventure.
