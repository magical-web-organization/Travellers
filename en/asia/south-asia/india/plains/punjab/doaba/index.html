Introduction to Doaba

Doaba is a region in the state of Punjab, India. It is located between the two rivers, Beas and Sutlej, and is the land of lush green fields, rich history, and vibrant culture. The region derives its name from the fact that it is a naturally occurring land strip between two rivers, do (two) and aba (waters). Doaba is renowned for its vibrant culture, bhangra dance, dhol beats, and lip-smacking food that is famous worldwide. In this travel guide, explore the cultural and natural gems of Doaba that make it one of the most sought-after travel destinations in India.

List of Places to Visit in Doaba

The region of Doaba has plenty of attractions for travelers, from historical sites to natural wonders. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Doaba.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kapurthala

Kapurthala, known as the “Paris of Punjab,” is a city located in Doaba that was once the princely state of Kapurthala. This city is known for its magnificent 19th-century palace, known as the Jagatjit Palace, which is now a museum.

Anandpur Sahib

Anandpur Sahib is a historical town where the 10th Sikh guru, Guru Gobind Singh, founded the Sikh warrior community, known as Khalsa. The town is home to several gurudwaras, including Takht Keshgarh Sahib, which is the birthplace of the Khalsa.

Jalandhar

Jalandhar is the oldest city of Doaba and is known for its rich history and culture. The city is home to several historical sites, including the Devi Talab Mandir, the Rangla Punjab haveli, and the Wonderland Amusement Park.

Natural Attractions of Doaba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Harike Wetland

Harike Wetland is a natural reserve located at the confluence of the Beas and Sutlej rivers. It is a paradise for bird watchers, as it is home to more than 200 species of migratory birds.

Kila Raipur

Kila Raipur is a small village located in Doaba and is famous for its rural Olympics. The annual event attracts thousands of visitors who come to witness the unique sporting events, including bullock cart racing, horse racing, and tug-of-war.

Bhakra Nangal Dam

The Bhakra Nangal Dam is one of the highest gravity dams in Asia and is located on the Sutlej River. It is a stunning architectural wonder that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Doaba

Doaba offers several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty. The following are some of the most popular routes:

  • Amritsar to Dharamsala via Jalandhar and Pathankot
  • Ludhiana to Ropar along the canal
  • Quila Mohd. Khan to Nurpur Bedi trek

Local Culture and Customs of Doaba

Doaba’s culture is unique and vibrant, with a strong influence from Sikh traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting historical sites or places of worship. Removing shoes and covering heads is also mandatory while visiting gurudwaras.

Best Time to Visit Doaba

The best time to visit Doaba is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature remains moderate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Doaba

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Doaba:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the culture and customs of the region
  • Carry sufficient cash as ATMs may not be easily accessible in rural areas

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious sites
  • Refrain from smoking or consuming alcohol in public places
  • Take photographs without seeking permission at historical sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Doaba

Doaba is generally a safe region to visit. However, visitors should take reasonable precautions to ensure their safety, including keeping their valuables secure and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Doaba

Visitors should ensure that they receive all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Doaba. They should also drink only bottled water and carry adequate medication for any pre-existing conditions.
