Introduction to Jalandhar

Jalandhar, located in the Doaba region of Punjab, is a thriving industrial city that is steeped in history and culture. The city has been an important part of India’s history and has been mentioned in various ancient texts.

The city is home to several religious sites, historical landmarks and museums, making it an ideal destination for visitors interested in exploring the rich history of India. Additionally, Jalandhar is also known for its scenic beauty and outdoor adventure activities, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Jalandhar

Jalandhar is a city with several tourist attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Prithvi’s Planet: Prithvi’s planet is a 3D planetarium with an impressive array of shows available that are perfect for adults and children alike.
  • Wonderland: This is an amusement and water park with over 20 different rides and attractions, most of which are suitable for younger children.
  • Devi Talab Mandir: This temple complex is located in the heart of Jalandhar City and is dedicated to goddess Durga.
  • Gurdwara Chhevin Padshahi: This is one of the most important Sikh pilgrimage sites in India and was visited by Guru Hargobind Singh Ji, the sixth Sikh Guru.
  • Mikveh Israel Synagogue & Jewish Cemetery: This is one of the only synagogues remaining in India, and it is still used for worship today.
  • Shaheed-E-Azam Sardar Bhagat Singh Museum: This museum displays the life and times of Sardar Bhagat Singh, a revolutionary and freedom fighter from India.

Natural Attractions of Jalandhar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pushpa Gujral Science City: This science park is a popular destination in Jalandhar and offers a wide range of activities, exhibits, and shows related to science and technology.
  • Mansa Devi Temple: This is an ancient temple located in the midst of a dense forest, with impressive views of the surrounding hills.
  • Rangla Punjab Haveli: This is a cultural village that showcases the traditional aspects of Punjabi culture, including dance, music, and cuisine.
  • Nehru Park: Located in the heart of the city, Nehru Park is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. It offers a green space to relax, exercise, and enjoy the fresh air.
  • Panch Mandir: This is a sacred Hindu site consisting of five temples that were built in the 19th century.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jalandhar

Jalandhar is surrounded by hills and forests, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few popular routes:

  • Sarna Hill: This is a popular trekking spot that offers breathtaking views of the city and surrounding hills.
  • Gurdwara Ber Sahib Trek: This trek starts from the Ber Sahib Gurdwara and takes you through scenic forest trails to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Phillaur Fort Trek: This trek takes you through a dense forest and offers views of the Phillaur Fort at the top of the hill.
  • Pushpa Gujral Science City Trek: This is a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Jalandhar: Activities and Tours

  • Go on a heritage walk with a local tour guide to learn about Jalandhar’s rich history and culture.
  • Visit a local farm and learn about traditional farming techniques and Punjabi cuisine.
  • Participate in a Sikh prayer session at one of the city’s Gurdwaras .
  • Take an early morning yoga class in Nehru Park.
  • Attend a live Punjabi music and dance performance at Rangla Punjab Haveli.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jalandhar

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to Jalandhar:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Devi Talab Mandir, Shaheed-E-Azam Sardar Bhagat Singh Museum and Nehru Park.
Day 2 Trek to Sarna Hill, followed by a visit to Rangla Punjab Haveli.
Day 3 Explore the City Centre (shopping district) and visit the Mikveh Israel Synagogue.
Day 4 Day-trip to Pushpa Gujral Science City and explore the surroundings.
Day 5 Visit Gurdwara Chhevin Padshahi, Panch Mandir, and Wonder Land.
Day 6 Visit Mansa Devi Temple and Phillaur Fort Trail.
Day 7 Participate in a Sikh prayer session followed by a visit to a local farm.

Food and Drink Scene of Jalandhar

Jalandhar is renowned for its flavourful Punjabi cuisine, and the food scene here is vibrant and varied. Here are a few dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Makki Ki Roti & Sarson Ka Saag: A popular Punjabi dish made with cornmeal flatbread and mustard greens.
  • Tandoori Chicken: Marinated in a mix of spices and yoghurt and cooked on a clay oven.
  • Butter Chicken: Chicken cooked in a creamy tomato sauce.
  • Chole Bhature: Fried bread served with a chickpea curry.
  • Lassi: A quintessential Punjabi beverage made of yoghurt and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Kesar Lassi: A sweeter version of lassi made with saffron and sugar.
  • Thandai: A spiced milk-based beverage served cold, usually made during the Holi festival.
  • Lemon Chaat: A refreshing drink made with lemon, sugar and chat masala.

Where to Stay in Jalandhar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jalandhar has a good range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and tastes. Here are some options:

  • Hotel President: A spacious hotel in the heart of the city with excellent amenities and services.
  • Ramada Jalandhar City Centre: A luxurious hotel that provides top-notch facilities and a premium experience.
  • New Punjab Hotel: A budget-friendly guesthouse that provides basic amenities at an affordable cost.

What to Buy in Jalandhar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Phulkari Dupattas: Intricately designed embroidered shawls and scarves.
  • Punjabi Jutti: Traditional ethnic footwear designed with colourful embroidery and beads.
  • Punjabi Jewellery: Traditional Indian jewellery with intricate designs and patterns.
  • Punjabi Wadiyan: Made from seasonal vegetables like mustard and cauliflower and are a popular snack in the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Jalandhar

Jalandhar has a rich tradition of Punjabi culture, which is colourful and vibrant. The people of Jalandhar are friendly and welcoming, and it is considered polite to greet people with a smile and a friendly handshake.

Modesty in dress is highly appreciated, and it is best to avoid wearing revealing clothing in public places.

Best Time to Visit Jalandhar

The best time to visit Jalandhar is between October and March, as the weather is cooler and pleasant during this time. Summers can be scorching, with temperatures rising up to 45 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jalandhar

Do:

  • Wear light cotton clothes during summers.
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Carry your passport and other important documents with you.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jalandhar

Jalandhar is a relatively safe city, but it is always advisable to take necessary precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night, carrying a copy of your identification documents, and not flashing money or valuables in public places.

Health in Jalandhar

It is important to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months, and to carry your necessary medication and prescriptions with you. It is advisable to drink bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Jalandhar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jalandhar

The nearest international airport to Jalandhar is Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport in Amritsar, which is approximately 90 kilometres from Jalandhar City. Jalandhar also has its own airport (Jalandhar Airport), but there are no international flights operating from here as yet.

Bus Stations Near Jalandhar

Jalandhar has its own bus stand, from where you can take a bus to various cities in Punjab and other neighbouring states.

Other Options

You can also reach Jalandhar by train, as the city is well-connected to other major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Jalandhar

Autorickshaws and taxis are the best options for getting around in Jalandhar. Public transport like buses is also available and can be used for travel within the city and nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Jalandhar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amritsar: Home to the Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh, and Wagah Border.
  • Ludhiana: Known for its textile industry and historical landmarks like Lodhi Fort.
  • Pathankot: Close to Dharamshala, the home of the Dalai Lama and the stunning hill station of McLeod Ganj.

Conclusion

Jalandhar is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient temples, trekking through hills and forests, or immersing yourself in Punjabi culture and cuisine, Jalandhar is the perfect destination. Plan your trip today and experience the best of Jalandhar!
