Introduction to Qadian

Located in the Majha region of Punjab, India, Qadian is a small town with a population of around 45,000. It is an important cultural center for the Ahmadiyya Muslim community, which was founded here in the late 19th century by Mirza Ghulam Ahmad. Qadian is also known for its stunning natural scenery, including lush green fields, sparkling rivers, and rolling hills with spectacular mountain views.

List of Places to Visit in Qadian

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Masjid Aqsa

Masjid Aqsa is one of the most significant religious and historical sites in Qadian. The mosque is located in the center of the town and is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the town. The mosque is open to visitors and offers a peaceful and serene atmosphere to explore.

2. The Ahmadiyya Buildings

The Ahmadiyya Buildings are a group of historic buildings, including the Dar-ul-Masih, the house of Mirza Ghulam Ahmad, Nur Manzil, and the mausoleum of Hazrat Mirza Ghulam Ahmad. Visitors can explore the stunning architecture and grounds, and learn about the philosophy and teachings of the Ahmadiyya community.

3. The Bahishti Maqbara

The Bahishti Maqbara is a cemetery where Mirza Ghulam Ahmad is buried. The cemetery is beautifully landscaped and offers a peaceful atmosphere for visitors to pay their respects.

Natural Attractions of Qadian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Beas River

The Beas River is a scenic and peaceful spot located just outside the town. Visitors can enjoy a picnic by the river or take a leisurely stroll along the banks. Fishing and other water activities are also available.

2. Kila Raipur Sports Festival

The Kila Raipur Sports Festival is held annually in February and features traditional rural sports competitions, such as tug-of-war, wrestling, and cart racing. The festival attracts people from all over the region and is a must-see for anyone visiting Qadian during that time.

3. Ranjit Sagar Dam

Ranjit Sagar Dam is a massive hydroelectric dam located just outside of Qadian. The dam provides stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys and offers visitors a chance to appreciate the technological ingenuity that went into building it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qadian

Qadian and the surrounding areas offer a range of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. The most popular routes include:

  • Bhatinda to Leh
  • Pathankot Trail
  • Qadian Riverside Trail

These routes offer visitors the chance to explore the stunning natural scenery and learn about the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Qadian: Activities and Tours

1. Ahmadiyya Buildings Tour

No visit to Qadian is complete without a tour of the Ahmadiyya Buildings. Visitors can explore the stunning architecture and learn about the history and philosophy of the Ahmadiyya community.

2. Beas River Adventure

Visitors can enjoy a range of water activities, such as fishing, boating, and kayaking, at the Beas River. Guided tours are also available.

3. Kila Raipur Sports Festival

The Kila Raipur Sports Festival is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that features a range of traditional rural sports competitions. Visitors can witness the athleticism and skill of local athletes.

Food and Drink Scene of Qadian

Must-Try Dishes

1. Makki ki Roti and Sarson ka Saag

Makki ki Roti and Sarson ka Saag is a popular Punjabi dish consisting of maize flour flatbread and a vegetable side dish made from mustard greens.

2. Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a famous North Indian dish made from chickpeas cooked in a spicy tomato-based sauce served with fried bread known as Bhatura.

3. Kulfi

Kulfi is a traditional Indian frozen dessert made from milk, sugar, and various flavorings.

Local Beverages

1. Kesar Lassi

Kesar Lassi is a traditional Indian drink made from yogurt, sugar, and saffron. It is a refreshing and cooling drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

2. Chai

Chai is a traditional Indian tea made with a mixture of spices and milk. It is a comforting and warming beverage that is perfect for cold winter days.

Local Culture and Customs of Qadian

Visitors to Qadian should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Covering your head is mandatory when visiting religious sites such as mosques or mausoleums. Visitors should also dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Qadian

The best time to visit Qadian is between October and March when the weather is milder and more pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qadian

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample the local beverages.
  • Always carry cash as credit cards may not be accepted everywhere.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, always drink bottled water.
  • Wear shorts or revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qadian

Qadian is generally a safe town, but visitors should always take precautions like they would while travelling anywhere else. Avoid walking alone in isolated areas during the night and always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Qadian

Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food. It is always a good idea to carry necessary medications for common ailments.

Getting to Qadian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qadian

The nearest airport to Qadian is Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport (ATQ) located in Amritsar, which is approximately 110 kilometers away from Qadian.

Bus Stations Near Qadian

Qadian Bus Station is located in the center of the town and connects it with major cities in Punjab and neighboring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or hire a car to reach Qadian from major cities.

How to Get Around in Qadian

Visitors can explore Qadian on foot or hire a taxi or a local driver.

Where to Go Next from Qadian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can head to the nearby city of Amritsar, which is home to the Golden Temple, one of the most revered symbols of Sikhism. Other nearby destinations include Pathankot, Gurdaspur, and Jalandhar.

Conclusion

Qadian is a beautiful and culturally rich town in Punjab that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the history and traditions of the region. From the Ahmadiyya Buildings to the stunning natural scenery, visitors are sure to find plenty of things to see and do in Qadian. With its rich culture and stunning outdoor locales, Qadian is a must-visit destination for any discerning traveler.
