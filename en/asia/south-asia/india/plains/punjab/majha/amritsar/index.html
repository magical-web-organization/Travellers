Introduction to Amritsar

Amritsar, located in the state of Punjab in India, is a city rich in culture, heritage, and history. It is home to the Golden Temple, the holiest shrine for Sikhs, and attracts millions of visitors from all over the world every year. The city also offers a plethora of other cultural and historical attractions, as well as an exciting food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Amritsar

If you’re planning a trip to Amritsar, these are the places that you should definitely visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Golden Temple: This is the main attraction in Amritsar and is a must-visit for all tourists. The temple is made of gold and is surrounded by a large holy pond where pilgrims take a dip to cleanse themselves.
  • Jallianwala Bagh: This historical site is where the tragic Amritsar Massacre took place in 1919, when British soldiers killed hundreds of unarmed Indian protesters.
  • Partition Museum: This museum tells the story of the partition of India and Pakistan in 1947 that led to the displacement of millions of people.
  • Maharaja Ranjit Singh Museum: This museum displays the life and times of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, also known as the Lion of Punjab, who was the ruler of the Sikh Empire in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Amritsar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wagah Border: This is the border crossing between India and Pakistan and is famous for its daily flag-lowering ceremony, also known as the Beating Retreat ceremony.
  • Harike Wetland and Bird Sanctuary: This is a perfect place for nature lovers and bird watchers. It is a habitat for a variety of bird species, including migratory birds.
  • Gobindgarh Fort: This historic fort offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the city of Amritsar, and also houses museums, galleries and a sound and light show.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amritsar

If you love to explore nature on foot or on wheels, Amritsar offers a variety of hiking and biking routes:

  • Mata Bala Sundri Trek: This 14-kilometer trek takes you through the beautiful green hills and rocky terrains, and offers stunning views of the city.
  • Khasa Trail: This trail is perfect for mountain bikers and offers challenging uphill and downhill sections with muddy tracks and forested areas to explore.

Best Things to Do in Amritsar: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tours: Amritsar is famous for its street food, and a food tour is the best way to experience the local cuisine.
  • Heritage Walks: Take a guided walk through the ancient streets of Amritsar and discover its rich history and culture.
  • Nightlife: Visit some of the local bars and pubs to enjoy the vibrant nightlife scene of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amritsar

If you have a week to spend in Amritsar, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh, and the Partition Museum
2 Explore the city’s heritage by visiting the Gobindgarh Fort, Akal Takht, and the Durgiana Temple
3 Take a day trip to Wagah Border and the Atari Railway Station
4 Enjoy a food tour of Amritsar
5 Explore the city’s natural beauty by visiting Harike Wetland and Bird Sanctuary
6 Go on a Mata Bala Sundri Trek or Khasa Trail
7 Shopping in the city’s bazaars and markets for souvenirs and local products

Food and Drink Scene of Amritsar

When in Amritsar, here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amritsari Kulcha: A soft and fluffy bread stuffed with spicy mashed potatoes, lentils, and onion.
  • Butter Chicken: Boneless chicken cooked in a rich and buttery tomato-based sauce.
  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink, perfect for hot summer days.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A milk-based drink infused with almonds, spices, and saffron.
  • Chai: The Indian version of tea, which is made with black tea leaves, milk, sugar, and aromatic spices.

Where to Stay in Amritsar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amritsar has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from hotels to hostels and guesthouses.

  • Luxury Hotels: If you’re looking for high-end accommodation, you can choose from the Taj Swarna, Radisson Blu Hotel Amritsar, and Hyatt Regency Amritsar.
  • Budget Hotels: For those on a tight budget, the Hotel Basant Residency, Hotel Ritz Plaza, and Hotel Lawrence are good options.
  • Hostels: Backpackers can choose from Zostel Amritsar, Joey’s Hostel, and Jugaadus Eco Hostel.

What to Buy in Amritsar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Amritsar, don’t forget to take back some souvenirs and local products:

  • Punjabi Jutti: These are traditional shoes made of leather and embroidered with intricate designs.
  • Phulkari Dupatta: A traditional embroidered shawl that is unique to the state of Punjab.
  • Pashmina Shawls: A soft and luxurious woolen shawl that is perfect for cold winter days.
  • Spices and Pickles: Amritsar is known for its aromatic spices and flavorful pickles, which make great gifts for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Amritsar

Amritsar is a city that is steeped in tradition and customs. Here are some basic etiquette and customs that you should be aware of:

  • Dress Code: When visiting the Golden Temple, make sure to dress modestly and cover your head. It is also recommended that you remove your shoes before entering.
  • Respect for Elders: Elders are highly respected in Punjabi society, and it is customary to touch their feet as a sign of respect.
  • Greeting: When greeting someone, it is customary to say “Sat Sri Akal”, which means “God is truth”.

Best Time to Visit Amritsar

The best time to visit Amritsar is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and cool. The summer months, from April to June, can be scorching hot, and the monsoon season, from July to September, can bring heavy rain and humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amritsar

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Amritsar:

Do:

  • Respect local customs: Make sure to dress modestly when visiting religious places and remove shoes before entering religious places.
  • Try local cuisine: Amritsar is famous for its street food, and you should definitely try some of the local delicacies.
  • Haggle: When shopping in the local markets, don’t be afraid to haggle for a better price.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs: Avoid taking pictures in religious places, smoking or drinking in public places and eating non-veg at vegetarian restaurants.
  • Litter: Keep the city clean and avoid littering in public places.

Safety and Health

Amritsar is generally a safe city, but it’s always advisable to take precautions while traveling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Amritsar

  • Beware of pickpockets: Keep your valuables safe, especially in crowded places like markets, and near bus or train stations.
  • Use reputable transportation: It is better to use radio taxis or Ola/Uber in order to avoid any fraud by the local taxi drivers.

Health in Amritsar

  • Drink bottled water: Avoid drinking tap water and use only bottled water for drinking and brushing teeth.
  • Get vaccinated: Before traveling, make sure you are up-to-date on routine vaccinations, especially if you plan to explore the surrounding areas.

Getting to Amritsar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amritsar is well connected to major cities in India and has several transportation options:

Airports Near Amritsar

  • Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport: Located just 11 km from the city center. It is well connected to major cities in India, and some international destinations like Dubai and Kuala Lumpur.

Bus Stations Near Amritsar

  • Amritsar Bus Stand: This is the main bus station in Amritsar and connects the city with other parts of Punjab and neighboring states.

Other Options

  • Trains: Amritsar Railway Station connects the city with other major cities in India.
  • Taxi Services: Radio taxis, Ola and Uber services are easily available in Amritsar and can be booked through mobile apps.

How to Get Around in Amritsar

The best way to get around in Amritsar is by taxi, auto-rickshaw, or local buses. You can also hire a bicycle or a scooter to explore the city on your own.

Where to Go Next from Amritsar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, here are some destinations near Amritsar that are worth visiting:

  • Chandigarh: Located around 230 kilometers from Amritsar, Chandigarh is a planned city known for its architecture and gardens.
  • Dalhousie: A picturesque hill station, located 190 kilometers from Amritsar, known for its natural beauty.
  • Dharamshala: Located 210 kilometers from Amritsar, is a popular destination in Himachal Pradesh, known for its scenic beauty, trekking, and adventure sports.

Conclusion

Amritsar is a city that will leave an indelible mark on your soul with its rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. Plan your trip today to experience the cultural heritage and traditions of Punjab for yourself.
