Introduction to Patiala

Located in the state of Punjab in India, Patiala is a cultural and historical city that offers a unique experience for visitors. Known for its Patiala Peg, which is a specific type of drink, the city is a hub of music, dance, and other traditional customs that have been passed down from generation to generation. The city is also known for its cultural festivals, cuisine, and notable landmarks that make it an ideal destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Patiala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Qila Mubarak Complex - This ancient fort dates back to 1764 and houses famous landmarks like Sheesh Mahal, Ran Baas, and Darbar Hall that are worth visiting.

  2. Moti Bagh Palace - Built-in the early 20th century, this palace offers an insight into the life of royalty in Patiala.

  3. Baradari Garden - Spread across 10 acres, this garden is home to stunning floral displays, sculptures, and fountains.

  4. Central State Library - This library houses more than 65,000 books on history, culture, and religion making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Patiala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Chhatarpur Temple - Located on the outskirts of Patiala, this temple offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and the city.

  2. Bir Moti Bagh Wildlife Sanctuary - This sanctuary is home to many bird species, making it ideal for birdwatchers.

  3. Baran Sky Garden - This garden provides a serene escape in nature.

  4. Patiala Zoo - Spread over 43 acres, the zoo boasts animals from around the world and is perfect for animal lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Patiala

Patiala offers many different routes for hiking and biking that offer stunning views of the city and surrounding hills. The Baradari Garden and Bir Moti Bagh Sanctuary are also perfect for hiking or enjoying a leisurely stroll.

Best Things to Do in Patiala: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Royal Family’s Museum - This museum offers a glimpse into the life of the royalty of Patiala and includes exhibits on their clothes, jewelry, and weapons.

  2. Attend Patiala Heritage Week - This annual event showcases the city’s most significant cultural, music, and historical events.

  3. Shop at the Old City Market - This market offers the best souvenirs, traditional clothes, fabrics, and handicrafts.

  4. Taste the Patiala Cuisine - From Tandoori dishes, Samosas to traditional Lassi, the cuisine is a must-try part of the city’s culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Patiala

Day 1: Visit Qila Mubarak Complex and explore Baradari Garden

Day 2: Visit Central State Library and Bir Moti Bagh Wildlife Sanctuary

Day 3: Visit Museum and Baradari Garden

Day 4: Attend Patiala Heritage Week

Day 5: Shopping and Taste the Patiala Cuisine

Day 6: Visit Chhatarpur Temple and take a walk at Baran Sky Garden

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Patiala

Must-Try Dishes

Patiala is known for its delicious Tandoori dishes like Tandoori Chicken and Tandoori Fish. Other dishes include Murgh Makhani, Butter Naan, and Chole Bhature.

Local Beverages

The Patiala Peg is a famous drink made with whiskey and is a must-try for visitors. The city also offers Lassi, sugarcane juice, and Masala chai, which are popular drinks amongst locals.

Where to Stay in Patiala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Patiala offers a wide range of accommodations to suit different budgets. Some popular options are Hotel Harpura, Hotel Grand Park, and Punjabi Niwas Hotel.

What to Buy in Patiala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase traditional clothing, handicrafts, and souvenirs from the Old City Market. Traditional clothes like the Patiala Salwar and a Phulkari Dupatta are popular items.

Local Culture and Customs of Patiala

The people of Patiala are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. The city has a rich cultural history that is celebrated through music, dance, and festivals. The traditional rituals of Sikhism are also widely followed in the city.

Best Time to Visit Patiala

The best time to visit Patiala is between October and March, as the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Patiala

Do:

  1. Dress modestly to respect local customs
  2. Remove footwear when entering a temple or shrine
  3. Experience local foods and attend cultural events
  4. Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  1. Smoke or drink in public places
  2. Use disrespectful language or actions towards locals
  3. Engage in any illegal activities
  4. Disrespect religious sites and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Patiala

Patiala is a relatively safe city, but travelers should still exercise caution, especially at night. Visitors should avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables around with them while exploring the city.

Health in Patiala

Visitors should take precautions against drinking tap water and consuming raw fruits and vegetables. Travelers should ensure they are up-to-date with their recommended vaccinations before traveling to India.

Getting to Patiala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Patiala

The nearest major airport to Patiala is the Chandigarh International Airport, located approximately 65 km from the city.

Bus Stations Near Patiala

Patiala is well-connected to major cities in India via bus. Some of the nearest bus stations are Patiala Bus Station and New Bus Stand.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Patiala via train from major cities in India, including Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai.

How to Get Around Patiala

Travel options within Patiala include taxis, auto-rickshaws, and local buses. Visitors can also rent bikes or bicycles to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Patiala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Chandigarh - Located approximately 65 km from Patiala, this city is famed for its architecture, gardens, and museums.
  2. Amritsar - Known for the Golden Temple and Jallianwala Bagh, this city is approximately 220 km from Patiala.
  3. Ludhiana - Famous for the Punjabi cuisine and shopping, this city is approximately 95 km from Patiala.

Conclusion

Patiala is a cultural hub that offers an enthralling experience for all visitors. From delicious Tandoori dishes to stunning natural scenery, the city has a lot to offer travelers. The city’s museums, historical sites, and local customs provide visitors with an insight into Punjabi culture and history. Patiala is the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic travel experience in India.
