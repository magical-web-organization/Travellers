Mathura is a beautiful city, located in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is widely known as the birthplace of Lord Krishna and attracts thousands of devotees every year. The city is an important pilgrimage center, and it offers a lot of cultural, religious, and historical attractions. Whether you are a history buff, a food lover, or an adventure seeker, Mathura has something special in store for you. In this comprehensive travel guide to Mathura, we will take a closer look at the must-visit places, the best things to do, the local culture, and useful travel information.

Introduction to Mathura

Mathura is located in the Doab region of Uttar Pradesh, which is between the rivers Ganges and Yamuna. The city boasts a rich history that dates back to the ancient times, and it has been a center of power for various empires, including the Kushan, Gupta, and Mughal dynasties. Today, the city is home to several impressive temples, historic monuments, and colorful bazaars that offer a glimpse into the vibrant life of the locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Mathura

Mathura has a deep-rooted cultural and religious significance, and it is important to respect the local customs and traditions. Some of the important customs include taking off your shoes before entering a temple, dressing modestly, and abstaining from alcohol and non-vegetarian food.

Best Time to Visit Mathura

The best time to visit Mathura is during the winter months, from November to March. The weather is pleasant, and the city sees a lot of festivals and celebrations during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mathura

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local food and beverages
  • Check out the lively bazaars of Mathura

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes
  • Disrespect religious sites and monuments
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Mathura

Mathura is generally safe for tourists, but it is advised to be cautious in crowded places and to keep an eye on your valuables. Foreigners should also register with the local police station on arrival.

Health in Mathura

Tourists should drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent stomach problems. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for common diseases like typhoid, hepatitis A and B, and tetanus before visiting the city.

Getting to Mathura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mathura

The nearest airport to Mathura is the Indira Gandhi International Airport in New Delhi, which is about 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mathura

Mathura has a well-connected bus network with major cities in Uttar Pradesh and neighboring states.

Other Options

Tourists can also opt for trains or taxis to reach Mathura from Delhi, Agra, or nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Mathura

Tourists can get around Mathura by auto-rickshaws or cycle-rickshaws which are a cheap and convenient mode of transport.

Where to Go Next from Mathura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Agra - famous for the Taj Mahal and other Mughal monuments
  • Fatehpur Sikri - a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Vrindavan - another sacred city regarded as the playground of Lord Krishna

Conclusion

Mathura is a treasure trove of ancient history, rich culture, and religious significance. The city offers something for all kinds of travelers - from temples and museums to outdoor adventures and food trails. To make the most of your Mathura trip, follow this guide, respect the local customs, and indulge in the vibrant flavors of Mathura cuisine.
