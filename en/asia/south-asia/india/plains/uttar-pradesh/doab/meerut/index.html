Introduction to Meerut

Meerut, located in the Doab region of Uttar Pradesh, is a city with a rich history and diverse culture. Known for its industrial significance and numerous historical sites, the city is also surrounded by lush forests and wildlife sanctuaries providing a refreshing break from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adrenaline-seeker, Meerut has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Meerut

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. John’s Church: This historical church is a must-visit for its beautiful architecture and serene atmosphere. Built-in 1819, it is one of the oldest churches in North India.
  • Appu Ghar: A perfect destination for families, Appu Ghar is a water park and amusement park combined offering a plethora of water rides and games to enjoy.
  • Hastinapur Wildlife Sanctuary: Located around 35 km away from Meerut, this wildlife sanctuary is home to various species of animals and birds, including tigers, leopards, hyenas, and peacocks.

Natural Attractions of Meerut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Suraj Kund Park: Located in the heart of the city, Suraj Kund Park is a beautiful park to enjoy an early morning walk or a picnic with family and friends.
  • Eco Park: Located on the outskirts of the city, this park features a beautiful lake, a small zoo, and a children’s park. It’s a perfect spot for a day trip with family.
  • Mansa Devi Temple: Located in Shivalik hills, this temple is known as one of the most revered temples in North India offering a beautiful view of the surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meerut

  • Garh Road to Suraj Kund Park: This route is perfect for those who enjoy a peaceful and easy walk in nature. The route features beautiful views of the city, and the lush greenery throughout the way.
  • Farming Route: The farming route is for those who love biking and exploring nature at a decent pace. Surrounded by fields on both sides, this route can be the best way to appreciate nature.

Best Things to Do in Meerut: Activities and Tours

  • Campfire and BBQ night: Spend your night in the lap of nature by lighting up a campfire and enjoying a delicious BBQ. Choose from numerous camping sites available in the surrounding forests.
  • Boating in Khurja Canal: Enjoy an evening boating ride along the Khurja canal and witness the picturesque sunset.
  • Heritage Walk: Explore the rich historical monuments and buildings of Meerut by taking a guided heritage walk through the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Meerut

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galouti Kebab: A melt-in-mouth kebab made with minced meat flavored with aromatic spices.
  • Tawa Chicken: The delicious Tawa Chicken is a popular dish in Meerut made with succulent pieces of marinated chicken cooked on a hot tawa.
  • Shahi Tukda: Shahi Tukda is a famous dessert made with pieces of bread, milk, sugar, and dry fruits.

Local Beverages

  • Naariyal Paani: Naariyal Paani, or coconut water, is a popular beverage that quenches your thirst on a hot day, and is easily available on the streets.
  • Sattu Drink: Sattu Drink, made from roasted gram flour, is a healthy and refreshing drink that’s unique to North India.
  • Kadak Chai: The fragrant and strong Kadak chai is perfect for those early mornings and refreshing afternoons in Meerut.

Where to Stay in Meerut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Country Inn & Suites: This hotel is known for its luxurious rooms, in-house restaurant and bar, and soothing spa services.
  • OYO Rooms: OYO Rooms offers budget-friendly accommodation with all necessary amenities like 24/7 room service, free Wi-Fi, and breakfast.
  • Bravura Gold Resort: Bravura Gold Resort offers comfortable accommodation with an on-site restaurant, outdoor pool, and fitness center.

What to Buy in Meerut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Brassware: Meerut is known for its brass products, including lamps, vases, and idols of gods and goddesses, making it ideal to grab for souvenirs or to gift someone special.
  • Panja Durries: Panja Durries, also known as hand-woven rugs, are unique to Meerut and are available in various designs, colors, and sizes.
  • Meerut knives: Meerut is known for its exceptional quality knives, available in different sizes, shapes, and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Meerut

Meerut is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. Conducting oneself with respect and politeness towards the locals is highly appreciated. Women should dress modestly, and it is customary to remove shoes before entering a holy place.

Best Time to Visit Meerut

The best time to visit Meerut is from October to March, when the temperatures are moderate, and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meerut

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisines and products.
  • Carry your ID proof while traveling.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes at holy places.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Engage in unlawful activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Meerut

Meerut is generally safe for tourists. However, it is recommended to stay alert and cautious in crowded places and to keep a watchful eye on personal belongings.

Health in Meerut

It is recommended to take the necessary precautions before traveling to Meerut, including taking adequate travel vaccinations, drinking only bottled water, and carrying essential medicines.

Getting to Meerut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Meerut

The nearest airport to Meerut is Indira Gandhi International Airport, located in Delhi, which is around 100 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Meerut

Meerut is well-connected with several neighboring cities via bus services, including Delhi, Haridwar, and Dehradun.

Other Options

One can also take a train from nearby cities like Delhi, Haridwar, and Dehradun to reach Meerut.

How to Get Around in Meerut

The best way to get around Meerut is via auto-rickshaws, local buses, or taxis. However, walking is also a viable option when exploring the city center.

Where to Go Next from Meerut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Delhi: The national capital of India, Delhi, is just 70 km away from Meerut, and with its fascinating history, diverse culture and mouth-watering street food, it makes for an easy day trip.
  • Rishikesh: The holy town of Rishikesh is perfect for experiencing spirituality, yoga, and adventure sports. It is located around 130 km away from Meerut.
  • Agra: Known for the magnificent Taj Mahal, Agra is located around 200 km away from Meerut and can be a great weekend getaway for history and architecture lovers.

Conclusion

Meerut is a city full of contrasts; history and nature, industry and wilderness. This travel guide covers everything you need to know to plan a trip to the city, including top attractions, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, where to eat, local customs, and more, making your visit to Meerut a memorable one.
