Introduction to Sarsai Nawar

Sarsai Nawar is a small town located in the Doab region of Uttar Pradesh, India. The town is situated on the banks of the Ganga Yamuna River and is surrounded by lush green fields. The town has a rich history and is believed to have been founded by the legendary king Yayati. Today, Sarsai Nawar is known for its scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and spiritual significance.

List of Places to Visit in Sarsai Nawar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Yayati Fort - Yayati Fort is one of the most important historical places in Sarsai Nawar. The fort was built by King Yayati and is believed to be over 5,000 years old. It was once a major center of power and influence in the region.

  2. Yogi Mahal - Yogi Mahal is another significant historical place in Sarsai Nawar. The palace was built by a local king in the 19th century and served as his residence.

  3. Sarsai Nawar Museum - Sarsai Nawar Museum is a small museum that showcases the cultural heritage of Sarsai Nawar. The museum has a collection of artifacts and exhibits that depict the history and culture of the town.

Natural Attractions of Sarsai Nawar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Seohara Lake - Seohara Lake is a scenic lake located just outside Sarsai Nawar. The lake is surrounded by lush green forests and is a popular spot for boating and picnicking.

  2. Chhota Haridwar - Chhota Haridwar is a holy place located on the banks of the Ganga Yamuna River. The place has significant spiritual importance and is visited by devotees from all over India.

  3. Pilibhit Tiger Reserve - Pilibhit Tiger Reserve is a wildlife sanctuary located near Sarsai Nawar. The sanctuary is home to several endangered species of animals and is a popular place for wildlife enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarsai Nawar

  1. Bariya Forest Reserve - Bariya Forest Reserve is a dense forest located near Sarsai Nawar. The forest is home to several species of plants and animals and is a popular spot for hiking and biking.

  2. Seohara Hills - Seohara Hills are a range of hills located near Sarsai Nawar. The hills offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape and are a popular spot for hiking and trekking.

Best Things to Do in Sarsai Nawar: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat Ride on the Ganga Yamuna River - A boat ride on the Ganga Yamuna River is a must-do activity when visiting Sarsai Nawar. The boat ride offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is a great way to relax and unwind.

  2. Visit the Seohara Temple - The Seohara Temple is a beautiful temple located on the banks of the Ganga Yamuna River. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a popular place of worship for locals and visitors alike.

Food and Drink Scene of Sarsai Nawar

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kachori - Kachori is a popular street food in Sarsai Nawar. It is a deep-fried pastry filled with spiced lentils or potatoes.

  2. Lassi - Lassi is a refreshing drink made with yogurt and flavored with spices and sugar.

Local Beverages

  1. Sugarcane Juice - Sugarcane juice is a popular local beverage in Sarsai Nawar. It is a refreshing drink made by pressing sugarcane.

  2. Thandai - Thandai is a traditional milk-based drink flavored with saffron, almonds, and other spices. It is popular during festivals and special occasions.

Where to Stay in Sarsai Nawar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Sarsai Nawar Palace - The Sarsai Nawar Palace is a heritage hotel located in the heart of Sarsai Nawar. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, excellent dining options, and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. The Seohara Hotel - The Seohara Hotel is a budget hotel located near the Seohara Temple. The hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Sarsai Nawar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts - Sarsai Nawar is known for its traditional handicrafts such as pottery, weaving, and embroidery. These make great souvenirs to bring home.

  2. Local Spices - Sarsai Nawar is known for its spices, particularly its special blend of garam masala. These can make great gifts for food lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarsai Nawar

Sarsai Nawar has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in its history and geography. The town is known for its religious fervor and devotion to Lord Shiva. The people of Sarsai Nawar are warm and friendly, and visitors are welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Sarsai Nawar

The best time to visit Sarsai Nawar is during the winter months, from October to February. The weather during these months is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarsai Nawar

Sarsai Nawar is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Sarsai Nawar

Visitors to Sarsai Nawar should take precautions against water-borne diseases by drinking bottled water and avoiding street food.

Getting to Sarsai Nawar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sarsai Nawar

The nearest international airport to Sarsai Nawar is in Delhi, which is approximately 250 km away. From Delhi, visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach Sarsai Nawar.

Bus Stations Near Sarsai Nawar

Sarsai Nawar is well-connected to other cities in Uttar Pradesh by bus. The main bus station in Sarsai Nawar is located in the town center.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Sarsai Nawar by train. The nearest railway station is in Moradabad, which is approximately 75 km away.

How to Get Around in Sarsai Nawar

The best way to get around Sarsai Nawar is by hiring a taxi or auto-rickshaw. Bicycles are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Sarsai Nawar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Sarsai Nawar is a hidden gem, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. With so much to do and see, Sarsai Nawar is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Uttar Pradesh.
