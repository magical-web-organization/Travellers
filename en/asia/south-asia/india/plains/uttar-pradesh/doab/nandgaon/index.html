Introduction to Nandgaon

Nandgaon is a small town located in the Doab region of Uttar Pradesh. This town is known for its unique blend of rural and urban lifestyles. Nandgaon is a great place to visit for those who want to experience traditional Indian culture and get away from the hustle and bustle of city life. Due to its location, Nandgaon is easily accessible from major cities and attractions in North India.

List of Places to Visit in Nandgaon

If you are planning a trip to Nandgaon, here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Radha Rani Temple: This is a must-visit temple dedicated to Radha, the Hindu goddess of love.
  • Madan Mohan Temple: This ancient temple is believed to have been built around 400 years ago and is dedicated to Lord Krishna.
  • Nand Bhavan: This palace was once a residence of the Nand rulers and is now a popular tourist attraction.
  • Sonkh: This town is home to many historical monuments, including Brijraj Swamiji Temple, which is believed to have been built during the Gupta period.

Natural Attractions of Nandgaon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Govardhan Hill: This is a famous place for devotees of Lord Krishna, due to its significance in Hindu mythology as Lord Krishna lifted this hill on his little finger.
  • Kusum Sarovar: This is a beautiful lake surrounded by a large garden and features a wide range of flora and fauna.
  • Bhramsarover: This lake is believed to be the place where Lord Brahma performed a sacrifice.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nandgaon

Nandgaon is surrounded by lush green forests, making it an ideal place for hiking and biking. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Govardhan Hill Parikrama: This is a pilgrimage route which is approximately 21km long and takes around 6-7 hours to complete.
  • Vrindavan Parikrama: This is a circular route that covers several temples in the Vrindavan area.
  • Barsana-Krishna Janmabhoomi-Kusum Sarovar: This route covers several important historical and cultural attractions in the region.

Best Things to Do in Nandgaon: Activities and Tours

  • Attend the Holi Festival: This colorful festival takes place in March and is best experienced in Nandgaon and Barsana.
  • Visit nearby villages and markets: This is a great way to experience rural Indian life and shop for local handicrafts and souvenirs.
  • Take a boat ride on Kusum Sarovar: This lake is a great place to relax and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • Attend a local cultural performance: Nandgaon is known for its traditional music and dance performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nandgaon

Day 1: Arrive in Nandgaon Day 2: Visit Radha Rani Temple and Madan Mohan Temple Day 3: Visit Bhramsarover and Kusum Sarovar Day 4: Explore the nearby town of Barsana Day 5: Go on a hiking/biking tour Day 6: Take a boat ride on Kusum Sarovar and attend a cultural performance in the evening. Day 7: Departure from Nandgaon

Food and Drink Scene of Nandgaon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peda: This sweet is made from milk, sugar and khoya, and is a popular dessert in this region.
  • Kachodi: This spicy snack is made out of puri stuffed with boiled potatoes and chickpeas, and is a must-try for foodies.
  • Mathura Peda: This sweet, made from khoya and sugar, is a popular delicacy in Nandgaon and Mathura.
  • Chhach or Buttermilk: This drink is refreshing and widely available across the region.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: This yogurt-based drink is a popular choice in Nandgaon and is often served with spices and fruits.
  • Sugarcane juice: This drink is made from fresh sugarcane and is a great way to beat the heat.

Where to Stay in Nandgaon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nandgaon has a wide range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some recommended options:

  • Hotel Basera Brij Bhoomi: This hotel has comfortable rooms with AC and free Wi-Fi.
  • Nidhivan Sarovar Portico: This hotel offers a luxury stay experience and has a beautiful swimming pool.
  • Radha Resorts: This hotel offers the opportunity to stay in beautiful huts with traditional decor.

What to Buy in Nandgaon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Various handicrafts made from wood carvings, stone carvings, and textiles can be found in the local markets.
  • Pichwai Paintings: These are intricate paintings of Lord Krishna, usually painted on cloth or paper.
  • Incense Sticks and Perfumes: The region is famous for its herbal perfumes and incense sticks.

Local Culture and Customs of Nandgaon

Nandgaon is known for its traditional Indian culture and customs. It is important to respect the local customs and dress modestly and appropriately. Visitors should also remove their shoes before entering a temple.

Best Time to Visit Nandgaon

The best time to visit Nandgaon is during the winter months (November to February) when the weather is pleasant and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nandgaon

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Carry a hat and sunscreen
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water

Don’t:

  • Wear short dresses/skirts or revealing clothing
  • Take pictures inside temples without asking for permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Nandgaon

Nandgaon is a safe place for tourists. However, it is important to keep an eye on your belongings and not leave them unattended in public places.

Health in Nandgaon

It is important to carry mosquito repellent creams and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria.

Getting to Nandgaon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nandgaon

The nearest airport to Nandgaon is the Agra Airport, which is approximately 77km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or hire a private car to reach Nandgaon.

Bus Stations Near Nandgaon

Nandgaon is well-connected by the bus network to various cities in Uttar Pradesh and Delhi.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to travel to Nandgaon by train, with Mathura Junction being the nearest railway station.

How to Get Around in Nandgaon

Visitors can hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw to get around in Nandgaon. Local buses are also available but might not be very reliable.

Where to Go Next from Nandgaon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mathura: The birthplace of Lord Krishna should not be missed, as there are popular temples and museums to explore.
  • Agra: The world-famous Taj Mahal is located in Agra, which is approximately two hours from Nandgaon.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historical city is approximately 90km from Nandgaon and worth a visit.

Conclusion

Nandgaon offers a unique blend of traditional Indian culture and modern living. With its diverse attractions and natural beauty, Nandgaon is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to northern India. Use this guide to plan your itinerary and make the most of your visit to this charming town.
