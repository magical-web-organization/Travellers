Introduction to Sarnath

Sarnath, located in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is a destination of great historical and cultural significance. It is believed to be the place where Gautama Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment, making it a revered pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide.

Apart from its religious importance, Sarnath is also home to several well-preserved remnants of its ancient past. From exquisite temples and monasteries to museums and archaeological sites, the city has a lot to offer visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Sarnath

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chaukhandi Stupa

Believed to have been built to commemorate the meeting of Buddha and his first disciples, Chaukhandi Stupa is a beautiful structure located in Sarnath. It is made of brick and stone and is adorned with beautiful carvings.

Archaeological Museum

The Archaeological Museum in Sarnath houses some of the most prized and ancient Buddhist artifacts in the world. The museum is home to several priceless items such as Buddha’s begging bowl, the Lion Capital of Ashoka, and the Buddha’s tooth relic.

Dhamek Stupa

Dhamek Stupa is one of the most impressive structures in Sarnath. It is believed to be the place where Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. The stupa stands tall at a height of 143 feet and is decorated with intricate carvings and inscriptions.

Natural Attractions of Sarnath: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Deer Park

Located opposite the Archaeological Museum, Deer Park is a serene space with well-manicured lawns and seemingly endless stretches of greenery. The park is home to several deer, making it a great spot for wildlife enthusiasts.

Ramnagar Fort

The Ramnagar Fort is a stunning structure located on the banks of the River Ganges. The fort is built in the typical Mughal style and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarnath

Sarnath offers several options for those looking to explore the city on foot or by bike. Some popular routes include the Archaeological Site Route, the Deer Park Route, and the Varanasi Route.

Best Things to Do in Sarnath: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the cultural and historical attractions, there are several other activities and tours you can participate in while in Sarnath. Some popular choices include attending the traditional Aarti ceremony by the River Ganges, taking a boat ride along the river, and exploring the local markets and souvenir shops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sarnath

Day 1: Visit the Archaeological Museum and the nearby Chaukhandi Stupa.

Day 2: Explore the Deer Park and the Sarnath Archaeological Site.

Day 3: Head to the nearby city of Varanasi and take a boat ride along the River Ganges.

Day 4: Visit the Ramnagar Fort and the nearby Sarnath Temple.

Day 5: Attend the traditional Aarti ceremony by the River Ganges in the evening.

Day 6: Take a bicycle tour of the nearby villages and countryside.

Day 7: Spend the day exploring the local markets and trying out the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Sarnath

Must-Try Dishes

Some popular dishes to try while in Sarnath include Kachori Sabzi, Golgappa, and Tamatar Chaat. For those with a sweet tooth, Ras Malai and Gulab Jamun are highly recommended.

Local Beverages

A popular local drink in Sarnath is the refreshing Thandai, which is made with a blend of spices, dry fruits, and milk. Another great choice is the Lassi, a yogurt-based drink that can be enjoyed in several flavors.

Where to Stay in Sarnath: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sarnath has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular choices include Hotel Buddha, Hotel Abhinandan Grand, and Madin Hotel.

What to Buy in Sarnath: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs to bring home from Sarnath include Buddhist prayer flags, handcrafted wooden artifacts, and traditional Indian clothing. Local markets and roadside stalls are great places to find these items.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarnath

Sarnath is a place steeped in history and culture, and visitors are expected to respect local customs and values. Dress modestly while visiting temples and religious sites, remove shoes before entering, and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Sarnath

The best time to visit Sarnath is from November to February when the weather is pleasant and sunny. The monsoon season, which runs from June to September, can lead to heavy rains, making the city difficult to navigate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sarnath

Do:

  • Remove shoes before entering religious sites.
  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and values.

Don’t:

  • Get involved in any illegal activities.
  • Cause harm or cruelty to animals.
  • Disrespect local customs and values.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarnath

Sarnath is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables, and be wary of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Sarnath

Tap water in Sarnath is not safe for consumption, so visitors should consume only bottled water or water that has been boiled and filtered. Visitors should also ensure they have the necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Sarnath: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sarnath

The nearest airport to Sarnath is the Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport in Varanasi, which is well-connected to major cities across India.

Bus Stations Near Sarnath

Sarnath is well-connected to nearby cities and towns by road, and there are several bus stations located in the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Sarnath by train, with the nearest railway station located in Varanasi.

How to Get Around in Sarnath

Sarnath is a compact city, and most attractions can be easily reached on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and autorickshaws are also available for hire, and buses are a great option for those looking to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Sarnath: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Sarnath, visitors can head to the nearby city of Varanasi, known for its mesmerizing Ghats and ancient temples. Other popular destinations include Allahabad and Lucknow, both of which offer a glimpse into the rich culture and history of Uttar Pradesh.

Conclusion

Sarnath is a fascinating destination that offers something for everyone. From its cultural and historical attractions to its vibrant markets and outdoor adventures, it is a destination that is sure to leave visitors with lasting memories. Plan your trip to Sarnath today and immerse yourself in the richness and diversity of Indian culture.
