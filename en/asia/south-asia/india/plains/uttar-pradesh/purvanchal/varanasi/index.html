Introduction to Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is one of the oldest cities in the world, with a history that dates back over 3,000 years. The city is considered to be the spiritual capital of India, and is known for its ghats, temples, and cultural richness. If you’re planning to visit Varanasi, be prepared to get swept away by its enchanting beauty and charm.

List of Places to Visit in Varanasi

Varanasi has a plethora of attractions that cater to different interests. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Varanasi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sarnath

Sarnath is a famous Buddhist pilgrimage site located only 10 km from Varanasi. This place is where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. Sarnath is home to a number of Buddhist temples, monasteries, and stupas. The famous Ashoka Pillar and the Lion Capital of Ashoka are also located here.

Ramnagar Fort

Ramnagar Fort is a historical fort located on the banks of the Ganges river. It was built in the 18th century by the Kashi Naresh Raja Balwant Singh. The fort houses a museum that showcases the royal collection, including vintage cars, weapons, paintings, and manuscripts.

Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum

Bharat Kala Bhavan is a museum located inside the premises of the Banaras Hindu University. It has an impressive collection of Indian paintings, sculptures, and textiles, dating back to the 1st century BC.

Natural Attractions of Varanasi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

River Ganges

The Ganges river is a holy river in India, and Varanasi is one of the cities where it flows. The city is famous for its ghats, which are steps leading to the river. These ghats are used for bathing, prayers, and cremations. You can take a boat ride on the river to witness the mesmerizing view of the ghats.

Chunar Fort

Chunar Fort is a historic fort located near Varanasi, on the banks of the Ganges river. It is a popular spot for rock climbing and trekking, offering a panoramic view of the river and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varanasi

Varanasi has several hiking and biking routes that offer a glimpse of local life and scenic views. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Varanasi to Sarnath: This is a 10 km biking route that takes you to Sarnath, a famous Buddhist pilgrimage site.
  • Assi Ghat to Raj Ghat: This is a 7 km hiking route along the Ganges river, offering mesmerizing views of the ghats and temples.

Best Things to Do in Varanasi: Activities and Tours

There are several things to do in Varanasi, ranging from spiritual and cultural activities to adventure sports. Here are some popular options:

  • Take a sunrise or sunset boat ride on the Ganges river.
  • Attend the Ganga Aarti ceremony, which takes place every evening at the Dashashwamedh Ghat.
  • Take a guided walking tour of the Old City, and discover the hidden alleys and street food.
  • Attend a classical music or dance performance at one of the local temples.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varanasi

If you’re planning to spend a week in Varanasi, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Varanasi and rest
2 Explore the Old City and take a walking tour
3 Visit the temples and ghats along the Ganges river
4 Take a day trip to Sarnath and Chunar Fort
5 Attend the Ganga Aarti ceremony in the evening
6 Take a boat ride on the Ganges river and witness the sunrise
7 Shop for souvenirs at the local markets

Food and Drink Scene of Varanasi

Varanasi is famous for its street food and sweets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chaat: A savory snack made of fried dough, chickpeas, and spices.
  • Lassi: A yoghurt-based drink, with flavors like mango, rose, and saffron.
  • Malaiyo: A winter dessert made of milk froth, garnished with saffron and nuts.
  • Kachori: A deep-fried pastry, filled with spiced lentils or potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A cold drink made of milk, nuts, and spices.
  • Paan: A betel leaf with various fillings, such as nuts, spices, and rose petals.

Where to Stay in Varanasi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Varanasi offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Taj Nadesar Palace: A luxury heritage hotel, located in the heart of the city.
  • BrijRama Palace: A 200-year-old palace, converted into a boutique hotel.
  • Shiva Ganges View: A budget-friendly guesthouse, located near the Assi Ghat.

What to Buy in Varanasi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Varanasi is known for its silk sarees, handicrafts, and souvenirs. Here are some popular shopping destinations:

  • Vishwanath Gali: A narrow alley lined with shops selling silk sarees, jewelry, and handicrafts.
  • Chowk: A local market, selling brassware, wooden toys, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Varanasi

Varanasi is a cultural hub, with a rich history and traditions. Here are some customs and behavior norms to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially while visiting temples and other religious places.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple.
  • Do not touch or step on offerings or prayer materials.
  • Respect local customs, such as the Ganga Aarti ceremony and cremation rituals.

Best Time to Visit Varanasi

The best time to visit Varanasi is from November to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The summers can be scorching, and the monsoons can be disruptive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varanasi

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Varanasi:

Do:

  • Take part in the Ganga Aarti ceremony.
  • Attend cultural performances and festivals.
  • Visit Sarnath and other nearby attractions.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of cremation rituals or mourners.
  • Smoke or drink in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially while visiting religious places.

Safety and Health

Visitors should take some precautions to ensure they have a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in Varanasi

  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.
  • Use only authorized taxis and auto-rickshaws.
  • Keep your valuables and documents safe.

Health in Varanasi

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Be cautious while eating street food, and choose reliable stalls.
  • Wear insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Varanasi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Varanasi is well-connected by air, road, and rail. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Varanasi

Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 22 km from the city center. It is connected to major cities in India, such as Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Varanasi

Varanasi is well-connected to neighboring cities and states by bus. The city has three main bus stations: Varanasi Junction, Varanasi Bus Stand, and Kashi Bus Depot.

Other Options

Varanasi is also well-connected by train, with several trains running to major cities in India. Private taxis and auto-rickshaws are also available for local travel.

How to Get Around in Varanasi

The best way to get around in Varanasi is on foot or by bicycle rickshaw. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are also available, but negotiate the fare before starting the journey.

Where to Go Next from Varanasi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some popular destinations to visit from Varanasi:

  • Allahabad: Another holy city, located about 120 km from Varanasi, known for the Kumbh Mela festival.
  • Chitrakoot: A spiritual town, located about 150 km from Varanasi, known for its temples and scenic views.
  • Lucknow: The capital city of Uttar Pradesh, located about 320 km from Varanasi, known for its cultural richness and cuisine.

Conclusion

Varanasi is a city like no other, with a unique blend of spirituality, culture, and history. Plan your visit well, and you’ll be rewarded with unforgettable experiences, ranging from boat rides on the Ganges to temple visits and street food adventures. Follow the safety and health tips, and respect the local customs, to make the most of your trip to Varanasi.
