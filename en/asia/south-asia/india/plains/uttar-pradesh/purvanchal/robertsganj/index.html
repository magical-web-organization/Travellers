Introduction to Robertsganj

Robertsganj is a small town located in the district of Sonbhadra in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. Nestled in the lap of nature, this quaint town is a perfect destination for anyone seeking tranquillity and an escape from the hustle-bustle of city life. While Robertsganj is still considered to be off the beaten path, it is slowly gaining popularity amongst travellers looking for an authentic Indian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Robertsganj

There’s plenty to explore in Robertsganj, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places in Robertsganj:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chandraprabha Wildlife Sanctuary: Witness the beauty of wildlife at the Chandraprabha Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to several species of animals such as tigers, leopards, and elephants. Apart from the wildlife, the sanctuary also offers a picturesque view of waterfalls and streams.

  • Sita Kunda: This ancient pool is believed to be the spot where Sita, the wife of Lord Rama, took a bath during their exile from Ayodhya. The serene surroundings and the mythological significance make Sita Kunda a must-visit site in Robertsganj.

  • Vindhya Vasini Devi Temple: A popular pilgrimage site, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Vindhya Vasini. The temple is surrounded by hills, and the scenic view adds to the spiritual experience.

Natural Attractions of Robertsganj: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kaimoor Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in the Kaimoor hills, the sanctuary is home to several species of wildlife, including tigers, leopards, wild boars, and antelopes. Visitors can enjoy nature walks, birdwatching, and trekking in the sanctuary.

  • Shakteshgarh Fort: This 16th-century fort offers stunning views of the Ganges river and the surrounding valleys. The fort is situated on a hilltop, and visitors need to climb several stairs to reach the top. However, the effort is worth it as one can enjoy a panoramic view of the entire town from the top.

  • Ganges River: Robertsganj is situated on the banks of the Ganges river, and a boat ride on the river is a must-do activity. The serene surroundings and the cool breeze make the experience all the more memorable.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Robertsganj

Robertsganj is surrounded by hills, and there are many hiking and biking routes that travellers can explore. The Vindhya range, Kaimoor hills, and Sonebhadra hills offer stunning views and challenging hikes for adventure seekers.

Best Things to Do in Robertsganj: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the sights and sounds of Robertsganj, there are several activities and tours that one can opt for:

  • River Rafting: The Ganges river offers an excellent opportunity for river rafting. The rapids are mild to moderate, making it a perfect activity for beginners.

  • Paragliding: Soar high into the sky and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the picturesque town of Robertsganj. Several tour operators offer paragliding packages in Robertsganj.

  • Cycling Tours: Explore the countryside on a bicycle and experience the local way of life. Several cycling tours are available in Robertsganj, offering travellers an authentic Indian experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Robertsganj

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for travellers visiting Robertsganj:

Day 1: Visit the Chandraprabha Wildlife Sanctuary Day 2: Explore the Vindhya Vasini Devi Temple and Sita Kunda Day 3: Trekking in the Kaimoor hills Day 4: River rafting on the Ganges river Day 5: Visit the Shakteshgarh Fort and enjoy paragliding Day 6: Cycling tour of the countryside Day 7: Explore the local markets and souvenirs shops

Food and Drink Scene of Robertsganj

Experience the unique flavors of Robertsganj with the local cuisine and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Litti Chokha: A popular dish in the region, Litti Chokha is made from roasted wheat balls stuffed with sattu and served with mashed potatoes and chili pickle.

  • Peda: A sweet made from condensed milk, Peda, is popular in northern India and is a must-try when in Robertsganj.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A refreshing drink made from milk, nuts, and spices. Thandai is a popular drink served during festivals and is a must-try when in Robertsganj.

  • Mahua: A local liquor made from Mahua flowers, Mahua is a popular drink amongst locals and is worth a try for travellers looking to experience the local culture.

Where to Stay in Robertsganj: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Robertsganj, catering to all budgets:

  • Budget Hotels: Hotel Anil Palace, Hotel Alok, Hotel Aman Palace

  • Mid-Range Hotels: Hotel Kuber Palace, Hotel Padmini Palace, Hotel Raj Palace

  • Luxury Hotels: Chingari Hotel and Resort, Hotel Royal Palace

What to Buy in Robertsganj: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Robertsganj with you with these must-buy souvenirs:

  • Handloom Sarees: The town is famous for its handloom sarees, which are made from locally sourced cotton and silk.

  • Wooden Handicrafts: Robertsganj is known for its wooden handicrafts, which include toys, decorative pieces, and furniture.

  • Handmade Soaps and Oils: Handmade soaps and oils are popular in the region and make a great gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Robertsganj

The town of Robertsganj is steeped in history and has a rich cultural heritage. Visitors are expected to dress modestly while visiting temples and other religious places. It is also customary to remove footwear before entering a temple.

Best Time to Visit Robertsganj

The best time to visit Robertsganj is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and the town is not too crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Robertsganj

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and other religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn a few Hindi phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Click pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Robertsganj

Robertsganj is a relatively safe town. However, travellers are advised to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding deserted areas at night, not carrying valuables, and using licensed taxis or autos.

Health in Robertsganj

Travellers are advised to carry a basic first-aid kit and medication for common ailments. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Robertsganj: Transportation Options and Logistics

Robertsganj can be reached by air, rail, or road.

Airports Near Robertsganj

The nearest airport is the Varanasi airport, which is about 150 km away from Robertsganj.

Bus Stations Near Robertsganj

Robertsganj is well-connected by buses, and several buses operate from major cities in Uttar Pradesh.

Other Options

Travellers can also hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Robertsganj.

How to Get Around in Robertsganj

The best way to explore the town is on foot. However, auto-rickshaws and taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Robertsganj: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Varanasi: A holy city and one of the oldest cities in India, Varanasi is located about 150 km away from Robertsganj.

  • Allahabad: An important pilgrimage site for Hindus, Allahabad is located about 150 km away from Robertsganj.

  • Chunar Fort: Located about 70 km away from Robertsganj, Chunar Fort is a popular tourist attraction known for its history and architecture.

Conclusion

Robertsganj is a perfect destination for those looking to escape the chaos of city life and immerse themselves in nature and spirituality. With its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and adventure activities, Robertsganj is a must-visit destination in Uttar Pradesh.
