Introduction to Saharanpur

Located in Uttar Pradesh, Saharanpur is a city in northern India known for its rich history and cultural heritage. Situated at the foothills of the Shivalik range, this city is known for its myriad of temples, forts and parks, making it a popular tourist destination. The city has something to offer to everyone, be it nature lovers, history enthusiasts, adventure seekers or foodies. Plan a trip to Saharanpur and indulge in the beauty of the city with our comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Saharanpur

Saharanpur is home to several cultural and natural attractions. Here are a few must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saharanpur Museum: Known for its rich collection of antiques, paintings, and sculptures, the museum provides a glimpse into the rich cultural history of Saharanpur.
  • Shakti Peeth Shakumbhari Devi temple: One of the revered Hindu temples in Saharanpur, this place is a must-visit for spiritual seekers.
  • Ancient Sarswati Temple: This ancient temple dedicated to the goddess Sarswati is located on the banks of the river Yamuna and is a testament to the rich Hindu heritage of Saharanpur.
  • Gaumukh Temple: Built in the 19th-century, this temple is located on a hilltop and offers a scenic view of the city.

Natural Attractions of Saharanpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khatling Glacier: Tucked away in the Garhwal Himalayas, this glacier is an ideal spot for trekking and offers awe-inspiring views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Deoband: A small town located near Saharanpur, Deoband is known for its tranquil surroundings and offers a perfect break from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Rajaji National Park: Home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, Rajaji National Park is an ideal spot for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saharanpur

Saharanpur offers several scenic routes for hiking and biking. Here are a few popular routes that you can explore:

  • Tons Valley Trek: This trek starts from Naugaon and takes you through dense forests and breathtaking valleys.
  • Upper Yamuna River Trek: Starting from Hanuman Chatti, this trek takes you through picturesque villages and offers splendid views of the Yamuna River.
  • Chakrata Trek: This trek is known for its verdant landscape and stunning views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Saharanpur: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the cultural and natural attractions of Saharanpur, there are several other things that you can do, such as:

  • Indulge in a culinary tour and savor the local cuisine, such as chaat, paranthas and kebabs.
  • Take a relaxing stroll in the lush green parks and gardens.
  • Visit the famous jaggery market and see how jaggery is made.
  • Attend the local fairs and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saharanpur

If you are planning a week-long trip to Saharanpur, here is an itinerary that you can follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Saharanpur

Saharanpur is known for its spicy and flavorful cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages that you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chaat: A popular street food made from fried dough, potatoes and spices.
  • Parantha: Thin, crispy, and buttery flatbread with a variety of fillings like potatoes, onion, and cottage cheese.
  • Kebabs: Soft and juicy meat or vegetable patties flavored with spices and herbs.
  • Jalebi: A popular dessert made from deep-fried batter dipped in syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Sugarcane juice: A refreshing drink made from sugarcane.
  • Lassi: A drink made from yogurt and flavored with spices and fruits.

Where to Stay in Saharanpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saharanpur offers a wide range of options for accommodations. Depending on your budget and preferences, you can choose from hotels, hostels or guesthouses. Here are a few popular options:

  • Hotel Castle: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Hotel Amber: A mid-range hotel located in a peaceful area.
  • Hotel Ashirwad: A budget hotel located near the railway station.

What to Buy in Saharanpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saharanpur is known for its woodwork and furniture, brassware and ornate carvings. Here are a few souvenirs that you can bring home:

  • Wooden toys: Famous for their craftsmanship and intricate designs, wooden toys make for an ideal souvenir.
  • Brassware and carvings: The brassware and carvings of Saharanpur are famous for their beauty and durability.
  • Handmade paper products: Made from recycled materials, these paper products are eco-friendly and perfect for gifting.

Local Culture and Customs of Saharanpur

Saharanpur is a melting pot of cultures and customs. Here are a few things that you should keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples and places of worship.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Saharanpur

The best time to visit Saharanpur is between October and February when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. Summers can be scorching, and monsoons can be unpredictable, so it is best to avoid these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saharanpur

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and savor the flavors.
  • Explore the cultural and natural attractions of Saharanpur.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter the streets or harm the local wildlife.
  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious places.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

While Saharanpur is considered a safe place, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions when traveling. Here are a few safety and health tips that you should keep in mind:

Safety in Saharanpur

  • Be vigilant and avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables.
  • Hire authorized guides and taxis.
  • Keep important documents like passports and tickets in a safe place.

Health in Saharanpur

  • Carry insect repellents to protect yourself from mosquitoes.
  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Seek medical attention if you feel unwell or have any health issues.

Getting to Saharanpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saharanpur

The nearest airport to Saharanpur is the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is approximately 142 km away. From here, you can take a train or bus to Saharanpur.

Bus Stations Near Saharanpur

Saharanpur is well-connected by road to major cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and Dehradun. The city has a well-maintained bus station, and you can take a bus to reach here.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Saharanpur, which is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Chennai.

How to Get Around in Saharanpur

Saharanpur has excellent transportation options like buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws. You can find several car rentals in the city, and you can take them to explore the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Saharanpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore the nearby destinations, here are a few options:

  • Dehradun: About 85 km away, Dehradun is a popular spot among tourists and is known for its beautiful scenery and pleasant weather.
  • Haridwar: Located about 55 km away from Saharanpur, Haridwar is a holy city known for its rich religious and cultural heritage.
  • Rishikesh: About 78 km away, Rishikesh is known as the ‘Yoga Capital of the World’ and is famous for its beautiful temples and mesmerizing river rafting experiences.

Conclusion

Saharanpur is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural surroundings. The city has something to offer to everyone, and you can indulge in a plethora of activities, from exploring the cultural attractions to indulging in outdoor adventures. Plan a trip to Saharanpur and experience the beauty and charm of this city.
