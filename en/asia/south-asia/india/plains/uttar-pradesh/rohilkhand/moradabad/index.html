Moradabad is a city located in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is situated at a distance of around 160 km from the national capital, New Delhi. Moradabad is famous for its brass handicrafts, which have a global reputation. Besides, it offers a range of attractions and activities, catering to various interests of tourists. This article is a comprehensive guide for those planning a trip to Moradabad. From places to visit to things to do, from accommodations to local customs, this guide covers it all.

Introduction to Moradabad

Moradabad is located in the Rohilkhand region of the state of Uttar Pradesh. It is situated on the banks of the Ramganga River, at an elevation of 198 meters above sea level. According to the 2011 census, the population of Moradabad is around 950,000 people.

List of Places to Visit in Moradabad

Moradabad offers a range of attractions and activities for tourists. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Moradabad:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moradabad Museum: It is one of the best museums in the region, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the area.
  • Sita Temple: It is a historical temple dedicated to Goddess Sita, located in the heart of the city.
  • Pataleshwar Temple: It is a cave temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, situated on the banks of the Ramganga River.

Natural Attractions of Moradabad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jim Corbett National Park: It is a famous wildlife sanctuary located at a distance of around 90 km from Moradabad, offering an exciting safari experience.
  • Bhimnath Falls: It is a picturesque waterfall located in the nearby town of Kaladhungi, offering a refreshing escape from the city’s hustle-bustle.
  • Tanda Falls: It is a scenic waterfall located at a distance of around 30 km from Moradabad, offering a mesmerizing view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moradabad

Moradabad is surrounded by hills, and it offers some of the best hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes in Moradabad:

  • Ramganga River Trail: This is a popular biking trail along the banks of the Ramganga River, offering scenic views of the countryside.
  • Moradabad to Nainital: This is a popular hiking trail covering a distance of around 75 km, offering panoramic views of the hills and valleys.
  • Bhimnath Trail: This is a popular hiking trail leading to the Bhimnath Falls, offering a refreshing experience in the lap of nature.

Best Things to Do in Moradabad: Activities and Tours

Moradabad offers a range of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some popular things to do in Moradabad:

  • Brass handicrafts tour: Moradabad is famous for its brass handicrafts, and a tour of the local workshops is a must-do activity.
  • Heritage walk: A guided heritage walk in the city showcases its rich cultural heritage.
  • Ramganga River rafting: Adventurous tourists can enjoy river rafting in the Ramganga River.

Food and Drink Scene of Moradabad

Moradabad has a rich and diverse food scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebabs: Moradabad is famous for its kebabs, which are made with mouth-watering spices and meat.
  • Biryani: This rice dish is a staple of Indian cuisine, and Moradabad offers a unique version that should not be missed.
  • Jalebi: A sweet dessert made with deep-fried batter and dipped in a sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt drink that is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Masala chai: A popular tea beverage infused with aromatic spices.

Where to Stay in Moradabad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moradabad offers a range of accommodations, catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • Hotel Mansarover Paradise: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of the city.
  • Hotel Samrat: This budget-friendly hotel offers basic amenities and is located close to the railway station.
  • The Palms Resort: This resort is situated amidst the lush greenery and offers luxury amenities.

What to Buy in Moradabad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moradabad is famous for its brass handicrafts. Tourists can buy a range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Brass ware: From figurines to utensils, brass ware is the most popular souvenir in Moradabad.
  • Jewelry: Moradabad offers a range of traditional jewelry made with brass and other metals.
  • Wooden toys: These are a popular souvenir for kids and are made with local wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Moradabad

Moradabad has a rich cultural heritage, and tourists can witness cultural festivals and traditions in the city. Travelers should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Moradabad

The best time to visit Moradabad is from October to March. The weather during this time is pleasant, offering a comfortable experience for tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moradabad

Here are some do’s and don’ts for tourists visiting Moradabad:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious places.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Photograph strangers without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moradabad

Moradabad is a safe city for tourists. However, travelers should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and avoiding late-night walks in secluded areas.

Health in Moradabad

Tourists should take some necessary health precautions when visiting Moradabad, such as drinking bottled water and carrying medication for common ailments.

Getting to Moradabad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moradabad is well-connected to major cities in India. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Moradabad

The nearest airport to Moradabad is Indira Gandhi International Airport, New Delhi, which is situated around 160 km from the city.

Bus Stations Near Moradabad

Moradabad has a bus station, from where tourists can take buses to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Tourists can also reach Moradabad via trains, cabs, and private vehicles.

How to Get Around in Moradabad

Moradabad offers several transportation options, including buses, cabs, and auto-rickshaws. Tourists can also rent a bike or a car to explore the city at their own pace.
