Introduction to Pilibhit

Pilibhit is a small district in Rohilkhand, situated in the northern part of the state of Uttar Pradesh in India. Known for its lush green forests, Pilibhit boasts of a rich flora and fauna diversity, and is regarded as one of the top wildlife destinations in the country.

Apart from its natural beauty, Pilibhit is steeped in history and culture, with several ancient temples and monuments dotting the landscape. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your ultimate trip to Pilibhit.

List of Places to Visit in Pilibhit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary - Covering an area of 400 sq km, this sanctuary is home to several rare and endangered species, such as tigers, leopards, rhinoceros, and elephants, among others. The sanctuary also has a museum that showcases the rich biodiversity of the region.
  • Gauri Shankar Temple - Built in the 18th century, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is renowned for its intricate carvings and architectural beauty.
  • Purnagiri Temple - Located on a hilltop, this temple is believed to be one of the 108 ancient shaktipeeths in India. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is regarded as a highly sacred place by the locals.
  • Sandalwood Forest - Located near the town of Puranpur, the Sandalwood Forest is known for its aromatic trees and is a popular picnic spot for locals.

Natural Attractions of Pilibhit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khaliya Top Trek - This 8 km trek from Munsiyari to Khaliya Top offers stunning views of the Panchchuli Range of the Himalayas and is a must-do for adventure seekers.
  • Jama Masjid - One of the country’s largest and oldest mosques, the Jama Masjid in Pilibhit is known for its imposing architecture and serene atmosphere.
  • Chuka Beach - Located near the Nepal border, this secluded beach on the banks of the Sharda River is a hidden gem and a great place to unwind.
  • Gomat Taal - This tranquil lake in Pilibhit is surrounded by lush green forests and is home to several species of fishes and migratory birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pilibhit

  • Pilibhit Forest Hike - A 12 km hike through the Pilibhit Forest Reserve, this trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and wildlife.
  • Sharda River Trek - This 5 km trek along the banks of the Sharda River is ideal for those looking to explore the natural beauty of Pilibhit.
  • Cycling Tour of Pilibhit - A 25 km cycling tour of Pilibhit, exploring its hidden corners and scenic spots, is a great way to experience the local culture and way of life.

Best Things to Do in Pilibhit: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari - Take a wildlife safari through the Pilibhit Forest Reserve to spot tigers, leopards, deer, and other native species.
  • Birdwatching - Pilibhit is a haven for bird watchers, so don’t forget to carry your binoculars and camera to spot resident and migratory birds.
  • Village Tour - Take a village tour of Pilibhit to experience the local culture, traditions, and cuisine.
  • River Rafting - Indulge in some thrilling river rafting on the Sharda River, which offers rapids ranging from Grade III to IV.

Food and Drink Scene of Pilibhit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bedmi Aloo - A popular breakfast dish in Pilibhit, this is a type of deep-fried bread served with potato curry.
  • Tunde Kebab - A famous non-vegetarian dish, Tunde Kebabs are made from minced meat blended with a mix of spices and cooked on a skewer.
  • Malai Lassi - A refreshing drink made from yoghurt, sugar, cream, and saffron, Malai Lassi is a perfect accompaniment to spicy Indian meals.

Local Beverages

  • Chai - The quintessential Indian tea, chai is a must-try for anyone visiting India, and Pilibhit is no exception.
  • Thandai - A sweet, mango-flavored milk drink, Thandai is a popular summer cooler in Pilibhit.

Where to Stay in Pilibhit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Woods Resort - Located near the Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary, The Woods Resort offers luxurious tents and cottages, surrounded by lush green forests.
  • Purvanchal Tourist Guest House - Situated in the heart of Pilibhit, this guest house offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and great accessibility to top tourist spots.
  • Kamla Nehru Hotels and Hostels - A budget-friendly option in Pilibhit, Kamla Nehru Hotels and Hostels offer clean rooms, basic amenities, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Pilibhit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sandalwood Products - Pilibhit is known for its Sandalwood Forest, and visitors can purchase items like incense sticks, soaps, and perfumes made from sandalwood.
  • Wooden Handicrafts - The local artisans of Pilibhit create beautiful and intricate wooden handicrafts, including toys, utensils, and showpieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Pilibhit

Pilibhit has a rich cultural heritage with a mix of Hindu and Muslim customs. The people of Pilibhit celebrate various festivals, such as Holi, Diwali, and Eid, with great fervor and enthusiasm.

While visiting temples and mosques, it is advisable to dress modestly and remove shoes before entering. When interacting with locals, it’s important to be respectful and polite, and seek permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Pilibhit

The best time to visit Pilibhit is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and cool. Summers can be harsh, with temperatures soaring above 40°C, while monsoons can bring heavy rainfall and make travel difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pilibhit

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and mosques.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry mosquito repellants, hats, and sunscreen when visiting wildlife parks and forests.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public places.
  • Litter in public areas or wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Carry plastic bags and other non-biodegradable items.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pilibhit

Pilibhit is generally a safe destination, but it’s advisable to take necessary precautions while visiting wildlife reserves and forests. Visitors should follow safety guidelines while trekking, hiking, or camping in remote areas.

Health in Pilibhit

Visitors should be cautious while drinking tap water or eating street food in Pilibhit as it may cause stomach upset. It’s advisable to carry bottled water and eat at hygienic restaurants.

Getting to Pilibhit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pilibhit

The nearest international airport to Pilibhit is the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is around 260 km away. From Delhi, one can take a taxi or a bus to reach Pilibhit.

Bus Stations Near Pilibhit

Pilibhit has a well-connected bus stand, with regular buses plying to and from major cities like Delhi, Lucknow, and Bareilly.

Other Options

One can also take a train to Pilibhit railway station, which is connected to all major cities in the country.

How to Get Around in Pilibhit

Local transportation options in Pilibhit include auto-rickshaws, cycle-rickshaws, and taxis. Visitors can also rent a bike or a car to explore the city and its nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Pilibhit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nainital - A picturesque hill station located around 160 km from Pilibhit, Nainital is known for its beautiful lakes and scenic landscapes.
  • Lucknow - The capital city of Uttar Pradesh, Lucknow is known for its rich history, culture, and culinary delights.
  • Jim Corbett National Park - Located around 140 km from Pilibhit, the Jim Corbett National Park is one of India’s oldest and most renowned wildlife destinations.

Conclusion

Pilibhit is a hidden gem, with a unique combination of natural beauty, wildlife, and ancient history. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Pilibhit has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of this enchanting destination.
