Introduction to Faizabad

Faizabad, also known as Ayodhya, is a city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is situated on the banks of the River Sarayu and is located approximately 130 km from Lucknow. Faizabad has a rich cultural history, and it is considered to be one of the holiest cities for Hindus as it is the birthplace of Lord Ram. The city is also famous for its Mughal architecture and monuments.

List of Places to Visit in Faizabad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ram Janmabhoomi Temple - This temple is situated at the birthplace of Lord Rama, and it is one of the most revered temples in India.
  • Hanuman Garhi - Hanuman Garhi is a temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, and it is situated near the banks of the River Sarayu.
  • Faizabad Museum - The Faizabad Museum houses a collection of Mughal artifacts, including paintings, statues, and manuscripts.
  • Gulab Bari - Gulab Bari is a garden and mausoleum of Nawab Shuja-ud-Daula, a former ruler of Faizabad.

Natural Attractions of Faizabad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bahu Begum Ka Maqbara - Bahu Begum Ka Maqbara is a tomb built by the Nawab of Faizabad in memory of his wife.
  • Ramkatha Park - Ramkatha Park is situated near Ram Janmabhoomi Temple and is a popular picnic spot for locals.
  • Jhokha Park - Jhokha Park is a beautiful garden situated near the banks of the River Sarayu.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Faizabad

  • Lucknow-Faizabad Cycle Trail - This cycling trail is 130 km long and starts from Lucknow, passing through several villages and towns before ending at Faizabad.
  • Kanak Bhawan Marg - This is a popular route for hiking and biking, and it passes through several temples and historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Faizabad: Activities and Tours

  • Heritage Walk - Take a guided tour through the heritage sites of Faizabad to get a glimpse of its rich cultural history.
  • River Rafting - Take a thrilling ride on the River Sarayu and experience the beautiful scenery of Faizabad.
  • Boat Ride - Take a boat ride on the River Sarayu at sunset to witness the beautiful view of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Faizabad

Must-Try Dishes

  • Litti Chokha - It is a popular snack in Faizabad made from roasted gram flour stuffed with sattu and served with chokha.
  • Galauti Kebab - These soft and melt-in-your-mouth kebabs are made from minced meat and a blend of spices.
  • Samosa - Faizabad is famous for its crispy and spicy samosas, which are a popular snack in the city.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi - A refreshing drink made from yogurt, water, and sugar or salt.
  • Tea - Tea is a popular beverage in Faizabad, and you can find several tea stalls and cafes in the city.

Where to Stay in Faizabad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Krishna Palace - This hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located near the city center.
  • Hotel Shivam Inn - This hotel is located near the railway station and offers affordable accommodation in Faizabad.
  • OYO 61314 Shakti Resort - This resort offers comfortable rooms and is situated in a peaceful area in Faizabad.

What to Buy in Faizabad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ayodhya Paintings - These paintings are made using natural colors and depict scenes from Hindu mythology.
  • Chikankari Embroidery - Chikankari is a popular embroidery style in Faizabad, and you can find several shops selling embroidered fabrics and clothes.
  • Zardozi Work - Zardozi work is a type of embroidery using metallic threads, and it is a famous craft of Faizabad.

Local Culture and Customs of Faizabad

Faizabad is a culturally rich city, and it is home to people of different religions and traditions. The people of Faizabad are known for their hospitality, and visitors are always welcomed warmly. However, it is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Faizabad

The best time to visit Faizabad is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and the temperature is mild. The summer months from April to June can be extremely hot and humid, and it is not recommended to travel during this time. The monsoon season lasts from July to September and brings heavy rainfall, which can cause inconvenience for travelers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Faizabad

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially in religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear too revealing clothes.
  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Faizabad

Faizabad is generally a safe city, but it is recommended to take precautions while traveling. It is advised to avoid traveling alone at night and to be aware of your surroundings. Keep your valuables safe and be cautious of pickpockets and scams.

Health in Faizabad

It is recommended to drink boiled or bottled water to avoid water-borne diseases. Always carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medication with you. It is advisable to wear a mask in crowded areas to prevent the spread of diseases.

Getting to Faizabad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Faizabad

The nearest airport to Faizabad is the Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport in Lucknow, which is approximately 130 km away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a bus to reach Faizabad.

Bus Stations Near Faizabad

Faizabad has a well-connected bus network, and you can take a bus from the nearby cities of Lucknow, Varanasi, or Ayodhya to reach Faizabad.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Faizabad, as it is well-connected to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Faizabad

The best way to get around Faizabad is by auto-rickshaws or cycle-rickshaws. You can also rent a car or a bike to explore the city on your own.

Where to Go Next from Faizabad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ayodhya - Ayodhya is a nearby city and is considered to be one of the holiest cities for Hindus.
  • Lucknow - Lucknow, the capital of Uttar Pradesh, is known for its rich cultural heritage and is famous for its food and architecture.
  • Varanasi - Varanasi, the spiritual capital of India, is known for its ghats, temples, and vibrant culture.
