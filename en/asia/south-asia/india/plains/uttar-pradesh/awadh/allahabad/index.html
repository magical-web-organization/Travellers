Introduction to Allahabad

Located in the Awadh region of Uttar Pradesh, Allahabad is situated on the confluence of three rivers - Ganges, Yamuna and Saraswati. The city is also known as Prayagraj, a name given by the Uttar Pradesh government in 2018. Allahabad is an important pilgrimage center for Hindus, who believe that taking a dip in the confluence of these rivers can purge one’s soul of all sins. The city is also known for its historical significance, with several monuments dating back to the Mughal Era.

List of Places to Visit in Allahabad

If you’re planning a trip to Allahabad, here are some of the top attractions you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Allahabad Fort

Built during the reign of Akbar, the Allahabad Fort stands majestically on the banks of the Yamuna river. It was also used as a military base during the British rule in India. The fort is home to several historical sites like the Ashoka Pillar, the Patalpuri Temple, and the Akshaya Vat.

Anand Bhavan

Anand Bhavan, the ancestral home of the Nehru family, is now a museum that houses memorabilia related to the Nehru-Gandhi family. The museum also exhibits pictures, letters, and personal belongings of Mahatma Gandhi and other freedom fighters.

Natural Attractions of Allahabad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Triveni Sangam

One of the main attractions in Allahabad, Triveni Sangam is the confluence of three rivers - Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati. The Sangam is a holy site for Hindus, who believe that taking a dip in its waters can purify one’s soul.

Alfred Park

A perfect place for picnics or a leisurely stroll, Alfred Park is one of the largest parks in Allahabad. It was built to commemorate Prince Alfred’s visit to India in 1870.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Allahabad

Allahabad has plenty of hiking and biking trails for adventure enthusiasts. Some popular routes are:

  • Allahabad-Kaudiyala-Badrinath
  • Allahabad-Banka-Bhagalpur
  • Allahabad-Lanka-Varanasi

Best Things to Do in Allahabad: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some exciting activities and tours to make your trip to Allahabad more memorable, here are a few options:

  • Take a boat ride on the Ganges to catch a glimpse of the numerous ghats and temples on its banks.
  • Visit the Khusro Bagh, a garden surrounded by tombs, mosques and an ancient well.
  • Take a heritage walk to explore the old city and its numerous heritage buildings.

Food and Drink Scene of Allahabad

No travel guide is complete without a guide to local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Allahabad:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kachori Sabzi: a popular street food made with deep-fried dumplings filled with a spicy potato filling and served with chutneys.
  • Prayagraj ka Paan: a betel leaf preparation that is a specialty of the region.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: a refreshing drink made with milk, nuts, and spices, usually served during festivals like Holi and Shivratri.
  • Lassi: a yogurt-based drink that is a popular summer drink in Uttar Pradesh.

Local Culture and Customs of Allahabad

Allahabad has a rich cultural and religious heritage. Some customs and cultural practices that are followed in the city include:

  • Taking a dip in the Triveni Sangam to purify one’s soul
  • The Kumbh Mela, held once every 12 years, attracts millions of pilgrims from across India and the world
  • The city is also known for its traditional music and dance forms like Kathak and Bhojpuri songs.

Best Time to Visit Allahabad

The best time to visit Allahabad is during the winter months from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Allahabad

Do:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting religious sites
  • Respect local customs and religious practices

Don’t:

  • Drink or bathe in the river during monsoon season
  • Litter or create noise pollution at public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Allahabad

Allahabad is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always better to be cautious while traveling alone or at night.

Health in Allahabad

Allahabad has several hospitals and medical facilities in case of emergencies. It’s advisable to carry adequate medication and consult a doctor in case of any health concerns.

Getting to Allahabad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Allahabad

The nearest airport to Allahabad is the Allahabad Airport, also known as Bamrauli Airport. It’s well-connected to major cities in India like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Allahabad

Allahabad has a well-connected bus service to other major cities in the region. The main bus station is the Allahabad Bus Station.

Other Options

Allahabad is also well-connected by train to major cities in India like Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai.

How to Get Around in Allahabad

Allahabad has a well-connected public transport system that includes buses and auto-rickshaws. Car rentals and taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Allahabad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can consider:

  • Varanasi, the spiritual capital of India
  • Chitrakoot, a popular pilgrimage site in Uttar Pradesh
  • Lucknow, the capital city of Uttar Pradesh

Conclusion

Allahabad is a city that’s steeped in culture, history, and religious heritage. It’s a must-visit destination if you’re looking to experience the beauty of a traditional Indian city with a modern touch. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore the hidden gems of Allahabad!
