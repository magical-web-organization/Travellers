Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Unnao

Unnao, a city nestled in the Awadh region in Uttar Pradesh, India, is known for its rich history, culture, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re up for a cultural trip or an adventure in the great outdoors, Unnao won’t disappoint. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to make the most of your visit to this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Unnao

Here are some must-visit attractions you need to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhitargaon Temple: This 5th-century AD temple is considered as one of the oldest surviving Hindu temples in North India.
  • Nawabganj Bird Sanctuary: Home to over 250 bird species, the Nawabganj Bird Sanctuary is a must-visit for birdwatchers and nature lovers.
  • Jama Masjid: One of the largest mosques in Uttar Pradesh built in the early 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Unnao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Unnao Lake: Popularly known as Gangagur, Unnao Lake is a great spot for boating, fishing, and picnicking.
  • Mud Fort: Surrounded by mud walls, the Mud Fort is a scenic spot ideal for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Unnao

Unnao is a great place for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Mud Fort Trek: This adventure trail takes you through scenic forests and leads you to the Mud Fort.
  • Kanpur to Unnao Bike Ride: A 40km route that takes you through lush green fields and rustic villages.

Best Things to Do in Unnao: Activities and Tours

  • Take a heritage walk: Explore the city’s rich cultural heritage by taking a guided heritage walk.
  • Visit a pottery village: Witness the art of pottery-making by visiting one of the pottery villages in the outskirts of Unnao.

Week-Long Itinerary for Unnao

Here’s a sample 7-day itinerary to help you plan your trip to Unnao:

Day 1 Explore the Mud Fort, admire the scenic views
Day 2 Visit the Bhitargaon Temple, Jama Masjid, and Swaminarayan Temple

Food and Drink Scene of Unnao

Food in Unnao is heavily influenced by Awadhi cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tundey Kebab: This popular Awadhi dish is made with finely minced meat and aromatic spices.
  • Nihari: A slow-cooked meat stew, traditionally eaten for breakfast.
  • Biryani: The Awadhi version of this rice dish is known for its unique flavors and cooking technique.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A sweet and creamy milk-based drink flavored with a blend of nuts and spices, often served during festivals.
  • Sharbat: A popular refreshing drink made with fruit extracts, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Unnao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Unnao has plenty of options for travelers of all budgets. Here are some options:

  • Hotel Central - A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Riviera Resort - A luxurious resort with a swimming pool and landscaped gardens.
  • Heritage Home Stay - A traditional Indian guesthouse with a warm and welcoming host.

What to Buy in Unnao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Unnao is known for its pottery-making and leather crafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to add to your shopping list:

  • Pottery: Unnao has several pottery villages where you can buy handmade clay pots, vases, and other earthenware.
  • Leather Products: The city is also known for its leather products like shoes, bags, and jackets.

Local Culture and Customs of Unnao

Unnao has a rich cultural heritage, and locals are proud of their traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Respect local customs: Unnao is a conservative city, and it’s essential to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Language: Hindi is the primary language spoken in Unnao, but English is widely understood.

Best Time to Visit Unnao

The best time to visit Unnao is between October and March when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Unnao

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Unnao:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Follow local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the city’s cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter and pollute the environment.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Unnao

Unnao is a relatively safe city, but it’s always essential to take necessary precautions when traveling.

  • Stay alert and avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Use official transportation services and licensed guides.

Health in Unnao

Here are some health tips to keep in mind during your visit to Unnao:

  • Stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Carry mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Seek medical attention immediately in case of any illness or emergency.

Getting to Unnao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Unnao

The nearest airport to Unnao is the Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport in Lucknow, which is about 40km away.

Bus Stations Near Unnao

Unnao has a well-connected bus station that operates buses to and from nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

You can also reach Unnao by train. It has its own railway station and is well-connected to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Unnao

Unnao has several transportation options to help you get around the city, including taxis, auto-rickshaws, and buses.

Where to Go Next from Unnao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Unnao is a great base to explore nearby destinations like Kanpur, Lucknow, and Ayodhya.

Conclusion

Unnao is a hidden gem in the Awadh region, offering a unique mix of rich history, culture, and stunning natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to explore and experience the best of Unnao. Happy travels!
