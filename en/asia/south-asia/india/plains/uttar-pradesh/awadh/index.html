Introduction to Awadh

Awadh, a region situated in the Northern part of India, is known for its rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and stunning landscape. The region comprises of 12 districts of Uttar Pradesh, including Lucknow, Faizabad, and Pratapgarh, known for their historical importance and cultural significance.

Awadh was ruled by several powerful dynasties, including the Mughals, and the Nawabs of Awadh, who left behind a legacy of grandeur and richness in the region’s architecture, art, and cuisine.

The region has something to offer for everyone, whether it’s exploring the historical monuments and museums, indulging in outdoor activities like hiking and camping, or trying the mouth-watering Awadhi cuisine.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we have put together everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Awadh.

List of Places to Visit in Awadh

There are several culturally significant and naturally beautiful places to visit in Awadh. Here are some of the must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bara Imambara, Lucknow
  • Chota Imambara, Lucknow
  • Rumi Darwaza, Lucknow
  • British Residency, Lucknow
  • Bahu Begum ka Maqbara, Faizabad

Natural Attractions of Awadh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dudhwa National Park, Lakhimpur Kheri
  • Nawabganj Bird Sanctuary, Unnao
  • Ayodhya Hills, Faizabad
  • Moti Jheel, Pratapgarh

Hiking/Biking Routes of Awadh

Awadh is a great destination for adventure enthusiasts, offering several hiking and biking routes that take you through beautiful landscapes and pristine surroundings. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Trails in Dudhwa National Park
  • Hikes to the Ayodhya Hills
  • Cycling routes through the rural villages of Awadh

Best Things to Do in Awadh: Activities and Tours

Awadh offers several exciting activities and tours that visitors can participate in. Here are some of the best things to do in Awadh:

  • Heritage walks in Lucknow
  • Food tours of Awadhi cuisine
  • Wildlife safaris in Dudhwa National Park
  • Hot air balloon rides over the city of Lucknow

Week-Long Itinerary for Awadh

A week in Awadh is sufficient time to explore the region’s beautiful sites and indulge in its delicious food. Here’s a perfect itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Lucknow and go sightseeing around the city center
Day 2 Visit the Bara Imambara and Chota Imambara, followed by a food tour of Awadhi cuisine
Day 3 Visit the British Residency and other notable landmarks in Lucknow
Day 4 Go on a safari through Dudhwa National Park and explore its wildlife
Day 5 Drive to Faizabad and visit the historical sites there
Day 6 Explore Ayodhya Hills and enjoy its scenic beauty and hiking trails
Day 7 Final day of the trip, indulge in some last-minute shopping and depart from Lucknow

What to Buy in Awadh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Awadh is known for its zardozi embroidery, chikan work, and traditional sweets. Here are some of the best souvenirs you can bring back:

  • Zardozi embroidery
  • Chikan work clothes and accessories
  • Handcrafted leather goods
  • Traditional sweets - Petha, Balushahi, and Ladoo

Local Culture and Customs of Awadh

Awadh has a rich and diverse cultural landscape, with several festivals and customs celebrated throughout the year. Visitors should respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Awadh

The best time to visit Awadh is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and cooler temperatures make outdoor activities like hiking and safari more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Awadh

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Litter the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Awadh

Awadh is a relatively safe region, but travelers should take necessary precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Awadh

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent any health issues. Visitors should also ensure they have the necessary vaccinations required for traveling to India.

Getting to Awadh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Awadh

  • Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport, Lucknow
  • Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport, Varanasi

Bus Stations Near Awadh

  • Charbagh Bus Station, Lucknow
  • Faizabad City Bus Station, Faizabad

Other Options

  • Taxis and car hire services are available

How to Get Around in Awadh

Visitors can travel around Awadh using various modes of transportation like taxis, auto-rickshaws, and buses. Visitors can also rent a car or motorbike if they prefer to explore the region’s rural landscapes at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Awadh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations like Varanasi or Agra, home to the iconic Taj Mahal. They can also explore other parts of Uttar Pradesh, like Allahabad and the holy city of Prayagraj.

Conclusion

Awadh is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a rich cultural experience coupled with stunning natural beauty and delicious cuisine. By following this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their perfect trip to Awadh and make memories that will last a lifetime.
