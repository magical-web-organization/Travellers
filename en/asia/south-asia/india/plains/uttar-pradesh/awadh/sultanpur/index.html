Introduction to Sultanpur

Sultanpur is a district located in the Awadh region of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is known for its rich cultural and historical heritage, as well as its natural beauty. Sultanpur is a popular destination for travelers who want to explore the region’s outdoor adventures, enjoy the local cuisine, and witness the customs and culture of the local people.

List of Places to Visit in Sultanpur

Sultanpur is home to a variety of attractions that cater to all types of travelers, from those interested in history and culture to those seeking outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Sultanpur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khwaja Khizar Tomb: This tomb is considered to be one of the oldest monuments in Sultanpur and is a representation of the city’s rich history.
  • Kudawala Mosque: This is one of the oldest mosques in Sultanpur, built during the 14th century. It is a popular destination for travelers interested in Islamic architecture.
  • Sri Krishna Janmasthan: This temple is one of the most significant religious sites for Hindus in the region. It is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Krishna.

Natural Attractions of Sultanpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary: This bird sanctuary is a paradise for birdwatchers and nature lovers. It is home to a variety of birds, including migratory birds that fly here during the winter months.
  • Kachari Tirth: This is a popular destination for those seeking adventure. It is a series of caves that can be explored by travelers.
  • Brahmavart Ghat: This is a scenic spot on the banks of the river Gomti. It is a popular destination for picnics and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sultanpur

Sultanpur is a great destination for those seeking outdoor adventures, such as hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Sultanpur:

  • Sultanpur to Kachari Tirth: This trek takes you to the Kachari Tirth caves and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Sultanpur to Malihabad: This biking route takes you through the countryside, where you can witness rural life in India.
  • Sultanpur to Brahmi Tirth: This is a popular trek that takes you to the top of a hill where you can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sultanpur: Activities and Tours

Aside from exploring attractions and hiking/biking routes, there are many other things to do in Sultanpur:

  • Take a cooking class: Learn how to cook local dishes and bring home recipes to impress your friends and family.
  • Visit local markets: Explore the local markets and pick up souvenirs to bring back home with you.
  • Take a heritage walk: Explore the city on foot and learn about the history and culture of Sultanpur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sultanpur

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Sultanpur, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Sultanpur

Sultanpur is known for its delicious cuisine, which is a blend of Awadhi, Mughlai, and Hyderabadi influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: A fragrant rice dish that is seasoned with spices and often includes meat, such as chicken or lamb.
  • Galouti Kebab: A tender, melt-in-your-mouth kebab that is made with minced meat and spices.
  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is often flavored with fruit or spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sharbat: A sweet, refreshing drink that is made with fruit syrups.
  • Kanji: A fermented drink that is made with carrots and spices.
  • Kahwa: A fragrant tea that is made with saffron and other spices.

Where to Stay in Sultanpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Gomti: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city, offering clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Hotel Park View: A mid-range hotel that is located near top attractions and offers spacious rooms with modern amenities.
  • Kamla Guest House: A guest house located near the bird sanctuary that offers a tranquil setting and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Sultanpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sultanpur is known for its handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry. Here are some top picks:

  • Chikankari Embroidery: A style of embroidery that is unique to the region and is often used to embellish fabrics such as saris and kurtas.
  • Zardozi: A form of metal embroidery that is often used to embellish fabrics such as wedding dresses and sherwanis.
  • Silver Jewelry: The region is known for its intricate silver jewelry, which often includes traditional designs such as paisley and floral motifs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sultanpur

The local people of Sultanpur are friendly and hospitable. It is important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and to remove your shoes before entering temples and mosques. Tipping is not expected but is appreciated for good service.

Best Time to Visit Sultanpur

The best time to visit Sultanpur is from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant. This is also the time when migratory birds visit the bird sanctuary. March to June can be quite hot, while July to October is the monsoon season and can be quite rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sultanpur

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Sultanpur:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without asking for permission.
  • Worry about haggling over prices at markets.
  • Drink tap water - always opt for bottled water.

Safety and Health

When visiting Sultanpur, it is important to take standard precautions such as keeping your valuables locked up and being aware of your surroundings. As for health, it is advisable to carry a first aid kit and to avoid street food that may not be prepared hygienically.

Safety in Sultanpur

Sultanpur is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. It is advisable to avoid walking alone late at night or in secluded areas.

Health in Sultanpur

It is important to take standard health precautions such as washing your hands frequently and carrying a first aid kit with you. Tap water is not safe to drink, so it is advisable to always drink bottled water.

Getting to Sultanpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sultanpur is well-connected to other cities in Uttar Pradesh and India. Here are the main transportation options:

Airports Near Sultanpur

  • Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport (Lucknow): About 135 km from Sultanpur.

Bus Stations Near Sultanpur

  • Sultanpur Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Sultanpur that connects the city to other destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Sultanpur Railway Station is located in the heart of the city and is well-connected to other major cities in India.
  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available in Sultanpur for those who prefer to explore the city at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Sultanpur

Sultanpur is a small city, so it is easy to get around on foot, especially when exploring the city center. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Sultanpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sultanpur is located in a region that is full of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Lucknow: The capital of Uttar Pradesh, Lucknow is known for its rich history and culture, as well as its delicious cuisine.
  • Ayodhya: A sacred city for Hindus, Ayodhya is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama and is home to many temples and shrines.
  • Varanasi: One of the oldest cities in the world, Varanasi is known for its spirituality and religious significance. It is located about 300 km from Sultanpur.

Conclusion

Sultanpur is a gem in the Awadh region of Uttar Pradesh. With its rich history, cultural heritage, natural beauty, and hospitality, Sultanpur is a great destination for travelers who want to experience the best of India. Whether you’re interested in hiking, birdwatching, or exploring historical sites, Sultanpur has something for everyone.
