Introduction to Dudhwa National Park

Nestled in the heart of the Terai region of Uttar Pradesh, Dudhwa National Park is a paradise for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers. Spread over an area of 490 sq. km, the national park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including the rare and endangered Bengal tiger, the one-horned rhinoceros, and the swamp deer. Along with the wildlife, Dudhwa National Park is also famous for its sal forests, grasslands, water bodies, and rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Dudhwa National Park

Dudhwa National Park is a culturally rich destination, and visitors must embrace local customs and traditions while visiting. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or religious place
  • Do not take photographs of local people without their permission
  • Respect the wildlife and do not pollute the environment

Best Time to Visit Dudhwa National Park

The best time to visit Dudhwa National Park is from November to May, when the weather is pleasant, and the wildlife sightings are the most common.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dudhwa National Park

Do:

  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen
  • Follow park rules
  • Hire local guides for safer jungle safaris

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment
  • Feed the animals
  • Use flash while taking wildlife pictures

Safety and Health

Safety in Dudhwa National Park

  • Follow the instructions of guides and park authorities
  • Avoid trekking alone
  • Do not venture into restricted areas

Health in Dudhwa National Park

  • Carry mosquito repellent and keep yourself covered
  • Drink only bottled water
  • Carry a first aid kit
