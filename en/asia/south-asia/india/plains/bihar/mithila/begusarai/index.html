A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Begusarai, Bihar

Located in the Mithila region of Bihar, Begusarai is a city rich in culture and history. Known for its numerous temples, museums, and historical sites, it’s an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore India’s rich and diverse heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve curated a list of the best places to visit, local cuisine, cultural customs, and much more to help you make the most of your trip to Begusarai.

List of Places to Visit in Begusarai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Chaiti Devi Temple - One of the most famous temples in Begusarai, Chaiti Devi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Chaiti Devi and is believed to be hundreds of years old. Visitors can witness the awe-inspiring architecture of the temple and experience the peaceful atmosphere.

  2. Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary - The largest freshwater oxbow lake in Asia, Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary attracts bird watchers from all over the world. The sanctuary boasts over 106 bird species, including migratory birds such as the Brahminy Duck, Cotton Teal, and Pintail.

  3. Begusarai Fort - A historical fort dating back to the 18th century, Begusarai Fort is an excellent destination for history buffs. The fort displays a fascinating blend of Mughal and Rajput architecture and is open to the public for exploration.

Natural Attractions of Begusarai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dandari Reserved Forest - A vast expanse of greenery and wildlife, Dandari Reserved Forest is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Visitors can explore the forest by hiking or biking, and spot a variety of flora and fauna on the way.

  2. Gorakhnath Temple - Located on the banks of the Ganges River, Gorakhnath Temple is a serene spot for those looking for a peaceful retreat. The temple’s architecture is inspired by Buddhist and Hindu styles, making it a fascinating visual experience.

  3. Simaria Ghat - Regarded as one of the most scenic spots in Bihar, Simaria Ghat is a popular destination for watching stunning sunrises and sunsets. Visitors can also take a dip in the Ganges river, which is considered holy by Hindus.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Begusarai

Begusarai offers many scenic routes for hiking and biking, which allow visitors to explore the city’s natural beauty and historical sites. Some popular routes include:

  1. Dandari Reserved Forest Hiking Trail - A six-kilometer trail that passes through the Dandari Reserved Forest, this route is ideal for hiking enthusiasts looking for an adventure.

  2. Begusarai to Kanwar Lake Biking Route - This biking route stretches over 20 kilometers, and takes visitors on a scenic journey through the city’s urban areas and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Begusarai: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a Cultural Walking Tour - Walking tours of Begusarai are available, which allow visitors to witness the city’s rich heritage and cultural diversity. Tourists can visit the city’s famous temples, historical monuments, and interact with the locals, learning about the city’s customs and traditions.

  2. Attend Local Festivals - Begusarai hosts many festivals throughout the year celebrating its diverse culture and customs. Some of the popular festivals include Chhath Puja, Holi, and Diwali.

Week-Long Itinerary for Begusarai

Day 1: Arrive in Begusarai and visit the Chaiti Devi Temple, explore the markets and enjoy local cuisine.

Day 2: Go on a cultural walking tour of the city visiting the Begusarai Fort, Simaria Ghat, and Gorakhnath Temple.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary, spotting migratory birds and enjoying scenic views.

Day 4: Visit the Bihar Museum to learn about the state’s rich cultural heritage.

Day 5: Explore the Dandari Reserved Forest by taking a hiking or biking trail.

Day 6: Attend a local festival and witness Begusarai’s vibrant, colorful culture.

Day 7: Depart from Begusarai after a memorable week of exploration.

Food and Drink Scene of Begusarai

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Litti Chokha - A popular street food in Bihar, Litti Chokha is made from roasted whole wheat dough, which is then filled with spicy sattu and served with a side of mashed brinjal and potato.

  2. Bihari Thali - A traditional North-Indian meal that usually consists of rice, dal, vegetables, and roti.

Local Beverages

  1. Sattu Sherbet - A refreshing drink made from sattu, roasted gram flour, mixed with water and spices.

  2. Thandai - A traditional Indian drink made by mixtures of almonds, rose petals, and fennel with milk

Where to Stay in Begusarai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Begusarai offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from budget guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some popular choices include:

  1. Hotel Rajhans - A budget-friendly hotel that provides comfortable rooms, free parking and free Wi-Fi.

  2. Hotel Pearl - A mid-range hotel located in the city center, providing guests with various amenities such as a restaurant, fitness center, and free Wi-Fi.

  3. Ramniwas Lodge - A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Begusarai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Madhubani Paintings - Begusarai is renowned for its vibrant Madhubani paintings, a traditional art form that depicts mythological and cultural themes. Visitors can buy these ethereal artworks as souvenirs to take back home.

  2. Khadi Garments - Begusarai is famous for its hand-woven cotton Khadi garments, available in different styles and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Begusarai

Begusarai has a diverse culture that is deeply rooted in traditions and customs. Here are a few customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of:

  1. Dress Code - It is advisable to dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.

  2. Respect for Elders - The locals regard their elders with reverence, and it is essential to show them respect and courtesy.

Best Time to Visit Begusarai

The best time to visit Begusarai is from October to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The temperature remains between 15-25 degrees Celsius, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Begusarai

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Try the local cuisine and explore the city’s vibrant culture.

Don’t:

  1. Photograph people without their permission.

  2. Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Begusarai

Begusarai is generally a safe city to visit, but like any other tourist destination, visitors should be cautious of pickpockets. It is advisable to keep valuable items locked away in a secure place, such as your hotel room safe.

Health in Begusarai

Visitors should take precautions while drinking tap water and eating street food. Drinking bottled water and eating food from reputable restaurants and cafes is recommended.

Getting to Begusarai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Begusarai

The nearest airport to Begusarai is the Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna, which is around 110 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Begusarai

Begusarai has a well-connected bus network, with frequent buses from nearby cities such as Patna, Kolkata, and Ranchi.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Begusarai, with the nearest railway station at Barauni Junction, which is around seven kilometers from the city center.

How to Get Around in Begusarai

Begusarai has a well-connected public transport system, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also hire a private car or bike to explore the city at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Begusarai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Nalanda - A historical city located around 130 kilometers from Begusarai, Nalanda is renowned for its ancient university and archaeological sites.

  2. Bodh Gaya - A pilgrimage site located around 160 kilometers from Begusarai, Bodh Gaya is known for its stunning Buddhist temples and monasteries.

Conclusion

Begusarai is a city that is steeped in history and culture, offering travelers a unique and unforgettable experience. From exploring the city’s many temples, museums, and historical sites, to trying the local cuisine and attending cultural festivals, there is something for everyone in Begusarai. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most of your trip to this beautiful city, immersing yourself in its rich cultural heritage while creating memories that will last a lifetime.
