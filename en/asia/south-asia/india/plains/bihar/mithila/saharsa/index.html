Introduction to Saharsa

Nestled in the northern region of Bihar, Saharsa is a small city renowned for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. Blessed with vibrant landscapes, ancient temples, and vibrant cuisine, this hidden gem of Bihar beckons intrepid travelers to explore its hidden wonders.

List of Places to Visit in Saharsa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saharsa is steeped in history and is home to several significant historical landmarks that bear witness to the region’s rich past. The most popular among them include Tarachandi Temple, an ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Tara, and the Kosi Barrage, a masterpiece of British engineering that controls the Kosi River’s flow.

The town also boasts of several significant museums, including the Saharsa Museum and the Bhagalpur Museum, where you can explore the region’s archaeological heritage, including ancient pottery, coins, sculptures, and artworks.

Natural Attractions of Saharsa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure and beautiful landscapes, a trip to Saharsa won’t disappoint. The town is surrounded by several natural wonders, including the Bihariji Temple, one of the most beautiful temples in the region, and the Shivaji Park, a beautiful park that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.

A visit to the Kosi River’s banks and the Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve, home to endangered species like the Ganges dolphin, Arna, Wild buffaloes, and several bird species, is highly recommended.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saharsa

Saharsa is blessed with several hiking and biking trails that let you explore the town’s hidden wonders. The most popular among them include exploring the shores of Kosi River, a scenic route that lets you admire the river’s beauty and soak in the peace and solitude. You can also explore the region’s countryside on a bicycle, riding through paddy fields, rural villages, and schools.

Best Things to Do in Saharsa: Activities and Tours

From spiritual experiences to outdoor adventures, Saharsa offers plenty of things to see and do. You can start your day with seeking blessings at one of the many temples in town before embarking on an adventure, like exploring the town’s natural beauty on a hiking or biking tour, or indulging in a birdwatching tour at the Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saharsa

Day 1: Visit Tarachandi Temple and Kosi Barrage Day 2: Explore Bihariji Temple and Shivaji Park Day 3: Visit the Saharsa Museum and explore the town’s heritage Day 4: Embark on a birdwatching tour to the Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve Day 5: Go on an outdoor adventure – hike, bike, or beach picnic Day 6: Experience a cultural tour of the town, visit local markets, and try local cuisine Day 7: Day trip to nearby attractions like Madhubani, Darbhanga or Muzaffarpur.

Food and Drink Scene of Saharsa

Must-Try Dishes

Saharsa offers a multitude of flavors and aromas that tantalize your taste buds. The town’s street food is highly popular and highly recommended by locals and tourists alike. Some of the must-try dishes include Bihari Thali, Litti Chokha, Sattu Paratha, Ghugni, and the signature dessert malpua, a deep-fried pancake glazed in sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

Try local favorites like kachhi lassi, flavored buttermilk, and “Sharbat,” a refreshing summer drink made with fruit juice, rose water, and sugar syrup.

Where to Stay in Saharsa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saharsa offers an array of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Some highly recommended options include Hotel Raj Haveli, Hotel P.S.P., and Hotel Rana Regency.

What to Buy in Saharsa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home some local souvenirs, check out local markets like the Saharsa Bazaar, which offers an array of handicrafts, jewelry, textiles that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Saharsa

Saharsa is steeped in traditional Hindu culture with a deep respect for its historical and religious heritage. Residents of Saharsa are warm and hospitable and welcome visitors with open arms, showing immense pride in their culture and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Saharsa

The best time to visit Saharsa is between October and March, when the weather is mild, and festivities like Chhath Puja, Diwali, and Holi add to the town’s vibrant charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saharsa

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and cultural practices
  • Wear appropriate clothing while visiting religious sites
  • Try local cuisine and attend local festivals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs
  • Photograph people without their permission
  • Litter or damage natural and historical landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saharsa

Saharsa is a safe place for travelers, provided they take necessary precautions like avoiding walking alone at night or traveling with trusted local guides when venturing into the countryside.

Health in Saharsa

Travelers should take necessary precautions such as drinking only bottled water, wearing protective clothing, and using mosquito repellants to avoid health issues like heatstroke and mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Saharsa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saharsa

The nearest airport to Saharsa is Patna airport, located 160km. Regular flights connect Patna to major cities across the country.

Bus Stations Near Saharsa

Saharsa is well-connected to nearby cities and towns through a network of state-run and private buses. The town has several bus stops and terminals, making it easy to travel across the region via bus.

Other Options

Travelers can also opt for taxis or rent private cars to travel to Saharsa.

How to Get Around in Saharsa

The best way to explore Saharsa is on foot or by bicycle. Local rickshaws and taxis are also available for travel within the city.

Where to Go Next from Saharsa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saharsa is surrounded by several other exciting towns and cities nearby, like the town of Madhubani, renowned for its stunning Mithila paintings, Darbhanga, famous for its Raj-era architecture, and Muzaffarpur renowned for its sweet lassi and ancient temples.

Conclusion

Saharsa is a hidden gem that is well worth a visit if you’re looking to explore the cultural and natural wonders of Bihar. Plan your trip using our comprehensive guide to make the most of your time in this vibrant town!
