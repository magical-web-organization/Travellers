This small town situated in the plains of Bihar offers a captivating mix of historical landmarks, picturesque landscapes, and bustling markets. With this tour guide, you can plan your visit to Katihar and explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay during your trip.

Introduction to Katihar

Katihar, located in the Mithila region of Bihar, is popularly known for its deep-rooted cultural heritage and agricultural significance. The town is situated on the banks of the river Kosi, a tributary of the Ganges, and is famous for its natural flood plains that make it a unique destination.

List of Places to Visit in Katihar

Katihar is a unique tourist destination as it offers a blend of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to enthrall visitors. Here is a list of places to visit in Katihar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kali Mandir: Kali Mandir is a popular religious center dedicated to Goddess Kali. It is situated in the main city and is known for its exquisite architecture and ancient feel.
  • Koshi Tappu Wildlife Sanctuary: This wildlife sanctuary is located on the outskirts of Katihar. It serves as a natural habitat for various migratory birds and other animals. Visitors can explore the sanctuary on foot or by boat.
  • Adina Mosque: The Adina mosque is situated in the nearby town of Pandua, and is an excellent example of Islamic architecture merged with Bengali and Hindi patterns. The mosque is a popular religious site for Muslims and non-Muslims alike.
  • Karnagarh Temple: This Hindu shrine is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is located in the town of Barari. The temple is famous for its architecture and the annual Shivratri festival.

Natural Attractions of Katihar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kosi River: One of the major attractions in Katihar is the Kosi River, which provides visitors with a chance to explore the natural floodplains of the river. Visitors can take a boat ride or go fishing in the river.
  • Bairia Forest: Bairia Forest is a popular destination for nature lovers as it provides a natural habitat for wildlife and is an excellent place for bird watching. Visitors can also opt for a jungle safari or trek the nearby hills.
  • Balrampur Wildlife Sanctuary: The Balrampur Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the neighboring district of Katihar and is home to various indigenous animals and birds. It is an excellent place for nature enthusiasts.
  • Koteshwar Mandir: The Koteshwar Mandir is a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and is situated in the nearby town of Manihari. The temple is situated atop a hill and provides visitors with a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Katihar

Katihar is a haven for adventure seekers, and the nearby hills and forests provide excellent hiking and biking trails. One of the popular hiking routes is Bairia forest, which provides visitors with a natural setting of waterfalls and dense foliage. Visitors also have the option of exploring the nearby hills on bicycles, making it an excellent way to experience the natural beauty of Katihar.

Best Things to Do in Katihar: Activities and Tours

Alongside the rich cultural and natural attractions of Katihar, the town also offers several activities and tours that visitors can try.

  • Fishing on the Kosi River: The Kosi River provides an excellent opportunity for visitors to catch fish. The river is abundant with fish like Rohu, Catla, and Singhi, making it an ideal destination for anglers.
  • Boat Safari: Visitors can opt for a boat safari on the Kosi River, which provides a chance to explore the nearby floodplains and spot various migratory birds.
  • Cultural Tours: Katihar is known for its deep-rooted cultural heritage, and visitors can opt for a cultural tour that showcases traditional dance forms, music, and art of the town.
  • Jungle Safari: Bairia Forest and Balrampur Wildlife Sanctuary are excellent places for a jungle safari. Visitors can spot various indigenous animals and birds while exploring the dense forests.

Food and Drink Scene of Katihar

Katihar offers a unique culinary experience, and visitors can try various local delicacies that are unique to the town. Here is a list of must-try dishes in Katihar:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Litti Chokha: A traditional food item of Bihar, Litti Chokha is a rural delicacy consisting of roasted wheat balls stuffed with spices and served with mashed potatoes and brinjal.
  • Thekua: Thekua is a sweet dish that originates from Bihar and is made up of wheat flour, jaggery, and coconut.
  • Pakoda: Pakoda is an Indian snack that is popular in Bihar and consists of gram flour and various vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chaas: Chaas, also known as buttermilk, is a refreshing drink made from yogurt and various spices.
  • Aam Panna: Aam Panna is a summer drink made from raw mango pulp, sugar, and various spices. It is a popular drink in Bihar during the summer season.

Where to Stay in Katihar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guests looking for a more affordable stay can opt for hostels and guesthouses like Hotel Priya Residency, Green Park Guest House, and Hotel Silver Palace.

What to Buy in Katihar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Katihar can purchase various souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some popular choices include:

  • Madhubani Paintings: Madhubani paintings are an integral part of Bihar’s art and culture and are a unique souvenir for visitors.
  • Handmade Pottery: Handmade pottery is a staple craft in Bihar and is an excellent choice for visitors who appreciate artisanal work.
  • Silk Sarees: Bihar is renowned for its silk sarees, which are a popular purchase among visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Katihar

Katihar is known for its deep-rooted cultural heritage, and visitors should adhere to local customs and traditions. The town is predominantly Hindu, and visitors should dress modestly and remove their footwear before entering religious sites. They should also seek permission before photographing people and places.

Best Time to Visit Katihar

The best time to visit Katihar is between October and February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town experiences extreme temperatures during summers and monsoons, making it uncomfortable for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Katihar

Visitors to Katihar should follow some Do’s and Don’ts to ensure a pleasant trip.

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough cash as ATMs are limited in the town.
  • Try the local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without seeking permission.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing while visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Stay out too late at night as the town can be unsafe.

Safety and Health

Katihar is considered safe for visitors, although it is always advisable to be vigilant while traveling. Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and should keep their belongings secure. As for health, visitors should carry sufficient insect repellent, as the region is prone to mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria.

Getting to Katihar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Katihar is well-connected by road and rail to major cities in Bihar and other parts of India. The nearest airport to Katihar is in Bagdogra, West Bengal, approximately 240 kilometers away. Here are some transportation options available:

Airports Near Katihar

  • Bagdogra Airport: Approximately 240 kilometers away, provides connectivity to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Katihar

  • Katihar Bus Station: Located in the city center, provides connectivity to nearby towns and cities.
  • New Bus Stand: Located on NH 31, provides connectivity to major cities in Bihar and other states.

Other Options

  • Train: Katihar Junction Railway Station is well connected to major cities in India, including Delhi, Kolkata, and Mumbai.
  • Road: Visitors can access Katihar by road, with good connectivity to the National Highway network.

How to Get Around in Katihar

Visitors can use various modes of transportation to get around in Katihar, including:

  • Auto Rickshaws: Auto rickshaws are a popular mode of transportation in Katihar and are available throughout the town.
  • Buses: Local buses provide connectivity to nearby towns and cities.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available for hire in Katihar, and visitors can use them for local transportation.

Where to Go Next from Katihar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Katihar is surrounded by several destinations that visitors can explore before moving to their next destination. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Purnia: Purnia is a nearby town that is famous for its ancient temples and cultural sites.
  • Siliguri: Siliguri is a bustling city that provides visitors with a chance to explore urban life and experience various shopping and dining options.
  • Darjeeling: Darjeeling is a hill station located a few hours away from Katihar and is known for its stunning views of the Himalayas and tea plantations.

Conclusion
