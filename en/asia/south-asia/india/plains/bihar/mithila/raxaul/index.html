Introduction to Raxaul

Raxaul is a small border town in the Mithila region of Bihar, India. Known for its vibrant culture, historical monuments, and picturesque landscapes, Raxaul is a hidden gem in the state of Bihar. Situated on the border of Nepal, Raxaul is an important trade center with a rich history and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Raxaul

Raxaul has many places to explore, from historical monuments to scenic natural landscapes to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to visit in Raxaul:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ram Janaki Temple: An ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Sita and Lord Ram, the Ram Janaki Temple is a popular pilgrimage spot for Hindus.

  • Pashupatinath Temple: Located in the neighboring country of Nepal, this magnificent temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the most sacred Hindu temples in the world.

  • Raxaul Museum: Explore the history and culture of Raxaul at this small museum. The museum houses a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents showcasing the history of Raxaul.

Natural Attractions of Raxaul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Patan Devi Hill: A popular picnic spot, Patan Devi Hill is located on the top of a hill and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Chandan Dam: Located near Raxaul, Chandan Dam is a popular spot for fishing and picnics. The serene natural surroundings make it an ideal spot for a day trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Raxaul

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, Raxaul has a few hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the region. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Kanchanrup Road: This scenic road is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or hike. It offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Chautariya: A challenging hiking route, Chautariya takes you through dense forests and offers breathtaking vistas of the Himalayan range.

Best Things to Do in Raxaul: Activities and Tours

In addition to sightseeing, Raxaul offers several activities and tours to make your trip more memorable. Here are some of the best things to do in Raxaul:

  • Visit local markets: Raxaul is known for its vibrant markets, where you can buy local handicrafts and souvenirs.

  • Take a cooking class: Learn to cook traditional Bihari cuisine with locals and take some delicious recipes back home with you.

Week-Long Itinerary for Raxaul

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Raxaul, here’s an itinerary that covers the top sights and experiences:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Ram Janaki Temple and the Raxaul Museum
Day 2 Explore the Patan Devi Hill and nearby villages
Day 3 Take a day trip to the Chandan Dam and enjoy a picnic by the lake

Day 7 Depart Raxaul

Food and Drink Scene of Raxaul

Bihari cuisine is known for its delicious flavors and unique cooking techniques. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Raxaul:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Litti Chokha: A popular Bihari dish made of roasted wheat balls stuffed with spiced mashed potatoes, served with a side of mashed eggplants and tomato and onion chutney.

  • Chana Ghugni: A spicy and tangy dish made of black chickpeas, boiled potato, onion, and a mix of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sattu: A traditional Bihari drink made from roasted chickpeas or barley.

  • Lassi: A sweet and refreshing yogurt drink often flavored with fruits or spices.

Where to Stay in Raxaul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Raxaul to fit every budget. Here are a few options:

  • Ram Janaki Guest House: Located near the Ram Janaki Temple, this guest house offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • Hotel Royal Residency: A mid-range hotel with a restaurant, spa, and fitness center.

  • Hotel Orchid Residency: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and excellent service.

What to Buy in Raxaul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Raxaul is known for its vibrant markets, where you can find unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the top items to bring home:

  • Madhubani Paintings: These colorful paintings, native to the Mithila region, are made using traditional techniques passed down from generations.

  • Local Handicrafts: Raxaul is known for its intricate woodwork, metalwork, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Raxaul

Raxaul is known for its vibrant culture and festivals. Some popular festivals celebrated in Raxaul include:

  • Chhath Puja: A popular Hindu festival celebrated in Bihar, Chhath Puja is dedicated to the Sun God and celebrates the bounties of nature.

  • Holi: The festival of colors, celebrated across India, is especially lively in Raxaul.

Best Time to Visit Raxaul

The best time to visit Raxaul is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. Summers can be hot and humid, and monsoons can be unpredictable and cause floods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Raxaul

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting temples and other religious places.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water. Stick to bottled water.

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Raxaul

Overall, Raxaul is a safe place to visit. However, like all tourist destinations, visitors should be cautious and take necessary precautions.

Health in Raxaul

Visitors to Raxaul should take necessary precautions to avoid water-borne diseases and other illnesses. It’s advisable to drink bottled water and eat at clean and hygienic restaurants.

Getting to Raxaul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Raxaul is well connected to major cities in India and Nepal. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Raxaul

  • Patna Airport (120 km away)

  • Gorakhpur Airport (135 km away)

Bus Stations Near Raxaul

  • Raxaul Bus Stand

  • Bairgania Bus Stand

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to the nearby town of Muzaffarpur and then take a taxi or bus to Raxaul.

How to Get Around in Raxaul

The best way to get around in Raxaul is by auto-rickshaw or taxi. Visitors can also rent a bike or hire a bicycle to explore the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Raxaul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Raxaul is well connected to several other tourist destinations in Bihar and Nepal. Here are a few options:

  • Kathmandu, Nepal: Explore the vibrant city of Kathmandu, Known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture.

  • Bodh Gaya: Visit the birthplace of Buddhism, Bodh Gaya is a pilgrimage site for Buddhists across the world.

Conclusion

Raxaul may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of experiences for travelers. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and vibrant markets, Raxaul is a must-visit destination in Bihar, India. Plan your trip today and experience the charm and beauty of Raxaul.
