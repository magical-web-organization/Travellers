Introduction to Sasaram

Sasaram is a historically and culturally rich city located in the Magadh region of Bihar in India. It is famous for its many ancient historical monuments, natural beauty, and religious significance. The city’s rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty make it an ideal tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Sasaram

Here’s a list of places you must not miss when visiting Sasaram:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rohtasgarh Fort: The Rohtasgarh Fort located on a hill in the Kaimur district, was built by Raja Harish Chandra in the 16th century. It offers a magnificent panoramic view and is an excellent example of ancient Hindu architecture.
  • Sher Shah Suri Tomb: An exceptional structure built in the memory of Sher Shah Suri by his son, the tomb is an architectural masterpiece. It features a rooftop garden, a museum, and a small mosque.
  • Shiv Temple: Located at the top of a hill in the heart of the city, the Shiv Temple is one of the most revered and sacred sites. It offers picturesque views of the surrounding area.
  • Veer Kunwar Singh Park: The park is named after the hero of the 1857 Indian Mutiny, Veer Kunwar Singh. With its lush gardens, fountains, and rest areas, it is an excellent place for a stroll.

Natural Attractions of Sasaram: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary: Located amidst the Kaimur Hills, the sanctuary offers a vast stretch of hills and valleys full of wildlife and forests. Trekking and camping tours are available here.
  • Tomb of Hasan Khan Suri: The tomb is built on the banks of River Son, offering a breathtaking view of the river.
  • Rohtas Dam: Built across the Son River, the dam offers an incredible view of the surrounding hills and forests. Boating is also available here.
  • Dargah of Khwaja Shamsuddin: A popular Muslim holy shrine located in the city’s heart. The 13th-century shrine is visited by people of all faiths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sasaram

Sasaram is an excellent place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some top routes you can take:

  • Hiking routes:
    • Rohtasgarh Fort to Deeprajgarh trek
    • Hiking in Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary
    • Shiv Temple to Kukkuteshwar Temple trek
  • Biking routes:
    • Sasaram to Rohtasgarh Fort
    • Sasaram to Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary
    • Sasaram to Aurangabad

Best Things to Do in Sasaram: Activities and Tours

Sasaram offers a variety of activities for tourists, including:

  • Heritage walk: Take a walk through the ancient city of Sasaram, exploring its rich culture and heritage.
  • Camping: Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary offers a unique camping experience surrounded by nature.
  • Wildlife Safari: Sasaram has several wildlife sanctuaries, and the Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary offers safari trips to explore its flora and fauna.
  • River Rafting: River Son offers a thrilling experience for river rafting enthusiasts.
  • Rock Climbing: The Kaimur Hills are an ideal location for rock climbing enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Sasaram

Sasaram is famous for its traditional cuisine, a blend of Bihari and North Indian food, prepared with unique spices and techniques. Don’t miss these dishes during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Litti-Chokha: A traditional dish of Bihar made with baked wheat flour dough stuffed with gram flour and spices, served with mashed boiled vegetables.
  • Dal Pitha: Rice dumplings stuffed with spiced mashed lentils and served with chutney.
  • Til Kuta: A dessert made with sesame seeds and jaggery.
  • Peda: A sweet made with milk, sugar, and cardamom powder.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A milk-based beverage made with various spices such as almonds, fennel seeds, melon seeds, rose petals, and black pepper.
  • Lassi: A refreshing drink made with yogurt, sugar, and fruits.

What to Buy in Sasaram: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sasaram is famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and local products. Here are some of the things you can buy as souvenirs:

  • Handwoven Sarees: Sasaram is known for its traditional handwoven sarees made with silk and cotton.
  • Pottery: The potters of Sasaram make unique clay pots, vases, and other home decor items.
  • Bihar handicrafts: You can buy various handicrafts from Bihar, including Madhubani paintings, bamboo crafts, and stone carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Sasaram

The people of Sasaram primarily follow Hinduism, Islam, and Sikhism. The city is known for its rich culture and history, which is reflected in its music, dance, and art. The locals are hospitable and friendly, welcoming visitors with warmth and kindness.

Best Time to Visit Sasaram

The best time to visit Sasaram is from September to February when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature is moderate. Avoid visiting during the summer season (April-June) when the temperature soars to almost 50°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sasaram

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol and non-vegetarian food at religious sites.
  • Disrespect local culture and traditions.
  • Litter or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sasaram

The streets of Sasaram are safe for tourists. However, it’s always advisable to be cautious and avoid venturing out alone late at night. Always keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Sasaram

Carry mosquito repellent when visiting Sasaram as it is a region prone to malaria and dengue fever. Also, be cautious of contaminated food and water, and always opt for purified bottled water.

Getting to Sasaram: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sasaram

The nearest airport to Sasaram is the Gaya International Airport, located approximately 95 km away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Sasaram.

Bus Stations Near Sasaram

The Sasaram bus terminal is located near the railway station and offers regular bus services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

You can take a train from major cities across India to Sasaram railway station, which is located in the heart of the city. Alternatively, you can hire a taxi or take a bus from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Sasaram

Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and buses are the best options for getting around the city. You can also rent a bike or car to explore Sasaram at your leisure.

Where to Go Next from Sasaram: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can visit after exploring Sasaram:

  • Bodh Gaya: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for the Mahabodhi Temple, where Gautama Buddha is said to have attained enlightenment.
  • Varanasi: Located approximately 300 km from Sasaram, Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major spiritual center for Hindus.
  • Nalanda: An ancient Buddhist university that was a center of intellectual and spiritual learning.

Conclusion

Sasaram is a must-visit destination for those who want to explore India’s rich cultural and historical heritage. Whether you’re interested in ancient architecture, natural beauty, or religious significance, Sasaram has something to offer for everyone.
