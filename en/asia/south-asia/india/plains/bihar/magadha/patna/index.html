Introduction to Patna

Patna, the capital city of Bihar, is a vibrant and historically significant city located in the northern part of India. Once the capital of the powerful Magadha empire, Patna is now a bustling metropolis that blends ancient and modern cultures seamlessly. Its rich historical heritage and religious significance have left an indelible mark on the city’s architecture, traditions, and lifestyle.

If you are planning to visit Patna, there is an abundance of activities and experiences that you can enjoy. From exploring the city’s numerous historical sites to tasting exquisite local cuisine, Patna offers a range of attractions to inspire various interests. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at Patna’s top tourist destinations, best things to do, local culture, and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Patna

No visit to Patna is complete without exploring its many attractions. Here is a list of places to visit in Patna:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Patna is a city steeped in history and culture. Its museums and historical sites offer visitors an opportunity to explore the city’s past and immerse themselves in its rich heritage.

  1. Patna Museum: Established in 1917, this grand museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about Patna’s rich history. Its collection includes rare archaeological finds, anglo-Indian art, and a range of exhibits showcasing the city’s cultural diversity.

  2. Mahavir Mandir: This is one of the most-respected religious destinations in Patna. Located in the heart of the city, it is believed to fulfill the desires of its devotees.

  3. Golghar: This iconic landmark was originally built for storing grain during famine-like situations. It now offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the city from the top.

  4. Har Mandir Saheb: This is one of the most sacred Sikh shrines in Patna, attracting a large number of visitors every year.

Natural Attractions of Patna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Patna is also home to some stunning natural attractions and provides thrill-seekers ample opportunities for outdoor adventure.

  1. Kumhrar: This historic site is home to the ruins of the Mauryan empire, making it a must-visit for history buffs.

  2. Eco Park: Spread across an area of 43 acres, the Eco Park is an ideal place to take a stroll amidst nature and enjoy picturesque views of a lake.

  3. Sanjay Gandhi Botanical Garden: This lush botanical garden houses an impressive range of exotic and rare plants that will fascinate visitors of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Patna

For those who love the great outdoors, Patna has plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

  1. Gandhi Maidan: This sprawling park, located in the heart of the city, has a dedicated walking and cycling track for fitness enthusiasts.

  2. Patna-Bakhtiarpur Cycle Highway: This cycling route is a popular weekend destination for locals and visitors alike. It offers scenic views of the countryside and is a must-visit for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Patna: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for things to do in Patna, there is no shortage of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  1. Walking tour of Ancient Patna: This walking tour takes visitors through the city’s historic sites, including Mahavir Mandir, Gandhi Maidan, Golghar, and several others, providing an excellent overview of Patna’s rich history and culture.

  2. Food Tour of Patna: This is the ideal activity for foodies, who can taste a range of delicious local delicacies like litti-chokha, sattu paratha, khaja, and much more.

Food and Drink Scene of Patna

No trip to Patna is complete without experiencing its gastronomic delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Litti-Chokha: This traditional dish of Bihar combines roasted wheat balls (litti) with mashed potatoes, spices, and vegetables (chokha). This dish is a staple in Patna’s cuisine.

  2. Sattu Paratha: Prepared with roasted gram flour, sattu paratha is a popular breakfast dish in Patna.

  3. Khaja: This sweet delicacy is made with flour, sugar, and ghee and is a famous sweet in Patna.

Local Beverages

  1. Thandai: A refreshing drink made with milk, almonds, cashews, cardamom, rose petals, and fennel seeds, this traditional beverage is a popular summer drink in Patna.

  2. Lassi: This yoghurt-based drink is a perfect accompaniment to a spicy meal and is commonly found in every corner of the city.

What to Buy in Patna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Patna, here are some unique souvenir ideas:

  1. Madhubani Paintings: These traditional paintings depict various themes like nature, mythology, and everyday life and are famous across Bihar.

  2. Bihari Litti Masala: This spice mix comes in various flavors and is a must-buy for those who relish Bihar’s signature dish, Litti Chokha.

Local Culture and Customs of Patna

Patna has a diverse cultural landscape that is steeped in its history and traditions. Some unique customs include:

  • Public display of affection is looked down upon and is often shunned in public places.
  • Respecting elders is the norm in Patna’s culture and is looked upon with high regard.

Best Time to Visit Patna

The ideal time to visit Patna is during the winter months, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Patna

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Patna:

Do:

  • Dress modestly in public places to avoid unwanted attention.
  • Try out the street food, but make sure it comes from a hygienic vendor.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions, and be sensitive to religious customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t engage with unauthorized tour guides who try to convince you to visit places off the beaten track.
  • Do not disrespect religious symbols and customs.
  • Do not litter public places and maintain hygiene.

Safety and Health

While Patna is safe for visitors, it is essential to keep these safety and health tips in mind:

Safety in Patna

  • Keep your valuables and cash secure and avoid wandering alone at night.
  • Be cautious while handling local transportation and negotiate fares in advance.

Health in Patna

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water and avoid eating street food from unhygienic vendors.
  • Carry necessary medication, repellant and maintain a safe distance from wild animals.

How to Get Around in Patna

The best way to explore Patna is by hiring a taxi or rental car for the day. Alternatively, the city offers a fleet of auto-rickshaws, e-rickshaws, and buses.

Where to Go Next from Patna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some exciting nearby destinations that you can explore from Patna include Bodh Gaya, Nalanda, and Rajgir, all of which have a rich cultural and historical heritage.

Conclusion

Patna is an enchanting city that is rich in history, culture, and warmth. From its ancient ruins to its bustling streets, there is always something fascinating to explore in Patna. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide helps you make the most of your trip to Patna and have a fulfilling experience.
