A Comprehensive Guide to Rewa, India for Travelers

Located in Eastern Madhya Pradesh, in the Plains region of India, Rewa is a destination full of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural richness. If you are planning a trip to this town, there are so many attractions and activities to keep you engaged. In this comprehensive guide to Rewa, India, we cover everything you need to know about this beautiful destination from best places to visit, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and much more.

Introduction to Rewa

Rewa is a small town situated on the banks of the Tamsa River. It is known for its rich history, wildlife, and charming natural beauty. It was once the capital of the princely state of Rewa, which was ruled by the Baghel Dynasty. The town is a perfect destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventurers.

List of Places to Visit in Rewa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rewa Fort: Known for its unique architecture and historical significance, this fort is a must-visit attraction in Rewa. It was built during the reign of Maharaja Bhawani Singh and is famous for its underground tunnels and majestic structure.

  • Govindgarh Palace: This palace was constructed during the British era and was once the summer residence of the Baghel rulers. It is known for its stunning architecture, beautiful gardens, and magnificent fountains.

  • Baghel Museum: This museum features a collection of ancient artifacts, sculptures, and paintings that reflect the history and culture of the Baghel dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Rewa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bandhavgarh National Park: This national park is one of the most famous wildlife sanctuaries in India. It is home to a large number of tigers, as well as other animals like leopards, sloth bears, and deer. It is also known for its beautiful landscape and is a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

  • Keoti Falls: This stunning waterfall is located at a distance of around 35 km from Rewa. It is a popular picnic spot and a perfect destination for adventure seekers.

  • Rani Talab: This lake is located in the center of the town and is a popular spot for boating and picnicking. It is surrounded by beautiful gardens and a perfect place to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rewa

  • Bandhavgarh National Park: This national park offers fantastic opportunities for hiking and biking. A guided safari inside the park is the best way to explore the wildlife and nature of the area.

  • Purwa Falls: This waterfall is located around 15 km from Rewa and is accessible via a scenic trek that passes through beautiful landscapes and villages.

Best Things to Do in Rewa: Activities and Tours

  • Safari in Bandhavgarh National Park: A Safari inside the national park is one of the best ways to explore the wildlife and nature of the area. You can opt for an early morning or an evening safari.

  • Boating in Rani Talab: Rent a boat and enjoy the serene surroundings of the beautiful Rani Talab.

  • Explore the history and culture: Visit historical monuments and museums in the town to learn about the history and culture of the Baghel Dynasty.

Food and Drink Scene of Rewa

When it comes to food and drink, Rewa has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bafla: A popular dish in Madhya Pradesh, Bafla is a wheat-based dish that is served with ghee and dal. It is a must-try dish when in Rewa.

  • Bhutte ka Kees: This is a dish made from grated corn that is cooked with spices and is a popular street food in Rewa.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: This is a popular drink in Rewa, and it is made from yogurt, milk, and sugar. It is an ideal drink to beat the heat during the summers.

  • Jaljeera: A refreshing drink made from cumin seeds and lemon, this drink is perfect for quenching thirst during the summers.

Where to Stay in Rewa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Rewa, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the popular ones are:

  • Hotel Lovely Palace: This is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the town.

  • Rewa Retreat Resort: Located on the outskirts of the town, this resort is perfect for those who want to enjoy nature and luxury.

  • Rewa Raj Vilas: This hotel is known for its traditional architecture and luxurious amenities. It is perfect for those who want to experience the royalty of the Baghel Dynasty.

What to Buy in Rewa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banana Fiber Crafts: These crafts are made from banana fibers and are a popular souvenir in Rewa.

  • Bagh Print Fabrics: These fabrics are made with block printing techniques that are unique to Rewa and are a popular buy among tourists.

Local Culture and Customs of Rewa

Rewa has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are warm and welcoming. The Baghel dynasty ruled Rewa for many years and has left a mark on the culture and customs of the town. The people of the town celebrate various festivals throughout the year, and visitors are welcome to join in the festivities.

Best Time to Visit Rewa

The best time to visit Rewa is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities. Summers can be scorching, and it is not recommended to visit during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rewa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.

  • Carry a water bottle and sunscreen lotion during outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public places.

  • Do not travel alone late at night.

  • Do not take pictures without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rewa

Rewa is a safe destination for tourists, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. However, it is essential to take basic safety precautions like not traveling alone late at night and not carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Rewa

Visitors are advised to take precautions against water-borne diseases by drinking bottled water and avoiding street food.

Getting to Rewa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rewa is well connected to major Indian cities and can be reached by air, train, and road.

Airports Near Rewa

  • Khajuraho Airport (204 km)

  • Jabalpur Airport (230 km)

Bus Stations Near Rewa

  • Rewa Bus Stand

  • Satna Bus Stand

Other Options

  • Train: Rewa has a railway station that is well connected to major cities in India.

  • Cab: Taxis and cabs are available for hire in Rewa.

How to Get Around in Rewa

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are available for hire in Rewa. However, it is recommended to fix a fare before hiring a taxi or rickshaw.

Where to Go Next from Rewa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Khajuraho (204 km)

  • Panna National Park (200 km)

Conclusion

Rewa is a destination that offers something for every kind of traveler, whether you are interested in history, nature, or adventure. With this comprehensive guide to Rewa, India, you are now well-equipped to plan a fantastic trip to this beautiful destination.
