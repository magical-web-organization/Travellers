Introduction to Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot is a small town located in the Eastern part of Madhya Pradesh, India, known for its cultural significance and natural beauty. It is believed to be the place where Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana spent 11 years of their 14-year exile period. The town is home to many ancient temples, ghats, and hills that hold great religious importance. The town also boasts of natural beauty with waterfalls, lakes, and rivers making it an ideal place for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Chitrakoot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chitrakoot is home to many museums and historical attractions, which give an insight into its rich culture and heritage. Some of these places to visit are:

  • Ram Ghat: This is the most popular ghat in Chitrakoot, and is believed to be the place where Lord Rama and his brothers took a dip in the river Mandakini during their exile period.
  • Kamadgiri Temple: It is a 5 km stretch of forest-covered hills that is said to be one of the five holiest places for Hindus. Kamadgiri Temple is located on a hill, which offers breathtaking views of the surroundings.
  • Bharat Milap Temple: It is believed to be the place where Lord Rama’s brother, Bharat, met him to persuade him to return to Ayodhya and take his rightful place as the king.
  • Hanuman Dhara Temple: This temple is located on a hill and is dedicated to Lord Hanuman. It is believed that if one takes a bath under the waterfall here, their sins will be washed away.

Natural Attractions of Chitrakoot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There are many natural attractions in Chitrakoot that provide a refreshing break from the heat and dust. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Janaki Kund: This is a natural spring which is believed to be the place where Sita used to bathe during her exile period. The Kund is located in a picturesque surrounding and has a serene ambience.
  • Gupt Godavari Caves: These caves are located on the banks of the Godavari River and are said to be the place where Lord Rama and Lakshmana stayed during their exile period. These caves have two chambers, and one can see a natural flow of water from the underground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot is an ideal place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. One can plan a day hike or bike ride to Kamadgiri Temple, which is located on a hill, and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. Another popular hiking and biking trail is Ramghat to Sphatik Shila, a 3.5 km stretch of trail that passes through many ghats and temples.

Best Things to Do in Chitrakoot: Activities and Tours

There are numerous things to do in Chitrakoot, catering to people of all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Take a dip in the holy waters of Ram Ghat and other ghats to attain spiritual purification.
  • Visit the famous Bharat Milap Temple and witness the mythological legend of Lord Rama and his brother Bharat.
  • Go on a nature walk to Janaki Kund, a natural spring believed to be the place where Sita used to bathe during her exile period.
  • Attend the Arti ceremony held every evening at Ram Ghat, where you can witness numerous devotees paying their respects to Lord Rama.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chitrakoot

Day 1: Arrive in Chitrakoot and visit Ram Ghat, Kamadgiri Temple, and Bharat Milap Temple. Day 2: Go on a day hike to Sphatik Shila. Day 3: Visit the Janaki Kund and the Gupt Godavari Caves. Day 4: Attend the Arti ceremony at Ram Ghat in the evening. Day 5: Indulge in local cuisine and try out the famous Chitrakoot Batti Chokha. Day 6: Explore nearby attractions like Allahabad (120 km), which is home to the famous Kumbh Mela. Day 7: Depart from Chitrakoot and head back home refreshed and rejuvenated.

Food and Drink Scene of Chitrakoot

Must-Try Dishes

The food of Chitrakoot is simple and traditional, and the town is known for its delicious street food. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Chitrakoot Batti Chokha: It is a popular dish made of wheat flour dumplings, which are immersed in a spicy potato gravy.
  • Banana Pakoda: It is a tasty snack where ripe bananas are coated in gram flour batter and deep-fried.
  • Peda: It is a popular sweet dish made of cow milk and sugar.

Local Beverages

The town offers a wide range of local beverages, which include:

  • Sharbat: It is a refreshing drink made from rose petals, mint, and lemon juice.
  • Thandai: It is a popular drink made from milk, nuts, and spices like saffron, cardamom, and fennel seeds.

Where to Stay in Chitrakoot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chitrakoot offers many options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Chitrakoot are:

  • Hotel Ram Darshan: It is a budget-friendly hotel offering comfortable and clean rooms, and is located in a prime location with easy access to all the major attractions.
  • MPT Tourist Bungalow: It is a government-run hotel located in the heart of the town, and offers beautiful views of the surroundings.
  • Nandanvan Guest House: It is a beautiful guest house located near Ram Ghat, and offers clean rooms and a peaceful ambience.

What to Buy in Chitrakoot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chitrakoot offers a range of souvenirs and local products that are worth buying as a memory of your trip. Some of the best things to buy in Chitrakoot are:

  • Bamboo and wooden showpieces: Chitrakoot is known for its beautiful handmade bamboo and wooden showpieces like lamps, penholders, and wall hangings.
  • Stone sculptures: The town is surrounded by natural rocks, and local artisans have carved many beautiful sculptures and idols from them.
  • Paintings: Chitrakoot is famous for its traditional paintings, which are inspired by mythology and depict various scenes from the Ramayana.

Local Culture and Customs of Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot has a unique culture and heritage, which is deeply rooted in the Hindu mythology of Ramayana. The town is known for its exceptional hospitality, and the locals are warm and welcoming. It is advised to dress modestly and respect the local customs while visiting the temples and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Chitrakoot

The best time to visit Chitrakoot is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature does not soar high. One can enjoy the natural beauty of the town during this time and attend many festivals and fairs like Ram Navmi, Makar Sankranti, and Diwali.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chitrakoot

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting the temples and other religious sites.
  • Try out the local cuisine and indulge in the street food scene of Chitrakoot.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter in the surroundings and maintain cleanliness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot is a relatively safe town, and tourists can move around freely. However, one should take basic safety precautions like taking care of their belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot does not have good healthcare facilities, and it is advised to carry basic medication for minor health issues. In case of an emergency, one can head to nearby cities like Allahabad or Satna, which have better healthcare facilities.

Getting to Chitrakoot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chitrakoot is well-connected to major cities in India via all modes of transportation.

Airports Near Chitrakoot

The nearest airport to Chitrakoot is Khajuraho Airport, which is about 170 km away.

Bus Stations Near Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot is well-connected by road, and one can take buses from major cities like Satna, Jhansi, and Allahabad.

Other Options

Chitrakoot also has a railway station, and many trains connect it to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

How to Get Around in Chitrakoot

Chitrakoot is a small town, and one can move around on foot or hire a bicycle or motorcycle for a day. One can also hire an autorickshaw or taxi to explore the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Chitrakoot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations that one can explore while on a trip to Chitrakoot. Some of the popular places are:

  • Allahabad: It is a city located about 120 km away from Chitrakoot, and is home to many religious sites like Triveni Sangam, Kumbh Mela, and Allahabad Fort.
  • Satna: It is a city located about 77 km away from Chitrakoot, and is known for its beautiful temples like Maihar Devi Temple, Jain Temple, and Venkat Bhagwan Temple.
  • Varanasi: It is a city located about 240 km away from Chitrakoot, and is considered to be one of the holiest places for Hindus. It is home to numerous temples and ghats, and one can explore the streets and alleys full of shops selling traditional handicrafts and artefacts.

Conclusion

Chitrakoot is a beautiful town, with a rich culture and heritage, and offers something for everyone. One can plan a peaceful retreat with family, attend religious events, or explore the beautiful natural surroundings. With our comprehensive travel guide, one can plan their trip to Chitrakoot without any hassle and have a memorable experience.
