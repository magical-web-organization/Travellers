Introduction to Bhopal

Bhopal, located in the central part of India, is the capital city of Madhya Pradesh. It is famous for its rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful lakes. Bhopal is also known as the ‘city of lakes’ as it has many natural and artificial lakes spread over the city.

The city is a perfect blend of old-world charm and modernity which makes it an interesting place to explore. Bhopal is an important tourist destination, attracting tourists from around the world. The city is also known for its delicious cuisine, museums, historical monuments, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Bhopal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Taj-ul-Masjid

Taj-ul-Masjid is one of the largest mosques in India and a prominent landmark of Bhopal. It was built in the 19th century and has a beautiful architecture mixing Mughal and Indian styles. The mosque is a popular destination for tourists and is known for its grandeur and spiritual significance.

2. Shaukat Mahal

Shaukat Mahal is an architectural marvel that combines Gothic, Mughal, and French styles. It was built by the Nawab of Bhopal in the 19th century and is now a popular tourist attraction. The palace is famous for its intricate carvings and beautiful design.

3. Upper Lake

The Upper Lake in Bhopal is a man-made lake built in the 11th century by King Bhoj. It is the biggest lake in Bhopal, and offers mesmerizing views of the sunset. The lake is also a popular spot for boating and water sports.

4. Bharat Bhawan

Bharat Bhawan is a multi-art complex built-in 1982. It is dedicated to promoting and preserving Indian art and culture. Bharat Bhawan is an important landmark of Bhopal and is known for its unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Bhopal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Lower Lake

The Lower Lake is another beautiful lake in Bhopal. It is also known as the ‘Chotta Talaab’ and is a popular spot for picnics, boating, and bird watching. The lake is surrounded by hills, making it an ideal location for a morning or evening walk.

2. Van Vihar National Park

Van Vihar National Park is a wildlife sanctuary and a popular tourist destination in Bhopal. The park features a diverse range of flora and fauna and offers stunning views of nature. Visitors can also take a jeep safari to explore the park.

3. Sair Sapata

Sair Sapata is a recreational park located near the Upper Lake. It is a popular family-friendly spot and features various games, rides, and water activities. Sair Sapata also offers a beautiful view of the lake and is a great spot for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhopal

Bhopal is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, making it an ideal place for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Bhopal.

  • Kerwa Dam Trail
  • Sair Sapata to Van Vihar National Park Trail
  • Bhadbhada Dam Trail
  • Rock Climbing at Ratapani Wildlife Sanctuary

Best Things to Do in Bhopal: Activities and Tours

1. Explore the Old City

The old city of Bhopal has a rich history and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the Mughal architecture of India. Visitors can explore various forts, palaces, and mosques in the area.

2. Taste the Local Cuisine

Bhopal is known for its delicious cuisine, and visitors should try the local specialties like biryani, kebabs, and poha. Bhopal also has a street food culture that is worth exploring.

3. Arts and Crafts Tour

Bhopal is famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and paintings. Visitors can take a tour of various markets and shops to explore the local handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhopal

Bhopal has a rich cultural history, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Some of the common customs of Bhopal include greeting people with a ‘namaste’ and removing footwear before entering a place of worship.

Best Time to Visit Bhopal

The best time to visit Bhopal is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhopal

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies
  • Carry cash for small transactions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious beliefs
  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Engage in illegal activities
  • Drink or smoke in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhopal

Bhopal is a relatively safe city, but visitors are advised to take precautions like avoiding deserted areas after dark and keeping valuable possessions safe.

Health in Bhopal

Visitors are advised to carry all necessary medication and avoid drinking tap water. It is also recommended to get a flu shot before traveling to Bhopal.
