Introduction to Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is a popular hill station located in the state of Madhya Pradesh in India. Surrounded by dense forests and beautiful hills, this picturesque town is an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Pachmarhi is a place where the past meets the present – it is steeped in history and rich culture, while also being a modern-day tourist hub. With a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, Pachmarhi is a must-visit destination in India.

List of Places to Visit in Pachmarhi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mahadeo Temple - An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the heart of Pachmarhi
  • Handi Khoh - A beautiful ravine that is popular for its scenic views and as a site of archaeological importance
  • Jata Shankar Caves - A series of natural caves believed to be the site where Lord Shiva hid from the demon Bhasmasur
  • Bee Fall - A beautiful waterfall that cascades down from a height of 35 meters
  • Satpura National Park - A protected area that is home to tigers, leopards, and various species of birds

Natural Attractions of Pachmarhi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dhoopgarh - The highest point in the Satpura Range, ideal for watching sunsets and sunrises
  • Pandav Caves - Believed to have been built during the Mahabharata era, these caves have historical and religious significance.
  • Apsara Vihar - A natural pool surrounded by hills and lush greenery with pristine water that is ideal for swimming
  • Chauragarh Temple - A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, which is located at a height of 1,300 meters and requires a trek of 3 km to reach.
  • Rajat Prapat - A magnificent waterfall that is a steep climb down and can be accessed through a 3 km trek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Dhoopgarh Circuit - A 6-km trek that takes you through the forests to the highest point in the Satpura Range
  • Satpura Wildlife Sanctuary Circuit - A 14-km scenic drive that takes you through the forests and wildlife sanctuary, allowing you to see tigers, leopards, and other animals
  • Apsara Vihar Circuit - A 4 km trek that takes you to Apsara Vihar and provides stunning views of the surrounding hills

Best Things to Do in Pachmarhi: Activities and Tours

  • Jeep Safari - A thrilling way to explore the Satpura National Park and wildlife sanctuary
  • Rock Climbing and Rappelling - Ideal for adventure enthusiasts, Pachmarhi offers several sites for rock climbing and rappelling
  • Cycling Tours - Take a leisurely cycle tour through the town and surrounding countryside, appreciating the scenic beauty of the region
  • Camping - Enjoy camping in the natural surroundings, under the stars and get close to nature

Week-Long Itinerary for Pachmarhi

Day 1: Visit Pandav Caves and Bee Fall Day 2: Jeep Safari to Satpura National Park Days 3-4: Trekking and rock climbing Day 5: Visit Jatashankar Caves and climb Chauragarh Temple Day 6: Visit Mahadeo Temple and Dhoopgarh Day 7: Relax, enjoy local cuisine, and plan next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Pachmarhi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhutte ki Kees - A dish made with grated corn and sautéed with spices
  • Bafla - A local Rajasthani dish made of wheat dough that is boiled and later baked, served with a dal, kadhi, and chutney
  • Poha - A local delicacy made of flattened rice flakes, mixed with roasted peanuts and spices

Local Beverages

  • Lassi - A refreshing drink made of yoghurt, sugar and plenty of ice.
  • Sharbat - A local drink made of various syrup and water, refresh you in summer heats.
  • Tea - Locally grown and served with most of the food in Pachmarhi, it’s a must-try.

Where to Stay in Pachmarhi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a wide range of accommodations available in Pachmarhi, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

Budget Accommodations

  • Gol Ghar Hostel and Café - located in the centre of the town, offering budget-friendly accommodation and easy access to local shops and restaurants.
  • Buddha Resort - A simple but comfortable guesthouse that is ideal for travellers on a tight budget

Mid-Range Accommodations

  • Highland Heritage - A family-friendly hotel located close to many of Pachmarhi’s major attractions, children’s activity area available here.
  • WelcomHeritage Golf View - A cosy, rustic-style hotel with spacious rooms, and comfortable amenities.

Luxury Accommodations

  • The Summer Tor - It offers traditional Indian hospitality with elegantly appointed rooms, suites, and cottages, nestled near a forest and waterfall.
  • The Colonial - A luxurious hotel that offers guests five-star amenities and world-class services, crowned with views of Pachmarhi’s lush forests.

What to Buy in Pachmarhi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tribal Artifacts - Handicrafts and textiles made by the local tribes, including bamboo baskets, pottery, and speciality fabrics
  • Sandalwood Products - Scented sandalwood oils, soaps, candles and other products,
  • Handmade Jewellery - Traditional jewellery made by local artisans with unique designs and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is home to several indigenous communities, each with its unique cultural practices.

  • Gond Tribe - Known for its traditional artwork and a type of music known as Pandvani.
  • Korku Tribe - Known for its unique style of dance performed by both males and females by sitting in a round formation.
  • Bhil Tribe - Known for their traditional dress and animal husbandry skills.

Best Time to Visit Pachmarhi

The best time to visit Pachmarhi is from October to June, when the weather is pleasant and dry, and the landscapes are green and crisp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pachmarhi

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Wear comfortable clothing and shoes for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural surroundings
  • Disrespect the local culture and beliefs
  • Wear revealing or tight clothing near religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is a relatively safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when travelling alone or at night.

Health in Pachmarhi

It is advisable to check with your doctor before leaving for Pachmarhi and take necessary vaccinations. Pack adequate mosquito repellent and sunscreen as for summers it may require extra protection. Don’t forget to keep enough water to keep hydrated while exploring the town’s outdoor scenery.

Getting to Pachmarhi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pachmarhi

The nearest airport to Pachmarhi is at Raja Bhoj Airport in Bhopal, which is approximately 200 km from the town. The airport has regular domestic flights from major Indian cities.

Bus Stations Near Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is well-connected to major cities in the region by road, and there are several bus services that operate daily from nearby cities including Jabalpur, Bhopal, Nagpur and Pipariya.

Other Options

The nearest railhead to Pachmarhi is at Pipariya Train station, which is well connected with major cities of India including Delhi, Mumbai, Gwalior and Varanasi. Taxi services provide easy travel to the town.

How to Get Around in Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is a small town and walking is an ideal way to get around. However, there are options for cycling, renting a scooter, a car, or hiring a local guide for travel between long distant areas.

Where to Go Next from Pachmarhi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jabalpur - It is famous for Marble rocks, Bhedaghat, and Chousat Yogini Temple, approximately 240 km from Pachmarhi.
  • Bhopal - Famous for caves, temples and lakes, approximately 220 km from Pachmarhi.
  • Kanha National Park - Home to Royal Bengal Tigers, Indian leopards, sloth bears, barasingha, traveling 250 km to the park.
  • Satpura Biosphere Reserve - Spread over 1500 sq km, filled with flora and fauna, lies approximately 120 km from the town.

Conclusion

Pachmarhi is a destination that offers a stunning blend of natural and cultural attractions. With so much to see and do, it is undoubtedly one of the best cities to visit in India. With this travel guide, beat the hustle and bustle of a vacation in a bigger city, relax and have a unique experience that only Pachmarhi can offer.
