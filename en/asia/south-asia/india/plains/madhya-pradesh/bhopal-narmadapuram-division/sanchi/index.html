Introduction to Sanchi

Sanchi is a small town in the Bhopal-Narmadapuram Division of Madhya Pradesh, India. It is famous for its ancient Buddhist sites and monuments, which are recognized as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. Sanchi is approximately 46 km from Bhopal, the capital city of Madhya Pradesh, and it is easily accessible by road.

The town is located on a hill and offers a beautiful view of the surrounding plains. The weather in Sanchi is pleasant throughout the year, but the best time to visit is between October and March.

List of Places to Visit in Sanchi

Sanchi is a treasure trove of ancient Buddhist monuments and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit places in Sanchi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sanchi Stupa: The Sanchi Stupa is one of the oldest stone structures in India and is considered a masterpiece of Buddhist art and architecture. The stupa was built in the 3rd century BCE and has four gateways, each adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures.

  • Sanchi Museum: The Sanchi Museum is located within the complex of the Sanchi Stupa and houses a collection of Buddhist artifacts, sculptures, and inscriptions.

  • Ashoka Pillar: The Ashoka Pillar is another famous historical site in Sanchi. It was erected by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE and bears his edicts and inscriptions.

Natural Attractions of Sanchi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Udayagiri Caves: The Udayagiri Caves are ancient rock-cut caves located near Sanchi. The caves are adorned with beautiful sculptures and are believed to have been built between the 4th and 5th centuries.

  • Vidisha: Vidisha is a nearby town that is known for its beautiful temples and scenic views. The town is located on the banks of the Betwa River and has several ancient temples dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sanchi

Sanchi and its surrounding areas offer several hiking and biking trails that allow travelers to explore the natural beauty of the region. Some popular hikes and bike rides include:

  • Sanchi to Udayagiri Caves: This route takes you through beautiful countryside and allows you to explore the ancient Udayagiri Caves.

  • Sanchi to Vidisha: This trail runs along the Betwa River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sanchi: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Sanchi Stupa and Museum to learn about the history and significance of these ancient Buddhist sites.

  • Explore the beautiful Udayagiri Caves and the nearby town of Vidisha.

  • Go on a hiking or biking trip to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sanchi

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Sanchi:

Day 1: Explore the Sanchi Stupa and Museum. Day 2: Take a day trip to Vidisha and explore the ancient temples in the town. Day 3: Go on a hike or bike ride to explore the countryside near Sanchi. Day 4: Visit the Udayagiri Caves and explore the beautiful sculptures and carvings. Day 5-7: Take a day trip to Bhopal and explore the city’s museums, markets, and historical sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Sanchi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poha: Poha is a popular breakfast dish in Sanchi and is made with flattened rice, onions, and spices.

  • Dal Bafla: Dal Bafla is a traditional dish of Madhya Pradesh and is made with wheat flour, ghee, and a spicy lentil curry.

  • Ladoo: Ladoo is a sweet dish made with gram flour and sugar and is a popular dessert in Sanchi.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: Lassi is a traditional Indian drink made with yogurt, sugar, and spices. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for a hot day.

  • Masala Chai: Masala Chai is a popular Indian tea that is made with black tea, spices, and milk. It is a must-try for tea lovers.

Where to Stay in Sanchi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Sanchi, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Sambodhi: Hotel Sambodhi is a hotel located near the Sanchi Stupa and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

  • MPT Tourist Motel: MPT Tourist Motel is a budget-friendly option that offers clean rooms and basic amenities.

  • Backpackers’ Island Hostel: Backpackers’ Island Hostel is a popular hostel that is located near the Sanchi Stupa and offers dormitory-style rooms and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Sanchi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Buddhist artifacts and sculptures: Sanchi is famous for its Buddhist sites and monuments, and you can find several shops selling artifacts and sculptures related to Buddhism.

  • Handicrafts: Madhya Pradesh is known for its handicrafts, and you can find several shops in Sanchi selling beautiful textiles, pottery, and other handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Sanchi

The local culture of Sanchi is heavily influenced by Buddhism, and the town is considered a sacred place by Buddhists around the world. The people of Sanchi are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to respect their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sanchi

The best time to visit Sanchi is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. Summers in Sanchi can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sanchi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

  • Touch or climb on the ancient monuments.

  • Engage in any activity that may harm the environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sanchi

Sanchi is a safe town for travelers, but it is always advisable to take basic precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Sanchi

Travelers to Sanchi should take precautions against common diseases like malaria and dengue fever. It is advisable to carry mosquito repellent and to avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Sanchi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sanchi is easily accessible by road, and several buses and taxis operate between Sanchi and Bhopal, which is the nearest major city.

Airports Near Sanchi

  • Raja Bhoj Airport, Bhopal (35 km)

Bus Stations Near Sanchi

  • Sanchi Bus Stand

Other Options

  • Taxis and shared jeeps are also available for hire from Bhopal.

How to Get Around in Sanchi

Sanchi is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance from each other. Bicycles and motorbikes are also available for hire in town.

Where to Go Next from Sanchi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bhopal: Bhopal is a beautiful city that is famous for its museums, markets, and historical sites.

  • Bhimbetka: Bhimbetka is an ancient rock shelter that is located near Bhopal and is known for its prehistoric cave paintings.

Conclusion

Sanchi is a beautiful town that is rich in history and culture. With its ancient Buddhist sites, scenic views, and delicious cuisine, Sanchi is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the rich cultural heritage of India.
