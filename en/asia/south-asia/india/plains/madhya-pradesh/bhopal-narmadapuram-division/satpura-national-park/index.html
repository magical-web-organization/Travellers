Introduction to Satpura National Park

Satpura National Park is a beautiful wildlife reserve located in the heart of India. It covers an area of 524 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The park is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and wildlife photographers. It is a great destination for those who want to explore the beauty of Central India and experience the thrill of a wildlife safari.

List of Places to Visit in Satpura National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Madai Caves: These ancient caves have beautiful rock paintings and carvings that showcase the history and culture of the region.

  2. Rajat Prapat Waterfall: Located on the Denwa River, this waterfall is a popular tourist spot in the area.

  3. Pachmarhi: This hill station is located near Satpura National Park and is known for its rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Satpura National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dhupgarh Point: This is the highest point in Pachmarhi and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding forests and hills.

  2. Satpura Range: This range is a part of the prominent Deccan Plateau and is known for its scenic beauty and pristine natural habitats.

  3. Tawa Reservoir: This reservoir is a great place for water activities such as boating, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Satpura National Park

  1. Pachmarhi to Satpura Range: This is a popular trek among adventure enthusiasts.

  2. Chota Mahadeo: This is a 6-kilometer trek that offers a scenic view of the surrounding hills.

  3. Madai Waterfall: This 6-kilometer trek takes you through dense forests and offers a view of the beautiful Madai waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Satpura National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Wildlife Safari: A wildlife safari is a must-do activity in Satpura National Park. You can spot a wide range of animals such as Bengal tiger, leopard, sloth bear, Indian giant squirrel, and many more.

  2. Elephant Ride: If you want to experience the wilderness of the park in a unique way, an elephant ride is a great option.

  3. Bird Watching: Satpura National Park is home to a wide range of bird species. You can spot some rare and beautiful birds during your visit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Satpura National Park

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel in Pachmarhi. Day 2: Explore the cultural attractions of the region, such as Madai Caves and Rajat Prapat Waterfall. Day 3: Trek to Dhupgarh Point and enjoy the scenic views of the Satpura Range. Day 4: Wildlife safari in Satpura National Park. Day 5: Relax and indulge in water activities at Tawa Reservoir. Day 6: Trek to Chota Mahadeo or Madai Waterfall. Day 7: Departure from Satpura National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Satpura National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bafla: This dish is a traditional delicacy of the region, made with wheat and ghee.

  2. Bhutte ki Khees: This is a popular street food in the region made with grated corn and spices.

  3. Poha: This is a simple yet flavorful dish made with flattened rice and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Sharbat: This is a sweet rose or mango syrup that is mixed with water and ice to make a refreshing drink.

  2. Lassi: A traditional yogurt-based drink that is perfect for beating the heat.

Where to Stay in Satpura National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pachmarhi: This hill station has a wide range of accommodation options such as hotels, guesthouses, and resorts.

  2. Madhai: This is a popular location for wildlife safaris, and there are several lodges and campsites in the area.

  3. Churna: This is an off-beat location near Satpura National Park and is known for its eco-tourism initiatives. There are a few homestays in the area that offer a unique local experience.

What to Buy in Satpura National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Satpura National Park is known for its tribal art and handicrafts. You can buy beautiful paintings, sculptures, and handcrafted items as souvenirs.

  2. Natural Products: The region is known for its spice plantations, and you can buy a variety of natural spices and herbs such as turmeric, coriander, and cumin.

Local Culture and Customs of Satpura National Park

The region is home to several indigenous tribes such as Gond and Baiga. The locals are known for their rich cultural heritage and traditional lifestyle. It is important to respect their customs and traditions while visiting the area.

Best Time to Visit Satpura National Park

The best time to visit Satpura National Park is between October and June. The weather is pleasant during this time, and wildlife sightings are more frequent. Monsoon season (July-September) is not recommended for visiting the park as the area can get flooded and safaris are not conducted during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Satpura National Park

Do:

  1. Carry sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable shoes.

  2. Respect the local culture and customs.

  3. Follow the instructions of the safari guide during the wildlife safari.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the park.

  2. Feed the animals or disturb their natural habitat.

  3. Smoke or light fires in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Satpura National Park

  1. Always follow the instructions of the safari guide and avoid venturing into restricted areas.

  2. Do not wander off alone in the park.

  3. Carry a first-aid kit and emergency contact numbers.

Health in Satpura National Park

  1. Drink only bottled water.

  2. Carry necessary medication and inform your guide about any medical conditions.

  3. Be cautious of insect bites and apply insect repellent.

Getting to Satpura National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Satpura National Park

  1. Raja Bhoj Airport, Bhopal (180 km)

  2. Jabalpur Airport (245 km)

Bus Stations Near Satpura National Park

  1. Pachmarhi Bus Stand (10 km)

  2. Churna Bus Stand (25 km)

Other Options

  1. Nearest Railway Station: Pipariya Railway Station (47 km)

  2. Private Taxis and Cabs: Available from major cities in Madhya Pradesh.

How to Get Around in Satpura National Park

Private taxis and cabs are available for hire to explore the area. You can also opt for local buses or rent a bike or scooter to explore the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Satpura National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bhopal: The capital city of Madhya Pradesh is a great place to explore the local culture, history, and architecture.

  2. Kanha National Park: This is another popular wildlife sanctuary in Madhya Pradesh known for its tiger sightings and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

Satpura National Park is a beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of wildlife, culture, and adventure. Plan your trip to this beautiful wildlife reserve and experience the beauty of Central India like never before.
