Introduction to Khajuraho

Khajuraho is a small town in Madhya Pradesh that is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage Site: the Khajuraho Group of Monuments. These 85 temples, built between the 9th and 11th century CE, depict the finest art and architecture of the Chandela dynasty.

The town is set in a peaceful backdrop of forests, lavish green fields and streams, rendering it an ideal travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Khajuraho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khajuraho Group of Monuments: This group of temples exemplifies the stunning artistry found during medieval India. It is a primary attraction for its carved rock art and sculpture.
  • Archaeological Museum: This museum preserves artifacts and remnants of structures discovered during excavations carried out around the site of the Khajuraho Group of Monuments.
  • Ajaigarh Fort: This fort, some 80 km away from Khajuraho, reflects on the grandiose aura of the Chandela Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Khajuraho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Panna National Park: home to tigers, panthers, bears, and deer, this park is just 35 km from Khajuraho.
  • Benisagar Lake: This serene lake is ideal for boating and picnics.
  • Ken Gharial Sanctuary: This sanctuary, home to Indian crocodiles, is a unique attraction.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khajuraho

  • Pandav Falls: Amidst the lush greenery, these falls are best suited for a family hike or a professional trail run.
  • Raneh Falls: Situated on the Ken river, these falls offer a perfect route for a mountainous hike.

Best Things to Do in Khajuraho: Activities and Tours

  • Light and Sound Show: This shows the story of Khajuraho and the Chandela Dynasty, narrated through illuminated monuments and light shows.
  • Ayurvedic Massage: What better to treat yourself than with an Ayurvedic massage? Unwind from stress with the traditional Indian healing technique while surrounded by the scenic landscape.
  • Taj Museum in Agra: Discover the architectural grandeur of India’s most famous monument, The Royal Palace, The 400 years old precinct had been recreated.

Food and Drink Scene of Khajuraho

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Bafla: A local savory dish made of lentils and wheat, is a must-try for all visitors.
  • Bhutte Ki Khees: A delicious mixture of corn and spices, this dish is best enjoyed with bread and Indian spices.
  • Jalebi: A sweet dish made of wheat flour, this dessert perfectly complements the savory dishes of Khajuraho.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A yogurt-based drink, popularly enjoyed for its refreshing taste.
  • Bhang: A drink made of milk and canna leaves, known to provide a mild to high intoxication for its consumers.
  • Fruit Juices: A healthy yet tasty option for those looking for non-alcoholic drinks in the town.

Where to Stay in Khajuraho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Surya: A mixture of traditional and modern architecture, the hotel offers facilities such as swimming pools.
  • Hotel Chandela: This hotel showcases modern amenities while simultaneously providing an insight into Indian heritage.
  • Usha Bundela Resort: With recreational activities like horse-riding and spas, this resort is the perfect blend of rest and recreation.

What to Buy in Khajuraho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chanderi sarees: This traditional garment is the perfect souvenir to take back home.
  • Gems and jewelry: Khajuraho is known for its semi-precious and precious jewelry, which also makes for gifting options.
  • Carpets and handicrafts: Perfect for decoration, carpets and handicrafts make great household gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Khajuraho

  • Khajuraho’s religious and moral principles demand tourists wear appropriate clothing, particularly when visiting temples and holy sites.
  • Visitors should always seek to remove their shoes before entering sacred sites.
  • Visitors should always seek to provide respect and decorum during ceremonies and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Khajuraho

The best time to visit Khajuraho is during the winter months from October to March when the temperature stays around 20ºC to 30ºC. In summers, the temperature rises to 40ºC and more, making it difficult for travelers to enjoy their visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khajuraho

Do:

  • Wear appropriate clothing while visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Take your shoes off before entering sacred sites.
  • Engage in the town’s traditional and cultural activities.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of a person without their consent.
  • Consume illicit drugs or substances;
  • Disrespectful to religious customs and moralities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khajuraho

Khajuraho is a relatively safe place. However, there might be minor issues on the streets of the town. Visitors are advised to exercise caution and avoid walking alone outside during the night.

Health in Khajuraho

With the prevalence of pandemics and diseases, travelers should approach the town with caution. It is best to take precautionary measures against respiratory and viral infections by carrying masks, sanitizers, and regular temperature checks.

Getting to Khajuraho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khajuraho

The nearest airport to Khajuraho is Khajuraho Domestic Airport, located approximately 2 km from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Khajuraho

Khajuraho Bus station connects to major cities of India such as Delhi and Agra and is well maintained.

Other Options

Another option available for travelers wanting to visit Khajuraho is through railways, with services like the ‘Mahamana Express’ offering both foreign and domestic travel services.

How to Get Around in Khajuraho

Renting a motorbike or hiring a taxi is the standard way of getting around Khajuraho. Auto-rickshaws are also available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Khajuraho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Orchha: Rich in history and culture, this little town showcases the grandeur of the Bundela Dynasty.
  • Kanha National Park: Known for its tigers and leopards. One can indulge in adventure activities like trekking, camping, and jungle safaris.
  • Allahabad: Located on the confluence of the Ganges and the Yamuna, this city holds a religious and eminent historical significance.

Conclusion

Khajuraho, with its historical and cultural significance and natural beauty, is a place that should not be missed on the Indian traveler’s itinerary. With this comprehensive guide in hand, visitors can experience Khajuraho in its totality. Be it art and history or adventure and nature, Khajuraho is bound to leave an indelible mark on its visitors.
