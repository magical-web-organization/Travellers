Introduction to Damoh

Located in the Jabalpur-Sagar Division of Madhya Pradesh, India, Damoh is a small town known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. It is situated in the plains and is surrounded by the Vindhya Range, which makes it a popular destination for hiking and outdoor activities. Apart from its natural attractions, Damoh is also home to several historically significant sites, including museums and ancient temples.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Damoh, we will take you through the town’s top attractions, hiking trails, culinary landscape, cultural customs, transportation options, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Damoh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kundalpur Jain Temple: This ancient temple, located on a hillock, is a significant pilgrimage site for Jains. It has several beautiful sculptures, including a unique 24 feet high statue of Lord Adinath.

  • Rani Durgavati Museum: Dedicated to the great queen of Gondwana, Rani Durgavati, this museum houses several artifacts and exhibits showcasing the area’s history and culture.

  • Pisanhari Ki Madiya: It is a historical site that dates back to the Mauryan period. It is a rock-cut cave that is believed to be the abode of Pisanhari, a Jain nun who attained enlightenment here.

Natural Attractions of Damoh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sonagiri: It is a hillock with 77 Jain temples carved out of stone. It offers a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and is an ideal place for nature lovers and hikers.

  • Chausath Yogini Temple: Situated on a hilltop, this 10th-century temple is dedicated to the 64 Yoginis or female deities. It is a picturesque location that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Tendukheda: It is a small town near Damoh that is renowned for its handmade silk sarees. A trip to Tendukheda is a must-do for shopaholics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Damoh

  • Sonagiri Trek: It is a moderately challenging trek that takes you through the quaint villages, dense forests and offers a panoramic view of the Vindhya Range.

  • Kundalpur Trek: It is an easy trek that takes you through the picturesque countryside to the Kundalpur Jain Temple.

Best Things to Do in Damoh: Activities and Tours

  • Attend the Damoh Utsav: Held every year, the Damoh Utsav is a cultural extravaganza that showcases the town’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Take a Heritage Walking Tour: Explore Damoh’s rich history and culture by taking a heritage walking tour.

  • Visit the Local Markets: Shop for souvenirs, handicrafts, and local products at the bustling markets of Damoh.

Week-Long Itinerary for Damoh

Day 1: Arrive in Damoh and visit the Kundalpur Jain Temple.

Day 2: Visit the Rani Durgavati Museum and explore the town’s heritage sites.

Day 3: Go for a Sonagiri trek.

Day 4: Visit the Chausath Yogini Temple and explore Tendukheda.

Day 5: Attend the Damoh Utsav.

Day 6: Take a heritage walking tour of the town.

Day 7: Visit the local markets and shop for local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Damoh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Bafla: It is a local delicacy made from steamed wheat balls and lentil curry. It is one of the most popular dishes in Damoh.

  • Ladoo: It is a sweet dish made from flour, sugar, and ghee. It is a must-try dish in Damoh.

  • Kachori: It is a crispy, deep-fried snack filled with spicy potatoes, onions, and peas.

Local Beverages

  • Sattu: It is a wholesome drink made from ground roasted gram flour mixed with water/milk, sugar/honey, and spices.

  • Thandai: A refreshing drink made with milk, ground nuts, and a mix of spices.

Where to Stay in Damoh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Swarda: A budget hotel located close to the town center.

  • Hotel Shree Laxmi Palace: A mid-range hotel with modern amenities and an excellent location.

  • Shri Bajrang Jain Dharamshala: A budget-friendly guesthouse mainly for Jain pilgrims.

What to Buy in Damoh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tendu Leaves: These dried leaves are used to make beedis or traditional cigarettes.

  • Handicrafts: Shop for beautiful handicrafts made by local artisans, including pottery, bamboo crafts, and beadwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Damoh

Damoh is home to several communities, including Jain, Brahmin, Gond, and Muslims. It is a religiously diverse town where people of different backgrounds live together peacefully. Visitors are expected to dress conservatively and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Damoh

The best time to visit Damoh is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Damoh

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Sample local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Litter or damage natural and historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Damoh

Damoh is a safe town to visit. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Damoh

Visitors are advised to carry prescribed medications and consult a doctor in case of any medical emergencies.
