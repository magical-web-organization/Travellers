Introduction to Panna National Park

Welcome to Panna National Park, a paradise for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers in India. Located in the Jabalpur-Sagar Division, in Madhya Pradesh, Panna National Park covers an area of 542 square kilometers and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including Bengal tigers, leopards, deer, antelopes, monkeys, reptiles, and birds. Besides, the national park also has historical significance as it houses the ruins of ancient temples and forts.

List of Places to Visit in Panna National Park

If you’re planning a trip to Panna National Park, here are the must-visit attractions you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pandava Caves: Visit the five ancient rock-cut caves that date back to the Buddhist period, located in the midst of natural surroundings.

  • Panna Mines: Explore the diamond mines that are more than 400 years old and learn about the history of the diamond industry in India.

  • Ken Gharial Sanctuary: The Ken River is the only habitat in India for long-nosed gharials, and this sanctuary is a must-visit for nature lovers.

Natural Attractions of Panna National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pandav Falls: Witness the beauty of the cascading waterfall that plunges from a height of 30 meters and listen to the roaring sound of water amidst the serene nature.

  • Panna National Park Jungle Safari: Enjoy the jeep and elephant safaris in the national park that give you a chance to see the Bengal tigers, leopards, and other wild animals up-close.

  • Raneh Falls: Admire the natural beauty of the spectacular canyon that was formed by volcanic eruptions and erosion.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Panna National Park

Panna National Park offers several trekking routes that let you explore the serene forests and scenic views. Some popular ones are:

  • Ken River Trek: This trek takes you through the buffer zone of Panna National Park and the Ken River, offering a chance to spot various birds and animals.

  • Panna to Khajuraho Trek: This trek starts from Panna National Park and ends in Khajuraho, passing through ancient temples, villages, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Panna National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and trekking, Panna National Park offers many exciting activities and tours. Here are a few of them:

  • Photography Tour: Explore the serene beauty of Panna National Park with a professional photographer who will guide you through the best locations to capture some amazing wildlife shots.

  • Bird Watching Tour: Panna National Park offers a chance to spot some rare and exotic bird species, and this tour is perfect for bird enthusiasts.

  • Forest Camping: Spend a night in the heart of the forest, under the stars, and listen to the sounds of the wild animals.

Food and Drink Scene of Panna National Park

Panna National Park offers a unique blend of local and traditional Indian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dal Bafla: A popular local dish made of steamed wheat balls, served with lentil soup.

  • Palak Puri: Crispy puris made of spinach and served with potato curry.

  • Keema Chura: Made of minced meat and puffed rice, this dish is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Enjoy a cup of Masala Chai, made of tea leaves and Indian spices, including ginger, cardamom, and cinnamon.

  • Lassi: A thick yogurt drink with flavors like mango, strawberry, and rose.

Where to Stay in Panna National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Panna National Park offers several accommodation options catering to all budgets and needs. Here are a few popular ones.

  • Ken River Lodge: A luxury eco-resort that offers cottages and tents, situated right next to the Ken River.

  • Panna Tiger Resort: A comfortable resort located in the buffer zone of Panna National Park, offering cottages with modern amenities.

  • Green Park Resort: A budget-friendly resort that offers tents and Rajasthan-style huts, located close to the national park.

What to Buy in Panna National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Panna National Park offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Handicrafts: Buy traditional handicrafts, including hand-woven carpets, jewelry, and pottery.

  • Khajuraho Miniatures: Buy miniature sculptures of the Khajuraho temples, made of stone or wood.

  • Diamonds: Panna is famous for its diamonds, and you can buy jewelry made of authentic Panna diamonds in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Panna National Park

Panna National Park is rich in culture and customs, deeply rooted in the history of Madhya Pradesh. Here are some local customs and traditions you should know.

  • Language: The official language of Panna National Park is Hindi, but many locals also speak English.

  • Festivals: Some popular festivals celebrated in Panna National Park include Diwali, Holi, and Dussehra.

Best Time to Visit Panna National Park

The best time to visit Panna National Park is between November and June when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is easily spotted.

Do’s and Don’ts of Panna National Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts that you should follow while visiting Panna National Park.

Do:

  • Carry a valid identity proof and permit while entering the national park.

  • Follow the instructions of the knowledgeable guide during the wildlife safari.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink alcohol while inside the national park.

  • Litter the national park and disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Panna National Park

While Panna National Park is relatively safe, you should take some precautions to avoid any mishap.

  • Carry insect repellents and sunscreen lotion.

  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes for trekking and outdoor activities.

Health in Panna National Park

It’s important to maintain your hygiene and take some precautions to avoid any health issues while visiting Panna National Park.

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food.

  • Carry necessary medicines and a first-aid kit.

Getting to Panna National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Panna National Park is well-connected by air, rail, and road network. Here are some options you can consider:

Airports Near Panna National Park

  • Jabalpur Airport: The nearest airport to Panna National Park, located at a distance of 200 km.

  • Khajuraho Airport: The second nearest airport to Panna National Park, located at a distance of 45 km.

Bus Stations Near Panna National Park

  • Panna Bus Stand: The nearest bus stand to Panna National Park, located at a distance of 25 km.

  • Khajuraho Bus Stand: Another popular bus stand located close to Panna National Park, at a distance of 45 km.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: You can hire a private taxi from Jabalpur, Khajuraho, or other nearby cities to reach Panna National Park.

How to Get Around in Panna National Park

You can explore Panna National Park by taking a jeep or elephant safari or by trekking on the designated trails. It’s advisable to hire a knowledgeable guide who can help you identify the wildlife, flora, and fauna and navigate through the national park.

Where to Go Next from Panna National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can check out after exploring Panna National Park:

  • Khajuraho: Known for its UNESCO World Heritage Site temples and erotic sculptures.

  • Bandhavgarh National Park: A popular tiger reserve located at a distance of 180 km from Panna National Park.

  • Sanchi: Known for its ancient Buddhist monuments and stupas, located at a distance of 350 km from Panna National Park.

Conclusion

Panna National Park is a stunning destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, wildlife, history, and culture. With this guide, you can plan your trip to Panna National Park with ease and enjoy every moment of your adventure. Remember to respect the local customs and traditions, follow safety tips, and carry back fond memories of this wonderful destination.
