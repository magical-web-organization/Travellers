Introduction to Kanha National Park

Located in the heart of Madhya Pradesh, Kanha National Park is a stunning preserved area where nature thrives in its full glory. The park is spread over an area of 940 square kilometers and is renowned for its lush green forests, diverse wildlife, and scenic landscapes. The park is home to several endangered species, including the Bengal tiger, Indian leopard, and Barasingha, making it a significant landmark in India’s conservation efforts. Additionally, travelers can experience the local culture and indulge in various outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Kanha National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kanha Museum: The museum is an excellent place to learn about the rich flora and fauna of the park, as well as the local Gond culture. The museum features a vast collection of flora and fauna specimens and offers an insight into the park’s history and geology.

Bamni Dadar: Perched at an altitude of 1110 feet, Bamni Dadar offers a panoramic view of the jungles of Kanha. This beautiful spot is also known as Sunset Point, where visitors can witness the sunset across the horizon.

Natural Attractions of Kanha National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jungle Safari: One of the must-do activities in Kanha National Park is a jungle safari, which is the best way to explore the park. Visitors can opt for a jeep or elephant safari to immerse themselves in the lush green forests and spot wildlife up close.

Kanha Meadows: The grassy meadows of Kanha offer stunning views of the surrounding hills and are ideal for picnicking, stargazing, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park boasts several hiking trails and biking routes, which are ideal for adventurous travelers. Visitors can opt for a guided hike or bike ride to explore the park and experience its biodiversity. Some popular routes include the Kanha Nature Trails, Shravan Tal Trail, and Kanha Cycle Trails.

Best Things to Do in Kanha National Park: Activities and Tours

Bamni Dadar Sunrise Tour: A visit to the Bamni Dadar during sunrise is a must-do experience in Kanha National Park. This tour offers a unique experience of witnessing the beautiful sunrise amidst the serene landscapes of Kanha.

Bird Watching Tour: Kanha National Park is home to over 250 species of birds, making it a bird watcher’s paradise. Visitors can opt for a bird-watching tour and spot beautiful birds like the Indian Roller, Crested Serpent Eagle, Indian Pitta, and many more.

Food and Drink Scene of Kanha National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Bafla: It is a local dish made with wheat dough, cooked over charcoal, and served with dal and ghee. It is a must-try dish for food lovers.

Sukha Mutton: Sukha Mutton is a popular meat dish in Kanha, made with tender pieces of mutton cooked with traditional Indian spices.

Local Beverages

Toddy: Toddy is a drink made from palms, and it is popular among locals. It is a mildly alcoholic drink with a sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Kanha National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The park offers several accommodation options to suit various budgets and preferences. Some popular options include Kanha Earth Lodge, Kanha Jungle Lodge, and Tuli Tiger Corridor. Additionally, visitors can opt for various campsites and homestays for a more authentic experience.

What to Buy in Kanha National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take back some souvenirs like handmade tribal crafts, kantha embroidery, and tribal paintings that are unique to Kanha National Park. Additionally, they can also pick up local products like honey, bamboo baskets, and organic produce from the markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanha National Park

The local culture of Kanha revolves around the Gond tribe, who have been the park’s traditional inhabitants for centuries. Visitors can experience the tribal culture and their lifestyle through various activities like dance performances, crafts, and cultural shows.

Best Time to Visit Kanha National Park

The best time to visit Kanha National Park is between November and June, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the park remains closed during the monsoon season, i.e., from July to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanha National Park

Do:

  • Follow the rules and regulations of the park strictly.
  • Hire a licensed guide for jungle safaris and other tours.
  • Wear light-colored clothes for visibility.
  • Carry water, a hat, and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the local flora and fauna in any way.
  • Enter the park without a permit.
  • Disturb the peace and tranquility of the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors are advised to follow the rules and regulations of the park and take the necessary precautions while trekking or hiking.

Health in Kanha National Park

Visitors are advised to carry medical supplies and take the necessary vaccinations before visiting Kanha National Park.

Getting to Kanha National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kanha National Park

The nearest airports to Kanha National Park are Jabalpur Airport (170 km), Raipur Airport (250 km), and Nagpur Airport (280 km).

Bus Stations Near Kanha National Park

The park is well-connected by several bus routes, and visitors can opt for various buses and coaches to reach the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a private taxi or a rented car to travel to Kanha National Park from nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Kanha National Park

Visitors can hire a jeep, elephant, or bicycle to explore the park. Additionally, guides and chauffeurs can also be hired for a more comfortable experience.

Where to Go Next from Kanha National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations near Kanha National Park include Bandhavgarh National Park, Pench National Park, Pachmarhi, Bhedaghat, and Jabalpur.

Conclusion

Kanha National Park is an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience nature, wildlife, and culture up close. With the right planning and preparation, visitors can have a fulfilling and enriching experience in this beautiful national park.
