A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park is a must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts, nature lovers, and adventure seekers. Located in the Madhya Pradesh state of India, it is one of the most popular national parks in the country. Whether you are interested in exploring historical sites, witnessing the diversity of flora and fauna, hiking, biking, or simply enjoying the scenic beauty of the park, Bandhavgarh has something for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Bandhavgarh National Park and make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park covers an area of 105 square kilometers and is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna. It is situated in the Vindhya Hills of central India and is known for its high density of tigers. In addition to tigers, the park is home to a variety of other animals, including leopards, wild dogs, sloth bears, Indian bison, and sambar deer. The park is also home to more than 250 species of birds, making it a bird watcher’s paradise.

The park has a rich history and is home to several ancient temples and forts. The Bandhavgarh Fort, which is located within the park, is believed to be more than 2000 years old and has a fascinating story to tell. The park is also home to several caves that have been home to people since prehistoric times.

List of Places to Visit in Bandhavgarh National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bandhavgarh Fort - A historic fort inside the park that offers a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.
  2. Baghel Museum - A museum located in the park that houses artifacts from the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Bandhavgarh National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bandhavgarh Hill - A hill that provides breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  2. Chakradhara - A scenic spot where you can enjoy a picnic and watch the sunset.
  3. Climbers Point - A rock formation that is ideal for rock climbing and offers stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bandhavgarh National Park

  1. Bandhavgarh Fort Hike - A scenic hike that takes you to the historic Bandhavgarh Fort.
  2. Climbers Point Hike - A challenging hike that takes you to the top of the Climbers Point rock formation.
  3. Bamboo Machaan Hike - A moderate hike that takes you to an observation deck where you can watch wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Bandhavgarh National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Wildlife Safari - A must-do activity in the park, where you can see tigers and other wildlife in their natural habitat.
  2. Bird Watching - A great activity for nature lovers, Bandhavgarh is home to more than 250 species of birds.
  3. Photography Tour - A guided tour that takes you to some of the best photography spots in the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bandhavgarh National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Bandhavgarh National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bafla - A traditional dish made of wheat and served with dal and ghee.
  2. Bhutte ki Khees - A dish made of grated corn, milk, and spices.
  3. Malpua - A dessert made of flour, milk, and sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

  1. Masala Chai - A spiced tea that is popular in the region.
  2. Lassi - A yogurt-based drink that is refreshing during hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Bandhavgarh National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Taj Mahua Kothi - A luxury hotel that offers a comfortable stay with modern amenities.
  2. Pugdundee Safari - A boutique hotel that is eco-friendly and located within the park.
  3. Bandhavgarh Jungle Lodge - A comfortable stay option that is perfect for the budget traveler.

What to Buy in Bandhavgarh National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts - Bandhavgarh is famous for its handicrafts made of bamboo and cane.
  2. Leather Products - The region is known for producing high-quality leather products.

Local Culture and Customs of Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh is home to several indigenous communities, whose customs and traditions are an integral part of the park’s cultural heritage. The region has a rich history, and it is important to respect the local customs while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Bandhavgarh National Park

The best time to visit Bandhavgarh National Park is from October to June. The park remains closed during the monsoon season, which lasts from July to September. Visiting during the winter months is ideal for spotting wildlife, while the summer months offer pleasant weather for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bandhavgarh National Park

Do:

  1. Follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  2. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  3. Hire a guide for wildlife safaris and trekking.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the park or disturb the wildlife.
  2. Approach wild animals on foot or in a vehicle.
  3. Wear bright colors that may scare the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park is safe for visitors, but it is important to follow the park’s rules and regulations. Avoid going off-trail, especially at night. In case of an emergency, contact the park authorities or your hotel.

Health in Bandhavgarh National Park

Make sure to carry insect repellent and sunscreen, as mosquito bites and sunburns are common in the park. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for diseases like typhoid and hepatitis A before visiting India. Drink bottled water and avoid uncooked food to prevent foodborne illnesses.

Getting to Bandhavgarh National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bandhavgarh National Park

The closest airport to Bandhavgarh National Park is Jabalpur Airport, located 170 km away. Other nearby airports include Khajuraho Airport (240 km) and Raipur Airport (290 km).

Bus Stations Near Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh is well-connected by roads to nearby cities like Jabalpur, Umaria, and Katni. Frequent bus services are available from these cities to Bandhavgarh.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can be rented from nearby cities to reach Bandhavgarh. Train services are also available from major cities to Umaria and Katni, which are the closest railway stations to Bandhavgarh.

How to Get Around in Bandhavgarh National Park

Jeep safaris are the most popular way to explore the park. Safari services are organized by the park authorities and private operators. Bikes and bicycles can also be rented for exploring the park on your own.

Where to Go Next from Bandhavgarh National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kanha National Park - Another popular national park in Madhya Pradesh known for its tigers and biodiverse flora and fauna.
  2. Panna National Park - A UNESCO World Heritage Site that is famous for its diamond mines and tiger reserve.
  3. Khajuraho - A historic town famous for its ancient temples and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Bandhavgarh National Park offers a unique combination of natural and cultural attractions that are sure to leave you spellbound.
