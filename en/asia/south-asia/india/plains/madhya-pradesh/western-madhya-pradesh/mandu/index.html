Introduction to Mandu

India has a lot of hidden gems that continue to fascinate travelers all over the world, and Mandu in the Western region of Madhya Pradesh is one of them. This small city is steeped in history and is home to some of India’s most fascinating ruins and ancient architecture. Mandu, also known as Mandavgad, was once the capital of the Malwa Sultanate during the 15th century and reflects a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture. This charming city has a rich heritage and is decorated with magnificent forts, palaces, tombs, gardens, and monuments. Mandu is an excellent destination for travelers who crave something different, peaceful, and historically fascinating.

List of Places to Visit in Mandu

Mandu is packed with historical monuments, enchanting palaces, and serene lakes that are sure to leave you captivated. Here are the top places to visit in Mandu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jahaz Mahal

Jahaz Mahal, also known as the ‘Ship Palace’, is one of the most impressive structures in Mandu. The palace reflects the unique fusion of Afghan and Hindu architecture and is situated between two artificial lakes. It used to be a harem palace during the reign of Sultan Ghiyas-ud-din-Khilji and was designed to resemble a ship floating on the water. The interior of the palace has intricately-designed apartments, pavilions, and arched gateways.

Hindola Mahal

Hindola Mahal, also known as the ‘Swing Palace’, is a beautiful Palace that displays a fusion of Afghan and Persian architectural styles. The palace’s name comes from the unique sloping walls that lean outward, which resemble a swinging motion. The palace was constructed during the reign of Hoshang Shah and was used for state ceremonies and royal celebrations.

Hoshang’s Tomb

Mandu is famous for its impressive tombs, with Hoshang’s Tomb being one of the most significant. Hoshang’s Tomb was constructed during the reign of Hoshang Shah and is one of the earliest examples of the architectural style used during the Sultanate period. The tomb is situated on the banks of the Tapti River and showcases intricate designs and carvings that reflect a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic styles.

Natural Attractions of Mandu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jahaz Mahal Sunrise Tour

Visitors to Mandu can take advantage of the epic sunrise tours that take place at Jahaz Mahal. The tour guides will take you through the palace, explaining the history and architecture in detail. The sunrise tour also provides breathtaking views of the two lakes surrounding the palace and the scenic surroundings.

Mandu Hiking Trails

Mandu is home to some excellent hiking trails that take you through the city’s most exceptional natural landscapes. The Mandu hiking trails provide visitors with an excellent opportunity to experience the city’s serene natural beauty up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mandu

Hiking Trails

  1. Roopmati’s Pavilion Hike: A 4 km hike that takes you up to Roopmati’s Pavilion, with views of the valleys and a stunning sunset/sunrise view.

  2. Baz Bahadur Palace Hike: A 3 km hike that leads to the Baz Bahadur Palace, providing visitors with stunning views of the Mandu plateau and the Vindhya hills.

  3. Jahaz Mahal Hike: A 1.5 km hike that takes you to Jahaz Mahal, with views of the two lakes surrounding the palace.

Biking Routes

  1. The Mandu Plateau Loop: A 25 km bike ride that takes you through the Mandu plateau and past some of the city’s most significant historical landmarks.

  2. The Mandu Fort Loop: A 20 km bike ride that takes you through the city’s forts and palaces, providing visitors with a unique perspective of the city’s history.

Best Things to Do in Mandu: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore the city on foot or bike: Mandu is best explored on foot or bike, giving visitors an opportunity to see the city up close and personal.

  2. Take a sunset/sunrise tour of Jahaz Mahal: The sunrise tour of Jahaz Mahal is an unforgettable experience, providing stunning views of the surrounding lakes and valleys.

  3. Visit the Hindola Mahal and Hoshang’s Tomb: The Hindola Mahal and Hoshang’s tomb are two of the most impressive architectural wonders in Mandu.

Food and Drink Scene of Mandu

The food scene of Mandu represents a fusion of Indian and Mughlai-style cuisine. Visitors to Mandu can find a variety of dishes like Chaats, Samosas, Kebabs, and Biryani, which are some of the most sought-after delicacies in North India.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Keema Biryani: A delicious biryani made with minced meat and fragrant Indian spices.

  2. Bhutte Ki Kees: A unique delicacy made with grated sweet corn that is slowly cooked to perfection with cream and spices.

  3. Samosa: A classic Indian snack stuffed with spiced vegetables or meat filling and deep-fried until crispy.

Local Beverages

  1. Sharbat: A refreshing drink made with a mixture of fruits, herbs, and spices mixed with water and sugar.

  2. Chai: A staple beverage in India, made from black tea, milk, and spices.

Where to Stay in Mandu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Mandu can choose from a variety of accommodation options, depending on their budget and preferences. Here are the top-rated options for accommodations in Mandu:

  1. Malwa Resort: A luxurious resort that provides guests with excellent amenities and services.

  2. Hotel Malwa Retreat: An affordable option for travelers who are on a budget but still looking for quality accommodation.

  3. Jhira Bagh Palace: A former royal palace that has been converted into a hotel, providing guests with a unique experience.

What to Buy in Mandu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Mandu can purchase a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home. The city is famous for handwoven textiles, pottery, and handicrafts. Some of the best shopping options in Mandu are:

  1. Local markets: Visitors can visit the local markets in Mandu and find an assortment of textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts.

  2. Mandu Haat: A weekly bazaar that takes place on Thursdays in Mandu, providing visitors with a unique shopping experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Mandu

Mandu is a predominantly Islamic city, and visitors to the city must respect the local religious customs and dress appropriately while visiting the city’s mosques and shrines. Visitors should also respect the city’s cultural heritage and be mindful of littering and maintaining cleanliness in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Mandu

The best time to visit Mandu is during the winter months, between October and March. The weather during this period is pleasant, making it easier for travelers to explore the city’s outdoor attractions and landmarks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mandu

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites.
  2. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  3. Explore the city’s natural landscapes and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public spaces or damage historical landmarks.
  2. Disrespect the local customs and attire inappropriately.
  3. Engage in any illegal activities or substance abuse.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mandu

Mandu is a generally safe city, with a low crime rate. Visitors should use common sense and be vigilant while exploring the city’s tourist areas and avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuables.

Health in Mandu

Visitors to Mandu should take necessary precautions to avoid any health-related issues. Travelers should stay hydrated, carry insect repellent, and avoid drinking the tap water.

Getting to Mandu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mandu

The nearest airport to Mandu is the Indore airport, which is approximately 100 km from the city. Visitors can use the airport’s taxi or bus services to reach Mandu.

Bus Stations Near Mandu

Mandu is well-connected to a variety of cities via the bus network. Bus services are available from Indore, Ujjain, and Dhar.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis and private cars to reach Mandu from the neighboring cities.

How to Get Around in Mandu

Mandu is best explored on foot or bike, providing visitors with a unique experience of the city’s natural landscapes and historical landmarks. Visitors can also use taxis or rickshaws to explore the city’s urban areas.

Conclusion

Mandu in Madhya Pradesh is a unique and mesmerizing destination that reflects India’s rich cultural heritage and architectural wonders. Mandu is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an escape from the bustling city life and exploring India’s hidden gems.
