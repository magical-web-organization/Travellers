Khargone, a city in the Western Madhya Pradesh region of India is a hidden gem with a rich cultural and historical significance. The city is famous for the countless temples, palaces, mountains, and picturesque views that draw tourists year-round. In this guide, we will explore the best things to do in Khargone, along with tips and recommendations for making the most of your trip.

Introduction to Khargone

Khargone is a city in Western Madhya Pradesh, in Plains, in India, in South Asia, in Asia. It is situated on the banks of the Kunda river, also known as the Bhima river. The region is famous for the Maratha and Mughal architecture, alongside forts, palaces, temples, and historical monuments. The fantastic views across the city and stunning landscapes attract tourists from all over the world. One of the most popular events that occur in Khargone is Navratri, which is celebrated with much grandeur for nine days.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Khargone boasts a rich cultural history, with numerous museums showcasing the bygone era. The National Museum of Khargone showcases the various tribes and their cultures through a variety of artifacts like clothes, jewelry, and handicrafts. The Rajwada Palace Complex is another must-visit cultural attraction; it was the home of the Holkar Dynasty for many years, and today, it is a museum that features a range of artifacts and exhibitions related to the royal family. Gommat Giri is an ancient shrine located in the hilly terrain that provides insight into the Jain culture.

Natural Attractions of Khargone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khargone is home to a multitude of natural attractions, including the Satpura Mountain ranges, the Narmada River, and the Kunda Caves, showcasing the wonders of nature while providing a range of exciting outdoor adventures, including trekking and rafting. For outdoor adventurers, this is the ultimate destination, with opportunities for paragliding, hot air ballooning, nature walks, trekking, and other adrenaline-pumping activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khargone

Khargone is famous for its scenic beauty and natural attractions, so the best way to explore the region is to hit the trails. One of the famous trails is the Pandavaleni Trek, a 6km hike that takes visitors through beautiful forests, waterfalls, and caves, while Rautpura Sahadev Trek is a 14km trail that provides a mesmerizing view of the Satpura mountain range. Cycling enthusiasts would enjoy the Kunda to Indira Sagar Dam trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Khargone

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Khargone is complete without indulging in the local delicacies. The street food scene in Khargone is vibrant, and tourists can enjoy snacks such as poha, kachori, jalebi, and samosas. For those who are looking for authentic dishes, try the popular dal bafla, a local rice dish served with batla, a dry lentil dish. The city is also famous for its ‘kachori’, a savory pastry stuffed with spice mix.

Local Beverages

When in Khargone, don’t forget to taste the local hot beverage called Chai, which is a perfect start to the day. For those looking for an uplifting experience, try Sula Vineyards, which is made with grapes grown locally. They’re also delicious smoothies made with local fruits like Mango and Guava.

Where to Stay in Khargone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Khargone offers a range of accommodation options catering to different tastes and budgets. Options range from budget hotels and lodges to premium hotels and resorts. Some of the most popular hotels are the Ahilya Fort, located on the banks of the Narmada River, and the Hotel Raj Palace, providing easy access to the city center.

What to Buy in Khargone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenir shops in Khargone sell curios and handmade items, including silver jewelry, carpets, pottery, wooden and metal crafts, and cotton textiles. However, the most famous item to bring home from Khargone is the Maheshwari saree - a six-yard long silk saree of great quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Khargone

Khargone is a city that values tradition and customs. Visitors should respect the local decorum, wear modest attire while visiting the religious shrines, and follow the cultural beliefs. The Bhordhar and Bhagoriya festivals are an essential part of the culture and offer a chance to witness traditional music and dance forms.

Best Time to Visit Khargone

The best time to visit Khargone is from October to March, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the natural scenery. The weather is pleasant, making it the perfect time to soak in the natural beauty on offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khargone

Safety and Health

Safety in Khargone

Khargone has a low crime rate, making it a safe city, but caution should be exercised, especially when venturing out of the city at night.

Health in Khargone

Khargone has well-equipped hospitals and health centers. It is advisable to carry medical insurance while traveling and ensure that all the necessary vaccinations are in place.

Getting to Khargone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Khargone is easy as the city is well-connected to other major cities. The nearest airport is the Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport in Indore, located about 100 km away. Buses are also a convenient option for tourists coming from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Khargone

Khargone has many transportation options, including buses, taxis, and rickshaws, making it easy to get around the city. Tourists can also rent bicycles or motorbikes from the numerous rental shops in the city.
