Introduction to Ratlam

Ratlam is a charming city located in the western part of Madhya Pradesh, India. This city is a perfect blend of history and modernity, with a rich cultural heritage that dates back to ancient times. Ratlam is famous for its street food, bustling bazaars, historical monuments, and scenic landscapes that make it an offbeat but fascinating travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Ratlam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kedareshwar Temple: This ancient temple is devoted to Lord Shiva and is believed to be one of the 12 most sacred Jyotirlinga shrines. The temple has intricate carvings that represent the Hindu mythology.

  • Kalika Mata Temple: This is another ancient temple dedicated to the goddess Kali. The temple has a unique architecture with a huge idol of the goddess Kali that is adorned with gold jewelry and a crown.

  • Jain Temple: The Jain Temple in Ratlam is a magnificent place of worship and features intricate carvings and a marble architecture that is awe-inspiring.

  • Ratlam Fort: This fort is an iconic landmark of Ratlam and offers a magnificent view of the city from the top. The fort dates back to the 17th century and has historical significance for the region.

Natural Attractions of Ratlam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bamnia Dam: The Bamnia Dam is a popular tourist spot for its scenic views and offers a tranquil atmosphere for travelers. Visitors can go for boating and indulge in nature walks.

  • Nalchha Park: Nalchha Park is a verdant oasis in the middle of the city and is a popular picnic spot for the locals. This park is home to a variety of flora and fauna and offers breathtaking views of the city.

  • Jhiri Lake: Jhiri Lake is a serene spot on the outskirts of Ratlam and offers picturesque views of the lake with its surrounding greenery. Visitors can go for boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ratlam

  • Ratlam-Jhabua Bike Route: A popular biking route that passes through a scenic rural countryside.

  • Ratlam-Dhodhar Trek Route: This route is a short trek that takes visitors through rocky terrain and has a picturesque view of the landscape. The trail ends at the Dhodhar Dam, which is a popular spot for picnics.

Best Things to Do in Ratlam: Activities and Tours

  • Street Food Tour: Ratlam is famous for its lip-smacking street food, and a guided tour of the local eateries is a must-do activity for food lovers.

  • Heritage Tour: A heritage tour of Ratlam’s historical monuments gives visitors an insight into the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  • Nature Walks: Ratlam has several natural trails that are perfect for nature enthusiasts who can go on a guided nature walk in groups.

Food and Drink Scene of Ratlam

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ratlami Sev: A popular street food snack made of fried chickpea noodles that are flavored with spices and herbs.

  • Poha: A traditional breakfast dish made with flattened rice, vegetables, and spices.

  • Ratlam Khaman: A soft and fluffy snack made of gram flour that is steamed and topped with chutney.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: A spicy milk tea that is a quintessential drink of India.

  • Lassi: A popular yogurt drink that is refreshing and healthy.

Where to Stay in Ratlam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Dawaat Palace: A budget hotel that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • Hotel Balaji Central: A mid-range hotel that has luxurious rooms and modern facilities.

  • Prospera Hotels and Resorts: A premium hotel chain that offers high-standard amenities and services.

What to Buy in Ratlam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ratlam Sev: The famous local snacks that come in various flavors that make an excellent souvenir.

  • Maheshwari Saree: A traditional saree that is made from handloom silk that is well-known for its vibrant colors and delicate designs.

  • Bamboo Crafts: Bamboo crafts, like watches, wall decorations, and showpieces, make excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ratlam

The culture of Ratlam is predominantly Hindu, and the local people are proud of their traditions and customs. Visitors must respect the religious and cultural norms of the city and dress modestly when visiting religious places. The city celebrates several festivals with great enthusiasm, and visitors can participate and experience the vibrant cultural heritage of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Ratlam

The best time to visit Ratlam is from October to March, where the climate is pleasant, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without the scorching heat of the summer season. During this period, the city hosts several festivals, offering visitors an insight into the local culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ratlam

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious places.
  • Sample the local cuisine and try the traditional street food.
  • Follow the cultural customs of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any form of unruly behavior that may offend the locals.
  • Hurt the sentiments of the locals by being disrespectful towards their traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ratlam

Ratlam is a safe city, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors should take standard precautions like not carrying too much cash, traveling in a group, and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Ratlam

Ratlam has several well-equipped hospitals and medical centers that offer quality healthcare facilities. Visitors should carry their prescribed medicines and take adequate precautions against water-borne diseases by drinking only bottled water.

Getting to Ratlam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ratlam

The nearest airport to Ratlam is Indore Airport, which is approximately 123 km away from the city. The airport is well-connected to all major cities in India and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Ratlam

Ratlam has excellent connectivity with several cities in Madhya Pradesh and other states through buses. Visitors can take a bus from the Ratlam Bus Station, which is located in the city center, to reach their desired destination.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the city and nearby places conveniently.

How to Get Around in Ratlam

Ratlam has several transportation options such as auto-rickshaws, taxis, and buses that make it easy for visitors to get around the city. Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ratlam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ujjain: Ujjain is a holy city located 150 km from Ratlam and is a popular pilgrimage center.

  • Mandu: Mandu is a historical town located 151 km from Ratlam and is famous for its stunning architecture and ancient monuments.

  • Indore: Indore is a bustling city located 124 km from Ratlam and is known for its rich cultural heritage, street food, and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Ratlam is an offbeat travel destination that is perfect for travelers who seek to explore the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Madhya Pradesh. This city offers a unique travel experience with its delicious street food, historical monuments, and scenic landscapes that are sure to leave visitors with unforgettable memories.
