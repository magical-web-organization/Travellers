Introduction to Dewas

Dewas is a hidden gem located in Western Madhya Pradesh, India, known for its rich culture and stunning natural beauty. It is a lesser-known tourist destination, but it is quickly gaining popularity among travelers. Dewas has a fascinating history, and the city is known for its temples, museums, and historical landmarks. It also has several outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and nature walks. Overall, Dewas is a great destination for those seeking an offbeat holiday experience.

List of Places to Visit in Dewas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kaila Devi Temple

One of the most popular religious sites in Dewas is the Kaila Devi Temple. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Kaila, and it is believed that the temple was built in the 17th century. The temple’s architecture and design are impressive, and it attracts thousands of devotees every year.

Chamunda Mata Temple

Another temple that must be on your list is the Chamunda Mata Temple. It is a shrine dedicated to the goddess Chamunda Devi, and it is located on a hill. The temple offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, and it is a great place to relax and meditate.

Dewas Museum

One of the best places to explore the history and culture of Dewas is the Dewas Museum. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s rich history. From ancient relics to modern art, there is something for every art and history lover.

Natural Attractions of Dewas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kheoni Wildlife Sanctuary

The Kheoni Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit for nature lovers. The sanctuary is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including deer, monkeys, and exotic birds like the Malabar Whistling Thrush. Visitors can take a jeep or an elephant safari to explore the sanctuary.

Kedareshwar Temple

The Kedareshwar Temple is a scenic temple situated on a hilltop. The temple offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and is a great place for a nature walk. It is also believed to be the origin of the Kshipra River, making it a significant site for those interested in Hindu mythology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dewas

Dewas has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Some popular routes include the Kheoni Wildlife Sanctuary Nature Trail, Shivgiri Trail, and the Kaila Hill Trail. These trails vary in difficulty level, so make sure to choose a route that suits your fitness level and interests.

Best Things to Do in Dewas: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the temples and museums, there are other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Dewas. Some popular options include:

  • Taking a cooking class to learn about the local cuisine
  • Visiting the nearby Bagli Fort
  • Going on a village tour to interact with the locals
  • Taking a photography tour to capture the city’s beauty

Week-Long Itinerary for Dewas

Day 1-3: Visit the Kaila Devi Temple, Chamunda Mata Temple, and Dewas Museum Day 4-5: Explore the Kheoni Wildlife Sanctuary and go on a nature walk Day 6: Take a cooking class and learn about the local cuisine Day 7: Visit Bagli Fort and go on a village tour to interact with the locals

Food and Drink Scene of Dewas

Must-Try Dishes

One of the things that you can’t miss in Dewas is its local cuisine. Some popular dishes that you must try include Bhutte ki Kees, Bhopali Gosht Korma, and Dal Bafla. These dishes are flavorful and unique to the region, giving you a true taste of Dewas.

Local Beverages

Dewas is known for its soothing and flavorful beverages. You can try masala chai, lassi, mango shake, and sugarcane juice at local food stalls and cafes while exploring the city.

Where to Stay in Dewas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Dewas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Dewas back home, then shop for handicrafts, Chanderi sarees, and local spices. These items are readily available in the markets and shops across the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Dewas

The locals of Dewas are friendly and hospitable. They enjoy engaging with visitors and sharing information about their culture and traditions. Some customs that you should be aware of include removing your shoes when entering temples and being respectful of the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dewas

The best time to visit Dewas is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and there is less humidity. The monsoon season from July to September is also a great time to visit as the city comes alive with lush greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dewas

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions
  • Remove your shoes when entering temples
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Interact with the locals and learn about their culture

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Dewas

Dewas is a relatively safe city to visit, but it is always advisable to take necessary precautions. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your belongings safe. In case of an emergency, dial 100 (police) or 108 (ambulance).

Health in Dewas

It is advisable to carry your own drinking water and avoid consuming street food if you have a sensitive stomach. Also, carry necessary medication if you have any pre-existing health conditions.

Getting to Dewas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dewas

The nearest airport to Dewas is Indore Airport, which is around 38 km away from the city. You can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Dewas.

Bus Stations Near Dewas

Dewas is well-connected by road, and there are several state-run and private buses that ply to and from the city. Some popular bus stations include Dewas Bus Station and Indore Bus Station.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Dewas Railway Station, which is well-connected to various parts of India.

How to Get Around in Dewas

Dewas has several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws, to get around the city. You can also rent a bike or a car if you want to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Dewas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations near Dewas include Indore, Ujjain, Pachmarhi, and Mandu. These places offer a mix of historical landmarks, natural beauty, and cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Dewas is a unique and offbeat destination that offers endless opportunities to explore, learn and relax. If you’re looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of city life, Dewas could be the perfect getaway for you. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and delicious cuisine, there’s something for every traveler in this hidden gem of Madhya Pradesh, India.
