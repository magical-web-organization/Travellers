Rohtak Division, located in Haryana, India, is a hidden gem for travelers seeking a glimpse into the rich and diverse culture of South Asia. This guide is designed to provide you with all the information you need to explore Rohtak Division, from the must-visit attractions to the best hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and where to stay.

Introduction to Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division is located in the Plains region of Haryana and is home to some of the most culturally rich and historically significant landmarks in India. It is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and bustling food and drink scene. Rohtak Division is easily accessible by air, road, and train, making it a great destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division is full of attractions that will fascinate history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure enthusiasts alike. Here are some of the must-visit places in Rohtak Division.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rohtak Division is steeped in history, and there are plenty of museums and historical places where you can learn more about the region’s past. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Tilyar Lake: A beautiful lake that is perfect for boating and fishing.
  • Khokhrakot Fort: A fort that dates back to the 17th century and was built by the Jats.
  • Bhindawas Wildlife Sanctuary: A popular destination for nature lovers, home to a wide range of bird species and other wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Rohtak Division: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rohtak Division is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, making it a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best natural attractions in the region:

  • Karna Lake: A serene lake that is perfect for picnics and boating.
  • Rohtak Zoo: A home to a diverse range of animal species, the zoo is a great place to visit with kids for an educational experience.
  • Loharu Fort: A beautiful fort known for its stunning architecture and historical significance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Jind to Jhajjar: A picturesque trail that covers 70 km and provides stunning views of the region.
  • Rohtak to Gohana: A scenic route that takes you through lush green fields and tiny villages.

Best Things to Do in Rohtak Division: Activities and Tours

Rohtak Division has plenty of activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Heritage Walks: Take a guided tour of Rohtak and learn about its rich cultural heritage.
  • Rural Tourism: Experience the rural life in Rohtak by visiting tiny villages and interacting with the locals.
  • Food Tours: Take a food tour of the region and discover its delicious cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rohtak Division

Here is a week-long itinerary for Rohtak Division that covers all the must-visit attractions in the region:

Day 1: Arrival in Rohtak, explore the local market. Day 2: Visit the Tilyar Lake and Khokhrakot Fort. Day 3: Explore the Bhindawas Wildlife Sanctuary. Day 4: Visit the Karna Lake and Rohtak Zoo. Day 5: Visit the Loharu Fort. Day 6: Take a heritage walk in Rohtak. Day 7: Departure from Rohtak.

Food and Drink Scene of Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division has a rich and diverse food and drink scene that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chole Bhature: A popular dish made with spicy chickpeas and fried bread.
  • Kadhi: A spicy yogurt-based curry that is often served with rice.
  • Lassi: A refreshing drink made with yogurt and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Chaach: A refreshing drink made with buttermilk and spices.
  • Thandai: A refreshing drink made with milk and a blend of almonds, fennel seeds, and other spices.

Where to Stay in Rohtak Division: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rohtak Division has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in the region:

  • Hotel Lotus: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Rohtak.
  • Devi Palace Hotel: A mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Maharaja Palace: A luxury hotel that boasts top-notch amenities and stunning views of the region.

What to Buy in Rohtak Division: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rohtak Division is home to plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as a memento of your trip. Some of the popular items include:

  • Phulkari Embroidery: A type of embroidery work that is specific to the Punjab region.
  • Handicrafts: The region is known for its intricate handicrafts, including pottery and woodwork.
  • Jootis: A type of traditional footwear that is popular in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division has a fascinating culture that is a unique blend of Hindu and Muslim traditions. Some of the customs and traditions that are prevalent in the region include:

  • Bangle Ceremony: A ceremony that is held to commemorate a girl’s transition into womanhood.
  • Phoolon Ki Holi: A festival that is celebrated in the region, where people throw flower petals at each other.

Best Time to Visit Rohtak Division

The best time to visit Rohtak Division is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the region is lush green. The summer months, from April to June, can be quite hot, while the monsoon season, from July to September, can bring heavy rains and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rohtak Division

When visiting Rohtak Division, it is essential to understand the local customs and practices. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Take off your shoes before entering a temple or mosque.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public places.
  • Eat or drink anything from roadside vendors.
  • Touch someone’s head without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division is generally a safe place to visit, but it is essential to take precautions like being aware of your surroundings, avoiding deserted areas at night, and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division is a relatively healthy place, but it is essential to take precautions and get vaccinated before your trip. It is also advisable to carry mosquito repellent and sunscreen.

How to Get Around in Rohtak Division

Rohtak Division is a relatively small region, and getting around is easy. Taxis and buses are readily available, and hiring a bike or scooter is a popular option for exploring the region at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Rohtak Division: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rohtak Division is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations in Haryana and the rest of India. Some of the popular nearby destinations include:

  • Gurgaon: A bustling city located about 100 km from Rohtak. It is known for its modern infrastructure, corporate hubs, and nightlife.
  • Chandigarh: The capital of Haryana, located about 250 km from Rohtak. It is known for its well-manicured gardens, clean streets, and modern architecture.

Conclusion

Rohtak Division is a hidden gem that has something to offer every type of traveler, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts to foodies. With this guide, you now have all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this culturally rich and naturally stunning region.
