A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Gurugram Division

Located in Haryana, North India, Gurugram Division is a destination that offers a mix of modern and cultural experiences to travelers. From exploring historical sites to indulging in mouth-watering street food, there is something for everyone in Gurugram. In this travel guide, we highlight the best things to do, where to stay, and what to see in Gurugram Division.

List of Places to Visit in Gurugram Division

Gurugram Division has plenty of cultural and natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sultanpur National Park Bird Sanctuary: A paradise for bird-watchers, the Sultanpur National Park Bird Sanctuary is home to over 250 species of birds.
  • Sheesh Mahal: Situated in the heart of Gurugram, Sheesh Mahal is a beautiful museum that displays the rich cultural heritage of Haryana.
  • Leisure Valley Park: A park that offers a peaceful retreat amidst the hustle-bustle of Gurugram, the Leisure Valley Park has verdant lawns, jogging tracks, and fountains.

Natural Attractions of Gurugram Division: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Damdama Lake: A popular picnic spot, Damdama Lake offers water sports facilities such as boating, kayaking, and rowing.
  • Sohna Hill Fort: Spanning across 3 km, Sohna Hill Fort is an abandoned fort that attracts hikers and trekkers with its scenic mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gurugram Division

Gurugram Division is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails for outdoor adventures:

  • Aravalli Biodiversity Park: A beautiful park that has well-marked trails, Aravalli Biodiversity Park is a great place for hiking, cycling and bird watching.
  • Sultanpur-Damdama Trail: An off-road biking and hiking trail that runs through the foothills of the Aravalli Range, Sultanpur-Damdama Trail offers scenic views and a challenging workout.

Best Things to Do in Gurugram Division: Activities and Tours

From food tours to sightseeing and adventure sports, there are plenty of things to do in Gurugram Division:

  • Food Tour: Indulge in a food tour to discover the hidden culinary gems of Gurugram. Sample local specialties like Kadhai Paneer, Dum Biryani, and Lassi.
  • Heritage Tour: A tour that explores the history and culture of Gurugram, the Heritage Tour takes travelers through the city’s historical sites and architectural marvels.
  • Adventure Sports: Enjoy a plethora of adventure sports like zip-lining, rock climbing, and bungee jumping at the Kingdom of Dreams theme park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gurugram Division

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Gurugram Division, here’s an itinerary that covers the region’s best attractions:

Day Activity
Day 1-2 Explore Gurugram’s historical sites
Day 3-4 Adventure sports and nature trails
Day 5-6 Visit museums, parks and cultural sites
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and relax

Food and Drink Scene of Gurugram Division

Gurugram is a foodie’s paradise, serving lip-smacking dishes from across the region. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kadhai Paneer: A signature dish of Haryana, Kadhai Paneer is a dry preparation of paneer cooked in an onion and tomato-based sauce.
  • Dum Biryani: A fragrant rice preparation that is cooked with meat or vegetables and spices like saffron and cardamom.
  • Butter Chicken: A Punjabi dish that has become a staple in Delhi and NCR, Butter Chicken is a creamy tomato-based chicken curry.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A popular beverage made with yogurt and water, flavored with sweet or salt and served chilled.
  • Chaach: A refreshing drink made with buttermilk, spices, and herbs.

Where to Stay in Gurugram Division: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gurugram Division has plenty of options for travelers looking for accommodation that fits their budget and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in the region:

  • Luxury Hotels: Choose from the likes of The Oberoi Gurgaon, The Leela, and Trident Hotel for a luxurious stay in the region.
  • Hostels: Backpackers can opt for budget-friendly hostels like Zostel Gurgaon and Backpacker Panda for a comfortable and affordable stay.
  • Guesthouses: For the more budget-conscious traveler, guesthouses like The Perch Arbor and Golden Tulip Suites are excellent options.

What to Buy in Gurugram Division: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gurugram Division is well-known for its handicrafts and street markets. Here are some things you must buy when in the region:

  • Handicrafts: Haryana is renowned for its handicrafts, including pottery, lacquer work, and bamboo craft.
  • Apparel: The region is also known for its handcrafted textiles and embroidery work.
  • Spices and Tea: Gurugram Division has some of the best tea and spice markets in India, where you can buy fresh tea leaves and a variety of spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Gurugram Division

Gurugram Division is an amalgamation of various ethnic communities, each having its own unique customs and traditions. Some of the customs that you should be aware of as a traveler include:

  • Greeting: Greet the locals with a polite ‘namaste’ or ‘satsriakal’.
  • Dress Code: While traveling to religious places, make sure you respect the dress code and dress conservatively.
  • Etiquette: It’s essential to show respect and consideration towards locals and other travelers.

Best Time to Visit Gurugram Division

The best time to visit Gurugram Division is from October to March when the climate is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gurugram Division

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and specialties.
  • Ask for permission before taking someone’s picture.

Don’t:

  • Haggling is a common practice in Gurugram, but don’t bargain aggressively or lowball vendors.
  • Don’t engage in public displays of affection.
  • Don’t photograph sensitive or restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gurugram Division

Gurugram Division is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s essential to be vigilant and take some precautions:

  • Avoid going to isolated or less populated areas.
  • Don’t carry too much cash or valuables.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and be cautious in crowded places.

Health in Gurugram Division

Gurugram Division has a few health risks that travelers should be aware of, including:

  • Motion sickness due to travel on hilly roads.
  • Sunburn or heatstroke during summer months.
  • Air pollution during winter months.

Getting to Gurugram Division: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gurugram Division is well-connected to major cities and towns in Haryana and Delhi NCR. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Gurugram Division

The Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL), New Delhi, is the closest airport to Gurugram Division, located about 20 km away.

Bus Stations Near Gurugram Division

Gurugram has several bus stations that are well-connected to major cities in the region, including Delhi and Jaipur.

Other Options

  • Trains: The nearest railway station is the Huda City Centre Metro Station in Gurugram, which is well-connected to Delhi.
  • Taxis: Taxis and car rentals are readily available to travelers.

How to Get Around in Gurugram Division

Gurugram Division has several transportation options to help travelers get around the region, including:

  • Taxis and auto-rickshaws: These are readily available on the streets and are perfect for short distances.
  • Metro: The Delhi Metro connects Gurugram Division to Delhi and other neighboring cities.
  • Buses: Haryana State Transport operates buses to various destinations in the region.

Where to Go Next from Gurugram Division: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of places to visit near Gurugram Division. Here are some options:

  • Delhi: A visit to the national capital of India is a must for any traveler to the region.
  • Jaipur: The Pink City of Jaipur is known for its rich culture, history, and architecture.
  • Agra: Visit the iconic Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, located in the city of Agra.

Conclusion

Gurugram Division is a fascinating destination that offers something for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, foodie, or culture enthusiast, Gurugram Division has attractions that will leave you spellbound. Use this comprehensive travel guide to make your trip to Gurugram Division a memorable one.
