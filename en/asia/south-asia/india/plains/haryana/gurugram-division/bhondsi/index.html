Introduction to Bhondsi

Bhondsi is a small village located in the Gurugram Division in Haryana, India. Though it’s a lesser-known destination, Bhondsi is a hidden gem that boasts of fascinating cultural attractions, beautiful natural scenery, and an array of exciting outdoor adventures. Travelers who want to experience authentic local life and off-the-beaten-path destinations will find Bhondsi to be the perfect choice.

List of Places to Visit in Bhondsi

Here is a list of some of the top attractions that you must visit when in Bhondsi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baba Nemi Chand Ji Maharaj Temple - A beautiful temple dedicated to Baba Nemi Chand Ji Maharaj, this site is highly revered among locals and draws many spiritual tourists.
  • Sheetla Mata Temple - A fascinating temple that is dedicated to the goddess of smallpox, Sheetla Mata is an important landmark of Bhondsi.
  • Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary - This sanctuary is home to many species of migratory birds that come from as far as Siberia. It is a treat to explore the wetlands and enjoy birdwatching.
  • Badshahpur Fort - Once a stronghold of the Mughal Empire, this fort is now a popular tourist destination that provides an excellent insight into the history and culture of India.

Natural Attractions of Bhondsi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aravali Biodiversity Park - A nature lover’s paradise, this park is spread across 153 hectares and is home to thousands of species of flora and fauna.
  • Damdama Lake - This beautiful lake is a popular picnic spot and is perfect for boating, kayaking, and other water sports.
  • Sohna Hills - Explore the beautiful Sohna Hills and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhondsi

Bhondsi has many beautiful and exciting hiking and biking routes, making it the ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Aravali Trails - One of the most popular trails in Bhondsi, this route takes you through the beautiful landscapes of the Aravali Range.
  • Bhondsi-Farrukhnagar Trail - This magnificent trail runs for approximately 20 km and is perfect for experienced hikers and bikers.
  • Sohna-Damdama Trail - Explore the stunning Sohna Hills on this exciting trail that ends at the picturesque Damdama Lake.

Best Things to Do in Bhondsi: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Bhondsi offers several other exciting things to do. Here are some of the best:

  • Wildlife Safari at Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary - Enjoy a thrilling wildlife safari at the Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary and marvel at the many species of birds in their natural habitats.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Experience the beauty of Bhondsi from a unique perspective with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Adventure Sports at Damdama Lake - Get your adrenaline pumping with many water sports, including boating, kayaking, and jet skiing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bhondsi

Here is a week-long itinerary to explore Bhondsi and its surroundings:

  • Day 1 - Arrive in Bhondsi, and explore the local markets, try the local cuisine, and visit nearby temples.
  • Day 2 - Visit the Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary and enjoy a thrilling wildlife safari.
  • Day 3 - Go on a hot air balloon ride and enjoy a bird’s eye view of Bhondsi.
  • Day 4 - Explore the Aravali Biodiversity Park and enjoy a leisurely picnic.
  • Day 5 - Go hiking on the Bhondsi-Farrukhnagar Trail.
  • Day 6 - Visit the Damdama Lake and enjoy kayaking, boating, and other water sports.
  • Day 7 - Head over to the Sohna Hills and enjoy the beautiful landscapes and scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhondsi

A visit to Bhondsi is incomplete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gajar Ki Kanji - A delicious fermented drink made from carrots, this is an excellent thirst quencher in the hot and humid weather of Bhondsi.
  • Peetha - A savory dish made from wheat flour, roasted gram flour, and jaggery, this dish is a staple dish of Bhondsi.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi - A refreshing drink made from beaten yoghurt, lassi is an excellent way to cool off after a day of sightseeing.
  • Masala Tea - A piping hot cup of masala tea, with a blend of Indian spices is a perfect way to start your day.

Where to Stay in Bhondsi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bhondsi has a range of accommodation options to suit different budget and preference. Here are some recommendations:

  • Garh Garhi Inn - This budget-friendly hotel provides fantastic services and is ideal for travelers looking for comfortable and affordable accommodation.
  • Tree Top Greens - A luxury resort located within the Aravali hills, this hotel offers excellent services and exciting outdoor activities.
  • OYO Rooms - This chain provides affordable and comfortable rooms with quality services.

What to Buy in Bhondsi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Bhondsi is complete without taking home some souvenirs. Here are some of the things to buy:

  • Khatta-Meetha Achar - This sweet and sour pickle is made from mangoes and is a popular snack in Bhondsi.
  • Madhubani Paintings - These paintings depict traditional scenes, and are made using natural dyes and pigments.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhondsi

The people of Bhondsi follow traditional Indian customs and maintain a close-knit community. They are warm and hospitable and welcome visitors with open arms. It is important to respect their way of life and dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious places.

Best Time to Visit Bhondsi

The best time to visit Bhondsi is between October and March when the weather is cool, the air is dry, and the skies are clear. During this time, the temperature is pleasant, and the landscapes are lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhondsi

When traveling to Bhondsi, it is important to keep in mind certain dos and don’ts to ensure a comfortable and hassle-free trip.

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and cultures
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places
  • Carry sufficient cash, as many places may not accept credit/debit cards

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t use single-use plastic
  • Don’t consume alcohol in public

Safety and Health

When traveling to Bhondsi, it is important to take appropriate safety measures and precautions to ensure your health and well-being.

Safety in Bhondsi

  • Avoid traveling alone at night
  • Carry a map or a travel guide when exploring unknown areas
  • Be careful of pickpockets and thefts

Health in Bhondsi

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water
  • Use a mosquito repellent when outdoors
  • Carry a basic first-aid kit with essential medicines

Getting to Bhondsi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bhondsi is accessible by air, train, and bus. Here are the various transportation options:

Airports Near Bhondsi

  • Indira Gandhi International Airport - The nearest international airport located in Delhi, approximately 30 km away from Bhondsi.

Bus Stations Near Bhondsi

  • Gurgaon Bus Stand - Located around 20 km away from Bhondsi, this bus stand provides services to different parts of the city.

Other Options

  • Train - The nearest railway station is Gurgaon Railway Station, located around 25 km away from Bhondsi.
  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available for hire both from Delhi and Gurgaon

How to Get Around in Bhondsi

Local buses, autos, taxis, and rickshaws are readily available in Bhondsi. It is advisable to hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the various attractions as they offer greater privacy and flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Bhondsi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bhondsi has many nearby destinations and attractions that are worth exploring. Here are some recommendations:

  • Manesar - This charming town is famous for its ancient shrines and has several activities, including adventure sports, hot air ballooning, and hiking.
  • Delhi - The capital city of India, Delhi is just a few hours away from Bhondsi and is home to many historical landmarks and world-famous cuisine.

Conclusion

Bhondsi is a destination that provides a perfect mix of history, culture, and outdoor activities. With its beautiful landscapes, authentic cuisine, and welcoming locals, visiting Bhondsi is sure to be an unforgettable experience. So next time you’re planning a trip to India, be sure to add Bhondsi to your itinerary!
