Introduction to Rewari

Rewari, a small town in the Gurugram division of Haryana, is known for its rich history and culture. The town is famous for its beautiful gardens, historical monuments, and traditional handicrafts. Its rustic charm attracts tourists from all over the world who come here to explore its beauty and uniqueness. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the best places to see, things to do, and food to try in Rewari.

List of Places to Visit in Rewari

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rewari has a rich history, and it reflects in the many historical sites and museums here. The top museums and historical places to visit in Rewari are:

  • Qutub Shahi Tombs: One of the most famous tourist spots in Rewari, the Qutub Shahi Tombs is a display of Islamic architecture that dates back to the 16th century.
  • Rewari Heritage Steam Locomotive Museum: This museum is a paradise for railway enthusiasts. It displays some of the oldest locomotives and coaches used in Indian Railways.
  • Baba Khetanath Temple: A famous temple located near Rewari, Baba Khetanath Temple is a great mix of architecture and history.
  • Ateli Mandir: A beautiful temple located in Ateli, Rewari, the Ateli Mandir is an amazing display of art and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Rewari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The beauty of Rewari lies in its simple yet beautiful natural attractions. The top natural attractions to visit in Rewari are:

  • Rewari Zoo: A must-visit place for animal lovers, the Rewari Zoo is home to a vast variety of animals and birds.
  • Sita Ram Temple: A beautiful temple located near a small pond in Rewari, Sita Ram Temple is perfect for a morning stroll and meditation.
  • Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary: A serene and beautiful park, the Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary is a great place for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rewari

Rewari has many hiking and biking routes that are perfect for someone looking for adventure. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Rewari are:

  • Bhiwadi-Alwar road: A 50-kilometer stretch that is perfect for road biking and cycling.
  • Bawal-Taoru route: A 30-kilometer stretch that is perfect for off-road mountain biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Rewari: Activities and Tours

Apart from seeing the cultural and natural attractions, there are many other things to do in Rewari. The best things to do in Rewari are:

  • Explore the local bazaars: Rewari is known for its traditional handicrafts, and you can find them at the local bazaars. The famous bazaars are in the vicinity of the famous Gurukul Road and Kapda Market.
  • Try the local cuisine: Rewari has a wide variety of spicy meat-based dishes and vegetarian cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes are mutton curry, paneer tikka, and sarson ka saag.
  • Attend a local festival: Festivals are a great way to experience the local culture. The Baisakhi festival is celebrated with great zeal and enthusiasm in Rewari.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rewari

Here’s a week-long itinerary for the best possible experience in Rewari:

Day 1: Arrive in Rewari and visit the Rewari Heritage Steam Locomotive Museum.

Day 2: Visit the Baba Khetanath Temple and explore the local bazaars.

Day 3: Hike and mountain bike the Bawal-Taoru route.

Day 4: Visit the Sita Ram Temple and the Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary.

Day 5-6: Visit nearby destinations like Gurugram and Delhi.

Day 7: Attend the Baisakhi Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Rewari

Must-Try Dishes

The food culture of Rewari is very different from what you would find in other parts of India. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Mutton Curry: A spicy mutton dish that is perfect with roti or naan.
  • Paneer Tikka: Marinated paneer cubes grilled over a charcoal fire and served with spices.
  • Sarson ka Saag: A popular vegan dish made with mustard leaves.

Local Beverages

Apart from food, Rewari has some unique local beverages, such as:

  • Lassi: A popular drink made with yogurt, milk, and sugar.
  • Masala Chai: A spicy tea made with Indian spices like cardamom, ginger, and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Rewari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rewari has many accommodation options, depending on your budget and preferences:

  • Heritage Haveli: A luxurious haveli-style accommodation in the heart of Rewari.
  • Jharna Jungle Lodge: A budget-friendly jungle lodge on the outskirts of Rewari.
  • Ishi Maya Palace: A mid-range hotel with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Rewari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rewari is famous for its traditional handicrafts and textiles. The top things to buy in Rewari are:

  • Sandook-Knitting: A traditional handicraft that involves knitting beads onto a wooden chest.
  • Phulkari: Decorative shawls and scarves with traditional embroidery.
  • Pottery: Earthenware made from terracotta clay.

Local Culture and Customs of Rewari

The local culture of Rewari is a mix of traditional and modern culture. Some of the notable customs and traditions are:

  • Jaat: The dominant caste in Rewari, who follow their age-old customs and traditions.
  • Sakhiyan: Traditional folk songs sung by women in praise of the lord.
  • Bhangra: A joyful dance performed by men during festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Rewari

The best time to visit Rewari is during the winter season, i.e., from October to March. The weather remains pleasant, and the humidity is relatively lower than in the summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rewari

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Rewari:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the local culture by attending festivals and carnivals.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Do not photograph locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rewari

Rewari is a safe town, and the crime rate is relatively low. However, it is always advised to take precautions, such as not venturing into unfamiliar areas at odd hours.

Health in Rewari

The healthcare facilities in Rewari are not very advanced. However, the town has a few government and private hospitals that can provide basic medical assistance.

Getting to Rewari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rewari

The nearest airport to Rewari is the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is around 87 km away.

Bus Stations Near Rewari

Rewari has a well-connected network of buses that connect it to nearby towns and cities. Some of the top bus stations in Rewari are:

  • Rewari Bus Stand
  • Bawal Bus Stand
  • Dharuhera Bus Stand

Other Options

Rewari is well-connected through railways, and there are multiple trains that connect Rewari to other parts of India.

How to Get Around in Rewari

The best way to get around in Rewari is by hiring a cab or auto-rickshaw. You can also rent a bike to explore the town at your pace.

Where to Go Next from Rewari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rewari is the perfect base to explore the nearby towns and cities, such as:

  • Gurugram: A fast-developing city known for its nightlife, shopping malls, and restaurants.
  • Delhi: The capital city of India, known for its rich heritage, historical monuments, and delicious street food.
  • Jaipur: The pink city of India, known for its palaces and forts, rich history, and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Rewari, with its unique mix of natural beauty, history, and culture, is the perfect destination for anyone looking for a truly immersive experience. So pack your bags and head to Rewari to explore this hidden gem of India.
