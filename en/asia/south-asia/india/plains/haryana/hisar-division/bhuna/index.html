Introduction to Bhuna

Bhuna is a small town in the Hisar Division of Haryana in India, known for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and natural beauty. It is located in the plains of South Asia and is a perfect destination for those who want to experience local life and enjoy outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Bhuna

Bhuna has a number of cultural and natural attractions that are worth exploring. Here is a list of some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhuna Fort: A historical fort built in the 18th century that served as a defense for the town. It has now been converted into a museum offering a glimpse into the rich history of the place.
  • Haveli: A 16th-century mansion built by a wealthy merchant. This Haveli still boasts original paintings and a unique architecture that reflects the cultural heritage of Bhuna.

Natural Attractions of Bhuna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bhuna Lake: A serene and peaceful lake offering scenic beauty and a perfect place to relax by or paddle in.
  • Kohliwala Lake: Another popular lake located just 2 km from Bhuna, that offers a mesmerizing view of the sunset.
  • Bhuna Bird Sanctuary: A perfect spot for bird-watching with a variety of bird species inhabiting the sanctuary.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhuna

Bhuna is a perfect location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes to explore:

  • Bhuna to Rania: This 8 km route takes you through the rural countryside of Bhuna to Rania, with beautiful scenery of the fields.
  • Bhuna to Damla: This 22 km route takes you through the scenic countryside, with a mix of slopes and flat terrains. You can experience the local culture and lifestyle along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Bhuna: Activities and Tours

  • Safari in Bhuna Wildlife Sanctuary: Experience the thrill of a jungle safari at Bhuna Wildlife Sanctuary, and explore the scenic natural beauty around.
  • Visit the Historical Temples: Pay your respects to the local deities and witness the religious and cultural heritage of Bhuna.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhuna

Haryana’s staple food is wheat-based and includes dishes made from roti, paratha, and chapati. Bhuna has a variety of local dishes that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Besan Masala Roti: A dish made of Gram floor, wheat flour and a mix of spices.
  • Lassi: A drink made from mixing yogurt with sugar and ice and sometimes topped off with flavorings such as saffron or mango.

Local Beverages

  • Thandai: A popular drink made from almonds, milk, sugar, and peppercorns, especially relished during Holi.
  • Chach: A drink made from mixing butter and curd is a refreshing drink and tasty drink for summers.

Where to Stay in Bhuna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bhuna offers a good selection of places to stay, for all budgets and preferences.

  • The Raj Palace is a luxury hotel that offers premium rooms with modern amenities and services.
  • Hotel Justa Panchkula is a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable and affordable rooms equipped with modern facilities and services.
  • Hotel Sahil Palace is a budget option that offers clean and basic rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Bhuna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jutti shoes: Handmade and beautiful footwear made with leather, and traditional embroidery.
  • Hand-crafted Pottery: Beautiful hand-crafted decorative pieces made out of clay that showcase the traditional culture of Bhuna.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhuna

Bhuna has a rich and diverse tradition and culture, with Hinduism being the predominant religion. Cultural customs and traditions are a mix of rural and urban influences, with festivals such as Diwali, Holi, and Lohri being the major celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Bhuna

The best time to visit Bhuna is between October to March when the weather is pleasant with a maximum temperature of 25º C, and the minimum at 5º C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhuna

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry sunscreen, hats or caps as the climate can be arid and scorching.
  • Support the local economy by purchasing local goods

Don’t:

  • Do not litter.
  • Do not carry alcoholic beverages and drugs, since alcohol is not acceptable in the local culture.
  • Do not wear excessively revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhuna

Bhuna is a relatively safe destination. However, it’s advisable to take necessary precautions such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.
  • Watch your belongings and do not flaunt valuables.

Health in Bhuna

Bhuna followed a healthy and hygienic lifestyle, with simple homemade foods. However, it’s essential to carry a water filter and maintain hygiene.

Conclusion
