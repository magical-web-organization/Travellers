Introduction to Hisar

Hisar is a city located in the Hisar Division of Haryana, India. It is the administrative headquarters of the district and is situated in the plains of South Asia. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, unique cuisine, and picturesque landscapes. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adventure enthusiast, Hisar has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hisar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Feroze Shah Palace Complex: This magnificent palace complex was built by Feroze Shah Tughlaq in the 14th century and features several structures, including a mosque, a mausoleum, and a palace. The complex is known for its intricate architecture and historical significance.

  2. Devi Bhawan Mandir: The Devi Bhawan Mandir is a popular Hindu temple located in the heart of Hisar. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.

  3. Lat Ki Masjid: This ancient mosque was built during the reign of Feroze Shah Tughlaq and is known for its unique architecture, which combines Indian and Islamic styles.

Natural Attractions of Hisar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Blue Bird Lake: This picturesque lake is located on the outskirts of Hisar and is a popular spot for boating and picnics. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.

  2. Agroha Dham: Agroha Dham is a religious pilgrimage site located in the heart of Hisar. The site is known for its scenic beauty and is visited by thousands of devotees every year.

  3. Khora Lake: This serene lake is located in the Khora colony and is a popular spot for bird watching and nature walks. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hisar

  1. Badopal-Benra Circuit: This cycling route is popular among adventure enthusiasts and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The route covers a distance of approximately 40 km and takes you through some of the most picturesque landscapes in the area.

  2. Hisar-Ratia Circuit: This hiking trail takes you through some of the most scenic spots in the Hisar district. The route covers a distance of approximately 15 km and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Hisar: Activities and Tours

  1. Heritage Walk: Take a guided heritage walk through the old town of Hisar and explore some of its most iconic landmarks and historical buildings.

  2. Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy a breathtaking bird’s-eye view of the city from a hot air balloon. The ride offers stunning views of the city’s skyline and surrounding countryside.

  3. Food Tour: Take a food tour of the city and sample some of its unique cuisine. Hisar is known for its delicious street food and local delicacies.

Food and Drink Scene of Hisar

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kachori: A delicious street food snack made from flour, lentils, and spices.

  2. Bhature: A popular breakfast dish made from fried bread and chana masala.

  3. Gulab Jamun: A classic Indian dessert made from fried dough balls soaked in sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

  1. Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is a perfect way to beat the heat.

  2. Chaas: An Indian yogurt drink, perfect for accompanying spicy meals.

Where to Stay in Hisar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Esse Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city, offering comfortable rooms and top-notch amenities.

  2. Hotel Midtown Grand: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.

  3. The Mirage Palace: A guesthouse near the city center, offering comfortable rooms and a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Hisar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Hisar is known for its intricate handicrafts, including pottery, woodwork, and textiles.

  2. Spices: Hisar is home to some of the most aromatic and flavorful spices in India. Make sure to bring home some of the local blends.

Local Culture and Customs of Hisar

  1. Clothing: Hisar is a conservative city, and visitors are advised to dress modestly while exploring the city.

  2. Greeting: People in Hisar are known for their warm hospitality, and greeting locals with a smile and a Namaste is considered good etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Hisar

The best time to visit Hisar is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and the city is alive with festivals and cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hisar

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Dress conservatively.

  3. Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in public displays of affection.

  2. Insult or disrespect local customs and traditions.

  3. Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hisar

Hisar is a relatively safe city, and visitors are advised to take standard precautions while exploring the city. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and be cautious when traveling alone at night.

Health in Hisar

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and avoid consuming street food from unhygienic sources.

Local buses and rickshaws are the most common means of transportation in Hisar. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Hisar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Chandigarh: The beautiful city of Chandigarh is located just a few hours’ drive from Hisar and is known for its stunning architecture and rich cultural heritage.

  2. Delhi: The bustling city of Delhi is located approximately 160 km from Hisar and is home to some of the most iconic landmarks and historical sites in India.

Conclusion

Hisar is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and unique cuisine. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover or adventure enthusiast, this city has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Hisar.
