Introduction to Jind

Located in the Hisar Division of Haryana in the plains of India, Jind is a small but culturally rich city that boasts of its history and heritage. The name of the city has been derived from Jaintapuri, which was an ancient Hindu pilgrimage centre. The city is surrounded by lush green fields and is known for its rich cultural heritage and colorful festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Jind

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Maham Tirth

Maham Tirth is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Jind. It holds great historical and cultural significance for the followers of Jainism. The temple complex is built over six acres of land and is surrounded by beautiful gardens. The temple is dedicated to Lord Mahavira and attracts devotees from all over the country.

Pandu-Pidara

Pandu-Pidara is an ancient site that is believed to be associated with the Mahabharata. According to legend, it was at this site that the Pandavas spent their last days of anonymity during their exile. The site consists of a cluster of small temples and is a popular attraction among history buffs.

Rani Talab

Rani Talab, also known as Queen’s Pond, is a popular tourist site in Jind. The pond is believed to have been built during the reign of Queen Chandravati, who was the wife of King Gajpat Singh. The pond is surrounded by beautiful gardens and is a popular spot for picnics and boating.

Natural Attractions of Jind: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bhuteshwar Nath Mahadev Temple

Bhuteshwar Nath Mahadev Temple is a popular attraction in Jind that is situated at the bank of a natural pond. The temple is believed to be around 800 years old and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple complex is surrounded by natural beauty and offers stunning views of the surroundings.

Jindal Park

Jindal Park is a beautiful garden and picnic spot in Jind that attracts locals and tourists alike. The park is spread over 26 acres of land and is full of lush greenery and colorful flowers. The park also has a small lake that adds to its charm.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jind

Jind offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that allow tourists to explore the surrounding natural beauty. The Bhuteshwar Nath Mahadev Temple hiking trail is a popular one that takes you through scenic forests and hills. Another popular route is the one that goes through Rani Talab and Pandu-Pidara sites.

Best Things to Do in Jind: Activities and Tours

Attend a Local Festival

Jind is known for its colorful festivals that are celebrated with great zeal and enthusiasm. The annual Jind Mela, which is held in February, is one of the most popular festivals in the city. Other festivals include the Teej Festival and the Holi Festival.

Visit a Local Market

The local markets in Jind offer a unique shopping experience where you can buy traditional clothing, jewelry, and handicrafts. The Jind Cloth Market is a popular market that sells a wide variety of fabrics and clothing.

Food and Drink Scene of Jind

Must-Try Dishes

Kachori

Kachori is a popular snack in Jind that is made with a spicy filling of potatoes, peas, lentils, and spices. It is served with chutneys and is a great option for a quick bite.

Lassi

Lassi is a refreshing drink made with yogurt, water, and sweeteners. It is a popular drink in Jind and is served in most of the local restaurants and food joints.

Local Beverages

Sugarcane Juice

Sugarcane juice is a popular drink in Jind that is made by extracting the juice from the sugarcane plant. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

Milk Punch

Milk Punch is a traditional drink in Jind that is made with milk, spices, and sugar. It is a great drink to warm you up during the cold winter days.

Where to Stay in Jind: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jind offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Some of the popular hotels in the city include Hotel Grand Park, Hotel Jindal Regency, and Hotel Jindal Palace.

What to Buy in Jind: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jind is known for its traditional clothing and handicrafts. Some of the popular souvenirs to buy in Jind include embroidered fabrics, juttis (traditional footwear), and lacquer jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Jind

Jind has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its festivals, food, and clothing. The people of Jind are friendly and hospitable, and it is important to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Jind

The best time to visit Jind is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and cool. The temperatures can soar up to 45 degrees Celsius during summer months, making it uncomfortable to explore the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jind

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.
  • Eat street food from unhygienic vendors.
  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jind

Jind is a safe city, but it is always advisable to take precautions while traveling, such as avoiding lonely places at night. It is also recommended to keep one’s belongings safe and curtail any unwanted attention.

Health in Jind

It is important to carry a medical kit while traveling to Jind. Always drink bottled water and avoid eating from unsanitary places. Avoid attractive deals if you feel unsure of their credibility.

Getting to Jind: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jind

The nearest airport to Jind is the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is around 150 km away. Taxis and buses are easily available from the airport to Jind.

Bus Stations Near Jind

Jind Bus Stand is the main bus station in the city, and it has frequent bus services to and from nearby cities and states.

Other Options

Jind is well-connected by rail, and the Jind Junction Railway Station has regular trains to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Jind

Auto-rickshaws are the most common mode of transportation in Jind. Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Jind: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular nearby destinations from Jind include Rohtak, Sonipat, and Kurukshetra. These cities have their own unique history and culture, and are worth exploring.

Conclusion

Jind is a city that is full of culture and heritage, and offers a unique travel experience to its visitors. With its beautiful temples, natural attractions, and traditional cuisine, Jind is a great destination for those looking for an offbeat travel experience. Follow our comprehensive guide to make the most of your trip to Jind.
