Pundri is a small town located in the Indian state of Haryana. The town is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. If you’re planning on visiting Pundri, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Pundri

Pundri is a quaint town in the Kaithal district of Haryana. The town is situated in the northern plains of India and shares its borders with Punjab and Rajasthan. Pundri is one of the important historical and cultural centers in Haryana. The town is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and scenic landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Pundri

Here are some of the top places to visit in Pundri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Devi Temple: This ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Durga is one of the oldest and most revered shrines in the town.
  • Kabul Shah Mosque: Built in the 17th century, this mosque is a testament to the town’s multi-faith culture.
  • Mamu Bhanja Fort: This fort dates back to the 16th century and is a striking example of the town’s architectural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Pundri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kapal Mochan: This serene pond is surrounded by lush greenery and is considered to be one of the most sacred places in the town.
  • Pundrik Rishi Lake: This man-made lake is located on the outskirts of the town and is a popular spot for picnics and boating.
  • Gogripur Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna and is a must-visit for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pundri

Pundri is a haven for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the town:

  • Pundri to Kapal Mochan: This scenic route takes you through the lush greenery of the town’s countryside and leads to the serene Kapal Mochan pond.
  • Pundri to Gogripur Wildlife Sanctuary: This trail takes you through some of the most beautiful and rugged landscapes in the town and ends at the Gogripur Wildlife Sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Pundri: Activities and Tours

Pundri offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Pundri:

  • Explore the town’s rich history: Take a walking tour of the ancient temples, mosques, and forts in the town and learn about its colorful past.
  • Go on a wildlife safari: Explore the Gogripur Wildlife Sanctuary on a guided safari tour and spot some of the rarest animals in the region.
  • Take a boat ride: Enjoy a peaceful boat ride on the Pundrik Rishi Lake and take in the beautiful scenery around you.

Food and Drink Scene of Pundri

Pundri is known for its delicious food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kachori: This crispy, deep-fried snack filled with spicy lentil filling is a popular snack in Pundri.
  • Makki ki Roti and Sarson ka Saag: This traditional Punjabi meal made of maize flour bread and mustard leaves curry is a must-try.
  • Lassi: This frothy, refreshing drink made of yogurt and sugar is a perfect summer beverage.

Local Beverages

  • Chaach: This traditional yogurt-based drink is a popular choice in Pundri during summers.
  • Thandai: This cooling drink made with milk, almonds, and spices is a must-try during the festive season.

Where to Stay in Pundri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pundri offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Pundri:

  • Hotel Kumar Residency: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and excellent service.
  • Hotel Royal Palm: This hotel is known for its plush rooms, excellent food, and top-notch service.
  • Hotel Haryana Palace: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with basic amenities and is a great budget option.

What to Buy in Pundri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Pundri, here are some souvenirs and local products that you can take home:

  • Handicrafts: Pundri is known for its beautiful handicrafts, including pottery, woodwork, and brassware.
  • Embroidered Clothes: The colorful and vibrant embroidered clothes of Pundri are a great souvenir to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Pundri

The people of Pundri are warm and welcoming, and the town has a rich tradition of multi-faith and multiculturalism. The majority of the population in Pundri is Hindu, but there is also a significant Muslim population.

Best Time to Visit Pundri

The best time to visit Pundri is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable. The summer months between April and June can be quite hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pundri

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Drink or eat anything from roadside vendors without checking for hygiene.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Enter religious places without removing shoes or covering your head.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pundri

Pundri is a safe town, but it’s always advisable to take the necessary precautions while traveling. Petty theft can be a problem, so it’s best to be careful with your belongings.

Health in Pundri

Pundri has a few hospitals and medical centers that provide basic healthcare services. However, it’s always advisable to carry a basic medical kit with you while traveling.

Getting to Pundri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pundri

The nearest airport to Pundri is the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, located about 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Pundri

Pundri is well-connected by road, and there are frequent buses from major cities in Haryana and Punjab.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or take a train to Pundri. The nearest railway station is located in Kaithal, which is about 20 km away from Pundri.

How to Get Around in Pundri

The best way to get around in Pundri is by hiring a taxi or taking a rickshaw. You can also explore the town on foot or rent a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Pundri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit from Pundri:

  • Kaithal: This ancient town is located about 20 km away from Pundri and is known for its rich cultural heritage and historical sites.
  • Chandigarh: This cosmopolitan city is located about 170 km away from Pundri and is known for its beautiful gardens, vibrant culture, and excellent food.

Conclusion

Pundri is a town that is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring its ancient temples and mosques, going on a wildlife safari, or just relaxing and enjoying the scenic views, Pundri has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Pundri and make the most of your visit.
