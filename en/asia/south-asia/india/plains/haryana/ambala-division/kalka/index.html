Introduction to Kalka

Kalka is a small town located in Ambala Division, in the Indian state of Haryana. Sitting at the foothills of the Shivalik Range, it is surrounded by lush green hills that offer some breathtaking views of nature. Kalka is an ideal destination for those who want to experience the hospitality of small-town India while enjoying its natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Kalka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kali Mata Temple: This temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Kali and is located on the National Highway-22. The temple is famous for its unique architecture and attracts a large number of visitors every year.
  • Pinjore Gardens: These historic gardens are located at a distance of 10 km from Kalka and have been designed in the Mughal style. The gardens are famous for their fountains, waterfalls, and beautiful flowers.

Natural Attractions of Kalka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kalka-Shimla Railway: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a 96-km-long railway track that connects Kalka to the hill station of Shimla. The train journey offers some breathtaking views of nature and is a must-do activity for tourists.
  • Timber Trail: Located on the Chandigarh-Shimla highway, Timber Trail is a cable car ride that takes visitors to the mountain top. It offers a panoramic view of the Shivalik Range and is an excellent spot for photographers.
  • Morni Hills: Morni Hills are located at a distance of 45 km from Kalka and are famous for their trekking and hiking trails. It is an ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalka

  • Kalka to Shimla: The Kalka-Shimla trekking route is a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts. The trek takes visitors through some of the most scenic routes in the Shivalik Range.
  • Morni Hills: The Morni Hills offer some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kalka. With a variety of trails available, it is an ideal destination for outdoor adventure enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Kalka: Activities and Tours

  • Kalka-Shimla Toy Train Ride: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a must-do activity when in Kalka. The train journey takes visitors through some breathtaking views of nature and offers a unique experience of small-town India.
  • Cable Car Ride: Timber Trail is a must-visit destination for visitors to Kalka. The cable car ride offers a panoramic view of the Shivalik Range and is an excellent spot for photography.
  • Trekking and Hiking: Kalka offers some of the best trekking and hiking routes in the Shivalik Range. Morni Hills and Kalka-Shimla trekking routes are popular destinations for adventure enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Kalka

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kadhi Chawal: Made with pakoras and yogurt-based Kadhi, this dish is a popular delicacy in Kalka.
  • Parathas: Stuffed parathas filled with a variety of vegetables or paneer is a popular breakfast dish in Kalka.
  • Chhole Bhature: A spicy dish made with chickpeas and served with light and fluffy Bhature.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A yogurt-based drink that is famous in Kalka and surrounding areas.
  • Chai: The iconic Indian tea is available in every tea shop in Kalka.

Where to Stay in Kalka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Dharam Villa: A budget-friendly hotel that offers basic facilities and comfortable rooms to its guests.
  • Hotel Sai Gardens: A mid-range hotel that has a beautiful garden and excellent food options.
  • Hotel Legend Sarovar Portico: A luxury hotel that offers excellent facilities and services to its guests.

What to Buy in Kalka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Kalka has an impressive range of handicrafts, including traditional jewelry, wooden carvings, and textiles.
  • Spices: Kalka is famous for its spices and has an extensive range of locally sourced spices.
  • Local Snacks: Kalka has a unique range of snacks, including traditional sweets like Laddoos and Burfi.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalka

Kalka follows the traditional customs and cultures of North India. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kalka

The best time to visit Kalka is from March to June and then from September to November. The weather is pleasant during these months, and visitors can enjoy the outdoor activities without feeling too hot or cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalka

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.
  • Bargain when shopping in local markets.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol or non-vegetarian food in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothes in temples or other religious places.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kalka

Kalka is a safe destination for tourists, and visitors should avoid roaming around alone at night. Visitors should also be cautious when trekking or hiking, as the terrain can be challenging.

Health in Kalka

Visitors should carry their medication and avoid tap water. It is recommended to drink bottled water, and visitors should avoid street food to prevent any health concerns.

Getting to Kalka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kalka

  • Chandigarh International Airport: Located at a distance of approximately 35 km, this is the nearest airport to Kalka.

Bus Stations Near Kalka

  • Kalka Bus Stand: The Kalka Bus Stand is located in the heart of the town and offers a convenient way to travel in and around Kalka.

Other Options

  • Kalka-Shimla Railway: The Kalka-Shimla Railway is a popular tourist attraction and offers a unique way to experience the beauty of the Shivalik Range.
  • Taxis and Private Cars: Visitors can hire taxis or private cars to travel from nearby destinations to Kalka.

How to Get Around in Kalka

Visitors can travel around Kalka using local buses or taxis. Auto-rickshaws are also available for shorter distances. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Kalka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shimla: The hill station of Shimla is located at a distance of 90 km from Kalka and is a popular destination for tourists.
  • Chandigarh: Chandigarh, the capital of Punjab and Haryana, is located at a distance of 30 km from Kalka and is known for its modern architecture and beautiful gardens.

Conclusion

Kalka is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of small-town hospitality and natural beauty. From hiking trails to toy train rides, Kalka has a lot to offer to its visitors. Its unique culture, delicious food, and friendly people make it an excellent destination for anyone who wants to experience the traditional culture of India.
