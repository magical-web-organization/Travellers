Introduction to Kaithal

Kaithal is a beautiful city located in Ambala Division of Haryana, one of the 29 states in India. This city is famous for its historical significance and is a popular destination among travelers seeking to explore ancient Indian culture. Kaithal has a rich history dating back to the Mahabharata era and is home to several historical monuments and temples.

List of Places to Visit in Kaithal

Kaithal is a city that will leave you mesmerized with its historical and religious significance. From museums to natural attractions, there is something for everyone in Kaithal.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Raja Sardar Singh Museum: This museum is one of the most reputed museums in Haryana and is dedicated to the life of the famous ruler and his struggle for freedom.
  • Tomb of Razia Sultan: This historical monument is dedicated to Razia Sultana, who was the first Muslim monarch who ruled Delhi.
  • Pehowa Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to have been visited by the five Pandavas during their exile.

Natural Attractions of Kaithal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mound of Sheikh Chehli: This is an exquisite place to visit in Kaithal, which is also known as the birthplace of Sheikh Minhaj-ud-din Abu Bakr Chehli.
  • Bhindawas Lake: This lake is a perfect place to visit if you’re looking for some quiet time in nature. The wildlife sanctuary can also be found nearby.
  • Kapal Mochan: This temple is located near the banks of the Kaushalya river and is believed to be a sacred spot for Lord Shiva.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaithal

If you’re looking for some adventure activities in Kaithal, you can hire a bicycle and explore the city on your own. You can start from the old city region and cycle towards the Kapal Mochan temple, a distance of around 15 km.

Best Things to Do in Kaithal: Activities and Tours

  • Learn about Kaithal’s history and culture by visiting the museums and ancient temples.
  • Enjoy nature and solitude, by visiting the Bhindawas Lake and Kapal Mochan.
  • Explore the city’s unique architectural beauty by visiting the Tomb of Razia Sultan and the recently renovated Pehowa Temple.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaithal

Kaithal is known for its amazing food culture, which offers you delicious food and drinks that you can’t resist. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jalebi: This sweet dish is popular in Kaithal and is a perfect blend of crispy and soft.
  • Parantha: One of Kaithal’s must-try breakfast dishes is parantha, which can be served with various savory fillings such as potatoes, paneer or cauliflower.
  • Chaat: Kaithal’s most popular food item is chaat, which is a savory snack consisting of crispy fried dough.

Local Beverages

  • Chhas: This is a refreshing drink that’s made by mixing buttermilk with ground cumin, mint, and salt.
  • Lassi: This is a popular local beverage in Kaithal, which is a blend of yogurt, water, and sugar or salt.

What to Buy in Kaithal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kaithal offers various products that reflect its ancient culture and heritage. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should definitely bring back home:

  • Traditional Clothing: Kaithal is famous for its traditional ethnic clothing.
  • Handicrafts: Kaithal is known for its handicrafts, such as pottery and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Kaithal

People in Kaithal take pride in their ancient culture, and you will find this reflected in their daily life. During festivals and special occasions, the people of Kaithal show their zest and enthusiasm by following their unique traditional rituals.

Best Time to Visit Kaithal

The best time to visit Kaithal is during the winter season (November to February) when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaithal

Do:

  • Be respectful towards the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaithal

Kaithal is a safe city for travelers. However, it’s always advisable to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Health in Kaithal

Kaithal has decent medical facilities in case of any medical emergency. It’s advisable to carry your basic medicines and consult your doctor before traveling.

Where to Go Next from Kaithal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Kaithal is undoubtedly one of the historic and cultural gems of Haryana. Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Kaithal has something or the other in-store for everyone. So pack your bags and head to Kaithal for an inspiring, peaceful, and unforgettable journey.
