Introduction to Ambala

Ambala is a beautiful city located in Haryana, India. The city is known for its strategic location as it lies on the border of Punjab and Haryana. Ambala is a major tourist destination and attracts visitors from across the country. It is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. The city has something to offer everyone, be it history buffs, adventure seekers, or foodies.

List of Places to Visit in Ambala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ambala is home to several historical places and museums that are worth visiting. Some of the top cultural attractions in the city include:

  • Ambala Cantonment Railway Station: This is a historic railway station that was built in the British era. It is known for its beautiful architecture and has been declared a heritage site by the Indian Railways.
  • Sis Ganj Gurudwara: This is a famous Gurudwara located in Ambala. It is believed to have been visited by Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Sikh Guru.
  • Badshahi Bag Gurudwara: This is another famous Gurudwara located in Ambala. It was built in the memory of a Muslim pir, who is believed to have had supernatural powers.
  • Jain Mandir: This is a famous Jain temple located in Ambala. It is known for its beautiful architecture and peaceful ambiance.
  • Ambala Museum: This is a museum that showcases the history and culture of Ambala. It has a collection of artifacts, antiques, and other items related to the city’s history and culture.
  • Kapil Muni Temple: This is a famous temple located on the banks of River Saraswati. It is believed to be the abode of Rishi Kapil, who is said to have written the famous Samkhya philosophy.

Natural Attractions of Ambala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its cultural attractions, Ambala is also known for its natural beauty. The city is surrounded by lush green forests, hills, and lakes, making it an ideal destination for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts. Some of the top natural attractions in the city include:

  • Church of Resurrection: This is a beautiful church located in Ambala Cantonment. It is known for its gothic architecture and serene ambiance.
  • Kali Mata Temple: This is a famous temple located on a hill in Ambala. It offers breathtaking views of the city and is a popular spot for picnics and outings.
  • Bhawani Amba Temple: This is another famous temple located on a hill in Ambala. It is known for its scenic surroundings and offers a panoramic view of the city.
  • The Ghaggar River: This is a famous river that flows through Ambala. It offers opportunities for various water sports and is a popular spot for fishing and picnics.
  • Rani Ka Talab: This is a beautiful lake located in Ambala. It is known for its serene atmosphere and is a popular spot for boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ambala

Ambala offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Some of the top hiking/biking routes in the city include:

  • Ambala Cantonment to Sadar Bazar: This is a popular biking route that takes you through the heart of the city. It offers a glimpse of Ambala’s culture and tradition.
  • Kali Mata Temple to Bhawani Amba Temple: This is a popular hiking route that offers breathtaking views of the city. It is a moderate hike that can be completed in a few hours.
  • Ambala Cantonment to Rani Ka Talab: This is another popular biking route that takes you through the lush green fields and offers beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Barara to Saha: This is a popular biking route that takes you through the picturesque villages on the outskirts of Ambala. It offers a glimpse of rural life in Haryana.
  • Ambala Cantonment to Shahbad: This is a popular biking route that takes you through the scenic countryside of Haryana. It is a long ride but offers plenty of opportunities for rest and refreshments along the way.

Best Things to Do in Ambala: Activities and Tours

Ambala offers plenty of things to do for visitors of all ages. Some of the top activities and tours in the city include:

  • Visit the heritage sites: Ambala has several heritage sites that are worth visiting. Some of the must-visit sites include Ambala Cantonment Railway Station, Sis Ganj Gurudwara, and Badshahi Bag Gurudwara.
  • Go on a nature trail: Ambala is blessed with natural beauty and offers plenty of opportunities for nature trails. Some of the top nature trails in the city include Kali Mata Temple to Bhawani Amba Temple and Ambala Cantonment to Rani Ka Talab.
  • Discover the city’s food scene: Ambala is known for its mouth-watering street food. Some of the must-try dishes include chole bhature, aloo tikki, and samosa.
  • Visit the local markets: Ambala has several markets that offer a glimpse of the city’s culture and tradition. Some of the top markets in the city include Sadar Bazar and Cloth Market.
  • Take a historical tour: Ambala has a rich history and boasts several historical places. You can take a historical tour of the city and learn about its past.
  • Enjoy the nightlife: Ambala has a vibrant nightlife scene and offers plenty of options for party lovers. Some of the top nightclubs in the city include Club Lavish and Tripti Hotel Bar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ambala

If you’re planning to visit Ambala for a week, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day 1: Visit the heritage sites in the city, including Ambala Cantonment Railway Station, Sis Ganj Gurudwara, and Badshahi Bag Gurudwara.

Day 2: Go on a nature trail to Kali Mata Temple and Bhawani Amba Temple.

Day 3: Take a historical tour of the city and visit the Ambala Museum, Kapil Muni Temple, and other historical places.

Day 4: Discover the city’s food scene and indulge in some mouth-watering street food.

Day 5: Visit the local markets and shop for souvenirs and local products.

Day 6: Explore the nightlife scene of the city and party at some of the top nightclubs.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations like Chandigarh, Panchkula, or Kurukshetra.

Food and Drink Scene of Ambala

Ambala is known for its delicious street food and local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chole Bhature: This is a famous Punjabi dish that is a must-try in Ambala. It is a spicy chickpea curry served with fluffy deep-fried bread.
  • Aloo Tikki: This is a popular snack in Ambala. It is made of mashed potatoes, spices, and herbs, and is deep-fried until crispy.
  • Samosa: This is a famous snack in Ambala that is loved by locals and visitors alike. It is a pastry stuffed with spicy potatoes and peas.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: This is a refreshing drink made of yogurt, sugar, and spices. It is a must-try in Ambala, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Chaach: This is a buttermilk beverage that is very popular in Ambala. It is a great way to cool down after a spicy meal.

What to Buy in Ambala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ambala is known for its handicrafts, textiles, and leather goods. Some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Phulkari Dupatta: This is a famous embroidery style in Punjab and Haryana. You can buy Phulkari Dupattas in Ambala’s local markets.
  • Leather bags and shoes: Ambala is famous for its leather goods. You can buy leather bags, shoes, and belts from the local markets.
  • Handicrafts: Ambala is known for its handicrafts, including pottery, woodwork, and brass items. You can buy them from local artisans or from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ambala

Ambala has a rich culture and tradition. The people of Ambala are friendly and welcoming. Here are some customs and traditions that you should be aware of:

  • Dress code: Ambala is a conservative city, and it is advisable to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Etiquette: The people of Ambala are warm and hospitable. It is courteous to greet people and exchange pleasantries when meeting someone.
  • Festivals: Ambala celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including Diwali, Holi, and Baisakhi. These festivals offer a glimpse of the city’s culture and tradition.

Best Time to Visit Ambala

The best time to visit Ambala is between October and March. The weather during this time is pleasant, and the city is buzzing with activity. The monsoon season, between July and September, should be avoided as the city receives heavy rainfall, and there are chances of flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ambala

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and street food.
  • Explore the city’s heritage and culture.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter the city.
  • Do not take pictures without permission, especially of people.
  • Do not wear revealing clothes in public places.
  • Do not disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ambala

Ambala is a safe city for visitors. However, it is advisable to take the following precautions:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be cautious when dealing with strangers.

Health in Ambala

Ambala has good medical facilities, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the city. However, it is advisable to take the following precautions:

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Avoid street food that is not well cooked.
  • Carry essential medications with you.

Getting to Ambala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ambala is well-connected to other parts of India by road, rail, and air. Here are the transportation options available to reach Ambala:

Airports Near Ambala

  • Chandigarh International Airport: This is the nearest airport to Ambala, located around 47 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Ambala

  • Ambala Bus Stand: This is the main bus station in Ambala, and buses from all major cities in Haryana and Punjab stop here.

Other Options

  • Train: Ambala Cantonment Railway Station is well-connected to other parts of India by rail.
  • Taxi: Taxis and cabs are easily available in Ambala and can be hired for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Ambala

Ambala has a good network of public transportation, including buses and taxis. You can also hire bicycles and motorcycles to explore the city on your own.

Where to Go Next from Ambala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ambala is located close to several popular tourist destinations in India. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Chandigarh: Located around 45 kilometers from Ambala, Chandigarh is known for its beautiful gardens and modern architecture.
  • Kurukshetra: Located around 40 kilometers from Ambala, Kurukshetra is a famous pilgrim destination known for its association with the Mahabharata.
  • Shimla: Located around 140 kilometers from Ambala, Shimla is a popular hill station known for its picturesque views and romantic ambiance.

Conclusion

Ambala is a beautiful city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or foodie, you’ll find plenty of things to do in Ambala. From its heritage sites and museums to its natural beauty and food scene, Ambala will leave you mesmerized with its charm and hospitality. So pack your bags, and head to Ambala for an unforgettable experience!
