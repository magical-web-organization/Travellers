Introduction to Ambala Division

Located in the plains of Haryana, in northern India, Ambala Division offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Known for its significant role in the Indian Independence Movement, the division is home to several historical sites, museums, and cultural attractions. This comprehensive guide will take you through the best places to visit, hiking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, local culture, and transportation options in Ambala Division.

List of Places to Visit in Ambala Division

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ambala Division is home to several culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places that attract visitors from all over the world. Here are the top attractions you should not miss:

  • Gandhi Museum: Housed in the Anand Bhawan, the museum showcases the life and times of Mahatma Gandhi, India’s father of the nation.
  • Badshahi Bag Gurudwara: This historical site holds an important place in the history of Sikh religion and is associated with Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Sikh Guru.
  • Karnal Fort: Located in Karnal, this historical site dates back to the 18th century and is famous for its architecture and historical significance.
  • Yamunanagar-Jagadhri Railway Station: The railway station has been declared a heritage site and is famous for its colonial-style architecture.

Natural Attractions of Ambala Division: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its rich cultural history, Ambala Division is also blessed with natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Here are the top attractions to explore:

  • Kalesar National Park: This wildlife sanctuary boasts abundant flora and fauna and offers a breathtaking view of the Shivalik hills.
  • Spotting migratory birds: Ambala Division is home to several wetlands, which offer the perfect habitat for migratory birds during the winter months.
  • Sukhna Lake: Located in Chandigarh, Sukhna Lake is a man-made lake that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and the city.
  • Pinjore Gardens: Spread over 100 acres, this Mughal-style garden offers a tranquil atmosphere and a stunning view of the Shivalik hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ambala Division

If you’re an adventure enthusiast, Ambala Division offers several hiking and biking routes that will take you through scenic views and historic sites. Here are the top routes you should explore:

  • Kalesar Mahadev Temple Trek: This trek takes you through a dense forest and offers a spectacular view of the Kalesar National Park.
  • Yadavindra Gardens Trek: This trek takes you through the Pinjore Gardens and offers a stunning view of the Shivalik hills.
  • Pine Forest Trail: This hike takes you through a dense pine forest and offers a stunning view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Ambala Division: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and hiking/biking, Ambala Division offers several activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are the top things to do in Ambala Division:

  • Food tour: The food scene in Ambala Division is diverse and flavorful, offering traditional dishes and street food that you should not miss.
  • Heritage walk in Chandigarh: Take a heritage walk in Chandigarh to explore its stunning architecture and history.
  • Crafts and Handloom tour in Panipat: Explore the weaving traditions of Panipat through a crafts and handloom tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Ambala Division

No trip to Ambala Division is complete without savoring its rich food and drink scene. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Samosa: A popular street food in Ambala Division, Samosa is a deep-fried pastry stuffed with spiced potatoes and peas.
  • Paratha: A famous breakfast dish, Paratha is a flaky bread stuffed with vegetables and served with yogurt or pickle.
  • Kadhi: Made with yogurt, gram flour, and lentil dumplings, Kadhi is a traditional Punjabi dish that is best served with steamed rice.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A popular Punjabi drink made with yogurt, Lassi is best when served chilled and flavored with fruit.
  • Chai: The staple beverage of India, Chai is a spiced tea made with milk and sugar and is perfect for any time of the day.
  • Thandai: A refreshing drink made with almonds, fennel seeds, and milk, Thandai is perfect for the scorching summer months of Ambala Division.

Where to Stay in Ambala Division: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ambala Division offers a range of accommodations, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Here are our top picks:

  • The Oberoi Sukhvilas Resort and Spa: Located in the foothills of the Shivalik range, this luxury resort offers world-class amenities and stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Hotel Sharada: A budget-friendly hotel in Karnal, Hotel Sharada is ideal for travelers looking for comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.
  • Haveli Heritage Inn: Located in Panipat, this guesthouse is housed in a 150-year-old heritage building and offers a glimpse of the local culture and history of the city.

What to Buy In Ambala Division: Souvenirs and Local Products To Bring Home

Ambala Division is famous for its handicrafts, handloom products, and food items that make for excellent souvenirs and gifts. Here are the top things to buy in Ambala Division:

  • Phulkaris: These embroidered shawls and dupattas are a traditional handloom product of Ambala Division and are available in several colors and designs.
  • Panipat Blankets: Known for its high-quality woolen blankets, Panipat is a must-visit for anyone looking for warm and cozy blankets.
  • Punjabi Jootis: These colorful, traditional shoes are a must-buy for anyone looking to add a traditional touch to their wardrobes.

Local Culture and Customs of Ambala Division

Ambala Division is rich in cultural traditions, customs, and festivals. Here are a few things you should know before visiting:

  • Language: The primary language spoken in Ambala Division is Hindi, Punjabi, and English.
  • Dress Code: It is advisable to dress modestly in Ambala Division, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Festivals: Major festivals celebrated in Ambala Division include Diwali, Holi, and Baisakhi.

Best Time to Visit Ambala Division

The best time to visit Ambala Division is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
