Introduction to Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra, a city in Haryana, India, is known for its cultural, historical, and religious significance. This is the place where the epic battle of the Mahabharata was fought between the Kauravas and Pandavas. The city is also revered for its sacred shrines, ancient temples, and other important historical sites. Kurukshetra is an excellent destination for those interested in immersing themselves in the rich culture and history of India.

List of Places to Visit in Kurukshetra

There are many places to visit in Kurukshetra, ranging from culturally important attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places in Kurukshetra:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kurukshetra Panorama and Science Centre: This is a museum depicting the Mahabharata war through dioramas and light and sound shows. It also has a science centre with an array of interactive exhibits on science.

  • Sthaneshwar Mahadev Temple: This is an ancient Hindu temple with a deep history in the Mahabharata. The temple worships Lord Shiva and is a popular place of worship for the city’s residents.

  • Brahma Sarovar: A designated heritage site by the Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH), Brahma Sarovar is a large sacred water tank that is believed to be the spot where Lord Brahma created the universe.

Natural Attractions of Kurukshetra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sheikh Chilli Tomb: This is a mausoleum dedicated to a famous Sufi saint from the 13th century. The tomb is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and is surrounded by a lush garden.

  • Kurukshetra Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to a variety of animals such as blackbuck, nilgai, and wild boar. It also has numerous hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities.

  • Holy Tanks: Kurukshetra is home to many sacred water tanks, namely Sannihit Sarovar, Brahma Sarovar, and Panchkula Tirth.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurukshetra

There are many hiking and biking routes that can be explored in Kurukshetra. The Kurukshetra Wildlife Sanctuary is the perfect place for hiking and wildlife spotting. The region is also home to several cycling tours that explore the sites and countryside outside of the city.

Best Things to Do in Kurukshetra: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Kurukshetra include:

  • Participate in the Gita Jayanti celebrations: Gita Jayanti is an annual event that celebrates the birth of the Bhagavad Gita. The event is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Kurukshetra, making it an unforgettable experience.

  • Take a guided tour of the historical sites: Kurukshetra’s heritage sites and museums are some of the city’s most iconic attractions. Guided tours offer an insight into the history and significance of each location.

  • Experience the vibrancy of the local bazaars: Kurukshetra’s bustling bazaars are perfect for buying local handicrafts, jewellery, and souvenirs. Don’t forget to haggle for the best prices!

Week-Long Itinerary for Kurukshetra

Food and Drink Scene of Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra is a culinary paradise, with a mix of both traditional and modern food options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kachori: A crispy snack filled with a mix of lentils, onions, and spices.

  • Parantha: A popular breakfast item made from flour stuffed with potatoes, vegetables, or paneer.

  • Paneer Tikka: Grilled cubes of paneer served with a spicy marinade.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing yoghurt-based drink flavoured with fruits or spices.

  • Chaach: A thin yoghurt-based drink that usually accompanies meals.

What to Buy in Kurukshetra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kurukshetra’s bazaars are known for their textiles, handicrafts, and jewellery. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Phulkari: A traditional embroidery form that is used to create stunning fabrics and garments.

  • Silver jewellery: Kurukshetra is known for its silver jewellery, which is crafted by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra has a rich heritage that is deeply ingrained in its local customs and traditions. Visitors are expected to dress modestly, cover their heads when entering places of worship, and remove their shoes before entering some temples.

Best Time to Visit Kurukshetra

The best time to visit Kurukshetra is from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season (June - September) can be wet and humid, while summers (April - June) can be sweltering.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurukshetra

Do:

  • Carry cash as many local establishments may not accept card payments.

  • Wear modest clothing when visiting places of worship.

  • Book your accommodations and tours in advance.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.

  • Hesitate to haggle at the local markets. It is expected!

  • Waste food in restaurants. Food is considered holy in Kurukshetra.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra is considered safe for tourists. However, it is advised to take precautions like not walking alone late at night or flashing valuable items in public as petty theft is common in tourist areas.

Health in Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra is a relatively healthy city, but travellers should drink bottled water and avoid street food to avoid water and food-borne illnesses. It’s also advised to acclimatize to the city’s weather conditions to avoid any health complications.

Getting to Kurukshetra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kurukshetra

The nearest airports to Kurukshetra are the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi (160 km) and the Chandigarh International Airport (92 km). From there, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Kurukshetra.

Bus Stations Near Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra is well-connected to other cities via a network of state-run and private buses. The main bus stand is located near the railway station and offers a range of bus services to and from other cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private taxis or rent cars to travel to Kurukshetra from nearby cities like Delhi or Chandigarh.

How to Get Around in Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra is a walkable city, and visitors can explore most of its attractions on foot or by bicycle. Another popular mode of transportation is auto-rickshaws, which offer convenient access to all parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Kurukshetra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular destinations to visit near Kurukshetra include:

  • Chandigarh: Known for its modern architecture, beautiful gardens, and delicious food.

  • Ambala: A historic city with a rich cultural heritage.

  • Delhi: The capital city of India and a vibrant destination renowned for its historical sites, shopping, and food scene.

Conclusion

Kurukshetra is a unique destination that appeals to visitors interested in the intersection of history, culture, and religion. With its plethora of museums, historical sites, natural wonders, and delicious food, Kurukshetra is an excellent destination for anyone looking to dive deep into the rich cultural heritage of India.
