Introduction to Puri

Puri is a bustling coastal city located in the eastern Indian state of Odisha. Reputed for housing the famous Lord Jagannath Temple, Puri is a popular pilgrimage destination that draws hordes of devotees annually. But the city has much more to offer than just its spiritual significance - from beautiful beaches to historical monuments to serene natural landscapes. The city’s warm and hospitable people, its delicious cuisine, and a thriving arts and crafts scene will win over the hearts of many.

List of Places to Visit in Puri

Here’s a list of top attractions and must-visit places in Puri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lord Jagannath Temple: One of the four holiest shrines in India, this temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is renowned for its annual Rath Yatra or chariot festival which sees three enormous chariots carrying the deities of Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra and sister Subhadra pulled through the streets by devotees.
  2. Konark Sun Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site famed for its remarkable architecture, the Konark Sun Temple is among the most revered Hindu temples in India. The temple was built in the 13th century and is shaped like a colossal chariot with 12 wheels, each seven feet in diameter.
  3. Chilika Lake: Asia’s largest brackish water lagoon, Chilika Lake provides sanctuary to an impressive variety of migratory birds during the winter season. The lake is also known for the scenic islands dotting its expanse, such as Parikud and the Kalijai Island, which is believed to be the abode of the goddess Kalijai Devi.
  4. Puri Beach: This pristine beach presents a breathtaking view of the deep blue sea, amidst which lies the magnificent Jagannath Temple. A morning walk along the beach is ideal to experience the tranquil and serene atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Puri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Puri Beach: This beautiful beach is an ideal place to laze around, sunbathe, or take a dip in the cool waters of the Bay of Bengal.
  2. Balukhand Wildlife Sanctuary: Spread across 87 square kilometers, the natural sanctuary is home to lush green forests, wild animals such as blackbuck, spotted deer, and foxes, and a number of exotic bird species. The sanctuary also has nature trails for a nature walk.
  3. Atharanala Bridge: An amazing spot for bird watching in Puri, Atharanala Bridge is a popular viewpoint that offers a stunning panoramic view of the Chilika Lake and surrounding areas.
  4. Pipili Village: Known for its applique work, Pipili Village is the go-to place for admirers of handicrafts. The village is a popular shopping destination, primarily for handcrafted sculptures, textiles, and home decor items.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puri

Puri is a great place for hiking and biking, given its amazing natural terrain and scenic landscape. Here are some hiking trails and biking routes around the city:

  • Konark-Puri Road: A scenic stretch of road that takes you through some of the most picturesque locations in Odisha.
  • Raghurajpur Artisan Village: A great place for a leisurely bike ride along quaint and rustic village roads lined with greenery.
  • Balukhand-Konark Wildlife Sanctuary: The forested terrain of Balukhand-Konark Wildlife Sanctuary is a great spot for trekking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Puri: Activities and Tours

From exploring the city’s cultural landmarks to enjoying its natural scenery, there is something for everyone in Puri. Here are some of the best things to do in Puri:

  • Experience the Rath Yatra: A remarkable spectacle that takes place once every year, the Rath Yatra or chariot festival sees the palanquins of Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra and sister Subhadra taken out in a grand procession through the city.
  • Enjoy Water Sports: Water sports such as surfing, parasailing, and speed boat rides are a great way to beat the heat and experience the thrill of adventure.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Puri has some of the most delicious food in Eastern India, so it’s a must-try for foodies. From street food to fine dining, the city has plenty of options to choose from.
  • Take Boat Rides: Boating in Chilika Lake is an unforgettable experience. The tranquil waters and breathtaking view of the islands make for a lovely way to spend an afternoon.
  • Witness Traditional Artforms: Puri is a great place to witness traditional Indian art forms such as Pattachitra, Odissi dance, and the Dhokra art form.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puri

Here’s a comprehensive seven-day itinerary that covers the most famous tourist spots in and around Puri:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Visit Lord Jagannath Temple and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Visit Konark Sun Temple in the morning. Drive down to Chilika Lake and enjoy the boat ride. Return to Puri and relax at Puri Beach.

Day 3: Experience the Rath Yatra festival. Spend the evening exploring Puri’s markets and indulging in street food.

Day 4: Visit Pipili Village and see the applique work of the locals. In the evening, witness the traditional Odissi dance.

Day 5: Enjoy water sports at Puri Beach. Take a bike ride to Raghurajpur Artisan Village.

Day 6: Trek/hike through Balukhand-Konark Wildlife Sanctuary. Take in the scenic views of the surrounding forests and the countryside.

Day 7: Visit Atharanala Bridge for bird watching in the morning, followed by some souvenir shopping. Departure from Puri.

Food and Drink Scene of Puri

Puri’s cuisine is a delightful mix of vegetarian and non-vegetarian delicacies. Some must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chenna Poda: A locally made dessert made by baking cottage cheese, sugar, and cardamom pods, perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.
  2. Khaja: A flaky pastry-like dessert that is both crunchy and sweet.
  3. Dalma: A tasty lentil-based vegetable stew, often prepared during festivals.
  4. Mahura: A creamy and delicious mutton curry, this is a staple of the local non-vegetarian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Pakhala: A popular drink made of rice that is left to ferment overnight in water. It’s often served with fish curry, roasted vegetables, or green chilies.
  2. Bel Shrabat: A refreshing drink made with ripe bel fruits, which are sweet and tangy.
  3. Coconut water: A refreshing and healthy beverage that is perfect for beating the heat.

Where to Stay in Puri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puri has an array of accommodation options for all budgets. There are some luxurious hotels like Mayfair Heritage, and Camelia Hotel & Resort, and more pocket-friendly options like Hotel Sonar Bangla and Hotel Nayak Plaza. There are plenty of guesthouses and hostels as well that cater to young backpackers.

What to Buy in Puri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping for souvenirs in Puri, a few things to keep in mind are the city’s rich artistic heritage and its craftsmanship. Some popular items include:

  • Pattachitra paintings: A widely popular artform in Odisha, Pattachitra paintings are done on palm leaves, canvas, or silk cloth.
  • Odissi musical instruments: Traditional Odissi instruments like Mardal (a percussion instrument) and Bansi (a bamboo flute) make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Textiles: Odisha is known for its intricate textiles, so textiles such as Sambalpuri sarees or Bhagalpuri silk stoles are worth the investment.

Local Culture and Customs of Puri

Puri’s people are known for their laid-back and hospitable nature. It’s important to dress according to the occasion and respect the sentiments of the devotees visiting the temples. Owing to the fact that Puri is a temple town, alcohol consumption in public places is prohibited.

Best Time to Visit Puri

The most favourable time to visit the city is between October and March when the climate is moderate, neither too hot nor too cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puri

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts when visiting Puri:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local food - it’s a must-try.
  • Strike a conversation with locals. They are known for being warm and welcoming.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting temples.
  • Litter on the beach or in public places.
  • Drink alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

When visiting Puri, the following safety and health measures should be kept in mind:

Safety in Puri

  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean, and keep an eye on the tides.
  • Avoid visiting isolated places at night, and stick to well-lit and populated areas.

Health in Puri

  • Keep bottles of water handy to avoid dehydration.
  • Carry mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue.

Getting to Puri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Puri is well-connected to the rest of the country by air, rail, and road.

Airports Near Puri

Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar is the nearest airport, located at a distance of 60 kilometres from Puri. Taxis are readily available from the airport to Puri.

Bus Stations Near Puri

Puri has a well-connected bus service which connects the city to Bhubaneswar, Cuttack, and other places in Odisha.

Other Options

Trains are a more popular option to reach Puri. Puri railway station is located in the heart of the city and is well-connected to other cities in India.

How to Get Around in Puri

Puri is a small city and can be explored on foot, but if you plan on travelling to neighbouring towns, taxis, and auto-rickshaws are readily available. Bikes and cycles are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Puri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that you can visit after Puri, such as:

  • Bhubaneswar: The capital city of Odisha is known for its Khandagiri and Udayagiri Caves and Lingaraj Temple.
  • Gopalpur: A beautiful town located in southern Odisha famous for its serene beaches, the nearest being the Gopalpur-on-Sea beach.
  • Chandrabhaga: An ancient town located 3 km from Konark where you can explore the Konark Sun Temple.

Conclusion

Puri is an excellent destination for travellers looking for a mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. The city is a haven for people who want to explore the rich cultural heritage of India, immerse themselves in history, and witness some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in Eastern India. With our comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your dream trip to Puri.
