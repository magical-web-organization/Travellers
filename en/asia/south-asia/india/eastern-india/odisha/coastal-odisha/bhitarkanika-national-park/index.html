Introduction to Bhitarkanika National Park

Located in Coastal Odisha, Bhitarkanika National Park is one of the most popular and biodiverse national parks in India. The park, which covers an area of 145 km2, is home to a large number of mangroves, saltwater crocodiles, olive ridley turtles, king cobras, and a variety of bird species.

Bhitarkanika National Park is one of the few places in India where you can enjoy both natural beauty and cultural heritage. From museums to historical places, outdoor adventures to scenic views, Bhitarkanika National Park has something for everyone.

In this tour guide, we will take a closer look at the Bhitarkanika National Park and its many attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Bhitarkanika National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dangmal: Dangmal is a small village located within the park that serves as the administrative center for the national park. It is also home to a museum that showcases the history, culture, and biodiversity of Bhitarkanika.
  2. Ekakula: Ekakula is an archaeological site that dates back to the 9th century AD. The site contains the ruins of a temple that was built by the Keshari dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Bhitarkanika National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Boat safari: The boat safari is one of the most popular activities in Bhitarkanika National Park. You’ll get to explore the creeks and waterways of the park and spot a variety of wildlife, including saltwater crocodiles and a multitude of bird species.
  2. Mangrove forests: The mangrove forests of Bhitarkanika are a sight to behold. They cover around 62 km2 of the park and are home to a large number of plant and animal species.
  3. Gahirmatha Beach: Gahirmatha Beach is one of the few beaches in India where you can see Olive Ridley Turtles. From November to February, the beach is littered with the eggs of Olive Ridley Turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhitarkanika National Park

  1. Trekking: Bhitarkanika National Park has several trekking routes that will take you through the dense forests and mangrove swamps. Make sure to go with a guide.
  2. Biking: There are several bike rental companies near the park that offer bikes for rent. For those who want to explore the park at their own pace, biking is a great option.

Best Things to Do in Bhitarkanika National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat safari: As mentioned before, the boat safari is a must-do activity in Bhitarkanika National Park, and it’s also one of the most popular.
  2. Turtle watching: The Olive Ridley turtles that come to Gahirmatha Beach to lay eggs are a sight to behold. Be sure to book a guided tour for this activity.
  3. Wildlife photography: With such diverse wildlife and natural scenery, Bhitarkanika National Park is a photographer’s paradise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bhitarkanika National Park

Day 1: Arrive in Bhitarkanika National Park Day 2: Take a boat safari and explore the mangrove forests Day 3: Trek through the park and spot wildlife Day 4: Visit Dangmal and the museum Day 5: Take a guided tour to Gahirmatha Beach and witness Olive Ridley turtles laying eggs. Day 6: Visit Ekakula, the archaeological site Day 7: Depart from Bhitarkanika National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Bhitarkanika National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chena Tarkari: Chena Tarkari is a traditional Odia dish made with cottage cheese and freshly ground mustard.
  2. Macher Jhol: Macher Jhol is a fish curry that is popular in Coastal Odisha.

Local Beverages

  1. Mahua: Mahua is an alcoholic beverage made from the flowers of the Mahua tree.
  2. Coconut Water: Coconut water is a refreshing drink that is readily available at most places in Bhitarkanika National Park.

Where to Stay in Bhitarkanika National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Bhitarkanika Nature Resort: This resort is located within the park and offers luxurious tent accommodations.
  2. Panthashala: Panthashala is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable accommodations near the park entrance.

What to Buy in Bhitarkanika National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Handicrafts such as traditional wall hangings and wood carvings make for great souvenirs.
  2. Decorative Items: Decorative items made using bamboo and coconut shells are popular in Bhitarkanika National Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhitarkanika National Park

The people of Bhitarkanika are friendly and welcoming. The local culture is heavily influenced by Hinduism, and the people celebrate several festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Bhitarkanika National Park

The best time to visit Bhitarkanika National Park is from November to February. During these months, the weather is pleasant and the park is bustling with wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhitarkanika National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Follow the rules and regulations of the national park.
  3. Take a guide with you for all outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or pollute the park.
  2. Disturb the wildlife or their habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhitarkanika National Park

Bhitarkanika National Park is safe for tourists, but it is important to take precautions such as carrying a first aid kit and staying hydrated.

Health in Bhitarkanika National Park

It is advisable to take anti-malaria medication before visiting Bhitarkanika National Park. In addition, it is recommended to carry insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Bhitarkanika National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bhitarkanika National Park

  1. Bhubaneswar Airport: Bhubaneswar Airport is the nearest airport to Bhitarkanika National Park, located around 130 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bhitarkanika National Park

  1. Bhadrak: Bhadrak is the nearest town to Bhitarkanika National Park, and it has several bus stations that are well connected to the rest of Odisha.

Other Options

  1. Train: The nearest railway stations to Bhitarkanika National Park are Bhadrak and Cuttack.

How to Get Around in Bhitarkanika National Park

The best way to get around in Bhitarkanika National Park is by boat or on foot. Guided tours are available, and it’s always advisable to take a guide with you for all outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Bhitarkanika National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Konark: Konark is located around 175 km from Bhitarkanika National Park and is home to the famous Konark Sun Temple.
  2. Puri: Puri is a coastal town located around 160 km from Bhitarkanika National Park, and it is famous for the Jagannath Temple.

Conclusion

Bhitarkanika National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Odisha. With its natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage, Bhitarkanika National Park has something for everyone. Whether you’re a wildlife enthusiast or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Bhitarkanika National Park is the perfect destination.
