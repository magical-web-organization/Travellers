Visit Kendrapara: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

If you’re craving salty sea air and beautiful beaches, Kendrapara, in Odisha, Eastern India, is a must-see destination. This small town is filled with both indoor and outdoor attractions, including cultural landmarks, scenic views and welcoming accommodations. In this comprehensive travel guide, you will discover all the top things to do, see, and experience in the beautiful coastal town of Kendrapara.

List of Places to Visit in Kendrapara

Whether you’re looking for local flavor, outdoor adventures, or historical landmarks, Kendrapara offers something for every traveler. Here are the top attractions that make this coastal Indian town a must-visit destination.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kendrapara Museum: Explore the history of Kendrapara and its people through photographs, artifacts, and exhibits. Learn about the town’s unique heritage and local customs.

  • Mahavinayak Temple: A prominent Hindu temple known for its stunning architecture and religious significance. Admire the detailed carvings and statues of Hindu deities, and participate in the daily prayers and rituals.

  • Ramachandi Temple: A popular pilgrimage spot among the locals. This temple is dedicated to Goddess Ramachandi and offers breathtaking views of the Bay of Bengal.

Natural Attractions of Kendrapara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gahirmatha Beach and Wildlife Sanctuary: Famous for its large population of Olive Ridley sea turtles, who come to nest on the beach every winter. Take a tour of the sanctuary to learn about the conservation efforts to protect these endangered species.

  • Bhitarkanika National Park: One of the largest mangrove ecosystems in India. Take a boat ride through the park to spot crocodiles, kingfishers, and other wildlife.

  • Bhitar Kanika Mangrove Forest: Explore the greenery and wilderness of the mangrove forest through a guided walk or boat tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kendrapara

If you’re looking for adventure, Kendrapara has several hiking and biking trails that showcase the town’s lush forests and stunning coastline. Here are some of the popular routes you can take:

  • Kendrapara to Gopinathpur: A scenic route that passes through lush banana and coconut plantations, and quaint villages.

  • Dangamala to Ekakula: A popular trek in the Bhitarkanika National Park. This trail takes you through the mangrove forests, and you can spot various bird species and crocodiles along the way.

  • Gahirmatha to Paradip: A coastal bike ride that offers stunning views of the Bay of Bengal.

Best Things to Do in Kendrapara: Activities and Tours

There are numerous fun-filled activities and tours that will keep you busy during your stay in Kendrapara. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Olive Ridley Turtle Tour: Witness the nesting and hatching of the endangered Olive Ridley sea turtles in the Gahirmatha Beach sanctuary.

  • Boat Ride in Bhitarkanika National Park: Take a tranquil boat ride through the mangrove forests, and spot various species of crocodiles, kingfishers, monkeys, and deer.

  • Water Activities: Participate in water activities such as fishing, boating, and jet skiing in the Chilika Lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kendrapara

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kendrapara, here’s an itinerary that will let you experience the best of the town:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Mahavinayak Temple and Ramachandi Temple
2 Take a tour of the Kendrapara Museum and learn about the town’s history
3 Explore the Bhitarkanika National Park, take a boat ride and visit the mangrove forest
4 Spend the day at the Gahirmatha Beach sanctuary, witness the Olive Ridley sea turtles nesting
5 Take a bike ride to the nearby town of Paradip
6 Participate in water activities such as fishing and boating in the Chilika Lake
7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine and hospitality in one of the town’s comfortable accommodations

Food and Drink Scene of Kendrapara

Kendrapara is famous for its seafood and unique flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machha Ghanta: A spicy fish curry made with potato, tomato, and a blend of local spices.

  • Kakharu Kofta: A unique vegetarian delicacy made with pumpkin and gram flour.

  • Mango Panna: A refreshing drink made with raw mango pulp, sugar, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sattu: A nutritious drink made with roasted Bengal gram flour, water, and spices.

  • Bael Sharbat: A sweet drink made with bael fruit pulp, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Kendrapara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kendrapara offers a range of comfortable accommodations that fit every budget and preference. Here are some of the town’s best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Bhitarkanika Nature and Wildlife Resort: This eco-friendly resort is located near the Bhitarkanika National Park and offers comfortable lodgings, picture-perfect views, and delicious meals.

  • Hotel Dolphin: A budget-friendly option that is located near the town’s tourist attractions. The rooms are clean, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

  • Hotel Royal Palace: A comfortable hotel that offers spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and delicious meals.

What to Buy in Kendrapara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kendrapara is known for its local handicrafts and souvenirs that make ideal gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the items to look out for:

  • Patta Chitra: Paintings made on palm leaves, with religious or historical themes.

  • Ganjapa Cards: Traditional Indian playing cards, with intricate designs and bright colors.

  • Tussar Silk Sarees: Locally woven silk sarees, with unique designs and vibrant colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Kendrapara

Kendrapara is a traditional Indian town that has its unique customs and culture. Here are some things to keep in mind during your stay in Kendrapara:

  • Temples: Dress conservatively when visiting the temples, and remember to remove your shoes before entering.

  • Food: Kendrapara is known for its spicy and flavorful cuisine. If you have a sensitive palate, be sure to specify the spice levels when ordering.

  • Greeting: Indian people are friendly and hospitable, and it’s polite to greet them with a Namaste (folded hands) or a smile.

Best Time to Visit Kendrapara

The best time to visit Kendrapara is during the winter months of November to February, when the weather is pleasant, and the sea turtles come to nest. The monsoon season, from June to September, brings heavy rains and is best avoided.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kendrapara

When visiting Kendrapara, it’s essential to respect the local customs and norms. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay:

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites or temples.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and flavors.

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are not allowed and should be avoided.
  • Do not litter; always dispose of the waste in designated areas.

Safety and Health

Kendrapara, like any other town, has its safety and health hazards. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Kendrapara

  • Always carry your identification and important documents with you.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Be careful when swimming in the sea; always check for warning signs and currents.

Health in Kendrapara

  • Always carry some basic medical supplies such as painkillers, antiseptics, and band-aids.
  • Avoid tap water and always drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Apply mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Kendrapara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kendrapara is easily accessible through various modes of transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Kendrapara

  • Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar (137 km away)

Bus Stations Near Kendrapara

  • Kendrapara Bus Stand
  • Paradip Bus Stand

Other Options

  • Taxis and auto-rickshaws are the primary modes of local transportation within the town.

How to Get Around in Kendrapara

Exploring the town’s attractions is easy with the following modes of transportation:

  • Auto-rickshaws and taxis are readily available.

  • Bicycles and motorbikes are available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Kendrapara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, you can explore some of the nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Puri: A popular pilgrimage destination known for the Jagannath temple and stunning beach.

  • Konark: Home to the Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and one of the most famous temples in India.

  • Chilika Lake: One of the largest brackish water lagoons in the world, known for its scenic views and water activities.
