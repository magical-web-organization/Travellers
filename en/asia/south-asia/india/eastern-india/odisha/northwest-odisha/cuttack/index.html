Introduction to Cuttack

Located in the northwest region of the Odisha state in India, Cuttack is a vibrant city that boasts of a rich history and cultural heritage. It is situated on the banks of the Mahanadi River and is renowned for its art, literature, and architecture. Often referred to as the “Silver City” of India, Cuttack is an unexplored gem that offers a perfect blend of historic attractions, natural wonders, and delectable cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Cuttack

If you are planning a visit to Cuttack, here is a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Netaji Birth Place Museum: A museum that is dedicated to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, a legendary freedom fighter, and displays artifacts and personal items from his life.

  • Barabati Fort: A medieval fort that is located on the banks of the Mahanadi River and is an epitome of ancient architecture.

  • Stone Revetment: A historical site that dates back to the 11th century, and is an example of the finest stone architecture of ancient times.

  • Paradeep Port: A natural harbor that is known for its scenic beauty and is a perfect spot for nature lovers.

  • Odisha Maritime Museum: A museum that showcases the glory and heritage of Odisha’s maritime history through maps, photographs, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Cuttack: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mahanadi River: A river that originates in Chhattisgarh and flows through Odisha, providing scenic views that are ideal for a peaceful getaway.

  • Dhabaleswar Island: An island located on the Mahanadi River, that is known for its famous Dhabaleswar Temple and is a popular picnic spot.

  • Bhitarkanika National Park: A national park that is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including crocodiles, turtles, and birds, and offers boat safaris and trekking expeditions.

  • Ansupa Lake: A freshwater lake that is surrounded by lush green forests and is a popular spot for bird watching and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cuttack

If you are an adventure enthusiast, Cuttack offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes that you can explore:

  • Mahanadi River Trail: A scenic route that follows the banks of the Mahanadi River, providing an excellent opportunity to experience the beauty of Cuttack’s countryside.

  • Bhitarkanika Trek: A trekking expedition that takes you through the dense forests of Bhitarkanika National Park and offers a thrilling experience of spotting wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Dhabaleswar Island Cycling: A cycling expedition that takes you on a tour of the famous Dhabaleswar Island, providing an opportunity to explore the island and soak in the scenic beauty of the Mahanadi River.

Best Things to Do in Cuttack: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the city’s natural and cultural attractions, here are some of the best things to do in Cuttack:

  • Visit Local Markets: Cuttack’s local markets are a treasure trove of handicrafts, textiles, jewelry, and local delicacies, offering an opportunity to indulge in some retail therapy.

  • Attend Festivals: Cuttack is known for its vibrant festivals like Bali Jatra, Durga Puja, and Kali Puja, which offer a glimpse into the city’s cultural heritage.

  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn to cook authentic Odia cuisine with a cooking class, which will take you through the process of preparing traditional dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Cuttack

Cuttack is known for its delectable cuisine that is sure to tantalize your taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chhena Poda: A spongy and caramelized dessert made with cheese and sugar.

  • Mutton Curry: A flavorful curry made with succulent mutton pieces and aromatic spices.

  • Dalma: A nutritious dish made with lentils, vegetables, and mustard oil.

  • Machha Bhaja: A crispy and spicy fried fish dish that is a specialty of Odisha.

Local Beverages

  • Poda Poda: A traditional drink made with fermented rice, coconut water, and jaggery.

  • Bel Pana: A refreshing drink made with bel fruit pulp, yogurt, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Cuttack: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cuttack has a wide range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guest houses.

  • Mayfair Lagoon: A luxury resort that features spacious rooms, world-class amenities, and scenic views of the Mahanadi River.

  • Mango House: A budget guest house that offers comfortable rooms and a warm and friendly atmosphere.

  • Hotel Pabitra Royal Regency: A mid-range hotel that offers well-appointed rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Cuttack: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cuttack is known for its traditional handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry, which make for excellent souvenirs for your trip.

  • Silver Filigree Jewelry: Handcrafted silver jewelry that is intricately designed and is a specialty of Cuttack.

  • Ikat Sarees: Handwoven textiles that are made using the unique tie-dyeing technique of Odisha.

  • Apparels: Odisha is famous for its handloom sarees, dress materials, and fabrics, which are made using natural fibers and traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Cuttack

Cuttack has a rich cultural heritage, and its people are known for their hospitality and friendliness. The city is deeply rooted in its traditions and customs and is known for its colorful festivals and religious celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Cuttack

The best time to visit Cuttack is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant, and the city is at its festive best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cuttack

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Cuttack:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try local cuisine and explore the city’s cultural heritage.

  • Be respectful to the local people.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any illegal activities.

  • Disrespect the culture, customs, and religious beliefs of the local people.

  • Wastefully use the natural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cuttack

Cuttack is a generally safe city, but like any other city, it is advisable to take some precautions:

  • Avoid traveling alone, especially during the night.

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.

  • Stay vigilant and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Cuttack

Cuttack has adequate healthcare facilities, but it is essential to take some precautions while traveling:

  • Stay hydrated and drink only bottled water.

  • Avoid eating street food.

  • Carry essential medicines and vaccinations if required.

Getting to Cuttack: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cuttack is well-connected by air, rail, and road, making it easy to reach the city from any part of India.

Airports Near Cuttack

  • Biju Patnaik International Airport: Located in Bhubaneswar, which is about 35 km from Cuttack.

Bus Stations Near Cuttack

  • Cuttack Bus Stand: Located in the heart of the city, which is well-connected to other parts of Odisha and India.

Other Options

  • Train: Cuttack has a railway station that is well-connected to other parts of India.

How to Get Around in Cuttack

Cuttack has a well-established public transportation system, which includes buses, auto-rickshaws, and taxis. Bicycles and motorbikes are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Cuttack: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cuttack is well-connected to several other destinations that are worth visiting, including:

  • Bhubaneswar: Located about 20 km from Cuttack, Bhubaneswar is famous for its ancient temples and monuments.

  • Puri: Located about 60 km from Cuttack, Puri is famous for its beautiful beaches and the Jagannath Temple.

  • Konark: Located about 70 km from Cuttack, Konark is famous for the Sun Temple, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Cuttack is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and delectable cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, you now have all the information you need to plan your next trip to Cuttack and make the most of your vacation.
