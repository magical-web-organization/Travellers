Keonjhar, a small district in the northwest of Odisha, is a hidden gem that mesmerises travellers with its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural vibrancy. It is situated in Eastern India, in the decan plateau region, surrounded by lush green forests, hills, and waterfalls. This comprehensive travel guide to Keonjhar will help you discover the best of this district, whether it’s exploring its historic landmarks or indulging in local cuisine. So, fasten your seatbelts and get ready to immerse yourself in the tranquil and serene environs of Keonjhar.

Introduction to Keonjhar

Keonjhar, also known as Kendujhar or Keunjhar, has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to prehistoric times. It is located 195 km from the state capital, Bhubaneswar, and is spread over an area of 8240 sq km. The district is home to several indigenous tribes like Juang, Munda, and Bhumij. The vibrant culture and traditions of these tribes are evident in the colourful festivals, arts, and crafts of Keonjhar.

List of Places to Visit in Keonjhar

Visiting Keonjhar without exploring its historical, natural, and cultural landmarks is impossible. Here are the must-visit places in Keonjhar-

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jagannath Temple: Located at the heart of the city, the Jagannath Temple is a famous pilgrimage site of Keonjhar. The temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra and is an epitome of Kalinga Architecture.
  • Khandadhar Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is located at a distance of 80 km from Keonjhar town. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valley and is one of the highest waterfalls in Odisha.
  • Pimiur Waterfall: A hidden gem in the lap of nature, Pimiur waterfall is situated amidst dense forests and is a perfect spot for nature lovers and birdwatchers.
  • Gonasika Temple: Gonasika temple is situated on a hilltop and offers panoramic views of the Gonasika hills. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to be a source of the holy Baitarani river.

Natural Attractions of Keonjhar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Badaghagara Waterfall: Badaghagara waterfall is located at a distance of 10 km from Keonjhar and is a perfect spot for a picnic with family and friends.
  • Sanaghagara Waterfall: This iconic waterfall is located in the core of the forest and can be accessed through a small trek of 1.5 km from the nearest distance.
  • Deogaon Dam: The Deogaon dam is situated on the Baitarani river and is a popular spot for angling and boating activities.
  • Krishna Prasad Bridge: The bridge is located over the Baitarani river and is surrounded by lush green forests that offer magnificent views of the river and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keonjhar

Keonjhar is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts who want to explore its scenic landscapes and trekking trails. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Keonjhar are-

  • Similipal National Park: Similipal National Park, located in the Mayurbhanj district of Odisha, is approximately 120 km from Keonjhar. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to several rare and endangered species of flora and fauna, including the Royal Bengal Tiger. The park offers a plethora of adventure activities like trekking, hiking, and camping.
  • Gonasika Trek: The Gonasika trek is a popular trekking trail that starts at the Gonasika temple and covers a distance of 15 km. It offers breathtaking views of the valley, forests and is a perfect spot for birdwatchers.
  • Sana Ghagara Trek: The Sana Ghagara trek is a short trek near the Sanaghagara waterfall that takes you through the dense forests of Keonjhar. It is an easy trek and is suitable for beginners.
  • Deogaon Dam Trek: The Deogaon dam trek is a popular trek that takes you through the lush green forests of Keonjhar and offers magnificent views of the Baitarani river.

Best Things to Do in Keonjhar: Activities and Tours

From visiting the historical landmarks to exploring the scenic beauty, Keonjhar offers a plethora of activities to keep you busy. Some of the best things to do in Keonjhar are-

  • Visit the Tribal Villages: Keonjhar is home to several indigenous tribes like Juang, Munda, and Bhumij. Visiting their villages, interacting with them, and learning about their customs and traditions is a unique experience in itself.
  • Enjoy Local Cuisine: The local cuisine of Keonjhar is a blend of spices and flavours unique to this region. Some of the must-try dishes are the pitha, the regional sweet, and the famous Odia thali, which is a platter of traditional Odia delicacies.
  • Attend Festivals: Keonjhar is a melting pot of culture and tradition, and several festivals like Durga Puja, Rath Yatra, and Karthik Puja are celebrated with much pomp and show.
  • Explore the Nature Trail: Keonjhar is a dream destination for nature enthusiasts, and excursions to waterfalls, trekking in the forests, and bird watching are popular activities.
  • Shop for Souvenirs: From handmade textiles to wooden sculptures, there is an abundance of local products that you can buy as souvenirs or gifts.

Food and Drink Scene of Keonjhar

The food and drink scene in Keonjhar is a perfect blend of traditional Odia cuisine and local flavours. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should indulge in-

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mudhi Mansa: A popular dish among the indigenous tribes of Keonjhar, Mudhi Mansa is made with boiled meat and puffed rice (Mudhi) and is flavoured with local spices.
  • Pitha: Pitha is a famous sweet dish of Odisha, and Keonjhar has its own version of the delicacy.
  • Tel Bhaja: Tel bhaja is a popular snack among the locals, and is made with chickpea flour, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sanjharia: Sanjharia is a local brew made with fermented rice and is a popular drink among the indigenous communities of Keonjhar.
  • Sulai: Sulai is a local drink made with fermented palm wine that is popular during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Keonjhar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Keonjhar has several accommodation options, ranging from affordable guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best options-

  • Panthanivas Keonjhar: Run by the Odisha Tourism Development Corporation, Panthanivas Keonjhar is a perfect blend of comfort and affordability.
  • Hotel Anand Palace: Located in the heart of the city, Hotel Anand Palace is a perfect option for those looking for luxury and comfort.
  • Hotel Bilas: Hotel Bilas is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable accommodation and all basic amenities.

What to Buy in Keonjhar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Keonjhar is famous for its handmade textiles, wooden sculptures, bell metal utensils, and brass products. Here are some of the best places to buy souvenirs and local products-

  • Haat Bazaar: The weekly Haat Bazaars of Keonjhar are perfect for buying local handicrafts and textiles.
  • Penganga Artifacts: Penganga Artifacts is a famous destination for buying brass products and wooden sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Keonjhar

Keonjhar is a melting pot of culture and tradition, and its local customs are unique and fascinating. The indigenous tribes of Keonjhar have a rich cultural heritage, and their festivals, ceremonies, and arts add to the cultural vibrancy of Keonjhar. The traditional dance form of Keonjhar, Ghumura, is a UNESCO-recognised intangible cultural heritage of humanity that originates from the district.

Best Time to Visit Keonjhar

The best time to visit Keonjhar is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for exploring the outdoor beauty of Keonjhar.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keonjhar

Do:

  • Respect the tribal customs and traditions while visiting the villages.
  • Carry insect repellent while trekking or exploring the natural trails.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and carry a water bottle while trekking or exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter, and always dispose of the trash properly.
  • Do not disturb the wildlife or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Do not visit the waterfalls during monsoon or heavy rainfall.

Safety and Health

Keonjhar is generally safe, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. However, taking precautions while trekking or exploring the natural trails is advisable. Here are some tips for a safe and healthy trip to Keonjhar-

Safety in Keonjhar

  • Always carry a first-aid kit while trekking or exploring the natural trails.
  • Avoid trekking or visiting waterfalls during monsoon or heavy rainfall.
  • Follow the park rules and regulations while visiting Similipal National Park.

Health in Keonjhar

  • Always carry insect repellent while trekking or exploring the woods.
  • Drink boiled or filtered water.
  • Consult a doctor immediately in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Keonjhar: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Keonjhar is the Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar, located at a distance of 195 km. Keonjhar is well-connected to the major cities of Odisha through road and rail networks. Here are some transportation options for reaching Keonjhar-

Airports Near Keonjhar

  • Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar: Located at a distance of 195 km, Biju Patnaik International Airport is the nearest airport to Keonjhar.

Bus Stations Near Keonjhar

  • Keonjhar Bus Stand: Keonjhar has a well-connected bus stand that offers services to several neighbouring cities like Bhubaneswar, Kolkata, and Jamshedpur.

Other Options

  • Railway Stations: The nearest railway stations to Keonjhar are Kendujhargarh (12 km) and Harichandanpur (50 km).
  • Taxis: Private taxis are available from Bhubaneswar and neighboring towns.

How to Get Around in Keonjhar

Local transportation in Keonjhar includes buses, taxis, and autos. Hiring a taxi or a private vehicle for exploring the district is a popular choice among tourists. However, trekking and biking are the best ways to explore the natural landscape of Keonjhar.

Where to Go Next from Keonjhar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Keonjhar is a gateway to several nearby destinations that offer unique experiences to the travellers. Here are some destinations that you can explore after visiting Keonjhar-

  • Bhitarkanika National Park: Located at a distance of 180 km from Keonjhar, Bhitarkanika National Park is known for its Mangrove forests and Crocodile Conservation Project.
  • Baripada: Baripada is a quaint town located at a distance of 110 km from Keonjhar. It is home to several temples and is a perfect destination for those interested in history and architecture.
  • Daringbadi: Daringbadi, located in the Kandhmal district of Odisha, is known as the ‘Kashmir of Odisha’. It is famous for its coffee and tea plantations and is located at a distance of 300 km from Keonjhar.

Conclusion

Keonjhar, with its rich heritage and natural beauty, is an offbeat destination that offers a unique and immersive experience to the travellers. From trekking in the woods to exploring the cultural landmarks and indulging in local cuisine, Keonjhar has something for everyone. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore the hidden gems of Northwest Odisha!
