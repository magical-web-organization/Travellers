Introduction to Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park is a wildlife sanctuary located in the northwestern region of Odisha, India. Spread over an area of 2,750 km², the park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The park comprises of dense forests, waterfalls, and several small rivers that make it one of the most popular nature destinations in India.

List of Places to Visit in Simlipal National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Simlipal National Park is surrounded by several historical places and museums that offer an insight into the rich cultural heritage of the state. Some notable attractions include:

  • Simlipal Tribal Museum: An excellent museum that showcases the cultural heritage of the tribes living in Simlipal.
  • Jagannath Temple: A popular Hindu temple located in the town of Baripada, which is renowned for its exquisite architecture and carvings.

Natural Attractions of Simlipal National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Simlipal National Park offers a range of outdoor adventure activities that allow tourists to experience the park’s natural beauty up close. Some popular things to do include:

  • Nature Walks: Visitors can explore the amazing flora and fauna of the park on foot by taking guided nature walks.
  • Jeep Safaris: Jeep safaris are a great way to explore the park’s scenic views and wildlife.
  • Trekking and Hiking: Simlipal offers fantastic trekking and hiking trails that are suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers.
  • Waterfall Visit: Visit Joranda and Barehipani Waterfall for a scenic view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park has several hiking and biking routes that offer visitors a chance to explore its beautiful landscapes. Some popular trails include:

  • Simlipal-Kuldiha-Anandpur Sanctuary Circuit: A scenic trail that passes through the dense forests, rivers, and small waterfalls.
  • Biking Trails: There are many exciting biking routes in and around the park that are suitable for both beginners and experienced bikers.

Best Things to Do in Simlipal National Park: Activities and Tours

Simlipal National Park offers a range of activities and tours that cater to the varied interests of visitors. Some popular things to do include:

  • Wildlife Safari: A must-do activity that allows tourists to see the park’s enchanting wildlife up close.
  • Tribal Village Tour: A great opportunity to discover the local tribes’ culture and way of life.
  • Photography Tour: Simlipal’s beautiful landscape and rich biodiversity provide an excellent opportunity for nature photography.

Week-Long Itinerary for Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park is a vast destination with lots to see and explore. A week-long itinerary will enable you to cover all the park’s significant attractions in a relaxed manner.

Day 1: Arrival and Relaxation Day Day 2: Wildlife Safari Day 3: Trekking and Hiking Day 4: Village Tour and Cultural Exploration Day 5: Photography Tour Day 6: Waterfall Visit and Jeep Safari Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Simlipal National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Simlipal National Park has a rich and diverse culinary tradition that reflects the local culture and environment. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Chhena Poda: A delicious dessert made from fresh cottage cheese, sugar, and cardamom.
  • Khichdi: A nutritious lentil and rice-based dish that’s ideal for a quick meal.
  • Machha Besara: A flavourful fish curry made with mustard paste, green chillies, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Mahua: A fermented drink made from Mahua flowers is common in this region.
  • Salap: A refreshing drink made from Orchid’s root and milk, adds a unique taste to experience.

Where to Stay in Simlipal National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Simlipal National Park has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Panthanivas Simlipal: It is the state’s government run tourist lodge and provides comfortable rooms to the visitors.
  • Private Resorts: Private resorts are available for those seeking a comfortable, luxurious stay.

What to Buy in Simlipal National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Simlipal’s art, craft, and souvenirs reflect the local culture and traditions. Some popular items to buy are:

  • Tribal Artifacts: Handmade products by the tribal people make a great souvenir.
  • Handicrafts: The town of Baripada is famous for its beautiful tribal handicrafts like Dokra, Jute and Cotton artifacts etc.

Local Culture and Customs of Simlipal National Park

The tribal culture of Simlipal is fascinating and unique, and visitors are encouraged to respect and learn more about it during their stay. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Dress Code: Visitors are advised to wear modest clothing that covers their shoulders and knees, particularly when visiting temples or tribal communities.
  • Sacred Places: Certain areas of the park are considered sacred by the local tribes, and visitors should avoid disturbing them.
  • Language: The local tribes speak a variety of languages, including Santhali, Ho, and Mundari.

Best Time to Visit Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park is open to visitors all year round, but the best time to visit is between October and February when the weather is mild, and the park’s vegetation is lush.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simlipal National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry appropriate warm clothing.
  • Take a guide with you during hiking, biking, and trekking.
  • Ensure to obtain proper permits when required.

Don’t:

  • Litter within the park.
  • Disturb the wildlife or try to feed them.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting a temple.
  • Disrupt the peaceful ambiance of the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take the following safety precautions:

  • Stay away from wild animals and maintain a distance.
  • Avoid trekking alone in remote areas.
  • Do not consume unpurified or local water.

Health in Simlipal National Park

Visitors are advised to carry mosquito repellent, sunscreen, and prescription medications. It is wise to consult a doctor before planning a visit to the park.

Getting to Simlipal National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Simlipal National Park

The nearest airport to Simlipal National Park is Bhubaneshwar, which is approximately 250 km away. Regular taxis and buses are available from the airport to the park.

Bus Stations Near Simlipal National Park

Baripada is the nearest town to the park, and several state-run and private buses connect it to other major cities and towns in the state.

Other Options

Private cabs and taxis are available from nearby towns and cities like Bhubaneswar, Kolkata, and Jamshedpur.

How to Get Around in Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park can be explored on foot, by bike, or by hiring a jeep. Hiring a guide is recommended for hiking, trekking, and wildlife safaris.

Where to Go Next from Simlipal National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Simlipal National Park is surrounded by several other popular destinations that can be visited on a day trip:

  • Chandipur Beach: A serene beach located approximately 100 km from the park.
  • Gopalpur-on-Sea: A charming coastal town renowned for its beautiful beaches and seafood.
  • Konark Sun Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its exquisite architecture and historical significance.

Conclusion

Simlipal National Park is a beautiful destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and a glimpse into the tribal way of life. A visit to this enchanting destination will provide memories to last a lifetime.
