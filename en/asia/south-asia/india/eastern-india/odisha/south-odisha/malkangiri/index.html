Introduction to Malkangiri

Malkangiri, the quaint town nestled in the southern region of Odisha, India, is a haven for nature enthusiasts who love to explore offbeat destinations. This town offers stunning views of nature, with lush green forests, tranquil lakes, and a diverse wildlife population. If you’re looking for a relaxing getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life, Malkangiri is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Malkangiri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Malkangiri Museum: The Malkangiri Museum is an excellent place for visitors to learn about the rich cultural and historical heritage of the region. The museum houses artifacts, art, and literature that showcase the life, culture, and traditions of Malkangiri.

  • Paper-mache Craft Village: The paper-mache craft village at Malkangiri is a must-visit for art enthusiasts. This village is home to skilled artisans who craft exquisite paper-mache products that are sold throughout the country.

Natural Attractions of Malkangiri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Satiguda Dam: The Satiguda Dam is a picturesque dam that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests. Visitors can also enjoy boating and fishing in the dam’s waters, which are rich in fish species.

  • Balimela Reservoir: The Balimela Reservoir is a popular picnic spot in Malkangiri. Surrounded by dense forests and hills, the reservoir offers a serene atmosphere for visitors to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malkangiri

Malkangiri is home to several scenic hiking and biking routes that offer a perfect blend of adventure and nature.

  • Chitrakonda Waterfall Trek - An excellent trek through dense forests and hills that culminates at the stunning Chitrakonda Waterfall.

  • Malkangiri-Deomali Trek - A challenging trek that takes visitors through rugged terrain and offers stunning views of dense forests, valleys, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Malkangiri: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safaris - Visitors can enjoy exciting wildlife safaris through the dense forests of Malkangiri, which are home to tigers, leopards, deer, and several species of birds.

  • Tribal Village Tours - Malkangiri is home to several tribal communities, who still follow their traditional way of life. Visitors can embark on tribal village tours to experience their unique way of life, customs, and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malkangiri

Day 1: Arrival at Malkangiri, visit Malkangiri Museum. Day 2: Explore the Satiguda Dam and enjoy boating and fishing. Day 3: Trek to the Chitrakonda Waterfall. Day 4: Visit the tribal villages of Malkangiri and experience their unique way of life. Day 5: Enjoy a wildlife safari through the dense forests of Malkangiri. Day 6: Visit the Balimela Reservoir and enjoy a picnic amidst nature. Day 7: Departure from Malkangiri.

Food and Drink Scene of Malkangiri

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pakhala: Pakhala is a traditional dish of Odisha, made using fermented rice and water. It is served with sides such as potato fry, fish fry, or pickles.

  • Santula: Santula is a vegetarian dish made using different local vegetables, lentils, and spices. It is served with steamed rice and is a typical Odia recipe.

Local Beverages

  • Mahula - A locally brewed alcoholic beverage made from the flowers of the Mahua tree.

Local Culture and Customs of Malkangiri

Malkangiri is home to several indigenous tribes, whose customs and traditions are unique and fascinating. Visitors can learn about their way of life, festivals, and rituals by visiting their villages and interacting with them.

Best Time to Visit Malkangiri

The best time to visit Malkangiri is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the nature’s beauty is at its prime.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malkangiri

Do:

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the tribal communities.
  • Stay hydrated and carry enough water during hikes and treks.
  • Carry comfortable footwear and clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Avoid littering and try to keep the environment clean.
  • Don’t disturb or harm the wildlife during safaris.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malkangiri

  • Stick to well-traveled paths during hikes and treks.
  • Stay vigilant during safaris and follow the rules set by the forest department.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Malkangiri

  • Visitors should carry mosquito repellent and wear full-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites.
  • It’s advisable to carry a first-aid kit while trekking and hiking.

Getting to Malkangiri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malkangiri

  • The nearest airport is the Visakhapatnam Airport, which is about 240 km from Malkangiri.

Bus Stations Near Malkangiri

  • Malkangiri has its bus stand, which is well-connected to other cities in Odisha and the neighboring states.

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire taxis or cars from nearby cities such as Visakhapatnam, Vizianagaram, and Koraput to reach Malkangiri.

How to Get Around in Malkangiri

Visitors can hire taxis or cars to move around in Malkangiri. Bikes and bicycles are also available for rent for those who want to go sightseeing on their own.

Where to Go Next from Malkangiri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jeypore: Jeypore is a scenic town known for its waterfalls, lakes, and historical monuments, such as the Jeypore Fort and the Jagannath Temple.

  • Koraput: Koraput is a popular hill station known for its coffee plantations, trekking trails and waterfalls, such as the Duduma Waterfall and the Kolab Reservoir.

Conclusion

Malkangiri is an offbeat destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, adventure, and culture. From picturesque waterfalls and tranquil lakes to exciting wildlife safaris and tribal village tours, Malkangiri has something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, unique cuisine, and friendly locals, Malkangiri is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a relaxing getaway amidst nature.
