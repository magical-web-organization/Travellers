Introduction to Chhattisgarh

Located in eastern India, Chhattisgarh is a culturally rich and scenic state that boasts of a diverse range of tourist attractions. From centuries-old temples and museums to natural wonders such as waterfalls and wildlife sanctuaries, the state has something to offer for every type of traveler. With friendly locals, delicious food, and a laid-back lifestyle, Chhattisgarh is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring India.

List of Places to Visit in Chhattisgarh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chhattisgarh State Museum

Located in the state capital of Raipur, the Chhattisgarh State Museum is an excellent place to learn about the art, culture, and history of the state. The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts, including ancient coins, sculptures, paintings, and textiles.

Bhilai Steel Plant

Spread over an area of nearly 12 square kilometers, the Bhilai Steel Plant is an unbelievably large industrial complex that is considered to be one of the largest steel plants in India. A visit to this plant will take you on a guided tour of the manufacturing process and give you a glimpse into the lives of the workers who operate the machinery.

Chitrakoot Falls

Located in the Bastar district of Chhattisgarh, the Chitrakoot Falls are a breathtaking natural wonder that is known as the ‘Niagara Falls of India.’ The falls are surrounded by lush green forests and are a popular picnic spot for locals and tourists alike.

Natural Attractions of Chhattisgarh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary

The Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, gaurs, and sloth bears. Visitors can take a jeep safari or elephant ride to explore the sanctuary and spot some of the native wildlife.

Kawardha Palace

Also known as the Bhoramdeo Palace, the Kawardha Palace is a stunning example of traditional Indian architecture. The palace was built in the 1940s and has over 100 rooms spread over four floors. Visitors can take a guided tour of the palace and admire the intricate designs and rich cultural heritage of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chhattisgarh

Mainpat Trek

The Mainpat Trek is a multi-day hike that takes you through the dense forests of Mainpat, a region known for its natural beauty and wildlife. The trek is ideal for seasoned hikers looking for a challenge and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Raipur Cycling Trail

The Raipur Cycling Trail is a popular route for cyclists looking to explore the city and its suburbs. The trail covers a total distance of 35 kilometers and takes you through some of Raipur’s most scenic neighborhoods and parks.

Best Things to Do in Chhattisgarh: Activities and Tours

Tribal Village Tour

Chhattisgarh is home to over 40 different tribes, each with its unique culture, customs, and way of life. Taking a tribal village tour will give you a chance to interact with some of these communities, learn about their history and traditions, and taste their traditional food.

Waterfall Tour

The state is home to several stunning waterfalls, including the Chitrakoot Falls, Tirathgarh Falls, and Amrit Dhara Falls. Visitors can take a guided tour of the waterfalls, which usually includes a hike through the surrounding forests and a dip in the cool waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Chhattisgarh

Must-Try Dishes

Bafauri

Bafauri is a savory pancake made from chana dal (split Bengal gram) and spices. The pancakes are shallow-fried in oil and served hot with green chutney.

Chousela

Chousela is a spicy snack made from rice flour, urad dal (split black lentils), and spices. The mixture is shaped into small balls and deep-fried until golden brown.

Local Beverages

Lassi

Lassi is a traditional Indian beverage made from yogurt, sugar, and spices. The beverage is usually served cold and is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Mahua

Mahua is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from the flowers of the Mahua tree. The drink has a fruity flavor and a slightly bitter aftertaste.

Where to Stay in Chhattisgarh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Courtyard by Marriott Raipur

The Courtyard by Marriott Raipur is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city. The hotel offers spacious rooms, a rooftop pool, and several dining options.

Backpacker Panda

Backpacker Panda is a budget hostel chain with several locations in Chhattisgarh. The hostels offer shared and private rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a common hangout area.

What to Buy in Chhattisgarh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bastar Art and Crafts

The Bastar region of Chhattisgarh is famous for its traditional handicrafts, including wooden carvings, bell metal items, and tribal jewelry. Visitors can buy these items at local markets and shops.

Bhilai Steel Plant Souvenirs

The Bhilai Steel Plant offers several souvenirs, including shirts, caps, and keychains. The items are available for purchase at the plant’s gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Chhattisgarh

The state is home to several different tribes, each with its unique culture and customs. However, most of the locals follow Hinduism and celebrate festivals like Diwali and Holi. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Chhattisgarh

The best time to visit Chhattisgarh is between October and March, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The summer months of April to June are hot and humid, while the monsoon season (July to September) can be unpredictable and unsafe for outdoor travel.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chhattisgarh

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.
  • Carry cash, as many smaller shops and restaurants do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Photograph locals without their permission.
  • Litter in public places or on the streets.
  • Drink the tap water, as it may not be safe to consume.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chhattisgarh

While Chhattisgarh is a generally safe place to travel, visitors are advised to exercise caution when traveling alone, especially at night. It is also recommended to avoid large crowds and political demonstrations, as they can turn violent.

Health in Chhattisgarh

Visitors are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria while traveling to Chhattisgarh. It is also recommended to carry a basic first-aid kit and any necessary medications while traveling.
