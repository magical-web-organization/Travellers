Introduction to Dantewada

Dantewada is a district located in the southern part of Chhattisgarh, one of the 28 states in India, and is considered one of the most important historical and cultural destinations in the state. It is situated at the confluence of two rivers – Shankini and Dankini, and is surrounded by verdant hills and dense forests, giving it a picturesque look. Dantewada is home to a number of ancient temples, beautiful waterfalls, and fascinating caves, all of which make it an ideal destination for those who love history, culture, and nature.

List of Places to Visit in Dantewada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Danteshwari Temple: One of the most important temples in the state, the Danteshwari Temple is believed to have been built by the Chalukya Kings in the 14th century. Devotees flock to the temple, which is dedicated to Goddess Danteshwari, throughout the year.
  • Bailadila Iron Ore Mines: A popular attraction among history enthusiasts, the Bailadila Iron Ore Mines have been in operation since 1968 and are one of the largest in the world. The mines were used by the British during their colonial rule in India.
  • Barsoor: A town located about 40 km away from Dantewada, Barsoor is home to a number of ancient temples dating back to the 10th century. The town also has an archeological site where you can see ruins of many old temples and monuments.

Natural Attractions of Dantewada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chitradhara Falls: These beautiful waterfalls are located near Jagdalpur and are fed by a stream that flows through a dense forest. Visitors can enjoy a dip in the falls’ refreshing waters while taking in the stunning scenery all around them.
  • Kanger Valley National Park: Spread over an area of about 200 square kilometers, the Kanger Valley National Park is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. The park is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, and many species of birds.
  • Dholkal: Located atop a hill, the Dholkal is a rock formation that has been carved by natural forces over thousands of years. Visitors can enjoy a panoramic view of the surrounding area from the top of the hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dantewada

  • Jhiram Valley Trek: This trek takes visitors through the lush green forests of Dantewada, showcasing the beauty of the natural surroundings of the region. The trek takes about six days to complete and covers a distance of around 70 km.
  • Bastar to Dantewada Biking Route: This biking route takes visitors through some of the most scenic routes in Chhattisgarh, including Bastar, which is dotted with colorful tribal villages, as well as the lush green forests of Dantewada.

Best Things to Do in Dantewada: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Tribal Villages: Dantewada is home to many tribal villages. Visitors can explore their unique lifestyles and customs by interacting with the locals and experiencing their traditional dance and musical performances.
  • Rafting: The River Indravati, which flows through the nearby town of Jagdalpur, offers many opportunities for white-water rafting. Visitors can enjoy a thrilling rafting experience while taking in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Shopping: Dantewada is famous for its handicrafts, especially the traditional Dhokra metal casting. Visitors can buy Dhokra art, tribal jewelry, and other local souvenirs from the weekly Haat bazaars.

Food and Drink Scene of Dantewada

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kosa: A traditional dish made with chickpea flour and served with rice, Kosa is popular throughout Chhattisgarh.
  • Nukti: These delicious, bite-sized snacks are made with urad dal and spices, and are perfect for a quick, filling snack.
  • Jalebi: This sweet, syrupy dessert is a popular favorite across India and is a must-try when in Dantewada.

Local Beverages

  • Mahua: This popular local liquor is made from the flowers of the Mahua tree and is enjoyed by many in the region.
  • Coffee: The coffee grown in the region is famous for its unique flavor and aroma, and is a must-try for coffee lovers.

Where to Stay in Dantewada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Aten: Located in the heart of the city, Hotel Aten is a popular choice among travelers. The hotel has comfortable rooms, a restaurant serving local and international cuisine, and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views.
  • Bastar Jungle Resort: This eco-friendly resort is located in the heart of the Kanger Valley National Park and offers visitors a chance to stay amidst nature. The rooms are well-appointed and the resort offers a range of adventure activities.

What to Buy in Dantewada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dhokra Art: This traditional metal casting art form is unique to the region and makes for great souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: Visitors can buy a range of handicrafts made by the tribal locals, including tribal jewelry, colorful bags, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Dantewada

Dantewada is known for its unique tribal culture. Visitors can learn more about local customs by attending traditional dance and musical performances, festivals, and by visiting tribal villages.

Best Time to Visit Dantewada

The best time to visit Dantewada is from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant. The summer months (April to June) can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season (July to September) brings heavy rainfall, making it difficult to travel.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dantewada

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Interact with the locals to learn about their traditional way of life.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Do not take pictures of the tribal people without their permission.
  • Do not visit the national park without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dantewada

Dantewada is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should avoid traveling alone at night and should take precautionary measures when venturing into isolated areas.

Health in Dantewada

Visitors should always carry a first aid kit with them and take necessary precautions, such as using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing, to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Dantewada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dantewada

The nearest airport to Dantewada is Swami Vivekananda Airport in Raipur, which is well-connected to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Dantewada

Dantewada has a well-connected network of buses that provide regular services from nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or use their own vehicle to reach Dantewada.

How to Get Around in Dantewada

Visitors can hire a taxi or use local buses to get around in Dantewada. Auto-rickshaws are also a convenient and inexpensive mode of travel.

Where to Go Next from Dantewada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jagdalpur: Located about 80 km away from Dantewada, Jagdalpur is famous for its natural attractions, including waterfalls, caves, and a national park.
  • Raipur: The state capital of Chhattisgarh, Raipur is a bustling city that is home to many historical and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Dantewada is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the unique tribal culture and natural beauty of the region. From ancient temples and historical sites to breathtaking waterfalls and adventure activities, Dantewada has something to offer every kind of traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip with ease and make the most of their time in Dantewada.
