Introduction to Kanker

Kanker is a hidden gem located in the heart of Chhattisgarh, in Eastern India. The city is surrounded by dense forests and hills, making it one of the most scenic destinations in the region. Kanker is well known for its rich cultural heritage, which is evident in the many museums and historical places one can visit here. From outdoor adventures to cultural immersion, Kanker has a lot to offer to tourists.

Kanker is a city that is full of surprises. There are so many hidden gems that are waiting to be explored. Here is a list of must-visit places in Kanker:

Best Things to Do in Kanker: Activities and Tours

Kanker has a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests, from cultural immersion to adventure sports. Here are some of the best things to do in Kanker:

  • Visit the Kanker Palace to witness the stunning architecture and learn about the history of the region.
  • Take a walk through the Tribal Museum to get an insight into the tribal culture of the region.
  • Go on a hike or bike ride to explore the scenic beauty that surrounds Kanker.
  • Take a dip in the Kanker Dam or spend a day fishing.
  • Participate in a tribal dance or music program to witness the rich cultural heritage of Kanker.

Food and Drink Scene of Kanker

Kanker is known for its rich and diverse cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chilauni: A pancake made from rice flour and gram flour, stuffed with vegetables.
  • Lai: A traditional dish made from rice flour and black gram.
  • Petha Sabzi: A vegetarian dish made from pumpkin and spices.
  • Jalebi: A sweet dish made of deep-fried wheat flour batter and dipped in sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Mahua: An alcoholic drink made from the flowers of the mahua tree.
  • Rukhdi: A non-alcoholic drink made from a blend of herbs and spices.

What to Buy in Kanker: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kanker is home to some unique handicrafts and local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the items you can consider taking home:

  • Bell Metal Craft: Bell metal is a traditional craft of the region that involves making utensils and decorative items from bronze and brass.
  • Kosa Silk: Kosa silk is a type of silk that is produced in Chhattisgarh, including Kanker. The silk is known for its sheen and texture.
  • Wooden Artefacts: Wooden artefacts are made by skilled craftsmen in Kanker and are known for their intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanker

Kanker is home to a rich and diverse culture that is steeped in traditions and rituals. The main tribes inhabiting the region include Maria, Dhurwas, and Gonds. The people here are welcoming and helpful, and it is common for tourists to be invited into homes for a cup of tea or a meal.

Best Time to Visit Kanker

The best time to visit Kanker is between October and February when the weather is cool and pleasant. The summers can be hot and humid, while the monsoons can make outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanker

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Engage in any unlawful activity.
  • Harm or disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanker

Kanker is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it is always best to exercise caution and be mindful of your surroundings. It is advisable to avoid walking alone at night and to keep your valuables safe.

Health in Kanker

The healthcare facilities in Kanker are limited, so it is always best to carry your medication and take the necessary precautions to prevent any diseases.

How to Get Around Kanker

The best way to get around Kanker is by hiring a taxi or renting a car. It is also possible to hire a bike or a scooter if you prefer a more adventurous mode of transport.
