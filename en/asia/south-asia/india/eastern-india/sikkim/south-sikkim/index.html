Introduction to South Sikkim

South Sikkim is a district in the Indian state of Sikkim, located in the eastern part of the country. It is known for its scenic beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures that attract tourists from all over the world. South Sikkim is also famous for its tea and cardamom plantations, which are a significant source of livelihood for the locals. The district is well connected by roads, and you can reach here from major cities in India. The nearest airport is in Bagdogra, which is about 120 km from South Sikkim.

List of Places to Visit in South Sikkim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Namchi Monastery: It is one of the oldest and most significant monasteries in South Sikkim, located near the town of Namchi. It is home to some of the most beautiful statues and artifacts, and its architecture is awe-inspiring.
  • Samdruptse Hill: This is a popular tourist destination in South Sikkim, located near Namchi. It is famous for its massive statue of Guru Padmasambhava and offers stunning views of the Himalayas and the surrounding valleys.
  • Ravangla: This is a small town in South Sikkim that is known for its historical significance. It is the site of a historic battle between Bhutia and Nepal forces, and you can visit the War Memorial to learn more about it.

Natural Attractions of South Sikkim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maenam Wildlife Sanctuary: If you love nature and wildlife, you must visit this wildlife sanctuary, located on the Maenam Hill in South Sikkim. The sanctuary is home to a vast range of flora and fauna, including the red panda, Himalayan black bear, and various bird species.
  • Khecheopalri Lake: This is one of the most sacred lakes in South Sikkim, located near the town of Pelling. It is believed to be a wish-fulfilling lake, and people come here to offer prayers and seek blessings. It is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a serene environment.
  • Temi Tea Garden: A visit to South Sikkim is incomplete without visiting the famous tea gardens in the district. The Temi Tea Garden is the only tea estate in the state, and here you can witness the entire tea-making process, from plucking to packaging. The garden also offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Sikkim

  • Yuksom to Dzongri: This is a popular trekking route in South Sikkim that offers stunning views of the Himalayas and the surrounding valleys. It is a moderate trek that takes about five days to complete. The route passes through several villages, waterfalls, and rivers.
  • Pedong to Rikisum: This is a scenic biking route in South Sikkim that passes through the Teesta River Valley. It offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas and the surrounding countryside. The route is a mix of uphill and downhill stretches and takes about seven hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in South Sikkim: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: The Teesta River is a popular destination for white water rafting in South Sikkim. The rapids are suitable for beginners as well as experienced rafters, and the experience is nothing short of exhilarating.
  • Paragliding: South Sikkim offers several paragliding sites, and you can enjoy a bird’s eye view of the Himalayas and the surrounding valleys while soaring in the sky.
  • Mountain Biking: South Sikkim is a haven for mountain biking enthusiasts, and you can explore the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage while cycling through its rugged terrain.

Food and Drink Scene of South Sikkim

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momos: These are steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat and are a popular snack in South Sikkim.
  • Thukpa: This is a popular noodle soup made with vegetables or meat and served with hot chili sauce.
  • Sel Roti: This is a traditional Nepali dish made with rice flour and deep-fried into a donut-shaped dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Chhaang: This is a traditional Tibetan alcoholic beverage made with millet or barley.
  • Butter Tea: This is a traditional tea made with yak butter and salt and is a favorite drink of the locals.

Where to Stay in South Sikkim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Elgin Mount Pandim: This is a luxury hotel located in Pelling that offers stunning views of the Himalayas and the surrounding valleys.
  • The Denmark House: This is a cozy guesthouse located near Ravangla that offers a homely atmosphere and affordable prices.
  • Alpine Inn: This is a budget-friendly hostel located in Namchi that is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in South Sikkim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: South Sikkim is famous for its handicrafts, including woolen shawls, bamboo products, hand-carved statues, and more.
  • Black Cardamom: This is a local spice that is known for its unique flavor.
  • Tea: You can bring home locally grown tea from the Temi Tea Garden.

Local Culture and Customs of South Sikkim

South Sikkim is home to a diverse range of cultures, including Nepalis, Bhutias, and Lepchas. The locals are warm and friendly, and their hospitality is unmatched. Buddhism is the dominant religion in the region, and you will find several monasteries and prayer flags throughout the district.

Best Time to Visit South Sikkim

The best time to visit South Sikkim is from March to June and from September to December. The weather is pleasant during these months, and you can enjoy the scenic beauty of the region without any hindrances.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Sikkim

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect the locals or their beliefs.
  • Purchase artifacts made from endangered species.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Sikkim

South Sikkim is a safe destination for tourists, but it is always important to take precautions. Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night and keep your belongings close to you at all times.

Health in South Sikkim

It is advisable to carry a first aid kit with you as medical facilities in South Sikkim are limited. You should also carry medications for altitude sickness if you plan to trek in the region.

Getting to South Sikkim: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Sikkim is well connected by roads, and you can reach here by bus or taxi from major cities in India. The nearest airport is in Bagdogra, which is about 120 km from South Sikkim. From Bagdogra, you can hire a taxi to reach your desired destination in South Sikkim.

Airports Near South Sikkim

  • Bagdogra Airport
  • Gangtok Airport

Bus Stations Near South Sikkim

  • Siliguri Bus Station
  • Kalimpong Bus Station

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or book a travel package for a hassle-free experience.

How to Get Around in South Sikkim

The best way to get around in South Sikkim is by hiring a taxi or a private car. You can also rent a bike or a scooter if you prefer to explore the region on your own.

Where to Go Next from South Sikkim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Darjeeling: This is a popular hill station located near South Sikkim that is known for its tea gardens and colonial architecture.
  • Gangtok: This is the capital city of Sikkim and is home to several monasteries, parks, and museums.
  • Kalimpong: This is a small town located near South Sikkim that is known for its scenic beauty and historical significance.

Conclusion

South Sikkim is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and culture aficionados. With its beautiful landscapes, historic sites, and cultural treasures, a trip to South Sikkim promises to be an unforgettable experience. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of India.
