Aritar is a hidden gem nestled in the Eastern Himalayas, in East Sikkim. This charming town is a perfect getaway for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure enthusiasts. With its stunning landscapes, inviting culture, and serene atmosphere, Aritar makes for an ideal destination to escape the crowds and dive into the heart of Sikkim. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to explore the cultural and natural treasures of Aritar and help you plan your trip to this scenic town.

Introduction to Aritar

Located at an elevation of 1,524 meters, Aritar is a small town in East Sikkim that offers a perfect blend of nature, culture, and adventure. The town is surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills, and serene lakes, making it a haven for hikers, bird watchers, and nature lovers. Besides its abundant natural beauty, Aritar is deeply rooted in its history and culture, and you can witness this in the town’s traditional architecture, museums, and festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Aritar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aritar Gumpa: Also known as Ari-Phodong, this 17th-century monastery is one of the most revered Buddhist sites in Sikkim. It boasts of exquisite architecture, ancient murals, and a rich collection of scriptures and artifacts. The monastery is also home to the famous Chaam masked dance, which is performed during the Pang Lhabsol festival.

  • Aritar Bungalow: This colonial-era bungalow was built by the British for the Artillery officers in the early 1900s. The bungalow’s architecture reflects the fusion of traditional Sikkimese and British styles. The bungalow is now a heritage property and offers accommodation to visitors.

  • Lampokhari Lake: This picturesque lake is one of the oldest natural lakes in Sikkim and is believed to have healing properties. The lake is surrounded by lush forests and colorful flowers, making it an ideal spot for a picnic or a leisurely walk.

Natural Attractions of Aritar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mulkharka Lake: This secluded lake is hidden inside the forests and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills. The lake is known for its crystal-clear waters and is a popular spot for fishing and camping.

  • Kali Khola Falls: This majestic waterfall is located on the way to the Lampokhari lake and offers a stunning view of the cascading water amidst the lush greenery. The waterfall is a popular spot for trekking and camping.

  • Arisa Village: This traditional Rai village is located on a hilltop and offers panoramic views of the Himalayan range. The village is known for its indigenous handicrafts, traditional houses, and rich cultural heritage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aritar

Aritar is surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular trails include:

  • Aritar to Pedong: This scenic trek takes you through the forests and countryside, offering stunning views of the Mt. Kanchenjunga range and the Teesta river. The 12 km trek takes around 4-5 hours.

  • Aritar to Mulkharka Lake: This trek takes you through the dense forests to the tranquil Mulkharka lake, offering a chance to witness the local wildlife and flora. The 11 km trek takes around 4-5 hours.

Best Things to Do in Aritar: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Aritar, some of which include:

  • Pang Lhabsol festival: This annual festival is celebrated at the Aritar Gumpa and is dedicated to the mountain deity, Kanchenjunga. The festival features a colorful procession, traditional masked dances, and music performances.

  • Bird Watching: Aritar is home to a diverse range of bird species, including the endemic Satyr Tragopan. Bird watchers can take a guided tour to spot these birds in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aritar

Day 1: Arrive at Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway station and drive to Aritar. Check in to your hotel and spend the day exploring the town’s local markets and food scene.

Day 2: Visit the Aritar Gumpa and learn about its history and culture. Attend the morning prayer ceremony and witness the monks performing the ancient rituals.

Day 3: Take a trek to the Mulkharka lake and spend the day fishing or camping by the lake.

Day 4: Visit the Lampokhari lake and Kali Khola falls and enjoy a picnic amidst the serene surroundings.

Day 5: Explore the local handicrafts and traditional houses in the Arisa village and witness the Rai culture firsthand.

Day 6: Take a guided bird watching tour and spot some of the rare bird species that inhabit the forests around Aritar.

Day 7: Attend the Pang Lhabsol festival and witness the traditional masked dances and music performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Aritar

When in Aritar, make sure to try the following dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thukpa: A traditional Sikkimese noodle soup made with vegetables and meat.

  • Momo: A steamed dumpling made with vegetables or meat and served with spicy sauce.

  • Phagshapa: A spicy pork dish served with rice or buckwheat noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Chaang: A fermented millet or rice beer served in wooden mugs.

  • Tongba: A fermented millet beer served in bamboo mugs.

Where to Stay in Aritar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Aritar, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxury resorts. Some popular options include:

  • The Aritar Resort: A luxury resort located amidst the lush forests and offering stunning views of the Himalayas.

  • The Silk Route Retreat: A heritage property that offers a glimpse of the British colonial architecture.

  • Blue Poppy Homestay: A budget-friendly homestay that offers a chance to experience the local culture and cuisine.

What to Buy in Aritar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Aritar, make sure to buy the following souvenirs:

  • Thangkas: Traditional Sikkimese paintings that depict religious themes.

  • Handicrafts: Local handcrafted items made from bamboo, cane, and wool.

  • Organic Tea: Aritar is known for its organic tea gardens that produce a variety of teas, including black and green tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Aritar

The local culture of Aritar is deeply rooted in its Buddhist and Rai traditions. The town’s festivals, architecture, and customs reflect this rich cultural heritage. As a visitor, it’s important to respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Aritar

The best time to visit Aritar is from March to June and from September to December when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. The town receives heavy rainfall during the monsoons, which can make outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aritar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the town or the surrounding areas.
  • Disturb the local wildlife or flora.
  • Take photographs of the monks during their prayer ceremonies without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aritar

Aritar is a relatively safe town, but it’s important to take the necessary precautions while traveling. It’s advisable to avoid venturing out alone at night and to keep your valuables safe.

Health in Aritar

Aritar is located at an altitude of 1,524 meters, and visitors may experience altitude sickness, especially during the first few days. It’s advisable to stay hydrated, avoid smoking and alcohol, and take breaks during hikes or treks.

Getting to Aritar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aritar is well-connected to major towns and cities in Sikkim and West Bengal. Some of the options to reach Aritar include:

Airports Near Aritar

  • Bagdogra Airport, West Bengal (110 km)

Bus Stations Near Aritar

  • Siliguri Bus Stand, West Bengal (90 km)
  • Gangtok Bus Stand (68 km)

Other Options

  • Shared Taxis/Private Vehicles: Visitors can hire shared taxis or private vehicles from Siliguri or Gangtok to reach Aritar.

How to Get Around in Aritar

Aritar is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also hire taxis or motorbikes from the town center to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Aritar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Aritar include:

  • Zuluk: A picturesque hamlet located on the historic Silk Route and known for its panoramic views of the Himalayas.

  • Gangtok: The capital of Sikkim and a popular tourist destination known for its vibrant culture, architecture, and food scene.

Conclusion

Aritar is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in Aritar. Explore this hidden gem in the Eastern Himalayas and discover the treasures that await you!
