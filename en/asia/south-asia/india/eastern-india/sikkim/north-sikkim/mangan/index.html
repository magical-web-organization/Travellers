Introduction to Mangan

Mangan is a small, charming town located in the northern district of Sikkim in Eastern India. It is situated at an altitude of 956 meters above sea level and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountain ranges. Mangan is also known as the gateway to North Sikkim as it is the district headquarters and the starting point for most North Sikkim tours. Although it is a small town, it offers a range of cultural and natural attractions and is an excellent destination for adventure and cultural buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Mangan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Palace of the Kreatong Tsodruk Sonam Gyalpo: The palace was built in the 19th century and provides a glimpse into the history and culture of the region. It is situated on a hill and offers a panoramic view of the town.
  • The Sikkim Himalayan Zoological Park: Home to rare and endangered species such as the Red Panda, Snow Leopard, and Himalayan Black Bear, this zoo is one of the most popular attractions in Mangan.
  • The Tingvong Heritage Village: This is a must-visit for those interested in the history and culture of the region. The village offers an insight into the traditional lifestyle of the Lepchas, the indigenous people of Sikkim.

Natural Attractions of Mangan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seven Sisters Waterfall: This magnificent waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and is a favorite spot for picnic-goers and photographers.
  • Tholung Monastery: Situated amidst picturesque surroundings, this ancient Buddhist monastery is a must-visit for those looking for some peace and solitude.
  • Singhik Viewpoint: This viewpoint offers a mesmerizing view of Mount Khangchendzonga, the third-highest peak in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mangan

  • The Yumthang Valley trek: This is one of the most popular treks in Sikkim and takes you through some of the state’s most picturesque landscapes.
  • The Mangan-Singhik trek: This trek takes you through verdant forests, quaint villages, and offers a panoramic view of the Singhik Valley.
  • Singalila Ridge Trek: This trek takes you through the border between India and Nepal, and offers breathtaking views of the Kangchenjunga range.

Best Things to Do in Mangan: Activities and Tours

  • Yak Safari: This is one of the most popular activities in Mangan, and takes you through some of the region’s most scenic landscapes.
  • River Rafting: The Teesta River offers exciting opportunities for river rafting enthusiasts.
  • North Sikkim Tour: The tour takes you through some of the most breathtaking landscapes in North Sikkim, such as Lachung, Lachen, and Yumthang Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Mangan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momos: These are steamed or fried dumplings filled with vegetables or meat and are a favorite street food in Sikkim.
  • Thukpa: This is a noodle soup prepared in a spicy broth and served with vegetables and meat. It is a common dish in Eastern India.
  • Chhang: This is a local beer made from fermented rice and is a must-try for anyone visiting Mangan.

Local Beverages

  • Butter tea: This is a tea mixed with yak butter and salt and serves to combat altitude sickness.
  • Chaang: This is a mild alcoholic beverage made from millet or rice and is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Mangan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Royal Lachen: This is a comfortable and family-friendly hotel that offers scenic views of the valley.
  • The Snow Valley Resort: This is a luxury resort with modern amenities and excellent service.
  • The Tashi Tagey Resort: This is a mid-range hotel nestled amidst lush green forests and offers a peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Mangan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Sikkim is famous for its intricate handicrafts, such as woven carpets, handcrafted woolen garments, and woven baskets.
  • Tea: Sikkim is known for its tea plantations, and you can find high-quality green tea and black tea in the local markets.
  • Spices: Sikkim is known for its aromatic spices such as cardamom, ginger, and cinnamon, and you can bring home a variety of spices from Mangan.

Local Culture and Customs of Mangan

The people of Mangan are friendly and hospitable, and their culture is deeply rooted in Buddhism. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. The town is home to several monasteries and temples, where visitors can learn about the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Mangan

The best time to visit Mangan is from March to June and from October to December when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the skies are clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mangan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry warm clothing, as the temperature drops significantly at night.
  • Travel with a local guide to learn about the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or disturb the local ecosystem.
  • Do not take photographs without permission.
  • Do not engage in any activities that harm the environment or the local communities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mangan

Mangan is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors are advised to be cautious while traveling alone at night and avoid carrying valuables.

Health in Mangan

Altitude sickness can be a concern, especially for those who are not used to high altitudes. Visitors are advised to rest for a day or two to acclimate to the altitude and drink plenty of fluids.

Getting to Mangan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mangan

The nearest airport to Mangan is Bagdogra Airport in West Bengal, which is 170 km away. The airport has regular flights to major Indian cities such as Mumbai, Delhi, and Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Mangan

Mangan is well connected by road and has regular bus services from nearby towns and cities such as Gangtok and Siliguri.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Mangan. The bumpy roads of Mangan can be seen as a thrilling adventure itself.

How to Get Around in Mangan

Mangan is a small town, and most places of interest can be reached on foot. Visitors can also hire a taxi or bike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mangan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gangtok: The capital of Sikkim, Gangtok is known for its scenic beauty, vibrant culture, and ancient monasteries.
  • Lachen: Known for its natural beauty and breathtaking landscapes, Lachen is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Lachung: This beautiful valley is known for its apple orchards, beautiful waterfalls, and stunning views of the Himalayas.

Conclusion

Mangan has much to offer for adventure enthusiasts, nature lovers, and those looking to experience the local culture and traditions of Sikkim. It is a perfect destination for those seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience and a chance to explore the beautiful Himalayan landscapes. Plan your visit to Mangan today, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience!
