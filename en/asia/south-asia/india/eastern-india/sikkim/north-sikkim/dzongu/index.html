Introduction to Dzongu

Dzongu is a beautiful hillside village located in North Sikkim near the Kanchenjunga mountain range. The village, which is only accessible by foot, is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, including rolling hills, lush forests, and sparkling rivers. Dzongu is home to the Lepcha tribe, an indigenous group with their unique culture and traditions. As a result, the village has a rich cultural history, and visitors can explore museums, historical sites, and local handicraft stores. Travel to Dzongu is an opportunity to explore the region’s natural beauty, culture, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Dzongu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lepcha Heritage Museum: This museum displays the unique culture and heritage of the Lepcha tribe. It showcases traditional dresses, headgears, agricultural tools, and equipment used in fishing, hunting and agriculture.

  • Dzongu Information Center: This center is dedicated to the conservation of biodiversity, flora, and fauna of Dzongu. It provides information about local tour guides, trekking routes, and precautions to be taken while trekking.

  • Kusongna Khavang: This place is a holy Lepcha ritualistic house made entirely of bamboo and used for religious and cultural ceremonies.

Natural Attractions of Dzongu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baktim Waterfall: This cascading waterfall is situated near Lower Dzongu and is the perfect spot for a picnic with scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Tholung Monastery: The Tholung Monastery is one of the oldest and largest Buddhist monasteries in North Sikkim. It is located in a scenic location surrounded by snow peaks and valleys.

  • Chungthang: Chungthang is a beautiful town located at the junction of the Lachen and Lachung Rivers. The town is surrounded by rolling hills and streams and is an excellent spot for adventure activities such as rock climbing and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dzongu

  • Dzongu Trek: This trek takes you through beautiful forests and past cascading waterfalls as you explore the natural beauty of Dzongu.

  • Mountain Biking: Dzongu offers excellent opportunities for mountain biking, with trails taking you through rolling hills and lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Dzongu: Activities and Tours

  • River Rafting: The Teesta River, located near Dzongu, offers excellent river rafting opportunities for adventure seekers.

  • Cultural Tour: Visitors can take a cultural tour of Dzongu to learn about the Lepcha culture and community. The tour includes a visit to the Lepcha Heritage Museum, ethnic handicraft stores and a traditional Lepcha meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Dzongu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sael Roti: Sael Roti is a traditional Nepali dish made using rice flour and water. The round-shaped bread is deep-fried and served with assorted curries.

  • Gundruk: Gundruk is a side dish that’s prepared using dried and fermented leafy greens. It’s eaten with rice or roti and is a popular Lepcha delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Chaang: Chaang is a local alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet or rice. It’s a traditional Lepcha drink and is served in bamboo mugs.

Where to Stay in Dzongu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hee Bermiok: Hee Bermiok is a small village located near Dzongu and offers a range of homestays and guesthouses for visitors to choose from.

  • Upper Dzongu: Upper Dzongu offers a variety of lodging options, from guesthouses to campsites for those looking for a unique experience.

What to Buy in Dzongu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase traditional handicrafts such as bamboo baskets, hand-carved wooden items, and woolen shawls from local shops and markets.

  • Local Produce: Visitors can also buy local produce such as fresh fruits, honey, cardamom, and ginger from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Dzongu

The Lepcha community is an integral part of Dzongu, and visitors must respect and follow their customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly and avoid taking photographs without permission. Additionally, visitors are encouraged to try local foods and participate in cultural activities to immerse themselves in the Lepcha culture.

Best Time to Visit Dzongu

The best time to visit Dzongu is between March and May when the weather is comfortable, and the flowers are in full bloom. The monsoon season between July and September is not recommended due to landslides and road closures, while the winter months from November to February are too cold for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dzongu

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.

  • Treat local residents with kindness and respect.

  • Take suitable precautions while trekking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.

  • Disrupt local festivals or ceremonies.

  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dzongu

  • Visitors are advised to be cautious while trekking, as the terrain in this region can be challenging.

  • Visitors should avoid consuming street food and drinking water, as it may not be hygienic.

Health in Dzongu

  • Visitors should carry a first aid kit and essential medication.

  • Visitors should consult a doctor before traveling to Dzongu as the region is home to many insects and malaria is common in this region.

Getting to Dzongu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dzongu

  • Bagdogra Airport: Bagdogra airport is the nearest airport to Dzongu, located 124 km away. Visitors can hire a taxi from the airport to reach Dzongu.

Bus Stations Near Dzongu

  • Siliguri: Siliguri is the nearest bus station to Dzongu, located 120 km away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus from the station to reach Dzongu.

Other Options

  • Shared Cabs: Shared cabs and taxis are available from Siliguri and Gangtok to Dzongu.

How to Get Around in Dzongu

  • Visitors can explore Dzongu on foot or by hiring a taxi or bike.

Where to Go Next from Dzongu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gangtok: Gangtok is the capital city of Sikkim and is located 70 km from Dzongu. Visitors can explore the city’s culture, cuisine, and shopping.

  • Lachung: Lachung is located 60 km from Dzongu and is a beautiful town with scenic views and adventure activities such as trekking and river rafting.

Conclusion

Dzongu offers a unique travel experience, with its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural history, and outdoor adventure activities. The region is home to the Lepcha tribe, and visitors can learn about their way of life, customs, and traditions. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the best places to visit, things to do and see, and the best places to stay and eat in Dzongu.
