Introduction to Geyzing

Geyzing is a scenic town located in West Sikkim in the Indian state of Sikkim. Surrounded by the majestic Himalayan ranges, this town is nestled at an elevation of 6,000 feet above sea level. Geyzing is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, offering numerous opportunities for trekking, hiking, and biking. Along with its stunning landscape, Geyzing is also known for its rich history, culture, and tradition. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, local customs, and safety tips for Geyzing.

List of Places to Visit in Geyzing

Here are some must-visit places in Geyzing:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rabdentse Ruins - The ruins of a 17th-century capital of the erstwhile kingdom of Sikkim. It offers stunning views of Mount Kanchenjunga and the surrounding forests.
  • Pemayangtse Monastery - A 300-year-old monastery known for its intricate wood carvings and religious artifacts.
  • Khecheopalri Lake - A sacred lake for Buddhists, revered for its spiritual significance and scenic beauty.
  • Tashiding Monastery - A significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists, located on a hilltop and offers panoramic views of the surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Geyzing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kanchenjunga National Park - A UNESCO World Heritage Site offering breathtaking views of Mount Kanchenjunga, the third-highest peak in the world. The park also houses diverse flora and fauna, including the red panda and snow leopard.
  • Singshore Bridge - The second-highest bridge in Asia, offering stunning views of the Rangit River and the surrounding valleys.
  • Barsey Rhododendron Sanctuary - A biodiversity-rich sanctuary renowned for its vibrant rhododendron flowers and rich flora and fauna.
  • Legship Hot Springs - Natural hot springs known for their therapeutic properties and serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Geyzing

Geyzing offers a range of hiking and biking routes, catering to different levels of expertise. Some popular routes include:

  • Tashiding to Yuksom - A scenic trek through forests and hills, passing by old monasteries and ancient chortens. It takes approximately three days to complete.
  • Barsey to Hilley Trek - A moderately challenging trek through forests and meadows, offering stunning views of the surrounding peaks and valleys. It takes approximately four hours to complete.
  • Geyzing to Kaluk Bicycle Trail - A scenic and easy biking trail that takes you through small villages and forests, offering panoramic views of the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Geyzing: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in Geyzing:

  • River Rafting in Teesta River - Experience the thrill of river rafting in the Teesta River that runs through the town.
  • Bird Watching Tours - Explore the diverse birdlife of Geyzing, which includes pheasants, sunbirds, and cuckoos.
  • Village Walks - Take a stroll through the quaint villages around Geyzing and explore the local way of life.
  • Sikkimese Cuisine Tasting - Sample the local delicacies of Sikkimese cuisine, which includes momos, thukpa, and chhurpi.

Food and Drink Scene of Geyzing

Must-Try Dishes

Some must-try dishes in Geyzing include:

  • Momos - Steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat, often served with spicy sauce.
  • Thukpa - A hearty noodle soup made with vegetables, meat, or eggs, seasoned with herbs and spices.
  • Chhurpi - A hard cheese made from yak or cow milk, served as a snack or used as an ingredient in dishes.
  • Gundruk - Fermented leafy greens, often served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

Some popular local beverages in Geyzing include:

  • Chaang - A traditional drink made from fermented millet, served in a bamboo container.
  • Tongba - A drink made from fermented millet, served in a bamboo container with a straw.
  • Thomba - A drink made from fermented millet, served hot.

Where to Stay in Geyzing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Geyzing offers a range of accommodation options, catering to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Yangthang Dzimkha Resort - A luxurious resort offering stunning views of the Himalayas and equipped with modern amenities.
  • The Elgin Mount Pandim - A heritage hotel located amidst forests and hills, offering a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture.
  • Hotel Red Palace - A budget hotel located in the heart of the town, offering basic amenities and services.
  • Zambala Lodge - A guesthouse run by a local family, offering a homely and comfortable atmosphere.

What to Buy in Geyzing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Geyzing include:

  • Handcrafted items - Geyzing is known for its intricate handicrafts, including traditional paintings, wood carvings, and textiles.
  • Sikkimese tea - Sikkim is renowned for its delicious tea, which includes various aromatic blends like Darjeeling tea and green tea.
  • Buddhist artifacts - Geyzing is known for its rich Buddhist heritage, and you can find various artifacts like prayer flags, incense sticks, and singing bowls.
  • Local spices - Sikkim is known for its unique blends of spices, including Sikkim cardamom and Sikkim pepper.

Local Culture and Customs of Geyzing

Geyzing is a melting pot of diverse cultures and traditions, with a predominantly Buddhist population. Here are some local customs and etiquette to keep in mind:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting monasteries and other religious sites, and remove your shoes before entering.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals, especially monks and nuns.
  • Do not disturb or touch the prayer flags or objects.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, and do not engage in activities that might offend the locals.

Best Time to Visit Geyzing

The best time to visit Geyzing is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the skies are clear, offering optimal conditions for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The monsoon season (June to August) should be avoided, as road transportation becomes difficult, and landslides are common.

Do’s and Don’ts of Geyzing

Do:

  • Carry warm clothes, as the temperature can drop significantly, especially during the winter months.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions, and be courteous to the locals.
  • Carry cash, as ATMs and credit card facilities are limited.
  • Be mindful of the environment and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Do not pluck flowers or plants from the national park or wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Do not engage in activities that might harm or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Do not leave the designated trails while trekking or hiking.
  • Do not use plastic bottles or bags, as they are not biodegradable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Geyzing

Geyzing is a relatively safe town, and violent crimes are rare. However, it is essential to take necessary precautions, especially while trekking or hiking. Some safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Hire a certified guide for trekking or hiking.
  • Carry a first-aid kit and necessary medicines.
  • Carry sufficient water and food.
  • Avoid trekking or hiking alone.

Health in Geyzing

Geyzing is a high-altitude area, and altitude sickness can be a concern for some travelers. To avoid altitude sickness, it is essential to acclimate gradually and stay hydrated. It is also recommended to carry necessary medications.

Getting to Geyzing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Geyzing is well-connected to major cities in India via road transport. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Geyzing

  • The nearest airport to Geyzing is Bagdogra Airport, located approximately 124 km away.

Bus Stations Near Geyzing

  • Geyzing is well-connected by road, and regular buses ply between major cities in Sikkim and neighboring states.

Other Options

  • Shared taxis and private cabs are also available from major cities in Sikkim and neighboring states.

How to Get Around in Geyzing

Geyzing is a small town, and most of the attractions and activities are within walking distance. Shared taxis and private cabs are also available, and you can negotiate the fare with the driver.

Where to Go Next from Geyzing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Geyzing:

  • Yuksom - A historical town located approximately 25 km from Geyzing, known for its ancient monasteries and religious artifacts.
  • Pelling - A scenic town located approximately 32 km from Geyzing, offering breathtaking views of the Himalayas and lush forests.
  • Namchi - A cultural town located approximately 50 km from Geyzing, known for its giant Buddha statue and other religious sites.

Conclusion
