Introduction to Pelling

Pelling is a small town in the West Sikkim district of Sikkim, India. Situated at an altitude of 2,150 meters, it is known for its breathtaking views of the Himalayas, lush greenery, and tranquil environment. Pelling also serves as the base for people who want to explore West Sikkim.

If you are planning a trip to Pelling, read on to discover the best places to visit, food to eat, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Pelling

Pelling has a lot of culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Pelling.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Pemayangtse Monastery
  2. Sangachoeling Monastery
  3. Rabdentse Ruins

The Pemayangtse Monastery is Pelling’s most significant attraction. This 1705 Monastery is one of the oldest of its kind in Sikkim and houses sacred artifacts and religious carvings. The Sangachoeling Monastery is another famous Monastery in Pelling. It was built-in 1697 and is known for its religious importance and wall paintings. Rabdentse Ruins is a historical site in Pelling that dates back to the seventeenth century. The site comprises the ruins of the former capital of Sikkim.

Natural Attractions of Pelling: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Khecheopalri Lake
  2. Khangchendzonga National Park
  3. Yuksom

Pelling also has several natural attractions that offer a serene and picturesque experience to its visitors. Khecheopalri Lake is one such place, known for its crystal clear water and abundant wildlife. The Khangchendzonga National Park, located just 32 km from Pelling, is home to Khangchendzonga, India’s highest peak. It is one of the most popular treks in Pelling. Yuksom, also known as the “meeting place of three lamas,” is another famous natural attraction in Pelling. This village is famous for trekking to different mountain passes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pelling

Pelling is known for its fantastic trekking routes that take you through breathtaking landscapes. Here are a few hiking/biking routes in Pelling:

  1. Dzongri Trek
  2. Singalila Trek
  3. Khecheopalri Trek

The Dzongri Trek is considered one of the most challenging treks in Pelling. It takes you through scenic views of valleys, forests, and rivers, and is best done during spring and autumn. The Singalila Trek takes you through the border of India and Nepal and offers stunning views of Mt. Everest, Mt. Kanchenjunga, and other peaks. The Khecheopalri Trek takes you through the Khecheopalri Lake and offers a unique experience of local culture and lifestyle.

Best Things to Do in Pelling: Activities and Tours

Pelling has a lot of activities and tours that you can do to make the most of your trip. Some of them are:

  1. Yak Safari
  2. Paragliding
  3. Mountain Biking

Yak Safari is a unique experience that takes you through the rugged terrain of Pelling, offering fantastic views of the Himalayas. Paragliding is another fun activity to try in Pelling. You can soar through the skies and admire the natural beauty of Pelling from above. Mountain biking is also a popular activity in Pelling, offering a thrilling experience through the steep slopes and rugged terrains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pelling

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long trip to Pelling:

Day 1: Arrive in Pelling and visit the Pemayangtse Monastery Day 2: Visit the Khecheopalri lake and trek around it Day 3: Trek through the Khangchendzonga National Park Day 4: Visit Yuksom, go kayaking or rafting in the Tshushah river Day 5: Paragliding and mountain biking Day 6: Visit the Sangachoeling Monastery and Rabdentse Ruins Day 7: Relax, shop for souvenirs and sample local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Pelling

Pelling offers a delectable range of food and beverages. Let’s take a look:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Thukpa
  2. Momos
  3. Saelroti

Thukpa is a hearty soup made with noodles, vegetables or meat, and spices. Momos are dumplings stuffed with a variety of fillings {chicken, pork, beef and vegetables}. Saelroti is a popular Nepali snack made with rice flour.

Local Beverages

  1. Chaang
  2. Tongba
  3. Kumchu

Chaang is a fermented beer made from barley or millet, and it is the local’s favorite. Tongba is a traditional hot drink made from fermented millet that is popular during winter. Kumchu is a rice-based alcohol that is distilled and consumed during special occasions.

Where to Stay in Pelling: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pelling Resort
  2. Norbu Ghang Resort
  3. Hotel Tashi Delek Regency

Pelling has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guest houses. Pelling Resort and Norbu Ghang Resort are two of the most popular options for those seeking luxury accommodations. Hotel Tashi Delek Regency offers a comfortable stay at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Pelling: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sikkimese handicrafts
  2. Prayer flags
  3. Sikkimese tea

Sikkimese handicrafts, prayer flags and Sikkimese tea are the top things to buy when in Pelling. You can pick up beautiful carpets, hand-woven shawls, and handcrafted wooden items from local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Pelling

The locals in Pelling are friendly and hospitable people. They follow the Buddhist religion, and you will find many colorful monasteries and prayer flags throughout the town. The best way to show respect to their culture is to dress modestly, remove your shoes before entering religious sites, and seek permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Pelling

The best time to visit Pelling is between September and November and March to May. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, offering the best views of the Himalayas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pelling

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine and beverages
  2. Respect the local culture and customs
  3. Be polite and courteous to the locals

Don’t:

  1. Litter or pollute the environment
  2. Disrespect religious sites and practices
  3. Wear revealing clothes

Safety and Health

Safety in Pelling

Pelling is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Avoid traveling alone at night and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Pelling

Make sure to carry insect repellent and apply it before going out. Always drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent stomach infections.

Getting to Pelling: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pelling

The nearest airport to Pelling is the Bagdogra Airport, located around 160 km away.

Bus Stations Near Pelling

The nearest major bus station is the Siliguri Bus Station, which is a 5-hour drive from Pelling.

Other Options

You can also travel to Pelling by taxi or private car.

How to Get Around in Pelling

The best way to get around Pelling is by hiring a taxi or a private car. You can also rent a bike or a scooter to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Pelling: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Gangtok
  2. Ravangla
  3. Darjeeling

Gangtok, Ravangla and Darjeeling are nearby destinations that are popular among tourists. These places offer a unique experience of the local culture, food, and lifestyle.

Conclusion

Pelling is a hidden gem of Sikkim that offers a plethora of attractions and activities to its visitors. It’s a perfect place for people who want to explore the natural wonders of the Himalayas and experience the local culture and cuisine. With this travel guide, you will be well-prepared to plan your trip to this beautiful destination.
