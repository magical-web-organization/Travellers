Introduction to Kendubillo

Nestled in the eastern part of India, in the state of West Bengal, lies the quaint town of Kendubillo. This sleepy town may not be on many tourist maps, but it is a hidden gem that is perfect for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. Kendubillo is home to tranquil forests, scenic landscapes, and culturally significant landmarks that offer a glimpse into the state’s rich heritage. The locals’ warm hospitality and delectable cuisine make it a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the real India.

List of Places to Visit in Kendubillo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Temples and Historical Places

Kendubillo is steeped in culture and history, with several important landmarks to explore. Here are some must-visit sites:

  • Kendubillo Rajbari: This palace is an architectural marvel, showcasing the grandeur and opulence of the bygone era. The intricate carvings, sprawling gardens, and majestic interiors will leave you awestruck.
  • Kendubillo Museum: This museum is a treasure trove of ancient artifacts, textiles, paintings, and sculptures that showcase the culture and history of Kendubillo.
  • Kendubillo Temple: This temple is a spiritual haven that attracts devotees from all over the country. The intricate carvings, the soothing chants, and the aroma of incense sticks make it a serene and tranquil place.
  • Kolkata Victoria Memorial: This magnificent marble structure is a tribute to Queen Victoria’s reign and boasts of lush gardens, stunning statues, and a museum that houses remarkable artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Kendubillo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kendubillo’s natural beauty is best experienced by getting out and exploring the great outdoors. Here are some top picks:

  • Buxa Tiger Reserve: This sprawling reserve is home to several exotic species of flora and fauna, including the Bengal tiger, elephant, and Indian one-horned rhinoceros.
  • Jayanti River: The Jayanti river originates from the foothills of the Himalayan range and flows through the Buxa Tiger Reserve. Visitors can indulge in angling, trekking, and birdwatching while enjoying the scenic beauty.
  • Buxa Fort Trek: The historic Buxa Fort is perched on a hilltop and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valley. The trek up to the fort is a challenging yet rewarding experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kendubillo

Kendubillo is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several trails to choose from. Some popular options include:

  • Buxa Raidak Trek: This trail is perfect for adventurers who want to conquer the rugged terrain and indulge in bird-watching and angling.
  • Buxa Tiger Reserve Trek: This trek is perfect for nature lovers, bird enthusiasts, and photographers looking to capture the beauty of the reserve in all its glory.
  • Buxa Hills Trek: This trail takes you through the dense forest cover and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

Best Things to Do in Kendubillo: Activities and Tours

Here are some fun activities and tours that you shouldn’t miss while in Kendubillo:

  • Cooking Classes: Learn the art of Indian cooking by taking a class with a local chef and preparing traditional dishes.
  • Cycling Tours: Explore Kendubillo on two wheels by taking a cycling tour around town.
  • Tea Estate Visit: Visit one of the many tea estates around Kendubillo and learn about the tea-making process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kendubillo

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Kendubillo:

Day 1: Arrival in Kendubillo and check-in at your hotel. Day 2: Visit Kendubillo Rajbari and Museum. Day 3: Experience the natural beauty of Buxa Tiger Reserve. Day 4: Trek up to Buxa Fort and enjoy the breathtaking views. Day 5: Take a cooking class and taste some local delicacies. Day 6: Go on a cycling tour of Kendubillo. Day 7: Visit a nearby tea estate and learn about the tea-making process.

Food and Drink Scene of Kendubillo

Must-Try Dishes

Kendubillo is known for its mouth-watering cuisine that blends Indian and Bengali flavors. Here are some dishes that you must try:

  • Pitha: These sweet or savory pancakes made with rice flour are a staple dish of Bengali cuisine.
  • Macher Jhol: This spicy fish curry is a Bengali classic.
  • Rasgulla: This sweet and spongy dessert made with cottage cheese and sugar is a popular dessert in West Bengal.

Local Beverages

Kendubillo’s local beverage scene is vibrant and offers refreshing drinks to beat the heat. Here are some must-try local beverages:

  • Sattu: Made with roasted gram flour, this drink is perfect for hot summer days and is an excellent thirst quencher.
  • Lassi: This yogurt-based drink is a must-have with any meal and is available in several flavors.

Where to Stay in Kendubillo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kendubillo offers several accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some top picks:

  • Kendubillo Rajbari Heritage Palace: This palace-turned-hotel offers luxurious accommodation and excellent amenities.
  • Kendubillo Backpackers Hostel: This hostel offers budget-friendly accommodation and is perfect for solo travelers and backpackers.
  • Kendubillo Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is centrally located and offers comfortable accommodation and personalized service.

What to Buy in Kendubillo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kendubillo is famous for its silk products, handicrafts, and tea. Here are some items that you should add to your shopping list:

  • Kendubillo Silk Sarees: These hand-woven sarees are a specialty of Kendubillo and make for a great souvenir.
  • Bakuli: These decorative hand fans are a popular handicraft item in Kendubillo.
  • Kendubillo Tea: You can’t leave Kendubillo without trying some of the locally produced tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Kendubillo

Kendubillo’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in history and tradition. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Respect: The locals are very hospitable, and it’s important to respect their beliefs and customs.
  • Attire: While there are no strict dress codes, it’s best to dress modestly while visiting places of religious significance.
  • Language: Bengali is the most widely spoken language in Kendubillo.

Best Time to Visit Kendubillo

The best time to visit Kendubillo is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. The monsoon season between June and September should be avoided due to heavy rainfall and roadblocks.
