Introduction to East

East India, situated in the state of West Bengal, is a land of diverse culture, natural beauty, and rich history. The region is gaining popularity among travelers for its breathtaking landscapes, scenic beauty, and important landmarks of Indian history. Being home to the city of Kolkata, a major cultural hub, East India is an ideal travel destination for both history buffs and nature enthusiasts alike. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a honeymoon couple, or a group traveler, East India has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in East

East India is filled with culturally significant attractions and natural beauty. Here’s a list of must-visit places in East India:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Victoria Memorial Hall, Kolkata
  2. Belur Math, Howrah
  3. Indian Museum, Kolkata
  4. Bishnupur, Bankura
  5. Hazarduari Palace, Murshidabad
  6. Cooch Behar Palace, Cooch Behar

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sundarbans Delta
  2. Darjeeling Hill Station
  3. Gorumara National Park, Jalpaiguri
  4. Sandakphu Trek, Darjeeling
  5. Neora Valley National Park, Kalimpong

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

East India is a perfect destination for travelers who seek adventure and experience. Here are some best things to do in East India:

  1. Take a heritage tour of Kolkata
  2. Jungle Safari in Sundarbans
  3. Trek to Sandakphu
  4. Visit Darjeeling’s tea plantations and enjoy tea tasting
  5. Explore the beauty of Ganges Delta

Local Culture and Customs of East

East India has a rich cultural heritage and traditions. Here are some customs and etiquettes to keep in mind while visiting:

  1. Greet locals with a smile
  2. Dress modestly while visiting temples and other religious places
  3. Remove your shoes before entering someone’s house
  4. Respect local customs and traditions

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East India is during the winter months between October and February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Here are some things to do and avoid while traveling to East India:

Do:

  1. Carry sufficient cash as many areas do not have ATM facilities
  2. Bargain while shopping in local markets
  3. Try different cuisines and local dishes

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter the surroundings
  2. Do not disrespect local customs and traditions
  3. Do not visit religious places without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East India is a relatively safe region to travel to. However, it’s always wise to take some precautions:

  1. Avoid areas with political tension
  2. Travel in groups and avoid traveling at night
  3. Carry necessary documents and keep them safe

Health in East

East India has a favorable health system. However, travelers should keep these tips in mind for a healthy and safe journey:

  1. Drink only bottled water
  2. Avoid eating from street vendors
  3. Carry a first-aid kit

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

To reach East India, you can choose from various transportation options:

Airports Near East

  1. Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport, Kolkata
  2. Bagdogra Airport, Darjeeling

Bus Stations Near East

  1. Esplanade Bus Station, Kolkata
  2. NJP Bus Terminal, Siliguri

Other Options

  1. Trains from major Indian cities to Kolkata
  2. Private taxis and cabs

How to Get Around in East

You can travel around East India via buses, taxis, and rented cars. In the city of Kolkata, the metro railway is the best option. In Darjeeling, you can take a shared taxi to reach different places.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

East India is a land of diverse culture and natural beauty. With its unique and authentic experiences, East India promises a memorable journey. Plan your trip well in advance and make sure to add some of these must-visit places in your itinerary for an unforgettable experience.
