Introduction to South

South Kolkata, located in West Bengal, Eastern India, is a vibrant and diverse region that offers visitors a unique cultural experience. From ancient temples to historic museums, delicious cuisine to natural beauty, South Kolkata has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll delve into the must-visit destinations and experiences that make South Kolkata such a special place.

List of Places to Visit in South

South Kolkata is home to a wealth of must-see destinations. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, nature, or adventure, there is something for everyone in this diverse region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Kolkata is steeped in history and culture, and there are many museums and historical sites that continue to pay tribute to these traditions. Some of the most popular tourist spots include:

  • Victoria Memorial: A sprawling white marble museum built in the memory of Queen Victoria, this iconic destination is one of the must-sees in Kolkata. Visitors can explore galleries displaying paintings, textiles, and artifacts from the British colonial era.
  • Birla Planetarium: Located in the heart of the city, this planetarium is the largest in Asia and a popular destination for both locals and tourists. With a range of shows and displays available, the Birla Planetarium is the perfect place for those interested in astronomy and science.
  • Indian Museum: The oldest and largest museum in India, the Indian Museum is a great place to learn about South Kolkata’s history and heritage. The museum features exhibits on anthropology, zoology, geology, and much more.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Those who love the great outdoors will be right at home in South Kolkata. From stunning parks to quaint lakes, there is something for everyone here.

  • Eco Park: Spread over 480 acres, Eco Park is a natural haven in the bustling city of Kolkata. Visitors can take a peaceful walk, hire a bicycle, or take a boat ride in a serene lake - this park has it all.
  • Botanical Garden: Spread over 273 acres and housing over 12,000 living plants, the Botanical Garden is the perfect place for nature lovers. Home to India’s oldest teak tree, this attraction is a must-visit for anyone coming to South Kolkata.
  • Rabindra Sarobar: This peaceful lake is perfect for a relaxing afternoon. Visitors can take a boat ride or enjoy a walk along the scenic banks of the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Adventurous travellers will be spoiled for choice in South Kolkata as there are many opportunities for hiking and biking in the region. Here are some popular routes:

  • Maidan: Located in the heart of the city, Maidan is the perfect place for a gentle walk. With plenty of greenery and open spaces, this is the ideal place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Hoogly River: Take a boat ride along the Hoogly River for stunning views of South Kolkata’s skyline. There are plenty of walking and cycling routes along the riverbank as well.
  • Kolkata South Heritage Trail: This cultural trail takes visitors through some of South Kolkata’s historic landmarks. Learn about the city’s rich history while exploring some of its most beautiful streets and buildings.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours available in South Kolkata to suit any type of traveller. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Food Tours: Kolkata is known for its delicious cuisine, and a food tour is a great way to experience the local delicacies. From street food to fine dining, there is something for everyone.
  • City Tours: Whether on foot or by bus, guided tours of the city are a great way to learn about the history and culture of Kolkata.
  • Heritage Walks: Take a guided walk through some of South Kolkata’s historic streets and buildings for a deeper insight into the city’s past.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Here’s a sample 7D/6N itinerary for anyone looking to explore South Kolkata:

Day 1: Arrive in Kolkata and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring Victoria Memorial and its surrounding gardens.

Day 2: Head to the Botanical Garden for a peaceful morning in nature. In the afternoon, head to the Indian Museum for a dose of culture.

Day 3: Take a food tour of Kolkata and sample some of the city’s best dishes.

Day 4: Visit the Birla Planetarium and learn about the stars and galaxies.

Day 5: Go on a heritage walk through some of South Kolkata’s historic streets and buildings.

Day 6: Take a boat ride along the Hoogly River for stunning views of the city’s skyline.

Day 7: Spend your final day exploring the city at your own pace before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South Kolkata is famous for its delicious cuisine, which is influenced by a mix of Bengali, British, and Nawabi traditions. Here are some must-try dishes during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kathi Rolls: A popular street food in Kolkata, Kathi rolls are typically made with spiced meats or vegetables wrapped in a paratha bread.
  • Rosogolla: A traditional Bengali sweet, Rosogolla is a spongy and syrupy dessert that is sure to delight your taste buds.
  • Fish Curry: Kolkata, being on the banks of the Ganges, has an excellent variety of fish curries for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Kolkata is known for its love for tea, and you will find countless tea stalls and shops serving a range of traditional and modern teas.
  • Lassi: This refreshing yogurt drink is a popular beverage that can be found throughout the city.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Kolkata has plenty of accommodation options to suit a range of budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended options:

  • The Lalit Great Eastern Kolkata: A luxurious heritage hotel that is perfect for those looking to splurge.
  • The Senator Hotel: A mid-range hotel that is perfect for families and couples.
  • Backpackers Park Kolkata: A budget-friendly hostel that is great for solo travellers and backpackers.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home from South Kolkata, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are some popular choices:

  • Dhokra Art: Made using the traditional Dhokra technique, these handmade figurines make for great souvenirs.
  • Tangail Sarees: These unique sarees are made using a unique weaving technique, and their bright colours and patterns make them a great choice.
  • Conch Shell Bangles: These beautiful bangles are made using conch shells and are a popular accessory in Kolkata.

Local Culture and Customs of South

South Kolkata has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and visitors should be aware of some of the customs and traditions of the region. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Sarees: The saree is the traditional dress of Bengali women, and it is often worn at weddings and other special occasions.
  • Durga Puja: This annual festival is celebrated throughout Kolkata and is a time for feasting, music, and prayer. Visitors should be sure to check the dates of the festival if they plan to visit during this time.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South Kolkata is during the winter months of November to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant. Monsoons start from June to September and the temperature stays humid and hot in the summers.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to South Kolkata:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the Food: Kolkata has an excellent food scene, and visitors should be sure to sample some of the local dishes during their stay.

Don’t:

  • Waste Water: Water is a precious resource in Kolkata, and visitors should conserve it whenever possible.
  • Photograph without Permission: Visitors should always ask permission before taking photographs of people, particularly in sensitive areas such as religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

While South Kolkata is generally safe for tourists, visitors should remain vigilant and keep a few safety tips in mind:

  • Avoid travelling alone at night: Travellers should avoid travelling alone at night, particularly in quiet areas.
  • Protect Valuables: Visitors should keep their valuables such as passports, cash, and jewellery safe at all times.

Health in South

Visitors should take certain precautions to protect their health during their stay in South Kolkata:

  • Drink bottled water: Visitors should avoid tap water and only drink bottled water to avoid getting sick.
  • Beware of Mosquitoes: Visitors should use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites, which can cause diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

Kolkata has one main airport:

  • Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport

Bus Stations Near South

South Kolkata is well-connected to other parts of the city via bus. Some of the major bus stations include:

  • Esplanade Bus Station
  • Karunamayee Bus Station

Other Options

Other transportation options to get to South Kolkata include:

  • Metro: Kolkata has a metro system that connects various parts of the city.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Kolkata and are a convenient way to get around.

How to Get Around in South

South Kolkata is well-connected, and there are many transportation options available. Some popular options include:

  • Metro: The Kolkata Metro is a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of transport in Kolkata for short distances.
  • Bus: Local buses connect South Kolkata with other parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to South Kolkata might be interested in:

  • Sundarbans: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger and is an ideal destination for a day trip from Kolkata.
  • Darjeeling: This hill town is famous for its tea plantations and offers stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • Varanasi: This holy city on the banks of the Ganges is one of the oldest and most sacred cities in India.

Conclusion

South Kolkata is a diverse and vibrant region that offers tourists a unique cultural experience. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, there is something for everyone in this beautiful part of India. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to South Kolkata and make the most of your experience in this magical destination.
