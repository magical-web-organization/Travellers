Introduction to Pandua (North Bengal)

Pandua is a picturesque town located in the Hooghly district of the Indian state of West Bengal. It is famous for its rich heritage and historical monuments that attract millions of tourists every year. It is known to be the capital of the Muslim Kingdom of Bengal during the 14th century.

The town is surrounded by lush green forests and is situated on the banks of the river Damodar, which adds to its natural beauty. Apart from the rich culture and history, what makes Pandua unique is the adventure activities such as hiking, biking, and trekking, one can indulge in.

List of Places to Visit in Pandua (North Bengal)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pandua has several culturally important museums and historical places. The most popular ones are:

  1. Adina Mosque: This is the largest mosque in India and an architectural marvel built during the 14th century.
  2. Gateway of Bara Sona Mosque: Located around 35 km from Pandua, this gateway is an exquisite example of the Indo-Islamic architecture of the 14th century.
  3. Shandeshawari Kali Temple: This temple is a famous Hindu pilgrimage site and is situated in the heart of the town.
  4. Hijli Mosque: This historical mosque is famous for its intricate terracotta carvings on the walls and is located around 70 km from Pandua.

Natural Attractions of Pandua (North Bengal): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the cultural significance, Pandua also has several natural attractions where visitors can indulge in outdoor adventures and soak in the scenic views. Some of the most popular ones are:

  1. Gadiara and Raichak: These are two popular riverfront towns located around 40 km away from Pandua, where one can indulge in boating, fishing, and taking a stroll along the river banks.
  2. Susunia Hills: Located around 80 km from Pandua, Susunia Hills is a popular trekking and rock-climbing destination with a breathtaking view.
  3. Gouranga Hill: This hill is located on the outskirts of the town and is famous for trekking and bird watching.
  4. Tamluk Salt Lake: This is a popular spot for camping, fishing, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pandua (North Bengal)

Biking and hiking are popular adventure activities in Pandua. The most popular biking and hiking routes are:

  1. Adina-Dakhil Darwaza Circuit: This route starts from Adina Mosque and ends at the Dakhil Darwaza gateway, covering almost 8 km.
  2. Bara Sona Mosque Circuit: This route covers the Bara Sona Mosque and the surrounding villages, covering around 15 km.
  3. Pandua-Susunia Hill Circuit: This is a challenging route that covers Pandua and the Susunia Hills, covering almost 50 km.

Best Things to Do in Pandua (North Bengal): Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and adventure activities, Pandua has several other things to offer to its visitors. Some of the best things to do when in Pandua are:

  1. Attend the local fairs and festivals that take place all around the year.
  2. Take a walk along the river banks and soak in the natural beauty.
  3. Try the local delicacies such as mishti doi and macher chop.
  4. Take a heritage tour of the town and visit the historical monuments and museums.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pandua (North Bengal)

A week-long itinerary for Pandua can be as follows:

Day 1: Arrival in Pandua, check-in to the hotel, and a leisure walk around the town. Day 2: Visit Adina Mosque and Dakhil Darwaza Gateway. Day 3: Indulge in adventure activities such as trekking and rock-climbing at Susunia Hills. Day 4: Take a tour of the Bara Sona Mosque and the surrounding villages. Day 5: Visit Shandeshawari Kali Temple and take a boat ride in Gadiara town. Day 6: Try the local food and take a heritage tour of Pandua town. Day 7: Departure from Pandua.

Food and Drink Scene of Pandua (North Bengal)

Must-Try Dishes

Pandua has a variety of delicious local cuisines that one must try when visiting the town. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  1. Mishti Doi: A sweet, sour, and creamy dessert made from fermented milk.
  2. Macher Chop: A fish croquette that is crisp on the outside and soft on the inside.
  3. Kosha Mangsho: A slow-cooked mutton curry.
  4. Luchi-Aloo Dom: Puffy fried bread served with spicy potato curry.
  5. Sandesh: A sweet made from cottage cheese and sugar.

Local Beverages

Pandua is famous for its sweet sugarcane juice, known as ganne ka ras. It is a refreshing drink that can be enjoyed chilled or at room temperature.

Where to Stay in Pandua (North Bengal): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pandua has several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. The most popular ones are:

  1. The Peerless Inn Pandua: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and excellent service.
  2. Hotel High Range: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms and good service.
  3. Pandua Tourist Lodge: A government-owned guest house that is affordable and offers basic amenities.

What to Buy in Pandua (North Bengal): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pandua is known for its cotton handloom sarees and marbled handicrafts. Visitors can also buy terracotta jewelry, hand-painted silk scarves, and clay utensils as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Pandua (North Bengal)

Pandua has a unique blend of cultures and customs, owing to its rich history and geographical location. The town has a predominantly Bengali population, with a significant Muslim population as well. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Pandua (North Bengal)

The best time to visit Pandua is between October and February, when the weather is pleasant and cool. The monsoon season, from June to September, should be avoided as heavy rainfall can cause flooding in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pandua (North Bengal)

Do:

  1. Dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Be careful while indulging in adventure activities such as hiking and trekking.
  3. Try the local delicacies and take a heritage tour of the town.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter in public places.
  2. Do not make noise or disturbance during visiting the mosques and temples.
  3. Do not engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pandua (North Bengal)

Pandua is a relatively safe town, and violent crimes are rare. However, visitors are advised to take precautions such as keeping their valuables safe and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Pandua (North Bengal)

Visitors are advised to take precautions against common illnesses such as malaria, dengue, and typhoid. It is recommended to consult a doctor before traveling and getting vaccinated.

Getting to Pandua (North Bengal): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pandua (North Bengal)

The nearest airport to Pandua is Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, located around 85 km away. From the airport, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to Pandua.

Bus Stations Near Pandua (North Bengal)

Pandua is well-connected through a network of buses and is easily accessible by road. The nearest bus stations are Tarakeswar and Haripal, which are around 30 km away.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to the nearest railway station in Tarakeswar or Haripal, and from there, take a taxi or a bus to Pandua.

How to Get Around in Pandua (North Bengal)

The best way to get around in Pandua is by hiring a taxi or a local motorcycle. Visitors can also take a cycle rickshaw or walk around the town.

Where to Go Next from Pandua (North Bengal): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pandua is surrounded by several tourist destinations that can be visited as a day trip or an excursion. The most popular ones are:

  1. Kolkata: The capital of West Bengal is located around 85 km away from Pandua and is famous for its colonial heritage and bustling streets.
  2. Chandannagar: A former French colony, Chandannagar is located around 40 km away and is famous for its historical monuments and cultural events.
  3. Shantiniketan: A small university town located around 120 km away from Pandua, known for its cultural heritage and handicrafts.

Conclusion

Pandua is a beautiful town with a rich cultural heritage and numerous adventure activities to indulge in. Visitors can explore its historical monuments, soak in the natural beauty, and indulge in the local cuisine. It is a must-visit destination for history buffs and adventure enthusiasts alike.
