Introduction to Cooch Behar

Nestled in the Northern Plains of West Bengal, Cooch Behar is a historic city with a rich cultural heritage. The city is known for its exquisite architecture, vibrant festivals, and friendly people. If you’re looking for a unique travel experience, Cooch Behar is the place to be!

List of Places to Visit in Cooch Behar

Here is a rundown of the top destinations and attractions in Cooch Behar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cooch Behar Palace: The Cooch Behar Palace is a stunning example of classical European architecture. The palace is an important landmark in the city and houses ancient sculptures, paintings, and manuscripts.

  • Madan Mohan Temple: One of the oldest temples in the city, this temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of Cooch Behar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rasikbil Bird Sanctuary: Located around 10 kilometers from Cooch Behar, the Rasikbil Bird Sanctuary is a haven for bird watchers. The sanctuary is home to over 250 species of birds, including exotic migratory birds from faraway lands.

  • Baneswar Shiva Temple: The Baneswar Shiva Temple is a serene and peaceful spot located in the heart of the city. Surrounded by lush greenery, the temple offers visitors a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cooch Behar

  • Bamandanga Forest Trail: The Bamandanga Forest Trail is a beautiful hiking trail that stretches through the dense forest. The trail offers visitors the chance to explore the local flora and fauna and enjoy incredible views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Torsa River Trail: The Torsa River Trail runs along the Torsa River, offering visitors stunning views of the river and the rugged terrain of the region. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and nature walks.

Best Things to Do in Cooch Behar: Activities and Tours

  • Festival Tours: Cooch Behar is known for its vibrant festivals and cultural celebrations. Visitors can take part in festivities such as the Rash Mela, Durga Puja, and Gajon.

  • Cultural Tours: Cultural tours offer visitors the chance to explore the rich cultural heritage of the region. Visitors can visit ancient temples, museums, and other cultural landmarks in the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Cooch Behar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pithe Puli: Pithe Puli is a traditional Bengali sweet delicacy made from rice flour, coconut, and jaggery. It is a must-try dish for all visitors to Cooch Behar.

  • Luchi Aloo Dum: A popular breakfast dish in West Bengal, Luchi Aloo Dum is a combination of puffed bread and spicy potato curry.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: Lassi is a refreshing and healthy yoghurt-based drink that is popular in Cooch Behar.

  • Chaang: Chaang is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet. The drink is popular during festive celebrations.

Where to Stay in Cooch Behar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Cooch Behar, catering to all budgets and preferences. Some recommended options include:

  • Neelambar Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city, Neelambar Hotel offers modern amenities and great service.

  • Hotel Royal Palace: Hotel Royal Palace is a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the city’s major attractions.

  • Moon Light Lodge: For those on a tight budget, Moon Light Lodge is a great option. This guesthouse offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Cooch Behar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tant Sarees: Tant Sarees are a popular type of saree in West Bengal. These sarees are made from cotton and come in a wide variety of colours and designs.

  • Bamboo Products: Bamboo is a versatile material that is used extensively in Cooch Behar. Visitors can buy handcrafted bamboo products such as baskets, mats, and decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Cooch Behar

Cooch Behar has a diverse, multicultural society with a rich history and religious traditions. Visitors should respect the local customs and dress conservatively. They should also remove their shoes before entering temples and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Cooch Behar

The best time to visit Cooch Behar is between October and March, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring the city’s attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cooch Behar

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Visit the city’s cultural landmarks and attend local festivals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cooch Behar

Cooch Behar is a relatively safe city, and visitors are unlikely to face any significant security concerns. However, it is always advisable to take standard safety precautions, such as securing valuables and not venturing out alone at night.

Health in Cooch Behar

Visitors should take appropriate vaccinations before traveling to Cooch Behar. They should also drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent stomach-related illnesses.

Getting to Cooch Behar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cooch Behar

The nearest airport to Cooch Behar is Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata. From there, visitors can take a cab or bus to Cooch Behar.

Bus Stations Near Cooch Behar

Cooch Behar has a well-connected bus network, and visitors can use the buses to reach the city from nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Cooch Behar by train. Cooch Behar Railway Station is well-connected to major cities in West Bengal and other parts of India.

How to Get Around in Cooch Behar

Commuting in Cooch Behar is easy and convenient. Visitors can take a cab or hire a bike to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Cooch Behar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary: Located around 18 kilometers from Cooch Behar, the Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can spot elephants, tigers, rhinos and other wildlife in the sanctuary.

  • Darjeeling: One of the most popular tourist destinations in West Bengal, Darjeeling is known for its tea plantations, stunning views of the Himalayan range, and cool weather.

Conclusion

Cooch Behar is a hidden gem in West Bengal, offering visitors an opportunity to explore the rich cultural heritage and natural wonders of the region. With its vibrant festivals, beautiful architecture, and delicious local cuisine, Cooch Behar is a must-visit destination for all travel enthusiasts.
