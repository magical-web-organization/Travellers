Introduction to Northern Plains

Nestled in the eastern part of India, West Bengal is home to the picturesque Northern Plains, a region known for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The Northern Plains are dotted with historic sites, lush forests, and exciting activities, making it an ideal destination for nature and adventure enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to do and see in Northern Plains, as well as provide tips on where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Plains

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Northern Plains boasts several iconic museums and historical sites. The following are some must-visit attractions:

  • Victoria Memorial Hall: A magnificent and stunning museum that celebrates the cultural and historical heritage of India.
  • Indian Museum: A national museum and the largest in India, the Indian Museum houses a vast collection of archaeological and anthropological artifacts.
  • Writers’ Building: A prominent colonial-era building that served as the headquarters of the East India Company in Kolkata.

Natural Attractions of Northern Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Northern Plains are also renowned for their stunning natural beauty, and outdoor activities are plentiful. Here’s a list of must-experience activities in this region:

  • Sundarban National Park: A natural wonder of the world, Sundarban is home to the world’s largest mangrove forest and a vast array of wildlife, including the Bengal tiger.
  • Darjeeling: A world-famous hill station known for its panoramic views of the Himalayas and its tea plantations.
  • Gorumara National Park: A wildlife sanctuary and national park that is home to elephants, Indian rhinoceroses, and several bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Plains

Hiking and biking in Northern Plains is an amazing way to explore the natural beauty and stunning landscapes. Some of the best routes for hiking and biking include:

  • The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this railway offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan mountain range.
  • Singalila Ridge: A picturesque route that offers unparalleled views of the Everest, Makalu, and Kanchenjunga mountain ranges.
  • Kalimpong: A scenic trail that winds through tea gardens, pineapple orchards, and local villages.

Best Things to Do in Northern Plains: Activities and Tours

From cultural excursions to adventure sports, Northern Plains offer plenty of options for every type of traveler. Here are some of the top things to do and experience:

  • River Rafting: Enjoy river rafting on the Teesta River, which is a popular activity in the region.
  • Bengali Cooking Classes: Take a cooking class to learn how to cook authentic Bengali dishes like Doi Maach and Chanar Jalebi.
  • Tea Garden Visit: Visit the tea gardens of Darjeeling and experience the tea-making process from plucking to brewing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Plains

Here is a suggested seven-day itinerary to make the most of your time in Northern Plains:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Kolkata and explore the Victoria Memorial, St. Paul’s Cathedral, and Mother Teresa’s Home.
  • Day 2-3: Travel to Sundarban National Park and enjoy a boat safari to spot the Bengal tiger.
  • Day 4: Visit Kalimpong and explore the town’s monasteries, orchids, and local markets.
  • Day 5-6: Explore the tea gardens of Darjeeling and enjoy scenic views of the Himalayas.
  • Day 7: Head back to Kolkata and take a Bengali cooking class before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Plains

No trip to Northern Plains is complete without trying some of the local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Macher Jhol: A spicy fish stew made with potatoes and onions.
  • Rasgulla: A famous Bengali dessert made from cheese balls and served in a sweet syrup.
  • Kolkata Rolls: A popular street food consisting of paratha and different fillings such as egg, chicken, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Darjeeling Tea: A tea that is famous throughout the world for its distinct aroma and flavor.
  • Aam Panna: A refreshing summer drink made of green mangoes and spices like black salt and cumin.

Where to Stay in Northern Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Plains offers a wide range of accommodation options. From luxury hotels to budget guesthouses, there’s something for everyone. Here are some recommendations:

  • Taj Bengal Kolkata: A luxurious five-star hotel in Kolkata known for its excellent service and amenities.
  • Singtom Tea Estate and Resort: A boutique resort in Darjeeling perched on the Singtom Tea Estate.
  • Ganga Kutir: A serene and peaceful retreat in Sundarban National Park that offers comfortable cottages.

What to Buy in Northern Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Northern Plains, there are several local products that you can bring back as souvenirs. Here’s a list of some popular ones:

  • Darjeeling Tea: Purchase some high-quality tea leaves from one of Darjeeling’s tea shops.
  • Handicrafts: Shop for traditional handicrafts like kantha embroidery, terracotta artifacts, and Balucheri sarees.
  • Sandesh: A Bengali sweet made from cottage cheese that can be found in various sweet shops in Kolkata.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Plains

Northern Plains is a region with a unique cultural heritage. Here are a few customs and traditions that are commonly observed:

  • Bengali New Year: Celebrated in mid-April, Bengali New Year is a significant event in the Bengali calendar.
  • Durga Puja: A five-day festival that celebrates the victory of good over evil and is observed in September or October.
  • Kumari Puja: A unique ritual where a young girl is worshipped as the living embodiment of the goddess Durga.

Best Time to Visit Northern Plains

The best time to visit Northern Plains is during the winter months, between November and February. The weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy the scenic views of the Himalayas and explore the tea gardens comfortably.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Plains

Here are some do’s and don’ts for travelers to Northern Plains:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the seafood.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry ample cash, as several local shops and markets do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in religious sites.
  • Consume tap water, always opt for bottled water.
  • Make insensitive comments about different cultures and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Plains

Northern Plains is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should always be cautious and take basic precautions like locking their valuables and avoiding unlit alleys at night.

Health in Northern Plains

Travelers to Northern Plains should take certain health precautions while traveling. It is recommended that you stay updated on your vaccinations and avoid consuming street food.

Getting to Northern Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northern Plains is well-connected to the rest of India, and several transportation options are available. Here are some of the best ways to get to Northern Plains:

  • Flight: Kolkata is a major airport in the region and receives flights from various parts of India.
  • Train: Train is a popular mode of travel in India and provides easy access to Northern Plains.
  • Bus: Several buses operate between Kolkata and the Northern Plains region.

Airports Near Northern Plains

  • Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport, Kolkata
  • Bagdogra Airport, Siliguri Bihar

Bus Stations Near Northern Plains

  • Esplanade Bus Station, Kolkata

Other Options

  • Private taxis are available for hire from Kolkata and neighboring areas.

How to Get Around in Northern Plains

Northern Plains offer various transportation options to get around. Here are some recommended ways:

  • Private Taxis: Private taxis are easily available and are the most convenient way to get around Northern Plains.
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws and shared cabs are inexpensive and are a good way to explore the city on a budget.

Where to Go Next from Northern Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are several nearby destinations that you can explore from Northern Plains. Here are some suggestions:

  • Kolkata: Known as the cultural capital of India, Kolkata is a vibrant city that’s famous for its street food, architecture, and culture.
  • Gangtok: The capital of Sikkim, Gangtok is a beautiful hill station that offers stunning views of the Himalayan mountain range.
  • Bhutan: Bhutan is a neighboring country to India and can be reached from Northern Plains with ease.

Conclusion

Northern Plains, West Bengal is a beautiful region in India that offers something for everyone. From natural beauty and adventure sports to culture and history, this region is packed with opportunities for all types of travelers. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your next trip to Northern Plains, West Bengal.
