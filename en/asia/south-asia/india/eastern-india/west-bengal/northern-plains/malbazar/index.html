Introduction to Malbazar

Located in the Northern Plains of West Bengal, Malbazar is a small but charming town that sits at the foothills of the Himalayas. The town is known for its lush greenery, scenic views, and peaceful atmosphere. Malbazar is a popular destination for tourists who are looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in nature and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Malbazar

Malbazar offers a plethora of attractions that cater to different interests. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gorumara National Park: This national park is home to many species of flora and fauna and is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. Here, visitors can take a jeep safari to explore the park and spot animals like one-horned rhinoceros, elephants, and tigers.

  • Chilapata Forest: A lesser-known but equally stunning forest, Chilapata is home to several rare species of birds and animals. Visitors can take an elephant safari or a jeep ride to explore the forest.

  • Jaldhaka River: The Jaldhaka river offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is a popular spot for picnics and swimming.

  • The Malbazar Palace: Built-in 1925, this palace is an excellent example of neo-classical architecture and has now been converted into a heritage hotel.

Natural Attractions of Malbazar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Latpanchor: This is Malbazar’s highest point, offering panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Visitors can go camping or trekking in the area.

  • Samsing: This quaint village is located at the foothills of the Himalayas and is known for its tea gardens and scenic beauty. Visitors can go on nature walks, bird watching, and trekking in the area.

  • Suntalekhola: Another charming village in the area, Suntalekhola is known for its picturesque landscapes and waterfalls. Visitors can go on hikes and explore the area on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malbazar

Malbazar offers several options for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Trek to Neora Valley National Park: This is a moderate to the difficult trek that takes visitors through the lush forests and scenic valleys of the Neora Valley National Park.

  • Cycling around tea gardens: Malbazar is surrounded by tea gardens, and cycling around them is a popular activity for visitors. The tea gardens offer picturesque views and a chance to interact with the local community.

Best Things to Do in Malbazar: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions and outdoor adventures mentioned above, here are some other things to do in Malbazar:

  • Visit Tea Factories: Malbazar is known for its tea, and visitors can visit tea factories to learn about the process of tea-making and sample different types of tea.

  • Shop in the Local Market: Malbazar has a bustling local market where visitors can buy local handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

  • Take a Cultural Tour: Visitors can take a cultural tour to learn about the history, traditions, and customs of the local communities.

Food and Drink Scene of Malbazar

Malbazar is known for its tea and traditional Bengali cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rasgulla: A famous Bengali sweet made with cheese balls and dipped in syrup.

  • Macher Jhol: A traditional Bengali fish curry made with spices and served with rice.

  • Luchi: A deep-fried bread made with flour and served with chana dal or potato curry.

Local Beverages

  • Darjeeling Tea: Malbazar is located in the Darjeeling district, and the area is known for its high-quality tea.

  • Aam Panna: A refreshing drink made with mango pulp, sugar, and mint leaves.

Where to Stay in Malbazar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malbazar offers several options for accommodation. Here are some popular options:

  • The Malbazar Palace: This heritage hotel is located in the heart of Malbazar and offers comfortable rooms and personalized service.

  • Hilltop Homestay: This cozy homestay is located in Samsing village and offers panoramic views of the valley.

  • Banasree Lodge: This budget-friendly lodge is located in the main market area of Malbazar and offers clean rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Malbazar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Malbazar is known for its tea, and visitors can buy high-quality tea from the local markets.

  • Handicrafts: The local markets offer a wide range of handicrafts made by the local communities, including textiles, pottery, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Malbazar

The locals of Malbazar are warm and welcoming, and visitors are expected to respect their customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially while visiting religious places.

  • Remove your shoes before entering temples or homes.

  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Malbazar

The best time to visit Malbazar is between October and April when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season between June and September should be avoided.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malbazar

Do:

  • Observe local customs and traditions.

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

  • Carry a light jacket or sweater, as the temperature can drop in the evenings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs, traditions, or people.

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.

  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malbazar

Malbazar is a relatively safe destination, and visitors are unlikely to face any major safety-related issues. However, it is always advisable to take basic precautions such as:

  • Do not carry large amounts of cash or valuables.

  • Use only registered taxis or transportation services.

  • Keep emergency contact numbers handy.

Health in Malbazar

Visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid common travel-related illnesses such as diarrhea and dehydration. It is advisable to:

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.

  • Wash hands frequently.

  • Carry basic medications and first-aid kits.

Getting to Malbazar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malbazar

  • Bagdogra Airport: Located approximately 70 kilometers from Malbazar, Bagdogra Airport is the nearest domestic airport.

  • Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport: This airport is located in Kolkata and is approximately 670 kilometers from Malbazar.

Bus Stations Near Malbazar

  • Tenzing Norgay Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Siliguri, located approximately 50 kilometers from Malbazar.

Other Options

  • Private taxis and cars are readily available from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Malbazar

Getting around in Malbazar is relatively easy as the town is quite small. Visitors can choose from several options:

  • Auto-rickshaws: These are the most popular mode of transportation in Malbazar and can be hired for short distances or for the day.

  • Cabs and Taxis: Private taxis and cabs are available for rent, and prices are negotiable.

  • Buses: Local buses are available for travel to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Malbazar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Malbazar is well-connected with several nearby towns and cities, including:

  • Siliguri: Located approximately 50 kilometers from Malbazar, Siliguri is a bustling town known for its markets and tea gardens.

  • Darjeeling: Known for its tea estates and scenic beauty, Darjeeling is a popular hill station located approximately 85 kilometers from Malbazar.

  • Kalimpong: A charming hill station located approximately 100 kilometers from Malbazar, Kalimpong offers stunning views of the Himalayas and the Teesta river.

Conclusion

Malbazar is a hidden gem in West Bengal that is perfect for those seeking to explore the hills and valleys of the region. The town offers something for everyone, including scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and delicious food. Visit Malbazar for an unforgettable experience!
