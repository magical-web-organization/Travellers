Introduction to Alipurduar

Located in the Eastern part of India in the state of West Bengal, Alipurduar is a quaint town surrounded by dense forests and tea gardens. The town is famous for its natural beauty, wildlife sanctuaries, and adventure activities.

List of Places to Visit in Alipurduar

If you’re planning a trip to Alipurduar, here are some of the must-visit places in and around the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary: Known for its one-horned rhinoceros, elephant, and deer population, this sanctuary is a treat for wildlife enthusiasts.
  • Buxa Fort: This historic fort is located on the Buxa hills and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.
  • Jayanti Mahakal Caves: These ancient caves are located within the Buxa Tiger Reserve and are famous among adventure seekers and history buffs.
  • Rasikbil Bird Sanctuary: This bird sanctuary is located on the outskirts of the town and is home to a wide variety of migratory and local bird species.

Natural Attractions of Alipurduar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chilapata Forest: Located near Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary, this forest is known for its elephant population and is an ideal destination for jungle safaris.
  • Buxa Tiger Reserve: This wildlife sanctuary is spread across an area of 760 square kilometers and is known for its dense forests and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Murti River: Located at the foothills of the Himalayas, this picturesque river is a popular destination for camping, fishing, and rafting activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alipurduar

Alipurduar is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the popular routes in the region:

  • Buxa Hills Trek: This trek covers a distance of around 18 kilometers and takes you through dense forests and hills.
  • Gorumara National Park Trek: This trek is ideal for wildlife enthusiasts and covers a distance of around 30 kilometers.
  • Murti to Jhalong Trek: This trek is ideal for beginners and covers a distance of around 10 kilometers.

Best Things to Do in Alipurduar: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Ride in the Murti River: Take a peaceful boat ride in the Murti River and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Elephant Safari in Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary: Get up close with the one-horned rhinoceros and elephants on an elephant safari in Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Tea Garden Tour: Take a tour of the tea gardens in Alipurduar and learn about the tea-making process.

Food and Drink Scene of Alipurduar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momos: These steamed dumplings are a popular snack in the region and come in various fillings such as vegetable, chicken, and pork.
  • Thukpa: This noodle soup is a staple dish in the region and is perfect for warming up on a chilly evening.
  • Luchi and Alur Torkari: This traditional Bengali dish consists of puffed bread and spicy potato curry.

Local Beverages

  • Darjeeling Tea: Alipurduar is known for its tea gardens, and a cup of Darjeeling tea is a must-try for tea lovers.
  • Chaang: This local alcoholic beverage is made from fermented millet or rice and is often served during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Alipurduar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodation options in Alipurduar:

  • Alipurduar Tourist Lodge: Located near the railway station, this lodge offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Ranger’s Camp: This campsite is located inside the Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary and offers tents for accommodation.
  • Jaldapara Tourist Lodge: Located within the Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary, this lodge is perfect for wildlife enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Alipurduar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Alipurduar is known for its tea gardens, and you can buy a variety of tea blends to take home with you.
  • Handicrafts: The region is known for its traditional handicrafts such as bamboo and cane products.
  • Local Spices: You can also buy local spices such as cardamom, cinnamon, and black pepper to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Alipurduar

The people of Alipurduar are warm and friendly, and the region has a rich cultural heritage. The customs and traditions in Alipurduar are similar to those of other Bengali communities.

Best Time to Visit Alipurduar

The best time to visit Alipurduar is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is easier to spot. The town experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, which lasts from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alipurduar

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.
  • Cover your head while visiting religious places.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the wildlife sanctuaries and national parks.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious places.
  • Harm or disturb the wildlife in the national parks and sanctuaries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alipurduar

Alipurduar is a relatively safe town, but it is recommended to take basic precautions such as avoiding deserted areas and taking care of your belongings.

Health in Alipurduar

It is recommended to take necessary vaccinations before visiting Alipurduar, especially if you’re planning to visit the national parks and sanctuaries. It is also advisable to carry a first aid kit with you.

Getting to Alipurduar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alipurduar

The nearest airport to Alipurduar is Bagdogra Airport, which is located around 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Alipurduar

Alipurduar has a bus station that connects it to other towns and cities in West Bengal.

Other Options

You can also hire a private taxi or drive to Alipurduar.

How to Get Around in Alipurduar

The best way to explore Alipurduar is on foot or by hiring a local taxi.

Where to Go Next from Alipurduar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Darjeeling: Known as the “Queen of Hills”, Darjeeling is a popular destination for its tea gardens, scenic views, and colonial architecture.
  • Kalimpong: Located around 100 kilometers from Alipurduar, Kalimpong is famous for its flower markets and Buddhist monasteries.
  • Siliguri: Located around 140 kilometers from Alipurduar, Siliguri is a bustling town and a gateway to other destinations in North Bengal and Sikkim.

Conclusion

Alipurduar is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and wildlife enthusiasts. With its natural beauty, diverse flora and fauna, and rich cultural heritage, Alipurduar offers a unique travel experience that you won’t forget anytime soon.
