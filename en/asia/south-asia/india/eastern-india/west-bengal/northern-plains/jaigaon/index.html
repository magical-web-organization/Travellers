Jaigaon, located in the Northern Plains of West Bengal, offers rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. This small town, situated on the border of India and Bhutan, serves as the gateway to explore the hidden gems of Eastern India. In this comprehensive guide, we present a detailed travel manual to help you make the most of your trip to Jaigaon.

Introduction to Jaigaon

Jaigaon, also known as Phuentsholing, stands as the primary commercial hub and entry point to Bhutan. The town attracts a large number of tourists due to its unique blend of cultures and breathtaking views. Travelers can experience traditional Bhutanese architecture on one side of the town while exploring the Indian way of life on the other.

Jaigaon is an affordable destination, and you can cover the entire town in a week or less. The best part? You won’t feel like you missed anything, even if you cut your trip short.

List of Places to Visit in Jaigaon

Here’s our list of must-visit places you shouldn’t miss out on during your trip to Jaigaon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bhutan Gate: Located between India and Bhutan, the Bhutan Gate provides a stunning view of the mountains as you cross the border into Bhutan.
  2. Zangtho Pelri Lhakhang: A famous temple in Bhutan, it’s an excellent example of Bhutanese architecture and is located close to the Bhutan Gate.
  3. Karbandi Monastery: A serene place that offers panoramic views of the plains and mountains, Karbandi Monastery is a must-visit place for its peaceful surroundings.
  4. Amo Chuu: A hidden gem in the town, Amo Chuu is a popular picnic spot with local tourists. The waterfall and the pond are perfect for a day out with family and friends.

Natural Attractions of Jaigaon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jaldapara National Park: Located 14 km from Jaigaon, Jaldapara National Park is a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts. It is home to one-horned rhinos, elephants, and Bengal tigers. Visitors can explore the park through safari rides and take full-day tours to explore its beauty.
  2. Chilapata Forest: A dense forest situated in Eastern Dooars region is 25km from Jaigaon. Home to rhinoceros, elephants, leopards, varieties of birds, and other little-known fauna, it’s a must-visit place for nature lovers.
  3. The Phuentsholing Park: A vast public park in the heart of Jaigaon, the Phuentsholing Park is famous for its floral garden representing Bhutan’s sixteen districts. It is also known for the giant statue of Buddha standing tall in the park.
  4. Torsa River Viewpoints: The Torsa river is the lifeline of Jaigaon, separating India and Bhutan, and provides many spots for photography or a quiet evening walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jaigaon

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, Jaigaon has plenty of hiking and biking routes to offer. We have listed some of the most popular routes for your reference:

  1. Gorumara National Park: Trek around the forest trails of Gorumara and spot endangered species of flora and fauna.
  2. Jaldhaka River: Admire the view of the mountains, gushing streams of Jaldhaka River that run parallel to the Bhutan border.
  3. Phuentsholing to Paro: A cycling route that leads from Phuentsholing to Paro, covering 175km of the most scenic landscapes and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Jaigaon: Activities and Tours

Besides the natural and cultural attractions, Jaigaon offers several exciting activities and tours for visitors to experience the town’s authenticity.

  1. Heritage Walks: Take a guided tour of Jaigaon’s old quarters and learn about the town’s history and culture.
  2. Food and Culture Walks: Experience the town’s vibrant food scene by walking through local markets and learning about the dishes and ingredients used in Bhutanese and Indian cuisine.
  3. Bhutanese Traditional Dancing: Attend the folk and traditional dance performances at the Karbandi Monastery, and immerse yourself in Bhutanese culture.
  4. River Rafting in Teesta and Torsa River: Enjoy the thrill of white-water rafting while admiring the beautiful landscapes of Jaigaon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jaigaon

Here’s our suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Jaigaon:

Day 1: Bhutan Gate, Zangtho Pelri Lhakhang, and Karbandi Monastery. Day 2: Jaldapara National Park and Chilapata Forest (Full-day tour). Day 3: Visit Phuentsholing Park and Amo Chuu. Day 4: Go on a Heritage Walk on the Indian Side of the town and visit local markets. Day 5: Trekking to Jaldhaka River (Full-day trek) Day 6: Take a food and culture walk and visit local handicraft markets. Day 7: River Rafting in Teesta and Torsa River

Food and Drink Scene of Jaigaon

Jaigaon has a diverse and vibrant food scene that blends the Indian and Bhutanese way of cooking. Don’t forget to try these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ema Datshi: A Bhutanese speciality made of cheese and chillies served with rice.
  2. Momos: Steamed dumplings stuffed with vegetables, chicken or pork, are a delicious snack or meal.
  3. Bhapa Ilish: A famous Indian Bengali fish preparation, made with steamed hilsa fish in mustard and other spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Butter Tea: A salty tea made with yak milk and butter, popularly consumed in Bhutan.
  2. Chang: A local beer made from barley, Chang is the perfect cocktail for a chill evening.

Where to Stay in Jaigaon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jaigaon is a budget-friendly destination, with many options for accommodation. Here are some of the popular choices for staying in Jaigaon:

  1. Bhutan Residence Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the Bhutan Gate.
  2. Royal Orchid Resort: A mid-range hotel offering comfortable rooms and standard amenities.
  3. Dooars Mountain Hotel and Spa: A luxury property offering a refreshing swimming pool, spa services, and an in-house restaurant.

What to Buy in Jaigaon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The local markets of Jaigaon offer a range of handicrafts and souvenirs. Here’s what you should consider for bringing back home:

  1. Bhutanese textiles: Bhutanese handwoven textiles like kiras, gho, and thans are famous for their quality and intricate designs.
  2. Traditional Handicrafts: Wood-carved articles like Bhutanese bows, arrowheads, and other traditional crafts are a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Jaigaon

Jaigaon follows a unique blend of Indian and Bhutanese cultures. The dress codes are quite different on either side of the town. While in Bhutan, locals dress in the traditional Bhutanese dress, Gho or Kira. On the Indian side, locals follow a more diverse dress code.

Best Time to Visit Jaigaon

The best time to visit Jaigaon is from September to May. The weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities during these months. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, the top tourist season is from October to December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jaigaon

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs of the town.
  2. Carry a valid passport while crossing the border via Bhutan Gate.
  3. Try local delicacies like Ema Datshi and Momo.
  4. Travel by government-authorized transportation services.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places or national parks.
  2. Take photographs of the locals without their permission.
  3. Wear revealing clothes or shorts while in Bhutan.
  4. Disrespect the national symbols or cultural monuments

Safety and Health

Safety in Jaigaon

Jaigaon is relatively safe for tourists. Be cautious while travelling at night and keep your belongings safe. Avoid travelling with large amounts of cash or valuable jewellery.

Health in Jaigaon

Carry your prescription medication while travelling to Jaigaon. The surrounding areas of Jaigaon are malarial zones, so it is advisable to take necessary precautions and get vaccinated before the journey.

Getting to Jaigaon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jaigaon is well-connected to the following transportation options:

Airports Near Jaigaon

  1. Bagdogra Airport, Siliguri (70km from Jaigaon)
  2. Paro International Airport, Paro, Bhutan (down the valley towards Bhutan)

Bus Stations Near Jaigaon

  1. Tenzing Norgay Bus Terminus at Siliguri (about 73 km from Jaigaon)
  2. Private buses usually ply between Siliguri and Jaigaon, covering the distance in 3 hours.

Other Options

  1. Taxis or Cabs: Book pre-paid taxis or private cabs to reach Jaigaon from Bagdogra or Siliguri.
  2. Train: The nearest railway station to Jaigaon is Hasimara Railway Station (around 20km).

How to Get Around in Jaigaon

Jaigaon is a small town, and most places can be covered on foot. Alternatively, you can hop on a local minibus or hire a bike to explore.

Where to Go Next from Jaigaon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time in hand, you can explore these nearby destinations from Jaigaon:

  1. Darjeeling: A charming hill station located in the Indian state of West Bengal, Darjeeling is known for its lush tea gardens and scenic beauty.
  2. Gangtok: The capital city of Sikkim, Gangtok is a popular destination among nature lovers with scenic beauty and adventure sports.
  3. Thimphu: The capital city of Bhutan, Thimphu is a vibrant city filled with cultural and historical monuments.

Conclusion

Jaigaon offers a unique blend of Indian and Bhutanese cultures and breathtaking scenery. It’s an off-the-beaten-track destination that’s easy on your wallet and offers unforgettable experiences. This guide has covered everything you need to know to travel to Jaigaon safely and comfortably.

Have a wonderful trip!
