West Bengal, located in Eastern India, is a state known for its rich history, culture, natural beauty, and diverse cuisine. It is home to iconic landmarks like the Victoria Memorial, the Howrah Bridge, and the Sunderbans. There are many exciting things to do and see in West Bengal, from exploring its many museums and historical sites to hiking and biking through the beautiful countryside.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to West Bengal. Let’s get started!

Introduction to West Bengal

West Bengal is the fourth most populous state in India, with a population of over 90 million. It is bordered by Bangladesh to the east, Nepal to the north, and the Indian states of Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, Sikkim, and Assam. It has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, with influences from Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Christianity.

The capital of West Bengal is Kolkata (formerly known as Calcutta), which is known for its colonial architecture, bustling markets, and delicious street food. Other major cities in West Bengal include Darjeeling, Siliguri, Asansol, Durgapur, and Howrah.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Bengal

West Bengal has many attractions and destinations to explore, so here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Kolkata, explore Victoria Memorial and Park Street
  • Day 2: Visit Belur Math, Dakshineswar Kali Temple, and Howrah Bridge
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Sunderban National Park
  • Day 4: Take a train to Darjeeling and explore the city
  • Day 5: Go on a trek to Sandakphu, or visit the tea gardens
  • Day 6: Visit the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute and Tiger Hill
  • Day 7: Return to Kolkata, visit the Indian Museum or Science City

Local Culture and Customs of West Bengal

West Bengal has a rich and diverse culture, with influences from different religions and traditions. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites
  • Remove shoes before entering temples
  • Greet people with “namaste” or “as-salamu alaykum”
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Best Time to Visit West Bengal

The best time to visit West Bengal is from October to March when the weather is pleasantly cool with fewer chances of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Bengal

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting West Bengal:

Do:

  • Try local cuisine
  • Immerse yourself in the local culture
  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Travel alone at night

Safety and Health

Safety in West Bengal

West Bengal is generally a safe state for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in West Bengal

Diseases like dengue fever, malaria, and hepatitis are common in West Bengal. It is advisable to take necessary preventive measures, such as using insect repellent, drinking bottled water, and avoiding street food.

How to Get Around in West Bengal

The best way to get around West Bengal is by taxi or rental car. Public transportation is also available but may not be as comfortable or convenient. In Kolkata, the metro rail and buses are a good option, while in other cities, taxis and rickshaws are easily available.
