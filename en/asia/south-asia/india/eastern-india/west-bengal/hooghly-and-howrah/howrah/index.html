Introduction to Howrah

Howrah is a city in the state of West Bengal, located on the west bank of the Hooghly River. With a rich history and cultural heritage, Howrah is known for its colonial architecture, religious sites, and stunning natural beauty. The city is a popular destination for tourists seeking a unique travel experience, filled with adventure, culture, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Howrah

If you’re planning a trip to Howrah, make sure to check out these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jubilee Bridge: This iconic steel bridge over the Hooghly River is a symbol of Howrah and a major tourist attraction.
  • Howrah Railway Station: One of the oldest and largest railway stations in India, Howrah railway station is a fine example of colonial architecture.
  • Indian Botanical Gardens: Established in 1787, this is the largest botanical garden in Asia and a great place to explore the local flora and fauna.
  • Belur Math: A serene and tranquil place that is a pilgrimage center for followers of Ramakrishna.

Natural Attractions of Howrah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Santragachi Jheel: This serene lake is an ideal spot for bird watching, picnicking, and relaxing.
  • Vidyasagar Setu: A cable-stayed bridge on the Hooghly River that connects Howrah with Kolkata, offering spectacular views of the city.
  • Shibpur Botanical Garden: Another botanical garden in Howrah that is less popular than the former.
  • Ganges River: Catch a boat tour or take a stroll along the Hoogly Ghat to witness the beauty of the most sacred river in India.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Howrah

For adventure lovers, Howrah has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. Some of the popular ones are:

  • Tupkhana Forest: An expansive forest with nature trails that is perfect for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Botanical Garden-Burrabazar route: A bike route that traverses through the streets of the bustling Burrabazar area to the serene botanical garden of Howrah.

Best Things to Do in Howrah: Activities and Tours

  • Heritage Walks: Take a tour of the city’s colonial architecture and learn about its history and culture.
  • Food Tours: Indulge in the local delicacies of Howrah and experience the city’s culinary scene.
  • River Cruises: Explore the Hooghly River by boat and experience the stunning views of the city skyline and vast nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Howrah

Day 1: Visit the iconic Jubilee Bridge, explore the bustling streets of Howrah, and sample the local cuisine.

Day 2: Take a religious tour of Belur Math, visit the Dakshineswar Kali Temple and enjoy a boat ride in the Ganges River.

Day 3: Hike the Tupkhana Forest trail and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

Day 4: Visit the Indian Botanical Garden, Shibpur Botanical Garden, and relax at the tranquil Santragachhi lake.

Day 5: Explore the colonial architecture of the Howrah Railway station, and take a walk around the Vidyasagar Setu bridge for some spectacular views of the city.

Day 6: Take a heritage walk and learn about the cultural significance of Howrah.

Day 7: Enjoy a river cruise and witness the beauty of the Hooghly River and the majestic views of Kolkata from the top.

Food and Drink Scene of Howrah

The food culture of Howrah is influenced by Bengali cuisine, which is known for its spices and flavors. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cholar Dal: A dish prepared with lentils and coconuts served with luchi.
  • Rosogolla: A dessert made out of chhena soaked in sweet syrup.
  • Fish Fry: One of the most popular seafood dishes that is deep-fried and flavored with spices.

Local Beverages

  • Lassi: A refreshing drink made with yogurt, water, and sugar, served cold during summer.
  • Tea: Masala chai or Darjeeling tea are the most popular options in Howrah.

Where to Stay in Howrah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Howrah has a range of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets. Some of the popular hotels include:

  • The Oberoi Grand: A luxury five-star hotel located in Kolkata.
  • Treebo Tryst Metropolis Kolkata: A budget-friendly hotel in Howrah with modern amenities.
  • Swissotel Kolkata: A five-star hotel with luxurious amenities and modern facilities.

What to Buy in Howrah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antique items: Howrah is known for its rich history and culture, and antique shops offer a range of unique items to take back as souvenirs.
  • Terracotta Products: The terracotta products from Howrah are popular among tourists, and you can find a range of handmade items like statues, vases, and utensils.

Local Culture and Customs of Howrah

Howrah has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions. The people of Howrah are known for their warmth and hospitality, and visitors are expected to reciprocate the same.

Best Time to Visit Howrah

The best time to visit Howrah is during the winter months (December to February). The weather is pleasant, and the humidity is relatively low, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Howrah

Do:

  • Greet locals with a smile and Namaste.
  • Wear appropriate clothing while visiting religious sites and follow the dress code.

Don’t:

  • Trust strangers and be cautious while traveling alone or at night.
  • Avoid using plastic bags and dispose of litter appropriately.

Safety and Health

Safety in Howrah

Howrah is a safe city to visit, but visitors are advised to take precautions and be vigilant, especially at night. It is best to avoid unlit, deserted areas and carry only the necessary items.

Health in Howrah

Visitors should take necessary vaccinations before traveling to Howrah. It is also essential to carry medicines for common ailments like diarrhea, fever, etc.

Getting to Howrah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Howrah

The nearest airport to Howrah is Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Howrah

Howrah Bus Terminus is a major bus station in the city, with frequent services to various parts of the region.

Other Options

The city is well connected by trains, and Howrah Junction is a major railway station in the region.

How to Get Around in Howrah

The most popular modes of transportation within the city are buses, taxis, and rickshaws. It is also possible to rent a car from any of the car rental agencies in the city for a comfortable journey.

Where to Go Next from Howrah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kolkata: The bustling city of Kolkata is only a few kilometers away from Howrah and offers a range of tourist attractions.
  • Mandarmani: A popular seaside town located just a few hours drive from Howrah.

Conclusion

Howrah is a vibrant destination that offers a range of cultural, natural, and historical attractions. With its warm and welcoming locals, delicious cuisine, and stunning vistas, a visit to Howrah is an experience you will never forget.
