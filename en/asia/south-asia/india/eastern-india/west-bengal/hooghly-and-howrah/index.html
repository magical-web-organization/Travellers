Introduction to Hooghly and Howrah

Hooghly and Howrah are two cities located in the Indian state of West Bengal, known for their rich cultural heritage and historical significance. These cities are located on the banks of the Ganges River, offering scenic views and a diverse range of attractions for tourists. Hooghly is known for its numerous temples and historical sites, while Howrah is home to industrial and commercial sectors. Overall, these two cities offer a perfect blend of Hindu mythology, colonial history, and modern development.

List of Places to Visit in Hooghly and Howrah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hooghly Imambara: This grand mosque was built in the early 19th century in the Mughal style. It is known for its intricate architecture, beautiful domes, and stunning decorations.

  • Bandel Church: This church is one of the oldest in India and was built in 1599 by the Portuguese. It is a fine example of colonial architecture and is known for its beautiful interiors.

  • Hangseshwari Temple: This ancient temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Kali and is believed to have been built in the 11th century. It is known for its intricate carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Hooghly and Howrah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rasulpur Jheel: This beautiful lake is located in Howrah and is a popular spot for boating and picnicking. It is surrounded by lush greenery and offers stunning views of the surroundings.

  • Garchumuk: This is a popular picnic spot located near Hooghly, known for its serene atmosphere and beautiful views of the Ganges River.

  • Dhoopchandi Mandir: This beautiful temple is located on the banks of the Ganges River and is a popular spot for watching stunning sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hooghly and Howrah

  • Hoogly Riverfront: This is a popular biking route that runs along the Hoogly River, offering stunning views of the surroundings. It is a great spot for a leisurely ride or a quick jog.

  • Botanical Garden Road: This is a great route for hiking or biking, which runs through the beautiful Botanical Garden in Howrah. It is known for its beautiful greenery and scenic beauty.

Best Things to Do in Hooghly and Howrah: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Heritage Walk: Take a walk through the serene streets of Hooghly and explore the historical landmarks of the city.

  • Visit the Museums: There are several museums in Hooghly and Howrah, which offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Sample some of the delicious local dishes, which are a mix of Bengali and Mughlai cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hooghly and Howrah

Day 1: Arrive in Hooghly and explore the Hooghly Imambara and the nearby temples.

Day 2: Take a walk around the city and explore the Bandel Church and other historical landmarks.

Day 3: Visit the nearby picnic spots such as Garchumuk and Rasulpur Jheel.

Day 4: Go on a biking/hiking tour of the Botanical Garden in Howrah.

Day 5: Explore the museums and try the local cuisine in Hooghly and Howrah.

Day 6: Visit the natural attractions such as Dhoopchandi Mandir and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 7: Departure from Hooghly and Howrah.

Food and Drink Scene of Hooghly and Howrah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Macher Jhol: This is a spicy fish curry which is a popular dish in the region.

  • Kosha Mangsho: This is a slow-cooked mutton curry, which is a favorite among locals.

  • Mishti Doi: This is a sweet yogurt-like dessert which is a must-try for those with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Chaa: This is a popular beverage which is a sweetened and flavored tea which is served with milk.

  • Aam Pora Shorbot: This is a refreshing drink made from roasted mangoes and jaggery.

Where to Stay in Hooghly and Howrah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several budget-friendly options available for accommodation in Hooghly and Howrah, such as guesthouses and budget hotels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Hotel Hindusthan International: This is a luxury hotel located in Howrah, which offers world-class amenities and services.

  • Hotel Shivam: This is a budget hotel located in Hooghly, offering comfortable rooms and basic amenities at an affordable price.

  • Hooghly Tourist Lodge: This is a government-run guesthouse located in Hooghly, which is a budget-friendly option for backpackers.

What to Buy in Hooghly and Howrah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Terracotta Crafts: These beautiful crafts are made from clay and are a specialty of Hooghly and Howrah.

  • Sarees: The region is known for its beautiful silk sarees, which are a must-buy for any visitor.

  • Bengali Sweetmeats: These delicious sweets are a must-try and make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hooghly and Howrah

The local culture of Hooghly and Howrah is a mix of Hindu and Muslim traditions, which is evident in their unique customs and practices. The people of these cities are warm and hospitable, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs.

Best Time to Visit Hooghly and Howrah

The best time to visit Hooghly and Howrah is between October and February, when the weather is pleasant and there are several local festivals and events that take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hooghly and Howrah

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.

  • Keep the city clean and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect local customs and practices.

  • Do not photograph people or places without their permission.

  • Do not engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hooghly and Howrah

Overall, Hooghly and Howrah are safe cities to visit. However, visitors are advised to be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night. It is recommended to use reputable cab services and avoid traveling with strangers.

Health in Hooghly and Howrah

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid consuming street food to prevent food-borne illnesses. It is recommended to carry necessary medications and consult a doctor before traveling to Hooghly and Howrah.

Getting to Hooghly and Howrah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hooghly and Howrah

The nearest airport to Hooghly and Howrah is the Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, which is located approximately 40 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Hooghly and Howrah

The cities are well-connected by bus services, with several private and state-run buses plying to and from the cities.

Other Options

There are several train stations located in and around Hooghly and Howrah, making it easy to access these cities by train.

How to Get Around in Hooghly and Howrah

The best way to get around in Hooghly and Howrah is by local transport, such as buses and cabs. Visitors can also take a leisurely walk or hire a bicycle to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Hooghly and Howrah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kolkata: The city of Kolkata is located approximately 40 kilometers from Hooghly and Howrah and is known for its rich cultural heritage, food, and shopping.

  • Digha: This is a popular coastal town located approximately 177 kilometers from Hooghly and Howrah, offering beaches, water sports, and fun-filled activities for visitors.

Conclusion

Hooghly and Howrah are two cities that offer a unique cultural experience and are a must-visit for those traveling to West Bengal. From historical monuments to natural attractions, these cities have something for everyone. Visitors can explore the local cuisine, interact with the friendly locals, and take in the beauty of the surroundings. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their itinerary and make the most of their trip to Hooghly and Howrah.
