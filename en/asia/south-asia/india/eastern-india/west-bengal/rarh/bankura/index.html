Introduction to Bankura

Bankura, located in the Rarh region of West Bengal, India, is a culturally-rich and naturally-beautiful destination. Known for its terracotta temples, lush green forests, and scenic landscapes, this sleepy town has something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Bankura is sure to impress you with its charm and beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Bankura

If you’re planning to visit Bankura, here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bishnupur: Home to several terracotta temples, Bishnupur is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a must-visit for history and art lovers.
  • Mukutmanipur: A beautiful man-made lake and Dam, Resembling this place to Great canyon of USA is surrounded with hills and lush green forests.
  • Susunia Hills: This ancient hill, located about 50 kilometers from Bankura town, is home to several rock-cut caves, temples, and inscriptions of regional importance. A major attraction for trekkers and rock climbers, the landscape here is breathtaking.
  • Jhilimili: Located in the Ayodhya hills, Jhilimili offers a panoramic view of the surrounding forest and nearby hills. Go trekking, or camp under the stars, this place is excellent for adventure lovers.

Natural Attractions of Bankura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Joyrambati and Kamarpukur: Joyrambati and Kamarpukur are two nearby villages, known for being the birthplaces of Maa Sarada and Sri Ramakrishna, respectively. Both these villages have beautiful temples and ashrams, and are popular spiritual destinations.
  • Biharinath Hill: Biharinath Hill, located at an altitude of 451 meters, offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. A trek to the top of the hill is a must-do activity for adventure junkies and nature lovers.
  • Palashbari Eco resort: With more than 730 mango trees and beautiful lake, the eco-resort is the perfect weekend getaway.
  • Garh Panchkot: Garh Panchkot, located about 40 kilometers from Bankura town, is an abandoned fort from the Panchakot kingdom. Explore the ruins, or just take a leisurely walk through the surrounding forests, this place is sure to captivate your imagination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bankura

Bankura has several hiking and biking trails, which can be explored on foot or on a cycle. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Bishnupur-Gokulnagar-Chilarai: This 25-kilometer-long trail takes you through the countryside, and lets you explore the terracotta temples and the local rural life.
  • Susunia Hills Trek: If you’re an adventure seeker, this trek to the top of Susunia Hills is a must-do activity. The trail is about 4 kilometers long and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Bankura: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours you can take in Bankura. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Terracotta Art Tour: Take a tour of the famous terracotta temples in Bishnupur, and learn about the art and craft of terracotta.
  • Jungle Safari: Explore the forests and wildlife of Bankura with a thrilling jungle safari at Joypur Forest.
  • Visit the local Haats (Flea Market): Visit the local markets in Bankura town, and shop for authentic terracotta artifacts, handloom textiles, and local handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bankura

Day 1 - Arrival in Bankura, and visit Biharinath Hill Day 2 - Explore the terracotta temples and art of Bishnupur Day 3 - Take a jungle safari at Joypur Forest Day 4 - Trek to the top of Susunia Hills Day 5 - Visit Garh Panchkot and Jhilimili Day 6 - Explore the spiritual side of Bankura, visit Joyrambati and Kamarpukur Day 7 - Departure from Bankura

Food and Drink Scene of Bankura

Bankura has a rich culinary tradition, and you can taste some of the most authentic Bengali dishes here. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Muri Ghonto: A traditional Bengali fish curry cooked with rice and spices.
  • Postor Bora: A popular snack made with poppy seeds, onions, and spices.
  • Paturi: A unique Bengali dish made with fish wrapped in banana leaves and cooked with mustard paste.

Local Beverages

  • Taal Pitha: A refreshing local drink made with the sap of the palm tree.
  • Ghonto: A popular alcoholic drink, made with fermented rice and served in a bamboo tube.

Where to Stay in Bankura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bankura has several options for travelers, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Palashbari Eco Resort: Located in the heart of Bankura, this eco-resort offers luxurious accommodations and excellent service.
  • The Biharinath Tourist Lodge: A budget-friendly option, located at the foot of Biharinath Hill.
  • Hotel Saptarshi: Located in Bishnupur, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Bankura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bankura is famous for its terracotta artifacts and handicrafts. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Terracotta Artifacts: Pick up some beautiful terracotta figurines, pottery, and other artifacts from Bishnupur.
  • Handloom Textiles: Bankura has a thriving handloom industry, and you can buy some of the best handcrafted sarees and fabrics here.

Local Culture and Customs of Bankura

Bankura has a rich cultural heritage, and its people are friendly and welcoming. The locals speak Bengali, and you can observe various religious and cultural festivities throughout the year. Respect local customs and traditions, dress modestly, and be polite while interacting with the local community.

Best Time to Visit Bankura

The best time to visit Bankura is between October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bankura

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty and scenic landscapes of Bankura.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the environment.
  • Don’t disrespect the religious or cultural beliefs of the local people.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bankura

Bankura is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always advisable to take precautions while travelling. Avoid travelling alone at night, keep your valuables safe, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bankura

Bankura has several hospitals and medical facilities, but it’s always advisable to carry a first-aid kit with you. Drink bottled water, avoid street food, and carry any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Bankura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bankura

The nearest airport to Bankura is the Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, which is about 245 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bankura

Bankura has a well-connected bus station, and you can avail of buses from Kolkata or other nearby cities to reach Bankura.

Other Options

You can also reach Bankura by train, as it has a well-connected railway station.

How to Get Around in Bankura

The best way to get around Bankura is by hiring a car or a bike. You can also use public transport options like buses and shared taxis.

Where to Go Next from Bankura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can explore:

  • Kolkata: The capital of West Bengal, Kolkata is about 150 kilometers from Bankura, and is a vibrant city, known for its colonial architecture, street food, and cultural heritage.
  • Bakkhali: A beautiful beach town, located about 200 kilometers from Bankura, Bakkhali is perfect for a seaside getaway.
  • Shantiniketan: The home of Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore, Shantiniketan is a cultural hub, located about 60 kilometers from Bankura.

Conclusion

Bankura is a hidden gem, located in the heart of West Bengal. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality, it’s the perfect place for a peaceful getaway. Explore the terracotta temples, trek through the forests, and soak in the local culture, Bankura is sure to charm and captivate you with its beauty and simplicity.
