Are you looking for an offbeat destination that offers a perfect blend of nature, history, and adventure? Look no further, as Purulia, a hidden paradise in the Rarh region of West Bengal, Eastern India, is the answer to your quest. With numerous natural and cultural attractions, Purulia has emerged as one of the trending tourist destinations in recent years.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we have curated a list of top places to visit, food and drink scenes, local culture and customs, safety tips, and more to help you plan your trip to Purulia.

Introduction to Purulia

Purulia, located in the southwestern part of West Bengal, is a scenic district known for its diverse landscapes, including hills, forests, waterfalls, and plateaus. The history of Purulia dates back to the early medieval period, and it was ruled by different dynasties like the Mauryas, Guptas, and Pals. The rich cultural heritage of the region is reflected in its art, architecture, and folklore.

List of Places to Visit in Purulia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Purulia Museum: Located in the heart of the city, Purulia Museum is a must-visit place for history buffs. The museum houses a rich collection of artifacts from the prehistoric, medieval, and modern periods.

  • Baranti: Baranti, a serene village located on the foothills of the Ayodhya range, is famous for its scenic beauty and historical significance. The ruins of the old fort and temple of the Chauhan dynasty add to the charm of the place.

  • Banda Deul Temple: Banda Deul, an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its exquisite terracotta sculptures and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Purulia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Joychandi Pahar: Joychandi Pahar, a picturesque and rocky hill station located on the Purulia-Ranchi road, offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The place is also popular for trekking, rock climbing, and adventure sports.

  • Ayodhya Hills: Ayodhya Hills, situated on the Orissa-Jharkhand-West Bengal tri-junction, is a lush green forest area popular for trekking, camping, and birdwatching.

  • Puncha Dam: Puncha Dam, a scenic spot located on the Kumari River, offers breathtaking views of the dam, hills, and valleys. The place is perfect for picnics, boating, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Purulia

Purulia is a paradise for trekkers, bikers, and adventurists. The region has numerous scenic trekking routes, including the popular routes of Joychandi Pahar, Ayodhya Hills, and Subarnarekha valley. The place is also perfect for exploring on a mountain bike, with routes like Baghmundi-Belilious-Jhalda-Silda and Baranti-Biharinath-Burnpur being the most popular ones.

Best Things to Do in Purulia: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and adventure sports, Purulia has a lot to offer for a fun-filled vacation. Here are some of the best things to do in Purulia:

  • Attend the Chhau Dance Festival: Chhau Dance, a traditional folk dance of Purulia, is a must-watch cultural experience. The festival is held annually in March-April and is a feast for the eyes and ears.

  • Explore the Charida Village: Charida Village, located near the Ayodhya Hills, is known for its unique tradition of making masks. The village is also famous for its terracotta artwork and rural lifestyle.

  • Visit the Bamni Falls and Turga Falls: Bamni Falls and Turga Falls, two stunning waterfalls in Purulia, are perfect for a refreshing dip and scenic walks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Purulia

To make the most of your trip to Purulia, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Purulia city tour, visiting Purulia Museum, and Banda Deul.

Day 2: Ayodhya Hills trekking and campsite stay.

Day 3: Joychandi Pahar adventure sports and trekking.

Day 4: Baghmundi-Belilious-Jhalda-Silda mountain biking.

Day 5: Visit the Charida village for the mask-making tradition and terracotta artwork.

Day 6: Bamni and Turga waterfalls and picnic.

Day 7: Departure for Kolkata or nearby places.

Food and Drink Scene of Purulia

If you are a food lover, Purulia has some unique dishes and local beverages to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Babath aka Goat Intestine Curry: Babath is a spicy curry made from goat’s intestine and is a popular dish in Purulia.

  • Pithas: Pithas, sweet or savory rice cakes, are a staple in the Purulia cuisine and are often served during festivals.

  • Mati Mahasha: Mati Mahasha, a special delicacy of Purulia, is a meal made entirely on earthenware and cooked on open flames.

Local Beverages

  • Handia: Handia, a fermented rice beer, is a popular local beverage in Purulia that is often enjoyed during festivals and cultural events.

  • Mahua: Mahua, a traditional alcoholic beverage made from the flowers of the Mahua tree, is a popular drink in the region.

Where to Stay in Purulia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Purulia offers a variety of options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Purulia:

  • Ranchi Road: Located near Joychandi Pahar, Ranchi Road offers some of the best hotels and resorts in Purulia.

  • Charida: Charida, famous for its mask-making tradition, has some affordable guesthouses and shelters for tourists.

  • Ayodhya Hills: Ayodhya Hills offers campsites, trekker’s huts, and resorts for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Purulia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take back some memories of Purulia, here are some souvenirs and local products that you must buy:

  • Terracotta Objects: Purulia is famous for its terracotta craftsmanship, and you can find a variety of exposed clay pottery, embroiderio shoes, etc. that would be the best memorabilia for your trip.

  • Masks: Charida is famous for its mask-making tradition, and you can find a variety of colorful masks made by the local artisans during festivals.

  • Handicrafts and art objects: Purulia is famous for its art and culture, and you can find a variety of handicrafts and art objects made by the local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Purulia

Purulia is a district with a rich cultural heritage. If you want to experience the local culture and customs of Purulia, we recommend the following activities:

  • Participate in Chhau Dance: Chhau Dance, a traditional dance form of Purulia, is an excellent way to experience the local culture and customs.

  • Visit Tribal Villages: Purulia has some tribal villages like Baghmundi, Kharagpur, and Jhalda that offer a glimpse into the unique lifestyles and customs of the region.

Best Time to Visit Purulia

The best time to visit Purulia is from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant, and the hills and valleys are lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Purulia

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Purulia:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and buy locally made souvenirs.

  • Carry necessary medicines and first-aid kit while trekking or camping.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.

  • Disrespect the local traditions and customs.

  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting temples or cultural events.

Safety and Health

While Purulia is a relatively safe destination, you should follow standard safety precautions while exploring the region. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Purulia

  • Carry a map and a local contact number while trekking or traveling to remote areas.

  • Do not venture into prohibited or restricted areas.

  • Keep a watchful eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Purulia

  • Carry mosquito repellent and use it to avoid mosquito bites.

  • Drink only filtered and boiled water.

  • Carry necessary medicines and first-aid kit while traveling.

Getting to Purulia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Purulia is well-connected to major cities like Kolkata, Ranchi, and Jamshedpur by road, rail, and air. Here are some transportation options to reach Purulia:

Airports Near Purulia

  • Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport, Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Purulia

  • Purulia Bus Stand, Purulia.

Other Options

  • You can also take a train to Purulia from major cities like Kolkata, Ranchi, and Jamshedpur.

How to Get Around in Purulia

The best way to explore Purulia is to hire a car or a bike. You can also take state-run buses or shared taxis to reach nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Purulia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can undertake from Purulia:

  • Asansol: Asansol, located about 120 km from Purulia, is a bustling city that offers a mix of history, culture, and modern amenities.

  • Jhargram: Jhargram, located about 70 km from Purulia, is a small town known for its natural beauty, tribal culture, and archaeological sites.

Conclusion

Purulia, with its diverse landscapes, rich history, and unique culture, is one of the hidden gems of Eastern India. We hope our comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip to Purulia and create unforgettable memories.
