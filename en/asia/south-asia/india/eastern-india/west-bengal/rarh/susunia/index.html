Introduction to Susunia

Located in Rarh, West Bengal, Susunia draws visitors with its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance. The town is known for its scenic views, adventurous rock climbing opportunities, and ancient rock carvings. In this guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Susunia, including top attractions, hiking routes, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Susunia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Susunia Hill: A prominent hill in the town which is famous for its ancient rock carvings and is believed to have been a center of civilization in ancient times.
  • Susunia Pahar Temple: A temple situated at the top of Susunia Hill, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Susunia Gram Panchayet Museum: This museum, located 6 km from Susunia, showcases the traditional art, culture, and agriculture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Susunia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Susunia Hill: The peak offers some of the most spectacular views of the surrounding landscape, and visitors can also venture into the nearby caves and waterfalls for an exciting adventure.
  • Rock Climbing: Susunia has some of the best rock climbing opportunities in the country, with challenging climbs that offer stunning views of the town and its surroundings.
  • Trekking: There are several trekking routes in and around Susunia, giving visitors the opportunity to explore the lush green forests and the rural landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Susunia

  • Susunia Hill Trekking Route: A popular trekking route that takes you to the top of the hill, through rocky terrain and stunning forests.
  • Kunjavan Trekking Route: A trekking route that leads to a waterfall and offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bhalki Machan Trekking Route: A trekking route that takes you to the top of a hill that offers panoramic views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Susunia: Activities and Tours

  • Rock Climbing Adventure: There are several rock climbing spots in Susunia, offering some of the most challenging climbs in the country.
  • Trekking Trail Adventure: There are several trekking trails in Susunia that take you through dense forests, over rocky terrain, and to peaceful waterfalls.
  • Cave Exploration: Susunia is home to several caves that are perfect for exploration, including the Pandeshwara cave and the Narasimha cave.

Food and Drink Scene of Susunia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Macher Kalia: A spicy fish curry that is a popular dish in West Bengal.
  • Luchi: A deep-fried puffed bread that is typically served with potato curry.
  • Rasmalai: A dessert made from cheese that is soaked in sweet, milky syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Cha: A tea made with milk and spices that is a staple in Indian cuisine.
  • Lassi: A yogurt-based drink that is popular throughout India.

Where to Stay in Susunia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Susunia Tourist Lodge: This lodge offers comfortable accommodations and is located close to many of the town’s top attractions.
  • Kolkata Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Hotel Susunia International: This hotel offers luxurious accommodations with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Susunia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Susunia is known for its handmade baskets, mats, and pottery, which make for great souvenirs.
  • Tea: Susunia is located near several tea plantations, making it an excellent place to buy local tea.
  • Spices: West Bengal is known for its flavorful spices, which make great gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Susunia

The people of Susunia are welcoming and friendly. The town has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should show respect for local traditions and customs. It is customary to remove shoes before entering a temple or a holy place.

Best Time to Visit Susunia

The best time to visit Susunia is during the winter months, between November and February, when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Susunia

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the caves.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside temples.
  • Litter or pollute the natural landscape.
  • Take photographs of the ancient rock carvings or interfere with them in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Susunia

Susunia is a safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping valuables close at hand.

Health in Susunia

Visitors should drink only bottled or boiled water, avoid eating street food, and carry a basic first-aid kit with them.

Getting to Susunia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Susunia

The nearest airport to Susunia is Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, which is about 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near Susunia

The nearest bus station is Bankura bus stand, which is 6 km away from Susunia.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Bankura railway station, which is about 20 km away from Susunia.

How to Get Around in Susunia

The best way to get around Susunia is by foot, but visitors can also hire a taxi or a private car.

Where to Go Next from Susunia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bankura: A historical town that is located about 20 km from Susunia and is known for its temples, palaces, and art.
  • Purulia: A town that is located about 70 km from Susunia and is known for its scenic beauty and traditional art and culture.
  • Bishnupur: A town that is located about 55 km from Susunia and is known for its terracotta temples and traditional handicrafts.

Conclusion

Susunia is a hidden gem in India that is worth exploring for its scenic beauty, adventurous opportunities, and rich cultural heritage. With this guide, you have all the information you need to plan your visit and have a memorable experience in Susunia.
