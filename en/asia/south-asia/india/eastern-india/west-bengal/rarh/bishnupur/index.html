Introduction to Bishnupur

Bishnupur, located in the Bankura district of West Bengal, is known as the cultural capital of Bengal due to its rich history, architecture, and art. Formerly the capital of the Malla kingdom, Bishnupur is famous for its terracotta temples, silk sarees, and pottery. The town is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Day 1 Arrive in Bishnupur and check in to your accommodation. Spend the day exploring the town and its temples.

Day 7 Depart Bishnupur for your next destination.

Best Time to Visit Bishnupur

September to February is the best time to visit Bishnupur as the weather is pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bishnupur

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering a temple or a house.
  • Explore the local markets for traditional crafts and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Do not disturb the locals by littering or playing loud music.
  • Do not wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bishnupur

Bishnupur is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is advisable to take precautions while traveling alone at night.

Health in Bishnupur

It is advisable to carry a first aid kit and necessary medication while traveling in Bishnupur. Consult your doctor before traveling and take precautions against mosquito bites.

How to Get Around in Bishnupur

The best way to get around Bishnupur is on foot, as most of the places are within walking distance. You can also rent a bicycle or hire a taxi to get around.
