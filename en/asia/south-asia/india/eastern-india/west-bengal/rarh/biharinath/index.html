Introduction to Biharinath

Nestled in the Rarh region of West Bengal, Biharinath is a small hill station located on the foothills of the Chhotanagpur Plateau. The gorgeous landscape of this quaint town is sure to enrapture any nature enthusiast. From sprawling green hills to lush dense forests, Biharinath is the ideal destination for people looking to escape the hubbub of city life and seek solace in the lap of nature.

List of Places to Visit in Biharinath

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Biharinath Hill Station

The Biharinath Hill Station is undoubtedly the most popular tourist attraction in Biharinath. It’s a beautiful landscape that offers a panoramic view of the surrounding hills and valleys. The hill station also has a rich historical background, dating back to the time of the British Raj. The place still retains the old-world charm of that era, and visitors can get a glimpse of the colonial architecture that once graced this region.

Biharinath Temple

The Biharinath Temple is a significant religious destination for locals and visitors alike. It is located at the top of the hill station and is considered one of the holiest places in the region.

Natural Attractions of Biharinath: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Biharinath Reserve Forest

The Biharinath Reserve Forest is a protected forest belt, stretching around the Biharinath Hill Station. It is home to a variety of animals, including tigers and leopards, making it an ideal location for wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can take a walk or a jeep safari through the forest to explore the beautiful flora and fauna of the region.

Ghutghutia Hills

The Ghutghutia Hills are a series of small hills located in the heart of Biharinath. This beautiful tract of land offers some of the best trekking routes in the region. Visitors can indulge in various adventure activities like rock climbing, trekking, and camping at this beautiful location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biharinath

Biharinath offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in West Bengal. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Biharinath Hill to Taldanga
  • Biharinath Hill to Ajodhya Hills
  • Biharinath Hill to Jhilimili

These routes are ideal for adventure enthusiasts looking to explore the region’s natural beauty while also getting their dose of adrenaline rush.

Best Things to Do in Biharinath: Activities and Tours

From exploring the charming village life to indulging in adventure sports, Biharinath has a lot to offer to visitors. Some of the best things to do in and around Biharinath are:

  • Take a stroll around Biharinath village and soak in the local culture and traditions.
  • Explore the Biharinath Reserve Forest and indulge in wildlife photography.
  • Enjoy a camping night in the wilderness of Ghutghutia Hills.
  • Trek or cycle through the rocky trails of the region to explore the scenic beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biharinath

Day 1: Arrive in Biharinath, check-in at hotel or guesthouse. Day 2: Explore the Biharinath Hill Station and visit the Biharinath Temple. Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Biharinath Reserve Forest. Day 4: Hike or bike through the Ghutghutia Hills. Day 5: Visit nearby villages and learn about the rich cultural history of the region. Day 6: Take a day trip to Ajodhya Hills and enjoy the scenic views. Day 7: Depart from Biharinath.

Food and Drink Scene of Biharinath

Must-Try Dishes

Biharinath is known for its delicious local cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Litti Chokha: A popular street food made of gram flour and sattu, served with mashed potato, brinjal and tomato chokha, and chutney.
  • Bamboo Shoot Fry: A side dish made of tender bamboo shoots, cooked with mustard paste, and served with steamed rice.
  • Momo: An Indian version of Chinese dumplings, made of minced meat or vegetables, steamed or fried, and served with spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

Biharinath is famous for its refreshing beverages, including thandai, sugarcane juice, and coconut water. The region also produces some of the best tea in India, so don’t forget to taste the local tea while you are here.

Where to Stay in Biharinath: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Biharinath offers comfortable accommodation options for tourists. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Biharinath Hill Resort: Located on the hill station, this resort offers comfortable accommodation with breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.
  • Biharinath Ecotourism Centre: A perfect place for adventure enthusiasts, this center offers camping and trekking facilities along with comfortable guesthouses.
  • The Wooden Cafe: Located in Biharinath village, The Wooden Cafe offers comfortable rooms with an in-house cafe serving delicious food.

What to Buy in Biharinath: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Biharinath’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty offer plenty of shopping opportunities for visitors. Some of the must-buy items from Biharinath are:

  • Bamboo Crafts: The region is famous for its bamboo crafts, including baskets, lamps, and other decorative items.
  • Tant sarees and Dhakai sarees: These sarees are made of locally grown cotton, woven by local artisans, and are known for their exquisite designs and comfort.
  • Tea: Biharinath produces some of the best tea in India, and it’s a must-try and buy for tea lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Biharinath

Biharinath has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are expected to respect and adhere to the local customs and traditions. Some of the cultural practices in Biharinath include:

  • Addressing elders and locals with respect, using proper salutations.
  • Removing footwear before entering religious places, homes, and some restaurants.
  • Dressing modestly when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Biharinath

The best time to visit Biharinath is between October to February when the weather is pleasant, and the monsoons have receded. The temperature may drop below 10°C at night, so pack warm clothes accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biharinath

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and shop for locally made crafts.
  • Carry cash as the region has limited ATMs and card payment facilities.
  • Get permits before trekking or camping in the forest.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the region.
  • Disrupt the wildlife in the Biharinath Reserve Forest.
  • Disrespect locals or religious places.
  • Travel alone at night in remote areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biharinath

Biharinath is a relatively safe town for tourists, but visitors should exercise caution and take necessary safety precautions. Some safety tips to keep in mind are:

  • Avoid travelling alone at night and in remote areas.
  • Keep your valuables safe, and do not leave them unattended.
  • Follow safety guidelines while indulging in adventure activities, and always wear safety equipment.

Health in Biharinath

Visitors to Biharinath should carry a basic medical kit, including prescribed medicines, insect repellents, and sunscreen. Visitors should also stay hydrated and avoid eating street food to prevent health problems.

Getting to Biharinath: Transportation Options and Logistics

Biharinath is well-connected to nearby cities and towns by roads, rail, and air.

Airports Near Biharinath

The nearest airport to Biharinath is the Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, which is around 178 km away.

Bus Stations Near Biharinath

Biharinath can be easily accessed by buses from nearby towns like Asansol, Durgapur, and Kolkata.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or a car from nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Biharinath

Biharinath is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. However, hiring a taxi or a private car is recommended for visiting nearby places.

Where to Go Next from Biharinath: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby attractions like:

  • Ajodhya Hills: A scenic hill station located around 80 km from Biharinath.
  • Baranti: A beautiful village located around 25 km from Biharinath, known for its serene landscape and natural beauty.
  • Maithon: A beautiful dam and a popular picnic spot located around 35 km from Biharinath.

Conclusion

Biharinath is a hidden gem in West Bengal, offering a perfect blend of nature, adventure, and culture. The town’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty make it an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore a new place off the beaten track. From stunning landscapes to rich historical sites and delicious local cuisine, Biharinath has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and embark on an unforgettable adventure in Biharinath.
