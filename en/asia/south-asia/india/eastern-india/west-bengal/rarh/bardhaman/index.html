Introduction to Bardhaman

Bardhaman, also known as Burdwan, is a historic city located in the Rarh region of West Bengal in Eastern India. This city has a rich cultural heritage and a plethora of attractions that make it a great choice for travelers.

Bardhaman is easily accessible by road, rail, and air, making it a popular destination for travelers from across India and abroad. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s ancient temples and museums, or going on an outdoor adventure, Bardhaman has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bardhaman

Bardhaman is home to a number of culturally important attractions, as well as natural wonders. Below we have listed some of the top places to visit when in Bardhaman:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baroduari: This grand palace built by the Raja of Burdwan in the 19th century is a fine example of European Gothic style architecture. The palace now serves as the campus of the University of Burdwan.
  • 108 Shiva Temples: Visit the complex of 108 Shiva temples, which is a prominent site for Shaivites.
  • Golapbag: This exquisite palace built in the 19th century is a must-visit for art lovers, as it houses an impressive collection of artifacts, including paintings, manuscripts and sculptures.
  • Bardhaman Rajbati: This magnificent palace built in the Italian style is now a museum that houses a rich collection of weapons, arms, and ammunitions.
  • Curzon Gate: This grand structure was built in the early 20th century to commemorate the visit of Lord Curzon, the then Viceroy of India. The gate is a fine example of the British colonial architecture.

Natural Attractions of Bardhaman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Riverside Park: Located on the banks of the Damodar River, this park is perfect for a peaceful picnic with stunning views of the river.
  • Joydev Kenduli: This is a small village located on the banks of Ajay River, around 40 km from Bardhaman. It is a place of pilgrimage and a hub of Baul music and culture.
  • Ramana Reservoir: This is an ideal location for a romantic getaway or to relax in nature’s lap surrounded by scenic beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bardhaman

Bardhaman has many scenic cycling and hiking routes that offer an unforgettable experience for adventure lovers. Some of the best routes include:

  • Kankaleshwari Circuit: This circuit starts from Kankalitala temple, and takes you through the nearby villages, forests and a canal road that offers incredible views of the countryside.
  • Nimtouri Circuit: This circular route starts and ends at Bardhaman railway station, and takes you through the towns and villages of Nimtouri, Kamnara and Dahapara.
  • Shaktigarh Circuit: This is a hill circuit that takes you to Shaktigarh, which is around 25 km from Bardhaman. The route passes through some beautiful villages, and is ideal for those who are looking for a challenging ride.

Best Things to Do in Bardhaman: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other activities to keep you entertained in Bardhaman. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Explore the Museums and Palaces: Visit the Palace of the Raja of Burdwan, Golapbag, and the Museum of Burdwan to learn about the history and culture of the region.
  • Attend a Baul Music Festival: Experience the magical Baul music festivals, which are held from November to February each year.
  • Shopping at Khadi Gramodyog: Visit the Khadi Gramodyog, which is a government-run regional handicrafts center selling beautiful souvenirs and handicrafts.
  • Take a River Cruise: Take a cruise down the Damodar River, which offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Bardhaman

Bardhaman boasts a rich food and drink scene, with something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bardhaman Special Mutton Curry: This dish is a spicy and flavorful dish made with tender mutton, tomatoes and onions, and goes perfectly with steamed rice.
  • Sondesh: This popular Bengali sweet is made from fresh chenna, sugar, and cardamom, and is a must-try when in Bardhaman.

Local Beverages

  • Kadak Chaa: The strong tea is the perfect pick-me-up after a long day of sightseeing.
  • Lassi: This sweet and refreshing yogurt drink is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Bardhaman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bardhaman has a number of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some of the popular hotels include:

  • The Peerless Sarovar Portico: This hotel is located near the center of Bardhaman, and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • The Fern Residency: This hotel is known for its luxurious spa, and is located in the heart of the city.
  • Hotel Sankhajit Palace: This hotel is located near the Nimtouri circuit, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Bardhaman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home from Bardhaman, here are some great options:

  • Terracotta Items: Bardhaman is known for its terracotta handicrafts, which include beautiful pots, vases and other items.
  • Batik Saris: These beautiful hand-dyed saris are a unique souvenir to take home from Bardhaman.

Local Culture and Customs of Bardhaman

Bardhaman is a unique blend of Bengali and Rajput cultures. Traditional customs are still followed here, so visitors should respect the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bardhaman

The best time to visit Bardhaman is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bardhaman

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the delicious local cuisine.
  • Visit the landmarks and museums.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious places.
  • Disrespect the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bardhaman

Bardhaman is generally safe for travelers, However, like any other city visitors should take precautions to avoid any petty crimes.

Health in Bardhaman

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, and may also need to take additional vaccinations for diseases like Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and Japanese encephalitis.

Getting to Bardhaman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bardhaman

The nearest airport to Bardhaman is the Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, which is around 110 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bardhaman

The Bardhaman bus stand is located in the center of the city, and offers both long and short distance services.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Bardhaman by train, with the Bardhaman Junction railway station being the most popular option.

How to Get Around in Bardhaman

Visitors can use auto-rickshaws or taxis to get around the city, while bicycles and motorcycles are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Bardhaman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations that make for great excursions from Bardhaman include Shantiniketan, Kolkata, and Asansol.

Conclusion

Bardhaman offers a great mix of culture, adventure, and relaxation, making it an ideal destination for both solo travelers and families. Plan your perfect Bardhaman getaway with our guide, and experience all that this charming city has to offer!
