A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Lava

Located in the Darjeeling Hills of West Bengal, Lava is a small hamlet that is slowly gaining popularity for its scenic beauty and peaceful environment. Surrounded by lush forests and hills, Lava offers a serenity that is hard to find in bustling cities. In this travel guide, we will take you through everything that you need to know about this hidden gem in Eastern India.

List of Places to Visit in Lava

Whether you are a nature lover or a history enthusiast, Lava has something for everyone. Here are the top places that you must visit in Lava:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lava Jamgyong Kongtrul Monastery: Founded in 1979, the monastery is one of the most important cultural attractions of Lava. The monastery is known for its beautiful architecture and extensive collection of religious artefacts.

  2. Neora Valley Interpretation Centre: Located near the entrance of the Neora Valley National Park, the centre houses a range of exhibits that depict the cultural, ecological, and historical aspects of the park.

Natural Attractions of Lava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Neora Valley National Park: Spread across 88 square kilometres, the park is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including the rare Red Panda. You can take a trek inside the park to witness its natural beauty and wildlife.

  2. Rishop: A small village located close to Lava, Rishop offers some of the most stunning views of the Himalayas. You can take a short trek to the Tiffin Dara Viewpoint to witness the sunrise over the mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lava

Lava is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Neora Valley National Park has several trekking trails that offer scenic views of the Himalayas and the forests. Some of the popular routes include:

  1. Lava to Kolakham Trek: A 3 km trek that takes you through the beautiful forests of the Neora Valley.

  2. Lava to Samthar Trek: A 4 km trek that takes you through the forests of the Samthar Plateau.

Best Things to Do in Lava: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and trekking, Lava has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things that you can do in Lava:

  1. Bird Watching: Lava is a paradise for bird watchers. You can spot several species of birds in the Neora Valley National Park.

  2. Stargazing: The clear night sky of Lava makes it an ideal place for stargazing. You can take a night walk to witness the beauty of the stars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lava

If you are planning to spend a week in Lava, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Reach Lava and acclimatize

Day 2: Visit Neora Valley National Park

Day 3: Trek to Kolakham

Day 4: Explore Rishop

Day 5: Take a long walk around the village

Day 6: Visit Lava Jamgyong Kongtrul Monastery and Neora Valley Interpretation Centre

Day 7: Depart from Lava

Food and Drink Scene of Lava

Lava has a limited food and drink scene, but there are a few must-try dishes and local beverages that you should not miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Momos: Steamed dumplings filled with minced meat or vegetables

  2. Thukpa: A soup made with noodles and vegetables

Local Beverages

  1. Darjeeling Tea: The world-renowned tea is grown in the nearby Darjeeling Hills and is a must-try for tea lovers.

Where to Stay in Lava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lava has a limited number of accommodation options, but you can easily find a place to stay that fits your budget and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  1. Blue Mountains Homestay: A cosy homestay located in the heart of Lava.

  2. Neora Valley Jungle Camp: A jungle camp located near the entrance of the Neora Valley National Park.

What to Buy in Lava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lava is known for its handicrafts and local products. Some of the best souvenirs that you can bring back home include:

  1. Handmade baskets and cane furniture

  2. Darjeeling Tea

Local Culture and Customs of Lava

The locals in Lava are warm and friendly. They follow a simple lifestyle and are deeply rooted in their culture and traditions. You can witness their way of life by interacting with them and participating in local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Lava

The best time to visit Lava is from September to May when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. The monsoon season from June to August should be avoided as the roads can become slippery and it is difficult to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lava

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions

  2. Hire a guide if you plan to trek inside the Neora Valley National Park

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the forests or the town

  2. Disturb the wildlife in the Neora Valley National Park

Safety and Health

Safety in Lava

Lava is a safe place to travel, but it is always advisable to take precautions. Here are a few safety tips:

  1. Avoid walking alone at night

  2. Keep your valuables safe

Health in Lava

Lava is a high altitude area, and it is advisable to acclimatize before starting any outdoor activities. Carry enough warm clothing and stay hydrated. In case of any medical emergency, there are a few clinics in the town.

Getting to Lava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lava is well-connected to the nearby towns and cities. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Lava

The nearest airport to Lava is Bagdogra Airport, which is located around 102 km away. You can take a taxi or a bus from the airport to reach Lava.

Bus Stations Near Lava

Lava is connected to the nearby towns and cities through regular bus services. You can take a bus from Siliguri, Darjeeling, or Kalimpong to reach Lava.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or a private car to reach Lava from the nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Lava

Lava is a small town and can be easily explored on foot. You can also rent a bicycle or a motorbike if you want to explore the nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Lava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can plan after exploring Lava:

  1. Darjeeling: Known for its tea gardens and stunning views of the Himalayas, Darjeeling is located around 33 km away from Lava.

  2. Kalimpong: A small town located around 33 km away from Lava, Kalimpong is famous for its orchids and cacti.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking for a peaceful getaway or an adventure-filled trip, Lava has something for everyone. Plan your trip to this hidden jewel of Eastern India, and discover the beauty of the Darjeeling Hills.
