Introduction to Kurseong

Kurseong is a beautiful hill town in the Darjeeling district of West Bengal, known for its breathtaking natural beauty and salubrious climate. Nestled amidst the lush green tea gardens and dense forests of the Eastern Himalayas, Kurseong is one of the lesser-known gems of North Bengal, waiting to be discovered by intrepid travellers looking for an offbeat destination.

Kurseong is renowned for its tea estates, scenic views, and historical significance. It is perfect for those who want to escape the hustle of the big cities and seek respite in the lap of nature. The town is located at an altitude of 1472m above sea level and is an ideal base for exploring the nearby hills and valleys.

List of Places to Visit in Kurseong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kurseong boasts of a rich history that is still alive in its old buildings, temples, and museums. Some of the must-visit historical and cultural attractions in Kurseong are:

  1. Netaji Museum: The museum is dedicated to the life of the great freedom fighter Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose, who stayed in Kurseong for some time.
  2. Giddapahar Durga Mata Mandir: The temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is a popular religious spot amongst the locals.
  3. Ambotia Shiva Temple: The temple is located amidst the tea gardens and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is believed to be more than 200 years old.
  4. Eagle’s Craig: Eagle’s Craig is a popular viewpoint that offers a panoramic view of the surrounding hills and valleys. It is also popularly known as the Suicide Point.

Natural Attractions of Kurseong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts can explore some of the stunning natural attractions of Kurseong. Some of the top natural attractions in Kurseong are:

  1. Makaibari Tea Estate: The Makaibari Tea Estate is situated on the slope of Kurseong hills and is the oldest organic tea estate in the world. The estate sprawls over a vast area and offers magnificent views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  2. Dowhill Park: Dowhill Park is a wonderful picnic spot for families located in the heart of Kurseong. The lush green park includes a deer park, a forest museum, and a children’s park.
  3. Ambootia Tea Garden: Ambootia Tea Garden is a 150-year-old garden that is famous for its organic teas. The garden also provides an idyllic setting for walks amidst the natural beauty of the Eastern Himalayas.
  4. Eagle’s Craig: As mentioned above, Eagle’s Craig is a popular viewpoint that offers a panoramic view of the surrounding hills and valleys. It is also popularly known as the Suicide Point.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurseong

Kurseong is an ideal destination for trekking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kurseong are:

  1. Namsu Samsu Trek: The trek starts from Kurseong and goes through the dense forest to Namsu and then to Samsu. The trek is of moderate difficulty and offers a chance to explore pristine natural locales.
  2. Dowhill to Mahanadi Trek: The trek is located in the cover of Mahanadi Wildlife Sanctuary and comes with a moderate level of difficulty. The trek covers some of the dense tropical forests, tea estates, and serene valleys.
  3. Cycling Tour of Kurseong: For cycling enthusiasts, the town offers a scenic ride from Kurseong to Darjeeling. The biking route covers dense forests, tea plantations, and small villages that are typical of Eastern Himalayan landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kurseong: Activities and Tours

Kurseong offers a range of activities to explore the town from trekking, bird-watching to even tea-tasting. Some of the best things to do in Kurseong are:

  1. Tea Tasting Tour: Tea is the biggest produce of Kurseong, and you can explore the tea plantations and factories to learn about the different varieties of tea and the tea-making process.
  2. Bird Watching in Kurseong: Kurseong is a bird-watching hotspot, and bird-enthusiasts can spot numerous birds, such as the Hill Partridge, Orange-Bellied Leafbird, and Black Bulbul.
  3. Mountain biking: There are several thrilling routes for mountain biking, like the downhill from Kurseong to Darjeeling, providing a rush of adrenaline to bicycle enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kurseong

Travellers can plan a week-long itinerary to explore Kurseong and its nearby areas. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Giddhapahar Durga Mata Mandir and Netaji Museum. Day 2: Explore Dowhill Park and Ambootia Tea Garden. Day 3: Trek to Eagle’s Craig and enjoy the panoramic view of the hills and valleys. Day 4: Visit Makaibari Tea Estate and know about the tea-producing process. Day 5: Go for a cycling tour from Kurseong to Darjeeling and witness scenic views. Day 6: Trek to Namsu and Samsu, and explore the calm forests. Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at your hotel before getting ready to leave.

Food and Drink Scene of Kurseong

Kurseong is famous for its organic tea and local Nepali cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Kurseong are:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Momos: Momos are a traditional Nepali dish, a form of steamed dumpling with meat or vegetable stuffing.
  2. Thukpa: Thukpa is a traditional noodle soup with vegetables or meat with a warm broth and is quite popular amongst the locals.
  3. Sael Roti: Sael Roti is a popular Nepali snack made with rice flour and is crispy from the outside and soft from the inside.

Local Beverages

  1. Tea: Kurseong is famous for its organic tea. The teahouses of Kurseong offer a range of teas to suit your taste buds.
  2. Tongba: Tongba is a traditional Himalayan beverage made from fermented millet and is served hot.

Where to Stay in Kurseong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kurseong has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from budget hotels to luxury resorts. Some of the best places to stay in Kurseong are:

  1. Cozy Nest Homestay: It is a budget-friendly homestay located in the heart of Kurseong with unobstructed views of the hills and valleys.
  2. Goomtee Tea Garden Retreat: It is a luxurious and eco-friendly stay located amidst the tea gardens and offers a wide range of amenities.
  3. Cochrane Place: Cochrane Place is an old-fashioned British Colonial-era bungalow that has been converted into a charming boutique hotel centrally located in Kurseong.

What to Buy in Kurseong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kurseong is famous for its organic tea, handicrafts, and clothes. Some of the best souvenirs to buy from Kurseong are:

  1. Tea: Kurseong produces some of the finest tea in the world, and you can bring home a pack of organic tea from the Makaibari or Ambootia Tea Estate.
  2. Handicrafts: Kurseong is famous for its handcrafted woolens, bamboo craft, and pottery.
  3. Clothes: Kurseong is famous for its handwoven shawls, stoles, and scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Kurseong

Kurseong is a town rich in history and culture. The town’s cultural influence is seen in its architecture, museums, and art galleries. The people of Kurseong are warm and friendly, and you can experience the true flavours of the Himalayan culture through their local cuisine, art, and music.

Best Time to Visit Kurseong

The best time to visit Kurseong is from March to May and from September to December, when the weather is pleasant and dry. The temperature ranges from 10 to 20 degrees Celsius during these months and makes it a perfect time for exploring the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurseong

Do:

  1. Carry warm clothes as the temperature can drop at night.
  2. Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  3. Respect local customs and traditions.
  4. Try local Nepali cuisine and tea.

Don’t:

  1. Litter the town or the natural attractions.
  2. Pluck fruits or flowers from the gardens.
  3. Wear revealing clothes at temples and other religious spots.
  4. Engage in conversations that may hurt local sentiments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kurseong

Kurseong is a relatively safe town with low crime rates, ever since the British colonial era. However, it is always recommended to be vigilant and cautious during your travels.

Health in Kurseong

The town has a hospital and several private health clinics that provide basic healthcare needs. However, it is necessary to carry basic medicines and consult a physician immediately in case of any emergency.

Getting to Kurseong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travellers can reach Kurseong by air or road. The nearest airport is Bagdogra Airport, and the nearest railway station is the New Jalpaiguri Railway Station, which is well-connected to major Indian cities.

Airports Near Kurseong

  1. Bagdogra Airport: The airport is located about 50 km from Kurseong and has regular flights from Kolkata, Delhi and other major cities.

Bus Stations Near Kurseong

  1. Siliguri Bus Station: Siliguri Bus Station connects Kurseong to neighbouring towns and cities.

Other Options

  1. Train: Kurseong is connected via rail to major cities like Kolkata and New Delhi, with several trains operating on this route.
  2. Taxi: Taxi services are available from Bagdogra Airport and New Jalpaiguri Railway Station to Kurseong.

How to Get Around in Kurseong

Travellers can explore the town on foot, taxis, or rental bikes. Local taxis and rental bikes are readily available and make for an excellent way to explore the town and its nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Kurseong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kurseong is located in the Darjeeling district, and some of the nearby destinations that one can explore are:

  1. Darjeeling: Darjeeling is just 30 km away from Kurseong and is known for its tea estates, natural beauty, and UNESCO world heritage site, the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway.
  2. Mirik: Mirik is a small hill town located at an altitude of 1767m above sea level and is renowned for its natural beauty and serene environment.
  3. Kalimpong: Kalimpong is located at an altitude of 1,247m above sea level and is famous for its orchids, tea gardens, and monasteries.

Conclusion

Plan your trip to Kurseong and experience the serene natural beauty, explore the cultural significance, and indulge in the traditional Himalayan hospitality. It is a perfect offbeat destination for travellers seeking a break from the monotony of city life. Enjoy the scenic views of the Himalayas, savour the local cuisine, and make memories that you will treasure for a lifetime.
