Introduction to Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary is a hidden gem nestled in the Murshidabad and Nadia districts of West Bengal, India. Spread across an area of 0.67 square kilometers, this wildlife sanctuary is a haven for nature lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, and adventure seekers. The sanctuary is famous for its dense forests, peaceful lakes, and diverse wildlife, making it an ideal destination for wildlife safaris, bird watching, hiking, and biking.

List of Places to Visit in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bethuadahari Temple: This ancient temple dates back to the 18th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple architecture is an excellent example of the traditional Bengali style.

Natural Attractions of Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Deer Park: The Deer Park is a popular attraction in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary. It is home to several species of deer, including spotted deer, barking deer, and sambar deer. You can take a nature walk through the park, and if you’re lucky, you might spot some deer grazing in the woods.

  • Chakdighi Bheries: The Chakdighi Bheries are two large and picturesque lakes with a tranquil ambiance. These lakes serve as an ideal spot for bird watching, picnics, and boating.

  • Wildlife Safari: Take a ride in a jeep or an elephant and venture into the wild to see some of the park’s most exotic wildlife, including Bengal tigers and Indian leopards.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Sunset Point Hiking Trail: A moderate hiking trail with a fantastic view at the summit. Perfect for hiking enthusiasts and fitness enthusiasts.

  • Cycling Route around Chakdighi Bheries: Embark on a cycling tour around the serene lakes for a peaceful and picturesque experience.

Best Things to Do in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

  • Walking Tours: Walk around the sanctuary with a guide and learn about the flora and fauna, local traditions and customs, and the history of the place.

  • Bird Watching: The sanctuary is a paradise for bird watchers, with over 120 species of birds identified in the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Deer Park followed by a nature walk along Sunset Point Hiking Trail
Day 2 Take a Wildlife Safari in the morning and relax by the lake in the afternoon

Food and Drink Scene of Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhetki Maacher Paturi: A traditional Bengali dish made with Bhetki fish marinated in mustard paste and tender banana leaves.

  • Shorshe Ilish: A popular Bengali recipe made with hilsa fish and a tangy mustard sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Aam Panna: A refreshing drink made from green mangoes, sugar, and mint leaves.

  • Nolen Gur Sharbat: A sweet and cooling drink made with nolen gur, a local form of jaggery.

Where to Stay in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Royal Jungle Retreat: A luxury hotel situated in the heart of the sanctuary. The hotel offers great views of the surrounding forests and lakes.

  • Bethuadahari Forest Resort: A budget-friendly resort located near the sanctuary, offering peaceful settings amid the tranquil surroundings.

What to Buy in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: The puppet museum near the sanctuary is a great place to purchase souvenirs like embroidered shawls, pottery, and traditional artifacts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

The locals speak Bengali and have a rich cultural heritage that dates back centuries. The area is famous for its traditional Bengali cuisine, festivals, and handicrafts. It is important to respect the local traditions and customs while visiting the sanctuary.

Best Time to Visit Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and wildlife sightings are at their highest. The monsoon season is between June and September, and the sanctuary remains closed during this time due to heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Respect nature and the wildlife
  • Carry a camera to capture the beautiful surroundings and wildlife
  • Stay on marked trails
  • Hire an experienced guide if venturing out into the wild

Don’t:

  • Disturb the animals
  • Litter the sanctuary
  • Smoke inside the sanctuary
  • Play loud music or make loud noises

Safety and Health

Safety in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

It is essential to follow the safety guidelines and instructions provided by the park authorities while visiting the sanctuary. Avoid wandering off-trail, and do not venture too close to the wild animals.

Health in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Carry sufficient water and snacks during your visit to stay hydrated and energized. Apply mosquito repellent to avoid insect bites, and avoid contact with stagnant water sources like ponds, which are potential breeding grounds for mosquitoes.

Getting to Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

The nearest airport to Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary is Kolkata Airport, located approximately 160 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

A bus service is available from Kolkata, Siliguri, and Malda to reach the sanctuary.

Other Options

Guests can also hire a taxi or a car to reach the sanctuary from nearby cities like Kolkata, Siliguri, and Malda.

How to Get Around in Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary

Walking, cycling, and wildlife safari are the most popular ways to get around in the sanctuary.

Where to Go Next from Bethuadahari Wildlife Sanctuary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kolkata: The cultural capital of India, located 160 kilometers away from the sanctuary.

  • Sundarbans: The largest delta in the world, located 100 kilometers away from the sanctuary.
