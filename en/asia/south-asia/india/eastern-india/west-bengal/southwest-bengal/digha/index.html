A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Digha, West Bengal

If you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation in India, Digha is the perfect destination. Located in West Bengal, this seaside resort town is a popular tourist spot, especially for those coming from Kolkata, as it’s only a five-hour drive away.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Digha, we’ll give you all the information you need to make the most out of your trip, from things to do and see to where to stay and eat. Whether you’re planning a week-long vacation or just a weekend getaway, this guide will help you plan your trip to Digha with confidence.

List of Places to Visit in Digha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Digha has a rich history dating back to the British colonial era. The town was a popular vacation spot for British officers, and the remnants of their architecture can still be seen today. Here are the top historical and cultural attractions you should consider visiting:

  • Digha Mohana Fish Market: Located near the new Digha beach, the Digha Mohana Fish Market is a must-visit for seafood lovers. You can find a variety of freshly caught fish, crabs, and lobsters here.
  • Marine Aquarium and Research Centre: This aquarium is located near the old Digha beach and boasts an extensive collection of marine life found in the Bay of Bengal.
  • Amarabati Park: A peaceful park located near the new Digha sea beach. The park has a musical fountain and various activities like an artificial lake, toy train, and games.

Natural Attractions of Digha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Digha is also known for its natural beauty, and there are several outdoor activities and scenic views that you can explore. Here are some recommendations:

  • Talsari Beach: Located around 7 km from the main town, this hidden gem is a must-visit for nature lovers. The beach has a unique red hue, and the greenery around it is soothing to the eyes.
  • Udaipur Beach: Another beach close to Digha, Udaipur Beach is a secluded spot with crystal-clear waters.
  • Mandarmani Beach: Located around 23 km from Digha, this pristine beach is known for its serene environment and is perfect for long walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Digha

For adventure enthusiasts, Digha has several trails suitable for hiking and cycling. Some popular routes for hiking and cycling are:

  • Digha Beach to Mohona: A scenic route that starts from the new Digha beach and ends at the Digha Mohana Fish Market. The route is around 5 km long and is perfect for a morning jog.
  • Digha to Shankarpur: This is a longer trail, spanning around 14 km between the two beaches. You can take a bike, hire a car or walk this path to witness the breathtaking views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Digha: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for activities to do in Digha, the town has various options. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Water sports: You can enjoy activities such as jet skiing, speed boating, and banana boat rides at the new Digha beach.
  • Horse riding: Horse riding is also available at the new Digha beach.
  • Tours: You can also take a tour of the town to explore the notable historical sites and understand Digha’s rich culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Digha

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring Digha and nearby areas:

Day 1-2: Relax at the beach, visit Amarabati Park, and check out the Marine Aquarium and Research Centre.

Day 3: Visit Talsari Beach, explore Mohona, and try some fresh seafood from the Digha Mohana Fish Market.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Shankarpur, visit the nearby temples, and explore the serene beaches.

Day 5: Go horse riding and water sports at the new Digha beach, have lunch at the beach-side shacks.

Day 6: Take a trip to Mandarmani to relax on the beach and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 7: Enjoy a tour of the town to explore the historical sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Digha

When it comes to food and drink in Digha, seafood is undoubtedly the primary attraction. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bhetki Macher Paturi: A popular Bengali delicacy, made with fish fillets, mustard paste, and wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Jumbo Prawn Curry: A spicy and tangy curry made with freshly caught prawns.

Local Beverages

  • Nolen Gur Sharbat: A refreshing drink made with palm jaggery, and is perfect for beating the summer heat.
  • Masala Lassi: This drink is a blend of yogurt, water, spices, and is a perfect drink for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Digha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Digha has several accommodation options, ranging from budget stays to luxury resorts. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Dolphin: This hotel is situated near the old beach and is perfect for budget travelers.
  • The Kodiakanal Resort: A beautiful resort in a serene location, providing luxury amenities with greenery.
  • The Sea Hawk: The Sea Hawk is a popular beachfront hotel with exceptional views of the sea.

What to Buy in Digha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While Digha is not known for its shopping scene, there are some local products that you should consider buying. Here are some recommendations:

  • Sea-Shells: You can find several beautiful shells on the beaches, and you can take them home as souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: You can find handcrafted items made using seashells, and these make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Digha

Digha has a rich culture that is a blend of Bengali and Anglo-Indian traditions. The town has always been a melting pot of cultures, thanks to the British influence. The locals are very friendly and welcoming, and appreciate it if you show respect for their culture.

Best Time to Visit Digha

The winter season (November to February) is the best time to visit Digha when the weather is pleasant and mild. Summers (March to June) can be harsh with temperatures going up to 36-38 degrees Celsius. Monsoons (July to September) can also be a good time to visit Digha if you love the rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Digha

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local seafood.
  • Take a tour of the town to learn about Digha’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches, keep the place clean.
  • Don’t swim in the sea in the monsoons, as the currents can be strong.
  • Don’t ignore safety measures while doing water sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Digha

Digha is generally a safe place, but as with any other tourist destination, you should take some precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid isolated areas, especially after dark.
  • Don’t accept food or drinks from strangers.
  • Keep your valuables secured and avoid flashing them in public places.

Health in Digha

When it comes to health, Digha doesn’t have any major health hazards. However, summers can be hot, and you should keep yourself hydrated. Additionally, it’s advisable to wear sunscreen and protective gear for water sports.

Getting to Digha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Digha is well-connected to other parts of West Bengal, and getting there is easy. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • By Air: The nearest airport to Digha is Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, which is approximately 200 km away.
  • By Train: Digha has a railway station, and trains run regularly from Kolkata and other major cities.
  • By Bus: You can also reach Digha by bus, as several private and state-run buses run from Kolkata and other major cities.

How to Get Around in Digha

There are several transportation options available to explore Digha:

  • Rickshaws: Rickshaws are popular and affordable transportation options in Digha, and you can easily hail one on the road.
  • Buses: Local buses are also available and are a pocket-friendly means of transportation.
  • Bikes and Cars: You can also hire bikes or cars from several rental companies to explore Digha at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Digha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Digha is also a great base to explore nearby destinations, such as:

  • Shankarpur: A nearby town known for its serene beaches and temples.
  • Mandarmani: A beautiful beach known for its peaceful environment and scenic views.
  • Tajpur: A hidden seaside gem that’s an excellent spot for adventure enthusiasts.

Conclusion

With its serene beaches, rich history, and cultural blend, Digha is a perfect destination for a beach vacation in India. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to Digha with confidence. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore the beauty of Digha!
