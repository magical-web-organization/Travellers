Hazaribagh Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tourist’s Handbook

Located in Jharkhand, Eastern India, Hazaribagh is a city that’s all about natural beauty, serene atmosphere, and lots of history to discover. The city is surrounded by dense forests and mountains that make it a quintessential destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. There’s much to see and do here that you may not want to miss. Here’s everything you need to know about Hazaribagh before you set off on your next adventure:

List of Places to Visit in Hazaribagh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hazaribagh Museum and Art Palace: This museum boasts an extensive collection of local artifacts, historical documents, and art pieces. It is an excellent place for history lovers who’d like to delve deeper into the city’s rich cultural heritage.
  2. Rajrappa Temple: Dedicated to the goddess Chinnamasta, Rajrappa Temple is a popular pilgrimage site that attracts thousands of devotees every year. The temple’s spectacular architecture and the tranquil atmosphere make it a must-visit place in Hazaribagh.
  3. Canari Hill: Also known as Kanheri Hill, this historical site dating back to the Mauryan Empire is home to numerous rock-shelters and inscriptions. It’s an excellent place for history enthusiasts who’d like to witness the past come alive.

Natural Attractions of Hazaribagh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary: Sprawling over an area of 184 square kilometers, the Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for nature lovers. With its scenic beauty and rich wildlife, this sanctuary offers an unparalleled experience for a wildlife enthusiast.
  2. Canary Hill: A popular hiking spot, Canari hill offers some of the most stunning views of the city. You can hike up to the peak to get panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.
  3. Konar Dam: Located on the Konar River, this dam is an engineering masterpiece that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. You can indulge in water sports such as boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hazaribagh

  1. Canary Hill Trek: As mentioned earlier, Canari Hill is a popular hiking spot that offers some of the city’s most beautiful views.
  2. Rajarappa Trek: The Rajarappa Trek takes you through the beautiful Rajarappa hills, surrounded by dense forests and streams.
  3. Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: The wildlife sanctuary offers several trekking routes that’ll take you closer to nature.

Best Things to Do in Hazaribagh: Activities and Tours

  1. Wildlife Safari: A trip to Hazaribagh wouldn’t be complete without an exciting wildlife safari in Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary.
  2. Visit Rajrappa Temple: Visit Rajrappa Temple and be mesmerized by the goddess Chinnamasta’s unique depiction.
  3. Spend a Day at Konar Dam: Enjoy the serenity of the Konar Dam and indulge in water sports and activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Hazaribagh

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Handia: A tribal rice-beer, Handia is an exotic drink that’s famous across Eastern India.
  2. Til Barfi: A sweet delicacy made from sesame seeds, til barfi is a must-try when visiting Hazaribagh.
  3. Litti Chokha: A traditional dish of Bihar and Jharkhand, Litti Chokha is a flavorful dish made with roasted flour balls, chana dal, and spices, served with a side of mashed potatoes or eggplant.

Local Beverages

  1. Handia: An alcoholic beverage made from rice and fermented in earthen pots.
  2. Mahua: A local alcoholic drink having floral undertones.
  3. Tea: Herbal tea with flavors inspired by Jharkhand’s culture and ingredients.

Where to Stay in Hazaribagh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Kailash International: A budget-friendly hotel located centrally and offering comfortable rooms and excellent service to its guests.
  2. Hazaribagh Spa and Resort: A luxury resort located amidst the serene hills and wild sanctuaries of Hazaribagh.
  3. Hill View Guest House: A guest house that offers picturesque views of the mountains and clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Hazaribagh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bamboo artifacts: Handicrafts made of Bamboo are a perfect souvenir to take home from Hazaribagh.
  2. Sikki Grass articles: They are articles made of rolled grass, which are popularly used to create jewelry, baskets, coasters, mats, and more.
  3. Stone sculptures: Jharkhand is known for its stone sculpture arts.

Local Culture and Customs of Hazaribagh

Hazaribagh is a land that is culturally diverse and has a multiethnic population. The city is famous for its cultural festivals such as the Sohrai, Karma, and Sarhul. The locals are warm-hearted and welcoming to tourists. Although it’s not mandatory, tourists are always encouraged to dress modestly in respect for the locals’ culture.

Best Time to Visit Hazaribagh

The period between October and April is the best time to visit Hazaribagh as the weather during this time is pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities like hiking, trekking, and wildlife safaris.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hazaribagh

Do:

  1. Respect local culture and customs.
  2. Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  3. Enjoy the natural beauty and wildlife of Hazaribagh.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local culture and customs.
  2. Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious sites.
  3. Disrespect the wildlife and natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hazaribagh

Hazaribagh is overall a safe city for travelers. However, as with any city, it’s always best to exercise caution and stay alert, especially when traveling alone at night. Tourists are advised to avoid any political rallies or protests.

Health in Hazaribagh

Tourists are advised to carry their medication and mosquito repellents. It’s essential to drink only bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Hazaribagh: Transportation Options and Logistics

  2. Ranchi Airport: 91 km away
  3. Patna Airport: 239 km away

Bus Stations Near Hazaribagh

  1. Hazaribagh Bus Stand: Located in the heart of the city
  2. Ranchi Bus Stand: 103 km away
  3. Patna Bus Stand: 232 km away

Other Options

Taxis, cabs, and private cars are readily available for tourists traveling to Hazaribagh from neighboring cities.

How to Get Around in Hazaribagh

Hazaribagh is a small town, and one can easily explore the city on foot. Alternatively, tourists can hire a taxi or a bike on rent to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Hazaribagh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ranchi: 103 km away, it is the state capital of Jharkhand and has some of the state’s most breathtaking spots like Hundru Falls, Pahari Mandir, and Dassam Falls.
  2. Netarhat: 126 Km away, Netarhat Hill Station is a perfect getaway from the hustle-bustle of city life.
  3. Patna: 239 km Away, Patna is known for its rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Hazaribagh is a perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore India off the beaten path. Whether you want to witness the city’s rich cultural heritage, indulge in outdoor adventures or cuisines, Hazaribagh is sure not to disappoint. So, when planning your next trip to India, don’t forget to visit Hazaribagh!
