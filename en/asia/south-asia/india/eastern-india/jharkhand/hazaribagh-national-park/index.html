Introduction to Hazaribagh National Park

Located in Jharkhand, in Eastern India, Hazaribagh National Park is a sprawling sanctuary that covers an area of approximately 184 square kilometers. The park is famous for its diverse wildlife, including tigers, leopards, deer, wild boar, and sloth bears, as well as its stunning natural landscapes, including rugged hills, forests, and waterfalls.

List of Places to Visit in Hazaribagh National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hazaribagh Museum: This museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts related to the history, culture, and wildlife of the region. Visitors can explore the various displays showcasing the lifestyle of the area’s tribal communities, as well as browse the extensive library of books and manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Hazaribagh National Park:

  • Canary Hill: Rising to a height of around 709 meters, Canary Hill is the highest peak in Hazaribagh. Visitors can enjoy a scenic panoramic view of the surrounding landscape from the top.

  • Konar Dam: The Konar Dam is a popular spot for picnics and relaxing by the water. Visitors can also take a dip in the refreshing waters of the reservoir.

  • Hazaribagh Lake: The Hazaribagh Lake is a serene spot that is perfect for nature lovers. Visitors can rent a boat and paddle around the lake or simply relax on the banks and take in the breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hazaribagh National Park

  • Bhadrakali Hill Trek: This trek takes around 3 hours and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It starts at the Bhadrakali Temple and ends at Canary Hill.

  • Dhardhari Waterfall Trek: This trek takes around 2 hours and leads visitors through lush forests to the cascading Dhardhari waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Hazaribagh National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Safari: A jeep or elephant safari is the best way to explore the dense forests and spot some of the park’s wildlife.

  • Birdwatching: Hazaribagh is home to a variety of bird species, including hornbills, eagles, and vultures. Visitors can spend hours strolling through the forests and watching the birds in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Hazaribagh National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Panch Phoran Tarkari: A vegetarian dish made with five spices and a variety of seasonal vegetables.

  • Handia: A locally brewed rice beer with a slightly sour taste.

Local Beverages

  • Chaang: A fermented millet beer that is traditionally consumed by the tribal communities of the region.

  • Mahua: A liquor made from the flowers of the mahua tree that is native to the area.

Where to Stay in Hazaribagh National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hazaribagh Tourism Hotel: This is the only government-run hotel in the area and offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

  • Hazaribagh Homestay: This homestay is located in the heart of the city and offers visitors a chance to experience the local way of life.

  • V Resorts: This luxury resort is nestled in the midst of nature and offers a range of amenities, including a swimming pool and spa.

What to Buy in Hazaribagh National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tribal handicrafts: The tribal communities of Hazaribagh are known for their intricate beadwork and woodcarvings, which make for unique and authentic souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hazaribagh National Park

  • Hazaribagh is home to several tribal communities, including the Santhal and Oraon tribes. Visitors should respect their culture and traditions by dressing modestly and asking for permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Hazaribagh National Park

The best time to visit Hazaribagh National Park is between November and June when the weather is pleasant, and the wildlife is more active.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hazaribagh National Park

Do:

  • Book safaris and tours in advance to ensure availability.

  • Pack light and comfortable clothing, mosquito repellent, and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the animals or litter in the park.

  • Wear brightly colored clothing as it can attract unwanted attention.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hazaribagh National Park

  • Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and be cautious of their surroundings.

  • The park is home to wildlife, some of which can be dangerous, so visitors should follow the rules and regulations of the park.

Health in Hazaribagh National Park

  • Visitors should ensure they are up-to-date with their vaccinations and carry a first-aid kit with essential medicines and supplies.

  • The park is located in a malaria-prone area, so visitors should take precautions such as using mosquito nets and insect repellent.

Getting to Hazaribagh National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hazaribagh National Park

  • Ranchi International Airport: Located approximately 98 kilometers from Hazaribagh, this is the nearest airport.

Bus Stations Near Hazaribagh National Park

  • Hazaribagh Bus Stand: This is the primary bus stand in the area and is located in the heart of the city.

Other Options

  • Private taxi services are available from nearby cities such as Ranchi and Dhanbad.

How to Get Around in Hazaribagh National Park

  • Jeeps and elephants are available for hire for safaris and wildlife tours.

  • Rental cars and motorcycles are available for hire for self-guided exploration of the park.

Where to Go Next from Hazaribagh National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Netarhat: A hill station located approximately 79 kilometers from Hazaribagh that is famous for its stunning natural landscapes.

  • Ranchi: The capital city of Jharkhand that is home to several attractions, including waterfalls and temples.

Conclusion

Hazaribagh National Park is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. From its diverse wildlife to its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage, there is something for every traveler in this beautiful sanctuary. Follow this comprehensive guide to get the most out of your journey to Hazaribagh National Park.
