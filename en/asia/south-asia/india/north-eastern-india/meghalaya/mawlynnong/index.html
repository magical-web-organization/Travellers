Introduction to Mawlynnong

Located in the eastern state of Meghalaya, India, Mawlynnong is known as the cleanest village in Asia. Surrounded by lush green forests and rolling hills, Mawlynnong is a beautiful and peaceful destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With its treehouses, waterfalls, living root bridges, and breathtaking views, Mawlynnong is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Meghalaya or the Northeast region of India.

List of Places to Visit in Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong has a variety of cultural and natural attractions, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Balang Presbyterian Church
  • Mawkdok Dympep Valley View
  • Dawki Bridge
  • Jakrem Hot Springs

Natural Attractions of Mawlynnong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Masinram Adventure Camp
  • Sky View Point
  • Rainbow Falls
  • Jingmaham Living Root Bridge
  • Nohwet Living Root Bridge

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Dawki to Mawlynnong Trek
  • David Scott Trail
  • Shillong Peak Trek

Best Things to Do in Mawlynnong: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Living Root Bridges
  • Explore the Balancing Rock
  • Trek to Pynursla

Week-Long Itinerary for Mawlynnong

Day 1: Arrive in Mawlynnong and explore the village.

Day 2: Visit Jingmaham Living Root Bridge and Nohwet Living Root Bridge.

Day 3: Take a trek to Pynursla and explore the Balancing Rock.

Day 4: Visit Sky View Point, Rainbow Falls, and Masinram Adventure Camp.

Day 5: Visit Dawki Bridge and Shnongpdeng for kayaking, fishing, and camping activities.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Shillong and explore the city.

Day 7: Depart from Mawlynnong.

Food and Drink Scene of Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong has a few local eateries that serve traditional Khasi cuisine. Some must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jadoh (rice with meat and spices)
  • Tungrymbai (fermented soybean dish)
  • Doh Neiiong (pork with black sesame seeds)

Local Beverages

  • Kwai (betel nut with lime and tobacco)
  • Sohiong (local rice beer)

Where to Stay in Mawlynnong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mawlynnong has a few guesthouses and homestays that offer basic accommodation options. Some popular ones are:

  • Mawlynnong Guesthouse
  • Riwai Homestay
  • Taba’s Homestay

What to Buy in Mawlynnong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mawlynnong is known for its handicrafts and woven goods made by the local women. You can also buy traditional Khasi clothing, betel nut cutters, and bamboo products.

Local Culture and Customs of Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong is a matrilineal society where women inherit property and children take their mother’s surname. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs and dress modestly while visiting local villages and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mawlynnong

The best time to visit Mawlynnong is from October to May when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mawlynnong

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites and local villages.
  • Take permission before taking photographs of locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Use plastic bottles or bags in the village.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong is considered safe for visitors, but it’s always best to take precautions while traveling. Make sure to carry a copy of your passport and other important documents. Avoid traveling alone at night and use licensed taxis or public transport.

Health in Mawlynnong

Make sure to carry mosquito repellent and wear full-sleeved clothes to prevent mosquito bites. Drink only bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach infections.

Getting to Mawlynnong: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Mawlynnong is in Guwahati, which is around 171 km away. From Guwahati, you can hire a taxi to reach Shillong and then take a shared jeep or taxi to Mawlynnong. The nearest railhead is in Guwahati, and there are regular buses and taxis from Guwahati to Shillong and Mawlynnong.

Airports Near Mawlynnong

  • Guwahati Airport (171 km)
  • Shillong Airport (90 km)

Bus Stations Near Mawlynnong

  • Shillong Bus Station (85 km)
  • Dawki Bus Station (4 km)

Other Options

  • Shared Taxis and Jeeps from Shillong and Guwahati

How to Get Around in Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong is a small village, and most attractions are located within walking distance. You can also hire a bicycle or motorbike to explore the village and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Mawlynnong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cherrapunji (55 km)
  • Shillong (85 km)
  • Mawsynram (66 km)

Conclusion

Mawlynnong is a hidden gem in Meghalaya, India, and offers a unique experience to travelers who are looking to explore offbeat destinations. With its natural attractions, local culture, and warm hospitality, Mawlynnong should definitely be on your travel list.
