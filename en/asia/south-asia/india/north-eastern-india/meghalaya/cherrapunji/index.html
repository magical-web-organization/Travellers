Introduction to Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji, also known as Sohra, is a small town located in the East Khasi Hills district of Meghalaya in North-Eastern India. It is known for holding the record for the highest annual rainfall on Earth, which has helped shape its natural beauty. Cherrapunji is surrounded by lush green hills, gushing waterfalls, and stunning landscapes that attract tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Cherrapunji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nongsawlia Presbyterian Church: This church is an important cultural landmark in Cherrapunji, built by Welsh missionaries in 1895. Its stunning architecture and beautiful surroundings make it worth a visit.

  2. Khasi Monoliths: Cherrapunji has many unique monoliths or upright stones, rich in cultural significance for local Khasi people. These monoliths are over 500 years old and are located in Mawshamok village.

  3. Dainthlen Falls: This beautiful waterfall boasts a legend of a giant serpent named Dainthlen who was believed to be slain near the falls. The falls are surrounded by lush greenery and are a great place for picnics and photo ops.

Natural Attractions of Cherrapunji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mawsmai Cave: This natural limestone cave is a popular tourist spot and is located about 6 km from Cherrapunji. The cave extends over 150 meters and features unique rock formations and water pools.

  2. Seven Sisters Falls: The Seven Sisters Falls are one of the most famous waterfalls in India and are located at the entrance of Cherrapunji. The falls get their name from the seven streams that flow down the rocks. Visitors can take a trek to the bottom of the falls to get a closer look.

  3. Double Decker Living Root Bridge: This unique bridge is made entirely out of interwoven roots of rubber trees and is located in Nongriat village. The bridge is a great example of sustainable architecture and is a must-visit spot for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji is an excellent place for hiking and exploring the natural surroundings. Here are some of the popular hiking/biking routes:

  1. Mawphlang Sacred Forest: This forest, located 25 km from Cherrapunji, is home to many rare species of plants and animals. The forest is considered sacred by the Khasi people and is a great place for hiking and bird watching.

  2. Wah Kaba Falls: This hidden waterfall is located near Sohliya village, and the trek to the falls is a great adventure for hiking enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy swimming and cliff jumping in the crystal-clear water of the falls.

  3. Mawlynnong Village: Located 80 km from Cherrapunji, Mawlynnong Village is known as the cleanest village in Asia. The village is surrounded by breathtaking views, and tourists can take a trek to the nearby natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Cherrapunji: Activities and Tours

  1. Cherrapunji Adventure Resort: This resort offers a wide range of activities such as zip-lining, rock-climbing, and trekking. Visitors can also stay in tree houses and enjoy the natural surroundings.

  2. Living Root Bridge Trek: Trekking to the Living Root Bridge is a great way to experience the unique sustainable architecture of Meghalaya. Visitors can take a guided tour to the bridge and learn about the Khasi culture.

  3. Sohra Tourism Fest: This annual festival held in December celebrates the unique culture and natural beauty of Cherrapunji. The festival includes various activities such as water rafting, cultural shows, and food fairs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cherrapunji

Day 1: Arrival, check-in at a hotel, and visit Nongsawlia Presbyterian Church.

Day 2: Trek to the Double Decker Living Root Bridge in Nongriat village and explore the local surroundings.

Day 3: Visit the Seven Sisters Falls and Mawsmai Cave.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Mawlynnong Village, the cleanest village in Asia, and explore the local attractions.

Day 5: Trek to Wah Kaba Falls and enjoy the scenic beauty.

Day 6: Visit Khasi Monoliths in Mawshamok village and Dainthlen Falls.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Cherrapunji

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Jadoh: A traditional dish made with rice and pork, Jadoh is a staple food in Meghalaya.

  2. Dohneiiong: A popular pork meat dish, cooked with black sesame seeds, ginger, and turmeric. It is traditionally served at special occasions like weddings and outdoor parties.

  3. Pumaloi: A sweet steamed rice cake similar to momos, but without filling.

Local Beverages

  1. Sohra Tea: The tea is grown on the hills of Cherrapunji and is known for its distinct flavor and aroma.

  2. Kyat: A locally brewed rice beer, Kyat, is a popular drink among locals in Meghalaya.

  3. Cha-Khnam: A refreshing drink made with fermented rice water and herbs, Cha-Khnam is a must-try beverage in Cherrapunji.

Where to Stay in Cherrapunji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pala Resort: A beautiful resort located amidst tea gardens, Pala Resort offers comfortable cottages with stunning views of the hills.

  2. Cherrapunjee Holiday Resort: A budget-friendly resort with well-equipped rooms and beautiful surroundings.

  3. Saimika Resort: A luxurious resort with a variety of rooms, including tree houses, and offers adventure activities like zip-lining.

What to Buy in Cherrapunji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Traditional Khasi Shawls and Dresses: These colorful garments are hand-woven by local artisans and make great souvenirs.

  2. Pineapple: Meghalaya is known for its juicy pineapples, and Cherrapunji has many local shops selling fresh fruits.

  3. Handicrafts: Cherrapunji has many street markets where visitors can find unique handicrafts made of bamboo and cane.

Local Culture and Customs of Cherrapunji

The Khasi people, who are the native tribe of Cherrapunji, have a rich cultural heritage of music, dance, and handicrafts. Visitors should respect cultural practices such as seeking permission before taking photos of locals or entering sacred places.

Best Time to Visit Cherrapunji

The best time to visit Cherrapunji is between October and May, when the weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without any rainfall. The monsoon season, between June and September, is not ideal for outdoor adventures as the area receives heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cherrapunji

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately: Visitors should dress modestly and wear comfortable shoes suitable for hiking and trekking.

  2. Try local food: Meghalaya has a unique cuisine that visitors should try.

  3. Respect local customs: Visitors should respect local customs and seek permission before taking photos.

Don’t:

  1. Litter: Visitors should avoid littering and maintain cleanliness in the surroundings.

  2. Disrespect cultural practices: Visitors should not disrespect local customs or enter sacred places without permission.

  3. Destroy flora and fauna: Visitors should not destroy plants or animals or harm the environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should keep in mind a few safety tips:

  1. Hire a licensed guide for trekking and other adventure activities.

  2. Avoid walking alone at night.

  3. Keep valuables safe in a hotel or locker.

Health in Cherrapunji

Visitors should be cautious about their health and take preventive measures:

  1. Drink bottled water and avoid eating street food without proper hygiene.

  2. Carry basic medication for common ailments.

  3. Protect skin and eyes from sun exposure and wear protective gear while trekking.

Getting to Cherrapunji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cherrapunji

The nearest airport to Cherrapunji is Guwahati Airport, located 148 km away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus from the airport to reach Cherrapunji.

Bus Stations Near Cherrapunji

Visitors can take a bus from Guwahati, Shillong, or other major cities to Cherrapunji. The bus station is located near the town center.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Cherrapunji. The roads are well-maintained, but visitors should be prepared for narrow hilly roads.

How to Get Around in Cherrapunji

Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to get around Cherrapunji. Buses and shared taxis are also available for commute, but they are not very reliable.

Where to Go Next from Cherrapunji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Shillong: The capital city of Meghalaya, Shillong, is located about 54 km from Cherrapunji and is known for its scenic beauty and vibrant culture.

  2. Dawki: A picturesque small town located on the India-Bangladesh border, Dawki, is known for its crystal-clear river and breathtaking beauty.

Conclusion

Cherrapunji is a gem in the North-Eastern region of India and is a must-visit place for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. From lush green landscapes to cultural heritage, Cherrapunji has something to offer for every traveler. Visitors should respect the local culture and take preventive measures for health and safety. Happy Exploring!
