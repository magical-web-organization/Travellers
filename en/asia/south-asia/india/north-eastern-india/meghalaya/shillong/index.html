Introduction to Shillong

Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, is a picturesque hill station located in the northeastern part of India. Often called the ‘Abode of Clouds,’ this scenic destination is surrounded by rolling hills, cascading waterfalls, and serene lakes. The city is known for its pleasant weather, rich culture, and amazing outdoor adventure opportunities.

List of Places to Visit in Shillong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shillong has a rich history and cultural heritage, which is reflected in its various museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit attractions in this category include:

  • Don Bosco Centre for Indigenous Cultures: This museum is devoted to showcasing the culture and traditions of Northeast India’s indigenous people through various exhibitions and interactive displays.
  • Lady Hydari Park: Named after the first lady of the state, this park features a mini zoo, a butterfly museum, and a small lake.
  • Ward’s Lake: Built in 1894, this artificial lake is a popular spot for boating and picnics.
  • Cathedral of Mary Help of Christians: Built-in 1936, this cathedral is a prominent landmark in Shillong with its Gothic-inspired architecture.

Natural Attractions of Shillong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shillong is known for its breathtaking natural beauty and outdoor adventure opportunities. Here are some of the top natural attractions you should visit:

  • Elephant Falls: Located just outside Shillong, these three-tiered waterfalls are a popular picnic spot and offer stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Shillong Peak: The highest point in Shillong, this peak offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding hills.
  • Mawphlang Sacred Forest: This ancient forest is considered sacred by the Khasi tribe and is a great place to trek and explore the area’s diverse flora and fauna.
  • Umiam Lake: This man-made lake offers various water sports activities, including kayaking, boating, and water skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shillong

Shillong offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, and there are many scenic trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • David Scott Trail: This is one of the most popular hiking trails in the region, and it takes you through the lush green forests and villages of Meghalaya.
  • Mawphlang Trekking Trail: This is a moderate trek through the Mawphlang Sacred Forest which offers a chance to view some rare flora and fauna.
  • Shillong-Pynursla Road Biking: This scenic road offers a great opportunity for cyclists to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Shillong: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above-mentioned attractions, there are several other exciting things to do in Shillong. Here are some of the best activities and tours you can enjoy:

  • Explore the Local Markets: Shillong is known for its bustling local markets, where you can buy everything from handicrafts and clothing to fresh produce and spices.
  • Rock Climbing and Rappelling: The hills around Shillong offer great opportunities for rock climbing and rappelling, and you can find several adventure companies offering guided tours.
  • Caving: Meghalaya is known for its extensive cave systems, and there are several tour companies that offer guided trips through the region’s caves.
  • Sunset Point: Visit Shillong’s sunset point near Shillong Peak for a mesmerizing sunset view amidst the lush green hills.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shillong

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Shillong, here’s a suggested itinerary for you:

Food and Drink Scene of Shillong

Must-Try Dishes

Shillong’s cuisine is robust, flavorful, and heavily influenced by tribal traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes in the city:

  • Jadoh: This is a traditional Khasi dish made of rice and meat cooked with ginger, onions, and turmeric.
  • Tungrymbai: This is a fermented soybean paste that is used as a dipping sauce or in curries and stews.
  • Pumaloi: This is a sticky rice dish wrapped in banana leaves and steamed to perfection.
  • Doh Khleih: This dish is made of minced pork and onions cooked with ginger, coriander leaves, and local spices.
  • DakJad: This is spicy chicken curry served with rice cakes.

Local Beverages

Meghalaya is known for its unique and refreshing beverages. Here are some worth trying:

  • Kwai: This is a betel nut-based snack, which is common among the Khasi tribes in Meghalaya. It’s a mild stimulant and often consumed during social gatherings.
  • Pineapple Beer: This is a refreshing and fizzy drink popular in the region, made from fermented pineapple pulp.
  • Syrngew ka Kong: This is a local kind of tea made from the bark of the tea plant along with other spices.

Where to Stay in Shillong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shillong offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and requirement. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Ri Kynjai: This eco-friendly resort overlooks the Umiam Lake and offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.
  • Cafe Shillong Bed and Breakfast: This guest house is located in the city center and features cozy rooms and a rooftop restaurant.
  • OYO Rooms: For budget travelers, OYO rooms offer clean and comfortable rooms at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Shillong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shillong is famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and unique local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in the city:

  • Hand-woven Shawls: These colorful shawls are made using traditional weaving techniques and are unique to the region.
  • Bamboo Handicrafts: Bamboo is widely available in Meghalaya, and you can find handicrafts made of the material in the local markets.
  • Spices and Tea: Meghalaya’s fertile soil makes it ideal for growing a variety of spices and tea. Be sure to bring back some black pepper, turmeric, and syngew tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Shillong

Shillong is home to several indigenous tribes, and their customs and traditions are evident in the city’s way of life. One of the central practices is the matrilineal system, where property and inheritance pass through the female line. The Khasi tribe, in particular, is known for their distinct music and dance forms, and you can see them perform during various festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Shillong

The best time to visit Shillong is from October to June when the weather is pleasant with mild temperatures. The monsoon season from July to September sees heavy rainfall, which often leads to landslides and road closures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shillong

When visiting Shillong, here are some things you should keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry enough cash as there are limited ATMs in the city.
  • Try the unique local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Do not drink tap water.
  • Do not litter in public places.
  • Do not disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Do not take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Shillong, here are some things to keep in mind for your safety and health:

Safety in Shillong

  • Shillong is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, it’s always recommended to travel in groups and take standard precautions like safeguarding your valuables and documents.
  • If you are trekking or going for outdoor adventures, it’s essential to follow precautions and take proper gear.

Health in Shillong

  • Shillong’s altitude can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. It’s preferable to take sufficient breaks and consume enough fluids.
  • it’s recommended to carry enough medications for minor ailments like fever and cold.

Getting to Shillong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shillong

The nearest airport to Shillong is in Guwahati, about 100 km away. From Guwahati, you can hire a taxi or take a shared vehicle like a bus or cab to Shillong.

Bus Stations Near Shillong

There are several bus stations in and around Shillong, including:

  • Shillong Bus Station
  • Guwahati Bus Station
  • Silchar Bus Station

Other Options

Sharing taxis and rental cars are two common ways to get around in the Northeast region. There are several tourist taxis available for rent each day.

How to Get Around in Shillong

Shillong’s city center is relatively walkable, and you can easily walk around to explore the area. If you prefer a more comfortable ride, you can hire a cab or auto-rickshaw. Public transportation, including buses, is reliable and affordable.

Where to Go Next from Shillong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shillong is well-connected to other destinations in Meghalaya, and several exciting excursions await:

  • Cherrapunjee: This charming little town is known for its waterfalls, caves, and unique root bridge.
  • Dawki: This small border town is known for its clear blue waters, making it an excellent place for nature lovers and water-sports enthusiasts.
  • Mawsynram: Another popular destination near Shillong, Mawsynram, is home to some of the wettest places on earth and features stunning landscapes and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Shillong is a charming and laid-back destination, perfect for a relaxing holiday amidst nature. The city offers plenty of exciting activities and attractions, including natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set for an unforgettable trip to Shillong.
