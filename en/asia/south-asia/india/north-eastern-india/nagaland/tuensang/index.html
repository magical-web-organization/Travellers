Tuensang Travel Guide - Plan an amazing trip to Tuensang

Tuensang is a beautiful town located in Nagaland, in North-Eastern India. It is situated near the Myanmar border and is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will share everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Tuensang.

List of Places to Visit in Tuensang

Tuensang is a beautiful town with a rich history and culture. There are many places to explore in Tuensang, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Changsangmongko
  • Chungliyimti
  • Noklak
  • Ngam
  • Sangsangnyu

Natural Attractions of Tuensang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Saramati
  • Intanki Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Khonoma Village
  • Dzukou Valley
  • Japfu Peak

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tuensang

Tuensang is surrounded by beautiful hills and mountains. There are many hiking and biking routes that you can explore. Some popular trails are:

  • Tuensang to Changsangmongko
  • Tuensang to Pongo village
  • Tuensang to Ngam village
  • Tuensang to Noklak village

Best Things to Do in Tuensang: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the town’s rich history and culture by visiting museums and historical places
  • Go on a jungle trek to the Intanki Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Climb to the top of Mount Saramati for breathtaking views of the town
  • Visit the Dzukou Valley, famous for its enchanting landscape and blooming flowers
  • Enjoy a scenic drive along the Khonoma village

Week-Long Itinerary for Tuensang

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Tuensang:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival, check-in, and visit to the local market
Day 2 Visit the Changsangmongko historical place and Ngam village
Day 3 Go on a trek to the Intanki Wildlife Sanctuary
Day 4 Visit Noklak village and Sangsangnyu village
Day 5 Drive to the Dzukou Valley and back to Tuensang
Day 6 Climb to the top of Mount Saramati and enjoy the views
Day 7 Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Tuensang

Tuensang has a unique cuisine that is a mix of Naga, North Indian, and Burmese cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes in Tuensang:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Pork
  • Fish Stew
  • Bamboo Shoot Fry
  • Aloo Mutter
  • Biryani

Local Beverages

  • Zutho (rice beer)
  • Thutse (tea)
  • Coffee

Where to Stay in Tuensang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tuensang has several budget-friendly and high-end accommodation options. Some popular ones are:

  • Hotel Livigstone
  • Tuyang Hotel
  • Tuensang Tourist Lodge

What to Buy in Tuensang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring back from Tuensang are:

  • Handmade Naga shawls
  • Traditional Naga jewelry
  • Tribal handicrafts
  • Bamboo products

Local Culture and Customs of Tuensang

The people of Tuensang are warm and welcoming. They follow a unique culture and tradition that is very different from mainstream Indian culture. You can witness the unique culture by attending the festivals and fairs celebrated here, such as the Aoling Festival.

Best Time to Visit Tuensang

The best time to visit Tuensang is between the months of October and March. The weather during this time is pleasant with temperatures ranging from 5°C to 20°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tuensang

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry warm clothes, as it can get chilly
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Carry or consume any illegal substance
  • Smoke in public places
  • Wear immodest clothes

Safety and Health

Safety in Tuensang

Tuensang is generally a safe place to travel to, but it is still recommended to be cautious of pickpockets and thefts in crowded areas.

Health in Tuensang

It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid tap water to prevent any stomach illnesses. Do carry a first aid kit and insect repellent as a precaution.

Getting to Tuensang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tuensang is well-connected by road to Dimapur, which is the nearest airport and the commercial hub of Nagaland. You can also opt for taxis, buses or shared Jeeps.

Airports Near Tuensang

The nearest airport to Tuensang is Dimapur Airport, which is located about 300 km away.

Bus Stations Near Tuensang

You can catch a bus to Tuensang from Kohima, which is the capital of Nagaland, or from Imphal, which is the capital of Manipur.

Other Options

You can also hire a private taxi to reach Tuensang from Dimapur or Kohima. It is advisable to book a taxi in advance.

How to Get Around in Tuensang

Private taxis and shared Jeeps are the most common modes of transport in Tuensang. Do bargain and fix the price beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Tuensang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations from Tuensang are:

  • Mokokchung
  • Mon
  • Kohima

Conclusion

Tuensang is an offbeat destination that has something for everyone. From natural attractions to cultural experiences, it offers a unique travel experience that is hard to find anywhere else. Plan a trip to Tuensang, and we guarantee that you will return with unforgettable memories.
