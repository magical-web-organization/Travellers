Introduction to Dimapur

Dimapur is an enchanting city in North-Eastern India, located in the state of Nagaland. It is the largest city in Nagaland and an important transportation hub in the region. The city offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture making it a must-visit destination in India.

List of Places to Visit in Dimapur

If you are looking for a new place to explore, Dimapur is an excellent option. Here are some of the best places to visit in Dimapur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kachari Ruins - Also known as the Dimapur stone carvings, these ruins hold testimony to the Ahom civilization that existed in the region. The ruins of the Kachari kingdom in Dimapur are over 800 years old and include 52 pillars that bear images of deities and mythical animals.

  2. Nagaland Science Centre - Located in the heart of the city, this science museum offers visitors an interactive and engaging environment to learn about science and technology.

  3. Chumukedima Village - Located in the hills outside of Dimapur, Chumukedima Village is home to the Ao tribe. The village is known for its architecture, handloom, handicrafts, and the hospitality of its people.

Natural Attractions of Dimapur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Shilloi Lake - Situated in the Phek district of Nagaland, Shilloi Lake is a natural freshwater lake surrounded by evergreen forests. It is considered one of the most beautiful lakes in India, and visitors can enjoy a boat ride or a picnic by the lake.

  2. Dimapur Green Belt - A perfect escape from the hustle-bustle of the city, this park offers a serene environment with lush greenery and fresh air. It is an ideal place for nature lovers and bird watchers.

  3. Rangapahar Reserve Forest - Situated on the outskirts of Dimapur, this reserve forest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The forest includes two trekking trails, the Hornbill Trek and Blyth’s Trek, which offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dimapur

If you are a fan of outdoor activities, Dimapur offers an array of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Medziphema-Diphupar Trail - This trail offers a beautiful view of the countryside and passes through rustic villages and lush greenery.

  2. Signal Basti Forest Trail - This trail passes through dense forest and offers a scenic view of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Tuli-Dimapur Trail - This trail is ideal for seasoned bikers and hikers who want to experience the rugged terrain of Nagaland.

Best Things to Do in Dimapur: Activities and Tours

Dimapur is a city of diverse cultures, traditions, and activities. Here are some of the best things to do in Dimapur:

  1. Visit the markets - Dimapur is known for its bustling local markets, where visitors can find a range of souvenirs, handicrafts, and clothes.

  2. Attend a local festival - Nagaland is famous for its tribal festivals, where visitors can experience the region’s rich culture, folk music, and dance.

  3. Take a cooking class - Nagaland is well known for its delicious cuisine. Visitors can take a cooking class to learn how to prepare traditional Naga dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Dimapur

Nagaland has a rich culinary culture, where traditional dishes are the most popular. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Dimapur:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bamboo shoot pickle - A popular pickled dish made by fermenting bamboo shoots with chili, mustard oil, and spices.

  2. Smoked Pork - A signature dish of Nagaland, smoked pork is cooked with fermented soybeans, ginger, garlic, and chili.

  3. Galho - A traditional Naga dish made with rice, vegetables, and meat.

Local Beverages

Nagaland is famous for its local brews, which are made with fermented rice, barley, or millet. Here are some of the most popular local beverages:

  1. Zutho - A fermented rice beer that is widely consumed in Nagaland.

  2. Thutse - A local wine made by fermenting millet.

Where to Stay in Dimapur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dimapur offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dimapur:

  1. Hotel Tragopan - A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city.

  2. The Gateway Hotel - A luxurious hotel with modern amenities and stunning views of the city.

  3. Beehive Hostel - A cozy hostel that offers a comfortable stay for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Dimapur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dimapur is home to local markets where visitors can find a range of souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Dimapur:

  1. Handicrafts - Dimapur is known for its handloom and handicrafts, including shawls, Naga tassels, and jewelry.

  2. Spices - Nagaland is famous for its organic spices, including chili, ginger, garlic, turmeric, and black cardamom.

  3. Tea - Nagaland is known for its high-quality tea, which is grown in the hilly regions of the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Dimapur

Nagaland is rich in culture and tradition, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and manners. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Dimapur:

  1. Dress modestly - Naga people are conservative, and it is best to dress modestly while visiting the region.

  2. Avoid smoking and drinking in public - Nagaland is a dry state, and it is best to avoid smoking and drinking in public places.

  3. Respect local customs - Nagaland has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dimapur

The best time to visit Dimapur is from October to March when the weather is dry and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dimapur

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Try the local cuisine.

  3. Learn about the culture and history of the region.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke or drink in public places.

  2. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  3. Wander alone in isolated places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dimapur

Dimapur is generally safe for travelers, but it is best to exercise caution while traveling alone or at night. It is also essential to ensure the safety of personal belongings.

Health in Dimapur

Nagaland is a hilly region with a tropical climate, and visitors should be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria. It is essential to apply insect repellent, carry a mosquito net, and stay hydrated to avoid health issues.

Getting to Dimapur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dimapur is well-connected to major cities in India by air, rail, and road. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Dimapur

Dimapur Airport connects the city to major cities in India, including Kolkata, Guwahati, and Delhi.

Bus Stations Near Dimapur

Dimapur is well-connected to major cities in the region by road. There are regular bus services from Guwahati, Imphal, and Kohima to Dimapur.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire taxis or cars to reach Dimapur from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Dimapur

There are several options for getting around in Dimapur. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are readily available, and buses connect different parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Dimapur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nagaland is home to several stunning destinations that are easily accessible from Dimapur. Here are some of the best places to visit near Dimapur:

  1. Kohima - The state capital of Nagaland, Kohima offers a glimpse into the Naga culture and history.

  2. Imphal - The capital city of Manipur, Imphal is known for its scenic beauty, historical monuments, and traditional handicrafts.

Conclusion

Dimapur is a beautiful city with a rich history and culture that offers visitors a unique experience. From delicious food to scenic views, hiking trails to cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in this enchanting city. A trip to Dimapur will leave you with unforgettable memories of one of India’s best-kept secrets.
