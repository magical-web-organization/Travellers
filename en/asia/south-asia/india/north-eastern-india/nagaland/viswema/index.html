Introduction to Viswema

Viswema is a peaceful village nestled in the northeast state of Nagaland, India. Surrounded by lush green hills, this quaint little village is home to the Angami tribe. The traditional Angami Naga houses, vibrant festivals, and the indigenous cuisine give tourists a glimpse into the deep-rooted culture and heritage of the region. Viswema offers scenic vistas, natural attractions, historical landmarks, and exciting outdoor adventures, making it a perfect destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Viswema

Viswema is home to several cultural and natural attractions that fascinate tourists.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Angami Naga Heritage Center: The Heritage Center showcases the cultural and traditional aspects of the Angami tribe, including local customs, festivals, attires, and handicrafts.
  • Kohima War Cemetery: Built-in memory of the brave soldiers who lost their lives during the Second World War, the cemetery is a serene place of remembrance.
  • Khonoma Village: Known as the “Green Village of India,” Khonoma emphasizes eco-friendly tourism and sustainable living practices.

Natural Attractions of Viswema: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dzukou Valley: A trekker’s paradise, the Dzukou Valley is a scenic trek route that rewards visitors with scenic vistas, natural landscape, and colorful meadows.
  • Mount Japfu: The second-highest peak in Nagaland, Mount Japfu offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and a fantastic trekking route.
  • Cheszu Waterfall: Surrounded by dense forests, the Cheszu waterfall is a hidden gem where visitors can swim or take refreshing dips in the cool waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Viswema

Viswema is a land of undulating hills, rugged terrain, and exuberant forests that offer several trekking and biking routes.

  • Viswema to Dzukou Valley: A scenic trek that takes visitors through the dense forests, hilly terrain, and colorful meadows of the valley.
  • Khonoma to Dzuleke: A breathtaking trek that takes visitors from Khonoma village to the neighboring village of Dzuleke, providing scenic views of Nagaland’s countryside.
  • Kigwema to Kisama: A moderate trek that takes visitors through the picturesque countryside of Nagaland, passing through traditional Naga villages and paddy fields.

Best Things to Do in Viswema: Activities and Tours

Viswema offers an array of activities and tours that cater to every kind of traveler.

  • Naga Tribal Village Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the traditional Angami Naga village, experiencing the local customs, cuisine, and handicrafts.
  • Jungle Safari: Visitors can witness Nagaland’s rich biodiversity and wildlife on a jungle safari, conducted by the Nagaland Forest Department.
  • Festival Tour: Nagaland is home to numerous cultural festivals such as the Hornbill Festival and Moatsu Festival. Visitors can experience the vibrant cultural heritage of Nagaland by attending these festivals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Viswema

Viswema offers a calm and peaceful environment, allowing visitors to unwind from their busy lives. Here’s a week-long itinerary for Viswema:

Food and Drink Scene of Viswema

Food is an integral part of Nagaland’s culture, and Viswema’s cuisine is a perfect blend of traditional and modern flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Pork with Bamboo Shoots: A typical Naga dish that’s cooked with smoked pork and fresh bamboo shoots, giving it a unique flavor and aroma.
  • Akibiye: A vegetarian dish made with mashed pumpkin and fermented soybean, which is a popular comfort food among the Nagas.
  • Bamboo Steamed Fish: A signature Naga dish where fish is wrapped in banana leaves and steamed inside bamboo tubes.

Local Beverages

  • Zutho: A traditional rice-beer that’s brewed at home and forms a crucial part of Nagaland’s social and cultural life.
  • Thutse: A sweet drink made of fermented millet, which is a popular beverage during the festivals.

Where to Stay in Viswema: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Viswema offers an array of accommodation options such as hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to every traveler’s needs.

  • Kohima Camp: A luxury glamping resort that offers comfortable tents, gourmet food, and exciting outdoor activities, located near Kohima town.
  • Pulebadze Homestay: A cozy homestay built-in traditional Angami architecture that offers visitors a glimpse into the local life and culture.
  • Niraamaya Retreats Aradura: A luxury resort situated amidst lush green forests, providing stunning views of the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Viswema: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Viswema’s tribal artisans are known for their exquisite handicrafts, woven textiles, and colorful dresses, which make perfect souvenirs to take back home.

  • Hornbill Necklace: A handcrafted necklace made with colorful beads that depict the famous Hornbill bird, a symbol of Naga culture.
  • Naga Shawls: Handwoven shawls made of soft wool or silk and colored with natural dye, which are a must-have in any Naga household.
  • Table Runners: Handmade table runners embroidered with intricate tribal designs that give visitors a glimpse into the local craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Viswema

Nagaland is home to several ethnic tribes, each with its customs and cultures. The Angami tribe of Viswema are known for their hospitality, traditional attire, and unique festivals. Visitors must respect the local culture, dress appropriately, and avoid any behavior that may offend the locals.

Best Time to Visit Viswema

The best time to visit Viswema is from October to March when the climate is pleasant, and the temperature ranges from 10 to 20 °C. The Hornbill Festival, held annually in Kohima, is also a great time to visit Nagaland and learn about the region’s culture and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Viswema

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Photograph locals without their permission.
  • Consume drugs or alcohol.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Viswema

Viswema is a safe and peaceful village, but visitors must take precautions and stay alert, especially during the night. Avoid going out alone or in remote areas, and stay in reputable accommodations.

Health in Viswema

The region is prone to malaria, dengue fever, and other mosquito-borne illnesses, so visitors must take the necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites. Always carry a mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Viswema: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Viswema

The nearest airport is the Dimapur airport, which is around 45 km from Viswema. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Viswema

Kohima town is well connected by buses from Guwahati, Imphal, and Dimapur.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or a private car from Kohima town to reach Viswema.

How to Get Around in Viswema

Viswema is a small village that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a taxi or an autorickshaw to go around the neighboring areas.

Where to Go Next from Viswema: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kohima: The capital of Nagaland is around 20 km from Viswema, which offers several cultural and historical attractions such as the Kohima War Cemetery, state museum, and the annual Hornbill Festival.
  • Imphal: The capital of Manipur state is around 118 km from Viswema and is a scenic town, offering several natural and cultural attractions such as the Loktak Lake, Kangla Fort, and the Women’s Market.

Conclusion

Viswema offers a perfect blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure that caters to every type of traveler. A visit to this quiet village in Nagaland is an excellent way to experience the rich heritage and traditions of India’s northeast region.
