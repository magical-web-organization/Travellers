Introduction to Deomali

Nestled in the Arunachal Pradesh state of India, Deomali stands tall at an elevation of 2,007 meters above sea level. The highest peak in the state, it is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Deomali is a quaint town with a rich cultural heritage and stunning views, making it an alluring destination for travelers. In this guide, we’ve compiled a list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, and everything else you need to know to plan a visit to Deomali.

List of Places to Visit in Deomali

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Deomali’s cultural heritage is visible through its various historical places and museums, such as:

  • Kamala Nehru Park: Located in the center of the town, this park is known for its breathtaking views of the surrounding Deomali range. It is a popular picnic spot among the locals and a great place to relax.

  • Deomali Mountain: Hiking lovers should not miss visiting this mountain, which also serves as a pilgrimage spot for the locals. The 3-km trek to the summit will offer you a 360-degree view of the town and its surroundings.

  • Namdapha National Park: Spread over 1,985 sq. km, this park is home to various species of flora and fauna, including the endangered snow leopard and red panda. Trekking, camping, and bird-watching are popular activities here.

Natural Attractions of Deomali: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an adventure seeker, Deomali will not disappoint you as it offers a plethora of outdoor activities and scenic views, such as:

  • Paya-Payi or the Twin Waterfalls: Located near the town, this waterfall cascade serves as a popular picnic spot and is ideal for a refreshing dip in the natural pool.

  • Bhismaknagar Fort: Situated in the nearby town of Hawai, Bhismaknagar Fort is a historical site that offers an insight into the history of the ancient kingdom of Chutia.

  • Mithun and Yak Breeding Farm: Located near the town of Namsai, visitors can witness the breeding of Mithun and Yak, which are native to the region. The breeding farm showcases the traditional methods of breeding, and also offers various local delicacies.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deomali

One of the best ways to explore the beauty of Deomali is by hiking or biking. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Bamboo Trail: This trail is ideal for trekking enthusiasts, as it offers an easy to moderate level trek through the dense forest area.

  • Chillipam Trail: This trail takes you through the lush green forest, providing an opportunity to explore the flora and fauna of the region.

  • Sunpura Jungle Trail: One of the toughest trails, this offers a challenging trek through the jungle, with stunning views of the Deomali range.

Best Things to Do in Deomali: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities that visitors can engage in Deomali, such as:

  • River Rafting: Enjoy the thrill of river rafting on the turbulent waters of the Brahmaputra river.

  • Bird Watching: Deomali is home to several species of rare birds, making it an ideal place for bird watching.

  • Shopping: Deomali offers a great opportunity for handicraft shopping. Visitors can buy traditional handicrafts, including handwoven textile, bamboo and cane products, and wooden carvings.

Food and Drink Scene of Deomali

Must-Try Dishes

Deomali offers a diverse selection of local dishes, with influences from the neighbouring states of Assam and Nagaland. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Apong: A fermented rice alcoholic beverage, Apong is a popular drink among the locals.
  • Bamboo Shoot Fry: Made from bamboo shoots, it is a popular snack and side dish in the region.
  • Gyapa Khazi: A spicy meat curry, usually made with beef or chicken and served with rice.

Local Beverages

Besides Apong, Deomali also offers other local beverages like:

  • Lemon Tea: A refreshing drink made from lemon and tea leaves.
  • Zutho: A millet beer, Zutho is popular among the locals during festive occasions.

Where to Stay in Deomali: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Deomali offers a variety of accommodation options, catering to all types of travellers. From budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts, visitors can choose from a range of options. Some of the popular places to stay include:

  • Deomali Nature Resort: Located a few kilometers from the town, this resort offers stunning views of the Deomali range.
  • Hotel Jora Palace: Located in the heart of the town, this budget-friendly hotel is ideal for budget-conscious travellers.

What to Buy in Deomali: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Deomali boasts a vibrant culture and heritage, with a plethora of traditional handicrafts that visitors can take home as souvenirs. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Handicrafts: Deomali is famous for its handwoven textile, bamboo and cane products, and wooden carvings.
  • Traditional Costumes: Visitors can uplift their wardrobe by buying colorful traditional costumes of the Apatani tribe.
  • Tribal Jewellery: The town is known for its traditional jewellery made by the local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Deomali

Deomali is a tribal town, and visitors should respect and adhere to their customs and traditions. Visitors should be aware that they may be asked to take their shoes off before entering the homes of their hosts. Visitors should also dress modestly and avoid provocative clothing.

Best Time to Visit Deomali

The best time to visit Deomali is from October to April when the weather is mild, and the region experiences less rainfall. The months of November and December are the most pleasant, with cool temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deomali

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry warm clothing, especially during winters
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the region

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting temples or religious places
  • Wandering alone at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Deomali

Deomali is a relatively safe place for tourists but it is suggested to take basic precautions to avoid theft and burglary. Visitors can contact the local police if they need any assistance.

Health in Deomali

Visit Deomali should not pose any health risks if basic health and hygiene principles are followed. Visitors are advised to take precautions against malaria, dengue, and other vector-borne diseases.

Getting to Deomali: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Deomali

The nearest airport to Deomali is Dibrugarh Airport, located at a distance of around 128 km. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to reach Deomali.

Bus Stations Near Deomali

The nearest bus station to Deomali is Roing Bus Station, located around 34 km from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the station to reach Deomali.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Deomali by hiring a private car or taxi from nearby towns or cities.

How to Get Around in Deomali

Deomali is a small town with limited public transport. Visitors can hire a taxi, motorbikes or cars to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Deomali: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Deomali is surrounded by several places to visit. Visitors can explore some of the following places:

  • Tezu: Located around 92 km from Deomali, Tezu is a beautiful small town that offers an insight into the tribal culture of the region.
  • Pasighat: Located around 150 km from Deomali, Pasighat offers breathtaking views of the Siang River and its scenic valleys. It is also a popular hub for trekking and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Deomali offers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural heritage, and lip-smacking cuisines. If you’re looking for an offbeat location to explore, Deomali should be on your must-visit list. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip without any trouble and enjoy every moment of your visit to Deomali.
