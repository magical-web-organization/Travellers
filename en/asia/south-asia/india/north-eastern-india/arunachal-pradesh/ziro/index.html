Introduction to Ziro

Ziro, a small town in the Indian state of Arunachal Pradesh, is a hidden gem that lies amidst rice-fields and rolling hills at a height of around 5,000 feet above sea level. Surrounded by the rugged Himalayas, this serene and tranquil place offers breathtaking views of lush green valleys, streams, and hills that are bound to leave you spellbound. Ziro is an idyllic destination for adventure enthusiasts, culture seekers, or those looking for a peaceful and rejuvenating getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Ziro

Ziro offers a plethora of activities to its visitors. Here are the top ones to explore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Talley Valley Wildlife Sanctuary: A vast expanse of untouched forested land that boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna. It’s famous for its bamboo orchards, ferns, and rare species like the clouded leopard, Himalayan black bear, and the wild yak. Trekking and camping tours are available.

  2. Ziro Puto Hill: A sacred hill that has been venerated by the local Apatani tribe in Ziro. The hillock offers a panoramic view of the town and is also home to the Shiva Lingam, which is believed to be placed in the hill by a saint 500 years ago.

  3. Tarin Fish Farm: A well-maintained fish farm that provides a unique opportunity to witness aquaculture practices in the region. You can also try fishing and catch some fresh and delicious Trout.

Natural Attractions of Ziro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ziro Valley: A picturesque valley with picturesque rice fields, patches of ferns, and bamboo groves. A stroll through the rice fields will offer mesmerizing views of the valley and the rolling hills.

  2. Midey: A wonderful place to watch the sunrise and sunset. The hilltop offers a fantastic view of the lush-green valley and the surrounding hills.

  3. Pine Grove: A serene pine forest that will provide an opportunity to unwind amidst the rustling of the leaves and chirping of the birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ziro

  1. Dolo Mando Trek: A trek that takes you to the top of the Dolo Mando hill, where one can catch a breathtaking view of the surrounding rice fields and the town. The trek runs through a dense forest filled with bamboo groves, ferns, and orchids.

  2. Talley Valley Trek: A two-day trek that takes you through the scenic beauty of the Talley Valley Wildlife Sanctuary. The trek covers viewpoints such as the Pangey Camp and an overnight halt at the Talley Valley campsite.

Best Things to Do in Ziro: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Local Markets: A visit to the local markets in Ziro is a must. Shop for souvenirs or try the regional cuisines such as bamboo shoot pickle, smoked meat, and millet beer.

  2. Participate in Festivals: The local festivals in Ziro are an enthralling experience where you can imbibe in the culture and traditions of the Apatani tribe.

Food and Drink Scene of Ziro

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Apong: A rice beer that is a favourite of the Apatani tribe.

  2. Zan: A winter delicacy made of finger millet, sesame seeds, and ginger.

  3. Pika Pila: A dish made of fermented bamboo shoot that is usually eaten with smoked meat or dried fish.

Local Beverages

  1. Apong: The traditional rice beer made by the locals.

  2. Bamboo Shoot Pickle: Pickle made of bamboo shoot that is cooked and preserved in oil.

Where to Stay in Ziro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Siiro Resort: A cozy and comfortable resort with a fantastic view of Ziro Valley.

  2. Ziro Valley Resort: A peaceful resort that offers a tranquil and rejuvenating experience.

  3. Ziro Holiday-inn: A comfortable stay option that offers good value for money.

What to Buy in Ziro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Handwoven baskets, bags, and bamboo crafts are popular souvenirs from Ziro.

  2. Ornaments: Silver and brass ornaments made by the local artisans are a must-buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Ziro

The Apatani tribe in Ziro is known for its unique customs and traditions. The tribe follows an environmental-friendly approach where they utilize the available resources to promote sustainable and eco-friendly living. Women of the tribe wear facial tattoos known as ‘Apatani Tattoos’. The tribe is known for its hospitality and warm nature, making any visitor feel welcomed.

Best Time to Visit Ziro

The best time to visit Ziro is between October and April when the climate is comfortable, and the skies are clear. Avoid visiting during the rainy season, as the terrains become slippery, making it difficult for treks and hikes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ziro

Do:

  1. Respect the customs and traditions of the local Apatani tribe.

  2. Try the local cuisine and explore the regional markets.

  3. Trek and camp through the scenic beauty of the valley and the surrounding hills.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the local customs or tradition.

  2. Litter in the valley or while trekking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ziro

Ziro is a safe place to visit, although it is always advisable to take precautions while trekking or exploring the surrounding areas. Avoid exploring isolated areas after dark.

Health in Ziro

The water supply in Ziro is limited, and you should also be cautious with the food you eat. Carry necessary medicines and a first-aid kit.

Getting to Ziro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ziro

The nearest international airport is Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport located in Guwahati, which is around 450 km from Ziro.

Bus Stations Near Ziro

There are regular bus services to Ziro from Itanagar, the capital of Arunachal Pradesh. Private taxis are also available from Itanagar.

Other Options

Hiring a private vehicle from Guwahati may be a good option if you want to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Ziro

Hiring a taxi or a bike is the most comfortable way to get around Ziro. Taxis and bikes operate on a rented basis. You can also explore the regions through trekking or walking.

Where to Go Next from Ziro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Itanagar: The capital city of Arunachal Pradesh, where you can explore Ganga Lake, the Ita Fort, and the Mini Zoo.

  2. Majuli: A beautiful destination that boasts of natural beauty and is known for its array of temples, monasteries, and cultural insights.

Conclusion

Ziro is a pristine destination that offers a calm and rejuvenating environment, scenic landscapes, and a glimpse into the rich culture and tradition of the Apatani tribe. The natural beauty of the place, combined with adventure, culture, and food, make Ziro a unique and delightful experience for any traveller. Plan a trip to Ziro for a soulful and memorable vacation amidst nature.
