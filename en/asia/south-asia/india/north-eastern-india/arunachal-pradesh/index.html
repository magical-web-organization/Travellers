Introduction to Arunachal Pradesh

Nestled in the northeastern corner of India, Arunachal Pradesh is a well-kept secret, untouched and unexplored. It is the easternmost state of India, and its name means “land of dawn-lit mountains.” It lies among the Himalayan mountains and shares its borders with Bhutan, Tibet, and Myanmar. It is home to more than 26 major tribes and sub-tribes, each with its own unique customs, culture, and language. Arunachal Pradesh is blessed with unparalleled scenic beauty, a rich culture, and a diverse wildlife. It is the perfect destination for an adventurous traveler looking to get off the beaten track and explore a region still largely unexplored.

List of Places to Visit in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh has something for everyone, from history buffs to nature lovers. Here are some of the must-visit places.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ita Fort

Ita Fort is a fortification in the heart of the city of Itanagar. It was built in the 14th century and is one of the most important historical landmarks in the state.

Jawaharlal Nehru Museum

Located in Itanagar, this museum provides an insight into Arunachal Pradesh’s history, culture and traditions.

Dirang Dzong

The Dirang Dzong is a 17th-century fort, located in the West Kameng district. It was built by the Monpa tribe and is a fine example of their architecture.

Natural Attractions of Arunachal Pradesh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tawang

Tawang is a picturesque town in the West Kameng district, surrounded by snow-clad mountains, meandering streams, and lush green valleys. It is famous for its Buddhist monastery, which is the largest in India.

Namdapha National Park

Namdapha National Park is the third-largest national park in India and is home to an incredible variety of flora and fauna. The park is a biodiversity hotspot and a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts.

Sela Pass

Sela Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass that connects Tawang to the rest of the state. It offers breathtaking views of snow-clad peaks and is a must-visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is a paradise for adventure sports enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hikes and bike routes in the state.

Hiking Routes

Gorichen Peak Trek

The Gorichen Peak Trek is a challenging trek that takes you through scenic landscapes and offers stunning views of Himalayan peaks.

Pangsau Pass Trek

The Pangsau Pass Trek is a moderate trek that takes you through beautiful forests, waterfalls, and meadows.

Biking Routes

Tawang-Bumla Pass

The Tawang-Bumla Pass is a popular bike route that takes you through beautiful mountain landscapes and offers stunning views of the Himalayas.

Tezpur-Bomdila-Tawang

The Tezpur-Bomdila-Tawang bike route is another popular route that takes you through some of the most beautiful spots in Arunachal Pradesh.

Best Things to Do in Arunachal Pradesh: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Arunachal Pradesh offers a host of other activities and tours.

River Rafting on Siang River

The Siang River is one of the most beautiful rivers in Arunachal Pradesh, and river rafting is a popular activity among adventure sports enthusiasts.

Visit the Local Markets

The local markets in Arunachal Pradesh offer a glimpse into the daily life of the locals. Here, you can find a range of unique and interesting local products.

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Arunachal Pradesh.

Food and Drink Scene of Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh has a diverse culinary tradition, with each tribe having its own unique cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Bamboo Shoot Fry

Bamboo Shoot Fry is a popular dish in Arunachal Pradesh, made with tender bamboo shoots, green chilies, garlic, and ginger.

Thukpa

Thukpa is a delicious noodle soup made with vegetables, meat, and/or eggs. It is a popular comfort food in the region.

Local Beverages

Apong

Apong is a local alcoholic drink made from rice or millet. It is a popular drink among the locals and is a must-try for tourists.

Zur

Zur is a local drink made from fermented millet. It is a popular drink during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Arunachal Pradesh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Arunachal Pradesh, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels.

Luxury Hotels

Tawang Regency

Tawang Regency is a luxury hotel in Tawang that offers beautiful views of the snow-clad Himalayas and comfortable rooms with all modern amenities.

Budget Hostels

Budget Tree House Hostel

Budget Tree House Hostel is a popular hostel in Itanagar that offers comfortable and affordable accommodation for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Arunachal Pradesh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arunachal Pradesh is famous for its beautiful handicrafts and textiles. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Carpets

The Monpa tribe of Arunachal Pradesh is famous for its hand-woven carpets made from yak hair, wool, or silk.

Bamboo Products

Bamboo is abundant in Arunachal Pradesh, and the locals make a range of beautiful and functional products from bamboo, such as baskets, cups, and plates.

Local Culture and Customs of Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh has a rich and diverse culture, with distinct customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting.

Dress Code

The locals in Arunachal Pradesh dress conservatively, and tourists are advised to dress modestly and respectfully.

Music and Dance

Music and dance are an integral part of the culture of Arunachal Pradesh, and tourists can witness traditional dance performances during festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Arunachal Pradesh

The best time to visit Arunachal Pradesh is from October to April. The weather during this time is pleasant, and the skies are clear, offering stunning views of the Himalayan peaks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arunachal Pradesh

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without seeking permission.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is generally safe for tourists, but it is advisable to take precautions and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Arunachal Pradesh

It is advisable to take precautions against malaria, especially if traveling to remote areas. It is also recommended to carry a basic medical kit with you.

Getting to Arunachal Pradesh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arunachal Pradesh

Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati is the nearest airport to Arunachal Pradesh.

Bus Stations Near Arunachal Pradesh

Naharlagun and Itanagar are the main bus stations in Arunachal Pradesh.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are available for hire in Guwahati and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Arunachal Pradesh

Private Taxis

Private taxis are the most convenient option for getting around in Arunachal Pradesh.

Shared Taxis

Shared taxis are a cheaper option and are available on most popular routes.

Where to Go Next from Arunachal Pradesh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Assam

Assam is a neighboring state of Arunachal Pradesh, known for its tea plantations, wildlife, and scenic beauty.

Meghalaya

Meghalaya is another neighboring state of Arunachal Pradesh, known for its beautiful waterfalls, caves, and traditional villages.

Conclusion

Arunachal Pradesh is a hidden gem, waiting to be explored. With its unparalleled scenic beauty, rich culture, and diversity, Arunachal Pradesh is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to get off the beaten track and explore a region still largely unexplored. Plan your trip, immerse yourself in the local culture, and make memories that will last a lifetime!
