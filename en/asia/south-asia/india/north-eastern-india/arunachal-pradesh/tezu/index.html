Your Comprehensive Guide to Tezu: Exploring the Land of Hidden Gems

Nestled in the remote state of Arunachal Pradesh in Northeastern India, Tezu is a town that has remained hidden from the public eye for too long. With lush green landscapes, snow-capped mountains, and an intriguing mix of culture and tradition, Tezu is a world apart from the hubbub of the city. Our comprehensive travel guide will take you on a journey through the hidden gem of Tezu.

List of Places to Visit in Tezu

Tezu has a plethora of attractions to offer, from culturally significant museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and stunning natural vistas.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture enthusiasts, Tezu boasts a great assortment of museums and heritage sites to explore. One of the must-visit places in Tezu is the Tezu Museum, which showcases the art, culture, and history of the Mishmi tribe. The Buddha Vihar, a Buddhist temple, is also a must-visit for its serene ambiance and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tezu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tezu is a paradise for nature lovers, with an array of options for outdoor activities and scenic views. The Kamlang Wildlife Sanctuary is a prime location to catch a glimpse of rare flora and fauna in their natural habitat. Trekking enthusiasts can indulge in hiking expeditions at Glow Lake, while the Botanical Garden offers a tranquil retreat amidst the chaotic city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tezu

Tezu is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hiking and biking routes that provide unforgettable experiences. Some popular routes include the Tezu-Digaru hiking trail or the Tezu-Wakro bike route that offers scenic views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Tezu: Activities and Tours

For those seeking adventure, Tezu offers a range of exciting activities and tours. Adventure lovers can try their hand at river rafting, kayaking or rock climbing at the Komsing river. One can also opt-in for a guided expedition to the Miao village for an immersive cultural experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tezu

Day 1: Arriving in Tezu and visiting the Buddha Vihar Day 2: Exploring the Tezu Museum and the Botanical Garden Day 3: Hiking or Biking through the scenic routes Day 4: Visiting Glow Lake and Kamlang Wildlife Sanctuary Day 5: River rafting, kayaking or rock climbing at the Komsing river Day 6: Guided expedition to the Miao village Day 7: Departing from Tezu

Food and Drink Scene of Tezu

Tezu offers an array of culinary delights that are authentic to the region. The Apong, a rice beer, and the Mishmi delicacy of smoked pork are some of the local dishes that one must-try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apong: Traditional rice beer
  • Medu Lamu: A sweet snack made from rice flour and jaggery
  • Smoked Pork: A local delicacy

Local Beverages

  • Apong: Traditional rice beer
  • Thukpa: A local soup made from vegetables and noodles

Where to Stay in Tezu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tezu offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of our recommendations include the Mishmi Hills Resort, Hotel Abasa, and Circuit House Tezu.

What to Buy in Tezu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tezu is famous for its handicrafts, and shopping is an experience in itself. Some of the must-buy items include hand-woven Mishmi shawls, bamboo and cane products, and traditional handcrafted pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Tezu

Tezu has a unique culture that is a fusion of Hinduism, Buddhism, and animistic beliefs. The Mishmi tribe is the main indigenous community of the region, and their customs and traditions are an integral part of the region.

Best Time to Visit Tezu

The best time to visit Tezu is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and mild. Summers can be quite humid, and monsoons can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tezu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Carry enough cash, as ATM facilities may be limited
  • Try local food and visit local markets

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Click photographs without taking permission
  • Litter and harm the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Tezu

Tezu is a safe destination, but one must take necessary precautions while venturing into remote areas. Avoid traveling alone and maintain caution while interacting with strangers.

Health in Tezu

Travelers need to be cautious and take necessary vaccines before visiting Tezu, as it is situated in a rural part of India. Also, carry adequate medication for common concerns such as diarrhea or altitude sickness.

Getting to Tezu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tezu is accessible via air and roadways. The nearest airport is Mohanbari Airport in Dibrugarh, while the closest railway station is Tinsukia. One can also take a bus or hire a taxi from nearby cities like Guwahati, Dibrugarh, or Tinsukia.

Airports Near Tezu

  • Mohanbari Airport in Dibrugarh

Bus Stations Near Tezu

  • Mayu-II Bus Station in Tezu
  • Super Bus Station in Co-operative

Other Options

  • Taxis and private cabs are available from nearby cities

How to Get Around in Tezu

Tezu is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or using public transportation like auto-rickshaws or local buses. Taxis can also be hired for a day trip to nearby locations.

Where to Go Next from Tezu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore beyond Tezu, some nearby attractions include:

  • Pasighat
  • Dibrugarh
  • Namdapha National Park

Conclusion

With its serene ambiance, rustic beauty, and varied attractions, Tezu is a hidden gem in Northeast India. Whether you are seeking adventures in the great outdoors or immersing yourself in the region’s culture, Tezu has something special to offer. Follow our comprehensive guide to discover the wonders of Tezu and experience the charm of Arunachal Pradesh like never before.
