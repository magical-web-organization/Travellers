The Ultimate Travel Guide to Lunglei, Mizoram

Are you looking for an offbeat destination for your next vacation? Look no further than Lunglei, a serene hill station nestled in the northeast state of Mizoram in India. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to Lunglei.

List of Places to Visit in Lunglei

Lunglei offers a mix of culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khawthlang Tuipui: A scenic waterfall that is considered sacred by the locals, located 5 km from Lunglei.
  • Thuamluaia Mual: A village that showcases the traditional way of life of the Lusei tribe, including bamboo weaving and pottery making.
  • Saza Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to several endangered species, including Hoolock Gibbons and Barking Deer.
  • Old Lunglei Village: A historic village that offers a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Mizoram.

Natural Attractions of Lunglei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lunglei Zoological Park: A small zoo that houses several species of animals and birds from Mizoram.
  • Lunglei Tlang: A beautiful hill that offers breathtaking views of the valley below.
  • Serlui B Dam: A serene reservoir where you can enjoy boating and fishing.
  • Benreu Peak: A popular hiking spot that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lunglei

Lunglei is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Lunglei to Tlibung: A scenic road trip that takes you through hills and valleys.
  • Vantawng Hriatrengna Lung: A beautiful trek that takes you to the stunning Vantawng waterfall.
  • Dentha Village Trail: A scenic bike ride that takes you through forests and fields.

Best Things to Do in Lunglei: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Lunglei. Here are the top activities and tours:

  • Traditional Dance Performance: Watch a live cultural performance by the locals.
  • Handicraft Shopping: Buy unique handicrafts, such as bamboo baskets and pottery, made by the locals.
  • Fishing: Catch some fish at the serene Serlui B Dam.
  • Boating: Enjoy a peaceful boat ride on the reservoir.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lunglei

If you’re planning to spend a week in Lunglei, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Khawthlang Tuipui and Serlui B Dam
Day 2 Explore Old Lunglei Village and Thuamluaia Mual

Day 6 Take a road trip from Lunglei to Tlibung
Day 7 Relax and unwind at Lunglei Tlang

Food and Drink Scene of Lunglei

No trip is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here’s what to eat and drink in Lunglei:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bai: A traditional stew made with vegetables and meat.
  • Misa Mach Poora: A spicy fish curry made with mustard oil.
  • Sa-um: A chutney made with fermented bamboo shoot.

Local Beverages

  • Zu: A traditional rice beer that is mildly alcoholic.
  • Cha: A hot tea made with milk and sugar.

Where to Stay in Lunglei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Lunglei, ranging from budget to luxury. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Floria: A budget hotel located in the heart of Lunglei.
  • Colonial Guest House: A colonial-style guest house with clean rooms and great views.
  • Marsemzawl Resort: A luxury resort located amidst pristine forests.

What to Buy in Lunglei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home some unique souvenirs, here’s what to buy in Lunglei:

  • Bamboo baskets: Handwoven baskets made by the locals.
  • Pottery: Handcrafted pottery, including tea cups and bowls.
  • Traditional shawls: Woven shawls made with traditional patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Lunglei

Lunglei is home to several ethnic tribes with unique customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs: Dress modestly and ask for permission before taking photographs of the locals.
  • Be polite: The locals are friendly and welcoming, so it’s important to be respectful.
  • Learn some local phrases: The locals speak Mizo, so it’s helpful to learn a few phrases to communicate with them.

Best Time to Visit Lunglei

The best time to visit Lunglei is from October to March, when the weather is cool and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lunglei

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Lunglei:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Carry warm clothing, as the weather can get chilly.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Hike and bike to explore the natural beauty of Lunglei.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Travel alone at night.

Safety and Health

Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Lunglei:

Safety in Lunglei

  • Lunglei is a safe place, but it’s important to take precautions against theft and pickpocketing.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit areas.
  • Carry a first-aid kit and insect repellent.

Health in Lunglei

  • Lunglei is a malaria endemic area, so take appropriate precautions.
  • Carry necessary medications, as there are limited medical facilities in Lunglei.
  • Drink only bottled water, and avoid street food.

Getting to Lunglei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lunglei is located about 170 km from Aizawl, the capital of Mizoram. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Lunglei

The nearest airport is Lengpui Airport in Aizawl, which is well-connected to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Lunglei

Lunglei has a bus station that is well-connected to Aizawl and other towns in Mizoram.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Lunglei from Aizawl.

How to Get Around in Lunglei

The best way to get around Lunglei is by hiring a taxi or renting a bike.

Where to Go Next from Lunglei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, here are some nearby destinations to explore:

  • Aizawl: The capital of Mizoram, known for its vibrant culture and delicious food.
  • Phawngpui: The highest peak in Mizoram, known for its stunning views and trekking.
  • Silchar: A picturesque town in Assam, known for its tea plantations and colonial architecture.

Conclusion

From scenic waterfalls to traditional villages, Lunglei has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and discover the hidden gems of this beautiful hill station.
