Introduction to Champhai

Champhai is a picturesque town located in the state of Mizoram, in North-Eastern India. The town is known for its scenic beauty, vibrant culture, and warm hospitality. Surrounded by verdant hills and stunning valleys, Champhai is the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Champhai, including the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and culture to experience.

List of Places to Visit in Champhai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mura Puk - A historical fort constructed during the reign of Sib Singh, the king of Mizoram in the 18th century.
  • Rihdil Lake - A stunning lake located on the Myanmar border known for its crystal-clear waters.
  • Kungawrhi Puk - A cave complex located near the India-Myanmar border that was used as a hideout during World War II.
  • Thasiama Seno Neihna - A cultural village that showcases the traditions and customs of the Mizo people.
  • Zokhawthar - A small town located on the India-Myanmar border that is famous for its market selling traditional Mizo handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Champhai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lamsial Puk - A beautiful cave featuring stalactites, stalagmites, and underground streams.
  • Lunglei - Tlabung - A scenic road trip that passes through lush green forests and picturesque villages.
  • Thasiama Se’Noo Neihna - A village that offers a glimpse into the traditional ways of Mizo life, including handloom weaving, basketry, and cane work.
  • Murlen National Park - A wildlife sanctuary that is home to rare and endangered species like the Hoolock Gibbon, Sambar Deer, and Tiger.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Champhai

  • Dampa Wildlife Sanctuary - Phawngpui Peak Trek
  • Phawngpui Peak Trek
  • Saiha - Phura - Laki - Lungpho - Phawngpui
  • Saza Wildlife Sanctuary Trek

Best Things to Do in Champhai: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Local Markets - The local markets of Champhai are famous for their handicrafts, fresh produce, and traditional Mizo cuisine.
  • Go Trekking and Camping - The hills around Champhai offer plenty of opportunities for trekking and camping, providing vistas of stunning views.
  • Try the Local Cuisine - Mizo cuisine is famous for its distinctive flavors, which include smoked meats, bamboo shoots, and fish.
  • Attend a Local Festival - Champhai is known for its vibrant festivals, which provide a glimpse into the traditions and customs of the Mizo people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Champhai

Day 1: Arrival and sightseeing to places like Mura Puk and Thasiama Seno Neihna. Day 2: Trekking through the beautiful Lunglei - Tlabung road and visiting local markets. Day 3-4: Biking through the Saiha - Phura - Laki - Lungpho - Phawngpui route, and camping at the summit. Day 5: Exploring the natural surroundings with Murlen National Park and Saza Wildlife Sanctuary Trek. Day 6: Visiting the Rihdil lake and Kungawrhi Puk cave. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Champhai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bai: A dish made of steamed vegetables, pork, and bamboo shoots, wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Vawksa Rep: A dish made of smoked pork cooked in a variety of spices and served with bamboo-shoot pickles.
  • Sawhchiar: A dish made of black rice with meat roasted in bamboo tubes.
  • Paanch Phoron Torkari: A vegetable curry flavoured with a unique blend of five spices.

Local Beverages

  • Zawlaidi: Made from fermented rice.
  • Chhang: A fermented millet or rice brew.
  • Zu: A rice wine.

Where to Stay in Champhai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ritz Hotel: A comfortable hotel with all modern amenities located in the heart of Champhai.
  • Hotel Olive: A luxury hotel offering stunning valley views and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Elk Hotel: A budget-friendly option featuring clean and comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.
  • Dormitory Style Accommodation: Guesthouses offering affordable hostel-type accommodations are also available in Champhai.

What to Buy in Champhai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Traditional Mizo handicrafts include bamboo and cane work, handloom weaving, and beadwork.
  • Textiles: Mizo textiles are famous for their unique designs and vibrant colors.
  • Spices: Local spices like cinnamon, black pepper, and cardamom are available in the markets of Champhai.

Local Culture and Customs of Champhai

The Mizo people are known for their rich culture and traditions. These include dance and music characterized by the use of unique instruments like the Chhepu and Khuang, along with their distinctive costumes.

Best Time to Visit Champhai

The best time to visit Champhai is between October and May, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities. The monsoon season from June to September is best avoided.

Do’s and Don’ts of Champhai

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry sufficient warm clothing if visiting in winters.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take necessary permits and permissions for trekking and camping.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public places.
  • Do not disturb the local fauna and flora.
  • Do not cause any damage to the natural surroundings.
  • Do not carry or consume any illicit substances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Champhai

Champhai is a relatively safe destination, but travelers should be aware of their surroundings and belongings during their visit.

Health in Champhai

It is advisable to drink only bottled or boiled water during your stay in Champhai. As with any travel, it is recommended to consult a doctor before travel to ensure all necessary vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Champhai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Champhai is well-connected with major cities of Mizoram and India via road and air.

Airports Near Champhai

The nearest airport to Champhai is Lengpui Airport, located at a distance of 200 km.

Bus Stations Near Champhai

Champhai is well-connected by bus to major cities like Aizawl, Silchar, Kolkata, and Guwahati.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire from Aizawl and Silchar to reach Champhai.

How to Get Around in Champhai

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are the primary mode of transport in Champhai.

Where to Go Next from Champhai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aizawl: The capital of Mizoram, known for its vibrant culture and stunning vistas.
  • Silchar: A historical town in Assam, famous for its tea gardens and natural beauty.
  • Agartala: The capital of Tripura, known for its cultural heritage and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Champhai is a destination that offers something for everyone - adventure, culture, history, and food. With its stunning natural beauty and warm hospitality, Champhai is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to discover the hidden treasures of North-East India. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has been useful in planning your visit to Champhai. Plan your visit today and experience the magic of Champhai!
