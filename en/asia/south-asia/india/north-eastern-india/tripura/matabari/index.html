Introduction to Matabari

Matabari is a small town located in the state of Tripura, in North-Eastern India. Known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, this town is still relatively unknown to tourists, making it an off-the-beaten-path destination to explore. The town is home to the famous Tripura Sundari Temple, which attracts thousands of devotees every year. Apart from the temple, Matabari offers beautiful landscapes, scenic views, and a vibrant local culture to explore.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the hidden gems of Matabari, covering everything from must-visit places and best activities to do to food and culture. So, pack your bags and get ready to embark on a journey of a lifetime.

List of Places to Visit in Matabari

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tripura Sundari Temple - The most iconic attraction in Matabari, the Tripura Sundari Temple is also known as the Tripureswari Temple. It’s a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus and is dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari, who is revered as one of the 51 Shakti Peethas.
  2. Ujjayanta Palace - Visit the Ujjayanta Palace to take a peek into the royal history of Tripura. Built-in 1901, the palace now serves as a museum, where you can see several exhibits on Tripura’s art, culture, and history.
  3. Neermahal Water Palace - Located on the Rudrasagar Lake, Neermahal Water Palace is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Tripura. It’s a beautiful palace built-in 1930 and has a mix of Hindu and Muslim architectural styles.
  4. Kunjaban Palace - Kunjaban Palace, previously the summer residence of Tripura’s Maharajas, is another must-visit attraction in the city. The palace is located amidst lush greenery and offers scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Natural Attractions of Matabari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary - The wildlife sanctuary located near Matabari is a must-visit for nature lovers. It’s home to a wide range of animals, including primates, deer, and reptiles.
  2. Dumboor Lake - Dumboor Lake is another popular tourist spot located near Matabari. It’s a natural lake surrounded by hills and forests and offers beautiful views, especially during sunrise and sunset.
  3. Baramura Eco Park - The Baramura Eco Park is located on the Baramura hills and is a perfect spot for trekking and hiking. The park also offers stunning views of the surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matabari

  1. Kunjaban Trail - This hiking trail takes you through the picturesque Kunjaban Palace and its lush green surroundings. It’s a moderately difficult trek, with some uphill climbs and is perfect for nature lovers.
  2. Baramura Trek - The Baramura Trek is a challenging and scenic trek that takes you through the Baramura hills. The trail is about 18 km long and is not recommended for beginners.
  3. Dumboor Trek - The Dumboor Trek is a moderate trek that takes you to the Dumboor Lake. It’s a relatively easy hike, with some uphill climbs, and offers stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Matabari: Activities and Tours

  1. Boating in Rudrasagar Lake - Take a scenic boat ride in Rudrasagar Lake and explore its beautiful surroundings.
  2. Bamboo Craft Workshop - Attend a bamboo craft workshop and learn about the traditional art of bamboo crafting.
  3. Sightseeing Tours - Take a guided sightseeing tour and explore Matabari’s famous attractions, including the Tripura Sundari Temple, Ujjayanta Palace, and Neermahal Water Palace.

Food and Drink Scene of Matabari

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chakhwi - A traditional Tripuri dish made with pork and ground spices; it’s a must-try for meat lovers.
  2. Wakching Bora - A crispy, fried Tripuri snack made with rice flour and gram flour.
  3. Muya Awandru - Muya Awandru is a vegetarian Tripuri dish made with chana dal and mixed vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Chouk - A traditional Tripuri rice beer, it’s a must-try for those who enjoy a good brew.
  2. Zawlaidi - A refreshing, non-alcoholic beverage made with lemon and other local fruits.

Where to Stay in Matabari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  2. Ginger Hotel - Ginger Hotel offers modern amenities, comfortable rooms, and excellent service. It’s located near Matabari’s famous attractions and public transportation.
  3. Rajarshi Lodge - Rajarshi Lodge offers affordable, comfortable rooms, and is located near the town’s famous attractions.

What to Buy in Matabari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bamboo Crafts - Matabari is known for its traditional bamboo crafts, including baskets, mats, and furniture.
  2. Handwoven Textiles - Tripura is known for its handwoven textiles, including sarees, stoles, and shawls.
  3. Spices - Tripura is home to several spices, including cinnamon, cardamom, and coriander. Bring home some local spices to add flavor to your meals.

Local Culture and Customs of Matabari

Matabari’s local culture is heavily influenced by its indigenous tribes, including the Tripuri, Reang, and Chakma. The town is known for its traditional music and dance, including the Wangala Dance and Garia Dance. The residents of Matabari are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are welcome to participate in local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Matabari

The best time to visit Matabari is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the region’s natural beauty is at its best. The monsoon season lasts from June to September and can make hiking and other outdoor activities difficult due to heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matabari

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Chouk and Chakhwi.
  3. Visit the Tripura Sundari Temple, Ujjayanta Palace, and Neermahal Water Palace.
  4. Attend a traditional dance or music performance.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious places.
  2. Throw garbage on the streets, and always clean up after yourself.
  3. Ignore safety precautions while hiking or trekking in the hills.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matabari

Matabari is a safe destination for tourists, and crime rates are relatively low. However, it’s always recommended to exercise caution while traveling and abide by safety guidelines.

Health in Matabari

Matabari is a malaria-endemic region, and travelers are advised to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites. Visitors are also advised to carry mosquito repellent, sunscreen, and other necessary medications while traveling.

Getting to Matabari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matabari

  1. Agartala Airport - Located about 60 km from Matabari, Agartala airport is the nearest airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Matabari

  1. Agartala Bus Terminal - Agartala Bus Terminal is located about 60 km from Matabari and offers regular bus services to the town.

Other Options

  1. Private Taxis - Private taxis are available for hire from Agartala to Matabari.
  2. Trains - It’s also possible to take a train from Agartala to Kumarghat, which is the nearest railway station to Matabari.

How to Get Around in Matabari

The best way to get around Matabari is by hiring a taxi or a private car. Buses and autos are also available, but they may not be the most convenient option for travelers.

Where to Go Next from Matabari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Jampui Hills - Known for its scenic views and pristine natural beauty, Jampui Hills is a must-visit destination near Matabari.
  2. Agartala - The capital city of Tripura, Agartala, offers several attractions, including the Ujjayanta Palace and the Tripura Sundari Temple.
  3. Unakoti - Located near Matabari, Unakoti is known for its ancient rock-cut figures and offers a fascinating glimpse into Tripura’s ancient history and culture.
