Introduction to North-Eastern India

North-Eastern India is a region that comprises eight states: Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura, and Sikkim. Each state is distinct in culture, language, and topography, making it a fascinating region to explore. It is located in the easternmost part of India and is surrounded by Bhutan, Tibet, Myanmar and Bangladesh.

Local Culture and Customs of North-Eastern India

North-Eastern India is known for its diverse ethnic and cultural heritage. Here are some of the culture and customs worth knowing before your trip:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and monasteries
  • Remove your shoes before entering sacred places
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Best Time to Visit North-Eastern India

The best time to visit North-Eastern India is during the winter months from November to March, when the weather is pleasant and dry. Avoid the monsoon season from June to September.

Safety and Health

Safety in North-Eastern India

North-Eastern India is generally safe for tourists, with very low crime rates. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions like not walking alone in isolated areas after dark.

Health in North-Eastern India

It’s advisable to take necessary vaccinations before visiting North-Eastern India, especially if you’re traveling during monsoon season. Also, carry medications for altitude sickness, as some areas in North-Eastern India are located at high elevations.
