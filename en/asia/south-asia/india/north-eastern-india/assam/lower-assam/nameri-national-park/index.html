Introduction to Nameri National Park

Nameri National Park is a scenic natural reserve located in the heart of Assam, in North-Eastern India. Spread across 200 sq km, the park is nestled between the eastern Himalayas and the Brahmaputra River. Nameri National Park is home to various species of flora and fauna, including the rare white-winged wood duck, elephant, tiger, and leopard.

In this travel guide, we will take a closer look at the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Nameri National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Nameri National Park

Nameri National Park has a lot to offer for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Here is a list of places to visit in the park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Doebe Tea Estate Museum: Learn about the history of tea in Assam at this museum located within the Doebe Tea Estate.
  • Bhalukpong Fort: This fort dates back to the 10th century and is an excellent example of ancient architecture.

Natural Attractions of Nameri National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nameri River: Enjoy kayaking or rafting along the Nameri River and take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Jia Bhoroli River: This river is famous for fishing, especially for the golden mahseer.
  • Trekking: There are several trekking routes in the park, including the Bodo Langai Trek and the Nameri Eco Camp Trek.
  • Wildlife Safaris: Take a jeep or elephant safari to explore the park and see its diverse wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nameri National Park

For adventure enthusiasts, Nameri National Park offers several hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Bodo Langai Trek: This 6 km trek takes you through dense forests and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Nameri Eco Camp Trek: This 8 km trek starts from the eco-camp and takes you through lush green forests to a natural pool.
  • Biking Trails: There are several biking trails available in the park. Choose one that suits your fitness and skill level.

Best Things to Do in Nameri National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Elephant Safari: Take an elephant safari to explore the park and spot its diverse wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and leopards.
  • River Rafting: Go for a thrilling rafting experience on the Nameri River and enjoy the scenery.
  • Bird Watching: Nameri National Park is a paradise for bird watchers, with over 300 species of birds to spot.
  • River Crossing: Cross the Jia Bhoroli River on a rope bridge for a fun adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nameri National Park

If you are planning to spend a week in Nameri National Park, here is a sample itinerary to follow:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the eco-camp. Rest and relax.
  • Day 2: Trek to Bodo Langai and explore the forests.
  • Day 3: Bird watching in the morning and elephant safari in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: River rafting on the Nameri River.
  • Day 5: Trek to the natural pool and enjoy swimming.
  • Day 6: River crossing and fishing on the Jia Bhoroli River.
  • Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Nameri National Park

Assamese food is known for its unique blend of spices and flavors. Here are some must-try dishes in Nameri National Park:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bamboo Shoot Fry: This dish is made with fresh bamboo shoots and is flavored with garlic, chilies, and mustard oil.
  • Fish Curry: Assamese fish curry is tangy and spicy, made with local herbs and spices.
  • Duck Roast: This dish is made with tender, juicy duck meat and flavored with ginger, garlic, and green chilies.

Local Beverages

  • Apong: Apong is a local rice beer made from fermented rice and is served in bamboo tubes.
  • Doi Chira: This is a traditional Assamese breakfast dish made with flattened rice and yogurt.

Where to Stay in Nameri National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nameri National Park offers a range of accommodation options for travelers. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Nameri Eco Camp: This is the only accommodation option inside the park and offers tents and cottages.
  • Jia Bhorelli Wild Resort: This resort is situated on the banks of the Jia Bhoroli River and offers comfortable cottages.
  • La Vue Resort: This resort is located outside the park and offers luxury cottages and tents.

What to Buy in Nameri National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some options:

  • Assam Tea: Assam is famous for its tea, and you can buy different varieties of tea in Nameri National Park.
  • Bamboo and Cane Products: Bamboo and cane products are popular in Assam, and you can buy baskets, trays, hats, and other items.
  • Handloom Products: Assam is known for its handloom products, including silk and cotton sarees and shawls.

Local Culture and Customs of Nameri National Park

Assam has a rich cultural heritage, and there are several festivals celebrated in the state throughout the year. Bihu is the most famous festival, celebrated in April, which marks the beginning of the Assamese New Year. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is easy to strike up a conversation with them.

Best Time to Visit Nameri National Park

The best time to visit Nameri National Park is from November to April when the weather is pleasant, and the park is open for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nameri National Park

Do:

  • Follow the park rules and regulations.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry water bottles and snacks for the trek.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes for the trek.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the park.
  • Don’t carry any plastic bottles or bags.
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife or their habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nameri National Park

Nameri National Park is generally safe for visitors. However, it is advisable to take precautions while trekking and hiking in the forests. Always follow the park rules and regulations and avoid entering restricted areas.

Health in Nameri National Park

Make sure to carry insect repellents and sunscreen to protect yourself from mosquito bites and sunburns. It is also advisable to carry a first aid kit with basic medicines, band-aids, and antiseptics.

Getting to Nameri National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nameri National Park is easily accessible by road and air.

Airports Near Nameri National Park

Tezpur Airport is the nearest airport to Nameri National Park, located about 40 km away. Guwahati Airport is another nearby airport, about 190 km away.

Bus Stations Near Nameri National Park

Several buses ply between Tezpur, Guwahati, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or take a private car to reach Nameri National Park.

How to Get Around in Nameri National Park

The best way to get around in Nameri National Park is on foot. Treks and hikes are the best ways to explore the park and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Nameri National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can explore after visiting Nameri National Park:

  • Kaziranga National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is famous for one-horned rhinoceroses and is located about 115 km away from Nameri National Park.
  • Tawang: Tawang is a town located in the Himalayas and is famous for its monasteries and scenic beauty.
  • Guwahati: Guwahati is the largest city in Assam and is famous for its temples, architecture, and local markets.

Conclusion

Nameri National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. With its scenic beauty, rich biodiversity, and range of activities, it offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
