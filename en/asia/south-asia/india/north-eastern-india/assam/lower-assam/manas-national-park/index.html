Introduction to Manas National Park

Manas National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Lower Assam, India. This beautiful park is home to an abundance of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, rhinos, and wild buffalo. It is situated at the foot of the Bhutan Himalayas and is bordered by the Manas River, which runs through the park. The park is well known for its scenic beauty and is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and anyone interested in learning about India’s rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Manas National Park

There are several places to visit in Manas National Park. We’ve categorized them for you to ensure you don’t miss any must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bodo villages - The Bodos are the largest ethnic group in the region and have their own distinct culture and traditions. Visiting one of their villages is an excellent way to learn about their way of life.

  • Manas Maozigendri Ecotourism Society (MMES) - This museum is devoted to the conservation efforts of Manas National Park and its surroundings. It is an excellent resource for learning about the natural and cultural history of the area.

  • Manas National Park Interpretation Centre - This centre is devoted to showcasing the flora and fauna of Manas National Park, as well as its cultural heritage.

  • Barpeta - Barpeta is a town nearby that is known for its ancient temples and monasteries. It is a sacred site for many Hindus.

Natural Attractions of Manas National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wildlife safaris - Manas National Park is home to an abundance of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, rhinos, and wild buffalo. A wildlife safari is an excellent way to see these creatures up close.

  • Bird watching - The park is home to over 450 species of birds, including the great hornbill.

  • Trekking - Trekking is an excellent way to explore the park’s beautiful landscape and scenic views.

  • River Rafting - The Manas River runs through the park, and river rafting is a popular activity among thrill-seekers.

  • Jeep Safari - A jeep safari is a quick and efficient way to explore the park’s rich flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manas National Park

For those who enjoy outdoor activities, Manas National Park has plenty of hiking and biking routes to choose from.

  • Mathanguri Ridge Trek - This trek takes you through some of the park’s most scenic areas, including the Mathanguri ridge, which offers panoramic views of the area.

  • Moigrim Reserve Forest Circuit Trek - This trek takes you through the forest reserve area of the park and is an excellent way to appreciate the park’s rich flora and fauna.

  • Biking Tour of western Assam - A biking tour is an excellent way to see the surrounding areas of Manas National Park while enjoying the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Manas National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Jungle safari - A jungle safari is an excellent way to see the park’s wildlife up close.

  • Explore local villages - Exploring nearby villages gives visitors an opportunity to experience the local culture and traditions.

  • Visit the Manas National Park Interpretation Centre - This centre is devoted to showcasing the flora and fauna of Manas National Park, as well as its cultural heritage.

  • River Rafting - The Manas River runs through the park, and river rafting is a popular activity among thrill-seekers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manas National Park

Day 1 – Arrive in Guwahati, drive to Manas National Park, and check into your hotel or guesthouse.

Day 2 – Enjoy a morning hike to the Mathanguri Ridge, followed by an afternoon jeep safari in the park.

Day 3 – Take a river rafting trip on the Manas River.

Day 4 – Visit the MMES museum and explore nearby Bodo villages.

Day 5 – Enjoy a bird-watching tour in the park, followed by an afternoon jeep safari.

Day 6 – Take a biking tour of Western Assam.

Day 7 – Depart from Guwahati.

Food and Drink Scene of Manas National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pitha - a traditional Assamese dish made with rice flour and sweet coconut filling.

  • Masor Tenga - a tangy fish curry made with tomato and lemon.

  • Traditional Assam Tea - This is a must-have drink in Assam, it is rich, strong and aromatic.

Local Beverages

  • Apong – a traditional rice beer brewed by locals.

  • Assam Tea - Assam is famous for its tea, and tourists can indulge in a variety of tea flavours from the regions around Manas National Park.

Where to Stay in Manas National Park: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

Manas National Park offers a variety of accommodation options to fit any budget. Here are a few options:

  • Bansbari Lodge - A luxury resort located within the park that offers comfortable accommodation and a range of outdoor activities.

  • Greenwood Resort - Another luxury resort located within the park. It offers a restaurant, spa and other amenities for guests.

  • Mathanguri Forest Lodge - A basic lodge that offers comfortable accommodation at affordable prices.

  • Gabharu Jungle Cottage - An eco-friendly cottage that is a great option for those looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience.

What to Buy in Manas National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bodo Weave - The Bodo tribe produces beautiful handwoven textiles that make excellent souvenirs.

  • Tea - Assam is famous for its tea, and it makes a great gift for tea lovers.

  • Handicrafts and Bamboo products - Bamboo is a prevalent material in the region, and visitors can purchase a wide range of handicrafts and products made from this versatile material.

Local Culture and Customs of Manas National Park

The people of the Assam region are known for their diverse cultural traditions, including dance, music, and cuisine. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Manas National Park

The best time to visit Manas National Park is from November to April when the weather is mild and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manas National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Hire a licensed guide to ensure you are not damaging the local flora and fauna.

  • Stay on designated trails and paths during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the park’s flora and fauna.

  • Purchase or consume products made from endangered animals.

  • Feed the park’s animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manas National Park

Visitors should be aware of potential dangers in the park, including wildlife and natural hazards such as steep trails and fast-flowing rivers. Hiring a guide is recommended to ensure your safety while exploring the park.

Health in Manas National Park

Visitors should take precautions when visiting Manas National Park, including taking anti-mosquito measures and being mindful of food and water hygiene.

Getting to Manas National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manas National Park

  • The nearest airport is Guwahati International Airport, which is approximately four hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Manas National Park

  • The nearest bus station is in Barpeta Road, which is located approximately 65 km from the park.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also hire a taxi or private car from Guwahati, which is the most convenient option.

How to Get Around in Manas National Park

The best way to explore Manas National Park is by hiring a guide who knows the area well. Jeeps, bikes or other vehicles can be rented for independent exploring when permitted.

Where to Go Next from Manas National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kaziranga National Park - Kaziranga National Park is located approximately 200 km from Manas National Park and is known for its one-horned rhinoceros population.

  • Majuli Island - Majuli Island is the world’s largest river island and is located approximately 200 km from Manas National Park.

Conclusion

Manas National Park offers an abundance of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re exploring local villages or trekking through the park’s scenic landscape, a visit to Manas National Park is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
