Introduction to Rangia

Rangia is a small town in Lower Assam, located on the banks of the mighty Brahmaputra River. Popularly known as the gateway to the west of Assam, Rangia is a hidden gem with many cultural and natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination for travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Rangia, including the best places to visit, things to do, food scene, and local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Rangia

Rangia is home to some of the most beautiful and culturally significant attractions in the region. Here’s a list of top attractions to visit in Rangia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sundaridiya Xatra - A spiritual and cultural center of Vaishnavite culture, Sundaridiya Xatra is an ancient temple that dates back to the 16th century.
  • Barnagarh Fort - This historical fort built during the Ahom dynasty is located in the heart of Rangia town and is a popular attraction among history buffs.
  • Kedareswara Temple - A famous Shiva temple located in Rangia, the Kedareswara Temple is a beautiful shrine that stands out with its exquisite architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Tichutiya Jheel - An ancient lake located in the heart of Rangia, Tichutiya Jheel is surrounded by beautiful trees and is an ideal spot for a picnic.

Natural Attractions of Rangia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brahmaputra Adventure Park - A popular adventure park located on the banks of the Brahmaputra River, it’s a perfect spot for outdoor activities such as kayaking, camping, and rock climbing.
  • Manas National Park - One of the most popular tourist spots near Rangia, Manas National Park is known for its diverse wildlife and scenic landscapes.
  • Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary - Located about 50 km from Rangia, Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary is famous for its one-horned rhinoceros population and is a must-visit spot for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rangia

Rangia is surrounded by scenic hills and forests that offer excellent hiking and biking routes. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Rangia-Kamrup Forest Division - A scenic route that takes you through the lush green forests of Kamrup district, filled with indigenous flora and fauna.
  • Brahmaputra River Track - A popular biking route that runs along the majestic Brahmaputra River, offering breathtaking views of the scenic landscapes surrounding the river.

Best Things to Do in Rangia: Activities and Tours

Apart from the scenic beauty, Rangia offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all age groups. Here are some of the popular activities and tours:

  • Heritage Walks - A guided tour that takes visitors through the historical places in Rangia, providing a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region.
  • River Rafting - An exhilarating activity for adventure-seekers that takes you on a thrilling ride through the rapids of the Brahmaputra River.
  • Tea Plantation Tours - Rangia is known for its tea plantations, and taking a tour of the tea estates offers a unique experience to visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Rangia

Rangia’s cuisine is a blend of local and regional food traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to experience:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Masor Tenga - A popular fish curry with a tangy flavor that is a staple dish in Assam cuisine.
  • Khar - A unique dish made from vegetable ash that is rich in antioxidants and is a traditional Assamese delicacy.
  • Pitha - A rice flour-based dessert that is steamed or fried and served with molten jaggery or coconut cream.

Local Beverages

  • Assam Tea - Rangia is known for its tea plantations, and Assam tea is a must-try beverage for tea lovers.
  • Apong - A locally brewed rice beer that is popular in the region and is a must-try for all adventurous drinkers.

Where to Stay in Rangia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rangia offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the popular places to stay:

  • Hotel Highway Palace - A budget-friendly option located close to Rangia railway station.
  • Hotel Rangmahal Palace - A mid-range hotel located in the heart of Rangia town, offering comfortable rooms and excellent facilities.
  • River View Guesthouse - A charming guesthouse located on the banks of the Brahmaputra river, offering stunning views and tranquil surroundings.

What to Buy in Rangia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rangia is known for its handicrafts and local products that make for great souvenirs. Here are some top products to bring home:

  • Assam Tea - A pack of tea from one of Rangia’s tea plantations makes for a perfect gift for tea lovers.
  • Handicrafts - Rangia is known for its handwoven textiles, bamboo products, and cane handicrafts that make for unique souvenirs.
  • Traditional Jewelry - Assam is known for its intricate and beautiful jewelry designs, and Rangia has several shops that sell traditional jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Rangia

Rangia is a multicultural town and is home to people belonging to different ethnic and religious groups. Here are some of the local customs to be aware of:

  • Namaskar - The traditional greeting in Assam is ‘Namaskar,’ which involves folding your hands and bowing your head as a sign of respect.
  • Dress Code - Rangia is a conservative town, and visitors are advised to dress modestly, especially while visiting religious shrines.
  • Festivals - Rangia celebrates a variety of festivals throughout the year, including Bihu, Durga Puja, and Eid, which offer a unique insight into the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Rangia

The best time to visit Rangia is from November to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rangia

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Rangia:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry a valid ID proof at all times.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public display of affection.
  • Harm or feed the wildlife in the national parks.
  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rangia

Rangia is a safe town, and visitors are advised to maintain the usual precautions while traveling.

Health in Rangia

Visitors should carry essentials such as insect repellent, sunscreen, and necessary medications to stay protected from insect-borne diseases and common health issues.

Getting to Rangia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rangia

The nearest airport to Rangia is Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati, which is located about 50 km away. From the airport, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Rangia.

Bus Stations Near Rangia

Rangia has a well-connected bus station with regular buses operating to and from Guwahati and other major towns and cities in Assam.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Rangia by train, with several passenger and express trains connecting Rangia to other major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Rangia

Rangia is a small town, and visitors can get around easily by walking or hiring a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Rangia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rangia is an excellent base to explore several nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the popular day trips from Rangia:

  • Guwahati - The largest city in Assam, Guwahati is a bustling city located about 50 km from Rangia and is known for its temples, museums, and bustling markets.
  • Hajo - A small town located about 25 km from Rangia, Hajo is a sacred pilgrimage site for Muslims, Buddhists, and Hindus and is known for its ancient temples and shrines.
  • Tezpur - A town located about 60 km from Rangia, Tezpur is known for its historical ruins, temples, and tea plantations.

Conclusion

Rangia is a hidden gem in Assam, offering a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions that cater to all types of travelers. With its scenic beauty, vibrant local culture, and delicious cuisine, Rangia is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the off-beaten track in Assam.
