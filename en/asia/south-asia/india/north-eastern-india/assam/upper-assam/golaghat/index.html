Are you looking for a peaceful and offbeat vacation destination? If you are, then Golaghat is the place for you. Located in Upper Assam in North-Eastern India, Golaghat is a small town that offers an escape from the chaos of city life. With a rich cultural heritage, tranquil natural surroundings, and scenic beauty, Golaghat has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Golaghat.

Introduction to Golaghat

Golaghat, meaning “the land of the hornbill,” is a small town in the state of Assam, India. This picturesque town is surrounded by lush green tea gardens, dense forests, and the Brahmaputra river. Golaghat is known for its tea plantations, rice fields, and the rich historical and cultural heritage of the Ahom dynasty. The town offers a tranquil and peaceful escape to those seeking a break from their hectic lives.

List of Places to Visit in Golaghat

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Golaghat:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Numaligarh Refinery Limited: A must-visit place for those interested in the industrial development of Assam. The refinery offers a tour of the premises, which includes a museum and an audio-visual presentation on the history of the refinery.

  2. Deoparbat Ruins: These ancient ruins date back to the Ahom dynasty and offer a glimpse into the past. The ruins are surrounded by beautiful greenery and are a great place for photography.

  3. Garampani Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and leopards, this sanctuary is a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can take a jeep safari or a guided trek to explore the sanctuary.

Natural Attractions of Golaghat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kaziranga National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this national park is famous for its one-horned rhinoceroses. It is also home to other wildlife, such as tigers, elephants, and water buffaloes.

  2. Doyang River: This pristine river is a great place for water sports like rafting and kayaking. The surrounding lush greenery is perfect for trekking and camping.

  3. Jatinga Bird Sanctuary: A unique bird sanctuary known for the mysterious phenomenon of bird suicides. Every year, during certain months, a large number of birds in this area commit suicide by flying into buildings and trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Golaghat

Hiking and biking are great ways to explore the beautiful surroundings of Golaghat. Here are some routes you can take:

  1. Brahmaputra River Banks: This scenic route offers breathtaking views of the Brahmaputra river and the surrounding lush greenery.

  2. Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary: Trek through the sanctuary and spot rare species like hoolock gibbons and clouded leopards.

  3. The Noa-Dihing River: This route is perfect for bikers as it offers a mix of countryside and river views.

Best Things to Do in Golaghat: Activities and Tours

  1. Tea Garden Tours: A visit to Golaghat is incomplete without a tour of its famous tea gardens. Visitors can take a guided tour of the tea plantations and learn about the tea-making process.

  2. Handloom and Handicraft Shopping: Golaghat is known for its handloom and handicraft products. Visitors can buy souvenirs like Assamese silk sarees, bamboo handicrafts, and tea leaves.

  3. Cultural Performances: Golaghat is known for its cultural heritage. Visitors can attend traditional dance and music performances like Bihu dance and Jhumur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Golaghat

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Golaghat:

Day 1 - Visit Numaligarh Refinery Limited and Deoparbat Ruins Day 2 - Explore Kaziranga National Park Day 3 - Trek to Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary Day 4 - Visit Garampani Wildlife Sanctuary Day 5 - Take a tour of the tea gardens and enjoy tea tastings Day 6 - Go shopping for handloom and handicraft products Day 7 - Attend a local cultural performance

Food and Drink Scene of Golaghat

Assamese cuisine is known for its simplicity and use of locally grown ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks in Golaghat:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Masor Tenga: Sour fish curry made with tomatoes and herbs.

  2. Xoruai Xaak: A dish made with mustard greens and potatoes.

  3. Khar: A preparation made with baking soda and a variety of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Apong: A fermented rice beer with a slightly sour taste.

  2. Assamese Black Tea: A strong and robust tea with a malty flavor.

  3. Pitha: A sweet made with rice flour and jaggery.

Where to Stay in Golaghat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Golaghat has a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Wild Mahseer - A heritage boutique hotel with comfortable rooms and a beautiful view of the tea gardens.

  2. Aranya Lodge - A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and excellent service.

  3. Circuit House - A government-run guesthouse with spacious rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Golaghat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Golaghat is famous for its handloom and handicraft products. Visitors can buy souvenirs like Assamese silk sarees, bamboo handicrafts, and tea leaves. Here are some recommended places to shop:

  1. Main Road Market - A bustling street market with a variety of shops selling clothing, handicrafts, and jewelry.

  2. Assam Emporium - A government-run store with a wide selection of handloom and handicraft products.

  3. Tea Estate Shops - Visitors can buy tea directly from the tea estates and learn about the tea-making process.

Local Culture and Customs of Golaghat

Golaghat has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. The majority of the population is Hindu and celebrates festivals like Bihu and Durga Puja. Visitors should dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering temples and other religious places.

Best Time to Visit Golaghat

The best time to visit Golaghat is from October to April when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities like trekking and camping. The monsoon season from May to September should be avoided as heavy rainfall can cause landslides and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Golaghat

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  3. Visit the tea gardens and learn about the tea-making process.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect religious places and customs.

  2. Litter or harm the environment.

  3. Wear revealing clothes in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Golaghat

Golaghat is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping their valuable belongings safe. It is recommended to take a guide or stick to the designated tourist areas while exploring.

Health in Golaghat

Visitors should be careful when drinking tap water and always opt for bottled water. Mosquitoes in the area can transmit diseases like dengue and malaria, so it is recommended to take preventive measures and use mosquito repellent.

Getting to Golaghat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Golaghat is well connected by road and air. Here’s how you can reach Golaghat:

Airports Near Golaghat

  1. Jorhat Airport (Jorhat)

  2. Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport (Guwahati)

Bus Stations Near Golaghat

  1. ASTC Bus Station (Golaghat)

  2. Private Bus Stand (Golaghat)

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Golaghat by train. The nearest train station is Furkating Junction, located around 15 km from Golaghat.

How to Get Around in Golaghat

Visitors can hire private taxis or take shared transport like buses and tempo travellers to get around Golaghat. Bikes and scooters are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Golaghat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Golaghat is surrounded by many tourist destinations that visitors can explore. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Majuli Island: A river island famous for its scenic beauty and cultural heritage.

  2. Sivasagar: A town known for its historical monuments and temples.

  3. Mon: A small town famous for the Longwa village, which is located on the border of India and Myanmar.

Conclusion

Golaghat is a hidden gem in North-Eastern India that offers a unique travel experience. From its peaceful surroundings and natural attractions to its rich cultural heritage and tea plantations, Golaghat has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Golaghat.
