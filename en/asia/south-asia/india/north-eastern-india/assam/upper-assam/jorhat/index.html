Introduction to Jorhat

Jorhat, located in Upper Assam, is a vibrant and bustling city known for its tea plantations, cultural significance, and natural beauty. It is often referred to as the ‘Tea Capital of India’ due to its vast tea estates that produce some of the world’s finest teas. Jorhat is also home to several museums, historical monuments, and natural attractions that make it a haven for both nature lovers and history buffs.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through Jorhat, Assam, and uncover the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Jorhat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jorhat is a city steeped in history and culture, and there is no shortage of museums and historical places to explore. Some of the top attractions in this category include:

  • Jorhat Gymkhana Club: Built during the British Raj, the Jorhat Gymkhana Club is a beautifully preserved colonial building that offers a glimpse into Jorhat’s colonial past.
  • Thengal Mansion: Thengal Mansion is a grand 19th-century aristocratic mansion that has been lovingly restored and now serves as a museum showcasing the lifestyle and traditions of the Ahom dynasty.
  • Majuli Island: Not technically in Jorhat, but a short ferry ride away, Majuli Island is the world’s largest riverine island and home to several ancient Assamese monasteries, including the famous Kamalabari Satra.

Natural Attractions of Jorhat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Jorhat has plenty to offer. The city is dotted with tea gardens, national parks, and scenic views that are sure to take your breath away. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Dhekiakhowa Bornamghor: This ancient temple is believed to have been built during the reign of the Ahom dynasty and is located in the middle of a lush forest. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the forest and enjoy the peace and quiet.
  • Kakochang Waterfalls: A short drive from Jorhat is the stunning Kakochang Waterfalls. The cascading waterfalls and lush greenery make it a popular picnic spot for locals and tourists alike.
  • Hoolock Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary: Located on the outskirts of Jorhat, the Hoolock Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary is home to several endangered species, including the Hoolock Gibbon and various species of primates.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jorhat

Jorhat offers several scenic hiking and biking routes that take you through tea gardens, paddy fields, and forests. Some of the top routes include:

  • Tea Garden Trail: Take a leisurely stroll through some of Jorhat’s famous tea gardens and discover firsthand how tea is grown, harvested, and processed.
  • Ferry Ride to Majuli Island: Rent a bike on Majuli Island and discover the island’s stunning natural beauty and ancient monasteries.
  • Jorhat to Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary: This scenic route takes you through lush forests and rolling hills and ends at the Hoolock Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Jorhat: Activities and Tours

Jorhat offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to all interests and budgets. Here are some of the best things to do in Jorhat:

  • Tea Tasting Sessions: Take a sip of Assam’s famous teas and learn about the different varieties and flavors at some of Jorhat’s top tea estates.
  • Cultural Tours: Explore the city’s rich cultural heritage and traditions with a visit to the Thengal Mansion, Jorhat Gymkhana Club, and other historic monuments.
  • Nature Walks: Explore Jorhat’s natural beauty with a leisurely walk through the Dhekiakhowa Bornamghor or tea gardens.
  • River Rafting: For thrill-seekers, Jorhat offers river rafting adventures on the Brahmaputra River.

For those who wish to explore Jorhat in-depth, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Jorhat

Must-Try Dishes

Jorhat offers a diverse culinary scene that is sure to satisfy any palate. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Masor Tenga: A tangy fish curry made with tomato, lemon, and other spices.
  • Pitha: A Assamese sweet dish made with rice flour, jaggery, and coconut.
  • Aloo Pitika: A simple yet delicious mashed potato dish served with mustard oil and herbs.

Local Beverages

No visit to Jorhat is complete without tasting its famous teas. Some of the best tea estates to visit include Sangsua, Manohari, and Cinnamora.

Where to Stay in Jorhat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jorhat offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets and needs. From luxury hotels to budget guesthouses, here are some of our top picks:

  • Thengal Manor: A luxurious heritage hotel located in the heart of Jorhat’s tea country.
  • Jorhat Gymkhana Resort: A colonial-style resort that offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Kaziranga University Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers and students.

What to Buy in Jorhat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jorhat offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make for excellent gifts or keepsakes. Some of the top items to buy include:

  • Assamese silk: Jorhat is known for its high-quality Assamese silk that is available in various designs and colors.
  • Axomiya gamusa: A traditional Assamese towel that is used as a headscarf or handkerchief.
  • Traditional handicrafts: Jorhat is home to several artisan villages that produce beautiful handicrafts such as bamboo furniture, pottery, and woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Jorhat

Jorhat has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are advised to show respect for local customs and traditions. Here are some important customs to keep in mind:

  • Namaste: The traditional Indian greeting of folding your hands and saying ‘Namaste’ is widely used in Jorhat.
  • Modesty and Conservative Clothing: Jorhat is a conservative city, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing.
  • Respect for Elders: In Assamese culture, elders are revered, and visitors are advised to show respect for the elderly.

Best Time to Visit Jorhat

The best time to visit Jorhat is from October to April when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season between June and September can be unpredictable and hinder sightseeing tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jorhat

Do:

  • Try local street food: Jorhat has some of the best street food that should not be missed.
  • Visit tea gardens: No visit to Jorhat is complete without a visit to its famous tea gardens.
  • Show respect for local culture: Jorhat has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are advised to show respect for local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing: Jorhat is a conservative city, and visitors are advised to avoid revealing clothing.
  • Disrespect elders and local customs: In Assamese culture, elders are revered, and visitors should show respect for the elderly and local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jorhat

Jorhat is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing. Avoid walking alone late at night and keep your valuables and money in a safe place.

Health in Jorhat

Jorhat is a relatively healthy city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid water-borne diseases. Drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food if you have a sensitive stomach. It is also advisable to get vaccinated against common diseases before travelling to Jorhat.

Getting to Jorhat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jorhat

The nearest airport to Jorhat is the Jorhat Airport, which is well connected to major Indian cities such as Kolkata, Guwahati, and Delhi. Taxis and buses are available from the airport to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Jorhat

Jorhat has a well-developed bus network that connects the city to major towns and cities in Assam and neighboring states. The main bus terminal is located in the city center.

Other Options

Jorhat is well connected to neighboring cities via the Indian railway network. Trains are available from major Indian cities such as Kolkata, Guwahati, and Delhi. Taxis and buses are also available for inter-city travel.

How to Get Around in Jorhat

Jorhat has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses and auto-rickshaws. Taxis are also available for hire. Renting a bike or car is also a popular option for tourists who want to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Jorhat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jorhat is a great base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the top destinations include:

  • Kaziranga National Park: Located a few hours’ drive from Jorhat, Kaziranga National Park is home to the famous one-horned rhinoceros and several other endangered species.
  • Sivasagar: A historic town located a short drive from Jorhat that was once the capital of the Ahom dynasty. Visitors can explore the ancient palaces, temples, and gardens.
  • Dibrugarh: A bustling city located in the heart of Upper Assam that is famous for its tea plantations and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Jorhat, Assam, is a must-visit destination for every traveler. From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural beauty, Jorhat has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, foodie, or adventure seeker, Jorhat won’t disappoint. Follow this travel guide and explore the best of Jorhat with ease.
