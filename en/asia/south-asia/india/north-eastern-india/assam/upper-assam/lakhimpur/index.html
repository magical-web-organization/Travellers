Introduction to Lakhimpur

Lakhimpur is a town located in Upper Assam, in the North-Eastern region of India. The town is a perfect blend of natural beauty and Raj era culture. The town is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, with the mighty Brahmaputra river flowing by its side. The town has a rich history and culture, with several historical sites and museums. It offers amazing outdoor experiences like trekking, fishing, and wildlife photography. Lakhimpur offers an ideal mix of culturally rich and scenic experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Lakhimpur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lakhimpur has a rich history and culture, which can be explored by visiting the following historical places:

  • Ghagra Waterfall: One of the popular spots in Lakhimpur is the Ghagra Waterfall. The waterfall is located close to the city and is surrounded by lush greenery. It is also a popular spot for picnics.

  • Laokhowa Wildlife Sanctuary: The Laokhowa Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit place for wildlife enthusiasts. The sanctuary is home to various fauna species like the Indian rhinoceros, wild buffalo, leopards, tigers, and wild elephants.

  • Kareng Ghar: Kareng Ghar is a historical palace located in Gargaon, Assam. The palace was built in the 18th century by the Ahom dynasty. The palace has now been turned into a museum showcasing the rich history of the Ahom dynasty and their culture.

Natural Attractions of Lakhimpur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lakhimpur is home to various natural attractions that offer scenic beauty and outdoor adventures. Some of the popular natural attractions in Lakhimpur include:

  • Kaziranga National Park: Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO world heritage site and is famous for its one-horned rhinoceros. The park is also home to various fauna species like tigers, elephants, and wild buffaloes. It is a must-visit for nature lovers.

  • Bogamati: Bogamati is a scenic village in Lakhimpur where visitors can enjoy fishing, upstream trekking, and village walks. The village is picturesque, located on the banks of the Brahmaputra River, and surrounded by lush green hills.

  • Sessa Orchid Sanctuary: The Sessa Orchid Sanctuary is a sanctuary for exotic orchids found in the region. The sanctuary is located at a higher altitude than Lakhimpur, offering scenic views of the hills and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakhimpur

Lakhimpur offers a range of hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Majuli Island: Majuli Island is the largest riverine island in the world and is accessible by ferry from Lakhimpur. The island is famous for its natural beauty and is one of the best places for trekking and biking.

  • Panbari Reserve Forest: Panbari Reserve Forest is a scenic forest located near Lakhimpur. The forest offers various trekking routes and is home to various fauna species like tigers, wild elephants, and leopards.

Best Things to Do in Lakhimpur: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and outdoor activities, there are a host of other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Lakhimpur. Some of these include:

  • Tea tasting: Lakhimpur is well-known for its tea gardens, where visitors can take a tour of the tea estates and taste various types of tea.

  • Visit Pottery Village: Lakhimpur is famous for its pottery, and one can take a tour of the local pottery village to see artisans at work and buy unique handmade souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakhimpur

Day 1: Visit Kareng Ghar, Lakhimpur Museum, and Poba Reserve Forest.

Day 2: Explore Laokhowa Wildlife Sanctuary and Assam Agricultural University.

Day 3: Visit Ghagra Waterfall, and take a boat ride on Brahmaputra river.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Majuli Island for trekking and biking.

Day 5: Tour Sessa Orchid Sanctuary and visit Bogamati for fishing.

Day 6: Visit Panbari Reserve Forest for trekking and wildlife photography.

Day 7: Take a tour of the tea gardens and pottery village.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakhimpur

Must-Try Dishes

Lakhimpur is famous for its tea gardens, and visitors must try out the local tea. Some of the other famous dishes include:

  • ** Pitha:** Pitha is a popular sweet dish of Assam and is made from rice flour, jaggery, and coconut.

  • ** Bamboo Shoot:** Bamboo Shoot is a famous dish in Assam and is made from bamboo shoot, potato, and chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Assamese Tea: Assamese tea is well known for its aroma, flavor, and strength. Visitors can take a tour of the tea gardens to see the process of tea-making.

Where to Stay in Lakhimpur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options when it comes to accommodation in Lakhimpur. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Brahmaputra: This hotel is located near the bus stand and offers excellent amenities like free Wi-Fi, room service, and on-site parking.

  • Kaziranga Resort: Kaziranga Resort is located near Kaziranga National Park and is an excellent option for nature enthusiasts. The resort offers several amenities like free Wi-Fi, swimming pool, and several outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Lakhimpur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the popular souvenirs and local products to bring back from Lakhimpur include:

  • Pottery: Lakhimpur is famous for its pottery, and visitors can buy unique handmade souvenirs from the pottery village.

  • Tea: Lakhimpur is well-known for its tea gardens, and visitors can take back some of the local tea as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakhimpur

The people of Lakhimpur are warm and friendly, and visitors must respect the local customs and traditions. The majority of the population in Lakhimpur follows Hinduism, and religious sentiments must be respected.

Best Time to Visit Lakhimpur

The best time to visit Lakhimpur is from November to April. The weather during this time is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities. The monsoon season, from June to September, should be avoided as heavy rainfall may affect sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakhimpur

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Carry a valid ID card at all times.

  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.

  • Click photos without taking permission.

  • Disrespect the religious sentiments of the local population.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakhimpur

Lakhimpur is generally safe, but visitors are advised to take necessary precautions like traveling in groups, avoiding secluded areas, and keeping valuable items safe.

Health in Lakhimpur

Visitors are advised to carry necessary medicines and take necessary precautions like using mosquito repellent, drinking bottled water, and maintaining personal hygiene.

Getting to Lakhimpur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakhimpur

The closest airport to Lakhimpur is Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati, which is about 300 km away from Lakhimpur.

Bus Stations Near Lakhimpur

Lakhimpur has a well-connected network of buses that connect it to major cities in Assam.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Lakhimpur. The closest railway station to Lakhimpur is Harmuti Railway Station, which is about 25 km away.

How to Get Around in Lakhimpur

Visitors can use local buses, taxis or auto-rickshaws to get around Lakhimpur.

Where to Go Next from Lakhimpur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular destinations near Lakhimpur include:

  • Majuli Island: Majuli Island is famous for its natural beauty and is one of the best places for trekking and biking.

  • Kaziranga National Park: Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO world heritage site and is famous for its one-horned rhinoceros.

Conclusion

Lakhimpur is a hidden gem of Assam, with its rich history and culture, scenic views, and a range of outdoor activities. Visitors can explore the town’s cultural and historical sites, indulge in outdoor adventures, or simply relax in the scenic beauty of the place. A visit to Lakhimpur is a must for anyone looking to explore the hidden gems of North-East India.
