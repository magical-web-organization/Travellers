Introduction to Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Upper Assam region of North-Eastern India. It’s home to the world’s largest population of Indian one-horned rhinoceros, along with other wildlife like tigers, elephants, and water buffaloes. With its vast swathes of tall elephant grass, marshland, and dense forests, the park offers visitors an immersive experience of India’s diverse flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Kaziranga National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kaziranga National Orchid and Biodiversity Park: Situated near the central range of the park, this unique park is home to various species of orchids and rare plants which flourish in this part of Assam.
  2. Kaliabor Manor: An old colonial-era mansion located near Kaliabor town, which is famous for its tea gardens and produces some of the best tea in Assam.
  3. Karbi Ethnic Village: A must-visit for those interested in the indigenous cultures of Assam, this village offers a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle of the Karbi tribe.

Natural Attractions of Kaziranga National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Agaratoli Range: A scenic range located on the eastern frontiers of the park, Agaratoli offers views of the Brahmaputra river, and visitors can spot flocks of migratory birds and animals from here.
  2. Elephant Safari: One of the most exciting ways to explore the park is through an elephant safari, which allows visitors to travel deeper into the grasslands and spot wildlife up close.
  3. Jeep Safari: Another thrilling way to experience the park is through a jeep safari, which traverses through various ranges of the park and offers panoramic views of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park is the perfect destination for adventure enthusiasts, and there are plenty of biking and hiking routes available inside the park. Some popular options include:

  1. Nature Trail: A guided walking tour in the Kukurakata hills range, which offers breathtaking views of the Brahmaputra river and countryside nearby.
  2. River Trail: A cycling trip along the Brahmaputra river, which is perfect for a relaxing day out and includes a visit to one of the nearby tea gardens.
  3. Trek to Pani Dihing Bird Sanctuary: A challenging seven-day trek that starts from Kaziranga and takes hikers all the way to the Pani Dihing Bird Sanctuary, a protected bird sanctuary on the outskirts of Assam.

Best Things to Do in Kaziranga National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Wildlife Photography: With its diverse wildlife and scenic surroundings, Kaziranga National Park is a photographer’s paradise. Visitors can participate in exclusive photography tours with experienced guides and capture the beauty of the park.
  2. River Rafting: The Brahmaputra river offers plenty of opportunities for water sports like rafting, kayaking, and canoeing. It’s an excellent option for adventure-seekers looking for an adrenaline rush.
  3. Cultural Tours: Visitors can take a trip to nearby villages and towns to explore the traditional cultures of Assam, including visiting tea gardens, silk mills, and craft workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kaziranga National Park

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Kaziranga National Park:

Day 1: Arrival at Kaziranga National Park, evening jeep safari to the Western Range. Day 2: Elephant safari to the Central Range, followed by a visit to the Kaziranga National Orchid and Biodiversity Park. Day 3: River Trail biking tour along the Brahmaputra river, followed by a visit to the Karbi Ethnic village. Day 4: Trek to the Agaratoli range for a picnic lunch, followed by a visit to the Kaliabor Manor. Day 5: Jeep safari to the Kohora Range, followed by a visit to the Hornbill Festival Grounds and a cultural tour of nearby villages. Day 6: Trek to Pani Dihing bird sanctuary, followed by a night stay at a nearby tea garden. Day 7: Departure from Kaziranga National Park, with an optional day trip to Guwahati city.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaziranga National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Assam Tea: The tea produced in the tea gardens near Kaziranga National Park is world-famous, and visitors can savor its unique taste in the many tea stalls and cafes nearby.
  2. Masor Tenga: A sour fish dish made with tomatoes, mustard, and other spices, a popular Assamese delicacy.
  3. Khar: A vegetable curry made with papaya puree, and other vegetables unique to North-Eastern India.

Local Beverages

  1. Apong: A local alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice, which is sour and slightly sweet.
  2. Assam Tea: As mentioned before, Assam tea is a must-try, and visitors can explore the many varieties available in the different tea gardens.

Where to Stay in Kaziranga National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some popular options for accommodation include:

  1. IORA Resort: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the park, offering stunning views of the surrounding wildlife and a range of amenities and services.
  2. Diphlu River Lodge: A boutique eco-lodge on the outskirts of the park, offering visitors a chance to experience the wilderness up close and personal.
  3. Wild Grass Lodge: A rustic guesthouse located in a nearby tea garden, offering visitors an authentic experience of the local culture and traditions.

What to Buy in Kaziranga National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Assam Tea: Visitors can buy different varieties of Assam tea from the various tea gardens near Kaziranga National Park.
  2. Traditional Assamese Clothes: Visitors can buy traditional dresses like Mekhla Chador, which is unique to Assam.
  3. Handicrafts: Assam is known for its intricate handicrafts, including bamboo baskets, pottery, and silk weaving.

Local Culture and Customs of Kaziranga National Park

Assam has a diverse blend of cultures and traditions, including those of the Karbi, Mishing, and Adivasi tribes. Visitors should respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples or other cultural landmarks. While English is widely spoken, it’s always helpful to learn some common phrases in the local dialect to communicate better with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Kaziranga National Park

The best time to visit Kaziranga National Park is from November to April when the park is open to tourists. November to February is an excellent time to spot migratory birds, while March and April are ideal for seeing rhinos and other wildlife up close.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaziranga National Park

Do:

  1. Follow the designated trails while trekking or hiking and avoid wandering off into secluded areas.
  2. Listen to your guide’s instructions during safaris or tours and do not disturb the animals or their natural habitats.
  3. Bring enough mosquito repellant and dress appropriately to protect yourself from insect bites or discomfort.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter inside or outside the park, as it can adversely affect the wildlife and the ecosystem.
  2. Do not purchase any animal body parts or souvenirs made from them, as it is illegal and can lead to legal hassles.
  3. Do not provoke the animals or try to get too close to them during safaris or tours for photo opportunities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaziranga National Park

Visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  1. Wild Animals: Visitors should always maintain a safe distance from wild animals and not try to provoke them in any way.
  2. Weather: The weather can be unpredictable, and visitors should always dress appropriately and carry emergency supplies like first aid kits and raincoats.
  3. Sun Exposure: Visitors should protect themselves from the harsh sun by wearing sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses.

Health in Kaziranga National Park

Visitors should follow these basic health precautions:

  1. Drink bottled or boiled water only to avoid water-borne illnesses.
  2. Carry any necessary medication or prescriptions with you during your trip.
  3. Make sure to get a yellow fever vaccine if traveling from a country with a high risk of malaria.

Getting to Kaziranga National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaziranga National Park

The nearest airport to Kaziranga National Park is the Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport, situated in Guwahati, which is about 238 km away. One can hire a taxi from the airport to the park.

Bus Stations Near Kaziranga National Park

The nearest bus station to Kaziranga is situated in Kohora, which is about 4 km away and offers bus services to nearby towns and cities. Visitors can take local taxis or auto-rickshaws to reach the park from the bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Guwahati to Furkating, which is the nearest railway station to the park. From there, one can hire taxis or take buses to reach the park.

How to Get Around in Kaziranga National Park

Visitors can opt for any of the following transportation modes to get around in Kaziranga National Park:

  1. Jeep Safari
  2. Elephant Safari
  3. Walking/Biking Tours
  4. Private Taxis

Where to Go Next from Kaziranga National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Majuli Island: Located on the Brahmaputra river, this island is famous for its ancient monasteries and Vaishnavite culture and is located about 185 km from Kaziranga.
  2. Shillong: The capital of Meghalaya, also known as the “Scotland of the East,” is a beautiful hill station about 245 km away from Kaziranga.
  3. Guwahati: The largest city in Assam, about 238 km away from Kaziranga, offers visitors a chance to explore the rich cultural heritage of the region and the famous Kamakhya temple.

Conclusion

Kaziranga National Park is a magical destination that offers something for everyone. From its mesmerizing wildlife to its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking landscapes, this park is a must-visit for anyone interested in exploring the wonders of Assam. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to Kaziranga National Park and have an unforgettable experience.
