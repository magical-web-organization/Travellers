Introduction to Dibrugarh

Dibrugarh is a charming town located in Upper Assam, which has a rich historical and cultural significance. Surrounded by the Brahmaputra and its tributaries, Dibrugarh is also famous for its tea gardens, oil refineries, and pristine natural beauty.

The town makes for a perfect destination for backpackers, shutterbugs, and those aspiring to taste the local Assamese delicacies. The local people are friendly and always ready to welcome visitors with open arms.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Dibrugarh, we will take you through some of the must-visit attractions in the town.

List of Places to Visit in Dibrugarh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tilinga Mandir - A Hindu temple known for its unique offering of bells, tied to the temple walls, and trees.
  • Radha Krishna Mandir - Another Hindu temple with magnificent architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Naharkatia Heritage Park - A park with relevance to the Indian freedom movement and featuring a statue of Mahatma Gandhi.
  • Lekai Chetia Maidam - A historic Ahom architecture at Lekai Chetia Gaon, Naharkatia.
  • Dibru Saikhowa National Park - A national park with a mix of evergreen and deciduous forests, rivers, and wetlands, and home to some rare and endangered wildlife, like Hoolock Gibbon, Assam Roofed Turtle, and Gangetic Dolphin.

Natural Attractions of Dibrugarh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dehing Patkai Wildlife Sanctuary - A wildlife sanctuary known for its vast dense rainforests with diverse wildlife, including leopards, slow loris, tigers, and elephants.
  • Jeypore Rainforest Trail - A trek through the Jeypore Rainforest, which boasts stunning mountain views and bird-watching opportunities.
  • Boga Lake - A serene lake famous for its pristine waters and scenic views.
  • Barbarua Maidam - A historic Ahom maidam representing the rich history of Assam.
  • Maguri Motapung Beel - A wetland area near Dibru Saikhowa with excellent bird-watching opportunities, including migratory birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dibrugarh

  • Wakro- Walong Trek - One of the most scenic trek routes of Eastern Himalaya.
  • Digboi- Margherita Cycle Expedition - A cycling expedition of 73 km from Digboi to Margherita via Ledo and Baragolai.

Best Things to Do in Dibrugarh: Activities and Tours

  • Go on Tea Estate Tours and witness the tea-making process from plucking to packaging and taste some of the finest Assam tea.
  • Visit the Namphake Village of the Tai Phake tribe, which gives a glimpse of their unique culture and traditions.
  • Attend Rongali Bihu festival celebrations, usually held in mid-April, featuring traditional dance, music, and food.
  • Try your luck at Angling (Fishing) at the riverbanks of the Brahmaputra and its tributaries.

Food and Drink Scene of Dibrugarh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Litti Chokha - A baked dough ball served with mashed potatoes and a variety of chutneys.
  • Assamese Thali - A traditional Assamese meal comprising rice, dal, a variety of vegetable curries, and a sweet dish.
  • Pakoras - Deep-fried gram flour coated fritters served with hot masala tea.

Local Beverages

  • Assam Tea - One of the finest teas in the world, brewed from the tea plantation of Assam.
  • Apong - A famous rice beer made by the Mishing tribe of Assam.
  • Xaj Pani - A refreshing drink of sugarcane juice.

Where to Stay in Dibrugarh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dibrugarh has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets.

  • Hotel Natraj - An affordable hotel in the town center.
  • Hotel Rainbow Regis - A mid-range hotel near the Dibrugarh railway station.
  • Mancotta Heritage Chang Bungalow - A luxurious heritage bungalow that once belonged to the influential Singpho tribe’s monarch.

What to Buy in Dibrugarh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Assam Tea - Indulge in the rich Assam Tea, which is famous worldwide.
  • Handloom products - Assamese silk and cotton Sarees, Mekhela Chadar, Gamusa, etc.
  • Traditional Assamese Jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Dibrugarh

Assam is known for its rich history and culture. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and tourists will feel welcomed in Dibrugarh. Some local customs to be mindful of include:

  • Cover your head while visiting temples
  • Remove your shoes while entering someone’s home
  • Ask for permission before taking someone’s picture

Best Time to Visit Dibrugarh

Dibrugarh can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to experience the town’s outdoor adventures and pristine natural beauty is between October and April. The weather during this period is pleasant, and the skies are clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dibrugarh

Do:

  • Explore the town on foot and experience the local culture.
  • Get a taste of Assamese Thali and Assam Tea.
  • Try Angling (Fishing) in the Brahmaputra River.
  • Remove your shoes while entering someone’s home or a temple.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the wildlife.
  • Venture in offbeat trails without a guide.
  • Take someone’s picture without asking for permission.
  • Enter any religious site without taking your head covered.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dibrugarh

Assam is generally safe for tourists, and the locals are welcoming and helpful. Still, tourists should take the following precautions:

  • Avoid venturing alone in isolated areas.
  • Use reliable transport services to move around the town.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings while in crowded public places.

Health in Dibrugarh

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Keep a first aid kit handy during treks and hikes.
  • Wear comfortable and sturdy shoes while trekking or biking.
  • Carry insect repellant while visiting the wildlife sanctuaries.

Getting to Dibrugarh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dibrugarh

The Mohanbari Domestic Airport is the nearest airport to Dibrugarh, situated at a distance of about 12 km from the town center. The airport is well connected to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Dibrugarh

Dibrugarh has two bus terminals - one at the Chowkidinghee market and the other at the DHSK College campus. Buses from these terminals connect to towns and cities in Assam and neighboring states.

Other Options

Trains from major cities of India regularly arrive at the Dibrugarh Railway Station. Additionally, taxis and private vehicles are readily available for hire.

How to Get Around in Dibrugarh

Exploring Dibrugarh is best done on foot or by hiring a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or a motorbike. Visitors can also rent bicycles and motorcycles at reasonable rates.

Where to Go Next from Dibrugarh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Majuli - The largest river island in the world, which has significant cultural and historical significance.
  • Kaziranga National Park - One of India’s finest wildlife reserves and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its one-horned Rhinoceros and other wildlife species.
  • Tawang - A picturesque town in Arunachal Pradesh, famous for its serene landscapes and the Tawang Monastery.

Conclusion

With this comprehensive travel guide, we have tried to bring out the best of Dibrugarh, from its natural beauty to cultural significance. Plan your visit to Assam’s Upper Assam region and explore the town’s heritage and stunning landscapes.
