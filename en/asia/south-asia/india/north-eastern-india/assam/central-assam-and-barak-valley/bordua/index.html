Introduction to Bordua

Located in Central Assam and Barak Valley, Bordua is a small town nestled in the scenic mountains and lush green forests of the North-Eastern region of India. Bordering the states of Meghalaya and Mizoram, Bordua offers a unique blend of cultures, traditions, and outdoor adventures that attracts tourists from all over the world. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Bordua has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bordua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bordua Satra - A popular religious site known for its intricate architecture and rich history.
  2. Bura-Chapori Wildlife Sanctuary - A nature reserve located on the banks of the Brahmaputra river.
  3. Baithapukhuri - A historical pond that dates back to the Ahom dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Bordua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Panimur Waterfall - A picturesque waterfall located amidst the lush green forests of Bordua.
  2. Khaspur - A scenic and historic village located near Bordua that celebrates the culture of Assam.
  3. Jatinga - A unique place known for its famous bird phenomenon where birds ‘commit suicide’ by flying into trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bordua

  1. Cox’s Trail - A scenic hiking route that takes you through the hills of Bordua.
  2. Longai Trail - A biking trail that passes through the wildlife reserve of Bura-Chapori.

Best Things to Do in Bordua: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a guided tour of the Bordua Satra and learn about the history and culture of the region.
  2. Go on a wildlife safari to the Bura-Chapori Wildlife Sanctuary and see the diverse flora and fauna of Assam.
  3. Visit the Panimur Waterfall and take part in outdoor activities such as camping, trekking, and swimming.
  4. Explore the local villages and learn about the local customs and way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bordua

Day Activity
1 Arrival and check-in at a hotel in Bordua

Food and Drink Scene of Bordua

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pitha - A traditional Assamese dish made with rice flour and coconut.
  2. Masor Tenga - A sour fish curry that is a staple of Assamese cuisine.
  3. Khaar - A dish made with lentils and vegetables that is unique to Assam.

Local Beverages

  1. Apong - A local rice beer made with fermented rice and yeast.
  2. Tea - Assam is famous for its tea gardens that produce some of the finest tea in the world.

Where to Stay in Bordua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Bordua Palace - A luxury hotel located in the heart of Bordua.
  2. Misty Meadows Guesthouse - A cozy guesthouse located near the Panimur Waterfall.
  3. Bordua Backpackers Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel located in the outskirts of Bordua.

What to Buy in Bordua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Traditional Assamese textiles such as Muga, Eri, and Pat silk.
  2. Handcrafted bamboo products such as baskets, mats, and furniture.
  3. Assam tea and Apong rice beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Bordua

The culture of Bordua is deeply inspired by the Ahom dynasty and the tribal communities of Assam. The people of Bordua are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors. The local customs and traditions are an integral part of their daily lives, and tourists can witness them through various cultural events and festivals held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Bordua

The best time to visit Bordua is between October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season is from June to September and brings heavy rainfall, making it difficult to explore the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bordua

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  3. Carry a valid identity proof.

Don’t:

  1. Consume drugs or other illicit substances.
  2. Disrespect the local religion and beliefs.
  3. Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bordua

While Bordua is a relatively safe place to travel to, it is recommended to take precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Bordua

It is advisable to carry a basic first aid kit and take necessary vaccines before traveling to Bordua. It is important to stay hydrated and avoid eating from roadside stalls to prevent food-related illnesses.

Getting to Bordua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bordua

The nearest airport to Bordua is Silchar airport, which is about 40 km away. Taxis and buses are available from the airport to reach Bordua.

Bus Stations Near Bordua

Bordua is well-connected to the other cities of Assam through roadways. There are several bus stations in and around Bordua that provide bus services to various destinations.

Other Options

One can also reach Bordua by train, with the nearest railway station being at Silchar.

How to Get Around in Bordua

Local buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws are the preferred modes of transportation to get around in Bordua. Bicycles and motorbikes can be rented from local shops for a more adventurous experience.

Where to Go Next from Bordua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Silchar - A bustling city located close to Bordua that is known for its historic temples and scenic beauty.
  2. Haflong - A picturesque hill station located in the nearby state of Meghalaya.
  3. Shillong - The capital city of Meghalaya known for its natural beauty and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Bordua is a hidden gem in the North-Eastern part of India with its pristine natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant local culture. By exploring its top attractions, outdoor adventures, food and drink scene, and more, travelers can experience the charm of Bordua and its surroundings to the fullest.
