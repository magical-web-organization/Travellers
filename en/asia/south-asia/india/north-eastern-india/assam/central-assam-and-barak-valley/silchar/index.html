Introduction to Silchar

Silchar is a picturesque city located in Central Assam and Barak Valley in the North-Eastern part of India. It is the second-largest city in the state of Assam and is known for its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and traditional cuisine. The city is surrounded by hills and tea gardens, making it a popular tourist destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Silchar

If you’re planning to visit Silchar, there are plenty of places to explore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khaspur: It is a historic town located around 20 kilometers from Silchar. The town has several ancient temples and palaces that belong to the Kachari Kingdom.
  • Silchar Museum and Archives: The museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of the region. The collection includes historical artifacts, photographs, and documents related to the freedom struggle of India.
  • Manipur Kalakshetra: It is a cultural center that promotes the traditional folk art and dance forms of Manipur. The center also hosts workshops and exhibitions related to these art forms.

Natural Attractions of Silchar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Barak River: It is a scenic river that flows through the city. You can take a boat ride along the river to enjoy the beautiful views of the surrounding hills and greenery.
  • Maibong: It is a small village located 50 kilometers from Silchar. The village is known for its scenic beauty and is surrounded by lush green forests, hills, and waterfalls.
  • Bhubaneswar Temple: It is an ancient temple located on the top of a hill. From the temple, you can enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silchar

Silchar offers several hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Khaspur to Maibong: It is a 20-kilometer route that takes you through scenic forests, hills, and waterfalls.
  • Shillong to Silchar: It is a 300-kilometer route that takes you through the beautiful hills and tea gardens of Assam and Meghalaya.

Best Things to Do in Silchar: Activities and Tours

  • Trekking: The surrounding hills and forests of Silchar offer several trekking routes that are perfect for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Tea Garden Tours: You can visit the tea gardens of Silchar and learn about the process of tea production.
  • Shopping: Silchar is known for its traditional handicrafts and textiles. You can visit the local markets to buy these products.

Food and Drink Scene of Silchar

The food and drink scene of Silchar is a combination of local and international cuisines. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pitha: It is a traditional Assamese dessert made from rice flour and jaggery.
  • Masor Tenga: It is a sour fish curry that is a popular dish in Assam.
  • Biryani: It is a popular South Asian dish made with spiced rice and meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tongba: It is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet and water.
  • Assam Tea: Silchar is known for its tea gardens, and the Assam tea is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Silchar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silchar offers several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

  • Hotel Ellora: It is a three-star hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Hotel Centre Palace: It is another popular hotel that offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • Sudipta Palace: It is a budget hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Silchar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silchar is known for its traditional handicrafts and textiles. Some popular souvenirs and local products include:

  • Mekhla Chador: It is a traditional Assamese attire, and Silchar is known for its intricate designs.
  • Cane Products: Silchar is known for its cane products such as baskets, mats, and furniture.
  • Handicrafts: You can visit the local markets to buy traditional handicrafts such as bamboo crafts, pottery, and brass products.

Local Culture and Customs of Silchar

Silchar is a blend of different cultures and traditions. The people of Silchar are friendly and welcoming, and they celebrate several festivals throughout the year such as Bihu, Durga Puja, and Eid.

Best Time to Visit Silchar

The best time to visit Silchar is from October to March. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy the scenic beauty of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silchar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and tea.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public spaces.
  • Do not disrespect local culture and traditions.
  • Do not take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silchar

Silchar is a safe city for tourists, but it is always recommended to take the necessary precautions such as not walking alone at night and keeping your belongings safe.

Health in Silchar

Silchar has several good hospitals and healthcare centers that offer quality medical services. It is recommended to carry your prescribed medications and consult a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Silchar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Silchar

The nearest airport to Silchar is Silchar Airport, which is located around 30 kilometers from the city. The airport is well-connected to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Silchar

Silchar has a well-connected bus network, and you can easily find buses from major cities in Assam.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can also travel to Silchar by train. The nearest railway station is Silchar Railway Station, which is well-connected to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Silchar

Silchar has a good local transportation network, and you can easily find taxis and auto-rickshaws to travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from Silchar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shillong: It is a popular tourist destination located around 200 kilometers from Silchar. The city is known for its natural beauty and is surrounded by hills and waterfalls.
  • Manipur: It is a neighboring state of Assam and is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Silchar is a beautiful city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. It is a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to explore the North-Eastern part of India. Plan your trip to Silchar today and experience the beauty of this charming city!
