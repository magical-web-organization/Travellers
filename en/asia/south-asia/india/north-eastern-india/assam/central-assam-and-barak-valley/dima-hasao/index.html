Introduction to Dima Hasao

Deep in the breathtaking mountains of Central Assam and the Barak Valley, lies the spectacularly beautiful district of Dima Hasao. Known for its scenic beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures, Dima Hasao is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and culture seekers. With its unmatched beauty, exotic flora and fauna and ancient cultural traditions, Dima Hasao is a unique travel destination that must be explored at least once in a lifetime.

List of Places to Visit in Dima Hasao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maibong Palace: Maibong was the capital of the Dimasa Kingdom from the 16th to 18th century. The palace ruins, capitol building, and ruins of the royal garden are must-visit attractions.
  • Richambera Mound: This is an excavated site that dates back to 8th-9th century. Exhibits of terracotta and ancient artifacts are on display to explore the history of the region.
  • Haflong Lake: Haflong lake is located in the heart of the town & offers plenty of recreational activities. Walk around the lake, shop at nearby stores, or try your hand at fishing for some unique Hangu fish in the lake.

Natural Attractions of Dima Hasao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jatinga Valley: Jatinga Valley is a bird watching paradise, which attracts bird watchers and ornithologists alike. Special birding festivals are organized in November and December, where the rare and endemic species can be seen.
  • Wao Kongso Waterfalls: The waterfall is nestled amidst lush greenery and is a popular weekend getaway spot. The area also has many trekking and mountain biking trails for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Dima Hasao Wildlife Sanctuary: The sanctuary is home to rare and unique species such as Hoolock Gibbon, hill mynah, slow loris, etc. Visitors can explore the sanctuary on jeep safaris or elephant rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dima Hasao

Dima Hasao is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a vast and varied terrain. The region has many trekking and biking trails, suitable for both seasoned trekkers and beginners.

  • Haflong Hill Trek: The Haflong Hill Trek is a three-day trek that takes trekkers through picturesque villages, tea gardens, and lush green forests.
  • Umrangshu Trek: This trekking route is moderately challenging and offers spectacular views of the Dima Hasao valley, and Wao Kongso waterfall.
  • Biking through the Barail range: This is a four-day biking trip through dense forests and scenic valleys, where visitors can enjoy the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Dima Hasao: Activities and Tours

  • Camping at Jatinga: Camping amidst nature’s beauty can be an awe-inspiring experience. Visitors can explore the birdlife and scenic landscapes while camping under the stars.
  • Tea plantations: Dima Hasao is famous for its tea plantations, where visitors can learn about the process of tea production, tea tasting, and tea gardening.
  • Fishing in lakes: Famed for its untouched nature, visitors can also enjoy fishing in the many lakes in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dima Hasao

Day 1: Arrival and relax at your resort. Day 2: Sightseeing tour of Haflong, Haflong Lake, and enjoy water sports activities. Day 3: Trekking to Umrangshu, Wao Kongso Waterftall. Day 4: Wildlife safari in Dima Hasao Wildlife Sanctuary, Gibbon Resort. Day 5: Jatinga Valley bird watching tour. Day 6: Cycling tour of the Barail hills. Day 7: Relax and pack for departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Dima Hasao

Must-Try Dishes

Dima Hasao has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its distinct cuisine that is prepared using local ingredients and age-old cooking methods. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Sunga saul Witok: Rice cooked in bamboo tubes is a traditional Dimasa dish that is known for its unique aroma and taste.
  • Pork Bhuna: A blend of pork cooked with a mix of spices and herbs, popular in the northeast region.
  • Chicken with Bamboo Shoot: A quintessential northeast delicacy that is a must-try while in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Chai: The local tea is fragrant and strong, a blend of traditional and modern tea making techniques.
  • Apong: A fermented rice beverage with a unique taste, perfect to enjoy with local delicacies.

Where to Stay in Dima Hasao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dima Hasao has a range of accommodation options, from comfortable hotels to budget-friendly hostels that cater to all kinds of tourists.

  • Pakshi Resort: Nestled in the heart of the valley, the Pakshi resort offers luxury accommodation facilities with world-class amenities and breathtaking scenic views.
  • North Cachar Hills Tourist Lodge: Located in Haflong, this budget-friendly lodge is ideal for travellers on a budget, with basic facilities and clean rooms.

What to Buy in Dima Hasao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Handicrafts: The district is known for its handicrafts and traditional items made by the Dimasa tribals, including bamboo baskets, traditional garments, and handloom fabrics.
  • Tea: Dima Hasao’s tea is well-known throughout the world for its distinctive taste and aroma.

Local Culture and Customs of Dima Hasao

Dima Hasao is known for its rich and diverse cultural heritage, and the Dimasa tribe is one of the major indigenous communities that pass their culture and customs through generations. Tourists are encouraged to respect their beliefs and always seek permission before clicking photographs at any ceremonial events.

Best Time to Visit Dima Hasao

The best time to visit Dima Hasao is between October and May, when the temperatures are cool and pleasant, and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dima Hasao

Do:

  • Respect the local cultures and customs of Dimasa tribe.
  • Keep clean and green by not littering, and carrying non-plastic utensils.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of tribal people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dima Hasao

Dima Hasao is generally safe for tourists, but it is important to follow basic safety measures to avoid any unpleasant experiences. Avoid venturing into isolated areas alone, especially during late night hours.

Health in Dima Hasao

Before planning your trip, it is always advisable to consult a physician for the necessary vaccinations, especially when traveling from another region or country. It is always best to carry a basic first aid kit with essential medication, in case of any emergency.

Getting to Dima Hasao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dima Hasao

The nearest airports to Dima Hasao are:

  • Kumbhirgram Airport, Silchar: Kumbhirgram Airport is the nearest airport to Dima Hasao (114 kms).

Bus Stations Near Dima Hasao

The nearest bus stations are:

  • Haflong Bus Stand: Regular buses connect Haflong to nearby cities like Silchar, Guwahati, and Shillong.
  • Maibang Bus Stand: Maibang has regular bus services to Guwahati and other northeastern states.

Other Options

  • Cabs and Taxis: Cabs and taxis are available in Dima Hasao, and are recommended for greater flexibility for transportation.

How to Get Around in Dima Hasao

The best way to get around Dima Hasao is by hiring local transportation services such as taxis, auto-rickshaws, or bikes.

Where to Go Next from Dima Hasao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Silchar: Silchar is located at a distance of 82 km from Haflong and is known for its scenic views and historical sites.
  • Shillong: The capital city of Meghalaya, Shillong, is known for its natural beauty, waterfalls, and vibrant culture and is located 234 km from Haflong.

Conclusion

Dima Hasao is a hidden gem that offers visitors the opportunity to explore stunning landscapes, authentic cultures, and experience adventurous activities. This comprehensive guide will help tourists explore the best of Dima Hasao by highlighting all the must-visit attractions, point out great accommodations and eateries, and providing essential travel tips to make every trip to Dima Hasao memorable.
