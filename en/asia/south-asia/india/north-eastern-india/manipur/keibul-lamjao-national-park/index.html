Introduction to Keibul Lamjao National Park

Located in the northeastern state of Manipur, India, Keibul Lamjao National Park is a unique wetland ecosystem that is home to an array of flora and fauna. It is the only floating national park in the world and is known for being the last natural habitat of the endangered Manipur Brow-Antlered Deer, also known as the Sangai Deer. The park covers an area of about 40 square kilometers and is situated in the middle of the Loktak Lake, the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India. The park’s pristine natural beauty and rich cultural heritage make it a popular tourist destination.

  • The people of Manipur are friendly and welcoming, making visitors feel right at home

Best Time to Visit Keibul Lamjao National Park

The best time to visit Keibul Lamjao National Park is from November to April when the weather is pleasant, and the flowers and trees are in full bloom. The park is also accessible during monsoons, but some of the activities may be restricted.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keibul Lamjao National Park

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and natural habitats.
  • Take guided tours or hire a local guide to explore the park.
  • Carry sufficient drinking water and wear comfortable clothing and shoes.
  • Enjoy the local culture and cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Carry plastic bags inside the park.
  • Engage in any activity that may harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keibul Lamjao National Park

The park is generally safe for visitors. However, it is essential to take necessary precautions while exploring the wilderness. Visitors should be cautious of the weather, and must avoid venturing out during heavy rains. It is advisable to take a local guide, especially while trekking or exploring the park’s natural surroundings.

Health in Keibul Lamjao National Park

Visitors should take precautions to avoid diseases like malaria, dengue, and other mosquito-borne illnesses. It is advisable to wear protective clothing, apply insect repellent, and carry mosquito nets while traveling in rural areas. Visitors should carry appropriate medication for any health issues pre-existing or otherwise.

Getting to Keibul Lamjao National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keibul Lamjao National Park

The nearest airport is Imphal International Airport, which is about 50 km from the park. There are regular flights from major cities across India.

Bus Stations Near Keibul Lamjao National Park

Imphal is well-connected by bus to most major cities in North-East India, including Guwahati, Dimapur, and Shillong.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to reach the park. Private and public transport options are available from Imphal.

How to Get Around in Keibul Lamjao National Park

The best way to get around in Keibul Lamjao National Park is to hire a local guide or take a guided tour. They provide the necessary insights and also take you to various attraction points around the park.

Where to Go Next from Keibul Lamjao National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Imphal - the capital city of Manipur
  • Loktak Lake - the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India
  • Khwairamband Bazaar - an all-women’s market in Imphal

Conclusion

With its breathtaking natural beauty, unique floating ecosystem, and rich cultural heritage, Keibul Lamjao National Park is a must-visit destination in Northeast India. The park offers numerous activities for visitors, including trekking, boating, and wildlife spotting, making it an ideal spot for adventure enthusiasts and wildlife lovers. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore the serene beauty of Keibul Lamjao National Park.
