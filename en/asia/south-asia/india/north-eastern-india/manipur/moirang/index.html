Introduction to Moirang

Nestled in the lush green valleys of Manipur, Moirang is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This small town is steeped in history and culture, making it the perfect destination for travelers seeking to explore off-the-beaten-path destinations in India. Originating from the Meitei language, the name Moirang means “a place where water is collected.” The town is located on the banks of the Loktak Lake and is surrounded by rolling hills, making it a picturesque location.

List of Places to Visit in Moirang

Moirang boasts a wide range of attractions that highlight its rich cultural heritage and scenic natural beauty. Below are the top places to visit in Moirang.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moirang Temple: Built-in the 12th century, this temple is one of the oldest in Manipur and is dedicated to Lord Thangjing - the god of Moirang.

  • INA Memorial: This museum commemorates the Indian National Army’s role in the country’s independence struggle and the Battle of Moirang.

  • Loktak Lake: This lake is the largest freshwater lake in North East India and is known for its unique ecosystem and the ecosystem’s flagship species - the Manipur brow-antlered deer.

Natural Attractions of Moirang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: This is the only floating national park in the world and is home to the endangered Manipur brow-antlered deer, sangai, as well as other species of wildlife.

  • Khonghampat Orchidarium: This orchid garden houses over 220 species of orchids, making it one of the biggest orchid reserves in India.

  • Thumal Bazaar: This traditional village showcases the local culture of Moirang and is famous for its handloom weaving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moirang

The hilly terrain and lush forests surrounding Moirang provide ample opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following are some of the popular routes that offer breathtaking views of the scenic locales.

  • Loktak Lake to Thanga: This 18 km route takes you along the banks of the lake, through wetlands and floating islands.

  • Loktak Lake to Sendra Island: This 20 km route takes you across the lake and ends at the Sendra Island tourist complex.

Best Things to Do in Moirang: Activities and Tours

Moirang offers a plethora of activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s interests. Below are some of the best things to do in Moirang.

  • Boat Ride on Loktak Lake: Explore the serene waters of the Loktak Lake on a country boat and witness the unique floating houses built on the phumdis or floating islands.

  • Visit to the INA Memorial: Pay your respects to the Indian National Army’s brave soldiers who fought for India’s freedom struggle in the Battle of Moirang.

Food and Drink Scene of Moirang

Moirang is famous for its unique blend of cuisines that include both Indian and Southeast Asian flavors. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages that you should not miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chamthong: This is a vegetable stew made with assorted vegetables and flavored with bamboo shoots.

  • Eromba: This is a typical Manipuri dish made with boiled vegetables mashed with smoked or fermented fish and chili.

  • Singju: This is a crunchy salad made with lotus stems and various vegetables, typically served as a starter.

Local Beverages

  • Yu/Zu: Made with fermented rice and served in bamboo tubes, this is the staple drink of Manipur.

  • Chaang: A traditional rice beer, with a mild alcoholic content and served in bamboo tubes.

Where to Stay in Moirang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moirang offers a wide range of accommodation options for every traveler’s needs, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • The Classic Hotel Moirang: This hotel provides comfortable rooms with all modern amenities like air conditioning, flat-screen TVs, and 24-hour hot water.

  • Moirang Tourist Home: This guesthouse offers clean and spacious rooms and is known for its impeccable service and hospitality.

What to Buy in Moirang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping in Moirang is a unique experience, and you must take home some of the local products and souvenirs.

  • Handloom Fabrics: Moirang is famous for its handloom weaving, and you must purchase some of the exquisite fabrics made by the local artisans.

  • Bamboo and Cane Handicrafts: From baskets to bamboo mugs, you can get a wide variety of handicrafts made of bamboo and cane.

Local Culture and Customs of Moirang

Moirang has a rich cultural heritage that is showcased through the town’s various festivals and customs.

  • Moirang Lai Haraoba: This is the most significant festival celebrated in Moirang and showcases the town’s indigenous culture.

Best Time to Visit Moirang

The best time to visit Moirang is from October to March. The weather during these months is pleasant, and the town is lush green, making it ideal for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moirang

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting the temples and other religious places.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Engage in wildlife poaching or illegal activities in the national park.
  • Litter the environment or the lake.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moirang

Moirang is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take precautions like avoiding visiting secluded areas at night.

Health in Moirang

The healthcare services in Moirang are limited, and it is always advisable to carry a first aid kit and any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Moirang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moirang is well-connected to the other parts of Manipur and other cities in India through various modes of transportation.

Airports near Moirang

  • Imphal International Airport: The airport is located approximately 45 km from Moirang town.

Bus Stations near Moirang

  • Imphal Bus Stand: This is the nearest bus station, located approximately 45 km from Moirang town.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: You can hire a private taxi from Imphal or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Moirang

The best way to get around Moirang is by hiring a rented car or taxi. Bicycles and motorbikes are also available for hire at nominal rates.

Where to Go Next from Moirang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Imphal: This is the capital city of Manipur and is known for its picturesque landscapes and rich cultural heritage.

  • Ukhrul: This town is located in the northeastern part of Manipur and is known for its scenic beauty.
