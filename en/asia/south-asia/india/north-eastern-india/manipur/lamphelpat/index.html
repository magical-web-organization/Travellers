Introduction to Lamphelpat

Lamphelpat is a quaint town located in the Imphal West district of Manipur in North-Eastern India. Known for its natural beauty and historical significance, Lamphelpat offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural insights, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals. The town is named after the ‘Lamphel’ plant found in abundance here which is traditionally used in weaving clothes.

List of Places to Visit in Lamphelpat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manipur State Museum

One of the must-visit places in Lamphelpat is the Manipur State Museum. The museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich history, art, and culture of Manipur. The museum displays various items like ancient manuscripts, coins, textiles, pottery, weapons, and various other historical artifacts.

Kangla Fort

Kangla Fort, also known as the Palace of Kangla, is a significant historical landmark in Lamphelpat. The fort was once the residence of the Manipuri Kings before it was destroyed by the British. Today, the fort has been restored and turned into a museum where visitors can learn about the history of Manipur.

Natural Attractions of Lamphelpat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Imphal River

The Imphal River is a beautiful waterway that flows through Lamphelpat. It is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and camping. It is said that the river is rich in fishes like catfish, eel, and many others.

Shirui Hill

Shirui Hill is located in the Ukhrul district of Manipur, just 80 kilometers from Lamphelpat. The hill is famous for its exotic flowers, the Shirui lily, that is found only in this region. It is also a great spot for hiking and scenic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lamphelpat

Some of the popular hiking and biking trails in Lamphelpat include:

  • Lamphelpat to Kangla Fort
  • Lamphelpat to ICCR (Indian Council for Cultural Relations) Complex
  • Lamphelpat to Sanjenthong

These routes offer beautiful sceneries of the town and rural Manipur.

Best Things to Do in Lamphelpat: Activities and Tours

Loktak Lake Excursion

Loktak Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India and is located near Lamphelpat. One of the best things to do in Lamphelpat is to take a day trip to the lake, where you can explore the floating islands, known as phumdis, and visit the Keibul Lamjao National Park, which is the only floating national park in the world.

Shri Govindjee Temple

The Shri Govindjee Temple is a famous religious landmark in Lamphelpat, built-in 1846 by King Gambhir Singh. The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha and attracts devotees from all over the country. The temple is one of the architectural wonders of Manipur, adorned with intricate carvings and exquisite designs.

Food and Drink Scene of Lamphelpat

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular Manipuri dishes to try in Lamphelpat include:

  • Chak-hao Kheer (black rice pudding)
  • Paknam (fish curry)
  • Chamthong or Kangsoi (local vegetable soup)
  • Singju (healthy vegetable salad)

Local Beverages

The local brew of Lamphelpat is known as Chingkao Kheer. It is made by cooking rice and yeast together, which is then left to ferment for a week or two.

Local Culture and Customs of Lamphelpat

The people of Lamphelpat and Manipur, in general, are very friendly and hospitable. The local culture is characterized by colorful festivals, traditional dance forms like Manipuri and Naga dance, and traditional attire. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Lamphelpat

The best time to visit Lamphelpat is during the winter months, from November to February. The weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lamphelpat

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and shop for local products
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their way of life

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or show public display of affection
  • Disrespect religious monuments or symbols
  • Litter or harm the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Lamphelpat

Lamphelpat is a safe town, and the crime rate is relatively low. However, visitors are advised to keep their valuables safe, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Lamphelpat

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like malaria, dengue, and Japanese encephalitis. It is advisable to carry insect repellents and wear long-sleeved clothes.

Getting to Lamphelpat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lamphelpat

The closest airport to Lamphelpat is the Imphal International Airport, located about 7 kilometers from the town center. From the airport, visitors can hire taxis or take buses to Lamphelpat.

Bus Stations Near Lamphelpat

The Imphal Bus Station is located in the heart of the city and is well connected to different parts of Manipur and neighboring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lamphelpat via trains that stop at nearby railway stations like Dimapur and Guwahati.
