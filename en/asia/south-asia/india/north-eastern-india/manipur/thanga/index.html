Thanga Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Thanga

Located in the North-Eastern region of India, Thanga is a small village in the state of Manipur, India. This serene and peaceful destination is surrounded by the Loktak Lake, which is the largest freshwater lake in Asia. The pristine natural beauty of Thanga and its surrounding areas have turned this place into a popular tourist destination. In this comprehensive travel guide of Thanga, we will show you some of the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and other essential information for a perfect trip to our peaceful village.

List of Places to Visit in Thanga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thanga has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its numerous museums and historical places. Here are few of them to explore:

  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: This is the only floating national park in the world, located in the Loktak Lake known for its rich biodiversity.
  • Thanga Memorial: A monument dedicated to the martyrs who lost their lives during the Indian independence movement.
  • Manipur State Museum: This museum houses artifacts related to the culture and history of Manipur, including the traditional clothing, musical instruments, weapons, and pottery.

Natural Attractions of Thanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thanga is a nature lover’s paradise with numerous outdoor activities and scenic views:

  • Loktak Lake: This is the most popular attraction in Thanga, and for a reason – it’s the largest freshwater lake in Asia and is studded with numerous floating islands called phumdis.
  • Thanga Island: This is a small island surrounded by the Loktak Lake.
  • Waithou Lake: This is a peaceful and serene lake in the town of Waithou, which is nearby Thanga.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thanga

Thanga has several hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the village. A few of these trails are:

  • Loktak Hiking Trail: This trail provides stunning views of the surrounding hills and nearby villages.
  • Waithou-Bishnupur Cycling Route: This 25 km route passes through various villages and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Thanga: Activities and Tours

Thanga has numerous activities one can try, such as:

  • Bird Watching: The Keibul Lamjao National Park is home to many migratory birds, making it one of the most popular spots for bird watching in the village.
  • Fishing: The Loktak lake is home to various fish species, including the famous Singju fish, which is also the state fish of Manipur. You can catch and cook your fish by the lake.
  • Boat Rides: Visitors can take boat rides on the Loktak lake to enjoy the serene and peaceful beauty of the village.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thanga

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Thanga, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Keibul Lamjao National Park and the Thanga Memorial.
  • Day 2: Explore the Loktak lake, take a boat ride and try fishing.
  • Day 3: Hike the Loktak Hiking Trail and visit the Waithou Lake.
  • Day 4: Visit the Manipur State Museum and explore Imphal.
  • Day 5: Cycle the Waithou-Bishnupur route and explore the nearby villages.
  • Day 6: Relax by the Loktak lake and try bird watching.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at your hotel or guesthouse before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Thanga

Thanga has a unique cuisine that reflects its cultural diversity. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Singju: A traditional Manipuri salad made with local veggies, topped with fresh fish, and a tangy dressing.
  • Chak-hao Kheer: A dessert made with black sticky rice, milk, sugar, and cardamom.
  • Bamboo Shoot Fry: A delicious stir-fried dish made with bamboo shoots.

Local Beverages

  • Yu: A type of rice beer that is famous in Manipur and often consumed during special occasions or gatherings.

Where to Stay in Thanga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thanga has numerous accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious. Here are a few options:

  • Loktak Lake Resort: This resort offers great views of the Loktak lake and has clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Thanga guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located at the heart of Thanga village.
  • Loktak Bamboo Hut: This is a unique accommodation option that provides visitors an opportunity to stay in traditional bamboo huts.

What to Buy in Thanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thanga has several local markets and shops where you can buy handmade souvenirs and local products. Here are a few of them:

  • Waikhong Thongal Bazaar: This market is known for its handcrafted textiles, including shawls, scarves and table runners made from local fabrics.
  • Loktak Handicrafts Emporium: This shop sells various handicrafts made by local artisans, including bamboo baskets, pottery, and traditional musical instruments.

Local Culture and Customs of Thanga

Thanga is a diverse village with a unique culture and customs. The people of Thanga are mainly Meiteis, and they practice their religion, Sanamahism. Visitors should respect the customs and traditions of the villagers.

Best Time to Visit Thanga

The best time to visit Thanga is between October and March when the weather is generally pleasant, and the lakes and surrounding areas are full of life.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thanga

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Thanga:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the villagers and their religion, Sanamahism.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious destinations.
  • Try the local cuisine and support the local economy by purchasing handmade souvenirs and products.

Don’t:

  • Litter the surrounding areas.
  • Disturb the ecological balance of the lake and its islands.
  • Take photos without seeking permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thanga

Thanga is a relatively safe destination. However, it’s always recommended to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety, such as carrying a map, avoiding late-night walks, and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Thanga

Visitors are advised to consult their doctor before traveling to Thanga, as the village is remote and may not have adequate medical facilities. Visitors should also carry anti-malarial medication, mosquito repellent, and sunscreen to avoid exposure to diseases and sunburns.

Getting to Thanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thanga

The nearest airport to Thanga is Imphal International Airport, which is around 70 km from the village.

Bus Stations Near Thanga

Imphal Bus Stand is the nearest bus station to Thanga, and it is well-connected to other parts of the state of Manipur.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Thanga by hiring a taxi or private car from Imphal or other nearby cities such as Guwahati or Dimapur.

How to Get Around in Thanga

Visitors can rent bicycles or motorbikes to explore the village and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Thanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Thanga are:

  • Imphal: The capital city of Manipur and an important cultural and commercial center of the region.
  • Ukhrul: A scenic district that is home to picturesque waterfalls, Liroi Hill, and the Shirui Lily Flower.

Conclusion

Thanga is a beautiful village that is surrounded by the scenic beauty of the Loktak Lake. The village has its unique culture and traditions, making it a perfect destination for those who seek to explore the lesser-known regions of India. We hope this comprehensive travel guide to Thanga has helped you plan your trip and make the most of this beautiful destination.
