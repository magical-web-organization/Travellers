Introduction to Andro

Andro is a small village located in the Imphal East district of Manipur, Northeast India. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and unique traditions that have been passed down for generations. This hidden gem is the perfect destination for those looking to explore the lesser-known regions of India and experience a unique mix of culture and nature.

List of Places to Visit in Andro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Andro Cultural Museum: The Andro Cultural Museum is a must-visit attraction that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region. It exhibits various artifacts, ancient manuscripts, traditional clothing, and weapons used by the local tribes over the centuries.
  • Chingnunghut Park: Chingnunghut Park is a historical site located in Andro, which commemorates the victory of the Manipuri army over the Burmese in 1824. It is also known for its beautiful garden and a scenic view of the surrounding hills.
  • Loktak Lake: Although not located in Andro, Loktak Lake is a nearby attraction that is worth visiting. It is the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India and is home to several floating islands (phumdis), which can be explored by taking a guided boat ride.

Natural Attractions of Andro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Andro Hill View Point: Andro Hill View Point is a scenic spot that offers a panoramic view of the hills and valleys surrounding Andro. It is a perfect place to witness the sunrise and sunset.
  • Kaina Hill: Kaina Hill is another popular attraction in Andro that offers a scenic view of the surrounding hills and valleys. It is also known for its ancient temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Pumlen Pat: Pumlen Pat is a beautiful lake located near Andro, which is known for boating and fishing. It offers a scenic view of the surrounding hills and is a perfect spot to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Andro

For adventure enthusiasts, Andro offers several hiking and biking trails that take you through the beautiful hills and valleys of the region. Some popular routes include:

  • Andro-Kaina Trek: The Andro-Kaina Trek is a scenic trek that takes you from Andro to Kaina, passing through beautiful hills and forests.
  • Andro-Pumlen Trek: The Andro-Pumlen Trek is another popular trek that takes you from Andro to Pumlen Lake, passing through scenic villages and forests.

Best Things to Do in Andro: Activities and Tours

  • Taste Local Cuisine: Andro is known for its unique blend of local cuisine, which includes dishes like Chamthong (fish stew), Ngari (fermented fish), and Eromba (spicy vegetable stew). Take a food tour and taste the local delicacies.
  • Participate in a Cultural Workshop: Andro offers several cultural workshops where you can learn traditional crafts, pottery making, and weaving from the locals.
  • Explore Hidden Trails: Take a guided tour of the hidden trails and explore the unexplored forests and hills of Andro.

Food and Drink Scene of Andro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chamthong: Chamthong is a fish stew made with local vegetables and herbs. It is a staple dish in Andro and is a must-try.
  • Ngari: Ngari is a fermented fish dish that is popular in Andro. It is served as a side dish and is often eaten with rice.
  • Eromba: Eromba is a spicy vegetable stew that is made with local herbs and vegetables. It is often served with rice or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Yu**: Yu is a traditional rice beer that is brewed by the locals. It is a popular beverage in Andro and is often served during festivals and ceremonies.

Where to Stay in Andro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Andro Resort: Andro Resort is a popular accommodation option in Andro. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a beautiful garden.
  • Sekmai Guest House: Sekmai Guest House is another popular accommodation option in Andro. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a scenic view of the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Andro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Pottery: Andro is known for its traditional pottery, which is made by the locals using ancient techniques. It is a perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Hand-woven Textiles: Andro is also known for its hand-woven textiles and clothing, which are made by the locals using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Andro

Andro is known for its unique cultural heritage, which is showcased in various festivals and ceremonies throughout the year. The locals are friendly and welcoming and are known for their hospitality. Some unique customs and traditions of Andro include:

  • Lai Haraoba Festival: Lai Haraoba is a festival that is celebrated in Andro to honor the local deities. It is a colorful festival that includes music, dance, and offerings to the deities.
  • Traditional Pottery Making: Pottery making is an ancient tradition in Andro, which is still practiced by the locals. They use traditional techniques and materials, making each piece unique.

Best Time to Visit Andro

The best time to visit Andro is during the winter season (October to February), when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season (June to September) should be avoided as it is prone to heavy rainfall, which can disrupt travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Andro

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Experience the local cuisine and participate in cultural workshops.
  • Be respectful to the locals and the environment.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any behavior that may offend the locals.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Andro

Andro is a safe destination for tourists, with low levels of crime. However, it is always advisable to take standard precautions such as keeping valuables safe and not traveling alone at night.

Health in Andro

Andro has adequate healthcare facilities, but travelers are advised to carry a basic medical kit consisting of medication for common ailments such as cold, fever, and diarrhea.

Getting to Andro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Andro can be reached by air, road, and rail. The nearest airport is Imphal Airport, which is around 24 km away from Andro. The nearest railway station is Dimapur Railway Station, which is around 215 km away from Andro.

Airports Near Andro

  • Imphal Airport (24 km)

Bus Stations Near Andro

  • Imphal Bus Station (23 km)
  • Guwahati Bus Station (288 km)

Other Options

  • Taxis and rental cars are available from Imphal and can be hired for travel to Andro.

How to Get Around in Andro

Andro is a small village, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, taxis and rental cars are available for those who wish to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Andro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Andro include:

  • Imphal: Imphal is the capital city of Manipur and is known for its rich history and culture.
  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: Keibul Lamjao National Park is located near Loktak Lake and is known for its unique ecosystem, which is home to the endangered Manipuri deer.
  • Ukhrul: Ukhrul is a beautiful town situated in the hills of Manipur, known for its picturesque landscapes and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Andro is a hidden gem in Northeast India, offering a unique mix of culture and nature. From its rich cultural heritage to its scenic beauty, there is something for everyone in Andro. Plan your trip to Andro today and experience the beauty of this lesser-known region of India.
