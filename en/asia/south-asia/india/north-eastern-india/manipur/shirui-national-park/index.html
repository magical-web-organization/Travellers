Introduction to Shirui National Park

Shirui National Park is nestled in the northeastern state of Manipur in India. It’s a remote and wild area, characterised by undulating hills, dense forests, and colourful wildflowers. The main attraction of the park is Shirui Lily, a rare and endangered flower that blooms once a year. Moreover, with dense vegetation, gushing water streams, and wildlife, the park comes alive in a different way throughout the year, and you need the right kind of guide to appreciate it to the fullest.

List of Places to Visit in Shirui National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main attractions of Shirui National Park is the local culture and customs, and in order to understand these fully, visitors should take a trip to the local museums and historical places. The best of which including:

  • Manipur State Museum: A treasure trove of historical artefacts, paintings, and portraits of kings that chronicle the origin and evolution of the region over centuries.

  • Kangla Fort: A fort built in the 17th century that was once the seat of power of Manipur’s rulers.

Natural Attractions of Shirui National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shirui National Park has a wealth of outdoor adventures and scenic vistas to offer. Here are some of the best:

  • Shirui Peak: At an altitude of 2,835 meters, Shirui Peak is the highest peak in the park, offering breathtaking views of the untouched valley below.

  • Khayang Peak: A short trek from the park entrance, Khayang Peak is an ideal spot to enjoy the sunrise over the hills and the valley.

  • Kachouphung Lake: A beautiful natural lake nestled amidst the hills, this spot is perfect for picnics, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shirui National Park

Shirui National Park is an ideal destination for hiking and biking. There are many scenic routes to choose from, the best of which include:

  • Shirui Peak Trek: This trek takes you to the top of the Shirui Peak, which offers a panoramic view of the park.

  • Khayang Peak Trek: This trek takes you to the top of Khayang Peak, where you can marvel at the beautiful sunrise views.

  • Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: This trek takes you through the beautiful Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary, where you can spot a variety of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Shirui National Park: Activities and Tours

Here are some activities and tours to enjoy during your visit to Shirui National Park:

  • Shirui Lily Festival: This is an annual festival held in May to celebrate the blooming of the rare Shirui Lily.

  • Bird Watching: With over 200 bird species, Shirui National Park is a bird watcher’s paradise.

  • Wildlife Safari: Take a wildlife safari to spot some of the rare and endangered species found in the park, such as the Hoolock Gibbon and the Sangai deer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shirui National Park

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Shirui National Park:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in

Day 2: Shirui Peak Trek

Day 3: Khayang Peak Trek

Day 4-5: Wildlife Safari and Bird Watching

Day 6: Visit to the Shirui Lily Festival

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Shirui National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some must-try dishes in Shirui National Park:

  • Bamboo Shoot Curry: A spicy curry made with locally grown bamboo shoots.

  • Iromba: A local dish made with mashed boiled vegetables, chili, and smoked fish.

Local Beverages

Here are some local beverages to try in Shirui National Park:

  • Chaak: A local rice beer brewed in traditional bamboo baskets.

  • Kombucha: A fermented tea that is a good digestive and immunity booster.

Where to Stay in Shirui National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a few options for accommodation in Shirui National Park that cater to different budgets, such as:

  • Hotel North Garden: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • Shirui Peak Resort: A mid-range option that provides an eco-friendly stay in wooden cottages amidst the forest.

  • Nakai Lake Resorts: A luxury option that offers a peaceful stay near Kachouphung Lake.

What to Buy in Shirui National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to take home from Shirui National Park:

  • Handmade Shawls and Wraps: The local shawls and wraps made from merino wool and silk are perfect for keeping warm during the winter months.

  • Local Handicrafts: Handmade bowls, baskets, and other products made from bamboo are perfect for adding an element of traditional Manipuri style to your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Shirui National Park

The local culture of Manipur is diverse and rich, and it’s worth taking some time to learn about it during your visit. Here are some customs and traditions to be mindful of:

  • Respect elders and greet people with “Khoinai” and “Khoiromba”.

  • Pay attention to the traditional dress of the people and be respectful of it.

Best Time to Visit Shirui National Park

The best time to visit Shirui National Park is between October and May, when the weather is pleasant and most flora and fauna can be spotted.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shirui National Park

Do:

  • Book guided tours to know about the park’s history, wildlife and culture.

  • Carry enough cash and carry your own water bottles to minimize plastic waste.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or skimpy clothing that could be deemed inappropriate.

  • Litter or damage the natural habitat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shirui National Park

Given that the park is located in a remote and hilly region, travellers are advised to follow basic safety precautions such as:

  • Be cautious while trekking or hiking.

  • Carry appropriate trekking gear such as comfortable shoes, a hat, and sunscreen, and avoid carrying valuables.

Health in Shirui National Park

Visitors to Shirui National Park are advised to take the necessary precautions related to health, such as:

  • Carry a basic first aid kit for minor injuries and ailments.

  • Carry insect repellent to prevent bites from insects and mosquitoes.

Getting to Shirui National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shirui National Park

The nearest airport to Shirui National Park is in Imphal. There are daily flights from major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Shirui National Park

The bus station in Imphal is the nearest bus stop to Shirui National Park. Buses are available from Guwahati and other major cities in the region.

Other Options

Private taxis and shared jeeps are the other modes of transport to get to Shirui National Park. It is always advisable to book your transport in advance for a hassle-free travel experience.

How to Get Around in Shirui National Park

Hiring a local guide is the best way to get around Shirui National Park. They offer customised tours and have a wealth of knowledge about the park’s history, culture, flora, and fauna.

Where to Go Next from Shirui National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Shirui National Park include:

  • Imphal: The capital city of Manipur is home to several museums, temples, and markets that offer a glimpse into the local life and culture.

  • Loktak Lake: A large freshwater lake famous for its floating phumdis (floating islands) and unique ecosystem.

  • Ukhrul: A small town nearby famous for the production of Naga shawls and handloom products.

Conclusion

Shirui National Park is a hidden gem in Manipur that offers an unforgettable experience for travellers who are willing to explore beyond the beaten path. Its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse flora and fauna make it an ideal destination for a unique and adventurous vacation. By following the tips and suggestions in this guide, you can make the most of your trip to Shirui National Park.
