Introduction to Manipur

Manipur is located in North-Eastern India, sharing borders with Nagaland, Assam, and Mizoram. The state is known for its scenic beauty, rich culture, and vibrant festivals. Manipur is often called the “Land of Jewels” due to its natural beauty and is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those interested in Indian culture.

List of Places to Visit in Manipur

There are various tourist attractions in Manipur, including culturally important landmarks and natural wonders. Here are some of the best places to visit in Manipur.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kangla Fort: The historical Kangla Fort was the palace of the Manipur kings and is now a popular tourist spot. The fort showcases the rich history and culture of Manipur.

  • Manipur State Museum: The Manipur State Museum has an impressive collection of tribal artifacts, costumes, and historical items. It is a great place to learn about Manipur’s rich heritage.

  • Shree Govindajee Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha and is considered one of the most important Hindu temples in Manipur. The temple has stunning architecture and is a must-visit for spirituality enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Manipur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loktak Lake: Considered the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, Loktak Lake is known for its floating islands, known as phumdis. The lake is also home to the endangered Manipur brow-antlered deer and is a popular tourist spot for bird watching and boating.

  • Khonghampat Orchidarium: The orchidarium features over 110 rare species of orchids, including the state flower of Manipur, the Siroi lily. It is a great place for nature lovers and photographers and is located near Imphal.

  • Dzukou Valley: The Dzukou Valley is a paradise for trekkers and hikers. The valley is home to some of the most beautiful flora and fauna in Northeast India and is a perfect escape from the bustle of city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manipur

Manipur has some of the best hiking and biking trails in Northeast India. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Shirui Peak Trek: The trek starts from the base camp of Ukhrul and goes up to the Shirui peak, which is the highest point in Manipur. The trek is challenging but rewarding, with breathtaking views of the surroundings.

  • Barak Waterfall Trek: The trek to Barak Waterfall starts from the campsite in Senapati district and is a moderate trekking trail. The trek is surrounded by lush green forests and offers amazing views of the waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Manipur: Activities and Tours

  • Imphal Polo Ground: Manipur is considered the birthplace of modern polo, and the Imphal Polo Ground is a popular spot for polo enthusiasts. Visitors can watch a game or take a guided tour to learn about the history of polo in Manipur.

  • Ningthoukhongjam: Ningthoukhongjam is a traditional Manipuri dance form that showcases the beauty and grace of Manipuri women. Visitors can attend dance performances or take a class to learn the dance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manipur

Day 1: Explore Imphal, visit Kangla Fort and Shree Govindajee Temple

Day 2: Visit Manipur State Museum, and then, take a tour of Khonghampat Orchidarium

Day 3: Trek to the Dzukou Valley

Day 4: Drive to Loktak Lake for bird watching and boating

Day 5: Visit the Barak Waterfall and go on a hiking trail

Day 6: Attend a Ningthoukhongjam dance performance and visit Imphal Polo Ground

Day 7: Travel back to Guwahati.

Food and Drink Scene of Manipur

Manipur cuisine is known for its unique flavors and use of local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chak-hao Kheer: A dessert made of black rice and condensed milk, Chak-hao Kheer is a specialty of Manipur.

  • Iromba: Iromba is a traditional dish of Manipur, made of mashed boiled vegetables mixed with fermented fish.

  • Singju: Singju is a salad made of finely chopped vegetables mixed with roasted gram flour and served with Ngari, a type of fermented fish.

Local Beverages

  • Chai: Locally brewed tea with milk and spices is a popular beverage in Manipur.

  • Rice Beer: Made by fermenting rice, Rice beer is a traditional alcoholic beverage in Manipur.

Where to Stay in Manipur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various options for accommodation in Manipur, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Classic Grande: A mid-range hotel in Imphal, offering comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

  • Hotel Imphal: The oldest hotel in Imphal with historically significant architecture, offering luxury rooms and suites.

  • Youth Hostel Imphal: A budget hostel located in the heart of Imphal, providing comfortable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Manipur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Manipur is renowned for its handicrafts, and visitors should browse local markets to buy unique souvenirs. Here are some popular options:

  • Phanek: A traditional Manipuri wraparound skirt, available in various colors and patterns.

  • Handloom fabrics: Manipur produces excellent quality handloom fabrics that are perfect for clothing or home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Manipur

Manipur has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from various tribes and communities. Visitors should respect local customs and follow dress codes while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Manipur

The best time to visit Manipur is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manipur

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Take off shoes before entering religious sites.

  • Try local cuisine and purchase traditional handicrafts.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious sites.

  • Litter and damage natural or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manipur

Manipur is generally safe for visitors, but it is always wise to take precautions while traveling. Stay away from protests or crowded areas and do not wander alone at night.

Health in Manipur

Visitors should consult a doctor before traveling to Manipur and get vaccinated against diseases such as typhoid, hepatitis A, and B. It is also essential to carry mosquito repellent and take precautions against malaria and dengue.

Getting to Manipur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manipur

The closest airport to Manipur is Bir Tikendrajit International Airport in Imphal. There are regular flights to major Indian cities, including Delhi and Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near Manipur

There are regular buses to Imphal from nearby cities such as Guwahati and Dimapur.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to get to Manipur and explore the state.

How to Get Around in Manipur

Visitors can use shared taxis or local buses to get around Manipur or can hire a private car with a driver for convenience.

Where to Go Next from Manipur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kohima: The capital of Nagaland, Kohima is known for its natural beauty and historical significance. Visitors can explore the markets, visit the War Cemetery, or trek to Dzukou Valley.

  • Kaziranga National Park: Located in Assam, Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is renowned for its one-horned rhinoceroses.

Conclusion

Manipur is a remarkable destination with a rich cultural and natural heritage. Visitors can experience the unique flavors of Manipuri cuisine, explore picturesque landscapes, and learn about the state’s history and culture. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan and enjoy your trip to Manipur.
