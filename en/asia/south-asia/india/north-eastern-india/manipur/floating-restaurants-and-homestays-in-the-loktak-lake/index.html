Introduction to Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake

Located in Manipur, in North-Eastern India, the Loktak Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India and an ultimate travel destination for nature enthusiasts. The lake is home to the world’s only floating national park, the Keibul Lamjao National Park, and is protected by the Ramsar Convention on Wetlands.

Floating restaurants and homestays are two of the most unique experiences that you can have in Loktak Lake. These places provide visitors with the opportunity to explore the lake’s beauty, taste the local cuisine, and engage with the local culture. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to the floating restaurants and homestays in the Loktak Lake in Manipur, India.

List of Places to Visit in Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

A visit to the floating restaurants and homestays at the Loktak Lake would be incomplete without exploring the cultural and historical attractions in the region. Here are a few places that you should visit during your stay:

  • INA Memorial: The Indian National Army Memorial is dedicated to the soldiers who fought for India’s independence from British rule.

  • Shaheed Minar: The Martyr’s Tower is a symbol of the state’s struggles against oppression and injustice.

  • Manipur State Museum: Located in Imphal, Manipur State Museum showcases the state’s rich history, culture and traditions.

Natural Attractions in Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty to do and see in Loktak Lake. Here are some of the best natural attractions to add to your travel itinerary:

  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: The only floating national park in the world, this park is home to the endangered Manipur brow-antlered deer or Sangai.

  • Sendra Island: Located in the center of the lake, Sendra Island offers scenic views of the lake, and is an ideal place for picnic or boating.

  • Phubala: A small village located on the southern tip of the lake, Phubala provides you with the opportunity to enjoy water sports like boating, kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake

The Loktak Lake area is home to a variety of scenic trails, ideal for hiking, biking and walking. Some of the most popular hiking routes include:

  • Loktak Kangjeibung Trail: A 7-km-long trail starting from the Kangjeibung International Football Stadium in Imphal, ideal for beginners.

  • Thanga Khekman Trail: A 3-km-long trail starting from Thanga, a village in the Bishnupur district, and ending at Khekan Island. This trail is perfect for those who are looking for an easy hike combined with water sports like kayaking or boating.

Best Things to Do in Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake: Activities and Tours

From water sports to cultural tours, there’s plenty to do in the floating restaurants and homestays of the Loktak Lake:

  • Kayaking: Explore the tranquil waters of the lake with kayaking.

  • Boating: Take a scenic boat ride across the lake and enjoy the scenic views of the lake.

  • Sunrise/Sunset Viewing: Watch the serene sunrise or sunset views over the lake from the floating restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake

No trip to the floating restaurants and homestays of the Loktak Lake is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chamthong: This vegetable stew is a traditional Manipuri dish made with mixed vegetables and rice.

  • Eromba: A spicy fish curry made with boiled vegetables.

  • Ngari: This is a fermented fish sauce which is used in several dishes for flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Black Tea: An aromatic black tea that is grown in the region and has a unique flavor.

  • Yu: A locally brewed rice beer that is a popular beverage in Manipur.

Where to Stay in the Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in the floating restaurants and homestays of the Loktak Lake region. Here are some of the best options to consider:

  • Sendra Park and Resort: This beautiful resort is located near Sendra Island and offers a range of accommodation options, including deluxe rooms, suites, and cottages.

  • Yelhoumee Resort: This eco-friendly resort is located on the banks of the Loktak Lake and offers a peaceful retreat to enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

What to Buy in the Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to the floating restaurants and homestays of the Loktak Lake can take home some souvenirs and local products to remember their trip. Here are some of the top souvenir options:

  • Handicrafts: Manipur is known for its handicrafts, especially its handloom products.

  • Bamboo Products: Manipur is also known for its bamboo products such as baskets, furniture, and decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake

The culture and tradition of the Manipuri people are unique and can be experienced while staying in the floating restaurants and homestays of the Loktak Lake. Here are some of the customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: The traditional attire for women is the Phanek and Innaphi, and for men, it’s the Dhoti and kurta.

  • Respect Elders: The Manipuri people hold great respect for their elders, and it is customary to greet them with respect and seek their blessings.

Best Time to Visit Floating Restaurants and Homestays in the Loktak Lake

The peak season to visit the floating restaurants and homestays of the Loktak Lake is from November to February. The weather during this time is pleasant, with clear skies and mild temperatures.
