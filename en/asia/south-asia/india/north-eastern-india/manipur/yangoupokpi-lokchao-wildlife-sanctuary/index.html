Introduction to Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Manipur in North-Eastern India, the Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary is a hidden gem for travelers seeking nature, culture, and adventure. It covers an area of 185 sq. km and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species such as the clouded leopard, sloth bear, and marbled cat. The sanctuary is also rich in cultural heritage, with several historical places and museums that showcase Manipur’s culture.

List of Places to Visit in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • INA Memorial Complex: A museum dedicated to the Indian National Army and their struggle for independence.
  • Khongjom War Memorial: A tribute to the brave soldiers who fought in the Anglo-Manipur War of 1891.
  • Manipur State Museum: An exhibit of the state’s history, art, and culture, including a collection of artifacts from the Manipuri royal family.

Natural Attractions of Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lochao Lake: A natural lake located inside the sanctuary, offering picturesque views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Peak: The highest peak in the sanctuary, offering a challenging trek for adventurous travelers.
  • Trekking Trails: Several trails for hiking and mountain biking, including the popular Yangoupokpi Trail and the Lokchao Trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

The sanctuary has several trekking and biking trails ranging from beginner-friendly to challenging. The trails offer scenic views of the surrounding mountains and forests, and travelers can spot various wildlife species along the way. Some popular trails are:

  • Yangoupokpi Trail: A 12 km long trail that leads to the Yangoupokpi Peak, passing through forests and valleys.
  • Lokchao Trail: This 17 km long trail covers the sanctuary’s most remote areas, offering a chance to spot rare wildlife species.
  • Khongjom Trail: This trail covers Khongjom, the site of the Anglo-Manipur War, and is ideal for travelers interested in history.

Best Things to Do in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours available in the sanctuary, catering to different interests and preferences. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Wildlife Safari: A guided tour that takes travelers through the sanctuary to spot various wildlife species such as the clouded leopard, marbled cat, and sloth bear.
  • Bird Watching: The sanctuary is home to over 200 bird species, and travelers can spot several of them by taking a guided tour.
  • Camping: Several camping sites inside the sanctuary offer travelers a chance to connect with nature and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Day 1: Arrival in Imphal, Manipur’s capital city. Day 2: Visit the INA Memorial Complex and the Khongjom War Memorial. Day 3: Explore the Manipur State Museum and shop for souvenirs at the nearby market. Day 4-5: Trek/bike along the Yangoupokpi Trail and camp overnight. Day 6: Take a wildlife safari and spot animals in their natural habitat. Day 7: Relax by Lochao Lake before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chak-hao kheer: A dessert made of black rice, milk, and sugar, a Manipuri specialty.
  • Chamthong: A vegetable stew made with seasonal vegetables, meat, and fish.
  • Singju: A Manipuri salad made of finely chopped vegetables, fish, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Yu: A local beer made from rice.
  • Kangsoi: A traditional soup made of meat and vegetables, usually eaten for breakfast.

Where to Stay in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options for travelers visiting the sanctuary, ranging from budget-friendly to luxury. Some popular options are:

  • Forest Guest House: A basic guesthouse located inside the sanctuary, offering a peaceful environment.
  • Classic Grande: A more luxurious option located in Imphal, offering modern amenities.
  • Hotel Imphal: Another luxury option located in Imphal, offering comfortable rooms and scenic views.

What to Buy in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Manipuri Shawls: These are handmade shawls made of silk or cotton and come in various colors and designs.
  • Bamboo and Cane Products: These are eco-friendly souvenirs made by local artisans, including baskets, matting, and furniture.
  • Black Rice: A local specialty that can be brought home and used to make traditional dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

The people of Manipur have a rich cultural heritage and are known for their traditional dance forms, music, and crafts. The sanctuary is home to several ethnic communities, and travelers can learn about their culture by visiting local villages and interacting with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit the sanctuary is from November to April when the weather is pleasant, and the wildlife is more active. Monsoons should be avoided as the sanctuary experiences heavy rainfall during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Respect the sanctuary’s rules and regulations.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.
  • Carry enough warm clothes during winters.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Go off the designated trails without an experienced guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

The sanctuary is generally safe for travelers, but they should take precautions like carrying enough water, staying on designated trails, and avoiding unknown animals.

Health in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Travelers should carry a basic first aid kit and necessary medications with them. They should also drink enough water and avoid consuming water from unknown sources.

Getting to Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

The nearest airport is in Imphal, which is well-connected to major Indian cities.

Bus Stations Near Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Imphal also has a well-connected bus station with several buses coming in from surrounding areas.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire taxis or private cars to reach the sanctuary from nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary

Travelers can explore the sanctuary on foot, hire a bike or car, or opt for a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: A national park located about 50 km from the sanctuary, home to the rare Manipur brow-antlered deer.
  • Loktak Lake: A freshwater lake located near Keibul Lamjao National Park, offering boating and sightseeing options.
  • Shri Govindjee Temple: A Hindu temple located in Imphal, one of the must-visit places for travelers.

Conclusion

The Yangoupokpi-Lokchao Wildlife Sanctuary offers travelers a chance to connect with nature, explore the region’s cultural heritage, and test their adventurous side. With several activities, places to visit, and accommodation options available, it’s the perfect destination for those seeking an offbeat travel experience in India.
