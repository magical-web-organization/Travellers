Introduction to Southern India

Southern India is a mesmerizing region that boasts breathtaking scenery, a rich cultural heritage, and diverse adventures. It encompasses four states – Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh – and two union territories – Pondicherry and Lakshadweep. The region is renowned for its ancient temples, colonial architecture, stunning beaches, and misty hills, making it a must-visit for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Southern India

Southern India is packed with destinations that offer something for everyone. From serene backwaters and ancient landmarks to bustling cities and hill stations, there are so many exciting places to visit in the region. Here are some of the top spots to add to your itinerary:

  • Tamil Nadu: Chennai, Mahabalipuram, Tanjore, Ooty, Coimbatore
  • Kerala: Kochi, Alleppey, Munnar, Kovalam, Thekkady
  • Karnataka: Bangalore, Mysore, Hampi, Coorg, Gokarna
  • Andhra Pradesh: Hyderabad, Visakhapatnam, Amaravati, Tirupati, Vijayawada
  • Pondicherry: Auroville, Sri Aurobindo Ashram, Promenade Beach
  • Lakshadweep: Kavaratti, Agatti, Island Hopping

Food and Drink Scene of Southern India

Southern India’s cuisine is diverse and delicious, featuring a mix of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The region is also known for its spices, which are used to flavor everything from curries to chutneys. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Southern India:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dosa: A crispy crepe made from rice batter and served with sambar and chutney.
  • Idli: Steamed rice cakes usually eaten for breakfast with chutney and sambar.
  • Biryani: A flavorful rice dish made with meat or vegetables and spices.
  • Appam: A soft and lacy pancake made with fermented rice batter and coconut milk.
  • Prawn Curry: A spicy and tangy curry made with freshly caught prawns and coconut milk.
  • Avial: A colorful mixed vegetable curry made with coconut, yogurt, and spices.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: A breakfast dish made with steamed rice flour and chickpea curry.
  • Kerala Parotta: A flaky, layered bread served with chicken or vegetable curries.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: Indian tea brewed with spices like ginger, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  • Filter Coffee: Strong, traditional South Indian coffee filtered through a metal filter and served with milk and sugar.
  • Toddy: A local alcoholic beverage made from fermented coconut water.
  • Neer Mor: A refreshing buttermilk drink seasoned with ginger, curry leaves, and coriander.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern India

Southern India has a unique and diverse culture, and visitors should be aware of local customs and etiquette. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your footwear before entering a temple or someone’s house.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.
  • Use your right hand to eat and give offerings.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Southern India

The best time to visit Southern India is between October and March when the weather is dry and cool. During this time, the region’s lush green landscapes are at their peak, and the festive season adds to the charm. However, if you’re looking to explore the hill stations, the monsoon season between June and September is the best time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern India

When traveling to Southern India, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, it’s delicious!
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable shoes.
  • Bargain politely when shopping in markets.
  • Take a boat ride through the backwaters.
  • Get an ayurvedic massage, it’s incredibly relaxing.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Eat street food if you have a sensitive stomach.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Accept food or drinks from strangers.
  • Hesitate to ask for help or directions.
