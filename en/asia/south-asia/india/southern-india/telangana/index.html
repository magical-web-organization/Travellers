Introduction to Telangana

Located in Southern India, Telangana is a state known for its rich history and culture, scenic landscapes, bustling cities, and delicious food. Since gaining statehood in 2014, Telangana has emerged as a must-visit destination in India, attracting visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient monuments, indulging in outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, Telangana has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

Local Culture and Customs of Telangana

The people of Telangana are known for their traditional values, warm hospitality, and love for their culture. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a temple or someone’s house. Telugu is the official language of the state, but many also speak Hindi and Urdu. The state celebrates many festivals, most notably, Bathukamma and Dasara.

Best Time to Visit Telangana

The best time to visit Telangana is during winter months, between the months of October to March. The temperature during this time is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Telangana

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and mosques.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Bargain while shopping in the flea markets.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter the streets or tourist attractions.
  • Do not eat beef, as it is considered taboo in the state.
  • Do not smoke in public places.
  • Do not click pictures of locals or their homes without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Telangana

As with any travel, it is important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid any kind of trouble or inconvenience. Tourists should avoid traveling alone at night and should always carry some form of identification. The state has a low crime rate, but it is advised to take care of your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Telangana

It is important to take precautions against common illnesses like heatstroke and traveler’s diarrhea during your visit. Always carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months. Make sure to only drink bottled water and avoid eating street food that may not be hygienic.

Getting to Telangana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Telangana

Hyderabad International Airport is the major gateway to Telangana. The airport connects to major cities in India as well as international destinations such as Dubai, Singapore, and Kuala Lumpur.

Bus Stations Near Telangana

The state has a vast network of public and private buses connecting various cities and towns. Hyderabad is also well-connected to major cities in India through state-run buses.

Other Options

You can also reach Telangana by train, with major railway stations in Hyderabad, Warangal, and Kazipet.

How to Get Around in Telangana

Public transport includes buses, auto-rickshaws, cabs, and trains. Most cities have easily accessible and affordable public transport options, but it is advised to book a private cab for more remote locations.
