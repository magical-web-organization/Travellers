Araku Valley Travel Guide

Araku Valley is a picturesque location nestled in the Northern Coast of Andhra Pradesh and is famous for being a scenic valley surrounded by lush green forests and beautiful mountains.

This travel guide covers everything you need to know about Araku Valley, including transportation options, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, food and drink, accommodations, and more. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, Araku Valley has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Araku Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Araku Valley has plenty of cultural attractions to explore, which offer a glimpse into the rich history and heritage of the region. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places include:

  • Tribal Museum: This museum showcases the unique culture and traditions of the tribal communities in the region.
  • Araku Valley Ananthagiri Hill Temple: This temple is located on top of a hill and offers spectacular views of the valley.
  • Borra Caves: The Borra Caves are natural caves located in the valley that offer a rich insight into the geology and history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Araku Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Araku Valley is famous for its scenic views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Katiki Waterfalls: It is a beautiful waterfall located near the Borra Caves.
  • Chaparai Waterfalls: Chaparai is a lovely waterfall surrounded by dense forests and hills.
  • Coffee Plantations: Araku Valley is famous for its coffee plantations which offer a unique experience of coffee plantations trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Araku Valley

Araku Valley provides its hikers and bikers with a range of options to explore the region’s natural beauty. Some of the top hiking/biking routes to explore include:

  • Ananthagiri to Araku Valley: This 25 km trek features dense forests, hills, and streams.
  • Borra Caves to Tyda: This trail is surrounded by the beautiful scent of coffee plantations and offers stunning views of the hills.
  • Araku Valley to Paderu: This trek features a range of terrains, including forests, hills, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Araku Valley: Activities and Tours

Araku Valley offers an extensive range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the popular things to do during your visit:

  • Take a train ride on the Araku Valley Express for a scenic journey through the hills and forests.
  • Explore the Borra Caves and learn about the history and geology of the area.
  • Visit the various viewpoints, such as the Chaparai and Katiki Waterfalls, to witness the stunning natural beauty of the region.
  • Take a coffee plantation tour to experience the unique process and flavors of the locally grown coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Araku Valley

For those planning a week-long trip to Araku Valley, here is a sample itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Araku Valley and take a relaxing stroll through the town. Day 2: Explore the Borra Caves, followed by a coffee plantation tour. Day 3: Visit the Katiki and Chaparai Waterfalls. Day 4: Trek from Ananthagiri to Araku Valley and take a train ride on the Araku Valley Express. Day 5: Visit the Tribal Museum and Ananthagiri Hill Temple. Day 6: Take a hike from Borra Caves to Tyda. Day 7: Depart from Araku Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Araku Valley

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine of Araku Valley is infused with the flavors of the region’s fresh ingredients, such as coffee and spices. Some of the dishes to try while in Araku Valley include:

  • Bamboo Chicken: A dish cooked inside bamboo stems, giving it a unique smoky flavor.
  • Bongulo Chicken: A spicy chicken dish with a unique flavor.
  • Chapatti: A traditional Indian flatbread served with a variety of sides.

Local Beverages

Araku Valley is famous for its locally grown coffee, which is exported worldwide. You can sip on some of the flavorsome coffee while exploring the many coffee plantations of the region. Other local beverages include:

  • Palm Wine: A fermented beverage made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Sankranti Drink: A sweet traditional beverage made from jaggery, sesame seeds, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Araku Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Araku Valley offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and tastes. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Haritha Valley Resort: A spacious resort surrounded by lush forests and hills.
  • Ushodaya Resort: A charming resort modeled after traditional Indian village life.
  • Ananya Resort: A resort located amidst coffee plantations with a spa and wellness center.

What to Buy in Araku Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Araku Valley is a shopper’s paradise, offering a range of souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some of the top items to consider:

  • Araku Valley Coffee: The locally grown coffee is a popular item to bring back home as a gift.
  • Handicrafts: Araku Valley is known for its traditional handicrafts, such as pottery and colorful woven cloth.
  • Spices: The region is famous for its diverse range of spices, such as turmeric, ginger, and chili.

Local Culture and Customs of Araku Valley

The people of Araku Valley are warm and hospitable, and visitors will find plenty of opportunities to immerse themselves in the local culture and customs. Some of the cultural practices worth experiencing include:

  • The Tribal Dance: The Tribal Dance is a traditional dance form performed to celebrate different festivals and occasions.
  • Worshiping Nature: The tribal communities in the valley offer their respect and prayers to nature due to the area’s beauty and fertility.
  • Handicrafts-making: The region highly values art and craft, and visitors can learn how to make bamboo and pottery crafts.

Best Time to Visit Araku Valley

The best time to visit Araku Valley is between October and March when the weather is mild, and the natural beauty of the region is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Araku Valley

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Plan your itinerary before arriving.

Don’t:

  • Litter in natural areas.
  • Take prohibited items into the valley, such as alcohol and drugs.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Araku Valley

Araku Valley is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as avoiding deserted areas at night and being mindful of personal belongings.

Health in Araku Valley

Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid consuming food from unhygienic places to prevent any health issues while traveling to Araku Valley.

Getting to Araku Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Araku Valley

The nearest airport to Araku Valley is Visakhapatnam Airport, located around 120 km away, which is well-connected to the major cities of India.

Bus Stations Near Araku Valley

Araku Valley has a bus station situated in the town which is connected to Visakhapatnam and nearby towns.

Other Options

You can take a private car, hire a taxi or join group tours to reach Araku Valley.

How to Get Around in Araku Valley

The best way to get around Araku Valley is by hiring a taxi or using the auto-rickshaws available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Araku Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Araku Valley offers a range of nearby destinations to explore, such as:

  • Visakhapatnam, a beautiful coastal city located around 120 km from Araku Valley.
  • Vizianagaram, a historic town situated around 70 km from Araku Valley.

Conclusion

Araku Valley is a hidden gem in Andhra Pradesh offering natural beauty, rich culture and heritage, and a range of outdoor activities. Whether you’re a coffee lover, an adventure seeker, or a culture enthusiast, Araku Valley has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit wisely, and you’re sure to come back with delightful memories of this serene valley.
