Introduction to Bhimavaram

Located on the Northern Coast of Andhra Pradesh in Southern India, Bhimavaram is a city known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty. The city is named after one of the Pandavas, Bhima, and has a bustling urban center with a mix of traditional and modern amenities. It’s also home to some of the most significant historical and cultural landmarks of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Bhimavaram

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sri Someswara Janardhana Swamy Temple: Built during the 9th century, this ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a testament to the architectural prowess of the Chalukya dynasty.
  • Gunupudi Someswara Temple: This temple complex is home to a variety of shrines dedicated to different deities. Its ancient architecture and intricate carvings are a sight to behold.
  • Dwaraka Tirumala: Located about 45 kilometers from Bhimavaram, Dwaraka Tirumala is a popular pilgrimage destination. The temple here is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara and is known for its serene ambiance and stunning scenic beauty.
  • Pancharama Kshetras: A group of five ancient temples located in the vicinity of Bhimavaram, the Pancharama Kshetras are considered to be among the holiest sites for Hindus in Andhra Pradesh. These temples are dedicated to Lord Shiva and are known for their intricate architecture and rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Bhimavaram: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mogalturu Village: Located about 10 kilometers from Bhimavaram, Mogalturu Village is known for its scenic beauty and tranquil ambiance. The village is surrounded by lush green paddy fields and is a popular spot for picnics and outdoor activities.
  • Perupalem Beach: Located about 20 kilometers from Bhimavaram, Perupalem Beach is a pristine sandy beach known for its scenic beauty and calm waves. Visitors can enjoy a day soaking up the sun or indulge in a variety of water sports like surfing, swimming, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhimavaram

  • Tadipudi Reservoir: A popular spot for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, the Tadipudi Reservoir offers scenic trekking trails that wind through the lush green forests surrounding the reservoir.
  • Kesavaram Reservoir: Another popular destination for outdoor activities, Kesavaram Reservoir offers a variety of trekking and biking trails through scenic terrain.

Best Things to Do in Bhimavaram: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Cultural Landmarks: Visit the ancient temples, museums, and historical landmarks to experience the rich cultural heritage of Bhimavaram.
  • Enjoy the Scenic Beauty: Take in the scenic beauty of the region by visiting the picturesque beaches, forests, and lakes.
  • Indulge in Adventure Sports: Go surfing, boating or swimming at Perupalem Beach, trek through the Tadipudi and Kesavaram Reservoirs, or go bird watching in the lush forests surrounding Bhimavaram.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhimavaram

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pootharekulu: A traditional dessert made from rice batter and jaggery.
  • Ghee Karam Dosa: A spicy version of the famous South Indian dish, made with ghee, chili powder, and onion.
  • Chegodilu: A crispy street snack made from rice flour, deep-fried and flavored with cumin seeds and chili powder.

Local Beverages

  • Talakaya Kura: A traditional curry made with mutton or beef.
  • Gongura Pachadi: A tangy chutney made with gongura leaves, onions, and spices.
  • Rayalaseema Ruchulu: A local liquor made from fermented toddy, with a sweet and sour taste.

Where to Stay in Bhimavaram: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Air View: This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of Bhimavaram and offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Hotel Sitara: A mid-range hotel located near the Sri Someswara Janardhana Swamy Temple, offering spacious rooms and modern amenities.
  • The Geeta Regency: A luxury hotel located in a tranquil neighborhood, offering lavish suites and premium amenities.

What to Buy in Bhimavaram: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kondapalli Toys: These traditional toys are made of lightweight wood and painted in bright colors. They are a popular souvenir for tourists visiting Bhimavaram.
  • Kalamkari Fabric: A traditional hand-painted fabric made using natural dyes, Kalamkari is known for its intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  • Brassware: Bhimavaram is known for its exquisite brassware, including lamps, statues, and other decorative items that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhimavaram

The people of Bhimavaram are known for their hospitality and traditional values. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Bhimavaram

The best time to visit Bhimavaram is from November to March, when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. The summer months can be quite hot and humid, making outdoor activities uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhimavaram

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in the regional flavors.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhimavaram

Bhimavaram is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone at night and avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables.

Health in Bhimavaram

Medical facilities in Bhimavaram are limited, and visitors should carry basic medication and first aid supplies. It’s also essential to take necessary precautions to prevent water-borne illnesses and insect bites.

Getting to Bhimavaram: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bhimavaram

The nearest airport to Bhimavaram is the Rajahmundry Airport, located approximately 53 kilometers away. The airport is well-connected to major cities in India, and travelers can easily book a taxi or hire a car from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Bhimavaram

Bhimavaram has a well-connected network of bus services, with regular buses plying to cities across Andhra Pradesh.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Bhimavaram from nearby cities like Rajahmundry or Visakhapatnam.

How to Get Around in Bhimavaram

Bhimavaram has a well-connected network of public transport, including buses, autorickshaws, and taxis. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire private cars to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Bhimavaram: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rajahmundry: Located about 80 kilometers from Bhimavaram, Rajahmundry is a bustling city known for its scenic beauty and cultural landmarks.
  • Visakhapatnam: A port city on the Eastern Coast of India, Visakhapatnam is known for its stunning beaches, ancient temples, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Vijayawada: A cosmopolitan city located on the banks of the Krishna River, Vijayawada is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Bhimavaram is a city steeped in history and tradition, with a unique blend of cultural landmarks and natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient temples, trekking through scenic terrain, or indulging in local cuisine, Bhimavaram has something to offer for everyone. So plan your visit today and discover the hidden gems of this charming city on the Northern Coast of Andhra Pradesh.
