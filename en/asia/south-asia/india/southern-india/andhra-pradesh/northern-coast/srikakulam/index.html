Introduction to Srikakulam

Located in the northern coast of Andhra Pradesh, Srikakulam is a district that offers a diverse range of experiences to visitors. The district is well known for its cultural significance, natural beauty, historical heritage, and outdoor adventures.

Adorned by the Bay of Bengal on its eastern side and closely surrounded by the Odisha state border, Srikakulam gives the best opportunity to explore South Asia’s unique coastal area.

List of Places to Visit in Srikakulam

Visitors can discover Srikakulam’s culture, history, and landscape through these following places of interest:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arasavalli Sun Temple: Dedicated to Lord Surya, the Sun God, this 7th-century temple boasts of stunning architecture and a serene ambience.

  2. Sri Golingeswara Swamy Temple: Located in the Gara mandal, this ancient temple was built in the 13th century during the reign of the Eastern Ganga dynasty.

  3. Srimukhalingam Temple: It is one of the significant temples of Lord Shiva built by the Kalinga dynasty.

  4. Sri Kurmanadha Temple: A temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva located at Srikurmam, near the Bay of Bengal.

  5. Kala Ashram: A center for art and culture, showcasing the region’s music, dance, and visual art forms.

Natural Attractions of Srikakulam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kalingapatnam Beach: A stunning golden sand beach located at the confluence of Vamsadhara River and the Bay of Bengal.

  2. Baruva Beach: A pristine and secluded beach that offers water sports and leisure time by the sea.

  3. Mandasa Hill: The highest point in Srikakulam district and a great spot for trekking and birdwatching.

  4. Narasimha Konda Hill: A hill where the temple of Lord Narasimha, one of the avatars of Lord Vishnu, is located.

  5. Ethipothala Waterfall: A picturesque waterfalls located near Addanki.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Srikakulam

Srikakulam is a great destination for hiking and biking, with plenty of trails and natural surroundings to explore. Some popular routes for hiking and biking include:

  1. Mandasa Hill Trekking: A moderate hike that explores the scenic beauty of the Mandasa village and hill.

  2. Kashyap Hills Trekking: A challenging trek that takes visitors through hills and forests.

  3. Saraswathi Mahal Trekking: A scenic trek that passes through lush green forests and natural streams.

  4. Kaviti to Kalingapatnam Cycling: A cycling tour of around 50 kilometers that takes visitors past the lush green countryside and fishing villages.

  5. Kakaravertla Forest Trekking: A peaceful trek that passes through the dense forests of the Eastern Ghats.

Best Things to Do in Srikakulam: Activities and Tours

From historical monuments to adventure sports, the best things to do in Srikakulam include:

  1. Explore local culture: Visit Kala Ashram or attend traditional dance festivals and music performances to get a glimpse of Srikakulam’s unique culture.

  2. Go on a temple tour: Explore the many ancient temples in Srikakulam district that are renowned for their architecture and spiritual significance.

  3. Enjoy nature: Spend time at the beaches, hills, and waterfalls, and savor the scenic views of Srikakulam.

  4. Learn about history: Visit museums and historical monuments to learn about Srikakulam’s rich history and cultural heritage.

  5. Take an adventure tour: Engage in water sports, trekking, birdwatching, and cycling to get a kick of adrenaline.

Food and Drink Scene of Srikakulam

Srikakulam has a diverse culinary culture that reflects the regions’ unique blend of influences. Visitors must try the following dishes and drinks during their visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bamboo Chicken: A local specialty made with chicken or mutton cooked inside bamboo sticks.

  2. Royyala Vepudu: Spicy prawn fry that is tender and juicy.

  3. Vanamala Biryani: A layered biryani made with aromatic rice and goat meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Toddy: A mildly alcoholic beverage made by fermenting the sap of the palm tree.

  2. Gongura Pachadi: A tangy chutney made with red sorrel leaves and spices.

Where to Stay in Srikakulam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Srikakulam offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets, including luxury hotels, mid-range hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Some popular options are:

  1. Ushodaya Resorts and Convention Center: A luxury resort that offers comfortable accommodations and contemporary amenities.

  2. OYO 28095 Hotel Priya: A mid-range hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  3. Hotel Royal Park: A budget hotel that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Srikakulam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Srikakulam can pick up some unique souvenirs and local products to take back home, including:

  1. Kondapalli Toys: Wooden toys that are handcrafted in Kondapalli, near Vijayawada.

  2. Srikakulam Sarees: Beautiful sarees with intricate designs and fine embroidery.

  3. Pattachitra Painting: Traditional paintings that are done on fabric or paper, depicting mythological themes and religious stories.

Local Culture and Customs of Srikakulam

Srikakulam has a rich and unique cultural identity that has been shaped by its history and geography. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, which include:

  1. Address elders with respect: Elders are highly respected in Srikakulam, and visitors should address them appropriately.

  2. Avoid physical contact: Physical contact is generally avoided in public, especially between members of the opposite sex.

Best Time to Visit Srikakulam

The best time to visit Srikakulam is during the winter months from October to March, which offer pleasant weather and lower humidity levels.

Do’s and Don’ts of Srikakulam

Here are some do’s and don’ts of Srikakulam:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions: Srikakulam has a unique cultural identity, and visitors should respect their customs and traditions.

  2. Carry sunscreen and mosquito repellent: As Srikakulam has a tropical climate, carrying sunscreen and mosquito repellent is advisable.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect religious beliefs: Visitors should not disrespect the religious beliefs and sentiments of the locals.

  2. Litter or damage natural surroundings: Visitors should not litter or damage natural surroundings, including beaches, hills, and forests.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Srikakulam need to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Srikakulam

  1. Stay alert: Visitors should stay alert and aware of their surroundings at all times.

  2. Beware of pickpockets and theft: Visitors should be careful of pickpockets and thefts, especially during peak tourist months.

Health in Srikakulam

  1. Drink safe water: Visitors should drink bottled mineral water or boiled water to avoid any water-borne illnesses.

  2. Carry Medicines: Visitors must carry their medical kit as per their medical history.

Getting to Srikakulam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Srikakulam is well-connected through multiple modes of transportation:

Airports Near Srikakulam

The nearest airports to Srikakulam are:

  1. Visakhapatnam International Airport: Located approximately 107 kilometers from Srikakulam district.

  2. Bhubaneswar Airport: Located approximately 268 kilometers from Srikakulam district.

Bus Stations Near Srikakulam

Srikakulam has a well-developed bus network that connects it to the major cities of Andhra Pradesh and Odisha. Some popular bus stations near Srikakulam are:

  1. Srikakulam APSRTC Bus Stand

  2. Ichchapuram Bus Stand

Other Options

  1. Trains: Srikakulam has its own railway station that is connected to major cities of Andhra Pradesh, Odisha and the rest of India.

  2. Taxis and Self-Drive: Taxis and self-drive cars are readily available in Srikakulam.

How to Get Around in Srikakulam

Visitors can hire taxis, take the local bus, or rent a bike to get around Srikakulam and explore its beautiful surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Srikakulam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Srikakulam is a great starting point for further exploration of the eastern coast of India. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  1. Visakhapatnam: A beautiful coastal city known for its beaches, museums, and temples.

  2. Puri: A culturally significant city known for its ancient temples, festivals, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Srikakulam is an excellent destination for visitors looking to explore South Asia’s unique coastal region’s both cultural and natural beauty. With a range of attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious cuisine, visitors are sure to have a memorable experience. So book your tickets today and get ready to explore the diverse beauty of Srikakulam.
