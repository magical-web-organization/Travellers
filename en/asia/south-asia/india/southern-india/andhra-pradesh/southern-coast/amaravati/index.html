Introduction to Amaravati

Located in the southern coast of Andhra Pradesh, Amaravati is a beautiful city that offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and nature. The city is situated on the banks of the Krishna River and is home to several significant Buddhist sites. From exploring the ancient ruins to indulging in outdoor adventures, Amaravati has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Amaravati

Here are the must-visit attractions in Amaravati:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amaravati Archaeological Museum: This museum houses some of the finest Buddhist artifacts and sculptures from the Amaravati region.

  • Amaravati Mahachaitya: The Mahachaitya is a significant Buddhist site and was once home to a grand stupa.

  • Undavalli Caves: These cave temples are famous for their intricate carvings and ancient architecture.

Natural Attractions of Amaravati: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prakasam Barrage: The Prakasam Barrage is a popular picnic spot and offers breathtaking views of the Krishna River.

  • Kondapalli Reserve Forest: This nature reserve is perfect for nature enthusiasts and offers a myriad of outdoor activities, including hiking, bird-watching, and more.

  • Bhavani Island: This beautiful island is a must-visit for nature lovers and offers boating, water sports, and other recreational activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amaravati

Amaravati is blessed with stunning natural beauty and offers several terrific hiking and biking trails. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Kondaveedu Fort Trek: This trek takes you through dense forests, stunning landscapes, and ends with magnificent views of the Kondaveedu Fort.

  • Prakasam Barrage to Kanaka Durga Temple: This biking route takes you through scenic routes alongside the Krishna River and ends at the famous Kanaka Durga Temple.

Best Things to Do in Amaravati: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Boat Ride on the Krishna River: A boat ride on the Krishna River is a unique experience that offers stunning views of the city.

  • Experience the Local Culture: Take a tour of the nearby villages to experience the local culture and traditions of Amaravati.

  • Visit the Local Bazaars: The local bazaars are a must-visit for foodies and shopaholics alike. Indulge in local delicacies and shop for souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amaravati

Day 1: Visit the Amaravati Archaeological Museum and Undavalli Caves

Day 2: Explore the Kondaveedu Fort Trek

Day 3: Take a boat ride on the Krishna River

Day 4: Visit the Bhavani Island and Prakasam Barrage

Day 5: Experience the local culture by taking a tour of nearby villages

Day 6: Shop at the local bazaars and try the local delicacies

Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Amaravati

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: The biryani in Amaravati is a must-try for foodies. The flavors are authentic, and the aroma is simply heavenly.

  • Gongura Pickle: This tangy and spicy pickle is made using the gongura leaves, which are native to Andhra Pradesh.

  • Pootharekulu: This traditional Andhra sweet is made using rice flour, sugar, and ghee.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: The filter coffee in Amaravati is a must-try for coffee lovers.

  • Sugarcane Juice: This refreshing drink is made by crushing fresh sugarcane and is a popular local beverage.

Where to Stay in Amaravati: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amaravati offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some of the popular options include:

  • The Gateway Hotel: This luxurious hotel is situated on the banks of the Krishna River and offers stunning views of the city.

  • Hotel Vijayawada Grand: This budget hotel offers comfortable rooms and all modern amenities at affordable prices.

  • Treebo Trend: This guesthouse is a perfect option for backpackers and offers clean and comfortable accommodation at budget prices.

What to Buy in Amaravati: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mangalagiri Cotton Sarees: These handwoven cotton sarees are native to Andhra Pradesh and make for perfect souvenirs.

  • Kondapalli Toys: These wooden toys are made by skilled artisans and make for perfect gifts for children.

Local Culture and Customs of Amaravati

Andhra Pradesh is known for its rich culture and traditions, and Amaravati is no different. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Amaravati

The best time to visit Amaravati is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amaravati

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly.

  • Try the local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public places.

  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amaravati

Amaravati is a safe city, and visitors are advised to take all necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Amaravati

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and carry insect repellents to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Amaravati: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amaravati

Vijayawada Airport, located 30 km from Amaravati, is the nearest airport.

Bus Stations Near Amaravati

Amaravati Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the city, and regular buses connect the city to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and auto-rickshaws are readily available in the city for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Amaravati

Auto-rickshaws and local buses are the most convenient ways to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Amaravati: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vijayawada: This bustling city is situated just 30 km from Amaravati and is famous for its ancient temples and beautiful landscapes.

  • Nagarjuna Sagar: This beautiful town is a popular tourist destination and is renowned for its serene lake and magnificent Buddhist structures.

Conclusion

Amaravati is a beautiful city that offers a perfect mix of culture, history, and nature. From exploring ancient ruins to indulging in outdoor adventures and trying local delicacies, Amaravati has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Amaravati today and experience the charm of this beautiful city!
