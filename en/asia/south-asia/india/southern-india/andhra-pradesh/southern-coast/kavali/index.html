Kavali, located in the Southern Coast of Andhra Pradesh, Southern India, is a hidden gem that has just recently started to receive the attention it deserves. This small city is a place of magic and wonder, offering its guests and locals alike an exciting blend of culturally significant sights, natural attractions, and delectable cuisine, making it a destination that should definitely be on your list for your next Indian vacation.

Introduction to Kavali

Kavali is a city with a rich history dating back to the days of the Satavahanas and the Cholas. Known for its natural beauty, the city is surrounded by greenery, and the Southern Coast is one of the best places in India to watch the sunrise and sunset.

List of Places to Visit in Kavali

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rachalooru Fort: Dating back to the 16th century, this fort served as a stronghold for the Rachakonda Kingdom. The fort boasts a rich history and gives visitors a peek into the ancient past.
  • Bhairava Konda: The temple situated on top of the hill is a holy spot for the devotees of Lord Shiva, and the whole temple compound is surrounded by natural beauty.
  • Sri Kurmam Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, and it is said to be the only temple in India where a half-man and half-tortoise form of Lord Vishnu is worshipped.

Natural Attractions of Kavali: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Udayagiri Fort: This ancient fort, which was once the seat of power for the Vijayanagar kings, sits atop a hill and provides a stunning panoramic view of the entire city of Kavali.
  • Kavali Beach: The Southern Coast of India is known for its fantastic beaches, and Kavali does not disappoint in this regard. Kavali beach is a great place to enjoy the sun, sand, and surf.
  • Kandaleru Dam: Situated on the River Kandaleru, this dam is a great spot for those looking to escape the city’s bustle and relax amidst nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kavali

  • Kandaleru Forest Reserve: This hiking and biking trail takes you through the beautiful Kandaleru Reserve, where you can see exotic wildlife like the Indian Bison, wild boars, and deer.
  • Gundlakamma Reserved Forest: This route snakes through the beautiful Gundlakamma Reserved Forest, which is home to a wide range of flora and fauna and is a great spot for bird-watching.

Best Things to Do in Kavali: Activities and Tours

  • Enjoy a boat ride on Kandaleru dam: If you’re looking for a peaceful way to spend an afternoon, consider taking a boat ride on the Kandaleru dam. You can rent a boat, and the view from the water is breathtaking.
  • Visit a surf school in Kavali beach: The Southern Coast of India has recently seen a surge in surfing, and Kavali beach is an excellent place to learn. There are several surf schools in the area that offer beginner lessons.

Food and Drink Scene of Kavali

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apams: A type of flatbread typically served with fish curry.
  • Gongura Mutton: A flavorful lamb curry made with gongura leaves (sour sorrel).
  • Mirchi Bajji: Spicy green chili fry served as a snack.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Kavali is famous for its delicious coffee, made with a blend of local beans and milk.
  • Tender Coconut Water: One of the best ways to beat the heat in Kavali is with a nice, cold glass of fresh coconut water.
  • Mango Lassi: A tasty and refreshing drink made with yogurt, mango pulp, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Kavali: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ravi Teja Lodge: A budget-friendly option in the heart of the city.
  • Hotel Brundavan: A mid-range hotel featuring clean, comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • The Gateway Hotel Beach Road: A luxurious hotel on Kavali beach offering stunning views and top-notch amenities.

What to Buy in Kavali: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kondapalli Toys: These are a traditional form of wooden toys made in Kondapalli village, located near Kavali.
  • Kalamkari Fabric: An ancient art of hand-painting on fabric that is still practiced today in Andhra Pradesh.
  • East Godavari Gold Jewelry: This jewelry is unique to the region and features intricate designs and fine craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Kavali

Kavali is a place steeped in tradition, and its people take great pride in their customs and culture. It is important to be respectful of these traditions while visiting the city.

Best Time to Visit Kavali

The best time to visit Kavali is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and the humidity levels are low. The monsoon season, which lasts from July to September, can be quite unpredictable and is best avoided.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kavali

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Tip your servers and tour guides.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without asking for permission.
  • Be disrespectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kavali

Kavali is a relatively safe city. However, tourists should take the usual precautions such as avoiding dinging out too late at night and not carrying around large sums of cash.

Health in Kavali

It’s always a good idea to consult a healthcare professional before traveling to any new destination. Make sure your vaccinations are up-to-date, and take necessary precautions, such as using insect repellent, to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Kavali: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kavali

The nearest airport to Kavali is the Chennai International Airport, which is around 160 km away. From Chennai, you can take a bus or hire a taxi to Kavali.

Bus Stations Near Kavali

Kavali has a large bus station that connects it with several cities in Andhra Pradesh, including Vijayawada and Hyderabad.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or take a train to reach Kavali. The city has a train station that is well-connected with several major cities in the region, including Chennai, Hyderabad, and Vijayawada.

How to Get Around in Kavali

The best way to get around in Kavali is by taxi or auto-rickshaw. Buses are another option, but they can be overcrowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Kavali: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mahabalipuram: A historic town located around 220 kilometers from Kavali, known for its impressive monuments and temples.
  • Tirupati: A holy city located around 250 kilometers from Kavali, famous for its Sri Venkateswara Temple.
  • Vijayawada: A bustling city located around 200 kilometers from Kavali, known for its lively streets and delicious food.

Conclusion

Kavali is a city that packs a punch with its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a mix of both, Kavali has something for everyone. Make sure to add it to your list of must-visit destinations on your next trip to India.
