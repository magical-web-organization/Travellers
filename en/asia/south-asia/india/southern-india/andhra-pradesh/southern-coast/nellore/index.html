Introduction to Nellore

Located on the Southern Coast of Andhra Pradesh, Nellore is a beautiful city that offers a perfect blend of ancient culture and modernity. The city may not be as popular as some of the other destinations in South India, but it is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Nellore is home to many natural and cultural attractions, including scenic hills, ancient temples, and stunning beaches. So, if you are planning to visit Andhra Pradesh, make sure you add Nellore to your travel bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Nellore

Whether you are a tourist or a local, there is always something new to discover in Nellore. Here are some must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Udayagiri Fort: A historical fort located on a hill, Udayagiri Fort offers a breathtaking view of the city. The fort was built during the Vijayanagara Empire and was later captured by the British. It has many attractions such as the Ranganatha Temple, a mosque, a pond, and more.

  • Jonnawada: An ancient town that is located 15 km from Nellore city, Jonnawada is home to the famous Sri Jonnawada Kamakshi Temple. This temple is dedicated to Goddess Kamakshi, a form of Goddess Parvati. The complex also houses shrines dedicated to other deities, including Lord Shiva, Lord Vishnu, and Lord Ganesha.

  • Pulicat Lake: The second-largest saltwater lake in India, Pulicat Lake is a popular tourist destination in Nellore. It is home to many migratory birds and also offers boat rides that give you a picturesque view of the lake.

Natural Attractions of Nellore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Venkatagiri Fort: A beautiful fort surrounded by hills, Venkatagiri Fort is one of the most popular attractions in Nellore. The fort was built in the 18th century and offers an amazing panoramic view of the surroundings.

  • Mypadu Beach: A beautiful and serene beach, Mypadu Beach is the perfect place to relax and unwind. The beach is known for its golden sand and crystal-clear water. It is also a popular spot for water sports such as jet skiing, parasailing, and more.

  • Kotha Koduru Beach: Another beautiful and serene beach, Kotha Koduru Beach is located about 25 km from Nellore. The beach is known for its soft white sand and beautiful sunset views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nellore

Nellore offers many options for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the beautiful natural scenery. Some of the popular hiking and biking spots in Nellore include:

  • Pulicat Lake Bird Sanctuary
  • Sri Venkateshwara National Park
  • Penchalakona Forest

Best Things to Do in Nellore: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting and fun things to do in Nellore. Here are some of the best activities and tours that you can enjoy during your stay:

  • Go on a temple tour and explore the many ancient temples in and around Nellore.
  • Take a boat ride on Pulicat Lake and enjoy the picturesque view of the lake and its surroundings.
  • Indulge in water sports such as jet skiing, parasailing, and water-skiing at Mypadu Beach.
  • Try the local culinary delights and explore the street food scene in Nellore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nellore

If you are planning to visit Nellore, here is a week-long itinerary that you can follow:

  • Day 1: Visit Udayagiri Fort and Ranganatha Temple.
  • Day 2: Explore the ancient town of Jonnawada and visit the famous Sri Jonnawada Kamakshi Temple.
  • Day 3: Visit Pulicat Lake and go on a boat ride.
  • Day 4: Explore the beautiful Venkatagiri Fort and its surroundings.
  • Day 5: Spend a day at Mypadu Beach and indulge in water sports.
  • Day 6: Visit the Sri Venkateshwara National Park and Penchalakona Forest.
  • Day 7: Explore the local food scene and try the many culinary delights of Nellore.

Food and Drink Scene of Nellore

Nellore is known for its delicious food and culinary delights. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chepala Pulusu: A spicy fish curry made with tamarind, onions, and red chili powder.
  • Gongura Pachadi: A chutney made with sorrel leaves, onions, and green chili.
  • Nellore Chepala Fry: A deep-fried fish dish marinated with spices and served with lemon wedges.

Local Beverages

  • Banana Lassi: A refreshing summer drink made with bananas and yogurt.
  • Madras Filter Coffee: A strong and aromatic coffee brewed with a metal filter.

What to Buy in Nellore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nellore is famous for its traditional handicrafts and textiles. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products that you can bring home:

  • Kalamkari textiles: Hand-painted cotton and silk fabrics that depict mythology and nature.
  • Nellore Toys: Wooden toys that are handmade and painted with bright colors.
  • Leather puppets: Traditional leather puppets that are used in puppet shows that depict folklore tales.

Local Culture and Customs of Nellore

Nellore has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its festivals, traditions, and customs. Some of the important festivals celebrated in Nellore include:

  • Ugadi: The Telugu New Year.
  • Brahmotsavam: A nine-day festival celebrated at Sri Venkateshwara Temple in Nellore.
  • Diwali: The festival of lights, celebrated with great enthusiasm across India.

Best Time to Visit Nellore

The best time to visit Nellore is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and cool. It is also the best time to enjoy outdoor activities and explore the natural attractions of Nellore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nellore

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and other religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the street food scene in Nellore.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing while visiting religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nellore

Nellore is a relatively safe city, but it is always better to take precautions to ensure that you have a safe and hassle-free trip. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Don’t carry too much cash or valuables while going out.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions while traveling alone.
  • Book your accommodation in advance to avoid any last-minute hassles.

Health in Nellore

Nellore has good healthcare facilities and hospitals, but it is always better to take necessary precautions to avoid any health-related issues. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Drink only bottled water or boiled water.
  • Use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Pack a first aid kit with basic medicines for emergencies.

Nellore has a well-established public transportation system, which includes buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws. You can also hire a car or a bike to explore the city on your own.

Where to Go Next from Nellore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider visiting after exploring Nellore:

  • Tirupati: A famous pilgrimage destination and home to the world-renowned Tirumala Venkateswara Temple.
  • Chennai: The capital city of Tamil Nadu, known for its rich history, culture, and food.
  • Pondicherry: A charming coastal town that was once a French colony and is known for its stunning beaches and architecture.

Conclusion

Nellore is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural and cultural attractions. The city has something to offer to everyone, and it is definitely worth visiting for anyone planning to explore South India. Make sure you follow the do’s and don’ts, take necessary precautions, and enjoy your trip to Nellore.
