Introduction to Srikalahasti

Srikalahasti is a beautiful town located in the Chittoor district, Andhra Pradesh, southern India. The town’s name is derived from its famous Srikalahasteeswara Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located on the banks of the River Swarnamukhi and is famous for its beautiful architecture, rich history, and culture. The town is surrounded by several hills, including the famous Seshachalam range.

List of Places to Visit in Srikalahasti

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Srikalahasteeswara Temple

The Srikalahasteeswara Temple is the most famous attraction in Srikalahasti. It is believed to be one of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams (“five elements” temples) dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple features beautiful architectural elements, such as sculptures and intricate carvings.

2. Sri Subramanya Swamy Temple

The Sri Subramanya Swamy Temple is dedicated to Lord Murugan (also known as Kartikeya), the son of Lord Shiva. The temple is located on a hill and offers a beautiful view of the town.

3. Bhakta Kannappa Temple

The Bhakta Kannappa Temple is a popular place of worship among locals and is dedicated to Kannappa, a devotee of Lord Shiva. The temple is located amidst beautiful surroundings and features intricate carvings and sculptures.

4. Kala Bhairava Temple

The Kala Bhairava Temple is dedicated to the fierce deity Kala Bhairava, who is believed to be a form of Lord Shiva. The temple features a beautiful statue of Kala Bhairava, and devotees offer prayers to seek his blessings.

5. Sri Chennakesava Swamy Temple

The Sri Chennakesava Swamy Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple features a beautiful statue of Lord Vishnu in a reclining posture, and its architecture is a mixture of South Indian and North Indian styles.

Natural Attractions of Srikalahasti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Talakona Waterfalls

The Talakona Waterfalls is one of the highest waterfalls in Andhra Pradesh, located amidst beautiful nature. The waterfalls offer an excellent opportunity for a refreshing dip and a perfect view.

2. Sri Venugopala Swamy Temple

The Sri Venugopala Swamy Temple is a picturesque temple located on the banks of the Swarnamukhi River. The temple features beautiful architecture, and the river offers an excellent view for tourists to click pictures.

3. Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Swamy Temple

The Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Swamy Temple is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, another form of Lord Vishnu. The temple is located on a hill and offers a beautiful panoramic view of the surroundings.

4. Sri Prasanna Varadaraja Swamy Temple

The Sri Prasanna Varadaraja Swamy Temple is a beautiful temple located on the banks of the Swarnamukhi River. The surrounding scenery is breathtaking and attracts many tourists who want to explore the beauty of nature.

5. Sri Vayulingeswara Swamy Temple

The Sri Vayulingeswara Swamy Temple is famous for its main deity, Lord Shiva, depicted as a linga. The temple is located on a hill and offers a beautiful view of the town and surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Srikalahasti

1. Chandra Drona Hill Trek

The Chandra Drona Hill Trek is a popular trekking trail among tourists who visit Srikalahasti. Trekking up to the hilltop provides a mesmerizing view of the surroundings, coupled with abundant natural scenery.

2. Seshachalam Hill Trek

The Seshachalam Hill Trek is a challenging trek that takes tourists through thick flora, rock formations, and a panoramic view of the entire surroundings. It’s a challenging trekking trail that is perfect for adventure-seekers.

Best Things to Do in Srikalahasti: Activities and Tours

1. Take a Holy Dip in Swarna Mukhi River

The Swarna Mukhi River is known for its sacredness and importance in Hindu mythology. Taking a holy dip in the river is a must-do activity when visiting Srikalahasti.

2. Visit the Local Bazaars

The local bazaars of Srikalahasti are the best places to witness the town’s unique culture and shop for souvenirs like traditional dresses, handicrafts, and jewelry.

3. Attend the Annual Rath Yatra Festival

The annual Rath Yatra Festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm and fervor in Srikalahasti. It is a multi-day event where the chariot of Lord Shiva is taken out and paraded through the town’s streets.

Food and Drink Scene of Srikalahasti

Must-Try Dishes

1. Pootharekulu

Pootharekulu is a popular Andhra dessert made with rice flour, ghee, and sugar. It is famous for its flaky texture and unique flavor.

2. Gongura Pachadi

Gongura Pachadi is a spicy chutney made with Sorrel leaves. It is served as a side dish in most of the local cuisine, including biryanis and curries.

3. Ulavacharu

Ulavacharu is a traditional Andhra soup made with horse gram. The soup has a unique flavor and is known to have several health benefits.

Local Beverages

1. Filter Coffee

Filter coffee is a popular beverage in Srikalahasti. The perfect blend of aromatic and flavorful coffee beans provides a unique taste and aroma to this drink.

2. Lassi

Lassi is a sweet and creamy yogurt drink that is iconic to Andhra Pradesh. It is a refreshing drink, especially in hot weather.

Where to Stay in Srikalahasti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Srikalahasti offers several accommodation options for tourists, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best options for stays include Hotel MGM Grand, Hotel Sri Shiva Shakti, and Hotel KPR Residency.

What to Buy in Srikalahasti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The local bazaars in Srikalahasti are famous for their unique handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry. Visitors can shop for items such as Kalamkari paintings, leather products, hand-woven baskets, and Bidriware.

Local Culture and Customs of Srikalahasti

The town of Srikalahasti is famous for its religious fervor and culture. The locals are traditionally inclined and celebrate several festivals, including Rath Yatra and Maha Shivaratri. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Srikalahasti

The best time to visit Srikalahasti is between September to March when the weather is pleasant and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Srikalahasti

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and do not litter.
  • Bargain respectfully at local bazaars.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting temples.
  • Show disrespect towards religious beliefs and practices.
  • Damage any historical or cultural monument or artifact.

Safety and Health

Safety in Srikalahasti

Srikalahasti is generally a safe town for tourists. Visitors should take the necessary precautions to keep their valuables safe and avoid traveling alone in secluded areas.

Health in Srikalahasti

Visitors should carry necessary medications when traveling to Srikalahasti. The town has several healthcare centers and hospitals to provide medical assistance if required.

Getting to Srikalahasti: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Srikalahasti

The nearest airport to Srikalahasti is the Tirupati International Airport, located about 33 km away.

Bus Stations Near Srikalahasti

Srikalahasti has several bus stations, including the Srikalahasti Bus Station and the APSRTC Bus Stand provided by Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Srikalahasti by train. The town is well-connected to other major cities by the Indian Railways.

How to Get Around in Srikalahasti

Tourists can explore Srikalahasti on foot or hire taxis, autorickshaws, or motorcycles. Bicycles are also available on rent for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Srikalahasti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tourists can explore several nearby destinations from Srikalahasti, such as Tirupati, Madanapalle, and Bangalore. Tirupati is famous for its temples, while Madanapalle is known for its natural beauty and trekking trails.

Conclusion

Srikalahasti is a beautiful town with an abundance of cultural and natural attractions. Plan your visit to this town and explore its unique offerings, including trekking trails, holy temples, and local cuisine. Make memories by visiting the town bazaars, where you can buy local handicrafts, sarees, and Bidriware to take back home.
