Introduction to Kurnool

Kurnool is a city located in the state of Andhra Pradesh in Southern India. The city is situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River and is part of the Rayalaseema region. Kurnool has a rich history, and it was originally the capital of Andhra Pradesh before the capital was shifted to Hyderabad. The city is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse cultures, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a food lover, Kurnool has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kurnool

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kurnool Fort

Kurnool Fort is an ancient fort that was once the center of power during the medieval period. The fort was built during the reign of the Vijayanagara Empire, and it has various structures including the palace, mosque, and temple. Kurnool Fort is a great place to explore history, and visitors can get a better insight into the heritage and architecture of South India.

Belum Caves

Belum Caves is a natural underground cave system that is around 3200 million years old. The caves are located in the village of Kolimigundla, and they are the longest cave system in India. Visitors can take guided tours of the caves, enjoy the picturesque views, and learn about the history and formation of the caves.

Kondareddy Burz

Kondareddy Burz is a tower-like structure located in the heart of the city. It is named after Kondereddy, a local leader who stood up against British rule. The structure has a statue of Kondereddy at its entrance, and visitors can climb up to the top to enjoy panoramic views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Kurnool: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mahanandi Temple

Mahanandi Temple is located in the Mahanandi village, and it is famous for its natural springs. The temple is surrounded by hills and lush green forests, and visitors can take a dip in the natural springs for rejuvenation.

Orvakal Rock Garden

Orvakal Rock Garden is a scenic park located around 20 kilometers from Kurnool. The park has various rock formations that have been naturally formed over the years. Visitors can take a walk through the park, enjoy the scenic views, and capture stunning photographs.

Ahobilam

Ahobilam is a mountain range located around 140 kilometers from Kurnool. It is a popular trekking destination, and visitors can explore the various temples and waterfalls during the trek. The mountain range is also home to various rare species of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurnool

Kurnool-Balachukki Bike Ride

The Kurnool to Balachukki bike ride is a popular route that covers around 200 kilometers. The route takes riders through the scenic countryside, hills, and forests, and it offers a challenging terrain for adventure seekers.

Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary Trek

The Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary Trek is a popular trekking route that covers around 10 kilometers. The trek takes visitors through the sanctuary, and they can spot various wildlife species such as blackbucks, Indian bustards, and pelicans. The trek also offers a great opportunity to appreciate the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Kurnool: Activities and Tours

Heritage Walk

Kurnool’s heritage walk is a great way to explore the city’s rich history and culture. The walk covers various historical monuments and landmarks, including the Kurnool Fort, Kondareddy Burz, and the Alampur Temples. Visitors can also enjoy street food and interact with the locals during the walk.

Tungabhadra River Rafting

Tungabhadra River Rafting is a thrilling adventure activity enjoyed by many visitors to Kurnool. The activity takes visitors along the Tungabhadra River, and it offers a chance to enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding landscape while also experiencing the adrenaline rush of rafting.

Food and Drink Scene of Kurnool

Must-Try Dishes

Ulava Charu

Ulava Charu is a traditional Andhra lentil soup made with horse gram lentils. The dish is famous for its unique flavor, and it is usually served with rice or roti.

Royyala Vepudu

Royyala Vepudu is a spicy shrimp fry dish that is a popular street food in Kurnool. The dish is made with a blend of spices and herbs and is usually served with chutney.

Local Beverages

Filter Coffee

Filter coffee is a popular beverage in Kurnool, and it is usually served with breakfast. The coffee is made with freshly ground coffee beans and is usually served with milk and sugar.

Badam Milk

Badam Milk is a sweet and creamy almond milk beverage that is popular in Kurnool. The drink is made with ground almonds, milk, sugar, and saffron.

Where to Stay in Kurnool: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Kurnool, catering to different budgets and preferences. Some of the popular hotels in Kurnool include the Haritha Hotel, Hotel Rajaveer Palace, and Vivera Grand.

What to Buy in Kurnool: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kurnool is famous for its handicrafts, and visitors can take home some unique souvenirs such as Kondapalli toys, Kalamkari paintings, and Banjara embroidery.

Local Culture and Customs of Kurnool

Kurnool is a culturally rich city that follows various customs and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to respect their religious rituals and beliefs. It is advised to dress modestly while visiting the temples and other religious places.

Best Time to Visit Kurnool

The best time to visit Kurnool is between October and February when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurnool

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ religious rituals and beliefs
  • Explore the natural beauty of Kurnool
  • Try the local cuisine and street food
  • Shop for handicrafts and souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the religious places and rituals
  • Litter around the city
  • Scream or shout in public places
  • Dress inappropriately while visiting religious places

Safety and Health

Safety in Kurnool

While Kurnool is generally a safe city, it is advised to avoid traveling alone at night or in isolated areas. Visitors should also be cautious of pickpockets and avoid carrying expensive items with them.

Health in Kurnool

Visitors should ensure that they are up to date with their routine vaccinations, including the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine. They should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, including dengue and malaria, by using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing.

Getting to Kurnool: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kurnool

The nearest airport to Kurnool is the Rajiv Gandhi International Airport in Hyderabad, which is around 213 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kurnool

Kurnool has a well-connected bus network, and visitors can take buses from major cities such as Hyderabad, Bangalore, and Chennai.

Other Options

Visitors can also take trains to the Kurnool Railway Station or hire a private car to reach the city.

How to Get Around in Kurnool

Visitors can get around Kurnool by taking local buses, auto-rickshaws, or taxis. It is advised to negotiate the fares before boarding to avoid any misunderstandings.

Where to Go Next from Kurnool: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hampi

Hampi is an ancient city located around 188 kilometers from Kurnool. The city is famous for its ruins and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mahbubnagar

Mahbubnagar is a city located around 110 kilometers from Kurnool. The city is famous for its ancient temples, including the Pillalamarri temple and the Sri Ranganayaka Swamy temple.

Conclusion

Kurnool is a beautiful city that offers visitors an opportunity to explore history, culture, and the natural beauty of South India. The city’s warm hospitality, delicious cuisine, and various adventure activities make it an ideal destination for travelers looking for a unique experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Kurnool with confidence, making the most of their time in the city.
