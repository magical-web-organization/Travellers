Introduction to Ramanagara

Ramanagara is a town located in the southeastern part of Karnataka, India. It is located in the Ramanagara district and is surrounded by hills and mountains, which makes it a perfect destination for outdoor activities and adventures. The region mainly consists of granite hills and is known for its silk industry, making it a popular destination for tourists. Whether you are a nature lover or an adventure seeker, Ramanagara has plenty to offer.

In this comprehensive travel guide for Ramanagara, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, culture, safety tips, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Ramanagara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Janapada Loka: Located 53 km from Ramanagara, this museum showcases traditional Indian art, crafts, and cultural artefacts.
  • Shivagange: This is a hill that is popular among trekkers and religious pilgrims as it has several temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Ranganatha Swamy Temple: This is a popular Hindu temple located in the heart of Ramanagara.
  • Kanva Reservoir: This is a picturesque lake that is ideal for boating and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Ramanagara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ramadevara Betta: This is a hill located about 50 km from Ramanagara, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding areas. It is popular for trekking and is home to several ancient temples.
  • Bilikal Rangaswamy Betta: This is a popular trekking destination that offers stunning views of the surrounding areas, especially during sunrise and sunset.
  • Mekedaatu: This is a rocky terrain located in the forested areas of Ramanagara that offers stunning views of the Cauvery River.
  • Kabbaladurga: This is a hill station located approximately 20 km from Ramanagara, which is popular for its trekking trails and ancient temples.
  • Channapatna: This is a small town located about 30 km from Ramanagara, which is famous for its wooden toys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramanagara

  • Ramadevara Betta Trek: This is a moderate trek that takes approximately two to three hours to complete. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.
  • Bilikal Rangaswamy Betta Trek: This is a challenging trek that takes approximately four to five hours to complete. It offers panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Kanva Dam Trek: This is an easy trek that takes approximately one to two hours to complete. It offers scenic views of the Kanva Dam.

Best Things to Do in Ramanagara: Activities and Tours

  • Camping: There are several campsites located in the hills of Ramanagara, which offer a unique camping experience amidst nature.
  • Rock Climbing: The granite hills of Ramanagara offer an ideal destination for rock climbing enthusiasts.
  • Bird Watching: The region is home to exotic birds such as the Indian roller, black kite, and red-wattled lapwing.
  • Silk shopping: Ramanagara is also known for its silk industry, and you can buy silk sarees and dress materials in the local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramanagara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bisi Bele Bath: This is a popular dish made with rice, lentils, and mixed vegetables. It is widely available in the local restaurants and food outlets.
  • Ragi Mudde: This is a nutritious dish made with finger millet flour and water. It is commonly eaten with curries or sambar.
  • Mysore Pak: This is a sweet dish made with gram flour, sugar, and ghee. It is a popular dessert in Ramanagara.

Local Beverages

  • Mango Lassi: This is a refreshing drink made with mango pulp and yogurt.
  • Filter Coffee: This is a popular South Indian beverage made with coffee beans and milk.

Where to Stay in Ramanagara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ramanagara Guesthouse: This is a budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Shanthi Guesthouse: This is another budget-friendly guesthouse that is located in the heart of Ramanagara.
  • Windflower Prakruthi Resort: This is a luxury resort that offers several amenities such as swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Ramanagara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Sarees and Dress Materials: Ramanagara is known for its silk industry, and you can buy silk sarees and dress materials in the local markets.
  • Wooden Toys: Channapatna is a small town located near Ramanagara, which is famous for its wooden toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramanagara

The majority of the people living in Ramanagara follow Hinduism. The local language is Kannada, but Hindi and English are also widely spoken. The region is known for its silk industry, and the locals practice several art forms such as Yakshagana and Dollu Kunitha.

Best Time to Visit Ramanagara

The best time to visit Ramanagara is between October to February, when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (June to August) as the trekking trails can become slippery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramanagara

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks when trekking.
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places.
  • Hike or trek alone after dark.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramanagara

  • Always inform someone about your whereabouts when trekking or hiking.
  • Avoid trekking during the rainy season.
  • Be aware of the wild animals when hiking in the forested areas.

Health in Ramanagara

  • Carry your own water and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Apply mosquito repellent when visiting forested areas.

Getting to Ramanagara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ramanagara

  • Bengaluru International Airport: This is the nearest airport to Ramanagara, which is located approximately 60 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ramanagara

  • Ramanagara Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Ramanagara, which connects to several cities and towns in Karnataka.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest railway station to Ramanagara is Bangalore City railway station, which is located approximately 48 km away.

How to Get Around in Ramanagara

The best way to get around in Ramanagara is by hiring a taxi or a local guide who can show you the best places to visit and trekking routes in the region.

Where to Go Next from Ramanagara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bangalore: This is the nearest city to Ramanagara, which is famous for its tech parks, shopping malls, and nightlife. It is located approximately 50 km away from Ramanagara.
  • Mysore: This is a historical city located approximately 110 km away from Ramanagara, which is famous for its palaces, temples, and gardens.
