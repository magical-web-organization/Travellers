Introduction to Bannerghatta National Park

Bannerghatta National Park is a wildlife sanctuary and trekking destination located in the Indian state of Karnataka, in Southern India. The park has dense forest cover, hills, and a variety of exotic flora and fauna.

Established in 1971, Bannerghatta National Park has been a popular destination for nature and adventure enthusiasts. The park is spread over an area of 104.27 sq. km, and it is home to a wide range of animals like the Bengal tiger, Indian leopard, Indian elephant, and many more.

Local Culture and Customs of Bannerghatta National Park

The people of Karnataka are deeply rooted in their traditions and customs. They are known for their warm hospitality and cultural diversity. The official language of the state is Kannada, and the majority of the population practices Hinduism.

Best Time to Visit Bannerghatta National Park

The best time to visit Bannerghatta National Park is from November to June. During this time, the climate is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bannerghatta National Park

Do:

  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  • Hire a guide before embarking on a trek or safari.
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or create any disturbance to the wildlife.
  • Feed the animals or birds.
  • Carry any plastics inside the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bannerghatta National Park

  • Avoid entering restricted areas of the park.
  • Preferably carry a first aid kit and insect repellent.
  • Wear suitable shoes and clothing.

Health in Bannerghatta National Park

  • Drink bottled water to prevent any waterborne illnesses.
  • Do not eat street food outside the park.
