Introduction to Bangalore Rural

Nestled in the southeastern part of Karnataka, Bangalore Rural is a delightful destination that offers a perfect blend of rural beauty and modern ambiance. The district is surrounded by the lush greenery of the Western Ghats and boasts an ancient history that dates back to the Hoysala Empire. From its serene natural landscapes and culturally rich historical sites to its vibrant food and drink scene, Bangalore Rural has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bangalore Rural

Whether you are a solo traveler or visiting with family or friends, there are plenty of things to do in Bangalore Rural. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nandi Hills: Located about 60 km away from Bangalore, Nandi Hills is a popular weekend getaway for locals and tourists alike. The hill is the site of an ancient fort and a temple dedicated to Nandi, the bull god of Hindu mythology. The hill is surrounded by stunning views and provides an excellent opportunity for hiking.
  • Devanahalli Fort: This 16th century fort was built by Mallabairegowda, a local chieftain who later became a feudal lord under the Vijayanagara Empire. The fort has seen many battles and has served as a strategic point for various rulers over the centuries. Today, it is an important historical landmark and a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Bangalore Rural: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manchanabele Dam: Located on the banks of the Arkavathy River, the Manchanabele Dam is a popular spot for picnicking and outdoor activities like boating and fishing. The picturesque surroundings, with the Savandurga Hill Range in the background, make it a perfect place for photography enthusiasts.
  • Muthyalamaduvu: Also known as the Pearl Valley, Muthyalamaduvu is a scenic waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. The water cascades down in a circular motion, earning the nickname ‘Pearl Valley’. The waterfall is located about 40 km from Bangalore and is a popular spot for a day trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bangalore Rural

Bangalore Rural is an excellent place for hiking enthusiasts. The district is surrounded by hills and forests, providing an excellent opportunity for explorers. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking routes:

  • Savandurga Hill: Located about 60 km from Bangalore, the Savandurga Hill offers a challenging trek up the steep granite slopes. Trekking to the top of the hill offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kunti Betta: Kunti Betta is a popular trekking destination located about 125 km from Bangalore. The two hills are joined by a narrow path and offer stunning views of the surrounding valley.

Best Things to Do in Bangalore Rural: Activities and Tours

Whether you are an adventure-seeker, history buff, or a foodie, Bangalore Rural has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Bangalore Rural:

  • Take a wine tour: Bangalore Rural is home to several vineyards that offer wine tours and tastings.
  • Visit a silk farm: The district is known for its silk production, and visiting a silk farm is a unique experience that gives you an insight into the production process.

Food and Drink Scene of Bangalore Rural

Bangalore Rural is a food lover’s paradise, with its diverse cuisine that caters to different tastes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bisi bele bath: A popular rice-based dish made with lentils, vegetables, and a blend of spices.
  • Ragi mudde: A nutritious dish made from finger millet flour, served with sambar or a curry.

Local Beverages

  • Nannari sherbet: A refreshing drink made with nannari roots and sugar syrup.
  • Tender coconut water: A natural refreshing drink that is rich in electrolytes and is perfect for beating the summer heat.

Where to Stay in Bangalore Rural: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, Bangalore Rural has a range of accommodation options to choose from. Some of the popular options are:

  • The Windflower Prakruthi: A luxury resort with spacious rooms and a range of leisure activities like spa treatments and adventure sports.
  • RD’s Nature Retreat: A budget-friendly resort located in the midst of nature, offering eco-friendly accommodation and a range of activities.

What to Buy in Bangalore Rural: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bangalore Rural is known for its silk production, and you can buy silk sarees, shawls, and fabrics at reasonable prices. Other souvenirs to consider include:

  • Sandalwood products: Bangalore Rural is known for its sandalwood oil and perfumes.
  • Handicrafts: The district is known for its woodwork, pottery, and brassware.

Local Culture and Customs of Bangalore Rural

Bangalore Rural has a rich culture that is deeply rooted in tradition. The locals are warm and hospitable, and some of the customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit are:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples.

Best Time to Visit Bangalore Rural

The best time to visit Bangalore Rural is between October and April. The weather during these months is mild, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bangalore Rural

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Bangalore Rural:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry sunscreen and insect repellent during outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Avoid smoking and drinking in public places.
  • Do not disrespect religious sites and sentiments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bangalore Rural

Bangalore Rural is a relatively safe district, but it is always best to remain vigilant and take necessary precautions like:

  • Avoid travelling alone at night.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.

Health in Bangalore Rural

The standard of healthcare in Bangalore Rural is reasonable, though it is always advisable to carry a basic medical kit that includes essentials like painkillers, antiseptic creams, and bandages.

Getting to Bangalore Rural: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bangalore Rural is accessible by road, rail, and air. Here are the different modes of transportation available:

Airports Near Bangalore Rural

  • Kempegowda International Airport: Located about 13 km outside Bangalore, Kempegowda International Airport is the nearest airport to Bangalore Rural.

Bus Stations Near Bangalore Rural

  • Bangalore Central Bus Station: Located in the heart of Bangalore, this is the main bus station in the city. Regular buses connect it to Bangalore Rural.

Other Options

  • Train: Bangalore Rural is well-connected by trains, with regular services to Bangalore and other cities.

How to Get Around in Bangalore Rural

The best way to get around Bangalore Rural is by hiring a taxi or renting a car. Buses and shared taxis are also available but are not as comfortable as private transport.

Where to Go Next from Bangalore Rural: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bangalore Rural is well-connected to several popular tourist destinations in Karnataka. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Mysore: Located about 150 km away from Bangalore Rural, Mysore is known for its historical sites, palaces, and a vibrant arts and crafts scene.
  • Hampi: Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage site located about 360 km from Bangalore Rural. The ancient ruins of Hampi are a testament to its glorious past.

Conclusion

Bangalore Rural is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of nature, culture, and history. With its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Bangalore Rural is a destination that is worth exploring.
