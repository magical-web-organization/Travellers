Introduction to Savandurga

Set in southeastern Karnataka, Savandurga is a beautiful town that boasts magnificent natural creations and rich cultural heritage. It is situated at an altitude of 1226 meters above sea level and is famous for its two towering hills that together make up the Savandurga Hill. Trekking enthusiasts and adventure seekers will find a plethora of opportunities to indulge in their favorite activities in Savandurga. The town is also known for its historical significance, which can be seen in ancient temples, forts, and other landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Savandurga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Savandurga Fort

The Savandurga Fort stands tall on top of a hill and is said to have been built as early as the 14th century. The fort was constructed by one of the feudal lords of Kempegowda dynasty and was later conquered by the Mysore rulers. The fort ruins still stand today and offer an amazing view of the entire town.

2. Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

The Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple is located at the base of the Savandurga Hill and is dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narasimha. The temple is one of the oldest in the town and is a prominent spot for the locals and tourists alike.

Natural Attractions of Savandurga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Savandurga Hill

The Savandurga Hill is the largest monolith in Asia and stretches for nearly 4 km. It is a perfect location for trekking enthusiasts and nature lovers. The trek to the top of the hill is steep, but the panoramic view of the entire town from the top is worth the effort.

2. Manchinbele Dam

Manchinbele Dam is an ideal spot for adventure sports such as kayaking, river rafting, and swimming. The dam backwater is a serene place to spend some time relaxing and enjoying nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savandurga

Savandurga is a popular spot for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The hill offers several trekking trails of varying difficulty levels. The trek to the top of the hill starts from the foothills, which takes around 3-4 hours to complete. Another popular trek is from the Narasimha Swamy Temple to the top of the hill, which is a 4-hour trek.

Best Things to Do in Savandurga: Activities and Tours

1. Bird Watching

Savandurga is home to a wide variety of bird species, making it an ideal location for bird watching. The best time for bird watching is during the winter months when migratory birds visit the region.

2. Village Tour

Take a village tour to experience rural life and learn about local customs and traditions. Visit nearby villages to see how the locals go about their daily life, and take part in cultural activities such as pottery making and cooking local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Savandurga

Must-Try Dishes

Savandurga is famous for its traditional South Indian cuisine, with dishes such as ragi balls, akki roti, and rave idli. Don’t forget to try the hot filter coffee, which is a specialty of the region.

Local Beverages

Try the local alcoholic beverage, toddy, which is made from the sap of the coconut tree. It’s a popular local drink and has a sweet and sour taste.

What to Buy in Savandurga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to shop for local products such as silk and cotton sarees, aromatic oils, and sandalwood carvings. Also, check out the local handicraft shops for handcrafted pottery and wooden items.

Local Culture and Customs of Savandurga

The locals of Savandurga are known for their warm hospitality and friendly demeanor. Always respect their customs and traditions, and dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious places.

Best Time to Visit Savandurga

The best time to visit Savandurga is from November to February as the weather is pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savandurga

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit nearby villages to learn about the local way of life.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Savandurga

Savandurga is a relatively safe town, but travelers should take the necessary precautions to avoid any inconvenience. Always carry a copy of your identity proof and emergency contact details with you.

Health in Savandurga

Savandurga is a relatively healthy region with no major health risks. However, travelers should carry adequate medication and consult their doctors before traveling.

Getting to Savandurga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Savandurga

The nearest airport to Savandurga is Kempegowda International Airport, Bengaluru, which is around 60 kilometers away. From the airport, travelers can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Savandurga.

Bus Stations Near Savandurga

The nearest bus stations are in Ramanagara and Magadi, which are connected to major cities in Karnataka. From the bus station, travelers can hire a taxi or a motorbike to reach Savandurga.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a car or bike from Bangalore and reach Savandurga. It is a 50-kilometer drive from Bangalore.

How to Get Around in Savandurga

The best way to get around Savandurga is by hiring a taxi or a motorbike. Travelers can also take public transportation such as buses or auto-rickshaws.

Where to Go Next from Savandurga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations from Savandurga are:

  • Ramanagara
  • Dodda Alada Mara
  • Nandi Hills

Travelers can explore these nearby destinations after completing their trip to Savandurga.

Conclusion

Savandurga is a hidden gem in South India that offers a unique travel experience for travelers to explore. From its natural wonders to its rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone. Visit this picturesque town to experience nature, culture, and adventure in one place.
