Introduction to Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Bhadra Tiger Reserve, located in the Chikmagalur district of Southern India, is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Established in 1998, it covers an area of 492.46 square kilometers and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna.

The reserve is named after the Bhadra River, which flows through it and provides a habitat for many aquatic species. The lush green forests, crystal clear streams, and serene surroundings make it an ideal destination for a peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary

The Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary is a popular tourist spot in the reserve. It is home to a wide variety of animals, including tigers, elephants, sambar deer, bison, and many more. Visitors can take a jeep safari and explore the sanctuary while enjoying the breathtaking views of nature.

Kudremukh National Park

Kudremukh National Park is another must-visit place in Bhadra Tiger Reserve. It is famous for its rolling hills, waterfalls, and rare species of fauna and flora. Visitors can trek through the forest and experience the beauty of nature.

Natural Attractions of Bhadra Tiger Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hebbe Falls

Hebbe Falls, located in the Chikmagalur district, is a famous tourist spot near the reserve. The falls drop from a height of 168 meters and offer a spectacular view of nature.

Mullayanagiri

Mullayanagiri, the highest peak in Karnataka, is located in the Chikmagalur district and serves as a popular trekking destination. The breathtaking view from the summit is a treat for every nature enthusiast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Bababudangiri Trek

The Bababudangiri Trek is a popular route for both hikers and bikers. It takes you through the lush green forests and scenic views of the mountains. The trek is challenging and offers a unique experience for adventure enthusiasts.

Kemmanagundi Trek

The Kemmanagundi Trek is another popular hiking route in the reserve, known for its picturesque views of nature. The trail takes you through coffee plantations, waterfalls, and scenic valleys.

Best Things to Do in Bhadra Tiger Reserve: Activities and Tours

Jeep Safari

Jeep Safari is an activity that every visitor must try while in the reserve. It is an excellent way to explore the surrounding forests and spot some of the rare fauna and flora. Visitors can book a jeep safari through the sanctuary officials.

Bird Watching

The Bhadra Tiger Reserve is a haven for bird watchers. It is home to over 250 species of birds, including the Indian pitta, Malabar trogon, and the great hornbill. Visitors can enjoy bird watching with the help of a local guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

Akki Rotti

Akki Rotti, a popular dish in Karnataka, is a rice-based bread that is served with chutney or curry. It is a staple food in the local cuisine and is a must-try dish for every visitor.

Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a South Indian dish that is famous all over the world. It is a thin crispy pancake made of rice batter and stuffed with a spicy potato filling.

Local Beverages

Filter Coffee

South Indian Filter Coffee is a must-try beverage for every coffee lover. It is made from freshly roasted coffee beans and served in small steel cups.

Tea

Tea is another popular beverage in the region. Visitors can enjoy a cup of tea at the local tea stalls while enjoying the scenic views of nature.

Where to Stay in Bhadra Tiger Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

River Tern Lodge

The River Tern Lodge is the only accommodation available in the reserve. The lodge offers a comfortable stay and provides various amenities, including a restaurant, swimming pool, and indoor games.

Homestays

There are many homestays available in the Chikmagalur district, which is a short drive from the reserve. Homestays offer an authentic experience of the local culture and are an excellent option for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Bhadra Tiger Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy various souvenirs and local products as a memory of their trip to the Bhadra Tiger Reserve. Some of the popular items include coffee, spices, honey, and handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bhadra Tiger Reserve

The local culture of the region is heavily influenced by the Hindu religion. Visitors are expected to dress conservatively and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bhadra Tiger Reserve

The best time to visit Bhadra Tiger Reserve is from October to February. The weather during this period is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy the scenic views of nature.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the reserve.
  • Carry proper gear while indulging in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the reserve.
  • Disturb the animals and birds.
  • Use plastic bags or bottles in the reserve.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Visitors are advised to take precautions while exploring the reserve. They should carry sufficient water and food and avoid going too deep inside the forest.

Health in Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Visitors should carry a basic first-aid kit with them. They should also take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and carry insect repellent.

Getting to Bhadra Tiger Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bhadra Tiger Reserve

The closest airport to Bhadra Tiger Reserve is the Mangalore International Airport, which is located approximately 170 kilometers from the reserve.

Bus Stations Near Bhadra Tiger Reserve

The Chikmagalur Bus Stand is the nearest bus station to the reserve, located approximately 38 kilometers away.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private taxi or take a train to reach the reserve.

How to Get Around in Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Visitors can explore the reserve on foot or take a jeep safari. Jeep safaris are available at the Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary and can be booked through the sanctuary officials.

Where to Go Next from Bhadra Tiger Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore many nearby destinations, including:

  • Chikmagalur
  • Kemmangundi
  • Mullayanagiri Peak
  • Kudremukh National Park

Conclusion

Bhadra Tiger Reserve is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the lush green forests, enjoy outdoor activities, and indulge in the local culture and cuisine. A trip to the reserve is an excellent way to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and experience the true beauty of nature.
