Introduction to Tiptur

Tiptur is a small town located in the Tumkur district of Central Karnataka, Southern India. It is known for its scenic beauty, ancient temples, and rich history. Tiptur is surrounded by the Western Ghats, making it a perfect spot for trekking, hiking and outdoor adventures. The town’s climate is pleasant throughout the year, making it a fine destination for a quick weekend getaway or a longer vacation. This comprehensive travel guide provides information on the places to visit, food, adventure activities, where to stay, and what to buy in Tiptur.

List of Places to Visit in Tiptur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Kaidala Temple

Kaidala Temple is a 600-year-old temple dedicated to Lord Chennakeshava. The temple’s architecture is a masterpiece of the Hoysala dynasty, with intricate carvings and sculptures engraved on the temple walls. It is a popular pilgrimage site near Tiptur.

2. Sri Siddheshwara Temple

Sri Siddheshwara Temple is a grand temple situated in a scenic location near the Tiptur reservoir. The architectural style of the temple is a blend of Vijayanagara and Dravidian styles, and it is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple has a large courtyard and a towering 70-feet tall rajagopuram.

3. Chinnaya Swamy Temple

Chinnaya Swamy Temple is an ancient temple located in the heart of Tiptur town. It is dedicated to Lord Venkateshwara and has an impressive architecture with a towering gopuram (tower). The temple attracts devotees from all over the state.

Natural Attractions of Tiptur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Tiptur Reservoir

Tiptur Reservoir is a beautiful lake located in the midst of lush greenery. It is a perfect spot for a picnic and to spend a relaxing day with family and friends. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing and bird watching in the serene surroundings.

2. Devarayanadurga Hills

Devarayanadurga Hills are a popular trekking spot near Tiptur, located about 16 km from the town. The hills are known for their scenic beauty and awe-inspiring views of the surrounding countryside. The hills also house a few ancient Hindu temples, making it a culturally significant spot.

3. Madhugiri Trek

Madhugiri is a hill near Tiptur with an elevation of 3930 feet. It is famous for its fort, which is built on top of the hill. Madhugiri trek is one of the most popular treks in the region, with a steep climb that rewards trekkers with breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tiptur

1. Kaidala Trek

Kaidala Trek is a moderate trek of about 3km, which takes you through a scenic landscape, offering a magnificent view of Kaidala Lake and the Western Ghats. The trek culminates in the Kaidala temple, which is a well-known pilgrimage site.

2. Tiptur Cycling Tour

Tiptur Cycling Tour is a popular way to explore the town’s scenic beauty and cultural attractions. The route starts from Tiptur Reservoir and passes through Kaidala Temple, Chinnayaswamy Temple, and the picturesque countryside before heading back to the reservoir.

Best Things to Do in Tiptur: Activities and Tours

1. Visit Tiptur Reservoir

A visit to Tiptur Reservoir is a must-do activity on your trip to Tiptur. Relax in the serene natural surroundings and enjoy a boat ride.

2. Trek to Madhugiri Fort

Trek to Madhugiri fort, an ancient monument located atop a hill near Tiptur. The fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and is said to be built by the Vijayanagara kings in the 17th century.

Food and Drink Scene of Tiptur

Must-Try Dishes

1. Maddur Vada

Maddur Vada is a popular snack food in Tiptur that originated in the nearby town of Maddur. It is a deep-fried snack made with a mixture of flour, rice flour, onions and spices, and is served with coconut chutney and sambar.

2. Akki Rotti

Akki Rotti is a traditional flatbread made with rice flour and vegetables. It is a staple food in the region, and locals often have it for breakfast. It is served with coconut chutney and spicy onion-tomato chutney.

Local Beverages

1. Filter Coffee

South Indian Filter Coffee is a popular beverage in Tiptur, and the town has many coffee shops that offer it. Filter coffee is made with freshly ground coffee beans, and served with hot milk and sugar.

2. Tambuli

Tambuli is a local drink made with yogurt, spices, and herbs. It is a refreshing beverage that helps cool the body in hot weather.

Where to Stay in Tiptur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Siri Residency

Siri Residency is a popular hotel in Tiptur that offers comfortable rooms, excellent food, and friendly staff. The hotel is located in the heart of the town, making it easily accessible from all the major attractions.

2. Farmstay Tiptur

Farmstay Tiptur is an excellent option for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The property has beautiful rooms, a farm-to-table restaurant, and many outdoor activities like trekking, bird watching, and fishing.

What to Buy in Tiptur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Sandalwood Products

Tiptur is famous for its sandalwood products, including sandalwood oil, incense sticks, and decorative items. These products make great souvenirs and gifts for friends and family.

2. Silk Sarees

Tiptur is known for its beautiful silk sarees, which are popular among women in the region. The sarees are made with high-quality silk and feature intricate designs and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Tiptur

Tiptur is a culturally and historically rich town, and the local people take great pride in their customs and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors and are always ready to share their culture.

Best Time to Visit Tiptur

The best time to visit Tiptur is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities. The temperature during this period ranges from 15-30 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tiptur

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and other religious sites
  • Carry a sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat while venturing outdoors
  • Try the local food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the natural surroundings
  • Don’t smoke or drink in public places as it is illegal in India
  • Don’t get too close to monkeys as they can be aggressive

Safety and Health

Safety in Tiptur

Tiptur is a safe town for tourists, and the locals are known for their hospitality towards visitors. However, it is always advisable to take precautions while travelling, such as keeping valuables safe and being cautious at night.

Health in Tiptur

Tiptur has many healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, to take care of any medical emergency. It is advisable to carry a first-aid kit while travelling and seek medical help in case of any health issues.

How to Get Around in Tiptur

Tourists can hire taxis, auto-rickshaws, and bikes to travel in and around Tiptur. Buses are also available connecting the town and the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Tiptur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Shimoga

Shimoga, located about 90 km from Tiptur, is a beautiful city surrounded by the Western Ghats. It is known for its ancient temples, waterfalls, and lush landscapes.

2. Hassan

Hassan is a historic town located about 90 km from Tiptur. It is home to many ancient temples and forts, and is known for its architecture and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Tiptur is a hidden gem in Southern India that is perfect for travellers seeking a blend of adventure and culture. The town’s natural beauty, ancient temples, and delicious food make it an ideal destination for all kinds of travellers. It is a must-visit place if you want to explore the less explored side of Karnataka.
