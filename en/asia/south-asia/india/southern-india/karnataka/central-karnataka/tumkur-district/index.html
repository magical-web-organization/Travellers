Tumkur (district) is a beautiful region located in Central Karnataka, one of the southern states in India. It is rich in history, culture, and natural wonders, making it a top destination for tourists and adventurers alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will give you a rundown of the must-visit places, food, culture, and safety and health measures to know before traveling to Tumkur. Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or just a short excursion, this guide will surely come in handy.

Introduction to Tumkur (District)

Tumkur district is situated in the heart of Karnataka and is bordered by Chitradurga, Hassan, Bengaluru Rural, and Ramanagara. It is easily accessible by various modes of transportation, such as buses and trains, making it a convenient destination for locals and foreigners alike. The district is famous for its beautiful hills, valleys, and majestic waterfalls, which draws nature lovers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Tumkur (District)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several significant historical places to visit in Tumkur that reflect the region’s rich history and diverse culture. Some of the must-visit historical places and museums are:

  • Madhugiri Fort: Madhugiri Fort is the second-largest monolith in the world after the Mount Athos in Greece. The fort was built in the 17th century, and it stands tall at an elevation of 3930 feet above sea level. It is a popular spot for trekking, and the view from the top of the fort is awe-inspiring.

  • Siddara Betta: Siddara Betta is a beautiful hill located 100 km from Bangalore, and it is famous for its ancient temples and caves. The hill is also known for its herbs and shrubs, which have medicinal properties.

  • Devarayanadurga: Devarayanadurga is a popular hill station situated 20 km from Tumkur town. The place is famous for its temples and scenic views. The Bhoga Narasimha and Yoganarasimha Temples are the major attractions here.

Natural Attractions of Tumkur (District): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers, Tumkur district offers several outdoor adventures and breathtaking views. Some of the best natural attractions are:

  • Shivagange: Shivagange is a beautiful hill located 56 km from Bangalore, with an elevation of 1,340 meters above sea level. It is an ideal place for trekking and rock climbing, and the view from the top of the hill is mesmerizing.

  • Kundana: Kundana is one of the hidden gems of Tumkur district, located just a few kilometers from Madhugiri. It is a lesser-known waterfall that gushes out from the rocky hills, and it’s a perfect spot for a day’s trip.

  • Namada Chilume: Namada Chilume is a small water spring located in the middle of a dense forest. It is believed to be the birthplace of the river Jayamangali, which passes through Tumkur town. The place is also home to various birds and animals, making it a must-visit spot for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tumkur (District)

Tumkur district offers several trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Trek to Madhugiri: The trek to Madhugiri is a challenging one and requires a high level of fitness. The trek is spread over a distance of 4 km, and the view from the top of the hill is breathtaking.

  • Shivagange Trek: Shivagange Trek is one of the most popular hiking trails in Karnataka. The trek takes you through lush green forests, rocky terrain, and steep climbs. The view from the top of the hill is awe-inspiring, making it a must-visit spot for adventure enthusiasts.

  • Biking to Devarayanadurga Hill: Biking to Devarayanadurga Hill is another exciting adventure for enthusiasts. The hill is located 20 km from Tumkur town, and biking to the hill is a thrilling experience.

Best Things to Do in Tumkur (District): Activities and Tours

Apart from trekking, biking, and enjoying the natural beauty of Tumkur, there are several other things to do in the district. Some of the best activities include:

  • Visit Temples: Tumkur district is home to several ancient temples that are steeped in history and culture. Some of the must-visit temples include Gubbi Channakeshava Temple, Yediyur Siddhalingeshwara Swamy Temple, and Bhoga Narsimha Temple.

  • Visit the Tumkur Turf Club: The Tumkur Turf Club is a popular horse racing track that hosts several races throughout the year. The racing season starts in October and ends in June.

  • Enjoy Local Cuisine: Tumkur is famous for its traditional cuisine, which includes dishes like Akki Rotti, Jowari Rotti, and Holige. The cuisine is spicy and flavorful, making it a must-try for food lovers.

Food and Drink Scene of Tumkur (District)

Tumkur district has a rich culinary tradition, which is reflected in its food scene. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Akki Rotti: Akki Rotti is a traditional Karnataka dish made of rice flour, coconut, and spices. It is a popular breakfast dish in Tumkur.

  • Jowari Rotti: Jowari Rotti is another popular dish made of jowar flour (sorghum), which is a staple food in Karnataka.

  • Holige: Holige is a sweet dish made of coconut, jaggery, and flour. It is a must-try dish during festivals.

Local Beverages:

  • Filter Coffee: Filter coffee is a popular beverage in Karnataka, and Tumkur is no exception. It is a strong, aromatic coffee that is brewed with a filter.

  • Buttermilk: Buttermilk is a refreshing drink made of yogurt, water, and spices. It is a popular drink during summers and is believed to have cooling properties.

Where to Stay in Tumkur (District): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tumkur district offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best accommodation options include:

  • Hotel Naveen Regency: Hotel Naveen Regency is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Tumkur town. It offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  • Hotel Sri Siddhartha: Hotel Sri Siddhartha is a mid-range hotel located near the Devarayanadurga hills. It offers modern amenities and is situated amidst lush greenery.

  • The Gateway Hotel: The Gateway Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the outskirts of Tumkur. It offers excellent rooms, world-class amenities, and is situated amidst beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Tumkur (District): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tumkur district is famous for its traditional handicrafts, textiles, and fruits. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Silk Saris: Tumkur is famous for its silk saris, which are made with pure silk and come in colorful designs.

  • Papad and Pickles: Tumkur is also famous for its papad and pickles, which are made with traditional ingredients and have a unique flavor.

  • Chikka Guava: Tumkur is known for growing one of the best varieties of guava in India, which is popularly known as Chikka Guava. It is a delicious fruit with a unique taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Tumkur (District)

Tumkur is a diverse district with a rich culture and tradition. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Dasara Festival: Dasara is a popular festival celebrated by the locals with great enthusiasm. It is a ten-day festival that marks the victory of good over evil.

  • Yakshagana: Yakshagana is a popular form of folk theater that is performed in Tumkur during festivals and special occasions. The performances are colorful and entertaining and showcase the rich cultural heritage of the district.

Best Time to Visit Tumkur (District)

The best time to visit Tumkur is between September and March. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the humidity is low, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tumkur (District)

Do:

  • Respect local customs: Tumkur is a diverse district with a rich culture and tradition. It is important to respect local customs and traditions while visiting the district.

  • Be mindful of the environment: Tumkur is home to several natural wonders and outdoor adventures. It is important to be mindful of the environment and not litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities: Engaging in illegal activities such as drug use or purchase is strictly prohibited in Tumkur.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tumkur (District)

Tumkur is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is important to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night: It is advisable to avoid traveling alone at night, especially in isolated areas.

  • Beware of pickpockets and scams: Beware of pickpockets and scams in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Health in Tumkur (District)

Tumkur is a relatively healthy region, and there are no major health concerns. However, it is advisable to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Drink bottled water: It is advisable to drink bottled water to avoid any water-borne diseases.

  • Apply mosquito repellent: Mosquitoes are prevalent in some parts of Tumkur, and it is advisable to apply mosquito repellent to avoid getting mosquito bites.

Getting to Tumkur (District): Transportation Options and Logistics

Tumkur is well-connected to other major cities in Karnataka and India. Some of the transportation options include:

  • Air: The nearest airport to Tumkur is Kempegowda International Airport in Bangalore, which is situated 80 km from Tumkur.

  • Train: Tumkur has a railway station, and several trains stop at the station.

  • Bus: Tumkur is well-connected by road, and several buses ply from Bangalore and other major cities.

How to Get Around in Tumkur (District)

Tumkur is a small district, and one can easily get around by various modes of transportation, such as:

  • Auto-rickshaws: Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of transportation in Tumkur and are widely available.

  • Buses: Tumkur has a well-established bus network that connects different parts of the district.

  • Taxis: Taxis are available on hire and can be booked through various online platforms.

Where to Go Next from Tumkur (District): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tumkur is surrounded by several popular destinations that are worth exploring. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Bangalore: Bangalore is the capital of Karnataka and is located just 70 km from Tumkur. It is a bustling city known for its nightlife, food, and shopping.

  • Mysore: Mysore is another popular destination located 150 km from Tumkur. The city is famous for its palaces, museums, and parks.

  • Hampi: Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located 350 km from Tumkur. The place is famous for its ancient temples, monuments, and ruins.

Conclusion

Tumkur district is a hidden gem of Southern India, offering a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
