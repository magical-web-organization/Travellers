Introduction to Chikmagalur (District)

Chikmagalur (District) is a picturesque hill station located in the southern Indian state of Karnataka. The district is nestled in the Cauvery Basin and is known for its natural splendor, adventure activities, and rich cultural heritage.

The region was ruled by various kingdoms over the centuries, including the Kadambas, Hoysalas, Vijayanagara Empire, and Mysore Kingdom. This has left its mark on the district, with many historical sites and artifacts still standing today.

Apart from its rich history, Chikmagalur (District) is also known for its coffee plantations, serene landscapes, and outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and trekking. With its pleasant climate and scenic beauty, Chikmagalur (District) is a beloved destination for locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Chikmagalur (District)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mullayanagiri: The highest peak in the district, Mullayanagiri offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for trekking and camping. It also has a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva at the summit.
  • Halebidu: Home to the ornate Hoysaleswara Temple, which features intricate carvings and sculptures depicting Hindu mythology.
  • Belur: Known for the impressive Chennakesava Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu and featuring striking sculptures and carvings.
  • Shravanabelagola: Home to the Bahubali statue, one of the largest monolithic statues in the world and an important site for Jain pilgrims.
  • Kodanda Rama Temple: A 17th-century temple dedicated to Lord Rama, featuring intricate carvings and a serene pond.

Natural Attractions of Chikmagalur (District): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baba Budangiri: A mountain range near Chikmagalur town, famous for its coffee plantations and the Baba Budan Dargah, a shrine honoring a Sufi saint.
  • Kudremukh National Park: A protected area renowned for its scenic beauty and diverse wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and bears.
  • Hebbe Falls: A popular waterfall nestled in the Western Ghats, offering a stunning view of the cascading water.
  • Kemmangundi: A hill station known for its beautiful gardens, scenic views, and hiking trails.
  • Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to a range of endangered species, including the Indian giant squirrel, Malabar trogon, and brown fish owl.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chikmagalur (District)

  • Kudremukh Trek: A challenging, two-day trek through the Kudremukh National Park, taking in stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests along the way.
  • Baba Budangiri Trek: A moderately difficult trek that takes you through the lush green hills and coffee estates of Baba Budangiri, finishing at the stunning Manikyadhara Falls.
  • Mullayanagiri Trek: A moderate hike to the highest peak in the district, offering beautiful views of the surrounding valleys and hills.
  • Kemmangundi Trek: A scenic hike through the lush green hills of Kemmangundi, taking in waterfalls and viewpoints along the way.

Best Things to Do in Chikmagalur (District): Activities and Tours

  • Coffee Plantation Tour: Take a guided tour of one of the many coffee plantations in the region, learning about the history and methods of coffee cultivation, and sampling some of the finest brews.
  • River Rafting: Raft down the Bhadra River, taking in the beautiful scenery and thrilling rapids along the way.
  • Zip-lining: Soar over the coffee plantations and lush green hills of Chikmagalur (District) on a thrilling zip-line adventure.
  • Cycling Tour: A guided cycling tour through the beautiful countryside, taking in the panoramic views and stopping at local farms and plantations along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Chikmagalur (District)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Akki Rotti: A traditional Kannada dish made from rice flour and served with a range of accompaniments such as chutney, curry, or yogurt.
  • Koli Saaru: A spicy chicken curry made with coconut, onions, and a range of aromatic spices.
  • Puliyogare: A tangy dish made from tamarind pulp and a mix of fragrant spices, served with rice.
  • Bisi bele bath: A flavorful rice dish made with lentils, vegetables, and a spicy masala.
  • Chapati: A staple bread made from wheat flour, cooked on a griddle, and served with a range of side dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Chikmagalur (District) is renowned for its coffee plantations, and a cup of freshly brewed coffee is a must-try.
  • Tea: The district also produces a range of high-quality teas, which are a popular alternative to coffee.
  • Badam Milk: A sweet and creamy milkshake made from almonds, milk, and a range of fragrant spices.

Where to Stay in Chikmagalur (District): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Gateway Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the lush green hills and featuring top-notch amenities such as a spa and swimming pool.
  • The Serai: A rustic-chic resort featuring spacious villas and surrounded by coffee plantations.
  • Eagle Eye Holidays: A homestay-style guesthouse, located on a hilltop and offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Nature Craft Homestay: A budget-friendly option for travelers, featuring cozy rooms and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Chikmagalur (District): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee: Chikmagalur (District) is renowned for its coffee plantations, and a bag of freshly roasted coffee beans is an ideal souvenir.
  • Silk Sarees: The region is known for its high-quality silk fabrics, which are intricately woven and come in a range of vibrant colors and designs.
  • Spices: Chikmagalur (District) is also famous for its spicy cuisine, and a range of aromatic spices such as pepper and cardamom can be purchased here.

Local Culture and Customs of Chikmagalur (District)

Chikmagalur (District) is known for its rich culture and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and values.

  • Dress Code: Modest attire is recommended when visiting temples and historical sites.
  • Language: The primary language spoken in the district is Kannada, although Hindi and English are also widely spoken.
  • Religion: The region is predominantly Hindu, although there is also a significant Muslim and Christian population.

Best Time to Visit Chikmagalur (District)

The best time to visit Chikmagalur (District) is between September and May, when the weather is pleasant, and the monsoon season has ended. The district experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon months of June, July, and August, which can make outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chikmagalur (District)

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture: Be respectful of the local customs and traditions when visiting temples and historical sites.
  • Carry Appropriate Clothing and Gear: Wear comfortable shoes, and carry light and loose clothing if you plan to indulge in outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.
  • Carry a Water Bottle: Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.

Don’t:

  • Litter or Pollute the Environment: Help preserve the pristine beauty of the region by disposing of waste responsibly.
  • Harm Wildlife: Do not disturb or harm any animals you may encounter during your visit.
  • Disrespect the Locals: Be polite and courteous to the locals and refrain from any rude or offensive behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chikmagalur (District)

Chikmagalur (District) is a relatively safe destination, although visitors are advised to take precautions such as avoiding isolated areas, especially after dark. Also, be mindful of your belongings while traveling in crowded areas.

Health in Chikmagalur (District)

Visitors should take certain health precautions when traveling to Chikmagalur (District), such as drinking only bottled water and carrying insect repellents. Also, make sure you have appropriate travel insurance before embarking on your trip.

Getting to Chikmagalur (District): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chikmagalur (District)

The nearest airport to Chikmagalur (District) is Mangalore International Airport, located about 150 kilometers away. The airport is well-connected to major Indian cities such as Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore.

Bus Stations Near Chikmagalur (District)

Chikmagalur (District) is well-connected to major cities in Karnataka by public buses operated by Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) and private operators.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Chikmagalur (District) by renting a car or hiring a taxi from Bangalore or Mangalore.

How to Get Around in Chikmagalur (District)

The best way to get around Chikmagalur (District) is by hiring a taxi or renting a car. Visitors can also use public buses or take guided tours organized by local travel agents.

Where to Go Next from Chikmagalur (District): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sringeri: A pilgrimage site known for the Sharada Temple and a revered seat of the Shankaracharya.
  • Jog Falls: One of the tallest waterfalls in India, located about 240 kilometers away from Chikmagalur (District).
  • Coorg: A popular hill station known for its coffee plantations and scenic beauty, located about 150 kilometers from Chikmagalur (District).

Conclusion

Chikmagalur (District) is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty, rich culture, and heritage of Karnataka. With its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and outdoor activities, it offers something for everyone.
