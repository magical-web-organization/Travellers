Introduction to Chikmagalur

Nestled in the foothills of the Western Ghats, Chikmagalur is a serene, picturesque town in Karnataka. It is situated in the Cauvery Basin, about 180 km from the state capital Bangalore. Chikmagalur is known for its coffee plantations, lush green hills, and stunning waterfalls. The town is surrounded by dense forests, national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Chikmagalur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chennakesava Temple

The Chennakesava Temple, situated in Belur, is an excellent example of Hoysala architecture. The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and its intricate carvings and sculptures will leave you awe-struck.

Halebidu

Halebidu, located about 30 km from Chikmagalur, was once the capital of the Hoysala Dynasty. The Hoysaleswara Temple here is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is famous for its intricate carvings and sculptures.

Jhari Waterfalls

Jhari Waterfalls is a picturesque waterfall located near Attigundi. The waterfall cascades down from a height of 300 feet and is surrounded by lush green forests.

Natural Attractions of Chikmagalur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mullayanagiri

Mullayanagiri is the highest peak in Karnataka and is a popular trekking destination. The trek to the summit is challenging but the beautiful scenery and stunning views are well worth the effort.

Kudremukh National Park

Kudremukh National Park is a sprawling wildlife sanctuary that spans over 600 sq km. The park is home to a stunning array of flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and more.

Bababudangiri Hills

Bababudangiri Hills is a beautiful mountain range located about 30 km from Chikmagalur. It is a popular spot for trekking and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chikmagalur

Chikmagalur is a popular destination for both hiking and biking. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Mullayanagiri Trek
  • Kemmangundi Trek
  • Baba Budangiri Trek
  • Kudremukh Trek

Local Culture and Customs of Chikmagalur

The majority of the population in Chikmagalur is Kannada speaking. The town has a rich cultural heritage, and the local music and dance forms are unique and vibrant. One of the most popular festivals celebrated in the town is the ‘Jamboo Savari’.

Best Time to Visit Chikmagalur

The best time to visit Chikmagalur is from September to January when the weather is pleasant and the rainfall is minimal. The temperature during this time varies between 15-20 degrees Celsius.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Chikmagalur

Chikmagalur is a relatively safe place to visit. However, travellers should exercise caution when travelling alone or at night.

Health in Chikmagalur

Travellers should be mindful of their health and hygiene when visiting Chikmagalur. They should drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent food-borne illnesses.

Getting to Chikmagalur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chikmagalur

The closest international airport to Chikmagalur is Kempegowda International Airport in Bangalore. From here, travellers can take a cab or a bus to Chikmagalur.

Bus Stations Near Chikmagalur

Chikmagalur is well connected to other major cities in Karnataka through a network of state-run buses. The main bus station in the town is the KSRTC Bus Stand.

Other Options

Travellers can also hire a cab from Bangalore or Mangalore to reach Chikmagalur.

How to Get Around in Chikmagalur

Travellers can hire a cab or a taxi to get around Chikmagalur. Buses and auto-rickshaws are also available for shorter distances.
