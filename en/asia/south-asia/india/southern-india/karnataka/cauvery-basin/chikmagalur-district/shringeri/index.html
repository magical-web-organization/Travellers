Introduction to Shringeri

Located in the picturesque district of Chikmagalur in Karnataka, Shringeri is a small town that is famous for its spiritual and cultural significance. It is home to the iconic Sharada Peetham, which is considered one of the most revered centers of learning and worship in Southern India. Shringeri also boasts of scenic natural beauty, which makes it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Shringeri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sharada Peetham: The Sharada Peetham is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Saraswati. It is the main attraction of Shringeri and is famous for its intricate carvings and architecture.
  • Sri Vidyashankara Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and was built in the 14th century. The temple is known for its unique architecture that combines the styles of Hoysala and Dravida schools of architecture.
  • Sringeri Mutt: The Sringeri Mutt is a monastery that was established in the 8th century by Adi Shankaracharya. It is a center of learning and attracts devotees from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Shringeri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Agumbe Ghat: Located just 25 km from Shringeri, Agumbe Ghat is a scenic mountain pass that offers breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. It is also known as the “Cherrapunji of South India” due to its high rainfall.
  • Mandagadde Bird Sanctuary: The Mandagadde Bird Sanctuary is located 30 km from Shringeri and is home to a variety of bird species, including pelicans, storks, and herons.
  • Kundadri Hills: The Kundadri Hills are a popular trekking destination near Shringeri. The hills offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and are also known for the ancient Jain temple located on top of the hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shringeri

  • Agumbe Trek: The Agumbe Trek is a popular hiking trail that starts from Shringeri and takes you through the lush rainforests of the region. The trek is known for its stunning waterfalls and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Balehonnur Hike: The Balehonnur Hike is a challenging trek that takes you through the Western Ghats. The trail passes through scenic tea estates and leads to a beautiful waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Shringeri: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Sharada Peetham: A visit to the Sharada Peetham is a must when in Shringeri. The temple is known for its spiritual significance and exquisite architecture.
  • Explore the Western Ghats: Shringeri is located in the Western Ghats, which makes it an ideal destination for trekking, hiking, and other outdoor activities.
  • Witness the Sunset at Agumbe Ghat: The Agumbe Ghat is known for its stunning sunset views. A visit to this scenic spot is a must when in Shringeri.

Food and Drink Scene of Shringeri

Must-Try Dishes

  • Savige (Vermicelli) Uppittu: A popular breakfast dish made with vermicelli, vegetables, and spices.
  • Akki Rotti: A rice flour flatbread that is served with spicy chutney and vegetable curries.
  • Vaangi Bath: A rice dish made with brinjal and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: South India is famous for its filter coffee, and Shringeri is no exception. The coffee is strong and aromatic and is a must-try.
  • Ginger Lemon Juice: A refreshing drink made with fresh ginger, lemon, and soda.

Where to Stay in Shringeri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sharada International Hotel: A luxurious hotel with spacious rooms and all modern amenities.
  • Advaith Lancer: A budget hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Sringeri Residency: A guesthouse that offers simple rooms and is located close to the Sharada Peetham.

What to Buy in Shringeri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Sarees: Shringeri is famous for its silk sarees that are intricately woven and come in a variety of colors and designs.
  • Sandalwood Products: Karnataka is known for its sandalwood, and Shringeri is a great place to buy sandalwood products like incense sticks, oils, and perfumes.

Local Culture and Customs of Shringeri

  • Shringeri is a spiritual destination, and it is important to dress modestly and conservatively when visiting temples and other places of worship.
  • It is customary to remove footwear before entering temples and other religious places.
  • Shringeri is known for its rich cultural heritage and is home to many festivals and celebrations throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Shringeri

The best time to visit Shringeri is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and cool. However, the monsoon season (June to September) is also a great time to visit as the region comes alive with lush greenery and beautiful waterfalls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shringeri

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious places.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals to learn more about their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife when trekking or hiking.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shringeri

Shringeri is a relatively safe destination, but it is important to take basic precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid traveling alone at night and be aware of your surroundings when walking on narrow roads.

Health in Shringeri

Make sure to carry a first-aid kit and any prescription medications you may need. It is also advisable to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent gastrointestinal problems.

Getting to Shringeri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shringeri

The nearest airport to Shringeri is the Mangalore International Airport, which is about 105 km away. The airport is well-connected to major cities in India and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Shringeri

Shringeri has its own bus station that is well-connected to major cities and towns in Karnataka.

Other Options

One can also travel to Shringeri by car or by hiring a taxi from nearby cities like Bangalore or Mangalore.

How to Get Around in Shringeri

The best way to get around in Shringeri is by foot or by hiring a taxi or an autorickshaw. Bikes and scooters are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Shringeri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chikmagalur: Located 85 km from Shringeri, Chikmagalur is a popular hill station that is famous for its coffee plantations and scenic beauty.
  • Udupi: Udupi is a coastal town that is famous for its beautiful beaches and is located about 140 km from Shringeri.

Conclusion

Shringeri is a hidden gem in Karnataka that offers a perfect mix of spirituality, culture, and nature. From stunning temples to scenic hikes, there is something for every type of traveler. So, pack your bags and visit Shringeri to experience the true essence of South India.
