Bandipur National Park is one of the most popular destinations for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts in Southern India. Located in the Cauvery Basin of Karnataka, it is home to an incredible array of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and various species of deer. In this comprehensive guide to Bandipur National Park, we will explore the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, food and drink scene, where to stay, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Bandipur National Park

Bandipur National Park is a protected area and a tiger reserve located at the foothills of the Nilgiri Mountains. Sprawling over an area of approximately 874 sq. km, the park is surrounded by a scenic landscape of forests, grasslands, and scrublands. It is also an excellent place to observe a wide variety of birds and butterflies.

List of Places to Visit in Bandipur National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gopalaswamy Betta Temple: Built in the 14th century, this temple is dedicated to Lord Venugopalaswamy and offers panoramic views of the park.
  • Himavad Gopalaswamy Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and is located at an altitude of 1454 meters. It is surrounded by acres of tea gardens and offers scenic views of the countryside.
  • Somanathapura Temple: This 13th-century temple is famous for its intricate carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Bandipur National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Safari: Take a safari to explore the park’s wilderness and spot wild animals in their natural habitat.
  • Trekking: Trek through the park’s forests and grasslands to enjoy stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Bird Watching: Bandipur National Park is a paradise for birdwatchers, with over 200 species of birds to spot.
  • Visit a Tribal Village: Meet the locals and learn about their way of life by visiting a nearby tribal village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bandipur National Park

  • Gopalaswamy Betta Trek: An easy trek that offers scenic views of the park from the Gopalaswamy Temple’s hilltop.
  • Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta Trek: A challenging trek to the summit of the Himavad Gopalaswamy Temple, offering panoramic views of the surrounding forests and tea estates.

Best Things to Do in Bandipur National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Jeep Safari: Join a jeep safari to explore the park’s wilderness and spot wild animals in their natural habitat.
  • Elephant Safari: An elephant safari is a unique and thrilling experience that allows you to observe wild animals from close proximity.
  • Bird Watching Tour: Bandipur National Park is a bird lover’s paradise, with over 200 species of birds to spot.

Food and Drink Scene of Bandipur National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: A fragrant rice dish with spices and meat or vegetables.
  • Kebabs: Tender pieces of grilled meat, usually served with a dipping sauce.
  • Coconut Curry: A spicy, creamy curry made with coconut milk and vegetables or meat.
  • Dosa: A popular South Indian dish made with fermented rice and lentil batter, served with chutney and sambar.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: A popular South Indian coffee made with freshly-ground coffee beans and served with hot foamy milk.
  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink made from fresh coconut water. It is a popular drink in Southern India, especially during the hot summer months.

Where to Stay in Bandipur National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Serai Bandipur: A luxury resort that offers spacious rooms, swimming pool, and spa services.
  • MC Resorts: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms, restaurant, and outdoor activities.
  • Country Club Wildlife Resort: An eco-friendly resort that offers tented accommodation, wildlife safaris, and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Bandipur National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Spices: Bandipur National Park is the perfect place to stock up on aromatic spices like cardamom, black pepper, and cinnamon.
  • Tea: The region is well known for its tea plantations, and you can take home some of the finest tea leaves as souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: The nearby tribal villages offer some beautiful handicrafts made from bamboo, wood, and other natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Bandipur National Park

The local culture of Bandipur National Park is heavily influenced by the region’s tribal communities. The tribal people have a rich tradition of dance, music, and storytelling, which are still practiced to this day. Visitors are requested to respect the local customs and dress modestly when entering places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Bandipur National Park

The best time to visit Bandipur National Park is from October to May, as the weather is pleasant, and the park is open to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bandipur National Park

Do:

  • Hire an experienced guide to explore the park.
  • Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  • Carry enough water and sunscreen when going on outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park, as it is a protected area.
  • Feed the wild animals, as it can be dangerous for both animals and humans.
  • Play loud music, as it can disturb the natural habitat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bandipur National Park

Bandipur National Park is a relatively safe destination, but visitors are advised to take precautions while going on outdoor activities. Always travel with a group and avoid venturing into the park after sunset.

Health in Bandipur National Park

The local medical facilities in Bandipur National Park are limited, so it is recommended to carry a basic medical kit and any medications that you may require.

Getting to Bandipur National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bandipur National Park

The nearest airport to Bandipur National Park is Mysore Airport, which is approximately 60 km away. Bangalore International Airport is another airport that is located about 220 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bandipur National Park

The nearest bus station to Bandipur National Park is Gundulpet, which is around 20 km away. Regular buses from Mysore and Bangalore ply to Gundulpet.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for private taxi services or self-drive cars to reach Bandipur National Park.

How to Get Around in Bandipur National Park

The best way to get around Bandipur National Park is by hiring a local guide, who can show you the best spots and ensure your safety while exploring the park on foot or in a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Bandipur National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mysore: Known as the ‘city of palaces,’ Mysore is just an hour’s drive away from Bandipur National Park and is famous for its palaces, temples, and museums.
  • Coorg: A beautiful hill station located about 200 km from Bandipur National Park, Coorg is known for its scenic beauty, coffee plantations, and wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Ooty: Located just a few hours away from Bandipur National Park, Ooty is a popular tourist destination famous for its tea plantations, gardens, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Bandipur National Park is a nature lover’s paradise that offers something for everyone. Whether it’s exploring the park’s wilderness on a safari, trekking through the forests and grasslands, or simply relaxing amidst nature, Bandipur National Park is a must-visit destination in Southern India. So pack your bags and head to Bandipur National Park to experience the beauty of nature like never before!
