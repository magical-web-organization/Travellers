Introduction to Kollegal

Kollegal is a scenic town nestled in the Eastern Ghats and the Cauvery Basin of Karnataka, India. The town is renowned for its textile industry and sericulture, making it a hub of silk production within Karnataka. Kollegal is a mix of nature, history, and culture, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and cultural travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Kollegal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mahadeswara Hill: Located about 20 km from Kollegal town, this hill is home to the 400-year-old Mahadeshwara Temple, which attracts pilgrims from all over India. The temple is dedicated to a form of Lord Shiva and is surrounded by lush green forests.
  • Chamarajanagar: Located about 50 km from Kollegal, this historic town is home to the Bhimeshwara Temple, which dates back to the 10th century. The temple is a significant example of Dravidian architecture, and the intricate carvings on its walls and pillars are awe-inspiring. The town is also home to the Biligiri Rangaswamy Wildlife Sanctuary, which is famous for its diverse flora and fauna.
  • Kollegal Fort: The Kollegal Fort is a testament to the town’s history and served as a strategic location for various kingdoms that ruled the region. The fort now stands in ruins, with only a few structures remaining. However, the fort still offers a splendid view of the town.

Natural Attractions of Kollegal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hogenakkal Falls: Located about 70 km from Kollegal, the Hogenakkal Falls is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in southern India. The falls are formed by the Kaveri River and cascade down the rocky terrain, creating a natural spa for visitors to enjoy.
  • BR Hills: Biligiriranga Hills (BR Hills) is a protected wildlife reserve located about 80 km from Kollegal. The hills are home to various species of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, elephants, and gaurs. Visitors can enjoy wildlife safaris and trekking in the hills.
  • Mekedatu: Located about 30 km from Kollegal, Mekedatu is a popular picnic spot that offers breathtaking views of the gorge formed by the Kaveri River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kollegal

The hills and forests surrounding Kollegal offer a paradise for hikers and bikers.

  • Hills around BR Hills: The hills around BR Hills offer several trekking and biking routes, providing breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and wildlife.
  • Hiking in Chamarajanagar: There are several hiking routes near Chamarajanagar town that lead to scenic waterfalls and rugged terrain.
  • Cycling in Kollegal: Rent a bicycle and explore the beautiful countryside of Kollegal at your leisure.

Best Things to Do in Kollegal: Activities and Tours

  • Silk Weaving Factory Tour: Kollegal is known for its silk production. Take a tour of a silk weaving factory to learn about the intricate process of silk production and experience the local culture.
  • Wildlife Safari: Take a jeep safari into the forests of BR Hills to spot tigers, elephants, leopards, and other wildlife.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the serene and peaceful landscape of Kollegal from above, gliding over the lovely terrain in a hot air balloon.

Food and Drink Scene of Kollegal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Obbattu: A sweet flatbread is stuffed with lentils, jaggery, and coconut, and lightly fried.
  • Bisi Bele Bath: This rice dish is a spicy and flavorful preparation made with lentils, rice, and a mix of whole and powdered spices.
  • Rava Kesari: This is a traditional Indian dessert made with semolina, sugar, and ghee, flavored with cardamom and saffron.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: South Indian coffee is known for its strong flavor and aroma. Try the local filter coffee, which is filtered through a metal mesh to give a rich, frothy texture.
  • Mango Lassi: A popular summer drink made with mango pulp, yogurt, and a touch of sugar. It’s perfect for a hot day!

Where to Stay in Kollegal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kollegal offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some popular options include:

  • Gowri Resort: Located close to the Mahadeshwara Hill, this resort offers comfortable rooms and scenic views of the surrounding forests.
  • K G N Residency: A budget-friendly option in the heart of Kollegal town. The hotel offers clean rooms and basic amenities.
  • Jungle Lodges BR Hills: This eco-friendly lodge is located in the middle of the BR Hills Wildlife Sanctuary and offers an immersive wildlife experience.

What to Buy in Kollegal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Products: Kollegal is known for its silk production. Purchase silk sarees, scarves, and other silk products as souvenirs.
  • Honey: Kollegal is home to numerous honey farms. Purchase pure, organic honey to take home.
  • Handmade Crafts: Several local shops sell handmade crafts such as woven baskets, pottery, and handicrafts made with natural fibers.

Local Culture and Customs of Kollegal

  • Language: Kannada is the primary language spoken in Kollegal.
  • Religion: Hinduism is the predominant religion, with several temples dedicated to Lord Shiva and other deities.
  • Festivals: Mahashivaratri, Ugadi, and Diwali are some popular festivals celebrated in Kollegal. The Mahadeshwara Temple celebrates its annual jatra festival, which attracts pilgrims from all over the country.

Best Time to Visit Kollegal

The best time to visit Kollegal is during the winter months (November to February) as the weather is pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The summer months (March to May) can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rains and often disrupts most activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kollegal

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Explore the forests and wildlife sanctuaries with a licensed guide.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the forests and wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Disrespect religious sentiments.
  • Travel alone at night in remote areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kollegal

  • Watch out for wild animals while trekking or venturing into forests.
  • Avoid carrying expensive valuables or jewelry while visiting tourist places.
  • Take necessary precautions while traveling at night.

Health in Kollegal

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Carry mosquito repellent while traveling in forested areas.
  • Take adequate precautions against insect bites.

Getting to Kollegal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kollegal

The nearest major airport to Kollegal is the Kempegowda International Airport in Bangalore, located about 160 km away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Kollegal.

Bus Stations Near Kollegal

Kollegal has a well-connected bus station with buses operating from Bangalore, Mysore, and Chamarajanagar. Private bus operators are also available for easy travel.

Other Options

Kollegal does not have a railway station. The nearest railway station is Mysore Junction, located about 65 km away. You can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Kollegal from there.

How to Get Around in Kollegal

The best way to get around Kollegal is by private transport, such as hiring a car or bike. Autos and taxis are also available, but they may not be readily available for local transport.

Where to Go Next from Kollegal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bandipur National Park: Located about 95 km from Kollegal, Bandipur National Park is one of the top wildlife sanctuaries in southern India.
  • Mysore Palace: Mysore is a historic city located about 65 km from Kollegal. Visit the Mysore Palace, an impressive example of Indo-Saracenic architecture.
  • Shivanasamudra Falls: Located about 60 km from Kollegal, the Shivanasamudra Falls is one of the largest and most beautiful waterfalls in Karnataka.

Conclusion

Kollegal is a hidden gem in Karnataka, offering an ideal mix of nature, culture, and history. Its serene landscapes, magnificent temples, and lovely silk products make it a must-visit destination in southern India. So, pack your bags and embark on a memorable journey to Kollegal – you won’t be disappointed!
