Introduction to Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is a picturesque bird sanctuary located in Mandya (district), in the Cauvery Basin, in Karnataka, India. Spread over an area of 40 acres, this bird sanctuary is home to a vast array of exotic birds, and it has also been designated as an Important Bird Area by Birdlife International. The sanctuary is situated on the banks of the Kaveri river, and it is a popular destination for bird lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is not just about birds and wildlife, it is also a place of historical and cultural significance. The sanctuary is home to several museums and historical places, including the following:

  1. Ranganathaswamy Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Ranganatha, and it is a must-visit place for anyone interested in South Indian architecture.
  2. Srirangapatna Fort: This fort is located on an island in the Kaveri river, and it was built by Tipu Sultan in the 18th century.
  3. Gumbaz: This mausoleum was built by Tipu Sultan in memory of his father, Hyder Ali.

Natural Attractions of Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Boat Ride: A boat ride is the best way to explore the bird sanctuary and observe the exotic bird species that reside here. River boats of varying sizes are available for hire, and they take you on a journey through the serene waters of the Kaveri river.
  2. Bird Watching: Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is home to over 170 species of birds, including painted storks, pelicans, herons, and ibises. It is a bird watcher’s paradise!
  3. Nature Walk: A nature walk around the sanctuary is an excellent way to explore the different types of flora and fauna found here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Although Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is not known for hiking or biking trails, visitors can enjoy short trails and paths that are dotted with scenic views, including a small hill and river views.

Best Things to Do in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary offers several activities and tours that visitors can indulge in. Some of the best things to do in the sanctuary are:

  1. Riverboat Cruise: The riverboat cruise is perfect for those who want to explore the bird sanctuary’s vast diversity of bird species.
  2. Nature Walk: Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the manicured gardens while observing numerous species of flowers and trees.
  3. Photography: The serene backdrop of the Kaveri River and the numerous species of birds make the bird sanctuary an ideal place for photography and videography.
  4. Fishing: Fishing enthusiasts can take a boat tour and fish for catfish, carp, and other river fish in the Kaveri river.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Day 2: Boat ride and bird watching at Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary. Day 3: Visit Srirangapatna Fort and Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace. Day 4: Visit the Ranganathaswamy Temple. Day 5: Boat ride in the morning and afternoon nature walk at Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary. Day 6: Fishing and Photography tour. Day 7: Check-out, departure for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bisi Bele Bath: A wholesome, hot and spicy dish served with chutney and raita that is a must-try.
  2. Mysore Pak: An authentic and delicious dessert that is a must-try for anyone visiting the bird sanctuary.

Local Beverages

  1. Filter Coffee: Aromatic and strong, the South Indian filter coffee is a must-try for coffee lovers.
  2. Sugarcane Juice: A refreshing beverage with natural sugar from sugarcane, perfect for quenching thirst on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Several accommodation options are available for visitors to the Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary. Some of the best place to stay are:

  1. The Ranganathittu Nature Camp: This camp offers tented accommodation and a unique jungle experience with various outdoor activities available.
  2. The Young Island Resort: This resort is located on the tranquil island of Srirangapatna and offers visitors a unique and luxurious resort experience.
  3. River View Guest House: This is a budget-friendly option offering a stunning view of the Kaveri river.

What to Buy in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sandalwood Items: Sandalwood is found in abundance in Karnataka and is a must-buy for anyone visiting the state.
  2. Silk Saris: Mysore silk saris are famous worldwide for their beauty and exquisite craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

The local culture of Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is heavily influenced by the folklore, religion, and history of the region. Visitors should respect the local customs while visiting the sanctuary.

Best Time to Visit Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

The best time to visit Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is from November to June. The weather during these months is pleasant, and the birds are visible in large numbers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Do:

  1. Hire a guide to fully understand the sanctuary’s biodiversity and historical significance.
  2. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  3. Follow the sanctuary’s rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  1. Disturb the birds or their habitat.
  2. Litter in the sanctuary.
  3. Smoke or carry any inflammable material.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

  1. Keep an eye on children at all times during the boat ride.
  2. Wear a life jacket during the boat ride.
  3. Keep a safe distance from the crocodiles that inhabit the river.

Health in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

  1. Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  2. Carry water bottles to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

The nearest airport to reach the Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is the Bangalore International Airport, located about 130 kilometers from the sanctuary.

Bus Stations Near Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Mysore Bus Stand is the nearest bus station to reach the Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary. It is located about 14 kilometers from the sanctuary.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are other options available to reach the sanctuary.

How to Get Around in Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Visitors can use boats provided by Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary to explore the sanctuary. Walking is another option for easy exploration of the manicured trail network.

Where to Go Next from Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Srirangapatna: The island town of Srirangapatna is located near the bird sanctuary and is home to several historical sites with cultural and religious significance.
  2. Mysore: Mysore is a popular tourist destination and is a charming city known for its rich history and stunning architecture.
  3. Shivanasamudra Falls: These scenic waterfalls are located approximately 75 kilometers from the bird sanctuary and offer visitors an unmatched view of the flowing river and wetland ecosystem.

Conclusion

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is a must-visit destination for nature and bird lovers, history buffs, and those who seek peace and serenity on the banks of a tranquil river. The bird sanctuary offers a unique and immersive experience filled with diverse bird species, lush flora, and historical sites. Visitors can indulge in numerous activities and tours, taste local delicacies, and pick up souvenirs to take home. Proper awareness of safety tips, understanding local customs, and choosing the right time of the year ensures a pleasurable and memorable visit to this magical bird sanctuary.
