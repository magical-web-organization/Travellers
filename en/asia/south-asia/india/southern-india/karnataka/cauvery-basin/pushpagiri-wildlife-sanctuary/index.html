Introduction to Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in the Cauvery Basin of Karnataka, Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary is a nature lover’s paradise. Established in 1987, the sanctuary spans an area of 892 square kilometers of dense forests, hills, and wildlife. It is known for its rich biodiversity and houses several endangered species of flora and fauna. From hiking and biking trails to cultural attractions, our guide will help you plan a trip to Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary.

List of Places to Visit in Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Madikeri Fort: Built by Mudduraja in the 17th century, this fort served as a defensive structure against invaders.
  • Omkareshwara Temple: A Hindu temple built in the 19th century, it features a blend of Islamic and Gothic styles of architecture.
  • Bhagamandala: A prominent pilgrimage site, it is located at the confluence of three rivers: Cauvery, Kannike, and Sujyoti.

Natural Attractions of Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pushpagiri Trek: This 10-kilometer trek offers a challenging yet rewarding experience as you climb to the peak of Pushpagiri hill.
  • Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to several species of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, Indian bison, and more.
  • Kote Betta: Also known as Fort Hill, Kote Betta offers panoramic views of the surrounding forests and hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Pushpagiri Trek: A 10-kilometer trek that starts from the sanctuary gate and takes you to the peak of Pushpagiri hill.
  • Brahmagiri Trek: A 4-kilometer trek that takes you through the Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari: Take a jeep safari through the sanctuary and spot exotic species of flora and fauna.
  • Waterfall Rappelling: Experience the thrill of rappelling down a waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Plantation Walk: Take a leisurely stroll through the coffee and tea plantations, learn about the local farming techniques, and try some freshly brewed coffee.

Food and Drink Scene of Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pandi Curry: A spicy pork curry that is a popular dish of Coorg cuisine.
  • Bamboo Shoot Curry: Prepared with locally grown bamboo shoots, this curry is a unique delicacy of the region.
  • Akki Roti: A gluten-free flatbread made with rice flour, it is typically served with coconut chutney.

Local Beverages

  • Coorg Coffee: Known for its rich aroma and strong flavor, Coorg coffee is a must-try for coffee lovers.
  • Coconut Water: The refreshing and nutrient-rich coconut water is a popular drink in the region.

Where to Stay in Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Tamara Coorg: A luxury resort located amidst coffee plantations and hills, it offers panoramic views and modern amenities.
  • Coorg Wilderness Resort: A serene resort located in the heart of the sanctuary, it offers luxurious tents and cottages amidst nature.
  • Mistletoe Homestay: A budget-friendly homestay that offers comfortable rooms and local hospitality.

What to Buy in Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coorg Coffee: Take home some freshly roasted Coorg coffee beans and enjoy a sip of nature at home.
  • handicrafts: Coorg is known for its unique handicrafts made of bamboo, wood, and silk.
  • spices: Take home some locally grown spices such as cardamom, pepper, and turmeric.

Local Culture and Customs of Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

The local culture of Coorg is deeply rooted in the traditions of its indigenous community, Kodavas. The Kodavas are known for their martial arts, cuisine, and unique dressing style. The most prominent festival celebrated in Coorg is the Kodava festival, also known as the Puttari festival, which marks the beginning of the harvesting season. The festival is a celebration of the rich cultural heritage of the Kodavas and includes traditional music, dance, and food.

Best Time to Visit Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

The ideal time to visit Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary is from October to May when the weather is pleasant, and the flora and fauna are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry your ID proof and permits when entering the sanctuary.
  • Follow the guidelines issued by the sanctuary authorities.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or pollute the sanctuary.
  • Do not carry any plastic bags, as they are banned in the sanctuary.
  • Do not disturb the wildlife or damage the vegetation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Carry a first-aid kit and insect repellent.
  • Hire a guide when trekking, hiking, or biking.
  • Stay on the marked trails and avoid shortcuts.

Health in Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Carry your necessary medication and prescriptions.
  • Drink only bottled water or sterilized water.
  • Avoid consuming street food or uncooked food.
