Introduction to Arsikere

Arsikere is a small town located in the Hassan district of Karnataka in Southern India. It is known for its historic structures, beautiful landscapes, and cultural heritage. The name “Arsikere” is derived from two words – “aru” meaning six and “sike” meaning tanks. Arsikere is believed to have been named after the six ancient tanks that once existed in the region. With its rich history and natural beauty, Arsikere is a must-visit for anyone travelling to Karnataka.

List of Places to Visit in Arsikere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ishvara Temple – This ancient temple was built during the Hoysala dynasty and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The intricate carvings on the walls and ceilings of the temple are a must-see for art lovers.
  • Chennakeshava Temple – Built in the 12th century AD, this temple is another popular attraction in Arsikere. It is known for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Pancha Lingeshwara Temple – Located in the heart of the town, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and has five lingams (representations of the deity) inside the sanctum sanctorum.
  • Shettihalli Rosary Church – This abandoned church is a popular tourist spot in Arsikere. It is submerged in water for most of the year, but during summer, one can see the structure of the church above the water level.

Natural Attractions of Arsikere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary – This sanctuary is located at a distance of 60 km from Arsikere and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can go on jeep safaris and trekking expeditions to explore the sanctuary.
  • Bandali Forest – This forest is located near the Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary and is a popular spot for nature lovers and bird watchers. The forest is home to a variety of rare bird species.
  • Nuggehalli Hills – These hills provide a panoramic view of the surrounding landscapes and offer a serene environment for trekking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arsikere

  • Nagamangala to Arsikere Trek – This trek passes through picturesque landscapes and small villages. It covers a distance of 14 km and takes about 4-5 hours to complete.
  • The Eclectic Arsikere Cycling Trail – This cycling trail covers a distance of 42 km and passes through lush green paddy fields, ancient temples, and historic structures.

Best Things to Do in Arsikere: Activities and Tours

  • Heritage Walks – Take a heritage walk through the town of Arsikere to get an insight into its rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Jeep Safaris – Explore the Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary on a jeep safari and catch a glimpse of its exotic flora and fauna.
  • Bird Watching Tours – Visit the Bandali forest for a bird watching tour and spot some rare species of birds in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Arsikere

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ragi Mudde – A popular dish made from finger millet and served with sambar or chutney.
  • Akki Rotti – A flatbread made from rice flour and served with coconut chutney.
  • Kadabu – Dumplings made from jaggery, coconut, and rice flour.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee – A type of coffee made by dripping hot water through a bed of coffee grounds.
  • Bisi Bele Bath – A spicy rice dish served with lentils and vegetables.

Where to Stay in Arsikere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Grand Ashok Hotel – A premium hotel located in the heart of the town.
  • Mayura Yatri Nivas – A budget-friendly hotel located near the Arsikere railway station.
  • Hotel Nisarga – A comfortable hotel located on the outskirts of the town.

What to Buy in Arsikere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Sarees – Arsikere is known for producing high-quality silk sarees with intricate designs.
  • Handicrafts – Visitors can also buy wooden handicrafts and showpieces made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Arsikere

Arsikere is a blend of different cultures and traditions. The local people are friendly and welcoming. Visitors are expected to dress modestly while visiting temples and other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Arsikere

The best time to visit Arsikere is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arsikere

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Carry cash as most places don’t accept cards.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural surroundings.
  • Take photographs without seeking prior permission.
  • Deface or damage any monuments or structures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arsikere

Arsikere is a safe place to visit. However, visitors are advised to be cautious while travelling to remote areas and keep their belongings safe.

Health in Arsikere

Visitors are advised to carry their own medicines and consult a doctor before visiting Arsikere. The local hospitals and clinics may not have adequate facilities.

Getting to Arsikere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arsikere

The nearest airport to Arsikere is the Kempegowda International Airport, Bangalore, which is approximately 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near Arsikere

Arsikere has its own bus station, which connects it to major cities in Karnataka and neighbouring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Arsikere by train. The Arsikere railway station is well-connected to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Arsikere

Visitors can hire taxis or autos to get around town. Buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Arsikere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chikmagalur – A hill station located at a distance of 120 km from Arsikere.
  • Hassan – A historical town located at a distance of 40 km from Arsikere.
  • Shivamogga – A popular tourist destination located at a distance of 100 km from Arsikere.

Conclusion

Arsikere is a hidden gem in Karnataka that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its ancient temples, scenic landscapes, and outdoor adventures, it is a must-visit for anyone travelling to South India.
