Belur Travel Guide - Explore the Best of Belur

Belur is a small town located in the Hassan district of Karnataka, known for its beautiful temples and historic significance. Belur was once the capital of the Hoysala Empire, which flourished between the 11th and 14th centuries. Belur is a great place to visit for travelers who are interested in history, culture, and natural beauty.

Introduction to Belur

Belur is situated in the Cauvery Basin in the southern region of India. The town is well connected by road and rail, and the nearest airport is the Kempegowda International Airport in Bangalore, which is approximately 222 km away. The town is surrounded by lush green hills, and the Yagachi River flows through the town. Belur has a pleasant climate throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the winter months, from October to March.

List of Places to Visit in Belur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Belur is home to some of the most significant temples and historical sites in southern India. Here is a list of the top attractions:

  1. Chennakesava Temple: The Chennakesava Temple is a masterpiece of Hoysala architecture, and it is one of the most beautiful temples in India. The temple took over a hundred years to complete, and its intricate carvings and sculptures are truly breathtaking. The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is a must-visit attraction in Belur.

  2. Hoysaleswara Temple: The Hoysaleswara Temple is another masterpiece of Hoysala architecture, and it is located in Halebidu, which is approximately 17 km away from Belur. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is famous for its intricate carvings and sculptures.

  3. Shravanabelagola: Shravanabelagola is an important Jain pilgrimage center located approximately 80 km away from Belur. The town is famous for its 57-feet tall monolithic statue of Lord Bahubali, which is one of the tallest monolithic statues in the world.

Natural Attractions of Belur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Belur is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, including hills, rivers, and forests. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions:

  1. Yagachi Dam: The Yagachi Dam is a popular picnic spot located on the Yagachi River, approximately 3 km away from Belur. The dam offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is a great place for a day out with family and friends.

  2. Kudremukh National Park: The Kudremukh National Park is a beautiful forest reserve located approximately 138 km away from Belur. The park is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, and elephants. The park offers jungle safaris and hiking trails, making it a must-visit attraction for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belur

Belur is surrounded by picturesque hills, making it a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Belur to Yagachi Dam: The route from Belur to Yagachi Dam is a popular biking route, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.

  2. Hike to the top of Chennakesava Temple: The hike to the top of Chennakesava Temple is an excellent way to explore the temple’s intricate carvings and sculptures.

Best Things to Do in Belur: Activities and Tours

Belur offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Belur:

  1. Visit the Chennakesava Temple and admire its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.

  2. Take a scenic drive through the hills surrounding Belur and enjoy the stunning scenery.

  3. Take a jungle safari in Kudremukh National Park and spot tigers, leopards, and elephants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belur

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to Belur:

Day 1: Visit the Chennakesava Temple and explore the town of Belur.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Halebidu and visit the Hoysaleswara Temple.

Day 3: Visit the Yagachi Dam and enjoy a picnic by the river.

Day 4: Take a jungle safari in Kudremukh National Park.

Day 5: Visit Shravanabelagola and admire the 57-feet tall monolithic statue of Lord Bahubali.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the hills surrounding Belur.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings of Belur.

Food and Drink Scene of Belur

Belur offers a unique culinary experience, with a variety of local dishes and beverages available. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bisi Bele Bath: Bisi Bele Bath is a traditional Karnataka dish made with rice, lentils, vegetables, and spices. It is a must-try dish for visitors to Belur.

  2. Akki Roti: Akki Roti is a traditional Karnataka flatbread made with rice flour, vegetables, and spices. It is a popular breakfast dish in Belur.

Local Beverages

  1. Filter Coffee: Filter coffee is a popular beverage in Karnataka, and it is a must-try for coffee lovers.

  2. Jolada Rotti: Jolada Rotti is a traditional Karnataka flatbread made with jowar flour. It is often served with spicy curries and is a must-try dish for visitors to Belur.

What to Buy in Belur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belur offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products for visitors to take home. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy in Belur:

  1. Handmade Scarves: Belur is famous for its handmade scarves, which are available in a variety of colors and patterns.

  2. Sandalwood Products: Sandalwood is a popular local product in Karnataka, and it is available in the form of soaps, oils, and incense.

Local Culture and Customs of Belur

Belur has a rich cultural heritage, and the town is famous for its intricate temples and architecture. Visitors to Belur should dress conservatively when visiting places of religious significance and should remove their shoes before entering temples.

Best Time to Visit Belur

The best time to visit Belur is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belur

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites
  • Explore the natural beauty of the surrounding hills and forests

Don’t:

  • Disrespect any religious or cultural beliefs

Safety and Health

Safety in Belur

Belur is a safe town for travelers, but visitors should take basic precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Belur

Belur has a basic healthcare infrastructure, but visitors should take basic precautions, such as drinking only bottled water and carrying a basic medical kit.

Belur is a small town, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Belur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belur is surrounded by a variety of popular destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some nearby destinations:

  • Halebidu: Halebidu is located approximately 17 km away from Belur and is famous for its historic Hoysaleswara Temple.
