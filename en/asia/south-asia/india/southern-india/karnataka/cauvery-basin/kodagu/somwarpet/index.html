Introduction to Somwarpet

Somwarpet is a small town located in the Kodagu district, in the Cauvery Basin of Karnataka, India. It’s known for its lush greenery, coffee plantations, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage. The town is surrounded by the Western Ghats, offering panoramic views of the hills, forests, and valleys. Somwarpet is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those who are seeking a break from the fast-paced city life.

List of Places to Visit in Somwarpet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tipu Sultan Fort

Tipu Sultan Fort is a 200-year-old fort built during the reign of the famous Mysore King, Tipu Sultan. It’s located in the heart of Somwarpet town and is a popular attraction for history buffs. The fort has been renovated and now serves as a government office.

Harangi Dam

Harangi Dam is a beautiful reservoir situated near the town of Kushalnagar, just 20 km from Somwarpet. The dam is built on the river Harangi and is surrounded by lush green mountains. It’s a favorite picnic spot for locals and tourists alike.

Natural Attractions of Somwarpet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mallalli Falls

Mallalli Falls is a stunning waterfall located in the Western Ghats, about 25 km from Somwarpet. To reach the waterfall, you need to trek through the lush green forest that offers an unforgettable experience. The fall is best visited during the monsoon season, offering a spectacular view.

Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

The Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary is located at the center of the Western Ghats and is famous for its diverse flora and fauna. The sanctuary is home to several endangered species of birds and animals, including elephants, tigers, and leopards. It’s a favorite spot for trekking, camping, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somwarpet

Somwarpet is surrounded by dense forests and undulating hills, making it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region include:

  • Boova Kallu Trek
  • Kumara Parvatha Trek
  • Nishani Motte Trek
  • Tadiandamol Trek

These routes take you through the heart of the Western Ghats, offering breathtaking views of the hills, forests, and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Somwarpet: Activities and Tours

Coffee Plantation Tour

Somwarpet is famous for its coffee plantations, and a tour of these plantations is a must-do activity. The tour takes you through the plantation, giving you an insight into the coffee-making process. You can also taste the locally grown coffee.

River Rafting

If you’re an adventure junkie, then river rafting in Barapole River is a must-do activity. The river is known for its Grade III and IV rapids, offering an adrenaline-pumping experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Somwarpet

Must-Try Dishes

Pandhi Curry

Pandhi Curry is a famous Kodava dish made with tender pork cooked in a blend of aromatic spices and herbs.

Kadambuttu

Kadambuttu is a popular Kodava dish made with steamed rice dumplings, served with spicy curries.

Local Beverages

Coffee

Somwarpet is known for its coffee plantations and local coffee. You can find freshly roasted coffee beans and coffee powder in the local markets.

Palm Wine

Palm wine, also known as toddy, is a popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented palm tree sap. It’s a must-try local drink.

  • The Windflower Resort & Spa

  • La Flora Jungle Hill Resort

What to Buy in Somwarpet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Coorg Coffee

Coorg coffee is known throughout India for its rich flavor and aroma. You can buy freshly roasted coffee beans or powder from the local markets.

Handicrafts

Kodagu is famous for its handicrafts, including traditional jewelry, wooden carvings, and silk sarees. You can find them in the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Somwarpet

The local culture of Somwarpet is heavily influenced by the Kodava culture, which is unique to the region. The Kodavas are known for their rich traditions, colorful costumes, and unique cuisine. The Kodava language is also widely spoken in the region.

Best Time to Visit Somwarpet

The best time to visit Somwarpet is between October and March when the weather is cool and pleasant. During this time, you can explore the natural beauty of the region without worrying about extreme weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somwarpet

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Carry a raincoat or umbrella during the monsoon season.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public places.
  • Litter the environment.
  • Click photos without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somwarpet

Somwarpet is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially during late hours.

Health in Somwarpet

It’s always advisable to carry a first-aid kit when traveling to Somwarpet. You should also carry sufficient medications for any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Somwarpet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Somwarpet

The nearest airport to Somwarpet is the Kempegowda International Airport in Bangalore, which is about 260 km away.

Bus Stations Near Somwarpet

There are several bus stations in Somwarpet, including KSRTC Bus Stand and Somwarpet Private Bus Stand. You can reach Somwarpet from Bangalore, Mysore, and other nearby cities by bus.

Other Options

You can also reach Somwarpet by train or hire a taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Somwarpet

The best way to get around Somwarpet is by hiring a taxi. You can also rent a motorbike or bicycle to explore the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Somwarpet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Madikeri

Madikeri is a popular hill station located just 34 km from Somwarpet. It’s famous for its stunning scenery, coffee plantations, and historic landmarks.

Kushalnagar

Kushalnagar is a small town, about 20 km from Somwarpet, known for its Tibetan settlement and the famous Namdroling Monastery.
