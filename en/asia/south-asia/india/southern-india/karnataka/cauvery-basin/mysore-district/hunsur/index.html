Introduction to Hunsur

Located in the Mysore district of Karnataka, Hunsur is a small town nestled in the lap of nature. It is situated in the Cauvery basin, which is known for its lush forests and serene landscapes. The town is famous for its cultural heritage, temples, and natural beauty. Hunsur is perfect for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy some peace and quiet amidst tranquil surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Hunsur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hunsur has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit places to explore:

  • Sri Veeranarayana Temple: Built during the Hoysala era, this temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is famous for its intricate carvings.
  • Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple: Located in Tigalarapete, this temple is dedicated to Lord Ranganatha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu.
  • Government Museum Hunsur: This museum is located in the heart of the town and houses a collection of rare artifacts, ancient coins, and sculptures.
  • Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary: This sanctuary is a paradise for birdwatchers and home to over 170 species of birds, including painted storks, spoonbills, and kingfishers.

Natural Attractions of Hunsur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hunsur is blessed with natural beauty and offers a host of outdoor activities, from trekking and hiking to wildlife safaris. Here are some of the natural attractions you must visit:

  • Nagarhole National Park: This national park is located a few kilometers away from Hunsur and is home to tigers, elephants, and more than 270 species of birds.
  • Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is situated on the border of Karnataka and Kerala and is known for its rich flora and fauna.
  • Dubare Elephant Camp: Located on the banks of the Cauvery River, this camp is a great place to interact with elephants and learn about their habitat and behavior.
  • Kaveri Nisargadhama: This natural island is surrounded by the Cauvery River and is known for its bamboo forests and deer park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hunsur

Hunsur is an ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts. The town offers numerous hiking and biking routes that are perfect for those who love to explore the outdoors on foot or by cycle. Here are some of the best routes to try out:

  • Talakad to Somanathapura: This route covers the picturesque villages of Talakad and Somanathapura and is popular for its historical temples and scenic landscapes.
  • Hunsur to Madikeri: This route is perfect for those who want to explore the Western Ghats and is known for its misty hills, coffee plantations, and waterfalls.
  • Nagarhole National Park to Iruppu Falls: This route is perfect for nature lovers who want to combine wildlife spotting with trekking and waterfall-hopping.

Best Things to Do in Hunsur: Activities and Tours

Hunsur offers a range of activities and tours that are perfect for every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Hunsur:

  • Visit a Wildlife Sanctuary: Nagarhole National Park and Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary offer great opportunities to spot tigers, elephants, and other wildlife.
  • Explore Historical Temples: Hunsur is home to numerous temples, including the Sri Veeranarayana Temple and the Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, which are known for their intricate carvings.
  • Interact with Elephants: Dubare Elephant Camp offers visitors a chance to get up close and personal with elephants and learn about their behavior.
  • Indulge in Adventure Activities: Hunsur offers activities such as trekking, biking, and camping that are ideal for adventure enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hunsur

To make the most of your trip, we suggest a week-long itinerary that covers the best of Hunsur:

Day 1: Arrival in Hunsur and visit Sri Veeranarayana Temple Day 2: Explore Nagarhole National Park Day 3: Visit Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple and Government Museum Hunsur Day 4: Trek to Somanathapura and visit the famous Keshava Temple Day 5: Visit Dubare Elephant Camp and indulge in adventure activities such as camping and rafting Day 6: Explore the Western Ghats and visit the Iruppu Falls Day 7: Departure from Hunsur

Food and Drink Scene of Hunsur

Must-Try Dishes

Hunsur’s cuisine is influenced by its neighboring regions, including Tamil Nadu and Kerala. Here are some dishes that you should try when in Hunsur:

  • Bisi Bele Bath: This is a traditional Karnataka dish made with rice, lentils, and spices.
  • Kadambuttu: These are steamed rice balls and are a popular breakfast option in Hunsur.
  • Nool Puttu: This is a noodle-like dish made with rice flour and is often served with coconut-based curries.

Local Beverages

Hunsur is known for its refreshing beverages such as coconut water, buttermilk, and sugarcane juice. For those looking to try something stronger, toddy, a local alcoholic beverage made from palm sap, is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Hunsur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hunsur offers a range of accommodation options ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Here are some options to choose from:

  • Windflower Resort: This eco-friendly resort offers luxury cottages amidst lush greenery and is perfect for those who want to unwind and relax.
  • Nisarga Homestay: This homestay offers guests a chance to experience the local culture and cuisine and is situated close to Nagarhole National Park.
  • Sathyam Homestay: This budget-friendly homestay is located in the heart of Hunsur and offers basic amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Hunsur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hunsur is known for its silk sarees, sandalwood products, and handicrafts made by the local artisans. Here are some must-buy items to bring home:

  • Silk Sarees: Hunsur is known for its pure silk sarees, which are handwoven using traditional techniques.
  • Sandalwood Products: Sandalwood is grown abundantly in the region and is used to make various products such as incense sticks, soap, and perfumes.
  • Handicrafts: Hunsur is known for its handicrafts such as wooden toys, leather chappals, and coconut shell products, which are unique and make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hunsur

Hunsur is home to a diverse mix of cultures and religions, with the majority of the population being Hindus. It is important to dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and other religious places. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering a temple.

Best Time to Visit Hunsur

The best time to visit Hunsur is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season from June to October can cause transportation disruptions due to heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hunsur

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Explore the local cuisine
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Take photographs in religious places without permission
  • Litter and pollute the environment
  • Disturb wildlife and their habitats

Safety and Health

Safety in Hunsur

Hunsur is generally a safe destination, but it is important to be cautious and take necessary precautions against petty thefts and scams. It is also important to follow safety guidelines when indulging in adventure activities.

Health in Hunsur

Hunsur has basic health facilities, but it is advisable to carry a first aid kit and necessary medicines when visiting the town. It is also important to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent gastroenteritis.

Getting to Hunsur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hunsur

The nearest airport to Hunsur is the Mysore Airport, which is located 48 km away. The airport is well-connected to major cities like Bangalore, Chennai, and Hyderabad.

Bus Stations Near Hunsur

Hunsur has a well-connected bus service, and buses run frequently to nearby cities like Mysore, Bangalore, and Mangalore.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire from major cities like Bangalore and Mysore.

How to Get Around in Hunsur

Hunsur is a small town, and most of the attractions are located within a radius of 10 km. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are available for hire, and they charge reasonable fares.

Where to Go Next from Hunsur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Madikeri: This is a popular hill station located 63 km away from Hunsur and is known for its coffee plantations and picturesque landscapes.
  • Mysore: This historic city is located 52 km away from Hunsur and is home to numerous palaces, museums, and art galleries.
  • Coorg: This is a popular destination located 97 km from Hunsur and is known for its scenic beauty and coffee plantations.

Conclusion

Hunsur is a hidden gem of Karnataka that is waiting to be explored. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, Hunsur has something for every traveler. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
