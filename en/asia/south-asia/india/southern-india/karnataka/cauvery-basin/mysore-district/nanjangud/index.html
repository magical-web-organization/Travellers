Introduction to Nanjangud

Nanjangud is a small town in the Mysore district of the southern state of Karnataka, India. It is located on the banks of the river Kapila (also known as Kabini), a tributary of the Cauvery River. The town is famous for its ancient temple complexes and scenic views, and is a popular spot for outdoor adventures like hiking and biking. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover or a thrill-seeker, Nanjangud has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Nanjangud

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nanjangud’s rich cultural heritage and religious significance can be seen in its ancient temple complexes and historical places.

Nanjundeshwara Temple

The Nanjundeshwara Temple is the most famous attraction in Nanjangud. The temple is devoted to Lord Shiva and is one of the oldest and biggest temples in southern India. It is believed to have been built by the Ganga dynasty in the 9th century and has since been renovated and expanded by various rulers.

Srikanteshwara Temple

The Srikanteshwara Temple is another famous temple in Nanjangud, dedicated to Lord Shiva’s son, Karthikeya. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture, intricate carvings, and the annual Rath Yatra festival, which attracts thousands of devotees.

Mysuru Palace

The Mysuru Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a beautiful palace located in the nearby city of Mysore. It was the residence of the Wodeyar family, the rulers of Mysore, and is now a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Nanjangud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nanjangud is surrounded by beautiful forests, hills, and rivers, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta

Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta is a beautiful hill located near Nanjangud. The hill is famous for its scenic views and is home to the Gopalaswamy Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Krishna.

Kabini River

The Kabini River is a major river in southern India and is a tributary of the Cauvery River. The river flows through Nanjangud and offers visitors the chance to enjoy boating, fishing, and other water sports.

Bandipur National Park

Bandipur National Park is located nearby Nanjangud and is known for its diverse wildlife, including tigers, elephants, and leopards. The park offers visitors the chance to enjoy jeep safaris and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanjangud

Nanjangud is surrounded by beautiful hills and forests, which make for great hiking and biking trails. Some popular hiking and biking routes in Nanjangud include:

  • The Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta trail to Gopalaswamy Temple
  • The Nanjundeshwara Temple trek
  • The Kallahalli-Kanakatte Trail

Best Things to Do in Nanjangud: Activities and Tours

Nanjangud offers a range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests.

Festivals and Fairs

Nanjangud is known for its many festivals and fairs, including the Rath Yatra at the Srikanteshwara Temple and the annual Car Festival at the Nanjundeshwara Temple.

Adventure Activities

For those looking for some adventure, Nanjangud offers a range of activities, including:

  • Hiking
  • Biking
  • White Water Rafting in Kabini River
  • Jeep safari in Bandipur National Park
  • Paragliding in Nandi Hills

Local Culture and Customs of Nanjangud

Like many other places in India, Nanjangud has a rich and diverse culture. Some local customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering temples and other religious sites.
  • Don’t eat or drink inside temples, and don’t touch or take anything from the altar.
  • Greet people with a traditional “Namaste” and respect elders.

Best Time to Visit Nanjangud

The best time to visit Nanjangud is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and mild. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season, which lasts from June to September, as the heavy rains can cause flooding and landslides.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanjangud

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Carry water and sunscreen when going on outdoor adventures.
  • Negotiate prices when shopping in local markets.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Speak disrespectfully to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanjangud

Nanjangud is generally a safe place for visitors. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night, keeping your valuables safe, and following traffic rules when driving.

Health in Nanjangud

Like the rest of India, healthcare facilities in Nanjangud can be limited, so it’s a good idea to carry a basic medical kit and any necessary medications. It’s also important to drink bottled water, avoid street food, and practice good hygiene to prevent getting sick.

Getting to Nanjangud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nanjangud

The nearest airport to Nanjangud is the Mysore Airport, which is around 20 km away from the town. The airport has frequent flights to major Indian cities like Bangalore, Chennai, and Mumbai.

Bus Stations Near Nanjangud

Nanjangud has a major bus station, which is well-connected to other major cities and towns in Karnataka and neighboring states. There are frequent buses from Bangalore, Mysore, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Private taxis and rental cars are also available for those who want to travel directly to Nanjangud or explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Nanjangud

Nanjangud is a small town, so walking or biking is the best way to explore the town. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can hire a taxi or use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Nanjangud: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Mysore (23 km)
  • Ooty (156 km)
  • Coorg (128 km)
  • Bangalore (166 km)

Conclusion

Nanjangud is a hidden gem in Karnataka, offering a mix of culture, nature, and adventure. From the ancient temple complexes to the stunning natural beauty, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Nanjangud and make memories that will last a lifetime.
