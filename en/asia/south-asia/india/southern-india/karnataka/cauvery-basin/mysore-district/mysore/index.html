Introduction to Mysore

Located in the Cauvery Basin in Karnataka, India, Mysore is a popular tourist destination renowned for its rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and delectable cuisine. The city is also known for its intricate crafts and vibrant shopping scene. Mysore is easily accessible by air, road, and rail, making it a convenient spot for a relaxing vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Mysore

When visiting Mysore, you will be overwhelmed with the sheer number of historical landmarks, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences it has to offer. Here are some must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums & Historical Places

Mysore is home to an array of remarkable museums and historical places, including:

  • Mysore Palace
  • Chamundi Hill and Temple
  • Jaganmohan Palace
  • St. Philomena’s Church

Natural Attractions of Mysore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Mysore has an abundant array of outdoor activities available, such as:

  • Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary
  • Brindavan Gardens
  • Karanji Lake Nature Park
  • Kukkarahalli Lake

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mysore

Mysore is blessed with a pleasant climate throughout the year, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking. Some popular hiking and biking trails include:

  • Balmuri Falls
  • Kunti Betta
  • Melkote Hill
  • Kabini River

Best Things to Do in Mysore: Activities and Tours

Some fun activities and tours to take while in Mysore are:

  • Discover the art of silk weaving by taking a tour of the silk factories in the city
  • Take an ayurvedic spa treatment in the scenic surroundings of Mysore
  • Explore the city on a heritage walk and learn more about Mysore’s incredible history

Week-Long Itinerary for Mysore

If you’re planning a longer stay in Mysore, below is a week-long itinerary that allows you to experience the city’s top spots:

Day 1 Arrival and Leisure Day
Day 2 Mysore Palace and Art Gallery
Day 3 Chamundi Hill and Temple, St. Philomena’s Church, and Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary
Day 4 Lalitha Mahal Palace, Jaganmohan Palace, and Brindavan Gardens
Day 5 Kukkarahalli Lake and Karanji Lake Nature Park
Day 6 Silk Factory Tour, Ayurvedic Spa Treatment, and Shopping around Devaraja Market
Day 7 Departure Day

Food and Drink Scene of Mysore

Mysore’s food scene is diverse and exciting, with a range of flavours and cuisines on offer. Here are a few of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mysore Pak
  • Masala Dosa
  • Puliyogare

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee
  • Masala Chai
  • Tender Coconut Water

Where to Stay in Mysore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mysore has a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some examples:

  • Luxury Hotels: Lalitha Mahal Palace Hotel, Windflower Resort and Spa, and Royal Orchid Metropole Hotel
  • Mid-Range Hotels: Pai Vista Hotel, Regenta Central Herald, and Southern Star Hotel
  • Budget Accommodation: KSTDC Mayura Hoysala, 3 Leaves Hotel, and Hotel Viceroy Comforts

What to Buy in Mysore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mysore is known for its silk and sandalwood offerings. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Mysore Silk Sarees
  • Sandalwood Products
  • Mysore Paintings

Local Culture and Customs of Mysore

Mysore is known for its vibrant culture, traditional customs, and value for sustainable living. Travellers are encouraged to respect local traditions, such as removing footwear before entering a temple and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mysore

The best time to visit Mysore is from October to February, during the winter months where the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mysore

Do:

  • Respect local customs and tradition
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Carry cash as many small vendors in the city do not accept cards

Don’t:

  • Litter the streets or disrespect the environment
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing in public places
  • Hesitate to ask for assistance from locals if you need help

Safety and Health

Safety in Mysore

Mysore is a relatively safe city, but as with any destination, visitors should take measures to ensure their safety, such as avoiding dark and deserted areas at night, hiring private transportation instead of using public transport, and watching out for scams.

Health in Mysore

It is advisable to drink bottled water when in Mysore and avoid tap water. Visitors should also stick to hygienic food outlets and restaurants to prevent illnesses.

Getting to Mysore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mysore

The nearest airport to Mysore is Mandakalli Airport (MYQ), which connects to major cities in India such as Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Chennai.

Bus Stations Near Mysore

Mysore has a well-connected network of bus stations, with buses plying regularly to major towns and cities in Karnataka and neighbouring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Mysore by taking a train to Mysore Junction Railway Station or hiring a private car or taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Mysore

Local transport options in Mysore include autorickshaws, taxis, and hired cars. Visitors can also rely on public buses operated by the Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) and Mysore City Transport Corporation (MCTC).

Where to Go Next from Mysore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mysore is located in a prime position for excursions to several neighbouring destinations:

  • Coorg: for scenic hill stations, coffee plantations and monasteries.
  • Hassan: for the ancient temple ruins of Belur and Halebidu.
  • Bandipur National Park: for wildlife tours and jungle safaris.

Conclusion

Mysore is a city rich in history, culture and natural beauty. With the above travel guide, visitors will have a well-rounded experience in this charming city. From the silk sarees and stunning architecture to the lush greenery and adventurous outdoor activities, Mysore truly is a traveller’s paradise.
