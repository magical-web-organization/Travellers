Krishnarajanagara is a hidden gem located in the southern part of Karnataka, India. It is nestled in the Cauvery Basin, surrounded by lush green forests, misty mountains, and pristine waterfalls. This charming town boasts of rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and a rejuvenating atmosphere. This comprehensive guide will take you on a journey to explore Krishnarajanagara’s best places to visit, things to do, what to eat, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara is a small town located in the Mysore district of Karnataka. It is home to diverse cultures, comprising of various ethnic groups and languages. It has a rich history dating back to the reign of the Wadiyar dynasty. This charming town is named after the deity Lord Krishna, and its natural beauty and historical sites attract travelers from all around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara has a lot to explore that reflects the cultural diversity, historical significance, and natural beauty. Below, we’ve highlighted some of the must-see places that Krishnarajanagara has to offer.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple: This temple has a significant place in the town’s religious history as it’s dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It is known for its splendid architecture built in a Dravidian style.

  • Ranga Mandira: It showcases the art, culture and the history of the Mysore kingdom. Visitors can witness the traditional dance, music, and performance shows that take place here.

  • Fort of Tipu Sultan: The fort is a place of significant historical importance for South-India and echoes the victorious moments of the Mysore dynasty. The fort displays various items belonging to Tipu Sultan, including his sword, clothing, and furniture.

  • Nimishamba Temple: It is dedicated to goddess Parvathi and attracts visitors with its stunning architecture and peaceful surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Krishnarajanagara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brindavan Gardens: A botanical park located in front of the KRS dam, packed with the varieties of plants and trees. It has a musical fountain show every evening that attracts lots of tourists.

  • KRS Dam: The dam is situated across the river Kaveri, and it is one of the most significant dams in India. Visitors can witness the scenic view and take a boat ride around the dam.

  • Shivanasamudra Waterfalls: It has two waterfalls, Barachukki and Gaganchukki, falling from a tall cliff. The beauty of the waterfalls lies in its view and its surroundings making it a perfect spot for trekking and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara has lush green forests and hillsides, which makes it perfect for hiking and biking tours. The Bananthimari State Forest can be a good option for trekking, while the hills around KRS dam are perfect for biking tours.

Best Things to Do in Krishnarajanagara: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safaris: Visit the nearby wildlife sanctuary and enjoy an encounter with majestic animals like Elephants, Bison, Leopards, and Sloth Bears.

  • Ayurvedic Massages and Yoga sessions: Krishnarajanagara is known for its serene environment and tranquil wide landscapes. Visitors can try Ayurveda-based massages and yoga sessions to rejuvenate themselves.

  • Shopping at Local Bazaars: Head to the bazaars and try some of the local sweetmeats, handicrafts, and handloom products, including silk sarees, clothing, and wooden articles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Krishnarajanagara

Day 1-2: Visit Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, Nimishamba Temple, and Fort of Tipu Sultan.

Day 3: Visit KRS Dam, Brindavan Gardens, and take an evening musical fountain show.

Day 4: Go trekking and visit Shivanasamudra Waterfalls.

Day 5: Spend the day at the nearby wildlife sanctuary.

Day 6-7: Enjoy the Ayurvedic massages and yoga sessions.

Food and Drink Scene of Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara offers a unique blend of culinary delights, ranging from spicy curries to sweetmeats. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Krishnarajanagara:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mysore Masala Dosa: A crispy dosa, filled with spicy potato filling, and served with coconut chutney, sambar, and tomato chutney.

  • Bisi Bele Bath: A spicy, tangy rice dish cooked with vegetables, lentils, spices, and tamarind pulp.

  • Mysore Pak: A sweet dessert made with gram flour, ghee, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: A typical South Indian coffee made by filtering the decoction with boiling water.

  • Masala Chai: A flavorful tea infused with spices such as ginger, cardamom, and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Krishnarajanagara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Krishnarajanagara offers various options for accommodations, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • The Green Hotel: A luxurious hotel with a colonial-style architecture, located in a peaceful environment amid the lush green garden.

  • KSTDC Hotel Mayura Kauvery Bhagya: A budget-friendly hotel located near the KRS Dam and offers a stunning view of the Dam.

  • Government Guest House: A historical building constructed during the British era, located close to the town’s main market.

What to Buy in Krishnarajanagara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the souvenirs and local products which visitors can take back home are:

  • Silk sarees and clothing.
  • Handicrafts and handloom products.
  • Sandalwood and teakwood articles.

Local Culture and Customs of Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara has a diverse culture and places a significant emphasis on religious rituals and traditions. Visitors need to respect the customs, traditions of the local community to avoid any inconvenience during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Krishnarajanagara

The best time to visit Krishnarajanagara is from October to February when the weather is pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Krishnarajanagara

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the local community.
  • Try local cuisine and buy local products.
  • Be cautious about the wildlife, insects, and snakes.

Don’t:

  • Avoid visiting during monsoons, especially during peak monsoon season.
  • Do not litter and maintain cleanliness at tourist spots.
  • Do not pluck any plants, flowers or harm the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara is a safe place to visit, however, visitors must be cautious when trekking or hiking in the forest areas. Taking necessary precautions is advisable to avoid any untoward incidents.

Health in Krishnarajanagara

Visitors must carry essentials like mosquito repellent, sunscreen, and required medicines to avoid any unwarranted health issues. Drinking bottled water is essential to prevent water-borne diseases.

Getting to Krishnarajanagara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Krishnarajanagara can be easily accessible by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Krishnarajanagara

Mysore Airport is the nearest airport located at a distance of 55 km from Krishnarajanagara.

Bus Stations Near Krishnarajanagara

Krishnarajanagara has a bus station located in the town, connecting it to other major cities in the state.

Other Options

Cabs and taxis can be hired to reach Krishnarajanagara.

How to Get Around in Krishnarajanagara

Visitors can rent bikes, scooters, or cars to explore the town at their own pace. Local buses are also available, and cabs can be hired to commute to other towns.

Where to Go Next from Krishnarajanagara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions that a visitor can plan to visit include:

  • The Mysore Palace: A magnificent palace located in Mysore city, which was once the residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and is known for its amazing architecture and artwork.

  • Coorg: A picturesque hill station located at a distance of 120 km from Krishnarajanagara.

  • Bandipur National Park: A massive tiger reserve located at a distance of 68 km from Krishnarajanagara.

Conclusion

Krishnarajanagara is a destination for travelers who love to explore the unknown, unravel the cultural history, and immerse themselves in the serene beauty of nature. If you are planning a visit to Southern India, make sure to add Krishnarajanagara to your itinerary and experience the tranquility and charm this town has to offer.
