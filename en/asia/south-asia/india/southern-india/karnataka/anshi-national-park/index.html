Introduction to Anshi National Park

Anshi National Park is a wildlife sanctuary and reserve forest nestled in the Western Ghats of Karnataka, Southern India. The park offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, encompassing a large portion of the Kali Tiger Reserve. The park’s sprawling 340 square kilometers stretches across the districts of Uttara Kannada and Belgaum, and is home to a diverse ecosystem of flora and fauna. Visitors to the park have the chance to explore nature through adventure activities and experience traditional culture through tours and local cuisine. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of Anshi National Park, listing places to visit, activities to experience, and local customs to learn.

List of Places to Visit in Anshi National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The cultural heritage of Anshi National Park is reflected in its architecture and preserved landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit sites:

  • Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary: Located on the outskirts of the park, Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary offers unparalleled natural beauty, with walking trails and scenic viewpoints.
  • Tribal Village: This is the hub of the native tribe living in the forest, and offers visitors a firsthand look at the culture of the local people.
  • Syntheri Rocks: Situated in the Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary, it’s a granite monolith structure surrounded by dense forests and a picturesque view.
  • Supa Dam: On the river Kali, this dam is surrounded by serene forest lands and offers stunning views of the river and the park.
  • Kavala Caves: One of the lesser-known sites in the park, the Kavala Caves are attractive with stalactites and stalagmites that are of archaeological significance.

Natural Attractions of Anshi National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural attractions of Anshi National Park are its beautiful landscapes, exotic wildlife, and diverse flora. Some of the most popular spots include:

  • Kali River: For a thrilling adventure, take a river rafting tour down the Kali river.
  • Trekking Trails: Trekking is an excellent way to explore the park, with several beautiful trails providing scenic views and access to wildlife.
  • Wildlife Photography: Visitors can spot tigers, leopards, black panthers, deer, and a wide range of bird species. Several designated spots for animal sightings are located throughout the park.
  • Magod Falls: Located in the dense forests of Jamboti, Magod Falls is a visual delight and a must-see destination.
  • Jenukallu Gudda: This offbeat destination offers picturesque views from the hilltop and is a perfect destination for trekkers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anshi National Park

Hiking and biking are popular ways to explore Anshi National Park, with several trails offering beautiful views of the park’s natural landscapes. Some of the top trails include:

  • Kavala Caves Trail: A 2.5 km trail that leads to the Kavala Caves and is relatively easy for beginners.
  • Pansoli Trail: A 3-km trail that starts at the northern end of the park and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest.
  • Homestay Trail: A 5-km trek that runs through the Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary, passing through tribal villages and tea plantations.

Best Things to Do in Anshi National Park: Activities and Tours

Explore the park by engaging in several adventure activities and tours.

  • River Rafting: A thrilling activity that takes you down the Kali River amidst challenging rapids.
  • Wildlife Safari: A guided jeep tour through the park where visitors can spot wild animals during their natural habitat.
  • Camping: Spend a night under the stars surrounded by the pristine wilderness of the park.
  • Birdwatching: An ideal activity for birding enthusiasts, the park is home to several species of birds like hornbills, barbets, and flycatchers.
  • Nature Walks: Exploring the forests on foot on guided walks and experience the flora and fauna of the jungle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anshi National Park

A weeklong itinerary to explore Anshi National Park:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Anshi National Park, check in to the hotel and relax.
  • Day 2: Experience the wide range of adventure activities the park has to offer, such as rafting, trekking and nature walks.
  • Day 3: Spend the day exploring Kavala Caves, Supa Dam, or Syntheri Rocks, and learn about the cultural heritage of the area.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at leisure with scenic views of the park.
  • Day 5-6: Enjoy a wildlife safari and see wild animals during their natural habitat.
  • Day 7: Depart from Anshi National Park and head towards Bangalore.

Food and Drink Scene of Anshi National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Anshi National Park is home to a diverse cuisine that incorporates local and traditional flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Bisi Bele Bath: A flavorful one-pot meal made of rice and lentils, cooked with vegetables and spices.
  • Neer Dosa: A thin and light crepe made of rice batter, served with coconut chutney.
  • Akki Roti: A gluten-free roti made with rice flour and is a staple food in the area.
  • Mysore Pak: A dessert made of ghee, sugar, and gram flour, known for its melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Local Beverages

People of Anshi National Park love their coffee! They drink filter coffee, a traditional South Indian style of coffee, and it’s a must-try. People also drink fresh sugarcane juice or coconut water, which is widely available in the park.

Where to Stay in Anshi National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors have a wide range of options for accommodations in Anshi National Park, ranging from budget-friendly guest houses to luxury resorts.

Here are some recommendations:

  • Jungle Lodges and Resorts, Dandeli: Offering numerous accommodations based on budget and preferences, the resort offers several adventure activities like trekking, bird watching, and nature walks.
  • Bison River Resort: This luxury resort located on the banks of the river Kali offers stunning valley and river views.
  • Hornbill River Resort: One of the best jungle resorts situated amongst a large forest area and part of the Uttara Kannada district.

What to Buy in Anshi National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anshi National Park is known for its bamboo and cane products, pottery, and tribal artifacts. Visitors can find several stalls and shops selling these items in the surrounding tribal areas. Besides, people can also buy some spices or coffee from the local market.

Local Culture and Customs of Anshi National Park

People of Anshi National Park have rich cultural traditions, and their way of life is inseparable from their environment. Visitors can experience the culture through various activities:

  • Tribal Dance: A vibrant dance style that reflects the culture of the local tribes.
  • Homestay visits: Visitors can stay with the locals and learn about their customs, traditions, and way of life.
  • Ganesha Chaturthi: This is a significant Hindu festival celebrated in September by the people of Anshi National Park, and people can join in the celebration with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Anshi National Park

The best time to visit Anshi National Park is between November and February, when the weather is pleasant, and chances of seeing wildlife is higher. However, people can also consider visiting during the monsoon season (June to September), when the forest looks lush green, and there are fewer visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anshi National Park

Do:

  • Follow Park Rules: Stay on designated trails and abide by the park’s regulations to ensure guest safety and prevent damage to the environment.
  • Canopy Walk: Experience walking over a canopy 25-30 ft above the ground and explore the wilderness from an aerial view.
  • Wildlife Safari: Take a guided safari and follow your guide’s instructions.

Don’t:

  • Litter: carry your waste and dispose of them in the designated areas.
  • Make noise: Loud noises can disrupt wildlife and impact the natural balance of the forest.
  • Feeding Animals: Do not feed wild animals, and it’s best to maintain a safe distance at all times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anshi National Park

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety when visiting Anshi National Park. Here are some essential tips:

  • Avoid Solo Travel: It’s better to travel in groups and avoid solo travel.
  • Beware of Monkeys: Monkeys can be aggressive and cause harm, so it’s better to avoid them.
  • Carry insect repellent: The park has several insects, and visitors are advised to carry insect repellent and use them when necessary.
  • Wear Proper Clothes: Wear clothes that cover the body as much as possible, and wear shoes designed for hiking to prevent injuries.

Health in Anshi National Park

Visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their health when visiting Anshi National Park. Here are some essential tips:

  • Drink bottled water: Avoid drinking tap water, and it’s better to stick to bottled water.
  • Have a First Aid kit: Visitors are advised to carry a basic first aid kit with them.
  • Carry Medications: If you have any underlying medical condition, it’s advisable to carry medications and inform respective authorities when necessary.

Other Options

The other way to reach Anshi National Park is to take a private cab, with several cab services operating throughout the region.

How to Get Around in Anshi National Park

The park has several options for getting around:

  • Private Cabs: Private cabs are readily available and can be booked for day trips and multi-day tours.
  • Public Transport: Local buses run through the park’s nearest towns and villages.
  • Bicycle Rentals: Bicycle rentals are available for visitors looking to explore the area at their pace.

Where to Go Next from Anshi National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can further explore the rich culture and natural beauty of Southern India by visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Goa: Famous for its beaches, waterfalls, and seafood, Goa is a popular tourist attraction and is just a short drive away from the park.
  • Gokarna: A small town known for its ancient temples, secluded beaches, and hippie culture, Gokarna is a perfect destination for a day trip.
  • Karwar: A beautiful coastal town on the Arabian Sea, Karwar is famous for its seafood, beaches, and stunning views.

Conclusion

Anshi National Park is an ideal destination for anyone looking to explore the natural beauty and culture of Southern India. The park’s diverse flora and fauna, combined with its rich cultural heritage, offer visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. Whether you’re interested in adventure activities like river rafting and trekking, cultural tours, or simply soaking up the atmosphere, Anshi National Park has something for everyone. With this guide, plan your visit, navigate the park, and discover local culture and cuisine to create memories that last a lifetime.
