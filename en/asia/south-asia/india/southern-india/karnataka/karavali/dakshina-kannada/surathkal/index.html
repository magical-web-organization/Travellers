Introduction to Surathkal

Surathkal, a small but rapidly developing town located in the southern part of Karnataka, is known for its pristine beaches, religious landmarks, and breathtaking natural beauty. It is a perfect destination for adventure enthusiasts, beach lovers, and history buffs. As part of the coastal region of Karnataka, Surathkal boasts a rich cultural heritage and a unique blend of traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Surathkal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Surathkal is known for its historical significance and has been a hub for trade and commerce for centuries. The following are the major tourist attractions in Surathkal, each with its distinct cultural and historical significance:

Shabari Ashram

Located at the confluence of the Nandini and the Pavanje rivers, Shabari Ashram has a religious significance for Hindus. It is believed that this is where Lord Rama met Shabari, a devotee of Lord Rama, during his exile. The ashram has a temple dedicated to Lord Rama and an idol of Shabari.

Tagore Park

Named after the famous Bengali poet Rabindranath Tagore, Tagore Park is a serene picnic spot that attracts tourists for its tranquil beauty. The park is home to a statue of the Nobel laureate and has a small lake where boating is available.

Sadashiva Temple

The Sadashiva Temple is a revered place of worship for both Hindus and Jains. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to have been built during the reign of the Kadamba dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Surathkal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surathkal is blessed with natural beauty, including the renowned Arabian Sea and vibrant flora and fauna. The following are the popular natural attractions in Surathkal:

Panambur Beach

Situated just 10 km away from Surathkal, Panambur Beach is a popular spot for adventure sports such as parasailing, jet skiing, and boating. It is also home to the annual International Kite Festival.

Tannirbhavi Beach

Another beautiful beach in Surathkal is Tannirbhavi Beach, which is less crowded and therefore ideal for those seeking solitude. This beach is known for its sparkling white sand and crystal-clear water, and it is an excellent spot for swimming.

Surathkal Lighthouse

Built-in 1972, the Surathkal Lighthouse is the most popular attraction in the area. It offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding area from its top. The lighthouse is open for visitors from 4.30 p.m. to 6.00 p.m.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surathkal

Surathkal offers several exciting hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure enthusiasts. Popular trekking spots around Surathkal include Kudlu Falls Trek, Ballarayanadurga Hill Trek, and Sitanadi Nature Trail. Bike rentals are also available in Surathkal, making it easier for visitors to explore the scenic surroundings at their own pace.

Best Things to Do in Surathkal: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the popular tourist attractions, Surathkal offers several activities that visitors can enjoy, such as:

Water Sports

For visitors who are fond of water sports, Surathkal offers an array of options, including parasailing, jet skiing, and boating.

Sightseeing Tours

Surathkal has some best sightseeing spots that visitors can explore with a guided tour, including Panambur Beach, Tannirbhavi Beach, and Surathkal Lighthouse.

Ayurvedic Massages

For those looking for a more relaxed holiday, Surathkal has several Ayurvedic massage centers that offer rejuvenating and therapeutic massages.

Food and Drink Scene of Surathkal

Must-Try Dishes

The food in Surathkal is a blend of coastal and South Indian cuisine. Some of the dishes that visitors must try are:

Kori Rotti

Kori Rotti is a popular local delicacy made of crispy rice wafers and spicy chicken curry.

Mangalorean Fish Curry

Mangalorean Fish Curry is a spicy fish curry made with coconut milk, tamarind, and a melange of spices that gives it a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

Surathkal offers a few unique beverages that tourists can try, such as:

Neer Dosa

Neer Dosa is a rice pancake that is extremely popular in Surathkal. It is typically served with coconut chutney and vegetarian or non-vegetarian curries.

Tender Coconut Water

Tender Coconut Water is the perfect thirst quencher and most commonly available on the streets of Surathkal.

Where to Stay in Surathkal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Surathkal has several lodging options that cater to different types of travelers. Some popular options are:

Budget Accommodation

Surathkal offers several budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses for travelers on a tight budget. Guesthouses such as Shiva Prasad Guest House, M.T.M Cottage, and Hotel Yadav International offer comfortable and affordable accommodation options.

Mid-range Accommodation

Mid-range accommodation options in Surathkal include Ocean Pearl Hotel, Hotel Ayush International, and Varsha International Hotel.

Luxury Accommodation

Luxury hotels in Surathkal, like Country Inn & Suites By Carlson and The Ocean Pearl, offer state-of-the-art amenities and facilities. They make for a perfect stay for those looking for luxury and comfort.

What to Buy in Surathkal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Surathkal is renowned for its local produce and handicrafts. Some of the unique items that visitors should take home as souvenirs include traditional jewelry, silk sarees, and cashew nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Surathkal

Surathkal has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can get a glimpse of that through the many festivals and customs celebrated here. Some of the popular festivals include Dasara, Deepavali, and Yemmar Utsav, a traditional folk festival.

Best Time to Visit Surathkal

The best time to visit Surathkal is between October to February when the temperature is pleasant, and the weather is dry. The monsoon season is between July to September, and it is not a good time to visit as heavy rains can disrupt travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surathkal

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bargain while shopping at street markets.
  • Take off your shoes before entering religious places.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious places.
  • Engage in public display of affection.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surathkal

Surathkal is a relatively safe place for travelers, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. However, visitors should take the standard precautions such as not venturing to isolated places after dark and practicing street-smartness.

Health in Surathkal

Visitors should carry an insect repellent particularly during the monsoon season, as there is a high risk of Malaria and Dengue. Visitors should also carry their essential medications as the variety of medicines available in Surathkal is limited.

Getting to Surathkal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Surathkal

Surathkal does not have an airport. The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport, which is located 23 km away from Surathkal.

Bus Stations Near Surathkal

Surathkal has its own railway station and is well-connected by the rail route. The nearest bus station to Surathkal is Mangalore KSRTC Bus Stand, which is approximately 21 km away.

Other Options

Another way to get to Surathkal is by hiring a taxi or self-driving. Visitors can also take local buses or autorickshaws to reach their destination within Surathkal.

How to Get Around in Surathkal

Surathkal is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. Visitors can also use public transport such as buses, taxis, and autorickshaws to get around.

Where to Go Next from Surathkal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Udupi

Located 60 km from Surathkal, Udupi is a quaint town that is famous for its ancient Krishna Temple and scenic beaches.

Mangalore

Mangalore is the largest city in the Dakshina Kannada district and is just 16 km away from Surathkal. It is a city that is reputed for its historical significance and has several tourist attractions to offer.

Conclusion

Surathkal is a hidden gem that is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure opportunities.
