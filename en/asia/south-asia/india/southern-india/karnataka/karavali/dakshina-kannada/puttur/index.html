Introduction to Puttur

Nestled in the heart of Karavali, a coastal region of Karnataka renowned for its natural beauty and cultural heritage, Puttur is a charming town that embodies the essence of South India. Its lush green landscapes, bustling marketplaces, and historic monuments make it a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists seeking an authentic travel experience.

In this guide, we will take you on a journey through Puttur’s rich history and culture, explore its natural wonders and outdoor adventures, and give you practical information on where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Puttur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Sri Mahalingeshwara Temple: This ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is a must-visit for those interested in South Indian architecture and religious traditions. The intricate carvings on the walls and pillars are a testament to the skill of the craftsmen who built it centuries ago.

  2. Sri Devi Annapoorneshwari Temple: Another popular place of worship in Puttur, this temple is devoted to the goddess of food and nourishment. The annual Rathotsava festival held here is a major event that attracts thousands of pilgrims from across the state.

  3. Kote Venkataramana Temple: This temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, built in the Dravidian style of architecture, is known for its exquisitely carved stone pillars and ornate ceiling.

  4. Puttur Town Hall: A historic monument built during the British rule, this elegant structure now serves as a cultural center and venue for various events and exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Puttur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kumaradhara River: This picturesque river, which originates from the Western Ghats and flows through Puttur, is a popular spot for picnics, fishing, and boating.

  2. Madhava Kripa Reservoir: Surrounded by verdant hills and forests, this serene reservoir is a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

  3. Puttur Narasimha Hill: A sacred hill believed to be the abode of Lord Narasimha, this site offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for trekking and hiking.

  4. Someshwara Wildlife Sanctuary: Located just a few kilometers from Puttur, this lush forest reserve is home to a variety of fauna, including elephants, tigers, and reptiles. Visitors can go on jeep safaris and nature walks to explore the sanctuary’s diverse ecosystems.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puttur

Puttur and its surrounding areas offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to indulge in their favorite activities. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region:

  1. Bisle Ghat: This scenic mountain pass, located about 50 km from Puttur, is a popular destination for both hikers and bikers. The route passes through dense forests, waterfalls, and expansive grasslands, offering stunning views of the Western Ghats.

  2. Kottigehara Forest Range: This pristine forest reserve, situated about 40 km from Puttur, is a great place to go hiking and wildlife spotting. The trails here are relatively easy and suitable for beginners.

  3. Kadambari Forest Range: Another beautiful forest reserve near Puttur, this area is known for its dense bamboo groves, serene waterfalls, and diverse birdlife.

Best Things to Do in Puttur: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to adventure activities, Puttur has something for everyone. Here are some of the most popular things to do in the town:

  1. Heritage Walks: Puttur is home to many historic buildings and monuments, and a heritage walking tour is a great way to explore them. Join a local guide to learn more about the town’s rich history and culture.

  2. Cooking Classes: South Indian cuisine is famous for its unique flavors and spicy dishes. Take a cooking class in Puttur to learn how to make traditional delicacies such as dosas, idlis, and chutneys.

  3. Jeep Safaris: For an adrenaline-packed adventure, go on a jeep safari through the Someshwara Wildlife Sanctuary. You will get to see some of the rarest and most exotic animals in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puttur

If you plan to spend a week in Puttur, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Puttur and spend the day exploring the town’s historic landmarks and local markets.

Day 2: Visit the Sri Mahalingeshwara Temple and Kote Venkataramana Temple, followed by a trek up the Puttur Narasimha Hill.

Day 3: Explore the natural attractions of Puttur, including the Kumaradhara River and Madhava Kripa Reservoir.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Bisle Ghat and go on a hiking or biking expedition through the Western Ghats.

Day 5: Visit the Someshwara Wildlife Sanctuary and go on a safari to see the region’s diverse fauna.

Day 6: Attend a cooking class and learn how to make some traditional South Indian dishes, followed by a leisurely afternoon exploring the town’s museums and cultural centers.

Day 7: Depart from Puttur or extend your trip to explore other parts of Karavali.

Food and Drink Scene of Puttur

No travel experience is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Puttur has a rich culinary tradition that combines the flavors of South India with influences from neighboring regions. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Biryani: A fragrant rice dish cooked with meat, spices, and herbs.

  2. Neer Dosa: A light and fluffy rice pancake served with coconut chutney and sambar.

  3. Goli Baje: A crispy and savory snack made with maida flour, yogurt, and spices.

  4. Kori Rotti: A spicy dish made with chicken curry and crispy rice wafers.

Local Beverages

  1. Tender Coconut Water: A refreshing and hydrating drink made from the juice of young green coconuts.

  2. Buttermilk: A creamy and tangy drink made from yogurt and spices.

  3. Filter Coffee: A strong and aromatic coffee brewed with a filter that gives it a distinctive taste.

Where to Stay in Puttur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puttur offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From high-end resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay in the town:

  1. Green Retreat: A luxurious resort set amid lush greenery, offering spacious rooms and suites, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant.

  2. Sri Poomale Residency: A mid-range hotel centrally located in Puttur, with clean and comfortable rooms and an in-house restaurant.

  3. Riverdale Homestay: A charming and cozy homestay located near the Kumaradhara River, offering personalized service and a peaceful ambiance.

What to Buy in Puttur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Puttur is known for its handicrafts, traditional textiles, and locally grown spices. Here are some of the best souvenirs and products to buy:

  1. Udupi Sarees: These handwoven sarees made with natural fibers are known for their softness and intricate designs.

  2. Halasina Kaai: A local variety of jackfruit that is used to make pickles, jams, and sweets.

  3. Betel Leaves and Areca Nuts: A staple in South Indian culture, these items are commonly used as a refreshing after-meal snack.

Local Culture and Customs of Puttur

Puttur has a unique cultural identity that is reflected in its festivals, art forms, and religious rituals. The town celebrates many Hindu festivals, including the Rathotsava, Brahmotsava, and Navaratri. Yakshagana, a traditional dance-drama, is a popular art form that depicts mythological tales and folklore.

Etiquette-wise, it is customary to remove your footwear before entering a temple or someone’s home. You should also dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Puttur

The best time to visit Puttur is between October and March when the weather is mild and pleasant. The monsoon season, from June to September, can be quite wet and humid, while the summer months, from April to May, can be uncomfortably hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puttur

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Puttur:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Carry cash as most local shops and vendors do not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Disrespect the local religious beliefs and practices

Safety and Health

Safety in Puttur

Puttur is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, petty thefts and scams can occur, especially in crowded areas and markets. As a precaution, it is advisable to keep your valuables secure and avoid venturing out alone at night.

Health in Puttur

Puttur has a few medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer basic healthcare services. It is recommended to carry a first aid kit and any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Puttur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puttur

The nearest domestic airport to Puttur is Mangalore International Airport, located about 45 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to reach Puttur.

Bus Stations Near Puttur

Puttur has a well-connected bus network that connects it to major cities in the region. The town has two bus stations, Puttur Central Bus Station and Puttur Private Bus Station, from where you can board buses to your destination.

Other Options

You can also reach Puttur by train, as the town has a railway station that is connected to major cities across the country. Taxis and private cabs are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Puttur

Puttur is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are also readily available for hire. Buses and trains are other affordable options for getting around the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Puttur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spare, you can explore the many other attractions in Karavali, such as:

  1. Udupi: A coastal town known for its delicious cuisine and historic temples.

  2. Chikmagalur: A scenic hill station renowned for its coffee plantations, waterfalls, and hill treks.

  3. Gokarna: A beachside paradise that attracts travelers seeking a laid-back vibe and yoga retreats.

Conclusion

Puttur is a hidden gem that offers travelers a glimpse into the vibrant culture and natural beauty of South India. Whether you’re a history buff, adventure enthusiast, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Follow this guide to make the most of your visit and experience the best that Puttur has to offer.
