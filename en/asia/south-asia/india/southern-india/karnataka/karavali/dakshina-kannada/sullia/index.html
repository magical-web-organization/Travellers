Introduction to Sullia

Sullia is a small town located in the Dakshina Kannada district of Karnataka, India. It lies at the foothills of the Western Ghats and is surrounded by lush green forests, scenic mountains, and gushing rivers. Sullia attracts travelers who are looking for a rejuvenating break from the hustle and bustle of city life. It is also a great stop for adventurers who want to explore the natural beauty of Karnataka.

List of Places to Visit in Sullia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sri Annapoorneshwari Temple - Located in Horanadu, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Annapoorneshwari. It is one of the most visited temples in the region and a must-visit for its beautiful architecture and religious significance.

Jain Basadi - This 800-year-old Jain temple is situated in Varadahalli and is renowned for its intricate carvings and unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Sullia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mallikarjuna Temple - This ancient temple is located on top of Mallikarjuna Betta hill and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Subramanya Kshetra - Another popular temple, Subramanya Kshetra is located in Kukke Subramanya and is dedicated to Lord Subramanya. It is surrounded by dense forests and is a perfect place for nature lovers and trekking enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sullia

Sullia to Kukke Subramanya Trek - This trek is about 14 km long and offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and hills. It is ideal for experienced trekkers.

Best Things to Do in Sullia: Activities and Tours

White Water Rafting - The Barapole river is known for its thrilling rapids and is a perfect spot for white water rafting.

Trekking in Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary - This wildlife sanctuary offers a wonderful opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Karnataka. Explore the untouched wilderness of the sanctuary and discover its diverse flora and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Sullia

Must-Try Dishes

Coorgi Chicken - This spicy and flavorful chicken dish is a must-try for non-vegetarian food enthusiasts.

Kadubu - Kadubu is a sweet dish made of rice flour and coconut, and is a popular breakfast item in Sullia.

Local Beverages

Kashaya - Kashaya is a traditional drink made of herbs and spices like ginger, coriander, and pepper, and is believed to have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Sullia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Madhuvan Serai - A luxurious hotel in Sullia, Hotel Madhuvan Serai offers excellent amenities and world-class services at a reasonable price.

Nisarga Homestay - If you’re looking for a more authentic and homely experience, Nisarga Homestay is a great option. It offers traditional food and comfortable accommodation in a serene environment.

What to Buy in Sullia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sandalwood Products - Sullia is famous for its sandalwood products like incense sticks, essential oils, and soap.

Coorgi Spices - Karnataka is known for its aromatic spices, and Sullia is no exception. You can buy freshly ground spices like pepper, cardamom, and cumin from local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Sullia

Sullia has a unique culture and is known for its traditional dance forms like Yakshagana and Bhuta Kola. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and it’s always a good idea to greet them with a smile and a “Namaste”.

Best Time to Visit Sullia

The best time to visit Sullia is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sullia

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and other religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any behavior that might offend the locals.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sullia

Sullia is generally safe for visitors, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions like carrying a copy of your ID and avoiding traveling alone at night.

Health in Sullia

Make sure to carry plenty of water while trekking, and wear comfortable and sturdy shoes. Also, be careful while consuming street food and always opt for healthy and hygienic options.

Getting to Sullia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sullia

The closest airport to Sullia is Mangalore International Airport, which is about 88 km away.

Bus Stations Near Sullia

Sullia has a well-connected bus station with daily buses to major cities like Bangalore, Mangalore, and Mysore.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are readily available from major cities like Mangalore and Bangalore.

How to Get Around in Sullia

Motorbikes and scooters are the most convenient way to get around Sullia, but it’s always a good idea to carry a map or GPS to navigate.

Where to Go Next from Sullia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coorg - Also known as the “Scotland of India”, Coorg is a stunning hill station situated in the Western Ghats, and is only 50 km away from Sullia.

Madikeri - Madikeri is another popular hill station near Sullia and is known for its coffee plantations and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Sullia is a hidden gem in Karnataka, and offers a perfect blend of adventure and relaxation. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, a history buff, or a food lover, Sullia has something for everyone. So pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable trip to this scenic town in Dakshina Kannada.
