Introduction to Barkur

Barkur is a small town in Udupi district, located in the southern part of Karnataka, India. It is a hidden gem with a rich history and cultural heritage. Barkur is surrounded by the Arabian Sea on one side and the Western Ghats on the other side, providing breathtaking views and unique natural features. Barkur is also renowned for its temples and serene atmosphere, making it an ideal place for tourists to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Barkur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Barkur Fort

Barkur Fort is the most prominent historical site in Barkur, and it was built in the 14th century. It is a magnificent structure that once served as the residence of the local rulers.

Ananteshwar Temple

Ananteshwar Temple is a popular place of worship in Barkur, and it is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is very old and has beautiful architecture, attracting devotees and tourists alike.

Natural Attractions of Barkur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Koodlu Theertha Falls

Koodlu Theertha Falls is a stunning waterfall located in the Western Ghats. It is a perfect place to visit for people who like to be surrounded by nature and seek outdoor adventures.

Kodi Beach

Kodi Beach is a serene beach famous for its golden sand and crystal clear water. The beach is surrounded by lush green trees and is an excellent spot for relaxing and rejuvenating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barkur

The Western Ghats offers several trails for hikers and bikers to explore the natural beauty of Barkur. Trekking to the Kudlu Theertha Falls is a popular hiking trail, which takes about 2 hours to reach the waterfall. Biking to Kodi Beach is another fun adventure activity that’s popular among tourists.

Best Things to Do in Barkur: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Barkur can enjoy various activities including boating, fishing, and swimming. Boat rides to the nearby islands are a popular tourist activity, and local guides can take visitors to places inaccessible by vehicle. Guided tours of the town and its attractions are also available, providing visitors with an in-depth understanding of the history and culture of Barkur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barkur

Day 1: Explore the Ananteshwar Temple Day 2: Visit the Barkur Fort Day 3: Take a bike ride to Kodi Beach Day 4: Go trekking to Koodlu Theertha Falls Day 5: Explore the local markets and shops Day 6: Take a boat ride to the nearby islands Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate on the Kodi Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Barkur

Must-Try Dishes

Barkur’s cuisine is diverse and offers unique dishes to try. A few must-try dishes are:

  • Goli Baje
  • Neer Dosa
  • Mangalore Buns
  • Chicken Ghee Roast
  • Fish curry

Local Beverages

The most common beverages in Barkur are tea, coffee, and coconut water. Additionally, visitors can try a variety of fruit juices and traditional drinks like Sol Kadhi.

Where to Stay in Barkur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodations options in Barkur for visitors, catering to all budgets. The most popular accommodations in town are guesthouses, homestays, and budget hotels. These accommodations offer visitors an authentic local experience and a chance to interact with the locals.

What to Buy in Barkur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Barkur is famous for its silk sarees, handloom products, and handicrafts. Visitors can also purchase local spices, coffee, and cashew nuts. For souvenir hunters, stores in Barkur offer a variety of souvenir items such as keychains, postcards, and fridge magnets.

Local Culture and Customs of Barkur

Barkur has a rich cultural heritage that includes traditional dances, music, and art. The predominant religion in Barkur is Hinduism, and the town has many temples dedicated to Hindu deities. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs and dress appropriately while visiting the temples. It’s also a general custom to remove shoes while entering temples and homes.

Best Time to Visit Barkur

The best time to visit Barkur is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities. The monsoon season in Barkur is between June and September, and tourists may face difficulties due to excessive rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barkur

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting local temples and religious sites
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Interact with the locals and learn about their traditions and lifestyle

Don’t:

  • Eat non-vegetarian food in public places
  • Litter around the town or on the beaches
  • Take photographs without obtaining permission from locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Barkur

Barkur is a safe town for tourists and has a low crime rate. However, visitors should take precautions to safeguard their belongings and be alert while walking in isolated areas at night.

Health in Barkur

Visitors should carry their medicines and consult their doctors before visiting Barkur. It is also recommended to drink bottled water to prevent waterborne diseases.

Getting to Barkur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barkur

The nearest airport to Barkur is the Mangalore International Airport (62 km). Flights from major cities like Bangalore, Mumbai, and Delhi operate from this airport.

Bus Stations Near Barkur

Buses connect Barkur with other major cities and towns in Karnataka. The Barkur Bus Station is located in the center of the town and has regular bus services to Udupi and Mangalore.

Other Options

Tourists can also take private taxis or rental cars to travel to Barkur.

How to Get Around in Barkur

Barkur is a small town, and walking is an ideal way to explore the town. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are also available for those who do not wish to walk.

Where to Go Next from Barkur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barkur is close to several popular tourist destinations and excursions including:

  • Udupi
  • Mangalore
  • Manipal
  • Agumbe

Conclusion

Barkur is an unexplored destination with immense natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and serenity. Its numerous temples, historical sites, and breathtaking natural features attract tourists from all over the world. Plan your trip to Barkur and explore the hidden gem of southern India.
