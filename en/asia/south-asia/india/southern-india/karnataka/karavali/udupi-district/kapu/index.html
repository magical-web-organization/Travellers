Introduction to Kapu

Kapu is a town located in Udupi district of Karnataka state in Southern India. Also known as Kaup, it is a serene coastal town nestled between Udupi and Mangalore. Kapu is known for its unspoiled beaches, lighthouses, temples, and picturesque landscapes. The town has a rich cultural heritage and is also famous for its local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Kapu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kaup Beach: A picturesque beach and one of the main attractions of Kapu. The beach is known for its serene beauty and stunning sunsets. It is also the home to a 100ft tall lighthouse, which offers spectacular views of the Arabian Sea.

  2. Kaup Beach Road: A scenic drive along the Kaup Beach road offers stunning views of the sea, rustic homes, and coconut trees lined up along the road.

  3. Anegudde Vinayaka Temple: A historic Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha. The temple is located in the village of Kumbashi and is believed to be one of the seven sacred murtis of Lord Ganesha in Udupi.

  4. St. Mary’s Island: A scenic island located off the Malpe coast, St. Mary’s Island is known for its unique hexagonal shaped basaltic rocks. It is also a popular place to relax on the beach, take a dip in the sea, or indulge in water sports.

  5. Udupi Sri Krishna Matha: A famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple is one of the most important pilgrim centers in South India and attracts thousands of devotees every year.

Natural Attractions of Kapu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Malpe Beach: One of the most popular beaches in Udupi, Malpe Beach is known for its golden sand, clear blue water, and scenic beauty. It is also a popular spot for water sports and boat rides.

  2. Manipal End Point: A scenic point located in Manipal, overlooking the valley below. It is a popular destination for trekking, picnics, and nature walks.

  3. Gomateshwara Statue: A magnificent 42 ft tall statue situated on a hill in Karkala town. The statue is considered one of the tallest Gomateshwara statues in the world and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  4. Kudlu Falls: A beautiful waterfall located in a dense forest near Hebri. The falls are located in a serene, tranquil setting and are an ideal place to relax and unwind.

  5. Agumbe: Known as the “Cherrapunji of South India,” Agumbe is a picturesque village located in Shimoga district. It is famous for its stunning sunsets, trekking trails, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapu

  1. Kapu Beach to Kodi Bengre Beach: A scenic hiking trail along the beach, which offers spectacular views of the sea and fishing villages.

  2. Manipal End Point Trek: A scenic trek through the lush, green forests surrounding Manipal. The trek offers stunning views of the valley below and is an ideal place for nature lovers.

  3. Kudlu Falls Trek: A moderate trek through a dense forest, leading to the stunning Kudlu Falls. The trail offers scenic views of the forest, and the waterfall is a perfect spot to relax and unwind in the lap of nature.

Best Things to Do in Kapu: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat Ride at Malpe Beach: Take a boat ride from Malpe Beach to St. Mary’s Island and marvel at the unique hexagonal basaltic rock formations.

  2. Wildlife Safari at Agumbe: Go on a wildlife safari at the Agumbe Rainforest Research Station and witness the rich biodiversity of the Western Ghats.

  3. Cycling Tour in Kapu: Explore the town of Kapu on two wheels with a cycling tour. The tour takes you through the beautiful countryside, fishing villages, and beachside roads.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Neer Dosa: A typical Udupi breakfast dish made with rice flour and coconut milk. It is usually served with coconut chutney or sambar.

  2. Kori Rotti: A Mangalorean delicacy made with crispy flatbread and spicy chicken curry. It is a must-try dish for all non-vegetarian food lovers.

  3. Goli Baje: A popular snack in Udupi, Goli Baje is a crispy, fluffy, fried snack made with maida and curd. It is usually served with coconut chutney.

Local Beverages

  1. Palm Wine: A popular local beverage made from fermented palm tree sap. It is a popular drink amongst the locals.

  2. Kashaya: A herbal tea made with spices, jaggery, and ginger. It is a popular drink and is believed to have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Kapu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Paradise Isle Beach Resort: A luxury beach resort located near Malpe Beach. It offers modern amenities, private beach access, and stunning sea views.

  2. Maui Mermaid Beach Resort: A budget-friendly beach resort located near Kaup Beach. It offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and direct beach access.

  3. Sea View Beach House: A traditional homestay located near Kodi Bengre Beach. It offers a homely atmosphere, fresh seafood, and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Kapu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Udupi Sarees: A traditional Udupi saree is famous for its vibrant colors and intricate designs. It is a perfect souvenir to take back home.

  2. Cashew Nuts: The coastal region of Karnataka is known for its cashew nut production. Locally sourced cashew nuts make for an ideal gift to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kapu

Kapu is a coastal town with a rich cultural heritage, influenced by various religions and ethnic groups. The people of Kapu are known for their hospitality and friendliness. The local cuisine is a blend of South Indian, Mangalorean, and Udupi cuisine. The town celebrates various festivals throughout the year, including Navaratri, Diwali, and Christmas.

Best Time to Visit Kapu

The best time to visit Kapu is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season from June to September should be avoided as the weather can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kapu

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately while visiting temples and other religious sites.

  2. Sample the local cuisine and try street food.

  3. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Consume alcohol in public places.

  2. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  3. Litter the beaches and other public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kapu

Kapu is a safe town for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take basic precautions like not carrying too much cash and valuables, avoiding secluded areas at night, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kapu

Kapu has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in and around the town. It is advisable to carry basic medications and insect repellents while traveling.

Getting to Kapu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kapu

The nearest airport to Kapu is the Mangalore International Airport, which is located about 40 km from the town. It has several domestic and international connections.

Bus Stations Near Kapu

Kapu is well-connected to major cities and towns in Karnataka through the state-run bus services. The nearest bus station to Kapu is Udupi, which is located about 13.5 km away.

Other Options

Private taxis and rental cars are also available to travel to Kapu. However, it is advisable to book these services from authorized providers.

How to Get Around in Kapu

The best way to get around in Kapu is by autorickshaws, which are easily available all around the town. Taxis and rental cars are also available for longer journeys and sightseeing.

Where to Go Next from Kapu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mangalore: Known as the “Gateway to Karnataka,” Mangalore is a bustling port city located about 60 km away from Kapu. It is known for its beaches, temples, and seafood.

  2. Agumbe: Known for its scenic beauty, trekking trails, and waterfalls, Agumbe is a picturesque village located about 69 km away from Kapu.

Conclusion

Kapu is a beautiful coastal town that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine.
