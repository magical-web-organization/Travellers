Introduction to Bankapura

Bankapura is a small town located in the Haveri district of Northwestern Karnataka in southern India. The town is known for its rich history, culture, and scenic beauty, making it an ideal destination for tourists seeking to explore the off-beaten-path. It is a peaceful destination that offers a glimpse into rural life in India, coupled with a few hidden gems of nature and architecture.

This travel guide is full of information on the best things to see and do in Bankapura, as well as helpful tips for planning your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Bankapura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As a town steeped in history, Bankapura boasts numerous historical landmarks and monuments that serve as a reminder of its rich past. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit:

  1. Bankapur Fort – Built-in 1625, the fort is a magnificent structure made of red laterite stone. Its unique architecture and design draw inspiration from various styles, including Jain and Hindu.

  2. Bankapur Peacock Sanctuary – This sanctuary is named after the Indian peafowl, which is found in abundance here. It is a great place to observe these beautiful birds in their natural habitat.

  3. Kamala Narayana Temple – This ancient temple is located in the middle of a large pond and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple is known for its unique architecture, intricate carvings, and expansive premises.

  4. Hukkeri Math – This historic monastery is the only place of its kind in Bankapura, dating back to the 17th century. The Math is an essential center of learning and knowledge on Hindu philosophy and religion.

Natural Attractions of Bankapura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bankapura is an excellent place for nature lovers, offering many scenic spots with stunning views that you will not find elsewhere. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  1. Shalmali River – One of the most serene and picturesque spots in Bankapura, Shalmali is an ideal spot for a picnic, where you can sit back and listen to the rushing waters.

  2. Kolageri Hill – This is an ideal place for hiking enthusiasts, known for its rocky terrain and steep ascents that lead to panoramic views from the top. It’s the perfect place for an outdoor adventure.

  3. Benne Hole Falls – Hidden in a small village, these striking cascades are worth the effort it takes to find them. The falls drop over three levels and offer an ideal spot for taking pictures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bankapura

Bankapura has a lot to offer when it comes to outdoor adventures. From scenic hikes to rugged mountain biking trails, this town has something for everyone, regardless of fitness level.

  1. Bankapur Forest Area – The Bankapur forest area, located near the town, offers a unique hiking experience. Here, you’ll get to enjoy the lush greenery and various wildlife such as peacocks, rabbits, and a variety of birds.

  2. Sirur Trek – At Sirur Hill, you can take a winding trail that leads to breathtaking views of the landscape. It is an ideal place to hike with friends and family.

Best Things to Do in Bankapura: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Bankapura, whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or interested in offbeat adventures.

  1. Heritage Walk – Go on a fascinating walk, and learn about the history and culture of Bankapura from an expert guide who will share information about the unique architecture and stories of the past.

  2. Peacock Safari – Go on a thrilling peacock safari at the Bankapura Peacock Sanctuary, where you can observe these gorgeous birds up-close and learn more about their habitat and lifestyle.

Here is a sample, week-long itinerary for exploring Bankapura:

Day 1 – Arrive in Bankapura, check into your accommodation followed by an evening stroll around the town Day 2 – Visit the Kamala Narayana Temple and explore the Banakapura Fort in depth. Day 3 – Take a trip to the Shalmali River and attend a one of a kind Heritage walk to learn about Bankapura’s rich history. Day 4 – Spend the day hiking on Kolageri Hill Day 5 – Take a Peacock Safari and explore the Banakapura Matha. Day 6 – Check out the Benne Hole Falls, and wind down with a drink at a local bar and grille. Day 7 – Wrap up your visit with some souvenir shopping and depart Bankapura

Food and Drink Scene of Bankapura

The cuisine of Bankapura is an interesting mix of South Indian and North Karnataka flavors, with dishes made from fresh vegetables and aromatic spices. Here are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Jolada Rotti – This gluten-free flatbread made out of millet flour is a staple food in Bankapura.
  2. Bisi Bele Bath – A delicious rice-based dish that features vegetables, lentils, and aromatic spices.
  3. Girmit – A savory snack made by mixing puffed rice with spices and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Mango lassi – A popular drink made with fresh mango puree, yogurt, sugar, and spices.
  2. Belgaum Kunda – A delightful dessert made with milk, sugar, and saffron.

What to Buy in Bankapura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bankapura offers a range of unique souvenirs to take home to your loved ones, such as:

  1. Banakapuri cotton sarees – Women’s clothing made from local cotton yarn and known for their durability and quality.
  2. Sandur Lambani Embroidery – A unique style of embroidery that dates back to the 14th century.

Local Culture and Customs of Bankapura

Bankapura is a beautiful amalgamation of different cultures and traditions, and the locals in this town are friendly and hospitable. While respecting local beliefs and customs, visitors are encouraged to engage, interact with, and learn from the town’s residents.

Best Time to Visit Bankapura

The best time to visit Bankapura is during winter (November to February) when the town’s temperatures are moderate, and the weather is pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bankapura

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs, traditions, and dress code.
  2. Pack appropriate clothing that covers your shoulders and knees.
  3. Carry enough cash, as ATMs are scarce in Bankapura.

Don’t:

  1. Take photos of people or places without permission.
  2. Enter holy places with footwear.
  3. Disrespect the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bankapura

Bankapura is a relatively safe town, but it’s always wise to take precautions to ensure your safety.

  1. Stick to well-lit streets and built-up areas when traveling at night.
  2. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items with you.
  3. Contact the authorities in case of any emergency.

Health in Bankapura

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions while traveling to Bankapura, including carrying a first aid kit and using mosquito repellent.

Getting to Bankapura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bankapura is well connected to major cities by road.

Airports Near Bankapura

The nearest airport to Bankapura is the Hubli Airport, which is about 91 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bankapura

Bankapura has a well-connected bus network, with several buses running to and from different towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and cars can be hired to reach Bankapura from other major cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Bankapura

The best way to explore the town is by walking, biking or renting a car to visit nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Bankapura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Unkal Lake – A beautiful lake close to the city of Hubli, known for its scenic beauty.
  2. Yana Caves – Natural limestone caves with an imposing, 120-meter high black granite hillock around it.

Conclusion

A trip to Bankapura is a perfect getaway for those in search of natural beauty, authentic food, rich history, and warm hospitality. With its serene ambiance, unique cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures, Bankapura offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of modern life, making it one of Karnataka’s hidden gems. Plan your trip and have a memorable experience in Bankapura.
