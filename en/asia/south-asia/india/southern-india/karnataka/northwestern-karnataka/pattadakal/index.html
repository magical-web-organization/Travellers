Introduction to Pattadakal

Pattadakal, located in Northwestern Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its exquisite temple architecture. It served as the capital of the Chalukya dynasty from the 6th to 8th centuries and is home to several ancient temples built in Dravidian and Nagara styles of architecture. Pattadakal is also revered for its picturesque landscape that features several hills, waterfalls and green valleys.

List of Places to Visit in Pattadakal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pattadakal Group of Monuments: The main attraction of Pattadakal, this group of ten temples houses remarkable examples of Dravidian and Nagara architecture.
  • Virupaksha Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Sangameshwara Temple: Located on the banks of the Malaprabha River, this temple is an excellent example of Chalukyan architecture.
  • Jain Temple: Showcasing exceptional craftsmanship, this temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Pattadakal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aihole: A short drive from Pattadakal, Aihole is an ancient settlement with over 125 temples of various shapes and sizes.
  • Badami Caves: Located roughly 22 kilometers away from Pattadakal, these caves are famous for their intricate carvings that depict Hindu mythology.
  • Banashankari Temple: Situated amidst lush greenery, this temple is known for its tranquil atmosphere and serene surroundings.
  • Tungabhadra Dam: An ideal spot for picnics, this dam offers great views of the surrounding hills and the Tungabhadra River flowing downstream.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pattadakal

  • Pattadakal to Aihole Trail: A 10-kilometer hike through the countryside, this trail offers excellent views of the temples and the surrounding villages.
  • Malaprabha River Trail: This 18-kilometer hike offers breathtaking views of the Malaprabha River and the lush green valley.
  • Pattadakal to Badami Trail: A 22-kilometer hike through the hills offers picturesque views of the Badami Caves and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Pattadakal: Activities and Tours

  • Chalukya Temple Tour: A guided tour of the Pattadakal temples offers a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region.
  • Jeep Safari: An adventurous jeep safari through the hills is a great way to experience the region’s unique landscape.
  • River Rafting: The Malaprabha River offers excellent rapids, making it a popular spot for adventurous river rafting.

Food and Drink Scene of Pattadakal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jaggery Holige: A sweet flatbread filled with jaggery, coconut and cardamom.
  • Maddur Vada: A spicy fritter made from chana dal and served with coconut chutney.
  • Mysore Pak: A dessert made from ghee, sugar and gram flour.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: A strong, sweet coffee that is a staple in Karnataka.
  • Kokum Sherbet: A refreshing drink made from kokum fruit, sugar and water.

Where to Stay in Pattadakal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Badami Court: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a rooftop restaurant with great views of the surrounding landscape.
  • KSTDC Mayura Chalukya: Located near the Pattadakal Group of Monuments, this hotel is ideal for those looking to explore the area.
  • Rajsangam International Hotel: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Pattadakal.

What to Buy in Pattadakal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sandalwood Products: Pattadakal is known for its high-quality sandalwood, which is used to make everything from incense sticks to handicrafts.
  • Ilkal Sarees: These traditional sarees are made from cotton and are known for their vibrant colors and intricate designs.
  • Handicrafts: Pattadakal is home to several artisans who make beautiful handicrafts from wood, bamboo, and cane.

Local Culture and Customs of Pattadakal

Pattadakal is a deeply religious place, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Pattadakal

The best time to visit Pattadakal is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pattadakal

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be open to new experiences.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the religious practices at temples and other places of worship.
  • Litter in the surrounding areas.
  • Buy or use illegal substances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pattadakal

Pattadakal is a safe place for travelers, and instances of crime are rare. However, it is advisable to take precautions and not venture out alone late at night.

Health in Pattadakal

Visitors are advised to carry mosquito repellents, as Pattadakal is in a tropical region and sees a lot of mosquitoes. It is also important to stay hydrated and carry a first-aid kit.

Getting to Pattadakal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pattadakal

The nearest airport to Pattadakal is the Belgaum Airport, roughly 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Pattadakal

Pattadakal is well-connected by bus to several major cities in Karnataka, including Badami, Aihole, and Hubli.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis or rent cars to travel to Pattadakal from nearby cities or towns.

How to Get Around in Pattadakal

Visitors can get around Pattadakal on foot, by bicycle, or by hiring a local taxi.

Where to Go Next from Pattadakal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hampi: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located roughly 140 kilometers away, famous for its ancient ruins and temples.
  • Goa: The famous beach town is just over 200 kilometers away, and is popular for its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife.
  • Gokarna: A popular pilgrimage destination, Gokarna is located roughly 240 kilometers from Pattadakal and is known for its pristine beaches and beautiful temples.

Conclusion

Overall, Pattadakal is a must-visit destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure enthusiasts. With its remarkable temples, beautiful landscape, and unique culture, it is sure to leave a lasting impression on every visitor.
