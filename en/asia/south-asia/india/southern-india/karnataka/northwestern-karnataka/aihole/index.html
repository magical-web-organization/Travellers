Introduction to Aihole

Located in the state of Karnataka, in Southern India, Aihole is a small village known for its rich historical and cultural heritage. It was the first capital of the Chalukya dynasty and served as an important religious, cultural, and political center between the 4th and 12th centuries. It is home to more than 100 ancient temples, with a mix of Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist styles.

Aihole is a great place to explore for history buffs, culture enthusiasts, and those who love outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. As you explore this fascinating place, you’ll discover ancient monuments, natural attractions, and hidden gems that will leave you awe-inspired.

List of Places to Visit in Aihole

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Durga Temple: Built in the 7th century, it is one of the oldest temples in Aihole and is dedicated to Goddess Durga. Its unique architecture and intricate carvings on the walls and pillars showcase the skilled craftsmanship of the Chalukya dynasty.
  • Lad Khan Temple: Considered to be one of the most beautiful temples in Aihole, the Lad Khan temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is adorned with exquisite carvings of deities, animals, and mythical creatures.
  • Ravana Phadi Cave Temple: This cave temple dates back to the 6th century and is one of the most unique temples in Aihole. It has intricate carvings of Lord Shiva, Goddess Parvati, and Lord Vishnu, and is believed to have been a meditation chamber for sages.
  • Dattatreya Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, Lord Shiva, and Lord Brahma. It has a unique design with 24 pillars that represent the 24 incarnations of Lord Vishnu.
  • Megalithic Burial Site: The Megalithic burial site in Aihole is a collection of prehistoric burial mounds that date back to the 3rd century BCE. It offers a glimpse of the early human settlement in the region and showcases the unique burial rituals of the period.

Natural Attractions of Aihole: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gowda Hill: This picturesque hill offers a panoramic view of the entire village and is a popular spot for trekking and birdwatching.
  • Badami Cave Temples: Located just 10 km from Aihole, the Badami Cave Temples are a group of four rock-cut cave temples that date back to the 6th century. They are renowned for their exquisite carvings and intricate sculptures.
  • Malaprabha Dam: Located just 5 km from Aihole, the Malaprabha Dam is a great spot for fishing, boating, and picnics. The scenic beauty of the dam and the surrounding hills attracts tourists from all over India.
  • Kudala Sangama: Located just 20 km from Aihole, Kudala Sangama is a popular pilgrimage spot that is situated at the confluence of the rivers Krishna and Malaprabha. It is surrounded by lush green forests and is a great spot for trekking and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aihole

  • Aihole to Badami Trail: This scenic trail takes you from Aihole to Badami through the hilly terrain of the region. It offers stunning views of the lush green forests, rocky hills, and ancient temples.
  • Gowda Hill Trek: The Gowda Hill trek is a popular trek that takes you to the top of Gowda Hill. It offers a panoramic view of Aihole village and the surrounding hills.
  • Bhutanatha Temple Trek: The Bhutanatha Temple trek takes you through the scenic forests and hills of Aihole, and ends at the Bhutanatha Temple. The temple is located on the banks of the Malaprabha River and is a great spot for picnics and relaxation.

Best Things to Do in Aihole: Activities and Tours

  • Temple Tour: Explore the ancient temples of Aihole with a guided tour that will take you to the most important temples in the region. Learn about the history, culture, and the unique architectural style of the temples.
  • Bike Tour: Rent a bike and explore the villages, temples, and scenic trails of Aihole at your own pace. Stop at local shops and eateries to taste the local food and interact with the locals.
  • Fishing Tour: Go fishing at the Malaprabha Dam and enjoy a peaceful day surrounded by nature. You can rent fishing equipment and boats from local vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Aihole

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jolada Roti: A traditional North Karnataka roti made from jowar flour that is usually eaten with curries and chutneys.
  • Bisi Bele Bath: A tasty rice dish made with vegetables, lentils, and spices that is usually served with chips and raita.
  • Holige: A sweet flatbread stuffed with jaggery, coconut, and cardamom that is a popular dessert in Karnataka.

Local Beverages

  • Kashaya: A traditional South Indian spicy, herbal tea that is infused with ginger, cardamom, and other spices.
  • Majjige: A refreshing buttermilk drink that is usually served with meals.
  • Nannari Sharbat: A cooling summer drink made from nannari roots that have cooling properties.

  • Badami Clarks Inn: A mid-range hotel located just 10 km from Aihole village. It offers air-conditioned rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.
  • KSTDC Hotel Mayura Chalukya: A popular hotel located just 3 km from Aihole village. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Aihole: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Channapatna Toys: These handmade wooden toys are a famous product of Karnataka and are popular souvenirs among tourists.
  • Ilkal Sarees: These colorful sarees are made of cotton and are available in a variety of designs and patterns.
  • Banana Fiber Crafts: These eco-friendly crafts are made from banana fiber and include items such as coasters, baskets, and bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Aihole

The people of Aihole are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. The local culture is deeply influenced by the Chalukya dynasty, and most of the festivals and rituals celebrated in Aihole are related to the ancient temples.

Best Time to Visit Aihole

The best time to visit Aihole is between the months of October and March when the weather is pleasant and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aihole

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting the temples.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or pollute the environment.
  • Do not take photographs of the locals without their permission.
  • Do not damage the ancient temples or monuments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aihole

Aihole is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and avoid venturing out at night.

Health in Aihole

It is advisable to carry insect repellents and medication for common ailments while visiting Aihole. Make sure to drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent any health issues.

Getting to Aihole: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aihole

The nearest airport to Aihole is the Belgaum Airport, which is located around 150 km away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Aihole.

Bus Stations Near Aihole

Aihole is well-connected by road to major cities in Karnataka. The nearest bus station is in Badami, which is located just 12 km away. You can take a bus or hire a taxi to reach Aihole from Badami.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or rent a car to drive to Aihole from other nearby cities such as Hubli, Bangalore, and Pune.

How to Get Around Aihole

The best way to explore Aihole is on foot or by renting a bike. You can also hire a taxi or local auto-rickshaw to travel around the village and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Aihole: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Badami: Visit the famous Badami Cave Temples and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region.
  • Pattadakal: Explore the UNESCO World Heritage site of Pattadakal and learn about the rich cultural heritage of Karnataka.
  • Hampi: Visit the famous archaeological site of Hampi and discover the ancient ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Conclusion

Aihole is a hidden gem of Karnataka that is waiting to be explored. From its ancient temples and natural attractions to its rich culture and delicious cuisine, Aihole has something to offer for every type of traveler. Plan your trip to Aihole today and discover the secrets of this fascinating village.
