Introduction to Hubli–Dharwad

Hubli–Dharwad is a city and the second-largest urban agglomeration in the state of Karnataka, India. The city is situated on the banks of the Mahadayi River in the Northwestern part of Karnataka. The twin cities are also known as the “Manchester of Karnataka” due to their roles in the cotton and textile industry. The city has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore a wide range of attractions, including historical sites, museums, natural parks, and religious places.

List of Places to Visit in Hubli–Dharwad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chandramouleshwara Temple: This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and features intricate carvings and sculptures, making it a significant cultural landmark.

  • Nrupatunga Betta Hill: This scenic hilltop offers a panoramic view of the city and is a great spot for a picnic.

  • Unakal Lake: This serene lake is surrounded by lush greenery and offers boating and other water sports activities.

  • Shri Siddharoodha Math: This temple is dedicated to the great saint Siddharoodha Swami and is visited by numerous devotees from around the world.

  • Indira Gandhi Glass House: A famous exhibition center in Hubli where various exhibitions, fairs, and expos are frequently held.

Natural Attractions of Hubli–Dharwad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sykes Point: This beautiful viewpoint offers breathtaking views of the Western Ghats and the surrounding valley.

  • Nuggikeri Fort: The ancient Fort dates back to the 16th century and is now a popular tourist attraction due to its architectural and historical significance.

  • Bhavanishankar Temple: This 18th-century temple has a beautiful architecture and is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu.

  • Shantinath Basadi Jain Temple: This Jain temple has beautiful architecture and is dedicated to Lord Shantinath.

  • Babanagar Lake: A popular picnic spot for locals with boating and natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hubli–Dharwad

  • Sykes Point Hiking Trail: The trail leads to the beautiful Sykes Point and offers stunning views of the Western Ghats.

  • Unakal Hill Hiking Trail: A moderately challenging hiking trail that leads to a hilltop that offers panoramic views of the Unaka Lake.

  • Bhairidevarakoppa Dam Biking Trail: A scenic biking trail that follows the Bhairidevarakoppa Dam and offers amazing views of the surrounding valley.

Best Things to Do in Hubli–Dharwad: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Museums: Explore the history and culture of Hubli-Dharwad by visiting museums like the City Central Museum and the Railway Museum.

  • Take a heritage walk: Take a walk through the twin cities and explore the historical and cultural significance of various landmarks like the Hubbali Fort, Nrupatunga Betta Hill, and others.

  • Visit the Temples: Visit the various ancient and culturally significant temples in Hubli-Dharwad, such as the Siddharoodha Swami Temple, Chandramouleshwara Temple, and many others.

  • Explore the Food Scene: Hubli-Dharwad is known for its sumptuous and mouth-watering cuisine, such as Jolada rotti, Pithla Bhakri, Kunda, and Kachori.

  • Take a boat ride on Unakal Lake: Enjoy the scenic beauty of Unakal Lake by taking a boat ride or indulging in other water sports activities.

  • Visit the Local Markets: Explore the vibrant and bustling local markets of Hubli-Dharwad and indulge in retail therapy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hubli–Dharwad

Day 1-2: Explore the historical landmarks of Hubli-Dharwad, like the Chandramouleshwara Temple, Siddharoodha Swami Temple, and the City Central Museum.

Day 3-4: Take a bike ride across the Bhairidevarakoppa Dam Biking Trail and Sykes Point Hiking Trail.

Day 5-6: Visit the natural wonders of the city, like Unakal Lake, Nrupatunga Betta Hill, and the Babanagar Lake.

Day 7: Enjoy the local food and shopping scene of Hubli-Dharwad.

Food and Drink Scene of Hubli–Dharwad

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jolada rotti: A traditional flat bread native to Karnataka and made from sorghum flour.

  • Pithla Bhakri: A famous vegetarian Maharashtrian dish that consists of Gram flour cooked with onions, chilies, and spices.

  • Kunda: A sweet milk dessert that is readily available in the local markets.

  • Kachori: A deep-fried pastry stuffed with a spicy mix of ground peas mixed with other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Badam Milk: A popular drink made from milk and almond paste, garnished with pistachios and saffron.

  • Tea and Coffee: Karnataka’s landscape allows for optimal coffee and tea growing and is prevalent in the region.

Where to Stay in Hubli–Dharwad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hubli–Dharwad has several options for travelers who want to stay in hotels or guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • The President Hotel: A luxury hotel in the heart of the city that offers modern amenities and great service.

  • Clarks Inn: An affordable hotel that offers excellent hospitality and all essential amenities.

  • Hotel Metropolis: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean rooms, good service, and value for money.

What to Buy in Hubli–Dharwad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Hubli–Dharwad is known for its intricately crafted and beautifully designed handicrafts made of wood, metal, and fabric.

  • Sarees: The city is known for its cotton and silk sarees, which are available in a range of designs and patterns.

  • Spices: The region has an abundance of organic and fresh spices that can be bought from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hubli–Dharwad

Hubli–Dharwad has a rich and diverse culture, and its residents have a deep-rooted appreciation for art, music, literature, and festivals. The people of Hubli–Dharwad are hospitable, friendly and also very respectful of customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hubli–Dharwad

The best time to visit Hubli–Dharwad is from November to February when the weather is pleasant, and the temperature remains mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hubli–Dharwad

Do:

  • Respect and follow the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.

  • Be polite and friendly with locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter the streets and public places.

  • Do not disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hubli–Dharwad

Hubli–Dharwad is generally a safe city, with police stations located across the city.

Health in Hubli–Dharwad

Visitors should carry a basic medical kit when traveling to Hubli–Dharwad. It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Hubli–Dharwad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hubli–Dharwad

  • Hubli Airport: Located 8 km from the city center and has flights from major cities in India.

  • Belgaum Airport: Located 91 km from the city center and has flights from major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Hubli–Dharwad

  • Hubli Bus Stand: Located in the heart of the city and has buses connecting to major cities in the state.

  • Dharwad Bus Stand: Located in the city center and has buses connecting to major cities in the state.

Other Options

  • Train: The Hubli Junction is a major railway station that is well-connected with major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Hubli–Dharwad

Local buses, auto-rickshaws, and taxis are popular modes of transportation in Hubli–Dharwad.

Where to Go Next from Hubli–Dharwad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Goa: Known for its beaches, local culture, and nightlife, Goa is just 150 km from Hubli–Dharwad.

  • Gokarna: A quaint coastal town known for its pristine beaches, scenic landscapes and ancient temples, and is just 165 km from Hubli–Dharwad.

  • Dandeli: A wildlife sanctuary known for its exotic flora, fauna, and river rafting activities, and is only 53 km from Hubli–Dharwad.

Conclusion

Hubli–Dharwad is a vibrant and culturally rich city that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and spirituality. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or food aficionado, Hubli–Dharwad has a lot to offer.
