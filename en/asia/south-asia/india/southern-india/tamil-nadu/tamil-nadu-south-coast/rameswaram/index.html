Introduction to Rameswaram

Rameswaram, located on Tamil Nadu’s southern coast, is a fascinating town known for its pristine beaches, ancient temples, and rich cultural heritage. This city holds a significant place in Hindu mythology as it is believed that Lord Rama built the famous Rama Setu Bridge here to reach Lanka to rescue his wife, Sita.

Rameswaram is a popular pilgrimage destination and attracts thousands of tourists from across India and around the world. It boasts ancient temples, historical places, tranquil beaches, and stunning natural landscapes. So, let’s explore the different places to visit, delicacies to try, and things to do in Rameswaram in this comprehensive travel guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rameswaram

Day 1: Visit Ramanathaswamy Temple, Kalam House, and Pamban Bridge.
Day 2: Explore the serene beauty of Kurusadai Island, Villoondi Tirtham, and The Art Gallery.
Day 3: Go on a boating adventure at Agni Theertham Boat House, followed by a visit to Dhanushkodi. Day 4: Visit the Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park, followed by a visit to Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Memorial. Day 5: Explore the charming Annai Indira Gandhi Bridge and bird watching at the Sethu Karai Bird Sanctuary. Day 6: Pay a visit to Gandhamathana Parvatham, followed by a cycling tour of Rameswaram.
Day 7: Enjoy a scenic drive to the Ariyaman Beach, followed by a visit to the Kothandaraswamy Temple.

Local Culture and Customs of Rameswaram

Rameswaram’s culture is deeply influenced by Hindu mythology and traditions. The locals are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature. The town celebrates various annual festivals like Sita Rama Kalyanam, Brahmotsavam, Theppotsavam, and Mahalaya Amavasya.

Best Time to Visit Rameswaram

The best time to visit Rameswaram is between October and April, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rameswaram

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress modestly.
  3. Stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter in public places.
  2. Do not argue with locals.
  3. Do not smoke or drink in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rameswaram

Rameswaram is a safe destination, but one should be aware of pickpockets and hinderances of the local water bodies.

Health in Rameswaram

Avoid drinking tap water and prefer bottled water. Wear mosquito repellent while you explore the outdoors.
