Introduction to Thanjavur

Nestled on the banks of the Cauvery River in Tamil Nadu, South India, Thanjavur, also known as Tanjore, is a fascinating cultural hub that attracts visitors from around the world. Regarded as the cradle of South Indian classical music and arts, Thanjavur was once a flourishing capital of the Chola dynasty and later the Nayakas, giving it a deep cultural heritage that is still evident in its magnificent temples, palaces, museums, and historical sites.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Thanjavur, we will explore the must-visit places, the best things to do, the top-rated hotels and guesthouses, and the local cuisine that makes Thanjavur a unique and exciting destination worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Thanjavur

Thanjavur is home to many fascinating cultural and natural landmarks that provide a glimpse into the region’s rich history and heritage. Here is a list of the top places that you must visit in Thanjavur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brihadeeswara Temple
  • Royal Palace Museum
  • Sarabha Vilasam
  • Sangeetha Mahal
  • Rajah Serfoji’s Sarasvati Mahal Library

Natural Attractions of Thanjavur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Schwartz Church
  • Kaveri River
  • Muthupet Mangrove Forest Reserve
  • Gangai Konda Cholapuram

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thanjavur

Thanjavur’s stunning natural beauty, combined with its rich cultural heritage, makes it a perfect place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Thanjavur:

  • Brihadeeswara Temple Circuit
  • Kaveri River Trail

  • Thanjavur to Gangai Konda Cholapuram Bike Ride

Best Things to Do in Thanjavur: Activities and Tours

Thanjavur offers a wide range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do and tours to take in Thanjavur:

  • Bazaar Walk: Explore the vibrant local markets of Thanjavur, including the famous Kumbakonam Market.
  • Tanjore Painting Workshop: Learn the ancient art of Tanjore painting from local craftsmen.
  • Veena Making Workshop: Learn the art of making traditional Indian musical instruments at the Sarasvati Mahal Library.
  • Temple Walk: Take a guided tour of the city’s magnificent temples, including the Brihadeeswara Temple and the Airavateswara Temple.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thanjavur

If you’re planning to spend a week in Thanjavur, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in this beautiful city:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Brihadeeswara Temple and Royal Palace Museum
Day 2 Learn Tanjore painting at a local workshop and visit the Kumbakonam Market
Day 3 Take a guided tour of the Airavateswara Temple and Muthupet Mangrove Forest Reserve
Day 4 Explore the Rajah Serfoji’s Sarasvati Mahal Library and take a veena-making workshop
Day 5 Take a hiking or biking tour along the Kaveri River Trail
Day 6 Visit Gangai Konda Cholapuram and take a temple walk
Day 7 Go on a Bazaar walk and enjoy the local cuisine at a Thanjavur restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Thanjavur

Thanjavur’s cuisine reflects its rich cultural heritage, with influences from the Chola and Nayak dynasties. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Thanjavur:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thanjavur-style Chicken Chettinad
  • Kumbakonam Degree Coffee
  • Keema Dosa
  • Paniyaram

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee
  • Neer Mor (Buttermilk)
  • Panchamirtham (Sweet Fruit Salad)
  • Koozh (Fermented Porridge)

Local Culture and Customs of Thanjavur

Thanjavur’s culture is deeply rooted in the region’s rich history and heritage. Visitors to Thanjavur should be aware of local customs and etiquette, including appropriate dress codes while visiting temples and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Thanjavur

The best time to visit Thanjavur is between October and March, as the weather is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thanjavur

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Thanjavur:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately while visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Make loud noises while visiting temples or religious sites.
  • Take photographs without seeking prior permission.
  • Litter or pollute public places.

Safety and Health

Thanjavur is generally a safe place, but visitors should take basic precautions such as avoiding isolated areas and carrying copies of important documents. Visitors should also ensure that they are up to date on any necessary vaccinations before traveling to Thanjavur.

Getting to Thanjavur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thanjavur is well-connected through air, rail, and road, making it easily accessible to visitors from all over the world. Here are some ways to get to Thanjavur:

Airports Near Thanjavur

  • Tiruchirappalli International Airport (62 km away)
  • Madurai International Airport (156 km away)

Bus Stations Near Thanjavur

  • Thanjavur New Bus Stand
  • Thanjavur Old Bus Stand

Other Options

  • Trains: Thanjavur Railway Station
  • Taxis: Private taxis and cabs are available for hire

How to Get Around in Thanjavur

Thanjavur has a good network of public transportation, including buses and auto-rickshaws, making it easy to get around the city. Visitors can also rent a bike or motorbike to explore the city at their own pace.
