Introduction to Devala

Devala is a quaint village situated in the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve in Western Tamil Nadu, India. This hidden gem is known for its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. It is an ideal destination for travelers who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and unwind amidst the serene surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Devala

There are numerous culturally significant and naturally beautiful attractions in Devala. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bhavani Temple: Situated on the banks of the Bhavani River, this ancient temple is dedicated to Goddess Bhavani, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. It is believed that the temple was built by King Mandhata in the Treta Yuga.

  • Chikkarampalayam Mosque: This beautiful mosque was built in the 17th century during the reign of Tipu Sultan. It is a fascinating example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

  • Kolaramma Temple: This 300-year-old temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali. It is said that the temple was built by the Hoysala kings who ruled over the region in the medieval period.

Natural Attractions of Devala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silent Valley National Park: This stunning national park is located in the Nilgiri hills and is a biodiversity hotspot. It is home to numerous endangered species of animals and plants. Visitors can go on safaris and hikes to explore the park.

  • Aanamalai Tiger Reserve: This tiger reserve is located in the Anaimalai Hills, which are part of the Western Ghats. It is home to numerous species of animals, including tigers, leopards, and elephants. Visitors can go on safaris and hikes to explore the reserve.

  • Kodanad View Point: This scenic view point offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The view from here is particularly spectacular during sunrise and sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Devala

Devala is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. There are numerous hiking and biking trails in and around the village that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests. Some popular routes include:

  • Bison Valley Trekking Trail: This trail is a 4-hour trek through the lush forests of the Nilgiris. It is home to numerous species of animals, including bison, elephants, and leopards.

  • Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: This 2-day trek takes visitors through the Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to numerous species of animals, including tigers, leopards, and elephants. The trek offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and hills.

Best Things to Do in Devala: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are numerous other activities and tours that visitors can enjoy in Devala. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Tea Plantation Tour: Devala is known for its tea plantations, and visitors can go on tours to learn about the history of tea production in the region and see how tea is processed.

  • Farm Tour: Visitors can go on tours of local farms to learn about traditional farming practices and see how crops are grown.

Food and Drink Scene of Devala

Devala is known for its traditional South Indian cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the region’s geography and climate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kothu Parotta: This dish is made from shredded parotta, which is a type of layered flatbread, and mixed with vegetables, eggs, and chicken.

  • Sambhar: This spicy and tangy lentil-based soup is a staple of South Indian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: This tea is made with black tea leaves, milk, and a blend of spices, including ginger, cinnamon, and cardamom.

  • Toddy: This alcoholic beverage is made from the sap of coconut trees and has a sweet and sour taste.

Where to Stay in Devala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Devala has numerous options for accommodation, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  • Bison Valley Lodge: This eco-friendly resort is located amidst the lush forests of the Nilgiris and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Tea Nest: This cozy guesthouse is situated in the heart of a tea plantation and offers visitors a chance to experience life on a working tea estate.

What to Buy in Devala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Devala is known for its traditional handicrafts, which are made by local artisans using traditional techniques. Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Handwoven textiles: Devala is known for its beautiful handwoven textiles, including saris, shawls, and scarves.

  • Wooden toys: These toys are traditionally made by hand by local artisans and come in a variety of shapes and sizes.

Local Culture and Customs of Devala

Devala has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The village is known for its traditional festivals and celebrations, which are attended by locals and visitors alike.

Best Time to Visit Devala

The best time to visit Devala is from October to May when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. The monsoon season, which lasts from June to September, can make outdoor activities challenging.

Safety and Health

Safety in Devala

Devala is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, particularly in crowded areas.

Health in Devala

Visitors to Devala should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites as the region is prone to diseases like malaria and dengue. It is advisable to carry mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Devala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Devala

The nearest airports to Devala are Coimbatore International Airport, which is located approximately 98 km away, and Calicut International Airport, which is located approximately 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Devala

Devala is well-connected by bus to nearby cities like Coimbatore and Ooty. Buses are a convenient and affordable mode of transportation.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Devala from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Devala

Devala is a small village, and most attractions can be easily accessed on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Devala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Devala is located in the Western Ghats and is surrounded by numerous other scenic destinations. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Ooty: This hill station is known for its stunning views of the Nilgiri hills and its tea plantations.

  • Coonoor: This hill station is located approximately 40 km from Devala and is known for its scenic vistas and colonial architecture.

Conclusion

Devala is a hidden gem located in the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve in Western Tamil Nadu. This destination is perfect for travelers who want to explore the natural beauty of the region and experience its rich cultural heritage. From outdoor adventures to historical attractions, Devala has something to offer everyone.
