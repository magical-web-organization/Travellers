Introduction to Hosur

Located in the northern part of Tamil Nadu, Hosur is an important industrial town that connects Bangalore and Tamil Nadu. The city is popularly known as the “Gateway to Karnataka.” Hosur is also home to some popular tourist destinations and is a perfect weekend getaway spot for those living in Bangalore.

If you’re looking for a break from the bustle of the city, Hosur is the ideal place to visit. From culture-rich historical sites and museums to scenic and natural beauty, Hosur offers visitors a mix of traditional, historical and cultural sites.

List of Places to Visit in Hosur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maragadhavalli Samedha Srinivasa Perumal Temple: This temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, is known for its vast complexes and grand architecture. The temple is particularly famous for its annual Brahmotsavam festival, which takes place in March every year.

  • Krishnagiri Fort: The fort was built by the Vijayanagara dynasty and served as an important strategic defence point. It’s now a popular tourist attraction.

  • Kelavarapalli Dam: This dam is built on the Palar River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding greenery. The dam is a popular picnic spot for locals and tourists alike.

  • Government Museum: The museum houses an extensive collection of ancient sculptures, coins, and artefacts, giving visitors a glimpse into Hosur’s rich cultural past.

Natural Attractions of Hosur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hogenakkal Falls: Located about 70kms from Hosur, Hogenakkal Falls, also known as “Niagara Falls of India,” is a spectacular sight. The falls attract visitors from all over the world.

  • Muthyalamaduvu: Also known as the “Pearl Valley,” Muthyalamaduvu is a popular picnic spot with beautiful waterfalls and lush greenery.

  • Chandira Choodeswarar Temple: This temple is home to a natural spring that has healing powers. The temple is surrounded by beautiful hills and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hosur

  • Chandrachoodeshwarar Temple Trek: This trek is a 3 km uphill climb that will take you to the Chandrachoodeshwarar temple at the top. The trek is moderate and suitable for both beginners and experienced trekkers.

  • Kottapadi Trail Trek: This trek is a 5 km trail through lush greenery and scenic landscapes. The trail takes you to Kottapadi Hills, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding region.

Best Things to Do in Hosur: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Mango Orchards: Hosur is famous for its mango orchards, and you can experience the taste of fresh mangoes right from the trees.

  • Horse Riding: You can enjoy horse riding at Hosur’s Stud Farm.

  • Cycling: Cycling can be enjoyed through the beautiful lush greenery and hills of Hosur. Bikes are available on rent for tourists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hosur

Day Plan
1 Visit the Maragadhavalli Samedha Srinivasa Perumal Temple, Government Museum, and Krishnagiri Fort
2 Trek to Chandrachoodeshwara Temple and visit Kelavarapalli Dam
3 Explore Hosur’s Stud Farm and visit the Mango Orchards
4 Enjoy outdoor activities such as cycling, horse riding, and rock climbing
5 Visit Hogenakkal Falls, located at 70kms distance from Hosur, and enjoy the beauty of the falls
6 Take a picnic at Muthyalamaduvu, a popular picnic spot with beautiful surroundings

Food and Drink Scene of Hosur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryani: Hosur is known for its famous Biryani, which is a must-try when you’re in the city.

  • Milky Samosa: Milky Samosas, which are creamy samosas, are popular street food in Hosur.

  • Kara Vadai: Kara Vadai, a popular snack made from black gram, is a must-try when you’re in Hosur.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: Filter Coffee or “Kaapi” is a popular beverage in Hosur and South India.

  • Coconut Water: Coconut water is a refreshing drink you can enjoy on the streets of Hosur.

Where to Stay in Hosur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Clarks Inn: Clarks Inn is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Hosur.

  • Gokul Lodge and Restaurant: Gokul Lodge & Restaurant is a budget hotel that offers comfortable stay and excellent food.

  • Hill View Hotels and Resorts: Hill View Hotels and Resorts provide a luxurious stay in Hosur with scenic views of the region.

What to Buy in Hosur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mangoes: Hosur’s mangoes are famous and make a perfect souvenir to bring back home.

  • Silk Sarees: Hosur is known for its handwoven silk sarees that are available in local shops.

  • Terracotta Pottery: You can buy beautiful terracotta pottery from local markets in Hosur.

Local Culture and Customs of Hosur

Hosur’s culture and customs are predominantly influenced by Hinduism. Most of the local festivals revolve around Hindu mythology.

Best Time to Visit Hosur

The best time to visit Hosur is between November and March when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hosur

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Be polite to the people you meet.
  • Plan your trip during the best season to visit.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public places.
  • Do not photograph locals without their permission.
  • Do not engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hosur

Hosur is generally safe for tourists, but it’s always advised to take necessary precautions. It’s essential to be alert and stay vigilant while exploring public places.

Health in Hosur

Visitors to Hosur are advised to be up-to-date on all standard vaccinations. It’s also recommended to carry a first aid kit with essential medical supplies.

Getting to Hosur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hosur

The nearest airport to Hosur is Bangalore’s Kempegowda International Airport, located about 47kms away.

Bus Stations Near Hosur

Hosur Bus Stand is the main bus station in Hosur and hosts several buses that run between Bangalore and Tamil Nadu.

Other Options

Hosur is well-connected by train, and the Hosur Railway Station is located in the city centre.

How to Get Around in Hosur

The best way to get around Hosur is by using local buses and taxis or renting a car. Bikes and cycles are also available on rent for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Hosur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bangalore: Bangalore is just 56kms away from Hosur and makes for a great destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.

  • Krishnagiri: Krishnagiri is a beautiful hill station that is located approximately 32kms from Hosur.

  • Salem: Salem, located 117kms away from Hosur, is a bustling city that offers visitors a breathtaking view of the hills and forests.

Conclusion

Hosur offers visitors a mix of culture, history, and scenic beauty. From historical attractions, natural wonders, to outdoor adventures, Hosur has it all. Plan your trip to Hosur, and do not forget to explore the beautiful surroundings and vibrant culture of this charming destination.
