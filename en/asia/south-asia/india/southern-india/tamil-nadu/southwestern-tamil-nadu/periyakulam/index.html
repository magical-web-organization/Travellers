Introduction to Periyakulam

Periyakulam is a picturesque town located in the Theni district of Tamil Nadu. Surrounded by the Western Ghats, it is known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. The town is located around 75 km from Madurai and is easily accessible by road.

List of Places to Visit in Periyakulam

Here are some of the top attractions that you must visit while in Periyakulam:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sri Maasi Mariamman Temple: Dedicated to the goddess Mariamman, this temple is a must-visit for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Veerapandi Gowmariamman Temple: Another important temple, it is said to have been built during the 18th century.
  • Kailasanathar Temple: Another ancient temple, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its exquisite sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Periyakulam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Meghamalai: Known for its tea plantations, stunning viewpoints, and waterfalls, Meghamalai is a great place for a day trip from Periyakulam.
  • Vaigai Dam: Located on the Vaigai river, this dam is a popular picnic spot and offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Kumbakkarai Falls: This waterfall, located around 8 km from Periyakulam, is one of the most picturesque in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Periyakulam

For adventure enthusiasts, Periyakulam offers some great opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some routes that you can explore:

  • Kodaikanal to Periyakulam: This is a scenic route that takes you through dense forests, tea plantations, and picturesque villages.
  • Kumbakkarai to Periyakulam: This route takes you through the beautiful Kumbakkarai hills and forests.

Best Things to Do in Periyakulam: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Periyakulam:

  • Tea plantation visits: Periyakulam is known for its tea production, and you can take a tour of the tea plantations to learn about the tea-making process.
  • Boat rides on Vaigai river: You can take a leisurely boat ride on the Vaigai river and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Visit Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary: Located about 100 km from Periyakulam, this sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and leopards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Periyakulam

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Periyakulam:

Day 1: Arrive in Periyakulam, explore the town

Day 2: Visit the Sri Maasi Mariamman Temple, Veerapandi Gowmariamman Temple, and Kailasanathar Temple

Day 3: Take a day trip to Meghamalai, visit tea plantations and waterfalls

Day 4: Visit Vaigai Dam and Kumbakkarai Falls

Day 5: Go on a hiking or biking trail, explore the Kodaikanal-Periyakulam route

Day 6: Visit the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Day 7: Depart from Periyakulam

Food and Drink Scene of Periyakulam

When it comes to food, Periyakulam has a lot to offer. Here are some dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mutton Chukka: A popular meat dish made with mutton, spices, and onions.
  • Parotta: A flaky, layered Indian bread that is usually served with a spicy meat or vegetable curry.
  • Athoos: A traditional breakfast dish made with rice flour, grated coconut, and jaggery.

Local Beverages

  • Masala Chai: A spiced tea that is a popular beverage in India.
  • Tender Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is a refreshing and healthy drink that is widely available in Periyakulam.

Where to Stay in Periyakulam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Periyakulam offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Fern Creek: This upscale resort offers luxurious rooms, stunning views, and a range of amenities, including a spa and pool.
  • Surya Holidays: A budget-friendly option, this guesthouse offers clean, comfortable rooms and friendly service.
  • Cardamom House: A homestay that offers a unique and authentic experience of the local culture and lifestyle.

What to Buy in Periyakulam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Periyakulam is known for its tea production, and you can buy a range of local tea products, including loose tea leaves, tea bags, and tea blends. You can also buy handmade crafts, silk sarees, and spices as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Periyakulam

Periyakulam has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by the Tamil and Jain communities. The people are welcoming and hospitable, and the town has a peaceful and laid-back vibe. It is important to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Periyakulam

The best time to visit Periyakulam is from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season, from June to September, can be unpredictable and might disrupt your travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Periyakulam

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Bargain while shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Use plastic bags and litter the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Periyakulam

Periyakulam is a safe town, with friendly and helpful locals. However, it is important to take general precautions, like avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping your valuables safe.

Health in Periyakulam

Periyakulam has a few hospitals and clinics that offer basic medical care. However, it is recommended to carry your own first-aid kit and necessary medication.

Getting to Periyakulam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Periyakulam

The nearest airport to Periyakulam is Madurai Airport, which is located around 75 km away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Periyakulam.

Bus Stations Near Periyakulam

Periyakulam has a bus stand that is well-connected to nearby cities and towns, including Madurai, Kodaikanal, and Dindigul.

Other Options

You can also reach Periyakulam by hiring a taxi or driving yourself. The roads are well-maintained, and the journey is scenic and enjoyable.

How to Get Around in Periyakulam

Periyakulam is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. You can also hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw to get around.

Where to Go Next from Periyakulam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore after your visit to Periyakulam:

  • Kodaikanal: A popular hill station that is known for its stunning landscapes and outdoor activities
  • Madurai: A city famous for its ancient temples and rich culture.
  • Munnar: A scenic hill station known for its tea plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Periyakulam is a charming town that is perfect for a relaxing and off-beat vacation. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture, it offers a unique and authentic experience of Tamil Nadu. So, pack your bags, visit Periyakulam, and explore the beauty of Southern India.
