Introduction to Kavalkinaru

Kavalkinaru is a small town in Far Southern Tamil Nadu, India. Often overshadowed by the famous temples in nearby Kanyakumari and Madurai, Kavalkinaru offers a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions. With its beautiful landscapes, deeply-rooted traditions, and friendly locals, this once obscure town is slowly gaining popularity among tourists and backpackers.

List of Places to Visit in Kavalkinaru

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sri Kashi Vishwanathar Temple

The Sri Kashi Vishwanathar Temple is one of the most important cultural attractions in Kavalkinaru. Built in the 19th century, the temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is renowned for its beautiful architecture.

Nagaraja Temple

The Nagaraja Temple is another must-visit cultural attraction in Kavalkinaru. Dedicated to Nagadevatha, the temple is known for its intricate carvings and beautiful artwork.

Sri Saradambal Temple

Sri Saradambal Temple is a beautiful temple in Kavalkinaru that is dedicated to the goddess Saraswati. Visitors can witness the stunning architecture and participate in the temple rituals and worship.

Natural Attractions of Kavalkinaru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vattaparai Falls

Vattaparai Falls is a beautiful waterfall located close to Kavalkinaru. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a peaceful escape from the bustle of the town.

Sadayandi Temple

The Sadayandi Temple is a scenic and serene location that is surrounded by beautiful hills and greenery. Visitors can enjoy exploring the temple grounds and enjoying the peacefulness of the area.

Kanyakumari Beach

Located about an hour from Kavalkinaru, Kanyakumari Beach is known for its beautiful sunrises and sunsets. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and taking in the beautiful views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kavalkinaru

The foothills near Kavalkinaru are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include the Kambam Ridge hike, the Thirukural Hills Trail, and the Vediappan Hills Trail.

Best Things to Do in Kavalkinaru: Activities and Tours

Marundeeswarar Temple Visit

Marundeeswarar Temple is a must-visit temple that is known for its beautiful architecture and ancient history. Visitors can also participate in temple rituals and worship.

Local Village Tour

Embark on a local village tour to witness the lifestyle of the locals and experience their culture and traditions. Visitors can also try their hand at traditional local activities such as weaving, cooking, and farming.

Local Market Tour

Visitors can explore the local markets of Kavalkinaru to shop for traditional handicrafts, beautiful fabrics, and souvenirs to bring back home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kavalkinaru

Day 1: Explore the Sri Kashi Vishwanathar Temple and the Nagaraja Temple Day 2: Visit the Vattaparai Falls and Sadayandi Temple Day 3: Hike or bike the Kambam Ridge hike or the Thirukural Hills Trail Day 4: Visit Kanyakumari Beach and witness the beautiful sunset Day 5: Take a local village tour and experience the local culture Day 6: Explore the local markets and buy some souvenirs Day 7: Visit the Marundeeswarar Temple and participate in temple rituals

Food and Drink Scene of Kavalkinaru

Must-Try Dishes

Kavalkinaru is famous for its traditional South Indian cuisine. Some must-try dishes include dosa, idli, sambar, and rasam.

Local Beverages

Visitors can try traditional local beverages such as filter coffee, hot masala tea, and fresh coconut water.

Where to Stay in Kavalkinaru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kavalkinaru offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include Masi Residency, Hotel New Santhi, and Hotel JSK Paradise.

What to Buy in Kavalkinaru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can shop for local handicrafts, beautiful fabrics, and traditional souvenirs such as wooden figurines and woven baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kavalkinaru

Kavalkinaru is a deeply traditional town, and visitors are expected to dress conservatively and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kavalkinaru

The best time to visit Kavalkinaru is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and the town is not too crowded with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kavalkinaru

Do:

  • Dress conservatively
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bargain when shopping in the local markets

Don’t:

  • Take photographs inside the temples without permission
  • Eat or drink in public during Ramadan

Safety and Health

Safety in Kavalkinaru

Kavalkinaru is generally a safe town for tourists, but visitors should be mindful of their belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Kavalkinaru

Visitors should ensure they have all the necessary vaccinations prior to their visit. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Kavalkinaru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Kavalkinaru by air, bus, or train. The nearest airport is Trivandrum International Airport, which is about two hours away. There are also regular buses and trains that connect Kavalkinaru to other major cities in Tamil Nadu.

Airports Near Kavalkinaru

  • Trivandrum International Airport (90 km)

Bus Stations Near Kavalkinaru

  • Tirunelveli Bus Station (25 km)
  • Nagercoil Bus Station (34 km)

Other Options

  • Kavalkinaru Railway Station

How to Get Around in Kavalkinaru

Visitors can get around Kavalkinaru by using local buses, auto-rickshaws, or hiring a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Kavalkinaru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kanyakumari

Located an hour away from Kavalkinaru, Kanyakumari is known for its stunning sunrises and sunsets, beautiful beaches, and rich history.

Tirunelveli

Tirunelveli is a historic temple town located about an hour away from Kavalkinaru. Visitors can explore the ancient temples and indulge in the delicious local cuisine.

Conclusion

Kavalkinaru is a hidden gem in Far Southern Tamil Nadu that offers a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions. From its beautiful landscapes to its deeply-rooted traditions, this once obscure town is slowly becoming a popular destination among tourists.
