Introduction to Nagercoil

Nagercoil is a town situated in the southernmost part of Tamil Nadu, India. The town is known for its diverse culture, stunning landscapes, and rich history that dates back to the ancient times. Nagercoil is also home to some of the prominent temples and churches in India. The town has a unique mix of Tamil and Keralite culture owing to its proximity to the border of Kerala. In this tour guide, we have put together a comprehensive list of places to visit, dishes to try, local customs to keep in mind, and much more that would make your visit to Nagercoil a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Nagercoil

Whether you are a culture enthusiast, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Nagercoil has a wide range of attractions to suit your interests.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nagercoil has a rich history, and the town takes pride in its ancient heritage. Some of the must-visit cultural and historical places are:

  • Padmanabhapuram Palace
  • Suchindram Temple
  • Nagaraja Temple
  • Mathur Aqueduct

Natural Attractions of Nagercoil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nagercoil is a town surrounded by natural treasures such as hills, beaches, and backwaters. Listed below are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Kanyakumari Beach
  • Vattakottai Fort
  • Thirparappu Water Falls
  • Muttom Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nagercoil

Nagercoil is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular hiking and biking routes in and around the town are:

  • Mottaiadi, Marthandam Road
  • The River Trek, Kalikesam
  • Valayathura Hill Trek, Valayathura

Best Things to Do in Nagercoil: Activities and Tours

Apart from the cultural and natural sights, Nagercoil has several fun activities and tours that you can enjoy:

  • Visit Kanyakumari, the southernmost tip of India
  • Take a tour of the Padmanabhapuram Palace
  • Explore the local markets and taste street food
  • Witness the sunset at the Thiruvalluvar Statue

Week-Long Itinerary for Nagercoil

If you have a week to spend in Nagercoil, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Padmanabhapuram Palace and Mathur Aqueduct
2 Visit Suchindram and Nagaraja Temples
3 Relax at Muttom Beach
4 Trek to Vattakottai Fort
5 Visit Kanyakumari Beach and the Thiruvalluvar Statue
6 Take a day trip to Poovar Island
7 Shop for souvenirs and enjoy local delicacies

Food and Drink Scene of Nagercoil

Nagercoil offers a unique combination of Tamil and Kerala cuisine, and there are plenty of delicious dishes to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef roast with Malabar parotta
  • Kozhikode biriyani
  • Meen Kuzhambu (fish curry)
  • Appam and stew

Local Beverages

  • Masala chai
  • Banana wine
  • Tender coconut water

Where to Stay in Nagercoil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nagercoil has plenty of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some of the popular options are:

  • Hotel Vettikatt Regency
  • Sparsa Resorts
  • Hotel Sea View
  • Bethel Guest House

What to Buy in Nagercoil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nagercoil is known for its spices, handcrafted items, and textiles. Some of the best places to shop for souvenirs and local products are the local markets and street shops located in and around the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Nagercoil

Nagercoil has a diverse cultural background influenced by Tamil and Keralite traditions. It is advisable to respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Nagercoil

The best time to visit Nagercoil is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and there is no rainfall. The town experiences heavy rainfall and high humidity during the monsoon season from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nagercoil

Do:

  • Cover your head and dress modestly when visiting temples
  • Carry an umbrella or raincoat during the monsoon season
  • Try local delicacies

Don’t:

  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places
  • Litter in public areas
  • Take pictures without permission from the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Nagercoil

Nagercoil is a relatively safe town, but it’s always advisable to take precautions while traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t carry too much cash while traveling
  • Avoid venturing out late at night
  • Always keep your valuables in a secure place

Health in Nagercoil

Nagercoil has some excellent healthcare facilities, but it’s always wise to take certain precautions to avoid falling sick. Always carry a bottle of water with you, and avoid street food if you have a sensitive stomach.

Getting to Nagercoil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nagercoil is well connected by air, rail, and road.

Airports Near Nagercoil

The nearest airport to Nagercoil is the Trivandrum International Airport, located in Kerala, which is around 68 km away. The airport is well connected to major cities in India.

Bus Stations Near Nagercoil

Nagercoil has a well-developed bus network, and it’s easy to find buses to the neighboring towns and cities. The popular bus stations in Nagercoil are:

  • Bus Stand Road
  • Colachel Bus Stand
  • Thuckalay Bus Stand

Other Options

You can also reach Nagercoil via train. The Nagercoil Junction railway station is well connected to major cities in India, including Chennai, Bangalore, and Mumbai.

How to Get Around in Nagercoil

Nagercoil has a reliable public transport system, including buses and taxis. Auto-rickshaws are also a popular mode of transport for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Nagercoil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nagercoil is surrounded by many beautiful towns and cities that you can explore. Some of the nearby destinations worth visiting are:

  • Kovalam
  • Poovar Island
  • Trivandrum
  • Tuticorin

Conclusion

Nagercoil is a beautiful destination that offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and nature. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Nagercoil and explore the best of what the town has to offer.
