Introduction to Sankarankoil

Nestled in the Far Southern region of Tamil Nadu, India, lies the enchanting town of Sankarankoil. Despite being lesser-known than other destinations in India, it is a hidden gem that offers a rich cultural and natural experience for adventurers looking for a unique and authentic experience in South Asia. The town’s name derives from “sankara,” a Hindu priest, and “koil,” meaning temple, indicating the area’s strong religious heritage.

Sankarankoil is renowned for the Sankara Narayana Swamy Temple, one of the most revered temples in the region, which attracts thousands of devotees each year. The town is also surrounded by the Western Ghats, offering stunning mountain views and untouched natural beauty. This travel guide will help you explore the best of Sankarankoil while also giving tips on where to stay, what to eat, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Sankarankoil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sankara Narayana Swamy Temple: Built-in dedication to Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva, this temple is a must-visit. The exquisite architecture, intricate carvings, and serene atmosphere draw in visitors from around the world.
  • Vallalar Koil: The shrine dedicated to one of the great leaders of South India, Saint Vallalar, is not to be missed. The temple is beautifully maintained and offers a peaceful escape from the hustle-bustle of the town.

Natural Attractions of Sankarankoil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Western Ghats: Sankarankoil’s location in the Western Ghats makes it an excellent place for nature lovers. The mountain range offers fantastic hiking opportunities with stunning views. Trekking to the nearby Agasthiyar Falls and Perumal Peak is an ideal way to explore the region’s natural beauty.
  • Andipatti Reserve Forest: This forest is a paradise for birdwatchers and wildlife enthusiasts. Here, you can observe native animals such as monkeys, bisons, and wild boars, as well as various species of birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sankarankoil

  • Agasthiyar Falls: The trek to Agasthiyar Falls is one of the most popular routes in Sankarankoil, taking approximately three hours to complete. The trail offers picturesque views of the Western Ghats, and the waterfall at the end is a stunning reward.
  • Perumal Peak: For a more challenging hike, consider Perumal Peak, which takes about four to five hours to complete. The trail is steep, and visitors should wear appropriate trekking attire and bring enough water.

Best Things to Do in Sankarankoil: Activities and Tours

  • Cultural Tours: Sankarankoil’s rich cultural heritage is best explored through a guided tour. Local operators offer tours of the town’s many temples and take visitors to witness traditional cultural events.
  • Wildlife Tours: For nature enthusiasts, wildlife tours are a great way to get up close with the region’s diverse flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sankarankoil

Day 1-2: Explore the town’s rich culture by visiting the Sankara Narayana Swamy Temple, Vallalar Koil, and other nearby temples.

Day 3-4: Take a hike or bike ride through the Andipatti Reserve Forest or Western Ghats.

Day 5: Join a cultural tour, exploring local customs and events.

Day 6-7: Go on a wildlife tour and marvel at the region’s diverse flora and fauna or take a leisurely stroll through the town and indulge in local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Sankarankoil

Must-Try Dishes

  • Idli: A savory steamed cake made from fermented rice and urad dal.
  • Dosai: A thin crepe made from rice and lentils, served with chutney and sambhar.
  • Pongal: A rice dish traditionally made as a breakfast dish, with moong dal, ghee, pepper, and cashew nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: Brewing freshly roasted coffee beans in a metal vessel with a perforated upper chamber is a traditional method found all over South India. Don’t miss out on a cup of authentic filter coffee!
  • Fresh Juices: Sankarankoil has many fruit vendors who sell delicious fresh juices that visitors can enjoy.

Where to Stay in Sankarankoil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sankarankoil has a plethora of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to high-end hotels.

  • KTDC Perumalmalai: A comfortable hotel with a beautiful view of the Western Ghats, makes this an ideal place to stay for visitors.
  • Ravine Lodge: This is a budget option located close to Sankara Narayana Swamy Temple, making it perfect for visitors looking for affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Sankarankoil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors must take home unique and authentic souvenirs from Sankarankoil, showcasing the town’s local culture and heritage.

  • Traditional Handicrafts: The town is renowned for its handmade handicrafts, including pottery, traditional jewelry, and leather items.
  • Spices and Tea: Visitors should take home South Indian spices and unique tea blends, found in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Sankarankoil

  1. It is customary to remove shoes before entering any temple or shrine.

  2. Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.

  3. Always ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Sankarankoil

The best time to visit Sankarankoil is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sankarankoil

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the town.
  • Disrespect the local culture or religion.
  • Ignore safety precautions while trekking or hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sankarankoil

Overall, Sankarankoil is considered to be a safe destination. However, visitors should take necessary precautions while trekking in the Western Ghats or Andipatti Reserve Forest.

Health in Sankarankoil

Visitors should be cautious when drinking water and may consider drinking bottled water only. Insect repellents and appropriate trekking attire are necessary during treks.

Getting to Sankarankoil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sankarankoil

The nearest airport is Madurai Airport, around 130 km away from Sankarankoil. One can hire a taxi or take a bus from the airport to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Sankarankoil

Sankarankoil Bus Station connects the town to other major destinations in Tamil Nadu.

Other Options

One can also hire a taxi or drive down to Sankarankoil from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Sankarankoil

One can explore the town on foot, or hire an autorickshaw or taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Sankarankoil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kanyakumari: Located about 100 km away, Kanyakumari is the southernmost tip of mainland India and a popular destination for tourists.

  • Theni: Located about 65 km away, Theni is renowned for its hilly terrain and fantastic trekking opportunities.

Conclusion

Sankarankoil is a charming destination in Southern India, offering a perfect blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. The town’s rich history, unique craft, delicious food, and warm hospitality make it a must-visit destination in Tamil Nadu. Whether you’re interested in exploring the Western Ghats or taking a cultural tour, Sankarankoil has something for everyone.
