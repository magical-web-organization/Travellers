Introduction to Muttukadu

Muttukadu is a hidden gem in the North Coast of Tamil Nadu, tucked between the Bay of Bengal and the Eastern Ghats. The village is famous for its scenic backwaters, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The laid-back atmosphere and friendly locals make Muttukadu a perfect destination for solo travelers, families, and couples. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best of Muttukadu, from top attractions to practical tips.

List of Places to Visit in Muttukadu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sri Vedagiriswarar Temple: A 12th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its archaic architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Dakshinachitra: A living museum showcasing the rich cultural heritage and lifestyle of South India through various art forms, crafts, and performances.
  • Cholamandal Artists’ Village: An artist community founded in 1966, showcasing contemporary and modern art exhibitions and workshops.
  • Mahabalipuram: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located 20 km from Muttukadu, famous for its ancient rock-cut temples and shore temples.

Natural Attractions of Muttukadu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muttukadu Backwaters: A popular spot for boating, fishing, and bird-watching. You can also enjoy a peaceful stroll along the lakeside.
  • The Madras Crocodile Bank Trust and Centre for Herpetology: A reptile park that houses various endangered and native species, including crocodiles, snakes, and turtles.
  • Covelong Beach: A pristine beach with coconut palms, calm waters, and water sports activities like surfing, swimming, and jet-skiing.
  • Mudaliarkuppam Backwater and Beach: A serene place to unwind and indulge in water sports like kayaking and banana boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Muttukadu

  • Muttukadu Eco Trail: A circular trail around the Muttukadu Lake, covering 13 km of scenic landscapes, plantations, and bird watching spots.
  • Ecr Beach road: A scenic biking route that offers stunning views of the Bay of Bengal and the coast.
  • Paranur Lake Trail: A flat trail that takes you on a 6 km ride alongside the Paranur Lake.

Best Things to Do in Muttukadu: Activities and Tours

  • Boating on Muttukadu Backwaters: Explore the tranquil backwaters of Muttukadu by boat or canoe.
  • Surfing and Water Sports at Covelong Beach: Get your adrenaline rushing with water sports like surfing, kayaking, and parasailing.
  • Heritage Walks in Mahabalipuram: Discover the fascinating history and architecture of Mahabalipuram with a guided heritage walk.
  • Fishing in Muttukadu Lake: Go fishing in the calm and quiet waters of the Muttukadu Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Muttukadu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chettinad Chicken: Spicy and flavorful chicken curry made with a blend of whole spices and coconut milk.
  • Crab Masala: Freshly caught crabs cooked in a savory masala made with onions, tomatoes, and spices.
  • Fish Fry: A popular South Indian dish made with freshly caught fish marinated in a mixture of spices and pan-fried to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Coffee: A strong and aromatic coffee made with milk and freshly ground coffee beans.
  • Tender Coconut Water: Refreshing and healthy drink made with fresh and chilled coconut water.
  • Tea: A refreshing cup of tea made with aromatic spices and freshly brewed tea leaves.

Where to Stay in Muttukadu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • InterContinental Chennai Mahabalipuram Resort: A luxury resort situated on the scenic East Coast Road, offering lavish rooms and suites, a swimming pool, and a beachfront restaurant.
  • The Centre Point Hotel: A comfortable and affordable hotel located in the heart of Muttukadu, offering cozy rooms and quick access to nearby attractions.
  • Chariot Beach Resort: A beachfront resort located in Mahabalipuram, offering a blend of luxury and serenity with its beach-facing rooms, pool, and spa.

What to Buy in Muttukadu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-Woven Silk Sarees: Traditional silk sarees made in the nearby Kanchipuram district, known for their exquisite quality and craftsmanship.
  • Stone Carvings: Intricately carved stone sculptures and figurines made by local artisans in Mahabalipuram.
  • Wooden Crafts: Handcrafted wooden artifacts and souvenirs, including figurines, wall hangings, and kitchen accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Muttukadu

Muttukadu is a cultural melting pot with a mix of Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam speaking communities. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and the village celebrates various festivals and traditions throughout the year. The traditional attire for women in Muttukadu is saree or salwar kameez, while men usually wear dhoti or lungi.

Best Time to Visit Muttukadu

The best time to visit Muttukadu is from November to February when the weather is pleasant and the humidity is low. The monsoon season from June to September is not recommended for travel, as the region experiences heavy rainfall and strong winds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muttukadu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain while shopping in local markets.
  • Practice good hygiene and carry hand sanitizer.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions early in the morning or late afternoon for cooler weather.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public places.
  • Drink unfiltered water or eat street food without proper hygiene.
  • Ignore the safety rules while engaging in water sports or adventure activities.
  • Disrespect local beliefs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Muttukadu

Muttukadu is a relatively safe place to travel, but it’s always advisable to practice basic safety measures like carrying a copy of your passport, avoiding deserted areas at night, and keeping an eye on your valuables. It’s also recommended to check the weather conditions before indulging in water sports activities.

Health in Muttukadu

Muttukadu has decent medical facilities and pharmacies, but it’s recommended to carry your prescribed medicines and first-aid kit. It’s also advisable to drink bottled or filtered water, wash your hands regularly, and carry mosquito repellent during the monsoon season.

Getting to Muttukadu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Muttukadu

  • Chennai International Airport: The nearest airport to Muttukadu, located around 30 km away. Flights connect to major cities in India and international locations.

Bus Stations Near Muttukadu

  • Chennai Central Bus Terminus: The nearest bus station to Muttukadu, located around 31 km away, connecting to various cities and towns in Tamil Nadu and neighboring states.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest major railway station is Chennai Central, which is well-connected to various cities in India.
  • Taxi/Car: Muttukadu is located on the East Coast Road, which connects Chennai with other coastal towns and cities. Taxis and cars can be hired for a smooth and hassle-free journey.

How to Get Around in Muttukadu

Muttukadu is a small village, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance. Other options for transportation include auto-rickshaws, taxis, and rented two-wheelers. Cycling is also a popular option for exploring the scenic trails and routes around Muttukadu.

Where to Go Next from Muttukadu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chennai: The capital city of Tamil Nadu, located around 30 km from Muttukadu, known for its fascinating history, culture, and cuisine.
  • Pondicherry: A former French colony located around 100 km from Muttukadu, known for its beautiful beaches, French-style architecture, and charming cafes.
  • Kanchipuram: A temple town located around 50 km from Muttukadu, known for its rich cultural heritage and hand-woven silk sarees.

Conclusion

Muttukadu is a fascinating destination that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and adventure.
