Introduction to Auroville

Nestled in the Tamil Nadu North Coast region, Auroville welcomes travelers from around the world to experience its utopian vision of a sustainable and spiritually fulfilling community. Founded in 1968 by Mirra Alfassa, Auroville aims to be a universal town where people of all nationalities live in harmony, working towards a common goal of human unity. Auroville receives visitors throughout the year and offers a plethora of experiences ranging from spiritual practices to outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Auroville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Historical Places, and Spiritual Sites

  • Sri Aurobindo Ashram: This ashram is the final resting place of the philosopher Sri Aurobindo and his collaborator Mirra Alfassa aka The Mother. Visitors can witness how the ashram carries on with the philosophies, values, and teachings of the pioneer’s spiritual research to achieve a state of consciousness beyond the ordinary.
  • Matri Mandir: Located at the heart of Auroville, Matri Mandir (Mother’s Temple) is a metallic sphere-shaped structure that serves as the meditative center of the community. Visitors can participate in the guided meditation sessions after registering in advance.
  • Bharat Nivas: Bharat Nivas is a cultural hub that celebrates Indian and global cultures through its exhibits, events, and workshops.
  • Centre d’Art: Creations in all media such as painting, sculpture, pottery, and other crafts are displayed at the Center d’Art. The gallery is known for promoting art of Aurovilians and Puducherry’s artists.

Natural Attractions of Auroville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Auroville Beach: Situated around 6 km away from the Auroville community, Auroville Beach is a secluded area with golden sand and a calming atmosphere where visitors can have a peaceful swim or enjoy sunbathing.
  • Pitchandikulam Forest: Located in the outskirts of the Auroville community, this 70-acre forest is home to various species of flora and fauna. Visitors can take part in eco-tours where they can learn about the region’s biodiversity.
  • Solitude Farm: This farm follows organic farming methods and promotes sustainable living. Visitors can participate in farm tours and cooking workshops.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auroville

  • Aspiration: This trail is around 6 km long and is perfect for beginners. The trail passes through the forest and is well marked.
  • Ousteri Lake: Auroville bike riders can opt for this trail and explore the region surrounding the scenic Ousteri Lake. The trail is approximately 30 km and allows visitors to take in the beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Auroville: Activities and Tours

  • Matrimandir Garden Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of the Matrimandir Gardens, which will take you through the unique flora of the region.
  • Auroville Food Tour: Visitors can take part in a guided Auroville food tour to discover the region’s delicious local cuisine.
  • Auroville Farm Tour: Visitors can take part in a guided tour of the farms and learn about organic farming practices pursued in Auroville.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auroville

Day 1: Visit the Sri Aurobindo Ashram and the Bharat Nivas.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Matrimandir Gardens and participate in the guided meditation sessions.

Day 3: Explore the Solitude Farm and take part in the farm tour.

Day 4: Have a relaxing day at Auroville Beach.

Day 5: Go on a hiking or biking trail around the region.

Day 6: Visit the Center d’Art.

Day 7: Take a food tour and savor the delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Auroville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tandoori Bread: Tandoori bread is a popular bread in India that is cooked in a clay oven. Visitors should try this bread as it is freshly made with spices and topped with ghee or butter.
  • Dosa: A popular South Indian dish, Dosa is a crepe made with rice and lentil flour and is served with chutney and sambar.

Local Beverages

  • Auroville Coffee: Auroville is known for its delicious coffee, which is grown organically.
  • Auroville Tea: Visitors should try the masala chai tea, a spiced milk tea with ginger, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves.

Where to Stay in Auroville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auroville Guesthouses: Auroville coordinators official guesthouses within the community. Visitors can choose options of a single or double room with various facilities such as hot water, fans, and a kitchenette.
  • Mango Hill: Mango Hill, located at the edge of Auroville, is a boutique hotel with a relaxing atmosphere and luxurious amenities such as a swimming pool, organic garden, and spa.

What to Buy in Auroville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Paper: Visitors can buy handmade paper made from recycled materials.
  • Auroville Pottery: Visitors may take home’s earthenware and stoneware pottery made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Auroville

Auroville is a harmonious community that values sharing, sustainability, and spirituality. The community members follow the rules of the Auroville Charter and operate on cashless transactions. Visitors are expected to maintain a spirit of harmonious community living.

Best Time to Visit Auroville

The best time to visit Auroville is between October and February, during the winter months when the climate is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auroville

Do:

  • Respect the Auroville community’s values and principles.
  • Follow the Auroville Charter, which outlines the community’s ethical principles.
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people, buildings, or public installations.

Don’t:

  • Smoke and consume alcohol in public.
  • Carry drugs, firearms, and other dangerous objects

Safety and Health

Safety in Auroville

Auroville is a safe destination, but visitors must take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as not leaving valuables in public places and locking doors and windows properly.

Health in Auroville

Visitors must stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and take safety measures to avoid contracting diseases such as dengue fever, which is endemic to the region.

Getting to Auroville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Auroville

  • Chennai International Airport: Chennai International Airport is the closest airport to Auroville, located approximately 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Auroville

  • Puducherry Bus Stand: The Puducherry bus stand is located approximately 15 minutes away from Auroville.

Other Options

Taxis are readily available from Chennai and can be hired to reach Auroville.

How to Get Around in Auroville

Visitors can hire bicycles, motorbikes, and cars to explore the region. Public transportation is also available from the Auroville community to Puducherry and other nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Auroville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puducherry: Located approximately 12 km away from Auroville, Puducherry is a charming destination that boasts a French colonial heritage.
  • Mahabalipuram: Located approximately 100 km from Auroville, Mahabalipuram is a historic town that is home to beautiful rock-cut temples and beaches.

Conclusion

Auroville offers visitors a unique travel experience that celebrates community living, sustainable practices, and spirituality. From natural wonders to cultural sites, this comprehensive guide provides visitors with all the information they need to make the most of their trip to this utopian community.
