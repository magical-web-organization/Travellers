Introduction to Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep is a group of 36 picturesque coral islands situated in the Arabian Sea, off the west coast of India. The region is known for its stunning coral reefs, pristine white-sand beaches and azure lagoons. The islands are also home to a vibrant culture that blends Islamic and Indian traditions, with influences from colonial Portuguese and Arab traders.

List of Places to Visit in Lakshadweep

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kavaratti Island: This island is famous for its beautiful mosque, Ujra mosque, which was built in the 17th century by an Arab saint.
  • Agatti Island: Agatti Island has an airport as well as beautiful coral reefs for snorkelling and scuba diving. It is also home to a lighthouse that was built by the British.

Natural Attractions of Lakshadweep: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kadmat Island: The long stretches of sandy beaches, blue lagoons, and coral reefs make Kadmat Island a popular destination for water activities like snorkelling and scuba diving.
  • Minicoy Island: The southernmost island in the Lakshadweep archipelago, Minicoy Island boasts impressive landscapes and beautiful beaches. Visitors can take a walk on the long jetty or go kayaking in the calm lagoon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakshadweep

Though there aren’t many hiking or biking trails on the islands, visitors can take bike rides or scenic walks to explore the beautiful beaches and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Lakshadweep: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to keep visitors busy in Lakshadweep:

  • Snorkelling and Scuba Diving: The warm and crystal-clear waters of the Arabian Sea around Lakshadweep provide diverse marine life, including colourful corals and fishes.
  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity in the region, with local fishermen offering trips on their boats, allowing visitors to experience this ancient practice.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Peaceful kayaking and canoeing through the lagoons is an excellent way to explore the beautiful environment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakshadweep

Day 1: Arrive in Agatti Island, take a boat from Agatti Island to Bangaram Island. Day 2: Explore Bangaram Island and its beautiful coral reefs. Day 3: Travel to Thinnakara Island for beautiful white sand beaches and lagoons. Day 4-5: Visit Kadmat Island for snorkelling, scuba diving, and exploring fishing villages. Day 6-7: Spend time in Kavaratti Island, exploring Islamic architecture and white sandy beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakshadweep

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Lakshadweep is known for its bountiful catch of seafood, including fresh lobsters, prawns, and fish. You can feast on delicious seafood platters and other local specialities in island restaurants.
  • Biriyani: This is a popular dish that is served in many restaurants and is made with local spices.
  • Laksa: This is a noodle-based soup that is infused with local and cultural flavours; it’s a must-try for foodies!

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: Coconut water from fresh coconuts is a popular drink in Lakshadweep, and it’s the best way to quench your thirst after a day in the sun.
  • Tea: Tea is a popular drink everywhere in India, and it’s no different in Lakshadweep.

Where to Stay in Lakshadweep: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lakshadweep has several options when it comes to accommodation:

  • Resorts: There are many luxurious resorts available on the islands, offering guests luxurious rooms and exquisite dining options.
  • Homestays and Guesthouses: You can enjoy a homely stay in many of the islands’ quaint guesthouses and homestays.
  • Camping: There are camping sites on some of the islands where visitors can set up camp, and it can be a great way to get close to nature.

What to Buy in Lakshadweep: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: There are several handicraft markets on various islands in Lakshadweep, where visitors can buy local handicrafts made of coconut shells, wood, and coral.
  • Spices: Spices grown in the region are also popular, including cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves.
  • Coconut Oil: Lakshadweep is known for its pure and natural coconut oil that can be used for cooking and as beauty products.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep has a fascinating cultural heritage, a blend of Islamic and Indian traditions. Visitors should respect local customs; for example, alcohol is not permitted on the islands, and visitors should dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Lakshadweep

The best time to visit Lakshadweep islands is between November and May. The weather is pleasant, and the water is calm for diving and snorkelling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakshadweep

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Pack plenty of sunscreen, light clothing, and comfortable shoes for a beach vacation.
  • Try the seafood and coconut-based dishes.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of local people without their permission.
  • Drink or consume alcohol on any of the islands.
  • Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep is a safe place for visitors but be aware of the tides and currents when swimming or surfing in open water. Also, watch out for the extreme heat of the sun.

Health in Lakshadweep

Visitors should avoid drinking or consuming unfiltered water when travelling in Lakshadweep to avoid illnesses like diarrhoea. Always carry a refillable water bottle.

Getting to Lakshadweep: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lakshadweep is accessible only by air and sea.

Airports Near Lakshadweep

Cochin International Airport (COK) is the nearest airport to Lakshadweep, located 404km away. From Cochin, you can take a connecting flight to the Agatti Island airport in Lakshadweep.

Bus Stations Near Lakshadweep

There are no bus stations near Lakshadweep. However, visitors can take a bus from the nearest city or town to the airport or port.

Other Options

There are several ferries that run from the mainland (Kerala) to Lakshadweep islands.

How to Get Around in Lakshadweep

Visitors can explore the islands by taking a boat, hiring a bicycle or motorbike, or simply walking. Visitors can also use autorickshaws and taxis, which are available on the islands.

Where to Go Next from Lakshadweep: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Lakshadweep, visitors can explore nearby destinations like Kerala, known as “God’s Own Country” and the spice garden of India.

Conclusion

Lakshadweep is a magical destination that has something to offer for everyone, from serene beaches to rich cultural heritage. The islands are a must-visit for anyone seeking a peaceful and relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life.
