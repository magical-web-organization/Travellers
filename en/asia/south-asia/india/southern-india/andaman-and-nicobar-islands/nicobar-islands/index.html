Are you looking for a place to truly escape from the hustle and bustle of modern life? Look no further than the Nicobar Islands, an archipelago located in the southernmost part of India. With its crystal-clear waters, soft sandy beaches, and untouched forests, Nicobar Islands offer an idyllic setting for your next getaway. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to the Nicobar Islands.

Introduction to Nicobar Islands

The Nicobar Islands is located in the Bay of Bengal, which is home to around 22 islands that offer unparalleled natural beauty. These islands are blessed with breathtaking landscapes that combine the blues of the ocean with emerald-green forests. Car Nicobar, Great Nicobar, and Little Nicobar are the three main islands that make up the archipelago. The relaxed pace of life here, combined with the beauty of the place, make Nicobar Islands an ideal destination to unwind and enjoy life’s simple pleasures.

List of Places to Visit in Nicobar Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in local history and culture, we recommend visiting the Anthropological Museum in Port Blair, the capital city of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Here, you can explore the rich heritage of the region through its superb collection of tribal artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the traditions, rituals, and lifestyle of the indigenous populations of the islands.

Another must-visit is the Cellular Jail, also located in Port Blair. This colonial-era prison was used by the British to house Indian political prisoners, including Mahatma Gandhi. The jail is now a museum that chronicles the Indian Independence movement and the struggles of those who fought against British rule.

Natural Attractions of Nicobar Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Nicobar Islands offer unforgettable natural beauty that will leave you mesmerized. From the coral reefs and untouched beaches to the forests and mangroves, this place is a paradise for nature lovers. Some of the top natural attractions of Nicobar Islands include:

  • Campbell Bay National Park: Located in Great Nicobar Island, this national park is home to rich biodiversity, and has a variety of flora and fauna. Here you can spot birds, sea creatures, and various other species that call the park home.

  • Indira Point: Located at the southernmost tip of India, Indira Point is a lighthouse that offers a panoramic view of the Indian Ocean. You can reach the lighthouse by boat or trekking to the spot over a rugged terrain.

  • Katchal Island Beach: This beautiful beach boasts pristine white sand fringed with coconut palms and clear waters. The serenity and beauty of this place make it an ideal spot for picnics and a perfect escape from the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nicobar Islands

For adventure enthusiasts, Nicobar Islands offer several hiking and biking routes that provide an opportunity to experience the unspoiled natural beauty of this place, and gives you an opportunity to explore the archipelago’s tucked-away corners. From rainforest treks in Great Nicobar to coastal walks in Car Nicobar, there are several different routes that cater to varying levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Nicobar Islands: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other things to enjoy in Nicobar Islands, including:

  • Diving: The crystal-clear waters surrounding Nicobar Islands offer excellent visibility making diving a great way to explore the beautiful coral reefs and abundant marine life.

  • Snorkelling: For those who prefer to stay close to the surface, snorkeling immerses the visitor in underwater wonders.

  • Kayaking: Nicobar Islands is an ideal destination for kayaking, with several waterways and beautiful bays that are perfect for paddling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nicobar Islands

For travelers who want to make the most of their time in Nicobar Islands, we’ve compiled a weeklong itinerary that includes the best the archipelago has to offer.

Day 1: Explore Port Blair, and visit the Anthropological Museum and Cellular Jail

Day 2: Take a boat to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park and indulge in some snorkeling and scuba diving

Day 3: Visit Campbell Bay National Park, and take a trek through the rainforest to see the incredible biodiversity of this place

Day 4: Experience the beautiful beaches of Katchal Island

Day 5: Go kayaking in one of the beautiful bays of Nicobar Islands

Day 6: Hike to Indira Point and soak up panoramic views of the Indian Ocean

Day 7: Enjoy your last day in Nicobar Islands by soaking up the sun on the beaches or exploring some of the local markets and souvenirs shops in Port Blair.

Food and Drink Scene of Nicobar Islands

No trip to the Nicobar Islands is complete without sampling the local cuisine. From fresh seafood to local vegetables and fruits, this archipelago has a bounty of delicious food to enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Curry: A signature dish in Nicobar Islands, this curry is made with coconut milk, spices, and fresh seafood, usually crab or fish.

  • Chutney Curry: A veggie lovers delight, chutney curry is made with local vegetables and coconut chutney, making it a healthy and delicious option.

  • Fried Rice: This local take on a popular dish is served with seafood or chicken, and makes for a satisfying main course.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: Coconut water is a popular refresher and thirst quencher in Nicobar Islands. The islands are dotted with coconut trees, so this beverage is readily available.

  • Palm Wine: Made from fermented coconut water, this traditional alcoholic beverage is a local favourite, but its strong flavour may take some getting used to.

Where to Stay in Nicobar Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite the secluded nature of Nicobar Islands, there are still several lodging options available for tourists. Some of the best places to stay on the islands, include:

  • Sea Shell Hotel: Located in Port Blair, this boutique hotel is an ideal place to stay for those who want a luxurious vacation.

-Shompen by Singhotel: This 3 star hotel in the heart of Port Blair provides comfortable and affordable lodging options for visitors.

  • Cocos Place: This family-run guesthouse is located in Car Nicobar and provides an authentic experience of the local way of life.

What to Buy in Nicobar Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Nicobar Islands are known for producing a variety of local handicrafts and food products. Some of the best souvenirs to buy include:

  • Handmade wooden products: From bowls and platters to decorative items, Nicobar Islands is renowned for producing exquisite wooden handicrafts.

  • Sea-shell Jewellery: As the beaches of the islands are abundantly littered with seashells, there are several shops where one can buy shell-based jewellery and other decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Nicobar Islands

The people of the Nicobar Islands are mainly of Mongoloid origin, with languages spoken here that are unique to this place. Adi and Bengali are the other main languages spoken by the people. The local tribes of the islands have significant cultural and religious importance and visitors are expected to respect their customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Nicobar Islands

The best time to visit Nicobar Islands is between November and April, when the weather is dry, and the temperature is moderate. Monsoon season falls between May and October, so avoid visiting during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nicobar Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Book your tickets and accommodation well in advance

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural surroundings
  • Ignore the safety protocols
  • Take pictures of unauthorized sites or people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Nicobar Islands

The Nicobar Islands is generally considered to be a safe place for visitors, but it is always advisable to take the necessary precautions.

Health in Nicobar Islands

It is important to have all the necessary vaccinations before visiting Nicobar Islands, as this place is prone to several diseases. Drinking only from boiled or bottled water is highly recommended.

Getting to Nicobar Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to reach Nicobar Islands is by taking a flight to Port Blair, the capital city of Andaman, and Nicobar Islands. Regular flights operate from major Indian cities like Chennai, Delhi, Kolkata, and Mumbai. From there, one can take a ferry to reach the Nicobar Islands.

Airports Near Nicobar Islands

  • Veer Savarkar International Airport (IXZ), located in Port Blair is the main airport in Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Bus Stations Near Nicobar Islands

  • There are no bus stations near Nicobar Islands.

Other Options

  • Ferries from Port Blair provide regular service to Nicobar Islands.

How to Get Around in Nicobar Islands

The best way to get around Nicobar Islands is by hiring a local guide or taking a guided tour. You can also rent a bike or car to explore the islands.

Where to Go Next from Nicobar Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations from the Nicobar Islands that are worth exploring. Some of the most popular are:

  • Ross Island: Located in the Port Blair, Ross Island is a British colonial ruin and is considered one of the most captivating heritage sites in the region.

  • Havelock Island: This beautiful island has some of the best beaches in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and water sports like snorkeling and scuba diving are popular here.

Conclusion

The Nicobar Islands is a beautiful and secluded destination that offers an unbeatable combination of untold natural beauty and unique cultural experiences. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a foodie, or just looking to relax and get away from it all, Nicobar Islands has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your perfect vacation to this beautiful destination.
