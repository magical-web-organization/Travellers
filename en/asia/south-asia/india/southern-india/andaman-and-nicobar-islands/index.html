Introduction to Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Located in the southern part of India, Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of more than 500 islands in the Bay of Bengal. This archipelago is known for its pristine beaches, lush forests, and vibrant marine life, making it a popular tourist destination for nature enthusiasts, beach lovers, and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cellular Jail National Memorial: A former colonial prison that is now a national monument, this place is a must-visit for anyone interested in Indian history and the independence struggle.
  • Anthropological Museum: This museum houses a large collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the indigenous tribes of Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  • Chatham Saw Mill: The oldest mill in Asia, this place is now a popular tourist attraction that showcases the history of the timber industry in the islands.

Natural Attractions of Andaman and Nicobar Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Radhanagar Beach: This is one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, known for its crystal-clear waters, golden sands, and picturesque sunsets.
  • Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Home to a rich variety of marine life, this park is a haven for scuba divers and snorkelers.
  • Mount Harriet National Park: Located on the highest peak in the islands, this park offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Baratang Island: This island is known for its mangrove forests, limestone caves, and natural mud volcanoes, making it a popular destination for nature walks and trekking.
  • Neil Island: This small island is perfect for cycling and exploring the local villages and beaches at a leisurely pace.

Best Things to Do in Andaman and Nicobar Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling: With its clear waters and diverse marine life, Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a paradise for underwater enthusiasts.
  • Island hopping: With so many islands to explore, taking a tour of the surrounding islands is a great way to see the natural beauty of Andaman and Nicobar.
  • Sea Walking: If you don’t want to scuba dive, try sea walking to see the underwater world without any special gear.

Food and Drink Scene of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Platter: Andaman and Nicobar Islands are known for their fresh seafood - try a platter with a variety of fish, prawns, and crabs.
  • Fish Curry: Made with local spices and coconut milk, this curry is a must-try for anyone who loves spicy food.
  • Biryani: Being part of India, you can try the Andaman version of Indian biryani here, and it’s piquant and light.

Local Beverages

  • Local Coconut Water: Get fresh coconut water from a street vendor and enjoy the refreshing taste.
  • Andaman Sarbat: Made with a mix of coconut water, lime juice, and local herbs, this drink is a great way to cool down in the heat.

Where to Stay in Andaman and Nicobar Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Port Blair: The capital city of the islands has a range of accommodation options, from budget guesthouses to luxury resorts.
  • Havelock Island: This island is known for its stylish beachside villas and resorts, offering a luxurious stay right on the beach.
  • Neil Island: This island has a range of budget-friendly options, from guesthouses and hostels to homestays and beachside cottages.

What to Buy in Andaman and Nicobar Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: You can buy handmade items made by the locals, including shell jewelry, wooden crafts, and bamboo products.
  • Spices: The islands are known for their unique blend of spices, including cloves, cinnamon, and black pepper.
  • Pearls: You can buy locally sourced pearls as a precious souvenir for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The indigenous tribes of Andaman and Nicobar Islands have a rich and unique culture that has been preserved for centuries. Be respectful of their customs and traditions, and try to learn about the local culture by interacting with the locals in a respectful way.

Best Time to Visit Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The best time to visit Andaman and Nicobar Islands is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry enough cash as ATMs are not common outside of Port Blair.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes suitable for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or other natural sites.
  • Disturb the local wildlife in any way.
  • Smoke or drink in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Andaman and Nicobar Islands are generally safe for tourists, but it’s important to take necessary precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid areas that are known for petty crime and take care of your belongings.

Health in Andaman and Nicobar Islands

It’s recommended to have all the required vaccinations up-to-date before traveling to Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Make sure to carry any prescription medication with you and stay hydrated.

Getting to Andaman and Nicobar Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair is the main airport serving the islands.

Bus Stations Near Andaman and Nicobar Islands

There are no bus stations in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Other Options

The only way to get to the islands is by sea, with regular ferries and Catamarans connecting Port Blair with the mainland.

How to Get Around in Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The best way to get around the islands is by hiring a taxi, renting a scooter or bicycle, or taking a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Andaman and Nicobar Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chennai: A bustling city in southern India, Chennai is an excellent place to explore Tamil Nadu’s culture and heritage.
  • Kolkata: Also known as the “Cultural Capital of India,” the city offers an array of historic monuments and cultural attractions for tourist exploration.
  • Bhutan: Known for its stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage, this country is an excellent place to experience mountain trekking and explore traditional Bhutanese culture.

Conclusion

While Andaman and Nicobar Islands may be a bit isolated, it offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience that’s well worth the effort of getting there. From natural sights and outdoor adventures to pristine beaches and flavorsome cuisine, there’s something for everyone on this scenic archipelago. Plan your trip well, be respectful of local customs, and have a great time exploring these beautiful islands.
