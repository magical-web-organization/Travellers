Introduction to Little Andaman

Little Andaman is a hidden gem located in the South Andaman district, one of the 572 islands of Andaman and Nicobar, Southern India. Surrounded by the Bay of Bengal on one side, and the Andaman Sea on the other, this serene coastal town boasts of dense mangrove forests, turquoise blue ocean, rolling hills, and picturesque beaches. Little Andaman is the perfect destination for travelers who prefer offbeat destinations and want to escape the crowd.

List of Places to Visit in Little Andaman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Netaji Nagar Beach: Named after the famous freedom fighter Subhash Chandra Bose, Netaji Nagar Beach is an unspoiled beach, which is perfect for experiencing a tranquil evening stroll.
  • Whisper Wave Waterfall: One of the most popular waterfalls in Little Andaman, Whisper Wave Waterfall is a stunning natural gem that cascades from a height of 20 meters, surrounded by lush green tropical vegetation.
  • Oil palm plantation: Get an insight into the cultivation of oil palms, which are grown in abundance in Little Andaman.
  • Butler Bay Beach: An offbeat and secluded beach famous for surfing.

Natural Attractions of Little Andaman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Surf Waterfall: A majestic waterfall, with water gushing down from the height of 600 feet, surrounded by dense vegetation and stunning rock formations.
  • Beaches: Little Andaman is blessed with some of the most pristine and serene beaches, such as Radhanagar Beach, Harminder Bay, and Kalapathar Beach. These beaches offer an amazing opportunity to relax, unwind and enjoy a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Hiking: Little Andaman is known for its breathtaking hiking trails, offering stunning views of the ocean, virgin forests, and rugged hills. Explore the Saddle Peak National Park, which is 16 km away from the town, and experience unique fauna and flora of the island.
  • Boat Rides: Take a boat ride and explore the stunning coastline and scenic backwaters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Andaman

  • Saddle Peak Trek: Experience the breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean and summit the highest point in the Andaman Islands on this adventurous trek.
  • Butler Bay to Netaji Nagar Beach Trek: Take a trek and explore the unspoiled beauty of the island, while witnessing stunning waterfalls, unique flora and fauna, and experiencing the beauty of its pristine beaches.
  • Forest Mountain Trail: This trail is a popular choice for birdwatchers and nature lovers, with diverse bird species and serene surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Little Andaman: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Little Andaman is known for its best surfing spots such as White Surf and the Butler Bay Beach. Whether you’re an amateur or a pro, surfing will be a must-try activity on your list.
  • Boat rides: Boat rides around the island offer stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding forests. Take a sunset boat ride at the mouth of the creek and enjoy the stunning sight of the sun setting into the ocean.
  • Sea kayaking: Explore the turquoise blue oceans of the Andaman Sea with a sea kayaking tour.
  • Dolphin watching: Take an early morning boat ride and witness one of the most playful marine creatures, dolphins, in their natural habitat.
  • Ziplining: Experience the adrenaline rush by ziplining over the lush green tropical forests of Little Andaman.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Andaman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Curry: A classic Andaman dish made with freshly caught fish and traditional spices.
  • Crab Masala: A spicy dish made with crabs cooked in a rich and flavorful masala.
  • Seafood Platter: A tasty and delicately cooked platter of seafood, featuring a variety of fish, crabs, and prawns.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink made from the water of young coconuts, perfect for rehydration.
  • Palm Wine: A traditional alcoholic drink that is made by fermenting the sap of the palm tree. It has a unique taste and is popular among locals.

Where to Stay in Little Andaman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

  • Barefoot Resort is one of the finest options in Little Andaman. It has luxurious rooms with a sea view and many world-class amenities.
  • Whisper Wave Resort is a serene and peaceful resort located in the heart of the rainforest.

Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Seventh Wave Hostel is a popular choice among backpackers, with comfortable and affordable accommodations.
  • Little Andaman Island Beach Guest House is a beachfront guesthouse. It offers comfortable rooms with all necessities, and a mesmerizing view of pristine beaches.

What to Buy in Little Andaman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sea Shells: The beaches of the Andaman Sea are abundant with beautiful sea shells. You can buy them as souvenirs or for decorative purposes.
  • Coconut Oil: Coconut oil is a staple product of the Andaman islands. It’s perfect for cooking and has excellent skincare benefits too.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local artisans make a variety of handmade crafts, such as bamboo products, cane items, and handmade silk fabric.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Andaman

Little Andaman is known for its cultural richness and kindness of locals. The locals of Little Andaman have their unique way of life, customs, and traditions. Most residents practice traditional Hinduism, and there are several temples and shrines dedicated to various gods and goddesses. It’s essential to respect local customs and sensibilities when visiting this peaceful island.

Best Time to Visit Little Andaman

The best time to visit Little Andaman is from November to April when the weather is dry and pleasant. The temperatures range from 20 to 30 degrees Celsius during this period, making it the ideal time to explore the pristine beauty of the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Andaman

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs
  • Carry appropriate clothing during your visit, as this is a conservative island
  • Keep the environment clean, and don’t litter
  • Use sustainable tourism practices

Don’t:

  • Over-expect in terms of luxury and world-class amenities
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially during temple visits
  • Damage or disturb the ecosystem, as Little Andaman is an eco-friendly island
  • Speak loudly or use abusive language

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Andaman

Little Andaman is known for its excellent safety record. However, it’s advised to be cautious about the sea’s currents and avoid venturing too deep at the beaches.

Health in Little Andaman

Visitors should get their vaccines and health check-ups done before visiting Little Andaman. Do carry mosquito repellents and sunblock, as the tropical climate can be harsh on the skin.

Getting to Little Andaman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Little Andaman

The Port Blair airport is the nearest airport to Little Andaman, which is approximately 12 hours away from the island by ferry.

Bus Stations Near Little Andaman

There are local bus services available in Port Blair, which provide transportation to Little Andaman.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for private taxi services, which are readily available in the main town area.

How to Get Around in Little Andaman

Walking is the best way to explore Little Andaman. You can also rent bikes and scooters for a more comfortable mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Little Andaman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Blair: The charming capital of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, that’s known for its beautiful coastline and rich history
  • Neill Island: A pristine paradise, rich in marine life and striking natural beauty.

Conclusion

Little Andaman is a natural paradise that offers stunning scenery, unique culture, and outdoor activities. It’s one of the least explored tourist destinations in India, and it’s perfect for those looking for an offbeat adventure. If you’re looking for an escape from bustling city life and want to experience serenity, Little Andaman is the perfect destination for you.
