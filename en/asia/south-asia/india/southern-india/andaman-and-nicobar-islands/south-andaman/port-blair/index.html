Introduction to Port Blair

Located in South Andaman, Port Blair is the capital city of Andaman and Nicobar Islands. This beautiful destination is a favorite spot among travelers for its turquoise waters, lush green forests, and pristine beaches. The city is a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture, and offers visitors everything from adventure sports to a rich and diverse culture.

List of Places to Visit in Port Blair

There are numerous attractions in Port Blair to visit, making it one of the most popular destinations in the Andaman Islands. Here are some must-visit places you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Port Blair boasts a rich history with a variety of cultural attractions that offer visitors a glimpse into the past.

  • Cellular Jail – Famous for its dark history, the Cellular Jail is a must-visit attraction. It was built by the British in 1906 to imprison political prisoners and freedom fighters. The jail was used to torture Indian freedom fighters, including Veer Savarkar and Mahatma Gandhi.
  • Anthropological Museum – The Anthropological Museum is a great place to explore the cultural heritage of the Andaman Islands. It houses a vast collection of artifacts about the ancient tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  • Samudrika Naval Marine Museum – Samudrika Naval Marine Museum is a fascinating place to learn about the marine life and ecology of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Natural Attractions of Port Blair: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ross Island – Ross Island, a beautiful island in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, is a great place for those interested in history and scenic views. You can explore its ruins, beautiful beaches, and lush green forests.
  • North Bay Island – North Bay Island is the perfect destination for water sports enthusiasts. It offers a variety of water sports activities, including scuba diving, snorkeling, and sea walking.
  • Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park – Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is an ideal destination for nature lovers. You can explore the park’s diverse marine life and beautiful coral reefs by boat, and enjoy activities like snorkeling and diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Blair

Port Blair is a great place for hiking and biking. There are several popular routes for biking and hiking, including:

  • Mount Harriet National Park – Mount Harriet National Park is the perfect destination for hiking enthusiasts. The park is home to a variety of fauna and flora, including the Andaman wild pig and giant robber crab.
  • Chidiya Tapu – Chidiya Tapu is a popular spot for bird watching and hiking. You can take a short trek to the hilltop to witness the stunning sunset views.
  • Saddle Peak National Park – Saddle Peak National Park in North Andaman is another popular destination for nature lovers. The park offers excellent opportunities for hiking and trekking.

Best Things to Do in Port Blair: Activities and Tours

Port Blair is a great destination for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best things to do in Port Blair:

  • Scuba Diving – Port Blair is a great place to experience scuba diving. You can explore the diversity of marine life and underwater beauty at the North Bay Island.
  • Snorkeling – Snorkeling is another exciting activity that you can enjoy at North Bay Island and Havelock Island.
  • Sea Walking – If you want to experience the magic of walking on the seafloor, then sea walking is a must-try adventure at North Bay Island.
  • Boat Tours – Boat tours are an excellent way to experience the beauty of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. You can explore the incredible beauty of the islands by taking a boat tour of the Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Blair

If you’re planning to spend a week in Port Blair, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1 Visit the Cellular Jail and enjoy light and sound show in the evening.
Day 2 Explore Ross Island and Chidiya Tapu.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Havelock Island, enjoy scuba diving, snorkeling, and beach activities.
Day 4 Visit Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park and indulge in water sports activities.
Day 5 Trek to Mount Harriet, enjoy breathtaking panoramic views, and visit the Anthropological Museum.
Day 6 Visit Samudrika Naval Marine Museum and take a boat tour of the islands.
Day 7 Relax, unwind, and shop for souvenirs and local products in Port Blair.

Food and Drink Scene of Port Blair

Port Blair is a culinary delight, with a variety of dishes that have their roots in Indian and Bengali cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Curry – A quintessential Andaman and Nicobar Islands dish made with a variety of fish and served with steamed rice.
  • Prawn Curry – Another popular seafood dish in Port Blair, made with fresh prawns and served with rice.
  • Bebinca – A traditional Goan dessert, made with a mixture of flour, sugar, and coconut milk, and flavored with nutmeg and cardamom.

Local Beverages

  • Tender Coconut Water – Refreshing and nutritious, tender coconut water is the perfect thirst quencher on a hot day in Port Blair.
  • Feni – A locally brewed alcoholic drink made from fermented cashew fruits.

Where to Stay in Port Blair: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Port Blair offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to mid-range hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Here are some popular options:

  • Sinclairs Bayview Port Blair – A luxury hotel with comfortable rooms and a picturesque view of the Bay of Bengal.
  • Hotel Sentinel – A mid-range hotel with modern amenities and a central location in Port Blair.
  • Andaman Castle – A guesthouse that offers affordable accommodation and a great location.

What to Buy in Port Blair: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Port Blair is known for its handicrafts, shell products, and local produce. Here are some items to consider taking back as souvenirs:

  • Shell Jewelry – Handmade jewelry made from shells is a must-buy in Port Blair.
  • Handicrafts – The handicrafts that you find at the Sagarika Government Emporium are highly recommended, these shops offer souvenir items like local hats, coasters, and shell items.
  • Spices – Port Blair has a wide variety of spices to choose from. Bring back the aroma and flavor of the Andamans with local spices like cinnamon, pepper, and cloves.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Blair

Port Blair is a multi-cultural place with beautiful traditions and customs. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Attire – Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites and religious places.
  • Religious Beliefs – Respect the local beliefs and customs when visiting religious sites.
  • Greeting Customs – It is customary to greet locals with a smile, and a respectful “Namaste.”

Best Time to Visit Port Blair

The best time to visit Port Blair is between October and May when the weather is favorable, with clear skies and a moderate climate. The water is still clear, and visibility remains good for water sports activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Blair

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Port Blair:

Do:

  • Respect the local values and culture.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the protected areas and parks.
  • Stay hydrated and wear comfortable clothes when going out.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave waste on the beaches.
  • Disturb the peace on the beaches.
  • Ignore safety warnings when indulging in water sports activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Blair

Port Blair is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s best to take necessary precautions.

  • Avoid venturing out alone at night.
  • Be cautious of petty theft and secure your belongings.
  • Follow safety guidelines and wear protective gear when indulging in water sports activities.

Health in Port Blair

It’s essential to carry your medical kit with essential medicines and first-aid essentials when traveling to Port Blair, which may be needed in case of medical emergencies. It’s important to stay hydrated and avoid contaminated water, especially near the beaches.

Getting to Port Blair: Transportation Options and Logistics

Port Blair is connected to Delhi, Kolkata, and Chennai by air, making it accessible from most parts of the country. Here are some transportation options to consider while traveling to Port Blair:

  • By air – Port Blair has an international airport strategically located, making it easy to reach.
  • By sea – Port Blair is accessible by sea as well, given that it has an operational harbor.

Airports Near Port Blair

  • Veer Savarkar International Airport – This airport is located in Port Blair and has domestic flights from most parts of India, making it the most preferred option for air travel.

Bus Stations Near Port Blair

  • Aberdeen Bazaar Bus Stand – The Aberdeen Bazaar is the central location in Port Blair, and the bus stand is here, adding convenience for land travel.

Other Options

  • By Ferry – Local ferries are another transportation option, as well as for traveling to other islands from Port Blair.

How to Get Around in Port Blair

Here are the transportation options available in Port Blair:

  • Taxis – Apart from public transportation, taxis are available in Port Blair and can take you anywhere in the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws – Auto-rickshaws are another popular transportation option available in Port Blair.
  • Mototaxis – A unique mode of transportation, mototaxis are also popular and can take you around the city.

Where to Go Next from Port Blair: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some destinations to consider visiting if you’re looking to extend your trip:

  • Havelock Island – Havelock Island is one of the most popular destinations in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands known for its pristine beaches, world-class scuba diving sites, and stunning sunsets.
  • Neil Island – Neil Island is a quieter, natural, and less crowded island that offers plenty of water sports activities and outdoor adventures.
  • Baratang Island – Baratang Island is a little further away than the previous two destinations mentioned, but it’s worth it. It offers an unmatched opportunity to witness the incredible limestone caves and natural beauty.

Conclusion

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a paradise for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those interested in the rich cultural heritage of India. Port Blair, the capital city of Andaman and Nicobar Islands, is the best place to experience it all. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip, providing you with all the necessary information you need about Port Blair.
