Introduction to Wandoor

Wandoor is a peaceful village in South Andaman and Nicobar Island located in Southern India. It is a popular tourist destination for nature lovers, photographers, and adventure enthusiasts. With its rich natural beauty, diverse flora and fauna, and culture and traditions, Wandoor is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Wandoor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Anthropological Museum: A perfect place to explore the history and culture of Andaman and Nicobar Islands. The museum showcases artifacts, weapons, and handicrafts of various indigenous tribes.
  • Cellular Jail: A national monument that reminds us of India’s struggle for freedom. The jail was used by the British to imprison Indian freedom fighters.

Natural Attractions of Wandoor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Witness the underwater marine life and coral reefs by going on a boat ride or snorkeling.
  • Jolly Buoy Island: A perfect place for adventure enthusiasts to go scuba diving, snorkeling, and swimming.
  • Wandoor Beach: A pristine beach with crystal clear waters, perfect for swimming and relaxing.
  • Red Skin Island: A popular spot for water sports activities, such as scuba diving, snorkeling, and banana boat rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wandoor

  • Mount Harriet National Park: Hike up to the second highest peak in Andaman and Nicobar Islands and enjoy the panoramic views of the island.
  • Chidiyatapu: Explore the dense forests and watch exotic birds while biking or hiking.

Best Things to Do in Wandoor: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Enjoy thrilling water sports activities like scuba diving, snorkeling, kayaking, parasailing, and banana boat rides.
  • Trekking: Hike through the beautiful forests and hills of Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat ride and witness the beautiful landscapes, coral reefs, and marine life of Wandoor.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wandoor

Day 1 Visit Wandoor Beach and spend the day relaxing and swimming.

Food and Drink Scene of Wandoor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Fresh fish, prawns, and lobsters.
  • Andaman Trunk Lagoon Crab: A special crab found only in Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is a popular thirst quencher in Wandoor.

Where to Stay in Wandoor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Emerald Gecko Hotel: A luxurious hotel that offers beautiful sea-facing rooms and cottages.
  • White Coral Hotel: A family-friendly hotel that offers comfortable accommodation at affordable rates.
  • Silver Sand Beach Resort: A beautiful beachside resort with excellent amenities and services.

What to Buy in Wandoor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Beautifully crafted wooden figurines, cane and bamboo articles and mats, shell articles, and tribal jewelry.
  • Pickles: Delicious pickles prepared from locally grown fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Wandoor

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are home to several indigenous tribes, and Wandoor is no exception. The tribes have their unique customs and traditions, and it is important to respect and honor them.

Best Time to Visit Wandoor

The best time to visit Wandoor is from November to February when the weather is pleasant and dry. The temperature remains between 22°C to 30°C during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wandoor

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the seafood and local cuisine.
  • Stay hydrated and carry sunscreen and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife and marine life.
  • Litter and pollute the environment.
  • Venture to restricted areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wandoor

Wandoor is generally considered a safe place. However, it is recommended to avoid venturing out alone at night, especially in isolated areas, and always carry a mobile phone for emergencies.

Health in Wandoor

It is essential to carry necessary medication if you have any pre-existing health conditions. The water in Andaman and Nicobar Islands is not suitable for drinking, so make sure to carry bottled water.

Getting to Wandoor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wandoor

  • Veer Savarkar International Airport, Port Blair (approximately 36 km)

Bus Stations Near Wandoor

  • Port Blair Bus Depot (approximately 34 km)

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are available at the airport and bus depot.
  • Ferries: Ferries operate between different islands in Andaman and Nicobar Archipelago.

How to Get Around in Wandoor

The best way to get around Wandoor is by hiring a taxi or renting a two-wheeler.

Where to Go Next from Wandoor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Blair: The capital city of Andaman and Nicobar Islands, offering a glimpse of the Indian culture and history.
  • Neil Island: A serene island famous for its pristine beaches, coral reefs, and water sports activities.

Conclusion

Wandoor is a perfect place to relax, unwind and witness the beautiful natural scenery, rich culture and history of Andaman and Nicobar Islands. It offers something for everyone, from adventure enthusiasts to families looking for a peaceful vacation. Follow these travel tips for a comfortable and memorable trip to Wandoor.
