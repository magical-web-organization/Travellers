Introduction to Neil Island

Neil Island, located in the southern part of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, is a tropical paradise that offers visitors a unique blend of rich culture, natural beauty and adventure. With its serene beaches, lush forest cover, and vibrant coral reefs, it’s no wonder that Neil Island is rapidly gaining popularity as a must-visit destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Neil Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bharatpur Beach - Named after the great freedom fighter and philosopher, Bharat Sevashram Sangha, Bharatpur Beach is known for its clear waters, soft sand, and a wide array of water sports such as snorkeling and diving.

  2. Natural Bridge - Also known as the Howrah Bridge, The Natural Bridge is a natural rock formation that was created over several centuries due to the action of waves, tides, and winds.

  3. Sitapur Beach - Sitapur Beach is famous for its stunning sunrise views, shallow waters, and lagoons that host an extensive range of water activities like scuba diving, swimming, and snorkeling.

  4. Lakshmanpur Beach - Boasting one of the most picturesque sunsets in the world, Lakshmanpur Beach offers visitors a chance to relax on the soft sands or embark on a cycling or trekking expedition to explore its serene surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Neil Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ramnagar Beach - Located on the eastern coast of Neil Island, Ramnagar Beach offers a spectacular view of sunrise over the horizon, making it an ideal spot for photography enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

  2. Neil Kendra - The most prominent tourist hub in Neil Island, Neil Kendra, hosts a variety of restaurants, souvenir shops, and other amenities.

  3. Sir Hugh Rose Island - A tiny uninhabited island known for its coral reefs and a hub for snorkeling, diving and fishing activities.

  4. Bampu Beach/Turtle Nesting Grounds - Located on the western coast of Neil Island, Bampu Beach is a secluded cove that is renowned for its tranquil ambience, shallow waters and marine turtles that come to the shores to nest and hatch their eggs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neil Island

  1. Natural Rock Formation Trek - Offering stunning views of the natural bridge and the surrounding sea, this trek is great for adventure enthusiasts who are looking for a unique trekking experience.

  2. Neil Island - Havelock Island Bike Trail - From Neil Island’s ferry port, a bike trail spans the entire stretch of the two islands, making it a popular activity for those who love adventure and appreciate nature.

Best Things to Do in Neil Island: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba Diving - With its pristine water and diverse marine life, scuba diving is a popular activity in Neil Island that is sure to leave a lasting impression. You will find some of the best scuba diving spots, including the Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park, Sir Hugh Rose Island and the Bharatpur beach.

  2. Snorkeling - If you are looking for a relatively easier, yet exciting way of enjoying the island’s diverse marine life, snorkeling is the perfect activity to try.

  3. Island Hopping Tours - Take a tour around Neil Island to enjoy the archipelago’s natural beauty and to visit some of the more popular tourist spots such as the Neil Kendra Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Neil Island

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish Curry - The island’s seafood-inspired dishes are heavily influenced by Indian, Thai and Burmese cuisine, making it a paradise for foodies.

  2. Dal and Rice - Enjoy a traditional Indian-style meal with Dal and rice while you take in the serene surroundings.

Local Beverages

  1. Coconut Water - A refreshing drink that is a must-try on this island. It is locally grown on the island and is sold by many street vendors as well.

  2. Papaya Juice - Made fresh upon request, Papaya Juice is an invigorating beverage that is a perfect pairing with your meals.

Where to Stay in Neil Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pearl Park Beach Resort - This ultra-modern architectural marvel offers luxurious accommodation with beachfront access, providing stunning views of the Bay of Bengal.

  2. Blue Planet Homestay - Offering cozy, comfortable and affordable accommodation, the Blue Planet Homestay is an ideal option for budget travelers.

  3. Sea Shell - A luxury resort overlooking the Laxmanpur Beach, created keeping in mind the needs of those who are seeking comfort in immersing themselves in nature’s lap.

What to Buy in Neil Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Jewellery - Neil Island’s unique jewellery designs reflect the island’s diverse culture and range from ethnic to western styles.

  2. Coconut Shell Handicrafts - The coconut shells from the coconut trees that cover the island are transformed into intricately designed handicrafts that can be easily carried back as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Neil Island

The locals of Neil Island are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, deeply rooted in a laid-back lifestyle. Agriculture and tourism are the main economic activities on the island, which have influenced the culture’s practices and customs. The predominant language spoken in the islands’ Hindi while English is widely spoken and understood as well.

Best Time to Visit Neil Island

The best time to visit Neil Island is during the winter (Oct - Feb). The weather is pleasant and mild with a temperature ranging between 20°C to 28°C. Monsoon is not an ideal time to visit the island as the weather can be unpredictable and can lead to the isolation of the island from the mainland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neil Island

Do:

  1. Respect the island’s natural beauty and ensure that it remains pristine by not littering.
  2. Carry sunscreen and wear comfortable clothing as the weather can be quite humid.
  3. Try the local seafood to truly experience the island’s culture.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t disrespect the island’s religious or cultural customs.
  2. Try not to visit the island during the monsoon season as the waters can be quite rough and unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neil Island

Neil Island is generally considered safe, but visitors are advised to be cautious and avoid walking alone in isolated areas especially after dark. Always check with the weather reports before planning to partake in any water sports activities.

Health in Neil Island

As the island has limited medical facilities, visitors are advised to carry their first aid and basic medical supplies during their visit. Also, it’s recommended that you carry sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and anti-allergic medication, especially if you are prone to allergies.

Getting to Neil Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neil Island

The nearest airport to Neil Island is at Veer Savarkar International Airport (IXZ) in Port Blair, which is approximately 30 km away from the island. From Port Blair, you can take a ferry to Neil Island which takes approximately 2 hours.

Bus Stations Near Neil Island

As Neil Island is a small island, buses don’t operate on the island. Visitors can rent bicycles or hire auto-rickshaws to explore the island.

How to Get Around in Neil Island

Bicycles, Scooters, and Auto-rickshaws are available for rent in Neil Island to make sure that visitors can easily get around to explore the island’s best offerings. Walking is also an excellent alternative, considering the island is relatively small and the weather remains pleasant.

Where to Go Next from Neil Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Neil Island is undoubtedly a paradise, there are tons of destinations in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands that are worth exploring. Havelock Island, Baratang Island and Radha Nagar Beach are easily accessible destinations in close proximity to Neil Island.

Conclusion

Neil Island is a great destination for those who are looking for adventure, relaxation, and a unique cultural experience. With its stunning beaches, historic and cultural landmarks, and a variety of activities to enjoy, a trip to Neil Island is definitely worth your while. Plan your itinerary in advance to make the most of your trip to this tropical paradise.
