Introduction

South Andaman, located in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, is a tropical paradise in Southern India. With its stunning beaches, lush green forests, and rich cultural heritage, it is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an unforgettable experience.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs, as well as practical information on getting there, safety, and health.

List of Places to Visit in South Andaman

South Andaman is home to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes and culturally significant landmarks in India. Here are some must-see places to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Anthropological Museum: This museum showcases the indigenous tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, including their culture, way of life, and history.

  • Cellular Jail: The infamous Cellular Jail was used by the British to imprison Indian freedom fighters. Today, it serves as a memorial to their struggle and legacy.

  • Samudrika Marine Museum: This museum has exhibits on the region’s marine life, ecology, and naval history.

Natural Attractions of South Andaman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Radhanagar Beach: Dubbed “Asia’s Best Beach” by Time magazine, Radhanagar is a picturesque strip of white sand and turquoise waters. It’s perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

  • Ross Island: A former British settlement, Ross Island is now a ghost town with remnants of colonial architecture and a rich heritage.

  • Mount Harriet National Park: Trekking through the tropical rainforest of Mount Harriet National Park is an adventure of a lifetime. The park is home to rare flora and fauna, as well as stunning views of the Andaman Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Andaman

South Andaman’s rugged terrain and lush forests make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular trails include:

  • Chidiya Tapu to Mount Harriet: This 16 km trail winds through the forests and hills of South Andaman, offering spectacular views of the sea and the landscape.

  • Wandoor to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: This 25 km trail is ideal for birdwatching and nature lovers. The trail passes through the forest and mangrove areas to reach the marine park.

Best Things to Do in South Andaman: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities and tours to choose from in South Andaman. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling: The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are popular diving spots, with underwater coral reefs, colorful marine life, and shipwrecks to explore.

  • Island hopping: South Andaman is surrounded by smaller islands, each with its own unique charm. Hop on a boat tour to discover hidden beaches, lagoons, and coves.

  • Seafood feasts: South Andaman’s coastal location means that seafood is a staple of the local cuisine. Try some of the freshest catches of squid, prawns, crabs, and lobsters at one of the many beachside shacks and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Andaman

To make the most of your time in South Andaman, we’ve put together a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Port Blair and visit the Anthropological Museum.

Day 2: Visit the Cellular Jail and Ross Island.

Day 3: Trek through Mount Harriet National Park.

Day 4: Take a boat tour to the nearby islands, such as Jolly Buoy, Red Skin, or Elephant Beach.

Day 5: Spend the day on Radhanagar Beach, diving into the clear waters.

Day 6: Explore the Wandoor to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park trail.

Day 7: End your trip with a seafood feast and a stroll by the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of South Andaman

South Andaman’s local cuisine is a mix of Indian, Chinese, and Southeast Asian flavors, with seafood taking center stage. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish curry: A spicy and tangy curry made with fresh fish, tomatoes, onions, and local herbs and spices.

  • Lobster thermidor: A creamy and cheesy baked lobster dish, perfected by the chefs of South Andaman.

  • Prawn coconut curry: A mild and creamy curry made with fresh prawns and grated coconut.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water: Sip on fresh coconut water, widely available throughout South Andaman, to beat the tropical heat.

  • Local beer: Try the local beer, Andaman Gold, brewed with locally sourced ingredients and a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in South Andaman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Andaman offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Seahorse Resort: Located on Radhanagar Beach, this resort offers luxurious villas with stunning sea views.

  • Blue Bird Resort: A budget-friendly option in Port Blair, with comfortable rooms and easy access to the city’s attractions.

  • Hornbill Nest Guest House: A charming guesthouse in Wandoor, surrounded by coconut groves and tranquility.

What to Buy in South Andaman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to take back home, here are some of the best options:

  • Pearl jewelry: South Andaman is famous for its lustrous pearls, which are handcrafted into stunning jewelry.

  • Wood carvings: Local artisans carve intricate designs on wood, such as boxes, coasters, and souvenirs.

  • Spices and tea: South Andaman produces some of the best spices and tea in India, so don’t forget to take home some of these fragrant delights.

Local Culture and Customs of South Andaman

South Andaman is home to many indigenous tribes, and visitors are welcome to explore their culture and way of life. However, it is important to respect their customs and traditions, such as avoiding photography in certain areas and seeking permission before entering their villages.

Best Time to Visit South Andaman

The best time to visit South Andaman is between October and May, when the weather is dry and the sea is calm. The monsoon season, from June to September, brings heavy rainfall and rough sea conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Andaman

To ensure a safe and enjoyable trip, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Carry sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the harsh sun.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.

  • Enter restricted areas or tribal reserves without permission.

  • Engage in any form of wildlife poaching or trading.

Safety and Health

South Andaman is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy:

Safety in South Andaman

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, particularly in crowded places.

  • Follow any safety instructions provided by the activity operator for water sports and similar activities.

Health in South Andaman

  • Take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue and malaria, by using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing in the evening.

  • Stay hydrated and carry a bottle of water with you at all times.

  • Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to South Andaman: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Andaman is accessible by air and sea. Here are some of the best options for getting there:

Airports Near South Andaman

  • Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair has flights from major Indian cities such as Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, and Kolkata.

Bus Stations Near South Andaman

  • There are no bus stations in South Andaman, but Port Blair has a well-connected bus network.

Other Options

  • Ferries and cruise ships: South Andaman is accessible by ferry or cruise ship from mainland India and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in South Andaman

Taxis and shared jeeps are the most popular mode of transportation in South Andaman. However, renting a scooter or bicycle is an excellent way to explore the island at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from South Andaman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Andaman is just one part of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and there are many other destinations worth exploring in this tropical paradise. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Neil Island: Home to stunning coral reefs, secluded beaches, and lush vegetation.

  • Havelock Island: Known for its diving spots and adventure sports, such as kayaking and parasailing.

  • Baratang Island: Famous for its limestone caves and mangrove forests.

Conclusion

South Andaman is a true gem in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful region. Happy travels!
