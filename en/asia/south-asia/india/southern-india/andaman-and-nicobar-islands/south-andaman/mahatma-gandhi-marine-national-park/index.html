Introduction to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is a beautiful protected area that covers a large part of the South Andaman Island and was created to preserve the unique marine wildlife in the area. The park is named after Mahatma Gandhi, a prominent freedom fighter in India. It is a popular tourist destination and attracts visitors from all over the world who come to enjoy its unspoiled natural beauty and scenic landscapes. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the major highlights of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park, including the best places to visit, the top things to do, local culture and customs, safety and health advice, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park offers a variety of places to visit that will cater to everyone’s preferences. Below are some of the top places to visit in the park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Anthropological Museum: A museum that displays the history and culture of the indigenous tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  2. Cellular Jail: A historical prison that was used by the British to incarcerate freedom fighters in India.
  3. Forest Museum: A museum that displays the flora and fauna of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Natural Attractions of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jolly Buoy Island: A popular island in the park that offers a stunning coral reef and crystal-clear waters, perfect for snorkeling and scuba diving.
  2. Red Skin Island: Another island in the park that offers spectacular coral reefs, pristine beaches, and lush green forests.
  3. Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: The national park itself offers amazing coral reefs and marine life, as well as stunning beaches and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park offers several hiking and biking routes to explore the natural beauty of the park. Some of the best routes include:

  1. Wandoor Beach to Jolly Buoy Island: A popular route that offers stunning views of the coral reefs, crystal-clear waters, and lush green vegetation.
  2. Munda Pahar Beach to Red Skin Island: A less-crowded route that offers secluded white sand beaches, wild forests, and spectacular coral reefs.
  3. Mount Harriet to Madhuban: A scenic route that offers panoramic views of the Andaman Sea, the surrounding hills, and the dense forests.

Best Things to Do in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Activities and Tours

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit everyone’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Experience the stunning coral reefs and marine life of Jolly Buoy Island and Red Skin Island.
  2. Glass Bottom Boat Rides: Explore the marine life of the park from the comfort of a glass-bottomed boat.
  3. Trekking and Camping: Explore the dense forests and rugged terrain of the park on foot, and camp overnight in the wilderness.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Here’s a sample itinerary that covers the best of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park:

Day 1: Explore Port Blair and visit the Anthropological Museum.

Day 2: Visit the Cellular Jail and attend the evening light and sound show.

Day 3: Visit Wandoor Beach and take a glass-bottomed boat ride to Jolly Buoy Island.

Day 4: Hike from Munda Pahar Beach to Red Skin Island, and spend the day snorkeling and relaxing on the beach.

Day 5: Take a ferry to Havelock Island and spend the day exploring its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush green forests.

Day 6: Trek to Madhuban and camp overnight in the wilderness.

Day 7: Visit Mount Harriet and enjoy panoramic views of the Andaman Sea and the surrounding hills.

Food and Drink Scene of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park offers a variety of local dishes and beverages to try out. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the park.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish Curry: A spicy and flavorful dish made with fresh seafood and a variety of herbs and spices.
  2. Chicken Tikka: Grilled chicken marinated in a variety of spices and served with a tangy tomato sauce.
  3. Chole Bhature: A popular North Indian dish made with chickpeas and served with deep-fried bread.

Local Beverages

  1. Coconut Water: A refreshing and hydrating drink made from fresh coconuts.
  2. Aam Panna: A tangy and sweet drink made from raw mangoes and spices.
  3. Lassi: A creamy and thick yogurt-based drink that comes in a variety of flavors.

Where to Stay in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in the park include:

  1. Wild Grass Resort: A luxury resort located in the heart of the national park that offers comfortable rooms, delicious food, and stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  2. Symphony Palms Beach Resort: A mid-range resort located near Wandoor Beach that offers clean and comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant that serves local and international cuisine.
  3. Backpacker Panda: A budget hostel that offers basic accommodation and facilities, including free Wi-Fi and a common kitchen.

What to Buy in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park offers a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  1. Handmade Crafts: You can find a variety of handmade crafts, including bags, jewelry, and sculptures, made by the local tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  2. Sea Shells: The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are known for their beautiful and unique sea shells, which make great souvenirs.
  3. Coconut Oil: You can find high-quality coconut oil made from fresh coconuts in the local markets and bring it home as a healthy and natural souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is located in a region with a rich and diverse cultural heritage. The local tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands have a unique culture and customs that are worth experiencing. Some of the local customs and traditions of the region include:

  1. Dance and Music: The local tribes are known for their traditional dance and music performances that reflect their culture and way of life.
  2. Tattooing: Tattooing is a traditional practice among the local tribes, and many tourists are inclined to try it during their visit.
  3. Traditional Crafts: The local tribes are skilled at creating beautiful and unique handicrafts, which make great souvenirs and gifts.

Best Time to Visit Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

The best time to visit Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is the winter season, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and dry. The monsoon season, from June to September, can be extremely rainy and humid, making it difficult to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  2. Stay on designated trails and follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  3. Carry sufficient water and snacks while trekking and hiking.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave plastic waste in the park or on the beaches.
  2. Disturb or harm the marine life or wildlife in the park.
  3. Smoke or light fires in the park or on the beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

  1. Be aware of the tides and currents when swimming, snorkeling, or diving in the park.
  2. Wear appropriate protective gear, such as life jackets and helmets, when participating in outdoor activities.
  3. Avoid wandering off the designated park boundaries or camping in the wilderness without permission.

Health in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

  1. Drink only bottled water or sterilized water to avoid water-borne diseases.
  2. Carry a sufficient supply of mosquito repellent and sunscreens to protect against insect and sunburn.

Getting to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is well-connected to major cities in India and the rest of the world. The park can be reached via air, road, and sea.

Airports Near Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

  1. Veer Savarkar International Airport, Port Blair: The nearest airport to the park, located about 25 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

  1. Port Blair Bus Stand: Regular buses from Port Blair are available to reach the park.

Other Options

  1. Ferries: Regular ferries are available from Port Blair to Wandoor Jetty, which is the entry point to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park.

How to Get Around in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park can be explored on foot, by bicycle, or by hiring a taxi or boat. Most of the places in the park are easily accessible via foot or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is located in a region that has a lot to offer. Below are some of the nearby destinations that are worth exploring:

  1. Ross Island: A beautiful island that was once a British administrative center, located near Port Blair.
  2. Baratang Island: A unique island that is home to the world-famous limestone caves, mangrove forests, and mud volcanoes.
  3. Diglipur: A remote destination that is known for its serene beaches, waterfalls, and the famous Saddle Peak.

Conclusion

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is a beautiful and serene destination that is a must-visit for all nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park’s unspoiled natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse marine life make it one of the most unique and fascinating destinations in India. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide has given you a good idea of what to expect in Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park and how to plan your visit to this beautiful destination.
