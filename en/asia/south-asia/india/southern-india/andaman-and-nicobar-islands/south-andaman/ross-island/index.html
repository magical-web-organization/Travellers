Introduction to Ross Island

One of the lesser-known destinations in India, Ross Island is a hidden gem in the South Andaman district of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Once the administrative headquarters of the British colony, Ross Island is now a popular tourist spot that boasts of scenic views, rich history, and a unique culture. Ross Island is a car-free zone, which makes it even more attractive to tourists who are looking for a peaceful and serene getaway from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Ross Island

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Ross Island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Ross Island Museum

The Ross Island Museum is the main attraction of the island and a must-visit for all tourists. The museum showcases the rich history and culture of Ross Island and its association with the British Raj.

2. Japanese Bunkers

During World War II, the Japanese had invaded the Andaman and Nicobar Islands and built bunkers around Ross Island for their army. These bunkers remain an important reminder of the island’s war history.

3. Protestant Church

Built by the British in 1858, the Protestant Church is the oldest structure on Ross Island. It was used for religious ceremonies and remains an important symbol of the island’s past.

Natural Attractions of Ross Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. North Bay Beach

Located on the northern tip of Ross Island, North Bay Beach is a pristine white sandy beach that offers stunning views of the island’s coastline. Snorkeling and scuba diving are popular activities here.

2. Smritika Museum

Located in the ruins of the British Commissioner’s House, Smritika Museum is a popular tourist spot that showcases the island’s flora and fauna. It is also an ideal place to enjoy the scenic views of the island.

3. Mount Harriet National Park

Located on the eastern side of Ross Island, Mount Harriet National Park is a paradise for nature lovers. The park is home to a variety of flora and fauna, and the trek to the peak of Mt. Harriet is a popular activity for adventure seekers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ross Island

Hiking Routes

1. North Bay Trail

The North Bay Trail is a scenic hiking route that takes you through dense forests and past picturesque beaches. The trail is approximately 2 kilometers long and takes around 2 hours to complete.

2. Munda Pahar Trail

The Munda Pahar Trail is a challenging hiking route that takes you to the highest point on Ross Island, Munda Pahar. The trail is approximately 5 kilometers long and takes around 3-4 hours to complete.

Biking Routes

1. Ross Island Loop

The Ross Island Loop is a popular biking route that takes you around the entire island. The route is approximately 6 kilometers long and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Ross Island: Activities and Tours

Here are the top things to do in Ross Island:

1. Scuba Diving and Snorkeling

Ross Island has several world-class diving locations that offer breathtaking views of the underwater world. Lighthouse, Aquarium, and North Bay are some of the popular diving spots.

2. Sea-walking

Sea-walking is a unique activity that lets you explore the underwater world without the need for scuba diving equipment. It is a hassle-free way to enjoy the wonders of the ocean.

3. Light and Sound Show

The light and sound show at Ross Island is a must-see attraction that showcases the island’s history and culture through a mesmerizing audio-visual experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Ross Island

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fish Curry

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are known for their seafood, and fish curry is a must-try dish for all tourists. The dish is made with locally caught fish and spices, and is served with rice.

2. Lobster

Freshly caught lobster is a culinary delight that is available at most restaurants on Ross Island. It is usually grilled or steamed and served with garlic butter.

Local Beverages

1. Tender Coconut Water

Tender coconut water is a refreshing drink that is easily available on Ross Island. It is a natural thirst quencher that is packed with essential minerals and vitamins.

2. Solkadi

Solkadi is a traditional drink made with coconut milk and kokum, a fruit that is native to the coastal regions of India. It is a tangy and refreshing beverage that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Ross Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ross Island has limited accommodation options, so it is recommended that you book your stay in advance. Here are some of the popular options:

1. Sea Princess Beach Resort

The Sea Princess Beach Resort is a popular luxury resort that offers stunning views of the beach and the ocean. It has several amenities like an outdoor pool, spa, and restaurant.

2. Peerless Resort

The Peerless Resort is a mid-range hotel that is located close to the Ross Island ferry jetty. The hotel has comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

3. Ross Island Retreat

The Ross Island Retreat is a budget guesthouse that is located on the island. It is a good option for backpackers and budget travelers who are looking for affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Ross Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Ross Island:

1. Sea Shells

Sea shells are a popular souvenir that you can buy from the local markets on Ross Island. They come in various shapes and sizes and make for a unique decorative item.

2. Handicrafts

The local markets on Ross Island have a variety of handicrafts that are made by the indigenous tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. These handicrafts make for a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Ross Island

The people of Ross Island are proud of their unique culture and traditions. The island is home to several indigenous tribes, and visitors are expected to respect their customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Ross Island

The best time to visit Ross Island is from October to May when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season from June to September should be avoided as it can disrupt transportation and other activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ross Island

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry a good quality sunscreen and apply it frequently.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island or in the ocean.
  • Use plastic bags or bottles.
  • Attempt to feed the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ross Island

Ross Island is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is recommended that you take the necessary precautions while participating in adventure activities like diving and trekking.

Health in Ross Island

Ross Island has a limited healthcare infrastructure, so it is recommended that you carry a basic first-aid kit and any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Ross Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ross Island can be accessed by boat from Port Blair. Here are the various transportation options:

Airports Near Ross Island

The nearest airport is Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair.

Bus Stations Near Ross Island

There is no bus service on Ross Island.

Other Options

You can book a ferry or a private boat from Port Blair to Ross Island.

How to Get Around in Ross Island

Ross Island is a car-free zone, so the only way to get around is by foot or by bicycle. Bicycles are easily available for rent on the island.

Where to Go Next from Ross Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Ross Island:

1. Havelock Island

Havelock Island is a popular destination that is known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters. It can be reached by ferry from Port Blair.

2. Neil Island

Neil Island is a small island that is located south of Ross Island. It is known for its quiet and secluded beaches.

Conclusion

Ross Island is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. This travel guide has covered everything from must-visit attractions to transportation options, making it the perfect companion for your trip to Ross Island. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore this culturally rich and scenic island.
