Introduction to Long Island

Nestled amidst the Andaman Sea, Long Island boasts breathtaking landscapes, crystal clear waters, and a vibrant local culture, providing an exciting destination for travelers seeking unique and enriching experiences. With pristine beaches, tropical rainforests, and a plethora of outdoor activities, Long Island has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Long Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Revisit Long Island’s rich history and cultural heritage at various cultural attractions like the Anthropological Museum, the Cellular Jail, and the Science Centre.

The Anthropological Museum showcases the tribes of Andaman and Nicobar Islands with a collection of their artifacts that include handicrafts, old photographs, tribal weaponry, musical instruments, and much more.

Apart from this, the Cellular Jail is a former colonial prison that was used to incarcerate Indian freedom fighters during the British reign of India. It now stands as a testament to Indian struggle for independence.

The Science Centre is a fantastic place for science buffs with its interactive exhibits on various principles of science.

Natural Attractions of Long Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts can satisfy their cravings with the island’s diverse range of landscapes, covering tropical rainforests, hidden waterfalls, stunning lagoons, and serene beaches. Explore Silversand Beach, Long Beach, and Laxmanpur Beach, where you can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and other water activities. Trekking lovers can also explore the deep virgin forests of Long Island and admire the beauty of the local flora and fauna.

The breathtaking views from Mount Harriet National Park and Ramnagar Beach will make any scenic lover’s trip worthwhile.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Island

The hiking and biking trails of Long Island can inspire hikers with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. The most popular trails include:

  1. Mount Harriet National Park Trail: a 16 km hike through dense forests and steep hills
  2. Lalaji Bay Trail: a 7 km trek through picturesque landscapes and a walkway made of concrete blocks
  3. Long Island Trail: a 6 km trail that passes through the enchanting forest and locales like Blue Bay Beach

Best Things to Do in Long Island: Activities and Tours

Long Island offers various activities and tours that will make your stay unforgettable. Some of these activities include:

  1. Camping and Bonfires: Enjoy an overnight camping trip on the beach with your friends or family.
  2. Snorkeling: Explore the stunning marine life of Long Island’s coral reefs with a diving trip.
  3. Island hopping: Discover the nearby islands, each with its own unique beauty, on a boat trip.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Island

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to explore Long Island to the fullest:

Day 1: Anthropological Museum and Cellular Jail

Visit the Anthropological Museum and learn about the tribes of Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Later, visit the Cellular Jail and discover the long-history of Indian freedom fighters in British colonial times.

Day 2: Hiking and Biking Trails

Explore the trails on Long Island, trek down the dense forest of Mount Harriet National Park, and bike to Lalaji Bay Beach.

Day 3: Scenic Views

Take a trip to Ramnagar Beach and catch the stunning sunrise, and then visit Vijaynagar Beach and admire its magnificent natural beauty.

Day 4: Camping and Bonfire Night

Get ready to experience an overnight camping trip on the beach with your loved ones, followed by a bonfire party.

Day 5: Snorkeling and Beach Hopping

Discover the stunning marine life of Long Island’s coral reefs with a diving trip. After that, head to the nearby Havelock Island and enjoy its gorgeous beaches and turquoise waters.

Day 6: Island Hopping and Boat Tours

Get on a boat trip and explore the nearby islands of Andaman and Nicobar.

Day 7: Local Life and Culture

Embrace the local life of Long Island by visiting Samudrika Museum, which showcases the local handicrafts and culture of the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Island

Must-Try Dishes

Seafood lovers can enjoy delicacies such as Fish Curry, Crab Curry, Lobster Curry, and Prawn Curry, prepared with traditional spices and local ingredients. Vegetarians can try the unique and flavorful regional dish, Kaju Curry, made with cashew nuts and creamy coconut jaggery.

Local Beverages

Relax and quench your thirst with local beverages like coconut water, coconut milk, and refreshing Aam Panna.

Where to Stay in Long Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Island has a variety of affordable accommodations, including hostels, guesthouses, and hotels to suit any budget. Kaalaaplanet Homestay, Tango Beach Resort, and White Coral are some of the most popular options available.

What to Buy in Long Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Long Island’s local culture and buy handmade artifacts such as wall hangings, shell ornaments, and carved coconut shells from the Aryamitra Handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Island

Traditionally, the locals of Long Island depend on fishing and agriculture for their livelihoods. They have managed to maintain their simple lifestyle and traditional customs and rituals. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect their way of living when visiting local markets and residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Long Island

October to May is the best time to visit Long Island as the weather is warm and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Island

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and necessary medical supplies for outdoor activities.
  • Book activities in advance.

Don’t:

  • Carry plastic bags and bottles on beaches as it can harm the environment.
  • Litter or damage any historical monuments or sites.
  • Buy any souvenir made of coral or seashells.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Island

Long Island is relatively safe. However, travelers are advised to follow usual safety precautions like wearing a mask and follow social distancing norms.

Health in Long Island

Mosquito-borne diseases are common in Long Island, so carry mosquito repellent and use it frequently. It is best to consult a doctor before traveling and get vaccines as per their advice.

Getting to Long Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Long Island can only be accessed via ship or ferry from Port Blair, the capital city of Andaman and Nicobar Islands, which is connected by air and sea to India’s mainland.

Airports Near Long Island

Port Blair’s Veer Savarkar International Airport is the nearest airport, connecting Long Island to other major Indian cities, including Chennai, Kolkata, and Delhi.

Bus Stations Near Long Island

There are no bus stations on Long Island. However, State-run buses, private taxis, and autos can take you to the ferry docks, which provide global connectivity to other islands of Andaman and Nicobar.

Other Options

Helicopter rides from Port Blair are also offered for those who prefer aerial views of the stunning island paradise.

How to Get Around in Long Island

Long Island is a small island; you can explore it on foot or by hiring a bike from one of the many rental shops on the island.

Where to Go Next from Long Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations that can be explored from Long Island are Havelock Island, Ross Island, and Neil Island.

Havelock Island is known for its beautiful Radhanagar Beach, and Ross Island is famous for its historic ruins dating back to the British era in India. Neil Island is a serene and picturesque landscape, perfect for those looking to relax.

Conclusion

Long Island, with its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, offers visitors a chance to explore a unique and enchanting paradise. With a variety of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local delicacies, Long Island is an ideal destination for travelers of all tastes and interests.
