Introduction to Diglipur

Diglipur is a town in the North and Middle Andaman district of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands in Southern India. Not as well-known as its southern counterparts, Diglipur is an unexplored gem. With its lush green forests, crystal-clear beaches, and scenic vistas, it offers a mystical experience of nature in its purest form. Here’s everything you need to know about visiting Diglipur.

List of Places to Visit in Diglipur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kalipur Beach: Located on the northern coast of Diglipur, this beach offers a blend of history and mythology. It was here where the first British astronomer Sir William Jones made astronomical observations in 1788, and it’s also where Lord Rama is said to have taken a dip in the sea.

  • Ross and Smith Islands: These twin islands are connected by a natural, white-sand bridge that emerges only at low tide. The water surrounding the islands is crystal clear, making it perfect for snorkeling and scuba diving.

  • Mud Volcano: Saddle Peak, the highest point of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, stands near the Diglipur-Mayanagri road. On the way to Saddle Peak, you will come across mud volcanoes, which are natural wonders, that unlike traditional fiery volcanoes, spew out mud and gas.

Natural Attractions of Diglipur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saddle Peak National Park: If you’re a nature lover, you’ll be in awe of the wide variety of flora and fauna in the Saddle Peak National Park. The park boasts of over 200 species of birds, reptiles, and mammals.

  • Lamiya Bay Beach: Surrounded by lush tropical forests, this beach is a perfect place to enjoy the natural beauty of Diglipur. The long shoreline is practically deserted, providing a serene environment for relaxation.

  • Kala Pathar Beach: With black sand and towering greenery, Kala Pathar is one of the most scenic beaches in Diglipur. The trek through the jungle to reach the beach provides an experience of solitude and tranquility.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Diglipur

  • Ramnagar Beach to Kalipur Beach: This 9-km walking trail spans the western coast of Diglipur and offers an excellent opportunity to enjoy the pristine surroundings.

  • Saddle Peak Trek: This trek leads up to the highest point on the island, Saddle Peak, and offers an opportunity to enjoy the stunning vista that stretches out to the Bay of Bengal.

Best Things to Do in Diglipur: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving at Ross and Smith Islands: The sea surrounding Ross and Smith Islands is a rich coral reef ecosystem that is perfect for underwater exploration.

  • Fishing in the Kalpong River: The river Kalpong has some serene spots perfect for fishing or just relaxing by the river bank.

Week-Long Itinerary for Diglipur

Day Activities
1 Hike from Ramnagar Beach to Kalipur Beach.
2 Trek along the Saddle Peak.
3 Visit Ross and Smith Islands for snorkeling and scuba diving.
4 Visit to Mud Volcano and Lamiya Bay Beach.
5 Explore the Saddle Peak National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Diglipur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Curry: Being one of the main ingredients around Diglipur, the fish curry is an absolute must-try for all food enthusiasts.

  • Seafood Platter: Comprising of a variety of dishes made from fish, prawn, crab, and other seafood, the seafood platter offers the best of seafood cuisine the region has to offer.

Local Beverages

  • Tender coconut water: Diglipur is famous for its tender coconut water, and it offers a refreshing drink that is ideal for the warm climate of the region.

  • Andaman Palm Wine: This locally brewed drink is made from the sap of the wild date palm tree and has an enviable taste.

Where to Stay in Diglipur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Turtle Resort: Located close to the beach, Turtle Resort offers comfortable rooms and a serene environment to unwind.

  • Pristine Beach Resort: Nestled between lush greenery and amid sparkling white sand, this resort is a perfect spot for nature lovers.

What to Buy in Diglipur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shell Crafts: With an abundance of seashells available in the region, you can find a multitude of shell-based souvenirs produced by the locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Diglipur

The locals of Diglipur primarily belong to the Tamil and Bengali communities. They lead a simple life and are known for their friendliness and hospitality. The people of Diglipur believe in preserving their cultural heritage while leading a more modern lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Diglipur

The best time to visit Diglipur is between November and May as the weather is pleasant and conducive to outdoor activities. June to September is the monsoon season and is best avoided as travel may be challenging due to heavy rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Diglipur

Do:

  • Respect the beliefs and customs of the locals.

  • Ensure that you carry sufficient cash as ATMs may not be readily available.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or forests.

  • Wear immodest clothing while visiting temples or other religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Diglipur

While Diglipur is considered safe for tourists, it’s still essential to exercise caution to avoid any incidents. Ensure that you have the necessary permits before venturing into protected areas, and make sure to keep an eye on your valuables at all times.

Health in Diglipur

It’s advisable to carry medications for common ailments, such as cold, fever, and stomach issues, as there may be limited medical facilities outside the town. It’s equally essential to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen to prevent sunburn.

Getting to Diglipur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Diglipur

The nearest airport to Diglipur is the Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair, which is well connected with major Indian cities.

Bus Stations Near Diglipur

There are no direct bus services available to Diglipur. However, regular buses operate between Port Blair and Mayabunder or Rangat, and you can avail yourself of these to reach Diglipur.

Other Options

Another option is to take a ferry from Port Blair to Diglipur, which is convenient and provides a beautiful view of the sea.

How to Get Around in Diglipur

Auto rickshaws, rented bikes or bicycles are the primary methods of transportation available in Diglipur, making it easy to navigate the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Diglipur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ross Island: The former British administrative headquarters provides an insightful glimpse into the colonial era of India.

  • Port Blair: The capital city of Andaman and Nicobar Islands, offering a bustling city life amidst spectacular natural surroundings.

Conclusion

Diglipur, with its natural beauty and serene ambiance, offers a unique travel experience that’s hard to find elsewhere. Plan your visit to Diglipur with this comprehensive guide, and explore one of the rare unexplored gems that Southern India has to offer.
