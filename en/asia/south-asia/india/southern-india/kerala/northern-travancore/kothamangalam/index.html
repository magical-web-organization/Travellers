Introduction to Kothamangalam

Nestled in the picturesque valley of the Western Ghats, Kothamangalam is a charming town in Kerala, India. Known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventurous spirit, Kothamangalam has something for everyone.

Whether you are interested in exploring museums and historical landmarks, immersing yourself in the local culture, or embarking on outdoor adventures, Kothamangalam is the perfect destination.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, must-try dishes, where to stay, local customs, transportation options, and nearby destinations to help you plan your perfect trip to Kothamangalam.

List of Places to Visit in Kothamangalam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mar Thoma Pontifical Shrine: A revered pilgrimage center dedicated to St. Thomas, one of the apostles of Jesus Christ.
  • Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary: A protected area that harbors a wide variety of bird species, including Painted Storks, Little Egrets, Purple Herons, and Black-crowned Night Herons.
  • Sree Poornathrayeesa Temple: A famous pilgrim center for devotees of Lord Vishnu, famous for its traditional architecture and grand festivals.
  • Hill Palace Museum: A stunning museum that showcases the royal heritage of the Kochi Kingdom through an impressive collection of artifacts and antiques.

Natural Attractions of Kothamangalam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thattekad Bird Sanctuary: A haven for bird-watching enthusiasts, housing over 500 different bird species, including endemics like the Malabar Grey Hornbill and Nilgiri Wood Pigeon.
  • Kadamattom Church: An ancient church known for its intricate carvings that depict biblical stories and scenes from the life of St. Thomas.
  • Bhoothathankettu Dam: A scenic spot where the Periyar River flows through an array of hills and picturesque landscapes.
  • Idamalayar Dam: A popular picnic spot and a gateway to the vast Idamalayar Wildlife Sanctuary, known for its rich flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kothamangalam

  • Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary: A beautiful area for hiking that features high-altitude wildlife habitats, waterfalls, and scenic views of the neighboring villages.
  • Munnar: A picturesque hill station that offers many trekking and cycling trails that pass through rolling tea gardens, dense forests, and waterfalls.
  • Vagamon: A serene and peaceful hill station with breathtaking views, numerous trekking trails, and opportunities for paragliding, rock climbing, and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Kothamangalam: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Cultural Heritage of Kothamangalam: Discover the town’s religious and historical landmarks on guided tours that lead you through the town’s traditional streets and beautiful architecture.
  • Rafting in Periyar River: Take a thrilling tour of the Periyar River that provides adventure packed on with beautiful scenery and a chance to glimpse the wildlife of the surrounding forests.
  • Bird-Watching Tours: Witness some of the beautiful and indigenous species of birds that are found in the Bird Sanctuaries of Kothamangalam
  • Take Cooking Classes: Relish in traditional preparations of Kerala cuisine and learn the techniques to make some of the local delicacies.

Food and Drink Scene of Kothamangalam

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu: Made with steamed rice flour and coconut shavings, this dish is typically eaten for breakfast with mashed plantains or curry.
  • Kerala Style Fish Curry: A popular local dish made with fish, coconut milk, and a blend of spices.
  • Appam: A soft and spongy pancake made using fermented rice batter that is typically served with vegetable or chicken stew.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: A traditional local alcoholic beverage made using fermented coconut flower sap.
  • Tea: Munnar is famous for its tea gardens, so take a sip of the freshly brewed tea that the region offers.

Where to Stay in Kothamangalam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

  • Green Valley Vista: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Kothamangalam, perfect for tourists who want to experience local culture and amenities.
  • Nature Zone Resort: A serene and eco-friendly resort located in Munnar, an ideal getaway for travelers who want to relax and rejuvenate amidst stunning natural surroundings.

Hostels

  • Zostel Munnar: A social and traveler-friendly hostel located in the heart of Munnar, offering comfortable accommodation and a lively atmosphere conducive to making new friends
  • GoStops Munnar: A backpacker-friendly hostel located in Munnar, featuring spacious dormitories and private rooms with fantastic views of the surrounding mountains.

Guesthouses

  • Kakkadampoyilil Homestay: A homestay located in a peaceful village, nestled amidst serene greenery and beautiful waterfalls, offering an authentic cultural experience.
  • Sun Mount View Guest House: Located in Munnar, this guesthouse offers stunning views of the tea gardens, comfortable accommodations, and traditional hospitality

What to Buy in Kothamangalam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Spices: Kothamangalam is known for its spice gardens, and you can find many authentic, flavorful local spices here.
  • Handicrafts: You can purchase traditional handicrafts, such as ornate wooden furniture, pottery, and embroidered fabrics.

Local Culture and Customs of Kothamangalam

Kothamangalam is known for its cultural heritage and local customs. Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites, and it’s customary to remove footwear before entering such places.

Best Time to Visit Kothamangalam

The best time to visit Kothamangalam is from September to March when the temperatures remain pleasant, and the weather is dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kothamangalam

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, including the famous Kerala-style fish curry.
  • Be careful during your outdoor adventures and follow the park ranger’s instructions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local religious customs and traditions.
  • Dress inappropriately while visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat during your outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kothamangalam

Kothamangalam is a safe place for tourists, but make sure to take proper precautions before embarking on outdoor adventures.

Health in Kothamangalam

Visitors should always carry a first-aid kit and insect repellent while traveling to Kothamangalam. It is recommended to drink only bottled water.

Getting to Kothamangalam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kothamangalam

  • Cochin International Airport: The nearest airport to Kothamangalam, approximately 25 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kothamangalam

  • Kothamangalam Bus Station: The town has a central bus station that offers regular services to Cochin, Munnar, and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Taxi or private car rental is a viable option for those who value added comfort and privacy.

How to Get Around in Kothamangalam

Kothamangalam is a small town where getting around is easy. You can rent a bike or car to explore the area on your own. However, it is advisable to take a guided tour of the region, given the various challenging terrains.

Where to Go Next from Kothamangalam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Munnar: A beautiful and tranquil hill station known for its breathtaking scenery and tea gardens.
  • Kochi: A charming port city on the west coast of India offers stunning beaches, historical landmarks, and nightlife.
  • Kumarakom: A picturesque village located on the Vembanoor Lake offers a serene and peaceful experience on a thrilling houseboat.

Conclusion

Kothamangalam is a hidden gem in Kerala, rich with cultural heritage and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, immersing yourself in the local culture or embarking on an adventure in the great outdoors, Kothamangalam is the perfect destination. Armed with this comprehensive travel guide, all that remains is to pack your bags and get ready to experience the exquisite charm of Kothamangalam, Kerala.
