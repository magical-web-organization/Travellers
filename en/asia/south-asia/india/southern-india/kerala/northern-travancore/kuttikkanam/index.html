Introduction to Kuttikkanam

Tucked away in the hilly landscapes of Northern Travancore in Kerala, India, Kuttikkanam is a serene mountain town that offers a refreshing change of pace from the bustling cities of India. With its lush greenery, cool weather, and culturally rich attractions, Kuttikkanam has become a popular destination among tourists seeking a unique and authentic experience in India. Here’s everything you need to know about visiting Kuttikkanam.

List of Places to Visit in Kuttikkanam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kuttikkanam is a place where history and culture meet. Here are some of the top culturally important attractions in Kuttikkanam that you should not miss:

  • Valanjamkanam Waterfall: Located near Valanjamkanam, this waterfall is nestled amidst the lush green forests and offers a breathtaking view. It is a perfect place to experience the beauty of nature.
  • Amritham Ayurveda Centre: This ayurvedic center provides excellent services and treatments for people who want to detoxify their mind and body. The center is run by well-trained professionals who use holistic and traditional methods to heal people.
  • Ayyappa Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, and it is one of the most famous temples in Kuttikkanam. The temple is located on the top of a hill, and it offers an incredible view of the surrounding area.
  • Panchalimedu: Panchalimedu is a hill station that is located around 5 km from Kuttikkanam. This place is known for its natural beauty and tranquility. It is a perfect place for those who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Natural Attractions of Kuttikkanam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kuttikkanam is surrounded by hills and valleys, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking to cycling, there are plenty of outdoor activities to experience in Kuttikkanam. Here are some of the top natural attractions of Kuttikkanam:

  • Parunthumpara: Located around 8 km from Kuttikkanam, Parunthumpara is a hill station that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. The place is best known for its trekking expeditions.
  • Vagamon: Vagamon is a serene hill station located around 25 km from Kuttikkanam. The place is known for its rolling meadows, tea plantations, and pine forests. The place is perfect for nature lovers.
  • Pullumedu: Pullumedu is a picturesque hill station located around 43 km from Kuttikkanam. The place is known for its stunning beauty, and it is a perfect place for those who want to escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Kurishumala: Kurishumala, also known as the Cross Hill, is a popular destination for religious and spiritual people. The place is located around 5 km from Kuttikkanam, and it offers an incredible view of the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kuttikkanam

Kuttikkanam is an excellent place for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced hiker/biker, Kuttikkanam has a trail for you. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes of Kuttikkanam:

  • Thekaipara to Vagamon: This is a 14 km trail that will take you through the stunning forests, grasslands, and hills. The trail is moderate in difficulty level, and it is perfect for those who want to experience the beauty of nature.
  • Vagamon to Murugan Mala: This is an 8 km trail that will take you through the lush green forests and tea plantations. The trail is perfect for both hiking and cycling.
  • Olikkudy to Parunthumpara: This is a 13 km trail that will take you through the beautiful hills and valleys of Kuttikkanam. The trail is moderate in difficulty level, and it is perfect for those who want to explore the natural beauty of Kuttikkanam.

Best Things to Do in Kuttikkanam: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things you can do in Kuttikkanam. Here are some of the best things to do in Kuttikkanam:

  • Explore the Tea Plantations: Kuttikkanam is known for its tea plantations, and a visit to them is a must. The tea plantations offer a picturesque view of the green hills and valleys.
  • Visit the Local Markets: Kuttikkanam has a vibrant market scene, and a visit to the local markets is a great way to experience the local culture and buy some souvenirs.
  • Paragliding: Paragliding is a popular activity in Kuttikkanam. You can enjoy the stunning views of the valley from above and experience the thrill of flying.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kuttikkanam

Here’s an example itinerary for a week-long trip to Kuttikkanam:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Kuttikkanam and explore the town
  • Day 2: Visit Ayyappa Temple and Valanjamkanam Waterfall
  • Day 3: Hike to Panchalimedu and explore the surrounding area
  • Day 4: Visit Vagamon and explore the tea plantations
  • Day 5: Experience Paragliding and visit local markets
  • Day 6: Trek to Pullumedu and admire the panoramic views
  • Day 7: Depart Kuttikkanam

Food and Drink Scene of Kuttikkanam

Must-Try Dishes

Kerala is known for its delicious food, and Kuttikkanam is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Kuttikkanam:

  • Kerala Parotta: Kerala Parotta is a popular bread that is flaky and layered. It’s usually served with chicken or vegetable curry.
  • Kerala Biriyani: Kerala Biriyani is a rice dish that is cooked with delicious spices and meat or vegetables.
  • Appam: Appam is a round pancake-like dish that is made with fermented rice batter.

Local Beverages

Kerala is known for its unique beverages that are loved by locals and tourists alike. Here are some of the local beverages to try in Kuttikkanam:

  • Toddy: Toddy is a fermented drink that is made from the sap of the coconut palm tree.
  • Sulaimani Tea: Sulaimani Tea is a special tea that is made with a blend of spices and black tea.
  • Sharjah: Sharjah is a refreshing beverage that is made with lime, sugar, and soda.

Where to Stay in Kuttikkanam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Kuttikkanam suiting everyone’s needs and preferences. Here are some of the top-rated accommodations in Kuttikkanam:

  • Holiday Vagamon: It is a five-star resort that offers a luxurious stay with modern amenities and stunning views.
  • Green Village: It is a budget-friendly option that provides an authentic Kerala experience with comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Kuttikkanam Homestay: It is a homestay that offers an opportunity to experience the local culture and food with a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Kuttikkanam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kuttikkanam is known for its unique handicrafts, spices, tea, and handloom fabrics. Here are some of the best things to buy in Kuttikkanam:

  • Spices: Kerala is known for its spices, and Kuttikkanam has some of the best spices in the region. You can buy spices like pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon from the local markets.
  • Tea: Kuttikkanam is known for its tea plantations, and you can buy some fresh tea leaves or tea bags from the local markets.
  • Handloom Fabrics: Kuttikkanam is known for its handloom fabrics that are made by local artisans. You can buy sarees, lungis, or towels made of cotton or silk.

Local Culture and Customs of Kuttikkanam

Kerala has a rich culture and tradition, and it is essential to learn about them before visiting. Here are some of the local culture and customs of Kuttikkanam:

  • Kerala Sadya: Kerala Sadya is a traditional feast that is served on special occasions or festivals. It consists of various vegetarian dishes that are served on a plantain leaf.
  • Kathakali: Kathakali is a traditional dance form that originated in Kerala. It is a mix of drama, dance, and music, and it tells stories from Indian mythology.
  • Ayurveda: Ayurveda is a system of medicine that originated in India. Kerala is known for its Ayurvedic treatments, and there are many Ayurvedic centers in Kuttikkanam.

Best Time to Visit Kuttikkanam

The best time to visit Kuttikkanam is from September to May when the temperature is pleasant and the weather is dry. The monsoon season from June to August brings heavy rainfall and makes it difficult to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuttikkanam

Do:

  • Respect the culture and traditions of Kerala.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.

Safety and Health

Kuttikkanam is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s essential to take necessary precautions like:

  • Avoid isolated areas.
  • Be cautious when traveling at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe.

Health in Kuttikkanam

Kuttikkanam has good medical facilities, and there are many clinics and hospitals in the area. However, it’s essential to take necessary precautions like:

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Wear mosquito repellent.
  • Carry essential medications.

Getting to Kuttikkanam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kuttikkanam

The nearest airport to Kuttikkanam is the Cochin International Airport, which is around 126 km away from Kuttikkanam.

Bus Stations Near Kuttikkanam

There are many bus stations near Kuttikkanam, and the most popular ones are:

  • Kuttikkanam Bus Stand
  • Pattumala Bus Stand
  • Peermade Bus Stand

Other Options

You can also reach Kuttikkanam by train. The nearest railway station is in Kottayam, which is around 70 km away from Kuttikkanam.

How to Get Around in Kuttikkanam

The best way to get around in Kuttikkanam is by hiring a taxi or auto-rickshaw. You can also rent a bike or a car to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Kuttikkanam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kuttikkanam is surrounded by many beautiful destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Thekkady: Thekkady is a beautiful town known for its wildlife sanctuaries and spice plantations. It is around 90 km away from Kuttikkanam.
  • Munnar: Munnar is a popular hill station known for its tea plantations and scenic beauty. It is around 60 km away from Kuttikkanam.
  • Alleppey: Alleppey is a beautiful coastal town known for its backwaters and houseboats. It is around 105 km away from Kuttikkanam.

Conclusion

Kuttikkanam, with its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious food, is an excellent destination for tourists seeking a unique and authentic experience in Kerala. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast or a culture lover, Kuttikkanam has something for everyone. With our comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Kuttikkanam and make the most out of it.
