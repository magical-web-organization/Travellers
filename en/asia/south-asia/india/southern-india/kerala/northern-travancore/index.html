Introduction to Northern Travancore

Northern Travancore is a region in the southern state of Kerala, India. Known for its cultural and natural attractions, Northern Travancore has drawn visitors from all over the world for many years. Kerala is often referred to as “God’s Own Country,” and Northern Travancore plays a major role in this assertion. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and accommodations in this region of Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Travancore

Northern Travancore is a diverse region with a multitude of tourist attractions, with something for everyone. Here are some of the top cultural and natural attractions in Northern Travancore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Krishnapuram Palace - A historical palace in Kayamkulam, Krishnapuram Palace was built by Marthanda Varma of Travancore in the 18th century. The palace houses a museum that showcases Kerala’s heritage.
  • Padmanabhapuram Palace - Situated in the town of Thuckalay, Padmanabhapuram Palace is a top cultural attraction in Northern Travancore. The palace was built-in 1601 AD and has a noteworthy collection of murals, antiques, and other artefacts.
  • Mannadi - A village in the Kollam District, Mannadi is known for its rich history and culture. The place is associated with Veluthampi Dalawa, who was a renowned freedom fighter during the British colonial rule in India. The village has a historic monument that depicts the life of Veluthampi Dalawa.

Natural Attractions of Northern Travancore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thenmala - Thenmala is a popular ecotourism destination in Kerala, with a prominent eco-tourism park. The place is surrounded by dense forests and offers activities like rock climbing, trekking, and boating.
  • Palaruvi Falls - Palaruvi Falls is a breathtaking waterfall located in Kollam district, renowned for its scenic beauty. The waterfall is surrounded by dense forests and hills and is a perfect spot for nature enthusiasts.
  • Varkala Beach - Varkala beach is one of the top beaches in Kerala, with a beautiful cliff that offers a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea. The beach also has mineral water springs that are believed to have medicinal properties.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Travancore

Northern Travancore is an ideal place for adventure enthusiasts, with several beautiful trails and routes to explore. Here are some hiking/biking routes to consider:

  • Konni Forest - Konni is a beautiful forest area near Pathanamthitta that is perfect for adventure seekers. The forest area offers many beautiful trails and routes, making it an ideal destination for hiking and trekking.
  • Punalur - Punalur is a beautiful town in Kollam district, known for its scenic beauty. The place offers many bike routes, making it an ideal destination for biking enthusiasts.
  • Shendurney Wildlife Sanctuary - Shendurney Wildlife Sanctuary is located in Kollam District, and offers plenty of trails and routes for nature lovers. The sanctuary has beautiful wildlife, flora, and fauna, making it an ideal destination for a day trip.

Best Things to Do in Northern Travancore: Activities and Tours

Northern Travancore has plenty of activities and tours to offer for visitors. Here are some of the top things to do in this region:

  • Backwater Cruise - A backwater cruise is a popular activity in Kerala. Most of the backwater cruises start from Alleppey and Kumarakom, and offer a unique experience to visitors.
  • Ayurvedic Massage - Ayurvedic massage is a traditional healing therapy that originated in India many centuries ago. Kerala is famous for its Ayurvedic massage, which is a popular activity among visitors.
  • Houseboat Stay - A houseboat stay is an ideal way to experience the backwaters of Kerala. The houseboats offer visitors a unique experience, with breathtaking views and a chance to see the local lifestyle.

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for visiting Northern Travancore:

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Travancore

Kerala is known for its delicious food, and Northern Travancore is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Northern Travancore:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam and Stew - Appam is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala, made of fermented rice flour and coconut milk. It is usually served with a vegetable or meat stew.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry - Puttu is a breakfast dish made of steamed rice and coconut, usually served with spicy chickpea curry.
  • Malabar Biriyani - Malabar Biriyani is a popular rice dish made with basmati rice, spices, and chicken or mutton.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy - Toddy is a local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It is a popular drink in Kerala and has a sour-sweet taste.
  • Sulaimani Tea - Sulaimani tea is a traditional tea of Kerala, which is a mix of tea leaves and spices. It is usually served as a digestive after meals.

Where to Stay in Northern Travancore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Travancore has a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Northern Travancore:

  • Taj Green Cove Resort & Spa - Taj Green Cove Resort & Spa is a luxury resort situated in Kovalam. The resort has a private beach and offers world-class amenities to its guests.
  • Zostel - Zostel is a chain of backpacker hostels located in various parts of India, including Alleppey in Northern Travancore. The hostel is an ideal place for budget travelers and offers comfortable accommodations.
  • Soma Palmshore - Soma Palmshore is a three-star hotel located in Kovalam, very close to the beach. The hotel offers comfortable accommodations and world-class amenities to its guests.

What to Buy in Northern Travancore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Travancore has many vibrant markets and bazaars offering a wide range of souvenirs and local products. Here are some things to buy in Northern Travancore:

  • Spices - Kerala is famous for its spices. You can find a wide range of spices in local markets, including pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  • Handicrafts - Northern Travancore has a rich tradition of handicrafts, including traditional masks, bamboo baskets, and pottery.
  • Tea and Coffee - Kerala is home to several tea and coffee plantations. You can find a wide range of tea and coffee products in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Travancore

Northern Travancore has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its art, music, and festivals. The region has a predominantly Hindu population, which celebrates several festivals throughout the year. Some of the famous festivals in Northern Travancore are Onam, Vishu, and Attukal Pongala.

Best Time to Visit Northern Travancore

The best time to visit Northern Travancore is between October and February, as the weather during these months is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Travancore

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Northern Travancore:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Wear revealing or skimpy clothes in public places.
  • Disrespect local traditions or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Travancore

While Northern Travancore is a safe destination for tourists, it is always advisable to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some safety tips to follow:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables and documents safe.
  • Be cautious while interacting with strangers.

Health in Northern Travancore

Northern Travancore is home to some of the best Ayurvedic spas and wellness centers in India. It is advisable to consult a medical professional before undergoing any therapy or treatment, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Northern Travancore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Travancore

  • Trivandrum International Airport - Trivandrum International Airport is the nearest airport to Northern Travancore. It is located in the state capital, Thiruvananthapuram, and is about 90 km from Kollam district.

Bus Stations Near Northern Travancore

  • KSRTC Bus Station, Kollam - KSRTC Bus Station in Kollam connects Northern Travancore with other parts of Kerala and neighboring states.
  • KSRTC Bus Station, Thiruvananthapuram - KSRTC Bus Station in Thiruvananthapuram is about 90 km from Kollam district and offers services to different parts of Kerala and neighboring states.

Other Options

  • Train - Northern Travancore is well-connected by train. Kollam Railway Station and Thiruvananthapuram Central Railway Station are the major railway stations in the area.
  • Taxi/Car Rental - Taxis and car rentals are widely available in Northern Travancore and can be hired for local travel and sightseeing.

How to Get Around in Northern Travancore

The best way to get around Northern Travancore is by renting a car or hiring a taxi. Buses and trains are also available, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable, especially during peak season.

Where to Go Next from Northern Travancore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Northern Travancore, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Thekkady - Thekkady is a beautiful destination in Kerala, located a few hours’ drive from Northern Travancore. It is famous for its wildlife sanctuary and aromatic spice plantations.
  • Kanyakumari - Kanyakumari is a famous tourist destination in Tamil Nadu, located at the southernmost tip of India. The town is well-known for its beautiful beaches, temples, and historical monuments.
  • Cochin - Cochin is a famous port city in Kerala, located a few hours’ drive from Northern Travancore. It is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and vibrant markets.

Conclusion

Northern Travancore is a beautiful destination in Kerala, offering visitors a unique experience of traditional Indian culture and natural beauty. Remember, Kerala is often referred to as “God’s Own Country,” and Northern Travancore is a significant part of its rich legacy.
