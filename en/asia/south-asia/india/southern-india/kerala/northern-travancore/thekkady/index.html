Introduction to Thekkady

Thekkady is a beautiful and serene destination located in Northern Travancore, in the southern Indian state of Kerala. This picturesque location is surrounded by beautiful hills and forests, and offers a plethora of natural and cultural attractions that are sure to enchant any traveler.

Local Culture and Customs of Thekkady

Thekkady is known for its rich cultural heritage, with influences from both Hindu and Christian communities. Visitors are advised to respect local customs, dress modestly, and remove shoes before entering places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Thekkady

The best time to visit Thekkady is from October to February, when the weather is cool and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thekkady

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Remove shoes before entering places of worship
  • Support local businesses

Don’t:

  • Carry or use plastic bags
  • Litter
  • Be disrespectful to local customs or traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Thekkady

Thekkady is a generally safe destination, but visitors are advised to take common precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and not walking alone at night.

Health in Thekkady

Visitors are advised to drink bottled or boiled water, and to take precautions against mosquito bites, as the region is prone to the spread of mosquito-borne diseases.

The best way to get around Thekkady is by private taxi or rental car, as public transportation options are limited.
