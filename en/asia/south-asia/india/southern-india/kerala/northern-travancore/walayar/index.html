Introduction to Walayar

Looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Southern India? Look no further than Walayar! Located in Northern Travancore, this hidden gem is a must-see for travelers seeking authentic experiences.

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious cuisine, Walayar has something to offer for everyone. And the best part? It’s still relatively undiscovered, so you won’t have to fight crowds to enjoy this unique destination.

List of Places to Visit in Walayar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Start your journey in Walayar by exploring its rich cultural heritage. The following museums and historical places are must-sees:

  • Walayar Dam and Reservoir: Located on the Palakkad-Thrissur border, this popular attraction is a stunning example of engineering prowess. Built during the 1940s, the dam still controls the water flow of the Bharathapuzha River.

  • Kerala Kalamandalam: Established in 1930, the Kerala Kalamandalam is an institution dedicated to preserving classical Indian performing arts. Visit the campus to witness performances, take classes, or simply admire the traditional architecture.

  • Fantasy Park: If you’re traveling with kids, don’t miss the Fantasy Park! Located near Malampuzha Dam, this amusement park features a variety of thrilling rides and attractions.

Natural Attractions of Walayar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers, rejoice! Walayar is home to a variety of stunning natural landscapes, including these outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Nelliyampathy is a hidden gem in Walayar that offers breathtaking views. Drive through the lush forests and tea plantations to reach this isolated hill station.

  • Malampuzha Dam is a popular tourist spot that boasts one of the largest gardens in Asia. The garden has a variety of attractions like butterfly park, aquarium, boating and a toy train.

  • Malampuzha Rock Garden: Created by Padmashree Nek Chand, Malampuzha Rock Garden is an amazing man-made art form with rock sculptures located inside an amusement park, it’s a perfect place to unwind and relax.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walayar

For adventure junkies, there’s no better way to experience Walayar than by hiking or biking through its scenic landscapes. Some of the top trails to consider include:

  • Nelliyampathy Hill Top: Take a challenging hike to the highest point of Nelliyampathy and enjoy panoramic views of Southern India from the hill top.

  • Malampuzha Riverside: Set along the banks of the Malampuzha River, this scenic route offers a leisurely bike ride for all fitness levels.

Best Things to Do in Walayar: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the natural and cultural attractions of Walayar, be sure to take part in some of these exciting activities and tours:

  • Kalaripayattu: Originating from Kerala, Kalaripayattu is one of the world’s oldest martial arts forms. Visit Kalari Centers in Walayar to see a live demonstration or even participate in a class.

  • Ayurvedic Spa Treatments: Take a break from sightseeing and indulge in an Ayurvedic spa treatment. Walayar offers a variety of facilities providing traditional medicinal treatments like panchakarma or a rejuvenation therapy.

Food and Drink Scene of Walayar

No trip to Walayar is complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: A traditional breakfast dish made with steamed rice cake and a spicy curry made from black chickpeas.

  • Karimeen Pollichathu: This dish comprises of small fish wrapped in banana leaf with a savory blend of spices and cooked over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: A popular alcoholic beverage made from palm nectar, toddy is often enjoyed alongside traditional Kerala cuisine.

  • Sambharam: A refreshing, yogurt-based drink seasoned with cumin, ginger and green chillies.

Where to Stay in Walayar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options available in Walayar to fit every budget and style. Some of the top choices include:

  • KTDC Tamarind Mannarkad: A government-run hotel located close to the Walayar Dam and Reservoir.

  • Nelliyampathy Hills Valley Resort: Offering stunning views of the surrounding hills, this secluded resort is the perfect place to unwind in nature.

What to Buy in Walayar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Walayar back home with you by shopping for these local souvenirs and products:

  • Handcrafted Bamboo Products: Experience the art of traditional Indian crafts by purchasing handmade bamboo items.

  • Spices: Kerala is famous for its spices, so be sure to stock up on some of the region’s freshest and most flavorful varieties!

Local Culture and Customs of Walayar

When visiting Walayar, it’s important to respect the local culture and traditions. Some of the customs unique to the area include:

  • Vishu: The traditional New Year of Malayalis, Vishu is celebrated in Walayar with great enthusiasm.

Best Time to Visit Walayar

The best time to visit Walayar is from October to February. The weather during this time is mild, and the region is less crowded than during peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walayar

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture: Be sure to dress conservatively and follow the customs and traditions of the region when visiting religious sites and cultural attractions.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Kerala is famous for its unique blend of spices and flavors – be sure to indulge in the delicious local cuisine!

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep Walayar’s natural landscapes beautiful by properly disposing of your trash.

  • Disrespect the Locals: Be respectful and friendly to the locals to ensure a pleasant and enjoyable trip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walayar

Overall, Walayar is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Some tips for staying safe in Walayar include:

  • Travel with a Companion: It’s always safer to travel with a companion, especially when exploring remote areas.

  • Avoid Night Travel: If possible, avoid traveling alone on roads at night.

Health in Walayar

Visitors to Walayar should take precautions to protect themselves from insect bites and waterborne illnesses. Here are some tips for staying healthy during your trip:

  • Always Drink Bottled Water: To avoid getting sick from contaminated water, stick to bottled or filtered water.

  • Apply Insect Repellent: Mosquitoes and other insects are prevalent in Walayar, so be sure to apply insect repellent before heading outdoors.

Getting to Walayar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Walayar can be accessed via several modes of transportation. Here are some of the most popular options:

Airports Near Walayar

The nearest airport to Walayar is Coimbatore International Airport (CJB), which is around 60 km from the destination.

Bus Stations Near Walayar

Walayar is well-connected with buses from Thrissur, Palakkad and Coimbatore as well. You can also get the buses from the major towns like Vadakkencherry and Mannarkkad.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: If you prefer to have more flexibility in your transportation, consider hiring a private car or taxi for your trip.

How to Get Around in Walayar

While walking is one option for getting around in Walayar, most travelers opt to hire a taxi or use local public transportation (JNNURM Buses) to get to different destinations.

Where to Go Next from Walayar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to explore more of Kerala, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Thrissur: A cultural hub, Thrissur is famous for its Pooram temple festival, Thrissur Pooram in April.

  • Coimbatore: A bustling city located just across the border in Tamil Nadu, Coimbatore is known for its textiles and engineering establishments.

Conclusion

Walayar might be a lesser-known destination in Southern India, but as you can see, it has plenty to offer visitors who are willing to explore! With its combination of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, it’s a must-see destination for any traveler looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path adventure.
