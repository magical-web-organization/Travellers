Introduction to Munnar

Munnar is a beautiful hill station located in the Northern Travancore region of Kerala. Nestled in the Western Ghats at an altitude of 1600 meters, it is a paradise for nature lovers. The name Munnar comes from the Tamil words “munnu” meaning three and “aar” meaning river, referring to the convergence of three rivers in the area. Munnar’s picturesque landscape and cool weather make it a popular destination among tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Munnar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tata Tea Museum: This museum showcases the history of tea plantations in Munnar. Visitors can learn about the process of tea-making and enjoy a cup of tea at the end of the tour.

  • Mattupetty Dam: Located near Munnar, this dam is a popular tourist spot. Visitors can enjoy boating, horse riding, and trekking.

  • Eravikulam National Park: This park is famous for the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and offers visitors stunning views of tea plantations.

Natural Attractions of Munnar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Top Station: Located at an altitude of 1700 meters, this is the highest point in Munnar offering breathtaking views of the Western Ghats and the valley below.

  • Anamudi Peak: The highest peak in South India, and a popular destination among hikers.

  • Echo Point: A popular picnic spot, known for its natural echo phenomenon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Munnar

  • Kolukkumalai: At an altitude of 8000 feet, this is the world’s highest tea estate, located 35 km from Munnar. Visitors can enjoy a scenic hike or bike ride to the estate and appreciate the breathtaking views.

  • Meesapulimala: Trek to the second highest peak in the Western Ghats at 8700 feet. This trek offers stunning views of the famous tea gardens of Munnar and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Munnar: Activities and Tours

  • Jeep Safaris: Explore the beauty of Munnar by hiring a 4x4 jeep, and travel on narrow roads and hills.

  • Elephant Rides: Visit the nearby Elephant Safari Park where you can ride on the back of an elephant and explore the area.

  • Tea Tasting: Enjoy some of the best tea in the world by tasting a variety of tea flavors in Munnar.

Food and Drink Scene of Munnar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam with Stew: Appam is a rice pancake that is a staple in Kerala, and is typically served with vegetable or chicken stew.

  • Meen Curry: Meen Curry is a spicy and tangy fish curry typical to Kerala.

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: A breakfast favorite in Kerala, Puttu is rice flour steamed in cylindrical shape, often served with kadala curry, made with black chickpeas.

Local Beverages

  • Sulaimani: A special tea preparation, made by mixing black tea with spices and lemon.

  • Toddy: A fermented alcoholic drink made from the sap of palm trees.

  • Buttermilk: A refreshing drink made from yogurt and spices.

Where to Stay in Munnar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Munnar has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget guesthouses to luxury resorts.

  • Green Magic Resort: Located in the middle of a tea plantation, this unique resort offers treehouses as accommodation.

  • The Fog Resort and Spa Munnar: A luxury resort that offers stunning views of the mountains and valleys of Munnar.

  • Dreamcatcher Plantation Resort: Located on a spice plantation, this resort offers a unique experience to visitors.

What to Buy in Munnar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Munnar is known for its tea, and visitors can buy a variety of tea flavors from the tea gardens and shops in the town.

  • Spices: Kerala is famous for its spices, and visitors can find a variety of spices and spice blends at local markets.

  • Handicrafts: Munnar has several shops selling handmade items such as jewelry, bags, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Munnar

Munnar is a multicultural town that celebrates various festivals throughout the year such as Onam, Easter, and New Year. The local culture is strongly influenced by the traditional art forms of Kerala such as Kathakali and Mohiniyattam.

Best Time to Visit Munnar

The best time to visit Munnar is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is pleasant and cool. The summer months from March to June are also a good time to visit, however, it can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Munnar

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and spices.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious places.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.

  • Take photographs without permission.

  • Touch or disturb wildlife in national parks and sanctuaries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Munnar

Munnar is a safe town to travel in, however, visitors should take precautions such as not venturing into remote areas alone, keeping valuables safe, and being cautious while trekking or hiking.

Health in Munnar

Munnar does not have any major health risks. However, visitors should keep themselves hydrated due to the cool climate and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as Dengue and Malaria.

Getting to Munnar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Munnar

The nearest airport to Munnar is Cochin International Airport, which is about 110 km away from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to reach Munnar.

Bus Stations Near Munnar

Munnar has a local bus station from where visitors can take buses to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Munnar from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Munnar

Munnar is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. However, for longer distances, visitors can hire a taxi or use local bus services. Visitors can also hire a bike or car to travel around Munnar.

Where to Go Next from Munnar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thekkady: A wildlife sanctuary located 90 km away from Munnar, known for its diverse flora and fauna, and scenic landscapes.

  • Alleppey: A major backwater destination in Kerala, known for its serene canals, houseboats, and beautiful landscapes.

  • Kumarakom: A scenic village located on the Vembanad Lake, known for its backwaters, houseboats, and bird sanctuary.

Conclusion

Munnar is a beautiful hill station that offers visitors a wide range of experiences, from scenic views and outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and local cuisine. This comprehensive travel guide will help visitors plan their trip to Munnar and explore the best it has to offer. Get ready for an unforgettable experience in Munnar!
