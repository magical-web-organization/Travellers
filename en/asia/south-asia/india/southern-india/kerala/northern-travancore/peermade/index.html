Welcome to Peermade, a quaint little town located in Northern Travancore in the Indian state of Kerala. Nestled amidst the lush green Western Ghats, Peermade is surrounded by tea, coffee, and spice plantations, dense forests, and rolling hills. The town is known for its serene landscapes, rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine. This guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Peermade.

Introduction to Peermade

Peermade’s history dates back to the early 17th century when it was an important trading center for spices and other commodities. The town derives its name from the Sufi saint, Peer Mohammad, who is believed to have meditated in the area. Peermade was later developed into a summer retreat by the royal family of Travancore.

Peermade’s climate is moderate throughout the year, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking to escape the scorching heat of the plains. The monsoon season, from June to September, is the best time to visit Peermade as the lush green landscapes come alive with the sound of gurgling streams and waterfalls.

Local Culture and Customs of Peermade

Peermade’s culture is a harmonious blend of Kerala’s indigenous traditions and the unique customs of the local tribes. Pachakuthira, a traditional dance form performed by the Oorali tribe, is one of the most iconic cultural events in Peermade. The town also celebrates several festivals, including Onam, Vishu, and Ayudha Puja with great pomp and show.

Best Time to Visit Peermade

The monsoon season, from June to September, is the best time to visit Peermade. The town is lush green during this time, and waterfalls and streams are in full flow. The weather is pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peermade

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress modestly while visiting temples and religious places.
  3. Try the local cuisine.
  4. Carry warm clothing during the monsoon season.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke in public places.
  2. Disrespect the local culture.
  3. Damage the environment.
  4. Take pictures of the locals without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peermade

Peermade is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, it’s best to be cautious and avoid isolated areas, especially after dark.

Health in Peermade

While Peermade doesn’t have any specific health concerns, travelers are advised to carry mosquito repellent and sunscreen. It’s also recommended to drink only bottled water.

Getting to Peermade: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peermade

The nearest airport to Peermade is Cochin International Airport, which is approximately 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Peermade

Kottayam and Kumily are the nearest bus stations to Peermade.

Other Options

Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and rented bikes are available for getting around Peermade.

How to Get Around in Peermade

The best way to explore Peermade is by foot, bike or car. Several local tour operators also offer guided tours by jeep or private vehicle.
