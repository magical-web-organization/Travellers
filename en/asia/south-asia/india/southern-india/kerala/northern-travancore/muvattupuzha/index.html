A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Muvattupuzha, Kerala

Located in Northern Travancore in the Indian state of Kerala, Muvattupuzha is a scenic town surrounded by lush vegetation, tranquil water bodies, and diverse wildlife. Known as the land of three rivers, Muvattupuzha stands out as an outdoor enthusiasts’ paradise. Beyond the beautiful natural scenery, the town boasts a rich cultural and historical heritage that draws visitors from around the world. This comprehensive travel guide highlights the must-visit attractions, hiking routes, food and drink scene, local customs, and safety tips for Muvattupuzha.

List of Places to Visit in Muvattupuzha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Muvattupuzha is rich in culture and history, and you can immerse yourself in the rich heritage by visiting the following museums and historical places:

  1. Kallil Temple - An ancient rock-cut temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is renowned for its intricate carvings, sculptures, and exquisite murals.

  2. St. Mary’s Orthodox Cathedral - A beautiful church located in Muvattupuzha town that dates back to the early 16th century. It is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful frescoes.

  3. Cherai Beach - Cherai Beach is a beautiful and tranquil beach located just an hour’s drive from Muvattupuzha. The beach offers a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea.

Natural Attractions of Muvattupuzha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Muvattupuzha’s natural beauty is a treat for the senses. From scenic waterfalls to wildlife sanctuaries and trekking trails, the town is brimming with natural wonders. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  1. Thattekad Bird Sanctuary - A haven for birdwatchers, the sanctuary is home to a variety of migratory and resident birds, such as White-bellied Heron, Malabar Pied Hornbill, and Kingfisher.

  2. Thommankuthu Waterfalls - This seven-step waterfall is popular for its beauty and natural charm. The lush green forests surrounding the waterfall make for a serene trekking trail.

  3. Idamalayar Dam - The dam provides an excellent opportunity to see the vast expanse of water and the surrounding lush forests. The area is quite popular for birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Muvattupuzha

Muvattupuzha is a prime location for trekking and cycling enthusiasts. Here is a list of some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Muvattupuzha:

  1. Muvattupuzha to Paniyeli Poru - A scenic trek through the lush forests and along the rocky cliffs to reach the Paniyeli Poru waterfall.

  2. Vagamon to Munnar - An exhilarating bicycle ride through the hill stations of Vagamon and Munnar, passing through tea and spice plantations and scenic landscapes.

  3. Nelliyampathy to Athirappilly - A challenging yet rewarding trek that takes you through stunning forests, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries on the way to Athirappilly falls.

Best Things to Do in Muvattupuzha: Activities and Tours

Muvattupuzha offers several activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the must-try experiences:

  1. Bamboo Rafting in Periyar River - Embark on a bamboo rafting expedition in the Periyar River, taking you through the lush forests, spotting exotic birds and animals.

  2. Elephant Ride in Kodanad - Visit the elephant training center in Kodanad and enjoy an elephant ride through the serene woods.

  3. Spice Plantation Tour in Munnar - Take a guided tour of the famous tea and spice estates of Munnar, where you can witness the unique process of tea-making and taste a variety of tea flavors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Muvattupuzha

For those planning to spend a week in Muvattupuzha, here’s an itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Muvattupuzha by visiting St. Mary’s Church and Kallil Temple.

  • Day 2: Go bird watching at Thattekad Bird Sanctuary and enjoy a picnic by the waterfalls in Thommankuthu.

  • Day 3-5: Go trekking in the hills of Munnar and enjoy a plantation tour.

  • Day 6-7: Take in the scenery and enjoy bamboo rafting at Periyar River, followed by elephant rides at Kodanad.

Food and Drink Scene of Muvattupuzha

Kerala is known for its varied cuisine, and Muvattupuzha is no exception. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam and Stew - Appam (rice pancakes) served with vegetable or meat stew is a classic Kerala breakfast.

  • Karimeen Pollichathu - A traditional Kerala dish that is steamed in banana leaves and flavored with spices, curry leaves, and tamarind.

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry - Puttu steamed rice cake, accompanied by kadala (black chickpeas) curry, is a popular mid-day snack.

Local Beverages

  • Chukku Kaapi - A concoction of coffee, ginger, and jaggery, served hot and believed to have various health benefits.

  • Sambharam - Buttermilk flavored with cumin and ginger, a refreshing drink that cools the body in hot weather.

Where to Stay in Muvattupuzha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Muvattupuzha has several accommodation options that can suit any budget. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  1. Club Mahindra Munnar - A luxurious resort located near Munnar that offers top-notch amenities and service.

  2. Kallelys Residency - A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Muvattupuzha town, offering comfortable rooms and food.

  3. Aruvi Tourist Home - A cozy guest house located near Thommankuthu waterfalls offering beautiful views and a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Muvattupuzha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Muvattupuzha is known for its unique handicrafts and eco-friendly products. Here is a list of must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Aranmula Kannadi - Handmade metal mirrors that are only crafted in Aranmula, a small village near Muvattupuzha.

  • Panchaloha Idols - Made from a five-metal alloy, these idols are unique to Kerala’s culture and craftsmanship.

  • Banana Fiber Products - Eco-friendly handicraft products made from banana fiber, ranging from baskets, bags, mats, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Muvattupuzha

Muvattupuzha is a place of diverse traditions and customs. Here are some of the most important cultural practices:

  • Kerala’s Onam Festival: A ten-day harvest festival that is celebrated during the months of August-September in Muvattupuzha.

  • Traditional Dances: Muvattupuzha hosts various traditional dance forms like Kathakali, Mohiniyattam and Koodiyattam.

  • Language: Malayalam is the most commonly spoken language in Muvattupuzha.

Best Time to Visit Muvattupuzha

The best time to visit Muvattupuzha is between October and March when the climate is salubrious, making it more pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muvattupuzha

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples, churches, and sacred sites.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.

  • Hinder the natural surroundings of Muvattupuzha.

  • Disrespect local customs and religious practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Muvattupuzha

Muvattupuzha is a relatively safe place, but precautions are necessary. Here are some safety tips:

  • Be cautious when trekking, and watch out for wild animals and rocky terrain.

  • Avoid walking in poorly lit areas or deserted spots during the night.

Health in Muvattupuzha

  • Make sure to carry a mosquito repellent, as the town is in a tropical climate that often leads to mosquito-borne diseases.

  • Drink homemade coconut water or boiled water to avoid catching any waterborne diseases.

Getting to Muvattupuzha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Muvattupuzha is easily accessible by air, train or bus.

Airports Near Muvattupuzha

The international airports near Muvattupuzha are Kochi International Airport and Calicut International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Muvattupuzha

Muvattupuzha Bus Station is located in the town center and connects to many nearby cities.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a local taxi is an excellent option to travel within Muvattupuzha.

How to Get Around in Muvattupuzha

The best way to get around Muvattupuzha is by hiring a local taxi or motorcycle, which can be rented easily in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Muvattupuzha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Alleppey - Known as the Venice of the East, Alleppey is a scenic town located on the banks of the backwaters.

  • Cochin - Cochin, also known as Kochi, is a vibrant port city known for its historic colonial architecture, beaches, and charming backwaters.

  • Thekkady - A peaceful and scenic village located in the heart of Kerala, known for its tranquil wildlife sanctuary and spice plantations.

Conclusion

Muvattupuzha has an abundance of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With a perfect combination of serene natural beauty and vibrant cultural experiences, Muvattupuzha is an excellent place to visit. Whether you want to trek into the forest, explore the local culture or taste the unique cuisine, Muvattupuzha has something to offer for every kind of traveler. Don’t miss out on this hidden gem of Kerala!
