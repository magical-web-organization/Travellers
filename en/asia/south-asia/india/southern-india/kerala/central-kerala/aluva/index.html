Ultimate Travel Guide to Aluva, Kerala

Located in Central Kerala, Aluva is a cultural and historic city that offers a diverse range of attractions, from scenic natural beauty to beautiful historic landmarks. Whether you’re an adventure-seeker or a history buff, Aluva has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we cover everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Aluva, Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in Aluva

Aluva has a rich cultural and historical legacy, which is evident from the plethora of attractions that it offers. Exploring these attractions is an excellent way to get to know the city better. Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Aluva:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shivarathri Manappuram: This temple is considered to be one of the oldest and most revered temples in Aluva. The temple attracts thousands of devotees every year, especially during the Shivarathri festival.
  • Sree Mahadeva Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the Sree Mahadeva Temple is another popular religious attraction in Aluva. The temple is famous for its annual festival, which is celebrated with much pomp and show.
  • Kalamassery: Located near Aluva, Kalamassery is a small town that is famous for its vibrant art and culture scene. It’s an excellent place to experience the local way of life.

Natural Attractions of Aluva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Periyar River: The Periyar River is one of the most significant natural attractions in Aluva. You can explore the scenic beauty of the river on a boat ride and even indulge in some fishing.
  • Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary: If you’re a nature lover, a visit to the Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary is a must. The sanctuary is home to a diverse range of bird species, making it an ideal spot for bird-watching and nature walks.
  • Hill Palace Museum: Located near Aluva, the Hill Palace Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum showcases the rich cultural and historical heritage of Kerala through its collections of artifacts, paintings, and murals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aluva

Aluva is surrounded by scenic landscapes and is an excellent destination for adventure seekers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Aluva:

  • Periyar River Bank: The Periyar River Bank is perfect for both hiking and biking. The route offers stunning scenic beauty and is an excellent spot for photography.
  • Thirunakkara Canal: The Thirunakkara Canal is another popular spot for biking and hiking. The route offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and is an ideal spot for a leisurely walk.

Best Things to Do in Aluva: Activities and Tours

Aluva offers a plethora of activities and tours that cater to every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Aluva:

  • Boat Rides on Periyar River: One of the best ways to explore the beauty of Aluva is to take a boat ride on the Periyar River. You can enjoy scenic views of the surrounding landscape and even indulge in some fishing.
  • Explore the City on a Bicycle: Rent a bicycle and explore the city like a local. Cycling is a great way to explore the city at your own pace and discover hidden gems.
  • Visit a Spice Garden: Kerala is famous for its spices, and a visit to a spice garden is a must if you want to learn about the region’s culinary heritage.

To make the most of your trip to Aluva, you can follow this week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Aluva

Aluva’s food and drink scene is as diverse as its cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala: Puttu and Kadala is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala. Puttu is made from rice flour, and Kadala is a curry made with chickpeas.
  • Ela Sadya: Ela Sadya is a traditional Kerala feast that consists of over 20 dishes served on a banana leaf.
  • Kappa and Fish Curry: Kappa and Fish Curry is a popular dish in Kerala. Kappa is made from mashed tapioca, and the fish curry is made with a spicy blend of coconut milk, tamarind, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: Toddy is a fermented coconut drink that is popular in Kerala.
  • Chaya: Chaya is the local version of tea in Kerala. It’s a strong, sweet tea that is often served with fresh coconut milk.

What to Buy in Aluva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aluva is famous for its spices, handicrafts, and silk sarees. Here are some must-buy items that you should include in your shopping list:

  • Spices: Aluva is famous for its spices, which include cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon.
  • Handicrafts: Aluva’s Handicrafts are made with traditional techniques and are unique to the region.
  • Silk Sarees: Aluva is a hub for silk sarees, and you can find a range of silk sarees that are unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Aluva

Aluva’s cultural heritage is a blend of various traditions and customs. Here are some local customs and practices that you should be aware of:

  • Dressing: Aluva is a conservative city, and you should dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Language: Malayalam is the local language in Aluva, but English and Hindi are also widely spoken.
  • Etiquette: Aluva has a strict code of etiquette, and you should always be respectful of local customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Aluva

The best time to visit Aluva is from September to February when the weather is pleasant and comfortable. The monsoon season (June-August) is also a great time to visit if you’re a nature lover.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aluva

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions: Aluva has a rich cultural heritage, and you should respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Aluva’s food scene is diverse, and you should try the local cuisine, including Puttu and Kadala, Kappa, and Fish Curry, and Ela Sadya.
  • Explore the City by Bicycle: Aluva is an excellent destination for cycling, and you should rent a bicycle and explore the city at your own pace.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Aluva is a beautiful city, and you should keep it clean by not littering.
  • Disrespect Temples and Religious Sites: Aluva has many important religious sites, and you should be respectful when visiting these places.
  • Ignore Local Customs and Practices: Aluva has a strict code of etiquette, and you should follow local customs and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aluva

Aluva is a safe city, but it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions while traveling. Some safety tips to keep in mind are:

  • Avoid Traveling Alone at Night: Aluva is a safe destination, but it’s always advisable to avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Use Licensed Taxis and Public Transport: When using taxis or public transport, always go for licensed operators to ensure your safety.

Health in Aluva

Aluva has excellent healthcare facilities, and you can find several hospitals and clinics in the city. However, it’s always advisable to carry a basic medical kit when traveling.

Aluva is a small city, and you can explore most of the attractions on foot. You can also rent a bicycle to explore the city at your own pace. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Aluva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aluva is a gateway to several nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular options that you can consider:

  • Kochi: Kochi, also known as the Queen of the Arabian Sea, is a popular tourist destination that’s located just 20 kilometers from Aluva.
  • Munnar: Munnar is a beautiful hill station that’s located approximately 130 kilometers from Aluva.
  • Kottayam: Kottayam, also known as the land of letters, is a historic city that’s located approximately 50 kilometers from Aluva.

Aluva is a cultural and historic city that offers a diverse range of attractions for travelers.
