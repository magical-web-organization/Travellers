Introduction to Kunnamkulam

Kunnamkulam is a small town situated in the Thrissur district of Central Kerala. It is a blend of historical significance and natural beauty. The town is a major pilgrimage center for both Hindus and Muslims, yet it remains off the tourist trail, providing an authentic experience. Kunnamkulam is located just 24 km north of Thrissur city and is easily accessible.

List of Places to Visit in Kunnamkulam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sree Krishna Swamy Temple

Sree Krishna Swamy Temple is one of the most significant historical and cultural landmarks in Kunnamkulam. The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and boasts a unique architecture style. Inside, there is a beautiful idol of Lord Krishna made of Panchaloham.

Cheraman Juma Masjid

Cheraman Juma Masjid is the first mosque in India and was built in 629 AD by Malik Ibn Dinar. The architectural style of the mosque is a blend of Hindu and Muslim styles, which is unique to the region.

Koodalmanickyam Temple

Koodalmanickyam Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Shakthi, and it is said to be over 2000 years old. The temple boasts both architectural and artistic beauty with its intricately designed carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Kunnamkulam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Athirappilly Falls

Athirappilly Falls is a natural wonder located just 25 km from Kunnamkulam. It is a picturesque waterfall situated in the Sholayar forest range. Visitors can enjoy a breathtaking view of the waterfall from a viewpoint.

Peechi-Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Peechi-Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected natural reserve located near Kunnamkulam. The sanctuary is home to a variety of fauna, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and many species of birds. Visitors can enjoy a jeep safari to explore the wildlife reserve.

Chavakkad Beach

Chavakkad Beach is one of the most popular beaches in the region, located just 10 km from Kunnamkulam. The beach is a picturesque spot to relax and witness a beautiful sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kunnamkulam

Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary

Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary is an ideal destination for hikers, located just 28 km from Kunnamkulam. The sanctuary boasts a diverse range of avian species, making it an excellent spot for birdwatchers.

Best Things to Do in Kunnamkulam: Activities and Tours

Explore the Local Markets

Kunnamkulam is famous for its thriving marketplaces, which offers a unique shopping experience with a variety of local delicacies, handicrafts, and jewelry.

Learn Kathakali

Kathakali is a traditional dance form of Kerala, and Kunnamkulam provides a unique opportunity to experience it. Visitors can learn Kathakali and even watch a live performance.

Food and Drink Scene of Kunnamkulam

Must-Try Dishes

Puttu and Kadala Curry

Puttu and Kadala Curry is a traditional breakfast staple of Kerala. Puttu is made of steamed rice, and Kadala curry is a side dish made of black chickpeas and a blend of spices.

Fish Curry and Rice

Fish Curry and Rice is a ubiquitous dish of Kerala, and it is an excellent way to explore the taste of fresh Kerala seafood.

Local Beverages

Coconut Water

Coconut water is a refreshing drink that can be found nearly everywhere in Kunnamkulam. It is a perfect way to stay hydrated on a hot day.

Tea

Kerala is famous for its tea plantations, and visitors can enjoy a bright cup of tea served with some local snacks.

Where to Stay in Kunnamkulam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kunnamkulam offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors can choose from budget-friendly options to luxurious resorts.

What to Buy in Kunnamkulam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kunnamkulam is famous for its handicrafts, jewelry, and local textiles. Visitors can also purchase spices, tea, and Kerala sweets to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kunnamkulam

Kunnamkulam is deeply rooted in Hindu and Muslim traditions, and visitors should be mindful of religious customs and practices. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kunnamkulam

The best time to visit Kunnamkulam is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and tourist activities are at their peak. The monsoon season between June and September is not recommended for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kunnamkulam

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites
  • Respect local culture and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or wildlife sanctuaries
  • Wear inappropriate clothing
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Kunnamkulam

Kunnamkulam is a safe destination, and visitors are advised to take usual precautions to avoid theft and non-violent crime.

Health in Kunnamkulam

Visitors are advised to come prepared with mosquito repellent and to take usual precautions to avoid foodborne illnesses.

Getting to Kunnamkulam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kunnamkulam

The nearest airport to Kunnamkulam is the Cochin International Airport, located about 68 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kunnamkulam

Kunnamkulam has a well-connected public transportation system, and visitors can easily take a bus from Thrissur City, which is just 24 km away.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to reach Kunnamkulam from the nearest airport or train station.

How to Get Around in Kunnamkulam

Visitors can rent a scooter or motorcycle to explore the town, or they can use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kunnamkulam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guruvayur

Guruvayur is a temple town located just 21 km from Kunnamkulam. It is famous for the Guruvayur Temple, which is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus.

Kochi

Kochi, also known as the “Queen of the Arabian Sea,” is a popular tourist destination located about 85 km away from Kunnamkulam. The city boasts a blend of Portuguese, Dutch, and Indian culture and history.

Conclusion

Kunnamkulam is a must-visit destination for those in search of authentic cultural and natural experiences. Visitors can explore ancient temples, try local cuisine, learn about traditional dance forms and explore nearby natural reserves. The town can be easily reached, and visitors can choose from a variety of accommodation options.
