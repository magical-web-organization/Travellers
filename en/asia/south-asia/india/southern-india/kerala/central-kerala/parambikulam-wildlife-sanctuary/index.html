Introduction to Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Nestled in the Western Ghats mountain range, Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is a natural paradise and one of the top wildlife sanctuaries in Kerala, Southern India. The sanctuary is spread over 285 square kilometers of lush tropical forest, dense greenery, and crystal-clear riverbeds, and it’s home to some rare and endangered wildlife species, such as the lion-tailed macaque, Nilgiri langur, and Great Indian hornbill. Besides wildlife, the sanctuary boasts breathtaking landscapes, waterfalls, and plenty of recreational activities for tourists to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Thoonakkadavu Sree Narayana Guru Temple: This temple is of historical importance and was founded by the great saint and social reformer Sree Narayana Guru. The temple complex includes a serene pond, a meditation hall, and a library.

  • Vengoli Sree Krishna Temple: This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and is situated at the foothills of the sanctuary. The temple is famous for its exquisite architecture and murals depicting scenes from the Hindu mythologies.

  • Parambikulam Dam and Reservoir: This dam is one of the major tourist attractions of the sanctuary, and it’s renowned for its panoramic views and water-based recreational activities, such as boating, kayaking, and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kannimara Teak Tree: Located on the way to Thunakaddy, this rare and ancient teak tree is believed to be over 400 years old and has a girth of 7.02 meters.

  • Parambikulam Viewpoint: This vantage point offers stunning views of the sanctuary’s valleys, mountains, and forests, and is a perfect place to catch a glimpse of the wildlife of the sanctuary.

  • Anappady Hills: Situated on the border of Tamil Nadu, Anappady Hills is another must-visit attraction in the sanctuary. The place offers a great trekking experience with magnificent views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary offers several trekking and biking routes, which provide visitors with a chance to explore the sanctuary’s natural beauty at their own pace.

  • Topslip Trekking Trail: This trek is one of the most popular treks in the sanctuary, and it takes visitors through the scenic forests, waterfalls, and grasslands, with a chance of spotting wildlife.

  • Kariyanshola Trekking Trail: This trek offers an adventurous and challenging trail full of rocky terrain, steep slopes, and dense vegetation.

  • Thoonakkadavu Trekking Trail: This trekking trail takes visitors through the sanctuary’s picturesque landscapes dotted with tea plantations, wildlife sightings, and ethnic hamlets.

Best Things to Do in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari: A wildlife safari is a must-do activity in the sanctuary, and visitors can opt for either an open-top jeep safari or an Elephant-back safari to experience the sanctuary’s flora and fauna.

  • Bamboo Rafting and Boating: The sanctuary also offers an opportunity for tourists to experience the tranquil waters of the sanctuary by undertaking a bamboo rafting or boating excursion.

  • Thekkady Night Jungle Patrol: Thekkady Night Jungle Patrol is an incredible opportunity for visitors to witness the nocturnal wildlife activities in the sanctuary.

Food and Drink Scene of Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

To get a taste of the local cuisine, visitors can try the following dishes and beverages in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed rice cakes and spicy chickpea curry.

  • Appam and Stew: Rice batter pancakes served with a vegetable or meat stew.

  • Kappa and Fish Curry: Boiled tapioca root and spicy fish curry.

Local Beverages

  • Karikku: Freshly squeezed coconut water.

  • Tea and Coffee: Kerala is famous for its tea and coffee plantations, and visitors can enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea or coffee at the local tea shops.

Where to Stay in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The sanctuary has several accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Parambikulam Tiger Reserve Room Cottages: These eco-friendly cottages situated inside the park are operated by the Forest Department and provide basic amenities to visitors.

  • Bamboo Grove Farmstay: Located near the sanctuary, this farmstay offers an eco-friendly and sustainable accommodation option with organic meals prepared from locally sourced ingredients.

  • Kairali - The Ayurvedic Healing Village: This luxury resort located in Palakkad, Kerala, offers a serene and tranquil environment with comprehensive Ayurvedic treatments and therapies.

What to Buy in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home souvenirs and local products from the sanctuary’s souvenir shops, including:

  • Spices: Kerala is famous for its spices, and visitors can bring home the best spices from the local markets.

  • Tea: Visitors can also take home the local tea varieties from the nearby plantations, such as Cardamom Tea, Masala Tea, and Ginger Tea.

  • Handicrafts and Souvenirs: The souvenir shops also offer a range of handicrafts and souvenirs made from local materials, such as organic honey, coconut shells, and bamboo.

Local Culture and Customs of Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

The local culture of Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is deeply rooted in the traditions of the indigenous tribes, such as the Kadar, Malasars, and Muduvars. Visitors can attend the festivals and cultural events held in the sanctuary to witness the traditional dance forms and music.

Best Time to Visit Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is from November to February, during the winter months when the weather is pleasant, and the flora and fauna of the sanctuary are in its full glory.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Here are some general valuable tips for visitors to follow while exploring the sanctuary:

Do:

  • Carry a valid photo ID proof and permit required to enter the sanctuary.

  • Hire a guide authorized by the Forest Department to ensure a safe and enjoyable trekking experience.

  • Respect and abide by the rules and regulations laid down by the Forest Department.

Don’t:

  • Litter the forest or disturb the wildlife.

  • Carry any kind of plastics inside the sanctuary.

  • Light a fire or smoke inside the forest or near any water source.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors need to take appropriate safety measures while exploring the sanctuary’s wilderness, such as wearing sturdy footwear, carrying water and snacks, and being aware of the surroundings.

Health in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors should carry essential medications and consult a doctor before undertaking any physical activity, such as trekking or bicycle rides.

Getting to Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

The nearest airport to Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is Coimbatore International Airport, located 125 km away from the sanctuary.

Bus Stations Near Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

The nearest bus stations to Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary are at Topslip and Pollachi, which are well-connected to major cities in Kerala and Tamil Nadu.

Other Options

Travelers can also opt for private taxis or self-drive cars to reach the sanctuary.

How to Get Around in Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

The best way to explore the sanctuary’s terrain is by taking a wildlife safari or hiring a guide for trekking and biking tours.

Where to Go Next from Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Silent Valley National Park: This park is located 80 km away from Parambikulam and is another prime wildlife sanctuary in Kerala, renowned for its tropical evergreen forests, rare and endemic species, and scenic waterfalls.

  • Nelliyampathy Hill Stations: Nelliyampathy Hill Stations is a picturesque destination located in Palakkad, Kerala, known for its stunning vistas, tea and coffee plantations, and trekking trails.

Conclusion

Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts seeking an escape from urban life. The sanctuary offers a range of activities, stunning landscapes, and an opportunity to witness the rich culture and traditions of the indigenous tribes. Plan your trip to Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary and experience the wilderness, serenity and beauty of Kerala.
