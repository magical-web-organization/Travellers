Introduction to Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in the central region of Kerala, Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary is a beautiful natural reserve covering about 125 sq km. The sanctuary is surrounded by majestic hills and home to a wide variety of flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Thrissur: There is a rich cultural heritage associated with the sanctuary which is showcased in the archaeological museum in Thrissur. The museum is located at a distance of 22 km from Peechi Vazhani.

  • Kerala Kalamandalam: This is a cultural hub where visitors can experience traditional Kerala arts and dance forms. It is a one-stop destination for art lovers.

Natural Attractions of Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peechi Dam: This picturesque dam is located on the Peechi river which is the main source of water for the sanctuary. Visitors can enjoy the scenic view of the dam and also go for boating.

  • Vazhani Dam: Built on the Vazhani river, this dam is another popular spot for visitors to enjoy the beauty of the sanctuary. The open air gym near the dam is a unique attraction.

  • Kauthuka Park: This eco-tourism park showcases the biodiversity of the sanctuary with different species of plants and animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

The sanctuary has many trails for hiking and biking that take visitors through the varied terrain and diverse landscapes. Some popular trails include:

  • Poothanpandi Trail: This trail takes visitors through dense forests and offers scenic views of the sanctuary.

  • Pachy Padi Trail: This trail starts from the Kauthuka park and winds through the forest.

Best Things to Do in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

  • Safari: Visitors can take a safari tour of the sanctuary to see animals in their natural habitat. The best time for safari is early in the morning or late afternoon.

  • Birdwatching: The sanctuary is home to over 100 species of birds so this activity is recommended for bird lovers.

  • Trekking: Visitors can hire a guide to trek through the sanctuary and explore its natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Day 1: Arrive at Peechi Vazhani and check into your accommodation. Day 2: Go on a safari tour of the sanctuary and spot animals. Day 3: Go hiking on the Peechi Trail and explore the natural beauty of the sanctuary. Day 4: Explore the Vazhani Dam and relax by the water. Day 5: Visit the Kerala Kalamandalam cultural hub and experience traditional Kerala arts and dance. Day 6: Go birdwatching and observe the diverse bird species of the sanctuary. Day 7: Depart from Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary.

Food and Drink Scene of Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kerala Seafood: Being located in the coastal area, Kerala is known for its seafood dishes.

  • Appam and Stew: This is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala, consisting of fluffy pancakes made from fermented rice flour with a coconut milk-based vegetable stew.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: A local beverage made from fermented coconut water

  • Nannari Sherbet: A refreshing drink made from the roots of the Nannari plant.

Where to Stay in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • KTDC Tamarind Greebwood: This is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the sanctuary, surrounded by natural beauty.

  • Peechi Ecogreen Resorts: This eco-friendly resort offers comfortable accommodations with stunning views of the sanctuary.

  • Evergreen Homestay: This homestay is located in a village nearby the Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary and offers an authentic Kerala experience.

What to Buy in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Nettipattom: Nettipattom is a traditional ornament used in Kerala temples, and makes for a unique souvenir.

  • Spices and Tea: Kerala is known for its spices and tea, which make great souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Kerala culture is a mix of local and global influences. The main language spoken in Kerala is Malayalam, and the major religion is Hinduism. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress conservatively.

Best Time to Visit Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit the sanctuary is from November to February when the weather is pleasant and cool. This is also the peak season for tourism.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Book your safari or trekking tours in advance.
  • Respect the natural beauty of the sanctuary and do not litter.
  • Follow the guidelines given by the sanctuary officials.

Don’t:

  • Do not feed or disturb animals in the sanctuary.
  • Do not smoke inside the sanctuary premises.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Always go on tours accompanied by a guide or naturalist.
  • Carry mosquito repellent as the sanctuary is located in a forested region.
  • Do not venture out alone at night.

Health in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Check with your doctor regarding vaccinations before traveling to the sanctuary.
  • Carry necessary medication along with you.

Getting to Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Cochin International Airport: Located 70 km away, this is the closest international airport to Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary.

Bus Stations Near Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Thrissur Bus Station: Located 20 km away, Thrissur Bus Station is the closest bus station to Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary.

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire a taxi from the airport or book a bus from the bus station to reach the sanctuary.

How to Get Around in Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary

One can explore the sanctuary on foot or by hiring a bike, or hiring a car or taxi for convenience.

Where to Go Next from Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Athirappilly Waterfalls: Located at a distance of 40 km, these waterfalls are a popular tourist destination.

  • Guruvayoor Temple: This famous temple is located at a distance of 30 km from Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary and is a holy pilgrimage site for Hindus.

Conclusion

Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit for nature lovers and those who seek adventure. The sanctuary has something for everyone - from cultural attractions to natural beauty, from outdoor adventures to relaxing by the water. With the tips included above, visitors can have a memorable trip to Peechi Vazhani Wildlife Sanctuary.
