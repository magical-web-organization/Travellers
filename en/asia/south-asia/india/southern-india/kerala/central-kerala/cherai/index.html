Introduction to Cherai

Located in central Kerala, Cherai is a picturesque beach town that attracts both domestic and international tourists. With its serene backwaters, coconut groves, and pristine beaches, Cherai exudes a quaint, old-world charm that never fails to entice its visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Cherai

Cherai has a plethora of attractions to suit every kind of tourist. Below are some of the must-visit places in Cherai.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cherai Gowreeshwara Temple - This ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and architecture.
  • Kuzhupilly Beach - Famous for its historic lighthouse, Kuzhupilly Beach is ideal for a peaceful evening stroll.
  • Pallipuram Fort - Built-in 1503 by the Portuguese, this fort is one of the oldest European constructions in India and stands as a testament to India’s colonial past.

Natural Attractions of Cherai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Munambam Beach - This pristine beach has a gorgeous view of the sunset and serves as an angling hub for its tourists.
  • Cherai Beach - Known for its calm and shallow waters, Cherai Beach is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Backwaters of Cherai - One of the best ways to explore Cherai’s backwaters is on a traditional houseboat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cherai

  • Cherai to Kuzhumpilly Road - This biking route takes you on a scenic tour of Cherai’s countryside and beaches.
  • Kottapilly to Cherai - This hiking route is perfect for those who want to explore the lush greenery of Cherai’s countryside.

Best Things to Do in Cherai: Activities and Tours

  • Boat ride in Backwaters - Rejuvenate your senses with a serene boat ride on Cherai’s backwaters.
  • Fishing - The backwaters of Cherai are a great spot for angling, where you can catch various fish species such as Mangrove Jack, Snapper, Sea Bass, and Barramundi.
  • Beach Activities - Cherai Beach offers a variety of beach activities such as jet skiing, surfing, parasailing, and banana boat rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cherai

Day Plan
1 Visit the Cherai Gowreeshwara Temple and explore the beach.
2 Take a houseboat ride on the backwaters of Cherai.
3 Go fishing and have a peaceful evening stroll on the beach.
4 Explore the Kuzhupilly Beach and Pallipuram Fort.
5 Rent a bike and tour the countryside.
6 Participate in beach activities at Cherai Beach.
7 Relax and unwind on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Cherai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karimeen Pollichathu - A popular Keralan dish made with pearl spot fish that is wrapped in a banana leaf and grilled over charcoal.
  • Appam Stew - Appam is a fluffy, bowl-shaped bread made from fermented rice flour, and enjoyed with a delicious veg or meat stew.

Local Beverages

  • Tender Coconut Water - A popular drink in Kerala, tender coconut water is sweet, refreshing and available in abundance in Cherai.
  • Toddy - A fermented drink made from coconut trees, toddy is a famous drink served in bars in the town.

Where to Stay in Cherai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cherai has a range of option for every kind of traveler. Some noteworthy options are:

  • Club Mahindra Cherai Beach - A luxury resort close to the beach, this resort boasts of amazing ocean views from its rooms.
  • Sea Lagoon Health Resort - A serene and peaceful resort, it is ideal for anyone who wants to take a break from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Marari Beach - A perfect beachfront hotel for travelers looking for a peaceful ambiance and direct access to the beach.

What to Buy in Cherai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cherai Handicrafts - Cherai is famous for its intricate handicrafts made of coir, coconut shells, and palm leaves.
  • Spices - Kerala is known as the land of spices, and accordingly, you will find a range of authentic and exotic spices in Cherai markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Cherai

Cherai has a rich cultural heritage that is evident in its temple architecture, dance forms, and traditional festivals such as Onam and Vishu. Respect for elders is valued in society, and it is customary to remove footwear before entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Cherai

October to March is the best time to visit Cherai, as the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoorsy activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cherai

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Use public transportation where possible.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink in public.
  • Disrespect local temples and religious beliefs.
  • Litter on the beach or streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cherai

Cherai is generally a safe place with very low crime rates. But, tourists should always prioritize their safety by avoiding secluded areas after dark and carrying identification documents at all times.

Health in Cherai

It is advisable to seek Medical attention if you experience illness or injury, and carry a first aid kit with you. Apply mosquito repellent if you are visiting outdoor areas, as Cherai is known to have a mosquito problem.

Getting to Cherai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cherai

Cochin International Airport is the closest airport to Cherai, which is approximately thirty kilometers by road.

Bus Stations Near Cherai

Cherai has its bus station from where buses regularly ply to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

One can also opt to hire a taxi or rent a car for a convenient commute to and from Cherai.

How to Get Around in Cherai

Hiring an autorickshaw or taxi is the most common way to get around in Cherai. The town is also bike-friendly, and bicycles are easily available on rent.

Where to Go Next from Cherai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fort Kochi - Fort Kochi is an amalgamation of Portuguese, Dutch, and British cultures and traditions.
  • Munnar - A scenic hill station full of tea plantations.
  • Alleppey - A must-visit for its backwaters, houseboats, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

With its tranquil beaches, lush backwaters, and serene countryside, Cherai offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Cherai will undoubtedly provide an unforgettable experience that’s a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.
