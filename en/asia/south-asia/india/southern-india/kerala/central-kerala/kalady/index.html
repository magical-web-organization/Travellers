Introduction to Kalady

Kalady is a small town located in Central Kerala, in Southern India. It is renowned for being the birthplace of Adi Shankaracharya, a great philosopher who is credited with reviving Hinduism and establishing the Advaita Vedanta philosophy. Kalady is located on the banks of the Periyar River and is surrounded by lush greenery, which makes it a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful and relaxing vacation amidst nature.

Kalady is home to several cultural and natural attractions that make it a popular tourist destination. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Kalady:

  • Shankara Janmabhoomi
  • Sri Ramakrishna Advaita Ashram

Natural Attractions of Kalady: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Local Culture and Customs of Kalady

Kalady is a traditional Indian town that follows customs and traditions that are unique to the region. Visitors are advised to respect the local culture and customs and dress appropriately when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Kalady

The best time to visit Kalady is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and the humidity is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalady

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious places.
  • Try local food and beverages.
  • Explore the town on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public.
  • Disrespect religious sentiments.
  • Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kalady

Kalady is a safe destination, but visitors are advised to take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid traveling alone at night and keep a close eye on your belongings.

Health in Kalady

Visitors should take precautions against common tropical diseases such as malaria and dengue. It’s advisable to carry insect repellent and wear protective clothing.

Kalady is well-connected to the rest of Kerala by road and rail. It is located 10 km from Cochin International Airport, which is the nearest airport. Here are some transportation options to reach Kalady:
