Introduction to Kochi

Located in Central Kerala, Kochi is a popular tourist destination for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Also known as the Queen of the Arabian Sea, Kochi is a vibrant and bustling city that is unlike any other in India. This historic port city has seen the passage of traders and merchants from across the world, which has left a significant influence on its food, culture, and people.

In this travel guide, we have covered everything you need to know about Kochi, including the list of places to visit, food, hiking/biking routes, local culture, customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Kochi

There is no shortage of unique things to see and do in Kochi. Here is a list of attractions you must visit during your stay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi is a neighborhood located in the southwest of Kochi. It is famous for its colonial buildings, narrow streets flanked by Chinese-style fishing nets, and European-style churches.

Jewish Synagogue

Located in the heart of Jew Town, the Jewish Synagogue is the oldest synagogue in the Commonwealth of Nations. It features intricate Belgian glass chandeliers and hand-painted Cantonese porcelain tiles.

Dutch Palace

The Dutch Palace, also known as Mattancherry Palace, is an impressive structure with an ornate entryway, large halls, and a courtyard garden. Inside, you will find beautiful 17th-century murals depicting scenes from Hindu mythology.

Kerala Folklore Museum

This museum houses artifacts and tools from various regions in Kerala, such as farming tools, musical instruments, costumes, and masks.

Natural Attractions of Kochi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kerala Backwaters

Kochi is the ideal gateway to exploring Kerala’s famous backwaters. Take a relaxing boat ride and enjoy the stunning views of lush green paddy fields, coconut groves, and peaceful villages.

Hill Palace Museum

The Hill Palace Museum is located in Tripunithura and is the largest archaeological museum in Kerala. It has a vast collection of artifacts, including weapons, paintings, and sculptures from ancient Kerala.

Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary

Mangalavanam is a protected bird sanctuary located in the heart of Kochi. It is home to over 200 species of birds, including migratory birds.

Cherai Beach

Located just outside the city, Cherai Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Kerala. Its calm waters and long stretches of clean sand attract beach lovers from all over the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Kochi

Kochi is a multicultural city with rich traditions, festivals, and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind when exploring the city:

  • Cover your head and remove your shoes while visiting religious places.
  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photographs.
  • Do not touch or disturb the fishing nets in the harbour area.

Best Time to Visit Kochi

The best time to visit Kochi is from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant. Monsoons last from June to September and can be heavy, making it difficult to get around.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kochi

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Bargain when shopping in local markets
  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious customs and traditions
  • Litter or pollute the environment
  • Drink tap water without boiling or filtering

Safety and Health

Safety in Kochi

Kochi is generally safe for tourists, but it’s essential to take a few precautions:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place.
  • Use licensed taxis and avoid hailing cabs off the street.

Health in Kochi

It’s essential to take necessary precautions while traveling to avoid falling sick:

  • Drink bottled water or boiled and filtered water.
  • Use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and malaria.
  • Carry your medication and prescriptions with you.
