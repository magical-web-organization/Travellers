Introduction to North Paravur

North Paravur, located in Central Kerala, is a hidden gem packed with rich culture and natural beauty. With its serene backwaters, historic sites, and lush greenery, this town is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life.

As you explore North Paravur, you’ll discover a mix of Hindu and Christian influences, which have shaped the town’s history and local traditions. Despite its small size, there are many activities to enjoy here, from hiking and biking to shopping for local handicrafts. This travel guide will provide all the information you need to plan your trip to North Paravur, Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in North Paravur

North Paravur offers a wide variety of attractions for visitors to enjoy, including culturally important museums and historical sites, as well as natural outdoor activities. Here are some of the must-see destinations in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malayattoor Church

Malayattoor Church is one of the most famous pilgrimage sites in North Paravur. Located on the banks of the Periyar River, the church is a testament to the deep Christian faith that permeates the town. Pilgrims come from all parts of Kerala to pray at this holy site.

Kottayil Kovilakam

Go back in time and discover the history of the royal family of North Paravur. Kottayil Kovilakam is a heritage palace that offers a peek into the lives and legacy of the rulers of the town. The palace is located near the peaceful backwaters and is open to visitors all year round.

Cherai Beach

With its sandy shores and clear blue waters, Cherai Beach is a popular destination for sunbathing and swimming. Visitors can also take a stroll along the beach or try their hand at water sports like kayaking and windsurfing.

Natural Attractions of North Paravur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thattekad Bird Sanctuary

Located just 20 kilometers from North Paravur, Thattekad Bird Sanctuary is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. This protected forest reserve is home to more than 500 species of birds, including many that are rare or endangered.

Kodanad Elephant Training Center

Kodanad Elephant Training Center is a unique destination that offers visitors the chance to see these magnificent animals up close. Visitors can watch the elephants bathing in the river or being trained in traditional techniques like timber dragging.

Periyar River

Periyar River is one of the most important waterways in Kerala and it flows right through North Paravur. Visitors can take a boat ride or go kayaking along the river to see the lush greenery and glimpse local villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Paravur

There are also plenty of hiking and biking trails in North Paravur that offer stunning views of the landscape. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Kumbalanghi mangroves
  • Paravur backwaters
  • Paniyeli Poru

Best Things to Do in North Paravur: Activities and Tours

In North Paravur, you’ll never run out of things to do. Here are some top experiences:

  • Enjoy a houseboat ride through the backwaters
  • Go birdwatching in Thattekad Bird Sanctuary
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Kerala dishes
  • Experience the local culture with a visit to a Kathakali dance performance

Week-Long Itinerary for North Paravur

Day 1: Visit Malayattoor Church and enjoy a boat ride on the Periyar River Day 2: Spend a day at Cherai Beach, enjoying the sun and sea Day 3: Discover the local history at Kottayil Kovilakam and visit the Kadambrayar Boating Center Day 4: Spend a day at Thattekad Bird Sanctuary, enjoying the natural beauty and birdwatching opportunities Day 5: Visit the Kodanad Elephant Training Center and learn about Kerala’s elephants and their culture Day 6: Go hiking or biking along the Paravur backwaters and then take a cooking class to learn more about traditional Kerala cuisine Day 7: Visit a Kathakali performance and immerse yourself in Kerala’s traditional culture.

Food and Drink Scene of North Paravur

Kerala is famous for its delicious cuisine, and North Paravur is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of dishes, from fish curry and rice to traditional snacks like banana chips.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Appam and Stew
  • Fish Curry and Rice
  • Spicy Fried Chicken (Nadan Kozhi Porichathu)
  • Banana Chips

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water
  • Tea
  • Fresh fruit juice

Local Culture and Customs of North Paravur

North Paravur has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the town’s local customs and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of these customs, which include:

  • Removing shoes before entering homes and temples
  • Dressing modestly, especially when visiting religious sites

Best Time to Visit North Paravur

The best time to visit North Paravur is between October and March, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The monsoon season, from June to September, can make travel difficult and some attractions may be closed during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Paravur

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly
  • Carry cash, as some small shops may not accept cards

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or customs
  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in North Paravur

North Paravur is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take basic precautions like:

  • Avoiding unlit areas at night
  • Keeping an eye on your valuables
  • Staying aware of your surroundings

Health in North Paravur

Visitors to North Paravur should take care to drink only bottled or boiled water and to avoid street food and uncooked vegetables. It’s also advisable to carry mosquito repellent and to seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to North Paravur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Paravur

Cochin International Airport is the nearest airport to North Paravur, located 23 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach North Paravur.

Bus Stations Near North Paravur

North Paravur has its own bus station, which connects the town with nearby destinations. Visitors can travel to North Paravur by bus from cities like Kochi, Trivandrum, and Kozhikode.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach North Paravur by train or taxi. The nearest railway station is in Aluva, 22 kilometers away.

How to Get Around in North Paravur

Visitors can easily get around North Paravur by taxi, auto-rickshaw, or rented vehicle like a scooter or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from North Paravur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations that visitors can explore from North Paravur, including:

  • Kochi
  • Munnar
  • Kollam
  • Alleppey
  • Thrissur

Conclusion

North Paravur is a unique destination that offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From exploring the local museums and historical sites to hiking through the lush greenery and sampling the delicious cuisine, there’s something for every traveler in this charming town. So pack your bags and get ready to discover the beauty of North Paravur, Kerala.
