Valapad Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Valapad is a charming town located in Central Kerala, in Southern India. It boasts a mix of natural and cultural attractions, making it a great destination for those looking to explore the region’s history and natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll go over everything you need to know to plan your trip to Valapad.

Introduction to Valapad

Valapad is a coastal town that lies at the confluence of the Bharathapuzha and Arabian sea. It is a historically significant place that was once the hub of trade and commerce in the region. The town is famous for its ancient temples, serene backwaters, and pristine beaches. It is also known for its cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Valapad

The following are the must-visit attractions that provide a glimpse into the history, culture, and natural beauty of Valapad.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kottakkal Bhagavathy Temple
  • Pattambi Nercha
  • Tirunavaya Temple
  • Kizhthali Temple
  • Kunchan Nambiar Smarakam
  • Viharasala Temple

Natural Attractions of Valapad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valapad Beach
  • Bharathapuzha River
  • Kakkayam Dam
  • Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Silent Valley National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valapad

Valapad has some of the most scenic biking and hiking routes in Kerala. The following are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Valapad - Mala - Kodungallur
  • Valapad - Thriprayar - Chavakkad
  • Valapad - Vadanappally - Nadavarambu

Best Things to Do in Valapad: Activities and Tours

Valapad offers a plethora of exciting activities and tours that cater to diverse interests. The following are some of the best things to do in Valapad:

  • Visit the ancient temples and learn about their history and significance.
  • Explore the serene backwaters on a houseboat or a canoe.
  • Witness the Kalari Payattu martial arts demonstration.
  • Indulge in local delicacies and street food.
  • Go bird watching and wildlife spotting.
  • Attend cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valapad

If you’re planning to spend a week in Valapad, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Kottakkal Bhagavathy Temple and explore its architecture and history.
2 Take a houseboat ride and explore the serene backwaters while enjoying local delicacies.
3 Enjoy a day trip to Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary and go on a wildlife spotting excursion.
4 Explore the beaches of Valapad and nearby towns like Vadanappally and Chavakkad.
5 Visit the Tirunavaya Temple and learn about its historical and cultural significance.
6 Attend a cultural event or festival and immerse yourself in local traditions.
7 Experience a Kalaripayattu martial arts demonstration and indulge in street food before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Valapad

Valapad has a rich gastronomic culture that centers around seafood, coconut, and spices. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Malabar Biriyani
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Avial
  • Fish Curry
  • Appam and Egg Roast

Local Beverages

  • Elaneer (Tender Coconut Water)
  • Sulaimani (Black Tea with Lime and Spices)
  • Sharbath (Sweet Drinks made with Rose or Lemon)

What to Buy in Valapad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Valapad is known for its handicrafts, spices, and traditional clothing. Some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products are:

  • Banana Chips
  • Ayurvedic Products
  • Kasavu Sarees and Dhotis
  • Handicrafts and Brasswares

Local Culture and Customs of Valapad

Valapad has a rich cultural heritage that is shaped by the influences of various ethnic and religious groups. The following are some of the customs and traditions that are prevalent in Valapad:

  • The town celebrates several festivals throughout the year that are characterized by elaborate processions, feasts, and cultural events.
  • The locals are known for their hospitality and warm nature.
  • Valapad is home to a significant Muslim population, and the culture and traditions of the community have a significant influence on the town’s customs.

Best Time to Visit Valapad

The best time to visit Valapad is from November to February when the weather is pleasant, and the town is bustling with cultural events and festivals. However, if you’re looking to enjoy water sports and outdoor activities, the monsoon season between June and September can be a great choice.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valapad

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local delicacies and beverages.
  • Carry light and comfortable clothing as the weather can be humid.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect any religious sentiments.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Engage in any illegal activities or behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valapad

Overall, Valapad is a safe town, and the locals are friendly and hospitable. However, it’s always advisable to be cautious and vigilant, especially when traveling solo or at night.

Health in Valapad

Valapad has excellent healthcare facilities, and most hospitals have English speaking staff. However, it’s always advisable to carry essential medications and consult a doctor before traveling to Valapad.

Getting to Valapad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Valapad is well connected by road, rail, and air. The following are the transportation options to reach Valapad:

Airports Near Valapad

  • Cochin International Airport (60 km)
  • Calicut International Airport (90 km)

Bus Stations Near Valapad

  • Valapad Bus Stand (Local Buses)
  • Thrissur Bus Stand (Interstate Buses)
  • Kunnamkulam Bus Stand (Interstate Buses)

Other Options

  • Hire a taxi from Cochin or Calicut to reach Valapad.
  • Take a train to Thrissur Railway Station and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to Valapad.

How to Get Around in Valapad

Valapad is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by hiring a taxi or auto-rickshaw. Bicycles and motorbikes are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Valapad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Valapad is located in a region that is rich in cultural, historical, and natural attractions. The following are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore:

  • Thrissur (23 km)
  • Guruvayur (25 km)
  • Malappuram (50 km)
  • Cochin (60 km)
  • Calicut (90 km)

Conclusion

Valapad is a hidden gem in Kerala that offers a harmonious blend of nature, culture, and history. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you can plan your trip to Valapad and explore all the fascinating attractions and activities it has to offer. So, pack your bags and get ready to experience the vibrant and dynamic culture of Valapad.
