Introduction to Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary

Nestled in the heart of Kerala, South India, Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary is a true paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Spread over an area of 85 square kilometers, this sanctuary is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, leopards, wild boars, and a variety of birds and reptiles.

Visitors can immerse themselves in the lush greenery and tranquil surroundings, explore the local culture, and experience myriad outdoor activities. From hiking and biking to wildlife safaris and birdwatching, Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary offers something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, activities to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary is predominantly known for its natural beauty, it also offers visitors the chance to explore local culture and history. Here are a few key cultural attractions to add to your itinerary:

  • Thumboormuzhi Dam and Gardens
  • Ethnographic Museum of Thrissur
  • Vilangan Kunnu
  • Archaeological Museum of Thrissur

Local Culture and Customs of Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary

As a visitor, it’s important to respect local culture and customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress appropriately, covering knees and shoulders when visiting temples
  • Remove footwear when entering temples or homes
  • Seek permission before taking photos of people or their property
  • Do not litter or damage natural or cultural heritage sites

Best Time to Visit Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary is between the months of August and March. This time period offers the most pleasant weather with fewer chances of rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Carry an ID card at all times
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the sanctuary
  • Respect the wildlife and refrain from feeding them
  • Stay on designated trails while hiking

Don’t:

  • Litter or dispose of waste inappropriately
  • Carry plastic bags or bottles
  • Smoke or light fires in the sanctuary
  • Carry firearms or other self-defense mechanisms

Safety and Health

Safety in Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary

While Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary is a safe place to visit, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions such as:

  • Avoid visiting remote areas of the sanctuary alone
  • Follow safety protocols while participating in adventure activities
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife

Health in Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary

While there are no mandatory vaccines or medications required to visit Chimmony Wildlife Sanctuary, it is always best to consult with a medical professional before traveling to India. Visitors are also advised to carry insect repellent, sunscreen, and first aid kits.

The sanctuary has limited public transportation options, so visitors are advised to arrange for private transportation for their entire stay. Popular options include renting cars or motorbikes, or hiring a taxi.
