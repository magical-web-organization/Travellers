Introduction to Chavakkad

Chavakkad is a quaint town situated in Central Kerala, in the southern part of India. It boasts of a rich cultural heritage and is a lesser-known gem of the state. The town is known for its breathtaking beaches, verdant paddy fields, and religious significance. It is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an offbeat location to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Chavakkad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chavakkad has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historical places and museums. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Thiruvanchikulam Mahadeva Temple: Built in the 9th century, this temple is one of the oldest in Kerala and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple: This temple is one of the most popular in the region and is dedicated to the goddess Bhadrakali.
  • Kerala Kalamandalam: A premier institution for performing arts, this place has been imparting training in traditional art forms such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Koodiyattam for the last 81 years.

Natural Attractions of Chavakkad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chavakkad is blessed with scenic natural beauty, making it a perfect place to enjoy outdoor activities. Some of the must-visit natural attractions are:

  • Chavakkad Beach: This beautiful beach is famous for its golden sands and picturesque sunset views.
  • Punnathurkotta Elephant Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to more than 50 elephants and provides an opportunity to observe these gentle giants up close.
  • Anamala Hills: These hills offer incredible scenic beauty and are an ideal spot for hiking and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chavakkad

Chavakkad has several trails ideal for hiking and biking. One of the most popular routes is the Tippy Village Trail, which offers spectacular views of lush green paddy fields and coconut plantations.

Best Things to Do in Chavakkad: Activities and Tours

Chavakkad offers several exciting activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best ones are:

  • Kathakali Performance: Watch a traditional Kathakali performance at Kerala Kalamandalam.
  • Village Tour: Take a guided tour to the nearby villages to learn about the local culture and lifestyles.
  • Elephant Ride: Take a ride on an elephant at Punnathurkotta Elephant Sanctuary.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chavakkad

Day 1: Arrival and rest

Day 2: Visit Thiruvanchikulam Mahadeva Temple and Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple

Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Chavakkad by visiting Chavakkad Beach and Anamala Hills.

Day 4: Take a village tour to learn about the local culture and traditions

Day 5: Visit Kerala Kalamandalam and attend a Kathakali performance

Day 6: Go for an elephant ride at Punnathurkotta Elephant Sanctuary

Day 7: Departure from Chavakkad

Food and Drink Scene of Chavakkad

Must-Try Dishes

Chavakkad is known for its spicy and delicious cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Meen Curry: A fish curry made with coconut milk and spices.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: A popular breakfast dish made with steamed rice cake and chickpea curry.
  • Ela Ada: A sweet delicacy made with rice flour, coconut, and jaggery.

Local Beverages

Kerala is known for its unique beverages, and Chavakkad is no exception. Some of the local beverages to try are:

  • Toddy: A locally brewed alcoholic beverage made from coconut palms.
  • Sambharam: A refreshing buttermilk-based drink flavored with ginger and green chilies.

Where to Stay in Chavakkad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chavakkad has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some of the best ones are:

  • Chavakkad Beach Resort: A beachfront resort with luxurious amenities.
  • Niya Nest Homestay: A budget-friendly homestay located just a few kilometers away from the beach.
  • Hotel Sangamam: A mid-range hotel located in the heart of the town.

What to Buy in Chavakkad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chavakkad is known for its traditional handicrafts and textiles. Some of the best things to buy as souvenirs are:

  • Handloom Products: Traditional handloom sarees, dhotis, and towels are popular among tourists.
  • Coir Products: Coir mats, bags, and baskets are eco-friendly and make for unique souvenirs.
  • Wooden Handicrafts: Locally made wooden toys, home decor items, and furniture are a must-buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Chavakkad

The locals of Chavakkad lead a simple and traditional lifestyle. The town is known for its rich cultural heritage and festivals. The most popular festival celebrated here is the Kumbha Bharani festival, which attracts thousands of devotees every year.

Best Time to Visit Chavakkad

The best time to visit Chavakkad is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chavakkad

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take off your footwear before entering a temple.
  • Eat local cuisines and try the unique beverages.
  • Carry mosquito repellent during the monsoon season.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting temples.
  • Litter or pollute the beaches and other natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the elephants at the Punnathurkotta Elephant Sanctuary.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chavakkad

Chavakkad is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advisable to take care of your belongings and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Chavakkad

Chavakkad has several hospitals and clinics that provide medical assistance for tourists. It is advisable to carry a first aid kit and necessary medications while traveling.

Getting to Chavakkad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chavakkad

The nearest airport to Chavakkad is Cochin International Airport, which is about 60 kilometers away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a bus to reach Chavakkad.

Bus Stations Near Chavakkad

Chavakkad has a bus station that connects it to major cities in Kerala.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or a taxi to reach Chavakkad from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Chavakkad

Chavakkad has an extensive bus network that connects it to nearby towns and cities. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Chavakkad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chavakkad is located close to several popular tourist destinations in Kerala. Some of the places to visit are:

  • Guruvayur: A famous temple town located just 6 kilometers away from Chavakkad.
  • Thrissur: Known as the cultural capital of Kerala, Thrissur is located 30 kilometers away from Chavakkad.
  • Athirapally Waterfalls: One of the most stunning waterfalls in Kerala, located 60 kilometers away from Chavakkad.

Conclusion

Chavakkad is a hidden gem of Kerala that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With its beautiful beaches, iconic temples, and exciting outdoor activities, Chavakkad is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an offbeat location to explore.
