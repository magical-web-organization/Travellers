Introduction to Kodungallur

Kodungallur is a historic town located in Central Kerala in southern India. Known for its rich cultural heritage and ancient temples, Kodungallur has been a popular pilgrimage site for centuries. It is located on the banks of the Periyar River, and surrounded by the Western Ghats to the east and the Arabian Sea to the west. The town is also known for its vibrant festivals, such as the Kodungallur Bharani Festival and Kavu Theendal Festival.

List of Places to Visit in Kodungallur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cheraman Juma Masjid

Cheraman Juma Masjid is one of the oldest mosques in India, located in Kodungallur town. It was built by Malik bin Dinar, an Arab missionary, in the 7th century AD. The mosque is said to have been built on the site where the Chera king, Chera Perumal, converted to Islam.

Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple

Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Bhadrakali. The temple is located on the banks of the Periyar River and is considered to be one of the most important temples in Kerala. The temple is known for its annual festival, the Kodungallur Bharani Festival.

Koodalmanickyam Temple

Koodalmanickyam Temple is an ancient Hindu temple located in Irinjalakuda near Kodungallur. The temple is dedicated to Lord Bharatha, the brother of Lord Rama. The temple is known for its beautiful carvings and sculptures, and the Melpathur Narayana Bhattathiri Smarakam, a monument dedicated to the poet Melpathur Narayana Bhattathiri.

Natural Attractions of Kodungallur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Munakkal Beach

Munakkal Beach is a beautiful and secluded beach located in Kodungallur. The beach is known for its golden sands and clear waters, and is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing. The beach also offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

Athirapally Waterfalls

Athirapally Waterfalls is a beautiful waterfall located near Kodungallur. The waterfall is about 80 feet high and is surrounded by lush green forests. It is a popular spot for trekking and photography, and also a good place to spot wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kodungallur

Kodungallur has plenty of options for those who love outdoor activities. There are several trekking and hiking routes that offer beautiful views of the Western Ghats and the Periyar River. One of the most popular routes is the Kodungallur to Irinjalakuda route, which takes you through some beautiful scenery and offers a chance to explore the local culture.

Best Things to Do in Kodungallur: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Kodungallur, from exploring ancient temples to enjoying the beautiful beaches. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Kodungallur Bharani Festival: One of the most vibrant festivals in Kerala, held annually in Kodungallur.
  • Backwater tours: Take a boat ride along the backwaters of Kodungallur and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Cherai Beach: Visit Cherai Beach, which is located just a short distance from Kodungallur town, and enjoy the beautiful views and water activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kodungallur

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your accommodation. Day 2: Visit the Kodungallur Bhagavathy Temple and the Cheraman Juma Masjid. Day 3: Explore the backwaters of Kodungallur with a boat ride or kayak tour. Day 4: Visit the Koodalmanickyam Temple and explore the nearby town of Irinjalakuda. Day 5: Trek to the Athirapally Waterfalls and enjoy the lush green forests and stunning views. Day 6: Relax on Munakkal Beach and enjoy the gorgeous scenery. Day 7: Departure from Kodungallur.

Food and Drink Scene of Kodungallur

Must-Try Dishes

Kodungallur is famous for its seafood and traditional Kerala dishes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Meen Curry: Spicy fish curry made with coconut milk and local spices.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu: Grilled fish wrapped in banana leaves and cooked with spices.
  • Appam with Stew: Soft, fluffy rice pancakes served with a vegetable or meat stew.

Local Beverages

Kerala is known for its tea and coffee, and Kodungallur is no exception. Some of the best local beverages to try include:

  • Masala Chai: Spiced tea made with milk, tea leaves, and local spices.
  • Filter Coffee: Strong coffee made with roasted beans and served with milk.
  • Payasam: A sweet, creamy dessert made with rice, milk, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Kodungallur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kodungallur has plenty of options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top options include:

  • KTDC Tamarind Easy Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Kodungallur town.
  • Soma Houseboat: A unique accommodation option that allows you to stay on a traditional Kerala houseboat.
  • The Malabar House: A luxury hotel located on the banks of the Periyar River, offering stunning views of the river and the Western Ghats.

What to Buy in Kodungallur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kodungallur is famous for its handicrafts and traditional textiles. Some of the top items to buy include:

  • Kasavu Sarees: Handwoven sarees made with local cotton and gold thread.
  • Coir Products: Handmade mats, baskets, and other products made with coir fiber.
  • Handicrafts: Traditional wooden toys, souvenirs made with coconut shells, and other handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Kodungallur

Kodungallur has a rich cultural heritage, and locals are proud of their traditions and customs. One of the most important festivals in the town is the Kodungallur Bharani Festival, which is held in honor of the goddess Bhadrakali. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Kodungallur

The best time to visit Kodungallur is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and dry. The town gets crowded during the festival season, especially during the Kodungallur Bharani Festival, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kodungallur

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  • Learn about the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Damage the natural environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kodungallur

Kodungallur is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety. It is always a good idea to carry a copy of your passport and other important documents with you, and to avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Kodungallur

Visitors to Kodungallur should take basic health precautions, such as drinking bottled water and using insect repellent to protect against mosquitoes. It is also a good idea to get vaccinated for common diseases like typhoid and hepatitis A before traveling to India.

Getting to Kodungallur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kodungallur

The nearest airport to Kodungallur is Cochin International Airport, which is located about 40 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach Kodungallur.

Bus Stations Near Kodungallur

Kodungallur has a bus station located in the town center, which is connected to major cities in Kerala and other nearby states.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kodungallur by train, with the nearest railway station located in Irinjalakuda.

How to Get Around in Kodungallur

Kodungallur is a relatively small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bike. Local buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Kodungallur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kodungallur is located in the heart of Kerala, and there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Some of the top options include:

  • Kochi: A vibrant city known for its Dutch colonial architecture and vibrant food scene.
  • Munnar: A hill station located in the Western Ghats, known for its stunning scenery and tea plantations.
  • Alleppey: A popular destination for backwater tours and houseboat stays.

Conclusion

Kodungallur is a beautiful and culturally rich destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient temples, relaxing on a beautiful beach, or trekking through lush forests, Kodungallur has something to offer. Visitors should be sure to try the local cuisine and learn about the town’s rich cultural heritage while exploring this vibrant and fascinating destination.
