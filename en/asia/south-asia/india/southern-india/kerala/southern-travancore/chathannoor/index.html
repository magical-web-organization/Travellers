Introduction to Chathannoor

Chathannoor is a town in the southern part of the Travancore region of Kerala, India, known for its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. The town is surrounded by lush green forests on the eastern side and the Ashtamudi Lake on the western side, making it a perfect getaway for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Chathannoor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sree Narayana Guru Mandiram: A memorial dedicated to Sree Narayana Guru, a social reformer and philosopher who worked for the upliftment of the socially and economically oppressed sections of the society.
  • Pallikkal Bhagavathy Temple: An ancient temple dedicated to the goddess Bhagavathy, located on the banks of the Kallada river. The temple is known for its panoramic views and unique architecture.
  • Kottukal Rock Cut Cave Temple: A historical temple that dates back to the 8th century AD, known for its rock-cut architecture and intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Chathannoor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ashtamudi Lake: A scenic lake with calm waters, surrounded by lush green forests and hills. The lake is ideal for boating, fishing, and bird watching.
  • Aryanad Sree Mahadeva Temple: A temple located atop a hill with panoramic views of the surrounding valley. The temple is known for its architecture and the annual temple festival.
  • Adavi Eco Tourism: A forest reserve with lush greenery, waterfalls, and natural springs. Visitors can enjoy trekking, bird watching, and camping in the jungle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chathannoor

  • Karakonam-Thenmala: A scenic route that passes through dense forests and waterfalls. The route is perfect for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Ariyankavu-Motta: A challenging route that passes through the western ghats, offering breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys. The route is ideal for experienced hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Chathannoor: Activities and Tours

  • Boat ride in Ashtamudi Lake: Take a boat ride to explore the tranquil waters and surrounding hills and forests.
  • Visit to Adavi Eco Tourism: Take a trek or go camping in the forest reserve, surrounded by lush greenery and waterfalls.
  • Explore local temples: Visit the ancient temples in the region known for their unique architecture and historical significance.

Food and Drink Scene of Chathannoor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alleppy Fish Curry: A spicy fish curry made with fresh fish and coconut milk.
  • Puttu: A popular breakfast dish made with rice flour and coconut.
  • Kappa and Meen Curry: Boiled tapioca served with spicy fish curry.

Local Beverages

  • Kerala Chai: A popular tea made with milk, sugar, and ginger.
  • Toddy: A local alcoholic beverage made from coconut palm sap.

Where to Stay in Chathannoor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kollam Beach Retreat: A beachfront resort with comfortable accommodation and modern amenities.
  • Kattil Beach Resort: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the beach.
  • The Raviz Resort and Spa, Ashtamudi: A luxury resort with stunning views of the Ashtamudi Lake.

What to Buy in Chathannoor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coconut Oil: The region is known for its high-quality coconut oil, which is used in cooking, hair care, and skin care.
  • Traditional Paintings: The region is known for its vibrant and colorful paintings that depict the local culture and traditions.
  • Spices: Chathannoor is known for its exquisite spices like black pepper, cardamom, and cloves.

Local Culture and Customs of Chathannoor

The culture of Chathannoor is deeply rooted in Hinduism and reflects the historical significance of the region. The locals celebrate various festivals throughout the year and host colorful processions and cultural shows.

Best Time to Visit Chathannoor

The best time to visit Chathannoor is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chathannoor

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and other places of worship.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter the surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chathannoor

Chathannoor is a relatively safe town, and visitors should take standard safety precautions while traveling.

Health in Chathannoor

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and avoid street food to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Chathannoor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chathannoor

  • Trivandrum International Airport: The nearest airport, located around 50 km from Chathannoor.

Bus Stations Near Chathannoor

  • Kottiyam Bus Stand: The nearest bus stand, located around 5 km from Chathannoor.

Other Options

  • Taxi/cab services are available from Trivandrum airport to Chathannoor.
  • Visitors can also take a train to Kollam, which is around 12 km from Chathannoor, and then take a taxi or bus to Chathannoor.

How to Get Around in Chathannoor

Visitors can hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw to get around the town, or they can use the local buses to explore nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Chathannoor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kollam: A historic port town located around 12 km from Chathannoor, known for its magnificent backwaters and traditional industries like cashew nut processing.
  • Thiruvananthapuram: The state capital, located around 50 km from Chathannoor, known for its stunning beaches, palaces, and museums.
