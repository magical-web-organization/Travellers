Introduction to Varkala

Nestled in Southern Travancore, Kerala, Varkala boasts of gentle seaside cliffs, pristine beaches, buzzing markets, ancient temples, and scenic backwaters. A quintessential South Indian town, Varkala is known for its cultural significance, breathtaking natural attractions, and exhilarating outdoor adventures.

Explore the magical land of Varkala with our comprehensive travel guide, featuring the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Varkala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Janardanaswamy Temple

Boasting of a rich history, the Janardanaswamy Temple is one of the most well-known temples in Kerala. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the temple is believed to have been built during the 12th century and is a testament to the region’s architectural marvels.

Anjengo Fort

Anjengo Fort is a historical landmark located near Varkala beach. Built during the British Colonial period, the fort is an excellent place to unravel the history of the region. Tourists can visit the lighthouse located within the fort and enjoy the panoramic views of the Arabian sea.

Natural Attractions of Varkala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Varkala Beach

Varkala Beach is a seaside wonderland with spectacular cliffs that rise along the coastline. Take a dip in the calm waters of the Arabian Sea or sunbathe by the sea cliffs. Tourists can also explore the areas surrounding the beach, which includes a vibrant market selling fresh seafood, handicrafts, and other unique souvenirs.

Kappil Lake

Kappil Lake is a serene spot nestled between the sea and the backwaters. Enjoy a boat ride and soak in breathtaking scenic views, including thick mangrove forests, palm groves, and coconut plantations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varkala

Varkala boasts of several trekking and cycling trails for adventure enthusiasts. Some popular trails include the Papanasam Cliff, Edava Beach, and Sand Beach.

Best Things to Do in Varkala: Activities and Tours

Ayurvedic Treatments

Varkala is renowned for its authentic Ayurvedic treatments. The town has several Ayurvedic spas that offer a range of massages, body treatments, and wellness therapies.

Beach Volleyball

In Varkala, beach volleyball is a popular recreational activity. Visitors can enjoy a friendly game of beach volleyball or watch the locals play.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varkala

For travelers looking to spend an entire week in Varkala, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Janardanaswamy Temple and Anjengo Fort
Day 2: Relax on Varkala Beach, enjoy watersports and a visit to the market
Day 3: Day trip to Kappil Lake
Day 4: Trek along Papanasam Cliff
Day 5: Cycling on Edava Beach
Day 6: Rejuvenate with Ayurvedic treatments
Day 7: Visit the Sand Beach and enjoy beach volleyball.

Food and Drink Scene of Varkala

Must-Try Dishes

Varkala is famous for its spicy food and seafood delicacies. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Kerala-style fish curry
  • Appam with stew
  • Kottu Parotta
  • Thattu Dosa
  • Erachi Varutharacha Curry

Local Beverages

Enjoy refreshing local beverages such as:

  • Toddy
  • Coconut water
  • Butter milk
  • Tea and Coffee

Where to Stay in Varkala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Varkala has a range of accommodation options for tourists. Some popular options include:

  • Gateway Varkala
  • Boho Beach Hostel
  • Skylark Beach Resort
  • Deshadan Cliff & Beach Resort

What to Buy in Varkala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists can bring back souvenirs such as:

  • Handicrafts
  • Seashells
  • Local spices and tea
  • Handwoven garments
  • Coconut oil

Local Culture and Customs of Varkala

Varkala has a rich cultural history and is known for its religious festivals, including the Janardana Swamy Temple Festival and the Sree Narayana Jayanthi.

Tourists are advised to respect local customs and dress appropriately while visiting temples and other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Varkala

The best time to visit Varkala is from October to March when the weather is mild, and humidity is low. Tourists can also visit the city during the monsoon season (June to September) to enjoy lush greenery and the cool breeze.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varkala

Do:

  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and seafood delicacies
  • Buy local handicrafts and products
  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals

Don’t:

  • Litter on beaches and other public places
  • Engage in illegal activities
  • Disturb the natural ecosystem

Safety and Health

Safety in Varkala

While Varkala is a relatively safe destination, tourists are advised to be cautious with their belongings and avoid unlit areas at night.

Health in Varkala

Tourists are encouraged to stay hydrated and carry a mosquito repellent as Varkala is a tropical region that is prone to mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Varkala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Varkala

The closest airport to Varkala is the Trivandrum International Airport, which is 40 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Varkala

Varkala has its bus stand and is well-connected to other major cities in Kerala.

Other Options

Tourists can also travel to Varkala by train, and the town has its railway station.

How to Get Around in Varkala

Tourists can get around Varkala by taxis, autos, buses, and rent-a-car services.

Conclusion

Varkala is a beautiful destination for travelers looking to explore the rich culture, heritage, and natural beauty of South India. With its lush greenery, serene beaches, adventure trails, and vibrant markets, Varkala is truly a traveler’s paradise.
