Edava, located in Southern Travancore, Kerala, is a small coastal town famous for its pristine beaches and scenic vistas. It is located about 30 kilometers from Thiruvananthapuram, the state capital, and is a popular tourist destination due to its proximity to the Arabian sea. The town is rich in tradition and culture and is home to many temples and museums. This comprehensive travel guide provides a list of must-visit places, things to do, and other relevant information to make your visit to Edava a memorable experience.

Introduction to Edava

Edava is a serene town located on the western coast of Kerala. It boasts of scenic sun-kissed beaches and countryside that are worth exploring. The town’s cool sea breeze, coupled with its natural beauty is a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town is known for its vibrant culture with several festivals and traditions that are unique to this part of Kerala. The people of Edava are warm and hospitable, making it a perfect place for tourists to experience the local lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Edava

Edava is a picturesque town and undoubtedly has some of Kerala’s best attractions. The place is known for its serene beauty and laid-back lifestyle. So pack your bags, grab your camera and let’s explore the best of Edava.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kanyakumari Beach: This is the southernmost tip of India and is a popular spot to watch the sunset, and it is just 30 kilometers away from Edava.
  • Padmanabhapuram Palace: It is a palace located in the Kanyakumari district of Tamil Nadu, and it is just 20 kilometers away from Edava. It is a magnificent palace with over 17 rooms.
  • Napier Museum: Located in the heart of Thiruvananthapuram city, it is an art and natural history museum famous for its impressive architecture.
  • Kuthira Malika Palace: Situated in the heart of Thiruvananthapuram city, it is a palace with over 1000 wooden horses placed around the palace, giving it a unique identity.

Natural Attractions of Edava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Edava Beach: This pristine beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Kerala and is ideal for taking a walk and getting some fresh air. This is a perfect place for nature lovers.
  • Akathumuri: It is a famous backwater in the Kollam district, and visitors can take a boat ride to explore the beauty of the sunrise and sunset.
  • Varkala Beach: This is another popular beach located 25 kilometers from Edava. This beach is famous for its golden sand and seawater.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edava

  • Kappil Beach Trek: It is a trek from the beach of Kappil to the estuary of Paravur backwaters, and it is an excellent route for beginners.
  • Varkala Paravur Backwaters Biking: It is an excellent biking route stretching from the south-west of Varkala to the Paravur backwaters.

Best Things to Do in Edava: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the beach: Take a stroll down the beach and enjoy the stunning views of the sea and sand. You can also participate in water sports such as swimming, surfing, and boat rides.
  • Visit the nearby temples: Edava is a place of worship, and you can enjoy visiting the different temples.
  • Experience the local lifestyle: Visit the villages of Edava and explore the local lifestyle.
  • Take a boat ride in Akathumuri: Take a boat ride and experience the beauty of the backwaters.
  • Attend the local festivals: Plan your visit during the festive season and experience the colourful traditions and pageantry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edava

Day 1: Arrival and city tour of Thiruvananthapuram city Day 2: Visit Padmanabhapuram Palace Day 3: Trekking to Edava beach and Akathumuri backwaters Day 4: Explore the Varkala beach and the Kuthira Malika Palace Day 5: Boat ride around the Paravur Lake and the Ashtamudi Lake Day 6: Visit the Napier Museum and the zoo located in Thiruvananthapuram city Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Edava

Edava has a rich culinary culture, and its street food is just as famous as its cuisine. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Puttu: Made from rice flour, Puttu is a dish that is usually served for breakfast. Served with curry and sweeteners like Jagerry, this is a good start to the day.
  2. Appam and Stew: Appam is a fluffy pancake served with a vegetable or meat stew. It is typically served for breakfast or dinner by local restaurants.
  3. Kappa: Made from tapioca, Kappa is a staple food of Kerala. It is usually served with fiery fish curry or beef curry.

Local Beverages

  1. Toddy: This is a natural alcoholic beverage made from the sap of coconut trees. Coconut trees thrive in abundance in this region.
  2. Butter Tea: It is a popular hot beverage made by adding mint, butter and tea that creates a creamy and smooth tea.

Where to Stay in Edava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Edava has a range of accommodation options ranging from budget guest houses to luxurious resorts. Few of the best places to stay in Edava are:

  1. The Raviz Resort and Spa, Kadavu: This luxurious hotel is located in the backwaters of Kozhikode and offers splendid views of the sea.
  2. OYO 10165 Home Hill View Stay in Varkala: This budget-friendly guest house offers comfortable and clean rooms with basic amenities.
  3. Dreams Beach Resort Edava: This beach resort located in Edava is famous for its stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

What to Buy in Edava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Edava is known for its handicrafts and has a thriving market for selling local products. Some local items to consider buying are:

  1. Coconut handicrafts: Made from the shells of the coconut, these handicrafts are unique to Edava.
  2. Tulaipanam: It is a traditional Keralite decoration, which is made by twisting colorful silk threads or strings into beautiful designs.
  3. Spices: Kerala is famous for its spices, and Edava has its own share of spice markets where you can buy them.

Local Culture and Customs of Edava

Edava is a quaint town with its own local customs and traditions. Respect for traditions is a must, and visitors are expected to dress conservatively while entering the temples. Smoking, drinking and other such habits are frowned upon in public places.

Best Time to Visit Edava

The best time to visit Edava is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edava

Do:

  • Carry appropriate clothing while visiting the temples.
  • Try the local cuisine and street food.
  • Visit the nearby tourist spots like Kanyakumari, Padmanabhapuram Palace, and Varkala beach.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter the beaches and other natural spots.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edava

Edava is a safe town with friendly locals. However, be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas and avoid walking alone at night. Also, swimming should be done only in designated areas as some parts of the sea can have dangerous currents.

Health in Edava

The town has medical facilities and hospitals, but it is advisable to carry your own basic medication, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions. Also, ensure you have travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Edava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edava

The nearest airport to Edava is Trivandrum International airport, which is about 30 kilometers away. There are regular buses and taxis available from the airport to Edava.

Bus Stations Near Edava

The Edava Bus station is well-connected with major cities and towns in Kerala, and regular buses ply in and out of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis or self-driven cars to reach Edava as the road connections to the town are excellent.

How to Get Around in Edava

The ideal way to explore Edava is on foot or by bike as it is a small town. Buses and taxis are also available, but they can be expensive during peak tourist seasons.

Where to Go Next from Edava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kollam: Located about 50 kilometers from Edava, Kollam is known for its waterways and serene backwaters.
  • Kanyakumari: This is the southernmost tip of India and is located about 30 kilometers away from Edava. It is a great place to watch the sunrise and sunset.
  • Varkala: This is another popular destination located about 25 kilometers from Edava.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Edava is a beautiful and serene town located in the western coast of Kerala and is a must-visit for every traveller who loves nature and appreciates culture. This comprehensive guide provides you with all the information you need to make your trip to Edava a memorable experience. So book your tickets, pack your bags and head on to explore the beautiful town of Edava.
