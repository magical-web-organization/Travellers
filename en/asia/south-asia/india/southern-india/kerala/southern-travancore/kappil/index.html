Introduction to Kappil

Tucked away in Southern Travancore, in Kerala, India, lies the hidden gem of Kappil. With its pristine beaches, lush greenery, and rich cultural heritage, Kappil is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to explore South India’s natural beauty.

As you make your way through Kappil’s winding roads, you’ll be greeted by the breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea on one side and the serene backwaters on the other. This idyllic location offers visitors the perfect blend of relaxation, adventure, and cultural immersion.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Kappil, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in this charming destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kappil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kappil is steeped in history and culture, and there’s no better way to explore it than by visiting its most notable museums and historical sites. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Kappil:

  • Kottavasal Velayudha Swami Temple: This ancient temple is the perfect place to learn about Kappil’s rich cultural heritage. Dedicated to Lord Muruga, it’s one of the most revered temples in the region.

  • Anchalummoodu Sree Bhagavathi Temple: This temple is another important cultural landmark in Kappil. It’s dedicated to the goddess Bhagavathi and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate sculptures.

  • Varkala Tunnel: The Varkala Tunnel is a fascinating piece of engineering history that played a crucial role in Kappil’s development. Built during the British era to transport goods to the Varkala port, the tunnel is now a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Kappil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kappil is a paradise for nature lovers, with its lush tropical forests, serene backwaters, and stunning beaches. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in Kappil:

  • Kappil Beach: Kappil Beach is a must-visit destination for any traveler to the region. With its crystal clear waters, golden sand, and picturesque views, it’s no wonder this beach is among the most popular in the area. Take a dip in the water or enjoy a relaxing stroll at sunset.

  • Golden Island: A small island located in the backwaters of Kappil, the Golden Island is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Surrounded by swaying palms and lush greenery, it’s the perfect spot for a picnic or a peaceful escape.

  • Kappil Lake: Kappil Lake is a serene body of water that’s ideal for canoeing or kayaking. Take a boat ride through its calm waters and enjoy the stunning scenery at every turn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kappil

For adventure seekers, Kappil has a wealth of opportunities to explore the great outdoors. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Kappil:

  • Poovar Island: This enchanting island can only be reached by boat and makes for an exciting hiking destination. Explore the lush tropical forests and take in the stunning views of the backwaters and sea.

  • Kappil to Varkala Beach: This scenic route takes you along the coastline, offering breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea. It’s a moderate hiking trail that’s suitable for most skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Kappil: Activities and Tours

Kappil has no shortage of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few of the best:

  • Ayurveda Treatments: Kappil is home to some of the best Ayurveda centers in India. Indulge in a relaxing massage, a wellness treatment, or a rejuvenating yoga session.

  • Boat Tours: Take a leisurely boat ride through the backwaters of Kappil and explore the hidden gems of the region. You might even spot some exotic wildlife along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kappil

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kappil, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrival in Kappil, check-in at the hotel, and explore Kottavasal Velayudha Swami and Anchalummoodu Sree Bhagavathi temples.

Day 2: Spend a day at Kappil Beach, enjoy water sports or a leisurely stroll at sunset.

Day 3: Visit the Golden Island and Kappil Lake.

Day 4: Take a boat tour through the backwaters and explore the stunning natural beauty of Kappil.

Day 5: Hike along the Poovar Island or take a bike ride on the Kappil to Varkala route.

Day 6-7: Indulge in some relaxing Ayurveda treatments or yoga sessions at one of Kappil’s wellness centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Kappil

Kappil offers visitors a diverse culinary experience that’s sure to please any palate. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam and Stew: A staple breakfast dish in Kappil, Appam and Stew is a delicious combination of fluffy Appam bread and spicy stew made with coconut milk and vegetables.

  • Puttu and Kadala: Another popular breakfast dish in Kappil, Puttu and Kadala is a hearty meal made with steamed rice flour and served with a flavorful chickpea curry.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: Toddy is a fermented coconut sap that’s a popular local beverage in Kappil. It’s a refreshing drink with a slight alcoholic content.

  • Kappil Tea: Kappil is known for its delicious tea, which is made with a blend of herbs and spices and served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Kappil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kappil offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Kappil:

  • The Raviz Resort and Spa: This luxurious resort offers stunning views of the backwaters and sea, as well as a range of amenities, including a spa, outdoor pool, and on-site restaurants.

  • Mayookham Resort: Mayookham Resort is a charming guesthouse located amidst lush greenery and serene backwaters. It’s the perfect spot for a peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Kappil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kappil offers visitors a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make for perfect keepsakes. Here are some of the top items to look out for:

  • Handmade Crafts: Kappil is known for its exquisite handmade crafts, including pottery, traditional textiles, and jewelry.

  • Spices: Kerala is known for its unique blend of spices, and Kappil is no exception. Stock up on some of the local blends to take home and savor.

Local Culture and Customs of Kappil

Kappil is a melting pot of culture, with a rich heritage that’s influenced by Hindu, Muslim, and Christian traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering temples and dressing modestly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Kappil

The best time to visit Kappil is from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and dry. The monsoon season between June and September can be unpredictable, with heavy rainfall and high humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kappil

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly in public.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites and beliefs.

  • Engage in risky water sports without proper safety measures.

  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kappil

Kappil is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Kappil

While Kappil doesn’t pose any major health risks, visitors should take precautions and stay hydrated, particularly during the hot summer months. It’s also a good idea to apply mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Kappil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kappil is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here’s a closer look at the various transportation options:

Airports Near Kappil

The nearest airport to Kappil is Trivandrum International Airport, which is around 50 kilometers away. Major airlines operate regular flights to Trivandrum from major Indian cities and some international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Kappil

Kappil is well-connected by road, with regular bus services from major cities across Kerala.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available, allowing visitors to explore Kappil and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Kappil

The best way to get around Kappil is by renting a bike or scooter, which are readily available from local shops. Alternatively, taxis and auto-rickshaws are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Kappil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kappil is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding areas, with plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Varkala: A popular coastal town located just a few kilometers from Kappil, Varkala is known for its stunning cliffs, pristine beaches, and relaxing atmosphere.

  • Poovar Island: Located around 20 kilometers from Kappil, Poovar Island is a serene and unspoiled island that’s perfect for nature lovers.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse culinary scene, Kappil is the perfect destination for any traveler to South India. Whether you’re looking for relaxation, adventure, or cultural immersion, Kappil has something to offer everyone, making it a must-visit destination for any trip to Kerala.
