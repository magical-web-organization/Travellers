Introduction to Braemore Estate

Located in Southern Travancore, in the Indian state of Kerala, Braemore Estate is a picturesque destination that offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in natural beauty and cultural richness. The estate spreads across a sprawling 400 acres and boasts of rich biodiversity, offering a plethora of activities and attractions to visitors. From natural wonders to cultural landmarks, hiking and biking trails to delicious food, Braemore Estate has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Braemore Estate

The local culture and customs of Braemore Estate are heavily influenced by Kerala’s history and traditions. Visitors can expect to experience vibrant festivals and traditional food and drink scenes, along with the warm hospitality of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Braemore Estate

The best time to visit Braemore Estate is from September to March, which is the winter season in Kerala. During these months, the climate is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without worrying about the heat and humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Braemore Estate

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Try the local food and drinks
  3. Wear comfortable shoes for hiking and biking
  4. Carry sunscreen and mosquito repellent
  5. Explore the lush green surroundings to the fullest

Don’t:

  1. Litter
  2. Take pictures without permission
  3. Peer into people’s homes, and respect their privacy
  4. Hike without a guide
  5. Disturb the wildlife and ecosystem

Safety and Health

Safety in Braemore Estate

Braemore Estate is generally considered a safe place for visitors. However, it is advisable to take precautions such as hiring a guide while hiking, carrying around minimal cash, and not wandering off the premises after dark.

Health in Braemore Estate

While the estate is surrounded by natural beauty, visitors must be aware of the risks of insect-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue fever. It’s essential to take all necessary precautions, such as wearing long-sleeved clothes and using mosquito repellent.
