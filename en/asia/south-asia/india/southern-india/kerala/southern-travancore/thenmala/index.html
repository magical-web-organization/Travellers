Introduction to Thenmala

Thenmala is a small village located in the Southern Travancore region of Kerala, South India. Also known as the “Honey Hill,” it is a serene escape from the bustling cities of India. Thenmala is not only famous for its abundant natural beauty but also has a rich cultural history with a number of museums, ancient temples, and palaces. This stunning destination is located close to the Punalur town in the Kollam district of Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in Thenmala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history and culture buff, then Thenmala has a lot to offer. The following are some of the places you should visit:

  • The Culture Zone: This museum highlights the rich cultural history of Kerala, including its art forms, festivals, and traditions. It is the perfect place to start your journey to Thenmala.

  • The Deer Rehabilitation Center: It is a unique and fascinating place to visit which allows you to see large populations of deer.

  • The Thenmala Dam: This engineering marvel is a popular spot for tourists to take a scenic tour of the area.

  • The Kollam Palace: This palace is a must-visit attraction if you are interested in Kerala’s architectural history.

  • Aryanamkunnu Palace: This magnificent palace takes you back to the history of Kerala’s traditional lifestyle and culture.

Natural Attractions of Thenmala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thenmala is surrounded by natural beauty, and the following are some of the best attractions to explore:

  • Palaruvi Falls: These beautiful falls are located amidst lush greenery and are considered one of Kerala’s most magical waterfalls.

  • Butterfly Safari: Take the Butterfly Safari to witness a beautiful and peaceful interaction with nature.

  • Thenmala Eco-tourism Park: The park has an abundance of beautiful flora and fauna and is considered the pride of Thenmala due to its natural beauty.

  • Adventure Zone: Are you an adventure enthusiast? Then don’t forget to visit the Adventure Zone, where you can enjoy activities such as rock climbing, rappelling, and river crossing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thenmala

  • Punalur-Meenmutty Falls: This route is one of the most popular hiking trails in Thenmala, surrounded by the beautiful greenery of the Western Ghats.

  • Bird Watching Trail: This route is ideal for bird lovers and is an excellent way to experience the tranquillity of the forests of Thenmala.

Best Things to Do in Thenmala: Activities and Tours

  • Eco-friendly tours: Explore the eco-friendly tours to get a glimpse of the diverse flora and fauna of Thenmala.

  • Bamboo Rafting in Shenduruney Wildlife Sanctuary: One of the most popular activities in Thenmala, a bamboo rafting tour in the Shenduruney Wildlife Sanctuary is an ideal way to experience the dense green forest and enjoy the tranquil waters of the sanctuary.

  • Tea Plantation Tour: You can explore the beautiful tea gardens in Thenmala and learn about the process of tea production.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thenmala

Day 1: Arrival and check-in to your accommodation

Day 2: Visit the Culture Zone, the Kollam Palace, and the Thenmala Dam.

Day 3: Explore the Butterfly Safari and Palaruvi Waterfalls.

Day 4: Take a hike through the beautiful Western Ghats and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 5: Bamboo Rafting in Shenduruney Wildlife Sanctuary.

Day 6: A visit to Tea Plantation along with a stroll through the beautiful gardens.

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Thenmala

Must-Try Dishes

When in Thenmala, taste the local cuisine, which includes:

  • Avial: A typical Kerala-style curry made with mixed vegetables, grated coconut, and spices.

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Puttu, a cylindrical-shaped steamed rice cake, and Kadala curry, a spiced curry made with black lentils, coconut milk, and other spices, is a delicious dish you should not miss out.

  • Idli and Sambar: A classic South Indian dish, idlis are steamed rice cakes, and sambar is a spicy lentil stew.

Local Beverages

When in Thenmala, indulge in local beverages such as:

  • Neer Mor: A refreshing spiced buttermilk drink that hydrates you during the humid days of Thenmala.

  • Tender Coconut Water: This is extracted from green tender coconut, and it is a natural hydrating drink.

  • Masala Chai: This is made with ginger and spices and is a favourite hot beverage in Kerala.

Where to Stay in Thenmala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thenmala offers several options for lodgings, including several budget-friendly hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Thenmala are:

  • The Tamarind Easy Hotel: Situated at the heart of Thenmala, this hotel provides amazing views of the hills and nature.

  • Harmony Eco Vistas: This homestay offers a comfortable and eco-friendly stay amidst the beauty of Thenmala.

  • The Western Ghats Resort: This resort is located in the middle of a lush green forest, providing visitors with an immersive experience of the natural surroundings.

What to Buy in Thenmala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring back some souvenirs to cherish your memories of Thenmala:

  • Local Spices and Tea: Kerala is famous for its spices and tea production.

  • Handmade Paper Items: These are crafted using traditional techniques and are ideal for gifting.

  • Kerala sarees: You can purchase gorgeous traditional sarees that showcase Kerala’s rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Thenmala

The people of Thenmala are respectful of their traditions and customs, and they encourage visitors to respect the local culture as well. Don’t hesitate to explore the cultural practices of the locals, experience their food, and participate in local festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Thenmala

The best time to visit Thenmala is during the winter season, from December to February. During this time, the temperature ranges from a comforting 10°C-25°C, making it comfortable for visitors. The monsoon season prevails from June to September, giving the region a lush green appearance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thenmala

Do:

  • Respect the cultural norms of the locals.

  • Explore the natural beauty of Thenmala with an open mind.

  • Keep the surroundings clean and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in temples or religious places.

  • Take any natural resource, including flora and fauna.

  • Disrupt or harm the natural environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thenmala

Thenmala is considered relatively safe for visitors, but you must always be aware of your surroundings and take precautions. Do not venture into the dense forests without a guide, and avoid walking alone in deserted areas.

Health in Thenmala

The forested areas of Thenmala are prone to the spread of certain diseases such as malaria, so it is essential to take precautions, such as using mosquito repellents and nets. Health care facilities are available in the nearest town.

Getting to Thenmala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thenmala

The nearest airport to Thenmala is Trivandrum International Airport, situated around 70 kilometres away. Another option is the Cochin International Airport, located around 180 kilometres away.

Bus Stations Near Thenmala

Thenmala is well-connected by road to various cities in Kerala. Buses are the most convenient and affordable mode of transportation, and Punalur Bus Station is the closest to Thenmala.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or self-drive, but unless you are familiar with local traffic rules, it’s recommended to opt for public transportation.

How to Get Around in Thenmala

Walk or cycle to explore the parks and natural attractions. Hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw to get around if needed. Public transportation is also available.

Where to Go Next from Thenmala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ponmudi: Ponmudi is a picturesque hill station located around 60 kilometres from Thenmala, known for its beautiful tea gardens and natural beauty.

  • Varkala: Varkala is a bustling coastal town located around 80 kilometres from Thenmala, known for its beautiful beaches and cliffside views.

  • Thekkady: Located around 110 kilometres from Thenmala, Thekkady is known for its beautiful wildlife reserve.

Conclusion

Thenmala is a beautiful place to visit if you are interested in natural beauty and cultural history. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now equipped to navigate and explore this beautiful location to the fullest. Remember to respect local customs and traditions, explore responsibly, and make beautiful memories while you are there.
