Introduction to Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park is a protected wildlife sanctuary located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. The park is situated in the Cardamom Hills and is spread across an area of 925 square kilometers. It is known for its lush green tropical forests, diverse flora and fauna, and picturesque lakes, rivers, and waterfalls. The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, gaurs, sambar deer, wild boars, and over 265 species of birds.

List of Places to Visit in Periyar National Park

Visitors to Periyar National Park can explore its culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and adventure sports activities. Here is a list of the top places to visit and things to do in the park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Green Park Ayurvedic and Spices Plantation
  • Kadathanadan Kalari Centre – A martial arts and cultural center
  • Mudra Cultural Center – Performances, workshops, and exhibitions

Natural Attractions of Periyar National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Periyar Lake – Enjoy boating, and see wildlife on the shores, like elephants and birdlife
  • Periyar Tiger Reserve – Spot tigers and other wild animals
  • Kumily – Famous for its spice markets

Hiking/Biking Routes of Periyar National Park

The park is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding forests and hills. The most popular routes include:

  • Periyar Tiger Trail – A night camping and wildlife trek
  • Cloud Walk – A trail that takes you to the highest point in the park
  • Green Walk – A leisurely trail through the forest

Best Things to Do in Periyar National Park: Activities and Tours

Periyar National Park offers several activities and tours for visitors to experience wildlife, nature, and local culture. Some of the best things to do in the park include:

  • Boating on Periyar Lake
  • Walking tours of spice plantations
  • Elephant rides through the park
  • Bamboo rafting on Periyar River
  • Birdwatching tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Periyar National Park

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Periyar National Park:

Day 1-2: Arrive in Periyar/Kumily and settle in. Take a guided tour of spice plantations and explore the local markets.

Day 3: Set out on a trek or hike through the park with a naturalist guide, and spot wildlife.

Day 4-5: Go for a bamboo rafting ride on the Periyar River or boating on Periyar Lake. Go for a bird watching tour.

Day 6-7: Take a cultural tour of Kerala and visit traditional villages, Kalari martial arts training centers, and explore the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Periyar National Park

Kerala is known for its flavorful cuisine, which is a blend of fresh local produce and spices. Periyar National Park also offers several dining options for visitors, where they can taste traditional Kerala dishes made with fresh, local ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam and stew – Rice pancakes with vegetable and meat stew
  • Avial – A vegetable curry
  • Puttu-kadala – Rice cakes with black gram curry
  • Malabar biryani – A flavored rice and meat dish

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water
  • Tea and Coffee

Where to Stay in Periyar National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Periyar National Park offers a wide range of accommodation facilities catering to different budgets and preferences. Here is a list of some popular options:

  • Thekkady Woods – A budget-friendly guesthouse
  • Spice Village – A luxurious eco-resort
  • Greenwoods Resort – A mid-range resort

What to Buy in Periyar National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Periyar National Park is famous for its spices, so it’s only natural that the best souvenirs consist of the locally grown spices. Visitors can find a range of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg in Kumily’s spice markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park is located in Kerala, which is a culturally rich Indian state with a unique history and customs. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs, including dressing modestly, especially when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Periyar National Park

The best time to visit Periyar National Park is from October to February as the weather is cool and dry. During this time, the park is bustling with wildlife species, and the river and lake levels are higher, making it an excellent time for boating and rafting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Periyar National Park

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts of visiting Periyar National Park:

Do:

  • Hire a knowledgeable guide/naturalist
  • Dress appropriately
  • Follow the park rules and regulations

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife
  • Make excessive noise and littering
  • Enter the park alone after dark

Safety and Health

Safety in Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park is a wild area and, therefore, presents some safety concerns. Visitors should be cautious when spotting wild animals and maintain a safe distance from them. They should also follow the park’s rules and regulations and avoid venturing out alone after dark.

Health in Periyar National Park

Before traveling to Periyar National Park, visitors should get all necessary immunizations and take preventive medication against malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Periyar National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The park is well-connected to major cities in India and can be reached through various transportation modes, including flights, trains, and buses.

Airports Near Periyar National Park

The nearest airport to Periyar National Park is the Madurai Airport, which is about 136 kilometers away. Other airports include the Cochin International Airport and the Trivandrum International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Periyar National Park

Kumily has a well-connected bus network that connects major cities in Kerala to the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private taxis or cars to reach Periyar National Park.

How to Get Around in Periyar National Park

The best way to explore the park is on foot or on a guided tour. Visitors can easily hire naturalist guides at the entrance of the park.

Where to Go Next from Periyar National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can extend their trip by exploring nearby destinations in Kerala, including:

  • Munnar – Hill station surrounded by tea gardens
  • Alappuzha – Famous for backwaters and houseboat cruises
  • Kumarakom – Bird sanctuaries and backwaters

Conclusion

Periyar National Park is a must-visit destination for nature and wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can explore its lush green forests, spot wildlife, and experience the unique cultural heritage of Kerala. With excellent accommodation options, delicious local cuisine, and a wide range of activities, Periyar National Park offers a memorable travel experience for everyone.
