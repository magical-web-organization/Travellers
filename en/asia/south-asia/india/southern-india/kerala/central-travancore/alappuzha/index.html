Introduction to Alappuzha

Located in Central Travancore, Alappuzha is a picturesque town in the state of Kerala, India. This town is also known as Alleppey and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the country. Famous for its backwaters, houseboats, and sandy beaches, Alappuzha is a must-visit destination for those who are looking to experience the true beauty of Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in Alappuzha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alappuzha Beach

Alappuzha Beach is one of the popular attractions in the town that draws visitors from all over the world. The beach is located close to the city center and is the perfect place to relax and unwind. The beach stretches for about 4 kilometers and offers visitors a picturesque backdrop of the Arabian Sea.

Krishnapuram Palace

Krishnapuram Palace is another cultural attraction in Alappuzha that is worth visiting. The palace was built in the 18th century and is now a museum that showcases the history and culture of the region. Visitors can see the exquisite mural paintings, antique furniture, and other artifacts that are housed here.

Natural Attractions of Alappuzha: Outdoor Adventures, and Scenic Views

Backwaters of Alappuzha

The backwaters of Alappuzha are undoubtedly one of the most popular tourist attractions in the town. Visitors can take a leisurely ride on a houseboat and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region. The backwaters are a network of rivers, canals, and lagoons that are interconnected and provide visitors with a unique experience.

Marari Beach

Marari Beach is another must-visit destination in Alappuzha. The beach is located about 11 kilometers from the town and is a peaceful getaway from the crowds in the city. Visitors can spend their time relaxing on the beach, taking long walks, or enjoying water sports like swimming and surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alappuzha

Alappuzha is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The town provides visitors with a range of trails that are perfect for exploring the region. Visitors can trek through the lush green hills in the area, cycle around the backwaters, or go on a nature walk to explore the flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Alappuzha: Activities and Tours

Explore the Backwaters of Alappuzha

One of the best things to do in Alappuzha is to take a houseboat tour of the backwaters. Visitors can rent a houseboat for a few hours or a few days and explore the region at their leisure. They can enjoy the scenic beauty of the backwaters, sample local cuisine, and experience the rich culture of Kerala.

Visit the Local Markets

Visitors can also visit the local markets in Alappuzha to experience the hustle and bustle of the town. They can shop for souvenirs, local handicrafts, and spices. The markets are also a great place to sample local snacks and delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alappuzha

Day 1: Visit Alappuzha Beach and Krishnapuram Palace. Day 2: Take a houseboat tour of the backwaters. Day 3: Visit Marari Beach and relax on the sandy shores. Day 4: Go on a nature walk, explore the hills, and visit the local villages. Day 5: Rent a bike and cycle around the backwaters. Day 6: Visit the local markets and sample local cuisine. Day 7: Depart Alappuzha and head to the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Alappuzha

Must-Try Dishes

Meen Curry

Meen Curry is a signature dish in Alappuzha and is a must-try for seafood lovers. It is a spicy fish curry that is made with coconut milk, tamarind, and a blend of Indian spices.

Appam and Stew

Appam and Stew is a popular breakfast dish in Alappuzha. It is a coconut milk-based stew that is served with soft, fluffy appams, a type of Indian pancake.

Local Beverages

Toddy

Toddy is a local alcoholic beverage that is made from fermented coconut sap. It is a popular drink in Alappuzha and is usually served in roadside shacks and local bars.

Elaneer

Elaneer, or tender coconut water, is a refreshing local beverage that is perfect for hot summer days. It is available at most street vendors and is a must-try when in Alappuzha.

Where to Stay in Alappuzha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget-friendly Options

Zostel Alleppey

Zostel Alleppey is a budget-friendly hostel located close to the beach. It offers dormitory-style accommodation and is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

Pokkara Beach Resort

Pokkara Beach Resort is a budget-friendly guesthouse located close to the beach. It offers clean and comfortable rooms and is perfect for families and couples.

Luxury Options

Punnamada Resort

Punnamada Resort is a luxury resort located on the backwaters of Alappuzha. It offers spacious and luxurious rooms, a swimming pool, and a spa.

Lemon Tree Vembanad Lake Resort

Lemon Tree Vembanad Lake Resort is a luxury resort located on the banks of Vembanad Lake. It offers plush rooms, a multi-cuisine restaurant, and a swimming pool. It is perfect for those who are looking for a luxurious stay in Alappuzha.

What to Buy in Alappuzha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy locally made handicrafts, fabrics, and spices from the local markets in Alappuzha. Some of the must-buy items include coconut shell artifacts, banana fiber bags, and freshly ground spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Alappuzha

Alappuzha has a rich culture and history that is embedded in its architecture, cuisine, and lifestyle. The people in the town are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should always be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Alappuzha

The best time to visit Alappuzha is from November to February when the weather is pleasant and cool. The monsoon season, from June to September, is also a great time to visit as the backwaters are filled with fresh water and the greenery of the region is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alappuzha

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious places.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets and beaches.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alappuzha

Alappuzha is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions like carrying a copy of their passport, avoiding dark and deserted areas at night, and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Alappuzha

Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent any gastrointestinal problems. It is also advisable to carry a basic first aid kit and consult a doctor before traveling to any destination in India.

Getting to Alappuzha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alappuzha

The nearest airport to Alappuzha is the Cochin International Airport, which is about 85 kilometers away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Alappuzha

Alappuzha has a well-connected bus network. The KSRTC bus station is located close to the town center and offers regular bus services to major cities in Kerala.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Alappuzha. The town has a railway station that is well-connected to major cities in India.

How to Get Around in Alappuzha

Visitors can rent a bike or a scooter to explore the town or take an auto-rickshaw to get around town. Visitors can also hire a houseboat to explore the backwaters at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Alappuzha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a popular tourist destination located about 15 km from Alappuzha. It is famous for its backwaters, bird sanctuary, and luxury resorts.

Cochin

Cochin, also known as Kochi, is a popular tourist destination in Kerala that is located about 53 km from Alappuzha. The city is famous for its colonial architecture, beaches, and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Alappuzha is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a unique experience. The town is rich in history and culture and has a wide range of attractions to suit all visitors. Visitors can explore the backwaters, take a houseboat tour, sample local cuisine, and visit cultural attractions like museums and temples. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Alappuzha with ease and explore this beautiful destination to the fullest.
