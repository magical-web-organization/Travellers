Introduction to Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a small village situated on the banks of Vembanad Lake in the Kottayam district of Kerala, Southern India. Known for its scenic beauty, rich culture, and exotic flora and fauna, Kumarakom is an ideal destination for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts alike. The village has a long history dating back to ancient days of India and offers a plethora of cultural sites, historical landmarks, and natural attractions to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Kumarakom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bay Island Driftwood Museum

The Bay Island Driftwood Museum is a unique attraction in Kumarakom that showcases a vast collection of driftwood sculptures. The museum houses over 150 pieces of art, each of which has been crafted from the roots and trunks of trees that have been washed ashore on the Bay Island beaches after being carried by the tides of the sea.

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary is spread across a vast expanse of around 14 acres and is home to a wide variety of avian species. It is a haven for birdwatchers who can spot migratory birds from various parts of the world, including the Siberian Cranes.

Vaikom Mahadeva Temple

The Vaikom Mahadeva Temple is an ancient Hindu temple built in the 12th century that stands today as one of the oldest and most revered temples in Kerala. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a must-visit cultural site that showcases the rich religious heritage of India.

Natural Attractions of Kumarakom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vembanad Lake

Vembanad Lake is the longest lake in Kerala, and one of the largest in the country. The lake is home to numerous species of fish, crabs, and prawns, and is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and birdwatching.

Kumarakom Beach

Kumarakom Beach is a pristine and serene beach that offers a tranquil escape from the hustle-bustle of the city. With its calm waters, soft sand, and breathtaking sunset views, it is an excellent place for a relaxing day out with family and friends.

Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall

The Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall is a stunning waterfall situated amidst lush green forests and scenic countryside. The waterfall is a popular spot for hiking and trekking and offers a mesmerizing view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. There are several scenic routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, winding through lush green forests, shimmering lakeshores, and quaint village roads. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary Trail
  • The Vembanad Lake Loop
  • The Coconut Grove and Paddy Fields Trail
  • The Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall Hike

Best Things to Do in Kumarakom: Activities and Tours

Houseboat Tours

One of the most popular things to do in Kumarakom is to take a serene houseboat tour along the tranquil backwaters. The houseboats are equipped with all modern amenities and offer a unique way to explore the scenic beauty of the surrounding landscape.

Ayurvedic Massages and Yoga

Kumarakom is renowned for its Ayurvedic treatments and massages that offer deep relaxation and rejuvenation. Visitors can also explore the ancient art of yoga at various ashrams and wellness centers in the area.

Village Walks

Visitors can take a leisurely walk through the quaint and rustic villages of Kumarakom to experience the local rural life and culture. It’s a great way to interact with the locals and learn about their daily routines, traditions, and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Kumarakom

Must-Try Dishes

Appam and Stew

Appam is a fermented rice batter pancake served with a vegetable or chicken stew, a popular local delicacy.

Karimeen Pollichathu

Karimeen Pollichathu, a tasty fish dish that is marinated with spices and wrapped in banana leaves before being grilled or roasted.

Local Beverages

Toddy

Toddy is a popular local drink made from the sap of palm trees, and a must-try for visitors who want to experience Kerala’s distinct food culture.

Tea and Coffee

Kerala is known for its aromatic tea and coffee, and Kumarakom offers an excellent selection of both.

Where to Stay in Kumarakom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kumarakom offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget, from luxury resorts and traditional houseboats to budget hotels and hostels. Some of the popular venues include:

  • The Zuri Kumarakom
  • Backwater Ripples
  • Lemon Tree Vembanad Lake Resort

What to Buy in Kumarakom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the popular souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring back home include:

  • Traditional handicrafts and brassware
  • Spices, teas, and coffee
  • Natural and Ayurvedic products such as coconut oil and bath salts

Local Culture and Customs of Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a melting pot of various cultures and customs that have evolved over centuries. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, dress conservatively when visiting religious sites, and always seek permission before taking photographs of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Kumarakom

The best time to visit Kumarakom is from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and soothing. Visitors can also experience the traditional festival of Onam, which is celebrated in August or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumarakom

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly in religious sites
  • Try the local food and beverages
  • Engage with the locals to learn about their culture and daily life

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Drink and drive
  • Wear shoes inside temples and other religious sites
  • Smoke or consume alcohol in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumarakom

Kumarakom is generally a very safe place for tourists. However, it’s always recommended to keep an eye on personal belongings, avoid travelling alone at night, and be cautious when indulging in adventure activities.

Health in Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a malaria-free region, but visitors should still take necessary precautions such as using insect repellent, wearing protective clothing, and consulting a doctor before travelling.

Getting to Kumarakom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kumarakom

The nearest airports to Kumarakom are:

  • Cochin International Airport (78 km)
  • Trivandrum International Airport (156 km)

Bus Stations Near Kumarakom

Kumarakom is well connected by road, and visitors can arrive at various bus stations near Kumarakom, including:

  • Kottayam Bus Terminal (15 km)
  • Cherthala Bus Station(22 km)
  • Alappuzha Bus Station (34 km)

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kumarakom by train, and the nearest railway station is at Kottayam. Taxis and private vehicles are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a small village, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bicycle. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Kumarakom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular destinations and excursions near Kumarakom include:

  • Alleppey backwaters
  • Kochi
  • Marari Beach
  • Thekkady

Conclusion

Kumarakom is a hidden paradise in Kerala that offers an enchanting blend of scenic beauty, rich culture, and adventure activities. Whether visitors want to experience the tranquil backwaters, explore the local culture and customs, or indulge in some Ayurvedic treatments, Kumarakom has something for everyone. By following this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to Kumarakom and leave with memorable experiences to cherish for a lifetime.
