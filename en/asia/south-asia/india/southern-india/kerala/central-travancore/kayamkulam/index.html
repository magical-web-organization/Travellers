Introduction to Kayamkulam

Welcome to Kayamkulam, a central Travancore town located in Kerala, India that will surprise you with its sheer beauty and blend of culture, nature, and history. Kayamkulam is situated on the eastern bank of the Kayamkulam Lake and is surrounded by lush greenery, serene backwaters, sandy beaches, and historic spots. The town is also known for hosting some of the most colorful festivals in Kerala, such as the Kayamkulam Boat Race and the Palli Kalakayal.

List of Places to Visit in Kayamkulam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kayamkulam has a rich cultural and historical heritage dating back to the 11th century. The town was ruled by the Kayamkulam Kingdom before merging with the Travancore Kingdom in the early 18th century. Some of the top cultural and historical attractions in Kayamkulam include:

  • Krishnapuram Palace
  • Oachira Temple
  • Kayamkulam Valiyapally
  • Perumthenaruvi Waterfall
  • Thirumullavaram Beach
  • Kottaram Temple
  • Kayamkulam Lake
  • Thangasseri Fort

Natural Attractions of Kayamkulam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for some adventure and natural beauty, Kayamkulam won’t disappoint you. From trekking trails to bird watching, and from lush green hills to the backwaters, this place has something for everyone. Don’t forget to visit:

  • Pandavan Rock
  • Mangrove Forests
  • Kakkattikunnu Hills
  • Arattupuzha Beach
  • Kayamkulam Bird Sanctuary

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kayamkulam

Kayamkulam offers some fantastic hiking and biking routes where you can explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are a few you should try:

  • Pallickathodu - Vayalar
  • Sadasukadavu - Pulincunnu
  • Mankompu - Cutchery Junction
  • Kayamkulam - Karunagappally

Best Things to Do in Kayamkulam: Activities and Tours

Kayamkulam offers a wide range of activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. Some of the best things to do here are:

Week-Long Itinerary for Kayamkulam

If you have a week to spare, here’s an ideal itinerary to explore everything that Kayamkulam has to offer:

  • Day 1: Visit Kayamkulam Lake and Krishnapuram Palace
  • Day 2: Visit Oachira Temple and Perumthenaruvi Waterfall
  • Day 3: Trek to Pandavan Rock and Explore Mangrove Forests
  • Day 4: Visit Thirumullavaram Beach and Kayamkulam Valiyapally
  • Day 5: Explore Kottaram Temple and Kayamkulam Bird Sanctuary
  • Day 6: Visit Thangasseri Fort and Arattupuzha Beach
  • Day 7: Relax with a Houseboat Stay and Ayurvedic Massage

Local Culture and Customs of Kayamkulam

Kayamkulam has a unique culture that is deeply rooted in tradition and history. The town celebrates various festivals and events throughout the year, such as:

  • Kayamkulam Boat Race
  • Palli Kalakayal
  • Onam
  • Vishu

Best Time to Visit Kayamkulam

The best time to visit Kayamkulam is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and the humidity is low. The town also experiences occasional showers during this time, making it ideal for exploring the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kayamkulam

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Carry mosquito repellent

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink in public places
  • Litter the surroundings
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs

Safety and Health

Safety in Kayamkulam

Kayamkulam is a relatively safe town for travelers, but it’s always best to take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone late at night
  • Keep your belongings safe
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas

Health in Kayamkulam

Carry insect repellents, sunscreens, and medicines for common ailments when visiting Kayamkulam. It is also advisable to drink only bottled or filtered water.

Getting to Kayamkulam: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Cochin International Airport (110 kilometers)
  • Trivandrum International Airport (103 kilometers)

Bus Stations Near Kayamkulam

  • KSRTC Bus Stand
  • Kayamkulam Private Bus Stand

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or rent a car to get to Kayamkulam from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Kayamkulam

Kayamkulam is a small town, and the best way to explore it is on foot or by hiring a local taxi or motorbike. You can also take a bus or autorickshaw for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Kayamkulam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kollam

  • Kovalam

Conclusion

Kayamkulam is a hidden gem in Kerala that is full of surprises. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, this town has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this wonderful destination that will leave a lasting impression on you.
