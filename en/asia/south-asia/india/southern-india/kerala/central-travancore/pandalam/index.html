Introduction to Pandalam

Located in Central Travancore region of Kerala, Pandalam is a beautiful town with a rich culture and history dating back to ancient times. Surrounded by lush green forests, Pandalam is a hidden gem for travelers who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and experience the serene beauty of Kerala’s backwaters.

Pandalam is a pilgrimage center for Hindus, with the Pandalam Palace being one of the most popular tourist attractions. The town also attracts nature lovers and adventure seekers with its diverse landscapes, including hills, waterfalls, and lakes.

List of Places to Visit in Pandalam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pandalam Palace

Pandalam Palace is one of the most popular tourist spots in the town, which is important for Hindu pilgrims. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Ayyappa, the presiding deity of the famous Sabarimala temple in Kerala, was born here. The palace has a rich history and is associated with the royal family of Pandalam. Visitors can view the collections of artifacts and antiques, including paintings, sculptures, and furniture.

Valiyakoikkal Temple

The Valiyakoikkal Temple is a 1000-year-old temple located near the Pandalam Palace. This temple is famous for its murals that depict the life of Lord Krishna. The temple also hosts the annual Pandalam festival, which attracts thousands of devotees from across the country.

Natural Attractions of Pandalam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Konni Elephant Training Centre

The Konni Elephant Training Centre is a must-visit for nature lovers, especially those fond of animals. Visitors can watch elephants being trained by mahouts, bathe in the river, and take elephant rides.

Charalkunnu Hill Station

Charalkunnu Hill Station, located 14 km from Pandalam, is a popular tourist destination known for its scenic beauty. Visitors can enjoy adventurous activities such as trekking, rock climbing, and camping.

Pandalam Para Waterfall

Pandalam Para Waterfall is a picturesque waterfall located in the midst of thick forests. Visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty of the waterfall and the serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pandalam

Pandalam to Malayalapuzha

This 18-km long trail is ideal for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Along the way, visitors can explore the traditional villages and lush green paddy fields, interacting with friendly locals.

Best Things to Do in Pandalam: Activities and Tours

Boat Ride in Pandalam Backwaters

Take a boat ride in Pandalam Backwaters and enjoy the stunning scenery of the lush green surroundings while spotting different species of birds.

Ayurvedic Treatment

What better way to relax than with an Ayurvedic spa treatment? Pandalam is a hub for Ayurvedic treatments, and visitors can indulge in massages, therapies, and treatments to rejuvenate their body, mind, and soul.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pandalam

Day 1: Arrive in Pandalam, visit Pandalam Palace, and explore the local market.

Day 2: Visit Valiyakoikkal Temple and take a boat ride in Pandalam Backwaters.

Day 3: Trek to Pandalam Para Waterfall and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 4: Take a visit to Konni Elephant Training Center.

Day 5: Relax and indulge in Ayurvedic treatments.

Day 6: Visit Charalkunnu Hill Station and indulge in outdoor adventure activities.

Day 7: Explore the villages around Pandalam and interact with locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Pandalam

Must-Try Dishes

Pandalam is famous for its traditional cuisine, with a mixture of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. Some must-try dishes include Appam with Stew, Kerala Style Fish Curry, and Karimeen Pollichathu.

Local Beverages

Sip on some traditional beverages such as Kallu (Toddy), a fermented palm sap, and Thiruvathira Kali (rice dessert), made with rice flour and jaggery.

Where to Stay in Pandalam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pandalam offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets, from luxurious hotels to affordable guesthouses. Some popular options include Hotel Vallabha Inn, Indraprastham Tourist Home, and UAE Residency.

What to Buy in Pandalam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some souvenirs from Pandalam such as traditional handicrafts, handloom sarees, and aromatic spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Pandalam

Pandalam has a rich Hindu cultural heritage and is a pilgrimage center for Hindus. Visitors must be respectful of the local customs, dress modestly while visiting temples, and remove their footwear outside temple premises.

Best Time to Visit Pandalam

The best time to visit Pandalam is during the winter months from October to February. The weather is comfortable and visitors can indulge in outdoor activities without the scorching heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pandalam

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting temples
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Interact with locals and experience the local culture

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol or non-vegetarian food inside temple premises
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Pandalam

Pandalam is a safe town, and visitors can explore the city without any major safety issues. However, visitors must take precautions while traveling alone and avoid deserted areas during the night.

Health in Pandalam

Visitors must keep themselves hydrated during their stay in Pandalam, use mosquito repellants to avoid mosquito bites, and avoid consuming street food.

Getting to Pandalam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pandalam

The nearest airport to Pandalam is the Trivandrum International Airport, located 93 km away.

Bus Stations Near Pandalam

Pandalam is well-connected to major cities in Kerala by road. Visitors can avail of private and public buses from Kochi, Trivandrum, and Kottayam.

Other Options

Visitors can hire taxis or rent cars to reach Pandalam from other cities in Kerala. Train services are also available from major cities.

How to Get Around in Pandalam

Visitors can avail of buses, taxis, or autorickshaws to get around Pandalam. These are affordable and easily available.

Where to Go Next from Pandalam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sabarimala

Sabarimala is a popular destination for Hindu pilgrims, located about 50 km from Pandalam.

Gavi

Gavi is a picturesque hill station located about 65 km from Pandalam, known for its scenic beauty and adventurous activities.

Conclusion

Pandalam is a perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the cultural, historical and natural beauty of South India. A town full of cultural festivities, lush green landscapes, and a serene backwater, Pandalam offers something for everyone. Visitors can indulge in outdoor adventure activities, Ayurvedic treatments, and taste traditional cuisine while experiencing the rich cultural heritage of the city.
