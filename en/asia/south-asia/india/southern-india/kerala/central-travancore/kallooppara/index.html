Introduction to Kallooppara

Kallooppara is a small town in Central Travancore, Kerala, located in Southern India. It is a quaint little place known for its scenic beauty, breathtaking views, and rich cultural heritage. The town is surrounded by hills and lush green forests, and the Pamba river flows through it. Kallooppara is an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Kallooppara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kallooppara is known for its rich cultural heritage and there are several museums and historical places that one can explore to get a glimpse of its past. Some of the must-visit places are:

1. Sree Subramanya Swami Temple

This temple is dedicated to Lord Muruga, son of Lord Shiva. It is one of the oldest temples in the town and attracts a large number of devotees. The temple’s architecture is unique and stunning, and the temple has a serene and peaceful atmosphere.

2. Mahadeva Temple

Mahadeva Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple has intricate carvings and sculptures that reflect the ancient Dravidian style of architecture. The temple is located in the heart of the town, making it easily accessible for tourists.

3. St. Thomas Orthodox Church

St. Thomas Orthodox Church is one of the oldest churches in Kallooppara. It has a unique architecture with beautiful stained glass windows and intricate carvings on its walls.

Natural Attractions of Kallooppara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kallooppara is known for its natural beauty and there are several outdoor adventures that one can embark on to explore the town’s beauty. Some of the must-visit natural attractions are:

1. Moovanthuruthu Waterfalls

The Moovanthuruthu Waterfalls is a must-visit attraction in Kallooppara. The waterfall is located in the middle of a dense forest and is surrounded by lush greenery. The waterfall can be reached through a short trek that is suitable for all ages.

2. Pamba River

The Pamba river is one of the longest rivers in Kerala and it flows through Kallooppara. The river is ideal for boating and is home to several species of fish. Tourists can also enjoy a picnic on the banks of the river and soak in the scenic beauty of the place.

3. Vellppathu Malam

Vellppathu Malam is a hill located near Kallooppara. The hill offers a stunning view of the valley and is a popular spot for trekking and hiking. The hill is also home to several endemic species of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kallooppara

Kallooppara is a haven for adventure enthusiasts, and there are several hiking and biking routes that tourists can explore. Some of the popular routes are:

  2. Pamba River Trail
  3. The Kallooppara Circuit

Local Culture and Customs of Kallooppara

Kallooppara has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its traditional dance and music. The town celebrates several festivals throughout the year, and tourists can participate in the festivities to get a glimpse of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Kallooppara

The best time to visit Kallooppara is from October to March as the weather is pleasant during this time of the year. The town also receives a significant amount of rainfall during the monsoon season from June to September, making it an ideal time for nature lovers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kallooppara

Kallooppara is a relatively safe town, and tourists need not worry about safety concerns. However, it is advisable to take caution and avoid wandering alone late at night in secluded areas.

Health in Kallooppara

Tourists must carry necessary medications and consult a doctor before traveling to Kallooppara. They must also carry a first-aid kit with basic medicines for common ailments.

Getting to Kallooppara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kallooppara

  1. Cochin International Airport (COK)
  2. Trivandrum International Airport (TRV)

Bus Stations Near Kallooppara

  1. Kallooppara Bus Station
  2. Thiruvalla Bus Station

Other Options

Tourists can hire a taxi or take a train to reach Kallooppara. The nearest railway station is located in Thiruvalla, which is about 18 km from Kallooppara.

How to Get Around in Kallooppara

Tourists can explore Kallooppara on foot or hire a taxi/auto-rickshaw to travel around the town. Bicycles are also available for hire in some areas.
