Introduction to Parumala

Parumala is a small village in Central Travancore, Kerala situated on the banks of Pampa River. It is a significant pilgrimage center for orthodox Christians as it is the birthplace of the famous Saint Gregorios of Parumala. The village is a perfect blend of natural beauty, pilgrimage importance, and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Parumala

There are a plethora of places to visit in Parumala that are sure to leave you awestruck. Here is a list of some of the best ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Gregorios Orthodox Church

The St. Gregorios Orthodox Church is the most famous place to visit in Parumala for its religious significance. It is the burial place of Saint Gregorios and the church is an impressive example of neo-gothic architecture.

Parumala Seminary

The Parumala Seminary is another significant heritage site to visit in Parumala. It was built by Saint Gregorios himself and is still currently operated as a Theological seminary.

Natural Attractions of Parumala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pampa River

Parumala is situated on the banks of the Pampa River, which is a must-visit spot for those who appreciate nature’s beauty. The river offers a picturesque landscape, and visitors can take a boat ride to enjoy the scenic surroundings.

Sunset View Point

The Sunset View Point is a perfect place to relax and unwind while enjoying a breathtaking view of the sun setting over the Western Ghats horizon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parumala

There are a lot of amazing hiking and biking trails in and around Parumala that are sure to cater to an adventurer’s soul. One such trek is the Parumala to Thiruvalla trek, which is a 5 km long trail that offers stunning views of the Pampa River.

Best Things to Do in Parumala: Activities and Tours

Explore the History and Culture

One of the best things to do in Parumala is to explore the history and culture of the place. Visitors can take a guided tour of the St. Gregorios Orthodox Church and the Parumala Seminary to learn more about the place’s cultural and religious significance.

Visit the Local Markets

A visit to the local markets is an excellent way to experience a slice of daily life in Parumala. Visitors can browse through the shops and buy local souvenirs and handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Parumala

Must-Try Dishes

While in Parumala, visitors can try some local dishes like the traditional Syrian Christian breakfast, Idiappam and chicken stew, and Kappa and Meen Curry.

Local Beverages

The local beverage in Parumala is the traditional drink, Sharbat, which is made with rose petals, sugar, and water.

Local Culture and Customs of Parumala

The local culture of Parumala is primarily influenced by Syrian Orthodox Christians. Visitors are expected to dress modestly while visiting the religious sites and follow the local customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Parumala

The best time to visit Parumala is between October and March when the climate is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parumala

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites
  • Exchange pleasantries like Namaste or Hello with the locals
  • Habitate the responsible tourist

Don’t:

  • Dress scantily or wear shorts while visiting religious sites
  • Consume narcotic drugs

Safety and Health

Safety in Parumala

Parumala is a safe place to visit, but visitors should be cautious of pickpockets and touts in crowded areas.

Health in Parumala

Parumala has a good healthcare system, and visitors can find hospitals and clinics in Thiruvalla town nearby.

Getting to Parumala: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are various modes of transportation available to reach Parumala, such as:

Airports Near Parumala

The nearest airport to Parumala is the Cochin International Airport, which is around 110 km away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus from the airport to reach Parumala.

Bus Stations Near Parumala

The closest bus station to Parumala is the Thiruvalla bus station, which is around 10 km from Parumala. Visitors can take a bus or hire a taxi from the bus station to reach Parumala.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private taxi or car from nearby towns like Kottayam or Kochi to reach Parumala.

How to Get Around in Parumala

Visitors can get around Parumala by hiring a taxi or an auto-rickshaw from the local taxi stand. As Parumala is a small village, it is also possible to explore the area on foot.

Where to Go Next from Parumala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thiruvalla

Thiruvalla, located around 10 km from Parumala, is a bustling town with several places to visit, including the Sree Vallabha Temple and the Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple.

Kumarakom

Kumarakom, located around 35 km from Parumala, is famous for its backwaters and bird sanctuaries. Visitors can take a boat ride and enjoy the scenic beauty of the area.

Conclusion

Parumala is a quaint village that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, religious significance and cultural heritage. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful holiday while exploring the various attractions that the village has to offer.
