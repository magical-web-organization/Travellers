Introduction to Chennithala

Chennithala is a small village located in the Central Travancore region of the Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its picturesque scenery, cultural attractions, and serene surroundings. The village is situated on the banks of the Pamba River and offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Chennithala is an ideal destination for those looking for a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Chennithala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Krishnapuram Palace - A magnificent palace built by the Maharaja of Travancore in the 18th century, known for its mural paintings and architecture.
  • St. Mary’s Orthodox Syrian Church - A popular pilgrimage center, known for its ancient Syrian Christian traditions.
  • Kadammanitta Devi Temple - A famous temple dedicated to the goddess Bhadrakali, known for its annual festival.
  • Kollam Beach - A scenic beach with a lighthouse and a port, famous for its seafood.

Natural Attractions of Chennithala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pullichira Church - A tranquil church located on a hill offering a panoramic view of the village and surrounding areas.
  • Mannarasala Sree Nagaraja Temple - A unique temple dedicated to the serpent god Nagaraja, surrounded by dense forests and wildlife.
  • Pamba River - A serene and tranquil river, ideal for boating, fishing, and river rafting.
  • Kuttikayam Mountain - A scenic viewpoint offering a stunning view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chennithala

  • Chennithala-Pullichira route - A scenic route through the hills and forests, offering breathtaking views of the village and surrounding areas.
  • Kollam Beach to Mannarasala Temple - A coastal route that takes you through some of the scenic beaches and historic sites in Chennithala.

Best Things to Do in Chennithala: Activities and Tours

  • Boating in Pamba River - Enjoy a peaceful and relaxing boat ride along the serene Pamba River.
  • Ayurvedic Spa Treatments - Indulge in traditional Ayurvedic therapies and wellness treatments at one of the many spas in Chennithala.
  • Fishing and Angling - Experience the thrill of freshwater fishing at the Pamba River or any of the inland ponds and lakes.
  • Shopping for Handicrafts - Visit the local markets and shops to buy exquisite handicrafts and souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Chennithala

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam and Stew - A traditional breakfast dish consisting of rice pancakes and vegetable stew.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry - A popular breakfast dish made of rice flour and served with black chickpea curry.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu - A spicy fish dish wrapped in banana leaves and grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy - A locally brewed alcoholic beverage made from coconut palms.
  • Chaaya - A traditional tea served with milk and jaggery.

Where to Stay in Chennithala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ashtamudi Villas - A luxury resort offering a range of amenities including a pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • Kollam Beach Retreat - A budget-friendly resort located near the beach and offering basic amenities.
  • Shanthitheeram Lakeside Resort - A serene resort located by the Pamba River, offering stunning views and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Chennithala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts - Chennithala is known for its exquisite handicrafts, particularly wood carvings and bronze figurines.
  • Spices - Kerala is known for its spices, and Chennithala is no exception. Buy locally grown spices like black pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  • Tea and Coffee - Kerala is known for its tea and coffee plantations, and Chennithala offers some of the best-quality tea and coffee.

Local Culture and Customs of Chennithala

Chennithala is largely influenced by its ancient traditions and customs. The people here are primarily Syrian Christians and follow the ancient customs and practices of the religion. The village is known for its arts and crafts, particularly wood carvings and bronze casting. The annual festivals and pilgrimages attract visitors from all over.

Best Time to Visit Chennithala

The best time to visit Chennithala is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and the village is vibrant with festivals and celebrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chennithala

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the scenic countryside and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Dress immodestly in religious sites or temples.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chennithala

Chennithala is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions and avoid venturing into unfamiliar territories alone at night.

Health in Chennithala

Chennithala has a well-established healthcare system that caters to tourists’ medical needs. However, it is always advisable to carry basic medical supplies and take necessary precautions like staying hydrated and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Chennithala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chennithala

The nearest airport to Chennithala is Trivandrum International Airport, located at a distance of 78 kilometers.

Bus Stations Near Chennithala

Chennithala has a well-connected bus station that operates regular buses to and from major cities in Kerala.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are available for hire from major cities in Kerala to Chennithala.

How to Get Around in Chennithala

The best way to get around Chennithala is by hiring a car or taking a taxi. Bicycles are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Chennithala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kollam - A bustling city known for its historic sites and beaches.
  • Alappuzha - A beautiful city known for its serene backwaters and houseboat cruises.
  • Thekkady - A scenic town known for its wildlife sanctuary and spice plantations.

Conclusion

Chennithala is a beautiful and serene village that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. With its scenic surroundings, ancient traditions, and beautiful attractions, Chennithala is a destination that should be on your list of must-visit places in Kerala.
