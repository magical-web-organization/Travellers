Introduction to Adoor

Located in the heart of Central Travancore, Adoor is a small town in the South Indian state of Kerala that boasts a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. With its serene backwaters, lush green hills, and diverse wildlife, Adoor is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Its unique cultural landscape, shaped by centuries of history and trade, also offers travelers a fascinating glimpse into South India’s rich cultural legacy.

List of Places to Visit in Adoor

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Adoor that should be on every traveler’s itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Krishnapuram Palace: This 18th-century palace, located about 47 km from Adoor, is a fine example of Kerala’s traditional architecture and boasts a beautiful collection of murals and antiques.
  • Padmanabhapuram Palace: This grand palace, located about 95 km from Adoor, was once the residence of the rulers of the erstwhile Travancore kingdom and is known for its exquisite craftsmanship and architectural grandeur.
  • Kottarakkara Sree Mahaganapathy Temple: This temple, located in Kottarakkara, about 14 km from Adoor, is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Kerala and is dedicated to Lord Ganesha.

Natural Attractions of Adoor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Punalur Hanging Bridge: This bridge, located about 33 km from Adoor, is one of the longest hanging bridges in Kerala and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple: This temple, located about 30 km from Adoor, is situated on the banks of the Pamba river and is known for its annual boat race and scenic surroundings.
  • Sasthamkotta Lake: This freshwater lake, located about 20 km from Adoor, is the largest of its kind in Kerala and is home to various species of aquatic animals and birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Adoor

Adoor is blessed with diverse terrain, ranging from hills to beaches, providing ample opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Some of the popular trails in Adoor are:

  • Konni Elephant Trail: This trail, located about 50 km from Adoor, takes you through the lush forests of the Konni region and offers a chance to spot wild elephants in their natural habitat.
  • Thenmala Eco-tourism Trail: This trail, located about 36 km from Adoor, takes you through the verdant forests of Thenmala and offers a chance to explore the region’s rich biodiversity.
  • Palaruvi Waterfall Trail: This trail, located about 32 km from Adoor, takes you to the stunning Palaruvi waterfall and offers a chance to soak in the serene beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Adoor: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Adoor offers a range of activities and tours that are sure to keep travelers engaged and entertained. Some of the best things to do in Adoor are:

  • Backwater Cruises: Adoor is blessed with a network of serene backwaters that offer a glimpse into rural Kerala’s lifestyle and culture. Travelers can take a backwater cruise on a traditional houseboat and savor the delicious local cuisine.
  • Ayurvedic Massages: Adoor is home to several Ayurvedic centers that offer a range of rejuvenating therapies and massages using natural ingredients and ancient techniques. These therapies are a perfect way to unwind and relax.
  • Kathakali Performances: Kathakali is a classical dance-drama that originated in Kerala and is known for its elaborate costumes, makeup, and expressions. Travelers in Adoor can experience a live Kathakali performance and admire the art form’s intricate nuances.

Food and Drink Scene of Adoor

Adoor’s food and drink scene is a delightful blend of flavors and traditions, reflecting the region’s diverse cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: This quintessential Kerala breakfast dish comprises steamed rice cakes and black chickpea curry, flavored with coconut and spices.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu: This iconic Kerala fish dish features pearl spot fish marinated in a blend of spices and wrapped in banana leaves before being grilled to perfection.
  • Appam and Stew: This is a popular Kerala breakfast dish made with fermented rice batter and coconut milk, served with vegetable or meat stew.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: This fermented drink is made from the sap of palm trees and has a sweet and sour flavor. It is a popular local beverage in Kerala and is often paired with spicy snacks.
  • Kattan Chaya: This is a strong, milky tea infused with spices like cardamom, ginger, and cinnamon. It is a staple beverage in Kerala and is often served with breakfast or as an afternoon pick-me-up.

Where to Stay in Adoor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Adoor offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, from luxury resorts to backpacker hostels. Here are some popular choices:

  • The Raviz Ashtamudi: This luxury resort is located on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake and offers stunning views of the backwaters. It features a range of amenities, including a spa and wellness center, swimming pool, and multiple dining options.
  • Kovilakathum Palace: This heritage property, located close to Adoor, offers travelers a chance to experience the grandeur and opulence of traditional Kerala architecture. It features spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving authentic Kerala cuisine.
  • Krishnatheeram Ayur Holy Beach Resorts: This beachfront resort, located about 70 km from Adoor, offers travelers a range of Ayurvedic therapies and massages while being surrounded by breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea.

What to Buy in Adoor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Adoor is a treasure trove of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementos. Here are some things you can buy in Adoor:

  • Handicrafts: Adoor is known for its vibrant handicrafts, including wooden carvings, bamboo products, and ceramic pottery. These traditional items make for unique and authentic souvenirs.
  • Spices: Kerala is renowned for its aromatic spices, and Adoor is no exception. You can buy fresh and flavorful spices like cardamom, pepper, and cloves to take home and add to your cooking.
  • Sari and Mundu: Kerala’s traditional garments, such as the sari and mundu, are known for their exquisite craftsmanship and unique motifs. You can buy these traditional garments in Adoor’s local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Adoor

Adoor’s culture is a unique blend of traditions and customs shaped by centuries of history and cross-cultural influences. Some important customs and practices to keep in mind when visiting Adoor are:

  • Respect for Religion: Kerala is home to people of diverse religions, and Adoor is no exception. Travelers should show respect for local customs and traditions when visiting religious sites and temples.
  • Dress Code: Adoor is a conservative town, and travelers should dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites. It’s best to avoid shorts or revealing outfits and cover your shoulders and knees.
  • Language: The official language of Kerala is Malayalam, but most people in Adoor can speak and understand English. However, it’s always a good idea to learn a few basic Malayalam phrases to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Adoor

The best time to visit Adoor is between November and February when the weather is dry and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The monsoon season, which lasts from June to September, brings heavy rain and can make travel difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Adoor

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Adoor:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and show respect for local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Hire a local guide for cultural tours and treks

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Disrespect local customs or religious beliefs

Safety and Health

Adoor is a safe and welcoming town, but travelers should take the usual precautions like keeping valuables safe and being wary of pickpockets. As for health, travelers should stick to bottled water, practice good hygiene, and take necessary precautions against mosquito bites, especially during the monsoon season.

Getting to Adoor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Adoor is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India, making it easy to reach by various means of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Adoor

The nearest airport to Adoor is the Trivandrum International Airport, located about 88 km away.

Bus Stations Near Adoor

Adoor has a well-connected bus station that connects it to other parts of Kerala and neighboring states.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Adoor from nearby cities and towns like Kottayam, Trivandrum or Kochi.

How to Get Around in Adoor

Adoor is a small town, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot or by hired transport. However, travelers can also use auto-rickshaws or local buses to get around the town quickly and conveniently.

Where to Go Next from Adoor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Adoor serves as a perfect gateway to explore other exciting destinations in Kerala, including:

  • Alleppey: Known as the “Venice of the East,” Alleppey is famous for its backwaters and is a popular destination for houseboat cruises.
  • Kumarakom: This charming village is located on the banks of Vembanad Lake and is known for its scenic beauty and bird watching opportunities.
  • Kochi: This cosmopolitan city is the cultural hub of Kerala and is known for its colonial architecture, spice markets, and street food scene.

Conclusion

Adoor is a hidden gem in Kerala’s heart, offering travelers an ideal blend of nature, culture, and adventure. With its pristine backwaters, ancient temples, and delicious cuisine, Adoor is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. Whether you’re an adventure junkie, a history buff, or a food lover, Adoor has something for everyone.
