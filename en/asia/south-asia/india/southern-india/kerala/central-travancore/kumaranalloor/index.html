Introduction to Kumaranalloor

Kumaranalloor is a culturally rich city in Central Travancore, Kerala, Southern India. This vibrant city is home to numerous temples, museums, and natural attractions that beautifully showcase the fascinating history and heritage of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Kumaranalloor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kumaranalloor is known for its captivating museums and historical monuments that offer a glimpse into the past. The most famous ones are:

  • Kumaranalloor Devi Temple
  • Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple
  • Thirunnakkara Mahadeva Temple

Natural Attractions of Kumaranalloor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kumaranalloor is blessed with picturesque natural scenery and outdoor activities that will leave you spellbound. The most notable ones are:

  • Vembanad Lake
  • Kumarakom bird sanctuary
  • Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumaranalloor

Kumaranalloor is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with a variety of beautiful hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Illickal Kallu
  • Poonjar Palace
  • Anamudi Peak

Best Things to Do in Kumaranalloor: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to indulge in while you’re in Kumaranalloor. Here are a few of the must-do options:

  • Watch a Kathakali performance
  • Explore the backwaters on a houseboat
  • Visit local markets for authentic shopping experiences

Week-Long Itinerary for Kumaranalloor

Day 1: Arrival in Kumaranalloor and temple visits Day 2: Explore the Kumarakom bird sanctuary Day 3: Visit Aruvikkuzhi waterfall and enjoy a picnic lunch Day 4: Spend the day hiking in Illickal Kallu Day 5: Take a houseboat ride through the Vembanad Lake Day 6: Discover local handicrafts and goods at the nearby markets Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day by the lake and watch the sunset

Food and Drink Scene of Kumaranalloor

Must-Try Dishes

Kumaranalloor is famous for its unique and delicious cuisine, and here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Appam and Egg Roast
  • Karimeen Pollichathu

Local Beverages

Some of the popular local drinks of Kumaranalloor include:

  • Tender Coconut Water
  • Ilaneer (Palm Wine)
  • Sugarcane Juice

Where to Stay in Kumaranalloor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kumaranalloor offers a range of accommodation options suitable for all budgets, including:

  • The Zuri Kumarakom Resort and Spa
  • Lake Palace Resort
  • The Coconut Lagoon

What to Buy in Kumaranalloor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kumaranalloor’s markets offer an array of souvenirs and local products perfect for gifting or memory-keeping. Some of the best items include:

  • Brass lamps and statues
  • Handmade Soap
  • Spices and tea

Local Culture and Customs of Kumaranalloor

Kumaranalloor’s unique culture is deeply rooted in the Hindu traditions and values. Here are a few customs and norms to keep in mind while travelling the city:

  • Dress modestly and remove footwear at temples
  • Respect the food and dining customs
  • Be mindful and avoid being overly intrusive

Best Time to Visit Kumaranalloor

The best time to visit Kumaranalloor is between October and March, during the winter season with mild temperatures and clear, sunny days. However, monsoon season from June to September is also a beautiful time to visit for the lush green scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumaranalloor

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Carry sufficient cash and change
  • Stay hydrated

Don’t:

  • Wear overly revealing clothing
  • Use foul language or gestures in public
  • Litter the streets or public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumaranalloor

Kumaranalloor is a safe city, and most areas are well-lit and heavily trafficked. However, keep the following safety tips in mind:

  • Avoid flashy displays of wealth or valuables
  • Use caution while walking alone at night
  • Keep emergency contact numbers handy

Health in Kumaranalloor

Kumaranalloor’s medical facilities are well-equipped and readily available. It’s essential to carry enough medication and take precautions against malaria, dengue, and other mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Kumaranalloor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kumaranalloor is easily accessible through multiple modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Kumaranalloor

  • Cochin International Airport (78 km)
  • Thiruvananthapuram International Airport (155 km)

Bus Stations Near Kumaranalloor

  • Kottayam Bus Stand (11 km)
  • Alappuzha Bus Stand (31 km)

Other Options

  • Private taxis
  • Trains from Kottayam Railway Station (12 km)

How to Get Around in Kumaranalloor

Kumaranalloor’s public transportation is well-connected and affordable. For local commutes, you can opt for:

  • Tuk-tuks or auto-rickshaws
  • Buses

Where to Go Next from Kumaranalloor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kumaranalloor’s surroundings offer multiple exciting destinations that are easy to explore from the city. Some of the best options include:

  • Alleppey Backwaters (20 km)
  • Vagamon Hill Station (60 km)
  • Munnar Hill Station (130 km)

Conclusion

Kumaranalloor is a hidden gem of South India, with so much to offer for all sorts of travellers. From the rich cultural heritage and natural attractions to the delicious cuisine and outdoor activities, Kumaranalloor promises an unforgettable experience.
