Introduction to Cherthala

Cherthala is a small town located in Central Travancore, a region in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Known for its scenic beauty, backwaters and peaceful surroundings, Cherthala is a popular tourist destination among both travellers seeking relaxation and adventure enthusiasts.

Surrounded by the Arabian Sea on one side, and the Vembanad Lake on the other, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. It’s an ideal travel destination for those looking to explore the local culture, indulge in outdoor activities, or just take a break from the hustle-bustle of city life.

In this travel guide, we’ve put together all the information you need to plan a trip to Cherthala, including places to visit, things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Cherthala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Krishnapuram Palace: Built in the 18th century by the Travancore king Marthanda Varma, Krishnapuram Palace is a historical monument with a unique architectural style. The palace houses several exhibits, including a museum that showcases artefacts from the Travancore era.
  • Pandavan Rock: According to Hindu mythology, the Pandavas, from the epic Mahabharata, are believed to have visited this rock during their exile. The rock has several interesting carvings and inscriptions.
  • Arookutty: Arookutty is a small town located near Cherthala. It’s known for a 100-year-old church, St. Augustine’s Church, which is a masterpiece of European architecture. The church features beautiful stained-glass windows and has a calm and peaceful atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Cherthala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arthunkal Beach: This picturesque beach has golden sand, clean waters, and a serene atmosphere. It’s a great place to relax and soak in the beauty of the Arabian sea. One can also experience water sports like parasailing, speed boating, and jet skiing.
  • Pathiramanal Island: Located in the Vembanad lake, Pathiramanal Island is a scenic and unspoilt natural beauty. The island is a birdwatcher’s paradise and is home to several species of migratory birds.
  • Thalikotta Seminary: The serene surroundings and peaceful atmosphere of this seminary are perfect for a quiet retreat. It’s a great place for meditation and yoga.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cherthala

  • Vembanad Lake: Tourists can take a bike or hike around the lake, which offers a scenic view of the surrounding area. It’s also possible to rent a boat or houseboat to experience the backwaters and take in the local culture.
  • Poochakkal-Chennamkary Road: This scenic route is perfect for bikers who love to explore the countryside. It passes through paddy fields, coconut groves and small villages.

Best Things to Do in Cherthala: Activities and Tours

  • Backwater Tours: A visit to Cherthala is incomplete without a backwater tour. Tourists can experience the local culture and scenic beauty of the Vembanad Lake through a boat or houseboat.
  • Ayurvedic Spa: Kerala is known for Ayurveda, an ancient system of medical treatment, and Cherthala is no exception. Tourists can indulge in Ayurvedic treatments or massages to rejuvenate and relax.
  • Vadakkekara Canal: The canal is a popular spot for fishing enthusiasts. Early morning is the best time to fish, and one can catch different species of fish.

Food and Drink Scene of Cherthala

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karimeen Pollichathu: A traditional Kerala fish delicacy, made with pearl spot fish, marinated in a blend of spices and wrapped in banana leaves before being grilled.
  • Appam with Chicken or Mutton Curry: A fluffy pancake made from fermented rice batter, which is served with spicy chicken or mutton curry.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Puttu is a steamed rice cake made with rice flour and coconut shavings. It is usually served with a spicy chickpea curry.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: A popular local alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut water.
  • Lemonade (Naranga Soda): A refreshing drink made from lemon, sugar and soda water.

Where to Stay in Cherthala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lake Palace Resort: A luxurious resort located on the banks of Vembanad Lake. The resort offers a range of amenities, including an outdoor pool, spa, and several dining options.
  • Marari Beach Resort: A rustic resort located on a beautiful secluded beach, this resort offers an authentic Kerala experience in a peaceful environment.
  • Lush Green Homestay: A budget-friendly homestay set amidst lush green gardens. It offers comfortable rooms, warm hospitality, and traditional Kerala cuisine.

What to Buy in Cherthala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coir Products: Kerala is famous for coir products made from coconut fibres. Tourists can buy coir mats, carpets, and handicrafts.
  • Spices: Cherthala is known for its spices. Tourists can buy a range of spices like cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon.

Local Culture and Customs of Cherthala

  • Carnatic Music: Cherthala is known for its rich history of Carnatic music. Many local festivals and events feature live performances from renowned Carnatic musicians.
  • Temple Festivals: Kerala is known for its elaborate temple festivals, and Cherthala is no exception. The annual festivals at the Sri Krishna Temple and St. Sebastian’s Church are popular among locals and tourists alike.

Best Time to Visit Cherthala

The best time to visit Cherthala is between October and February when the weather is pleasant and mild. The monsoon season from June to September should be avoided as it can lead to flooding and waterlogging in the backwaters and surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cherthala

Do:

  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious places.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and street food.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Litter or pollute the backwaters or beaches.
  • Carry too much cash or valuables while travelling.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cherthala

Cherthala is a relatively safe place for tourists, but it’s always advisable to exercise caution and take necessary precautions like avoiding dark alleys at night, not carrying too much cash or valuables, and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Cherthala

Tourists should drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent infections or other health issues. Additionally, it’s recommended to carry your own first aid kit with basic medication and mosquito repellent.

Getting to Cherthala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cherthala

  • Cochin International Airport: Located about 55 km away from Cherthala, Cochin International Airport is the nearest airport.

Bus Stations Near Cherthala

  • Cherthala Bus Station: Cherthala Bus Station is well-connected by roads to nearby towns and cities like Alappuzha, Kochi, and Trivandrum.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest railway station is Alappuzha Railway Station, which is about 15 km away from Cherthala. It’s well-connected to major Indian cities like Mumbai, New Delhi, and Chennai.

How to Get Around in Cherthala

Tourists can hire auto-rickshaws or taxis to get around Cherthala. Motorbikes and bicycles are also available for rent for those who want to explore the countryside at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Cherthala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Alappuzha: Also known as the ‘Venice of the East’, Alappuzha is famous for its backwaters, beaches and houseboats.
  • Kochi: The commercial capital of Kerala, Kochi is a vibrant city with a rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Kumarakom: Located about 20 km away from Cherthala, Kumarakom is a beautiful village famous for its bird sanctuary and backwaters.

Conclusion

Cherthala is a hidden gem in the heart of Kerala that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and tranquillity. With its serene backwaters, picturesque beaches, beautiful architecture, and warm hospitality, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone seeking an authentic Kerala experience. Plan your trip to Cherthala now and make beautiful memories that will last a lifetime!
