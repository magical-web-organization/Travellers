Introduction to Pathanamthitta

Located in Central Travancore, Pathanamthitta is a quaint town in the state of Kerala, India. Known for its natural beauty, lush greenery, and culturally rich heritage sites, Pathanamthitta is a hidden gem in the southern part of India. This town is considered to be the ‘Pilgrim Capital of Kerala’ because it is home to a myriad of ancient temples and churches.

List of Places to Visit in Pathanamthitta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll have a field day exploring the historical and heritage sites that Pathanamthitta has to offer. Some of the must-visit destinations in this town are:

  • Sabarimala Ayyappa Temple, known to host one of the world’s largest annual pilgrimages.
  • Malayalapuzha Devi Temple, an ancient Hindu temple dating back to the 9th century.
  • Perumpazhuthoor Mahadeva Temple, a beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple, a magnificent temple famous for its exquisite mural paintings.
  • St. George’s Orthodox Church, an ancient church dating back to the 4th century.

Natural Attractions of Pathanamthitta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts, Pathanamthitta is a paradise-like destination. Here are some breathtaking natural attractions that you must explore on your trip:

  • Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls, a majestic waterfall surrounded by thick green forests and natural rock formations.
  • Pamba River, a beautiful river that originates from the Sabarimala hills.
  • Achankovil River, the fifth longest river in Kerala, known for its serene beauty and calm waters.
  • Konni Elephant Training Center, a popular destination for animal lovers, offering hands-on experiences with elephants.
  • Parunthumpara Hill View Point, a scenic hilltop offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta has an abundance of beautiful trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes for you to explore on your trip:

  • Gavi Trail, a scenic trail that passes through dense forests and offers sightings of exotic wildlife.
  • Nilackal-Kalaketty Trail, a popular trail that takes you on a journey through tea and spice plantations.
  • Charalkunnu Trail, a beautiful trail that offers panoramic views of the scenic mountains and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Pathanamthitta: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Pathanamthitta has several fun activities and tours to make your trip memorable. Here are a few options for you:

  • Bamboo Rafting, a unique adventure that takes you on a bamboo raft down the crystal clear waters of the Pamba River.
  • Ayurvedic Spa & Massage, relax and rejuvenate yourself with traditional Ayurvedic Spa & Massage treatments.
  • Houseboat Cruise, explore the breathtaking backwaters of Kerala by cruising down the tranquil waters on a luxurious houseboat.
  • Jeep Safari, take a thrilling off-road jeep safari through the dense forests to spot exotic wildlife and view stunning natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pathanamthitta

Here’s an itinerary for your week-long trip to Pathanamthitta:

Day 1: Visit Sabarimala Ayyappa Temple and Malayalapuzha Devi Temple. Day 2: Travel to Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls and enjoy bamboo rafting down the Pamba River. Day 3: Visit Achankovil River and Konni Elephant Training Center. Day 4: Travel to the Parunthumpara Hill View Point and enjoy a scenic hike. Day 5: Visit Perumpazhuthoor Mahadeva Temple and Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple. Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing ayurvedic spa and massage treatment. Day 7: Explore the backwaters of Kerala on a luxurious houseboat cruise.

Food and Drink Scene of Pathanamthitta

Must-Try Dishes

Pathanamthitta is famous for its authentic Malayali cuisine, and you must try these traditional dishes on your trip:

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry, steamed rice cake with chickpea curry.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu, spicy and tangy grilled fish.
  • Avial, a mixed vegetable curry with coconut and yogurt.
  • Appam and Stew, soft and fluffy pancakes served with flavorful chicken or vegetable stew.
  • Banana Chips, crispy banana chips made from unripe bananas.

Local Beverages

On your visit to Pathanamthitta, you must taste the famous local beverages:

  • Chukku Kappi, hot and spicy coffee made with ginger and other spices.
  • Elaneer, refreshing drink made with tender coconut water.
  • Toddy, fermented drink made from coconut trees.

Where to Stay in Pathanamthitta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There’s no shortage of accommodations in Pathanamthitta. Here are some options that offer excellent comfort and convenience:

  • The Gateway Hotel, a luxurious hotel offering great amenities and stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Hotel Hills and Hues, a beautiful boutique hotel located in the heart of Pathanamthitta, offering great service and amenities.
  • Dewland Hotels, a budget-friendly option located near the town center, offering comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Hostel Central, a cozy and affordable option for backpackers, offering a shared lounge and kitchen.

What to Buy in Pathanamthitta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pathanamthitta is famous for its traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some local products that you must bring home:

  • Aranmula Metal Mirror, a unique and famous artwork made of polished brass and copper.
  • Cotton Sarees and Mundu, traditional Kerala-style clothing.
  • Handicrafts, made of coconut shells, bamboo, and wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is a land of diverse cultures, with people of various religions and beliefs living harmoniously. The town is known to celebrate many festivals, such as:

  • Sabarimala Festival, an annual pilgrimage to the Sabarimala Ayyappa Temple.
  • Onam, a popular harvest festival celebrated in Kerala.
  • Vishu, a festival that marks the beginning of the Malayali New Year.

Best Time to Visit Pathanamthitta

The best time to visit Pathanamthitta is between December and February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pathanamthitta

Do:

  • Respect the customs and culture of the people.
  • Take off your shoes before entering temples and religious places.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately.
  • Try out the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Do not disrespect religious places or customs.
  • Do not shout or speak loudly in public places.
  • Do not use drugs or engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. However, tourists should still take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded places.
  • Do not travel alone during late hours in secluded or unfamiliar areas.

Health in Pathanamthitta

Tourists should take necessary precautions and vaccinations before traveling to Pathanamthitta. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water or boiled water and avoid street food to prevent water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Pathanamthitta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pathanamthitta

The nearest airport to Pathanamthitta is the Trivandrum International Airport, located approximately 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta has a well-connected bus station, which operates several buses to other parts of Kerala, including Trivandrum, Cochin, and Kottayam.

Other Options

Tourists can also travel to Pathanamthitta via taxis or private cars from the Trivandrum International Airport or the railway station located in Chengannur.

How to Get Around in Pathanamthitta

The best way to travel around Pathanamthitta is by taxi, auto-rickshaw, or local buses. Tourists can also rent a car or a bike to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Pathanamthitta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pathanamthitta is well-connected to several other beautiful destinations in Kerala, such as:

  • Alleppey, known as the ‘Venice of the East,’ known for its peaceful backwaters and beautiful beaches.
  • Kumarakom, a beautiful lake town famous for its beautiful resorts and bird sanctuary.
  • Munnar, a world-famous hill station home to beautiful tea plantations and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Pathanamthitta is an ideal destination for tourists who want to explore the natural and cultural heritage of Kerala. With its rich history, unique traditions, and picturesque landscapes, Pathanamthitta is a hidden gem waiting to be explored.
