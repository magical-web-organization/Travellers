Introduction to Ottapalam

Ottapalam is a charming town located in the Palakkad district of Kerala, a south Indian state known for its lush greenery, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage. The town is dotted with temples, churches, and historical landmarks that showcase the vibrant culture of Kerala. Ottapalam is also an excellent gateway to explore the natural beauty of nearby forest ranges, wildlife sanctuaries, and waterfalls.

List of Places to Visit in Ottapalam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kandath Tharavad: A 200-year-old heritage mansion filled with antique artifacts, furniture, and jewelry that display the rich history of Kerala.
  • Kollengode Palace: A 17th-century palace that once belonged to the Zamorin rulers of Kerala, featuring traditional Kerala-style architecture.
  • Pattambi Nercha: An annual festival dedicated to the local Muslim saint, Vavar, that showcases an array of cultural events, music, and food.

Natural Attractions of Ottapalam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silent Valley National Park: A lush rainforest that is home to a variety of rare and exotic animals, birds, and plants.
  • Meenvallam Waterfalls: A picturesque waterfall located in the Western Ghats mountain range, surrounded by dense forest.
  • Dhoni Hills: A stunning hill station that offers panoramic views of the Palakkad district.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ottapalam

  • Western Ghats Trail: A multi-day trek through the lush Western Ghats mountain range, featuring spectacular views, waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.
  • Silent Valley Trek: A challenging trek through one of the last remaining virgin rainforests in India, featuring a variety of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Ottapalam: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the local bazaars and markets to discover traditional handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry.
  • Attend a Kathakali performance, a traditional dance-drama form that originated in Kerala.
  • Take cooking classes to learn the art of traditional Kerala cuisine, such as appam and stew.

Food and Drink Scene of Ottapalam

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appam and stew: Rice pancakes served with vegetable or meat stew
  • Puttu and kadala curry: Rice flour steamed in cylindrical bamboo or metal containers and served with chickpea curry
  • Malabar biryani: Spiced rice and meat dish cooked in layers with caramelized onions and herbs

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water: The refreshing water inside a young coconut
  • Sulaimani tea: A traditional spiced black tea made with cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger

Where to Stay in Ottapalam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Gateway Hotel Munnar by Taj (Luxury): A luxury hotel offering stunning views of the Western Ghats mountain range.
  • Hotel Royal Inn (Mid-Range): A comfortable and centrally located hotel with modern amenities.
  • Dhanalakshmi Residency (Budget): A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Ottapalam.

What to Buy in Ottapalam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Handmade wicker baskets, pottery, and wooden carvings crafted by local artisans.
  • Spices: Kerala is known for its array of aromatic spices, including cloves, cardamom, and black pepper.

Local Culture and Customs of Ottapalam

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples, mosques, and homes.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as not eating beef, which is considered taboo in Hindu culture.

Best Time to Visit Ottapalam

The best time to visit Ottapalam is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and there is minimal rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ottapalam

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and attend cultural events to fully experience the vibrant culture of Kerala.
  • Respect the environment and wildlife by following responsible travel practices.
  • Take precautions to avoid mosquito bites to prevent mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activity that harms the environment or wildlife, such as littering or feeding animals.
  • Disrespect religious or cultural practices.
  • Drink tap water, which may not be safe for consumption.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ottapalam

  • Petty theft can occur in crowded areas, so keep your belongings secure.
  • Take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, which can transmit dengue and malaria.
  • Use caution when hiking or trekking, especially during the rainy season, as trails can be slippery and dangerous.

Health in Ottapalam

  • Tap water may not be safe for consumption, so stick to bottled water.
  • Consider getting vaccinated for diseases like hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus before traveling to India.

Getting to Ottapalam: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Coimbatore International Airport, located 90 km from Ottapalam, is the nearest airport.
  • Ottapalam is well-connected by bus to major cities in Kerala and neighboring states.
  • Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of local transportation in Ottapalam.

How to Get Around in Ottapalam

  • Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of getting around Ottapalam.
  • Buses are also available to travel locally and to neighboring towns and cities.
  • Taxis can be rented for a day or for longer periods to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Ottapalam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palakkad: A city known for its ancient temples, historic sites, and scenic beauty.
  • Kochi: A popular coastal city famous for its colonial architecture, beaches, and backwater cruises.
  • Munnar: A picturesque hill station known for its tea plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries.

Conclusion

Ottapalam is a hidden gem in Kerala that offers a glimpse into the vibrant culture and natural beauty of the state. From exploring historical landmarks and trying traditional Kerala cuisine to hiking in rainforests and attending cultural festivals, Ottapalam has something for everyone. Follow our comprehensive guide to make the most of your visit to this stunning town in Kerala.
