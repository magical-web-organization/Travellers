Introduction to Chittur

Chittur-Thathamangalam is a rural town in the Palakkad district of Kerala, India. It is home to many temples and heritage sites, scenic locations, and cultural attractions. The town is situated on the banks of the rivers Bharathapuzha and Thootha, and the Western Ghats mountain range forms a beautiful backdrop to the town. The town has a predominantly agricultural economy, and fostered the growth of culture, music, and art over the years.

List of Places to Visit in Chittur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Chittur is steeped in ancient history and culture. Here are some of the must-visit historical and cultural attractions:

Chittur Gurumadam

Chittur Gurumadam is an ancient Brahminical study centre, dating back to the 10th century. It was a hub of Vedic learning in ancient times.

Shoranur Junction Railway Heritage Museum

The railway museum showcases the historical significance of Shoranur junction railway station, one of the oldest railway stations in India. The museum displays several locomotives and equipment used in early railways.

Kattil Madom Temple

Kattil Madom Temple is an ancient temple built in the traditional Kerala architectural style. It is a perfect representation of the religious and cultural heritage of the region.

Palakkad Fort

Palakkad Fort is an ancient fort located in the heart of Palakkad town. It was built by Hyder Ali of Mysore and was later captured by the British in 1784.

Natural Attractions of Chittur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chittur is known for its scenic beauty, and there are numerous natural attractions in and around the town that you should visit:

Pattambi Nercha

Pattambi Nercha is a popular festival in the region, which takes place in February every year. It is a week-long celebration, where people from all over the region come together to celebrate the heritage and culture of the town.

Mangalam Dam

Mangalam Dam is a popular picnic spot in the region, located around 18 kilometres from Chittur. The dam was built in 1956 on the river Mangalam, and it is surrounded by lush green hills, making it an ideal place for trekking.

Meenvallam Waterfalls

Meenvallam Waterfalls is the perfect spot for nature lovers. It is located around 37 kilometres from Chittur and is surrounded by lush green forests and hills.

Silent Valley National Park

Silent Valley National Park is a biodiversity hotspot and a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. It is located around 100 kilometres from Chittur and has a dense forest area, home to various wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chittur

Chittur offers some of the best biking and hiking routes in the region. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  1. Chittur – Nelliampathy – Valparai
  2. Chittur – Meenvallam Waterfalls – Kollengode
  3. Chittur – Silent Valley National Park – Attappady

Best Things to Do in Chittur: Activities and Tours

Chittur offers numerous opportunities for adventure lovers and culture enthusiasts alike. Here are some of the best activities to try:

Visit the Local Temples

Chittur is renowned for its magnificent temples that depict the cultural heritage of the region. The temples are unique, celebrate the local customs and rituals and are worth a visit.

Trekking and Hiking

Chittur offers several trails that offer picturesque views of the mountains and forests. The attappadi trek is a popular trek that takes you through the Silent Valley National Park.

Attend the Local Festivals

Chittur hosts several festivals that celebrate the local customs and culture of the region. Attending these festivals is a great way to experience the vibrant culture of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chittur

Here is an itinerary for a week-long visit to the town of Chittur:

Day 1: Visit the Chittur Gurumadam and learn about the ancient Vedic culture and tradition of the region.

Day 2: Visit Kattil Madom Temple and Palakkad Fort to explore the ancient history of the town.

Day 3: Take a journey to Mangalam Dam and enjoy the serene beauty of the hills.

Day 4: Explore the Meenvallam Waterfalls and trek to the top for a magnificent view of the valley.

Day 5: Attend the Pattambi Nercha festival and experience the local culture and customs.

Day 6: Visit the Silent Valley National Park and take a trek through the dense forests.

Day 7: Take a day to relax and enjoy the local cuisine and local hospitality of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Chittur

Must-Try Dishes

Chittur’s cuisine is famous for its flavourful dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Appam and Stew

Appam and Stew is a popular breakfast dish in the region, made with rice flour, coconut milk, and spices.

Puttu and Kadala Curry

Puttu and Kadala Curry is a traditional breakfast dish, made with steamed rice flour and served with spicy chickpea curry.

Nadan Kozhi Curry

Nadan Kozhi Curry is a popular chicken curry that derives its rich flavour from a blend of spices, coconut milk, and curry leaves.

Local Beverages

Toddy

Toddy is a locally produced alcoholic beverage made from palm sap. The drink is mildly alcoholic and has a sweet flavour.

Tea

Tea is a popular beverage in the region and is generally consumed hot, with a blend of aromatic spices.

  3. Coir products, like coir mats, ropes, and baskets

Local Culture and Customs of Chittur

Chittur has a unique culture and customs, heavily influenced by the region’s rich history and heritage. Some of the customs worth mentioning include:

  1. Makarasankranti Pattambi Nercha Festival
  2. Locals take pride in their handcrafted items, unique art, and folk music
  3. Traditional dress like Mundu, Neriyathum, saree, and blouse are essential to local attire

Best Time to Visit Chittur

The best time to visit Chittur is between the months of October and March, as the weather is favourable during this time. The monsoon season, from June to September, is best avoided as there are frequent rains and high humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chittur

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions
  2. Try local cuisine and drinks
  3. Contribute to the local economy by buying handicrafts and local products

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples
  2. Litter in public places
  3. Disrespect local customs or sacred sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Chittur

Chittur is generally a safe town, but it’s advisable to take basic precautions while travelling. Always secure your belongings, avoid isolated areas at night-time, and be cautious while using public transportation.

Health in Chittur

The town has government and private hospitals, and you can easily find pharmacies and medical shops in the town for any medical emergencies.

Conclusion

Chittur is a hidden gem of Kerala, offering visitors an opportunity to experience the rich history and heritage of the region. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, Chittur has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this beautiful town in Southern India.
