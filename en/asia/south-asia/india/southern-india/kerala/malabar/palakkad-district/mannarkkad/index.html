Mannarkkad Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Mannarkkad is a hidden gem in the Palakkad district of Kerala, India. Situated in the Malabar region of Southern India, Mannarkkad is a stunning destination with a unique culture, delicious food, and natural beauty that will leave you mesmerized. Here is a comprehensive tour guide to help you discover the best of Mannarkkad.

Introduction to Mannarkkad

Mannarkkad is a small town located in the Western Ghats, surrounded by lush green forests, rolling hills, and wildlife sanctuaries. The town is a reflection of Kerala’s rich cultural heritage, with ancient temples, historic monuments, and museums that showcase the region’s history and traditions.

The town is dotted with waterfalls, trekking trails, and scenic vistas that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Mannarkkad is also known for its vibrant markets and local festivals, which provide a great way to experience the town’s unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Mannarkkad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Valluvanadu History Museum: The museum houses artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the cultural history of the Valluvanadu region.
  2. Kottayi Kovilakam: This palace was built in the 18th century and is an example of traditional Kerala architecture.
  3. Sree Parthasarathy Temple: The temple, located in the heart of Mannarkkad, is one of the oldest in the region and is known for its intricate carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Mannarkkad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Silent Valley National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to rare and endangered species of plants and animals.
  2. Attappady Hills: The hills are a popular trekking destination and offer stunning views of the surrounding valleys.
  3. Meenvallam Waterfalls: Located in a dense forest, the waterfall is a great spot for a picnic and is surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mannarkkad

  1. Silent Valley Trek: The trek to the Silent Valley National Park takes you through dense forests, grasslands, and hilly terrain.
  2. Meenvallam Trek: The trek to Meenvallam Waterfalls is a moderate trek that takes you through dense forests, streams, and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Mannarkkad: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit Tribal Villages: The town is surrounded by tribal villages that offer a glimpse into the unique culture and traditions of the local tribes.
  2. Attend Local Festivals: Mannarkkad is known for its vibrant festivals, including the Theyyam Festival, which celebrates the region’s unique culture and traditions.
  3. Wildlife Safari: Take a wildlife safari at the Silent Valley National Park and spot the rare and endangered species of animals and birds that call this park home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mannarkkad

Day 1: Visit Valluvanadu History Museum and Kottayi Kovilakam. Day 2: Trek to Silent Valley National Park. Day 3: Visit Meenvallam Waterfalls, and take a nature walk in the surrounding forests. Day 4: Attend a local festival, such as the Theyyam Festival or the Mannarkkad Pooram. Day 5: Visit Sree Parthasarathy Temple and nearby tribal villages. Day 6: Take a wildlife safari at Silent Valley National Park. Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa or yoga center.

Food and Drink Scene of Mannarkkad

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Puttu and Kadala Curry: A traditional Kerala breakfast of steamed rice cake and chickpea curry.
  2. Appam and Stew: A light and fluffy rice pancake served with a fragrant coconut-based stew.
  3. Erachi Varutharacha Curry: A spicy and flavorful beef curry made with roasted coconut and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Coconut water: A refreshing and hydrating drink that is readily available in the town.
  2. Tea and Coffee: Mannarkkad is known for its fresh and flavorful tea and coffee.

Where to Stay in Mannarkkad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Rainforest Resort: A luxury resort situated in a pristine forest environment, offering a range of activities and accommodations.
  2. Green Nest Plantation Stay: A homestay located in a tea plantation, offering a peaceful and serene atmosphere.
  3. Kallancherry Retreat: A budget-friendly retreat located in the heart of the town, offering comfortable rooms and common spaces.

What to Buy in Mannarkkad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Spices: Mannarkkad is known for its exceptional spices, including cardamom, black pepper, and cinnamon.
  2. Handloom Products: The town is known for its fine silk sarees and cotton fabrics, handwoven by local artisans.
  3. Tea and Coffee: Bring home some of the flavorful and aromatic tea and coffee produced in Mannarkkad.

Local Culture and Customs of Mannarkkad

Mannarkkad has a unique blend of cultures, heavily influenced by its tribal communities. One of the most prominent customs is the Theyyam dance, a ritualistic dance form that is performed during the local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Mannarkkad

The best time to visit Mannarkkad is between August and March, when the weather is pleasant and mild. The monsoon season, between June and August, is also a good time to visit for those who enjoy the rain and lush greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mannarkkad

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting temples or tribal villages.
  2. Respect the local culture and customs.
  3. Carry your ID and travel documents with you at all times.

Don’t:

  1. Consume alcohol or drugs in public places.
  2. Pluck flowers or plants from the national park or surrounding forests.
  3. Interact with the local wildlife outside of designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mannarkkad

Mannarkkad is a safe and peaceful town, and incidents of crime are rare. However, it is advisable to take standard precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and remaining aware of your surroundings.

Health in Mannarkkad

Mannarkkad has adequate medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, it is advisable to carry necessary medications and consult a doctor before embarking on any outdoor activities.

Getting to Mannarkkad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mannarkkad

The nearest airport to Mannarkkad is Coimbatore International Airport, located approximately 60 km away. Another option is Calicut International Airport, located approximately 90 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mannarkkad

Mannarkkad is well-connected to major cities in Kerala by bus. The town has its own bus stand, from where regular buses operate to Palakkad, Coimbatore, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available from Coimbatore and Calicut airports.

How to Get Around in Mannarkkad

The best way to get around Mannarkkad is by hiring a taxi or auto-rickshaw. Buses are also available for travel to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Mannarkkad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Malampuzha Dam: A popular picnic spot and a great place for boating, located approximately 55 km away.
  2. Nelliyampathy Hills: A lush green mountain range with trekking trails and breathtaking views, located approximately 60 km away.

Conclusion

Mannarkkad is a hidden gem in the Palakkad district of Kerala, India, waiting to be explored. Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Mannarkkad has something to offer for everyone. Visit this charming town and experience Kerala’s rich culture and natural beauty like never before.
