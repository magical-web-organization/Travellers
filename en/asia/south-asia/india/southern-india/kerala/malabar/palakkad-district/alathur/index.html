Introduction to Alathur

Alathur, a town located in Palakkad district, is a perfect destination for those who seek to escape the buzz of city life and indulge in the rustic charm of Kerala. The town is situated in the Malabar region of Kerala and is well-known for its rich cultural heritage, serene backwaters, and lush greenery.

Tourists can expect a lot of natural beauty in Alathur, which is encompassed by the Western Ghats in the east and the Arabian Sea in the west. The town is also popular for its wide range of flora and fauna, which attracts nature lovers from across the world.

Read on to discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local customs to know in Alathur, Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in Alathur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palakkad Fort

The Palakkad Fort, which dates back to the 18th century, is one of the most iconic landmarks of the town. The fort has a rich historical and cultural significance and has been a witness to several historic events. The fort also boasts of lush green gardens and several cultural programs are hosted here during important festivals.

Tipu Sultan’s Fort

Tipu Sultan’s Fort, located in Palakkad, is a significant historical monument of Alathur. The fort was built by Tipu Sultan, the ruler of Mysore, and stands as a testament to the architectural marvel of the bygone era. The fort is an epitome of the blend of Indian and Islamic architecture.

Kollengode Palace

Kollengode Palace is yet another cultural attraction in Alathur, known for its ancient architecture and rich cultural heritage. The palace boasts of stunning paintings and carvings, including intricate woodwork and ceiling paintings. Tourists can also partake in a guided tour of the palace to learn about its cultural and historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Alathur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Malampuzha Dam

The Malampuzha Dam is one of the most iconic landmarks of Alathur and is known for its scenic beauty. The dam is set amidst lush greenery and is surrounded by a picturesque garden which is adorned with several sculptures and fountains.

Nelliampathy

Nelliampathy is a stunning hill station that is located at a distance of around 60 km from Alathur. Set amidst lush greenery and tea plantations, the hill station boasts of scenic waterfalls, trekking trails and picture-perfect views.

Silent Valley National Park

The Silent Valley National Park, located around 60 km from Alathur, is one of the most popular attractions in the region, which is renowned for its rich biodiversity. Tourists can spot several rare species of flora and fauna here and engage in trekking, birdwatching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alathur

Alathur boasts of several hiking and biking routes, which provide tourists with the perfect opportunity to explore the town’s scenic beauty on foot or on a cycle.

Pothundy Dam

Pothundy Dam is located at a distance of around 17 km from Alathur and is a popular spot for cycling and hiking. The dam is surrounded by lush greenery and offers stunning views of the surrounding regions.

Siruvani Dam

Siruvani Dam is a popular choice for hiking enthusiasts and is located at a distance of around 90 km from Alathur. The dam is surrounded by pristine forests and offers scenic views of the Western Ghats.

Best Things to Do in Alathur: Activities and Tours

Visit a Tea Plantation

Alathur is known for its fragrant and flavorful tea that is grown in the region. Tourists can visit one of the several tea plantations in the town to partake in a guided tour and witness the process of tea-making.

Experience Traditional Kerala Cuisine

Alathur is known for its lip-smacking cuisine, which is rooted in traditional Kerala flavors. Tourists can sample local delicacies such as puttu and kadala curry, appam and stew, and the famous Kerala biryani.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alathur

Day 1 - Arrive in Alathur and explore the town’s cultural attractions such as Palakkad Fort and Tipu Sultan’s Fort.

Day 2 - Visit a tea plantation and learn about the process of tea-making. Head to Nelliampathy for stunning panoramic views of the region.

Day 3 - Enjoy a leisurely day exploring the scenic Malampuzha Dam and its surrounding gardens.

Day 4 - Hike or bike around Siruvani Dam for breathtaking views of the Western Ghats.

Day 5 - Head to the Silent Valley National Park for a day of trekking and wildlife spotting.

Day 6 - Explore the cultural significance of Kollengode Palace and its stunning paintings and carvings.

Day 7 - Experience the local cuisine of Alathur at one of the town’s many eateries.

Food and Drink Scene of Alathur

Must-Try Dishes

Alathur is known for its traditional Kerala cuisine, which is dominated by rice, fish, coconut and spices.

Puttu and Kadala Curry

Puttu and kadala curry is a popular breakfast dish in Alathur, made with steamed cylinders of ground rice and lentils, and chickpea curry.

Appam and Stew

Appam and stew is a popular lunch and dinner dish, made of fermented rice pancakes and vegetable or chicken stew.

Kerala Biryani

Kerala biryani is a flavorful biryani made with local spices and slow-cooked meat, usually duck or mutton.

Local Beverages

Tea

Alathur tea, grown in tea plantations in the region, is highly sought after for its distinctive aroma and flavor.

Toddy

Toddy is a local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It is served fresh and is a popular drink in Alathur.

Where to Stay in Alathur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alathur offers several accommodation options, which cater to the varied budgets of tourists.

Best Luxury Accommodations

Kairali - The Ayurvedic Healing Village

Kairali - The Ayurvedic Healing Village is a luxury resort located in Palakkad and is an ideal choice for those seeking to indulge in wellness activities and Ayurvedic therapies.

Thusaragiri

Thusaragiri is a luxury resort located amidst scenic landscapes and tea plantations in Nelliyampathy.

Best Budget Accommodations

Sahyadri Ayurvedic Heritage Resort

Sahyadri Ayurvedic Heritage Resort is a budget-friendly accommodation option that offers a host of wellness activities and Ayurvedic therapies.

Vrindavan Mist City

Vrindavan Mist City is a cozy guest house located in Palakkad and is a perfect choice for budget-conscious travelers who seek comfort and convenience.

What to Buy in Alathur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Coconut Shell Handicrafts

Coconut shell handicrafts are a popular item in Alathur, and tourists can purchase intricate hand-carved coconut shell products that include jewelry, decorative items, and more.

Spices

Alathur is known for its fragrant spices, and tourists can purchase locally produced spices such as cardamom, pepper, cinnamon, and cloves.

Handloom Fabrics

Handloom fabrics such as lungis, sarongs, and saris, are popular items in Alathur and can be purchased from local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Alathur

The culture of Alathur is deeply rooted in its rich heritage, and the town is known for its vibrant festivals and rituals, which are celebrated with great fanfare. The people of Alathur are warm and welcoming, and visitors are expected to follow local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Alathur

The best time to visit Alathur is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and mild. However, tourists can also visit Alathur during the monsoon season, from June to September, to witness the lush greenery and the stunning waterfalls in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alathur

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry a copy of your identification at all times
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Keep noise levels down, particularly at night

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Drink alcohol or smoke in public places
  • Litter or damage the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Alathur

Alathur is a safe town for tourists to visit, but it is always advisable to remain alert and avoid unlit and deserted areas. It is also recommended to carry a copy of your identification at all times.

Health in Alathur

Visitors are advised to take basic precautions such as drinking bottled water and avoiding uncooked street food to stay healthy. Visitors are also recommended to carry necessary medication and a hat to protect themselves from the sun.

Getting to Alathur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alathur is well-connected by roadways, railways, and airways.

Bus Stations Near Alathur

  • Alathur Bus Stand

Other Options

  • Train - Palakkad Railway Station is the nearest railway station to Alathur.
  • Car - Visitors can hire a taxi or a self-drive car to get to Alathur.

How to Get Around in Alathur

Alathur is well-connected by local buses and taxis. Visitors can also hire two-wheelers to get around the town and explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Alathur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palakkad

Palakkad, a town located 40 km from Alathur, is a popular destination known for its cultural and historical significance. The town is home to several historical monuments, fortresses and is a perfect destination for those seeking to explore the rich heritage of Kerala.

Coimbatore

Coimbatore, located around 67 km from Alathur, is a vibrant city that is popular for its textile industry and beautiful landscapes. The city also boasts of stunning temples and museums.

Conclusion

Alathur, a town located in Palakkad district, is a perfect destination for tourists who seek to explore the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Kerala. The town is home to several historical landmarks, scenic waterfalls, and trekking trails, making it a perfect destination for adventure enthusiasts. Moreover, Alathur is known for its flavorful local cuisine and warmth and hospitality of its people. So, plan a trip to Alathur to experience the true essence of Kerala.
