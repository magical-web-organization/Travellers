Introduction to Pattambi

Pattambi is a small town in the Palakkad district of Kerala, Southern India, located on the banks of the Bharathapuzha river. This town is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and temples.

The town has a significant role in the history of Kerala and played a crucial role in the Indian Independence Movement. Pattambi’s beauty lies in its traditional houses and historical monuments, making it a must-visit destination for tourists.

Here is a comprehensive guide to Pattambi that includes the must-visit places, things to do, food, and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Pattambi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kunchan Nambiar Smarakam: A memorial dedicated to Kunchan Nambiar, a renowned poet and satirist from Kerala.
  • Thirunavaya Nava Mukunda Temple: One of the famous Hindu temples in Kerala, which is dedicated to Lord Vishnu.
  • Vettakkorumakan Temple: A famous temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Thiruvallathol Maha Vishnu Temple: An ancient temple known for its annual festival “Thiruvathira,” which is primarily celebrated by women.
  • Pattambi Nercha: A famous festival celebrated annually in Pattambi, where people congregate in large numbers.

Natural Attractions of Pattambi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bharathapuzha River: Enjoy the scenic beauty of the Bharathapuzha river by taking a relaxing boat ride or simply enjoying the view.
  • Thiruvanchikulam Temple: A holy place on the banks of the river, surrounded by serene greenery is perfect for some peace and relaxation.
  • Kavil Kadavu: A picturesque location on the bank of the river, where families often gather for picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pattambi

  • Perumkadavu: A scenic and relaxing cycling trail
  • Kavil Kadavu to Peruvayal: A trek that takes you through the beauty of small villages and paddy fields.

Best Things to Do in Pattambi: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local markets: Pattambi is famous for its local handicraft products such as pottery and rugs made from coir fibers.
  • Attend Pattambi Nercha: Experience the vibrant and joyous atmosphere when several devotees gather to make offerings at the local temple.
  • Boat rides: Take a relaxing boat ride along the Bharathapuzha River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pattambi

Day 1: Visit Kunchan Nambiar Smarakam and Thirunavaya Nava Mukunda Temple
Day 2: Take a boat ride on the Bharathapuzha River and relax in Kavil Kadavu Day 3: Visit Vettakkorumakan Temple and Thiruvallathol Maha Vishnu Temple
Day 4: Take a bike ride to Perumkadavu and explore local markets
Day 5: Visit the Thiruvanchikulam Temple and relax by the river
Day 6: Attend Pattambi Nercha Festival
Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby towns of Mannarkkad and Ottapalam.

Food and Drink Scene of Pattambi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: A popular breakfast dish in Kerala made of steamed rice flour and served with a curry made from black chickpeas.
  • Payasam: A dessert made with rice or vermicelli, coconut milk, sugar, and cardamom.
  • Fish Curry: A spicy curry made with local fish.

Local Beverages

  • Chukku Kappi: A medicinal drink made from dried ginger and coffee.
  • Toddy: A local alcoholic beverage made from coconut sap.

Where to Stay in Pattambi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pattambi Heritage Resort: A heritage resort that blends old-world charm with modern amenities.
  • The Palms Regency: A luxurious hotel that offers comfortable accommodations.
  • Pattambi Tourist Home: A budget-friendly option for travelers.

What to Buy in Pattambi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Pattambi is known for its distinctive clay pottery.
  • Rugs made from coir: Pattambi is also famous for rugs made from coir fibers.

Local Culture and Customs of Pattambi

Pattambi is famous for its local art forms and cultural events such as:

  • Nadodi Nritham: A traditional dance form performed by the Adivasi tribes
  • Theyyam: A ritualistic dance form performed in temples
  • Panchavadyam: A musical ensemble performed during temple festivals.

Best Time to Visit Pattambi

The best time to visit Pattambi is from September to February when the climate is pleasant, and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pattambi

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or cultural events.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter.
  • Do not disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Do not take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pattambi

Pattambi is a safe town, but travelers should exercise caution, particularly when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Pattambi

Travelers should drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent waterborne diseases. It’s advisable to carry bug repellent, particularly in the rainy season.

Getting to Pattambi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pattambi

The nearest airport to Pattambi is Cochin International Airport, which is approximately 96 km away.

Bus Stations Near Pattambi

Pattambi is well-connected by buses, and the Pattambi bus stand is the main bus station. Buses from major cities such as Kochi, Thrissur, and Palakkad run regularly to Pattambi.

Other Options

One can also take a train to Pattambi Railway Station, which is connected to major cities in Kerala and beyond.

How to Get Around in Pattambi

Auto-rickshaws are the most convenient mode of transportation in Pattambi, and they are readily available. Bicycles can also be hired to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Pattambi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mannarkkad: Known for its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, this town is famous for its ancient temples.
  • Ottapalam: Located in the Palakkad district, Ottapalam is known for its historical significance and lush greenery.

Conclusion

Pattambi is a town with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty to offer. From ancient temples to the Bharathapuzha river, there’s plenty to keep travelers engaged. Visit Pattambi to experience the joy and cultural diversity of Kerala.
