Introduction to Shornur

Shornur is a small town located in the Palakkad district of Kerala, a southern state in India. Known for its rich cultural heritage and awe-inspiring natural beauty, Shornur is a popular tourist destination among nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers alike. The town is situated between the Western Ghats and the Bharathappuzha River, making it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Shornur

If you’re planning a trip to Shornur, here are some must-see destinations to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Shornur Railway Station: Built-in 1860, Shornur Railway Station is one of the oldest and busiest railway stations in Kerala. The station’s architecture is a blend of Indian and European styles.
  2. Kollengode Palace: Located about 12 kilometers from Shornur, Kollengode Palace is a 17th-century palace that once served as the headquarters of the local ruling family. The palace is known for its stunning architecture and intricate wood carvings.
  3. Tipu Sultan Fort: Located in the nearby town of Palakkad, Tipu Sultan Fort is a 18th-century fort built by the legendary ruler Tipu Sultan. The fort’s architecture showcases a mix of Indian and Islamic styles.
  4. Palakkad Fort: Located in the heart of Palakkad town, Palakkad Fort is a 18th-century fort that once served as the stronghold of the local rulers. The fort is known for its stunning architecture, which blends Indian and European styles.

Natural Attractions of Shornur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Silent Valley National Park: Located about 50 kilometers from Shornur, the Silent Valley National Park is a biodiverse region situated in the Western Ghats. The park is home to many endangered species, including tigers, leopards, and elephants.
  2. Malampuzha Dam: Located about 25 kilometers from Shornur, Malampuzha Dam is a popular picnic spot known for its beautiful garden and amusement park. You can also take a boat ride on the reservoir to enjoy the scenic views.
  3. Nelliampathy Hills: Located about 80 kilometers from Shornur, Nelliampathy Hills is a popular hill station known for its cool weather and stunning views. You can take a scenic drive up the mountain and enjoy the lush greenery and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shornur

Shornur offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes to explore.

  1. Bharathapuzha River Trail: A scenic route that follows the meandering path of the Bharathapuzha River. The trail offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding countryside.
  2. Silent Valley Trek: A challenging trek that takes you through the dense forests of Silent Valley National Park. The trek offers a chance to spot the park’s many unique flora and fauna.
  3. Nelliyampathy Hills Bike Ride: A scenic bike ride that takes you through the winding roads of Nelliyampathy Hills. The ride offers stunning views of the hills and the surrounding valleys.

Best Things to Do in Shornur: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fun and exciting things to do in Shornur, here are some options.

  1. Take a heritage tour of Shornur: Explore the town’s rich history and culture with a guided tour of its many landmarks and attractions.
  2. Enjoy a boat ride on Bharathpuzha River: Relax and take in the scenic views of the river with a leisurely boat ride.
  3. Explore Silent Valley National Park: Take a guided trek through the park to explore its many hidden wonders.
  4. Experience the local cuisine: Savor the flavors of Kerala with a food tour that takes you to some of Shornur’s best restaurants and street food carts.

Food and Drink Scene of Shornur

Shornur is known for its unique and flavorful cuisine, which blends Indian and Malabari influences. Here are some dishes and local beverages to try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Appam and stew: A delicious breakfast dish made with fermented rice batter and served with vegetable stew.
  2. Kozhikodan biriyani: A flavorful biriyani made with long-grain rice and topped with fried onions and cashews.
  3. Fish curry: A spicy and tangy curry made with fresh fish and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  1. Sulaimani: A refreshing lemon tea flavored with spices like cardamom and cinnamon.
  2. Tender coconut water: A naturally sweet and refreshing drink that is perfect for hot days.
  3. Banana shake: A thick and creamy milkshake made with ripe bananas and milk.

  2. Hotel Kumaragiri: A mid-range hotel located in a quiet neighborhood, featuring spacious rooms and a friendly staff.
  3. Shornur Residency: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the town center, featuring clean rooms and basic amenities.

  2. Spices: Kerala is known for its aromatic and flavorful spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves.

Local Culture and Customs of Shornur

Shornur is a culturally rich town with a blend of Hindu, Muslim, and Christian traditions. Visitors are advised to respect the local customs and dress modestly while in public places.

Best Time to Visit Shornur

The best time to visit Shornur is between the months of October and March when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shornur

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately while visiting temples and other religious sites.
  2. Try the local cuisine and enjoy the street food.
  3. Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in public displays of affection.
  2. Consume alcohol or drugs in public places.
  3. Damage or deface the local monuments and attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shornur

While Shornur is generally a safe destination for tourists, it is recommended to take precautions like avoiding deserted areas and traveling in groups in the evening.

Health in Shornur

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and avoid consuming raw or uncooked food to prevent stomach infections. It is also recommended to carry a mosquito repellent as there is a risk of mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Shornur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shornur

The nearest airport to Shornur is the Cochin International Airport, located about 100 kilometers away. Taxis and buses are available from the airport to Shornur.

Bus Stations Near Shornur

The town has a well-connected bus station that is serviced by various private and public bus companies. Buses connect Shornur to various destinations in Kerala and neighboring states.

Other Options

The nearest railway station to Shornur is the Shornur Junction, which is one of the busiest railway stations in Kerala. Trains connect Shornur to various cities across India.

How to Get Around in Shornur

Shornur is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by renting a bicycle. Autos and taxis are also available for hire to explore the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Shornur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more destinations in Kerala, here are some nearby places to consider.

  1. Palakkad: A bustling town located about 30 kilometers from Shornur, Palakkad is known for its stunning forts, temples, and gardens.
  2. Thrissur: Located about 50 kilometers from Shornur, Thrissur is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant festivals.
  3. Coimbatore: Located just across the border in Tamil Nadu, Coimbatore is a bustling city known for its textile industry and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Shornur is a hidden gem of Kerala that offers a blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie or an adventure seeker, Shornur has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the charm of this wonderful town for yourself.
