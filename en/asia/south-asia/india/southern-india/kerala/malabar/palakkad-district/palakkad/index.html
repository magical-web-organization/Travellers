Introduction to Palakkad

Palakkad, located in the Malabar region of Kerala, is a land that is blessed with natural beauty, cultural wonders, and a rich history. Popular for its lush green landscapes, Palakkad is often referred to as the “Gateway of Kerala”. With plenty of historical monuments, natural attractions, and delicious cuisine, Palakkad is a perfect destination for a peaceful yet adventurous holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Palakkad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palakkad Fort

Built-in 1766, the Palakkad Fort is one of the most popular attractions of Palakkad. The fort was once an important stronghold of the Mysore kingdom and has a rich history.

Shoranur Junction Railway Station

Shoranur Junction Railway Station is known as the ‘Gateway to Malabar’. The station has some interesting facts about history, geography, and culture of the region.

Kalpathy Heritage Village

Kalpathy Heritage village is a beautiful village situated on the banks of Kalpathy river. With traditional houses, temples and narrow roads, the village is a perfect place to witness the ancient traditions and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Palakkad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Silent Valley National Park

The Silent Valley National Park is spread across an area of 90 sq km and is known for its biodiversity. The park is home to many endangered species, including the Lion-tailed Macaque.

Malampuzha Dam and Gardens

Malampuzha Dam and Gardens is a popular picnic spot among locals and tourists alike. The dam offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and has beautiful gardens, a children’s park, and a toy train.

Nelliyampathy Hill Station

Nelliyampathy Hill Station is a scenic hill station located 52 km away from Palakkad. The hill station is known for its beautiful tea plantations and is a perfect destination for hiking and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palakkad

Dhoni Hills

Dhoni Hills is situated 15 km from Palakkad, and is a popular trekking spot. With a height of 4000 ft, the hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Siruvani Waterfalls

Siruvani Waterfalls is a beautiful waterfall located on the outskirts of Palakkad. The uphill trek to the waterfall is quite challenging but worth it for the beautiful views.

Best Things to Do in Palakkad: Activities and Tours

Attending Festivals

Palakkad is known for its colorful festivals, including Poorams, which is an annual festival held in most of the temples in and around Palakkad from February to May.

Exploring Palakkad on a Cycle

Renting a cycle is an excellent way to explore the nooks and corners of Palakkad. Rental agencies like Cycle Hoppa offer cycles of various sizes and types.

Food and Drink Scene of Palakkad

Must-Try Dishes

Kerala cuisine is known for its use of spices, coconut, and fresh seafood. Some of the must-try dishes in Palakkad include:

  • Palakkad Pachadi
  • Ela Sadya
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Beef Fry

Local Beverages

Coconut water and toddy are popular beverages of Palakkad. Toddy is a fermented drink made from the sap of the coconut palm, and it is commonly enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

Where to Stay in Palakkad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palakkad has numerous options for accommodation, from luxurious resorts to budget hostels. Some popular options include:

  • Kairali - The Ayurvedic Healing Village
  • Hotel Fort Palace
  • Srichackra International

What to Buy in Palakkad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palakkad is known for its traditional handicrafts, such as brass lamps, wood-carvings, and banana fiber mats. Other popular items to buy include:

  • Spices
  • Tea
  • Local snacks and sweets

Local Culture and Customs of Palakkad

The people of Palakkad are known for their love for arts and culture. The region is known for its unique music form called “Sopanam Sangeetham” and classical dance form “Kathakali”. The region also follows unique customs and traditions that are worth experiencing and exploring.

Best Time to Visit Palakkad

The best time to visit Palakkad is between November and March. The weather is pleasant during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palakkad

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bargain while shopping
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of locals

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting temples
  • Litter in public places
  • Enter places of worship with shoes on

Safety and Health

Safety in Palakkad

Palakkad is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is advisable to take precautions and avoid visiting isolated areas after dark.

Health in Palakkad

Palakkad has good medical facilities, with several hospitals and clinics. It is advisable to carry necessary medication and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Palakkad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Palakkad

The nearest airport to Palakkad is Coimbatore International Airport, which is located 57 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Palakkad

Palakkad has a well-connected bus network, and numerous buses run to cities like Coimbatore, Thrissur, and Kochi from Palakkad Bus Station.

Other Options

One can also reach Palakkad via a train as Palakkad Junction Railway Station is well connected to major cities of the country.

How to Get Around in Palakkad

Palakkad has a well-developed transport system, with buses, taxis and auto-rickshaws being the most popular modes of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Palakkad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coimbatore

Coimbatore, known as the “Manchester of South India,” is just an hour away from Palakkad. The city is known for its textile industry, natural attractions, and commercial centers.

Thrissur

Thrissur is known as the “cultural capital of Kerala” and is located just a few hours away from Palakkad. The city is famous for its ancient temples, colorful festivals, and rich history.

Conclusion

Palakkad, with its unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty, is a fascinating destination for travelers. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing holiday or an adventurous one, Palakkad has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Palakkad with ease and make the most of your time in this beautiful region of India.
