Introduction to Mattanur

Mattanur is a small town located in the Kannur District of Kerala, in Southern India. It has a rich cultural heritage and is surrounded by lush green landscapes. Mattanur is also known for its beautiful temples, historic landmarks, and natural beauty.

The town has a vibrant community that celebrates a range of festivals throughout the year, with colorful processions, music, dance, and feasts. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Mattanur has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mattanur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mattanur is known for its rich cultural heritage and historic landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Mattannur Sree Mahadeva Temple

Mattannur Sree Mahadeva Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, and is one of the most popular attractions in Mattanur. The temple is built in the traditional Kerala style of architecture and attracts devotees from all over India.

Vaidyaratnam Ayurveda Museum

The Vaidyaratnam Ayurveda Museum is dedicated to the ancient system of Indian Medicine, Ayurveda. It showcases the history, theory, and practice of this holistic system of medicine. The museum has a vast collection of rare Ayurvedic books, instruments, and artifacts.

Kannur Fort

Kannur Fort is a historical landmark, built by the Portuguese in the 16th century. The fort has witnessed many battles and is known for its impressive architecture, including a moat and bastions.

Natural Attractions of Mattanur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mattanur is surrounded by lush green landscapes with numerous scenic views and outdoor activities to explore. Here are some of the best natural attractions:

Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary

Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area that sprawls over the western slopes of the Western Ghats. It is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and leopards. The sanctuary also features a range of trekking routes and camping options.

Ezhimala Hill Station

Ezhimala Hill Station is located near Mattanur and is known for its unique geological features and panoramic views. The hill station is home to a variety of flora and fauna, and is a popular spot for hiking and trekking.

Kavvayi Backwaters

Kavvayi Backwaters is a serene and peaceful spot located near Mattanur, known for its scenic views and traditional houseboat rides. The backwaters are dotted with small islands, forests, and temples, and are a popular spot for birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mattanur

Mattanur is surrounded by beautiful landscapes that offer ample opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

Banasura Hill Trek

Banasura Hill Trek is a challenging route that takes visitors through rolling hills, valleys, and forests. It offers some of the most breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.

Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary Trek

Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary Trek is an adventurous route that offers a glimpse of the unique biodiversity of the Western Ghats. The trek takes visitors through forests, streams, and grasslands, and is home to a range of endemic flora and fauna.

Mattanur-Kallar Trail

Mattanur-Kallar Trail is a scenic and relatively easy route that offers stunning views of Ezhimala Hill Station and the surrounding areas. The trail is perfect for cycling or a leisurely walk.

Best Things to Do in Mattanur: Activities and Tours

Mattanur offers tourists a range of activities and tours to experience its unique culture and outdoor beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Mattanur:

Ayurvedic Spa Treatment

Mattanur is known for its traditional Ayurvedic medicine practices. Tourists can enjoy a relaxing and rejuvenating Ayurvedic spa treatment, which is famous for its natural remedies and relaxation techniques.

Traditional Dance Performances

Mattanur is known for its vibrant and colorful festivals throughout the year, and tourists can witness traditional dance performances and live music shows.

Houseboat Cruise

Tourists can also enjoy a relaxing houseboat cruise on the Kavvayi Backwaters, enjoying the scenic views and peaceful atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mattanur

Here’s a week-long itinerary on how to explore the best of Mattanur:

Day 1: Visit the Mattannur Sree Mahadeva Temple and experience the traditional rituals and culture of Kerala.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Vaidyaratnam Ayurvedic Museum and witness the ancient practices and history of Ayurvedic medicine.

Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of the Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary and experience the unique biodiversity of the Western Ghats.

Day 4: Enjoy a day trip to the historic Kannur Fort and soak in the impressive architecture and scenic views.

Day 5: Go on a hike or bike ride around the Ezhimala Hill Station and experience the panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

Day 6: Take a relaxing houseboat cruise on the Kavvayi Backwaters and experience the traditional Kerala way of life.

Day 7: Experience the local cuisine and culture of Mattanur by trying out traditional dishes and visiting local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Mattanur

Kerala is famous for its spicy and flavorful cuisine, and Mattanur is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Puttu and Kadala Curry

Puttu and Kadala Curry is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala, made with steamed rice flour, grated coconut, and spicy chickpea curry.

Malabar Biryani

Malabar Biryani is a spicy and aromatic rice dish, cooked with a variety of meats and spices.

Appam and Stew

Appam and Stew is a popular lunch dish in Kerala, made with rice flour pancakes and a coconut milk-based stew.

Local Beverages

Tender Coconut Water

Tender Coconut water is a refreshing and healthy drink, served in fresh coconut shells.

Chai

Chai is a popular tea beverage served all over India, made with aromatic spices like cardamom, ginger, and cinnamon.

Toddy

Toddy is a local alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut sap and is a must-try for visitors looking to sample some local drinks.

Where to Stay in Mattanur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mattanur has a range of accommodation options for every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Kayaloram Heritage Lake Resort

Kayaloram Heritage Lake Resort is a luxury resort located on the banks of the Kavvayi Backwaters, offering stunning views and all the modern amenities.

Kannur Beach House

Kannur Beach House is a budget-friendly guesthouse located near the Kannur Beach and offers clean and comfortable rooms.

Abbirami Tourist Home

Abbirami Tourist Home is a mid-range hotel located in the heart of Mattanur, with easy access to all the major attractions and amenities.

What to Buy in Mattanur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs to buy in Mattanur:

Traditional Handicrafts

Mattanur is famous for its traditional handicrafts, including coir products, woodcarvings, and handwoven fabrics.

Spices and Tea

Kerala is known for its aromatic spices and tea and is a must-buy for food enthusiasts.

Ayurvedic Products

Mattanur is known for its Ayurvedic medicine practices, and visitors can buy authentic Ayurvedic medicines, oils or cosmetics from the local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Mattanur

Mattanur has a rich cultural heritage and traditions, and it’s essential to respect local customs and etiquette. Here are some tips:

  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or a house
  • Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of people
  • Avoid public displays of affection

Best Time to Visit Mattanur

The best time to visit Mattanur is during the winter season (December to February), when the weather is pleasant and temperate. The monsoon season (June to September) offers lush green landscapes, but the heavy rainfall can make it difficult to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mattanur

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and explore local markets
  • Dress modestly

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings
  • Vulgar Language

Safety and Health

Safety in Mattanur

Mattanur is a safe place to visit, but it’s always advisable to take certain safety precautions, such as:

  • Travel in a group, especially during the night time
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place and avoid carrying large amounts of cash
  • Follow the local laws and regulations

Health in Mattanur

Kerala is known for its Ayurvedic medicine practices, and visitors can avail themselves of its treatments. But it’s always advisable to carry a first aid kit with you, drink bottled water and stay hydrated.

Getting to Mattanur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mattanur is easily accessible by road, rail, and air. Here are some means:

Airports Near Mattanur

The nearest airport to Mattanur is the Kannur International Airport, which is around 25 kilometers away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Mattanur

Mattanur has a well-connected bus network with daily services to Kannur, major cities in Kerala, and surrounding towns.

Other Options

Visitors can opt for a taxi or rented car to explore the town and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Mattanur

Getting around Mattanur is relatively easy with its well-connected roads, rickshaws, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Mattanur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mattanur is located near several popular tourist destinations and makes a great base for further exploration. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

Kannur

Kannur is a coastal city and a major tourist attraction, known for its historic landmarks and beach vistas.

Wayanad

Wayanad is another nearby hill station, known for its scenic beauty, rolling hills, and tea plantations.

Coorg

Coorg, often called the Scotland of India, is a scenic and idyllic hill station known for its tea and coffee plantations.

Conclusion

Mattanur is a hidden gem that offers a glimpse into the culture and landscape of Kerala, With its ancient temples, Ayurvedic medicine practices, outdoor adventures, and scenic beauty, it’s the perfect destination for those looking for a unique cultural experience.
