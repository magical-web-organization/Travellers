Mahe is a small town in Northern Kerala located in the Kannur district, known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning views, and outdoor adventures. This town is steeped in history, and as you explore its cultural attractions and scenic surroundings, you’ll be transported back in time. In this travel guide, we’ll be covering everything you need to know about Mahe, from the top places to visit and things to do to the best places to stay and what to eat.

Introduction to Mahe

Mahe is a bustling town steeped in history and tradition. Nestled between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, this small town boasts a blend of French and Indian influences, evident in its unique architecture and rich cultural heritage. Mahe was once a French colony and is now part of India. The town is known for its stunning views, scrumptious food, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Mahe

Mahe is a small but culturally rich town, with various places of interest to explore. Below are some of the top places to visit in Mahe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mahe De Karinthalam: This is a historical site with a small museum that showcases Mahe’s interesting history.
  • French War Memorial: This is a landmark that commemorates the soldiers who lost their lives in the battle between the French and the British forces in 1721.
  • Mahe River: Take a leisurely walk along the river or hop on a boat ride and admire the town’s scenic views.
  • St. Theresa’s Church: This church has been around for more than 200 years and is an outstanding example of French colonial architecture.

Natural Attractions of Mahe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valapattanam River: This scenic river offers breathtaking views and a host of water activities such as kayaking and rafting.
  • Jawahar Bal Bhavan Park: This park offers a wide range of activities for families, including a play area for children, a mini zoo, and boating.
  • Mahe Beach: Known for its calm waters and golden sands, this beach is an excellent spot for sunbathing, swimming, and beach activities.
  • Dharmadam Island: This island offers stunning views and is a great place for picnics, beach walks and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mahe

Mahe is surrounded by lush greenery and is an excellent destination for hiking and bike rides. A few places worth exploring include:

  • Hiking the Western Ghats: You can enjoy scenic views of the Indian Ocean and explore the lush greenery of the Western Ghats.
  • Bike Ride to Peralassery: A bike ride to Peralassery will take you through beautiful countryside roads, through the woods and along the backwaters.

Best Things to Do in Mahe: Activities and Tours

Mahe town is bustling with many activities and tours that visitors can take. Here are a few:

  • Fort Mahe Walk: Join a guided walking tour of Fort Mahe, which was built by the French in the late 18th century, and listen to stories about its rich history.
  • Spice Plantation Tour: Visit a nearby spice plantation and learn more about the spices that grow in this region and how they’re used in cooking.
  • Cooking class: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare some of the local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mahe

A week in Mahe is enough to explore the town and its surroundings. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Mahe:

Days Activities
Day 1 Explore the town, visit the French War Memorial, and learn more about Mahe’s rich history.
Day 2 Visit the Dharmadam Island for some beach activities, watersports, and relax in the sun.
Day 3 Take a spice plantation tour and cooking class, learn about the spices and cooking methods used.
Day 4 Hike or bike ride to Peralassery and explore the beautiful countryside.
Day 5 Take a guided walking tour of Fort Mahe
Day 6 Visit the Valapattanam River and experience water activities like kayaking and rafting.
Day 7 Relax at Mahe Beach and soak up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Mahe

Mahe is known for its delicious food, which boasts a blend of Indian and French influences. The seafood here is fresh and locally caught, and the spices are mouthwatering. Here are a few dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Curry: made with fresh seafood from the Arabian Sea and a variety of spices
  • Kozhikkoden Biryani: A unique form of biryani made with short-grain rice cooked with chicken or mutton and spices
  • Sulaimani Chai: A sweet and tangy tea made with sweeteners like sugar or honey and flavoured with cardamom.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: A local alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut water
  • Sharbat: A refreshing drink made with rose syrup and water
  • Lemon Tea: Tea flavoured with freshly squeezed lemon juice and is widely consumed across Kerala.

Where to Stay in Mahe: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

Mahe has various accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to more upscale hotels. Below are a few options for you to consider:

  • Raviz Beach Resort: This beach resort offers plush accommodations and stunning sea views.
  • Mahe Beach Resort: This resort has comfortable rooms, beach access, and a lovely swimming pool.
  • Pozhiyoram Beach Resort: Provides comfortable accommodations at an affordable price and is located next to the beach.

What to Buy in Mahe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mahe is known for its handicrafts, traditional jewellery, spices, and other souvenirs that make excellent gifts or keepsakes. Some popular items to purchase here include:

  • Traditional Jewellry: Mahe is known for its beautiful traditional jewellery made of brass and copper that make excellent souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: Kerala has a rich tradition of handicrafts, and you’ll find many beautiful items made of wood, coir, and textiles in Mahe’s markets.
  • Spices: Kerala is known for its spices, and you can find a range of carefully sourced and packaged spices to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mahe

Mahe’s culture is unique, with a blend of Indian and French influences. The people here are warm and hospitable, and visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions. Respecting local dress codes, such as covering knees and shoulders while visiting religious sites, is crucial.

Best Time to Visit Mahe

The best time to visit Mahe is from November to February when the weather is pleasant, and it’s not too hot. The monsoon season is from June to September, which may affect outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mahe

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local food
  • Carry adequate cash as some places may not have card payment facilities.

Don’t:

  • Wear offensive clothing
  • Restrict your exploration - Mahe has many hidden gems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mahe

Mahe is a relatively safe town for travellers. However, it’s essential to take care of your belongings, especially in crowded markets and tourist spots.

Health in Mahe

Mahe has many hospitals and medical facilities that provide excellent care. It’s advisable to carry adequate health insurance before visiting Mahe and carry essential medication for any pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Mahe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mahe can be accessed by several modes of transportation:

Airports Near Mahe

  • Calicut International Airport is the nearest airport, approximately 110 km away.
  • Kannur International Airport, located approximately 21 km away, is another option for visitors.

Bus Stations Near Mahe

  • Mahe Bus Station is well connected with nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Taxis, car rentals, and private cars are also available for travel within the city.

How to Get Around in Mahe

The best way to explore Mahe is on foot, as the town is small and walkable. Alternatively, bicycles and motorbikes can be rented from local establishments. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Mahe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mahe is located in Kannur District, and there are many places to visit nearby, including:

  • Tellicherry: An old Dutch port with a rich history and fabulous beaches.
  • Thalassery: Famous for its 150-year-old Thalassery Fort and beautiful beaches.
  • Kannur: A commercial center famous for its serene beaches and backwaters.

Conclusion

Mahe, with its rich cultural heritage, scenic views, stunning beaches, and delicious food, is a fantastic destination for those looking to experience Kerala’s unique charm. Use this guide to explore this beautiful town and enjoy its many attractions.
