Introduction to Payyanur

Payyanur is a beautiful town located in the northern district of Kannur, in the Malabar region of Kerala. It is known for its beautiful beaches, historical importance, and natural beauty. The town is surrounded by hills, backwaters, and lush green forests. Payyanur is a perfect destination for those who want to explore the rich history of South India, experience its natural beauty, and indulge in its delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Payyanur

Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Payyanur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Payyanur Pavithreswaram Temple - This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is famous for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.
  2. Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary - This sanctuary is home to a variety of flora and fauna and offers breathtaking views of nature.
  3. Kavvayi Backwaters - A beautiful and serene lagoon system that is perfect for a peaceful getaway.
  4. Parassinikkadavu Snake Park- A unique park housing different species of snakes, including some rare ones.
  5. Madayipara - A large plain of undulating hills with unique vegetation and rare medicinal plants.
  6. Odathil Mosque - An ancient mosque with beautiful architecture and rich history.

Natural Attractions of Payyanur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ezhimala Hill - A beautiful hill in the Arabian Sea with stunning views and a lighthouse at the top.
  2. Payamkutti Beach – Located 16 km from Payyanur, the beach offers stunning views of the sunrise and sunset.
  3. Paithalmala - A beautiful hill station with breathtaking views, trekking trails, and cascading waterfalls.
  4. Palakkayam Thattu - A beautiful hill station known for its scenic views, trekking trails, and a stunning treehouse.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Payyanur

Payyanur offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in India. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  1. Ezhimala Hill Trek - This trek offers stunning views of the Arabian sea and the surrounding hills.
  2. Palakkayam Thattu Trek - This trek takes you through some of the most beautiful trails in the region and ends with a stunning view of the hill station.
  3. Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary Trek - This trek takes you through the serene forest and offers a glimpse of the wildlife.
  4. Madayipara Trek - This trek takes you through the large plain of hills with unique vegetation and rare medicinal plants.

Best Things to Do in Payyanur: Activities and Tours

Payyanur offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s needs. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Payyanur:

  1. Explore the Backwaters - A peaceful boat ride through the Kavvayi backwaters.
  2. Ayurvedic Spa Treatment - Experience authentic Ayurvedic treatment in one of the many spas in Payyanur.
  3. Tasting Local Cuisine- Indulge in the local cuisine such as fish curry, appam, and kappa at the many eateries in Payyanur.
  4. Attend the Annual Theyyam Festival - A fascinating annual cultural festival that takes place in Payyanur.
  5. Visit Various Temples - Explore the ancient temples in Payyannur, each with its unique architecture and rituals.

Food and Drink Scene of Payyanur

Payyanur has a rich culinary tradition, famous for its seafood cuisine. Here are some of the popular dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish Curry - Fish curry is a popular dish in Payyanur, made with fresh fish in coconut milk and spicy masala.
  2. Tapioca - Tapioca is a popular dish in Payyanur, served with spicy coconut chutney.
  3. Appam and Chicken Stew – A popular breakfast dish, a rice pancake with a mild chicken stew.
  4. Malabar Biriyani – A traditional spicy rice dish that comes with either chicken, mutton or fish.

Local Beverages

  1. Sukku Malli - A traditional Ayurvedic drink made with ginger & other spices with medicinal values.
  2. Chukku Kapi - A traditional coffee beverage made with spices and brown sugar.

What to Buy in Payyanur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Payyanur is known for its intricate handicrafts, coconut products, and other interesting items. Here are some of the most popular souvenirs and products to bring back:

  1. Coconut Products - Payyanur is famous for its coir and coconut shell products.
  2. Handicrafts & Weaving Products- You can find handwoven baskets, hats, and mats in the local markets in Payyanur.
  3. Spices - Payyanur has a rich history of spice trade, and you can find some of the best spices such as pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.

Local Culture and Customs of Payyanur

Payyanur has a rich cultural heritage that revolves around temple festivals, traditional dance, and martial arts. The Theyyam festival is the most famous cultural event held in Payyanur, and it is celebrated from December to April. The local people are warm and welcoming, and the culture and traditions are deeply rooted in their everyday life.

Best Time to Visit Payyanur

The best time to visit Payyanur is from November to February when the weather is pleasant, and the humidity is low. The monsoon season in Kerala can last from June to September and can cause landslides, floods, and other travel disruptions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Payyanur

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Payyanur:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine and attend cultural events.
  3. Keep yourself hydrated as it is hot and humid throughout the year.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect religious sentiments.
  2. Wear revealing clothes while visiting temples or other places of worship.
  3. Pluck flowers or touch artifacts at the temples and museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Payyanur

Payyanur is a safe place to travel, but it is always best to take precautions when traveling alone or at night. It is recommended to carry some form of Identification, especially for international travelers. Payyanur is known for its rich culture and tradition, and women travelers must always respect the local culture and customs.

Health in Payyanur

Water-borne diseases are common in Payyanur, and it is recommended to drink bottled water or boil water before drinking. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria are prevalent, and it is recommended to carry mosquito repellent and medicinal kit.

Getting to Payyanur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Payyanur

The closest airport to Payyanur is Kannur International Airport, which is 40 km away. You can hire a cab or take a bus from the airport to reach Payyanur.

Bus Stations Near Payyanur

Payyanur has a well-developed bus network that connects it to nearby cities such as Kannur and Mangalore. Private taxis are also available for hire.

Other Options

Hiring a private cab or self-driving is a popular option for tourists who want to explore Payyanur and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Payyanur

Payyanur is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot or by renting a bicycle or scooter. Auto-rickshaws and private taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Payyanur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Payyanur is surrounded by several beautiful destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  1. Bekal - A beautiful and historical beach town located 80 km from Payyanur.
  2. Kannur- A historical city, famous for its beaches and culture, located 30 km from Payyanur.
  3. Wayanad - A beautiful hill station and wildlife sanctuary located 120 km from Payyanur.
  4. Coorg - A stunning hill station known for its coffee plantations, located 100 km from Payyanur.

Conclusion

Payyanur is a hidden gem that has something to offer every traveler, be it history, culture, nature, or food. The town’s rich heritage and natural beauty make it a perfect destination for anyone looking to explore Kerala’s history, tradition and nature. We hope this Payyanur travel guide will help you plan your next trip to this beautiful town, and experience the best of what it has to offer.
