Ultimate Travel Guide to Kurumathur, Kerala

Kurumathur, situated in the Kannur district of Malabar region in Kerala, is often referred to as a hidden gem. This quaint village offers something for everyone, from its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, hiking and biking trails, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality of its friendly locals. In this article, we’ll provide a comprehensive travel guide to help you explore the best of Kurumathur.

List of Places to Visit in Kurumathur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Anjarakandy Biological Park - This park is home to many species of animals and birds, including tigers, leopards, deer, and peacocks.
  2. Arakkal Museum - This former palace of the Arakkal Dynasty has been converted into a museum that showcases the rich heritage of the area.
  3. Kottiyoor Mahadeva Temple - This ancient Hindu temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and attracts devotees from all over the country.
  4. St. Angelo Fort - Built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, this well-preserved fort offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding landscape.
  5. Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach - This is India’s longest drive-in beach and it’s a popular spot for sunset walks, picnics, and swimming.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Paithalmala - This hill station is known for its lush green landscape and breathtaking views. It’s a popular spot for hiking and camping.
  2. Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary - This sanctuary is home to many endangered species such as the Malabar civet, Sambar deer, and sloth bear.
  3. Valapattanam River - This river offers picturesque views and is popular for boating and fishing.
  4. Muzhappilangad Beach - This picturesque beach is perfect for sunbathing, surfing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurumathur

  1. Paithalmala Trek - This trek takes you through the thick forests and grasslands of Paithalmala, and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  2. Aralam Wildlife Trek - This trek allows you to explore the wildlife sanctuary, spot rare birds and animals, and enjoy the wilderness.
  3. Muzhappilangad Beach Cycling - This cycling route takes you through the scenic coastal roads of the Arabian Sea, coconut groves, and quaint fishing villages.

Best Things to Do in Kurumathur: Activities and Tours

  1. Traditional Ayurvedic Spa - Unwind and rejuvenate your body with Kerala’s traditional ayurvedic spa treatments.
  2. Backwater Cruises - Take a leisurely ride on a houseboat and explore the tranquil backwaters of Kerala.
  3. Kalaripayattu Performance - Watch a traditional martial arts performance that originated in Kerala.

Food and Drink Scene of Kurumathur

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Malabar Parotta - A popular bread served with rich curries, is a must-try dish.
  2. Beef Fry - A spicy and juicy meat dish with tender pieces of beef.
  3. Fish Curry - Kerala’s famous spicy fish curry served with steamed rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Toddy - A local alcoholic beverage made from fermented coconut water.
  2. Nannari - A refreshing drink made from the root of the Nannari plant.

Where to Stay in Kurumathur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Kurumathur Homestays - Experience the warm hospitality of the locals by staying with a family in a homestay.
  2. Malabar Residency - This hotel offers comfortable rooms and is centrally located.
  3. Green Gates Hotel - A luxurious hotel that is located close to many of the major tourist attractions.

What to Buy in Kurumathur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handloom Sarees - Buy traditional handloom sarees made by the local artisans.
  2. Spices and Herbs - Take home a pack of freshly-ground Malabar spices and herbs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kurumathur

  1. Dress Code - Kerala is a conservative state, dressing modestly is advisable.
  2. Ettikettu Festival - A colorful annual festival celebrated in honor of the goddess Kali.

Best Time to Visit Kurumathur

The best time to visit Kurumathur is from October to February when the weather is pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurumathur

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine, including the famous Malabar Biriyani.
  3. Take off your shoes before entering a temple or someone’s home.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter in public places.
  2. Do not smoke or consume alcohol in public places.
  3. Do not disturb the wildlife and their habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kurumathur

Kurumathur is a safe destination for travelers, but as with any place, it’s always advisable to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kurumathur

It’s important to stay hydrated and protected from the sun, especially during the summer months. Mosquitoes are also a common problem in Kerala, so make sure to carry mosquito repellents.

Getting to Kurumathur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kurumathur

The nearest airports are Kannur International Airport and Calicut International Airport, both around 60-70 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kurumathur

Kurumathur is well-connected by bus services to major cities and towns in Kerala.

Other Options

Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and rented cars are also available for transportation within and around Kurumathur.

How to Get Around in Kurumathur

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are the easiest way to get around Kurumathur. Buses and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Kurumathur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kannur - A vibrant coastal town known for its beaches, historic monuments, and vibrant culture.
  2. Wayanad - A serene hill station known for its natural beauty, wildlife, and tribal settlements.
  3. Coorg - A popular destination in neighboring Karnataka, known for its picturesque landscape, coffee plantations, and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

Kurumathur offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, adventure, and relaxation. The warm hospitality of the locals, delicious cuisine, and stunning landscapes make Kurumathur an ideal destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. Follow this ultimate travel guide to Kurumathur, Kerala, and make the most of your visit to this hidden gem.
