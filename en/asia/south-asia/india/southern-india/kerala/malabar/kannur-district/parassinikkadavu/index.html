Introduction to Parassinikkadavu

Nestled in the Kannur district of Malabar, Kerala, Parassinikkadavu is a beautiful destination that boasts of rich heritage and natural beauty. The district is famous for its stunning beaches, serene backwaters, and lush greenery. The district is also known for its religious sites and cultural attractions, which attract visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Parassinikkadavu

Parassinikkadavu has a wide array of landmarks to explore, from cultural attractions to natural treasures. Listed below are the places to visit while in Parassinikkadavu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Parassinikkadavu Snake Park: A park dedicated to reptiles, the snake park is home to various snake species, including the King Cobra.

  2. Muthappan Temple: A 5th-century temple that is dedicated to the deity Muthappan. The temple is known for its unique rituals, including the Theyyam dance.

  3. Ezhimala: A historical site that dates back to the ancient period, Ezhimala is known for the Chovva and Kizhunna temples, which are built on hills.

Natural Attractions of Parassinikkadavu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Payyambalam Beach: A serene beach that is perfect for long walks, Payyambalam Beach is known for its golden sand and clear waters.

  2. Dharmadam Island: A beautiful island that is accessible only by boat, Dharmadam Island is covered in coconut palms and is surrounded by pristine waters.

  3. Madayipara: A hillock that is known for its biodiversity and stunning views, Madayipara is an excellent trekking destination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parassinikkadavu

Parassinikkadavu is known for its scenic hiking and biking routes. Some popular routes include:

  1. Paithalmala: A hill station that is known for its breathtaking views of the valley, Paithalmala is a perfect hiking destination.

  2. Kanjirakolly: An off-road biking trail that is known for its stunning views of the forest.

Best Things to Do in Parassinikkadavu: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a boat tour of the serene backwaters of Parassinikkadavu, and soak in the scenic views.

  2. Sample the local cuisine by exploring the food scene, which is known for its seafood, especially fish curry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Parassinikkadavu

Day 1: Visit the Muthappan Temple

Day 2: Explore the Parassinikkadavu Snake Park, followed by a boat tour of the backwaters.

Day 3: Hike to Paithalmala and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 4: Visit Ezhimala and the nearby temples.

Day 5: Sample the local cuisine and explore the food scene.

Day 6: Take a bike tour of Kanjirakolly.

Day 7: Relax at Payyambalam Beach and soak in its serene beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Parassinikkadavu

Parassinikkadavu is a melting pot of different traditions and cultures. The locals are known for their hospitality and warmth. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and interact with the locals to get a glimpse into their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Parassinikkadavu

The best time to visit Parassinikkadavu is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parassinikkadavu

Do:

  1. Be respectful towards the local culture and traditions.

  2. Try the local cuisine and sample the local beverages.

  3. Interact with the locals to get a glimpse into their way of life.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places.

  2. Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

  3. Wear shorts or skimpy clothing while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parassinikkadavu

Parassinikkadavu is generally safe for visitors. However, one should take the following precautions:

  1. Avoid visiting isolated areas at night.

  2. Keep valuables in a safe place.

  3. Observe caution while swimming in the sea.

Health in Parassinikkadavu

Visitors should take precautions against waterborne diseases, such as cholera and typhoid. Drink only bottled water, and avoid raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Parassinikkadavu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parassinikkadavu

The nearest airports to Parassinikkadavu are the Kannur International Airport and the Calicut International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Parassinikkadavu

Parassinikkadavu has a well-connected bus network with frequent services to nearby destinations. The nearest bus station is the Kannur bus station.

Other Options

Guests may also use trains or taxis for transportation to and from Parassinikkadavu.

How to Get Around in Parassinikkadavu

Taxis, buses, and auto-rickshaws are popular modes of transportation in Parassinikkadavu. Visitors may also rent motorbikes and bicycles for exploring the district at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Parassinikkadavu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bekal Fort

An 18th-century fort that is known for its stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

Thalassery Fort

A historical site that was built by the British East India Company in the 18th century.

Conclusion

Parassinikkadavu is a beautiful district that is known for its cultural attractions, natural beauty, and lip-smacking cuisine. With this guide’s help, visitors can explore the district and have an unforgettable experience filled with adventure and beauty.
