Introduction to Kunnamangalam

Kunnamangalam is a small town located in the Kozhikode district of Malabar in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Known for its scenic beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, Kunnamangalam attracts a steady stream of tourists throughout the year. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, foodie, or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Kunnamangalam has something in store for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kunnamangalam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pazhassi Raja Museum and Art Gallery

Located in East Hill, Pazhassi Raja Museum and Art Gallery is a popular tourist attraction in Kunnamangalam. This museum and art gallery is dedicated to the memory of the great warrior king Pazhassi Raja, who fought against the British East India Company in the 18th century. The museum has a fine collection of ancient coins, sculptures, and paintings.

Tali Temple

One of the oldest temples in Kerala, Tali Temple is a must-visit cultural attraction in Kunnamangalam. The temple complex houses several shrines dedicated to different deities, including Lord Shiva, Lord Vishnu, and Lord Ganesha. The architecture of the temple is a mix of Dravidian and Kerala styles.

Natural Attractions of Kunnamangalam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kakkayam Dam and Reservoir

Located about 40 km from Kunnamangalam, Kakkayam Dam and Reservoir is a scenic spot offering breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests. The dam and reservoir are a popular spot for boating, fishing, and birdwatching.

Peruvannamuzhi Dam and Reservoir

Another popular spot for nature lovers, Peruvannamuzhi Dam and Reservoir is located about 25 km from Kunnamangalam. The dam and reservoir offer spectacular views of the surrounding hills and lush green forests. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and picnicking at the site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kunnamangalam

Chaliyar River Trail

The Chaliyar River Trail is a popular hiking and biking route in Kunnamangalam. The trail runs along the picturesque Chaliyar river and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests. The trail is relatively easy and suitable for beginners.

Kakkayam Forest Trail

The Kakkayam Forest Trail is a challenging hiking and biking route that runs through the lush green forests surrounding the Kakkayam Dam and Reservoir. The trail offers stunning views of the reservoir and the surrounding hills. Due to the challenging terrain, the trail is recommended only for experienced hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Kunnamangalam: Activities and Tours

Boating and Fishing at Kakkayam and Peruvannamuzhi Dams

Boating and fishing are popular activities at the Kakkayam and Peruvannamuzhi Dams and Reservoirs. Visitors can rent boats and fishing gear from local vendors and spend a relaxing day on the water.

Ayurvedic Massage and Spa Treatments

Kunnamangalam is home to several traditional Ayurvedic massage and spa centers that offer a range of rejuvenating treatments. Visitors can indulge in massages, facials, and other treatments using natural herbs and oils.

Food and Drink Scene of Kunnamangalam

Must-Try Dishes

Kunnamangalam is famous for its traditional Kerala cuisine, which is a mix of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in Kunnamangalam include:

  • Meen Curry (Fish Curry)
  • Avial (Mixed Vegetables in Coconut Curry)
  • Malabar Paratha (Layered Flatbread)
  • Appam (Rice Pancake)
  • Payasam (Sweet Pudding)

Local Beverages

Kunnamangalam is famous for its fresh coconut water, which is available at most roadside stalls. Visitors can also enjoy traditional Kerala coffee and tea at local cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Kunnamangalam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in and around Kunnamangalam that cater to visitors of all budgets. Some of the popular options include:

  • The Raviz Kadavu
  • Park Residency
  • Copper Folia Resort

What to Buy in Kunnamangalam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kunnamangalam is famous for its traditional handicrafts and handloom products, which make for great souvenirs. Visitors can shop for:

  • Kasavu Sarees (Traditional Kerala Sarees)
  • Coconut Shell Products
  • Handmade Pottery
  • Wooden Handicrafts

Local Culture and Customs of Kunnamangalam

Kunnamangalam is a predominantly Hindu town, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. Alcohol and non-vegetarian food are not served in most public places, including beaches and parks.

Best Time to Visit Kunnamangalam

The best time to visit Kunnamangalam is between September and March, when the weather is pleasant and dry. The monsoon season (June to August) should be avoided, as heavy rains can disrupt travel plans and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kunnamangalam

Do:

  • Carry appropriate clothing and sunscreen
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Carry cash, as many places do not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing
  • Drink or eat non-vegetarian food in public places
  • Disrespect religious or cultural symbols
  • Litter or damage natural or cultural attractions

Safety and Health

Safety in Kunnamangalam

Kunnamangalam is a relatively safe town, although visitors are advised to be cautious when traveling alone at night or in secluded areas. Petty crimes like theft and pickpocketing can occur, so it’s important to keep valuables safe.

Health in Kunnamangalam

Visitors are advised to carry mosquito repellent and other necessary medicines, as mosquitoes and other insects are common in the region. It’s also important to stay hydrated and carry a first-aid kit while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Kunnamangalam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kunnamangalam is well-connected to major cities in Kerala and other parts of India via road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Kunnamangalam

The nearest airport to Kunnamangalam is Calicut International Airport, which is located about 25 km away. Taxis and buses are readily available from the airport to Kunnamangalam.

Bus Stations Near Kunnamangalam

Kunnamangalam is well-connected to nearby cities and towns by bus services operated by the Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) and private operators. Buses travel to nearby destinations like Kozhikode, Malappuram, and Wayanad.

Other Options

Taxis and auto-rickshaws are also readily available for travel within Kunnamangalam and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Kunnamangalam

Visitors can explore Kunnamangalam and nearby areas by taxi, auto-rickshaw, or rented bike/car. Buses are also available for travel within the town.

Where to Go Next from Kunnamangalam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kozhikode

Known as the “City of Spices,” Kozhikode is a bustling city located about 10 km from Kunnamangalam. The city is famous for its historic landmarks, beaches, and food.

Wayanad

Located about 80 km from Kunnamangalam, Wayanad is a scenic hill station known for its tea and spice plantations, trekking trails, and wildlife sanctuaries.

Conclusion

Kunnamangalam is a hidden gem in Kerala that has something in store for everyone. With its rich history, natural attractions, and cultural charm, Kunnamangalam is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring southern India.
