Koyilandy, located in the Kozhikode District of Malabar in Kerala, India, is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by many travelers visiting Southern India. This charming coastal town boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant local culture that will leave a lasting impression on any traveler who decides to visit. In this comprehensive travel guide to Koyilandy, we highlight the top things to see and do, where to stay, and what to eat and drink.

Introduction to Koyilandy

Koyilandy, also known as Quilandy, is a small town of just under 60,000 people located on the Malabar coast of Kerala. It has a long and storied history that dates back to the 12th century when it was a prominent center for spice trade. Today, it is one of the most charming towns in Kerala and is home to several fascinating historical sites, beautiful beaches, and an array of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Koyilandy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Koyilandy is rich in cultural history, and there are several museums and historical places you should visit during your trip. Some popular attractions include:

  • Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala: This is a renowned Ayurvedic treatment center that provides traditional Ayurvedic treatments to both locals and tourists.
  • Koyilandy Palace: This is a beautiful palace located in the heart of the town and was the residence of the erstwhile king of Koyilandy.
  • Valiya Juma Masjid: This is a historic mosque built in the 10th century and is known for its stunning architecture.

Natural Attractions of Koyilandy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Koyilandy is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are several outdoor activities that you can engage in. Here are some popular attractions:

  • Kappad Beach: This is a beautiful beach that is known for its scenic views and serene atmosphere. It is also the place where Vasco da Gama first set foot on Indian soil.
  • Thusharagiri Waterfalls: This is a stunning waterfall located in the Western Ghats and is surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Kakkayam Dam: This dam is located in the Kakkayam range of the Western Ghats and is a popular spot for trekking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Koyilandy

Koyilandy is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and is a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes:

  • Kizhakkoth Bird Sanctuary: This is an excellent place for bird watching, and you can also take a leisurely walk through the sanctuary.
  • Peruvannamoozhi Dam: This is a beautiful spot that is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular spot for picnics.
  • Kakkayam Dam: This is a great spot for hiking, and you can trek up to the reservoir to enjoy stunning views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Koyilandy: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities that you can indulge in while in Koyilandy. Here are some popular ones:

  • Ayurvedic Massage: Koyilandy is known for its traditional Ayurvedic treatments, and you can indulge in a relaxing massage during your stay.
  • Boat Rides: You can take a boat ride along the Kadalundi river and enjoy the serene surroundings.
  • Local Market Tours: You can take a tour of the local markets and try some of the delicious local street food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Koyilandy

If you’re planning to spend a week in Koyilandy, here is a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Koyilandy

Kerala is known for its delicious food, and Koyilandy is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Malabar Biriyani: This is a famous rice dish that is cooked with local spices and meat.
  • Kozhikode Halwa: This is a popular sweet made with wheat, sugar, and ghee.
  • Fish Curry: Kerala is known for its seafood, and the fish curry in Koyilandy is one of the best in the state.

Local Beverages

  • Sulaimani: This is a refreshing tea made with lemon, sugar, and spices.
  • Tender Coconut Water: This is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Koyilandy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Koyilandy offers an array of accommodation options that cater to every budget. Some popular options include:

  • Beach Heritage Inn: This is a beautiful heritage property located close to Kappad Beach.
  • Blue Mermaid Homestay: This is a cozy homestay that is perfect for budget travelers.
  • Westway Hotel: This is a modern hotel that offers all the amenities that you would expect from a luxury hotel.

What to Buy in Koyilandy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Koyilandy is known for its handicrafts and local products. Some popular items to bring home include:

  • Coconut Shell Bags: These are handmade bags that are made from coconut shells and are unique to the region.
  • Banana Fiber Mats: These mats are made from banana fibers and are known for their durability and beautiful designs.
  • Spices: Kerala is known for its spices, and you can buy a variety of spices and blends from Koyilandy.

Local Culture and Customs of Koyilandy

Koyilandy has a rich culture, and it is essential to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: Kerala is a conservative state, and it is important to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Respect Local Customs: Koyilandy has several local customs and traditions, and it is important to respect them.
  • Learn Basic Phrases: The locals in Koyilandy speak Malayalam, so learning a few basic phrases can help you communicate better.

Best Time to Visit Koyilandy

The best time to visit Koyilandy is from October to March when the weather is mild and pleasant. The monsoon season starts in June and lasts until September, so it is best to avoid visiting during these months.

Safety and Health

Getting to Koyilandy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Koyilandy

The nearest airport to Koyilandy is the Calicut International Airport, which is located 30 km away.

Bus Stations Near Koyilandy

Koyilandy has a bus station that is well-connected to several cities in Kerala.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Koyilandy, as it is located on the Calicut-Mangalore railway line.

How to Get Around in Koyilandy

Koyilandy is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, you can also hire a taxi or take a local bus to get around.

Where to Go Next from Koyilandy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations that you can visit from Koyilandy include:

  • Kozhikode: This is a bustling city located just 30 km away from Koyilandy and is known for its beautiful beaches, historical sites, and delicious food.
  • Wayanad: This is a beautiful hill station that is located around 80 km from Koyilandy and is known for its stunning landscapes and natural beauty.
  • Bekal: This is a beautiful fort located around 150 km from Koyilandy and is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Koyilandy is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. This charming coastal town in Kerala offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, delicious food, and warm hospitality that will make your trip truly memorable.
