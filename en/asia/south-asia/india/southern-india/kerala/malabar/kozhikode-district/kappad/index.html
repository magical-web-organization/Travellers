Introduction to Kappad

Nestled in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, Kappad is a quaint coastal town that boasts of rich history, natural beauty, and cultural significance. It is believed to be the place where the Portuguese navigator Vasco da Gama landed in India in 1498, kickstarting the sea route that changed the course of history. Over the years, Kappad has evolved into a popular tourist destination, drawing visitors with its unspoiled beaches, lush greenery, and charming local culture.

This guide will help you plan your trip to Kappad and explore its best features, activities and places of interest.

List of Places to Visit in Kappad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kappad has a rich history that dates back several centuries, and it is reflected in the numerous cultural and historical landmarks scattered across the town. Here are some of the best places to visit in Kappad for culturally significant attractions:

1. Kappad Beach

Kappad Beach is famous as the spot where Vasco da Gama landed in India. The picturesque beach is surrounded by coconut groves, and offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea. Visitors can take a stroll along the shore, swim in the sea, or relax in the shade of the trees.

2. Pazhassiraja Museum

The Pazhassiraja Museum is located in East Hill, Kozhikode, and offers a glimpse into the rich history of Malabar. The museum showcases a range of artifacts that reflect the local culture, including ancient coins, pottery, and weapons.

3. Beypore Fort

Built in the 16th century, the Beypore Fort is one of the most iconic landmarks in Kappad. The fort was built by the Portuguese, and played a key role in the region’s history. Visitors can explore the ruins of the fort, and take in the stunning views of the sea.

Natural Attractions of Kappad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kappad is naturally blessed with exquisite beauty, and offers a wide range of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best places to visit in Kappad for natural attractions:

1. Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary

The Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary is located around 20 km from Kappad, and is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The sanctuary is home to over a hundred species of birds, including migratory birds that come from as far as Siberia.

2. Thusharagiri Waterfalls

Located around 50 km from Kappad, the Thusharagiri Waterfalls are a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can trek the picturesque trails that wind through the forest, and take a dip in the refreshing waters of the waterfalls.

3. Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

The Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area that covers over 344 square kilometers of land. The sanctuary is home to a range of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and leopards. Visitors can take a jeep safari to explore the sanctuary, and witness the beauty of nature up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kappad

Kappad offers a range of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the town’s natural beauty and cultural landmarks. Some of the best trails include:

  • Kappad Beach Trail: A scenic trail that runs along the coastline, offering stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

  • Beypore Heritage Trail: A heritage trail that takes visitors through the historic town of Beypore, showcasing the town’s cultural landmarks and architecture.

  • Thiruvangoor Trail: A scenic trail that winds through lush green forests, and takes visitors to the stunning Thiruvangoor temple.

Best Things to Do in Kappad: Activities and Tours

Kappad offers a range of activities and tours that cater to visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do in Kappad include:

  • Beach Activities: Visitors can indulge in a range of beach activities, such as swimming, sunbathing, and beach volleyball.

  • Water Sports: Kappad also offers a range of water sports, including jet skiing, kayaking, and parasailing.

  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can take cultural tours that showcase the town’s rich history and heritage, such as the Vasco da Gama trail and the Beypore Heritage trail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kappad

A week-long itinerary for Kappad can include the following activities:

  • Day 1: Explore Kappad Beach and its surroundings.
  • Day 2: Visit the Pazhassiraja Museum and Beypore Fort, followed by a trip to the Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary.
  • Day 3: Trek to the Thusharagiri Waterfalls and spend the day exploring the scenic surroundings.
  • Day 4: Take a cultural tour of Beypore, followed by a visit to the Kozhikode beach.
  • Day 5: Explore the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary and take a jeep safari to see wild animals in their natural habitat.
  • Day 6: Visit the stunning Thiruvangoor temple and take a nature walk in the surrounding forests.
  • Day 7: Indulge in beach activities, and relax in the serene surroundings of Kappad.

Food and Drink Scene of Kappad

Kappad is known for its delicious cuisine, which is a unique blend of local flavors and traditional cooking techniques. Some of the best dishes to try in Kappad include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Malabar Biryani: A spicy rice dish cooked with tender chicken or mutton.

  • Kozhikode Halwa: A sweet dish made with wheat, sugar, and ghee, served as a dessert.

  • Thalassery Biriyani: A delicious rice dish made with aromatic spices and tender meat.

Local Beverages

  • Sulaimani: A refreshing black tea with lemon and spices, popular in Kappad and throughout Kerala.

  • Neeranjana: A local drink made with neem leaves, honey, and water, known for its medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Kappad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kappad offers a wide range of accommodation options that cater to the needs of visitors of all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Kappad are:

  • Raviz Kadavu: A luxury resort with a private beach, spa, and outdoor pool.

  • The Beach Heritage Inn: A charming guesthouse located near Kappad Beach, offering comfortable stay and modern amenities.

  • Kappad Beach Resort: A budget-friendly resort that offers clean and comfortable rooms, and easy access to the beach and other attractions.

What to Buy in Kappad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kappad is known for its unique handicrafts and traditional products that make excellent souvenirs and gifts. Some of the best things to buy in Kappad include:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramic ware.

  • Handwoven cotton and silk fabrics in traditional patterns and designs.

  • Spices and tea blends, including cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon.

Local Culture and Customs of Kappad

Kappad has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its local customs and traditions. Visitors are advised to respect the local beliefs and practices, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites or participating in cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Kappad

The best time to visit Kappad is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kappad

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Plan your trip in advance, and make reservations for accommodation and activities.

  • Take precautions to protect yourself from the sun and heat.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local beliefs and customs.

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Engage in illegal activities or substances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kappad

Kappad is a relatively safe town for visitors, but it is advisable to take precautions to ensure your safety. Visitors are advised to stay alert and aware of their surroundings, and avoid dark and isolated areas at night.

Health in Kappad

Kappad has several medical facilities and pharmacies, but visitors are advised to carry their own medication and consult a doctor before travelling. Dehydration and sunburn are common health concerns in Kappad, and visitors are advised to stay hydrated and use sunscreen when outdoors.

Getting to Kappad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kappad is well-connected to major cities and towns in Kerala, and can be easily accessed by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Kappad

The nearest airport to Kappad is Calicut International Airport, located around 23 km away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Kappad

Kappad can be reached by bus from Kozhikode, which is around 16 km away from the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or private car to reach Kappad from nearby cities and destinations.

How to Get Around in Kappad

Kappad is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw for local transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kappad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kappad is surrounded by several popular tourist destinations in Kerala, including:

  • Wayanad: A beautiful hill station located around 100 km from Kappad, known for its serene surroundings and natural beauty.

  • Malappuram: A historic town located around 60 km from Kappad, known for its religious sites and cultural landmarks.

  • Bekal: A coastal town located around 180 km from Kappad, known for its stunning backwaters and ancient fort.

Conclusion

Kappad is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Kappad has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you are all set to plan your trip, explore the town’s best attractions, and have a memorable vacation in Malabar.
