Introduction to Kozhikode District

Kozhikode District, also known as Calicut, is located in the Malabar region of Kerala in Southern India. It is a historically significant city that was once the capital of the powerful Zamorin dynasty. Today, Kozhikode is known for its beautiful beaches, rich culture, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Kozhikode District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kozhikode has a fascinating history, which is reflected in the many cultural attractions in the city. Some must-visit historical and cultural landmarks include:

Pazhassiraja Museum

Located in East Hill, the Pazhassiraja Museum showcases interesting artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the region’s history, from its pre-colonial days through to the Indian Revolution. The museum is spread over a vast area and is home to an impressive collection of antique coins, ancient pottery, bronze medals, and ivory carvings.

Kalipoika Palace

The Kalipoika Palace is located on the banks of the Kallayi River and was once the home of the King of Kozhikode. The palace is now a grand museum, showcasing ancient murals, portraits, antique furniture, and historic documents. The palace is a must-visit destination for history buffs, but its beauty is sure to captivate anyone who visits.

Natural Attractions of Kozhikode District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kozhikode is not only culturally significant but also has a plethora of natural attractions perfect for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best natural attractions in the district:

Kappad Beach

Kappad Beach is one of the most beautiful and serene beaches in Kozhikode District. It is known for its tranquil waters and its historical significance, as it was the place where Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama first set foot in India in 1498. Spend a day at Kappad Beach and soak in the scenic beauty and historical importance.

Thusharagiri Waterfalls

Thusharagiri Waterfalls is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The waterfall is located amidst a lush green forest, and visitors can enjoy trekking, rock climbing, and swimming in the natural pool below the falls. It is a must-visit destination for those who enjoy adventure and natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kozhikode District

Kozhikode offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Southern India. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

Malabar Hill Trail

The Malabar Hill Trail is a popular hiking route that offers stunning panoramic views of the sea and the city. The trail is a moderate trek, covering a distance of 9 km, and is best enjoyed during the cooler months between November and March.

Beypore-Bhatt Road Route

The Beypore-Bhatt Road Route is a popular biking route in Kozhikode District. The route offers picturesque views of the backwaters and the surrounding countryside. The 25 km ride is a challenging but rewarding route, best enjoyed during the monsoon season between June and August.

Best Things to Do in Kozhikode District: Activities and Tours

Kozhikode District has something to offer for everyone, from adventure seekers to culture buffs. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

Visit the Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary

The Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary is a haven for bird watchers and nature lovers. The sanctuary is home to over 100 species of birds, including migratory birds like the sandpiper, kingfisher, and tern. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk along the river and watch the birds in their natural habitat.

Take a Traditional Boat Ride in Kozhikode

One of the best ways to explore Kozhikode is by taking a traditional boat ride. The boats are locally known as ‘vallom,’ and visitors can enjoy a peaceful ride through the backwaters while taking in the stunning views and enjoying the company of the locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kozhikode District

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Kozhikode District that covers the best destinations in the area:

Day 1: Visit the Pazhassiraja Museum and Kalipoika Palace

Day 2: Head to Kappad Beach and spend the day exploring the scenic beauty

Day 3: Trek to Thusharagiri Waterfalls and enjoy an adventure-filled day

Day 4: Explore the Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary and take a traditional boat ride.

Day 5: Visit the Kozhikode Beach and enjoy the sunset

Day 6: Take a traditional pottery class in the village of Kumbala

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at your hotel or exploring local markets and shops for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Kozhikode District

Must-Try Dishes

Kozhikode is known for its delicious cuisine, which is a blend of traditional Kerala cooking and Arabian influences. Here are some must-try dishes:

Kozhikode Biriyani

Made with fragrant long-grain rice, tender chicken or mutton, and a blend of spices, the Kozhikode Biriyani is a popular dish in the area.

Banana Chips

Banana chips are a popular snack in South India, and the Kozhikode version is especially delicious. Fresh bananas are sliced thinly, fried until crispy and then seasoned with salt and chilly powder.

Local Beverages

Kozhikode is famous for its tea and coffee. Here are some local beverages worth trying:

Sulaimani

Sulaimani is a special tea preparation made with lemon, dried ginger, and spices. It is a popular beverage consumed after a meal.

Chai

Kozhikode’s chai is unique in that it is made with spices like cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom. It is a perfect way to kick start your day by enjoying in the morning time.

Where to Stay in Kozhikode District: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

Kozhikode has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some popular options include:

The Gateway Hotel

Located near the Kozhikode beach, The Gateway Hotel is a luxurious hotel with world-class amenities like a pool and spa. The hotel offers beautiful sea-facing rooms with stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

Zostel Kozhikode

Zostel Kozhikode is a great option for travelers on a budget. The hostel is located in the heart of the city and offers clean and comfortable dormitories as well as private rooms at reasonable prices.

What to Buy in Kozhikode District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kozhikode is famous for its authentic, handcrafted products. Here are some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home:

Handmade Soaps

Kozhikode is home to several local soap makers producing high-quality, all-natural soaps made with coconut oil and other natural ingredients. These soaps make perfect souvenirs or gifts for friends and family.

Spices

Kozhikode is famous for its spices, especially pepper and cardamom. Visitors can stock up on high-quality, local spices during their stay in the district.

Local Culture and Customs of Kozhikode District

The people of Kozhikode are known for their hospitality and are very welcoming to visitors. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and shrines.

  • Remove shoes before entering homes, temples, and mosques.

  • Do not point your feet at anyone, as it is considered disrespectful.

  • Always ask permission before taking photographs of people or their belongings.

Best Time to Visit Kozhikode District

The best time to visit Kozhikode is between September and May. The weather during this period is pleasant, with cool temperatures and occasional rainfall. June through August is the monsoon season, and it is best to avoid travel during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kozhikode District

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Keep a copy of your ID with you at all times.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures or videos of people without permission.

  • Use drugs or indulge in any illegal activities.

  • Disrespect religious sites or cultural symbols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kozhikode District

Kozhikode is a relatively safe district, but visitors should take the usual precautions to stay safe. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas after dark.

  • Keep your valuables secure at all times.

  • Be aware of your surroundings, and report any suspicious activity to the authorities.

Health in Kozhikode District

Kozhikode is a safe destination from a health perspective, but visitors should take the usual precautions to stay healthy. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

  • Wash your hands frequently and carry alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against the sun and mosquitoes.

Getting to Kozhikode District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kozhikode is well-connected to other major Indian cities via road, rail, and air. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Kozhikode District

The Calicut International Airport is the nearest airport to Kozhikode District. It is well-connected to major Indian cities like Mumbai, Chennai, and Delhi.

Bus Stations Near Kozhikode District

The Kozhikode Bus Stand is located in the heart of the city and is well-connected to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Kozhikode by train or by hiring a taxi from nearby cities like Kannur, Thrissur, or Kochi.

How to Get Around in Kozhikode District

Kozhikode is a relatively small district, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by public transportation. Buses and auto-rickshaws are the most common modes of transportation within the city.

Where to Go Next from Kozhikode District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kozhikode is surrounded by several popular destinations that are well worth a visit. Here are some nearby destinations to explore:

Wayanad

Located just a few hours from Kozhikode, Wayanad is a stunning district known for its coffee plantations, scenic waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries.

Kannur

Kannur is a coastal district located north of Kozhikode. It is known for its pristine beaches, historic forts, and traditional theyyam dance performances.

Conclusion

Kozhikode District is a culturally rich and naturally beautiful destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a culture buff, Kozhikode offers something for everyone. From scenic beaches to historical landmarks, the district is sure to captivate and inspire.
