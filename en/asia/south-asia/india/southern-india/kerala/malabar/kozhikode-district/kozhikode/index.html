Introduction to Kozhikode

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a coastal city located in the Malabar region of Kerala, Southern India. Known for its rich cultural heritage, Kozhikode was once a major trade center for spices and textiles, and is now a bustling city with a vibrant local scene. The city boasts a mix of historic landmarks, natural beauty, and modern attractions, making it an ideal destination for both history buffs and nature enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Kozhikode

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pazhassiraja Museum and Art Gallery

One of the most popular tourist attractions in Kozhikode, Pazhassiraja Museum and Art Gallery is dedicated to the memory of the great Pazhassiraja Kerala Varma, a popular folk hero who fought against the British East India Company. The museum displays a wide range of artefacts from his life and era, including sculptures, paintings, coins, and armour.

Mananchira Square

A historic square located in the heart of Kozhikode city, Mananchira Square is an ideal place to relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of the city. Surrounded by several heritage buildings, the square also houses a musical fountain and an open-air theatre.

Kozhikode Beach

One of the most popular hangouts in the city, Kozhikode Beach is an ideal place to relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of the Arabian Sea. The beach also boasts several food and drink stalls, and is known for its mouth-watering local delicacies.

Natural Attractions of Kozhikode: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kakkayam Dam and Reservoir

A perfect spot for adventure lovers, Kakkayam Dam and Reservoir is located about 45 km from Kozhikode. The site is ideal for trekking, rock climbing, and rappelling. Visitors can also enjoy the scenic beauty of the dam and reservoir.

Thusharagiri Waterfalls

Located about 50 km from Kozhikode, Thusharagiri Waterfalls is a popular tourist destination famous for its picturesque beauty. Visitors can enjoy an early morning trek or go on a picnic with their friends and family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kozhikode

Adyarathri Bike Trip

A 22 km stretch from Kozhikode to Adyarathri, this bike trip is ideal for adventure enthusiasts who want to explore the scenic beauty of the city. The route passes through several paddy fields, rivers, and historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Kozhikode: Activities and Tours

Calicut Heritage Walk

A guided tour that takes visitors on a walk through the historic sites of Kozhikode, the Calicut Heritage Walk is a must-do activity for history lovers. The tour includes visits to the Kozhikode Beach, Mananchira Square, and Pazhassiraja Museum and Art Gallery, among other places.

Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary

Located about 19 km from Kozhikode, Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary is home to over a hundred species of birds. Visitors can enjoy bird watching, photography, and nature walks in the sanctuary.

Food and Drink Scene of Kozhikode

Must-Try Dishes

Dum Biryani

A local delicacy in Kozhikode, Dum Biryani is a dish of spicy, flavorful rice and meat that is cooked over a slow fire to infuse the flavors together. It is often served with raita, a yogurt-based side dish.

Fish Curry

A staple dish of the region, Fish Curry is a spicy and tangy curry made with fish, coconut milk, and spices. It is often served with rice or bread.

Local Beverages

Sulaimani Tea

A popular drink in Kozhikode, Sulaimani tea is a sweet and tangy black tea that is infused with lemon and other spices.

Sharjah Shake

A refreshing drink made with milk, ice cream, and fruit syrups, the Sharjah Shake is a popular drink among locals and tourists alike.

What to Buy in Kozhikode: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Banana Chips

A popular snack in Kozhikode, Banana Chips are crispy, savory chips made with ripe bananas and spices.

Handcrafted Artifacts

Kozhikode is also known for its unique handmade artifacts, such as coconut shell lamps, mat weaving, and wood carvings, that you can take home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kozhikode

Kozhikode has a rich and vibrant cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, language, food, and customs. The Malayalam language is widely spoken in the region, and the city is famous for its traditional art forms such as Theyyam and Kathakali.

Best Time to Visit Kozhikode

The best time to visit Kozhikode is between October and May, when the weather is pleasant and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kozhikode

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and street food.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious places.
  • Carry sunscreen and a hat while visiting outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry alcohol or drugs to public places.
  • Visit secluded areas after dark.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kozhikode

Kozhikode is a generally safe city, but visitors are advised to take precautions against petty crimes such as theft and pickpocketing. It is also essential to respect local customs and traditions to avoid any unwanted confrontations with the locals.

Health in Kozhikode

Visitors to Kozhikode are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It is also essential to carry a first aid kit and stay hydrated while exploring the outdoor attractions of the city.

Getting to Kozhikode: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kozhikode

Kozhikode International Airport is the nearest airport to the city, which is well-connected to major cities across India.

Bus Stations Near Kozhikode

Kozhikode has bus stations that are well-connected to various cities in Kerala and neighboring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kozhikode by train or private car.

How to Get Around in Kozhikode

Kozhikode has a well-connected transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws. It is also possible to rent a bike or motorbike to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Kozhikode: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wayanad

Located about 87 km from Kozhikode, Wayanad is a beautiful hill station that is known for its scenic beauty and wildlife sanctuaries.

Kannur

Located about 92 km from Kozhikode, Kannur is a historic city that is known for its pristine beaches, ancient forts, and temples.

Conclusion

Kozhikode is a beautiful city that is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. With its mix of ancient landmarks, scenic attractions, and modern amenities, Kozhikode is an ideal destination for travelers who want to explore the best of Kerala’s cultural capital. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of Kozhikode.
