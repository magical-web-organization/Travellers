Introduction to Thamarassery

Thamarassery is a small town located in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, a state in Southern India. The town is situated on the Western Ghats mountain range, offering breathtaking views of scenic landscapes and lush green valleys. Thamarassery is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful temples, and stunning natural attractions that continue to attract tourists worldwide. In this travel guide, we’ll explore places to visit, things to do, what to eat, where to stay, and how to get around in Thamarassery.

List of Places to Visit in Thamarassery

  • Thusharagiri Waterfalls: Located in the midst of dense forests, Thusharagiri Waterfalls is a paradise for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy trekking, rock climbing, and bird watching in this stunning location.
  • Thikkotti Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse was built during the British Raj era and offers panoramic views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Chembra Peak: This peak is the highest in the Western Ghats range and is a must-visit spot for hikers and adventure enthusiasts.
  • Kozhippara Falls: Known for its beauty and serenity, Kozhippara Falls is a perfect spot for picnics, birdwatching, and nature walks.
  • Mishkal Mosque: This 14th-century mosque is an architectural marvel and one of the oldest mosques in the region.
  • Pazhur Padippura: This ancient gate was once used as the entrance to the Thiruvambadi Temple and is now a popular tourist attraction.
  • Kakkayam Dam: This dam is famous for its stunning views and offers a peaceful ambiance for visitors to relax in.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pazhassi Raja Museum and Art Gallery: This museum is named after Pazhassi Raja, a legendary king of Kerala, and showcases ancient artifacts, paintings and sculptures, and other items related to the region’s history and culture.
  • Vadakara Sand Banks: This historic site is believed to have been the location where legendary poet and scholar Thunchaththu Ezhuthachan is said to have composed famous works.
  • Mananchira Square: Mananchira Square is a unique square that houses many historic buildings and landmarks in the town center.

Natural Attractions of Thamarassery: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thiruvambadi Hills: Trekking in Thiruvambadi Hills is a popular activity among tourists who visit Thamarassery. Visitors can also enjoy the scenic views of lush green valleys and dense forests.
  • Mavoor Hills: Mavoor Hills is another popular trekking spot, known for its serene beauty, tea plantations, and waterfalls.
  • Kakkayam River: This river is famous for its stunning rapids and is a perfect spot for adventure sports such as river rafting and kayaking.
  • Lakkidi Viewpoint: Lakkidi Viewpoint offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats mountains, making it an ideal spot for nature photography and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thamarassery

  • Chembra Peak Trek: This trek is one of the most challenging hikes in the region, taking visitors to the peak of Chembra, the highest mountain in the Western Ghats.
  • Thusharagiri Trek: The Thusharagiri trek takes visitors through dense forests, tea plantations, and the stunning Thusharagiri Waterfalls.
  • Kakkayam Trek: The Kakkayam Trek is a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts, who hike through lush green forests and explore hidden waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Thamarassery: Activities and Tours

  • Thusharagiri Waterfalls Tour: Visitors can explore the serene beauty of Thusharagiri Waterfalls while trekking, rock climbing, and bird watching.
  • Chembra Peak Trekking Tour: Chembra Peak is the highest mountain in the Western Ghats, and visitors can explore the beauty of this region while trekking.
  • Kakkayam River Rafting Adventure: Adventure enthusiasts can experience the thrill of river rafting and kayaking in the rapids of the Kakkayam River.

Food and Drink Scene of Thamarassery

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pathiri: A type of bread made from rice flour.
  • Biryani: A spiced rice and meat dish that is popular throughout India.
  • Idiappam: A rice flour-based dish that is similar to spaghetti.
  • Pazham Pori: A fried banana fritter that is a popular snack.
  • Puttu: A dish made from steamed rice and coconut that is a breakfast staple in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: A fermented drink made from the sap of coconut or palm trees.
  • Lassi: A thick yogurt-based drink that is sweetened and flavored with fruit.

Where to Stay in Thamarassery: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kairali Heritage: A luxury hotel that offers traditional Kerala-style accommodation.
  • The Gateway Hotel: A modern hotel that features a spa, fitness center, and swimming pool.
  • Kakkayam Tourist Home: A budget-friendly lodging option that is located near the Kakkayam Dam.

What to Buy in Thamarassery: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coconut Shell Handicrafts: Coconut shells are used to create a range of handicrafts, including bowls, vases, and lamps.
  • Spices: Thamarassery is home to some of the best spices in the region, including black pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  • Tea: Tea plantations are common in the region, and visitors can purchase high-quality tea products to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Thamarassery

Thamarassery is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural landmarks. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering religious places.

Best Time to Visit Thamarassery

The best time to visit Thamarassery is between September and May when the weather is pleasant, and the rainfall is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thamarassery

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural landmarks and religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places or natural attractions.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thamarassery

Thamarassery is a safe place for tourists to visit. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as not walking alone at night and not leaving their belongings unattended.

Health in Thamarassery

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent illnesses such as diarrhea and stomach upset. It is also recommended to take mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue and malaria.

Getting to Thamarassery: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thamarassery

The nearest airport to Thamarassery is Calicut International Airport, located approximately 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near Thamarassery

Thamarassery is well-connected by bus, with regular buses running to and from nearby cities such as Kozhikode and Wayanad.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis or rent cars to travel to Thamarassery.

How to Get Around in Thamarassery

The best way to get around in Thamarassery is by taxi or rented car. Buses are also available for traveling to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Thamarassery: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kozhikode: Known for its historic landmarks and beaches, Kozhikode is a must-visit destination near Thamarassery.
  • Wayanad: Wayanad is famous for its scenic beauty and wildlife sanctuary and is located approximately 40 km away.
  • Malappuram: Malappuram is a historic town known for its traditional handicrafts and is located approximately 65 km away.

Conclusion

Thamarassery, Kerala, is a must-visit destination for tourists who wish to explore the region’s natural and cultural heritage. From stunning waterfalls and mountain peaks to traditional handicrafts and ancient temples, Thamarassery has something to offer everyone. Whether visitors wish to hike through the forests or relax in the serene ambiance of Kakkayam Dam, Thamarassery is the perfect destination for a memorable holiday.
