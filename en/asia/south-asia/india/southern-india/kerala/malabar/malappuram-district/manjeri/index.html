A Comprehensive Guide to Manjeri

Manjeri, a culturally-rich town in the Malappuram district of Kerala, is a popular tourist destination, yet relatively undiscovered. Experiencing Manjeri is like stepping back in time to an era when Kerala’s cultural roots ran deep. It is a gem that shouldn’t be missed by anyone who loves art, culture, and history.

List of Places to Visit in Manjeri

Manjeri is home to a range of places of cultural significance, which include museums, historical places, and natural attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit in Manjeri.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kerala Kala Mandalam: This prestigious institution was established to promote and preserve the art and culture of Kerala. They offer a range of courses in classical dance forms such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Koodiyattam, among others.
  2. Thunchath Ezhuthachan Malayalam University: Named after the father of Malayalam literature, Thunchath Ezhuthachan, this academic institution is dedicated to the promotion of the Malayalam language and literature.
  3. Kottakunnu: A famous historical site, Kottakunnu hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year. The site is home to a range of ancient temples and monuments that date back to the 15th century.
  4. Thirumandhamkunnu Bhagavathi Temple: Dedicated to the goddess Bhagavathi, this temple is one of the most visited in Kerala. The temple hosts the annual Bharani festival, which is one of Kerala’s most famous festivals.
  5. Pazhayangadi Mosque: This 500-year-old mosque is known for its unique architecture and is a must-visit for those interested in Islamic culture.

Natural Attractions of Manjeri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Nedumkayam Rainforest: Manjeri is home to the Nedumkayam rainforest, which is located on the banks of the Chaliyar River. The rainforest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare species such as the Lion-tailed macaque and the Great Indian Hornbill.
  2. Kavu and Maala river: The Kavu and Maala river is a great place to go for a swim, go fishing, or just relax and take in the beautiful scenery.
  3. Vilangadu Hills: The Vilangadu Hills are a famous trekking destination in Manjeri. The trek to the top takes around 4 hours, and the view from the top is worth it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manjeri

Manjeri is a great place for those who enjoy hiking or biking. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  1. Nedumkayam Rainforest Trek: Trek through the Nedumkayam rainforest and take in the beautiful scenery along the way. The trek takes around 3-4 hours.
  2. Vilangadu Hills Trek: Trek to the top of the Vilangadu Hills, which take around 4 hours. The view from the top is breathtaking, and the trek is considered to be moderately difficult.
  3. Cycling around Manjeri: Manjeri is a great place to explore on a bike, and there are plenty of bike rental shops in town. Take a leisurely ride around town and take in the local culture and scenery.

Best Things to Do in Manjeri: Activities and Tours

  1. Kathakali and Kalaripayattu Shows: Attend a local Kathakali or Kalaripayattu show to witness the art and culture of Kerala.
  2. Ayurvedic Treatments: Kerala is known for its Ayurvedic treatments, and Manjeri has some of the best spas in the district.
  3. Boat Rides: Take a leisurely boat ride along the Maala River and take in the local scenery.
  4. Shopping: Manjeri is known for its silk sarees, handicrafts, and handloom products. Take some time to explore the local markets and shops to find unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Manjeri

Manjeri is known for its rich cultural heritage, which includes classical dance forms such as Kathakali and Koodiyattam. The town is also known for its heritage sites and museums, which reflect the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Manjeri

The best time to visit Manjeri is during the winter season (October-April) when the weather is pleasant and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manjeri

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  2. Respect local customs and traditions.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter.
  2. Do not disrespect local customs and traditions.
  3. Do not carry plastic bags or single-use plastics with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manjeri

Manjeri is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take precautions such as:

  1. Be aware of pickpockets and other petty thefts.
  2. Be careful when trekking or hiking.

Health in Manjeri

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria. It’s also important to drink bottled water and avoid eating street food that may not be hygienic.

Getting to Manjeri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manjeri is situated in the Malappuram district of Kerala and is easily accessible using a variety of transportation options.

Airports Near Manjeri

The nearest airport to Manjeri is Calicut International Airport, which is around 21 km away.

Bus Stations Near Manjeri

Manjeri has a well-connected bus station, with buses running regularly to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Manjeri by train or hire a taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Manjeri

Manjeri is a small town and can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or hire a taxi to help them get around the town.

Where to Go Next From Manjeri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Manjeri:

  1. Calicut City: Visit the bustling city of Calicut to explore its rich history and culture.
  2. Wayanad: Wayanad is a scenic hill station that is known for its tea plantations and spice gardens.
  3. Ooty: Ooty, also known as Udhagamandalam, is a beautiful hill station in Tamil Nadu that is known for its beautiful scenery and tea plantations.

Conclusion

Manjeri is a beautiful and culturally rich town that is worth visiting for those who love art, history, culture, and the outdoors. With its beautiful rainforests, historical monuments, and delicious cuisines, Manjeri is a gem that should not be missed.
