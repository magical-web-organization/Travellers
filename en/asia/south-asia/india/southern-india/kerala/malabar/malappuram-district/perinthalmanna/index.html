Introduction to Perinthalmanna

Perinthalmanna is a beautiful town located in the Malappuram district of Kerala, India. It is known for its scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage, which makes it a popular destination among tourists. Perinthalmanna is also known as the “Land of the Heroes” as it is the birthplace of several warriors and freedom fighters who fought against the British rule. The town is surrounded by lush green hills, sprawling paddy fields, and serene water bodies, which make it a perfect place for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Perinthalmanna

If you’re planning a trip to Perinthalmanna, here is a list of must-visit places that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kunchan Smarakam Fort: This fort is the birthplace of Kunchan Nambiar, the popular 18th-century Malayalam poet.
  • Kelathiyoor lndoor Stadium: This stadium hosts various cultural and sports events throughout the year.
  • Thirumandhamkunnu Bhagavathy Temple: This temple is dedicated to Goddess Bhagavathy and is a major pilgrimage center in the state of Kerala.
  • Mampuram Mosque: This mosque is a famous Muslim pilgrimage center and is known for its traditional architecture.
  • Pazhayangadi Mosque: A famous mosque in Malabar region, considered to be one of the oldest muslim heritage locations at Malappuram.

Natural Attractions of Perinthalmanna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Padinjarathara Dam: This is a picturesque dam located on the Bharathappuzha river, which provides a breathtaking view of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Kottakkunnu: This is a beautiful hill station located in the heart of Perinthalmanna town, which offers a panoramic view of the entire town and its surroundings.
  • Akkarappacha Waterfalls: This majestic waterfall is situated in the midst of dense forests and is a perfect spot for trekking and camping.
  • Moulana Hospital and Research Centre and Medi Park: This place is well known for its walkways, gardens, fountains and its massive granite columns etc.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perinthalmanna

Perinthalmanna has several hiking and biking routes, which are perfect for adventure enthusiasts. The most popular ones include:

  • Kottakkunnu: This hill station has several trekking trails that offer breathtaking views of the town and its surroundings.
  • Chinakkathoor Kavu: This is a sacred grove located in the heart of the town, which has several trekking trails that lead to nearby hills and paddy fields.
  • Padinjarathara Dam: This dam has several trekking and biking trails that run along the Bharathappuzha river and offer stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Best Things to Do in Perinthalmanna: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, Perinthalmanna also offers several exciting activities and tours that you can engage in during your visit. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Ayurvedic Treatment: Perinthalmanna is known for its Ayurvedic treatments, which are based on ancient Indian medicine practices. You can avail of these treatments at various Ayurvedic spas and wellness centers located in and around the town.
  • Traditional Music and Dance Shows: Kerala is known for its rich cultural heritage and Perinthalmanna is no exception. You can attend traditional music and dance shows, such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Thullal, which are held at various venues throughout the town.
  • Cycling Tours: You can explore the town and its surroundings on a bicycle, which is an eco-friendly and affordable way of transportation. Several cycling tours are organized by local operators, which take you through scenic routes and offer a unique perspective of the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Perinthalmanna

Perinthalmanna offers a unique culinary experience that combines traditional Kerala cuisine with unique local flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Malabar Biryani: A fragrant and flavorful dish made with Basmati rice, spices, and meat or vegetables, which is a popular delicacy in Malabar region.
  • Pathiri and Chicken Curry: Pathiri is a thin, round flatbread made with rice flour, which is paired with spicy chicken curry and is a staple dish in Perinthalmanna cuisine.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Puttu is a cylindrical steamed rice cake, which is paired with spicy black chana curry and is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala.

Local Beverages

  • Sulaimani: A traditional Muslim beverage made with black tea, lemon, and spices such as cardamom and cinnamon.
  • Lemon Tea: A refreshing and tangy beverage made with black tea, lemon, and sugar.
  • Sweet Lassi: A yogurt-based drink that is sweetened with sugar and flavored with cardamom and saffron.

What to Buy in Perinthalmanna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to buy souvenirs or local products to bring back home, here are some suggestions:

  • Handloom Products: Perinthalmanna is known for its handloom textiles such as sarees, lungis, and towels, which are made using traditional techniques by local artisans.
  • Spices: Kerala is known for its spices and Perinthalmanna is no exception. You can buy fragrant spices such as cardamom, black pepper, and turmeric, which are grown locally and are of high quality.
  • Handicrafts: You can buy beautiful handicrafts such as wooden carvings, brass lamps, and pottery, which are made by local artisans and are unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Perinthalmanna

Perinthalmanna has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in traditional Indian customs and practices. The locals are friendly and welcoming and take pride in their cultural heritage. Some customs that you should be aware of while visiting Perinthalmanna include:

  • Dressing Etiquette: While Perinthalmanna is a modern town, it is important to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Greeting Etiquette: When greeting someone, it is customary to say “Namaste” with folded hands, which is a traditional Indian greeting.
  • Handshake Etiquette: While handshakes are common in modern settings, it is important to note that some locals might prefer a traditional Indian greeting instead.

Best Time to Visit Perinthalmanna

The best time to visit Perinthalmanna is from October to March, which is the winter season. During this time, the weather is pleasant and comfortable, making it perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The summer season, from April to June, can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season, from July to September, can be rainy and unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perinthalmanna

Here are some do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind while visiting Perinthalmanna:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Be respectful towards the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, which are unique to the region.
  • Engage in outdoor activities and tours to explore the town and its surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activities that might harm the local environment or wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs and practices.
  • Consume alcohol or smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Perinthalmanna is a safe and peaceful town, with a low crime rate. However, it is important to take basic precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash and keeping valuable items such as passports and credit cards safe. In terms of health, it is advisable to carry a first aid kit and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Perinthalmanna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perinthalmanna is well connected to the rest of Kerala and India through various modes of transportation. Some of the most popular options include:

Airports Near Perinthalmanna

  • Calicut International Airport: This airport is located 50 kilometers away from Perinthalmanna and is well connected to major cities in India and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Perinthalmanna

  • Perinthalmanna KSRTC Bus Station: This bus station is located in the heart of the town and is well connected to major destinations in Kerala.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Perinthalmanna

Perinthalmanna is easily navigable by foot or by bicycle. You can also hire taxis or auto-rickshaws to travel within the town and its surroundings.
