Introduction to Malappuram

Malappuram, a bustling city in the Malabar region of Kerala, is a traveler’s paradise. It holds a significant place in the cultural and historical landscape of Kerala. Malappuram is a melting pot of different cultures, languages, and religions. The city is known for its elegant mosques, ancient temples, and churches. Malappuram also attracts tourists with its unique cuisine, natural attractions, and adventurous activities. This comprehensive travel guide will introduce you to the best places to visit, must-try dishes, lodging options, and transportation options in Malappuram.

List of Places to Visit in Malappuram

Malappuram offers a plethora of culturally rich, natural, and historical attractions that appeal to every type of traveler. These are the top places to visit in Malappuram:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kadampuzha Bhagavathy Temple: This ancient temple is dedicated to Goddess Bhagavathy and is known for its exquisite architecture.
  • Kottakkunnu: This vantage point offers panoramic views of the city and is home to a children’s park, an open-air theater, and a swimming pool.
  • Pazhayangadi Mosque: This ancient mosque is the final resting place of the Muslim saint, Muhammad Shah. It features unique architecture with an elaborately carved wooden pulpit.
  • Thirunavaya Nava Mukunda Temple: This temple on the banks of the Bharathappuzha river is an architectural masterpiece.
  • Kerala Kalamandalam: This institution is dedicated to promoting and preserving the traditional art forms of Kerala like Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Koodiyattam. Here, you can witness performances and learn about the art forms’ history and techniques.

Natural Attractions of Malappuram: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kodikuthimala: This scenic hill station offers breathtaking views of the surrounding tea plantations and waterfalls.
  • Ponnani Estuary: This estuary is a popular spot for birdwatching and boating. It is also home to a lighthouse that offers panoramic views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary: This bird sanctuary is home to over 100 species of birds and is a must-visit for birdwatchers.
  • Nilambur Teak Museum: This museum showcases the history of the teak industry in Kerala and features a collection of rare teak artifacts and relics.
  • Jungle Safari at Silent Valley National Park: Adventure seekers can explore the park’s dense forests, spot wildlife, and trek to the highest peak in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malappuram

Malappuram is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. These are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Malappuram:

  • Charalkunnu trail: This scenic trail starts from Kozhikode and passes through dense forests, waterfalls, and tea gardens. It ends at Charalkunnu, a picturesque hill station.
  • Adivaram to Vythiri: This biking route passes through tea gardens, spice plantations, and dense forests. You can stop at viewpoints along the way for breathtaking views of the Western Ghats.
  • Mappila Bay Kayaking: Paddle through the backwaters of Malappuram and experience the unique way of life on the coast.

Best Things to Do in Malappuram: Activities and Tours

Malappuram offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s needs. These are some of the best things to do in Malappuram:

  • Kalaripayattu Performance: Watch a live performance of Kalaripayattu, an ancient martial art form that originated in Kerala.
  • Visit a Spice Plantation: Explore the spices that make Kerala cuisine so flavorful and learn about their cultivation and history.
  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn how to make dishes like idiyappam, puttu, and kozhukkatta from a seasoned cook.
  • Backwater Cruise: Take a leisurely boat ride on the backwaters and witness the everyday life of the locals.
  • Eco-tourism: Get up close and personal with nature by exploring forests, plantations, and waterfalls.

Food and Drink Scene of Malappuram

Malappuram’s unique cuisine is a blend of Mughal, Kerala, and Arab influences. The region is known for its spices, rice-based dishes, and desserts. These are the must-try dishes and beverages in Malappuram:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thalassery Biriyani: This is an aromatic mixed-rice dish with layers of spices, meat, and vegetables. It is served with onion and cucumber raita.
  • Pathiri: These thin rice pancakes are made with rice flour and served with meat or vegetable curries.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: This is a traditional breakfast dish made with steamed rice flour and served with chickpea curry.
  • Chatti Pathiri: This layered pastry is stuffed with meat, eggs, and vegetables and is a popular snack in Malappuram.

Local Beverages

  • Sulaimani Tea: This is a black tea infused with herbs, spices, and lemon. It is served with sugar or honey.
  • Sharbat: This is a sweet and refreshing drink made with rose syrup, lime juice, and ice.

Where to Stay in Malappuram: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malappuram offers a range of lodging options to suit every budget and preference. These are some of the best places to stay in Malappuram:

  • Mango Hotels Malappuram: This is a boutique hotel located close to the city center and provides modern amenities.
  • Hotel Copper Folia: This eco-friendly hotel is located near the Kodikuthimala hills and features a beautiful infinity pool.
  • Paragon Hotel: This is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Malappuram and offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Pranavam Homestay: This is a cozy homestay in Ponnani that offers comfortable rooms and delicious home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Malappuram: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Malappuram is famous for its handicrafts, fabrics, and spices. These are the top products to buy in Malappuram:

  • Kasavu Sarees: These sarees are made with cotton and are known for their gold borders and elegant designs.
  • Coconut Shell Handicrafts: These handicrafts are made with coconut shells and come in various shapes and sizes.
  • Teak Wood Products: These products, made with rare teak wood, include carvings, furniture, and kitchenware.
  • Spices: Buy the exotic spices like cardamom, pepper, cinnamon, and cloves that are grown in Malappuram’s plantations.

Local Culture and Customs of Malappuram

Malappuram reflects the cultural diversity of Kerala with its mosques, temples, and churches. The traditional dance forms, attire, and cuisine of Malappuram have Mughal, Kerala, and Arab influences. The people of Malappuram are hospitable and friendly, and their daily life revolves around fishing, agriculture, and handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit Malappuram

The best time to visit Malappuram is between October and March. The weather during this time is pleasant, with cool breezes and low humidity. The monsoon season between June and September is not the ideal time to visit Malappuram as the rains could hamper outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malappuram

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and temples.
  • Respect the local customs and practices.
  • Try local cuisine and participate in cultural activities.
  • Carry sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and hats when going on outdoor activities.
  • Bargain at local markets when buying souvenirs and handicrafts.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol or non-vegetarian food near places of worship.
  • Photograph locals without their consent.
  • Litter or cause any damage to the environment.
  • Engage in any immoral or illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malappuram

Malappuram is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, travelers should take precautions while traveling at night, avoid deserted areas, and always keep valuables locked in the hotel room safe.

Health in Malappuram

Travelers should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria by wearing long-sleeved clothing and using mosquito repellent. They should also carry their necessary medication along with the required vaccinations to prevent water-borne and food-borne diseases.

Getting to Malappuram: Transportation Options and Logistics

Malappuram is well-connected by road, rail, and air. These are the transportation options available to get to Malappuram:

Airports Near Malappuram

  • Calicut International Airport: This is the nearest airport, located 26 kilometers from Malappuram.

Bus Stations Near Malappuram

  • Malappuram KSRTC Bus Station: This is a local and state bus station located in the city center.
  • Perinthalmanna KSRTC Bus Station: This is another bus station located 16 kilometers from Malappuram.

Other Options

  • Trains: Malappuram Junction, Tirur, and Kuttippuram are the nearest railway stations to Malappuram.
  • Taxis and Auto-rickshaws: Taxis and auto-rickshaws are readily available to move around Malappuram.

How to Get Around in Malappuram

Malappuram offers several modes of transportation to get around the city. These are the transportation options available to travel within Malappuram:

  • Taxis and Auto-rickshaws: These are the easiest and most convenient modes of transportation to get around Malappuram.
  • Local Buses: These are the cheapest mode of transportation and connect Malappuram to neighboring towns and cities.
  • Car Rentals: You can rent a car with a driver or self-drive options at Malappuram for convenient transportation within the city.

Where to Go Next from Malappuram: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Malappuram is surrounded by several beautiful destinations and attractions that are worth exploring. These are some of them:

  • Kozhikode: This bustling city is known for its beaches, museums, and historic sites.
  • Wayanad: This hill station is known for its beautiful coffee plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  • Thrissur: This cultural capital of Kerala is known for its ancient temples, colorful festivals, and art forms.

Conclusion

Malappuram is a beautiful city that has something to offer every traveler. Whether you are looking for an adventure in the forests, a glimpse into the local culture, or a relaxing vacation, Malappuram has it all. Plan your trip today and explore one of the most amazing cities in Kerala.
