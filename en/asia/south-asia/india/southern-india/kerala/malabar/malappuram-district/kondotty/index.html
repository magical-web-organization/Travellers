Introduction to Kondotty

Welcome to Kondotty, a small town in the Malappuram District of the southern Indian state of Kerala. Despite being a lesser-known destination, Kondotty has plenty of unique attractions such as historical landmarks, beautiful natural scenery, and vibrant culture. The town is famous for its contribution to aviation history, as it is home to the first airport in Kerala. Here, you can explore traditional houses, charming streets, and a peaceful atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Kondotty

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aripra Mosque: The Aripra Mosque is highly regarded as one of the oldest, heritage-rich congregational mosques in India. The mosque accommodates various Islamic practices, rituals, and ethnic customs of the population, making it a spiritual center from ancient times.

  • Beypore Beach: Beypore is a small coastal fishing town located in Kondotty, about 10 kilometers from Calicut. Known for its shipbuilding yards and its stunning beaches, this place is perfect for exploring the local culture and grabbing some delicious seafood.

  • Mannoor Malika Heritage House: Mannoor Malika Heritage House is one of the oldest traditional houses in Kondotty town. The building features unique architecture, a variety of antique furniture, and old photographs.

Natural Attractions of Kondotty: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kakkadampoyil: Kakkadampoyil is a small hamlet with a scenic view of thirteen waterfalls. It is one of the best places to visit in Kondotty, offering many activities for tourists such as trekking, bird watching, camping, and other adrenaline-rushing activities.

  • Kondotty Hill Garden: The Kondotty Hill Garden is a beautiful park located at the heart of the town. The park has a serene atmosphere, lush green lawns, and several water features. It is an ideal place to relax, take a walk, and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kondotty

The best way to explore the beautiful nature of Kondotty is by hiking or biking. Here are the two most popular trails:

  • The Kakkadampoyil Trail: This trail is located in the Kakkadampoyil hills and has a distance of about 6 kilometers. The trail is perfect for trekking enthusiasts and offers an adventurous experience with stunning waterfalls and a stream running through.

  • The Beypore Trail: This trail starts from Kondotty town and ends in the scenic Beypore beach. It is about 10 kilometers long and is suitable for bike lovers.

Best Things to Do in Kondotty: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the First Aviation Museum in India: Kondotty has the First Aviation Museum in India that showcases diverse aspects of aviation history, several aircraft models, and engines.

  • Explore the Culture of the Town: Explore and shop for traditional clothing, such as saris, lungis, and mundus, in the local markets to get a sense of the local culture.

  • Participate in Cultural Festivals: Experience Kondotty’s vibrant culture through its colorful festivals such as Muthappan Chavittu, Vishu, and Onam.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kondotty

Here is a comprehensive week-long itinerary for Kondotty:

Day 1: Explore Aripra Mosque and Mahe Beach. Day 2: Visit the Beypore beach and explore the traditional houses in Mannoor Malika Heritage House. Day 3: Take on the Kakkadampoyil trekking trail. Day 4: Go for a soothing walk in Kondotty Hill Garden. Day 5: Visit the First Aviation Museum in India. Day 6: Shop for traditional clothing at the local markets and hit the local stores to buy some souvenirs. Day 7: Participate in a cultural festival or relax in the scenic Beypore beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Kondotty

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kerala Porotta and Beef Fry: It is an authentic dish, where the porotta is layered flatbread made with flour, lightly fried and served hot with succulent beef fry.

  • Biryani: Kondotty has one of the best biryani delicacies in Kerala, with a perfect blend of spices and flavor. It usually includes basmati rice cooked with lamb/mutton/chicken or vegetables, infused with spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sambharam: Ideal for warm climates, this drink is a blend of yogurt, ginger, curry leaves, and crushed ice. It helps to cool down the body, providing an instant refresher.

  • Toddy: A traditional alcoholic beverage extracted from Palm trees, toddy is a favorite of many locals in Kondotty.

Where to Stay in Kondotty

Here are some of the best accommodation options to choose from in Kondotty:

  • Puthooram Ayurvedic Resort: It is located amidst beautiful plantations, offering traditional Kerala-style wooden cottages, spa, and Ayurvedic treatments.

  • Kondotty Gateway: It is a modern hotel, located in the heart of Kondotty with world-class amenities and luxurious rooms.

What to Buy in Kondotty: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: The local markets of Kondotty have an array of beautiful handicrafts, such as clothing, artificial jewelry, brass items, and other souvenirs.

  • Spices: Kerala is famous for its spices, and Kondotty is no exception. You can purchase spices such as cardamom, black pepper, and cinnamon.

Local Culture and Customs of Kondotty

Kondotty’s local culture and customs are deeply rooted in Islamic traditions. Women usually wear Burqa/Hijab, and men wear Mundu, a traditional dress with a cloth tied around the waist. Be respectful of local customs and traditions, dress modestly, and ask permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Kondotty

The best time to visit Kondotty is between September to March, as the weather is pleasant, and the cultural festivals are a must-see during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kondotty

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry comfortable footwear for outdoor adventures.
  • Try the local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Carry/use plastic bags.
  • Wear revealing outfits in public places.
  • Enter mosques or other religious structures with footwear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kondotty

Kondotty is a relatively safe place to travel to; however, it is recommended to be vigilant at night and avoid traveling alone.

Health in Kondotty

It is essential to carry insect repellent, especially in the rainy season, as there is a risk of diseases such as Malaria and Dengue. Drink plenty of fluids and use sunscreen while traveling in the summer season.

Getting to Kondotty: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kondotty

  • Calicut International Airport (CCJ) is the nearest airport, located at 3 kilometers away from Kondotty. It has frequent flights to major cities in India and some international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Kondotty

  • The nearest bus stations to Kondotty are Malappuram Bus Station and Perinthalmanna Bus Station that connect to other cities of Kerala and the rest of the country.

Other Options

  • Taxis, autos, and rented bikes and cars are available in Kondotty for local transport.

How to Get Around in Kondotty

Kondotty is easily accessible by foot or cycle. You can rent bikes and scooters in Kondotty town if required.

Where to Go Next from Kondotty: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Calicut Beach is a short 30-minute drive away and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the coast and historical landmarks.

  • Tirur is a small town located about 25 kilometers from Kondotty, known for its pristine beaches, ancient architecture, and religious temples.

Conclusion

Kondotty, with its rich culture, history, and natural beauty, is waiting to be explored. Whether you are a history lover, nature lover, or a foodie, Kondotty has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and create unforgettable memories.
