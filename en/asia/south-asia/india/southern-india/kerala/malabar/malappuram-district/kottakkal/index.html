Introduction to Kottakkal

Kottakkal is a town in Malappuram district, located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. The town is known for its Ayurveda institutes, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. The town is located about 12 miles east of Malappuram and is accessible via train, bus, and taxi.

Kottakkal is steeped in history and has played a significant role in the cultural and economic development of the region. The town is home to several ancient temples and museums that take you on a journey through the past of the Indian subcontinent.

List of Places to Visit in Kottakkal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kottakkal is home to several museums and historical attractions that offer visitors an opportunity to learn about the rich history and culture of the region. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala - The Arya Vaidya Sala is a renowned Ayurvedic institution and hospital. Visitors can tour the facility and learn about the various Ayurvedic treatments and therapies offered at the center.
  • Vaidyaratnam Ayurveda Museum - The museum is dedicated to promoting the traditional Ayurvedic system of medicine. Visitors can learn about the history of Ayurveda and the various treatments and medicines used in the system.
  • Kottakkal Juma Masjid - One of the oldest mosques in Kerala, Juma Masjid is known for its unique architecture and historical significance. The mosque is an important pilgrimage site for the Muslim community in Kerala and attracts visitors from all over the world.
  • Triprangode Siva Temple - A must-visit attraction for those interested in Indian architecture, the Triprangode Siva Temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple is an excellent example of the traditional Kerala style of temple architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kottakkal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kottakkal is also blessed with natural beauty, and visitors can explore the region’s scenic landscapes, waterfalls, and forests. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions in the town:

  • Padinjarekara Beach - The beach is located on the Malabar coast and is one of the most beautiful beaches in Kerala. Visitors can engage in water sports and enjoy the scenic views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Aruvippuram Waterfalls - The waterfall is located in a serene and peaceful location, surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can take a dip in the pool and enjoy the breathtaking views.
  • Nilambur Teak Museum and Rainforest - The rainforest is home to a variety of wildlife and flora. Visitors can take a guided tour of the forest and learn about the local ecosystem. The museum showcases the history and significance of teak production in Kerala.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kottakkal

For adventure seekers, Kottakkal is a great place for hiking and biking. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region include:

  • Charalkunnu to Kulamavu Trek - The trek offers breathtaking views of the hills and valleys of Kerala. The trek passes through several tea and spice plantations, and visitors can enjoy the scenic vistas while learning about the local produce.
  • Pookode Lake to Thusharagiri Waterfalls Trek - The trek is a great option for those looking to combine hiking and swimming. The trek passes through dense forests and ends at the Thusharagiri waterfalls. Visitors can take a dip in the pool and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Kottakkal: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in Kottakkal. Visitors can take a tour of the town and learn about the history and culture of the region. Some of the must-do activities include:

  • Ayurveda Massage and Treatment - Kottakkal is known for its Ayurveda treatments and massages. Visitors can book an appointment at one of the renowned Ayurvedic centers and experience the healing powers of Ayurveda.
  • Cultural Dance and Music Performances - The Kottakkal Palace hosts several cultural performances throughout the year. Visitors can attend these performances and enjoy traditional Indian dance and music.
  • Food and Spice Tours - Kerala is known for its spices and culinary traditions. Visitors can take a guided tour of the local spice and food markets and learn about the various spices and ingredients used in Kerala cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kottakkal

A week-long itinerary for Kottakkal can include the following:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the cultural and historical attractions of Kottakkal, including the Vaidyaratnam Ayurveda Museum and the Kottakkal Juma Masjid.
  • Day 3-4: Enjoy the scenic beauty of the region by visiting Padinjarekara Beach and Aruvippuram Waterfalls.
  • Day 5-6: Go on a hiking or biking tour of the region, such as the Charalkunnu to Kulamavu Trek or the Pookode Lake to Thusharagiri Waterfalls Trek.
  • Day 7: Try Ayurvedic massage at Kottakal Arya Vaidya Sala or attend a cultural dance and music performance at Kottakkal Palace.

Food and Drink Scene of Kottakkal

Must-Try Dishes

Kerala cuisine is famous for its use of spices and fresh ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes in Kottakkal include:

  • Appam and Stew - A famous breakfast dish, Appam is a rice pancake served with a stew made from potatoes and coconut milk.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry - Puttu is a cylindrical steamed rice cake served with spicy chickpea curry. It is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala.
  • Fish Curry and Rice - Kerala is known for its fresh seafood. Fish Curry is a spicy and tangy dish made with fresh fish, coconut milk, and traditional Indian spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sulaimani - A traditional drink made from black tea, lemon, and spices like ginger and clove.
  • Coconut Water - Fresh coconut water is available throughout the town and is an excellent way to beat the heat.

Where to Stay in Kottakkal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kottakkal has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Some of the best places to stay in the town include:

  • Arya Vaidya Sala Nilayam - A budget hotel located near the Kottakkal Ayurveda Institute.
  • Kanulia’s Stay - A mid-range guesthouse located near the Kottakkal Juma Masjid.
  • Riva Residency - A luxury hotel located near the Padinjarekara Beach.

What to Buy in Kottakkal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kottakkal is known for its Ayurvedic products, spices, and handicrafts. Some of the best things to buy in the town include:

  • Ayurvedic Oils and Medicines - The Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala produces a variety of Ayurvedic oils and medicines that are known for their healing properties.
  • Spices and Tea - Kottakkal is famous for its spices and tea. Visitors can buy high-quality spices like cardamom, black pepper, and cinnamon.
  • Handicrafts - Kottakkal is also known for its traditional handicrafts like Kathakali masks, Nettipattom (elephant decorations), and Nilavilakku (traditional oil lamps).

Local Culture and Customs of Kottakkal

Kerala culture is distinct from the rest of India and has a rich tradition of art, literature, and music. The people of Kottakkal are friendly, hospitable, and proud of their cultural heritage. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kottakkal

The best time to visit Kottakkal is between October and March when the weather is pleasant, and the humidity levels are low. This also coincides with the peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kottakkal

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local delicacies and flavours.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that harm the environment.
  • Refuse hospitality offered by locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kottakkal

Kottakkal is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors are advised to take basic precautions like keeping their valuables safe and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Kottakkal

Kottakkal is home to several Ayurvedic centers and hospitals that offer high-quality healthcare services. Visitors are advised to take basic precautions like carrying a first aid kit and following basic hygiene practices.

Getting to Kottakkal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kottakkal

The nearest airport to Kottakkal is the Calicut International Airport, which is located about 33 miles away from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Kottakkal.

Bus Stations Near Kottakkal

Kottakkal is well-connected by bus to major cities in Kerala like Malappuram, Kozhikode, and Thrissur. Visitors can take a bus from these cities to Kottakkal.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi from nearby cities or take a train to the Kottakkal Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Kottakkal

Kottakkal is a small town, and visitors can explore the town on foot. Taxis and buses are also available for those who want to travel longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Kottakkal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kottakkal is located in a beautiful region of Kerala and is surrounded by several exciting destinations. Some of the nearby places to visit include:

  • Malappuram - A cultural and historical city located about 12 miles from Kottakkal.
  • Wayanad - A scenic hill station located about 60 miles away from Kottakkal.
  • Ooty - A popular tourist destination located about 85 miles away from Kottakkal, known for its beautiful hills and tea plantations.

Conclusion

Kottakkal is a hidden gem in the beautiful region of Malabar, Kerala. The town offers visitors an opportunity to explore the traditional Indian culture, Ayurvedic treatments, and scenic landscapes. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Kottakkal and make the most of their time in this beautiful town.
