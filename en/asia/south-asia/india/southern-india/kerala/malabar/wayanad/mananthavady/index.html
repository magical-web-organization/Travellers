Introduction to Mananthavady

Mananthavady is a beautiful town located in the Wayanad district of Kerala in Southern India. It is a popular tourist destination as it is surrounded by beautiful hills and valleys that offer breath-taking scenic views. Mananthavady has a unique culture and is famous for its temple festivals, which are celebrated with great enthusiasm by the locals.

List of Places to Visit in Mananthavady

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • Pazhassi Raja Tomb: The tomb is a well-known historical attraction, which is dedicated to the Kottayam King, Pazhassi Raja. The beautiful architecture of the tomb attracts many tourists.

  • Thirunelli Temple: This historic temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is believed to be one of Kerala’s oldest temples.

  • Edakkal Caves: These caves are located about 10 kilometers from the town and are likely around 8000 years old.

  • Pakshipathalam: This hidden gem is located near the Brahmagiri Hills and is a must-visit site for nature lovers.

Natural Attractions of Mananthavady: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kabini River: Kabini river originates in Wayanad and flows through Mananthavady. It is one of the best places to enjoy kayaking and rafting in Wayanad.

  • Kuruva Island: Nestled in the middle of the Kabini River, Kuruva Island is a beautiful place to visit. It’s an oasis of serenity and breathtaking views of the Kabini river are calming.

  • Banasura Sagar Dam: The second largest earth dam in Asia, Banasura Sagar dam is located about 20 kilometers from Mananthavady. The scenic landscape surrounding the dam provides a picture-perfect setting for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mananthavady

  • Chembra Peak: The highest peak in Wayanad, Chembra Peak is a popular spot for trekkers and hikers.

  • Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary: Trekking through the sanctuary provides an opportunity to observe the native wildlife of the sanctuary, like deer, elephants, and langurs.

Best Things to Do in Mananthavady: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the traditional markets of Mananthavady to buy homemade sweets and spices that are unique to Kerala.

  • Participate in the temple festivals that are celebrated in Mananthavady. Some of the famous temple festivals are Thirunelli Festival and Tholpetty Festival.

  • Take a rural cycling tour to explore the nearby village and its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Mananthavady

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu, which is steamed rice cake, and curry is a popular breakfast dish in Mananthavady.

  • Fish Curry or Meen Curry is a must-try dish that is commonly found in Mananthavady.

  • Aviyal is a traditional Kerala dish and is an essential part of the Sadhya or lunch platter.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy, which is locally known as Kallu, is made from coconut milk and can be found in the local market of Mananthavady.

  • Cardamom Tea, also known as Chaya, it’s a popular beverage that is unique to Kerala.

Where to Stay in Mananthavady: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pepper Green Villas: With its peaceful and serene surroundings and well-appointed rooms, Pepper Green Villas is an excellent option for those looking for a tranquil stay at an affordable price.

  • Wayanad Silverwoods Resort: Magnificent vistas, luxurious amenities, and impeccable service make Wayanad Silverwoods Resort the go-to option for those who prefer an indulgent stay.

What to Buy in Mananthavady: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade soaps with natural ingredients are a must-buy souvenir.

  • Spices like cardamom, black pepper, cloves, and cinnamon are grown locally, and it’s a great opportunity to take some fresh spices back home for your family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Mananthavady

Mananthavady is a culturally rich town, and the locals are proud of their heritage. It’s essential to respect local customs and traditions, dress modestly while visiting temples, and take your shoes off before entering a temple.

Best Time to Visit Mananthavady

The best time to visit Mananthavady is from October to May when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mananthavady

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.

  • Try the local cuisine and buy local products as souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Damage natural or cultural sites of Mananthavady.

  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mananthavady

Mananthavady is relatively safe, and violent crimes are rare. However, it’s always best to take general precautions, like not going out alone at night and always locking your doors.

Health in Mananthavady

Malaria is prevalent in the region. It is recommended to take preventive measures like taking antimalarial medication and using mosquito repellent.

Getting to Mananthavady: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bus Stations Near Mananthavady

How to Get Around in Mananthavady

There are various options for moving around Mananthavady like auto-rickshaws and buses. However, renting a car or a bike can be a more comfortable and convenient option for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Mananthavady: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mysore, located about 120 kilometers from Mananthavady, is worth visiting. Home of the Mysore Palace, and the city has numerous enchanting sites such as Mysore Zoo and Brindavan Gardens.

  • Coorg is a picturesque hill station located within the Western Ghats of Karnataka, is only 40 kilometers from Wayanad.

Conclusion

Mananthavady is a hidden gem with a rich cultural heritage, tantalizing cuisine, and remarkable natural beauty. It has so much to offer for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers, making it an unforgettable destination for any traveler. Book your tickets now and create memories for a lifetime.
