Introduction to Manjeshwar

Manjeshwar, a small city located in the Kasaragod district of Kerala, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its cultural richness and natural beauty, the city gets its name from the presiding deity of the Manjeshwaram Mahadeva Temple, which is also one of the main attractions of the city.

If you are looking to experience Kerala’s distinctive blend of culture, religion and nature, Manjeshwar should definitely be on your bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Manjeshwar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manjeshwar boasts of a rich cultural heritage, with various historical and religious sites located throughout the city. The following are some of the most popular attractions:

  • Manjeshwaram Mahadeva Temple: The famous temple, known for its unique architecture, sits at the heart of the city and attracts devotees from across the country.

  • Anandashram: A spiritual center located in Kanhangad, just 20 km away from the city, that hosts daily meditation sessions and offers accommodations for those seeking a peaceful retreat.

  • Hosdurg Fort: Built-in 16th century by the Ikkeri Nayaks, the fort is an impressive structure that served as an important military stronghold.

Natural Attractions of Manjeshwar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Manjeshwar is surrounded by lush greenery and serene beauty. There are several outdoor activities and natural attractions to explore while you’re here:

  • Valiyaparamba Backwaters: Take a boat ride and soak in the tranquil beauty of the backwaters.

  • Parasinikadavu Muthappan Temple: Located on the banks for Valapattanam river, this temple is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular spot for picnics and family outings.

  • Kottekkad River: This scenic river is perfect for kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manjeshwar

Manjeshwar is surrounded by beautiful hills and mountains, making it a great location for hikers and bikers. Some of the popular trails in Manjeshwar include:

  • Kammadam – Pathy Hill: This trail takes you through hills and forests, giving you stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Manne Lion Rock: This challenging trail leads to the top of a rocky outcrop, offering panoramic views of the city and the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Manjeshwar: Activities and Tours

If you are searching for some exciting activities to do while in Manjeshwar, look no further:

  • Jeep Safari: Sign up for a jeep safari and explore the beautiful countryside and hills of Manjeshwar.

  • Fishing: Try your hand at fishing in the Kottekkad river or the Valiyaparamba backwaters.

  • Temple Tours: Manjeshwar is a popular pilgrimage site, and there are many tours available that will take you to the most important temples.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manjeshwar

If you’re planning to spend a week in Manjeshwar, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Manjeshwar

Must-Try Dishes

Kerala is famous for its spicy and flavourful cuisine, and Manjeshwar is no exception. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: This is a traditional Kerala breakfast dish made from steamed rice flour and served with a spicy chickpea curry.

  • Fish Curry: Kerala is a coastal state, and therefore seafood is a staple in their cuisine. Try the spicy and tangy fish curry with rice.

  • Appam and Stew: This is a popular breakfast dish made from fermented rice and coconut milk, and is served with a mildly spiced vegetable or meat stew.

Local Beverages

Kerala is also famous for its unique beverages. Here are some of the must-try drinks in Manjeshwar:

  • Toddy: A local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of coconut palms.

  • Kattan Chaaya: A strong and aromatic tea, perfect for a quick pick-me-up.

Where to Stay in Manjeshwar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Manjeshwar. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Green Land Residency: A budget hotel located in the heart of the city, with comfortable and clean rooms.

  • Nalanda Resort: A luxury resort located just outside the city, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Coffee Bean Homestay: This homestay is run by a local family and offers a unique and authentic experience of Kerala’s culture and hospitality.

What to Buy in Manjeshwar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for something to bring back home, Manjeshwar offers a variety of souvenirs and local products. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Spices: Kerala is known for its distinctive spices, such as black pepper, cardamom and cloves. These make for great souvenirs to take back home and use in your cooking.

  • Handicrafts: Local artisans create beautiful handicrafts made from coconut shells, wood and cane.

Local Culture and Customs of Manjeshwar

Manjeshwar is home to a variety of people from different religions and cultures. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: When visiting places of worship, make sure you dress modestly and cover your shoulders and knees.

  • Respect: Respect the local culture and customs; ask permission before taking photos of people and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Manjeshwar

The best time to visit Manjeshwar is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and conducive to outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manjeshwar

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Respect local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural surroundings.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manjeshwar

Manjeshwar is a safe and peaceful city. However, it’s always wise to take precautions:

  • Don’t venture out alone at night.

  • Take care of your belongings.

Health in Manjeshwar

Kerala has a high incidence of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria, so it’s essential to take precautions while traveling. Bring mosquito repellent and take measures to avoid getting bitten.

Getting to Manjeshwar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manjeshwar

The nearest airport to Manjeshwar is Mangalore Airport, which is about 80km away.

Bus Stations Near Manjeshwar

There are several state-run and private bus services that operate to and from Manjeshwar.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Manjeshwar.

How to Get Around in Manjeshwar

The best way to get around Manjeshwar is by renting a bike or scooter, which can be easily arranged at most hotels and homestays. Taxis and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Manjeshwar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Manjeshwar is well connected to other popular destinations in Kerala, such as Kasaragod, Bekal, and Kannur. You can also explore the beautiful beaches of Goa, which is just a few hours’ drive away.

Conclusion

Manjeshwar is a city that you should experience at least once in your lifetime. With its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, it offers a unique and unforgettable adventure. Whether you are looking for a spiritual retreat, outdoor adventure or a cultural experience, Manjeshwar has something for everyone.
