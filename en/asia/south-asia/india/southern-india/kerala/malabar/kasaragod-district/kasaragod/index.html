Introduction to Kasaragod

Kasaragod, located in the Malabar region of Kerala, in Southern India, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its pristine beaches, historical sites, and a rich cultural heritage, Kasaragod offers an unparalleled experience to visitors.

The land of Kasaragod is bordered by the vast Arabian Sea to the west and the Western Ghats to the east, offering an unspoiled natural beauty that is hard to find elsewhere. The locals here speak a unique mix of Malayalam, Kannada, and Tulu, and the blend of different cultures in this region is fascinating to explore.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll dive right into the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Kasaragod

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bekal Fort - The largest and most well-preserved fort in Kerala, built in the 17th century by Tipu Sultan.
  2. Chandragiri Fort - Built in the 17th century by Sivappa Nayak, this fort is perched atop a hill and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding areas.
  3. Bela Church - A historic church that was established in the 16th century, this is a great place to marvel at the intricate architecture and to learn about Christianity in Kasaragod.

Natural Attractions of Kasaragod: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kappil Beach - A serene and secluded beach with turquoise waters and soft sand.
  2. Ranipuram - Also known as the Ooty of Kerala, this hill station is surrounded by dense forests and offers a breathtaking view of the Western Ghats.
  3. Bekal Beach - A picturesque beach surrounded by coconut groves, where you can relax and take a stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kasaragod

  1. Madayipara - A scenic landscape located in the heart of Kasaragod which offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the Western Ghats.
  2. Kottancheri Hills - An adventurous trekking route located near the historic Bekal Fort with panoramic views of the Arabian Sea.
  3. Nileshwar - A small village located on the banks of the Kariangode River, known for its rugged terrains and scenic beauty, and is perfect for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Kasaragod: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the traditional theyyam dance performance, a unique ritual art form that is performed in the temples and shrines of Kasaragod.
  2. Explore the backwaters of Kasaragod on a houseboat which offers an immersive experience of the traditional lifestyle of the locals.
  3. Learn about the different tribes in Kasaragod and their culture through the “Kavu” tour, an initiative by the government to promote eco-tourism in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Kasaragod

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Malabar Biriyani - A flavorful biriyani dish made with a blend of spices, rice, and meat.
  2. Fish Curry - A quintessential dish of Kasaragod, typically made with local fish and coconut milk.
  3. Kadala Curry - A savory curry made with chickpeas and spices, usually eaten with appam or puttu.

Local Beverages

  1. Tender Coconut Water - A refreshing drink made from fresh coconuts, perfect for hot summer days.
  2. Chaya - A sweet and milky tea with a hint of spices, usually served with a snack on the side.
  3. Palm Wine - A local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.

Where to Stay in Kasaragod: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Bekal Fort Homestay - Located near the historic Bekal Fort, this homestay offers an authentic experience of the local lifestyle with home-cooked meals and personalized attention.
  2. Kappil Beach Resort - A luxury resort located on the serene Kappil Beach which offers an immersive experience of the natural beauty of Kasaragod.
  3. Chandragiri Fort Heritage Resort - A historic resort located near the Chandragiri Fort, equipped with modern amenities and a stunning view of the surrounding areas.

What to Buy in Kasaragod: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kasaragod Sarees - A traditional saree unique to Kasaragod, with intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  2. Kasaragod Pepper - Known for its quality and aroma, Kasaragod Pepper is a must-buy for spice lovers.
  3. Kasaragod Cashews - Fresh and locally sourced cashews that are perfect for snacking or to take back home as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Kasaragod

The locals in Kasaragod have a unique blend of cultures, with influences from Kannada, Malayalam, and Tulu. The people here are warm and welcoming, and their traditional rituals and art forms are fascinating to explore. The theyyam dance performance is a must-see, and the colorful attire and headdresses of the performers are a sight to behold.

Best Time to Visit Kasaragod

The best time to visit Kasaragod is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The monsoon season from June to September is also a great time to visit if you enjoy lush green landscapes and peaceful backwaters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kasaragod

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious places and shrines.
  2. Try the local cuisine and learn about the different spices and ingredients used in the dishes.
  3. Engage with the locals and learn about their culture and heritage.

Don’t:

  1. Do not disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Do not wear revealing clothing when visiting religious places and shrines.
  3. Do not litter and keep the surroundings clean and green.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kasaragod

Kasaragod is a safe and welcoming place for tourists, and crime rates are relatively low. However, it is always better to be cautious and avoid traveling at night to remote areas. Always carry identification and avoid confronting anyone who appears to be suspicious.

Health in Kasaragod

The healthcare infrastructure in Kasaragod is well-established, and there are several hospitals and clinics available. It is always advisable to carry a basic medical kit with essentials like painkillers, antiseptics, and band-aids. It is also recommended to get vaccinated against water-borne and mosquito-borne diseases, as these are prevalent in the region.

Getting to Kasaragod: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kasaragod

  1. Calicut International Airport - Located about 200 km from Kasaragod, this airport is well-connected to major cities in India and the Middle East.
  2. Mangalore International Airport - Located about 60 km from Kasaragod, this airport is well-connected to major cities in India and the Middle East.

Bus Stations Near Kasaragod

  1. Kasaragod Bus Stand - Located in the heart of the city, this bus stand is well-connected to major cities in Kerala and Karnataka.
  2. Mangalore Bus Stand - Located about 60 km from Kasaragod, this bus stand is well-connected to major cities in the region.

Other Options

Private taxis and cars can also be hired from nearby cities like Mangalore and Kannur. The roads are well-maintained, and self-drive car rentals are also available.

How to Get Around in Kasaragod

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are readily available in Kasaragod, and they are a convenient way to get around the city. Buses are also available, and they are the cheapest mode of transportation. For a more immersive experience, cycle rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Kasaragod: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kannur - Known for its beautiful beaches and historic sites, Kannur is located just a few hours from Kasaragod.
  2. Wayanad - Known for its rolling hills, beautiful waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries, Wayanad is located a few hours from Kasaragod.
  3. Coorg - Known for its coffee plantations, lush green hills, and wildlife, Coorg is a popular getaway destination from Kasaragod.

Conclusion

Kasaragod is a destination that has something for everyone - from history buffs to nature enthusiasts. With its scenic beauty, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality, Kasaragod is a must-visit destination in Kerala. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Kasaragod.
