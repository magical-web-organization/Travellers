Kasaragod District Travel Guide

Kasaragod District is located in the Malabar region of Kerala, Southern India. Known for its beautiful landscapes and rich history, Kasaragod is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic Indian experience. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the must-see attractions, best things to do, where to stay, local food and drink, and more to help you plan your trip to Kasaragod District.

List of Places to Visit in Kasaragod District

Kasaragod is home to a wide range of attractions that cater to travelers of all interests. From historical sites to natural wonders, here are some of the best places to visit in Kasaragod District.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bekal Fort: Built in the 17th century, this historic fort is one of the most popular attractions in Kasaragod District.
  • Ananthapura Lake Temple: This ancient temple is believed to be the original seat of Lord Anantha, the deity of Lord Vishnu.
  • Madhur Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is known for its stunning architecture and intricate wood carvings.
  • Malik Dinar Mosque: This historic mosque is believed to have been built by Malik Ibn Dinar, a famous Arab preacher.

Natural Attractions of Kasaragod District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kappil Beach: This picturesque beach offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea, and is an ideal spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Chandragiri Fort: This scenic fort is located atop a hill, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Ranipuram: This lush hill station offers excellent opportunities for hiking and trekking.
  • Valiyaparamba Backwaters: These tranquil backwaters offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kasaragod District

Kasaragod District is home to several scenic hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Some of the best routes include:

  • Bekal Fort Trail: This scenic trail starts at the base of Bekal Fort and winds through lush greenery and historic landmarks.
  • Kottancheri Hills Trail: This challenging trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is ideal for experienced hikers.
  • Chandragiri-Palakkunnu Trail: This scenic trail takes visitors through the tranquil village of Palakkunnu and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Kasaragod District: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Kasaragod District has something to offer every traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in the region.

  • Take a boat ride on the Valiyaparamba backwaters and explore the region’s rich flora and fauna.
  • Attend a local Kathakali dance performance to learn more about the region’s vibrant performing arts scene.
  • Join a guided tour of the historic Bekal Fort and learn about the region’s rich history.
  • Relax on the pristine beaches of Kasaragod and soak up the sun and sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kasaragod District

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kasaragod, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time.

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel or guesthouse. Day 2: Visit the historic Bekal Fort, followed by a relaxing boat ride on the Valiyaparamba backwaters. Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Madhur Temple, followed by a visit to the Kappil Beach. Day 4: Explore the scenic Chandragiri fort and hike along the Chandragiri-Palakkunnu Trail. Day 5: Enjoy a local Kathakali dance performance in the evening. Day 6: Visit the beautiful Ranipuram hill station and hike along the Kottancheri Hills Trail. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Kasaragod District

Kasaragod District is known for its delicious cuisine, which is a fusion of Kerala’s traditional flavors and local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu: This traditional breakfast dish is a steamed rice and coconut cake, served with spicy curry.
  • Fish molee: This delicate fish curry is made with coconut milk and a blend of spices.
  • Malabar Biriyani: This flavorful biriyani is made with fragrant spices and tender meat or vegetables.
  • Toddy: This local alcoholic beverage is made from the sap of palm trees and is a popular drink among locals.

Local Beverages

  • Filter Kaapi: This strong and aromatic coffee is a staple in Kerala’s cuisine.
  • Sambharam: This refreshing drink is made with yogurt, water, and spices, and is a great way to beat the heat.

Where to Stay in Kasaragod District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kasaragod District offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Here are some options to consider:

  • Taj Bekal Resort & Spa: This luxurious resort is located near the Bekal Fort and offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Kanan Beach Resort: This beachfront resort offers modern amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Zostel: This popular hostel chain offers affordable and comfortable dormitories and private rooms.
  • Homestays: Many locals offer homestays, providing visitors with a unique opportunity to experience Kerala’s warmth and hospitality firsthand.

What to Buy in Kasaragod District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kasaragod District is known for its local handicrafts and unique products. Here are some souvenirs to bring home.

  • Kasaragod sarees: These beautiful hand-woven sarees are made with a blend of cotton and silk.
  • Nettipattom: These ornamental elephant headdresses are a popular decoration in many homes in Kerala.
  • Cashew nuts: Kasaragod is known for producing some of the best cashew nuts in the world

Local Culture and Customs of Kasaragod District

As a predominantly Muslim region, Kasaragod has a unique culture and set of customs. Visitors should respect local dress codes and customs and avoid consuming alcohol in public.

Best Time to Visit Kasaragod District

The best time to visit Kasaragod District is between October and April, when the weather is comfortable and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kasaragod District

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect local wildlife and nature.
  • Tip service staff where appropriate

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public spaces.
  • Disrespect local religious and cultural customs.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Photograph people without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kasaragod District

Kasaragod District is generally safe for travelers, but visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid petty theft and scams.

Health in Kasaragod District

Visitors should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and waterborne illnesses. It is recommended to bring bug repellent and only drink bottled or filtered water.

Getting to Kasaragod District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kasaragod District is easily accessible by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near Kasaragod District

The nearest airport to Kasaragod District is the Mangalore International Airport, which is located approximately 64 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kasaragod District

Rajiv Gandhi Bus Terminal is the main bus station in Kasaragod, which connects the district to other major cities in Kerala and Karnataka.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Kasaragod District

Kasaragod District is well-connected by buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws. Visitors can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the region independently.

Where to Go Next from Kasaragod District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kasaragod District is surrounded by several popular destinations, making it an ideal starting point for further exploration. Some nearby destinations to consider include:

  • Coorg: Located just across the border in Karnataka, Coorg is known for its lush green forests and scenic waterfalls.
  • Wayanad: This scenic hill station is located in the neighboring district of Kerala, and is known for its tea plantations and wildlife reserves.

Conclusion

Kasaragod District is a hidden gem in Southern India, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to explore all that the region has to offer. So pack your bags, book a trip, and discover the wonders of Kasaragod District for yourself.
