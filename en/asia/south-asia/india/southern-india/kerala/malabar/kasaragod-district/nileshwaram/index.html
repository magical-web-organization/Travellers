Nileshwaram, located in the Kasaragod District of Malabar in Kerala, is a charming town that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. With beautiful scenery, fascinating history and culture, and an array of outdoor activities, Nileshwaram is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Nileshwaram.

Introduction to Nileshwaram

Nileshwaram is a small town located in the northernmost district of Kerala. The town is known for its cultural heritage and scenic beauty. Nestled amidst lush greenery and coconut groves, Nileshwaram offers a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle and customs of the local people of Malabar. The town is easily accessible by road and is well connected to the rest of Kerala.

List of Places to Visit in Nileshwaram

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nileshwaram is home to a number of museums and historical places that give visitors insight into the region’s rich cultural history. Some must-visit places include:

  • Kavil Bhavan Yoga and Cultural Center: This center is a hub for various cultural activities, including music, dance, and yoga. Visitors can participate in cultural events or attend yoga classes that are conducted here.
  • Nileshwaram Palace: This palace was once the residence of the rulers of Nileshwaram. It is now a heritage site and a museum that displays antique furniture, woodcarvings, and other artifacts.
  • Theyyam Performance: Theyyam is a traditional ritualistic dance form that is performed in many temples across the Malabar region. Visitors can experience the colorful costumes and unique dance moves of this captivating performance.

Natural Attractions of Nileshwaram: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Nileshwaram has plenty of outdoor activities and scenic vistas. Here are some outdoor attractions that visitors should add to their itinerary:

  • Anandashram: This ashram is located on a hilltop and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can enjoy the peaceful atmosphere and participate in yoga and meditation sessions.
  • Nileshwaram River: The river that flows through the town offers a scenic boat ride. Visitors can also enjoy fishing or picnic on the riverbanks.
  • Kottancheri Hills: Located 3 km from Nileshwaram, these hills offer a great trekking experience. The views from the top are breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nileshwaram

Nileshwaram is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are a number of trails that lead through forests, hilltops, and villages. Some recommended routes include:

  • Nileshwaram to Kottancheri Hills: This scenic route takes you through villages and paddy fields before reaching the hills. The trail is perfect for beginners and intermediate-level hikers.
  • Nileshwaram to Mavilakadapara: This trail is ideal for biking enthusiasts. The path leads through scenic landscapes and provides an excellent opportunity to explore the nearby villages.

Best Things to Do in Nileshwaram: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Nileshwaram has a plethora of fun activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some must-try activities include:

  • Theyyam Tour: Visitors can participate in a guided tour of Theyyam performances that take place in various temples in the region.
  • Spice Plantation Tour: The region is known for its spice plantations. Visitors can take a guided tour of the spice gardens and learn about the different spices and herbs grown here.
  • Yoga and Wellness Retreats: Nileshwaram is an ideal destination for yoga enthusiasts. Visitors can take part in yoga and wellness retreats conducted by experienced instructors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nileshwaram

To make the most of your visit to Nileshwaram, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Nileshwaram and check into your hotel. Day 2: Visit the Nileshwaram Palace and go on a guided tour of the museum. In the evening, attend a Theyyam performance. Day 3: Take a yoga class at the Kavil Bhavan Yoga and Cultural Center. Afternoon, visit Anandashram and watch the sunset from the hilltop. Day 4: Visit a spice plantation and take a guided tour of the gardens. Day 5: Go for a hike to Kottancheri Hills. Day 6: Visit nearby beaches and enjoy water sports activities. Day 7: Depart from Nileshwaram.

Food and Drink Scene of Nileshwaram

The food and drink scene in Nileshwaram mainly revolves around traditional Kerala cuisine. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: This is a popular breakfast dish in Kerala. Puttu is a steamed cylinder-shaped rice cake, and kadala curry is a spicy curry that is made with black chickpeas.
  • Appam and Stew: Appam is a type of pancake made with fermented rice batter, and stew is a mild, creamy coconut milk-based curry that is usually made with vegetables or meat.
  • Fish Curry: Nileshwaram is located near the coast and is known for its seafood. Fish curry is a staple dish in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: Toddy is a local alcoholic drink made from the sap of the coconut tree.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is available throughout Nileshwaram and is a refreshing beverage.

Where to Stay in Nileshwaram: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nileshwaram has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some recommended places to stay include:

  • Kandath Tharavad Heritage Resort: This heritage resort is located in a 300-year-old traditional Kerala house and offers a glimpse into the local culture and lifestyle.
  • Shanthi Kunnj Homestay: This homestay offers comfortable accommodation, home-cooked meals, and a warm and friendly atmosphere.
  • Malabar Ocean Front Resort and Spa: This luxury resort is located near the beach and offers a range of facilities and amenities, including a spa and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Nileshwaram: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nileshwaram is known for its handicrafts and local products. Some must-buy items include:

  • Coir Products: Coir products are made from coconut fibers and are popular in the region. Visitors can buy coir rugs, mats, and other household items.
  • Handicrafts: Handmade brass items, wooden crafts, and pottery are some of the handicrafts that visitors can buy in Nileshwaram.

Local Culture and Customs of Nileshwaram

Nileshwaram is a town that is deeply rooted in traditional culture and customs. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs and dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Nileshwaram

The best time to visit Nileshwaram is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and the humidity is low. However, the town is also beautiful during the monsoon season (June to September) when the surroundings are lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nileshwaram

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Buy local handicrafts and products.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activity that harms the environment.
  • Crumple, damage or otherwise disrespect currency as it is against Indian law.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nileshwaram

Nileshwaram is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take normal precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings. Avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Nileshwaram

Visitors should take extra care to avoid mosquito bites to minimize the risk of mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and malaria. It is also recommended that visitors drink only bottled or filtered water to prevent water-borne diseases.

Getting to Nileshwaram: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nileshwaram is well-connected to the rest of Kerala by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Nileshwaram

The nearest airport to Nileshwaram is Mangalore International Airport, which is about 70 km away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Nileshwaram from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Nileshwaram

Nileshwaram has a well-developed bus system that connects it to other parts of Kerala. The nearest bus stations to Nileshwaram are in Kasaragod and Kannur.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a taxi or hire a car to reach Nileshwaram from nearby cities like Mangalore or Kannur.

How to Get Around in Nileshwaram

Nileshwaram is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Nileshwaram: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nileshwaram is well-situated for travelers that want to explore other parts of Kerala. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Bekal Fort: This 17th-century fort is located about 25 km from Nileshwaram and is a popular tourist attraction.
  • Kannur: This historic port town offers a range of cultural and historical attractions, including temples, museums, and beaches.
  • Wayanad: This lush, green district is known for its wildlife, coffee plantations, and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Nileshwaram is a town that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. From traditional museums and historical places to scenic hikes and outdoor adventures, Nileshwaram has something for everyone. Visitors to this charming town will be captivated by its beauty and culture, making it an excellent destination for an off-the-beaten-track experience.
