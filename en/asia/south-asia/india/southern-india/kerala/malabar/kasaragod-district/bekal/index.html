Introduction to Bekal

Bekal is a charming coastal town located in Kasaragod District, in the Malabar region of Kerala, India. This quiet town is known for its stunning beaches, serene backwaters, and ancient fortifications. It’s an ideal destination for travelers looking for a peaceful getaway with a touch of history and culture. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know about Bekal, including the best places to visit, delicious local cuisine, and tips for a safe and enjoyable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Bekal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bekal’s rich cultural heritage is reflected in its many historical sites and museums. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Bekal are:

  • Bekal Fort: This ancient fort located on the shoreline of Bekal Beach is one of the largest and best-preserved forts in Kerala. It was built by Shivappa Nayaka of the Keladi dynasty in the 17th century and is a perfect example of ancient Indian architecture. Visitors can explore the fort’s massive walls, underground tunnels, and observation towers while enjoying breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Ananthapura Lake Temple: This is the only lake temple in Kerala and is said to be the original seat of Lord Anantha, the serpent god. The temple is situated on an island in the middle of a lake and is accessible only by boat. The tranquil surroundings and stunning architecture make it a perfect spot for a peaceful escape from the city.
  • Bekal Beach Park: This recently developed park is a popular attraction for families and nature lovers. It has a beautiful garden, children’s play area, and an amphitheater for cultural events. Visitors can also enjoy stunning views of the Arabian Sea from the park.

Natural Attractions of Bekal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bekal’s pristine beaches, lush green countryside, and tranquil backwaters offer visitors endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Bekal are:

  • Kappil Beach: This secluded beach is located about 6 km from Bekal town and is famous for its natural beauty and tranquil atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and stunning views of the sea.
  • Valiyaparamba Backwaters: These serene backwaters are located near Bekal and offer visitors a chance to explore the tranquil countryside while cruising on a houseboat. Visitors can also try fishing and kayaking in the backwaters.
  • Ranipuram: This breathtaking hill station is located about 85 km from Bekal and offers visitors a chance to enjoy scenic trekking trails, waterfalls, and natural beauty at its best. Visitors can also enjoy birdwatching and camping in this serene location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bekal

Bekal offers visitors some of the best hiking and biking trails in Kerala. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  • Bekal Fort to Kappil Beach: This scenic trail offers visitors a chance to explore the historic Bekal Fort and enjoy stunning views of the Arabian Sea before reaching the tranquil Kappil Beach.
  • Bekal to Kanhangad: This cycling route takes visitors through the lush green countryside of Bekal and offers stunning views of the backwaters and paddy fields.
  • Bekal to Ranipuram: This challenging hiking trail takes visitors through dense forests, cascading waterfalls, and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats.

Best Things to Do in Bekal: Activities and Tours

Bekal offers visitors a wide range of fun activities and tours to enjoy during their stay. Here are some popular options:

  • Houseboat cruise in Valiyaparamba Backwaters: This is a must-do activity for visitors, and it’s a great way to explore the tranquil waters of Valiyaparamba while enjoying stunning views of the countryside.
  • Ayurveda spa treatments: Bekal is famous for its ayurvedic spa treatments, which are designed to relax the mind and rejuvenate the body. Visitors can enjoy a variety of treatments, including massages, yoga, and meditation.
  • Traditional fishing experience: Visitors can try fishing using traditional Chinese fishing nets in the serene backwaters of Bekal. This is a great way to experience the local fishing culture and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bekal

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help visitors make the most of their stay in Bekal:

Day 1: Arrive in Bekal and check-in to your hotel. Relax and unwind at your hotel or take a stroll along Bekal Beach.

Day 2: Explore the historic Bekal Fort and enjoy stunning views of the Arabian Sea. Visit Ananthapura Lake Temple and enjoy a peaceful boat ride in the tranquil lake.

Day 3: Take a houseboat cruise in Valiyaparamba Backwaters and enjoy the serene natural beauty of the place.

Day 4: Take a trip to Ranipuram and enjoy a day trekking and exploring the scenic beauty of the Western Ghats.

Day 5: Relax and rejuvenate with ayurvedic spa treatments and massages.

Day 6: Try your hand at traditional fishing and learn about the local fishing culture.

Day 7: Enjoy a day trip to nearby attractions like Coorg or Mysore.

Food and Drink Scene of Bekal

Bekal’s food and drink scene is rich and diverse, with a wide range of local delicacies and beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Bekal:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Malabar Biryani: This is a flavorful rice dish made with aromatic spices, chicken or mutton, and vegetables. It’s a local delicacy that’s a must-try for visitors.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Puttu is a steamed rice cake with grated coconut, and kadala curry is a spicy curry made with black chickpeas. It’s a famous breakfast dish in Kerala and a must-try for visitors.
  • Appam and Stew: Appam is a fluffy rice pancake, and stew is a fragrant curry made with potatoes, carrots, and coconut milk. It’s a delicious and healthy snack that’s popular in Kerala.

Local Beverages

  • Toddy: Also known as palm wine, toddy is a popular local beverage made from the sap of coconut palms. It’s a refreshing and unique drink that’s a must-try in Bekal.
  • Sulaimani: This is a refreshing and aromatic black tea infused with lemon, cardamom, and other spices. It’s a popular post-meal drink in Kerala and a must-try for visitors.

Where to Stay in Bekal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bekal offers visitors a variety of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Here are some popular places to stay in Bekal:

  • Taj Bekal Resort & Spa: This is a luxurious five-star resort located on the banks of Kappil Beach. It offers visitors an unmatched experience of comfort and luxury.
  • Nirvana @ Bekal: This is a stylish and modern hostel located near Bekal Fort. It’s a great budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Bekal Palace Resorts: This is a cozy and comfortable guesthouse located near Bekal Beach. It’s a perfect option for families and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Bekal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bekal offers visitors a unique shopping experience, with a wide range of local products and souvenirs to choose from. Here are some popular items to buy in Bekal:

  • Spices: Kerala is famous for its spices, and Bekal is no exception. Visitors can buy a variety of spices like black pepper, nutmeg, and cardamom, which are grown locally.
  • Handicrafts: Bekal is known for its handwoven textiles and handicrafts. Visitors can buy products like cotton sarongs, hand-embroidered bags, and wooden crafts.
  • Ayurvedic products: Bekal is famous for its ayurvedic treatments, and visitors can buy ayurvedic products like massage oils, herbal teas, and aromatic candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Bekal

Bekal has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be aware of the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Remove footwear while entering temples and other religious sites.
  • Revere local customs and traditions and seek permission before taking photographs.
  • Use your right hand for eating, and avoid using your left hand.

Best Time to Visit Bekal

The best time to visit Bekal is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. The monsoon season, from June to September, can be unpredictable and may interfere with outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bekal

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Tip hotel staff and guides.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Try local crafts and products.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Use your left hand for eating.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bekal

Bekal is a safe and friendly town for visitors, but it’s always advisable to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Do not venture into unknown areas.
  • Be aware of pickpockets and keep your valuables safe.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated while outdoors.

Health in Bekal

Visitors should take precautions to stay healthy while in Bekal. Here are some tips:

  • Drink only bottled water, and avoid street food.
  • Protect yourself against mosquito bites.
  • Get a travel health insurance policy before arriving in Bekal.
  • Carry a small first aid kit with essential medications.

Getting to Bekal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bekal

The nearest airport to Bekal is Mangalore International Airport, which is about 70 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Bekal.

Bus Stations Near Bekal

Bekal has a well-connected bus station that connects it to nearby cities and towns. Visitors can take a bus from Mangalore, Kasaragod, or Kannur to reach Bekal.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bekal by train. The nearest railway station is in Kasaragod, which is about 16 km away.

How to Get Around in Bekal

Visitors can get around Bekal by taxi, autorickshaw, or rental bikes. Bikes are a popular and convenient mode of transport in Bekal and can be rented from local vendors.

Where to Go Next from Bekal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bekal is well connected to nearby destinations, making it a great base for exploring other parts of Kerala. Here are some popular destinations to visit:

  • Coorg: This beautiful hill station in Karnataka is located about 180 km from Bekal and offers stunning treks, waterfalls, and plantations.
  • Mysore: This historic city is located about 200 km from Bekal and offers visitors a chance to explore ancient palaces, temples, and museums.
  • Kannur: This vibrant coastal town is located about 85 km from Bekal and offers visitors a chance to enjoy stunning beaches, temples, and local cuisine.

Conclusion

Bekal is a charming and peaceful coastal town that offers visitors a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its picturesque beaches, serene backwaters, and ancient fortifications, it’s an ideal destination for travelers looking for a quiet getaway. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to Bekal, Kerala, and makes your visit a safe and memorable one.
