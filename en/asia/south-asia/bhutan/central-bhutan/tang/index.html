Introduction to Tang

Nestled in the heart of Central Bhutan, Tang is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming hospitality of the friendly locals. Unlike many popular tourist destinations, Tang remains largely untouched by the modern world, providing a rare glimpse of the traditional way of life in rural Bhutan. Surrounded by misty mountains, verdant forests, and pristine rivers, Tang is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Moreover, this quaint town is also home to several must-visit museums and historical landmarks that showcase the country’s fascinating history and artistic traditions.

In this comprehensive guide to Tang, we’ll take you on a journey of discovery through this enchanting region of Bhutan, highlighting the top attractions, best hikes and biking routes, local food and drink scene, where to stay, how to stay safe and healthy, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Tang

Here are some of the top places to visit in Tang:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ugyen Choling Palace Museum: This beautiful 19th-century palace-turned-museum is a must-visit destination for those who want to learn more about the history, culture, and lifestyle of the Bhutanese aristocracy. The museum displays a vast collection of artifacts, including weapons, costumes, furniture, and religious objects, providing a glimpse into the lavish lifestyle of the Bhutanese nobles.

  • Tang Rimochen Lhakhang: Located on a hill overlooking the Tang valley, this ancient temple is one of the oldest in Bhutan, dating back to the 8th century. The temple houses several ancient relics, including a golden statue of Guru Rinpoche, and offers panoramic views of the valley below.

  • Chume Weaving Centre: This traditional weaving center is a fascinating place to visit for those interested in textiles and weaving. The center, run entirely by local women, produces some of the finest hand-woven fabrics in the country.

Natural Attractions of Tang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bumthang Owl Trek: This three-day hike is a great way to explore the stunning landscapes and diverse wildlife of the Bumthang Wildlife Sanctuary. The trek takes you through pristine forests, across rugged mountain passes, and past crystal-clear streams, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Keep an eye out for the elusive Himalayan owls that inhabit the region.

  • Tang Heritage Trail: This easy walk takes you through the quaint villages and lush farmlands of the Tang valley, providing a glimpse into the traditional way of life in rural Bhutan. You’ll also visit several ancient temples and monasteries along the way, including the Tang Rimochen Lhakhang.

  • Ura Valley: Located just a short drive from Tang, the Ura Valley is one of the most picturesque valleys in Bhutan. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains and dotted with colorful prayer flags and traditional farmhouses, the Ura Valley offers a serene retreat from the hustle and bustle of modern life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tang

Tang is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of trails and paths that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Tang:

  • Chamkhar River Trail: This relatively easy hike takes you along the banks of the Chamkhar river, through lush forests and secluded villages, offering picturesque views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Tang Valley Loop Trail: This scenic bike ride takes you through the heart of the Tang valley, past ancient temples, weaving centers, and picturesque farmhouses. You’ll also have the opportunity to sample some of the local cuisine and interact with the friendly locals along the way.

  • Bumthang Cultural Trek: This challenging trek takes you through some of the most remote and beautiful landscapes in Bhutan, including the pristine forests and rugged mountain passes of the Bumthang Wildlife Sanctuary. The trek also gives you an opportunity to interact with the rural communities and learn about their traditions and customs.

Best Things to Do in Tang: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Tang:

  • Explore Local Villages: Take a leisurely stroll through the traditional villages of Tang, and get a taste of the local way of life. You’ll have the opportunity to interact with the friendly locals, sample some of the delicious local cuisine, and learn about their rich cultural heritage.

  • Indulge in Local Cuisine: Bhutanese cuisine is famous for its unique blend of spicy and savory flavors. Don’t miss the opportunity to taste some of the local dishes, such as Ema Datshi (spicy cheese and chili stew), Kewa Datshi (potato and cheese stew), and Jasha Maru (spicy chicken stew).

  • Attend Local Festivals: Bhutan is famous for its colorful festivals, which celebrate the country’s rich cultural heritage and religious traditions. Tang is no exception, with several festivals held throughout the year, featuring traditional dance performances, music, and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Tang

Bhutanese cuisine is a unique blend of spicy and savory flavors, influenced by the country’s location between India and China. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Tang:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: This spicy cheese and chili stew is a staple of Bhutanese cuisine, and a must-try dish in Tang.

  • Kewa Datshi: Made with potatoes and cheese, this dish is a hearty and delicious comfort food that’s perfect for the chilly mountain climate of Tang.

  • Jasha Maru: This spicy chicken stew is a favorite among locals, and is typically served with rice or noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Suja: This traditional butter tea is a staple of Bhutanese culture, and is typically served with breakfast or during social gatherings.

  • Ara: This locally brewed rice wine is a popular beverage in Bhutan, and is often served during festivals or celebrations.

Where to Stay in Tang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tang offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the top options:

  • Ugyen Choling Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located within walking distance of the Ugyen Choling Palace Museum, and offers comfortable rooms and delicious local cuisine.

  • Jakar Village Lodge: This family-run lodge is located in the heart of the Tang valley, and offers cozy rooms and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Amankora Bumthang: This luxurious resort is located just a short drive from Tang, and offers world-class amenities, including a spa, restaurant, and bar.

What to Buy in Tang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tang is known for its high-quality textiles, handmade crafts, and traditional Bhutanese souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to buy in Tang:

  • Yathra: This hand-woven textile is made from the wool of the local sheep, and is renowned for its intricate patterns and vibrant colors.

  • Handicrafts: Tang is home to several talented artisans who produce a wide range of handicrafts, including pottery, wood carvings, and jewelry.

  • Traditional Bhutanese Clothing: You can buy traditional Bhutanese clothing, including the famous gho and kira, in Tang’s many markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Tang

Bhutanese culture is deeply rooted in Buddhist traditions and values, and is characterized by a strong sense of community and respect for nature. Visitors to Tang should be aware of the following customs and traditions:

  • Dress Code: Bhutanese people have a strict dress code, particularly when visiting religious sites. Visitors are required to wear clothing that covers their arms and legs, and must remove their shoes before entering temples and other sacred places.

  • Food and Drink: Bhutanese people take great pride in their cuisine, and visitors should be prepared to try new dishes and flavors. It’s also customary to offer a small amount of food or drink to guests as a sign of hospitality.

  • Respect for Nature: Bhutanese people have a deep respect for nature, and visitors should take care not to disturb the environment or wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Tang

The best time to visit Tang is during the dry season, from October to May. During this time, the weather is pleasant and mild, and the skies are clear, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. However, if you’re interested in experiencing the local festivals, such as the Tang Festival held in February, you should plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Tang:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Bhutanese people take their customs and traditions very seriously, and visitors should be respectful of their values and beliefs.

  • Try New Foods: Bhutanese cuisine is delicious and unique, and visitors should be open to trying new dishes and flavors.

  • Explore the Outdoors: Tang is blessed with stunning natural beauty, and visitors should take advantage of the hiking and biking trails to explore the surrounding countryside.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Religious Sites: Bhutanese people hold their religious sites in high regard, and visitors should avoid behaving inappropriately or disrespectfully.

  • Litter or Pollute: Bhutan is known for its pristine environment, and visitors should take care not to litter or pollute the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Bhutan is a safe and peaceful country, with a low crime rate and a strong sense of community. However, visitors should take the following precautions to ensure their safety and health:

Safety in Tang

  • Altitude Sickness: Tang is located at a high altitude, and visitors may experience altitude sickness. It’s important to stay hydrated, get plenty of rest, and avoid strenuous activities until you acclimate to the altitude.

  • Natural Hazards: Tang is prone to natural hazards such as landslides and flash floods, particularly during the monsoon season. Visitors should stay informed of local weather conditions and take appropriate precautions.

Health in Tang

  • Vaccinations: Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date on their routine vaccinations before traveling to Bhutan. Depending on your travel plans, additional vaccinations may be recommended, such as hepatitis A and typhoid.

  • Food and Water Safety: Visitors should be cautious when consuming food and water in Bhutan, and should avoid tap water and street food. It’s recommended to drink bottled water and eat at reputable restaurants.

Getting to Tang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tang is located in the central Bhutan region, and can be reached by road or air. The nearest airport is located in Paro, and there are also several bus and taxi services available from the nearby towns.

Airports Near Tang

  • Paro International Airport: Located approximately 215 kilometers from Tang, Paro Airport is the only international airport in Bhutan, and serves as the main gateway to the country.

Bus Stations Near Tang

  • Bumthang Bus Station: Located in Jakar, approximately 11 kilometers from Tang, the Bumthang Bus Station offers regular bus services to other major towns in Bhutan, including Thimphu and Punakha.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: Visitors can also hire a private taxi to travel from Paro or other nearby towns to Tang. This is the most convenient option, but also the most expensive.

How to Get Around in Tang

Tang is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, visitors can also hire a taxi or bike to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Tang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tang is located in the heart of Bhutan, and many other popular destinations are within easy reach. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Jakar: Known as the “Switzerland of Bhutan,” Jakar is located just a short drive from Tang, and is home to several important cultural and religious sites.

  • Phobjikha Valley: Located approximately 130 kilometers from Tang, the Phobjikha Valley is a pristine natural wilderness, home to several rare and endangered species, including the black-necked crane.

  • Punakha: Located approximately 165 kilometers from Tang, Punakha is the ancient capital of Bhutan, and is home to several important temples and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Tang is a hidden gem in the heart of Bhutan, offering visitors a rare glimpse into the traditional way of life in rural Bhutan, as well as stunning natural scenery and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Tang. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Tang and explore this enchanting region of Bhutan.
