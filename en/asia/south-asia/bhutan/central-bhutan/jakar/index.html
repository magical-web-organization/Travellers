Introduction to Jakar

Nestled in the heart of Bhutan, Jakar is a hidden gem that provides a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. With stunning landscapes and historical monuments, it’s no surprise that Jakar has become a popular destination for travelers seeking a meaningful and authentic experience.

This comprehensive guide will take you on a tour of Jakar, providing valuable insights to help you plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Jakar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jakar is a town deeply ingrained in Bhutanese culture, with various prominent historical sites worth exploring. Here are the must-visit places to discover:

  1. Jakar Dzong: Built in the 17th century, this ancient fortress serves as a monastic and administrative center.
  2. Jambay Lhakhang: This sacred temple is one of the 108 temples built by the Tibetan King Songtsen Gampo to subdue evil spirits in the 7th century.
  3. Kurjey Lhakhang: This temple complex is one of the most sacred sites in Bhutan, and it is here that Guru Rinpoche is said to have left his body imprint on a rock.
  4. Tamzhing Monastery: Built-in 1501, this monastic complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is famous for its exquisite murals and traditional teachings.

Natural Attractions of Jakar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jakar’s landscape and natural beauty are breathtaking, and adventurous souls will not be disappointed. Here are the must-visit natural attractions:

  1. Bumthang Owl Trek: A two-day trek with a campout under the starry sky, this trek takes you through lush forests, past serene streams, and gives you a glimpse into local life in Bhutan.
  2. Domkhar Valley: Ideal for nature lovers, this valley is home to picturesque apple orchards and offers stunning views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks.
  3. Mebar Tsho: A sacred lake surrounded by stunning mountains, it is believed that Terton Pema Lingpa discovered treasures hidden within its depths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jakar

Jakar offers some awe-inspiring routes for hikers and bikers. Here are some popular ones:

  1. Bumthang Cultural Trek: This 3-day trek takes you through local villages, breathtaking landscapes, and serene forests, culminating in spectacular views of the Himalayas.
  2. Jakar to Ngang Lhakhang: This 3-hour trek takes you through peaceful forests, rural villages, and lush valleys, and offers stunning views of Jakar and the surrounding mountains.
  3. Jakar to Tang Valley: This 2-day trek takes you through picturesque apple orchards, quaint temples, and tranquil streams, giving you a wholesome taste of Bhutan’s rural life.

Best Things to Do in Jakar: Activities and Tours

From cultural exploration to adventure escapades, Jakar offers something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Jakar:

  1. Attend a local festival: Jakar Tsechu is one of the most famous festivals celebrated every October, a great opportunity to witness Bhutan’s vibrant culture.
  2. Visit a local farm and learn farming techniques: Bhutanese agriculture is unique, and visiting a local farm will give you a profound understanding of how they live off the land.
  3. Crossbow Shooting: Enjoy a traditional sport that is ingrained in Bhutanese culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Jakar

Bhutanese cuisine is a unique blend of flavors, and Jakar’s food scene offers an array of mouth-watering delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ema Datshi: Bhutan’s national dish made from chili peppers and cheese.
  2. Jasha Maru: Spicy chicken dish with vegetables.
  3. Phaksha Paa: Pork dish with vegetables and spicy chilies.

Local Beverages

  1. Ara: A locally brewed rice wine served in traditional bamboo vessels.
  2. Butter tea: A salty tea made from tea leaves, yak butter, and water.

Where to Stay in Jakar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jakar offers a wide range of accommodations to suit different tastes and budgets. Here are some options:

  1. Amankora Bumthang: A luxury resort located in Bumthang Valley, offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks.
  2. Swiss Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse in the heart of Jakar town.
  3. Ugyenling Hotel: A mid-range hotel with a terrace offering spectacular views of the Jakar area.

What to Buy in Jakar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bhutan is known for its exquisite handicrafts, and Jakar is no exception. Here are some things to bring back home as souvenirs:

  1. Yathra: Traditional Bhutanese woven textiles made from yak hair or sheep wool.
  2. Wooden Bowls and Boxes: Hand-carved wooden bowls and boxes make perfect decorative pieces.
  3. Handmade paper products: Bhutanese handmade paper is made from the bark of the Daphne plant and is perfect for writing notes and letters.

Local Culture and Customs of Jakar

Bhutanese culture is one of the most fascinating aspects of a trip to Jakar. Here are some local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Dress Code: Bhutanese dress conservatively, and it’s essential to cover your shoulders and knees.
  • Taking off shoes: It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering a temple or a local home.
  • Respect for elders: The Bhutanese respect their elders, and it’s customary to acknowledge them with a nod.

Best Time to Visit Jakar

The best time to visit Jakar is during the dry season from October to December and March to May. The weather is pleasant when temperatures range from 10°C to 25°C, and with less rainfall, it is easier to hike and explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jakar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring comfortable walking shoes and appropriate clothing.
  • Try the local cuisine and enjoy cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission at religious sites.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting temples.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jakar

Jakar is a safe destination, and the locals are welcoming and friendly to visitors. However, visitors should always take precautions and avoid traveling alone at night or in deserted areas.

Health in Jakar

Visitors should take care of their health by drinking bottled water, avoiding raw and undercooked foods, and wearing sunscreen to prevent sunburn. Altitude sickness may be a concern when hiking or trekking, and visitors should take respiratory illness seriously and seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Jakar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jakar

The nearest airports to Jakar are Paro International Airport (PBH) and Gelephu Airport (GLU). Both airports are serviced by Royal Bhutan Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Jakar

Buses are a popular mode of transport in Bhutan, and Jakar has a well-connected system of buses from both Paro and Thimphu.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire to reach Jakar from Paro or Thimphu.

How to Get Around in Jakar

Jakar is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Alternatively, visitors can hire bicycles or motorcycles to explore the town and surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Jakar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Jakar, visitors can explore nearby destinations, including:

  1. Paro: Home to Bhutan’s most iconic site – Tiger’s Nest Monastery and Paro Valley, known for its beautiful scenery.
  2. Thimphu: Bhutan’s capital city and a vibrant destination known for its bustling markets, museums, and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Jakar is a charming and beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of culture, adventure, and natural beauty. It’s an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic and meaningful experience that will stay with them for a lifetime. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan and explore Jakar with ease, and make lasting memories of Bhutan.
