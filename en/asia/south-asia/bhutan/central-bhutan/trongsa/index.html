Introduction to Trongsa

Nestled in the central region of Bhutan, Trongsa is a charming town that visitors are often pleasantly surprised to find. The town is perched on a steep ridge and sits at an altitude of 2,200 metres, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. Trongsa is not only rich in history and culture, but it also boasts an array of natural attractions.

In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, the most exciting activities to do, food and drink you cannot miss, where to stay, how to get around, and everything else that you need to know to plan your trip to Trongsa.

List of Places to Visit in Trongsa

Trongsa has a plethora of places to visit, ranging from cultural to natural attractions. Here are some must-visit places in Trongsa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Trongsa Dzong: Built in 1648, Trongsa Dzong is the most impressive dzong (fortress) in the country and the ancestral home of Bhutan’s royal family.

  • Ta Dzong Museum: Once a watchtower, this museum now showcases Bhutanese history and culture through artifacts, paintings, and other exhibits.

  • Kuenga Rabten Palace: Former winter residence of the second king of Bhutan, Jigme Wangchuck, this palace is an excellent example of traditional Bhutanese architecture.

Natural Attractions of Trongsa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phobjikha Valley: Known for its stunning natural beauty, the valley is home to the endangered black-necked cranes, which migrate here each winter.

  • Chendebji Chorten: This ancient chorten (stupa) was built in the 18th century and is surrounded by a verdant forest, making it an ideal spot for a picnic and bird watching.

  • Nubi Village: A charming village known for its bamboo basket weaving and pristine environment, Nubi Village is located on the banks of the Mangde Chhu River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trongsa

Trongsa’s natural beauty and rugged terrain make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular trails to explore:

  • Duer Hot Springs Trek: A four- or five-day trek that takes hikers through stunning landscapes, including rhododendron forests and mountain passes.

  • Nubi Gewog Nature Trail: A moderate two-hour hike that takes hikers through forests, shrines, and small villages.

  • Trongsa Bumthang Bike Ride: A scenic three-day bike ride that goes from Trongsa to Bumthang, taking the cyclists through pristine forests and stunning valleys.

Best Things to Do in Trongsa: Activities and Tours

Apart from the places to visit and the trails to hike, there are many other activities and tours that will make your visit to Trongsa unforgettable:

  • Black-necked Crane Festival: This annual festival celebrates the arrival of the endangered black-necked crane in the Phobjikha Valley.

  • Traditional Hot Stone Bath: A popular wellness activity, a hot stone bath is heated by burning wood underneath the tub and is said to cure various ailments.

  • Tshojo Village Tour: This cultural tour takes visitors to the local village to learn about bamboo basket weaving and to try traditional Bhutanese food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trongsa

Day 1: Arrive in Trongsa and visit Trongsa Dzong and Ta Dzong Museum

Day 2: Hike the Nubi Gewog Nature Trail and have a traditional hot stone bath

Day 3: Visit Kuenga Rabten Palace and drive to Phobjikha Valley

Day 4: Explore Phobjikha Valley and do a Black-necked Crane Festival tour (if during November)

Day 5: Drive to Bumthang and explore the town

Day 6: Hike the Duer Hot Springs Trek

Day 7: Visit Chendebji Chorten and dine at a local restaurant before departing Trongsa

Food and Drink Scene of Trongsa

Bhutanese cuisine is not for the faint-hearted, with dishes often being spicy and served with a side of ema datshi (chili cheese). Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Datsi Vegetable: Bhutan’s national dish made with chili and cheese sauce (ema datshi) is served with vegetables, meat or red rice.

  • Phaksha Paa: Pork cooked with spicy red pepper, cheese and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Ara: A clear, distilled spirit made from fermented rice or millet, found in most bars and restaurants.

  • Suja: A salty tea made by adding butter and salt to tea typically served with breakfast.

Where to Stay in Trongsa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trongsa has plenty of options for accommodation to suit every traveler’s budget.

  • Yangkhil Resort: A luxurious hotel located in a serene surrounding.

  • Drukgyel Guest House: An affordable guest house overlooking the Trongsa valley.

  • Pelden Hotel: A new upscale hotel located just outside the town.

What to Buy in Trongsa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trongsa offers an array of local products to take home as souvenirs. Here are some of the most popular items:

  • Hand-woven textiles: Traditional Bhutanese fabrics, including kiras (traditional dress worn by women) and ghos (traditional dress worn by men) are made with brightly coloured patterns.

  • Bamboo baskets and Souvenirs: Exquisite baskets, bowls, and other trinkets can be found in Nubi Village.

Local Culture and Customs of Trongsa

Trongsa is a melting pot of different ethnic groups, and local customs vary by region. However, Bhutan’s national religion, Vajrayana Buddhism, is deeply entrenched in Bhutanese culture, and visitors should be respectful when visiting religious sites and interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Trongsa

The best time to visit Trongsa is during the dry season from October to December and February to April when the weather is pleasant, and visibility is excellent. Winter months from December to February are also ideal for those who love cold weather and want to see the black-necked cranes in Phobjikha Valley.

## Do’s and Don’ts of Trongsa

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try Bhutanese cuisine, including ema datshi, phaksha paa, and suja.

  • Shop for local handicrafts and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Engage in Public Display Affection (PDA).

  • Smoke in public.

  • Take photographs inside temples or other religious sites without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trongsa

Travelling to Bhutan is considered safe. However, it is always a good idea to follow standard safety measures, such as locking your hotel room and keeping your belongings with you at all times.

Health in Trongsa

Trongsa has basic healthcare facilities. However, it is recommended to purchase travel health insurance before you travel. It’s advisable to drink bottled water and avoid eating from street vendors or anywhere that hygiene standards are questionable.

Getting to Trongsa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Reaching Trongsa requires several modes of transportation, and here are some of the options available:

  • By Air: You can fly to Paro International Airport, and from there, it’s a six-hour drive to Trongsa.

  • By Bus: There are several bus services that run from the neighbouring towns of Bumthang, Wangdue Phodrang, and Punakha to Trongsa.

  • By Car: Hiring a taxi or driving yourself is a convenient way to reach Trongsa, and it’s often the quickest mode of transportation in Bhutan.

Airports Near Trongsa

The nearest airport to Trongsa is Paro International Airport, which is about 200 km away from Trongsa.

Bus Stations Near Trongsa

Trongsa has a small bus station located in the town center, and buses also arrive and depart from the nearby towns of Bumthang, Wangdue Phodrang, and Punakha.

Other Options

One of the most popular ways to explore Bhutan is through organized tours that can take care of logistical arrangements, accommodations, and meals.

How to Get Around Trongsa

Walking is an excellent option to get around Trongsa, and most places are within walking distance from each other. Taxis are available if you prefer not to walk. Renting a car is also an option if you wish to explore the surrounding areas independently.

Where to Go Next from Trongsa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trongsa is the gateway to the eastern and western regions of Bhutan. Here are some nearby destinations you can explore:

  • Bumthang: Known for its lush forests, traditional handicrafts, and ancient monasteries, Bumthang is a popular destination among visitors to Bhutan.

  • Wangdue Phodrang: A small town located south of Trongsa, famous for its hot springs, Wangdue Phodrang is a great place to relax and rejuvenate.

  • Punakha Valley: The former capital of Bhutan is known for its stunning dzongs, beautiful landscapes, and adventure activities such as white water rafting.

Conclusion

Trongsa offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a culture vulture, Trongsa has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip to Bhutan and include Trongsa in your itinerary, and you won’t be disappointed.
