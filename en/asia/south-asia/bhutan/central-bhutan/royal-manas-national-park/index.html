Introduction to Royal Manas National Park

Nestled in the heart of Central Bhutan in South Asia, Royal Manas National Park is a must-visit destination for all nature lovers. Spread over an area of 1,057 square miles, it is the oldest and largest national park in Bhutan. Established in 1966, this park is named after the Manas River, which flows through the park and is the lifeline for its flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Royal Manas National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manas Wildlife Sanctuary: This is the largest protected area in Bhutan and a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Royal Manas National Park. It is home to many rare and endangered species, including the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, Asian elephant, and clouded leopard.
  • Zangdopelri Monastery: Located in Panbang Gewog, this is a beautiful Buddhist monastery with intricate wood carvings and murals.
  • Khomshar Guest House: This historical guesthouse is one of the oldest buildings in the area and has hosted many important guests, including royalty.

Natural Attractions of Royal Manas National Park: Outdoor Adventures, and Scenic Views

  • Manas River: This is a must-see attraction in the park, and offers visitors the chance to witness the beauty and tranquility of the river and its surroundings.
  • Tingtibi Hot Springs: This is a natural hot spring that is great for relaxing and rejuvenating your body and soul.
  • Bhutan-India Friendship Park: This park is a symbol of the long-standing friendship between the two countries and is a peaceful place to enjoy a picnic or take a stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Royal Manas National Park

  • Manas Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: This is a popular trek that takes you through the lush forests and grasslands of the sanctuary and offers stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • Pemaling Trek: This trek takes you through remote villages, high mountain passes, and beautiful valleys, and is considered one of the most challenging treks in Bhutan.

Best Things to Do in Royal Manas National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari: Take a wildlife safari in the Manas Wildlife Sanctuary to see its rich flora and fauna.
  • River Rafting: Enjoy the thrill of river rafting in the Manas River and experience the rush of adrenaline.
  • Cultural Tour: Take a cultural tour and visit museums, monasteries, and historical places in the park to learn about Bhutanese culture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Royal Manas National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: This is a popular Bhutanese dish made with chili peppers and cheese sauce.
  • Jasha Maroo: This is a spicy chicken dish that is perfect for those who love a bit of heat in their food.

Local Beverages

  • Suja: This is a butter tea that is the national drink of Bhutan, and is perfect for warming up on cold days.
  • Ara: This is a local wine made from rice, and is a great way to unwind and relax after a long day of sightseeing.

Where to Stay in Royal Manas National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Khomshar Guest House: This is a historical guesthouse that offers a glimpse into Bhutanese culture and history.
  • Tashi Gasel Lodge: This lodge is located in the heart of the park and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Panbang Rest House: This guesthouse is located in Panbang Gewog and is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Royal Manas National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted items: Bhutan is famous for handcrafted items, such as textiles, jewelry, and wood carvings, which make for great souvenirs.
  • Traditional clothing: Bhutanese traditional clothing, like the gho and kira, is vibrant, colorful, and elegant, and is great for those who want to take a piece of Bhutan back home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Royal Manas National Park

Bhutanese culture is heavily influenced by Buddhism, and their customs and traditions reflect this. Visitors are encouraged to show respect for local customs and beliefs, such as removing their shoes before entering a monastery or temple.

Best Time to Visit Royal Manas National Park

The best time to visit Royal Manas National Park is between November and March when the weather is dry and pleasant. The park is closed during the monsoon season from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Royal Manas National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious places.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park or on the trails.
  • Disturb the wildlife while on safari.

Safety and Health

Safety in Royal Manas National Park

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions while trekking or hiking, such as staying on marked trails and carrying a first-aid kit. Clothing should be weather-appropriate, and proper footwear should be worn.

Health in Royal Manas National Park

There are no medical facilities within the park, and visitors are advised to carry their own medical supplies and medications.

Getting to Royal Manas National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Royal Manas National Park

  • Paro International Airport
  • Guwahati International Airport

Bus Stations Near Royal Manas National Park

  • Jaigaon Bus Stand
  • Gelephu Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private taxis or cars to reach the park.

How to Get Around in Royal Manas National Park

Visitors can explore the park on foot, on bicycle or on horseback. Private taxis with drivers are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Royal Manas National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thimphu: The capital city of Bhutan is a great place to explore its culture and history.
  • Paro: Home to the iconic Tiger’s Nest Monastery, this is a perfect place to relax and unwind after a visit to Royal Manas National Park.

Conclusion

A visit to Royal Manas National Park is a must-do for anyone who loves nature and adventure. From trekking and hiking to river rafting and wildlife safaris, there is something for everyone in this beautiful park.
