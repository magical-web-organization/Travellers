Introduction to Jigme Dorji National Park

Jigme Dorji National Park is the largest protected area in Bhutan and is located in western Bhutan. The park covers 4,316 square kilometers of the Himalayan mountain range, and is home to exotic wildlife, stunning landscapes, and several cultural and natural attractions. Jigme Dorji National Park is a prime destination for tourists looking to experience Bhutan’s unique culture and natural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Jigme Dorji National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bhutan is a highly traditional and cultural country that treasures its history and heritage. Jigme Dorji National Park offers several culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places.

Wangduephodrang Dzong

Wangduephodrang Dzong is a fortress built in 1638 and is located in the Wangduephodrang district, near the entrance to Jigme Dorji National Park. It is one of Bhutan’s most historic dzongs and was destroyed by a fire in 2012. However, reconstruction efforts are underway to restore the dzong back to its former glory.

Jigme Dorji Wangchuck National Referral Hospital Museum

Located within the Jigme Dorji Wangchuck National Referral Hospital, the museum has several exhibits dedicated to Bhutan’s healthcare system, traditional medicines, and the history of the hospital.

Natural Attractions of Jigme Dorji National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jigme Dorji National Park boasts spectacular natural attractions that offer outdoor adventures and scenic views. It is a must-visit destination for eco-tourists, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers.

Phobjikha Valley

Phobjikha Valley is a glacial valley located in the Black Mountains of Bhutan. It is a protected area and a popular tourist spot due to its biodiversity, which includes the endangered black-necked crane. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll in the valley and explore its picturesque landscape.

Jhomolhari Trek

Jhomolhari Trek is a popular trekking route in Bhutan that takes hikers through the stunning landscape of Jigme Dorji National Park. The trek offers stunning views of Mount Jhomolhari, which is the second-highest peak in Bhutan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jigme Dorji National Park

Jigme Dorji National Park has several hiking and mountain biking routes that offer thrilling adventures and breathtaking views.

Gasa Tshachhu Trek

The Gasa Tshachhu Trek is a moderately challenging trek that takes hikers through the picturesque Gasa valley. The trek offers stunning views of the Gasa hot springs and the majestic Himalayan mountain range.

Jakar to Ngang Lhakhang Trek

The Jakar to Ngang Lhakhang Trek is a moderate trek that takes hikers through the scenic Bumthang valley. The trek offers stunning views of the traditional Bhutanese villages and the beautiful countryside.

Best Things to Do in Jigme Dorji National Park: Activities and Tours

The best things to do in Jigme Dorji National Park are activities and tours that offer visitors unique experiences and a chance to explore Bhutan’s culture and natural heritage.

Yak Safari

Visitors can take a Yak Safari tour in Jigme Dorji National Park to experience Bhutan’s traditional mode of transportation. The tour offers stunning views of the park’s scenic landscapes, as well as a chance to interact with the friendly yaks.

Traditional Bhutanese Archery

Archery is a national sport in Bhutan, and visitors can take a traditional Bhutanese Archery lesson in Jigme Dorji National Park. The lesson offers a chance to experience Bhutan’s vibrant culture and to learn about the country’s traditional sports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jigme Dorji National Park

A week-long itinerary for Jigme Dorji National Park can include various activities that offer visitors an opportunity to explore Bhutan’s unique culture and natural heritage.

Day 1: Arrive in Paro and explore the cultural sites in Paro such as the National Museum and Rinpung Dzong.

Day 2: Visit Tiger’s Nest Monastery, one of Bhutan’s most iconic landmarks.

Day 3: Drive to Punakha and visit the Punakha Dzong.

Day 4: Explore the Gasa Tshachhu Trek and soak in the Gasa hot springs.

Day 5: Take a Yak Safari Tour and explore the park’s scenic landscapes.

Day 6: Experience traditional Bhutanese archery and explore the local markets.

Day 7: Depart for Paro International Airport.

Food and Drink Scene of Jigme Dorji National Park

Bhutanese cuisine is a unique blend of Indian, Chinese, and Tibetan influences, and visitors can explore its flavors and taste some of the local dishes in Jigme Dorji National Park.

Must-Try Dishes

Ema Datshi

Ema Datshi is a spicy dish made with chilies and a local cheese called datshi. It is considered Bhutan’s national dish and is a must-try for visitors.

Phaksha Paa

Phaksha Paa is a popular pork dish cooked with spicy red chilies and radish. It is a delicious and hearty meal that is perfect for cooler weather.

Local Beverages

Ara

Ara is a traditional Bhutanese beverage made by distilling rice wine. It is a popular drink among the locals and is often served during festivals and celebrations.

Suja

Suja is a traditional Bhutanese butter tea made by simmering tea leaves in butter and water. It is a popular drink in Bhutan and is often served as a welcome drink to visitors.

Where to Stay in Jigme Dorji National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jigme Dorji National Park offers visitors a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Gasa Hot Springs Resort

Gasa Hot Springs Resort is a popular accommodation option that offers visitors a chance to relax and rejuvenate in the park’s natural hot springs.

Jigme Dorji Wangchuck National Park Campsites

The Jigme Dorji Wangchuck National Park offers several campsites that are ideal for visitors looking to experience Bhutan’s natural beauty up close. Visitors can stay in tents and experience the country’s stunning landscapes at night.

What to Buy in Jigme Dorji National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bhutan offers several unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home as a reminder of their trip to Jigme Dorji National Park.

Hand-woven Textiles

Hand-woven textiles are a popular souvenir that visitors can purchase in Jigme Dorji National Park. The textiles are made by local artisans and showcase Bhutan’s vibrant colors and intricate patterns.

Hand-Carved Masks

Hand-carved masks are another popular souvenir that visitors can purchase in Bhutan. The masks are often used in traditional Bhutanese dance performances and are a unique reminder of the country’s vibrant culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Jigme Dorji National Park

Bhutan is a highly traditional and cultural country, and visitors should be mindful of the local customs and culture.

Dress Code

Bhutanese people take their dress code seriously and visitors are required to dress modestly. It is recommended to wear long pants and dresses that cover the knees.

Dzongkha Language

Dzongkha is the official language of Bhutan, and visitors are encouraged to learn a few basic phrases, such as greetings and common expressions. Locals appreciate the effort, and it is an excellent way to connect with the local community.

Best Time to Visit Jigme Dorji National Park

The best time to visit Jigme Dorji National Park is from September to November and March to May. The weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it ideal for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jigme Dorji National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately and modestly.
  • Carry a waterproof jacket and rain gear.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Touch or disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jigme Dorji National Park

Jigme Dorji National Park is a safe destination for tourists, and locals are friendly and hospitable. Visitors should exercise basic safety precautions, such as not venturing out alone in isolated areas, keeping valuables secure, and taking precautions while trekking.

Health in Jigme Dorji National Park

Visitors should consult their doctor before traveling to Bhutan and ensure that they have all the necessary vaccinations. Carry a medical kit with basic medication and any prescribed medication. Visitors should also take precautions against altitude sickness while trekking.

Getting to Jigme Dorji National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jigme Dorji National Park is accessible by road and air, and there are several options for transportation.

Airports Near Jigme Dorji National Park

Paro International Airport is the closest international airport to Jigme Dorji National Park, and it is well-connected to major cities in Asia.

Bus Stations Near Jigme Dorji National Park

Bhutan has an extensive bus network, and visitors can easily travel by bus from major cities to Jigme Dorji National Park.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars or taxis to travel to Jigme Dorji National Park.

How to Get Around in Jigme Dorji National Park

The best way to get around in Jigme Dorji National Park is by hiring a local guide or taxi. The roads in the park can be challenging to navigate, and a knowledgeable guide can provide information about the park’s history, culture, and wildlife.

Where to Go Next from Jigme Dorji National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jigme Dorji National Park is located in western Bhutan and is surrounded by several nearby destinations and excursions.

Punakha

Punakha is a historically significant town located near Jigme Dorji National Park. It is home to the famous Punakha Dzong and is a must-visit destination for visitors to Bhutan.

Paro

Paro is a charming town located in Bhutan’s Paro Valley, near the entrance to Jigme Dorji National Park. It is home to several cultural sites, including the iconic Tiger’s Nest Monastery.
