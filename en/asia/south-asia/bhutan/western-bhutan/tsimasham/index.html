Introduction to Tsimasham

Tsimasham is a small town located in the Western part of Bhutan, known for its cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. It is the capital of the Chukha district and is situated alongside the Thimphu-Phuentsholing national highway. The town is surrounded by mountains, forests, and rivers, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Tsimasham

Tsimasham offers a range of cultural and natural attractions, including museums, historical places, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Here are some of the notable places to visit in Tsimasham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tashichho Dzong: Also known as the ‘Fortress of the Glorious Religion,’ Tashichho Dzong is a grand Buddhist monastery and fortress located on the banks of the Wang Chu River. It is the administrative seat of the Government of Bhutan and houses various government offices, including the King’s throne room and the central monastic body.
  • Dobji Dzong: Dobji Dzong is a fortress-monastery located on a hilltop and is known for its unique architecture and beautiful wall murals.
  • Chumey Nature Reserve: Spread over an area of 30,000 hectares, the reserve is home to numerous species of birds, animals, and plants, including the endangered black-necked crane.

Natural Attractions of Tsimasham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tsimasham Hot Spring: The Tsimasham Hot Spring is a natural hot spring that is believed to have medicinal benefits. Visitors can soak in the hot springs and enjoy a relaxing time while taking in the beautiful surroundings.
  • Phuentsholing Town: Phuentsholing is a vibrant town located near the Tsimasham border and is known for its bustling markets and vibrant culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsimasham

Tsimasham offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Bhutan, with its mountainous terrain offering challenges for both amateur and expert trekkers. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Tsimasham:

  • Druk Path Trek: This trek covers a distance of 80 km and takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes of Bhutan, including high altitude lakes, mountains, and rhododendron forests.
  • Jomolhari Trek: The Jomolhari Trek is a difficult and challenging trek that covers a distance of 217 km and requires expert trekking skills. The route takes you through beautiful valleys, forests, and mountains, offering an unforgettable experience.

Best Things to Do in Tsimasham: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Tsimasham offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Tsimasham:

  • Visit the Tashichho Dzong and learn about the history and culture of Bhutan.
  • Soak in the Tsimasham Hot Springs and enjoy a relaxing time in the midst of nature.
  • Explore the Phuentsholing Town and experience the vibrant culture and lifestyle of Bhutan.
  • Enjoy bird watching and wildlife photography in the Chumey Nature Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsimasham

Bhutanese cuisine is known for its spicy and flavorful dishes, with chilies being a staple ingredient in most of the dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Tsimasham:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: Ema Datshi is the national dish of Bhutan and is made with chilies and cheese.
  • Phaksha Paa: Phaksha Paa is a spicy pork dish that is cooked with chilies and other spices.
  • Jasha Maroo: Jasha Maroo is a chicken dish flavored with onions, garlic, and chilies.

Local Beverages

  • Ara: Ara is a traditional Bhutanese drink that is made by fermenting rice, maize, or wheat.
  • Suja: Suja is a traditional Bhutanese tea made with butter and salt.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsimasham

Bhutanese culture is deeply rooted in Buddhism, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the important things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples, monasteries, and other religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Tsimasham

The best time to visit Tsimasham is during the autumn months (September to November) and spring months (March to May) when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsimasham

Tsimasham is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables in a safe place.
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Tsimasham

Visitors are advised to carry necessary medications and consult a doctor before traveling to Tsimasham. It is also important to drink bottled or boiled water to avoid any water-borne diseases.
