Introduction to Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

Nestled in the Western region of Bhutan, Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve is a scenic and culturally-rich tourist destination that will leave you awe-struck. It covers 609 square miles, is the country’s second-largest protected area, and is home to endangered animals such as snow leopards, Bengal tigers, and Himalayan black bears. The park is named after Bhutanese King Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck.

Local Culture and Customs of Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

Bhutanese society is deeply rooted in Buddhism and traditional Chinese culture. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Dress modestly and respect the local dress code
  • Remove your shoes before entering temples and monasteries
  • Do not touch religious artifacts
  • Do not smoke or drink alcohol in public places
  • Always ask for permission before taking photographs

Best Time to Visit Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

The ideal time to visit Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve is during the spring season (March to May) or the fall season (September to November). The weather is mild, and the skies are clear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

The park is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors are advised not to venture into the deep woods without a guide.

Health in Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

Travelers are advised to take precautions when it comes to altitude sickness, dehydration, and other minor ailments that may arise from being in a remote wilderness area.

Getting to Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

The closest airport to Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve is Paro International Airport, which is about 80 miles from the park.

Bus Stations Near Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

Buses are a very common mode of transportation in Bhutan, and there are frequent bus services that run from Thimphu, Bhutan’s capital, to Wangdue Phodrang, the largest town near Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve.

Other Options

Renting a private car or hiring a taxi is a convenient and comfortable alternative for traveling from Paro International Airport, which is about a three-hour drive from the park.

How to Get Around in Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve

The most convenient and suitable mode of travel within Jigme Khesar Strict Nature Reserve is by car. Visitors can rent a private car or hire a taxi. Trekking and hiking can be great ways to explore the park’s natural beauty closely.
