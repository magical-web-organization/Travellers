Introduction to Khuruthang

Khuruthang is a small town located in Western Bhutan, nestled in the Punakha Valley. It is the district capital of Punakha and is strategically situated at the confluence of two rivers, Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu. Khuruthang offers visitors the opportunity to explore Bhutanese history, culture, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Khuruthang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Punakha Dzong: Also known as Pungthang Dewachen Phodrang, Punakha Dzong is one of the most prominent and significant dzongs in Bhutan.
  • Chimi Lhakhang: A Buddhist monastery dedicated to the famous Buddhist saint, Drukpa Kunley.
  • Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten: A beautiful and intricate chorten located on a hilltop overlooking Punakha Valley.
  • Wangdue Phodrang Dzong: A fortress dating back to the 17th century, which houses the district administration offices.

Natural Attractions of Khuruthang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pho Chhu Suspension Bridge: A 160-m bridge that offers unmatched views of the Punakha Valley.
  • Punakha Suspension Bridge: Another suspension bridge that spans the Mo Chhu River and offers panoramic vistas of the valley.
  • Kabes Bumthang Trek: A famous trekking trail that passes through Kabesa village and offers marvelous views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes.
  • Punakha Valley: A lush, verdant valley known for its terraced farmland, scenic views, and quaint villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khuruthang

  • Dochula Pass Hike: A popular hike that offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas and the Punakha Valley.
  • Biking in Khuruthang: Rent a bike and explore the town and the surrounding areas, stopping at scenic spots along the way.

Best Things to Do in Khuruthang: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on Mo Chhu River: Take a thrilling and adventurous ride down the Mo Chhu River and enjoy the scenic views of the valley.
  • Fishing: Go fishing in the pristine waters of the Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu rivers.
  • Punakha Tshechu Festival: A colorful and vibrant annual festival held to celebrate Bhutanese heritage and culture.
  • Farmhouse Stay: Get a taste of the local lifestyle by staying in a traditional Bhutanese farmhouse and experience the warm hospitality of the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Khuruthang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: A spicy Bhutanese dish made with chilies and cheese.
  • Phaksha Paa: A pork dish cooked with spicy chilies and vegetables.
  • Momos: Steamed dumplings filled with vegetables, beef or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Suja: A salted butter tea, which is a staple of Bhutanese households.
  • Brewed Beer: Bhutan has a growing brewery industry, with locally brewed beers available in many bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Khuruthang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Meri Puensum Resort: A luxury resort located by the river, offering stunning views of the Punakha Valley.
  • Damchen Resorts: A family-run resort that provides a comfortable and relaxing stay amidst the natural beauty of Punakha Valley.
  • Hotel Zangtopelri: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Khuruthang town, perfect for those looking for a convenient and affordable stay.

What to Buy in Khuruthang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Bhutanese clothes and textiles: Purchase traditional Bhutanese clothing and textiles made by local artisans.
  • Handicrafts and wooden items: Browse through the local shops and stores to find a wide variety of handcrafted souvenirs and wooden items.

Local Culture and Customs of Khuruthang

Bhutan is known for its deep-rooted Buddhist culture and Khuruthang is no exception. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions. One should always remember to remove their shoes before entering monasteries and other religious places.

Best Time to Visit Khuruthang

The best time to visit Khuruthang is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and clear skies provide a stunning view of the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khuruthang

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Carry warm clothing, as temperatures can drop at night.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Take pictures without permission in religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khuruthang

Bhutan is known for being a safe and peaceful country, and Khuruthang is no exception. Visitors should however take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Khuruthang

Visitors to Bhutan are required to have a decent level of fitness and good health. Visitors should also carry all necessary medications and seek medical care in case of any emergencies.

Getting to Khuruthang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khuruthang

The closest airport to Khuruthang is Paro International Airport, which is located about 3 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Khuruthang

The best way to reach Khuruthang is by road. Buses and taxis are available from Thimphu, Paro, and other major towns and cities of Bhutan.

Other Options

Private taxis and rental cars are also available for hire for those who want more flexibility and independence in their travel.

How to Get Around in Khuruthang

The best way to get around Khuruthang is by foot or bike. Taxis are also available for hire for those who prefer to travel in comfort.

Where to Go Next from Khuruthang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thimphu: The capital city of Bhutan, located about 1.5 hours away from Khuruthang by car.
  • Paro: A beautiful and historic town located about 3 hours away from Khuruthang, famous for its iconic Tiger’s Nest Monastery.
  • Gangtey: A peaceful and scenic valley located about 3 hours away from Khuruthang, known for its stunning landscapes and unique wildlife.

Conclusion

Khuruthang is a hidden gem tucked away in Western Bhutan, offering visitors a unique and authentic experience of Bhutanese culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Khuruthang has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and discover the wonders of this beautiful destination.
