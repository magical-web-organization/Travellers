Introduction to Phuentsholing

Phuentsholing is a bustling town located in Western Bhutan, near the border with India. It is often referred to as the “gateway to Bhutan,” as it is the primary entry point for visitors coming from India. Despite being a border town, Phuentsholing has its charm, with various cultural and natural attractions, activities, and delicious food.

The town has a significant importance in Bhutan‘s economy and culture, being a hub of trade and commerce from India to Bhutan. Alongside the Indian kingdom of West Bengal, Phuentsholing offers a journey to the western regions of Bhutan. With its friendly locals and a lovely atmosphere, Phuentsholing is a must-visit destination in Bhutan.

List of Places to Visit in Phuentsholing

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bhutan Gate: The Bhutanese entrance gate on the border with India, known as the “Bhutan Gate,” is one of the most prominent landmarks of Phuentsholing. Its intricate designs and the bright colors draw visitors’ attention, and it is an excellent spot for photography.

  2. Zangto Pelri Lhakhang: Zangto Pelri Lhakhang is a Buddhist temple located in the heart of Phuentsholing town. This temple, built-in 1990 by the Queen Mother Ashi Kesang Wangchuk, is an homage to Guru Rinpoche, the master who introduced Buddhism to Bhutan, and it is one of the essential religious sites in the country.

  3. Amo Chhu Crocodile Breeding Center: The Amo Chhu Crocodile Breeding Center is home to many endemic and non-endemic crocodiles and is an exciting place to visit. Visitors can learn about the lives and habits of these reptiles and watch the caretakers feed them.

Natural Attractions of Phuentsholing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Karbandi Monastery: Located at a high altitude above the Phuentsholing town, the Karbandi Monastery provides visitors with panoramic views of the Bhutan-India border and a distant view of the snow-capped Himalayan ranges. The monastery has a serene atmosphere and is the perfect spot for meditation and contemplation.

  2. Torsa River Side: The Torsa River is one of the major attractions of Phuentsholing. The river is home to diverse species of fish and provides habitat for a variety of bird species. Visitors can enjoy a soothing walk along the river bank or opt for adventurous activities like water rafting and kayaking.

  3. Phuntsholing Park: The Phuntsholing Park is an excellent spot for nature lovers, with well-manicured gardens, flowers of different kinds, and a kid’s play area. A walk around the park can help visitors unwind and relax while enjoying the surrounding’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phuentsholing

  1. Phuentsholing-Bangkok Trail via Chiang Mai: This trail provides a combined bicycle and motorbike route that takes visitors from Phuentsholing to Bangkok, passing through Chiang Mai in Thailand. Biking enthusiasts can challenge themselves with the 792 kilometers’ ride and enjoy the scenic beauty of the cities and towns on the route.

  2. Phuentsholing-Paro-Bumthang Trekking: This walking trail is one of the popular trekking routes in Bhutan, which takes visitors through the country’s cultural and natural heritage sites. The trek takes around 15 days and crosses through some of Bhutan’s most pristine forests, tranquil valleys, and peaceful hamlets.

The Best Things to Do in Phuentsholing: Activities and Tours

  1. Indian Market Visit: The Indian Market in Jaigaon, located on the other side of the Bhutan-India border, is bustling with activity year-round. Visitors can visit the market and purchase souvenirs, clothing, handicrafts, and more at reasonable prices.

  2. Chomolhari Trekking: The Chomolhari Trek is a wonderful experience for visitors to Phuentsholing who would prefer more challenging terrain. Located in Paro, the trek lasts 9 days and takes hikers through the spectacular snow-clad Himalayan mountain ranges.

Food and Drink Scene of Phuentsholing

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ema Datshi: Ema Datshi is a Bhutanese national dish made with chili peppers and cheese. The dish has a unique taste and is served with rice.

  2. Jasha Maroo: Jasha Maroo is a spicy Bhutanese chicken dish cooked with garlic, ginger, and chili.

Local Beverages

  1. Suja: Suja is a sweetened butter tea, a popular beverage in Bhutan. The butter serves as a counterbalance to the bitter flavor of the tea and creates a creamy texture.

  2. Ara: Ara is a local distilled wine made from rice, barley, or maize. It is a traditional drink of Bhutan and is often consumed with traditional dishes.

Where to Stay in Phuentsholing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Bhutan Residence: A 3-star hotel located near the main city center, providing guests with comfortable accommodations and excellent amenities.

  2. Hotel Druk: A luxurious 4-star hotel situated near the central bus terminal, boasting of an on-site restaurant, fitness center, and a rooftop swimming pool.

What to Buy in Phuentsholing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kira and Gho: Kira and Gho are traditional Bhutanese dress-wears for women and men, respectively. They are made from hand-woven fabric, often with intricate designs.

  2. Taktsang Monastery miniature: Visitors can purchase a miniature replica of the Taktsang Monastery, one of Bhutan’s most visited tourist destinations.

Local Culture and Customs of Phuentsholing

Bhutanese people have a distinct culture that emanates their daily life. One of the special aspects of Bhutan is that it is the only country in the world to measure its success by Gross National Happiness instead of the Gross Domestic Product. Bhutanese people value good manners and respect for others, and visitors should try to adhere to these values. It is also essential to dress appropriately in religious sites and avoid pointing with the feet or touching someone’s head.

Best Time to Visit Phuentsholing

The ideal time to visit Phuentsholing is from March to May and September to November. During these periods, the weather is pleasant and conducive for outdoor activities, and visitors can enjoy the beauty of the town and surrounding areas. June to August is the monsoon season, and it is not advisable to travel during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phuentsholing

Do:

  1. Embrace the Bhutanese culture, learn the language and indulge in their values.

  2. Treat the locals with respect and try to converse with them to get to know their way of life better.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the religious beliefs of locals.

  2. Engage in littering and avoid damaging any cultural artifacts or sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phuentsholing

Phuentsholing is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to face any significant safety issues. However, it is advisable to seek the services of authorized guides or travel agencies to avoid any unfortunate events.

Health in Phuentsholing

Bhutan has a good healthcare system, and Phuentsholing has several private hospitals and clinics for visitors. Visitors should ensure that they have vaccinations for diseases such as Tetanus, Meningitis, and Hepatitis before traveling to Bhutan.

Getting to Phuentsholing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phuentsholing

The closest airport to Phuentsholing is the Paro International Airport, located 160 km away. Several airlines operate flights to Paro, including Druk Air and Bhutan Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Phuentsholing

Phuentsholing has an extensive bus network that connects it with major cities and towns across the country. The National Highway-27 runs through the town, providing easy access to locations in Bhutan and India.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to arrive in Phuentsholing by train or taxi from the neighboring Indian town of Hasimara.

How to Get Around in Phuentsholing

Phuentsholing is a small town, and visitors can easily walk around to explore its various attractions. Another popular option is to take a taxi or local buses to travel from one point to another.

Where to Go Next from Phuentsholing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Paro: Paro is a beautiful town located around five hours’ drive from Phuentsholing, known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty.

  2. Thimphu: The capital city of Bhutan, Thimphu, is located around six hours’ drive from Phuentsholing. The city is home to some of Bhutan’s significant religious and historical sites.

Conclusion

Phuentsholing is a fantastic town that offers visitors a chance to experience Bhutanese culture and natural beauty. It is a tourist-friendly destination with various options for accommodation, food, and activities. Visitors to Phuentsholing are sure to have a memorable experience, and the town is an excellent starting point to explore the rest of Bhutan.
