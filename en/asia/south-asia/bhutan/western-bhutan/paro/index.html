Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Paro, Bhutan

Paro is a picturesque valley town in Western Bhutan, known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure trails. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your trip to Paro.

Introduction to Paro

Paro is located in Western Bhutan and is a town surrounded by stunning mountains and greenery. It is an important cultural center in Bhutan, and every year, it attracts a large number of visitors who come to explore its natural beauty and cultural richness.

List of Places to Visit in Paro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Paro Rinpung Dzong: A fortress-monastery that serves as the administrative center and religious institution in Paro. The dzong is also famous for its impressive architecture and beautiful interiors.
  • National Museum of Bhutan: The museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of Bhutan, with exhibits showcasing Bhutanese art, textiles, weapons, and more.
  • Kichu Lhakhang: One of the oldest temples in Bhutan, dating back to the 7th century, and a sacred site to the Bhutanese people.
  • Drukgyel Dzong: A fortress that served as a defense against invading Tibetan forces in the past. It is now in ruins, but still offers stunning mountain views.

Natural Attractions of Paro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tiger’s Nest Monastery: An iconic monastery perched on a cliff, it requires a challenging trek to reach the top, but the breathtaking views make it worth it.
  • Chele La Pass: The highest motorable pass in Bhutan, it offers stunning views of the Himalayas and is an excellent spot for hiking and picnics.
  • Jigme Dorji National Park: A vast expanse of untamed wilderness that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including endangered species such as the snow leopard, takin, and red panda.
  • Champaca Trail: A popular hiking trail that takes visitors through beautiful forests and scenic landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paro

  • Taktsang Palphug Monastery Trek: Also known as the Tiger’s Nest Trek, it takes visitors through a beautiful forest and up steep hills to reach the famous Tiger’s Nest monastery.
  • Druk Path Trek: A beautiful hiking trail that takes visitors through stunning vistas of the Himalayas, pristine forests, and clear mountain lakes.
  • Chele La Nature Trek: A moderate trek, it takes visitors through stunning mountain landscapes and forests, and offers views of the sacred mountain, Jomolhari.

Best Things to Do in Paro: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: A unique way to experience the beauty of Paro from up above.
  • Cycling Tour: A fun way to explore Paro’s scenic trails and stunning landscapes.
  • Cultural Tours: A chance to learn about Bhutan’s unique culture and visit its most important historical and cultural sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paro

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Paro:

Day 1: Arrive in Paro, visit Paro Rinpung Dzong.

Day 2: Trek to Tiger’s Nest Monastery.

Day 3: Explore the town of Paro and visit the National Museum of Bhutan.

Day 4: Drive to Chele La Pass, and go on a picnic.

Day 5: Visit Kichu Lhakhang and Drukgyel Dzong.

Day 6: Go on a cycling tour and explore the countryside.

Day 7: Depart from Paro.

Food and Drink Scene of Paro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: Bhutan’s national dish, it’s a spicy cheese and chili stew.
  • Phaksha Paa: A popular pork dish, cooked with radish and chili flakes.
  • Momos: Steamed dumplings filled with vegetables, meat, or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: A salty tea made with yak butter, a staple in traditional Bhutanese cuisine.
  • Ara: A type of rice wine that is brewed at home and served at festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Paro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Zhiwa Ling Hotel: A luxury hotel with beautiful architecture and views of the Paro Valley.
  • Yanki Resort: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and beautiful mountain views.
  • Tenzinling Resort: A mid-range hotel that offers a quiet retreat with stunning views of the Paro Valley.

What to Buy in Paro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven Textiles: Bhutanese textiles are famous for their intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  • Handicrafts: Bhutanese handicrafts such as traditional masks, wooden bowls, and prayer wheels make for unique souvenirs.
  • Bhutanese Incense: Bhutanese incense is made with natural ingredients and is used in religious ceremonies and meditation.

Local Culture and Customs of Paro

Bhutanese culture is deeply rooted in Buddhism, and visitors are expected to show respect for religious and cultural customs, such as removing shoes before entering a temple or dzong, covering shoulders, and refraining from public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Paro

The best time to visit Paro is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. If you’re looking to witness Bhutan’s famous festivals, plan your visit during the months of February, June, or December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paro

Do:

  • Respect Bhutanese culture and customs.
  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Learn a few words of Dzongkha, Bhutan’s national language.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Disrespect religious and cultural customs.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paro

Paro is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take precautions such as not leaving their belongings unattended and being aware of their surroundings when out and about.

Health in Paro

Visitors should take precautions against altitude sickness when visiting high altitude locations such as Tiger’s Nest Monastery and Chele La Pass. It’s also essential to have adequate travel insurance and to carry any necessary medication.

Getting to Paro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paro

Paro International Airport is the only international airport in Bhutan, with flights from major cities such as Delhi, Bangkok, and Kathmandu.

Bus Stations Near Paro

Paro is well connected to other towns in Bhutan via bus services operated by Bhutan Post.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or arrange for a tour operator to provide transportation.

How to Get Around in Paro

Paro is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and bikes are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Paro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thimphu: Bhutan’s capital city, known for its modern architecture and bustling markets.
  • Punakha: A scenic town known for its stunning dzongs and beautiful natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Paro is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Bhutanese culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Paro with ease and make the most of your visit.
