Introduction to Bhutan

Bhutan is a South Asian country that is located in the eastern Himalayas. Also known as the ‘Land of the Thunder Dragon’, Bhutan is a small country where the people, culture and traditions are deeply rooted in Buddhism. Bhutan is famous for being one of the happiest countries in the world, with a unique approach to measuring progress called Gross National Happiness.

Travelers to Bhutan will be fascinated by the country’s untouched natural beauty, as well as the vibrant culture and rich history of this unique destination. This comprehensive tour guide will take you through the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink options, cultural customs, and transportation options in Bhutan.

Whether you are interested in learning about Bhutan’s longstanding history and culture or exploring the country’s natural beauty, Bhutan has plenty of places to visit. Here are a few attractions to add to your itinerary:

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bhutan

Bhutan’s mountainous terrain offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Asia. Whether you are an experienced hiker or just starting, these routes offer unforgettable experiences.

  • Druk Path Trek
  • Snowman Trek
  • Jigme Dorji National Park Trek
  • Bumthang Owl Trek
  • Black Mountain Trek

Best Things to Do in Bhutan: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Bhutan. Here are some recommended things to do:

  • Whitewater rafting
  • Hot springs therapy
  • Rafting and Kayaking
  • Archery
  • Stargazing

Week-Long Itinerary for Bhutan

If you’re short on time or just want a sample itinerary, here’s a week-long tour route of Bhutan:

Day Itinerary
1 Arrival in Paro and sightseeing at Ta Dzong and Rinpung Dzong
2 Hiking to Tiger’s Nest Monastery
3 Exploring the capital Thimphu and museums
4 Visiting Punakha and Wangduephodrang
5 Hiking to Phobjikha via Lobesa vally and Chimmi Lhakhang
6 Hiking in Bumthang valley
7 Enjoying the hot springs of Phobjikha and departure

Food and Drink Scene of Bhutan

Bhutanese cuisine is unique and delicious, with a focus on spicy flavors and colorful ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi (spicy chile and cheese sauce)
  • Phaksha Paa (pork with chilies and radish)
  • Jasha Maroo (spicy chicken stew)
  • Red Rice
  • Momos (steamed dumplings)

Local Beverages

  • Butter tea
  • Ara (traditional Bhutanese liquor)
  • Suja (salted butter tea)
  • Bangchang (locally brewed beer)

What to Buy in Bhutan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bhutan is known for producing high-quality handicrafts, including textiles, wood carvings, and paintings. Here are some suggestions for souvenirs to buy:

  • Hand-woven textiles such as kiras and ghos
  • Wooden bowls and carvings
  • Bhutanese masks
  • Traditional Thangka paintings

Local Culture and Customs of Bhutan

Bhutanese culture is deeply rooted in Buddhism, with customs and traditions that are unique to the country. Here are some important customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for elders and authority figures
  • The importance of non-violence and compassion
  • The national dress (gho and kira)
  • The ritual of circumambulation
  • The tradition of offering butter lamps in temples

Best Time to Visit Bhutan

The best time to visit Bhutan is in the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild, and visibility is good. However, the winter months of December to February also provide an opportunity to enjoy the snow-covered mountains with clear skies.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bhutan

Here are some important things to keep in mind when visiting Bhutan:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly and respectfully
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Learn a few words of Dzongkha (Bhutanese language)
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Bhutan responsibly

Don’t:

  • Touch religious artifacts without permission
  • Disrespect the King, Queen or religious figures
  • Take pictures without permission at cultural or religious sites

Safety and Health

Bhutan is generally a safe and peaceful country. Here are some important things to keep in mind when it comes to safety and health:

Safety in Bhutan

  • The crime rate in Bhutan is low, and tourists are unlikely to encounter any trouble
  • Altitude sickness is common when traveling to Bhutan’s higher elevations

Health in Bhutan

  • Medical facilities outside of the larger cities are limited
  • Travelers should be up to date on their vaccinations and bring any necessary medications with them

How to Get Around in Bhutan

While the country’s small size makes getting around easy, hiring a car is the most convenient way to travel in Bhutan.
