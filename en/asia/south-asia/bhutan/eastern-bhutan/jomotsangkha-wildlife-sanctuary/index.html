Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary - A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary is an exquisite natural habitat in eastern Bhutan that is famous for its incredible biodiversity and rich cultural heritage. Covering an area of 334 sq. km, the sanctuary is located in the foothills of the Himalayas and is home to a variety of wildlife, including Bengal tigers, red pandas, clouded leopards, Asian elephants, and many more. With its stunning landscapes, ancient temples, and vibrant culture, Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and cultural enthusiasts.

Introduction to Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area in southeastern Bhutan, bordering the Indian state of Assam. The sanctuary was established in 1998 with the aim of safeguarding the unique flora and fauna of the region. The sanctuary is characterized by its pristine forests, rolling hills, and sparkling rivers that are home to an abundance of wildlife.

The sanctuary is also home to several Bhutanese ethnic groups, including the Monpas, Sherpas, and Sharchops, who have their unique cultures, customs, and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary boasts a range of remarkable destinations that visitors can explore, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary Museum: A must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts that provides an informative insight into the park’s unique flora and fauna.
  • Jomotsangkha Dzong: Built-in the 18th century, Jomotsangkha Dzong is a must-visit historical site that showcases the unique architecture of Bhutan.

Natural Attractions of Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khaling Neoli Wildlife Sanctuary: Located near the Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary, Khaling Neoli Wildlife Sanctuary is another must-visit attraction that is popular for its breathtaking landscapes and diverse wildlife.
  • Tingtibi-Manas National Park Trek: A challenging but rewarding trek that leads you through the stunning landscapes of Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary boasts several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary Trek: A scenic trek that takes you through some of the most spectacular landscapes and wildlife habitats of the sanctuary.
  • Pemagatshel Biking Trail: A biking trail that leads you through the lush forests, charming villages, and stunning landscapes of Pemagatshel.

Best Things to Do in Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary offers visitors a plethora of exciting activities to choose from, including:

  • Wildlife Safari: Explore the park’s incredible biodiversity and spot some of the rare and endangered species.
  • Cultural Tour: Immerse yourself in the rich culture and traditions of the Bhutanese ethnic groups who inhabit the sanctuary.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary for exploring Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary:

Day 1: Arrive in Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary and explore the museum and historical sites.

Day 2: Go on a wildlife safari to spot some of the park’s incredible wildlife.

Day 3: Hike the Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary Trek.

Day 4: Explore the nearby Khaling Neoli Wildlife Sanctuary.

Day 5: Go on a cultural tour of the villages and towns in the sanctuary.

Day 6: Take the Pemagatshel Biking Trail.

Day 7: Depart Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary.

Food and Drink Scene of Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary offers a unique food and drink scene that showcases the traditional cuisine and local beverages of Bhutan. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: Bhutan’s national dish made with chili peppers and cheese.
  • Goep: A traditional Bhutanese stew made with meat, vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Ara: A traditional Bhutanese alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice, maize, or wheat.
  • Butter tea: A salty tea made with yak butter, salt, and tea leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary is home to several Bhutanese ethnic groups, each with its unique customs, culture, and traditions. Visitors are advised to respect the local customs and dress conservatively when visiting cultural and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary is from October to December when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Observe wildlife from a safe distance.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or their natural habitat.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Take pictures without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take precautionary measures to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Stay with a guide when exploring the sanctuary.
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife and avoid making sudden movements.

Health in Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors to Jomotsangkha Wildlife Sanctuary are advised to take the necessary precautions to avoid health hazards, such as:

  • Get vaccinated for diseases like malaria and dengue fever.
  • Drink only bottled or boiled water to avoid water-borne diseases.
