Introduction to Trashigang

Located in Eastern Bhutan, Trashigang is a culturally-rich and scenic city known for its historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, and traditional festivals. Trashigang is Bhutan’s largest district and is home to various ethnic groups, including the Tshanglas, Monpas, and Kurtoeps. The city is situated in the eastern Himalayas and offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Trashigang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Trashigang Dzong: This impressive fortress was built in 1659 and played a significant role in Bhutan’s history. The dzong now houses various government offices and a monastic body.
  • Trashigang town: Take a stroll through the city’s vibrant center and visit local shops and restaurants.
  • Gom Kora: This serene temple is located on a hilltop and offers panoramic views of the river valley. It is known for its meditation caves and is popular among Buddhist pilgrims.
  • Radhi village: This traditional village is known for its textile industry and colorful patterned weaves. Learn about the weaving process and purchase souvenirs at one of the local shops.

Natural Attractions of Trashigang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kanglung National Park: This vast park covers over 169 square miles and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan range. Enjoy trekking, bird-watching, and camping in the wild.
  • Khardung La: This pass offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is a popular spot for photography and hiking enthusiasts.
  • Yonphula Centennial National Park: This park was established to celebrate the 100-year anniversary of Bhutan’s monarchy. The park is known for its diverse wildlife, including tigers, leopards, and elephants.
  • Trashigang Tsechu: This annual festival is held in the Trashigang Dzong and celebrates Bhutanese culture through dances, music, and colorful costumes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trashigang

  • Merak-Sakteng Trek: This challenging trek takes you through the remote villages of Merak and Sakteng and offers panoramic views of the Himalayas. The trek usually lasts around six days.
  • Kanglung-Samdrup Jongkhar Trek: This scenic trek takes you through the Kanglung National Park and offers encounters with various wildlife species such as Himalayan black bears, Langurs, and musk deer. It usually lasts around seven days.

Best Things to Do in Trashigang: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Archery: Try your hand at Bhutan’s national sport, Archery. Various archery tournaments are held throughout the year.
  • Eco-Tourism: Visit the Kanglung National Park or Yonphula Centennial National Park and participate in guided tours from expert ecologists.
  • Festival Tours: Join the locals and partake in a cultural celebration such as Trashigang Tsechu.
  • Fishing: Fly fishing can be done at various sites along the Drangme Chu River.

Food and Drink Scene of Trashigang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ema Datshi: This spicy dish is made with chilies and cheese and is the national dish of Bhutan
  • Jasha Maroo: This flavorful chicken stew is a popular Bhutanese dish.
  • Kewa Datshi: This potato and cheese dish is a vegetarian-friendly option.

Local Beverages

  • Suja: This traditional butter tea is a popular drink in Bhutan and is made by boiling tea leaves with yak butter and salt.
  • Ara: This locally distilled rice wine is a staple drink in Bhutan and is drank during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Trashigang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Druk Deothjung Resort: This resort offers comfortable rooms and a stunning view of the Trashigang valley.
  • Trashigang Lodge: This charming guesthouse offers traditional Bhutanese decor and a cozy environment.
  • Hotel Rinchen Ling: This modern hotel offers clean rooms and various amenities.

What to Buy in Trashigang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Textiles: Take home a piece of Bhutanese culture with a purchase of traditional textile products handmade by local artisans.
  • Handicrafts: Local handicrafts such as prayer flags, wooden masks, and Bhutanese knives make great unique souvenirs.
  • Fresh Produce: Take home fresh fruits or nuts grown in the fertile valleys of Trashigang.

Local Culture and Customs of Trashigang

Bhutanese culture is deeply rooted in spiritual beliefs and is often characterized by the nation’s commitment to Gross National Happiness. Trashigang locals are friendly and hospitable and often engage in traditional music and dancing. It is important to respect Bhutanese customs and to dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Trashigang

The best time to visit Trashigang is during the spring season from March to May and autumn season from September to November when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summer can also be enjoyable but can be rainy, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trashigang

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of locals
  • Express gratitude through traditional greeting “Kuzuzangpo” or “Namaste”.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas as Bhutan is a tobacco-free nation.
  • Disrespect the country’s cultural and religious customs
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trashigang

Trashigang is generally a safe city for tourists. However, it is advisable to take precautions and avoid walking alone in dark, isolated areas.

Health in Trashigang

When visiting Trashigang, it is essential to take precautions against high altitude sickness, especially when engaging in activities that involve high altitudes. It’s also essential to stay hydrated and practice good hygiene.

Getting to Trashigang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trashigang

  • Paro International Airport: This is the primary international airport in Bhutan and is located around 470 kilometers from Trashigang.
  • Yongphula Domestic Airport: This small domestic airport is located approximately 120 kilometers from Trashigang.

Bus Stations Near Trashigang

  • Trashigang Bus Station: This is the primary bus station in the district and offers regular intercity services.
  • Samdrup Jongkhar Bus Station: This is another nearby bus station that offers intercity services.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired to reach Trashigang from other parts of Bhutan.

How to Get Around in Trashigang

The best way to get around Trashigang is by local taxis or buses. However, during peak tourist seasons, it’s better to book a private car.

Where to Go Next from Trashigang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Samdrup Jongkhar: This nearby town offers various cultural attractions, including a traditional marketplace and ancient temples.
  • Bumthang: Known as the cultural heart of Bhutan, Bumthang offers scenic landscapes and cultural landmarks such as monasteries and temples.

Conclusion

With its cultural richness and scenic beauty, Trashigang is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Bhutan. From exploring historical landmarks to experiencing outdoor adventures, Trashigang offers a wide range of activities that will leave you with unforgettable memories. Plan your ideal itinerary today and take a step into the traditional and scenic city of Trashigang.
