A Comprehensive Guide to Tashiyangtse - The Hidden Gem of Bhutan

Located in the eastern part of Bhutan, Tashiyangtse is a hidden gem with Tibetan-style architecture and unspoiled natural beauty. Despite its small size, Tashiyangtse boasts several cultural landmarks, scenic hiking trails, mouth-watering local cuisine, and warm hospitality. In this comprehensive guide, we take a closer look at what makes Tashiyangtse such a unique destination.

Introduction to Tashiyangtse

Tashiyangtse is a district located in the heart of eastern Bhutan. It is known for its beautiful monasteries, traditional architecture, and picturesque landscapes. Visitors to Tashiyangtse can expect to see colorful prayer flags fluttering amidst the greenery and stunning views of the Himalayan mountain range. Tashiyangtse is also the home of the famous Chorten Kora, which draws a large number of visitors during its annual festival.

List of Places to Visit in Tashiyangtse

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The National Institute for Zorig Chusum, also known as the School of Traditional Arts, is one of the must-visit places in Tashiyangtse. It offers visitors an opportunity to see traditional Bhutanese arts and crafts being made by skilled artisans, such as woodcarving, painting, and weaving. The Chorten Kora is another important landmark in Tashiyangtse. This stupa built in 1740 is a popular destination for pilgrims and offers magnificent views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Tashiyangtse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts, Tashiyangtse has a lot to offer. The famous Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is located nearby, where visitors can spot rare species like the Himalayan Black Bear, the Red Panda, and Musk Deer. The Samdrup Jongkhar Wildlife Sanctuary, located an hour’s drive away, offers breathtaking views of the eastern Himalayas and is also home to several endangered species. For hiking enthusiasts, the Yongla Goenpa trail offers a scenic trek through pristine forests, making it one of the most popular hikes in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tashiyangtse

Tashiyangtse has several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the popular hiking trails include the Yongla Goenpa Trail and the Sakteng Trek. The Sakteng Trek is a week-long trek that takes visitors through the verdant forests of the Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary and offers stunning views of the Himalayan mountains. For mountain bike enthusiasts, the Chazam to Kuri Yumtsho route offers a thrilling ride through breathtaking landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Tashiyangtse: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities that visitors to Tashiyangtse can indulge in. Visitors can enjoy local festivals, such as the Chorten Kora Festival or witness the Jambay Lhakhang Drup, which is celebrated every October. The Wangchuck Centennial Park is also a great place to visit, which offers a glimpse into Bhutan’s efforts at conservation and sustainable development.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tashiyangtse

Day 1: Visit the School of Traditional Arts

Day 2: Explore the Chorten Kora

Day 3: Trek the Yongla Goenpa trail

Day 4: Visit the Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Day 5: Experience the Chazam to Kuri Yumtsho mountain bike route

Day 6: Attend a local festival or explore the Wangchuck Centennial Park

Day 7: Relax and explore the local town of Tashiyangtse

Food and Drink Scene of Tashiyangtse

Must-Try Dishes

Tashiyangtse is known for its unique cuisine that blends Indian and Tibetan culinary traditions. Among the must-try dishes are Ema Datshi, a spicy dish made with cheese and chili, and Shakam Paa, a dish made with dried beef and local vegetables. Visitors should also try Ara, a potent rice wine served in bamboo cups.

Local Beverages

Bhutanese tea, which is made with butter, salt, and toasted rice, is a staple beverage in Tashiyangtse. Visitors can also try the local alcohol, including Ara and Bangchang, a beer made with millet and hops.

Where to Stay in Tashiyangtse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tashiyangtse offers several options for visitors to stay, including hotels, guesthouses, and homestays. Some of the popular hotels in Tashiyangtse include Druk Deothjung Resort and Khang Residency. For visitors looking for a more authentic experience, homestays are a great option and can be arranged with local tour operators.

What to Buy in Tashiyangtse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tashiyangtse is known for its traditional handicrafts and textiles, including handwoven baskets, traditional wear like the Kira and Gho, and handcrafted wooden products. Visitors can buy these items at the Craft Gallery, located in Tashiyangtse town.

Local Culture and Customs of Tashiyangtse

Tashiyangtse is known for its traditional festivals, including the Chorten Kora Festival and the Jambay Lhakhang Drup. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and observe cultural customs during festivals and while visiting religious sites. Visitors should not touch religious artifacts, smoke or drink in religious sites, or disturb the local wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Tashiyangtse

The best time to visit Tashiyangtse is in the months of September to November when the weather is mild and the skies are clear. During these months, visitors can witness the fall festivals and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and bird watching.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tashiyangtse

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Bring warm clothing and waterproof gear for outdoor activities
  • Take off your shoes while entering religious sites

Don’t:

  • Touch religious artifacts
  • Smoke or drink in religious sites
  • Disturb the local wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Tashiyangtse

Tashiyangtse is a safe destination with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions while hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Tashiyangtse

Visitors are advised to consult a doctor and take necessary vaccinations before traveling to Bhutan. It is also recommended to carry mosquito repellent and medication for altitude sickness.

Getting to Tashiyangtse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tashiyangtse

Tashiyangtse does not have an airport. The closest airport is the Paro International Airport, which is approximately 550 km away.

Bus Stations Near Tashiyangtse

Buses from Thimphu to Tashiyangtse are operated by the Bhutan Postal Corporation and take approximately 12 hours to cover the distance.

Other Options

Private taxis can also be arranged with local tour operators. Visitors can also opt for a helicopter transfer from Paro International Airport to Tashiyangtse.

How to Get Around in Tashiyangtse

The most common mode of transport in Tashiyangtse is by car or taxi. Visitors can also opt for bicycles, which can be rented from local tour operators.

Where to Go Next from Tashiyangtse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby town of Trashigang is known for its beautiful landscapes and traditional markets. Visitors can also explore the neighboring district of Lhuntse, which is known for its weavers and traditional textiles.

Conclusion

Tashiyangtse is a unique destination that offers a perfect balance of cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, and warm hospitality. From hiking and biking to local festivals and traditional cuisine, Tashiyangtse is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty and culture of Bhutan.
