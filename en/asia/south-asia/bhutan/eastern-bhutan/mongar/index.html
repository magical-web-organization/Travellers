Introduction to Mongar

Mongar is a district in Eastern Bhutan that is surrounded by scenic hills and valleys. This hidden gem of Bhutan is known for its peaceful atmosphere and rich cultural heritage. With its breathtaking natural vistas, historic sites, and local traditions, Mongar is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Bhutanese experience.

List of Places to Visit in Mongar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the major attractions of Mongar is its cultural heritage. The district is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the ancient Bhutanese civilization.

Mongar Dzong

Mongar Dzong is a historic fortress that dates back to the 1930s. This impressive building is perched on a hill overlooking the town of Mongar, and it served as the administrative center of the district for many years. Today, Mongar Dzong is a significant tourist attraction in the region and is open to the public for guided tours.

Lhuentse Dzong

Lhuentse Dzong is one of the most remote fortresses in Bhutan and is known for its unique architecture and intricate woodwork. It is located about 77 kilometers away from Mongar town and is often included in the itinerary of those who are visiting Mongar.

Zhongar Dzong

Zhongar Dzong is another historic fortress in the area that is worth visiting.Built in 1651, it is one of the oldest dzongs in Bhutan.

Natural Attractions of Mongar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its cultural attractions, Mongar is also famous for its natural beauty. The district is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Narphung La Pass

Narphung La Pass is a mountain pass located about 25 kilometers from Mongar town. This pass offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas, and the surrounding valleys and meadows. It is a popular spot for picnics and day trips.

Drametse-Ngatshang Trek

The Drametse-Ngatshang Trek is a scenic hiking trail that takes you through the countryside of Mongar. This trek is famous for its picturesque landscapes, and it offers a chance to interact with the locals and learn about their way of life.

Kori La Pass

Kori La Pass is a popular biking route that offers panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges. The route takes you through small villages and scenic valleys, and it is a great way to explore the beauty of Mongar on two wheels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mongar

Mongar is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The district is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer the perfect opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Mongar include the Drametse-Ngatshang Trek, the Kori La Pass, and the Narphung La Pass.

Best Things to Do in Mongar: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other things to do in Mongar. Some of the must-try activities and tours in the district include:

Hot Stone Bath

Experience the traditional Bhutanese hot stone bath, which is said to have therapeutic properties. The bath involves soaking in a wooden tub filled with hot water and stones, which are heated over an open fire.

Traditional Archery

Archery is the national sport of Bhutan, and Mongar is home to several archery ranges where you can experience this traditional sport.

Farmstay

Experience the rural life of Bhutanese farmers by staying with a local family. This is an excellent way to learn about their agriculture practices and their simple way of living.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mongar

For visitors looking to spend a week in Mongar, here’s a possible itinerary:

Day 2: Trek to the Drametse Monastery.

Day 3: Visit Lhuentse Dzong.

Day 6: Hiking to Korila Pass.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Mongar

Mongar is known for its authentic Bhutanese cuisine, which is characterized by its spicy and savory flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Mongar:

Must-Try Dishes

Ema Datshi

This is a signature Bhutanese dish made with chilies and cheese.

Jasha Maroo

Jasha Maroo is a spicy chicken dish that is popular in Bhutan and Tibet.

Momos

Momos are a type of dumpling that can be filled with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Butter Tea

Butter Tea is a traditional drink made with tea leaves, butter, and salt.

Ara

Ara is a local alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice.

Where to Stay in Mongar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Mongar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mongar is home to several local markets where you can find a variety of handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some products to consider:

Bhutanese Textiles

Bhutanese textiles are renowned for their intricate designs and vibrant colors. You can find a range of textiles, including hand-woven scarves, traditional dresses, and blankets.

Handicrafts

Mongar is known for its traditional handicrafts, including wooden bowls, bamboo baskets, and handmade paper products.

Local Culture and Customs of Mongar

Mongar is home to several unique customs and traditions that are rooted in the Bhutanese way of life. Some of the prominent customs and festivals in Mongar include the Tshechu festival and the Domkhar festival.

Best Time to Visit Mongar

The best time to visit Mongar is from March to May and from October to December. During this time, the weather is mild, and the skies are clear, allowing for better visibility of the surrounding landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mongar

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Show respect to the locals and their customs.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or a home.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their consent.
  • Disrespect religious sites and customs.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mongar

Mongar is a relatively safe destination. However, visitors are advised to take precautions and be mindful of their surroundings.

Health in Mongar

Visitors to Mongar are advised to seek medical advice and take necessary vaccinations before traveling to Bhutan. It is also recommended to carry a first-aid kit and insect repellent.

Getting to Mongar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mongar is well connected to the rest of Bhutan by road. The nearest airport is in Paro, and visitors can either take a flight or a bus to Mongar.

Airports Near Mongar

The nearest airport to Mongar is Paro International Airport, which is about 10 hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Mongar

Mongar is well connected by buses, and visitors can take a bus from Thimphu or Paro to reach Mongar.

Other Options

Private taxis are also available for hire, and visitors can negotiate with drivers for the best rates.

How to Get Around in Mongar

The best way to get around in Mongar is by hiring a private taxi or using the public transportation system.

Where to Go Next from Mongar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mongar is a great starting point for exploring the rest of Bhutan. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

Trashigang

Trashigang is a scenic town located about 90 kilometers away from Mongar. This town is known for its unique architecture and cultural sites.

Bumthang

Bumthang is a picturesque valley located in Central Bhutan. This valley is home to several historic sites and is often referred to as the cultural heartland of Bhutan.

Conclusion

Mongar is a hidden gem in Eastern Bhutan, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore its stunning landscapes, historic sites, and local traditions for a truly authentic Bhutanese experience. Whether you are an adventure enthusiast or a history buff, Mongar has something for everyone.
