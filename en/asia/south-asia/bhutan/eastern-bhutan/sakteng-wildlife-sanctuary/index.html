Introduction to Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is located in eastern Bhutan, South Asia. It is one of the country’s most unique and impressive wildlife sanctuaries, covering an area of over 740 square kilometers. Established in 2003, the sanctuary was created to protect the unique biodiversity that is found in the eastern region of Bhutan.

Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including over 40 species of mammals, 300 species of birds, and a variety of reptiles, amphibians, and insects. With its rolling hills, dense forests, and winding streams, it is also a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in the history and culture of Bhutan, there are several places you can visit within Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary. Some of the top cultural attractions include:

  1. Gorsam Chorten: This ancient temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in eastern Bhutan. It is located in the northwestern part of the sanctuary and is known for its breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

  2. The Royal Farm: The Royal Farm is located in the heart of the sanctuary and is a great place to learn about Bhutanese agriculture and traditional farming practices. You can also visit the local farmers’ market and sample some of the fresh produce.

Natural Attractions of Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is a nature lover’s paradise, with breathtaking scenery and a wide range of outdoor activities available. Here are some of the top natural attractions that you won’t want to miss:

  1. Phongmey Valley: This beautiful valley is located in the central part of the sanctuary and is home to verdant forests and crystal-clear streams. It is a great place to go hiking, bird watching, or simply to enjoy the stunning scenery.

  2. The Black-necked Crane Information Centre: The Black-necked Crane Information Centre is located in the western part of the sanctuary and is dedicated to the conservation of the endangered Black-necked Crane. Visitors can learn about the habitat and behavior of these magnificent birds and even spot them in the wild.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

For outdoor enthusiasts, Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. The Lhabab Trek: This challenging multi-day trek takes you through some of the most remote parts of the sanctuary, passing through pristine forests, remote villages, and high-altitude passes.

  2. The Yonphula Trek: This relatively easy trek is a great option for those looking for a less strenuous hiking experience. It takes you through beautiful valleys and forests, passing by several traditional Bhutanese villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Wildlife Watching: With its diverse range of flora and fauna, Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is the perfect place to go wildlife watching. Visitors can spot everything from tigers and leopards to rhinoceros and elephants.

  2. Cultural Tours: For those interested in learning about Bhutanese culture and history, there are several cultural tours available. These tours often include visits to ancient temples, monasteries, and traditional villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

If you are planning a trip to Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary, here is a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1-2: Arrive in Bhutan and travel to Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary. Spend the first two days exploring the sanctuary’s natural attractions, such as Phongmey Valley and the Black-necked Crane Information Centre.

Day 3-4: Spend the next two days hiking one of the sanctuary’s many scenic trails. The Lhabab Trek is a great option for experienced hikers, while the Yonphula Trek is better suited for those looking for a more leisurely walk.

Day 5-6: Take a cultural tour of the sanctuary, visiting ancient temples and traditional villages. Don’t forget to sample some of the delicious local cuisine along the way.

Day 7: Depart Bhutan.

Food and Drink Scene of Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Must-Try Dishes

Bhutanese cuisine is known for its use of spicy chilies and unique flavor combinations. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary:

  1. Ema Datshi: This spicy cheese and chili dish is a staple of Bhutanese cuisine and is a must-try for any visitor.

  2. Jasha Maru: This savory chicken curry is another popular Bhutanese dish that is well worth trying.

Local Beverages

Bhutan is also known for its unique beverages. Here are two of the most popular:

  1. Butter Tea: This rich, creamy tea is a traditional Bhutanese beverage made with tea leaves, butter, and salt.

  2. Ara: A traditional Bhutanese rice wine, Ara is made from fermented rice and is often served during special occasions and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options available in and around Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary. Here are some of the top choices:

  1. The Royal Farm Homestay: This traditional guesthouse is located in the heart of the sanctuary and offers comfortable rooms and delicious home-cooked meals.

  2. Trashigang Lodge: This luxury hotel is located just outside the sanctuary and offers spacious rooms, stunning views, and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home from Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary, here are some of the top choices:

  2. Local honey: Bhutanese honey is highly prized for its purity and unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Bhutanese culture has a strong emphasis on Buddhism and revolves around family, community, and the environment. Here are some customs to keep in mind while visiting Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

  2. Remove your shoes before entering homes or temples.

Best Time to Visit Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and dry. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be very cold and snowy.

Health in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Getting to Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

The nearest airport to Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is Paro International Airport, which is located in western Bhutan.

Bus Stations Near Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

There are several bus stations located near Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary, including the Samdrup Jongkhar Bus Station and the Trashigang Bus Station.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire in Bhutan, and some tour companies offer guided tours of Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary.

How to Get Around in Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

The best way to explore Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary is on foot or by bike. However, taxis and private cars are also available for hire if needed.
