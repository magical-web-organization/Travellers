Introduction to Phrumsengla National Park

Phrumsengla National Park is a pristine wilderness area in Eastern Bhutan, covering over 905 sq. km of stunning landscapes and unique wildlife. Located between Bhumthang, Trongsa, and Zhemgang, it is a natural wonderland with dense forests, cascading waterfalls, crystal-clear rivers, and snow-peaked mountains. The park is home to many endangered and rare species like the Asiatic black bear, takin, snow leopard, red panda, and the Himalayan Monal Pheasant.

Local Culture and Customs of Phrumsengla National Park

  • The traditional attire of Bhutan is called ‘Kira’ for women and ‘Gho’ for men.
  • Buddhism is the main religion and belief system of Bhutan.
  • Respect for elders and those in positions of authority is an essential part of their culture.
  • The national sport is archery, and many Bhutanese also enjoy playing football.

Best Time to Visit Phrumsengla National Park

The best time to visit Phrumsengla National Park is from March to May and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, visiting during the offseason can also offer a unique experience and avoiding the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phrumsengla National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.
  • Keep the environment clean.

Don’t:

  • Point or touch religious artefacts.
  • Smoke or litter in public.
  • Cause harm to any wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phrumsengla National Park

  • Carry adequate warm clothing and rain gear when trekking in the park.
  • Hire a certified guide when trekking or hiking.
  • Be cautious when crossing rivers, as water levels can rise quickly.

Health in Phrumsengla National Park

  • Make sure to carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medication with you.
  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Be aware of altitude sickness when travelling to higher elevations.
