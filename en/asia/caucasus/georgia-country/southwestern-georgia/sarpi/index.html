If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Georgia, you won’t want to miss Sarpi. Tucked away in the southwest corner of the country, Sarpi offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. Whether you’re an intrepid traveler or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Sarpi has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll show you the best things to do, see, and eat in Sarpi.

Introduction to Sarpi

Sarpi is a small village located in the region of Adjara, in southwestern Georgia. It sits on the coast of the Black Sea and is just a short drive from the Turkish border. Despite its small size, Sarpi is an important transportation hub, connecting Georgia to Turkey and beyond.

The village has a rich history, dating back to the 19th century when it was a major center of the tea trade. Today, Sarpi is home to several cultural attractions, including museums and historical sites, as well as stunning natural scenery, such as mountains, waterfalls, and beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Sarpi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sarpi is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich past. Some of the top attractions include:

  • The Tea Museum: Learn about the history of tea cultivation in Georgia and sample some of the finest teas in the region.
  • Gonio Fortress: This ancient Roman fortification dates back to the 1st century AD and is one of the oldest and best-preserved fortresses in the country.
  • Lazika Archaeological Museum: This museum showcases artifacts from the ancient kingdom of Lazica, which was located in the region around Sarpi.

Natural Attractions of Sarpi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sarpi is also a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top natural attractions in the area include:

  • Mtirala National Park: This park is home to several hiking trails that lead to waterfalls, mountain vistas, and more. It’s also home to a variety of flora and fauna, including several endemic species.
  • Batumi Botanical Garden: Just a short drive from Sarpi, this botanical garden is home to a stunning array of plant species from around the world.
  • Sarpi Beach: This pristine Black Sea beach is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing in the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarpi

Sarpi is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and coastline. Some of the top hiking routes include:

  • Mtirala National Park: This park is home to several trails that range in difficulty from easy to challenging. The Waterfall Trail is a must-see, taking hikers through dense forest to a stunning waterfall.
  • Makhuntseti Waterfall: This easy hike leads to a beautiful waterfall that cascades down a cliff face.
  • Kvariati-Sarpi Bike Route: This 12 km bike route follows the coastline from Sarpi to the nearby village of Kvariati and offers stunning views of the Black Sea.

Best Things to Do in Sarpi: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something to do in Sarpi, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Take a tour of one of the region’s many wineries and sample some of the world-class wines that Georgia is known for.
  • Batumi Cable Car Ride: Take a scenic ride up the mountain to enjoy panoramic views of Batumi and the surrounding coastline.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Georgian dishes with a local chef.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sarpi

If you’re planning a trip to Sarpi, here’s a one-week itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Tea Museum and take a hike in Mtirala National Park. Day 2: Take a tour of Gonio Fortress and visit the Lazika Archaeological Museum. Day 3: Relax on Sarpi Beach and take a dip in the Black Sea. Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Georgian dishes. Day 5: Explore Batumi Botanical Garden and take a cable car ride up the mountain. Day 6: Visit a local winery and enjoy a wine tasting tour. Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Batumi and explore its many attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Sarpi

Georgian cuisine is famous around the world for its bold flavors and unique combinations of ingredients. Sarpi is no exception, and there are plenty of delicious dishes and beverages to try during your visit. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khachapuri: This cheese-filled bread is a staple of Georgian cuisine and comes in many varieties.
  • Khinkali: These meat-filled dumplings are a Georgian specialty and are usually served with a spicy dipping sauce.
  • Mtsvadi: This dish consists of grilled chunks of meat (usually pork or lamb) served on skewers.

Local Beverages

  • Georgian Wine: Georgia has been producing wine for over 8,000 years, and the country is famous for its unique “qvevri” winemaking technique.
  • Chacha: This grape-based brandy is a popular after-dinner drink in Georgia and is known for its high alcohol content.

Where to Stay in Sarpi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sarpi has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to upscale hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Guesthouse Nana: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the Black Sea.
  • Sarpi Beach Camp: This budget-friendly option offers tents and bungalows just steps from the beach.
  • Sheraton Batumi Hotel: This upscale hotel is just a short drive from Sarpi and offers luxurious amenities such as a spa and restaurant.

What to Buy in Sarpi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring back from Sarpi, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some popular items include:

  • Local Honey: Georgia is famous for its honey, which comes in many varieties and is known for its rich flavor.
  • Handwoven Textiles: You can find beautiful handwoven textiles such as carpets and tablecloths in the markets of Sarpi.
  • Chacha: Bring home a bottle of this unique Georgian brandy as a gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarpi

Georgian culture is steeped in tradition and hospitality, and this is evident in the warm welcome that visitors receive in Sarpi. Some important customs to be aware of include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Toasting is an important part of Georgian culture, so be prepared to raise a glass frequently.

Best Time to Visit Sarpi

The best time to visit Sarpi is in the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the busiest time of year, so be prepared for crowds. If you prefer a quieter experience, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of spring (April-May) or fall (September-October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Sarpi

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including traditional dishes such as khinkali and khachapuri.
  • Learn about the history of tea cultivation in the region by visiting the Tea Museum.
  • Take a hike in Mtirala National Park to experience the stunning natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Drink tap water, as it may not be safe to drink.
  • Try to bring back more than two bottles of wine per person when leaving Georgia.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarpi

Overall, Sarpi is a safe destination for travelers. However, be aware of your surroundings and take normal precautions such as keeping your valuables secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Sarpi

There are no specific health risks associated with traveling to Sarpi. However, it’s always a good idea to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and to use caution when trying new foods or drinks.

Getting to Sarpi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sarpi

The nearest airport to Sarpi is Batumi International Airport, which is located approximately 19 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or public transportation to get to Sarpi.

Bus Stations Near Sarpi

There are several bus stations near Sarpi, including the Batumi Central Bus Station and the Sarpi Bus Station. You can take buses from Tbilisi and other major cities to get to Sarpi.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or take a shared taxi (known as a marshrutka) to Sarpi from Batumi or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Sarpi

The best way to get around Sarpi is by car or taxi. There are no public transportation options within the village, so it’s a good idea to have your own transportation or to arrange for a taxi or private driver.

Where to Go Next from Sarpi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Sarpi, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Some top picks include:

  • Batumi: This vibrant seaside city is just a short drive from Sarpi and offers plenty of attractions, including beaches, museums, and botanical gardens.
  • Mt. Kazbek: This stunning mountain in the Caucasus range is a popular destination for hikers and climbers.
  • Tbilisi: Georgia’s capital city is located several hours’ drive from Sarpi but is well worth the trip for its historic sites, art galleries, and lively dining scene.

Conclusion

Sarpi is a hidden gem in Georgia, offering a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking to hike in the mountains, relax on the beach, or explore ancient history, Sarpi has something for everyone. Plan your next adventure today!
