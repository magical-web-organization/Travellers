Introduction to Likhauri

Likhauri is a small village located in the southwestern part of Georgia, nestled in the Caucasus Mountains. Despite its size, Likhauri boasts of stunning natural beauty, rich cultural attractions, and a pleasant climate that makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and gastronomic adventurers. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to discover the hidden gem of Southwestern Georgia.

List of Places to Visit in Likhauri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Likhauri is steeped in history and culture, and the village has several museums and historical places that are worth a visit. These include:

  • Svaneti Museum of History and Ethnography: Located in the nearby town of Mestia, this museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich history and culture of the Svaneti region, including traditional costumes, crafts, and household items.

  • Lamaria Church: This ancient church is a beautiful example of Georgian architecture and is believed to date back to the 10th century. It is located in the village of Adishi, a short drive from Likhauri.

Natural Attractions of Likhauri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Likhauri’s natural beauty is truly awe-inspiring, and there are plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Likhauri include:

  • Ushba Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is one of the most beautiful natural wonders of the Svaneti region. It is located in the nearby village of Becho and is accessible via a short hike.

  • Tetnuldi Ski Resort: If you’re visiting Likhauri during the winter months, be sure to hit the slopes at Tetnuldi Ski Resort. This popular ski destination offers a range of slopes for skiers of all levels and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Likhauri

Likhauri is surrounded by beautiful hiking and biking trails, and there’s no shortage of opportunities to explore the great outdoors. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Likhauri include:

  • Mestia to Ushguli Trek: This 4-day trek takes you through some of the most beautiful and remote areas of the Svaneti region. You’ll pass through several villages, see stunning mountain views, and experience the local culture firsthand.

  • Mount Tetnuldi Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of Mount Tetnuldi, the third-highest peak in Georgia. It is only recommended for experienced hikers, but the views from the top are truly spectacular.

Best Things to Do in Likhauri: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Likhauri. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the beautiful countryside of Likhauri on horseback. There are several local guides who offer horseback riding tours of the region.

  • Cultural Tours: Take a guided tour of the local museums and historical sites to learn more about the rich history and culture of the Svaneti region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Likhauri

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Likhauri, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Likhauri, settle in, and explore the village on foot.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour of the surrounding countryside.

Day 3: Visit the Svaneti Museum of History and Ethnography in Mestia.

Day 4: Explore the nearby village of Adishi and visit the Lamaria Church.

Day 5: Take a horseback riding tour of the region.

Day 6: Visit the Ushba Waterfall and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 7: Enjoy a day of skiing at Tetnuldi Ski Resort before departing Likhauri.

Food and Drink Scene of Likhauri

Must-Try Dishes

Georgian cuisine is legendary, and Likhauri is no exception. Some of the must-try dishes in the region include:

  • Khachapuri: This cheese-filled bread is a Georgian classic and is a must-try when visiting Likhauri.

  • Sulguni Cheese: This local cheese is often used in traditional Georgian dishes and is delicious on its own or as a topping for khachapuri.

  • Khinkali: These dumplings are usually filled with meat or cheese and are a popular snack in Georgia.

Local Beverages

Georgian wine is also famous around the world, and Likhauri has several local wineries that are worth a visit. Some of the most popular local wines include:

  • Saperavi: This red wine is one of the most popular in Georgia.

  • Rkatsiteli: This white wine is also popular in the region and is often paired with Georgian cuisine.

Where to Stay in Likhauri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Likhauri has a few accommodation options that cater to different styles and budgets.

  • Guesthouses: For a truly local experience, consider staying in a local guesthouse. These typically offer a more intimate experience and a taste of traditional Georgian hospitality.

  • Hotels: Likhauri has several hotels that cater to both business and leisure travelers, with modern amenities and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Likhauri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Likhauri include:

  • Svanuri salt: This unique salt is made from local herbs and spices and is a popular seasoning in Georgian cuisine.

  • Svanuri wool: Likhauri is known for its high-quality woolen products, including hats, scarves, and blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Likhauri

Georgian culture and customs are unique and fascinating, and it’s worth taking the time to learn about them during your visit to Likhauri. Some of the local customs to be aware of include:

  • Supra: Georgian feasts known as supras are a common occurrence in the region and involve copious amounts of food, drink, and toasting.

  • Tamada: A tamada is a toastmaster who presides over the supra and is responsible for leading the toasts.

Best Time to Visit Likhauri

The best time to visit Likhauri is from late spring to early fall (May to September), when the weather is warm and dry and the hiking routes are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Likhauri

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, including Georgian wine and cheese.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes in religious sites.
  • Do explore the great outdoors and take advantage of the region’s hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, as it may cause stomach issues.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural attractions during your visit.
  • Don’t be disrespectful towards local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Likhauri

Likhauri is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always advised to exercise caution and common sense. Some tips for staying safe in Likhauri include:

  • Stick to well-lit and well-populated areas, especially at night.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items with you.
  • Always lock your accommodation and keep your belongings secure.

Health in Likhauri

While Likhauri is generally a healthy destination, it’s always good to take some precautions. Here are some tips for staying healthy during your visit:

  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach issues.
  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as ticks can be an issue in the area.
  • Make sure you have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Likhauri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Likhauri

The nearest airports to Likhauri are:

  • Kutaisi International Airport: This airport is located about 125km from Likhauri and offers direct flights to several European destinations.
  • Tbilisi International Airport: This airport is about 425km from Likhauri and offers a wider range of international connections.

Bus Stations Near Likhauri

The nearest bus station to Likhauri is in Mestia, about 10km away. Daily buses connect Mestia to other Georgian destinations and regional centers.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are also available for those who prefer a more comfortable and convenient option.

How to Get Around in Likhauri

The best way to get around Likhauri is by foot or horseback. Alternatively, taxis and private transfers can be arranged for longer distances or to visit more remote attractions.

Where to Go Next from Likhauri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Likhauri is a great base from which to explore the rest of the Svaneti region. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Ushguli: This remote mountain village is one of the highest settlements in Europe and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Georgia’s Black Sea Coast: The coastal city of Batumi and the seaside resort of Kobuleti are easily accessible from Likhauri and offer a different experience of Georgia.
  • Mestia: This nearby town is the cultural and administrative center of the Svaneti region and is home to several important historical sites and museums.

Conclusion

Likhauri is a hidden gem in Southwestern Georgia that offers a combination of stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re an outdoor adventurer, a history buff, or a foodie, Likhauri has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today and discover the magic of this off-the-beaten-path destination.
