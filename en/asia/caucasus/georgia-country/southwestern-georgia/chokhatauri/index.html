Introduction to Chokhatauri

Chokhatauri is a small town located in the southwestern province of Georgia, known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by the lush green forests of the Caucasus Mountains, this charming town has something to offer to everyone.

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply looking for an off-the-beaten-path travel experience, Chokhatauri provides a glimpse of traditional Georgian life that is rarely seen by tourists.

In this guide, we will help you plan the perfect trip to Chokhatauri, by highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, and local food and drink specialties.

List of Places to Visit in Chokhatauri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Archeological Museum of Chokhatauri: This museum showcases the rich cultural history of the region, with exhibits ranging from ancient pottery and coins to medieval weaponry and textiles.
  • The Mgvimevi Monastery: This 6th-century monastery, nestled in a peaceful valley, is one of the most important religious sites in Georgia, and features stunning frescoes and intricate stonework.
  • The Martvili Monastery: Nestled in scenic hills, this 7th-century monastery contains some of the most well-preserved examples of medieval Georgian architecture, with iconic stone arches and ornate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Chokhatauri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Okatse Canyon: This breathtaking natural wonder features stunning waterfalls, crystal-clear pools, and a thrilling suspension bridge that provides panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Kintrishi Nature Reserve: Home to diverse flora and fauna, this nature reserve is a paradise for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. Trails wind through lush forests and meadows, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Chokhatauri Arboretum: This botanical garden showcases the region’s diverse plant life, with a vast collection of rare trees, flowers, and shrubs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chokhatauri

Chokhatauri is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many scenic routes that lead you through the stunning landscapes of the Caucasus Mountains. Some popular routes include:

  • The Okatse Canyon Trail: This 2.7km trail takes you through the heart of the Okatse Canyon, with stunning views of waterfalls and natural pools.
  • The Kintrishi Trail: This challenging 20km trail takes you through the Kintrishi Nature Reserve, with stunning views of waterfalls, rivers, and the surrounding mountains.
  • The Chokhatauri Arboretum Trail: This easy 1.3km trail takes you through the lush gardens of the Chokhatauri Arboretum, with stunning views of rare trees and flowers.

Best Things to Do in Chokhatauri: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Wine Tasting Tour: Chokhatauri is famous for its fine wines. Take a tour of local wineries and savor some of the region’s best vintages.
  • Take a Horseback Riding Tour: Experience the beauty of the Caucasus Mountains on horseback.
  • Visit Local Markets: Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting a traditional Georgian bazaar, where you can find everything from fresh produce to handmade souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Chokhatauri

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khachapuri: A traditional Georgian cheese bread that is a staple of local cuisine.
  • Chakapuli: A delicious lamb stew with herbs, sour plums, and white wine.
  • Mtsvadi: Grilled pork or lamb skewers, usually served with onions and a vinegar-based sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Georgian Wine: Georgia has a long tradition of wine-making, and local wines are famous for their rich flavor and unique aroma.
  • Chacha: A strong, clear grape brandy that is often served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Chokhatauri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chokhatauri has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Rioni: A comfortable mid-range hotel located in the heart of Chokhatauri, with easy access to local attractions.
  • Guesthouse Ushba: A cozy guesthouse overlooking the Caucasus Mountains, with beautiful views and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • Hostel Riverside: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of the town, with shared dorms and private rooms available.

What to Buy in Chokhatauri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: Bottles of local wines make excellent souvenirs or gifts for friends and family back home.
  • Handmade Crafts: Visit a local market or artisan shop to find unique handmade products such as woolen carpets, ceramics, and traditional clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Chokhatauri

Chokhatauri has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, music, and art. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites or using polite language when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Chokhatauri

The best time to visit Chokhatauri is between May and September when the weather is sunny and warm. However, the town can be crowded during peak tourist season, so visitors looking for a quieter experience may prefer to visit in the shoulder season (April and October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Chokhatauri

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local wine and cuisine.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local culture or customs.
  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chokhatauri

Chokhatauri is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low rates of violent crime. However, visitors should take usual safety precautions such as locking valuables in hotel safes, avoiding unlit areas at night, and being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Chokhatauri

Visitors to Chokhatauri should ensure that they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella. It is also recommended that visitors bring any necessary medications with them, as pharmacies in Chokhatauri may have limited stock.

Getting to Chokhatauri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chokhatauri is easily accessible by several forms of transportation. Some options include:

Airports Near Chokhatauri

  • Batumi International Airport: The closest international airport to Chokhatauri, located approximately 75km to the west.
  • Tbilisi International Airport: Located approximately 190km to the east.

Bus Stations Near Chokhatauri

  • Chokhatauri Bus Station: The main bus station in Chokhatauri, with daily connections to major cities throughout Georgia.

Other Options

  • Private car: Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi from Tbilisi to Chokhatauri.
  • Train: A train service connects Chokhatauri to major cities throughout Georgia.

How to Get Around in Chokhatauri

The best way to explore Chokhatauri is by foot or bicycle, as many of the town’s attractions are located within walking or cycling distance. Taxis are also available for visitors who need transportation.

Where to Go Next from Chokhatauri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Batumi: Located approximately 75km to the west, Batumi is a coastal city known for its beautiful beaches, bustling nightlife, and modern architecture.
  • Kutaisi: Known as the “second city” of Georgia, Kutaisi is a historic city located approximately 60km to the east of Chokhatauri, with numerous cultural and natural attractions.
  • Svaneti: This remote mountainous region in northern Georgia is a haven for hikers and nature lovers, with stunning landscapes and traditional villages.

Conclusion

Chokhatauri is a hidden gem of Georgian tourism that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With this comprehensive guide, you are now ready to plan your perfect trip to this picturesque town, with all the information you need on where to stay, what to eat, and what to do. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, historic landmarks, or just a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, Chokhatauri is a destination that should not be missed.
