Introduction to Poti

Located in the northwestern region of Georgia, Poti is a charming city that should not be missed during your travels in Georgia. With a unique blend of cultural heritage and outdoor adventure opportunities, Poti attracts visitors seeking to experience the best of both worlds.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do and see in Poti, local cuisines, accommodation and transportation options, as well as tips to help you make the most of your visit to this wonderful city.

List of Places to Visit in Poti

Poti boasts a wide range of attractions, ranging from culturally significant museums and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor activities. Here are some of the must-visit places in Poti.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Poti Archeological Museum: This museum houses a collection of fascinating archeological artifacts from the Bronze Age to the Middle Ages. It is perfect for history lovers and provides insights into the evolution of life in Poti over the ages.
  • Poti City Hall: This 19th century building is a testament to the city’s history and cultural heritage. It is one of the oldest town halls in Georgia with a unique architecture.
  • Poti Lighthouse: One of the iconic structures of Poti is this lighthouse, which provides visitors with stunning views of the sea and the surrounding areas.
  • Poti Cathedral: This beautiful Orthodox cathedral is one of the city’s most recognizable landmarks, and it is a must-visit for its stunning architecture and historical relevance.

Natural Attractions of Poti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kolkheti National Park: This nature reserve is a paradise for nature lovers. Visitors can explore its lush forests, wetlands and freshwater lakes while viewing a variety of wildlife.
  • Mtirala National Park: This is another national park that is perfect for hikers, bird watchers and nature lovers. The park features a unique microclimate and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Kolkhida Beach: This stunning beach is one of the must-see attractions in Poti, with its crystal-clear waters and white sand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poti

Poti offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with several trails catering to all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Mtirala National Park Trail: Known for its lush vegetation and wildlife, this trail is perfect for nature enthusiasts seeking an adventure.
  • Kolkheti National Park Trail: This trail offers stunning scenery as it meanders through the park’s wetlands, lakes, and forests.
  • Poti - Kutaisi Cycling Route: This route connects the cities of Poti and Kutaisi, passing through several picturesque villages along the way. It is ideal for cycling enthusiasts and provides incredible views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Poti: Activities and Tours

In Poti, there is no shortage of activities and tours to keep visitors occupied. Here are some suggestions:

  • Boat tours: Enjoy the spectacular views of the seascape and coastal landscapes aboard a boat.
  • Fishing: Sea fishing is one of the popular activities in Poti, and there are several fishing tour operators in the region.
  • Beach activities: At Kolkhida beach, visitors can enjoy a range of beach activities such as swimming, sunbathing, and beach volleyball.
  • City tours: Explore the city on foot or with a guided city tour to discover the many cultural and historical sites that Poti offers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poti

If you’re planning a week-long itinerary for Poti, here’s a suggested schedule that covers the city’s top attractions:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the city’s historical and cultural sites, including Poti Archeological Museum, Poti City Hall, Poti Lighthouse, and Poti Cathedral
Day 2 Visit Kolkheti National Park, take a boat tour, and enjoy the scenery.
Day 3 Hike the trails at Mtirala National park and explore the park’s unique microclimate and diverse flora and fauna.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Kutaisi and discover its cultural and historical landmarks, such as the Bagrati Cathedral and the Gelati Monastery.
Day 5 Visit the Kolkhida beach and enjoy beach activities such as swimming and sunbathing.
Day 6 Take a city tour of Poti and discover the city’s lesser-known attractions, such as the Poti Botanical Garden area.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine, go souvenir shopping, and prepare for your journey onwards.

Food and Drink Scene of Poti

Must-Try Dishes

Poti’s cuisine is a blend of traditional Georgian and Black Sea cuisines, which makes it an exciting culinary destination. Some local favourite dishes include:

  • Chakhokhbili: A Georgian dish consisting of chicken stew with tomatoes, onions, and spices.
  • Adjaruli Khachapuri: A delicious Georgian bread filled with cheese, butter, and egg.
  • Kharcho: A beef or lamb soup with walnuts and spices.
  • Sinori: A popular Black Sea dish consisting of cornmeal dough stuffed with cheese and baked in a pot.

Local Beverages

No trip to Poti would be complete without sampling some of the local beverages. Some of the must-try drinks include:

  • Wine: Poti produces high-quality wine, and visitors can sample some of the region’s finest at local wineries.
  • Cha-cha: A clear spirit distilled from grape skins and stems. It is a popular drink among locals and visitors alike.
  • Beer: Georgian beer is an excellent thirst quencher on hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Poti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Poti offers an array of accommodation options that suit different budgets and interests.

  • Poti Palace Hotel: This four-star hotel is located in the city center and offers luxurious accommodation and a wide range of amenities, including a pool and a spa.
  • Motel-George: A budget-friendly option, this hostel is a short distance from the city center and features comfortable, clean rooms, and a friendly staff.
  • Saba Guest House: A comfortable bed and breakfast run by a friendly family, Saba Guest House is located close to the Kolkhida beach and offers delicious Georgian breakfast.

What to Buy in Poti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Poti has a thriving local market where visitors can find a range of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Handmade crafts: Poti is famous for its handmade pottery, wood carvings, and Georgian carpets.
  • Spices: Georgian spices are renowned for their aroma and distinct flavors. The local market offers a wide range of spices, including saffron, black pepper, and cinnamon.
  • Wine and Cha-cha: Poti produces high-quality cha-cha and wine, which make for a unique and memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Poti

Georgian culture is characterized by hospitality, warmth, and close family ties. Visitors should respect these traditions and customs when interacting with locals. For example, it is customary to bring gifts when visiting someone’s home, and it is polite to remove shoes before entering a Georgian home.

Best Time to Visit Poti

The best time to visit Poti is between June and September when the weather is warm, and the sea is ideal for swimming and other beach activities. The autumn season is also a great time to visit as the hills and valleys surrounding Poti turn into a picturesque golden colour.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poti

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Use a water bottle to reduce the amount of plastic waste.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash at public sites.
  • Don’t engage in street vending, it is illegal.
  • Don’t enter religious sites without proper attire.

Safety and Health

Safety in Poti

Poti is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should be vigilant and take precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended, especially at the beach. It is also recommended to keep a copy of your passport and travel documents with you at all times.

Health in Poti

Public health in Poti is relatively good, and there are several hospitals and clinics available for medical emergencies. It is recommended that visitors obtain travel medical insurance before leaving home.

Getting to Poti: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Poti

The nearest international airport to Poti is Tbilisi International Airport, approximately 290 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Poti from Tbilisi. There is a small airport in Poti, but it is primarily for domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Poti

There are several bus stations in Poti that provide regular bus services to the surrounding cities and towns. Visitors can take a marshrutka (shared-taxi) or a bus to reach Poti from towns and cities in Georgia.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Poti. There are several car rental companies available in Tbilisi.

How to Get Around in Poti

Poti is a small city that can easily be explored on foot, by bike or by renting a taxi. Taxis in Poti are inexpensive, and visitors can hail one on the street or book them using apps like Yandex and Bolt.

Where to Go Next from Poti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Poti is an excellent starting point for exploring other parts of Georgia. Here are some destinations that you can visit from Poti:

  • Batumi: A coastal city renowned for its modern architecture, beautiful seaside boulevard and cultural landmarks.
  • Kutaisi: The second-largest city in Georgia, Kutaisi, promises to immerse visitors in the country’s ancient history and culture.
  • Mestia: This picturesque village nestled in the heart of the Caucasus Mountains offers a wide range of outdoor adventures, such as hiking, skiing and more.

Conclusion

Poti is an exceptional destination that offers visitors a range of cultural, historical, natural and outdoor vacation opportunities. With a range of activities and attractions, locals and visitors alike will enjoy the diverse offerings of this beautiful Georgian city. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Poti is the perfect travel destination for you!
