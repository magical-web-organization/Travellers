Introduction to Anaklia

Nestled in the northwestern part of Georgia, Anaklia is a charming resort town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. It is situated on the coast of the Black Sea, near the border with Abkhazia. Anaklia is a small town, but it is packed with numerous tourist attractions, such as historic landmarks, beautiful beaches, scenic hiking trails, and so much more. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Anaklia, so you can make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Anaklia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anaklia has a rich cultural history that dates back to ancient times, and there are many museums and historical sites that you can visit to learn more about it.

Anaklia Fortress

Situated on the picturesque hill overlooking the town, Anaklia Fortress is an ancient castle that dates back to the 17th century. The fortress offers a fascinating glimpse into the town’s rich history and affords visitors a stunning view of the sea and the town below.

Martvili Monastery

Located about an hour’s drive from Anaklia, Martvili Monastery is an ancient Georgian Orthodox church that dates back to the medieval period. Visitors can wander through the beautiful church and admire the stunning frescoes and murals that adorn its walls.

Natural Attractions of Anaklia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Anaklia is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, and visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, swimming, and more.

Anaklia Beach

One of the main attractions in Anaklia is its beautiful beach. The wide, sandy shore is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and playing in the water. The beach is lined with cafes and restaurants where visitors can enjoy a meal or a drink while taking in the view.

Inguri River

Located about half an hour’s drive from Anaklia, the Inguri River is a stunning natural wonder that offers visitors the chance to take a relaxing boat ride and explore the beautiful landscape at a leisurely pace.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anaklia

Anaklia is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a wide range of hiking and biking trails that wind through the beautiful mountains and forests that surround the town.

Kintrishi Trail

The Kintrishi Trail is a beautiful hiking route that leads visitors through the stunning Kintrishi Protected Areas. The trail is about 10 km long and offers stunning views of the mountains and forests that surround Anaklia.

Anaklia-Ganmukhuri Bike Trail

The Anaklia-Ganmukhuri Bike Trail is a popular route for biking enthusiasts. The trail runs along the coast of the Black Sea, offering visitors a stunning view of the sea and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Anaklia: Activities and Tours

Anaklia offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours to suit every taste and interest.

Boat Tour of the Black Sea

Visitors can take a boat tour of the Black Sea to explore the coastline and see some of the other attractions in the area, such as the Inguri River and the nearby Svaneti region.

Wine Tasting Tour

Georgia is known for its delicious wine, and visitors can take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards to sample some of the best wines the region has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anaklia

Anaklia has so much to offer that visitors could easily spend a week or more exploring the town and surrounding areas. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your visit.

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Anaklia Fortress and Anaklia Beach
Day 2 Explore the Kintrishi Trail and go on a boat tour of the Black Sea
Day 3 Take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards
Day 4 Visit the Martvili Monastery and explore the nearby Svaneti region
Day 5 Relax on the beach and enjoy the local cuisine
Day 6 Explore the Anaklia-Ganmukhuri Bike Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Anaklia

Georgia is known for its delicious cuisine, and Anaklia is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of local dishes and beverages during their stay.

Must-Try Dishes

Khachapuri

Khachapuri is a traditional Georgian dish that consists of bread filled with cheese, typically served hot and fresh.

Khinkali

Khinkali is a type of dumpling that is filled with meat, vegetables, and spices, and is typically served as an appetizer or side dish.

Local Beverages

Georgian Wine

Georgian wine is world-renowned for its delicious flavor and high quality. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of local wines during their stay in Anaklia.

Where to Stay in Anaklia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anaklia offers visitors a wide range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget.

Hotel Anaklia

Hotel Anaklia is a popular choice for visitors who want to stay in the heart of the town, close to all the major attractions.

Anaklia Guesthouse

Anaklia Guesthouse is a great option for visitors who want a more personal and relaxed experience. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Anaklia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anaklia offers a wide range of local products and souvenirs that visitors can bring home to remember their trip, such as handmade ceramics, traditional Georgian textiles, and delicious local honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Anaklia

Anaklia has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Anaklia

The best time to visit Anaklia is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is packed with tourists and locals enjoying the sunshine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anaklia

  • Explore the natural beauty of the town
  • Sample the local cuisine and wine

Safety and Health

Safety in Anaklia

Anaklia is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors are encouraged to take normal safety precautions, such as locking their doors at night and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Anaklia

Visitors should be up to date on their routine vaccinations and should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, as mosquito-borne illnesses like Zika virus and West Nile virus have been reported in the area.

Getting to Anaklia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Anaklia is accessible by a number of transportation options, including air, bus, and private vehicles.

Airports Near Anaklia

The nearest international airport to Anaklia is Batumi Airport, which is about 109 km south of Anaklia.

Bus Stations Near Anaklia

There are several bus stations in the vicinity of Anaklia, including Zugdidi Bus Station and Poti Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or hire private drivers to travel to Anaklia from other parts of Georgia.

How to Get Around in Anaklia

Anaklia is a small town, and most of the major attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Anaklia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anaklia is located near several other popular tourist destinations in Georgia. Visitors can take day trips to explore these destinations, including:

  • Batumi, a lively resort town on the Black Sea
  • Mestia, a scenic mountain village in the Svaneti region
  • Tbilisi, Georgia’s capital city with rich culture and history

Conclusion

Anaklia is a wonderful destination that offers a unique blend of cultural history, natural beauty, and adventure. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore the local museums, or conquer a challenging hiking trail, there’s something for everyone in Anaklia. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your visit and make the most of your time in this charming Georgian town.
