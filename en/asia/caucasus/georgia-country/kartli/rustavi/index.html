Introduction to Rustavi

Located in Kartli, Georgia, Rustavi is a charming city that boasts of rich history, culture, and natural beauty. The city is a melting pot of cultures and traditions and offers tourists a chance to experience the true Georgian way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Rustavi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you love history and architecture, Rustavi will not disappoint you. The city has several museums and historical sites that are worth visiting, including:

David Gareja Monastery

The David Gareja Monastery is an important religious site and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The complex consists of several monasteries and chapels that are built into the rocky hills. The monastery dates back to the 6th century and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Rustavi Fortress

The Rustavi Fortress is a 4th-century castle that served as a defensive structure during the Roman period. The castle has been partially preserved and offers tourists an insight into the architecture and defense strategies of the time.

Natural Attractions of Rustavi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rustavi is surrounded by scenic landscapes and offers several outdoor activities for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the natural attractions in Rustavi include:

Javakheti Plateau

The Javakheti Plateau is a high plateau region located in southern Georgia, near the border with Armenia and Turkey. The plateau is home to several beautiful lakes, including Lake Paliastomi, which is a popular spot for fishing and bird watching.

Tbilisi National Park

The Tbilisi National Park is located just a few kilometers from Rustavi and is a popular spot for hiking and camping. The park offers several trails that pass through scenic landscapes and provide stunning views of the surrounding region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rustavi

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, Rustavi has several hiking and biking routes that you can explore. Some popular routes include:

  • The David Gareja Monastery Trail
  • The Rustavi Bike Trail
  • The Javakheti Plateau Trail

Best Things to Do in Rustavi: Activities and Tours

Rustavi offers several activities and tours that you can enjoy during your visit. Some popular activities and tours include:

  • Wine Tasting Tour
  • Rustavi City Tour
  • Cooking Classes

Week-Long Itinerary for Rustavi

Day 1: Arrive in Rustavi and explore the city center.

Day 2: Visit David Gareja Monastery and go on a wine tasting tour.

Day 3: Explore the natural attractions of Rustavi, including Tbilisi National Park and Javakheti Plateau.

Day 4: Visit Rustavi Fortress and learn about the history of the city.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional Georgian dishes.

Day 6: Go on a day trip to Tbilisi and explore the capital city.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene in Rustavi.

Local Culture and Customs of Rustavi

Georgians are known for their hospitality and warm nature, so it’s not surprising that Rustavi has a welcoming and friendly culture. It’s important to respect local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and removing your shoes before entering a Georgian home.

Best Time to Visit Rustavi

The best time to visit Rustavi is between May and October when the weather is warmer and there are more outdoor activities and cultural events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rustavi

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Learn a few Georgian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rustavi

Rustavi is a safe city, but it’s important to take precautions when traveling, including:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.
  • Follow local customs and traditions to show respect to the locals.

Health in Rustavi

Visitors to Rustavi should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella (chickenpox), polio, and influenza.
