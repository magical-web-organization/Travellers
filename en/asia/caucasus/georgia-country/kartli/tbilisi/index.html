Introduction to Tbilisi

Tbilisi, the capital of Georgia, is a city situated in the Kartli region of the Caucasus. It is widely known for its rich history, vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and stunning natural landscape. The city is a melting pot of traditional and modern values, a fusion of east and west that is evident in its architecture, music, art, and cuisine. Tbilisi attracts millions of tourists every year, and once you discover its unique charm, you’ll understand why.

Food and Drink Scene of Tbilisi

Tbilisi’s food scene is a reflection of its unique cultural blend, with influences from Russia, the Middle East, Persia, and Europe. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khinkali (Georgian Meat Dumplings)
  • Khachapuri (Georgian Cheese Bread)
  • Chakhokhbili (Georgian Chicken Stew)
  • Lobio (Georgian Bean Stew)
  • Shashlik (Grilled Meat Skewers)

Local Beverages

  • Georgian Wine (Saperavi, Rkatsiteli, Kindzmarauli)
  • Chacha (Georgian Grape Brandy)
  • Tarragon Lemonade
  • Borjomi (Mineral Water)

Local Culture and Customs of Tbilisi

Georgian culture is steeped in tradition, and visitors are encouraged to learn about local customs and etiquette. Here are some notable cultural practices:

  • Hospitality and Guest Etiquette
  • Georgian Supra (Traditional Feast)
  • Georgian Folk Music and Dance
  • Orthodox Christianity

Best Time to Visit Tbilisi

The best time to visit Tbilisi is during the spring and fall when temperatures are mild and the crowds are fewer. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs and Etiquette

  • Disrespect Local Customs and Traditions
  • Show Public Displays of Affection

Safety and Health

Safety in Tbilisi

Tbilisi is generally a safe city to visit. However, tourists should exercise caution when traveling at night and in crowded areas. Pickpocketing can be an issue, so keep a close eye on your belongings.

Health in Tbilisi

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Tbilisi. Tap water is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water or boiled water.

Getting to Tbilisi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tbilisi

Tbilisi International Airport is the main airport serving the city, with flights to major European and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Tbilisi

Tbilisi Central Bus Station is located in the city center and offers both local and regional bus services.

Other Options

Tbilisi can also be reached by train from Yerevan, Armenia, and Baku, Azerbaijan. Shared taxis are also a popular option for getting around within Georgia.

How to Get Around in Tbilisi

Tbilisi has an extensive public transportation system, including buses, metro, and taxis. The metro is the most affordable and efficient way to get around, with two lines currently in operation.
