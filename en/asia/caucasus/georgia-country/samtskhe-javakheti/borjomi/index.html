Borjomi is a charming resort town located in the Samtskhe-Javakheti region of Georgia, tucked away in the breathtaking Caucasus Mountains. The town is famous for its mineral water sources, dense forests, and natural beauty. With so much to explore, from outdoor adventures to cultural highlights, plan your trip to Borjomi with this comprehensive travel guide. Explore the best of Georgia’s natural beauty in Borjomi.

Introduction to Borjomi

Borjomi is a popular tourist destination in Georgia, known for its healing mineral waters and natural beauty. Once a favorite among Russian aristocracy, the town offers visitors a glimpse into the country’s rich cultural heritage, as well as exciting outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Borjomi

There are many places to visit in and around Borjomi. Here is a list of the key attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Borjomi local history museum
  • Ktsia-Tabatskuri nature reserve
  • Rabati Castle
  • Historical Museum of Borjomi

Natural Attractions of Borjomi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Borjomi Central Park
  • Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park
  • The Green Monastery
  • Meskheti Range

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borjomi

Borjomi offers a variety of stunning hiking and biking routes that traverse the area’s breathtaking natural beauty. Here are some of the best:

  • Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park: Offers several hiking routes, ranging from easy to hard, with beautiful scenery and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Tsikhisjvari: A scenic bike trail that features steep climbs and beautiful, panoramic views.
  • Meskheti Range: Offers stunning vistas of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Borjomi: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an adventure-seeker, culture buff, or foodie, Borjomi has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Borjomi:

  • Take a dip in the town’s mineral water baths and Spa centers.
  • Explore the Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park with hiking or horse riding tours.
  • Visit the local wineries and try the famous Georgian wine.
  • Visit the Rabati Castle and museums to learn about the town’s history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Borjomi

Day 1: Visit the local museums and Rabati Castle, and learn about the town’s history.

Day 2: Take a tour of the Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park and enjoy the scenic vistas and diverse flora and fauna.

Day 3: Try the local cuisine and sample the famous Georgian wine.

Day 4: Hike the Meskheti Range and experience the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Day 5: Visit the local wineries and try the famous Georgian wine.

Day 6: Day trip to the nearby town of Akhaltsikhe and tour the Sapara Monastery.

Day 7: Take a dip in the town’s mineral water baths and Spa centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Borjomi

Georgian cuisine is famous for its unique flavors and ingredients, and Lorjomi is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khinkali (dumplings stuffed with meat and spices)
  • Khachapuri (cheese-filled bread)
  • Lobiani (bean-stuffed bread)
  • Mtsvadi (Georgian-style barbecued meat)
  • Chakhokhbili (chicken stew with tomatoes and herbs)

Local Beverages

  • Borjomi mineral water
  • Georgian wine

Local Culture and Customs of Borjomi

Georgian culture is known for its rich heritage, music, and dance. When visiting Borjomi, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Borjomi

The best time to visit Borjomi is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Borjomi

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Borjomi.

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Visit the Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Be rude or aggressive to locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Borjomi

Borjomi is a safe place to visit. However, it’s always essential to take precautions to ensure your safety. Don’t wander around deserted or unfamiliar areas at night and be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Borjomi

Borjomi is known for its healing mineral waters, and visitors often enjoy taking a dip in the town’s baths and spas. However, it is always advisable to drink bottled water and take precautions when eating street food or trying local cuisine.

Getting to Borjomi: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to reach Borjomi, including by air, bus, or train.

Airports Near Borjomi

The nearest airport to Borjomi is Tbilisi International Airport, which is about 165 km away.

Bus Stations Near Borjomi

There are several bus stations in Borjomi, including the Borjomi central bus station and Borjomi railway station.

Other Options

Another option is to take a train from Tbilisi to Borjomi. The train journey takes about three hours and is a scenic way to reach Borjomi.

How to Get Around in Borjomi?

Borjomi is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot. However, if you want to travel further afield, you can take a bus or hire a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Borjomi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Georgia, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering. Here are some of the best:

  • Akhaltsikhe: Visit the Sapara Monastery.
  • Vardzia: Visit the 12th-century cave city.

Conclusion

Borjomi is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience Georgia’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. With activities and attractions to suit all tastes and budgets, it’s no wonder that Borjomi is becoming an increasingly popular tourist destination. Plan your trip carefully and make the most of your time in this charming Georgian town.
