Abastumani is a picturesque mountain resort town located in the Samtskhe-Javakheti region of Georgia. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know about Abastumani, including its cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more.

Introduction to Abastumani

Abastumani is a charming retreat nestled among the Lower Caucasus Mountains. Its cool, crisp mountain air and lush natural surroundings make it the perfect place to relax and unwind. The town is situated at an altitude of 1,500 meters (4,921 ft) above sea level, and it is known for its mild climate, clear skies, and pristine nature.

Abastumani has a rich history dating back to the 19th century, when it was developed as a health resort by the Russian imperial family. Today, it is a popular destination for Georgians and tourists alike, offering a wide range of cultural and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Abastumani

Are you planning a trip to Abastumani for the first time? Here’s a list of must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nikoloz Baratashvili House-Museum - this museum is dedicated to the life and work of Nikoloz Baratashvili, one of Georgia’s most famous poets.
  • Abastumani Astrophysical Observatory - founded in 1932, this observatory is renowned for its cutting-edge research in astrophysics and atmospheric sciences.
  • Saint Andrew Church - this beautiful 19th-century church is located on a hill overlooking Abastumani and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Historical Ethnography Museum of Abastumani - this museum showcases the traditional culture and way of life of the local people.

Natural Attractions of Abastumani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Abastumani Forest Reserve - this protected area is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including beech and oak trees, bears, lynx, and wolves. Visitors can enjoy hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife photography.
  • Abastumani Central Park - this park is a great place to relax and enjoy a picnic. It features a small lake, walking paths, and playgrounds for children.
  • Zekari Pass - located at an altitude of 2,058 meters (6,752 ft) above sea level, this pass offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Tskhratskaro Lake - this lake is located in the Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park and is a popular spot for fishing and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abastumani

Abastumani is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Below are some popular routes:

  • Mount Kanobili - this challenging trail takes you to the summit of Mount Kanobili, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Abastumani-Green Lake Trail - this scenic trail takes you through the Abastumani Forest Reserve and ends at the beautiful Green Lake.
  • Bike Tour to Gomistskali Canyon - this thrilling bike tour takes you through the stunning Gomistskali Canyon, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Abastumani: Activities and Tours

Abastumani offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Tour of Abastumani Astrophysical Observatory - take a guided tour of this world-renowned observatory and learn about the latest discoveries in astrophysics.
  • Horseback Riding in Abastumani Forest Reserve - explore the beautiful Abastumani Forest Reserve on horseback and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Abastumani Food and Wine Tour - sample traditional Georgian cuisine and wines with a local expert guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Abastumani

Here’s our suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Abastumani:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel Day 2: Visit the Abastumani Observatory and take a guided tour Day 3: Explore the Abastumani Forest Reserve on foot or horseback Day 4: Take a day trip to Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park and Tskhratskaro Lake Day 5: Visit the Nikoloz Baratashvili House-Museum and the Historical Ethnography Museum Day 6: Enjoy a leisurely day in Abastumani Central Park Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Abastumani

Abastumani offers a unique culinary experience that reflects the flavours and traditions of the region. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mtsvadi - grilled Georgian pork kebabs
  • Khachapuri - traditional Georgian cheese-filled bread
  • Badrijani Nigvzit - eggplant rolls filled with walnut paste

Local Beverages

  • Georgian Wine - Georgian wine is famous worldwide, and Abastumani produces its own excellent varieties.
  • Borjomi - This naturally carbonated mineral water is sourced from a spring in nearby Borjomi.

Where to Stay in Abastumani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Guesthouse and Wine Cellar Qesani - this cozy guesthouse is known for its warm hospitality and delicious homemade Georgian food and wine.
  • Hotel Abastumani Palace - this elegant hotel is located in the heart of Abastumani and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

Local Culture and Customs of Abastumani

Abastumani has a unique cultural heritage that reflects the blending of Georgian, Armenian, and Russian traditions. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect the local dress code and conservative values when visiting mosques or churches.
  • Do not wear shoes inside someone’s home or a traditional Georgian restaurant.
  • Bring small gifts when invited to someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Abastumani

The best time to visit Abastumani is from late spring to early fall (May to September) when the weather is warm and pleasant. Winter visitors can enjoy skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abastumani

Do:

  • Try traditional Georgian foods and wines.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Keep the environment clean and tidy.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home or a traditional Georgian restaurant.
  • Speak loudly in a quiet area.
  • Take pictures of local people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abastumani

Abastumani is a safe destination for travelers, but it is always advisable to take precautions to avoid any mishaps. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.
  • Keep your valuables in a secure place and avoid flashing them in public.
  • Follow the local traffic rules and regulations.

Health in Abastumani

Abastumani has a well-equipped hospital and several pharmacies. However, it is always advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Abastumani: Transportation Options and Logistics

Abastumani is located approximately 250 km (155 miles) from Tbilisi, the capital of Georgia. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Abastumani

The closest airport is Kutaisi International Airport, located approximately 170 km (106 miles) away from Abastumani. Tbilisi International Airport is also an option, located approximately 270 km (168 miles) away.

Bus Stations Near Abastumani

There are several bus companies that operate daily services to Abastumani from Tbilisi, Kutaisi and other cities in Georgia.

Other Options

Private car transfers and car rentals are also available from Tbilisi and Kutaisi to Abastumani.

How to Get Around in Abastumani

Abastumani is a small town and easy to get around by foot. Taxis are also available for longer distances.
