Introduction to Akhalkalaki

Akhalkalaki is a small town located in the southern part of Georgia, sandwiched between the Turkish and Armenian borders. Despite its size, it is a culturally rich destination with a handful of historical attractions, natural wonders and outdoor activities to enjoy. In this guide, we’ll help you discover the best of Akhalkalaki, from its stunning landscapes to its delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Akhalkalaki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, Akhalkalaki is a must-visit destination with several museums and historic sites that offer a glimpse into the town’s past.

  • Akhalkalaki Museum: Housed in a Soviet-era building, the museum showcases artifacts from the region’s prehistoric and medieval periods, as well as its more recent Soviet history.

  • Khoshapeti Church: This medieval church is one of the oldest in Georgia, dating back to the 7th century. It features unique frescoes and is considered an important pilgrimage site for Georgian Orthodox Christians.

  • Vardzia: While not technically in Akhalkalaki, Vardzia is a must-visit destination for history buffs. This massive cave city is carved into the side of a cliff and dates back to the 12th century. It was once a thriving monastery and includes chapels, monastic cells, and even a wine cellar.

Natural Attractions of Akhalkalaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Akhalkalaki is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure or simply soaking up the views.

  • Lake Paravani: The largest lake in Georgia, Lake Paravani is located just outside of Akhalkalaki and is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and hiking.

  • Bavra Waterfall: This picturesque waterfall requires a short hike to reach, but the views are well worth the effort. It’s particularly beautiful in the spring when the snow is melting and the waterfall is at its strongest.

  • Javakheti Plateau: This vast highland area is dotted with small villages and is famous for its traditional way of life. Visitors can hike, bike or horseback ride across the hills, or simply take in the stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akhalkalaki

Akhalkalaki is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking routes to choose from.

  • Aragatsotn Loop Trail: This 14km trail takes visitors through the hills and forests surrounding Lake Paravani. There are several scenic spots along the way, including a panoramic viewpoint overlooking the lake.

  • Javakheti Plateau Trail: For a longer hike, try this 52km loop that takes hikers across the highland meadows and through traditional Javakheti villages.

Best Things to Do in Akhalkalaki: Activities and Tours

Akhalkalaki offers several opportunities for visitors to immerse themselves in local culture and explore the town and its surroundings.

  • Nordic Skiing: During the winter months, Lake Paravani freezes over and becomes a popular destination for Nordic skiing. Several tour operators offer guided tours and ski equipment rentals.

  • Wine Tasting: The region surrounding Akhalkalaki is known for its wine production, and visitors can sample some of the best local wines at the Akhalkalaki Wine Cellar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akhalkalaki

If you have a week to spend in Akhalkalaki, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Akhalkalaki and explore the town’s historic sites and museums.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Vardzia.
  • Day 3: Hike to Bavra Waterfall.
  • Day 4: Nordic ski on Lake Paravani.
  • Day 5: Visit the Akhalkalaki Wine Cellar for a tasting.
  • Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Javakheti Plateau.
  • Day 7: Depart from Akhalkalaki.

Food and Drink Scene of Akhalkalaki

Must-Try Dishes

Georgian cuisine is world-renowned for its bold flavors and unique ingredients, and Akhalkalaki is no exception.

  • Khinkali: These meat-filled dumplings are a staple in Georgian cuisine and are especially delicious when paired with a glass of local wine.

  • Kharcho: A hearty soup made with beef, rice, and Georgian spices.

  • Khashi: A traditional winter dish made from cow or sheep feet cooked in a spicy broth.

Local Beverages

In addition to its wine production, Akhalkalaki is also famous for its mineral water.

  • Borjomi: This carbonated mineral water is sourced from a nearby spring and is said to have health benefits.

  • Jermuk: A similar mineral water to Borjomi, Jermuk is sourced from Armenia and is also popular in Akhalkalaki.

Where to Stay in Akhalkalaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Akhalkalaki has several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to more upscale hotels.

  • Grand Akhalkalaki Hotel: A modern and comfortable hotel located in the town center.

  • Tatev Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse in a traditional Javakheti village, offering guests a chance to experience local culture.

  • Javakheti Eco-Hotel: Located in the hills outside of Akhalkalaki, this eco-friendly hotel offers stunning views and plenty of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Akhalkalaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For souvenir shopping, head to one of the local markets or shops to find unique gifts and local products.

  • Wool Products: Javakheti is known for its wool production, and visitors can find everything from traditional wool carpets to cozy sweaters and hats.

  • Arseniko Cheese: This local sheep cheese is made in the nearby villages and is a delicious souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Akhalkalaki

The Javakheti region has a unique culture that blends traditional Georgian elements with the customs of its Armenian and Turkish neighbors. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and sensibilities.

Best Time to Visit Akhalkalaki

The best time to visit Akhalkalaki is in the spring and summer when the weather is mild and the hiking trails and outdoor activities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akhalkalaki

Do:

  • Dress modestly and appropriately, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local cuisine and wine.
  • Learn basic Georgian phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Take photos without asking for permission.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akhalkalaki

Akhalkalaki is generally a safe destination for visitors, but it’s still important to take usual precautions, such as avoiding nighttime walks in unfamiliar areas and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Akhalkalaki

Visitors should drink only bottled water and be aware of the potential for altitude sickness when hiking in the highlands.

Getting to Akhalkalaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akhalkalaki

The nearest international airport to Akhalkalaki is Tbilisi International Airport, located approximately 300 km away. From there, visitors can take a bus, taxi or rental car to Akhalkalaki.

Bus Stations Near Akhalkalaki

Several buses run between Tbilisi and Akhalkalaki daily, with most departing from Didube Bus Station.

Other Options

For a more scenic journey to Akhalkalaki, consider taking the train from Tbilisi to Gyumri, Armenia, and then taking a bus or taxi to Akhalkalaki.

How to Get Around in Akhalkalaki

The town is small enough to explore on foot, and visitors can also rent a bicycle or take a taxi for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Akhalkalaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore more of Georgia, consider visiting the nearby city of Borjomi, known for its hot springs and national park. Alternatively, head south to the Armenian border to visit the ancient town of Gyumri.

Conclusion

Akhalkalaki may not be the most well-known destination in Georgia, but it’s certainly worth a visit for those looking to explore the country’s off-the-beaten-path destinations. From its historic sites to its beautiful natural landscapes and delicious cuisine, Akhalkalaki has something for every kind of traveler.
