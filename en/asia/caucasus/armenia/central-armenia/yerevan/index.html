Introduction to Yerevan

Located in central Armenia, in the Caucasus region of Asia, Yerevan is a city steeped in rich history and culture. It is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, and its strategic location has made it a hub for trade, art, and innovation over the centuries. Today, Yerevan is a bustling metropolis that boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, cultural landmarks, and plenty of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Yerevan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yerevan is full of museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into its rich past. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Armenian Genocide Memorial Complex: A powerful museum and memorial dedicated to the victims of the Armenian Genocide.
  • Matenadaran: A repository of ancient Armenian manuscripts and other rare books.
  • Erebuni Fortress: The ruins of an ancient fortress that dates back to the 8th century BC.
  • Armenian National Museum of History: A comprehensive museum with exhibits covering all aspects of Armenian history.
  • Yerevan Cascade: A unique outdoor sculpture museum that contains works by both Armenian and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Yerevan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yerevan is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, and there are plenty of ways to explore its scenic outdoor attractions. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Mount Ararat: One of the most iconic landmarks in Armenia, this snow-capped mountain offers stunning views and is said to be the resting place of Noah’s Ark.
  • Garni Gorge: A scenic gorge that offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning views.
  • Lake Sevan: One of the largest freshwater lakes in the world, Lake Sevan is a popular destination for swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Khosrov Forest State Reserve: A protected area with forests, canyons, and hiking trails that offer a glimpse into Armenia’s diverse flora and fauna.
  • Victory Park: A park with panoramic views of Yerevan and the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yerevan

For those who love outdoor adventures, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails around Yerevan. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Hrazdan Gorge: A scenic hike that follows the Hrazdan River and offers breathtaking views of the city and surrounding cliffs.
  • Khosrov Forest State Reserve: This protected area has plenty of hiking trails that are perfect for exploring the forest and canyons.
  • Dilijan National Park: Located about an hour’s drive from Yerevan, this park has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Mount Aragats: The highest peak in Armenia, Mount Aragats has several hiking trails that are perfect for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Yerevan: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Yerevan. Some of the best include:

  • Food and wine tours: Yerevan has a vibrant food scene, and there are plenty of tours that offer the chance to sample local dishes and wines.
  • City tours: Walking tours of the city are a great way to explore Yerevan’s rich history and culture.
  • Armenian dance classes: For those who want to experience Armenian culture firsthand, taking a dance class is a must-do activity.
  • Hot air balloon rides: A hot air balloon ride over Yerevan offers a unique perspective on the city and its surroundings.
  • Skiing and snowboarding: The Tsaghkadzor ski resort, located about an hour’s drive from Yerevan, offers plenty of slopes for winter sports enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yerevan

If you have a week to spend in Yerevan, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the city on foot, visiting landmarks such as Republic Square and the Armenian Genocide Memorial Complex.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Garni Temple and Geghard Monastery, two UNESCO World Heritage Sites located outside the city.
  • Day 3: Visit museums and historical sites such as Matenadaran and the Armenian National Museum of History.
  • Day 4: Hike or bike in the Khosrov Forest State Reserve.
  • Day 5: Take a food and wine tour of the city.
  • Day 6: Visit Lake Sevan and enjoy water activities such as swimming and boating.
  • Day 7: Take a hot air balloon ride over the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Yerevan

Must-Try Dishes

Armenian cuisine is known for its fresh ingredients and bold flavors. Some of the must-try dishes in Yerevan include:

  • Khorovats: Grilled meat, usually pork or beef, marinated in herbs and spices.
  • Lavash: Thin, soft flatbread that is a staple in Armenian cuisine.
  • Dolma: Grape leaves stuffed with a mixture of rice, herbs, and sometimes meat.
  • Harissa: A porridge made from wheat and meat that is traditionally served during the winter months.
  • Armenian wine: Armenia has a long history of winemaking, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries around Yerevan that offer tastings and tours.

Local Beverages

In addition to wine, Yerevan also has plenty of local beverages to try, including:

  • Tan: A sour yogurt drink that is usually served cold.
  • Tarkhun: A sweet, carbonated drink made from tarragon.
  • Coffee: Armenian coffee is strong and usually served with a piece of lokum, or Turkish delight.
  • Beer: Yerevan has several microbreweries that offer a variety of locally brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Yerevan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yerevan has plenty of accommodation options for all budgets. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Marriott Yerevan: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Envoy Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms.
  • Tufenkian Historic Yerevan Hotel: A boutique hotel located in a historic building in the city center.
  • Grand Hotel Yerevan: A classic hotel with a rooftop pool that offers stunning views of the city.

What to Buy in Yerevan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Yerevan include:

  • Armenian cognac: This high-quality brandy is a favorite among locals and makes a great souvenir.
  • Khachkar: A carved stone cross that is an iconic symbol of Armenian culture and religion.
  • Armenian carpet: Armenian carpets are known for their intricate designs and high quality.
  • Lavash: Purchase a package of this delicious flatbread to bring home.
  • Armenian wines: Bring home a bottle of Armenian wine as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Yerevan

Armenians are known for their warm hospitality, and visitors to Yerevan can expect a friendly reception. Respect for elders and family values are an important part of Armenian culture, and visitors are expected to show respect for these values as well. Additionally, dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Yerevan

The best time to visit Yerevan is during the spring (April to June) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. However, the city is beautiful year-round, and there are plenty of indoor activities to enjoy during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yerevan

Do:

  • Do try Armenian food and wine.
  • Do dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Do be respectful of Armenian customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect Armenian cultural values, such as respect for elders and family.
  • Don’t take photos in churches without asking permission.
  • Don’t visit the country without doing some research on its history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yerevan

Yerevan is a safe city for tourists, but as with any city, there is always a risk of petty crime. Visitors should take precautions such as carrying valuables in a secure bag and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Yerevan

Tap water is safe to drink in Yerevan, but visitors with sensitive stomachs may want to stick to bottled water. Visitors should also be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Armenia.

Getting to Yerevan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yerevan

Zvartnots International Airport is the main airport serving Yerevan. It is located about 12 kilometers from the city center and offers flights to destinations all over the world.

Bus Stations Near Yerevan

The Yerevan Central Bus Station is located in the city center and offers bus connections to destinations all over Armenia and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Yerevan by train, with connections to destinations such as Tbilisi, Georgia, and Tabriz, Iran.

How to Get Around in Yerevan

Yerevan is a walkable city, and many of its major landmarks are within walking distance of each other. There are also plenty of public transportation options, including buses, minibusses, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Yerevan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Yerevan include:

  • Tatev Monastery: A stunning monastery that is located about four hours’ drive from Yerevan.
  • Dilijan National Park: A scenic nature reserve that is located about an hour’s drive from Yerevan.
  • Khor Virap Monastery: A historic monastery located at the foot of Mount Ararat, about an hour’s drive from Yerevan.
  • Gyumri: The second-largest city in Armenia, Gyumri is known for its historic architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • Lake Sevan: A popular destination for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Conclusion

Yerevan is a vibrant, welcoming city with a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and plenty of cultural landmarks and activities to explore. Whether you’re interested in city tours, hiking adventures, or food and wine tastings, Yerevan has something for everyone.
