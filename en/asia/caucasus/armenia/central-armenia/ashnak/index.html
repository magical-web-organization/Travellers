Introduction to Ashnak

Located in central Armenia, Ashnak is a small, but charming village that boasts of scenic views and natural wonders. This hidden gem is perfect for those who are looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience, as it is relatively untouched by mass tourism.

In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know about Ashnak, including a list of places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a family with kids, or a group of friends, Ashnak has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ashnak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ashnak Museum - this small museum showcases the history and culture of Ashnak, and is a great place to learn about the village’s past.
  • Saghmosavank Monastery - located just a few kilometers from Ashnak, this medieval monastery offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hovhannavank Monastery - similar to Saghmosavank, this monastery is also located near Ashnak and boasts of beautiful architecture and breathtaking views.

Natural Attractions of Ashnak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Aragats - this dormant volcano offers a challenging, yet rewarding, hiking experience. At 4,090 meters, it is the highest mountain in Armenia, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding region.
  • Amberd Fortress - another must-visit attraction near Ashnak, Amberd Fortress is a 7th-century fortress that offers stunning views of Mount Aragats and the surrounding valleys.
  • Shaghapavank Monastery - located in a remote and peaceful area, this 12th-century monastery is worth the visit for its beautiful architectural elements and peaceful atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashnak

Ashnak is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Some of the best routes include:

  • Hiking trail to Mount Aragats - this challenging trail starts from Ashnak and leads to the summit of Mount Aragats.
  • Biking trail to Amberd Fortress - this intermediate-level trail takes bikers through picturesque landscapes and ends at the Amberd Fortress.
  • Hiking trail to Shaghapavank Monastery - this easy trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is perfect for families with kids.

Best Things to Do in Ashnak: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding tours - explore the beauty of Ashnak on horseback with guided tours that take you through the scenic valleys and mountains surrounding the village.
  • Village tours - learn more about the history and culture of Ashnak by taking a guided tour of the village, which includes visits to local museums, markets, and historic sites.
  • Stargazing - enjoy the clear night skies of Ashnak by joining a stargazing tour, which includes telescopes and guided tours by astronomers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashnak

Day 1: Arrive in Ashnak and take an afternoon stroll through the village. Day 2: Take a guided village tour and visit local museums and historic sites. Day 3: Hike to Mount Aragats. Day 4: Take a horseback riding tour of the surrounding landscape. Day 5: Visit Amberd Fortress and the nearby Saghmosavank Monastery. Day 6: Visit Hovhannavank Monastery and Shaghapavank Monastery. Day 7: Relax in Ashnak and enjoy the peaceful surroundings before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashnak

Ashnak is known for its traditional Armenian cuisine, which is characterized by grilled meats, fresh herbs, and delicious pastries. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khoravats (grilled meats)
  • Khashlama (slow-cooked meat and vegetables)
  • Ghapama (pumpkin stuffed with rice, dried fruits, and nuts)
  • Lavash (Armenian flatbread)

Local Beverages

  • Matzoon (Armenian yogurt drink)
  • Tan (Armenian tea with lemon and mint)
  • Armenian wine (Ashnak is known for its wineries and vineyards)

Where to Stay in Ashnak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ashnak has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some popular choices include:

  • Ashnak Guest House - a charming guesthouse located in the heart of the village, offering comfortable rooms and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Aragats Hotel - a modern hotel located near the Mount Aragats hiking trails, offering stunning views and excellent amenities.
  • Arman’s B&B - a budget-friendly guesthouse located in a quiet area, featuring comfortable rooms and a friendly host.

What to Buy in Ashnak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade ceramics - Ashnak is known for its traditional pottery, which makes for a great souvenir or gift.
  • Local honey - Ashnak has a thriving beekeeping industry, and the honey produced here is known for its high quality and delicious flavor.
  • Lavash - the traditional Armenian flatbread is a staple food in Ashnak, and is often sold in local markets and bakeries.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashnak

The people of Ashnak are known for their hospitality and friendliness towards visitors. It is customary to greet people with a smile and a handshake, and to show respect for elders and authority figures.

In terms of dress code, visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and to cover their heads and remove their shoes before entering churches and monasteries.

Best Time to Visit Ashnak

The best time to visit Ashnak is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the hiking trails are open. The peak tourist season is during July and August, so visitors who prefer fewer crowds may want to visit in the shoulder seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashnak

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try traditional Armenian cuisine and local products.
  • Learn some basic Armenian phrases and greetings.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Enter churches and monasteries without removing your shoes and covering your head.
  • Touch or climb on historic monuments and buildings.
  • Use disrespectful language or gestures towards locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashnak

Ashnak is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a peaceful atmosphere. Visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Ashnak

Travelers are advised to take necessary health precautions, such as getting vaccinated and carrying a basic first aid kit. It is also important to drink bottled water and avoid uncooked foods to prevent stomach illnesses.

Getting to Ashnak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashnak is located about 30 kilometers from Yerevan, the capital of Armenia, and can be easily accessed by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Ashnak

  • Zvartnots International Airport - located in Yerevan, this is the closest airport to Ashnak.

Bus Stations Near Ashnak

  • Central Bus Station - located in Yerevan, this is the main bus station that offers services to Ashnak.
  • Voskevaz Bus Station - located near the village of Voskevaz, this is another convenient option for travelers coming from Yerevan.

Other Options

  • Taxis - taxis can be easily found in Yerevan, and can take you directly to Ashnak.

How to Get Around in Ashnak

The best way to get around Ashnak is by foot, as the village is small and easy to navigate. Visitors who wish to explore the surrounding area can rent a car or hire a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Ashnak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yerevan - the vibrant capital of Armenia is just 30 kilometers away, and offers a variety of historic sites, museums, and restaurants.
  • Dilijan - located in the Tavush Province, Dilijan is known for its pristine forests, stunning lakes, and beautiful monasteries.
  • Lake Sevan - the largest lake in Armenia is just over an hour’s drive from Ashnak, and offers beautiful beaches, fishing, boating, and camping opportunities.

Conclusion

From its beautiful natural scenery to its warm hospitality and rich cultural heritage, Ashnak has plenty to offer for travelers who are seeking an authentic Armenian experience. With this comprehensive guide, we hope that you are armed with all the information you need to plan your trip and make unforgettable memories in this charming village.
