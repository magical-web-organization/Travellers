Introduction to Artashat

Artashat is a small town located in Central Armenia, in the Caucasus region of Asia. Despite its size, it has a rich history and culture, dating back to the 2nd century BCE when it was founded by King Artashes I. It has served as the capital of Armenia and a significant military stronghold in the past. Today, it is a peaceful and welcoming town with plenty to offer for tourists looking for an authentic Armenian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Artashat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Armenian Genocide Memorial and Museum- This museum provides insight into the events surrounding the Armenian Genocide that occurred in the early 20th century. It is a somber experience, but it is essential for understanding Armenian history.
  • Ararat Valley Country Club- This club is home to a large supply of archaeological artifacts from the pre-Christian and early Christian periods, including household items, jewelry, and church manuscripts.
  • Khor Virap- This monastery is a revered pilgrimage site and where St. Gregory the Illuminator was imprisoned and later released.
  • Zoological Museum- The Zoological Museum contains various stuffed animals that display the fauna living in Armenia.

Natural Attractions of Artashat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Ararat- The site of Noah’s Ark and locals consider Mount Ararat spiritual and notable. It is ideal for photography with a stunning sunrise in the morning.
  • Shireni Canyon- The canyon is an excellent place for hiking, cycling, or horse riding, with its stunning cliffs, valleys, and natural waterfalls.
  • Ararat Valley- An agricultural plain which is where the Biblical Mount Noah landed, the valley is home to a range of archeological discoveries and picturesque farms.
  • Ijevan Wine Factory - Located over a rolling hill, visitors can choose from various wine tasting options with picturesque views of the valley and the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Artashat

  • Khor Virap to Areni- A popular hike that takes 6-7 hours with incredible views of Mount Ararat, vast vineyards, stunning churches, and a chance of exploring the Areni Winery.
  • Astghik Waterfall - An excellent hiking trail, and the waterfall is a breathtaking sight. Water cascades down from the top of the cliffs, making for an amazing viewing experience.

Best Things to Do in Artashat: Activities and Tours

  • Wine tasting- Armenian wine is well known worldwide, and tour packages are available from several wineries in the Ararat Valley region. Visitors can participate in wine tastings and learn about the winemaking process.
  • Kakavaberd Fortress Tour- a guided tour of the Kakavaberd fortress is a perfect option for history enthusiasts. The Fortress dominates the view over the Armenian countryside.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride- an excellent way to see the countryside and historic sites, is to take a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Artashat

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khorovats- Armenian barbecue is a must-try. The meat is cooked on a skewer and served with lavash bread and fresh vegetables.
  • Dolma- a stuffed vegetable dish with minced meat, onions, and herbs, flavored with spices.
  • Lahmajun- a pastry dish topped with minced meat, spices, and fresh herbs, making for an affordable snack.

Local Beverages

  • Armenian Wine- Armenian wine is considered one of the best wines globally, with various wineries producing a range of reds, whites, and roses.
  • Ararat Brandy- The Ararat Brandy is a notable product of Armenia, making it a popular drink with locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Artashat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Silachi Hotel- located in the city center, this hotel is a comfortable and affordable option for tourists.
  • Wine and Light- a guesthouse with ten rooms, this option is famous for being clean and budget-friendly.
  • Lilia Hotel- a luxurious option, located in the town center. It provides guests with top-notch customer service.

What to Buy in Artashat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Armenian pottery- these handmade cups, ceramic plates, and vases, among others, make great gifts for friends and families.
  • Armenian crafts- Armenian crafts vary from embroidered products, silver or gold jewelry, handmade rugs, and several other products that tourists can purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Artashat

Armenia is a hospitable country, where guests are treated with respect, and locals are willing to help visitors navigate the town. Armenian locals are friendly and are cheerfully willing to share information and stories about their culture and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Artashat

The best time to visit Artashat is from May to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are possible. The weather is good for hiking, exploring the countryside, and visiting wineries. The fortresses and museums are open, making it convenient for tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Artashat

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches, which means covering arms, legs, and head.
  • Try the local wines and cuisine.
  • Bring enough cash as ATMs are limited.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect religious monuments or traditions.
  • Don’t litter as Armenia is beautiful and pristine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Artashat:

Armenia is a safe country, and Artashat is a welcoming town. Tourists should take the usual safety precautions when traveling, such as keeping an eye on their valuables and avoiding traveling alone at night.

Health in Artashat

Tourists can bring basic over-the-counter medication to manage any minor injuries or illnesses. For more severe health issues, the town has a small hospital and a private clinic for visitors.

Getting to Artashat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Artashat

The nearest airports are the Shirak International Airport and Zvartnots International Airport, in the capital city, Yerevan.

Bus Stations Near Artashat

The city bus station is the primary source of transportation in the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or car overseas to visit neighboring regions on day trips.

How to Get Around in Artashat

Taxis are readily available and affordable within Artashat city. Tourists also have the option to hire a car and driver, which is an affordable and convenient mode of transport.

Where to Go Next from Artashat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yerevan- The capital city is a vibrant location for cafes, concerts, music, and leisure activities. Travel time: 30 mins.
  • Oshakan- A small town known for its beautiful old churches and Armenian manuscripts. Travel time: 40 mins.
  • Geghard- A monastery carved out of a rockface by medieval monks. Travel time: 1 hr.

Conclusion

Artashat is a perfect location for tourists looking for an authentic Armenian experience. It boasts rich history, cultural landmarks, and impressive outdoor adventures. The town is welcoming, hospitable, and has several accommodation options for visitors to choose from. Tourists will never tire of exploring the town and the surrounding area, experiencing various food and drink options, and buying Armenian souvenirs.
