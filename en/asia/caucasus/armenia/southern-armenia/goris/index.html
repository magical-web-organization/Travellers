Goris, a small town located in Southern Armenia, is popular for its cultural attractions and natural beauty. Set amidst the rocky mountains of the Syunik region, Goris offers breathtaking views, outdoor adventures, and a glimpse of the local culture and customs. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Goris has something to offer for everyone. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the beautiful town of Goris, including the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

Introduction to Goris

Goris is a small town located in Southern Armenia, in the Caucasus region. It is nestled amidst the rocky mountains of the Syunik region and is surrounded by stunning natural scenery. The town is known for its beautiful old buildings, narrow streets, and unique architecture. It is home to many cultural and historical sites and is an excellent destination for travelers interested in exploring the rich history and culture of Armenia.

List of Places to Visit in Goris

Goris has many cultural and natural attractions that are worth visiting. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, here are the best places to visit in Goris:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Old Goris: The old town of Goris is a scenic place with narrow streets, unique architecture, and traditional stone houses. Take a stroll through the winding lanes and explore the charming old houses and courtyards.

  2. Zorats Karer: Also known as the “Armenian Stonehenge,” Zorats Karer is a prehistoric archaeological site that dates back to the Bronze Age. It consists of many megalithic stones arranged in a circular pattern, and visitors can witness the beautiful sunrise over the mountains from this location.

  3. Hin Khot: A medieval fortress located on a rocky hill overlooking Goris. The fortress dates back to the 17th century and has an interesting history.

  4. Goris Museum of Regional Studies: This excellent museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the cultural and natural history of the region. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, including ancient coins, textiles, and ceramics.

Natural Attractions of Goris: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tatev Monastery and the Wings of Tatev: A must-visit attraction in Goris, the Tatev Monastery is a medieval monastery complex that’s an architectural marvel. Take the world’s longest cable car, the Wings of Tatev, to reach the monastery and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  2. Devil’s Bridge: Located just outside Goris, Devil’s Bridge spans the Vorotan River and is a natural arch formed by erosion. It’s a scenic spot that’s perfect for photography and nature walks.

  3. Khndzoresk: A village located about 10 km from Goris that’s famous for its caves and hiking trails. The Old Khndzoresk Cave Village is a must-visit site, where visitors can explore ancient dwellings and tombs carved into the cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goris

Goris is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails that provide visitors with an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Goris:

  1. The Devils Bridge Trail: A moderate hiking/biking trail that takes visitors through scenic mountain landscapes and leads to the Devil’s Bridge.

  2. The Khndzoresk Trail: A hiking trail that takes visitors through the scenic village of Khndzoresk and its caves.

  3. The Old Goris Trail: A moderate hiking trail that takes visitors through the charming Old Goris neighborhood and its traditional stone houses.

Best Things to Do in Goris: Activities and Tours

Goris offers visitors a range of activities and tours that provide a unique experience of the town and its surroundings. Here are some of the best things to do in Goris:

  1. Take a Tour of Tatev Monastery: Join a guided tour of the Tatev Monastery and learn about its rich history and architecture.

  2. Go on a Wine Tasting Tour: Take a tour of the local wineries and taste some of the best Armenian wines.

  3. Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the stunning natural scenery of Goris from a bird’s eye view on a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Goris

A week-long itinerary for Goris can be broken down into the following days:

Day 1: Explore Old Goris and the Goris Museum of Regional Studies.

Day 2: Visit Zorats Karer and Hin Khot and take a tour of the Old Goris neighborhood.

Day 3: Take the Wings of Tatev cable car to reach the Tatev Monastery and explore its surroundings.

Day 4: Go on a wine tasting tour and sample some of the local wines.

Day 5: Explore the Khndzoresk Cave Village and the surrounding hiking trails.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning and spend the rest of the day relaxing at your hotel/guesthouse.

Day 7: Depart from Goris for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Goris

Armenian cuisine is famous for its rich flavors and unique spices, and Goris is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Goris:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khorovats: Armenian-style grilled meats, usually made with pork, beef, or lamb.

  2. Dolma: Stuffed grape leaves with rice and herbs, typically served as an appetizer.

  3. Harissa: A traditional Armenian porridge made with wheat and meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Tan: A traditional Armenian yogurt drink.

  2. Armenian Wine: Armenia has a long history of wine-making, and visiting the local wineries to taste some of the best Armenian wines is a must.

Where to Stay in Goris: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Goris has a range of accommodation options that cater to different traveler needs and budgets. From luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay in Goris:

  1. Mirhav Hotel: This upscale hotel is located in the heart of Goris and offers guests comfortable rooms, a bar, and a restaurant.

  2. Goris Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in the city center with comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  3. Goris Guesthouse: A budget guesthouse that provides clean and comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and an outdoor seating area.

What to Buy in Goris: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Goris offers visitors a range of souvenirs and local products that are unique to the town and region. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. Armenian Handicrafts: Goris is known for its traditional handicrafts, including rugs, pottery, and embroidery, which make excellent souvenirs.

  2. Local Wine: Armenia has a rich history of wine-making, and buying a bottle of Armenian wine is an excellent way to remember your trip to Goris.

Local Culture and Customs of Goris

Goris is a town that’s deeply rooted in Armenian culture and customs. Visitors are expected to respect the local traditions and customs when visiting the town.

Best Time to Visit Goris

The best time to visit Goris is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its best. Summers can be scorching hot, and winters can be chilly, so plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goris

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.

  2. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  3. Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  1. Take photographs of people without their permission.

  2. Enter religious sites without proper attire.

  3. Leave trash behind and respect the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goris

Goris is a safe town for travelers, but visitors are advised to take precautions when traveling in remote areas and to avoid venturing out alone at night.

Health in Goris

Goris has a few medical facilities, including a hospital and a few clinics. It’s essential to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies when traveling to Goris.

Getting to Goris: Transportation Options and Logistics

Goris is accessible by road and air. Here are the different transportation options:

Airports Near Goris

The nearest airport to Goris is the Zvartnots International Airport in Yerevan, which is about a 5-hour drive from Goris.

Bus Stations Near Goris

Goris has a bus station that connects the town to other parts of Armenia. Public transport can be unreliable, so visitors are advised to hire a private car or taxi for transportation.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or a taxi from Yerevan or nearby cities to reach Goris.

How to Get Around in Goris

Goris is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also hire a car or a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Goris: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Goris is a great place to explore the natural and cultural beauty of Armenia. Some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can consider include:

  1. Jermuk: A spa town located about 60 km from Goris, known for its mineral-rich hot springs.

  2. Noravank: A medieval monastery complex located about 40 km from Goris, renowned for its unique architecture and natural surroundings.

Conclusion

Goris is a hidden gem in Armenia, with breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural attractions, and unique local experiences.
