Introduction to Tatev

Tatev is a must-visit destination in Southern Armenia. Tatev, which means “give wings” in Armenian, is known for its stunning views of the surrounding mountains, deep canyons, and the iconic Tatev Monastery, which is the heart and soul of the region. The monastery dates back to the 9th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Tatev complex also includes several other churches, chapels, and centuries-old buildings that have been restored with great care.

This travel guide aims to be your ultimate source of information for Tatev, providing you with everything you need to know about what to do, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get there. So, let’s get started!

Local Culture and Customs of Tatev

  • Respect for the elderly is a big emphasis in Armenian culture.
  • Visitors should dress conservatively when visiting places of worship.
  • It is customary to remove shoes before entering homes and some other establishments.

Best Time to Visit Tatev

The best time to visit Tatev is during the spring or autumn. Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer moderate temperatures and beautiful colors of the changing seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tatev

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry cash, as some places do not accept credit cards
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without permission
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect cultural and religious monuments

Safety and Health

Safety in Tatev

Tatev is a generally safe destination; however, visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings. Petty crime is not uncommon, and it is advisable to take necessary safety measures.

Health in Tatev

Visitors must remain hydrated throughout their stay in Tatev. They are advised to drink only bottled water and to bring medications for common ailments such as headaches, fever, and stomach flu.
