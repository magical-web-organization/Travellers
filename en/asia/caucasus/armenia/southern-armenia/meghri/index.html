Meghri Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Meghri, Southern Armenia

Located in Southern Armenia, Meghri is a beautiful city surrounded by towering mountains and breathtaking scenery. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Meghri. From the cultural and natural attractions to the best activities, where to stay and what to eat, we’ve got you covered.

Introduction to Meghri

Meghri is a small city in the southern part of Armenia, close to the Iranian border. It is situated in the Meghri Mountains, which are part of the Caucasus mountain range. The city has a rich history, dating back to the 5th century B.C. It has gone through many changes over the years, including being ruled by different empires, but has always been an important cultural and commercial center.

Meghri has a semi-arid climate, with hot summers and cold winters. The best time to visit is in spring or autumn when the temperatures are mild.

List of Places to Visit in Meghri

Meghri has many cultural and natural attractions that you must see during your visit. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Meghri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Meghri Museum of Regional Studies: The museum is located in the center of the city and showcases the region’s history, culture and folklore. It houses artifacts such as old coins, ancient tools, and traditional clothing.

  • Surb Grigor Lusavorich Church: This ancient church is located on a hill near the city center and has a beautiful view of the surrounding mountains. It was built in the 17th century and is an important religious and cultural monument of the region.

Natural Attractions of Meghri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Meghri Canyon: This breathtaking Canyon is located near the Iranian Border and is considered to be one of the deepest canyons in the world. It’s a great spot for hiking, with trails leading through the canyon’s stunning rock formations.

  • Hot Springs of Arjatkhlan: Located in a nearby village, Arjatkhlan hot springs are known for their healing properties. The mineral water of these hot springs is believed to be beneficial for various health issues.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meghri

Meghri has many hiking and biking trails that allow you to explore the area’s stunning scenery. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Meghri – Kapan: This route takes you from Meghri to Kapan, passing through beautiful landscapes and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Meghri – Iran Border: A hiking trail that takes you to the Iranian border, offering views of Meghri Canyon along the way.

Best Things to Do in Meghri: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Meghri. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Wine Tasting: Meghri is known for its wine production. Take a tour of the local vineyards and wineries and taste some of the region’s finest wines.

  • Visit a Local Market: The city’s market offers an authentic experience of Armenian culture. This bustling market is full of local produce, handicrafts, and traditional clothing.

Food and Drink Scene of Meghri

Food in Meghri is simple and delicious, with a range of traditional dishes that you must try during your stay.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khashlama: A hearty, meaty stew made with lamb or beef, potatoes, and vegetables.

  • Armenian-style barbecue: This popular dish typically consists of grilled meat or fish, served with plenty of side dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Meghri is known for its wine production, with full-bodied red wines being most popular.

  • Armenian Coffee: This strong coffee is brewed in a traditional brass pot called a “jazzva”.

Where to Stay in Meghri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Meghri has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Meghri:

  • Sisian Hotel and Restaurant: This hotel is located near the Meghri Museum of Regional Studies and offers comfortable, spacious rooms.

  • Arevik Guest House: A lovely guesthouse with a beautiful garden, stunning views and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Meghri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Meghri offers a range of souvenirs and local products that you can take home with you. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Armenian Carpets: These intricately woven carpets are a valuable item that can be both decorative and functional.

  • Wine: Meghri is known for its wine production, making it an excellent souvenir to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Meghri

Meghri has a rich culture and customs that you should be aware of during your visit. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Conservatively: The region is mainly conservative, so dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions: Be mindful of the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas.

Best Time to Visit Meghri

The best time to visit Meghri is in spring (April-June) or autumn (September-November) when the temperatures are mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meghri

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Meghri:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs - Be respectful and mindful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try the Local Wine - Meghri is known for its wine production, so be sure to try some of the local wines.

Don’t:

  • Be Disrespectful of Religious Sites - Be respectful of the local religious sites, especially when visiting churches or monasteries.

  • Assume Everyone Speaks English - While English is widely spoken in the city, it’s always good to learn some basic phrases in the local language.

Safety and Health

Safety in Meghri

Meghri is a safe city, with low crime rates. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions when traveling, such as keeping your valuables safe and avoiding travel at night.

Health in Meghri

Medical services in Meghri are limited, and it’s recommended to have travel health insurance before your trip. The tap water in Meghri is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water or boiled water.

Getting to Meghri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Meghri

The nearest airport to Meghri is the Zvartnots International Airport in Yerevan, which is approx. 365km away. There are no direct flights to Meghri.

Bus Stations Near Meghri

Meghri has a bus station from where local buses run to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

It’s also possible to rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Meghri. This gives you more flexibility to explore the region’s stunning scenery.

How to Get Around in Meghri

Meghri is a walkable city, and you can easily explore the city center on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding mountains, you’ll need to hire a car, bike or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Meghri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Meghri:

  • Tatev Monastery: Located 65 km from Meghri, the Tatev Monastery is a popular excursion with stunning views and incredible history.

  • Armenian-Iranian Border: Take a day trip to the Armenian-Iranian border and explore the Iranian side of the border.

Conclusion

Meghri is a stunning destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression. It offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you will have all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Meghri.
