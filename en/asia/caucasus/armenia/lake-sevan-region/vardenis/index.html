Introduction to Vardenis

Vardenis is a town located in the Lake Sevan Region of Armenia, which is considered to be the largest region in the country. The town is surrounded by beautiful mountains and stunning natural landscapes. Vardenis is an ideal destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Visitors can explore the town’s cultural heritage sites, indulge in the local cuisine, and participate in outdoor activities while being immersed in the town’s striking natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Vardenis

Visitors to Vardenis have a range of places to explore, from cultural attractions to natural wonders. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Vardenis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vardenis Museum of History and Culture
  • Iron Age Cemetery of Vardenis
  • Fortress of Haykadzor
  • Meghri Fortress
  • Jermuk Waterfall

Natural Attractions of Vardenis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Armaghan
  • Artanish Peninsula
  • Lake Sevan
  • Shorzha Waterfall
  • Shamb Waterfall

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vardenis

Vardenis is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with several beautiful routes to explore. The Armaghan Mountain Trail, Artanish Peninsula Trail, and Shorzha Waterfall Trail are some of the most popular routes in Vardenis.

Best Things to Do in Vardenis: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the town’s cultural and natural heritage, there are many things visitors can do in Vardenis. Here are some popular activities and tours in Vardenis:

  • Wine Tasting Tours
  • Kayaking on Lake Sevan
  • Cultural Walking Tours
  • Photography Tours
  • Hot Springs and Wellness Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Vardenis

Armenian cuisine is known for its rich flavors and unique combinations. In Vardenis, visitors can find traditional local dishes, such as khorovats (barbecue), stuffed grape leaves, and spicy lamb soup. Besides, the town is renowned for its wine, particularly the Voskevaz Winery.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khorovats (Barbecue)
  • Armenian Barley Soup
  • Dolma (Stuffed Grape Leaves)
  • Ghapama (Pumpkin dish)

Local Beverages

  • Armenian Coffee
  • Voskevaz Winery Wine
  • Tarkhana (Fermented yogurt drink)

Where to Stay in Vardenis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vardenis has several accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some options:

  • Gardenia Hotel: Located in the heart of Vardenis, this hotel offers excellent amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Jermuk Seal: This resort is located 20 km from Vardenis and offers stunning views of Mount Ararat.
  • Arthurs Aghveran Resort: Located 40 km from Vardenis, this resort offers luxurious accommodation amidst vast landscapes.

What to Buy in Vardenis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Armenian Rug

Local Culture and Customs of Vardenis

Armenians are proud of their rich cultural heritage, and Vardenis is no different. Visitors can explore the town’s cultural attractions, indulge in traditional cuisine and participate in events and festivals to experience local culture. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Vardenis

The best time to visit Vardenis is from May to October when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty of the town to its fullest during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vardenis

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Carry local currency (Armenian Dram).

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Take pictures of military installations.
  • Wear shoes inside religious buildings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vardenis

Vardenis is a relatively safe town, but visitors are advised to be careful while exploring remote areas. It’s recommended not to leave valuables unattended in public places and to keep an eye on belongings while travelling.

Health in Vardenis

Medical facilities are limited in Vardenis, and visitors are advised to carry a medical kit with essential medications. Drinking water from the tap is not recommended, and visitors are advised to carry a water bottle and refill it from reliable sources.

Getting to Vardenis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vardenis

The nearest airport is Zvartnots International Airport in Yerevan, located 147 km from Vardenis. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Vardenis from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Vardenis

There are several bus stations in Yerevan that offer bus services to Vardenis. Visitors can take a bus from the Kilikia Bus Station or the Sayat-Nova Bus Station to reach Vardenis.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or taxi from Yerevan to reach Vardenis. The journey takes approximately 2 - 3 hours.

How to Get Around in Vardenis

Vardenis is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town by walking or cycling. Taxis are readily available at reasonable rates to explore remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Vardenis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Vardenis can explore other popular destinations in Armenia, such as

  • Jermuk: A popular spa town famous for its hot springs.
  • Tsaghkadzor: An ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts offering hiking, skiing and other outdoor activities.
  • Dilijan: A tranquil retreat, surrounded by lush forests and serene landscapes.

Conclusion

Vardenis offers visitors a unique opportunity to immerse themselves in the rich culture and stunning natural beauty of Armenia. With a wide range of attractions and activities to explore, visitors can enjoy a perfect blend of adventure and relaxation. Plan your trip to Vardenis and discover the hidden gem of Armenia.
