Introduction to Tumanyan

Nestled in the northern region of Armenia, Tumanyan is a scenic and historically rich town surrounded by majestic forests and mountains. It is named after the famous Armenian writer and poet Hovhannes Tumanyan and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Whether you’re interested in admiring breathtaking natural landscapes, exploring cultural and historical landmarks, or indulging in local cuisine, Tumanyan has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tumanyan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tumanyan Museum: The museum honors the life and accomplishments of Hovhannes Tumanyan, showcasing his literary works, manuscripts, and personal belongings.
  • Paleo-Christian Cemetery: The cemetery boasts an array of historical tombstones from the 5th to 17th centuries, reflecting the region’s unique cultural heritage.
  • Makaravank Monastery: A sublime 10th-century Armenian Apostolic Church situated amidst the verdant hills of Tavush Province.

Natural Attractions of Tumanyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dsegh Nature Trail: Get lost amidst the breathtaking Dsegh forest and enjoy the serene surroundings while strolling along the picturesque trails.
  • Lake Parz: Surrounded by dense forests, Lake Parz is one of the most beautiful natural wonders of the region, offering visitors stunning scenic views, fishing opportunities, and boat rides.
  • Hiking to the Dsegh forest: Lovers of hiking are encouraged to explore the Dsegh forest for amazing views and breathtaking scenery, ranging from waterfalls to serene nature trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tumanyan

  • Tavush Hiking/Biking Trail: This trail offers a picturesque view of the Tavush region and features different hiking and biking paths, boasting some of the most idyllic spots in Tumanyan.
  • Dsegh-Kachachkut Trail: Another excellent trail, which runs across the dense forests of Dsegh all the way to Kachachkut village, giving hikers a spectacular view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Tumanyan: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding: Tumanyan offers horse riding activities that allow visitors to explore the region while also experiencing the thrill of riding a horse in the mountains.
  • Arts and Crafts Workshop: Visitors can enjoy a fun and educational workshop to learn about the traditional arts and crafts of the Tavush region.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Guests can partake in a wine tour, sampling unique wines and spirits while learning about the wine culture and history of Tumanyan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tumanyan

Day 1: Visit the Tumanyan Museum and the Paleo-Christian Cemetery.

Day 2: Hike along the Dsegh forest and visit the Makaravank Monastery.

Day 3: Explore the picturesque Lake Parz.

Day 4: Join an arts and crafts workshop and learn about traditional Armenian handicrafts.

Day 5: Take a horse riding tour in the Tavush region.

Day 6: Go on a wine tour and experience the wine culture of Tumanyan.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beauty of Tumanyan at your own pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Tumanyan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khorovats: An Armenian version of the grilled meat dish, traditionally served with lavash and fresh herbs.
  • Ghapama: A popular Tavush dish consisting of a baked pumpkin stuffed with rice, dried fruits, nuts, and honey.
  • Lavash: A thin, crispy flatbread popularly served with different dips and spreads.

Local Beverages

  • Lemonade: A refreshing traditional Armenian beverage made from fresh lemons and sugar.
  • Beer: Tumanyan produces some of the best quality locally brewed beers in Armenia.
  • Vodka: For those who love strong spirits, Tumanyan offers a taste of traditional Armenian vodka, famous for its unique flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Tumanyan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Green Stone Hostel: This cozy and comfortable hostel is located in the heart of Tumanyan town, offering affordable accommodation and stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Hotel Siner: A luxurious and spacious hotel located near the Dsegh forest, featuring an outdoor pool, a sauna, and a restaurant serving traditional Armenian dishes.
  • Guesthouse Tsirani: A pleasant guesthouse surrounded by apple orchards, ideal for those who love nature and tranquility.

What to Buy in Tumanyan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase locally made souvenirs such as ceramic pottery, hand-knitted sweaters, and other Armenian themed items.
  • Honey: Tumanyan produces high-quality honey made with natural ingredients from the surrounding forests and meadows.
  • Wine: The region is known for the production of high-quality Armenian wine, which makes for an excellent gift to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tumanyan

Armenians are known for their hospitality and warmth towards guests. It is customary to show respect to elders, especially when entering a home. Visitors should be prepared to remove their shoes upon entering households, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Armenians take pride in their cuisine, and it is customary to sample traditional dishes when offered.

Best Time to Visit Tumanyan

The best time to visit Tumanyan is during the summer season from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities. However, Tumanyan is equally beautiful during the winter months, offering an entirely different experience with snow-covered landscapes, skiing opportunities, and snowmobiling adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tumanyan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when entering religious institutions.
  • Embrace the hospitality and kindness of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the culture or traditions of the locals.
  • Litter or harm natural surroundings when undertaking outdoor activities.
  • Visit historical sites without proper permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tumanyan

Tumanyan is a safe place for travelers, with little to no reports of violent crime. However, it is always advisable to be cautious while traveling and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Tumanyan

Tumanyan has access to good quality healthcare facilities, and visitors are advised to have proper health insurance. Travelers should also be careful while undertaking outdoor activities to prevent injuries or accidents.

Getting to Tumanyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tumanyan

  • Shirak International Airport: Located in Gyumri, 60 miles from Tumanyan.
  • Zvartnots International Airport: Located in Yerevan, 80 miles from Tumanyan.

Bus Stations Near Tumanyan

  • Vanadzor Bus Station: Located in Vanadzor, 20 miles from Tumanyan.
  • Yerevan Bus Station: Located in Yerevan, 80 miles from Tumanyan.

Other Options

Taxis are readily available and can be hailed on the streets. Car rentals are also available with several reputable rental companies operating in the region.

How to Get Around in Tumanyan

The most popular form of transportation in Tumanyan is by car. Taxis and rental cars can be used for daily travel. However, visitors are also encouraged to hike and bike through the beautiful countryside.

Where to Go Next from Tumanyan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Sevan: One of Armenia’s most significant landmarks, featuring crystal clear waters and several cultural monuments.
  • Haghpat and Sanahin Monasteries: Stunning 10th-century monasteries, included in the UNESCO World Heritage list.
  • Lori Fortress: A medieval Armenian fortress located on the banks of the River Dzoraget.

Conclusion

With its captivating natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and fantastic hospitality, Tumanyan is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Armenian experience. Whether one is an adventure-seeker or a history buff, Tumanyan has something enjoyable for everyone.
