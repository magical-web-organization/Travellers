Introduction to Spitak

Spitak is a picturesque town located in the Lori Province of Armenia. The town is situated on the banks of the Kasagh River and surrounded by mountains, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. The town was completely destroyed by an earthquake in 1988, but it has since been rebuilt and is now a peaceful and charming destination that offers a glimpse into Armenia’s rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Spitak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Spitak Museum of Local Lore: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of the town. It houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage.
  2. Spitak Church: This beautiful 19th-century church is located in the center of the town and is a testament to the town’s enduring faith.
  3. Haghpat Monastery: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located about 30 km away from Spitak and is a must-visit for anyone interested in Armenian architecture and history.

Natural Attractions of Spitak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Aragats: This majestic mountain is the highest peak in Armenia and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. There are several hiking trails that lead to the summit, offering a unique opportunity to experience the beauty of the Armenian highlands.
  2. Kasakh River: This pristine river is a great place to go fishing, kayaking, or just relaxing by the water.
  3. Makaravank Monastery: This remote monastery is located on a hilltop and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It is an ideal destination for hikers and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spitak

Spitak is known for its beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer a unique opportunity to experience the area’s natural beauty up close. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  1. Mount Aragats Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of Mount Aragats and offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Kasakh River Trail: This easy hiking trail follows the Kasakh River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Makaravank Trail: This moderate hiking trail takes you to the remote Makaravank Monastery and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Spitak: Activities and Tours

  3. Take a Hiking or Biking Tour: There are many hiking and biking tours available that offer a unique opportunity to experience the area’s natural beauty up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Spitak

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khorovats: This is a traditional Armenian barbecue dish that features marinated meat cooked over an open flame.
  2. Manti: These are small dumplings filled with meat or vegetables and served with yoghurt.
  3. Lavash: This is a traditional Armenian flatbread that is often served with meals.

Local Beverages

  1. Armenian Brandy: Armenian brandy is known for its quality and is a must-try for anyone visiting Spitak.
  2. Wine: Armenia has a long history of winemaking, and the local wines are known for their unique character and flavor.

Where to Stay in Spitak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Spitak, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the most popular options include:

  1. Lori Hotel: This hotel is located in the center of the town and offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices.
  2. Shushan Guest House: This guest house is located in a quiet residential area and offers a cozy, home-like atmosphere.
  3. Haghpat Monastery Hotel: This hotel is located near the Haghpat Monastery and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Spitak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Armenian Carpets: Armenian carpets are known for their unique designs and quality and make a great souvenir to bring home.
  2. Apricot Products: Armenia is known for its apricots, and you can find a variety of apricot products, such as dried apricots and apricot jam, in Spitak.
  3. Armenian Crafts: There are many local artisans in Spitak who produce handmade crafts, such as pottery and wood carvings, that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Spitak

Armenia has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its food, music, language, and architecture. Armenian hospitality is also well-known, and it is customary to offer guests food and drink when they visit your home. It is also important to be respectful of Armenian traditions and customs when visiting Spitak, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Spitak

The best time to visit Spitak is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also beautiful during the winter months, when the mountains are capped with snow and the town takes on a magical, fairy-tale-like quality.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spitak

Do:

  2. Try the local food and drink.
  3. Explore the hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  3. Make unnecessary noise in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spitak

Spitak is generally a safe destination. However, it is always a good idea to take basic precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Spitak

There are no major health concerns in Spitak, and the town has several medical facilities that can handle minor health issues. It is always a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, just in case.
