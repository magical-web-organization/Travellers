Introduction to Noyemberyan

Noyemberyan is a small town located in the northernmost part of Armenia, surrounded by rolling hills and greenery. Once an important trade hub along the Silk Road, Noyemberyan has now become a hidden gem for nature and history enthusiasts. This quaint town is known for its scenic views, ancient monasteries, and hiking trails that lead to picturesque landscapes.

In this Noyemberyan travel guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, cultural and natural attractions, food and drink, accommodations, transportation, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Noyemberyan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aknaberd Fortress: One of the oldest fortresses in Armenia built during the 5th-6th century, showcasing the military architecture of the ancient Armenian people.
  • Aknaberd Basilica: A 5th-century basilica that once served as a royal and religious center for the Armenian Kingdom of Lori.
  • Horomayr Monastery: A historic monastery located in a stunning natural setting, offering panoramic views of the mountains and valleys that surround it.
  • Noyemberyan Historical Museum: An enriching experience of the town’s history, featuring exhibits and artifacts related to the local culture, economy, and daily life.
  • Yenokavan Canyon: An awe-inspiring natural wonder situated in the middle of a canyon with stunning rock formations and waterfalls.

Natural Attractions of Noyemberyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tavush Canyon: A beautiful canyon, perfect for hiking, rock climbing, or just a relaxing stroll, boasting beautiful scenery and a large variety of local flora and fauna.
  • Shivani Sanctuary: A natural reserve that features beautiful landscapes and nature, with a diverse range of wildlife to observe, such as wolves, bears, eagles, lynx, and more.
  • Lastiver Cave: A beautiful cave complex located deep in the forest, with stunning landscapes and beautiful waterfalls in the area.
  • Yenokavan Canyon Zipline: For adrenaline junkies, enjoy an unforgettable zip line over the Yenokavan Canyon, marveling at the breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noyemberyan

Noyemberyan has numerous hiking and biking trails that would suit people with a wide range of experience, from beginners to avid mountain hikers. Check out these trails when you visit:

  • Tavush Canyon Hiking Trail: A challenging yet beautiful trail winding through the Tavush Canyon, crossing the river multiple times and offering breathtaking views.
  • Lake Gosh Hiking Trail: A beautiful hike that includes mountainous terrains, stunning waterfalls, and a beautiful lake at the end.
  • Jukhtak Monastery Hiking Trail: A hike leading to the historic Jukhtak Monastery, situated on a dramatic cliff overlooking the valley below.

Best Things to Do in Noyemberyan: Activities and Tours

  • Lake Gosh: Take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of Lake Gosh, enjoy a family picnic on the shore, or take a boat ride.
  • Yenokavan Canyon: For adrenaline seekers, enjoy an unforgettable zip line over the Yenokavan Canyon, and marvel at the breathtaking views along the way.
  • Horomayr Monastery: Visit the historic Horomayr Monastery to admire the beautiful architecture and rich history, adding to your overall scenic experience.
  • Aknaberd Fortress: Explore the ancient architectural ruins of the Aknaberd Fortress, and pay homage to the Armenian Kingdom’s military past,

Week-Long Itinerary for Noyemberyan

  • Day 1: Arrive in Noyemberyan and explore the surrounding area
  • Day 2: Visit Lake Gosh and its surroundings, including waterfalls and small caves, have a picnic or go boating.
  • Day 3: Hike Tavush Canyon and explore the canyon’s unique rock formations and waterfalls.
  • Day 4: Visit the Horomayr Monastery for breathtaking panoramic views of the valley.
  • Day 5: Discover the ancient ruins of the Aknaberd Fortress and the Aknaberd Basilica.
  • Day 6: Redefine your adrenaline rush as you go zip lining over Yenokavan Canyon.
  • Day 7: Explore the Noyemberyan Historical Museum, learn about local Armenian customs and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Noyemberyan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Harissa: A traditional Armenian dish combining roasted meat, wheat, and butter, flavored with the Longan fruit
  • Khinkali: A Georgian dish, steamed dumplings with a meat or vegetable stuffing
  • Tolma: A dish filled with meat, rice, and vegetable stuffing then wrapped around leaves from grape vines.

Local Beverages

  • Duduk: A fruity drink prepared from dried apricots or peaches, which can be served both hot or cold.
  • Armenian cognac: A smooth and flavorful drink made from Armenian grapes and aged in oak barrels.

Where to Stay in Noyemberyan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shikahogh Guest House: A lovely guest house with comfortable rooms, rich culinary experience, and located near Tavush Canyon.

  • Hotel Beirut: A hotel in the heart of the town that features a bar and restaurant, including several elegant rooms and caters to tourists from different countries.

What to Buy in Noyemberyan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade rugs and scarves: Armenian traditional rugs and scarves are known for their unique design and are available in different sizes and colors.
  • Armenian brandy and wine: Well known Armenian drinks that make wonderful souvenirs for family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Noyemberyan

Armenians are friendly and hospitable people. In Noyemberyan, it is customary to greet with a handshake, and if you are a guest at someone’s house, be sure to bring a small gift, such as dried fruits, chocolates, or flowers. Additionally, as a sign of respect, it is advised that you avoid pointing your fingers while speaking with others.

Best Time to Visit Noyemberyan

The best time to visit Noyemberyan is from late spring to early autumn, running from May to October. However, the town is also beautiful in winter, and you can enjoy winter sports such as skiing, snowshoeing, and sledding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noyemberyan

Do:

  • Carry bottled water while hiking.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Litter in the natural parks or hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noyemberyan

Noyemberyan is a safe place for visitors. However, it is still necessary to take care of yourself and your belongings. Keep important documents and cash in a hotel safe or a secured bag. Make sure to stay on the established hiking paths and do not venture into unfamiliar areas without a guide.

Health in Noyemberyan

When traveling to Noyemberyan, ensure that you have travel insurance and that your routine immunizations are up-to-date. Additionally, drink bottled water, and avoid tap water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Noyemberyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Noyemberyan

The nearest airport to Noyemberyan is the Shirak International Airport, located about 107 Km away. Another airport is Yerevan International Airport, located about 182 Km away.

Bus Stations Near Noyemberyan

There are several bus stations in Yerevan, where you can take a bus to Dilijan or Vanadzor. From there, you can take a taxi or another bus going to Noyemberyan.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or rent a car in Yerevan to get to Noyemberyan yourself.

How to Get Around in Noyemberyan

The best way to get around in Noyemberyan is by car, bike or walking. Taxis are also available for your convenience.

Where to Go Next from Noyemberyan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Areni Wine Region: A wine-growing region located in the heart of the Vayots Dzor province, known for its rich history and excellent wines.
  • Dilijan National Park: A national park with a rich and diverse flora and fauna, including several species of plants and animals.

Conclusion

Noyemberyan is a picturesque town that is worth visiting for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural landscapes. We hope this Noyemberyan travel guide helps you plan your trip and get the best out of your experiences in Northern Armenia.
