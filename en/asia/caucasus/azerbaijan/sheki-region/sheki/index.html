Introduction to Sheki

Nestled in the Caucasus region of Azerbaijan, Sheki offers a unique blend of history, nature, culture, and adventure. With its stunning old town, elaborate palaces, scenic landscapes, and warm hospitality, Sheki attracts travelers from all around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Sheki

Here are some of the must-visit places to include in your Sheki itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sheki Khan’s Palace: Built in the 18th century, this magnificent palace showcases the opulence and grandeur of the Sheki Khanate. Marvel at its intricate frescoes, ornate carvings, and impressive architecture.

  • Sheki History Museum: Learn about the history, culture, and traditions of Sheki through artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays.

  • Caravanserai: A historic Silk Road inn that has been converted into a museum showcasing traditional Azerbaijani crafts such as pottery, carpet weaving and metalwork.

Natural Attractions of Sheki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kish Albanian Church: Marvel at the ancient Albanian church that dates back to the 1st century AD.

  • Sheki Observatory: Enjoy views of the stunning mountainous landscape and gaze at the stars at this observatory.

  • Gabaland Amusement Park: Take your family for a day of fun with thrilling roller coasters and other rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sheki

For outdoor enthusiasts, Sheki offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking through its scenic countryside. Some popular routes include:

  • Sheki-Bash Kyungyut Trail: A moderate 4.5 km trail that takes you through lush forests, past rushing streams and to the top of a mountain peak for a panoramic view.

  • Sheki-Haji Badal Trail: A challenging 11 km trail that takes you through the mountains to a remote village and back.

  • Biking Trail: A 22 km bike trail that follows the Silk Road and passes through ancient villages, dense forests, and stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Sheki: Activities and Tours

Sheki offers a wide range of activities and tours for travelers of every interest. Some top-rated options include:

  • Cooking Classes: Learn to make traditional Azerbaijani dishes like plov, dolma, and qutab from a local chef.

  • Cultural Tours: Visit local artisans and craftsmen, shop at traditional markets, and learn about Azerbaijan’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Soar above the stunning landscape and view Sheki from a unique perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sheki

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Sheki:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Sheki, explore the old town, and visit the Sheki Khan’s Palace.

  • Day 2: Visit the Sheki History Museum and the Caravanserai, and take a cooking class to learn to make traditional Azerbaijani dishes.

  • Day 3: Head out for a day of outdoor adventure, either hiking or biking through the countryside.

  • Day 4: Take a cultural tour, visiting local artisans and craftsmen, and shopping at traditional markets.

  • Day 5: Relax and enjoy a hot air balloon ride over Sheki.

  • Day 6: Visit the Kish Albanian Church and the Sheki Observatory.

  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Zaqatala or Gabala.

Food and Drink Scene of Sheki

Sheki is renowned for its delicious cuisine and vibrant food culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Plov: A rice and meat dish that is a staple of Azerbaijani cuisine.

  • Dolma: Stuffed grape leaves filled with rice, herbs, and meat.

  • Qutab: A savory stuffed pancake typically filled with meat, cheese, or herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A refreshing and slightly sour yogurt-based drink.

  • Black tea: A popular Azerbaijani beverage, often served with sugar or jam.

  • Sherbet: A sweet fruit-based syrup or drink made from fruit, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Sheki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sheki offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some popular options include:

  • Sheki Palace Hotel: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of the old town and offers elegant rooms, a spa, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

  • Sheki Saray Hotel: This 4-star hotel is set in a restored caravanserai and offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and a sauna.

  • Guesthouses: For a more authentic and budget-friendly experience, consider staying in a guesthouse run by a local family. Many offer comfortable rooms, home-cooked meals, and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Sheki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sheki is famous for its local crafts and unique products. Some popular souvenirs include:

  • Sheki Carpets: These finely woven carpets are made using traditional techniques and are sought after by collectors.

  • Sweet Baklava: A delicious pastry made from layers of phyllo dough and honey.

  • Chestnut Honey: A sweet and fragrant honey that is produced in the surrounding countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Sheki

Respect local customs and traditions when visiting Sheki. Some important tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly in public places, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a mosque.

  • Ask for permission before taking someone’s picture.

  • Don’t touch or disturb any religious objects or monuments.

Best Time to Visit Sheki

The best time to visit Sheki is from April to June or from September to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. Avoid visiting during the summer months when temperatures can soar.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sheki

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Sheki:

Do:

  • Bring appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.

  • Try traditional Azerbaijani dishes and local beverages.

  • Learn some basic Azerbaijani phrases.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

  • Touch any religious artifacts or monuments.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sheki

Sheki is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, be cautious of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Sheki

Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid tap water to prevent gastrointestinal illnesses. Also, it is important to ensure you have adequate medical coverage and vaccinations, especially for Hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Sheki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sheki

The nearest airports to Sheki are the Gabala International Airport and the Ganja International Airport, both of which are approximately 1.5 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Sheki

Sheki has a central bus station that provides regular services to Baku, Ganja, Zagatala and other major cities. The buses are comfortable and air-conditioned and the journey is affordable.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Sheki from Baku or other regional cities.

How to Get Around in Sheki

You can easily navigate around Sheki on foot or by taking a taxi. Taxis are affordable and convenient, and many drivers speak English.

Where to Go Next from Sheki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting and picturesque destinations to explore near Sheki, such as:

  • Gabala: A popular ski resort town that offers adventure sports, shopping, and entertainment.

  • Zaqatala: A charming mountain town known for its natural beauty, hiking trails, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Sheki is a hidden gem of Azerbaijan, offering a wealth of attractions, activities, and experiences. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide and explore everything this beautiful region has to offer.
