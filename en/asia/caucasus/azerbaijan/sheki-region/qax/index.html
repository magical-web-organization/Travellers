Introduction to Qax

Located in the Sheki Region of Azerbaijan, Qax is a stunning destination that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From exploring ancient ruins to hiking through picturesque mountains, there’s something for everyone in Qax. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Qax, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!

List of Places to Visit in Qax

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Qax has a rich history dating back to ancient times, and there are many cultural and historical sites to explore. Here are some top places to visit:

Qax Fortress

Built in the 18th century, the Qax Fortress is one of the most impressive historical sites in the region. It was once a stronghold of the Shirvanshahs, a medieval dynasty that ruled Azerbaijan.

Archeological Ruins of Galeykhudat

The Archeological Ruins of Galeykhudat are a group of ancient ruins that date back to the 5th century BC. Visitors can see the remnants of an ancient city, including walls, tombs, and other structures.

House of Natavan

The House of Natavan is a historic mansion that once belonged to a famous Azerbaijani poetess named Natavan. Today, the house is a museum showcasing the life and works of the poetess.

Natural Attractions of Qax: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Qax offers a wide range of natural attractions to explore. Here are some top places to visit:

Tufandag Mountain

Tufandag Mountain is the highest peak in the region, reaching an altitude of 4,242 meters. Visitors can hike to the summit, or take a cable car for stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Sakina-Khanum Mosque

Located in the village of Salakhli, the Sakina-Khanum Mosque is a beautiful example of traditional Azerbaijani architecture. The mosque is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery, including forests and mountains.

Qax Waterfall

The Qax Waterfall is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking. The waterfall is located in a picturesque valley surrounded by dense forests, and visitors can hike to the top for a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qax

Qax is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many scenic trails to explore. Here are some top hiking and biking routes:

Goshgarchay Trail

This 17-kilometer trail takes visitors through some of the most picturesque areas of Qax, including dense forests, rolling hills, and beautiful valleys.

Tufandag Trail

For those who love a challenge, the Tufandag Trail is a 24-kilometer route that takes visitors to the top of Tufandag Mountain. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, but is only recommended for experienced hikers.

Goygol Lake Trail

The Goygol Lake Trail is a popular hiking and biking route that takes visitors to one of the most beautiful lakes in the region. The trail is relatively easy, and visitors can enjoy stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Qax: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring cultural and historical sites, there are many other fun activities to enjoy in Qax. Here are some top things to do:

Horseback Riding

Explore the beautiful countryside on horseback with a guided tour. There are many operators in the area that offer horseback riding tours for visitors of all levels.

Sailing on Goygol Lake

Take a scenic boat tour of the beautiful Goygol Lake. Visitors can rent boats and sail around the lake, taking in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Traditional Azerbaijani Cooking Class

Learn how to cook traditional Azerbaijani dishes with a cooking class. Many restaurants and hotels offer cooking classes for visitors, where they can learn to make dishes like plov and dolma.

Food and Drink Scene of Qax

Must-Try Dishes

Azerbaijani cuisine is a delight for foodies, and Qax is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes:

Plov

Plov is a traditional Azerbaijani dish made with saffron-flavored rice, vegetables, and meat. It’s often served as the main course at special occasions and celebrations.

Dolma

Dolma is a popular Azerbaijani appetizer made with grape leaves stuffed with a mixture of rice, herbs, and meat. It’s often served with yogurt and bread.

Kebabs

Kebabs are a staple of Azerbaijani cuisine, and there are many different varieties to try. Some popular types include lamb kebabs, chicken kebabs, and shashlik.

Local Beverages

In addition to the delicious food, Qax also has a vibrant beverage scene. Here are some top local beverages to try:

Azerbaijani Tea

Azerbaijani tea is served black with a cube of sugar or jam. It’s more than just a drink – it’s a staple of Azerbaijani culture and often served to guests as a sign of hospitality.

Ayran

Ayran is a traditional Azerbaijani yogurt drink that’s a refreshing alternative to soda. It’s often served with meat dishes and is a great way to cool down on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Qax: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qax has a variety of accommodation options to suit all travelers. Here are some top choices:

Qax Hotel

Qax Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city. It offers spacious rooms, a spa, and a restaurant serving traditional Azerbaijani cuisine.

Qax Otel

Qax Otel is a budget-friendly option located near the city center. It offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

Guesthouse Sakina-Khanum

Guesthouse Sakina-Khanum is a charming guesthouse located in the village of Salakhli. It offers cozy rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Azerbaijani food.

What to Buy in Qax: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors who want to bring a piece of Qax home with them, there are many local products to choose from. Here are some top souvenirs:

Pomegranate Juice

Pomegranate juice is a popular beverage in Azerbaijan, and Qax is known for its high-quality pomegranates. Visitors can buy fresh pomegranate juice to take home as a souvenir.

Azerbaijani Carpets

Azerbaijani carpets are famous around the world for their intricate patterns and high quality. Visitors can buy carpets and other hand-woven textiles as souvenirs from local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Qax

Azerbaijani culture is rich and diverse, with many unique customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Qax:

  • Hospitality is very important in Azerbaijani culture, and visitors are often invited into people’s homes for tea or a meal.
  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • It’s considered impolite to refuse food or drink when offered by your host.

Best Time to Visit Qax

The best time to visit Qax depends on your preferences. The summer months, from June to August, are the warmest and offer the best hiking and outdoor activities. The spring and fall, from March to May and September to November, offer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds. Winter, from December to February, is cold and snowy, but offers the chance to ski on Tufandag Mountain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qax

Do:

  • Try traditional Azerbaijani food and drink.
  • Visit historical sites and museums.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their culture.
  • Photograph people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qax

Qax is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing. Additionally, visitors should be aware of their surroundings when hiking, as accidents and injuries can occur.

Health in Qax

Visitors should make sure to have all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Azerbaijan. The most common health concern for visitors is food and water safety, so be sure to drink bottled water and avoid uncooked or undercooked food.

Getting to Qax: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qax

The nearest airport to Qax is Gabala International Airport, which is located about 37 kilometers from the city center. Alternatively, visitors can fly into Baku Heydar Aliyev International Airport and take a bus or taxi to Qax.

Bus Stations Near Qax

Qax has a bus station located in the city center, offering transportation to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore the region independently, or hire a private driver for a more personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Qax

The best way to get around Qax is by car or taxi. There are also buses available, but they can be crowded and infrequent.

Where to Go Next from Qax: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, there are many other great destinations to visit near Qax. Here are some top choices:

  • Sheki: A nearby city known for its beautiful architecture and traditional Azerbaijani crafts.
  • Gobustan: A UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its ancient rock carvings and mud volcanoes.
  • Baku: The capital city of Azerbaijan, with a vibrant culture and many historical sites to explore.

Conclusion

Qax is a stunning destination with a rich history, beautiful natural landscape, and delicious food. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan your dream trip to Qax. So pack your bags and get ready for an adventure you’ll never forget!
