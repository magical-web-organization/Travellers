Introduction to Zaqatala

Situated in the picturesque Sheki Region of Azerbaijan, Zaqatala is a hidden gem in the Caucasus region of Asia. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture, Zaqatala attracts travelers looking for authentic experiences off the beaten path. The town is surrounded by rolling hills, dense forests, and serene lakes – making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Zaqatala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zaqatala History Museum Located in the center of town, the Zaqatala History Museum boasts a collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the town’s rich past. Visitors can learn about the region’s Azerbaijani, Georgian, and Russian influences through exhibits that include jewelry, weapons, traditional costumes, and everyday objects.

Zaqatala Fortress Built in the 1830s, the Zaqatala Fortress is a well-preserved historical site that provides a glimpse into Azerbaijan’s tumultuous past. The fortress served as a strategic point in the region, serving as a shelter for the local population during times of war and unrest.

Natural Attractions of Zaqatala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gadim Qala Gadim Qala, also known as the Old Castle, is a natural fortress in the Gadimoba village of Zaqatala. Visitors to Gadim Qala can explore the lush greenery of the surrounding forest and learn about the region’s history and culture.

Mest Dagi Mest Dagi, the highest peak in Zaqatala, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Visitors can take a hiking route up to the peak, which is suitable for both experienced hikers and beginners alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zaqatala

Zaqatala is a hiker’s paradise, offering a range of routes that cater to all levels of experience. The three of the most popular routes are:

Goyazan Mountain Trail Located near the village of Goyazan, this trail offers magnificent views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The route takes approximately 4-5 hours to complete and is suitable for intermediate hikers.

Kemal’s Trail Named after the Nobel Prize-winning Azerbaijani author, Kemal’s Trail is a moderate level hiking trail that takes visitors through the lush forest of Zaqatala to Gakh. The trail takes approximately 7-8 hours to complete, but the stunning scenery along the way makes it worthwhile.

MestDagi Peak Trail For experienced hikers, the MestDagi Peak Trail is a 6-hour hike that takes visitors to the highest point in Zaqatala. The trail is challenging, but the panoramic views from the top make it all worthwhile.

Best Things to Do in Zaqatala: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding Azerbaijan is known for its love of horses, and Zaqatala is no different. Visitors can take a horseback riding tour around the town or venture further into the surrounding countryside.

Traditional Carpet Weaving Zaqatala is also famous for its traditional carpet weaving. Visitors can take a tour of the local carpet workshops and learn about the intricate process of carpet weaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Zaqatala

Must-Try Dishes

Kufta Bajour Kufta Bajour is a traditional Azerbaijani dish made with minced meat, onions, and tomatoes, cooked in a clay pot over an open flame. The dish is a staple in Zaqatala and can be found in most local restaurants.

Gutab Gutab is a type of Azerbaijani flatbread stuffed with cheese, greens, or meat. Though it can be found throughout Azerbaijan and the Caucasus region, Zaqatala’s version is particularly delicious.

Local Beverages

Chay Azerbaijan is known for its tea culture, and Zaqatala is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a cup of traditional Azerbaijani black tea with sugar cubes, while taking in the stunning scenery of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Sharbat Sharbat is a sweet, syrupy drink made from fruit, flower petals, or herbs. It is a refreshing drink after a long day of hiking or exploring.

Where to Stay in Zaqatala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nariman Narimanov House-Museum For a unique cultural experience, visitors can stay at the Nariman Narimanov House-Museum, a beautifully decorated traditional Azerbaijani guesthouse.

Hotel Samir Hotel Samir is a modern hotel located in the heart of Zaqatala, offering comfortable rooms and amenities for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Zaqatala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Honey Zaqatala is known for its delicious honey, made from the wildflowers that grow in the surrounding forests.

Traditional Carpets Zaqatala’s traditional carpets are made using the highest quality wool and silk threads, each with a unique design and pattern.

Local Culture and Customs of Zaqatala

Zaqatala’s culture is a blend of Azerbaijani, Georgian, and Russian influences, making it a unique destination for travelers. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress modestly while exploring the town and surrounding areas.

Best Time to Visit Zaqatala

The best time to visit Zaqatala is between May and October, when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its best. Visitors should avoid traveling to the region during the winter months, when the roads can become treacherous and cold temperatures can make outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zaqatala

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly
  • Try the local cuisine, including Kufta Bajour and Gutab
  • Bring comfortable hiking shoes and clothing

Don’t:

  • Take photos without asking for permission
  • Disturb the local wildlife or damage the natural environment
  • Assume that everyone speaks English

Safety and Health

Safety in Zaqatala

Zaqatala is a generally safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from petty theft and stay informed about local travel advisories.

Health in Zaqatala

As with any travel destination, visitors should take basic health precautions in Zaqatala, such as drinking bottled water and wearing sunscreen. Visitors should also make sure they are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Azerbaijan.

Getting to Zaqatala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zaqatala

The nearest airport to Zaqatala is the Gabala International Airport, located about 60 kilometers from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Zaqatala from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Zaqatala

There are several bus stations in Azerbaijan that serve Zaqatala, including the Baku Bus Station and the Ganja Bus Station.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are also available for travelers who want to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Zaqatala

The best way to get around Zaqatala is by car or taxi, as public transportation can be unreliable. Visitors who plan to do a lot of hiking should consider renting a car or hiring a local guide to take them to the trails.

Where to Go Next from Zaqatala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sheki Located about 100 kilometers from Zaqatala, Sheki is a historic city famous for its ancient palaces, mosques, and caravanserais.

Ganja Azerbaijan’s second-largest city, Ganja, is located about 190 kilometers from Zaqatala and boasts a rich cultural heritage and beautiful architecture.

Conclusion

Zaqatala is a hidden gem in Azerbaijan, offering stunning natural scenery, rich history and culture, and a range of outdoor activities. Visitors can hike to the top of Mest Dagi peak, explore the region’s history and culture at the local museums and landmarks, and indulge in delicious local cuisine. With plenty of accommodation options and easy access to nearby destinations, Zaqatala is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Azerbaijani experience.
