Ismailli, located in Sheki Region in Azerbaijan, is a must-visit destination for any traveler seeking to explore the Caucasus. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality, Ismailli is a perfect destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike. This comprehensive travel guide covers all you need to know before you set off on your Ismailli adventure.

Introduction to Ismailli

Ismailli is a historic town in the Sheki Region that dates back to the 19th century. It is located between the Greater Caucasus Mountains and the Caspian Sea, making it a hub for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and skiing. The town also boasts several museums and cultural landmarks, making it a great destination for history and culture enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Ismailli

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ismailli has several museums and historical places that anyone interested in the region’s culture will find fascinating. Some must-visit landmarks are:

  • Sheki Khan’s Palace: This 18th-century palace is an architectural masterpiece with its intricate mosaic patterns and colorful stained glass windows.
  • Tanga Altı: Located in Gabala Road, this 19th-century bridge built in slate stone is an architectural wonder and a symbol of Azerbaijani culture.
  • Ismailli History and Ethnography Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Ismailli and the surrounding areas.
  • Momina Khatun Mausoleum: An ancient 12th-century burial place of Momina Khatun, the spouse of the Shirvanshah ruler.
  • Yeddi Gozal Waterfall: A famous local waterfall with a stunning view of nature.

Natural Attractions of Ismailli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ismailli has some of the best outdoor adventures in the Caucasus region. Some of the places you should head to for natural beauty include:

  • Ismailli National Park: This park covers an area of over 52,000 hectares and includes diverse flora and fauna, making it a great destination for nature lovers.
  • Lyaraman Plateau: With its picturesque scenery, this plateau is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and camping.
  • Goygol Lake: A stunning glacial lake surrounded by the Lesser Caucasus Mountains, which is a great place for fishing or a boat ride across the lake.
  • Tufandag Mountain Resort: One of the top ski resorts in the Caucasus region is located in Ismailli.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ismailli

Ismailli is a haven for hiking, trekking, and cycling enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Ismailli-Qabala trail: A 25 km trail from Ismailli to Gabala that offers a spectacular view of the Greater Caucasus Mountains.
  • Imamzade-Kish-Yeddi Gozal trail: This 7 km trail leads you to the iconic Yeddi Gozal Waterfall while passing through the dense forest, which is great for bird watching.
  • Lyaraman Plateau Trail: A 28 km trail with stunning views of the Caspian Sea and the Lesser Caucasus Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ismailli: Activities and Tours

Ismailli has more to offer than outdoor activities and cultural attractions. Here are some other things to do in Ismailli:

  • Hot springs therapy in Istisu or Shaki Michalchilar.
  • Grape harvest tours: Grape is a crucial agricultural product of Azerbaijan, and Ismailli is famous for its vineyards.
  • Cycling tour around Ismailli’s beautiful scenery.
  • Paragliding: Experience the breathtaking view of Ismailli from the sky.

Food and Drink Scene of Ismailli

Azerbaijani cuisine is diverse, and Ismailli is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Piti: A meat dish cooked in clay pot that is slow cooked for several hours until the meat is soft. It is served with bread and seasoned with aromatic herbs and spices.
  • Qutab: A thin layered pancake stuffed with minced meat, cheese, or greens.
  • Ismailli Sharab: A home-made sweet wine made from local grapes.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt drink that is great for hot summer days.
  • Black tea with jam: Azerbaijani style tea with various jams as sweeteners.
  • Sharbat: A refreshing drink made with water and flower extract.

Local Culture and Customs of Ismailli

Azerbaijani culture is rich, and the hospitality of the people of Ismailli is unparalleled. To get the most out of your trip, it is essential to respect cultural values and customs.

Best Time to Visit Ismailli

The best time to visit Ismailli is from May to September, when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ismailli

Here are some cultural do’s and don’ts every traveler should keep in mind when visiting Ismailli:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly in temples or mosques
  • Respect elders – it is essential in Azerbaijani culture
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home

Don’t:

  • Say anything offensive about Islam, the Azerbaijan government or its socio-cultural systems.
  • Use inappropriate or aggressive body language, profanity or behaviors that could be considered disrespectful with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ismailli

Ismailli is a safe town, but it is always best to take precautions when visiting any new location.

Health in Ismailli

Azerbaijan has a good health care system, with many hospitals and clinics available to visitors. It is crucial to have travel insurance in case of any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Ismailli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the ways you can get to Ismailli:

Airports Near Ismailli

The nearest international airports to Ismailli are:

  • Heydar Aliyev International Airport in Baku, which is a four-hour drive to the town center
  • Gabala International Airport, which is about 50 km from Ismailli.

Bus Stations Near Ismailli

Ismailli has a centrally located bus station with several bus routes available to/from Baku, Gabala, Ganja, and other cities in Azerbaijan. Some recommended bus services providers are:

  • Nabran Bus Company
  • Avrora Taxi
  • Samukh Bus

Other Options

If you prefer private transport, you can hire a car from Baku or other major cities, or hire a taxi.

How to Get Around in Ismailli

Ismailli’s transportation options include buses, shared taxis and private cars. Taxis are the most popular way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Ismailli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ismailli is a great base to explore the surrounding areas. Some nearby destinations you should visit include Sheki, Gabala, and Quba.

Conclusion

Ismailli is a unique destination that offers an unforgettable experience to all its visitors. With its beautiful outdoor scenery, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, this town is a perfect addition to your travel list. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to make the most out of your Ismailli adventure!
