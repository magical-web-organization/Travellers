Introduction to Lahich

Nestled in the Sheki Region of Azerbaijan in the Caucasus, Lahich is a charming village known for its traditional architecture, cobbled streets, and local handicrafts. The town has a rich history dating back to medieval times and is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Lahich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lahich is home to several historical landmarks, including the oldest mosque in Azerbaijan, the Juma Mosque. The mosque has been in use since the 8th century and features stunning architecture and intricate frescoes.

Visitors can also explore the Lahic History Museum, which offers a glimpse into the town’s past, including exhibits on traditional crafts, handmade carpets, and agro-economy.

Natural Attractions of Lahich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy the spectacular views and outdoor adventures around Lahich. The village is situated in the foothills of the Greater Caucasus mountains, making it an ideal spot for hiking, horseback riding, and mountain biking.

One popular hiking trail leads to the nearby waterfall, where visitors can enjoy a refreshing swim in the cool waters. Or, for a more relaxing experience, visitors can stroll through the lush forests and gardens of the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lahich

For outdoor enthusiasts, Lahich offers several hiking and biking routes through the mountains and surrounding valleys. The trails range in difficulty from easy to challenging, and visitors can choose from guided or self-guided tours.

One of the popular routes is the hike to the nearby waterfall, where you can take a dip in the refreshing waters. Another option is a bike trip to the nearby village of Kish, where visitors can enjoy its beautiful medieval architecture.

Best Things to Do in Lahich: Activities and Tours

From exploring the town’s historical landmarks to experiencing its natural beauty, there’s no shortage of things to do in Lahich.

Visitors can take part in cooking classes and learn to make local delicacies, such as plov (rice pilaf) and dolma (stuffed grape leaves). Or, visit one of the town’s handicraft workshops and watch local artisans create handmade carpets and copper products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lahich

Day 1: Explore the historical landmarks of Lahich, including the Juma Mosque and the Lahic History Museum.

Day 2: Take a guided bike trip to the nearby village of Kish and explore its beautiful medieval architecture.

Day 3: Hike to the nearby waterfall and enjoy a swim in its refreshing waters.

Day 4: Take part in a cooking class and learn to make local delicacies, such as plov and dolma.

Day 5: Visit one of the town’s handicraft workshops and watch local artisans create handmade carpets and copper products.

Day 6: Take a guided tour through the surrounding valleys and enjoy the scenic views and lush forests.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the town, or take a day trip to nearby attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Lahich

Must-Try Dishes

Lahich is famous for its traditional cuisine, which features many delicious dishes made from fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Some of the must-try dishes include plov, a rice pilaf typically made with lamb, carrots, and onions. Another popular dish is dolma, stuffed grape leaves filled with a mixture of rice, herbs, and spices.

Local Beverages

No trip to Lahich would be complete without trying the local beverages. One popular drink is tea made from local herbs, such as mint or thyme. Another popular beverage is qutab, a type of savory crepe filled with meat or vegetables.

Where to Stay in Lahich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lahich offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and traveler. Visitors can choose from hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, many of which offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

One popular option is the Lahic Guesthouse, which features traditional architecture and rustic charm. For those looking for more luxurious accommodations, the Sheki Palace Hotel is a great choice, with its beautiful design and many amenities.

What to Buy in Lahich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lahich is famous for its local handicrafts, made by skilled artisans using traditional methods. Visitors can find a wide range of products, from handmade carpets and copper products to pottery and textiles.

One of the most popular souvenirs is the traditional Lahiji carpet, known for its intricate designs and vibrant colors. Another popular item is the copper teapot, used for brewing traditional Azerbaijan tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Lahich

Lahich is a conservative town with strong ties to its traditional culture and customs. Visitors should be respectful of these customs and dress modestly when visiting local landmarks or religious sites.

One tradition visitors may encounter is the giving of gifts, which is customary when visiting someone’s home or business. It is also common for visitors to remove their shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Lahich

The best time to visit Lahich is during the spring or early summer, when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. This is also the best time for outdoor adventures, such as hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lahich

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting local landmarks or religious sites
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting public places
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Ignore the local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Lahich

Lahich is generally a safe town, and visitors need not be overly concerned about safety. However, it is always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Lahich

Visitors to Lahich should take basic health precautions, such as drinking bottled water and using insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Lahich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lahich

The nearest airports to Lahich are the Heydar Aliyev International Airport in Baku and the Ganja International Airport in Ganja. From either airport, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Lahich.

Bus Stations Near Lahich

There are several bus stations near Lahich, including the Sheki Bus Station and the Lankaran Bus Station. Buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel around Azerbaijan.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lahich by taking a train to the town of Sheki and then taking a taxi or bus to the village. Another option is to hire a private driver or car rental, which offers more flexibility and convenience.

How to Get Around in Lahich

Lahich is a small village with a compact layout, making it easy to get around on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Lahich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Lahich, including:

  • Sheki: A beautiful town known for its medieval architecture and historic landmarks.
  • Ganja: The second-largest city in Azerbaijan, famous for its beautiful parks and gardens.
  • Gobustan National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient petroglyphs and other geological formations.

Conclusion

Lahich is a hidden gem in Azerbaijan that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the country’s traditional culture and customs. From exploring historical landmarks to experiencing the natural beauty of the town, there’s something for everyone in Lahich. So, pack your bags and get ready for a memorable journey to this beautiful village.
