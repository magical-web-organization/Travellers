A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Baku, Azerbaijan

Baku, the capital city of Azerbaijan, is a vibrant city that sits on the western coast of the Caspian Sea. The city is known for its UNESCO-listed Old City, a unique blend of medieval and modern architecture, and its rich culture. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there are plenty of things to do and see in this bustling city. In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive overview of Baku, with information on where to stay, what to do, and the best time to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Baku

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Baku is home to several museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the rich culture and history of the city. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Old City (Icherisheher)
  • Maiden Tower (Giz Galasi)
  • National Museum of History of Azerbaijan
  • Azerbaijan Carpet Museum
  • Museum of Miniature Books
  • Palace of the Shirvanshahs

Natural Attractions of Baku: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who enjoy outdoor adventures and scenic views, Baku is home to several attractions:

  • Flame Towers
  • Baku Boulevard
  • Yanardag Fire Mountain
  • Ateshgah Fire Temple
  • Gobustan National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baku

Baku is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts with several trails to explore:

  • Baku Mountain Park
  • Absheron National Park
  • Qobu Bikes

Baku has no shortage of exciting activities and tours, including:

Food and Drink Scene of Baku

Must-Try Dishes

Baku is known for its traditional Azerbaijan cuisine, including:

  • Plov (pilaf)
  • Dolma (stuffed grape leaves)
  • Kebab (grilled meat)
  • Lavangi (fish wrapped in dough)
  • Qutab (flatbread stuffed with herbs and cheese)

Local Beverages

Azerbaijan is famous for its tea culture, with several local teas to try, including:

  • Black tea with jam
  • Matzoon (fermented milk)
  • Ayran (yogurt drink)
  • Sari (herbal tea)

Local Culture and Customs of Baku

Azerbaijan culture is a blend of Eastern and Western traditions. Here are a few customs to keep in mind when visiting Baku:

  • Cover your head and shoulders when entering a mosque or religious site
  • Shake hands with your host when entering/leaving a place
  • Refrain from discussing politics or religion

Best Time to Visit Baku

The best time to visit Baku is between March and May or from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, making it difficult to explore the city on foot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baku

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Negotiate prices in markets

Don’t:

  • Express political opinions
  • Take photos without permission
  • Smoke in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Baku

Baku is generally a safe city for tourists. However, it’s always important to take precautions when traveling to a new location, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Baku

It’s recommended to drink bottled water while in Baku to avoid any potential health issues.
