Introduction to Karabakh

Karabakh, also known as Nagorno-Karabakh, is a disputed territory in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia. Despite being internationally recognized as part of Azerbaijan, Karabakh is de facto controlled by ethnic Armenian authorities. Located between the Lesser Caucasus and Great Caucasus mountain ranges, Karabakh is home to breathtaking natural landscapes, rich history, and diverse culture.

Whether you are interested in exploring ancient sites, indulge in outdoor activities or savor the local cuisine, Karabakh has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of top-rated places to visit in Karabakh:

Best Things to Do in Karabakh: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Guided Tour of Shushi

Week-Long Itinerary for Karabakh

Day 1: Arrive in Stepanakert, the capital of Karabakh. Visit the Stepanakert History Museum and stroll around the central market.

Day 2: Explore Shushi, the cultural capital of Karabakh. Take a guided tour of the city and visit the Ghazanchetsots Cathedral.

Day 3: Hike to the Ughtasar Petroglyphs and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Day 4: Visit the Gandzasar Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its historic architecture and cultural significance.

Day 5: Go wine tasting in the Areni Wine Region and learn about the winemaking traditions of Karabakh.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Lake Sevan and hike around the area to take in the beautiful scenery.

Day 7: Spend the day in Dadivank Monastery and explore the nearby Barda River Circuit.

Food and Drink Scene of Karabakh

The local cuisine of Karabakh is a delicious blend of Azerbaijani and Armenian influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to savor while in Karabakh:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab
  • Dolma
  • Piti
  • Lavash
  • Baklava

Local Beverages

  • Armenian brandy
  • Tarkhun (a non-alcoholic carbonated drink made from tarragon)
  • Matzun (Armenian yogurt)
  • Shan tea (Armenian herbal tea)
  • Dried fruit vodka

What to Buy in Karabakh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Karabakh is known for its traditional handicrafts, including carpets, pottery, and artwork. Some popular souvenirs to buy while in Karabakh are:

  • Karabakh wall art
  • Armenian rugs
  • Khachkars (traditional Armenian stone crosses)
  • Traditional pottery and ceramics
  • Dolma-making supplies

Local Culture and Customs of Karabakh

The people of Karabakh are known for their hospitality and warm generosity. Some cultural norms to be aware of before visiting Karabakh include:

  • Always remove your shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Eat with your right hand and drink with your left hand
  • Avoid discussing sensitive political topics

Best Time to Visit Karabakh

The best time to visit Karabakh is between April and October when the weather is mild and sunny. During this period, you can participate in outdoor activities and explore the region’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Karabakh

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Explore the beautiful outdoor landscapes and architecture
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Discuss sensitive political topics
  • Wear revealing clothing in public spaces
  • Show disrespect to religious sites and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Karabakh

Karabakh is generally safe for tourists, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should be aware of the landmines remaining from past conflicts along the Armenian border.

Health in Karabakh

Medical facilities in Karabakh are limited, and travelers are advised to bring necessary prescriptions, vaccines, and medical supplies with them. It is also important to drink bottled water, wash hands regularly, and avoid street food to prevent any health issues.

Getting to Karabakh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Karabakh

The nearest international airports to Karabakh are in Yerevan, Armenia and Baku, Azerbaijan. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Stepanakert.

Bus Stations Near Karabakh

There are bus services from Yerevan and Baku that take you directly to Stepanakert.

Other Options

You can also rent cars or hire local drivers to take you to Karabakh from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Karabakh

The best way to get around Karabakh is by taxi or private car. You can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to explore the region’s natural beauty.
