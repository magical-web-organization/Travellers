Introduction to Qazakh

Qazakh is tucked away in the northwest of Azerbaijan, bordering Georgia and Armenia. With a rich history and cultural heritage, Qazakh offers an array of outdoor activities and picturesque landscapes. The city is known for its delicious cuisine, hospitality and unique souvenirs.

List of Places to Visit in Qazakh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Qazakh City Museum

Located in the heart of the city, Qazakh City Museum showcases the rich history and culture of the region, dating back to ancient times. The museum has a vast collection of artifacts, including textiles, weapons, jewelry, coins and ceramics from the early Bronze Age to the present day.

Ismayilli Fortress

This impressive fortress is situated on a hilltop and offers outstanding views of the surrounding countryside. Dating back to the 18th century, Ismayilli Fortress has been restored to showcase Azerbaijan’s rich architectural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Qazakh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jafar Jabbarli Lake

A perfect place to escape busy city life, Jafar Jabbarli Lake is situated in the picturesque Jafarli village, surrounded by lush forests and green hills. Boating, fishing and picnicking are the most popular activities here.

Ilisu Waterfall

Located in the village of Ilisu, this waterfall is a hidden gem that offers scenic views and a chance to cool off in the hot summer months. The hike to the waterfall is moderately challenging, but the views are worth the effort.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qazakh

Kurekchay Mountains Trail

This moderate hiking trail takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the region, including mountainous valleys, natural springs and lush forests.

Biking in Goltug Mountain

The Goltug mountain range offers a challenging but highly rewarding biking experience. The trail runs through the foothills and offers breathtaking views of stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Qazakh: Activities and Tours

Qazakh City Tour

Explore the cultural heritage of Qazakh City with a guided tour. Visit the historic landmarks, museums, and bazaars, and enjoy local foods and cultural experiences.

Ilisu Waterfall Adventure

Hike to the Ilisu Waterfall with a local guide and experience the beauty of the Azerbaijani wilderness. Enjoy a picnic near the waterfall and take a refreshing swim in the natural pool.

Food and Drink Scene of Qazakh

Must-Try Dishes

Plov

This classic Azerbaijani dish is made with rice, saffron, and pork or lamb. The dish boasts a range of flavors and is a perfect example of Azerbaijan’s culinary heritage.

Qutab

Qutab is a classic Azerbaijani flatbread filled with a variety of delicious ingredients such as cheese, minced meat, herbs and spices, and pomegranate seeds.

Local Beverages

Ayran

Made of yoghurt, water and salt, ayran is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot Azerbaijani summers.

Black tea

Tea is an integral part of Azerbaijani culture and the go-to drink for social and family events. The tea is served with sugar or traditional sweets.

Where to Stay in Qazakh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qazakh Guest House

This cozy guest house offers comfortable rooms, delicious breakfast, and warm hospitality. The guest house is located in the heart of the city and is a perfect place to relax after a long day of exploring the surroundings.

Hotel Caspian

Located just a stone’s throw from the city center, Hotel Caspian is a modern hotel with spacious rooms, complimentary breakfasts, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Qazakh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional carpets and rugs

Azerbaijani carpets are renowned for their intricate designs and high-quality materials. You can find carpets and rugs in traditional markets and souvenir shops.

Pomegranate products

Azerbaijani pomegranates are famous for a reason. Pomegranate juice, jams, and spices are all popular products available for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Qazakh

Azerbaijanis are a hospitable and welcoming people. Upon visiting someone’s home, it is customary to bring the host a small gift such as sweets or pastries. Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites. Azerbaijanis place high importance on their guest’s comfort and happiness, so it’s essential to be respectful and polite to your hosts.

Best Time to Visit Qazakh

The best time to visit Qazakh is from April to June and September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qazakh

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and enjoy the hospitality of the locals.
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Show disrespect to local customs and traditions.
  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photographs of military or police facilities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qazakh

Qazakh is a safe city with low crime rates. It is always best to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Qazakh

It’s essential to be up to date on your routine vaccinations before your trip to Qazakh. The water is not always safe for drinking, so it’s best to stick to bottled water.

Getting to Qazakh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qazakh

The nearest international airport to Qazakh is Heydar Aliyev International Airport in Baku, approximately 340km away.

Bus Stations Near Qazakh

Qazakh is well connected by public transport, with regular buses to Baku, Ganja, and neighboring towns.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer more flexibility.

How to Get Around in Qazakh

The best way to explore Qazakh and its surroundings is by rental car or taxi. Public transportation is available, but it is infrequent and often crowded.

Where to Go Next from Qazakh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sheki

Located 85km southeast of Qazakh, the historic town of Sheki is famous for its stunning architecture and beautiful landscapes.

Ganja

Ganja is one of Azerbaijan’s oldest cities and a popular destination for travelers. Located approximately 135km south of Qazakh, the city is known for its beautiful parks, historic sites, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Qazakh is a fascinating city that has something to offer for every traveler. With its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality, Qazakh will leave you with lifelong memories of your trip to Azerbaijan.
