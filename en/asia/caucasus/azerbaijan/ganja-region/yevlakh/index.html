Introduction to Yevlakh

Yevlakh is a small city located in the Ganja Region of Azerbaijan. It is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. The city is a popular destination for tourists who want to experience the true essence of Azerbaijan.

List of Places to Visit in Yevlakh

Here are some of the top places to visit in Yevlakh:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Karabakh Carpet Weaving Museum: This museum houses a collection of beautifully woven carpets that depict the rich cultural history of Azerbaijan.

  • Yevlakh History Museum: The Yevlakh History Museum showcases the history and culture of the entire region, dating back to the ancient times.

  • Maiden’s Tower: A well-preserved tower dating back to the 12th century that is considered a must-see for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Yevlakh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bash Dashagil Mud Volcanoes: This natural attraction offers a unique experience where visitors can witness the eruption of mud from small volcanoes.

  • Turyanchay Natural Reserve: This reserve is perfect for nature enthusiasts who want to experience the lush flora and fauna of the region.

  • Goy Goyun Waterfall: One of the most beautiful natural sites in Yevlakh, the Goy Goyun waterfall provides a stunning view of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yevlakh

Yevlakh offers some stunning hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Turyanchay Natural Reserve Hiking Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking enthusiasts who want to explore the beautiful countryside of Yevlakh.

  • Elizabeth Polak Biking Trail: This biking trail is popular among tourists who want to explore the city’s beautiful scenery while enjoying a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Yevlakh: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Karabakh Carpet Weaving Museum and learn about the art of carpet weaving.

  • Explore the city’s architecture and history on a walking tour of central Yevlakh.

  • Experience the natural beauty of the region on a guided tour of the Turyanchay Natural Reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yevlakh

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Yevlakh:

Day 1: Visit the Karabakh Carpet Weaving Museum and the Yevlakh History Museum.

Day 2: Embark on a hiking trip to the Turyanchay Natural Reserve.

Day 3: Explore the city’s local markets and indulge in the delicious street food.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Goy Goyun Waterfall and soak in the stunning view of the surrounding mountains.

Day 5: Go on a biking trip on the Elizabeth Polak Biking Trail.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Ganja, Azerbaijan’s second-largest city, and explore its cultural and historical sites.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Yevlakh’s many spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Yevlakh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pakhlava: A popular Azerbaijani dessert made of layers of thinly rolled dough and filled with chopped nuts and honey.

  • Piti: A traditional Azerbaijani soup made with mutton, vegetables, and chickpeas.

  • Dolma: A dish made of grape or cabbage leaves filled with a mixture of minced meat, rice, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

  • Tea: Tea is a staple beverage in Yevlakh and the rest of Azerbaijan.

  • Pomegranate juice: Azerbaijan is known for its delicious pomegranates and pomegranate juice.

Where to Stay in Yevlakh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yevlakh offers a range of accommodations for tourists, depending on their budget and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Qubek Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Yevlakh.

  • Nargiz Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house that offers basic amenities and a comfortable stay.

  • Baku Hostel: A backpacker-friendly hostel that is ideal for solo travelers.

What to Buy in Yevlakh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Azerbaijani Carpets: Yevlakh is known for its beautiful handmade carpets that make for perfect souvenirs.

  • Pomegranate Juice: Azerbaijan produces some of the best pomegranate juice in the world, and it makes for a healthy and delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Yevlakh

Azerbaijan is a Muslim-majority country, and visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs. Some of the cultural nuances to keep in mind while visiting Yevlakh include:

  • Dress modestly in public places.

  • Remove shoes before entering a mosque.

  • Do not eat or drink in public during the month of Ramadan.

Best Time to Visit Yevlakh

The best time to visit Yevlakh is during the spring and autumn months (March-June and September-November). The weather is mild and pleasant during these months, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yevlakh

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take a guided tour to learn about the city’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

  • Disrespect the locals or their religious beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yevlakh

Yevlakh is generally a safe city for tourists, but visitors are advised to take basic safety precautions like not carrying too much cash or valuables while sightseeing.

Health in Yevlakh

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and carry a medical kit with essential medicines. It is also recommended to get vaccinated against common diseases like hepatitis and typhoid before visiting Azerbaijan.

Getting to Yevlakh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yevlakh

The nearest airport to Yevlakh is Ganja International Airport, which is approximately 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near Yevlakh

Yevlakh has a central bus station that connects the city to other parts of Azerbaijan. The station is located in the city center and is easily accessible.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Yevlakh by train or car. The city is well-connected to other parts of Azerbaijan by road and rail.

How to Get Around in Yevlakh

Yevlakh is a relatively small city, and most of its attractions are easily accessible on foot or by taxi.

Where to Go Next from Yevlakh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions that tourists can explore while in Yevlakh include:

  • Ganja: Azerbaijan’s second-largest city is known for its rich history and culture.

  • Baku: Azerbaijan’s capital city is known for its modern architecture and vibrant nightlife.

  • Sheki: A picturesque city known for its beautiful historical buildings and scenic views.

Conclusion

Yevlakh is a hidden gem in Azerbaijan that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, tourists can experience everything that Yevlakh has to offer and take back memories that will last a lifetime.
