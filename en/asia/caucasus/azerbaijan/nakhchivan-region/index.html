Introduction to Nakhchivan (region)

Nakhchivan is a culturally diverse and historically rich region in Azerbaijan, situated in the Caucasus mountain range. It is an autonomous republic that shares borders with Armenia, Iran, and Turkey. Nakhchivan is known for its unique blend of Islamic and Turkic cultures, and its traditional architecture, food, and handicrafts.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhchivan (region)

Nakhchivan has a plethora of landmarks and attractions that reflect its cultural and geographic diversity. Here is a list of must-visit sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Alinja Castle
  2. Nakhchivan City Walls
  3. Nakhchivan State Historical Museum
  4. Mausoleum of Yusif ibn Kuseyir

Natural Attractions of Nakhchivan (region): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mammadbayli Canyon
  2. Batabat Lake
  3. Ashabi-Kahf Cave
  4. Nakhchivan plain

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakhchivan (region)

Nakhchivan is a haven for hikers and bikers, with various routes available through the region’s natural terrain. Some popular trails are:

  1. Julfa-Batabat Trail
  2. Gorgud-Gulabird Trail
  3. Ashabi-Kahf Cave Trail

Best Things to Do in Nakhchivan (region): Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Nakhchivan offers a range of activities and guided tours. Some of the best things to do are:

  1. Visit Baghirsag rock paintings
  2. Attend the Novruz Festival
  3. Explore Tigranakert archaeological site
  4. Taste local cuisine at Ordubad bazaar

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhchivan (region)

Nakhchivan’s cuisine is a reflection of the region’s diverse cultural influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Piti - a soup made with lamb and chickpeas
  2. Qutab - a stuffed flatbread with herbs and cheese
  3. Turshu Kebab - seasoned lamb meatballs

Local Beverages

  1. Ayran - a yogurt-based drink
  2. Sharbat - a sweet fruit drink
  3. Tea - Azerbaijan’s national hot beverage

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhchivan (region)

Nakhchivan has a rich cultural heritage and unique traditions that visitors should be aware of. Some customs to keep in mind are:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  2. Remove shoes before entering homes or sacred sites
  3. Greet locals with “salam” or “salam aleikum”

Best Time to Visit Nakhchivan (region)

The best time to visit Nakhchivan is from May to October when the weather is pleasant and dry. The peak tourist season is July to August, so expect crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhchivan (region)

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to help you have a hassle-free trip to Nakhchivan:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Carry identification at all times
  3. Use official taxis or pre-arrange transport

Don’t:

  1. Take photographs of military sites and personnel
  2. Use drugs or engage in illegal activities
  3. Travel alone in remote areas at night

Safety and Health

Here is some information about safety and health in Nakhchivan:

Safety in Nakhchivan (region)

Nakhchivan is generally a safe region to visit. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty crimes in crowded areas. It is also important to avoid political demonstrations and protests, which can turn violent.

Health in Nakhchivan (region)

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and consider getting vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B. It is also important to prevent mosquito bites, as there have been occasional cases of malaria reported.

How to Get Around in Nakhchivan (region)

The best way to get around Nakhchivan is by private car or taxi. Public transport is limited, and roads can be challenging to navigate for those unfamiliar with the region.
