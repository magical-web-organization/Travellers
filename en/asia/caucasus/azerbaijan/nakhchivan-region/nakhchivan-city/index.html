Introduction to Nakhchivan City

Located in the far-western region of Azerbaijan, in the Caucasus mountains, Nakhchivan City is a picturesque destination that boasts of its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning natural scenery. As a city located on the border of Iran and Turkey, Nakhchivan has a unique blend of cultures that can be seen in its architectural designs, cuisine, and way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhchivan City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nakhchivan State Historical and Architectural Reserve
  2. Momina Khatun Mausoleum
  3. Yusif ibn Kuseyir Mausoleum
  4. Nakhchivan Khan’s Palace
  5. Prophet Noah’s Tomb

The above list includes some of the must-visit museums, mausoleums, and historical structures that showcase the ancient culture of Nakhchivan.

Natural Attractions of Nakhchivan City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Batabat Lake
  2. Garabaghlar Tombstones
  3. Ashabi-Kahf Cave

For outdoor enthusiasts, Nakhchivan offers a plethora of natural attractions that are worth exploring. From scenic landscapes to adrenaline-pumping activities such as hiking, trekking, and hunting, Nakhchivan has something for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhchivan City

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Piti (stewed lamb with chickpeas)
  2. Dovga (a yoghurt-based soup)
  3. Kufte Bozbash (a meatball stew)
  4. Shakh Pilaf (a saffron-infused rice dish)

Local Beverages

  1. Doymaj (a sour buttermilk)
  2. Sharbat (a fruit syrup mixed with water)
  3. Nakhchivan Wine

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhchivan City

Nakhchivan City has a rich cultural heritage that is expressed through its traditional clothing, cuisine, music, and dance. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices to enjoy an authentic experience of the city.

Best Time to Visit Nakhchivan City

The best time to visit Nakhchivan City is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. Avoid visiting during the summer months as it can get quite hot during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhchivan City

Do:

  1. Dress conservatively in public places.
  2. Be respectful of locals’ religion and customs.
  3. Try the local cuisine and participate in cultural events.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of people without their permission.
  2. Disrespect public monuments and historic sites.
  3. Travel alone at night in isolated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakhchivan City

Nakhchivan City is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should take precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Nakhchivan City

Visitors should be aware of the health risks associated with eating street food and ensure that they drink bottled water.

Bus Stations Near Nakhchivan City

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Nakhchivan City from Tabriz, Iran via a daily flight.

How to Get Around in Nakhchivan City

Getting around in Nakhchivan City is easy via public transportation such as buses, taxis and also rented cars.

Nakhchivan City is one of the hidden gems of Azerbaijan that offers a unique blend of culture, history, natural beauty, and adventure.
