Introduction to Sukhumi

Sukhumi is a charming coastal town in the northwestern region of Abkhazia, situated in the heart of the Caucasus mountain range. Sukhumi has a rich history and culture, dating back to the ancient Greek and Roman times. Despite being overlooked by many travelers, Sukhumi has a lot to offer, from stunning natural landscapes to cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine.

In this comprehensive guide to Sukhumi, we will take you on a journey to the best places to visit, hike, dine, stay, and learn about the local culture. Whether you are a solo traveler, a couple or a group of friends, Sukhumi should not be missed on your travel itinerary for Caucasus.

List of Places to Visit in Sukhumi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in exploring the rich cultural heritage of Sukhumi, here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • The Sukhumi State Museum of History and Local Lore: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Abkhazia and has an extensive collection of artifacts, costumes, and photographs.
  • The Sukhumi Ethnographic Museum: This museum showcases the traditional lifestyle, customs, and crafts of the local Abkhazian people.
  • The Abkhazian State Drama Theater: This beautiful theater, built-in 1880, is known for its stunning architecture, rich history, and diverse performing arts programs.
  • The Sukhumi Botanical Garden: This lush garden, established in 1840, is home to over 4000 plant species from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Sukhumi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an adventure seeker and love to explore the raw natural beauty of a place, Sukhumi has a lot to offer. Some of the best natural attractions that you should not miss are:

  • The Ritsa Lake: Surrounded by dense forests and stunning scenery, the Ritsa lake is one of the most beautiful natural spots in Sukhumi, and it is an ideal place for boating, fishing, and camping.
  • The Novy Afon Cave: This underground wonderland is a must-visit attraction for caving enthusiasts that dates back over a million years.
  • The Psyrtskha River: This untamed river offers an adrenaline-fueled experience of rafting, kayaking, and other water sports.
  • The Holy Mountain of Athos: Located on the outskirts of Sukhumi, this mountain offers a breathtaking view of the city and the Black Sea, and it is considered the holiest site in Abkhazia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sukhumi

Sukhumi offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the Caucasus region. With its diverse landscapes, Sukhumi is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes that you should explore:

  • The Sukhumi Botanical Garden Trail: This trail takes you through the lush greenery of the botanical garden, where you can admire the diverse plant life and learn about the region’s flora and fauna.
  • The Coastal Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the Black Sea and takes you through a series of beaches, coves, and cliffs.
  • The Akarmara Trail: This trail takes you through the Akarmara valley, where you can admire the natural beauty of the region, including waterfalls, forests, and meadows.
  • The Mtirala National Park Trail: This trail takes you through the dense forests of Mtirala National Park, where you can spot rare species of animals and plants.

Best Things to Do in Sukhumi: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Sukhumi offers a plethora of activities and tours to keep you entertained and engaged. Some of the best things to do in Sukhumi are:

  • Visit Abkhazia’s traditional vineyards and wineries to taste local wine and cheese.
  • Take part in a traditional Abkhazian cooking class and learn how to cook local dishes such as pkhali, chakhokhbili, and khinkali.
  • Join a guided sightseeing tour of Sukhumi to learn more about the city’s history and culture.
  • Go paragliding or hang gliding to get a bird’s eye view of the region.
  • Visit the Abkhazian State Circus, known for its unique blend of acrobatics, juggling, and clown acts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sukhumi

If you are planning a week-long trip to Sukhumi, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1 - Arrival in Sukhumi

  • Check into your hotel or hostel and explore the city center at your leisure.

Day 2 - Cultural Attractions

  • Visit the Sukhumi State Museum of History and Local Lore and the Sukhumi Ethnographic Museum.
  • Have lunch at a local restaurant and try some of the traditional Abkhazian dishes.
  • Visit the Abkhazian State Drama Theater in the evening and watch a performance.

Day 3 - Natural Attractions

  • Visit the Ritsa Lake and take a boat tour of the lake.
  • Go hiking in the forests around the lake and have a picnic lunch.
  • Return to Sukhumi in the evening and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 4 - Sightseeing Tour

  • Take a guided sightseeing tour of Sukhumi and visit the Holy Mountain of Athos and the Sukhumi Botanical Garden, among other attractions.
  • Have lunch at a local restaurant and taste some of the local wines and cheeses.

Day 5 - Outdoor Adventures

  • Go for a rafting trip on the Psyrtskha River, followed by a picnic lunch.
  • Visit the Novy Afon Cave and explore the underground tunnels.
  • Return to Sukhumi in the evening and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 6 - Hiking/Biking Tour

  • Go on a hiking/biking trip to the Akarmara Trail or the Coastal Trail, depending on your preference.
  • Have lunch at a local restaurant and relax on the beach.
  • Return to Sukhumi in the evening and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 7 - Departure

  • Check out of your hotel/hostel and depart from Sukhumi.

Food and Drink Scene of Sukhumi

Abkhazian cuisine is a combination of flavors and influences from the region’s diverse history and culture. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages that you should indulge while in Sukhumi are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pkhali: A type of Georgian vegetable pate made of spinach, beetroot or cabbage and mixed with walnuts, garlic and herbs.
  • Chakhokhbili: A traditional Abkhazian stew made of chicken with tomatoes, peppers, onions, and herbs.
  • Khinkali: A traditional Georgian dumpling filled with spiced meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Abkhazian wine: Abkhazia has a long tradition of winemaking, and the region’s mild climate and fertile soil make it ideal for growing grapes. If you are a wine lover, make sure to taste some of the local wines, such as lykhny or anoush, during your visit.
  • Tarkhun: A refreshing carbonated drink made from tarragon and other herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Sukhumi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sukhumi has a range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the top recommendations are:

  • Ritsa Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near the Ritsa Lake, offering stunning views and a range of amenities such as a spa, swimming pool, and restaurant.
  • Hotel Aurora: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city, offering clean and comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.
  • Guest House Lana: A cozy and inviting guesthouse offering comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden to relax in.

What to Buy in Sukhumi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sukhumi offers a range of local products and souvenirs that will make a perfect memento of your trip. Here are some suggestions:

  • Local wines and cheeses, such as lykhny and anoush wines, and smoked cheese.
  • Traditional Abkhazian clothes and accessories, such as hats, shoes, and jewelry.
  • Handmade crafts and souvenirs, such as ceramics, paintings, and woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Sukhumi

Abkhazia has a unique culture and history that reflects the region’s diversity and influences from neighboring countries, such as Russia, Georgia, and Turkey. If you want to learn more about the local culture and customs, here are some suggestions:

  • Attend a traditional Abkhazian dance or music performance at the Abkhazian State Drama Theater.
  • Visit the Sukhumi Ethnographic Museum to learn about the region’s traditional crafts, clothing, and customs.
  • Interact with the local people and try to learn some of the basic phrases in the Abkhazian language.

Best Time to Visit Sukhumi

The best time to visit Sukhumi is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, keep in mind that summer is also the peak tourist season, and the prices may be higher than other times of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sukhumi

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  • Try to sample the local cuisine and engage with the local people.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a home.

Don’t:

  • Do not photograph military installations or personnel without permission.
  • Do not engage in any political discussions with the locals.
  • Do not be disrespectful towards the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sukhumi

Sukhumi is a relatively safe destination, but travelers should exercise caution when dealing with strangers or wandering outside the city center after sunset. Keep your valuables and personal belongings safe, and avoid political demonstrations or large gatherings.

Health in Sukhumi

Travelers should take appropriate precautions when it comes to their health and safety. Make sure to update your routine vaccinations before traveling, and carry a first aid kit with you. Abkhazia has limited medical facilities, so travelers with specific medical needs should consult a doctor before their trip.

Getting to Sukhumi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sukhumi

The nearest international airport to Sukhumi is Sochi International Airport, located about 140 km away. From Sochi, you can take a taxi, bus, or private transfer to Sukhumi.

Bus Stations Near Sukhumi

There are several bus stations in Sukhumi, including the Abkhazia Central Bus Station and the Sukhumi Inter-City Bus Station. Buses run regularly to and from neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

If you are traveling from Georgia, you can take a marshrutka (minibus) from Tbilisi Central Bus Station or the Batumi Central Bus Station to Sokhumi.

How to Get Around in Sukhumi

Sukhumi is a relatively small town, and you can easily navigate the city center on foot. Taxis, buses, and minibusses are also available for exploring the region and traveling outside the city.

Where to Go Next from Sukhumi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spare, Sukhumi offers several nearby destinations that are worth a visit, such as:

  • The city of Gagra, known for its historical landmarks and beautiful beaches.
  • The Bzyb Valley, a nature reserve with stunning scenery and a range of outdoor activities.
  • The village of Anakopia, believed to be the oldest settlement on the Black Sea coast.

Conclusion

Sukhumi may not be as well-known as other tourist destinations in Caucasus, but it offers a unique blend of history, culture, and nature that is worth exploring. From visiting museums and historical sites to hiking through lush forests and tasting the local cuisine, Sukhumi has something to offer for every type of traveler. So, pack your bags and head to Sukhumi for an unforgettable experience!
