Introduction to Taichung

Taichung is a culturally rich and vibrant city located in Central Taiwan, known for its modern architecture, bustling night markets, and natural beauty. The city has become a popular tourist destination because of its proximity to Sun Moon Lake, breathtaking views of the mountains, and a unique blend of traditional and modern culture.

Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or the best local cuisine, Taichung has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the city’s must-see destinations, local customs, and best-kept secrets.

List of Places to Visit in Taichung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Taichung has a rich history and culture, and there are several museums and historical sites that every traveler must visit.

  • National Taichung Theater: This stunning modern architecture is an artistic and cultural landmark worth visiting. Designed with a unique perspective that showcases both traditional and modern elements, the theater is a perfect place for cultural performances and events.

  • Paochueh Temple: A peaceful haven located in the heart of Taichung City, Paochueh Temple is the largest Buddhist temple in the city. It is a popular destination for spiritual seekers, and its architecture and natural surroundings provide a serene and peaceful atmosphere.

  • National Museum of Natural Science: The museum houses over 40 exhibit areas featuring a broad range of topics from ancient dinosaurs to space exploration. The museum is an educational experience for both adults and children.

  • Rainbow Village: This popular tourist destination is a street-art lover’s paradise. The unique and colorful murals in Rainbow Village depict a nostalgic and charming atmosphere of traditional life in Taiwan.

Natural Attractions of Taichung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the outdoors, Taichung has got you covered. From stunning forests to natural hot springs, there’s no shortage of outdoor attractions for adventurers to explore.

  • Dongshi Forest Garden: Known as the lungs of central Taiwan, the garden is a living showcase of Taiwanese forests. The garden features tree ferns, towering bamboo groves, and the ever-present greenery that makes you feel like you’re walking straight into a real-life fairyland.

  • Guguan Hot Springs: Guguan Hot Springs is a natural gem of Taichung, which attracts locals and tourists alike. The area is surrounded by beautiful mountains, and the hot springs are said to have therapeutic properties that can relieve the pains of the body and soul.

  • Asia University Luce Chapel: The chapel is a hidden gem that provides a peaceful and serene atmosphere for contemplating life’s deeper aspects. The chapel’s innovative and modern design will leave you in awe.

  • Sun Moon Lake: Arguably the most popular destination in Taiwan, Sun Moon Lake is a magnificent work of nature. The lake covering over 7.93 square kilometres, nestled in the mountains of Nantou, is home to local rare species of fish and wildlife. Here, you’ll be enchanted by the serene and tranquil setting that makes it a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taichung

Taichung’s beautiful natural settings make it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

  • Dakeng Scenic Area: The Dakeng Mountains are a series of foothills in Taichung, and the Dakeng Scenic Area is a popular, scenic retreat for those seeking nature and tranquility.

  • Hou-Feng Bikeway: The bikeway is a converted railway track surrounded by mountain landscapes, which leads to the nearby Hou-Feng Harbor and embraces stunning views of the sea.

  • Wanggaoliao Trail: A popular hiking trail in Taichung that offers some of the best views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Dongfeng Bicycle Greenway: A scenic bike path that takes cyclists through the heart of Taichung City.

Best Things to Do in Taichung: Activities and Tours

Taichung is full of exciting activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s interests.

  • Taichung Night Markets: Taichung is famous for its lively night markets such as Fengjia Night Market, which boasts over 200 stalls with local food, clothing, and souvenirs.

  • Aerial View of Taichung: Take flight on a hot-air balloon ride that offers a breathtaking bird’s eye view of Taichung’s natural beauty and cityscapes.

  • Taichung Bike Tours: Enjoy a bike tour of the city and explore some of its rich history, culture, and natural beauty sites.

  • Zhongxiao Night Market Hop-on-hop-off Food Tour: Join one of the most unique food tours in Taichung which allows you to hop off and taste the city’s most sumptuous street foods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taichung

Here is a suggested itinerary for 7 days in Taichung:

Food and Drink Scene of Taichung

Must-Try Dishes

Taichung is famous for its unique and diverse culinary treats, and there are several dishes that every visitor should sample.

  • Bubble Tea: Bubble Tea originated in Taiwan, and Taichung is no exception to the trend. Be sure to try its famous bubble tea during your stay.

  • Sun Cake: Sun Cakes are a traditional Taiwanese treat made of pastry that is filled with malt sugar and are a perfect source of energy on a busy day.

  • Braised Pork Rice: Locally known as “Lu Rou Fan,” this traditional Taiwanese dish features a combination of rice, braised pork, and gravy.

  • Stinky Tofu: A smelly but delicious dish that includes brined tofu deep-fried to perfection and served with spicy sauce and pickled vegetables.

Local Beverages

Taichung also offers several local beverages worth trying.

  • Taiwan Beer: A popular beer brand in Taiwan, made with the highest quality ingredients and considered the national drink of Taiwan.

  • Bubble Tea: As mentioned earlier, Bubble Tea is Taiwan’s most famous drink that has taken over the world with a unique combination of tea, ice, milk, and tapioca pearls that burst in your mouth.

  • Oolong Tea: Oolong tea is a locally grown favorite drink of Taichung that features the perfect blend of sweet and floral flavors.

  • Shaoxing Rice Wine: Locally known as “Mijiu,” Shaoxing Rice Wine is made from glutinous rice and is a popular drink at gatherings and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Taichung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taichung offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste, from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses.

  • Millennium Vee Hotel Taichung: A luxurious hotel in central Taichung City with modern amenities.

  • The Pace Stay: An affordable and chic hostel located in Taichung Central City.

  • Old School Guruguru Hostel: A creative and laid-back lodging option that is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

  • 53 Hotel: A stylish and modern boutique hotel located in the heart of Taichung’s shopping district.

What to Buy in Taichung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taichung is famous for its locally produced products and souvenirs, such as:

  • Sun Cake: A traditional Taiwanese pastry that is perfect for gifting to friends and family back home.

  • Oolong Tea: Locally grown and flavoured, buying Oolong Tea is the perfect way to bring a little piece of Taichung back home.

  • Brooches: Taichung is famous for delicate and intricately designed brooches, made of beads, threads and feathers, a perfect accessory for your collection.

  • Home Furnishings: Taichung is also famous for its unique and innovative home furnishing shops, selling distinctive pieces of furniture and decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Taichung

Taiwanese culture, particularly in Taichung, is a mix of traditional and modern beliefs and practices. Here are a few customs and etiquette points to remember:

  • Remove your shoes when entering a Taiwanese home or temple.

  • Tipping is not customary in Taiwan.

  • In temples, it’s customary to bow twice, with your hands in front of your chest.

  • Refrain from pointing with your finger; instead, use your chin, lips, or hand.

Best Time to Visit Taichung

The best time to visit Taichung is from November to February, where the weather is relatively cool and suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taichung

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and visit the Night Markets.

  • Learn basic Mandarin words.

  • Try to use chopsticks as often as possible.

  • Respect local customs and be mindful of local dress codes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be loud and disrespectful in public places.

  • Avoid smoking in no-smoking-zones.

  • Don’t touch the top of someone’s head, as this is considered rude in Taiwan.

  • Avoid discussing politics or religion.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taichung

Taichung is a safe city, and tourists face no major threats. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind during your stay:

  • Keep valuable possessions safe and avoid carrying around large sums of cash.

  • Be cautious when walking around at night and avoid secluded areas.

  • Be aware of traffic and follow traffic rules when biking or walking.

Health in Taichung

Taichung is a relatively clean and hygienic city. However, it’s essential to keep a few things in mind for maintaining good health:

  • Drink bottled or filtered water, especially in more rural areas.

  • Remember to use sunscreen, even on cloudy days.

  • Wear mosquito repellent during mosquito season, typically from May to October.

Getting to Taichung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taichung

Taichung International Airport is located in the city’s Taichung District, with direct international flights and several domestic flights. It’s easy to get to from anywhere in the city.

Bus Stations Near Taichung

Taichung Bus Station is located in Taichung Central City and offers bus services to several popular destinations in Taiwan.

Other Options

Taiwan’s train system is an efficient and affordable way to get around, with trains connecting Taichung to most major cities in Taiwan. Car rentals and taxis are also available in the city.

How to Get Around in Taichung

The most convenient way to get around Taichung is by renting a bicycle or scooter. Taichung City also has an extensive bus system and a BRT system that offers a low-cost and eco-friendly option for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Taichung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that are worth visiting:

  • Chiayi: Chiayi is a charming city located in western Taiwan, with cultural landmarks and scenic beauty such as the famous Alishan Range and a unique night market.

  • Nantou: Nantou is home to Sun Moon Lake, one of the most beautiful lakes in Taiwan, and is also known for its tea plantations and natural hot springs.

  • Tainan: Tainan is the oldest city in Taiwan, with a rich cultural and historical legacy, including temples, landmarks, and historical sites.

Conclusion

Taichung is a city that has something to offer for everyone. From its natural beauty to its unique culture and culinary treats, there’s no shortage of unforgettable experiences in Taichung. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your visit and make the most of your time in this fascinating city.
