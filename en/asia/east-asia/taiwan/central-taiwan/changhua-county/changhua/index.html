Introduction to Changhua

Located in Central Taiwan, Changhua is a charming county known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and scenic natural beauty. From ancient temples to contemporary museums, from outdoor adventures to delicious food, Changhua has something to offer every type of traveler.

In this guide, we will take you through the must-see attractions of Changhua, including culturally significant landmarks and natural wonders. From hiking and biking routes to activities and tours, from accommodation choices to local customs and etiquette, we’ll cover all the essentials to help you plan the ultimate trip to Changhua.

List of Places to Visit in Changhua

Here are some of the top places to add to your Changhua itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taiwan Glass Gallery: A fascinating museum showcasing Taiwan’s rich glass-making culture.
  • Lukang Old Street: A well-preserved street from the Qing Dynasty, featuring traditional architecture and local products.
  • Baguashan Buddha: A 22-meter tall Buddha statue overlooking the city, known for its stunning views of the surrounding areas.
  • Changhua Confucius Temple: One of the oldest Confucius temples in Taiwan, known for its beautiful architecture and serene atmosphere.
  • Yuanlin Temple: A stunning temple complex with ornate decorations, intricate wood carvings, and beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Changhua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baguashan Trail: A popular hiking trail with breathtaking views of Changhua and neighboring counties.
  • Gaomei Wetlands: A vast expanse of natural wetlands home to flocks of migratory birds and stunning sunsets.
  • Lugang Longshan Temple: A beautiful temple located on a picturesque hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Taiwan Black Kite Conservation Area: A protected area where you can spot the majestic black kites in their natural habitat.
  • Tianwei Highway Garden: A scenic garden featuring colorful flowers, beautiful landscapes, and interesting sculptures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changhua

Changhua offers several hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Baguashan Trail: A challenging hiking trail with stunning views of the city and surrounding areas.
  • Huatan Bicycle Trail: A 23-kilometer cycling route taking you through beautiful countryside and quaint towns.
  • Tianwei Highway Garden Bicycle Trail: A scenic 12-kilometer route taking you through beautiful gardens, parks, and green spaces.

Best Things to Do in Changhua: Activities and Tours

  • Try local snacks at the Changhua Night Market.
  • Take a cycling tour of the city’s cultural landmarks.
  • Visit the Taiwan Glass Gallery and learn about the art of glass-making.
  • Explore the beautiful gardens of the Tianwei Highway Garden.
  • Attend a traditional Chinese opera performance at the Yuanlin Temple.

Week-Long Itinerary for Changhua

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Changhua, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Changhua, check-in to your hotel, explore the Changhua Night Market
2 Visit the Taiwan Glass Gallery, Lukang Old Street, and Baguashan Buddha
3 Go hiking on the Baguashan Trail, visit the Confucius Temple, and Yuanlin Temple
4 Take a cycling tour of the city’s cultural landmarks, visit the Black Kite Conservation Area
5 Spend the day at the Gaomei Wetlands, watch the sunset at the Gaomei Lighthouse
6 Visit the Tianwei Highway Garden, take a scenic drive around Changhua
7 Depart from Changhua and travel to your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Changhua

Changhua is famous for its street food and local snacks. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ba-Wan: a steamed dumpling filled with pork, bamboo shoots, and mushrooms
  • Dan-Shui Flat Noodles: a local specialty made with a flavorful broth and chewy noodles
  • Oyster Omelet: a savory omelet made with fresh oysters, vegetables, and eggs
  • Ah-Poh Meatballs: a popular meatball dish made with pork, bamboo shoots, and Chinese herbs
  • Shaved Ice: a refreshing dessert made with shaved ice and a variety of toppings, such as fruit, jelly, and syrup

Local Beverages

Changhua is also known for its refreshing beverages. You can try:

  • Sugar Cane Juice: a refreshing drink made with freshly squeezed sugar cane juice
  • Eight Immortal Tea: a herbal tea made with eight different Chinese herbs
  • Pearl Milk Tea: a Taiwanese specialty made with black tea, milk, and tapioca pearls

Where to Stay in Changhua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Changhua has a variety of accommodation choices to suit every budget and preference. Some of the popular options include:

  • Alishan House: a luxurious hotel located on a picturesque mountain offering serene views and excellent facilities.
  • Ying Zhen Hotel: a modern hotel located in the heart of Changhua, offering comfortable rooms and great service.
  • Eda Happiness Guesthouse: a charming guesthouse offering cozy rooms and a homely atmosphere.
  • C.S. Palace Hotel: a budget-friendly hotel located near the Changhua Train Station, offering basic amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • 168 Motel: a unique and quirky motel located near Baguashan Buddha, offering themed rooms and fun decor.

What to Buy in Changhua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some souvenirs and local products you can consider buying in Changhua include:

  • Changhua Mochi: a popular local snack made with glutinous rice and sweet fillings such as peanut, sesame, or red bean.
  • Tung Blossom Honey: a special type of honey made from the nectar of Tung trees, found only in Changhua.
  • Changhua Glassware: intricately crafted glassware with unique designs and colors, found at various glass galleries and showrooms.
  • Lukuang Style Tea: a special type of tea made with local tea leaves and a unique fermentation process, found in various tea shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Changhua

Changhua culture is heavily influenced by Chinese culture, with many customs and traditions reflecting this influence. Some important things to keep in mind while traveling to Changhua include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Do not touch or point at someone with your feet as it is considered rude.
  • Do not leave chopsticks sticking up in your food as it is considered bad luck.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Changhua

The best time to visit Changhua is during the fall season (September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the scenery is stunning with the colorful leaves. Spring (March to May) is also a good time to visit, with cherry blossoms in full bloom, though it can be a bit crowded during the peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changhua

Some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Changhua include:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for outdoor activities.
  • Carry a map or a portable Wi-Fi device.
  • Keep valuable belongings safe.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect religious sites and artifacts.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Touch or disturb wildlife in natural reserves and parks.
  • Carry large amounts of cash.

Safety and Health

Changhua is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and good healthcare facilities. Here are some important health and safety tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Changhua

  • Keep your valuable belongings safe and secure.
  • Take care when walking or cycling on unfamiliar roads.
  • Be aware of traffic when crossing roads, especially in busy areas.
  • Follow local customs and etiquette.

Health in Changhua

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Consult a doctor before traveling if you have any health concerns.
  • Practice good hygiene.

Getting to Changhua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Changhua is well connected by various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Changhua

Taichung International Airport is the closest airport to Changhua, located approximately 30 minutes away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Changhua

Changhua Bus Station and Changhua High-Speed Rail Station are the two main bus stations in the county, offering various routes to nearby cities and attractions.

Other Options

You can also travel to Changhua via train or taxi. The Changhua Train Station is located in the city center, offering convenient access to the county’s main attractions.

How to Get Around in Changhua

Changhua is easily navigable by public transportation, and various modes of transportation are available. Here are some options:

  • Bus: The Changhua Bus Station is the main bus station in Changhua, operating various routes to nearby cities and attractions.
  • Train: The Changhua Train Station is located in the city center, offering convenient access to the county’s main attractions.
  • Taxi: Taxis are also available in Changhua, and can be easily hailed on the street or booked through ride-hailing apps such as Uber.

Where to Go Next from Changhua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Changhua is surrounded by several popular destinations and excursions that you can consider visiting after your trip. Some nearby locations include:

  • Taichung: a bustling city known for its night markets, street art, and museums.
  • Sun Moon Lake: a scenic lake surrounded by mountains and forests, offering boating and hiking opportunities.
  • Alishan: a picturesque mountain area famous for its tea plantations, hiking trails, and views of the sunrise.
  • Nantou: a county known for its natural beauty, including rolling hills, gorges, and forests.

Conclusion

Changhua may be a small county, but it is full of hidden gems and surprises for travelers. From cultural landmarks to natural wonders, the county has something to offer for every type of adventurer. With this guide in hand, you can plan an unforgettable trip to Changhua and experience the best of Taiwanese culture and hospitality.
