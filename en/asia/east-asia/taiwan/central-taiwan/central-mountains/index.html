Introduction to Central Mountains

Located in the heart of Taiwan, the Central Mountains offer a stunning natural landscape and rich cultural attractions. Sandwiched between the Western Taiwan mountain range and the Coastal mountain range, the Central Mountains consist of rugged terrain, including towering peaks, deep valleys, and winding rivers. The region is home to several aboriginal tribes, each with their unique traditions, beliefs, and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Central Mountains

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Central Mountains offer a range of culturally significant attractions. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places include:

  • National Museum of Natural Science in Taichung
  • National Taiwan Museum of Fine Arts in Taichung
  • Wufeng Lin Family Mansion and Garden in Taichung
  • Dasyueshan Forest Recreation Area in Nantou
  • Puli Brewery Factory in Nantou

Natural Attractions of Central Mountains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Central Mountains are also home to numerous natural attractions, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best natural sights in Central Mountains include:

  • Alishan National Scenic Area
  • Sun Moon Lake in Nantou
  • Hehuanshan National Forest Recreation Area
  • Cingjing Farm in Nantou
  • Taroko National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Mountains

For avid hikers and bikers, the Central Mountains are a paradise. The region offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Taiwan. Some of the best routes include:

  • Xitou Nature Education Area in Nantou
  • Dakeng Hiking Trail in Taichung
  • Hehuanshan East Peak Trail in Nantou
  • Yushan National Park in Ren’ai
  • Snow Mountain Trail in Taichung

Best Things to Do in Central Mountains: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, the Central Mountains offer a range of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in the Central Mountains include:

  • Rafting in the Jiji River
  • Ziplining in Sun Moon Lake
  • Visiting lavender fields in Nantou
  • Taking a night safari at Dongshi Forest Recreation Area
  • Stargazing in Cingjing Farm

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Mountains

If you have a week to spare in Central Mountains, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Central Mountains

Must-Try Dishes

The Central Mountains are famous for their unique cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Sun cakes (Taiwanese pastry)
  • Mochi
  • Shanlinxi Black Tea
  • The Hakka dish “lei cha,” or pounded tea, which is made of ground tea leaves, herbs, peanuts, and seeds that are pounded with a pestle in a large bowl, then served as a soup with rice

Local Beverages

Aside from tea, the Central Mountains also offer a range of local beverages. Some of the best to try include:

  • Honey wine
  • Fruit wines (such as plum or peach)
  • Beer from the Puli Brewery Factory

The Central Mountains boast a wide range of accommodation options suitable for every budget. Some of the best places to stay in Central Mountains include:

  • Fushin Hotel in Taichung
  • LeaLea Garden Hotels - Sun Moon Lake in Nantou
  • Cingjing Guest House in Nantou
  • Chingjing Maple Leaf Villa in Nantou
  • Henan Homestay in Jiufen Village

What to Buy in Central Mountains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from the Central Mountains include:

  • Tea leaves and tea sets
  • Hakka mochi
  • Indigenous handicrafts and textiles
  • Specialty fruits (such as persimmons and plums)
  • Handmade soaps and beauty products

Local Culture and Customs of Central Mountains

The Central Mountains are home to several indigenous tribes, each with their unique customs, traditions, and beliefs. Visitors to the region should be respectful of the local culture and traditions. Some of the customs to be aware of include:

  • Removing shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Tipping is not required or expected
  • Asking permission before taking photos of people or their homes
  • No smoking or littering in national parks and other public areas

Best Time to Visit Central Mountains

The best time to visit the Central Mountains is during autumn (September to November) when the colors of the fall foliage are at their peak. The weather is also milder during this season, making it ideal for hiking and outdoor activities. Spring (March to May) is also an excellent time to visit, as the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Mountains

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take breaks often while hiking to avoid altitude sickness
  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking
  • Keep an open mind and try new foods and experiences
  • Learn some basic Chinese phrases before arriving

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas or national parks
  • Take photos of people and their homes without permission
  • Trespass on private property
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples and other religious sites
  • Rely on credit cards in rural areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Mountains

While the Central Mountains are relatively safe, visitors should take appropriate safety precautions when hiking and participating in other outdoor activities. Some safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Inform others of your hiking plans and estimated time of return
  • Bring adequate water and snacks on hikes
  • Wear sturdy shoes and appropriate clothing for the weather conditions
  • Be cautious when encountering wildlife, such as snakes and bears
  • Always follow the instructions of your tour guide or local authorities

Health in Central Mountains

Visitors to the Central Mountains should take appropriate health precautions, such as staying hydrated and wearing sunscreen. Altitude sickness can also be a concern for some visitors, so it is recommended to acclimate to the higher elevations gradually. Visitors should also be aware of the potential risks of consuming untreated water or contaminated food.

Getting to Central Mountains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central Mountains

The nearest airport to the Central Mountains is Taichung International Airport (RMQ), which is approximately 30 kilometers from the city center. Other nearby airports include Taipei Taoyuan International Airport (TPE) and Kaohsiung International Airport (KHH).

Bus Stations Near Central Mountains

The primary bus stations serving the Central Mountains are the Taipei Bus Station and Taichung Bus Station. From there, visitors can take buses to various destinations in the Central Mountains.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Taichung Railway Station and then transfer to a bus or taxi to their final destination in the Central Mountains. Rental cars and scooters are also available for those who prefer to drive themselves.

How to Get Around in Central Mountains

The best way to get around the Central Mountains is by car or scooter. Alternatively, visitors can take public transportation, such as buses or taxis. However, bus schedules may be limited, and taxis can be expensive for long distances.

Where to Go Next from Central Mountains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the Central Mountains, visitors may want to consider visiting nearby destinations, such as:

  • Taipei, Taiwan’s capital city
  • Tainan, a historic city in southern Taiwan
  • Kenting National Park, located on the southern tip of Taiwan
  • Hualien, home to Taroko National Park

Conclusion

With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural attractions, and diverse range of activities and tours, the Central Mountains offer something for every type of traveler. Whether you come for the hiking or the local cuisine, this region is sure to leave a lasting impression.
