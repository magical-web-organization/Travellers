Discovering Puli: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Nestled in the heart of Taiwan’s Nantou County, Puli offers visitors a remarkable combination of cultural significance and natural beauty. This charming town is where east meets west, with a rich indigenous culture and historical connections to the Japanese colonial era. Surrounded by green hills and stunning vistas, Puli is a popular destination for outdoor adventurers, hikers, bikers, and nature lovers.

Whether you’re visiting Puli for the first time or are a repeat visitor, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip, featuring top places to visit, hikes and bike routes, best things to do, food scene, where to stay, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Puli

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As you plan your visit to Puli, make sure to include these culturally significant attractions on your itinerary:

  1. National Taiwan Craft Research and Development Institute - This is a museum that showcases traditional crafts from all over Taiwan. Here, you can see artisanal textiles, wood and bamboo crafts, pottery, and more.

  2. Puli Formosan Aboriginal Culture Village - This vast theme park offers visitors an immersive experience into the local Atayal culture. Explore the Atayal village, participate in cultural activities, and watch traditional performances.

  3. New Tile House Hakka Cultural District - This cultural heritage center features restored traditional Hakka houses that take visitors back in time to the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Puli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Puli boasts an array of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor adventurers and nature lovers:

  1. Sun Moon Lake - The most famous attraction in the area, this stunning alpine lake is surrounded by towering peaks and lush forests. Visitors can take a boat ride, hike the trails around the lake, or explore the temples and shrines that dot the shoreline.

  2. Xiangshan Viewing Platform - For a panoramic view of Puli and its surrounding areas, head to this picturesque viewing platform. Accessed by a short hike, the platform offers uninterrupted views of the town and the surrounding areas.

  3. Rainbow Waterfall - Tucked away in a scenic gorge, this 80-meter-high waterfall offers visitors an unforgettable experience. Visitors can hike to the top of the waterfall or get up-close to the cascading water from a viewing platform below.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Puli

Puli is also the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are three of the best trails for hiking and biking:

  1. Lugu Mountain Trail - This hiking trail takes you to the summit of Lugu Mountain for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountain ranges and valleys. The trail is a 12 km round trip and takes approximately 6 hours to complete.

  2. Green Way Hiking and Biking Trail - This 18-kilometer-long trail follows the old Taiwan Railways Administration narrow-gauge railway, which was closed down in 1998. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is suitable for both hikers and bikers.

  3. Sun Moon Lake Bicycle Path - Starting from the visitor center, this 29 km long path follows the circumference of Sun Moon Lake, offering stunning views of the lake, the surrounding mountains, and the lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Puli: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Puli offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are a few popular options:

  1. Tea Tasting Experience - The fertile soil and favorable climate in Puli make it an ideal location for tea cultivation. Visitors can tour local tea plantations and learn about the different varieties of tea cultivated in the region.

  2. Night Market Experience - The Puli Night Market is a bustling open-air market that offers visitors a chance to browse and shop for local goods and try tasty food.

  3. Sightseeing Cruise on Sun Moon Lake - Visitors can take a leisurely cruise around Sun Moon Lake, enjoying the scenery and the cultural sights along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puli

If you’re planning on staying in Puli for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town of Puli and visit the National Taiwan Craft Research and Development Institute.

Day 2: Visit the Puli Formosan Aboriginal Culture Village and experience the local Atayal culture.

Day 3: Hike the Lugu Mountain Trail and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Day 4: Visit the Xiangshan Viewing Platform and enjoy panoramic views of the town and the surrounding areas.

Day 5: Take a sightseeing cruise around Sun Moon Lake and explore the temples and shrines that dot the shoreline.

Day 6: Enjoy tea tasting and learn about the different varieties of tea cultivated in the region.

Day 7: Visit the Green Way Hiking and Biking Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene in Puli

Puli is known for its rich culinary scene, which features a mix of Taiwanese, Hakka, and indigenous cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  1. Mao Xue Wang (Sliced Pork with Blood Soup) - This Hakka dish features sliced pork cooked in a hot and spicy soup made with pork blood.

  2. Bamboo Rice - This is a traditional dish made with sticky rice, pork, mushrooms, and bamboo shoots that is cooked inside a bamboo tube.

  3. Atayal Smoked Pheasant - A traditional dish of the Atayal people, this smoked pheasant is cooked with seasonal vegetables and herbs.

Local Beverages:

  1. Sun Moon Lake Black Tea - Grown in the region around Sun Moon Lake, this tea is known for its fruity aroma and rich flavor.

  2. Plum Wine - Made from local plums, this sweet and sour wine is a popular drink among locals.

  3. Ginger Tea - Made with fresh ginger, this fragrant tea is perfect for chilly evenings.

Where to Stay in Puli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puli has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  1. Famous Hotel Puli - This 4-star hotel features modern amenities, spacious rooms, and a rooftop swimming pool with stunning views of the town and the surrounding peaks.

  2. Pulicheng Villa - A traditional style guesthouse that offers visitors a chance to experience local culture and hospitality.

  3. Bamboo Culture Park - Located near Sun Moon Lake, this resort offers luxurious rooms, modern amenities, and easy access to the lake.

What to Buy in Puli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Puli hosts a variety of local products that make great gifts. Here are some things to buy:

  1. Tea - The Sun Moon Lake area is famous for its tea, and visitors can purchase a variety of tea blends and tea sets.

  2. Hakka Cookies - The famous local cookies are made with almond flour, butter, and cranberries. These cookies are famous throughout Taiwan.

  3. Cypress Woodcarving - This traditional handicraft features intricate carvings made from cypress wood, a popular regional product.

Local Culture and Customs of Puli

Puli’s rich cultural heritage is heavily influenced by its indigenous population, Japanese colonial history, and Hakka culture. As a visitor, it’s important to respect the local customs and culture. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  1. Puli has a significant indigenous community, and visitors should take time to learn about the Atayal culture and traditions.

  2. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, such as removing their shoes before entering traditional homes or buildings.

  3. Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines or attending cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Puli

The best time to visit Puli is during the spring and fall, when temperatures are mild, and the scenery is beautiful. The region has a subtropical climate, with a rainy season from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puli

Do:

  1. Take time to appreciate the beautiful scenery and natural attractions.

  2. Try the local cuisines and beverages.

  3. Learn about the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Littering or trespassing on private property.

  2. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  3. Harm or disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puli

Puli is generally a safe place for visitors, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Puli

Puli is a relatively safe area when it comes to health risks. However, visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Japanese encephalitis.

Getting to Puli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puli

The nearest airport to Puli is Taichung International Airport, which is located approximately 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Puli

Puli Bus Station is located in downtown Puli and offers connections to major cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Puli by train or car.

How to Get Around in Puli

The best way to get around Puli is by renting a scooter, bicycle or a car. Taxis are also available but may be more expensive than other options.

Where to Go Next from Puli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Puli, visitors can easily explore the surrounding area, including:

  1. Yushan National Park - Taiwan’s tallest mountain, the park is home to stunning alpine scenery, hiking trails, and hot springs.

  2. Aboriginal Culture Villages - Explore Taiwan’s indigenous cultures through visits to the numerous aboriginal culture villages located in the region.

  3. Taiwan’s East Coast - Known for its rugged coastline, dramatic cliffs, and stunning scenery, Taiwan’s East Coast offers a unique contrast to Puli’s alpine scenery.

Conclusion

Puli is a charming destination that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventure. From scenic vistas to cultural attractions, hiking and biking trails, and delicious local cuisine, Puli is a must-visit destination on any trip to Taiwan. With this comprehensive travel guide as your companion, you’re sure to create unforgettable memories in Puli.
