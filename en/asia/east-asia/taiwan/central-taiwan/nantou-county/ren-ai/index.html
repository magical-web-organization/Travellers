Introduction to Ren’ai

Nestled in the Central Mountain Range of Taiwan, Ren’ai Township in Nantou County is a hidden gem for outdoor enthusiasts and cultural aficionados alike. Boasting stunning natural landscapes and historical sites, Ren’ai offers visitors an unforgettable experience. This comprehensive guide will help you navigate top attractions, hiking/biking routes, food, accommodations, local customs, safety, and transportation options in Ren’ai, Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Ren’ai

Ren’ai offers various cultural and natural attractions that are sure to cater to different interests. Here are some of the town’s top spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Mona Rudao Memorial Monument

Named after the indigenous hero Mona Rudao, this monument commemorates his sacrifice and spirit of resistance against the Japanese army during the Wushe Incident in 1930. The exhibition hall features historical displays, cultural art, and ancient documents.

2. Cingjian Skywalk

The Cingjian Skywalk provides visitors an opportunity to see the beauty of the mountains from a bird’s eye view. The bridge is 1,998 meters above sea level where you can see an unobstructed 360-degree view of the surrounding mountains.

3. Qingjing Veterans Farm

This quaint attraction farm doubles as an agricultural educational center and a recreational park. Visitors can enjoy pretty scenery, go camping, hiking, and sightseeing among the farm’s multiple flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Ren’ai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Hehuan Mountain

For seasoned hikers Hehuan Mountain, which spans over 3,000 meters, is a must-visit. Located at the junction of the Central Mountain and Xueshan Mountain ranges, you can witness the stunning sunrise and sunset views here.

2. Green Green Grasslands

The most famous attraction in Ren’ai is the Green Green Grasslands, a vast meadow that used to be grazing land. Visitors can relish the natural beauty of sheep, horses, and cattle grazing around the area’s extensive landscape, while enjoying outdoor recreational activities such as carting, pony riding, and other attractions.

3. Maple Forests

The Maple Forests are a visual delight located in Ren’ai’s Hehuan Mountain area. These trees’ leaves are vibrant red every autumn, adding a beautiful element to the lush scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ren’ai

Ren’ai is known for its both easy and rigorous hiking and biking routes, often featuring beautiful scenic views. Here are some of the most popular:

1. Camellia Garden Trail

The Camellia Garden Trail is a beautiful hiking and biking route where visitors can enjoy stunning views of Camellia Hill and the surrounding Central Mountain Range.

2. Lushan Trail

The Lushan Trail is an easy, stroller-friendly hiking trail that takes you through a breathtaking ancient urban and rural landscape, allowing you to take in Taiwan’s beauty at your own pace.

3. Wuling Sixiu

For an ultramodern hike, start with the Wuling Sixiu Trail. The trail takes you past glaciers, sharp rocky mountain peaks, and island formations that will make you feel like you’re walking through a Hollywood movie scene.

Best Things to Do in Ren’ai: Activities and Tours

No trip to Ren’ai is complete without experiencing some of the town’s popular activities and tours. Below are some options to consider:

1. Alpaca Farm

Ren’ai’s Alpaca Farm is not your ordinary petting zoo – it’s home to more than 100 alpacas from different countries. Visitors can interact with and feed these furry animals up-close.

2. Stargazing

The open sky and clear air make Ren’ai an ideal place for stargazing. You can join a stargazing tour, where professionals will teach you how to use the correct equipment and guide you through the night sky.

3. Firefly Season

Every year, during late April and May, firefly season comes to Ren’ai. The fireflies perform a magical dance in the mountain air, making for a bright and colorful display.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ren’ai

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Ren’ai:

1 Arrive and rest

7 Depart from Ren’ai

Food and Drink Scene of Ren’ai

Ren’ai offers various local dishes and beverages that are popular amongst locals and tourists. Below are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Grilled lamb

Ren’ai’s Grilled Lamb is one of the most famous dishes in the region. The secret recipe and cooking process have been passed down for generations, and it’s a definite must-try.

2. Firms Tofu

Named after its texture, Ren’ai’s famed Firm Tofu is a delicious, protein-packed food that comes with a distinct flavor.

3. Guanguang Tea

Ren’ai provides a premium line of tea, the Guanguang Tea, which is known for its mild, light flavor, and health benefits.

Local Beverages

1. Snow and Ice

The Snow and Ice of Ren’ai is a local drink that comprises fresh fruit juice and ice. The name comes from the cooling property of the drink, which makes it refreshing in Ren’ai’s hot climate throughout summer.

2. Mijiu

Mijiu is a sweet-sharp alcoholic drink made from rice that’s famous in Ren’ai. Many restaurants in the region provide this wine, and it couples well with the regionally famous grilled lamb.

Where to Stay in Ren’ai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ren’ai offers a range of accommodations to suit travelers’ different preferences and budgets. Here are some recommended options:

1. Ming Ging Farm

The Ming Ging Farm offers guests a memorable experience, with a range of accommodations that range from small cottages to luxurious suites. It’s a great place to relax, with the added advantage of being near stunning natural scenery.

2. Yakto Lodge

If you’re looking for a cozy, rustic experience in Ren’ai, consider staying at Yakto Lodge. The refined architecture, modern amenities, and friendly staff make this lodge an excellent place to unwind while enjoying the beautiful environment.

3. Sunkist Villa

For a four-star experience, consider staying at the Sunkist Villa. The hotel has elegantly furnished rooms, a beautiful landscape, an outstanding restaurant.

What to Buy in Ren’ai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ren’ai has many traditional handicrafts and local merchandise that visitors can purchase and bring back home as a souvenir. Here are some options:

1. Aboriginal Products

Ren’ai is home to various indigenous tribes whose cultural heritage is represented in the handicrafts created in the region. Look for locally made pottery, weaving, and carvings made out of bamboo, rope, and other materials.

2. Jiufen Cake

Jiufen Cake is the Ren’ai version of traditional mochi cake, made of granulated sugar, sesame, and peanuts. This dessert is a pleasant souvenir for friends or family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ren’ai

Ren’ai’s culture and customs are heavily influenced by the region’s indigenous inhabitants’ heritage. Visitors must be mindful of local etiquette and customs, including respecting the locals and their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ren’ai

The best time to visit Ren’ai is from April to mid-June when the weather is comfortable and you can witness the firefly season. If you’re a winter sports enthusiast, visit between December and February, when the town has the highest chance of snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ren’ai

Do:

  • Be mindful of local etiquette and customs.
  • Respect the locals and their traditions.
  • Try out local delicacies and handicrafts.
  • Be respectful of natural areas and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any property.
  • Smoke in prohibited areas.
  • Use flash photography during star-gazing tours.
  • Disturb the fireflies during firefly season.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ren’ai

Ren’ai is generally safe, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take care when hiking or biking along rugged terrain and should always follow safety protocols.

Health in Ren’ai

In Ren’ai, healthcare facilities and clinics are complete with medical professionals who can provide medical services in the event of an illness or injury.

Getting to Ren’ai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ren’ai

The closest airport to Ren’ai is Taichung International Airport, which provides direct and connecting flights from different countries and cities.

Bus Stations Near Ren’ai

Frequent bus services connect Ren’ai with neighboring towns and cities, including Taipei, Taichung, and other locations across the island.

Other Options

Rental cars, scooters, and bikes are other transportation options to consider. They provide you with more flexibility and convenience when exploring the town and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Ren’ai

Public transport, including buses and taxis, is efficient and reliable for getting around town. Alternatively, renting a bike or scooter is an adventure of its kind, and a fantastic way to see the sights at closer proximity and off the beaten path.

Where to Go Next from Ren’ai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Antique shop hopping in Jiufen is a great way to end your Ren’ai adventure. Other interesting destinations within reach include the scenic Sun Moon Lake and the mountain town of Puli.

Conclusion

Ren’ai, Taiwan is a town filled with natural and cultural beauty that is waiting to be explored. With its stunning landscapes, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Ren’ai guarantees a memorable visit. When you visit, be sure to prepare well, and follow our guide to have an unforgettable experience.
