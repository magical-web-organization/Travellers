Introduction to Jhushan

Located in the heart of Nantou County in Central Taiwan, Jhushan is a small township that has plenty to offer for tourists looking for an authentic Taiwanese experience. Jhushan is known for its rich cultural heritage, picturesque landscapes, and outdoor adventures that attract tourists worldwide.

List of Places to Visit in Jhushan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jhushan Old Street: This 100-year-old street is a must-visit for history and culture enthusiasts. Explore the traditional architecture and local shops selling souvenirs and Taiwanese snacks.

  2. Jhushan Tianshi Temple: Built during the Qing dynasty, this temple is famous for its intricate wood carvings, architecture, and religious significance.

  3. Zhushan Liukuei Natural Reserve: A designated national scenic area, this nature reserve boasts breathtaking views of Mount Dabajian and Danda Forest.

Natural Attractions of Jhushan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Dabajian: This is the second-highest peak in Taiwan and is popular among hikers and nature lovers. The trail to the peak is challenging, but the stunning views are worth the effort.

  2. Xitou Nature Education Area: A beautiful nature reserve with towering trees, lush greenery, and serene streams. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll or go on a guided tour.

  3. Sun Moon Lake: One of the most famous tourist destinations in Taiwan, the lake’s surrounding landscape is awe-inspiring. Visitors can take a boat tour, walk around the lake, or cycle on a rental bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jhushan

  1. Dabajianshan Trail: A challenging eight-hour hike that rewards trekkers with stunning views of the valley, forests, and mountains.

  2. Xitou to Lugu Trail: A six-hour trek that takes visitors through a dense forest, streams, and waterfalls.

  3. Sun Moon Lake Cycling Trail: A 30km cycling trail around the picturesque lake. It takes around three hours to complete and is perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers.

Best Things to Do in Jhushan: Activities and Tours

  1. Tea Tasting: Jhushan is home to several tea plantations and factories, where visitors can learn about the tea-making process, sample different teas, and enjoy the scenic views.

  2. Hot Spring Bathing: Nantou County is famous for its hot springs, and there are several resorts and spas in Jhushan, where visitors can relax and rejuvenate in the mineral-rich waters.

  3. Camping: There are several campsites in Jhushan that offer camping facilities and outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and swimming.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jhushan

Day 1: Arrive in Jhushan and explore the old street and Tianshi Temple

Day 2: Hike Mount Dabajian and enjoy the stunning views

Day 3: Visit Sun Moon Lake and go on a boat tour

Day 4: Go on a cycling tour around the lake and taste local delicacies

Day 5: Explore Xitou Nature Reserve and go on a guided tour

Day 6: Discover the Liukuei Natural Reserve and go camping

Day 7: Relax in a hot spring resort and enjoy local tea

Food and Drink Scene of Jhushan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bamboo Rice: Rice cooked in a bamboo tube with mushrooms, vegetables, and chicken or pork.

  2. Fragrant Rice Noodle Soup: A hearty soup made with rice noodles, marinated beef or pork, seasonal vegetables, and herbs.

  3. Hakka Rice Cake: A sweet glutinous rice cake made with sesame, sugar, peanuts, and rice flour.

Local Beverages

  1. Tea: Jhushan is known for its tea production, and visitors can enjoy a variety of teas like black tea, Oolong tea, and Jasmine tea.

  2. Fruit Wine: Made with locally grown fruits like plums, persimmons, and mulberries, these wines are sweet and refreshing.

  3. Taiwanese Beer: Several local breweries in Taiwan offer craft beer and unique flavors like passion fruit, lychee, and grapefruit.

Where to Stay in Jhushan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Xitou Bamboo Grove Resort: A luxury resort with bamboo-themed rooms, hot spring facilities, and spa services.

  2. Jhushan Guest House: A traditional guesthouse located in the old street offering comfortable accommodation and local breakfast.

  3. Jhushan 183 Hostel: A trendy hostel with private rooms and dorms, common areas, and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Jhushan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tea: Choose from a variety of teas like black tea, Oolong tea, and Jasmine tea. Make sure to taste before buying.

  2. Handicrafts: Jhushan is known for its woodcarving, pottery, and bamboo products.

  3. Fruits: Jhushan is famous for its fruits like persimmons, peaches, and plums. Buy the seasonal fruits from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Jhushan

  1. Respect the elders and authorities
  2. Be mindful of the local customs and traditions
  3. Remove shoes before entering a temple or shrine

Best Time to Visit Jhushan

The best time to visit Jhushan is between March and May and from September to December. This is when the weather is mild, and tourists can enjoy the natural beauty of the region without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jhushan

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions.

  2. Try local food and drinks.

  3. Take photos of the stunning landscapes and landmarks.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural surroundings

  2. Disrespect any religious or cultural monuments.

  3. Speak loudly or cause disturbances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jhushan

Jhushan is a safe destination with low crime rates. Visitors should take common precautions like not leaving valuables unattended and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Jhushan

Visitors should take necessary precautions like drinking bottled water, wearing sunscreen and mosquito repellent, and seeking medical attention in case of illness.

Getting to Jhushan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jhushan

  1. Taichung International Airport (69km from Jhushan)

  2. Taipei Taoyuan International Airport (Approx. 190km from Jhushan)

Bus Stations Near Jhushan

  1. Nantou Bus Station (37km from Jhushan)

  2. Taichung Bus Station (71km from Jhushan)

Other Options

  1. Train: Visitors can take a train to Taichung Railway Station and then hire a taxi or take a bus to Jhushan.

  2. Car Rentals: Several car rental services are available at the airports and cities.

How to Get Around in Jhushan

Visitors can take a taxi, rental car, or use the local bus services to explore Jhushan’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Jhushan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Puli: A charming town known for its night market, Sun Moon Lake, and handicrafts.

  2. Xitou: A nature reserve with bamboo forests, hiking trails, and scenic views.

  3. Taichung: A bustling city with cafes, museums, parks, and shopping centers.

Conclusion

Jhushan is a hidden gem in Taiwan that has something for everyone, from adventure activities to cultural experiences. Plan your trip to Jhushan using this comprehensive travel guide and uncover the beauty and charm of this vibrant region.
