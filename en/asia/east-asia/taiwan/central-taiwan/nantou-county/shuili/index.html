Introduction to Shuili

Located in the center of Taiwan in Nantou County, Shuili is a quiet and charming town situated in a gorgeous valley surrounded by mountains. Visitors who come to Shuili will be captivated by the natural beauty of the region, as well as the town’s distinct local culture and charming small-town atmosphere. From breathtaking scenic views to outdoor adventures, and cultural landmarks, Shuili offers a wide range of attractions perfect for any kind of traveler.

Whether you’re seeking an escape from the stresses of daily life or looking for a unique cultural experience, Shuili has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Shuili

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shuili has several fascinating cultural attractions that visitors can explore. The following are the most popular museums and historical sites in the area:

The Shuili Snake Kiln Ceramics Cultural Park

The Shuili Snake Kiln Ceramics Cultural Park is a popular attraction in Shuili that showcases traditional Taiwanese ceramics. In this park, visitors can see a kiln that is shaped like a snake, which is why it is called the snake kiln. Visitors can also watch traditional ceramic-making demonstrations, learn about the history of ceramics in Taiwan, and even try their hand at creating their pottery pieces.

Jiji Railway Station

The Jiji Railway Station is a railway station with a unique history. During the 1990 earthquake in Taiwan, the station was severely damaged, and instead of tearing it down, the local government turned it into a museum. The station now showcases a collection of old trains and railway equipment, and it is a popular spot for visitors interested in the history of transportation in Taiwan.

Shuili Xiangshang Visitor Center

The Shuili Xiangshang Visitor Center is another popular cultural attraction in Shuili that visitors should not miss. The center features exhibits showcasing the history and culture of the region, as well as providing information for visitors looking to explore the local area.

Natural Attractions of Shuili: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Besides cultural and historical sites, Shuili is also home to many natural wonders. The following are some of the most popular outdoor activities and scenic sites to visit:

Shuili Snake Kiln Ceramics Cultural Park

The same ceramics park that hosts traditional ceramic-making demonstrations also features breathtaking scenic views that visitors can enjoy. The park is located on a hill, making it an excellent location for taking in the stunning mountain views that surround the town of Shuili.

Sun Moon Lake

Sun Moon Lake is one of the top natural attractions in Taiwan, and it is easily accessible from Shuili. The lake is a popular spot for tourists and locals alike and offers numerous activities, such as boating, hiking, and hot spring bathing.

Xitou Forest Recreation Area

Xitou Forest Recreation Area is another popular site for visitors who love nature. The area features beautiful trails that are great for hiking, as well as stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shuili

Hiking and biking are some of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of Shuili, and the town has numerous trails available for visitors to explore. The following are some of the most popular routes:

Shuili River Nature Trail

The Shuili River Nature Trail is a scenic path that follows the Shuili River, providing visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests. The trail is relatively easy, making it an excellent option for visitors of all ages and fitness levels.

Jilong Mountain Trail

Jilong Mountain Trail is a more challenging hike that rewards visitors with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys of Shuili. The trail is suitable for experienced hikers who are looking for a more adventurous experience but can be challenging for beginners.

Xitou Nature Education Area

The Xitou Nature Education Area is an excellent option for visitors who want a more leisurely hike. The area features various trails, each with its unique beauty, that visitors can explore at their own pace.

Best Things to Do in Shuili: Activities and Tours

Shuili offers a wide range of activities and tours that visitors can participate in to make the most out of their visit. Some of the most popular activities include:

Bamboo Rafting

Bamboo rafting is a unique way to explore the beauty of Shuili’s nature. Visitors can unwind as they float down the scenic Shuili River on traditional bamboo rafts.

Bicycle Tours

Bicycle tours are a great way to see Shuili’s stunning scenery up close while getting some exercise. Many operators offer guided bicycle tours around the town and the surrounding mountains.

Tea Plantation Tours

Shuili is known for its expansive tea plantations, and visitors can explore them through tea plantation tours. The tours take visitors through the plantations, providing them with insights into the tea-making process while enjoying scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Shuili

Visitors can experience local cuisine at various local eateries around Shuili. The following are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Bamboo Rice

Bamboo rice is a local specialty dish in Shuili. The dish is prepared by stuffing seasoned rice with various meats and vegetables inside bamboo and cooking it over a fire.

Stinky Tofu

Stinky tofu is a classic Taiwanese snack food that is not for the faint of heart. The tofu is known for its strong smell, but it has a soft, creamy texture and a unique flavor that many visitors enjoy.

Local Tea

Shuili is known for its tea plantations, and visitors can’t leave town without trying the local tea. Gong Ting tea and Oriental Beauty tea are just some of the popular varieties available.

Local Beverages

Plum Juice

Plum juice is a refreshing beverage popular in Shuili. The juice is made by fermenting plums, resulting in a tangy, slightly sour flavor that many visitors love.

Where to Stay in Shuili: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shuili has a range of accommodation options that cater to visitors with different budgets and preferences. The following are some top picks:

Shuili Hotel

Shuili Hotel is a four-star hotel located in the center of Shuili town. The hotel features a restaurant and bar, fitness center, and conference room. Rooms are clean, well-appointed, and comfortable.

The Cubehouse

The Cubehouse is a unique type of accommodation in Shuili that allows visitors to stay in a cube-shaped cabin nestled amidst nature. The cabins have panoramic windows that provide stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Slow Hostel

Slow Hostel is perfect for visitors traveling on a tighter budget. The hostel provides guests with clean, comfortable beds and shared kitchens and bathrooms.

What to Buy in Shuili: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shuili is known for its tea, pottery, and bamboo products. Visitors can find these and other locally made souvenirs at the region’s various markets and shops. Some popular picks include:

  • Handmade Tea Sets
  • Bamboo Products such as Fans and Baskets
  • Pottery and Ceramics such as Cups, Plates, and Bowls

Local Culture and Customs of Shuili

Shuili has a strong local culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. The people of Shuili highly value respect, harmony, and community, and visitors should keep this in mind while interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Shuili

The best time to visit Shuili is from April to October when the weather is mild, and the town’s natural splendor is in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shuili

Do:

  • Show respect to local customs and traditions
  • Engage with the local community and immerse yourself in their culture
  • Enjoy local cuisine and wine

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause harm to the environment
  • Use flash photography at temples or cultural sites
  • Disrupt local traditions or ceremonies

Safety and Health

Safety in Shuili

Shuili is generally safe for visitors, but it’s essential to take usual precautions such as securing your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Shuili

Shuili experiences hot and humid weather, so visitors should take care to stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun.

Getting to Shuili: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shuili

The nearest airport to Shuili is Taichung International Airport, located approximately 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Shuili

Shuili Bus Station is located near the town center and offers regular services to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also book taxis and private car services to get to Shuili from Taichung or Taipei airports.

How to Get Around in Shuili

Walking is the best way to explore Shuili’s town center, but for attractions further afield, visitors can use the frequent bus services or hire private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Shuili: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Puli

Puli is a charming town located just 20 kilometers from Shuili that offers visitors a similar range of attractions, such as stunning scenery, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Shuili is a hidden gem located in the heart of Taiwan that offers a unique blend of cultural experiences and natural beauty. From scenic views to outdoor adventures, and cultural landmarks, Shuili has something for everyone, making it an excellent destination for travelers of all kinds.
