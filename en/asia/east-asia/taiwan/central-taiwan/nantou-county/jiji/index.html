Your Comprehensive Guide to Visiting Jiji, Taiwan

Nestled in the heart of Nantou County, in Central Taiwan, Jiji is a charming town that captivates visitors with its rich culture, natural beauty, and exciting activities. From historic landmarks and scenic views to hiking and biking routes, Jiji has plenty to offer for travelers of all interests.

Introduction to Jiji

Jiji is a small town located in Nantou County, in Central Taiwan. It is well-known for its historic landmarks, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. Jiji is a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers, who come to explore its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Jiji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jiji is steeped in history and culture, and it boasts a number of museums and historical sites that are worth visiting. Some of the top cultural attractions in Jiji include:

  • Jiji Wuchang Temple: One of the oldest temples in Taiwan, Jiji Wuchang Temple is dedicated to the god of war and is famous for its ancient architecture and intricate artwork.
  • Jiji Railway Museum: Housed in a former train station, this museum exhibits the history of Jiji’s railway system and its contribution to Taiwan’s development.
  • Jiji Old Street: A charming street lined with traditional Taiwanese shops and vendors selling souvenirs and local delicacies.

Natural Attractions of Jiji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jiji’s natural beauty is second to none, with lush greenery, majestic mountains, and crystal-clear rivers. Some of the top natural attractions in Jiji include:

  • Shuili Snake Kiln: A stunning ceramics factory that has been in operation for over a century, the Snake Kiln offers visitors the chance to see traditional pottery-making techniques.
  • Jiji Green Tunnel: A scenic path lined with lush greenery, the Green Tunnel is popular with hikers and bikers.
  • Ershui Visitor Center: A popular spot for birdwatching, the Visitor Center also offers kayak rentals and guided tours of the nearby lakes and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiji

Jiji is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and scenery. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Jiji include:

  • Puli-Jiji Bike Path: A 20km long bike path that winds through picturesque countryside, the Puli-Jiji Bike Path is one of the most scenic routes in Taiwan.
  • Shanlinsi Trail: This 1.6km trail leads visitors to the summit of Shanlinsi Mountain, where they can enjoy panoramic views of Jiji and its surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Jiji: Activities and Tours

Jiji has no shortage of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Jiji include:

  • Rafting in the Pachang River: Visitors can take a thrilling rafting trip down the Pachang River, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Fruit Picking in Jiji: Jiji is famous for its fresh, delicious fruit. Visitors can pick their own fruits, including strawberries, peaches, and persimmons, at one of the town’s many orchards.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take a hot air balloon tour of the stunning Nantou County, where you can take in the breathtaking views of the landscapes from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jiji

Day 1: Jiji Old Street, Jiji Wuchang Temple, and Jiji Railway Museum. Day 2: Shuili Snake Kiln and Jiji Green Tunnel. Day 3: Ershui Visitor Center and Puli-Jiji Bike Path. Day 4: Shanlinsi Trail and rafting in the Pachang River. Day 5: Fruit picking and a hot air balloon ride. Day 6: Rest day, relax at a local hot spring. Day 7: Depart Jiji, or travel to nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Jiji

Jiji’s food and drink scene is rich and diverse, with plenty of local specialties to try. Some must-try dishes in Jiji include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pig’s Blood Cake: A Taiwanese specialty made from pig’s blood and glutinous rice, Pig’s Blood Cake is a popular snack in Jiji.
  • Oyster Omelet: A savory dish made with fresh oysters and eggs, Oyster Omelet is a favorite among locals.
  • Sun Cake: A sweet pastry filled with malt sugar, Sun Cake is a famous delicacy in Jiji.

Local Beverages

  • Dong Ding Oolong Tea: Grown at the famous Dong Ding Mountain in Nantou County, Dong Ding Oolong Tea is one of the best-known teas in Taiwan.
  • Shaoxing Wine: A type of rice wine that originated in Shaoxing, China, Shaoxing Wine is a popular drink in Jiji.

Where to Stay in Jiji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jiji has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Jiji include:

  • Jiji Happin Hotel: A comfortable and affordable hotel located in the heart of Jiji.
  • Yi Yuan Resort: A luxury resort that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.
  • Hao Si Chuan Homestay: A charming and cozy guesthouse that provides a warm and welcoming atmosphere to guests.

What to Buy in Jiji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jiji is famous for its handicrafts, local specialties, and fresh fruits. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Ceramics: Jiji is famous for its traditional pottery-making techniques, and visitors can purchase ceramics and other pottery products at the Snake Kiln.
  • Fresh Fruit: Jiji is known for its fresh, delicious fruit. Visitors can purchase a variety of fruits, including strawberries, peaches, and persimmons, at one of the town’s many orchards.
  • Peanuts: Jiji is famous for its roasted peanuts, which make a great snack or souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiji

Jiji is a town steeped in history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn and respect its local customs and traditions. Some of the customs and traditions in Jiji include:

  • Temple Etiquette: Visitors to Jiji’s temples are expected to remove their shoes and dress modestly out of respect for the gods and worshippers.
  • Tea Culture: Tea is an integral part of Taiwanese culture, and visitors to Jiji can learn about the traditions and etiquette of tea-drinking.
  • Festivals: Jiji hosts many festivals throughout the year, including Lantern Festival and Mid-Autumn Festival. Visitors are welcome to join in the celebrations and enjoy the lively atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Jiji

The best time to visit Jiji is in the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. The summer months can be hot and humid, and the winter months can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiji

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and specialties.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in temples or other religious sites.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore safety precautions during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jiji

Jiji is generally a safe town, but visitors should exercise the same level of caution as they would in any unfamiliar place. Some safety tips for Jiji include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night.
  • Keep valuables locked up in a safe at your hotel or guesthouse.
  • Follow safety precautions during outdoor activities, such as hiking or rafting.

Health in Jiji

Jiji is a relatively healthy town with no major health concerns for visitors. However, visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any foreign country, such as drinking bottled water and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Jiji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jiji is easily accessible by public transportation, with several options available for getting to the town.

Airports Near Jiji

The nearest airport to Jiji is Taichung International Airport, located about 50 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Jiji

Jiji Bus Station is located in the center of town, and buses run regularly to and from other major cities in Taiwan, including Taipei and Taichung.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Jiji Railway Station, which is located in the heart of town, or hire a taxi or rental car to travel to Jiji.

How to Get Around in Jiji

Jiji is a small town, and most attractions and points of interest are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the town at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Jiji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jiji is located in the heart of Nantou County, which offers plenty of attractions and points of interest for visitors to explore. Some nearby destinations and excursions from Jiji include:

  • Sun Moon Lake: A stunning lake surrounded by mountain peaks, Sun Moon Lake is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and boating.
  • Cingjing Farm: A beautiful highland farm that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain scenery, Cingjing Farm is a great destination for nature lovers.
  • Lukang: A historic town located on the western coast of Taiwan, Lukang boasts traditional shops and temples that showcase the rich cultural heritage of Taiwan.

Conclusion

Jiji is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse of Taiwan’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. With plenty of attractions, activities, and points of interest, Jiji is a must-visit destination for travelers to Taiwan. Whether you’re looking to explore historic landmarks or experience the great outdoors, Jiji has something for everyone.
