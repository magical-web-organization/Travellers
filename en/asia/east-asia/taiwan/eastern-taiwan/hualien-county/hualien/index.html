Introduction to Hualien

Located in the eastern part of Taiwan, Hualien is a mesmerizing place known for its natural and cultural beauty. Surrounded by the Central Mountain Range and the Pacific Ocean, Hualien offers visitors a wealth of outdoor adventures and scenic views. The city is also rich with cultural heritage, from local customs to significant historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Hualien

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or history buff, there’s no shortage of places to visit in Hualien. The following is a list of must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Dong Hwa University Museum: A must-visit for history lovers, this museum offers a glimpse into the history of Taiwan and the region.
  • Hualien Cultural Creative Industries Park: Located in an old Japan-style sugar factory, this park offers a unique experience to visitors.
  • Chihsing Tan Katsuo Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Taiwan’s katsuo fishery industry.

Natural Attractions of Hualien: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taroko National Park: Taroko National Park is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Hualien. Its stunning natural landscapes, hiking trails, and viewpoints make it a paradise for nature lovers.
  • Qixingtan Beach: Qixingtan Beach is a beautiful shoreline that offers a peaceful escape from the daily hustle.
  • Liyu Lake: Surrounded by mountains, Liyu Lake is a serene and scenic destination that offers visitors a relaxed atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hualien

Hualien is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. The city offers a range of hiking and biking routes that cater to every level of difficulty. Some of the popular routes worth mentioning are:

  • The Zhuilu Old Trail: This trail offers breathtaking views of the Taroko Gorge and is regarded as one of the most scenic hikes in Taiwan.
  • The Nan’an Waterfall Trail: This hike is a must-do for those who want to experience the lush greenery and natural beauty of Hualien.

Best Things to Do in Hualien: Activities and Tours

Hualien is a place that offers a variety of exciting activities and tours. Some of the most popular things to do in Hualien are:

  • River tracing: This activity is a popular choice for thrill-seekers who want to enjoy nature in a unique way.
  • Whale watching: Hualien is one of the best places to go whale watching in Taiwan.
  • Night markets: Hualien’s night markets offer a range of delicious and unique foods as well as local handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hualien

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Hualien, then the following itinerary is worth considering:

Day 1-2: Explore Taroko National Park Day 3: Go river tracing or whale watching Day 4: Visit Hualien Cultural Creative Industries Park Day 5: Hike the Zhuilu Old Trail Day 6: Visit Liyu Lake and Qixingtan Beach Day 7: Shop at the local markets and souvenir stores

Food and Drink Scene of Hualien

Hualien is also a place that offers a range of delicious and unique foods, as well as local beverages. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mochi: This sweet rice cake is a specialty of Hualien and a famous souvenir.
  • Salted Duck: A popular dish among locals, salted duck is a must-try when in Hualien.
  • Seafood: Being a coastal city, Hualien is famous for its seafood, and you can have it in myriad ways.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Tea: Bubble tea is a popular drink in Taiwan and worth trying when in Hualien.
  • Rice Wine: One of the most famous local beverages in Hualien is Rice Wine, known for its rich aroma and taste.

Where to Stay in Hualien: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hualien offers many accommodation options for tourists. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Hualien are:

  • Silks Place Taroko: This hotel is located in the heart of Taroko National Park and offers luxurious accommodation.
  • Chantai Hotel: This hotel is located near Hualien City’s night market.
  • Hualien Wow Hostel: This hostel is an affordable option for backpackers.

What to Buy in Hualien: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re thinking about buying souvenirs or local products, then Hualien has a range of options for you. Some of the most popular items are:

  • Aboriginal crafts
  • Tea
  • Mochi

Local Culture and Customs of Hualien

Hualien is home to many indigenous tribes, and their customs and traditions play an important role in the city’s culture. Some of the things to be aware of when visiting Hualien are:

  • Respect for elders is highly valued in Hualien’s culture.
  • When visiting temples, dress appropriately and remove shoes before entering.

Best Time to Visit Hualien

The best time to visit Hualien is from March to May when the weather is pleasant, and the cherry blossoms are blooming. However, the city can be visited all year round, and each season has its charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hualien

Do:

  • Try the local food
  • Learn about the culture and traditions of local indigenous tribes
  • Take care of the environment

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way
  • Disrespect elders or cultural customs
  • Take photos without asking for permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Hualien

Hualien is generally a safe place for tourists, but like any other city, visitors should exercise caution and take appropriate safety measures. Some tips to stay safe in Hualien are:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid carrying valuable items.
  • Use only licensed and well-reviewed tour operators.

Health in Hualien

Hualien has excellent medical facilities, and visitors can easily seek medical help if needed.

Getting to Hualien: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hualien

The nearest airport to Hualien is Hualien Airport, which has direct flights to and from Taipei.

Bus Stations Near Hualien

Hualien Bus Station is the main bus station in the city and offers regular bus services to and from other parts of Taiwan.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Hualien by train, car, or scooter.

How to Get Around in Hualien

The best way to get around Hualien is by scooter, bike, or car. Taxis are also available, but they can be costly. It’s worth noting that the roads in Hualien can be steep and winding in some areas, so drivers should exercise caution.

Where to Go Next from Hualien: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Taiwan, the following nearby destinations and excursions are worth considering:

  • Taitung: A coastal city famous for its hot springs and famous local cuisine.
  • Yilan: Known for its hot springs, coastlines, mountains, and aboriginal culture.

Conclusion

Hualien is a breathtaking city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Hualien has something for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to explore the best of what Hualien has to offer fully.
