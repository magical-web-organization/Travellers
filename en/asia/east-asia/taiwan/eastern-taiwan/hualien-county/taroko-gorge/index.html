Introduction to Taroko Gorge

Taroko Gorge is a natural wonder located in the Hualien County of Eastern Taiwan. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Taiwan due to its breathtakingly beautiful landscapes, impressive cliffs, waterfalls, emerald green rivers, and unique cultural heritage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taroko Gorge

Taroko Gorge is home to some of the most amazing hiking and biking trails in Asia. Some of the popular routes for hikers and cyclists include:

  • Hehuanshan Main Peak Trail - this route is perfect for experienced hikers and promises a spectacular scenic view.
  • Shakadang Trail – a winding 4.4 km trail through the gorge, with stunning turquoise waters and a hanging suspension bridge.
  • Zhuilu Trail - enjoy panoramic views of the canyon from a 700-meter cliff trail with an ultimate height experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Taroko Gorge

Taroko Gorge is home to various indigenous culture and customs of the indigenous community. Their artistic and cultural heritage can be legible in handcrafted accessories and clothes, local music, and traditional dances.

Best Time to Visit Taroko Gorge

The best time to visit Taroko Gorge in Taiwan is during Autumn (September-November) and spring (April-May). The weather is mild and pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor exploration.

Safety in Taroko Gorge

How to Get Around in Taroko Gorge

While some of Taroko’s sights are accessible by bike or car, public transport is limited, and a guided tour can be the best option to maximize your time and explore the best of the region.
