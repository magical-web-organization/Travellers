Introduction to Ruisui

Ruisui is a small town located in Hualien County, Eastern Taiwan. The town is famous for its hot springs and scenic beauty, making it a popular destination for travelers seeking relaxation and outdoor adventures. Ruisui is also known for its rich culture and history, with many museums and historical sites in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruisui

  • Respect for elders and polite behavior is highly valued
  • Remove shoes indoors
  • No tipping is necessary

Best Time to Visit Ruisui

The best time to visit Ruisui is during the spring (April-June) and fall (October-November) seasons when the weather is cool and the scenery is breathtaking. Avoid visiting in the summer months (July-September) due to the hot and humid weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruisui

Do:

  • Appreciate the beauty of nature and respect the environment
  • Follow the local customs and manners
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas or on hiking trails
  • Disrespect local traditions and beliefs
  • Smoke in public or indoor areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Ruisui

Ruisui is generally a safe place for travelers. However, be cautious when hiking or participating in outdoor activities, follow safety guidelines, and prepare for emergencies.

Health in Ruisui

Bring any required medication with you since some drugs may be hard to find. Taiwan has a universal healthcare system, and medical facilities are modern and well-equipped.
