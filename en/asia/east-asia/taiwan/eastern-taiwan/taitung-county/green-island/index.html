Introduction to Green Island

Green Island is a small volcanic island situated off the eastern coast of Taiwan. The island, also known as “Lutao” in the local dialect, is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With its crystal clear waters, amazing marine life, lush forests, and scenic views, Green Island is the perfect destination for those who want to explore the beauty of Eastern Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Green Island

Green Island offers a variety of attractions for visitors. Here are some of the must-see places to visit on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Green Island has a rich cultural history, and visitors can learn about it by visiting the following museums and historical places:

  • Green Island Human Rights Culture Park: This park is dedicated to preserving the memory of political prisoners who were sent to Green Island during Taiwan’s martial law period. Visitors can learn about Taiwan’s democratic movement through interactive exhibits and guided tours.
  • Green Island Lighthouse: Built in 1939 by the Japanese, this lighthouse stands as a reminder of Taiwan’s colonial past. Visitors can climb up to the top for breathtaking views of the island.
  • Old Chinese Bunkhouses: These bunkhouses were used to house prisoners during the martial law period. Today, they are preserved as a reminder of Taiwan’s tumultuous past.

Natural Attractions of Green Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Green Island is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with many natural attractions to explore:

  • Sea Turtle Hatchery Center: This center is dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles that visit Green Island’s shores. Visitors can observe the sea turtles up close and learn about their habits and behavior.
  • Jhaorih Hot Springs: These natural hot springs are located on the southern part of the island, and offer a relaxing way to end your day of adventure.
  • Xiao Liu Qiu: This small island off the coast of Green Island is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving. Visitors can see a variety of colorful marine life in the clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Green Island

Green Island is a small island, but it has a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Mt. Guanyin Hiking Trail: This trail is the most popular on the island, leading hikers to the top of Mt. Guanyin for panoramic views of the island and the sea.
  • Old Military Defense Trail: This trail leads visitors through the dense forests and along the rugged coastline of the island, passing by old military bunkers and fortresses.
  • Coastal Bike Path: Rent a bike and follow the coastal bike path around the island for stunning views of the ocean and the island’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Green Island: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking, biking, and exploring the island’s natural wonders, there are many other things to do on Green Island. Here are a few of the best activities and tours:

  • Scuba Diving: Green Island is known for its amazing diving spots with abundant coral reefs and diverse marine life. Visitors can take a scuba diving tour to snorkel or dive to explore the underwater world.
  • Glass Bottom Boat: If you’re not keen on diving, take a glass-bottom boat ride and enjoy a clear view of the sea and the marine life below.
  • Whale Watching Tour: From late May to early September, visitors can take a whale watching tour to spot humpback whales and other marine creatures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Green Island

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Green Island:

Day 1: Visit the Green Island Human Rights Culture Park and the Green Island Lighthouse.

Day 2: Take a tour of the island’s natural wonders and enjoy a dip in the Jhaorih Hot Springs.

Day 3: Go on a scuba diving trip and explore the underwater world of Green Island.

Day 4: Rent a bike and ride along the coast, taking in the scenic views of the island.

Day 5: Visit Xiao Liu Qiu for a day of snorkeling and enjoying the island’s natural beauty.

Day 6: Take a trip to the neighboring Orchid Island and experience the unique culture of the Tao people.

Day 7: Relax on the beach or take a whale watching tour to spot humpback whales and other marine creatures.

Food and Drink Scene of Green Island

Green Island is known for its unique cuisine, incorporating fresh seafood and local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saury Fish: This local delicacy is served grilled or fried and is a popular dish on the island.
  • Sea Urchin: Green Island is famous for its fresh sea urchin, which is served raw as sashimi or cooked in a variety of ways.
  • Millet Wine: This traditional alcoholic beverage is made from fermented millet and is a must-try for visitors to the island.

Local Beverages

  • Green Island Beer: This locally brewed beer is made with pure water from the island and is a favorite among visitors and locals alike.
  • Sour Plum Juice: This refreshing and cooling beverage is perfect for hot summer days on the island.

Where to Stay in Green Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Green Island offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Green Island Bali resort: This upscale resort offers luxurious villas with private pools and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Green Island Youth Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is a great choice for solo travelers and backpackers.
  • The Old Man and the Sea B&B: This cozy guesthouse offers warm hospitality and a peaceful atmosphere for a relaxing stay on the island.

What to Buy in Green Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Green Island can take home a variety of unique souvenirs and local products as a reminder of their trip. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Sea Urchin Products: Purchase sea urchin souvenirs and food products such as sea urchin sauce and crackers.
  • Black Coral Jewellery: Buy a unique piece of jewellery made from black coral, a type of marine organism found near Green Island.
  • Millet Wine: Take home a bottle of Green Island’s traditional millet wine or pick up a brewing kit to make your own at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Green Island

Green Island has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Taiwanese, Han Chinese, and Tao indigenous culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Hi-Si Festival: This is the biggest festival of the year for the Tao people, and is celebrated with music, dance, and traditional rituals in the third month of the lunar calendar.
  • Bunun Harvest Festival: This festival is a celebration of the harvest season and is a vital cultural event for the Bunun people, one of the indigenous tribes of Taiwan.
  • Conserving the Environment: Visitors should be mindful of their impact on the island’s natural environment and follow local regulations.

Best Time to Visit Green Island

The best time to visit Green Island is from April to November when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm for water activities such as diving, snorkeling, and swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Green Island

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture: Be respectful of the island’s cultural heritage and take part in local customs and traditions.
  • Follow Regulations: Help conserve the natural environment by following local regulations and not littering.
  • Stay Hydrated: Bring plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.

Don’t:

  • Damage the Coral Reefs: Be mindful of your impact on the marine environment, and avoid stepping on or damaging the coral reefs.
  • Take Rocks or Coral: Taking rocks or coral from the island is illegal and could harm the local ecosystem.
  • Take Risks: Be cautious when participating in outdoor activities and follow safety guidelines to avoid accidents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Green Island

Green Island is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be cautious when participating in outdoor activities and follow safety guidelines to avoid accidents.

Health in Green Island

Green Island has a medical clinic and a pharmacy, but visitors should bring their own medications as it may not be available on the island. It’s essential to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Green Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Green Island is only accessible by boat or plane from the city of Taitung.

Airports Near Green Island

The closest airport to Green Island is Taitung Airport in Taitung City.

Bus Stations Near Green Island

The best way to reach Green Island is to take a ferry from Fugang Fishing Port in Taitung City.

Other Options

There are also helicopter and chartered flight services available for those who want a faster and more comfortable way to get to Green Island.

How to Get Around in Green Island

Green Island is a small island, and visitors can explore most of the attractions on foot or by cycling around the island. Motorbike rentals and scooter rentals are also available for those who prefer a faster way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Green Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations you can visit from Green Island:

  • Orchid Island: Take a ferry to Orchid Island and experience the unique culture of the Tao people.
  • Taitung City: Visit one of the quaint little boutiques or coffee shops, the Tiehua Music Village, and the Taitung Forest Park.
  • Kenting National Park: Head south to the tropical paradise of Kenting National Park, known for its stunning beaches, beautiful coastline, and lush forests.

Conclusion

Green Island is a little slice of paradise in Taiwan, with its beautiful natural surroundings, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventurer, or simply want to relax in a peaceful environment, Green Island has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to plan your perfect trip to Green Island!
