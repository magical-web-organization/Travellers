Introduction to Taitung

Taitung is a scenic county located in the southeastern part of Taiwan. It is surrounded by mountains, forests, and the Pacific Ocean, making it a popular destination for outdoor activities and natural beauty. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and where to stay in Taitung.

List of Places to Visit in Taitung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taitung Railway Art Village: A former railway station transformed into an art village, showcasing the cultural history of the region.
  • Beinan Cultural Park: An archaeological museum displaying the life and culture of the aboriginal peoples of Taitung.
  • Taitung Forest Park: A park with trails that allow visitors to see the flora and fauna of the region.

Natural Attractions of Taitung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sanxiantai: A unique natural bridge on the coast of Taitung with stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • East Rift Valley: A scenic drive through Taitung’s countryside with opportunities to stop and explore small towns and hot springs.
  • Jhiben National Forest Recreation Area: A forested area with hiking trails, waterfalls, and a hot spring for soaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taitung

Taitung is a great place for hiking and biking with many trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding nature. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Guanshan Bicycle Trail: A 47-kilometer trail that takes you through small towns and scenic countryside.
  • Luye Gaotai: A popular spot for hot air ballooning and paragliding with hiking trails and stunning views of the East Rift Valley.
  • Jiaming Lake Trail: A challenging hike through the mountains with incredible views of the lake and surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Taitung: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Taitung’s coastline offers some of Taiwan’s best surf spots.
  • Whale Watching: See humpback whales during their migration season between December and April.
  • Cultural Festivals: Taitung hosts cultural festivals throughout the year, including the East Coast Land Arts Festival, Taitung Hot Air Balloon Festival, and the Harvest Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taitung

Day 1-2: Explore Taitung’s cultural attractions such as the Beinan Cultural Park and Taitung Railway Art Village.

Day 3-4: Spend time in nature at Jhiben National Forest Recreation Area and Sanxiantai.

Day 5: Bike along the Guanshan Bicycle Trail and explore small towns along the way.

Day 6: Soak in the hot springs of Luye Gaotai and take in the stunning views of the East Rift Valley.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby Orchid Island, known for its unique culture and pristine beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Taitung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rosong: A traditional dish of rice with vegetables, seafood, and meat.
  • Donghe Big Rice Cake: A type of rice cake with red bean paste, considered a local specialty.
  • Taitung Milkfish: Taitung is known for its freshwater fish, and milkfish is the most popular.

Local Beverages

  • Taitung Tea: Taitung produces high-quality tea, and some of the most famous types are Oriental Beauty and Jade Oolong.
  • Beer: Taitung has several local breweries, including South Island Brewery and Spring House Beer.

Where to Stay in Taitung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sheraton Taitung Hotel: A luxury hotel located near Taitung City with a spa, pool, and restaurant.
  • Sunny Day Hostel: A budget-friendly option in Taitung City with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Stone House: A unique guesthouse located in the mountains of Taitung with stunning views, a hot spring, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Taitung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Taitung Tea: As mentioned, Taitung produces high-quality tea that makes an excellent souvenir.
  • Pottery: There are several pottery studios in Taitung, and you can buy handmade pottery as a souvenir.
  • Dried Fruits: Taitung is known for its sun-dried fruits, including mango, pineapple, and jackfruit.

Local Culture and Customs of Taitung

Taitung is home to several aboriginal tribes, and their culture is an important part of the region. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Respect for elders: In the aboriginal culture, elders are highly respected and regarded as the keepers of wisdom and knowledge.
  • Tribal tattoos: Many members of the indigenous tribes have traditional tattoos on their face and body. Visitors should be respectful when asking about them.

Best Time to Visit Taitung

The best time to visit Taitung is from October to December when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taitung

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taitung

Taitung is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions and avoid leaving valuables unattended. It is also important to be aware of the risks of natural disasters, such as typhoons and earthquakes.

Health in Taitung

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations and take measures to prevent mosquito bites, as there is a risk of dengue fever and Zika virus in Taiwan. It is also advisable to purchase health insurance before your trip.

Getting to Taitung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taitung

Taitung Airport is the main airport in the area, with flights to and from Taipei, Kaohsiung, and other parts of Taiwan.

Bus Stations Near Taitung

Taitung Bus Station is located in the city center, with buses going to cities throughout Taiwan.

Other Options

Train service is also available to Taitung, with trains going to and from Taipei, Kaohsiung, and other parts of Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Taitung

Taitung is a small city, and most places are easily accessible by walking or renting a bike. Taxis are also available, and buses are a convenient option for traveling to other parts of the county.

Where to Go Next from Taitung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kenting: A popular beach destination located in the southern part of Taiwan.
  • Green Island: A small island off the coast of Taitung with excellent diving and snorkeling opportunities.
  • Hualien: A scenic city located north of Taitung, known for its stunning natural beauty and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Taitung is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience Taiwan’s natural beauty, cultural history, and outdoor adventures. Plan your trip today with our comprehensive travel guide and get ready to explore everything that Taitung has to offer.
