Introduction to Dawu

Nestled in the heart of Taitung County in Eastern Taiwan, Dawu is a charming town surrounded by picturesque mountains and the Pacific Ocean. Known for its tranquil vibe and natural beauty, this destination promises an immersive experience and an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city life. The town boasts of diverse geography and offers an array of activities for visitors to enjoy. From exploring museums and historical landmarks to hiking and biking, Dawu is an ideal travel destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Dawu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dawu’s rich history and culture reflect in the many museums and historical landmarks in the town. Visitors can explore the following attractions:

  • Huadong Valley Storytelling Museum: This interactive museum showcases the history, arts, and folklore of the East Rift Valley. Visitors can explore the exhibits, watch traditional performances and participate in hands-on workshops.
  • Taiwan Indigenous Cultural Park: Learn about the aboriginal culture of Taiwan at this park. It houses several exhibitions showcasing the indigenous peoples’ traditional way of life and cultural heritage.
  • Dawu Old Town: Take a stroll through the historic town of Dawu and witness the traditional architecture and beautiful surroundings of this hidden gem.

Natural Attractions of Dawu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dawu’s beautiful landscape is one that nature enthusiasts will appreciate. With its lush forests, beautiful mountains, and scenic coastline, visitors can embark on the following outdoor activities and recreational adventures:

  • Chike Mountain: Located in Dawu Township, Chike Mountain is a must-visit for hiking enthusiasts. It offers a challenging trek, beautiful mountain views along with gorgeous waterfalls and hot springs.
  • Cape Dawu: Take a trip to the southernmost point of Taitung County, which offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Luye Plateau: An elevated plain situated in the eastern part of Taitung, Luye Plateau is famous for hot air balloon rides, cycling, and paragliding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dawu

Dawu’s mountainous terrain is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails in the area.

  • Chike Mountain Trail: An 11.2km hike, starting from Dawu train station to Chike Mountain peak, offering stunning views of the mountains, forests, and waterfalls.
  • Bike Route 193: This scenic route runs from Dawu Train Station to Taitung County Government and is an ideal spot for cycling through the lush greenery of Taiwan’s East Coast.
  • Road to Cape Dawu: A picturesque drive along with the east coast of Taiwan, leading to Cape Dawu.

Best Things to Do in Dawu: Activities and Tours

From hiking and cycling to exploring museums and cultural landmarks, Dawu offers an array of activities to keep you entertained throughout your stay. We recommend the following:

  • Go for a hot-air balloon ride over the Luye Plateau, where you will be amazed by the panoramic views of Taitung.
  • Take a trip to the Fuyuan National Forest Recreation Area, an ideal spot to relax, have a picnic or go for a stroll.
  • Visit the Green Island and enjoy snorkeling, diving or kayaking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dawu

Day 1-2: Explore Dawu’s history and culture by visiting the Huadong Valley Storytelling Museum, Taiwan Indigenous Cultural Park, and Dawu Old Town.

Day 3-4: Spend these days exploring the beautiful nature of Dawu. Hike the Chike Mountain trail, cycle through Route 193, and drive along the road to Cape Dawu.

Day 5-6: Indulge in outdoor activities like hot air balloon rides, visits to Fuyuan National Forest Recreation Area, and a trip to Green Island

Day 7: Take a day trip to Taitung City and explore the city’s museums and markets before ending your trip to Dawu.

Food and Drink Scene of Dawu

Dawu offers an exquisite culinary scene, with plenty of local delicacies to sample. When in Dawu, try the following dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tofu pudding: made from soybeans, sugar, and other flavors, this dish is a staple dessert in Dawu.
  • Pork rice bowl: A popular delicacy in Taitung, the braised pork rice bowl is a must-try dish for meat lovers.
  • Taiwanese sausage: With its sweet and savory taste, the Taiwanese sausage offers a unique and delicious experience for travelers.

Local Beverages

  • Aiwen Herbal Tea: A traditional Taiwanese herbal tea made from a unique blend of natural herbs and sweetened with sugar syrup.
  • Cold brew coffee: You can find excellent brewed coffee in Dawu, made from fresh local beans.
  • Fruit tea: Made from locally sourced fresh fruits and herbs like lemongrass, this refreshing drink is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Dawu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dawu offers an array of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Dawu hot spring resort: A perfect retreat from the bustling city, this resort offers a relaxing stay to its guests, with hot spring facilities and beautiful mountain views.
  • Dawu old town guesthouse: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Dawu town, decorated with traditional Taiwanese furnishings and offering guests comfortable stays.
  • F Hotel Chihpen: A luxurious hotel located 30 minutes away from Dawu, offering upscale facilities like hot spring pools, restaurants, and recreation areas.

What to Buy in Dawu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Before you leave Dawu, don’t forget to buy some souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Dawu Tofu Pudding: Take home the popular desserts of Dawu as a sweet reminder of your trip.
  • Handmade soap: You can find various handcrafted soaps made with local ingredients like camphor and cypress.
  • Aboriginal Handicrafts: You can buy several pieces of traditional handicrafts like bamboo baskets, beadwork, and pottery from the Taiwan Indigenous Cultural Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Dawu

Dawu has a rich culture steeped in history and tradition. Tourists should be aware of the following customs and etiquettes:

  • Always respect the local culture, traditions, and people.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or other places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Dawu

The best time to visit Dawu is from November to April when the weather is mild and not too hot. Summer months are also ideal, but temperatures can be high, making it challenging to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dawu

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local delicacies and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  • Avoid littering or damaging the natural environment.
  • Do not take pictures of the local people without their consent.
  • Do not be loud or disrespectful towards local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dawu

Dawu is a safe destination for travelers. However, tourists should take the following precautions during their stay:

  • Beware of petty thefts in crowded areas, tourist spots and markets.
  • Do not leave your valuables unattended.

Health in Dawu

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to protect their health when in Dawu, including:

  • Avoid Tap water, drink only bottled water.
  • Practice mosquito prevention when exploring the outdoors.
  • Seek immediate medical attention if experiencing any severe symptoms.

Getting to Dawu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dawu

The closest airports to Dawu are Taitung Airport and Kaohsiung International Airport. Taitung Airport is approximately an hour’s drive from Dawu, while Kaohsiung International Airport is around three hours away.

Bus Stations Near Dawu

Dawu has a bus station serving different locations in Taiwan. The Taitung main station is also a convenient option with more frequent services to Dawu.

Other Options

One can also reach Dawu by taking a train from Taipei. The journey time is around four and a half hours, with frequent services throughout the day.

How to Get Around in Dawu

Several options are available for visitors to explore Dawu’s surrounding areas, including renting a car, hiring a taxi, or using public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Dawu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dawu’s proximity to Taitung County’s other famous destinations makes it an ideal base for your Taiwan travels. Here are some of the top places to visit nearby:

  • Taitung City: A bustling coastal city famous for its traditional markets, cultural attractions, and landmarks.
  • Green Island: A volcanic island, known for its scenic beauty, coral reefs, and marine life.
  • Luye Hot Springs: One of the oldest hot springs in Taiwan, offering a tranquil, relaxing getaway amid nature.

Conclusion

Dawu is an ideal destination for travelers looking for natural beauty, vibrant culture, and off-the-beaten-path experiences. With its vast history, beautiful landscapes, delicious food, and hospitable locals, Dawu offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience for visitors. Use this guide to plan your trip to Dawu and have an immersive experience in this tropical paradise!
