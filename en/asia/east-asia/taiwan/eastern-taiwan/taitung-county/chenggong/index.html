Introduction to Chenggong

Chenggong is a small town located in Taitung County, on the east coast of Taiwan. It is an ideal destination for travelers who love nature, outdoor activities, and cultural immersion. Chenggong’s pristine beaches, lush forests, and rich cultural heritage make it a must-visit place for every traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you discover the best places to visit, hike, eat, stay, and enjoy in Chenggong, Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Chenggong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chenggong is a town steeped in history, and there are plenty of culturally significant sites to visit. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Chenggong:

  • Dulan Aboriginal Cultural Center: This center showcases the indigenous culture and history of Taiwan’s aboriginal people. It is the perfect place to learn about the region’s history, customs, and traditions.
  • Chenggong Seashore Park: This park is a great place to relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the east coast of Taiwan. It features a seawater pool, a lighthouse, and historic underground tunnels.
  • Nan’an Waterfall: This beautiful and secluded waterfall is hidden deep in the nearby mountains. Visitors can take a guided tour to discover the natural beauty of the region.

Natural Attractions of Chenggong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are a lover of nature, then Chenggong is the perfect destination for you. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Chenggong:

  • Sanxiantai: This scenic area features an iconic arch bridge that connects the mainland to a small island. Visitors can enjoy hiking, sightseeing, and birdwatching.
  • Chishang Rainbow Bridge: This stunning natural wonder is located in the nearby town of Chishang. It features colorful lights that illuminate the bridge at night, creating a beautiful rainbow effect.
  • Doufu Cape: This cape is a popular spot for hiking, fishing, and picnics. It offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chenggong

Chenggong’s beautiful natural scenery and abundant trails make it a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Jialulan Coastal Trail: This trail is perfect for nature lovers who want to enjoy the serenity and beauty of the coastline. It offers stunning scenic views of the Pacific Ocean and the rugged coastline.
  • Ciushan Forest Trail: This trail takes hikers through a lush forest and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is perfect for those who want to immerse themselves in nature.
  • Taimali Trail: This trail is known for its steep slopes and challenging terrain. It offers adventurous hikers a chance to explore some of the region’s most rugged and remote landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Chenggong: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Chenggong offers visitors a range of exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Chenggong:

  • Surfing: Chenggong’s stunning beaches and perfect waves make it the ideal destination for surfing enthusiasts.
  • Cultural Tours: Guided tours are available for visitors who want to learn more about the indigenous culture and history of Taiwan’s aboriginal people.
  • Night Markets: Chenggong’s night markets are a must-visit for foodies and those who love shopping. They offer a range of delicious street food, local products, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chenggong

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Chenggong, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Chenggong

Must-Try Dishes

Chenggong is known for its delicious seafood and traditional Taiwanese dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Sashimi: Fresh sashimi is a must-try when visiting Chenggong. The town’s proximity to the ocean means that seafood is always fresh and abundant.
  • Oyster Vermicelli: This local delicacy features vermicelli with oysters, minced pork, and vegetables. It is a specialty of the region and is often enjoyed as a breakfast dish.
  • Aboriginal Cuisine: Chenggong is home to many aboriginal tribes, each with their unique cuisine. Visitors can enjoy traditional dishes such as millet wine, wild boar meat, and bamboo rice.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Chenggong has plenty to offer. Here are some of the must-try local beverages:

  • Taiwanese Tea: Taiwan is famous for its tea, and Chenggong is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a range of tea varieties, including oolong, green, and black tea.
  • Fruit Juice: Chenggong is home to many tropical fruits, including mangoes, oranges, and pineapples. Fresh fruit juice is a healthy and refreshing drink to enjoy on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Chenggong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chenggong offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in Chenggong:

  • Rayyan Seaside Homestay: This beautiful guesthouse overlooking the ocean is perfect for those who want to relax and unwind.
  • Naruhana Hotel: This hotel offers modern amenities and breathtaking views of the sea and mountains.
  • Rice Home Guesthouse: This budget-friendly guesthouse is perfect for backpackers and those on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Chenggong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chenggong is known for its local arts and crafts, which make for unique souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Chenggong:

  • Handmade Pottery: Chenggong is famous for its high-quality pottery, which makes for beautiful and functional home decor.
  • Aboriginal Weaving: Visitors can purchase traditional handicrafts such as woven baskets, bags, and mats made by the local aboriginal tribes.
  • Sea Salt: The region’s proximity to the ocean makes it an ideal place to produce high-quality sea salt. Visitors can purchase local sea salt as a souvenir or to use in cooking.

Local Culture and Customs of Chenggong

Chenggong is home to many aboriginal tribes, each with their unique customs and traditions. Visitors should respect the local culture by dressing modestly, asking for permission before taking photographs, and avoiding sensitive topics such as politics and religion.

Best Time to Visit Chenggong

The best time to visit Chenggong is from April to November when the weather is warm and dry. However, visitors should be aware that typhoons can occur during this period and bring heavy rains and strong winds. The off-season, from December to March, is also a good time to visit Chenggong as the weather is cooler and crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chenggong

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and traditional dishes.
  • Do dress modestly when visiting temples and sacred sites.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t take photographs without asking for permission.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or religion.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chenggong

Chenggong is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take normal precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding unsafe areas at night.

Health in Chenggong

Chenggong is generally safe and healthy to visit. However, visitors should drink bottled water, take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, and seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Chenggong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chenggong is located on the east coast of Taiwan and is easily accessible by air, bus, or train.

Airports Near Chenggong

The closest airport to Chenggong is Taitung Airport, which is located about 35 kilometers away. Alternatively, Kaohsiung International Airport is a larger airport that offers connections to major cities around the world and is located about 160 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Chenggong

Chenggong is well-connected to the rest of Taiwan by bus. Visitors can take buses from Taipei or Kaohsiung to Taitung, and then take a local bus to Chenggong.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Chenggong by train. The nearest train station is located in neighboring Taitung, and visitors can take a taxi or bus to Chenggong from there.

How to Get Around in Chenggong

Chenggong is a small town that is easily navigable by foot, bike, or scooter. Visitors can rent bikes or scooters to explore the town or take a taxi for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Chenggong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chenggong is a gateway to many other beautiful destinations in Taitung County. Here are some of the nearby destinations and day-trip options:

  • Luye Highland: This scenic area is home to hot air balloon rides, tea plantations, and a range of other outdoor activities.
  • East Rift Valley: This stunning valley features idyllic small towns, hot springs, and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Green Island: This island is a popular destination for snorkeling, diving, and enjoying the natural beauty of Taiwan’s coastline.

Conclusion

Chenggong, Taiwan, is a destination that has something for everyone. From stunning natural scenery to rich cultural heritage, Chenggong offers visitors an authentic and unforgettable travel experience. With the help of this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your visit to Chenggong and discover all that this wonderful destination has to offer.
