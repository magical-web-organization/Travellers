Introduction to Changbin

Changbin is a small township in Taitung County, located in Eastern Taiwan. It is known for its scenic views, rich culture, and fantastic outdoor adventures. Visitors to Changbin can take in the breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding mountains while exploring the local culture and enjoying outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Changbin

Changbin has numerous culturally important attractions and natural wonders that are a must-visit for every traveler. Here are some of the top places to visit while in Changbin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Changbin Old Street: Take a stroll down the popular Changbin Old Street, a traditional market street lined with wooden buildings. Here you can find plenty of souvenirs, traditional handicrafts, and local snacks.
  • National Museum of Prehistory: Discover the rich history and culture of Taiwan’s indigenous tribes at the National Museum of Prehistory. The museum houses a fantastic collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history of Taiwan’s earliest inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Changbin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Liyu Lake: This stunning alpine lake is a popular spot for fishing, birdwatching, and leisurely walks. Visitors can rent paddle boats or bicycles and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Changbin Coastline Scenic Area: Stretching over 13 kilometers, this scenic area offers panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding mountains. Visitors can take a stroll along the scenic area and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changbin

Changbin has some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Taiwan. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Walami Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking enthusiasts who want to explore Taiwan’s lush forests and natural beauty. The trail is 20 kilometers long and is located in the Walami National Park.
  • Hualien-Taitung Coastal Route: The Hualien-Taitung Coastal Route is one of the most scenic bike routes in Taiwan, with stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Changbin: Activities and Tours

Changbin is home to numerous fantastic activities and tours. Here are some of the top things to do while in Changbin:

  • Whale Watching: Visitors can take a boat tour and enjoy the magnificent sight of whales swimming in the Pacific Ocean. The best time for whale watching is from May to September.
  • Hot Spring Soak: Relax and unwind in the soothing hot springs of Jhihben Hot Spring Area. The area is famous for its therapeutic properties and is the perfect spot to recharge after a day of exploring.

Food and Drink Scene of Changbin

Changbin is home to some fantastic traditional Taiwanese dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bawan: A traditional Taiwanese snack made with minced pork and vegetables wrapped in a chewy dough.
  • Suncake: A flaky pastry filled with shredded malt sugar and butter, often eaten as a dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Longan Honey: A sweet, fruity honey made from the nectar of longan flowers.
  • Pearl milk tea: A refreshing beverage consisting of tea, milk, and chewy tapioca balls.

Where to Stay in Changbin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Changbin has numerous accommodation options for every budget and preference. Here are some of the best options available:

  • Changbin Backpackers Hostel: This cozy hostel is perfect for budget travelers and offers a fantastic location near Changbin Old Street.
  • Taitung Naruwan Galaxy Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in Taitung City, offering fabulous amenities and great views of the surrounding area.

What to Buy in Changbin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Changbin is famous for its handmade pottery. Visitors can purchase beautiful, locally-made teapots and other pottery items.
  • Kavalan Whiskey: This award-winning whiskey is distilled in Yilan County, only a short drive from Changbin.

Local Culture and Customs of Changbin

Changbin has a rich cultural history, influenced by the local indigenous people and the Chinese settlers. Visitors can take part in local cultural festivals and traditions, such as the Harvest Festival, where locals celebrate the harvest season by offering traditional foods and performing dances.

Best Time to Visit Changbin

The best time to visit Changbin is from late spring to early autumn (April to October). The weather is pleasant during this time, and travelers can enjoy numerous outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changbin

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the traditional food and beverages.
  • Visit the local museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Changbin

Changbin is a safe place for visitors, and crime rates are generally low. However, travelers should still take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Changbin

Healthcare facilities in Changbin are of high quality, and visitors can seek medical assistance at the local hospitals or clinics. However, travelers should still take precautions to avoid dehydration and heat stroke during summer months as temperatures can be high.

Getting to Changbin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Changbin is easily accessible by air, bus, or train. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Changbin

  • Taitung Airport (TTT)

Bus Stations Near Changbin

  • Taitung Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: Visitors can take a train from Taipei City or Kaohsiung City and reach Taitung City, which is only 30 minutes away from Changbin.
  • Car: Visitors can also rent a car and explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Changbin

Changbin is a small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. Visitors can also take taxis or ride-share services for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Changbin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taitung City: This vibrant city is famous for its fantastic night markets and cultural attractions.
  • Green Island: This stunning volcanic island is located off the coast of Taitung and is famous for its coral reefs and crystal-clear waters.
  • Lanyu Island: Another island off the coast of Taitung, Lanyu is renowned for its traditional Tao culture and beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Changbin is a fantastic destination for anyone looking for stunning natural scenery, rich cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors will have all the information they need to plan their perfect trip to this hidden gem in Taiwan.
