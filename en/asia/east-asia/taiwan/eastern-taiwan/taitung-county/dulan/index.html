Introduction to Dulan

Dulan is a small Aboriginal township located in Taitung County, on the east coast of Taiwan. The area is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage and laid-back lifestyle. With its pristine beaches, lush forests, awe-inspiring mountains, and vibrant arts scene, Dulan offers visitors a unique and memorable experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top things to do in Dulan, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, hiking routes, local cuisine, accommodations, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Dulan

Whether you’re seeking cultural enlightenment or an outdoor adventure, Dulan has much to offer. Below is a list of the top places to visit in Dulan, organized into two categories: culturally important attractions, and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dulan Sugar Factory

One of the most popular tourist attractions in Dulan is the Dulan Sugar Factory, a former sugar-processing plant that has been converted into an art and cultural center. The factory features exhibitions space, art galleries, and a museum that highlights the area’s sugar production history.

Old Dulan Village

Old Dulan Village is an ancient settlement of the Amis tribe, offering a glimpse into the traditional way of life of the local Aboriginal people. Visitors can see the thatched-roof houses, participate in cultural activities, and learn about the tribe’s rich rituals, beliefs, and customs.

Taitung Railway Art Village

The Taitung Railway Art Village is a beautifully restored railway station that now serves as an art center. It showcases contemporary art by local and international artists and hosts cultural events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Dulan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

WaGaLiGong Surf Hostel and Boardshop

Surfing is one of the most popular activities in Dulan, and WaGaLiGong Surf Hostel and Boardshop is the perfect place to experience it. They offer accommodation for surfers and non-surfers alike, surfing lessons, board rentals, and a range of other outdoor activities.  

Dulan Mountain

Dulan Mountain, also known as the “Sleeping Beauty,” is one of the most iconic landmarks in Dulan, offering a picturesque backdrop for the town. It is a popular spot for hiking and camping, with several trails leading to its summit.

Jinzun Beach

Jinzun Beach is a beautiful stretch of golden sand, known for its picturesque surfing waves, clear blue waters, and stunning sunsets. For surfers, it is famed for its warm water, consistent waves, and year-round surf conditions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dulan

Dulan has plenty of hiking and biking routes for taking in its stunning natural beauty. Below are some popular routes:

The Old Taitung Railway Route

The Old Taitung Railway Route is an easy and scenic cycling and hiking trail that runs from the Taitung Railway Station to Dulan, passing through traditional Aboriginal villages, small towns, and lush farmlands.

Shenmu Trail

The Shenmu Trail is a challenging hiking route that offers stunning views of Dulan’s mountain ranges, forests, and waterfalls. The trail is 6 km long and takes around 3-4 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Dulan: Activities and Tours

Besides the cultural and outdoor attractions mentioned above, there are plenty of other things to do in Dulan. Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy:

Gouding Ropeway

Gouding Ropeway is a scenic cable car ride that takes visitors over the lush forests of Dulan Mountain, offering breathtaking views of the town and the Pacific coast.

Dulan Sunrise Tour

The Dulan Sunrise Tour is a popular tour that takes visitors to the top of Dulan Mountain, from where they can see the sunrise over the Pacific Ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dulan

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring Dulan and the surrounding area:

Day 1: Arrive in Taitung

Arrive in Taitung and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the town and its attractions, such as the National Museum of Prehistory and the Taitung Railway Art Village.

Day 2: Discover Dulan’s Art Scene

Visit the Dulan Sugar Factory and check out the art exhibitions and galleries. Spend the afternoon exploring the Taitung City Forest Park and the Tiehua Music Village.

Day 3: Hiking and Surfing in Dulan

Hike the Shenmu Trail, and then head to WaGaLiGong Surf Hostel and Boardshop for a surfing lesson and a cold beer.

Day 4: Relax and Enjoy Beach Time

Spend the day relaxing on Jinzun Beach, swimming in the clear waters, and soaking up the sun.

Day 5: Explore the Aboriginal Culture of Dulan

Visit the ancient village of Old Dulan and participate in traditional Aboriginal activities, such as weaving and drum-making.

Day 6: Take a Trip on the Gouding Ropeway

Take the Gouding Ropeway to the top of Dulan Mountain and see the town and the Pacific Ocean from above. In the evening, enjoy a sunset dinner on the beach.

Day 7: Depart from Taitung

Check out of your hotel and depart from Taitung, taking with you memories of your amazing trip to Dulan.

Food and Drink Scene of Dulan

Dulan offers visitors a range of delicious, locally sourced and creative foods that reflect the unique cultural heritage of its inhabitants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Bawan

Bawan is a traditional Aboriginal dish made of glutinous rice stuffed with minced pork, mushrooms, and bamboo shoots, served in a thick soup.

Niou Rou Mian

Niou Rou Mian is a Taiwanese beef noodle soup, with tender meat, rich broth, and chewy handmade noodles.

Local Beverages

Shoushan Coffee

Shoushan Coffee is a specialty coffee grown on the nearby Shoushan Mountain. It has a rich flavor and a smooth, nutty taste.

Taiwan Beer

Taiwan Beer is a refreshing and crisp lager that is popular throughout Taiwan.

Where to Stay in Dulan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dulan has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious boutique hotels. Here are some recommendations:

Gigasan Native Garden

Gigasan Native Garden is a beautiful guesthouse with a Taiwanese Aboriginal theme, offering comfortable rooms, stunning gardens, and excellent service.

Dulan Backpacker

Dulan Backpacker is a budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Dulan, with clean and cozy rooms, a friendly atmosphere, and a laid-back style.

What to Buy in Dulan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dulan is known for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, offering visitors a range of unique and creative souvenirs and gifts. Here are some recommendations:

Handmade Knick-Knacks

Dulan is full of artists and craftsmen who create handmade ceramics, jewelry, and woven items. These unique and beautiful products are perfect to bring home as gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Dulan

Dulan has a rich cultural heritage, with a history spanning thousands of years of Aboriginal heritage, and more recently, Taiwanese culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect local traditions and customs, especially when visiting Aboriginal communities.

Best Time to Visit Dulan

Dulan is a year-round destination, with different seasons offering different experiences. The best time to visit is from March to June and from September to December, when the weather is mild, and the outdoor conditions are perfect for hiking, surfing, and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dulan

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Pick up after yourself and keep the environment clean

Don’t:

  • Wear bathing suits in public areas
  • Make noise after hours
  • Disrespect the local religion and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Dulan

Dulan is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions as they would in any other destination. Be sure to secure your valuables and belongings, avoid walking alone late at night, and take care when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Dulan

Taiwan has excellent healthcare facilities, but visitors should carry adequate medical insurance and be prepared for any medical emergencies. Be sure to protect yourself against mosquito bites by wearing long-sleeved clothing and using insect repellents.

Getting to Dulan: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to Dulan is by taking the train or bus to Taitung City, and then taking a taxi or rental car to Dulan.

Airports Near Dulan

The nearest airport to Dulan is Taitung Airport, which is serviced by domestic airlines. International visitors can fly to Taipei or Kaohsiung and then take a domestic flight to Taitung.

Bus Stations Near Dulan

Taitung Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Dulan, and it has frequent connections with other major cities in Taiwan.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore Dulan and its surroundings independently.

How to Get Around in Dulan

Dulan is a small town, and most attractions are within easy walking distance. Visitors can also take taxis or rent bicycles or motor scooters to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Dulan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dulan is located in a beautiful area of Taiwan, surrounded by other fantastic destinations worth exploring. Some ideas for nearby excursions include:

  • Hualien City and Taroko National Park: Known for its stunning natural landscapes, Taroko National Park is a must-visit attraction in Taiwan.
  • Whale watching in Taitung: Visitors can take a boat trip to see the dolphins and whales in the Pacific Ocean.
  • Green Island: This small island off the coast of Taitung is a great place to snorkel, dive, and explore.

Conclusion

Dulan is a hidden gem in Taiwan, offering visitors a unique and memorable experience. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, there’s something for everyone in this friendly and welcoming town. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan your next trip to Dulan and explore all the wonders it has to offer.
