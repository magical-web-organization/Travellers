Introduction to Chishang

Located in Taitung County in Eastern Taiwan, Chishang is a charming rural township that boasts breathtaking natural scenery, lively local culture, and unique food culture. Known for its stunning rice paddies, picturesque countryside, and numerous hiking and biking trails, Chishang is a must-visit destination for travelers who seek unforgettable outdoor adventures and experience authentic Taiwanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Chishang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chishang Old Street: A historical street that dates back to the Japanese colonial era, where visitors can admire the traditional architecture and shop for local souvenirs and snacks.
  • Chishang Catholic Church: A beautiful church with a charming red brick exterior and stained glass windows that is a rare find in rural Taiwan.
  • Donghe Bridge: An iconic suspension bridge that spans across Beinan River, offering a stunning view of the surrounding natural scenery.

Natural Attractions of Chishang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mr. Brown Avenue: A picturesque road that earned its name from a popular Taiwanese coffee brand that filmed a commercial here, offering a scenic view of the lush green rice paddies.
  • Dapo Lake: A serene lake surrounded by dense forests that is a popular spot for boating and fishing.
  • Guanshan Waterfront Park: A peaceful park with a beautiful lake and a relaxing atmosphere ideal for a picnic or leisurely stroll.
  • Chishang Green Tunnel: A scenic route lined with towering bamboo and shade trees that form a natural tunnel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chishang

  • Luye Gaotai: A popular paragliding site with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and coastline.
  • Fenghuang Waterfall Trail: A moderate trail that leads to a beautiful waterfall and stunning views of the surrounding valley.
  • Chishang Donghe cycling trail: A scenic bike trail that spans over 20 km, offering beautiful views of rice paddies, countryside villages, and the stunning coastline.

Best Things to Do in Chishang: Activities and Tours

  • Take a hot air balloon ride during the annual Chishang International Balloon Festival.
  • Experience a traditional rice harvest and learn about the hard work and art of farming from local farmers.
  • Take part in a rice-themed cooking class and learn how to cook delicious Taiwanese dishes using locally grown rice.
  • Rent a motorbike and cruise along the scenic routes around Chishang.

Food and Drink Scene of Chishang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chishang rice: A fragrant and flavorful strain of rice that is locally grown and served in many restaurants.
  • Rice milk: A refreshing drink made from locally grown rice that is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Sticky rice balls: A traditional Taiwanese dessert made from glutinous rice and filled with sweet or savory filling.

Local Beverages

  • Taipower beer: A popular beer brand that is produced in Taitung County and enjoyed by locals.
  • Shaoxing wine: A Chinese rice wine that is widely available in Taiwan and often used in cooking.

Where to Stay in Chishang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Slow Time B&B: A cozy and charming guesthouse that is located close to the Chishang rice paddies and offers stunning views of the surrounding nature.
  • Skyline B&B: A modern and stylish guesthouse that provides a luxurious stay with breathtaking views of the mountains and rivers.
  • Tinshed Hotel: A unique hotel with a contemporary design that offers a peaceful and relaxing stay in the heart of Chishang.

What to Buy in Chishang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chishang rice: A must-buy souvenir that is highly praised for its unique taste and aroma.
  • Handmade pottery: Traditional pottery crafts that are made by local artists and unique to Chishang.
  • Taipower beer: A great gift for beer enthusiasts and a unique souvenir of Eastern Taiwan.

Local Culture and Customs of Chishang

Chishang prides itself on its strong agricultural roots and the role of rice farming in shaping the local culture. Visitors can witness the hard work and dedication of local farmers as they cultivate rice paddies and learn about traditional rice harvest techniques. Chishang also has a rich history and culture, influenced by various ethnic groups such as the Bunun, Chaozhou, and Hakka.

Best Time to Visit Chishang

The best time to visit Chishang is during the fall, between September and November, when the rice paddies turn into golden waves and offer stunning views. The annual Chishang International Balloon Festival, usually held in June or November, is also an excellent opportunity to witness the beauty of Chishang from a birds-eye view.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chishang

Do:

  • Explore the countryside and appreciate the natural beauty of Chishang.
  • Learn about the hard work and art of rice farming from local farmers.
  • Try local cuisine and support small local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the rice paddies and countryside.
  • Disrespect local culture and customs.
  • Take photographs of the locals without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chishang

Chishang is a relatively safe area with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping their valuables safe and not walking alone at night.

Health in Chishang

Chishang has clean and safe food and water, but visitors should still take necessary health precautions such as drinking bottled water and wearing sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Chishang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chishang

The nearest airports to Chishang are Taitung Airport and Kaohsiung International Airport, both of which offer domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Chishang

Chishang Bus Station is located in the center of the township and offers bus connections to nearby cities such as Taitung, Hualien, and Taipei.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, take a taxi, or hire a private driver to get to Chishang from nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Chishang

The best way to get around Chishang is by renting a motorbike or a bicycle to explore the countryside and scenic routes. Visitors can also take buses or taxis to get to nearby attractions and destinations.

Where to Go Next from Chishang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taitung: A lively coastal city with a bustling night market, stunning beaches, and excellent local cuisine.
  • Hualien: A nature-lover’s paradise with the majestic Taroko National Park, hot springs, and charming night markets.
  • Kenting: A tropical seaside resort with white sandy beaches, gorgeous coral reefs, and a lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Chishang offers a unique blend of natural beauty, local culture, and excellent cuisine that make it an ideal destination for those who want to escape the city and experience rural Taiwan. Whether you’re exploring the rice paddies, cycling along scenic routes, or indulging in local food, Chishang is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories.
