Introduction to Yilan

Yilan is a beautiful county located in Eastern Taiwan, widely renowned for its stunning natural scenery, invigorating hot springs, and the unique cultural heritage it has carefully preserved over the centuries. It is a destination that’s perfect for travelers looking for an authentic experience, and it has so much to offer.

The location of Yilan provides a perfect base for outdoor enthusiasts to explore some of Taiwan’s most breathtaking natural gems, from the dramatic Taroko Gorge to the warm, crystal-clear waters of Jiaoshi Hot Springs. In addition, there’s the fascinating cultural story of the county that has been expertly kept alive with fascinating museums and an array of historic and cultural sites to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Yilan

If you are wondering how to spend your time in Yilan, don’t worry! There’s plenty to see and do.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yilan has a rich cultural history, and here are some places you should add to the list:

  • Yilan Traditional Art Center: If you want to immerse yourself in the culture of Yilan, this is the place to go. The center showcases the traditional art of Yilan, including calligraphy, folk art, puppet theatre, and more.
  • Luodong Forestry Culture Park: This place is unique as it showcases the history and development of the forestry industry in Yilan. Visitors can learn about the sustainable forest products of Yilan and see how they’ve been used over the years.
  • Jimmy Square: This spot is perfect for Instagram lovers because of its unique and colorful architecture. It’s a memorial statue of one of Yilan’s most famous sons, Jimmy Liao, who is a well-known illustrator of children’s books.

Natural Attractions of Yilan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yilan is renowned for its stunning natural scenery, and a visit here is not complete without exploring its natural wonders. Here are some places to put on your list:

  • Wufengqi Waterfall: It’s one of Yilan’s most iconic attractions, and it’s easy to see why. The waterfalls from a 10-meter high cliff, and the area around it is filled with picturesque nature.
  • Jiaoxi Hot Springs: A perfect spot to escape from the city and relax your muscles. Jiaoxi is one of the most famous hot springs in Taiwan, and the natural scenery around it is breathtaking. You can also stroll around the Lanyang Museum nearby.
  • Taipingshan National Forest Recreation Area: Located over a mountain, this spot is the perfect place to enjoy the fresh air. It’s a fantastic spot for hiking, with its array of trails, and spectacular panoramas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yilan

Yilan’s natural beauty is best explored by foot, and the many hiking and biking trails in the area provide an excellent means of doing so. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Yilan Coastal Route: This 130 km round trip route is perfect for cycling enthusiasts who want to take in some of Taiwan’s most picturesque coastal scenery. The route is dedicated to cyclists, and there are many rest areas and bike shops along the way.
  • Caoling Historic Trail: This trail is both historically significant and challenging. The hike is about 8.5 km round trip and takes you through a historic route that dates back to the Qing Dynasty. The trail passes through some beautiful mountains and has many rewarding views.
  • Miaoli Lishan Trail: This hiking route is a lesser-known option but one that should not be missed. The road has many winding turns and rocky paths but provides stunning views of the surrounding area. The trail starts at the Lishan visitor center, and it’s highly recommended to start early to avoid crowds.

Best Things to Do in Yilan: Activities and Tours

Yilan is a destination that’s perfect for travelers looking for an authentic experience. Here are some things to keep in mind as you plan your trip:

  • Take a Dongshan River Eco Tour: The tour takes you deep into Yilan’s wilderness, where you can learn about the ecosystem and spot some wildlife. It’s a fun activity to do if you’ve been in the city for too long.
  • Visit Taiwan’s largest orchid garden: This beautiful garden is located on the outskirts of Zhongli, and it’s home to thousands of orchids of different colors and varieties. A walk around the garden is sure to soothe your senses.
  • Experience Chinese medicine: Yilan has a rich history in Chinese medicine, and it’s the perfect place to indulge in some traditional healing. You can either visit a Chinese medical center or join a guided tour and learn how to make some Chinese medicine.

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Yilan.

Food and Drink Scene of Yilan

Yilan has a reputation for delicious cuisine, and below are some of the must-try dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yilan Hakka Spareribs: This dish is a great representation of Hakka cuisine. The pork spareribs are cooked with garlic and a range of spices, making them tender, juicy, and flavorful.
  • Luodong Night Market Snacks: A trip to Yilan isn’t complete without indulging in the yummy street food of Luodong Night Market. From perfectly fried chicken to savory dumplings and sweet desserts, you’ll find everything here.
  • Mountain water rice: There’s nothing more satisfying than having a bowl of rice in Taiwan, and Yilan’s mountain water rice is famous throughout Taiwan. This type of rice is grown in the hills around Yilan, and it has a unique flavor that’s worth trying.

Local Beverages

  • Yi Lan Cha: Yilan has become famous for their “Keemun” black tea. The tea is a high grade black tea that can only be made from the tea leaves of Yilan county.
  • Famous Yilan Soya Milk: A refreshing drink for those who love something sweet. Yilan soya milk is famous throughout Taiwan for its rich, smooth taste. It’s perfect for a pick-me-up during the day.
  • Taiwanese beer: Taiwan is becoming famous for their great selection of beer, and many breweries can be found in Yilan. Try some of their lesser-known brands and enjoy the taste of Taiwan’s successful beer scene.

Where to Stay in Yilan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yilan offers a variety of accommodation options for all budgets. Below are some recommendations:

  • Hotel de L’EAU Spa: This hotel is perfect for those who want to relax and spoil themselves with a spa treatment. The hotel has hot springs and a beautiful pool on site. It’s perfect for a romantic trip.
  • LUODONG MINIMALIST COTTAGE: This hostel is an affordable option for travelers who want to stay near Luodong Night Market. The rooms are clean and comfortable, and the staff is very friendly.
  • The Place Yilan: This accommodation is perfect for those who want to stay close to nature. The hotel is located on a hill, and the views from the rooms are breathtaking.

What to Buy in Yilan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yilan is a great place to buy some unique souvenirs to take back home, so make sure to visit these stores:

  • Yilan Handmade Soap: Made with all-natural ingredients, these soaps are perfect for someone who loves nature and skincare. Choose from a range of fragrances and designs.
  • Sanxing Scallion Oil Noodles: This is a unique gift for your foodie friends. Scallion oil noodles are a local specialty of Yilan, and you can find them in many stores throughout the city. They’re delicious and easy to pack up and bring home.
  • Yilan Embroidery: Yilan embroidery is a famous representation of local craftsmanship. The intricate design and careful attention to detail make these works of art worth buying. You can find embroidery products ranging from bags and phone cases to wall hangings.

Local Culture and Customs of Yilan

Yilan has a rich cultural history, and to get the most out of your trip, it’s worth learning about some of its customs and traditions. Here are some tips:

  • Respect the elders: In Taiwanese culture, respect for elders is very important. Always greet older people before speaking and show proper manners.
  • Bring a gift when visiting someone: It is customary to bring a small gift when visiting someone in Taiwan. It can be something simple like a box of cookies or a small souvenir from your home country.
  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home: In Taiwanese culture, it is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home. Keep this in mind, especially if you have a lot of outdoor activities planned during your trip.

Best Time to Visit Yilan

Yilan is a destination that can be visited year-round. However, the best time to visit is during the autumn months of September to November. The weather during these months is perfect for exploring, with temperatures averaging 22°C. In addition, Yilan hosts the International Children’s Folklore & Folkgame Festival during this time, showcasing the unique culture of Yilan to the world.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yilan

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Yilan:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering a temple or a traditional Taiwanese home.
  • Bring a small gift when visiting someone’s home.
  • Learn a few phrases in Mandarin. Locals are friendly and will appreciate the effort.

Don’t:

  • Touch Buddha statues or any other religious artifacts.
  • Smoke in public areas or inside buildings.
  • Be too loud or aggressive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yilan

Yilan is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. However, it’s worth keeping the following tips in mind:

  • Be aware of traffic. Taiwanese traffic can be chaotic and unpredictable, so always be aware when crossing roads.
  • Take caution when drinking alcohol. The Taiwanese may be known for their love of beer, but it’s important always to drink responsibly.
  • Keep your valuables close. Like any other destination, it’s worth keeping an eye on your personal belongings.

Health in Yilan

Yilan is a destination that is quite safe when it comes to health. Taiwan’s healthcare system is renowned for its quality and efficiency. In case of emergencies, the country has adequate facilities and hospitals with English-speaking staff.

Getting to Yilan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yilan

The nearest airport to Yilan is Taipei Songshan Airport (TSA), located in Taipei. It takes about an hour and a half to travel from the airport to Yilan by car or by train.

Bus Stations Near Yilan

If you are traveling to Yilan from Taipei or other parts of Taiwan, the bus is one of the most convenient options. The two major bus stations in Taipei for buses to Yilan are Taipei Bus Station and Nangang Bus Station.

Other Options

Yilan is well connected by trains, and there are many stations throughout the city. Taking a train is a great option if you are coming from Taipei, Hualien, or other parts of Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Yilan

The best way to get around in Yilan is by renting a car. It is also possible to take the bus or train, but it’s not as convenient. Taxis and bike rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Yilan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re in Yilan for a while and want to explore other parts of Taiwan, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Hualien: A perfect destination for nature lovers. The famous Taroko Gorge is located here, and there are many other stunning hikes to explore.
  • Keelung: Keelung is a seaside city famous for its seafood and bustling markets. It’s the perfect spot to indulge in some fresh seafood and explore the urban landscape.
  • Jiufen: A charming mountaintop village, just an hour’s drive from Yilan. The village is famous for its narrow, winding streets, tea shops, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Yilan is a hidden gem in Taiwan that offers visitors the perfect combination of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture vulture, this destination has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be ready to explore what Yilan has to offer like a local!
