Introduction to Luodong

Located in Yilan County on the east coast of Taiwan, Luodong is a scenic town with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. The town is known for its vibrant night market, historical landmarks, and outdoor recreation opportunities. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and temples or hiking through lush forests and enjoying panoramic views, Luodong offers an array of experiences for travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Luodong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Luodong Forestry Culture Park: Learn about the history of Taiwan’s forestry industry and explore traditional wooden buildings at this museum and park.
  • Luodong Cultural Working House: Discover the traditional art and crafts of Taiwan, including calligraphy, embroidery, and pottery, at this hands-on cultural center.
  • Luodong Sports Park: This park is a popular spot for families with sports fields, playgrounds, and a swimming pool.

Natural Attractions of Luodong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wufengqi Scenic Area: Hike through lush forests and enjoy stunning coastal views at this natural reserve.
  • Luodong Night Market: Experience the buzz of Taiwan’s famed night market with an array of street food, souvenirs, and entertainment.
  • Luo Dong Sports Park: This park includes a skate park, a basketball and volleyball court, and an archery range.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luodong

  • Yilan Green Onion Field Hiking Trail: This scenic trail takes you through lush farmland and pastures to enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Dafu Mountain Forest Recreation Area: Ideal for families with kids, this park features easy trails and a playground surrounded by lush greenery and a stream.
  • Dongshan River Bike Trail: Rent a bike and cruise along the river on this relaxed path that passes by temples, bridges, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Luodong: Activities and Tours

  • Fu An Temple: Built in 1796, this temple is one of the oldest and most historic temples in Taiwan.
  • Paoma Historic Trail: Take this scenic trail that passes by historic sites, tea plantations, and scenic views.
  • Traditional Arts and Memorial Hall of Yilan: Discover the traditional arts and crafts of Taiwan at this historic center.

Food and Drink Scene of Luodong

Must-Try Dishes

  • Luodong Night Market Snacks: Try local delicacies such as stinky tofu, oyster omelets, and egg pancakes.
  • Dongshan River Aborigine Tribe Cuisine: Feast on spicy and flavorful dishes unique to Taiwan’s aboriginal culture.
  • Bigger Burger: Sink your teeth into juicy burgers with local ingredients such as Taiwan Beef, pickles, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Taiwanese Tea: Savor the taste of Taiwan’s famous tea culture with a cup of local oolong or green tea.
  • Bubble Milk Tea: Indulge in the sweet, creamy, and chewy goodness of Taiwan’s most famous beverage, made with milk and tapioca pearls.
  • Taiwanese Fruit Juice: Quench your thirst with fresh and delicious juices made from tropical fruits such as mango, papaya, and pineapple.

Where to Stay in Luodong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Silks Place Yilan: This 5-star hotel offers luxurious accommodations and stunning mountain views
  • Luodong Guest House: This affordable guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and great location close to attractions.
  • Sophie’s Hostel: This lively hostel offers clean rooms and a fun atmosphere for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Luodong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Green Onion Pancakes: These savory pancakes are a staple food in Luodong, and make for a tasty and easy-to-pack souvenir.
  • Taiwanese Tea: Take home a pack of high-quality oolong or green tea from one of the local tea shops to share with your loved ones.
  • Aboriginal Crafts: Shop for unique handicrafts such as woven baskets, pottery, and wood carvings made by Taiwan’s aboriginal tribes.

Local Culture and Customs of Luodong

Luodong has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the diverse history and identity of Taiwan. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as taking off their shoes before entering someone’s home, and avoiding pointing with their fingers. Learning a few basic Chinese phrases, such as hello and thank you, can go a long way in terms of showing appreciation for the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Luodong

The best time to visit Luodong is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. Avoid visiting during the typhoon season, which typically runs from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luodong

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture
  • Try local foods and beverages
  • Pack comfortable walking shoes and outdoor gear for hiking and biking

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Touch other people’s belongings
  • Wear revealing clothing in temples or other religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Luodong

Luodong is generally considered a safe and welcoming town for travelers. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Luodong

Tap water in Luodong is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne diseases and take necessary precautions, such as applying mosquito repellent and wearing loose-fitting clothing.

Getting to Luodong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Luodong

Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport is the closest major airport to Luodong and is located approximately 100 kilometers away. The airport is easily accessible via public transportation or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Luodong

The Luodong Bus Station is located in the center of town and offers direct connections to destinations throughout Taiwan.

Other Options

It’s also possible to reach Luodong by train from Taipei, which takes approximately 2 hours.

How to Get Around in Luodong

Luodong is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by renting a bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances or for those who prefer not to bike or walk.

Where to Go Next from Luodong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yilan City: Take a day trip to explore this cozy city rich in art, culture, and shopping.
  • Jiaoxi Hot Springs: Relax and rejuvenate in natural hot springs located in this charming town just a short drive from Luodong.
  • Taitung: Take a scenic train ride to this neighboring county known for its scenic beaches, mountains, and indigenous culture.

Conclusion

Luodong offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor recreation opportunities that make it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic and memorable Taiwan experience. From hiking through lush forests to indulging in local food and drink, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Plan your trip to Luodong today and prepare to be amazed by all that this hidden gem has to offer.
