Introduction to Dongshan

Dongshan is a small township located in Yilan County, in Eastern Taiwan. It is surrounded by picturesque mountains and boasts a unique blend of traditional and modern Taiwanese culture. Its peaceful way of life, coupled with its scenic beauty, make it a must-visit destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Dongshan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dongshan River Water Park

The Dongshan River Water Park, located in the heart of Dongshan, is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. The park features a variety of water activities, including swimming, boating, and fishing. It also features stunning vistas of the surrounding mountains.

Luodong Forestry Culture Garden

The Luodong Forestry Culture Garden is a museum dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of the forestry industry in Taiwan. Visitors can learn about the tools and techniques used to work with wood, and can even try their hand at the craft themselves.

Luodong Night Market

The Luodong Night Market is a bustling hub of activity located in the heart of Dongshan. It features a variety of street vendors, selling everything from local delicacies to handmade crafts. It is a must-visit destination for foodies and culture enthusiasts alike.

Natural Attractions of Dongshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wufengqi Waterfall

The Wufengqi Waterfall is a stunning natural wonder located in the mountains around Dongshan. The waterfall is easily accessible via a short hike, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Dongshan River Bike Path

The Dongshan River Bike Path is a scenic route that runs along the Dongshan River, offering visitors the chance to experience the natural beauty of the area by bike. The path is well-maintained and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and nearby ocean.

Northeast Coast National Scenic Area

The Northeast Coast National Scenic Area is a protected area located along the coast of Dongshan. It features a variety of natural wonders, including volcanic rock formations and stunning vistas of the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dongshan

Baimi Trail

The Baimi Trail is a challenging hiking route that takes visitors through some of the most remote and beautiful areas of Dongshan. The route is marked by a series of rugged trails and steep inclines, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Yuemeikeng Trail

The Yuemeikeng Trail is a moderate hiking route that takes visitors through some of the most stunning natural scenery in Dongshan. The route features diverse terrain, including bamboo forests, lush valleys, and rocky peaks, and offers a great opportunity to experience the unique flora and fauna of the area.

Best Things to Do in Dongshan: Activities and Tours

Dongshan River Rafting

Dongshan River Rafting is a popular activity for visitors looking for an adrenaline rush. The river features class III and IV rapids, making for an exciting and unforgettable adventure.

Seafood Tours

Dongshan is famous for its seafood, and there are a variety of tours available for visitors looking to sample the local delicacies. These tours offer visitors the chance to experience traditional Taiwanese seafood dishes, as well as learn about the history and culture of the local fishing industry.

Food and Drink Scene of Dongshan

Must-Try Dishes

Lu Rou Fan

Lu Rou Fan is a traditional Taiwanese dish made with braised pork belly and rice. It is a staple of Dongshan’s food scene and is a must-try for visitors looking to experience the area’s culinary culture.

Mian Xian

Mian Xian is a popular noodle dish in Dongshan made with handmade noodles, mushrooms, and meat. It is a comforting and savory dish that is perfect for a rainy day.

Local Beverages

Honey Tea

Honey Tea is a popular beverage in Dongshan made with locally sourced honey and black tea. It is refreshing and sweet, and is a great way to cool off on a hot day.

Malt Beer

Malt Beer is a locally brewed beer that is popular with locals and tourists alike. It is a light and crisp beer that is perfect for sipping on a warm day.

Where to Stay in Dongshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silk Tree Hotel

The Silk Tree Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Dongshan. It offers spacious rooms with stunning views of the surrounding mountains, as well as a variety of on-site amenities including a spa and restaurant.

Yilan Dongshan Sakura B&B

The Yilan Dongshan Sakura B&B is a cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Dongshan. It features comfortable rooms with traditional Taiwanese decor, as well as a variety of on-site amenities including a garden and bike rentals.

What to Buy in Dongshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tea

Dongshan is famous for its tea, and there are a variety of tea shops throughout the area selling high-quality loose-leaf tea. Visitors can sample the different varieties and bring home their favorite flavors as a souvenir.

Handmade Crafts

Dongshan is home to a variety of skilled craftsmen, producing everything from pottery to woodworking. Visitors can browse the different shops and markets throughout the area, picking up unique handmade crafts to bring home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Dongshan

Dongshan is a deeply traditional area with its own unique customs and culture. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and be respectful of local customs and traditions. It is also worth noting that Mandarin Chinese is the primary language spoken in the area, and visitors may encounter some language barriers when communicating with locals.

Best Time to Visit Dongshan

The best time to visit Dongshan is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Visitors should avoid the summer months, as the weather can be quite hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dongshan

Do:

  • Do try local delicacies and sample the area’s traditional food and drink.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Do learn about local customs and culture, and be respectful of these traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t visit religious sites in revealing or inappropriate attire.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dongshan

Dongshan is generally a safe area, but visitors should take care when participating in outdoor activities, and should follow any posted safety guidelines. It is also important to be aware of local traffic laws when walking or biking in the area.

Health in Dongshan

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Dongshan. It is also important to take precautions against mosquito bites, as Dengue fever is a potential risk in the area.

Getting to Dongshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dongshan

The nearest major airport to Dongshan is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, located approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Dongshan

Dongshan is served by a variety of bus stations, including the Luodong Bus Station and Yilan Bus Station. Visitors can easily travel to Dongshan from these stations via local bus or taxi.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Dongshan via private car or bike, or by booking a tour that includes transportation to and from the area.

How to Get Around in Dongshan

Visitors can easily navigate Dongshan via local bus, taxi, or bike rental. Bikes are the preferred method of transportation for many visitors, as they offer a great way to explore the area’s natural beauty and scenic views.

Where to Go Next from Dongshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yilan City

Yilan City is located just a short bus or train ride away from Dongshan, and offers a variety of attractions including museums, parks, and shopping districts. It is a great destination for visitors looking to experience more of Taiwanese culture and cuisine.

Wuling Farm

Wuling Farm is a scenic nature reserve located in the mountains near Dongshan. It offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and birdwatching, and is a great destination for visitors looking to experience the natural beauty of Taiwan.

Conclusion

Dongshan is a hidden gem in Taiwan, offering visitors the chance to experience traditional Taiwanese culture and stunning natural landscapes. From hiking and biking to visiting historical sites and indulging in local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this beautiful area. Plan your trip today to experience all that Dongshan has to offer.
