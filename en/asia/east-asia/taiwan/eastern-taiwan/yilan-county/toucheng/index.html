Introduction to Toucheng

Toucheng is a small coastal town located in Yilan County, in Eastern Taiwan. It is known for its natural beauty, including stunning beaches, lush forests, and picturesque mountains. The town is also home to several significant historical sites, offering a fascinating look into Taiwan’s past. From exciting outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks, there is something for everyone in Toucheng.

List of Places to Visit in Toucheng

If you’re planning a trip to Toucheng, here are some of the must-see attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Toucheng Old Street is a bustling marketplace that has preserved much of Toucheng’s traditional architecture. Visitors can stroll down the street and sample various snacks and beverages while browsing local crafts and souvenirs.
  • Toucheng Cultural Center is a museum showcasing the history and culture of Toucheng. The center features several exhibitions that explore the town’s indigenous heritage, as well as its connection to the sea.
  • Beiguan Tidal Park is a scenic park that houses several historical sites. Visitors can see the ruins of Beiguan Tidal Fort, a Qing Dynasty defensive structure, as well as the Beiguan Mosque, one of Taiwan’s oldest mosques.

Natural Attractions of Toucheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wai’ao Beach is one of Toucheng’s most beautiful and popular destinations. The long, sandy beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or relaxing with a book.
  • Toucheng Farm is a large agricultural area that is home to a variety of crops, such as rice, bananas, and pineapples. Visitors can explore the farm, take a guided tour, or even pick their own fruit.
  • Caoling Historic Trail is a scenic hiking trail that spans six kilometers along the narrow ridge of Caoling Mountain. The trail offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toucheng

  • Jiaoxi Bike Trail is a popular cycling route that takes visitors through several small towns and scenic routes, including a beautiful stretch along the Jiaoxi River. The trail is easy enough for beginners but offers enough challenges to keep more experienced riders entertained.
  • Toucheng Stargazing Park is a beautiful park that offers visitors a chance to observe the stars in the dark, clear skies of Toucheng. Visitors can hike up to the viewing platform or even join a guided tour.

Best Things to Do in Toucheng: Activities and Tours

  • Longkang Ecologic Park is a serene park that offers a myriad of activities and attractions, including a bird-watching tower, an insect museum, and several hiking trails.
  • Toucheng Leisure Farm is a beautiful farm that provides visitors with a unique opportunity to get up close and personal with local animals, including cows, chickens, and sheep.
  • Toucheng Wetlands Park is a scenic park that offers a tranquil respite from the hustle and bustle of the city. The park is home to several unique bird species and is ideal for nature lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Toucheng

If you’re planning to spend a week in Toucheng, here is a sample itinerary you can use as a guide:

Day Activities
1 Visit Toucheng Old Street and try some local snacks. Explore Toucheng Cultural Center.
2 Visit Beiguan Tidal Park and explore the historical sites. Take a dip at Wai’ao Beach.
3 Hike along Caoling Historic Trail and enjoy the scenic views.
4 Cycle along Jiaoxi Bike Trail and explore the surrounding small towns.
5 Observe the stars at Toucheng Stargazing Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Toucheng

No trip to Toucheng is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lu Rou Fan is a popular Taiwanese dish of braised pork belly served over rice. The pork is cooked in soy sauce, sugar, and a variety of spices, creating a sweet and savory flavor.
  • Seafood is a staple of Toucheng’s cuisine, thanks to its coastal location. Visitors can expect fresh catches of fish, squid, crab, and other seafood.
  • Suncakes are a popular Taiwanese pastry made with several layers of flaky, buttery crust and a sweet, rich filling.

Local Beverages

  • Yilan Beer is a popular local beer made in Yilan County. Visitors can sample a variety of flavors, including golden ale, amber ale, and dark ale.
  • Baozhong Tea is a light and fragrant tea that is grown in the hills around Toucheng. The tea is known for its pale color and subtle flavors, making it an ideal accompaniment to a meal or a snack.

Where to Stay in Toucheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Toucheng has a range of accommodation options to suit various budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Silks Place Yilan is a luxurious resort with stunning views of the ocean and the mountains. The hotel features several restaurants, a spa, and an indoor/outdoor pool.
  • Toucheng Hostel is a budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Toucheng Old Street. The hostel offers a variety of room types, including dormitories and private rooms.
  • The Must Garden is a charming guesthouse with beautiful gardens and comfortable rooms. The guesthouse is located near several of Toucheng’s historical sites and provides an authentic touch to the town’s stay.

What to Buy in Toucheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Toucheng:

  • Chen Tou Hsiang Sun Cake Shop is one of Toucheng’s most famous pastry shops, trading in the sweet, layered Suncakes. The shop also sells other pastries, such as pineapple cakes and nougat.
  • Dongshan River Water Park is a popular water park that provides fun activities for visitors of all ages. The park sells souvenirs such as waterballs, caps, and towels.
  • Yilan Distillery is a well-known distillery located in Yilan County, producing some of the world’s top whiskeys, including the award-winning Kavalan Whiskey.

Local Culture and Customs of Toucheng

Toucheng has a rich history, and its culture is influenced by its indigenous roots, as well as its connection to the sea. Visitors can experience this fascinating culture through the town’s museums, historical sites, and local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Toucheng

The best time to visit Toucheng is during the spring or autumn when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toucheng

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including seafood, suncakes, and Yilan beer.
  • Embrace the outdoors by hiking, biking, or strolling along the beach.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, and take the time to learn about the town’s history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or in other public areas.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Ignore safety precautions, such as hiking or swimming without proper equipment and guidance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toucheng

Toucheng is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as:

  • Keeping valuables secure, such as in a hotel safe.
  • Being aware of surroundings and not walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Toucheng

Taiwan’s healthcare system is top-notch, and visitors can rest assured that medical care is available if needed. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance and consult with their healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Toucheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Toucheng

Taipei Songshan Airport is the closest airport to Toucheng, located approximately 55 kilometers away. Taoyuan International Airport is also within reasonable distance, with travel time usually around one and half hours.

Bus Stations Near Toucheng

There are several bus stations in Yilan County that provide service to Toucheng, including Yilan Bus Station and Luodong Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take trains to Luodong Station, then bus or taxi to reach Toucheng.

How to Get Around in Toucheng

Visitors can hire a taxi, rent a bicycle or scooter, or take public transport to explore Toucheng and its surrounding areas. Biking is popular due to several trails in the area and allows for a more relaxed pace, while car rentals are another option for those who prefer flexibility and freedom.

Where to Go Next from Toucheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations near Toucheng include:

  • Jiaoxi Township is famous for its thermal springs, and visitors can relax in one of the many public hot springs or private baths.
  • Taipei is Taiwan’s vibrant capital city, offering a range of cultural, artistic, and commercial sites and experiences.
  • Wulai is a scenic mountain town known for its hot springs, waterfalls, and indigenous culture.

Conclusion

Toucheng is a charming town with a lot to offer. From its unique culture to its fascinating history, visitors are sure to enjoy exploring this gem of Eastern Taiwan. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, delicious food or simply a relaxing vacation away from the hustle and bustle of the city, you’ll find it all in Toucheng.
