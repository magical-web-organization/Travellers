Introduction to Yuanshan

Yuanshan is a township located in Yilan County, Taiwan. Its name translates to “Garden Mountain” in Chinese, owing to the town’s lush greenery and natural beauty. The town is situated in the northern part of Taiwan, with Taipei City just a two-hour drive away. It is accessible via bus or train from Taipei, making it an easy day trip for travelers.

Yuanshan is known for its tranquil beauty, old temples, and fantastic views of the surrounding mountains. Its ideal location makes it a wonderful getaway destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Explore the culturally rich and naturally beautiful area of Yuanshan with this comprehensive tour guide.

List of Places to Visit in Yuanshan

There are various places to visit in Yuanshan that cater to different interests. Below is a list of must-visit places in Yuanshan that covers both culturally important attractions and natural sites.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yuanshan Folklore Museum: Also known as the “Little Museum of Yilan”, this museum documents the history and culture of the region through a range of exhibits, including historic photographs, folk music performances, and art pieces by local artists.

  • Chuguo Temple: This temple was built in 1738 and is one of the oldest in Yuanshan. It features a beautiful wooden structure and a collection of traditional Chinese art and calligraphy.

  • Wenlan Temple: This Taoist temple is dedicated to the worship of the God of Literature, Wen Chang, and is believed to bring success in academic pursuits. The temple features intricate carvings and beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Yuanshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yuanshan National Forest Recreation Area: This beautiful forested area is ideal for hiking and camping. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the many walking paths and admire the lush flora and fauna of the region.

  • Pisilian Trail: This 10-kilometer trail is a great option for nature lovers who enjoy hiking. The trail winds through the beautiful countryside, passing by waterfalls and towering trees.

  • Yueh Hai Ching Temple: This temple sits on a beautiful hill overlooking the town of Yuanshan. It features beautiful gardens, traditional Chinese architecture, and sweeping views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yuanshan

Yuanshan is an excellent place for hikers and bikers. Besides the Pisilian Trail mentioned earlier, there are several other beautiful routes to choose from. Some of the most popular hikes and bike trails include:

  • Dongshan River Bike Trail: This 23 kilometer trail starts from the Dongshan River biking track in Yilan and runs to the Lanyang Museum in Toucheng Town. It takes about two and a half hours to complete the entire trail.

  • Pinglin Bike Trail: This 5 kilometer trail is perfect for beginners who want to experience the beautiful countryside of Yuanshan from the saddle of their bikes. The trail is relatively flat and runs along the riverside for a peaceful ride.

  • Guishan Island Bike Trail: This trail is more advanced and runs 45 kilometers in total, taking bikers around the beautiful Guishan Island. The trail is challenging but rewarding, offering stunning views of the sea and the island.

Best Things to Do in Yuanshan: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours available in Yuanshan for visitors. Some of the best things to do in Yuanshan include:

  • Experience traditional crafting: There are several workshops in Yuanshan where visitors can try their hand at traditional crafting techniques. Visitors can learn how to make pottery, dye fabrics, or create their own paper lanterns.

  • Explore the night market: The Yuanshan Night Market is a great place to experience the local food culture. Visitors can enjoy a range of street foods, snacks, and desserts.

  • Visit nearby hot springs: Yuanshan is surrounded by several natural hot springs, making it an excellent location for some rest and relaxation. Visitors can choose from several hot spring resorts and day spas, which offer a range of facilities and treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Yuanshan

Yuanshan is known for its delicious food culture, which is heavily influenced by the ingredients and flavors of Taiwan’s indigenous peoples. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aboriginal Meat Rice: This dish features steamed rice mixed with tender meat cooked in traditional aboriginal seasoning.

  • Yilan Noodles: Made with locally-grown rice flour, Yilan noodles are served in a hot and savory broth and topped with tender meat or seafood.

  • Sticky Rice Dumplings: These sweet and chewy dumplings are stuffed with red beans, sesame seeds, or peanuts, and are popular during festivals and special events.

Local Beverages

  • Taiwanese Oolong Tea: This classic Taiwanese tea is known for its floral and fruity aroma and delicate flavor.

  • Rice Wine: A popular drink among locals, rice wine is distilled from fermented rice and has a sweet and smooth taste.

  • Yilan Green Tea: This tea is grown in the mountainous region of Yuanshan and is known for its fresh and fragrant taste.

Where to Stay in Yuanshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yuanshan has several accommodation options for travelers, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular places to stay in Yuanshan include:

  • Grand Hyatt Taipei: This luxurious hotel features stylish and spacious rooms and suites, as well as a range of amenities such as a spa, fitness center, and several restaurants.

  • Uni-Resort Yilan: This resort features modern rooms and suites, as well as an outdoor pool, tennis courts, and a range of outdoor activities.

  • Cheers Hostel: This hostel offers affordable and comfortable dormitory-style rooms with shared facilities, as well as a cozy lounge and bar.

What to Buy in Yuanshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several items that visitors can buy in Yuanshan as souvenirs or gifts for friends and family. Some of the common local products include:

  • Lanterns: Yuanshan is famous for its lanterns, which are handmade using traditional methods and materials.

  • Pottery: There are several workshops in Yuanshan where visitors can purchase handmade pottery items such as tea cups, plates, and bowls.

  • Yilan Scallion Oil: This spicy condiment is made with locally-sourced ingredients and is a staple in Taiwanese households.

Local Culture and Customs of Yuanshan

Yuanshan is a town steeped in traditions and customs that date back to its early history. Some of the notable cultural customs and traditions include:

  • Lantern Festival: The Lantern Festival is a significant holiday in Yuanshan, where people light and release lanterns to form a beautiful sky full of wishes.

  • Taiwanese Opera: Yuanshan is home to several Taiwanese opera theaters, where visitors can experience this traditional art form.

  • Dragon Boat Festival: This festival is held to commemorate the famous Chinese poet Qu Yuan and is celebrated throughout Taiwan with boat races and traditional meals.

Best Time to Visit Yuanshan

The best time to visit Yuanshan is from March to May and from September to November, during the cooler and drier seasons. This is when visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking without being stifled by the heat and humidity of the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yuanshan

Do:

  • Take part in traditional crafting workshops: Visitors can experience the local culture and learn new crafting techniques by participating in traditional workshops.

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Yuanshan is a town steeped in traditions and customs that date back to its early history. Visitors should respect these traditions and be mindful of local customs.

  • Bring comfortable shoes and clothing: Yuanshan is a great destination for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Visitors should pack comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for these activities.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any illegal activities: Visitors should be mindful of local laws and regulations and avoid engaging in any illegal activities.

  • Disrespect religious sites and artifacts: Yuanshan has several temples and religious sites that are considered sacred by the locals. Visitors should be respectful of these sites and refrain from touching or damaging any artifacts.

  • Litter or damage the environment: Yuanshan is known for its natural beauty, and visitors should take care not to litter or damage the environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yuanshan

Yuanshan is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should always be cautious and vigilant, especially in tourist areas.

Health in Yuanshan

Yuanshan has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should invest in health insurance and carry any necessary prescriptions with them in case of emergencies.

Getting to Yuanshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Yuanshan is relatively easy, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Yuanshan

The nearest airport to Yuanshan is Taipei-Tao Yuan International Airport, which is about an hour and a half away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Yuanshan

There are several bus stations near Yuanshan, including Taipei Bus Station and Taipei City Hall Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Yuanshan by train, with daily services running from Taipei to Yilan.

How to Get Around in Yuanshan

The best way to explore Yuanshan is by renting a bike or car. There are several rental agencies in the town, offering affordable rates for bikes and cars.

Where to Go Next from Yuanshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Yuanshan, including:

  • Beipu Old Street: This famous street is located just a short drive away from Yuanshan and features well-preserved architecture and tasty snacks.

  • Jiaoxi Hot Spring: Jiaoxi Hot Spring is a popular resort town known for its natural hot springs and scenic views.

  • Toucheng Old Street: This historic street is located just a short drive away from Yuanshan and features a range of shops, cafes, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Yuanshan is a great destination for travelers looking to experience Taiwan’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. With a range of activities, attractions, and places to stay, visitors are sure to have a memorable time in this tranquil town in Yilan County.
