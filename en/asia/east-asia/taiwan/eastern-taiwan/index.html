Introduction to Eastern Taiwan

Eastern Taiwan is a hidden gem that offers a plethora of natural and cultural attractions for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and history buffs alike. From towering mountains to pristine beaches, from ancient temples to modern museums, Eastern Taiwan has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this magical part of Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Taiwan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eastern Taiwan is home to some of the most culturally significant sites in Taiwan. Here are some must-visit museums and historical places:

  • National Museum of Prehistory
  • Taitung Railway Art Village
  • Beinan Cultural Park
  • Luye Aboriginal Museum
  • Hualien Cultural Creative Industries Park
  • Farglory Ocean Park

Natural Attractions of Eastern Taiwan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eastern Taiwan is famous for its breathtaking natural landscapes and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Taroko Gorge National Park
  • Yushan National Park
  • East Coast Scenic Area
  • Emei Lake
  • Green Island
  • Orchid Island
  • Sanxiantai

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Taiwan

Eastern Taiwan has some of Taiwan’s most challenging but rewarding hiking and biking trails. Here are some popular routes:

  • Mount Hehuan Traverse
  • Qilai Mountain Trail
  • Xinshe Rice Terraces
  • Zhuilu Old Trail
  • Walami Trail
  • Cijing Bike Lane

Best Things to Do in Eastern Taiwan: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for adventure or relaxation, Eastern Taiwan has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • River tracing
  • Surfing lessons
  • Whale watching
  • Hot air balloon ride
  • Aboriginal cultural experience
  • Traditional tea ceremony

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Taiwan

If you have a week to explore Eastern Taiwan, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Hualien and visit Taroko Gorge National Park Day 2: Explore Hualien City and the East Coast Scenic Area Day 3: Visit Taitung City and Beinan Cultural Park Day 4: Take a day trip to Green Island Day 5: Go river tracing in Sanxiantai Day 6: Visit the National Museum of Prehistory in Taitung Day 7: Return to Taipei via train or plane

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Taiwan

Learning about the local culture and customs can enhance your travel experience. Here are some interesting facts about Eastern Taiwan:

  • The area is home to several indigenous tribes, including the Amis, Bunun, and Paiwan.
  • The traditional architecture of Eastern Taiwan includes the Hakka-style houses and wooden houses built by the indigenous tribes.
  • The local festivals include the Taiwan Balloon Festival, East Coast Land Arts Festival, and Yanshui Fireworks Festival.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Taiwan

The best time to visit Eastern Taiwan is during the spring (March-May) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid, while winter (December-February) is cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Taiwan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Enjoy the stunning natural scenery.
  • Book your accommodation and activities in advance.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash behind in the natural attractions.
  • Be disrespectful to the local people and culture.
  • Wear revealing clothing in temples or other religious sites.
  • Travel without appropriate insurance coverage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Taiwan

Eastern Taiwan is generally safe for travelers. However, it is advisable to be cautious when hiking or doing outdoor activities, as the terrain can be challenging. Additionally, it is important to stay up to date on weather warnings and advisories.

Health in Eastern Taiwan

Taiwan has an excellent healthcare system, and Eastern Taiwan is no exception. Medical facilities are modern and well-equipped, and most doctors speak English. However, it is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, as healthcare costs can be high.

Getting to Eastern Taiwan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Taiwan

The main airports near Eastern Taiwan are:

  • Hualien Airport
  • Taitung Airport

Bus Stations Near Eastern Taiwan

  • Hualien Bus Station
  • Taitung Bus Station

Other Options

  • Trains: The Taiwan Railways Administration operates trains that connect Taipei to Hualien and Taitung.
  • Car rental: Rental cars are available at major airports and cities in Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Eastern Taiwan

Getting around in Eastern Taiwan is easy and affordable. The best way to travel is by renting a scooter, which can cost as little as $15 per day. Alternatively, you can take local buses, trains, or book a private tour.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Taiwan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Taiwan, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Taipei: Taiwan’s vibrant capital city that offers a mix of ancient culture and modern innovation.
  • Kenting National Park: A popular beach destination in the south of Taiwan.
  • Sun Moon Lake: A scenic lake that is also an important cultural site for the Thao people.
  • Alishan National Forest Recreation Area: A mountain resort that offers stunning views of the sunrise and the sea of clouds.

Conclusion

Eastern Taiwan is a traveler’s paradise that offers a unique blend of natural wonder and cultural richness. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan an unforgettable journey to this beautiful part of Taiwan.
