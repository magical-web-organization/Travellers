Introduction to Shei-pa National Park

Shei-pa National Park covers an area of 788 square kilometers and is located in the central part of Taiwan. It is one of the nine national parks in Taiwan and was established in 1992. The park is home to many unique natural wonders and is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, activities to do, and transportation options to reach Shei-pa National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Shei-pa National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wuling Farm

Wuling Farm is a popular tourist destination located in the northeastern part of Shei-pa National Park. It was originally used as an experimental farm during the Japanese colonial period and was opened to the public in 2004. Visitors can enjoy a variety of seasonal fruits, flowers, and vegetables throughout the year.

Fonghuanggu Bird and Ecology Park

Fonghuanggu Bird and Ecology Park is a popular destination for bird watching enthusiasts. It is home to more than 158 species of birds, including the rare Taiwan Blue Magpie. The park also offers guided tours and bird-watching activities.

Guguan Recreation Area

Guguan Recreation Area is located on the highway between Taichung and Hualien. The area is famous for its hot springs and stunning scenery. It is also home to many hiking trails that range from easy to difficult.

Natural Attractions of Shei-pa National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Snow Mountain

Snow Mountain, also known as Xue Mountain, is the second-highest peak in Taiwan. It is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The trail to the summit is challenging, but the views are worth the effort.

Dabajian Mountain

Dabajian Mountain, also known as Big-A Mountain, is a popular destination for both hikers and nature lovers. It is one of the easiest hikes in the park, and the summit offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Dongpu Hot Springs

Dongpu Hot Springs is a natural hot spring located in the central part of Shei-pa National Park. The spring water has a temperature of around 70 degrees Celsius, and the area is surrounded by beautiful mountain views. It is a perfect destination for those looking to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shei-pa National Park

Shei-pa National Park is a hiker’s paradise, with many trails ranging from easy to difficult. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the park:

Hiking Routes

Snow Mountain Main Peak Trail

The trail to Snow Mountain’s main peak is one of the most challenging hikes in Taiwan. It takes two days and involves camping overnight. The trail can be icy and treacherous, so hikers need to be well prepared.

Dabajian Mountain Trail

The trail to Dabajian Mountain is easy, making it a popular destination for hikers of all ages. The hike takes around two to three hours, and the summit offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Xueba National Trail

The Xueba National Trail is a long hike that covers some of the most beautiful areas of Shei-pa National Park. The trail takes around three to four days to complete and involves camping overnight.

Biking Routes

Fonghuanggu Forest Trail

The Fonghuanggu Forest Trail is a popular destination for mountain bikers. The trail is relatively easy and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest.

Dasyueshan Forest Trail

The Dasyueshan Forest Trail is one of the most challenging mountain biking trails in Taiwan. The trail is about 48 kilometers long and involves steep climbs and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Shei-pa National Park: Activities and Tours

There are many activities to enjoy in Shei-pa National Park. Here are some of the best:

Hot Springs

Shei-pa National Park is home to many hot springs, including Dongpu Hot Springs and Guguan Hot Springs. Soaking in a natural hot spring is the perfect way to relax after a long day of hiking.

Bird Watching

Shei-pa National Park is home to many bird species, including the rare Taiwan Blue Magpie. Visitors can enjoy bird watching tours and guided activities.

Photography

Shei-pa National Park is a photographer’s paradise, with stunning views and unique natural wonders. Visitors can capture amazing shots of the surrounding landscape and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shei-pa National Park

Here is a week-long itinerary for Shei-pa National Park:

Day 1: Arrive in the park and relax at Dongpu Hot Springs.

Day 2: Hike to the summit of Snow Mountain and camp overnight.

Day 3: Continue hiking on the Xueba National Trail and camp overnight.

Day 4: Finish the Xueba National Trail and relax at Fonghuanggu Bird and Ecology Park.

Day 5: Mountain biking on Dasyueshan Forest Trail.

Day 6: Visit Wuling Farm and enjoy seasonal fruits, flowers, and vegetables.

Day 7: Depart from the park and explore nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Shei-pa National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Shan Ge Noodles

Shan Ge Noodles is a famous noodle dish in the park. The noodles are made with local ingredients and served with a savory broth and vegetables.

Mountain Vegetables

Shei-pa National Park is home to many unique mountain vegetables, including wild ginger, bitter gourd, and Chinese artichoke.

Local Beverages

Plum Wine

Plum wine is a popular alcoholic beverage in Taiwan. It is made with local plums and has a sweet and slightly sour taste.

Oolong Tea

Oolong tea is a popular beverage in Taiwan that is frequently served with meals. It has a unique flavor and aroma and is said to have many health benefits.

Where to Stay in Shei-pa National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in and around Shei-pa National Park. Here are some of the best:

Hotels

Full Moon Spa

Full Moon Spa is a luxury hotel located in the Guguan Recreation Area. The hotel offers stunning mountain views, hot springs, and delicious dining options.

Yashe Villa Hotel

Yashe Villa Hotel is a boutique hotel located near Wuling Farm. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, stunning views, and excellent service.

Hostels

Dongpu Youth Activity Center

The Dongpu Youth Activity Center is a popular destination for backpackers and budget travelers. The hostel offers basic accommodations and is located in the heart of the park.

Fonghuanggu Ecological Youth Hostel

The Fonghuanggu Ecological Youth Hostel is a popular destination for young travelers. The hostel is located near Fonghuanggu Bird and Ecology Park and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Shei-pa National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Products

Dried Persimmons

Dried persimmons are a popular local product in Shei-pa National Park. They are made with ripe persimmons and are sweet and flavorful.

Honey

The park is home to many bee farms, producing high-quality honey. The honey is sweet and has many health benefits.

Souvenirs

Taiwan Blue Magpie Souvenirs

The Taiwan Blue Magpie is a rare bird species found in Shei-pa National Park. Souvenir shops sell various items, including t-shirts, postcards, and keychains.

Local Culture and Customs of Shei-pa National Park

The local people in Shei-pa National Park are predominantly of the Atayal Aboriginal tribe. Visitors can experience their vibrant culture and traditions, including music, dance, and crafts. It is important to respect their customs and traditions when visiting the park.

Best Time to Visit Shei-pa National Park

The best time to visit Shei-pa National Park is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden weather changes at any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shei-pa National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Bring insect repellent spray and sunblock.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the environment in any way.
  • Smoke or start fires in the park.
  • Feed or disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shei-pa National Park

Visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

Weather Changes

Weather changes can occur suddenly in the park, so visitors should always be prepared for unexpected weather conditions.

Trail Conditions

Trails can be steep, rocky, and treacherous, so visitors should always hike with caution and follow trail markers.

Wildlife

Visitors should never feed or disturb wildlife, especially snakes, because some are poisonous.

Health in Shei-pa National Park

Visitors should be aware of the following health concerns:

Sunburn

The park can be hot and sunny, so visitors should wear protective clothing and use sunblock.

Dehydration

Hiking and outdoor activities can cause dehydration, so visitors should bring plenty of water and stay hydrated.

Insects

Insects, especially mosquitoes, can be prevalent in the park, so visitors should bring insect repellent spray.

Getting to Shei-pa National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shei-pa National Park

The closest airport to Shei-pa National Park is Taichung International Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus or a taxi to the park.

Bus Stations Near Shei-pa National Park

There are several bus stations near Shei-pa National Park, including:

  • Wuling Bus Station
  • Songbao Bus Station
  • Puli Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to reach the park.

How to Get Around in Shei-pa National Park

The best way to get around Shei-pa National Park is by hiking, biking, or driving. Visitors can also take guided tours or hire a local guide.
