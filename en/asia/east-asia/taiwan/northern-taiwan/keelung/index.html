Introduction to Keelung

Keelung is a bustling port city located in northern Taiwan, in East Asia. Just a short distance from the capital city of Taipei, Keelung is a popular destination for travelers looking to explore Taiwan’s rich history, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Keelung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Keelung Maritime Plaza: A must-visit for history buffs, Keelung Maritime Plaza tells the story of the city’s maritime heritage through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • Baimiwong Fort (Ershawan Fort): This historic fort was built in the late 1800s to protect Keelung from invasion. Today, visitors can explore its tunnels, admire its cannons, and enjoy panoramic views of the city and sea.
  • The Keelung Temples: Keelung is home to a number of beautiful temples, including the Dianji Temple, Bisha Temple, and Tianhou Temple, each with its unique history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Keelung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chaojing Park: A popular spot for hiking and picnicking, Chaojing Park offers stunning views of Keelung’s coastline and the surrounding mountains.
  • The Yehliu Geopark: This geological wonderland features as well-known rock formations such as “Queen’s Head” and “Mushroom Rocks” and is an impressive site to behold.
  • Heping Island Park: Located on a small island just off the coast of Keelung, Heping Island Park is a popular spot for camping, swimming, and enjoying panoramic ocean views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keelung

  • Teapot Mountain: One of the most popular hiking routes in Keelung, Teapot Mountain offers incredible views of the coastline and the famous “Turtle Island” in the distance. The trail is moderately challenging and takes approximately two to three hours to complete.
  • Badouzi Coastal Park: A scenic coastal path that stretches for kilometers, Badouzi Coastal Park offers a relaxing bike ride or hike along the sea, with stunning views of the ocean and the mountains surrounding the city.

Best Things to Do in Keelung: Activities and Tours

  • Day trip to Jiufen and Pingxi: These charming towns located on the outskirts of Keelung are famous for their picturesque old streets, tea houses, and sky lanterns.
  • Night market experience: Keelung’s famous Miaokou Night Market offers a wide range of local food and drink vendors with friendly staff and amazing local cuisines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keelung

Food and Drink Scene of Keelung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Eel noodles (鰻麵): Served in a flavorful broth with melt-in-your-mouth eel meat and chewy rice noodles, this is a Keelung specialty not to miss!
  • Oyster omelets (蚵仔煎): A crispy fried omelet filled with fresh oysters and topped with sweet and sour sauce.
  • Braised pork over rice (滷肉飯): Slow-cooked tender pork over steamed white rice - this simple but tasty dish is a staple in Keelung.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble tea: As one of the world’s most famous beverages, Bubble tea was actually invented in Taiwan, and Keelung has some of the best bubble tea shops in Taiwan.
  • Kaoliang: A strong and fragrant traditional Taiwanese liquor that is often consumed with meals.
  • Taiwan Beer: One of Taiwan’s most popular beers, Taiwan Beer is a refreshing way to beat the heat in Keelung’s hot summers.

Where to Stay in Keelung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Evergreen Laurel Hotel Keelung: A modern and stylish hotel, the Evergreen Laurel is located in the city center and offers spacious rooms and great views of the ocean.
  • Sleepy Dragon Hostel: A cozy and affordable hostel near the Miaokou Night Market, Sleepy Dragon Hostel features comfortable rooms and a welcoming staff.
  • Keelung Imperial Hotel: Built in the early 20th century, this historic hotel is situated in a prime location and offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Keelung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oyster crackers: A crunchy snack made with ground oysters and flour and flavored with a savory blend of spices.
  • Seafood products: Keelung is famous for its seafood, and visitors can take home dried fish, squid, and other specialty items.
  • Handmade Soap: Using natural ingredients such as olive oil, coconut oil, and shea butter, these handmade soaps are a must-buy for those who love natural products.

Local Culture and Customs of Keelung

  • Night markets: Keelung is famous for its bustling night markets, where visitors can experience local food and culture.
  • Worshiping practices: Keelung has many temples with different cultures and worships, and visitors should respect and follow the temple’s rules and customs during their visit.

Best Time to Visit Keelung

The best time to visit Keelung is from April to June when the weather is pleasant and the scenery is at its best. The summer season (June to September) is hot and humid, and the winter season (December to February) can be damp and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keelung

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs at temples and other important sights.
  • Sample the delicious seafood and local specialties at the night markets.
  • Wear comfortable shoes when hiking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings of parks while hiking or biking.
  • Overestimate traffic time and plan ahead as rush hours might create long traffic jams.
  • Take unauthorized pictures of local residents without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keelung

Keelung is a safe city for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Tourists should take precautions and be careful in crowded areas such as night markets. Also, it is recommended to keep valuable items safe with you.

Health in Keelung

The tap water in Keelung is safe to drink, and the city has many hospitals and medical facilities in case of emergency. It is always a good idea to bring necessary medication as prescriptions may not be readily available.

Getting to Keelung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keelung

Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport is the closest airport to Keelung, located approximately 45 km away, with buses and taxis available to transfer guests to Keelung.

Bus Stations Near Keelung

Keelung Bus Terminal is well-connected to other cities in Taiwan, including Taipei, Taichung, and Kaohsiung.

Other Options

For travelers who prefer private transportation, rental cars and taxis are options that are easy to find and offer direct service to your destination.

How to Get Around in Keelung

Keelung features an efficient public transportation system, including buses and taxis, which are priced affordably. Renting bikes is also possible for visitors, creating an inexpensive way to explore Keelung’s countryside and coastlines.

Where to Go Next from Keelung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taipei: The bustling capital city of Taiwan is just a short drive or train ride away and offers endless options for sightseeing, shopping, and dining.
  • Yilan: Located to the southeast of Keelung, Yilan County is home to beautiful natural scenery, hot springs, and cultural attractions.
  • Jinguashi: A former gold mining town, Jinguashi offers visitors an opportunity to learn about Taiwan’s history and admire stunning mountain views.

Conclusion

From the stunning rock formations at Yehliu Geopark to the bustling night markets, Keelung is a destination that offers something for every traveler. With its rich culture, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Keelung is a must-visit place in Taiwan. Come and explore, eat, and enjoy Keelung!
