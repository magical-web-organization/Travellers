Introduction to Neiwan

Nestled in the northern part of Taiwan, Neiwan is a charming small town that is rapidly becoming popular among travelers. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and delicious food. Located just a few hours away from Taipei, Neiwan has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Neiwan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Neiwan Old Street: This street is lined with traditional shophouses that have been converted into shops selling food, souvenirs, and handicrafts.
  • Wufengchi Waterfall: This waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and is a favourite spot among hikers and nature lovers.
  • Neiwan Theater: This theater dates back to the Japanese colonial era and is now used for performances and events.
  • Erziping Trail: This stunning trail offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Natural Attractions of Neiwan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taizhong River: The river offers a variety of water activities, such as rafting and kayaking.
  • Neiwan Hot Springs: Soak in the hot springs and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.
  • Qingqing Grasslands: This area offers magnificent views of the surrounding mountains and is a popular spot for photographers.
  • Beipu Cold Springs: These refreshing natural springs are a great option for those looking to cool off during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neiwan

  • Erziping Trail: This 4.5km trail is a great option for those looking for a leisurely hike with stunning views.
  • Xiangtian Lake Trail: This 3.3km trail is a favourite among local photographers due to its stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Shengxing Station to Neiwan Old Street: This 6.5km trail follows the old railway tracks and offers a unique perspective of the town.

Best Things to Do in Neiwan: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tour: Sample the delicious local delicacies, such as red quinoa and mountain vegetables.
  • Cycling Tour: Explore the beautiful countryside on two wheels.
  • Rafting/Kayaking: Experience the thrill of the Taizhong River rapids.
  • Night Market: Get your shopping fix and try more delicious food at the night market.
  • Eco-tour: Explore the vast forests and learn about Neiwan’s flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neiwan

Day Itinerary
1 Arrive in Neiwan, explore the town centre and try some local food
2 Hike the Erziping Trail and visit the Wufengchi Waterfall
3 Visit the Neiwan Theater and take a cycling tour of the countryside

5 Take a day trip to neighbouring towns, Zhouli and Jiji

7 Experience the night market and go on an eco-tour of the vast forests around Neiwan

Food and Drink Scene of Neiwan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Red Quinoa: This nutritious grain is grown locally and is used in a variety of dishes.
  • Mountain Vegetables: Fresh, seasonal vegetables picked from the surrounding mountains.
  • Wild Boar Soup: A hearty soup made with wild boar meat and local spices.
  • Roasted Chicken: A traditional dish served with rice and fresh vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Plum Wine: Made from locally grown plums, this sweet wine is a favourite among locals.
  • Tea: The hills surrounding Neiwan produce excellent tea, and there are many tea shops where you can try different varieties.

Where to Stay in Neiwan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Neiwan Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the town centre, with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Fairy Tale Castle: A unique guesthouse that is housed in a castle-inspired building.
  • Hua Yuan Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Neiwan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hakka handicrafts: Neiwan is famous for its traditional handicrafts such as weaving, pottery, and embroidery. These make great gifts for loved ones.
  • Tea: Bring home a variety of locally grown teas to enjoy long after your trip.
  • Mountain vegetables: If you love to cook, consider buying some of these fresh, seasonal vegetables to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Neiwan

Neiwan is a town with a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the local Hakka community. It is known for its traditional architecture, handicrafts, and cuisine. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to explore and learn about the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Neiwan

The best time to visit Neiwan is during the spring and autumn months, between March and November. The weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists during these months. Avoid the summer months of June to August, as temperatures can be hot and humid, and there could be heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neiwan

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be mindful of your impact on the environment.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neiwan

Neiwan is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, as with any destination, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Neiwan

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Neiwan, but it is advisable to drink bottled water to avoid any potential stomach upsets. Pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as the sun can be strong, and mosquitoes are commonplace.

Getting to Neiwan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neiwan

  • Taoyuan International Airport: This is the closest international airport, located approximately 2.5 hours away by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Neiwan

  • Hsinchu Bus Station: Buses operate regularly between Hsinchu and Neiwan.
  • Zhongli Bus Station: Buses operate regularly between Zhongli and Neiwan.

Other Options

  • Train: Neiwan is easily accessible by train, with regular services connecting it to Taipei, Hsinchu, and Taichung.

How to Get Around in Neiwan

The best way to get around Neiwan is on foot or by bike. The town is small, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Bicycle rental shops are located throughout town, and renting a bike is a great way to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Neiwan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhouli: A charming town located approximately 20km from Neiwan, known for its Hakka architecture and beautiful countryside.
  • Jiji: A small town located approximately 30km from Neiwan, known for its picturesque train station and scenic surroundings.
  • Taichung City: Taiwan’s third-largest city, located approximately an hour’s drive from Neiwan, known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant food scene.

Conclusion

Neiwan is a beautiful, charming town that is rapidly becoming a popular destination among travelers to Taiwan. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and delicious local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Neiwan. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or just a change of scenery, Neiwan should definitely be on your travel list.
