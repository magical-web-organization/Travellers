Introduction to Chupei

Chupei is a charming city located in the Hsinchu County of Taiwan, known for its rich culture, captivating natural beauty, and warm hospitality. The city is situated in Northern Taiwan, overlooking the scenic mountains and surrounded by lush greeneries, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and cultural explorers.

List of Places to Visit in Chupei

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Neiwan Old Street

Neiwan Old Street is a lively and bustling street filled with traditional shops and vendors that have been around for generations. The old street is famous for its authentic delicacies, including the renowned Hakka dishes, pastries, and snacks. The street is also home to ancient buildings, such as the Neiwan Police Station, built during the Japanese colonial era, which has been renovated and turned into a museum and library.

Beipu Old Street

Beipu Old Street is another popular destination in Chupei, renowned for its historic architecture and rich cultural heritage. The street is home to several traditional shops and vendors, opening up an opportunity for visitors to learn about the local arts and culture, including pottery, straw weaving, and calligraphy.

Taiwan Coal Mine Museum

Taiwan Coal Mine Museum is an excellent destination for visitors who want to learn more about the history of coal mining in Taiwan. The museum is home to several exhibits that showcase the development of the coal mining industry in Taiwan, including machinery, tools, photographs, and dioramas.

Natural Attractions of Chupei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lion’s Head Mountain (Shihtoushan)

Lion’s Head Mountain, also known as Shihtoushan, is a beautiful mountain range located in Chupei, offering breathtaking panoramic views to its visitors. The mountain range is home to several hiking trails that lead hikers to the summit, where they can enjoy a beautiful view of the surrounding area.

Green World Ecological Farm

Green World Ecological Farm is a must-visit location, especially for families with young children, who can experience farm life on this field-to-table organic farm. Visitors can learn about the organic farming methods used to grow crops such as vegetables, fruits, and herbs. The farm also has a vast collection of domestic and exotic animals, including ostriches, eagles, and guinea pigs.

Wufengqi Waterfall

Wufengqi Waterfall, located in the mountain regions of Chupei, is a beautiful natural wonder that cascades down a cliff from a height of about 30 meters. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a picturesque setting to enjoy the tranquility of nature.

Chupei is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts to explore hiking and biking trails. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Neiwan Old Railway Line Trail
  • Lion’s Head Mountain trail
  • Beipu Trail
  • Green World Ecological Farm Trail

Best Things to Do in Chupei: Activities and Tours

  • Attend a Hakka festival
  • Join the one-day Tung Blossom Festival in March or the Shin Kong Wu Ho Su Memorial Fireworks Show in November
  • Visit a tea plantation and learn about tea processing
  • Take a stroll around Beipu Old Street and soak in the atmosphere
  • Go cycling alongside the lush green fields

Food and Drink Scene of Chupei

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hakka dishes, such as Hakka-style braised pork and stuffed tofu
  • Traditional Taiwanese snacks, such as peanut brittle and taro cakes
  • Custom bubble tea which can be found in the districts in the area

Local Beverages

  • Browse local tea houses and savor a freshly brewed oolong tea or Taiwan’s famous bubble tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Chupei

  • The Hakka people are the majority in the area and have a distinct culture and language - visitors to Chupei can learn a lot about it by taking part in the festivals and events in the area.
  • When interacting with the locals, be friendly and show respect.
  • Always remove shoes before entering a host’s home, and generally observe local customs and etiquettes.

Best Time to Visit Chupei

The best time to visit Chupei is during the spring and autumn months. April and May have spring flowers in bloom and are among the most beautiful times of the year in Taiwan. The weather is pleasant, and the humidity is low, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chupei

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try out traditional Hakka cuisine and Taiwanese snacks
  • Participate in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Smoke in public areas
  • Wear revealing clothing in temples or other religious areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Chupei

Chupei is relatively safe. However, visitors should always take precautions and be mindful of their surroundings. We recommend using a travel bag with anti-theft features and avoiding the display of valuables.

Health in Chupei

The tap water in Chupei is considered safe to drink. However, to stay healthy, visitors should avoid undercooked food and unfiltered tap water. The sun can also be strong, so make sure to use sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Conclusion
