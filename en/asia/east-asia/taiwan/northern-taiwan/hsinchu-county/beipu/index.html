Beipu, located in Hsinchu County in northern Taiwan, is a charming destination that offers a blend of culture, nature, and adventure. This traditional town boasts historical sites, breathtaking scenery, and delectable cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Beipu has something to offer. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your trip to Beipu.

Introduction to Beipu

Beipu is a small town with a population of around 10,000 people. It’s known for its Hakka culture, which includes traditional architecture, cuisine, and festivals. Beipu has preserved many of its historical buildings and cultural practices, making it a unique destination in Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Beipu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Beipu Old Street - This pedestrian street is lined with well-preserved historical buildings, including traditional wooden structures, temples, and shops offering Hakka handicrafts and snacks.

  2. Beipu Cold Spring - This natural spring is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike. The crystal-clear water flows from a bamboo pipe into a small pool, creating a relaxing and refreshing atmosphere.

  3. Guangfu Temple - This temple was built in the 18th century and is known for its intricate carvings and ornate decorations. It’s one of the oldest and most important temples in Beipu.

Natural Attractions of Beipu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Maolishan Scenic Area - This park offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding mountains. It also features numerous hiking trails that lead to waterfalls, caves, and other natural attractions.

  2. Green World Ecological Farm - This farm is a great place to experience nature and learn about sustainable agriculture. Visitors can go on guided tours, pick fruits and vegetables, and try various farm-to-table dishes.

  3. Cilan Forest Recreation Area - This forest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare birds and butterflies. It’s a great place for hiking, birdwatching, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beipu

  1. Beipu Great Wall Trail - This trail follows the ancient defensive wall that surrounded Beipu in the 18th century. It offers stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

  2. Maolishan Trail - This challenging trail leads up Maolishan Mountain and offers spectacular views of the town and surrounding mountains.

  3. Beipu Cycling Route - This 10-kilometer route takes cyclists through the town’s historical streets and countryside. It’s a great way to explore Beipu and get some exercise at the same time.

Best Things to Do in Beipu: Activities and Tours

  1. Hakka Cultural Tours - Beipu offers guided tours that showcase the town’s Hakka culture, including traditional cuisine, architecture, and festivals.

  2. Hot Spring Bathing - Beipu has several hot spring resorts where visitors can relax and rejuvenate in natural hot springs.

  3. Fruit Picking - Beipu is known for its abundant fruit orchards, and visitors can go fruit picking in season.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beipu

Day 1: Explore Beipu Old Street and Cold Spring Day 2: Hike the Maolishan Trail and visit Green World Ecological Farm Day 3: Visit Guangfu Temple and Cilan Forest Recreation Area Day 4-6: Take part in Hakka Culture tours, hot spring bathing, and fruit picking Day 7: Depart Beipu for your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Beipu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lei Cha - This traditional Hakka dish consists of steamed rice, vegetables, and ground tea leaves. It’s served with a savory broth and is a unique and healthy meal option.

  2. Hakka Stuffed Tofu - This dish features fried tofu stuffed with pork and vegetable filling, and is a flavorful and satisfying comfort food.

  3. Hakka Dumplings - These steamed dumplings are filled with ground pork, mushrooms, and other vegetables. They’re a classic Hakka dish and a must-try in Beipu.

Local Beverages

  1. Hakka Tea - This unique tea is made by grinding tea leaves, grains, and nuts together. It’s a common drink in Hakka communities and is known for its nutty flavor and health benefits.

  2. Hakka Rice Wine - This alcoholic beverage is made by fermenting glutinous rice and is a popular drink in Beipu. It has a sweet and mellow flavor and is often served during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Beipu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Beipu Homestay - This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a family-style atmosphere. It’s located in the heart of Beipu Old Street and is a great option for budget travelers.

  2. Silks Place Taroko - This luxury resort is located in nearby Hualien County and offers stunning views of Taroko Gorge. It’s a great option for those looking for a more upscale lodging experience.

What to Buy in Beipu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hakka Handicrafts - Beipu is known for its traditional handicrafts, including wood carvings, bamboo crafts, and straw weavings. These unique and beautifully crafted items make great souvenirs or gifts.

  2. Beipu Tea - Beipu produces high-quality tea, including green tea, black tea, and oolong tea. Buying some tea to take home is a great way to remember your trip to Beipu.

Local Culture and Customs of Beipu

Beipu is a town rich in Hakka culture and traditions. Visitors should respect the local customs and practices, including removing shoes before entering temples and private homes, and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Beipu

The best time to visit Beipu is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beipu

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including Hakka dishes and teas.
  • Do explore the town’s historic streets and buildings.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t take photos or disturb the wildlife in natural areas.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or religious practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beipu

Beipu is a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Beipu

Beipu has good medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them but can easily find medical help if needed.

Getting to Beipu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beipu

The nearest airport to Beipu is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is located about 70 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a bus or train to Hsinchu City, and then take a local bus or taxi to Beipu.

Bus Stations Near Beipu

Beipu is easily accessible by bus from Hsinchu City and Taipei City. There are several bus stations in Hsinchu and Taipei that provide regular bus service to Beipu.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Hsinchu City or Taipei City to Beipu. However, driving in Taiwan can be challenging for foreign visitors, so it’s recommended to hire a driver or take public transportation instead.

How to Get Around Beipu

Beipu is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Local buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer to use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Beipu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Hsinchu City - This nearby city is known for its history, culture, and technology. Visitors can explore the city’s many temples, museums, and night markets.

  2. Taroko Gorge - This stunning natural wonder is located in nearby Hualien County and offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and breathtaking scenery.

Conclusion

Beipu is a hidden gem in northern Taiwan that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re looking to explore historic sites, enjoy outdoor activities, or indulge in delicious food, Beipu has something to offer. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Beipu.
