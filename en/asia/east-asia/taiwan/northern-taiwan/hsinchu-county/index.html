Introduction to Hsinchu County

Located in Northern Taiwan, Hsinchu County is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic natural landscapes, and technological advancements. The county comprises numerous townships, including Hsinchu City, Zhubei City, and Jhudong Township. Hsinchu City is the administrative capital of the county and is famous for its science and technology parks, while the other townships offer a glimpse of traditional Taiwanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Hsinchu County

There is no dearth of attractions in Hsinchu County, be it natural wonders, cultural sites, or adventure activities. Here is a list of must-visit places in Hsinchu County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hsinchu City God Temple: A Taoist temple in Hsinchu City, which is one of the oldest and most revered temples in Taiwan.
  • Green Grass Lake: A picturesque lake on the outskirts of Hsinchu City, known for its lotus blooms and romantic bridges.
  • Hukou Old Street: A quaint street in Zhubei City, featuring traditional old houses and shops selling quirky souvenirs and local snacks.
  • Beipu Old Street: A charming street in Beipu Township, showcasing the architecture, cuisine, and customs of the Hakka community.

Natural Attractions of Hsinchu County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nanliao Port: A scenic port in Hsinchu City, where visitors can enjoy fresh seafood and stroll along the waterfront.
  • Lion’s Head Mountain: A popular hiking spot in Xinfeng Township, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Neiwan: A tranquil village in Hengshan Township, which is renowned for its tea plantations and scenic hiking trails.
  • Shei-Pa National Park: A vast wilderness area on the border of Hsinchu County, where visitors can explore deep gorges, hot springs, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hsinchu County

Hsinchu County is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts, offering numerous trails and paths through its mountains and countryside. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Baoshan Reservoir Trail: A serene 8-kilometer trail around the Baoshan Reservoir, featuring lush forests and scenic views.
  • Guanwu Forest Recreation Area Trail: An 8-kilometer trail in Shei-Pa National Park, where visitors can hike through the stunning high-altitude forests.
  • Nanliao Coastal Bike Path: A 7-kilometer bike path along the scenic coastline near Hsinchu City, which takes visitors through fishing villages and natural wonders.
  • Grand Garden Trail: A 10-kilometer trail in Bitan Township, featuring picturesque gardens, waterfalls, and suspension bridges.

Best Things to Do in Hsinchu County: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, Hsinchu County also offers several exciting activities and tours, such as:

  • Night Market Tour: A tour of the famous night markets in Hsinchu City, where visitors can sample local snacks, drinks, and shopping.
  • River Rafting: An adventure activity in the Bitan River, where visitors can navigate the rapids and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Fruit Picking: A popular activity in various townships of Hsinchu County, where visitors can pick and taste various fruits like strawberries, peaches, and grapes.
  • Hakka Cultural Experience: A tour of the Hakka villages and homes, where visitors can learn about the customs, history, and cuisine of the Hakka people.

Food and Drink Scene of Hsinchu County

Hsinchu County is renowned for its delicious cuisine and local beverages, which are influenced by Chinese, Hakka, and indigenous culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Hsinchu County:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oyster Omelette: A popular Taiwanese snack made with eggs, oysters, and starch.
  • Hakka Style Stir Fried Rice Cake: A traditional Hakka dish made with sliced rice cakes stir-fried with pork and vegetables.
  • Bamboo Rice: A fragrant dish made with sticky rice, chicken, and mushrooms cooked inside bamboo.
  • Shaobing: A flaky bread roll stuffed with sesame seeds, scallions, and bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Tea: A Taiwanese beverage made with tea, milk, and pearls or jelly.
  • Alisan Tea: A high-quality tea from the Alishan Mountains, which is famous for its delicate aroma and taste.
  • Kaoliang Liquor: A strong liquor made from sorghum, which is popular among Taiwanese people.
  • Hsinchu Biandang: A portable lunchbox that contains rice, meat, and vegetables, which is a favorite among local workers.

Where to Stay in Hsinchu County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hsinchu County offers several excellent options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Hsinchu County:

  • Ambassador Hotel Hsinchu: A 5-star hotel in Hsinchu City, offering luxurious rooms and suites, along with a spa, restaurants, and a fitness center.
  • The Carlton Hotel-Beipu: A boutique hotel in Beipu Township, featuring stylish rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a cafe serving local cuisine.
  • Lion’s Den Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in Xinfeng Township, offering clean and cozy rooms, along with a shared kitchen and free Wi-Fi.
  • Green Roof Homestay: A charming guesthouse in Jianshi Township, offering comfortable rooms, home-cooked meals, and mountain views.

What to Buy in Hsinchu County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hsinchu County is famous for its handicrafts, tea, and local produce, which make perfect souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Hsinchu County:

  • Hakka Textiles: Handmade fabrics, embroidery, and clothing, which showcase the rich traditions of the Hakka people.
  • Alishan Tea: High-quality tea leaves and tea sets from the Alishan Mountains, which are famous for their distinctive aroma and flavor.
  • Golden Pillow Rice: Aromatic and flavorful sticky rice, cooked with meat, mushrooms, and spices inside a bamboo tube.
  • Fruits: Fresh fruits like strawberries, peaches, and grapes, which are famous for their sweetness and juiciness.

Local Culture and Customs of Hsinchu County

Hsinchu County has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, influenced by various ethnic groups and historical events. Here are some of the unique customs and traditions of Hsinchu County:

  • Hakka Culture: The Hakka people have a distinct language, cuisine, and architecture, which can be explored in various townships of Hsinchu County.
  • Lantern Festival: Hsinchu City celebrates the Lantern Festival, where people release sky lanterns into the air, symbolizing good luck and fortune.
  • Dragon Boat Festival: The Dragon Boat Festival is celebrated in various townships of Hsinchu County, with boat races, food, and dragon dance performances.
  • Ghost Month: The seventh lunar month is considered the Ghost Month in Taiwan, and people offer prayers and offerings to appease the spirits of their ancestors.

Best Time to Visit Hsinchu County

The best time to visit Hsinchu County is in the spring and autumn seasons, from March to May and September to November, respectively. The weather during these months is mild and comfortable, with fewer crowds and lower prices. However, Hsinchu County is a year-round destination, and each season has its unique charms and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hsinchu County

Do:

  • Do try the delicious and diverse cuisine of Hsinchu County, which reflects its cultural heritage.
  • Do visit the various temples and historical sites of Hsinchu County, which offer fascinating insights into Taiwanese history and spirituality.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites and rural areas.
  • Do take public transportation, which is cheap and efficient, and avoid driving in the cities, which can be confusing and congested.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places, as it is considered disrespectful and can result in hefty fines.
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs or cultural sensitivities, like taking pictures without permission, pointing with your feet, or sitting on the MRT floor.
  • Don’t be overly loud or expressive in public, as Taiwanese culture values harmony and politeness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hsinchu County

Hsinchu County is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take basic precautions, like keeping valuables safe, avoiding deserted areas at night, and using licensed taxis.

Health in Hsinchu County

Hsinchu County has an excellent healthcare system, with numerous hospitals and medical clinics available. Visitors should have travel insurance and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, like dengue fever and Japanese encephalitis.

Getting to Hsinchu County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hsinchu County is easily accessible from Taipei and other major cities in Taiwan, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Hsinchu County

The nearest airport to Hsinchu County is Taipei Taoyuan International Airport, which is about 40 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or train to Hsinchu City, Zhubei City, or other townships in the county.

Bus Stations Near Hsinchu County

Hsinchu Bus Station and Zhubei Bus Station are the main bus terminals in Hsinchu County, offering connections to various cities and towns in Taiwan.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the Taiwan High-Speed Rail to Hsinchu Station, which is well-connected to various townships in Hsinchu County. Additionally, taxis, rental cars, and private cars are also available for hire in the county.

How to Get Around in Hsinchu County

Hsinchu County has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses and trains, which connect the major towns and attractions of the county. Additionally, taxis, rental cars, and bicycles are also available for hire in various townships.

Where to Go Next from Hsinchu County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hsinchu County is surrounded by several exciting destinations and excursions, offering a glimpse of different aspects of Taiwan. Here are some of the best places to visit nearby:

  • Taichung City: Known for its cultural heritage, night markets, and museums, Taichung City is just an hour away from Hsinchu County.
  • Nantou County: A scenic county in central Taiwan, famous for its tea plantations, sun moon lake, and hiking trails.
  • Taipei City: The capital city of Taiwan, which is renowned for its night markets, shopping areas, and cultural sites.
  • Keelung City: A vibrant city near the northern coast of Taiwan, known for its seafood, night markets, and harbors.

Conclusion

Hsinchu County is a fascinating destination in Taiwan, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure activities. Whether you want to explore the historic temples and streets, hike through the lush forests, or taste the local delicacies, Hsinchu County has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan a memorable trip to Hsinchu County and discover the best of Taiwan’s northern region.
