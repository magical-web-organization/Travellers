Introduction to Shuangxi

Shuangxi is a small district located in the northern part of New Taipei City, Taiwan. The district sits in a valley and is surrounded by mountains, providing nature lovers with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and river tracing. Shuangxi is also home to several cultural and historical landmarks such as temples, museums, and a traditional market.

List of Places to Visit in Shuangxi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zha Muhe Museum of Art - A museum dedicated to showcasing the works of Taiwan’s renowned painter Zha Muhe.
  • Shuangxi Historical Trail - A walking trail that offers visitors a glimpse into Shuangxi’s history and culture.
  • Shuangxi Old Street - A traditional market where visitors can purchase local snacks, souvenirs, and handicrafts.
  • Nanshianggong Temple - A historical temple built during the Qing Dynasty and dedicated to the goddess Mazu.

Natural Attractions of Shuangxi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shuangxi Health Trail - A scenic hiking trail that offers beautiful views of the valley and the surrounding mountains.
  • Shuangxi Suspension Bridge - A pedestrian bridge that spans across the Shuangxi River and offers beautiful views of the river and the mountains.
  • Shuangxi Riverside Park - A beautiful park along the Shuangxi River that offers a peaceful atmosphere for picnics or strolls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shuangxi

  • Zha Muhe Trail - A moderate hiking trail that leads to the Zha Muhe Museum of Art.
  • Shuangxi Health Trail - A scenic hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the valley and the surrounding mountains.
  • Shuangxi Biking Trail - A cycling trail that provides riders with beautiful views of the valley and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Shuangxi: Activities and Tours

  • River Tracing - A thrilling activity that involves following a river’s path downstream, climbing over boulders, and swimming in deep pools.
  • Biking Tours - A fun way to explore Shuangxi and the surrounding area.
  • Night Market - A bustling market that comes alive in the evening hours and offers visitors a chance to try local food and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Shuangxi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bamboo Rice - Rice cooked with bamboo shoots and served in a bamboo tube.
  • Stinky Tofu - Fermented tofu that has a strong odor but is delicious to taste.
  • Potato Snacks - A variety of snacks made from potatoes and served deep-fried or boiled.

Local Beverages

  • Shuangxi Tea - A type of tea made from tea leaves grown in the surrounding mountains.
  • Taiwan Beer - A popular beer from Taiwan that is refreshing and goes well with local dishes.

Where to Stay in Shuangxi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lai Shan Hotel - A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Shuangxi Ming Shan Yuan Homestay - A cozy homestay that offers comfortable rooms and home-cooked meals.
  • Shuangxi Yashe Guesthouse - A traditional guesthouse that offers a unique experience in a beautiful natural setting.

What to Buy in Shuangxi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bamboo Products - Beautifully crafted bamboo products such as pens, chopsticks, and baskets.
  • Tea Leaves - High-quality tea leaves grown in the surrounding mountains are perfect souvenirs for tea lovers.
  • Traditional Taiwanese toys - Toys made from bamboo and wood, such as the traditional Taiwanese spinning top, are unique souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Shuangxi

  • Religion - The majority of Shuangxi’s population follows Buddhism, Taoism, or a combination of both.
  • Festivals - The most important festival in Shuangxi is the Mazu Pilgrimage, held every three years in honor of the goddess Mazu.
  • Etiquette - It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.

Best Time to Visit Shuangxi

The best time to visit Shuangxi is between September and November when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the scenery is at its best. However, Shuangxi is a year-round destination, and each season offers its unique set of attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shuangxi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take your shoes off before entering someone’s home or a temple.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the local culture or religious beliefs.
  • Litter or disrespect nature.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shuangxi

Shuangxi is a generally safe district to visit, and serious crime is rare. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings when walking at night.

Health in Shuangxi

The tap water in Shuangxi is safe to drink, but it is advisable to bring a reusable water bottle and refill it from filtered water stations or bottled water to reduce plastic waste. Visitors should also bring any necessary medications, and it is advisable to purchase travel insurance before arriving in Taiwan.

Getting to Shuangxi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shuangxi

The nearest airport to Shuangxi is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is located approximately 50 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or train to reach Shuangxi.

Bus Stations Near Shuangxi

The nearest bus station to Shuangxi is the New Taipei City Bus Shuangxi Station, which is located in the center of the district.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Ruifang Station or Taipei Main Station and then take a bus to Shuangxi. Taxis and rental cars are also available, but visitors are advised to have their international driver’s license and familiarize themselves with local driving laws.

How to Get Around in Shuangxi

The best way to get around Shuangxi is by walking, cycling, or hiking. Taxis are available but can be expensive, and there is no public transportation available.

Where to Go Next from Shuangxi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pingxi - A charming town known for its sky lanterns and old-fashioned train station.
  • Jiufen - A mountain town famous for its tea houses, street food, and stunning views of the northern coast.
  • Jinguashi - A former gold mining town that is now a popular destination for its museums, traditional architecture, and panoramic views.

Conclusion

Shuangxi is a wonderful destination in northern Taiwan that offers a combination of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities. Visitors to Shuangxi can enjoy hiking or biking in the mountains, exploring the local culture, trying the delicious local cuisine, and even river tracing for those seeking adventure. With so much to offer, Shuangxi is a must-visit destination for all kinds of travelers.
