Introduction to Wugu

Located in New Taipei City, Wugu is a small town that offers a perfect blend of culture, natural beauty, and urban development. It may not be the most popular tourist destination in Taipei, but it has its charm that is worth exploring.

Wugu has a rich history and culture that still reflects in its modernity. The town boasts of its beautiful parks, scenic hiking trails, historic temples, and delectable local dishes that will enchant even the most seasoned travelers. A trip to Wugu is a perfect retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city and a chance to appreciate the simple things that nature offers.

In this comprehensive Wugu travel guide, we will explore the town’s best attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation options, nearby destinations, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Wugu

Wugu has a lot to offer to its visitors with its natural and cultural landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit places in Wugu.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baishihu Recreation Area- A scenic man-made lake surrounded by towering mountains that provide a stunning backdrop. The lake is an excellent spot for a picnic, and you can also do some boating.

  • Xishi Temple - A 200-year-old temple dedicated to the Chinese goddess of love and marriage, Mazu. The temple is famous for its beautiful architecture, colorful decorations, and religious ceremonies.

  • Shimen Archway- It’s a historically significant structure that marks the entrance to the town. It was built in the 18th century to honor a general who sacrificed his life in the war.

  • Kangxi Grassland- A vast recreational area with beautiful greenery, artificial lakes, and scenic views. You can enjoy horse riding, cycling, and outdoor activities here.

Natural Attractions of Wugu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wugu Shimen Reservoir- The reservoir is a popular spot for fishing and bird watching. The lush green forests surrounding the lake are perfect for a leisurely walk or a picnic.

  • Miaoli River- The river streams from the mountains to the heart of Wugu, and along the way, it creates a picturesque gorge that is ideal for hiking and enjoying the natural beauty.

  • Zhongxing Park- A beautiful park that offers a natural playground surrounded by greenery. You can plan a relaxing picnic, or take a stroll, and enjoy the serene beauty of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wugu

Wugu is famous for its scenic trails that offer some of the best natural views around Taipei. Here are some of the most popular routes to explore.

  • Miaoli Riverside Trail- An easy trail along the Miaoli River, which is perfect for a morning jog or a leisurely walk. The trail runs for approximately 5 km.

  • Baishihu Circuit Trail- A moderate level trail that circles around the beautiful Baishihu Lake. The trail takes approximately two hours and offers stunning scenery.

  • Dakeng Trail- A challenging hiking trail, this path is perfect for adventurers looking for an adrenaline rush. This famous trail climbs up to the top of the mountain, and the view from the top makes the hike worth it.

Best Things to Do in Wugu: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and outdoor activities, Wugu offers many other things that will make your trip unforgettable.

  • Visit Local Markets- Wugu is famous for its night markets, and you can find delicious local food, souvenirs, and clothing here.

  • Try Local Dishes- Some of the must-try dishes in Wugu include braised pork, pork belly, and sausages. You can enjoy these dishes in local restaurants.

  • Attend Festivals- Wugu celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year, including the Lantern Festival, Dragon Boat Festival, and Mid-Autumn Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Wugu

If there’s one thing that’s not to be missed in Wugu, it’s the local food and drink scene. Wugu is famous for its delicious braised pork, pork belly, and sausages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you should sample while in town.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braised Pork- A classic Taiwanese dish, this is a must-try in Wugu. The pork is marinated for hours in soy sauce and other spices and slow-cooked to perfection.

  • Pork Belly- Another classic dish, pork belly is cooked crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. It’s usually served with rice and other toppings.

  • Sausages- Wugu is famous for its sausages, which are known for their unique flavor and texture. They’re usually grilled or fried and served with chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pearl Milk Tea- Popular all over Taiwan, this drink is a mixture of black tea, milk, and chewy tapioca balls. It’s a refreshing drink perfect for a hot day.

  • Taiwanese Beer- There are many local beers in Taiwan, such as Taiwan Beer, 18 Days, and Golden Monkey. They’re brewed with the freshest ingredients and are perfect with Wugu’s local dishes.

Where to Stay in Wugu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wugu has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay while in town.

  • Wugu Taitung- A comfortable hotel that offers beautifully decorated rooms, modern amenities, and great service.

  • Rachel’s Garden- A spacious guesthouse with a large garden area that’s perfect for a relaxing stay. The rooms are cozy, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

  • Wugu Nice House- A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable rooms. It’s an excellent option for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Wugu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, Wugu offers a wide range of items. Here are some of the things that you should buy while in town.

  • Local Sausages- Wugu’s sausages are famous across Taiwan and make for an excellent souvenir. You can find them in most of the local markets.

  • Dried Fruit- Wugu is known for its dried fruit, and you can find a wide range of fruits, including pineapples, mangoes, and bananas, in the local markets.

  • Tea- Taiwan is famous for its tea, and you can find some of the best-quality tea in Wugu. Consider buying some tea leaves as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Wugu

Wugu is a small town with a rich cultural heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. As a traveler, it’s essential to observe the local customs and manners. Here are some tips to keep in mind while in Wugu.

  • Remove Shoes- When entering someone’s house or a temple, it’s customary to remove your shoes.

  • Respect Local Culture- Wugu is a town with strong cultural traditions, and it’s essential to respect them. Do not disrespect local customs or practices.

  • Be Polite- Taiwanese people are polite and respectful, and it’s important to reciprocate. Use “Ni Hao” and “Xie Xie” when greeting others, and don’t raise your voice in public.

Best Time to Visit Wugu

The best time to visit Wugu is during the spring and autumn months, between March and October. The weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities without worrying about the heat or rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wugu

Here are some do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind while in Wugu.

Safety and Health

Wugu is a relatively safe town, but it’s always essential to take basic safety precautions while traveling. Here are some tips to keep in mind.

Safety in Wugu

  • Keep an eye on your belongings while in public places.
  • Always lock your hotel room.
  • Avoid going out late at night in isolated areas.

Health in Wugu

  • Carry basic first-aid supplies with you while hiking or biking.

Getting to Wugu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wugu is easy to reach from Taipei, and public transportation is readily available.

Airports Near Wugu

  • Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport (TPE)- The nearest international airport to Wugu is the Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is approximately 45 km away.

Bus Stations Near Wugu

  • Wugu Bus Station- The Wugu Bus Station is the main bus station that serves the town. You can find buses to other cities and towns in Taiwan from here.

Other Options

  • Taxi- Taxis are readily available in Taipei and take approximately 30 minutes to reach Wugu.

How to Get Around in Wugu

Wugu is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. You can also hire a bike or scooter to explore the area. Local buses also run regularly through the town, and taxis are available on call.

Where to Go Next from Wugu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Taiwan, there are many nearby destinations that you can visit from Wugu, such as:

  • Yangmingshan National Park- A beautiful national park known for its hot springs, hiking trails, and scenic views.

  • Tamsui District- A historic district known for its beautiful sunsets, street food, and cultural landmarks.

  • Jiufen Old Street- A charming old street famous for its narrow alleyways, teahouses, and street food.

Conclusion

Wugu is a hidden gem in Taipei that’s worth exploring. The town’s scenic hiking trails, natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and delicious local food make it a perfect retreat from the city’s hustle and bustle. Follow this comprehensive Wugu travel guide to make the best of your trip to this picturesque town in New Taipei City, Taiwan.
