Introduction to Banqiao

Banqiao is a bustling district located in New Taipei, in Northern Taiwan. The area offers a perfect blend of modern and traditional culture with plenty of attractions for both history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts. In this guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know about Banqiao, including places to visit, food to try, where to stay, and more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Banqiao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lin Family Mansion and Garden

Lin Family Mansion and Garden is a beautifully preserved cultural site that serves as a living example of Taiwanese architecture and craftsmanship. This 19th-century complex was once the residence of the Lin family, a prominent family during the Qing Dynasty. Visitors can explore the stunning gardens and admire the various traditional decorative elements such as intricate wood carvings and colorful murals.

Fu Jen Catholic University

Fu Jen Catholic University is one of the oldest and popular higher education institutions in Taiwan. The university houses several historical buildings, including the St. Dominic’s Priory Chapel, which is known for its Gothic-style design and stained glass windows.

Yingge Ceramics Museum

Yingge Ceramics Museum is a great place for visitors interested in learning more about Taiwan’s rich ceramic heritage. The museum displays an impressive collection of Chinese porcelain and Taiwanese pottery.

Natural Attractions of Banqiao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fuzhong 15 Peak

Nature lovers must include Fuzhong 15 Peak in their itinerary. The mountain offers stunning views of Banqiao and Taipei City. Hikers can choose from several trails, including the popular Fuzhong Trail, which takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

Dahan River Recreation Zone

Dahan River Recreation Zone is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike. The park features several walking paths, cycling tracks, and picnic areas. Visitors can also rent kayaks or rafts to explore the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banqiao

Banqiao is a great place for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. Apart from Fuzhong 15 Peak, visitors can also explore the Xinzhuang and Tucheng districts’ bike paths, which offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Banqiao: Activities and Tours

Banqiao Night Market

No trip to Taiwan is complete without visiting a night market. Banqiao Night Market is one of the largest and most popular in Taipei. Visitors can enjoy an overwhelming variety of Taiwanese street foods, including stinky tofu, oyster omelet, and bubble tea.

New Taipei City Art Museum

Art lovers must visit the New Taipei City Art Museum, which showcases a vast collection of contemporary and traditional artwork by Taiwanese artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Banqiao

Day 1: Visit Lin Family Mansion and Garden and Fu Jen Catholic University. In the evening, explore Banqiao Night Market.

Day 2: Hike Fuzhong 15 Peak and take in the stunning views of Banqiao.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Taipei City and visit Taipei 101, National Palace Museum, and Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall.

Day 4: Visit Yingge Ceramics Museum and explore the pottery shops in the area.

Day 5: Visit New Taipei City Art Museum and take a stroll in Banqiao Metropolitan Park.

Day 6: Explore the bike paths of Xinzhuang and Tucheng districts.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a day in Dahan River Recreation Zone.

Food and Drink Scene of Banqiao

Must-Try Dishes

Beef Noodle Soup

One of Taiwan’s most popular dishes, Beef Noodle Soup, is a must-try in Banqiao. The dish consists of tender beef cooked in a flavorful broth with noodles and vegetables.

Oyster Omelet

Oyster Omelet is a savory street food that can be found in Banqiao Night Market. The dish consists of a crispy omelet filled with juicy oysters and topped with a sweet and sour sauce.

Local Beverages

Bubble Tea

Bubble Tea is a Taiwanese beverage that has gained popularity worldwide. The drink consists of tea or fruit-flavored milk mixed with chewy tapioca balls.

Taiwan Beer

Taiwan Beer is a refreshing lager that pairs well with Taiwanese street food. Visitors can find the beer in most restaurants and bars in Banqiao.

What to Buy in Banqiao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ceramics

Yingge Ceramics is famous for its pottery and ceramics. Visitors can find a range of ceramic products, including teapots, bowls, and vases, in the pottery shops in the area.

Traditional Snacks

Banqiao Night Market is a great place to find traditional Taiwanese snacks such as pineapple cake, sun cake, and nougat candies.

Local Culture and Customs of Banqiao

Banqiao is a modern city that remains true to its traditional values. Visitors are advised to dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and to remove their shoes when entering homes.

Best Time to Visit Banqiao

The best time to visit Banqiao is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild, and the tourist crowds are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banqiao

Do:

-Try new food and explore the night markets. -Visit the cultural sites and learn about Taiwan’s history and heritage. -Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or smoke in public areas.
  • Do not discuss sensitive political or religious topics in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banqiao

Banqiao is a safe city, and visitors can feel comfortable walking around at night. However, like any other tourist destination, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and take necessary precautions.

Health in Banqiao

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water. Taiwan has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can seek medical help from hospitals and clinics in the area if needed.

Getting to Banqiao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Banqiao

The closest airport to Banqiao is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is around 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Banqiao

Banqiao Bus Station is the central terminal in the area with bus services connecting Banqiao to other parts of Taipei and other cities in Taiwan.

Other Options

Visitors can take the metro to Banqiao Station, which is located in the heart of the city.

How to Get Around in Banqiao

The most convenient way to get around in Banqiao is by taking the metro or by renting a bike. Visitors can also use buses to travel to other parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Banqiao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taipei City

Taipei City is just a short metro ride away from Banqiao and offers plenty of attractions to explore, including night markets, museums, and historical sites.

Yangmingshan National Park

Yangmingshan National Park is a beautiful nature reserve located about an hour’s drive from Banqiao. The park offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is home to hot springs and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Banqiao offers a perfect mix of modern and traditional culture and is a great destination for history and outdoor enthusiasts. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your next trip to Banqiao. Happy travels!
