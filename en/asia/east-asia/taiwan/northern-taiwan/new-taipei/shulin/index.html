Introduction to Shulin

Located in New Taipei, Northern Taiwan, Shulin is a charming district filled with cultural attractions, fantastic scenery, and delicious local food. Its proximity to Taipei city makes it an ideal destination for day trips or a weekend getaway. It is easy to get to Shulin as it is just a thirty-minute drive or Taipei Metro ride away.

List of Places to Visit in Shulin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Jing-Mei Human Rights Memorial and Cultural Park

The Jing-Mei Human Rights Memorial and Cultural Park is an educational and recreational park, which was formerly a military prison. Today, it serves as a reminder of Taiwan’s dark past and provides visitors with an insight into the country’s political history. The museum houses a variety of exhibits and displays that document Taiwan’s martial history and also serves as a cultural center providing regular art and music events.

2. Sanxia Old Street

Sanxia Old Street is a well-preserved historical quarter, characterized by the traditional red-brick and tile-roofed shops. Here, you can find various traditional Taiwanese snacks and delicacies, including rice cakes and mochi. Sanxia Old Street is also an excellent place to shop for souvenirs, including handmade pottery, calligraphy items, and tea.

3. Lao Tse Culture Museum

The Lao Tse Culture Museum is a living museum and a great place to learn about Taoist philosophy and culture. The museum offers visitors access to a Taoist library and various relics, ancient texts, and artwork. Occasionally, seminars and workshops on Taoist philosophy are held for locals and tourists.

Natural Attractions of Shulin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Maokong Scenic Area

Maokong Scenic Area is a popular hiking spot that offers stunning views of Taipei City. The area is known for its sprawling tea plantations, and visitors can enjoy a cup of the famous Taiwan tea. The scenic area also offers an abundance of natural beauty, fresh air, and tranquility, making it an ideal place for a peaceful retreat.

2. Guanyin Mountain

Guanyin Mountain is an excellent spot for nature lovers and hikers. It offers fantastic views of Taipei City and the surrounding mountains. There are several hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult, so visitors can find a trail that suits their fitness level. Guanyin Mountain is also famous for its cherry blossoms in the spring, which attract many visitors from around Taiwan.

3. Shimen Reservoir

Shimen Reservoir is a stunning man-made reservoir and offers visitors a wide range of activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking. The reservoir is particularly famous for its water sports activities and provides visitors with a scenic and peaceful escape from the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shulin

1. Yingtaopu Trail

This trail is an eight-kilometer route that offers an amazing view of the Keelung River basin, old temples, and a refreshing waterfall. It is an intermediate hiking trail and would take around four hours to complete.

2. Green Corridor

The Green Corridor is a popular bike path that runs along the Keelung River. The bike path is thirty-six kilometers long and provides fantastic views of Taipei City. The path is well-prepared, and visitors can rent a bike at several bike rental shops in Shulin.

Best Things to Do in Shulin: Activities and Tours

1. Tea Tasting

Shulin is known for its tea plantations and is a great place to try some of the best teas in Taiwan. Visitors can take a tea-tasting tour of the Maokong Scenic Area, where they can learn about the art of tea-making and sample some of the most popular types of tea.

2. Night Market Tours

Shulin Night Market is a fantastic place to experience the street food culture of Taiwan. The market is full of food stalls offering a variety of Taiwanese snacks and delicacies, from stinky tofu to beef noodle soup. Visitors can take a night market tour and sample some of Shulin’s famous street food.

Food and Drink Scene of Shulin

Must-Try Dishes

1. Tofu Pudding

Tofu pudding is a popular dessert in Shulin, and it is a must-try dish for food lovers. It is made with soft tofu, sweet ginger syrup, and red beans. You can find this dish at the Shulin Night Market or at any of the local restaurants in town.

2. Beef Noodle Soup

Beef noodle soup is a staple dish in many Taiwanese restaurants, and Shulin is no exception. The soup is made with tender beef, fresh noodles, and flavorful herbs and spices. It is served with a side of pickled vegetables, making it a perfect meal any time of day.

Local Beverages

1. Taiwan Tea

Taiwan is famous for its tea, and Shulin is an excellent place to try some of the best teas that the country has to offer. The best places to try Taiwan tea are at the Maokong Scenic Area or at any of the local tea shops throughout Shulin.

2. Taiwan Beer

Taiwan Beer is a popular local beer and is an excellent way to try the local brew. It is light and refreshing and pairs well with spicy foods or snacks like peanuts and fried chicken.

Where to Stay in Shulin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. The Loft Hotel

The Loft Hotel is a beautiful boutique hotel located in the heart of Shulin. The hotel offers modern and luxurious rooms with excellent amenities, including an on-site restaurant, spa, and gym. The Loft Hotel is perfect for couples or business travelers.

2. Hua Fan Hotel

Hua Fan Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel that offers affordable and comfortable rooms. The hotel is located near the Shulin Night Market, making it an excellent choice for food and night market enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Shulin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Taiwan Tea

Taiwan tea makes an excellent souvenir or gift to bring home to family and friends. You can find a wide variety of teas at the Maokong Scenic Area or at any of the local tea shops throughout Shulin.

2. Pottery

Shulin is known for its traditional pottery, and visitors can find a wide variety of handmade pottery at the Sanxia Old Street. Pottery makes an excellent souvenir or gift, and visitors can choose from a variety of styles and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Shulin

Shulin has a unique and vibrant culture, and visitors should take care to respect local customs and traditions. It is customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple. In addition, waste segregation is taken seriously, so make sure to dispose of litter in the proper bins.

Best Time to Visit Shulin

The best time to visit Shulin is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms are in bloom. The summer months can be hot and humid, and the winter months can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shulin

Do:

  • Try Taiwan tea
  • Explore the night markets
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Speak loudly in public places
  • Disrespect local religion and beliefs

Safety and Health

Safety in Shulin

Shulin is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is always best to practice common sense and be cautious. Visitors should take care to secure their belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Shulin

Healthcare facilities in Shulin are excellent, and visitors can expect high-quality medical care from local doctors and hospitals. It is recommended that visitors purchase travel insurance before visiting Shulin.

Getting to Shulin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shulin

The nearest airport to Shulin is Taoyuan International Airport, which is approximately forty-five minutes away by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Shulin

Shulin Bus Station connects with several other cities and towns within Taiwan. It is located near the Shulin Night Market and is easily accessible by taxi or public transport.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the Taipei Metro to Shulin, which is a quick and affordable way to get to the district. The nearest metro station to Shulin is the Shulin Station on the Orange Line.

How to Get Around in Shulin

The best way to explore Shulin is on foot, bike, or by public transport. Visitors can rent a bike at one of the many bike rental shops in town or take the Taipei Metro to explore the nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Shulin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taipei City

Taipei City is just a thirty-minute drive or metro ride away from Shulin, making it an ideal place to visit next. Taipei is known for its vibrant nightlife, street food, and world-class museums and galleries.

Tamsui

Tamsui is a charming coastal town just a short distance from Shulin. It is known for its beautiful sunsets, fresh seafood, and old Dutch-style architecture.

Conclusion

Shulin offers visitors a wealth of attractions and experiences, from cultural and historical landmarks to outdoor activities and delicious local cuisine. With its easy access to Taipei City and other nearby destinations, Shulin is an ideal place to visit for anyone looking to experience the best of Taiwan.
