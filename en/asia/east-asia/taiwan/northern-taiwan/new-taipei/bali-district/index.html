Introduction to Bali District

Located in the New Taipei City of Northern Taiwan, Bali District is famed for its cultural attractions, natural beauty, and rich history. This charming destination is perfect for solo travelers, couples, and families alike. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the best of Bali District and plan a perfect itinerary for a memorable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Bali District

Bali District is home to a plethora of attractions, ranging from museums and historical landmarks to picturesque parks and natural wonders. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Bali District:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yingge Ceramics Museum: Discover the art and history of Taiwanese ceramics with a visit to this fascinating museum.
  • Sanxia Old Street: Wander through the charming alleyways of Sanxia Old Street, lined with traditional architecture, local shops, and street food stalls.
  • Zushi Temple: A popular destination for locals and tourists alike, Zushi Temple is a historical temple built during the Qing Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Bali District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jianshanpi Trail: Hike the scenic Jianshanpi Trail to enjoy breathtaking views of the Guanyin Mountain Range.
  • Bitan Scenic Area: A picturesque location beside the Xindian River, Bitan Scenic Area is a perfect destination for a relaxing afternoon picnic or a romantic evening stroll.
  • Yingge Rock: Explore the unique rock formations of Yingge Rock, with its stunning views of the coastline and crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bali District

Bali District is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of hiking and biking trails to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Caoling Historic Trail: This 8-kilometer trail offers a chance to explore historical landmarks, lush forests, and scenic waterfalls.
  • Pingxi Crags Trail: Hike the Pingxi Crags Trail to enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, with a chance to light a sky lantern at the end.
  • Guanyinshan Trail: A challenging hike with its steep ascent and rugged terrain, Guanyinshan Trail rewards visitors with panoramic views of the Taipei Basin at the top.

Best Things to Do in Bali District: Activities and Tours

Bali District offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to explore its cultural and natural attractions. Here are some of the top experiences:

  • Pottery Class in Yingge: Join a pottery class in Yingge and learn the art of making beautiful ceramics from local masters.
  • Taipei Zoo and Maokong Gondola: Explore the Taipei Zoo, one of the largest zoos in Asia, and take a scenic ride on the Maokong Gondola for panoramic views of the city.
  • Sanxia and Jiufen day tour: Join a day tour to explore the charming towns of Sanxia and Jiufen, with a chance to sample local delicacies and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Bali District

Bali District offers a range of traditional Taiwanese cuisine and famous dishes, as well as local beverages for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try food and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stinky Tofu: A local favorite, Stinky Tofu is deep-fried tofu with a pungent aroma and unique taste.
  • Bubble Tea: Originating from Taiwan, Bubble Tea is a refreshing drink with tea, milk, and tapioca balls.
  • Dan Bing: Dan Bing is a thin pancake rolled with egg and scallions, served with soy sauce and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Kaoliang Liquor: A strong liquor made from fermented sorghum, Kaoliang Liquor is a popular drink for celebrations and gatherings.
  • Taiwan Beer: Taiwan Beer is a refreshing lager beer, perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Oolong Tea: Taiwan is famous for its high-quality Oolong tea, with its unique flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Bali District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bali District offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top picks:

  • Monka Hotel: A trendy boutique hotel with spacious rooms, modern amenities, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Like Bali Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel with clean and comfortable dormitories, private rooms, and a communal kitchen.
  • Sleepbox Hotel Bali: A unique capsule hotel offering compact yet cozy rooms, with a common lounge area and shared bathroom facilities.

What to Buy in Bali District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bali District is renowned for its pottery, ceramics, and handicrafts, making it a perfect destination for souvenir shopping. Here are some top picks:

  • Yingge Ceramics: Handcrafted ceramics and pottery from Yingge make perfect gifts and souvenirs, with each piece carrying its own unique style and design.
  • Fengyuan Cakes: A sweet treat made from glutinous rice and stuffed with red bean paste or peanuts, Fengyuan Cakes are a popular snack for locals and tourists alike.
  • Calligraphy Brushes: Bali District is known for its traditional calligraphy brushes, with its soft bristles perfect for delicate strokes and lettering.

Local Culture and Customs of Bali District

Bali District is famous for its rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect the customs and traditions of the locals. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Temple Etiquette: When visiting temples or other religious sites, dress modestly and remove shoes before entering.
  • Greeting and Respect: Greet locals with a smile and a nod, and avoid touching or patting them on the head, which is considered disrespectful.
  • Smoking Ban: Smoking is prohibited in public places, including streets, restaurants, and bars.

Best Time to Visit Bali District

Bali District can be visited year-round, with its mild climate and comfortable temperatures throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the temperatures are pleasant and the rainfall is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bali District

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bali District:

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Sample authentic Taiwanese dishes and street food for a unique culinary experience.
  • Respect Local Customs: Respect the local culture, traditions, and beliefs, and be mindful of dress codes when visiting places of worship.
  • Learn Some Mandarin: Learning a few Mandarin phrases can go a long way in communicating with the locals and showing appreciation for the culture.

Don’t:

  • Littering: Do not litter or leave trash in public places, as it is considered disrespectful and can result in fines.
  • Disrespectful Behavior: Be respectful, polite, and courteous towards the locals, and avoid behavior that can be seen as inappropriate or offensive.
  • Photography Rules: Some places, such as temples, museums, and certain districts, may have strict rules on photography and videography. Follow the guidelines to avoid trouble.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bali District

Bali District is a safe and peaceful destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Traffic Safety: Be careful when crossing streets and walking on sidewalks, as traffic can be heavy and chaotic.
  • Natural Hazards: Be aware of natural hazards, such as typhoons, floods, and earthquakes, and follow the guidelines of local authorities in case of emergencies.

Health in Bali District

Bali District has a well-developed healthcare system, with hospitals and clinics offering quality medical services. However, visitors are advised to take some precautions for their health:

  • Water Safety: Drink only bottled or boiled water, and avoid raw or undercooked food to prevent gastrointestinal issues.
  • Insect Bites: Use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothes to prevent mosquito bites and other insect-related illnesses.

Getting to Bali District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bali District is located in the New Taipei City of Northern Taiwan, easily accessible via various transportation options:

Airports Near Bali District

  • Taipei Songshan Airport: Located within Taipei City, approximately 30 minutes by car or public transportation.
  • Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport: The main international airport in Taipei, approximately 60 minutes by car or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Bali District

  • Taipei Bus Station: Located in Taipei City, with multiple bus routes to Bali District and nearby areas.
  • New Taipei City Bus Station: Located in New Taipei City, with buses connecting to various destinations in Bali District and beyond.

Other Options

  • Metro: Taipei Metro (MRT) has several stations in Bali District, providing easy access to various attractions and landmarks.
  • Taxi/Car Rental: Taxis and car rental services are readily available, providing flexibility and convenience for travelers.

How to Get Around in Bali District

Bali District has a well-developed transportation system, making it easy and convenient for visitors to explore the region:

  • Metro: The Taipei Metro (MRT) has several stations in Bali District, connecting to various attractions and destinations.
  • Bus: Local buses connect different parts of Bali District, with frequent services and affordable fares.
  • Taxi/Car Rental: Taxis and car rental services are widely available, providing flexibility and convenience for travelers.

Where to Go Next from Bali District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bali District is a perfect base to explore nearby destinations and excursions in Taiwan:

  • Taipei City: The bustling capital city of Taiwan, featuring a range of cultural, historical, and modern attractions.
  • Jiufen: A charming town famed for its narrow alleys, traditional architecture, and scenic views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Wulai: A scenic town surrounded by lush forests and waterfalls, famous for its hot springs and Aboriginal culture.

Conclusion

Bali District is a perfect destination for travelers seeking to explore the cultural and natural attractions of Northern Taiwan. With its rich heritage, scenic landscapes, and friendly locals, Bali District offers a memorable experience for visitors of all ages and interests. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this vibrant destination.
