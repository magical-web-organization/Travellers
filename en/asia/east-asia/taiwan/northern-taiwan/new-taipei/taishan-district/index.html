Local Culture and Customs of Taishan District

Taiwan’s culture is a unique blend of Chinese, Japanese, and indigenous influences. Similarly, Taishan District has its customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of. Here are some customs and cultural traits that you might encounter during your visit:

  • Always take off your shoes before entering any house or temple.
  • Be mindful of the body language and avoid being too touchy or affectionate.
  • Respect elders and authority figures, bowing is a common way of showing respect.
  • Try to learn a few Mandarin phrases to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Taishan District

The best time to visit Taishan District is from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. Springtime from March to May is also a popular season for visitors to the region. Summers might be too hot and humid for some, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taishan District

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Try the local food and beverages
  3. Carry a map and a guidebook with you
  4. Ask for help if you need assistance

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect authorities or elders
  2. Leave your trash on the streets or in nature
  3. Expect locals to speak English fluently
  4. Be rude or impolite to anyone

Safety and Health

Safety in Taishan District

Taishan District is generally safe for visitors. However, it is always a good idea to take basic precautions such as keeping your valuables with you and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Taishan District

Tap water in Taiwan is safe to drink, but it is always a good idea to stick to bottled water when you are traveling. Medical facilities are readily available in Taishan District, and there are plenty of pharmacies and clinics that you can visit in case of an emergency.

Getting to Taishan District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taishan District is well connected to other parts of Taiwan via various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Taishan District

The nearest airport is Taipei Songshan Airport, which is around 16 kilometers away from Taishan District.

Bus Stations Near Taishan District

The most convenient bus stations are Taishan Station and Taishan Interchange.

Other Options

You can also reach Taishan District via taxis or rental cars. However, rental cars are not recommended for foreigners unless you have experience driving in Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Taishan District

Taishan District is well connected by buses, taxis, and the Taipei Metro. Renting a bicycle is another popular way to explore the district, especially if you plan to go on hiking or biking routes.

Where to Go Next from Taishan District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taishan District is a great base to explore other attractions and destinations in the region, including:

  1. The bustling city of Taipei, with its temples, markets, and food scene.
  2. The scenic Beitou hot springs area, known for its hot springs and hiking trails.
  3. The historic old town of Tamsui, with its riverside promenade, markets, and temples.

Conclusion

Taishan District is a hidden gem in Northern Taiwan that offers visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty and rich culture of the region. From hiking and biking routes to museums and temples, there is something for everyone in Taishan District. Plan your trip today and make lasting memories in this unique corner of Taiwan.
