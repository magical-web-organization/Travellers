Introduction to Shenkeng

Shenkeng is a district in the northeastern part of New Taipei City, Taiwan. It was once a prosperous coal-mining town, but today, it is known for its tofu-making industry, temples, and scenic hiking trails. The town is easily accessible from Taipei, making it a popular getaway for city-dwellers looking for a quick escape into nature.

List of Places to Visit in Shenkeng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shenkeng Old Street

Located in the heart of Shenkeng town, Shenkeng Old Street is a popular tourist destination that features a variety of local snacks and souvenirs. The street is lined with traditional Taiwanese buildings that date back to the Qing Dynasty (1644-1912).

Shenkeng Tofu Culture Museum

The Shenkeng Tofu Culture Museum is a fascinating place that tells the story of the town’s tofu-making industry. Visitors can learn how tofu is made, see antique tofu-making equipment, and sample different types of tofu.

Shenkeng Fushoushan Farm

The Shenkeng Fushoushan Farm is a unique attraction that sits atop a mountain overlooking the town. The farm is known for its strawberry fields and butterfly garden, where visitors can observe a variety of butterfly species.

Natural Attractions of Shenkeng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Elephant Mountain Hiking Trail

The Elephant Mountain Hiking Trail is one of the most popular hiking trails in Taiwan. The trail offers stunning views of Taipei 101, the Xinyi District, and the surrounding mountains. The hike takes approximately 30-45 minutes to complete and is suitable for all skill levels.

Shenkeng District Biking Trail

The Shenkeng District Biking Trail is a scenic 20-kilometer cycling route that takes visitors through the countryside and up into the mountains. Along the way, cyclists will pass through picturesque villages, rice paddies, and fruit orchards.

Maokong Gondola

The Maokong Gondola is a cable car that takes visitors up into the mountains for panoramic views of Taipei City and the surrounding countryside. The gondola ride is approximately 4.3 kilometers long and takes about 20-30 minutes to travel one way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shenkeng

Elephant Mountain Hiking Trail

The Elephant Mountain Hiking Trail is a short but challenging hike that offers stunning views of Taipei City. The trail is well-maintained and easy to follow, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

Shenkeng District Biking Trail

The Shenkeng District Biking Trail is a 20-kilometer cycling route that takes visitors through the beautiful countryside and up into the mountains. Along the way, cyclists can enjoy scenic views of rice paddies, fruit orchards, and charming villages.

Best Things to Do in Shenkeng: Activities and Tours

Shenkeng Tofu Culture Tour

The Shenkeng Tofu Culture Tour is a popular activity for foodies and history buffs alike. The tour takes visitors through the town’s tofu-making facilities, where they can observe the process, learn about the history of tofu-making in Shenkeng, and sample different types of tofu.

Shenkeng Night Market

The Shenkeng Night Market is a bustling hub of activity after dark. The market features a variety of local snacks and souvenirs, as well as games and entertainment for all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Shenkeng

Must-Try Dishes

Stinky Tofu

Stinky Tofu is a staple of Taiwanese cuisine and a must-try for adventurous foodies. The tofu is fermented for several days in a brine of vegetables and meat, resulting in a pungent aroma and a unique flavor.

Tofu Pudding

Tofu Pudding is another popular dish in Shenkeng. The dish is made from smooth and silky tofu that is flavored with sweet syrup and toppings such as peanuts, red beans, and fruit.

Local Beverages

Bubble Tea

Bubble Tea is a Taiwanese specialty that has taken the world by storm. The drink is made from sweetened tea, milk, and chewy tapioca balls that give the drink its signature “bubble” texture.

Taiwan Beer

Taiwan Beer is a crisp and refreshing lager that is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike. The beer is made using locally sourced ingredients, including aromatic Taiwanese hops.

Where to Stay in Shenkeng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shenkeng Homestays

Shenkeng Homestays are a popular choice among budget-conscious travelers. These small guesthouses offer clean, comfortable rooms, and a chance to experience Taiwanese hospitality firsthand.

Private Airbnb Homes

Private Airbnb Homes are another option for those looking for affordable accommodation in Shenkeng. These homes offer a chance to live like a local and experience daily life in Taiwan.

What to Buy in Shenkeng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tofu Products

Tofu Products, including traditional blocks of tofu, flavored tofu, and tofu pudding, are popular souvenirs to bring home from Shenkeng.

Tea

Tea is another popular product of Shenkeng, particularly Oolong tea, which is grown in the surrounding hills. Visitors can purchase tea leaves from local shops and factories.

Local Culture and Customs of Shenkeng

Shenkeng is a predominantly Hakka town, and visitors can see glimpses of Hakka culture in the local architecture, food, and traditions. Traditional festivals and ceremonies, such as the Shenkeng Qingshan Temple Festival, also offer a chance to experience local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Shenkeng

The best time to visit Shenkeng is in the spring or fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shenkeng

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food, including stinky tofu and tofu pudding.
  • Hike up Elephant Mountain for stunning views of Taipei City.

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize historical sites.
  • Disrespect local traditions or religious practices.
  • Wear revealing clothing in temples or other sacred places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shenkeng

Shenkeng is a safe place for visitors; however, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Visitors should also take care when hiking or biking on mountain trails.

Health in Shenkeng

Taiwan is generally a safe and healthy destination. Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations and take adequate precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever.

Getting to Shenkeng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shenkeng

The nearest airport to Shenkeng is Taipei Songshan Airport, which is approximately 13 kilometers away. Taoyuan International Airport is also a popular option, with many international flights arriving each day.

Bus Stations Near Shenkeng

There are several bus stations in Taipei City that offer services to Shenkeng, including Taipei Main Station, Ximen Bus Station, and Taipei City Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or Uber from Taipei City to Shenkeng, which takes approximately 20-30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Shenkeng

Shenkeng is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take local buses to nearby attractions or hire a taxi for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Shenkeng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taipei City

Taipei City is just a short drive or bus ride away from Shenkeng, making it an ideal destination for a day trip. Visitors can explore the city’s vibrant night markets, museums, and historic landmarks, including Taipei 101, the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall, and the National Palace Museum.

Jiufen

Jiufen is a mountain town located on the north coast of Taiwan, famous for its winding alleyways and picturesque tea houses. Visitors can explore the town’s narrow streets, sample local delicacies, and take in the breathtaking coastal views.

Conclusion

Shenkeng is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by travelers to Taiwan. With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and delicious food, it’s the perfect getaway for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of Taipei City. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, exploring local culture, or trying new foods, Shenkeng has something to offer for everyone.
