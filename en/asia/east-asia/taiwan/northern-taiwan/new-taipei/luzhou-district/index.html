Your Complete Travel Guide to Luzhou District, Taiwan

Located in New Taipei, Luzhou District is a beautiful blend of culture, history, and nature. With its rich heritage, beautiful scenery, and fascinating landmarks, it’s a destination that should not be missed. Here’s our comprehensive guide to everything you need to know about Luzhou District, Taiwan.

Introduction to Luzhou District

Luzhou District is a diverse district that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re into history, culture, or nature, there’s something in Luzhou for you. The district has a rich history that dates back to the Qing Dynasty, and you can see this in the various historical and cultural attractions located throughout the area. Luzhou is also a great place for nature enthusiasts as there are plenty of outdoor activities and scenic spots to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Luzhou District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. New Taipei City Yingge Ceramics Museum - Known as the “Pottery Town,” Yingge is the perfect place to learn more about Taiwan’s pottery culture, and the museum is the perfect place to start.
  2. Xinzhuang Historical Home - This Qing-era home provides a fascinating look into the life of the aristocracy at the time.
  3. Tzuchi Gong - A temple in Luzhou that dates back to the 1700s.

Natural Attractions of Luzhou District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Zhouji Trail - This trail takes you through beautiful mountains, valleys, and rivers, will showcase the natural beauty the area has to offer.
  2. Zhoumei Park - Located along the Tamsui River, this park is a perfect place to take a stroll and enjoy the scenery.
  3. Dajia Riverside Park - A great place for a picnic or to rent a bike and take a leisurely bike ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luzhou District

  1. Dajia Riverside Park to Guandu Nature Park Bike Trail - This 7.8-kilometer trail is perfect for nature lovers with plenty of opportunities to view birds and other wildlife.
  2. Zhoumei Park to Daming Temple Day Hike - A moderate 4-kilometer hike that affords stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Luzhou District: Activities and Tours

  1. Craft Beer Tasting at the Yonghe Brewery - This brewery has been around since the 1920s, and they offer tours and tastings of their incredible craft beers.
  2. Hot Spring Experience at the Fushan Hot Springs - Relax and unwind in these natural hot springs that are surrounded by beautiful scenery.
  3. Visit the Luzhou Night Market - This bustling night market has a wide variety of dishes to try, from traditional Taiwanese cuisine to more unique options.

Week-Long Itinerary for Luzhou District

Day Destination
1 Visit the New Taipei City Yingge Ceramics Museum and try your hand at pottery making.
2 Take a hike along Zhouji Trail in the morning and relax at Fushan Hot Springs in the afternoon.
3 Enjoy a day in nature by spending the day at Zhoumei Park and Dajia Riverside Park.
4 Take a bike tour of the area, starting at Dajia Riverside Park and ending at Guandu Nature Park.
5 Explore the historical sites of Xinzhuang Historical Home and Tzuchi Gong.
6 Spend the day trying out different craft beers at Yonghe Brewery.
7 Finish the week by exploring Luzhou Night Market and trying some of the delicious street food options.

Food and Drink Scene of Luzhou District

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Braised Pork Rice - A staple in Taiwanese cuisine, this dish of tender pork on a bed of fragrant rice is a must-try.
  2. Oyster Omelets - A popular street food dish in Taiwan, these omelets are packed with fresh oysters and are delicious.
  3. Stinky Tofu - A dish unique to Taiwan, it’s one of the most loved or hated dishes in the country.

Local Beverages

  1. Bubble Tea - Also known as boba tea, it’s a Taiwanese classic that can now be found all over the world.
  2. Taiwan Beer - A beer that’s been brewed in Taiwan since 1945 and is the perfect accompaniment to any meal.

Where to Stay in Luzhou District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Park City Hotel Luzhou Taipei - A modern hotel in the heart of Luzhou with perfect access to all of the major attractions in the area.
  2. Color Fun Inn - A cozy hostel with a friendly atmosphere that’s perfect for travelers looking for a more budget-friendly option.

What to Buy in Luzhou District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pottery and Ceramics - Pottery is Luzhou’s specialty, so make sure to pick up a piece to take home.
  2. Tea - Taiwan is known for its tea, and Luzhou has plenty of tea shops where you can pick up some of the best.

Local Culture and Customs of Luzhou District

  • Luzhou is a conservative area, so dress modestly when visiting religious sites like temples.
  • Always remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Tipping is not customary, but it’s always appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Luzhou District

The best time to visit Luzhou is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. The summer months can be hot and humid, while winter temperatures can drop quite low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luzhou District

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Visit the cultural and historical attractions.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful in religious sites.
  • Waste food or drink.
  • Be too loud in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Luzhou District

Luzhou District is generally a safe destination, but it’s always a good idea to take the usual precautions, such as avoiding dark alleys at night and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Luzhou District

There are no major health concerns in Luzhou, and the tap water is safe to drink. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Luzhou District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Luzhou District

The closest airport to Luzhou District is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is approximately a 40-minute drive to the district.

Bus Stations Near Luzhou District

The most convenient bus station to Luzhou is the Yonghe Bus Station, which offers frequent connections to the district.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also options for getting to Luzhou, but be prepared for some heavy traffic depending on the time of day.

How to Get Around in Luzhou District

The easiest way to get around Luzhou is by public transportation. The district is well-connected by buses and trains, and both are affordable and convenient.

Where to Go Next from Luzhou District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Yingge - A town that’s famous for its pottery and ceramics.
  2. Danshui - A popular tourist destination known for its scenic views and seafood.
  3. Beitou - A hot springs destination famous for its geothermal activity and natural hot springs.

Conclusion

Luzhou District is a unique and beautiful destination that is sure to satisfy any traveler’s appetite for culture, history, and nature. From trying out the local food and drink to exploring the historical sites and taking in the scenery, there’s something for everyone in Luzhou. So don’t hesitate to include Luzhou in your next trip to Taiwan.
