Introduction to Sanchong

Sanchong, located in New Taipei, Northern Taiwan, is a rapidly growing city with a rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, a vibrant food scene, and warm and hospitable locals. It is conveniently located close to Taipei City, making it an ideal destination for a day trip or an extended stay. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, cultural customs, and more in Sanchong.

List of Places to Visit in Sanchong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sanchong has a wealth of museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the city’s cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Sanhe Night Market: A bustling night market with a plethora of street foods, shopping, and more.

  • Yingge Old Street: A historic street that is home to dozens of stores selling pottery, ceramics, and other handicraft items.

  • Yingge Ceramics Museum: A museum dedicated to the art of ceramics that houses a vast collection of ancient and contemporary pottery pieces.

  • Laojia Tou Park: A public park that is an ideal spot for taking a leisurely walk or a picnic.

Natural Attractions of Sanchong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sanchong has a wide range of outdoor adventures and scenic views to offer to nature enthusiasts. Some of the top outdoor attractions are:

  • Horseshoe Bend Trail: A popular hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountain range.

  • Longfengshan Forest Park: A nature reserve that is home to an array of flora and fauna species.

  • Jinzhenzhi Waterfall: A stunning waterfall that cascades down from a height of 40 meters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sanchong

Sanchong has numerous trails and routes that are perfect for hiking and biking. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in Sanchong are:

  • Xishan Hiking Trail: A 5.5-km trail that is perfect for intermediate level hikers.

  • Lingzhi Biking Trail: A well-maintained 10-km trail that is ideal for mountain biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Sanchong: Activities and Tours

Sanchong offers a multitude of activities and tours to cater to every tourist’s interests. Some of the most popular things to do in Sanchong are:

  • Taipei 101 Tour: A guided tour to the iconic Taipei 101 building that offers stunning views of the cityscape.

  • Street Food Tour: A food tour that takes you through the bustling night markets of Sanchong and Taipei City.

  • Hot Spring Tour: A day tour to the Wulai Hot Spring that offers a relaxing and rejuvenating experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Sanchong

Taiwan, in general, is famous for its vibrant food scene, and Sanchong is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Sanchong:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Xiao long bao: A steamed dumpling filled with soup and pork.

  • Three-cup chicken: A delicious chicken dish cooked in soy sauce, rice wine, and sesame oil.

  • Beef noodle soup: A popular noodle soup dish that is filled with tender beef chunks and aromatic herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble tea: A refreshing tea-based drink that originated in Taiwan and is now popular worldwide.

  • Taiwan Beer: A light and refreshing beer that is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Sanchong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sanchong has a wide range of accommodation options suitable for every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sanchong:

  • Ximen Citizen Hotel: A modern and stylish hotel that offers excellent amenities and convenient location.

  • Solo Singer Inn: A cozy guesthouse that offers a home-away-from-home experience.

  • Happy Family Hostel: A friendly and affordable hostel that is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Sanchong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sanchong is famous for its handmade pottery and ceramics products. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Pottery and ceramics items: Beautiful and intricate pottery items make for the perfect souvenir to remind you of your trip.

  • Tea: Taiwan is famous for its wide variety of tea blends, and Sanchong is no exception.

Local Culture and Customs of Sanchong

The culture in Sanchong is heavily influenced by Chinese traditions and customs. It is customary to greet elders or seniors first and to address people by their titles or last names. Tipping is not expected but is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Sanchong

The best time to visit Sanchong is from March to May, and September to November when the temperatures are mild, and the skies are clear. Avoid the summer months (June to August) as they can be extremely hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sanchong

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Carry a copy of your passport at all times.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local religious beliefs and practices.

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sanchong

Sanchong is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Use common sense and avoid any suspicious or shady areas.

Health in Sanchong

Taiwan has an excellent healthcare system, and medical facilities in Sanchong are no exception. It is always a good idea to carry a comprehensive travel insurance plan that covers emergency medical care.

Getting to Sanchong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sanchong is easily accessible from Taipei City and other major cities in Taiwan. Here are some transportation options to get to Sanchong:

  • Taipei Taoyuan International Airport: The nearest international airport to Sanchong is the Taipei Taoyuan International Airport.

  • Bus: Buses are a convenient and affordable way to get to Sanchong from Taipei City and other parts of Taiwan.

  • Taxi: Taxis are easily available but can be expensive compared to buses and trains.

How to Get Around in Sanchong

Sanchong has a comprehensive public transportation system that includes buses and trains. Both options are convenient and affordable. Taxis are also easily available, but fares can be higher.

Where to Go Next from Sanchong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sanchong is located close to several other popular destinations in Taiwan. Here are some of the best places to visit nearby:

  • Taipei City: Taipei City is just a short train ride away and offers a wide range of cultural, historical, and modern attractions.

  • Yilan: Yilan is just a short drive away and is known for its natural hot springs, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Sanchong, with its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, delicious food, and friendly locals, has something for every traveler. A visit to this charming city is a must-do on any trip to Taiwan.
