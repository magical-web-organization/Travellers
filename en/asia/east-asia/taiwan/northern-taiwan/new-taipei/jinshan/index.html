Introduction to Jinshan

Jinshan is a beautiful coastal town located in New Taipei, Northern Taiwan. From its stunning landscapes to rich cultural history, Jinshan offers some of Taiwan’s most awe-inspiring experiences.

Visitors flock to Jinshan for its beautiful sandy beaches, sparkling blue waters, and abundant natural resources. Beyond the beautiful scenery, Jinshan is a town steeped in history and tradition, offering cultural attractions and historical landmarks, as well as hiking and biking trails, water sports, and more.

Read on for our comprehensive guide to Jinshan, Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Jinshan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jinshan has a rich history that is reflected in its many cultural attractions, from museums to ancient temples. Here are some must-see cultural landmarks in Jinshan:

  • Jinshan Old Street: This historic street is lined with traditional shops and food vendors. Here, you’ll find a wide range of cultural and historical landmarks, including the Jinshan Presbyterian Church, the Shangyuan Temple, and more.
  • Jinshan Folk Culture Centre: This museum displays the rich folk customs and practices of the region, showcasing everything from traditional crafts to food and beverage traditions.
  • Caoling Historic Trail: This trail is full of historic landmarks, from the stone pagoda of the Datunshan Temple to the former site of the Taiwanese Railroad Company. Along the way, you’ll also find stunning views of the coastline and nearby mountains.

Natural Attractions of Jinshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jinshan is home to some of Taiwan’s most breathtaking natural attractions, from beautiful beaches to lush forests. Here are some of the best natural wonders to explore in Jinshan:

  • Jinshan Beach: Known for its pristine sand and blue waters, Jinshan Beach is a popular destination for both locals and visitors. Spend a day swimming, sunbathing, or surfing in the waves.
  • Yeliou Geopark: This geopark is home to unique rock formations, including the famous Queen’s Head rock. Visitors can explore the park’s many trails, scenic viewpoints, and learn about the region’s geological history.
  • Fengguikou Nature Trail: This trail is ideal for hiking and is known for its stunning natural landscapes, including waterfalls, lush forests, and stunning mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jinshan

Jinshan is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, offering hundreds of kilometers of trails for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try in Jinshan:

  • Jinshan Youth Activity Centre Trail: This trail takes visitors through stunning forest landscapes, past waterfalls, and ends with sweeping views of the coast.
  • Caoling Historic Trail: This trail is full of historic landmarks and stunning views of the coastline and nearby mountains.
  • Yehliu Geopark: This park has over 20 trails to explore, each offering unique viewpoints and natural landscapes to discover.

Best Things to Do in Jinshan: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many cultural and natural attractions, Jinshan also offers a variety of exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best ways to spend your time in Jinshan:

  • Surfing: Hit the waves at Jinshan Beach, where visitors can rent boards and take surfing lessons.
  • Beach Hopping: Jinshan is home to dozens of beaches, from small coves to large stretches of sand, making it the ideal destination for a beach-hopping adventure.
  • Hot Springs: Jinshan’s hot springs are world-renowned for their therapeutic properties, offering visitors a chance to relax, rejuvenate, and take in stunning natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jinshan

Day 1: Arrival and check-in, followed by a visit to Jinshan Old Street and the Shangyuan Temple.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Datunshan to see the Shimen Reservoir, followed by a visit to the Jinshan Folk Culture Centre.

Day 3: Explore the Yeliou Geopark and visit the Queen’s Head rock formation.

Day 4: Head to Jinshan Beach for a day of swimming and surfing, followed by a relaxing soak in one of the town’s hot springs.

Day 5: Take a bike tour of the city, visiting local landmarks and the Fengguikou Nature Trail.

Day 6: Visit the Zhonghe Shrine before enjoying a sunset dinner at one of Jinshan’s seaside restaurants.

Day 7: Departure from the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Jinshan

Must-Try Dishes

Jinshan is known for its seafood, but visitors can also find a wide range of other local dishes to try, including:

  • Tofu Pudding: A sweet dessert made from soy milk
  • Seafood Noodles: Any dish made from fresh seafood, typically served with noodles
  • Sweet Potato Fries: Freshly fried sweet potato fries made with locally sourced sweet potatoes

Local Beverages

Jinshan’s hot springs are famous for their natural springs, which have been used for centuries to create unique local beverages. Some popular local drinks include:

  • Hot Spring Water: Straight from the hot springs and believed to have many health benefits
  • Bamboo Vinegar: Made from the bamboo charcoal used to filter water in the area
  • Plum Juice: Made from locally grown plums and believed to be a natural cure for sore throats

Where to Stay in Jinshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jinshan is home to a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jinshan:

  • Gloria Prince Hotel: A luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the nearby mountains and coastline
  • Bali Jump Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers private and dorm-style rooms
  • Happiness Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Jinshan Old Street

What to Buy in Jinshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jinshan offers a wide range of unique souvenirs and locally made products that make the perfect gift or memento of your trip. Here are some favorite local products to bring home:

  • Sea Salt: Jinshan’s sea salt is famous for its pure, natural flavor and is a must-buy for foodies.
  • Traditional Crafts: Visitors can find a wide range of traditional crafts in Jinshan Old Street, including pottery, embroidery, and more.
  • Hot Spring Products: Many of Jinshan’s hot springs are known for their therapeutic properties, and visitors can bring home a range of skincare and wellness products made from hot spring water.

Local Culture and Customs of Jinshan

Jinshan is a town with many unique cultural customs and practices, many of which are reflected in the town’s landmarks and attractions. Visitors are encouraged to learn more about the region’s traditions, including:

  • Taiwan’s Indigenous Culture: Jinshan is home to a number of Indigenous communities, and visitors can learn about their customs and way of life at local museums and cultural centers.
  • Religious Festivals: Jinshan’s Shangyuan Temple and other landmarks regularly host religious festivals and ceremonies, which offer a chance to learn more about local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Jinshan

The best time to visit Jinshan is from March to May or September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Visitors can avoid the summer crowds and the high temperatures, which can often reach 90°F or more.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jinshan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes in religious sites.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and hot springs.
  • Try traditional local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Raise your voice or become aggressive in public, as this is considered very impolite in Taiwanese culture.
  • Leave trash or litter in natural or cultural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jinshan

Jinshan is a very safe place to visit. Crime rates in the region are quite low, and visitors are generally free to explore without worry. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take basic precautions against petty theft and other crimes.

Health in Jinshan

Visitors to Jinshan are advised to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and should take basic precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses. Visitors should also use sunscreen and take proper measures to stay hydrated in the town’s hot and humid climate.

Getting to Jinshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jinshan

The nearest airport to Jinshan is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport. From the airport, visitors have several options to reach Jinshan, including taxis, shuttle buses, or rented cars.

Bus Stations Near Jinshan

Jinshan is well-connected by bus, with regular services operating to and from Taipei, Yilan, and other regional destinations. Visitors can check the schedules and routes on the Taiwan Tourism Bureau’s website.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Jinshan by taxi or rented car, from Taipei or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Jinshan

Jinshan is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take taxis or use the regional bus services to get around.

Where to Go Next from Jinshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jinshan is located near several other popular destinations in Taiwan, including:

  • Taipei: Taiwan’s bustling capital city is just a short drive or bus ride away from Jinshan, offering visitors a chance to explore the city’s many cultural attractions and vibrant nightlife.
  • Yilan: This nearby city is home to a number of interesting museums, traditional markets, and other cultural landmarks.
  • Hsinchu: Known for its stunning parks, hot spring resorts, and rich history, Hsinchu is an ideal place to visit after exploring Jinshan.

Conclusion

Jinshan is a town that offers a wide range of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions, making it the perfect destination for visitors looking to experience the best of Taiwan. From its beautiful beaches to its lush forests and hot springs, Jinshan has something to offer everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your ultimate trip to Jinshan, Taiwan.
