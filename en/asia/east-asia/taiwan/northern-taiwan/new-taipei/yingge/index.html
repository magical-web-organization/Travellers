Introduction to Yingge

Nestled in the vibrant city of New Taipei, Yingge is a charming suburb that boasts incredible natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and world-renowned ceramic art. Known as the “Ceramic Capital of Taiwan,” Yingge is home to countless pottery studios, museums, and shops that are a testament to the town’s age-old tradition of producing exquisite ceramics.

Whether you’re an art-lover, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or culture aficionado, Yingge is the perfect destination for you. In this definitive guide, we’ll explore all the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and culture to experience in Yingge.

List of Places to Visit in Yingge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yingge Ceramics Museum: One of Taiwan’s finest museums, the Yingge Ceramics Museum showcases over 2000 ceramic works from around the world, including ancient Chinese ceramics, Japanese Raku ceramics, and Taiwanese contemporary pottery. The museum also offers a series of workshops and classes on ceramic making.

  • Sanxia Old Street: A charming pedestrian street lined with traditional Taiwanese buildings, Sanxia Old Street is a must-visit for history buffs and culture enthusiasts. You can sample delicious local street food, shop for souvenirs and handicrafts, and get a glimpse into the daily lives of Taiwanese people in the past.

Natural Attractions of Yingge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Daxi Scenic Area: A delightful spot for those who love nature and outdoor activities, the Daxi Scenic Area boasts breath-taking landscapes, peaceful forests, and winding trails ideal for hiking, cycling or simply enjoying a leisurely stroll. The highlight of the place is its waterfalls, which are simply stunning.

  • Yingge Rock: A massive boulder perched on top of a hill, the Yingge Rock offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the town and surrounding areas. It’s perfect for taking pictures, enjoying the sunset, and admiring the natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yingge

  • Yingge Bike Trail: A scenic bike trail that runs along the Dahan River, the Yingge Bike Trail is perfect for cyclists of all ages and abilities. The trail starts from the Yingge Railway Station and goes all the way to the Daxi Scenic Area, passing through picturesque countryside scenery and several attractions en route.

Best Things to Do in Yingge: Activities and Tours

  • Ceramic Making Workshops: For anyone interested in the art of ceramics, Yingge offers plenty of opportunities to learn and practice it. Many studios offer workshops for visitors to make their own pottery or paint a ceramic figurine, and you can take home your creations as unique keepsakes.

  • Night Market in Sanxia: Open every weekend, the Sanxia Night Market is a must-visit for foodies and shopaholics. The market is packed with street vendors selling a wide range of local street foods, snacks, and drinks, as well as clothes, accessories, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yingge

Food and Drink Scene of Yingge

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dan Bing: a type of Taiwanese omelet filled with spring onions, served with soy sauce or chili sauce.

  • Beef Noodle Soup: a bowl of hearty and flavorful beef broth with noodles, braised beef, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble tea: invented in Taiwan, bubble tea is a popular drink made with tea, milk, and chewy tapioca pearls.

  • Taiwan Beer: a refreshing, light-bodied lager that is popular among locals and tourists alike.

Where to Stay in Yingge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fuxing Villa Hotel: A stylish and modern hotel located near the town center, Fuxing Villa Hotel boasts well-appointed rooms, excellent service, and a restaurant serving delicious Taiwanese cuisine.

  • Hua Du Hotel: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to stay in the heart of Yingge, Hua Du Hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Yingge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramic Tableware: Yingge’s pottery studios offer a wide range of exquisite ceramic tableware, from plates and cups to teapots and vases.

  • Tea: Taiwan is famous for its high-quality tea, and you can find many tea shops in Yingge selling locally sourced and blended tea leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Yingge

Yingge is a town deeply rooted in traditional Taiwanese culture, and its people are friendly and welcoming to visitors. It’s customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home, and it’s polite to bow when greeting elders or people of higher status.

Best Time to Visit Yingge

The best time to visit Yingge is between October and December when the weather is cool and dry, and the autumn foliage is at its peak. Spring (March to April) is also a good time to visit when the cherry blossoms are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yingge

Do:

  • Learn some basic Mandarin phrases before visiting.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head or pat someone on the back, as it’s considered impolite.

  • Leave chopsticks lying on the bowl or plate after finishing a meal.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yingge

Yingge is a safe and peaceful town, and crime rates are relatively low. However, tourists should be mindful of their belongings and avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuable items when travelling. It’s also a good idea to take precautions against pickpockets in crowded places.

Health in Yingge

Tap water in Yingge is safe to drink, and medical care is widely available in Taiwan. However, it’s advisable to take out travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and accidents.

Getting to Yingge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yingge

The nearest international airport to Yingge is Taipei Taoyuan International Airport, which serves flights from major international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Yingge

Yingge Bus Station is the primary bus station in the town, serving buses to nearby towns and Taipei City.

Other Options

  • The Taiwan High-Speed Rail connects Taipei City to southern and western parts of the island, with a stop at Taoyuan Station.

  • Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire in Yingge.

How to Get Around in Yingge

The most convenient and eco-friendly way to explore Yingge is on foot, as most attractions are within walking distance. Cycling is another popular option, and you can rent bicycles at several places in town.

Where to Go Next from Yingge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Danshui: A coastal town that used to be a major port of Northern Taiwan, known for its historic buildings, local delicacies, and stunning sunsets.

  • Shifen: A charming old town famous for its sky lantern festival, where visitors can release lanterns into the sky with wishes written on them.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and diverse range of activities, Yingge is a destination that has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip to this delightful town and explore its wonders. Happy travels!
