Introduction to Tucheng

Tucheng, a district in New Taipei City, is a hidden gem in East Asia that’s often overlooked by travelers. This small town has a rich history, scenic beauty, and delicious food that make it a must-visit destination in Taiwan. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Tucheng, including the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Tucheng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tucheng has a rich history that dates back to the Qing Dynasty. Here are some of the top culturally important places to visit in Tucheng:

  • Tucheng Old Street: This street features traditional architecture and shops selling local products such as ceramics, souvenirs, and delicious street food.

  • Tucheng Fort: This fort was built in the 19th century to defend Tamsui River from pirates and foreign invaders. Today, it’s a popular tourist attraction with a history museum and panoramic views of the city.

  • Taipei County Yingge Ceramics Museum: This museum showcases historic and contemporary ceramics made by local and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Tucheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tucheng is also home to many beautiful natural attractions. Here’s a list of our favorites:

  • Shuliaozi and Baishawan Beach: These beaches are located on the northern coast of Taiwan and offer beautiful views of the Pacific Ocean.

  • Guanyinshan National Scenic Area: This mountain provides panoramic views of the coastline and is home to several hiking trails.

  • Wulai Waterfall: This waterfall is located in the nearby Wulai district and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tucheng

Tucheng is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Caoshan Trail: This trail is located in Guanyinshan National Scenic Area and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and ocean.

  • Tucheng River Cycling Path: This path follows the Tucheng River and offers a scenic route for biking enthusiasts of all levels.

  • Jingmei Trail: This trail is located in nearby Wenshan district and offers stunning views of the Taipei city skyline.

Best Things to Do in Tucheng: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Tucheng offers a variety of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Tucheng:

  • Taipei Night Tour: This tour takes visitors on a nighttime tour of Taipei, including a visit to Taipei 101 and a traditional Taiwanese dinner.

  • Scooter Tour: This tour takes visitors on a scenic ride through the countryside, visiting local farms and scenic spots.

  • Hot Spring Tour: Tucheng is home to several hot springs, and this tour takes visitors on a relaxing journey to soak in the healing waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tucheng

To help you plan your time in Tucheng, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Tucheng Fort and Old Street
  • Day 2: Hike Caoshan Trail and visit Guanyinshan National Scenic Area
  • Day 3: Take a scooter tour of the countryside and visit a local farm
  • Day 4: Relax in the hot springs
  • Day 5: Visit Taipei County Yingge Ceramics Museum
  • Day 6: Visit Baishawan Beach and Shuliaozi Beach
  • Day 7: Explore the Wulai district and visit Wulai Waterfall

Food and Drink Scene of Tucheng

No trip to Taiwan is complete without trying the delicious food. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to try in Tucheng:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lu Rou Fan: This dish consists of braised pork served over rice and is a popular comfort food in Taiwan.

  • Taiwanese Fried Chicken: This dish is made by marinating chicken in soy sauce and spices, then coating it in sweet potato starch and deep-frying it.

  • Taiwanese Oyster Omelet: This dish consists of a thin, crispy omelet filled with small oysters and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Tea: This beverage originated in Taiwan and consists of tea mixed with milk and tapioca pearls.

  • Taiwanese Beer: Taiwan has a growing craft beer scene, and many local breweries offer delicious and unique beers to try.

Where to Stay in Tucheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tucheng offers a variety of accommodation options to fit any budget. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Tucheng:

  • Grand Forward Hotel: This hotel offers modern rooms and is located close to Tucheng Fort and Old Street.

  • GoGo Hotel: This hotel is located near the MRT and offers affordable rooms with friendly staff.

  • Fuhong Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Tucheng and offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Tucheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tucheng is home to many local products and souvenirs that make great gifts for friends and family back home. Here are some of the top products to buy in Tucheng:

  • Ceramics: Tucheng is known for its ceramics, and there are many shops selling traditional and contemporary pieces.

  • Tea: Taiwan produces some of the best teas in the world, and there are many tea shops selling high-quality blends.

Local Culture and Customs of Tucheng

Taiwanese culture is a unique blend of Chinese, Japanese, and Western influences. Here are some of the local customs to keep in mind when visiting Tucheng:

  • Respect for Elders: In Taiwanese culture, elders are highly respected, and it’s important to show deference and respect to them.

  • Face Saving: “Face” is an important concept in Taiwanese culture, and it’s important to avoid embarrassing or shaming someone in public.

Best Time to Visit Tucheng

The best time to visit Tucheng is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer (June to August) can be hot and humid, and winter (December to February) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tucheng

To help you navigate Taiwanese culture, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Wear conservative clothing: Taiwanese culture values modesty, and it’s best to avoid revealing clothing.

  • Remove shoes in homes and temples: It’s customary to remove shoes before entering homes and temples in Taiwan.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head: Touching someone’s head is considered disrespectful in Taiwanese culture.

  • Chopsticks etiquette: Don’t use chopsticks to point at someone or leave them sticking up in a bowl, as it is considered bad luck.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tucheng

Tucheng is a safe and welcoming destination, but it’s important to take precautions like you would in any large city. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep valuable items out of sight: Pickpocketing can be a problem in crowded areas, so keep your belongings secure.

  • Stay alert at night: While Tucheng is generally safe at night, it’s important to stay alert and avoid dark, unfamiliar areas.

Health in Tucheng

Taiwan has a high-quality healthcare system, and Tucheng has several hospitals and clinics. It’s a good idea to make sure your healthcare insurance covers medical care in Taiwan.

Getting to Tucheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tucheng is conveniently located near several transportation hubs, making it easy to get to from other parts of Taiwan.

Airports Near Tucheng

The nearest airport to Tucheng is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is located about 30 minutes away by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Tucheng

Tucheng is connected to other parts of Taiwan by several bus routes. Buses from Taipei to Tucheng take about 30 minutes.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available and may be a more convenient option for travelers with specific travel needs.

How to Get Around in Tucheng

Tucheng is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Buses and taxis are also available and are a good option for longer trips or getting to and from transit hubs.

Where to Go Next from Tucheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tucheng is located in Northern Taiwan, which is home to many other destinations worth visiting. Here are some of the top nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Taipei: Taiwan’s capital city offers endless possibilities for entertainment, culture, and food. Highlights include Taipei 101, night markets, and the National Palace Museum.

  • Wulai: This nearby district is home to scenic waterfalls, hot springs, and a thriving indigenous culture.

  • Danshui: This charming coastal town offers a glimpse into traditional Taiwanese culture with historic architecture, street food, and beautiful views of the river.

Conclusion

Tucheng is a hidden gem in Taiwan that offers a unique blend of culture, history, natural beauty, and delicious food. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan the perfect trip to Tucheng and explore all this town has to offer. So, pack your bags, get ready to experience Taiwan’s rich culture and be amazed by the beauty of Tucheng!
