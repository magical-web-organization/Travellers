Are you planning a trip to Taiwan and looking for a destination that combines culture, nature, and adventure? Look no further than Tamsui, a district of New Taipei in northern Taiwan. Tamsui, also known as Danshui, is located at the mouth of the Tamsui River and the Taiwan Strait, offering stunning views of the sunset and a perfect place to relax. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the history, attractions, food, culture, safety, and logistics of Tamsui, as well as nearby excursions to make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Tamsui

Tamsui has a rich history that dates back to the 17th century. It was an important trading port during the Qing Dynasty and was occupied by various foreign powers, such as the Spanish, Dutch, and Japanese. Today, Tamsui is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the world for its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Tamsui

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort San Domingo: This former Spanish fort was later used by the British and the Japanese as a consulate. Today, it is a popular museum that features exhibits on the history and culture of Tamsui.
  • Tamsui Old Street: This historical street offers visitors a glimpse of what life was like during Taiwan’s colonial period. It is home to various shops, restaurants, and cafes.
  • Aletheia University Museum: This museum showcases the history of Christianity in Taiwan through exhibits of artifacts, artworks, and historical documents.
  • Tamsui Customs Wharf: This stunning waterfront area offers picturesque views of the Tamsui River and the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Tamsui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fisherman’s Wharf: This scenic area features a lively boardwalk, restaurants, souvenir shops, and stunning views of the sunset over the Taiwan Strait.
  • Guanyin Mountain: This popular hiking spot offers breathtaking views of Tamsui and the surrounding mountains from the top. It is a moderately challenging hike, but the views are worth the effort.
  • Bali Left Bank Park: This riverside park offers a peaceful escape from the city, with its walking paths, picnic areas, and stunning sakura trees in the spring.
  • Linshanbi Coastal Recreation Area: This scenic area features a rugged coastline, stunning rock formations, and an old lighthouse that offers panoramic views of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tamsui

Tamsui is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails. Here are some popular routes:

  • Tamsui River Bike Path: This 18-kilometer trail follows the Tamsui River and offers stunning views of the city and the river. Rent a bike and explore at your own pace.
  • Mount Guanyin Hiking Trail: This 5-kilometer hiking trail leads to the top of Mount Guanyin and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Bali Left Bank Bike Path: This easy 6-kilometer trail follows the Bali Left Bank and offers beautiful views of the river and the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Tamsui: Activities and Tours

  • Tamsui Fisherman’s Wharf Sunset Cruise: Take a romantic sunset cruise along the Tamsui River and enjoy stunning views of the sunset over the Taiwan Strait.
  • Tamsui Old Street Food Tour: Sample some of the best local snacks and dishes in Tamsui on this guided food tour.
  • Danshui Bike Tour: Rent a bike and explore Tamsui on your own, or join a guided tour that takes you through the city’s historical and cultural landmarks.
  • Bali Ferry to Fisherman’s Wharf: Take the Bali ferry from Tamsui to Fisherman’s Wharf for a scenic ride on the river.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tamsui

Day 1: Tamsui Old Street, Fort San Domingo, and Little White House. Day 2: Mount Guanyin Hiking Trail and Linshanbi Coastal Recreation Area. Day 3: Bali Left Bank Park and Bali Ferry to Fisherman’s Wharf. Day 4: Tamsui Fisherman’s Wharf Sunset Cruise and Aletheia University Museum. Day 5: Tamsui River Bike Path and Fisherman’s Wharf. Day 6: Danshui Bike Tour and Tamsui Customs Wharf. Day 7: Relax and enjoy Tamsui’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Tamsui

Must-Try Dishes

  • A-Gei: This local snack consists of tofu stuffed with vermicelli, and served with sauce and vegetables.
  • Iron Eggs: These marinated and boiled eggs have a unique taste and texture that is popular among locals and tourists alike.
  • Oyster Vermicelli: This famous Tamsui dish features vermicelli noodles cooked with oysters, garlic, and other ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Tamsui Iron Goddess Tea: This local tea is made from tea leaves grown in the nearby mountains and has a rich flavor and aroma.
  • Tamsui Pineapple Juice: This refreshing drink is made from locally grown pineapples and is a perfect thirst-quencher on a hot day.
  • Tamsui Beer: This locally brewed beer is a popular choice among beer lovers and pairs well with local snacks and dishes.

Where to Stay in Tamsui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fullon Hotel Danshui Fishermen’s Wharf: This luxurious hotel is located on the waterfront and offers stunning views of the Tamsui River and the mountains.
  • Tamkang University Guesthouse: This affordable guesthouse is operated by the university and offers basic amenities in a peaceful and convenient location.
  • The Fisher Hotel: This modern hotel is located near Tamsui Old Street and offers spacious rooms and a rooftop garden with stunning views.

What to Buy in Tamsui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tamsui Iron Eggs: These marinated eggs make for a unique and tasty souvenir.
  • Tamsui Pineapple Cakes: These sweet and flaky cakes are a popular souvenir among tourists and make for a perfect gift.
  • Local Tea: Bring home some locally grown tea as a delicious and healthy souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tamsui

  • Respect for elders: In Taiwanese culture, respecting elders is an important part of social etiquette.
  • Tipping is not required: Tipping is not a common practice in Taiwan as it is in the West.
  • Taking off shoes: It is common to take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or some other places such as temples.

Best Time to Visit Tamsui

The best time to visit Tamsui is during the autumn and winter, from September to February, when the weather is cool and comfortable, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring attractions. The rainy season from May to August can make some outdoor activities more challenging, but it’s a good time to try Tamsui’s local snacks and dishes indoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tamsui

Do:

  • Explore Tamsui Old Street for unique shopping and eating opportunities
  • Rent a bike or go hiking to experience the beautiful scenery
  • Take in the sunset at Fisherman’s Wharf or Guanyin Mountain

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes indoors when entering a temple or someone’s home
  • Drink tap water, as it is not safe for drinking
  • Be loud, rudely and litter. Respect the environment and other people who live there.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tamsui

Tamsui is a relatively safe place to visit, much like most of Taiwan. However, it’s still important to take precautions like any tourist destination, especially at night, avoid dark and deserted paths, keep an eye on your belongings, don’t leave your drinks unattended and stay alert for anyone suspicious.

Health in Tamsui

Tap water is not safe to drink in Taiwan, so it’s important to drink only bottled water or beverages. Also, it’s large to bring high-protection sunscreen or wear long sleeves to avoid sunburn during the day.

Getting to Tamsui: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Tamsui: The nearest airport is Taipei Songshan Airport, which is about 30 minutes away by car, and Taoyuan International Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.
  • Bus Stations Near Tamsui: The Tamsui Bus Station connects Tamsui to other parts of New Taipei and Taipei, while the Bali Left Bank Bus Station connects to the Chiufen, Jiufen and Wuflu Prison in Keelung.
  • Other Options: The MRT Red Line is another convenient option as it has two Tamsui Stations, Tamsui Station in downtown Tamsui and Hongshulin Station northeast of Tamsui.

How to Get Around in Tamsui

  • Bike: Renting a bike is a convenient way to explore Tamsui and its scenic surroundings. There are bike rental shops near the Tamsui MRT station or at Fisherman’s Wharf.
  • MRT: Taking the MRT is the most convenient way to get around Tamsui and Taipei.
  • Bus: Local buses are another affordable option for getting around Tamsui.

Where to Go Next from Tamsui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chiufen and Jiufen: These old towns offer beautiful scenery with winding alleys and unique architecture. They are easily accessible by bus or taxi from Tamsui.
  • Wulai Hot Spring: This mountain resort town is known for its hot spring baths, scenic waterfalls, and aboriginal culture. You can take a bus from Tamsui to Wulai.
  • Taipei: Taiwan’s capital offers a wide range of cultural, culinary, and entertainment options. The MRT offers easy access in less an hour from Tamsui.

Conclusion

Tamsui offers a delightful mix of history, natural beauty, and cultural landmarks that will satisfy any traveler’s quest for unique experiences. With our comprehensive guide, you can now plan your trip to Tamsui and make unforgettable memories.
