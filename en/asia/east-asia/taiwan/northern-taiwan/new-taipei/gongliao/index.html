Introduction to Gongliao

Gongliao is a charming district located in New Taipei, in Northern Taiwan. Known for its beautiful natural scenery and rich cultural history, Gongliao is a popular destination for both local and international tourists. The district is situated in a scenic location with the nearby mountains and ocean, making it a great spot for outdoor adventures. Additionally, Gongliao is known for its traditional cuisine and unique cultural customs. This comprehensive guide provides everything you need to know to make the most of your visit to this beautiful district in Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Gongliao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gongliao offers visitors an opportunity to learn about the history and culture of Taiwan. The district is home to several notable museums and historical sites worth visiting. These include:

  • Jiufen Old Street: Located just a short drive from Gongliao, Jiufen Old Street is a must-visit for anyone interested in the historical and cultural significance of Taiwan. Famous for its steep alleys, old architecture, and lanterns, this charming street is also home to numerous food vendors, souvenir shops, and tea houses.

  • Houtong Cat Village: Houtong Cat Village is a charmingly eccentric attraction located just a short drive from Gongliao. The village is populated by dozens of friendly, fluffy cats, and it has become a popular destination for cat lovers and curious tourists alike.

  • Gongliao Old Street: A bustling, traditional street that’s been around for more than 200 years, Gongliao Old Street is a great place to go for shopping and eating. Explore the different stores, grab some street food or sit down for a delicious local meal.

Natural Attractions of Gongliao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to cultural attractions, Gongliao also offers visitors the chance to explore the natural beauty of Taiwan. Here are some of the top natural attractions you won’t want to miss:

  • Yin Yang Sea: Known as the “Yin Yang Sea” because of the contrasting colors of blue and yellow, this geological wonder is a popular destination for visitors to Gongliao. The sea features unique rock formations and is a great spot to go for a swim or simply take in the stunning views.

  • Shuangxi River Rafting: For those who love a good thrill, Shuangxi River Rafting is a popular activity in Gongliao. The river runs through a beautiful canyon and visitors can enjoy the rapids and waterfalls while surrounded by stunning scenery.

  • Wai’ao Beach: Located just a short drive from Gongliao, Wai’ao Beach is a beautiful, sandy beach that’s perfect for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing. The beach is surrounded by lush green mountains and blue waters, making it a great spot for photos and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gongliao

Gongliao is also home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in Taiwan. With its coastal location and surrounding mountains, Gongliao has something to offer for hikers and bikers of all levels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Caoling Historic Trail: This hiking trail is located in the Caoling area of Gongliao, which is full of lush natural scenery and historical landmarks, such as the Zhenbian Battery and the Beiguan Tidal Marker. The trail is around 9.2 km long and takes approximately 4 hours to complete, passing through dense forests, streams, and waterfalls.

  • Sandiaojiao Trail: Sandiaojiao Trail is an easy and flat walk, accessible from the Sandiaojiao Lighthouse which stands over 300m above sea level. This trail offers stunning panoramic sea views and fascinating rock formations.

  • Gongliao Coastal Trail: This trail offers a chance to hike along the coast of Gongliao, and explore the rugged shoreline, dip in hidden beaches and take in the stunning views of the ocean, mountains and picturesque fishermen villages.

Best Things to Do in Gongliao: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of fun activities and tours to enjoy in Gongliao. The following are some of the activities we recommend to make the most of your visit to Gongliao:

  • Cooking Class: Taking a cooking class in Gongliao is a great way to learn about the local cuisine and culture. You can learn to cook dishes such as beef noodle soup, fried rice, and spring rolls. Cooking classes are offered at various local guesthouses in Gongliao.

  • Hot Spring: Gongliao is home to several natural hot springs that offer visitors a chance to relax and unwind in the midst of Taiwan’s stunning natural scenery.

  • Surfing: Wai’ao beach, which is located just a short drive from Gongliao, is home to some of Taiwan’s best surfing spots. Surfboards and wetsuits can be rented and lessons are available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gongliao

A week in Gongliao will provide ample time to explore the best of this quaint district. Here is a sample itinerary for a week in Gongliao:

Day Activity
1 Visit Houtong Cat Village and explore the area
2 Enjoy a day of hiking along the Caoling Historic Trail

4 Relax in one of Gongliao’s hot springs
5 Try surfing at Wai’ao beach

7 Hike the Sandiaojiao Trail and spend some time at Yin Yang Sea

Food and Drink Scene of Gongliao

No trip to Gongliao is complete without trying the local cuisine. Gongliao is known for its rich and flavorful dishes that use fresh, seasonal ingredients. Here are some dishes and beverages you won’t want to miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Noodle Soup: This hearty and spicy beef soup is a staple of Taiwan’s culinary scene and Gongliao is no exception. The soup broth is made of beef bones, radish and spices, then served with noodles, tender beef, and a variety of toppings such as chopped scallion, pickled vegetables or hot chilli oil.

  • Stinky Tofu: Some say it smells like a trashed diapers, but stinky tofu is heaven in a bite. Made with bean curd and then fermented, this snack has a strong flavour with a crispy, soft texture.

  • Kao Rou: Also known as grilled pork, it’s a flavourful meaty dishes which has a caramelized crust and juicy interior.

Local Beverages

  • Oolong tea: Taiwan is known for its tea, and Gongliao has its own tea varieties, with the popular Tsaochuan Oolong Tea made in nearby Pinglin. Oolong tea is a mixture of traditional green tea, black tea, and is left to mature to produces an earthy fragrance that goes well with Gongliao’s spicy dishes.

  • Craft Beer: Just on the outskirts of Gongliao, the East Coast Brew Co. is a craft brewery that’s grown popular with locals and tourists alike. The brewery produces a range of refreshing beers that pair well with Gongliao’s hearty dishes, with the brewery offering tours alongside a tap room.

Where to Stay in Gongliao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gongliao offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some of the top options:

  • Love Life Hot Spring B&B: This romantic and trendy bed and breakfast is located next to the countryside providing a relaxing hot spring experience with breathtaking views.

  • Mario’s Mystical Lake Resort: This eco-friendly, family-run resort is located next to a serene lake and provides a luxurious stay close to nature.

  • New Bay Hotel: Situated on the coast of Gongliao, the New Bay Hotel is a popular option for those who want to enjoy the beach and nearby outdoor adventures. The hotel’s restaurant serves fresh seafood dishes.

What to Buy in Gongliao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gongliao is known for its unique products that make great souvenirs to take home. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to look out for:

  • Pleated Tea: Gongliao is famous for its Pleated Tea, which is a local specialty tea that has a unique spiral shape. This tea is made by a local family living in the district, who hand-craft each tea from tea leaves sourced from Maokong.

  • Hand-crafted Bags: Gongliao’s old street is dotted with stores selling hand-crafted bags, made from natural materials such as cloth, bamboo and jute. These bags can be personalized and make great gifts or souvenirs.

  • Beautea: Beautea is a brand of natural skincare products made in Gongliao, using local ingredients such as Taiwan Hinoki and green tea. These high-quality and environmentally friendly skincare products are available to purchase from different stores and local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Gongliao

Gongliao has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its traditional festivals and customs. To fully embrace the culture of Gongliao, visitors are encouraged to experience the following:

  • Pingxi Sky Lantern Festival: Gongliao is located close to Pingxi, where the annual Sky Lantern Festival takes place. This special event involves the release of hundreds of colorful paper lanterns into the sky, and it’s a must-see experience.

  • Taiwanese Opera: Gongliao is one of the few areas in Taiwan where Taiwanese opera is still performed regularly. Visitors can enjoy a performance and learn more about this unique artform.

Best Time to Visit Gongliao

The best time to visit Gongliao is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gongliao

Do:

  • Do carry your passport with you at all times, as random ID checks may occur.
  • Do learn a few local phrases, such as “Ni hao” (hello) and “xie xie” (thank you).
  • Do dress modestly when visiting temples or shrines.
  • Do try the local cuisine and unique cultural experiences.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the environment.
  • Don’t forget to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Don’t take pictures without permission, especially of people and places of worship.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gongliao

Gongliao is generally a safe and welcoming district. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables out in the open, and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Gongliao

Taiwan has an excellent healthcare system, and there are medical facilities available in Gongliao. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Gongliao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gongliao

Taipei Taoyuan International Airport(TPE) is the closest and most convenient airport to Gongliao. Visitors can take the airport shuttle, train or taxi to get to Gongliao district.

Bus Stations Near Gongliao

Gongliao Bus Station is situated near Gongliao train station and provides a convenient, affordable and comfortable way to explore and travel around the Gongliao district.

Other Options

Another convenient way to get to Gongliao is by taxi or drive yourself there. Gongliao is a 45-minute drive from Taipei City and 20 minutes from Keelung City.

How to Get Around in Gongliao

Gongliao is a small district that can be explored easily by foot, bicycle, or scooter. There are several rental options available for those who wish to explore at their leisure, such as the Waima Cycling Route near the beach, for example or you can rent a scooter from one of the many rental stores around and explore at your own pace. Taxis are also available, for more convenient transportation, although they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Gongliao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gongliao’s location means it is easy to explore nearby destinations by bus, train or taxi, and it is close to several other top tourist spots in Taiwan. Here are some of the best excursions and destinations within reach of Gongliao:

  • Keelung City: A 20 minute drive from Gongliao, Keelung city is a bustling coastal town with a wide range of seafood and night markets.

  • Taipei: As the capital city, Taipei is one of Taiwan’s most popular destinations to visit. It’s a 45-minute drive from Gongliao, and offers plenty of exciting things to see and do.

  • Shifen Waterfall: Just a 40-minute drive away is the majestic Shifen Waterfall, surrounded by lush forests, and located in the picturesque mountain village of Pingxi.

Conclusion

Gongliao is a charming district that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural traditions. With so much to see and do, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit to this beautiful corner of Taiwan. Whether you’re interested in exploring local cultures, eating delicious food, or enjoying outdoor adventures, Gongliao has something for everyone.
