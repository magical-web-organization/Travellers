Introduction to Pinglin

Located in New Taipei, northern Taiwan, Pinglin is a small town surrounded by mountains and rivers. Known for its oolong tea production and tea culture, Pinglin is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The town also boasts historical sites, natural attractions, and outdoor activities. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Pinglin and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Pinglin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pinglin is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s rich history, culture, and traditions.

  • Pinglin Tea Museum: Learn about tea production and tea culture in Pinglin at this museum. Visitors can take part in tea-making and tea-tasting activities.

  • Dahua Tea Plantation: Visit the largest tea plantation in Pinglin and learn about the tea-making process. The plantation offers guided tours and tea-tasting sessions.

  • Pinglin Old Street: Explore the traditional architecture and local shops at this historical street.

  • Li Mei-shu Memorial Gallery: Discover the life and works of the renowned Taiwanese painter and calligrapher Li Mei-shu at this museum.

  • Pinglin Shihmen Reservoir Scenic Area: Enjoy the scenic views of the reservoir and surrounding mountains. The area also has hiking trails and picnic spots.

Natural Attractions of Pinglin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pinglin’s natural attractions offer visitors a chance to enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views.

  • Pinglin Bicycle Trail: Explore the town and its surroundings by bike. The trail spans 14 kilometers and offers beautiful views of the mountains and rivers.

  • Pinglin River Waterfall Trail: A 3-kilometer hike that leads to a scenic waterfall. The trail is suitable for both beginner and experienced hikers.

  • Hou-tung River Nature Trail: Another scenic hike that offers views of the river and nearby mountains. The trail is 6 kilometers long and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

  • Maokong Gondola: Take a cable car ride to Maokong, a mountainous area known for its tea plantations and scenic views. The ride offers panoramic views of Taipei and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pinglin

Pinglin offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the town and its surroundings. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Pinglin Bicycle Trail
  • Pinglin River Waterfall Trail
  • Hou-tung River Nature Trail
  • Maokong Gondola Trail
  • Xiaoyoukeng Trail
  • Jiantan Mountain Trail

These trails vary in difficulty level, duration, and scenery. Visitors are advised to check trail conditions and weather before embarking on a hiking or biking trip.

Best Things to Do in Pinglin: Activities and Tours

Aside from exploring the town’s natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Pinglin. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Tea-Making Workshops: Learn how to make traditional Taiwanese tea at Pinglin Tea Museum or Dahua Tea Plantation.

  • Oolong Tea Tasting: Try Pinglin’s famous oolong tea at local tea shops and tea houses.

  • River Rafting and Kayaking: Experience the thrill of rafting or kayaking down Hou-tung River.

  • Mountain Climbing: Explore the nearby mountains and hiking trails.

  • Night Market Hopping: Sample local street food and snacks at Pinglin’s night markets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pinglin

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Pinglin:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at hotel. Explore Pinglin Old Street and Dahua Tea Plantation.

Day 2: Visit Pinglin Tea Museum and Li Mei-shu Memorial Gallery. Take a bike ride along Pinglin Bicycle Trail.

Day 3: Hike Hou-tung River Nature Trail and enjoy a picnic by the river. Visit Pinglin Shihmen Reservoir Scenic Area.

Day 4: Take a cable car ride to Maokong and explore the tea plantations. Try local tea and snacks at tea houses.

Day 5: Go river rafting or kayaking down Hou-tung River. Sample street food at Pinglin’s night market.

Day 6: Explore Jiantan Mountain Trail and enjoy panoramic views of Taipei and its surroundings.

Day 7: Departure from Pinglin.

Food and Drink Scene of Pinglin

Aside from its tea culture, Pinglin also offers a variety of local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oolong Tea Chicken: A local dish made with oolong tea leaves and chicken. The chicken is marinated in tea and spices for several hours, giving it a unique flavor.

  • Baozi: Steamed buns filled with meat, vegetables, or sweet beans.

  • Tofu Pudding: A sweet dessert made with soybean milk and served with fruits or syrup.

  • Taro Balls: Chewy balls made with taro flour and served with sweet soup or milk.

Local Beverages

  • Oolong Tea: Pinglin’s famous tea is a must-try for tea lovers. It has a unique flavor and aroma, thanks to the town’s climate and soil.

  • Bubble Tea: A popular Taiwanese drink made with milk tea and tapioca pearls.

  • Soybean Milk: A refreshing drink made with soybean and served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Pinglin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pinglin has several accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

  • Hotel Modern Puli: A luxurious hotel located in nearby Puli City. It offers elegant rooms, outdoor pool, and spa services.

  • Pinglin Homestay: A cozy guesthouse located in the town center. It offers comfortable rooms and home-cooked meals.

  • Puli is Coming Hostel: A budget hostel located in Puli City. It offers dormitory-style rooms and common areas.

  • Mu Wu Lodge: A rustic lodge located in the mountains. It offers wooden cabins with private balconies and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Pinglin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pinglin’s tea culture has inspired a variety of souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home.

  • Oolong Tea: Buy some of Pinglin’s famous oolong tea to enjoy at home or as a gift for tea enthusiasts.

  • Tea Sets: Look for traditional tea sets made of porcelain or clay. They come in various designs and sizes.

  • Tea Snacks: Try some of Pinglin’s tea-infused snacks, such as tea cookies, tea eggs, or tea candies.

  • Handicrafts: Look for handmade crafts, such as pottery, woodcarvings, or calligraphy paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Pinglin

Pinglin’s tea culture and traditions are deeply ingrained in its local customs and festivals. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Tea Etiquette: When drinking tea with locals, it’s important to respect their tea culture and follow proper tea etiquette. This includes waiting for the tea to be poured for you, holding the cup with both hands, and using respectful language.

  • Tea Festivals: Pinglin hosts several tea festivals throughout the year, such as the Tea-Picking Festival and the Bamboo Shoots and Tea Festival.

  • Religious Practices: Pinglin has several temples and shrines where locals practice their religious beliefs. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Pinglin

The best time to visit Pinglin is during spring or autumn, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town gets crowded during peak tea-picking season, which is in April and May. Visitors can also enjoy the scenic views and outdoor activities during the cooler months of November to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pinglin

Do:

  • Respect local customs and practices.
  • Try Pinglin’s oolong tea and tea culture.
  • Explore the town’s natural and cultural attractions.
  • Follow proper tea etiquette when drinking tea with locals.
  • Enjoy local street food and snacks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and practices.
  • Litter or damage natural or cultural attractions.
  • Insult or disrespect locals.
  • Speak loudly or use foul language in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pinglin

Pinglin is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to follow common safety precautions, such as locking their doors and windows at night, and avoiding dark and secluded areas at night.

Health in Pinglin

Pinglin has good healthcare facilities and services. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance and consult their doctors before traveling to Taiwan. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect oneself from the sun and insects during outdoor activities.

Getting to Pinglin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pinglin

The closest airport to Pinglin is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is located around 60 kilometers away. Visitors can take a bus or taxi from the airport to Pinglin.

Bus Stations Near Pinglin

Pinglin is accessible by bus from several cities in Taiwan, such as Taipei, Taichung, and Kaohsiung. The bus station is located in the town center, near Pinglin Old Street.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Pinglin. However, it’s important to note that driving in Taiwan can be challenging, especially for foreigners who are not familiar with local traffic rules and signs.

How to Get Around in Pinglin

Pinglin is a small town that can be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take local buses to nearby attractions or hire a taxi for a more convenient and comfortable ride.

Where to Go Next from Pinglin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pinglin is a great starting point for exploring the nearby cities and towns of Taiwan. Here are some suggested destinations and excursions:

  • Taipei: Taiwan’s capital city is only around 40 kilometers away from Pinglin. Visitors can explore the city’s cultural and historical attractions, shopping districts, and night markets.

  • Puli: Located on the outskirts of Nantou County, Puli is known for its scenic views and cultural attractions, such as the Chung Tai Chan Monastery and the National Taiwan Craft Research and Development Institute.

  • Sun Moon Lake: A picturesque lake located in the heart of Taiwan’s central mountain range. Visitors can enjoy water sports, hiking, and biking, and explore the lake’s temples and landmarks.

Conclusion

Pinglin offers visitors a chance to experience Taiwan’s rich tea culture, natural beauty, and traditional customs. From hiking trails and outdoor activities to museums and historical sites, Pinglin has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip to Pinglin and make the most of your visit.
