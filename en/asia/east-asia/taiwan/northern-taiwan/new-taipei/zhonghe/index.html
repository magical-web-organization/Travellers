Zhonghe is a bustling district in New Taipei, located in Northern Taiwan. This vibrant area is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and mouth-watering food scene. Whether you’re an adventurer, history buff, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Zhonghe. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the must-see attractions, the best places to stay, and the top things to do in Zhonghe.

Introduction to Zhonghe

Zhonghe is a bustling district in New Taipei, bordering Taipei City to the east. It’s a blend of old and new, with ancient temples, traditional markets, and modern skyscrapers. Some of the top attractions in Zhonghe include the Lin Family Mansion and Garden, the Zhonghe Cultural Center, and the Banqiao District Coast. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking, as well as immerse themselves in the local culture and cuisine. With its rich history and vibrant present, Zhonghe is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Zhonghe

Zhonghe is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and delicious food. Here are some of the attractions you can’t afford to miss while visiting Zhonghe.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lin Family Mansion and Garden: This beautifully preserved mansion and garden is a testament to the wealth and power of the Lin Family during the Qing dynasty.
  • Zhonghe Cultural Center: A modern cultural center that showcases traditional arts and crafts, as well as contemporary music and dance performances.
  • Banqiao District Coast: An important transportation hub and home to the largest train station in Taiwan, the Banqiao District is also home to numerous restaurants, night markets, and shopping centers.

Natural Attractions of Zhonghe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yingge Rock: A scenic rock formation located in the middle of the Dahan River, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and waterfalls.
  • Dajia Riverside Park: A beautiful park with a jogging track, playground, and bike path along the banks of the Dahan River.
  • Maokong: A scenic area known for its tea plantations, with stunning views of Taipei City and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Zhonghe

Zhonghe is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails that offer spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Some of the best routes include:

  • Yingge Rock Trail: A 3-hour hike that takes you to the top of Yingge Rock, with breathtaking views of the river and mountains.
  • Xiaoguanyinshan Trail: A 6-hour hike that takes you to the top of Xiaoguanyinshan Peak, with panoramic views of Taipei City and the Pacific Ocean.
  • Dajia Riverside Park Bike Path: A scenic bike path that follows the banks of the Dahan River, with stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Zhonghe: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Zhonghe.

  • Taipei-North Coast Plantation Tour: A half-day tour that takes you to visit tea plantations and pineapple farms in the Zhonghe area.
  • Traditional Paper Making Experience: Learn how to make traditional paper from rice straw and bamboo, a time-honored craft in Taiwan.
  • Night Market Tour: Explore the vibrant night markets of Zhonghe, where you can sample local delicacies, shop for souvenirs, and mingle with the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhonghe

Zhonghe is known for its mouth-watering food scene, with traditional Taiwanese dishes and local specialties like beef noodles and dumplings. There are also plenty of local beverages to quench your thirst, such as milk tea and locally-produced beer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Noodle Soup: A hearty dish of slow-cooked beef, noodles, and broth, served hot and steaming.
  • Oyster Omelette: A savory omelette made with fresh oysters, vegetables, and eggs.
  • Dumplings: A popular snack food in Taiwan, filled with ground meat, vegetables, or seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Milk Tea: A sweet and creamy tea-based drink with tapioca pearls, a Taiwanese specialty.
  • Taiwan Beer: A classic lager beer produced in Taiwan, perfect for washing down those spicy dumplings.

Where to Stay in Zhonghe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhonghe offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Grand Forward Hotel: A modern hotel with spacious rooms and stunning views of Taipei City.
  • Hotel Regulus: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Chen Yan Hostel: A cozy guesthouse in the heart of Zhonghe, with comfortable beds and a warm atmosphere.

What to Buy in Zhonghe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Zhonghe, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as a memento of your trip.

  • Pineapple Cakes: A sweet and crumbly cake filled with pineapple paste, a popular Taiwanese treat.
  • Tea: Taiwan is famous for its high-quality tea, with oolong and green tea being some of the most popular varieties.
  • Handmade Paper Products: Bring home a unique and beautiful souvenir made from traditional rice straw and bamboo paper.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhonghe

Taiwanese culture is rich and varied, influenced by Chinese, Japanese, and indigenous traditions. Visitors to Zhonghe can experience the locals’ hospitality, festivals, and customs, such as:

  • Ghost Month Festival: A month-long festival in August where the locals offer food and incense to their deceased ancestors.
  • Temples: Zhonghe is home to numerous temples, such as the Guanxi Temple, dedicated to the goddess of mercy, and the Zhonghe Matsu Temple, honoring the goddess of the sea.
  • Night Markets: Experience the vibrant atmosphere and local cuisine at the night markets, a popular Taiwanese tradition.

Best Time to Visit Zhonghe

The best time to visit Zhonghe is during the fall and winter months, from November to February, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are thinner. However, if you’re looking to experience the festivals and nightlife, the summer months are the best, from June to August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhonghe

Here are some tips to keep in mind while visiting Zhonghe:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions: Be mindful of local customs, such as removing your shoes before entering temples.
  • Try the street food: The night markets and street food stalls are an essential part of Taiwanese culture, so take the opportunity to sample the local specialties.
  • Explore the outdoors: Zhonghe is renowned for its natural beauty, so be sure to take advantage of the numerous hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Taiwan is serious about keeping its environment clean, so make sure to dispose of your trash properly.
  • Smoke in prohibited areas: Smoking is not allowed in public areas in Taiwan, so be mindful of the designated smoking areas.
  • Disrespect the locals: Taiwanese people are friendly and hospitable, so be polite and respectful when interacting with them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhonghe

Taiwan is a relatively safe country, with low rates of violent crime and theft. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions while traveling, such as:

  • Stay alert: Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Take care of your belongings: Keep your valuables close to you and be vigilant of pickpockets.
  • Use common sense: Avoid walking alone at night, and be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Zhonghe

Taiwan has excellent healthcare services, with modern facilities and highly trained medical staff. Visitors are advised to take basic precautions, such as:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen: Protect your skin from harmful UV rays.
  • Be aware of food hygiene: Make sure to choose reputable restaurants and street vendors, wash your hands frequently, and avoid tap water.

Getting to Zhonghe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zhonghe is well-connected to Taipei City and the rest of Taiwan. Here are some transportation options to get to Zhonghe:

Airports Near Zhonghe

  • Taipei Songshan Airport: Located in the heart of Taipei City, just a 20-minute drive from Zhonghe.
  • Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport: The largest airport in Taiwan, located about a 45-minute drive from Zhonghe.

Bus Stations Near Zhonghe

  • Zhonghe Bus Station: A major bus station in the area, with connections to Taipei and other destinations throughout Taiwan.
  • Banqiao Bus Station: One of the largest bus stations in Taiwan, located in the Banqiao District, just a short drive from Zhonghe.

Other Options

  • Metro: The Taipei MRT system offers easy access to Zhonghe, with numerous stops throughout the district.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available throughout Taipei City, and the ride to Zhonghe takes about 20 minutes.

How to Get Around in Zhonghe

Zhonghe is a compact district, and most of the attractions are within walking or biking distance. There are also plenty of public transportation options, such as buses and the Taipei MRT system, which make it easy to get around. Taxis are readily available, and bike rentals are also a popular option.

Where to Go Next from Zhonghe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Taiwan, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Zhonghe:

  • Taipei: Just a short ride away, Taipei City offers endless attractions and activities, from world-class museums to night markets and street food.
  • Yangmingshan National Park: A stunning national park just north of Taipei City, with hot springs, hiking trails, and gorgeous flower fields.
  • Jiufen: A picturesque mountain town, famous for its narrow streets, red lanterns, and tea houses.

Conclusion

Zhonghe is an eclectic district in Northern Taiwan that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for travelers. From its natural beauty and outdoor adventures to its cultural landmarks and mouth-watering food scene, Zhonghe is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Taiwan. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Zhonghe.
