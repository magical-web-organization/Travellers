Introduction to Sanxia

Sanxia is a quaint, charming town located in the northern part of Taiwan. The name Sanxia means “three gorges,” which refers to the three valleys that surround the area. Sanxia is known for its rich history, traditional architecture, and picturesque landscape. It’s a perfect destination for travelers interested in Taiwan’s culture, history, and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Sanxia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sanxia is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s rich culture and history.

1. Sanxia Old Street

Sanxia Old Street is a must-visit destination for travelers interested in Taiwan’s traditional architecture and culture. The street is lined with beautifully preserved Japanese buildings that date back to the early 1900s. Here, you can find an array of local handicrafts, snacks, and souvenirs.

2. Zushi Temple

Zushi Temple is one of the most famous temples in Taiwan. The temple was built over 200 years ago and is dedicated to the god of war, Guangong. The temple is known for its intricate carvings, colorful decorations, and unique architecture.

3. Yingge Ceramics Museum

Yingge is a small town located near Sanxia that’s known for its pottery and ceramics. The Yingge Ceramics Museum houses a vast collection of pottery and ceramics from all over Taiwan. The museum also offers workshops where visitors can try their hand at pottery-making.

Natural Attractions of Sanxia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sanxia is surrounded by lush forests, rugged mountains, and picturesque valleys. It’s a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

1. Qingshui Terrace

Qingshui Terrace is a natural wonder located in the nearby Lala Mountain Scenic Area. The terrace is made up of a series of natural rock formations that resemble a staircase leading up to the sky. The terrace offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

2. Daxi Waterfall

Daxi Waterfall is one of the most beautiful and accessible waterfalls in Taiwan. It’s a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers. The waterfall is surrounded by lush forests and offers a refreshing respite from the hot summer months.

3. Sanxia River

The Sanxia River is a scenic river that runs through the heart of Sanxia. The river is a popular destination for kayaking, rafting, and fishing. It’s also a great place to hike along the riverbank trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sanxia

Sanxia is surrounded by several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Here are a few of the best routes to try:

1. Sanxia-Pinglin Bikeway

The Sanxia-Pinglin Bikeway is a scenic route that runs along the Sanxia River. The trail is flat and easy, making it perfect for families and beginner cyclists. The route passes through several small villages, rice paddies, and scenic waterways.

2. Dabajian Mountain Trail

Dabajian Mountain is the highest peak in the Sanxia area. The trail to the summit is challenging but offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail is best tackled by experienced hikers and takes approximately 6-8 hours to complete.

3. Bogu Trail

The Bogu Trail is a scenic hiking trail that runs through the lush forests of Sanxia. The trail is moderately difficult and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Along the way, hikers can stop at several temples and shrines.

Best Things to Do in Sanxia: Activities and Tours

1. Take a Traditional Taiwanese Tea Ceremony

Sanxia is known for its tea culture. Visitors can participate in a traditional tea ceremony to learn more about Taiwan’s tea culture and history.

2. Make Your Own Pottery

Take a pottery-making workshop at one of the local ceramics centers. Here, visitors can learn about Taiwan’s pottery industry and make their own unique creations.

3. Visit the Sanxia Night Market

The Sanxia Night Market offers an array of local food and drinks, as well as traditional souvenirs and handicrafts. It’s a great opportunity to experience local culture and cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sanxia

Day 1: Visit Sanxia Old Street and Zushi Temple

Day 2: Take a pottery-making workshop at one of the local ceramics centers; hike the Bogu Trail

Day 3: Visit the Yingge Ceramics Museum; explore the Sanxia River on a kayak

Day 4: Take a traditional Taiwanese tea ceremony; hike the Dabajian Mountain Trail

Day 5: Visit Qingshui Terrace; visit the Sanxia Night Market

Day 6: Explore the nearby Fuxing district; visit local farms and fruit orchards

Day 7: Relax at a hot spring resort; visit a local spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Sanxia

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fried Taro Balls

Fried Taro Balls are a traditional Taiwanese snack that’s popular in Sanxia. The balls are made with mashed taro, filled with minced pork and vegetables, and deep-fried until golden and crispy.

2. Sanxia Sembei

Sanxia Sembei is a type of rice cracker that’s made with glutinous rice flour and sesame seeds. The crackers are baked until crispy and fragrant and often served with tea.

3. Sanxia Sticky Rice Dumplings

Sanxia Sticky Rice Dumplings are a local specialty that’s typically served during the Dragon Boat Festival. The dumplings are stuffed with a variety of fillings, such as pork, mushrooms, and bamboo shoots, wrapped in bamboo leaves, and boiled until tender.

Local Beverages

1. Sanxia Tea

Sanxia is known for its tea culture. Visitors can sample a variety of local teas, including oolong, black, and green tea.

2. Lychee Wine

Lychee Wine is a popular Taiwanese alcoholic beverage that’s made with fresh lychee fruit. The wine is sweet, fragrant, and refreshing.

3. Pineapple Juice

Taiwan is famous for its sweet and juicy pineapples. Visitors can sample fresh pineapple juice at one of the local juice bars.

Where to Stay in Sanxia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sanxia Zizai Hotel

The Sanxia Zizai Hotel is a popular hotel located in the heart of Sanxia. The hotel offers spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Sanxia Memory Guesthouse

The Sanxia Memory Guesthouse is a cozy guesthouse located near Sanxia Old Street. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, traditional decor, and a relaxing atmosphere.

Sanxia Xinglong Hostel

The Sanxia Xinglong Hostel is a budget-friendly hostel located in the Fuxing district of Sanxia. The hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and easy access to nearby hiking trails.

What to Buy in Sanxia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Ceramics and Pottery

Sanxia is known for its pottery and ceramics industry. Visitors can buy traditional Taiwanese teapots, bowls, and vases at one of the local ceramics centers.

2. Handicrafts and Souvenirs

Sanxia Old Street is a great place to find local handicrafts and souvenirs. Visitors can buy traditional Taiwanese paper products, woodcarvings, and calligraphy works.

3. Tea

Sanxia is known for its tea culture. Visitors can bring home local Taiwanese teas, such as oolong, green, and black tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Sanxia

Sanxia is known for its rich culture and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering temples and dressing modestly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Sanxia

The best time to visit Sanxia is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable. The cherry blossoms bloom in March and April, making it a popular time to visit. The fall months of September and October offer pleasant weather and stunning autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sanxia

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local Taiwanese food and drinks
  • Participate in local cultural activities and tours

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Eat or drink in temples

Safety and Health

Safety in Sanxia

Sanxia is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be cautious when hiking and biking on the mountain trails.

Health in Sanxia

Sanxia is a generally healthy destination. However, visitors should be aware of the heat and humidity during the summer months and take precautions to avoid heat stroke and dehydration.

Getting to Sanxia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sanxia

The closest airport to Sanxia is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers away from Sanxia. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or a taxi into the town.

Bus Stations Near Sanxia

Sanxia Bus Station is located in the center of the town and offers bus connections to Taipei and other parts of Taiwan.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or hire a car to explore the surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Sanxia

Visitors can explore Sanxia on foot or by renting a bicycle. Taxis are also available in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Sanxia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Taipei City

Taipei City is the capital of Taiwan and is located approximately 30 kilometers away from Sanxia. Taipei is known for its bustling night markets, world-class museums, and stunning skyline views.

2. Jiufen

Jiufen is a charming mountain town located on the northeast coast of Taiwan. The town is famous for its narrow alleyways, traditional teahouses, and stunning ocean views.

3. Shifen

Shifen is a small town located in the Pingxi Valley, approximately 20 kilometers away from Sanxia. The town is famous for its sky lantern festival and stunning waterfall.

Conclusion

Sanxia is a beautiful and charming destination in northern Taiwan that offers visitors a glimpse into Taiwan’s rich culture, history, and landscape. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, traditional tea ceremonies, or exploring the town’s historic buildings, Sanxia has something for every traveler.
