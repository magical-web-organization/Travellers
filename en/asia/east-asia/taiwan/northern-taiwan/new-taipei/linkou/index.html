Introduction to Linkou

Linkou is a vibrant district located in New Taipei, in northern Taiwan. It is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike, thanks to its beautiful natural scenery, diverse outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage. Linkou is conveniently located just a short drive from Taipei city, making it an excellent option for a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Linkou

To make the most of your trip to Linkou, check out our list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Linkou Guandi Temple: Constructed during the Qing Dynasty, this beautiful temple is dedicated to Guan Yu, the god of war and literature. It is a popular spot for local religious festivals and ceremonies.
  • Linkou Historical House: This well-preserved Japanese-style house dates back to the Japanese colonial period and showcases traditional Taiwanese architecture and design.
  • Street Art Alley: Located in the heart of Linkou, this alley is a hub of creative street art and urban culture.

Natural Attractions of Linkou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xinbeitou Hot Springs: Take a dip in the natural hot springs, which are said to have therapeutic properties and healing effects. The lush green surroundings make it a peaceful and relaxing experience.
  • Wulai Waterfall: This magnificent waterfall is one of the largest in Taiwan, standing at over 80 metres tall. Enjoy a scenic hike to the waterfall or take a cable car to the top for panoramic views.
  • Yangmingshan National Park: This beautiful national park is located just north of Taipei, and is home to stunning hiking trails, hot springs, and scenic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Linkou

Linkou is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hiking and biking routes to suit all levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Maokong Mountain Trail: This challenging trail is known for its steep inclines and stunning views over Taipei City.
  • Xizhi River Bike Trail: This 22-km bike trail takes riders through scenic countryside and along the beautiful Xizhi River.
  • Sunset Trail: This easy hike is perfect for families and offers beautiful views of the sunset over Taipei City.

Best Things to Do in Linkou: Activities and Tours

  • TaipeiEYE: This cultural center offers traditional Chinese dance and opera performances, showcasing the rich heritage of Taiwanese culture.
  • Linkou Night Market: Discover a range of delicious local street food and unique souvenirs at this bustling night market.
  • Fishing at Yuanshan Reservoir: Head to the reservoir for a relaxing day of fishing and take in the beautiful natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Linkou

Food and Drink Scene of Linkou

When it comes to food and drink, Linkou has a wide variety of delicious options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taiwanese Oyster Omelette: A popular Taiwanese dish made with eggs, oysters, and vegetables.
  • Braised Pork Rice: A traditional dish made with tender, juicy pork, served over a bed of rice.
  • Stinky Tofu: A pungent, fermented tofu that is a true Taiwanese delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Tea: This sweet and refreshing tea-based drink is a staple of Taiwanese cuisine.
  • Taiwan Beer: A light and refreshing beer, brewed locally in Taiwan.

Where to Stay in Linkou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Linkou offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you prefer a high-end hotel or a cozy guesthouse, you are sure to find something to suit your needs.

Some popular options include:

  • Le Meridien Taipei: This luxury hotel offers beautiful views over Taipei City and is located just a short drive from Linkou.
  • CampDavid Resort: A peaceful and relaxing guesthouse located in the lush countryside of Linkou.
  • Linkou La Chambre: A cozy and affordable guesthouse located in the heart of Linkou.

What to Buy in Linkou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Taiwan is famous for its high-quality teas, so be sure to pick up some local tea varieties to bring home.
  • Ceramics: Taiwan is known for its beautiful pottery and ceramics, and many shops in Linkou sell handmade ceramics.
  • Handicrafts: Check out the local artisanal shops in Linkou for unique and beautiful handicrafts to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Linkou

Linkou is a diverse and multicultural city, with influences from Chinese, Japanese, and indigenous Taiwanese cultures. Respect for elders, family values, and hospitality are important aspects of Taiwanese culture, and visitors should take care to observe and respect local customs and customs.

Best Time to Visit Linkou

Linkou can be visited year-round, as the climate is mild and pleasant throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is during the late autumn and winter months (October to March), when the weather is cool and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Linkou

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a home or a temple.
  • Respect local etiquette, such as bowing and saying thank you.

Don’t:

  • Point your feet towards temples or statues of religious figures.
  • Touch others’ heads or hair, as it is considered rude in Taiwanese culture.
  • Talk about sensitive political topics, such as Taiwan-China relations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Linkou

Linkou is generally a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Linkou

Taiwan has a high standard of healthcare, with well-equipped hospitals and emergency services. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance and take the necessary precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Linkou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Linkou is conveniently located just a short 30-minute drive from Taipei City, making it easily accessible from various parts of Taiwan. Here are some transportation options to help you get to Linkou:

Airports Near Linkou

  • Taipei Songshan Airport: Located just 11 km from Linkou, this domestic airport offers flights to various cities within Taiwan.
  • Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport: A larger international airport located 35 km from Linkou, with flights to various destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Linkou

  • Linkou Bus Station: Located in the heart of Linkou, this bus station offers connections to various destinations throughout Taipei City and beyond.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Taipei City and Linkou, and are a convenient option for those not familiar with the area.
  • Private Car: Visitors can also hire private cars or drivers to take them to Linkou from Taipei City.

How to Get Around in Linkou

Linkou is a small district that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. However, for those who prefer public transportation, the Linkou Metro Station and bus system provide convenient and affordable options for getting around the district and connecting to nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Linkou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taipei City: Just a short 30-minute drive from Linkou, Taipei is a bustling metropolis with plenty of cultural attractions and world-class dining options.
  • Jiufen: This charming historic town is located just 45 minutes from Linkou, and is famous for its winding streets and breathtaking coastal views.
  • Tamsui: Located just 30 minutes from Linkou, Tamsui is a scenic coastal town with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful waterfront views.

Conclusion

Linkou is a beautiful and vibrant district that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or delicious local cuisine, Linkou has it all. Start planning your trip today and discover the best of northern Taiwan!
