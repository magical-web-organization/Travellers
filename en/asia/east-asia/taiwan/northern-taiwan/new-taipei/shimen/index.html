Introduction to Shimen

Located in the northern part of Taiwan and part of the New Taipei City, Shimen is a small district that boasts not only a great variety of natural scenery but also rich cultural heritage. A popular destination among locals and tourists alike, Shimen is home to a number of notable attractions such as cultural and historical museums, breathtaking natural attractions, hiking and biking trails, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Shimen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shimen Wenhua Park - This park is home to the Shimen Literature Memorial Hall, which was built in honor of the late Taiwanese novelist, Qiu Shi Guang. Visitors can see various exhibitions of his works and life, along with other cultural displays in the memorial hall.
  • Shimen Folk Cultural Museum - A must-visit for history enthusiasts, this museum showcases the everyday life and history of Shimen’s residents.
  • Jingtong Railway Station - Known as one of Taiwan’s most well-preserved train stations and was built during the Japanese colonization in 1933. Visitors can admire its classic architecture and beautiful surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Shimen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shimen Reservoir - A great spot for sightseeing and water sports, the Shimen Reservoir is located in the beautiful mountain region of Shimen. Visitors can go on boat rides, fish, have a picnic, or explore the surrounding trails.
  • Shimen Mountain - Offering spectacular views of the coastline and the city below, Shimen Mountain is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts. Visitors can take a brisk walk through the challenging hill trail leading to the top.
  • Erziping Trail - Another famed mountain trail in Shimen, Erziping is well-known for its scenic views and the presence of the local butterfly species. Visitors can enjoy a nice picnic while admiring the breathtaking view along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shimen

  • Erziping Trail - Suitable for hikers and bikers, this trail is around 2.8 km long and offers a moderately challenging terrain.
  • Shimen Reservoir Trail - A 5-km round trip hike with friendly terrain for all ages, this hiking route offers stunning views of the reservoir and surrounding landscapes.
  • Shimenting Trail - A great way to explore Shimen’s traditional architecture, this trail offers a glimpse of old Taiwan with its old bridges, farms, and temples.

Best Things to Do in Shimen: Activities and Tours

  • Shimen Sunset Tour - Witness the beauty of Shimen’s sunset from the Shimen Reservoir Visitor Center. The tour includes a relaxing boat ride and a sightseeing tour of the area by car.
  • Bike Tour in Shimenting - Visitors can go on a guided bike tour around Shimenting where they can see traditional Taiwanese architecture and enjoy local foods, plants, and scenic views.
  • Farm Experience Tour - Perfect for families and kids, this tour allows visitors to explore local farms, learn about the animals, and pick fresh fruits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shimen

Day Itinerary
1 Visit Shimen Folk Cultural Museum, the Umeyama Trail, and have dinner at a local restaurant.
2 Go on a bike tour in Shimenting, then visit the Jingtong Railway Station and shop at local stores.
3 Hike through the Erziping Trail, and have a picnic near the butterfly habitat.
4 Explore the Beitou Historical and Cultural sites, dine in a local restaurant, and sample local wine.
5 Visit the Strawberry Farm and Shimen Reservoir, participate in Shimen Sunset Tour.
6 Enjoy the great outdoors with a bike ride around the reservoir and have dinner at a local restaurant.
7 Visit the Shimen Literature Memorial Hall, relax at hot springs resort and dine on local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Shimen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braised pork rice - A popular Taiwanese dish with juicy pork, special sauce, and rice.
  • Dan Bing - A traditional Taiwanese breakfast dish, it has a crispy texture and is usually filled with egg, onion, and other toppings.
  • Oyster Vermicelli Soup - A hearty soup with a clear broth, vermicelli noodles, and fresh oysters.

Local Beverages

  • Taiwanese Tea - Known for its quality and taste, Taiwanese tea is a must-try for any tea lover.
  • Taiwan Beer - A popular beer brewed in Taiwan.
  • Fruit Tea - A refreshing drink made up of tea and freshly sliced fruits.

Where to Stay in Shimen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shimen Waterfront Hotel - A modern and spacious hotel with a great view of Shimen Reservoir, it also has a heated rooftop pool and hot springs for guests.
  • Shimen Homestay - A comfortable homestay equipped with basic necessities and a genuine Taiwanese experience.
  • Classic Hotel - Located near the train station, it’s a convenient choice and offers clean and modern rooms.

What to Buy in Shimen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Folding Fans - Shimen is known for its high-quality folding fans made of bamboo, paper, and silk.
  • Handmade Soap - A popular and natural souvenir from Shimen, these soaps are made from locally sourced tea and herbs.
  • Hakka Tea - A popular tea brand in Taiwan, Hakka Tea offers a variety of unique tea flavors and blends.

Local Culture and Customs of Shimen

Shimen is known for its rich cultural heritage and is home to a large population of Hakka people. Visitors should be respectful of traditional customs and dress appropriately when visiting temples or other important places.

Best Time to Visit Shimen

Spring and autumn are the best times to visit Shimen as they offer comfortable weather, colorful scenery, and popular festivals like the Shimen Bathing Festival held in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shimen

Do:

  • Respect local customs, culture, and religion.
  • Dress appropriately especially when visiting temples or shrines.
  • Follow the rules and instructions of tour guides and local authorities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or smoke in public areas.
  • Touch or take anything from historical sites or museums.
  • Disrespect any cultural practices or people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shimen

Shimen is a safe place with a low crime rate. Visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Shimen

Shimen has good medical facilities, but visitors should prepare enough medicine and any necessary medical equipment if needed.

Getting to Shimen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shimen

  • Taipei Songshan Airport - 52 km away
  • Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport - 82 km away

Bus Stations Near Shimen

  • Gongguan Bus Station
  • Taoyuan Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also take trains or taxis to get to Shimen from nearby cities and destinations.

How to Get Around in Shimen

The best way to explore Shimen is by renting a bicycle or a scooter. Visitors can also take advantage of the local shuttle buses, taxi services, or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Shimen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yehliu Geopark - Known for its spectacular rock formations and unique scenery, Yehliu Geopark is located 50 km away from Shimen.
  • Pingxi Old Street - A nostalgic place full of traditional culture, local stores, and restaurants, it is only 22 km away from Shimen.
  • Jiufen Old Street - A popular tourist spot, Jiufen Old Street is known for its winding streets, classic architecture, and night view of the nearby Keelung City.

Conclusion

Shimen may be a small district in Taiwan, but it boasts a lot of exciting destinations and activities for visitors. From museums and traditional architecture to outdoor adventures and natural attractions, Shimen is sure to provide visitors with a one-of-a-kind experience. Remember to respect the local culture and customs and have a safe and enjoyable trip!
