Introduction to Jinguashi

Jinguashi is a small town located in northern Taiwan, known for its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural scenery. Originally a gold mining town during the Japanese colonial period, Jinguashi has since transformed into a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world.

The town’s narrow streets are lined with traditional houses, temples, and museums, showcasing its history and local culture. Visitors can also explore Jinguashi’s nearby forests and mountains, offering unforgettable outdoor experiences.

In this guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Jinguashi, from the top attractions to lodging and dining options. Let’s begin!

List of Places to Visit in Jinguashi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gold Museum - Learn about Jinguashi’s gold mining history and how it shaped the town’s culture and development.
  • Yingyang Sea - Marvel at this unique geological phenomenon where two contrasting colors of water meet.
  • Jinguashi Shinto Shrine - Pay your respects at this charming Japanese-style shrine, built in 1898.
  • Remains of the 13 Levels - Explore the remains of the mining shafts that go 13 levels deep, an important historical landmark of the gold mining industry.

Natural Attractions of Jinguashi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pingxi Line - Take a scenic train ride through lush green mountains and charming villages, including Jinguashi, on this historical railway route.
  • Juifang Fishing Port - Watch local fishermen bring in their daily catch and sample fresh seafood at the market.
  • Golden Waterfall - Admire the stunning sight of a waterfall that glitters with gold-like water due to the abundance of minerals in the water.
  • Teapot Mountain - Challenge yourself to hike up this steep trail leading to stunning views of the surrounding mountains, valleys, and sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jinguashi

Jinguashi is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with many exceptional hiking and biking trails:

  • Caoling Historic Trail - A 6.7 km historic trail that takes you through the lush green mountains and picturesque countryside of Taiwan.
  • Teapot Mountain Trail - A challenging 5.3 km hike that rewards you with stunning panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Pingxi Crags Hike - A 4.4 km hike that takes you through some of Taiwan’s most spectacular natural scenery, with towering rock formations and crystal clear streams.

Best Things to Do in Jinguashi: Activities and Tours

  • Tasting local seafood - Try local seafood dishes, such as grilled squid and shrimp, at one of the many seafood restaurants in Jinguashi.
  • Mining for Gold and other precious metals - Experience the thrill of gold panning and learn how to identify metals in the gold mining museum.
  • Taking photos with Cat Village - Take photos with the adorable cats that roam around Jinguashi, attracting visitors from all over.

Food and Drink Scene of Jinguashi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled squid - Freshly caught squid cooked on a grill and served with a variety of dipping sauces.
  • Fried fish cakes - Made of ground fish seasoned with herbs and spices.
  • Braised pork - Pork cooked in a sweet and savory sauce and served with steamed rice.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Tea - A Taiwanese classic, this sweet and creamy beverage comes with chewy tapioca pearls at the bottom.
  • Fresh Juices - Made from seasonal fruits, these refreshing drinks are perfect for hot summer days.
  • Taiwan Beer - The local favorite, this crisp lager is perfect with spicy food and seafood dishes.

Where to Stay in Jinguashi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Golden Hot Spring Hotel - Located in the mountains, this hotel offers breathtaking views and access to hot springs.
  • Hollow Castle B&B - A charming guesthouse with rustic interiors and excellent service.
  • Jinguashi Goldmine B&B - A restored goldmine turned bed and breakfast, this unique accommodation offers a glimpse into the town’s history.

## What to Buy in Jinguashi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gold products - Jinguashi is still home to several gold shops and jewelry makers that use locally mined gold to create unique pieces.
  • Cat souvenirs - Cat lovers will find plenty of souvenirs, such as plush toys and keychains, featuring the town’s famous feline residents.
  • Traditional teas - Taiwan is famous for its tea production, and Jinguashi is no exception. Look for local tea shops offering high-quality, traditional teas.

Local Culture and Customs of Jinguashi

  • Respect local customs - Jinguashi is a traditional town with deep roots in Chinese and Japanese culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as taking off shoes before entering temples and not touching religious artifacts.
  • Learning basic phrases - Many locals speak little English, so learning a few basic phrases in Mandarin or Taiwanese can go a long way.
  • Tipping - Tipping is not customary in Taiwan, so there is no need to tip service staff.

Best Time to Visit Jinguashi

The best time to visit Jinguashi is from October to March when the weather is cooler and drier. However, the town is always beautiful and worth visiting year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jinguashi

Do:

  • Take plenty of photos - Jinguashi is an Instagram-worthy destination, so don’t forget your camera!
  • Try local cuisine - Jinguashi’s food scene is second to none, so be sure to try as many local dishes as possible.
  • Respect local customs - As mentioned earlier, respect for local customs is highly appreciated and a must.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Jinguashi is a small town, and everyone should do their part to keep it clean and beautiful.
  • Touch artifacts - As mentioned earlier, avoid touching anything in the temples and museums.
  • Disrespect the locals - Jinguashi is a friendly town, and visitors should be respectful and polite to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jinguashi

Jinguashi is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as locking their hotel room and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Jinguashi

Jinguashi has several medical clinics and hospitals, and visitors should have no problem finding medical assistance if needed. However, visitors should still bring any necessary medications and insurance cards.

Getting to Jinguashi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jinguashi

  • Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport - The main airport in Taiwan, located about one hour and thirty minutes from Jinguashi.
  • Taipei Songshan Airport - A domestic airport in Taipei City, located about one hour from Jinguashi.

Bus Stations Near Jinguashi

  • Ruifang Bus Station - Buses to Jinguashi depart from Ruifang Bus Station, which is accessible by train from Taipei City and other parts of Taiwan.

Other Options

  • Private Transportation - Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Jinguashi from Taoyuan Airport, Taipei City, or other destinations in Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Jinguashi

The best way to get around Jinguashi is on foot, as the town’s narrow streets are perfect for walking. Visitors can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the outskirts of the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Jinguashi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taroko Gorge - A spectacular marble gorge located in Hualien County, about a two-hour drive from Jinguashi.
  • Shifen Waterfall and Village - A charming village famous for its sky lanterns and beautiful waterfall, located about a thirty-minute drive from Jinguashi.
  • Keelung City - A harbor city known for its seafood, night markets, and scenic views, located about a forty-five-minute drive from Jinguashi.

Conclusion

Jinguashi is a beautiful town that offers something for every traveler, from history and culture to outdoor adventures and local cuisine. With this guide, you’re now equipped with all the knowledge you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming destination. Enjoy your stay!
