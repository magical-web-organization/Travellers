Introduction to Jiufen

Nestled in the mountains of northern Taiwan, Jiufen is a charming town that is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Once a gold mining town during the Japanese colonial era, Jiufen is now a popular tourist destination that draws visitors from all over the world. Known for its winding alleyways, quaint teahouses, stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, and mouth-watering street food, Jiufen is a hidden gem that is just waiting to be explored.

List of Places to Visit in Jiufen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jiufen Old Street – The heart and soul of Jiufen, this bustling street is lined with red lanterns, souvenir shops, and food vendors selling everything from pineapple cakes to stinky tofu.
  • Jinguashi Gold Ecological Park – Located just a short drive from Jiufen, this museum is dedicated to the history of gold mining in Taiwan and offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of the miners who once lived and worked here.
  • Shengping Theater – Built in the 1930s during the Japanese colonial era, this theater has been lovingly restored to its former glory and is now used to screen classic Taiwanese films and host cultural events.
  • Museum of the Tea Culture – Housed in a traditional Taiwanese courtyard house, this museum showcases the rich history and culture of tea in Taiwan and offers visitors the chance to sample various types of tea.

Natural Attractions of Jiufen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keelung Mountain Hiking Trail – This challenging hiking trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and coastline and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • A-Mei Tea House – Perched on a hill overlooking the town, this iconic teahouse is famous for its panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Jiufen.
  • Yin-Yang Sea – Located just a short drive from Jiufen, this natural wonder is named for its unique blend of blue and green waters and offers visitors the chance to take a relaxing boat ride or go sea kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiufen

Jiufen is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of trails and routes to choose from. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, there’s something for everyone in Jiufen. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Jiufen include:

  • The Keelung Mountain Hiking Trail
  • The Sandiaoling Waterfall Trail
  • The Tamsui River Cycling Trail

Best Things to Do in Jiufen: Activities and Tours

  • Tea Tasting and Tea Ceremony – Jiufen is known for its rich tea culture, and there are plenty of opportunities to sample various types of tea and learn about the local tea-making traditions.
  • Food Tours – Jiufen is famous for its street food, and there are plenty of food tours available that will take you on a culinary journey through the town’s winding alleyways.
  • Night Market – Every evening, Jiufen transforms into a bustling night market, where visitors can try a variety of delicious Taiwanese street food and sample the local nightlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jiufen

Day 1: Explore Jiufen Old Street Day 2: Visit the Jinguashi Gold Ecological Park and Shengping Theater Day 3: Hike the Keelung Mountain Hiking Trail Day 4: Take a relaxing boat ride at Yin-Yang Sea Day 5: Attend a tea ceremony and visit the Museum of the Tea Culture Day 6: Enjoy an evening at the night market Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as the Elephant Trunk Rock or Yehliu Geopark

Food and Drink Scene of Jiufen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Balls – These chewy, flavorful balls of fish paste are a staple of Taiwanese street food and can be found all over Jiufen.
  • Pineapple Cakes – Sweet, crumbly pastries filled with pineapple paste, these cakes are a popular souvenir item and make for a delicious snack.
  • Stinky Tofu – Love it or hate it, stinky tofu is one of Taiwan’s most famous street foods and is a must-try for any adventurous foodie.

Local Beverages

  • Taiwanese Tea – From oolong to green to black, Jiufen is famous for its rich tea culture and there are numerous teahouses and shops where visitors can sample different types of tea.
  • Bubble Tea – Invented in Taiwan, bubble tea is a sweet, milky tea that is typically served with chewy tapioca pearls.

Where to Stay in Jiufen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • A-Mei Tea House – This iconic teahouse also offers cozy rooms with stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Jiufen Yijing Guesthouse – Located in the heart of Jiufen, this guesthouse offers traditional Taiwanese-style rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  • Golden 101 Guesthouse – With spacious, modern rooms and a rooftop terrace, this guesthouse is a popular choice for travelers who want to soak up the stunning views of Jiufen.

What to Buy in Jiufen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pineapple Cakes – A delicious and iconic Taiwanese snack, pineapple cakes are a must-buy souvenir when visiting Jiufen.
  • Tea – With its rich tea culture, Jiufen is a great place to buy high-quality Taiwanese tea, which makes for a unique and thoughtful gift.
  • Handicrafts – From wooden toys to handmade pottery, Jiufen is home to a wide range of skilled artisans who sell their wares in local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiufen

Jiufen is a town that is steeped in history and culture, and it’s important for visitors to respect the local customs and traditions. Some tips for experiencing Jiufen’s local culture include:

  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home – It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a Taiwanese home or temple.
  • Practice good table manners – In Taiwan, it is considered impolite to leave food on your plate, so make sure to only take what you can eat.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Mandarin – Many of the locals in Jiufen do not speak English, so knowing a few basic Mandarin phrases will go a long way in helping you communicate with them.

Best Time to Visit Jiufen

The best time to visit Jiufen is from late autumn to early spring (November to March), when the weather is cool and dry. Summers in Jiufen can be hot and humid, and typhoons are common during the summer months. However, if you’re a fan of fireworks, the annual Jiufen Lantern Festival (usually held in February or March) is not to be missed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiufen

Do:

  • Try the street food – Jiufen is famous for its street food, so be sure to come hungry and ready to sample a variety of delicious treats.
  • Take in the views – From stunning coastal vistas to lush mountain landscapes, Jiufen is home to some of Taiwan’s most beautiful scenery.
  • Learn about the local culture and history – Jiufen is a town that is steeped in history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites that offer insight into the town’s rich past.

Don’t:

  • Litter – It’s important to respect the environment and keep Jiufen clean and beautiful for future generations to enjoy.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals – Taiwanese people are friendly and welcoming, and it’s important to treat them with respect and kindness.
  • Disrupt the local culture or traditions – Remember that you are a guest in Jiufen and it’s important to be mindful of the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jiufen

Jiufen is a safe town with low crime rates, but visitors should still take common sense precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Jiufen

Tap water in Jiufen is safe to drink, but some visitors may experience stomach upset due to differences in the local cuisine. It’s always a good idea to bring along some over-the-counter medication for stomach issues just in case.

Getting to Jiufen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jiufen is located about an hour’s drive from Taipei, and there are several ways to get there.

Airports near Jiufen

The closest airport to Jiufen is Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, which is about an hour and a half’s drive from the town.

Bus stations near Jiufen

There is a frequent bus service from Taipei to Jiufen, with buses departing from various locations throughout the city.

Other options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Jiufen, but this option can be more expensive than taking public transportation.

How to Get Around in Jiufen

Jiufen is a small town that is best explored on foot. However, visitors who want to explore the surrounding area may want to rent a scooter or bicycle from one of the many rental shops in town.

Where to Go Next from Jiufen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taipei – Taiwan’s bustling capital city is just an hour’s drive from Jiufen and offers visitors a wide range of cultural, historical, and culinary experiences.
  • Yehliu Geopark – Located about 30 minutes’ drive from Jiufen, this natural wonder is home to a stunning collection of unique geological formations, including the iconic “Queen’s Head” rock formation.
  • Pingxi Old Street – Known for its charming, traditional architecture and stunning views of the surrounding mountains, Pingxi Old Street is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Taiwan.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, culture vulture, or foodie, Jiufen has something for everyone. From its winding alleyways to its stunning coastal views, Jiufen is a town that is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. So why not pack your bags and discover this hidden gem for yourself?
