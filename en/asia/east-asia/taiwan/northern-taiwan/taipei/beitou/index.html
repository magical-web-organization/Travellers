Introduction to Beitou

Beitou is a charming district situated in Taipei, the capital city of Taiwan. This tranquil haven is famous for its hot springs, parks, and scenic views of the surrounding mountains. The district has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Japanese colonial period, and it offers visitors an opportunity to learn about the history and traditions of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Beitou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Beitou is home to several museums and historical sites that provide visitors with a glimpse of the district’s culture and heritage. Some of the notable ones include:

  • Beitou Hot Springs Museum: This museum showcases the history and evolution of the hot springs in Beitou, and visitors can learn about the various types of hot springs and their health benefits.

  • Taipei Public Library Beitou Branch: Featuring a unique architectural design that makes it blend seamlessly with the surroundings, this library houses a vast collection of books and provides a serene atmosphere for reading.

  • Beitou Museum: This museum provides visitors with insights into the Japanese colonial period of Taiwan, including the architecture, art, and lifestyle of the period.

Natural Attractions of Beitou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beitou is also famous for its natural attractions that allow visitors to engage in outdoor activities while enjoying the beauty of the surroundings. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Yangmingshan National Park: This park is famous for its scenic views of the surrounding mountains and offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the region’s natural beauty.

  • Beitou Hot Springs Park: This park features several hot springs, waterfalls, and lush greenery, making it a perfect spot for relaxation and rejuvenation.

  • Guandu Nature Park: This park is located near the confluence of two rivers and offers visitors a chance to see a wide variety of birds and other flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beitou

Beitou has several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the scenic beauty of the region while engaging in physical activity. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Beitou Hot Spring Valley Trail: A 1.6 km trail that winds through the hot springs and lush greenery of the Beitou Hot Springs Park.

  • Yangmingshan Mountain Trail: This 8 km trail provides visitors with stunning views of the mountains and valleys around Beitou.

Best Things to Do in Beitou: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural and cultural attractions, Beitou offers visitors several activities and tours that allow them to experience the local culture and traditions. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Hot Spring Soaking: Visitors can soak in the hot springs, either in public or private venues, to experience the therapeutic effects of the hot springs.

  • Beitou Food Tour: A food tour of Beitou allows visitors to sample the district’s delicious cuisine while learning about the local food culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beitou

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to Beitou:

Day 1: Beitou Hot Springs Museum, Hot Spring Soaking at Beitou Hot Spring Park, Hiking in the Yangmingshan Mountain Trail

Day 2: Taipei Public Library, Beitou Food Tour

Day 3: Taipei Public Library, Guandu Nature Park

Day 4: Beitou Museum, Hot Spring Soaking at a Private Venue, Beitou Night Market

Day 5: Tamsui Old Street, Tamsui Fisherman’s Wharf

Day 6: Taipei 101, National Palace Museum

Day 7: Raohe Night Market, Elephant Mountain

Food and Drink Scene of Beitou

Must-Try Dishes

Beitou is known for its diverse food scene, and visitors can try several unique dishes that are exclusive to the region, including:

  • Beitou Tofu Pudding: A dessert made from soybeans that is served hot or cold and can be topped with various nuts and fruits.

  • Mochi: A sweet, chewy rice cake that can be found in several flavors and is a popular snack in Beitou.

Local Beverages

Visitors can also try several unique beverages in Beitou, including:

  • Tea: Beitou is famous for its tea production, and visitors can try various types of tea, including oolong, green tea, and black tea.

  • Plum Juice: A refreshing drink made from plums that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Beitou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beitou has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Beitou Hot Spring Resort: A luxurious resort that offers visitors private hot spring baths and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • The Gaia Hotel: A boutique hotel that features an eco-friendly design and provides visitors with a peaceful atmosphere.

  • Sleepy Dragon Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable accommodation and friendly service.

What to Buy in Beitou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring back several unique souvenirs and local products from Beitou, including:

  • Beitou Tofu Pudding: Visitors can bring this popular dessert back home as a gift for friends and family.

  • Tea: Beitou’s tea is famous for its unique aroma and taste, making it a perfect gift for tea lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Beitou

Beitou has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors must respect the local customs and traditions. Some of the customs include:

  • Removing shoes before entering homes or temples

  • Not eating or drinking while on public transportation

Best Time to Visit Beitou

The best time to visit Beitou is between March and May and between September and November, when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are less than during the peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beitou

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

  • Smoke in areas designated as non-smoking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beitou

Beitou is generally safe for visitors, but they should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and taking care of their belongings.

Health in Beitou

Beitou is famous for its hot springs, which have several health benefits. Visitors with certain medical conditions should consult a doctor before soaking in the hot springs.

Getting to Beitou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beitou

Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport is the nearest airport to Beitou, and visitors can take a taxi or bus to get to the district.

Bus Stations Near Beitou

Beitou Bus Station is the main bus station in Beitou, and visitors can take a bus to various destinations in the district and the surrounding area.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or hire a car to get around Beitou and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Beitou

Visitors can get around Beitou by taking a bus, taxi, or renting a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Beitou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can also visit several nearby destinations from Beitou, including:

  • Taipei: The capital city of Taiwan is famous for its historical and cultural attractions and its bustling food scene.

  • Tamsui: A charming town near Beitou that is home to several historical sites and has a laid-back atmosphere.

Conclusion

Beitou is an excellent destination for travelers looking to explore Taiwan’s culture, natural beauty, and hot springs. From its museums and historical sites to its outdoor adventures and food scene, Beitou offers something for everyone. Visitors can soak in the hot springs, explore the district’s parks and hiking routes or indulge in the local cuisine and products.
