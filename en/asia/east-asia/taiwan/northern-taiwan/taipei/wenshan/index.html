Wenshan Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tourist Guide for Wenshan

Wenshan, located in the southern part of Taipei, is one of the twelve districts in Taipei City. It is filled with scenic spots and cultural attractions for tourists to explore. The district features a blend of historical landmarks, cultural activities, and natural wonders that appeal to tourists looking for a peaceful setting. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, Wenshan has much to offer. In this travel guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know before visiting Wenshan.

Introduction to Wenshan

Wenshan is among the older districts within Taipei and is famous for its rich history, numerous temples, lovely parks, and lush green mountains. A unique feature of Wenshan is the abundant forest cover, making it a great place for nature lovers. It’s divided into three very different areas, including Wenshan District, Muzha District, and the Jingmei District. Each of these areas has its specialty, and together they provide an abundance of options for tourists to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Wenshan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wenshan is home to numerous museums and historical places that reflect Taiwanese culture, history, and heritage. The following are some examples of museums and historical places that you can explore:

  • National Palace Museum
  • Taipei 228 Memorial Museum
  • Lin Family Mansion and Garden
  • Fort San Domingo
  • Jingmei Human Rights Memorial and Cultural Park

Natural Attractions of Wenshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Wenshan district is dominated by green mountains featuring scenic hikes and bike rides. For those who love nature, some of the important natural attractions include:

  • Maokong Gondola
  • Wenshan Hot Springs
  • Wulai Waterfall
  • Xindian Riverside Park
  • Bitan Scenic Area

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wenshan

For outdoor enthusiasts, the following hiking and biking routes are worth exploring:

  • Nanhu Riverside Hiking Trail
  • Elephant Mountain Hiking Trail
  • Xiangshan Hiking Trail
  • Jiankou Great Wall Hiking Trail
  • Wenshan Biking Route

Best Things to Do in Wenshan: Activities and Tours

Wenshan offers a wide range of activities and tours for tourists, including:

  • Dine at Maokong Gondola
  • Hot springs therapy at Wenshan Hot Springs
  • Enjoy tea at Thousand Island Lake
  • Take a cycling or biking tour around Wenshan
  • Join a guided walking tour around Huashan Cultural Park

Food and Drink Scene of Wenshan

Must-Try Dishes

Taiwan is renowned for its street food, and Wenshan is no exception. Some of the must-try foods in the district include:

  • Danzi Noodles (porridge noodles)
  • Stinky Tofu
  • Scallion Pancakes
  • Braised Pork Rice
  • Bubble Tea

Local Beverages

Some of the local beverages worth trying include:

  • Three Wishes Tea
  • Aiyu Jelly Drink
  • Sugar Cane Juice
  • Taiwan Beer
  • Century Egg with Green Plum Juice

Local Culture and Customs of Wenshan

Taiwanese culture is an amalgamation of various Southeast Asian and Chinese cultures. In Wenshan, it’s evident in the many festivals, local cuisine, and historical sites. To respect the local culture and customs of Wenshan, visitors should consider the following:

  • Take your shoes off when entering a local’s home
  • Do not point fingers when speaking
  • Do not pat someone on the head
  • Do not touch people with your foot
  • Do not show the soles of your feet in public places

Best Time to Visit Wenshan

The best time to visit Wenshan is from March to May and September to November. During these times, the weather is comfortable and mild, providing the perfect environment for outdoor adventures. The district can get overcrowded during peak seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wenshan

Do:

  • Be respectful of other people’s cultures
  • Research about the places you want to visit
  • Learn a few words in Mandarin

Don’t:

  • Engage in prohibited activities
  • Overstay your visa
  • Litter or vandalize public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Wenshan

Wenshan is relatively safe, and tourists don’t have to worry about their safety. However, it’s essential to take precautionary measures when exploring the district, such as being vigilant with your valuables.

Health in Wenshan

Wenshan doesn’t have many health concerns, but tourists should take mosquito repellent, especially during the summer season. It’s important to have travel insurance before visiting Wenshan.

Bus Stations Near Wenshan

How to Get Around in Wenshan

Wenshan has an efficient public transport system consisting of buses, trains, and taxis. Tourists can purchase an EasyCard or iPASS to pay for transport fares. Cycling and walking are popular options to explore Wenshan.

Conclusion

Wenshan district, with its combination of history, culture, and natural beauty, is a must-visit place in Taipei. The district’s unique blending of urban and rural lifestyles is what makes it an interesting destination. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve covered some of the significant tourist attractions, the best time to visit, and cultural customs. With the information in this guide, you’re ready to plan your trip and explore Wenshan.
