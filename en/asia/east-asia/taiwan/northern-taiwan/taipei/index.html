Introduction to Taipei

Taipei, the capital city of Taiwan, is a bustling metropolis that boasts a unique blend of modernity and tradition. It’s home to some of the world’s most impressive skyscrapers, mouth-watering street food, and modern public transportation systems, as well as its historic temples and intricate Chinese architecture. Taipei is a favorite destination for international tourists who come to see its magnificent beauty, a glimpse of ancient Chinese culture, and its hospitable people. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore Taipei’s best offerings and help you plan your perfect trip to this cosmopolitan city.

Local Culture and Customs of Taipei

In Taipei, locals mostly speak Mandarin Chinese, and most people observe Chinese culture and customs. It is customary to remove your shoes in someone’s home, and always try not to step on someone’s doormat. Bowing or nodding is also a sign of respect. Taiwanese people are hospitable and always willing to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit Taipei

The best time to visit Taipei is between October and December. It’s cooler and less humid, making it an ideal time to visit outdoor attractions like Yangmingshan National Park. Spring (March-May) is also a beautiful time to visit when the weather is warm, and flowers are blooming.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taipei

Taipei is known to be a relatively safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate. Still, it’s always advisable to be vigilant and take necessary precautions, such as keeping your valuables close and not leaving your belongings unattended.

Health in Taipei

Taipei is well-equipped to deal with medical emergencies, and there are many hospitals and clinics readily available. Tap water is safe and drinkable in Taipei, so there’s usually no need to buy bottled water.

Getting to Taipei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taipei is well-connected to the rest of the world with its international airport, Taoyuan International Airport (TPE). Here are a few transportation options to consider:

  • Air: Taoyuan International Airport links Taipei to many major cities in Asia and other parts of the world.
  • Bus: Taipei has an extensive bus network that offers affordable transportation to other parts of the city.
  • Metro: Taipei’s MRT system is excellent, affordable, and easy to navigate.
  • Taxi: Taxis are another option for getting around, but they can be more expensive than other modes of transportation.

Airports Near Taipei

  • Taoyuan International Airport (TPE)
  • Taipei Songshan Airport (TSA)

Bus Stations Near Taipei

  • Taipei Bus Station - reachable by the MRT

Other Options

  • Car rental services
  • High-speed rail network

How to Get Around in Taipei

Taipei has a well-developed transportation system, making it easy to navigate around the city. Here are some options for getting around:

  • Metro: Taipei’s MRT system is affordable, efficient, and easy to navigate.
  • Bus: Taipei’s bus network is extensive, and bus rides are quite affordable.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Taipei, and they are metered.
