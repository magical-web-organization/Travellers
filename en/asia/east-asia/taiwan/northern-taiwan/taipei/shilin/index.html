Introduction to Shilin

Shilin is a popular district located in the northern part of Taipei, Taiwan. It is known for its famous night market, where tourists can enjoy a variety of Taiwanese street food and local products while experiencing the bustling atmosphere of the city. Apart from the night market, Shilin is also home to various cultural and natural attractions that make it a great destination for travelers. This travel guide will give you a comprehensive overview of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Shilin, Taipei.

List of Places to Visit in Shilin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Palace Museum

The National Palace Museum houses one of the largest collections of Chinese art in the world, with over 700,000 pieces. Visitors can explore the museum’s vast collection of ancient artifacts, paintings, and calligraphy. Don’t miss the Jadeite Cabbage, one of the museum’s most popular exhibits.

Shilin Official Residence

The Shilin Official Residence was once the home of Chiang Kai-shek, the former President of the Republic of China. This historical landmark is now open to the public and showcases the life and legacy of the former president through exhibitions and guided tours of the residence and surrounding gardens.

Zhishan Cultural and Ecological Garden

The Zhishan Cultural and Ecological Garden is a perfect place to relax and enjoy the tranquility of nature. The garden features a traditional Chinese garden, a butterfly farm, and an ecology pond. Visitors can also participate in activities like Chinese calligraphy or tea ceremonies.

Natural Attractions of Shilin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yangmingshan National Park

Yangmingshan National Park is the perfect destination for nature lovers. The park is home to several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains. The park is also famous for its hot springs, where visitors can relax and unwind after a long day of exploring.

Beitou Hot Springs

Beitou Hot Springs is another popular place for visitors to unwind and relax in Taipei. The area is known for its natural hot springs and scenic views of the mountains. Visitors can also explore the Beitou Hot Spring Museum, which explains the history of hot springs in the area.

Taipei Astronomical Museum

The Taipei Astronomical Museum is a great place to learn about the universe and explore the stars. The museum features interactive exhibits, a planetarium, and an observatory where visitors can observe the moon and stars.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shilin

Xiao You Keng Trail

The Xiao You Keng Trail is a popular hiking trail in Yangmingshan National Park. The trail offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains. It is an easy hike suitable for beginners.

Guandu Nature Park

The Guandu Nature Park is a great place for biking enthusiasts. The park features several biking trails that offer scenic views of the mountains and the river.

Best Things to Do in Shilin: Activities and Tours

Shilin Night Market

The Shilin Night Market is one of the most famous night markets in Taiwan. It offers a wide variety of street food, local products, and souvenirs. The market is open every day from 5:00 pm to midnight.

Taipei Zoo

The Taipei Zoo is a great place for families to spend a day. The zoo is home to over 4,000 animals from all over the world. The zoo’s star attraction is the Giant Panda exhibit, where visitors can see the adorable pandas up close.

Shilin Ghost Town

The Shilin Ghost Town is an abandoned village that has become a popular spot for photographers and urban explorers. The village was abandoned due to a landslide in the 1960s and now serves as a cultural landmark.

Food and Drink Scene of Shilin

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a popular snack in Taiwan, and the Shilin Night Market offers some of the best. The chicken is usually marinated in a variety of spices and then deep-fried to perfection.

Oyster Omelet

The oyster omelet is another popular Taiwanese street food. The omelet consists of a mixture of eggs, oysters, and vegetables, and is usually topped with a sweet and sour sauce.

Bubble Tea

Bubble tea originated in Taiwan and has become a popular drink all over the world. The tea is usually mixed with milk and sugar and then topped with tapioca pearls.

Local Beverages

Taiwan Beer

Taiwan Beer is a popular brand of beer in Taiwan. It is a refreshing and light beer that goes well with any Taiwanese dish.

Oolong Tea

Oolong tea is a popular tea in Taiwan. It is a semi-fermented tea that has a unique flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Shilin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Grand Hotel

The Grand Hotel is an iconic hotel in Taipei. The hotel features luxurious rooms and suites, as well as several restaurants and a spa. It is located near several cultural attractions and provides easy access to the city center.

Taipei Metro Stay-Shihlin Stay

The Taipei Metro Stay-Shihlin Stay is a great budget option for travelers. The hotel features clean and comfortable rooms and is located near several local restaurants and shops.

SleepBox Hotel

The SleepBox Hotel offers a unique and affordable accommodation option for travelers. The hotel features capsule-style rooms that provide complete privacy and comfort.

What to Buy in Shilin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pineapple Cakes

Pineapple cakes are a popular Taiwanese dessert. They are small, square-shaped cakes filled with pineapple jam and covered in a buttery crust. They make a great souvenir to bring home.

Paper Umbrellas

Paper umbrellas are a traditional Taiwanese handicraft. They are made from paper and bamboo and come in a variety of colors and designs.

Taiwanese Tea

Taiwan is known for its high-quality tea. Oolong tea, in particular, is a popular tea that makes a great souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Shilin

Shilin is a great place to experience traditional Taiwanese culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering a home or temple, and bowing as a sign of respect when meeting someone new.

Best Time to Visit Shilin

The best time to visit Shilin is during the spring or fall. The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than in the summer. The Shilin Night Market is open year-round and is a great place to visit in the evenings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shilin

Do:

  • Try the street food at the Shilin Night Market.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Yangmingshan National Park.

Don’t:

  • Litter.
  • Ignore signs indicating cultural or religious areas.
  • Touch artifacts in museums or historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shilin

Shilin is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items.

Health in Shilin

Taiwan has a good healthcare system, and visitors can rest assured that medical care is readily available in case of emergencies. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Dengue fever, by wearing long sleeves and pants, using mosquito repellent, and staying indoors during peak mosquito hours.

Getting to Shilin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shilin

The closest airport to Shilin is the Taipei Songshan Airport. It is located about 30 minutes away from Shilin by car and provides domestic flights to major cities in Taiwan.

The Taipei Taoyuan Airport is another option, and it provides international flights to major cities all over the world. It is located about 45 minutes away from Shilin by car.

Bus Stations Near Shilin

The Taipei City Bus Station is the main station in the city and provides bus service to Shilin. The trip takes approximately 20 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the subway to Shilin. The Shilin Station is located on the Tamsui-Xinyi Line and provides easy access to the district.

How to Get Around in Shilin

Shilin is easily navigable by foot or by public transportation. The Taipei Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) provides easy access to most of the district’s attractions. Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a bike to explore the district at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Shilin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tamsui

Tamsui is a historic district located north of Taipei City. It is known for its waterfront and night market, which features a variety of Taiwanese street food and local products. Visitors can also explore the Tamsui Old Street, which provides a glimpse into Taiwan’s history.

Jiufen

Jiufen is a scenic mountain town located east of Taipei City. The town is known for its winding streets and traditional tea houses. Visitors can also explore the Jiufen Gold Mine Museum, which tells the story of the town’s mining history.

Conclusion

Shilin is a great destination for travelers looking to experience traditional Taiwanese culture and explore the city’s natural and cultural attractions. The district offers a wide variety of things to do and see, from hiking in Yangmingshan National Park to shopping at the Shilin Night Market. Visitors will also enjoy the local food scene and the variety of accommodation options available. Plan your trip to Shilin, Taipei, and discover all that this vibrant district has to offer.
