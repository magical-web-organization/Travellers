Introduction to Neihu

Located in the northern part of Taipei, Neihu is a rapidly growing district that boasts a balanced mix of natural and urban attractions. It’s a popular destination for tourists who seek outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Neihu

Here are the must-see destinations in Neihu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National Palace Museum - This world-renowned institution houses over 700,000 Chinese imperial artifacts, making it one of the largest museums in the world.
  2. Lin An Tai Historical House & Museum - Built in the 18th century, this traditional Taiwanese home is a well-preserved example of Chinese architecture.
  3. Neihu Pauji Temple - A Taoist temple that dates back to the 17th century and features intricate carvings and stunning artwork.

Natural Attractions of Neihu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Yangmingshan National Park - This volcanic national park is home to hot springs, hiking trails, and stunning views of Taipei.
  2. Dahu Park - A recreational park featuring a large lake, paddle boating, and scenic walking trails.
  3. Maokong Gondola - A cable car ride that takes you to the top of a mountain for breathtaking views of the city and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neihu

For those interested in outdoor adventures, Neihu has a variety of hiking and biking trails:

  1. Songren Trail - A challenging hiking trail that features stunning views of Taipei and the surrounding mountains.
  2. Guandu Nature Park - A scenic route that takes you through a wetland ecosystem and offers views of migratory birds.
  3. Rainbow Riverside Bike Trail - A family-friendly bike trail that follows a river and offers sightings of wildlife such as turtles and herons.

Best Things to Do in Neihu: Activities and Tours

  1. Taiwanese Tea Experience - Neihu is famous for its tea culture, and visitors can participate in tea ceremonies and even pick their tea leaves.
  2. Night Market Experience - A must-try in Taipei, Neihu’s night market is a foodie’s paradise, with lots of street food vendors and traditional games stalls.
  3. DIY Class Experience in Zhongzheng & Youya Xincun Farmers Association - Visitors can participate in hands-on classes such as ceramic making or fruit carving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neihu

Day 1: Explore Neihu’s Cultural Landmarks Day 2: Hike Yangmingshan National Park Day 3: Visit Dahu Park and Maokong Gondola Day 4: Enjoy a Taiwanese Tea Experience Day 5: Go on a Sunset Hike at Songren Trail Day 6: Try Local Cuisine at Neihu Night Market Day 7: Participate in a DIY Class

Food and Drink Scene of Neihu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beef Noodle Soup - A Taiwanese staple, Neihu has many variations of this popular dish.
  2. Pan-Fried Dumplings - A beloved street food in Taipei, you can find these pan-fried dumplings in Neihu’s night market.
  3. Oyster Noodles - A savory dish made with fresh oysters and rice noodles.

Local Beverages

  1. Bubble Tea - A Taiwanese invention, this refreshing drink features black tea and tapioca pearls.
  2. Taipei Beer - A locally brewed beer that’s popular among locals and visitors alike.
  3. Whiskey Tasting Experience - Taiwan is known for its award-winning whiskey, and visitors can enjoy a tasting experience in Neihu.

Where to Stay in Neihu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. W Taipei - A luxurious hotel in the heart of Neihu, offering views of the city from every room.
  2. Taipei Marriott Hotel - A stylish hotel that features an outdoor pool and fitness center.
  3. Mei Stay - A cozy hostel that offers private rooms and a shared lounge.

What to Buy in Neihu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Taiwanese Tea - Neihu’s tea culture is celebrated throughout Taiwan, and visitors can take home high-quality teas.
  2. Yilan Pudding - A popular Taiwanese dessert made with custard and caramel.
  3. Ceramics - Neihu is known for its pottery, and visitors can buy handcrafted ceramics as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Neihu

  1. Respect for elders - Taiwanese culture values respect for elders, so it’s important to show deference to older people.
  2. Tipping - Tipping is not expected in Taiwan, so you don’t have to leave a tip at restaurants or hotels.
  3. Gift-giving - Bringing a small gift when visiting someone’s home is a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Neihu

The best time to visit Neihu is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild, and the temperature is comfortable. The cherry blossoms in Yangmingshan National Park are particularly beautiful during the spring season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neihu

Do:

  1. Try Taiwanese tea and local cuisine.
  2. Explore Neihu’s cultural landmarks and natural attractions.
  3. Show respect for local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Disrespect elders or local customs.
  3. Haggle at street markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neihu

Taiwan is one of the safest countries in the world, and Neihu is a relatively safe district. However, be cautious when crossing the street and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Neihu

Taiwan has a high standard of healthcare, and there are several hospitals and clinics in Neihu. It’s recommended that visitors purchase travel insurance before coming to Taiwan.

Getting to Neihu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neihu

Taipei Songshan Airport is the closest airport to Neihu, and it’s about a 30-minute ride by taxi or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Neihu

The Taipei Bus Station and the Taipei City Hall Bus Station offer several routes that pass through Neihu.

Other Options

The Taipei MRT system is an affordable and convenient way to get around Taipei. There are several MRT stations in Neihu, including Gangqian Station, Neihu Station, and Xihu Station.

How to Get Around in Neihu

Walking is a great way to explore Neihu’s nature trails and parks, and the Taipei MRT system is ideal for getting around the district and the rest of Taipei.

Where to Go Next from Neihu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Beitou Hot Springs - Just a short train ride away, Beitou is known for its hot springs and beautiful scenery.
  2. Tamsui Old Street - A popular tourist destination featuring traditional Taiwanese architecture and delicious street food.
  3. Jinguashi Gold Mine - An abandoned gold mine that has been turned into a tourist attraction with museums and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Neihu is a district that offers something for every traveler, whether it’s outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or local cuisine. With its scenic views, cultural heritage, and vibrant atmosphere, Neihu is a must-visit destination in Taipei.
