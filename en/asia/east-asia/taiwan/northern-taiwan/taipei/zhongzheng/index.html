Introduction to Zhongzheng

Zhongzheng is a district located in the heart of Taipei, Taiwan’s vibrant capital city. It is known for its cultural landmarks, bustling markets, and a range of outdoor activities that will excite any adventurer. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the top attractions and things to do in Zhongzheng and help you plan the perfect trip to this bustling district.

List of Places to Visit in Zhongzheng

Zhongzheng is a fascinating district that offers a mix of culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here are some must-see places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Taiwan Museum: Explore the history, culture, and natural environment of Taiwan with over 60,000 exhibits.
  • Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall: A grand monument dedicated to Taiwan’s former president, surrounded by beautiful gardens, and a great place for picnics and summer concerts.
  • Taipei 228 Memorial Museum: An important and informative museum that sheds light on the tragic event that took place on February 28, 1947.
  • Presidential Office Building: A historic structure featuring a grand facade with a rich political history.
  • Longshan Temple: One of the oldest and most revered temples in Taipei, surrounded by traditional markets with plenty of local street food stalls.

Natural Attractions of Zhongzheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Daan Forest Park: A massive park with plenty of green space, trails, and various activities for all ages.
  • Taipei Botanical Garden: Beautiful landscape garden in the heart of the city with over thousands of plant species, floral displays, and a lagoon.
  • National Taiwan University Hospital Rooftop Garden: Incredible Secret Garden with spectacular views of the Taipei 101
  • Dajia Riverside Park: A relaxing park alongside a calm river complete with biking and walking pathways, river cruising, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhongzheng

Zhongzheng offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Xiangshan (Sun Yat-Sen) Trail: A popular hiking trail with stunning views of Taipei 101.
  • Jiantan Mountain Trail: A moderate hiking trail located near Shilin Night Market with views of the Taipei basin.
  • Treasure Hill Artist Village Trail: A unique trail dotted with artist materials and numerous murals created by local art enthusiasts.
  • Taipei Riverside Bikeway: A scenic bike route that runs along the Tamsui River, perfect for biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Zhongzheng: Activities and Tours

  • Bike tours: Perfect way to explore the city and its hidden gems by bicycle with guided tours.
  • Soup dumpling tasting: Visit renowned restaurants and taste some of the best soup dumplings in the district.
  • Street Food Tours: A must-do tour to taste delicious Taiwanese delicacies at local night markets, like the famous Ningxia Night Market.
  • learn to make traditional Taiwanese bubble tea and indulge in a refreshing cold drink.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhongzheng

Day 1: Explore the National Taiwan Museum. Day 2: Visit the famous Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall. Day 3: Go for a hike on Xiangshan (Sun Yat-Sen) Trail. Day 4: Tour the Taipei 228 Memorial Museum. Day 5: Visit the National Taiwan University Hospital Rooftop Garden, and then enjoy shopping at the Ximending shopping district. Day 6: Go to Taipei Botanical Garden and explore the city scenic spots. Day 7: Wrap up your trip by taking a food tour, trying the traditional soup dumplings, and walking around the local night markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhongzheng

Taipei offers a great variety of food, and Zhongzheng is no exception. Here are some popular dishes you must-try in Zhongzheng:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soup dumplings or ‘Xiao Long Bao’.
  • Scallion Pancake (Shallot Cake)
  • Beef Noodle Soup
  • Taiwanese-style Pork Chop Rice
  • Oyster Omelette
  • Stinky Tofu

Local Beverages

  • Bubble tea made with fresh tea leaves and milk comes with various exotic flavors and healthy ingredients.
  • Taiwan beer and Golden Medal Taiwan Beer, which is lighter with a smooth finish.
  • Fresh fruit juice, with a variety of options to choose from.

Where to Stay in Zhongzheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhongzheng is home to a range of excellent accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Three recommendations are:

  • The Play Design Hotel: With only 13 rooms, this hotel embraces environmental values and offering a unique design from different professionals.
  • Caesar Metro Taipei: Luxurious hotel providing elegant rooms and amenities, with excellent services and located in the centre of the city.
  • NiHao Café Hotel: Affordable option with artistic ambiance, great location, and friendly staff ready to make your stay exceptional.

What to Buy in Zhongzheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shilin Official Home (Jingmei Ceramics) tea cups and a tea pitcher shaped in Bamboo with good quality tea leaves.
  • Lots of trendy clothing options available at Ximending Market
  • Taiwanese delicacies to share with friends and family, such as the famous pineapple cakes, sun biscuits, or Taiwanese nougat.
  • Kitchenware, pottery, and Taiwan’s cultural products.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhongzheng

Taiwanese are always polite and respectful, so they appreciate those who follow their customs. It’s always nice to do a little research beforehand:

  • Address people with their title accompanied by the family name, like Director Chen or Mrs. Lee.
  • Always wash your hands before a meal and say thank you after you have finished eating.
  • Taking off the shoes when entering someone’s house or temple is customary in Taiwan.

Best Time to Visit Zhongzheng

Spring (March to May) and Autumn (September to November) are the best time to visit as the weather is neither too hot nor too cold. Winters can be rainy and chilly, while summers are pretty hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhongzheng

Do:

  • Carry enough cash while visiting small food markets and street vendors to get the best deals.
  • Use your right hand when using chopsticks or when making contact, i.e., exchanging business cards.
  • Try to learn a few basic phrases like “Hello,” “Thank you,” and “Please”.

Don’t:

  • Don’t tip anyone except for spas or luxury restaurants.
  • Don’t let your personal trash be dropped on the streets or other public places in the district.
  • Don’t engage in public drinking, smoking, or drug use.

Safety and Health

While Zhongzheng is considered a pretty safe district, it is always best to take precautionary measures, just like any other tourist destination worldwide.

Safety in Zhongzheng

  • Watch out for pickpockets in crowded places.
  • Do not carry your passport with you at all times, but instead, take a passport copy or take a photo in the passport of the first pages.
  • Always take care of your personal belongings especially when commuting.

Health in Zhongzheng

Taipei pioneers in modern medical facilities and advanced medical technology. Carry enough supply of common medications is a must, as medical supplies are costly in Taiwan.

Getting to Zhongzheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zhongzheng is located in the center of Taipei, which makes it quite accessible.

Airports Near Zhongzheng

Taipei International Airport (TPE) is a major airport located in Taoyuan, the northern part of Taiwan. One can take a taxi, bus or the metro train to reach Zhongzheng. The travel time is around 45-60 minutes depending on the mode of transport.

Bus Stations Near Zhongzheng

Taipei Main Bus Station is the central transportation hub for Taiwan’s extensive bus system. Bus No. 220, 22, 205, 209, and 277 all provide direct transport to Zhongzheng district.

Other Options

The Rapid Transit System (MRT) is one of the most convenient and easy to use modes of transportation in Taipei as it is simple to navigate and affordable.

How to Get Around in Zhongzheng

Walking is one of the best ways to get around and explore in Zhongzheng. Buses and the MRT are also readily available, reliable, and affordable options for transportation. However, taxis are usually more costly but also widely available.

Where to Go Next from Zhongzheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beitou Hot Springs and Geothermal Valley: Around an hour from Zhongzheng by MRT, experiencing hot springs at Taipei’s Beitou district is a must-visit.
  • Taipei 101: An iconic landmark building in the heart of Taipei, reachable by the MRT easily.
  • National Palace Museum: A museum that has a fabulous collection of artifacts, antiques, fascinating artwork, and much more.

Conclusion

Zhongzheng is an exciting district to visit with plenty of things to do and see. From exploring cultural and historical landmarks to enjoying the great outdoors and savoring some local cuisine, Zhongzheng has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has been helpful in planning your trip, and we wish you a memorable and enjoyable stay in Taipei’s vibrant district of Zhongzheng.
