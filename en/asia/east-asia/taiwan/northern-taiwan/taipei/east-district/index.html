Introduction to East District

Located in Taipei, Northern Taiwan, East District is a bustling district known for its amazing attractions, cultural landmarks, and unique experiences. The district is dotted with museums, parks, and scenic peaks and offers visitors ample opportunities to explore Taiwan’s stunning natural beauty. In this guide, we’ve compiled a comprehensive list of the top places to visit, things to do, and tips to help you plan your itinerary for the perfect travel experience in East District.

List of Places to Visit in East District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East District is steeped in history and culture, and what better way to discover it than by visiting some of its museums and historical places?

Taipei 101

Taipei 101 is one of the tallest buildings in the world, and the view of Taipei from its observatory is simply breathtaking. It’s an amazing feat of engineering and one of the most recognizable landmarks in Taiwan.

National Palace Museum

The National Palace Museum showcases a vast collection of artifacts from Chinese history, including rare scrolls, pottery, and artifacts from the Imperial Palace. It’s a must-visit for anyone interested in Chinese history and culture.

Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall

The Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall memorializes the founder of the Republic of China, Dr. Sun Yat-sen. It’s a grand, imposing structure with a beautiful park and a tranquil lake.

Natural Attractions of East District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East District is also home to some incredible natural attractions that offer stunning views and outdoor adventures.

Elephant Mountain

Located just a short hike from Taipei 101, Elephant Mountain offers an incredible panoramic view of Taipei’s skyline. It’s the perfect spot for a sunset picnic or a morning workout.

Da’an Forest Park

Da’an Forest Park is a sprawling green space in the heart of the city. It’s a popular spot for locals to relax and exercise, with walking trails, bike rentals, and a lake for paddle-boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East District

East District is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

Xiangshan Hiking Trail

Xiangshan Hiking Trail is located right behind Taipei 101 and offers hikers a challenge as they climb over 500 steps to the top. The trail is well-manicured, safe and offers one of the best views of Taipei.

Taiwan Cycle Route 1

For cyclists or bike enthusiasts,Taiwan Cycle Route 1 is the ultimate challenge. Also known as the “Round-the-Island” route, it’s a 900-mile journey loop that encircles the entire island of Taiwan. The stretch of the route that passes through East District offers some of the most amazing views that Taiwan has to offer.

Best Things to Do in East District: Activities and Tours

East District offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours to explore the culture, food, and natural beauty of the region.

Taipei Street Food Tours

Taipei is famous for its street food scene, and a guided street food tour offers visitors the opportunity to sample the best of the city’s culinary delights. You can book your tour with either a private guide or in a guided group tour.

Taipei City Bus Tours

One of the best ways to explore Taipei is through the hop-on/hop-off bus tours. These tours provide a comprehensive introduction to the city and allows visitors to hop on and off to explore key attractions at their leisure.

Week-Long Itinerary for East District

If you’re planning a week-long trip to East District, here’s an itinerary to maximize your stay:

Day 1: Visit Taipei 101 Observatory and Xinyi Shopping District. Day 2: Explore the National Palace Museum and the Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall. Day 3: Take a day trip to the Beitou Hot Springs. Day 4: Take a bike ride around Taipei City via Cycle Route 1. Day 5: Visit the Elephant Mountain hiking trail and Da’an Forest Park. Day 6: Take a food tour of Taipei’s street food scene. Day 7: Visit the Huashan 1914 Creative Park and end the day with a night market trip.

Food and Drink Scene of East District

East District’s food and drink scene is legendary, and here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

Beef Noodle Soup

Beef noodle soup is a comfort food staple in Taipei, and every local has their favourite spot for a steaming bowl. This is a must-try for first-time visitors.

Dan Bing

Dan Bing is a Taiwanese-style egg crepe served with a variety of fillings like scallions and pork floss. It’s a popular breakfast food and can be found in most breakfast shops.

Local Beverages

Bubble Tea

Bubble tea is one of Taiwan’s most famous drinks, and visitors simply cannot leave Taiwan without trying it. There are hundreds of bubble tea shops spread throughout the city.

Taiwan Beer

Taiwan Beer is a favourite among locals, and it’s a light, refreshing beer that’s perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in East District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East District has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget, such as:

Hostels

East District is home to several budget-friendly hostels, which offer decent accommodation options for backpackers and solo travellers.

Luxury Hotels

Luxury hotels are dotted around East District, offering modern amenities and comfort to travellers seeking the best experience of their life.

What to Buy in East District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East District is famous for its cultural souvenirs, and here are some must-buy items:

Pineapple Cakes

Pineapple cakes are an iconic Taiwanese treat made from buttery pastry and sweet pineapple jam filling. They make for a perfect gift for loved ones.

Taiwanese Tea

Taiwan is famous for handcrafted teas, and visitors can take home locally sourced Oolong or Jasmin tea.

Local Culture and Customs of East District

Mandarin Chinese is the official language in East District; however, there is a language barrier for non-Chinese speakers. Visitors should use simple English when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit East District

The best time to visit East District is during the autumn and winter months when it is drier and cooler. The climate in Taiwan can be quite humid, and this generally makes summers uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of East District

Do:

  • Carry an umbrella during monsoon seasons.
  • Take photos of attractions but respect private property by avoiding posting photographs of residential areas and restricted monuments.
  • Try local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter, Taiwan has strict littering laws
  • Touch or move religious objects
  • Smoke in non-smoking areas

Safety and Health

East District is generally a safe place to visit, but it is wise to exercise caution when exploring the area.

Safety in East District

Be cautious when crossing busy streets and watch for motorbikes, a significant hazard to pedestrians.

Health in East District

Make sure to stay hydrated during hot weather, and carry sunscreen and mosquito repellent during the summer months.

Getting to East District: Transportation Options, and Logistics

Visitors to East District can easily arrive via different means of transportation.

Airports Near East District

Taipei Songshan Airport is the closest airport to East District, located just 15 minutes from the district by taxi.

Bus Stations Near East District

The Taipei Bus Station is located in the city centre, with bus locations all over the city zone.

Other Options

The Taipei Rapid Transit System, known as the Taipei MRT or Taipei Metro, connects Taipei city to different routes across the district.

How to Get Around in East District

Visitors to East District can explore the area with ease using different transportation options like MRT, Bikes, or by Taxi.

Where to Go Next from East District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East District is well-connected to many other destinations in Taiwan, including the following popular locations:

  • National Palace Museum.
  • Shilin Night Market.
  • Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall.

Conclusion

East District is a great city for exploration and adventure. This comprehensive guide has provided everything you need to know about the district’s must-see attractions, outdoor adventures, and famous cuisine. Plan your trip, soak in the city’s culture and history, and bring back some wonderful memories that you will cherish for years to come.
