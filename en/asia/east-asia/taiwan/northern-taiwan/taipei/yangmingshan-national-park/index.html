Introduction to Yangmingshan National Park

Yangmingshan National Park is a vast nature reserve located in the north of Taipei, Taiwan. The park is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich wildlife, and geothermal hot springs. Its landscapes are diverse, ranging from rolling hills and mountain ranges to volcanic craters and hot springs.

The park is also home to several cultural attractions, including museums, temples, and historical landmarks. Whether you’re looking to explore nature or learn about the local culture, Yangmingshan has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Yangmingshan National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yangmingshan National Park Museum - This museum showcases the park’s natural history, geology, and ecology through interactive exhibits and multimedia installations.
  • Zhishan Cultural and Ecological Garden - Located on the northwest edge of the park, this garden features a variety of plants and animals native to Taiwan.
  • Chingtienkang - A popular viewpoint that offers incredible panoramic views of Taipei City, Beitou, and the park.
  • Lengshuikeng Hot Spring - A geothermal hot spring that is believed to have healing properties.

Natural Attractions of Yangmingshan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qixing Mountain - The highest peak in Yangmingshan National Park, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Xiaoyoukeng - A scenic area with bubbling hot springs and fumaroles (steam vents).
  • Taiwan National University Sports Center - This area offers a variety of outdoor activities, including basketball, archery, and rock-climbing.
  • Xiaoguanyin Waterfall - A picturesque waterfall located in the southern part of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yangmingshan National Park

Yangmingshan National Park is a popular destination for hikers and bikers due to its numerous trails and roads. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the park include:

  • Qixing Mountain Trail - A challenging but rewarding hike to the top of Qixing Mountain, which offers stunning views of the city and the park.
  • Yangmingshan Bicycle Trail - A scenic 10-kilometer route that takes you through some of the park’s most beautiful landscapes.
  • Jhuzihhu Trail - A moderate hike that takes you through the park’s forests and offers views of Taipei City.
  • Taiwan National University Sports Center Trail - A short but picturesque trail that takes you through the park’s lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Yangmingshan National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Experience the hot springs - The park is known for its hot springs, and there are several places throughout the park where you can soak and relax.
  • Take a guided tour - There are several guided tours available that will take you through the park and provide you with a wealth of information about its geology, flora, and fauna.
  • Explore the cultural attractions - There are several museums and historical landmarks in the park that are worth exploring.
  • Go hiking or biking - The park has numerous trails and roads that are ideal for hiking and biking.

Food and Drink Scene of Yangmingshan National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup - A hearty dish of noodles, beef, and vegetables in a savory broth.
  • Stinky Tofu - A unique dish made from fermented tofu that has a strong odor but a delicious taste.
  • Bubble Tea - A famous Taiwanese drink that consists of sweetened tea, milk, and tapioca pearls.

Local Beverages

  • Taiwan Beer - A refreshing lager that is widely available throughout the country.
  • Oolong Tea - A fragrant and flavorful tea that is grown in Taiwan’s mountainous regions.
  • Freshly squeezed fruit juice - A popular and healthy drink that is made from locally grown fruits.

Where to Stay in Yangmingshan National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Yangmingshan National Park, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Yangmingshan Tienlai Resort & Spa - A luxurious resort that offers hot springs, spa treatments, and scenic views of the park.
  • Yangmingshan Youth Activity Center - A budget-friendly option that offers dormitory-style accommodations and access to hiking trails.
  • Datong Hot Spring Resort - A mid-range option that features hot springs, a restaurant, and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Yangmingshan National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Yangmingshan National Park include:

  • Tea and Tea Sets - Taiwan is famous for its tea, and there are several tea shops in the park where you can purchase high-quality tea and tea sets.
  • Handmade Soap - Many of the hot spring resorts in the park make their own handmade soap using natural ingredients.
  • Local Foods and Snacks - There are several food stalls throughout the park that sell local foods and snacks, such as pineapple cake, nougat candy, and dried fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Yangmingshan National Park

The local culture in Yangmingshan National Park is heavily influenced by the indigenous Taiwanese people who have lived in the area for thousands of years. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting the park include:

  • Respect for elders and authority figures - Traditionally, Taiwanese society places a high value on respecting elders and authority figures.
  • Temple Etiquette - When visiting temples in the park, it is customary to remove your shoes before entering and to be respectful of the sacred space.
  • Tipping - Tipping is not customary in Taiwan, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small amount of loose change is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Yangmingshan National Park

The best time to visit Yangmingshan National Park is during the spring (March - May) and fall (September - November) months when the temperatures are comfortable, and the weather is mild. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yangmingshan National Park

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and wear comfortable shoes for hiking or biking.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Respect the park’s natural environment and wildlife.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the park’s flora or fauna.
  • Ignore warning signs or safety guidelines.
  • Disturb or harm the park’s wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yangmingshan National Park

Yangmingshan National Park is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to be aware of some safety tips:

  • Always follow the park’s safety guidelines and warning signs.
  • Be cautious when hiking or biking on steep or uneven terrain.
  • Take precautions against ticks, which can carry Lyme disease.

Health in Yangmingshan National Park

The park is known for its geothermal hot springs, which are believed to have healing properties. However, it’s important to be aware of some health tips:

  • Be cautious when entering hot springs, as the water can be very hot.
  • If you have any health issues or concerns, consult with a doctor before visiting the park.
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself against the sun.

Getting to Yangmingshan National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yangmingshan National Park

The closest airport to Yangmingshan National Park is Taipei Songshan Airport (TSA), which is located about 10 kilometers from the park.

Bus Stations Near Yangmingshan National Park

There are several bus stations near Yangmingshan National Park, including:

  • Yangmingshan Bus Station - Located in the park’s central area, this bus station is a major transportation hub for visitors.
  • Beitou Bus Station - Located in Taipei’s Beitou District, this bus station offers direct routes to the park.

Other Options

  • MRT - The park is accessible by MRT via the Tamsui-Xinyi Line.
  • Taxi - Taxis are widely available throughout Taipei and can take you directly to the park.

How to Get Around in Yangmingshan National Park

The easiest way to get around Yangmingshan National Park is by car or scooter. However, if you prefer to travel by public transportation or on foot, there are several options available, including buses, taxis, and hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Yangmingshan National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after your visit to Yangmingshan National Park include:

  • Beitou Hot Springs - This nearby hot spring town offers several public and private hot spring baths, as well as hot spring resorts and hotels.
  • Tamsui Old Street - Located in Taipei’s Tamsui District, this historic street is known for its food vendors, street performers, and souvenir shops.
  • National Palace Museum - One of the most popular museums in Taipei, this museum features a vast collection of Chinese art and artifacts.

Conclusion

Yangmingshan National Park is a beautiful and diverse nature reserve that offers something for every visitor. Whether you’re interested in exploring the park’s cultural attractions, going on outdoor adventures, or relaxing in hot springs, Yangmingshan is a must-visit destination in Taipei. By following the tips and recommendations in this guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this stunning park.
