Introduction to Jibei

Jibei is a small township located on the eastern coast of Baisha Island, one of Penghu’s 90 islands off the western coast of Taiwan. Known for its serene atmosphere, crystal-clear waters, rustic villages, and breathtaking sunsets, Jibei offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Despite its size, Jibei is home to a variety of historical, cultural, and natural attractions that cater to visitors of all ages and interests. From museums and temples to hiking and biking trails, Jibei is a hidden gem in the heart of Taiwan that awaits discovery.

In this ultimate guide to Jibei, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your trip, including must-visit attractions, outdoor activities, recommended accommodations, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Jibei

Jibei, like most of Taiwan, has a rich cultural heritage that stems from its location and the people who have resided on the island. Understanding the local culture and customs will help you appreciate and respect the people and their ways of life. Some cultural practices to be aware of include:

  1. Greeting with a bow or handshake
  2. Removing shoes before entering a home or temple
  3. Using chopsticks instead of a fork and knife
  4. Refraining from touching or patting someone’s head

Best Time to Visit Jibei

While Jibei can be visited all year round, the best time to visit is from March to May when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the flowers are in full bloom. The fall season from October to December is also a good time to explore Jibei, as the temperature is cooler, and the skies are clearer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jibei

Before embarking on your trip to Jibei, it’s important to know the local do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  1. Wear comfortable clothes and shoes for outdoor activities
  2. Respect the locals’ privacy and personal space
  3. Carry cash, as some establishments do not accept credit cards
  4. Tip service providers in restaurants, bars, and salons

Don’t:

  1. Litter, as Jibei’s environment is fragile
  2. Speak loudly in public places or on the phone
  3. Touch or lean against historical sites or artifacts.
  4. Smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Travelers to Jibei should always consider their safety and health during their trip. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Jibei

  1. Steer away from unmarked or forbidden areas, especially near the water.
  2. Use life jackets while participating in water activities.
  3. Avoid swimming or diving alone.
  4. Always lock up your belongings in the hotel safe.

Health in Jibei

  1. Protect yourself from the sun by using sunscreen and wearing hats and sunglasses.
  2. Stay hydrated and use bottled or boiled water.
  3. Have a first-aid kit on hand for minor injuries.
