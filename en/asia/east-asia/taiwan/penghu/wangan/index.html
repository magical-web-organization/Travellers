Introduction to Wangan

Nestled on a small island in Penghu, Taiwan, Wangan is a hidden gem that boasts breathtaking views, crystal-clear waters, and rich cultural heritage. It is a destination that offers the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, and one that is steeped in history and culture. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you on a tour of Wangan and give you a glimpse of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and cultural customs unique to this beautiful island.

List of Places to Visit in Wangan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wangan Lighthouse
  2. Wangan Old Street
  3. Wangan Cultural Village
  4. Penghu Living Museum
  5. Penghu Four-eyed Well

Wangan Lighthouse

Built in 1864 during the Qing Dynasty, Wangan Lighthouse is one of the oldest lighthouses in Taiwan. Standing at 33 meters tall, it provides breathtaking views of the island and the surrounding sea.

Wangan Old Street

A bustling street filled with food vendors, souvenir shops, and traditional buildings that date back to the Qing Dynasty, Wangan Old Street is a perfect place to immerse yourself in the island’s rich history and culture.

Wangan Cultural Village

Wangan Cultural Village is a museum that showcases the daily lives of Wangan’s inhabitants in the past. You can find displays of traditional cooking equipment, fishing tools, and even traditional wedding clothes.

Penghu Living Museum

Penghu Living Museum is a reconstructed 300-year old building that served as a traditional family home. Visitors can explore the different rooms and experience what it was like to live on the island in the past.

Penghu Four-eyed Well

The Penghu Four-eyed Well is located in the center of Wangan and is considered to be one of the island’s most iconic landmarks. Legend has it that the well has healing properties and is one of the most photographed sites on the island.

Natural Attractions of Wangan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Shanshui Beach
  2. Fenggui Cave
  3. Lintou Park
  4. Yuwengdao Lighthouse
  5. Wangan Miniature Scenic Area

Shanshui Beach

Located on the eastern side of Wangan, Shanshui Beach is a beautiful and peaceful stretch of coastline with crystal-clear waters and pristine sands. It is the perfect place to relax, sunbathe, and swim.

Fenggui Cave

Fenggui Cave is a natural wonder located on the western side of Wangan. Visitors can explore the cave’s various tunnels and caverns that stretch for over 800 meters. Be awed by the stalactites and stalagmites that have been formed over thousands of years.

Lintou Park

Located on the northern tip of the island, Lintou Park is a beautiful hillside park that offers breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding islands. Visitors can hike up to the top of the hill to catch a glimpse of the majestic vista.

Yuwengdao Lighthouse

The Yuwengdao Lighthouse is located on a small island just off the coast of Wangan. Visitors can take a ferry to the island and climb up the lighthouse to enjoy panoramic views of the sea and surrounding landscape.

Wangan Miniature Scenic Area

The Wangan Miniature Scenic Area is a beautifully landscaped garden, complete with miniature replicas of famous landmarks from around the world, such as the Eiffel Tower, Statue of Liberty, and the Colosseum.

Food and Drink Scene of Wangan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Penghu Crab
  2. Peanut Soup with Rice Balls
  3. Fish Soup

Penghu Crab

Penghu Crab is a local delicacy that is found only in Wangan. It is known for its sweet and tender meat that is caught fresh from the surrounding waters.

Peanut Soup with Rice Balls

Peanut Soup with Rice Balls is a beloved dessert that is made with glutinous rice balls and served in a sweet peanut soup. It is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Fish Soup

Fish Soup is a popular dish in Wangan made with fresh fish and a variety of local vegetables. It is a hearty and healthy meal that is enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  1. Penghu Sorghum Liquor
  2. Jasmine Tea

Penghu Sorghum Liquor

Penghu Sorghum Liquor is a potent alcohol made with locally grown sorghum. It has a unique flavour profile that is beloved by locals and is a must-try for any adult visiting Wangan.

Jasmine Tea

Jasmine Tea is a refreshing and fragrant tea that is commonly served in restaurants and cafes across the island. It is made with locally grown jasmine flowers and is a perfect way to cool down on a hot day.

What to Buy in Wangan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Penghu Dried Fish
  2. Shell and Coral Jewelry

Penghu Dried Fish

Penghu Dried Fish is a tasty and unique souvenir that is perfect for anyone who loves seafood. It is made with fresh fish caught from the surrounding waters and is a popular snack enjoyed by locals.

Shell and Coral Jewelry

Shell and Coral Jewelry is a beautiful and unique souvenir that is made with locally sourced materials. It is a perfect way to remember your trip to Wangan and also makes for a great gift.

Wangan Embroidery

Wangan Embroidery is a traditional craft that has been passed down for generations on the island. It is a delicate and intricate art form that is used to make beautiful clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Wangan

Wangan is a destination that is steeped in history and culture. The island has a strong sense of community, and locals take pride in their unique customs and traditions. Here are some cultural practices to keep in mind when visiting Wangan:

  1. Respect for elders is an essential part of the local culture.
  2. Remove shoes before entering someone’s home.
  3. Be mindful not to waste food.

Best Time to Visit Wangan

The best time to visit Wangan is during the spring and autumn months, which fall from March to May and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the island is less crowded than during the peak summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wangan

Do:

  1. Do try the local cuisine and drinks.
  2. Do wear comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.
  3. Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  2. Don’t take pictures without permission.
  3. Don’t disrespect elders or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wangan

Wangan is a relatively safe destination, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should be mindful of safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities and exploring the island’s natural landscapes.

Health in Wangan

Taiwan has a well-developed healthcare system, and there are hospitals and medical clinics located in major cities and towns across the island. It is recommended that visitors purchase medical insurance before traveling to Taiwan to avoid any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Wangan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wangan

The nearest airport to Wangan is the Penghu Airport, which offers several domestic flights to major cities across Taiwan.

Bus Stations Near Wangan

There are bus stations located in major cities and towns across Penghu, with a regular service to Wangan.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from the main island of Taiwan to Penghu and then take a bus or taxi to Wangan.

How to Get Around in Wangan

Wangan is a small island that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who prefer to travel by car.
