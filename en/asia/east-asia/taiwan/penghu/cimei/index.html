Cimei, a small island located in the Penghu archipelago, is a hidden gem of Taiwan’s travel circuit. Although it is less talked-about than the bustling cities, Cimei has a charm of its own, offering a tranquil, peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of modern life. Here is a comprehensive travel guide that will help you plan a trip to Cimei.

Introduction to Cimei

Cimei Island is located in the Penghu archipelago, a group of islands situated to the west of Taiwan. The island has an area of 6.57 square kilometers and a population of approximately 500 people. Cimei is a small, rural island that offers visitors a laid-back and peaceful environment. The island is known for its stunning natural beauty, scenic beaches, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Cimei

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cimei Salt pan: Located on the southern part of Cimei, the salt pan has been in operation for centuries and is an essential part of the island’s history. Visitors can witness the process of salt making or even try their hand at making some for themselves.
  • Cimei Shengtai Temple: This temple is one of the oldest and most historically significant structures on the island, dating back to the Qing Dynasty. It is a popular place to visit for those interested in traditional Chinese architecture and religious practices.
  • Cimei Flying Fish Cultural Museum: The museum showcases the traditional method of capturing flying fish, which is unique to the Penghu Islands. Visitors can learn about the cultural significance of flying fish in Penghu, and even try their hand at catching one of these iconic creatures.

Natural Attractions of Cimei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cimei Beach: The Cimei Coastline is a stunning sight to behold, complete with picturesque beaches, clear blue waters, and captivating rock formations. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk along the coastline, swim in the ocean or relax on the sandy beaches.
  • Chengqing Lake: Located in the center of Cimei Island, the Chengqing Lake is a serene lagoon surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake and explore its hidden caves and underwater world.
  • Li Gang Village: A small village on the edge of Cimei Island is known for its unusual rock formations – “the Dharma’s Pillows”. These large boulders look like giant pillows stacked on top of each other and offer an extraordinary sight.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cimei

  • Pinnacle Ridge Trail: Pinnacle Ridge trail is a scenic hiking trail that leads to the highest point in Cimei – “Xishan Mountain”. The trail provides magnificent views of the surrounding coastline, mountains, and valleys.
  • Cimei Coastal Cycle Route: This 8 km cycle route is an excellent way to explore Cimei’s coastline, with several viewing points and rest areas along the route, making it ideal for a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Cimei: Activities and Tours

  • Flying Fish Catching Experience: The Penghu Islands are famous for the traditional method of capturing flying fish. Visitors can try their hand at catching these elusive creatures, an experience unique to the islands.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: With crystal-clear waters, Cimei Island is an ideal location for water activities like snorkeling and diving. Visitors can enjoy the marine life underwater or explore sunken shipwrecks.
  • Horse Riding: A popular activity on the island, visitors can take a horse-riding tour of the island and enjoy the stunning views of the coastline and countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Cimei

Cuisine in Cimei offers a mix of Taiwanese and seafood dishes that are fresh, flavorful, and delicious. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: As an island, seafood is the specialty of Cimei. Try the fresh seafood at one of the local restaurants or night markets.
  • Nane Pie: A glutinous rice and soybean paste cake, a popular snack among locals and tourists.
  • Tutong Gao: A layered steamed cake made with rice flour and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Sorghum Liquor: This classic Penghu drink is made from fermented sorghum and has a strong, pungent taste.
  • Penghu Beer: This locally crafted beer is made from Penghu spring water and is an excellent choice for a refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Cimei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cimei offers a few options for accommodation, ranging from cozy guesthouses to comfortable hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Cimei Backpacker: A cozy hostel with a friendly host, located in the heart of Cimei.
  • Xin Xia B&B: A comfortable guesthouse located near the beach, with clean and well-maintained rooms.
  • Penghu Cimei Snail House: A unique guesthouse with a relaxing ambiance, known for its delicious seafood cuisine.

What to Buy in Cimei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Penghu Sea Salt: Take home some high-quality sea salt produced on the island, which is famous for its purity and taste.
  • Nane Pie: This tasty snack makes for a great souvenir, available in various packaging sizes.
  • Traditional Taiwanese handicrafts: Visit the local markets to get traditional items like wooden combs, bamboo cups, and handmade pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Cimei

Cimei’s local culture and customs are deeply influenced by its religious beliefs, where folklore and traditions are still prevalent. Visitors should respect these beliefs and customs while on the island.

Best Time to Visit Cimei

The best time to visit Cimei is between September and November when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are minimal. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and windy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cimei

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the island.
  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, particularly the seafood.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Overstay in the national parks or restricted areas.
  • Disturb the ecology or disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cimei

Cimei is a safe destination for travelers, and crime rates are relatively low. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Cimei

The island has a limited number of medical facilities, so visitors should bring adequate medication and consult their doctor before traveling. It is also advisable to carry sufficient bottled water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Cimei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cimei is accessible via air and sea transportation options.

Airports Near Cimei

The nearest airport is Magong Airport located on the main Penghu Island. From there, visitors can take a ferry to Cimei Island.

Bus Stations Near Cimei

There are several bus stations on the main Penghu Island from where visitors can take a bus to the ferry terminal to Cimei Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car on the main Penghu Island to get to the ferry terminal to Cimei Island.

How to Get Around in Cimei

Cimei is a small island, and visitors can get around easily by renting a bicycle, scooter or by walking.

Where to Go Next from Cimei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Magong City: The capital city of the Penghu Islands offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Qimei Island: This nearby island is known for its natural beauty, scenic views, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Cimei Island offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it an unforgettable experience. Whether you want to relax on the beach, explore the coastline, or delve into the island’s rich cultural heritage, Cimei has something to offer for all types of travelers. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan a memorable trip to Cimei and experience its charm for yourself.
