Introduction to South Penghu Marine National Park

South Penghu Marine National Park is a nature lover’s paradise located in Penghu, Taiwan. This national park is home to crystal clear waters, rich marine life, and stunning coastal landscapes. The area is also steeped in history, with several important cultural and historical sites to explore. Visitors to South Penghu Marine National Park can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, snorkeling, and exploring local caves. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything this beautiful area has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in South Penghu Marine National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about local history and culture, be sure to visit some of the following attractions:

  • Penghu Living Museum
  • Penghu Tianhou Temple
  • Penghu Old House
  • Penghu Great Bridge
  • Magong City God Temple

Natural Attractions of South Penghu Marine National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Penghu Marine National Park is teeming with natural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit spots:

  • Qimei Dazhou Island
  • Jibei Island
  • Qimei Banyan Garden
  • Fenggui Cave
  • Whale Cave
  • Penghu Columnar Basalt Reserve

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Penghu Marine National Park

Exploring the park’s stunning landscapes on foot or by bike is a must-do. These are the best hiking and biking routes in South Penghu Marine National Park:

  • Qimei Great Path
  • Beiliao Coastal Path

  • Cimeiyu Eco-Trail
  • Wanchao Hill Path

Best Things to Do in South Penghu Marine National Park: Activities and Tours

South Penghu Marine National Park offers a variety of fun activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in the park:

  • Snorkeling and diving
  • Boat tours

Week-Long Itinerary for South Penghu Marine National Park

If you have a week to spend in South Penghu Marine National Park, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Penghu Living Museum and Old House
  • Day 2: Explore Qimei Dazhou Island and Banyan Garden
  • Day 3: Hike the Beiliao Coastal Path and visit Fenggui Cave
  • Day 4: Join a snorkeling or diving tour
  • Day 5: Visit the Penghu Great Bridge and Whale Cave
  • Day 6: Take a boat tour and go whale watching
  • Day 7: Relax on one of the park’s beautiful beaches

Local Culture and Customs of South Penghu Marine National Park

South Penghu Marine National Park has a rich cultural heritage that visitors should be aware of. Here are some of the park’s customs and traditions:

  • Lion dances during religious festivals
  • Traditional fish netting techniques
  • Local rites and ceremonies
  • Penghu opera

Best Time to Visit South Penghu Marine National Park

The best time to visit South Penghu Marine National Park is between March and October. During this time, you’ll be able to enjoy the warm weather and participate in outdoor activities without worrying about rain. If you’re hoping to see whales, plan your visit between April and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Penghu Marine National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry a reusable water bottle
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities
  • Take only memories and leave only footprints
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Damage coral or disturb marine life
  • Litter or leave trash behind
  • Approach marine animals too closely
  • Touch or disturb historical artifacts
  • Ignore safety warnings

Safety and Health

Safety in South Penghu Marine National Park

South Penghu Marine National Park is generally safe for visitors, but there are a few things you should keep in mind:

  • Be aware of tides when exploring local caves
  • Follow safety instructions during water activities
  • Wear sunscreen and use insect repellent
  • Carry a map and stay on designated trails

Health in South Penghu Marine National Park

Visitors to South Penghu Marine National Park should be aware of the following health considerations:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
  • Be cautious when eating street food
  • Wear appropriate clothing to protect against mosquitoes
  • Seek medical attention if you experience serious symptoms
