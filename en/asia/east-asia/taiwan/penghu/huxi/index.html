Introduction to Huxi

Huxi, located in the Penghu archipelago of Taiwan, is a charming town known for its historical sites, stunning natural landscapes, and unique cultural offerings. The town is known for its traditional architecture, friendly locals, and delicious seafood. Tourists visiting Huxi will have an opportunity to immerse themselves in Taiwanese culture, nature, and history.

List of Places to Visit in Huxi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Huxi Four-Eyed Well

The Huxi Four-eyed Well is a historic site that has been preserved for over a century. The unique structure is named after its four openings, which are used to extract water from the well. The well offers a glimpse into traditional Taiwanese architecture and the importance of water in daily life.

Penghu Old House

Penghu Old House is a historical landmark that dates back to the Ming Dynasty. The house is a popular tourist attraction and is known for its intricate wood carvings, traditional courtyard, and more than 60 bedrooms.

Fenggui Cave

Fenggui Cave is a limestone cave that is over 1,000 years old. Visitors can explore the cave’s unique formations, including stalactites, stalagmites, and underground rivers. The cave is also home to a colony of bats, and visitors can catch a glimpse of these fascinating creatures.

Natural Attractions of Huxi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Huxi Salt Mountain

The Huxi Salt Mountain is a unique natural wonder that is made of salt. The mountain is known for its pink and white hues, which are created by the reflection of sunlight off the salt crystals. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain for stunning panoramic views of the island.

Huxi Fisherman’s Wharf

Huxi Fisherman’s Wharf is a popular spot for tourists to relax and enjoy the sea breeze. The wharf offers picturesque views of the Penghu Islands and is an excellent location for watching the sunset.

Jioguanyu Island

Jioguanyu Island is a small uninhabited island located off the coast of Huxi. Visitors can take a boat to the island and explore its pristine beaches and clear waters. The island is also home to a variety of sea creatures, including dolphins and sea turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huxi

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Huxi. Visitors can explore the town’s natural beauty and cultural landmarks by taking one of the many hiking and biking routes. Some of the popular routes include the Huxi Three Giants Scenic Area, the Huxi Coastal Trail, and the Huxi Salt Mountain Trail.

Best Things to Do in Huxi: Activities and Tours

Penghu Ocean Fireworks Festival

The Penghu Ocean Fireworks Festival is a popular event that takes place every year in Huxi. The festival is known for its spectacular fireworks display and attracts tourists from all over the world.

Seafood Feast

Huxi is known for its fresh seafood, and visitors can indulge in a seafood feast at one of the town’s many seafood restaurants.

Cultural Tours

Visitors can take a cultural tour of Huxi, which includes visits to historical landmarks, museums, and temples. The tours provide visitors with a comprehensive understanding of Huxi’s history and heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Huxi

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Noodles with Seafood

Fried noodles with seafood is a popular dish in Huxi. The dish is made with fresh noodles, a variety of seafood, and vegetables. It is served hot and is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Pineapple Cake

Pineapple cake is a popular dessert in Taiwan, and Huxi is no exception. The cake is made with fresh pineapple and is a delicious treat for those with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

Sorghum Wine

Sorghum wine is a traditional drink in Taiwan, and Huxi is known for its production of high-quality sorghum wine. Visitors can enjoy a glass of sorghum wine at one of the local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Huxi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Penghu Bayview Resort

Penghu Bayview Resort is a luxurious hotel located in Huxi. The hotel offers stunning views of the ocean and is known for its excellent service.

Penghu Youth Activity Center

Penghu Youth Activity Center is a budget-friendly option for travelers. The hostel offers basic amenities and is an excellent option for those traveling on a shoestring budget.

What to Buy in Huxi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salt

Salt is a significant product in Huxi, and visitors can buy high-quality salt to bring home as a souvenir. The salt is available in various forms, including salt crystals and salted fish.

Stone Carvings

Stone carvings are a traditional art form in Huxi. Visitors can buy stone carvings as a unique and authentic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Huxi

Huxi is a culturally rich town, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. It is customary to remove shoes before entering a home or temple, and visitors should dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Huxi

The best time to visit Huxi is from March to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and windy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huxi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • litter or damage natural habitats
  • Be disrespectful to locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Huxi

Huxi is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Tourists should be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Huxi

Huxi offers quality healthcare to tourists, and visitors can seek medical attention at the Penghu Hospital if necessary. It is advisable to bring any necessary medication and to stay hydrated during the hot summer months.

Getting to Huxi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huxi

The closest airport to Huxi is the Magong Airport, which is located approximately 30 minutes away by car. Visitors can take a taxi from the airport to Huxi.

Bus Stations Near Huxi

Visitors can take a bus from Taipei to Huxi. The journey takes approximately 6 hours, and buses run regularly.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Huxi by ferry. Ferries run regularly from the city of Kaohsiung and take approximately 6 hours.

How to Get Around in Huxi

Visitors can rent bicycles, scooters, or cars to get around Huxi. The town is small and easy to navigate, and many of the attractions are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Huxi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Magong

Magong is the largest city in Penghu and is known for its bustling night markets, historical landmarks, and breathtaking beaches. Visitors can take a ferry or a bus to Magong.

Qimei Island

Qimei Island is a small island located off the coast of Penghu. Visitors can take a ferry to Qimei Island and explore its scenic beauty, including its coral reefs and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Huxi, Penghu, is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, nature, and history. This comprehensive travel guide provides tourists with everything they need to plan a memorable trip to Huxi. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, indulging in fresh seafood, or enjoying scenic views, Huxi has something for everyone.
