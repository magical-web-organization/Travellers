Introduction to Nangan

Nangan is a picturesque island in the Matsu archipelago of Taiwan. The island is a popular destination for tourists seeking a mix of cultural significance, stunning views and outdoor adventures. Nangan is home to significant historical and cultural sites, mountainous terrain and scenic vistas. If you are visiting Taiwan, a trip to Nangan is a must-do! In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Nangan.

List of Places to Visit in Nangan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nangan is rich in history and culture. Some of the must-see museums and historical places include:

  1. Matsu Folk Culture Museum: This museum showcases the traditions, ethnic groups, and local customs of the Matsu Islands.
  2. Matsu National Scenic Area Administration: This historical site has been well-preserved and offers a peek into the turbulent history of Matsu.
  3. Matsu Artillery Fort: Built during the Qing Dynasty, this fortress is now a museum and features displays and artifacts related to the military history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Nangan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nangan offers incredible natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Some of the popular natural attractions are:

  1. Guanyinshan Trail: The hike to the Guanyinshan peak offers breathtaking views of Yanshan and Beigan Islands and the nearby mainland of China.
  2. Zhongfu Cliffs: These magnificent cliffs offer a beautiful backdrop to a vibrant shoreline and are accessible by road and boat.
  3. Shitou Cave: Located near the north of the island, this cave system offers a unique subterranean experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nangan

Nangan offers some of the most beautiful hiking/biking routes in Taiwan. Some of the popular trails include:

  1. Camel Mountain Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the surrounding islands and the sea.
  2. Renai Road: This route is suitable for biking and offers scenic views of the coastline.
  3. Liumin Village Trail: A relatively easy hike, this trail offers flat terrain and beautiful views of the ocean.

Best Things to Do in Nangan: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of exciting things to do on this beautiful island. Some of the best activities and tours in Nangan are:

  1. Matsu Island Cultural Festival: Enjoy traditional music, dance, and food at this annual festival held in July.
  2. Dolphin Watching: Take a boat tour to see the playful dolphins swimming in the sea.
  3. Snorkeling and Diving: The warm and clear waters of Nangan are perfect for a fun snorkeling or diving adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nangan

For a perfect week-long itinerary, consider the following:

Day 1:

Arrive in Nangan and check out the Matsu Folk Culture Museum.

Day 2:

Hike up the Guanyinshan Trail.

Day 3:

Tour the Matsu Artillery Fort and then head to the Zhongfu Cliffs for some scenic views.

Day 4:

Explore the Shitou Cave and take a dip in the nearby beach.

Day 5:

Bike down the Renai Road and visit the Tonghou Settlement for some local cuisines.

Day 6:

Catch a boat to the neighboring island of Beigan and explore its unique landscape.

Day 7:

Spend a day relaxing and enjoying the coast before heading back to the mainland.

Food and Drink Scene of Nangan

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Nangan, make sure to try some of the local delicacies.

  1. Oyster Vermicelli: Made from fresh oysters and vermicelli noodles, this dish is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  2. Braised Pork Rice: A popular Taiwanese dish, this meal is served with tender pieces of braised pork and rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Awamori: A traditional Okinawan liquor native to the Ryukyu Islands.
  2. Hua Diao: A type of Chinese wine that is made from fermented glutinous rice.

Where to Stay in Nangan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nangan offers a wide range of accommodations ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular options are:

  1. Nangan Hotel: Located on the main street of the island, this hotel offers spacious and well-decorated rooms.
  2. Gugeng Hotel: This accommodation option is located near the seashore and offers a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Nangan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Nangan, look for these unique souvenirs:

  1. Nangan Mahjong Set: Made from black walnut, this mahjong set features unique patterns and characters.
  2. Dried Seaweed: Harvested in Nangan, this dried seaweed is packed with umami flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Nangan

Nangan is a unique blend of the Taiwanese and Fujianese cultures. Some customs and traditions to be aware of while visiting include:

  1. Leaving shoes outside: It is customary to remove shoes when entering homes, temples, and some public places.
  2. Respect for elders: Elders are highly respected in the local culture and are often addressed using formal titles.

Best Time to Visit Nangan

The best time to visit Nangan is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny. The island can be visited all year round, but typhoon season is from late summer to early fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nangan

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Hike and bike responsibly, and follow the designated trails.
  3. Clean up after yourself and leave no trace.

Don’t:

  1. Swim in prohibited areas.
  2. Litter or damage the local environment.
  3. Bring harmful items or animals to the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nangan

Nangan is a generally safe destination for tourists. However, it is advisable to take the following precautions:

  1. Be aware of any typhoon warnings and stay indoors if necessary.
  2. Avoid swimming in prohibited areas or during times of dangerous weather conditions.
  3. Follow designated trails when hiking and biking.

Health in Nangan

Taiwan has a good healthcare system that provides quality care for its citizens and visitors. Nangan has a hospital and several clinics in case of emergencies.

Getting to Nangan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nangan

The nearest international airport is Taipei’s Taoyuan International Airport. From Taipei, visitors can take a domestic flight to Matsu Beigan Airport, then a ferry to Nangan.

Bus Stations Near Nangan

Taima Bus Terminal is the main transportation hub in Nangan that offers a regular bus service from the mid-Taiwan to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from Keelung port in Taipei to Matsu and then to Nangan.

How to Get Around in Nangan

Nangan is a tiny island, and visitors can walk, bike or scooter from one place to another. Rental car services are available, but driving is not advisable for tourists unfamiliar with the area.

Where to Go Next from Nangan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more, consider visiting the neighboring islands. Some popular excursions include:

  1. Beigan Island: Take a ferry to explore the beautiful scenery and enjoy some delicious seafood cuisine.
  2. Dongyin Island: This peaceful island is famous for its lighthouse and scenic views.

Conclusion

Nangan is a beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural significance. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Nangan has something for everyone to enjoy. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your visit to Nangan.
