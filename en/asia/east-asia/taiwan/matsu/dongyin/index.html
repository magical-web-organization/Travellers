Introduction to Dongyin

Dongyin is a small island located in the northwest of Taiwan, in the Matsu archipelago. It is known for its stunning scenery, historical attractions, and tranquil vibe. Dongyin is a popular destination among nature lovers, outdoor adventurers, and history enthusiasts. Whether you want to hike in the mountains, kayak along the coastline, or explore the ancient tunnels and forts, Dongyin has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Dongyin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dongyin has a rich history and culture, which can be explored through its museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Dongyin include:

  • Cihyou Temple: Cihyou Temple is a Taoist temple dedicated to the god of city walls. It is one of the oldest and most revered temples in Dongyin.
  • Beihai Tunnel: Beihai Tunnel is a series of tunnels and forts built during the Qing Dynasty to protect the island from invaders. The tunnel is over 200 meters long and is open for visitors to explore.
  • Yongliangcheng Fortress: Yongliangcheng Fortress is a historical military fortification built in the Ming Dynasty. It offers spectacular views of the sea and surrounding islands.

Natural Attractions of Dongyin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dongyin is blessed with natural beauty, from rugged mountains to crystal-clear waters. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Dongyin:

  • Beihai Wetland: Beihai Wetland is a natural reserve that covers over 100 hectares of land, including marshes, reed beds, and ponds. It is home to a variety of plant and animal species, including migratory birds.
  • Dongyong Lighthouse: Dongyong Lighthouse is a historic lighthouse located on the top of a hill. From the top of the lighthouse, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the sea and the surrounding islands.
  • Dabaisha Beach: Dabaisha Beach is a secluded beach that offers crystal-clear waters and soft sand. It is a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dongyin

Dongyin has a variety of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore its natural beauty and scenic views. Here are some of the best trails in Dongyin:

  • Cihuyan Trail: Cihuyan Trail is a 3-kilometer hiking trail that starts from Cihou Temple and leads to the top of a hill. From the top, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Beigan Island and the Taiwan Strait.
  • Qinbi Bicycle Path: Qinbi Bicycle Path is a scenic route that connects Qinbi Village to Nangan. The route passes through picturesque fields, villages, and hills, and offers beautiful views of the sea.
  • Tongxinling Trail: Tongxinling Trail is a 2-hour hiking trail that leads to a scenic lookout point. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and sea.

Best Things to Do in Dongyin: Activities and Tours

Dongyin offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to experience the island’s culture and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Dongyin:

  • Kayaking: Kayaking is a popular activity in Dongyin, which allows visitors to explore the island’s coastline and hidden bays.
  • Fishing: Fishing is another popular activity in Dongyin, which offers visitors the chance to catch fresh seafood and experience the local way of life.
  • Island Hopping: Island hopping is a popular tour in Dongyin, which allows visitors to explore the surrounding islands and their unique attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dongyin

If you’re planning to spend a week in Dongyin, here’s a suggested itinerary that covers the island’s highlights:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your accommodation
  • Day 2: Explore Beihai Wetland and Beihai Tunnel
  • Day 3: Hike the Tongxinling Trail and visit Yongliangcheng Fortress
  • Day 4: Visit Cihou Temple and Qinbi Village
  • Day 5: Bike the Qinbi Bicycle Path and visit Dongyong Lighthouse
  • Day 6: Kayak along the coastline and go fishing
  • Day 7: Island hopping tour to nearby islands

Food and Drink Scene of Dongyin

Dongyin is known for its fresh seafood, local delicacies, and traditional drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Dongyin:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Noodle Soup: A hearty soup made with fresh seafood, noodles, and vegetables.
  • Oyster Omelet: A savory omelet made with fresh oysters, eggs, and vegetables.
  • Barbecued Squid: Grilled squid that is seasoned with spices and served with a dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Mijiu: A traditional rice wine that is sweet and mellow.
  • Kaoliang Liquor: A strong liquor made from fermented sorghum.

Where to Stay in Dongyin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dongyin offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dongyin:

  • Dongyin Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Dongyin town. It offers comfortable rooms and a communal kitchen.
  • Dongyin Homestay: A family-run homestay located near Beihai Wetland. It offers clean and spacious rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Dongyin Hotel: A modern hotel located in the center of Dongyin town. It offers comfortable rooms and amenities like a restaurant and a souvenir shop.

What to Buy in Dongyin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dongyin offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for family and friends. Here are some of the best things to buy in Dongyin:

  • Seafood Snacks: Dongyin is known for its fresh seafood snacks, such as dried squid and fish balls.
  • Traditional Crafts: Dongyin’s artisans produce a range of traditional crafts, such as pottery, calligraphy, and wood carving.
  • Local Produce: Dongyin produces a variety of local produce, such as tea, honey, and peanuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Dongyin

Dongyin has a unique culture and customs that reflect its history and way of life. Here are some of the key aspects of Dongyin’s local culture:

  • Taoism: Taoism is the dominant religion in Dongyin, and many of the island’s temples and shrines are dedicated to Taoist deities.
  • Fishing Culture: Fishing is an important part of Dongyin’s culture and economy, and many of the island’s festivals and celebrations are related to fishing.
  • Folk Art: Dongyin has a rich tradition of folk art, including puppetry, opera, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Dongyin

The best time to visit Dongyin is from April to October, when the weather is warm and dry. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and kayaking. The peak season is from July to August, when the island is bustling with tourists and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dongyin

When visiting Dongyin, it’s important to be respectful of the island’s culture and customs. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the Environment: Dongyin’s natural beauty is its biggest attraction, so make sure to take care of the environment by not littering and following the designated trails.
  • Try Local Food: Dongyin is known for its fresh seafood and local delicacies, so indulge in the island’s cuisine.
  • Learn Some Mandarin: While English is spoken in Dongyin, it’s always helpful to learn some Mandarin to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in Public Areas: Smoking is prohibited in most public areas in Dongyin, including parks, beaches, and transportation hubs.
  • Wear Revealing Clothing: Dress modestly when visiting temples and shrines, and avoid wearing revealing clothing in public places.
  • Take Photos Without Permission: It is considered impolite to take photos of people and their homes without their permission. Always ask before taking any pictures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dongyin

Dongyin is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  • Rip Currents: The waters around Dongyin can have strong currents, so make sure to swim in designated areas and follow the lifeguards’ instructions.
  • Hiking and Biking Accidents: The trails in Dongyin can be steep and slippery, so wear appropriate shoes and clothing, and be cautious when hiking or biking.
  • Mosquitoes: Mosquitoes are common in Dongyin, especially during the rainy season, so bring insect repellant and wear long sleeves and pants when outdoors.

Health in Dongyin

Dongyin has a decent healthcare system, with a hospital and several clinics on the island. However, visitors should take the following precautions to stay healthy:

  • Drink Bottled Water: Tap water in Dongyin is not drinkable, so make sure to drink bottled water, which is widely available on the island.
  • Watch What You Eat: While Dongyin’s cuisine is delicious, some visitors may experience stomach problems due to unfamiliar ingredients or spices. Bring antacids or other medication if you have a sensitive stomach.
  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: Dongyin can get very hot and sunny during the summer months, so wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses when outdoors.

Getting to Dongyin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dongyin is located in the Matsu archipelago, which is accessible by plane, ferry, or car. Here are the transportation options and logistics for traveling to Dongyin:

  • Flight: Visitors can fly to Matsu Beigan Airport, which is located on Beigan Island, and then take a ferry to Dongyin. The flight from Taipei to Matsu takes about 50 minutes.
  • Ferry: Visitors can take a ferry from Fuzhou, China, or Keelung, Taiwan, to Matsu, and then take a local ferry to Dongyin. The ferry ride from Fuzhou to Matsu takes about 3 hours, while the ferry ride from Keelung to Matsu takes about 10 hours.
  • Car: Visitors can drive to the port in Keelung and park in the designated parking area before taking the ferry to Matsu.

How to Get Around in Dongyin

Dongyin is a small island and most of its attractions can be reached on foot, but there are also several transportation options available:

  • Bicycle: Visitors can rent bicycles on the island, which is a great way to explore the local villages and scenic routes.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available on the island, but they can be expensive and may not be able to access some of the remote areas.
  • Bus: Public buses operate on Dongyin, connecting the major villages and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Dongyin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dongyin is part of the Matsu archipelago, which has several other islands worth visiting. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Dongyin:

  • Beigan Island: Beigan Island is home to several historical sites and natural attractions, such as Juguang Tower and Tieban Reef.
  • Nangan Island: Nangan Island is the largest island in the Matsu archipelago and offers a range of cultural and natural attractions, such as the Matsu Folk Culture Village and the Zhaishan Tunnel.
  • Wuhan Island: Wuhan Island is a remote island that is home to a variety of wildlife, such as seabirds and sea turtles.
