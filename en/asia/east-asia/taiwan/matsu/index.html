Introduction to Matsu

Matsu is a small archipelago of islands located off the coast of China in the East China Sea. The islands are governed by Taiwan but remain closer to the Chinese mainland than to Taiwan. Matsu receives its name from the patron goddess Mazu, who is believed to protect fishermen and sailors.

Matsu is known for its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and unique culture. The islands were historically a strategic military location, as they stand just a few kilometers from the Chinese coast. Visitors can explore old forts, tunnels, and other military installations that date back to the Cold War. Natural attractions include beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and marine life, and the fascinating culture and friendly locals make Matsu a must-visit destination in Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Matsu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beihai Tunnel - This massive tunnel was originally constructed for military purposes during the Cold War. Visitors can explore the underground tunnels and see the impressive engineering that went into its construction.

  • Mazu Temple - The Mazu Temple is a must-visit attraction in Matsu. Located in Nangan Island, it is dedicated to the goddess Mazu and has been a place of worship for locals and visitors for centuries.

  • Matsu Folk Culture Village - This cultural village showcases the rich history and customs of Matsu through interactive exhibits and traditional performances. Visitors can also try their hand at traditional crafts such as pottery and weaving.

Natural Attractions of Matsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beihai Tunnel Scenic Area - This area is home to beautiful scenic views, offering stunning photography opportunities. A short hike will take visitors to the entrance of the Beihai Tunnel.

  • Dongyong Lighthouse - Located in Dongyin Island, this beautiful lighthouse offers incredible views of the ocean and surrounding islands.

  • Dongyin Beach - This beautiful white sand beach is the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the peaceful atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matsu

Matsu is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The islands offer a variety of scenic trails that are suitable for all levels of hikers and bikers. Some of the best hiking routes in Matsu include:

  • Cisong Scenic Trail - This 5-kilometer trail offers stunning views of the ocean and is known for its unique rock formations.

  • Nangan-Ci Island Trail - This trail takes hikers from Nangan Island to Ci Island, offering beautiful views of the surrounding islands.

  • Juguangyu Trail - This 5.5-kilometer trail takes hikers through a beautiful forest and ends with a stunning view of the ocean.

Best Things to Do in Matsu: Activities and Tours

Matsu offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone. Some of the best things to do in Matsu include:

  • Fishing Tours - Join a local fishing tour and experience the traditional fishing culture of Matsu.

  • Island Hopping Tours - Explore the different islands in Matsu by joining an island hopping tour.

  • Creative Workshops - Join a creative workshop and learn traditional crafts such as pottery and weaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Matsu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Hotpot - Matsu is known for its delicious seafood, and a seafood hotpot is a must-try dish for visitors.

  • Shrimp Rolls - These deep-fried shrimp-filled rolls are a popular snack in Matsu.

  • Dong Ding Oolong Tea - Matsu is also known for its tea, and Dong Ding Oolong tea is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Kanpai! Matsu Kaoliang Liquor - This strong distilled liquor is a must-try for visitors to Matsu.

  • Taiwan Beer - This locally brewed beer is a refreshing choice for visitors to Matsu.

Where to Stay in Matsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matsu offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts, there is something for every traveler. Some of the best places to stay in Matsu include:

  • Yueyang Island Resort - This luxurious resort is located on Yueyang Island and offers stunning views of the ocean.

  • Lodging Lodge - This budget-friendly hostel is located in Nangan Island and is a great choice for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Matsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matsu is famous for its local products, including:

  • Matsu Peanuts - These locally grown peanuts are a great snack to bring back home as a souvenir.

  • Matsu Salt - This high-quality sea salt is a must-try for visitors to Matsu.

Local Culture and Customs of Matsu

Matsu has a rich culture and unique customs that visitors should be aware of. For example, visitors should expect to remove their shoes before entering some buildings, and should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Matsu

The best time to visit Matsu is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the scenery is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matsu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and local products.

  • Be mindful of the environment and dispose of waste properly.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside buildings that require removal of shoes.

  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.

  • Leave garbage or litter behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matsu

Matsu is generally a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should take the usual precautions such as securing their valuables and paying attention to their surroundings.

Health in Matsu

Visitors to Matsu should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and take the necessary precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as wearing insect repellent.

Getting to Matsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matsu

Visitors to Matsu can fly into Taipei or Kinmen airports, which both offer connecting flights to Matsu.

Bus Stations Near Matsu

There are bus stations in both Nangan and Beigan Islands, which offer transportation to different parts of Matsu.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Matsu by ferry from other nearby islands or by private boat.

How to Get Around in Matsu

Visitors can explore Matsu by bike, scooter, or car rental. Some of the main attractions, such as the Beihai Tunnel, are also easily accessible by foot.

Where to Go Next from Matsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Matsu can also explore nearby destinations such as Kinmen Island, which offers a unique blend of Taiwanese and Mainland Chinese culture. Other nearby destinations include Penghu and Taichung.

Conclusion

Matsu is a unique destination that offers a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and a distinct culture. Visitors can explore old forts, tunnels, and other military installations, as well as enjoy outdoor adventures and local traditions. With a range of accommodation options, transportation options, and activities to choose from, Matsu is a must-visit destination in Taiwan.
