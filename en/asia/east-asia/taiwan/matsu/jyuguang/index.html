Introduction to Jyuguang

Jyuguang is a small town located on the eastern coast of the Matsu Islands, Taiwan. Although often overlooked by tourists, Jyuguang boasts a rich history and stunning natural scenery that is worth exploring. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your next trip to Jyuguang, including the top places to visit, activities to do, food and drink, and cultural insights.

List of Places to Visit in Jyuguang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jyuguang Old Street: Stroll along this quaint street and admire the traditional Taiwanese-style architecture.
  • Jyuguang Cihou Temple: This historical temple was built in the late 19th century and is dedicated to the sea goddess Mazu.
  • Jyuguang War and Peace Memorial Park: Commemorates the soldiers who fought in the 1950s Kinmen War, as well as promoting peace between Taiwan and mainland China.
  • Nangan Visitor Center: Learn more about the history and culture of Matsu Islands.

Natural Attractions of Jyuguang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beihai Tunnel: A historical military tunnel that was built during the 1960s and offers an interesting glimpse into the area’s past.
  • Jyuguang Camel Rock: This natural wonder is a must-visit spot in Jyuguang, and its unique rock formations make it perfect for photos.
  • Jyuguang Juguangliao Beach: This remote and peaceful beach offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Jyuguang Fengluoye Natural Reserve Area: One of the most scenic spots in Jyuguang, with a variety of hiking trails and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jyuguang

  • Beihai to Fuao Trail: This 10-kilometer trail offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape.
  • Jyuguang Cihou Temple to Juguangliao Beach Trail: This 4-kilometer trail takes you through forests and along the coast, and offers plenty of photo opportunities.
  • Jyuguang Jieshou Trail: This short but challenging 2-kilometer hiking trail leads to the top of Jieshou Peak and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Jyuguang: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping: Take a ferry to other nearby islands such as Beigan, Dongyin or Xiju to explore their unique culture and landscapes.
  • Fishing: Matsu’s waters are rich in fish, making it a prime destination for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Discover the diverse marine life and coral reefs found around the Matsu Islands.
  • Kayaking: Paddle through the serene waters of Jyuguang and discover the hidden coves and beaches along the coast.
  • Sightseeing: Visit any of the scenic spots in Jyuguang and soak up the picturesque landscapes that Taiwan has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Jyuguang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jyuguang’s Seafood: From lobsters to freshly caught fish, the seafood here is a must-try. One of the most popular dishes is Jyuguang’s Taroko Lobster Noodle Soup.
  • Matsu’s Kaoliang Liquor: This Taiwanese liquor is made from fermented sorghum and is a staple drink in Matsu. A popular choice is the Kaoliang Liquor 58%, which won the gold medal in the World Spirits Competition.
  • Habanero Rice: This traditional Taiwanese dish is made from rice, peanuts, dried shrimp, and a spicy sauce made from dried habanero chili peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Charcoal-Roasted Coffee: Matsu’s coffee is unique in that it is roasted over charcoal, giving it a distinct smoky flavor.
  • Taiwanese Tea: Taiwan is known for producing some of the best tea in the world, and Jyuguang is no exception. Try traditional green tea or go for a more floral-tasting oolong.

Where to Stay in Jyuguang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Matsu B&B: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Jyuguang and is perfect for those looking for a comfortable and convenient stay.
  • Jyuguang Seagull Fashion Hotel: This stylish hotel offers stunning sea views and a range of amenities, including a fitness center and spa.
  • Sea Breeze Homestay: This adorable guesthouse is located just a stone’s throw away from Juguangliao Beach and offers a relaxing and peaceful stay.

What to Buy in Jyuguang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster Products: From dried seafood to luxurious gourmet products such as lobster oil or curry sauce, there are many items available that utilise Jyuguang’s famous lobster.
  • Matsu Products: Matsu is known for producing premium-quality Kaoliang liquor and traditional tea, which is perfect to enjoy at home or to give as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Jyuguang

Jyuguang’s local culture is heavily influenced by its geographical location. As a small island community, the people here are known for their kindness and hospitality. The island also has a long military history, which is evident in the old military tunnels and missile bases that can be found throughout the area.

Best Time to Visit Jyuguang

The best time to visit Jyuguang is from September to November when the weather is comfortable and the crowds are few. During this time, visitors can enjoy the autumn foliage, comfortable temperatures, and have a chance to attend the local festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jyuguang

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local seafood and delicacies.
  • Explore the nature and landscape of Jyuguang.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on the beaches or hiking trails.
  • Enter restricted military areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture or people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jyuguang

Jyuguang is generally safe for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still be cautious, especially when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. It is also important to follow the instructions provided by local authorities in cases of emergency, such as typhoons or earthquakes.

Health in Jyuguang

There are no major health concerns in Jyuguang. However, visitors should take precautions against the sun, bring mosquito repellent, and drink bottled water to prevent waterborne illnesses.

Getting to Jyuguang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jyuguang

The nearest airport to Jyuguang is the Matsu Nangan Airport, which receives daily flights from Taipei, Taichung or Taipei Songshan Airport.

Bus Stations Near Jyuguang

Jyuguang can be reached by bus from the cities of Taipei, Taoyuan or Taichung via Xiamen or Kinmen County.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Jyuguang by taking a ferry from any of the nearby islands, including Dongyin, Beigan, and Xiju.

How to Get Around in Jyuguang

The best way to explore Jyuguang is on foot, especially around the Old Street and scenic areas. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to travel around the island easily.

Where to Go Next from Jyuguang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beigan Island: Known for its gorgeous white sand beaches, traditional Fujianese-style architecture, and fresh seafood.
  • Dongyin Island: An island packed with natural beauty, including stunning rock formations and crystal-clear waters that are perfect for diving and snorkeling.
  • Kinmen Island: Another island group with a rich history and a variety of attractions and activities for visitors.

Conclusion

Jyuguang is a hidden gem in Taiwan that is definitely worth a visit. Whether you are interested in immersing yourself in the area’s culture, exploring natural wonders, or simply relaxing on a tranquil beach, there is something for everyone in Jyuguang. With this travel guide, you are all set to plan your next adventure to this breathtaking island paradise.
