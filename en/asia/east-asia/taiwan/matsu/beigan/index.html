Your Comprehensive Beigan Travel Guide: Best Places to Visit, What to Do, and More

Located in Matsu, Taiwan, Beigan is a hidden gem that offers travelers an array of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking to embark on an epic hike, marvel at historical landmarks or simply savor traditional Taiwanese dishes, Beigan has something to offer everyone.

In this comprehensive Beigan travel guide, we’ll help you plan your itinerary with our top picks of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, local food and drinks, accommodations and more.

Introduction to Beigan

Beigan is one of the inhabited islands that make up the Matsu Islands, located in the Taiwan Strait around 22 miles off the coast of Fujian Province in China. The island is known for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, and traditional way of life. Unlike many other popular destinations in Taiwan, Beigan remains relatively untouched by mass tourism, making it a unique destination for travelers seeking off-the-beaten-path experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Beigan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Beihai Tunnel: Also known as the “North Sea Tunnel,” it is a well-preserved underground military facility that was built by the military during the Cold War era. Visitors can tour the tunnel and gain an insight into the island’s military history.

  2. Jiming Temple: Built in the 18th century, Jiming Temple is a popular landmark in Beigan, famous for its intricate carvings and vibrant colors. It is said that the temple was built by local residents who were seeking divine protection against pirates.

  3. Wuqiu Lighthouse: Standing tall on the highest point of Beigan Island, Wuqiu Lighthouse has been in operation since 1871, and is one of the oldest lighthouses in Taiwan. Visitors can admire the panoramic views of the island from the top of the lighthouse.

Natural Attractions of Beigan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Donglin Lake: A stunning emerald green lake surrounded by imposing mountains, Donglin Lake is a popular spot for hiking, fishing, and picnics. Hikers can follow the trail around the lake and admire the views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Qinbi Village: A traditional village that is home to ancient stone houses, narrow alleys, and stunning views of the ocean. Visitors can wander around the village and learn about the area’s historical legacy.

  3. Zhongyang Mountain: The highest peak on Beigan Island, Zhongyang Mountain offers breathtaking panoramic views of the island and surrounding sea. Hiking to the top of the mountain is a popular activity among outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beigan

  1. Donglin Lake Hiking Trail: This hiking trail is a 2.5-kilometer loop around Donglin Lake, and takes around 45 minutes to complete. The trail offers stunning views of the lake, running streams, and majestic mountains.

  2. Zhongyang Mountain Hiking Trail: The Zhongyang Mountain Hiking Trail is a challenging hike that takes around 2.5 hours to complete. The trail offers stunning views of the Beigan Island and its surrounding sea.

  3. Beigan Island Biking Route: This 21-kilometer bike ride around Beigan Island offers stunning views of the island’s coastline, traditional villages, and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Beigan: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing Tour: Join a local fisherman on a fishing tour and experience the traditional way of life in Beigan. You’ll get to enjoy the sunset views while waiting for fish to bite, and maybe even cook and eat your catch of the day.

  2. Snorkeling and Diving: Beigan Island is home to an abundant marine life and a variety of diving spots for snorkelers and divers. The crystal-clear waters offer great visibility and a chance to glimpse sea turtles, fish, and other sea creatures.

  3. Beach Day at Tungyin Beach: Spend a relaxing day on the stunning Tungyin Beach, located on the southeastern coast of Beigan. The white sandy beach offers panoramic views of the sea and surrounding mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beigan

Day 1: Visit Beihai Tunnel, Jiming Temple, and Wuqiu Lighthouse

Day 2: Hike to the top of Zhongyang Mountain and enjoy the panoramic views

Day 3: Take a fishing tour and learn about the traditional way of life in Beigan

Day 4: Explore Qinbi Village and Donglin Lake

Day 5: Go snorkeling or diving, and explore the rich marine life of the area

Day 6: Enjoy a beach day at Tungyin Beach and relax in the sun

Day 7: Rent a bike and explore the island’s stunning coastline

Food and Drink Scene of Beigan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Seafood Soup: Made with fresh fish, shrimp, and other seafood, this soup is a local delicacy.

  2. Hu Jiao Bing: A traditional baked pastry that is stuffed with ground pork and black pepper.

  3. Pork Jerky: A popular snack made with dried and salted pork.

Local Beverages

  1. Liuzhou Wine: Made with glutinous rice, this sweet and fragrant wine is a popular drink among locals.

  2. Juice made from Beigan apples: Beigan Island is known for its sweet and crispy apples, which are often used to make refreshing juices.

Where to Stay in Beigan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Beigan Jianfeng Eco Park: A charming eco-park that offers accommodation in traditional Taiwanese-style wooden cabins. Each cabin is surrounded by lush greenery, and some rooms even have sea views.

  2. Fu Hua Hostel: A cozy guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

  3. Beigan Backpacker Hostel: A popular hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms, as well as a common area where guests can unwind and socialize.

What to Buy in Beigan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Beigan Apples: Sweet and crispy apples that are unique to Beigan Island.

  2. Beigan Peanut Candy: A popular snack made with peanuts and caramelized sugar.

  3. Hand-painted Postcards: Beautifully hand-painted postcards that feature local landmarks and natural scenery.

Local Culture and Customs of Beigan

  1. Lion Dragon Dance: A traditional dance that is performed during many of the island’s festivals and celebrations.

  2. Matsu Religion: The island is known for its deep spiritual connection to the Matsu goddess, and many local residents practice the Matsu religion.

Best Time to Visit Beigan

The best time to visit Beigan is during the spring and autumn months (March to May and September to November), when the temperature is mild and there is less rainfall. Summer months (June to August) can be hot and humid, while winter months (December to February) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beigan

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions, including the Matsu religion.

  2. Try the local cuisine, especially seafood and traditional Taiwanese snacks.

  3. Explore the island’s natural beauty and hidden gems.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave garbage on the streets or scenic spots.

  2. Damage or vandalize historical landmarks or natural attractions.

  3. Disturb the peace and quiet of the island’s traditional villages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beigan

Beigan is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to take basic safety precautions like locking your doors at night, not leaving your valuables unattended, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Beigan

The tap water is safe to drink in Beigan, but it’s always a good idea to bring a reusable water bottle and fill up at public fountains. Mosquitoes can be a nuisance during the summer months, so bring insect repellent.

Getting to Beigan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beigan

The nearest airport to Beigan is the Matsu Nangan Airport, which is served by flights from Taipei.

Bus Stations Near Beigan

Ferry services from the Matsu Islands to Taiwan are available from the Port of Nangan in Nangan Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Beigan Island by taking a ferry from Matsu Island.

How to Get Around in Beigan

The easiest way to get around Beigan Island is by renting a scooter or bicycle. Taxis are also available, but may be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Beigan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Nangan Island: The largest of the Matsu Islands, Nangan is home to historical landmarks, natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Visitors can reach Nangan by ferry from Beigan Island.

  2. Taiwan Main Island: Visitors can take a ferry from the Matsu Islands to Keelung, a vibrant port city in northern Taiwan. From there, visitors can explore Taipei and other popular destinations in Taiwan.

Conclusion

Beigan Island is a hidden gem that offers travelers an unique, off-the-beaten-path experience. From cultural landmarks and outdoor adventures to delicious local cuisine and traditional festivals, Beigan has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the best that Beigan Island has to offer!
