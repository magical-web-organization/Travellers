Introduction to Pingtung

Located in the southern part of Taiwan, Pingtung is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. The city is known for its natural beauty, delicious food, and rich cultural heritage. Nestled between the mountains and the sea, Pingtung offers a unique mix of outdoor activities and urban attractions. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Pingtung has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Pingtung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of Marine Biology and Aquarium

Located in Checheng Township, the National Museum of Marine Biology and Aquarium is one of the largest aquariums in Asia. The museum houses over 10,000 marine animals from around the world, including sharks, dolphins, and sea turtles. Visitors can also see a variety of exhibits on marine life and conservation.

Chihkan Tower

Chihkan Tower is a historic site located in the heart of Tainan City. Built by the Dutch in 1653, the tower served as a center for political and military activities during the Qing Dynasty. Today, the tower is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of Tainan’s rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Pingtung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kenting National Park

Kenting National Park is a popular destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park covers an area of 37,000 hectares and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy hiking, snorkeling, and surfing in the park, as well as explore scenic areas like the Eluanbi Lighthouse and the White Sand Bay.

Dapeng Bay National Scenic Area

Dapeng Bay National Scenic Area is a picturesque bay located in Donggang Township. The area is known for its stunning views of the sea and the mountains, as well as its diverse marine life. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities in the area, such as boating, fishing, and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pingtung

Maolin National Scenic Area

Maolin National Scenic Area is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts. The area is home to a variety of hiking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging climbs. Visitors can hike through lush forests, past mountain lakes, and along scenic ridges.

Pingtung Bike Trail

The Pingtung Bike Trail is a 23-kilometer trail that runs from Nanpien to Fangliao. The trail takes riders through picturesque coastal towns, past rice paddies, and along scenic roads. Along the way, visitors can stop at local cafes and seafood restaurants to enjoy Pingtung’s famous cuisine.

Best Things to Do in Pingtung: Activities and Tours

Pingtung Tea Tour

Pingtung is famous for its high-quality tea, and visitors can take a tea tour to learn about the region’s tea culture. The tour takes visitors to local tea farms, where they can see how tea is grown and processed. Visitors can also enjoy a tea tasting and purchase tea to bring home.

Night Market Tour

Pingtung is home to several night markets, which offer a variety of local food and crafts. Visitors can take a night market tour to sample regional delicacies like oyster omelets, grilled squid, and shaved ice. They can also browse stalls selling souvenirs and handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Pingtung

Must-Try Dishes

Donggang Fisherman’s Wharf

Donggang Fisherman’s Wharf is a popular destination for seafood lovers. Visitors can enjoy a variety of fresh and delicious seafood dishes, such as grilled squid, steamed fish, and crab congee.

Oyster Omelet

Pingtung is famous for its oyster omelets, a local delicacy made with fresh oysters and eggs. Visitors can try this delicious dish at local night markets and seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

Kaoliang Liquor

Kaoliang liquor is a traditional Taiwanese liquor made from sorghum. The liquor is known for its strong aroma and high alcohol content. Visitors can try a variety of Kaoliang liquor brands at local bars and restaurants.

Bubble Tea

Bubble tea is a popular Taiwanese drink made with tea, milk, and tapioca balls. Visitors can try a variety of bubble tea flavors at local tea shops and cafes.

Local Culture and Customs of Pingtung

Pingtung has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its complex history and diverse population. Some important cultural customs and practices include:

  • Traditional temples and festivals
  • Classical music and dance performances
  • Local agriculture and fishing practices

Best Time to Visit Pingtung

The best time to visit Pingtung is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers in Pingtung can be hot and humid, while winters can be rainy and cool.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pingtung

Pingtung is generally a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take common sense precautions to avoid crime and accidents.

Health in Pingtung

The tap water in Pingtung is generally safe to drink. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses and other common travel health risks.

Getting to Pingtung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pingtung

The nearest airport to Pingtung is Kaohsiung International Airport, which is located about 40 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or train to Pingtung.

Bus Stations Near Pingtung

Pingtung has several bus stations, including the Pingtung Bus Station, which serves most of the city and surrounding areas. Visitors can also take buses from Kaohsiung and other nearby cities to Pingtung.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Pingtung by train, which provides easy access to the city from Kaohsiung and other major cities in Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Pingtung

Pingtung has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses and trains that connect the city to other parts of Taiwan. Visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Pingtung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations and excursions near Pingtung include:

  • Tainan City: Explore the historic city of Tainan, which is known for its temples and traditional architecture.
  • Kenting National Park: Continue your outdoor adventure by exploring the scenic beauty of Kenting National Park.
  • Meinong: Visit the quaint town of Meinong, famous for its traditional paper umbrellas and Hakka culture.

Conclusion

Pingtung is a hidden gem in Southern Taiwan, with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a foodie, a history buff, or an outdoor enthusiast, Pingtung has something for everyone. Plan your trip to this charming city today and discover all that it has to offer.
