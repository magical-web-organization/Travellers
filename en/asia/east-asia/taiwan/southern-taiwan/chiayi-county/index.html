Introduction to Chiayi County

Located in the southern part of Taiwan, Chiayi County is known for its scenic views, historic sites, and outdoor adventures. The county covers an area of 1,903 square kilometers and is home to over 500,000 people. Chiayi City, the county seat, is a bustling metropolis with a mix of traditional and modern architecture. In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, see, and experience in Chiayi County.

List of Places to Visit in Chiayi County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chiayi County has a rich cultural heritage that spans centuries. Some of the most popular attractions in the county are museums and historical sites that showcase Taiwan’s history and culture. Here are three must-visit locations:

  1. Southern Branch of the National Palace Museum: This museum is located in Taibao City and features exhibits on Chinese art, history, and culture. Some of the most impressive displays are the ancient ceramics, jades, and calligraphy.

  2. Alishan Forest Railway: Built in 1912, this railway was once used to transport timber out of the Alishan National Scenic Area. Today, it’s a popular tourist attraction and a great way to explore the area’s stunning scenery.

  3. Hinoki Village: This former logging village has been transformed into a cultural and creative hub. Visitors can explore the unique architecture, watch traditional Taiwanese performances, and shop for souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Chiayi County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chiayi County is home to several natural attractions that offer breathtaking views and outdoor adventures. Here are three places you shouldn’t miss:

  1. Alishan National Scenic Area: This area is a must-visit for its stunning mountainous landscapes, beautiful sunrise views, and scenic hiking trails.

  2. Yushan National Park: Covering an area of over 100,000 hectares, Yushan National Park is home to Taiwan’s highest peak, Mount Jade. Visitors can hike, camp, and explore the park’s lush forests and stunning waterfalls.

  3. Tsou Ma Lai Farm: This eco-tourism farm offers visitors a chance to get up close with farm animals and learn about sustainable agriculture. The farm’s beautiful surroundings also make it a great place for hiking and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chiayi County

Chiayi County has several hiking and biking routes that are suitable for all levels of fitness. Here are three popular routes:

  1. Zhuqi Bikeway: This 4.4-kilometer bike path follows the route of an old railway track and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  2. Alishan National Scenic Area: This area has several hiking trails, including the popular Zhaoping Trail. The trail takes about three hours and offers stunning views of the sunrise and sunset.

  3. Xinyi Township: This township is known for its scenic hiking trails, including the Xiongfei Trail, which takes about two hours to complete and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Chiayi County: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the natural attractions of Chiayi County, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy. Here are three:

  1. Chiayi City Night Market: This bustling night market is a foodie paradise. Visitors can try local specialties like chicken rice, oyster omelet, and bubble tea.

  2. Tea farm tour: Chiayi County is known for its tea plantations, and visitors can take a tour of a tea farm to learn about the tea-making process.

  3. Hot air balloon ride: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic landscapes of Chiayi County for a unique perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chiayi County

Day 1: Explore Chiayi City, including the Wenhua Road Shopping Area and the Chiayi Historical Museum.

Day 2-3: Explore the Alishan National Scenic Area, including hiking the Zhaoping Trail and watching the sunrise from Alishan Mountain.

Day 4: Visit the Southern Branch of the National Palace Museum in Taibao City.

Day 5-6: Explore the Yushan National Park, including hiking the Baiyang Trail and camping in the park.

Day 7: Take a tea farm tour and end the trip with a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Chiayi County

Must-Try Dishes

Chiayi County is known for its unique blend of Taiwanese and Hakka cuisines. Here are three dishes you shouldn’t miss:

  1. Turkey Rice: This dish is a local specialty that consists of rice topped with shredded turkey and gravy.

  2. Aiyu Jelly: This refreshing dessert is made with a type of jelly made from the seeds of a fig-like fruit.

  3. Iron Egg: This snack is made by repeatedly steaming and marinating eggs until they turn black and chewy.

Local Beverages

There are several local beverages that visitors to Chiayi County should try:

  1. Bubble tea: This popular Taiwanese drink originated in Taichung City and can be found all over Chiayi County.

  2. Shui Xian Tea: This oolong tea is a specialty of Chiayi County and is known for its bright color and floral aroma.

  3. Kaoliang Liquor: This strong liquor is made from sorghum and is a staple at local celebrations and festivals.

Where to Stay in Chiayi County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chiayi County has several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Chiayi City and the surrounding areas. Here are three recommended options:

  1. Hotel Day Plus: This hotel is located in the heart of Chiayi City and offers modern amenities and excellent service.

  2. Alishan Shermuh Hotel: This hotel is located near the entrance to the Alishan National Scenic Area and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  3. Tea Cloud B&B: This bed and breakfast is located in Meishan Township and offers a unique tea-themed experience for its guests.

What to Buy in Chiayi County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Chiayi County can bring home several unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  1. Yushan tea: This high-quality tea is grown in the mountains of Chiayi County and is a popular souvenir.

  2. Pineapple cakes: These sweet and delicious cakes are made with pineapple jam and are a popular Taiwanese snack.

  3. Handcrafted pottery: Chiayi County is known for its pottery, and visitors can find handcrafted teapots, bowls, and other ceramics at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Chiayi County

Chiayi County has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its festivals and traditions. Here are three customs and traditions to be aware of:

  1. Fireworks: Fireworks are an important part of many festivals and celebrations in Chiayi County.

  2. Hakka culture: Chiayi County has a significant Hakka population, and visitors can learn about Hakka customs and traditions at museums and cultural centers.

  3. Lantern Festival: This festival takes place every year in February and is celebrated across Chiayi County with lantern displays and parades.

Best Time to Visit Chiayi County

The best time to visit Chiayi County is in the fall months of September to November when the weather is cooler and the autumn foliage is at its peak. The spring months of March to May are also a great time to visit when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chiayi County

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  3. Take part in the festivals and celebrations.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural attractions.
  2. Disrespect religious sites or symbols.
  3. Speak loudly or create disturbances in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chiayi County

Chiayi County is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping their belongings safe and avoiding unlit or isolated areas at night.

Health in Chiayi County

Visitors to Chiayi County should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling. They should also take precautions to avoid mosquito bites to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Chiayi County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chiayi County

The nearest airport to Chiayi County is the Chiayi Airport, which is served by domestic flights from Taipei and Kaohsiung.

Bus Stations Near Chiayi County

There are several bus stations in Chiayi City that connect to other destinations in Taiwan, including Taipei, Kaohsiung, and Tainan.

Other Options

Visitors to Chiayi County can also take the high-speed railway, which connects to major cities in Taiwan such as Taipei, Kaohsiung, and Taichung.

How to Get Around in Chiayi County

The best way to get around Chiayi County is by rental car or motorcycle. There are also buses and taxis available, but they may not be as convenient for exploring the more remote areas of the county.

Where to Go Next from Chiayi County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Chiayi County can easily travel to nearby destinations such as Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Kenting National Park for further adventures and exploration.

Conclusion

Chiayi County is a hidden gem in southern Taiwan, offering stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique culinary experiences. By following this guide, travelers can plan a memorable trip to this beautiful part of Taiwan.
