Introduction to Dounan

Dounan is a rural township located in Yunlin County, Southern Taiwan. It is situated just 15 kilometers away from Douliu City, the county seat of Yunlin. Dounan is surrounded by rolling hills and beautiful landscapes, making it an ideal spot for travelers who are seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views. The town is also rich in culture, with museums and historical sites that reflect its past. Moreover, Dounan boasts of delicious local foods and warm hospitality, which are sure to delight any tourist.

List of Places to Visit in Dounan

Whether you’re an adventurer, history buff, or foodie, Dounan has something for you. Here is a list of places you don’t want to miss during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dounan Ceramics Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in ceramics. It showcases a wide range of exhibits, from traditional Chinese ceramics to contemporary pottery.
  • Mingde Reservoir is a historical reservoir that was constructed during the Japanese colonial period. It is surrounded by scenic views and boasts of a tranquil atmosphere.
  • Tou Tian Kong Shrine is a cultural landmark of the town. The shrine is located on top of a hill, offering a panoramic view of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Dounan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yuntan Waterfall is a hidden gem located in the hills of Dounan. The trail leading to the falls is an easy hike and offers breathtaking views of the mountains and forests.
  • Baozang Temple is a revered temple set amidst a tranquil forest. The temple is surrounded by lush greenery and boasts of stunning views of the mountains.
  • Shuiquanhujia Botanical Garden is a beautiful garden filled with exotic plants, flowers, and trees. It is a perfect spot for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dounan

Dounan is a paradise for avid hikers and bikers. Here are some popular routes:

  • Dounan Zijin Mountain Trail: This 8-kilometer trail takes you to the top of Zijin Mountain, the highest peak in Dounan. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Dounan Sanceng Trail: This 4.3-kilometer trail takes you through lush forest and ends at Baozang Temple.
  • Dounan Hsingfengku Trail: This 5-kilometer trail is a relatively easy hike that takes you through small streams and bamboo forests.

Best Things to Do in Dounan: Activities and Tours

  • Ceramics Workshop: Take a pottery class at Dounan Ceramics Museum and learn about the traditional techniques of making ceramics that have been passed down for centuries.
  • Fishing Day Trip: Head to Mingde Reservoir for a day of fishing with locals. Get a chance to catch some of the fresh water fish that are only found in this area.
  • Tea Tasting: Visit a tea plantation and taste some of the finest tea that Dounan has to offer. Get a chance to learn about the tea-making process and tea culture in Taiwan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dounan

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long trip to Dounan:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Dounan Ceramics Museum and take a pottery class.
Day 2 Go on a hiking trip to Dounan Zijin Mountain Trail.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Mingde Reservoir for fishing and sightseeing.
Day 4 Visit Tou Tian Kong Shrine and Shuiquanhujia Botanical Garden.
Day 5 Take a biking trip to Dounan Hsingfengku Trail.
Day 6 Explore Baozang Temple and go on a tea-tasting tour.
Day 7 Take a rest day and enjoy local foods and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Dounan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Milkfish Congee - a local delicacy made of rice porridge and milkfish.
  • Tofu Pudding - a sweet dessert made of soft tofu, red beans, and syrup.
  • Beef Noodle Soup - a hearty noodle soup with beef broth and tender meat.

Local Beverages

  • Oolong Tea - Dounan is a famous producer of oolong tea, which is a staple drink of the town.
  • Luo Han Guo Tea - a sweet and refreshing tea made of the fruit of the monk fruit plant.
  • Shaoxing Wine - a sweet rice wine that pairs well with local dishes.

Where to Stay in Dounan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dounan Villa - a cozy and affordable guesthouse situated in the heart of Dounan.
  • Luo Xing Yuan Homestay - a traditional Taiwanese-style homestay that offers a unique cultural experience.
  • Ace Villa Hotel - a luxurious hotel with top-notch amenities and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Dounan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics - Dounan is famous for its ceramic products, such as teapots, tea cups, and vases.
  • Oolong Tea - Bring home some of the finest oolong tea that Dounan has to offer.
  • Local Snacks - Don’t forget to try some of the local snacks, such as dried mango, pineapple cake, and milkfish jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Dounan

  • Hospitality - The locals of Dounan are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature.
  • Religion - The town is predominantly Buddhist and Taoist, with many temples and shrines throughout the area.
  • Ceramics Culture - Dounan has a rich ceramics culture that dates back to the Qing Dynasty. It is considered as an important part of the town’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Dounan

The best time to visit Dounan is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and comfortable. During these seasons, you can enjoy outdoor activities without worrying about the heat or cold. The summer season can be quite hot and humid, while the winter season can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dounan

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local foods and drinks.
  • Take part in cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting temples or shrines.
  • Display disrespect towards the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dounan

Dounan is a safe place to travel, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is still important to take precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and valuables.

Health in Dounan

Dounan has adequate healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. It is recommended to have travel insurance and consult with a doctor before visiting. Additionally, remember to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Dounan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dounan is easily accessible from Douliu City, which connects to other major cities in Taiwan. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Dounan

  • Taichung Airport (68 km away)
  • Kaohsiung Airport (89 km away)
  • Taipei Airport (165 km away)

Bus Stations Near Dounan

  • Douliu Bus Station (15 km away)
  • Chiayi Bus Station (44 km away)
  • Taichung Bus Station (61 km away)

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Douliu City and can take you directly to Dounan.
  • Rental Car: Renting a car is another option for getting to Dounan. However, be aware that the roads can be narrow and winding in some areas.

How to Get Around in Dounan

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around in Dounan, as the town is small and easy to navigate. Additionally, taxis and rental cars are available for longer distance trips or if you have mobility issues.

Where to Go Next from Dounan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Alishan National Forest Recreation Area (67 km away) - a scenic mountainous area with hiking trails and tea plantations.
  • Chiayi City (40 km away) - a bustling city with a rich history and cultural attractions, such as the Wenhua Road Night Market.
  • Gukeng Green Tunnel (21 km away) - a beautiful green tunnel formed by old bamboo trees.

Conclusion

Dounan is a hidden gem in Southern Taiwan, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality. Experience the town’s unique ceramics culture, breathtaking landscapes, and delicious local foods and drinks. Don’t miss out on the opportunity to explore this beautiful corner of Taiwan.
