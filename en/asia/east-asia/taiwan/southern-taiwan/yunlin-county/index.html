Introduction to Yunlin County

Yunlin is a scenic county in southern Taiwan that offers plenty of natural and cultural attractions. Located in the central-western part of the island, Yunlin is a small county that is often overshadowed by more popular destinations in Taiwan like Taipei, Taichung, and Kaohsiung. But despite its small size, Yunlin has a lot to offer visitors, from stunning mountain scenery to historic temples and museums.

Whether you’re interested in hiking in the mountains, trying delicious Taiwanese cuisine, or learning about local history and culture, Yunlin has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight some of the top things to do and see in Yunlin County, Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Yunlin County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yunlin is home to several museums and cultural attractions that showcase the region’s history and traditions. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Yunlin Story House: This historic mansion was built in 1920 and is now a museum that showcases the history of the local sugar industry. Visitors can learn about the production of sugar and see exhibits on sugar-related artifacts and tools.
  • Puzi City God Temple: This ornate temple is dedicated to the City God, who is believed to be the protector of the town. Visitors can explore the temple’s beautiful architecture and learn about the tradition of worshipping the City God.
  • Beigang Chaotian Temple: This impressive temple was built in the Ming dynasty and is an important place of worship for the Chinese sea goddess Mazu. Visitors can see the intricate wood carvings and other artistic details that make the temple so impressive.
  • Douliu Cultural Center: This modern museum showcases the art and culture of Yunlin and Taiwan in general. Visitors can see exhibitions of traditional Taiwanese art, contemporary art, and multimedia installations.

Natural Attractions of Yunlin County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its cultural attractions, Yunlin also has plenty of natural beauty to explore. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in the county:

  • National Yunlin University of Science and Technology: This is not only a university campus, but also a beautiful park that is open to the public. The campus boasts lush greenery, stunning architecture, and plenty of open spaces for relaxing.
  • Dongshi Forest Garden: This nature reserve is a favorite destination for hikers and bird watchers. Visitors can trek through the forest and spot exotic birds, butterflies, and other wildlife along the way.
  • Jhuoshuei River: This scenic river is a popular spot for kayaking and rafting. Visitors can rent equipment and go on guided tours to experience the river’s rapids and stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yunlin County

If you’re interested in hiking or biking, Yunlin has plenty of options for you to explore. Here are some great routes to check out:

  • Douliu River Riverside Bike Path: This 10-kilometer bike path follows the Douliu River and offers stunning views of the surrounding scenery. It’s a great option for beginners or families with children.
  • Taizi Mountain Trail: This intermediate-level hiking trail takes visitors to the top of Taizi Mountain, which offers breathtaking views of the Yunlin countryside. It’s a challenging hike, but definitely worth the effort.
  • Sihu Great Lake Trail: This 30-kilometer bike path circles Sihu Lake and offers scenic views of the lake and the surrounding mountains. It’s a great way to explore the area if you’re looking for a longer ride.

Best Things to Do in Yunlin County: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Yunlin that go beyond just sightseeing, there are plenty of tours and activities available as well. Here are some of the best:

  • Yunlin Cycling Tour: This guided bike tour takes visitors through the countryside, passing through rice paddies, traditional villages, and scenic mountains. It’s a great way to experience the local culture and scenery.
  • Yunlin Food Tour: This tour takes visitors on a culinary adventure through Yunlin, showcasing the region’s delicious traditional cuisine. Sample local delicacies like steamed buns, bamboo shoot soup, and candied fruit.
  • Yunlin Scenic Flight: If you’re looking for a unique way to see the county, consider taking a scenic flight. You’ll get an aerial view of the stunning mountain and river scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yunlin County

If you have a week to explore Yunlin, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Puzi City God Temple, Yunlin Story House, and Douliu Cultural Center.
  • Day 2: Hike Taizi Mountain Trail, visit Beigang Chaotian Temple, and explore the mountain scenery.
  • Day 3: Visit National Yunlin University of Science and Technology, Dongshi Forest Garden, and Jhuoshuei River.
  • Day 4: Bike Douliu River Riverside Bike Path and Sihu Great Lake Trail.
  • Day 5: Join a Yunlin Food Tour and try some of the region’s best local cuisine.
  • Day 6: Take a Yunlin Cycling Tour and explore the countryside.
  • Day 7: Take a scenic flight and see the county from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Yunlin County

Must-Try Dishes

Yunlin is famous for its delicious traditional cuisine. Here are some dishes you shouldn’t miss:

  • Salted Duck: This dish features pieces of duck that are marinated in a mixture of soy sauce and spices, then salted and dried. It’s a popular snack in Yunlin and is often served with rice wine.
  • Taro Balls: This sweet dessert is made from taro root, which is boiled and mashed to make a dough, then formed into balls and boiled again. The balls are then served in a sweet syrup or with shaved ice.
  • Rice Cake: This savory snack is made from glutinous rice that is steamed and then mashed into a dough. It’s usually stuffed with pork or other fillings and then grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

Yunlin is also known for its local beverages. Here are some of the best:

  • Douliu Rice Wine: This traditional rice wine is made from glutinous rice and is one of the most popular alcoholic beverages in Yunlin. It’s typically served with salted duck or other snacks.
  • Green Bamboo Shoot Juice: This refreshing drink is made from fresh bamboo shoots that are blended with sugar and water. It’s a popular summer drink in Yunlin and is said to have several health benefits.
  • Hakka Tea: This tea is a blend of black tea and tea leaves that is brewed with orange peel, licorice, and other herbs. It’s a popular drink in Yunlin and can be found in many local tea shops.

Where to Stay in Yunlin County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yunlin has plenty of options for accommodation, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget hostel. Here are some top picks:

  • Yu Hua Hotel: This modern hotel is centrally located in Douliu and offers spacious rooms with all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.
  • Chinatrust Hotel: This hotel is located near Beigang Chaotian Temple and has a lovely rooftop garden where you can relax and enjoy the view.
  • Evergreen Palace Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the countryside and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It’s a great option if you’re looking for a quiet and relaxing stay.
  • Tomato Hostel: This budget hostel is located in Taixi Township and offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Yunlin County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home from Yunlin, here are some local products to consider:

  • Bamboo Shoots: Yunlin is famous for its fresh and delicious bamboo shoots, which are often used in local cuisine. You can buy them fresh or canned to take home.
  • Fruit Tea: There are many local tea shops in Yunlin that sell delicious fruit-infused teas. Consider buying some to take back home or as gifts for friends and family.
  • Handmade Crafts: Yunlin has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans creating beautiful pottery, ceramics, and other handmade items that make great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Yunlin County

Yunlin has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions that are still practiced today. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Burning Incense: Visitors to temples in Yunlin may observe locals burning incense as an act of offering and worship. It’s customary to light the incense and hold it with both hands while bowing.
  • Respecting Elders: In Taiwanese culture, it’s important to show respect to elders. When encountering an older person, it’s customary to address them formally and use respectful language.
  • Eating with Chopsticks: In Taiwan, chopsticks are the primary eating utensil. Visitors should be familiar with how to use chopsticks and be respectful when eating in public settings.

Best Time to Visit Yunlin County

The best time to visit Yunlin is between November and March when the weather is cool and dry. The summer months (June to August) can be hot and humid, while typhoons are common in the fall (September to October). Spring (April to May) is also a good time to visit, but expect occasional rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yunlin County

Do:

  • Learn basic Mandarin phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and tea.

Don’t:

  • Show up at a temple or other place of worship in shorts or revealing clothing.
  • Leave trash behind in public areas.
  • Speak loudly or behave disrespectfully in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yunlin County

Yunlin is generally a safe place, with low rates of violent crime. However, visitors should still take precautions to stay safe:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Use caution when crossing the busy streets.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Yunlin County

Overall, Taiwan has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors should have no trouble finding adequate medical care in case of an emergency. However, visitors should still take precautions to stay healthy:

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during the hot summer months.
  • Be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses and take precautions to avoid bites.

Getting to Yunlin County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yunlin County

The nearest airport to Yunlin is Taichung International Airport, which is located about 66 kilometers to the north. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Yunlin.

Bus Stations Near Yunlin County

There are several bus stations in Yunlin County, including the Douliu Bus Station, the Puzi Bus Station, and the Huwei Bus Station. Visitors can take a bus from these stations to various destinations around the county.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, you can take a train from Taipei or Kaohsiung to various locations in Yunlin. There are also rental car options available for those who prefer to drive.

How to Get Around in Yunlin County

Public transportation is a great option for getting around Yunlin. Buses and trains run regularly throughout the county and are affordable and convenient. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to travel independently.

Where to Go Next from Yunlin County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Taiwan, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Chiayi: This nearby city is known for its beautiful temples, scenic parks, and delicious street food.
  • Alishan National Scenic Area: This stunning mountain area is a famous destination for its scenic views, hiking trails, and beautiful sunrises.
  • Sun Moon Lake: This picturesque lake is surrounded by beautiful mountains and is a popular destination for boating, hiking, and sightseeing.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or delicious cuisine, Yunlin County has something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, Yunlin is a hidden gem that is definitely worth exploring. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and discover all that Yunlin has to offer.
