Introduction to Tainan

Tainan is a historically significant city, being Taiwan’s ancient capital until Taipei took over that position in the late 19th century. As Taiwan’s oldest city, Tainan has preserved its legacy through the array of impressive structures and temples dotted in its old town. Tainan’s well-preserved historical centre, which was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2008, make it a must-visit destination in Taiwan.

This comprehensive Tainan travel guide will help you plan a visit to this landmark city, allowing you to explore the cultural, natural, and exciting landmarks. You’ll get to learn about the best things to do, attractions to visit, where to stay, food to eat, the local customs, and so much more.

List of Places to Visit in Tainan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tainan city is famed for its cultural attractions, which preserve the city’s unique history while offering visitors a glimpse into the past. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • National Museum of Taiwan Literature
  • Chimei Museum
  • Confucian Temple
  • Anping Fort
  • Koxinga Shrine

Natural Attractions of Tainan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, Tainan provides a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts to hike and bike trails, take scenic walks, and take beaches. Some of the top natural attractions to check out include:

  • Sicao Green Tunnel
  • Taijiang National Park
  • Anping Tree House
  • Wushantou Reservoir

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tainan

Tainan’s beauty is best explored through its various hiking and biking routes that offer the most breathtaking views of natural and cultural landmarks. Some of the hiking/biking routes to check out include:

  • Siraya National Scenic Area
  • Tainan Canal / Garden Night Market area
  • Dadong Mountain Trail
  • Cigu Lagoon

Best Things to Do in Tainan: Activities and Tours

Tainan has something for everyone, from family-friendly activities to romantic destinations. Some of the best things to do in Tainan include:

  • Tainan historic walking tour
  • Visit to Tainan night markets
  • Try the famous Tainan street food
  • Explore Tainan Creative Park
  • Visit the oldest Taoist temple, Kuan Kung Temple

Week-Long Itinerary for Tainan

Day 1-2: Explore the Tainan Cultural Centre, Anping Fort, Anping Tree House, and the famous Garden Night Market.

Day 3-4: Discover the city’s historic sightseeing places like Fort Provintia, Confucius Temple, the oldest Taoist temple Kuan Kung Temple, and the National Museum of Taiwan Literature.

Day 5: Explore the Dahu Trail, which covers Dahu Ecological Park, Dahu Guangxing Paper Factory, and Dahu Mausoleum Museum.

Day 6: Discover the local parks such as Tainan Park and Taipei National University of Arts, the Guan Zi Ling Hot Spring area, and watch the indigenous dancing displays.

Day 7: Visit the Dadong Mountain Recreational Park, Nakashi art village, and enjoy traditional Taiwanese food in local eateries.

Local Culture and Customs of Tainan

Tainan is a city steeped in tradition and history. Some customs and cultural practices to keep in mind during your visit include:

  • Do not step on the door sill when entering a room.
  • Remove your shoes when entering homes and temples.
  • Avoid pointing with your finger as it is considered impolite in Taiwan.

Best Time to Visit Tainan

The best time to visit Tainan is between February to April and October to November when the climate is cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tainan

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local delicacies
  • Explore the city’s history

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public
  • Do not touch the gods or religious statues in temples
  • Do not make loud noises in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tainan

Tainan is a safe city for travelers, with low levels of crime. However, it is essential to keep your valuables safe when exploring crowded areas.

Health in Tainan

Tap water in Tainan is safe to drink, and healthcare facilities are available in case of emergencies. Visitors should carry mosquito repellent during mosquito season (May to October) to avoid mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Tainan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tainan

Tainan is served by the Tainan Airport, which is approximately 6 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tainan

The Tainan Bus Station is the main hub for local and regional bus routes.

Other Options

Tainan also has a reliable train system that connects to other major cities in Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Tainan

Tainan has a well-established public transit system that includes buses and trains. Alternatively, you can rent bicycles or stroller bikes to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Tainan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that one can visit after exploring Tainan, including:

  • Kaohsiung
  • The hot springs of Guanziling, Baihe and Guguan
  • Kenting National Park

Conclusion

Tainan is a jewel that should be at the top of your travel list. With a rich history dating back to the 17th century, the city offers a glimpse into the past with its impressive temples, museums, and historical monuments. From hiking and biking to trying out delicious local dishes, Tainan provides a perfect mix of outdoor and indoor activities, no matter your interests. Plan your trip today, and experience this must-visit destination for yourself.
