Introduction to Kenting National Park

Nestled in the southernmost part of Taiwan, Kenting National Park is a nature lover’s paradise. Spread across 33,268 hectares, the park comprises rugged coastline, verdant forests, and awe-inspiring landscapes. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, with more than 1,200 species of plants, 300 species of birds, and 150 species of butterflies.

Local Culture and Customs of Kenting National Park

Kenting is home to Taiwan’s aboriginal groups, who have preserved their unique culture and way of life for centuries. Visitors can immerse themselves in traditional rituals and ceremonies, explore ancient villages, and learn about local arts and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Kenting National Park

The best time to visit Kenting National Park is from October to March, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Avoid visiting during the summer months (June to August), when the temperatures can soar above 90°F and crowds can be overwhelming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kenting National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Stay on designated hiking trails
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen when swimming
  • Keep the park clean by carrying out your trash

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb the wildlife
  • Feed the animals
  • Take plants or coral from the park
  • Use single-use plastics

Safety and Health

Safety in Kenting National Park

Kenting National Park is generally safe for travelers, with low crime rates and few hazards. However, visitors should be aware of the potential dangers of swimming in the ocean, especially during the monsoon season (May to September).

Health in Kenting National Park

Kenting National Park is located in a subtropical climate zone, so visitors should be prepared for hot and humid weather. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, and protect yourself from the sun with a hat and sunscreen.

How to Get Around in Kenting National Park

The best way to get around Kenting National Park is by renting a scooter or bicycle. This allows visitors to explore the park at their own pace and avoid traffic congestion. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to drive.
