Introduction to Kaohsiung

Kaohsiung is a beautiful, natural city located in Southern Taiwan. Known as the Harbor Capital, it is the largest port in Taiwan and plays an important role in the country’s economy. Kaohsiung is a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike, offering plenty of cultural, natural, and historical attractions. With its warm climate, beautiful landscapes, and a wide range of attractions, Kaohsiung should be high on any traveler’s itinerary when visiting Taiwan.

List of Places to Visit in Kaohsiung

Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit in Kaohsiung:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Museum of Taiwan History: This museum showcases the history of Taiwan, including its indigenous cultures, Chinese and Japanese colonial periods, and contemporary Taiwan.
  • Kaohsiung Museum of Fine Arts: This art museum showcases the works of local and international artists.
  • The British Consulate at Takao: Built-in 1865 and culturally important, the British consulate demonstrates the colonial history of the city. Others include the Takao Railway Museum, the Takao Old Street, and the Holy Rosary Cathedral.
  • The Koxinga Shrine: This shrine honors the memory of Koxinga, a legendary general who drove out the Dutch from Taiwan.

Natural Attractions of Kaohsiung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shoushan (Monkey Mountain): A prominent hiking spot in the city, an opportunity to watch monkeys, historical sites, divine wind temple, and a great view of the port.
  • Lotus Pond: This scenic water body, surrounded by different temples and adorned with temples and pavilions, is a unique place to visit in Kaohsiung. The best known include the Dragon Tiger Tower and the Confucius Temple.
  • Maolin Scenic Area: A remote scenic destination, the area offers spectacular waterfalls, authentic aboriginal villages, and the world-renowned Purple Butterfly Valley.
  • Cijin Island: Located just off the Kaohsiung coast, Cijin Island is a great place to go swimming, go for walks or eat fresh seafood by the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaohsiung

  • Love River: A network of walking and biking routes around the riverbanks and town, and fantastic nighttime light shows can be seen here.
  • The Central Park: The park is home to several gardens, a lake, and several jogging and biking paths.

Best Things to Do in Kaohsiung: Activities and Tours

  • Kaohsiung Bike Tour: An exciting way to explore the city, and you can either take self-guided or guided trail programs.
  • Fo Guang Shan Tour: A fantastic tour experience to learn about Buddhism, watch incredible performances, religious artifacts and learn about the religion at the largest Buddha image in the world.
  • Jet Skiing: You may engage in some water sports across the nearby waters bodies or enjoy a peaceful ride around the marine sanctuary.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kaohsiung

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Kaohsiung:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Koxinga Shrine, Shoushan Hiking Trail, and Fengyi Academy
2 Explore Kaohsiung’s museums including the National Museum of Taiwan History, Takao Railway Museum, and the Kaohsiung Museum of Fine Arts
3 Visit Lotus Pond, the Dragon Tiger Tower, JingZaiJiao Tile-Paved Salt Fields and Pier-2 Art Center
4 Take a day trip to Cijin Island
5 Visit Maolin National Scenic Area, the Purple Butterfly Valley, and the Meinong district
6 Explore Fo Guang Shan, the largest Buddhist temple complex in Taiwan
7 Finish the week with a relaxing day at the beach or enjoying a bike ride through the Central Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaohsiung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lao Shu Fen: A local delicacy is a noodle soup made of sticky vermicelli paired with savory sauce and deep-fried fish
  • Oyster Omelette: A dish made using flour, eggs, vegetables with a topping of meat or seafood.
  • Bawan: A clan of starchy dumpling, filled with savory meat or peanuts, greens, and marinated radish.
  • Pineapple Cakes: A famous dessert dried pineapple fruit wrapped with crumbly crust pastry.

Local Beverages

  • Bubble Tea: One of Taiwan’s most popular beverages, bubble tea is made with tea, sugar syrup and is shaken with ice and milk. Tapioca pearls are optional and served around the world under different brands.
  • Taiwanese Beer: There are many local breweries in Taiwan, so hit up a restaurant or bar and try the local beers.

Kaohsiung locals’ culture, language, and behavior somehow resemble that of nearby residents, but you might encounter strange cultures, practices, and beliefs at the occasional. Taipei and other cities, most religious festivals are celebrated or followed poorly or observe the Way, especially to worship the certain Gods.

Best Time to Visit Kaohsiung

Kaohsiung enjoys a subtropical climate, which means it is generally warm and humid throughout the year, with occasional typhoons in late summer and early autumn. The best time to visit Kaohsiung is between November and January, when the weather is cooler and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaohsiung

Do:

  • Note and Speak basic chinese: Fewer people in Kaohsiung speak or understand English, so try to learn some basic Chinese, such as greetings, ordering food or understanding directions.
  • Dress appropriately: Dress appropriately when visiting religious places or formal events, ensuring the knees and shoulders should be covered.
  • Always carry a photocopy of your Passport: This records your visa page, and the entry stamp is required for identification purposes.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public: There is a hefty fine for smoking in public or designated zones.
  • Never step on Offering Items: Taiwan’s culture forbids this practice.
  • Do not place chopsticks upright: This act is associated with the incense sticks which have been used at funerals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaohsiung

Kaohsiung city has improved security over the last decades, and travelers can expect peace and safety. Petty theft in crowded areas or crowded public transport is common, and you should always be vigilant of valuables.

Health in Kaohsiung

Tap water in Kaohsiung is not drinkable, so only drink purified or bottled water when in the city. Mosquitos remain common pests in Kaohsiung, so bring an insect repellent and frequently apply it to the exposed skin areas. Always wear face masks or possibly carry a portable air purifier during winter months to prevent inhaling the air’s polluted dust.

Getting to Kaohsiung: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are various modes of transportation available to reach Kaohsiung:

Airports Near Kaohsiung

  • Kaohsiung International Airport: This airport offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kaohsiung

  • Kaohsiung Main Station: This station offers bus services to various destinations in Taiwan.

Other Options

  • Kaohsiung High-Speed Rail Station: A great transport option if you’re coming from Taipei or other parts of Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Kaohsiung

Public transportation is affordable and accessible for visitors, including buses, metro or subway, and taxis. There are also bike rental shops and the local on-demand taxi service for your convenience.

Where to Go Next from Kaohsiung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taipei: The capital city of Taiwan and the country’s heart
  • Tainan: An old and charming city near Kaohsiung with interconnected temples, traditional architecture and more history to learn about.
  • Kenting: An exciting trip to the southernmost national park, beaches, fresh seafood, and water activities.

Conclusion

Kaohsiung is not just the largest port city in Taiwan it offers stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and scrumptious local cuisine. The people are friendly, and the city is clean and safe, ensuring a comfortable and enjoyable visit. There are plenty of activities, outdoor adventures, and scenic landscapes to be explored in Kaohsiung, making it a must-visit destination for tourists in Taiwan.
