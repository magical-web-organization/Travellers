A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Haikou

Haikou is a city located in the province of Hainan, in South China. It is a popular tourist destination due to its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant city life. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan a perfect trip to Haikou.

List of Places to Visit in Haikou

Haikou offers a range of cultural and natural attractions for travelers to explore. Here is a list of must-visit places in Haikou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hainan Provincial Museum: This museum showcases the cultural heritage of Hainan with a collection of ancient artifacts, traditional handicrafts, and interactive exhibits.
  • Haikou Old Town: This historic area offers a glimpse into the traditional way of life in Haikou with its ancient buildings and narrow streets.
  • Wugong Temple: One of the oldest temples in Hainan, Wugong Temple is dedicated to the Five Officials of China’s Tang and Song Dynasties.

Natural Attractions of Haikou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yanoda Rainforest Cultural Tourism Zone: A lush rainforest park with hiking trails, waterfalls, and ziplining adventures.
  • Dongzhai Port Natural Reserve: A wetland ecological park with over 140 species of birds to watch and other wildlife.
  • Qiongshan Lingwei Mountain: A range of mountains with beautiful hiking trails offering stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haikou

Haikou has several great hiking and biking routes with beautiful views of the city and its natural surroundings. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Hainan Tropical Rainforest National Park: A park with hiking trails through pristine rainforest and waterfalls.
  • Qiongshan Lingwei Mountain: Hiking routes through the mountains with stunning views of the city.
  • Haidian Island Coastal Road: A scenic bike ride along the coastline with beautiful beaches.

Best Things to Do in Haikou: Activities and Tours

Haikou offers several activities and tours for travelers to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Haikou:

  • Learn about Hainan’s traditional culture with a visit to the Hainan Provincial Museum
  • Explore Haikou Old Town and its ancient buildings.
  • Visit Dongzhai Port Natural Reserve to observe the wildlife and wetland environment.
  • Take a ziplining adventure in Yanoda Rainforest Cultural Tourism Zone

Week-Long Itinerary for Haikou

Here is a week-long itinerary for Haikou:

Day 1 Arrival in Haikou, check into hotel and explore the city.
Day 2 Visit Hainan Provincial Museum and Haikou Old Town
Day 3 Explore Yanoda Rainforest Cultural Tourism Zone and take a zipline tour
Day 4 Visit Dongzhai Port Natural Reserve
Day 5 Hike Qiongshan Lingwei Mountain
Day 6 Take a scenic bike ride along Haidian Island Coastal Road
Day 7 Depart from Haikou

Food and Drink Scene of Haikou

Haikou offers a range of delicious local cuisine and beverages for travelers to experience. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Haikou:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hainanese chicken rice: A popular dish made of fragrant rice cooked in chicken broth with tender slices of chicken.
  • Hele crab: A local specialty of Haikou, the Hele crab is known for its fresh, tender meat and sweet flavor.
  • Wenchang chicken: Another local chicken-based dish, Wenchang chicken is known for its tender meat and fragrant, flavorful broth.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water: As Hainan is known for its coconuts, coconut water is a popular beverage in Haikou.
  • Hainan coffee: Made from locally grown coffee beans, Hainan coffee has a unique flavor that is worth trying.

Where to Stay in Haikou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haikou has a range of options for travelers to choose from when it comes to accommodations. Here are some recommended options:

  • Shangri-La Hotel: A luxury hotel located downtown with a range of amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and pool.
  • Haikou Banana Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located near Haikou Old Town.
  • Hilton Haikou: A top-rated hotel located in the heart of Haikou with a range of amenities, including a fitness center and pool.

What to Buy in Haikou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Haikou has several unique local products that are worth bringing home as souvenirs. Here are some popular options:

  • Coconut products: As Hainan is known for its coconuts, there are several coconut-based products to choose from, including coconut oil, coconut candy, and coconut milk soap.
  • Shell carvings: Haikou is known for its beautiful shell carvings, which can be found in several gift shops in the city.
  • Wenchang chicken: As we mentioned earlier, Wenchang chicken is a local specialty of Haikou and can be found in several markets throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Haikou

Haikou has a rich cultural heritage with several unique customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Haikou:

  • As in most of China, it is important to greet people with a friendly “Ni hao” or “hello” when meeting them.
  • When visiting temples or other religious sites, make sure to dress modestly and remove your shoes.
  • Eating with chopsticks is the norm in Haikou, so it’s worth practicing your skills before your trip.

Best Time to Visit Haikou

The best time to visit Haikou is from November to April when the weather is mild and there is less rainfall. However, be prepared for peak tourist season during Chinese New Year and the National Day holiday in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haikou

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Haikou:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, especially Wenchang chicken and coconut water.
  • Explore the natural attractions of Haikou, including Yanoda Rainforest Cultural Tourism Zone and Qiongshan Lingwei Mountain.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, including dressing modestly when visiting temples.

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove any artifacts in museums or historical sites.
  • Bargain excessively in markets or shops.

Safety and Health

Here is some important information about safety and health in Haikou:

Safety in Haikou

Haikou is generally a safe city, but it is important to take precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas or on public transportation.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean, as there may be strong currents.

Health in Haikou

It is a good idea to consult your doctor before traveling to Haikou to ensure that you are up-to-date on your vaccinations. Here are some additional health tips:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated in the hot, humid climate.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to prevent sunburn.
  • Be cautious when eating street food to avoid food-borne illnesses.

Getting to Haikou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Haikou is accessible by several transportation options, making it easy for travelers to reach the city.

Airports Near Haikou

  • Haikou Meilan International Airport is the main airport serving Haikou, with flights from several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Haikou

  • Haikou East Bus Station is the main bus station serving Haikou, with connections to several cities in Hainan and other parts of China.

Other Options

  • Ferries and boats can also be used to reach Haikou from other cities in Hainan and Taiwan.

How to Get Around in Haikou

Haikou has several transportation options for getting around the city, including:

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Haikou and are a convenient way to travel around the city.
  • Buses: Haikou has a network of public buses that reach most areas of the city.
  • Bikes: Bike rentals are available throughout Haikou, making it possible to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Haikou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Haikou is located in the province of Hainan, which offers several other destinations and excursions worth exploring. Here are some nearby cities and attractions to consider:

  • Sanya: A popular beach destination in Hainan with stunning views and clear water.
  • Wuzhishan: A mountainous area in Hainan with hiking trails and beautiful scenery.
  • Boao: A small town known for its serene atmosphere, beaches, and waterways.

Conclusion

Haikou is a city rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in South China. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope that you have all the information you need to plan a perfect trip to Haikou.
