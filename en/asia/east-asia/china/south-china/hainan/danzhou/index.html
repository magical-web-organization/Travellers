Introduction to Danzhou

Danzhou is a small city located in the northwest of Hainan, China’s tropical island province. It is known for its rich culture, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious local cuisine. Unlike other popular destinations in Hainan, such as Sanya and Haikou, Danzhou remains largely undiscovered by tourists, making it an excellent choice for those looking for an authentic cultural experience.

In addition to its cultural attractions, Danzhou is home to several national parks and nature reserves, providing plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures such as hiking and cycling.

List of Places to Visit in Danzhou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Danzhou Museum
    This museum showcases the history and culture of Danzhou and the Hainanese people. The exhibits include artifacts, photos, and documents that date back to ancient times.

  • Dahe Village
    Dahe Village is a historical site that dates back over 1,300 years. The site features traditional Hainanese architecture, as well as ancient wells, wells, and houses.

  • Xuwenjian’s Former Residence
    This residence was once the home of Xuwenjian, a famed scholar and educator from Danzhou. The building has been preserved and turned into a museum, showcasing the life and work of the renowned scholar.

Natural Attractions of Danzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fengmu Ridge National Forest Park
    This is one of the most popular national parks in Danzhou, offering visitors stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The park’s trails are suitable for hiking, cycling, and bird-watching.

  • Goulou Scenic Area
    Goulou Scenic Area is a natural reserve with an abundance of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the park’s trails, enjoy the stunning views of the Danxia landform, or relax by the crystal-clear river.

  • Hainan Tropical Wildlife Park and Botanical Garden
    Spanning over 1,200 acres, Hainan Tropical Wildlife Park and Botanical Garden is home to over 4,000 animals from 200 different species. Visitors can admire exotic animals such as tigers, pandas, and giraffes, as well as tropical plants and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Danzhou

  • Fengmu Ridge National Forest Park Hiking Trail
    This scenic trail takes hikers through the park’s dense forest, past stunning waterfalls and breathtaking viewpoints. The trail is suitable for experienced hikers.

  • Goulou Scenic Area Hiking Trail
    This trail is relatively flat, making it an excellent choice for novice hikers. As you walk along the trail, you will be treated to panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Xinglong Park Biking Route
    This bike route takes visitors through the city’s historic sites and parks, providing them with a unique perspective of the local culture and lifestyle.

Best Things to Do in Danzhou: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Local Cuisine
    Danzhou is known for its delicious local cuisine, which includes seafood, fruits, and vegetables. Visitors can participate in cooking classes, food tours, and taste some of the island’s freshest and most flavorful dishes.

  • Visit the Local Markets
    Danzhou’s local markets offer visitors a glimpse of daily life on the island. The markets are a hive of activity, with vendors selling everything from fresh produce to handcrafted souvenirs and artwork.

  • Participate in Local Festivals
    Danzhou is known for its vibrant festivals, which include the Spring Festival, the Dragon Boat Festival, and the Mid-Autumn Festival. These festivals provide visitors with a unique opportunity to experience local culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Danzhou

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood
    Danzhou is located on the coast, making it the perfect place to try fresh seafood dishes such as steamed fish, fried shrimp, and lobster.

  • Hele Crab
    Hele crab is a local specialty that can be eaten raw or steamed. The dish is known for its sweet and succulent flavor.

  • Mangosteen
    Mangosteen is a tropical fruit that grows abundantly on the island. It is known for its juicy flesh and sweet taste.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Juice
    Hainan is famous for its coconut trees, and visitors to Danzhou can try fresh coconut juice, which is known for its refreshing taste and numerous health benefits.

  • Shanlan Wine
    Shanlan wine is a local rice wine that has been produced in Danzhou for over 2,000 years. The wine is famous for its fragrant aroma and smooth taste.

Where to Stay in Danzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Danzhou Yijia Family Inn
    This inn offers comfortable and affordable accommodation for travelers. The rooms are clean and spacious, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

  • Hainan Tielu Hostel
    This hostel is located in the heart of Danzhou and offers travelers a comfortable and convenient place to stay. The hostel features private and dormitory-style rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and lounge area.

  • Haishu Luting Guesthouse
    This guesthouse is located in the scenic Goulou Scenic Area and offers visitors a peaceful and relaxing place to stay. The rooms are traditional Hainanese-style buildings that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

What to Buy in Danzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hainanese Brocade
    Hainanese brocade is a traditional fabric that is woven by hand. The fabric is known for its intricate designs and vivid colors.

  • Hele Crab
    Visitors can bring home live or packaged Hele crab as a tasty and unique souvenir.

  • Hainanese Coffee
    Hainanese coffee is grown in the mountains of Hainan and is known for its rich and full-bodied flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Danzhou

Danzhou has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, architecture, and cuisine. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions.

  • Hainanese Opera
    Hainanese opera is a traditional form of Chinese opera that is unique to Hainan. Visitors can attend performances and learn more about this fascinating art form.

  • Hainanese Architecture
    Hainanese architecture is characterized by its intricate carvings, bold colors, and unique blend of Chinese and Southeast Asian influences.

  • Hainanese Beliefs
    The people of Hainan have deep-rooted beliefs in feng shui and ancestral worship. Visitors can learn more about these beliefs by visiting local temples and shrines.

Best Time to Visit Danzhou

The best time to visit Danzhou is from October to April when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can be hot and humid, making it difficult to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Danzhou

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Learn about local culture and history

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage fragile marine ecosystems
  • Take photographs of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Danzhou

Danzhou is a safe city for visitors, but it is recommended that visitors take general safety precautions such as avoiding dark and unlit areas at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Danzhou

Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent food poisoning. It is recommended that visitors take out travel insurance before visiting Danzhou.

Getting to Danzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Danzhou

  • Haikou Meilan International Airport
    Haikou Meilan International Airport is the nearest airport to Danzhou and is located approximately 100 kilometers away.

  • Sanya Phoenix International Airport
    Sanya Phoenix International Airport is a major airport in Hainan and is located approximately 200 kilometers away from Danzhou.

Bus Stations Near Danzhou

  • Danzhou Bus Station
    Danzhou Bus Station is the main bus station in the city, providing routes to and from major destinations on the island.

  • Haikou Bus Station
    Haikou Bus Station is located in the center of Haikou and offers routes to and from Danzhou.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Haikou or Sanya to Danzhou, or take a train to the nearby city of Qionghai and take a bus from there.

How to Get Around in Danzhou

Visitors can get around Danzhou by taxi, bus, or bicycle. Taxis are readily available and inexpensive, while buses are a more affordable option for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Danzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Qionghai
    Qionghai is located approximately 40 kilometers away from Danzhou and is known for its hot springs and scenic parks.

  • Sanya
    Sanya is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Hainan and is known for its beaches, tropical weather, and luxury resorts.

  • Haikou
    Haikou is the capital of Hainan and is known for its historic sites and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Danzhou is a hidden gem of South China, offering visitors a unique and immersive cultural experience. From its museums and historical sites to its natural attractions and delicious local cuisine, Danzhou is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the authentic side of Hainan.
