Introduction to Paracel Islands

Paracel Islands is a group of islands and reefs located in the South China Sea, near Hainan Province in mainland China. The islands are under the jurisdiction of China and are known for their pristine beaches, clear waters, and rich marine biodiversity.

Traveling to Paracel Islands requires some planning, as there are limited transportation options and accommodations available on the islands. However, for those who are looking for a secluded island getaway with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, Paracel Islands are a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Paracel Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paracel Islands have a rich history, and there are several museums and historical sites on the islands that offer a glimpse into the region’s past. Some of the notable attractions include:

  • Paracel Islands Museum: This museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of the islands, with exhibits on traditional fishing methods, local handicrafts, and marine life.

  • Woody Island Library: This library houses a collection of historical and cultural materials about the island’s early inhabitants and life on the islands.

Natural Attractions of Paracel Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Paracel Islands are known for their stunning natural beauty and offer opportunities for outdoor adventures such as snorkeling, diving, hiking, and biking. Some of the natural attractions to visit on the islands include:

  • Duncan Island: This is one of the most popular islands among tourists for its sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning panoramic view.

  • Rocky Island: This island boasts a breathtaking view of the South China Sea and is known for its spectacular coral reefs and varied marine life ideal for diving and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paracel Islands

The islands are relatively small, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Woody Island Trails: This is a network of trails that connect different areas of Woody Island, offering a chance to explore the island’s flora and fauna.

  • Rocky Island Trails: The trails on Rocky Island offer a chance to explore the island’s rugged terrain and enjoy panoramic views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Paracel Islands: Activities and Tours

Paracel Islands offer a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the top experiences include:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The islands boast a rich marine biodiversity, with a plethora of colorful fish, coral reefs, and other sea creatures. Snorkeling and diving are some of the popular activities on the islands.

  • Fishing: Fishing is an essential part of the island’s economy, and visitors can take part in the traditional fishing practices of the local fishermen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paracel Islands

A week-long itinerary for Paracel Islands could include:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Woody Island and visit the Paracel Islands Museum and Woody Island Library.

  • Day 3-4: Take a snorkeling or diving tour to explore the pristine waters surrounding Rocky Island.

  • Day 5-6: Hike or bike the trails on Duncan Island and enjoy the stunning views of the South China Sea.

  • Day 7: Spend a quiet day relaxing on the beach and enjoying the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Paracel Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Paracel Islands are famous for their seafood. Some of the popular dishes to try on the islands include:

  • Steamed abalone: A delicacy that is highly sought after for its sweet and tender meat.

  • Shark-fin soup: A traditional Chinese dish that is made with shark fins, chicken broth, and various other ingredients.

Local Beverages

Coconut water is the popular beverage on the islands. Visitors can enjoy fresh coconut water or try some of the locally brewed coconut wine.

Where to Stay in Paracel Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are limited accommodation options on the islands, with most visitors opting to stay in one of the guesthouses or hostels. Some of the popular options include the Paracel Islands Hotel, Woody Island Guesthouse, and Rocky Island Hostel.

What to Buy in Paracel Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Paracel Islands can bring home a range of souvenirs and local products. Some of the popular items include handicrafts, traditional snacks, and hand-woven baskets made from coconut leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Paracel Islands

The islands have rich cultural traditions, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions. Some of the essential customs to keep in mind include dressing modestly, removing shoes before entering a temple or mosque, and avoiding public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Paracel Islands

The best time to visit Paracel Islands is between March and October when the weather is warm and dry, and the sea is calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paracel Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and other essential items for the trip.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that could harm the marine environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paracel Islands

Paracel Islands are generally safe, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Paracel Islands

Visitors should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites by wearing long sleeves and pants, using insect repellent, and sleeping under a mosquito net. It is also recommended to drink only bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Paracel Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

The islands are accessible by boat or plane from several locations in China, including Sanya, Haikou, and Guangzhou.

Airports Near Paracel Islands

Sanya Phoenix International Airport is the closest airport to the islands, with regular flights from major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Paracel Islands

There are no bus stations on the islands, but there are buses that run from several cities in Hainan Province to the ports where visitors can take a ferry to the islands.

Other Options

Individuals can also book private boat tours to the islands from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Paracel Islands

The islands are relatively small, and visitors can explore them on foot or by bike. Ferries and private boats can be used to travel between the islands.

Where to Go Next from Paracel Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby cities of Sanya and Haikou offer a range of attractions and activities for visitors, including beaches, hot springs, and historical sites.

Conclusion

Paracel Islands offers visitors a unique island experience with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and a chance to learn about the local culture and customs. With its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rich marine biodiversity, the islands are a must-visit destination for nature lovers and those looking for a relaxing island getaway.
