Introduction to Dongxing

Tucked away in the southern reaches of Guangxi, Dongxing is a small border town that serves as the gateway to Vietnam. A unique blend of Chinese, Vietnamese, and local ethnic minority cultures, Dongxing offers a rich tapestry of experiences for the intrepid traveler. From the stunning natural scenery of Nonggang National Nature Reserve to the fascinating historical sites marking the region’s complex past, Dongxing is a hidden gem just waiting to be explored.

List of Places to Visit in Dongxing

Culturally Important Attractions

  • Dongxing Museum: This small museum showcases the region’s long and varied history, from ancient times to the present day. Exhibits include artifacts from the early Dong Son culture, as well as a wide range of objects illustrating the influence of Vietnamese, Chinese, and other cultures on the region.

  • Dongxing Confucius Temple: Located in the heart of the town, this beautifully preserved temple is a testament to the enduring influence of Confucianism in Chinese culture. Visitors can explore the ornate halls and courtyards, and learn about the customs and beliefs that have shaped Chinese society for centuries.

Natural Attractions

  • Nonggang National Nature Reserve: One of the most breathtaking natural wonders in China, Nonggang National Nature Reserve is a pristine wilderness area teeming with rare flora and fauna. Visitors can take guided hikes to see the stunning karst landscape, explore the intricate cave systems, and observe some of the 65 endangered animal species that call the reserve home.

  • Tongling Gorge: A scenic wonderland of crystal-clear waters, towering cliffs, and lush forest, Tongling Gorge is a great place for outdoor adventures. Visitors can take rafting trips or try their hand at rock climbing, or simply hike or bike around the area’s many trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes

  • Nonggang Trekking Trail: This 10-kilometer trail is a popular hike through some of Nonggang’s most picturesque landscapes. Visitors can expect to see towering karst formations, lush forest, and plenty of wildlife along the way.

  • Tongling Gorge Bike Trail: This scenic bike trail takes visitors through some of the most breathtaking scenery in Guangxi, from cliffside paths overlooking the river to tranquil forest glades.

Best Things to Do in Dongxing

  • Visit the Guangxi-Vietnam Border Gate: Located just outside Dongxing, the Guangxi-Vietnam Border Gate is a fascinating cultural landmark that showcases the intersection of Chinese, Vietnamese, and local ethnic minority cultures. Visitors can observe the daily bustle of commerce and trade, soak up the vibrant atmosphere, and learn about the complex history of the border region.

  • Take a boat ride on the Beilun River: A tranquil trip down the Beilun River is a great way to unwind and soak up the stunning natural scenery of South China. Visitors can spot wildlife, explore remote villages, and learn about the customs and culture of the people who call the area home.

Food and Drink Scene of Dongxing

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banh Trang Tron: This Vietnamese-style salad is a popular street food in Dongxing, featuring rice paper sheets cut into thin strips and tossed with a variety of ingredients like shredded chicken, vegetables, and peanuts. The dish is often served with a spicy sauce and lime wedges.

  • Guilin Rice Noodles: This dish, consisting of chewy rice noodles in a rich broth, is a staple of Guangxi cuisine. Visitors can try variations with different meats and spices, or sample local specialties like “oil tea,” a hot beverage made with tea leaves and a variety of ingredients like ginger and nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Beerlao: A popular Vietnamese brew, Beerlao is a light, refreshing beer that pairs well with spicy foods and outdoor activities.

  • Liangcha: A traditional Chinese herbal tea, Liangcha is a cooling beverage made with a variety of herbs and flavors like mint and chrysanthemum.

Where to Stay in Dongxing

  • Nanning Hotel: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Dongxing, just steps from the town’s main attractions. Guests can enjoy spacious, comfortable rooms, Wi-Fi access, and a variety of amenities like a fitness center and business center.

  • Sapa Homestay: For a more authentic and immersive experience, visitors can opt for a homestay in nearby Sapa, Vietnam. Host families offer comfortable accommodations, home-cooked meals, and opportunities to learn about Vietnamese culture and customs.

What to Buy in Dongxing

  • Handicrafts: Dongxing and the surrounding area is home to a vibrant community of artisans producing a wide range of crafts, from traditional ceramics and pottery to hand-woven textiles and intricate wood carvings.

  • Local Specialty Foods: Visitors can stock up on Guilin Rice Noodles and other regional delicacies to bring home as gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Dongxing

Dongxing is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from both China and Vietnam, as well as local ethnic minority communities. Visitors should be prepared to encounter a wide range of customs and traditions, from Confucianism and Taoism to Buddhism and Vietnamese folk religion. Cultural sensitivity and a willingness to learn and respect local customs and beliefs will go a long way in creating a rewarding travel experience in Dongxing.

Best Time to Visit Dongxing

For the best weather and outdoor activities, the best time to visit Dongxing is between September and November, when temperatures are mild and the weather is dry. Spring is also a good time to visit, with comfortable temperatures and colorful blooming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dongxing

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and behavior in religious or cultural sites.

  • Sample the local cuisine, including street food and regional specialties.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal or unethical activities, including drug use and wildlife trafficking.

  • Treat the border area as a novelty or tourist attraction; be aware of the complex history and cultural significance of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dongxing

Dongxing is generally a safe place for travelers, with low rates of violent crime and theft. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions like keeping valuables secure, avoiding unlit or isolated areas after dark, and being aware of traffic and road hazards when exploring the area by bike or scooter.

Health in Dongxing

Visitors should consult their healthcare provider before traveling to Dongxing to ensure they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations and other health precautions. Mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria are a risk in some areas, so taking measures to avoid insect bites is important.

Getting to Dongxing

Airports Near Dongxing

The nearest airport to Dongxing is Nanning Wuxu International Airport, about 220 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Dongxing.

Bus Stations Near Dongxing

Dongxing has a major bus station with connections to major cities in Guangxi and beyond. Visitors can also take a bus from Nanning to Dongxing.

Other Options

For a more immersive and adventurous journey, visitors can take a boat up the Beilun River from the port city of Fangchenggang.

How to Get Around in Dongxing

Most of Dongxing’s major attractions are within walking distance of the town center, but visitors can also rent bikes or scooters to explore the area more extensively. Taxis and buses are also available for longer trips or to get to more remote destinations.

Where to Go Next from Dongxing

Nanning

The capital city of Guangxi, Nanning is a vibrant cultural center with a rich history and a wide range of attractions, from ancient temples to modern art galleries.

Guilin

One of the most popular tourist destinations in China, Guilin is known for its stunning natural scenery, including the famous Elephant Trunk Hill and karst formations along the Li River.

Conclusion

Dongxing is a unique and rewarding travel destination that offers a glimpse into the rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty of Southern China. With a little planning and preparation, visitors can explore the area’s many attractions, enjoy delicious cuisine, and create long-lasting memories of this hidden gem in Guangxi.
