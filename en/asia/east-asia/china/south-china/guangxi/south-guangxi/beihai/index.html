Introduction to Beihai

Beihai is a popular coastal city located on the southwest coast of Guangxi, China. With its crystal-clear waters, pristine beaches, and abundant tropical scenery, Beihai offers a wonderful natural experience for travelers. In addition, Beihai boasts of its rich cultural heritage and history, with a number of historical sites and cultural attractions to explore.

This comprehensive tour guide for Beihai covers all you need to know about the city, including places to visit, activities to do, local culture and customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Beihai

Beihai is home to a number of must-visit attractions. Here are some of the best places to visit in Beihai.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beihai Underwater World: This is a great place for families to visit. The aquarium is home to over 10,000 sea creatures and features a large underwater tunnel.

  • Beihai Silver Beach: This is one of the most popular beaches in Beihai, renowned for its clear waters and pristine sand. Besides swimming and sunbathing, there are plenty of water sports activities to enjoy.

  • Guangxi Pearl Museum: This museum showcases the history of pearls in Guangxi.

  • Weizhou Island: It is a beautiful island located in the Beibu Gulf. It boasts a volcanic landscape with natural hot springs and beautiful beaches.

Natural Attractions of Beihai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beihai National Forest Park: This park is a beautiful natural attraction located in the Beihai region. It is home to beautiful landscapes, waterfalls, and hiking trails.

  • Mangrove National Nature Reserve: It is a large wetland area in Beihai that is home to diverse species of birds, including rare birds such as seagulls and egrets.

  • Nanzhu Palace: This is a beautiful palace located near the coast in the Beihai region. It is surrounded by beautiful gardens and lakes and is an ideal place to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beihai

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Beihai, thanks to its beautiful natural landscapes. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Beihai:

  • Beihai National Forest Park trail: This trail is a great place to explore the natural beauty of Beihai’s forests and enjoy a peaceful walk.

  • Beihai Coastal Road: It is an excellent biking route that spans over 30 kilometers along the Beihai coast. This route is perfect for cycling enthusiasts who want to catch beautiful views of the Beihai coastline.

Best Things to Do in Beihai: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Beihai:

  • Beihu Beach: This is the most popular tourist destination in Beihai. It is a beautiful beach with white sand and crystal-clear waters, ideal for swimming and sunbathing.

  • Fishing Village: The fishing village is one of the most interesting places to visit in Beihai, offering travelers a glimpse into the local culture and customs.

  • Beibu Gulf: It is a large gulf in Beihai that offers excellent kayaking and snorkeling activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beihai

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Beihai:

Day 1: Explore Beihu Beach and local seafood restaurants Day 2: Visit the underwater world aquarium and go kayaking in Beibu Gulf Day 3-4: Visit Weizhou Island and explore the island’s natural hot springs and volcanic landscape Day 5: Visit Nanzhu Palace and hike in the Beihai National Forest Park Day 6-7: Explore the Mangrove National Nature Reserve and the Guangxi Pearl Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Beihai

Beihai boasts of a rich food and drink scene, giving you a chance to indulge in local delicacies and experience different cuisines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Being a coastal city, Beihai is famous for its fresh seafood. The local restaurants serve delicious seafood dishes like shrimp, crab, and oyster.

  • Hotpot: Hotpot is a popular dish in Beihai, offering a variety of meat options and sauces to choose from.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Milk: Beihai is known for its sweet and refreshing coconut milk, which is an ideal way to cool down on a hot day.

  • Luosifen: It is a popular noodle soup dish made with snails and river snails.

Where to Stay in Beihai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beihai offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget.

  • Shangri-La Hotel Beihai: It is one of the most luxurious hotels in Beihai and offers a wide range of deluxe amenities.

  • Beibu Gulf Hotel: This hotel is also a great option for those looking for a comfortable stay at a reasonable price.

  • Green Tree Inn: It offers affordable accommodation without compromising comfort and quality.

What to Buy in Beihai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Beihai has a number of shops selling local arts and crafts, souvenirs, and other goods. Here are some of the best things to buy in Beihai:

  • Pearls: Guangxi Pearl Museum offers a wide range of pearl products, including necklaces and earrings.

  • Jade: Jade is a popular local product in Beihai that is available in many shops.

  • Souvenirs: The local markets offer a wide range of souvenirs, including keychains, T-shirts, and other trinkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Beihai

It is important to understand local culture and customs to have a respectful and enjoyable experience in Beihai.

  • Clothing: Dress conservatively, covering shoulders and knees, especially when visiting temples or other important cultural sites.

  • Local festivals: Beihai is home to a number of festivals, which are great opportunities to experience local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Beihai

The best time to visit Beihai is between late spring and early autumn when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the scenery is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beihai

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Beihai:

Do:

  • Explore the local culture: Beihai offers a rich cultural experience that is worth exploring, especially for first-time visitors.

  • Try local cuisine: Beihai is known for its delicious seafood and local delicacies, so be sure to try them.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural environment: Beihai boasts of stunning natural landscapes that must be treated with respect.

  • Disregard local customs: Be sure to be respectful of the local culture and customs while visiting Beihai.

Safety and Health

It is always important to stay safe and healthy while traveling.

Safety in Beihai

Beihai is a relatively safe city, but it is important to be cautious of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Beihai

Beihai doesn’t have any specific health concerns, but travelers should be careful with seafood consumption and water cleanliness.

Getting to Beihai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beihai is easily accessible by air, train, or bus.

Airports Near Beihai

Beihai Fucheng Airport (BHY) is located about 20 kilometers from the city center and offers direct flights to a number of destinations in China.

Bus Stations Near Beihai

Beihai Bus Terminal offers buses to and from major cities in China.

Other Options

Train services to Beihai are also available from major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Beihai

Beihai is a small and compact city, making it easy to travel around. Taxis, buses, and bicycles are readily available for local transportation.

Where to Go Next from Beihai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nanning: It is the capital city of Guangxi, located about 2 hours from Beihai by train.

  • Guilin: It is another attractive destination, known for its mountain views and limestone caves.

Conclusion

Beihai is a wonderful city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and history. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to this coastal paradise and explore its must-visit attractions, local cuisine, and cultural heritage.
