Introduction to Sanjiang

Sanjiang is a small autonomous county located in Northern Guangxi, China. The area is famous for its Dong minority culture, traditional wind and rain bridges, and picturesque hills. The county is surrounded by mountains on three sides, with the Rong River flowing through the middle. With its stunning natural scenery, vibrant cultural heritage, and friendly locals, Sanjiang is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to China.

List of Places to Visit in Sanjiang

Sanjiang is full of cultural and natural attractions that are worth exploring. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Sanjiang:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge - This iconic bridge is the largest and most magnificent among Sanjiang’s wind and rain bridges. It is a unique architectural masterpiece that reflects the skill, ingenuity, and creativity of the Dong people. Visiting the Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge is a wonderful opportunity to admire the breathtaking views of the river and the surrounding hills.

  2. Sanjiang Drum Tower - The Sanjiang Drum Tower is a significant historical landmark that reflects the Dong people’s unique architectural style. Built-in 1894, the drum tower served as a social and religious center for the local community. Today, the tower is a museum that showcases the Dong people’s culture, traditions, and history.

  3. Dong Village Museum - The Dong Village Museum is an excellent place to learn about the Dong people’s culture, customs, and traditions. The museum exhibits a vast collection of Dong artifacts, including clothing, tools, musical instruments, and sculptures. The museum’s highlight is the beautiful Dong village that preserves the traditional Dong-style architecture.

Natural Attractions of Sanjiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Linxi Hill - This hill is a natural wonder with a height of 762 meters. It is famous for its beautiful landscape, lush green forests, and stunning panoramic views of Sanjiang. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities in Linxi Hill.

  2. Rongshui Miao Autonomous County - Rongshui Miao Autonomous County is located about 70 kilometers south of Sanjiang. This beautiful county is a tribute to the Miao minority’s rich culture and traditions. The county is famous for its stunning natural scenery, colorful costumes, and unique festivals.

  3. Hetang International Rice Terraces - The Hetang International Rice Terraces are located about 30 kilometers west of Sanjiang. The terraces are a testament to the skill and ingenuity of the local farmers who transform the hills into beautiful agricultural landscapes. Visitors can explore the terraces and learn about the traditional farming techniques of the Dong people.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sanjiang

Sanjiang is a paradise for hikers and bikers who enjoy exploring the beautiful natural landscapes. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Sanjiang:

  1. Linxi Hill Hiking Trail - This hiking trail is a wonderful way to explore the beautiful Linxi Hill. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the hills, forests, and rivers as they hike through the trail. The trail is approximately 10 kilometers long and takes around 5 hours to complete.

  2. Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge to Sanjiang Drum Tower Biking Trail - This biking trail is a great opportunity to explore the cultural and historical landmarks of Sanjiang. The trail is approximately 15 kilometers long and takes around 2 hours to complete. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery of Sanjiang’s hills and rivers.

  3. Hetang International Rice Terraces Hiking Trail - This hiking trail is a must-try for anyone who enjoys exploring the Chinese countryside. The trail is approximately 5 kilometers long and takes around 2 hours to complete. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery of the terraces as they hike through the trail.

Best Things to Do in Sanjiang: Activities and Tours

Sanjiang offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Sanjiang:

  1. Dong Minority Cultural Performance - The Dong Minority Cultural Performance is a unique experience that allows visitors to immerse themselves in the Dong culture. The performance showcases the Dong’s traditional dances, music, and costumes. Visitors can even join the performers on stage and learn some traditional dance steps.

  2. Bicycle Tour - A bicycle tour is a great way to explore the scenic landscapes of Sanjiang’s countryside. Visitors can rent a bicycle and explore the beautiful rice fields, hills, and rivers at their own pace. The bicycle tour is suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

  3. Dong Folk Song Concert - The Dong Folk Song Concert is a must-see for music lovers. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful melodies and harmonies of the Dong’s folk songs. The concert is usually held in the evening and creates a magical atmosphere under the stars.

Food and Drink Scene of Sanjiang

Sanjiang has a unique and delicious food and drink scene. The area is famous for its spicy and sour flavors that reflect the Dong and Miao people’s culinary traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish hotpot - Fish hotpot is a popular dish in Sanjiang that combines fresh and spicy ingredients. The dish consists of fresh local fish, rice noodles, mushrooms, vegetables, and a spicy broth.

  2. Dog meat - Dog meat is a traditional dish enjoyed by the Dong and Miao people. The locals believe that eating dog meat enhances their strength and endurance. The dish is usually served with ginger and garlic sauce.

  3. Glutinous rice cakes - Glutinous rice cakes are sweet and sticky cakes made from glutinous rice flour. The cakes are usually served with sweet bean paste or red dates.

Local Beverages

  1. Rice wine - Rice wine is a popular alcoholic beverage in Sanjiang. The wine is made from fermented glutinous rice and has a sweet and sour taste.

  2. Tea - Sanjiang is famous for its green tea, which has a refreshing taste and a light aroma. Visitors can enjoy a cup of tea at local tea houses and learn about the tea-making process.

Where to Stay in Sanjiang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sanjiang offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. Visitors can choose from hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top accommodation options in Sanjiang:

  1. Sanjiang Dongxiang Guesthouse - The Sanjiang Dongxiang Guesthouse is a beautiful traditional Dong-style guesthouse. The guesthouse is located near the Sanjiang Drum Tower and offers comfortable rooms with panoramic views of the surrounding hills and river.

  2. Sanjiang International Hotel - The Sanjiang International Hotel is a modern hotel that offers excellent amenities and services. The hotel is located near the Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge and features spacious rooms with comfortable beds and modern bathrooms.

  3. Sanjiang Youth Hostel - The Sanjiang Youth Hostel offers affordable accommodation for backpackers and budget travelers. The hostel is located near the Dong Village Museum and features comfortable bunk beds, a cozy common room, and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Sanjiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sanjiang has a vibrant local market that offers a variety of souvenirs and local products. Visitors can shop for unique handicrafts, traditional costumes, and local snacks. Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. Dong Embroidery - Dong embroidery is a traditional craft that uses colorful threads to create intricate designs on fabric. Visitors can buy handmade Dong embroidery pieces, such as tablecloths, cushion covers, and blouses.

  2. Bamboo Products - Bamboo is a common material used in Sanjiang’s traditional crafts. Visitors can buy handmade bamboo baskets, hats, and furniture.

  3. Sanjiang Chili Sauce - Sanjiang Chili Sauce is a famous local product that adds a spicy and flavorful kick to any dish. Visitors can buy jars of Sanjiang Chili Sauce to bring back home and enjoy the unique taste of Sanjiang.

Local Culture and Customs of Sanjiang

Sanjiang has a rich and unique culture that reflects the Dong and Miao people’s traditions and beliefs. Here are some of the local customs and practices:

  1. Dragon Boat Festival - The Dragon Boat Festival is a significant festival celebrated by the Dong people. The festival is held on the fifth day of the fifth lunar month and includes dragon boat races, folk performances, and traditional food.

  2. Folk dances - Folk dances are an essential part of the Dong and Miao culture. The dances are performed during festivals and social gatherings and often involve colorful costumes and traditional music.

  3. Worship of ancestors - The Dong and Miao people believe in the importance of worshipping their ancestors. They often hold ceremonies to honor their ancestors and seek their guidance and blessings.

Best Time to Visit Sanjiang

The best time to visit Sanjiang is from April to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. The spring and autumn seasons offer excellent opportunities to explore Sanjiang’s beautiful natural landscapes, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Visitors should avoid visiting Sanjiang during the winter months when the temperatures are cold and the weather is wet and gloomy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sanjiang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Sanjiang:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or their property.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Follow the rules and regulations while hiking or biking in the countryside.
  • Bargain while shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Engage in behavior that is offensive or disrespectful.
  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Take pictures of dead or injured animals.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Touch or disturb cultural relics or artifacts.

Safety and Health

Sanjiang is a safe and healthy destination for travelers. However, visitors should take some precautions to ensure their safety and well-being while traveling. Here are some safety and health tips for visitors:

Safety in Sanjiang

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings while exploring Sanjiang’s countryside.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night or in secluded areas.
  • Take precautions against mosquitoes and other insects to prevent bites and diseases.
  • Keep your valuables and documents in a safe place.

Health in Sanjiang

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Wash your hands frequently to prevent infections.
  • Wear sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any illness or injury.

Bus Stations Near Sanjiang

Other Options

How to Get Around in Sanjiang

Sanjiang is a small county, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire taxis to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Sanjiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Sanjiang is a hidden gem in China that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With its rich history, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, Sanjiang is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to China.
