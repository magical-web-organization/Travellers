Introduction to Longsheng

Located in North Guangxi, Longsheng is a mountainous county in China, known for its picturesque landscapes, ethnic minority groups, and rich cultural history. With over 27,000 hectares of terraced rice paddies, Longsheng is a popular destination among domestic and international tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Longsheng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jinkeng Rice Terrace: The Jinkeng Rice Terrace is one of the largest and most stunning rice terraces in China. The terrace is divided into seven sections, each showcasing a different stage in the rice farming process.

  • Zhonghua Tusi Culture Village: The Zhonghua Tusi Culture Village is a historical site that was once home to a local ruling family during the Ming Dynasty. Visitors can learn about the culture and customs of the Tusi people through exhibits, performances, and traditional workshops.

  • Ping’an Village: Ping’an Village is a small but charming village located on a hilltop overlooking the rice terraces. The village is home to the Zhuang ethnic minority, which make up a sizable portion of the population in Longsheng.

Natural Attractions of Longsheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Longji Rice Terrace: The Longji Rice Terrace is another popular terraced rice paddy that is located in Longsheng. With over 13,000 hectares of terraces, the Longji Rice Terrace is a great place to hike and take in the picturesque scenery.

  • Hot Springs in Longsheng: There are several natural hot springs located in Longsheng that are known for their healing properties. The hot springs are a great place to unwind after a day of hiking or exploring.

  • Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terrace: The Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terrace is a unique attraction in Longsheng that features a series of terraced rice paddies that are said to resemble the scales of a dragon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longsheng

  • Hiking in Jinkeng Rice Terrace: The Jinkeng Rice Terrace offers several hiking trails that range from easy to difficult. The most popular trail is the Seven Stars with Moon Trail, which takes visitors through different sections of the rice terrace.

  • Biking in Longsheng: Biking is another popular activity in Longsheng. There are several biking routes that take visitors through the rice paddies and to nearby villages.

Best Things to Do in Longsheng: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Minority Village: Longsheng is home to several ethnic minority groups, including the Yao, Miao, and Zhuang people. Visitors can take a tour of these villages to learn about their culture and customs.

  • Rafting on the Li River: The Li River is a scenic river that runs through Longsheng. Visitors can take a rafting tour down the river to take in the stunning scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Longsheng

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braised Pork with Bamboo Shoots: This dish is a local specialty of Longsheng and features tender braised pork with fresh bamboo shoots.

  • Chou Doufu: Chou Doufu is a fermented tofu dish that is popular throughout China. Longsheng is known for its particularly delicious version of this dish.

Local Beverages

  • Bamboo Leaf Tea: Bamboo Leaf Tea is a local tea that is made by brewing dried bamboo leaves. It has a light, refreshing flavor and is said to have many health benefits.

  • Rice Wine: Rice Wine is a popular alcoholic beverage in Longsheng that is made by fermenting rice. It has a sweet taste and is often served during special occasions and festivals.

Where to Stay in Longsheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Longsheng Hot Spring Resort: The Longsheng Hot Spring Resort is a luxury hotel that is located near the hot springs. It offers several amenities, including a spa, gym, and pool.

  • Longsheng Ping’an Hotel: The Longsheng Ping’an Hotel is a traditional guesthouse that is located in Ping’an Village. It offers comfortable rooms and is in close proximity to the rice terraces.

  • Backpacker’s Hostel: The Backpacker’s Hostel is a budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms. It is a great option for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Longsheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Longsheng is known for its handicrafts, including embroidered clothing and woven baskets.

  • Tea: Bamboo Leaf Tea and other local teas are a great souvenir to bring home from Longsheng.

Local Culture and Customs of Longsheng

  • Ethnic Minority Groups: Longsheng is home to several ethnic minority groups, including the Yao, Zhuang, and Miao people. Visitors should be respectful of their culture and customs during their visit.

  • Traditional Festivals: Longsheng celebrates several traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Long Street Banquet and the Zhuang Singing Festival.

Best Time to Visit Longsheng

The best time to visit Longsheng is during the autumn (September to November) when the temperatures are mild, and the rice terraces are at their most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longsheng

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and tea.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and villages.

Don’t:

  • Waste food or water.
  • Speak loudly in public areas or disturb others.
  • Damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Longsheng

Longsheng is a safe destination, and visitors should not encounter any safety concerns during their visit. However, it is always best to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Longsheng

Longsheng is a relatively healthy destination for visitors. However, visitors should take precautions and drink bottled water instead of tap water to avoid any potential health issues.

Getting to Longsheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Longsheng

The closest airport to Longsheng is the Guilin Liangjiang International Airport, which is approximately 100 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Longsheng.

Bus Stations Near Longsheng

Longsheng has several bus stations that connect it to nearby cities, including Guilin and Yangshuo.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Guilin and then take a bus or taxi to Longsheng.

How to Get Around in Longsheng

The easiest way to get around Longsheng is by taxi or hired car. It is also possible to rent a bike or hike to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Longsheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Guilin: Guilin is a popular destination in China known for its beautiful landscapes and cultural heritage.

  • Yangshuo: Yangshuo is a scenic town that is known for its limestone mountains and picturesque countryside.

Conclusion

Longsheng is a must-visit destination in China for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural history, and ethnic minority groups.
