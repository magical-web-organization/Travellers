Introduction to North Guangxi

North Guangxi is a region located in South China and is a popular tourist destination for its stunning natural scenery and rich cultural heritage. It is surrounded by mountains and boasts some of the most spectacular landscapes in China. The region is inhabited by ethnic minorities, including the Zhuang people, who have their own unique customs and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in North Guangxi

North Guangxi has a rich cultural and natural heritage that offers visitors an unforgettable travel experience. Here are some of the best places to visit when exploring the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Guilin Museum: Guilin Museum is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Guilin city and its surrounding areas. The museum houses a collection of more than 50,000 artifacts and exhibits, including bronze wares, pottery, and ancient calligraphy.

  • Longji Rice Terraces: The Longji Rice Terraces, also known as the Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terraces, are located in Longsheng County. The rice terraces have been cultivated for over 650 years by the Zhuang people and are a stunning sight to behold.

  • Jingjiang Wangcheng City: Jingjiang Wangcheng City was built during the Ming and Qing dynasties and served as the capital of Guangxi Province. The city has well-preserved ancient city walls, gates, and buildings, offering visitors a glimpse into ancient Chinese history and culture.

Natural Attractions of North Guangxi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Li River: The Li River is a scenic river that runs through Guilin and Yangshuo. It is famous for its karst hills, crystal-clear water, and tranquil countryside. Taking a boat tour down the Li River is a popular way to admire the scenery and relax.

  • Yangshuo West Street: Yangshuo West Street is a bustling pedestrian street that is lined with traditional Chinese architecture, souvenir shops, and authentic restaurants. It is a great place to experience the charm of Chinese culture and buy some souvenirs.

  • Elephant Trunk Hill: Elephant Trunk Hill is one of Guilin’s most famous landmarks. The hill resembles an elephant drinking water from the Li River and is a popular spot for taking pictures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Guangxi

North Guangxi is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The region has a variety of hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Moon Hill: Moon Hill is a popular destination for hikers and rock climbers. It is named for the crescent-shaped hole that penetrates one of the karst peaks in the area.

  • Yulong River: The Yulong River is a scenic river that runs through Yangshuo. It is a great place for kayaking, bamboo rafting, and bike riding.

  • Ping’an Village: Ping’an Village is located in the Longji Rice Terraces area and is surrounded by rice fields and mountains. Hiking in this area is a must-do activity for travelers visiting North Guangxi.

Best Things to Do in North Guangxi: Activities and Tours

North Guangxi is full of unique and exciting activities and tours. Visitors can explore the region’s natural wonders, learn about local cultures and traditions, and taste delicious food.

  • Cormorant Fishing Show: Cormorant fishing is a traditional fishing method that has been used for centuries in China. Visitors can watch cormorant fishing shows at night along the Li River.

  • Zhuang Minority Village: The Zhuang Minority Village is a great place to learn about the culture and lifestyle of the local Zhuang people. Visitors can see traditional dancing, admire the architecture, and taste traditional cuisine.

  • Bamboo Rafting: Bamboo rafting down the Yulong River is a peaceful and scenic way to explore the beauty of the countryside while enjoying the fresh air and sunshine.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Guangxi

Here is a sample week-long itinerary that highlights some of the best things to do and see in North Guangxi:

Day 1: Arrive in Guilin and spend the day exploring the city.

Day 2: Visit the Longji Rice Terraces and hike through the rice fields in Ping’an Village.

Day 3: Take a bamboo rafting trip down the Yulong River and visit the Yangshuo West Street.

Day 4: Explore the Guilin Museum and climb the Elephant Trunk Hill.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Chinese dishes.

Day 6: Visit the Jingjiang Wangcheng City and enjoy a cormorant fishing show along the Li River.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenery on a boat tour down the Li River.

Food and Drink Scene of North Guangxi

North Guangxi has a diverse cuisine that incorporates local ingredients and traditional cooking methods. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Guilin Rice Noodles: Guilin Rice Noodles are a famous local dish made with rice flour and served in a savory broth with meat, vegetables, and spices.

  • Beer Fish: Beer Fish is a locally popular fish dish that is pan-fried with beer and served in a spicy sauce.

  • Stuffed Snails: Stuffed Snails are a delicacy in North Guangxi and are filled with minced pork, garlic, and ginger.

Local Beverages

  • Xiangjian Wine: Xiangjian Wine is a popular local wine made from glutinous rice and is known for its rich aroma and flavor.

  • Tea: North Guangxi is one of China’s major tea-producing regions, and visitors can enjoy local tea at teahouses throughout the area.

Where to Stay in North Guangxi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Guangxi has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Guilin Shangri-La Hotel: The Guilin Shangri-La Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Guilin that offers stunning views of the Li River and the surrounding hills.

  • Ad Hostel: Ad Hostel is a budget-friendly hostel located in Yangshuo that offers comfortable dormitory rooms and private rooms.

  • Jinkaiyue Hotel: The Jinkaiyue Hotel is a popular guesthouse located in the Longji Rice Terraces area that offers cozy rooms and delicious local food.

What to Buy in North Guangxi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Guangxi is known for its handicrafts and local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy when visiting the region:

  • Guilin Fan: Guilin Fan is a traditional Chinese fan that is made from bamboo and silk and is great for keeping cool in the hot summer months.

  • Longji Tea: Longji Tea is a type of Chinese green tea that is grown in the Longji Rice Terraces area and is known for its unique aroma and flavor.

  • Brocade: Brocade is a type of silk fabric that is made using traditional weaving techniques and is used to create traditional Chinese clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of North Guangxi

North Guangxi is home to several ethnic minorities, including the Zhuang people, who have their own unique customs and traditions. Visitors should show respect for local cultures by following these customs:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.

  • Ask for permission before taking photos of local people.

  • Avoid pointing with your finger, as it is considered rude.

Best Time to Visit North Guangxi

The best time to visit North Guangxi is from April to October when the weather is warm and pleasant. Off-peak seasons from November to March offer lower prices and fewer crowds, but the weather may be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Guangxi

Do:

  • Carry tissue paper with you, as it is not always provided in public restrooms.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Spit or litter in public places.

  • Touch or climb on ancient buildings or monuments.

  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Guangxi

North Guangxi is generally a safe travel destination, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid theft and other crimes. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

  • Keep your belongings close to you in crowded areas.

  • Use reputable taxis or transportation services.

Health in North Guangxi

North Guangxi is a relatively healthy travel destination, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid illnesses. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water.

  • Wash your hands frequently.

  • Get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and cholera before traveling.

Getting to North Guangxi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Guangxi

The main airport serving North Guangxi is Guilin Liangjiang International Airport, which is located approximately 35 kilometers from Guilin city.

Bus Stations Near North Guangxi

Guilin Bus Station and Yangshuo Bus Station are the two main bus stations serving North Guangxi. Buses run regularly from these stations to major cities in China.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach North Guangxi by train or by car.

How to Get Around in North Guangxi

The best way to get around North Guangxi is by taxi, bus, or bike. Taxis are readily available in major cities, and buses run regularly between cities and tourist attractions. Biking is a great way to explore the countryside and enjoy the scenery at a slower pace.

Where to Go Next from North Guangxi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Guangxi is a great destination for exploring other parts of China, including:

  • Hong Kong: Hong Kong is a vibrant city-state that boasts a rich cultural heritage and a modern skyline. Visitors can enjoy shopping, dining, and exploring the city’s historical sites.

  • Shenzhen: Shenzhen is a modern city that is known for its high-tech industries and modern architecture. Visitors can enjoy shopping, dining, and exploring the city’s parks and museums.

  • Guangzhou: Guangzhou is a historic city that is known for its traditional architecture, delicious food, and cultural attractions. Visitors can explore ancient temples, museums, and parks.

Conclusion

North Guangxi is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore the beauty and culture of South China. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality, North Guangxi offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. By following this travel guide, visitors can plan their perfect itinerary and make the most of their time in the region.
