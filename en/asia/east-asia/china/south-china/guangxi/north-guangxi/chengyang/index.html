Introduction to Chengyang

Chengyang is a beautiful and culturally rich county located in North Guangxi, China. This region is known for its picturesque landscapes characterized by rolling hills, rivers, and rice paddies. Chengyang’s beauty and rich cultural heritage attract tourists from all over the world who come here to experience the beautiful natural scenery, explore the local culture, and taste the delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Chengyang

Chengyang has many interesting places to visit that cater to all kinds of travelers. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, there is something for everyone in Chengyang.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The region is steeped in rich history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that can deepen your appreciation of it.

Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge

The Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge is a magnificent example of Dong architecture and engineering. This covered bridge, also known as Chengyang Bridge, was built in the early 20th century to connect various Dong ethnic groups living on the two sides of the Linxi River. This iconic bridge is about 64.4 meters long, 3.4 meters wide, and 10.6 meters high.

Dong Villages

Chengyang is home to several Dong Villages, a UNESCO World Heritage site, that houses a number of traditional Dong houses. These houses, made of wood and stone, with layered eaves and ornate woodcarvings, are a testament to the stunning architecture of the Dong ethnic community.

Huangluo Yao Village

Huangluo Yao Village is another must-visit destination in Chengyang. This is where the world-famous Yao Women’s Hairdo culture is preservered – the world’s longest hair culture. It is believed that Yao women cut their hair only once in their lives, which is then passed down as a family heirloom through generations.

Natural Attractions of Chengyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chengyang is rich in natural beauty and offers scenic views and outdoor adventures to explore.

Detian Waterfall

Detian Waterfall, located in the Guangxi autonomous region, is a natural wonder and a must-see destination in Chengyang. This waterfall is located on the Sino-Vietnamese border and is one of the most beautiful and grand waterfalls in China.

Longji Rice Terraces

The Longji Rice Terraces are located in Longsheng County, a few hours away from Chengyang. These terraces are an amazing feat of agricultural engineering that stretches for miles, providing a breathtaking view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Sanjiang Drum Tower

Sanjiang Drum Tower is an impressive three-story structure that marks the entrance to the town of Sanjiang. It is a significant cultural symbol of the Dong people and was built in the Ming Dynasty in the early 15th century. It offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is a must-visit attraction in Chengyang.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chengyang

The best way to explore Chengyang and its natural beauty is on foot or by bike. Chengyang offers visitors several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views and a chance to explore the region’s natural splendor.

Longji Rice Terraces Hiking Trail

This 6-7 hour hiking trail takes you through the Longji Rice Terraces, where you can explore the beautiful landscape and learn about the local culture.

Biking Tour in Chengyang

A guided bike tour in Chengyang is the perfect way to experience the region’s natural beauty and local culture. You can explore the Dong Villages, Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge, and other cultural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Chengyang: Activities and Tours

Chengyang offers visitors a wealth of activities and tours to choose from. From cultural tours to nature hikes, there is something for everyone.

Minority Culture Show

Located in Sanjiang, the Minority Culture Show is a daily performance that celebrates the Dong people’s rich cultural heritage through dance, music, and traditional dress.

Rafting on the River

The Liu River offers great opportunities for White-water rafting, which is a popular activity here. Visitors can rent rafts and explore the beautiful scenery of the river that flows through the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chengyang

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Chengyang that covers the top attractions in the region and offers a chance to experience the local culture.

Day Plan
Day 1 Visit Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge, explore Dong Villages, and watch a traditional performance.
Day 2 Visit Detian Waterfall and Nonggan Village.
Day 3 Hiking at Longji Rice Terraces.
Day 4 Explore the Sanjiang Drum Tower and visit the Chengyang rice fields.
Day 5 Cycling tours and enjoy local food.
Day 6 Visit Huangluo Yao Village and experience the longest hair culture in the world.
Day 7 Relaxing day walking around the charming Demohao village or Rejiang market in Sanjiang.

Food and Drink Scene of Chengyang

Chengyang is known for its delicious local cuisine and unique beverages. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

Bamboo Rice

Bamboo Rice is a local specialty of the Dong ethnic group in Chengyang. The dish is made by cooking seasoned sticky rice with bamboo leaves over an open flame.

Luo Soup

Luo Soup is a traditional soup made with pork and sour bamboo shoots, which gives it a tangy flavor.

Local Beverages

Rice liquor

Rice liquor, or rice wine, is a popular drink in Chengyang. It is made by fermenting glutinous rice, and has a sweet, mild flavor.

Bamboo Tea

Bamboo tea, made from fresh bamboo leaves, is a local drink which is believed to have numerous health benefits.

Where to Stay in Chengyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chengyang offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and tastes. From luxury hotels to budget guesthouses, there are plenty of options to choose from.

Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge Inn

The Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge Inn is located near the iconic Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge. It offers comfortable rooms with beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Longji Star Wish Resort

Located near the Longji Rice Terraces, this resort offers comfortable rooms with stunning views, and is the perfect base to explore the surrounding area.

What to Buy in Chengyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chengyang offers a range of local products that make for great souvenirs. Here’s a list of must-buy items.

Ethnic Clothing

Chengyang’s ethnic clothing is unique and colorful, and makes for a great souvenir or gift for friends and family.

Dong Brocade

Dong brocade, a traditional weaving technique, is used to create intricate designs for clothes or tapestries. It is a must-buy item for textile enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Chengyang

Chengyang is home to the Dong ethnic community, which has a rich cultural heritage. Visitors can witness this cultural richness while exploring the region’s historical sites and attending traditional performances.

Best Time to Visit Chengyang

The best time to visit Chengyang is March to October, when the weather is pleasant and there are many cultural festivals to attend. Travelers should be aware that summer months tend to be more crowded than other times due to school holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chengyang

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and historical sites.

  • Use chopsticks instead of a fork and knife to eat.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside the house.

  • Take photographs without asking permission first.

  • Touch someone’s head or shoulder without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chengyang

Chengyang is a safe destination but, like any other place, visitors should take care of their valuables and avoid scenic spots during periods of high tourist traffic.

Health in Chengyang

Visitors should drink bottled water and be wary of eating street food to avoid getting food poisoning or other ailments.

Getting to Chengyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chengyang is well-connected to other major cities in China and can be accessed by air or land.

Airports Near Chengyang

The closest airport to Chengyang is Liuzhou Bailian Airport (LZH), located about a three-hour drive away from Chengyang.

Bus Stations Near Chengyang

There are several bus stations in Chengyang, including the Sanjiang Bus Station, where visitors can board a bus to other nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Chengyang by train. The closest railway station is located in Sanjiang and has daily connections to other major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Chengyang

The best way to get around Chengyang is by taxi, bike, or on foot. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding regions.

Where to Go Next from Chengyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chengyang is surrounded by many other popular destinations in China that offer further cultural and natural sightseeing opportunities.

Guilin

Guilin, a popular Chinese city known for its stunning landscapes, is only a few hours away from Chengyang.

Yangshuo

Yangshuo, located in the same region as Guilin, is another popular destination known for its natural beauty and authentic local experience.

Conclusion

Chengyang is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to explore the beauty of China’s Guangxi province. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and delicious local cuisine, Chengyang has something to offer to every traveler. By following this guide, you can make the most of your trip to this fascinating destination.
