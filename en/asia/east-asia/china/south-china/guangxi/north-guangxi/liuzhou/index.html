Introduction to Liuzhou

Liuzhou is a city located in North Guangxi, which is a province in South China. The city is situated on the banks of the winding Liujiang River, which is surrounded by green mountains and gorgeous scenery. Liuzhou is known for its abundant natural resources, including rivers, forests, karst caves, and hills. It is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, food lovers, and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Liuzhou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Liujiang Ancient Town

Liujiang Ancient Town is a well-preserved ancient town located in the outskirts of Liuzhou city. It was built about 500 years ago during the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) and has a history of over 700 years. It is home to many cultural relics, including ancient temples, archways, and bridges.

Liuzhou Museum

Liuzhou Museum is a provincial-level museum that hosts over 100,000 cultural relics, including pottery, bronze, jade, and other artifacts. The museum has three exhibition halls, where visitors can learn about the culture, history, and art of Liuzhou.

Natural Attractions of Liuzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yufeng Mountain Scenic Area

Yufeng Mountain Scenic Area is a national 4A rated scenic spot, located in the northern suburb of Liuzhou. It covers an area of over 762 hectares and features majestic peaks, waterfalls, and numerous caves. Visitors can go hiking, rock climbing, and swimming while enjoying the amazing natural scenery.

Longtan Karst Landform

Longtan Karst Landform is a world-renowned karst landscape, located about 25 km southeast of Liuzhou. It is known for its unique features such as double towers, double caves, and sinkholes. Visitors can explore the caves, go boating on the underground river, and enjoy panoramic views of the karst mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liuzhou

Liuzhou is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The city has many hiking and biking trails with various difficulty levels that offer breathtaking scenic views.

Xiangshan Mountain Hiking Trail

The Xiangshan Mountain Hiking Trail is a popular trail amongst local residents. The summit of the mountain provides a panoramic view of Liuzhou city. Additionally, visitors can discover ancient architectures, like the Xiangshan Temple and the Xiangshan Pagoda, which date back to the Tang Dynasty (618-907).

Liujiang River Biking Trail

The Liujiang River Biking Trail is a 27 km trail that stretches along the Liujiang River. The trail is accessible to all levels of riders and features many scenic spots along the river.

Best Things to Do in Liuzhou: Activities and Tours

Taste Local Food

Liuzhou is not only famous for its natural attractions but also its authentic local cuisine. Try hearty dishes like Luosifen (river snail rice noodles) and rice flour noodles. Don’t forget to try local snacks and desserts like fried rice cakes and sugar cake.

Join a River Rafting Tour on Liujiang River

Liujiang River is a great place to enjoy water sports, and river rafting is a popular activity. Join a river rafting tour to get the most out of your visit to Liuzhou.

Week-Long Itinerary for Liuzhou

Day 1: Visit Liuzhou Museum and Liujiang Ancient Town

Day 2: Explore Longtan Karst Landform

Day 3: Hike Yufeng Mountain Scenic Area

Day 4-5: Join a River Rafting Tour on Liujiang River

Day 6: Enjoy a day of local food tasting, including Luosifen (river snail rice noodles), rice flour noodles, fried rice cakes, and sugar cakes.

Day 7: Shopping for souvenirs at the local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Liuzhou

Must-Try Dishes

Luosifen (River Snail Rice Noodles)

Luosifen is a popular local dish made of rice noodles, river snails, and various condiments like pickles, peanuts, and bamboo shoots. It is famous for its spicy and sour taste.

Rice Flour Noodles

Using rice flour as the main ingredient and served with various soup bases, rice noodles are a staple food in Liuzhou. The flavors can range from spicy to sour or savory.

Local Beverages

Sanhuaizhi (Three Flowers Tea)

Sanhuaizhi is a traditional tea made by boiling osmanthus, jasmine, and honeysuckle flowers. This tea is known for its fragrance and health benefits.

Rice Wine

Rice wine is a popular local beverage in Liuzhou. Made from glutinous rice and fermented for months in earthen jars, it is a sweet and fragrant wine that is especially enjoyed during the Chinese New Year.

Where to Stay in Liuzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Liuzhou city offers various accommodation options for visitors, including luxury hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

Liuzhou Grand Hotel

Liuzhou Grand Hotel is a 5-star luxury hotel located in the heart of Liuzhou. It offers elegant rooms, multiple restaurants, a spa, and fitness facilities.

Liuzhou Wanda Realm Hotel

This hotel is located in Wanda Plaza commercial district and features a swimming pool, fitness center, and meeting rooms.

What to Buy in Liuzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home unique local souvenirs and products as a reminder of their trip to Liuzhou. Here are a few recommendations:

Jinjiu Liquor

Jinjiu liquor is a famous liquor in Guangxi Province that is well known for its fragrant, smooth taste.

Maclura Pomifera Vinegar

Maclura Pomifera vinegar is a traditional vinegar made from the pomelo skin. It is famous for its sour taste and health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Liuzhou

Liuzhou is home to a rich culture and customs. The locals are welcoming and hospitable, and they deeply respect and honor their traditions and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Liuzhou

The best time to visit Liuzhou is in the autumn from September to November when the weather is pleasant, and nature is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liuzhou

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Liuzhou.

Don’t:

  • Climb or damage ancient relics or architectures.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Disrespect locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liuzhou

Liuzhou is generally a safe city for tourists. However, it would be best to keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas.

Health in Liuzhou

Liuzhou has a hygienic and healthy environment. However, visitors are highly recommended to avoid drinking tap water and to carry mosquito repellents during summer visits.

Getting to Liuzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Liuzhou has great transportation connections to other parts of China. Visitors can get to Liuzhou by the following options:

Airports Near Liuzhou

  • Liuzhou Bailian Airport - the closest and most convenient airport to Liuzhou.

Bus Stations Near Liuzhou

  • Liuzhou Bus Station - a major bus station with buses connecting to all parts of China.
  • Liuzhou South Bus Station - mainly serving buses traveling to cities in Guangdong, Guangxi, and other southern regions.

Other Options

Visitors can also take trains to Liuzhou with good connectivity to major cities, including Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen.

How to Get Around in Liuzhou

Getting around in Liuzhou is easy and convenient. Visitors have the following options:

  • Subway
  • Public Buses
  • Taxis
  • Bicycle

Where to Go Next from Liuzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Liuzhou is a gateway to many nearby destinations and excursions. Here are a few recommendations that are worth a visit:

  • Guilin: A city famous for its incredible natural landscapes, including karst mountains and the Li River.
  • Yangshuo: A town surrounded by natural scenery, perfect for outdoor activities like rock climbing, exploring caves, and bamboo rafting.
  • Sanjiang: A small town known for its unique wooden houses and the Zhaoxing Dong Village.

Conclusion

Liuzhou is a hidden gem that deserves a visit. It offers tourists unique experiences with its natural and cultural attractions, local cuisine, and vibrant traditions. Plan your visit to Liuzhou and discover the beauty of South China.
