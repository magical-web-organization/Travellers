Introduction to Baise

Located in North Guangxi, Baise is a picturesque city known for its scenic views, rich history, and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by lush green mountains and gorgeous valleys, Baise has everything that a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker would want. From fascinating museums and well-preserved historical sites to stunning natural landmarks and charming hiking trails, Baise has it all.

In this comprehensive tour guide of Baise, we will cover everything you need to know before planning your trip, including where to stay, what to eat, must-visit attractions, local customs and safety tips, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Baise

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baise Uprising Memorial Hall: Dedicated to the Baise Uprising in 1929, this museum showcases the history of China’s rural revolution and provides insight into the country’s modern history.
  • Jintian Uprising Memorial Hall: Commemorating the Jintian Uprising in 1927, this museum displays the achievements of the Communist Party and tells the story of the local revolution.
  • Xilang Mountain National Forest Park: This park is home to a plethora of natural and cultural attractions, including the Dai village, the Xilang Mountain Scenic Area, and the Yingna River scenic zone.

Natural Attractions of Baise: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Daming Mountain Scenic Area: Located in the Daming Mountains, this area features stunning natural scenery, including waterfalls, deep gorges, and dense forests, and it’s a popular destination for hiking and camping.
  • The Baise Panyang River National Scenic Area: A stunning place to explore the natural beauty of Baise, this scenic area is known for its crystal-clear water, majestic green mountains, and picturesque scenery.
  • Jiuchong Scenic Area: This breathtaking scenic area is home to many species of rare plants and animals and features several hiking trails that are perfect for exploring the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baise

  • The Daming Mountain Trail: This trail is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts, offering breathtaking views of the mountain range, waterfalls, and lush green forests for those who are up for the challenge.
  • The Baise Panyang River Trail: This trail runs along the Panyang River and is a delightful option for those who want to get outdoors and experience the natural beauty of Baise up close.
  • The Leye-Fengshan Biking Trail: This 48-kilometer trail is located in Leye County and is perfect for those who love cycling. It features breathtaking scenery, including limestone peaks, lush forests, and crystal-clear water spots.

Best Things to Do in Baise: Activities and Tours

  • Biking in Leye-Fengshan Scenic Area: As mentioned earlier, this area is known for its excellent biking trails and abundant natural beauty.
  • Exploring the karst caves at Shuiyue Cave: This sprawling cave system is a natural wonder that includes several caves, each featuring unique stalactites, stalagmites, and underground streams.
  • Visiting the Gupo Ancient Town: This historic town dates back to the Tang Dynasty and is considered one of the most well-preserved ancient towns in China.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baise

Day 1: Explore the local food scene of Baise Day 2: Visit local museums and historical sites Day 3: Hike the Daming Mountain Scenic Area Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Baise by biking the Leye-Fengshan Trail Day 5: Visit the Gupo Ancient Town Day 6: Explore the karst caves at Shuiyue Cave Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Baise

Food and Drink Scene of Baise

Must-Try Dishes

  • Luosifen: A rice noodle soup that is a local specialty in Baise. It’s made with snail soup, pork bones, and sour bamboo shoots.
  • Baise Yezi: A traditional Baise dish that consists of fish, seafood, meat, and vegetables cooked in a bamboo steamer.
  • Zongzi: A rice dish wrapped in bamboo leaves and stuffed with various fillings.

Local Beverages

  • Guangxi Sanhua Liquor: A fermented rice wine that is considered one of the most well-known alcoholic beverages in Guangxi.
  • Baise Orange Juice: Made from fresh oranges grown in Baise City, this drink is a delicious and refreshing treat that showcases the city’s produce.

Where to Stay in Baise: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Koyo Hotel Baise: One of the largest luxury hotels in Baise, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and breathtaking views of the city.
  • Baise International Hotel: This hotel is located in the center of Baise, making it a convenient option for those who wish to explore the city’s major attractions.
  • Xinyi Guest House: A cozy and affordable guesthouse that provides a warm and homely atmosphere for travelers.

What to Buy in Baise: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bamboo and rattan products: Baise is famous for its bamboo and rattan craftsmanship, and you can find woven baskets, chairs, and other household items at local markets.
  • Silver jewelry: The Bai nationality living in Baise is known for their silver jewelry, so you might want to check out some of the local silver shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Baise

  • Bai Culture: Baise is home to the Bai People, who have their own unique culture and customs, such as the “Sanjie Zhou” festival, where people celebrate by holding a torch festival in honor of their patron saint.
  • Baise Dialect: While most people in Baise can speak Mandarin, the local dialect is Baisehua.

Best Time to Visit Baise

The best time to visit Baise is from March to November when the weather is warm, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. If you wish to avoid the peak tourist season, visit Baise in March, April, or November, when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baise

Do:

  • Try the local food, especially Luosifen and Baise Yezi.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as Bai Culture and dialects.
  • Visit the various museums and historical sites

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended
  • Engage in any illegal activities
  • Disrespect the local customs and cultures

Safety and Health

Safety in Baise

As in any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings, especially when visiting crowded areas. Also, be careful when traveling on public transportation and keep an eye on your personal belongings at all times.

Health in Baise

When traveling to Baise, make sure to pack any necessary medication and consult a doctor before traveling if you have any medical conditions. Additionally, make sure to drink plenty of water and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Baise: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baise

  • Baise Youjiang Airport: The closest airport to Baise is Youjiang Airport, located about 18 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Baise

  • Baise Bus Station: This station is located in the city center and serves both domestic and international destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Although there are no direct trains to Baise yet, visitors can take a train to nearby cities, such as Nanning, and then take a bus to Baise.

How to Get Around in Baise

Getting around Baise is easy with the public transportation system, which includes buses and taxis. There are also bike rentals available for those who want to explore the city by bike.

Where to Go Next from Baise: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nanning: The capital city of Guangxi and a popular tourist destination in China. It takes about 3 hours to reach Nanning by bus from Baise.
  • Guilin: A scenic city located in the northeast of Guangxi Province, known for its stunning natural scenery, including the Li River and karst mountains. It takes about 8 hours to reach Guilin by bus from Baise.
