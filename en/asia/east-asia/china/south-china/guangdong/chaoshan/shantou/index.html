Introduction to Shantou

Shantou is a bustling coastal city in Chaoshan, Guangdong Province, South China. It is a popular destination among Chinese tourists for its rich cultural heritage, delicious seafood, and scenic coastal views. The city is home to over 5 million people and boasts a vibrant nightlife, a great food scene, and many historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Shantou

Shantou offers visitors a mix of historical, cultural, and natural sights. Here are some of the top places to visit in Shantou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Chenghai Museum

The Chenghai Museum is an excellent stop for those interested in regional history. The museum houses more than 20,000 cultural artifacts, including pottery, jade carvings, and local handicrafts. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of the Hakka people, who have lived in the region for centuries.

The Maritime Silk Road Museum

The Maritime Silk Road Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of the Silk Road. The museum showcases artifacts and artwork from the ancient Maritime Silk Road, which connected China with Europe and Africa by sea. Exhibits include a replica of a Tang dynasty ship, which visitors can board.

The Guangji Bridge

The Guangji Bridge dates back to the Song dynasty and is one of the oldest stone bridges in Guangdong Province. The bridge is 372 meters long and is decorated with stone lions, dragons, and phoenixes. A walk across the bridge affords beautiful views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Shantou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The South Mountain

The South Mountain is a great spot for nature lovers. Visitors can hike up the mountain and enjoy panoramic views of Shantou’s coastline. The mountain is also home to the Shantou Zoo, which houses more than 300 animals.

Nan’ao Island

Nan’ao Island is a popular destination for beach-goers. The island has several clean and well-maintained beaches, including the Xiao Jin Sha Beach and the Da Jin Sha Beach. Visitors can also hike up the island’s highest peak, which offers stunning views of the surrounding sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shantou

Shantou has many hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The South Mountain Trails
  • The Liyuhe Cycling Path
  • The Nan’ao Island Coastal Trail

Visitors should make sure to bring sunscreen and plenty of water while hiking or biking, especially in the summer months.

Best Things to Do in Shantou: Activities and Tours

Shantou offers many activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Shantou:

  • Try the local seafood, which is fresh and delicious.
  • Watch a traditional Chinese puppet show at the Gong Ping Puppet Show Theatre.
  • Take a boat tour of the Nan’ao Island mangrove forests.
  • Visit the Shantou Grand Theatre, which hosts performances ranging from Chinese opera to contemporary dance.
  • Join a tour of the Hakka roundhouses, unique structures built by the Hakka people that feature fortified walls and an open-air courtyard.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shantou

For visitors who have a week to spend in Shantou, here is an itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Visit the Chenghai Museum and the Guangji Bridge.

Day 2: Explore the South Mountain and hike its trails.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of the Nan’ao Island mangrove forests and relax on the beach.

Day 4: Visit the Hakka roundhouses and learn about the lifestyle of the Hakka people.

Day 5: Visit the Maritime Silk Road Museum and have a picnic in the nearby park.

Day 6: Explore the Shantou Grand Theatre and watch a performance.

Day 7: Visit the Shantou Zoo and go shopping in the local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Shantou

Shantou is renowned for its seafood and local delicacies. Visitors should make sure to try some of the following dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Teochew beef hotpot: a spicy and flavorful hotpot made with beef, vegetables, and herbs.
  • Oyster omelette: a savory omelette made with fresh oysters, egg, and vegetables.
  • Chaozhou-style fish balls: fish balls made with minced fish and seasoning, typically served in a steaming hot broth.

Local Beverages

  • Shantou lychee wine: a sweet and fruity wine made from locally grown lychees.
  • Chaozhou Gongfu tea: a traditional Chinese tea made with special brewing techniques.

Where to Stay in Shantou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shantou has several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Shantou:

  • Shantou International Hotel: a luxury hotel located in the city center, with spacious rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Jinjiang Inn Shantou Hengshan Road: a budget hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Vatica Shantou Longhuai Fashion City: a modern hotel with convenient location for shopping and dining.

What to Buy in Shantou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a variety of souvenirs and local products in Shantou. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Teochew embroidery: delicate and colorful embroidery made with highly-skilled techniques that reflect the region’s culture and aesthetic.
  • Teochew snack boxes: a box filled with various traditional snacks that are savory or sweet.
  • Oyster sauce: Shantou’s oyster sauce has a strong flavor and is a popular condiment in Chinese cooking.

Local Culture and Customs of Shantou

Shantou locals have a deep appreciation for tradition and culture. Visitors should make sure to be respectful of local customs, such as removing shoes before entering homes or temples, and dressing modestly.

Best Time to Visit Shantou

The best time to visit Shantou is during the spring and autumn seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, while the winter is chilly with occasional rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shantou

Visitors to Shantou should keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Mandarin or Cantonese.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Spit in public or litter.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shantou

Shantou is generally a safe city for visitors. However, visitors should keep an eye on their belongings and be careful in crowded areas.

Health in Shantou

Visitors to Shantou should make sure to drink only bottled water and avoid street food that may not be properly cooked. Visitors should also consider getting travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Shantou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shantou

The nearest airport to Shantou is the Jieyang Chaoshan International Airport, which is about 35 kilometers away from the city center. Visitors can take a bus or taxi from the airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Shantou

Shantou has several bus stations with routes to nearby cities and towns. The Shantou East Bus Station is the largest and most frequently used station.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Shantou by train or ferry. The Shantou Railway Station provides regular services to major cities in China, while there are several ferry terminals that operate routes to Hong Kong and Macau.

How to Get Around in Shantou

Shantou has an efficient public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and metro lines. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Shantou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shantou is a great starting point for exploring nearby destinations. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Chaozhou: Known for its ancient Lingnan structures and distinct cuisine, Chaozhou is a must-visit for visitors interested in regional culture and history.
  • Xiamen: Located on the coast of Fujian province, Xiamen is a beautiful coastal city that blends traditional and modern architecture.
  • Meizhou Island: A scenic island located off the coast of Shantou, Meizhou Island is known for its crystal-clear waters, white sand beaches, and abundant marine life.

Conclusion

Shantou is a beautiful coastal city with a rich history and culture. Visitors to the city can explore a range of attractions, including museums, temples, natural parks, and scenic beaches. With its delicious food, excellent accommodation options, and efficient public transportation system, Shantou is a destination that should be on every visitor’s itinerary.
