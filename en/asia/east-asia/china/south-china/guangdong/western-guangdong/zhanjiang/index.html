Introduction to Zhanjiang

Zhanjiang is a coastal city located in Western Guangdong province, in Southern China. It has a rich history and culture, with many museums and historic sites to explore. Zhanjiang is also known for its natural beauty, with pristine beaches, lush forests, and scenic mountains. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or local cuisine, Zhanjiang has something for everyone!

List of Places to Visit in Zhanjiang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zhanjiang Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Zhanjiang at this comprehensive museum.
  • Yunfu Xinxing Ancient Town: Explore this well-preserved ancient town and see traditional architecture and daily life.
  • Maoming Taozhou Ancient City: Visit this ancient city, established in the Tang Dynasty, and learn about the history of the area.
  • Former Residence of Luo Ruiqing: Discover the life and legacy of this important Chinese Communist leader at his former home.

Natural Attractions of Zhanjiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Donghai Island: Relax on the pristine beaches of this island, which boasts crystal-clear waters and colorful coral reefs.
  • Dongshan Mountain Forest Park: Hike through the lush forests of this mountain park and enjoy stunning views of the city below.
  • Huguangyan Scenic Area: Visit this natural wonder, a volcanic crater lake surrounded by picturesque scenery.
  • Guilin Bay Mangrove Forest Park: Take a boat ride through this beautiful mangrove forest and admire the unique ecosystem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhanjiang

  • Lianjiangkou Mountain Trail: Hike to the top of this mountain and enjoy panoramic views of the city and the sea.
  • Zhanjiang Seashore Trail: Cycle along the coastline and take in the stunning ocean views along this scenic route.
  • Leizhou Peninsula Coastal Trail: Trek along this rustic route and explore the rugged coastline and small fishing villages.

Best Things to Do in Zhanjiang: Activities and Tours

  • Zheng Ancestral Hall Cultural Park: Attend a traditional lion dance performance and learn about Chinese culture at this historic site.
  • Zhanjiang Bay Bridge: Take a scenic drive across this iconic bridge and marvel at the engineering feat.
  • Xuwen Longevity Theme Park: Relax in the natural hot springs and learn about Chinese medicine at this wellness-focused attraction.
  • Zhanjiang Ocean Kingdom: Explore this aquatic wonderland, home to thousands of marine creatures.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhanjiang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Zhanjiang-style Seafood: Sample the freshest catches of the day at local seafood restaurants.
  • Liangbanfen: Try this refreshing dish, made with cold noodles and a variety of vegetables and meats.
  • Rice Porridge with Mixed Congee: Enjoy this hearty and healthy breakfast dish, made with rice and a variety of grains and seeds.

Local Beverages

  • Maotai Liquor: Sample this strong and aromatic Chinese liquor, considered a national treasure.
  • Zhanjiang Beer: Try the local brew at Zhanjiang Beer Company, one of the largest breweries in Southern China.
  • Herbal Tea: Sip on a cup of fragrant herbal tea and indulge in the health benefits of traditional Chinese medicine.

Where to Stay in Zhanjiang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Crowne Plaza Zhanjiang: Stay in this luxury hotel with panoramic views of the city and the ocean.
  • Zhanjiang Seaside Hotel: Enjoy a comfortable stay at this beachfront hotel with easy access to the water.
  • Zhanjiang Happy Stay Hostel: Save money and meet other travelers at this budget-friendly hostel in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Zhanjiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sea Salt: Take home a bag of high-quality sea salt, harvested from the crystal-clear waters of Donghai Island.
  • Jasmine Tea: Buy a fragrant box of jasmine tea, a specialty of Zhanjiang.
  • Coral Jewelry: Adorn yourself with beautiful jewelry made from locally sourced coral.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhanjiang

  • Respect for Elders: In Chinese culture, elders are highly respected and obeyed. Be sure to show deference and politeness to older people.
  • Gifting Etiquette: When giving gifts in China, it’s polite to refuse the gift at first before graciously accepting it.
  • Table Manners: It’s impolite to leave chopsticks upright in a bowl of rice, as it resembles incense at a funeral.

Best Time to Visit Zhanjiang

The best time to visit Zhanjiang is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cool and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhanjiang

Do:

  • Try local seafood dishes at a restaurant on the beach.
  • Visit cultural attractions, such as the Zhanjiang Museum and Yunfu Xinxing Ancient Town.
  • Take a boat ride through the Guilin Bay Mangrove Forest Park.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in temples or other religious sites.
  • Cause loud noise or commotion in public places.
  • Insult Chinese culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhanjiang

Zhanjiang is generally a safe city, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping your belongings secure.

Health in Zhanjiang

Health risks in Zhanjiang include air pollution and mosquitoes carrying the Zika virus. Be sure to protect yourself from mosquitoes by wearing repellent and covering up exposed skin.

Getting to Zhanjiang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhanjiang

  • Zhanjiang Airport: Located about 10 km east of the city center, this airport serves domestic flights within China.

Bus Stations Near Zhanjiang

  • Zhanjiang Central Bus Station: Located in the city center, this station serves buses to other parts of Guangdong province and nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Zhanjiang has a major train station with connections to major cities in China.
  • Ferry: There are ferries that connect Zhanjiang to other coastal cities in Guangdong province and Hainan Island.

How to Get Around in Zhanjiang

Public transportation options in Zhanjiang include buses and taxis. It’s also possible to rent bicycles in the city center for a more eco-friendly and enjoyable way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Zhanjiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Guangzhou: Travel to this bustling metropolis, known for its cuisine, shopping, and cultural attractions.
  • Sanya: Visit this tropical paradise on Hainan Island, with white sand beaches, clear waters, and a laid-back vibe.
  • Hong Kong: Explore this world-famous city, known for its impressive skyline, delicious food, and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Zhanjiang is a beautiful and culturally rich city, with plenty of attractions and activities to keep visitors entertained. Whether you want to explore historic sites, hike through lush forests, or relax on pristine beaches, Zhanjiang has something for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re ready to plan your trip to Zhanjiang and discover all that this vibrant city has to offer!
