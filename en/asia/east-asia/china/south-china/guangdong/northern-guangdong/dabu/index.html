Introduction to Dabu

Dabu is a small town located in the northern part of Guangdong, China. It is located between two major cities, Guangzhou and Shantou. Despite its small size, Dabu has a rich history and culture that attracts visitors from all over the world. In this guide, we will introduce you to the top attractions, food, and local culture of Dabu.

List of Places to Visit in Dabu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dabu Hakka Museum: This museum presents the history and culture of the Hakka people, including their architecture, customs, and clothing. The museum also showcases a collection of Hakka relics and artifacts.

  • Dabu Tongling Temple: The temple is one of the oldest in Dabu, with a history that dates back to the Tang Dynasty. It features unique architecture, including a five-story pagoda, and is a popular destination during traditional festivals such as the Lantern Festival.

  • Pingtian Village: This village is renowned for its unique architecture. Buildings are constructed with stone and wood, and all feature a distinctive cave-like entrance that protects the house from rain and wind.

Natural Attractions of Dabu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dabu Mountain National Geopark: This geopark covers an area of 104 square kilometers and features scenic views, natural scenery, geology, and cultural relics.

  • Lingguang Village: This village is located on the top of a mountain, surrounded by lush greenery. It is a popular destination for hiking, and visitors can enjoy scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Hucun Canyon Scenic Area: This area features a canyon with crystal clear water, surrounded by towering cliffs. Visitors can enjoy activities such as swimming, fishing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dabu

  • Dabu Mountain National Geopark: Visitors can hike several trails that showcase the natural beauty of the geopark, including the Luming Lake Trail, which leads to the Luming Lake, and the Thousand Step Corridor, which features scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Hucun Canyon Scenic Area: The Hucun Canyon trail is a popular hiking destination, with scenic views of the canyon and its crystal-clear water.

Best Things to Do in Dabu: Activities and Tours

  • Hiking and Climbing Tours: Visitors can enjoy hiking and climbing tours of the Dabu Mountain National Geopark, led by knowledgeable guides who can provide information about the park’s geology and history.

  • Biking Tours: Visitors can rent bikes and explore Dabu’s scenic countryside on two wheels.

  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can take cultural tours that showcase the local customs and traditions of Dabu, including visits to the Dabu Hakka Museum and Pingtian Village.

Food and Drink Scene of Dabu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stewed Chicken with Mushrooms: This dish is a local specialty, made from fresh mushrooms and slow-cooked chicken.

  • Hakka Deep-Fried Pork: This dish features tender and juicy pork that is coated in a crispy coating and served with a side of vegetables.

  • Pumpkin Rice Cake: This dessert features lightly sweetened pumpkin puree that is wrapped in a mochi-like rice cake.

Local Beverages

  • Longjing Tea: This tea has a light, nutty flavor and is grown in the surrounding hills of Dabu.

  • Lingguang Honey: This honey is made from bees that live in the Lingguang village, and is known for its unique flavor and health benefits.

Where to Stay in Dabu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dabu Golden Peak Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Dabu, and features comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves local cuisine.

  • Hucun Canyon Resort: This resort is located in the Hucun Canyon Scenic Area and features rooms with scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Lingguang Village Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the Lingguang Village, and features rooms with traditional Hakka architecture.

What to Buy in Dabu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hakka Embroidery: This embroidery is famous in Dabu, and features intricate designs and colorful threadwork.

  • Hakka Tea: This tea is grown in the surrounding hills of Dabu and is known for its unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Dabu

Dabu is known for its rich Hakka culture, which includes unique architecture, customs, and traditions. Visitors can witness traditional festivals such as the Lantern Festival and experience the hospitality of the local people.

Best Time to Visit Dabu

The best time to visit Dabu is from September to November when the temperature is comfortable, and the weather is clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dabu

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink

  • Visit the Dabu Hakka Museum

  • Learn about the local Hakka culture

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural environment

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting temples

  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Dabu

Dabu is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should still take precautions such as keeping their belongings safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Dabu

Visitors should be up to date on their routine vaccinations before traveling to Dabu. It’s also recommended to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Dabu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dabu

The closest airports to Dabu are Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport and Shantou Waisha Airport.

Bus Stations Near Dabu

There are several bus stations in Dabu, including the Dabu Bus Terminal and the Hucun Canyon Scenic Area Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Dabu by train or car. There are several highways that connect Dabu to other major cities in Guangdong.

How to Get Around in Dabu

The best way to get around in Dabu is by car or taxi. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Dabu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Meizhou: This city is known for its Hakka culture and architecture and is a short distance from Dabu.

  • Chaozhou: This city is famous for its ancient architecture and cuisine and is a great destination for foodies.

  • Shantou: This city is known for its bustling port and colorful architecture.

Conclusion

Dabu is a hidden gem in Guangdong that has much to offer visitors who are looking for natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious food. We hope this guide has inspired you to plan your trip to Dabu and experience all that this town has to offer.
