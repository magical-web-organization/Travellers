Introduction to Heyuan

Heyuan, located in Northern Guangdong, is a city known for its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to a number of historical sites, museums, and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both cultural enrichment and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more in Heyuan.

List of Places to Visit in Heyuan

Here’s a list of the top attractions in Heyuan that you shouldn’t miss on your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heyuan Museum: This museum is home to a collection of over 10,000 dinosaur fossils, making it the largest collection in China.

  • Dongyuan Prehistoric Site: This archaeological site dates back to over 5,000 years ago and is believed to have been the capital of the Baiyue tribe.

  • Taihe Ancient Cave: This cave is home to a number of ancient inscriptions and carvings dating back to the Han Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Heyuan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Luofu: This mountain range is famous for its natural beauty and is home to a number of hiking trails, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Xinfengjiang National Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to a number of rare and endangered species, including the Chinese alligator and the Chinese sturgeon.

  • Zijin Mountain Scenic Area: This area is home to a number of natural attractions, including waterfalls, hot springs, and ancient trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Heyuan

Heyuan is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the city:

  • Mount Luofu Hiking Trail: This trail takes you through the stunning Mount Luofu mountain range and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Xiaotianyu Scenic Spot Hiking Trail: This trail takes you through the Xiaotianyu Scenic Spot, which is home to a number of natural attractions, including waterfalls and caves.

  • Taihe Ancient Cave Hiking Trail: This trail takes you through the Taihe Ancient Cave and offers stunning views of the ancient inscriptions and carvings.

Best Things to Do in Heyuan: Activities and Tours

When in Heyuan, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy. Here are some of the best:

  • Rafting on the Xijiang River: The Xijiang River is an ideal destination for rafting, offering both calm waters and rapids.

  • Camping in the Zijin Mountain Scenic Area: The Zijin Mountain Scenic Area is an ideal destination for camping, offering scenic views and plenty of outdoor activities.

  • Visiting the Heyuan Museum: The Heyuan Museum is a must-visit for history enthusiasts, offering a comprehensive collection of dinosaur fossils.

Food and Drink Scene of Heyuan

Heyuan is known for its delicious cuisine and unique local drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Heyuan Spiced Goose: This dish is made with tender goose meat and a blend of traditional spices, making it a local specialty.

  • Baked Catfish: This dish features baked catfish that’s seasoned with a blend of spices and served with soy sauce and sweet and sour sauce.

  • Hakka Rice: This dish is a local staple, featuring steamed rice that’s mixed with a variety of vegetables and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Maojian Tea: This is a local variety of green tea that’s grown in the Mount Luofu mountain range and is known for its rich aroma and flavor.

  • Huangjiu: This is a type of Chinese rice wine that’s brewed and aged in traditional clay jars.

  • Heyuan Mango Juice: The mangos grown in Heyuan are renowned for their sweet and juicy flavor, making Heyuan mango juice a must-try local beverage.
