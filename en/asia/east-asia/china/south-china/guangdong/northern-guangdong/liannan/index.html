Introduction to Liannan

Located in northern Guangdong in South China, Liannan is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The county is home to picturesque landscapes, traditional villages, and a rich cultural heritage. With its perfect blend of the old and the new, Liannan has something to offer every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Liannan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maozhou Island: Located on the Pearl River, Maozhou Island is known for its fish farms and historic sites. Visitors can explore ancient temples, shrines, and watchtowers, as well as indulge in fresh seafood.
  • Yueyun Pagoda: A seven-story tower built in 1073 AD, the Yueyun Pagoda is an important cultural site in Liannan. Visitors can climb up to the top to get a panoramic view of the surrounding scenery.
  • Liannan Yao Cultural Museum: The museum is dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of the Yao ethnic minority in Liannan. Visitors can learn about the Yao’s traditional customs, dress, and handicrafts.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cloud Forest Park: A forest park located on the mountainous terrain of Liannan, Cloud Forest Park has beautiful scenery, including steep cliffs, flowing streams, and dense forests. The park is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and rock climbing.
  • Jiulong Valley: Famous for its waterfall, Jiulong Valley is a perfect spot for those wanting to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city. The valley has breathtaking landscapes, including a natural cave, and visitors can indulge in water activities such as swimming, diving, and fishing.
  • Daxie Village: Daxie Village is a traditional village, located in the Daxie River Valley, with terraced fields, lush greenery, and small brooks running through the peaceful countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liannan

  • Jiulong Valley Trail: A moderate hiking trail that takes visitors through the forests and cascading waterfalls of Jiulong Valley, this route is perfect for nature lovers.
  • Yelin Ancient Trail: A scenic biking trail, the Yelin Ancient Trail leads up from Pingshan Village, an ancient town with traditional buildings, to a mountaintop, providing visitors with panoramic views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Liannan: Activities and Tours

  • Night Boat Ride on the Xinfeng River: Take a boat ride at night on the Xinfeng River to see the beautiful scenery and the sparkling lights of the town.
  • Hiking to the Peak of Shikengkong: A challenging hike, visitors can climb to the peak of Shikengkong Mountain, which is over 1600m tall, and get a stunning view from the top.
  • Tour of Daxie Village: Visitors can take a leisurely walk around the picturesque Daxie Village, interact with the locals, and see traditional macadamia nut harvesting.

Food and Drink Scene of Liannan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yao Cuisine: Liannan is famous for its Yao cuisine, including Baoyu or stuffed fish and Nianshou, a pork and bamboo shoot soup. Other specialties include Yanping Rice and Luo Jian, a glutinous rice dumpling.
  • Macadamia Nuts: This local specialty is a must-try for foodies, as the town is famous for its production of high-quality macadamia nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Long’an Wine: This is a traditional rice wine made from glutinous rice and spring water, giving the wine a slightly sweet and floral flavor.

Where to Stay in Liannan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Liannan Crown Prince Hotel: A modern 4-star hotel in the heart of the town, the hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.
  • Yue Yun Hotel: Located in the Yueyun Pagoda area, this hotel offers spacious rooms, a banquet hall, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views.

What to Buy in Liannan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Macadamia Nuts: These nuts are renowned for their exceptional quality and are a popular souvenir that one can take back home. They are available in many shops and souvenir stores in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Liannan

The town has an ethnic diversity where the locals speak various dialects of Chinese, with the Yao minority being the major ethnic group. Being part of the Cantonese-speaking region, the locals are friendly and welcoming.

Best Time to Visit Liannan

The best time to visit Liannan is during the autumn and winter seasons, from October to March, when the climate is cool and dry, making it pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liannan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try local food and drink
  • Prepare for outdoor activities by bringing warm clothing and comfortable shoes

Don’t:

  • Avoid littering, and keep the environment clean
  • Do not take pictures or videos in places where it is prohibited.
  • Do not disrespect the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liannan

Liannan is considered to be one of the safest places in China. However, it is always advisable to take precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding going out in secluded areas at night.

Health in Liannan

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions before traveling to Liannan, such as taking vaccinations and consulting a doctor, especially if they plan to indulge in outdoor activities.

Getting to Liannan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liannan

The nearest airport to Liannan is Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, located approximately 238 km away.

Bus Stations Near Liannan

  • Liannan Bus Station
  • Liuxi County Bus Station

Other Options

  • Taxis and private cars can be arranged from Guangzhou to Liannan
  • Trains from Guangzhou South Railway Station can take visitors to Liannan.

How to Get Around Liannan

The best way to explore Liannan is to rent a bike or a car or hire a local guide if you want to go for a hike or go exploring nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Liannan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhaoqing: A city with beautiful scenery and historic sites, located approximately 100 km away from Liannan.
  • Guangzhou: A vibrant metropolis and the capital of Guangdong, located 217 km away from Liannan.

Conclusion

Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a food enthusiast, Liannan has something to offer. With its scenic landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Liannan should be on every traveler’s itinerary when visiting China.
