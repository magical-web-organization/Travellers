Introduction to Danxiashan

Danxiashan, located in Northern Guangdong, China, is a ridge of stunning red sandstone peaks and rugged cliffs with a rich history that dates back over 1300 years. It is home to several rare plant and animal species and is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and hikers.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your trip to Danxiashan, including where to go, what to see, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Danxiashan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-see attractions in Danxiashan is the Tannan Scenic Area, which features ancient temples, buildings, and inscriptions of significant cultural heritage importance. The Tannan Museum, located in the area, showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the local community.

Another popular attraction is the Xianglong Scenic Area, home to the Xianglong Temple, which was built in the early Tang Dynasty and has been beautifully preserved. The temple is dedicated to the Buddhist monk Xianglong and features intricate carvings and a stunning pagoda.

Natural Attractions of Danxiashan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Danxiashan is known for its natural beauty, and visitors can explore the area through hiking, rock climbing, and cycling. One of the most popular hikes is the Danxia Mountain Trail, a 5-mile trek that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Another breathtaking natural attraction is the Jinjiang River Scenic Area, which features breathtaking waterfalls, lush forests, and stunning rock formations. Visitors can also go rafting, fishing, and hiking in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Danxiashan

Danxiashan has several hiking and biking trails, each offering stunning views of rolling hills, forests, and valleys. The area’s terrain is challenging, and visitors must come prepared with sturdy hiking and biking shoes.

Some popular hiking trails include the Yangtianhu Trail, the Danxia Mountain Trail, and the Xianglong Trail. Mountain biking can be enjoyed on scenic routes, including the Chengbei Scenic Area or the Shiniuzhai Scenic Area.

Best Things to Do in Danxiashan: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other things to do in Danxiashan, such as:

  • Visit the Tannan Scenic Area Museum, which showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the local community.
  • Explore the Xianglong Scenic Area, home to the Xianglong Temple, which was built in the early Tang Dynasty and has been beautifully preserved.
  • Visit the Jinjiang River Scenic Area for rafting, fishing, and hiking.
  • Attend local fairs and festivals, where you can taste delicious local cuisines, buy souvenirs, and experience the rich culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Danxiashan

If you are planning to visit Danxiashan for a week, here is the suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Spend these days exploring the Tannan Scenic Area and the Xianglong Scenic Area.
  • Day 3: Hike the Danxia Mountain Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Day 4: Visit the Jinjiang River Scenic Area and go rafting, fishing, and hiking.
  • Day 5: Attend local fairs and festivals and experience the rich culture of the area.
  • Day 6-7: Relax and explore the local area.

Food and Drink Scene of Danxiashan

Danxiashan offers a variety of local delicacies that visitors must try, from spicy Sichuan-style vegetables to Hot and Sour Soup. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spicy Sichuan-style vegetables
  • Hot and Sour Soup
  • Cantonese-style steamed fish
  • Danxia roasted pig

Local Beverages

  • Erguotou (a type of Chinese liquor)
  • Xiajing (a local alcohol brewed from rice)

What to Buy in Danxiashan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Danxiashan can buy a variety of locally made products and souvenirs, including:

  • Local handicrafts, such as stone carvings, pottery, and embroidery
  • Tea and tea-related products
  • Spices, herbs, and other flavors to take home

Local Culture and Customs of Danxiashan

Danxiashan is a unique cultural destination with a rich history and traditions. Visitors should respect local customs, dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and ask for permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Danxiashan

The best time to visit Danxiashan is from October to December when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its peak. January and February see crisp temperatures that create frozen waterfalls and ethereal ice formations. Summer might not be the best time to visit as the temperature can reach up to 35°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Danxiashan

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and respectfully when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local delicacies and tea.
  • Carry cash as it may not be possible to use credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs of people without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Danxiashan

Danxiashan is a safe destination for travelers; however, visitors should take necessary precautions while hiking and exploring the area.

Health in Danxiashan

Make sure to carry necessary medication, insect repellent, and drinking water to ensure you stay healthy during your trip.

Getting to Danxiashan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Danxiashan

The nearest airport to Danxiashan is Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, which is about 107 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Danxiashan

There are several bus stations near Danxiashan, including Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Hong Kong.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Shaoguan City, which is about 12 miles away from Danxiashan.

How to Get Around in Danxiashan

The best way to get around Danxiashan is on foot, by bike or by car, and visitors can hire a taxi to take them around the area.

Where to Go Next from Danxiashan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Danxiashan is close to several other major cities and attractions, including Shaoguan City, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen.

Visitors can take a day trip to explore some of these cities, including:

  • Enjoy Cantonese cuisine in Guangzhou.
  • Shop for souvenirs or visit theme parks in Shenzhen.
  • Visit historic sites in Shaoguan City, including the Beiluo Ancient City and Nanhua Temple.

Conclusion

Danxiashan is a hidden gem in Northern Guangdong, China, and a unique destination for nature enthusiasts and hikers. It offers a range of activities, from hiking to exploring ancient temples and enjoying delicious local cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide at your disposal, you can plan your trip to Danxiashan and experience the natural beauty and rich culture of the area.
