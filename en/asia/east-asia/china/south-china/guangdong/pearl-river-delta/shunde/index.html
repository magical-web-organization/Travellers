Shunde Travel Guide: The Ultimate Trip Planner

Located in the picturesque Pearl River Delta of Guangdong, Shunde is a cultural and historical hub in China. Despite being relatively lesser-known to many international tourists, the city is a hidden gem worth exploring. With its well-preserved ancient towns, natural landscapes, and mouth-watering cuisine, Shunde offers a diverse range of experiences to travelers. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on an exciting journey through the best places to visit, hike/bike, eat, stay, shop, and more, in Shunde.

List of Places to Visit in Shunde

Whether you’re an avid history buff or nature enthusiast, Shunde has plenty of attractions to pique your interest. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Shunde:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Qinghui Garden: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Qinghui Garden is a tranquil oasis of Classical Chinese architecture, with stunning gardens, ponds, and pavilions.
  • Shunde Museum: This museum showcases the cultural roots and artistic history of Shunde with a vast collection of exhibits dating back to ancient times.
  • Xinjiang Battle Memorial: Visit this site to learn about the significant role Shunde played in the Xinjiang battle against Japanese aggression, with well-preserved war relics, air-raid tunnels, and a military command post.

Natural Attractions of Shunde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shunde Mount Danxia: This scenic park features stunning geological formations known as ‘Danxia Landforms’ that create a visual feast of rocky cliffs and red sandstone peaks. One of the most popular features of Mount Danxia is the walkway that leads around the main peaks, providing magnificent views of the area.
  • Nanpuxi: A scenic and peaceful getaway from bustling city life, Nanpuxi is the perfect place to enjoy the serene surroundings and natural scenery. Located close to Foshan, you can also take a stroll around the beautifully preserved ancient village on the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shunde

For adventurous travelers, Shunde offers many hiking/biking trails that take you through some of the most beautiful landscapes and picturesque villages. Here’s a list of some top routes to try:

  • Shunfengshan Park: This park is situated near the city and offers a challenging hiking trail that leads to the top of Feng Chan Shan’s rugged peak. This trail provides stunning vistas of the Pearl River Delta and the surrounding countryside.
  • Nanhai Baofeng Mountain: An excellent choice for hikers of all levels, this mountain range is blessed with magnificent scenery, lush forests, and crystal-clear streams.
  • Shunde Lake: For nature lovers, this serene lake offers an easy hiking and biking trail that encircles the lake, providing a peaceful oasis for a pleasant day out.

Best Things to Do in Shunde: Activities and Tours

Besides exploring the cultural and natural landmarks, there are plenty of fun things to do in Shunde. Here are some of the best activities to experience:

  • Shunde Food Circle: Shunde is famous for its excellent cuisine, and the best way to discover it is by taking a Shunde Food Circle tour. This tour takes you around the city’s best food spots, street vendors, and hidden eateries, allowing you to taste the local flavors.
  • Fengshui Tour: Shunde is known for its unique Fengshui culture, and you can explore this fascinating aspect of the city with a Fengshui tour. Learn about the principles of Fengshui and discover how they influence the construction and layout of the city’s buildings.
  • Shunde Painting Academy: This academy is Shunde’s pride and joy and offers a unique opportunity to learn traditional Chinese painting with guidance and instruction from renowned master painters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shunde

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help plan your trip to Shunde:

Food and Drink Scene of Shunde

Shunde’s gastronomic scene is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse range of seafood, street snacks, and legendary desserts on offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Laskin Chicken: A famous Shunde specialty, Laskin chicken is a cook-to-order dish that is prepared with fresh, high-quality chicken meat, aromatic herbs, and a secret sauce that elevates the flavors to a whole new level.
  • Shunde Fried Oyster Cake: A crispy oyster pancake with an oyster filling, topped with shallots and chili garlic sauce, is a popular street food that’s easily available.
  • Mingxinhong Pork Sausage: Made using the freshest pork and soft glutinous rice, this signature dish of Shunde is a perfect blend of sweet and savory flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Shunde Chrysanthemum Tea: A classic and refreshing tea, Shunde Chrysanthemum Tea is a natural detoxifier and tonic that’s perfect to cool down on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Shunde: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Shunde, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • City Comfort Inn (Shunde Ronggui): If you’re looking for comfort, convenience, and a great value-for-money experience, then City Comfort Inn in Shunde Ronggui is an excellent choice for you.
  • Deity Hotel: Rated as one of the best hotels in Shunde, Deity Hotel is a luxurious 5-star property with stunning views, modern amenities, and excellent hospitality services.
  • Thousand Light Lake Resort: This resort is located on the scenic Thousand Lights Lake and offers a tranquil atmosphere and comfortable stay among nature.

What to Buy in Shunde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shunde is a great place to pick up some unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some top options:

  • Shunde Silk Embroidery: A traditional and famous handicraft of Shunde, the Silk Embroidery is a stunning piece of artwork that showcases local artisans’ skill and craftsmanship.
  • Shunde Mahjong Set: Mahjong is a popular game in Shunde, and you can find beautifully crafted Mahjong sets made from bamboo and ivory that make meaningful souvenirs.
  • Shunde Snacks: Shunde’s food scene is not only limited to restaurants but also has a rich array of street snacks and delicacies that you can take home, such as beef and pork floss, crispy dragon beard candy, and much more.

Local Culture and Customs of Shunde

Shunde is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, with a unique blend of Canton, Hakka, and Fengshui influences. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it’s common to greet each other with a hearty smile and a handshake. Respect for the elderly and authority is an integral part of the culture.

Best Time to Visit Shunde

The best time to visit Shunde is during the autumn months (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and the scenic views are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shunde

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Shunde:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Carry an umbrella or a hat for protection from the sun and rain.
  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in some street snacks.
  • Bargain while shopping in local markets – it’s a common practice.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places – it is prohibited by law.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting religious or conservative areas.
  • Disregard traffic signals or regulations while driving or crossing the road.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shunde

Shunde is a relatively safe city, with low levels of crime and violence. However, it’s advisable to take standard safety precautions, such as safeguarding your valuables, being vigilant in public areas, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Shunde

While Shunde is a safe and clean city, travelers should still take precautions such as carrying a first-aid kit, drinking only bottled water, and practicing good hygiene habits such as washing hands frequently.

Getting to Shunde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shunde is well-connected by air, train, bus, and taxi services. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Shunde

  • Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport: located 55 kilometers from Shunde and has daily flights to major domestic and international destinations.
  • Foshan Shadi Airport: located 33 kilometers from Shunde and has daily flights to several domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Shunde

  • Shunde Coach Terminal: located in the heart of Shunde and has buses to and from major cities in China.
  • Foshan Nanhai Coach Station: located 17 kilometers from Shunde and has buses to some of the most popular tourist destinations in China.

Other Options

  • Train: Shunde is well-connected by train services, with convenient links to other major cities in China.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Shunde, and the fares are relatively affordable.

How to Get Around in Shunde

The best way to get around Shunde is by public transport, such as bus or subway services. Taxis are also readily available and are a good option for short trips. Another fun and eco-friendly way to explore Shunde is to rent bikes, which are available for hire at several locations throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Shunde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have the time, you can explore some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Shunde, such as:

  • Foshan: A cultural and historical city that’s famous for its ceramics, martial arts, and numerous temples.
  • Guangzhou: The capital of Guangdong and one of the most vibrant and dynamic cities in China, is a cultural melting pot, with a rich history, excellent food scene, and modern skyscrapers.
  • Macau: An hour’s drive from Shunde, Macau is a former Portuguese colony that blends European and Chinese cultures, with world-class entertainment options and casino resorts.

Conclusion

Shunde is a city that offers a mix of history, culture, nature, and culinary delights that make it a unique destination in China. We hope this travel guide helps you plan your perfect trip to Shunde, and you enjoy your stay in this beautiful and welcoming city.
