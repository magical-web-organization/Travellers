Introduction to Shenzhen

Located in the heart of the Pearl River Delta in Guangdong province in South China, Shenzhen is a popular tourist destination known for its modern architecture, thriving technological sector, and scenic landscapes. It was once a small fishing village, but it has since transformed into one of China’s fastest-growing cities and a global economic powerhouse for manufacturing and technology.

Shenzhen is also a cultural hub, home to many museums, art centers, and theme parks. The city offers visitors a myriad of activities and attractions to explore, making it an excellent destination for a memorable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Shenzhen

There are many attractions to see and explore in Shenzhen. Here are some of the most popular:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Shenzhen Museum: This museum is home to over 20,000 pieces of art, sculptures, and antiques that showcase the rich history and culture of Shenzhen and China.
  2. Deng Xiaoping Statue: Deng Xiaoping is a famous leader in Chinese history who played a critical role in the country’s modernisation. A visit to his statue provides insight into his legacy and contributions to modern China.
  3. Dapeng Ancient City: This historic walled city was built in the 14th century and has since been lovingly restored to give visitors an authentic glimpse into ancient China.
  4. Zhongshan Park: This park is home to the beautiful Zhongshan Monument and is a great place for a stroll through the gardens.
  5. OCT-LOFT Creative Culture Park: This multicultural district boasts art galleries, museums, exhibitions and much more, showcasing global art and creative talent.

Natural Attractions of Shenzhen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wutong Mountain: Standing tall at 944 meters, Wutong Mountain is the highest peak in Shenzhen and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city that are simply stunning.
  2. Dameisha and Xiaomeisha Beaches: These beautiful beaches offer visitors a perfect place to relax, play in the ocean, and enjoy beautiful sunsets.
  3. Nanshan Mountain: This scenic spot is located in the bustling Nanshan district and offers beautiful views of Shenzhen Bay and Hong Kong.
  4. Shenzhen Safari Park: This park is an animal paradise where you can see all types of animals from around the world, such as tigers, lions, and pandas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shenzhen

Shenzhen has some of the most exciting and scenic routes for hikers and bikers alike. Here are some of the top picks:

  1. Dameisha Circuit: This 22 km path is perfect for both hikers and bikers and goes along the beautiful coastline of Shenzhen, with sea views throughout the entire route.
  2. Nanshan Mountain Hike: The Nanshan Mountain hike is a challenging trail that rewards hikers with stunning views of the coast and surrounding city.
  3. Wutong Mountain Hike: This hike is a bit less challenging than Nanshan, but equally rewarding with some of the most spectacular views of the city and coastline.
  4. Sha Tau Kok Hike: Located in eastern Shenzhen, this hike offers visitors the chance to see beautiful views of the surrounding countryside, mountains and small villages dotted along the way.

Best Things to Do in Shenzhen: Activities and Tours

  1. Window of the World: This theme park is home to replicas of world-famous landmarks and tourist attractions from around the globe.
  2. Shenzhen Happy Valley: This amusement park is a must-visit for families and thrill-seekers alike, with rides and games for all ages.
  3. Dafen Oil Painting Village: This colorful neighborhood is home to over 1,200 artists and craftsmen, creating works of art from scratch in their workshops. Visitors can buy a unique souvenir or even commission their own painting.
  4. Dafo Buddhist Temple: This magnificent temple is one of the most important in Southern China and is a must-visit for travelers looking to immerse themselves in local Chinese culture and customs.
  5. Shenzhen Bay Park: This bustling park is a favorite among locals and is a great place for walks or picnics with stunning views of Shenzhen Bay.
  6. Nanshan District: This district is home to high-tech souvenir shops, restaurants with panoramic views, and plenty of nightlife entertainment options.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shenzhen

Day 1 - Visit the Shenzhen Museum and check out the nearby Deng Xiaoping Statue before heading to the Dapeng Ancient City for an immersion in history and culture.

Day 2 - Spend the day on the stunning beaches of Dameisha and Xiaomeisha where you can relax or take a dip in the refreshing waters.

Day 3 - Head to Nanshan Mountain to enjoy a spectacular view of Shenzhen Bay and the surrounding mountainous areas.

Day 4 - Take a visit to the famous Window of the World, and wonder at the replicas of famous landmarks around the world.

Day 5 - Explore the various exhibitions and museums of the OCT-LOFT Creative Culture Park.

Day 6 - Take a trip to the magnificent Shenzhen Bay Park and the adjacent Coastal City Shopping Mall to enjoy some shopping and entertainment.

Day 7 - Visit the Dafo Buddhist Temple on the outskirts of Shenzhen, an important cultural attraction in southern China.

Food and Drink Scene of Shenzhen

When it comes to food and drink, Shenzhen has plenty to offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Dim Sum: Whether you want dumplings or buns, Shenzhen has some of the best dim sum dishes in China.
  2. Snake Soup: This traditional dish hails from Guangdong province and is a delicacy in Shenzhen.
  3. Wonton Noodles: This popular breakfast meal is one of the staples of Chinese cuisine and a must-try when in Shenzhen.
  4. Claypot Rice: A hearty and filling meal, made with rice, meat, and vegetables, cooked in a clay pot to give it an authentic and robust flavour.
  5. Cantonese-style Roast Goose: This dish is a favorite among locals, well-loved for its crispy skin and juicy flesh.

Local Beverages

  1. Daxia Riverside Beer: Made with mineral-rich water from the Daxia River, this beer is a popular and refreshing drink in Shenzhen.
  2. Dragonfly Tea: This fragrant tea can be found all around Shenzhen and comes in a variety of flavours.
  3. Baishamen Boatman Liquor: This locally-produced liquor is a type of Chinese white wine that is brewed with water from the local mountains and has a unique taste.

Where to Stay in Shenzhen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shenzhen has a wide variety of accommodations for all kinds of travelers. Here are some recommended options:

Hotels:

  1. Four Points by Sheraton Shenzhen: Located in the city center and close to many major attractions.
  2. Huaqiang Plaza Hotel Shenzhen: A luxury hotel in the bustling Huizhou district.
  3. Grand Mercure Shenzhen Oriental Ginza: A comfortable and cozy hotel located in the Futian Central Business District.

Hostels and Guesthouses:

  1. Shenzhen Old Street Youth Hostel: This charming hostel offers both private and dormitory-style accommodations.
  2. GreenTree Inn Shenzhen Dongmen: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city right next to many popular shopping centers.
  3. The Bauhinia Hotel (Fairy Bridge): A budget-friendly boutique-style hotel near the city’s famous attractions.

What to Buy in Shenzhen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shenzhen is a cultural hub with vibrant markets, shops, and stores selling a range of unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the top recommendations:

  1. Electronics: Shenzhen is known as the Silicon Valley of China and its electronics market is famed for quality and pricing.

  2. Tea: Dragonwell and Longjing tea from Hangzhou area can be found in the many Tea shops around town.

  3. Arts and Crafts: From oil paintings to pottery, from wood carvings to jewelry, Shenzhen has something for everyone’s budget and style.

  4. Pamphlets and Books: To learn more about Shenzhen or China, or for language learners, pamphlets and books are available in many stores around the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Shenzhen

Shenzhen is a melting pot of cultures, with a mix of western and eastern traditions. Tipping is not expected, but it is appreciated. When visiting temples or other religious sites, appropriate dress is essential. Shenzhen is also a clean and environmentally-conscious city, so littering and smoking are strictly forbidden.

Best Time to Visit Shenzhen

The best time to visit Shenzhen is from October to December when temperatures are mild, and the skies are clear. The spring season from late March to May is also great when the vegetation is in full bloom, and temperatures are still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shenzhen

Do:

  1. Be respectful of Chinese culture and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine and take time to shop and explore local markets.
  3. Take public transportation to reduce the impact of CO2 emissions and explore the city like a local.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or smoke in public spaces.
  2. Don’t take photographs of people without their express permission.
  3. Don’t engage in disruptive, disrespectful or aggressive behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shenzhen

Shenzhen, like most cities in China, is safe and secure with low levels of crime. It’s still a urban environment, so one should always be aware of your surroundings and avoid poorly lit areas, isolated streets or be aware of their belongings.

Health in Shenzhen

Healthcare in Shenzhen is generally high-quality and modern, with both Western and traditional medical facilities available. Make sure you have up-to-date health insurance and travel documents in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Shenzhen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shenzhen is a bustling city with many transportation options for visitors. Here are some helpful tips:

Airports Near Shenzhen

  1. Shenzhen Bao’an International Airport - 40 km from the heart of the city, with plenty of connections to all Chinese cities and world destinations.

Bus Stations Near Shenzhen

  1. Shenzhen Bus Station: Located in the city center, it offers direct connections to major cities in China.
  2. Shenzhen North Bus Station: Located in the Bao’an district, it is a good option for visitors heading outside of the city.

Other Options

Other ways to get to Shenzhen include trains, ferries, and taxis, but these are not recommended for first time visitors to China who may not be familiar with the transport system. A rental car is another option, but not always convenient as parking is scarce and expensive in Shenzhen.

How to Get Around in Shenzhen

Getting around Shenzhen is easy and affordable, with many transportation options available, including the bus, subway, and taxis. Biking and walking are also excellent ways to explore the city and take in the sights and sounds of everyday life.

Where to Go Next from Shenzhen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Guangzhou: This vibrant city is just a one-hour train ride away and offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the culture and history of the region.

  2. Hong Kong: Located just across the border, Hong Kong is a bustling metropolis and a shopper’s paradise with excellent dining options and attractions.

  3. Zhuhai: This beautiful seaside city is just a short ferry ride away and offers visitors a chance to unwind and explore the coastline and quaint city center.

Conclusion

Shenzhen is a fascinating and dynamic city with a rich culture and history, beautiful scenery, and exciting attractions. This comprehensive travel guide has highlighted some of the best things to see and do in Shenzhen, along with practical tips and recommendations for visitors. Plan your visit to Shenzhen today and immerse yourself in the unique blend of modern and traditional Chinese culture that this beautiful city has to offer.
