East is a vibrant and bustling city located in the Pearl River Delta in Guangdong, Southern China. The city is known for its modern skyscrapers, bustling markets, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the best things to do, places to visit, food and drink scene, local culture, and customs of East.

Introduction to East

East is a city that offers visitors plenty of options to explore. From ancient cultural heritage to modern skyscrapers, nature lovers to foodies, East has it all. It is a city that blends the old with the new; visitors can explore ancient temples and museums or enjoy the modern shopping centers and theme parks. The area surrounding East is known for its natural beauty, with hills and waterways attracting hikers, cyclists, and kayakers.

List of Places to Visit in East

Visitors to East are spoilt for choice with attractions to explore; below is a list of must-visit places in East.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shenzhen Museum

Shenzhen Museum is a great place to learn about the history of the city. The museum has a large collection of artefacts that display the city’s transformation over the years.

Dapeng Fortress

Dapeng Fortress is a well-preserved ancient fortress that dates back to the Ming Dynasty. It is located near the ocean and has stunning views of the bay.

Diwang Mansion

Located in the heart of East, Diwang Mansion is a towering 69-floor skyscraper. The top floors of the building have a viewing platform that offers stunning views of the city.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wutong Mountain

Wutong Mountain is the highest peak in the area, standing at 944 meters high. Visitors can enjoy hiking the mountain trails or take a cable car to the top to enjoy the panoramic views.

Fairy Lake Botanical Gardens

Fairy Lake Botanical Gardens is a beautiful park that is home to over 10,000 species of plants. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll around the gardens, boat ride, or picnic by the lake.

Dameisha Beach

Located on the eastern outskirts of East, Dameisha Beach is a beautiful sandy beach that stretches over two kilometers. Visitors can enjoy sunbathing, swimming, and taking part in water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East is surrounded by beautiful hills and waterways that offer visitors plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Below are the top hiking/biking routes in East.

Wutong Mountain Hike

Wutong Mountain hike is a challenging hike that takes visitors to the highest peak in the area. The trail is steep, and it takes about three hours to reach the top.

East Lake Bike Trail

East Lake Bike Trail is a scenic 20-kilometer bike trail that starts at East Lake and winds its way through beautiful countryside and lakes.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

East is a city that offers visitors plenty of activities to do and tours to explore. Below are some of the best things to do while in East.

Shenzhen Theme Parks

East is home to several world-class theme parks that offer visitors endless fun and entertainment. Some of the most famous ones include Happy Valley, Splendid China Folk Culture Village, and Window of the World.

The East Kayaking Tour

The East Kayaking Tour is a fun way to explore the waterways in the area. Visitors can take guided tours that take them through winding rivers and beautiful countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

Below is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to East.

Day 1: Visit East Museum and Dapeng Fortress Day 2: Hike Wutong Mountain and visit Diwang Mansion Day 3: Experience Shenzhen Theme Parks Day 4: Visit Fairy Lake Botanical Gardens and Dameisha Beach Day 5: Explore East Kayaking Tour Day 6: Visit East Art Museum and East Science and Technology Museum Day 7: Shop and eat in the Luohu Commercial Center

Food and Drink Scene of East

East is known for its diverse food culture. Visitors can try everything from street food to fine dining restaurants. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in East.

Must-Try Dishes

Dim Sum

East is a city famous for its Dim Sum, small steamed or fried dumplings filled with meat, vegetables or seafood.

Char Siu

Char Siu is a Cantonese-style BBQ pork that is often eaten with rice, noodles or sandwiches.

Local Beverages

Tsingtao Beer

Tsingtao Beer is a local beer made in Shandong Province, China. Visitors can enjoy the beer in local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Below are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in East.

Grand Hyatt Shenzhen

The Grand Hyatt Shenzhen is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of East. The hotel offers visitors stunning views of the city skyline and boasts an excellent restaurant and spa service.

Shenzhen Green Oasis Hotel

The Shenzhen Green Oasis Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel that offers visitors comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a central location.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East is known for its excellent shopping options. Visitors can buy everything from electronics to clothes to souvenirs. Below are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Handicrafts

Handicrafts such as calligraphy art, silk embroidery, and carvings are excellent souvenirs to bring home from East.

Tea & Coffee

Tea and coffee are popular products in East. Visitors can buy everything from traditional Chinese tea to locally roasted coffee.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East is a city that has retained much of its traditional culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the locals, learn about the culture and take part in local festivities.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is from September to November and from March to May when the weather is favorable, and the crowds are lighter.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Do:

  • Learn basic Chinese phrases.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough cash, as some places do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Throw trash on the streets.
  • Wear revealing or suggestive outfits in public.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East is a safe city, and visitors can move around without fear. However, visitors should be cautious while in crowded places and avoid carrying valuables in public.

Health in East

Visitors should take precautions while eating street food and drink bottled water. Visitors should also carry necessary medication and a medical kit, and purchase travel insurance before heading to East.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

East can be reached by air or by road. Below are the best transportation options and logistics to get to East.

Airports Near East

The nearest airport to East is Shenzhen Bao’an International Airport, which is about 35 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near East

Visitors can also reach East by bus. The most popular bus stations are the Luohu Bus Station and the Futian Bus Station.

How to Get Around in East

East has an efficient public transportation system comprising buses, taxis, and the metro. Visitors can purchase a transport card that allows them to access all the public transport options.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East is a city that is well-connected to other parts of Guangdong Province. Visitors can head to Guangzhou, Zhuhai or Macau, all of which are within a few hours’ travel time.

Conclusion

East is a city that has something for everyone. From ancient cultural heritage to modern skyscrapers, nature lovers to foodies, East has it all. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs, learn about the culture, and take part in local festivities. We hope this comprehensive tour guide helps visitors plan their trip and make the most of their time in East.
