Introduction to Kaiping

Kaiping is a beautiful city located in Jiangmen, in Pearl River Delta, in Guangdong, in South China. It is rich in culture and history, with a number of historical sites and natural attractions that are worth exploring. The city is known for its unique architecture, including the famous Kaiping Diaolou, which are fortified multi-story watchtowers made of brick or stone.

Our ultimate travel guide to Kaiping will cover everything you need to know before visiting this beautiful city. From the best places to visit, to the food to eat, and where to stay, we’ve got you covered. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Kaiping

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kaiping has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore. Here are a few of our top picks:

1. Kaiping Diaolou and Villages

The Kaiping Diaolou and Villages are a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring over 1,800 different structures. These fortified buildings were built between the 16th and 20th centuries and are a unique combination of Chinese and Western architectural styles.

2. Li Garden

Li Garden is a beautiful example of traditional Chinese gardening. It was built in the early 20th century and features beautiful pavilions, rockeries, and ponds.

3. Xinhui Confucian Temple

The Xinhui Confucian Temple is one of the oldest and largest Confucian temples in China. It was first built in the 11th century and rebuilt several times over the centuries.

Natural Attractions of Kaiping: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kaiping is also home to a number of beautiful natural attractions. Here are a few of our top picks:

1. Ma-Xianglong Scenic Area

The Ma-Xianglong Scenic Area is a beautiful park that features a number of different hiking trails and scenic viewpoints. It is also home to the Ma-Xianglong Tower, which offers amazing views of the surrounding countryside.

2. Tanjiang River

The Tanjiang River is a beautiful and serene river that runs through Kaiping. There are a number of different cruises and boat tours available that allow you to explore the river and surrounding area.

3. Jiangmen Underground Neolithic Site

The Jiangmen Underground Neolithic Site is a fascinating archaeological site that dates back to the Neolithic period. It is believed to be one of the largest and most important prehistoric sites in southern China.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaiping

Kaiping is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are a number of different hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are a few of our top picks:

1. Tanjiang River Biking Trail

The Tanjiang River Biking Trail is a beautiful 16-kilometer route that follows the Tanjiang River. It is a great way to explore the countryside and take in the beautiful scenery.

2. Ma-Xianglong Scenic Area Hiking Trail

The Ma-Xianglong Scenic Area Hiking Trail is a challenging 7-kilometer trail that takes you through the beautiful Ma-Xianglong Scenic Area. It is a great way to explore the park and take in the beautiful views.

3. Xinhui Beach Biking Trail

The Xinhui Beach Biking Trail is a scenic 30-kilometer route that follows the coastline of the Xinhui District. It is a beautiful and relaxing way to explore the area.

Best Things to Do in Kaiping: Activities and Tours

Kaiping has a number of great activities and tours to choose from. Here are a few of our top picks:

1. Diaolou Tour

A Diaolou tour is a great way to explore the unique architecture of Kaiping. There are a number of different tours available that take you to different Diaolou throughout the city.

2. Tanjiang River Boat Tour

A Tanjiang River Boat Tour is a great way to explore the beautiful scenery along the river. There are a number of different tours available that range in length and price.

3. Traditional Chinese Tea Ceremony

A traditional Chinese tea ceremony is a great way to experience Chinese culture. There are a number of different tea shops throughout Kaiping that offer these ceremonies.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaiping

Must-Try Dishes

Kaiping is known for its delicious food, with a number of unique dishes to try. Here are a few of our top picks:

1. Sanbu

Sanbu is a popular Kaiping dish made of rice noodles, ginger, garlic, and pork. It is a delicious and filling meal that is perfect for lunch or dinner.

2. Shuangpi Noodle Soup

Shuangpi Noodle Soup is another popular Kaiping dish. It is made with handmade noodles, pork, mushrooms, and a variety of herbs and spices.

3. Huangqiao Sesame Cake

Huangqiao Sesame Cake is a popular dessert in Kaiping. It is made with glutinous rice flour, sesame seeds, and sugar, and is a delicious way to end a meal.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Kaiping has some great options. Here are a few to try:

1. Kaiping Rice Wine

Kaiping Rice Wine is a local specialty, made from glutinous rice and distilled in earthenware jars. It is a strong and flavorful drink that is perfect for sipping.

2. Cantonese Tea

Cantonese Tea is a popular beverage in Kaiping. It is typically served with dim sum and is a great way to experience Chinese culture.

Where to Stay in Kaiping: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kaiping has a number of great accommodation options to choose from. Here are a few of our top picks:

1. Chikan International Hotel

The Chikan International Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Kaiping. It features a number of amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and restaurant.

2. Yingde Hotel

The Yingde Hotel is a great budget-friendly option in Kaiping. It offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location near many of the city’s top attractions.

3. Kaiping Diaolou Lin Du Lou Inn

The Kaiping Diaolou Lin Du Lou Inn is a charming guesthouse located in the heart of the Kaiping Diaolou and Villages. It offers traditional Chinese-style rooms and a peaceful setting.

What to Buy in Kaiping: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

2. Kaiping Rice Wine

3. Handmade pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Kaiping

If you’re visiting Kaiping, it’s important to be aware of the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be respectful when visiting temples and other sacred sites.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Respect elders and authority figures.

Best Time to Visit Kaiping

The best time to visit Kaiping is between October and December, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The springtime, from March to May, is also a great time to visit, with pleasant weather and beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaiping

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaiping

Kaiping is generally a safe city for travelers. However, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid theft and other crimes. It’s also a good idea to carry a copy of your passport and to leave valuables in a secure location.

Health in Kaiping

Kaiping has a number of hospitals and medical facilities available to travelers. However, it’s important to take precautions to avoid illness, such as drinking bottled water and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Kaiping: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaiping

The closest airport to Kaiping is Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, which is located about 140 kilometers away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or shuttle bus to Kaiping.

Bus Stations Near Kaiping

Kaiping has a number of different bus stations, including the Kaiping Bus Station, which offers regular services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

There are also a number of train stations in the area, with regular services to major cities in China. You can also rent a car or hire a driver to explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Kaiping
