Introduction to Liwan

Liwan is a culturally-rich district located in Guangzhou, southern China. Historically speaking, this district has been an important trading hub dating back to the 7th century. Today, visitors can experience a blend of the old and the new that coexists in its streets. The district is home to a number of historical buildings, museums, temples, shopping streets, and outdoor attractions. This comprehensive guide will take you through everything you need to know about exploring Liwan.

List of Places to Visit in Liwan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chen Clan Academy

Built in 1888, the Chen Clan Academy is a premire example of local Lingnan architecture. It now serves as the Guangdong Folk Art Museum which displays a collection of local folk art, porcelain, and carving arts.

Liwan Museum of History and Culture

The museum covers 5,000 years of Chinese history, with a focus on the Lingnan area. Historical artifacts and exhibits detailing the cultural, social and economic development of Liwan are on display.

Lizhiwan Park

A hidden gem in Liwan, this park’s dense forests and greenery offer a natural respite from the bustling city. It also features an ancient altar to Wu Zu and a 4-story pagoda.

Natural Attractions of Liwan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Baiyun Mountain

Baiyun Mountain offers stunning views of the Pearl River Delta and the city, along with a variety of activities such as hiking, rock climbing, and camping.

Liwanhu Park

Located near the Pearl River, Liwanhu Park features scenic walkways, gardens, and a boating lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liwan

Litchi Bay Creek to Shangxiajiu Street

This route takes visitors from Litchi Bay Creek through the heart of Liwan to the shopping district of Shangxiajiu Street. A great option for exploring the cultural and historical landmarks of the district.

Baiyun Mountain Hiking Trails

The trails surrounding Baiyun Mountain offer a mix of easy and difficult terrain, and are suitable for biking or hiking.

Best Things to Do in Liwan: Activities and Tours

Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

A lively shopping street that winds through the core of the historical city center, showcasing the best of traditional Cantonese culture with unique handicrafts, local snacks, and street performances.

Cantonese Opera performances

Liwan is the birthplace of Cantonese opera, and there are several local theaters to take in a performance. The Cantonese Opera Art Museum is also worth a visit for those less familiar with the art form.

Food and Drink Scene of Liwan

Must-Try Dishes

Shunde-style Cuisine

Popular dishes include twice-cooked pork, roast goose, and tofu pudding.

Dim Sum

A Cantonese staple, dim sum can be found throughout Liwan, including the famous dim sum restaurant Taotaoju.

Local Beverages

Cantonese Tea

Liwan is known for its special blend of Cantonese tea, which is a refreshing and healthy beverage that is perfect for any time of day.

Coconut Water

Coconut water can be found in kiosks throughout Liwan as a refreshing and healthy snack.

Where to Stay in Liwan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Liwan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Liwan is a great place for picking up souvenirs, with various markets and stores offering crafts, jewelry, traditional clothing, tea, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Liwan

Liwan is a traditional district where customs and culture have been preserved for centuries, and visitors should respect the locals’ way of life. Traditional structures are a product of its rich history and the inhabitants of Liwan take pride in its culture and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Liwan

The best time to visit Liwan is between October and December, when the weather is cool and pleasant, and the skies are clear. Spring (March to May) is also beautiful, but can be rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liwan

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Pack for different weather conditions
  • Respect the local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter or show disrespect to local landmarks or shrines
  • Bargain too aggressively when shopping, it is considered impolite

Safety and Health

Safety in Liwan

Liwan is generally a safe place to travel, with low crime and violence rates. It is always recommended to take common sense precautions when travelling anywhere.

Health in Liwan

The tap water in Liwan is not recommended for consumption, so be sure to use bottled water for drinking and brushing teeth. Local hospitals can be found across the city, but private medical facilities may be more expensive.

Getting to Liwan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liwan

Guangzhou can be easily reached via the Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, which is located 28km (17 miles) away from Liwan. Shuttle busses and cabs are available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Liwan

The Guangzhou city bus network is extensive and connects to the major districts of the city. Several buses stop at Liwan.

Other Options

Taxis and shuttle services are popular methods of travel around Liwan and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Liwan

Liwan is a fairly compact district, and most popular sites are within walking distance. Travelers can rent bicycles to cover more distances or take local buses, taxis or ride-hailing services.

Where to Go Next from Liwan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Foshan City

Just 40 minutes away from Liwan is the historic and culturally-rich Foshan City, home of the Ancestral Temple and the Foshan Lion Dance.

Guangzhou City Center

The bustling and ultra-modern Guangzhou City Center is just a bus or taxi ride away, boasting plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Liwan is an incredible district with a mix of natural, cultural, and historical attractions worth exploring. Whether you’re interested in hiking, shopping, or dining, there is something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide and plan your trip to Liwan today!
