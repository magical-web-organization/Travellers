Introduction to Bengbu

Bengbu is a city in Anhui province, located in South-central China. The city’s name translates to “Oyster Wharf” in English, as it was one of the biggest centers of freshwater pearl cultivation in China. Bengbu is known for its rich cultural heritage, mouth-watering cuisine, and stunning natural sights. With so many attractions on offer, Bengbu is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore East Asia.

List of Places to Visit in Bengbu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bengbu is steeped in history and culture, making it the perfect destination for those interested in exploring China’s cultural heritage. Some of the must-see museums and historical places in Bengbu include:

  • Bengbu Museum: Bengbu Museum is a treasure trove of historical artifacts that provides a glimpse into the city’s rich cultural heritage. From fossils and pottery to ancient calligraphy and jade, the museum has something for everyone.

  • Huaihe River Cultural Park: Huaihe River Cultural Park is a sprawling parkland that celebrates Bengbu’s connection with the mighty Huaihe River. The park is home to several historical monuments and cultural artifacts, including the Chuzhou Tower, a 200-year-old pagoda, and the Zhongdu City Wall, which dates back to the Ming dynasty.

  • Yuhua Temple: Built over a thousand years ago, Yuhua Temple is one of the most important Buddhist temples in the region. The temple complex comprises several ornate prayer halls, towering pagodas, and tranquil gardens that make it a serene oasis in the heart of Bengbu.

Natural Attractions of Bengbu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bengbu is renowned for its breathtaking natural scenery, lush forests, and crystal-clear lakes. Some of the top natural attractions in Bengbu include:

  • Nanhu Lake Park: Nanhu Lake Park is a scenic park that surrounds the city’s centerpiece, Nanhu Lake. The park features paved walkways, colorful gardens, and scenic viewpoints that offer stunning vistas of the lake, making it a perfect spot for a lazy afternoon stroll or a romantic picnic.

  • Bengshan National Forest Park: The Bengshan National Forest Park is a massive nature reserve that extends over 51 square kilometers. The park is home to over 390 species of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for hikers, nature lovers, and bird watchers. Some of the park’s top attractions include the dramatic karst formations, crystal-clear waterfalls, and serene bamboo groves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bengbu

Bengbu is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore China’s stunning natural landscapes on foot or bike. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Bengbu include:

  • Chuzhou Mountain: Chuzhou Mountain is a 1100-meter peak located in the heart of Bengbu. The mountain is a popular destination for hikers and mountain climbers as it offers sweeping vistas of the city and its surrounding landscape.

  • Nanhu Lake Bike Trail: For those looking for a leisurely ride, the Nanhu Lake Bike Trail is the perfect choice. The trail winds its way around the perimeter of Nanhu Lake, providing breathtaking views of the lake and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Bengbu: Activities and Tours

Bengbu is a vibrant city that offers an array of exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Some of the best things to do in Bengbu include:

  • Pearl Farm Tour: Take a tour of Bengbu’s famous pearl farms and learn about the ancient art of freshwater pearl cultivation. Visitors can witness the entire pearl cultivation process, from harvesting of the pearls to the sorting and grading of the pearls.

  • Chinese Calligraphy Workshop: Bengbu is renowned for its calligraphy, and visitors can take a workshop to learn the art of Chinese calligraphy. The workshops are hosted by local experts who teach visitors the basics of Chinese calligraphy and provide tips on how to create beautiful, intricately detailed characters.

Food and Drink Scene of Bengbu

Bengbu’s cuisine is famous for its bold flavors and fresh ingredients. Visitors to Bengbu can sample an array of local delicacies, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braised Shrimps with Scallion: A popular Bengbu dish, Braised Shrimps with Scallion features succulent shrimp cooked in a savory mixture of scallion, soy sauce, and spices.

  • Three Delicacies of Bengbu: Three Delicacies of Bengbu is a local specialty that features a delicious combination of braised beef, pork belly, and tofu.

  • Liangpi: Liangpi, or cold skin noodles, is a refreshing summer dish made of hand-pulled noodles, cucumber, and a spicy dressing made of sesame paste, vinegar, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Bengbu Pearl Milk Tea: Bengbu’s famous pearl milk tea is a must-try for tea lovers visiting the city. The creamy, sweet milk tea is studded with chewy pearls made of tapioca.

  • Baijiu: Baijiu is a potent Chinese liquor that is celebrated for its distinct flavor and aroma. Bengbu is known for its premium baijiu, which is made using traditional techniques and locally sourced ingredients.

  • Ibis Hotel Bengbu: A budget-friendly option, Ibis Hotel Bengbu offers clean and comfortable rooms and a convenient location in the city center.

What to Buy in Bengbu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bengbu is known for its unique handicrafts, local snacks, and premium teas. Some of the best souvenirs to bring back from Bengbu include:

  • Pearls: Bengbu is one of the biggest producers of freshwater pearls in China, and visitors can purchase a range of pearl jewelry, including necklaces, earrings, and bracelets.

  • Chalk Carvings: Bengbu is famous for its intricate chalk carvings, which are made using special techniques and locally sourced chalk. Visitors can purchase these stunning carvings as a unique souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Bengbu

Bengbu has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditional festivals, religious practices, and daily life. Visitors to the city can experience the local culture by participating in one of the many festivals, such as the Huaihe River Dragon Boat Festival and the Bengbu International Kite Festival.

Best Time to Visit Bengbu

The best time to visit Bengbu is between March and November, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city is at its liveliest. The city is also home to several festivals and events during this time, making it a perfect time to experience the local culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bengbu

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including the famous Bengbu braised shrimp and pearl milk tea.
  • Visit the local markets and souks to experience the vibrant atmosphere and pick up some souvenirs.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, such as removing your shoes when entering a temple or other sacred place.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or customs, such as by wearing inappropriate clothing or behaving in an offensive manner.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bengbu

Bengbu is a relatively safe city for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid pickpocketing and be wary of their personal belongings in public places.

Health in Bengbu

Visitors to Bengbu should be aware of the high pollution levels in the city and take precautions to avoid pollution-related health issues, such as wearing a mask when necessary and avoiding outdoor activities during smog alerts.
