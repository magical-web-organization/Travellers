Food and Drink Scene of Hefei

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stinky Tofu - A beloved Chinese street food, stinky tofu is a fermented tofu dish that is deep-fried and seasoned with spicy sauce. It has a strong aroma but a surprisingly mild taste.

  2. Braised Pork Belly - This succulent pork dish is slow-cooked in soy sauce, sugar, ginger, and other spices until it is melt-in-your-mouth tender.

  3. Fried Rice Cakes - These crispy, chewy rice cakes are a popular breakfast food in Hefei. They are stir-fried with soy sauce, scallions, and other seasonings.

Local Beverages

  1. Huangshan Maofeng Tea - This high-quality green tea comes from the Huangshan Mountain region and is known for its light, floral aroma and refreshing taste.

  2. Huoshan Huangya Tea - Another popular tea variety from Anhui Province, this yellow tea is made from pure tea buds and has a mild, nutty flavor.

Where to Stay in Hefei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Four Points by Sheraton Hefei, Baohe - This upscale hotel is located in Hefei’s central business district and offers stylish rooms and suites, an on-site restaurant, and a spa.

  2. Hefei Shushan Yuelu Villa Resort - This resort is located in the peaceful Shushan area and offers charming guestrooms, a scenic outdoor pool, and a restaurant that serves local cuisine.

  3. Hefei Urban Backpackers Hostel - This budget-friendly hostel is located in Old Hefei and offers private and dormitory-style rooms, a communal kitchen, and a rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Hefei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hui Ink Stones - These exquisite ink stones are a traditional Chinese calligraphy tool made from local Hui Rock. They are highly sought after for their fine texture and durability.

  2. Gujing Gongjiu - This famous Chinese liquor is made in Anhui Province and has a distinct floral aroma and a sweet, mellow taste. It is often served at banquets and special occasions.

  3. Anhui Tea - Anhui Province produces several famous tea varieties, including Huangshan Maofeng Tea, Huoshan Huangya Tea, and Qimen Hongcha (Keemun Black Tea).

Local Culture and Customs of Hefei

The people of Hefei are proud of their heritage and deeply influenced by traditional Chinese culture. Visitors should always show respect for local customs and traditions, including taking off shoes before entering someone’s home and greeting elders with a bow or nod.

Best Time to Visit Hefei

The best time to visit Hefei is from April to October, when the weather is mild and comfortable. However, visitors should be aware that summers can be hot and humid, with occasional typhoons and heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hefei

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine - Hefei is known for its delicious street food and traditional dishes.

  2. Explore the city on foot - Hefei’s historic streets and charming neighborhoods are best explored on foot.

  3. Learn about local culture - Take the time to visit museums and historical sites to learn more about Hefei’s rich cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water - Always drink bottled water to avoid getting sick.

  2. Argue or haggle aggressively - Chinese culture values harmony and respect, so it is best to avoid confrontations.

  3. Wear revealing clothing - While Hefei is a modern city, it is still important to dress modestly and respectfully.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hefei

Hefei is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas and should take precautions with their valuables.

Health in Hefei

Visitors should be aware that air pollution levels can be high in Hefei, especially in winter. It is recommended to wear a mask if sensitive to air pollution. Visitors should also be cautious of food safety and avoid eating raw or undercooked meat or seafood.

Getting to Hefei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hefei

  1. Hefei Xinqiao International Airport - Located about 30 minutes by car from downtown Hefei, the airport offers direct flights to major cities in China and some international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Hefei

  1. Hefei South Bus Station - Located about 20 minutes by car from downtown Hefei, the station offers regular coach services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  1. Train - Hefei Railway Station is a major transportation hub that offers high-speed rail services to major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

How to Get Around in Hefei

Hefei has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and a metro network. Visitors can purchase a rechargeable IC card to use on buses and the metro, making it easy and convenient to travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from Hefei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Huangshan Mountain - This stunning natural landmark is located about 4 hours by bus from Hefei and is known for its spectacular landscapes and scenic hiking trails.

  2. Taiping Lake - This tranquil lake is located about 3 hours by bus from Hefei and is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and hiking.

Conclusion

Hefei is a vibrant and dynamic city that offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, there is always something exciting to see and do in Hefei. We hope that this comprehensive guide has given you all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Hefei, China.
