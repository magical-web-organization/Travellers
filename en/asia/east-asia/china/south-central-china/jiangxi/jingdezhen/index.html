Introduction to Jingdezhen

Jingdezhen is a small city located in the south-central province of Jiangxi in China. Known as the “Porcelain Capital” of the world, Jingdezhen has a long history of producing high-quality porcelain ceramics that are famous across the globe. The city is surrounded by mountains, rivers, and lush greenery, making it a beautiful destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Jingdezhen

Jingdezhen has many culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures to enjoy. Here are some of the must-visit destinations in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jingdezhen Ceramic Historical and Cultural Museum: Discover the history of Jingdezhen’s porcelain industry, see amazing collections of historical pottery, and learn about the ancient production techniques used by local craftspeople.
  • Changnan Kiln Site: Visit the site of an ancient ceramic kiln dating back to the Song Dynasty (960-1279 AD) and witness the ruins of the kiln and ancient pottery-making tools.
  • Imperial Kiln Museum: Explore the imperial kiln, which produced porcelain exclusively for the Chinese royal family during the Ming (1368-1644 AD) and Qing (1644-1911 AD) Dynasties.

Natural Attractions of Jingdezhen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lushan Mountain Range: Hike through the beautiful Lushan Mountain range, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests, hills, and valleys.
  • Wanfu Lake: Go boating, fishing, kayaking or swimming at Wanfu Lake, which is surrounded by the stunning natural beauty of Jingdezhen.
  • Sanbao International Porcelain Art Village: Take a stroll through this beautiful village featuring local ceramics artists’ studios, where porcelain pieces are turned into masterpieces.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jingdezhen

For adventure enthusiasts, Jingdezhen offers several excellent hiking and biking routes, including the Lushan Circle Trails, Jingdezhen to Yaoli Ancient Village Trails, and the Jingdezhen to Sanbao Porcelain Art Village Trails.

Best Things to Do in Jingdezhen: Activities and Tours

  • The Porcelain Experience: Create your masterpieces of porcelain pottery in workshops in Jingdezhen, as per ancient methods.
  • Visit the local Night Market: Jingdezhen night market is bustling with many stalls selling ceramics, souvenirs, handmade crafts, and local street food.
  • Explore the city’s bustling streets: Stroll around the city’s vibrant streets lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jingdezhen

Day 1: Visit Jingdezhen Ceramic Historical & Cultural Museum Day 2: Visit Changnan Kiln Site and the Imperial Kiln Museum Day 3: Hike through Lushan Mountain range Day 4: Visit Wanfu Lake and Sanbao International Porcelain Art Village Day 5: Explore the city’s vibrant streets, cafes and restaurants Day 6: Attend a Porcelain Experience workshop Day 7: Visit the Night Market

Food and Drink Scene of Jingdezhen

The food scene in Jingdezhen is a mix of traditional Chinese cuisine and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Porcelain Chicken: a delicious preparation of chicken stewed in a pot made of porcelain.
  • Xianliang Mian: A Jingdezhen noodle dish with minced meat and vegetables that is a popular street food in the city.
  • Huajuan: a traditional savory snack made with flour, sugar, and meat, commonly served for breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Longxue Tea: A type of green tea produced in Jingdezhen that is known for its long, twisting leaves and fruity flavor.
  • Porcelain Wine: A traditional wine that is fermented and aged in porcelain jars, giving it a unique, floral aroma and taste.

What to Buy in Jingdezhen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jingdezhen is famous for its porcelain ceramics, and visitors can purchase ceramics throughout the city. Other popular souvenirs and local products to bring home include hand-woven bamboo baskets, tea, and exquisite silk products.

Local Culture and Customs of Jingdezhen

Jingdezhen’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in the city’s rich ceramic history. Many locals still follow ancient traditions and customs, especially during traditional festivals like the Spring Festival, Dragon Boat Festival, and Mid-Autumn Festival.

Best Time to Visit Jingdezhen

The best time to visit Jingdezhen is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city’s natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jingdezhen

Do:

  • Respect local culture and traditions.
  • Carry cash as many places don’t accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other holy places.
  • Use chopsticks to point at things.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jingdezhen

Jingdezhen is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions against petty crime, such as pickpocketing.

Health in Jingdezhen

Visitors should drink only boiled or bottled water, avoid uncooked food, and follow standard hygiene practices to avoid any health risks while traveling in Jingdezhen.

Getting to Jingdezhen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jingdezhen

  • Jingdezhen Luojian International Airport: Jingdezhen’s main airport that connects the city to various domestic destinations in China.

Bus Stations Near Jingdezhen

  • Jingdezhen Bus Station: Located in the city center, which provides bus services to various domestic destinations in China.

Other Options

  • High-Speed Train: The city is connected to China’s high-speed train network, with daily services connecting Jingdezhen to major cities like Shanghai, Beijing, and Guangzhou.

How to Get Around Jingdezhen

Jingdezhen has an extensive network of buses and taxis providing rapid and reliable connectivity throughout the city. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Jingdezhen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jingdezhen is surrounded by many exciting destinations, including the ancient village of Yaoli, Lushan National Park, and Poyang Lake.

Conclusion

Jingdezhen is a city that is not just about porcelain ceramics but is also home to many cultural, natural, and historical attractions. From outdoor adventures to learning about ancient pottery-making techniques, Jingdezhen is a must-visit for anyone traveling to China. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their visit and explore everything that this beautiful city has to offer.
