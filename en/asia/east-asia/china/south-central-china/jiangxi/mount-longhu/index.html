A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Mount Longhu, China

Mount Longhu is a scenic mountain range in Jiangxi Province, South-central China. Considered one of the four holy mountains of Taoism, Mount Longhu boasts a rich cultural heritage in addition to its natural beauty. With stunning views, hiking trails, temples, and museums, Mount Longhu has something for everyone.

Introduction to Mount Longhu

Mount Longhu, also known as Dragon Tiger Mountain, is located in Yingtan City and has been listed as a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site since 2008. It is a popular destination for tourists seeking natural beauty and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Longhu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Longhu Palace: Longhu Palace is a Taoist temple and the most famous attraction on Mount Longhu. The temple dates back to the Song Dynasty and is known for its unique architecture, beautiful gardens, and ancient trees.
  2. Dragon and Tiger Mountain Scenic Area Museum: The museum showcases the history, culture, and natural beauty of Mount Longhu. It is a great place to learn about Taoism and the local folklore.
  3. Huashan Rock Painting: The Huashan Rock Painting is a UNESCO World Heritage site and features prehistoric rock paintings dating back 3,000 years.

Natural Attractions of Mount Longhu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Huilong Valley: Huilong Valley is a scenic area situated at the foot of Mount Longhu. It is known for its waterfall, pools, and lush vegetation, making it a popular place for hiking and picnicking.
  2. Lianhua Peak: Lianhua Peak is the highest peak on Mount Longhu and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Cable Car Ride: Take a cable car ride to the summit of the mountain to experience breathtaking views of the entire area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Longhu

  1. Huilong Valley Trail: This trail is a moderate level hike and takes approximately 2 hours to complete.
  2. Tianhuangping Trail: This trail is a more challenging route and takes approximately 4.5 hours to complete, offering you spectacular views of Mount Longhu.

Best Things to Do in Mount Longhu: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit Dragon and Tiger Mountain Scenic Area
  2. Take a walk along the Huilong Valley Trail
  3. Take a dip in one of the natural pools of Huilong Valley
  4. Witness the sunrise at Lianhua Peak
  5. Take a cable car ride to the summit of the mountain

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Longhu

Day 1 Arrive in Yingtan City and settle into accommodations. Visit the Dragon and Tiger Mountain Scenic Area Museum in the afternoon.
Day 2 Hike Huilong Valley Trail and visit Huilong Valley scenic area.
Day 3 Take a cable car ride to the summit of Mount Longhu and witness the stunning views.
Day 4 Visit Longhu Palace and explore the unique architecture and beautiful gardens.
Day 5 Explore the Lianhua Peak scenic area and witness the sunrise.
Day 6 Visit Huashan Rock Painting and learn about its history and significance.
Day 7 Depart from Yingtan City.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Longhu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hakka Stuffed Tofu: A local specialty dish featuring tofu stuffed with pork mince.
  2. Lianhua Baijiu: A local liquor made from fermented rice and sorghum.

Local Beverages

  1. Green Tea: Mount Longhu is known for its high-quality green tea.
  2. Jasmine Tea: The area is also known for its aromatic jasmine tea.

Where to Stay in Mount Longhu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Mount Longhu Shangri-La Resort: A luxury hotel with beautiful views of the mountain.
  2. Dragon and Tiger Mountain Villa: A mid-range accommodation option with convenient access to the scenic area.
  3. Huilong Valley Scenic Area Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option located within the scenic area.

What to Buy in Mount Longhu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Dragon and Tiger Mountain Tea: High-quality tea grown on the mountain.
  2. Lianhua Baijiu: A popular local liquor.
  3. Handmade Pottery: Pottery made by local artisans using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Longhu

Mount Longhu is a sacred mountain in Taoism, and visitors should be respectful of the local traditions and customs. The local Hakka culture is also an essential part of the area’s heritage and should be respected as well.

Best Time to Visit Mount Longhu

The best time to visit Mount Longhu is in the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. The summer months (June to August) can be scorching and crowded, while the winter months (December to February) are cold and often snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Longhu

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately: Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  2. Learn about the local culture: Take the time to learn about Taoism and Hakka culture before visiting the area.
  3. Respect the environment: Visitors should take care not to litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs: Visitors should be respectful of the local traditions and customs.
  2. Be loud or disruptive: Show respect to other visitors and to the environment by keeping noise levels down.
  3. Leave litter: Keep the environment clean and tidy by disposing of any rubbish properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Longhu

Visitors should take care when hiking the trails around Mount Longhu and should always follow safety guidelines. Make sure to carry enough water, wear suitable hiking shoes and carry a mobile phone with you. Visitors should also take care when swimming in natural pools in the area.

Health in Mount Longhu

Visitors should take care when consuming food and water in the area. Stick to bottled or boiled water and avoid consuming street food or foods that may have gone bad.

Getting to Mount Longhu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Longhu

The nearest airports to Mount Longhu are:

  1. Jingdezhen Airport: Located about 60 km away from Mount Longhu.
  2. Nanchang Changbei International Airport: Located about 200 km away from Mount Longhu.

Bus Stations Near Mount Longhu

There are several bus stations in Yingtan City that serve Mount Longhu, including:

  1. Yingtan Bus Station: Located in the downtown area of Yingtan City.
  2. Yingtan West Bus Station: Located in the western part of Yingtan City.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Mount Longhu from Yingtan City.

How to Get Around in Mount Longhu

Visitors can get around Mount Longhu by bus, taxi, or on foot. There are also cable car rides available to take visitors to the summit of the mountain.

Where to Go Next from Mount Longhu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Wuyuan County: Known for its picturesque villages and stunning countryside, located about 80 km away from Mount Longhu.
  2. Sanqing Mountain: A popular scenic spot located about 150 km away from Mount Longhu.

Conclusion

Mount Longhu is a beautiful and culturally significant destination that offers visitors a unique experience of natural beauty and local heritage. With its stunning landscapes, ancient architecture, and rich history, it is a must-see destination for travelers to China. Plan your trip to Mount Longhu today and experience all the area has to offer!
