Introduction to Ruijin

Ruijin is a city located in South-central China, specifically in the Jiangxi Province. It is known as the “Red Capital of China” due to its significant role in the Chinese Communist Revolution. Ruijin has become an important tourist destination for its rich cultural heritage and natural scenic spots.

Visitors can explore the city’s history and learn about its role in shaping the Chinese government’s structure. Additionally, hiking, biking, and outdoor activities also make Ruijin a perfect destination for adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Ruijin

Discover the top attractions and must-visit destinations in Ruijin.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ruijin Revolutionary Memorial Museum: This museum is dedicated to displaying the cultural and historical heritage of Ruijin. It showcases the role of Ruijin in the Chinese Communist Revolution and the creation of New China.
  • Zhou Enlai Memorial Hall: It is the former residence of Zhou Enlai, the first Premier of the People’s Republic of China. The site is now a museum showcasing his personal life and political career.
  • Yutang Revolutionary Site: This site was the location of the Yutang Conflict, a critical event in Chinese history during the Chinese Revolution. The site offers visitors a glimpse into the Chinese Communist Party’s history.

Natural Attractions of Ruijin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ganjiang River: This river flows through Ruijin and is an ideal spot for boating and river rafting activities. The surrounding scenery is stunning, with lush greenery and forests.
  • Laozu Mountain Scenic Area: Enjoy exploring nature at Laozu Mountain Scenic Area by hiking through the lush mountains and scenic views of the Ganjiang River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ruijin

Ruijin offers several trails for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Ruijin include:

  • Zhuangyuan Mountain Hiking Trail: This trail offers a medium-level hiking experience that takes visitors through towering pine trees, natural waterfalls, and stunning vistas of the city’s landscape.
  • The Ancient Chaoshan Path: This hiking trail passes through ancient architectural sites, with over 60 archaic bridges from different dynasties of China.

Best Things to Do in Ruijin: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Ruijin:

  • Visit the Local Markets: Ruijin’s markets offer visitors the opportunity to experience local food, clothing, and art. These markets provide insight into the city’s unique culture and traditions.
  • Sunrise at Laozu Mountain Scenic Area: Start your day by hiking into the Laozu Mountain Scenic Area to watch the sunrise.
  • Spa at Wenquan Resort: Unwind and rejuvenate yourself at the Wenquan Resort’s famous hot springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Ruijin

Ruijin is known for its unique cuisine and drinks. Here are some dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rice Noodles (Mi Fen): Ruijin’s signature dish, made from rolled rice noodles served hot with fresh vegetables and meat.
  • Sha Qi Ma: A fried pastry covered in sesame and maltose syrup.
  • Ya Tui: Ruijin’s famous barbecue dish made of marinated chicken skewers.

Local Beverages

  • Ganjiang Tuo Liquor: Ganjiang Tuo Liquor is a famous alcoholic beverage in Ruijin. It is made from glutinous rice, corn, wheat, and the local water.

Where to Stay in Ruijin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ruijin offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some recommended options:

  • Wenquan Resort Hotel: Located in the scenic area, the hotel offers relaxing hot spring baths, distinctive rooms, and breathtaking views.
  • Ruijin Golden Grain Business Hotel: Located in the city center, this hotel is an excellent option for business travelers as it offers conference and meeting rooms.
  • Ruijin Jinhui Hotel: This hotel offers a comfortable and luxurious stay with its state-of-the-art facilities, spacious rooms, and excellent services.

What to Buy in Ruijin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some of the best souvenirs and local products from Ruijin:

  • Red Army Long March Statue: These statues depict the soldiers from the Chinese Red Army.
  • Rice Noodles: Take home some locally made Ruijin Mi Fen.
  • Hemp Fabric: Ruijin is famous for producing high-quality hemp fabrics.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruijin

Ruijin is deeply rooted in Chinese Communist culture and heritage. It is polite to greet people with “Ni hao” or “Nin hao” in more formal settings. Respect the older generations and their traditions as they hold a significant value of the city’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Ruijin

The best time to visit Ruijin is from March to May and September to November. During these months, the city has pleasant weather, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 27°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruijin

Do:

  • Respect cultural heritage sites and monuments in the city.
  • Try local cuisine and explore local markets.
  • Be friendly and respectful towards locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful towards the country’s past or political system.
  • Don’t litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ruijin

Ruijin is a relatively safe city, and the crime rate is low. However, you should take standard precautions such as avoiding walking alone in remote areas after dark and always being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Ruijin

Tap water in Ruijin is not drinkable, so be sure always to buy bottled water. Moreover, the city is not heavily polluted, so concerns about air pollution are minimal.

Getting to Ruijin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ruijin is connected to major cities by different means of transportation, including:

Airports Near Ruijin

  • Ganzhou Huangjin Airport: Located approximately 1 hour 30 minutes away from Ruijin by car.

Bus Stations Near Ruijin

  • Ruijin Bus Station: Located in the city center, it offers intercity buses to all major destinations.

Other Options

  • Taxis and Private Hires: Taxis and private car hires are widely available.

How to Get Around in Ruijin

Ruijin offers numerous transportation options to visitors, including buses, taxis, and private cars. Taxis are relatively affordable, and the city’s center is also conveniently walkable.

Where to Go Next from Ruijin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ruijin is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations, including:

  • Ganzhou: This city is known for its rich cultural heritage, and it offers visitors great history and cultural sites.
  • Fujian Tulou: Located in the Fujian Province, these ancient Chinese dwellings are impressive and are listed as World Heritage Sites by UNESCO.

Conclusion

Ruijin offers visitors a unique cultural experience and opportunities to explore nature. From the scenic mountain ranges, the picturesque Ganjiang River to the historical Museums and attractions, Ruijin has something for everyone. Plan a visit to Ruijin to explore its unique culture and stunning scenery.
