Introduction to Lushan

Lushan, located in South-central China, is a picturesque city famous for its scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The city sits at an altitude of 1,500 meters above sea level and offers a pleasant climate year-round. Lushan is also regarded as the birthplace of Chinese Taoism, and you can witness its influence throughout the city.

List of Places to Visit in Lushan

Lushan is home to several cultural and natural attractions that you should not miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lushan Museum: With more than 20 exhibition halls, the museum has a collection of over 70,000 pieces of natural and cultural relics.
  • White Deer Cave Academy: This renowned academy served as a meeting place for scholars of the Tang Dynasty.
  • Yunju Temple: One of the most impressive Buddhist temples in China, Yunju Temple is known for its vibrant frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Lushan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lushan National Park: The park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to 99 peaks, 18 waterfalls, and impressive mountain landscapes.
  • Botanical Garden: It is the largest botanical garden in Asia and home to over 3,000 plant species.
  • Lushan Lulin Lake: This scenic lake has some excellent hiking trails and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lushan

Lushan has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the region.

Hiking Routes

  • Lushan National Park: There are several hiking trails in the park that vary according to their difficulty level.
  • Dahuaishu Scenic Area: This scenic area has a mix of paved and dirt trails perfect for hiking.
  • Lushan Botanical Garden: The garden offers both easy and challenging trails for hikers.

    Biking Routes

  • Xianrendong: This biking trail is ideal for experienced mountain bikers and offers challenging terrain.
  • Qianyuan Mountain: The trail offers a mix of uphill and downhill cycling and boasts stunning mountain views.
  • MGB Bike Trail: This stunning trail is an excellent option for beginners and takes you around the beautiful Lushan National Park.

Best Things to Do in Lushan: Activities and Tours

  • Tianqiao Ancient Street: This famous street is a must-visit and is renowned for its traditional architecture and delicious street food.
  • Tea Picking and Tasting: Lushan is famous for producing high-quality tea, and you can go tea picking and tasting at several tea plantations.
  • Cable Car Ride: Take a cable car ride to the top of Mount Lu to get fantastic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Yamabiko Forest Park: Get an adrenaline rush by zip-lining and trying other adventure activities at the park.
  • Lushan Hot Springs: Take a soothing dip in one of Lushan’s numerous hot springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Lushan

In Lushan, you will be spoilt for choice when it comes to food and drink.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lushan Green Tea Duck: This dish is made by cooking duck with Lushan’s famous green tea.
  • Kuotang Fish Soup: This fish soup is made using local fish and has a savory and spicy taste.
  • Jingdezhen Black Bean Noodles: These handmade noodles are cooked in a black bean sauce, making them rich and flavorful.

Local Beverages

  • Lushan Green Tea: This tea is famous for its high quality and is a must-try when in Lushan.
  • Baimao Hou: Baimao Hou is a famous variety of green tea that is grown in Lushan, and it has a light and delicate flavor.
  • Lushan Spring Water: The spring water in Lushan is fresh and pure, and it’s an ideal drink to quench your thirst on hot days.

Where to Stay in Lushan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lushan has several accommodation options to suit every budget.

High-End Hotels

  • Lushan Resort: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the mountains and has world-class amenities.
  • Lushan Hotel: Located in the heart of Lushan, this hotel is ideal for tourists and business travelers alike.
  • Grand Metropark Resort Lushan: The hotel is surrounded by natural beauty and is known for its excellent service.

Mid-Range Hotels

  • Lushan Yinruilin International Hotel: This hotel is perfect for travelers who seek a comfortable stay at an affordable price.
  • Lushan Hotel 1929: The hotel is located in a beautiful historical building and has a unique design and comfortable rooms.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Fenghuang Hostel: This hostel is located close to the Lushan National Park, and it’s perfect for budget travelers.
  • Lushan Zijin Hostel: This hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms and is an excellent option for backpackers.

What to Buy in Lushan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Green Tea: Lushan’s high-quality green tea is a must-buy when visiting the city.
  • Bamboo Products: Lushan is known for producing high-quality bamboo products such as baskets, chopsticks, and handicrafts.
  • Maojian Jade: This rare type of jade is found in Lushan and is a unique souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lushan

  • Taoism: Lushan has a profound influence on Chinese Taoism, and the city has several significant Taoist temples.
  • Tea Culture: Lushan is famous for its tea culture, and you can see tea plantations and take part in tea picking and tasting activities.
  • Folklore and Festivals: Lushan has a colorful tradition of folk tales and festivals such as the “Lushan Xiufeng Festival” and “Laughter Festival.”

Best Time to Visit Lushan

The best time to visit Lushan is between March and November when the weather is pleasant, and the city’s natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lushan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn some basic Mandarin phrases.

Don’t:

  • Touch sacred objects or statues without permission.
  • Take photographs of locals without their consent.
  • Waste food or water, as they’re precious resources in Lushan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lushan

  • Lushan is a safe city for tourists, and the crime rate is low.
  • Always be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Lushan

  • The tap water in Lushan is not safe for consumption, so it is best to stick to bottled water.
  • Bring insect repellent and sunscreen when visiting Lushan as the sun and insects can be intense.

Getting to Lushan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lushan

  • Nanchang Changbei International Airport: This airport is the nearest major airport and is 200 kilometers away from Lushan.

Bus Stations Near Lushan

  • Jiujian Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and offers regular bus services to several major cities in China.
  • Lushan Bus Station: The station offers bus services to major cities in Jiangxi Province.

Other Options

  • High-speed trains are a convenient option for traveling to Lushan.
  • Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Lushan

The best way to get around Lushan is by public transportation such as buses and taxis. You can also rent a car or bike to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Lushan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wuyuan County: Wuyuan is known for its stunning countryside and ancient architecture.
  • Jingdezhen: Known as the “Porcelain Capital of China,” Jingdezhen is famous for its high-quality ceramics.
  • Wuyi Mountains: Located in Fujian Province, Wuyi Mountains is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is renowned for its sheer cliffs and rich biodiversity.

Conclusion

Lushan offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and adventure activities. From stunning hiking trails and hot springs to historical sites and delicious food, Lushan has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Lushan and explore one of China’s most beautiful and fascinating destinations.
