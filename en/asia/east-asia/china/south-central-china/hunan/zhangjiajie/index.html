A Comprehensive Guide to Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie is a city in Hunan, China that is known for its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by majestic mountains, deep caves, and beautiful rivers, it is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and culture seekers. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical sites, there is no shortage of things to do in Zhangjiajie. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll show you all the best things to see and do, where to stay and eat, local customs and do’s and don’ts, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie has a plethora of attractions to offer tourists. The city is home to some of the most magnificent landscapes in China, making it a top choice for exploring outdoor wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zhangjiajie is home to several museums and historical sites. One of the most famous is the Zhangjiajie Museum, which showcases the history and culture of the Tujia people, who are a minority ethnic group in the area. The museum houses many exhibits, including traditional Tujia clothing, handicrafts, farming tools, and artworks. Other notable cultural attractions in Zhangjiajie include:

  • Junsheng Sandstone Painting Institute
  • Tujia Folk Customs Park
  • Puguang Temple

Natural Attractions of Zhangjiajie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is the most famous natural landmark in the city, featuring an enchanting natural landscape that includes verdant forests, deep valleys, and rocky cliffs. Tourists can ride the cable car or hike and see the famous peaks, such as the Hallelujah Mountain and the Southern Sky Column, which are the inspiration behind the floating Hallelujah Mountains in the movie Avatar. Zhangjiajie also boasts several scenic areas, including:

  • Baofeng Lake
  • Tianmen Mountain
  • Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhangjiajie

Tourists can explore Zhangjiajie’s unique terrain and landscapes by hiking and biking. Some of the popular routes in Zhangjiajie include:

  • The Golden Whip Stream: A 10km long hiking route that passes through the beautiful scenery of the Zhangjiajie National Forest Park.
  • Tianzi Mountain: A scenic trail that meanders through the Tianzi Mountain Nature Reserve to offer breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks.
  • Yellow Dragon Cave: A 10km long cycling trail through a natural cave with dazzling rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Zhangjiajie: Activities and Tours

Zhangjiajie offers many exciting activities and tours that cater to different interests and ages. Some of the activities include:

  • Glass Bridge: Walk on the world’s longest and highest glass bridge at Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon, hanging over a gushing river, and see the park from bird’s-eye view.
  • Cable Car Ride: Save energy and take a ride to the top of Tianmen Mountain and take a walk through the dizzying walkways and entertainment activities provided.
  • Rafting: Enjoy the thrilling yet calm experience of meandering along the streams of the picturesque area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhangjiajie

Planning your week-long itinerary in Zhangjiajie is a breeze. Our suggested itinerary could include:

  • Day 1: Hike through Golden Whip Stream.
  • Day 2: Explore the unique scenery of Wulingyuan Scenic and Historic Interest Area.
  • Day 3: Visit the stunning beauty of Tianzi Mountain.
  • Day 4: Take a walk along the Glass Bridge.
  • Day 5: Marvel at the spectacular rock formations in Yellow Dragon Cave.
  • Day 6: Visit Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon and take part in various activities.
  • Day 7: Visit the museums and historical sites, such as the Zhangjiajie Museum and the Tujia Folk Customs Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhangjiajie

No visit to Zhangjiajie is complete without tasting the delicious local cuisine. Hunan Province is famous for its spicy food and flavorful dishes. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • La Rou: A traditional Hunan dish that features smoked pork soaked in honey.
  • Spicy Crayfish: An addictive dish featuring spicy stir-fried crayfish with garlic and chili peppers.
  • Maodu: A dish that consists of diced pork, chili pepper, and garlic.

Local Beverages

Tourists can quench their thirst with a variety of local drinks such as:

  • Mao Jian Tea: A green tea that is grown in the mountains of Hunan Province.
  • Baisha Wine: A rice-based spirit distilled in Zhangjiajie and is a staple in local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Zhangjiajie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhangjiajie offers a variety of accommodation options that will suit every need and budget. There are several top-rated hotels that offer luxury amenities and excellent service, such as:

  • Pullman Zhangjiajie Hotel
  • Wulingyuan Marriott Hotel
  • Zhangjiajie The One International Youth Hostel

What to Buy in Zhangjiajie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists can take home lovely souvenirs and unique local products from Zhangjiajie. Some popular items include:

  • Xiangxi Crossbow: A traditional weapon made from bamboo, crafted by local craftsmen.
  • Tujia Embroidery: A traditional folk art that is delicate and colorful.
  • Zhangjiajie Tea: A delicious tea grown and produced in the mountains of Zhangjiajie, Hunan.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie is a city that is rich in ethnic diversity and ancient culture. The Tujia people, who are one of the largest ethnic minorities in China, have a long history and unique customs that tourists can learn about. Some cultural customs may include:

  • Dragon Boat Festival: This festival is celebrated annually in June with boat racing and rice dumplings display.
  • Tujia & Miao New Year: A celebration of the new year in the early spring with dragon and lion dance and firework.

Best Time to Visit Zhangjiajie

The best time to visit Zhangjiajie is in spring from March to May or in autumn from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city is less crowded. Tourists can enjoy the stunning natural landscapes and avoid the peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhangjiajie

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash with you, as not all vendors and shops may accept credit cards.
  • Respect wildlife and do not litter.

Don’t:

  • Enter restricted areas or climb barriers for a selfie or a photo.
  • Purchase products made from endangered animals.
  • Hike alone, always have a guide or a companion with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie is generally safe for tourists, but like anywhere in the world, it’s always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Tourists should keep their belongings safe, especially when in crowded areas, and avoid flashing valuables in public places. The terrain can be quite challenging; it’s essential to take the proper precautions when hiking, biking or participating in an adventurous activity.

Health in Zhangjiajie

The local tap water is not potable, so it’s essential to drink bottled water or boiled water. Tourists should also take precautions against mosquito bites, especially during rainy seasons, to avoid the mosquito-borne disease.

Getting to Zhangjiajie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zhangjiajie is accessible by air, rail, and land. The city has an airport that has flights from several major Chinese cities. Also, tourists can take a bullet train from several big cities in China to Zhangjiajie’s train station. It is advisable to book in advance.

Airports Near Zhangjiajie

  • Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport

Bus Stations Near Zhangjiajie

  • Zhangjiajie Central Bus Station

Other Options

Tourists can take a taxi or hire a private car to get to Zhangjiajie if they don’t want to use public transportation.

How to Get Around in Zhangjiajie

Tourists can get around the city using various modes of transportation, including:

  • Public Bus: Bus services cover the city’s major attractions and are cheap.
  • Taxi: Taxis are commonly used in the city, except for the rural areas.
  • Rental Cars: Tourists can also rent cars to drive themselves and explore the city at its best.

Where to Go Next from Zhangjiajie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tourists can also explore nearby destinations and take exciting excursions when they visit Zhangjiajie. Some of the options include:

  • Fenghuang Ancient City: A scenic and historical town located nearby known for its well-preserved ancient structures, customs, and traditions.
  • Wuhan: A lively and vibrant city located about 400 kilometers from Zhangjiajie that offers access to museums, shopping, and historical sites.

Conclusion

Zhangjiajie is a city that should not be missed when visiting China. Full of adventure, natural landscapes and cultural experiences, it has something to offer for every kind of traveler. Start planning your trip now with our helpful guide and get ready to experience this beautiful destination.
