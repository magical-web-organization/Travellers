Introduction to Jishou

Located in south-central China in the Hunan province, Jishou is a hidden gem that has been overshadowed by the more popular cities in the region. The city is surrounded by mountains, rivers, and valleys, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Moreover, Jishou’s rich culture and history can be seen in its museums, temples, and markets. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through everything you need to know to make the most out of your Jishou trip.

List of Places to Visit in Jishou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tuojiang Ancient Town - A preserved ancient town that boasts of a history dating back to the Tang Dynasty.
  • Zhangjiajie National Forest Park - This park is famous for its towering sandstone pillars that are said to have inspired the scenery in the movie “Avatar.”
  • Qianyang Ancient Town - Another ancient town with traditional Huizhou culture architecture.
  • Wulingyuan Scenic Area - One of China’s UNESCO World Heritage sites known for its scenic views and unique geological characteristics.
  • Eighteen Scenes of Jishou - A collection of 18 scenic spots in Jishou that are famous for their natural beauty and cultural significance.

Natural Attractions of Jishou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fenghuangshan Scenic Area - This scenic area is known for its natural beauty and unique rock formations. It is perfect for hiking and camping.
  • Baimashan National Forest Park - A beautiful park that is home to many rare plant and animal species. It offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Tongdao Dong Autonomous County - A mountainous county with breathtaking landscapes and unique Dong ethnic group architecture.
  • Yangming Cave - An underground cave system that features stalactites, stalagmites, and underground streams.
  • Jingzhou Hot Spring - A natural hot spring in the mountains, where visitors can relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jishou

  • The Red Flag Canal Scenic Area - A scenic area that is famous for its historical significance, as it was built by the locals during the Chinese Civil War.
  • The Slope Bridge Miao Village - This village is known for its unique Miao ethnic group customs and traditional architecture.
  • The Jiuhua Mountain Scenic Area - A beautiful mountain range that offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys.
  • The Dehang Miao Village - A village that is located in a scenic area, where visitors can hike and explore the area’s natural beauty.
  • The Wuling Mountain Scenic Area - A mountain range that is known for its unique geological features and stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Jishou: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride - This is an exciting way to explore the beautiful scenery of Jishou from high above.
  • Visit the Local Markets - Jishou has several markets where you can buy souvenirs, traditional arts, and crafts, and local food.
  • Explore the Museums - Jishou has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its many museums.
  • Take a Tour of the Ancient Towns - Jishou is home to several ancient towns that showcase traditional Huizhou culture architecture.
  • Try the Local Cuisine - Jishou has a unique and delicious cuisine that is a must-try for any food lover.

Food and Drink Scene of Jishou

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hunan-style Smoked Sausage - A cured sausage that is smoked over firewood, giving it a unique and delicious flavor.
  • Fried Rice Cake - A savory and crispy dish made from rice flour, scallions, and other ingredients.
  • Stinky Tofu - A polarizing dish that is made by fermenting tofu until it has a distinct smell and flavor.
  • Hunan-style Spicy Fish Head - A spicy and flavorful dish made with fresh fish head and hot peppers.
  • Rice Noodle Soup - A traditional breakfast dish made with rice noodles and a variety of toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Nanqiao Rice Wine - A traditional rice wine that is made by fermenting glutinous rice.
  • Huzhou Tea - A famous tea that is grown in the mountains around Jishou.
  • Wuling Red Wine - A locally produced red wine that is made from grapes grown in the Wuling Mountain region.

Where to Stay in Jishou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jiahui International Hotel - A luxury hotel that is located in the heart of Jishou.
  • Jishou Wuyi Hotel - A mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and good amenities.
  • Jishou Bojang Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Jishou Liangcha Boutique Guesthouse - A unique and stylish guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and personalized service.
  • Tuojiang Qinghe Inn - A cozy inn located in the heart of Tuojiang Ancient Town.

What to Buy in Jishou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea - Jishou is known for its high-quality tea, and there are many varieties to choose from.
  • Traditional Arts and Crafts - Jishou has a rich cultural heritage, and traditional arts and crafts are a great souvenir to bring home.
  • Hunan-style Smoked Sausage - This delicious cured sausage is a great souvenir for any food lover.
  • Shimen Stone - A unique stone that is found in Jishou and is said to have healing properties.
  • Batik Fabric - A traditional fabric that is made by hand and features unique patterns and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Jishou

Jishou has a rich culture that is reflected in its traditional architecture, arts, and crafts, and cuisine. It is also home to several minority ethnic groups, including the Miao, Dong, and Yao, each with its unique customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and etiquette and learn about the different cultural practices during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Jishou

The best time to visit Jishou is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jishou

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and visit the local markets.
  • Respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Jishou, including its mountains and rivers.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Hike or explore the mountains without proper guidance or equipment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jishou

Jishou is a safe city for tourists, with low levels of crime. However, visitors are advised to be cautious when using ATMs or carrying valuables, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Jishou

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid tap water to prevent stomach-related illnesses. It is also a good idea to bring any necessary medications, as some medications may not be readily available in Jishou.

Getting to Jishou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jishou is accessible by air, rail, and road. Visitors can fly into the nearby Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport, take a train or bus to Jishou, or drive from neighboring cities.

Airports Near Jishou

  • Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport

Bus Stations Near Jishou

  • Jishou South Bus Station
  • Jishou North Bus Station

Other Options

  • High-speed trains from Changsha or other nearby cities.
  • Taxis or private cars, which can be hired at the airport or train stations.

How to Get Around in Jishou

Visitors can use local buses, taxis, or private cars to get around Jishou. Bicycles and scooters can also be rented in some areas, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Jishou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhangjiajie - This city is famous for its stunning natural scenery and unique rock formations.
  • Fenghuang Ancient Town - A well-preserved ancient town that showcases traditional Miao ethnic group architecture.
  • Changsha - The capital of Hunan province and home to many historical and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Jishou is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique and authentic experience of Chinese culture and natural beauty. With its rich history and culture, stunning scenery and outdoor activities, delicious cuisine, and friendly people, Jishou is a must-visit city for anyone traveling to south-central China.
