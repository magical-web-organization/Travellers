Introduction to Changde

Located in the heart of Hunan Province, Changde is a charming city that boasts rich history, natural beauty, and cultural diversity. The city is famously known as the hometown of the great Chinese philosopher, Wang Fuzhi, and is named after a legendary figure in Chinese folklore. With its beautiful scenery, famous historical sites, and vibrant cultural life, Changde is an excellent destination for both domestic and international tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Changde

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wang Fuzhi Memorial Hall: Learn about the life and legacy of the famous Chinese philosopher, Wang Fuzhi, and view his calligraphy and other works.
  2. Tiandu ancient city ruins: Explore the remains of a city that dates back to the Han Dynasty and see ancient artifacts.
  3. Changde Poem Wall: Admire the impressive collection of over 3,000 poems inscribed on this wall.

Natural Attractions of Changde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Taohuayuan: Wander through the scenic landscapes that inspired Tao Yuanming’s famous poem “Peach Blossom Spring”.
  2. Zixing Hot Spring: Relax in the therapeutic waters of this natural hot spring.
  3. Qinglaihe Canyon: Hike through this picturesque canyon and take in the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changde

  1. Taohuayuan Scenic Area Hiking Trail: This 5-kilometer trail winds through beautiful forests and leads to stunning views of the Taohuayuan Scenic Area.
  2. Wulingyuan Hiking Trail: This 8-kilometer trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, waterfalls, and valleys.
  3. Xiangxi River Cycling Trail: Explore the beauty of the Xiangxi River on this 30-kilometer cycling trail that winds through quaint villages and scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Changde: Activities and Tours

  1. Changde Night Market: Sample delicious local cuisine and shop for souvenirs at this lively market.
  2. Changde Jinshi Hot Spring: Soak in the soothing hot springs and indulge in a relaxing massage or spa treatment.
  3. Changde Fishing Village: Try your hand at fishing and learn about the local fishing culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Changde

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Changde Stinky Tofu: A popular Hunanese snack that will challenge your taste buds with its strong aroma and flavor.
  2. Changde Rice Wine: A traditional rice wine that has a rich and smooth taste, perfect for pairing with spicy Hunanese cuisine.
  3. Changde Dumplings: These delicate dumplings are filled with pork and mixed vegetables and served with a savory dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuocha Tea: A type of compressed tea that is brewed into a dark, flavorful beverage.
  2. Osmanthus Wine: A sweet and fragrant wine made from osmanthus flowers.

Where to Stay in Changde: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Vienna International Hotel: A modern and comfortable hotel in the city center.
  2. Changde Eden Hotel: A luxurious five-star hotel with excellent amenities and services.
  3. Changde Red Memory Hostel: A charming and affordable hostel in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Changde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Changde Dongting Lake Pearls: These freshwater pearls make for a unique and valuable souvenir.
  2. Changde Embroidery: Admire the intricate designs of this traditional craft, and bring home a piece of Changde’s cultural heritage.
  3. Changde Teapots: These handmade teapots are crafted from the clay of the Yixing region and are renowned for their beauty and functionality.

Local Culture and Customs of Changde

Changde has a rich cultural history that includes ancient customs and traditions that are still observed today. Visitors to the city should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, which include honoring ancestors, celebrating festivals and holidays, and observing the rules of etiquette and social behavior.

Best Time to Visit Changde

The best time to visit Changde is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The cherry blossoms of Taohuayuan are in full bloom during March and April, making it an especially beautiful time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changde

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  3. Try the local cuisine and sample traditional Hunanese dishes.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  2. Take photos or videos of people without their permission.
  3. Be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Changde

Changde is a safe city, though visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, particularly in crowded areas or late at night. Petty theft and scams can occur, so it’s important to keep valuables secure and be cautious when approached by strangers.

Health in Changde

Changde has good medical facilities, with several hospitals and clinics located throughout the city. Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves against common illnesses, such as wearing insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Changde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Changde

  1. Changsha Huanghua International Airport: Located approximately 200 kilometers from Changde, this airport offers domestic and international flights.
  2. Zhangjiajie Hehua International Airport: Located approximately 150 kilometers from Changde, this airport offers domestic flights to major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Changde

  1. Changde South Bus Station: Located in the city center, this bus station offers regular services to nearby cities and towns.
  2. Changde West Bus Station: Located on the outskirts of the city, this bus station offers services to more remote destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Changde by train or car, though it’s important to note that the city is located in a mountainous region, and driving conditions can be challenging.

How to Get Around in Changde

Changde has an extensive public transportation system, including buses and taxis, as well as a bike-sharing system that allows visitors to easily explore the city. Walking is also a great way to explore Changde’s beautiful parks and scenic attractions.

Where to Go Next from Changde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Zhangjiajie: Visit the stunning Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, known for its towering sandstone columns and spectacular views.
  2. Fenghuang Ancient Town: Explore this picturesque ancient town, famous for its well-preserved Ming and Qing Dynasty buildings and unique culture.
  3. Wulingyuan Scenic Area: Hike through this beautiful and rugged landscape, which is home to a rich biodiversity of plant and animal species.

Conclusion

Changde is a beautiful and culturally rich city that offers something for everyone, from scenic natural attractions to historical sites and cultural traditions. Whether you’re interested in hiking, sightseeing, or trying new foods and drinks, Changde is a must-visit destination for any traveler to China.
