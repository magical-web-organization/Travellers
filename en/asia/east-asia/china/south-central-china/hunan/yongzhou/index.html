Introduction to Yongzhou

Yongzhou is a city located in Hunan Province, South-central China. Its name means “eternal prefecture” in Chinese, and it is located in the foothills of the Wuling Mountains. This vibrant city has a rich history and a unique culture that fascinates all visitors. It is an excellent destination for travelers who want to explore China beyond the tourist tracks.

List of Places to Visit in Yongzhou

Here are some of the top places to visit in Yongzhou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yongzhou Museum: This museum houses various exhibits on Yongzhou’s culture, history, and art. It has over 100,000 antiques, such as pottery, jade, weapons, and bronze artifacts, that date back to ancient times.
  • Dayun Temple: Also known as “The Great Cloud Monastery,” this ancient temple is the largest Buddhist temple in the city. It is home to several well-preserved ancient buildings, including the magnificent Thousand-Hand Guanyin Hall.

Natural Attractions of Yongzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jiulongshan National Forest Park: This impressive national forest park is known for its natural landscape and scenic views. Visitors can go hiking, camping, bird-watching, or picnicking amid the rich vegetation and fresh air.
  • Yao Mountain Forest Park: As the highest peak in Yongzhou, Yao Mountain offers a panoramic view of the city center and the surrounding mountains. It is an ideal place for enjoying the sunrise or the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yongzhou

Yongzhou has a wealth of hiking and biking trails that traverse the mountains, lakes, and forests of the region. Whether you are an experienced hiker or a novice one, there are routes suitable for all levels. Here are some popular ones:

  • Daqiao Valley: This valley is renowned for its crystal-clear waterfalls, refreshing streams, and verdant forests. There is a well-maintained hiking trail that runs along the valley that takes approximately four hours to complete.
  • Shixiang Lake Bicycle Route: This 8km route meanders along the picturesque Shixiang Lake and offers cyclists breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and water.

Best Things to Do in Yongzhou: Activities and Tours

  • Join the Dragon Boat Festival: The Dragon Boat Festival is celebrated annually in Yongzhou, and its main event takes place on the Zi River, where teams of paddlers compete in dragon boats. It is a festive, traditional event that attracts crowds from all around the country.
  • Explore the local markets: The bustling markets of Yongzhou offer a unique experience to travelers. Visitors can sample various street food, purchase souvenirs, and observe local artisans in action.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yongzhou

Here is a seven-day itinerary that covers the best of Yongzhou:

  • Day 1: Yongzhou Museum and Dayun Temple
  • Day 2: Daqiao Valley hike
  • Day 3: Yao Mountain and Zi River Walk
  • Day 4: Shixiang Lake Bicycle Route
  • Day 5: Jiulongshan National Forest Park
  • Day 6: Local food tour and market visit
  • Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Yongzhou

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spicy Chicken Soup: This dish is a local delicacy that features tender chicken cooked in spicy broth.
  • Yunan Noodle Soup: This soup is one of Yongzhou’s comfort foods and consists of thick noodles in a rich, savory broth.
  • Xiang Wetfish Soup: This soup is a specialty of the Hunan region, and its ingredients include freshwater fish, ginger, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Yongzhou Tea: Yongzhou has a rich tea culture, and visitors can sample various high-quality teas, including black, green, and oolong tea.
  • Hongjinsha Wine: This rice wine is a popular alcoholic beverage in Yongzhou and is often served during celebratory occasions.

Where to Stay in Yongzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yongzhou offers a diverse range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some popular choices:

  • Yongzhou Hotel: A four-star hotel in the city center with comfortable rooms and convenient amenities.
  • Cape Oxford Hotel: A five-star luxury hotel that offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  • Furongyuan Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that provides basic amenities and strategic location.

What to Buy in Yongzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yongzhou has a rich handicraft culture, and visitors can purchase unique souvenirs to commemorate their trip. Here are some suggestions:

  • Yongzhou Lacquerware: This traditional handicraft involves applying layers of lacquer to create intricate designs on wood, pottery, or bamboo products.
  • Yongzhou Embroidery: This embroidery is famous for its fine details, vibrant colors, and intricate patterns.
  • Yongzhou Tea: As mentioned earlier, Yongzhou produces high-quality tea that is popular among tea enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Yongzhou

Yongzhou has a blend of multiple cultures, customs, and traditions. Visitors can observe the traditional festivals and ceremonies celebrated by the locals, such as the Dragon Boat Festival, the Mid-Autumn Festival, and the Chinese New Year. It is important to respect local traditions and customs while traveling in Yongzhou.

Best Time to Visit Yongzhou

The best time to visit Yongzhou is from late March to early June, and from late September to early November. During these months, the weather is pleasant and mild, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yongzhou

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Yongzhou by hiking or biking.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in disrespectful behavior in a temple or a religious site.
  • Take pictures without permission, especially of people’s faces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yongzhou

Yongzhou is a relatively safe city, and visitors can explore the city and its attractions without much trouble. Like in any other city, it is recommended to keep an eye on your personal belongings and avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

Health in Yongzhou

Water in Yongzhou is often not potable; it is advisable to stick to bottled water or boiled water. Visitors should also take proper precautions against mosquitoes and other insects, particularly during the rainy season.

Getting to Yongzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yongzhou is easily accessible by various modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Yongzhou

  • Changsha Huanghua International Airport: This airport is located approximately 236 kilometers from Yongzhou, and it offers flights to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Yongzhou

  • Yongzhou East Bus Station: This bus station operates buses to and from many cities in Hunan province.
  • Yongzhou Bus Station: This station manages buses that travel to destinations throughout the province and to other parts of China.

Other Options

  • Train: Yongzhou Train Station is located in the city center and has trains running to nearby cities like Shenzhen, Guangzhou, and Wuhan.
  • Taxi or Private Car: Taxis or private cars are readily available for hire in Yongzhou, and they are ideal for getting around the city or for traveling in and out of town.

How to Get Around in Yongzhou

Yongzhou’s transportation system is quite developed, and visitors have multiple options for getting around, such as:

  • Bus: Yongzhou has a well-connected network of buses that covers most areas and attractions of the city.
  • Taxi: Taxis in Yongzhou are affordable and convenient, and they are available for hire from designated stands or by hailing them on the street.
  • Bicycle or Electric Scooter: Many visitors choose to rent bikes or electric scooters to explore the city or to travel short distances.

Where to Go Next from Yongzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some popular destinations near Yongzhou that are worth visiting:

  • Changsha: Hunan’s provincial capital, known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine.
  • Shaoyang: A city located in the south of Hunan Province, known for its beautiful scenery and laidback lifestyle.
  • Zhangjiajie: A scenic area renowned for its dramatic sandstone pillars, lush forests, and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Yongzhou is a hidden gem in China that boasts natural wonders, unique culture, and excellent food. This travel guide has covered the essential information that travelers need before visiting Yongzhou. Whether you are an adventure seeker or a culture enthusiast, Yongzhou has something for everyone. Start planning your trip to Yongzhou, and experience the treasures of this amazing city.
