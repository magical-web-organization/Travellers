Introduction to Jiangyong

Jiangyong is a small but culturally-rich town located in the Hunan province of China. This ancient town dates back to over 1,300 years and is officially recognized as a key historical and cultural town by the Chinese government.

Jiangyong has a population of just under 10,000 people and is often regarded as one of the best-preserved ancient towns in all of Hunan Province. The town boasts a rich cultural heritage and is full of ancient buildings, streets, and temples that have been preserved to their original ancient architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Jiangyong

Culturally Important Attractions

Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jiangyong Museum: The museum has a wide range of exhibits that showcase the culture, art, architecture, and history of Jiangyong.
  2. Baogong Temple: This ancient temple was built during the Tang Dynasty and features stunning architecture and artwork.
  3. Baoji Temple: Built in the late Ming and early Qing Dynasty, this temple features magnificent ancient architecture and exquisite artwork.

Natural Attractions of Jiangyong

  1. Taohuajiang River: The Taohuajiang River runs through Jiangyong and is one of the most picturesque spots in the region.
  2. Mount Fenghuang: This mountain is located just outside Jiangyong and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  3. Hengshan Mountain: Located just a few hours’ drive from Jiangyong, Hengshan Mountain is a must-visit for those who love hiking and nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiangyong

  1. Taohuajiang River Trail: This easy hike takes you along the banks of the Taohuajiang River and allows you to take in some of the most beautiful views in the region.
  2. Mount Fenghuang Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of Mount Fenghuang and rewards you with breathtaking views of Jiangyong and the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Jiangyong: Activities and Tours

  1. Cultural Walking Tour: Take a walking tour of Jiangyong’s ancient buildings and temples to experience the rich cultural heritage of the region.
  2. Taohuajiang River Rafting: Experience the beauty and excitement of the Taohuajiang River with a guided rafting tour.
  3. Mount Hengshan Hike: For the adventurous traveler, a hike up Mount Hengshan is a must-do activity.

Food and Drink Scene of Jiangyong

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Dong’an chicken: marinated chicken cooked in beer and served with peppers and vegetables.
  2. Pork belly in soy sauce: braised pork belly with soy sauce, ginger, and garlic.
  3. Hunan-style smoked tofu: diced tofu with garlic, scallions, and smoked meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Rice wine: a traditional alcoholic beverage made from glutinous rice.
  2. Jiangxiang tea: a type of tea produced in the Hunan province that has a strong and earthy flavor.
  3. Soy milk: a popular non-alcoholic drink in Jiangyong, made from soybeans.

Where to Stay in Jiangyong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Kaisa International Hotel: a luxurious 4-star hotel located in the heart of Jiangyong.
  2. Cuiyuan Inn: a quaint and affordable guesthouse offering comfortable rooms and personalized service.
  3. GreenTree Inn: a popular and affordable chain hotel located near the center of Jiangyong.

What to Buy in Jiangyong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Jiangyong paper-cuts: intricate paper-cutting artworks that have been done in Jiangyong for centuries.
  2. Tujia embroidery: embroidered textiles that are made by the Tujia people of Jiangyong.
  3. Honey: the region is known for producing high-quality honey that is popular among tourists and locals alike.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiangyong

Jiangyong is known for its rich culture and customs. Visitors can experience this unique culture through traditional festivals, music, dance, and art. One of the most popular festivals in Jiangyong is the Dragon Boat Festival, which is celebrated in the fifth lunar month of the Chinese calendar.

Best Time to Visit Jiangyong

The best time to visit Jiangyong is during the spring and autumn months (March to May, and September to November). The weather during these months is mild and comfortable, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiangyong

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Enter someone’s home without being invited.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jiangyong

Jiangyong is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, it is always important to be vigilant and take common-sense precautions when traveling.

Health in Jiangyong

Visitors to Jiangyong should make sure they are up-to-date with their routine vaccinations. The town does not have a hospital, so it is recommended to bring a first-aid kit along.

Getting to Jiangyong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jiangyong

The nearest airport to Jiangyong is the Changsha Huanghua International Airport, which is about 240 km away.

Bus Stations Near Jiangyong

Jiangyong is well-connected by bus to surrounding cities, including Changsha, Shaoyang, and Yiyang.

Other Options

Travelers can also take a taxi, rent a car or book a private car from nearby cities to get to Jiangyong.

How to Get Around in Jiangyong

The most convenient way to get around Jiangyong is on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Jiangyong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Changsha: the capital city of Hunan province, located a few hours’ drive from Jiangyong.
  2. Zhangjiajie: famous for its stunning natural scenery, located about 200 km away from Jiangyong.
  3. Fenghuang Ancient Town: a well-preserved ancient town located about 3 hours’ drive from Jiangyong.

Conclusion

Jiangyong is a small but culturally-rich town that is full of ancient buildings, temples, and other landmarks. It is a must-visit location for anyone interested in experiencing the rich culture and history of Hunan province.
