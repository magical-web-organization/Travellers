Introduction to Changsha

Nestled in the heart of South-central China’s Hunan province, Changsha is a vibrant city that boasts a mix of rich history, stunning natural scenery, and bustling modernity. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Changsha has something for you.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, best things to do, local culture and customs, safety tips, and more, to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Changsha.

List of Places to Visit in Changsha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Changsha has a rich cultural heritage, and a tour of its museums and historical places is a must-do for any visitor. Here are a few must-visit spots:

  • Hunan Provincial Museum: Known for its vast collection of cultural relics from the Han dynasty to the Qing dynasty, Hunan Provincial Museum is a perfect place to learn about the region’s ancient history and culture.
  • Yuelu Academy: Founded in the 10th century, Yuelu Academy is one of China’s four greatest ancient academies. It features ancient architecture and is known for its connection to Confucian studies.
  • Orange Island Park: Situated in the middle of the Xiangjiang River, Orange Island Park is home to a statue of revolutionary leader Mao Zedong, as well as several scenic trails and pavilions.
  • Mawangdui Han Tombs: Mawangdui Han Tombs is an important archaeological site that houses the ancient tombs of the Marquises of Dai. The site was excavated in the 1970s and has more than 3,000 relics, including silk products and bamboo texts.
  • Tianxin Pavilion: Located at the center of Changsha city, Tianxin Pavilion is a famous Taoist temple and one of Changsha’s top historical sites.

Natural Attractions of Changsha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Changsha’s natural beauty is also a big draw for tourists. Here are some natural attractions that you should not miss:

  • Mount Yuelu: With an elevation of 300 meters, Mount Yuelu promises breathtaking views of Changsha city. It also offers several hiking trails, including the famous Yuelu Mountain Scenic Route.
  • Shaoshan: Located in the west of Changsha City, Shaoshan is the birthplace of Mao Zedong and has become a tourist destination for its rich revolutionary history.
  • The Xiangjiang River: The Xiangjiang River is the mother river of Changsha, and a boat tour offers stunning views of the city skyline.
  • Fenghuang Ancient Town: Located a few hours from Changsha, Fenghuang Ancient Town features well-preserved architecture, scenic views, and is a perfect weekend getaway from the bustling city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changsha

Changsha has several hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views of the city and its surrounding natural beauty. Here are a few popular routes:

  • Yuelu Mountain Scenic Route: This 11-kilometer scenic route is the most popular hiking route in Changsha. It starts at the foot of Mount Yuelu and passes through many scenic spots like Lushan Temple and Yunlu Palace.
  • West Meixi Lake Greenway: West Meixi Lake Greenway is an 8.3-kilometer route that is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk. It offers scenic views of the lake and the surrounding greenery.
  • Huangxing Road Walking Street: Huangxing Road Walking Street is a pedestrian walkway in the heart of Changsha city. This route is perfect for those who want to experience the city’s bustling atmosphere while enjoying the sights along the way.

Best Things to Do in Changsha: Activities and Tours

Changsha offers many exciting activities and tours that cater to all kinds of interests. Here are some of the best things you can do in Changsha:

  • Experience Xiang Opera and Changsha culture: Xiang opera is a local traditional opera form and is a unique way to experience Changsha’s culture. Many theatres in the city provide opportunities to watch one of these performances.
  • Visit a night market: Changsha is known for its lively night markets, where you can try some of the local cuisine and purchase souvenirs.
  • Take a cooking class: Changsha is famous for its spicy food, and taking a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Hunanese dishes is a great way to immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Explore Changsha’s underground world: Changsha is home to the world’s longest pedestrian tunnel, measuring 1,030 meters long. It runs under the Xiangjiang River and connects Changsha’s two banks.
  • Go rafting on the Lishui River: Located 50 kilometers from Changsha, Lishui River is well-known for its clean water and exciting rapids, making it a popular destination for rafting.

Food and Drink Scene of Changsha

Must-Try Dishes

Changsha cuisine is famous for its spiciness and unique flavors. Here are some dishes that you must try:

  • Stinky tofu: Known for its pungent aroma, stinky tofu is a fermented tofu dish that has a crispy exterior and a soft and creamy interior.
  • Spicy crayfish: A famous Hunanese dish, the spicy crayfish is a dish made with freshwater crayfish, garlic, ginger, red chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns.
  • Changsha-style rice noodle: A popular breakfast dish in Changsha, this dish features thin and flat rice noodles served with a variety of toppings and a spicy broth.

Local Beverages

Apart from its spicy cuisine, Changsha is also known for its beverages. Here are some local drinks that you should try:

  • Xiangju: Xiangju is a distilled liquor made from glutinous rice and wheat. It is known for its mellow and sweet taste.
  • Changsha Bamboo Leaf Green Tea: This tea has a unique flavor and aroma as it is wrapped in bamboo leaves during the processing.

Where to Stay in Changsha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Changsha has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Changsha:

  • Wyndham Grand Plaza Royale Furongguo Changsha: Located in the heart of Changsha’s central business district, Wyndham Grand Plaza Royale Furongguo Changsha is a luxurious option with excellent amenities.
  • Morning Music Hotel: A budget-friendly option, Morning Music Hotel is located in the heart of the city and has clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Floral Hotel Heshang: This charming guesthouse is located in the historic city center and offers quaint and peaceful rooms.

What to Buy in Changsha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Changsha is known for its art, crafts, and local products. Here are some things that you should consider taking home:

  • Hunan Embroidery: Hunan Embroidery is famous for its exquisite techniques and unique patterns. This embroidery can be made into clothing, bags, and other accessories.
  • Xiangxi Porcelain: Xiangxi porcelain is a famous traditional handicraft in Changsha. This porcelain is known for its thin texture and elegant patterns.
  • Mao Memorabilia: Changsha is Mao Zedong’s birthplace, and you can find a wide range of Mao-themed souvenirs like statues, busts, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Changsha

Changsha has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect elders: In Chinese culture, respecting elders is of utmost importance. If you meet an elder, it is customary to address them politely.
  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home: Chinese people generally remove their shoes before entering someone’s house as a sign of respect for the homeowner.
  • Use chopsticks correctly: Chopsticks are a common utensil in China, and it’s important to use them correctly. Do not stick chopsticks vertically into a bowl of rice, as it is considered a bad omen.

Best Time to Visit Changsha

The best time to visit Changsha is from March to May and September to November when the temperature is comfortable, and there is less rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changsha

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and local beverages.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs.
  • Visit historical sites and museums to learn about the rich cultural heritage of Changsha.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the culture and customs of Changsha.
  • Engage in illegal or unethical activities.
  • Forget to carry a mask as air pollution is a big concern in Changsha.

Safety and Health

Safety in Changsha

Changsha is a safe city with low crime rates, but tourists should take precautions like they would in any other city. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas.
  • Keep your valuables secure.
  • Carry a map and know the location of emergency services near your accommodation.

Health in Changsha

Changsha has excellent medical facilities, and tourists can easily access them, but it’s important to take some precautions:

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Wash your hands frequently to avoid infections.

Getting to Changsha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Changsha

  • Changsha Huanghua International Airport: This is the nearest airport to Changsha city and connects to major cities in China and some international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Changsha

  • Changsha Wuguangcheng Bus Station: Offers regular bus services to cities in the Hunan province and to nearby provinces.
  • Changsha South Bus Station: This station offers long-distance bus services to major cities in China.

Other Options

  • Train: Changsha Railway Station connects to cities across China.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Changsha and are an affordable way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Changsha

Changsha has a good transportation network, and visitors can choose between public transportation, taxis, and rental cars. Here are a few options:

  • Metro: Changsha Metro is the most convenient way to travel within the city.
  • Bus: Changsha has an extensive bus network, and buses are a budget-friendly way to get around the city.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Changsha and are an affordable way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Changsha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Changsha is an excellent base to explore other nearby destinations. Here are some places you can visit:

  • Zhangjiajie National Forest Park: Famous for its breathtaking natural landscape, Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is a few hours from Changsha and perfect for a day trip or a weekend getaway.
  • Fenghuang Ancient Town: This ancient town is known for its well-preserved architecture and scenic views and is a perfect weekend getaway from Changsha.
  • Yueyang Tower: Located in Yueyang, a small town a few hours from Changsha, Yueyang Tower is an important cultural site and one of the Three Great Towers of Jiangnan.
