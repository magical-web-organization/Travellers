Introduction to Shiyan

Shiyan is a city located in South-central China, in the province of Hubei. It is often overlooked by tourists, but it is a great destination for those who want to experience traditional Chinese culture and beautiful natural scenery. The city is renowned for its tea, silk, and the world-famous Shennongjia Forest, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will give you an overview of the top things to do in Shiyan, where to stay, what to eat, and other essential travel information.

List of Places to Visit in Shiyan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shiyan is rich in culture and boasts a variety of museums and historical places. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • Wulonggou Scenic Area: This stunning area is located in the Shennongjia National Nature Reserve and features beautiful mountains, waterfalls, and deep valleys. Visitors can take a cable car to the mountaintop and enjoy the breathtaking views.

  • Maojian National Tea Garden: Known for its high-quality tea, the Maojian National Tea Garden is a popular destination for tea enthusiasts. Visitors can learn about the tea-making process and sample different varieties of tea.

  • Shiyan Museum: This museum features a vast collection of artifacts that showcase the city’s history and cultural heritage. It is an excellent place to learn about the region’s history and people.

Natural Attractions of Shiyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shiyan is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Shennongjia National Nature Reserve: This protected area is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to rare species such as the Giant Panda and Golden Monkey. Visitors can hike, camp, and trek through the beautiful mountains and forests.

  • Wudang Mountains: Located in the Northwest of Hubei province, the peaks of the Wudang Mountains are home to numerous Taoist temples and monasteries. The area is considered sacred to Taoist religion and attracts many visitors each year.

  • Jiulongzhai Scenic Area: This scenic area features a beautiful waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can hike through the forest and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shiyan

Shiyan is also an ideal destination for those who enjoy hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Jingmen to Shennongjia: This challenging route takes visitors through mountainous terrain, dense forests, and stunning waterfalls. It is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the Shennongjia area.

  • Wudangshan Peak Trail: This moderate to challenging hike takes visitors up to the highest peak in the Wudang Mountains. It offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Shiyan: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the natural wonders of Shiyan, visitors can also enjoy a variety of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the Local Markets: Shiyan has several markets where visitors can purchase local products, including tea, silk, and handicrafts.

  • Take a Tea Tasting Tour: The Maojian National Tea Garden offers tea tasting tours where visitors can sample different varieties of tea and learn about the tea-making process.

  • Explore the Taoist Temples: The Wudang Mountains are home to numerous Taoist temples and monasteries. Visitors can explore the ancient architecture and learn about Taoist religion.

Food and Drink Scene of Shiyan

Shiyan has a unique food culture influenced by its local ingredients and traditional cooking methods. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maojian Tea: This famous tea is grown in the Shennongjia area of Shiyan and is known for its strong fragrance and refreshing taste.

  • Shennongjia Bamboo Shoots: These tender bamboo shoots are a seasonal delicacy and are often used in traditional Chinese dishes such as stir-fry.

  • Wudang Taoist Vegetarian Cuisine: Due to its religious significance, the Wudang Mountains are famous for their vegetarian cuisine. Visitors can experience the unique flavors of Taoist cuisine at one of the many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: This strong liquor is a favorite among locals and is often served at banquets and celebrations.

  • Maojian Tea: As mentioned earlier, Maojian tea is a staple beverage in Shiyan, and visitors can enjoy it at numerous tea houses and cafes.

Where to Stay in Shiyan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shiyan has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Shiyan Wudangshan International Hotel: This luxurious hotel is located in the downtown area of Shiyan and offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

  • Wudangshan Hostel: Located in the Wudang Mountains, this hostel is a great option for those who want to experience the natural beauty of the area and meet fellow travelers.

  • Shennongjia Mountain Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the heart of the Shennongjia National Nature Reserve and offers stunning views and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Shiyan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shiyan has a variety of unique and high-quality products that make great souvenirs or gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Silk Products: Shiyan is famous for its high-quality silk, and visitors can purchase silk products, including clothing, accessories, and home decor.

  • Tea: Maojian tea is a popular purchase among visitors to Shiyan, and there are several tea shops and markets where visitors can purchase the tea.

  • Handicrafts: Shiyan has a vibrant local arts and crafts scene, and visitors can purchase handmade pottery, paintings, and other unique items.

Local Culture and Customs of Shiyan

Shiyan has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and traditions. Here are some essential things to know:

  • Respect for elders: Chinese culture places great importance on respect for elders, and visitors should show deference to older people.

  • Importance of Tea: Tea is an essential part of Chinese culture, and visitors should be prepared to drink plenty of it while in Shiyan.

  • Quiet and Discreet: Chinese culture values quiet and modesty, and visitors should avoid being too loud or boisterous in public places.

Best Time to Visit Shiyan

The best time to visit Shiyan is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. It can be extremely hot in the summer, and the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shiyan

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Shiyan has a unique food culture, and visitors should try the local specialties.

  • Take Part in Cultural Activities: Shiyan has a vibrant culture, and visitors should take part in local festivities and try to learn about the traditions and customs.

  • Be Respectful: Chinese culture places great importance on respect, and visitors should be respectful of the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Touch Artifacts in Museums: It’s essential to respect the cultural relics in museums and avoid touching or damaging them.

  • Speak Too Loudly in Public Places: Chinese culture values quiet and discretion in public places, and visitors should avoid being too loud or disruptive.

  • Overindulge in Alcohol: Baijiu is a popular drink in Shiyan, but visitors should drink responsibly and avoid overindulging.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shiyan

Shiyan is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid Scams: As with any travel destination, visitors should be aware of scams and avoid giving money to individuals or organizations that seem suspicious.

  • Be Careful Crossing the Street: Traffic in China can be chaotic, and visitors should be cautious when crossing the street and look both ways before crossing.

Health in Shiyan

Shiyan is generally a healthy place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some tips:

  • Drink Bottled Water: The tap water in Shiyan is not safe to drink, and visitors should drink bottled water instead.

  • Wear Sunscreen: The sun can be strong in Shiyan, and visitors should wear sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Shiyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shiyan

The nearest airport to Shiyan is Wudangshan Airport, which has flights to several domestic destinations. Visitors can also fly into Wuhan Tianhe International Airport and take a train or bus to Shiyan.

Bus Stations Near Shiyan

Shiyan has several bus stations that provide transportation to nearby cities and towns. The most well-known station is the Shiyan Central Bus Station, located in the downtown area.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Shiyan, with several trains running daily from major cities in China. There are also several highways that connect Shiyan to other major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Shiyan

The most convenient way to get around Shiyan is by taxi, which is relatively inexpensive. Visitors can also take the bus or rent a bike to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Shiyan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that visitors can consider visiting after exploring Shiyan:

  • Xi’an: Located about 5 hours away from Shiyan, Xi’an is known for its Terracotta Warriors and ancient city walls.

  • Wuhan: Located about 4 hours away from Shiyan, Wuhan is the capital of Hubei Province and offers a bustling city experience.

  • Chongqing: Located about 6 hours away from Shiyan, Chongqing is a sprawling metropolis known for its hotpot cuisine and unique architecture.

Conclusion

Shiyan may not be the most well-known travel destination in China, but it offers a unique cultural and natural experience. From stunning scenic areas to delicious local cuisine, there is plenty to see and do in Shiyan. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to visit this fascinating city and explore all it has to offer.
