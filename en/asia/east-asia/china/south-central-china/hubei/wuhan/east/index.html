Introduction to East

Located in Wuhan, Hubei, South-central China, East is a bustling part of the city known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic views, and delicious cuisine. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the best places to visit, hidden gems, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and where to stay in East.

List of Places to Visit in East

East has something for everyone, with many culturally important attractions, natural reserves, scenic views, and delicious food. Below are some of the notable places to visit in East:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hubei Provincial Museum, which houses a vast collection of cultural and historical artifacts
  • Yellow Crane Tower, a historical site that dates back to the Song Dynasty
  • Guiyuan Buddhist Temple, which is one of the largest Buddhist temples in Wuhan
  • Baotong Temple, a beautiful Taoist temple that dates back to the Ming Dynasty

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • East Lake Scenic Area, a beautiful natural reserve with picturesque views, perfect for hiking, biking, and picnics
  • Wuhan Botanical Garden, which is known for its wide variety of plants and flowers from different parts of the world
  • Han Street, a modern street designed to blend perfectly with the natural landscape of the area, offering an exciting shopping and dining experience
  • Mulan Lake National Wetland Park, a wetland reserve that is home to various exotic bird species and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East is a haven for hikers and bikers, with several miles of well-maintained trails and paths that cut through forests, hills, and along lakeshores. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • East Lake Greenway, a 10-mile path that follows the shore of East Lake and offers spectacular views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Jinshan Greenway, a 3-mile paved path designed for bikers that passes through several parks and scenic areas of East.
  • Lumo Mountain, a 1.5-mile hike that takes you to the top of Lumo Mountain, where you can see some of the most beautiful views of the East Lake Scenic Area.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, East offers many other exciting activities and tours for visitors, such as:

  • Dragon Boat Festival, an annual event that takes place on East Lake, where competitors row traditional boats to the beat of drums and to the cheers of the crowds.
  • Wuhan Wanda Theme Park, a modern theme park that offers thrilling rides, including a roller coaster and water rides.
  • Wuhan Happy Valley, another popular theme park with a wide range of activities for visitors of all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

If you are planning a week-long trip to East, below is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Hubei Provincial Museum
Day 2 Yellow Crane Tower and Guiyuan Buddhist Temple
Day 3 Baotong Temple and Wuhan Botanical Garden
Day 4-5 Hiking, Biking, and exploring the East Lake area
Day 6 Han Street and Mulan Lake National Wetland Park
Day 7 Wuhan Wanda Theme Park

Food and Drink Scene of East

East is known for its savory and spicy cuisine, featuring local delicacies such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Re Gan Mian, a spicy noodle dish that is popular in Wuhan
  • Doupi, a dish made from tofu, meat, and vegetables
  • Xiaolongxia, spicy crayfish that is a favorite among locals

Local Beverages

  • Taihe Watermelon and Pear Juice, a refreshing blend of watermelon and pear juice
  • Huangchuan Three-Flowers Tea, a tea made from chrysanthemum, honeysuckle, and pagoda flower

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Sheraton Grand Wuhan Hankou Hotel, a 5-star hotel with excellent amenities and services
  • Go-Yin-Chu-Hotels Branch East Lake, a budget-friendly guesthouse that is clean and comfortable
  • Wuhan Mandi International Youth Hostel, a hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms at affordable rates

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking to buy souvenirs or local products to bring home, some of the popular choices include Huangpi Glass, Jianli Rice Wine, and handmade pottery and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East is steeped in rich cultural heritage, and locals are proud of their customs and traditions. Some of the local customs include:

  • Bowing to elders as a sign of respect
  • Serve tea as a sign of hospitality
  • Avoiding “four” as it sounds similar to the word for death

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is between March and May and September and November when the weather is pleasant, and the number of tourists is low. However, if you are looking to experience the colorful culture and festivals, you may want to visit during the Dragon Boat Festival in June or the Mid-Autumn Festival in September.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully when visiting temples or other religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and nightlife
  • Learn a few phrases in Mandarin

Don’t:

  • Touch or point with your feet as it is considered rude.
  • Eat or drink while walking on the streets
  • Discuss sensitive topics such as politics or religion with strangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East is generally safe for visitors, but it is always good to take precautions such as:

  • Lock your valuables in the hotel safe
  • Stay in well-lit areas and avoid walking alone at night

Health in East

Before traveling to East, make sure you update your routine vaccinations and take malaria pills if necessary. It is also advisable to drink bottled water, avoid street food, and practice good personal hygiene.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East

East can be accessed by several airports, including Wuhan Tianhe International Airport and Ezhou Wuchang Airport.

Bus Stations Near East

Several bus stations, including Wuhan East Bus Station, Wuhan Wangjiawan Bus Station, and Wuhan Yangchunhu Bus Station, serve East.

Other Options

You can also access East by train, taxi or private car.

How to Get Around in East

Getting around in East is easy, with several transportation options such as public buses, taxis, and bikes for rent. You can also explore East on foot or hire a guide for an insider’s view.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Wuhan, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, such as:

  • Wudang Mountain, a mountain range known for its Taoist temples and natural beauty
  • Hankou Jiangtan Park, a beautiful public park that offers scenic views of the city
  • Yellow Crane Tower Park, a park that offers spectacular views of the Yangtze River

Conclusion

East is a beautifully diverse part of Wuhan, offering something for everyone, from its rich cultural heritage to its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. This comprehensive travel guide highlights the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around in East. Plan your trip to East today and experience the best of Wuhan’s East!
