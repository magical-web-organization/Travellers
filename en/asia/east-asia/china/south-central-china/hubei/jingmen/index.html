Jingmen is a city located in the Hubei province of South-central China. With a history that dates back to the Han dynasty, Jingmen boasts many culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Jingmen, the top hiking and biking routes, the best places to stay, where to go next, and more!

List of Places to Visit in Jingmen

Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Jingmen has something to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Jingmen classified according to their themes:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jingmen Ancient City Wall
  2. Jingmen Museum
  3. Jingchu Culture Square
  4. Wudang Mountains
  5. Yangtze River Dam

Natural Attractions of Jingmen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mt. Tianmen National Forest Park
  2. Jingmen Nanshan Mountain Scenic Area
  3. Guangji Temple
  4. Lushui Lake Scenic Area
  5. Xingdoushan National Forest Park

Local Culture and Customs of Jingmen

Jingmen is a city steeped with history and culture, and it is important to respect local customs and traditions when visiting. Some of the customs that may be different from other regions are:

  1. People offer tea to their guests to show respect.
  2. Red symbolizes good luck and joy.
  3. Elderly people are highly respected, and they are expected to be addressed as “aunty” and “uncle.”

Best Time to Visit Jingmen

The best time to visit Jingmen is from March to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Temperatures range from 15-26 °C during these months, which is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jingmen

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Jingmen:

Do:

  1. Try the local specialties such as Wudang mountain ginseng soup and Jingmen tofu skin
  2. Respect the local customs and traditions
  3. Bargain while shopping in the local markets

Don’t:

  1. Talk loudly or be disrespectful to local customs and traditions
  2. Take photographs of locals without their permission
  3. Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing

Safety and Health

Safety in Jingmen

Jingmen is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s always good to be aware of your surroundings and belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Jingmen

It is important to drink clean water and to wash hands before eating to avoid stomach and digestive infections. Avoid drinking tap water unless it’s boiled first.

Conclusion

Jingmen is a spectacular destination that is known for its natural attractions and historical architecture. From the Wudang Mountains to the Jingmen Ancient City Wall, there is no shortage of must-visit landmarks and destinations. With its unique food and drink scene, rich history, beautiful hikes, and cultural experiences, Jingmen is definitely worth visit on your next trip to China.
