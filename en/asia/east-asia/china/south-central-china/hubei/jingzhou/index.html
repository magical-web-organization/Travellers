Introduction to Jingzhou

Located in south-central China, Jingzhou is a city with rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful scenery. With a history spanning over 2,000 years, Jingzhou is dotted with ancient relics, museums, and historical sites that tell the story of China’s captivating past. Being a port city surrounded by the magnificent Changjiang River and Yangtze River, Jingzhou is also home to beautiful landscapes, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. It’s a city that offers something for everyone, so whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply seeking a new adventure, Jingzhou has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Jingzhou

Jingzhou boasts of a wide range of places to visit, each with its unique charm and appeal. Here are some of the must-see highlights of Jingzhou that you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jingzhou Ancient City Wall: This ancient wall dates back over 2,000 years and was first built during the Three Kingdoms period. It’s a testament to the city’s significant place in Chinese history, and visitors can walk along the top of the wall to take in the views of the city and surrounding countryside.
  • Jingzhou Museum: The museum is dedicated to showcasing the history of the Jingzhou region, with an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits dating back to the Han Dynasty (220 BC to 220 AD) and Ming Dynasty (1368-1644 AD).
  • The Tomb of Guiyuan: Guiyuan, the second emperor of Jingzhou, was buried here with his wife in 128 AD. The tomb is an impressive structure with intricate carvings depicting the emperor’s life and accomplishments.

Natural Attractions of Jingzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Wugongshan National Forest Park: Mount Wugongshan is a scenic mountain range located in the western part of Jingzhou, offering an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts or hiking enthusiasts. The park is filled with ancient temples, scenic views of the mountainous terrain as well as forests and waterfalls.
  • The Jingzhou Changjiangtourism Scenic Area: This area offers visitors the opportunity to see the confluence of the Changjiang River and Yangtze River – two of China’s longest rivers. You can take a boat ride on the river, rent a bike, visit nearby temples or picnic by the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jingzhou

Jingzhou offers many opportunities for hikers and bikers with its stunning scenery and natural wonders. Here are some popular trails perfect for hiking, biking, or running:

  • Mount Wugongshan National Forest Park: The park has several trails with breathtaking scenic views and is great for hiking, mountain climbing, and rock climbing.
  • Changjiangtourism Scenic Area: The area has various trails to choose from, each offering a different degree of hiking experience, and you can also rent bicycles to explore the region.

Best Things to Do in Jingzhou: Activities and Tours

Jingzhou is filled with many activities and tours that will keep you busy throughout your stay. Some of the different things to engage in the city include:

  • Tai Chi with a Local Master: Tai Chi is a popular activity that draws locals and tourists alike. You can take classes with a local master to learn more about this ancient martial art.
  • Shuimo (Chinese Calligraphy) Class: Learn the art of Chinese calligraphy with an experienced master.
  • ChangjiangTourism Lights Show: Witness stunning views of Jingzhou’s ancient city walls, temples, fountains, and local performances in the evening – it’s an experience that you’ll never forget.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jingzhou

Here’s a suggested 7-day itinerary for Jingzhou, which includes exploring the highlights of the city and its surroundings:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Jingzhou and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon getting familiar with the city and its surroundings. Visit Jingzhou Ancient Wall and the Museum in the evening.
  • Day 2: Get a taxi or ride a bike to Shizhuyuan Scenic Area and Yandi Shennong Temple. Spend the afternoon hiking or mountain climbing at Mount Wugongshan National Forest Park.
  • Day 3: Take a ChangjiangTourism Scenic Area boat tour and then visit the Tomb of Guiyuan.
  • Day 4: Day trip to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park in Huaihua, Hunan, where you will relax and explore the nature reserve.
  • Day 5: Do a Tai Chi class in the morning and then visit ancient Jinan city and the Confucius Temple.
  • Day 6: Take a stroll through Seasnail Eco-Park or rent a bicycle and explore the Jiangzhou area.
  • Day 7: Visit the Dongmen Ancient City, the Huanghe Lou, and the Xianpang Mountains in the morning before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Jingzhou

Jingzhou offers a variety of mouth-watering local dishes and drinks that will tantalize your taste buds while you’re exploring the city. Here are some of the must-try foods and drinks in Jingzhou:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spicy Crawfish: Jingzhou is famous for this delicacy, which is peeled, stir-fried with garlic, green onion, coriander, and chili peppers.
  • Huajuan: This is a popular traditional snack that symbolizes luck and prosperity in Chinese culture made from sticky rice mixed with meat, mushrooms, and green onions, wrapped in lotus leaf, and steamed.
  • Eel rice noodles: This delicious main meal is thick rice noodles with slices of eel’s meat, cooked with vegetables and spices, and served hot.
  • Wufangzhai bean paste: This tasty sweet paste made from mashed red beans and sugar is often used as a filling in traditional Chinese pastries.

Local Beverages

  • Jingzhou Huangguoshu Liquor: Huangguoshu is a famous local brand of distilled wine, which has been made in Jingzhou for over 2,000 years.
  • Jingzhou Tea: Jingzhou produces some of the best green tea in China and is a must-try for visitors to the city.

Where to Stay in Jingzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jingzhou has plenty of accommodation options, ranging from high-end hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Crowne Plaza Jingzhou: Located in the heart of the city, the Crowne Plaza is a luxurious five-star hotel offering world-class amenities and services.
  • Dongfang Hotel Jingzhou: This hotel is budget-friendly and offers a comfortable stay with its excellent facilities.
  • Jingzhou Ancient Town Guesthouse: This traditional guesthouse is located inside Jingzhou’s old city and is a great option for travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Jingzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jingzhou is known for its beautiful traditional and modern handicrafts, and it’s an excellent destination for souvenir shopping. Here are some of the local products you can bring home as gifts:

  • Jingzhou embroidery: This beautiful embroidery uses silk threads to create intricate patterns on fabric, making it an excellent souvenir to take back home.
  • Jingzhou porcelain: The city’s porcelain is well-known for its blue and white designs and is a must-buy for pottery lovers.
  • Jingzhou handicrafts: You can find a wide range of traditional handicrafts, including bamboo weaving, paper cutting, and wood carving in Jingzhou.

Local Culture and Customs of Jingzhou

Jiangzhou takes pride in its rich culture, and it’s always essential to understand and appreciate local traditions and customs while traveling. Some of the customs to keep in mind while in Jingzhou include:

  • Respect for elders and Ancestors: Jingzhou places great importance on the respect for elders and the living memory of relatives who have now passed on. Respectful forms of address are abundantly used in this regard.
  • Eating and Drinking: A standard feature of Jiangzhou meals is the ‘family style’ – where you order several dishes and share them in a group. It’s essential to eat everything with chopsticks or spoons and drink only hot water.
  • Festivals and Celebrations: Jingzhou has various traditional festivals and celebrations, including the Jingzhou Lantern Festival, celebrated on the fifteenth day of the Chinese New Year, and the Zhuge Liang Temple Fair.

Best Time to Visit Jingzhou

The best times to visit Jingzhou are spring (March to May) and autumn (October to November). The weather is comfortable and mild during these seasons and perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and site seeing. Summers (June to August) can be sweltering, while winters (December to February) are very cold and less conducive for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jingzhou

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Jingzhou:

Do:

  • Carry cash: Many smaller shops and restaurants in Jingzhou don’t accept credit cards, so it’s essential to carry some cash with you.
  • Dress appropriately: When visiting temples, dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering. Also, avoid dressing too casually at hotel lobbies and fine-dining restaurants.
  • Learn some Mandarin: Bring your phrasebook or use translation apps to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water: Always drink bottled water to avoid any stomach discomfort.
  • Don’t take photos without permission: Ask for permission before taking pictures of people, especially in areas like markets, where people may not want their images taken.
  • Don’t tip: Tipping isn’t practiced anywhere in China, including Jingzhou, so there’s no need to leave any extra money.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jingzhou

Jingzhou is generally considered safe, with low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard precautions like carrying your passports and valuables while out and about, keeping an eye on cash and being alert in crowded areas, and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Jingzhou

It’s essential to take proper precautions to avoid being sick while visiting Jingzhou, including:

  • Vaccinations: Speak to your doctor before you travel to ensure you are up to date on all required vaccinations.
  • Hygiene: Avoid drinking tap water and use bottled water for brushing your teeth. Also, wash your hands frequently and avoid uncooked or undercooked food.
  • Medical Insurance: Get comprehensive health insurance before traveling to make sure you’re covered in case of sickness or medical emergency.

Getting to Jingzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jingzhou is well-connected with other major cities in China, and getting there is quite easy. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Jingzhou

The nearest airport to Jingzhou is the Yichang Sanxia Airport, located approximately 214 km from the city. You can take a taxi or shuttle bus from the airport to Jingzhou.

Bus Stations Near Jingzhou

The Jingzhou bus station is the city’s main terminal for buses and coaches. You can take direct buses from Jingzhou to various destinations in China, including Wuhan, Changsha, and Yichang, among others.

Other Options

Jingzhou is also connected via trains to several major cities in China, including Shanghai, Beijing, and Guangzhou. For those who prefer traveling by car, there are car rental services available in Jingzhou, with a variety of vehicle options to choose from.

How to Get Around in Jingzhou

Getting around Jingzhou is quite easy and requires a bit of walking, biking, or taking a taxi. Taxis are readily available and an affordable transportation option in Jingzhou, but always make sure you agree on a fare before getting in. Many areas in Jingzhou are bike-friendly, and you can conveniently rent a bike to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Jingzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jingzhou is located in a region rich in attractions and natural wonders, so there are plenty of destinations and excursions to explore nearby. Some of the places you can visit from Jingzhou include:

  • Zhangjiajie National Forest Park: Located in the nearby city of Huaihua, Hunan, this natural wonder offers incredible views and outdoor activities.
  • Wuhan: The capital of Hubei Province, Wuhan boasts a rich cultural heritage and is home to many museums and historical sites.
  • Yellow Crane Tower: Located in the city of Wuhan, this ancient tower offers stunning views of the Yangtze River and surrounding landscapes.

Conclusion

Jingzhou is a city rich in culture and history, filled with natural wonders and outdoor adventures offering exciting activities and landmarks to explore. It’s an excellent destination for anyone looking to experience China’s unique blend of ancient culture and modern life. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums or hiking, Jingzhou has something to satisfy anyone’s interests. With this guide, you’re all set to explore and appreciate Jingzhou truly.
