Introduction to Guyuan

Located in Ningxia Province, Guyuan is a prefecture-level city with a population of over 1 million people. The city is situated in the semi-arid and mountainous region of Northwestern China, and its unique landscapes, rich culture, and history attract visitors from all around the world. With an average elevation of 2,000 meters, Guyuan boasts fascinatingly diverse geology and is renowned for its mineral resources and abundant wildlife. Guyuan is also known for its ancient ruins and cultural relics that have been dated back to over 2,000 years ago, making it a prestigious historical and cultural city.

List of Places to Visit in Guyuan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Guyuan Museum: Located in Yinchuan, the museum features various exhibitions, including a section dedicated to the Ming Dynasty’s history and culture.
  • Shuidonggou Relics: It’s an archaeological site with a history dating back to over 30,000 years ago, making it a significant historical and cultural site in China.
  • Sangke Prairie: A famous site for photography, the place is famous for its beautiful landscape, vast grasslands, and herds of sheep and other grazing animals.
  • Baijiacheng Kingdom Ruins: The site was built during the Wei and Jin Dynasties and is located in Taole, providing a glimpse into China’s ancient history.

Natural Attractions of Guyuan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sanguan Mountain: The mountain is a famous UNESCO Global Geopark with unique geology, featuring rare fossils and stunning natural landscapes. It’s an excellent spot for hiking and exploring geological wonders.
  • North Xumi Mountain National Forest Park: Located in Jingyuan County, it’s a paradise for nature lovers, with breathtaking views of rivers, mountains, lakes, and pristine forests. Visitors can indulge in hiking, camping or exploring the extensive forest trails.
  • Xumishan Grottoes: It’s an attractive cultural and artistic site that boasts over 160 well-preserved Buddhist caves’ one of the most magnificent Grotto in China.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guyuan

If hiking or biking is on your agenda, Guyuan has some stunning trails that are worth exploring. You can take a hike in the North Xumi Mountain National Forest Park or along the Sangke Prairie where you can trek along the high plateau’s undulating, winding paths while enjoying the beautiful natural landscapes. Another excellent spot for hiking is Sanguan Mountain with trails looping along the cliffs, good for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Guyuan: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to keep you engaged and entertained in Guyuan. One of the unique things to try is renting a traditional ox-drawn cart tour at the Sangke Prairie, where you can have a slow and relaxing ride surrounded by serene natural beauty. For those seeking adventure, take a thrilling zipline ride at the North Xumi Mountain National Forest Park or take a self-driving adventure on an ATV in Sanguan Mountain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Guyuan

  • Day 1: Explore the ancient ruins of Baijiacheng Kingdom Ruins and immerse in its rich history.
  • Day 2-3: Hike the mountain trail at North Xumi Mountain National Forest Park, and experience the beauty of deep forest and stunning views of rivers and mountains.
  • Day 4: Discover the archaeological site of Shuidonggou Relics and get a glimpse of China’s ancient history.
  • Day 5: Take a break and enjoy a ride on an ox-drawn cart in Sangke Prairie.
  • Day 6: Explore the unique geological features and fossils at Sanguan Mountain.
  • Day 7: End your trip with a leisurely stroll through Xumishan Grottoes.

Food and Drink Scene of Guyuan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Xiangyun Niangpi: It’s a type of cold noodles made of wheat flour and served with vegetables and sesame sauce.
  • Guyuan Mutton: The locals are particularly fond of mutton dishes due to the city’s rich pastoral culture. You can enjoy mutton dishes roasted, boiled, or sautéed in many restaurants.
  • Guyuan Roubing: It’s a thin slice of mutton used to wrap the leek and sautéed with a special sauce.

Local Beverages

Guyuan is known for its special local rice wine and strong liquor made of buckwheat praised for their unique flavor and aroma. The locals prefer to drink tea as their favorite hot beverage, particularly the famous mountain Pu’er tea.

Where to Stay in Guyuan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options to choose from in Guyuan, ranging from budget-friendly to high-end hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Guyuan Hotel: A high-end hotel located in the heart of Guyuan City with luxurious rooms and modern amenities.
  • Jingfeng Hotel: A mid-range hotel offering clean, comfortable rooms and excellent customer service.
  • Jinye Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse with modest rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Guyuan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists can choose from a variety of souvenirs, including:

  • Wool products: The city is famous for its woolen products, and visitors can buy woolen blankets, mittens, and scarfs.
  • Hua Shan Medicinal Materials Market: The market offers numerous medicinal herbs, skincare products, and ginseng roots.
  • Floral paintings: The city is known for its delicate flower drawings, depicting blooming peonies, chrysanthemums, and magnolias.

Local Culture and Customs of Guyuan

The locals in Guyuan are warm and hospitable, and their customs and traditions reflect a mix of nomadic and farming cultures. Visitors are expected to show respect for the local traditions and customs, particularly during festivals such as the Yinchuan Bayanbulak Festival, which is held in August every year.

Best Time to Visit Guyuan

The best time to visit Guyuan is between June to September when the city experiences mild temperatures and lovely weather. Springtime is the next best season when flowers are in bloom; however, be prepared for strong winds interspersed throughout this season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guyuan

Do:

  • Try the local mutton dishes and traditional ox-driven cart ride at Sangke Prairie.
  • Take a guided tour of the North Xumi Mountain National Forest Park or learn about the Grotto at Sanguan Mountain.
  • Respect the locals’ customs and traditions and immerse yourself in the culture.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Engage in any behavior that will damage the natural and cultural environment in your travels.
  • Spit or litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guyuan

Guyuan is a very safe place to visit. Violent crime is rare, and tourists can explore with ease. However, it’s always advisable to take the necessary precautions as you would do in any other city. Avoid wandering off alone at night and try to stay at well-lit areas.

Health in Guyuan

Visitors are urged to consult with their local travel clinic before traveling to China for up-to-date recommendations on vaccination and other health-related travel precautions.

Getting to Guyuan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Guyuan

The closest airport to Guyuan is the Yinchuan Hedong International Airport, which is approximately 188 kilometers away. Travelers can take a taxi or bus from Yinchuan to Guyuan, which takes roughly three hours.

Bus Stations Near Guyuan

Guyuan is well-connected by bus, both domestically and internationally. Visitors can take a bus from Yinchuan or Lanzhou to Guyuan, with the travel time approximately three hours and four hours, respectively.

Other Options

Travelers can also use the local train station, which is located in Guyuan County, and provides overnight train services to major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Guyuan

Travelers can easily get around Guyuan by taxi, bus, or bike rental. Hiring a car with a driver is also a popular option, particularly for visitors traveling to remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Guyuan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can continue their exploration of the Ningxia province or travel further afield to cities such as Xi’an, Zhangye, or Dunhuang.

Conclusion

Guyuan is an incredible travel destination that offers visitors a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions. From its stunning mountain ranges, UNESCO Global Geopark, ancient ruins, rich culture, and delicious cuisine, visitors are sure to have a memorable time here. Whether you are looking for adventure, history, culture, or relaxation, Guyuan has something for every traveler.
