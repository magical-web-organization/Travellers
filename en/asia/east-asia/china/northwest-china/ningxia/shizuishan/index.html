Introduction to Shizuishan

Shizuishan is a prefecture-level city in Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, located in the northwestern part of China. As one of the smallest cities in the region, it has a rich cultural heritage and natural landscape to offer visitors. Shizuishan is surrounded by sand dunes, snow-capped mountains and flowing rivers, creating diverse and contrasting scenery that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Visitors can explore the many cultural attractions, taste delicious local cuisine, and experience the unique customs of the Hui people during their visit to Shizuishan. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with a list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, local culture, safety tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Shizuishan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shizuishan Hui Museum: This museum showcases the unique culture of the Hui people, China’s largest Muslim minority group. Visitors can learn about their history, art, culture, and way of life.
  • Hengshan Hanging Temple: This temple is built into the side of a cliff and dates back to the Northern Wei Dynasty. It is a perfect example of ancient Chinese architectural techniques and showcases the rich history of Buddhism in China.
  • Helan Mountain Petroglyphs: This collection of petroglyphs dates back to the Western Xia Dynasty and can be found in the Helan Mountains. The carvings depict scenes from ancient life, including hunting, farming, and religious rituals.

Natural Attractions of Shizuishan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zhenbeibao Scenic Area: This area offers beautiful views of the Yellow River and is a perfect spot for hiking and picnicking. Visitors can see 28 different peaks, each with its name, and enjoy the stunning views of the river valley.
  • Shahu Mountain National Forest Park: This park is located in the northern part of Shizuishan and offers beautiful scenery with diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can hike or mountain bike on the trails and enjoy the views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Reshui Desert: Located on the outskirts of Shizuishan, this desert is perfect for adventure seekers. Visitors can go sandboarding, camel riding, and camping in the vast and picturesque dunes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shizuishan

  • Helan Mountain Hiking Trail: This trail spans about 50 km and offers beautiful views of the Helan Mountains. The trail winds through steep slopes and valleys and passes through several ancient tombs and temples.
  • Huangyangtan Scenic Area: The hiking trail in the Huangyangtan Scenic Area is around 3 km long and passes through a colorful canyon. The canyon is filled with natural springs, flowering plants, and lush greenery, making it a favorite spot for photographers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Shizuishan: Activities and Tours

  • Hui Culture Experience: Visitors can learn more about the Hui people by taking part in traditional cultural activities such as paper cutting, Hui opera, and other folk art performances.
  • Yellow River Rafting: Adventure seekers can take a rafting tour along the Yellow River, starting from the Zhenbeibao Scenic Area. The rafting experience uses high-quality safety equipment and is suitable for people of all ages.
  • Shizuishan Festivals: The city hosts various festivals throughout the year, such as the Helan Mountain Wine Festival, the Shizuishan Water Splashing Festival, and the Shizuishan International Marathon.

Food and Drink Scene of Shizuishan

Must-Try Dishes

  • 回苏肉: A traditional Hui dish made with sliced beef, onion, and peppers. It is served with a rich and spicy sauce and is especially delicious when eaten with rice.
  • 手抓羊肉: This dish is made with roasted lamb and served with bread or naan. The meat is tender and flavorful, and the bread is crispy and chewy.
  • 驴打滚: A popular dessert in Shizuishan, this sweet treat is made with glutinous rice flour and red bean paste. It is shaped like a roll and coated in sesame seeds.

Local Beverages

  • Helan Mountain Wine: This famous wine is made from grapes grown on the slopes of the Helan Mountains. It has a sweet aroma and a smooth and mellow taste.
  • Qipao Tea: A traditional tea made with natural herbs grown in the Ningxia region. It is known for its health properties and is a favorite among the locals.

Where to Stay in Shizuishan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shizuishan Hui Hotel: This hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms with traditional Hui decor. It also has a restaurant that serves delicious local cuisine.
  • Ningxia Kangyuan Hotel: This hotel is located near Zhenbeibao Scenic Area and offers spacious rooms with beautiful views of the Yellow River. It also has a restaurant and lounge that serves both Chinese and Western dishes.
  • Shizuishan Folk Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in a traditional courtyard house and offers a unique experience for visitors. It has clean and comfortable rooms with modern amenities and serves delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Shizuishan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Helan Mountain Wine: Visitors can buy bottles of this famous wine as a souvenir or as a gift for friends and family.
  • Hui Embroidery: This traditional craft is practiced by the Hui people and is known for its intricate design and bright colors.
  • Ningxia Dates: Known for their sweet and juicy taste, these dates are a popular snack in Ningxia and are usually sold in decorative boxes.

Local Culture and Customs of Shizuishan

The Hui people are the largest Muslim minority group in China and have their unique culture and customs. Visitors should be respectful of their customs, dress modestly, and follow local etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Shizuishan

The best time to visit Shizuishan is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny. The temperature can reach up to 30°C in the summer months, and visitors can enjoy various outdoor activities such as hiking, rafting, and exploring the desert. During the winter months, the temperature can drop to -10°C, and the city may experience snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shizuishan

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and attending cultural events.
  • Try local food and drink and experience the unique customs of the Hui people.
  • Respect local customs and etiquette, such as taking off shoes before entering someone’s home or mosque.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission, especially of local people and religious sites.
  • Show disrespect towards the Hui people and their culture.
  • Engage in any illegal or immoral activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shizuishan

Shizuishan is a safe city, and visitors can explore the city and its surroundings without worrying about their safety. However, visitors should take normal precautions such as not carrying large amounts of money or valuables when traveling and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Shizuishan

Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid tap water to prevent stomach illnesses. Visitors should also carry their necessary medication and speak to their doctor before traveling to the region if they have any underlying medical conditions.

Getting to Shizuishan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shizuishan

  • Yinchuan Hedong International Airport: This airport is located in Yinchuan, the capital city of Ningxia, and is around 35 km away from Shizuishan.

Bus Stations Near Shizuishan

  • Shizuishan Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and offers regular services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Train: Yinchuan Railway Station is located in Yinchuan and offers train services to other cities in China.

How to Get Around in Shizuishan

Visitors can take buses, taxis, or hire private vehicles to get around Shizuishan. The city is compact, and many attractions are located within a short distance from each other.

Where to Go Next from Shizuishan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yinchuan: The capital city of Ningxia is around 70 km from Shizuishan and offers historical sites and cultural attractions, such as the Ningxia Museum and the Western Xia Tombs.
  • Zhongwei: This city is around 100 km from Shizuishan and is known for its fortress, Gao Miao Temple, and the UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Tengger Desert and its Singing Dunes.

Conclusion

Shizuishan is an exceptional destination for visitors who want to explore the rich culture and diverse landscape of Ningxia. Visitors can enjoy natural attractions, cultural sites, delicious cuisine, and unique customs during their visit. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip with ease and enjoy all that Shizuishan has to offer.
