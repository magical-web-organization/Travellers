Introduction to Yongning

Located in the Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region in Northwest China, Yongning is an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. The city is nestled between the rolling hills of the Yellow River Valley and the vast expanses of the Gobi Desert, making it a perfect gateway to scenic adventures.

Yongning is known for its rich historical and cultural heritage, dating back to the Western Xia Dynasty that ruled the region from the 11th to the 13th century. Ancient temples, museums, and historical sites abound in and around the city, providing insight into local traditions and customs. With its stunning natural beauty and eco-friendly tourism initiatives, Yongning is an ideal destination for adventurous travelers seeking to immerse themselves in the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Yongning

Yongning offers a diverse range of attractions, including museums, historical sites, natural landmarks, hiking trails, and more. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Western Xia Mausoleum: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a vast complex of ancient tombs and monuments from the Western Xia Dynasty, offering a unique glimpse into the lives and beliefs of the rulers of the region.

  • Ningxia Museum: This modern museum showcases the history and culture of Ningxia, including exhibitions on folk art, calligraphy, and Buddhism.

  • Helan Mountain Rock Art: The rock art of Helan Mountain is one of the most extensive and well-preserved collections of ancient rock art in China, featuring images of animals, people, and religious symbols.

Natural Attractions of Yongning: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sand Lake Scenic Area: (Shapotou Scenic Area) This natural wonder boasts vast sand dunes, crystal-clear lakes, and a wealth of rare plant and animal species. Visitors can enjoy camel rides, desert hikes, and water sports.

  • Zhangye Danxia National Geological Park: Just a short drive from Yongning sits one of China’s most unique national parks. Known for its colorful rock formations, stunning mountain ranges and hiking trails, Zhangye Danxia is a must-visit destination.

  • Helan Mountains Scenic Area: This scenic area nestled in the foothills of the Helan Mountains offers stunning views of the Yellow River and the surrounding valley. With its winding trails and serene spiritual sites, it’s a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Yongning.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yongning

Yongning offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from gentle strolls through scenic parks to challenging mountain treks. Some popular routes include:

  • Shuidonggou National Forest Park: This park has a variety of hiking trails through pine forests and scenic meadows.

  • Western Xia Mausoleum Forest Park: This park surrounds the UNESCO World Heritage site of the same name and offers several hiking routes in the natural environment of yellow river basin with a mix of marsh and hills.

Best Things to Do in Yongning: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Riding: Experience the desert life with a peaceful stroll atop a camel’s back at the Sand Lake scenic area.

  • Wine Tasting: Ningxia is a growing destination for fine wine, and several wineries offer tours and tastings of local varietals.

  • Ningxia ‘Sai Ma’ Horses: These high-stepping horses are a prized breed in Ningxia and make for an unforgettable cultural experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yongning

Day 1: Arrive in Yongning, explore the city center, taste local specialties, and visit the Ningxia Museum. Day 2: Visit the Western Xia Mausoleum complex and explore the traditional architecture and culture of the Western Xia. Day 3: Take a camel ride and view the stunning Sand Lake Scenic Area. Day 4: Head to the Helan Mountains Scenic Area and hike or drive up to the spiritual Helan Mountains along with tea drinking and scenic views sightseeing. Day 5: Visit Shuidonggou National Forest Park and take a leisurely walk or bike ride in the natural habitat. Day 6: Go horse riding and wine tasting at some of Ningxia’s local wineries that are found in the nearby mountains. Day 7: Depart Yongning and head on to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Yongning

Yongning boasts a rich food culture with many local specialties worth trying. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ningxia Lamb: This tender and juicy lamb is raised in the region’s high-altitude meadows and is a hallmark of Ningxia cuisine.

  • Sour Plum Juice: This refreshing drink is made from fermented plums and is a popular local beverage enjoyed throughout the year.

Local Beverages

  • Zongzi: These glutinous rice dumplings stuffed with meat, mushrooms, and other fillings are a symbol of traditional Chinese cuisine and culture.

  • Grape Wine: Given the huge grape growing scene in Ningxia, wineries are bringing their methods from the west and hoping to bring the grape wine culture to the area too.

Where to Stay in Yongning: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yongning has a range of accommodation options at varying price points, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

  • Ningxia PartYard International Hostel: Situated in the heart of the city, it has a western style and clean amenities with decent pricing.

  • Helanshan Grand Hotel: A more luxurious option with scenic views and quality services, albeit a larger price point.

  • Helan Country Guesthouse: Experience local hospitality with this cozy and comfortable guesthouse, situated just at the foot of the Helan Mountains.

What to Buy in Yongning: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Goji Berries: Ningxia is known for its high-quality, antioxidant-rich goji berries, which can be found in many local shops and markets.

  • Woolen Handicrafts: The region’s sheep-raising history has led to a high-quality wool industry, and visitors can find unique woolen products such as blankets and clothing at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Yongning

Yongning has a rich cultural heritage, with a large population of Hui Muslims and active Buddhist and Taoist communities. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including covering up when visiting religious sites and avoiding alcohol during Islamic religious festivals.

Best Time to Visit Yongning

The best time to visit Yongning is during the spring and autumn, when the temperature is mild and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter is cold and dry, though the sand dunes offer a unique winter-sports setting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yongning

Do:

  • Research and pre-plan your itinerary to make the most of your time in Yongning.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without permission in religious places.

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public places.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yongning

Yongning is generally safe for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should still exercise common sense and take necessary precautions, such as locking up valuables and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Yongning

Visitors should be aware of the risks of sun exposure and dehydration when hiking and exploring outdoor areas. It’s recommended to pack sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water to avoid heatstroke and other heat-related illnesses.

Getting to Yongning: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yongning

The nearest airport is Yinchuan Hedong International Airport, which has multiple daily flights to and from most major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Yongning

The main long-distance bus station is Yongning Bus Station, which has departures to many cities in and around Ningxia.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and private drivers are also available for hire, with prices varying depending on the distance and the mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Yongning

Taxis and buses are the most convenient ways to get around Yongning city. Buses may have irregular schedules, so it’s best to check beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Yongning: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhongwei: Located 47km south of Yongning, this ancient city is known for its stunning Bell and Drum Tower and adventurous dune buggies rides in Zhongwei-Shapotou Tourist Zone.

  • Yinchuan: The capital city of Ningxia, located about 70km from Yongning is known for its unique architecture, such as the Nanguan Mosque and the Zhongshan Pagoda.

Conclusion

Yongning is an amazing destination with a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, Yongning has something to offer for everyone.
