Introduction to Zhongwei

Zhongwei is a prefecture-level city situated in the north-central part of Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region in Northwest China. The city has a rich historical and cultural heritage and is renowned for its scenic beauty, making it an excellent tourist destination. Zhongwei is located at the intersection of the Tengger Desert and the Yellow River and offers an abundance of natural beauty.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through some of Zhongwei’s best attractions. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, we will cover everything to make your tour enjoyable.

List of Places to Visit in Zhongwei

If you’re in Zhongwei, you can’t miss out on some of the most renowned cultural and natural attractions. Below are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shapotou Scenic Spot: This destination is located in the southern part of the Tengger Desert and provides scenic views of the desert, the Yellow River, and unique sand dunes.

  • Sanguankou Folk Culture Village: It is an ancient village located in a valley surrounded by hills. This village offers an insight into traditional Chinese rural culture and architecture.

  • Gao Temple: This temple is an ancient temple built during the Tang Dynasty and is one of the most significant cultural heritage sites in Zhongwei.

  • Zhongwei Ancient City Wall: This city wall is over 600 years old and offers a unique experience of the life of the locals in Zhongwei.

Natural Attractions of Zhongwei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tengger Desert: Known as one of the four largest deserts in China, the Tengger Desert offers spectacular desert views that include the famous water-penetrating section of the Yellow River (Hukou Waterfall).

  • Xumishan Grottoes: This destination is renowned for its unique cliff caves and Buddhist carvings that date back to the Northern Wei Dynasty.

  • Helan Mountain National Forest Park: This park is a combination of natural scenery, cultural heritage, and adventure activities. It has several walking routes and is known for its scenic beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhongwei

Zhongwei offers a vast range of hiking and biking routes which provide an exemplary opportunity to explore the scenic beauty of the city. Some of the famous trails are:

  • Yueya Bay to Shapotou: This route follows the Yellow River and has excellent views of the sand dunes.

  • Shapotou to Tengger Desert: This route offers a unique experience of the desert, sand dunes, and the Yellow River.

  • Helan Mountain National Forest Park: This park offers several hiking trails and scenic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Zhongwei: Activities and Tours

Zhongwei offers a wide range of activities and tours to its visitors. Some of the best things to do in Zhongwei include:

  • Yellow River Rafting: Experience the thrill of rafting on the Yellow River with beautiful views of the surrounding areas.

  • Camel Riding in Tengger Desert: This activity is the perfect way to experience the beauty of the Tengger Desert.

  • Helan Mountain Wine Tour: Take a tour of the vineyards and wineries in the Helan Mountain region and taste some of the best wines in China.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhongwei

Zhongwei offers a unique blend of traditional Chinese cuisine and unique local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Liangpi: Liangpi is a local dish made from wheat or rice flour and is mixed with vinegar, chili oil, and spicy sauce.

  • Lan Zhou Dao Xiao Mian: A noodle dish made from beef broth and hand-pulled noodles.

  • Liang Fen: A salad made with jelly noodles, coriander, garlic, peanuts, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Wolfberry Wine: A wine made from the Wolf Berry fruit and has a sweet and sour taste.

  • Yellow River Carp Wine: A wine made from carp that are caught in the Yellow River and has a unique flavor.

  • Zhong Wei Shuijing: This mineral water comes from the Helan Mountains and is known for its unique taste.

Where to Stay in Zhongwei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhongwei has several accommodation options to choose from, ranging from luxury hotels to budget guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Zhongwei:

  • Crowne Plaza Zhongwei: A luxury 5-star hotel providing upscale accommodation to its guests.

  • Helan Mountain International Hotel: A modern and well-equipped 4-star hotel located in the heart of Zhongwei.

  • Zhongwei Liujiaxia International Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel providing good accommodation for backpackers.

What to Buy in Zhongwei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhongwei’s rich culture and heritage have resulted in some unique shopping opportunities. Here are some of the best local products to buy as souvenirs:

  • Helan Jade: Helan Mountain is known for the unique jade stones found there, and visitors can buy jade trinkets as souvenirs.

  • Inkstones: Zhongwei is famous for its inkstones, which are commonly used by calligraphers and artists.

  • Haoshun Vinegar: This vinegar is made from high-quality qingzhen rice, and it has a unique flavor and aroma.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhongwei

Zhongwei has a rich cultural heritage that is still prevalent in the daily lives of the people. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and be mindful of their behavior. Some significant things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.

  • Ask permission before taking photographs of locals.

  • Refrain from smoking in public places.

  • Avoid eating beef in Muslim restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Zhongwei

The best time to visit Zhongwei is from April to October when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhongwei

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Carry loose clothing and comfortable footwear.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

  • Keep a valid ID document with you at all times.

Don’t:

  • Engage in unethical and immoral practices.

  • Discard waste in public places.

  • Get involved in arguments with locals.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhongwei

Zhongwei is a relatively safe place, with low crime rates. However, visitors are still advised to take precautions and be mindful of their belongings and surroundings.

Health in Zhongwei

Visitors are advised to be cautious while drinking water in Zhongwei. It is recommended that they stick to bottled water or boiled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Zhongwei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhongwei

Zhongwei does not have its airport, but the closest airport is Yinchuan Hedong International Airport. The airport is approximately 100 km away, and visitors can take a taxi or bus to Zhongwei.

Bus Stations Near Zhongwei

Zhongwei Bus Station is the main bus station and most convenient for arriving visitors. It has regular buses from nearby cities like Yinchuan and Lanzhou.

Other Options

Another convenient way to get to Zhongwei is by train. The Zhongwei Railway Station is well-connected to several cities in China, including Lanzhou, Xian, and Beijing.

How to Get Around in Zhongwei

Zhongwei has several transportation options available to visitors, including buses, taxis, and hired vehicles. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorcycles to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Zhongwei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more destinations around Zhongwei, here are some places to consider:

  • Yinchuan: The capital of Ningxia and boasts of several cultural and historical destinations like the Western Xia Tombs and 108 Pagodas.

  • Lanzhou: Known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, Lanzhou is famous for its Gansu Provincial Museum, Yellow River Scenery Park, and waterwheels.

  • Inner Mongolia: This region is famous for its natural beauty, vast desert landscapes, and unique culture.

Conclusion

If you’re planning a trip to Zhongwei, you’re in for a treat. The city offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it an excellent tourist destination.
