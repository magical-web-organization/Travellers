Introduction to Shenmu

Located in the northernmost part of Shaanxi Province, Shenmu is a fascinating destination in Northwest China. It is known for its history, natural beauty, and unique culture. Despite being off-the-beaten-path, Shenmu is a great destination for adventurous travelers looking to explore China beyond the well-trodden tourist trail. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Shenmu, from must-see attractions to practical information on transportation and safety.

List of Places to Visit in Shenmu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Shenmu Cultural Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of Shenmu, from its ancient past to modern times. Don’t miss the exhibition on the famous Yellow River and its impact on the region.

  2. Fuxi Temple: Dedicated to the Chinese mythological figure Fuxi, this temple has a long history and is a must-visit for those interested in religion and folklore.

  3. Baojiayu Castle Ruins: These ruins are part of the Great Wall of China and are a testament to the region’s rich military history. The site offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Shenmu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wangjiazhai Canyon: This scenic canyon features unique rock formations, waterfalls, and a suspension bridge. It’s a great spot for hiking and picnicking, and the views are breathtaking.

  2. Zhanghe National Wetland Park: This park is the largest freshwater wetland in Northwest China and is home to a diverse array of plant and animal species. Take a boat ride to explore the wetlands or enjoy a leisurely stroll on the boardwalks.

  3. Liujiapo Scenic Area: This area is known for its beautiful rock formations and scenic views. It’s a great place for hiking, and there are several well-marked trails to choose from.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shenmu

  1. Taiping Forest Park Hiking Trail: This hiking trail is about 6 km long and leads through a lush forest with plenty of scenic vistas. The trail is well-marked and suitable for hikers of all skill levels.

  2. Miaoling Mountain Trail: This challenging hiking trail is about 12 km long and leads to the top of Miaoling Mountain. The trail is steep and rocky, but the views from the summit are well worth the effort.

  3. Yulin-Guyuan Highway Cycling Route: This cycling route is about 137 km long and leads through some of the most scenic areas of Shenmu. The route starts at Yulin and ends at Guyuan, passing through picturesque villages and stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Shenmu: Activities and Tours

  1. Yellow River Rafting Tour: Take a thrilling rafting tour down the Yellow River and enjoy the stunning scenery along the way. This is a great activity for adventure-seekers and nature-lovers alike.

  2. Folk Cultural Village Tour: This tour takes you to the heart of the local culture, where you can learn about traditional arts and crafts, music, and food. It’s a great way to experience the unique culture of Shenmu.

  3. Ancient City Wall Night Tour: Take a guided tour of the ancient city wall of Shenmu at night and see the city from a different perspective. The tour includes a traditional Chinese dinner and ends with a visit to a local night market.

Food and Drink Scene of Shenmu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Yangrou Paomo: This hearty meal consists of shredded lamb served in a thick bread soup. It’s a local specialty and a must-try for foodies.

  2. Biang Biang Noodles: These thick, hand-pulled noodles are served with a spicy sauce and a variety of toppings. They’re a popular street food in Shenmu.

  3. Tanghulu: This sweet snack consists of skewered fruits, like hawthorns, coated in sugar syrup. It’s a popular treat for locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  1. Juniper Berry Wine: This strong, aromatic wine is made from juniper berries and is a favorite drink among locals.

  2. Yellow River Beer: This locally brewed beer is light and refreshing and goes well with spicy food.

Where to Stay in Shenmu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Shenmu Xianju Business Hotel: This hotel is centrally located in the city and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  2. Yunhui Hotel: This hotel is located near the Wangjiazhai Canyon and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. It’s a great choice for nature-lovers.

  3. Motel 168 Shenmu: This budget hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at a reasonable price. It’s a good choice for travelers on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Shenmu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Terracotta Warriors Replica: These miniature replicas of the famous Terracotta Warriors of Xi’an make great souvenirs and gifts.

  2. Juniper Berry Soap: This locally made soap is made from juniper berries and is known for its antiseptic properties and refreshing scent.

  3. Huangshan Maofeng Tea: This high-quality green tea is grown in nearby Huangshan and is a popular souvenir among tea-lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Shenmu

Shenmu has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this through the city’s architecture, cuisine, and daily life. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it’s easy to strike up conversations with them. It’s important to respect local customs, like taking off shoes before entering a home or temple, and to dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Shenmu

The best time to visit Shenmu is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are cold and dry, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shenmu

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Learn about the local culture and customs
  • Visit the natural attractions, like the Wangjiazhai Canyon and Zhanghe National Wetland Park

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other cultural sites
  • Take photos without asking permission from locals
  • Litter or disrespect the natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Shenmu

Shenmu is generally a safe destination, but it’s important to take basic precautions, like avoiding unlit areas at night and protecting valuables from pickpockets. When hiking or exploring natural areas, always follow safety guidelines and don’t take unnecessary risks.

Health in Shenmu

The tap water in Shenmu is not safe to drink, so always drink bottled water or boiled water. The air quality can also be poor, especially during the winter months, so it’s a good idea to wear a face mask if you have respiratory issues.

Getting to Shenmu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shenmu

The nearest airport to Shenmu is Yulin Yuyang Airport, which is about 150 km away. It has regular flights to major Chinese cities, like Beijing and Shanghai.

Bus Stations Near Shenmu

Shenmu has a central bus station that offers direct connections to major cities in China, like Xi’an and Yulin.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire, but it’s important to negotiate the fare beforehand and to ensure that the driver has proper licensing and insurance.

How to Get Around in Shenmu

Public buses and taxis are the most convenient and affordable ways to get around Shenmu. Bicycles can also be rented from local vendors, which is a great way to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Shenmu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Xi’an: The ancient capital of China is located about 300 km from Shenmu and is known for its rich history, including the Terracotta Warriors and the ancient city wall.

  2. Yulin: This city is located about 80 km from Shenmu and is known for its stunning natural scenery, including the Zhenbeitai Mountain and the Red Stone Gorge.

Conclusion

Shenmu is a hidden gem in Northwest China, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, cultural experiences, or just exploring a different side of China, Shenmu is well worth a visit. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!
