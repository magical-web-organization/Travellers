Introduction to Xianyang

Xianyang, located in the northwest part of China’s Shaanxi Province, is a city with a rich history dating back to the Qin Dynasty. It was once the capital for thirteen dynasties and is home to many historical sites and cultural relics. Visitors can explore terracotta warriors, ancient tombs, and temples, as well as indulge in the city’s vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Xianyang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Terracotta Army

Undoubtedly one of the top highlights of Xianyang, the Terracotta Army is a world-renowned attraction. This UNESCO World Heritage Site was discovered in 1974 by farmers and is one of the greatest archaeological finds of the 20th century. The Terracotta Army consists of thousands of life-sized clay soldiers and horses arranged in battle formations to protect Emperor Qin Shi Huang in the afterlife.

Qianling Mausoleum

Situated on Liangshan Mountain, Qianling Mausoleum is the final resting place of Emperor Gaozong and Empress Wu Zetian of the Tang Dynasty. The complex contains over 60 pristinely preserved murals, which depict the life of the two rulers.

Famen Temple

Famen Temple, located in Fufeng County, is a famous Buddhist temple that dates back to the Eastern Han Dynasty. The highlight of the temple is the Famen Temple Pagoda, which houses several ancient Buddhist relics that are believed to be incredibly valuable. Visitors can also explore the temple’s museum and admire the architecture of the surrounding buildings.

Natural Attractions of Xianyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hukou Waterfall

Located on the Yellow River, Hukou Waterfall is the second-largest waterfall in China and is a breathtaking natural attraction. Visitors can take a scenic boat ride to the base of the waterfall, or hike to the top for stunning views of the surrounding canyon.

Mount Li

Mount Li, also called Li Shan, is a popular hiking destination located just outside of Xianyang. It is home to several scenic trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can also take a cable car to the summit for panoramic views of Xianyang and the surrounding area.

Phoenix Temple

Phoenix Temple is a scenic temple located on Phoenix Mountain that offers stunning views of Xianyang and the surrounding countryside. The temple is also home to several cultural relics, including a 1000-year-old bell and a jade Buddha statue.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xianyang

Mount Hua Shan

Mount Hua Shan, located just two hours from Xianyang, is one of China’s most popular hiking destinations. Visitors can hike along the famous Plank Walk or challenge themselves with the steep ascent to the summit. The trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Xian City Wall

The Xian City Wall, built during the Ming Dynasty, stretches for 14 kilometers and is the largest city wall in China. Visitors can rent bikes and explore the wall at their own pace, taking in stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside along the way.

Best Things to Do in Xianyang: Activities and Tours

Tang Dynasty Show

The Tang Dynasty Show is a popular cultural activity that showcases traditional Chinese music, dance, and costumes from the Tang Dynasty. The show is held at the Tang Dynasty Palace, and visitors can enjoy a delicious dinner while watching the performance.

Dumpling Banquet

Xi’an is famous for its dumplings, and visitors can indulge in a traditional dumpling banquet at one of the many restaurants around the city. The banquet includes over a dozen different types of dumplings, each with its filling and flavor.

Food and Drink Scene of Xianyang

Must-Try Dishes

Biang Biang Noodles

Biang Biang Noodles are a specialty of the Shaanxi province and are made by hand-pulling wide, flat noodles. The noodles are served in a spicy sauce or soup and are often topped with chili oil, garlic, and green onions.

Roujiamo

Roujiamo is a popular street food in Xianyang and is sometimes referred to as the Chinese hamburger. The dish is made by stuffing a thick bread roll with shredded pork, beef, or lamb before steaming or baking.

Local Beverages

Baijiu

Baijiu is a strong Chinese liquor that is made from rice, wheat, or barley. It is usually consumed during special occasions or as part of business and social gatherings.

Green Tea

Green tea is a staple drink in Xianyang and is often served as a welcome drink in hotels and guesthouses. Visitors can also find several tea houses in the city that serve a variety of different teas.

Vienna International Hotel Xianyang

The Vienna International Hotel Xianyang is a budget-friendly option located in the center of Xianyang. The hotel features comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Xianyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Terracotta Warriors Replica

Visitors can purchase small replicas of the Terracotta Warriors as a memorable souvenir of their trip to Xianyang. The replicas are available in several sizes, and visitors can watch local artisans create them by hand.

Hua Shan Tea

Hua Shan Tea is a popular local tea that is grown on the slopes of Mount Hua. Visitors can purchase several varieties, including black tea, green tea, and oolong tea, at local tea shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Xianyang

Xianyang has a rich cultural heritage dating back thousands of years. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the importance of family, the respect for elders, and the practice of Confucianism and Taoism. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and removing their shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Xianyang

The best time to visit Xianyang is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Visitors should avoid visiting during the summer months (June to August) when temperatures are hot and humid, and during the winter months (December to February), when temperatures can drop below freezing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xianyang

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including dumplings and biang biang noodles.
  • Visit historical sites, such as the Terracotta Army and Qianling Mausoleum.
  • Take a hike in the surrounding mountains, such as Mount Li and Hua Shan.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Haggle aggressively when shopping at local markets.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xianyang

Xianyang is a safe city for travelers, although visitors should take precautions against pickpocketing and scams. Visitors should also exercise caution when hiking in the mountains and should never hike alone.

Health in Xianyang

Visitors should take precautions to prevent foodborne illnesses and should only drink bottled water. Visitors should also be aware of the high levels of air pollution in the city and should consider wearing a mask during periods of high pollution.

Getting to Xianyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xianyang

The nearest airport to Xianyang is Xianyang International Airport, which is located just a few kilometers from the city center. The airport offers domestic and international flights to several destinations.

Bus Stations Near Xianyang

Xianyang has several bus stations, including the Xianyang Bus Station and Xianyang Yellow River Bus Station, which offer regular bus connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Xianyang by train, with several daily trains arriving at the Xianyang Railway Station from major cities across China.

How to Get Around in Xianyang

Visitors can get around Xianyang by taxi, bus, or bicycle. Taxis are readily available throughout the city, and visitors should make sure to negotiate the fare before getting in. Buses are an affordable option for getting around the city and are easy to navigate with the help of local maps.

Where to Go Next from Xianyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Luoyang

Luoyang is a historic city located just a few hours from Xianyang and is home to several important cultural attractions, including the Longmen Grottoes and the White Horse Temple.

Xi’an

Xi’an, the capital of Shaanxi Province, is located just a short distance from Xianyang and is home to several famous attractions, including the Terracotta Army, the Muslim Quarter, and the Wild Goose Pagoda.

Conclusion

Xianyang is a hidden gem in China’s Shaanxi province, offering visitors a fascinating blend of history, culture, and stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient tombs and temples, hiking in the surrounding mountains, or indulging in the city’s vibrant food and drink scene, Xianyang has something to offer every traveler.
