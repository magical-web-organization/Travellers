Introduction to Zhangye

Located in the Gansu province of Northwest China, Zhangye is a hidden gem for travelers seeking unique and off-the-beaten-path experiences. With a rich cultural history and breathtaking natural landscapes, Zhangye has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Zhangye

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zhangye Danxia National Geological Park:

This geological park is famous for its stunning multi-colored rock formations that look like a rainbow, and for being one of the top natural attractions in the country. The park offers hiking trails, sightseeing bus tours, and other outdoor activities.

  • Giant Buddha Temple:

One of the largest indoor reclining Buddha statues in China can be found at this temple. The temple grounds also feature beautiful gardens and traditional Chinese architecture.

  • Wooden Tower of Suoyang City:

This ancient tower was built during the Tang Dynasty and is the only remaining wooden tower from this era in China. The tower offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Zhangye: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mati Temple:

Located on the top of a mountain, the temple has spectacular views of the valley below. The temple itself is an architectural masterpiece made up of multiple buildings, gardens, and sculptures.

  • Qilian Mountains:

Located in the northeast of the city, the Qilian Mountains offer stunning views and great opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and mountaineering.

  • Zhongshan Bridge Scenic Area:

This area features the Zhongshan Bridge, which is considered a cultural relic due to its historical significance. The scenery around the bridge is also breathtaking, with mountainous terrain and a river flowing underneath.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhangye

  • Maling Gorge:

This is a scenic hiking trail that runs along a river and through pristine forests. The trail is filled with natural beauty, including waterfalls and unique rock formations.

  • Heihe River:

This biking route offers stunning views of the Heihe River and the surrounding mountains. The trail passes through local villages, giving visitors a glimpse into the daily lives of the locals.

Best Things to Do in Zhangye: Activities and Tours

  • Riding Camels in the Gobi Desert:

A trip to Zhangye isn’t complete without riding a camel in the Gobi Desert. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of the vast desert while experiencing the traditional way of life of the locals.

  • Traditional Chinese Cooking Classes:

Learn how to prepare traditional Chinese dishes by taking cooking classes from locals. The classes are taught in small groups, and the meals are enjoyed together.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhangye

Must-Try Dishes

  • Zhangye Roast Lamb:

This dish is the city’s specialty and is considered one of the most delicious lamb dishes in China. The lamb is roasted until it is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

  • Shougan Mian:

This is a traditional Uighur dish that consists of hand-pulled noodles and tender beef or lamb cooked in a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • China Wine:

This is a traditional Chinese rice wine that is popular in Zhangye. The wine is fermented with glutinous rice and has a slightly sweet taste.

  • Junmai Sake:

This sake is made with rice and water from the Qilian Mountains. The high-quality ingredients give the sake a smooth and subtle taste.

Where to Stay in Zhangye: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Zhangye Qicai Shiguang Hotel:

This hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable and affordable rooms. The hotel also has a restaurant and bar on-site.

  • Wutong Hostel:

This hostel is located near Zhangye Danxia National Geological Park and offers private and shared rooms. The hostel also has a common area where guests can mingle and relax.

  • Shangri-La Guesthouse:

This cozy guesthouse is located in the old town and offers private rooms with traditional Chinese decor. The guesthouse also has a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the city.

What to Buy in Zhangye: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Zhangye Wool Carpets:

These hand-woven carpets come in a variety of sizes and colors and are made with high-quality wool from local sheep.

  • Suoyang Black Tea:

This tea is grown in the Suoyang region and has a strong and robust flavor. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat illnesses.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhangye

  • Dress Code:

Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

  • Tipping:

Tipping is not expected in China, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Zhangye

The best time to visit Zhangye is in the spring (April - June) and fall (September - November). The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than in the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhangye

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks:

Zhangye is known for its delicious food and drink, so be sure to try as many local dishes and drinks as possible.

  • Respect local customs:

Be respectful of the locals’ customs and dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the environment:

Zhangye’s natural landscape is its biggest draw, so be sure to dispose of trash properly and leave no trace when hiking or camping.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhangye

Zhangye is a safe city for travelers. However, visitors should always take the usual precautions like securing valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Zhangye

Visitors should drink only bottled water and be sure to wash their hands frequently. It is also a good idea to bring any necessary medications and to seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Zhangye: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhangye

  • Zhangye Ganzhou Airport:

This airport is located 15 kilometers away from the city center and operates flights to several cities in China.

  • Jiayuguan Airport:

This airport is located 150 kilometers away from Zhangye and operates flights to several cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Zhangye

  • Zhangye Bus Station:

This bus station is located in the city center and operates buses to several cities in the region.

  • Zhangye West Bus Station:

This bus station is located in the west of the city and operates buses to several cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Zhangye from several major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Zhangye

Visitors can get around in Zhangye by taking taxis, public buses, or renting a car or scooter. Bicycles are also popular and can be rented from local shops.

Where to Go Next from Zhangye: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jiayuguan:

This historical city is located 150 kilometers from Zhangye and is home to the famous Jiayuguan Fortress.

  • Dunhuang:

This city is located 380 kilometers from Zhangye and is home to the famous Mogao Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Zhangye is a unique and exciting city that offers visitors a chance to experience both Chinese culture and stunning natural beauty. With its many attractions and activities, Zhangye is a destination that should not be missed by any traveler to China.
