Introduction to Pingliang

Tucked away in the northwest corner of China lies Pingliang, a city with a rich cultural heritage and a serene natural landscape. Pingliang is situated in the Gansu province and is known for its high-quality fruits and vegetables, including grapes, pears, and apples.

This historic city has been inhabited since the Neolithic period and has played a significant role in Chinese history. Pingliang was an important hub on the ancient Silk Road where merchants would stop to rest and trade. The city is filled with ancient temples and pagodas that offer glimpses into the area’s rich history.

Pingliang boasts a pleasant climate, with a dry and mild weather that makes it an ideal destination year-round. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or just a peaceful getaway, Pingliang is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Pingliang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kongtong Mountain

Kongtong Mountain is a visual delight and a must-visit destination in Pingliang. It is located about 15 kilometers from Pingliang’s city center, and it’s the birthplace of Taoism in China. The mountain has a unique shape, resembling a massive crab crawling along the ground.

The mountain is home to numerous Taoist temples, pagodas, and other structures that are spread throughout the area. The temples and pagodas are built on the mountain’s steep ledges, making for a breathtaking view. You can climb up to the crest of the mountain for an excellent scenic view of the landscape below.

Guangren Temple

Guangren Temple is a famous Buddhist temple located in the center of the city. The temple was constructed during the Eastern Han period and is famous for its distinctive historical and cultural features. Guangren Temple is a sprawling complex that comprises a variety of buildings, including gatehouses, garden pavilions, and several halls.

The temple boasts numerous ornate statues, including a 2000-year-old bronze statue of Guanyin, the goddess of mercy. Visitors can take part in Buddhist rituals and learn about the history of the temple during their visit.

Natural Attractions of Pingliang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shuiliandong National Forest Park

Shuiliandong National Forest Park is a picturesque national park located to the east of the Kongtong district. The park covers more than 100 hectares and is home to lush forests, mountain ranges, and a tranquil lake.

The park boasts a range of hiking trails, including the Shuiliandong trail and the Daxiushan trail, which provide excellent views of the surrounding scenery. Visitors can also enjoy boating in the lake or relax in the park’s serene environment.

Gantao Temple

Gantao Temple is situated on the slopes of the Gantao mountain and dates back to the Ming Dynasty. The temple is a secluded meditation center that offers stunning views of the surrounding natural scenery. The temple also has some unique features, including a glass-walled meditation room that opens up to the surrounding mountain views.

The temple has several hiking trails that take visitors through the scenic mountain ranges and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pingliang

Huanghe Shilin Scenic Area

The Huanghe Shilin Scenic Area is a vast natural site that is famous for its unique rock formations that resemble forests. The area is also home to several scenic walking trails that are perfect for hiking and biking.

Visitors can rent bikes to explore the area and enjoy the stunning vistas while they pedal along. The scenic area also has several restaurants and food stalls where you can stop and refuel after a day of biking or hiking.

Best Things to Do: Activities and Tours

Pingliang City Walking Tour

The Pingliang City Walking Tour is a fantastic way to explore the city’s cultural landmarks, historical sites, and bustling markets. The walking tour takes visitors through the city’s most popular destinations, including the Guangren Temple, the Pingliang Museum, and the Kongtong Mountain Scenic Area.

The tour guides visitors through the city’s fascinating history and lively culture, offering insight into the local flavor that makes Pingliang unique.

Food and Drink Scene of Pingliang

Must-Try Dishes

Pingliang Apples

Pingliang Apples are one of the city’s most famous products, renowned for their juicy sweetness and crisp texture. Visitors can buy fresh Pingliang Apples from the local markets and directly from the orchards that surround the city.

Pingliang Mutton Soup

Pingliang Mutton Soup is a traditional local dish that has been enjoyed in the region for over a century. The soup is flavored with fragrant spices, including ginger, cinnamon, and black cardamom, and is served with freshly baked bread.

Local Beverages

Kongtong Tea

Kongtong Tea is a traditional tea that has been enjoyed in the region for over a thousand years. The tea is known for its unique smoky flavor and deep aroma, which is derived from the region’s distinctive terroir.

Pingliang Wine

Pingliang Wine is a famous local wine that is enjoyed across China. The wine is made from locally sourced grapes and has a rich, fruity flavor that pairs well with local dishes.

Where to Stay in Pingliang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pingliang offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Hotels, hostels, and guesthouses are available throughout the city.

Five-Star Hotels

InterContinental Resort Jiuzhai Paradise

The InterContinental Resort Jiuzhai Paradise is a luxurious five-star hotel situated on the outskirts of the city. The hotel boasts spacious guest rooms, an outdoor swimming pool, several dining options, and a spa.

Budget Accommodation

Home Inn

Home Inn is a budget-friendly chain of hotels in China that offers clean and comfortable rooms at a reasonable price. There are several Home Inn locations throughout Pingliang, offering convenient access to the city’s attractions and amenities.

What to Buy in Pingliang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pingliang Apples

Pingliang Apples are the perfect souvenir to bring back from your trip to Pingliang. The city’s apples are renowned for their quality and can be easily found in local markets and directly from the orchards.

Kongtong Tea

Kongtong Tea is a traditional local tea that makes a great gift for tea enthusiasts. You can find high-quality Kongtong Tea in local markets and specialty shops throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Pingliang

Pingliang is home to a rich and diverse cultural heritage. The city’s inhabitants and visitors alike should respect local customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and temples and remove their shoes before entering.

Best Time to Visit Pingliang

Pingliang’s mild weather and abundant sunshine make it an ideal destination year-round. Spring and autumn are the best times to visit as the weather is comfortable and mild.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pingliang

Pingliang is generally a safe city to visit, and visitors can explore the town without major concerns.

Health in Pingliang

Visitors should drink bottled water and be aware of food hygiene standards to avoid any potential health risks.

Getting to Pingliang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pingliang

Lanzhou Zhongchuan International Airport is the closest airport to Pingliang, located approximately 190 kilometers away. The airport offers daily flights to several major Chinese cities.

Bus Stations Near Pingliang

Pingliang Bus Station is the main bus station in the city and offers regular bus services to several Chinese cities, including Lanzhou and Xi’an.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are available for tourists who want to explore the city on their own terms.

How to Get Around in Pingliang

Pingliang is a small city and can be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer journeys or to destinations outside of the city center.

Where to Go Next from Pingliang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lanzhou

Lanzhou is the capital of Gansu province and a vibrant city with a fascinating history. Visitors can explore the city’s unique culture, fantastic cuisine, and get a taste of modern Chinese life.

Maijishan Grottoes

Maijishan Grottoes is a well-known Buddhist grotto site located in Tianshui, Gansu province. The site boasts numerous Buddhist statues and rock-cut temples that are over 1600 years old.

Conclusion

Pingliang combines natural beauty and rich history into a unique destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you explore the city on foot or by bike, indulge in local cuisine or immerse yourself in the ancient culture and history, you’re sure to leave Pingliang feeling a sense of awe and wonder.
