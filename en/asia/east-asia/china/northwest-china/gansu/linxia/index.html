Introduction to Linxia

Linxia is a small city located in Northwest China, bordering the Gansu and Qinghai Province. Also known as Hezhou, Linxia is a well-known destination for travelers seeking a unique experience of Chinese culture and tradition. This region boasts natural beauty, stunning scenery, and cultural heritage, making it the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic travel experience in China.

List of Places to Visit in Linxia

Here are the top places to visit in Linxia.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • China Hui Culture Park: This park offers visitors an insight into the Hui culture that exists within Linxia, displaying various forms of Muslim architecture and customs, including the famous Hui food market.
  • Linxia Museum: The museum showcases the history and culture of Linxia, along with a vast collection of ancient treasures and Chinese art.

Natural Attractions of Linxia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gahai Lake: The Gahai lake is a high-altitude lake known for its crystal clear waters and stunning views that reflect the surrounding mountains. It serves as the perfect spot to take a stroll, relax, and enjoy the scenery and fresh air.
  • Bingling Temple Grottoes: Located in the Bingling Temple caves, these grottoes are a UNESCO World Heritage site and provide visitors with a unique look into ancient Chinese architecture and art.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Linxia

  • Mazong Mountain: Mazong Mountain offers a challenging hiking trail, with stunning views and awe-inspiring natural scenery along the way. It is fitting for experienced adventurers who want to witness the best views from the region’s highest mountain.
  • Dabanshan Forest Park: Located at the southern end of Linxia, Dabanshan Forest Park is a popular destination for bike enthusiasts due to its scenic beauty and peaceful surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Linxia: Activities and Tours

  • Arts & Craft Tour: Take a tour of Linxia’s workshops and learn skills like wood-carving, paper-cutting, and other unique Chinese art forms from local craftsmen.
  • Hui Food Tours: Enjoy the Hui foods in Linxia with a local tour guide who takes you through food markets and small restaurants with authentic flavors.

Food and Drink Scene of Linxia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Niángpí: A fresh, wet noodle made with fermented wheat, served cold with thin slices of cucumber, bean sprouts, crushed garlic, vinegar, soy sauce, and chili oil.
  • Kāo yáng táng hún (烤羊汤魂): A stew made with lamb, potatoes, and vegetables and served with a side of homemade bread.

Local Beverages

  • Linxia Yak Yogurt: A traditional dairy product that is enjoyed by locals due to its silky, creamy texture and sweet, tangy taste.
  • Linxia Baijiu: A local Chinese liquor with a distinct taste, brewed from grains and offering a strong, potent flavor.

Where to Stay in Linxia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hualian Hotel: Conveniently located near the city center, the hotel offers guests a comfortable stay with modern amenities and a wide range of food options.
  • Xin Yuan Riverside Hotel: Situated by the river, the hotel provides a peaceful ambiance and excellent views, with spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Linxia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gannan Tibetan Carpet: This handmade rug is a perfect souvenir to bring home, made from sheep wool and featuring unique designs and vibrant colors.
  • Hui Clothing and Accessories: Visitors can purchase traditional Hui clothing like robes and hats, along with ornate jewelry to take home as a memento of their trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Linxia

Linxia has a vibrant and diverse culture due to its location as a cultural and trade hub in northwest China. It is the residence of Hui Muslim cultures, which are marked by Arabic-style architecture, Islamic faith, and customs, and unique cuisine. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions while exploring this beautiful region.

Best Time to Visit Linxia

Linxia can be visited any time of the year, but the best time is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the weather is mild and the temperature is pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy, so visitors need to pack accordingly.

  • Buy Local souvenirs

  • Avoid smoking in public areas
  • Don’t use a camera in the mosque area
  • Don’t Discuss politics freely

Safety and Health

Safety in Linxia

Linxia is a safe travel destination, but visitors are advised to take precautions such as avoiding crime-prone areas at night and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Linxia

The official language is Mandarin Chinese, but locals speak Mandarin with a distinct accent. Visitors are advised to carry a medical kit with them that is adequately stocked.

Getting to Linxia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Linxia

Linxia does not have its airport. The nearest international airports are Gansu Airport and Xi ‘an Airport.

Bus Stations Near Linxia

The Linxia bus station is the main bus transport station that operates public buses and connects with other cities in Gansu province and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can rent cars to get around Linxia’s nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Linxia

  • By Bus: The most affordable option for traveling within Linxia, with multiple bus routes available.
  • By Taxi: Visitors can travel by taxi, but it is relatively costly.
  • By Bicycle: Visitors can rent bicycles to explore areas that are inaccessible by bus or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Linxia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dunhuang: A famous Silk road ancient city that is home to the famous Mogao caves, beautiful sand dunes and the beautiful Crescent Lake.
  • Xi’an: A historical city filled with UNESCO world heritage sites and beautiful architecture that includes the Terra Cotta Warriors and the beautiful Hua Shan mountain.

Conclusion

Linxia is a hidden gem of China with beautiful natural scenery, cultural heritage, and a unique blend of tradition and modernity. This guide provides visitors with comprehensive information about everything required to plan and enjoy an authentic Linxia experience. No matter the reason for your visit, Linxia is an incredible place that should be on your China travel list.
