Introduction to Guazhou

Located in Gansu province of Northwest China, Guazhou is a small county-level city that has largely remained off the tourist radar. But with its cultural landmarks and natural beauty, Guazhou is fast becoming popular among domestic tourists. The city has a history dating back to the Western Han dynasty and has a unique mix of Han and Hui Muslim cultures.

List of Places to Visit in Guazhou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lingyan Temple - A Tang dynasty Buddhist temple with a large collection of religious relics.
  • Guazhou Ancient City - An abandoned city from the Han dynasty.
  • Linxia Hui Autonomous Prefecture Museum - A museum dedicated to the history and culture of the Hui Muslim people.

Natural Attractions of Guazhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zhangye Danxia Geological Park - Famous for its colorful sandstone formations that resemble a rainbow.
  • Matisi Grottoes - A collection of Buddhist grottoes and temples carved into the cliffside.
  • Yugan Valley - A scenic valley with sand dunes and a river that winds through.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guazhou

  • The Shiyang River Trail - A 50-kilometer trail that follows the Shiyang River and offers scenic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • The Xixia Ridge Trail - A challenging 18-kilometer trail with steep ascents and descents that provides panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Matisi Grotto Trail - An easy and scenic 4-kilometer trail that leads to the famous Matisi Grottoes.

Best Things to Do in Guazhou: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Danxia Landform - Take a jeep tour of the Zhangye Danxia Geological Park and marvel at the unique landscape.
  • Visit Buddhist Grottoes - Take a tour of the Matisi Grottoes and spend some time exploring the ancient temples
  • Attend the Linxia Carnival - A lively festival held in August that showcases Hui Muslim culture, art, music, and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Guazhou

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lanzhou Beef Noodles - A classic Chinese dish with a unique Guanzhou twist.
  • Liangpi - A cold dish made from wheat noodles, sliced vegetables, and chili oil.
  • Yugan Fish - A local fish dish served with a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Medicinal Liquor - A potent drink prepared with Chinese herbs and liquor.
  • Xunhua Wine - A sweet rice wine made with locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Guazhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Guazhou Hengxing Business Hotel - A basic hotel with clean rooms and central location.
  • Zhangye Chahua Business Hotel - A budget hotel with comfortable rooms and a good location.
  • Japanses Capsule Hotel - A unique experience with rooms inspired by Japanese capsules.

What to Buy in Guazhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Xiahe Beads - Traditional Tibetan prayer beads made from yak bone and colorful stones.
  • Hui Embroidery - A traditional embroidery style unique to the Hui Muslim people.
  • Dried Fruits - A popular snack made from locally grown fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Guazhou

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious and cultural sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions and ask permission before taking photographs.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Mandarin Chinese to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Guazhou

The best time to visit Guazhou is during the spring and autumn months (April to June and September to November) when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months (July to August) can be hot and humid, and the winter months (December to February) can be very cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guazhou

Do:

  • Try local specialty dishes like Liangpi and Lanzhou Beef Noodles.
  • Hike or bike through the picturesque trails around Guazhou.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in overly affectionate behavior in public.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guazhou

Guazhou is generally a safe city, but it’s always advisable to take basic safety precautions, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Guazhou

It’s essential to travel with updated vaccinations and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Guazhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Guazhou

  • Zhangye Ganzhou Airport - Located 80 kilometers from Guazhou, with daily flights from major airports within China.

Bus Stations Near Guazhou

  • Guazhou Bus Station - Serves all the major cities in Gansu province, including Dunhuang and Zhangye.

Other Options

  • Private Car - Another option is to hire a private car to take you to and from Guazhou and other cities within Gansu province.

How to Get Around in Guazhou

The best way to get around in Guazhou is by foot, bike, or taxi. Biking and hiking are popular activities, and bike and scooter rentals are widely available. Taxis are an affordable option for short distances, and most drivers don’t speak English, so it’s helpful to have your destination written down in Mandarin.

Where to Go Next from Guazhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhangye - A city famous for its Danxia Landform and rich history.
  • Dunhuang - A city that has preserved its ancient Silk Road history and is home to the famous Mogao Caves.
  • Lanzhou - A provincial capital that’s home to several historical landmarks and museums.

Conclusion

Guazhou may be a small city, but it packs a punch when it comes to natural beauty, history, and culture. From the Danxia Landform to the Matisi Grottoes, Guazhou has something for everyone. By following our travel guide, you’re guaranteed to have an unforgettable experience in this charming city.
