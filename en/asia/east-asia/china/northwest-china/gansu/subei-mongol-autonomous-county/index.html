Introduction to Subei Mongol Autonomous County

Subei Mongol Autonomous County is located in Northwest China’s Gansu province, situated in a remote region of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. This county is situated at an altitude between 3000m and 4000m above sea level, offering a stunning combination of lush green mountains, beautiful rivers, and vast grasslands. The county is surrounded by beautiful scenery, and it is rich in traditional ethnic culture, including the nomadic lifestyle of the local Mongolian people.

List of Places to Visit in Subei Mongol Autonomous County

From historical landmarks, natural attractions, hiking and biking, to cultural experiences and outdoor activities, Subei Mongol Autonomous County has something for everyone. Here is the ultimate list of places to visit in Subei Mongol Autonomous County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chaka Salt Lake: As one of the most beautiful landscapes in the region, Chaka Salt Lake has become a popular venue and a must-visit spot in Subei Mongol Autonomous County. The unique landscape is formed by salt flats, dunes, and mountains which offer breathtaking views.
  • Ta’er Monastery: This ancient monastery is one of the most important Tibetan Buddhism temples in Northwest China. It is also known as Kumbum Monastery, as it is the birthplace of Tibetan Buddhism’s founder, Je Tsongkhapa.
  • Bingling Temple Grottoes: This is a Buddhist temple built more than 1,600 years ago. The highlight of this attraction is the well-preserved murals and sculptures on the cliffs, with the largest sculpture standing at 27 meters tall.

Natural Attractions of Subei Mongol Autonomous County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Menyuan Hulunbuir Prairie: This vast grassland is the largest natural pasture in northwestern China. Visitors can enjoy horse riding, yak riding, archery, and fishing activities.
  • Chaka Wind Power Base: The Chaka wind power base is the perfect spot for nature lovers, with its beautiful landscapes and wind turbines. Visitors can enjoy a breathtaking view of the mountains, plains, and wind turbines against the clear blue sky.
  • Yabrai Glacier: This huge icy mass is a must-visit in Subei Mongol Autonomous County. It is one of the largest glaciers in the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau and a popular spot for hiking.

Subei Mongol Autonomous County is renowned for its scenic hiking and biking routes. There are numerous routes that cater to all levels of hikers and bikers, from beginner to expert. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to explore:

Food and Drink Scene of Subei Mongol Autonomous County

Local cuisine in Subei Mongol Autonomous County is diverse and delicious, and it features a range of dishes that you won’t find anywhere else. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shouzhuafan: A dish made with rice, meat, vegetables, and spices. This is a popular dish among locals and tourists alike.
  • Guokui: This bread is baked and filled with lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Stewed lamb: The locals’ favorite dish is the stewed lamb, which is tender and succulent.

Local Beverages

  • Fermented milk tea: The classic Mongolian traditional beverage is made by adding milk, tea, and salt to fermented cattle or sheep milk.
  • Tibetan Butter Tea: Tea made with yak butter and salt, and it is enjoyed by locals every day.
  • Qinghai barley wine: This wine is a type of traditional alcoholic beverage made from barley, and it has a unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Subei Mongol Autonomous County

Subei Mongol Autonomous County has a rich cultural heritage, which is evident in the lifestyle and customs of the local people. Locals wear traditional clothing, and they place a high value on rural living, as well as celebrating traditional festivals such as the Nadam Festival and the New Year.

Best Time to Visit Subei Mongol Autonomous County

The best time to visit Subei Mongol Autonomous County is from April to October when the temperature is mild to cool. It’s an excellent opportunity to explore the vast open grasslands and stunning landscapes on mild days with clear blue skies.

Safety and Health

Safety in Subei Mongol Autonomous County

Tourists visiting Subei Mongol Autonomous County should always use their common sense and take steps to safeguard their personal property. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas. It is also essential to stay hydrated and protect your skin from prolonged exposure to the sun.

Health in Subei Mongol Autonomous County

Tourists are advised to carry insect repellent and a first-aid kit, sufficient supply of any medication required, and acquire comprehensive insurance cover. Additionally, visitors should be advised that the high altitude of Subei Mongol Autonomous County may have side effects such as headaches, fatigue, and dizziness.
