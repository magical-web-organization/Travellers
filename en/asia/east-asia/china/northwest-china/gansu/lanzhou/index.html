Introduction to Lanzhou

Lanzhou is the capital of Gansu Province, China. Known for its beef noodles, Lanzhou is a tourist destination with a mix of cultural and historical attractions, natural wonders, fantastic food, and high mountains. The city blends ancient imperial China with modern urban culture, making it a unique and fascinating destination.

List of Places to Visit in Lanzhou

Whether you’re a foodie, history buff, or nature lover, there are many places to visit in Lanzhou. We’ve compiled a list of the top sights to help you plan your Lanzhou itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lanzhou Museum: A comprehensive museum showcasing the history and culture of Lanzhou and Gansu Province.
  • Iron Bridge of Yellow River: An iconic bridge built over the Yellow River during the Qing Dynasty.
  • White Pagoda Mountain Park: A park with a white pagoda on top of the mountain, offering a panoramic view of the city.
  • Xiguan Mosque: A mosque founded in the 14th century and one of the largest in China.

Natural Attractions of Lanzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bingling Temple Grottoes: A series of Buddhist caves with intricate carvings and a beautiful waterfall.
  • Gansu Provincial Museum: A museum dedicated to the natural history of Gansu Province.
  • Zhongshan Bridge: A historic bridge over the Yellow River with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Maiji Mountain Scenic Area: A park with a famous grotto and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lanzhou

For those looking for an active vacation, Lanzhou offers some stunning hiking and biking routes. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Yellow River Scenic Area: A beautiful route that runs along the Yellow River with stunning views.
  • Lanzhou New District Cycling route: A scenic route that offers beautiful views of the city.
  • Baiyin Canyon: A beautiful and challenging hiking trail with breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Lanzhou: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxed or adventurous vacation, Lanzhou has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Lanzhou:

  • Taste Beef Noodles at the Gansu Beef Noodle Restaurant: A signature dish of Lanzhou and a must-try for foodies.
  • Take a boat trip on the Yellow River: Relax and enjoy Lanzhou’s scenery from a different perspective.
  • Watch a Chinese Opera: A unique way to immerse yourself in Chinese culture.
  • Hire a bike and explore the city by bike: Lanzhou has some fantastic bike routes that offer beautiful views of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lanzhou

If you’re planning to spend a week in Lanzhou, here’s a suggested itinerary to maximize your time:

Day 1: Visit White Pagoda Mountain Park and Xiguan Mosque

Food and Drink Scene of Lanzhou

Lanzhou is famous for its beef noodles, but there’s a lot more to Lanzhou’s food scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to consider:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lanzhou-style Beef Noodles: A signature dish of Lanzhou made with handmade noodles, beef broth, and tender beef slices.
  • Dingxi Mutton Soup: A hearty soup made with local mutton, potatoes, and rice noodles.
  • Chuanr: A popular street food made of grilled meat on skewers.

Local Beverages

  • Zhangye Keg: A local beer that’s light and refreshing.
  • LanMaJi: A tea house that serves tea from various regions in China.

What to Buy in Lanzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for something to bring home as a souvenir, Lanzhou has a lot to offer. You can buy:

  • Silk carpets and embroidery
  • Tibetan knives and swords
  • Traditional Chinese Medicine herbs and remedies
  • Dried fruit and nuts

Local Culture and Customs of Lanzhou

Lanzhou has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take pride in their traditions. Here are some tips to respect local customs and culture:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or mosque.
  • Don’t touch people’s heads or feet, as it’s considered disrespectful in Chinese culture.
  • Learn a few basic Chinese phrases to communicate effectively with locals.

Best Time to Visit Lanzhou

Lanzhou has a semi-arid climate, with hot summers and cold, dry winters. The best time to visit Lanzhou is between April and October, when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lanzhou

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling in Lanzhou:

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Respect local custsoms and culture.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t drink tap water, stick to bottled water.
  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lanzhou

Lanzhou is considered a safe city for tourists. However, as with any city, it’s important to take basic safety measures, such as keeping your valuables safe and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Lanzhou

Tap water in China should be avoided, including Lanzhou, as it’s not safe to drink. Other than that, Lanzhou is a relatively healthy city, with no major health concerns.

Getting to Lanzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are various ways to reach Lanzhou:

  • Air: Lanzhou Zhongchuan International Airport is the nearest airport to the city center.
  • Train: Lanzhou Railway Station connects Lanzhou to other major cities in China.
  • Bus: There are various buses that connect Lanzhou to other cities in Gansu Province.

How to Get Around in Lanzhou

Lanzhou has a well-connected public transport system, including buses and taxis. Taxis are inexpensive in Lanzhou, making them a convenient mode of transportation. If you want to explore the city at your own pace, rent a bike, and explore Lanzhou at your leisure.

Where to Go Next from Lanzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lanzhou is located in the heart of China, making it an excellent starting point to explore other parts of China. Some popular destinations near Lanzhou are:

  • Xiahe: A small town known for its Tibetan culture and scenic views.
  • Zhangye: Known for its Danxia landform and stunning natural views.
  • Jiayuguan: Home to the Great Wall’s western end and the magnificent Jiayuguan Fort.

Conclusion

Lanzhou is a city that has something for everyone. From its rich cultural heritage to stunning nature, delicious food, and proximity to other exciting destinations, Lanzhou has a lot to offer. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your Lanzhou trip and enjoy all that the city has to offer.
