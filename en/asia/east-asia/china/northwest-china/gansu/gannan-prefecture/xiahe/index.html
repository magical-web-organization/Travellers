Introduction to Xiahe

Xiahe, located in Gannan (prefecture), in Gansu, in Northwest China, is a stunning destination that is a must-see for adventure-seekers and culture enthusiasts. Known for its mix of natural beauty and cultural heritage, Xiahe is one of the top tourist destinations in the country. From hiking in the nearby mountains to exploring the historic monasteries and museums, there is something for everyone in Xiahe.

List of Places to Visit in Xiahe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Xiahe is home to several culturally significant attractions that provide an insight into the region’s rich heritage. The Labrang Monastery, one of the six great monasteries of the Gelug School of Tibetan Buddhism, is a must-visit. Other historical sites include the Gongtang Pagoda, a nine-story structure that dates back to the Ming Dynasty, and the Xialu Temple, an ancient temple that has been impeccably preserved.

Natural Attractions of Xiahe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Xiahe is surrounded by natural beauty, including the Gannan Grasslands, the Sangke Grasslands, and the Gahai Lake. The picturesque views are a sight to behold and offer visitors an opportunity to escape city life and explore the great outdoors. Outdoor enthusiasts can also hike in the mountains, with the most popular routes being the Yellow River Stone Forest Scenic Area and the Daxia River Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xiahe

The hiking and biking routes in Xiahe offer an opportunity to see different aspects of the region’s natural beauty. A popular route is the hike from Labrang Monastery to Zhagana, which takes you through forest landscapes, along rivers, and past stunning waterfalls. For bikers, the Gannan Circuit is a great option for exploring the region’s unique scenery.

Best Things to Do in Xiahe: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many more activities and tours available in Xiahe. Visitors can take a horseback ride through the stunning grasslands, watch traditional Tibetan dance performances, or experience a therapeutic bath in the famous Xiahe hot springs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xiahe

Day 1: Visit Labrang Monastery and explore Xiahe’s old town. Day 2: Hike in the Sangke Grasslands or visit Gahai Lake. Day 3: Take a horseback ride through the Gannan Grasslands. Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Daxia River Valley. Day 5: Experience the Xiahe hot springs or attend a traditional Tibetan dance show. Day 6: Bike the Gannan Circuit. Day 7: Visit the Gongtang Pagoda and the Xialu Temple before departing Xiahe.

Food and Drink Scene of Xiahe

Must-Try Dishes

Xiahe is known for its unique cuisine that blends Tibetan and Chinese flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include the Tibetan noodles, roasted lamb, and Yak Meat. You also can’t leave Xiahe without trying the famous Yogurt, which has distinct sour and creamy taste.

Local Beverages

When in Xiahe, experience the sister towns of Hezuo and Langmusi famous for its little Dao beer brewery. Alongside its tasty microbeers, the surrounding region is famous for its herbal teas such as goji berry, chrysanthemum, and jasmine tea.

Where to Stay in Xiahe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Xiahe have a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The Chen Dze Hotel, located in the heart of Xiahe’s old town, is a favorite among travelers. For those on a budget, the Yak Butter Inn and the 3 Fishes Hostel are great options that offer clean and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Xiahe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Xiahe, be sure to pick up some unique souvenirs and local products. The area is famous for its Tibetan handicrafts, including jewelry, rugs, and prayer flags. The Yak wool clothing is a must-have as well, which will keep you warm during the winter months.

Local Culture and Customs of Xiahe

Xiahe is a Tibetan region, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Dress modestly and ask for permission before taking photos in local monasteries. Visitors should also greet locals with a respectful nod and keep their shoes outside when entering specific sites.

Best Time to Visit Xiahe

The best time to visit Xiahe is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and the attractions are lush with greenery. However, if you prefer cooler weather and fewer crowds, consider visiting during the fall months (September to November), when the foliage is a stunning array of colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xiahe

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting monasteries and other religious sites.
  • Bring a backup camera battery as the weather is generally cold and can drain your battery quickly.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their consent.
  • Disrespect or touch any religious artifacts.

Safety and Health

Xiahe is generally a safe destination, and visitors should take standard precautions when traveling in China. Travelers should be aware of the high altitude and drink plenty of water to avoid altitude sickness. It is advisable to carry a basic first aid kit and any necessary medications.

Getting to Xiahe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xiahe

The nearest airport to Xiahe is Lanzhou Zhongchuan International Airport, which is located approximately 280 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or hire a private car to Xiahe.

Bus Stations Near Xiahe

Xiahe has a bus station located in the center of town, with regular buses to and from nearby cities such as Lanzhou and Xining.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Lanzhou or Xining and then take a bus to Xiahe. There are also private car services available that can take you directly to Xiahe.

How to Get Around in Xiahe

The best way to explore Xiahe is on foot, as most attractions are located within walking distance of each other. For those who prefer to drive, there are car rental services available in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Xiahe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Xiahe, visitors can explore nearby destinations such as Langmusi, a charming town that straddles the border of Gansu and Sichuan provinces. Also, nearby Hezuo and Zhangye sit along the Silk Road and are filled with history and attractions to explore.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure amongst natural surroundings or a cultural immersion in the Tibetan heritage, Xiahe has everything you need to make a memorable trip. From its history to stunning natural scenery, the combination of excitement and relaxation in Xiahe makes for an unforgettable experience. Be respectful, take precautions and explore!
