Introduction to Maqu

Maqu (马曲) is a charming city located in the Gannan Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture, Gansu Province, in Northwestern China. It is situated at an altitude of over 3,700 meters above sea level, making it one of the highest cities in China. Maqu is a great stopover for tourists headed to other parts of the region, with its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Maqu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Maqu County Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and traditions of Maqu and the surrounding Tibetan areas. You can see various historical artifacts, traditional costumes, religious artifacts, and much more.

  2. Yünnü Temple and Pagoda: Built in the Tang Dynasty, this temple is one of Maqu’s hidden gems. The temple complex boasts a giant statue of the Buddha, ancient murals, intricate sculptures, and other architectural marvels.

  3. Maqu Mosque: This mosque is one of the oldest and largest in Maqu. It is a beautiful blend of Chinese and Islamic architecture and offers a unique cultural experience.

Natural Attractions of Maqu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jiuqu River: The Jiuqu River offers a stunning view of the Maqu grasslands and is a great spot for picnicking, fishing, and relaxing. The surrounding area is also rich in wildlife and offers an excellent opportunity for bird watching.

  2. Ta’er Mountain: This magnificent mountain is a must-visit spot in Maqu. You can enjoy hiking, camping, or just soaking in the scenic views of the valley below.

  3. Gongtang Monastery: This ancient monastery is set on a hillside and offers awe-inspiring views of the surrounding peaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maqu

  1. Ta’er Mountain Trail: This hiking trail is the best way to explore the picturesque Ta’er Mountain. You can enjoy the breathtaking views of the mountains, river, and valleys.

  2. Maqu Grassland Trail: The Maqu Grassland Trail offers some of the most stunning views of the city. You can bike or hike along the vast grassland and take in the natural beauty.

  3. Yünnü Temple Trail: The Yünnü Temple Trail is perfect for those looking for a more challenging hike. The trail is steep and narrow, but the views of the surrounding mountains and valleys are worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Maqu: Activities and Tours

  1. Maqu Cultural Tour: This tour takes you through the city’s rich cultural heritage, including visits to museums, temples, and other historical sites.

  2. Maqu Grassland Tour: Take in the vast expanse of Maqu’s grasslands and enjoy camping, horse riding, and other outdoor activities.

  3. Maqu River Tour: Explore the Jiuqu River with a boat tour and admire the stunning scenery and abundant wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Maqu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tibetan Butter Tea: This traditional drink is made with yak butter, tea leaves, and salt. It is a popular drink among the locals and offers a unique flavor.

  2. Roasted Lamb: Roasted lamb is a staple in the Maqu region, and you can find it at most local restaurants. The lamb is roasted on a spit over an open flame and is succulent and tender.

  3. Zhá cài Ròu: Zhá cài ròu is a stir-fried dish made with slices of cured beef and spicy pickled vegetables. It is a popular appetizer in Maqu and offers a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  1. Chang: Chang is a traditional Tibetan beer made from fermented barley. It is a mild and refreshing drink that goes well with spicy foods.

  2. Yogurt: Yogurt is a staple among the Tibetan people, and you can find it at most local shops. The yogurt is thick, creamy, and has a tangy flavor.

Where to Stay in Maqu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Gongtang Monastery Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It is located near the Gongtang Monastery and is an ideal location for those looking for a peaceful and quiet stay.

  2. Maqu Grassland Hostel: This hostel is located on the Maqu Grassland and offers a unique experience of living among the Tibetan people. The rooms are comfortable, and the hostel offers camping, horse riding, and other outdoor activities.

  3. Maqu County Hotel: The Maqu County Hotel is a modern hotel located in the city center. The rooms are comfortable and offer all modern amenities.

What to Buy in Maqu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tibetan Jewelry: The region is famous for its unique and intricate jewelry made with silver, turquoise, and coral. You can find beautiful pieces at local souvenir shops.

  2. Tibetan Carpets: Tibetan carpets are known for their beautiful designs and quality. You can find a range of carpets, from traditional designs to modern styles, at local shops.

  3. Tibetan Thangka Paintings: Thangka paintings are traditional Tibetan paintings depicting Buddhist deities, scenes, and motifs. They are available in various sizes and styles and make for a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Maqu

The Tibetan culture is deeply rooted in Maqu and the surrounding areas. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and you should respect their religious customs and beliefs. It is customary to remove shoes when entering a temple, and you should not touch any religious artifacts or statues.

Best Time to Visit Maqu

The best time to visit Maqu is from May to October when the weather is mild, and the grasslands are green. The winters can be harsh, with temperatures dropping below freezing and snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maqu

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Carry a warm jacket, even in summers, due to the high altitude.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Touch any religious artifacts or statues.
  2. Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  3. Engage in activities that could harm wildlife or the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maqu

Maqu is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it is advisable to take necessary precautions while traveling, such as keeping your valuables safe and not wandering alone in isolated areas.

Health in Maqu

Maqu’s high altitude can cause altitude sickness, especially for those who are not accustomed to it. It is advisable to take proper precautions and consult a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Maqu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maqu

  1. Gannan Xiahe Airport: The Gannan Xiahe Airport is the closest airport to Maqu, located about 200 km away. It operates flights to major Chinese cities like Xi’an and Chengdu.

  2. Lanzhou Zhongchuan Airport: The Lanzhou Zhongchuan Airport is located about 500 km away from Maqu and operates flights to major Chinese cities like Beijing and Shanghai.

Bus Stations Near Maqu

  1. Maqu Bus Station: The Maqu Bus Station operates buses to major cities in the region, such as Lanzhou, Xiahe, and Xining.

  2. Hezuo Bus Station: The Hezuo Bus Station is located about 60 km away from Maqu and operates buses to major Chinese cities.

Other Options

  1. Taxi and Private Cars: Taxis and private cars are a popular option for traveling in the region. However, it can be expensive, and it is advisable to negotiate the price before hiring.

  2. Trains: The nearest train station is in Lanzhou, with trains to various Chinese cities.

How to Get Around in Maqu

The best way to get around Maqu is by hiring a taxi or private car. However, you can also rent a bike or hire a horse to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Where to Go Next from Maqu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Xiahe: Xiahe is located about 150 km away from Maqu and is home to the famous Labrang Monastery, one of the largest and most important Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in the world.

  2. Hezuo: Hezuo is about 70 km away from Maqu and is a popular stopover for those headed to other parts of the region. You can explore traditional markets and enjoy Tibetan cuisine in Hezuo.

Conclusion

Maqu is a charming city situated in the heart of the Gannan region. It offers a unique blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an offbeat experience. Plan your trip to Maqu, and discover the beauty and charm of this unique city!
