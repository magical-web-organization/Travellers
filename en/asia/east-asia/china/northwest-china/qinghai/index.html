Introduction to Qinghai

Nestled in the northwestern part of China lies Qinghai, a hidden gem that is often overlooked by tourists. Despite its lack of popularity, this province boasts a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty that’s worth exploring. Qinghai is known for its abundant wildlife, vast grasslands, and the blue Qinghai Lake, the largest saltwater lake in China.

Whether you’re a nature lover, a culture seeker, or an adventure junkie, there’s something for everyone in Qinghai. To help you plan your trip, we’ve put together a guide to the best places to visit, things to do, and food to eat in Qinghai.

List of Places to Visit in Qinghai

Qinghai is a vast province, and there are numerous places to explore. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tibetan Plateau Museum: This museum traces the history and culture of the Tibetan Plateau and is a great place to learn about the area’s traditions.
  • Kumbum Monastery: Located in Xining, this monastery is one of the most important Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in the country and attracts visitors from all over the world.
  • Dongguan Mosque: Xining is also home to this well-known mosque that dates back to the Ming dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Qinghai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qinghai Lake: The shimmering blue waters of Qinghai Lake are a sight to behold. Visitors can spend an entire day exploring the area and taking in the stunning views.
  • Kanbula National Forest Park: This park is a great place to experience the natural beauty of Qinghai. Visitors can hike, bike, or take a cable car to the top of the mountain to get a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.
  • Chaka Salt Lake: This colorful salt lake is a photographer’s paradise. The stunning hues of blue and green contrast beautifully against the stark desert landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qinghai

Qinghai is a paradise for nature lovers, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. These are some of the best routes to take:

  • Qilian Mountain Trek: This trek is perfect for those seeking a challenging hiking experience. The trail takes you through wildflower meadows, glacial lakes, and snow-capped peaks.
  • Biking around Qinghai Lake: For those who’d like a more leisurely experience, cycling around Qinghai Lake is a popular activity. The entire route is around 200 km and offers stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.
  • Hiking in the Tibetan Plateau: The vast grasslands of the Tibetan Plateau offer inspiring views and a chance to immerse oneself in nature. The options are endless, from gentle day hikes to multi-day treks.

Best Things to Do in Qinghai: Activities and Tours

Qinghai offers a range of unique activities and tours that are worth experiencing:

  • Tibetan Culture and Nomadic Life Tour: This four-day tour takes you through the Tibetan Plateau, showing you the local culture and way of life of the nomadic peoples who call this region home.
  • Watch the Bird-View of Grassland in AERO: Located near Qinghai Lake, AERO is a unique way to experience the beauty of Qinghai from above. Take a ride in a hot air balloon or a paraglider to see the stunning scenery from a different perspective.
  • Relax in a Tibetan Hot Spring: After a day of hiking or biking, relax in one of the many hot springs located throughout Qinghai. Many of these hot springs offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Qinghai

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Qinghai, this itinerary will give you a taste of what the province has to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Xining and visit Dongguan Mosque and Kumbum Monastery
Day 2 Head to Kanbula National Forest Park for hiking and scenic views
Day 3 Take a full day to explore Qinghai Lake and its surrounding area
Day 4 Visit the Tibetan Plateau Museum and learn about the local culture
Day 5 Take a guided cycling tour around Qinghai Lake
Day 6 Hike in the Tibetan Plateau and explore its stunning grasslands
Day 7 Relax and unwind at a local hot spring.

Food and Drink Scene of Qinghai

Trying local food is an essential part of any travel experience, and Qinghai is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tibetan Yogurt: This creamy yogurt is a staple in Tibetan cuisine and has a slightly sour taste that is both refreshing and satisfying.
  • Milk Tea: This is a staple drink in Qinghai and Tibet, made with yak butter, salt, and tea leaves. It’s creamy, salty, and delicious.
  • Mutton Skewers: Mutton is a popular meat in Qinghai, and these skewers are a great way to try it. They’re typically grilled and seasoned with cumin and chili powder.

Local Beverages

  • Qingke Wine: Made from highland barley, this alcoholic drink is a must-try in Qinghai. It has a slightly sweet taste and is often served warm in small bowls.
  • Butter Tea: Another popular tea, this drink is made with milk, butter, and tea leaves. It’s rich and creamy and perfect on a cold day.

Where to Stay in Qinghai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qinghai has a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. These are some of the best:

  • Hostel International Xining: This hostel is located in Xining and is a great budget option. It’s clean, comfortable, and conveniently located near many of the city’s attractions.
  • Qinghai Lake Earth-house: This unique guesthouse is located on the shores of Qinghai Lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It’s a great option for those who want to be close to nature.
  • Jinyu Hotel: For those who want a more luxurious experience, Jinyu Hotel is a great option. It’s located in Xining and offers stylish rooms, excellent service, and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Qinghai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Qinghai has a range of unique products that make great souvenirs. These are some of the best:

  • Thangka: These traditional Tibetan paintings are intricate and beautiful and make a great addition to any home.
  • Yak Wool Products: Yak wool is a popular material in Qinghai and is used to make everything from blankets to clothing.
  • Highland Barley Wine: Bring home a bottle of Qingke wine or beer as a unique souvenir and to enjoy a taste of Qinghai at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Qinghai

Before visiting Qinghai, it’s essential to understand and respect the local culture and customs. These are some key points to keep in mind:

  • Religion: Religion and spirituality are an essential part of daily life in Qinghai, and it’s important to be respectful of this. When visiting religious sites, dress modestly and follow local customs.
  • Taboos: Eating dog meat and horse meat is taboo in Qinghai, and it’s important to avoid doing so to respect the local customs.

Best Time to Visit Qinghai

The best time to visit Qinghai is from May to August. The weather is warm, and the grasslands are green and lush. September to November is also a beautiful time to visit, with changing leaves creating a colorful landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qinghai

When visiting Qinghai, there are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Eat dog meat or horse meat.
  • Disrespect local culture and customs.
  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Qinghai is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s essential to take precautions when exploring the area.

Safety in Qinghai

  • Be aware of altitude sickness when visiting high-elevation areas.
  • Always carry identification and emergency contact information with you.
  • Check the weather before heading out and dress accordingly.

Health in Qinghai

  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid getting sick.
  • Bring altitude sickness medication if necessary.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing to prevent sunburn.

Getting to Qinghai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Qinghai is easy to reach via several transportation options:

Airports Near Qinghai

  • Xining Caojiabao International Airport: This airport is located in Xining, making it a convenient entry point for those exploring the city and surrounding areas.

Bus Stations Near Qinghai

  • Xining Bus Station: Located in the center of Xining, this bus station offers services to many destinations throughout Qinghai.
  • Geermu Bus Station: This bus station is located in Geermu and is a convenient entry point for those visiting the western part of Qinghai.

Other Options

  • Train: There are several train options to reach Qinghai, with Xining being the most popular station.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore Qinghai at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Qinghai

Once you’ve arrived in Qinghai, there are several transportation options to get around:

  • Bus: Buses are an affordable and convenient way to get around Qinghai. They’re available in most cities and towns and offer services to most major attractions.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Xining and other major cities throughout Qinghai.
  • Rent a Car: Renting a car is an excellent option if you’d like to explore Qinghai at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Qinghai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Qinghai is well-connected to many popular destinations. Here are some places to consider visiting after your trip to Qinghai:

  • Tibet: Bordering Qinghai to the south, Tibet is a popular destination known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.
  • Sichuan: To the east of Qinghai lies Sichuan, a province known for its spicy cuisine, stunning landscapes, and rich history.
  • Gansu: Head east from Qinghai to Gansu, a province known for its rich history and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Qinghai is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its unique blend of culture and nature, there’s something for everyone in this stunning province. From exploring the local history to marveling at the natural beauty, a trip to Qinghai is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that’s not to be missed.
