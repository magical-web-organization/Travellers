Xining, located in the Qinghai province of Northwest China, is a captivating destination that offers a blend of cultural, natural, and culinary delights. Whether you’re looking to explore historical sites, immerse yourself in the region’s natural beauty or indulge in local cuisine, Xining has something to offer every type of traveler.

Introduction to Xining

As the capital city of Qinghai, Xining serves both as a hub for transportation and as a gateway to the rest of the province. With a history dating back over 2000 years, the city is home to numerous temples, museums, and ancient sites that bear witness to its rich past.

One of the most striking features of Xining is its position at the northeastern edge of the Tibetan Plateau. The city’s unique geography makes it an ideal starting point for exploring the surrounding mountains, lakes, and grasslands that are home to a wide variety of flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Xining

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Xining is steeped in history and culture, and there are numerous attractions that highlight this heritage. Some of the notable sites to visit include:

  • Ta’er Monastery: Located 25 km southeast of Xining, Ta’er Monastery is one of the most important Tibetan Buddhist sites in the region. Founded in 1560, the monastery is home to over 600 monks and features stunning architecture and intricate artwork.

  • Dongguan Mosque: One of the largest and most important mosques in China, Dongguan Mosque is a fascinating example of Islamic architecture. With a history that dates back over 600 years, the mosque is a must-visit for those interested in the region’s Muslim culture.

  • Qinghai Provincial Museum: This modern museum showcases the history and culture of Qinghai, with exhibits covering everything from local wildlife to ancient artifacts and traditional crafts.

Natural Attractions of Xining: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its cultural attractions, Xining also boasts some of the most stunning natural scenery in China. Some of the best outdoor activities and scenic spots include:

  • Qinghai Lake: Located just 150 km from Xining, Qinghai Lake is the largest saltwater lake in China and a popular destination for boating, cycling, and hiking.

  • Kumbum Monastery: Situated on a hilltop just outside Xining, Kumbum Monastery is famous for its stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill and explore the monastery and nearby temples.

  • Menyuan Honeysuckle Valley: This picturesque valley is known for its wild honeysuckle blossoms, which cover its hillsides in a sea of yellow and orange during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xining

Xining and its surrounding areas offer some of the best hiking and biking routes in China. Some of the most popular trails and routes include:

  • Qianguqing Nature Reserve: This vast protected area is home to some of the most spectacular landscapes in the region, including mountains, rivers, and glaciers.

  • The Yellow River Scenic Area: Located just outside Xining, this scenic area includes both cultural and natural attractions, including the Lijiaxia Reservoir and the Danxia Landform.

  • Riyue Mountain: This mountain range is located just 80 km north of Xining and offers some of the most challenging and rewarding hiking routes in the area, with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Xining: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural heritage, its natural wonders, or its culinary scene, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Some of the most popular include:

  • Food Tours: Sample local delicacies and street food on a guided tour of Xining’s food scene.

  • Hiking and Biking Tours: Join a guided tour of the area’s best trails and routes, with experienced guides who can help you make the most of your time.

  • Cultural Tours: Explore the city’s most important historical and cultural sites with a knowledgeable guide, learning about the region’s fascinating history and traditions along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Xining

No trip to Xining is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Skewers: One of the most famous dishes in the region, lamb skewers are charcoal-grilled and seasoned with cumin, salt, and other spices.

  • Maocai: A hearty stew made with meat, vegetables, and bean curd, maocai is a popular street food in Xining.

  • Niu Rou Mian: This beef noodle soup is a staple of Qinghai cuisine, with a rich broth and tender slices of beef.

Local Beverages

  • Yak Milk: A staple of Tibetan culture, yak milk is a nutritious drink that is enjoyed both hot and cold.

  • Butter Tea: Another traditional Tibetan beverage, butter tea is made by churning butter and tea leaves together to create a rich, creamy drink.

  • Qingke Wine: This fermented barley wine is a popular drink in Qinghai, with a slightly sour flavor and a high alcohol content.

Where to Stay in Xining: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xining has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Sofitel Xining: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Xining, with stylish rooms and excellent dining options.

  • Xining Jinjiang International Hotel: A modern four-star hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location near the city’s main attractions.

  • Xining Real Love Hostel: A popular hostel located in the city center, with comfortable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Xining: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Xining is known for its handicrafts and local products, including:

  • Tibetan Carpets: These intricately woven carpets are handcrafted by local artisans and make a beautiful addition to any home.

  • Thangka Paintings: These intricate paintings on silk or paper are a traditional form of Tibetan art.

  • Tibetan Medicine: Xining is home to numerous traditional Tibetan medicine shops, where you can purchase herbal remedies and supplements.

Local Culture and Customs of Xining

Xining is a city with a diverse mix of ethnic groups and cultural traditions. It’s important to respect local customs and dress modestly when exploring the city’s cultural landmarks and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Xining

The best time to visit Xining is in the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is mild and dry. However, be prepared for cool temperatures in the evenings, as the city is at a high altitude.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xining

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry cash, as some smaller shops and restaurants may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect local customs or religious beliefs.
  • Be too loud or rowdy in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xining

Xining is a relatively safe city, with low levels of crime. However, it’s always important to be vigilant and keep your valuables secure, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Xining

Xining’s high altitude can be a challenge for some visitors, so it’s important to stay hydrated and take it easy when first arriving in the city. Air pollution can also be a problem in the winter months, so be prepared with a mask if necessary.

Getting to Xining: Transportation Options and Logistics

Xining is easily accessible by air, train, and bus. Some of the main transportation options include:

Airports Near Xining

  • Caojiabao International Airport: Located just 30 minutes from the city center, Xining’s airport offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Xining

  • Xining West Bus Station: Located in the city center, this bus station offers long-distance buses to destinations throughout Qinghai and beyond.

  • Xining South Bus Station: This station offers buses to destinations in southern Qinghai, including Lanzhou and Xining.

Other Options

  • Train: Xining is a major rail hub, with connections to cities throughout China.

How to Get Around in Xining

Xining’s public transportation system includes buses and taxis, as well as bike rentals for those who want to explore the city on two wheels. Walking is also a great way to explore the city, with many of the main attractions located within easy walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Xining: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous destinations to explore beyond Xining, including:

  • Qinghai Lake: Located just 150 km from Xining, Qinghai Lake is an essential stop for those looking to explore the region’s natural beauty.

  • Zhangye: This city in neighboring Gansu province is famous for its Danxia Landform, a geological wonder that features striking red sandstone formations.

  • Lanzhou: The capital of neighboring Gansu province, Lanzhou is an important transportation hub with a rich history and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Xining is a destination that offers something for everyone, from its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural scenery and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a solo traveler or visiting with family and friends, you’re sure to find something to love in this fascinating city in Northwest China.
