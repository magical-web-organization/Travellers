Introduction to Guide

Guide, situated in Qinghai province in Northwest China, is a breathtakingly beautiful destination that attracts visitors with its stunning natural scenery, rich culture, and history. The city sits at an altitude of 3,000 meters, making it an ideal spot for those looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination. This comprehensive travel guide will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, and food to try in Guide, China, to make your trip a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Guide

If you are in Guide for the first time and looking for some quintessential tourist spots, here is a list of must-see destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ping’an Kangho Tibetan Museum: The Ping’an Kangho Tibetan Museum is the perfect place to learn about Tibet’s unique culture and history, as well as the artistic traditions of the local region. The museum houses an extensive collection of Tibetan artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and artwork.

  • Guoluo Folk Museum: The Guoluo Folk Museum is a small and charming museum that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Guoluo Prefecture in Qinghai. The museum’s collection includes traditional clothing, jewelry, pottery, and artwork representing the local culture.

  • Yushu Horse Racing Festival: If you visit Guide in June, be sure to check out the Yushu Horse Racing Festival. This annual event is a popular celebration of Tibetan culture featuring horse racing, dancing, and music. It attracts tourists from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Guide: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qinghai Lake: Qinghai Lake is the biggest saltwater lake in China and is a nature lover’s paradise. The pristine blue waters surrounded by the snow-capped mountains make it a perfect place for a picnic, boating, or birdwatching. The best time to visit is between May to October when the weather is mild.

  • Kanbula National Forest Park: The Kanbula National Forest Park is famous for its magnificent natural scenery, including impressive canyons, glaciers, alpine meadows, and dense forests. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities here, such as hiking, camping, and trekking.

  • Ta’er Monastery: The Ta’er Monastery is a beautiful temple complex situated at the foot of the mountain, surrounded by lush greenery. The complex is famous for its outstanding architecture, including a 55-meter high Buddha statue, stunning murals, and intricate woodwork. Visitors can also hike around the temple complex and enjoy the beautiful natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guide

If you are a hiking or biking enthusiast, Guide has got you covered. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in Guide:

  • Guide to Maizhokunggar hike: This 150-kilometer trail from Guide to Maizhokunggar is one of the best hiking trails in Qinghai. Along the way, you will be treated to stunning views of snow-capped mountains, lush forests, and pristine glacial lakes.

  • 18 Bends bike trail: The 18 Bends bike trail is an adrenaline-pumping adventure that takes you along a winding mountain road that consists of 18 hairpin turns. Along the way, you will experience breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Guide: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for some exciting activities to do in Guide, here are some of the best things to do:

  • Horseback Riding: Experience what it feels like to ride a horse in the open meadows of the Qinghai Plateau. Horseback riding is a popular activity in Guide, and you can arrange for a guided tour at one of the local stables.

  • Photography Tour: Guide offers spectacular natural scenery that is perfect for photography enthusiasts. You can arrange for a photography tour and explore some of the best spots for taking stunning pictures in and around Guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Guide

Food and drink are an essential part of any trip to Guide, China. Here are some of the local dishes and beverages that you must try when in Guide:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tibetan Yak Meat: Tibetan Yak Meat is a popular dish in Guide and is prepared using traditional methods that bring out the flavor of the meat. The meat is usually cooked on a charcoal stove or in a hotpot and is served with a side of barley bread or noodles.

  • Guoluo Rice Cakes: Guoluo Rice Cakes are a local favorite and are made using glutinous rice and sugar. The cakes are then steamed and served with a variety of side dishes, including pickled vegetables and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: Butter Tea is a traditional Tibetan tea that is made using fermented yak butter, tea leaves, and salt. The tea is rich and creamy and is popular among the locals in Guide.

  • Chang: Chang is a locally brewed alcoholic beverage that is made using barley and is similar to beer. The beverage is popular among the Tibetan people and is served with a side of traditional snacks.

Where to Stay in Guide: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guide offers a range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Qinghai Lake Lotus Hotel: This luxurious hotel is situated near Qinghai Lake and offers stunning lake views, modern amenities, and the best local cuisines. The hotel also offers various activities and tours for its guests.

  • Xihai Hostel: Xihai Hostel is a budget option that is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers. The hostel features comfortable rooms, friendly staff, and is in close proximity to various tourist spots.

What to Buy in Guide: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Guide is known for its unique handcrafted souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Guide:

  • Tibetan Silver Jewelry: Tibetan Silver Jewelry is an excellent souvenir option for those interested in the local culture. The jewelry features intricate designs and is often handmade using traditional methods.

  • Thangkas: Thangkas are traditional Tibetan paintings that are done on silk or cotton. They typically feature religious motifs and are often used as home decor or as a meditation aid.

Local Culture and Customs of Guide

Guide is a melting pot of cultures and customs. The city is predominantly Tibetan, and their culture is prevalent in the local lifestyle. Visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when venturing into traditional monasteries and temples. Respect local customs and cultures, and remember to ask before taking photographs of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Guide

The best time to visit Guide is from May to October when the weather is mild and the natural scenery is at its best. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and boating, and attend various cultural festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guide

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly, especially for religious sites
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of the locals

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Guide

Guide is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take precautionary measures, such as carrying a copy of your passport and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Guide

Guide is at a high altitude, so it is essential to acclimatize gradually. Visitors are also advised to carry an adequate supply of medication for any pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Guide: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Guide is easy, as it is well-connected via airports, railways, and buses.

Airports Near Guide

The nearest airport to Guide is the Xining Caojiabao International Airport, located approximately 140 km away. From the airport, you can book a taxi or a bus to reach Guide.

Bus Stations Near Guide

Guide is well-connected via bus routes to various cities in Qinghai province. The Xining Bus Station is the main hub for bus services to and from Guide.

Other Options

Trains are also available from major cities in China, such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Chengdu. Guided tours are another popular option for those who prefer organized travel.

How to Get Around in Guide

Guide is a small city, and the best way to explore it is on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and buses are also available for those looking to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Guide: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are looking to explore nearby destinations, here are some of the best options:

  • Zhangye Danxia National Geological Park: The Zhangye Danxia National Geological Park is a unique natural wonder of multi-colored rock formations that are simply breathtaking.
