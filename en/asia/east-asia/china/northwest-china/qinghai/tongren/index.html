Introduction to Tongren

Located in Qinghai province, in the northwest of China, Tongren is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Boasting a rich cultural history, stunning natural scenery, and a vibrant local community, Tongren offers visitors an immersive travel experience that includes everything from hiking through breathtaking mountains to trying authentic local cuisine. The town is home to several ethnic groups, including Tibetan and Hui Muslims, adding to its unique charm.

List of Places to Visit in Tongren

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wutun Monastery: One of the largest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in the region, Wutun Monastery offers visitors a fascinating insight into Tibetan culture and religion. The monastery’s golden roof and colorful prayer flags make for some great holiday photos.
  • Rongwo Gonchen Monastery: Another impressive Tibetan Buddhist monastery, Rongwo Gonchen Monastery is surrounded by stunning mountain scenery and houses many priceless Buddhist art pieces.
  • Long Wu Temple: A recently restored Taoist temple, Long Wu Temple is a must-visit when in Tongren. The temple boasts a unique architectural style and colorful décor.

Natural Attractions of Tongren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Guomari Tibetan Village: A Tibetan village located near the top of the mountain, Guomari offers panoramic views of the surrounding scenery. The village is a great place to experience authentic Tibetan culture and customs.
  • Qinghai Lake: One of the largest lakes in China, Qinghai Lake is a popular destination for visitors due to its stunning natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and camping on the lake shore, and witness the unique wildlife.
  • Huangnan Mountain: Known as the “Huangshan of Qinghai,” Huangnan Mountain offers visitors a chance to hike through some of the most stunning mountain scenery in China. The landscape is dotted with waterfalls, hot springs, and ancient trees that are thousands of years old.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tongren

  • Guomari to Nianbo monastery: A two-day trek that takes you through some of the most stunning mountain scenery in the region. Venture to the summit of the mountain, which reaches almost 4000 meters above sea level.
  • Taer Monastery to Wutun Monastery: A popular trek among tourists, this route takes you through a series of Buddhist monasteries, traditional villages, and scenic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Tongren: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Tibetan family: Visit a traditional Tibetan family and learn about their customs and traditions. Try some yak butter tea and homemade yogurt, and enjoy a unique cultural experience.
  • Watch a traditional Tibetan dancing show: Experience the colorful culture of Tibet by attending a local dance show. You will be amazed by the traditional costumes and energetic performances.
  • Take a photography tour: Tongren is known for its stunning scenery, which makes it a perfect destination for photographers. Join a photography tour to discover some of the most photogenic spots in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Tongren

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yak Meat Hotpot: A popular local specialty, yak meat hotpot is a hearty and warming dish that is perfect for cold winter days.
  • Tibetan Noodle Soup: A filling and satisfying soup that is made with handmade noodles and a rich broth.
  • Zanba: A staple food of Tibetans, zanba is a mixture of roasted barley flour, butter or oil and tea, served either sweet or salty.

Local Beverages

  • Lhasa Beer: A local favorite, Lhasa Beer is a refreshing brew that is perfect for enjoying after a day of exploring the town.
  • Butter Tea: A traditional Tibetan beverage, butter tea is an acquired taste but worth trying for its unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Tongren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tongren Chengxiang Hotel: A comfortable and affordable hotel located in the town center, Tongren Chengxiang Hotel offers guests clean and spacious rooms and excellent service.
  • Tongren Sanjiangyuan Hotel: Located near the bus station, this hotel is a convenient base for exploring the surrounding area. It offers comfortable and modern rooms and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Tongren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tibetan incense: Handmade from natural ingredients such as flowers and herbs, Tibetan incense is known for its soothing and calming properties.
  • Handicrafts: From colorful thangka paintings to intricately carved wooden masks, Tongren is a great place to pick up some unique handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tongren

  • Prayer flags: You will see prayer flags fluttering in the wind everywhere you go in Tongren. The flags are inscribed with prayers and blessings and are intended to spread goodwill and happiness.
  • Respect the local customs: Tongren has a rich and unique culture that should be respected at all times. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and ask for permission before entering homes or monasteries.

Best Time to Visit Tongren

The best time to visit Tongren is March to October when the weather is mild and dry, and the natural scenery is at its most vibrant. Temperatures can drop below freezing in winter, so make sure to pack warm clothing if you are planning a visit during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tongren

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and attend cultural events
  • Take lots of photos of the stunning scenery

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture in any way
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Take photos where it is not permitted

Safety and Health

Safety in Tongren

Tongren is generally a safe place to visit. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings at all times. It is also recommended to check with local authorities before venturing into more remote areas.

Health in Tongren

Altitude sickness can be a concern in Tongren, particularly if you plan on doing any strenuous activities such as hiking. Visitors are advised to take things slowly at first, drink plenty of water, and consider taking altitude sickness medication if necessary.

Getting to Tongren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tongren

  • Xining Caojiabao International Airport (262 km from Tongren)
  • Lanzhou Zhongchuan International Airport (422 km from Tongren)

Bus Stations Near Tongren

  • Tongren Bus Station: Located in the town center, this bus station offers frequent connections to nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Take a train to Xining, and then take a bus to Tongren.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore the surrounding area at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Tongren

The best way to get around Tongren is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can rent bicycles from local shops and explore the town and surrounding areas at their leisure. Taxis are also available but can be expensive compared to other transportation options.

Where to Go Next from Tongren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Xiahe: Located in Gansu Province, Xiahe is a picturesque town that is home to the impressive Labrang Monastery.
  • Yushu: This vibrant town is home to several Tibetan Buddhist monasteries and is a great place to experience authentic Tibetan culture.
  • Guide: A small town located on the Tibetan Plateau, Guide is known for its stunning mountain scenery and traditional Tibetan architecture.

Conclusion

Tongren is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the unique culture and stunning natural scenery of China’s north-west. With so much to see and do, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem. Plan your trip today and get ready to discover everything that Tongren has to offer!
