Introduction to Golmud

Golmud is a charming city situated in the Qinghai province of Northwest China. It is a beautiful region known for its serene natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Golmud is set amongst the Kunlun and the Tanggula mountain ranges, making it a popular hub for outdoor adventurers.

Visitors to Golmud can explore the city’s beautiful temples, fascinating museums, and enchanting cultural landmarks, as well as indulge in local cuisine and unique shopping experiences. This guide will help you discover the best of Golmud.

List of Places to Visit in Golmud

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Golmud has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its incredible museums and historic landmarks. The main cultural attractions in Golmud include:

  1. Golmud Museum: This is an excellent place to learn about the culture and history of the region. The museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the local heritage and customs.

  2. Golmud Cultural Square: This is a public space where visitors can enjoy traditional performances, such as folk dancing and singing.

  3. Salt Lake Beach: This is a natural salt lake that provides stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It is a popular spot for picnics, walks and taking in the serene environment.

Natural Attractions of Golmud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Golmud’s natural beauty is legendary, and visitors can explore the scenic views and outdoor adventures in the region. Some of the prominent natural attractions in Golmud are:

  1. The Kunlun Mountains: They are an extension of the Himalayas and offer fantastic views and hiking trails.

  2. Qarhan Salt Lake: This is one of the world’s largest inland salt lakes with breathtaking scenery.

  3. Chumar River: This is a wild and untamed river offering white-water rafting and excellent fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Golmud

Golmud provides a range of hiking and biking routes for those seeking an adventure in the great outdoors. Some of the best hiking and biking trails to explore whilst in Golmud are:

  1. Kunlun Mountains Trek: This epic trek takes visitors to the heart of the Kunlun Mountains and showcases some of the region’s most scenic views.

  2. Dunhuang-Golmud Ancient Silk Road Trail: This historic route offers stunning views of ancient ruins from the legendary Silk Road.

  3. Cycling the Qinghai Lake Trail: This trail follows the shoreline of the famous Qinghai Lake and is one of the most beautiful biking routes in China.

Best Things to Do in Golmud: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy while in Golmud. Some of the best things to do in Golmud are:

  1. Hot Springs Relaxation: Golmud has several natural hot springs near its vicinity. Many visitors take this opportunity to relax and unwind after a long day of hiking.

  2. Visit to a Yak Breeding Farm: The yak is an integral part of the traditional Tibetan way of life. Visitors can learn more about the yak and watch trained herders working with the animals.

  3. Explore the Chumar River: For those looking for an aquatic adventure, the picturesque Chumar River provides an opportunity to fish, white-water raft or kayak.

Week-Long Itinerary for Golmud

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Golmud:

Day1: Arrival in Golmud, and visit the Golmud Museum

Day 2-4: Hiking in the Kunlun Mountains or the Dunhuang Golmud Trail.

Day 5: Relax at the hot springs and visit a Yak Breeding Farm

Day 6: Explore Qarhan salt lake and Chumar River.

Day 7: Departure from Golmud.

Food and Drink Scene of Golmud

The food scene in Golmud is both unique and diverse, with plenty of opportunities to taste the local cuisine. Here are some of the best dishes and beverages to try while in Golmud:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Niangpi: A popular dish made with hand-cut strips of rolled-out dough, topped with a blend of savory ingredients, including pickled vegetables and chili oil.

  2. Juecai Rou Si Mian: A heartwarming bowl of noodles with shredded meat and fresh vegetables, topped with a savory, aromatic sauce.

  3. Qingke Wine: A locally brewed wine that has a slightly sweet, light taste with a lingering aroma of barley and grain.

Local Beverages

  1. Butter Tea: A favorite drink and staple of the Tibetan people, made with tea leaves, yak butter, and salt.

  2. Chaga Mushroom Tea: A medicinal drink said to provide various health benefits, prepared using wild chaga mushrooms.

Where to Stay in Golmud: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Golmud can choose from several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Golmud:

  1. Golmud Yinhu Hotel: A luxurious hotel in the heart of the city with beautiful rooms, fitness facilities, and excellent on-site dining.

  2. Golmud Ze-Ka Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms, on-site restaurants, and close proximity to local shops and amenities.

  3. Golmud International Youth Hostel: A friendly and affordable youth hostel with comfortable dorm rooms and communal spaces that encourage socializing among guests.

What to Buy in Golmud: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Golmud is a haven for shoppers looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home. Some of the best items to buy in Golmud include:

  1. Tibetan Handicrafts: These traditional hand-woven Tibetan textiles, such as colorful rugs, shawls, and wraps, are lovely gift items that will last a lifetime.

  2. Yak Wool Clothing: Yak wool is a prized material in the region and can be found in various clothing items such as jackets, hats, and gloves.

  3. Thangkas: These elaborate, colorful paintings on cotton or silk frequently depict Buddhist deities, revered teachers, or Tibetan landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Golmud

Golmud’s population is predominantly Tibetan, and visitors can learn much about the traditions and customs of the Tibetan people. Visitors should respect local customs, dress modestly in temples, and remove shoes before entering religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Golmud

The best time to visit Golmud is in spring and autumn when the weather is mild and pleasant, and nature is at its picturesque best. It is better to avoid visiting Golmud between late June to August when the temperatures can soar above 35^oC.

Do’s and Don’ts of Golmud

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately for temples and religious buildings.

  2. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  3. Try local cuisine and shop for souvenirs.

Don’t:

  1. Take photographs of people without permission.

  2. Disrespect local beliefs and customs.

  3. Litter or harm the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Golmud

Golmud is a safe city to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautionary measures as they would in any other city.

Health in Golmud

Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations and seek medical advice before traveling. Altitude sickness could be a concern due to Golmud’s high altitude, rising above 2800m above sea level.

Getting to Golmud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Golmud is well-connected to other parts of China via various transportation options, including air, rail, and bus.

Airports Near Golmud

The nearest airport to Golmud is Golmud Airport, which offers several domestic flights to other regional airports.

Bus Stations Near Golmud

Golmud Bus Terminal is the city’s main Bus Station where visitors can take a bus to Lhasa, Xining, and Dulan.

Other Options

Golmud is also connected to other cities by train, and overnight sleeper trains from Xining and Lanzhou are available.

How to Get Around in Golmud

Visitors can utilize buses and taxis to get around Golmud easily. Additionally, bicycles and motorcycles are available for rent in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Golmud: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Golmud is an excellent base for exploring other parts of the Qinghai province and the incredible natural beauty of the region. Some nearby destinations and worthwhile excursions from Golmud include:

  1. The Qinghai-Tibet Railway: The world’s highest railway passes through this region, and a train ride through the picturesque landscapes should be a must-see.

  2. Qinghai Lake: This is one of the most scenic areas in China and is famous for its blue-green water and surrounding snow-capped mountains.

  3. The Oasis Towns of Dunhuang and Turpan: These historically important towns sit on the ancient Silk Road and are known for their extraordinary cultural treasures.

Conclusion

Golmud is the perfect destination for adventurers, hikers, and people looking to experience Chinese and Tibetan culture. Discover Golmud’s unique natural beauty and rich cultural heritage while enjoying enriching outdoor activities, delicious cuisine, and relaxing accommodation options.
