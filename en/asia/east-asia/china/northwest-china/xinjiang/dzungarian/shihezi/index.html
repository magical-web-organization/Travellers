Introduction to Shihezi

Nestled in the Dzungarian Basin in the northwestern region of Xinjiang, China, Shihezi is a small city that is making a big impression on travelers. Shihezi was built as a military base in the 1950s, but the city has since grown into one of the most prosperous cities in Xinjiang. Visitors to Shihezi can expect to be welcomed with open arms by locals who are proud of their city’s unique history and multi-ethnic culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring the great outdoors, experiencing local culture, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local food and drink scene, Shihezi has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Shihezi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shihezi has a rich cultural heritage. Visitors to the city can experience this heritage first hand by visiting its museums and historical sites.

Shihezi Museum

The Shihezi Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Shihezi’s history. Located in the city center, the museum showcases artifacts from the city’s military past and highlights the achievements and contributions of its multi-ethnic population.

Shihezi University

Shihezi University is one of the top universities in China for agriculture, engineering, and medicine. The campus is a mix of modern and traditional architecture, making it a great place to explore. Visitors can also check out the university’s museum, which features exhibits on the many different ethnic groups that call Xinjiang home.

Natural Attractions of Shihezi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shihezi is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes. From scenic views of mountains to hiking and cycling trails, there’s no shortage of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Shihezi Park

Shihezi Park is the most popular park in the city. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the outdoors. The park features lush greenery, beautiful flowers, and stunning views of the Tianshan Mountains.

Laoye Mountain

Laoye Mountain is located on the outskirts of the city and is a popular destination for hiking and cycling. The mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area and is a great place to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shihezi

Shihezi has several great hiking and biking routes that offer visitors a chance to experience the city’s beautiful natural landscapes up close and personal.

Huayuan Mountain Hiking Trail

The Huayuan Mountain Hiking Trail is a scenic and challenging trail that takes hikers through some of Shihezi’s most beautiful natural landscapes. The trail is approximately ten kilometers long, with plenty of stops for rest and scenic views along the way.

Gongnaisi Reservoir Biking Route

The Gongnaisi Reservoir Biking Route is a fun and challenging route that takes bikers through some of Shihezi’s most beautiful rural landscapes. The route is approximately 12 kilometers long and offers a mix of dirt and paved roads.

Best Things to Do in Shihezi: Activities and Tours

Shihezi has a variety of activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Shihezi:

Join a City Tour

A city tour is a great way to explore Shihezi and learn about its history and culture. The tour takes visitors to some of the city’s most popular attractions, including the Shihezi Museum and Shihezi University.

Attend a Cultural Event

Shihezi is home to a rich and diverse mix of ethnic cultures, and the city hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year. Visitors can attend traditional festivals and ceremonies, taste local cuisine, and experience traditional music and dance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shihezi

For those planning to spend a week in Shihezi, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and City Tour

Arrive in Shihezi and explore the city’s landmarks with a guided city tour.

Day 2: Shihezi Park and Laoye Mountain

Visit Shihezi Park in the morning, then hike up Laoye Mountain in the afternoon for stunning views of the area.

Day 3: Shihezi University and Museum

Spend the day exploring the Shihezi University campus and the city’s museum, learning about the region’s fascinating history.

Day 4: Cycling at Gongnaisi Reservoir

Cycle along the scenic Gongnaisi Reservoir route, stopping for lunch and rest along the way.

Day 5: Local Culture and Customs

Experience the local culture and customs with a visit to one of Shihezi’s traditional festivals or ceremonies.

Day 6: Outdoor Adventures

Spend the day exploring the great outdoors with a visit to one of the city’s natural attractions, such as Huayuan Mountain Hiking Trail.

Day 7: Departure

Say goodbye to Shihezi and catch a flight or bus to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Shihezi

Must-Try Dishes

Shihezi is known for its unique cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the region’s diverse ethnic groups. Here are some must-try dishes:

Da Pan Ji

Da Pan Ji is a delicious chicken dish that’s cooked with potatoes, onions, peppers, and other vegetables. It’s a hearty and filling meal that’s perfect after a long day of exploring.

Noodle Soup

Noodle soup is a staple dish in Shihezi. The soup is made with handmade noodles, vegetables, and meat, and it’s served piping hot and flavorful.

Local Beverages

Shihezi is also known for its unique beverages, many of which are made from local ingredients. Here are some beverages to try:

Milk Tea

Milk tea is a popular beverage in Shihezi. It’s made from tea, milk, and sugar, and it’s perfect for a mid-day pick-me-up.

Fruit Juice

Fruit juice is another popular beverage in Shihezi. The city is known for its delicious local fruits, so be sure to try some freshly squeezed juice while you’re here.

Where to Stay in Shihezi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shihezi has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

Budget-Friendly: Shihezi Youth Hostel

The Shihezi Youth Hostel is a great option for travelers on a budget. The hostel has basic amenities and is located in the city center, making it a convenient base for exploring Shihezi.

Mid-Range: Shihezi Greenland International Hotel

The Shihezi Greenland International Hotel is a mid-range option that offers comfortable rooms and top-notch amenities. The hotel is located near many of the city’s top attractions, making it a great choice for those who want to be in the heart of the action.

Luxury: Sheraton Shihezi Hotel

The Sheraton Shihezi Hotel is a top-notch luxury hotel that offers spacious rooms, gourmet dining, and a host of other amenities. The hotel is located near some of the city’s best restaurants and shopping, making it a great choice for those who want to indulge a bit.

What to Buy in Shihezi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shihezi is known for its unique products, many of which are made from local ingredients or materials. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

Handmade Carpets

Shihezi is known for its high-quality handmade carpets. Visitors can purchase carpets directly from local artisans, or from one of the many carpet shops around the city.

Local Spices and Seasonings

Shihezi is known for its unique blend of spices and seasonings, which are used in many local dishes. Visitors can purchase these spices and seasonings at local markets and shops around the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Shihezi

Shihezi is home to a rich and diverse mix of ethnic cultures, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect these cultures while exploring the city. Be sure to dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending traditional events, and always ask permission before taking photos of local people.

Best Time to Visit Shihezi

The best time to visit Shihezi is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers in Shihezi can be hot and humid, while winters are cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shihezi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Explore the city’s natural landscapes

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Ignore safety precautions while exploring the city

Safety and Health

Safety in Shihezi

Shihezi is generally a safe city for visitors, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas, and stay away from any large gatherings or protests.

Health in Shihezi

Tap water in Shihezi is not safe to drink, so be sure to stick to bottled or boiled water while you’re here. It’s also a good idea to pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as the city can get hot and buggy during the summer months.

Getting to Shihezi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shihezi

The nearest airport to Shihezi is the Shihezi Huayuan Airport, which offers flights from a number of domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Shihezi

Shihezi has several bus stations that offer service to nearby cities and regions. Be sure to check the bus schedules and buy tickets in advance, as they can sell out quickly during peak travel times.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Shihezi by train or car. The city is located near the G30 Expressway, which connects to other major cities in Xinjiang and China.

How to Get Around in Shihezi

Shihezi is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot, bike or taxi. Visitors can also take buses or hire a private car for longer journeys or day trips outside the city.

Where to Go Next from Shihezi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kanas Lake

Kanas Lake is a stunning alpine lake located in the Altay Mountains about 500 kilometers north of Shihezi. It’s a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Urumqi

Urumqi is the capital city of Xinjiang and is known for its vibrant mix of cultures and impressive history. Visitors can explore the city’s many museums and historical sites, as well as enjoy its many restaurants and shops.

Conclusion

Shihezi is a hidden gem in the northwestern region of Xinjiang, China. With its beautiful natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique local cuisine, Shihezi offers something for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re here for a week or just a few days, be sure to take the time to explore all that this amazing city has to offer.
