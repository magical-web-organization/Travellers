Introduction to Hami

Located in Dzungarian, in Xinjiang, in Northwest China, Hami is a desert oasis known for its melons, history, and outdoor adventures. The city, which is home to the Uighur ethnic group, has a rich cultural heritage that dates back thousands of years. Its strategic location at the crossroads of the ancient Silk Road made Hami an important hub for trade and cultural exchange.

Whether you want to explore the city’s historical landmarks, discover its natural beauty, or immerse yourself in local culture, Hami has something to offer every traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the top things to do and see in Hami, best places to eat and stay, local customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Hami

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hami Museum: Located in the city center, Hami Museum features artifacts and exhibits about the city’s history and culture. The museum has a collection of ancient documents, paintings, sculptures, and handicrafts.
  • Jiaohe Ruins: Jiaohe Ruins, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is an ancient ruined city located on a plateau at the center of the Turpan Basin. The Ruins are a breathtaking sight, and their unique architecture showcases the influence of both Buddhist and Islamic styles.
  • Tianchi Scenic Area: Situated northeast of Hami, the Tianchi Scenic Area offers stunning views of a high-altitude lake surrounded by mountains. The area is home to a wealth of diverse flora and fauna, including the argali sheep and the snow leopard.

Natural Attractions of Hami: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yadan National Geological Park: Known for its unique landscapes of wind-eroded rocks, Yadan National Geological Park is located about 180km southwest of Hami. The park is popular for hiking, camping, and exploring the desert landscapes.
  • Gobi Desert: The Gobi Desert covers a vast area of China, and Hami is no exception. Travelers can explore the desert’s vast expanse, marvel at its sand dunes, and learn about the local flora and fauna.
  • Hami Melon Valley: Located east of the city, Hami Melon Valley is a picturesque area known for its delicious melons. Visitors can taste the local fruit and explore the valley’s charming landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hami

  • Tianchi Scenic Area: The Tianchi Scenic Area offers a range of hiking trails that lead to stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Gobi Desert: Hiking in the Gobi Desert is a popular activity for adventurous travelers. Visitors can explore the desert’s dunes and discover its unique flora and fauna.
  • Yadan National Geological Park: The park’s hiking trails take you through the park’s unique rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Hami: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Local Markets: Hami is home to a range of local markets where visitors can find everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts.
  • Attend a Musical Performance: Local music is an important part of Uighur culture. Attend a performance to experience the city’s vibrant sound and color.
  • Visit the Hongshan Park: The Hongshan Park is a picturesque park in the center of the city. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk, relax in the park’s teahouse, and take in views of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hami

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Hami:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Hami and check into your hotel
Day 2 Explore the Hami Museum and Jiaohe Ruins
Day 3 Visit the Tianchi Scenic Area and hike around the lake

Food and Drink Scene of Hami

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast Lamb: Roast lamb is a staple in Uighur cuisine. The meat is seasoned with spices and slow-roasted over an open flame, resulting in a tender and flavorful dish.
  • Hami Melon: Hami is famous for its melons, and visitors should take advantage of the opportunity to try the juicy and sweet fruit.
  • Nang Bread: Nang bread is a type of flatbread that is baked in a clay oven. The bread is usually served with butter, cheese, or a range of meats.

Local Beverages

  • Black Tea: Black tea is a staple beverage in Uighur culture. The tea is usually sweetened with sugar and served with milk.
  • Kumiss: Kumiss is a fermented drink made from mare’s milk. It has a sour taste and is usually served cold.

Where to Stay in Hami: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hami Tian Yue Hotel: Located in the city center, Hami Tian Yue Hotel is a modern hotel with spacious rooms and a range of amenities.
  • Hami Youth Hostel: Hami Youth Hostel is an affordable and convenient option for travelers on a budget. The hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms.
  • Yipin Garden Hotel: Yipin Garden Hotel is a charming guesthouse located in the Hami Melon Valley. The guesthouse offers traditional Uighur-style rooms and a range of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Hami: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hami Melons: Hami Melons make a great souvenir for your friends and family back home. Look for vendors selling dried melon slices or packaged melons that can be easily transported.
  • Handicrafts: Hami is known for its traditional handicrafts, including silk embroidery, pottery, and woodcarvings. Look for local vendors selling handmade goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Hami

Hami is home to a distinct culture that is heavily influenced by the Uighur ethnic group. Visitors should be aware of local customs and etiquette to ensure a smooth and respectful trip. For example, it is customary to remove your shoes before entering a mosque and to dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Hami

The best time to visit Hami is from June to August when temperatures are warmest and outdoor activities are in full swing. The desert climate means that temperatures can be extreme, with hot summers and cold winters. Travelers should be prepared for weather fluctuations and pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hami

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Take off your shoes before entering a mosque or religious building.
  • Try the local cuisine and learn about the region’s culinary traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Touch religious artifacts or objects without permission.
  • Speak loudly or behave disrespectfully in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hami

Hami is a relatively safe city, but travelers should take normal precautions to ensure their safety. Be aware of your surroundings and be cautious when traveling alone at night.

Health in Hami

Travelers should be aware of the potential health risks associated with traveling to a desert region. Be sure to stay hydrated and protect your skin from the sun. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you.

Getting to Hami: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hami

Hami Airport is located about 40km north of the city and has connections to several major Chinese cities.

Bus Stations Near Hami

The main bus station in Hami is located near the city center and offers connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Train travel is another option for travelers visiting Hami. The city is served by the Lanzhou-Xinjiang Railway, which connects to several major Chinese cities.

How to Get Around in Hami

Taxis and buses are the most common modes of transportation in Hami. Taxis are readily available and are an affordable way to get around the city. Buses also offer a convenient and affordable option.

Where to Go Next from Hami: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hami is a great starting point for exploring the wider Xinjiang region. Some nearby destinations worth visiting include:

  • Turpan: Turpan is known for its unique landscapes and rich cultural history. Visitors can explore the ancient ruins, visit local vineyards, and learn about the region’s Islamic heritage.
  • Korla: Korla is a charming city located on the banks of the Tarim River. The city is known for its beautiful parks, bustling markets, and stunning natural surroundings.

Conclusion

Hami is a city rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, you’ll find plenty to see and do in Hami. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-prepared to plan your itinerary and make the most of your trip to this unique corner of China.
