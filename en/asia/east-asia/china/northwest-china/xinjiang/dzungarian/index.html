Introduction to Dzungarian

Located in Northwest China’s Xinjiang province, Dzungarian is a region that offers a mix of cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and outdoor adventure opportunities. With its stunning landscapes, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, Dzungarian makes for a fantastic destination for any traveler.

Food and Drink Scene of Dzungarian

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Roast Lamb (Kawap), which is a popular dish amongst the Kazakh and Mongolian Chinese
  2. Dapanji, a spicy chicken dish that is a Chinese Muslim speciality
  3. Laghman (pronounced as Lah-mian) which is a stir-fried noodle dish with vegetables and meat
  4. Samsa, which is baked meat or vegetable pastry that is very popular in Xinjiang

Local Beverages

  1. Milk tea with butter, which is a traditional drink of the Kazakh people
  2. Horse milk wine, called kumiss, which is another drink that the local people drink regularly

Local Culture and Customs of Dzungarian

Dzungarian is a region where a large number of ethnic Kazakhs live. To show respect for their culture, it’s essential to follow some basic customs such as:

  • When entering someone’s home, remember to take off your shoes
  • Address the elderly respectfully as “Auntie” and “Uncle”
  • Always try to avoid discussions on sensitive topics such as politics and religion

Best Time to Visit Dzungarian

The best time to visit Dzungarian is during the autumn season (September to November) when the weather is sunny and comfortable, making it an ideal time to explore the natural landscapes and to engage in outdoor activities. Summers (June to August) are also popular, but the temperatures can rise up to 35°C in some areas, while winters (December to March) are very snowy with temperatures dropping below freezing levels.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dzungarian

Do:

  • Respect the culture of the region’s Ethnic minorities
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Learn some simple Mandarin and Kazakh phrases in advance
  • Take care of your belongings while going on treks and hikes

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Talk loudly in public places
  • Drink excessively in public places
  • Take photographs of people without their consent

Safety and Health

Safety in Dzungarian

Dzungarian is considered safe with low crime rates, but it’s still good to maintain certain precautions:

  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded public areas
  • Stay alert and cautious while taking public transportation
  • Follow local regulations and obey no photo areas
  • Be aware of extreme weather changes

Health in Dzungarian

It is essential to take precautions against altitude sickness as some areas are above 2000 meters above sea level. If possible, gradually acclimate yourself to different altitudes to prevent illness. Additionally, it’s always essential to drink bottled or boiled water to avoid any contamination.

Getting to Dzungarian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dzungarian

The nearest airport to Dzungarian is the Urumqi International Airport, which is four hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Dzungarian

Dzungarian has an excellent road network and is well linked to several cities in the region. There are multiple bus service providers that operate between Dzungarian and neighboring cities like Urumqi and Altay.

Other Options

One can also take trains from Urumqi via stations such as Kuitun and Shihezi, which are all well-connected with other major cities of China.

How to Get Around in Dzungarian

Taxis are easily available in almost every part of Dzungarian. Alternately, buses and minibusses serve as cost-effective and convenient options for traveling around. For adventure lovers, renting a bike or taking a horse ride are some of the most popular alternatives for moving around different areas.
