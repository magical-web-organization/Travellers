Introduction to Kashgar

Kashgar, located in Tarim, in the Xinjiang region of Northwest China, is a city steeped in ancient history and vibrant culture. It is an important stop on the ancient Silk Road, which was the trade route connecting China to the rest of the world. With mesmerizing natural scenery, colorful markets, rich traditions, and fascinating architecture, Kashgar is a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to experience the diversity of Asia.

List of Places to Visit in Kashgar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Id Kah Mosque: This is the largest mosque in China and an important symbol of the Uighur people’s culture and religion.
  2. Abakh Khoja Tomb: This tomb, located in the western suburbs of Kashgar, is a testament to the Islamic heritage of the Uighur people.
  3. Kashgar Old Town: This fascinating maze of narrow streets and traditional houses is an important cultural site and offers a glimpse into the history of Kashgar.
  4. Xinjiang Regional Museum: This museum chronicles the region’s diverse history and includes exhibits on the Silk Road, ancient cultures, and modern Xinjiang.

Natural Attractions of Kashgar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Karakul Lake: This beautiful alpine lake is located at the foot of the stunning Muztagh Ata Mountain and is a popular destination for hiking and camping.
  2. Pamir Plateau: This vast plateau is renowned for its stunning scenery, including snow-capped peaks, river valleys, and deserts.
  3. Bazaar Street: This colorful marketplace is where the local people go shopping and offers a great opportunity to see the customs and traditions of Kashgar.
  4. Shipton’s Arch: This unique rock formation is located in Kashgar’s western mountains and is the highest natural arch in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kashgar

  1. Karakoram Highway: This world-famous road runs through the Himalayas and connects Kashgar to Islamabad, Pakistan.
  2. Kashgar to Karakul Lake: This 3-day trek is a popular option for those looking to explore the scenic beauty of Kashgar’s mountains and lakes.
  3. K2 Base Camp: This challenging 10-day trek takes hikers to the base of the second-highest mountain in the world and offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks and glaciers.

Best Things to Do in Kashgar: Activities and Tours

  1. Attend a Uighur Cultural Show: This vibrant showcase of music, dance, and traditional costumes is a great way to experience the rich culture of the Uighur people.
  2. Visit a Local Family: Many local families invite visitors into their homes to experience traditional Uighur hospitality and cuisine.
  3. Take a Camel Ride: These iconic animals are synonymous with the Silk Road and offer a unique way to explore the desert landscapes around Kashgar.

Food and Drink Scene of Kashgar

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Laghman: This traditional noodle dish is commonly found in Central Asia and is made with hand-pulled noodles, meat, and vegetables.
  2. Plov: Another Central Asian staple, Plov is a rice dish made with meat, carrots, onions, and spices.
  3. Nang Bread: This flatbread is a staple of Uighur cuisine and is commonly eaten with butter tea or soup.
  4. Roast Lamb: Kashgar is known for its delicious lamb dishes, and many local restaurants serve succulent, slow-roasted lamb seasoned with cumin and other spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Butter Tea: This salty, hot tea with butter is a traditional Uighur drink and is commonly served with Nang bread.
  2. Grape Wine: Grapes are grown in the region, and many local families produce their own grape wine.
  3. Melon Juice: Kashgar is famous for its sweet melons, and many vendors sell fresh melon juice on the streets of the city.

Where to Stay in Kashgar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  2. Radisson Blu Kashgar: This luxurious hotel boasts excellent service and top-notch amenities for those who want to stay in comfort.
  3. Uighur Life Hotel: This hotel is decorated in traditional Uighur style and offers a unique and welcoming experience for travelers.

What to Buy in Kashgar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Silk Carpets: These handwoven carpets are an important part of Kashgar’s artistic heritage and are available in many shops around the city.
  2. Jade Jewellery: The region is known for its high-quality jade, and there are many markets in Kashgar that sell beautiful jade necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.
  3. Traditional Uighur Hats: These unique hats are a symbol of Uighur culture and can be found in many shops and markets throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Kashgar

Kashgar is home to a diverse array of ethnic groups, including Uighurs, Tajiks, and Han Chinese. The region has a proud history and rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its art, food, music, and architecture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes in religious sites and conservative attire in public places.

Best Time to Visit Kashgar

The best time to visit Kashgar is during the spring (April to June) and the fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and the scenery is at its most stunning during these seasons. Summers in Kashgar can be hot, with temperatures reaching 35°C, while winters can be cold, with temperatures below freezing and occasional snowstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kashgar

Do:

  1. Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  2. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  3. Try local Uighur cuisine and attend a cultural show.
  4. Take the time to learn some of the local language and greetings.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t take photos of local people without their permission.
  2. Don’t disrespect Islamic customs and beliefs.
  3. Don’t leave valuables unattended in public places.
  4. Don’t do anything that could be viewed as disrespectful or offensive to local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kashgar

Kashgar is generally safe for travelers, but visitors should be aware of the risk of petty theft, especially in crowded areas such as markets and train stations. The region is also prone to earthquakes, and travelers should be mindful of the latest news and updates on the country’s disaster response plan.

Health in Kashgar

Visitors should be cautious when drinking tap water and should carry a supply of bottled water with them at all times. The region is also prone to dust storms and high levels of pollution, and travelers with respiratory issues should take appropriate precautions. It is advisable to have travel insurance that covers emergency medical treatment and repatriation.

Getting to Kashgar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kashgar

Kashgar Airport is located about 10 km from the city center and is served by several domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Kashgar

The city also has several bus stations that connect to nearby cities and towns, including the Kashgar Long-distance Bus Station and the Kashgar South Bus Station.

Other Options

The Kashgar Train Station offers train services to several cities in China, including Urumqi and Beijing. Visitors can also travel by taxi or private car, although roads in the region can be rough and challenging to navigate.

How to Get Around in Kashgar

Taxis are the most popular mode of transportation in Kashgar, and visitors can easily hail one on the streets of the city. There are also local buses and minibusses that serve the city and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Kashgar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Urumqi: This regional capital is located about 1,000 km away from Kashgar and is home to several historical attractions and scenic destinations.
  2. Turpan: This desert town is known for its ancient ruins and natural wonders, including the Grape Valley and the Flaming Mountains.
  3. Hotan: This oasis town is famous for its jade and silk industries and is home to several historical and cultural sites.

Conclusion

Kashgar is a hidden gem in Northwest China, offering travelers a unique opportunity to explore the history, traditions, and stunning natural beauty of the region. With its colorful markets, ancient architecture, and delicious cuisine, this city offers an experience that is both exotic and familiar.
