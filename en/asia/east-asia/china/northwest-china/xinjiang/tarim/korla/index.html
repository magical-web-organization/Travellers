Introduction to Korla

Nestled in the heart of Tarim in Xinjiang, Korla is a hidden gem in Northwest China waiting to be discovered. Surrounded by the towering Tianshan Mountains and the vast Taklamakan Desert, Korla is a city of natural wonders and rich cultural treasures. It boasts a unique blend of ethnic groups, including Uyghur, Chinese, Kazakh, and Mongolian, making it an ideal destination for those seeking authentic experiences.

This ultimate guide to Korla will provide you with everything you need to know about this extraordinary city, from the best places to visit to the local culture and customs. Let’s begin!

List of Places to Visit in Korla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Korla Museum: Located in the city center, this museum showcases the city’s history, culture, and art, with exhibits featuring ancient artifacts, folk costumes, and traditional musical instruments.
  • Jiaohe Ruins: This site contains the remains of an ancient city that dates back more than 2,000 years, including temples, palaces, and residential areas. It offers a glimpse into the region’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Iron Door Pass: This pass is a strategic point on the ancient Silk Road and was once an important trade route. Visitors can explore the ruins of the ancient fortifications and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Natural Attractions of Korla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tianchi Lake: This stunning alpine lake is located in the Tianshan Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped peaks and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can enjoy hiking, boating, fishing, and photography.
  • Bayinbuluke Grassland: This vast grassland is a paradise for nature lovers and offers stunning views of the horizon stretching across the grassy plains. Visitors can engage in activities such as horse riding, camping, and stargazing.
  • Kuqa Grand Canyon: This canyon is a geological wonder, with towering rock formations, waterfalls, and crystal-clear streams. Visitors can hike through the canyon and explore its natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Korla

  • Tianshan Mountain Range: With its spectacular peaks and valleys, the Tianshan Mountains offer some of the most exhilarating hiking and biking routes in the region. Visitors can choose from a variety of routes, including the Kuche Grassland and the Southern Mountain Range.
  • Kuqa Grand Canyon: This canyon offers challenging hiking and biking routes, with steep inclines, rocky terrain, and narrow trails. Visitors should be prepared for a strenuous but rewarding adventure.
  • Goundi River: This river is a popular spot for hiking and biking, with its picturesque landscapes and peaceful surroundings. Visitors can also enjoy fishing and picnicking along the riverbanks.

Best Things to Do in Korla: Activities and Tours

  • Silk Road Tour: Korla is situated on the ancient Silk Road, and visitors can explore the region’s rich cultural heritage by taking a Silk Road tour. There are several tour operators offering guided tours of historical sites, ancient temples, and local markets.
  • Ethnic Festival: Korla is home to a diverse community of ethnic groups, and visitors can experience their unique traditions and customs by attending one of the many ethnic festivals held throughout the year. Highlights include the Uyghur Eid al-Fitr celebration and the Mongolian Nadam festival.
  • Hot Springs: Korla is blessed with several natural hot springs, and visitors can relax and rejuvenate in the warm waters while enjoying the stunning natural scenery. The most popular hot springs include the Bayinbuluke Jinshan Hot Springs and the Kuche Hot Springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Korla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lagman: A hearty noodle dish, often served with lamb or beef and seasoned with cumin, garlic, and chili.
  • Kawaplar: Skewered lamb or beef, marinated in spices and grilled over an open flame.
  • Samsa: A pastry filled with lamb or beef and onions, seasoned with cumin and black pepper.

Local Beverages

  • Kumiss: A slightly sour and carbonated alcoholic beverage made from fermented mare’s milk.
  • Dawazi: A tea made from a local plant, with a refreshing and slightly bitter flavor.
  • Xinjiang Red Wine: A fruity and bold wine made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Korla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Korla Hotel: Located in the city center, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.
  • Kashgar Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly option, with dormitory and private rooms, a shared kitchen, and a lounge area.
  • Silk Road Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse, with comfortable rooms, a courtyard, and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

What to Buy in Korla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carpet and Rugs: Korla is famous for its hand-woven carpets and rugs, with intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  • Jade and Agate: The region is also known for its high-quality jade and agate, which make excellent souvenirs.
  • Peacock Feather Fan: A unique handicraft made from peacock feathers, which are woven together to create a beautiful and practical fan.

Local Culture and Customs of Korla

Korla has a diverse cultural heritage, with a mix of ethnic groups and religions. The city is predominantly Muslim, and visitors should respect local customs, such as dressing modestly in public and not eating or drinking in public during Ramadan.

Best Time to Visit Korla

The best time to visit Korla is from May to October, when the weather is warm and dry. July and August are the peak season, so visitors should book their accommodations and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Korla

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Dress modestly in public places.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Discuss sensitive political or religious issues.

Safety and Health

Safety in Korla

Korla is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on their belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Korla

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude of the Tianshan Mountains and take precautions to prevent altitude sickness, such as drinking plenty of water and acclimatizing slowly. It is also important to carry sunscreen and insect repellent in the outdoor areas.

Getting to Korla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Korla is accessible by air, bus, and train.

Airports Near Korla

Korla Airport is located 15 kilometers north of the city center and offers flights to major cities in China, such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

Bus Stations Near Korla

Korla Bus Station is located in the city center and offers buses to nearby cities, such as Urumqi, Kashgar, and Kuqa.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Korla, as the city is connected to the national railway network.

How to Get Around in Korla

Visitors can get around Korla by taxi, bus, or bicycle. Taxis are the most convenient option and are readily available throughout the city. Buses are a cheaper option, but they can be crowded and confusing for non-Chinese speakers. Bicycles are a great way to explore the city at a leisurely pace and can be rented from hotels and bicycle shops.

Where to Go Next from Korla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kashgar: Known as the “Pearl of the Silk Road,” Kashgar is a fascinating city with a rich history and unique culture. Visitors can explore the old town, attend a bazaar, and visit historical sites, such as the Tomb of Abakh Khoja.
  • Kuqa: This ancient city is known for its Buddhist temples, historical sites, and stunning natural scenery. Highlights include the Kizil Thousand Buddha Caves and the Subashi Ruins.
  • Taklamakan Desert: The largest desert in China, the Taklamakan offers visitors an unforgettable adventure, with its towering sand dunes, oasis towns, and Silk Road ruins.

Conclusion

Korla is a city of contrasts, with natural wonders and cultural treasures waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Korla has something for everyone. We hope this ultimate guide has inspired you to explore this fascinating city and its surrounding region. Safe travels!
