Introduction to Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China, one of the world’s most iconic landmarks, spans over 13,000 miles and was built over 2,000 years ago. It’s a testament to China’s rich history and cultural legacy, as well as an impressive display of ancient engineering skills. This guide will take you through the best things to do in the Great Wall of China, from exploring culturally significant attractions to hiking and biking routes, local food and drink, and practical tips on safety and logistics.

List of Places to Visit in Great Wall of China

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visiting the Great Wall of China means immersing yourself in China’s long and dynamic history. Some of the most intriguing cultural attractions include the Ming Tombs, where 13 emperors of the Ming Dynasty are buried, the Summer Palace, a stunning garden built in the Qing Dynasty, and the Forbidden City, where the rulers of China lived for centuries.

For a more in-depth look at the wall’s long history, the Great Wall Museum is your go-to destination. It offers a comprehensive overview of the wall’s construction and its cultural significance, as well as exhibitions on military strategy, customs and artwork related to the Great Wall.

Natural Attractions of Great Wall of China: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Great Wall of China isn’t only a historical monument; it also offers some of the most breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. While there are numerous ways to enjoy the wall’s natural beauty, hiking and biking routes are among the most popular activities.

The Simatai section of the Great Wall is known for its stunning lake views, mountain terrain, and foggy landscape. The Badaling section, the most visited part of the wall and the closest to Beijing, is perfect for accessible hikes that give you an up-close experience of the wall’s design and architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Wall of China

For some of the most breathtaking views of the Great Wall of China, hiking or biking is a must. Dongbei Yanqing Great Wall is a popular trail that offers breathtaking views of the wall and the surrounding natural beauty along the way.

Best Things to Do in Great Wall of China: Activities and Tours

Exploring the Great Wall of China with a guided tour is a great way to maximize your time and see the most important sites. Some of the most popular tours focus on the cultural heritage of the wall, while others offer more active experiences, such as biking and hiking.

One of the most popular tours is the Beijing Classic Day Tour, which covers the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square and the Mutianyu Great Wall. If you’re looking for a more active experience, the Beijing Mutianyu Great Wall Bike Tour takes you along some of the best trails while providing stunning views of the architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Wall of China

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine around the Great Wall of China is diverse, aromatic, and delicious. Some of the must-try dishes include Peking roast duck, a savory duck dish that’s often served with pancakes and scallions, and jiaozi, a type of dumpling made with a variety of fillings such as pork or vegetables.

Local Beverages

There are plenty of local beverages to quench your thirst, including beer, tea, and baijiu, a clear alcoholic beverage made from sorghum. Huanghe Laobaigan is a popular baijiu brand that’s perfect for sipping on a cold day.

Where to Stay in Great Wall of China: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Staying in a traditional guesthouse or hostel is an excellent way to experience the local culture and meet other travelers. Some of the best guesthouses and hostels around the Great Wall of China include the Brickyard Retreat in Mutianyu and the Jinshanling Inn.

What to Buy in Great Wall of China: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bringing home souvenirs is a great way to remember your trip to the Great Wall of China. Some of the most popular items include handmade silk carpets, traditional paintings, and porcelain teapots.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China plays an important role in Chinese culture and is a symbol of the country’s strength and perseverance. To show respect for local customs and traditions, dress modestly, remove your shoes before entering homes or temples, and always ask before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Great Wall of China

The best time to visit the Great Wall of China is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and the crowds are more manageable. If you’re a winter sports enthusiast or want to experience the wall in the snow, plan your visit during December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Wall of China

Do:

Wear comfortable shoes and dress in layers Bring sunscreen, water, and snacks Be respectful of local culture and customs

Don’t:

Litter or damage the wall Take rocks or other souvenirs from the wall Feed wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Wall of China

While the Great Wall of China is a safe destination, it’s essential to stay alert and take precautions. Avoid walking or hiking alone, be aware of your surroundings, and carry a map or GPS to avoid getting lost.

Health in Great Wall of China

To ensure good health during your trip, avoid drinking tap water, wash your hands regularly, and pack a first-aid kit.

Getting to Great Wall of China: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Wall of China

The Beijing Capital International Airport is the closest airport to the Great Wall of China and is around an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Great Wall of China

There are two major bus stations in Beijing: the Beijing Jinyu Hutong Bus Station and the Beijing Gaojiayuan Bus Station. Both offer daily services to the Great Wall of China.

Other Options

Another popular transportation option is hiring a taxi or car to take you from your hotel directly to the wall.

How to Get Around in Great Wall of China

The wall is easily accessible via public transportation or car hire, and tours offer a convenient way to see the major attractions. If you’re interested in hiking or biking, there are various trails and routes available along the wall.

Where to Go Next from Great Wall of China: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

With so much to see and do in China, there are plenty of exciting destinations to visit next. You could head south to the riverside city of Guilin, which is famous for its stunning natural scenery and the iconic Li River, or east to Shanghai, a bustling metropolis known for its vibrant architecture.

Conclusion

The Great Wall of China is an awe-inspiring destination that offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the many cultural attractions, hiking or biking along the wall, or trying out some of the local cuisine, this guide has everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this iconic landmark.
