Introduction to Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou is a fascinating city located in the central region of the Henan province in China. It’s known as the “Green City” due to its numerous parks and natural scenery. Zhengzhou is also a significant transportation hub of China, hence making it an excellent tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou city offers various miniature cultural experiences, from museums and historical monuments, to natural attractions such as scenic views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the most popular tourist spots in Zhengzhou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shaolin Temple: This legendary temple is renowned for being the birthplace of Shaolin Kung Fu.
  • Henan Museum: The Henan Museum is home to over 130,000 pieces of historical artifacts, including the national treasure, ‘Jade Cong.’
  • Erqi Memorial Tower: A historical monument that has witnessed Zhengzhou’s growth and transformation over many years.
  • White Horse Temple: This temple is considered as China’s first Buddhist temple and was built during the Eastern Han Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Zhengzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellow River Scenic Area: The Yellow River Scenic Area is a breathtaking natural destination featuring mountains, rivers, and lakes.
  • Mount Song’s Scenic Area: This scenic area is a popular hiking spot in China and boasts natural beauty and unparalleled views.
  • Zhengdong New District Park: This green park is a favorite destination for the locals and showcases the modernity of Zhengzhou.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou region features a diverse range of hiking and biking trails, offering a unique opportunity to explore nature and the city. Some of the popular hiking/biking trails include:

  • Shaolin Kung-Fu Footways: This trail leads to the Shaolin Temple, a famous martial arts site and offers a picturesque landscape of the temple.
  • Xinzheng Taihang Grand Canyon Scenic Area: A 10km hiking trail that offers stunning views of canyons and valleys.
  • Mount Song National Forest Park: This park offers the chance to explore nature with an extensive network of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Zhengzhou: Activities and Tours

  • Watch a Kung Fu Performance: Witness world-renowned skills of Shaolin Kung Fu performers who showcase different styles of martial arts.
  • Experience Traditional Chinese Medicine: Learn about and experience traditional Chinese medicine’s healing power at various medicine centers in Zhengzhou.
  • Visit a Night Market: Explore the colors, sounds, and tastes of local Zhengzhou street food while walking through a vibrant night market.
  • Attend the Zhengzhou International Shaolin Wushu Festival: Enjoy an event that celebrates martial arts in the world and is hosted in Zhengzhou.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhengzhou

Day 1: Arrive in Zhengzhou and visit Guanlin Temple in Kaifeng City. Day 2: Visit the Yellow River Scenic Area in Zhengzhou city. Day 3: Explore the Henan Museum in Zhengzhou City. Day 4: Experience the natural beauty of Mount Song scenic area and watch a Kung Fu Performance in Shaolin Temple. Day 5: Go for hiking trails at Xinzheng Taihang Canyon and visit a local night market afterward. Day 6: Learn about traditional Chinese medicine in the city. Day 7: Attend the Zhengzhou International Shaolin Wushu Festival and watch some of the world’s best martial artists in action.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou is known for its unique and delicious cuisine, including local food items such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Xi Hong Shi Chao Ji Dan: Fried tomato and egg is a local dish that is savory and hearty.
  • Kao Fu: Grilled gluten with a sweet and spicy sauce.
  • Water-boiled duck: Flavorful and spicy duck dish popular in Zhengzhou.

Local Beverages

  • Sorghum Liquor: A popular alcoholic drink made by fermenting sorghum.
  • Taiji Spring Water: A famous mineral water brand from Zhengzhou city.

Where to Stay in Zhengzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhengzhou has various accommodation options to choose from, catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Wanda Vista Zhengzhou: A five-star hotel located in the central business district of Zhengzhou city.
  • Yitel Zhongyuan Road: A budget-friendly hotel that offers affordable and comfortable rooms.
  • 7 Days Premium Hotel: A guesthouse-style accommodation that offers a cozy and homely environment.

What to Buy in Zhengzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhengzhou is known for its rich history and culture, and you can find many unique souvenir items and products. Consider buying:

  • Zhengzhou-style Embroidery: A local handicraft that uses silk threads to embroider elaborate designs.
  • Jade Carvings: Jade carvings are a traditional local product that is collective and friendly priced.
  • Kaifeng-style Baked Crisp Noodles: A crispy and savory snack with a history of over a thousand years.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s essential to respect local customs and traditions. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Elders/Landlords are Respected: Pay respect to elders, landlords, and locals by using appropriate etiquette.
  • Don’t Overtip: Unlike western countries, overtipping in China is unnecessary and can seem inappropriate.
  • Celebrate Festivals: China celebrates various festivals throughout the year, such as the Chinese New Year and the Mid-Autumn Festival.

Best Time to Visit Zhengzhou

The best time to visit Zhengzhou is during the spring and autumn months (April to May and September to November), when the city weather is mild and pleasant, and the scenery is beautiful. Avoid visiting during summertime (June to August) when it’s hot and humid or winter (December to February) when it’s cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhengzhou

Do:

  • Use an Appropriate Greeting: “Nǐ hǎo” is a common way to say hello in Zhengzhou.
  • Carry Appropriate Identification: Always carry some ID, such as a passport or driving license.

Don’t:

  • Spitting: Avoid spitting in public places as it is considered vulgar and offensive.
  • Rush Hour: Try and avoid rush hours (7-9 am and 5-7 pm) to prevent overcrowding and long queues.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou is a safe city for tourists, but as in any city, there are some safety considerations to take into account.

  • Watch your Belongings: Be aware of pickpockets and scammers in crowded areas such as train stations, tourist sites, and markets.
  • Avoid Drinking Tap Water: Avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled or boiled water instead.

Health in Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou has adequate healthcare facilities, but as a visitor to China, it’s recommended to have health insurance in place.

Getting to Zhengzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport is the major airport located in Zhengzhou city and is serviced by both international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou is well-connected by bus stations that operate regional and inter-city buses.

Other Options

  • Train: Zhengzhou is a major railway hub, and it’s easy to travel to other parts of China.
  • Metro: Zhengzhou has a modern and extensive metro system.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Zhengzhou, and it’s an affordable mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Zhengzhou

Zhengzhou has an extensive and modern public transportation system, including buses, metro, taxis, and hired cars.

Where to Go Next from Zhengzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Luoyang: Famous for its Longmen Grottoes and White Horse Temple, Luoyang is a history and cultural hotspot for tourists.
  • Kaifeng: Full of an array of historical sites that showcase the ancient Northern Song Dynasty, Kaifeng is a must-visit destination from Zhengzhou.
  • Jiujiang: Located in adjacent Jiangxi Province and known as a scenic spot with plenty of beautiful lakes and mountains.

Conclusion

Zhengzhou is an exciting city with rich natural beauty, history, culture, and cuisine. It’s an excellent tourist destination that provides visitors with unique experiences and memories.
