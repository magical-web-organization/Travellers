Introduction to South Henan

Located in the northern part of China, Henan is home to many historical and cultural attractions. South Henan, in particular, is known for its connection to ancient China’s political and economic center. The region is a mix of urban and rural areas, with bustling cities and quiet, idyllic countryside. There are many things to see and do in South Henan, from exploring the region’s rich history to enjoying the natural beauty of the area.

List of Places to Visit in South Henan

If you’re planning a trip to South Henan, there are many places you should consider visiting. Below is a list of the top attractions in the area, divided into categories for your convenience.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Shaolin Temple - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Shaolin Temple is a must-visit for anyone interested in martial arts or Chinese culture. The temple is famous for its connection to the Shaolin Kung Fu style and is the birthplace of Zen Buddhism.
  2. Longmen Grottoes - These ancient Buddhist cave temples are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and hold over 100,000 statues and images of Buddha and his disciples.
  3. Luoyang Museum - Located in the city of Luoyang, this museum features exhibitions on the region’s history, culture, and art.

Natural Attractions of South Henan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Yuntai Mountain - This scenic mountain range is perfect for hiking and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. Songshan Mountain - This mountain range is known for its natural beauty, and there are several hiking trails that take you past waterfalls, forests, and flowers.
  3. Yellow River Scenic Area - Located in the city of Zhengzhou, this park features several scenic spots along the Yellow River, including the famous Kaifeng Iron Tower.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Henan

South Henan is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include the Yuntai Mountain Scenic Area and the Qinling Mountains.

Best Things to Do in South Henan: Activities and Tours

  1. Shaolin Kung Fu Show - Experience the awe-inspiring martial arts skills of the Shaolin monks in a traditional performance.
  2. Luoyang Peony Festival - Taking place in April each year, this festival features the blooming of thousands of peonies in Luoyang.
  3. Yellow River Cruise - Take a boat trip along the Yellow River for the chance to see stunning scenery and learn about the river’s cultural significance.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Henan

If you have a week to explore South Henan, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Zhengzhou and visit Yellow River Scenic Area Day 2-3: Explore the Longmen Grottoes and Luoyang Museum Day 4-5: Visit the Shaolin Temple and take part in a Kung Fu class or show Day 6-7: Enjoy the natural beauty of Yuntai Mountain and Songshan Mountain

Food and Drink Scene of South Henan

No trip to South Henan is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Luoyang Water Banquet - A traditional banquet featuring over 20 cold dishes and various soups, all made with the region’s clean water.
  2. Kaifeng Steamed Buns - These fluffy, white buns are filled with delicious meat or vegetable fillings.
  3. Yanji Salted Duck - Duck is preserved with salt and medicinal herbs, and served as an appetizer or main dish

Local Beverages

  1. Kaifeng Naked Oat Wine - This wine is made from naked oat, a cereal grain that is a specialty of the region.
  2. Songquan Tea - This tea is grown in the Songshan Mountains and is known for its health benefits and delicious taste.
  3. Mung Bean Drink - A sweet and refreshing drink made from mung beans and sugar.

Where to Stay in South Henan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in South Henan, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular hotels include the Hilton Zhengzhou, Holiday Inn Luoyang City Centre, and Yuntai Mountain International Culture & Convention Center.

What to Buy in South Henan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Zhengzhou Xingyang Pear - These pears are a famous local product and are known for their juicy and sweet taste.
  2. Kaifeng Traditional Embroidery - This intricate embroidery technique has been passed down for generations in Kaifeng and features delicate patterns and designs.
  3. Songshan Tai Chi Clothing - If you’re interested in martial arts, consider purchasing some Tai Chi clothing, which is a popular souvenir in South Henan.

Local Culture and Customs of South Henan

South Henan is known for its rich cultural heritage, including traditional music, dance, and art. The region is also home to many unique festivals and celebrations, such as the Luoyang Peony Festival and the Dragon Boat Festival. When visiting South Henan, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions, such as covering your shoulders and knees when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit South Henan

The best time to visit South Henan is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. However, if you’re interested in attending one of the region’s many festivals, it’s best to plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Henan

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages
  3. Learn basic Mandarin phrases to communicate with locals

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect religious sites or beliefs
  2. Damage or remove artifacts from historical sites
  3. Wear revealing clothing in public

Safety and Health

Safety in South Henan

South Henan is a safe region to visit, but tourists should take normal precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables secure.

Health in South Henan

Tap water is not safe to drink in South Henan, so it’s recommended that visitors stick to bottled water. South Henan is also in a moderate-risk area for malaria, so take preventive measures such as using insect repellent and sleeping under mosquito nets.

Getting to South Henan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Henan

The closest airports to South Henan are Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport and Luoyang Beijiao Airport. Both airports offer regular domestic flights to major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near South Henan

There are several bus stations in South Henan that offer regular bus services to nearby towns and cities. Some popular bus stations include Zhengzhou Bus Station and Luoyang Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to South Henan by train, with regular services to Zhengzhou and Luoyang from major cities in China.

How to Get Around in South Henan

There are many transportation options in South Henan, including public buses, taxis, and bicycles. For visitors looking to explore the region’s natural beauty, renting a car or hiring a private driver is also a good option.

Where to Go Next from South Henan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, consider visiting some nearby destinations from South Henan, such as Xi’an, Shanxi, or Beijing. All of these destinations offer unique cultural and historical attractions and are easily accessible by train or bus from South Henan.

Conclusion

South Henan is a fascinating region of China with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to South Henan and has inspired you to explore this beautiful part of the world.
