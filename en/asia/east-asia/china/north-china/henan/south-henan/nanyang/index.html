Introduction to Nanyang

Nanyang is a hidden gem in the South Henan Province of North China. It is surrounded by mountains, rivers, national parks, and historical sites. Nanyang is one of the most ancient cities in the world, dating back to the Neolithic period, and has been a center of various dynasties of China. Nanyang is often overlooked by travelers but it offers numerous things to do and see, including cultural and natural attractions, delicious food, and a unique local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Nanyang

When you visit Nanyang, there are many places you can’t afford to miss. Here is a list of top places to visit in Nanyang.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Henan Provincial Museum

This museum houses the art and relics of over 5,000 years of Chinese history. The collections include ceramics, bronze ware, jade, calligraphy, and painting, among others.

Nanyang Museum

It is a comprehensive museum that showcases the history and culture of Nanyang city from Neolithic times to modern times. The museum is divided into six parts: prehistoric culture, ancient times, Ming and Qing dynasties, modern times, natural resources, and animal specimens.

Xixia Tomb

Located in Yangshe township, the Xixia Tomb is the largest and most well-preserved tomb of the Western Xia Dynasty (1038-1227) in China. It is known for its unique architecture, delicate murals, and well-preserved cultural relics.

Baolun Temple

Baolun Temple is a classical Buddhist temple known for its exquisite architecture and amazing murals. It was first built in the Tang Dynasty and has undergone numerous renovations throughout history.

Natural Attractions of Nanyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Shiyan Scenic Area

Located in the northwest of Nanyang, Mount Shiyan Scenic Area is known for its imposing peaks, waterfalls, and hot springs. The area is perfect for hiking and camping.

Baotianman National Nature Reserve

Baotianman National Nature Reserve is one of the most diverse national parks in China. It is home to over 2400 species of plants and 570 species of animals, including some of the rarest animals in China.

Wuzhi Mountain Forest Park

Wuzhi Mountain Forest Park is a popular destination for nature lovers. It is known for its various hiking trails, waterfalls, and towering peaks. Tourists can also participate in various activities like paragliding, horseback riding, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanyang

Nanyang is known for its many hiking and biking routes, which offer an excellent way to explore the city’s natural beauty. Some popular hiking and biking routes in Nanyang include Mount Shiyan Scenic Area, Shixiang Lake, and Wuzhi Mountain Forest Park, among others. These routes are perfect for travelers and adventurers of all skill levels.

When in Nanyang, visitors can engage in many exciting activities and can take informative tours. Some of the best things to do in Nanyang include:

Food and Drink Scene of Nanyang

Nanyang has a unique and diverse food scene influenced by both northern and southern Chinese cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Nanyang.

Must-Try Dishes

Sanmen Noodles

Sanmen Noodles are a traditional dish in Nanyang made with a variety of seasonings and toppings. The noodles are long and thin, and are often served with diced pork, mustard, scrambled eggs, and other vegetables.

Rice Dumplings

Rice Dumplings are a traditional snack usually eaten around the Dragon Boat Festival. They are made with glutinous rice and various fillings, such as pork, mushrooms, and preserved vegetables.

Laba Toufu

Laba Toufu is a popular traditional food in Nanyang made with fermented beans. It has a unique flavor and can be served as a snack or a side dish.

Local Beverages

Buffalo Milk

Buffalo Milk is a refreshing and healthy beverage in Nanyang made by boiling buffalo milk and adding sugar and other ingredients. It is believed to have medicinal qualities and is enjoyed by both locals and tourists alike.

Nanyang Tea

Nanyang Tea is a highly-regarded Chinese tea and a perfect choice for tea lovers. The tea has a light, sweet taste and is often served with cakes or snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Nanyang

Nanyang has a unique local culture influenced by its ancient history and geographical location. Here are some of the cultural customs that you should keep in mind when in Nanyang:

  • Be respectful to elders
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious venues
  • Avoid whistle-blowing or loud talking in public places

Best Time to Visit Nanyang

Nanyang has a temperate climate with four distinct seasons. The best time to visit Nanyang is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and comfortable. The temperature during these months ranges between 10°C-25°C (50°F-77°F). Summer can be hot and humid, with temperatures frequently above 30°C (86°F), while winter can be cold with occasional snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanyang

Do:

  • Try the local street food
  • Respect the traditional customs and beliefs
  • Try to learn some basic Chinese phrases

Don’t:

  • Raise your voice or become aggressive in public
  • Take photos without permission
  • Pour tea for yourself in a group setting

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanyang

Nanyang is a relatively safe city, and its crime rate is low. The city also has an efficient public security system, making it a safe place for visitors. However, it is still advisable to be cautious when traveling and to always secure your belongings.

Health in Nanyang

Nanyang has advanced medical facilities and staff dedicated to providing excellent medical care to patients. However, it is recommended that visitors purchase adequate travel insurance and consult a medical professional before traveling to Nanyang.

Nanyang is a fascinating city that is often overlooked by travelers. However, it offers some of the most spectacular natural and cultural attractions in China.
