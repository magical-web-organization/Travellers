Introduction to Luoyang

Luoyang is a city located in West Henan province in North China. It was the capital of 13 dynasties in ancient times, which makes it a city rich in history and culture. Today, Luoyang attracts visitors from all over the world to its museums, historical sites, natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. In this travel guide, we will explore the must-see sites, top things to do, local food and drinks, best places to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Luoyang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Longmen Grottoes - A UNESCO World Heritage site consisting of thousands of Buddhist sculptures and cave temples carved into limestone cliffs.
  2. Luoyang Museum - A museum dedicated to the archaeological finds of Luoyang and surrounding areas, dating back to the Neolithic period.
  3. White Horse Temple - One of the oldest Buddhist temples in China, built in 68 AD.
  4. Guanlin Temple - A temple dedicated to Guan Yu, a famous general in the Three Kingdoms period, where visitors can also see many ancient relics and artifacts.
  5. Sui and Tang Dynasties Relics Botanical Garden - A botanical garden featuring cultural relics and ruins from the Sui and Tang dynasties.

Natural Attractions of Luoyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Baiyun Mountain - A mountain that offers stunning views of the Luoyang cityscape and numerous hiking trails.
  2. Yuntai Mountain - A national geopark with unique rock formations, waterfalls, rivers, and scenic views.
  3. Jiguan Cavern - A scenic spot with a karst cave system and underground river.
  4. Luoyang National Peony Garden - A garden featuring a vast collection of peonies, the national flower of China.
  5. Wangcheng Park - A park built on the ruins of the Eastern Zhou dynasty capital, with beautiful gardens, lakes, and ancient buildings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luoyang

  1. Longmen Shan - A 20-kilometer mountain hike that offers stunning views of rural Luoyang and the Longmen Grottoes.
  2. Mao Mountain - A scenic mountain hike that takes visitors through historic sites, waterfalls, and thick forests.
  3. Xiyuan Scenic Area - A biking trail that winds through the Xiyuan Scenic Area and provides a stunning view of the Luoyang cityscape.

Best Things to Do in Luoyang: Activities and Tours

  1. Hot Air Balloon Ride - Enjoy an unforgettable view of Luoyang and its surrounding areas while floating in a hot air balloon.
  2. Kung Fu Show - Take in a traditional kung fu show that showcases the famous Shaolin martial arts.
  3. River Cruise - Go on a river cruise to view the stunning limestone cliffs and other natural wonders of the Luoyang region.
  4. Night Market Tour - Explore the bustling night markets of Luoyang, filled with local street food and crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Luoyang

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Luoyang Water Banquet - A banquet served with 24 cold dishes that are dipped into hot water before eating.
  2. Guan Tang Bao - Delicious soup-filled buns that have a rich history in Luoyang’s cuisine.
  3. Shaolong Noodles - Noodles made from a unique recipe that has maintained its popularity for hundreds of years.
  4. Rou Jia Mo - A sandwich filled with stewed pork meat and seasoned with a variety of spices.
  5. Luoyang Mutton Soup - A soup made with tender mutton and clear broth.

Local Beverages

  1. Mead - A traditional Chinese fortified wine made from honey and barley.
  2. Jun - A refreshing fermented green tea that is mildly alcoholic.
  3. Peanut Milk - A creamy, delicious drink made with peanuts that is perfect for hot days.

  5. Hanting Express Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel chain that offers clean and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Luoyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Luoyang Peony - A beautiful and fragrant flower that is the city’s signature product.
  2. Luoyang Brush Calligraphy - Traditional Chinese calligraphy brushes that have been made in Luoyang for centuries.
  3. Luoyang Bronze - Bronze products that have been produced in Luoyang since ancient times, including pots, vases, and other decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Luoyang

Luoyang is a city with a rich cultural heritage that dates back thousands of years. The city’s inhabitants are proud of their cultural legacy, which is evident in local customs, manners, and traditions. Visitors to Luoyang are encouraged to be respectful of local customs and to observe local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Luoyang

The best time to visit Luoyang is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The peak tourist season is in April and May, when the city’s famous peonies are in bloom. However, visitors can enjoy the city’s attractions and activities throughout the year, except for the hot and humid summer months and the cold winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luoyang

Do:

  1. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine, including Luoyang Water Banquet and Shaolong Noodles.
  3. Visit the Longmen Grottoes and other historical sites.
  4. Explore the scenic natural wonders of Luoyang, such as Baiyun Mountain and Yuntai Mountain.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs or culture.
  2. Speak loudly or behave disorderly in public.
  3. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  4. Damage or deface historical sites, man-made or natural.

Safety and Health

Safety in Luoyang

Luoyang is generally a safe city, with low crime rates compared to many other cities in China. Visitors are advised to be aware of pickpocketing and to keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Luoyang

Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication with them and to take basic precautions against illnesses. It is also important to drink only bottled water or boiled and cooled tap water to avoid the risk of stomach problems.

Getting to Luoyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Luoyang

The nearest international airport to Luoyang is Xinzheng International Airport in Zhengzhou, approximately two hours away by car or train. Luoyang Airport is a small airport that offers limited flights to and from other cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Luoyang

Luoyang has two major bus stations, Luoyang Long-distance Bus Station and Luoyang Passenger Transport Terminal. Visitors can take buses to many cities in China from these stations.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Luoyang by train from major cities in China. Luoyang Railway Station is located in the city center and serves several high-speed railway lines.

How to Get Around in Luoyang

Buses and taxis are the main modes of transportation available in Luoyang. Visitors can also rent bicycles or join organized tours for certain activities, such as hiking or visiting historical sites.

Where to Go Next from Luoyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Shaolin Temple - A famous Buddhist temple and the birthplace of Shaolin Kung Fu, located in Dengfeng, approximately one hour from Luoyang by car or bus.
  2. Zhengzhou - The capital of Henan province and a bustling modern city with several top attractions, including the Yellow River Scenic Area and the Henan Museum, located approximately two hours from Luoyang by train or bus.
  3. Xian - The ancient capital of several Chinese dynasties and home to the terracotta army, located approximately four hours from Luoyang by high-speed train or car.

Conclusion

Luoyang is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and plenty to offer visitors from historical sites to natural wonders to delicious local cuisine. Whether you are interested in exploring ancient ruins, going on outdoor adventures, or just trying tasty new foods, Luoyang has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and experience one of the most fascinating and unique destinations in China.
