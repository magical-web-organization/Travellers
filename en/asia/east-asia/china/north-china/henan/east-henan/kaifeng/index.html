Introduction to Kaifeng

Kaifeng, located in East Henan, China, is a city rich in history and culture. As the capital of several dynasties and kingdoms throughout Chinese history, it holds a significant place in the country’s past. The city is home to numerous museums, historical landmarks, and cultural attractions that showcase its diverse heritage.

Apart from its cultural significance, Kaifeng boasts of beautiful natural landscapes, including the Yellow River and Henan’s lush green forests. The city also has an exciting food and drink scene, and locals are known for their warmth and hospitality, making Kaifeng a popular tourist destination in China.

List of Places to Visit in Kaifeng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kaifeng’s rich history has left its mark on the city, with numerous museums and historical landmarks showcasing the different periods the city has gone through. You’ll want to visit:

  • Henan Museum: Home to a vast collection of historical and cultural artifacts from the province and China as a whole.
  • Iron Pagoda: A beautiful building from the Song Dynasty, made of cast-iron and still standing strong after 900 years.
  • Bianjing Park: The former site of the city’s imperial palace during the Northern Song Dynasty, and now a park with a lake and beautiful flower gardens.
  • Kaifeng City Wall: Dating back to the Sui Dynasty, this is one of the oldest and most well-preserved city walls in China.
  • Kaifeng Jews Museum: A fascinating museum showcasing the history of the Jewish community in Kaifeng that can be traced back over a thousand years.

Natural Attractions of Kaifeng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will not be disappointed by the beautiful natural landscapes in and around Kaifeng. Whether you enjoy hiking, biking, or simply taking in beautiful views, here are some places you should visit:

  • Yellow River Scenic Zone: A picturesque spot to watch the sunset and take a boat tour on the Yellow River.
  • Longting Park: A large park with a lake in the middle and picturesque bridges, ideal for a relaxing picnic.
  • Dragon Pavilion Park: Built-in 978 during the Northern Song Dynasty, it offers stunning views of the city from the top.
  • Xiangguo Monastery: Originally built-in 555 during the Northern Qi Dynasty, it has beautiful scenes and offers an excellent space for Buddhist learning.

You can rent bikes to explore the city and its surroundings — specifically the city’s ancient walls or the Banqiao Amusement Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaifeng

If you’re an adventure enthusiast, Kaifeng has many excellent cycling and hiking trails, such as the 180km-long West Taihang Mountains Scenic Area with Mt. Song as its center.

Best Things to Do in Kaifeng: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the museums and historical sites, here are some other must-try activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Xiangfu Temple Fair: This traditional festival held during the Chinese New Year offers local food, lantern decorations, and traditional performances.
  • Qingming Riverside Landscape Garden: A cultural landscape park, where visitors can dress in traditional clothes, take part in traditional activities, and watch performances.
  • Chinese paper cutting: Join a workshop and learn how to create impressive intricate designs from paper.
  • Farming experience: The agricultural tourism sector is becoming increasingly popular, and you can join a farm tour to learn how to grow crops.
  • Local food tours: Sample Kaifeng’s famous dishes during a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kaifeng

Here is a week-long itinerary for Kaifeng that includes some of the places and activities mentioned above:

  • Day 1: Visit Henan Museum and the Iron Pagoda.
  • Day 2: Explore the Banqiao Amusement Park and go cycling along the ancient city walls.
  • Day 3: Take a boat tour on the Yellow River and visit the Bianjing Park.
  • Day 4: Visit the Kaifeng City Wall and experience paper cutting.
  • Day 5: Enjoy some downtime at Longting Park and learn about Buddhist practices at Xiangguo Monastery.
  • Day 6: Visit the Kaifeng Jews Museum and attend a traditional Chinese opera performance.
  • Day 7: Take a farm tour, learn how to cook local dishes, and bid farewell to Kaifeng.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaifeng

Must-Try Dishes

The Kaifeng cuisine is world-renowned, and here are some must-try dishes to add to your culinary journey:

  • Kaifeng Steamed Buns: A delicious snack made from finely cooked dough stuffed with pork, soup, and spices.
  • Kaifeng Roast Duck: Similar to Peking Duck but served with thin crepes, green onions, and hoisin sauce.
  • Braised Pork with brown sauce: Classic home-cooked dish popular for its sweet and salty taste.
  • Jinhu Fish Soup: A traditional soup made up of catfish and pepper and various Chinese herbs.
  • Fried Scallion Pancake: A pancake fried with chopped scallions, sesame seeds, and served hot.

Local Beverages

Kaifeng locals enjoy tea and wine as the primary beverages. You can enjoy picking tea leaves and sipping them during a tea workshop at Osmanthus Lake. The city also has wineries, breweries, and night markets where you can sample local drinks.

Where to Stay in Kaifeng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kaifeng has several accommodation options ranging from hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to luxury resorts. The most popular areas to stay include the old city, Songcheng Road, and Gulou district. Here are some recommendations:

  • Kaifeng Yuxiangren Guesthouse: Located in the heart of the old city and offers traditional Chinese-style rooms.
  • Kaifeng Jinyuan Yujing Hotel: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and located near the Yellow River Scenic Area.
  • Ur Ju House: This hostel is the ideal place to meet fellow travelers while staying in a traditional Chinese courtyard.

What to Buy in Kaifeng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When touring Kaifeng, you can buy souvenirs and local products such as:

  • Kaifeng Embroidery: Intricately designed silk textile woven into flower patterns with unique styles.
  • Kites: Colorful kites used during festivals and celebrations in Kaifeng.
  • Handicrafts: Traditional paper cutting, porcelain, jade, and wooden handicrafts are readily available in the city’s markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Kaifeng

Taoism, Buddhism, and Confucianism are the most commonly practiced religions in Kaifeng, contributing to the city’s culture and customs. Visitors should respect the tradition by wearing appropriate clothing while visiting religious landmarks and events. Respect to elders and traditions is also much appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Kaifeng

The best time to visit Kaifeng is during April and May in spring and September through October in autumn. The weather is generally moderate, and one gets to experience the city during its festive seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaifeng

Do:

  • Learn some basic Chinese phrases for communication with the locals.
  • Visit the city’s museums and historical landmarks and experience the culture and history of Kaifeng.
  • Try the famous Kaifeng cuisine.
  • Engage the locals in conversation and learn more about their traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious monuments, traditions, and customs.
  • Make sudden and loud noises around religious sites.
  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaifeng

Kaifeng is relatively safe, but it’s always best to take caution when traveling in unfamiliar destinations. Keep your belongings safe and secure when in crowded areas, and beware of pickpockets while visiting.

Health in Kaifeng

Tap water in Kaifeng is unsafe for drinking, and visitors should only consume boiled or bottled water. Visitors should also keep hydrated and wear sunscreen and hat during hot and sunny days to prevent sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Kaifeng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaifeng

Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport is the nearest airport to Kaifeng, located 72 kilometers away. It’s a busy airport with several carriers operating both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kaifeng

Kaifeng Bus Station operates buses from major Chinese cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Xi’an, among others. The station also has several buses that connect visitors to nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Kaifeng Railway Station, which connects to major national cities like Beijing and Shanghai. Rideshare, taxis, and private cars can be used as a means of transport within the city.

How to Get Around in Kaifeng

Kaifeng has a well-connected transportation network, with buses, trains, and taxis operating throughout the city. Visitors can rent bicycles and tour the city, enjoy a ride around the ancient city walls, or hire a taxi for convenience. There’s also the town’s public bus system that travellers can use.

Where to Go Next from Kaifeng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kaifeng is located at the heart of Henan, with many attractions within a stone throw away. You can take an excursion to other destinations such as:

  • Zhengzhou: Henan’s capital city is home to the famed Shaolin Temple and the Henan Museum.
  • Luoyang: Another ancient city in Henan with the UNESCO-listed Longmen Grottoes.
  • Anyang: Home to the Oracle Bone Inscriptions and other historical sites.

Conclusion

Kaifeng is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Chinese culture, history, and natural landscapes. The city offers an endless array of attractions, activities, and dining options for visitors to explore. Whether you’re interested in museums, festivals, or outdoor adventures, Kaifeng has something for everyone. Plan your trip and get ready for a fantastic experience.
