Introduction to Tianjin

Tianjin is a bustling metropolitan city located in Northern China, known for its rich history and vibrant culture. As one of the four municipalities directly under the Central Government of China, it serves as a vital transport hub, connecting Beijing and other parts of China to the ports of the coast of China. It boasts of several attractions ranging from cultural heritage sites to natural scenery and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Tianjin

Tianjin has several places to visit, from its cultural heritage to its natural scenery. Below are some of the top places worth exploring in this metropolitan city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tianjin Museum: This museum exhibits the rich history, culture, and development of Tianjin. Its exhibition halls showcase artifacts that date back to China’s prehistoric times, as well as more contemporary displays of the region’s development over the years.
  • Ancient Culture Street: It is the most popular and culturally rich pedestrian street lined with traditional architecture, numerous shops selling local products, and handcrafts from the region, making it a favorite destination for tourists.
  • Porcelain House: An art gallery and historical mansion made entirely of broken pieces of ceramics, making it a unique attraction, both for its architecture and the exhibitions of art that it holds inside.
  • Haihe Cultural Square: This sprawling plaza showcases magnificent sculptures, fountains and hosts various cultural events, and is a must-visit spot for tourists seeking insights into the local culture.

Natural Attractions of Tianjin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Panshan Mountain: Located in Jixian County, it offers an excellent getaway from the hustle and bustle of the city life, with its natural scenery and breathtaking views from the summit.
  • Binhai Aircraft Carrier Theme Park: Tianjin is home to China’s first aircraft carrier, turned into a theme park featuring a museum, a science park, and other entertaining activities for visitors.
  • Tianjin Water Park: It is a scenic park encompassing expansive lakes, lush greenery, and over 1200 species of aquatic plant life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tianjin

Tianjin offers several hiking and biking routes, varying from leisurely countryside to steep mountains, with spectacular views of the city’s natural beauty. Below are some of the popular ones:

  • Jixian Valley Scenic Area: This valley boasts stunning waterfalls, lush greenery, and ancient temples hidden within its midst. One can go on easy hikes and enjoy the natural scenery and the tranquility of the place.
  • Yangliuqing Ancient Town: Located in the northwestern part of Tianjin, Yangliuqing Ancient Town features winding alleys with traditional houses and the old city walls stretching miles away. One can rent bikes and cycles through the town’s alleys and explore its cultural heritage and many sights.

Best Things to Do in Tianjin: Activities and Tours

Tianjin offers numerous activities and tours that cater to various interests. Below are some of the best things to do in Tianjin.

  • Take a Food Tour: Tianjin offers a great culinary experience for foodies. Go on a food tour and try its famous delicacies, such as Goubuli Steamed Buns, Erduoyan Fried Rice Cake, and Mahua Pastry.
  • Visit the Tianjin Eye Ferris Wheel: It is the world’s tallest Ferris wheel at 120 meters high and offers spectacular views of the city skyline.
  • Watch a Chinese Opera: Traditional Chinese opera is a time-honored art form in this region. Book a ticket to watch a performance at the Tianjin Grand Theater or the Tianjin People’s Art Theater.
  • Take a Canal Tour: As part of the Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal, experience the Tianjin section’s beauty by taking a boat tour from its docks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tianjin

Below is a suggested itinerary from the vast selection of activities and tours available in Tianjin over a week:

Day Suggested Itinerary
Day 1-2 Visit Ancient Culture Street, Porcelain House, and take a canal tour.
Day 3-4 Explore the Panshan Mountain and Binhai Aircraft Carrier Theme Park.
Day 5 Visit the Tianjin Eye Ferris Wheel and take a Chinese Opera show

Food and Drink Scene of Tianjin

Tianjin has a rich culinary tradition, and its local cuisine is famous for its bold flavors and unique taste. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goubuli Steamed Buns: This steamed bun delicacy is one of Tianjin’s signature dishes, typically stuffed with minced meat, mushroom, and other fillings.
  • Erduoyan Fried Rice Cake: A savory cake stuffed with minced meat and other condiments, served with sweet-sour sauce.
  • Mahua Pastry: Flaky deep-fried strips of dough, coated with sesame seeds and sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Tianjin Artisanal Beer: Brewed locally in the city, the artisanal beer has a unique taste and is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Tianjin Plum Juice Made from fresh plums and water, it has a crisp, refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Tianjin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tianjin offers a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Below are some of the top recommendations:

  • Conrad Tianjin: Located in the heart of the city, this luxurious five-star hotel offers excellent amenities and sprawling views of the city’s skyline.
  • The First Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in Nankai District, with clean rooms and friendly staff that offer excellent recommendations for nearby attractions.
  • Nikko Tianjin: A mid-range hotel in the Hexi District, offering comfortable rooms and wellness facilities, such as a sauna and swimming pool.

What to Buy in Tianjin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tianjin is known for its handicrafts and local products. Below are some of the items to consider buying and bringing home as souvenirs.

  • Clay Figurines: Made of moist clay and then baked, handcrafted clay figurines are the town’s signature souvenir and are available in various sizes and designs.
  • Yangliuqing New Year Paintings: Traditional Chinese New Year paintings are available in this region’s shops and make for beautiful decorations or souvenirs.
  • Niren Suanzi: Another traditional Tianjin specialty, Niren Suanzi, is jam-packed with a walnut kernel and has a sweet, nutty taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Tianjin

Tianjin’s local culture and customs are unique and steeped in history. Visitors should be aware of the following cultural customs:

  • Respect Personal Space: It is customary in Tianjin to respect personal space and avoid standing too close to someone else.
  • Gift-Giving: Gift-giving is a common practice in Tianjin when visiting someone’s home or when invited for dinner.
  • Tea Culture: Tea-serving is an integral part of the Tianjin culture, and guests are usually served tea when visiting someone’s home or meeting someone.

Best Time to Visit Tianjin

The best time to visit Tianjin is during Autumn and Spring, from March to May and September to November, respectively, when the temperatures are mild and pleasant. The city can be visited in summer, but it can be quite hot and humid, and the winter weather can be quite chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tianjin

Visitors to Tianjin should consider the following Do’s and Don’ts to ensure they have a pleasant experience:

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Tianjin is famous for its unique culinary heritage, so make sure to try its signature dishes.
  • Respect the Culture: Observe local customs and be respectful when visiting temples or other attractions.
  • Carry Cash: Although credit cards are widely accepted, having cash is essential in case of emergencies.

Don’t:

  • Use Chopsticks Inappropriately: Do not stick chopsticks upright in a bowl or cross them, as this is considered bad luck in Chinese culture.
  • Raise Your Voice in Public: Speaking loudly or shouting in public is considered rude in Tianjin’s culture.
  • Wear Shoes Indoors: When entering someone’s home, it’s customary to remove your shoes before entering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tianjin

Tianjin is a relatively safe city, and visitors need not worry about their safety. However, take note of the following safety tips:

  • Be cautious of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas or public transportation.
  • Take care when crossing the roads, as traffic moves fast and sometimes disregards the traffic lights.

Health in Tianjin

The healthcare system in Tianjin is generally good, with several international-standard hospitals and medical facilities. Medical insurance is highly recommended, and visitors should always carry their travel insurance policy. Travelers should also:

  • Be cautious when dining out, especially with street food, as it may carry a higher risk of food-borne illnesses.
  • Stay hydrated as dehydration can be common during summer months.

Getting to Tianjin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tianjin is well connected with several transportation options available to visitors. Below are some of the top means of transport to get to and around Tianjin.

Airports Near Tianjin

  • Tianjin Binhai International Airport: Located approximately 13 km from the city center, it connects a few international destinations and some domestic airports in mainland China.

Bus Stations Near Tianjin

  • Tianjin International Cruise Home Port: Located in Tianjin, it operates a regular bus service with most other cities in China.

Other Options

  • Train: Trains from various cities regularly operate to Tianjin.
  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available in some parts of the city, but be aware of traffic conditions and the lack of parking spaces.

How to Get Around in Tianjin

Getting around in Tianjin is convenient and easy, with several transport means such as taxis, subways, and buses. The city is also bike-friendly, with bike lanes available in most main roads.

Taxi

Taxis are available and are metered. The starting rate is CNY10, with rates increasing depending on the distance covered.

Subway

Tianjin has a modern subway system with several lines operating throughout the city. The rates are dependent on the distance covered, and tickets can be purchased at the kiosks found in the subway stations.

Bus

Tianjin also has an extensive bus network, with several routes operating throughout the city. The fares are usually CNY2-3, making it an affordable means of transport in the city.

Bike

Bikes can be rented via mobile applications such as Mobike and Ofo, which operate bike-rental services throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Tianjin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tianjin is located near several other towns and cities, offering an excellent opportunity to explore wider Northern China.

  • Beijing: The nation’s capital and located approximately 120 km from Tianjin, is a popular excursion from the city, and offers a vast array of attractions, including the Great Wall of China and the Forbidden City.
  • Peking Man Site at Zhoukoudian: A historical site located approximately 50km from the city, it offers a glimpse of ancient human history and is a designated UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Binhai New Area: This area boasts unique attractions such as a giant amusement park and the largest underwater observatory in Asia, offering unforgettable experiences for visitors.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Tianjin is an impressive destination that offers something for all types of visitors. With its rich cultural heritage, unique gastronomy, natural scenery, and recreational activities, spending time in Tianjin is an experience every traveler would cherish. To have a hassle-free travel experience, make sure to plan your trip in advance and observe the local customs and traditions.
