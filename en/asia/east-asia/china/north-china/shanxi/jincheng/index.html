Introduction to Jincheng

Jincheng is a prefecture-level city in the central part of Shanxi province, North China. The city has a long history and rich culture, offering visitors an array of experiences to enjoy. Jincheng is famous for its coal industry, but it also boasts spectacular natural landscapes, unique local customs, and delicious food.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jincheng has a rich history and culture, and there is no better way to discover its ancient past than visiting the many museums and historical places in the city. The following are some of the best places to learn about the area’s fascinating history and culture:

  • Wang Family Courtyard Museum: The Wang Family Courtyard is a large traditional Chinese compound located in Lingshi County, Jincheng. The museum showcases various exhibits, including cultural relics, calligraphy, and paintings.
  • Tianzhu Mountain Forest Park: The park features hiking trails, scenic overlooks, and a temple complex that dates back to the Tang Dynasty.
  • Qiao Family Compound: The Qiao Family Compound is a well-preserved traditional Chinese residential complex in Pingyao County, Jincheng. It is an excellent example of Chinese folk residential architecture.
  • Zuoquan Scenic Area: Zuoquan is a scenic area located in the northeast of Jincheng. It boasts stunning natural landscapes, including mountains, forests, and rivers. The area is also home to a variety of historical and cultural attractions, such as the Guanyin Cave, the Liancheng Temple, and the Sanguan Palace.

Natural Attractions of Jincheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jincheng has plenty of breathtaking natural landscapes, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city’s sparkling rivers, magnificent mountains, and tranquil lakes provide breathtaking scenery for visitors. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Jincheng:

  • Liuqing River Scenic Area: The Liuqing River Scenic Area lies by the southeastern suburb of Jincheng. The area boasts impressive views of mountains, forests, and rivers that are famous for their beauty.
  • Tai Chi Lake: Tai Chi Lake is a stunning mountain lake located in the Jincheng area. The lake is surrounded by dense forests and grasslands, making it an ideal spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Yao Temple Scenic Area: The Yao Temple Scenic Area, located in Qinyuan County, boasts fascinating cultural relics, beautiful natural scenery, and a serene environment.
  • Guandi Mountain National Forest Park: Guandi Mountain National Forest Park is the largest natural park in Jincheng. It has rich wildlife, beautiful scenery, and a rich cultural history. Be sure to check out the Guandi Mountain Temple, which dates back to the Northern Wei Dynasty (386–534 AD), and the Thousand Buddha Cave, decorated with thousands of Buddha statues.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jincheng

Jincheng is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, as there are plenty of hiking and biking routes available throughout the city. Here are some great hiking/biking routes in Jincheng:

  • Tai Chi Lake Trail: The Tai Chi Lake Trail offers a beautiful and relatively easy hike that takes you through lush forests and offers stunning views of Tai Chi Lake.
  • Guandi Mountain Trail: The Guandi Mountain Trail offers a challenging hike to the top of Guandi Mountain, where hikers can enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Zuoquan Scenic Area Trail: The Zuoquan Scenic Area Trail offers a great opportunity to explore the beautiful natural scenery of the region. The trail offers several viewpoints with stunning views of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Jincheng: Activities and Tours

Jincheng has much to offer to visitors, with many activities and tours to take part in:

  • Coal Mine Tour: Jincheng is known for its rich coal industry, and visitors can take a tour of one of the coal mines to learn about the process and history of mining in the area.
  • Painting Class: Visitors can attend a Chinese painting class in one of the local studios or art schools to learn about traditional Chinese painting techniques.
  • Calligraphy Class: Visitors can also learn calligraphy from a local master or teacher and experience one of China’s most popular traditional arts.
  • Tai Chi Class: Visitors can attend a Tai Chi class with a local teacher and learn about this ancient martial art.

Food and Drink Scene of Jincheng

Jincheng’s food and drink scene is a mix of local flavors and traditional Chinese cuisine. Here are some of the dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Sanzi: A dish made from fried dough that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Often served as a snack.
  • Meat Roujiamo: Jincheng’s version of the meat roujiamo sandwich is a must-try for anyone visiting the city.
  • Liuqing River Fish Stew: A famous local dish made with fish from the Liuqing River, cooked in a clay pot with vegetables, tofu, and noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Fenjiu: Fenjiu is a type of Baijiu, a traditional Chinese distilled spirit made from grain. Fenjiu has a unique taste and is a favorite among locals.
  • Chrysanthemum tea: Chrysanthemum tea is a popular beverage in Jincheng, known for its light, refreshing taste and health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Jincheng

Jincheng is a cultural hub that offers visitors an opportunity to learn about Chinese traditions, customs, and culture. Here are some local customs to keep in mind while visiting Jincheng:

  • Respect Elders: Chinese people value respecting elders, so be sure to show proper respect to seniors during your stay in Jincheng.
  • Dining Customs: When dining in Jincheng, it is customary to serve food to others, use chopsticks instead of forks, and avoid leaving chopsticks sticking upright in a bowl of rice.
  • Tea Culture: Drinking tea is a traditional Chinese pastime, and Jincheng is home to many tea houses and tea cultures.

Best Time to Visit Jincheng

The best time to visit Jincheng is from March to November when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its most stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jincheng

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drink scene
  • Visit the many museums and historical places in the city

Don’t:

  • Do not take pictures of people without their permission
  • Do not leave chopsticks sticking upright in a bowl of rice or food

Safety and Health

Safety in Jincheng

Jincheng is a safe city for travelers, but it is always best to take the necessary precautions:

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings
  • Keep valuables like money, passports and credit cards in a safe place
  • Only use reputable taxis or follow the transportation guide below.

Health in Jincheng

Jincheng’s tap water is not safe to drink, so be sure to only consume bottled water during your stay.

Getting to Jincheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jincheng

Taiyuan Wusu International Airport is the closest airport to Jincheng, located approximately 200km from the city. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Jincheng.

Bus Stations Near Jincheng

Jincheng Bus Station is the main bus station in the city, serving buses to destinations throughout the province and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Jincheng. The city has multiple train stations, including Jincheng Railway Station, Jincheng South Railway Station, and Jincheng North Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Jincheng

The best way to get around Jincheng is by taxi or public transportation. Visitors can also rent a bike or e-bike to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Jincheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jincheng is centrally located in Shanxi province, making it an ideal starting point for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Pingyao Ancient City: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Pingyao Ancient City is a well-preserved medieval city located 90km south of Jincheng.
  • Mount Wutai: A Buddhist mountain located 200km south of Jincheng, Mount Wutai is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning temples and natural scenery.
  • Yungang Grottoes: Located in Datong, a city 250km north of Jincheng, the Yungang Grottoes are a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for their amazing Buddhist cave art.
