Your Comprehensive Guide to Hailar, Inner Mongolia

Nestled in Eastern Inner Mongolia, Hailar is an ethereal destination that offers an enchanting mix of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and exotic culture. Dubbed as the “Pearl of the Grassland,” Hailar is an ideal place for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and culture enthusiasts.

In this guide, we’ll give you a comprehensive overview of Hailar, including the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Hailar

Hailar and its surrounding areas are filled with breathtaking sights and attractions. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hulunbuir Museum: Located in the Hulunbuir Prairie City, this museum showcases the natural history and culture of Hulunbuir.
  • Hulun Lake Nature Reserve: Hulun Lake is the largest lake in northern China and a natural heritage site. The reserve is known for its stunning views and unique wildlife.
  • Inner Mongolia Tiger Park: This park is a conservation area for endangered tigers and other wildlife species. Visitors can watch live shows featuring tigers and witness feeding sessions.
  • Manzhouli Matryoshka Dolls Square: This is an eccentric tourist spot where visitors can see and purchase Russian souvenirs and experience Russian culture.

Natural Attractions of Hailar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hulunbuir Grasslands: The vast grasslands of Hulunbuir are some of the most beautiful in China. Here, you can enjoy horseback riding, camping, and hot air balloon rides.
  • Genhe Wetlands: Genhe Wetlands is a natural reserve that boasts more than 1,000 lakes, rivers, and marshlands. Visitors can canoe, fish, and swim in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Ergun Wetlands: Ergun Wetlands is located at the confluence of two major rivers, the Ergun River and the Hailar River. Visitors can explore the wetlands on foot or on horseback.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hailar

Hailar is a hub for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Below are some recommended routes:

  • Northeast Hulunbuir Prairie Trail: This is a scenic trail that takes visitors through the Hulunbuir Prairie, offering breathtaking views of the local wildlife and flora.
  • Hulunbuir City to Shiwei Town Trail: This is a challenging trail that follows the Ergun River, offering stunning views of the Ergun Wetlands.
  • Hulunbuir to Manzhouli Trail: This is an easy trail that passes through picturesque areas, including the Manzhouli Grassland and the Genhe Wetland Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Hailar: Activities and Tours

  • Hulunbuir Grassland Tours: These tours offer visitors a chance to camp, watch ethnic Mongolians’ traditional performances, and learn about the local culture and history.
  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Hailar, and visitors can explore the stunning scenery of the Hulunbuir grasslands on horseback.
  • Skiing: Hailar has a ski resort where visitors can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Hailar

No trip to Hailar is complete without trying the local dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try foods and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mongolian Barbecue: A local specialty, Mongolian barbecue is a grilled meat dish served with vegetables and sauces.
  • Boiled Fish: A regional dish, boiled fish is cooked in a spicy broth and served with vegetables and noodles.
  • Airag: This is a traditional Mongolian beverage made from fermented horse milk.

Local Beverages

  • Hulunbuir Beer: This is a local beer brand produced in Hulunbuir. It’s a refreshing drink perfect for hot summer days.
  • Nen River Wine: Nen River Wine is made from the wild grapes that grow along the banks of the Nen River. The wine has a unique taste and aroma.

Where to Stay in Hailar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Hailar, ranging from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hulunbuir Prairie Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Hulunbuir Prairie and offers stunning views of the grassland. It’s a perfect base for exploring the surrounding areas.
  • JinJiang Inn Select Hailar Railway Station: This is a budget hotel located near the Hailar Railway Station. It’s convenient for visitors who want to explore Hailar City.
  • Yijia Shangwu Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent service at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Hailar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hailar is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some things to buy:

  • Mongolian Traditional Clothes: You can buy traditional Mongolian clothes such as the Deel, a long traditional coat, and hats for souvenirs.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Handmade jewelry such as bracelets, necklaces, and rings made of agate, jade, and other precious stones are popular souvenirs in Hailar.
  • Wild Fungus: Hulunbuir is famous for its wild fungus, including morels, truffles, and other unique varieties. Visitors can buy these as souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Hailar

Hailar is an ethnic Mongolian city that still maintains its traditional customs and culture. Here are some tips for visitors:

  • Respect Local Customs: When visiting local people or doing business with them, it’s important to follow their ways and customs.
  • Dress Appropriately: When visiting temples and mosques, dress modestly in long pants and shirts with sleeves.
  • Learn Some Mongolian Phrases: Mongolian is the official language of Hulunbuir, and the locals mainly speak Mongolian and Chinese. Learning some basic phrases can deepen cultural exchanges and enrich your travel experiences.

Best Time to Visit Hailar

The best time to visit Hailar is from June to September when the weather is mild and comfortable. During this period, the Hulunbuir Grassland is lush and green, and visitors can enjoy various outdoor activities. In winter, the region is covered in snow, and Hailar Ski Resort is open for skiing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hailar

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Hailar:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions.
  • Dress Appropriately When Visiting Religious Sites.
  • Try the Local Dishes and Beverages.
  • Learn Some Basic Mongolian Phrases.

Don’t:

  • Take Photos Without Permission.
  • Disturb the Local Wildlife, Especially Endangered Species.
  • Litter, Smoke or Spit on the Streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hailar

Hailar is a relatively safe city for visitors. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions such as not carrying large sums of money or valuables and be vigilant when traveling at night.

Health in Hailar

The tap water in Hailar may not be safe for drinking. Therefore, it’s advisable to stick to bottled water. Visitors should also ensure they are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Hailar.

Getting to Hailar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider when traveling to Hailar:

Airports Near Hailar

Hailar Dongshan International Airport is the main airport serving the city. It has connections to major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

Bus Stations Near Hailar

The bus is the most common form of public transportation in Hailar. There are several bus stations in the city, including the Hailar Bus Station, the Hulunbuir Tourism Bus Station, and the Genhe Bus Station.

Other Options

  • Trains: Hailar has a railway station that connects to major cities in China, including Harbin, Beijing, and Shanghai.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Hailar but it’s necessary to negotiate a fair price before the trip.

How to Get Around in Hailar

Here are some ways to get around Hailar:

  • Taxi: Taxis are the most convenient way to get around the city.
  • Bicycle Rental: Many hotels and shops offer bicycle rentals for visitors who want to explore the city on their own.
  • Public Bus: Public buses crisscross the city and are an affordable way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Hailar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Hailar, consider visiting neighboring destinations such as Manzhouli, the Hulunbuir Prairie City, and Arxan.

Conclusion

Hailar is a unique and captivating destination that offers an enchanting blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural treasures. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Hailar!
