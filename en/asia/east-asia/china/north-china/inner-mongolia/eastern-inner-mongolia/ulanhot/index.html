Introduction to Ulanhot

Located in eastern Inner Mongolia, Ulanhot is a fascinating destination for travelers who are interested in learning about local cultures, exploring natural landscapes, and trying authentic Mongolian food. The city, which was established in the late 17th century, is the capital of the Hinggan League and home to a mix of ethnic groups including Mongols, Han Chinese, and Manchus. In this travel guide, we’ll take a look at the top things to do and see in Ulanhot, as well as offer tips on where to stay, how to get around, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Ulanhot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ulanhot Museum: This museum offers visitors a chance to learn about the history and culture of the Hinggan League. Some of the exhibits include traditional Mongolian costumes, ancient artifacts, and cultural relics.

  • XilinGol Grassland: This area is located to the west of Ulanhot and offers visitors a chance to see traditional Mongolian yurts, watch horse races, and try authentic Mongolian food.

  • Hulunbuir Grassland: Located to the northeast of Ulanhot, this grassland is the largest in China and offers stunning views of rolling hills, vast prairies, and grazing livestock. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore on their own.

  • Larix Forest: This forest is located to the south of Ulanhot and offers a chance to see vibrant fall foliage and go hiking along scenic trails.

  • Hunshandak Desert: This desert is located to the north of Ulanhot and is a popular destination for camel rides, sandboarding, and stargazing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ulanhot

  • Hulunbuir Grassland Trail: This scenic trail offers visitors a chance to hike through rolling hills, grassy plains, and lush forests. Highlights include sweeping views of the Hulunbuir Grassland and a chance to see wild horses and other local wildlife.

  • Larix Forest Trail: This trail offers a chance to hike through a serene forest filled with larch trees that turn golden in the fall. Visitors can also spot local birds and wildlife.

  • Hunshandak Desert Trail: This trail offers visitors a chance to explore the dramatic sand dunes of the Hunshandak Desert and discover hidden oases and nomadic camps.

Best Things to Do in Ulanhot: Activities and Tours

  • Horse trekking: This activity is a classic Mongolian experience and offers a chance to explore the stunning hilly landscapes of the grasslands.

  • Camel rides: A perfect activity for families, a camel ride is a great way to take in the beauty of the Hunshandak Desert.

  • Star-gazing: The clear skies above Ulanhot are perfect for stargazing, and visitors can take a tour to learn about the constellations and the stories behind them.

  • Cultural shows: An opportunity to connect with Mongolian culture, cultural shows are an absolute must-do in Ulanhot. There are several shows that showcase traditional music, dance, and storytelling.

Food and Drink Scene of Ulanhot

Must-Try Dishes

  • Baijiu: A staple Mongolian drink, Baijiu is a strong liquor that is made from sorghum and wheat.

  • Meat dishes: Mongolians have a love for meat, and the cuisine is dominated by pork, beef, mutton, and horse meat dishes.

  • Khuushuur: This deep-fried meat pie is a classic Mongolian snack that is perfect for on-the-go.

  • Airag: A refreshing Mongolian drink made from fermented mare’s milk.

Local Beverages

  • Milk tea: A creamy and sweet tea that is a staple in Mongolian households.

  • Sour milk: A sour and tangy drink that is often mixed with flour to create a refreshing summer drink.

What to Buy in Ulanhot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cashmere products: Inner Mongolia is famous for its high-quality cashmere, and visitors can purchase scarves, gloves, and sweaters made from the soft wool.

  • Mongolian boots: Leather boots with intricate stitching are popular souvenirs to take home.

  • Mongolian artwork: Visitors can find unique and beautiful pieces of Mongolian art, including paintings, sculptures, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Ulanhot

The people of Ulanhot have strong ties to Mongolian culture and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and avoid behaviors that may be seen as disrespectful. Mongolians have a strong sense of hospitality, and guests are often greeted warmly and made to feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Ulanhot

The best time to visit Ulanhot is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Winter months (November-March) can be extremely cold and snowy, and may not be an ideal time to visit for some travelers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ulanhot

Do:

  • Respect local customs: Be respectful of local traditions and customs.

  • Experience local culture: Take part in cultural shows and try traditional Mongolian food.

  • Pack for the weather: Make sure to bring warm clothing during the winter months.

Don’t:

  • Waste food: Mongolians view food as a precious commodity and wasting food is considered disrespectful.

  • Litter: Keep the natural landscapes of Ulanhot clean by avoiding littering.

  • Be rude: Avoid being rude or disrespectful to locals or guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ulanhot

Ulanhot is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be cautious when traveling alone at night and avoid flashing valuables or carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Ulanhot

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude in Ulanhot and may experience symptoms such as shortness of breath or headaches. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect against exposure to the sun.

Getting to Ulanhot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ulanhot

The closest airport to Ulanhot is Hailar Dongshan Airport, which offers flights to several Chinese cities including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

Bus Stations Near Ulanhot

The Ulanhot Bus Station offers daily services to many cities in Inner Mongolia and beyond.

Other Options

Taxi services are available in Ulanhot, and visitors can also rent a car or hire a driver to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Ulanhot

Travelers can get around Ulanhot by taxi or by renting a car or bicycle. Public transportation options are limited.

Where to Go Next from Ulanhot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hailar: A nearby city that is known for its stunning natural landscapes and unique architecture.

  • Manzhouli: A border city that offers a chance to see Mongolian and Russian cultures collide.

  • Harbin: The capital of Heilongjiang Province and a popular destination for winter sports and the Harbin International Ice and Snow Festival.

Conclusion

Ulanhot is a destination that offers a chance to explore the stunning natural landscapes of Inner Mongolia, learn about local cultures and traditions, and try authentic Mongolian food. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can plan the perfect trip to this unique and fascinating destination.
