Introduction to Manzhouli

Located in eastern Inner Mongolia, Manzhouli is a captivating city that blends Russian and Chinese cultures. It is known for its unique attractions, including a Russian-style train station, a “Moscow Street” with iconic onion domes, and an ice and snow world that showcases ice sculptures from around the world. Manzhouli is also near the world-renowned Hulunbuir Prairie, one of China’s largest prairies.

List of Places to Visit in Manzhouli

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sino-Russian Western Borders Museum - a museum that showcases the history and culture of the Sino-Russian border region.
  • Manzhouli National Gate Scenic Area - a unique attraction that features Russian-style architecture and a replica of George Washington’s home in Virginia.
  • Big Buddha Temple - a temple complex that showcases Buddhist art and architecture.
  • Zhalainuoer Mosque - a prominent Islamic mosque that features Chinese characteristics in its architecture.

Natural Attractions of Manzhouli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hulunbuir Prairie - one of the biggest and most picturesque prairies in China.
  • Manzhouli Ski Resort - a ski resort that offers a range of slopes catering to skiers of all abilities.
  • Black and White Forest Park - a picturesque park with a unique display of black and white trees.
  • Xingkai Lake Scenic Area - a breathtaking natural attraction that features various water activities, such as boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manzhouli

  • Manzhouli National Gate Scenic Area Hiking Trail - a hiking trail that winds around the Russian-style buildings and lush landscapes in the area.
  • Hulunbuir Prairie Trek - a trek that offers stunning views of the rolling hills and endless expanse of grassland stretching as far as the eye can see.
  • Xingkai Lake Biking Route - a biking route that offers a peaceful and tranquil ride around the lake’s tranquil shores.

Best Things to Do in Manzhouli: Activities and Tours

  • Ice and Snow World - a winter paradise that enables visitors to witness stunning ice sculptures from around the world.
  • Russian-styled Souvenir Shopping - Shopping enthusiasts cannot miss the opportunity to explore the unique souvenir shops scattered around the city, selling everything from Russian chocolates, traditional Russian nesting dolls, to Chinese traditional art.
  • Horseback Riding on the Prairie - an adventurous activity that allows visitors to explore the vast Hulunbuir Prairie.
  • Trans-Siberian Railway Experience - a unique experience that enables visitors to explore the Russian railroads in Inner Mongolia.

Food and Drink Scene of Manzhouli

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nanpeng Rou - Inner Mongolian braised pork.
  • Mongolian Milk Tea - a classic beverage that soothes the soul and warms the body.
  • Shouzhua Yangrou - a dish made of gripped lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Arctic Ocean Beer - a locally brewed beer that contains distinctive flavors and qualities.

Where to Stay in Manzhouli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Harbin Manzhouli Arden Hotel - a lavish hotel located in the heart of the city that provides stunning views and memorable experiences.

What to Buy in Manzhouli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Russian Nesting Dolls - a unique souvenir that combines Russian and Chinese cultures.
  • Fur Hats and Scarfs - a perfect gift to bring home from the chilly Inner Mongolian weather.
  • Black Pottery - a traditional handicraft made by the Mongolian people which involves using black soil with unique shapes and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Manzhouli

  • The region’s unique history and cultures characterized by the influence of both Russia and China.
  • The Kazakhi people’s vibrant and rich culture.

Best Time to Visit Manzhouli

The best time to visit Manzhouli is June to September, during the region’s best weather. June to August is a perfect season to visit the Prairie sights, and September’s golden and cool season represents the best time for a scenic and peaceful experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manzhouli

Do:

  • Respect the local cultures and customs.
  • Keep yourself warm during the bitterly cold winter season.
  • Take the unique opportunity to experience the Trans-Siberian Railway routes.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect the sacred places, including temples, mosques, and other religious sites.
  • Do not pick the flowers or other natural plants in parks.
  • Do not block public spaces and roads while taking photographs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manzhouli

Manzhouli is a safe place to visit; however, the visitors should take precautionary measures in crowded places, and they must ensure to keep their belongings carefully.

Health in Manzhouli

Visitors to Manzhouli should take into account the enhanced health and hygiene regulations in place after the pandemic. They would have to ensure proper sanitization measures are in place before any interaction with locals or other visitors.

Getting to Manzhouli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manzhouli

  • Manzhouli Xijiao Airport - a domestic airport situated in the city.

Bus Stations Near Manzhouli

  • Manzhouli Coach Station - the primary station that serves various domestic routes in Inner Mongolia.

Other Options

  • Travel by train through the Trans-Siberian Railway route from Russia.
  • Take long-distance taxis, taking you from most major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Manzhouli

  • Buses are the cheapest way to get around the area.
  • Taxis and bike rentals are a more flexible option for travelers.

Where to Go Next from Manzhouli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Manzhouli is a unique and impressive travel destination that offers new experiences and cultural fusion. Visitors can enjoy scenic landscapes, adventurous activities, and explore the culture immersed in Chinese and Russian confluence. From the skiing resorts to the historic museums and beautiful prairies, this travel guide to Manzhouli provides an unforgettable and comprehensive experience for every type of traveler.
