Jining Travel Guide - Exploring Inner Mongolia’s Hidden Gem

Welcome to Jining, Inner Mongolia’s best-kept secret! This charming city may not be on every traveler’s itinerary, but it’s definitely worth a visit. Surrounded by the beautiful grasslands of Inner Mongolia, Jining boasts a fascinating history, a vibrant culture, and breathtaking natural landscapes.

In this comprehensive Jining travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best attractions, activities, hikes, food, and cultural experiences this city has to offer. Discover how to get there, where to stay, and what to do in Jining.

List of Places to Visit in Jining

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jining has a rich history that dates back to the Han Dynasty. The city’s museums and historical places provide a glimpse into the past and the culture that shaped this region. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Jining Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts that highlight the history and culture of Jining and the surrounding region.
  • Qufu Confucius Temple: Just a short drive from Jining is the birthplace of Confucius, the famous Chinese philosopher and educator. The temple showcasing his teachings is an important destination for those interested in Chinese history and culture.
  • Mount Niushou: This mountain features several temples and historical buildings that offer stunning views of Jining.

Natural Attractions of Jining: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jining’s scenery is breathtaking, and visitors can explore its natural beauty in many ways. From hiking and biking to horse-riding and bird-watching, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in Jining:

  • The Yellow River Wetlands: This nature reserve provides opportunities to observe many rare bird species and experience the peaceful atmosphere of the wetlands.
  • Jining Grasslands: Experience the vast expanse of typical Inner Mongolian grasslands, and take a camel ride or horse ride in it.
  • Taibai Lake: This beautiful lake situated on the outskirts of the city provides the ideal location for a relaxing picnic and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jining

For those who love hiking and biking, there are many exciting trails around Jining. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced adventurer, these routes offer unparalleled views of Inner Mongolia.

  • Taibai Lake Hiking Route: This easy trail winds around Taibai Lake and offers excellent views.
  • Jining North-South Axis Scenic Route: This 20km long bike path starts in the north of Jining and ends in the south. It passes through some of the city’s most scenic spots and is perfect for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Jining: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the city’s museums and natural wonders, there are also many exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Jining.

  • Horse Riding: Take a horse ride at Jining Grasslands and experience a typical Inner Mongolian rural life.
  • Exploring the Ancient City Wall: Take a stroll along the ancient city wall and see the town from a different perspective.
  • Folk Art Experiences: Several museums in Jining offer the opportunity to try your hand at traditional arts, such as paper cutting, calligraphy, and painting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jining

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long trip to Jining:

Day 1: Arrive in Jining, check into a hotel, and explore the city center, including the ancient city wall and local markets.

Day 2: Visit Qufu Confucius Temple, Mount Niushou, and Taibai Lake.

Day 3: Take a camel ride or horse-ride in the Jining grassland and visit Baotu Spring Scenic Area.

Day 4: Visit Jining Museum, and The Yellow River Wetlands.

Day 5: Take a hike on the Taibai Lake Hiking Route, Kaolin scenic spot and Jining Jiaxi wetland park.

Day 6: Visit a local family to understand their nomadic lifestyle and also take a horse-ride in it.

Day 7: Depart from Jining and continue your journey in Inner Mongolia or back to your hometown.

Food and Drink Scene of Jining

No travel guide is complete without mentioning the local food scene, and Jining has plenty of delicious dishes to try. Here are some of the best dishes and drinks to sample in Jining.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast Whole Lamb: Jining is famous for its roast lamb, which is a must-try dish. The lamb is seasoned with herbs and spices and roasted on a spit for hours, resulting in a succulent and flavorful dish.

  • Fried Chestnuts: Jining is said to have the best chestnuts in China, and they are often fried and served as a snack.

  • Mongolian Hot Pot: This spicy hot pot is a typical Inner Mongolian dish that features vegetables, meat slices, and a spicy broth.

Local Beverages

  • Milk Tea: This tea is made with milk and infused with a unique blend of tea leaves, giving it a creamy, mild flavor. It’s a favorite beverage in Inner Mongolia.

  • Mongolian Milk Wine: This traditional drink is made by fermenting milk with wine. It’s sweet, refreshing and typically enjoyed during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Jining: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jining has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels

  • Shangri-la Hotel Jining: This 5-star hotel offers luxury accommodation with modern amenities.

  • Thank Inn Plus Hotel: This budget-friendly option is located in the city center and offers comfortable guest rooms.

  • Inner Mongolia Guesthouse: This guesthouse is situated in the beautiful and peaceful countryside, offering visitors a tranquil retreat.

What to Buy in Jining: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for beautiful souvenirs or unique items to bring home, Jining has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to buy in Jining:

  • Chestnuts: Jining’s famous chestnuts are a great souvenir.

  • Horse Hair Crafts: Buy traditional horse hair crafts, including paintings, toys, ornaments and accessories.

  • Jining Grassland Products: Grassland sourced products including Inner Mongolian lamb wool overcoat and butter.

Local Culture and Customs of Jining

Jining is a city with rich cultural heritage. It is the birthplace of Confucius, and hence, Confucianism is widely followed. When visiting, pay attention to these customs:

  • Etiquette: Respect the locals and follow proper etiquette when entering temples or other important cultural sites.

  • Dress Code: Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Jining

The best time to visit Jining is from April to October, when the weather is mild, and attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jining

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Don’t miss the opportunity to try some delicious local dishes.

  • Learn about Local Culture: Jining has a rich cultural heritage, and taking the time to learn about it will enhance your experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Wear Revealing Clothes: Dress appropriately and respectfully when visiting temples or other religious sites.

  • Don’t Litter: Keep the environment clean and litter-free.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jining

Jining is a generally safe place for travelers. However, it is still recommended to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Jining

It is recommended to drink only bottled water while in Jining to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Jining: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jining

The nearest airport to Jining is Jining Qufu International Airport(曲阜机场), which is located about 29km away, and Taishan Airport(泰山机场) is another alternative much further away, located about 80km away.

Bus Stations Near Jining

Jining has 2 main bus stations, Jining Bus Station and Jining Coach Station - both are situated in the city’s center.

Other Options:

There are also several trains that run between Jining and other major cities in China, as well as highways that connect the city to other parts of Inner Mongolia.

How to Get Around in Jining

Local transportation options in Jining include taxis, public buses, and bike rentals. Taxis are widely available and are the easiest way to get around the city. Public buses are also an affordable option, but they can be crowded during peak hours.

Where to Go Next from Jining: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Inner Mongolia, there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting. These include:

  • Hohhot: The capital city of Inner Mongolia, known for its stunning grasslands and unique culture.

  • Ordos: This city is known for its Genghis Khan Statue and offers visitors an insight into Mongolian history.

  • Yinchuan: This city boasts unique Islamic architecture, the Helanshan Mountains, and Ningxia wine.

Conclusion

Jining may not be a popular tourist destination, but it offers travelers a unique glimpse into the lives of Inner Mongolians. From its rich cultural heritage to its breathtaking natural landscapes, Jining has something for everyone. We hope this Jining travel guide inspires you to visit this hidden gem and explore all it has to offer. Safe travels!
