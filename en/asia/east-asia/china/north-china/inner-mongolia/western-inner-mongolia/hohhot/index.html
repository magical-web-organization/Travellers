Introduction to Hohhot

Located in Western Inner Mongolia, Hohhot is a fascinating city that offers a unique blend of Chinese and Mongolian cultures. Formerly known as Guihua, Hohhot has a rich history dating back to the Qing Dynasty. As the capital of Inner Mongolia, Hohhot is home to many cultural and historical landmarks, as well as stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking through the grasslands, or trying local delicacies, Hohhot is a must-visit destination in China.

List of Places to Visit in Hohhot

When it comes to things to see and do in Hohhot, there is no shortage of options. From cultural landmarks to beautiful natural attractions, here’s our list of the top places to visit in Hohhot.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hohhot is home to many historical and cultural landmarks that are worth a visit. Some of the most popular museums and historical places include:

  • Inner Mongolia Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Inner Mongolia. Exhibits include artifacts from the Neolithic period, as well as traditional Mongolian costumes and art.
  • Dazhao Temple: This is the oldest and largest Tibetan Buddhist temple in Hohhot. It was built during the Ming Dynasty and is home to many important Buddhist relics.
  • Zhaojun Tomb: This tomb is dedicated to Wang Zhaojun, a Han Dynasty princess who married a Xiongnu Chanyu. The tomb is significant because it represents the relationship between the Han and Xiongnu cultures.

Natural Attractions of Hohhot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its rich history and culture, Hohhot is also known for its stunning natural scenery. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in Hohhot:

  • Gegentala Grasslands: These grasslands are located about 145 km outside of Hohhot and are a popular destination for hiking, horseback riding, and camping.
  • Five-Pagoda Temple: This temple is located in the Wuta Temple complex and features five pagodas that were built during the Liao Dynasty. It is also surrounded by beautiful gardens and scenic views.
  • Huitengxile Grasslands: These grasslands are located about 150 km outside of Hohhot and are known for their stunning landscapes and Mongolian culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hohhot

For outdoor enthusiasts, Hohhot offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Xilamuren Grasslands: This grassland is located about 90 km outside of Hohhot and offers many hiking and biking opportunities.
  • Daqing Mountain: This mountain is located about 12 km from Hohhot and offers some of the best hiking trails in the area. It’s also home to many different species of flora and fauna.
  • Inner Mongolia Grand Canyon: This canyon is located about 130 km outside of Hohhot and offers many hiking opportunities. It’s also known for its stunning waterfalls and rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Hohhot: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Hohhot, there are plenty of options. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Mongolian Culture Show: This show features traditional Mongolian music, dance, and costumes. It’s a great way to get an authentic glimpse into Mongolian culture.
  • Inner Mongolia Grassland Tour: This tour takes you to some of the most beautiful grasslands in Inner Mongolia, where you can experience Mongolian culture and enjoy outdoor activities.
  • Hohhot City Tour: This tour takes you to the city’s top landmarks and includes transportation and a knowledgeable guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hohhot

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Hohhot, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Inner Mongolia Museum and Dazhao Temple Day 2: Visit the Gegentala Grasslands and go horseback riding Day 3: Hike Daqing Mountain and visit Five-Pagoda Temple Day 4: Take a Mongolian Culture Show tour Day 5: Visit Huitengxile Grasslands and camp overnight Day 6: Explore Zhaojun Tomb and the Inner Mongolia Grand Canyon Day 7: Relax and enjoy local cuisine in Hohhot

Food and Drink Scene of Hohhot

No trip to Hohhot is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mongolian Hot Pot: This is a traditional Mongolian dish that features thinly sliced lamb or beef cooked in a hot pot of broth. It’s usually served with various dipping sauces and vegetables.
  • Fried Milk Cake: This is a sweet dish made with fermented milk and sugar that is fried into a cake-like shape. It’s crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside.
  • Inverted Milk Tea: This is a popular local beverage made with black tea, milk, and sugar. It’s called “inverted” because the milk is poured in first, followed by the tea.

Local Beverages

  • Mongolian Milk Wine: This beverage is made by fermenting mare’s milk and has a slightly sour taste. It’s a popular drink in Inner Mongolia and is usually served at local festivals.
  • Suutei Tsai: This is a traditional Mongolian tea made with milk, water, and salt. It’s a savory beverage that pairs well with local dishes.

What to Buy in Hohhot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, Hohhot offers many unique options. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mongolian Carpets: These carpets are handmade and feature traditional Mongolian designs. They make great wall decorations or floor coverings.
  • Mongolian Bowls: These bowls are made from wood or metal and are used for serving tea or other beverages. They make great gifts.
  • Cashmere Scarves: Inner Mongolia is known for its high-quality cashmere. A cashmere scarf makes a great souvenir and a functional accessory.

Local Culture and Customs of Hohhot

Hohhot is a fascinating city that offers a unique blend of Chinese and Mongolian cultures. Here are some of the customs and traditions you might encounter:

  • Mongolian Dress: Many locals and tourists wear traditional Mongolian clothing, such as the Deel, a long, loose dress, or the traditional Mongolian boots.
  • Naadam Festival: This festival is held in July and features traditional Mongolian sports such as horse racing, archery, and wrestling.
  • Tea Etiquette: When drinking tea, it’s customary to offer the cup to others before pouring and to hold the cup with both hands as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Hohhot

The best time to visit Hohhot is between May and September when the weather is warm and dry. However, if you’re interested in winter sports, you might want to visit between December and February, when the city’s ski resorts are in full swing.

  • Learn about local customs and traditions.
  • Respect the environment and wildlife.

Safety and Health

Hohhot is a generally safe city for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy:

Safety in Hohhot

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Hohhot

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Be mindful of food hygiene and cleanliness.
  • Carry a small first aid kit with essential medications.

Getting to Hohhot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hohhot is easily accessible by air, train, and bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Hohhot

  • Hohhot Baita International Airport: This airport is located about 15 km from the city center and serves both domestic and international flights.
  • Baotou Airport: This airport is located about 140 km from Hohhot and serves domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Hohhot

  • Hohhot Long-distance Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and offers buses to many cities in Inner Mongolia and beyond.
  • Hohhot West Bus Station: This bus station is located on the outskirts of the city and offers buses to nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Hohhot Railway Station is located in the city center and offers trains to many cities in China.
  • Private Car/Taxi: Private cars and taxis are available for hire in Hohhot and can be a convenient way to get around.

How to Get Around in Hohhot

Hohhot offers many transportation options for getting around the city. Here are some options:

  • Bus: Public buses are available throughout the city and are an affordable way to get around.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available and are a convenient way to get around. Make sure to negotiate the fare before getting in the car.
  • Bicycle: Many hotels and hostels offer bike rentals, which can be a fun and eco-friendly way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Hohhot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Hohhot, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Baotou: This city is located about 140 km from Hohhot and is known for its steel production and scenic spots such as Wudangzhao Monastery and Resonant Sand Bay.
  • Xilinhot: This city is located about 400 km from Hohhot and is known for its stunning natural scenery, including the Baerhu Grasslands and Taibus Banner.
  • Zhangye: This city is located about 1000 km from Hohhot and is home to the famous Danxia Landform and the Mati Temple.

Conclusion

Hohhot is a fascinating city that offers a unique blend of cultures, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking through the grasslands, or trying local delicacies, Hohhot has something for everyone.
