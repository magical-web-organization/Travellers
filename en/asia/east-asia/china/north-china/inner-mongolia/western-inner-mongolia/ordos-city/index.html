Introduction to Ordos City

Ordos City is a hidden gem located in Western Inner Mongolia, in Inner Mongolia, North China. This city is enriched with heritage, cultural significance, and stunning natural sights. Ordos City was originally inhabited 30,000 years ago by the Xirong tribe, and the city has a long and fascinating history, including playing a critical role in the establishment and growth of the Silk Road.

Today, Ordos City is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, historic cultural sites, and delicious culinary delights. The city is also home to many nomadic tribes, including the Mongolians and Kazakhs, and is a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience authentic Chinese culture and heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Ordos City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ordos Museum

The Ordos Museum is a must-visit attraction for travelers to Ordos City. This museum has a vast collection of exhibits related to the history of the city, including ancient pottery, weapons, and clothing. Visitors can learn about the earliest inhabitants of the region, the Xirong tribe, and experience the traditional way of life of the nomadic tribes of Inner Mongolia.

Mausoleum of Genghis Khan

The Mausoleum of Genghis Khan is another significant historical site in Ordos City. This massive complex is dedicated to the famous Mongolian ruler, Genghis Khan, and showcases his life, legacy, and achievements. It’s a great place for history buffs as it provides a chance to learn about the impact of Genghis Khan on China’s and Mongolia’s history.

Genghis Khan Square

Genghis Khan Square is located in the heart of Ordos City and is a popular gathering spot for locals and visitors alike. This public square is dedicated to the famous Mongolian ruler and has a statue of him as an eye-catching centerpiece. Visitors can stroll around the square and see the locals dance and sing to traditional Mongolian music.

Natural Attractions of Ordos City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Resonant Sand Gorge National Geopark

The Resonant Sand Gorge National Geopark is an extraordinary natural attraction in Ordos City. The park gets its name from the incredible resonating sand dunes, which make a sound when people walk on them. The park offers a range of activities, including desert trekking, sandboarding, and camel riding.

Helan Mountains

The Helan Mountains are a striking natural attraction that borders Inner Mongolia and Ningxia Province. This mountain range is perfect for lovers of hiking and nature enthusiasts, offering some of the most stunning vistas in China. Visitors can indulge in a range of outdoor activities, including rock climbing, mountain trekking, and camping.

Yellow River Canyon Scenic Area

The Yellow River Canyon Scenic Area is a breathtaking natural attraction located just a few hours from Ordos City. This stunning area features dramatic gorges, cascading waterfalls, and majestic peaks that offer a stunning backdrop for photography. Visitors can explore the area by taking a boat ride or enjoying a scenic hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ordos City

Mausoleum of Genghis Khan to the Yellow River

This hiking and biking route is a popular scenic trail that starts at the Mausoleum of Genghis Khan and ends at the Yellow River. The trail encompasses the vast and rugged desert terrain, and visitors can explore the area’s traditional Mongolian culture, including yurts, herds of animals, and local farming practices.

Resonant Sand Gorge Nature Trail

This nature trail is perfect for hikers and bikers looking for a challenging and rewarding experience. The Resonant Sand Gorge Nature Trail offers stunning views of the surrounding dunes, scenic vistas, and hidden oases that dot the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Ordos City: Activities and Tours

Camel Riding Experience

Camel riding is a popular activity in Ordos City, and visitors can experience the unique sensation of riding a camel on the Resonant Sand Gorge National Geopark or the Gobi Desert. It’s an excellent opportunity to connect with the local culture and enjoy the natural beauty of Inner Mongolia.

Traditional Mongolian Clothing Experience

Visitors can try on traditional Mongolian clothing and take part in a photoshoot to capture the beautiful memories of their trip to Ordos City. Many cultural centers and museums in the city offer this experience for visitors, and it’s a great way to experience local traditions and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Ordos City

Must-Try Dishes

Huushuur

Huushuur is a traditional Mongolian dish that is similar to a fried dumpling. The filling of this dish is usually made of ground meat, onions, and spices, and it’s often served as a starter or snack.

Kebabs

Kebabs are a popular street food in Ordos City, made with skewered meat that is cooked over charcoal. Visitors can try a variety of kebabs, including lamb, beef, and chicken, and enjoy the traditional spices that are used in local cuisine.

Local Beverages

Airag

Airag, also known as kumis, is a fermented horse milk drink that is a traditional beverage in Mongolia. This drink has a unique sour taste and is known for its health benefits, including improving digestion and boosting the immune system.

Milk Tea

Milk tea is a popular beverage in Inner Mongolia, and it’s made with black tea and milk. The tea has a slightly sweet taste due to the addition of sugar, and it’s often served as a refreshing drink on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Ordos City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crowne Plaza Ordos

The Crowne Plaza Ordos is a luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the desert and easy access to many of the city’s attractions. The hotel has excellent amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and several dining options.

Inner Mongolia Jinjiang International Hotel

The Inner Mongolia Jinjiang International Hotel is a modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms and great facilities. It’s located near the city center and is an excellent choice for visitors who want to explore the city’s cultural and historical sites.

What to Buy in Ordos City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cashmere Products

Cashmere is one of the most popular products from Ordos City, and visitors can purchase a range of cashmere products at local markets and shops. Scarves, hats, and sweaters are among the most popular items, and they make for excellent souvenirs to bring back home.

Mongolian Yurt

A Mongolian yurt, also known as a Ger, is a traditional portable dwelling used by nomads in Mongolia. Visitors can purchase a smaller version of these yurts as a unique souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ordos City

Odos City is enriched with heritage, culture, and tradition. The city’s traditional Mongolian culture is celebrated with various cultural festivals and events, including the Naadam Festival, which showcases traditional Mongolian sports such as horse racing, archery, and wrestling.

The traditional Mongolian culture also features a unique form of music, dance, and art, which visitors can experience in many cultural centers and museums.

Best Time to Visit Ordos City

The best time to visit Ordos City is during the spring and autumn months, from April to May and September to October. The weather is mild, and the skies are clear, making it an excellent time for outdoor activities and exploring the city’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ordos City

Do:

  • Respect the city’s cultural traditions and heritage
  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Touch people’s heads or shoulders without permission
  • Drink or smoke in public
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or traditional ceremonies

Safety and Health

Safety in Ordos City

Overall, Ordos City is a safe destination for visitors. However, travelers should take normal safety precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding unlit or isolated areas at night.

Health in Ordos City

Visitors should be mindful of their health while visiting Ordos City. It’s essential to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen as the city has a dry and arid climate. It’s also advisable to carry necessary medications and seek medical attention in case of any illness.

Getting to Ordos City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ordos City

The closest airport to Ordos City is Ordos Ejin Horo Airport, which is about 30 kilometers away from the city center. Visitors can take a bus, taxi, or private transfer to get to their hotels or other destinations in the city.

Bus Stations Near Ordos City

Ordos City has several bus stations, including the main Ordos Bus Station and Ordos West Bus Station, offering connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to travel to Ordos City via train, with regular services available from major cities such as Beijing and Shanghai.

How to Get Around in Ordos City

Getting around Ordos City is easy, with several transportation options available, including buses, taxis, and private transfers. Visitors can also rent a bike or a car to explore the city’s natural beauty at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ordos City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hohhot

Hohhot is the capital city of Inner Mongolia and is located about 280 kilometers from Ordos City. This city is known for its stunning temples, traditional markets, and exciting nightlife.

Yinchuan

Yinchuan is the capital city of Ningxia Province and is located about 430 kilometers from Ordos City. This city is famous for its historical sites, including the Western Xia Tombs, and the scenic vistas offered by the Yellow River.

Conclusion

Ordos City is a fascinating destination for travelers who want to experience authentic Chinese culture and the stunning natural beauty of Inner Mongolia.
