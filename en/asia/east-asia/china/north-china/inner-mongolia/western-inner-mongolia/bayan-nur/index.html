Introduction to Bayan Nur

Bayan Nur is a picturesque city located in Western Inner Mongolia, in North China. It is the largest and the central city of Bayan Nur Prefecture, surrounded by the vast Ordos Plateau, connecting the Gobi Desert in the south, and the grasslands in the north. What makes Bayan Nur unique is its blend of cultural and natural wonders. The city is home to a lot of historical sites, museums, and natural wonders, making it the perfect escape for culture enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Bayan Nur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bayan Nur Museum

Bayan Nur Museum is the largest museum in Inner Mongolia, which covers an area of 40,000 square meters. The museum collection has a comprehensive collection of ethnic and regional history, natural history, and folk customs in Inner Mongolia, including exhibits focused on archaeology, traditional Mongolian customs, and art.

Xinglong Mountain Rock Paintings

These are ancient rock paintings found in Xinglong Mountains, dating back to the late Neolithic era. The rock paintings depict images of human beings, animals, and natural scenery, showcasing the ancient way of life of the people who lived in the area.

Natural Attractions of Bayan Nur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Resonant Sand Bay

Resonant Sand Bay is a stunning desert landscape situated on the bank of the Yellow River. The sand dunes are known for the unique ‘singing sand phenomenon’ which happens when the sand slides down the dune, creating a high-pitched sound. The area is perfect for a day trip with activities such as sandboarding, camel riding, and hiking.

Dana Lake

Dana Lake is a majestic lake located in the middle of the Tengger Desert. This saline lake is a natural wonder that changes its colors throughout the year, depending on the season. Visitors can witness the beauty of the lake on foot along the boardwalk or opt for a camel or horseback ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bayan Nur

Tengger Desert

Tengger Desert is the fourth largest desert in China, covering an area of over 36,700 km². The desert is ideal for hiking and biking, with beautiful landscapes of sand dunes, oases, and rocky outcrops. The region is also home to a variety of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species.

Best Things to Do in Bayan Nur: Activities and Tours

Genghis Khan’s Mausoleum

Genghis Khan’s Mausoleum is a beautiful monument that pays homage to the great Mongol leader. Visitors can take a guided tour of the mausoleum, which includes a display of ceramics, paintings, and other artifacts.

Bayan Nur Ethnic Customs and Culture Park

The Bayan Nur Ethnic Customs and Culture Park showcases the traditional way of life of the Mongolian people. Visitors can witness the daily routine of herdsmen, sample local food, and attend traditional performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Bayan Nur

Must-Try Dishes

Roast Whole Sheep

Roast Whole Sheep is a signature dish that is prepared during festive occasions. It’s a dish that has been passed down from generations to generations, using only the best ingredients and traditional methods.

Milk Tea

Milk tea is a traditional drink in Inner Mongolia, typically made from milk, tea, and sugar. It’s a drink that is served in almost every home, restaurant, and teahouse in the region.

Local Beverages

Aobao Wine

Aobao Wine is a traditional drink of the Mongolian people, made by fermenting yogurt and milk. It has a mild, sour taste and a creamy texture that makes it an excellent drink for warmer days.

Huanghe Beer

Huanghe Beer is a popular local beer that is brewed in Bayan Nur. The beer is made with the highest quality barley and hops, resulting in a beer that has a rich, full-bodied flavor.

What to Buy in Bayan Nur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bayan Nur is famous for its unique handicrafts, including woolen clothes, horsehair and leather items, carpets, and silverware. Some popular souvenirs to bring back home include Mongolian boots, Mongolian-style hats, and Mongolian-style yurts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bayan Nur

Bayan Nur is a region with rich history and unique culture. The Mongolian people are known for their hospitality and are proud of their rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the locals’ traditions and customs, including dress codes, table etiquette, and religious practices.

Best Time to Visit Bayan Nur

The best time to visit Bayan Nur is from May to October. The weather during this time is warm and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and desert adventures. Winters can be extremely cold, with temperatures dropping below freezing point.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bayan Nur

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry cash as credit cards are not widely accepted.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission.
  • Make fun of the locals’ customs and traditions.
  • Disrespect the environment by leaving trash in tourist sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bayan Nur

Bayan Nur is a generally safe place for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take precautionary measures when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and desert adventures.

Health in Bayan Nur

Visitors are advised to check with their doctor for vaccinations and bring necessary medication and first aid kits. It is recommended to drink bottled water to avoid stomach upsets.

Getting to Bayan Nur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bayan Nur

Bayan Nur has a domestic airport, which offers flights to Beijing, Hohhot, and Zhengzhou.

Bus Stations Near Bayan Nur

There are three bus stations in Bayan Nur, namely the Bayan Nur Long-Distance Bus Station, the Bayan Nur West Long-Distance Bus Station, and the Bayan Nur South Long-Distance Bus Station. Visitors can take a bus to/from cities such as Hohhot, Alxa, Yinchuan, and Baotou.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car/jeep or ride a bicycle to explore the region.

How to Get Around in Bayan Nur

The best way to get around in Bayan Nur is by taxi or hiring a private car. Visitors can also ride on horseback/camel or hike around the natural attractions.

Conclusion

Bayan Nur is a hidden gem in Inner Mongolia, filled with cultural and natural wonders. Visitors can explore the city’s rich history and traditions, indulge in outdoor adventures or simply take in the scenic beauty of the region. So, pack your bags and get ready for an authentic Mongolian experience!
