Introduction to Bayanhot

Bayanhot is a hidden gem located in Western Inner Mongolia, in the northern part of China. The city is often overlooked by tourists in favor of more popular destinations, but that’s precisely what makes it a unique and authentic travel experience. Bayanhot is rich in both natural beauty and rich cultural significance. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Bayanhot.

List of Places to Visit in Bayanhot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bayanhot is home to several culturally significant museums and historical sites that are worth a visit. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Ordos Museum: The Ordos Museum showcases the culture and history of Inner Mongolia through its impressive architecture, fascinating exhibits, and informative displays.
  • Genghis Khan Mausoleum: Located in Erdos City, a short drive from Bayanhot, the Genghis Khan Mausoleum is a memorial to one of the world’s greatest conquerors. The site features a museum, tomb, and statues of Genghis Khan’s family members.
  • Daqingshan National Forest Park: Known for its stunning natural beauty, Daqingshan National Forest Park is also home to several historical sites, including the ruins of a Shang Dynasty city.

Natural Attractions of Bayanhot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bayanhot’s natural beauty is its biggest draw. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Bayanhot:

  • Badain Jaran Desert: As one of the largest deserts in China, the Badain Jaran Desert is a must-visit attraction in Bayanhot. Highlights include seeing the singing sand dunes, visiting the crystal clear lakes and admiring the ancient rock art.
  • Yellow River: The Yellow River is China’s second-longest river and runs through Inner Mongolia. Take a scenic hike along the Yellow River Canyon, which features stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Xiangshawan Desert: As a small desert located in the eastern part of Inner Mongolia, Xiangshawan Desert features picturesque sand dune formations that are great for sightseeing and outdoor activities like sandboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bayanhot

Bayanhot is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts with many hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of our recommended routes:

  • Badain Jaran Desert Loop: Take an eight-day hiking and biking trip starting from Badain Jaran Desert and seeing the endless dunes, lake clusters and villages
  • Ordos Loop: This one-week bike trip goes from the Ordos Grassland to the Yellow River Valley and takes in many of Bayanhot’s natural attractions and cultural highlights.

Best Things to Do in Bayanhot: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting the top attractions in Bayanhot, there are many other things to do in the city. Consider taking part in one of the following activities and tours:

  • Sandboarding: Try sandboarding while exploring the picturesque sand dunes of Xiangshawan Desert
  • Horseback Riding: Take a horseback riding tour of the Ordos Grassland for a taste of the inner Mongolia traditional way of living

Week-Long Itinerary for Bayanhot

Here’s our suggested week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip to Bayanhot.

Day 1: Arrive in Bayanhot and settle into your accommodation

Day 2: Visit the Ordos Museum and Genghis Khan Mausoleum in Erdos City

Day 3: Explore the Badain Jaran Desert and its surroundings

Day 4: Hike around Daqingshan National Forest Park

Day 5: Take a horseback riding tour of the Ordos Grassland

Day 6: Visit Xiangshawan Desert and try sandboarding

Day 7: Relax and do some local shopping before departing Bayanhot for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Bayanhot

Bayanhot’s cuisine is influenced by northern China, and the local food scene is built primarily around meat dishes, particularly lamb and beef.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mongolian Hotpot: A hearty meal of sliced meats, vegetables, and noodles cooked in a pot of simmering broth.
  • Shouzhuafan: A traditional rice dish that has been slowly cooked in a delicious gravy of meat broth.
  • Niubaojian: A popular lamb or beef soup in the Mongol style originally served to the Mongolian nobles

Local Beverages

  • Milk Tea: It’s a traditional drink common in inner Mongolia with black tea, milk and sugar that is refreshing after a long day of exploring.
  • Kumi: A sour milk drink is another beverage cherished in inner Mongolia, and often poured into a bowl shared by locals.

Where to Stay in Bayanhot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Bayanhot is a smaller city, it has plenty of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the top places to stay in Bayanhot:

  • Jinjiang International Hotel: A four-star hotel for those looking for a comfortable night stay with extra amenities.
  • Longjin International Hotel: A budget-friendly option, the Longjin International Hotel is perfect for visitors traveling on a budget.

What to Buy in Bayanhot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that you should take home from Bayanhot. They include:

  • Cashmere Products: Known for producing high-quality cashmere products, Bayanhot is a great place to buy authentic sweaters, shawls, and scarves.
  • Inner Mongolian Silverware: Silver is a prominent craft in Inner Mongolia, and shine pieces can make for eye-catching souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bayanhot

Inner Mongolia has a unique culture that visitors to Bayanhot should acquaint themselves with. Here are some of the most notable customs:

  • Nomadic culture: Bayanhot’s inhabitants have been traditionally engaged in semi-nomadic lifestyles, and the landscape has been important in shaping their unique customs and way of life.
  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding was once a way of life for the Mongolian people, and horses are an important symbol of their culture.

Best Time to Visit Bayanhot

The best time to visit Bayanhot is from May to October. During this time, you can enjoy pleasant weather, with average temperatures ranging from 15°C-25°C. You can also avoid Bayanhot’s chilly winter season that runs from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bayanhot

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Bring appropriate clothing for both warm and cold weather
  • Try local cuisine and products.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt local communities and wildlife
  • Disrespect the environment by littering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bayanhot

Bayanhot is a safe city with low crime rates. That said, travelers should still take the necessary precautions like not walking alone at night.

Health in Bayanhot

Travelers should be aware of the potential risk of altitude sickness in Bayanhot due to its high elevation. Bring the proper medications for altitude sickness and stay hydrated.

Getting to Bayanhot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bayanhot is a relatively isolated city in Inner Mongolia. Nevertheless, there are several transportation options available:

Airports Near Bayanhot

  • Erdos Ejin Horo Airport: Located approximately 60 km from Bayanhot.

Bus Stations Near Bayanhot

  • Bayanhot Central Bus Station: Located in the city center, the central bus station links the city to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Hiring a private car: Visitors looking for a more convenient option can hire a car and driver to get them around town.

How to Get Around in Bayanhot

The most convenient way to explore Bayanhot is via taxi or bike. Buses also run to some of the outlying areas of the city but be prepared for a crowded and slow journey.

Where to Go Next from Bayanhot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bayanhot is a great jumping-off point for exploring Inner Mongolia’s surrounding areas. Consider taking a day trip to see the following attractions:

  • The Inner Mongolia Prairie
  • The Huanghe River
  • The Mausoleum of Emperor Jingdi

Conclusion

Bayanhot is a fascinating destination for travelers interested in getting off the beaten track and exploring Northern China’s unique culture, history, and landscapes. With this guide, you now have all the information you need to start planning your trip to Bayanhot.
