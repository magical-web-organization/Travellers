Introduction to Northern Suburbs

Northern Suburbs is located in Beijing, in North China. The area boasts of rich cultural significance, breathtaking natural wonders, and an abundance of outdoor activities. Northern Suburbs is a must-visit destination for those seeking to immerse themselves in traditional Chinese culture and explore natural beauty.

In this guide, we will take you through everything you need to know about Northern Suburbs, including a list of famous places to visit, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, what to buy, local cuisine and culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Suburbs

Northern Suburbs has plenty of tourist attractions to offer. From culturally significant museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and stunning scenic views, here is a list of places you must visit in Northern Suburbs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ming Tombs: This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts of 13 emperors’ mausoleums and is popularly known as the ‘13 Tombs.’ Situated at the foot of the Tianshou Mountain, this site is a unique representation of Chinese architecture and culture.
  • The Summer Palace: Built during the Qing dynasty, the Summer Palace is a magnificent palace complex with a vast lake, gardens, pavilions, and walkways. The Summer Palace is an excellent example of imperial garden design.
  • The Old Summer Palace: Built-in 1709, the Old Summer Palace was once a grand imperial palace that boasted of over 40 scenic locations, including pavilions, bridges, and lakes. The palace was destroyed by British and French forces in 1860, and now stands as a reminder of China’s tumultuous history.

Natural Attractions of Northern Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Great Wall of China: The Great Wall is one of the wonders of the world and is a must-visit attraction. Northern Suburbs provides access to different sections of the wall, including the Juyongguan and Mutianyu Great Wall sections.
  • The Fragrant Hills Park: This popular park boasts colorful foliage in the autumn and the scenery is just breathtaking.
  • The Silver Mountain Pagoda Forest: This pagoda forest is a serene and peaceful place to get away from the bustling city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Suburbs

Northern Suburbs is a haven for outdoor activities enthusiasts. It offers multiple hiking and biking routes that boast spectacular views and challenging terrains. Here is a list of some of the most popular routes in Northern Suburbs:

  • The Great Wall Hike: The Great Wall of China is a popular hiking route in Northern Suburbs. The Juyongguan and Mutianyu sections offer a scenic view, and hikers can experience some breathtaking views.
  • Yundu Mountain Bike Trail: This challenging trail is a combination of long climbs, sharp descents, and technical terrain. This route takes riders through the breathtaking landscape of Northern Suburbs.

Best Things to Do in Northern Suburbs: Activities and Tours

Northern Suburbs is more than just hiking and biking. There are plenty of other activities and tours you can indulge in. Here are a few activities you should add to your itinerary:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Experience the Northern Suburbs’ landscape like never before by taking a hot air balloon ride.
  • Attend a Kung Fu Show: Discover the art of Kung Fu in Northern Suburbs. The Red Theater features performances by Kung Fu masters, and it’s a great way to immerse yourself in traditional Chinese culture.
  • Visit a local winery: If you enjoy good wine, take a tour of one of Northern Suburbs’ wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Suburbs

Northern Suburbs offers a unique culinary experience, and it is home to various traditional dishes that you cannot miss. Here is a list of must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peking Roast Duck: This is Beijing’s most famous food item, and the Northern Suburbs offers several excellent restaurants that prepare it to perfection.
  • Zhajiangmian: Zhajiangmian is a traditional Beijing noodle dish served with pork sauce and vegetables.
  • Jianbing: Jianbing is a crispy crepe that’s stuffed with eggs, vegetables, meat, and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Beijing Yogurt: This drink is a unique kind of yogurt brewed with Chinese herbs like wolfberry, ginseng, and green tea.
  • Plum Juice: Plum juice is a refreshing drink, and it’s made by blending plums with sugar and water.

Where to Stay in Northern Suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re looking for a place to stay in Northern Suburbs, there are several options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The Brick Yard Hotel: This hotel is situated in the artistic district of Beijing, and it boasts of beautifully designed rooms and breathtaking views.
  • The Commune by the Great Wall Hotel: This hotel offers traditional Chinese-style rooms with modern amenities, and it’s located at the base of the Great Wall.
  • The Orchid Hotel: The Orchid Hotel is a small boutique hotel located in the heart of Beijing’s hutongs. It offers a unique experience for travelers who want to immerse themselves in traditional Chinese culture.

What to Buy in Northern Suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Suburbs is famous for its unique crafts and arts, and there are plenty of markets and shops where you can find souvenirs and local products. Here’s a list of items you should consider taking back home:

  • Tea Sets: Northern Suburbs is famous for its porcelain tea sets, and you can find them in various shapes and sizes.
  • Jade Carving: Jade is a precious stone in China, and Northern Suburbs offers carved jade sculptures, pendants, and earrings that make perfect souvenirs.
  • Cloisonné Products: Cloisonné is a traditional Chinese craft where wire is used to form patterns on a metal surface. It is a unique art form that produces beautiful vases and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Suburbs

Northern Suburbs is a traditional area with significant cultural heritage. It’s important to be aware of their customs so that you can be respectful and appreciate the local culture.

  • Language: Mandarin is the primary language in Northern Suburbs, but most locals can speak English.
  • Etiquette: It’s important to greet locals respectfully, and it’s common to exchange business cards.
  • Religion: Buddhism and Taoism are prevalent in Northern Suburbs, and temples and shrines are a common sight.

Best Time to Visit Northern Suburbs

The best time to visit Northern Suburbs is in the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). The weather is mild during these seasons, and you can enjoy the stunning landscape without extreme temperatures or humidity.

Getting to Northern Suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northern Suburbs is easily accessible through various transportation modes:

Airports Near Northern Suburbs

  • Beijing Capital International Airport: This airport is the primary airport in Beijing and is about 30 kilometers away from Northern Suburbs.

Bus Stations Near Northern Suburbs

  • Dongzhimen Transport Hub: This hub is located in the center of Beijing and provides access to several bus routes that connect to Northern Suburbs.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are common in Beijing and can take you to Northern Suburbs from Beijing city center.
  • Metro: Beijing has an extensive metro system, and visitors can take line 8 and Bus 936 to the Ming Tombs and Great Wall.
