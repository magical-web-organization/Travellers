Introduction to Forbidden City

Forbidden City, also known as the Imperial Palace, is a magnificent palace complex located in the center of Beijing, China. With a history spanning over six centuries, Forbidden City was the seat of power for emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties, and covers an area of 72 hectares with over 980 buildings.

The Forbidden City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the most well-preserved imperial palace complexes in the world. Tourists from all over the world come to experience the rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture of Forbidden City.

List of Places to Visit in Forbidden City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palace Museum

Palace Museum is one of the main attractions in Forbidden City. The museum houses an extensive collection of imperial artifacts, art, and historical objects. The museum is divided into three main sections: the outer court, the inner court, and the imperial gardens. The exhibits include imperial jewelry, paintings, ceramics, and ancient documents.

Meridian Gate

Meridian Gate is the main entrance to Forbidden City. It is the largest and most important gateway and is guarded by two majestic stone lions from the Ming dynasty.

Hall of Supreme Harmony

Hall of Supreme Harmony is the largest imperial palace hall and the ceremonial center of Forbidden City. The hall was used for important ceremonies and has a high platform with a magnificent golden throne.

Hall of Central Harmony

Hall of Central Harmony was the private retreat of the emperor and was used for relaxation and meditation.

Natural Attractions of Forbidden City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jing Shan Park

Jing Shan Park is a beautiful park located to the north of Forbidden City. Visitors can climb to the top of the man-made hill and enjoy a panoramic view of Forbidden City.

Imperial Garden

The Imperial Garden is a tranquil and verdant garden located at the northern end of Forbidden City. It features pavilions, rockeries, and carefully manicured gardens.

Nine Dragon Screen

Nine Dragon Screen is a stunning wall of glazed tiles featuring nine dragons. It is located to the east of Forbidden City and is one of the most well-preserved examples of its kind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forbidden City

Visitors can take a leisurely stroll or bike the alleys and paths inside Forbidden City. Bikes can be rented at the entrance gate of the palace. There are also hiking trails around Jing Shan Park that offer amazing views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Forbidden City: Activities and Tours

Visitors can take guided tours that provide a more in-depth understanding of the history and architecture of Forbidden City. There are also various cultural activities such as calligraphy and Chinese folk art classes that are available.

Food and Drink Scene of Forbidden City

Must-Try Dishes

Beijing Roast Duck

Beijing roast duck is a famous dish that originated from the imperial kitchens. The skin is crispy and the meat is tender, making it a must-try dish for any visitor to Forbidden City.

Shandong Famed-Braised Sea Cucumber

Shandong famed-braised sea cucumber is a delicacy made with sea cucumber, which is marinated in a savory sauce and cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

Chinese Tea

Chinese tea is a popular beverage in Forbidden City. It is served both hot and cold and is known for its soothing and relaxing effects.

Baijiu

Baijiu is a traditional Chinese liquor that is distilled from grains and has a high alcohol content.

Where to Stay in Forbidden City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many lodging options available to visitors of Forbidden City. There are luxurious hotels, cozy guesthouses, and affordable hostels.

The Peninsula Beijing

The Peninsula Beijing is a luxurious hotel located just a few minutes’ walk from Forbidden City. The hotel features a rooftop bar, spa services, and multiple dining options.

Sitting on the City Walls Courtyard House

Sitting on the City Walls Courtyard House is a cozy guesthouse that features traditional Chinese architecture and a peaceful courtyard.

Peking Station Hostel

Peking Station Hostel is an affordable hostel located near the main entrance of Forbidden City. The hostel features dormitories, private rooms, and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Forbidden City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy souvenirs such as postcards, keychains, and magnets at the souvenir shops within Forbidden City. Traditional Chinese products such as silk, calligraphy brushes, and tea sets can also be found in the local markets in the surrounding area.

Local Culture and Customs of Forbidden City

Visitors to Forbidden City should be aware of and respectful of local customs and traditions. Hat and dress codes must be followed at all times. It is also important to be aware of the environmental impact of tourism and to respect the natural beauty of the area.

Best Time to Visit Forbidden City

The best time to visit Forbidden City is during the spring and autumn when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forbidden City

Do:

  • Wear respectful clothing.
  • Follow local customs and traditions.
  • Respect the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of the imperial artifacts and exhibits.
  • Touch or damage any historical artifacts or architecture.
  • Litter or behave disrespectfully.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forbidden City

Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions against pickpocketing and theft. It is also important to be aware of the environmental impact of tourism and to respect the natural beauty of the area.

Health in Forbidden City

It is recommended to wear sunscreen, stay hydrated, and take regular breaks during outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of the local air quality and take necessary precautions if sensitive to pollution.

Getting to Forbidden City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Forbidden City

Beijing Capital International Airport is the closest airport to Forbidden City.

Bus Stations Near Forbidden City

Bus stops are located throughout Beijing and are an affordable transportation option to Forbidden City.

Other Options

Visitors can take a taxi or private car to Forbidden City. There are also local tours available that offer transportation and sightseeing packages.

How to Get Around in Forbidden City

Visitors can walk, bike, or take a guided tour to get around Forbidden City.

Where to Go Next from Forbidden City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Great Wall

The Great Wall is a breathtaking feat of engineering and is located just a few hours’ drive from Forbidden City. The wall meanders through the countryside and offers stunning panoramic views.

Temple of Heaven

Temple of Heaven is a stunning example of imperial architecture and is located just south of Forbidden City. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for fantastic acoustics in its main hall.

Conclusion

Forbidden City is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Chinese culture and history. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and scenic beauty, Forbidden City offers a one-of-a-kind experience that is sure to leave a lasting impression.
