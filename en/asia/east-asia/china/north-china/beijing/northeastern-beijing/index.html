Introduction to Northeastern Beijing

Northeastern Beijing is a captivating region of China’s bustling capital city that tourists often miss. Its cultural and historical significance, combined with its ample natural beauty, make it a must-visit for anyone planning a trip to Beijing. This guide will take you through all the essential information you need to know before you embark on your adventure in Northeastern Beijing.

List of Places to Visit in Northeastern Beijing

Northeastern Beijing is a massive area that encompasses several significant neighborhoods, each with their unique charm and attractions. Here are the top places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • 798 Art Zone: Located in Dashanzi, 798 Art Zone is an iconic art district that showcases contemporary Chinese art. It has become a hub for art galleries, cafes, boutiques, and bars that cater to a trendy crowd.
  • Today Art Museum: Located in Chaoyang Park, Today Art Museum is one of the most influential contemporary art museums in China. It’s a five-story building that contains a series of rotating exhibitions, seminars, and lectures.
  • China Railway Museum (Beijing): Located in the Dongjiao area, this museum covers the history of China’s railway from the steam age to modern times. It’s a great spot for families with children as it has plenty of interactive exhibits.
  • Lama Temple: Located in the Dongcheng district, the Lama Temple is a famous Tibetan Buddhist temple built in the 17th century. It’s a well-preserved temple and one of the largest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries outside of Tibet.

Natural Attractions of Northeastern Beijing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mutianyu Great Wall: Located in the Huairou District, this section of the Great Wall is less crowded, making it perfect for those who prefer to avoid tourist traps. It’s a great spot for hiking, and you can choose to hike to the top of the wall, ride a cable car or take a toboggan ride down.
  • Yosemite National Park of China: Located in Huairou District, this park is often compared to the national parks in the United States. With breathtaking scenery, waterfalls, clear lakes, pine forests, and hiking trails, it’s an ideal spot for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeastern Beijing

Northeastern Beijing is a great place for hiking and biking. Some of the top routes include:

  • Gubeikou to Jinshanling: This trail is quite challenging, but it rewards hikers with some stunning views of the Great Wall.
  • Jiankou to Mutianyu: This seven-kilometer hike is a must-do for those who want to experience a mix of challenging terrain and beautiful scenery.
  • Huairou Reservoir: This is an easy route for those who want to take a leisurely ride or walk. The path around the reservoir is flat and surrounded by trees.

Best Things to Do in Northeastern Beijing: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Chinese dishes by taking a cooking class from a local chef.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take to the skies over Beijing on a hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning bird’s eye views of the capital and its surroundings.
  • Temples and Monastery Tours: Explore the various temples and monasteries in Northeastern Beijing with a licensed guide who can provide historical and cultural context.

Here’s an example of a week-long itinerary for Northeastern Beijing for travelers who want to explore the area thoroughly:

Food and Drink Scene of Northeastern Beijing

No trip to Beijing is complete without trying out the local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peking Roast Duck: This is a classic Beijing dish, a whole duck roasted in a specific way to achieve crispy skin and tender meat. Several restaurants in Northeastern Beijing are famous for this dish.
  • Zhajiangmian: A hearty noodle dish served with meat, vegetables, and a savory black bean sauce.
  • Jiaozi: These are Chinese dumplings stuffed with meat or vegetables, boiled or steamed, and served hot.

Local Beverages

  • Jiāng: A type of fermented drink made from rice, sorghum or barley.
  • Baijiu: A strong distilled drink made from grain.
  • Moutai: A type of baijiu that originated in Guizhou Province, it’s a frequently featured drink of Chinese banquets.

Where to Stay in Northeastern Beijing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northeastern Beijing has a wide range of accommodation options, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels, and everything in between. Here are some examples:

  • Peking Yard Hostel: This hostel located in Dongcheng is housed in a traditional courtyard complex and offers a unique experience of staying in a historic setting.
  • Beijing Marriott Hotel Northeast: This hotel is located in the Chaoyang area and provides luxurious accommodations, including an indoor pool, fitness center, and spa.
  • NUO Hotel Beijing: Located in the Dongcheng area, this award-winning five-star hotel combines modern design with traditional Chinese elements.

What to Buy in Northeastern Beijing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cloisonné: These are enameled metal objects that are intricately designed and colored, such as vases, bowls, and figures.
  • Pearls: Freshwater and Akoya pearls are widely available in Beijing, and Northeastern Beijing has several pearl markets that sell high-quality pearls.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeastern Beijing

  • Language: Mandarin Chinese is the official language of China. While there is some use of English in tourist areas, it’s a good idea to carry a Mandarin phrasebook or translator app.
  • Etiquette and Punctuality: Punctuality is highly valued in China, so it’s essential to be on time for any planned activities. Respect for elders and authority figures is also a crucial cultural value in China.
  • Dress Code: While there is no strict dress code in China, it’s essential to dress appropriately according to the occasion and culture, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Northeastern Beijing

The best time to visit Northeastern Beijing is during the spring and autumn season, from March to May and from September to November. The weather is pleasant, and the temperature is mild, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeastern Beijing

Do:

  • Carry a face mask: Beijing suffers from severe air pollution, especially during the winter months. It’s advisable to carry a face mask and avoid outdoor activities during the worst of the pollution.
  • Learn some basic Mandarin: Learning some basic Mandarin Chinese can help you navigate your way around local markets and communicate effectively with locals.
  • Watch out for scams: Scams are prevalent in tourist areas, so it’s essential to be careful and do research before parting with your money.

Don’t:

  • Avoid tap water: Drinking tap water is not recommended in China. It’s advisable to drink bottled water or carry a personal water filtration system.
  • Don’t show affection publicly: Public displays of affection are not common in China, so it’s best to refrain from hugging, kissing or holding hands in public.
  • Don’t disrespect cultural or religious customs: Northeastern Beijing has several cultural and religious sites, and it’s crucial to respect the customs and traditions of these places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northeastern Beijing

Overall, Northeastern Beijing is considered a safe region for tourists. However, it’s essential to exercise caution and take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding dark, deserted areas at night, watching out for pickpockets, and not carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Northeastern Beijing

It’s advisable to carry travel insurance that includes medical coverage when traveling to China. It’s also important to take precautions against air pollution, such as carrying a mask, and avoiding street food.

Getting to Northeastern Beijing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northeastern Beijing

  • Beijing Capital International Airport: Located 30 km northeast of downtown Beijing. It’s the main airport in Beijing, and there are several transportation options available, including buses, taxis, and the subway.

Bus Stations Near Northeastern Beijing

  • Dongzhimen Bus Station: Located in Dongcheng District, this bus station provides bus services to several destinations within Beijing, as well as other cities in China.

Other Options

  • Subway: Beijing has an efficient subway system that connects most areas of the city. Northeastern Beijing has several subway lines that offer easy access to key destinations.
  • Taxis and Ride-hailing apps: Taxis are readily available in Beijing. However, it’s advisable to use ride-hailing apps such as Didi, which is similar to Uber, to avoid language barriers and scams.

How to Get Around in Northeastern Beijing

The best way to get around Northeastern Beijing is by subway or bus. Both options are affordable, efficient, and provide easy access to most key destinations in this part of the city.

Where to Go Next from Northeastern Beijing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Datong: Located in Shanxi Province, Datong is famous for its ancient Buddhist grottoes.
  • Tianjin: Located on China’s northeast coast, Tianjin is famous for its colonial buildings, seafood, and natural scenery.

Conclusion

Northeastern Beijing is a fascinating region of the city with plenty to see, do, and experience. From its historic sites to natural wonders to renowned cuisine, there is something for every kind of traveler. With this comprehensive guide, we hope you’re inspired to explore all that Northeastern Beijing has to offer.
