The Ultimate Travel Guide to Dongzhimen and Ditan, Beijing

Nestled in the heart of Beijing, Dongzhimen and Ditan are two historic neighborhoods brimming with culture, landmarks, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore Chinese history, hike scenic trails, or indulge in local cuisine, Dongzhimen and Ditan have something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around in Dongzhimen and Ditan.

List of Places to Visit in Dongzhimen and Ditan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ditan Park: This vast park is home to the Temple of Earth, an ancient temple that dates back to the Ming Dynasty. The temple is surrounded by lush greenery, winding paths, and serene lakes, making it an ideal spot for picnics, jogging, and Tai Chi.
  • Beijing Confucius Temple: This stunning temple was built over 700 years ago and is dedicated to Confucius, the great Chinese philosopher. Visitors can explore the temple’s ornate architecture, tranquil courtyards, and ancient scriptures.
  • Dongyue Temple: This Taoist temple is dedicated to the deity of Mount Tai, one of China’s most revered mountains. The temple features intricate carvings, winding staircases, and stunning views of Beijing’s skyline.

Natural Attractions of Dongzhimen and Ditan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jingshan Park: This picturesque park is home to Jingshan Hill, which offers panoramic views of Beijing’s Forbidden City. The park is also known for its cherry blossoms in the spring and vibrant foliage in the fall.
  • Shichahai Lake: This historic lake is surrounded by charming hutongs, ancient architecture, and traditional teahouses. Visitors can explore the lake by boat, rent a bike, or simply stroll along the shores.
  • Houhai Lake: Another scenic lake that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. It’s a popular spot for boating, kayaking, and enjoying traditional Beijing snacks at the numerous restaurants and cafes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dongzhimen and Ditan

  • Fragrant Hills Park: This scenic park is located in the western suburbs of Beijing and offers over 20 hiking trails that lead to stunning viewpoints and historic landmarks.
  • Miyun Reservoir: This large freshwater lake is less than two hours from Beijing’s city center and offers numerous biking trails that wind through forests, hills, and traditional rural villages.
  • Mutianyu Great Wall: For the ultimate hiking experience, visitors can hike along the stunning Mutianyu section of the Great Wall. The trail offers breathtaking views of the wall’s watchtowers, snaking paths, and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Dongzhimen and Ditan: Activities and Tours

  • Beijing Food Tour: Get a taste of Beijing’s iconic cuisine by taking a guided food tour that explores the city’s traditional eateries, markets, and street food vendors.
  • Hutong Bike Tour: Explore Beijing’s historic alleyways and traditional neighborhoods by taking a leisurely bike tour through the city’s winding hutongs.
  • Acrobatic Show: Catch a dazzling acrobatic performance that showcases the agility, strength, and artistry of Chinese acrobatics.

Food and Drink Scene of Dongzhimen and Ditan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peking Roast Duck: A classic Beijing dish that features crispy duck skin, tender meat, and thin pancakes wrapped with scallions and hoisin sauce.
  • Zhajiangmian: A popular noodle dish that is typically made with a fermented soybean paste and topped with diced vegetables and meat.
  • Hotpot: A communal cooking experience where diners cook their own meats, seafood, and vegetables in a hotpot of broth.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: A strong liquor that’s made from fermented grains and is commonly served at formal occasions and banquets.
  • Jasmine Tea: A fragrant tea that’s popular in Beijing and is often served with dim sum and other light meals.
  • Yogurt Drink: A refreshing drink that’s made from fermented yogurt and served cold.

Where to Stay in Dongzhimen and Ditan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Hyatt Beijing: A luxurious hotel that is located in the heart of Beijing and offers stunning views of the city’s skyline.
  • Cote Cour Hotel: A charming boutique hotel that is located in a traditional courtyard house and offers a tranquil escape from the city’s hustle and bustle.
  • Peking Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that is located in the heart of Dongzhimen and offers a social atmosphere and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Dongzhimen and Ditan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cloisonne Ware: This traditional enamelware is known for its vibrant colors and intricate designs and makes for a unique souvenir or gift.
  • Pearl Jewelry: Beijing is famous for its freshwater pearls, which are often used to make elegant jewelry pieces.
  • Silk Products: Beijing is also known for its high-quality silk products, including scarves, clothing, and bedding.

Local Culture and Customs of Dongzhimen and Ditan

  • Respect for the Elderly: Chinese people place a high value on respecting their elders and showing deference to those who are older or in authority.
  • Avoid Losing Face: Chinese people often avoid confrontation and strive to maintain a harmonious social atmosphere by avoiding actions that could cause someone to lose face or be embarrassed.
  • Gift-Giving: In Chinese culture, gift-giving is a common practice for expressing gratitude, building relationships, and showing respect. However, it’s important to choose an appropriate gift and to present it in a polite manner.

Best Time to Visit Dongzhimen and Ditan

The best time to visit Beijing is in the spring (March-May) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are fewer. However, be prepared for occasional sandstorms in the spring and heavy smog in the winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dongzhimen and Ditan

Do:

  • Learn Some Mandarin: Knowing some basic Mandarin phrases can go a long way in communication and showing respect to locals.
  • Embrace Local Customs: Take the time to learn about local customs and show respect for traditional practices and beliefs.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Beijing is known for its unique and flavorful cuisine, so don’t be afraid to sample some new dishes and flavors.

Don’t:

  • Touch the Head: In Chinese culture, touching someone’s head is considered disrespectful and should be avoided.
  • Wear Revealing Clothing: Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing or beachwear when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Disrespect National Symbols: Refrain from disrespecting national symbols or authorities, as this can be seen as disrespectful and could lead to legal repercussions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dongzhimen and Ditan

Beijing is a relatively safe city, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft, especially in crowded tourist areas. Avoid flashing valuable items and keep a close eye on your belongings at all times.

Health in Dongzhimen and Ditan

Beijing’s air pollution can be a concern for some visitors, particularly those with respiratory issues. Be sure to bring any necessary medications and consult a doctor before traveling if you have any health concerns.

Getting to Dongzhimen and Ditan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dongzhimen and Ditan

  • Beijing Capital International Airport: The nearest airport to Dongzhimen and Ditan, located approximately 20 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Dongzhimen and Ditan

  • Dongzhimen Bus Station: The nearest bus station, located in the heart of Dongzhimen.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Beijing and are a convenient way to get around the city. Just be sure to use a licensed taxi and to negotiate the fare before getting in the car.
  • Subway: Beijing’s subway system is extensive and inexpensive, with numerous stations located near popular tourist attractions.

How to Get Around in Dongzhimen and Ditan

  • Bike Rentals: Renting a bike is a popular way to explore Beijing’s historic neighborhoods, parks, and attractions. Bikes can be rented by the hour or day from numerous rental shops around the city.
  • Walking: Many of Beijing’s iconic landmarks and attractions are located within walking distance of each other, making walking a convenient and enjoyable way to explore the city.
  • Public Transportation: Beijing’s subway system and bus network are both extensive and convenient for getting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Dongzhimen and Ditan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Forbidden City: Located just a few kilometers from Dongzhimen and Ditan, the Forbidden City is a must-visit attraction that showcases Chinese history, architecture, and culture.
  • Summer Palace: This stunning palace complex features beautiful gardens, pavilions, and lakes and is located just 12 kilometers northwest of Beijing’s city center.
  • Chengde: A historic city located in Hebei Province, approximately 250 kilometers from Beijing. Chengde is known for its imperial resorts, temples, and scenic parks.

Conclusion

Dongzhimen and Ditan are two of Beijing’s most vibrant and historic neighborhoods, offering visitors a wealth of cultural landmarks, natural beauty, and exciting activities. From exploring traditional hutongs and ancient temples to hiking scenic trails and indulging in local cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Dongzhimen and Ditan. We hope this travel guide has provided you with everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this dynamic part of China.
