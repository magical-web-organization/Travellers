Xicheng Travel Guide - Complete Tourist Information

Situated in the heart of Beijing, Xicheng offers a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of China. This district is steeped in history, with a plethora of museums, temples, and palaces to explore. Not only does it offer a glimpse into China’s past, but it also offers a taste of modern China, with plenty of shops, restaurants, and markets. This travel guide will help you get the most out of your visit to Xicheng and discover what makes it such a fascinating district.

List of Places to Visit in Xicheng

Xicheng is filled with many significant landmarks and attractions that display its rich cultural heritage. Here are some not to be missed places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Forbidden City: A UNESCO World Heritage site that served as the imperial palace for nearly five centuries.
  • Beijing’s Ancient Observatory: Aoluguya Hutong is home to China’s oldest observatory.
  • Prince Gong’s Mansion: Built in 1777, this mansion is one of the most well-preserved imperial residences.

Natural Attractions of Xicheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xicheng Park: This park offers a relaxing retreat from the busy city, with stunning lakes and traditional Chinese pavilions.
  • Beihai Park: A popular attraction for locals and tourists, this park is home to the White Dagoba and an island in the middle of the lake.
  • Shichahai Lake: Located in the heart of the old city, Shichahai Lake is known for its beautiful views and historic buildings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xicheng

Cycling enthusiasts and hikers will relish exploring Xicheng, which offers various scenic routes for them to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Hutong Bike Tour: Cycle along the narrow alleys of Xicheng district, observing traditional Chinese architecture.

Best Things to Do in Xicheng: Activities and Tours

Xicheng district offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours that offer something for everyone, such as:

  • Kung Fu Show: Watch a traditional Chinese Kung Fu performance at Red Theatre.
  • Beijing Opera: Experience Beijing Opera at Huguang Guild Hall or Chang’an Grand Theatre, two of the best places to attend this unique Chinese art form.
  • Acrobatics Show: Watch the breathtaking skills of acrobats under the soaring high ceilings of Chaoyang Theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xicheng

To maximize your visit to Xicheng, we recommend the following itinerary for a week-long stay:

Day Itinerary
1 Visit the Forbidden City
2 Explore Beihai Park, and hike the Yingshan Mountain Trail
3 Visit the National Centre for the Performing Arts (NCPA)
4 Discover the Beijing Ancient Observatory and Prince Gong’s Mansion
5 Cycle along the Hutongs and Shichahai Lake
6 Take a day trip to the Great Wall of China
7 Explore the Xicheng Park and the nearby markets

Food and Drink Scene of Xicheng

As one of Beijing’s oldest districts, Xicheng offers an array of dining experiences. Here are some of the best places to try traditional local food:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Zhajiangmian: A very popular Chinese dish made of thick wheat noodles with soybean paste.
  • Roast Duck: A Beijing classic dish that slowly roasted the whole duck over an open fire until the skin becomes crispy and golden.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: A type of Chinese liquor made from grains or sorghum.
  • Hua Diao Wine: A sweet and fragrant Chinese wine made from sticky rice.

Where to Stay in Xicheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xicheng district offers plenty of options for accommodation, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels.

Some of the top-rated hotels are:

  • Grand Hyatt Beijing: A luxury hotel located near the Tiananmen Square.
  • Shichahai Shadow Art Performance Hotel: Offers an authentic Chinese cultural experience with its unique art performances.
  • North Garden Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located centrally in Xicheng.

What to Buy in Xicheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Xicheng can bring home many souvenirs and local products that depict the unique culture of the district, such as:

  • Tea: Xicheng is known for its tea, which makes an excellent souvenir for tea enthusiasts.
  • Cloisonneware: A traditional Chinese craft that features intricate designs on copper plates.

Local Culture and Customs of Xicheng

Xicheng’s culture and customs epitomize the essence of Beijing’s old city. Visitors should address elderly citizens as “Ayi” or “Uncle” as a sign of respect. Beijing residents are known for their friendliness and hospitality towards visitors, so don’t be shy to ask for directions or recommendations.

Best Time to Visit Xicheng

The best time to visit Xicheng is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. Avoid travelling during the summer months, as it can get hot and humid, and the winter months can get frigid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xicheng

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission.
  • Haggle excessively when shopping in markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xicheng

Xicheng is a safe district, with low levels of crime. As in any city, visitors should be mindful of their personal belongings, especially when travelling during peak tourist season.

Health in Xicheng

Xicheng is home to various hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Medical services are readily available, and most doctors and medical staff speak English.

Getting to Xicheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Xicheng via various means of transportation.

Airports Near Xicheng

  • Beijing Capital International Airport: Located 35km northeast of Xicheng, it’s the primary entry point to Beijing.

Bus Stations Near Xicheng

  • Beijing Central Bus Station: Provides bus services to major cities in China.

Other Options

  • Train Stations: Beijing has several train stations, providing connections to other cities within China.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and ride-hailing services are widely available in Beijing.

How to Get Around in Xicheng

Visitors can get around Xicheng via various modes of transportation.

  • Public Transit: Xicheng is connected to the Beijing Subway’s Line 1 and Line 2, providing access to various landmarks and attractions.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available, and most drivers understand simple English.

Where to Go Next from Xicheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Xicheng is well-positioned for visitors to explore other parts of China. Here are some places worth visiting:

  • The Great Wall of China: One of China’s most well-known landmarks, it’s just a day trip from Xicheng.
  • Tianjin: A nearby coastal city famous for its European-style architecture.
  • The Summer Palace: Located in the Haidian district, it’s another UNESCO World Heritage Site that’s worth a visit.

Conclusion

Xicheng district showcases China’s rich cultural heritage, offering an array of landmarks, attractions, food, and entertainment. Visitors to Xicheng can immerse themselves in the local culture while also exploring vibrant city life, making it an ideal destination for solo travellers, couples, and families alike.
