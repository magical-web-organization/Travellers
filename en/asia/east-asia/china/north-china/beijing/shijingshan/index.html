Introduction to Shijingshan

Shijingshan is a suburban district located in the western part of Beijing, the capital city of China. It covers an area of 85 square kilometers and has a population of over 500,000 people. The district is known for its cultural attractions and natural scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Shijingshan

Shijingshan offers a variety of attractions for travelers to explore. The following is a list of must-visit places in the district.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shijingshan Amusement Park: This amusement park is one of the most popular in Beijing and attracts visitors from across the country and beyond. It features a wide range of rides and attractions, including roller coasters, water rides, and a Ferris wheel.
  • Shijingshan Geological Museum: This museum showcases the geological history of China and the world. It features a collection of fossils, minerals, and other geological specimens from various periods of Earth’s history.
  • Buddhist Temples: There are several Buddhist temples in Shijingshan that are worth a visit, including the Guang’an Temple and the Fahai Temple.

Natural Attractions of Shijingshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fengtai Park: This park is located in the southern part of Shijingshan and offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. It features a lake, walking trails, and a variety of trees and flowers.
  • Xiangshan Park: This park is located in the northwestern part of Shijingshan and is known for its mountain hiking trails and scenic views of downtown Beijing.
  • Xishan National Forest Park: This park is located in the western part of Shijingshan and features a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shijingshan

Shijingshan offers several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the most popular routes:

  • Fengtai Park Walking Trail: This trail circles the lake in Fengtai Park and provides a tranquil escape in the heart of the city.
  • Xiangshan Park Hiking Trail: This trail leads up to the summit of Xiangshan Mountain and offers breathtaking views of Beijing and the surrounding countryside.
  • Xishan Mountain Biking Trail: This trail takes bikers through the rugged terrain of Xishan National Forest Park and is suited for experienced riders.

Best Things to Do in Shijingshan: Activities and Tours

Shijingshan offers a variety of activities and tours for travelers to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in the district:

  • Shijingshan Amusement Park: This park is a must-visit for thrill-seekers and families with children.
  • Buddhist Temple Tours: Take a tour of the various Buddhist temples in Shijingshan to learn more about the local culture and history.
  • Hiking and Biking Tours: Join a guided tour of the hiking and biking trails in Shijingshan to discover the natural beauty of the district.

Food and Drink Scene of Shijingshan

Shijingshan offers a range of local food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jingyun Roujiamo: This dish is a local specialty of Shijingshan and is essentially a Chinese-style “hamburger” made with flatbread, pork, and various spices and sauces.
  • Shui Jian Bao: These pan-fried pork buns are crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside.
  • Ma La Tang: This spicy soup is made with a variety of meats, vegetables, and spices and is a popular street food in Shijingshan.

Local Beverages

  • Jingxiangzi: This herbal tea is made with a mixture of Chinese medicinal herbs and is said to have various health benefits.
  • Beijing Yanjing Beer: This local beer is popular throughout Beijing and goes well with a variety of local dishes.

Safety and Health

Shijingshan is a relatively safe and healthy place to visit. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Shijingshan

  • Be Aware of Scams: Always be aware of potential scams and be cautious when giving money to strangers.
  • Stay Safe at Night: Avoid walking alone at night and stay in well-lit, populated areas.
  • Be Vigilant in Crowded Places: Be aware of pickpockets and other types of theft in crowded places.

Health in Shijingshan

  • Water Safety: Do not drink tap water in Shijingshan. Stick to bottled or boiled water.
  • Stay Hydrated: Always make sure to stay hydrated, especially during hot weather.
  • Be Prepared for Altitude: If hiking in the mountains, be aware of the potential for altitude sickness and come prepared with medication as needed.
