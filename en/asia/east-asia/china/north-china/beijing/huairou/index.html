Introduction to Huairou

Huairou is a fascinating district of Beijing, located in the northeastern part of the city, about 60 kilometers from the center. It is situated at the base of the Yan Mountains and has an abundant scenic beauty found nowhere else in the region. The area is famous for its natural landscapes, including hills, mountains, forests, and rivers, making it a popular destination for domestic and foreign travelers alike. Huairou is the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, and it has so much to offer - from historical sites to outdoor adventures. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive guide to Huairou, providing you with all the information you need to plan your dream trip to this beautiful place.

List of Places to Visit in Huairou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hongluo Temple

Hongluo Temple is a famous Buddhist temple located at the foot of the Hongluo Mountain Scenic Area in Huairou. This ancient temple dates back more than 1,000 years and is renowned for its beautiful natural surroundings, rich history, and ancient architecture. Here, visitors can admire various traditional Chinese Buddhist art such as brick carvings, stone sculptures, and murals. The temple has a beautiful garden with an artificial waterfall and a small corridor for sightseeing, making it a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Mutianyu Great Wall

Mutianyu Great Wall is considered one of the best-preserved sections of the Great Wall of China. Spanning more than 2 kilometers and comprising 22 watchtowers, it is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the area. The wall’s structure is remarkably well-preserved, and visitors can explore the surrounding towers, walls, and battlements, all while enjoying some stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Qinglong Gorge

The Qinglong Gorge, located in the northern part of Huairou District, is famous for its beautiful scenery, which features lush vegetation on either side of the valley, rock formations, and several waterfalls. The area is ideal for hiking and exploring the natural scenery. At the entrance to the gorge, visitors can visit the Qinglong Temple, an ancient temple with a history dating back more than 1000 years. From here, you can also take a boat ride down the river for a unique perspective of the valley.

Natural Attractions of Huairou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hongluo Mountain Scenic Area

The Hongluo Mountain Scenic Area is located on the northern side of Huairou and has a total area of about 28 square kilometers. It is a beautiful area with scenic views and clear water. The area is known for its dense forests, rolling hills, and tranquil surroundings, which make it an ideal location for hiking, camping, and rock climbing. The Hongluo Temple, mentioned earlier in this guide, is located in the scenic area, and visitors can easily spend a whole day exploring both.

Yanqi Lake

Yanqi Lake is located in the north of Huairou and is known for its crystal clear water and picturesque scenery. The lake is surrounded by mountains, and there are several ancient buildings to admire. Visitors can take a stroll along the lake shore, rent a boat and paddle around the lake or have a picnic with friends and family.

Huairou Reservoir

Huairou Reservoir is one of the biggest man-made lakes in Beijing, covering an area of more than 200 square kilometers, and it is the primary source of drinking water for this region’s residents. Undoubtedly, this beautiful lake also serves as a tourist destination for inland sailing, freshwater fishing, outdoor water sports, and enjoying beautiful sunrises and sunsets.

Local Culture and Customs of Huairou

Huairou is a small town deeply immersed in ancient Chinese culture. The locals are friendly and hospitable, making visitors feel at home. As you explore Huairou, it’s essential to respect its customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly, follow local etiquette, and refrain from littering, as China is working to become a greener country.

Best Time to Visit Huairou

The best time to visit Huairou is from April to October when the weather is mild and the natural scenery is at its best. During these months, the temperature is warm, and you can avoid the cold and windy winter weather that sometimes occurs in the northern parts of China.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huairou

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs
  2. Dress modestly when visiting temples or historical sites
  3. Keep the environment clean

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public areas
  2. Touch or remove artifacts in historical sites
  3. Take photos of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Huairou

Huairou is a relatively safe area, but visitors should still take necessary safety precautions to avoid accidents or thefts. Ensure that you have travel insurance, as medical attention can be expensive. Be cautious in environments where there is high water or during heavy rain or snow seasons.

Health in Huairou

While Huairou is a safe place to visit, it’s always important to consider your health. Bring any necessary medication and pay extra attention to food hygiene. Avoid eating uncooked or undercooked food and drink only bottled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Huairou: Transportation Options and Logistics

The most convenient way to get to Huairou is by private car or taxi, but this can be expensive. Alternatively, visitors can take the subway to Huairou North Station, then take a bus or taxi to their destination. There are also intercity trains running from Beijing to Huairou.
