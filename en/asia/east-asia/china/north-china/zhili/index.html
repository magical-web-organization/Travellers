Introduction to Zhili

Zhili, located in North China, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This region is full of history, culture, and natural beauty. Zhili was once home to the imperial capital of Beijing, and has been an important centre for culture and commerce in the region for centuries. With its rich historical sites, great food scene, and stunning natural scenery, Zhili has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Zhili

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beijing Ancient Observatory: The Beijing Ancient Observatory dates back over 500 years, and features numerous astronomical instruments from China’s past.
  • Baoding Double Pagoda: This impressive pair of pagodas is located in the Baoding Buddhist temple and is over 1,000 years old.
  • Kaiyuan Temple: This ancient temple dates back to the Tang dynasty and is one of the most visited temples in the area.
  • Puji Temple: This traditional Buddhist temple has a history dating back over 1,000 years, and is well known for its stunning architecture.

Natural Attractions of Zhili: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Juma River: The Juma River is a popular spot for rafting, kayaking, and fishing, and is surrounded by scenic hiking trails.
  • Guangfu Temple: Located in the mountains, this temple is known for its stunning views over the surrounding countryside.
  • Cangyan Mountain: This national park is one of the most beautiful in the area, with its jagged peaks and stunning views.
  • Baishi Mountain: This mountain range is a popular spot for hiking and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhili

Zhili is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Cangyan Mountain Hike: This is a challenging hike that takes you to the top of the Cangyan Mountain range, with views over the surrounding countryside.
  • Juma River Bike Trail: This trail takes you along the stunning Juma River, through scenic countryside and past ancient temples and ruins.
  • Huanghuacheng Great Wall Hike: This hike takes you along one of the most scenic sections of the Great Wall of China, with stunning views over the surrounding countryside.
  • Baodi Mountain Hike: This hike takes you through dense forests and up steep hills to reach the summit of Baodi Mountain, with panoramic views over the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Zhili: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Imperial Palace: This ancient palace was once home to the imperial families of China, and today it is a popular tourist attraction.
  • Attend a Peking Opera performance: This traditional Chinese art form is a must-see while in Zhili, with its colorful costumes and unique music.
  • Explore the Night Markets: Zhili is famous for its night markets, where you can find a wide range of local foods, souvenirs, and entertainment.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: For a unique perspective on the natural beauty of Zhili, consider taking a hot air balloon ride over the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhili

Day 1: Explore the Imperial Palace and Beijing Ancient Observatory Day 2: Visit Baodi Mountain and hike to the summit. Day 3: Take a day trip to see the Baoding Double Pagoda and Kaiyuan Temple. Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic countryside. Day 5: Enjoy the night markets in Zhili. Day 6: Explore the Juma River bike trail and go kayaking or fishing. Day 7: Relax at Guangfu Temple and take in the stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhili

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beijing Duck: A famous dish of the region, roasted duck is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, served with pancakes, green onions, and sweet bean sauce.
  • Baozi: These steamed buns stuffed with meat, vegetables, or sweet red bean paste are a popular breakfast food in Zhili.
  • Zha jiang mian: A staple in the local diet, this dish consists of thick noodles topped with sauce made from fermented soybeans and minced meat.
  • Ma la tang: A spicy and savory hot pot dish that features a variety of ingredients cooked in a flavorful broth.

Local Beverages

  • Huangjiu: This is a traditional yellow rice wine that has been produced in China for thousands of years.
  • Jasmine Tea: This fragrant tea is a popular choice in China and is enjoyed for its delicate flavor and aroma.
  • Er Guo Tou: This strong distilled spirit is a popular choice for drinking in Zhili, with an alcohol content of up to 56%.

Where to Stay in Zhili: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Crowne Plaza Hotel: This five-star hotel is located in the city center and offers luxurious rooms and amenities.
  • 7 Days Inn: This budget hotel chain offers clean and comfortable rooms and is a great choice for travelers on a budget.
  • Zhili Guesthouse: This traditional guesthouse is located near many of the top attractions in Zhili, and offers a unique cultural experience.

What to Buy in Zhili: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cloisonne: This traditional Chinese art form involves the use of copper wire and colored enamel to create beautiful designs.
  • Silk Products: Zhili is known for its high-quality silk products, including scarves, clothing, and bedding.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are plenty of markets and shops in Zhili where you can find beautiful handmade crafts, including pottery, paper cutting, and embroidery.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhili

Zhili has a rich history and culture, with many customs and traditions still practiced today. One of the most important holidays in the region is the Chinese New Year, celebrated with fireworks, dragon dance performances, and traditional foods. Respect for elders and family values are also important cultural values in Zhili.

Best Time to Visit Zhili

The best time to visit Zhili depends on your interests and preferences. Generally, spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) are the most pleasant times of year, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer (June to August) can be hot and humid, and winter (December to February) can be cold and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhili

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be aware of local customs and traditions, and be respectful of the culture.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Zhili is known for its delicious food, so make sure to try some local dishes while you are here.
  • Be Prepared: Bring appropriate clothing and gear if you plan to explore the outdoors, including hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Cultural Sites: Treat cultural sites with respect and avoid any disrespectful behavior.
  • Be Disruptive: Avoid being disruptive in public places, and be mindful of others around you.
  • Forget to Bargain: Bargaining is a common practice in markets and shops in Zhili, so don’t forget to negotiate prices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhili

Generally, Zhili is a safe place to visit, but travelers should still take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Be aware of your surroundings, and keep your valuables secure.

Health in Zhili

Travelers should take precautions to avoid illness while in Zhili. Be sure to drink boiled or bottled water, and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.

Getting to Zhili: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zhili is easy to reach by public transportation, with a variety of options available.

Airports Near Zhili

  • Beijing Capital International Airport: This is the closest international airport to Zhili, and is located about 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Zhili

  • Zhili Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the city of Zhili, with frequent services to other cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: There are several train stations in the region, with services to other cities in China.
  • Private Car: Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Zhili

Getting around Zhili is easy, with plenty of transportation options available. Public buses and taxis are readily available, and bike rentals are also common.

Where to Go Next from Zhili: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beijing: China’s capital city is just a few hours away by train or bus, and is home to numerous historical and cultural attractions.
  • Shanghai: Known for its skyline and modern architecture, Shanghai is one of China’s most popular destinations.
  • Qinhuangdao: This coastal city is known for its seafood, beaches, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Zhili is one of China’s best-kept secrets, with its rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are a foodie, a history buff, or an outdoor enthusiast, there is something for everyone in Zhili. With its great transportation links, a wide range of accommodation options, and friendly locals, Zhili is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore China’s fascinating culture and landscapes.
