Introduction to Qingdao

Located in the northeastern province of Shandong, Qingdao is a bustling coastal city known for its picturesque beaches, rich history, and delicious cuisine. The city has a unique blend of Chinese and European influences, thanks to its former status as a German colony in the late 1800s. Today, Qingdao is a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers looking to experience the best of what China has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Qingdao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Tsingtao Beer Museum: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the history and culture of China’s most famous beer, with exhibits showcasing the brewing process, marketing campaigns, and even a beer-tasting room.
  • Zhanqiao Pier: One of Qingdao’s most iconic landmarks, this historic pier offers stunning views of the surrounding ocean and nearby buildings, and was once a vital transportation hub for the city.
  • Qingdao Protestant Church: Built in 1908 by German missionaries, this beautiful church is a testament to Qingdao’s unique cultural heritage, with its blend of Gothic and Chinese architectural styles.

Natural Attractions of Qingdao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laoshan Mountain: A popular destination for hiking and outdoor recreation, Laoshan Mountain boasts stunning landscapes and breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Qingdao Beaches: With its long stretches of sandy coastline and crystal-clear waters, Qingdao is known for its beautiful beaches, including the sprawling Golden Beach and the upscale Beach No. 3.
  • Qingdao Botanical Garden: This serene green space, located on the southern outskirts of the city, offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of urban life, with picturesque walking paths, ponds, and gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qingdao

  • Laoshan Mountain: As mentioned, this mountain is an excellent spot for hiking and scenic walks. Its natural beauty makes it a popular destination with locals and tourists alike.
  • Qingdao Coastline: Cycling is the perfect way to take in the city’s stunning ocean views. The highly recommended routes include along the coastline from Zhanqiao Pier to May Fourth Square.

Best Things to Do in Qingdao: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Beer Tour: Qingdao’s beer history is well-known, and touring the city’s breweries with a knowledgeable guide is a fun and educational experience.
  • Explore Qingdao’s German Architecture: The city’s unique cultural heritage is reflected in its architecture. Exploring the beautiful buildings of the former German concession is a fantastic way to spend a day in Qingdao.
  • Visit Qingdao Aquarium: This state-of-the-art attraction boasts thousands of marine animals, beautiful exhibits, and a 4D cinema experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Qingdao

Must-Try Dishes

  • Qingdao-style Seafood: With its coastal location, Qingdao is famous for its fresh seafood, including fish, clams, and shrimp.
  • Roast Duck: A traditional dish with a distinct Qingdao taste.
  • Hot Pot: A communal dish served at the table, with a selection of meats and vegetables cooked in a fragrant broth.

Local Beverages

  • Tsingtao Beer: The famous beer dominates the local bar scene and is brewed here. Drinking beer has become a unique cultural symbol of Qingdao.
  • Chinese Tea: Drinking tea is a common ritual in China, with many tea shops found throughout the city.
  • Fruit Juice: Qingdao has an abundance of fruit shops serving fresh juice, a must-try for travelers seeking refreshment.

Where to Stay in Qingdao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Luxury Hotels: The city boasts several high-end hotels, including the InterContinental Qingdao, which towers over Olympic Sailing Centre and the St. Regis Qingdao, located near the beach.
  • Budget-Friendly Hostels: Qingdao has plenty of budget-friendly hostels located throughout the city, offering affordable accommodations for budget-conscious travelers.
  • Homestays or Guesthouses: A more intimate experience, homestays, and guesthouses are often run and operated by locals, making it a unique way to immerse oneself in the local culture and customs.

What to Buy in Qingdao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tsingtao Beer Memorabilia: The Tsingtao brand memorabilia items such as T-shirts and coasters, make the perfect keepsakes for beer enthusiasts.
  • Tiger Skin Plaster: The traditional remedy plaster is popular amongst locals and visitors alike.
  • Seafood Products: Fresh or preserved seafood, such as fish, anchovies, and shrimp, can be purchased from local markets as they make excellent gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Qingdao

  • Language: Mandarin Chinese is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
  • Traditions and Customs: Visitors should be mindful of respecting the local customs and etiquette, such as taking shoes off while entering traditional buildings, and be aware that public displays of affection are not customary nor appropriate in China.

Best Time to Visit Qingdao

The best time to visit Qingdao is during the shoulder seasons between March and May or September-November, when the temperatures are mild and the skies clear, making it ideal weather for outdoor activities. Chinese New Year and National Holidays periods are often busy, and visitors should expect crowds and higher room rates during these periods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qingdao

Do:

  • Try local dishes: The cuisine in Qingdao is unique and offers a combination of Chinese and European influences.
  • Explore the city’s beer culture: Qingdao is known for its beer, and visitors should not leave without visiting the beer museum or indulging in a cold brew.
  • Take time to immerse yourself in the culture: Qingdao is a city with a rich history and unique heritage. When visiting, take the time to learn about the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the culture: It is important to be mindful of the local customs and traditions while visiting Qingdao.
  • Be careless with your belongings: Like any tourist destination, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qingdao

Qingdao is relatively safe and has lower crime rates compared to other large cities in China. However, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and be cautious when venturing into unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Qingdao

The tap water in Qingdao is not potable, so it is recommended that visitors drink bottled water. It is also advisable to bring essential medications if necessary and take out health insurance coverage.

Getting to Qingdao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qingdao

The city’s main airport is Qingdao Liuting International Airport (TAO). It serves both domestic and international flights from various destinations such as Japan, South Korea, Southeast Asia, Russia, and North America.

Bus Stations Near Qingdao

Qingdao has several long-distance bus stations serving various destinations around Shandong Province and neighboring provinces.

Other Options

Several train stations serve Qingdao, and travelers can arrive from several major cities across China, such as Beijing and Shanghai.

How to Get Around in Qingdao

Public transportation includes buses, taxis, and metro services, with metro services expanding quickly in the city. Taxis are affordable, but visitors should ensure they have enough cash as most drivers do not accept credit cards. The city’s bike-sharing system is also a popular way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Qingdao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Weifang is one hour from Qingdao and is known for its kite and Chinese paper-cutting industries. The city’s annual kite festival is held annually in April and is a popular tourist destination.
  • Qufu is the hometown of Confucius, one of China’s most famous philosophers. There are several tourist attractions, including the Temple of Confucius, and the Kong Family Mansion. It is approximately 2 hours by train from Qingdao.

Conclusion

Qingdao is a fascinating destination, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and culinary delights. With its perfect mix of history and modern-day amenities, Qingdao should feature on every traveler’s bucket list. Plan the trip of a lifetime and make memories that will last forever in this lovely city of North China.
