Introduction to Jinan

Jinan, the capital of Shandong province, is a city steeped in history and culture. Also known as the “City of Springs,” Jinan is home to numerous natural springs and boasts a well-preserved ancient city wall. With a history dating back to the Neolithic period, Jinan offers a unique blend of ancient culture and modern development. With this guide, you can plan the perfect itinerary to explore this fascinating city in North China.

List of Places to Visit in Jinan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Baotu Spring Park: This park offers a scenic view of the famous Baotu Spring, which has been regarded as the “first spring under heaven” for centuries. This location offers the perfect opportunity to relax and enjoy nature.
  2. Daming Lake Park: This park is centered around Daming Lake and is surrounded by beautiful pavilions and gardens. Visitors can explore the surrounding ancient architecture, including the famous Xiaoqingcheng, a palace from the Qing Dynasty.
  3. The Lingyan Temple: Built during the Jin Dynasty over 1,600 years ago, this Buddhist temple is a must-visit for those interested in Chinese religious culture.

Natural Attractions of Jinan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Tai: Located just outside of Jinan, Mount Tai is one of the Five Great Mountains of China and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can hike to the peak to see stunning views and explore the ancient temples and shrines along the way.
  2. Qingdao: A coastal city just three hours from Jinan, Qingdao offers a unique combination of natural beauty and history. Visitors can explore the beaches, hike in the nearby mountains, and visit the famous Tsingtao Brewery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jinan

  1. Quancheng Square to Baotu Spring Park: This route takes visitors through the heart of Jinan and offers stunning views of the city’s parks and ancient architecture.
  2. Daming Lake to Lingyan Temple: This route offers the opportunity to explore some of the most significant cultural landmarks in Jinan while enjoying nature and outdoor exercise.

Best Things to Do in Jinan: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore local cuisine: Jinan is known for its tasty cuisine, including Shandong-style dishes like sweet and sour carp, Tai’an-style braised chicken, and beef soup with crispy pancake.
  2. Visit local markets: For those looking to experience the daily life of Jinan locals, a visit to one of the city’s markets is essential. The Kuanhouli market offers the opportunity to taste traditional snacks and buy souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jinan

Day 3: Hike the route from Daming Lake to Lingyan Temple
Day 4: Day trip to Mount Tai

Food and Drink Scene of Jinan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Quancheng roast duck: This dish features whole ducks that are carefully roasted and served with various sides, including condiments and pancakes.
  2. Shahe roast chicken: This dish is made by steaming the chicken with herbs and then roasting it to create a unique flavor and texture.

Local Beverages

  1. Jinan brewed beer: Jinan has a long history of brewing beer, and the city is home to a variety of breweries that visitors can tour and sample beer.
  2. Ya pear juice: Known for its health benefits, Ya pear juice is a popular drink in Jinan and throughout Shandong province.

Where to Stay in Jinan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hanting Hotel: Located in the city center, this hotel offers modern amenities and a convenient location.
  2. 7Days Inn: This budget-friendly hotel chain has locations throughout the city and provides basic amenities for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Jinan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Lacquerware: Jinan is known for its lacquerware, which is produced using traditional methods and is a popular souvenir for visitors.
  2. Pearls: Jinan is located near the ocean, and visitors can find high-quality pearls and jewelry at reasonable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Jinan

  1. Language: Mandarin is the official language of China, but many locals in Jinan also speak Shandong dialect.
  2. Tipping: While not mandatory, tipping is becoming more common in China. If you receive excellent service, a standard tip is 10% of the total cost.

Best Time to Visit Jinan

The best time to visit Jinan is during the spring and autumn months. The spring months of March to May are ideal for enjoying the blooming flowers and mild temperatures. The autumn months of September to November offer cooler temperatures and beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jinan

Do:

  1. Do try to learn some Mandarin phrases before your visit.
  2. Do carry toilet paper and hand sanitizer, as these items may not be available in public restrooms.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t disrespect local customs, including touching or moving religious artifacts.
  2. Don’t eat or drink excessively in public places, as this behavior may be frowned upon.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jinan

Jinan is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing. Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables close at hand.

Health in Jinan

Visitors should take standard health precautions, including washing hands frequently and avoiding local tap water.

Getting to Jinan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jinan

  1. Jinan Yaoqiang International Airport: Located just 20 kilometers outside the city center, this airport offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Jinan

  1. Jinan Long-Distance Bus Station: Located in the northeast of the city, this bus station offers long-distance buses to nearby cities and provinces.

Other Options

  1. Train: Visitors can also take the train to Jinan, as the city is a major railway hub in North China.

How to Get Around in Jinan

  1. Public Transportation: The city has a comprehensive public transportation system that includes buses and a metro system.
  2. Taxi: Taxis are widely available throughout the city and are a convenient way to get around, but be sure to take a licensed taxi and negotiate the fare before getting in.

Where to Go Next from Jinan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Qufu: The birthplace of Confucius and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  2. Tai’an: Home to Mount Tai and other scenic areas.
  3. Qingdao: A coastal city famous for its beer, beaches, and seafood.

Conclusion

With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can explore Jinan and its surrounding areas with confidence. From natural springs to ancient buildings, Jinan is a city full of unique experiences and cultural significance. Plan your trip today and experience the best of North China!
