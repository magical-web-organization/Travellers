Penglai, located in the eastern part of Shandong Province, is a charming coastal city that’s steeped in history, legend, and natural beauty. According to Chinese mythology, Penglai is one of the legendary “Four Seas’ Islands” where the goddesses of the sea lived. Today, Penglai is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and delicious seafood. Whether you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Penglai has something to offer for every type of traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide to Penglai, we’ll share with you the top things to do, see, eat, and buy in this captivating city.

Introduction to Penglai

Penglai is a city with a long and fascinating history. The city has been inhabited for over 2,000 years, and it has played an important role in Chinese history, particularly during the Tang and Song dynasties. Penglai is also known for its connection to Chinese mythology, as it is believed that the goddesses of the sea lived on one of the Four Seas’ Islands, which is thought to be located near Penglai.

List of Places to Visit in Penglai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in Chinese history, Penglai has plenty of museums and historical places to explore. One of the most popular is the Penglai Pavilion, which was first built during the Tang dynasty, and rebuilt several times since then. The pavilion offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape, and it’s a great place to learn about the history and culture of the region.

Another must-visit attraction is the Danya Mountain Scenic Area, where you can see ancient shrines, pavilions, and temples. The area is also home to the famous Chaoyang Temple, which was established in the Song dynasty and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Penglai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Penglai is also surrounded by natural beauty, from its sandy beaches to its rocky cliffs, and everything in between. One of the best places to take in the scenery is the Penglai Seawater Baths, where you can relax in natural hot springs while taking in the breathtaking views of the sea.

For those looking for outdoor adventures, Penglai has plenty of options. The Yantai Penglai International Seashore Holiday Resort is a popular destination for swimming, surfing, and other water sports, while the Shihuiyu Scenic Area offers hiking trails and stunning views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Penglai: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting the city’s attractions, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to take part in while in Penglai. One popular option is to take a fishing tour where you can catch your own seafood and have it cooked up for you on the spot. For something more adventurous, you can take a paragliding or hang-gliding tour to get a bird’s-eye view of the city and its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Penglai

Must-Try Dishes

Penglai is known for its fresh seafood, and one of the most popular dishes to try is the Seafood Hotpot. The hotpot is filled with a variety of fresh seafood, along with vegetables and noodles. Another popular dish is the Steamed Prawns, which are cooked with garlic and ginger for a delicious and flavorful meal.

Local Beverages

The local drink of Penglai is the Lu Mountain Spring Water, which is said to have healing properties thanks to its high mineral content. It’s also a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Penglai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Penglai, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. If you’re looking for a unique experience, consider staying at one of the city’s traditional guesthouses, where you can immerse yourself in the local culture and customs.

What to Buy in Penglai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Penglai is known for its fresh seafood, and you can bring home some local products such as dried seafood, fish skin, and fish sauce. You can also find traditional Chinese crafts like bamboo baskets, lacquerware, and paper cuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Penglai

Penglai has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. The local people are friendly and welcoming, and it’s common to greet each other with a handshake or a nod. It’s important to dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Penglai

The best time to visit Penglai is in the spring and autumn, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer can be hot and humid, while the winter can be cold and windy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Penglai

Do:

  • Explore the cultural and natural attractions of the city
  • Try the fresh seafood and local drinks
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage cultural sites
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting temples or cultural sites
  • Engage in inappropriate behavior or language

Safety and Health

Safety in Penglai

Penglai is a relatively safe city, but it’s important to take normal precautions like keeping your valuables safe and avoiding unlit areas at night. It’s also important to be aware of the tide when visiting the beach.

Health in Penglai

Penglai has a modern healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the city.

Getting to Penglai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Penglai

The nearest airport is Yantai Penglai International Airport, which has flights to major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Penglai

There are several bus stations in Penglai, with buses to nearby cities like Yantai and Weihai.

Other Options

You can also take a train from Beijing or Shanghai to Yantai, and then take a bus or taxi to Penglai.

How to Get Around in Penglai

There are buses and taxis available in Penglai, as well as bicycle rentals for those who want to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Penglai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider, including:

  • Yantai, a coastal city famous for its wine and seafood
  • Weihai, another coastal city with beautiful beaches and natural scenery
  • Qufu, the birthplace of Confucius and a UNESCO World Heritage site

Conclusion

Penglai is a city with a rich history and culture, stunning natural beauty, and delicious seafood. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s historical sites, experiencing its natural wonders, or immersing yourself in the local culture, Penglai has something to offer for every traveler. Plan your visit with this comprehensive travel guide, and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
