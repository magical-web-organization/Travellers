Introduction to Qinhuangdao

Located in the eastern part of Hebei Province, Qinhuangdao is a coastal city in China. Known for its beautiful seaside scenery and rich cultural heritage, the city attracts millions of tourists from all over the world every year. Qinhuangdao has a long history with a range of cultural, art, and historical marvels that cater to every traveler’s preference.

List of Places to Visit in Qinhuangdao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or an art lover, Qinhuangdao has plenty of unique cultural and historical attractions that you should add to your itinerary.

Shanhaiguan Pass

If you’re interested in China’s history, Shanhaiguan Pass is where you’ll find remnants of the Great Wall of China. Shanhaiguan pass was considered the easternmost end of the Great Wall when it was first built to prevent enemies from invading China. The pass was subsequently modified and extended throughout various dynasties in China to protect the empire.

Qinhuangdao Museum

Located in the heart of the city, the Qinhuangdao Museum is known for housing some of the rarest historical artifacts in China. The museum exhibits are divided into four categories- natural resources, human development, social life and various cultural relics, making it an excellent destination for an educational tour.

Beidaihe Scenic Area

Beidaihe Scenic Area presents the most beautiful and unfettered coast in China, encompassing a rugged stretch of coastline and lush green hills. Here, you can take long walks along the sparkling ocean or visit the famous Wanli Changcheng, an ancient Great Wall fort.

Natural Attractions of Qinhuangdao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Qinhuangdao is surrounded by some of the most beautiful natural wonders that are perfect for exploring outdoors.

Golden Sand Beach

Golden Sand Beach is the most popular beach in Qinhuangdao. The beach is known for its clear waters, golden sand, and picturesque scenery making it the perfect place to relax and take in the natural beauty.

Laolongtou

Laolongtou is the start of the Great Wall on the east coast of China, part of Shanhaiguan Pass Scenic Area. It features broad beaches, amazing rock formations, and a variety of sea life. The site is also perfect for taking relaxing strolls in the evenings.

Lianfengshan

Lianfengshan Scenic Area is located close to the city center of Qinhuangdao. The site is known for its natural beauty, with its vast expanse of greenery and a mix of natural rock formations. Exploring the area is like embarking on an incredible journey with every turn, and it’s a must-visit location for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qinhuangdao

Qinhuangdao is a hiking and biking paradise for those who love to be in nature. The city has a range of trails and routes that cater to different levels of outdoor enthusiasts.

Mountain Taihang

Mountain Taihang boasts towering peaks surrounded by rushing torrents, hidden caves, and rugged terrain. The hike to the top is not for the faint of heart, but the view makes it worth the effort.

Beach Park to Zhongshan Park

For a biking adventure, cycle from Beach Park to Zhongshan Park. You’ll get to take in breathtaking sea views and a mix of ancient and modern architecture along the way.

Best Things to Do in Qinhuangdao: Activities and Tours

Qinhuangdao hosts several unique activities and tours for visitors to explore the city’s culture, history, natural beauty, and local cuisine.

Evening Harbour Cruise

A nighttime Cruise on Shipo Bay is one of the most enjoyable activities to do in Qinhuangdao. The scenic view of the seaport is more stunning than during the day, with its many lights illuminating the night sky.

Yellow Sea National Forest Park

Host to one of China’s grandest forests, the Yellow Sea National Forest Park is a 10,000-hectare park located about 25 km from Qinhuangdao. You can take a tour of the landscape, and explore the sea of trees, ravines, and peaks of the park

Food and Drink Scene of Qinhuangdao

Qinhuangdao has a rich culinary culture that showcases the city’s unique cultural heritage. Visitors can expect to feast on delicate seafood dishes and local delicacies that are worth trying.

Must-Try Dishes

Laodingba

Laodingba is a fragrant and delicious snack that has been popular in Qinhuangdao for over 100 years. Laodingba is made of mutton, beef, and pork meat cooked in a spicy sauce.

Steamed Grass Carp

Steamed Grass Carp is a famous dish in Qinhuangdao, known for its tender and juicy fish meat dipped in pungent sesame oil, soy sauce, and chilies.

Local Beverages

Pijiu

Pijiu is a beer that originated from Hebei Province in China. Chia Tai Qingdao Beer Industrial Museum is a must-visit for beer lovers to learn about the production history.

What to Buy in Qinhuangdao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pearls

Qinhuangdao is well known for its pearls, and you can find a range of authentic pearl products, including jewelry, at the local pearl market.

Seafood

Qinhuangdao is also known for its fresh seafood, so bring home some dried scallops, shrimp, or fish, which make excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Qinhuangdao

The Qinhuangdao people are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and the city offers a blend of different cultural aspects. Here are a few customs to keep in mind when visiting.

  • Respect the Chinese culture and traditions
  • If invited to a local home, bring a small gift
  • Do not litter

Best Time to Visit Qinhuangdao

The best time to visit Qinhuangdao is from April to September when the climate is mild, and the coastal breeze is comfortable. The winters in Qinhuangdao are cold with snow, and the city becomes quieter between December and February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qinhuangdao

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and street food
  • Do respect Chinese customs and traditions
  • Do take your passport with you when visiting attractions

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the street
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing
  • Don’t discard the table scraps on the table in a restaurant

Safety and Health

Safety in Qinhuangdao

Qinhuangdao, like many other major cities in China, is relatively safe. But, like any other destination, there are risks to be aware of, including pickpocketing and petty theft.

Health in Qinhuangdao

It’s advisable to get medical advice before traveling to China. The standard of healthcare in Qinhuangdao is adequate, though it is essential to buy health insurance before visiting.

Getting to Qinhuangdao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qinhuangdao

Qinhuangdao has no airport. The nearest airport is Beidaihe Qinhuangdao Shanhaiguan Airport (BPE) located 24km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Qinhuangdao

Qinhuangdao Central Bus Station is the main bus station in the city, offering transportation to major cities in China, including Beijing, Tianjin, and Tangshan.

Other Options

You can also take the train to Qinhuangdao, which is a convenient and affordable option. There are several stations in the city, including Qinhuangdao Station, Shanhaiguan Station, and Changli Station.

How to Get Around in Qinhuangdao

The best way to get around Qinhuangdao is through the bus, subway system, or taxi. Buses are the cheapest and most convenient way to travel, and they operate throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Qinhuangdao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beijing

Located about 300km away from Qinhuangdao, Beijing is one of the most popular travel destinations in China and has a range of historical, cultural and modern attractions.

Chengde

Chengde is popularly referred to as the summer resort of the Beijing emperors. It has several historical and cultural attractions, including the Chengde Mountain Resort and Pule Temple.

Conclusion

Qinhuangdao is a must-visit destination in China. From historical sites to natural wonders and local culture, there’s something for everyone. Plan your trip well in advance, prepare adequately for the trip, and make sure to try out the delicious local cuisine.
