Baishan is one of the most beautiful cities in northeast China, located in the province of Jilin. Known for its breathtaking natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Baishan is a popular destination among domestic and international tourists alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Baishan, exploring its must-see attractions, best things to do, local cuisine, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction

Baishan is surrounded by mountains and rivers, making it a paradise for nature lovers. The city is also rich in history and culture, with a long and fascinating past. Visitors can enjoy hiking, skiing, and cycling while exploring the city’s scenic spots, as well as visiting its historical and cultural landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Baishan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Changbai Mountain Natural Museum: A fascinating museum showcasing the region’s natural and cultural history, including exhibits on volcanic eruptions, biological diversity, and traditional customs of local ethnic groups.
  • Changbai Mountain National Nature Reserve: This natural reserve is home to some of the most beautiful and remote forest landscapes in China, including not only China’s largest virgin forest, but also its largest cluster of waterfalls, and its deepest volcanic lake.
  • Tomb of the Qing Emperor: Located in the Baihe District, this picturesque site is the burial site of Emperor Qianlong’s mother. It is a popular destination for visitors who want to learn more about Chinese history.

Natural Attractions of Baishan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Changbai Mountain: Known as the “heavenly mountain” of China, this is one of the most spectacular and breathtaking natural sights in China. Visitors can explore the mountain and its hot springs, lakes, and waterfalls.
  • Heavenly Lake: A beautiful lake situated at the top of Changbai Mountain, which remains frozen for most of the year. Visitors can enjoy traditional activities such as fishing, boating, and swimming in summer.
  • Songjiang River: A beautiful stretch of river winding through the city, the Songjiang River is perfect for hiking or cycling. The river is especially stunning in autumn, with the leaves changing to vibrant colors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baishan

  • Changbai Mountain National Nature Reserve: There are many hiking and biking trails in the reserve that can be explored by visitors. Among these, the Tianchi Lake Route is the most popular option, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains. Visitors can also choose from a range of other trails, all offering wonderful views.
  • Songjiang River Trail: This beautiful river trail winds through stunning countryside, providing an excellent opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Baishan: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing: In winter, visitors can enjoy skiing and snowboarding at Changbai Mountain. The resort is the largest in Northeast China, offering access to more than 40 different slopes.
  • Rafting: The Songjiang River is perfect for rafting, with sections ranging from gentle paddling to white-knuckle rapids.
  • Cycling: Baishan is a beautiful city for cycling, with many scenic routes winding through the mountains and rivers.
  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can explore the city’s cultural and historical sites, including Changbai Mountain National Nature Reserve, the Tomb of the Qing Emperor, and the Baishan Folk Customs Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baishan

Day 1-2: Visit Changbai Mountain National Nature Reserve and explore the beautiful scenery of the area.

Day 3-4: Ski or snowboard at Changbai Mountain in winter, or enjoy a rafting trip down the Songjiang River in summer.

Day 5-6: Explore the local history and culture of Baishan by visiting the Tomb of the Qing Emperor, the Baishan Folk Customs Museum, and enjoying a cultural show.

Day 7: Visit Heavenly Lake and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Baishan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yanji Korean BBQ: A popular dish in Baishan, featuring marinated beef, pork or chicken grilled at the table in front of you.
  • Changbaishan Trout: A local specialty, this fish is served in many restaurants in the area, usually steamed with vegetables and spices.
  • White Mountain Ginseng: A special ingredient found in many dishes in Baishan, such as soups and stews. It is believed to have many health benefits.

Local Beverages

  • Baishan Liquor: Made from local grains and mountain spring water, this liquor is a must-try for visitors to the area.
  • Changbaishan Tea: A high-quality tea produced in the region, perfect for relaxing after a long day of sightseeing.

Where to Stay in Baishan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options available to visitors in Baishan, including luxury hotels, budget hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

  • Tianxing Hotel: A luxury hotel located near Changbai Mountain, offering stunning views and world-class amenities.
  • Baishan Xiaotian’e Hotel: A budget-friendly option, featuring comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Baishan Longyuan Guesthouse: A warm and welcoming guesthouse with beautiful mountain views.

What to Buy in Baishan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ginseng: The region around Baishan is known for producing some of the best ginseng in China, an important ingredient in traditional medicine.
  • Changbaishan Tea: A high-quality tea that is exquisite in taste and aroma is perfect to buy for your loved ones.
  • Handicrafts: Visitors can buy handmade crafts and souvenirs created by local artists and craftsmen, such as embroidered cloth shoes and traditional ethnic costumes.

Local Culture and Customs of Baishan

Baishan is home to several ethnic minority groups, including the Korean and Manchu people. The locals are warm and hospitable, and visitors are advised to respect local customs and traditions. It is forbidden to smoke in most public places and to throw garbage in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Baishan

The best time to visit Baishan is from June to October when the weather is pleasant and the scenery is at its most beautiful. If you want to experience skiing or snowboarding, the best time to visit is from December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baishan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering.
  • Bring warm clothing if you’re visiting in winter.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Photograph or record local people without their permission.
  • Scuba-dive in Heavenly Lake.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baishan

Baishan is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are very low. Visitors are advised to take precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and not leaving valuable items unattended.

Health in Baishan

Baishan has good medical facilities, and visitors can access medical services in case of an emergency. Visitors are also advised to drink bottled water to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Baishan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baishan

The nearest airport to Baishan is Yanji Chaoyangchuan International Airport, which is located about 120 kilometers away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Baishan

Baishan has several bus stations that serve the city and its surrounding areas. Most buses arrive and depart from the long-distance bus station in the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Baishan by train from major cities in China. There are also several car rental services available if you prefer to explore the area on your own.

How to Get Around in Baishan

Visitors can take buses or taxis to get around Baishan city. Taxis are widely available and relatively inexpensive, while buses offer a more affordable option.

Where to Go Next from Baishan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yanji City: A nearby city known for its Korean cuisine and culture.
  • Ji’an City: A historic city located on the banks of the Songhua River.
  • Jilin City: A popular city in Northeast China known for its natural landscapes, including the scenic Songhua River.

Conclusion

Baishan is a destination that offers something for everyone, from nature and outdoor adventure to cultural and historical exploration. Visitors can experience the breathtaking landscapes, delicious local cuisine, and friendly local people that make Baishan such a unique and unforgettable travel destination. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your trip to Baishan and explore all that this beautiful city has to offer!
