Introduction to Jingyuetan National Forest Park

Jingyuetan National Forest Park is a beautiful natural reserve located in Jilin, Northeast China. The park is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, amazing outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage. The park covers an area of 200 square kilometers and is home to several endangered animal species, including the Siberian tiger and the Manchurian crane.

If you’re looking for a place to immerse yourself in nature and explore the rich cultural heritage of Northeast China, then Jingyuetan National Forest Park is the perfect destination for you.

Local Culture and Customs of Jingyuetan National Forest Park

  • Jingyuetan is home to several ethnic minority groups, including the Manchu, Korean, and Mongolian.
  • The local dialect is Jilin Northeastern Mandarin.
  • Traditional festivals include the Lunar New Year, the Mid-autumn Festival, and the National Day.

Best Time to Visit Jingyuetan National Forest Park

The best time to visit Jingyuetan National Forest Park is from December to February when the snow and ice activities are at their peak. However, visitors can also enjoy the beautiful scenery and outdoor activities during the spring, summer, and autumn months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jingyuetan National Forest Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take part in outdoor activities responsibly and with care for the environment.
  • Keep the park clean and dispose of trash properly.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harm the local wildlife.
  • Damage or remove any park property or natural resources.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Make loud noises that may disturb other visitors or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jingyuetan National Forest Park

  • Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and skiing.
  • Follow all posted signs and instructions from park officials.
  • Inform others of your planned routes and expected arrival times.
  • In case of emergency, contact park officials or emergency services immediately.

Health in Jingyuetan National Forest Park

  • Visitors should take measures to protect themselves from the cold and to avoid altitude sickness when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Ensure that you have appropriate vaccinations and travel insurance before your visit.
