Introduction to Baihe

Baihe is a county-level city located in the northern part of Jilin Province, China. Located on the banks of the Songhua River and surrounded by beautiful mountains, Baihe is known for its picturesque landscapes and natural wonders. The area is home to many ethnic minorities such as the Manchu, Korean, and Mongolian, making it a vibrant and multicultural tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Baihe

Here are the top tourist destinations in Baihe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Baihe Museum - This museum showcases the history and culture of Baihe, including the famous white crane (baihe) which is the city’s namesake.
  2. Duanbian Mountain Fortress - A historical site that played a vital role in the Chinese resistance against Japanese occupation in 1931.
  3. Manjing Ancient Town - A well-preserved ancient town that dates back to the Ming Dynasty, offering visitors a glimpse into Chinese history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Baihe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hongshi Gorge Scenic Area - This scenic area offers breathtaking views of Hongshi Gorge and the Songhua River, perfect for hiking, boating, and picnicking.
  2. Shuangfeng Mountain - A must-visit for hikers and nature enthusiasts, this mountain offers stunning views of the city from the top.
  3. Baihe Wetland Park - A protected wetland that is home to various wildlife and bird species. Visitors can take a boat ride or hike to explore the park’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baihe

  1. Shuangfeng Mountain Hike - A challenging hike up Shuangfeng Mountain, offering rewarding views at the top.
  2. Baihe Wetland Park Bike Ride - Rent a bike and take a leisurely ride around the scenic Baihe Wetland Park.
  3. Hongshi Gorge River Rafting - An exciting way to explore the beautiful Hongshi Gorge while paddling down the river.

Best Things to Do in Baihe: Activities and Tours

  1. Hot Spring Experience - Relax and unwind at one of the many hot springs in the area.
  2. Skiing in Shanghu Lake Resort - Perfect for winter sports enthusiasts looking to hit the slopes.
  3. Cultural Performances - Watch traditional performances by ethnic minority groups, including the Manchu and Korean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baihe

Day 1: Explore Baihe’s museums and historical sites Day 2: Take a hike up Shuangfeng Mountain Day 3: Visit the Baihe Wetland Park and go for a boat ride Day 4: Learn about Baihe’s ethnic minorities at cultural performances Day 5: Relax at a hot spring Day 6: Go river rafting down Hongshi Gorge Day 7: Ski at Shanghu Lake Resort

Food and Drink Scene of Baihe

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Baihe White Crane - A local specialty dish made with white crane meat and various herbs and spices.
  2. Zhanggui Hujiao Bing - A savory pancake filled with meat and spices, a popular street food in Baihe.
  3. Baihe Fermented Bean Curd - A tangy and flavorful side dish made from fermented tofu.

Local Beverages

  1. Baihe Rice Wine - A traditional rice wine brewed in Baihe and enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.
  2. Milk Tea - A popular drink in the area, often enjoyed with tapioca pearls or other toppings.

Where to Stay in Baihe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Baihe Junpeng Hotel - A stylish and modern hotel located in the heart of Baihe, offering comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  2. Hanting Hotel - A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and basic amenities.
  3. Yijia Courtyard Guesthouse - A charming guesthouse with traditional Chinese architecture and decor.

What to Buy in Baihe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Baihe White Crane Products - Buy some white crane-themed souvenirs such as keychains, magnets, or shirts.
  2. Traditional Korean Dress - Baihe has a significant Korean population, and visitors can buy traditional Korean dresses as souvenirs.
  3. Medicinal Herbs - Baihe is known for its abundance of medicinal herbs, and visitors can purchase some as souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Baihe

Baihe is a multicultural city, and visitors should be respectful of the different customs and traditions of the ethnic minorities living in the area.

Best Time to Visit Baihe

The best time to visit Baihe is from May to October, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baihe

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs
  2. Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural and cultural heritage sites
  2. Ignore the guidelines and regulations in place
  3. Disrespect the local people or their beliefs

Safety and Health

Safety in Baihe

Baihe is a relatively safe city, but visitors should exercise caution and take necessary precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Health in Baihe

Baihe has excellent healthcare facilities and services, but visitors should take precautions to avoid getting sick or injured, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Baihe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baihe

  1. Jilin Changchun Longjia International Airport - The nearest international airport to Baihe, located around 3.5 hours away by car.
  2. Tumen Nanping Airport - A smaller airport located around 45 minutes away from Baihe by car.

Bus Stations Near Baihe

  1. Baihe Bus Station - The main bus station in the city, with regular services to nearby towns and cities.
  2. Baihe North Bus Station - Another bus station located in the northern part of Baihe.

Other Options

  1. Train - There are several train stations located in nearby cities such as Changchun and Jilin, with services to Baihe.
  2. Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car and drive to Baihe, but should be mindful of driving regulations and traffic conditions in China.

How to Get Around in Baihe

Local buses and taxis are available in the city, and visitors can also rent a bike or hire a car to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Baihe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Changchun - The capital city of Jilin Province, known for its historical sites and winter sports activities.
  2. Jilin City - Another major city in Jilin Province, famous for its natural wonders such as the Jingpo Lake and Beidahu Ski Resort.
  3. Baicheng - A city located in western Jilin Province that offers a unique blend of cultures, architecture, and scenery.

Conclusion

Baihe is a hidden gem in Northeast China, with its natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip to Baihe and discover all that the city has to offer.
