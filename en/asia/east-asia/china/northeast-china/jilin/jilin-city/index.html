Introduction to Jilin (City)

Jilin (City) is a prefecture-level city in Northeast China, located in the heart of Jilin Province. It is an important transportation and logistics hub for the region and a popular tourist destination known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and historical significance.

The city boasts a high-altitude climate that is relatively cool and dry throughout the year, making it a great escape from the summer heat. Visitors can explore the stunning natural landscapes, hike in the many parks, and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities. There are also many historic sites and cultural attractions, including museums, temples, and ancient ruins for visitors to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Jilin (City)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jilin (City) is steeped in rich cultural history, and there are many museums and historical places for visitors to discover:

  • The Jilin Provincial Museum showcases the natural history, culture, and customs of the region. Visitors can see exhibits on prehistoric animals, ancient civilization, traditional handicrafts, and modern art.

  • The Manchukuo State Department Museum is a former government building that now houses exhibits on the history of the Manchukuo state, which was established in 1932 and lasted until the end of World War II.

  • The Songhua Lake Scenic Area is one of the most popular destinations in the city, with stunning views of the lake, mountains, and forests. Visitors can also explore the ancient ruins left by the Liao and Jin dynasties.

Natural Attractions of Jilin (City): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jilin (City) is blessed with a diverse range of natural landscapes that provide endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • The Beishan scenic area offers hikers stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside. The area is popular with both locals and tourists and has hiking trails suitable for all levels of fitness.

  • The Changbai Mountains National Nature Reserve is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in China. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountains, see the famous “Heavenly Lake,” and explore the area’s many natural hot springs.

  • The Moon Lake Scenic Area is a nature reserve that features a beautiful lake, picturesque hills, and lush forests. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake or hike the trails to see the park’s rich flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jilin (City)

Jilin (City) has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to experience the region’s natural beauty up close:

  • The Xianghai National Nature Reserve has several hiking trails that take visitors through the area’s unique wetland ecosystem, home to a variety of rare bird and plant species.

  • The Jingyuetan National Forest Park has many kilometers of mountain biking trails with varying degrees of difficulty. Visitors can explore the lush forests and stunning mountain scenery on two wheels.

  • The Jilin Longtan Mountain Scenic Area offers a range of hiking trails that take hikers through the area’s unique rock formations, caves, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Jilin (City): Activities and Tours

Jilin (City) offers visitors an array of activities and tours that make for a memorable travel experience:

  • The Jilin Rime Festival is one of the most popular winter festivals in China, featuring stunning ice sculptures, live performances, and traditional food and drink.

  • The Harbin Ice and Snow Festival is another winter festival that is worth visiting. It features an array of ice sculptures, exhibitions, and cultural events.

  • The Jilin Changchun Film City is a popular destination for film lovers. Visitors can explore the film sets and learn about the history of Chinese cinema.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jilin (City)

To make the most of your trip to Jilin (City), we recommend the following itinerary:

5 Take a day trip to the Changbai Mountains and see the famous “Heavenly Lake”

Food and Drink Scene of Jilin (City)

Visitors to Jilin (City) can enjoy a variety of local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saozi Noodle (烧子面) is a Jilin specialty. It is a noodle dish made with meat, vegetables, and a spicy sauce.

  • Baijiu (白酒) is a Chinese liquor that is popular in Jilin. It is made from grain and has a strong flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Harbin beer is a popular beer brand in China and is readily available in Jilin.

  • Jilin has several local tea varieties, including Maojian Tea (毛尖茶) and Black Teacakes (黑茶饼).

Where to Stay in Jilin (City): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jilin (City) has a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget hostels:

  • The Hilton Jilin is a luxury hotel located in the city center. It features an indoor pool, spa, and fitness center.

  • The Jinjiang Inn Jilin Train Station is a budget hotel located close to the city’s train station. It offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  • The Jilin Guesthouse is a traditional guesthouse located in the city center. It offers an authentic experience of Jilin culture and is a popular choice for visitors seeking a unique travel experience.

What to Buy in Jilin (City): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jilin (City) has several local products that make for great souvenirs:

  • Jilin panax ginseng is a well-known Chinese medicine that is believed to have many health benefits.

  • Jilin porcelain is a unique porcelain style that originated in the region and is prized for its delicate design and craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Jilin (City)

Jilin (City) has a unique culture that is heavily influenced by the region’s history and geography:

  • The Jilin dialect is a variant of Mandarin Chinese that is spoken in the city and throughout the province.

  • The region is known for its traditional handicrafts, including wood carving, paper cutting, and embroidery.

  • The city has several important festivals and celebrations that attract visitors from all over China, including the Jilin Rime Festival and the Harbin Ice and Snow Festival.

Best Time to Visit Jilin (City)

The best time to visit Jilin (City) is from May to October when the weather is mild and the parks and natural attractions are open to visitors. The winter months are also a popular time to visit for the Jilin Rime Festival and the Harbin Ice and Snow Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jilin (City)

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Mandarin Chinese before your trip to help you communicate with locals.

  • Try local dishes and beverages to experience the unique flavors of Jilin.

Don’t:

  • Wear too revealing clothing in public places, as it may offend local customs.

  • Take photographs without permission, especially in cultural or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jilin (City)

Jilin (City) is a relatively safe city for travelers, but visitors should take normal precautions to avoid becoming a victim of petty crime.

Health in Jilin (City)

Visitors to Jilin (City) should take particular care in the winter months, as the cold weather can be harsh and cause health problems. It is also important to be aware of the risk of altitude sickness when visiting the higher elevations of the Changbai Mountains.

Getting to Jilin (City): Transportation Options and Logistics

Jilin (City) is a transportation hub in Northeast China, and there are several ways to get there:

Airports Near Jilin (City)

  • Changchun Longjia International Airport (CGQ) is the nearest airport to Jilin (City) and is approximately 80 km away.

  • Shenyang Taoxian International Airport (SHE) is also close to Jilin (City) and is approximately 250 km away.

Bus Stations Near Jilin (City)

  • Jilin (City) has several bus stations located throughout the city, including the Jilin Bus Station and the Jilin Long-distance Bus Station.

Other Options

  • High-speed trains connect Jilin (City) with major cities in Northeast China, including Changchun, Harbin, and Shenyang.

  • Taxis and private car hire are also available for travelers who prefer a more private and customized transportation option.

How to Get Around in Jilin (City)

Public transportation in Jilin (City) includes buses and taxis. Taxis are relatively cheap and are a good option for short trips around the city. Buses are also affordable and offer several routes that connect the major attractions and neighborhoods in the city.

Where to Go Next from Jilin (City): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jilin (City) is well-connected to other popular destinations in Northeast China:

  • Changchun, the provincial capital, is just a short train ride away and is home to several important cultural and historical sites, including the Puppet Emperor’s Palace and the Jingyuetan National Forest Park.

  • Harbin, known for its stunning ice sculptures and architecture, is also easily accessible by train or bus from Jilin (City).

Conclusion

Jilin (City) is a fascinating city that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide in hand, you can plan your trip to Jilin (City) with confidence and discover all that this exciting destination has to offer.
