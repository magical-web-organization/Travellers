Xianghai National Nature Reserve: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Looking for a breathtaking natural wonderland to explore? Look no further than Xianghai National Nature Reserve. Nestled in Jilin Province in Northeast China, this reserve boasts stunning landscapes, unique cultural attractions, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker, a history buff, or simply looking to soak up some of China’s natural beauty, Xianghai National Nature Reserve should be at the top of your travel list. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll give you all the information you need to plan the perfect trip.

Introduction to Xianghai National Nature Reserve

Xianghai National Nature Reserve covers an area of 32,000 hectares and is located in the eastern part of Jilin Province. The reserve is named after Lake Xianghai, one of the largest freshwater lakes in China, which is situated at the heart of the reserve. Xianghai is home to a wide variety of plant and animal species, including numerous endangered species such as red-crowned cranes and white-naped cranes. The reserve also has significant cultural and historical importance, with several important museums and historical sites located within its borders.

List of Places to Visit in Xianghai National Nature Reserve

When it comes to travel destinations, Xianghai National Nature Reserve has a little something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Xinglonggou Site Museum: This museum is located about 5 kilometers southwest of Xianghai Lake and features artifacts from the Xinglonggou culture, which flourished in the region over 5,000 years ago.
  • Haolingbaoji Site Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Bohai people, who once inhabited the region around Xianghai Lake.
  • Qingfeng Temple: This temple dates back over 1,000 years and is located at the foot of Dajiangshan Mountain, offering visitors the chance to see some of the region’s most stunning natural scenery.

Natural Attractions of Xianghai National Nature Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xianghai Lake: This is the centerpiece of the reserve and the perfect spot for a boat ride or lakeside picnic.
  • Dajiangshan Mountain: This mountain offers some of the most breathtaking hiking trails in the reserve, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Shennong Stream: This stream features an abundance of wildlife and is a popular spot for kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xianghai National Nature Reserve

For those looking to get some exercise while exploring the natural wonders of Xianghai National Nature Reserve, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Dajiangshan Mountain Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is perfect for more experienced hikers.
  • Shuangyanggou Bike Trail: This trail is a bit easier and is perfect for mountain biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Xianghai National Nature Reserve: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other exciting activities to enjoy in Xianghai National Nature Reserve. Here are a few ideas:

  • Take a boat ride on Xianghai Lake and soak up the stunning views.
  • Join a guided tour of the reserve to learn more about its unique ecosystem and cultural history.
  • Try your hand at fishing in one of the reserve’s streams or lakes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xianghai National Nature Reserve

If you’re planning a longer trip to Xianghai National Nature Reserve, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Xianghai National Nature Reserve and settle into your accommodations.
  • Day 2-4: Spend these days exploring the natural wonders of the reserve, taking in stunning vistas, hiking trails, and other outdoor activities.
  • Day 5: Visit some of the reserve’s museums and cultural attractions, learning about the history and heritage of the region.
  • Day 6: Join a guided tour of the reserve to learn more about its unique ecosystem and cultural history.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy a leisurely day, perhaps taking a boat ride or trying fishing.

Food and Drink Scene of Xianghai National Nature Reserve

No trip to Xianghai National Nature Reserve would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked fish: This local delicacy can be found in many of the reserve’s restaurants and is cooked over an open flame for a delicious smoky flavor.
  • Jilin-style hotpot: This spicy and savory dish is perfect for cold days and is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: This strong Chinese liquor is a popular drink in the region and pairs perfectly with many of the local dishes.
  • Xuehua beer: This local beer is brewed using the region’s snowmelt and has won numerous awards for its taste.

Where to Stay in Xianghai National Nature Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options available in and around Xianghai National Nature Reserve to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are a few popular choices:

  • Shengya International Hotel: This hotel offers luxurious amenities and stunning lake views.
  • Xiangyuan Hotel: This hotel is more budget-friendly while still offering comfortable accommodations.
  • Guesthouses: There are plenty of guesthouse options in the nearby town of Bayishan, offering a more authentic local experience.

What to Buy in Xianghai National Nature Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to take home from your trip to Xianghai National Nature Reserve? Here are a few ideas:

  • Red-crowned crane figurines: These cute and delicate figurines make great gifts for bird lovers.
  • Xuehua beer: This local beer is a popular choice for bringing home as a memento of your trip.
  • Handmade crafts: There are plenty of local artisans selling handcrafted goods like pottery, textiles, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Xianghai National Nature Reserve

Xianghai National Nature Reserve has a deep and fascinating history, and visitors can learn more about its cultural heritage through the numerous museums and historical sites in the reserve. The local culture is heavily influenced by the region’s natural beauty and wildlife, with many traditions and practices centered around the environment.

Best Time to Visit Xianghai National Nature Reserve

The best time to visit Xianghai National Nature Reserve is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the flora and fauna are in full bloom. However, the reserve is beautiful year-round, so visitors can enjoy its natural wonders no matter what season they visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xianghai National Nature Reserve

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and local cultural heritage.
  • Try some of the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take plenty of photos to capture the stunning scenery.

Don’t:

  • Bring any pets into the reserve.
  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

When it comes to traveling to Xianghai National Nature Reserve, there are a few important things to keep in mind for your safety and health.

Safety in Xianghai National Nature Reserve

  • Always follow park rules and regulations.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and don’t wander off marked trails.
  • Take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and other insect bites.

Health in Xianghai National Nature Reserve

  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations before traveling.
  • Be sure to bring any necessary medication or medical equipment with you.
  • Bring a first aid kit and know basic first aid skills.

Getting to Xianghai National Nature Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

For visitors coming from outside the region, getting to Xianghai National Nature Reserve can seem daunting. Here are a few options and logistical considerations to help make the trip easier.

Airports Near Xianghai National Nature Reserve

  • Changchun Longjia International Airport: This is the closest airport to the reserve, located about 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Xianghai National Nature Reserve

  • Jilin Central Bus Station: Buses from this station run to nearby Bayishan, which is about 10 kilometers from the reserve.
  • Tonghua Bus Station: Buses from this station run to Bayishan and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis can be hired in nearby cities like Jilin or Changchun for transport to the reserve.
  • Car rental: Car rental is also an option for those who prefer to have their own transportation.

How to Get Around in Xianghai National Nature Reserve

Once you’re in the reserve, there are a few options for getting around to the various attractions and activities:

  • Walking: Many of the reserve’s trails and scenic areas can be accessed on foot.
  • Biking: Bikes can be rented in certain areas of the reserve for those who prefer to ride.
  • Boat ride: A boat ride on Xianghai Lake is a unique way to take in the stunning surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Xianghai National Nature Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels beyond Xianghai National Nature Reserve, there are plenty of options in the surrounding area. Here are a few ideas:

  • Changbai Mountains: Located about six hours northeast of Xianghai National Nature Reserve, the Changbai Mountains offer some of China’s most spectacular natural scenery.
  • Dalian: This coastal city is about six hours east of the reserve and is known for its stunning beaches and seafood cuisine.
  • Harbin: Harbin is about four hours northwest of the reserve and is famous for its annual Ice and Snow Festival, held every winter.

Conclusion

All in all, Xianghai National Nature Reserve is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring China’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking to hike through stunning mountain trails, learn about the region’s fascinating history, or simply kick back and take in the breathtaking views, Xianghai National Nature Reserve has something for everyone.
