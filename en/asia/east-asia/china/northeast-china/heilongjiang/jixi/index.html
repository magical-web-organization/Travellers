Introduction to Jixi

Jixi is one of the most beautiful cities in Northeast China, located in the Heilongjiang province. This city rich in coal mines and heavy industries is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique travel experience. It has a population of over 3 million people, many of whom are ethnic minority groups.

List of Places to Visit in Jixi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jixi Coal Mine Museum

The Jixi Coal Mine Museum is one of the most important museums in the region. It showcases the city’s coal mining history and the equipment used for extraction. It is highly recommended for those interested in seeing the technology that made Heilongjiang a rich region.

Jixi City Museum

The Jixi City Museum is perfect for history enthusiasts. It highlights the city’s 6,000 years of history and the transformation of the region over the years. The exhibition includes ancient artifacts, cultural relics, and a wide range of historical documents.

Natural Attractions of Jixi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Xingkai Lake

Xingkai Lake is a sprawling lake that spans over 2,100 square kilometers. This beautiful freshwater lake is a natural beauty that attracts tourists from all over the world. It has scenic views and crystal-clear waters that are perfect for swimming and boating.

Jiayin Grasslands

Jiayin Grasslands are one of the most beautiful grasslands in China that stretch over 130,000 hectares. They offer breathtaking views of the natural landscape and abundant wildlife. It’s perfect for camping, hiking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jixi

Shuangyashan Scenic Area

The Shuangyashan Scenic Area offers one of the most breathtaking views of Jixi. The hiking and biking routes offer an avenue for tourists to explore the scenic area. The area is covered by dense forests and offers visitors a chance to see several bird species, wildflowers, and a variety of animals.

Best Things to Do in Jixi: Activities and Tours

Visit the Underground Castle in Jixi

The Underground Castle in Jixi offers visitors a unique experience of the ancient city of Jixi. It offers an opportunity to step back in time and see how life was like in the ancient city. The castle was originally built over 300 years ago and offers a glimpse of ancient Chinese royalty.

Take a Trolleybus Tour of Jixi

The Trolleybus tour of Jixi offers a unique method of exploring the city’s historical landmarks. Tourists get a visual treat of the city’s architectural grandeur and historical significance. The Trolley bus tour offers a very affordable way to get a comprehensive tour of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Jixi

Must-Try Dishes

Stewed Sea Cucumber

Stewed Sea Cucumber is one of the most popular dishes in Jixi. It is a Chinese delicacy that is highly regarded for its rich and unique taste. It is usually served with rice and a side of vegetables.

Peking Duck

Peking Duck is another delicacy that Jixi is famous for. It is a dish that has been enjoyed in China for over 1,500 years. The dish is famous for its crispy skin and tender meat.

Local Beverages

Black Tea

The region is known for its high-quality black tea that has a unique flavor. The tea leaves are fermented and dried to give the beverage its strong flavor.

Rice Wine

Rice Wine is another popular beverage that is brewed in Jixi. The wine is made from fermented rice and has a sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Jixi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jixi Haisheng Hotel

The Jixi Haisheng Hotel is a luxurious hotel that offers visitors comfort and elegance. It is located in the central business district of Jixi and is within walking distance of several restaurants and cafes.

Jixi Railway Hotel

The Jixi Railway Hotel is a mid-range hotel that offers visitors the comfort of a big city hotel. It has several comfortable rooms that are perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Jixi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional Paintings

Traditional paintings are a must-buy when you visit Jixi. The paintings are colorful and reflect the region’s rich artistic heritage. They are a perfect souvenir to take home as a reminder of your memorable trip.

Local Herbs and Spices

Jixi is home to several unique herbs and spices that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries. Purchasing them is an excellent way to take a bit of Jixi home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Jixi

Jixi is a melting pot of culture and traditions that are unique to the region. The region has a rich ethnic culture that is celebrated through various festivals and events. Visitors should take time to learn about the culture and customs of the region to better appreciate their visit.

Best Time to Visit Jixi

The best time to visit Jixi is during the summer months (June to August). The weather is temperate and perfect for outdoor activities. However, visitors should be aware that summer is the peak season. Visiting during early spring (April to May) or late fall (September to October) can provide milder temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jixi

Do:

• Respect the local customs and traditions. • Be polite to locals, including street vendors and shop keepers. • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the culture.

Don’t:

• Overstep boundaries or be too forward. • Disrespect the local culture or traditions. • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jixi

Jixi is generally a safe city, with low crime rates. It is best to take precautions such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings at night.

Health in Jixi

Visitors to Jixi should be aware of the high levels of pollution in the region. It is recommended to wear a protective mask on particularly bad days, such as high smog days.

Getting to Jixi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jixi

Jixi Xingkaihu Airport is the nearest airport that serves Jixi. It has daily flights to several cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

Bus Stations Near Jixi

The bus station in Jixi provides daily connections to several cities in China, including Harbin, Dalian, and Qingdao.

Other Options

The train is another great option when traveling to Jixi. The city is well-connected to the rest of China by rail, with daily trains to Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

How to Get Around in Jixi

Jixi is a relatively small city, and the best way to get around is by bus or taxi. Buses are cheap, efficient, and run throughout the entire city. Taxis are also readily available and are a more convenient way to travel.

Where to Go Next from Jixi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harbin

Harbin is located 199 kilometers from Jixi and is known for its ice and snow festivals, as well as its stunning architecture.

Shuangyashan

Shuangyashan is a small city located 49 kilometers from Jixi. It is a famous destination for summer getaways, especially for its beautiful scenery and hot springs.

Conclusion

Jixi has so much to offer, from its natural beauty to its rich cultural history, delicious food, and warm hospitality. Visitors who explore this city will leave with a profound appreciation for its charm and beauty. Plan your trip today and enjoy all that Jixi has to offer!
