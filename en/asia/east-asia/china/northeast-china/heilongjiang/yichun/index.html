Introduction to Yichun

Located in the northeastern province of Heilongjiang, Yichun offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and rich cultural heritage of Northeast China. Often overshadowed by larger cities like Harbin and Changchun, Yichun is a hidden gem that’s worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Yichun

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yichun is home to many museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Yichun City Museum - This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts that showcase the history and culture of Yichun and the surrounding region.
  • Baiyinhua Revolutionary Memorial Hall - This museum commemorates the revolutionary history of Yichun and the struggles faced by the Red Army during the Long March.
  • Fusi Mountain Relic Park - This park features the ruins of the Fusi Mountain Antiaircraft Artillery Base, which was built during the Second World War.

Natural Attractions of Yichun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yichun is known for its beautiful natural scenery and many outdoor activities. Some of the top destinations for nature lovers include:

  • Xianghualing National Forest Park - This park is home to some of the most pristine forests in Northeast China and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Yueliangwan Scenic Area - Located on the banks of the Songhua River, this scenic area offers visitors the chance to take a leisurely stroll along the river and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
  • Shangzhi Ski Resort - Located just outside of Yichun, this ski resort offers visitors the chance to hit the slopes and enjoy some winter sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yichun

There are many opportunities for hiking and biking in Yichun. Some of the top routes include:

  • Fusi Mountain Trail - This hiking trail winds through the scenic Fusi Mountain Relic Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Yichun Forest Park Trail - This trail takes visitors through the beautiful forests of Yichun and offers a chance to spot local wildlife.
  • Yichun City River Trail - This biking route takes visitors along the tranquil Songhua River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Yichun: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Yichun has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Visit a Traditional Chinese Medicine Center - Yichun is home to many traditional Chinese medicine centers where visitors can learn about the ancient practice and receive treatments.
  • Take a Boat Tour of the Songhua River - This leisurely boat tour offers stunning views of Yichun’s natural beauty and is a must-do for first-time visitors.
  • Attend a Local Cultural Festival - Yichun hosts many cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Yichun Winter Tourism Festival and the Baiyinhua International Forest Tourism Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Yichun

Must-Try Dishes

Yichun is known for its hearty, Northeastern-style cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include:

  • Xian’er Kao Rou - This dish consists of skewered and grilled meat (usually lamb or pork) that is marinated in a spicy sauce.
  • Pai Gu Rou - This dish features tender, slow-cooked pork ribs that are marinated in a flavorful sauce.
  • Sanxian Guoba - This dish is a stir-fry of potatoes, eggplant, and green peppers served over a bed of crispy rice.

Local Beverages

Yichun is known for its strong, flavorful beverages. Here are some local favorites:

  • Cassia Wine - This sweet, aromatic wine is made from the bark of the cassia tree and is enjoyed on special occasions.
  • Yi Jian Tea - This tea is made from the leaves of a local plant that grows in the area and has a rich, earthy flavor.
  • Qie Zi Su - This frothy, pink drink is made from a local species of milkweed and is popular during the summer months.

Where to Stay in Yichun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yichun offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and traveler type. Here are some top options:

  • Jinjiang Inn Yichun Train Station - This budget-friendly hotel is conveniently located near Yichun’s train station and offers clean, comfortable rooms.
  • Yichun Grand Hotel - This upscale hotel offers luxurious amenities like an indoor pool, fitness center, and spa.
  • Yichun Youth Hostel - This cozy hostel is a great option for budget-conscious travelers who want to meet fellow backpackers.

What to Buy in Yichun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Yichun include:

  • Cassia Bark - This fragrant spice is used in many traditional Chinese dishes and is a staple of Yichun cuisine.
  • Yichun Maotai - This high-quality local liquor is beloved by locals and visitors alike.
  • Baiyinhua Tea - This tea is made from a rare species of tea tree that only grows in Yichun and has a smooth, full-bodied flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Yichun

Yichun has its own unique traditions and customs that are worth learning about. Some of the most important include:

  • Respect for Elders - Chinese culture places a high value on respecting and caring for elders, and this is especially true in Yichun.
  • Appreciation for Nature - Yichun’s residents have a deep appreciation for the natural world and take great care to preserve the area’s forests and wildlife.
  • Strong Work Ethic - Yichun is known for its hardworking and entrepreneurial population, and this spirit is reflected in many aspects of local life.

Best Time to Visit Yichun

The best time to visit Yichun is in the spring and summer months (April-August), when the weather is mild and pleasant. If you’re interested in winter sports, the area’s ski resorts are open from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yichun

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore Yichun’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Cause damage to the environment.
  • Disrespect elders or authority figures.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yichun

Yichun is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take common-sense precautions to prevent theft and other crimes. Some tips include:

  • Keep valuables like cash and passports in a secure location.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid risky situations.

Health in Yichun

Yichun’s tap water is not safe to drink, so visitors should stick to bottled water. It’s also a good idea to pack a basic first-aid kit and any necessary prescription medications.

Getting to Yichun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yichun

The nearest airport to Yichun is Heilongjiang Mohe Airport, which is about 200km from the city center. From there, travelers can take a bus or taxi to Yichun.

Bus Stations Near Yichun

Yichun has several bus stations, including the Yichun West Bus Station and Yichun CBD Bus Station. Buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel within Heilongjiang province.

Other Options

Some travelers may choose to hire a private car or driver to explore Yichun and the surrounding areas. This can be a more expensive option, but may be worth it for those who prefer a more personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Yichun

Yichun has a comprehensive public transportation system that includes buses and taxis. There are also bike rental options available for those who want to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Yichun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yichun is well-connected to other cities and towns in Heilongjiang province, making it a great starting point for further explorations. Some nearby destinations include:

  • Harbin - Known for its ice sculptures and Russian-influenced architecture, Harbin is a must-visit destination in the area.
  • Heihe - This border city is a gateway to Russia and offers visitors a chance to explore the region’s rich history and culture.
  • Mohe - Known as the “Arctic Village” of China, Mohe is the northernmost city in the country and offers stunning views of the Aurora Borealis.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant local culture, Yichun is a hidden gem in Northeast China. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, you’re sure to find something to love in this charming city.
