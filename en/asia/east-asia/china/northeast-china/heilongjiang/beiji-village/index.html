Introduction to Beiji Village

Beiji Village is located in Northeast China, in the Heilongjiang province, known for its stunning natural beauty, breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality. Beiji Village, a small rural community, is a popular destination among tourists due to its breathtaking scenery, pristine countryside, and rich cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Beiji Village

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Beiji Folk Museum: This museum showcases the region’s unique rural heritage, including traditional farming and fishing equipment, musical instruments, and folklore. Visitors can learn about the traditional life and customs of the local people.

  2. Beiji Historical Site: The Beiji Historical Site is an ancient monument where visitors can witness the milestones of the history of Beiji Village. There are centuries-old wooden houses and temples constructed with ancient architectural techniques.

Natural Attractions of Beiji Village: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Beiji Forest Park: Beiji Forest Park is located in the southeast of the village, covering an area of ​​22,000 mu. It is known for its dense forests, flowing streams, and undulating hills. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the lush greenery to appreciate the stunning wilderness.

  2. Beiji Wetland Park: The Beiji Wetland Park is a vast wetland area, covering an area of ​​about 7,277 hectares. Visitors can enjoy the serene natural surroundings amongst the wetlands, which act as a breeding ground for various rare animals and plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beiji Village

  1. Beiji Mountain Trek: This 2-hour trek takes visitors through hills and forests to reach the summit of Beiji Mountain. The peak offers breathtaking views of the scenic landscapes of the village.

  2. Beiji Cycling Trail: Visitors can hire bikes and explore the rolling hills and countryside of the village, Biking is the perfect activity to soak in the tranquil surroundings at leisure.

Best Things to Do in Beiji Village: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing Tour on the Beiji River: The Beiji River offers a perfect spot for fishing lovers. Fishing tackle is readily available, and the village also provides professional guides to take visitors to the right spot for an exquisite fishing experience.

  2. Cultural Food Tour: Beiji Village is known for a wide range of delicacies, including soy milk, roast whole lamb, and rice-cooked dish. A tour of the local markets, culinary workshops, and restaurants is an excellent way to experience the village’s unique food culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Beiji Village

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Soy Milk: Beiji Village is known for its authentic Chinese soy milk. With its smooth taste and creamy texture, it is a great way to start the day.

  2. Roast Lamb: Beiji locals roast whole lamb over a charcoal fire to bring out the rich flavor of the meat. The beef and lamb are marinated with a secret recipe and cooked to perfection, offering an explosion of flavors in every bite.

Local Beverages

  1. Beiji Rice Vinegar: Beiji rice vinegar is a local specialty made from the purest water, which is rich in minerals.

  2. Beiji Tea: Beiji tea is made from high-quality tea leaves and is known for its rich aroma, unique flavor, and smooth taste.

Where to Stay in Beiji Village: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Beiji Country Lodge: Nestled in the heart of the village, Beiji Country Lodge, is a cozy countryside guesthouse, with comfortable rooms, and excellent food.

  2. Beiji International Hotel: Beiji International Hotel is a luxurious hotel offering modern facilities, excellent service, and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Beiji Village: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Embroidery: Beiji is famous for its embroidery technique originating from the locals tradition. Visitors can purchase the handicrafts directly from the local vendors.

  2. Beiji Black Garlic: Beiji black garlic is known for its rich nutritional value and unique flavor. It is an excellent souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Beiji Village

The village’s rich cultural heritage is deeply rooted in traditions and customs passed down over generations. Visitors can witness and experience the unique Beiji cultures and customs through local festivals, dance performances, and workshops.

Best Time to Visit Beiji Village

The best time to visit Beiji Village is during the warm summer months between June and August when visitors can enjoy the most pleasant weather and the blossoming nature of the region. Winter is also a great time to visit Beiji Village for winter activities such as skiing and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beiji Village

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions;
  • Try the local dishes;
  • Hire a local guide for exploring the area to learn more about the village’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment;
  • Disturb the local wildlife;
  • Take photographs of locals without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beiji Village

Beiji Village is a safe and peaceful village, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take basic precautions such as staying in safe and secure accommodations and avoid dark alleys and remote areas at night.

Health in Beiji Village

Visitors should ensure that they are medically up-to-date and vaccinated for any potential risks such as flu or other communicable diseases. It is advisable to stay hydrated, use sunscreens, and protect themselves against mosquito bites.

Getting to Beiji Village: Transportation Options and Logistics

  1. Airports Near Beiji Village: Heilongjiang’s main airport is the Harbin Taiping International Airport, which is located about 80 miles from Beiji Village.

  2. Bus Stations Near Beiji Village: The village is accessible by buses from Harbin, Qiqihar, and other nearby cities, with frequent buses making the journey throughout the day.

  3. Other Options: Visitors can also hire a private car, which can be arranged through their hotel to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in Beiji Village

Visitors can explore the village on foot or hire a bike. Taxis are also available in selected locations.

Where to Go Next from Beiji Village: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Harbin: Harbin, the city of ice, is nearby to Beiji village- home to the world-renowned Harbin International Ice and Snow Sculpture Festival.

  2. Qiqihar: Qiqihar, another scenic city 150km from Beiji village, boasts a rich culture and history, including the Zhalong Wetland nature reserve, one of the world’s largest wetland areas.

Conclusion

Beiji Village offers an unforgettable rural escape, with panoramic views of the countryside, rich cultural experiences, and unforgettable culinary experiences. Plan a trip to Beiji Village for an experience that is as diverse as it is enthralling.
