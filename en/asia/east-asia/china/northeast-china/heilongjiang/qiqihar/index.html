Introduction to Qiqihar

Nestled in the northeastern corner of China, Qiqihar is a charming city with a rich history and natural beauty. One of the largest cities in Heilongjiang province, Qiqihar is home to diverse ethnic groups and a mix of modern and traditional culture. Visitors flock to Qiqihar to explore its many museums, historical sites, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a cultural experience, a scenic escape, or a taste of local cuisine, Qiqihar has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Qiqihar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Qiqihar has a long and fascinating history that is showcased in its many museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit attractions for culture lovers:

  • The Museum of General Ma Zhanshan - One of the most famous museums in Qiqihar, this site showcases the life and achievements of General Ma Zhanshan, a legendary figure in Chinese history.
  • The Zhalong Nature Reserve Museum - This museum is dedicated to the Zhalong Nature Reserve, a wetland reserve that is home to over 300 species of birds.
  • The Qiqihar Museum - Located in the city center, this museum features exhibits on the history and culture of Qiqihar, including artifacts from the Qing dynasty.
  • The Guangfu Ancient Town - This well-preserved ancient town offers a glimpse into traditional Chinese culture and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Qiqihar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Qiqihar’s location on the Songnen Plain and proximity to the Zhalong Nature Reserve make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some natural attractions that should be on your itinerary:

  • Zhalong Nature Reserve - Covering over 2,100 square kilometers, the Zhalong Nature Reserve is a wetland oasis that attracts bird-watchers from around the world.
  • Dali Lake - This scenic lake is surrounded by rolling hills and is a popular destination for boating and fishing.
  • The Qiqihar Forest Park - A sprawling park on the outskirts of the city that features hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of Qiqihar.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qiqihar

Qiqihar’s natural beauty and rural landscapes make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes to consider:

  • Zhalong Nature Reserve Hike - This hike takes you through the Zhalong Nature Reserve, where you can spot rare bird species and other wildlife.
  • Dali Lake Bike Trail - Rent a bike and explore the scenic surroundings of Dali Lake on this leisurely bike trail.
  • The Qiqihar Forest Park Hike - This hike takes you through the lush forests of Qiqihar and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Qiqihar: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, Qiqihar has no shortage of things to do. Here are some top activities and tours to consider:

  • Watch the Crane Dance - Visit the Zhalong Nature Reserve in the early morning to witness the stunning crane dance.
  • Explore the Ancient Town of Guangfu - Take a stroll through this charming town and soak up its historic charm.
  • Try Local Cuisine - Qiqihar is known for its delicious cuisine, with specialties like sturgeon, milk tea, and crispy beef pancakes.
  • Visit the Local Markets - Head to the local markets to shop for souvenirs and locally-made products like rugs and jade carvings.
  • Enjoy the Nightlife - Qiqihar has a vibrant nightlife scene, with bars, clubs, and restaurants that stay open late into the night.

Week-Long Itinerary for Qiqihar

To make the most of your visit to Qiqihar, consider this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museum of General Ma Zhanshan and enjoy the crane dance at Zhalong Nature Reserve.
  • Day 2: Explore the ancient town of Guangfu and sample local cuisine.
  • Day 3: Take a bike ride around Dali Lake and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 4: Visit the Qiqihar Museum and shop for souvenirs at local markets.
  • Day 5: Take a hike in the Qiqihar Forest Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Harbin and explore its famous ice and snow sculptures.
  • Day 7: Enjoy the nightlife in Qiqihar and try some local beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Qiqihar

Qiqihar is known for its delicious cuisine, with a mix of traditional and modern dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sturgeon - A local delicacy, sturgeon is typically steamed or fried and served with soy sauce and other condiments.
  • Crispy Beef Pancakes - These savory pancakes are made with beef, eggs, and green onions and are a popular street food in Qiqihar.
  • Milk Tea - This sweet, milky tea is a popular beverage throughout China, but Qiqihar is known for its unique blend.

Local Beverages

  • Qiqihar Beer - This local brew is made with locally-sourced barley and hops and has a refreshing taste.
  • Erlongshan Mineral Water - Sourced from the Erlongshan Mountains, this mineral water is believed to have health benefits.

Where to Stay in Qiqihar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qiqihar has a range of accommodations to suit any budget, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • Shangri-La Hotel Qiqihar - This upscale hotel offers elegant rooms, a fitness center, and several dining options.
  • Hanting Express Qiqihar Railway Station - This budget-friendly hotel offers clean, comfortable rooms and a convenient location near the railway station.
  • Qiqihar International Youth Hostel - This hostel features dormitory-style rooms, a shared kitchen, and a game room.

What to Buy in Qiqihar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Qiqihar offers plenty of shopping opportunities for souvenirs and locally-made products. Here are some top options:

  • Rugs and Carpets - Qiqihar is known for its hand-woven rugs and carpets, which make great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Jade Carvings - Jade is a symbol of luck and prosperity in China, and Qiqihar is known for its skilled carvers.
  • Local Snacks and Treats - Pick up some sturgeon jerky, crispy beef pancakes, or other local snacks to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Qiqihar

Qiqihar is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from Han, Manchu, Mongolian, and other ethnic groups. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including tea ceremonies, dumpling-making, and other cultural practices.

Best Time to Visit Qiqihar

The best time to visit Qiqihar depends on your preferences, but generally the spring and autumn months offer mild weather and fewer crowds. The winter months can be cold and snowy, but also offer opportunities to see the famous ice and snow sculptures in nearby Harbin.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qiqihar

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the city’s many museums and historical sites.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Overstep boundaries with locals or at cultural sites.
  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Qiqihar is generally a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should take precautions when exploring outdoors or in unfamiliar areas. Drink bottled water and practice good hygiene to avoid illness.

Getting to Qiqihar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Qiqihar is easily accessible by air, train, or bus. Here are some options for getting there:

Airports Near Qiqihar

  • Qiqihar Sanjiazi Airport - This airport offers domestic flights to major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Qiqihar

  • Qiqihar Bus Station - This bus station offers domestic connections to other cities in Heilongjiang and beyond.

Other Options

  • Qiqihar Railway Station - Qiqihar has a major railway station that offers connections to other cities in China, including Harbin.

How to Get Around in Qiqihar

Qiqihar is a relatively compact city and is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Taxis and public buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Qiqihar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Qiqihar is well-connected to other destinations in northeastern China. Here are some options for nearby excursions:

  • Harbin - Known for its ice and snow sculptures, Harbin is a popular destination in winter and is only a few hours from Qiqihar by train.
  • Mudanjiang - This scenic city is known for its incredible natural beauty and is about four hours from Qiqihar by train.
  • Changchun - The capital of Jilin province, Changchun is a major transportation hub and is about five hours from Qiqihar by train.

Conclusion

Qiqihar may not be as well-known as other Chinese cities, but it offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and history that is sure to enchant visitors. Whether you’re a foodie, a nature lover, or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Qiqihar. Plan your visit today and discover the charms of this hidden gem in northeastern China.
