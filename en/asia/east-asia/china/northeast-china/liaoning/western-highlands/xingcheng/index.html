Introduction to Xingcheng

Xingcheng is a small town located in the Western Highlands of Liaoning province in Northeast China. The town has a rich history dating back over 2,000 years and is known for its well-preserved ancient city walls and towers. Xingcheng is also home to beautiful natural scenery, including beaches, lakes, and mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Xingcheng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Xingcheng Ancient City Wall: The best-preserved and most extensive ancient city wall in China, spanning over 2.5 kilometers in length and 12 meters in height.
  • The East and West Watchtowers: Built over 600 years ago, these towers were once used to defend the city against pirates and now offer stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • The Ruins of Liaoyang Guandi Temple: A historical site dating back to the Ming Dynasty, famous for its stone carvings and murals.

Natural Attractions of Xingcheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xingcheng Beach: A beautiful stretch of coastline with clear water and white sand, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Wulong Lake: A picturesque lake surrounded on three sides by mountains, offering a tranquil retreat for fishing, boating, and hiking.
  • Mount Xianma: A popular hiking spot with breathtaking views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xingcheng

Xingcheng offers many scenic hiking and biking routes that take visitors through beautiful natural landscapes and provide stunning views of the town and surrounding area. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Xianma Sweet Lake Scenic Area: A picturesque area with lush forests, sparkling lakes, and beautiful waterfalls, offering a variety of hiking and biking trails.
  • Dongluzi Mountain: A mountain ridge that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside from its peak.
  • Xingcheng Ancient City Wall: The 2.5-kilometer long ancient city wall makes for a beautiful and relatively easy walk with stunning views of the town and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Xingcheng: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing at Wulong Lake: This tranquil lake is a popular spot for fishing enthusiasts and offers rainbow trout, carp, and catfish.
  • Visiting Xingcheng’s Ancient Town: Stroll along the narrow streets of Xingcheng’s ancient town, where you’ll find beautiful gardens, ancient temples, and local handicraft stores.
  • Lingyan Mountain Scenic Area: A beautiful scenic area with waterfalls, forests, and a historic temple.

Food and Drink Scene of Xingcheng

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Being located on the coast, Xingcheng is famous for its fresh seafood dishes. Don’t miss out on the hot and sour seafood soup, fried shrimp and squid, and steamed fish.
  • Pork Belly: A local specialty, pork belly is slow-cooked in a rich sauce and is incredibly tender and flavorful.
  • Lefen: A rice noodle dish that is a local favorite, Lefen is traditionally served with minced meat, vegetables, and a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Huangjiu: A traditional Chinese rice wine that is sweet and slightly alcoholic, Huangjiu is a great pairing with many local dishes.
  • Kuding Tea: A type of Chinese tea made from the leaves of the Kuding tea plant, it has a bitter taste but is believed to have numerous health benefits.
  • Jiuquan: A type of Chinese beer brewed locally, Jiuquan is a refreshing drink for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Xingcheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Xingcheng Hotel: A luxurious hotel located within walking distance of Xingcheng’s ancient town and the beach.
  • Xingcheng Yudong Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms and a shared lounge.
  • The Ancient City Guesthouse: Located within the ancient city wall, this guesthouse offers rooms with traditional Chinese décor and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Xingcheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Xingcheng has a rich tradition of handicrafts, including silk embroidery, paper-cutting, and clay figurines.
  • Local Liquor: Huangjiu, a traditional Chinese rice wine, and Jiuquan, a locally brewed beer, make excellent souvenirs to bring home.
  • Seafood Snacks: Xingcheng is famous for its seafood, and you can find many vendors selling dried octopus, fish, and shrimp that make for tasty and unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Xingcheng

Xingcheng has a rich cultural and historical heritage, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Always dress modestly when visiting religious sites and remove your shoes before entering temples. It’s also customary to bring a small gift when visiting someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Xingcheng

The best time to visit Xingcheng is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and comfortable, and there are fewer crowds. Summer months can be hot and humid, and the beaches can get crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xingcheng

Do:

  • Explore Xingcheng’s Ancient Town: This is a must-see attraction during your visit to Xingcheng.
  • Try the local seafood: Xingcheng is renowned for its fresh seafood, which shouldn’t be missed.
  • Take a boat tour of Wulong Lake: Soak up the peaceful surroundings and enjoy the scenery.

Don’t:

  • Avoid littering: Littering is strictly prohibited in public areas, and offenders may face a fine.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs: Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites and attending cultural events.
  • Do not raise your voice in public or engage in public displays of affection as these are generally frowned upon.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xingcheng

Xingcheng is a safe place to visit, but visitors should always take standard precautions, such as not walking alone at night or carrying large amounts of cash. In case of an emergency, dial 110 for the police or 120 for an ambulance.

Health in Xingcheng

The tap water in Xingcheng is safe for drinking, but visitors should stick to bottled water as a precaution. Mosquitoes can be a problem during the summer months, so bring plenty of insect repellant.

Getting to Xingcheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xingcheng

The nearest airport to Xingcheng is Shenyang Taoxian International Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Xingcheng.

Bus Stations Near Xingcheng

Xingcheng has several bus stations that connect the town to other cities in the region, including Shenyang, Dalian, and Beijing.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Jinzhou or Chaoyang and then take a bus to Xingcheng.

How to Get Around in Xingcheng

Xingcheng is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis are also available for longer journeys, but visitors should confirm the fare before getting in.

Where to Go Next from Xingcheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jinzhou: A nearby city famous for its Jinzhou Ancient City Wall and the Jinzhou Museum.
  • Panjin: A coastal city known for its wetland ecological reserve and the “red beach,” a natural phenomenon where the beach turns red in autumn.
  • Dalian: A modern port city with stunning beaches, historic sites, and a vibrant dining and nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Xingcheng is a hidden gem in Northeast China that offers visitors a fascinating glimpse into traditional Chinese culture and history. With its well-preserved ancient city wall, stunning natural scenery, and delicious local cuisine, Xingcheng is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience.
