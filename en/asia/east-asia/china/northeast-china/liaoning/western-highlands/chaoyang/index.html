Introduction to Chaoyang

Are you looking for a travel destination in China that is off the beaten path? Then Chaoyang is the perfect place for you. Located in Western Highlands, in the Liaoning province of Northeast China, Chaoyang is a hidden gem that boasts an impressive collection of museums, historical places, natural wonders, and picturesque landscapes. Whether you are looking to explore the outdoors, indulge in the local culture or just relax in the serene countryside, Chaoyang has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Chaoyang. From the best places to visit, to the top-rated activities and tours, we’ve got you covered. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Chaoyang

Chaoyang is a city full of wonders and hidden gems. Here are some of the best places to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Chaoyang Museum: Learn about the history of Chaoyang and its cultural heritage through the artifacts, paintings, and exhibits that are on display.
  2. Chaoyang Ancient City Wall: Explore the well-preserved remains of the ancient city wall, which was constructed during the Ming Dynasty (1368 – 1644).
  3. Dongzhong Cave Scenic Area: Admire the stunning natural rock formations that have been shaped by millions of years of erosion.

Natural Attractions of Chaoyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Linghai Forest Park: Hike through the forested trails and admire the serene beauty of the lake and towering peaks.
  2. Xishan National Forest Park: This park offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain ranges and a serene pavilion that is popular for watching the sunrise and sunset.
  3. Shenwo Reservoir Scenic Area: This area offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chaoyang

If you love the outdoors, you’ll be happy to know that Chaoyang offers a wide range of hiking/biking options. Here are some of the best trails:

  1. Dongzhong Cave Hiking Trail: This is a scenic 7-kilometer trail that takes you through the beautiful countryside and stunning rock formations of the Dongzhong Cave Scenic Area.
  2. Linghai Mountain Hiking Trail: This is a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the mountain ranges and Linghai Forest Park.
  3. Chaoyang Ancient City Wall Biking Trail: This trail leads you through the ancient city wall and offers a unique perspective of Chaoyang’s rich history.

Best Things to Do in Chaoyang: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider during your visit to Chaoyang:

  1. Chaoyang Cultural Day Tour: This guided tour will take you to the most culturally significant places in the city, including museums, historical sites, and temples.
  2. Outdoor Adventure Tour: This tour will take you to some of the best natural wonders in Chaoyang, including Linghai Forest Park and Shenwo Reservoir Scenic Area.
  3. Shenwo Reservoir Boating Adventure: Rent a boat and explore the peaceful waters of Shenwo Reservoir.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chaoyang

If you’re planning to spend a week in Chaoyang, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Arrival and Chaoyang Ancient City Wall tour Day 2: Dongzhong Cave Scenic Area tour and hiking Day 3: Linghai Mountain hiking and Linghai Forest Park tour Day 4: Shenwo Reservoir Scenic Area outdoor adventure tour Day 5: Cultural day tour of Chaoyang Day 6: Shenwo Reservoir boating adventure Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Chaoyang

No trip to Chaoyang is complete without indulging in its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chaoyang Fried Rice: A simple yet delicious dish made with rice, egg, and a variety of meats and vegetables.
  2. Black Bean Noodles: A local favorite made with thick, chewy noodles, spicy sauce, and a mix of vegetables and meat.
  3. Huntington Pork: A savory pork dish made with a special sauce and aromatic spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Chaoyang Pear Wine: A popular local wine made with fresh pears and other natural ingredients.
  2. Chaoyang Ginger Tea: A warm and soothing tea made with fresh ginger, sugar, and hot water.

Where to Stay in Chaoyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chaoyang has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some top-rated options:

  1. Lavande Hotel Chaoyang: A modern hotel with spacious rooms and amenities such as free Wi-Fi and on-site restaurants.
  2. Qian Yun Lou Courtyard Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse featuring beautifully decorated rooms and a peaceful courtyard.
  3. Chaoyang Huatian Hotel: A luxurious hotel with a spa, fitness center, and on-site restaurants.

What to Buy in Chaoyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chaoyang is known for its intricate handicrafts and traditional products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  1. Linghai Embroidery: Handmade embroidered items, such as bags, clothes, and wall hangings with intricate designs.
  2. Chaoyang Paper Cut: Traditional Chinese paper-cut art featuring designs of animals, flowers, and other cultural symbols.
  3. Chaoyang Ginger: Fresh ginger grown locally and used in many local dishes and drinks.

Local Culture and Customs of Chaoyang

Chaoyang has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its art, music, and festivals. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it’s customary to greet them with a smile and a nod. It’s also important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and to avoid being overly noisy or disruptive in public places.

Best Time to Visit Chaoyang

The best time to visit Chaoyang is during the spring and autumn months (April to June, September to November), when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its prettiest. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chaoyang

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages
  3. Explore the natural beauty of Chaoyang

Don’t:

  1. Be overly noisy or disruptive in public places
  2. Ignore local customs and etiquette
  3. Leave trash or litter in public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Chaoyang

Chaoyang is generally a safe place for visitors. However, it is always advisable to keep your belongings secured when out and about, and to avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas at night.

Health in Chaoyang

Chaoyang has good medical facilities and hospitals that are equipped to deal with most health issues. However, it’s essential to carry local health insurance, as medical costs can be high.

Getting to Chaoyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chaoyang

The nearest airport to Chaoyang is the Shenyang Taoxian International Airport, which is about 160 kilometers away. From here, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Chaoyang.

Bus Stations Near Chaoyang

Chaoyang has several bus stations that provide regional and intercity transport. The most popular ones are the Chaoyang North Station and the Chaoyang South Station.

Other Options

You can also get to Chaoyang by train or by car. Chaoyang has a train station that connects it to major cities in China. If you prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies in Chaoyang, and the city is connected to other parts of China by a network of highways.

How to Get Around in Chaoyang

Chaoyang has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and bicycles for rent. Taxis are reasonably priced, and buses are an affordable option for traveling longer distances. If you prefer to explore the city at your own pace, renting a bicycle is a great option.

Where to Go Next from Chaoyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations and day trips to consider:

  1. Shenyang: This historic city is known for its beautiful gardens, palaces, and museums. It’s located just over 2 hours away from Chaoyang by train.
  2. Beijing: China’s capital city is home to several historic landmarks, including the Great Wall of China and the Forbidden City. It’s located about 4 hours away from Chaoyang by train.
  3. Dalian: This coastal city is famous for its beaches, seafood, and modern architecture. It’s located about 4 hours away from Chaoyang by train.

Conclusion

Chaoyang may not be as famous as some of the other cities in China, but it is definitely worth a visit. With its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and unique cuisine, Chaoyang is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to Chaoyang. Safe travels!
