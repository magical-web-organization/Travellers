Introduction to Jinzhou

Jinzhou is a hidden gem located in Western Highlands, in Liaoning province, Northeast China. The city is surrounded by beautiful mountains and overlooked by the majestic Taihang Mountain Range, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Jinzhou is also rich in history and culture, as it has been a vital military and economic center for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Jinzhou

Jinzhou offers something for everyone, whether you are interested in history, culture or nature. Here are the top attractions to see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jinzhou Museum: This museum offers a unique insight into the history of Jinzhou and its role in shaping the region’s culture and civilization. You can see relics and artifacts from the Neolithic period up to the Qing Dynasty, as well as learn about the city’s famous iron and steel industry.

  • Taihang Mountain Grottoes: These grottoes are located at the foot of the Taihang Mountain range and offer a spectacular view of the surrounding nature. The grottoes are filled with carvings and paintings that are more than 1500 years old, telling the story of Buddhism in the region.

  • Chaoyang Tower: This ancient tower was built in 1068 AD and is now a protected cultural heritage site. The tower is an excellent example of Liao dynasty architecture and offers fantastic views of the city and the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Jinzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Linghe National Forest Park: This park is home to some of the most stunning views in Northeast China, with lush forests and waterfalls. The park offers a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and camping.

  • Jinxiu Mountain Scenic Area: This area is one of Jinzhou’s hidden gems, located only 30km from the city center. It offers some of the best views of the Taihang Mountain range and is perfect for a scenic hike or bike ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jinzhou

Jinzhou offers a range of hiking and biking routes which are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best:

  • Jinxiu Mountain Scenic Area: This area has several hiking trails with varying difficulty levels, from easy walks to challenging hikes. The trails offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Taihang Mountain Scenic Area: This is another popular destination for hikers and bikers, as it offers a range of trails that traverse through some of the most beautiful and scenic landscapes in China.

Best Things to Do in Jinzhou: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours to enjoy in Jinzhou, catering to all interests:

  • Cycling tours: Jinzhou offers some of the best cycling routes in Northeast China, with scenic views of the Taihang Mountain range.

  • Hiking tours: For those who love the great outdoors, Jinzhou has some of the best hiking trails in the region, such as the Linghe National Forest Park or Jinxiu Mountain Scenic Area.

  • Culture and History tours: Jinzhou’s rich history and culture are worth exploring, and there are several tours that take visitors to historical sites such as Jinzhou Museum or the Chaoyang Tower.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jinzhou

Here’s an example of a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Jinzhou:

Day 1 Morning: Arrival at Jinzhou. Afternoon: Explore Jinzhou Museum, one of the most comprehensive museums in the region.
Day 2 Morning: Visit Taihang Mountain Grottoes with stunning carvings and paintings that are more than 1500 years old. Afternoon: Take a stroll around Chaoyang Tower and enjoy its unique architecture.
Day 3 Morning: Visit the Linghe National Forest Park and explore its lush forests and waterfalls. Afternoon: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Tieling Lake, located just outside the city.
Day 4 Morning: Take a cycling tour through Jinzhou’s countryside and enjoy the scenic views. Afternoon: Learn about Jinzhou’s iron and steel industry at the Anshan Iron and Steel Museum.
Day 5 Morning: Explore Jinxiu Mountain Scenic Area with its beautiful scenery and hiking trails. Afternoon: Visit Kongwang Mountain, a sacred Taoist mountain with stunning views.
Day 6 Morning: Take a day trip to Shenyang, the capital city of Liaoning province. Visit Shenyang Palace Museum and learn about the region’s rich history. Afternoon: Explore Zhongjie Pedestrian Street, one of Shenyang’s famous shopping districts.
Day 7 Morning: Visit the Fenghuangshan National Geological Park, famous for its breath-taking landscapes and geological wonders. Afternoon: Rest and pack before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Jinzhou

Jinzhou’s food and drink scene is diverse, with many delicious local dishes to try and unique drinks to sample.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jinzhou Style Grilled Oysters: A popular local dish, the oysters are grilled until they are crispy on the outside but tender on the inside. They are usually served with garlic and soy sauce.

  • Jinzhou Style Pork Shank: This dish is made by slow-cooking a pork shank in a broth made of soy sauce, sugar, and spices, until it becomes tender and flavorful.

Local Beverages

  • Dongbei Baijiu: This is a strong Chinese spirit that is popular in Northeast China. It’s made from various grains, such as wheat, barley, and corn, and is usually consumed as a shot.

  • Jinzhou Beer: Jinzhou has its local brewery, so don’t forget to try the local brews when you are visiting the city.

What to Buy in Jinzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jinzhou is famous for its iron and steel industry, and you can buy several souvenirs and local products made of metal, such as knives, chopsticks, and ornaments. You can also find traditional Chinese handicrafts, such as paper-cuttings and embroidery.

Local Culture and Customs of Jinzhou

Jinzhou has a rich history and culture, and you can learn about it through visiting the city’s museums, the Taihang Mountain Grottoes, and the Chaoyang Tower. The city is also known for its traditional craft making, and visitors can participate in classes and workshops to learn how to make local crafts.

Best Time to Visit Jinzhou

The best time to visit Jinzhou is in the spring and autumn, as the weather is mild and comfortable. The city can be hot and humid in the summer and very cold in the winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jinzhou

  • Try the local dishes and drinks.

  • Take advantage of Jinzhou’s outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any behavior that could be seen as disrespectful to the local culture.

  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Take photos of people or places without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jinzhou

Jinzhou is a relatively safe city, but standard precautions should be taken, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and not carrying large amounts of money or valuables in crowded areas.

Health in Jinzhou

The tap water in Jinzhou is not safe to drink, so always drink bottled water. Make sure you have comprehensive health insurance before visiting China.

Getting to Jinzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jinzhou is easily accessible by air, train, and bus.

Airports Near Jinzhou

The nearest airport is Jinzhou Xiaolingzi Airport. Several airlines offer daily flights from major Chinese cities.

Bus Stations Near Jinzhou

Jinzhou has several bus stations that connect it to major cities in China, such as Beijing, Shenyang, and Dalian.

Other Options

The city is also connected to the national railway network, making it easy to reach by train from any corner of China.

How to Get Around in Jinzhou

Jinzhou offers several transportation options, including taxis, buses, and the subway. Taxis are affordable, and the buses and subway are an excellent way to explore the city and its outskirts.

Where to Go Next from Jinzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jinzhou is a great starting point for exploring the Northeast China region. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Shenyang: The capital city of Liaoning province is just a short train ride away and offers travelers plenty to see and do, such as the Shenyang Palace Museum and Zhongjie Pedestrian Street.

  • Panjin Red Beach: This is one of China’s most beautiful natural wonders, located just a short distance outside Jinzhou. The beach gets its unique red color from the Suaeda Salsa plant, which thrives in the area.

Conclusion

Jinzhou is a hidden gem of Northeast China that deserves to be explored. With its rich history, culture, and stunning natural landscapes, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone who loves adventure and outdoor activities. Whether you’re planning a short trip or a more extended stay, there’s something for everyone in Jinzhou, so don’t miss out!
