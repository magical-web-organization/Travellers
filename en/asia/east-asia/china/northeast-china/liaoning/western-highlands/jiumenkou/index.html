Jiumenkou is a hidden gem nestled in the Western Highlands of Liaoning, which is a province located in Northeast China. This secluded, yet fascinating destination offers a diverse array of cultural, historical, and natural attractions, as well as excellent opportunities for outdoor activities and sightseeing. In this ultimate guide to Jiumenkou, we will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, and travel tips to make the most out of your trip to this off-the-beaten-track destination.

Introduction to Jiumenkou

Located in the city of Liaoyang in Liaoning Province, Jiumenkou is a small town famous for its ancient bridge, which is also the main attraction in the area. The Jiumenkou Bridge, also known as the Nine-Arched Bridge, is believed to be the oldest and largest stone bridge in China, dating back to the early Ming Dynasty around 1380. The bridge spans over the Hun River and is a unique example of ancient Chinese engineering and architecture. Apart from this, Jiumenkou is also home to several other cultural and natural landmarks that offer great experiences to travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Jiumenkou

Jiumenkou boasts a plethora of attractions that appeal to all types of tourists, ranging from history buffs to nature enthusiasts. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Jiumenkou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jiumenkou Bridge: This historic bridge is the main attraction in Jiumenkou and should not be missed during your trip. Take a walk on the bridge and admire its unique architecture and beautiful views of the Hun River.

  2. Baita Temple: Located near the Jiumenkou Bridge, Baita Temple is a beautiful Buddhist temple built in the Tang Dynasty. The temple is famous for its unique architecture and magnificent statues of Buddha.

  3. China Liaoning Jiumenkou Great Wall Museum: This is a fascinating museum that showcases the history and culture of the Great Wall of China. The museum has an extensive collection of relics and artifacts related to the construction and maintenance of the Great Wall.

Natural Attractions of Jiumenkou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Honglu Mountain: Located in the western part of Jiumenkou, Honglu Mountain is a beautiful scenic area that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Hiking to the top of the mountain is a popular activity for tourists.

  2. The Hun River: The Hun River is an ideal spot for boating, fishing, and camping. The river also offers incredible views of the Jiumenkou Bridge and the surrounding mountains.

  3. Qingliangshan National Forest Park: This national park situated near Jiumenkou is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. The park offers excellent opportunities for hiking, camping, and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiumenkou

Jiumenkou is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering several hiking and biking trails that lead to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the area. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes in Jiumenkou:

  1. Jiumenkou to Honglu Mountain: This is a moderate-level hiking trail that takes around 4-5 hours to complete. The route takes you through beautiful forests, scenic hills, and stunning waterfalls before reaching Honglu Mountain.

  2. Hun River Cycling Route: This is a popular cycling route that takes you along the Hun River, offering beautiful views of nature and the Jiumenkou Bridge.

  3. Qingliangshan National Forest Park Hiking Trail: This is a challenging hiking trail that takes you through a dense forest and steep hills, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Jiumenkou: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor activities and sightseeing, Jiumenkou also offers several exciting things to do and tours to take during your visit. Here are some of the best experiences to try in Jiumenkou:

  1. Boat Tour on Hun River: Take a boat tour on the Hun River and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and the Jiumenkou Bridge.

  2. Temple Visits: Visit the Baita Temple and other nearby temples, where you can meditate, learn about Buddhism, and admire the beautiful architecture and artwork.

  3. Experience Local Culture: Attend local festivals and events, such as the Jiumenkou Bridge Festival and the Dragon Boat Festival, where you can experience the traditions, folklore, and cuisine of Jiumenkou.

Food and Drink Scene of Jiumenkou

No trip to Jiumenkou would be complete without trying the local cuisine and drinks. Jiumenkou’s food culture is influenced by its neighboring provinces and has a unique blend of flavors and spices. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Jiumenkou:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Horse and Donkey Meat Dishes: Jiumenkou is famous for its horse and donkey meat dishes, such as Kebabs and Stew. These dishes are usually served with vegetables and noodles.

  2. Matou Stewed Chicken: Matou Stewed Chicken is a popular local delicacy in Jiumenkou. The chicken is stewed with ginger, onions, and other spices, resulting in a tender and flavorful dish.

Local Beverages

  1. Liaoning Rice Wine: This is a traditional rice wine made from fermented rice and is a popular drink in Liaoning Province.

  2. Herbal Tea: Jiumenkou’s herbal tea is made from a blend of natural herbs and spices, and is believed to have several health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiumenkou

Jiumenkou has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs that are worth experiencing during your visit. Here are some of the local culture and customs of Jiumenkou:

  1. Dragon Boat Festival: This festival is celebrated annually in June and features boat races, dragon dances, and traditional food and games.

  2. Folklore and Legends: Jiumenkou has several interesting folklore and legends associated with its history and culture. The locals are happy to share their stories and traditions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Jiumenkou

The best time to visit Jiumenkou is during spring and autumn, when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are breathtaking. The summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.
