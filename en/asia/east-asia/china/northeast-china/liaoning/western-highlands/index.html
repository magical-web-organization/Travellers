Introduction to Western Highlands

Western Highlands is a region located in Liaoning Province of Northeast China. The region boasts stunning natural landscapes, rich historical and cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. It is an ideal destination for adventure seekers, history buffs, foodies, and anyone who loves to explore new places.

List of Places to Visit in Western Highlands

Here are some of the top places to visit in Western Highlands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Benxi Shuidong National Park: This national park is home to the famous Benxi Water Cave, which includes a boat ride through an underground river. The park also has many hiking trails and scenic views.

  • Fushun War Criminals Management Center: This historical site is dedicated to the Japanese war criminals who were sentenced to imprisonment in Fushun after World War II. Visitors can learn about their stories and the punishment they received.

  • Benxi Shuidonggou National Forest Park: This forest park offers visitors the chance to explore ancient petroglyphs, rock formations, and stunning natural scenery.

Natural Attractions of Western Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pingding Mountain: This UNESCO World Heritage site offers stunning mountain views and outdoor activities, such as hiking trails and rock climbing.

  • Xingcheng Beach: This beach is a perfect getaway for those looking to relax by the sea. With its crystal clear water and white sandy beaches, it is a tranquil paradise that one should not miss while visiting Western Highlands.

  • Liaohe River: This river spans across several Chinese provinces, and is a perfect spot to go rafting, kayaking, or just enjoy a scenic river cruise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Highlands

Western Highlands offers a range of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Some of the must-try trails include:

  • Shenyang-Suizhong Off-road Bike Path: This biking trail spans 200 kilometers and offers stunning views of the countryside and coastline.

  • Jangsu Mountain Scenic Area: This hiking trail is popular among locals and offers breath-taking views of nature and wildlife.

  • Guanshan National Forest Park: This park offers challenging hikes that lead to stunning views of the forest, rivers, and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Western Highlands: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural attractions and hiking trails, Western Highlands also offers several fun-filled activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Shenyang Imperial Palace: This ancient palace is the former residence of the Qing Dynasty’s emperors and offers history buffs a glimpse into the royal lifestyle of a bygone era.

  • Biking tours in Western Highlands: Explore the scenic beauty of Western Highlands on a bike tour, led by experienced guides who are familiar with the most picturesque routes and sites.

  • River rafting on the Liaohe River: This adventure activity is great for those who love adrenaline-pumping experiences. With experienced guides, visitors can safely enjoy the rapids and scenic views of the Liaohe River.

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Western Highlands:

Food and Drink Scene of Western Highlands

Western Highlands has delicious local cuisine that visitors must try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Naicha: A savory, meat-stuffed bun that is a popular street food in Western Highlands.

  • Liaoning-style Noodles: A famous local dish made with flat noodles, seafood, and vegetables in a spicy and savory sauce.

  • Shenbei Shaguo: A hotpot dish that includes a range of ingredients that visitors can cook in a pot at their table.

Local Beverages

  • Laobaigan: A traditional fermented alcoholic beverage made from sorghum.

  • Suanmeitang: A sweet and sour drink made with plum and honey.

  • Milk Bubble Tea: A Taiwanese tea-based drink that is popular in Western Highlands.

Where to Stay in Western Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Western Highlands has several options for accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • Dawn Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in downtown Shenyang, with modern amenities and a prime location.

  • Roseiban Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers, this hostel offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price point.

  • Jinjiang International Hotel: A large, 5-star hotel with amenities such as a pool, spa, and fitness center.

What to Buy in Western Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that visitors can take back from Western Highlands:

  • Naicha Buns: These savory meat buns are a popular snack in Western Highlands and make for a great souvenir to bring back home.

  • Suanmeitang Tea: A sweet and sour plum tea can be purchased as a souvenir.

  • Jinsanjiao Tea: A local tea grown in the foothills of the mountains of Western Highlands is a great souvenir for tea lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Western Highlands

Western Highlands is a region with a rich cultural heritage. The people of Western Highlands have their unique customs and festivals that are worth exploring. The Lantern Festival is a famous annual event held in Liaoning Province that visitors can experience and learn more about the culture.

Best Time to Visit Western Highlands

The best time to visit Western Highlands is between April and October when the weather is mild and dry, and the natural scenery is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western Highlands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Carry your ID and travel documents with you at all times.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious or sacred sites.

Don’t:

  • Do not engage in illegal activities, behavior, or drugs.

  • Do not litter or damage the natural or cultural sites.

  • Do not disrespect the local customs and beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Western Highlands

Western Highlands is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors are advised to take normal safety precautions, such as avoiding deserted areas at night and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables in public.

Health in Western Highlands

Visitors are advised to be up-to-date with their routine vaccinations before traveling to Western Highlands. Travelers should also take steps to prevent mosquito bites and avoid consuming local waters that are not safe.

Getting to Western Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to get to Western Highlands:

Airports Near Western Highlands

  • Shenyang Taoxian International Airport: The nearest airport to Western Highlands is located in Shenyang, and it offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Western Highlands

  • Shenyang Central Bus Station: A major bus station that offers frequent bus services to Western Highlands.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to rent a car, take a taxi or join guided tours to explore Western Highlands.

How to Get Around in Western Highlands

Public transportation, such as buses and subways, is available for visitors to get around Western Highlands. Taxis and ride-share services such as Didi, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Western Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some destinations to explore after visiting Western Highlands:

  • Shenyang: Explore the ancient city and visit Manchu-style palaces and buildings such as the Shenyang Imperial Palace and Zhaoling Mausoleum of Qing Dynasty.

  • Dandong: Visit the stunning Yalu River Park, Yalu River Broken Bridge or venture into North Korea, separated by the Yalu River.

Conclusion

Western Highlands offers visitors a rich cultural and natural experience with adventure activities, cultural sites, delicious cuisine, and luxury accommodations. Explore the region’s stunning landscapes, historic sites, and bustling cities, and take back memories that will last a lifetime.
