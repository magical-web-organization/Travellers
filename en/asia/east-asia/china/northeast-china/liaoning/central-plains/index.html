Introduction to Central Plains

Central Plains is located in Liaoning province in Northeast China. It is an economically important region and one of the birthplaces of the Chinese civilization. It is home to a vast array of cultural and natural attractions, including historical sites, outdoor recreation areas, and traditional villages.

List of Places to Visit in Central Plains

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Central Plains:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shenyang Imperial Palace: This UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site served as the imperial palace of the Qing Dynasty in Northeast China.

  • Marshal Zhang’s Mansion: This historical site is a mix of Chinese and Western architectural styles and is dedicated to the celebrated marshal Zhang Zuolin.

  • Fuling Tomb of Qing Dynasty: This is the final resting place of Nurhaci, the founder of the Qing Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Central Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xianghai National Nature Reserve: This reserve includes a scenic lake that is home to many species of birds and other wildlife.

  • Qianshan National Park: Visitors can enjoy scenic mountain views while hiking or taking a cable car to the top.

  • Yiwulü Mountain Scenic Area: This area is home to a mix of natural and man-made attractions, including waterfalls, temples, and pagodas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Plains

  • Shenyang Olympic Park Tiyuguan Road: This scenic route features parks, gardens, and the Shenyang Olympic Stadium.

  • Qianshan National Park: There are several hiking and biking trails with stunning views.

  • Xianren Peak Scenic Area: This remote area offers quiet, off-the-beaten-path hiking routes.

Best Things to Do in Central Plains: Activities and Tours

  • Shenyang International Ice and Snow Festival: This annual festival showcases ice sculptures and other winter-themed activities.

  • North Street: This pedestrian street is known for its shopping and restaurants.

  • Liaoning Science and Technology Museum: This museum has interactive and engaging exhibits on science and technology.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Plains

The food in Central Plains is influenced by the region’s location between China and Korea. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shenyang Fried Rice: This dish features fluffy rice with a mix of vegetables, meat, and eggs.

  • Mantou: This is a popular Chinese steamed bun and a staple in many meals.

  • Liaoning Clams: These seafood specialties are typically boiled until tender and served in a spicy soup.

Local Beverages

  • Rice Wine: This is a traditional liquor made from fermented rice and is a popular local beverage.

  • Shenqu Liquor: This is a strong alcoholic beverage made from distilled spirits and fermented grains.

Where to Stay in Central Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shenyang is the largest city in Central Plains and offers a wide range of accommodations to suit different preferences and budgets. Some popular choices include:

  • Kempinski Hotel Shenyang: This hotel offers luxury accommodations and is conveniently located in the city center.

  • Shenyang International Youth Hostel: This hostel offers budget-friendly accommodations for backpackers and solo travelers.

  • Minghui International Hotel: This hotel is located near the Shenyang International Convention and Exhibition Center and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Central Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Central Plains include:

  • Wooden Carvings: This handicraft is a traditional art form in Northeast China and features intricate designs crafted from wood.

  • Korean Clothes and Accessories: Central Plains is home to many Korean communities, and visitors can buy traditional clothing and accessories in local markets.

  • Seafood and Dried Fruits: These are popular food souvenirs that are perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Plains

Central Plains has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience its unique customs and traditions. Some notable practices include:

  • Traditional Festivals: Central Plains celebrates many festivals throughout the year, including the Lantern Festival and Mid-Autumn Festival.

  • Northeastern Dialect: The local dialect has a distinct accent and vocabulary that is different from standard Mandarin.

  • Folklore: Central Plains is home to many folk songs, dances, and other traditions that reflect the region’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Central Plains

The best time to visit Central Plains is from April to October, as this time offers the most pleasant weather and the most outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Plains

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

  • Visit historical sites and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local people or customs.

  • Take photos of people without permission.

  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Plains

Central Plains is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should watch out for:

  • Pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas.

  • Traffic accidents, especially when crossing the street or riding a bike.

  • Altitude sickness when hiking or visiting high altitude areas.

Health in Central Plains

Visitors should take precautions to avoid contracting illnesses or diseases while in Central Plains. Some tips include:

  • Drinking only bottled water or boiled water.

  • Using mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

  • Getting vaccinated against common illnesses like hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Central Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central Plains

  • Shenyang Taoxian International Airport: This airport serves the city of Shenyang and is located about 20 miles from the city center.

  • Anshan Teng’ao Airport: This airport serves the city of Anshan and is located about 10 miles from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Central Plains

  • Shenyang North Bus Station: This station serves long-distance bus routes to many destinations in China.

  • Shenyang East Bus Station: This station serves routes to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Train: The rail network in Central Plains is extensive, and there are many high-speed trains that connect the region to other cities in China.

  • Taxi/Car Rental: Taxis and car rentals are widely available in Central Plains, and visitors can also opt for ride-sharing services like Didi.

How to Get Around in Central Plains

Public transportation options in Central Plains include buses, subways, and taxis. Visitors can also opt to rent a bike or scooter to explore the cities and countryside.

Where to Go Next from Central Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Central Plains include:

  • Dalian: This coastal city is known for its beaches and seafood cuisine.

  • Harbin: This city is famous for its annual Ice and Snow Festival and winter activities.

  • Changbai Mountains: This scenic mountain range is located on the border of China and North Korea and is home to the Changbai Waterfall and Changbai Hot Springs.

Conclusion

Central Plains in Liaoning, China, is a region that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions, including historical sites, outdoor recreation areas, and traditional villages. With its rich history and culture, local delicacies, and varied landscapes, Central Plains is perfect for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in China.
