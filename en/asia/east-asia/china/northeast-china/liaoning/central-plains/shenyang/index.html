Introduction to Shenyang

Shenyang is the largest city in Liaoning province in northeastern China. With a long and rich history dating back to the Qing dynasty, Shenyang is home to numerous historical and cultural attractions as well as modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Shenyang

If you’re planning a trip to Shenyang, here are some of the top places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Shenyang Imperial Palace - also known as the Mukden Palace, this UNESCO World Heritage site was the former palace of the Qing dynasty’s emperors.
  2. Liaoning Provincial Museum - located in the heart of Shenyang, this museum showcases the history and culture of Liaoning province.
  3. Marshal Zhang’s Mansion - once the residence of the war hero Zhang Zuolin, this mansion provides a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the warlords of the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Shenyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Qipan Mountain Scenic Area - located in the northeast of Shenyang, this area is home to stunning rock formations and hiking trails.
  2. Dongling Park - a beautiful park with hiking trails, gardens, and scenic views of the city.
  3. Zhaoling Mausoleum - the final resting place of Emperor Huang Taiji of the Qing dynasty, this mausoleum is surrounded by picturesque landscape and offers a glimpse into ancient Chinese culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shenyang

  1. Shenyang Botanical Garden - a scenic garden with numerous trails for hiking and biking
  2. Fuling Tomb - located outside the city, this tomb offers stunning views and a challenging hike.

Best Things to Do in Shenyang: Activities and Tours

  1. Eat at a local night market - try local specialties like stewed pig’s feet, fried dumplings, and spicy lamb skewers.
  2. Take a historic walking tour - explore the rich history and architecture of Shenyang with a knowledgeable guide.
  3. Watch a performance at the Shenyang Grand Theatre - this state-of-the-art theatre hosts world-class performances and is a must-see.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shenyang

Day 1-2: Visit the cultural attractions - spend a day or two exploring the Imperial Palace, Liaoning Provincial Museum, and Marshal Zhang’s Mansion.

Day 3-4: Enjoy the natural attractions - hike and bike through Qipan Mountain Scenic Area and Dongling Park.

Day 5-6: Take in modern Shenyang - explore the city’s shopping and dining scene, and catch a performance at the Shenyang Grand Theatre.

Day 7: Visit a nearby attraction - take a day trip to Fushun’s Wu’aer Mountain to see stunning rock formations and ancient ruins.

Food and Drink Scene of Shenyang

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stewed Pig’s Feet - a traditional Shenyang dish and made with pickled vegetables and soy sauce.
  2. Fried Dumplings - crispy dumplings filled with meat or vegetables.
  3. Spicy Lamb Skewers - grilled lamb skewers seasoned with cumin and chili.

Local Beverages

  1. Harbin Beer - a popular beer throughout China, especially in the northeast.
  2. Erguotou - a strong and potent spirit made from sorghum.

Where to Stay in Shenyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Somerset Heping Shenyang - a luxurious hotel in the city center.
  2. Shenyang Grace Inn Hotel - a budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  3. Shenyang Sinuoai Yizuo Apartment - a spacious and modern apartment-style accommodation.
  4. Shenyang Carat Hotel - a mid-range hotel with great amenities, including a swimming pool and gym.

What to Buy in Shenyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Shenyang Jade - this white jade is unique to Shenyang and makes for a beautiful souvenir.
  2. Northeastern Snacks - pick up boxes of local snacks like fried dough twists and sunflower seeds as gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Shenyang

Traditionally, Shenyang has been a center of military and political power in China. As a result, local culture values strength, bravery, and resilience. Visitors should be respectful of these values and traditions when interacting with locals. Additionally, taking shoes off before entering a home or temple is customary in Shenyang.

Best Time to Visit Shenyang

The best time to visit Shenyang is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shenyang

Do:

  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Negatively comment on China’s political system or government.
  • Touch or remove artifacts or objects from historic sites or museums.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shenyang

Shenyang is generally a safe city, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should still be cautious when wandering alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Shenyang

Tap water in Shenyang is not safe for drinking, and visitors should opt for bottled water. Air pollution can also be a concern in the city, especially during winter months.

Getting to Shenyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shenyang

Shenyang Taoxian International Airport is the main airport serving the city and receives international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Shenyang

Shenyang has several bus stations servicing nearby cities and towns, including Shenyang North Bus Station and Shenyang South Bus Station.

Other Options

High-speed trains connect Shenyang with Beijing, Dalian, and other major cities throughout China.

How to Get Around in Shenyang

Shenyang has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses and metro lines. For those who prefer, taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Shenyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Beijing - the capital city of China is just a short train ride away from Shenyang and is home to numerous historic and cultural attractions.
  2. Dalian - a coastal city with beautiful beaches and seafood restaurants.
  3. Changbai Mountain - a stunning natural area located near the border of North Korea.

Conclusion

Shenyang is a fascinating city with a rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and a vibrant culture. By following this guide, visitors can enjoy everything that Shenyang has to offer while also being mindful of local traditions and customs.
