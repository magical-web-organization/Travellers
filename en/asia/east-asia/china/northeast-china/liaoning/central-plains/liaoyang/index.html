Introduction to Liaoyang

Liaoyang is a city in Liaoning province, located in Northeast China. It is situated in the central region known as Central Plains. The city has a rich cultural and historical heritage and is home to several significant sites that attract tourists from around the world. While most people visit Liaoyang to witness historical landmarks, the city has several natural attractions and outdoor adventures that showcase its scenic beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Liaoyang

Liaoyang has several culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places, as well as natural attractions for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • White Pagoda Park: A large park located in the center of Liaoyang city, home to White Pagoda, also known as Baita. It is a historical monument and a Buddhist temple that boasts of beautiful scenery.

  • Liaoyang Museum: A must-visit museum that features ancient artifacts from the Liaoyang region, including ceramic objects, bronze ware, and more. The museum also has a gallery showcasing the history and culture of Liaoyang city.

  • Daixi Lake Scenic Area: Located in the northeast of Liaoyang city, the Daixi Lake Scenic Area is a breathtakingly beautiful place of natural beauty. It is an excellent spot for hiking, picnicking, and sightseeing.

  • Shoushan Zoo: This zoo features rare species of animals, such as the Siberian tiger, red-crowned crane, and other wildlife. It is an ideal place to visit with family and friends.

  • Dahua Ancient City: An ancient walled city that dates back to the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644). It features well-preserved architecture, temples, pavilions, and more.

Natural Attractions of Liaoyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qianfoshan Scenic Area: A beautiful and serene place that features a 570-meter peak, which is an excellent spot for hiking and camping. The park also has many waterfalls and ancient temples.

  • Liaoyang Lake Park: A beautiful park located on the outskirts of Liaoyang city. It features a vast lake, which is surrounded by trees and greenery. The Lake Park is an excellent spot for a peaceful walk or picnic.

  • Qingliangshan National Forest Park: Located in the southeast of Liaoyang city, the Qingliangshan National Forest Park is a beautiful place to visit, especially during autumn. Some of the highlights of the park include the vast forest, waterfalls, and autumn foliage.

  • Shanhai Pass: A famous mountain pass located in the east of Liaoyang city. It is a popular spot for hiking and climbing, and also provides scenic views of surrounding mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liaoyang

Liaoyang has several hiking and biking routes that take visitors through some of the city’s most beautiful natural landscapes. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Liaoyang Lake Trail: This trail follows the shoreline of Liaoyang Lake and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding forests.

  • Liaoyang Temple Circuit: A hiking trail that takes visitors to several temples and historical sites in the city.

  • Cuihu Trail: A scenic hike that takes visitors through the beautiful Cuihu Park, where they can witness the beauty of the lake, temple, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Liaoyang: Activities and Tours

  • Hot-Air Ballooning: Experience the beauty of Liaoyang from the air with a hot-air balloon ride. Several companies offer hot-air balloon rides that take visitors over Liaoyang’s most beautiful landscapes.

  • Bike Rentals: Visitors can rent a bike and explore Liaoyang’s hiking and biking trails. There are several bike rental companies located in the city.

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour on Liaoyang Lake and witness the beauty of the city’s natural landscapes from the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Liaoyang

Food and Drink Scene of Liaoyang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Liaoyang Quick Fried Chicken: A popular dish in Liaoyang that features crispy fried chicken served with a sweet and spicy dipping sauce.

  • Tanghulu: A Chinese street food that consists of fruit, usually hawthorn, skewered on a stick and coated in a hard candy glaze.

  • Sanxingdui Chicken Soup: A comforting soup made with chicken and various Chinese herbs, known for its restorative properties.

Local Beverages

  • Maotai: A famous Chinese liquor that is known for its distinct aroma and taste. It is made in the Guizhou province of China.

  • Liaoyang Black Tea: A black tea brewed and consumed in Liaoyang city. It is known for its aroma and taste.

Where to Stay in Liaoyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Liaoyang has several accommodation options ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Ramada Plaza Liaoyang: A luxurious 4-star hotel located in the city center.

  • Hanting Express Liaoyang Xinyun Street: A budget hotel located in the city center that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

  • Roaming Youth Hostel Liaoyang: A popular hostel located in the heart of Liaoyang city.

What to Buy in Liaoyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Liaoyang Jade: A beautiful green jade that is found in Liaoyang and is said to have healing properties.

  • Sanxingdui Chicken Soup Packets: Locally produced chicken soup packets, perfect for making the famous Sanxingdui Chicken Soup at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Liaoyang

  • Huanqiang Pagoda Festival: A festival celebrated in Liaoyang every year on the 16th day of the fifth lunar month. It is a festival of worship and commemorates the city’s history and culture.

  • Liaoyang Dragon Dance: A traditional dance that is performed during celebrations and festivals in Liaoyang. The dance features a long dragon costume that is carried by several performers.

Best Time to Visit Liaoyang

The best time to visit Liaoyang is during the autumn season, from September to November, when the weather is mild, and the trees turn into beautiful hues of orange and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liaoyang

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Visit the cultural and historical landmarks.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Use disrespectful language or gestures.
  • Damage or deface the historical landmarks.
  • Be careless with personal belongings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liaoyang

Liaoyang is generally a safe city; however, visitors should exercise caution when walking alone at night and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Liaoyang

Visitors should make sure to drink bottled water and be wary of consuming street food that may not be properly cooked or stored.

Getting to Liaoyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liaoyang

The nearest airport to Liaoyang is Shenyang Taoxian International Airport, which is located about 60 kilometers from the city.

Bus Stations Near Liaoyang

Liaoyang has two bus stations, the Liaoyang Bus Station and the Liaoyang Long-Distance Bus Station. Both stations offer regular bus services to other cities and towns in Liaoning province.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Liaoyang; the city has two train stations, Liaoyang Railway Station and Liaoyang East Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Liaoyang

Liaoyang has several transportation options, including taxis, buses, and bikes. Visitors can also rent a car if they prefer to drive themselves.

Where to Go Next from Liaoyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shenyang: A large metropolitan city located about an hour’s drive from Liaoyang. Shenyang is home to several historical landmarks and has a vibrant food and drink scene.

  • Panjin: Located about 100 kilometers from Liaoyang, Panjin is a popular destination for birdwatching, beachcombing, and enjoying the beautiful red seaweed that is native to the area.

  • Dalian: Located about two hours from Liaoyang, Dalian is a beautiful coastal city with several beaches, cafes, and shopping areas.

Conclusion

Liaoyang is a beautiful city with a rich cultural and historical heritage. It offers visitors a wide range of attractions and activities, including natural landscapes, museums, hiking trails, and more. Whether you’re a history buff or outdoor enthusiast, Liaoyang has something for everyone.
