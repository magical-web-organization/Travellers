Dalian is a vibrant coastal city located in the Eastern Hills region of Liaoning Province in Northeast China. Known as the “Northern Pearl” of China, it boasts a mix of modern skyscrapers, historic sites, scenic parks, and beautiful beaches. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, adventure, or cuisine, Dalian has something for everyone.

Introduction to Dalian

Dalian is situated on a large peninsula, surrounded by the Yellow Sea to the east and the Bohai Sea to the west. It has a population of over 6 million people and is one of China’s most important seaports, serving as a strategic gateway for trade and economic development.

The city has a rich history and culture, influenced by a variety of factors, including its location on the Pacific Rim, its role as a colonial trading port, and its contributions to the industrialization of China. Today, Dalian is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its unique blend of modernity and tradition.

List of Places to Visit in Dalian

When it comes to things to see and do in Dalian, the options are endless. Here are some of the top attractions that you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dalian Modern Museum - This museum showcases modern art and design from around the world, as well as featuring an impressive collection of Chinese contemporary art.

  • Dalian Zhongshan Square - Located in the heart of the city, this square is surrounded by European-style buildings that have been well-preserved over the years.

  • Lushun Museum - This museum is located in Lushun, a district of Dalian, and provides an in-depth look at the area’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Dalian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xinghai Square - One of the largest seaside squares in the world, Xinghai Square boasts stunning views of the sea and is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike.

  • Binhai Road - This scenic road stretches along the coastline for over 30 kilometers and offers breathtaking views of rugged cliffs, sandy beaches, and rolling waves.

  • Dalian Forest Zoo - This zoo is home to a wide range of animals, including tigers, elephants, and monkeys. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of attractions, such as a cable car ride and a water park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dalian

If you love the great outdoors, Dalian has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Laohutan Scenic Area - This scenic park boasts a variety of trails that wind through lush forests, along the coast, and past picturesque lakes.

  • Tiger Beach Ocean Park - This park is home to a range of hiking and biking paths, with stunning views of the mountains and the sea.

  • Xinghai Park - This park offers a variety of trails for both hikers and bikers, as well as plenty of opportunities to stop and take in the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Dalian: Activities and Tours

In addition to the above attractions, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Dalian. Here are some of the most popular activities and tours:

  • Visit the Dalian Port - This bustling port is a hub of activity, with cargo ships coming and going from all over the world.

  • Explore the Dalian Discoveryland Theme Park - This theme park features a variety of rides and attractions, including roller coasters, water rides, and a dinosaur exhibit.

  • Try the Local Cuisine - Dalian is known for its delicious seafood, and there are plenty of restaurants and street vendors where you can sample the local fare.

Food and Drink Scene of Dalian

When it comes to food and drink in Dalian, the highlight is undoubtedly the seafood. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled squid - This delicious street food is made by grilling fresh squid on a stick until it’s crispy and tender.

  • Sautéed clams in black bean sauce - This classic Dalian dish features fresh clams cooked in a savory black bean sauce.

  • Seafood Hot Pot - This hot pot is filled with a variety of fresh seafood, including shrimp, fish, and scallops, simmered in a flavorful broth.

Local Beverages

  • Dalian Beer - This popular local beer is brewed using traditional methods and a unique recipe, resulting in a crisp and refreshing taste.

  • Boiled Corn Juice - This unusual drink is made by boiling fresh corn and sugar together, resulting in a sweet and creamy beverage.

  • Sea Buckthorn Juice - This tangy juice is made from the fruit of the sea buckthorn plant, which is grown in the mountains around Dalian. It’s packed with vitamins and other nutrients, making it a healthy and refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Dalian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dalian has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Shangri-La Hotel Dalian - This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of the city and features spacious rooms, a variety of dining options, and a fitness center and spa.

  • Dalian Ibis Hotel - This budget-friendly hotel is located near the city center and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.

  • Dalian Smilestone Hostel - This cozy hostel is located in a quiet residential area and features a communal kitchen, comfortable beds, and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Dalian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and gifts to bring home from Dalian, here are some of the best options:

  • Seafood snacks - Dalian is famous for its seafood, and there are plenty of shops and vendors where you can find a variety of dried fish, squid, and other seafood snacks.

  • Pearl Jewelry - Dalian is home to a number of pearl farms, and you can find a range of beautiful pearl jewelry at local shops and markets.

  • Rustic pottery - The local pottery in Dalian is known for its rustic charm and unique designs, and it makes a great souvenir or gift for someone special.

Local Culture and Customs of Dalian

The people of Dalian are friendly and hospitable, and they take great pride in their city and its history. When visiting Dalian, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Beijing Opera - Beijing Opera is a popular form of entertainment in Dalian, and you may be able to catch a performance at one of the local theaters.

  • Flower Markets - Dalian is known for its beautiful flower markets, which are held throughout the year and offer a wide variety of fresh flowers and plants.

  • Temple Fairs - Temple fairs are a common sight in Dalian, particularly during the Lunar New Year. These festivals feature food, games, arts and crafts, and other traditional activities.

Best Time to Visit Dalian

The best time to visit Dalian depends on what you’re looking for. The summer months (June-August) are the most popular, particularly for beachgoers and outdoor enthusiasts. The winter months (December-February) are colder, but also offer unique opportunities for skiing and other winter sports. Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are mild and pleasant, with fewer tourists and more affordable prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dalian

When visiting Dalian, there are a few things you should keep in mind in order to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine - Dalian is known for its delicious seafood and other culinary delights, so be sure to sample as many local dishes as possible.

  • Explore the city by bike - Dalian is a bike-friendly city, with plenty of bike lanes and rental options available.

  • Visit the beaches - Dalian has several beautiful beaches that are perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and other beach activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions - Be sure to dress appropriately and be respectful of local customs and practices.

  • Leave valuables unattended - Like any other city, Dalian has its share of pickpockets and thieves, so be sure to keep your valuables close at all times.

  • Ignore traffic laws and signals - Pedestrians and cyclists should be cautious when crossing the street, paying attention to traffic signals and watching out for cars and buses.

Safety and Health

Dalian is generally a safe and healthy city, but there are a few things to keep in mind when traveling there.

Safety in Dalian

  • Be cautious in crowded areas - Crowded markets and tourist areas can be a magnet for pickpockets and thieves.

  • Watch out for traffic - Cars and buses in Dalian can be aggressive, so be sure to look both ways before crossing the street.

  • Take precautions against the sun - Dalian can be hot and sunny in the summer months, so be sure to wear sunscreen and a hat.

Health in Dalian

  • Be cautious with street food - While Dalian’s street food can be delicious, it’s important to make sure that it’s cooked properly and is safe to consume.

  • Stay hydrated - Dalian can be humid in the summer months, so be sure to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

  • Get your vaccines - Before traveling to Dalian, make sure that your vaccines are up to date and that you have any necessary prescriptions from your doctor.

Getting to Dalian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dalian is easily accessible by air, train, and bus. Here are some of the most popular transportation options:

  • Airplane - Dalian has its own airport, Dalian Zhoushuizi International Airport, which offers both domestic and international flights.

  • Train - Dalian is served by several train stations, including Dalian Railway Station and Dalian North Railway Station.

  • Bus - Dalian has several long-distance bus stations that offer connections to cities throughout China and beyond.

Airports Near Dalian

  • Dalian Zhoushuizi International Airport - Dalian’s main airport, located just outside the city center.

  • Shenyang Taoxian International Airport - A larger airport located in the nearby city of Shenyang.

Bus Stations Near Dalian

  • Dalian Tourist Bus Station - Located near the city center, this bus station offers connections to other cities in Liaoning Province.

  • Dalian Express Bus Station - Located near the Dalian Railway Station, this bus station offers connections to cities throughout China.

Other Options

  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available throughout Dalian, but be sure to negotiate the fare in advance.

  • Bike Rental - Bikes can be rented for a nominal fee at several locations throughout Dalian, making it easy to explore the city on two wheels.

How to Get Around in Dalian

Dalian is a bike-friendly and walkable city, but it also has a variety of transportation options for those who prefer to travel by car or public transportation. Here are the best ways to get around:

  • Bike - Bikes can be rented for a nominal fee and are a great way to explore the city at a leisurely pace.

  • Subway - Dalian has a modern subway system with several lines that connect the city’s major attractions and neighborhoods.

  • Bus - Dalian has an extensive bus system that covers the entire city and offers an affordable and convenient option for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Dalian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Northeast China, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Shenyang - The capital city of Liaoning Province, Shenyang is home to a number of historic sites and attractions, including the Imperial Palace and the Shenyang Botanical Garden.

  • Dandong - Located on the border with North Korea, Dandong is a fascinating and unique destination, offering stunning views of the Yalu River and a chance to glimpse life in two different cultures.

  • Changbai Mountain - This stunning mountain range is located on the border between China and North Korea and features a variety of hiking trails and scenic vistas.

Conclusion

Dalian is a beautiful city with a rich history, unique culture, and plenty of attractions to explore. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply relaxing on the beach, there’s something for everyone in Dalian. So why wait?
