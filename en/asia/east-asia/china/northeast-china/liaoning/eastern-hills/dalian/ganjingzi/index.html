Introduction to Ganjingzi

Located in the eastern hills of Dalian, in the Liaoning province of northeast China, Ganjingzi is a hidden gem known for its beautiful natural scenery and cultural attractions. Here you can explore historical sites, museums, hiking and biking trails, and enjoy local cuisine and hospitality. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at this beautiful district, and highlight some of the must-see sights and experiences to help you plan your trip to Ganjingzi.

List of Places to Visit in Ganjingzi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ganjingzi has a rich history and cultural heritage, which can be seen in its many museums and historical sites. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit:

  • Xinghai Square: Located on the shore of Xinghai Bay, this iconic square is the largest in Asia and a great place for a stroll or picnic.

  • Dalian Museum of Natural History: Discover the diverse flora, fauna, and geology of the region in this well-curated museum.

  • Dalian Forest Zoo: Get up-close with wildlife from around the world, see giant pandas and other species of endangered animals.

  • Dalian Shell Museum: This museum features an incredible collection of shells from around the globe, with a focus on local marine diversity.

Natural Attractions of Ganjingzi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ganjingzi’s natural wonders can be found in the eastern hills, where visitors can enjoy hiking, mountain biking, and other outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Laohutan Scenic Area: This area features beautiful gardens, trails, and a stunning aquarium where you can see a variety of sea creatures.

  • Tiger Beach: This secluded beach is popular for its crystal-clear waters and white sands. The perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxation.

  • Dalian Forest Park: With over 373 hectares of dense forest, hills, and grasslands, this park is a perfect spot for hiking, picnicking, and birdwatching.

  • Jinshitan National Tourist Resort: This beautiful coastal area is popular for camping, glamping, and watersports like surfing, kiteboarding, and windsurfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ganjingzi

For outdoor enthusiasts, Ganjingzi offers many beautiful hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging climbs. Some popular routes include:

  • Dalian Forest Park: Visitors can stroll through the park and hike to the top of the highest peak, which offers a beautiful view of the surrounding area.

  • Xinghai Park to Fujiazhuang Beach: This scenic route winds along the coast and through the hills, and offers beautiful views of the bay.

Best Things to Do in Ganjingzi: Activities and Tours

With so many beautiful sights and experiences to choose from in Ganjingzi, it can be hard to decide what to do first. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Walking Tour of Dalian Old Town: Discover the rich history and culture of Dalian’s Old Town, with a guided walking tour that takes you through the highlights of this charming district.

  • Food Tour: Taste the local cuisine of Ganjingzi, with a food tour that includes visits to some of the best restaurants and street food stalls in the area.

  • Biking Tour of the Eastern Hills: Take a guided biking tour of the beautiful eastern hills of Ganjingzi, and explore some of the hidden trails and scenic viewpoints.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ganjingzi

If you’re planning to spend a week in Ganjingzi, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore Xinghai Square and visit the Dalian Museum of Natural History.

  • Day 2: Spend the day at Laohutan Scenic Area and see the Sea World Aquarium.

  • Day 3: Take a walking tour of Dalian Old Town, and try the local delicacies.

  • Day 4: Hike in Dalian Forest Park and picnic in the hills.

  • Day 5: Visit the Dalian Shell Museum, and take a sunset walk along the bay.

  • Day 6: Bike through the eastern hills of Ganjingzi and explore the scenic views.

  • Day 7: Spend the day at Tiger Beach, swimming or sunbathing, and relax.

Food and Drink Scene of Ganjingzi

Must-Try Dishes

Ganjingzi is known for its unique blend of Chinese and Korean cuisine, with a focus on fresh seafood and local vegetables. Here are some dishes to try:

  • Stir-fried clams with spicy sauce: Fresh clams stir-fried with garlic, ginger, and a spicy sauce.

  • Zhajiang noodle: A hearty noodle soup with a sauce made of fermented soybean paste, pork, and vegetables.

  • Fried eggplant rolls: Eggplant stuffed with meat and vegetables, deep-fried and served with a dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Ganjingzi offers a variety of local beers, wines, and spirits, as well as traditional Chinese teas. Some popular choices include:

  • Baijiu: A strong liquor made from fermented grains, commonly served at celebrations and banquets.

  • Dalian Beer: A local brew with a smooth and light taste, perfect for hot summer days.

  • Korean rice wine: A sweet and mild rice wine, perfect for pairing with spicy dishes.

Where to Stay in Ganjingzi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ganjingzi has a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some top choices for your stay in Ganjingzi:

  • Shangri-La Hotel Dalian: A five-star hotel overlooking the bay, with luxurious amenities, a spa, and multiple dining options.

  • Ibis Styles Hotel: A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms, modern amenities, and a central location.

  • Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms, friendly staff, and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Ganjingzi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping, Ganjingzi has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Seafood: Take home some of the freshest seafood in China, including clams, oysters, and abalone.

  • Korean Ginseng: Ganjingzi’s location near the Korean border means you can find high-quality Korean ginseng, used for centuries for its medicinal properties.

  • Pearls: Dalian is famous for its pearls, with many shops selling high-quality freshwater and saltwater pearls.

Local Culture and Customs of Ganjingzi

The local culture of Ganjingzi is a blend of Chinese and Korean traditions, with a focus on hospitality, family, and community. Etiquette and customs are similar to those in other parts of China, but locals often mix Mandarin and Korean in their everyday conversation.

Best Time to Visit Ganjingzi

The best time to visit Ganjingzi is during the spring and autumn months, from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, and the winter months can be cold and windy, so it’s best to avoid those times if you don’t enjoy extreme temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ganjingzi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Speak Mandarin, but try to learn a few Korean phrases.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb the wildlife.

  • Take photographs without permission.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ganjingzi

Ganjingzi is generally a safe and friendly place for tourists, with low crime rates and helpful locals. However, it’s always wise to take precautions, such as avoiding deserted areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded places.

Health in Ganjingzi

Ganjingzi has good healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical care in case of emergencies. However, it’s a good idea to pack any necessary medication and travel with proper health insurance.

Getting to Ganjingzi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ganjingzi

The closest airport to Ganjingzi is Dalian Zhoushuizi International Airport, which receives domestic and international flights from major cities in China and other countries. Visitors can take a taxi or airport shuttle bus to reach their accommodation in Ganjingzi.

Bus Stations Near Ganjingzi

Dalian has several bus stations, including the main bus station in the city center and the North Bus Station. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Ganjingzi.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, bike or take a private transfer from the airport, ferry terminal or the railway station.

How to Get Around in Ganjingzi

Getting around Ganjingzi is easy, with a well-connected public transportation system that includes taxis, buses, and subways. Visitors can also rent bikes or hire a car for maximum flexibility and independence.

Where to Go Next from Ganjingzi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region, Ganjingzi is located near several other popular destinations, including:

  • Dalian: The vibrant port city of Dalian is known for its modern architecture, beaches, and shopping.

  • Shenyang: The capital city of Liaoning province, Shenyang is home to museums, temples, and historic sites, including the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Shenyang Imperial Palace.

  • Changbai Mountains: This beautiful mountain range on the border between China and North Korea is famous for its stunning vistas, hot springs, and winter sports.

Conclusion

Ganjingzi is a beautiful and exciting destination, with something to offer for all kinds of travelers. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, adventure, or relaxation, Ganjingzi won’t disappoint. We hope this guide provides you with all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to this hidden gem in eastern China.
