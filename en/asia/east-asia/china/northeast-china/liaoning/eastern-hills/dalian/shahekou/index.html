Shahekou is a hidden gem located in the Eastern Hills of Dalian, in Liaoning province in Northeast China. Tucked away from the hustle and bustle of the city, Shahekou offers a tranquil space for outdoor enthusiasts and culture buffs alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Shahekou, we’ve covered everything you need to know to plan your trip, from the top attractions to the best things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Shahekou

Shahekou is a mountainous area in Dalian that was once an important industrial hub. Today, it’s home to several top-notch universities, research centers, and a thriving tourism industry, thanks to its natural beauty and rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Shahekou

Exploring Shahekou begins with visiting the must-see attractions that the area has to offer. Here’s a list of the cultural and natural attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dalian Natural History Museum: A fascinating museum showcasing over 200,000 exhibits on the area’s natural history and cultural heritage.

  • Dalian Modern Museum: A modern museum with interactive exhibits that showcase the cultural and industrial development of Dalian.

  • Lvshun Museum: A museum dedicated to the military history of Dalian, with a focus on the Russo-Japanese War.

Natural Attractions of Shahekou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laohutan Ocean Park: A famous park that features an aquarium, marine museum, and amusement park.

  • Dalian Forest Zoo: A zoological park in the forest that’s home to over 200 species of animals, including endangered species like the Siberian tiger and the white-lipped deer.

  • Binhai Road: A scenic coastal road that offers breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shahekou

Shahekou is home to several scenic hiking and biking routes that offer a great opportunity to explore the area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  • Shahekou Forest Park: A lush green park with a stunning waterfall that makes for a perfect hike.

  • Youhao Square - Honeymoon Bay: A scenic coastal route that’s perfect for cycling.

  • East Coast Nature Scenic Area: A picturesque hiking trail that offers fantastic views of the sea and the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Shahekou: Activities and Tours

Shahekou is not just about natural beauty; there are plenty of activities and tours that you can participate in to fully experience the area. Here are some of the best things to do in Shahekou:

  • Take a Hot Spring Bath: Relax and rejuvenate in one of Shahekou’s many hot spring resorts.

  • Rock Climbing: Challenge yourself with a rock climbing adventure in the mountains of Shahekou.

  • Explore local villages: Take a day trip to explore the hidden villages around Shahekou and learn about the local culture and customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shahekou

To make the most out of your trip to Shahekou, here’s a recommended itinerary for a week-long stay:

Day Itinerary
1 Visit Laohutan Ocean Park and Lvshun Museum
2 Take a scenic hike in Shahekou Forest Park
3 Cycle along the East Coast Nature Scenic Area
4 Take a day trip to local villages around Shahekou
5 Explore the Dalian Natural History Museum and Dalian Modern Museum
6 Experience local hot springs and take a dip in the area’s natural hot spring waters
7 Go rock climbing in one of the area’s designated climbing areas

Food and Drink Scene of Shahekou

Shahekou is not only known for its natural beauty and cultural heritage but also for its delectable cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Being located on the coast, Shahekou is known for its fresh and delicious seafood dishes, including prawns, oysters, and crabs.

  • Braised Pork with Honey: A flavorful pork dish that is braised in honey and soy sauce.

  • Zhongzi: A traditional Chinese dish consisting of sticky rice and different fillings wrapped in bamboo leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Harbin Beer: A popular Chinese beer that’s smooth and refreshing.

  • Dalian Red Wine: A local wine with a rich and fruity flavor that pairs well with local cuisine.

Where to Stay in Shahekou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Shahekou, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Grand Hyatt Dalian: A luxurious hotel with beautiful ocean views and an excellent restaurant.

  • Holiday Inn Dalian Hot Spring: A hot spring resort that offers a relaxing and rejuvenating stay.

  • Dalian Manhao Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Shahekou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shahekou is known for its local products, including handmade crafts, special teas, and medicinal herbs. Here are some items that you should consider taking home:

  • Handmade crafts: Take home local handicrafts, including Dongbei embroidery, jujube-wood carving, and paper cutting.

  • Special Teas: Enjoy a cup of local tea and bring home some loose-leaf tea to savor.

  • Medicinal Herbs: Visit the local herb markets and take home some medicinal herbs like ginseng, wolfberry, and dried shiitake mushrooms.

Local Culture and Customs of Shahekou

Shahekou has a rich cultural heritage with several local customs that visitors can experience. Here are some cultural traditions that you should know about:

  • Zhongyuan Festival: A traditional holiday celebrated on the 15th day of the seventh lunar month with a focus on ancestor worship.

  • Dragon Boat Festival: A holiday celebrated on the fifth day of the fifth lunar month with a focus on dragon boat races and eating zongzi.

Best Time to Visit Shahekou

The best time to visit Shahekou is from May to October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shahekou

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Carry enough cash, as some places may not accept credit cards.

  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent for hiking trips.

Don’t:

  • Engage in disrespectful behavior or litter in natural areas.

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

  • Drink tap water; always opt for bottled water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shahekou

Shahekou is a relatively safe area, but visitors should take precautions like staying aware of their surroundings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Shahekou

Visitors should be aware of mosquitoes and ticks during the summer months and take necessary precautions like wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Shahekou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shahekou is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including flights, buses, and trains.

Airports Near Shahekou

  • Dalian Zhoushuizi International Airport: The closest airport to Shahekou, located about 30 km away.

Bus Stations Near Shahekou

  • Dalian Passenger Transport Terminal: The main bus station in Dalian, located about 15 km away from Shahekou.

Other Options

  • Train: Visitors can take a train to Dalian Railway Station and take a taxi or bus to Shahekou.

How to Get Around in Shahekou

Buses and taxis are the primary modes of transportation for getting around Shahekou. Visitors can also hire a bike or explore on foot.

Where to Go Next from Shahekou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shahekou is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations like:

  • Dalian: A coastal city known for its beaches, parks, museums, and shopping centers.

  • Lushun: A historical port town known for its military importance and scenic views.

  • Dandong: A city located on the Chinese-North Korean border, known for its beautiful natural scenery and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Shahekou is the perfect destination for nature lovers, culture enthusiasts, and those looking for a tranquil place to relax. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to explore the best that Shahekou has to offer. Plan your trip, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
