Asia has always been known for its diverse range of cultures, history, and natural scenery. And for those looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience, Zhongshan in Dalian’s Eastern Hills, Northeast China, is the perfect destination. Famed for its picturesque views, abundant natural resources, and rich historical and cultural heritage, Zhongshan is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Zhongshan’s must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, food scene, local culture, safety, and health, transportation options and nearby destinations, making your Zhongshan trip an unforgettable experience.

Introduction to Zhongshan

Located in Dalian’s Eastern Hills in Liaoning, China, Zhongshan is a stunning area that has been designated as a national scenic area by the Chinese government. The area is widely renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, lush forests, and cultural heritage, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history lovers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Zhongshan

Zhongshan boasts numerous attractions that are a testament to its rich cultural and natural heritage. Here are some must-visit destinations in Zhongshan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zhongshan Square: A historical landmark that was built in 1920, Zhongshan Square features a classic European architectural style that is reminiscent of its colonial past.

  • The Russian Folk Museum in Dalian: As a tribute to Dalian’s unique Russian cultural heritage, the Russian Folk Museum features various exhibits that showcase the city’s history, culture, and art.

Natural Attractions of Zhongshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zhongshan Park: Zhongshan Park is renowned for its lush greenery, rocky hills, and a wide range of picturesque gardens, making it an ideal spot for picnicking, hiking, and relaxing.

  • Jinshitan National Scenic Area: Located in the small town of Jinshitan, the Jinshitan National Scenic Area features a wide range of natural attractions, including rocky cliffs, sandy beaches, and crystal clear waters. It’s a must-visit destination for outdoor lovers and adventurers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhongshan

Zhongshan’s natural scenery is abundant, with numerous hiking and biking trails that offer panoramic views of the area. Some of the notable hiking/biking routes include:

  • Zhongshan Park Trail: This trail takes you on a scenic route through some of the park’s most beautiful gardens, hills, and man-made lakes.

  • Dalian Bathing Beach Coastal Cycling Route: If you’re looking for a relaxed and enjoyable cycling experience, the Dalian Bathing Beach Coastal Cycling Route is the perfect choice.

Best Things to Do in Zhongshan: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Zhongshan has a lot to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Zhongshan:

  • Enjoy a Cup of Tea: Zhongshan is renowned for its tea culture, and there are numerous tea houses where you can sample different varieties of tea while taking in the stunning views of the area.

  • Take a Hot Spring Bath: There’s no better way to relax after a long day of hiking or biking than taking a dip in one of Zhongshan’s hot springs. The hot springs are rich in minerals that are good for your skin and help to detoxify your body.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhongshan

For those looking to spend a week exploring Zhongshan, here is an outline itinerary:

Day 1 to 3: Explore the Cultural and Historical Attractions around the city

Day 4 to 5: Hiking and Biking around Zhongshan Park

Day 6 to 7: Relaxing with a Hot Spring Bath and a Cup of Tea

Food and Drink Scene of Zhongshan

Zhongshan’s food and drink scene offers a rich variety of dishes and beverages that are unique to the area’s culture and history. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Liaoning Meat Pie: This savory and flaky pastry is a classic dish in the Liaoning province and is filled with a flavorful mix of meat, vegetables, and spices.

  • Dandong Fried Rice Cake: This dish is a local delicacy in Dandong, and it’s made by frying rice cakes until they are crispy on the outside and soft and chewy on the inside.

Local Beverages

  • Zhuyeqing Tea: This tea is grown locally in Zhongshan and is known for its floral and sweet taste.

  • Baijiu: Baijiu is a traditional Chinese alcoholic beverage that’s made from fermented grains, and it’s especially popular in the Northeast.

Where to Stay in Zhongshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodation options in Zhongshan that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the most recommended places to stay in Zhongshan:

  • Grand Hyatt Dalian: A luxurious hotel that’s located with Zhongshan Square and offers excellent views of the city and the harbor.

  • Hanting Hotel: A budget-friendly option that’s located in the heart of the city, making it easy to access different attractions and activities.

What to Buy in Zhongshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhongshan is renowned for its tea, antiques, and handicrafts that are unique to the area’s culture and history. Here are some of the souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Tea: Zhongshan’s tea is highly regarded for its flavorful taste and exceptional quality, making it a perfect gift for friends and family.

  • Antiques and Handicrafts: Zhongshan boasts an abundant supply of antiques and handicrafts that are unique to the area’s culture, including carved wooden models of traditional Chinese buildings and porcelain figurines.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhongshan

Zhongshan’s culture has been influenced by its rich history and natural scenery, making it a unique and vibrant destination. Here are some local customs and practices to keep in mind:

  • Respect for Elders: The elderly are highly regarded in Zhongshan’s culture, and it’s essential to show respect for them in any interaction.

  • Tea Culture: Tea is central to Zhongshan’s culture, and there are numerous tea house and ceremonies that showcase the area’s tea culture and history.

Best Time to Visit Zhongshan

The best time to visit Zhongshan is in the spring and autumn when the weather is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploration. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter can be cold and harsh.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhongshan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the area.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Take care of the environment and follow environmental conservation practices.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or engage in disruptive behavior in public.
  • Disregard local customs and traditions.
  • Damage or vandalize natural or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhongshan

Zhongshan is generally safe for visitors, but it’s essential to take precautions, such as keeping your belongings secure and not walking alone at night.

Health in Zhongshan

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to avoid contracting illnesses such as heat stroke, dehydration, and altitude sickness. It’s essential to stay hydrated and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun.

Getting to Zhongshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zhongshan is accessible by air, rail, and bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Zhongshan

  • Dalian Zhoushuizi International Airport: This airport is located 30km away from Zhongshan and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Zhongshan

  • Dalian North Bus Station: This station is located in the downtown area and offers direct buses to the Zhongshan area.

Other Options

  • Train: There’s a train station in Dalian that connects to other major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Zhongshan

The most convenient way to get around in Zhongshan is by taxi, which is readily available in the area. Biking, walking, and public transportation are also viable options.

Where to Go Next from Zhongshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring, including:

  • Dalian City: Dalian City is a bustling port city that boasts a rich cultural heritage, vibrant nightlife, and abundant natural attractions.

  • Shenyang City: Shenyang City is renowned for its imperial history and cultural heritage, making it a must-visit destination for history lovers.

Conclusion

Zhongshan in Dalian’s Eastern Hills is a hidden gem that boasts stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and historical landmarks. With numerous attractions, activities, and accommodation options, Zhongshan is the perfect destination for a memorable trip. While visiting Zhongshan, take the time to explore the area’s history, hike and bike through the stunning landscapes, enjoy the local delicacies, and immerse yourself in the culture and traditions of the area. Your Zhongshan trip will be one for the books!
