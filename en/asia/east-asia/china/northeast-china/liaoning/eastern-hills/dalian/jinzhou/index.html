Introduction to Jinzhou

Jinzhou is a prefecture-level city in Liaoning Province, situated in the Northeast of China. It is located on the coast of the Bohai Sea and is known for its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural history, and modern urban development.

List of Places to Visit in Jinzhou

Jinzhou offers visitors a unique experience with cultural and natural attractions that are not found anywhere else. Here are some of the must-visit places in Jinzhou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jinzhou Museum (晋州博物馆): This museum showcases the rich cultural history of Jinzhou and has exhibits on various topics, such as calligraphy, jade artifacts, and ancient pottery.
  • Liaoxi Guild Hall (辽西会馆): This historical building was constructed during the Qing Dynasty and is a masterpiece of Chinese architecture. It was previously used as a gathering place for local businessmen and has now been converted into a museum that displays historical items.
  • Diaoshuihu Scenic Spot (钓水湖风景区): This scenic spot is home to several ancient temples and pavilions, as well as a tranquil lake that is perfect for boating or picnicking.

Natural Attractions of Jinzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laotie Mountain (老铁山): This scenic area has a beautiful mountain range with lush greenery and a peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, picnicking, and other activities.
  • Beihong Park (北红公园): This park boasts beautiful gardens and stunning views of the city. It is the perfect place to relax and unwind after a long day of exploring.
  • Wafangdian Hot Spring Resort (瓦房店温泉度假村): This resort offers several kinds of outdoor hot spring pools, ideal for relaxing your muscles and enjoying the natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Jinzhou

Jinzhou is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, here are the best routes for that:

  • Laotie Mountain Trail (老铁山步道): It is a 5 km-long route and takes around 2 hours to complete. It offers a moderate level of difficulty provides beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Wulongtan Trail (五龙潭步道): This 6-km trail takes visitors through the beautiful Wulongtan area, featuring beautiful natural scenery and waterfalls.
  • Jianshan Mountain Bike Trail (建山山地自行车步道): Located in the city center, this trail is over 10km long and offers a thrill to mountain bikers of all levels with beautiful views.

Best Things to Do in Jinzhou: Activities and Tours

Jinzhou has plenty of things to do and offers activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Jinzhou:

  • Boat Tour (游船游览): Explore the Bohai Sea and Jinzhou Bay on a boat tour, where you can enjoy beautiful views of the coastline and city skyline.
  • Antique Market (古董市场): This market offers visitors an opportunity to buy authentic antiques, traditional crafts, and souvenirs that are unique to Jinzhou.
  • Kite Flying (放风筝): Jinzhou is famous for kite-flying, and visitors can participate in this outdoor activity on various locations across the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jinzhou

Day 1: Arrive in Jinzhou and check-in at your hotel, walk around Jinzhou Old Town. Day 2: Visit the Jinzhou Museum and stopover at Diaoshuihu Scenic Spot. Day 3: Go for hiking/biking Laotie Mountain Trail, enjoy the beautiful and peaceful atmosphere of the old forest. Day 4: Take a boat tour and enjoy the stunning views of the Bohai Sea and city skyline. Day 5: Visit Liaxi Guild Hall and the Beihong Park and unwind in hot springs at Wafangdian Hot Spring Resort. Day 6: Explore the scenic Wulongtan Trail or spend the day shopping for souvenirs at local markets. Day 7: Depart from Jinzhou for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Jinzhou

No guide to Jinzhou would be complete without mentioning its food and drink scene. Jinzhou boasts a diverse culinary scene, with many delicious local dishes that visitors must-try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Dumplings (肉饺子): One of the most famous dishes in Jinzhou, these dumplings are filled with juicy minced pork and served with a flavorful dipping sauce.
  • Crispy Fried Chicken (炸鸡): This dish is fried to perfection and served with spices and seasonings, making it a popular main course in Jinzhou.
  • Eight Treasures Tea (八宝茶): This traditional tea is unique to the region and is made with a blend of dried flowers and fruits, creating a unique and refreshing taste.

Local Beverages

  • Jinzhou Beer (晋州啤酒): This locally brewed beer is a popular option among locals, with a light and crisp taste that pairs well with local dishes.
  • Laobaigan Liquor (老白干): This is a potent liquor made with barley and is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Jinzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jinzhou has a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly to luxury options. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jinzhou:

  • Jinzhou Hotel (晋州大酒店): This hotel has spacious rooms and modern amenities, making it an excellent option for visitors looking for a comfortable stay.
  • Shangri-La Hotel Jinzhou (香格里拉大酒店): This luxurious hotel overlooks Jinzhou Bay and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. It has various amenities like a fitness center, swimming pool, and a spa.
  • Jinzhou Youth Hostel (晋州民宿): This hostel provides budget-friendly accommodation with shared facilities, ideal for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Jinzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take a piece of Jinzhou with them by bringing back local souvenirs and products. Here are some unique items to purchase in Jinzhou:

  • Paper-cut art (剪纸): These intricate paper-cuttings are a traditional folk art and make great souvenirs.
  • Laobaigan Liquor (老白干): This locally produced liquor is a favorite among residents and a great souvenir for liquor enthusiasts.
  • Jade Artifacts (玉器): Jinzhou is known for its jade art and visitors can purchase various jade items, including jewelry, figurines, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Jinzhou

Jinzhou has a rich cultural history, and locals have maintained their customs over the years. Visitors to Jinzhou should respect local customs and try to learn about the culture.

Best Time to Visit Jinzhou

The best time to visit Jinzhou is during the autumn months, from September to November, when the weather is mild and the foliage is in full bloom. Visitors can also visit in the summer, from June to August, when beaches and parks are full of locals and tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jinzhou

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and take part in local activities.
  • Learn about the culture and customs of Jinzhou
  • Be respectful of locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be insensitive to local traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jinzhou

Jinzhou is a safe city to visit, and visitors can walk around at night with no risk. However, visitors should be careful of pickpockets and should keep valuables safe while out and about.

Health in Jinzhou

Visitors should take precautions while travelling in Jinzhou, such as drinking bottled water and being wary of street food. Visitors should also bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Jinzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jinzhou

  • Jinzhou Xiaolingzi Airport (JNZ): This airport has domestic flights to other parts of China, including Beijing and Shanghai.

Bus Stations Near Jinzhou

  • Jinzhou Bus Station: Visitors can take a bus from nearby cities such as Shenyang, Dalian, and Beijing.
  • Jinzhou West Long-distance Bus Station: Provides bus services to Wafangdian, Xiong’an, and other neighboring cities.

Other Options

  • Train: Jinzhou Station is located in the center of the city and provides easy access and transport to and from the city.
  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore the surrounding areas and scenic routes.

How to Get Around in Jinzhou

Jinzhou has a well-developed transportation system, making it easy for visitors to get around the city. The most popular transportation options in Jinzhou include taxi, bus and subway.

Where to Go Next from Jinzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jinzhou is the perfect starting point for various nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Shenyang: The capital of Liaoning Province, Shenyang offers a mix of modern attractions and historical landmarks. It is just a two-hour drive from Jinzhou.
  • Dalian: Known for its beaches and seafood, Dalian is a popular destination for outdoor activities, shopping, and foodies. It is about 3 hours away by car from Jinzhou.
  • Panjin Red Beach (盘锦红海滩): This stunning beach is famous for its bright red seaweed and picturesque landscape, it is just two hours drive away from Jinzhou.

Conclusion

Jinzhou is a wonderful destination for visitors looking to immerse themselves in Chinese culture, explore nature and enjoy local cuisine. It has something for everyone, from its rich cultural history to its modern urban developments. Plan your trip well, and explore everything that Jinzhou has to offer!
