Introduction to Lüshunkou

Lüshunkou, also known as Port Arthur, is a district located in Dalian, in the Liaoning province of China. This historic district is famously known for being a former naval base of the Imperial Russian Navy and later the Japanese Navy. Today, Lüshunkou is a popular tourist destination for those who are interested in learning about history, experiencing outdoor activities, and indulging in local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Lüshunkou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lüshunkou has a rich cultural history, and visitors can explore it by visiting a variety of museums and historical sites in the district.

  • Laohutan Ocean Park: This park is home to a variety of marine animals and features an aquarium and various exhibits.

  • Lüshun Museum: This museum has a collection of more than 25,000 cultural relics and exhibits, providing insight into the Lüshunkou’s history and culture.

  • Port Arthur Memorial Hall: This hall is dedicated to the soldiers who fought during the Russo-Japanese War and contains artifacts and memorials from the conflict.

Natural Attractions of Lüshunkou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lüshunkou is a stunning destination with plenty of outdoor activities and natural wonders to explore.

  • Lüshun South Gate: This is the entrance to the city, and it is a prime spot to catch sight of the harbor scenery.

  • Tiger Beach Park: This 83-acre park is home to a beautiful beach, a botanical garden, and a variety of activities, such as camping and hiking.

  • Taiping Bay Tourist Resort: A great place to relax and sunbathe while taking in the scenic views of the surrounding mountains and beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lüshunkou

Lüshunkou is also a great destination for outdoor activities like hiking and biking, and visitors can explore the city through several picturesque routes.

  • Shuiwan Valley Scenic Area: This route is perfect for hiking and exploring the beautiful waterfalls and rock formations.

  • Xiaoling Mountain: A great spot for hiking enthusiasts, featuring a few easy and challenging trails.

Best Things to Do in Lüshunkou: Activities and Tours

Lüshunkou boasts a variety of activities that cater to different interests.

  • Beach Walk and Horse Riding: A very popular and enjoyable activity in Lüshunkou is walking on the beach and taking a horse ride.

  • Boat Tour of the Harbor: A must-do activity to explore the beautiful harbor area from a new angle.

  • Russian Street in Lüshun: A beautiful street that is the perfect place to experience the historic Russian influence in the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Lüshunkou

Must-Try Dishes

Lüshunkou has a plethora of culinary delights that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Seafood: Lüshunkou has vast coastlines, so seafood is a must-try. Some famous seafood dishes include boiled shrimp, squid soup, and roasted oysters.

  • Red Braised Pork: Pork is a staple in Chinese cuisine, and Lüshunkou is no exception. The red-braised pork in Lüshunkou is famous for its unique aroma and taste.

  • Stewed Chicken with Soy Sauce: This dish is made with Lüshun brewing soy sauce, chicken soup, and a blend of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Lüshun Brewing Soy Sauce Wine: This wine is a unique blend of soy sauce and rice wine, providing a distinct flavor in every sip.

  • Yi Dong Tea: A traditional Chinese tea, also known as “foliage”, made from young leaves of maple trees.

Where to Stay in Lüshunkou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lüshunkou has a wide range of accommodations available, from hotels and hostels to guesthouses.

  • Dalian Lushun Golden Port Hotel: A luxurious 5-star hotel located in Taiping Bay Tourist Resort.

  • Dalian Zhuquan Capsule Hotel: A budget-friendly hostel that is suitable for solo travelers.

  • Lushun Tropic Fish Guest House: A unique stay option that overlooks the beautiful Liujia Bay.

What to Buy in Lüshunkou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Russian Matryoshka Dolls: A unique souvenir to take home from the historic Russian Street in Lüshun.

  • Lüshun Soy Sauce: The Lüshun brewing soy sauce is a famous product that visitors can buy at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Lüshunkou

  • Taboos: Avoid wearing bright colors, as it is considered impolite in local customs.

  • Greeting: In Lüshunkou, a handshake is a common greeting for men, while women typically nod their heads in acknowledgement.

Best Time to Visit Lüshunkou

The best time to visit Lüshunkou is during the spring or autumn, from April to June or September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lüshunkou

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Visit the museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing, especially at cultural or religious sites.

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lüshunkou

Lüshunkou is a generally safe destination, and crimes against tourists are relatively low. However, visitors should be cautious and take necessary precautions.

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place.

  • Beware of pickpockets and scams.

  • Make sure to have a reliable source of transportation, especially at night.

Health in Lüshunkou

Lüshunkou has decent medical facilities, but visitors should have travel insurance in case of emergencies. It is also important to bring any necessary medication.

Getting to Lüshunkou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lüshunkou

The nearest airport is Dalian Zhoushuizi International Airport, which is approximately 67 kilometers away from Lüshunkou.

Bus Stations Near Lüshunkou

The main bus station is Lüshun Long Distance Bus Station, where visitors can take buses to various destinations within China.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lüshunkou via train, private car, or taxi.

How to Get Around Lüshunkou

The most convenient way to travel within Lüshunkou is by taxi as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also rent bicycles or use ride-sharing apps.

Where to Go Next from Lüshunkou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dalian: A vibrant metropolis, which is just 40 minutes away by high-speed train.

  • Shenyang: The capital city of Liaoning province, which is around 3 hours by train.

  • Panjin Red Beach: A natural scenic area located south of Lüshunkou, just 2 hours’ drive away.

Conclusion

Lüshunkou is a wonderful destination to explore China’s rich culture and heritage. With its natural beauty, fascinating history, and delicious cuisine, Lüshunkou is a must-visit spot in China.
