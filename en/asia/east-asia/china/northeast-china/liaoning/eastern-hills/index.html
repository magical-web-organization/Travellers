Introduction to Eastern Hills

Eastern Hills, located in Liaoning Province in Northeast China, is a collection of mountains, hiking trails, cultural attractions, and local cuisines. It is a perfect destination for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts, offering a balance of scenic beauty and historical significance.

This comprehensive guide to Eastern Hills will provide you with all the necessary information you need to plan a successful trip.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Qingyuan Manchu Museum: This museum displays the lifestyle, art, and culture of the Manchu people, one of the ethnic minorities in China.
  • Shenyang Imperial Palace: A UNESCO World Heritage site, the palace was built in the seventeenth century for the Qing dynasty emperors.
  • Baita Park: This park features the iconic White Pagoda, which is a symbol of Shenyang city.
  • Marshal Zhang’s Mansion: A palace built in the eighteenth century for a Qing imperial general famous for his victories over the Russians and Japanese in Northeast China.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Qian Shan: The highest peak in Eastern Hills, it offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Shenyang Botanical Garden: The garden is home to thousands of plant species, including rare and endangered ones. It is a perfect location for a nature walk and exploration.
  • Taiqing Palace Nature Reserve: The reserve is home to hundreds of species of plants and animals, including wild boar, leopard cats, and musk deer. It also features a Taoist temple with a long history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Hills

  • Taipingshan Trail: This trail starts from Taipingshan Forest Park and leads to Mount Qian Shan with a total of 14 kilometers.
  • Qing Dynasty Mountain Trail: This trail is about 8 kilometers long and takes you through the scenic Qing dynasty mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Hills: Activities and Tours

  • White Pagoda at Baita Park: Enjoy the beautiful White Pagoda, a symbol of Shenyang city.
  • Dandong Yalu River Broken Bridge: This bridge was destroyed during the Korean War and has never been rebuilt. You can visit the bridge and watch the North Korean border from a distance.
  • Shenyang Imperial Palace: Explore the palace and learn about the history of the Qing dynasty and Manchu culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Hills

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival in Shenyang and check-in at your hotel. Visit the Shenyang Imperial Palace.
Day 2 Take a hike to Taiqing Palace Nature Reserve.
Day 3 Visit the Qingyuan Manchu Museum and Marshal Zhang’s Mansion.
Day 4 Participate in a bike tour to the Dandong Yalu River Broken Bridge.
Day 5 Explore Mount Qian Shan and take in the breathtaking views.
Day 6 Visit the Shenyang Botanical Garden and take a nature walk.
Day 7 Departure from Shenyang.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Hills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dumplings: Dumplings are one of the most popular dishes in Shenyang, and the locals make them in various shapes and flavors.
  • Braised Pork: A slow-cooked dish with soy sauce, sugar, and other spices. It is served with rice.
  • Hotpot: An ideal dish to enjoy in winter, featuring a pot of hot broth with fresh vegetables, meat, and seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Shenyang Beer: Shenyang beer is a favorite in Liaoning Province and is known for its crisp and refreshing taste.
  • LIAOHAI Liquor: A strong alcoholic drink made from sorghum and wheat. It is often consumed during festivals and weddings.

Where to Stay in Eastern Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shangri-La Hotel Shenyang: A five-star luxury hotel located in the heart of Shenyang city.
  • Hanting Express Shenyang Imperial Palace: A budget-friendly option within walking distance of the Shenyang Imperial Palace.
  • Home Inn Plus Shenyang Taishan Plaza: A mid-range hotel with great amenities and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Eastern Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rosewood Furniture: The Manchu people have a long history of woodcarving, and rosewood is a popular material for furniture.
  • Manchu Traditional Wear: The Manchu traditional wear is known for its elegance and unique design.
  • Dried Seafood: Dried seafood is a popular souvenir in Liaoning Province, particularly the scallops and abalone.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Hills

Eastern Hills is home to several ethnic minorities, including the Manchu people, who have a distinct language, culture, and tradition. Visitors are recommended to learn about local customs, etiquette, and dress appropriately to show respect to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Hills

The best time to visit Eastern Hills is during the spring and autumn seasons, between March and May, and September to November when the climate is mild and nature is vibrant. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Hills

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately and modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn basic Chinese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Touch or move any religious artifacts or antiques.
  • Express any negative opinions about China or its leadership.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Hills

Eastern Hills is generally safe, with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take basic precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas alone at night and keeping an eye on valuables.

Health in Eastern Hills

Visitors should consult their doctor before traveling to China and ensure that they have all the necessary vaccines and medications. It is also advisable to carry a first-aid kit and travel insurance.

Getting to Eastern Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Hills

  • Shenyang Taoxian International Airport: The closest international airport, located about 30 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Hills

  • Shenyang Bus Station: The main bus station in Shenyang, with connections to several cities in China.

Other Options

  • Train: Shenyang Railway Station is the main station, connecting several cities in China.

How to Get Around in Eastern Hills

Eastern Hills can be explored by foot or by bike, with the latter being a popular option for travelers. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dalian: A coastal city in Liaoning Province known for its beaches and seafood.
  • Harbin: The capital city of Heilongjiang Province, known for its annual Ice and Snow Festival.
  • Changbai Mountain: A beautiful mountain range on the border between China and North Korea, known for its hot springs and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Eastern Hills is a unique destination that offers a balance of nature, culture, and history. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip to Liaoning Province and discover everything that Eastern Hills has to offer.
