The Ultimate Qianshan National Park Guide

Introduction to Qianshan National Park

Qianshan National Park is a picturesque area in the Eastern Hills of Liaoning Province, Northeast China. Covering around 44 square kilometers, this popular tourist spot is home to the Qianshan Mountain – one of the top 10 famous mountains in China.

Qianshan National Park is known for its stunning scenery, rich cultural heritage, and diverse flora and fauna. It’s an ideal destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Qianshan National Park

Here are the top places to visit in Qianshan National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Qianshan Museum: A comprehensive museum showcasing the park’s cultural heritage, with exhibits including ancient pottery, bronze ware, and jade.

  • Shenyang Imperial Palace: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this palace served as the residence of the early Qing Emperors and is known for its well-preserved architecture, ornate decorations, and valuable cultural relics.

  • Shenyang Zhongshan Park: A serene public park featuring beautiful landscapes, pavilions, and historic buildings.

Natural Attractions of Qianshan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qianshan Mountain: The highlight of the park, this mountain is known for its unique ridges, peaks, and waterfalls. There are several trails for trekking and climbing, and visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding forests and lakes.

  • Qianshan Lake: A large, serene lake with crystal clear water, surrounded by lush vegetation and scenic spots.

  • Fairy Cave: A popular hiking destination with a legendary love story attached, this cave is known for its unique stalactites and stalagmites that resemble fairy tales.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qianshan National Park

There are numerous hiking and biking routes in and around Qianshan National Park. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Qianshan-Zhangshi Scenic Route: A 15 km journey that takes you through scenic locations across the park, including the Zhangshiyan Scenic Area, and other scenic spots of Xianrendong and Liguangsi.

  • Qianshan Mountain Climbing Route: A 7.5 km long trek that starts from the visitor center and leads you to the top of Qianshan Mountain.

  • Chezhong Temple - Yuhuang Peak Route: This 7 km long route is known for its beautiful scenery, including ancient temples, mountain streams, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Qianshan National Park: Activities and Tours

Here are some other fun things to do in Qianshan National Park:

  • Take a cable car ride up to the peak of Qianshan Mountain for panoramic views of the park.

  • Explore the local villages around the park, such as the Manchu Ethnic Village, and experience traditional Chinese culture.

  • Visit the hot springs and relax after a long day of hiking or biking.

  • Take a boat ride on Qianshan Lake and enjoy the tranquil scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Qianshan National Park

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Qianshan National Park:

Day 1: Arrive and check into your hotel/guesthouse Day 2: Visit Qianshan Mountain and take a cable car ride to the peak Day 3: Explore the Zhangshiyan Scenic Area and Chezhong Temple Day 4: Visit the Shenyang Imperial Palace and Shenyang Zhongshan Park Day 5: Take a boat ride on Qianshan Lake and relax at the hot springs Day 6: Visit the Qianshan Museum and other cultural attractions Day 7: Depart from Qianshan National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Qianshan National Park

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Qianshan National Park:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Manchu Hot Pot: A traditional hot pot with lamb, beef, and vegetables.

  • Daoxiao Noodles: Handmade noodles with various toppings, such as beef, pork, or vegetables.

  • Seafood: Fresh seafood dishes such as crab, shrimp, and fish are widely available in the park.

Local Beverages

  • Shenyang Bird’s Nest: A health drink made from edible bird’s nests that are rich in nutrients and minerals.

  • Shenyang Huangjiu: A traditional Chinese wine made from glutinous rice and yeast.

What to Buy in Qianshan National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy in Qianshan National Park:

  • Handicrafts: Locally-made handcrafts, such as jade ornaments and paintings, are perfect souvenirs.

  • Medicinal herbs: The park is known for its medicinal herbs, such as ginseng and wolfberry, which are excellent for health and make great gifts.

  • Tea: Qianshan is famous for its tea plantations, and you can find various kinds of tea, such as green tea and black tea, in the shops around the park.

Local Culture and Customs of Qianshan National Park

Qianshan National Park has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its Manchu ethnic culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices, such as showing reverence when visiting temples and shrines.

Best Time to Visit Qianshan National Park

The best time to visit Qianshan National Park is from April to October when the weather is pleasant and the park is in full bloom. However, the peak tourist season is during Chinese New Year (January or February) and National Day (October 1-7).

Do’s and Don’ts of Qianshan National Park

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and wear comfortable shoes.

  • Bring sunscreen, a hat, and insect repellent.

  • Respect local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter, spit, or vandalize park property.

  • Disturb or harm the flora and fauna in the park.

  • Buy or take home rare or endangered plant and animal species without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qianshan National Park

  • Follow park rules and regulations for your safety.

  • Stay hydrated and bring enough water for your hikes.

  • Beware of slippery and uneven terrain in some areas of the park.

Health in Qianshan National Park

  • Make sure to get a travel vaccination before your trip, if necessary.

  • Bring necessary medications and first aid supplies.

  • Avoid drinking tap water and eat only cooked foods to prevent stomach issues.

Getting to Qianshan National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to get to Qianshan National Park:

Airports Near Qianshan National Park

  • Shenyang Taoxian International Airport (SHE) is the nearest airport to Qianshan National Park, located around 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Qianshan National Park

  • Shenyang Bus Station: Take the bus from Shenyang to Anshan and get off at Qianshan National Park.

  • Anshan Bus Station: Take the bus to Qianshan National Park.

Other Options

  • Taxi or private car: You can hire a taxi or a private car for a more comfortable and convenient trip.

  • Train: Take the train from Shenyang to Anshan and then take the bus to Qianshan National Park.

How to Get Around in Qianshan National Park

The best way to get around Qianshan National Park is on foot, and most of the scenic spots are within walking distance. You can also rent a bike or take a cable car to Qianshan Mountain’s peak.

Where to Go Next from Qianshan National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations to visit after your trip to Qianshan National Park:

  • Shenyang: A vibrant city with a rich historical and cultural legacy, home to the Shenyang Imperial Palace, the Royal Tombs of the Qing Dynasty, and other attractions.

  • Panjin Red Beach: A natural wonder and major tourist attraction of red-tinged wetland renowned for its biodiversity, located around 60 km away from Qianshan.

  • Dalian: A modern seaside city with beautiful beaches, scenic coasts, and attractions such as Dalian Tiger Beach Ocean Park and Mount Lao National Forest Park.

Conclusion

Qianshan National Park offers a unique and unforgettable experience, with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and diverse activities for every visitor.
