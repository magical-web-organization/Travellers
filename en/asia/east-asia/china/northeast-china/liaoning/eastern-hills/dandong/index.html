Introduction to Dandong

Located in the Eastern Hills of Liaoning, in Northeast China, Dandong is a popular tourist destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor adventures. Bordering North Korea, Dandong is known for the historic Yalu River Broken Bridge, which was damaged during the Korean War and stands as a poignant symbol of the region’s troubled past.

Visitors to Dandong can also explore a range of exciting attractions, including museums, national parks, and scenic hiking and biking trails. With a growing food and drink scene, an abundance of affordable accommodation options, and easy access to nearby airports and bus stations, Dandong is an ideal destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Northeast China.

Local Culture and Customs of Dandong

Dandong has a rich history and culture, with a blend of Chinese, Korean, and Manchu influences. Visitors can experience traditional Northeastern cuisine, watch performances featuring traditional dance and music, and learn about the region’s fascinating history and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Dandong

The best time to visit Dandong is from April to October when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. However, visitors should be aware that summer can be hot and humid, so it is important to pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dandong

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Take photos of military installations
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Dandong

Dandong is considered a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone at night and avoid displaying expensive belongings in public.

Health in Dandong

Visitors to Dandong should ensure that they are up to date with routine vaccinations and take appropriate precautions to avoid food-borne illnesses. It is also important to drink only bottled water.
