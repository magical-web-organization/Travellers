Introduction to Taizhou

Located in the eastern part of China, Taizhou is a beautiful city in the Jiangsu province. Taizhou is known for its natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture. The city’s combination of both urban and rural landscapes makes it an ideal place for tourists seeking adventure or relaxation. Taizhou is home to great restaurants, museums, temples, and ancient villages that attract travelers from all over the world. In this guide, we will explore the hidden gems of Taizhou, including the best places to visit, dine, and experience.

List of Places to Visit in Taizhou

Taizhou offers something for everyone- from natural parks, historical landmarks, to local markets, and delicious street food. Here are some must-visit places while in Taizhou.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Taizhou Museum

If you are interested in learning about Taizhou’s rich history, there is no better place to start than the Taizhou Museum. The museum showcases ancient artifacts, paintings, and sculptures from the city’s past, all of which offer great insight into the area’s culture and evolution. Open from Tuesdays to Sundays, the museum is conveniently located at No. 99 Haibin South Road, just a short walk from the bustling city center.

Tiantai Mountain

Taizhou is home to two Buddhist mountains, each of which offers breathtaking views and fascinating temples. Tiantai Mountain is a must-visit for tourists interested in exploring the city’s religious culture. The mountain is home to several temples, including Guoqing Temple, which has been standing for over 1,600 years. The mountain is open to visitors year-round and attracts thousands of pilgrims from all over China.

Natural Attractions of Taizhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Xianju National Park

Located at the foot of the Xianju Mountains, Xianju National Park offers a picturesque view of the city’s lush countryside. Boasting a diverse range of flora and fauna, nature lovers can explore its forests, gorges, and waterfalls that are home to various rare bird species. Visitors can hike, bike, or even take a cable car to the top of the mountain for breathtaking views of the park.

Tiantai Mountain Scenic Area

Tiantai Mountain Scenic Area is one of the most popular attractions in Taizhou city. The area offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and Pagodas. Visitors can opt to hike or take a cable car to a higher altitude to enjoy stunning landscapes. The scenic area is open year-round, making it an ideal destination for tourists wanting to soak in the beauty of Taizhou’s nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taizhou

Taizhou is known for its picturesque hiking and biking trails. These trails offer a green and peaceful escape to the bustling city life. Some of the most popular hiking routes include:

  • Guoqing Temple Hiking Trail
  • Xianju Mountain Hiking Trail
  • Lulianwang Hiking Trail

Cycling enthusiasts can bike along the Baixianhu Lake Wetland Park or explore Taizhou’s city center streets on a shared bike.

Food and Drink Scene of Taizhou

Taizhou’s culinary scene is a mixture of northern and southern Chinese cuisine. As a coastal city, Taizhou is famous for its seafood, but it also has a wide range of other dishes that cater to different preferences. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks while visiting Taizhou.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish in Sweet and Sour Sauce (蕃茄红煨鱼)
  • Braised Abalone (红烧鲍鱼)
  • Crab with Sticky Rice (蟹粉小籽粥)
  • Wonton Soup (馄饨汤)

Local Beverages

  • Taizhou Wine (泰州糟醸酒)
  • Chinese Tea (茶)

What to Buy in Taizhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taizhou is famous for its art, craft, and local products. Visitors can head to local markets or souvenir shops to buy gifts or souvenirs to take back home. The most popular souvenirs of Taizhou include:

  • Taizhou Embroidery

  • Bamboo and Wooden Handicrafts

Local Culture and Customs of Taizhou

Taizhou has a rich cultural heritage with roots dating back thousands of years. The locals highly value their culture and adhere to certain customs and rituals. Visitors must respect the local customs to avoid any mishap. Some of the essential customs of Taizhou include:

  • Addressing elders with honorific titles
  • Taking off shoes while entering a home
  • Using chopsticks while dining
  • Avoiding the number four, which is considered unlucky

Best Time to Visit Taizhou

The best time to visit Taizhou is during the autumn season (September-November). The weather conditions during this time are pleasant with bright blue skies, and an average temperature between 15 to 25 degrees Celsius. The warm weather also makes this time perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and boating.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taizhou

Taizhou is considered a safe city for tourists. Visitors should take standard precautions like not carrying valuable items while going to crowded places, not walking alone in isolated areas, and being aware of pickpockets in tourist areas.

Health in Taizhou

The local water in Taizhou is not safe for drinking without boiling or filtering. Visitors must carry bottled water or opt for safe drinking water sources. As with other Chinese cities, in Taizhou, air pollution may create problems for those with respiratory issues.

How to Get Around in Taizhou

Public transportation is widely available in Taizhou. Visitors have different options to choose from, including taxis, local buses, shared bikes, and scooters. Taxis are most convenient for tourists and are available around the city. Visitors can rent bikes or scooters for a more affordable and green commuting option.

Where to Go Next from Taizhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby cities and attractions from Taizhou. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Shanghai – The bustling city offers cultural and historic sites, fine dining, and great shopping district.
  • Hangzhou - A scenic city offers one of the most beautiful West Lake, temples, parks, and offers the best Chinese Green Tea.
  • Ningbo – A coastal city offers a high number of historic and cultural landmarks, temples, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Taizhou is a great city for travelers interested in exploring China’s rich history, culture, and natural beauty. The city offers a mix of urban and rural landscapes that cater to different interests. The local food, hospitality, and customs make it a perfect destination for tourists seeking an authentic Chinese experience. With this comprehensive guide of Taizhou, travelers can make the most of their trip by discovering the city’s hidden gems, cuisines, and culture.
