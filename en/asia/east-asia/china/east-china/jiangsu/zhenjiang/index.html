Introduction to Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang is a charming city located in the eastern part of China in the Jiangsu province. The city is surrounded by hills and rivers which create stunning landscapes, and a pleasant climate all year round. Zhenjiang is an ancient city with a unique blend of historical culture and modern development, making it a must-visit destination for travelers who enjoy culture, scenic views, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Zhenjiang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zhenjiang boasts a rich history that dates back to the ancient times, and there are several museums and historical places that showcase this heritage. Here are some of the culturally significant attractions you must visit:

Jinshan Temple

One of the most famous Buddhist temples in China, the Jinshan Temple, is located on a hill overlooking the Yangtze River. The temple was built in 1334 and is known for its stunning architecture and lovely surroundings. Visitors can explore the temple complex that includes the Hall of Heavenly Kings, the Hall of Sakyamuni, and the Guanyin Pavilion.

Xijin Ferry

For a unique experience, take a ride on the Xijin Ferry, which has been in existence for over 1,300 years. The ferry is hand-pulled by ferryman along the Yangtze River and is an excellent opportunity to observe the traditional way of life in Zhenjiang.

Beigu Hill Scenic Area

Beigu Hill Scenic Area is a must-visit attraction in Zhenjiang, which offers breathtaking views of the city and its surroundings. The area features several temples, pagodas, gardens, and historical sites that are worth exploring.

Natural Attractions of Zhenjiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the historical sites, Zhenjiang has a lot to offer for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the natural attractions you should not miss:

Nanshan Scenic Area

Nanshan Scenic Area is a popular destination for hiking and exploring. It is located just outside the city and features several hills, waterfalls, forests, and scenic views. This area is perfect for travelers who enjoy the outdoors and looking for solitude.

Dantu Scenic Area

Dantu Scenic Area is an excellent place to escape the busy city life and spend some time in nature. The area features several lakes, walking trails, and scenic views where you can relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Jiaoshan Scenic Area

Jiaoshan Scenic Area is a mountainous region located in the western part of the city. This area is famous for its stunning views of the Yangtze River and the surrounding hills. The area is also home to several temples and historical sites that are worth exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang is a great place to explore on foot or bike, and there are several hiking and biking routes available. Here are some of the most popular routes:

Zhenjiang City Wall

The Zhenjiang City Wall is a well-preserved rampart built during the Ming Dynasty. The wall is 6.5 kilometers long and provides an excellent opportunity for hiking or biking while enjoying the city’s history and culture.

Jiaoshan Mountain Trail

The Jiaoshan Mountain Trail is an excellent option for hikers who enjoy challenging terrains and stunning views. The trail is located in Jiaoshan Scenic Area and takes approximately four hours to complete.

Nanshan Mountain Trail

Nanshan Mountain Trail is a great option for travelers who want to explore the natural beauty of Zhenjiang. The trail is located in Nanshan Scenic Area and consists of steep ascents and descents, waterfalls, and dense forests.

Best Things to Do in Zhenjiang: Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Zhenjiang, there is something for every type of traveler. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in Zhenjiang:

River Cruise

A river cruise along the Yangtze River is an excellent way to explore the scenic beauty of Zhenjiang. Several companies offer river cruises, and you can choose from day or night cruises to suit your preference.

Food Tour

Zhenjiang has an excellent food scene, and a food tour is the perfect way to try out local delicacies. Must-try dishes include sesame paste noodles, vinegar fish, fried rice balls, and soy sauce duck.

Traditional Chinese Medicine Experience

If you are interested in traditional Chinese medicine, Zhenjiang is an excellent place to learn more about it. Several clinics and healing centers offer acupuncture, cupping, and other traditional methods of healing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhenjiang

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Zhenjiang, here’s an itinerary that covers the best of Zhenjiang:

Day 1: Visit Jinshan Temple and take a boat ride on the Yangtze River.

Day 2: Explore Beigu Hill Scenic Area and visit the Zhenjiang Museum.

Day 3: Hike the Nanshan Mountain Trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Nanjing and visit historical sites such as the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and the Confucius Temple.

Day 5: Visit Dantu Scenic Area and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Day 6: Take a food tour and try out local delicacies.

Day 7: Visit Jiaoshan Scenic Area and hike the Jiaoshan Mountain Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang has a rich culinary heritage, and the food scene is diverse and flavorful. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to experience:

Must-Try Dishes

Vinegar Fish

Vinegar fish is a signature dish of Zhenjiang’s cuisine. It consists of fish cooked in a unique blend of Zhenjiang vinegar, ginger, and sugar. The result is a perfect balance of sour and sweet flavors, making it a favorite of locals and tourists alike.

Sesame Paste Noodles

Sesame paste noodles are another popular dish that originated in Zhenjiang. The dish features noodles cooked in savory sesame sauce and topped with crispy cucumbers, carrots, and bean sprouts.

Soy Sauce Duck

Soy sauce duck is a classic Chinese dish that originated in Zhenjiang. It features duck meat cooked in soy sauce and served with rice or noodles.

Local Beverages

Huangjiu

Huangjiu is a type of Chinese rice wine that’s popular in Zhenjiang. The wine is smooth and sweet, making it an excellent pairing with spicy dishes.

Zhenjiang Vinegar

Zhenjiang Vinegar is a type of black vinegar that’s widely used in Chinese cooking. The vinegar is made from fermented rice and has a unique flavor that’s ideal for marinating or salad dressing.

What to Buy in Zhenjiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhenjiang has many unique local products that make great souvenirs or gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

Sesame Products

Sesame products are a significant part of Zhenjiang’s culinary heritage, and the city produces some of the best sesame oil, paste, and candies in China.

Red Lacquerware

Zhenjiang is known for its high-quality red lacquerware, which is made from a unique blend of sap, wax, and pigment. The items range from small jewelry boxes to larger furniture pieces and are a beautiful addition to any home.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang’s culture is a blend of Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting Zhenjiang:

Greeting

When greeting someone in Zhenjiang, it’s customary to nod slightly and say “Ni hao” (hello). Handshakes are common, but avoid hugging or kissing, as it’s not customary in China.

Tipping

Tipping is not a common practice in China, including Zhenjiang. Most restaurants and cafes have already included a service charge to the bill.

Best Time to Visit Zhenjiang

The best time to visit Zhenjiang is between March and May when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the flowers are in bloom. Another great time to visit is from September to November when the weather is cool and pleasant, and the autumn foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhenjiang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Zhenjiang:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Mandarin or download a translation app before your trip.
  • Carry a copy of your passport and visa with you at all times.

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove artifacts from historical sites.
  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in public areas.
  • Vandalize or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang is a safe city, and violent crime is rare. However, like any other city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take appropriate safety measures.

Health in Zhenjiang

The tap water in Zhenjiang is not safe to drink, so it’s recommended to stick to bottled water. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria are prevalent in some areas, so be sure to take appropriate preventive measures such as using insect repellents and taking antimalarial medication.

Getting to Zhenjiang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhenjiang

The nearest airport to Zhenjiang is Changzhou Benniu Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers from the city center. Another option is to fly into Shanghai Pudong International Airport, which is around 200 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang has several bus stations that serve different destinations. The main bus station is Zhenjiang Bus Station, which is located in the city center and offers regular services to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Zhenjiang has a high-speed railway station, which connects it to major cities such as Shanghai, Nanjing, and Hangzhou. It’s easy to take a train from these cities and reach Zhenjiang in just a few hours.

How to Get Around in Zhenjiang

Zhenjiang has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent bicycles from various rental stations located throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Zhenjiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nanjing

Nanjing is a fascinating city located just 120 kilometers northeast of Zhenjiang. The city has a rich history and features several historical sites such as the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, the Confucius Temple, and the Nanjing Massacre Memorial.

Hangzhou

Hangzhou is another beautiful city located just 200 kilometers south of Zhenjiang. The city is famous for its beautiful West Lake, which has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Conclusion

Zhenjiang is a beautiful city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical sites, hiking in the great outdoors, or trying out local delicacies, Zhenjiang has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Zhenjiang today to experience the best of this charming Chinese city.
