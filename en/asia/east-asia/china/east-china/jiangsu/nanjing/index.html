Introduction to Nanjing

Known as the “Southern Capital” of China, Nanjing is a historic city with a rich cultural and natural heritage. Situated in Jiangsu province, in East China, Nanjing has been an important center of culture, politics, and economics for thousands of years. The city has a unique blend of ancient and modern elements, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore China’s rich traditions while enjoying modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Nanjing

With so much to explore in Nanjing, it can be challenging to decide where to start. To make things easier, we have listed some of the city’s top attractions below.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum: This is the final resting place of Zhu Yuanzhang, the first emperor of the Ming Dynasty.

  • Nanjing Museum: One of the largest museums in China, this museum houses over 400,000 artifacts related to China’s history and culture.

  • Presidential Palace: This palace served as the headquarters of the Nationalist government during the late 1920s and early 1930s.

  • Zhonghua Gate: This giant city gate, also known as the Gate of China, is a famous landmark of Nanjing and a symbol of China’s ancient civilization.

Natural Attractions of Nanjing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xuanwu Lake: This picturesque lake is located in the center of the city and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or boating.

  • Purple Mountain: This scenic area boasts stunning views of the city and is home to several historical sites, including the Ming Tombs and Xiaoling Mausoleum.

  • Qinhuai River: A cruise down this river is a great way to see the city’s landmarks while enjoying the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanjing

If you are an adventure lover, Nanjing has plenty of options for you. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in the city include:

  • Purple Mountain: There are several hiking trails on the mountain, ranging from easy to challenging, and offering scenic views of the city and surrounding nature.

  • Xuanwu Lake: A bike ride around the lake is a great way to take in the views and enjoy some fresh air.

  • Nanjing City Wall: The 600-year-old city wall offers a panoramic view of the city and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or hike.

Best Things to Do in Nanjing: Activities and Tours

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, there is something to do in Nanjing for everyone. Some of the best things to do in Nanjing include:

  • Visit Confucius Temple: This temple is dedicated to the Chinese philosopher Confucius and is a great place to learn about Chinese culture and history.

  • Sip tea at a teahouse: Enjoy the art of Chinese tea culture with a cup of tea at a local teahouse.

  • Visit a silk factory: Nanjing is famous for its silk production, and visiting a silk factory is a great way to learn about its production process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nanjing

To help you make the most of your time in Nanjing, we have created a week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, Confucius Temple, and Zhanyuan Garden.

Day 2: Explore Purple Mountain, including the Sun Yat-Sen Mausoleum, Linggu Temple, and Meiling Palace.

Day 3: Visit the Nanjing Museum and Presidential Palace, followed by a stroll along the Qinhuai River.

Day 4: Take a tour of the city wall, followed by a bike ride around Xuanwu Lake.

Day 5: Visit Jiming Temple and Chaotian Palace, followed by a visit to a traditional Chinese medicine pharmacy.

Day 6: Explore the food scene of Nanjing, including a visit to the famous Fuzimiao Street for street food.

Day 7: Visit a silk factory and shop for souvenirs before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Nanjing

Nanjing is famous for its distinctive cuisine, influenced by its long history and location near the Yangtze River. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salted Duck: A Nanjing specialty, the salted duck is known for its crispy skin and succulent meat.

  • Nanjing Salted Duck Soup: This soup is made with salted duck, shredded bamboo, and other ingredients, and is a perfect meal on a cold day.

  • De Ta Si Rou: A popular pork dish, it is usually marinated with soy sauce and sugar and served with steamed buns.

Local Beverages

  • Plum Wine: A traditional Chinese wine made from plums.

  • Suan Cai Yu: A spicy fish dish typically paired with rice wine or beer.

Where to Stay in Nanjing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nanjing offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  • Jinling Hotel Nanjing: A five-star hotel located in the city center, offering luxurious accommodation and excellent amenities.

  • Hanting Express Nanjing Hunan Road: A budget-friendly hotel that is convenient for travelers, located close to many attractions in the city.

  • Huijiazhuba Guesthouse: A guesthouse that offers excellent value for money, located in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Nanjing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nanjing is known for its silk products, pottery, and carved objects. Some of the best souvenirs to bring back include:

  • Nanjing Brocade: A type of silk fabric that is handwoven and highly valued for its intricate designs.

  • Purple Clay Teapots: A traditional teapot made from clay found only in Nanjing.

  • Jinling Lanterns: Colorful paper lanterns that are a symbol of Nanjing’s culture and history.

Local Culture and Customs of Nanjing

Nanjing has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Addressing People: Address older people as “uncle” or “auntie,” regardless of whether they are related.

  • Eating Etiquette: When dining, it is customary to leave a little bit of food on your plate as a sign of respect for the host.

  • Gifting: Giving gifts is an important part of Chinese culture. It is customary to bring a small gift when visiting someone’s home or meeting someone for the first time.

Best Time to Visit Nanjing

The best time to visit Nanjing is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and nature is at its best. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanjing

Do:

  • Try the delicious local food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to carry cash, as many places may not accept credit cards.
  • Leave without trying the Nanjing Salted Duck.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanjing

Nanjing is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, travelers should take basic precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night in less-traveled areas and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Nanjing

Travelers to Nanjing should be aware of the air pollution in the city, which can be a problem, especially during the winter months. It is recommended to carry a mask and take regular breaks in clean air if needed.

Getting to Nanjing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nanjing

Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG) is the main airport serving the city, and it is located approximately 35 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Nanjing

There are several bus stations in Nanjing, including the main station of Nanjing Bus Terminal, which provides service to cities all over China.

Other Options

The train and taxi are also popular transportation options.

How to Get Around in Nanjing

Travelers can use the local buses, taxis, and metro to get around in Nanjing. A transportation card can be used for multiple modes of transport.

Where to Go Next from Nanjing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nanjing is a great base for exploring other cities in China. Some popular destinations for excursions from Nanjing include:

  • Shanghai: China’s biggest city, located a few hours away from Nanjing, Shanghai is known for its modern architecture and international flavor.

  • Hangzhou: Famous for its beautiful natural scenery and West Lake, Hangzhou is a popular destination for domestic and international tourists.

Conclusion

Nanjing is a city with a rich cultural and natural heritage, offering something for every kind of traveler. With a variety of attractions, food scene, accommodation options, and transportation, Nanjing is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to China.
