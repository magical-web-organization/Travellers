Introduction to Nantong

Nantong, a prefecture-level city in the Jiangsu Province of China, is located in the Yangtze River Delta Economic Zone. It is widely known for its rich cultural heritage, fascinating history, and magnificent landscapes. Nantong has something for everyone, from grand temples and museums to stunning parks and gardens.

List of Places to Visit in Nantong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history enthusiast, you will love exploring Nantong’s cultural landmarks. Here are some popular historical and cultural attractions.

  • Nantong Museum: One of the biggest museums in Jiangsu, Nantong Museum exhibits historical artifacts and cultural relics of China and the region.
  • Tongzhou Ancient City: Visit Tongzhou Ancient City to see the remains of the city’s wall and gate tower that date back to the Ming and Qing dynasties.
  • Taiping Heavenly Kingdom History Museum: Learn about the fascinating history of the Taiping uprising at this museum.
  • Nantong City Wall: Explore one of the few remaining sections of the ancient city wall in Nantong.
  • Hao River Scenic Area: Take a river cruise and enjoy the beautiful scenery along the Hao River.

Natural Attractions of Nantong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nantong is home to several natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some outdoor activities and scenic spots that you should not miss.

  • Langshan Scenic Area: Langshan is one of the highest peaks in the region, offering a panoramic view of Nantong’s beautiful landscapes.
  • Xinyuan Canal Scenic Area: This scenic area is famous for its picturesque water channels and bridges that are surrounded by beautiful old architecture.
  • Nantong Binjiang National Forest Park: Situated on the banks of the Yangtze River, this national forest park offers a wide range of outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Wetland Park: With a vast area of over 74 hectares, Nantong Wetland Park is home to several species of birds and aquatic animals. The park also features a beautiful lake, lush forests, and gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nantong

Langshan National Forest Park

Langshan National Forest Park is divided into four scenic spots, namely Sheshan, Yingzhou, Taohuajian, and Shuilian Cave. Sheshan is the highest peak in the park, offering a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains and beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Nantong: Activities and Tours

Nantong is full of activities to suit any type of traveler’s needs. Here are some of the best things to do in Nantong:

  • Visit Nantong’s bustling Night Market: Try local street food and shop for souvenirs while exploring Nantong’s famous Night Market.
  • Take a boat ride on the Hao River: Explore the city by water and enjoy Nantong’s beautiful scenery and the traditional cityscape.
  • Participate in a local temple fair: Join the enthusiastic crowds at Zhangshan Temple’s annual fair, which includes a variety of cultural performances and activities.
  • Watch a cultural show at Nantong Theater: Enjoy traditional performances like Nantong Opera, classical Chinese dance, and martial arts shows.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nantong

Day 1: Visit Nantong Museum and Taiping Heavenly Kingdom History Museum Day 2: Explore Tongzhou Ancient City and Nantong City Wall Day 3: Go on a scenic river cruise along the Hao River and visit the Wetland Park Day 4: Take a hike in Langshan National Forest Park Day 5: Explore Xinyuan Canal Scenic Area
Day 6: Visit the Night Market and Nantong Theater Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at Nantong Binjiang National Forest Park

Food and Drink Scene of Nantong

Must-Try Dishes

Nantong is famous for its unique and delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Jianghai Crayfish: Fresh crayfish boiled with Sichuan pepper, garlic, and other spicy seasonings.
  • Tongzhou Grilled Cake: Popular street food made with sesame or bean paste and crispy dough.
  • Nantong Glutinous Rice Dumplings: Steamed dumplings filled with sweet and savory fillings made from beans, meat, or eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Suqian Black Tea: Known for its delicate aroma and smooth taste, Suqian Black Tea is one of the most famous tea varieties in China.
  • Osmanthus Wine: A sweet and fragrant wine made from the Osmanthus flower that is popular among locals.

Where to Stay in Nantong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nantong offers various accommodation options that can cater to any traveler’s needs. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, here are some popular options:

  • InterContinental Nantong: A five-star hotel located in the city center and offers luxurious amenities and excellent service.
  • Jinjiang Inn Nantong Pedestrian Street: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Nantong’s downtown shopping district.
  • Motel 168 Nantong Renmin Middle Road: A comfortable hotel located near some of Nantong’s most famous attractions.

What to Buy in Nantong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nantong is known for producing a wide range of local products that are unique and make excellent souvenirs. Here are some of the most popular products that you can buy in Nantong:

  • Yangtze River Pearls: Known for its luster and unique shape, Yangtze River Pearls are an excellent souvenir
  • Nantong Silk: Silk is a traditional handicraft in Nantong that is famous for its glossy appearance and soft texture.
  • Zitang Wine: A type of Chinese rice wine that is made using traditional methods and has a unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Nantong

Nantong has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs that have been passed down through generations. Here are some cultural aspects that you should know before visiting Nantong:

  • The Dragon Boat Festival: Nantong celebrates the Dragon Boat Festival every year in June, where locals participate in dragon boat races and eat rice dumplings.
  • Traditional Teahouses: Teahouses are an essential part of local life, where people gather to socialize and enjoy tea and snacks.
  • Nantong Opera: Nantong Opera is a traditional form of opera that is unique to Nantong and the surrounding areas.

Best Time to Visit Nantong

The best time to visit Nantong is during spring and autumn, specifically between March and May, and September and October. These seasons bring pleasant weather, and the city’s landscapes are stunning during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nantong

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in Chinese before visiting Nantong. The locals will appreciate the effort.
  • Try the local cuisine and attend a traditional cultural event.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the local culture and religion.
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions and cultural landmarks.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nantong

Nantong is a safe place for tourists, and the crime rate is generally low. However, it is always better to take basic precautions such as avoiding dark alleys, protecting your valuables, and not carrying large amounts of cash around with you.

Health in Nantong

Nantong has several hospitals and clinics that can provide medical care, including the Nantong First People’s Hospital, the Nantong Tumor Hospital, and the Nantong Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital.

Getting to Nantong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nantong is well-connected to other major cities in China and is easily accessible by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Nantong

  • Nantong Xingdong Airport: The most convenient airport for international travelers is Nantong Xingdong Airport, which is located about 13 kilometers from Nantong’s city center.

Bus Stations Near Nantong

  • Nantong Long-Distance Bus Station: The main bus station in Nantong offers regular bus services to major cities like Shanghai, Nanjing, and Yangzhou.

Other Options

  • Train: Nantong Railway Station offers regular services to other cities like Shanghai and Nanjing.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Nantong and are a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Nantong

The most convenient way to get around Nantong is by taxi, which is efficient and affordable. It is also possible to explore the city on foot or by bicycle.

Public Transportation in Nantong

  • Bus: Nantong has an extensive bus system that connects the major tourist destinations and neighborhoods in the city.
  • Subway: Currently, there is no subway system in Nantong.

Where to Go Next from Nantong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Suzhou: Known for its classical gardens, Suzhou is a city located 150 kilometers from Nantong.
  • Yangzhou: Yangzhou is a city situated about 190 kilometers from Nantong, known for its beautiful gardens, canals, and ancient architecture.
  • Shanghai: Shanghai, known for its iconic skyline, is located 120 kilometers from Nantong.

Conclusion

Nantong is a charming city that offers a glimpse of traditional Chinese culture and stunning natural scenery. It is the perfect blend of modern and ancient China, offering visitors something unique and unforgettable. If you’re looking for a destination that offers culture, history, and natural beauty, Nantong is the perfect place to visit.
