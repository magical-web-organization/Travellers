Introduction to Kunshan

Kunshan, a small city in Jiangsu Province, is an excellent destination for a cultural and scenic getaway from the hustle and bustle of Shanghai. It boasts a history of over 2000 years, and its long-standing traditions promise an authentic experience for visitors. Located between Shanghai and Suzhou, it is well-connected to major cities via various transport modes, making it an ideal destination for weekend trips.

List of Places to Visit in Kunshan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kunqu Opera Museum: A must-visit museum for opera enthusiasts, the museum documents the history, training process, and costumes of the Kunqu Opera, a traditional Chinese opera.
  2. Old Kunshan Town: Dating back to the Ming Dynasty, this charming town is a perfect place to stroll around, enjoy local snacks, and admire the well-preserved architecture.
  3. Yuezhong Art Museum: A 150-year-old mansion turned art gallery, Yuezhong Art Museum showcases traditional Chinese artworks, including calligraphy, paintings, jade carvings, and ceramics.
  4. Shanghai Auto Museum (Kunshan Branch): A paradise for car lovers, the museum displays vintage and rare automobiles. Don’t miss the opportunity to ride the classic cars and explore the 4D cinema.

Natural Attractions of Kunshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Yangcheng Lake: Famous for its premium hairy crabs, the lake is also an enchanting spot for boating, especially in autumn when the osmanthus trees on the lake bank are in full bloom.
  2. Suzhou Kunshan Forest Park: Home to over 120 plant species and ten animal species, the park is an excellent escape into nature. The park also offers camping sites and adventure activities like zip-lining and archery.
  3. Kunshan Bailu Park: A serene park with beautiful lake views and walking trails dotted with traditional Chinese pavilions and gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kunshan

  1. Yangcheng Lake Wetland: An eco-tourism destination with breathtaking views of the lake and lush green paddy fields. Rent a bike or choose the hiking trail to explore the area.
  2. Kunshan Grand Canal: A bike tour along the historic canal is a perfect way to indulge in scenic beauty and history with several ancient bridges, water towns, and cultural relics on the way.
  3. Xiashi Scenic Area: A paradise for hiking enthusiasts, the scenic area offers a diverse range of trails amidst waterfalls, bamboo forests, and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Kunshan: Activities and Tours

  1. Kunshan Water Towns Tour: Book a guided tour of the nearest water towns like Zhouzhuang, Tongli, and Luzhi to immerse yourself in the intricate water systems, bridges, and old streets.
  2. Kunshan Hot Springs Tour: Relax in the natural hot springs and enjoy wellness therapies like massages and facials in the nearby hot springs resort towns like Tangshan and Taoyuan.
  3. Blossom Season Tour: Visit Kunshan in spring (mid-March to April) and witness the picturesque sea of flowers in the Flower Expo Garden, Tianxiangyuan, and Shidongkou.

Food and Drink Scene of Kunshan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hairy Crab: A specialty in Kunshan, the hairy crab is a local delicacy during autumn. It is usually steamed and served with ginger and vinegar.
  2. Squirrel Fish: A crispy outer layer with tender meat, the squirrel fish is a popular fish dish transformed into a traditional Chinese style in Kunshan.
  3. Mianjian Guanxiang: A sweet snack made by stuffing glutinous rice flour dough with sweet bean paste.

Local Beverages

  1. Kun Opera Tea: A refreshing drink made of chrysanthemum, lotus seeds, and rock sugar, believed to aid digestion and relieve inflammation.
  2. Luzhi Rice Wine: A high-quality traditional Chinese wine made of glutinous or regular rice with a mellow taste and low alcoholic content.

Where to Stay in Kunshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Tonino Lamborghini Hotel Kunshan City Center: A luxurious hotel with well-furnished rooms, a heated pool, and a rooftop bar.
  2. Kunshan King Kong Garden Hotel: A budget-friendly option with cozy rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant serving Chinese and Western cuisines.
  3. Garden International Hotel Kunshan: A hotel with spacious rooms, a gym, a spa, and a restaurant with excellent views of the city.

What to Buy in Kunshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kunqu Opera Masks: Colorful masks with different expressions used in the Kunqu Opera performances.
  2. Silk Products: High-quality silk products like scarves, ties, and beddings are readily available in Kunshan.
  3. Liuhe Tower Tea: A speciality tea from Liuhe county famous for its unique aroma and taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Kunshan

  1. Kunqu Opera: A traditional Chinese opera with a history of over 600 years, Kunqu opera is the pride of Kunshan and a reflection of the city’s culture.
  2. Pingtan: A storytelling art performed with music and sing-song voice, Pingtan is prevalent in the Shanghai-Kunshan region.

Best Time to Visit Kunshan

Kunshan has a subtropical monsoon climate, with four distinct seasons. The best time to visit is in spring (mid-March to April) when the flowers are in full bloom, or autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and the hairy crabs are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kunshan

Do:

  1. Learn some basic Mandarin phrases.
  2. Respect the local customs and culture.
  3. Take off your shoes before entering a home or temple.

Don’t:

  1. Eat or drink in public transportation.
  2. Take pictures or videos without permission in private places.
  3. Wear revealing clothes when visiting temples or places of worship.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kunshan

Kunshan is a safe city with low crime rates. However, take precautions against pickpocketing and street scams in crowded areas.

Health in Kunshan

Tap water in Kunshan is not drinkable, and visitors should stick to bottled water. Check the air quality index before visiting in colder months.

Getting to Kunshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kunshan

  1. Shanghai Pudong International Airport
  2. Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport

Both are about an hour’s drive from Kunshan.

Bus Stations Near Kunshan

  1. Kunshan South Bus Station
  2. Kunshan Passenger Transport Center

Both have direct buses to Shanghai, Suzhou, and other major cities in Jiangsu Province.

Other Options

Kunshan is well-connected to Shanghai and Suzhou via high-speed trains, and getting around within the city is convenient with buses and taxis.

How to Get Around in Kunshan

Buses, taxis, and bikes are available in Kunshan. Bike rental stations are prevalent in the city, and bike-sharing apps like Ofo and Mobike are also widely used.

Where to Go Next from Kunshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Suzhou: Known for its beautiful gardens, canals, and silk industry, Suzhou is only half-hour train ride from Kunshan.
  2. Shanghai: The global city and financial center of China, Shanghai is an hour’s drive from Kunshan and full of tourist attractions, entertainment, and shopping options.
  3. Hangzhou: The picturesque city famous for its West Lake, tea plantations, and silk industry, Hangzhou is a two-hour drive from Kunshan.

Conclusion

Kunshan, a small city with significant cultural and natural attractions, is an excellent weekend getaway destination. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to plan for a memorable trip to Kunshan with ease. Be ready to indulge in authentic Chinese culture, food, and adventure amidst stunning natural landscapes.
