Introduction to Changshu

Located in the eastern part of China, Changshu is a quiet yet beautiful city in Jiangsu Province. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture, Changshu offers visitors an unforgettable experience.

Changshu is a small city located between Suzhou and Wuxi. The city is divided into four districts, each with its distinct charm. The downtown area is modern and bustling, while the countryside offers visitors a chance to explore traditional Chinese villages and natural landscapes.

Changshu is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a peaceful retreat, away from the hustle and bustle of big cities. In this guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit in Changshu, hiking/biking routes, the food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Changshu

Changshu has a rich history and a lot to offer for visitors. From culturally significant museums to natural attractions, there’s something for everyone in Changshu. Here is a list of the best places to visit in Changshu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Changshu Museum of Archeology
  2. Shang Lake Scenic Area
  3. Yushan Mountain National Forest Park
  4. Xingfu Temple

Natural Attractions of Changshu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Shangfangshan National Forest Park
  2. Changshu Binjiang Wetland Park
  3. Jiulongshan Forest Park
  4. Lutang Fountainhead Wetland Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changshu

Changshu has many hiking and biking routes, offering visitors a chance to explore the city’s natural beauty up close. The following are some of the best trails in Changshu:

  1. Shangfangshan National Forest Park – 8-kilometer long trail with beautiful scenic views.
  2. Jiulongshan Forest Park – a 15-kilometer trail through dense forests and beautiful lakes.
  3. Lutang Fountainhead Wetland Park – a 5-kilometer trail through an eco-park with stunning waterways and vegetation.
  4. Yushan Mountain National Forest Park – a 10-kilometer trail with beautiful mountain views and traditional Chinese architecture.

Best Things to Do in Changshu: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of fun and exciting things to do in Changshu. Whether you’re interested in cultural activities or outdoor adventures, Changshu has something for you. Here is a list of the best things to do in Changshu:

  1. Visit the Xingfu Temple – a peaceful temple with beautiful architecture and interesting history.
  2. Take a boat ride in Shang Lake – a beautiful lake surrounded by mountains and forests.
  3. Visit Changshu Museum of Archeology – a window into Changshu’s rich history and culture.
  4. Take a hike in Shangfangshan National Forest Park – one of the most scenic spots in Changshu.

Where to Stay in Changshu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Changshu has a diverse range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Changshu:

  1. Crowne Plaza Changshu - a luxurious hotel with stunning views of the city.
  2. Hanting Club Changshu Yushan Road – a budget-friendly option with clean and modern rooms.
  3. Changshu Yuxing Guest House – a traditional Chinese guesthouse with beautiful architecture and elegant rooms.

What to Buy in Changshu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Changshu is famous for its silk and textile industry, and visitors can buy unique and beautiful products made from silk, cotton, and wool. Here are some of the best things to buy in Changshu:

  1. Silk products
  2. Suzhou embroidery
  3. Handmade pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Changshu

Changshu has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience the city’s unique customs and traditions. Here are some cultural highlights of Changshu:

  1. Dragon and lion dances during the Chinese New Year celebrations.
  2. Changshu International Tourism Festival in September.
  3. Traditional Chinese Tea culture.

Best Time to Visit Changshu

The best time to visit Changshu is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changshu

When visiting Changshu, the following are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try local cuisine and drinks.
  3. Learn some basic Chinese phrases before your trip.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothes in public places.
  2. Enter temples and religious sites without removing hats and shoes.
  3. Photograph locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Changshu

Changshu is a relatively safe city with low levels of crime. However, visitors are advised to take usual precautions, such as avoiding dark alleys at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Changshu

Visitors should be up to date with routine vaccines, and it is advisable to consult a travel health specialist before traveling to Changshu.

Getting to Changshu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Changshu is well-connected to other cities in China, and visitors can easily access the city via plane, train, or bus.

Airports Near Changshu

The nearest international airport to Changshu is Shanghai Pudong International Airport, located about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Changshu

Changshu has two main bus stations: Changshu Bus Station and Changshu Long-distance Bus Station. Both stations provide services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Changshu by train. Changshu Railway Station provides services to major cities in China, including Shanghai and Beijing.

How to Get Around in Changshu

Changshu has a well-connected network of buses, taxis, and trains. Buses and taxis are the most convenient modes of transportation for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Changshu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Changshu is conveniently located near other popular tourist destinations in Jiangsu Province. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Suzhou – a beautiful city famous for its canals and gardens.
  2. Wuxi – a bustling city with a fascinating history and beautiful landscapes.
  3. Nanjing – a city that played an integral part in Chinese history.

Conclusion

Changshu is a hidden gem in Jiangsu Province, offering visitors an unforgettable experience. From stunning natural landscapes to unique local cuisine, Changshu has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your next trip to Changshu.
