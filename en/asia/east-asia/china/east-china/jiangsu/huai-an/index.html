Introduction to Huai’an

Huai’an is a prefecture-level city in eastern China, located in the Jiangsu province. The city is known for its rich culture, beautiful natural scenery, and tourist attractions. Huai’an boasts of over 7000 years of history and has been the birthplace of many notable scholars and cultural figures in China. Today, Huai’an is a popular tourist destination for both domestic and international travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Huai’an

Huai’an has a lot to offer for tourists. From historical landmarks to beautiful natural scenery, there are plenty of places to visit in Huai’an. Here are some top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Huai’an Chuxiu Zhonghua Cultural Museum: A museum that explores the history of Huai’an and the cultural heritage of China.
  • Huai’an Yansi Temple: An ancient temple that has been restored and is home to several historic relics.
  • Zhou Enlai’s Memorial Hall: A shrine that honors Comrade Zhou Enlai, a revolutionary and statesman in China.
  • Jinshan Temple: A temple that was first built during the Tang Dynasty and is now a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Huai’an: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hongze Lake: A freshwater lake that is the fourth-largest in China.
  • Xuyi’s Luoma Lake: A scenic area that is home to natural caves and stunning scenery.
  • Huai River: The longest river in Jiangsu that flows through Huai’an and offers boat tours.
  • Jinshan Hill Scenic Area: A beautiful place with stunning views of the city and its mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huai’an

Huai’an has a range of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the city and its surroundings. Here are some popular routes:

  • Xuyi’s Huaishan National Forest Park: A national park that’s popular for hiking, trekking and camping.
  • Huai’an Hexia Ancient Canal Scenic Spot: A scenic spot that spans across 12 towns and offers a range of biking trails.
  • Qinshan Hill Scenic Area: A popular spot for hiking that’s known for its beautiful scenery and Shuiwu Square.

Best Things to Do in Huai’an: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Huai’an. Here are some top options:

  • The Dragon Boat Festival: A traditional festival that is hosted annually in Huai’an in celebration of the Dragon Boat Race.
  • The Huai Opera: A local art form unique to Huai’an that features traditional music and dance performances.
  • Huai’an Park: A popular park that is perfect for taking a stroll or a picnic relaxing afternoon.
  • Watch local artisans create pottery at Fuqiao Kiln.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huai’an

If you have a week to spend in Huai’an, you can cover all the top attractions while indulging in local culture and cuisine. Here’s how:

Day 1: Visit the Huai’an Chuxiu Zhonghua Cultural Museum and explore the city’s ancient history.

Day 2: Head to Hongze Lake and take a boat tour or relax by the lake, enjoying the fresh air and nature around you.

Day 3: Visit the Yansi Temple and Jinshan Temple, both of which are key landmarks in the city.

Day 4: Experience some of the area’s outdoor beauty and hiking opportunities, visiting either Xuyi’s Huaishan National Park or Qinshan Hill Scenic Area.

Day 5: Spend a leisurely day experiencing the local park culture at the Huai’an Park, taking time to take in some of the performers and artists.

Day 6: Explore the local ceramics scene by visiting Fuqiao Kiln and observing artisans create traditional pottery.

Day 7: Finish off your week with a night out in Huai Opera or take a cooking class and learn how to make your favorite Huai’an soups!

Food and Drink Scene of Huai’an

Huai’an is known for its rich cuisine, which features a wide range of flavors and dishes. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Qingjiang Fish: A signature dish of Huai’an that is made with fresh, local ingredients.
  • Tianmu Lake Crab: A delicious seafood dish made with locally caught crabs.
  • Huaiyang Cuisine: Famously cooked with fresh ingredients and health-giving ingredients creating complex flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Huai’an Taishan Beer: A local beer that is brewed and served exclusively in Huai’an.
  • Huaiyang Wines: A popular local product made with fermented rice, usually paired with Huai’an’s rich cuisine.

Where to Stay in Huai’an: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Huai’an, catering to all budgets and preferences. Here’s a list of must-consider places to stay:

  • Crowne Plaza Huai’an: A prominent hotel that offers great amenities, including tennis courts and a swimming pool.
  • Vienna Hotel: A mid-range hotel that offers modern amenities, including a restaurant and fitness center.
  • Yujie Hostel: A budget-friendly option that provides comfortable accommodation for those on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Huai’an: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huai’an is known for its rich cultural heritage, and there are plenty of local products to bring back as souvenirs. Here are some options:

  • Huaihe Rice Wine: A popular type of rice wine that is brewed and sold exclusively in the city of Huai’an.
  • Chinese Knot: Traditional Chinese Knotted handicrafts is popular in Huai’an, they make great souvenirs.
  • Nantong Silk: Luxurious and high-quality silk products, including bedding, scarves, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Huai’an

The city of Huai’an has a rich cultural heritage that has been passed down for centuries. Some customs to explore and engage with while you’re there:

  • Huaiyang Cuisine: Experience the rich, diverse flavors of Huaiyang cooking, which draws from varied culinary traditions in the region.
  • The Huai Opera: An artistic performance exclusive to the Huai’an region that has been performed for over a hundred years.
  • Huai Opera mask: Huai Opera style masks are popular regional souvenirs.

Best Time to Visit Huai’an

The best time to visit Huai’an is in the spring or fall seasons, when temperatures are mild and the city offers the best scenery. The summer and winter seasons can be uncomfortably hot and cold respectively, so it’s best to avoid these times of the year when travelling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huai’an

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of Huai’an, which include taking care during local occasions and avoiding religious issues.
  • Take care not to litter, and keep the city streets clean and tidy.
  • Try the city’s local cuisine and explore its diverse and unique flavors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect locals when exploring temples and other cultural landmarks.
  • Don’t take the city’s culture and traditions lightly, instead, respect and integrate with the local community during your stay.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huai’an

Huai’an is generally a safe city. That said, it’s always a good idea to take precautions when travelling in unfamiliar places, particulary when it comes to your personal belongings. Keep your valuable safe when exploring crowded places.

Health in Huai’an

China is home to unique pathogens, and it is critical tourists familiarize themselves with appropriate vaccinations and precautions before visiting. With that said, Huai’an is relatively low-risk for diseases, so taking general health precautions should suffice.

Getting to Huai’an: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are plenty of transportation options for getting to and around Huai’an. Here’s a list of the most convenient and reliable transport options:

Airports Near Huai’an

The nearest airport is Huai’an Lianshui Airport, which connects Huai’an to other cities in China such as Shanghai, Beijing, and Guangzhou. It is located roughly 37.3km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Huai’an

Huai’an has several bus stations that offer services to neighbouring cities and towns. The main bus station is located in the city’s downtown area.

Other Options

There are ample car rental options available at the airport and in the city center. Taxis are also widely available, so getting around should be easy, even for those who are new to the area.

How to Get Around in Huai’an

Getting around Huai’an is pretty straight forward. Public buses and taxis are available and affordable, and you can also rent cars from local car rental companies. Walking, biking, or hiking can also be a great way to take in the scenery and explore the city’s natural beauty at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Huai’an: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Huai’an is situated in eastern China and is surrounded by several interesting and culturally rich cities. Here are some nearby destinations to explore and excursion:

  • Nantong: A city known for it’s great silk, and historic relics of ancient history.
  • Suzhou: A stunning city, rich in canals, gardens and historic architecture.
  • Yangzhou: Another popular tourist destination known for it’s rich history, it’s convenient location, and the popular Slender West Lake.

Conclusion

Huai’an is a great city to visit in China, offering a perfect mix of culture, natural beauty, and accessible amenities. With so much to offer, there is certainly something for everyone in Huai’an making it an excellent spot for a city break or a longer trip. The city is rich in history and culture, offering the perfect opportunity to explore and learn something new. We hope you enjoy your trip to Huai’an!
