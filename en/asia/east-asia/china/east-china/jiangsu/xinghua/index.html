Introduction to Xinghua

Xinghua, located in the northern part of Jiangsu province, China, is a hidden gem awaiting to be discovered. Known as the “making place of vinegar and garlic,” the city has a unique food culture that attracts many visitors.

Apart from its culinary reputation, Xinghua is known for its well-preserved ancient architecture and picturesque natural scenery. The city has a deep cultural heritage and is the birthplace of many famous literary figures.

List of Places to Visit in Xinghua

Whether you’re here for a day or a week, these places should be on top of your list when visiting Xinghua:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lingyan Mountain scenic area - Home to Lingyansi Temple and the renowned Taohua Island, the Lingyan Mountain Scenic Area is a must-visit. Be prepared to witness some of the best views of Xinghua’s natural beauty.
  • Xinghua Ancient Town - Once one of the most prosperous commercial centers in the region, this 800-year-old town boasts of ancient waterways, temples, and architectural sites.
  • Xinhua Confucian Temple - Built-in the mid-Ming dynasty, the Xinhua Confucian temple and has stood as an important cultural center for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Xinghua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xinghua National Forest Park - Spanning across 70,000 acres, Xinghua National Forest Park is a nature lover’s paradise. Here you’ll find breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, waterfalls, and more.
  • Tianmu Lake - The largest freshwater lake in Jiangsu, Tiannmu Lake offers visitors a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. There are plenty of water activities, natural scenery, and cultural experiences here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xinghua

One of the best ways to explore Xinghua’s natural beauty is on foot or bike, and there are plenty of hiking or biking routes to discover. Some of the best include:

  • Lingyan Mountain - The Lingyan shan Trail is a relatively easy hike, and the views of the surrounding mountains are worth the sweat.
  • Tianmu Lake - The cycling routes near Tiannmu Lake are well-maintained and offer a mix of natural scenery and cultural experiences.

Best Things to Do in Xinghua: Activities and Tours

  • Try the Local Specialty Foods - Xinghua is famous for its vinegar and garlic-based dishes, and there are plenty of street stalls and restaurants to indulge in.
  • Explore Local Vinegar Workshops - Discover the traditional methods used to make Xinghua’s famous vinegar.
  • Take a Boat Ride on Taohua Island - Get up close and personal with the beauty of Lingyansi Temple and nearby mountains by taking a boat ride on Taohua Island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xinghua

Day 1: Xinghua Ancient Town, Xinhua Confucian Temple

Day 2: Lingyan Mountain Scenic Area, Taohua Island

Day 3: Xinghua National Forest Park

Day 4: Tianmu Lake

Day 5: Explore Vinegar Workshops, Try Local Food

Day 6: Hike Lingyan Mountain

Day 7: Relax and Explore on Bicycles

Food and Drink Scene of Xinghua

Must-Try Dishes

  • Xinghua Preserved Pork - A local delicacy that can be enjoyed both fresh and dried. It is usually eaten thinly sliced and served with garlic.
  • Braised Fatty Pork - Another local dish served with Xinghua vinegar, garlic, and soy sauce.
  • Fried Dough Sticks Soaked in Vinegar - A savory breakfast food popular among locals.

Local Beverages

  • Xinghua Vinegar - Made from glutinous rice, the vinegar is aged in earthenware jars for years, giving it a sweet and sour taste.
  • Black Tea - Xinghua produces high-quality black tea that is popular among tea-lovers.

Where to Stay in Xinghua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Xinghua Tianmu Lake Merry Manor Hotel - A beautiful hotel located near Tianmu Lake that oﬀers stunning views.
  • Xinghua Hantian City Hotel - A 3-star hotel located near Lingyan Mountain Scenic area.
  • Tianmu Yicheng Holiday Hotel - A 4-star hotel with great amenities near Xinghua National Forest Park.

What to Buy in Xinghua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Xinghua Vinegar - A jar of Xinghua vinegar makes a great souvenir for yourself or a loved one.
  • Preserved Pork - Xinghua Preserved Pork can be easily packed and transported, making it the perfect souvenir for meat lovers.
  • Black Tea - Xinghua is known for its black tea, and a package of high-quality tea leaves is a great souvenir for tea enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Xinghua

Xinghua holds a deep cultural heritage, and it is worth learning about some of the customs and traditions that shape its identity:

  • Folk Art - Xinghua is famous for its folk art, particularly paper-cutting and embroidery.
  • Temple Fairs - Temple fairs are an important part of Xinghua’s customs, and they are held throughout the year.
  • Respect for the Elderly - Respect for the elderly is an integral part of Xinghua’s culture, and visitors are expected to acknowledge this, too.

Best Time to Visit Xinghua

The best time to visit Xinghua is from April to November when the weather is relatively warm, with comfortable temperatures around 20-25°C. During this time, the natural scenery is at its best, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to participate in.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xinghua

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local specialty foods, including vinegar and garlic dishes
  • Explore the beautiful natural scenery, especially Lingyan Mountain and Tianmu Lake

Don’t:

  • Touch ancient relics or damage the natural environment
  • Speak loudly or excessively in public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Xinghua

While Xinghua is generally considered safe, visitors should take necessary precautions, particularly while exploring the natural scenery, as some areas may have steep trails and are prone to landslides or flooding.

Health in Xinghua

Tap water in Xinghua is not drinkable, so it’s essential to carry bottled water. Visitors are also advised to take precautions against mosquitos and other insects and to carry sunscreen and other necessary protections against the sun.

Getting to Xinghua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xinghua

  • Sunan Shuofang International Airport (WUX) - Located 156 km away, Sunan Shuofang is the nearest airport to Xinghua.

Bus Stations Near Xinghua

  • Xinghua Bus Station - Xinghua bus station is located in the center of the city and provides multiple bus connections to nearby cities and towns, including Shanghai, Nanjing, and Hangzhou.

Other Options

  • Taxi Service - Taxis are readily available within the city and can be used for short distances.
  • Bike Rental - Visitors can rent bikes to explore Xinghua and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Xinghua

Public transportation, such as buses and taxis, is readily available within Xinghua. Bicycles are also available for rent and are an excellent way to explore the city’s natural scenery on one’s own

Where to Go Next from Xinghua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yangzhou - A historical city known for its beautiful gardens and ancient architecture is located 78 km away and is a popular nearby destination.
  • Nanjing - Located 120 km away, the grand capital of Jiangsu province is full of historical landmarks, museums, and a thriving food and entertainment scene.

Conclusion

Regardless of your interests, Xinghua has something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and unique culinary culture, visitors will undoubtedly appreciate the city’s charm and beauty. Make sure to indulge in the local food, explore the historical landmarks, and appreciate the city’s local customs and traditions while visiting.
