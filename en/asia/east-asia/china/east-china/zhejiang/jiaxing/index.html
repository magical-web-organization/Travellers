Introduction to Jiaxing

Jiaxing is a city located in the northeast of Zhejiang Province in China. It is a crucial transportation hub between Shanghai and Hangzhou, making it a popular stop for travelers. Jiaxing is surrounded by water and boasts beautiful scenery, a rich history, and a thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities or exploring the local culture, Jiaxing has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Jiaxing

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Xitang Ancient Town: This charming town is known for its well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture, including ancient bridges and canals. It’s a great place for a leisurely stroll and to take in the local culture.

  • Qiuyue Academy: This 16th-century academy was established during the Ming dynasty and is an excellent example of traditional Chinese architecture. It boasts beautiful gardens, intricate carvings, and a rich history.

  • South Lake: This scenic lake is known for its beautiful gardens and historic buildings, including the former residence of Mao Dun, a famous Chinese writer. It’s an excellent place to relax and enjoy some time in nature.

Natural Attractions of Jiaxing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wuzhen Water Town: This well-preserved water town is famous for its ancient architecture and beautiful canals. Take a boat ride to explore the town from a different perspective, enjoy some local food, and shop for souvenirs.

  • Jiaxing South Lake Scenic Area: This area boasts beautiful scenery with ancient temples, gardens, and picturesque lakes. Take a boat ride, go for a walk or hike and get some stunning photos.

  • Nanbei Lake Park: This park is a great place to take a break from the city and enjoy some fresh air. It’s a popular spot for fishing, boating, picnicking, and appreciating the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiaxing

  • Jiaxing South Lake Scenic Area: There are several hiking trails around South Lake with views of the lake and historic buildings. It’s a great opportunity to get some exercise and explore the area at the same time.

  • Wuzhen Water Town: Rent a bike to explore this charming water town at your own pace. You’ll discover hidden alleys and ancient buildings that you might miss by walking.

Best Things to Do in Jiaxing: Activities and Tours

  • Tasting the Local Cuisine: The food scene in Jiaxing is diverse and impressive. Try some traditional dishes like Jiaxing dumplings, South Lake fish, and sweet Osmanthus cakes.

  • Visit a Tea Plantation: Jiaxing is well-known for its tea, and there are several tea plantations around the city. Tour one of the tea farms and learn about the process of growing tea, from harvest to brewing and tasting.

  • Join a Calligraphy Class: Calligraphy is an important art form in Chinese culture. Join a class to learn the basics of calligraphy and create a unique souvenir to take home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jiaxing

Day 1: Explore Xitang Ancient Town, including its bridges and canals

Day 2: Visit the Qiyue Academy and Nanbei Lake Park for a picnic

Day 3: Boat ride and hike around South Lake

Day 4: Rent a bike and cycle around Wuzhen Water Town

Day 5: Visit a tea plantation in Jiaxing and taste different types of tea

Day 6: Calligraphy class and souvenir shopping

Day 7: Visit the art museum and enjoy the local cultural scene

Food and Drink Scene of Jiaxing

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiaxing Dumplings: These are small dumplings filled with pork and shrimp, served with a savory sauce.

  • South Lake Fish: The fish is cooked in a traditional style with soy sauce and green onion. It’s a local specialty and a must-try when visiting Jiaxing.

  • Osmanthus Cake: These small cakes are made with Osmanthus flowers and glutinous rice flour, and have a sweet taste and light floral fragrance.

Local Beverages

  • Jiaxing Rice Wine: This wine is produced locally and has a long history in the area. It’s made with sticky rice and has a unique and pleasant taste.

  • Longjing Tea: Jiaxing is also known for its Longjing tea, which is a high-quality, fragrant, and subtle tea.

Where to Stay in Jiaxing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Doubletree by Hilton Jiaxing: This hotel is located in the city center and offers modern amenities and excellent service.

  • Jiaxing Sunshine Hotel: This hotel has a great location by South Lake and offers beautiful lake views.

  • Qingtong Guesthouse: This traditional guesthouse is located in Xitang Ancient Town and has a charming atmosphere with Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

What to Buy in Jiaxing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jiaxing Rice Wine: Pick up a bottle or two of this locally brewed wine to take home as a unique and authentic souvenir.

  • Tea: With many tea farms in the area, Jiaxing is an excellent place to pick up some high-quality tea to enjoy at home.

  • Cloisonne: This is a traditional Chinese enamelware that’s often used for decorative purposes. There are many local artisans in Jiaxing who make beautiful cloisonné pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiaxing

  • Respect for the Elderly: In Chinese culture, it’s essential to show respect for the elderly. Address them by their respectful titles and show politeness in your interactions.

  • Customs and Etiquette: Taking your shoes off before entering a home or temple is customary in Chinese culture. Also, it’s respectful to offer a small gift or token of appreciation to your host.

Best Time to Visit Jiaxing

The best time to visit Jiaxing is during the spring (March - May) and autumn (September - November) when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. However, Jiaxing is generally pleasant to visit all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiaxing

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Jiaxing has a diverse food scene, so be sure to try some traditional dishes and local specialties.

  • Practice Basic Mandarin Phrases: Many locals in Jiaxing do not speak English, so it’s helpful to know a few basic Mandarin phrases to communicate.

Don’t:

  • Offend the Local Customs: Show respect for local customs, such as taking off your shoes inside homes or temples.

  • Over-Tip: Tipping is not customary in China, and giving too much money can be seen as impolite or disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jiaxing

Jiaxing is a safe city, and visitors generally don’t experience any serious safety issues. However, it’s still important to practice basic safety precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Jiaxing

It’s crucial to take precautions to prevent heatstroke and sunstroke during the summer months. It’s best to stay hydrated and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun during peak hours. Drinking bottled water is also recommended to avoid any stomach issues.

Getting to Jiaxing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jiaxing

  • Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport: This airport is approximately 65 kilometers away from Jiaxing and offers domestic and international flights.

  • Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport: This airport is approximately 110 kilometers away from Jiaxing and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Jiaxing

  • Jiaxing Bus Station: This station is located in the city center and offers buses to neighboring cities and towns.

  • Jiaxing South Bus Station: This station is located near South Lake and offers buses to destinations in and around Zhejiang Province.

Other Options

  • Train: Jiaxing has a high-speed train station that connects to Shanghai and Hangzhou.

  • Taxi/Car Rental: Taxis are readily available in Jiaxing, and car rentals are also an option for visitors who want to explore the city on their own.

How to Get Around in Jiaxing

Public transportation, such as buses and trains, is a convenient and affordable way to get around Jiaxing. Taxis are also readily available, although it’s essential to make sure the driver uses the meter. Renting a bicycle is also an excellent way to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Jiaxing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hangzhou: This nearby city is famous for its West Lake, beautiful scenery, and culinary delights.

  • Shanghai: This is the largest city in China and boasts an impressive skyline, rich history, and fantastic shopping and dining options.

Conclusion

Jiaxing is a charming and beautiful city that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich culture and history of China. Whether you’re interested in exploring the natural scenery, trying the local food, or learning about the local culture, Jiaxing has something to offer. With convenient transportation options, excellent accommodations, and friendly locals, Jiaxing is definitely worth a visit.
