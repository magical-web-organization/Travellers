Introduction to Anji

Anji is a stunning town located in the northern part of Huzhou, in Zhejiang Province, East China. Known for its towering bamboo forests, peaceful tea plantations, and adventure activities, Anji is a nature lover’s paradise. It is a perfect destination to escape the hustle and bustle of the cities and spend some quality time amidst the serene surroundings. In this travel guide, we have curated a list of the best places to visit, things to do, and tips to plan your dream trip to Anji.

List of Places to Visit in Anji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit cultural attractions in Anji is the Zhejiang Anji Bamboo Museum. It is home to over 400 species of bamboo and offers a fascinating insight into the history and cultural significance of bamboo in china. Another place to explore the local history is the Lingfeng Temple, a well-preserved ancient Buddhist temple that dates back to the Southern Song Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Anji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Anji boasts a variety of natural attractions that make it a popular destination. Some of the top places to visit include Tianhuangping Scenic Area, where you can enjoy walking trails, scenic waterfalls, and rock formations. Another popular destination is the Star Film Town, where numerous Chinese films and TV dramas have been shot. The stunning bamboo forests here are the perfect place to hike, bike or take a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anji

Anji’s scenic trails and paths are ideal for hikers and bikers of all levels. One of the most popular hikes is the Anji Bamboo Forest Hiking Trail, a 5-km-long trail that winds its way through the tranquil bamboo groves. Another popular route to explore the area is the Jiangnan Tianchi Scenic Hiking Trail, which leads to a sparkling lake amid the lush green mountains.

Best Things to Do in Anji: Activities and Tours

Apart from the scenic beauty, Anji offers a range of activities to indulge in, such as bamboo rafting, mountain climbing, and hot air ballooning. You can also visit the tea plantations and indulge in a tea-picking activity or try your hand at making your tea. Additionally, there are some excellent guided tours available that offer a unique insight into the local culture and lifestyle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anji

A week in Anji would offer ample time to explore the town and its surroundings. Here is an example itinerary to help plan your visit:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a hotel
  • Day 2: Visit Tianhuangping Scenic Area and Lingfeng Temple
  • Day 3: Explore the bamboo forests and take a bamboo rafting tour on the river
  • Day 4: Visit the tea plantations and take a tea-picking activity
  • Day 5: Go hiking to Jiangnan Tianchi Lake
  • Day 6: Enjoy adventure activities like rock climbing or hot air ballooning
  • Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Anji

Must-Try Dishes

Anji cuisine is known for its use of fresh ingredients and unique taste. Some of the must-try dishes include the Anji Stewed Bamboo Shoots, Anji White Tea, and the Tofu Soup with Bamboo Shoots. The area is also known for its quality fruit, such as persimmons, oranges, and kiwis, which make an excellent dessert.

Local Beverages

Anji is particularly famous for Anji White Tea, one of the top ten Chinese teas. The climate and soil of Anji are ideal for growing tea, which results in a unique taste and aroma. Visitors can also try “Bamboo Wine,” a unique wine infused with bamboo shoots and other Chinese herbal ingredients.

Where to Stay in Anji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation options in Anji vary from luxury hotels to budget guesthouses. Luxury options include the JW Marriott Hotel Zhejiang Anji and the Anji Kaishe Boutique Hotel, which provide elegant rooms and upscale amenities. Budget options include the Anji Love Greeting Inn and the Anji Countryside Guesthouse, which offer cozy rooms and a more rustic experience.

What to Buy in Anji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take back a piece of Anji with you, look for the handmade bamboo crafts, including bamboo baskets, chopsticks, and fans. You can also buy Anji White Tea and Bamboo Wine to enjoy back at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Anji

Anji has a rich cultural heritage that has been preserved for centuries. Visitors can witness the locals’ everyday life in the tea plantations and bamboo forests. The area is also known for its traditional crafts, including bamboo weaving and tea processing.

Best Time to Visit Anji

The best time to visit Anji is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is pleasant, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can experience occasional snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anji

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and tea
  • Carry insect repellent while hiking or visiting natural areas

Don’t:

  • Touch or damage the bamboo forests or tea plantations
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Anji

Anji is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s essential to take basic precautions such as safeguarding your valuables and not wandering into unknown areas at night.

Health in Anji

Travelers should take standard precautions to avoid common illnesses such as stomach bugs and colds. Visitors should also be cautious while hiking or doing adventure activities to avoid injuries.

Getting to Anji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anji

The nearest airport to Anji is Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport, which is about 90 km away. From there, travelers can take a taxi or bus to Anji.

Bus Stations Near Anji

Anji has several bus stations connecting to cities like Huzhou, Shanghai, and Hangzhou. The Anji Long-distance Bus Station and Anji North Bus Station are two of the most commonly used bus stations.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car or hiring a private driver to explore the region more efficiently.

How to Get Around in Anji

Travelers can get around in Anji using taxis, public buses, or renting a bike. We recommend renting a bike to explore the scenic trails and pathways.

Where to Go Next from Anji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anji’s location makes it an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations. Some of the popular places to visit include Moganshan, known for its stunning mountain scenery and hot springs, and the charming water town of Wuzhen, known for its well-preserved ancient architecture and canal system.

Conclusion

Anji is a hidden gem in East China that offers a unique and unforgettable experience. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or a glimpse into Chinese culture, Anji has it all. With our comprehensive travel guide, we hope you can plan your dream trip and make the most of your time in Anji.
