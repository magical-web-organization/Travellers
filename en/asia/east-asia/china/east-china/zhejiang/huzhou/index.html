Introduction to Huzhou

Located in the Zhejiang province of East China, Huzhou is a city that boasts of a rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and historical significance. Huzhou is home to several picturesque water towns, ancient temples, and stunning natural landscapes. The city’s strategic location, excellent transport links, and welcoming locals make it an ideal destination for both domestic and international tourists.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Huzhou.

List of Places to Visit in Huzhou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Feiying Pavilion

Located on the top of Jingshan Hill, the Feiying Pavilion is one of Huzhou’s most iconic landmarks. The Pavilion’s unique design, coupled with its panoramic views of the Yangtze River, makes it a popular spot for photography enthusiasts.

Nanxun Ancient Town

Nanxun is one of the famous ancient water towns in China, and it is an exemplary representation of the southern Chinese architectural style. The canal flowing through the town adds to its charm, and visitors can soak in the peaceful environment while exploring the town’s various attractions.

Natural Attractions of Huzhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mogan Mountain Scenic Area

Mogan Mountain is one of Huzhou’s most popular natural attractions. With its lush forests, clear streams, and magnificent mountain trails, it’s an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the mountain on foot or bike, and its breathtaking views are not to be missed.

Lotus Park

Located in the heart of Huzhou, the Lotus Park is a serene oasis amidst the hustle and bustle of the city. The park’s main attraction is the stunning lake filled with lotus flowers, and visitors can rent a boat to explore the lake or simply relax on one of the many benches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huzhou

Hiking Trails

Huzhou’s natural landscapes and scenic beauty make it an exciting destination for hiking enthusiasts. The best hiking trails in Huzhou are located in the Mogan Mountain Scenic Area, where visitors can explore winding valleys, waterfalls, and stunning mountain landscapes.

Biking Routes

Huzhou has several biking routes suitable for all levels of bikers. The city’s flat terrain and excellent bike trails make it an ideal destination for leisurely bike rides. One of the most popular biking routes is the Yangtze River Greenway, which offers stunning views of the river and its surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Huzhou: Activities and Tours

Explore the Ancient Water Towns

Huzhou is home to several ancient water towns, and they provide a fascinating glimpse into the city’s rich history and culture. Visitors can explore the narrow streets, ancient temples, and traditional Chinese architecture while soaking in the tranquil environment.

Visit the Zhebei Grand Canyon

The Zhebei Grand Canyon is one of Huzhou’s best-kept secrets. Visitors can hike along the winding canyon trail, explore the unique rock formations, and soak in the stunning scenery. The canyon also offers several adrenaline-pumping activities like bungee jumping, zip lining, and rock climbing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huzhou

Food and Drink Scene of Huzhou

Must-Try Dishes

Huzhou Fried Rice

Huzhou Fried Rice is a delicious dish made with fluffy rice, soy sauce, and a variety of meats and vegetables. It’s a popular dish in the region and can be found in most local restaurants.

Niangao

Niangao is a traditional Chinese cake made with glutinous rice flour and brown sugar. It’s a popular snack in Huzhou and can be found in most local bakeries.

Local Beverages

Longjing Tea

Longjing tea, also known as Dragon Well tea, is a high-quality green tea grown in Huzhou. It’s a popular local beverage and can be found in most restaurants and cafes.

Baijiu

Baijiu is a traditional Chinese rice wine that’s popular in Huzhou. It’s a strong alcoholic beverage and is often served at special occasions and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Huzhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sheraton Huzhou Hot Spring Resort

The Sheraton Huzhou Hot Spring Resort is a luxurious five-star resort that offers stunning views of Lake Taihu. It features an outdoor hot spring pool, a spa, and several dining options.

Huzhou International Hotel

The Huzhou International Hotel is a comfortable, centrally located hotel that offers excellent service and comfortable rooms. It’s an ideal choice for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Huzhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silk Products

Huzhou is known for its silk production, and visitors can purchase a variety of silk products like scarfs, bags, and clothing.

Mogan Mountain Tea

Mogan Mountain tea is a high-quality green tea that’s grown in the region. Visitors can purchase the tea at local markets and souvenir shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Huzhou

Huzhou has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. It’s customary to greet people with a simple “Ni hao” (hello), and visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Tipping is not a common practice in China, and visitors should always negotiate prices when shopping at local markets.

Best Time to Visit Huzhou

The best time to visit Huzhou is during the spring and autumn months (March to May and September to November), when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best. Summers in Huzhou can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huzhou

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Huzhou Fried Rice and Niangao.
  • Explore the ancient water towns and scenic landscapes.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink tap water.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Enter religious sites without proper attire.
  • Take photographs without seeking permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huzhou

Huzhou is a safe city, and visitors should take normal precautions to avoid petty theft and scams. It’s advisable to keep valuables in a safe place and avoid travelling alone at night.

Health in Huzhou

Visitors should seek medical advice before travelling to Huzhou and ensure they have all recommended vaccinations. It’s advisable to drink bottled water, avoid raw vegetables and fruits, and maintain good hygiene practices.

Getting to Huzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huzhou

The nearest airports to Huzhou are the Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport and the Shanghai Pudong International Airport. Both airports offer several connecting flights to domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Huzhou

Huzhou has two major bus stations - The Huzhou North Bus Station and The Huzhou Central Bus Station. Both stations offer several bus routes to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Huzhou is well connected to other parts of China by train and road. Visitors can take a train to Huzhou Railway Station or reach the city by bus or car.

How to Get Around in Huzhou

Huzhou has an excellent public transport system, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent a bike or e-scooter to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Huzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hangzhou

Hangzhou is one of the most beautiful cities in China and is situated just a short distance from Huzhou. Visitors can explore its stunning West Lake, ancient temples, and beautiful gardens.

Wuxi

Wuxi is a picturesque city located in Jiangsu Province and is known for its beautiful scenery and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore its many ancient temples and gardens, and enjoy its famous local cuisine.

Conclusion

Huzhou is a beautiful city that offers something for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring its history and culture, or just want to relax amidst stunning natural landscapes, Huzhou has it all. Follow this comprehensive travel guide, and you’ll be sure to have an unforgettable experience.
