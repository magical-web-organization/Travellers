Introduction to Nanxun

Nestled in the scenic district of Huzhou, Zhejiang province in China, lies the charming water town of Nanxun. Known for its historic landmarks, ancient architecture, and breathtaking scenery, Nanxun is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored.

This little town boasts a rich cultural heritage, dating back to the Ming dynasty. In addition to its historical significance, Nanxun has also earned itself a spot on the UNESCO World Heritage list due to its well-preserved cultural sites.

With its tranquil rivers and winding canals, Nanxun is a picturesque destination that attracts visitors from all over the world. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, you’ll never run out of things to do in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Nanxun

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Former Residence of Zhang Shiming

The former residence of Zhang Shiming is one of the most significant historical landmarks in Nanxun. Built during the Qing dynasty, this impressive mansion features remarkable architecture and intricate carvings. This residence was home to Zhang Shiming, a prominent merchant during the Ming and Qing dynasties.

2. Baijianlou

Baijianlou, a five-storey pagoda built during the Song dynasty, is an essential cultural landmark in Nanxun and a testament to the ancient craftsmanship. The pagoda is situated on top of a hill and offers visitors panoramic views of the entire town.

3. Little Lotus Garden

Little Lotus Garden is a beautiful garden located on the outskirts of Nanxun. It is a classical Chinese garden, featuring elegant pavilions, rock formations, and koi ponds. The garden is a great spot for a leisurely walk or a picnic with friends and family.

Natural Attractions of Nanxun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Taoist Temple Scenic Area

The Taoist Temple Scenic Area is located in the heart of Nanxun and is home to many historical attractions. The scenic area is a lush, green space that boasts ancient architecture, including temples, pavilions, and pagodas. It’s the perfect spot to escape the hustle and bustle of the town and enjoy the scenic beauty.

2. Nanxun Ancient Town

Nanxun Ancient Town is a quaint little town with narrow streets and alleyways that wind between buildings constructed during the Qing dynasty. Visitors can explore this town on foot or by boat and admire the unique architecture and remarkable ancient buildings.

3. Wuzhen Water Town

Wuzhen Water Town is a nearby town located about 12 kilometers away from Nanxun. It is another beautiful water town in the area, with winding canals, cobblestoned alleys, and traditional architecture. Visitors can take a boat ride or explore the town to experience the unique charm of Wuzhen.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanxun

1. Xishan Park

Located on top of Xishan Mountain, this park overlooks the entire Nanxun town. Visitors can hike up the mountain or ride a cable car to reach the top. The park offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape, including lush greenery and the winding river.

2. Cycle around the town

One of the best ways to experience Nanxun is by cycling around the town. Visitors can rent bicycles and ride along the narrow streets and alleys, admiring the ancient buildings and beautiful scenery along the way.

Best Things to Do in Nanxun: Activities and Tours

Zhujiajiao Water Town Tour

Located a short distance away from Nanxun, the Zhujiajiao Water Town Tour offers visitors the chance to explore another nearby beautiful water town. The tour includes a boat ride through the canals, a visit to ancient buildings, and exploring the local markets.

Chinese Silk Culture Tour

Experience the rich Chinese silk culture by touring the silk factories located around Nanxun. These factories produce some of the finest silk in China, and visitors can witness the intricate process of silk production and learn about the history and cultural significance of silk in China.

Food and Drink Scene of Nanxun

Must-Try Dishes

Nanxun is a particularly famous place for its rice wine and sweet pork preserves. Local cuisine utilizes the Zhejiang cuisine cooking style and emphasizes the taste and color of the dishes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Sweet Pork Preserves
  • Rice Wine Chicken
  • Dongpo Pork
  • Rose Bean Cake

Local Beverages

Nanxun is famous for its rice wine, which is brewed using the traditional methods of the town. Visitors can enjoy local rice wine at many of the restaurants in town.

Where to Stay in Nanxun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nanxun has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some of the popular hotels include:

  • Nanxun Blossom Hill Boutique Hotel
  • Fisherman’s House
  • Placid Waters Qingxin Villa

There are also many guesthouses and youth hostels available for budget travelers, offering affordable and comfortable accommodation in the heart of Nanxun.

What to Buy in Nanxun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rice Wine

Nanxun is known for its locally brewed rice wine, a must-try for any visitor to the town. Visitors can purchase bottles of rice wine to enjoy back home or give as gifts to friends and family.

Silk Products

Silk is one of the most significant cultural products in China, and visitors to Nanxun can purchase a range of beautiful silk products, including scarves, clothing, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Nanxun

Nanxun has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore the unique customs and traditions of the town by attending local festivals and events. Some of the significant festivals include the Lantern Festival and the Water Festival.

Best Time to Visit Nanxun

The best time to visit Nanxun is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. The town is particularly beautiful during the spring months when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanxun

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Visit the historical landmarks and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Damage or disturb historical sites and ancient buildings.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanxun

Nanxun is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any tourist destination, such as safeguarding valuables and staying alert in crowded areas.

Health in Nanxun

Nanxun has a relatively clean environment, and visitors are unlikely to experience any major health problems. However, it is always advisable to drink bottled water and take appropriate precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Nanxun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nanxun

The nearest airport to Nanxun is Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport, located about 100 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or shuttle from the airport to Nanxun.

Bus Stations Near Nanxun

Nanxun has a bus station that connects it to other towns and cities in the region. Visitors can take a bus from the Huzhou Bus Terminal to Nanxun.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or book a tour to Nanxun.

How to Get Around in Nanxun

Nanxun is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take a boat ride to explore some of the scenic canals and rivers within the town.

Where to Go Next from Nanxun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hangzhou

Hangzhou is a beautiful city located about 60 kilometers from Nanxun. The city is famous for its stunning West Lake, picturesque parks, and historical landmarks.

Shanghai

Shanghai is a bustling metropolis located about 130 kilometers away from Nanxun. Visitors can explore the city’s modern skyscrapers, traditional landmarks, and world-class shopping and dining.

Conclusion

Nanxun is a hidden gem in the heart of Huzhou, Zhejiang. With its rich cultural heritage, ancient architecture, and stunning natural scenery, it is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a unique and unforgettable experience. From exploring historical landmarks to biking around the town, there is something for everyone in Nanxun. Visit Nanxun today and uncover the hidden gems of this charming water town.
