Introduction to Lin’an

Lin’an, located in the mountains of Zhejiang province, is a small city that offers big surprises. This hidden gem is known for its awe-inspiring natural beauty, rich cultural history, and mouth-watering cuisine. The city is situated on the banks of the Fuchun River, surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests and ancient temples. Lin’an is the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Chinese experience, off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Lin’an

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lin’an Museum

The Lin’an Museum is a must-visit for those interested in Chinese history and culture. The museum showcases artifacts spanning from the Neolithic period to the present day, including pottery, jade, and calligraphy.

Baishi Mountain

Baishi Mountain is situated on the outskirts of Lin’an and is considered one of the most sacred mountains in China. The mountain features many temples, including the famous Baosi Temple, which dates back to the Tang Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Lin’an: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fuchun River Scenic Area

The Fuchun River Scenic Area is a breathtakingly beautiful region that encompasses the Fuchun River and surrounding mountains, hills, and forests. The area is known for its pristine natural scenery and features several hiking, biking, and camping options.

Yunhe Terraced Fields

The Yunhe Terraced Fields are a UNESCO World Heritage Site that spans over 70,000 hectares, featuring breathtaking views of stepped rice paddies and rolling hills. The area is also home to many ancient villages with traditional architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lin’an

There are many hiking and biking routes in Lin’an, including the challenging Baishi Mountains trail and the more leisurely Fuchun River trail. The trails offer scenic views of the city and surrounding countryside and are a great way to experience Lin’an’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Lin’an: Activities and Tours

Rafting on the Fuchun River

Rafting on the Fuchun River is a must-do activity in Lin’an. The rapids offer a thrilling experience, and the scenic views of the surrounding mountains and forests are unforgettable.

Tea Plantation Tour

Lin’an is home to many tea plantations, and a tour of one of these plantations is a great way to experience Chinese tea culture. Visitors can learn about the history and production of tea and even try their hand at picking leaves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lin’an

Day 1 Arrival and exploration of the city center
Day 2 Visit the Lin’an Museum and the historic Baosi Temple
Day 3 Hike the Baishi Mountains Trail and enjoy the breathtaking views
Day 4 Bike along the Fuchun River Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch
Day 5 Take a rafting trip down the Fuchun River
Day 6 Visit a local tea plantation and learn about Chinese tea culture
Day 7 Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Lin’an

Must-Try Dishes

Dongpo Pork

Dongpo Pork is a famous Zhejiang dish that consists of tender pork belly cooked with ginger, soy sauce, and sugar. The dish is named after Su Dongpo, a famous Chinese poet from the Song Dynasty.

Shaoxing Wine

Shaoxing Wine is a Chinese rice wine that originated in the Shaoxing region of Zhejiang province. It is often used in cooking and is also enjoyed as a drink.

Where to Stay in Lin’an: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lin’an has many accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some popular options include the Fuchun Resort, Lin’an Hotel, and Zhejiang Lantian Baiyun Conference Center.

What to Buy in Lin’an: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fuchun River Tea

Fuchun River Tea is a type of green tea produced in the Fuchun River Scenic Area. It is known for its fresh and smooth taste and is a great souvenir to bring home.

Xianghu Lake Pearls

Xianghu Lake Pearls are natural freshwater pearls produced in the Xianghu Lake region of Lin’an. The pearls are known for their high quality and unique colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Lin’an

Lin’an has a rich cultural history and is home to many traditional festivals and celebrations. The city is known for its tea culture, and visitors can learn about the history and production of Chinese tea.

Best Time to Visit Lin’an

The best time to visit Lin’an is from April to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. The city is particularly beautiful in the fall when the trees change color, and the leaves start to fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lin’an

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially Dongpo Pork and Fuchun River Tea.
  • Visit the many temples and museums to learn about Chinese history and culture.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings, including hiking trails and scenic areas.
  • Take photographs of local people without their permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lin’an

Lin’an is a safe and friendly city, but visitors should take normal precautions, such as avoiding dark alleys and carrying valuables discreetly.

Health in Lin’an

Visitors should take precautions regarding water and food hygiene to avoid digestive disorders. It is advisable to drink bottled water and avoid raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Lin’an: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lin’an

The nearest airport to Lin’an is Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport, which is about 50 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Lin’an

There are several bus stations in Lin’an, including the Lin’an Bus Station and the Lin’an Long-distance Bus Station. Buses run regularly to Hangzhou and surrounding cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Hangzhou and then transfer to a bus to Lin’an. Alternatively, private car rentals and taxis are available.

How to Get Around in Lin’an

The best way to get around Lin’an is by bus or taxi. Buses run regularly to all parts of the city, and taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Lin’an: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hangzhou

Hangzhou is a city located about 100 kilometers from Lin’an and is known for its West Lake, temples, and museums.

Huangshan

Huangshan is a famous mountain range located about 400 kilometers from Lin’an and is known for its stunning views and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Lin’an is a hidden gem in East China, offering visitors an authentic Chinese experience with its natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious cuisine. From hiking and biking to rafting and tea tours, there is something for everyone in this charming city. Discover Lin’an for yourself and immerse yourself in the beauty and traditions of China.
