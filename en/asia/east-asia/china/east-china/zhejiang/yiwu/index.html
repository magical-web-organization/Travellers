Yiwu Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Yiwu is a small city located in the Zhejiang Province of China, famous for its large wholesale market that attracts businesses from all over the world. However, Yiwu has much more to offer than just the market. It is a beautiful city with a rich history and culture, and a breath-taking countryside. In this travel guide, we will help you explore the best of Yiwu.

Introduction to Yiwu

Yiwu is a small city with a population of about 1.2 million people. It is located in the eastern part of China, and it is known for its vast trading market that sells everything from electronics to clothing to souvenirs. Despite being a small city, Yiwu is a unique blend of modernity and ancient culture.

List of Places to Visit in Yiwu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yiwu has a rich history, and it’s evident in the numerous historical places and museums dotted around the city. These are some of the significant cultural attractions:

  • Ningbo Hemudu Site Museum: It’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site that houses relics from the Neolithic Age.
  • Chouzhou Park: Located in the middle of the city, the park celebrates Yiwu’s rich history and showcases ancient sculptures.
  • Lingxiu Cave Scenic Area: A beautiful and serene park with stunning natural scenery and cultural history. It’s a must-visit place.

Natural Attractions of Yiwu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yiwu is a city of greenery, and it’s surrounded by beautiful hills, valleys, and forests. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Yiwu Xiuhu Park: The park is located in the city center and offers a tranquil environment with stunning views of its lake.
  • Yiwu River Scenic Area: The scenic area showcases the city’s beautiful waterways with wooden bridges, pagodas, and other traditional Chinese buildings.
  • Yiwu Forest Park: Located in the city’s outskirts, the forest park is a home to a vast variety of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yiwu

Yiwu has numerous biking and hiking routes that give you a chance to explore its natural beauty. Here are the top hiking and biking routes:

  • Yiwu Forest Park: The park has various hiking trails with varying degrees of difficulty. Visiting the park is an excellent activity for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Chouzhou Park: A perfect place to relax and enjoy the beautiful park scenery. A bike ride around the park is an excellent way to unwind and enjoy the views.
  • Yunnan Folk Village: A biking trail that offers you the chance to explore Chinese local culture with beautiful views of Yunnan’s architecture.

Best Things to Do in Yiwu: Activities and Tours

Yiwu offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Whether you’re passionate about history, culture, or nature, there’s something here for you. Here are the top things to do in Yiwu:

  • Visit Yiwu International Trade Market: It’s China’s largest small commodity wholesale market and offers visitors an experience like no other.
  • Experience Local Customs and Culture: Yiwu is famous for its cultural customs, ranging from food to traditional festivals. Visiting during the Lunar New Year or Mid-Autumn Festival will give you a glimpse of these customs.
  • Yiwu Nightlife: Despite being a small city, Yiwu has a vibrant nightlife with various bars and nightclubs. A visit to one of these spots is a great way to unwind after a day of exploring the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Yiwu

Must-Try Dishes

Yiwu cuisine is a unique blend of different styles with its own distinct flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Dongyang Lamb: A famous lamb dish cooked in Dongyang style. It’s aromatic and flavorful.
  • Ningbo Dumplings: Dumplings stuffed with minced pork, shrimp, and crabmeat. A local delicacy.
  • Chouzhou Sesame Cake: A sweet cake made with black sesame seeds, wheat flour, and sugar. It’s crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Local Beverages

Yiwu has a rich tea culture, and it’s famous for its high-quality teas. Here are some of the popular local beverages:

  • Pu’er Tea: A fermented tea that has a smoky flavor and is perfect for souvenirs.
  • Yiwu Green Tea: A high-quality green tea that’s grown in the Yiwu mountain range.

Where to Stay in Yiwu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yiwu has an enormous range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Yiwu Marriott Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the city center, within walking distance of the trade market.
  • Best Western Premier Ocean Hotel: A four-star hotel located in the Business Center of Yiwu.
  • Yiwu Friend Hotel: A budget-friendly option located near Yiwu International Trade Market, perfect for tourists.

What to Buy in Yiwu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yiwu is known for its small commodity exports, and you can find cheap, quality products here. Here are some suggestions:

  • Small Commodity Souvenirs: You can find various small commodity souvenirs such as scarves, hats, and gloves in the trade market.
  • Chinese Traditional Brocade: A type of silk cloth that’s embroidered with intricate designs. It’s a perfect accessory for dresses or traditional Chinese clothing.
  • Tea Leaves and Tea Sets: You can find high-quality tea leaves and tea sets in Yiwu’s many tea shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Yiwu

Yiwu is known for its traditional customs, and it’s essential to understand and respect these customs as a visitor. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Appropriate Dress Code: While there are no strict dress codes, it’s essential to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  • Gifting: Gift-giving is an important part of Chinese culture. When visiting someone, it’s customary to bring a gift.
  • Table Manners: When dining in China, slurping noodles or drinking soup is acceptable. It’s also customary to pour tea for others before oneself.

Best Time to Visit Yiwu

The best time to visit Yiwu is during the spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) seasons. The weather is mild during these seasons, and there’s less humidity than in the summer. However, be prepared for crowds during the spring festival and other important cultural holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yiwu

Do:

  • Learn Basic Chinese: Knowing a few Chinese phrases will make your trip much more comfortable and enjoyable.
  • Respect Local Customs: As mentioned earlier, understanding and respecting local customs is essential when visiting Yiwu.
  • Bring Appropriate Clothing: Pack according to Yiwu’s weather expectations.

Don’t:

  • Discuss Controversial Topics: Avoid discussing sensitive topics such as politics or religion that could offend locals.
  • Buy Counterfeit Products: While it might seem tempting, purchasing counterfeit products is illegal and jeopardizes your safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yiwu

Yiwu is a relatively safe city, and you can wander around without any worries. But, be careful of pickpockets in busy market areas.

Health in Yiwu

The tap water in Yiwu is not suitable for drinking, so it’s essential to carry bottled water. It’s advisable to carry a first aid kit along with bug spray to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Yiwu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yiwu

  • Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport: Located 198 km from Yiwu, it offers domestic and international flights.
  • Yiwu International Airport: Located 11 km from the city center, it has domestic flights within China and international flights to neighboring countries.

Bus Stations Near Yiwu

Yiwu has two bus stations:

  • Yiwu Long-Distance Bus Station: Located near the train station, it provides long-distance bus services to major cities in China.
  • Yiwu Binwang Bus Station: Located in the city center, it offers bus services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

The train is also an excellent option for getting to Yiwu, as it connects the city to Shanghai, Hangzhou, and other major cities.

How to Get Around in Yiwu

Yiwu has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and the metro. The most convenient option is taking a taxi, which is readily available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Yiwu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yiwu’s location in Zhejiang Province means there are plenty of destinations nearby that make for excellent day trips. Here are some popular ones:

  • Hangzhou: A picturesque city, famous for the stunning West Lake, which is only 1.5 hours by bus from Yiwu.
  • Shaoxing: A riverside city, known for producing some of the best Shaoxing wine, which is only an hour from Yiwu.
  • Ningbo: A coastal city with many historical sites, which is only three hours away by high-speed train.

Conclusion

Yiwu, despite being China’s smaller cities, has a lot to offer. From its vast trade market, scenic views, natural beauty, to important cultural and historical sites, Yiwu will leave a lasting impression. Plan your trip well, and you’ll discover the beauty of China that Yiwu has to offer.
