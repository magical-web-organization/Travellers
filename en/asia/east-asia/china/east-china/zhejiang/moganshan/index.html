Introduction to Moganshan

Moganshan is a mountainous region located in Deqing County, Zhejiang Province, China. It is often referred to as the “Hamptons of China” due to its popularity as a summer resort for Shanghai’s elites. With its beautiful natural scenery, rich culture, and charming rural lifestyle, Moganshan attracts visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Moganshan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moganshan Museum

Dedicated to the history and culture of Moganshan, this museum features a collection of artifacts, photos, and documents showcasing the region’s traditional heritage and modern development.

  • Cha’an Temple

One of the oldest temples in Moganshan, Cha’an Temple dates back to the Tang Dynasty and is known for its impressive architecture and beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Moganshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moganshan National Park

With its lush forests, cascading waterfalls, and picturesque lakes, Moganshan National Park is a natural wonderland that offers visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in the beauty of nature.

  • Lingyun Mountain Scenic Area

Known for its stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, Lingyun Mountain Scenic Area boasts a range of hiking and trekking routes that challenge both novice and experienced hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moganshan

  • Moganshan Bamboo Forest Trail

Stretching over 15 km, this trail weaves through the bamboo forests filled with sounds of chirping birds and rustling leaves. The trail features several natural spots, including the famous Dragon Pool Waterfall and the Sacred Bamboo Grove.

  • Lingyun Mountain Hiking Trail

This trail takes hikers through Lingyun Mountain Scenic Area and offers amazing panoramic views of Moganshan. The trail is approximately 10 km long and takes roughly 4 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Moganshan: Activities and Tours

  • Moganshan Art Center

A hub for the local art scene, Moganshan Art Center is a place where visitors can immerse themselves in contemporary art exhibitions, performances, and workshops.

  • Tea Plantation Visit

Visitors can indulge in the region’s rich tea culture by visiting a tea plantation, learning about tea production, and trying different types of fragrant teas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moganshan

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Moganshan Museum and take a walk around the Old Town
Day 2 Hike the Moganshan Bamboo Forest Trail and experience the Dragon Pool Waterfall
Day 3 Explore Lingyun Mountain Scenic Area and challenge yourself with the Lingyun Mountain Hiking Trail

Day 7 End your trip by enjoying the beautiful sunset at the Moganshan National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Moganshan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jinhua ham

Known as one of the “Three Hams” of China, Jinhua ham is a cured meat that has been produced in the region for more than 1,000 years.

  • Fish Head Stew

This local dish features steamed fish head, mushrooms, and other ingredients, cooked in a fragrant and savory broth.

Local Beverages

  • Moganshan Wine

Made from locally grown grapes, Moganshan wine has a distinct taste and aroma that is favored by wine lovers.

  • Longjing Tea

Regarded as the “Queen of Tea,” Longjing tea is a world-renowned tea produced in the nearby West Lake area.

Where to Stay in Moganshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Naked Stables Private Reserve

A luxury resort built with sustainability in mind, Naked Stables Private Reserve offers its guests an authentic experience of living in the countryside while indulging in modern amenities.

  • Zunlu Boutique Hotel

Located in the heart of the Old Town, Zunlu Boutique Hotel is a beautifully restored heritage building that showcases Moganshan’s rich architectural heritage.

What to Buy in Moganshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moganshan bamboo products

Rattan baskets, bamboo hats, and other handmade bamboo products are popular souvenirs visitors can take home.

  • Local tea

Visitors can bring home a taste of Moganshan by purchasing local tea varieties such as Longjing tea and Huangshan Maofeng Tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Moganshan

Moganshan is known for its hospitality and friendly locals. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and practice cultural sensitivity throughout their stay.

Best Time to Visit Moganshan

The best time to visit Moganshan is from April to November when the weather is pleasant, and flowers are in full bloom. Summers can be hot and rainy, while winters can be severely cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moganshan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Sample local cuisines and tea
  • Take warm clothes in winters

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Disrespect local religious sites or customs
  • Bargain excessively

Safety and Health

Safety in Moganshan

Moganshan is a safe area, with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as keeping valuable items safe and avoiding dark alleys at night.

Health in Moganshan

Visitors to Moganshan are advised to consult a doctor before traveling to ensure that all necessary vaccinations and health precautions are taken.

Getting to Moganshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Moganshan

  • Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport Located approximately 70 km from Moganshan, Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport is the closest airport.

  • Shanghai Pudong International Airport Located approximately 200 km from Moganshan, Shanghai Pudong International Airport offers international flights to many destinations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Moganshan

  • Hangzhou East Bus Station Located in the heart of Hangzhou, Hangzhou East Bus Station offers frequent buses to Moganshan.

  • Deqing Bus Station Located in Deqing County, Deqing Bus Station offers direct buses to Moganshan.

Other Options

  • Taxi Visitors can hire a taxi from Hangzhou or Deqing to take them directly to Moganshan.

  • Private Car Rental Visitors can rent a car and drive to Moganshan.

How to Get Around in Moganshan

Visitors can explore Moganshan on foot, by bike or by taxi. Shuttle services to nearby attractions are also available.

Where to Go Next from Moganshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hangzhou Located approximately 70 km from Moganshan, Hangzhou is famous for its picturesque West Lake, temples, and tea plantations.

  • Shanghai Located approximately 200 km from Moganshan, Shanghai is China’s largest city, offering visitors a diverse range of attractions, such as the iconic Bund, the Shanghai Tower, and historical temples.

Conclusion

Moganshan is a hidden gem that offers visitors an opportunity to take a break from city life and explore the beauty of nature and rich cultural heritage. With its natural wonders, charming lifestyle, and friendly locals, Moganshan is a destination worth adding to your travel itinerary.
