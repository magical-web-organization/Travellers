Introduction to Lishui

Located in the southwestern region of Zhejiang province, Lishui is a city packed with natural beauty, historical sites, and delicious food. The city is surrounded by mountains and rivers, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city is divided into four districts, and each of them has its own unique attractions to explore. The city is also known for its tea plantations and silk industry.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the must-visit places, the best things to do, the local culture and customs, and everything else you need to know about Lishui.

List of Places to Visit in Lishui

Here are the top attractions every visitor must see in Lishui:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Longquan Celadon Museum: One of the most significant collections of Longquan celadon pottery in China, the museum showcases over 3,000 pieces of exquisite pottery made over a span of 1,000 years. It is a must-visit for art and history enthusiasts.

  • Shizhongshan Stone City: A unique historical site from the Eastern Jin dynasty, the area contains the ruins of an ancient city constructed entirely from local stone in a remote mountainous location. The site features walls, gates, towers, and several other stone structures.

  • Chuzhou Ancient City: Located in the Yonghong Town of Jinyun County, the ancient city is a popular destination for exploring the rich history of China. The scenic beauty of this place attracts visitors from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Lishui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nanming Mountain: At an elevation of over 1,500 meters, Nanming Mountain is one of the highest peaks in Southeast China. The area features stunning scenic views of the surrounding landscape, including a sea of clouds and spectacular sunrises/sunsets.

  • Songyang Jianhe River: This is a famous destination for rafting, and the river has breathtaking scenery with several rocky islands and crystal clear water. There is also a nearby ancient village that’s well worth exploring.

  • Qingtian Stone Carving: Known as the “Town of Stone Carving,” Qingtian County is a popular destination to appreciate the intricate stone carvings that are still produced using traditional methods. The work of local artisans is highly respected and highly valued in China.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lishui

Lishui has plenty of hiking and biking routes that give visitors a chance to enjoy the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best options:

  • Bamboo Forest Hiking Trail: This 6-kilometer trail takes visitors on an unforgettable journey through a pristine bamboo forest. On the way, visitors can admire a stunning waterfall and enjoy bird watching.

  • Xianzhu Mountain Biking Trail: The trail is designed for experienced mountain bikers and has a variety of jumps, technical sections, and steep drops. The area also features several viewpoints where visitors can admire the stunning natural beauty.

  • Longquan Mountain Hiking Trail: Considered one of the most beautiful and rewarding hikes in Lishui, the trail leads visitors through picturesque villages, dense forests, and stunning mountain scenery.

Best Things to Do in Lishui: Activities and Tours

  • Tea Plantation Tour: Lishui is known for its high-quality tea, and visiting a tea plantation is a great way to learn about the history and culture of tea production in the area. Many plantations offer tours that include tea tastings.

  • Silk Industry Tour: Lishui is also known for its silk industry, and visitors can tour factories that still produce silk using traditional methods. These tours offer a chance to learn about the history and craft of silk production in the region.

  • Hot Spring Bathing: After hiking or biking, relax in one of the many hot springs near Lishui. The area features several hot springs and spas that offer a variety of bathing experiences, including mineral-rich waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lishui

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Lishui that covers all the must-visit attractions:

  • Day 1: Visit the Longquan Celadon Museum and learn about the art of celadon pottery.
  • Day 2: Take a bamboo forest hiking trail and visit a nearby village for an authentic Chinese meal.
  • Day 3: Visit the Chuzhou Ancient City and explore the rich history of the city.
  • Day 4: Take a silk industry tour and visit a tea plantation for a tea tasting experience.
  • Day 5: Head to Xianzhu Mountain Biking Trail to experience the real thrill of mountain biking.
  • Day 6: Go on a hot spring adventure and enjoy mineral-rich waters.
  • Day 7: Explore the best of natural Lishui by visiting the Qingtian Stone Carving attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Lishui

Lishui’s food scene is rich in flavor and variety. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lishui Noodle Soup: A hot and spicy soup made with local wheat noodles, scallions, and red chilies. It’s perfect for cold weather.

  • Bamboo Rice: A dish made by stuffing sticky rice into fresh bamboo and cooking it over an open flame. The result is a delicious smoky flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Lishui Wine: Made from local grapes, Lishui Wine is famous for its sweet taste and rich flavor. Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting experience at many wineries in the area.

  • Jinyun Mountain Spring Water: Jinyun Mountain Spring Water is a popular local beverage that is known for its crisp and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Lishui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lishui offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Jupiter Hotel: Located in the heart of the city, the Jupiter Hotel offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and rivers.

  • Ejin Inn: A cozy and welcoming guesthouse that offers affordable accommodation, local food, and a warm atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lishui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Celadon Pottery: Celadon pottery is the most famous local product of Lishui. Visitors can purchase beautifully crafted pieces in most markets.

  • Bamboo Products: Bamboo is widely available in the area and visitors can find a variety of goods made from bamboo such as chopsticks, baskets, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Lishui

  • Tea Ceremony: Tea plays a vital role in Lishui’s culture, and visitors can participate in a traditional tea ceremony to learn about the history and significance of tea culture.

  • Dragon Dance: The dragon dance is a popular cultural performance in Lishui that is believed to bring good luck and fortune.

Best Time to Visit Lishui

The best time to visit Lishui is in the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and comfortable. The natural scenery is breathtaking during these seasons, and visitors can also enjoy local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lishui

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Lishui has its unique customs and traditions, and it’s essential to respect them while visiting the area.

  • Try Local Cuisine: Lishui has some fantastic culinary experiences on offer, and it’s worth trying local dishes to get a taste of the local flavors.

  • Practice Good Manners: Visitors to Lishui must practice good manners and follow the basic etiquettes and respect towards the local people.

Don’t:

  • Waste Resources: Lishui is known for its beautiful natural scenery, and visitors must make an effort to avoid wasting natural resources.

  • Disrespect Local Traditions: Tourists must avoid offending the local traditions and customs while visiting and respect the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lishui

Lishui is a relatively safe destination for visitors, but tourists must take necessary precautions to ensure their safety at all times. Especially when visiting the outdoor adventures, visitors must be cautious and well prepared.

Health in Lishui

To ensure visitors’ health at all times, it is crucial to adhere to good personal hygiene, including washing hands regularly, drinking safe water, and wearing a face mask to protect oneself from air pollution or infections. It is also essential to have medical coverage and travel insurance.

Getting to Lishui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the most popular transportation options to help visitors reach Lishui:

Airports Near Lishui

Lishui Airport is located in Yongqiang Town and mainly serves flights from other parts of China.

Bus Stations Near Lishui

There are many long-distance bus stations in Lishui, and visitors can book bus tickets for traveling to nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Lishui by train or private car. There are several rental car services available for visitors.

How to Get Around in Lishui

The most convenient mode of transportation in Lishui is by taxi. Visitors can also opt for public buses, rental cars, or bicycles for a more eco-friendly mode of transportation to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Lishui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations from Lishui worth visiting are:

  • Wenzhou: A beautiful port city known for its stunning coastal sceneries and mountains.

  • Yandangshan Mountains: A mountain range located in the northeastern part of Zhejiang province, the area is known for its natural beauty, waterfalls, and ancient temples.

  • Fuyang: A small town located in the Anhui province and surrounded by beautiful mountains and lakes. Fuyang is famous for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural scenery.

Conclusion

Lishui is a city with remarkable natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. The city is a perfect destination for those seeking a unique Chinese cultural experience combined with natural adventures. This comprehensive guide will help visitors plan their trip to Lishui and explore the city’s best attractions, food scene, and culture.
