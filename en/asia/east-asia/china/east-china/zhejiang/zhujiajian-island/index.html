Introduction to Zhujiajian Island

Zhujiajian Island is a hidden gem in Zhejiang, East China. This small island, which stretches only 5.6 square kilometers, is one of the most beautiful yet least known tourist destinations in the country. Surrounded by crystal-clear waters and lush vegetation, Zhujiajian Island boasts stunning beaches, captivating cultural attractions, and thrilling outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Zhujiajian Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The island has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can learn about its past by visiting its museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions on the island include:

  • The Maritime Silk Road Museum, which showcases the ancient maritime trade routes that once linked China with other countries

  • The Navy Stele Pavilion, a historical monument that commemorates the brave naval soldiers who protected the Chinese coast

  • The Zhujiajian Island Folk Museum, which offers a fascinating insight into the local customs and traditions

Natural Attractions of Zhujiajian Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zhujiajian Island’s stunning natural scenery makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day at the beach or a thrilling adventure, the island has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-see natural attractions on the island:

  • Nansha Beach, a secluded beach with crystal-clear water and soft, white sand

  • Dongsha Scenic Area, a picturesque area with stunning views of the sea and the surrounding mountains

  • Daqingshan National Forest Park, a pristine forest with hiking trails and stunning views

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhujiajian Island

For visitors who love to hike or bike, Zhujiajian Island has plenty of opportunities to explore the island’s scenic beauty. The island’s unspoiled natural environment makes it an ideal place for these activities, and visitors can enjoy the following popular routes:

  • Daqingshan National Forest Park Hiking Trail: A 7-kilometer trail that offers spectacular views of nature

  • Zhujiajian Island Coastal Bike Route: A 12-kilometer scenic biking trail that runs along the island’s coastline

Best Things to Do in Zhujiajian Island: Activities and Tours

Zhujiajian Island offers a wide range of activities to suit every interest. Here are some of the best things to do on the island:

  • Enjoy a relaxing day at the beach: Zhujiajian Island has a number of stunning beaches that are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.

  • Take a boat tour: Explore the surrounding waters of the island and discover hidden coves and beaches with a scenic boat tour.

  • Visit the local markets: The island’s markets are a great place to shop for souvenirs, local crafts, and fresh seafood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhujiajian Island

If you’re planning a week-long vacation to Zhujiajian Island, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Arrive on the island, settle into your accommodation, and explore the local markets.

Day 2: Spend the day lounging on the beach or taking a boat tour around the island.

Day 3: Visit the Maritime Silk Road Museum and the Navy Stele Pavilion to learn about the island’s history.

Day 4: Hike the Daqingshan National Forest Park trail and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 5: Take a bike tour along the island’s coastline and enjoy the scenic beauty of the island.

Day 6: Visit the Zhujiajian Island Folk Museum and learn about the local customs and traditions.

Day 7: Enjoy your last day on the island relaxing on the beach or taking any activities you might have missed.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhujiajian Island

Must-Try Dishes

Zhujiajian Island boasts some of the most delicious cuisine in the region. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Seafood cuisine: Fresh seafood is a staple on the island. Grilled oysters and seafood noodles are some of the specialties.

  • Zhujiajian Four Seasons Vegetable: This local side dish features stir-fried seasonal vegetables and is a must-try for vegetarians.

Local Beverages

Some of the local beverages on the island include:

  • Yuhuan wine: Made from rice and barley, this wine is a popular local drink.

  • Zhujiajian Island Tea: This tea is grown locally and has a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Zhujiajian Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhujiajian Island offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget. From luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some popular places to stay on the island include:

  • Zhoushan Xilei Ecological Boutique Hotel: This eco-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the sea.

  • Zhujiajian Time Dream Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is located right on the beach and offers a unique island experience.

What to Buy in Zhujiajian Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhujiajian Island has a number of local products and souvenirs that make great gifts or keepsakes. Some popular items include:

  • Shell handicrafts: Local artisans create beautiful objects using shells from the surrounding waters.

  • Seafood Snacks: Visitors can find dried seafood and seaweed snacks at the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhujiajian Island

The island has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to ancient times. Visitors to the island can experience this culture through the local customs and traditions. Some examples of the island’s unique customs include:

  • Folk festivals: Zhujiajian Island hosts a number of festivals throughout the year, including the Spring Festival and the Mid-Autumn Festival.

  • Dragon Boat Racing: Dragon Boat Racing is a popular sport on the island and is celebrated during the Dragon Boat Festival.

Best Time to Visit Zhujiajian Island

The best time to visit Zhujiajian Island is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm. During this period, visitors can enjoy all the outdoor activities and stunning natural scenery the island has to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhujiajian Island

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting historical or religious places.

  • Protect the environment and do not litter or damage natural resources.

Don’t:

  • Swim in unapproved areas, as there may be dangerous currents.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhujiajian Island

Zhujiajian Island is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety. For example, visitors should keep an eye on their belongings and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Zhujiajian Island

Visitors should take standard precautions to protect their health while visiting Zhujiajian Island. The island has a fully equipped medical center in case of emergencies.

Getting to Zhujiajian Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zhujiajian Island can be reached by air or by bus. The nearest airport is Zhoushan Putuoshan Airport, which is approximately 20 kilometers from the island. Visitors can also take a direct bus from Shanghai, Ningbo, or Hangzhou to Zhujiajian Island.

Airports Near Zhujiajian Island

  • Zhoushan Putuoshan Airport

  • Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport

Bus Stations Near Zhujiajian Island

  • Shanghai South Bus Station

  • Ningbo Bus Station

  • Hangzhou East Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from nearby cities such as Shanghai, Ningbo, and Hangzhou to Zhujiajian Island.

How to Get Around in Zhujiajian Island

Visitors can travel around the island by taxi, bus, or bicycle. Bicycles are a popular way to explore the island’s natural beauty, while taxis are more convenient for visitors who want to travel quickly between places.

Where to Go Next from Zhujiajian Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zhujiajian Island is located in a picturesque area with a number of other stunning destinations nearby. Some popular excursions from Zhujiajian Island include:

  • Putuo Mountain, one of the four sacred mountains of Buddhism in China, is only a short ferry ride away from Zhujiajian Island.

  • The ancient water town of Xitang is a popular destination for visitors who want to experience traditional Chinese architecture and culture.

Conclusion

Zhujiajian Island is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique combination of stunning natural beauty, fascinating cultural attractions, and thrilling outdoor activities.
