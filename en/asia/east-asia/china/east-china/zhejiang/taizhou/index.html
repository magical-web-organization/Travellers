Introduction to Taizhou

Taizhou is a city located in Zhejiang province, situated on the eastern coast of China. The city boasts an impressive blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions, making it a popular destination among tourists. Taizhou is often referred to as the “land of agriculture and fisheries,” and accordingly, seafood is a major part of the local diet. The city has a history stretching back more than 2,000 years, and its rich cultural heritage is evidenced by the range of historical sites, museums, and art galleries scattered throughout the city.

List of Places to Visit in Taizhou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The cultural heritage of Taizhou is reflected in the many museums and historical places in the city. Some of the must-visit places here include:

  • Taizhou Museum: Located in the downtown area of the city, the Taizhou museum showcases the history and culture of the city. The museum has exhibits that span the city’s ancient dynasties, as well as artworks and artifacts from local artists.
  • Linhai Ancient City: Linhai ancient city is a well-preserved historic town located south of Taizhou. It consists of an old city wall, two gates, and a bell tower. The town dates back to the 4th century AD, and visitors can explore its cobbled streets and ancient buildings.
  • Tiantai Mountain Scenic Area: Located about an hour from the city, Tiantai Mountain Scenic Area is home to several Buddhist temples and stunning natural scenery.

Natural Attractions of Taizhou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Taizhou is surrounded by natural beauty, from lush green mountains to gorgeous beaches. Some of the best natural attractions to explore here include:

  • Xianju National Park: This National Park is located in the south of Taizhou and is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in the area. Visitors can hike through the forest or climb to the top of Mount Xianju for a breathtaking view of the landscapes.
  • Sanmen Bay: Sanmen Bay is a scenic area located in the north of Taizhou. It features crystal clear water and fine white sand beaches. Visitors can swim, sunbathe or enjoy a range of water sports in the area.
  • Yandang Mountain Scenic Area: Located to the northeast of the city, Yandang Mountain Scenic Area is another popular natural attraction in Taizhou. The area is characterized by dramatic rock formations, waterfalls, and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taizhou

Taizhou is an excellent place for outdoor adventurers, with several hiking/biking trails available for visitors to explore. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Taizhou include:

  • Tiantai to Yaozhuang: This 7km hiking trail takes visitors through scenic countryside, ancient villages, and small fields.
  • Xianju to Longjing: This 16km biking route is perfect for those looking to explore the mountainous area around Taizhou. The route passes through several villages and through the stunning Xianju National Park.

Best Things to Do in Taizhou: Activities and Tours

In addition to the many cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of activities and tours for you to enjoy in Taizhou. Some of the best activities and tours in Taizhou include:

  • Explore the Taihou Old Street: Visitors can stroll down the old street of Taihou and shop for souvenirs, try traditional snacks and get a feel for the local culture.
  • Learn Traditional Crafts: Visitors can visit one of the many workshops in Taizhou to learn traditional crafts such as wood carving and bamboo weaving.
  • Enjoy Seafood: Seafood is a major part of the local diet in Taizhou, and visitors can take part in an authentic seafood feast or learn how to cook local dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taizhou

For a comprehensive tour of Taizhou, consider the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Taizhou Museum and explore the downtown area. Day 2: Head to the Linhai ancient city and visit the stunning scenery along the route. Day 3: Go hiking in the Xianju National Park and spend the night at one of the many guesthouses in the area. Day 4: Enjoy the Sanmen Bay and its beautiful beaches, and try some local seafood dishes. Day 5: Visit the Tiantai Mountain Scenic Area and explore the Buddhist temples in the area. Day 6: Take part in traditional crafts workshops, learn how to cook local dishes, and shop for souvenirs. Day 7: Visit the Yandang Mountain Scenic Area and enjoy the stunning natural beauty on offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Taizhou

Must-Try Dishes

Taizhou is known for its seafood, and some of the must-try dishes in the city include:

  • Fried Sandworm: A popular snack in Taizhou, sandworms are fried and served crispy.
  • Steamed crab: Freshly caught crabs are the perfect complement to the ocean view in Taizhou.
  • Ginger Duck: Ginger Duck is Taizhou’s signature dish prepared by cooking duck meat with ginger and other spices.

Local Beverages

Taizhou is well-known for its tea and other traditional beverages like rice wine. Visitors can try these beverages at local tea shops or wineries.

Where to Stay in Taizhou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taizhou offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular places to stay in Taizhou include:

  • Grand New Century Hotel: A 5-star hotel with a convenient location in the downtown area, offering comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Lanting Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in a quiet area of the city, with a good range of amenities.
  • Leidu Hotel: A traditional-style guesthouse located in the Xianju National Park, offering breathtaking mountain views and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Taizhou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Taizhou can purchase a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Bamboo products: Taizhou is known for its excellent bamboo products, such as baskets and chopsticks.
  • Tea: Taizhou’s famous tea, such as the Da Hong Pao, is the perfect gift for tea lovers.
  • Hand-carved soapstone: Soapstones are hand-carved by Taizhou artisans and make for unique and beautiful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Taizhou

Taizhou has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions of the city. Some of the notable customs include:

  • Greeting: People in Taizhou greet others by saying “Nei Hle?”, which means “have you eaten?”
  • Taoism: Taoism is the dominant religion in Taizhou, and visitors can visit local temples to learn about the religion.

Best Time to Visit Taizhou

The best time to visit Taizhou is during the autumn months (September – October) when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taizhou

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood dishes, such as zhejiang-style steamed fish or fried shrimp.
  • Do visit one of the many ancient villages in Taizhou, where you can see beautiful traditional architecture and lifestyles of the old farming community.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave without shopping for souvenirs, such as hand-carved soapstones or silk products.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing if visiting in the winter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taizhou

Taizhou is generally a safe city for visitors, with low levels of crime. Visitors should take usual precautions and avoid walking alone in secluded or poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Taizhou

There is no particular health risk associated with visiting Taizhou.

Getting to Taizhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taizhou

  • Taizhou Luqiao Airport: Located in Huangyan District, Taizhou Luqiao International Airport is about 20km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Taizhou

  • Taizhou Bus Station: Located in the city center, Taizhou Bus Station provides bus service to many cities in Zhejiang province.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Taizhou by train, with daily service connecting Taizhou to Shanghai and Hangzhou.

How to Get Around in Taizhou

Visitors to Taizhou can use public transportation, such as buses and taxis, to get around the city. Additionally, visitors can rent a car to explore the surrounding areas independently.

Where to Go Next from Taizhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Shanghai: Taizhou to Shanghai is just a 3-hour train ride away.
  • Wenzhou: Wenzhou is a 3-hour drive from Taizhou, and famous for its leather goods industry.

Conclusion

Taizhou is a beautiful city located in the heart of Zhejiang province, offering everything from natural beauty to cultural heritage, delicious food, and a broad range of activities. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most out of their (week-long) stay in Taizhou by exploring all this beautiful city has to offer.
