Introduction to Mount Putuo

Mount Putuo, located in the Zhejiang province of China, is a sacred Buddhist island and an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. The island is accessible by ferry from both Shanghai and Ningbo and is home to numerous magnificent temples and monasteries. Known for its peaceful atmosphere, stunning natural beauty, and spiritual significance, Mount Putuo provides an unforgettable experience for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Putuo

Mount Putuo offers an abundance of attractions, including culturally important sites, natural landmarks and outdoor activities, temples, monasteries and more. Here’s a list of must-visit places while you’re on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Puji Temple: this ancient temple is one of the island’s largest attractions and is famous for its unique architecture, beautiful gardens, and intricate stonework.
  • Fayu Temple: located northwest of the Puji Temple, the Fayu Temple is home to a famous 800-year-old statue of Guanyin, the Buddhist goddess of mercy.
  • Puji Natatorium: built during the Ming Dynasty, the Puji Natatorium was designed to accommodate large-scale religious performances involving water, and is still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Mount Putuo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hundred-step Beach: this natural wonder is a small beach located at the bottom of a steep staircase. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and overlooks the sea.
  • Putuo Forest Park: the park boasts over 800 types of plant species, including the rare White Cedar and natural bamboo. The park is home to a unique landscape, including waterfalls, caves, and a natural orchard.
  • Ehuangzhang Scenic Area: this scenic area offers impressive views of the Qiantang River and is a popular spot for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Putuo

  • Foding Mountain: a popular hiking trail, Foding Mountain is located in the southern region of the island and offers a challenging climb up a steep path.
  • Zhujiajian Scenic Area: this scenic area is known for its breathtaking coastal scenery and is a perfect spot for biking and hiking enthusiasts.
  • Putuo Old Street: this historic street is located in the heart of the island and is perfect for leisurely biking or strolling.

Best Things to Do in Mount Putuo: Activities and Tours

  • Check out one of the island’s annual festivals, such as the Putuoshan International Vegetarian Cultural Festival or the Putuo pilgrimage ceremony.
  • Visit some of the island’s temples and monasteries, including the Fayu Temple or the famed Huiji Temple.
  • Take a guided walking or biking tour to learn about the island’s rich culture and history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Putuo

Day 1: Puji Temple and Xiaojing Bay Day 2: Fayu Temple and the Golden Beach Day 3: Putuo Old Street and the Nanhai Guanyin Statue Day 4: Hiking in Foding Mountain or biking in Zhujiajian Scenic Area Day 5: Exploring Ehuangzhang Scenic Area Day 6: Puji Natatorium and visiting other monasteries Day 7: Relaxation day at Hundred-step Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Putuo

Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine on Mount Putuo is primarily vegetarian due to the island’s Buddhist traditions. Some of the must-try vegetarian dishes include:

  • Putuoshan Buddha’s Vegetarian Feast: A popular dish, featuring a variety of vegetarian delicacies.
  • Huiji Temple’s Vegetarian Buns: Famous throughout the province, these vegetarian buns are stuffed with fresh vegetables and are a great snack.

Local Beverages

Local drinks include tea, herbal tonics, and Korean-style fermented drinks. Water is available free of charge at all of the monasteries and temples on the island.

Where to Stay in Mount Putuo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mount Putuo offers a range of accommodations, including luxury hotels, budget hostels and guesthouses. Here are our top picks:

  • Putuoshan Hotel: this luxury hotel is located on the beach and boasts impressive views of the surrounding mountains and sea.
  • Putuo Mingsheng Hotel: a budget-friendly option, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and is conveniently located close to the island’s attractions.
  • Putuo Wenxinju Guesthouse: this traditional guesthouse offers a unique experience with a serene atmosphere and quaint rooms.

What to Buy in Mount Putuo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Incense: used in Buddhist ceremonies, incense is an important product on the island and available in a variety of scents and packaging.
  • Buddhist Beads: traditionally used for prayer, these beads are available in many styles and materials.
  • Paintings of the island: various forms of artwork depict the island’s impressive landscape and architecture.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Putuo

Mount Putuo has a deep connection to Buddhism and is an essential pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. Visitors should be respectful of the island’s traditions and customs, including dressing modestly and refraining from loud or disruptive behavior in the temples and monasteries.

Best Time to Visit Mount Putuo

The best time to visit Mount Putuo is between April and October when the weather is mild and comfortable. Avoid visiting during the peak tourist season, which is from June to August when the island can be crowded and congested.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Putuo

Do:

  • Respect the island’s Buddhist traditions and customs.
  • Be mindful of noise levels in temples and monasteries.
  • Dress modestly when entering the temples and monasteries.

Don’t:

  • Bring any non-vegetarian food or items that are offensive to the Buddhist faith.
  • Take pictures of the worshippers without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Putuo

Overall, Mount Putuo is a safe and peaceful destination. Visitors are advised to be cautious when participating in outdoor activities and to make sure that they have adequate supplies and equipment. Visitors should also be aware of any warnings relating to high seas or extreme weather conditions.

Health in Mount Putuo

Visitors should be cautious when drinking water in China, and it is best to only consume bottled water. Visitors should ensure that they have up-to-date vaccinations if they plan to participate in outdoor activities or explore the more remote areas of the island.

Getting to Mount Putuo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Putuo

The closest airport to Mount Putuo is Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG). From here, visitors can take a ferry from either the Shanghai or Ningbo ports to reach the island.

Bus Stations Near Mount Putuo

Buses are available from Shanghai, Hangzhou, and Ningbo to reach the Putuo Mountain Passenger Transport Center. From here, visitors can take a ferry to the island.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to take a train to the nearby Ningbo or Hangzhou and then take a bus to the port to catch a ferry to Mount Putuo.

How to Get Around in Mount Putuo

The island is small enough to explore on foot or by bike, which can be rented from various vendors on the island. Taxis are also available, but it is best to negotiate a price before getting into the cab.

Where to Go Next from Mount Putuo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations include:

  • Ningbo: a lively port city with a rich cultural heritage and picturesque coastal scenery.
  • Hangzhou: a famous tourist destination with a serene lake, historic temples, and famous West Lake.
  • Shanghai: a bustling, cosmopolitan city with world-class restaurants, nightlife, and shopping.

Conclusion

Mount Putuo is a unique and awe-inspiring destination that offers visitors an opportunity to explore China’s rich cultural and religious traditions. Whether you’re interested in natural beauty, outdoor activities or spiritual enlightenment, Mount Putuo is the perfect destination for your next adventure.
