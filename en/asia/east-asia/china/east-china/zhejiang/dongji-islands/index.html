Introduction to Dongji Islands

Located in Zhejiang province on the eastern coast of China, Dongji Islands is a cluster of nearly 30 small islands nestled in the East China Sea. With crystal-clear waters, idyllic beaches, and lush greenery, Dongji Islands is a paradise for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and travelers seeking a tranquil and off-the-beaten-path destination.

List of Places to Visit in Dongji Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dongji Island Museum: Explore the history and culture of Dongji Islands through a range of exhibits that showcase the region’s archaeology, fishing heritage, and natural treasures.

  • Dongji Temple: Pay your respects at this serene Buddhist temple, which dates back to the Tang Dynasty and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.

  • Former Residence of Chen Qimei: Visit the former home of Chen Qimei, a prominent statesman and revolutionary who played a key role in China’s modern history.

Natural Attractions of Dongji Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Shengshan: Hike to the summit of Mt. Shengshan, the highest peak in Dongji Islands, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding islands and sea.

  • Crescent Bay: Relax on the palm-fringed sands of Crescent Bay, one of the most beautiful beaches in Dongji Islands.

  • Moon Bay: Explore the underwater world of Moon Bay, which is teeming with colorful fish and other marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dongji Islands

Dongji Islands boasts a wealth of hiking and biking trails that take you through stunning natural scenery and offer opportunities for adventure and exploration. Some of the must-try routes include:

  • Shengshan Mountain Trail: This moderate trail takes you through dense forests, past scenic viewpoints, and up to the summit of Mt. Shengshan.

  • Coastal Bike Trail: Rent a bike and pedal along the coast, stopping at beaches, fishing villages, and scenic lookout points along the way.

  • Yueya Bay Ecological Trail: Walk this peaceful trail and admire the diverse flora and fauna of Dongji Islands, including rare bird species and flowering plants.

Best Things to Do in Dongji Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of Dongji Islands and explore the vibrant coral reefs and underwater landscapes.

  • Fishing Villages Tour: Discover the unique culture and cuisine of Dongji Islands by visiting local fishing villages and sampling fresh seafood.

  • Nature Photography: Capture the stunning natural beauty of Dongji Islands through your lens and take home unforgettable memories and images.

Food and Drink Scene of Dongji Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dongji Island Seafood Rice: A delicious and filling rice dish topped with oysters, shrimp, and other fresh seafood.

  • Li Wong’s Fried Rice: This classic fried rice dish is named after a local chef and is made with flavorful cured meat and eggs.

  • Fenghua Tea: Savor the fragrant and refreshing taste of Dongji Islands’ famous green tea, which is grown in the local tea plantations.

Local Beverages

  • Shaoxing Wine: This famous Chinese rice wine is a popular accompaniment to seafood dishes and has a rich and complex flavor.

  • Dongji Island Beer: Try this locally brewed beer, which is made with fresh spring water and has a crisp and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Dongji Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dongji Islands offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxurious beachfront resorts to cozy guesthouses and budget-friendly hostels, here are some of the best places to stay in Dongji Islands:

  • Dongji Island Resort Hotel: Enjoy a luxurious stay at this spacious and elegant resort, which boasts stunning sea views and a range of amenities, including a spa, swimming pool, and multiple dining options.

  • Dongji Island Youth Hostel: Stay in a comfortable and affordable dorm or private room at this friendly and welcoming hostel, which offers easy access to the island’s top attractions.

  • Dongji Island Homestay: Experience local hospitality and culture by staying in a traditional homestay, where you can enjoy home-cooked meals and interact with friendly locals.

What to Buy in Dongji Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea Products: Take home some of Dongji Islands’ famous green tea, which is available in a variety of flavors and packaging options.

  • Shell and Coral Crafts: Shop for unique and beautiful items made from local seashells and coral, including jewelry, home decor, and accessories.

  • Seafood Snacks: Stock up on tasty and authentic local snacks like dried squid, fish jerky, and kelp chips, which make great gifts for foodies and gourmet enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Dongji Islands

Dongji Islands has a rich and fascinating culture, shaped by its history, geography, and local traditions. Some of the key aspects of Dongji Islands’ culture and customs include:

  • Fishing Heritage: Dongji Islands has a long and proud history of fishing, which is reflected in its cuisine, festivals, and daily life.

  • Buddhist Influence: Buddhism has played an important role in Dongji Islands’ history and culture, as evidenced by its numerous temples and religious landmarks.

  • Festivals and Celebrations: Dongji Islands hosts a range of colorful and vibrant festivals throughout the year, including the annual Fishing Festival, the Mid-Autumn Festival, and the Dragon Boat Festival.

Best Time to Visit Dongji Islands

The best time to visit Dongji Islands is from May to September, when the weather is sunny and warm, and the sea is calm enough for water activities. However, be prepared for higher tourist crowds during this period, especially in July and August, which are peak travel seasons.

If you prefer more tranquility, you can also consider visiting Dongji Islands during the shoulder seasons of March to April or October to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the scenery is still beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dongji Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.

  • Try local cuisine and delicacies, including fresh seafood and green tea.

  • Protect the environment and follow eco-friendly practices, such as taking your trash with you and avoiding harmful activities like littering and overfishing.

Don’t:

  • Damage or disturb coral reefs and other marine ecosystems.

  • Wear revealing clothing or inappropriate attire when visiting religious sites or public areas.

  • Engage in any illegal or unethical activities, such as drug use or wildlife trafficking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dongji Islands

Dongji Islands is generally safe for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly and welcoming local population. However, as with any travel destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as:

  • Being aware of your surroundings and avoiding isolated or unfamiliar areas, especially at night.

  • Keeping your valuables secure and not displaying them openly or leaving them unattended.

  • Following traffic regulations and taking appropriate safety measures when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Dongji Islands

Dongji Islands has good medical facilities and services, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic health precautions, such as:

  • Drinking only bottled or filtered water and avoiding tap water.

  • Bringing any necessary medications or prescriptions with you, and carrying them with you at all times.

  • Using insect repellent and protective clothing to avoid bites from mosquitoes or other insects.

Getting to Dongji Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dongji Islands

The nearest airport to Dongji Islands is Ningbo Lishe International Airport, which is located about 120 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Zhoushan city and then take a ferry to Dongji Islands.

Bus Stations Near Dongji Islands

There are several bus stations in Zhoushan city that offer services to Dongji Islands, including the Zhoushan Passenger Transport Center, Donggang Long-distance Bus Station, and Putuo Long-distance Bus Station.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or taxi to take you directly to Zhoushan city or to the ferry terminal, or you can join a guided tour that includes transportation from nearby cities like Shanghai or Hangzhou.

How to Get Around in Dongji Islands

Dongji Islands is relatively small, and you can easily explore most of the main attractions on foot, by bike, or by local taxis or buses. If you’re planning to do a lot of hiking or visiting more remote areas, you may also consider renting a scooter or motorcycle.

Where to Go Next from Dongji Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhoushan City: Explore the bustling port city of Zhoushan, which is home to a range of cultural landmarks, shopping areas, and restaurants.

  • Putuo Mountain: Visit the sacred Putuo Mountain, one of China’s most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites, and admire its beautiful temples and scenic vistas.

  • Ningbo City: Discover the rich history and culture of Ningbo, a charming coastal city known for its ancient architecture, museums, and gardens.

Conclusion

Dongji Islands is a hidden gem of China, offering stunning natural beauty, rich culture and history, and a range of exciting activities and experiences. Whether you come for a weekend getaway or a longer adventure, this enchanting destination is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories and a deep appreciation for the beauty of East Asia.
