Introduction to Fuyang

Fuyang, a prefecture-level city located in the northwest of Zhejiang province in East China, is one of the hidden gems of China. With a history dating back over two thousand years, Fuyang boasts cultural attractions, natural attractions, and outdoor activities for every type of traveler. This city is a perfect blend of traditional Chinese culture and modern development.

In this travel guide, we will provide a comprehensive list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, activities, must-try local dishes, where to stay, local culture and customs, and more. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, Fuyang has something to offer. So, let’s dive right in!

List of Places to Visit in Fuyang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fuchunjiang River Scenic Area - This area offers stunning views of the Fuchun River and showcases the unique architectural styles of ancient China.
  • Fuyang District Museum - A visit to this museum will take you on a journey through Fuyang’s history and its cultural heritage.
  • Dahua Cultural Park - One of the largest cultural parks in China, Dahua Cultural Park features numerous pavilions, gardens, and lakes that display the rich history and culture of China.
  • Tongzhan Ancient Town - This old town is a charming spot with ancient architectures and alleys. It is a must-visit place in Fuyang.
  • Xitang Water Town - This ancient water town surrounded by canals and bridges will transport you to the Ming and Qing dynasties.

Natural Attractions of Fuyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qiandao Lake - Known as the “Thousand Island Lake,” Qiandao Lake consists of 1078 small islands, with clean waters and beautiful views.
  • Wujiangyuan Scenic Area - This area is full of green forests, waterfalls, and streams.
  • Yinzhou Park - Located in downtown Fuyang, this urban park is the perfect place for a relaxing walk or picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fuyang

  • Lishui Rock - This is a popular hiking route, featuring a number of unique rock formations and a stunning view of the surrounding mountains.
  • Moganshan - This area offers several hiking and biking trails, taking you through lush forests and rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in Fuyang: Activities and Tours

  • Cycling tour - Explore Fuyang’s scenic countryside and historic sites on a bike tour.
  • Boat tour on Fuchunjiang River - Enjoy the beautiful scenery of Fuchun River, and take in the unique architecture of ancient China.
  • Fuyang Hot Air Balloon Experience - Get a bird’s eye view of Fuyang and its surroundings on an unforgettable hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Fuyang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beggar’s Chicken - This is an exotic dish, wrapped in lotus leaves, with a unique taste and texture.
  • Dongyang Fried Rice - Served in a bamboo basket, this rice dish is known for its distinctive flavor and aroma.
  • Shaoxing Rice Wine - One of China’s most famous rice wines, it is aged for years to develop its unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Fuyang Tea - This special tea is made with locally grown tea leaves and is known for its rich taste and aroma.
  • Yang Feng Xia ‘18’ Danshan Vinegar - Made from locally grown rice, it is a staple in Chinese cuisine.
  • Fuyang Dried Persimmons and Ginger Candy - These are popular local sweets and make perfect souvenirs to take back home.

Where to Stay in Fuyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hyatt Place Fuyang - A luxury hotel in Fuyang, with spacious rooms and excellent service.
  • Zhixin Holiday Inn - A budget hotel option, with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Yujia Inn - A guesthouse with a traditional Chinese courtyard garden, offering a more authentic and cultural experience.

What to Buy in Fuyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fuyang Tea - This locally grown tea is a perfect souvenir to take back home.
  • Jasmine-Scented Sandalwood Fans - These beautifully crafted fans are made from sandalwood and have a fragrance of jasmine.
  • Huangzehe Cambium Paintings - This unique style of Chinese painting uses the natural colors and textures of the cambium layer of trees.

Local Culture and Customs of Fuyang

  • Duanwu Festival - This annual festival celebrates the life of a famous Chinese poet, Qu Yuan. The festival features dragon boat races, traditional eatables, and a colorful carnival atmosphere.
  • Lion Dance - This traditional folk dance is performed on special occasions to ward off evil spirits and bring good fortune.
  • Dragon and Phoenix Wedding - This is a traditional wedding ceremony, symbolizing happiness and prosperity for the couple.

Best Time to Visit Fuyang

The best time to visit Fuyang is in spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). The weather during these months is pleasant, with mild temperatures and low humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fuyang

Do:

  • Respect the local folk customs and ways of life.
  • Enjoy the local gastronomy and nightlife.
  • Carry your passport and other essential documents with you.

Don’t:

  • Behave offensively or disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Drink tap water, it is not safe for consumption.
  • Over-rely on social media platforms, as they are heavily monitored in China.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fuyang

Fuyang is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it is advisable to take standard precautions like keeping valuables safe, avoid deserted areas at night, and carrying some form of identification with you.

Health in Fuyang

It is advisable to get all the necessary vaccinations and health checkups before traveling to China. It is also essential to carry adequate health insurance to cover medical emergencies.

Getting to Fuyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fuyang

Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport is the nearest major airport to Fuyang, located at a distance of about 120 km. There are shuttle buses available from the airport to Fuyang.

Bus Stations Near Fuyang

Fuyang Bus Station is the main bus station, located in downtown Fuyang, with regular bus services to nearby cities and provinces.

Other Options

Trains are also a good option to travel to Fuyang. The nearest railway station is Tonglu Railway Station, located about 35 km away from Fuyang.

How to Get Around in Fuyang

The best way to explore Fuyang is by renting a car or taking a taxi. Buses and bike rentals are other viable options to get around the city and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Fuyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fuyang is an excellent starting point for exploring other nearby destinations like Hangzhou, Suzhou, and Wuxi. These cities offer similar attractions and are popular destinations among travelers.

Conclusion

Fuyang is undoubtedly a destination worth visiting, with a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor activities.
