Introduction to Zhoushan

Zhoushan is a group of more than one thousand islands located in the East China Sea. It is one of the busiest ports in China and is famous for its seafood and beautiful scenery. This area is rich in history and culture, and is home to several famous temples and shrines. Zhoushan is a popular tourist destination where you can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, swimming, rock climbing, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Zhoushan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Putuo Mountain: This is one of the most famous sites in Zhoushan. It is a Buddhist shrine that is believed to be the home of the bodhisattva Guanyin. The mountain has many temples and shrines, and visitors can climb to the top for a stunning view of the surrounding area.

Qufu Island: This is an important historical site where visitors can see ancient Chinese buildings and the remains of the Qing Dynasty’s naval base. It is a picturesque island with beautiful scenery and is home to several temples.

Natural Attractions of Zhoushan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Daishan Island: This is one of the biggest tourist attractions in Zhoushan. It is a beautiful island with sandy beaches and crystal-clear water. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and other beach activities. Daishan Island is also famous for its seafood.

Nansha Beach: This is a secluded beach on Shengsi Island. It is surrounded by mountains and has clear water. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountains for a panoramic view of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhoushan

Putuo Mountain Hiking Trail: This is a popular hiking trail that takes visitors up to the top of Putuo Mountain. It is a challenging hike, but the view from the top is worth the effort.

Daishan Island Biking Trail: This is a scenic biking trail that takes visitors around the island. It is a leisurely ride with stunning views of the beach and surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Zhoushan: Activities and Tours

Fishing tour: Zhoushan is famous for its fishing industry. Visitors can take a fishing tour and learn about the local fishing culture. They can also enjoy a fresh seafood feast on the boat.

Rock climbing: Zhoushan has several great spots for rock climbing. Visitors can climb the cliffs and enjoy a stunning view of the sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhoushan

Must-Try Dishes

Zhoushan seafood: Fresh seafood is a must-try in Zhoushan. Visitors can enjoy a variety of seafood dishes like grilled fish, seafood hotpot, and more.

Baishan (white mountain) tea: This is a famous tea produced in Zhoushan. It is a high-quality green tea with a unique flavor and fragrance.

Local Beverages

Tuopai Shede (a type of wine): This is a famous local wine in Zhoushan. It is aged in jars and has a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Zhoushan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sheraton Zhoushan Hotel: This is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city. It offers stunning views of the sea and has all the amenities for a comfortable stay.

Shengsi Qihuapu Hostel: This is a budget-friendly hostel located on Shengsi Island. It is a great place to meet other travelers and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the island.

What to Buy in Zhoushan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhoushan pearls: Zhoushan is famous for its pearls. Visitors can buy high-quality pearls at the local markets.

Zhejiang silk: This is a famous silk produced in Zhejiang province. Visitors can buy high-quality silk products like scarves, clothes, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhoushan

Zhoushan is known for its fishing culture. Visitors can experience the local fishing culture by taking a fishing tour and learning about the local fishing techniques.

Best Time to Visit Zhoushan

The best time to visit Zhoushan is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny. The peak tourist season is from July to August, so visitors should plan their trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhoushan

Do:

  • Try the local seafood
  • Visit the local temples and shrines
  • Learn about the local fishing culture

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples and shrines
  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Smoke in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhoushan

Zhoushan is a relatively safe city. Visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Zhoushan

Visitors should be careful about the food they eat and make sure to wash their hands regularly to prevent any illnesses.

Getting to Zhoushan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhoushan

Zhoushan Putuoshan Airport: This is the main airport in Zhoushan. It serves domestic flights to major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Zhoushan

Zhoushan Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Zhoushan. It serves buses to major cities in China.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry to Zhoushan from nearby cities like Ningbo and Shanghai.

How to Get Around in Zhoushan

Visitors can get around Zhoushan by bus, taxi, or bike. Biking is a popular option for visitors as it allows them to enjoy the beautiful scenery of the islands.

Where to Go Next from Zhoushan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ningbo: This is a nearby city that is famous for its historical sites and modern attractions.

Shanghai: This is a major city in China and is famous for its modern architecture and bustling nightlife.

Conclusion

Zhoushan is a beautiful destination with stunning landscapes, rich history, and culture. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, learn about the local fishing culture, and indulge in delicious seafood. With this travel guide, visitors can plan a comprehensive itinerary that covers all the best places to visit, things to do, and places to stay in Zhoushan.
