Introduction to Longquan

Longquan is a scenic city located in the south-central part of Zhejiang province, China. This picturesque city sits on the slopes of mountains, surrounded by lush forests, and features a number of historical sites and natural attractions. Longquan has a rich cultural heritage, famous for its ancient, distinct cultural relics, and is home to one of the four renowned porcelain production centers in China.

If you’re looking to explore a less crowded, less expensive, less touristy destination in China, Longquan is the perfect place for you.

What to Buy in Longquan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Longquan is famous for its porcelain products, which is one of the best things to buy in this scenic city. Local markets and shops offer a variety of porcelain products like tea sets, vases, bowls, and more. Apart from porcelain, you can also buy locally-made tea, white tea, green tea, and so on.

Local Culture and Customs of Longquan

Longquan has a rich culture and unique customs that are worth experiencing. The city has a strong association with pottery, tea culture and Daoism. The locals here are known for their hospitality and are very welcoming to tourists.

Best Time to Visit Longquan

The best time to visit Longquan is usually from March to November, when the weather is warm and the flowers bloom around. During this time, you can enjoy the scenic beauty of Longquan and explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longquan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Visit historic sites and natural attractions
  • Shop for local products as souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions
  • Use flash photography inside museums or sacred places
  • Touch ancient relics or historical objects in museums

Safety and Health

Safety in Longquan

Longquan is a relatively safe and secure destination. Petty theft and pick-pocketing are rare, but you should always be cautious of your belongings when traveling.

Health in Longquan

Tap water is not potable in Longquan, so be sure to drink bottled water or boiled water. Wear appropriate clothing, use mosquito repellent, and take basic precautions to prevent illness.

Getting to Longquan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Longquan

The nearest airport to Longquan is Lishui Airport, which is located about 90 km away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Longquan

Regular bus services operate to Longquan from nearby cities like Hangzhou and Shaoxing.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or take a private car to Longquan.

How to Get Around in Longquan

Longquan is a relatively small city, so walking or cycling is the most convenient way to get around. Taxis and buses are also available if you want to travel further.
