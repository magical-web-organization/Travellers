Introduction to East Fujian

East Fujian is a region in Fujian, China, located on the southeast coast and facing the Taiwan Strait. It is comprised of several cities and counties, including Fuzhou, Putian, Quanzhou, and Xiamen. East Fujian has a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and delicious local cuisine. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in East Fujian.

List of Places to Visit in East Fujian

Whether you are interested in culture, history, nature, or adventure, there is something for you in East Fujian. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gulangyu Island: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Xiamen, known for its colonial architecture, beaches, and museums.

  • Fujian Tulou: Unique earthen buildings found in the mountainous regions of Fujian, including the Yongding and Nanjing Tulou clusters. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Wuyi Mountains: A scenic area with steep cliffs, deep gorges, and ancient temples, located in the north of Fujian.

  • Kaiyuan Temple: A historic Buddhist temple in Quanzhou, founded in the 6th century and known for its intricate carvings and architecture.

Natural Attractions of East Fujian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mawei Island: A small island located in the Fuzhou Bay, known for its rocky coastline, forests, and temple.

  • Fuzhou National Forest Park: A nature reserve located in the suburbs of Fuzhou, with hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic views.

  • Mount Wuyi: A mountain range located on the border of Fujian and Jiangxi provinces, known for its stunning scenery, tea plantations, and outdoor adventures.

  • Xiamen Botanical Garden: A large botanical garden located in Xiamen, with over 6,000 species of plants and trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Fujian

East Fujian has some excellent hiking and biking routes, with stunning scenery and a variety of difficulty levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Qingyuan Mountain: A challenging hiking trail in Fuzhou, with waterfalls, caves, and scenic views.

  • Gulangyu Island: A great place for a leisurely bike ride or walk, with colonial architecture and beaches to explore.

  • Nanji Mountain: A scenic hiking trail near Wuyishan, with a summit at 1,300 meters above sea level.

Best Things to Do in East Fujian: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting things to do in East Fujian, including cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and unique tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Tea Ceremony: Visit a traditional tea plantation in Fujian and learn about the history and culture of tea in China.

  • Snorkelling: Explore the coral reefs and marine life of Xiamen Island with a guided snorkelling tour.

  • Food Tour: Sample the delicious local cuisine of East Fujian on a guided food tour, including specialties like seafood, noodles, and tea.

  • Waterfall rappelling: For the more adventurous, try your hand at water rappelling in one of East Fujian’s many scenic waterfalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Fujian

If you have a week to explore East Fujian, here’s a sample itinerary to get the most out of your trip:

Day Activity
1 Arrival in Fuzhou, explore Wuyi Square and West Lake
2 Visit Fujian Tulou in Yongding, return to Fuzhou
3 Travel to Quanzhou, visit Kaiyuan Temple and Qingjing Mosque
4 Take a scenic drive to Xiamen and explore Gulangyu Island
5 Visit the Xiamen Botanical Garden and Hulishan Fortress

7 Departure from Wuyishan or Xiamen Airport

Food and Drink Scene of East Fujian

East Fujian is known for its fresh seafood, flavorful noodle dishes, and unique tea culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buddha Jumps Over the Wall: A famous seafood soup made with over 20 ingredients, including shark’s fin, abalone, and shrimp.

  • Zhangzhou-style Noodles: Thin and chewy noodles, served hot or cold with a variety of sauces and toppings.

  • Oyster Omelette: A crispy and flavorful omelette made with fresh oysters and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tie Guan Yin: A type of oolong tea grown in Fujian, known for its floral aroma and smooth taste.

  • Fujian Baijiu: A strong distilled liquor made from rice, wheat, or corn.

  • Xiamen Coffee: A unique style of coffee, made with local beans and sweetened with condensed milk.

Where to Stay in East Fujian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Fujian has a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top picks:

  • Shangri-La Hotel, Fuzhou: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Fuzhou, with excellent amenities and stunning city views.

  • Easy-Inn, Xiamen: A comfortable and affordable hostel located on the popular Zhongshan Road, with clean rooms and a friendly staff.

  • Wuyi Mountain Mingxuan Hostel: A charming guesthouse located in the scenic Wuyi Mountains, with cozy rooms and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in East Fujian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts from East Fujian, here are some local products to consider:

  • Tea: Buy some high-quality oolong tea from a local plantation or tea shop.

  • Hakka Embroidery: A traditional style of embroidery from the Hakka people, available in many shops and markets in Fujian.

  • Lacquerware: Beautifully crafted wooden items, such as bowls, plates, and jewelry boxes.

Local Culture and Customs of East Fujian

East Fujian has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from various ethnic groups and historical periods. Some notable customs and traditions include:

  • Hakka Culture: The Hakka people are a distinct ethnic group who migrated to Fujian centuries ago, and their unique culture and language can be seen throughout the region.

  • Minnan Culture: Another important cultural group in East Fujian, the Minnan people have a distinct language, food, music, and architecture.

  • Dragon Boat Festival: One of the most important festivals in East Fujian and throughout China, the Dragon Boat Festival celebrates the life and legacy of the poet Qu Yuan.

Best Time to Visit East Fujian

The best time to visit East Fujian is during the spring and autumn months, from March to May and September to November. The weather is mild and pleasant, and there are many cultural festivals and outdoor activities to enjoy. Avoid visiting during the summer months, as it can be hot and humid with frequent typhoons, and during the winter months, as it can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Fujian

To have a pleasant and respectful experience in East Fujian, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Take the time to learn about the culture and customs of the people you are visiting.

  • Be polite and friendly: East Fujian locals are known for their hospitality and kindness, so show them the same respect and courtesy.

  • Try the local food and drinks: East Fujian has a unique and delicious culinary scene, so be sure to sample some of the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect cultural sites and monuments: East Fujian has many historic and religious sites, so be respectful and follow any rules or guidelines.

  • Take photos without permission: Ask for permission before taking photos of people or private property.

  • Be disruptive or disrespectful in public: Follow local etiquette and customs, and be mindful of others around you.

Safety and Health

East Fujian is generally a safe and healthy place to visit, but it’s important to take some precautions:

Safety in East Fujian

  • Beware of pickpockets: Like any other tourist destination, there is a risk of pickpocketing or theft, so keep an eye on your valuables and use common sense.

  • Watch out for traffic: Traffic can be chaotic and unpredictable, especially in cities like Fuzhou and Xiamen, so exercise caution when crossing the street or driving.

  • Be aware of natural hazards: Typhoons, earthquakes, and landslides can occur in East Fujian, especially during the summer months.

Health in East Fujian

  • Drink bottled water: Tap water in East Fujian may not be safe to drink, so stick to bottled water or other bottled drinks.

  • Take mosquito repellent: Mosquitoes can be a problem in some areas, especially during the summer months, so use insect repellent and cover up as necessary.

  • Get vaccinated: Check with your doctor before traveling to East Fujian, and make sure you are up-to-date on any necessary vaccinations.

Getting to East Fujian: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Fujian is easily accessible by air, bus, or train. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near East Fujian

  • Fuzhou Changle International Airport: Located in the suburbs of Fuzhou, with flights to many domestic and international destinations.

  • Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport: Located in Xiamen, with flights to many domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near East Fujian

  • Fuzhou South Bus Station: A major bus station in Fuzhou, with long-distance buses to many cities in China.

  • Xiamen Hubin Bus Station: A major bus station in Xiamen, with long-distance buses to many cities in China.

Other Options

  • High-speed train: East Fujian has several high-speed train stations, with connections to many major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.

  • Ferry: Xiamen has several ferry ports, with connections to many nearby islands and Taiwan.

How to Get Around in East Fujian

Getting around in East Fujian is easy and convenient, with many transportation options available. Here are some ways to get around:

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in major cities like Fuzhou and Xiamen, and are relatively affordable.

  • Bus: Buses are a cheap and efficient way to get around East Fujian, with many routes and services available.

  • Bicycle: Renting a bicycle is a great way to explore East Fujian at your own pace, and is a popular choice on Gulangyu Island and in Xiamen.

Where to Go Next from East Fujian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore China, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Hangzhou: A beautiful city known for its stunning West Lake and historic temples, located just a few hours north of East Fujian.

  • Jiuzhaigou National Park: A scenic nature reserve in Sichuan Province, with turquoise lakes, waterfalls, and snowy peaks.

  • Taipei, Taiwan: Take a ferry or flight from Xiamen to visit Taiwan’s vibrant capital city, with a rich cultural scene, night markets, and delicious food.

Conclusion

East Fujian is a fascinating and beautiful region with so much to explore and discover. Whether you are interested in culture, history, nature, or adventure, East Fujian has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to visit this incredible destination and experience all it has to offer.
