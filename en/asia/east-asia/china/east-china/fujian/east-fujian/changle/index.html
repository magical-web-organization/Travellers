Introduction to Changle

Located in the southeastern part of Fujian province, Changle is a county-level city with a rich cultural and natural heritage. It boasts of scenic views, cultural landmarks, and an exciting food scene that adds to its charm.

List of Places to Visit in Changle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Changle Museum

The Changle Museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts that showcase the county’s history, culture, and tradition. Here, visitors can learn about the county’s involvement in the Fujian Rebellion and view stunning ceramics and stone carvings.

Gengwu Academy

Gengwu Academy is one of the most significant historical landmarks in Changle, built in the Ming Dynasty. It served as a school that imparted the Confucian principles and teachings, which were integral to the Chinese society then.

Natural Attractions of Changle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shanqi Peak

Shanqi Peak is located in the center of Changle and is a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. It boasts of natural scenery, including stunning waterfalls and a breathtaking view of the city.

Taihe Golden Beach

Taihe Golden Beach is the perfect place to relax and unwind with its clear blue waters and miles of sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changle

Changle has many hiking and biking routes that offer picturesque views of the surrounding natural landscapes. Two popular hiking routes are the Changle-Baima ridge walking trail and the Shanqi Old Road. Cyclists can take the Changle-Xianyou cycling path, which stretches 50 kilometers and features remarkable views.

Best Things to Do in Changle: Activities and Tours

Cooking Classes

Changle’s food scene is renowned for its authentic taste and variety. Take cooking classes to learn traditional dishes like Fujian Fried Rice, White Pepper Crab, and Oyster Omelette.

Tea Tasting Tour

Changle is known for its tea production, offering different varieties of tea, such as white tea, black tea, and scented tea. Take a tea tasting tour where you can explore tea gardens and learn about the tea-making process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Changle

Day 1: Visit Changle Museum and Gengwu Academy, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant, and walk around the city center.

Day 2: Visit Shanqi Peak and Taihe Golden Beach.

Day 3: Take a cooking class and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 4: Hike the Changle-Baima trail.

Day 5: Tour tea gardens and participate in a tea tasting experience.

Day 6: Visit the Qingyun Temple, which dates back to the Song Dynasty and enjoy famous vegetarian meals.

Day 7: Take a cycling tour of Changle-Xianyou Cycling Path.

Food and Drink Scene of Changle

Must-Try Dishes

Fujian Fried Rice

Fujian Fried Rice is a dish that originates from Changle and is a must-try for any visitor. The dish is prepared using dried shrimp, mushrooms, and diced Chinese sausages.

White Pepper Crab

White Pepper Crab is a delicacy in Changle, best enjoyed in the summer months when the crabs are at their freshest. They are seasoned with white pepper and cooked in hot oil until crispy.

Local Beverages

Changle Tea

Changle produces tea, which is famous for its unique taste, aroma, and health benefits. There are several varieties of tea available, including white tea and black tea.

Where to Stay in Changle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luxe Hotel

Luxe Hotel is a 5-star hotel that offers luxurious accommodations and excellent amenities, located in the heart of Changle.

Daisy Hostel

Daisy Hostel is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a friendly environment.

What to Buy in Changle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Changle has many souvenirs and local products to bring home, including tea, ceramics, and carved stone items.

Local Culture and Customs of Changle

Changle has preserved its cultural and traditional heritage, evident in its ancient temples, art, and music. The people here are friendly and hospitable, and visitors can learn about their customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Changle

The best time to visit Changle is from April to October because of the pleasant weather, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the scenic views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changle

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the natural and cultural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious landmarks.
  • Leave your travel documents behind.
  • Speak loudly or rudely in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Changle

Changle is a safe city, but it’s always advisable to take standard safety precautions while traveling, like securing your belongings and staying alert while in crowded areas.

Health in Changle

Changle has excellent medical facilities, and visitors can seek treatment at the county hospital or the Changle People’s Hospital.

Getting to Changle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Changle

The nearest airport to Changle is the Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport, approximately 50 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Changle

The Changle Bus Station is the main bus station serving the county, with scheduled buses running to and from Xiamen and other cities in Fujian.

Other Options

Private taxis and ride-sharing apps are easily available in Changle. Bicycles and motorbikes can also be rented.

How to Get Around in Changle

Changle has a comprehensive bus network, with buses running to all major destinations in the county. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer a more convenient mode of transport.

Where to Go Next from Changle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Xiamen

Xiamen is a popular seaside city located approximately 40 kilometers from Changle. It offers beautiful beaches, stunning cultural landmarks, and bustling night markets.

Quanzhou

Quanzhou is a historical city positioned approximately 90 kilometers from Changle, home to ancient temples and renowned for its tea production.

Conclusion

Changle offers visitors an immersive experience into the county’s rich heritage, culture, and natural landscapes. With its fantastic food scene, scenic views, and cultural landmarks, Changle is undoubtedly a hidden gem in Fujian that’s worth exploring.
