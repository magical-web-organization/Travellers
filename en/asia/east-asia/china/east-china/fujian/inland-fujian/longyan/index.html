Introduction to Longyan

Located in Inland Fujian, Longyan is a beautiful, historic city that offers a glimpse into ancient Chinese culture mixed with modern developments. The city is home to some of the most beautiful natural scenery in China, with rolling hills, streams, and lakes. Longyan is an ideal destination for any traveler who seeks authenticity and wants to experience traditional China at its finest. In this guide, we’ll help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Longyan.

List of Places to Visit in Longyan

Longyan has plenty of attractions to offer visitors. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yongding Tulou - A series of striking earthen fortresses that have been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Longyan Cultural Centre - A showcase of Longyan’s history, culture, and traditions
  • Wuyi Palace - A royal palace that once served as a center of power and politics in ancient China
  • Tianluokeng Tulou - A cluster of a five round tulou buildings that are also part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site

Natural Attractions of Longyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Wuyi - A beautiful UNESCO World Heritage Site that boasts stunning natural scenery and hiking trails.
  • Guanzhai Mountain - A mountain that’s famous for its ravishing scenery and outdoor activities
  • Fu’an River - A scenic spot where visitors can enjoy sightseeing, boating, fishing, and more
  • Dragon Cave - A cave network that’s full of natural wonders such as stalactites and stalagmites

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longyan

Longyan has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Xiamei Ancient Trail - A 30km trail that passes through charming villages and eye-catching scenery
  • Guanzhai Mountain - A mountain with well-marked hiking and biking trails. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views and scenic views.
  • White Lotus Mountain - A scenic spot that’s perfect for trekking and off-road biking.
  • Tieshi Mountain - A mountain with stunning views of valleys and peaks.

Best Things to Do in Longyan: Activities and Tours

  • Experience the ancient Chinese culture by visiting Yongding Tulou
  • Relax in the hot springs of Guanzhai Mountain
  • Sample Longyan’s delicious cuisine, including Hakka cuisine with its unique flavors and cooking style.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, boating, fishing, and mountain biking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Longyan

Day 1: Arrive in Longyan and explore the city center Day 2: Visit Wuyi Palace and its surrounding scenic spots Day 3: Explore the Guanzhai Mountain Day 4: Head to the Yongding Tulou and explore the unique architecture of the earth buildings Day 5: Explore the picturesque scenery around the Fu’an River Day 6: Hike or bike through Tieshi Mountain Day 7: Take a day trip to Mount Wuyi and enjoy its scenic beauty

Food and Drink Scene of Longyan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hakka Lei Cha - A famous green tea soup dish made with crushed tea leaves, vegetables, and peanuts
  • Meat and vegetable dumplings - A popular steamed dish filled with pork or mixed vegetables
  • Baozi - Steamed buns stuffed with a range of different fillings
  • Crispy Duck - A crunchy duck dish that’s served with a soy sauce dip

Local Beverages

  • Chinese Rice Wine - A strong and delicious alcoholic drink made from fermented rice
  • Jinjiang Beer - A famous local beer brewed in the nearby city of Jinjiang

Where to Stay in Longyan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Longyan has a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, including luxurious hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and traditional guesthouses. Whether you’re looking for a five-star experience or something more authentic, you’ll find the perfect accommodation in Longyan.

Here are some hotels to consider:

  • Kempinski Hotel Xiamen - A luxurious five-star hotel that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains
  • Days Hotel & Suites Mingdian - A four-star hotel located in the city center, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to attractions
  • 7 Days Inn Longyan West Lake Park - A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms

What to Buy in Longyan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Longyan is famous for its tea, handicrafts, and Hakka snacks. Here are some of the local products to consider bringing home:

  • Longyan tea - A famous tea that’s grown in the local mountainous area
  • Hakka embroidery - A traditional handicraft that includes a range of clothing and accessories
  • Lei Cha powder - A unique tea powder that’s used to make Hakka Lei Cha soup
  • Xianhuang dried bean curd - A traditional Hakka snack

Local Culture and Customs of Longyan

Longyan is home to the Hakka people, a hard-working and resourceful ethnic group with their own unique culture, customs, and dialect. Visitors can experience the Hakka culture through the food, handicrafts, and ancient buildings in the area. One of the most notable things about Hakka customs is that they often use natural materials to construct their buildings, which gives them a unique architectural style that’s recognized around the world.

Best Time to Visit Longyan

The best time to visit Longyan is in spring (March - May) and autumn (September - November) when the weather is comfortable, and the scenery is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longyan

Do:

  • Try the unique Hakka cuisine
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Visit local markets and shops to support local businesses

Don’t:

  • Touch or damage historical buildings
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Insult or demean any local customs or traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Longyan

Longyan is a generally safe city with low crime rates. Visitors should still take common sense precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in Longyan

Visitors should check with their doctors to ensure they have the necessary vaccinations and medications before traveling to China. Travelers should also purchase travel health insurance and take necessary steps to prevent mosquito bites during the warmer months.

Getting to Longyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Longyan is easily accessible by air, bus or train.

Airports Near Longyan

The nearest airport is Longyan Guanzhishan Airport, which operates domestic flights to major cities such as Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Beijing.

Bus Stations Near Longyan

Longyan East Bus Station, Longyan Bingjiang Bus Station, and Longyan North Bus Station all offer regular services to the surrounding cities and tourist attractions.

Other Options

Longyan also has several train stations that provide services to major Chinese cities such as Guangzhou, Shanghai, and Beijing.

How to Get Around in Longyan

Longyan has an extensive public transportation system consisting of buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Longyan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Xiamen - A coastal city known for its beaches, temples, and unique architecture.
  • Fuzhou - A historic city with a rich cultural heritage, featuring sprawling bazaars, towering temples, and some of the best traditional street food in the region.
  • Mount Wuyi - A scenic mountain range and UNESCO World Heritage Site that’s famous for its lush forests, tea plantations, and awe-inspiring scenery.

Conclusion

Longyan offers visitors a unique and authentic experience of Chinese culture, history, and natural beauty. From its earthen fortresses to the picturesque mountain ranges that surround the city, Longyan has something for everyone. With our comprehensive guide, travelers can get the most out of their visit and create memories that will last a lifetime.
