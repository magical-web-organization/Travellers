Jinjiang is a small city located in South Fujian, in the eastern part of China. With a population of just over 1 million people, it’s not as well-known as some of the larger cities in the country, but it’s still a fascinating and culturally rich destination for travelers. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs to keep in mind during your travels to Jinjiang.

Introduction to Jinjiang

Jinjiang is located in the heart of the South Fujian region, which is known for its unique dialect, architecture, and food. The city is situated on the southeast coast of China, and it’s surrounded by mountains on one side and the sea on the other. Jinjiang is a dynamic city, with a mix of modern and traditional buildings, bustling markets, and peaceful parks. It’s also known as the “sportswear capital of China,” as many of the world’s top athletic brands have their factories and headquarters there.

List of Places to Visit in Jinjiang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jinjiang has a rich cultural heritage, and there are many museums and historical sites worth visiting. Here are a few must-see attractions:

  • Jinjiang Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Jinjiang, from its earliest inhabitants to its modern sports industry. It’s a great place to learn about the city’s culture and heritage.
  • Xinjie Ancient Town: This ancient town is located in the heart of Jinjiang, and it’s home to some of the city’s oldest buildings and temples. It’s a great place to wander around and soak up the historic atmosphere.
  • Meizhou Island: This island is located just off the coast of Jinjiang, and it’s home to some of the most beautiful beaches and scenic views in the region. It’s a popular destination for hiking, swimming, and outdoor activities.

Natural Attractions of Jinjiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jinjiang is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are many opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are a few highlights:

  • Meifeng Mountain National Forest Park: This park is located just outside of Jinjiang, and it’s home to some of the most breathtaking views in the region. There are many hiking trails and outdoor activities to enjoy, and it’s a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Jinjiang Baihuayan Scenic Spot: This scenic spot is located in the heart of Jinjiang, and it’s known for its beautiful gardens and historic architecture. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Fuzhen Temple: This temple is located on a hill overlooking Jinjiang, and it offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding landscape. It’s a great place to visit during sunset or sunrise for a truly memorable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jinjiang

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, there are many hiking and biking routes to explore in Jinjiang. Here are a few popular options:

  • Meifeng Mountain Hiking Trail: This trail offers some of the most stunning views in the region, and it’s a great way to explore the natural beauty of Jinjiang.
  • Xianyou Cycling Route: This cycling route takes you through the countryside around Jinjiang, where you’ll see quaint villages, rice paddies, and scenic views of the mountains and sea.
  • Jinjiang Coastal Walk: This walking route follows the coastline of Jinjiang, and it’s a great way to explore the city and see some of its most beautiful landmarks and attractions.

Best Things to Do in Jinjiang: Activities and Tours

Jinjiang has many activities and tours to offer, from cultural experiences to sports and outdoor adventures. Here are a few highlights:

  • Sports Factory Tour: As the “sportswear capital of China,” Jinjiang is home to many famous athletic brands, including Anta, Xtep, and 361 Degrees. You can take a tour of one of these factories to see how they make their products and learn about the history of sports in Jinjiang.
  • Cultural Experiences: Jinjiang has a rich cultural heritage, and there are many opportunities to learn about its traditions and customs. You can take a calligraphy class, learn how to make traditional Fujian tea, or visit a local temple to see how the locals practice their religion.
  • Outdoor Adventures: There are many outdoor activities to enjoy in Jinjiang, including hiking, biking, swimming, and beachcombing. You can explore the natural beauty of the region on your own, or you can join a guided tour to learn about the local flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jinjiang

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Jinjiang, here’s a possible itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Jinjiang and settle into your accommodations. Spend the afternoon exploring the city’s markets and shops.

Day 2: Visit the Jinjiang Museum to learn about the city’s history and culture. In the afternoon, take a trip to Meifeng Mountain National Forest Park for some hiking and outdoor adventure.

Day 3: Explore the streets of Xinjie Ancient Town, and visit some of the city’s oldest temples and buildings. In the afternoon, take a trip to Meizhou Island for some beachcombing and relaxation.

Day 4: Visit the Fuzhen Temple, and take in the stunning views of Jinjiang from its hilltop location. In the afternoon, visit the Baihuayan Scenic Spot for some tranquil gardens and historic architecture.

Day 5: Take a sports factory tour to see how some of the world’s most famous athletic brands make their products. In the afternoon, join a calligraphy class to learn about traditional Chinese writing.

Day 6: Take a cycling tour through the countryside around Jinjiang, and explore some of the region’s quaint villages and rice paddies.

Day 7: Spend your final day in Jinjiang relaxing on the beaches of Meizhou Island or exploring the city’s local markets and shops. Say goodbye to this beautiful and culturally rich city.

Food and Drink Scene of Jinjiang

Jinjiang is known for its unique cuisine, which is a mix of Fujianese and Taiwanese flavors. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bak Kut Teh: A flavorful pork soup dish that originated in Malaysia and Singapore, but is now popular throughout southeast Asia.
  • Oyster Omelette: A crispy omelette filled with fresh oysters and vegetables.
  • Fried Rice Noodles: A simple and delicious dish made from stir-fried rice noodles with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Fujianese Tea: This tea is known for its light and delicate flavor, and it’s often served with dim sum or other small dishes.
  • Qiongzhou Liquor: A local liquor made from fermented rice, which is often served at celebratory events and special occasions.

Local Culture and Customs of Jinjiang

Jinjiang is a city with a strong cultural identity, and there are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind during your travels. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect for Elders: In Chinese culture, elders are highly respected, so it’s important to show deference to older people that you meet.
  • Table Manners: Table manners are very important in China. It’s important to use chopsticks correctly and to avoid making noise while eating.
  • Bargaining: Bargaining is common in markets and shops, so it’s important to negotiate politely and with respect for the vendor.

Best Time to Visit Jinjiang

The best time to visit Jinjiang is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jinjiang

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your travels to Jinjiang:

Do:

  • Research Local Customs: It’s always a good idea to do some research before visiting a new culture or country, so you can be aware of local customs and traditions.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Jinjiang has a unique and delicious cuisine, so make sure to try some of the local dishes during your visit.
  • Be Open-Minded: China has a rich and complex culture, so try to approach your travels with an open mind and a willingness to learn.

Don’t:

  • Touch People’s Heads: In Chinese culture, the head is considered a sacred part of the body, so it’s impolite to touch someone’s head without their permission.
  • Point with Your Finger: Pointing with your finger is impolite in Chinese culture, so use an open hand or nod your head to signal directions or emphasis.
  • Criticize the Government or Culture: Chinese people are proud of their culture and country, so it’s important to avoid criticizing the government or culture during your travels.

Safety and Health

Jinjiang is a relatively safe city for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are a few tips for staying healthy and safe during your visit:

Safety in Jinjiang

  • Stay Alert in Crowded Places: Crowded markets and train stations can be targets for pickpockets and thieves, so keep an eye on your belongings and stay alert in these areas.
  • Avoid Nighttime Travel: It’s generally safer to avoid traveling alone at night, especially in areas that are poorly lit or unfamiliar.
  • Be Aware of Traffic: Chinese traffic can be chaotic and unpredictable, so be cautious when crossing the street and always use designated crosswalks.

Health in Jinjiang

  • Stay Hydrated: The climate in Jinjiang can be hot and humid, so it’s important to drink plenty of fluids to avoid dehydration.
  • Wear Sunscreen: Protection from the sun is important during your travels, especially if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Be Prepared for Air Pollution: Air pollution can be a problem in Chinese cities, including Jinjiang, so consider wearing a mask if you have respiratory problems or sensitivity to pollution.
