Haicang Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour

Haicang is a hidden gem located in South Fujian, China, with a rich culture and beautiful outdoor scenery. Its proximity to Xiamen makes it an excellent destination for a weekend trip or a week-long visit. Whether you’re interested in culture, food, outdoor adventures or just want to relax, Haicang has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best attractions, activities, and food that Haicang has to offer.

Introduction to Haicang

Haicang is a district of Xiamen located on the western side of the Haicang Bay. It’s an important industrial zone of Xiamen and has gained prominence for its economic development. Despite the rapid modernization, Haicang has preserved its ancient culture, beautiful countryside, and traditional way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Haicang

Haicang has a lot to offer for tourists with its diverse attractions ranging from culturally important attractions, scenic natural attractions, to modern shopping centers.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haicang has a rich history that can be seen in its ancient architecture, monuments, and museums. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Haicang Museum: It’s a museum that showcases the history and culture of Haicang. It houses thousands of priceless artifacts and relics, including ceramics, calligraphy, and ancient artworks.
  • Shenshan Temple: It’s a beautiful temple with a history of over 1200 years. It’s dedicated to the Buddhist goddess of mercy and has a magnificent statue of her.
  • Former Residences of Revolutionary Martyrs: This is a collection of historic houses which belonged to the revolutionary martyrs. It serves as a reminder of the struggles and sacrifices of the revolutionaries.

Natural Attractions of Haicang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Haicang is blessed with a beautiful countryside and scenic views. There are plenty of outdoor activities for adventure lovers, such as hiking, biking, and water sports. Some of the most famous natural attractions are:

  • Jiulongkou Scenic Area: It’s a beautiful scenic area located in the northeast of Haicang. It has several waterfalls, rivers, and streams with crystal clear water. You can even indulge in water sports like rafting and kayaking here.
  • Wutong Hill: This hill is a popular hiking spot and is the highest point in Xiamen. You can enjoy panoramic views of the city and surrounding countryside from the top.
  • Fantian Temple: This temple is nestled in the mountains and offers a peaceful and serene environment for relaxation and meditation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haicang

Haicang has several hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. The scenic beauty of the countryside makes these routes an exciting experience for all. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Jiulongkou Scenic Area Hiking Trail: It’s a 6km trail that takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery of the area.
  • Wutong Hill Hiking Trail: It’s a 7km trail that takes you to the top of the Wutong Hill. It’s a bit steep, but the panoramic view from the top makes it worth it.
  • Sanya Bay Biking Trail: This is a 17km trail that takes you along the beautiful coastline of Haicang. It’s an excellent way to explore Haicang’s natural beauty and enjoy the sea breeze.

Best Things to Do in Haicang: Activities and Tours

Haicang has several activities and tours to cater to different interests. Some of the best things to do in Haicang are:

  • Explore Haicang’s food scene: Haicang is famous for its sea-food delicacies, such as fish balls, shrimp dumplings, and fish soup. The cuisine is a blend of Fuzhou and Xiamen flavors, which creates a unique taste.
  • Take a boat ride: A boat ride is a popular way of exploring Haicang’s beautiful coastline and natural beauty. You can enjoy the sunset along the way.
  • Visit local markets: Haicang has many local markets that sell fresh seafood, vegetables, and fruits. These markets offer a glimpse into the local way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haicang

Haicang is an excellent destination for a week-long trip. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Haicang and check-in to your hotel. Spend the day exploring Haicang’s food scene.

Day 2: Visit Shenshan Temple, Haicang Museum, and Fantian Temple. Enjoy the beautiful views and learn about the culture and history of Haicang.

Day 3: Take a boat ride along the coastline and enjoy the sunset. Go shopping in the evening in downtown Haicang.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Jiulongkou Scenic Area and enjoy water sports like rafting and kayaking.

Day 5: Explore Wutong Hill and visit the Former Residences of Revolutionary Martyrs.

Day 6: Take a bike ride along the Sanya Bay Biking Trail and soak in the beautiful views of the coastline.

Day 7: Depart from Haicang, and head back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Haicang

Haicang’s food scene is a blend of Fuzhou and Xiamen cuisines. Some of the must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Soup: It’s a local delicacy made with fresh fish and vegetables. Its aroma and taste are fragrant, and it’s a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Fried Oysters: Haicang’s fried oysters are crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside. They are served with chili sauce, which adds a spicy kick to the flavor.
  • Shrimp Dumplings: Haicang’s shrimp dumplings are made with fresh shrimps and pork wrapped in thin dough. They are steam cooked and served with soy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pu Erh Tea: This is a local tea that’s well-known for its medicinal properties. It’s a fermented tea that aids digestion and has a rich flavor.
  • Rice Wine: Rice Wine is a traditional alcoholic beverage that’s brewed with glutinous rice. It has a strong aroma and a subtle sweetness.

Where to Stay in Haicang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Haicang, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular ones are:

  • Pullman Xiamen Powerlong: It’s a luxurious hotel that’s conveniently located in downtown Haicang. Its state-of-the-art amenities make it a perfect stay for business and leisure travelers.
  • Xiamen Impression Hostel: It’s a budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Haicang. Its clean rooms and friendly staff make it a popular choice among backpackers.
  • You Pin Hostel: It’s a stylish and eco-friendly hostel that’s located in downtown Haicang. Its garden and rooftop terrace provide a peaceful retreat for relaxation.

What to Buy in Haicang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Haicang is famous for its handicrafts, which are perfect as souvenirs. You can buy these at local markets or souvenir shops. Some of the items to buy are:

  • Tea Sets: Haicang has some of the most beautiful tea sets that are perfect for tea lovers.
  • Handmade Crafts: You can buy handmade crafts like pottery, calligraphy, and embroidery, which make excellent souvenirs.
  • Seafood: Haicang’s seafood is fresh and delicious. You can buy some from the local markets and bring it home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Haicang

Haicang has a rich culture and history that can be seen in its customs and way of life. The local people are friendly and hospitable and are proud of their heritage. Some of the customs and etiquettes that you should follow are:

  • Greetings: The locals greet each other with a simple “ni hao” or “hello.” It’s respectful to use their formal titles when addressing them.
  • Table Manners: The locals eat with chopsticks and use a spoon for soup. It’s polite to finish all the food in your bowl.
  • Business Meeting Etiquettes: It’s customary to exchange business cards during a meeting. It’s polite to use both hands when receiving or giving the card.

Best Time to Visit Haicang

The best time to visit Haicang is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the natural scenery is at its best. The summer months can be hot and humid, and the winter months can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haicang

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in outdoor activities.
  • Learn some basic Mandarin phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or behave inappropriately.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Take photographs of people or places without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haicang

Haicang is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s important to take some precautions to ensure safety.

  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas and tourist spots.
  • Follow traffic rules while driving or cycling.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure in your hotel room.

Health in Haicang

Haicang’s medical facilities are well-equipped and meet international standards. However, it’s advisable to take some precautions for good health.

  • Drink bottled water to avoid water-borne diseases.
  • Take necessary vaccinations before traveling.
  • Carry a first-aid kit with you.

Getting to Haicang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Haicang can be easily reached by air, train, or bus.

Airports Near Haicang

  • Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport: It’s the nearest airport to Haicang, located approximately 12km away.

Bus Stations Near Haicang

  • Xiamen Bus Station: It’s the main bus station in Xiamen and is located approximately 16km away from Haicang.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Xiamen and are a convenient way of reaching Haicang.
  • Trains: Xiamen Railway Station has trains that connect to major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Haicang

Haicang is a small district and can be easily explored on foot. However, there are several transportation options available.

  • Taxis: Taxis are easily available and are a convenient way of getting around Haicang.
  • Buses: Haicang has an extensive bus network that connects to other parts of Xiamen.
  • Bicycles: Bicycles can be rented from local shops and are a great way of exploring Haicang’s countryside.

Where to Go Next from Haicang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Haicang’s proximity to Xiamen and other nearby cities makes it an excellent base for further exploration. Some of the nearby destinations are:

  • Gulangyu Island: It’s a small island located in Xiamen and is a UNESCO world heritage site.
  • Quanzhou: It’s a historic town located approximately 80km away from Haicang and is famous for its ancient architecture.

Conclusion

Haicang is a hidden gem in South Fujian, China, with a rich culture and beautiful outdoor scenery. It’s an excellent destination for a weekend trip or a week-long visit. Its diverse attractions ranging from culturally important attractions, scenic natural attractions, to modern shopping centers make it a perfect getaway for all ages.
