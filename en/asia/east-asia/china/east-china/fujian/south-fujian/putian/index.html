Introduction to Putian

Located in the South Fujian area of China, Putian is a charming destination that boasts a rich cultural and natural heritage. It is best known for its notable historical landmarks, beautiful temples, and fascinating museums. Putian also has some fantastic outdoor sights to explore, including scenic hiking routes, picturesque beaches, and verdant parks. In this comprehensive travel guide of Putian, we will take a closer look at all the top attractions in this city, as well as handy travel tips, and a list of the best things to do and see.

List of Places to Visit in Putian

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Putian Hanjiang Building: Discover China’s ancient architecture at this shrine, which dates back to 1136. The building’s unique design and intricate carvings speak to the architectural skill of the Song Dynasty. The inner courtyard and main hall are both worth a visit.
  • Meizhou Island: This island is steeped in history, and visitors can still see many of the structures built in previous times. There are temples, mansions, and ancestral halls, and some are over 500 years old.
  • Mazu Temple: This temple is an important place of worship for Taoists, and it is dedicated to the goddess of the sea. The Mazu Temple is one of the oldest in China and is an excellent representation of traditional Chinese architecture.
  • Putian Tianhou Palace: This palace was built around the 7th century, and it is one of the oldest and most historically significant buildings in Putian. The Tianhou Palace is a must-visit for anyone interested in Chinese history.
  • Putian Chenghuang Temple: Chenghuang Temple was built during the reign of Emperor Qianlong. The architecture here is traditional, with large curved roofs, and the space is decorated with stunning dragons, lions, and other Chinese motifs.

Natural Attractions of Putian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Qinglanjiao Mountain Scenic Area: At nearly 1000 meters tall, Qinglanjiao Mountain is a great hiking spot for those who prefer a secluded view of the forest. You’ll find various routes that offer some epic views of the surrounding area.
  • Nanshan Mountain Scenic Area: This Scenic area is 13 km from Putian City, and it’s a nature lover’s dream. At 830 meters tall, Nanshan Mountain is Putian’s highest peak. The area boasts a botanical garden, waterfall, caves, temples, and scenic viewpoints.
  • Sizhou Tower Park: This park is steeped in history, and there are many architectural masterpieces to admire, including the Sizhou Tower, which is nearly 1,200 years old. Visitors can take a relaxing stroll or enjoy a picnic here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Putian

Putian offers several fantastic hiking and biking routes. The best options include the Qinglanjiao Mountain Scenic Area and Nanshan Mountain Scenic Area. Both of these areas have designated hiking trails, with varying levels of difficulty. Some trails are easier and perfect for beginners or families with children, while others require some experience and are more challenging.

Best Things to Do in Putian: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Museums: Putian has some great museums that offer insights into China at its best. The Putian Museum and the Putian City Museum are both worth visiting.
  • Go to the Beach: Xianyou Golden Beach is the perfect spot to soak up sea views and relax by the water’s edge.
  • Explore the Temples: Putian is home to many incredible temples, including the Mazu Temple and the Chenghuang Temple.
  • Get Your Cultural Fix: Gain insight into Putian’s culture by attending a traditional performance at the Putian Theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Putian

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Putian:

Day 3: Xianyou Golden Beach

Day 4: Sizhou Tower Park and Putian Hanjiang Building

Food and Drink Scene of Putian

Must-Try Dishes

  • Drunken crab: This dish is unique to the southern Fujian region, and it is made by marinating fresh crabs in Chinese liquor. The dish is known for its texture, flavor, and aroma and is considered a delicacy in Putian.

  • Fatt Kueh: A breakfast food that has been around in China for over 1000 years. It is a savory rice cake with various fillings like red pork, peanuts, and chives.

  • Sweet Mung Bean Soup: A popular dessert in the region usually made with Mung beans, sugar, and pandan leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Putian coffee: This type of coffee is made from a unique strain of coffee beans grown in the mountains around Putian. The coffee has a distinct taste that’s reminiscent of chocolate, and it’s a must-try for coffee lovers.
  • Putian Huangjiu (Yellow wine): This is a famous type of rice wine in Putian, and it is intended as either a beverage or cooking ingredient.

Where to Stay in Putian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Putian offers several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Crowne Plaza Putian: A 5-star hotel that offers luxurious accommodations.
  • Putian Yijia Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that provides comfortable rooms great for families or groups.
  • Putian Small Bottle Inn: This hostel is clean and modern, and it offers affordable accommodations for backpackers.

What to Buy in Putian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Putian Peanut Candy: A popular local snack made with fresh peanuts, sugar, and maltose. The eater is crunchy and a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.
  • Putian Wūlóng Tea: Oolong tea is famous in Fujian province, and Putian is no exception. Wulong tea is robust and has a rich flavor.
  • Handwoven Rush Mats: These mats are made of natural woven grass, and they are used for sleeping or sitting.

Local Culture and Customs of Putian

  • Honesty: Putian’s locals are known for being trustworthy and have high moral standards, making Putian a safe place for visitors.

  • Traditional Festivals: Putian celebrates various traditional festivals throughout the year. Make sure you check the calendar to see if any events are taking place during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Putian

The best time to visit Putian is between September and November, when the weather is mild, and rainfall is reasonable. This is perfect for those who wish to explore the city’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Putian

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of people in Putian.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take your time and enjoy the city’s slower pace of life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful.
  • Don’t litter or damage historical landmarks.
  • Don’t negotiate prices unless you are purchasing from a street vendor.

Safety and Health

Safety in Putian

Putian is known for being a safe place to visit. However, it’s always useful to take basic safety precautions while traveling, including keeping an eye on personal belongings and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Putian

Putian has a relatively clean and hygienic environment. Visitors should still take standard precautions like washing hands, drinking bottled water, and wearing sunblock.

Getting to Putian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Putian

The closest airport to Putian is the Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport. The airport is around 130 kilometers from Putian, so visitors can take a bus or car rental to get into the city center.

Bus Stations Near Putian

Putian has several bus stations that offer services serving nearby cities, including Fuzhou, Xiamen, and Quanzhou.

Other Options

Trains are a popular way to get around China, and Putian has its train station that connects Putian with other major cities like Fuzhou, Xiamen, and Shenzhen.

How to Get Around in Putian

Putian’s public transportation system is comprehensive and affordable. Buses, taxis, and rental cars are all available for getting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Putian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Xiamen: A charming coastal city famous for its beaches, Gulangyu Island, and Buddhist temples.
  • Quanzhou: A city famous for its historic architecture and rich cultural heritage.
  • Fuzhou: This capital of Fujian has a mix of ancient historic sites, parks, and museums.

Conclusion

Putian is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in China’s rich cultural and natural heritage. The city is filled with fascinating landmarks and beautiful scenery, making it perfect for those looking for a peaceful getaway. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next trip to Putian, China, and enjoy all this fantastic city has to offer.
