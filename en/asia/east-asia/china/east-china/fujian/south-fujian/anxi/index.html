Introduction to Anxi

Anxi is a county located in the south of Fujian province, China. Famous for its tea and porcelain, Anxi has also become a popular tourist destination due to its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Visitors can spend days exploring the unique blend of old and new, traditional and modern, urban and rural that defines this charming county. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Anxi.

List of Places to Visit in Anxi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anxi Museum

The Anxi Museum displays a wide range of artifacts and exhibits, including traditional Anxi tea-making tools, rare pottery, and an extensive collection of calligraphy and paintings. The museum provides a fascinating insight into the history and culture of Anxi county and its people.

Sanmu Temple

One of the most significant Buddhist temples in the region, Sanmu Temple, is tucked away amid the lush greenery of Anxi. With its intricate carvings, golden Buddhas, and surreal atmosphere, the temple provides a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Natural Attractions of Anxi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Junxi

The Mount Junxi nature reserve is a must-see destination for nature enthusiasts. Located in the heart of Anxi, it provides visitors with stunning views of the surrounding mountain ranges, colorful flora, and fauna, and peaceful trekking trails.

Qingshuiyan Scenic Spot

The Qingshuiyan Scenic Spot is also a popular destination for those seeking to soak in the natural beauty of Anxi. The lush forest, rocky cliffs, and winding streams provide a perfect backdrop for photography enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anxi

Anxi county is famous for its biking and hiking routes, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the beautiful countryside of Fujian. Popular routes include the Sanmu Temple Route, Jianlin Stream Route, Tea Route, and Xishan Route.

Best Things to Do in Anxi: Activities and Tours

Tea Plantations

Anxi is renowned for its tea plantations, and a tour of the tea fields is a must-see experience. Visitors can learn about the tea-making process from picking the leaves to brewing the perfect cup of tea. One can even enjoy a tea ceremony during their visit to Anxi.

Village Tour

A village tour in Anxi is an incredible way to learn about the local culture and lifestyle of Anxi county’s people. There are many small villages in Anxi that provide unique glimpses into rural China.

Food and Drink Scene of Anxi

Anxi offers a unique culinary experience, with its delicious local cuisine and an extensive range of beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Tie Guan Yin Tea-Flavored Chicken

Tie Guan Yin Tea-Flavored Chicken is a local specialty popular among visitors to Anxi. The chicken is marinated with Tie Guan Yin tea and rocksugar to create a unique and savory flavor that delights the taste buds.

Guanyin Tea

This special tea is among the most popular products of the town. Visitors can taste the tea in various ways as locals offer different varieties and different ways of preparation.

Local Beverages

Liushen

Liushen, a kind of liquor is a popular drink of Anxi, made of rice and flavoured with the root of Chinese herbal medicine Galangal.

Where to Stay in Anxi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anxi has a wide range of accommodation options for visitors with different budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from spacious hotels, cozy guesthouses, or budget-friendly hostels. Some of the most popular options include the Sino Swiss Hotel, Shidao Inn, and Anxi Junyi Hostel.

What to Buy in Anxi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anxi is renowned for its tea and porcelain, making them popular souvenirs to buy when visiting the town. Visitors can also purchase beautiful handcrafted pottery, traditional Chinese clothes, local snacks and other items from local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Anxi

Anxi has a rich cultural heritage that can be seen in its architecture, art, and traditions. The locals are deeply respectful of their history and traditions and are eager to share them with visitors. Visitors can witness traditional tea ceremonies, dragon dances, and other celebrations that play an essential part in the cultural fabric of the town.

Best Time to Visit Anxi

The best time to visit Anxi is during the autumn months, from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the autumn foliage adds to the stunning natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anxi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Mandarin to communicate with the locals better.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards the locals or their customs and beliefs.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Speak too loudly or disturb the peace of the surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anxi

Anxi is generally a safe location, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid becoming a victim of theft or pickpocketing. It is also essential to be mindful of traffic when walking or cycling on the roads.

Health in Anxi

Visitors should take the usual health precautions when visiting Anxi. It is advisable to drink bottled water, avoid street food, and carry sunscreen in the hot summer months.

Getting to Anxi: Transportation Options, and Logistics

Airports Near Anxi

The closest airport to Anxi is Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport, which is approximately 100 km away from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Anxi.

Bus Stations Near Anxi

Anxi has two bus stations: Anxi Bus Station and Anxi Passenger Bus Station. Buses from other major cities in the region, such as Xiamen, Zhangzhou, and Quanzhou, arrive at both stations regularly.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Quanzhou from major cities in the region, and then take a bus to Anxi.

How to Get Around in Anxi

Visitors to Anxi can rent a bicycle or a car to explore the town and its surroundings. Taxis are also available for hire, although visitors should ensure the driver uses the meter or agrees on a fare beforehand to avoid overcharging.

Where to Go Next from Anxi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anxi is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding regions of Fujian province. Visitors can explore the historic port city of Xiamen, visit the UNESCO world heritage site of Tulou, or explore the stunning coastal landscapes of Quanzhou.

Conclusion

Anxi offers a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors who want to explore the natural and cultural hidden gem of south Fujian. With its stunning scenery, rich heritage, and charming locals, Anxi provides an immersive experience that leaves a lasting impression on visitors. Plan your trip to Anxi today and discover the magic of this vibrant oasis in Fujian, China.
