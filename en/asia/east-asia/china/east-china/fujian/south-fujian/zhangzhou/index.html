Introduction to Zhangzhou

Zhangzhou is a quaint city situated in South Fujian province, Eastern China. The city is steeped in rich history and culture, which makes it a popular destination amongst tourists visiting China. Zhangzhou boasts a plethora of attractions, including stunning natural landscapes, centuries-old architecture and ancient relics.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhangzhou

Zhangzhou is famous for its rich culture and customs. Most locals are involved in pottery and weaving, and the art of producing ceramic wares has been passed down to generations of people in this city. The city is also known for its numerous temples and ancient buildings, which are treasured by locals and visitors alike.

Best Time to Visit Zhangzhou

The best time to visit Zhangzhou is during autumn and spring, between September and November and March through May, respectively. During these seasons, temperatures are mild, and the scenery is at its most picturesque.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhangzhou

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try out the delicious local cuisine.
  3. Wear comfortable and appropriate clothing when visiting temples and other holy sites.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Take photos of people without their permission.
  3. Enter holy sites without permission or taking photos when it is not allowed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhangzhou

Zhangzhou is generally a safe city, but it is always wise to be cautious when traveling. Tourists should beware of pickpockets in crowded areas and keep their valuables safe.

Health in Zhangzhou

Tourists should carry sufficient health and travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. Tap water in China is not safe for drinking, so bottled water is recommended.

Getting to Zhangzhou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhangzhou

  1. Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport: Located 57 km away from Zhangzhou.
  2. Quanzhou Jinjiang International Airport: Located 77 km away from Zhangzhou.
  3. Fuzhou Changle International Airport: Located 94 km away from Zhangzhou.

Bus Stations Near Zhangzhou

  1. Zhangzhou Bus Station
  2. Xiamen Bus Station

Other Options

Tourists can also take the train to Zhangzhou from either Fuzhou or Xiamen.

How to Get Around in Zhangzhou

Zhangzhou boasts an efficient transport system, and tourists can travel around the city via taxis, buses, and hired cars. The city also has well-built cycling paths, where tourists can rent bikes to move around and enjoy the scenic views.

Where to Go Next from Zhangzhou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Xiamen Island
  2. Mount Wuyi
  3. Longyan Tianzhu Mountain National Forest Park

Conclusion

Zhangzhou is a city bursting with culture, history, and natural beauty. Visitors will be spoilt for choice when it comes to things to do and see. With so many attractions, including ancient temples, natural parks, and hiking trails, tourists will undoubtedly have a memorable time exploring this Chinese gem.
