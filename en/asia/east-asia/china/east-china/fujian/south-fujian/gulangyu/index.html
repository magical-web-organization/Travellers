If you’re looking for a unique travel experience in China, put Gulangyu Island on your list. This small island near Xiamen boasts several cultural and natural attractions, stunning views, delicious food, and a laid-back atmosphere. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you all the best places to explore, eat, sleep, and visit in Gulangyu Island to make the most out of your trip.

Introduction to Gulangyu

Gulangyu Island, also known as “Piano Island,” is a small car-free island located just off the coast of Xiamen City, in the Fujian Province of China. The island is a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site due to its unique architecture and historical significance. Gulangyu has a rich history as a trading port, a colonial enclave, and a center for art and music. Today, it’s a popular destination for both domestic and foreign tourists seeking a peaceful and picturesque getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Gulangyu

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Gulangyu Island to learn about its culture and history, appreciate its natural beauty, and have a fun time:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gulangyu Piano Museum: This museum showcases a collection of antique pianos and other Western music instruments dating back to the 19th century. Visitors can learn about the history of piano music in China and enjoy live performances by professional pianists.
  • Gulangyu Museum of History and Culture: This museum exhibits the history and culture of Gulangyu Island, including its architecture, arts, and people. It’s located in a restored colonial house and features a beautiful garden.
  • Sunlight Rock: This scenic spot is the highest point on Gulangyu Island and offers panoramic views of Xiamen City and Taiwan Strait. There’s also a historical pavilion and a temple on the rock.

Natural Attractions of Gulangyu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shuzhuang Garden: This tranquil garden is built around a natural rock formation and features a picturesque lake, bridges, pavilions, and colorful flowers. It’s a perfect place for a peaceful stroll or a picnic.
  • Gulangyu Beaches: There are several small but charming beaches on Gulangyu Island where you can sunbathe, swim, and soak up the sea breeze. Some popular ones include Sunlight Rock Beach, Gangzaihou Beach, and Neicuoao Beach.
  • Riguangyan Scenic Area: This scenic area is located on the southeastern coast of Gulangyu and features a rugged coastline, a lighthouse, and spectacular rock formations. It’s a great place for hiking, bird-watching, and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gulangyu

One of the best ways to explore Gulangyu Island is on foot or by bike. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Island Loop Trail: This trail circles the entire island and covers about 7 km. It takes you through various neighborhoods, parks, and scenic spots. You can rent a bike or walk at your own pace.
  • Zhongshan Road Art Street: This street is a famous shopping and dining destination on Gulangyu Island. You can stroll along the colorful alleyways and discover unique souvenirs, handicrafts, and snacks.
  • Riguangyan Scenic Area Trail: This trail is a challenging hike that takes you through the rugged coastline, past the lighthouse, and up to the peak of the scenic area. It offers stunning views and a sense of adventure.

Best Things to Do in Gulangyu: Activities and Tours

Gulangyu Island has plenty of fun activities and tours to offer. Here are some ideas:

  • Take a Guided Music Tour: Gulangyu Island has a rich musical history and is home to many talented musicians. You can join a guided tour to learn about the island’s music scene, visit music schools and studios, and even try playing the piano yourself.
  • Attend a Cultural Show: There are several venues on Gulangyu Island that host traditional music and dance shows, such as the Hakka Museum and the Shuzhuang Garden Theatre. You can enjoy a colorful and lively performance while savoring local snacks.
  • Try Local Snacks: Gulangyu Island is famous for its snacks, such as peanut soup, fried oysters, and sesame cakes. You can find many small food stalls and restaurants on the island that serve these treats, often with a unique twist.
  • Go Shopping: Gulangyu Island has many souvenir shops, street vendors, and boutiques that sell local crafts, jewelry, clothes, and artwork. You can find one-of-a-kind items that reflect the character of the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gulangyu

If you have a week to spare, you can follow this itinerary to explore the best of Gulangyu Island:

Day 1: Arrive in Gulangyu, settle in your accommodation, and explore the nearby neighborhoods. Day 2: Visit some of the cultural attractions of Gulangyu, such as the Piano Museum, the Museum of History and Culture, and the Sunlight Rock. Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking tour of the island, such as the Island Loop Trail or the Zhongshan Road Art Street. Day 4: Relax on one of the beaches, such as Sunlight Rock Beach, Gangzaihou Beach, or Neicuoao Beach. Day 5: Take a guided music tour or attend a cultural show, and sample some local snacks. Day 6: Go shopping for souvenirs and local products, and take some scenic photos. Day 7: Depart from Gulangyu Island and continue your travels.

Food and Drink Scene of Gulangyu

Gulangyu Island has a unique food and drink scene that reflects its multicultural history and coastal location. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peanut Soup: This sweet and warm soup is a traditional snack from southern Fujian. It’s made with peanuts, glutinous rice, and sugar, and is often served with crispy fried dough sticks.
  • Fried Oysters: This crispy and savory dish is a popular snack from Taiwan and is commonly found on Gulangyu Island. It’s made by coating fresh oysters with flour and egg, and deep-frying them until golden brown.
  • Sesame Cake: This soft and fragrant cake is a local specialty from Gulangyu Island. It’s made with glutinous rice flour, sesame paste, and sugar, and is often eaten as a breakfast or dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Seaweed Soup: This savory and nutritious soup is made with seaweed, tomatoes, eggs, and various spices. It’s a popular soup on Gulangyu Island and is said to have medicinal properties.
  • Oolong Tea: This fragrant and rich tea is one of the most famous teas in China, and is often grown in Fujian Province. You can find many tea shops and teahouses on Gulangyu Island that serve high-quality oolong tea.

Where to Stay in Gulangyu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options on Gulangyu Island that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended choices:

  • Gulangyu Jiahe Hotel: This elegant and cozy hotel is located in the heart of Gulangyu Island and offers spacious rooms with antique decor, modern amenities, and free Wi-Fi. It also has a garden and a restaurant.
  • Jinshiwan Guesthouse: This budget-friendly guesthouse is situated in a quiet neighborhood on Gulangyu Island, and features clean and comfortable rooms with air conditioning and private bathrooms. It also has a terrace and a shared kitchen.
  • Mingfa Hotel: This luxury hotel is located on the seaside of Gulangyu Island and boasts panoramic views of the sea and Xiamen City. It offers stylish and spacious rooms and suites with a private balcony or terrace, a spa, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Gulangyu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gulangyu Island is a great place to shop for unique and meaningful souvenirs that showcase its culture and history. Here are some ideas:

  • Handicrafts: You can find many handmade crafts on Gulangyu Island, such as paper-cutting, embroidery, and wood-carving. These crafts often depict traditional scenes, such as dragons, phoenixes, and landscapes.
  • Minnan Prints: Minnan prints are a type of traditional art from the southern Fujian region, which features colorful and intricate patterns of flowers, animals, and people. You can find many Minnan-printed clothes, scarves, bags, and accessories on Gulangyu Island.
  • Tea: Fujian Province is known for its high-quality tea, especially oolong tea. You can visit a tea shop or tea house on Gulangyu Island to sample and buy different types of tea, and learn about the tea-making process.

Local Culture and Customs of Gulangyu

Gulangyu Island has a diverse and vibrant culture that reflects its historical and geographical features. Here are some key aspects of the local culture:

  • Music and Art: Gulangyu Island has a long history of music and art, and is home to many renowned musicians, painters, and sculptors. You can enjoy live music performances, visit art galleries, and attend cultural events on the island.
  • Architecture: Gulangyu Island has a unique architectural style that blends Chinese, Western, and Southeast Asian elements. Many of the buildings on the island are historic mansions, churches, and schools, that are open to the public.
  • Multiculturalism: Gulangyu Island has been a melting pot of different cultures, religions, and languages throughout its history. You can find influences from China, Europe, Japan, and Taiwan in the food, fashion, and customs of the island.

Best Time to Visit Gulangyu

The best time to visit Gulangyu Island is from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. Spring (March to May) is also a good season, with blooming flowers and fewer tourists. Avoid visiting Gulangyu Island during Chinese holidays, such as National Day (October 1-7) and Spring Festival (usually in January or February), when the island can become packed and noisy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gulangyu

To have a pleasant and respectful trip to Gulangyu Island, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Follow the local dress code and etiquette, and avoid behaviors that are considered offensive or illegal, such as littering, smoking in public places, and photographing people without permission.
  • Try Local Food and Drinks: Embrace the local flavors and cuisines, and try new dishes and drinks that you won’t find elsewhere.
  • Support Local Businesses: Buy souvenirs and products from local vendors, and tip generously for good service.
  • Be Curious and Open-Minded: Learn about the local culture, history, and lifestyle, and engage with the locals respectfully.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Environment: Keep the island clean and tidy, and don’t damage or remove any natural or cultural resources.
  • Cause Noise or Disturbance: Respect the local residents’ privacy and quietness, and avoid making loud noises or playing music in public areas.
  • Violate Laws or Regulations: Obey the local laws and regulations, and don’t engage in any illegal or unethical activities, such as drug use or gambling.
  • Underestimate the Weather: Always check the weather forecast and prepare appropriate clothing and gear, especially during typhoon season (July to September).

Safety and Health

Gulangyu Island is generally safe and clean, but it’s always wise to take precautions and be aware of potential risks.

Safety in Gulangyu

  • Watch Your Belongings: Be vigilant with your belongings in public areas, especially in crowded places and on public transportation.
  • Be Careful on Uneven Surfaces: Some parts of Gulangyu Island’s streets and alleys can be uneven or slippery, so be careful when walking or biking.
  • Pay Attention to Traffic: Although there are no cars on the island, there are plenty of bicycles and electric scooters, so watch for them when crossing the streets.

Health in Gulangyu

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially in hot and humid weather, to avoid dehydration and heat stroke.
  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: Wear sunscreen, a hat, and lightweight clothing to avoid sunburn and heat exhaustion.
  • Avoid Tap Water: Don’t drink tap water on Gulangyu Island. Instead, buy bottled water or use a water purifier.

Getting to Gulangyu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gulangyu Island is accessible only by boat or ferry, as there are no cars or motorized vehicles on the island. Here are some transportation options you can consider:

  • Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport: This is the nearest major airport to Gulangyu Island, and is located about 15 km away from the ferry terminals. You can take a taxi or a shuttle bus from the airport to the ferry terminals.
  • Wutong Ferry Terminal and Dongdu Cruise Terminal: These two terminals are the main departure points from Xiamen City to Gulangyu Island. They offer frequent ferry services from early morning till late evening, with a round trip ticket costing around CNY 35-50.
  • Other Options: You can also take a private boat or a water taxi to Gulangyu Island, depending on your budget and schedule.

How to Get Around in Gulangyu

Since there are no cars or taxis on Gulangyu Island, the main modes of transportation are by foot, by bicycle, or by electric scooter. You can rent bikes or scooters at many rental shops on the island, with prices ranging from CNY 10-50 per hour or CNY 40-100 per day. You can also use a map or a smartphone app to navigate your way around Gulangyu Island easily.

Where to Go Next from Gulangyu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Gulangyu Island, you can continue your trip to some nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Xiamen City: Xiamen City is a modern and vibrant metropolis that offers a great contrast to the peaceful and historic Gulangyu Island. You can visit attractions such as Nanputuo Temple, Xiamen University, and Zhongshan Road Pedestrian Street.
  • Kinmen Island: Kinmen Island is a small island located just off the coast of the Fujian Province of China, but is a part of Taiwan. It’s known for its military history, traditional architecture, and scenic beaches. You can take a ferry from Xiamen City to Kinmen Island and explore the island for a day or two.
  • Wuyi Mountain: Wuyi Mountain is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in the northern part of Fujian Province. It’s famous for its natural scenery, such as the Nine Bend River, the Water Curtain Cave, and the Immortal’s Cave. You can take a train or a bus from Xiamen City to Wuyi Mountain and spend a few days exploring the national park.

Conclusion

Gulangyu Island is a hidden gem in China that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and relaxation. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re equipped with all the essential information and tips to plan your trip to Gulangyu Island, and create unforgettable memories. Get ready to embark on a journey of discovery and delight, and immerse yourself in the magic of Piano Island.
