Introduction to Jimei

Located in South Fujian of East China, Jimei is a culturally rich city filled with fascinating landmarks and attractions. Known as the hometown of Tan Kah Kee, a famous Chinese philanthropist, the city offers visitors a blend of ancient Chinese architecture, stunning natural scenery, and modern amenities.

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or local culture and cuisine, Jimei has something to offer for every traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll highlight the must-visit attractions, the best things to do in Jimei, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Jimei

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jimei School Village

Jimei School Village, also known as Jimei Xuecun, is a historic complex consisting of ten buildings. The complex was built by Tan Kah Kee, who wanted to provide education for poor students in the region. The architecture of the buildings combines Chinese and western design elements, creating a unique cultural landscape that’s both beautiful and educational.

Jiageng Park

Another notable attraction in Jimei is Jiageng Park, dedicated to the memory of Tan Kah Kee’s son, Jiageng. The park comprises several gardens, including a bamboo forest, a lotus pond, and a rose garden. Visitors can also see the towering statue of Jiageng in the center of the park, which is the perfect spot for taking stunning photographs.

Former Residence of Tan Kah Kee

The Former Residence of Tan Kah Kee is a must-visit for history buffs. Tan Kah Kee lived in the house from 1919 to 1939, and the house has since been converted into a museum to showcase his life and contributions. The museum has three floors, one dedicated to Tan Kah Kee’s humanitarian work, another displaying historical artifacts, and a third detailing the local culture.

Natural Attractions of Jimei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jimei Dragon Boat Pond

Jimei Dragon Boat Pond is a great place to experience local sports and culture. Visitors can watch dragon boat races or participate in them during the Dragon Boat Festival. Additionally, there’s a park next to the pond perfect for a leisurely stroll or picnic.

Turtle Garden

Turtle Garden is a picturesque park that features winding streams, small bridges, and plenty of trees. Visitors can walk along the trails and enjoy the natural scenery, or feed the turtles and fish that populate the park’s ponds.

Jimei Hill Scenic Spot

For stunning views of the city, head to Jimei Hill Scenic Spot. The park offers incredible panoramic views of Jimei and the surrounding countryside. The park has hiking trails for the adventurous travelers, and a cable car for those who prefer a more leisurely experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jimei

Jimei Hill Scenic Spot Hiking Trail

At Jimei Hill Scenic Spot, there is a 4-kilometer hiking trail that runs through the park. The trail offers fantastic scenic views along the way, including a small river, lake, waterfalls, and rock formations.

Jimei-Xinglin Coastal Cycling Route

The Jimei-Xinglin Coastal Cycling Route is around 20 kilometers long and offers an excellent opportunity for travelers who want to explore the coastal areas. The route offers scenic views of the coast, small villages, and fishing boats.

Best Things to Do in Jimei: Activities and Tours

Take a Day Trip to Gulangyu Island

A famous tourist destination near Jimei is Gulangyu Island, a small car-free island just a short ferry ride away from Xiamen. On Gulangyu, travelers can visit colonial mansions, museums, and unique shops or taste local seafood specialties like oysters and clams.

Take a Food Tour

Jimei has a vibrant street food scene that’s a must-try for any visitor. Take a food tour with a local guide to taste authentic and delicious Fujian cuisine, such as snails, oyster omelets, and fish balls.

Food and Drink Scene of Jimei

Must-Try Dishes

Oyster Omelet

Oyster Omelet is a popular snack in Jimei, made with eggs, oysters, and sweet potato starch. It’s a must-try dish that can be found in almost every local market.

Curry Rice

Curry Rice is a famous Fujianese dish that’s popular in Jimei. The dish consists of pork belly, potatoes, and carrots served over a bed of fluffy rice and flavored with a rich curry sauce.

Local Beverages

South Fujian Tea

South Fujian is famous for its tea, and Jimei is no exception. Visitors can try different varieties of white, green, and oolong tea in the local tea house.

Rice Wine

Rice Wine is a popular beverage in Fujian, and Jimei has some excellent local varieties. The wine is usually made using glutinous rice and is a must-try for anyone who enjoys local brews.

Where to Stay in Jimei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel One Xiamen Jimei

Hotel One Xiamen Jimei is a modern hotel with excellent facilities and a convenient location, close to the attractions of Jimei. The hotel’s rooms are clean, comfortable, and come with quality amenities.

Wutaishan Hotel

Wutaishan Hotel is a beautiful hotel nestled in the hills behind Jimei Hill Scenic Spot. The hotel has stunning views of the mountains and is an ideal spot for travelers who want to experience the natural beauty of the area.

What to Buy in Jimei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jimei Candy

Jimei Candy is a famous local candy made with cane sugar, ginger, and sesame seeds. The candy has a unique flavor that’s a must-try for anyone visiting Jimei.

Local Crafts

Jimei has a vibrant crafts scene, and visitors can find many unique items for sale in the local markets. Popular crafts include hand-carved bamboo products, cloth shoes, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Jimei

Jimei is a multi-ethnic city with a blend of traditional and modern customs. It’s polite to greet locals with a nod or a smile, and visitors should respect local traditions, such as not touching statues or relics in the temples. Additionally, visitors should also dress modestly and cover their shoulders and legs when entering places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Jimei

The best time to visit Jimei is during the spring or autumn seasons, from March to May, and then again from September to November. During these seasons, the weather is comfortable, and rainfall is much less than during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jimei

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Taste the local cuisine
  • Visit the landmarks and attractions

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in public places
  • Touch the statues or relics in the temples
  • Rudely barge into a queue or cut in line

Safety and Health

Safety in Jimei

Jimei is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large sums of money. Also, be careful when crossing the streets as traffic can be hectic.

Health in Jimei

The tap water in Jimei is not safe for drinking, so it’s recommended to use bottled water. Additionally, visitors should also take some insect repellent and wear sunscreen to protect against mosquito bites and sunburn.

Getting to Jimei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jimei

The nearest airport to Jimei is Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Jimei. Alternatively, there’s also a high-speed railway station and a ferry port in Xiamen, which connects to Jimei.

Bus Stations Near Jimei

Jimei Bus Station is the main bus station in the city, and it’s well-connected to other parts of Fujian and China. Visitors can take a bus from Jimei to nearby cities and towns or to other parts of the country.

Other Options

There are also taxis, private cars, and rental bikes available for visitors who want to explore Jimei at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Jimei

The most convenient way to get around Jimei is by taxi or private car. Some visitors may also want to rent a bike and explore the city’s scenic areas. Additionally, there’s also a local bus service which offers routes around Jimei and to other local destinations.

Where to Go Next from Jimei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Jimei, visitors can explore other seaside destinations such as Xiamen, Quanzhou, or the national park of Wuyi Mountain. These destinations offer a unique blend of stunning natural scenery, historical landmarks, and traditional Chinese culture.

Conclusion

Jimei offers visitors a diverse range of experiences, from exploring historic landmarks and museums to hiking and biking through scenic areas. Additionally, visitors can enjoy local foods, shop for souvenirs, and learn about local customs and culture. With so many things to see and do, Jimei is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience the diversity of China.
