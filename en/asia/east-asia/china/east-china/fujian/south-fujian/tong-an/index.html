Introduction to Tong’an

Tong’an is a county-level city situated in South Fujian, Fujian province, China. Located at the foot of Luoxiao Mountain, Tong’an is blessed with lush greenery, gorgeous scenery, and rich cultural heritage. As one of the birthplaces of traditional Minnan culture, Tong’an has numerous historical sites and museums that offer visitors a glimpse into the region’s unique history and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Tong’an

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tong’an Confucian Temple: This ancient temple is dedicated to the great Chinese philosopher Confucius and is considered one of the most well-preserved Confucian temples in southern China. Visitors can admire the intricate carvings and architecture of the temple’s buildings and learn about Confucian philosophy and its influence on Chinese culture.

  • Tong’an Museum: Located in the town center, the Tong’an Museum displays a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the city’s history, art, culture, and folklore. Visitors can see ancient ceramics, calligraphy, paintings, and other cultural relics that tell the story of Tong’an’s past.

  • Tong’an Ancient City: The Tong’an Ancient City is a well-preserved historical site from the Ming Dynasty. Visitors can take a walk on the old city wall and explore the narrow streets and alleyways that are lined with Ming and Qing-style architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tong’an: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Luoxiao Mountain National Forest Park: With an elevation of 1,732 meters (5,682 feet), Luoxiao Mountain is a beautiful natural park that offers spectacular views of Tong’an and its surroundings. The park boasts pristine forests, crystal-clear streams, and waterfalls that are perfect for hiking, trekking, and camping.

  • Haixia Olympic Center: Haixia Olympic Center is a large sports complex that is situated by the coast of Tong’an Bay. Visitors can enjoy various water sports activities such as surfing, kayaking, and swimming at the center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tong’an

Tong’an has numerous hiking trails and biking routes that are suitable for visitors of all fitness levels. The most popular hiking trail is the Luoxiao Mountain Trail, which takes around 4-5 hours to complete and rewards hikers with breathtaking views of the city and its surroundings. Another popular route is the Tong’an Bay Biking Trail, which takes you along the beautiful coastline of Tong’an Bay.

Best Things to Do in Tong’an: Activities and Tours

  • Tong’an Street Food Tour: Take a guided tour of Tong’an’s bustling night markets and sample the city’s mouth-watering street food offerings.

  • Confucian Temple Cultural Experience: Immerse yourself in Confucian culture and traditions with a visit to the Tong’an Confucian Temple. Visitors can participate in various cultural activities such as calligraphy, seal carving, and tea ceremonies.

  • Tong’an Bay Fishing Experience: Experience the local way of life by joining the Tong’an Bay Fishing Experience tour. Visitors can learn traditional fishing methods from local fishermen and catch their own fish.

Food and Drink Scene in Tong’an

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fuzhou Fish Balls: Made with fresh fish, sweet potato starch, and other ingredients, Fuzhou Fish Balls are a popular snack in Tong’an.

  • Buddha Jump Over The Wall: This exotic dish is made with various types of meat, seafood, and vegetables and is slow-cooked in a clay pot for hours.

  • Tong’an Meat Dumplings: These savory dumplings are filled with minced meat, vegetables, and spices, wrapped in thin dough and steamed to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Fujian Oolong Tea: Fujian Oolong Tea is known for its fragrant, fruity, and floral aroma and its smooth and mellow taste.

  • Huangjiu: Huangjiu is a type of traditional Chinese rice wine that is made by fermenting rice, wheat, and other grains. It has a rich, earthy flavor and is often served warm during the winter months.

Where to Stay in Tong’an: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tong’an offers a wide range of accommodations options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Tong’an include:

  • Luyou Hotspring Resort: This luxurious resort is situated in the scenic Luyou Valley and offers guests hot spring baths, spa treatments, and access to hiking trails and other outdoor activities.

  • Tong’an Villager Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located near the Tong’an Ancient City and offers guests a unique experience of living in a traditional Chinese village house.

  • Tong’an Kuxiange Hostel: This hostel is situated in the city center and offers clean and comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Tong’an: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tong’an Lacquerware: Tong’an is known for its traditional lacquerware crafts, which include vases, boxes, and other decorative items.

  • Tong’an Pepper: Tong’an Pepper is a type of chili pepper that is grown locally and used in many traditional dishes in the region.

  • Tong’an Tea: Fujian Oolong Tea is one of the most famous types of tea in China, and Tong’an is a popular region for growing this tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Tong’an

Tong’an is well known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. Many of the region’s cultural practices and customs are deeply rooted in Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering someone’s home, addressing elders with the proper title, and showing respect for Confucius and his teachings.

Best Time to Visit Tong’an

The best time to visit Tong’an is from March to May and from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant. During these months, visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery, outdoor activities, and cultural festivals without being overwhelmed by the heat and crowds of the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tong’an

Do:

  • Learn some basic Mandarin phrases before your trip.

  • Try the local street food and dishes.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect Confucius or other cultural icons.

  • Don’t touch or take things from religious sites without permission.

  • Don’t hike or trek alone in remote areas.

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tong’an

Tong’an is a relatively safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions such as keeping an eye on their valuables and avoiding traveling alone at night.

Health in Tong’an

Tong’an has a good healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the city. Visitors should make sure they have proper medical insurance and take any necessary vaccinations or medications before their trip.

Getting to Tong’an: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tong’an

  • Fuzhou Changle International Airport: This is the nearest international airport to Tong’an, located around 35 kilometers (22 miles) from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tong’an

  • Tong’an Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Tong’an and operates buses to and from other cities in Fujian Province.

Other Options

  • Train: Fuzhou Railway Station is around 45 kilometers (28 miles) from Tong’an and connects to major cities in China.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Tong’an and can be a convenient way to get around the city and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Tong’an

Tong’an has a comprehensive public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and rental bikes. Buses are the most affordable and convenient way to get around the city, while taxis are a good option for those who prefer more privacy and comfort. Visitors can also rent bikes and explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Tong’an: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fuzhou: Fuzhou is the capital city of Fujian Province and is known for its rich history, culture, and cuisine.

  • Wuyi Mountains: The Wuyi Mountains are a UNESCO World Heritage site located around 200 kilometers (124 miles) from Tong’an. The mountain range is famous for its stunning scenery and biodiversity.

  • Changting: Changting is a historic town in Fujian Province that is known for its tulou buildings, which are unique circular structures that were built by the Hakka people.

Conclusion

Tong’an is a hidden gem in the heart of Fujian Province, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a foodie, Tong’an has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and return home with unforgettable memories of this beautiful and charming city.
