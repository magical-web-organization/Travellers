Introduction to Xiamen

Located in South Fujian, in Fujian Province, Xiamen is a coastal city that boasts a rich history, beautiful scenery, and delicious food. It’s known for its colonial architecture, beaches, and the beautiful Gulangyu Island, which has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site. Xiamen is also home to several universities, making it a center for learning and research.

List of Places to Visit in Xiamen

Here are some of the must-visit places in Xiamen:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gulangyu Island: A pedestrian-only island that boasts colonial architecture, beautiful beaches, and several museums and art galleries. It’s also home to the famous Piano Museum.
  • Nanputuo Temple: A Buddhist temple that dates back to the Tang Dynasty. It’s located at the foot of Wulao Peaks and boasts stunning views of Xiamen University and the sea.
  • Hulishan Fortress: A historical fortress that dates back to the Qing Dynasty. It’s located on the southern coast of Xiamen Island and is home to a museum that showcases the history of the fortress and the city.

Natural Attractions of Xiamen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jiageng Park: A beautiful park located on the southeastern coast of Xiamen Island. It boasts stunning views of the sea, the city skyline, and the famous Gulangyu Island.
  • Wanshi Botanical Garden: A large botanical garden that covers an area of 4.93 square kilometers. It’s home to over 6,000 species of plants, making it a great place to explore nature.
  • Xiamen Botanical Garden: Another large botanical garden that covers an area of over 4 square kilometers. It’s located on the eastern slope of Yuandang Lake and is home to several themed gardens, including a bamboo garden, a desert garden, and a palm garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xiamen

  • Xiamen Island Ring Road: A 56-kilometer road that circles Xiamen Island. It’s a popular route for cycling and offers stunning views of the sea, mountains, and city.
  • Yanwu Bridge Cycling Track: A 9.2-kilometer cycling track that spans from the Huli District to Jimei District. It’s a great route for cycling and runs parallel to the Yanwu Bridge.

Best Things to Do in Xiamen: Activities and Tours

  • Ferry to Gulangyu Island: Take a ferry to the famous Gulangyu Island to explore its colonial architecture, beaches, and museums.
  • Food Tours: Xiamen is known for its delicious seafood, so be sure to try some of the local dishes, such as seafood hotpot, oyster omelet, and fried squid.
  • University Tours: Xiamen is home to several universities, including Xiamen University, which is known for its beautiful campus and architecture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xiamen

Day 1: Arrive in Xiamen and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit Gulangyu Island and explore its colonial architecture, beaches, and museums. Day 3: Explore Nanputuo Temple and Hulishan Fortress. Day 4: Go hiking or cycling on Xiamen Island Ring Road or Yanwu Bridge Cycling Track. Day 5: Visit Jiageng Park and Xiamen Botanical Garden. Day 6: Take a university tour. Day 7: Visit Wanshi Botanical Garden or take a food tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Xiamen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Hotpot: Fresh seafood cooked in a flavorful broth.
  • Oyster Omelet: A dish made with oysters, eggs, and vegetables.
  • Fried Squid: Crispy fried calamari that’s perfect as a snack.

Local Beverages

  • Hakka Rice Wine: A traditional Hakka drink made from glutinous rice.
  • Xiamen Coffee: A unique blend of coffee that’s made with beans from Xiamen and other parts of China.

Where to Stay in Xiamen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xiamen has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some popular options:

  • Luxury Hotels: Kempinski Hotel Xiamen, Westin Xiamen, Pan Pacific Xiamen.
  • Mid-Range Hotels: Hotel Indigo Xiamen Harbour, Swiss Grand Xiamen, Marco Polo Xiamen.
  • Budget Hotels: Xiamen International Conference Center Youth Hotel, Mola Mola Hostel, Seashine Hotel.

What to Buy in Xiamen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gulangyu Island Souvenirs: Postcards, magnets, and other souvenirs featuring the famous island.
  • Xiamen Tea: Xiamen is known for its tea, so be sure to bring some home as a souvenir.
  • Egrets Carvings: Egrets are a symbol of Xiamen, and you can find beautifully carved egrets in many shops throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Xiamen

  • Hakka Culture: The Hakka people are an ethnic group that’s native to Xiamen. They have their own language, food, and customs, which are still evident in the city today.
  • Egret Culture: Egrets are a symbol of Xiamen, and people in the city celebrate the Egret Festival every year to honor these graceful birds.

Best Time to Visit Xiamen

The best time to visit Xiamen is from October to December when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are smaller. Spring is also a good time to visit when the flowers are in bloom, but it can be crowded during the Chinese New Year holiday.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xiamen

Do:

  • Try the seafood: Xiamen is known for its delicious seafood, so be sure to try some while you’re in the city.
  • Explore the colonial architecture: Xiamen has a rich history, and its colonial architecture is some of the best in China.
  • Be respectful at temples and other cultural sites: When visiting temples and other cultural sites, be sure to dress appropriately and be respectful of the customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Xiamen is a clean city, so be sure to dispose of your trash properly.
  • Don’t disrespect the culture: Xiamen has a rich history and culture, so be sure to be respectful of the customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xiamen

Xiamen is a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.
  • Be cautious of traffic when crossing the street.

Health in Xiamen

The tap water in Xiamen is not safe to drink, so be sure to stick to bottled water or boiled water. It’s also a good idea to bring some basic medical supplies, such as band-aids and pain relievers.

Getting to Xiamen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xiamen

  • Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport: The main airport that serves Xiamen and the surrounding areas. It’s located on the northeastern coast of Xiamen Island.

Bus Stations Near Xiamen

  • Xiamen Bus Station: The main bus station in Xiamen, located in the city center. It serves buses to and from other cities in Fujian and China.

Other Options

  • Train: Xiamen is connected to other cities in China by train, with high-speed trains that run to cities such as Shanghai and Guangzhou.
  • Ferry: Xiamen is also a port city, and ferries run from Xiamen to other nearby islands and cities.

How to Get Around in Xiamen

  • Bus: Xiamen has a comprehensive bus system that connects the city and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Xiamen and are a good option for getting around the city.
  • Bike: Xiamen has a bike-sharing system, with bikes available for rent throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Xiamen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhangzhou: A city located to the south of Xiamen that’s known for its historical sites and delicious food.
  • Quanzhou: A city located to the north of Xiamen that boasts a rich history and a famous maritime museum.

Conclusion

Xiamen is a beautiful city that’s full of history, culture, and delicious food. Whether you’re interested in exploring its colonial architecture, hiking through the mountains, or trying the local cuisine, Xiamen has something for everyone. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
