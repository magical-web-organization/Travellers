Introduction to Lake Tai

Lake Tai, also known as Taihu, is a beautiful freshwater lake located in the Jiangsu Province of East China. It is the third-largest freshwater lake in China, covering an area of about 2,338 square kilometers. Lake Tai is surrounded by historical cultural greats, beautiful towns and villages, and breathtaking scenery.

Tourists from all over the world are attracted to Lake Tai for its natural and cultural beauty. From lakeside outdoor activities to historical museums, from local food to beautiful scenery, Lake Tai has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Tai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taihu Lake Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and ecology of the Taihu Lake region through various exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Xishan:
    • Xishan Island Scenic Area: It is an island in the middle of Lake Tai where visitors can explore ancient temples and pavilions, hike, go fishing and camping, or relax in secluded beaches.
    • Turtle Head Peninsula: It is the best place to witness the breath-taking sunrise and sunset of Lake Tai. Also, visitors can explore the temples, gardens, and countryside of the peninsula.
  • Suzhou Taihu Lake National Wetland Park: This park on the east side of Lake Tai is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, and visitors can explore the wetlands through boat rides, cycling, and hiking.

Natural Attractions of Lake Tai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour to enjoy the lake’s stunning blue water and see the local culture and traditions of Taihu Lake in China from the water.
  • Xie Yuan Garden: This garden has a beautiful lake view and features a waterfall, pavilion, corridors, and traditional architecture
  • Wuzhong Ancient Town: This town has traditional Chinese architecture, including Suzhou-style gardens, temples, museums, and more. Visitors can also visit the Zhongshan Lu Shopping District to shop for local souvenirs and cuisines.
  • Tianshan Mountain: Tianshan Mountain is best known for its impressive peaks, waterfalls, and stunning scenery. Visitors can explore the park on foot, bike or take a cable car.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Tai

The Taihu Lake National Scenic Area has numerous trails that offer breathtaking views of the lake and its surroundings. Some of the best hiking/biking routes include:

  • Turtle Head Peninsula: This peninsula offers some of the most popular hiking and biking trails in the area. Visitors can explore the area for about three hours while witnessing the stunning lake and countryside views.
  • Wuzhong Ancient Town to Lianghong: This trail features ancient Chinese villages, pavilions, and temples, culminating in Lianghong Village with its beautiful waterfalls.
  • Zhongshan Hill and Waterfront Park: This park offers a 6-kilometer hike or bike ride around the beautiful lakefront scenery.

Best Things to Do in Lake Tai: Activities and Tours

  • Taihu Lake Boat Tour: Take a boat tour to see the lake and nearby attractions while learning about the local culture and traditions of Taihu Lake.
  • Suzhou Taihu Lake National Wetland Park Adventure Tour: Explore the park through various activities such as hiking, cycling, and boat rides, and witness the diversity of plant and animal species in the region.
  • Cycling around Lake Tai: Cycle along the picturesque lakeside trails that offer stunning scenery and cities such as Suzhou, Wuxi, and Changzhou.
  • Taihu Lake Water Sports: Visitors can enjoy water activities such as jet skiing, kayaking, sailing, and windsurfing.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Tai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yangcheng Lake Crab: This famous freshwater crab is world-renowned for its tender, sweet, and savory taste. Visitors can enjoy this dish steamed, fried, or served with noodles.
  • Spare Ribs Stewed in Soy Sauce: This dish features juicy and tender meat cooked with soy sauce and is incredibly popular among tourists.
  • Fried Shrimp with Longjing Tea Leaves: This dish is a delicious mix of shrimp and Longjing tea leaves stir-fried together.

Local Beverages

  • Taihu Lake Tea: Taihu Lake produces a wide range of fine teas, including the world-famous Longjing tea.
  • Taihu Lake Mineral Water: Taihu Lake water is delicious and pure, perfect for refreshing and rehydrating.

Where to Stay in Lake Tai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Lake Tai, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Shangri-La Hotel, Wuxi: This luxurious hotel offers lakeshore views and various amenities such as outdoor pools, spas, fitness centers, and 24-hour room service.
  • Lake View Hotel, Suzhou: This hotel overlooks Lake Tai and features a beautiful garden and convenient access to Wuzhong Ancient Town.
  • Taihu Lake 365 Youth Hostel, Wuxi: This youth hostel offers inexpensive accommodation options and organized tours.

What to Buy in Lake Tai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Taihu Lake produces a variety of high-quality teas, including Longjing, Dahongpao, and Wuyishan tea.
  • Wooden Carvings: The region is known for its exquisite wooden carvings, including furniture, household items, and artworks.
  • Pearls and Jewellery: Lake Tai is home to China’s largest pearl production base, so visitors can shop for pearls and jewelry made from freshwater pearls.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Tai

  • Traditional Festivals: Taihu Lake is well-known for its traditional festivals, including the Dragon Boat Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival and the Double Ninth Festival. Visitors can experience local customs, music, and food during these festive times.
  • Chinese Gardens: Taihu Lake is known for its traditional Chinese gardens, such as Xie Yuan Garden and Tiger Hill Suzhou Garden. These gardens showcase Chinese architectural and cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Lake Tai

The best time to visit Lake Tai is during the spring and autumn months from April to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. The cherry blossoms look beautiful during spring, and autumn colors are breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Tai

Do:

  • Do drink local tea and water as they are pure, fresh and taste unique.
  • Do follow local customs and etiquette when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Do try local cuisine such as Yangcheng Lake crab and spare ribs stewed in soy sauce.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute the lake as it is home to various aquatic wildlife.
  • Don’t wear inappropriate clothing while visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Don’t forget to bargain while shopping in local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Tai

Lake Tai is a safe destination, and travelers can enjoy a peaceful and secure stay in the area. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and should carry important documents and money safely.

Health in Lake Tai

While in Lake Tai, visitors should drink boiled or bottled water and avoid tap water. They should also carry mosquito repellant as there may be mosquitos in the area.

Getting to Lake Tai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Tai

The nearest airports to Lake Tai include:

  • Sunan Shuofang International Airport, Wuxi: The airport is located about 14 kilometers from downtown Wuxi and provides regional and international connections.
  • Hongqiao International Airport, Shanghai: The airport is located 120 kilometers from Lake Tai and provides both domestic and international connections.

Bus Stations Near Lake Tai

Some of the major bus stations near Lake Tai include:

  • Wuxi Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and provides regular bus connections to surrounding cities and attractions.
  • Suzhou North Bus Station: The station is located about 9 kilometers from the city center and provides bus connections to Lake Tai and other destinations in the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Beijing, Shanghai, or Hangzhou to Wuxi, Suzhou, or Changzhou, or hire a taxi or private car for convenient transportation.

How to Get Around in Lake Tai

While in Lake Tai, visitors can use public transportation systems such as buses, trains or opt for taxi, motorcycle, and bike rentals to explore the region’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Lake Tai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Lake Tai include:

  • Suzhou: Known as the Venice of the East, Suzhou is just 10 kilometers from Lake Tai and is famous for its canals, classical Chinese gardens, and silk production.
  • Wuxi: The city of Wuxi is located on the southern shore of Lake Tai and is famous for its freshwater pearls, delicious fish, and beautiful scenery.
  • Hangzhou: The city of Hangzhou is about 180 kilometers from Lake Tai, offering visitors an opportunity to explore the magnificent West Lake, temples, and tea plantations.

Conclusion

Lake Tai (Taihu) is a befitting name for this breathtaking heavenly lake. It is known for its picturesque scenery, lovely climate, and welcoming locals. In addition, Lake Tai offers cultural, historic, and natural attractions. Explore and experience the cool breeze and stunning scenes along Lake Tai – a true refreshing experience!
