Introduction to Qingpu

Nestled in the western suburbs of Shanghai, Qingpu is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This district boasts a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture, making it a must-visit destination. From the scenic Dianshan Lake to the historic Qingpu Ancient Town, you’re sure to discover something unforgettable in Qingpu.

List of Places to Visit in Qingpu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Qingpu Ancient Town: Built in the Ming and Qing Dynasties, Qingpu Ancient Town is a historic district filled with picturesque waterways, bridges, and quaint shops. You can explore the town’s cultural heritage and traditional architecture, as well as experience local cuisine, arts, and crafts.
  • Zhujiajiao Ancient Town: Located in the north of Qingpu, Zhujiajiao is another ancient water town that dates back over 1,700 years. The town is famous for its charming canals, stone bridges, and classical gardens, which give visitors a glimpse into a bygone era.
  • Shanghai Auto Museum: If you’re a car enthusiast, the Shanghai Auto Museum is a must-visit. The museum showcases China’s automotive history, including rare and exotic cars from around the world. You can also participate in interactive exhibits and ride simulators.

Natural Attractions of Qingpu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dianshan Lake: Surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills, Dianshan Lake is Qingpu’s largest freshwater lake. The lake offers a range of water sports like kayaking, fishing, and water skiing. You can also enjoy a leisurely stroll along the boardwalk, enjoy some local snacks, and take in the serene views.
  • Xujing Peach Blossom Festival: Every spring, Xujing Peach Blossom Festival blooms, an incredible natural phenomenon. The festival is celebrated with various cultural activities, food stalls, and performances. You can take a scenic boat ride, rent a bicycle, and have a relaxed picnic amid the stunning peach blossom trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qingpu

  • Zhaotun Mountain: Situated in the northwest of the district, Zhaotun Mountain is a scenic spot with an extensive network of hiking trails. These paths lead through lush forests and offer sweeping views of Qingpu’s rolling hills and verdant landscapes. You can also explore ancient structures like the Zhaotun Temple and the Zhaotun Reservoir on the mountain.

Best Things to Do in Qingpu: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Local Farms: At Qingpu’s scenic rural areas, visitors can discover serene surroundings and local traditions. You can visit the Weijiajia Strawberry Farm and pick your own strawberries to enjoy on site. Another option is the Xianxia Grape Farm, where you can roam through the vineyards and sample the sweet grape harvest.
  • Qingpu Hot Springs: Relaxing in Qingpu’s natural hot springs is one of the popular indoor activities in winter. Located in the midst of the scenic Qingpu Mountains, the hot springs welcome visitors with thermal pools, saunas, and massages.

Food and Drink Scene of Qingpu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab with Yellow Wine: This dish is famous in Qingpu for its unique combination of fresh crab meat and local yellow wine. The wine’s aroma adds complexity and depth to the seafood, resulting in a delicious and intriguing flavor.
  • Qingpu Mutton: Qingpu’s frozen mutton is incredibly tender and savory, making for a perfect local dining experience. It’s often paired with a savory broth that gives it an even richer taste.

Local Beverages

  • Yellow Wine: Yellow wine is a type of rice wine that the locals love in Qingpu. The wine has a sweet and mild taste and is often served during festivals and ceremonies. If you’re a wine lover, don’t miss tasting this unique local specialty.

Where to Stay in Qingpu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Le Meridien Shanghai, Qingpu: This five-star hotel offers guests modern facilities, luxurious rooms, and picturesque garden views.
  • Aloft Shanghai, Qingpu: Aloft Shanghai belongs to Marriott International offers a playful twist on luxury living. This boutique hotel features comfortable rooms, urban design, and relaxed spaces to mingle.

What to Buy in Qingpu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woven Stool: A woven stool is a traditional handicraft in Qingpu. It’s made of bamboo or grass and is an excellent souvenir to bring home as a decor piece or also for everyday use.
  • Lotus Root Powder: Lotus Root Powder is an essential ingredient in Qingpu cuisine. It’s a unique gift to bring home for your loved ones who enjoy innovating in the kitchen.

Local Culture and Customs of Qingpu

Qingpu has a long history of culture and traditions, which dates back over 1,700 years. The people here are proud of their heritage and actively preserve it. You can experience their culture through traditional folk arts, such as the popular paper-cutting and embroidery crafts. The locals are also passionate about celebrations like the Qingpu temple fair and Spring Festival, which are filled with lively traditions and cultural displays.

Best Time to Visit Qingpu

The best time to visit Qingpu is during the autumn season from September to November. The temperature is mild, and the air is fresh, making it perfect for outdoor activities. During this time of year, Qinpu’s scenic locations, like Dianshan lake, shine with various hues of gold, red, and orange.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qingpu

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and visit historic sites.
  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural landscapes and historic sites.
  • Show disrespect to local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qingpu

Qingpu is a safe and peaceful district with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take standard precautions such as securing valuables, using registered transportation, and avoiding venturing into unsafe areas at night.

Health in Qingpu

Malaria is not a risk in Qingpu. However, visitors are advised to get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B. Also, drinking tap water is not recommended as it’s not safe for consumption.

Getting to Qingpu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qingpu

The closest airport to Qingpu is Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport. You can also access Qingpu from Shanghai Pudong International Airport by taking the metro line two.

Bus Stations Near Qingpu

Qingpu Bus Station located in Qingpu’s central urban area is a convenient way to arrive at Qingpu from Shanghai.

Other Options

Taxi and Metro are two other modes of public transportation that connect Qingpu to Shanghai’s city center and surrounding districts.

How to Get Around in Qingpu

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Qingpu and an easy way to travel around the city. They are relatively inexpensive for short distances
  • Buses: The bus is also an affordable, convenient, and safe way of getting around Qingpu with numerous routes and bus stops.

Where to Go Next from Qingpu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shanghai: Take a day or two to explore the urban attractions and nightlife of downtown Shanghai.
  • Hangzhou: Visit Hangzhou for a relaxing weekend getaway in the famous West Lake.

Conclusion

With a rich blend of history, natural beauty, culture, and delicious food, Qingpu offers unforgettable experiences that visitors will cherish. Come visit and explore the hidden side of Shanghai, a district where traditions and modernity seamlessly coexist.
