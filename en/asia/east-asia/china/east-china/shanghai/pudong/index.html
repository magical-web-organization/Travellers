Introduction to Pudong

Pudong is a district located in the heart of Shanghai, China. It is home to some of the most iconic skyscrapers in the world, including the Shanghai Tower and the Oriental Pearl Tower. Pudong is a melting pot of cultures, sights, and sounds that attract millions of tourists every year. Get ready to explore the rich culture, natural landscapes, and distinctive cuisine of Pudong.

List of Places to Visit in Pudong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The district of Pudong boasts a rich history and culture, which can be explored through a range of museums and historical sites. The China Art Museum is a must-visit destination for art lovers, with a vast collection of Chinese contemporary art. The Shanghai Science and Technology Museum is also worth a visit, with interactive exhibits on science and technology.

For history enthusiasts, the Shanghai History Museum offers a glimpse into the city’s past, while the Zhoupu Ancient Town provides a window into ancient Chinese life.

Natural Attractions of Pudong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Pudong is renowned for its modern skyscrapers, it is also home to some stunning natural attractions. The Dishui Lake is one such destination, offering a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The Fengxian Bay Wetland Park is also worth a visit, with scenic hiking trails and opportunities for bird watching.

Check out the Shanghai Disney Resort for a fun-filled day with family and friends, and take the Bund Sightseeing Tunnel to cross the Huangpu River and enjoy a spectacular view of the Puxi skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pudong

Pudong is the perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. The most popular route is the Sheshan National Forest Park, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The Xinchang Ancient Town is another popular destination, with its scenic riverfront and peaceful atmosphere.

If you prefer biking, then head to the Nanhui Mandarin Ducks Lake Area, which is famous for its scenic bike trails. Alternatively, explore the district on foot by walking along the Huangpu River bank, which offers fantastic views of the skyline and the river.

Best Things to Do in Pudong: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Pudong. Take a cruise on the Huangpu River at night and watch the magnificent display of lights and colors unfold before your eyes. Take a yoga class in the Lujiazui Central Green to relax and unwind, or improve your culinary skills with a cooking class.

For adventure seekers, don’t miss out on the opportunity to go bungee jumping or skydiving at the Shanghai Bungee Jumping Center. Alternatively, take a helicopter tour of the city and see the famous landmarks from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pudong

Day 1: Visit the China Art Museum and the Oriental Pearl Tower Day 2: Explore the Fengxian Bay Wetland Park and the Shanghai Disney Resort Day 3: Take a bike tour of the Nanhui Mandarin Ducks Lake Area and visit the Xinchang Ancient Town Day 4: Walk along the Huangpu River bank and take a yoga class in Lujiazui Central Green Day 5: Visit the Shoreline Park and enjoy a picnic overlooking the Huangpu River Day 6: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Chinese dishes Day 7: Go bungee jumping or skydiving at the Shanghai Bungee Jumping Center

Food and Drink Scene of Pudong

Must-Try Dishes

Pudong offers a unique dining experience with a mouthwatering array of local delicacies. Try the ‘beggar’s chicken’ at Donghuang Restaurant, a slow-cooked whole chicken that is tender and flavorful. Another must-try dish is the ‘conghua-style braised goose’ at Jiuqu Hutong, which is cooked in a special sauce and served with crispy skin.

Local Beverages

Aside from its delicious cuisine, Pudong is also famous for its tea culture. Head to a local teahouse, such as the Shanghai Classic Tea House, and try some of the finest teas China has to offer. Alternatively, try some of the local beers, such as the Jiuhua Mountain Lager or the Lamusang Lager.

Where to Stay in Pudong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pudong offers a range of accommodation options for different tastes and budgets. For the ultimate luxury experience, stay at the Pudong Shangri-La hotel, which offers stunning views of the city skyline. Alternatively, for a more budget-friendly option, stay at the Jinjiang Inn or the Motel 168.

What to Buy in Pudong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pudong is famous for its shopping scene, offering everything from luxury boutiques to local street markets. For souvenirs, head to the Qipu Road Clothing Market or the Shanghai IFC Mall. For local products, try the ‘Coconut Dried Shrimp’ or the ‘Blueberry Crystal Cake’, both of which are unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Pudong

As with any foreign destination, it is essential to respect the local culture and customs when visiting Pudong. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or traditional villages, and learn basic phrases in Mandarin Chinese to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Pudong

The best time to visit Pudong is during the spring or autumn months when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. The winter months can be cold, while the summer months can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pudong

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Learn basic Mandarin Chinese phrases
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or traditional villages
  • Tipping is not customary but appreciated

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas
  • Take pictures of strangers without their consent
  • Speak loudly or make excessive noise in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Pudong

Pudong is a relatively safe destination for tourists, although it is essential to take regular precautions against petty theft and pickpocketing. Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas, and use licensed taxis or transportation services.

Health in Pudong

Pudong has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and medical centers available in the district. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance, and take basic precautions against food and water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Pudong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pudong

The Pudong International Airport is located in the district and is the primary entry point for visitors to the region. The airport is well-connected to the city through bus, taxi, and metro services.

Bus Stations Near Pudong

Pudong has several bus stations, including the Pudong Bus Station and the Lujiazui Bus Station, which connect the district to other cities and regions in China.

Other Options

There are several high-speed trains that run to and from Pudong, connecting the district to major cities in China. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from the Bund to visit the nearby Chongming Island.

How to Get Around in Pudong

Pudong has an extensive transport network, including several metro lines, buses, and taxis. The metro is the most convenient and affordable option for getting around the district, with stops at all the major attractions and hotels. Taxis are also readily available, but make sure to use licensed services and negotiate the fare beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Pudong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pudong is conveniently located near other popular destinations in China, such as Hangzhou and Suzhou. A day trip to the nearby water towns, such as Zhujiajiao or Zhouzhuang, is also highly recommended.

Conclusion

Pudong is a vibrant and exciting district that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and modernity. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a foodie or a culture enthusiast, Pudong has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the wonders of this captivating destination.
