Introduction to Zhujiajiao

Zhujiajiao is a picturesque town situated in the Qingpu District near Shanghai, China. The town is well-known for its stunning waterways and ancient bridges that give visitors an authentic glimpse into traditional Chinese culture. Its narrow streets are lined with quaint shops and restaurants that serve up delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Zhujiajiao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zhujiajiao Ancient Town Museum

The Zhujiajiao Ancient Town Museum is located in the heart of Zhujiajiao and is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning more about the town’s history. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Kezhi Garden

Kezhi Garden is a beautifully preserved Ming Dynasty garden that offers visitors a tranquil escape from the bustle of Zhujiajiao. The garden features a pond, rock formations, and lush vegetation.

Natural Attractions of Zhujiajiao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dadian Lake

Dadian Lake is a scenic spot situated to the south of Zhujiajiao. The area boasts a beautiful lake surrounded by lush greenery and hills. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely boat ride or go hiking in the surrounding hills for some of the best panoramic views.

Shanghai Grand View Garden

The Shanghai Grand View Garden is a 10-hectare park that features stunning pavilions, gardens, and waterways. The garden is also home to the Qing Dynasty Writer Cao Xueqin Memorial Hall, which is a must-visit for fans of Chinese literature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhujiajiao

Zhujiajiao offers many scenic hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the town and its surrounding countryside. One of the most popular routes is the Dadian Lake Scenic Area Hiking Trail, which takes hikers on a scenic journey through the hills and valleys surrounding Dadian Lake.

Best Things to Do in Zhujiajiao: Activities and Tours

Zhujiajiao Water Village Tour

A Zhujiajiao Water Village Tour is a must-do activity for anyone visiting the town. Visitors can take a relaxing boat ride down the canal and explore the town’s many historic bridges and buildings.

Local Food Tour

A local food tour is a great way to sample some of the town’s most delicious cuisine. Visitors can sample local street food at the many food stalls throughout the town or enjoy a sit-down meal at one of the town’s many restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhujiajiao

Must-Try Dishes

Zhujiajiao’s cuisine is famous for its delectable dishes.

  • Zhuxiugou Shengjian (steamed dumplings)
  • Shui Zhu Yu (fish stew)
  • Lion’s Head Meatballs

Local Beverages

Zhujiajiao is famous for its traditional Chinese tea. Visitors can sample a variety of teas at local teahouses throughout the town.

Where to Stay in Zhujiajiao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zhujiajiao Kezhi Garden Inn

Zhujiajiao Kezhi Garden Inn is a charming guesthouse located close to Kezhi Garden. The inn offers comfortable, affordable accommodation and a range of amenities.

Zhujiajiao Xinyuanju Boutique Hotel

Zhujiajiao Xinyuanju Boutique Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Zhujiajiao. The hotel offers stylish rooms and suites, top-notch amenities, and excellent service.

What to Buy in Zhujiajiao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Zhujiajiao can find a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home as gifts or keepsakes. Some popular items include:

  • Traditional Chinese calligraphy and paintings
  • Handmade crafts and jewelry
  • Local teas and spices

Local Culture and Customs of Zhujiajiao

Zhujiajiao has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and traditions. It is customary to remove shoes before entering a local home, and visitors should dress respectfully when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Zhujiajiao

The best time to visit Zhujiajiao is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons. The weather is mild, and the town is less crowded during these times of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhujiajiao

Do:

  • Explore the town on foot or by bike
  • Sample local street food and tea
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Smoke in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhujiajiao

Zhujiajiao is a safe town to visit, and violent crime is rare. Visitors should take the normal precautions to protect their valuables and belongings.

Health in Zhujiajiao

Visitors to Zhujiajiao should be sure to pack any necessary medications and consult with their doctor before traveling. The town has several hospitals and medical clinics that offer a range of services.

Getting to Zhujiajiao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhujiajiao

The nearest airport to Zhujiajiao is Shanghai Pudong International Airport, which is located approximately 50 kilometers away from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Zhujiajiao

Zhujiajiao has several bus stations that offer connections to nearby towns and cities, including Shanghai.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or private car to reach Zhujiajiao.

How to Get Around in Zhujiajiao

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around Zhujiajiao. Visitors can also hire a taxi or take a bus to explore the surrounding areas.

Conclusion

Zhujiajiao is a charming town with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Visitors can explore its ancient waterways, sample local cuisine, and immerse themselves in traditional Chinese culture. Whether you’re looking for an adventure or a relaxing escape, Zhujiajiao has something for everyone.
