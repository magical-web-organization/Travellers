Introduction to Baoshan

Baoshan is a district located in the northern part of Shanghai, China. With unique cultural and natural attractions, Baoshan is a hidden gem worth exploring. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your visit to Baoshan, from places to visit to where to stay and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Baoshan

Baoshan has a range of attractions, from historic museums to scenic parks. Here are some of the top places to visit in Baoshan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wusong Paotaiwan Wetland Park
  2. Baoshan Martyrs’ Cemetery
  3. Sihang Warehouse Battles Memorial
  4. Liuhe Pagoda

Natural Attractions of Baoshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Gucun Park
  2. Shidai Guangchang
  3. Yunyi Park
  4. Dagu Mountain Forest Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baoshan

Baoshan offers a range of hiking and biking routes, allowing visitors to explore its scenic areas. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Gucun Park Hiking Trail
  2. Dagu Mountain Forest Park Hiking Trail
  3. Jinshan Coastal Bike Trail

Best Things to Do in Baoshan: Activities and Tours

Baoshan has plenty of activities and tours to offer, from water sports to cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do in Baoshan:

  1. Visit the Wusong Paotaiwan Wetland Park and take a boat ride to spot the local bird life.
  2. Visit Baoshan Martyrs’ Cemetery for a glimpse into Chinese history.
  3. Join a dumpling-making class for a hands-on culinary experience.
  4. Take a hot air balloon ride for a bird’s-eye view of Baoshan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baoshan

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Baoshan, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day Activities

Local Culture and Customs of Baoshan

Baoshan has a rich cultural identity, with customs and traditions that reflect China’s diverse history. Here are some of the local customs and culture of Baoshan:

  1. Red lantern festivals
  2. Lattice window architecture
  3. Shanghai Dialect

Best Time to Visit Baoshan

The best time to visit Baoshan is during the autumn months (September-November), when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing colors. Spring (March-May) is also a great time to visit, with milder temperatures and blooming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baoshan

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try local dishes and drinks.
  3. Bring a camera to capture the scenic views.
  4. Learn a few basic Chinese phrases.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local culture.
  2. Pet wild animals.
  3. Litter or damage the environment.
  4. Bargain excessively when shopping.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baoshan

Baoshan is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are a few safety tips to keep in mind when visiting Baoshan:

  1. Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  2. Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded tourist areas.
  3. Use licensed taxis and public transportation.
  4. Stay alert when crossing the street.

Health in Baoshan

Baoshan has good medical facilities, but it’s important to take precautions to avoid illness. Here are a few tips to stay healthy in Baoshan:

  1. Stay hydrated and drink bottled water.
  2. Avoid uncooked or undercooked foods.
  3. Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  4. Pack any necessary medication and visit a doctor if necessary.
