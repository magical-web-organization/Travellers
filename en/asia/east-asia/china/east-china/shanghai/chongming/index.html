Introduction to Chongming

Located in Shanghai, Chongming is the third-largest island in China and is known for its rich cultural heritage, unspoiled natural beauty, and abundant wildlife. The island is divided into three main towns: the westmost town of Chengqiao, the central town of Dongping, and the eastern town of Nanmen. Surrounded by the Yangtze River to the north and east, and the East China Sea to the south, Chongming is home to numerous wetlands, lakes, and forests, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Chongming

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dongtan Wetland Park - This wetland reserve is a paradise for bird lovers, housing over 290 species of migratory birds.
  2. Xisha Wetland Park - This park is home to rare and endangered species of waterfowl and is a great place for bird watching.
  3. Dongping National Forest Park - A beautiful park with lush forests, lakes, and wildlife, it is also the site of the historical Dongping Ancient City.
  4. Shanghai Museum of Coast Defense - This museum is a testament to Shanghai’s rich maritime history, and its numerous forts and gun batteries.

Natural Attractions of Chongming: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Chenjia Town Beach - A beautiful sandy beach overlooking the East China Sea, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  2. Mingzhu Lake Park - A serene lakeside park with paddleboats, fishing, and a pagoda with stunning views of the surrounding nature.
  3. Yueliangwan Park - A beautiful park with hiking trails, cycling paths, flower gardens, and scenic views of the Yangtze River.
  4. Dongtan International Bird-Watching Center - A unique eco-tourism destination, with guided bird-watching tours and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chongming

  1. Chongming Island Greenway - An extensive network of hiking and cycling trails, stretching over 70 kilometers, with beautiful views of the beaches, wetlands, and forests.
  2. Hengsha Island - A neighboring island that can be reached by ferry, famous for its sandy beaches, fishing villages, and scenic cycling routes.

Best Things to Do in Chongming: Activities and Tours

  1. Bicycle Tours - A great way to explore the island’s hidden treasures, join a guided bike tour around the scenic countryside and enjoy the fresh air.
  2. Bird Watching - With over 400 bird species recorded on the island, bird watching is one of the most popular activities for visitors to Chongming.
  3. Kayaking and Canoeing - Paddle your way through the wetlands and lakes of Chongming for a unique perspective on the island’s stunning natural beauty.
  4. Hiking and Camping - With so many scenic hiking trails and campsites on the island, spend a night under the stars and wake up to the amazing views.

Food and Drink Scene of Chongming

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Roast Duck - A traditional dish in Chongming, slow-roasted duck is served with crispy skin and succulent meat.
  2. Lotus Root Soup - A fragrant soup made with fresh lotus roots, tomatoes, and pork ribs, perfect for a night in.
  3. Sugar-coated Peanuts - A popular snack in Chongming, these caramelized peanuts are sweet and crunchy.

Local Beverages

  1. Chongming Rice Wine - A famous local wine, made with high-quality rice and water, served warm, and perfect for a cold winter night.
  2. Qilin Black Beer - A popular local brew, rich and malty, with a subtle sweetness and a smooth finish.

  2. Chongming G7 Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel in Nandu, with clean rooms and easy access to local transport.
  3. Backstreet Youth Hostel - Located in Dongping Town, this hostel offers affordable shared dorms and private rooms, with a communal kitchen and a cozy lounge area.

What to Buy in Chongming: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Herbal Tea - Made with fresh herbs and flowers, Chongming’s herbal teas are known for their medicinal properties and soothing aroma.
  2. Handicrafts - Locally made baskets, pottery, and woodcarvings are beautiful souvenirs to bring back home.
  3. Fresh Seafood - The fish markets in Chengqiao and Nanmen are the best places to buy fresh fish and seafood, perfect for a home-cooked meal.

Local Culture and Customs of Chongming

Chongming is a peaceful island community with rich cultural traditions, including the Chongming Dragon Boat Festival, which takes place every year in May or June. The festival features colorful dragon boat races, traditional music, and dance performances, and local cuisine. Another major event is the Chongming International Biennale, a celebration of contemporary art, held every two years, which attracts artists and visitors from all over the world.

Best Time to Visit Chongming

The best time to visit Chongming is from April to November when the weather is pleasant, and the natural scenery is at its best. June to August is peak tourist season, with higher prices and larger crowds, but the beaches and water sports are top notch.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chongming

Do:

  • Do try the local delicacies, such as roast duck, sugar-coated peanuts, and Chongming rice wine.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs, such as covering up when entering temples, and removing shoes before entering a traditional home.
  • Do explore the scenic countryside and natural parks, such as Dongtan Wetland Park and Chongming Island Greenway.

Don’t:

  • Don’t pollute the environment, and dispose of garbage in designated areas.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and mosquito repellent, especially if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Don’t be afraid to haggle at local markets, but do so respectfully and with a smile.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chongming

Chongming is a relatively safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night, and to keep your belongings safe.

Health in Chongming

Chongming doesn’t have any major health risks or concerns for travelers, but it’s always important to take basic precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated. If you do fall ill, seek medical attention at one of the local clinics or hospitals.

Getting to Chongming: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chongming

The closest airport to Chongming is Shanghai Pudong International Airport, with frequent flights to and from other major cities in China and around the world.

Bus Stations Near Chongming

There are several bus stations in Shanghai that offer connections to Chongming, including Hongkou Bus Station, Shanghai South Bus Station, and Shanghai East Bus Station.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Chongming is by ferry, which departs from Baoyang Road Ferry Terminal in Shanghai and takes around one hour to reach the island.

How to Get Around in Chongming

The best way to get around Chongming is by bicycle, as there are numerous bike rental shops in the major towns, and the island has a well-developed network of cycling trails. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive and may not always adhere to meters, so be sure to negotiate the price beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Chongming: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shanghai

A trip to Chongming can easily be combined with a visit to Shanghai, one of the most vibrant cities in China. Explore the city’s rich history and culture by visiting the Bund, Yuyuan Garden, and the Shanghai Museum, or indulge in some shopping, dining, and entertainment at Nanjing Road, Xintiandi, and the French Concession.

Suzhou

Known as the “Venice of the East,” Suzhou is a beautiful city with numerous canals, bridges, and classical gardens. Take a day trip from Chongming to Suzhou to visit the ancient water town of Tongli, the Suzhou Silk Museum, or the peaceful Lingering Garden.

Conclusion

Chongming Island is a must-visit destination for lovers of nature, culture, and adventure. With its unspoiled landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and charming local community, a trip to Chongming is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
