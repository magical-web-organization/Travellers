Introduction to Hongkou

Hongkou is a historical district in the northeastern part of Shanghai, China. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, with numerous museums and historical sites that showcase the city’s past. However, Hongkou is not just about its past – the district also boasts stunning natural attractions, fun activities, delicious food, and friendly locals. Whether you’re a culture vulture, nature lover, or just seeking a new adventure, Hongkou has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hongkou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hongkou Jewish Refugees Museum: This museum documents the history of Jewish refugees who fled to Shanghai during World War II and found a safe haven in Hongkou.
  • Lu Xun Park and Former Residence: Dedicated to the famous Chinese writer Lu Xun, this park features his former residence, several pavilions, gardens, and a museum showcasing his life and works.
  • Duolun Cultural Street: This pedestrian street is lined with old buildings that have been converted into cafes, bookshops, galleries, and museums dedicated to Chinese writers and artists.
  • 1933 Contemporary Arts Center: Once a slaughterhouse, this unique building now houses art galleries, exhibition spaces, and creative businesses.
  • Hongkou Football Stadium: One of the oldest football stadiums in China, this venue hosts many local and national games.

Natural Attractions of Hongkou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wujiaochang Park: This park features a lake, gardens, walking trails, and several sports facilities, including a football field and a basketball court.
  • Huangxing Park: Located along the Huangpu River, this park offers stunning views of the city skyline and the river. It has a running track, a playground, and pavilions.
  • Shanghai Bay Wetland Park: This park is an eco-tourism area that has a large wetland area with diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can go bird-watching, boating, cycling, and hiking.
  • Shanghai Ocean Aquarium: Located near the Oriental Pearl Tower, this aquarium houses over 15,000 marine creatures from around the world, including sharks, sea turtles, and penguins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hongkou

  • Hongkou to Yangpu Riverside Route: This route follows the river and passes through several parks and neighborhoods, showcasing the city’s vibrant culture and history.
  • Hongkou to Dalian Road Route: This route is a great way to explore the city’s architecture, as it passes by many old buildings and landmarks, such as the Garden Bridge and Tilanqiao Prison.
  • Hongkou to Suzhou Creek Route: This route takes you along the banks of the Suzhou Creek, where you can enjoy the views of the water and the city on either side.

Best Things to Do in Hongkou: Activities and Tours

  • Shanghai Circus World: This venue hosts amazing performances of acrobatics, juggling, trapeze, and other circus acts.
  • Tianzifang: This alleyway features many boutique shops, craft stores, restaurants, and cafes. It is a great place to shop for souvenirs and gifts.
  • Shanghai Tower: The tallest building in China and the second tallest in the world, this skyscraper offers stunning views of the city from its observation deck.
  • Shanghai Propaganda Poster Art Centre: This museum showcases a collection of over 6,000 posters from the Cultural Revolution era, providing a glimpse into China’s recent past.
  • Hongkou Football Stadium Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of this historic stadium and learn about its role in the city’s sports culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Hongkou

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sheng Jian Bao: A type of Chinese dumpling that is pan-fried and filled with pork, vegetables, and soup.
  • Dan Bing: A type of Chinese pancake that is filled with eggs, scallions, and other ingredients of your choice.
  • Hong Shao Rou: A classic Shanghai-style braised pork dish that is slow-cooked with soy sauce, sugar, and spices.
  • Xiao Long Bao: Another type of Chinese dumpling, this time filled with pork and soup. They’re usually steamed and served with ginger and vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Suanmeitang: This is a traditional Chinese drink made with fermented rice or barley and flavored with sugar and lemon.
  • Wuyi Yancha: A type of Chinese tea that comes from the Wuyi Mountains in Fujian Province. It has a smoky, roasted flavor and is often served with dim sum.
  • Huangjiu: A type of fermented rice wine that is popular in Shanghai and other parts of China. It has a sweet, floral taste and is often served at weddings and festivals.

Where to Stay in Hongkou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Renaissance Shanghai Zhongshan Park Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of Hongkou and offers great amenities, such as a spa, fitness center, and restaurants.
  • Shanghai Hanting Hotel: A budget hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms, and is conveniently located near several subway stations.
  • Phoenix Hostel: A cozy and affordable hostel that offers private and shared rooms, a communal kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with great views of the city.

What to Buy in Hongkou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shanghai-style Silk Scarves: These scarves are made of high-quality silk and feature beautiful designs inspired by the city’s history and culture.
  • Shanghai Shikumen Keychains: These keychains are shaped like the traditional Shikumen houses that are a hallmark of Shanghai’s architecture.
  • Shanghai-style Snacks: Shanghai is known for its delicious street food, such as fried dumplings, scallion pancakes, and steamed buns. You can buy them in local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Hongkou

  • Etiquette: Chinese people value politeness and respect, so it’s important to be courteous and considerate when interacting with locals. Bowing slightly and using polite language (e.g. ‘Ni hao’ for hello) is appreciated.
  • Language: Mandarin Chinese is the official language of China, but locals in Hongkou may also speak Shanghainese, which is a dialect of Chinese. Learning a few phrases in either language can help you communicate better.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in China, but small gratuities may be appreciated in some situations, such as in restaurants or for tour guides.
  • Gift-Giving: Gift-giving is a common practice in China, especially during holidays and business dealings. Gifts should be wrapped and presented with both hands, and should not be opened immediately.

Best Time to Visit Hongkou

The best time to visit Hongkou is during the spring and autumn months (April-June and September-November), when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months (July-August) can be hot and humid, while the winter months (December-February) can be quite cold, with occasional snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hongkou

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a copy of your passport and necessary documents with you at all times.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when using public transportation or walking on the streets at night.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals or their culture.
  • Engage in illegal or inappropriate behavior.
  • Carry large amounts of cash or valuables with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hongkou

Hongkou is a relatively safe district, but visitors should be mindful of their belongings and surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots. Petty theft can occur, such as pickpocketing or bag-snatching, so keeping your wallet and phone secure is recommended. Avoid dark alleys or poorly-lit areas at night, and be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Hongkou

Visitors should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations and consult with their healthcare provider about additional vaccinations based on their travel plans. The tap water in China is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water or boiled/filtered water. It’s also advisable to carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Hongkou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hongkou

Shanghai has two international airports – Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG) and Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport (SHA). Both airports offer various transport options to Hongkou, such as taxis, buses, and subway lines. The journey from PVG can take around 1.5 hours, while the journey from SHA can take between 30-45 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Hongkou

There are several long-distance bus stations in Shanghai that offer routes to other cities in China. The main station is Shanghai General Bus Station, which is located in Zhabei District, and is accessible by several subway lines.

Other Options

Shanghai is well-connected by high-speed trains to other major cities in China, such as Beijing, Nanjing, and Hangzhou. There are also several ferry services that operate to and from the nearby cities of Suzhou and Wuxi.

How to Get Around in Hongkou

Hongkou is easily accessible by public transportation, such as subways, buses, and taxis. Metro Line 4 and Line 8 both run through the district, and there are several bus lines that connect Hongkou to other parts of the city. Taxis are also relatively cheap and convenient, but be sure to use licensed taxis with working meters.

Where to Go Next from Hongkou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Bund: This iconic waterfront promenade is located in nearby Huangpu District and features stunning views of the city skyline and the Huangpu River.
  • Zhujiajiao Water Town: A charming ancient town located west of Shanghai, known for its picturesque canals, bridges, and temples.
  • Nanjing Road: A bustling shopping street located in Huangpu District, featuring many high-end stores, boutiques, and restaurants.
  • Chongming Island: A beautiful island located northeast of Shanghai, known for its unspoiled nature, wetlands, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Hongkou is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Chinese culture, history, and nature. With its rich selection of attractions, diverse activities, and delicious food, Hongkou has something for everyone. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple seeking a romantic trip, or a family with kids, Hongkou is the perfect place to explore and discover.
