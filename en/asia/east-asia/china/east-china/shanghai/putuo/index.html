Introduction to Putuo

Nestled in the western part of Shanghai, Putuo District is one of the city’s hidden gems. Known for its numerous temples and beautiful natural landscapes, Putuo is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to explore Shanghai beyond its skyscrapers and shopping malls. From magnificent temples to stunning gardens, from verdant mountains to white-sand beaches, Putuo has something to offer for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, the most delicious foods to try, and the best ways to explore this charming district of Shanghai.

List of Places to Visit in Putuo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Putuo Museum

The Putuo Museum is dedicated to preserving the Buddhist culture of the region. Located in the heart of Putuo, this museum houses a vast collection of artifacts related to Buddhism, including ancient statues, scripts and relics. Visitors can learn about the history and development of Buddhism in China, as well as admire beautiful artworks and sculptures.

Jade Buddha Temple

The Jade Buddha Temple is a famous tourist attraction in Putuo, located in the northwest part of the district. The temple features two towering jade Buddha statues, imported from Burma. The temple also houses numerous other Buddhist scriptures and artworks, including wall paintings, carvings, and sculptures, making it an ideal place to learn about Chinese Buddhism.

Natural Attractions of Putuo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Putuo Mountain

The Putuo Mountain is located about 5 km from central Putuo and is known for its lush greenery and breathtaking views. It is considered one of the most scenic mountains in Shanghai, with several hiking trails suitable for different intensities of hikes. The highlight of the hike is the famous Thousand Step Beach, where visitors can enjoy the seaside breeze and take in stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Shengsi Islands

The Shengsi Islands are a group of islands located south of Putuo District. The islands are endowed with stunning natural landscapes, including lush green hills, crystal clear waters, and beautiful beaches. Visitors can enjoy a scenic island-hopping tour, try out local seafood and travel back in time by visiting some of the rustic villages that dot the islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Putuo

Putuo Mountain Route

This route includes a visit to the Thousand Step Beach and Putuo Mountain. The hike starts from the foot of the mountain and goes all the way up to reach the top of the mountain, taking in stunning vistas and magical landscapes along the way.

Chongming Island Biking Route

The Chongming Island Biking Route is a 20km long trail that takes visitors to explore the natural splendors of Chongming Island. The route includes stops at different scenic spots, including wetlands, farmland, and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Putuo: Activities and Tours

Pujiang Cruises

Pujiang River is an essential waterway in Shanghai, stretching from Putuo District to the Huangpu River. Pujiang Cruises offers a relaxing and insightful tour along the river, taking in the colorful riverside life and stunning skyline views of the city.

Chinese Cuisine Cooking Classes

China is known for its flavorful cuisine, and Putuo is no exception. Visitors can learn the art of cooking traditional Chinese cuisine by taking a cooking class that offers hands-on learning of classic Chinese dishes such as dumplings, hot pot, and fried rice.

Food and Drink Scene of Putuo

Must-Try Dishes

Pan-Fried Baozi

Pan-Fried Baozi is a local specialty in Putuo, featuring soft steamed buns filled with pork, chicken, or shrimp and then fried to create a crispy golden crust.

Moon Cakes

Moon cakes are a traditional Chinese pastry and are especially popular during Mid-Autumn Festival. Putuo’s moon cakes have a variety of fillings like black sesame, red bean, or even truffle, and are a must-try for any food lover.

Local Beverages

Tsingtao Beer

Tsingtao Beer is a famous beer brand in China and is brewed in Qingdao city in Shandong Province. This beer is readily available in most restaurants and bars in Putuo, and pairs excellently with spicy Chinese cuisine.

Pearl Milk Tea

Pearl Milk Tea is a popular Taiwanese drink that has become a favorite of many in Putuo. It is a mixture of black tea, milk, and chewy tapioca pearls, and is perfect to cool you off during hot summer days.

What to Buy in Putuo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jade Products

Putuo is famous for its jade products, from miniature Buddha statues to jade bracelets and other jewelry. These make excellent souvenirs and gifts, and there are many shops in Putuo selling different types of jade items.

Moon Cakes

Moon cakes are not only a must-try dish but also an excellent souvenir to bring back home. The pastry is packaged in elegant boxes that make them the perfect gift for family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Putuo

Putuo is a predominantly Buddhist district, and it is customary to dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites. It is also a sign of respect to remove shoes before entering temples and to not touch or take photos of statues without permission.

Best Time to Visit Putuo

The best time to visit Putuo is during the spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) seasons when the weather is mild and comfortable. Avoid the summer season (June-August) when it is hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Putuo

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Taste the local foods and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people or religious items without permission.
  • Litter in public areas.
  • Touch or move Buddhist statues.

Safety and Health

Safety in Putuo

Putuo is a relatively safe district, and visitors are unlikely to encounter crime or violence. However, like with any place, it is best to take precautions and keep valuables safe.

Health in Putuo

Putuo is well equipped with modern medical facilities, and visitors can easily find pharmacies and hospitals in the district. However, it is essential to carry travel insurance and necessary medications when visiting any foreign country.

Getting to Putuo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Putuo

The closest airport to Putuo is Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport, which is about 15 km away, and is easily accessible by taxi, metro or bus.

Bus Stations Near Putuo

Putuo is well connected with other parts of Shanghai through several bus stations, including Long-Distance Bus Station, Xujiahui Bus Station, and Shanghai South Bus Station.

Other Options

The North Railway Station in Shanghai is a good option for those who want to travel by train or take the high-speed rail, hence connecting Putuo to other parts of China.

How to Get Around in Putuo

Putuo has an extensive network of public transportation, including buses, metros, and taxis. The district is relatively small, and most places can be reached within a 30-minute drive.

Where to Go Next from Putuo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Suzhou

Suzhou, located about 100 km west of Shanghai, is one of China’s most beautiful cities, famous for its stunning gardens, canals, and historic architecture. Visitors can take a day trip from Putuo and explore Suzhou’s lovely waterways and charming streets.

Hangzhou

Hangzhou, located about 200 km southwest of Shanghai, is another popular destination famous for its picturesque West Lake and historic temples. Visitors can take a train or bus from Shanghai to reach Hangzhou easily.

Conclusion

Putuo is one of Shanghai’s less-known districts, but it offers a rich cultural and natural heritage, making it an ideal destination for any traveler seeking to explore Shanghai beyond its busy city center. From magnificent temples to stunning natural landscapes, from delicious local foods to comfortable accommodation options, Putuo has it all. Take our guide, pack your bags, and get ready to embark on an unforgettable journey through one of Shanghai’s hidden gems.
