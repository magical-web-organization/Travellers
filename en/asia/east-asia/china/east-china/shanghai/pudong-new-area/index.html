If you’re planning a visit to Shanghai, Pudong New Area should be on your must-visit list. Located in the eastern part of the city, Pudong is known for its modern skyline and vibrant cultural scene. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best attractions, activities, food, and accommodations in Pudong New Area.

Introduction to Pudong New Area

Pudong New Area is a booming district of Shanghai that has undergone significant development in recent years. Known for its towering skyscrapers and bustling business district, Pudong was once farmland and countryside. However, since the 1990s, the district has seen a major transformation, with the construction of the Lujiazui financial district and other commercial areas. Today, Pudong attracts travelers from around the world for its unique blend of modernity and tradition.

List of Places to Visit in Pudong New Area

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Pudong New Area. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shanghai Tower: At 632 meters, this impressive skyscraper is China’s tallest building and the second-tallest in the world. Visitors can take an elevator to the top for panoramic views of the city.
  • Shanghai World Financial Center: Standing at 492 meters, this unique skyscraper is known for its distinctive trapezoid hole at the top. The observation deck offers stunning views of the city.
  • Shanghai Museum of Science and Technology: One of Shanghai’s top museums, the Museum of Science and Technology boasts hands-on exhibits on science, technology, and innovation.
  • Oriental Pearl Tower: This iconic tower is one of Shanghai’s most recognizable landmarks. Visitors can take a glass-bottomed walkway high above the city, or enjoy the views from the observation deck.
  • Nanpu Bridge: This cable-stayed bridge spans the Huangpu River and is a popular spot for photographers and sightseers.
  • Shanghai History Museum: This museum showcases the history of Shanghai through interactive exhibits and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Pudong New Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Century Park: This large park offers a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city. Visitors can rent boats, take a relaxing stroll, or participate in outdoor activities like tai chi and yoga.
  • Dongtan Wetland Park: This massive park is home to diverse wildlife and offers a glimpse into the region’s natural beauty. Visitors can go birdwatching, take a bike ride, or explore the wetlands on foot.
  • Fuxing Island: This small island on the Huangpu River is a hidden gem. Visitors can take a ferry across the river and explore the island’s quaint streets and shops.
  • Binjiang Forest Park: This forested park offers scenic views of the Huangpu River and a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pudong New Area

Pudong New Area has some excellent hiking and biking routes. Here are a few to check out:

  • Jinjiang Amusement Park Greenway: This scenic greenway runs alongside the Huangpu River and is a popular spot for cycling and walking.
  • Dishui Lake Loop: This 10-kilometer loop offers tranquil views of the lake and the surrounding countryside.
  • Xinchang Ancient Town to Dongtan Wetland Park: This 20-kilometer route is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Pudong New Area: Activities and Tours

Pudong New Area is full of fun and unique things to do. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Shanghai Disney Resort: This world-class theme park offers a variety of rides and attractions for all ages.
  • Cirque du Soleil: The famous circus troupe has a permanent residence in Shanghai, offering awe-inspiring performances that blend acrobatics, music, and art.
  • Shanghai Tower Art Center: This cutting-edge exhibition space showcases the latest in contemporary art.
  • Maglev Train Ride: Take a ride on the world’s fastest commercial train, which travels from Pudong International Airport to Longyang Road at 430 km/h.
  • Shanghai French Concession Walking Tour: Explore one of Shanghai’s most picturesque districts on foot, with an expert guide to show you the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pudong New Area

If you have a week to spend in Pudong New Area, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the sights and sounds of Lujiazui, including the Shanghai Tower and World Financial Center.
  • Day 2: Visit the Shanghai Museum of Science and Technology and take a stroll through Century Park.
  • Day 3: Explore the city’s history at the Shanghai History Museum and take a ferry to Fuxing Island.
  • Day 4: Get a taste of local culture with a visit to the ancient water town of Zhujiajiao, just outside of Shanghai.
  • Day 5: Go birdwatching and hiking at the Dongtan Wetland Park.
  • Day 6: Take a trip to Shanghai Disney Resort or see a performance of Cirque du Soleil.
  • Day 7: End your trip with a scenic bike ride along the Jinjiang Amusement Park Greenway.

Food and Drink Scene of Pudong New Area

No trip to Pudong New Area is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Xiaolongbao: These steamed soup dumplings are a Shanghai classic, with delicate wrappers and savory fillings.
  • Pan-fried pork buns: Similar to Xiaolongbao, these buns are pan-fried for a crispy exterior and juicy pork filling.
  • Lion’s head meatballs: These hearty meatballs are made with ground pork and vegetables and are a popular comfort food.
  • Shanghai-style noodles: Shanghai is known for its noodle dishes, which range from savory to spicy.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: This strong Chinese liquor is often served at banquets and celebrations.
  • Erlaocha: This sweet tea is made with jasmine flowers and is a refreshing drink on hot days.
  • Shikumen: This local beer is brewed in Shanghai and is a popular drink among locals.

What to Buy in Pudong New Area: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Shanghai has a long history of tea culture, and you can find a variety of tea shops selling high-quality teas.
  • Calligraphy and artwork: Shanghai has a thriving artistic scene, and you can find beautiful calligraphy and artwork in galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Pudong New Area

Shanghai has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Etiquette: It’s best to err on the side of formality when interacting with locals. Use formal titles and proper greetings.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not common in China. In restaurants, a service charge is typically included in the bill.
  • Customs: Shanghai has its own unique customs and traditions, such as the Mid-Autumn Festival and the Dragon Boat Festival.

Best Time to Visit Pudong New Area

The best time to visit Pudong New Area is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pudong New Area

Here are a few things to remember during your visit to Pudong New Area:

Do:

  • Use public transportation: Shanghai has an excellent public transportation system, including buses, subways, and taxis.
  • Try the local cuisine: Shanghai has some of the best food in China, so be sure to try a variety of dishes!
  • Take in the views: Pudong’s skyline is one of the most impressive in the world, so be sure to take plenty of photos.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive: Shanghai is a bustling city, but be mindful of noise levels in public spaces.
  • Leave valuables unattended: As with any big city, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings.

Safety and Health

Here’s what you need to know about staying safe and healthy in Pudong New Area:

Safety in Pudong New Area

Pudong New Area is generally a safe district, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Pudong New Area

Shanghai has good medical facilities and hospitals, but it’s always best to take precautions against illness. Make sure to wash your hands regularly and drink plenty of bottled water.
