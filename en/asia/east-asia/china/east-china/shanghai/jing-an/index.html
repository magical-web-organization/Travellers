Jing’an, located in central Shanghai, is a bustling cosmopolitan district that boasts a rich cultural legacy and stunning natural scenery. Only a stone’s throw away from many of Shanghai’s most famous landmarks, such as the Bund and the French Concession, Jing’an is a must-visit destination for any traveler interested in exploring both the traditional and modern facets of China. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey of discovery, highlighting the best things to do and see in Jing’an, including cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. Introduction to Jing’an

Jing’an is a district in central Shanghai that has been a hub of commerce and culture for centuries. It’s named after the famous Jing’an Temple, a Buddhist temple that dates back to the Song Dynasty (960-1279 AD) and is now a popular tourist attraction. Jing’an is home to a diverse population of locals, expats, and travelers, and has some of Shanghai’s best shopping, dining, and entertainment options. Whether you’re interested in exploring the district’s cultural heritage, trying out new cuisines, or simply soaking up the atmosphere, Jing’an has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Jing’an

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jing’an is home to some of Shanghai’s most important cultural landmarks, including museums, temples, and historical sites. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Jing’an Temple: A 1,700-year-old Buddhist temple that features traditional Chinese temple architecture and a rare collection of Buddhist statues.
  • Shanghai Natural History Museum: A modern museum with interactive exhibits showcasing China’s ecological diversity and natural resources.
  • Shanghai Propaganda Poster Art Centre: A unique museum that displays a collection of over 6,000 vintage Chinese propaganda posters from the mid-20th century.
  • Former Residence of Mao Zedong: A historical site that once housed the Communist Party’s Shanghai Committee during the Cultural Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Jing’an: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jing’an is also known for its stunning natural scenery, parks, and outdoor activities. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Daning Lingshi Park: A beautiful park with winding paths, tranquil ponds, and picturesque gardens. It’s a popular spot for picnics, morning exercises, and Tai Chi.
  • Suzhou Creek: A waterway that flows through the district and is lined with historic buildings, warehouses, and bridges. It’s a great place to take a leisurely walk or bike ride along the waterfront.
  • Yanzhong Guangchang Park: A spacious public park with gardens, fountains, and a lake. It’s a popular spot for jogging and cycling, and hosts various outdoor events throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jing’an

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventures, Jing’an has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the top routes include:

  • Daning Greenway: A 10-kilometer scenic trail that runs along the Daning River and features lush greenery and picturesque views.
  • North-South Elevated Road: A 35-kilometer elevated bikeway that stretches across central Shanghai and offers stunning cityscape views.
  • Moganshan Road: A historic road that winds through the Moganshan Hill Scenic Area and offers a glimpse into Shanghai’s past.

Best Things to Do in Jing’an: Activities and Tours

Jing’an has a diverse range of activities and tours to suit all interests, from cooking classes to art exhibitions to city tours. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Shanghai Art Museum: A modern museum that showcases contemporary Chinese art and hosts various cultural events and exhibitions.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Chinese dishes with local chefs and immerse yourself in the flavors of Jing’an.
  • Street Art Tours: Explore Jing’an’s vibrant street art scene with local guides and discover hidden gems and urban art installations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jing’an

Jing’an is a vibrant district with plenty of activities and sights to explore. If you’re planning a trip to Jing’an, we recommend the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Jing’an Temple and Shanghai Natural History Museum
  • Day 2: Daning Lingshi Park and Suzhou Creek
  • Day 3: Former Residence of Mao Zedong and Shanghai Art Museum
  • Day 4: Cooking Class and Yanzhong Guangchang Park
  • Day 5: Moganshan Road and Street Art Tour
  • Day 6: North-South Elevated Road and Shanghai Propaganda Poster Art Centre
  • Day 7: Relaxation and Shopping

Food and Drink Scene of Jing’an

Jing’an is home to a diverse array of cuisines and local specialties, from street food to upscale restaurants to international fare. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jianbing: A traditional Chinese breakfast dish made with a pancake, egg, scallions, and spices.
  • Xiaolongbao: Steamed pork dumplings with a thin wrapper and soup inside.
  • Shengjianbao: Pan-fried pork dumplings with a crispy bottom and juicy filling.
  • Spicy Crayfish: Freshwater crayfish cooked in a spicy sauce with garlic and chili peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: A strong distilled liquor made from sorghum or other grains.
  • Huangjiu: A traditional Chinese wine made from fermented rice, wheat, or barley.
  • Bubble Tea: A sweet and refreshing tea-based drink with tapioca pearls.

Where to Stay in Jing’an: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jing’an has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxurious hotels to affordable hostels to cozy guesthouses. Some of the top options include:

  • The Jing An Shangri-La: A five-star hotel with upscale amenities and elegant rooms.
  • Le Tour Traveler’s Rest Youth Hostel: A popular choice for backpackers and budget travelers, with dormitory and private rooms available.
  • Shanghai Fish Inn Bund: A cozy guesthouse with a friendly atmosphere and a convenient location near the Bund.

What to Buy in Jing’an: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jing’an is a great place to shop for unique souvenirs and local products, from traditional handicrafts to trendy fashion items. Some of the best shopping destinations include:

  • Nanjing Road: Shanghai’s premier shopping street, with a wide range of department stores, boutiques, and souvenir shops.
  • Jiuguang Department Store: A luxury shopping mall with upscale international and Chinese brands.
  • Changshu Road Fabric Market: A bustling market with a wide range of fabrics, textiles, and tailoring services.

Local Culture and Customs of Jing’an

Jing’an is a cosmopolitan district with a rich cultural heritage and a diverse population. To show respect for local customs and traditions, it’s important to keep the following tips in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or religious sites.
  • Use both hands when giving or receiving gifts, business cards, or other items.
  • Avoid giving clocks, knives, or other sharp objects as gifts, as they are considered bad luck.
  • Be mindful of cultural differences when interacting with locals, such as punctuality, personal space, and politeness.

Best Time to Visit Jing’an

The best time to visit Jing’an is during the spring or autumn, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be chilly and rainy. However, Jing’an is a year-round destination with plenty of indoor and outdoor activities to enjoy, so you can plan your trip according to your preferences and interests.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jing’an

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Jing’an, here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Mandarin phrases, such as greetings and directions.
  • Try new foods and drinks, and immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Dress for the weather and wear comfortable shoes for walking or biking.
  • Carry a map, a guidebook, or a translation app.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection, as it’s considered impolite in Chinese culture.
  • Speak loudly or in a confrontational manner.
  • Touch people’s heads or shoulders, as it’s considered rude.
  • Bargain aggressively or haggle excessively with street vendors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jing’an

Jing’an is a safe district with low crime rates and a high level of public security. However, like any bustling city, it’s important to take some basic precautions to stay safe:

  • Keep your valuables, such as money, passport, and electronics, in a secure place.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Use licensed taxis or public transportation instead of unmarked or unofficial vehicles.
  • Follow traffic rules and signs, and use designated pedestrian crossings when crossing the street.

Health in Jing’an

Jing’an is a modern and well-equipped city with quality medical care and facilities. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic health precautions during your trip:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid water-borne diseases.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites, especially during the summer season.
  • Carry some basic medication and first-aid supplies, such as headache pills and band-aids.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues or emergencies.

Getting to Jing’an: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jing’an is located in central Shanghai and is easily accessible by various transportation options, including:

  • Airports: Shanghai Pudong International Airport and Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport are both located within an hour’s drive from Jing’an, with multiple transportation options available.
  • Metro: Jing’an is served by several metro lines, including Line 2, Line 7, and Line 13, which provide convenient connections to other parts of Shanghai.
  • Bus: Jing’an has several bus stations and routes that serve the district and connect to other parts of Shanghai.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Jing’an, but it’s important to use licensed and metered taxis to avoid scams or overcharging.

How to Get Around in Jing’an

Jing’an is a compact district that can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by public transportation. Some of the most convenient and efficient transportation options include:

  • Metro: Shanghai’s metro system is affordable, clean, and efficient, with several lines that serve Jing’an and other districts.
  • Bike Share: Jing’an has several bike sharing services, such as Mobike, ofo, and Hello Bike, that allow you to rent bikes on the go.
  • Taxi: Taxis are a convenient and affordable option for short distances or when you have heavy luggage or bags.

Where to Go Next from Jing’an: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jing’an is located in the heart of Shanghai, and many of the city’s most famous attractions and districts are within a short distance. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • The Bund: A waterfront area that showcases Shanghai’s colonial past and modern skyline, with stunning views of the Huangpu River.
  • French Concession: A historic district that features tree-lined streets, art deco buildings, and trendy cafes and boutiques.
  • Shanghai Tower: One of the tallest skyscrapers in the world, with observation decks that offer panoramic views of the city.
  • Zhujiajiao Water Town: A traditional Chinese water town that features winding canals, old bridges, and ancient buildings.
