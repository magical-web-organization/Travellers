Introduction to Old City

Old City, located in the heart of Shanghai, is a glimpse into the city’s past. This historical district is home to several museums, temples and traditional architecture that dates back to the Ming and Qing dynasties. Whether you’re interested in history or simply want to experience the old Shanghai charm, Old City is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Old City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shanghai Museum of Traditional Chinese Medicine

Located in the heart of Old City, the Shanghai Museum of Traditional Chinese Medicine offers an immersive experience about the history and usage of Chinese medicine. Here, visitors can learn about the different herbs, treatments and systems involved in traditional Chinese medicine.

Yuyuan Garden

Yuyuan Garden is a beautiful oasis located in the heart of Old City. Featuring traditional Chinese architecture and designs, this garden offers visitors an opportunity to relax and unwind. In addition, there are several tea houses and restaurants nearby where visitors can try local cuisine.

City God Temple

Built during the Ming Dynasty, the City God Temple is a popular attraction in Old City. This temple is dedicated to the City God, who was believed to protect the community against disasters and bring good fortune.

Natural Attractions of Old City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Huangpu River Cruise

Take a cruise on the Huangpu river for beautiful views of the city skyline. Admire the towering skyscrapers on one side and colonial architecture on the other, for a unique perspective of Shanghai.

Fuxing Park

Fuxing Park is a serene park nestled in the heart of Old City. Here you can relax and take in the beautiful scenery or participate in activities such as tai chi or ballroom dancing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old City

Shikumen Walking Tour

Explore Old City’s Shikumen architecture by taking a walking tour through the winding alleys and narrow streets. These traditional neighborhoods offer a unique glimpse into life in old Shanghai.

Rent a Bike and Explore

Rent a bike and explore the local neighborhoods of Old City at your own pace. This is an excellent way to experience the charm and vibe of the city.

Best Things to Do in Old City: Activities and Tours

Shanghai’s Hidden Alleyways Tour

Join a walking tour of the hidden alleyways of Old City, where you can discover hidden gems and learn about the local lifestyle and history.

Chinese Cooking Class

Take a Chinese cooking class in a traditional Shikumen home and learn about the rich flavors and techniques involved in Chinese cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Old City

Must-Try Dishes

Xiaolongbao

Xiaolongbao is a famous Chinese dumpling that can be found all over Shanghai. These small, steamed dumplings are filled with soup and can be found in many restaurants in Old City.

Hairy Crab

During the autumn months, the hairy crab is one of the most popular dishes in Shanghai. This local delicacy is highly prized for its sweet and succulent meat.

Local Beverages

Baijiu

Baijiu is a traditional Chinese liquor made from fermented grains. It has a high alcohol content and is often used for toasting at formal or celebratory events.

Tea

Tea is an important part of Chinese culture, and there are several traditional tea houses in Old City where you can try different types of tea while enjoying the serene environment.

Where to Stay in Old City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Indigo Shanghai on the Bund

Located on the border of Old City, the Hotel Indigo Shanghai offers modern amenities alongside traditional Chinese design.

Mingtown Etour Youth Hostel

The Mingtown Etour Youth Hostel is a budget-friendly option that still offers great amenities, such as a rooftop terrace with scenic views.

What to Buy in Old City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Antiques

Old City is the perfect place to go antiquing, with several shops and markets selling traditional Chinese art and furniture.

Silk Products

Silk products are a great souvenir to bring back from Shanghai, and there are several shops in Old City selling silk products such as scarves, clothing and home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Old City

Shanghai, like much of China, has its own nuances and customs that visitors should be aware of. Be sure to research the appropriate customs when visiting sacred sites, as well as respecting cultural differences while visiting the city.

Best Time to Visit Old City

The best time to visit Old City is in the autumn and spring months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. However, keep in mind that certain festivals or events may attract larger crowds during these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old City

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or temple.
  • Use both hands when giving or receiving gifts or business cards.
  • Say “ni hao” or “hello” when entering a store or restaurant.

Don’t:

  • Touch or pat someone’s head as it is considered rude.
  • Discuss sensitive topics such as politics or religion.
  • Use your cell phone in a temple or other sacred site.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old City

Old City is generally a safe area, but keep in mind common safety precautions such as watching out for pickpockets and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Old City

Be sure to drink bottled water and avoid street food vendors that may not have proper sanitation practices. It is also recommended to have a basic understanding of Mandarin to communicate your ailments or allergies to medical professionals.

Getting to Old City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Old City

The two closest airports to Old City are the Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport and the Shanghai Pudong International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Old City

The Shanghai Old City Terminal Bus Station is located in the heart of Old City, and offers several bus routes to other parts of the city.

Other Options

Opting for the metro system is a popular and convenient way to travel within Shanghai. There are several metro stops near Old City, including Yu Garden Station and Dashijie Station.

How to Get Around in Old City

Walking is the best way to explore Old City, as many of the attractions and neighborhoods are within close proximity of each other. Biking is also a popular option for those who want to cover more ground.

Where to Go Next from Old City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Bund

Located just across the Huangpu River, The Bund is one of Shanghai’s most iconic attractions, offering a skyline view of the city.

French Concession

Experience a different side of Shanghai by visiting the French Concession, known for its tree-lined streets, trendy shops and restaurants.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in learning about Chinese medicine or exploring traditional alleyways, Old City is a worthwhile destination. With a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, Old City is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.
