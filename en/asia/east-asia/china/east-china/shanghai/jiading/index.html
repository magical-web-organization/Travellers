Introduction to Jiading

Jiading is a district located in Shanghai, East China. It is an ancient city with a rich history dating back to the Ming and Qing dynasties.

The city boasts a diverse array of attractions, from historical sites and art centers to parks and nature reserves. It is also famous for its delicious food and friendly locals.

Whether you’re looking for a cultural experience or an outdoor adventure, Jiading has something for everyone. Read on to discover the best places to visit, where to eat and stay, and how to get around this fascinating city.

List of Places to Visit in Jiading

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jiading has a wealth of cultural attractions that give visitors a glimpse into its rich history.

Confucian Temple

The Confucian Temple is a 700-year-old temple dedicated to Confucius, the great philosopher and educator in ancient China. It was first built in the Ming Dynasty and has undergone several renovations. The temple features beautifully landscaped gardens, ancient steles, and calligraphy galleries.

Jiading Museum

The Jiading Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. It displays more than 60,000 historical artifacts, including pottery, porcelain, and jade from the Ming and Qing dynasties, as well as exhibits on local folk customs and revolutionary history.

Fahua Pagoda

The Fahua Pagoda is a 400-year-old pagoda located in the heart of Jiading’s downtown area. The seven-story pagoda is a masterpiece of Ming Dynasty architecture and offers stunning views of the surrounding cityscape.

Natural Attractions of Jiading: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jiading is home to several beautiful parks and nature reserves that provide ample opportunities for hiking and biking.

Xincheng Park

Xincheng Park is an urban park located in the heart of Jiading. It features pedestrian paths, colorful gardens, and a playground for children. It’s a great place for an afternoon stroll or a morning jog.

Anting Old Street

Anting Old Street is a charming street lined with traditional shops and restaurants. It’s a great place to wander and explore the local culture. Visitors can also rent a bike and explore the area’s beautiful countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiading

The best way to explore Jiading’s scenic routes is on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to take in Jiading:

  • Qianwei Ecological Park: This park offers a variety of trails for hikers and bikers that wind through stunning scenery, including lakes and lush forests.

  • Huilongtan Park: This park features a scenic lake and several hiking and biking trails. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy nature.

  • Guancheng Waterfront Park: This park is located on the banks of the Tianshan River and is popular with locals and visitors alike. It offers excellent hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the surrounding river and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Jiading: Activities and Tours

Jiading offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best:

  • Jiading Water Town Tour: This tour takes visitors on a journey through Jiading’s ancient water towns, showcasing the city’s traditional architecture and way of life.

  • Food Tasting Tour: Jiading is famous for its delicious food, and a food tour is a great way to experience the local cuisine. Visitors can sample everything from street snacks to upscale restaurants.

  • Kunqu Opera Performance: Kunqu Opera is a form of Chinese classic opera that originated in Jiading. Visitors can attend a live performance at the Jiading Museum or other local theaters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jiading

For visitors planning a week-long trip to Jiading, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Jiading’s historical attractions, including the Confucian Temple and Jiading Museum.

Day 2: Take a bike tour of Anting Old Street and surrounding countryside.

Day 3: Visit Xincheng Park for a morning walk and then head to Qianwei Ecological Park for an afternoon hike.

Day 4: Take a food tasting tour of Jiading and sample the city’s delicious cuisine.

Day 5: Attend a Kunqu Opera performance at the Jiading Museum.

Day 6: Visit Guancheng Waterfront Park for a morning bike ride and then spend the afternoon at Huilongtan Park.

Day 7: Take a day trip to Shanghai and explore the city’s iconic landmarks, such as the Bund and Oriental Pearl Tower.

Food and Drink Scene of Jiading

Jiading is known for its delicious cuisine, particularly its local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiading Roast Duck: A juicy, flavorful roast duck that’s marinated and cooked using traditional techniques.

  • Jiading Baozi: These are steamed buns stuffed with savory fillings like pork, shrimp, or vegetables.

  • Jiading Shiquan Yu: This is a local fish dish that’s cooked in a sweet and sour sauce and served with vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Jiading Rice Wine: Made from glutinous rice and sweet potatoes, Jiading Rice Wine is a popular local beverage.

  • Jiading Oolong Tea: This is a high-quality tea that’s grown in the surrounding mountains and known for its floral aroma and smooth taste.

Where to Stay in Jiading: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jiading offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference.

  • Courtyard by Marriott Shanghai Jiading: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Jiading’s central business district.

  • Hanting Express Shanghai Jiading Chengxi: A budget hotel located near the Confucian Temple and other historical attractions.

  • Jiading Kaida Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in the city center, offering easy access to shopping and dining.

What to Buy in Jiading: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jiading is known for its handicrafts, particularly its hand-painted porcelain and bamboo products.

  • Jiading Blue and White Porcelain: This is a type of hand-painted porcelain that’s been made in Jiading for over 500 years. It’s known for its intricate designs and beautiful colors.

  • Jiading Bamboo Products: Jiading is also famous for its bamboo products, including baskets, plates, and cups. These make great souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiading

Jiading has a rich cultural heritage that’s evident in its architecture, food, and customs. Here are some examples:

  • Confucianism: Jiading has a strong Confucian tradition, which is reflected in its temples and monuments dedicated to the great philosopher.

  • Dragon Boat Festival: This traditional festival is celebrated in Jiading and features dragon boat races, traditional foods, and other activities.

Best Time to Visit Jiading

The best time to visit Jiading is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are cold and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiading

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Jiading:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

  • Take part in cultural festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.

  • Litter or damage local attractions.

  • Ignore safety warnings or guidelines.

Safety and Health

Jiading is a relatively safe city for visitors, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Jiading

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables in public places.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

  • Be cautious when crossing busy streets or intersections.

Health in Jiading

  • It’s advisable to drink bottled water instead of tap water.

  • Be careful when eating street food or in local restaurants as sanitation standards may vary.

  • Bring any necessary medications and relevant health documents.

Getting to Jiading: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jiading is easily accessible from Shanghai and other major cities in China. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Jiading

  • Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport: Located 30 km from Jiading, and offers domestic and international flights.

  • Shanghai Pudong International Airport: Located 60 km from Jiading, and offers international flights to cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Jiading

  • Jiading Bus Station: Located in the city center and has buses to other cities in China.

  • Shanghai South Bus Station: Located in downtown Shanghai and offers buses to other major cities in China.

Other Options

  • Train: The Shanghai Railway Station offers trains to other cities in China, including high-speed trains.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Jiading and offer a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Jiading

In Jiading, the most popular ways to get around are by taxi, bus, or bike. Taxis are readily available, and buses run on a regular schedule. Biking is also popular, especially for exploring the city’s scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Jiading: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jiading is conveniently located near several other popular destinations. Here are some nearby excursions:

  • Shanghai: Just a short drive or train ride away, Shanghai is a bustling metropolis with a host of attractions, including the iconic Oriental Pearl Tower and The Bund.

  • Suzhou: Located just an hour’s drive from Jiading, Suzhou is famous for its classical gardens, water towns, and silk culture.

Conclusion

Jiading is a culturally rich and naturally beautiful city that’s well worth a visit. From historical attractions and scenic parks to delicious food and friendly locals, there’s something for everyone in Jiading. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to this fascinating city.
