Introduction to Nanhui

Nanhui is a district located in the southeastern suburbs of Shanghai, China. This picturesque region boasts a rich cultural and historical heritage, as well as breathtaking natural scenery. With its museums, historical sites, hiking trails, and food scene, there’s plenty to explore in Nanhui.

List of Places to Visit in Nanhui

Whether you’re interested in exploring Nanhui’s cultural heritage, discovering its natural wonders, or sampling its famous cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this district. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nanhui Old City - Step back in time and explore the charming streets, temples, and gardens of Nanhui’s well-preserved old city.
  • Shanghai Natural History Museum - Learn about the natural history of the region at this fascinating museum featuring exhibits on geology, paleontology, and more.
  • Tianfei Palace - Visit the largest Taoist temple in Shanghai, dedicated to the goddess of the sea, and take in the stunning architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Shanghai Maritime Museum - Delve into the maritime history of Shanghai through interactive exhibits and immersive displays.

Natural Attractions of Nanhui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Donghai Bridge - Take a stroll or bike ride along the world’s longest sea bridge, and enjoy the stunning views of the coast and sea.
  • Binjiang Forest Park - Explore the lush greenery and peaceful lakes of this expansive park, ideal for picnics, cycling, and hiking.
  • Nanhui Taohua Village - Visit this charming village, surrounded by orchards and fields of flowers, and enjoy the breathtaking blossoms in spring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanhui

Nanhui has plenty of scenic trails and bike routes that allow visitors to take in the natural beauty of this region. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Donghai Bridge Hiking and Biking Trail - Stretching over 30km, this trail runs alongside the Donghai Bridge and offers stunning views of the coast.
  • Binjiang Forest Park Hiking Trail - This easy hike takes visitors through the picturesque forest park, with its tranquil lakes and lush greenery.
  • Gaodong Wetland Reserve Biking Trail - This trail winds through the stunning wetlands of Nanhui, attracting a variety of bird species, and is a popular spot for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Nanhui: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the cultural and natural attractions, Nanhui also boasts a wide range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Nanhui include:

  • Food tours - Sample some of the locally produced specialties, such as dried seafood, tofu, and honey.
  • Water activities - Take a boat ride or go fishing on the nearby seas or rivers.
  • Cultural performances - Enjoy traditional Chinese performances, such as acrobatics, martial arts, and opera.

Food and Drink Scene of Nanhui

Nanhui is renowned for its traditional cuisine, which features local specialties such as dried seafood, tofu, and honey. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nanhui Dried Seafood - This dish features locally-sourced dried seafood, such as abalone, scallops, and seaweed.
  • Nanhui Tofu - This dish is made from locally-sourced tofu, which is known for its creamy texture and delicate flavor.
  • Nanhui Honey - This sweet treat is made from the local bees, which feed on the orchards and flower fields in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Shanghai Barley Tea - This refreshing tea is brewed from roasted barley and has a nutty flavor.
  • Nanhui Plum Wine - This sweet wine is made from locally-sourced plums and is a popular drink during festive occasions.

Where to Stay in Nanhui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nanhui boasts a range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • InterContinental Shanghai Wonderland - This luxurious hotel is situated in a former quarry and features stunning architecture and world-class amenities.
  • Nanhui Oushe Guesthouse - This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Nanhui Old City and features traditional Chinese decor and a serene courtyard garden.
  • Home Inn Shanghai Nanhui Hedong Road - This budget-friendly hotel is located in the city center, close to the main attractions and transport links.

What to Buy in Nanhui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nanhui is known for its locally-produced specialties, making it the perfect place to shop for souvenirs and gifts. Here are some top items to bring home:

  • Nanhui Dried Seafood - This makes for a great gift for food lovers and is easily transportable.
  • Nanhui Tofu - This delicacy can be vacuum-packed and makes for a unique gift for foodies.
  • Nanhui Honey - This sweet treat is available in various sizes and packaging and is perfect for taking back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Nanhui

Nanhui has a rich cultural heritage, with strong Taoist and Buddhist influences. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites. Nanhui is also known for its celebration of traditional festivals and customs, such as the Spring Festival and the Dragon Boat Festival.

Best Time to Visit Nanhui

The best time to visit Nanhui is from March to May or from September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Avoid visiting during the summer months, when it can be hot and humid, and during the winter season when it can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanhui

Visitors to Nanhui should follow some basic rules and etiquettes to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and specialties.

Don’t:

  • Damage the natural or cultural heritage sites.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Overuse of plastic products.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanhui

Nanhui is generally a safe place to visit, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should take common precautions like not leaving valuables unattended, especially in crowded places.

Health in Nanhui

Visitors to Nanhui should ensure that they have adequate medical insurance. The local hospitals are generally of good quality, but communication may be difficult for non-Chinese speakers. Visitors should also take steps to protect themselves against mosquito bites, as mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria are endemic in this region.

Getting to Nanhui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nanhui is easily accessible from Shanghai and other nearby cities, thanks to its well-connected transport links. Here are some key transport options:

Airports Near Nanhui

  • Shanghai Pudong International Airport - This airport is located around 20km from Nanhui and has direct flights to most major cities worldwide.
  • Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport - This airport is located around 50km from Nanhui and has connections to other cities in China and neighboring countries.

Bus Stations Near Nanhui

  • Shanghai South Bus Station - This station has regular direct buses to Nanhui and other destinations in the region.
  • Pudong Airport Bus Station - This station has frequent buses to Nanhui and other parts of Shanghai.

Other Options

  • Taxi - Taxis are widely available and a convenient way to get around the city.
  • Metro - There is a metro line connecting Nanhui to central Shanghai and other parts of the city.

How to Get Around in Nanhui

The best way to get around Nanhui is by taxi, metro, or bicycle. Visitors can also hire private cars with drivers to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Nanhui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Nanhui, visitors can head to some nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Hangzhou - This scenic city, located around 180km from Nanhui, is famous for its picturesque West Lake and tea plantations.
  • Suzhou - This charming city, located around 120km from Nanhui, is known for its classical Chinese gardens and canals.
  • Shanghai - This bustling metropolis, located just a few kilometers from Nanhui, offers a range of cultural, culinary, and nightlife experiences.

Conclusion

Nanhui is a hidden gem worth exploring for anyone visiting Shanghai. From its rich culture and history to its scenic natural beauty, there’s something for everyone in this district. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to experience the best that Nanhui has to offer!
