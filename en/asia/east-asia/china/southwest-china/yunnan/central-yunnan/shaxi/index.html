Introduction to Shaxi

Located in central Yunnan, Shaxi is a charming ancient town with a fascinating cultural heritage that dates back over 2000 years. Shaxi is a well-preserved ancient town that has been around since the Tang and Song dynasties, with remarkable examples of traditional Bai style architecture and a legacy of the tea-horse trade route that traversed the region. Inspiring landscapes, tranquil surroundings, stunning natural vistas, and unique structures make Shaxi a fascinating place to explore.

Local Culture and Customs of Shaxi

Shaxi is home to the Bai people and has a unique cultural heritage that is steeped in history and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to observe local customs and respect the cultural heritage of the Bai people.

Best Time to Visit Shaxi

The best time to visit Shaxi is between September and November, as the weather is mild, and the area is engulfed in beautiful fall colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shaxi

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the local people
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites
  • Try traditional local cuisine
  • Learn some basic Chinese phrases

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Touch or damage ancient artifacts and ruins
  • Disrespect the environment and natural landmarks

Safety and Health

Safety in Shaxi

Shaxi is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to secure their belongings and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Shaxi

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and take basic precautions to prevent illness.

Getting to Shaxi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shaxi

The nearest airport to Shaxi is the Dali Airport (DLU), which is approximately 80 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Shaxi

There are several bus stations in the area, but the closest to Shaxi is the Jianchuan Bus Station, which is located about 30 kilometers away.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car, motorcycle, or bicycle to explore the area or take a guided tour.

How to Get Around in Shaxi

Visitors can get around Shaxi by hiring a car or bicycle, taking a taxi or hopping on a local bus.
