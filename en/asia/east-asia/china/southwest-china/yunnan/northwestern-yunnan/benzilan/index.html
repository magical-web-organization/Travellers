If you are looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in China that is culturally rich and naturally beautiful, then you should definitely visit Benzilan. Situated in the northwestern part of Yunnan province, this small town is surrounded by lush greenery, snow-capped mountains, and crystal clear rivers. Benzilan is known for its traditional Tibetan culture, delicious food, and warm hospitality. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Benzilan and make the most out of your visit.

Introduction to Benzilan

Benzilan is a small town located on the famous Sichuan-Tibet Highway (National Road 318). It is a part of the Deqin County, which is situated in the northwestern part of Yunnan province. Benzilan is surrounded by hills and mountains, and it is situated on the banks of the Jinsha (also called Lancang) river. The town is known for its traditional Tibetan culture and unique architecture. The local people are warm and friendly, and they welcome visitors with open arms. Benzilan is a perfect destination for those who want to experience Tibet and its culture, without actually going to Tibet.

List of Places to Visit in Benzilan

Benzilan is a small town, but there are many places to visit and things to do here. Here is a list of top places to visit in Benzilan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Benzilan Monastery: This 300-year-old monastery is a must-visit attraction in Benzilan. It is located on a hill overlooking the town, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and river.

  2. Benzilan Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the local Tibetan people. It has a collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures that represent the Tibetan way of life.

Natural Attractions of Benzilan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jinsha River: The Jinsha river is the lifeline of Benzilan. Visitors can go for a walk along the riverbank or take a boat ride to enjoy the scenic views of the river and the surrounding mountains.

  2. Meili Snow Mountain: This is one of the most beautiful mountain ranges in China, and it is a must-visit attraction in Benzilan. Visitors can take a cable car ride to the top of the mountain to enjoy the spectacular views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benzilan

  1. Benzilan to Feilaisi: This is a popular hiking route that takes visitors from Benzilan to Feilaisi, a small town situated on the slope of Meili Snow Mountain. The route offers stunning views of the snow-capped mountains and the Jinsha river.

  2. Benzilan to Cizhong: This is another popular hiking route that takes visitors from Benzilan to Cizhong, a small village situated in the heart of the vineyards. The route offers scenic views of the river and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Benzilan: Activities and Tours

  1. Taste local Tibetan food: Benzilan is known for its delicious Tibetan food, such as yak meat, butter tea, and tsampa. Visitors can sample these dishes in local restaurants and cafes.

  2. Attend a Tibetan festival: Benzilan hosts several Tibetan festivals throughout the year, such as the Tibetan New Year, the Horse Racing Festival, and the Harvest Festival. These festivals are colorful and vibrant, and they offer visitors a glimpse into the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Benzilan

If you have one week to spend in Benzilan, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Benzilan and explore the town.
  • Day 2: Visit the Benzilan Monastery and the Benzilan Museum.
  • Day 3: Go for a hike from Benzilan to Feilaisi.
  • Day 4: Take a cable car ride to Meili Snow Mountain.
  • Day 5: Visit the Cizhong village and explore the vineyards.
  • Day 6: Attend a local Tibetan festival.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views of the Jinsha river.

Food and Drink Scene of Benzilan

Benzilan is known for its traditional Tibetan food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Yak meat: This is a staple food of the local Tibetan people. Visitors can try yak meat dishes in local restaurants and cafes.

  2. Butter tea: This is a traditional Tibetan tea made with butter and salt. It is a unique and delicious drink that is popular among the locals.

Local Beverages

  1. Tibetan barley wine: This is a popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented barley. It has a unique taste and is commonly served during festivals and celebrations.

  2. Yak butter beer: This is a non-alcoholic beverage made from yak butter, sugar, and water. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Benzilan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Benzilan has a good range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Benzilan Shangri-La Hotel: This is a luxury hotel situated on the banks of the Jinsha river. It offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding mountains.

  • Woniuzhuang Hostel: This is a budget-friendly hostel located in the center of Benzilan. It offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  • Wuman Guesthouse: This is a traditional Tibetan house that has been converted into a guesthouse. It offers a unique and authentic experience for visitors.

What to Buy in Benzilan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Benzilan is known for its handmade crafts and local products. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home:

  • Tibetan carpets and rugs: These are handcrafted carpets and rugs that are made by the local Tibetan people. They come in a variety of designs and colors.

  • Tibetan incense: This is a popular product that is used for religious and spiritual purposes. It is made from natural herbs and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Benzilan

Benzilan is a town that is deeply rooted in Tibetan culture and customs. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs by dressing modestly, avoiding loud noises, and asking for permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Benzilan

The best time to visit Benzilan is from April to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild and dry, and the scenery is spectacular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Benzilan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local Tibetan food and drink.
  • Attend a local festival.

Don’t:

  • Engage in loud and disruptive behavior.
  • Take photographs without asking for permission.
  • Disrespect the local religion and beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Benzilan

Benzilan is a safe town, and the local people are friendly and welcoming. However, visitors should take precautions, such as carrying a map and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Benzilan

Visitors should be aware of altitude sickness, as the town is situated at a high altitude. It is recommended to acclimatize slowly and drink plenty of water.

Getting to Benzilan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Benzilan is located on the famous Sichuan-Tibet Highway (National Road 318). Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Benzilan

  • Diqing Shangri-La Airport: This is the nearest airport to Benzilan, and it is located about 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Benzilan

  • Deqin County Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Deqin county, and it offers regular bus services to Benzilan.

Other Options

  • Private car hire: Visitors can hire a private car to travel to Benzilan from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Benzilan

Benzilan is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot. Taxis and bicycles are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Benzilan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider:

  • Shangri-La: This is a famous tourist destination in Yunnan province, and it is situated about 200 kilometers away from Benzilan.

  • Lijiang: This is another popular tourist destination in Yunnan province, and it is situated about 400 kilometers away from Benzilan.

Conclusion

Benzilan is a hidden gem in the Northwestern Yunnan province of China. With its stunning natural scenery, rich culture and traditions, and warm hospitality, it is a destination that should not be missed.
