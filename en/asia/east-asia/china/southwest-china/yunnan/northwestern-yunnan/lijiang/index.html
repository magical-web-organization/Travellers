Introduction to Lijiang

Nestled in northwestern Yunnan, Lijiang is a ancient and picturesque city that dates back to the Song Dynasty (960-1279 CE). The city is surrounded by stunning snow-capped peaks and boasts a well-preserved old town area that is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can explore traditional Naxi architecture and ancient streets, while also immersing themselves in the vibrant local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Lijiang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Town of Lijiang: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is the most popular attraction in the city. The old town features ancient buildings, narrow alleys, and elaborate bridges that are perfect for exploring.
  • Lijiang Mural: The Lijiang Mural is a group of colorful murals that dates back to the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644 CE). The murals depict local customs, myths and legends, and historical events.
  • Yufeng Temple: This ancient temple was built during the Qing Dynasty (1644-1912 CE) and is dedicated to the bodhisattva Manjusri. Visitors can admire the stunning architecture and intricate sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Lijiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jade Dragon Snow Mountain: This imposing mountain range offers breathtaking views and several outdoor activities such as trekking, skiing, and cable car rides.
  • Lijiang Ancient Tea Horse Road: This ancient route was used to transport tea, salt, and other goods between Yunnan and Tibet. Today, visitors can hike a section of the road and learn about the history and culture of the region.
  • Black Dragon Pool: This idyllic park is home to a tranquil lake, arched bridges, and lush gardens. It is a perfect spot for a relaxing stroll or picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lijiang

  • Tiger Leaping Gorge: This famous trail offers some of the most stunning views in China, as hikers navigate their way through towering cliffs and a rushing river.
  • Baisha Village: This picturesque village is located near the mountains and offers an excellent opportunity for visitors to explore the rich culture of the Naxi people.
  • Lashi Lake: This gorgeous lake offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of Lijiang, with scenic bike trails around the water’s edge.

Best Things to Do in Lijiang: Activities and Tours

  • Naxi Orchestra Performance: Enjoy a live performance of traditional Naxi music and dance at the Dongba Palace.
  • Naxi Calligraphy Class: Learn the unique history and techniques of Naxi calligraphy, with lessons from local artists.
  • Jade Dragon Snow Mountain Glacier Park: Take a cable car ride to the top of the mountain and experience the stunning glaciers and ice sculptures up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Lijiang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yak Meat: This local delicacy is usually incorporated into traditional Naxi dishes, such as hotpots and stir-fries.
  • Naxi Zanba: This hearty and filling dish is made from roasted barley flour and served with hot water or tea.
  • Lijiang Baba: This crispy pancake is often filled with savory ingredients such as meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Naxi Wine: This sweet rice wine is often served during festivals and special occasions.
  • Butter Tea: This salty and creamy tea is a staple in Tibetan and Naxi culture.
  • Pu’er Tea: This is one of China’s most famous teas, and is made from fermented leaves grown in the Yunnan region.

Where to Stay in Lijiang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Songtsam Linka Lijiang: This luxury hotel boasts stunning views of the surrounding mountains and offers guests a chance to stay in traditional Naxi-style rooms.
  • Lijiang Emerald Boutique Hotel: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of the old town and features spacious rooms and a rooftop terrace.
  • Timeless Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and offers clean and comfortable accommodation.

What to Buy in Lijiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Naxi Embroidery: This colorful and intricate embroidery is a traditional craft of the Naxi people and is often used to decorate clothing and accessories.
  • Lijiang Tea: The Yunnan region is famous for its tea production, and visitors can bring home high-quality Pu’er and black teas.
  • Local Handicrafts: Lijiang is a hub for traditional crafts such as wood carving, paper cutting, and pottery. Visitors can purchase these unique items as souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Lijiang

The local culture in Lijiang is heavily influenced by the Naxi people, who have lived in the region for centuries. Visitors should be respectful of the traditional customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites. The Naxi people have their own script, Dongba script, which is considered one of the world’s last living pictographic languages.

Best Time to Visit Lijiang

The best time to visit Lijiang is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Summer months can be crowded and hot, while winters are cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lijiang

Do:

  • Explore the city’s ancient streets and bridges
  • Try local dishes and attend traditional Naxi performances
  • Purchase local handicrafts and support the local economy

Don’t:

  • Dress immodestly when visiting religious sites
  • Litter or damage the ancient structures
  • Bargain too aggressively when shopping

Safety and Health

Safety in Lijiang

Lijiang is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions against theft and scams. It’s also important to be aware of the risk of altitude sickness when visiting higher elevations.

Health in Lijiang

Visitors should take precautions against food and waterborne illnesses, and should also be aware of the risk of altitude sickness when visiting higher elevations. It’s recommended to drink only bottled water and to avoid street food unless you are confident that it has been cooked and prepared hygenically.

Getting to Lijiang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lijiang

  • Lijiang Sanyi International Airport: This airport is located about 28 km outside of Lijiang and serves domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Lijiang

  • Lijiang Bus Terminal: This terminal is located in the north of the city and provides bus connections to major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • High-Speed Train: There is a high-speed train connection between Lijiang and Kunming, which allows visitors to travel between the two cities in just a few hours.

How to Get Around in Lijiang

The best way to get around in Lijiang is to explore the ancient city by foot. Taxis are readily available and can be useful for longer trips or for travel to the city’s outskirts. Bikes and e-bikes are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Lijiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shangri-La: This idyllic mountain town is located about 3 hours from Lijiang and is known for its stunning natural scenery and rich Tibetan culture.
  • Dali: This ancient city is about 2 hours from Lijiang and is known for its scenic views, ancient architecture, and vibrant local culture.
  • Kunming: The capital of Yunnan Province is about 7 hours from Lijiang and offers visitors a chance to explore the city’s history, culture, and cuisine.

Conclusion

Lijiang is a beautiful place to visit, with its stunning natural scenery, ancient temples, and vibrant local culture. Visitors can explore the city’s ancient streets, sample local cuisine, and immerse themselves in the traditional customs and crafts of the Naxi people. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, historical landmarks, or cultural experiences, Lijiang has something for everyone.
