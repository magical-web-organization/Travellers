Introduction to Mengla

Mengla lies in Xishuangbanna, to the southern part of Yunnan in China, East Asia. The county is home to diverse ethnic groups such as Dai, Hani, and Yao, each with its own unique cultural variations. The city is a place of natural beauty and historical landmarks that make it a perfect spot for anyone looking for adventure off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Mengla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ouyang Xiu Memorial Hall – A place where you can learn about the life of Ouyang Xiu, a renowned scholar from the Song Dynasty.
  • Peacock Lake – One of the historical landmarks in Mengla, this lake is home to the Peacock Princess of the Dai minority.
  • Mengla Tropical Rainforest National Nature Reserve - A rich tropical jungle located in the southern part of Hainan, which hosts various plant and wildlife species.

Natural Attractions of Mengla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gaozhuang Mountain - A beautiful mountain chain that towers over Mengla.
  • Gaihai Forest Park – A scenic location with its own waterfall, ideal for hiking, camping, and picnicking.
  • Kaeng Krachan National Park - A beautiful park that boasts scenic views of mountains, rivers, and forests in Thailand, located just a few miles from Mengla.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mengla

Mengla boasts numerous hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the southern part of China.

  • Mengla Tropical Rainforest National Nature Reserve - A great location for hiking, bird watching, and forest exploration.
  • Gaozhuang Mountain - A beautiful hiking spot with breathtaking views of the neighboring landscape.
  • Kaeng Krachan National Park - A suitable destination for hiking trails, camping, rafting, and picnic spots.

Best Things to Do in Mengla: Activities and Tours

Mengla offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and budgets.

  • Rafting along the Mekong River – A thrilling experience organized by local tour operators.
  • Visiting the ethnic villages of Dai, Hani, and Yao - A chance to learn and experience the customs and traditions of the local communities.
  • Take part in traditional festivals like the Water-Sprinkling Festival and the Torch Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mengla

Day 1: Explore the Peacock Lake

Day 2 - 3: Visit the Mengla Tropical Rainforest National Nature Reserve

Day 4: Explore the historical landmarks of Mengla and visit Ouyang Xiu Memorial Hall.

Day 5 - 6: Experience the outdoor adventures of Gaozhuang Mountain

Day 7: Relax at Kaeng Krachan National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Mengla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papaya salad – A refreshing and tangy salad made from shredded papaya, chili, and fermented fish sauce.
  • Bamboo cooked chicken – A steamed chicken dish cooked with bamboo shoots and Chinese herbs.
  • Dai-style sour fish soup – A tangy and spicy seafood soup made with sour bamboo shoots, Chinese herbs and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu - A popular Chinese liquor made from rice.
  • Coconut milk – A sweet and refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Mengla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mengla has a range of accommodation options that cater to different tastes and budgets.

  • Jinqiu Mountain Villa - A beautiful mountain resort located near Gaozhuang Mountain, offers various accommodation options and outdoor adventure activities like rock climbing, hiking, and zip-lining.
  • Yijia Zhan Guesthouse - A guesthouse located in the heart of the city, offering comfortable accommodation and authentic local dining experiences.
  • Gaihai Forest Park Campsite - A perfect option for outdoor lovers, with camping amenities and hiking trails.

What to Buy in Mengla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dai minority handicrafts such as embroidered clothes, woven and bamboo baskets, and silver jewelers.
  • Local tea produce such as Pu’er tea and Luoshuidong tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Mengla

Mengla has vibrant ethnic cultures, and visitors have the chance to experience their everyday lives and unique customs.

  • Water-Sprinkling Festival – A traditional festival held annually in mid-April by the Dai minority, where people play water games, dance and sing.
  • Torch Festival – A festival celebrated by the Hani and Yi minority in August to drive away evil spirits, also a chance to try local food, drink, and traditional clothing.

Best Time to Visit Mengla

The best time to visit Mengla is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this season, the weather is mild, and outdoor activities such as hiking and sightseeing are possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mengla

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and products.
  • Learn about the ethnic cultures at festivals and through interactions with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mengla

Mengla is a safe place to travel, crime rates in the area are low. Nevertheless, typical precautions should be taken, such as being aware of your surroundings, staying in well-lit areas at night, and avoiding carrying large sums of money.

Health in Mengla

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to prevent mosquito bites, as Dengue Fever is prevalent in the area. It is advisable to drink bottled water, especially in rural areas, to avoid water-borne infections, and carry basic medical supplies.

Getting to Mengla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mengla

  • Jinghong Gasa International Airport (JHG), less than 100km from Mengla city.

Bus Stations Near Mengla

  • Mengla Bus Station, managed by the Yunnan Province Transportation, has bus services connecting Mengla to other major cities in Yunnan.

Other Options

Taxis and private vehicles are also available for hire around the city.

How to Get Around in Mengla

Mengla has an extensive transport network connecting the city with various attractions and neighboring communities. Buses, taxis, and rented vehicles are available for travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from Mengla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jinghong - The capital city of Xishuangbanna, which lies about 260 km from Mengla, offers more cultural and outdoor exploration.
  • Mekong River - A great destination for cruising and sightseeing.
  • Pu’er Tea Fields - Located 80km from Jinghong, offers scenic views of tea gardens and their production processes.

Conclusion

Mengla offers a perfect mix of cultural and natural experiences, making it an ideal destination for anyone seeking to explore southern China. Visitors can learn about local customs and traditions, indulge in local cuisine and products, and engage in various outdoor activities like hiking and rafting. There should be no hesitation in adding Mengla to your traveling bucket list.
