Anning is a city in the Kunming Prefecture of Yunnan province in Southwest China. It is well-known for its cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant local culture, making it a must-visit destination in China. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your trip to Anning.

Introduction to Anning

Anning is a city of over 100,000 people located in the Yunnan province of China. The city has a rich cultural heritage, with plenty of museums and cultural attractions to explore. It is also home to some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views in the country, making it the perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anning

For hiking lovers, the Western Hills offer several trails of varying distances and difficulties. With breathtaking views of the Dianchi Lake, you get to appreciate the natural landscape more. Biking enthusiasts, too, get to explore the sprawling city by renting bikes from the many bike rental shops in Anning city.

Best Things to Do in Anning: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Huashan National Park, one of the most beautiful national parks in China.
  • Explore Luoping County’s terraced fields during the flowering season.
  • Sample Yunnan’s local delicacies with a food tour.
  • Bathe in a hot spring for a relaxing day out
  • Appreciate Yunnan’s tea culture by visiting a tea plantation and tea house.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anning

Day 1-2: Explore Kunming City and its popular attractions, such as the Stone Forest and Western Hills, among others.

Day 3-4: Travel to Dali Old Town and immerse yourself in Bai culture for two days.

Day 5-6: Head to Lijiang Old Town, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site with stunning architecture and a vibrant atmosphere.

Day 7: Visit the Baisha Ancient Town and admire its history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Anning

Anning is very diverse, with a population of many ethnicities. Most celebrations revolve around local events and customs, such as rice planting, the Torch Festival, and others.

Best Time to Visit Anning

The best time to visit Anning is from March to September, as the weather is temperate and less rainy. Also, there are several cultural festivals during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anning

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be sure to bargain in markets and stalls especially if you’re purchasing souvenirs
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public areas
  • Show any vulgar behavior or disrespect to locals
  • Trace or damage rock formations when exploring natural sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Anning

Anning is a generally safe city. However, it is advisable to take precautionary measures such as keeping your luggage and valuables secure in crowded areas, investing in a local 4G SIM card, and avoiding disclosing sensitive information to strangers.

Health in Anning

Ensure you equip yourself with a mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illness. For specific health queries, consult your doctor.

Getting to Anning: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anning

The nearest airport to Anning is Kunming Changshui International Airport, around 46.7km from Anning city. You can take a bus or taxi to the city.

Bus Stations Near Anning

Anning has two bus stations, Anning Long-distance Coach Station and Anning Xiaoqiao Coach Station. Both offer bus services to cities nearby.

Other Options

The city is also accessible via train services from Kunming city.

How to Get Around in Anning

The most commonly used modes of transport in Anning are buses, taxis, and rented bicycles. The buses are the most affordable, while taxis are slightly more expensive but convenient. Bicycle rentals are also abundant, and they are cheap and give you the freedom of exploring a vast area of the city at your pace.
