Introduction to Shilin

Shilin is a small county located in Kunming Prefecture, Yunnan Province, in Southwest China. It is best known for the Stone Forest, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, that is made up of towering limestone formations that resemble a forest made of stone. Shilin is also home to several museums and cultural centers that showcase local history and art.

List of Places to Visit in Shilin

When visiting Shilin, there are plenty of places to explore, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kunming Shilin National Scenic Area: a 350 sq km scenic area featuring the Stone Forest, lakes, waterfalls, and caves.
  • Ashima Cultural Park: a cultural park that celebrates the local Yi minority culture.
  • Kunming Museum: a museum that showcases the history of Yunnan Province and its people.
  • Guandu Ancient Town: a historic town that dates back to the Tang Dynasty.

Natural Attractions of Shilin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stone Forest: a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is made up of towering limestone formations that resemble a forest made of stone.
  • Jiuxiang Scenic Spot: a scenic spot that is home to a series of underground caves and waterfalls.
  • Green Lake Park: a picturesque park that features a lake, pagodas, and willow trees.
  • Yi Minority Park: a cultural park that celebrates the local Yi minority culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shilin

Shilin offers several hiking and biking routes for those looking to explore the great outdoors. Some popular routes include:

  • Stone Forest Scenic Area Hiking Trail: a 5.5 km trail that takes visitors through the heart of the Stone Forest.
  • Xiaojiu River Cycling Trail: a 30 km trail that takes visitors along the Xiaojiu River and through the surrounding countryside.
  • Guandu Ancient Town Hiking Trail: a 3.5 km trail that takes visitors through the historic Guandu Ancient Town.

Best Things to Do in Shilin: Activities and Tours

Some of the top things to do and see in Shilin include:

  • Enjoying the scenic views and natural wonders of the Stone Forest and Jiuxiang Scenic Spot.
  • Immersing yourself in the local culture at Ashima Cultural Park and Yi Minority Park.
  • Exploring the historic Guandu Ancient Town and Kunming Museum.
  • Taking part in outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and rock climbing.

Food and Drink Scene of Shilin

Shilin is known for its fresh and flavorful cuisine, which is influenced by both Chinese and Yi minority cultures. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steamed Pot Chicken: a local Yunnan delicacy that is flavored with ginger, garlic, and rice wine.
  • Guoqiao Mi Xian: a popular noodle soup that features rice noodles, beef, and a spicy broth.
  • Grilled Fish on Stone: fresh fish that is grilled over a hot stone and served with spicy seasoning.

Local Beverages

  • Pu’er Tea: a fermented tea that is known for its health benefits.
  • Xiaoqu Wine: a locally-produced barley wine that is a popular drink in Yunnan Province.
  • Shilin Black Beer: a dark beer that is brewed using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Shilin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shilin offers a variety of accommodations for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Shilin Yinruilin International Hotel: a 5-star hotel located in the heart of Shilin County.
  • Shilin Yitian Youth Hostel: a budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable accommodations.
  • Shilin Nanxun Guesthouse: a traditional guesthouse that features local architecture and amenities.

What to Buy in Shilin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some top souvenirs and local products to bring home from Shilin include:

  • Handmade Yi Minority Jewelry: intricate pieces of jewelry that showcase the unique culture and craftsmanship of the local Yi minority.
  • Pu’er Tea: a popular Yunnan tea that is known for its health benefits.
  • Local Handicrafts: visitors can purchase handmade pottery, textiles, and other handicrafts that showcase the local culture and traditions.

Local Culture and Customs of Shilin

Shilin, along with the rest of Yunnan Province, has a rich cultural heritage that is influenced by the region’s diverse ethnic groups, including the Yi minority. Visitors are encouraged to explore and learn about the local culture and customs during their trip to Shilin, including traditional clothing, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Shilin

The best time to visit Shilin is during the spring and fall months (March to May and September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly and wet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shilin

When visiting Shilin, there are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind to ensure a pleasant and respectful trip:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated in the hot weather.

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on the limestone formations in the Stone Forest.
  • Take pictures of local people without their consent.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shilin

Shilin is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as:

  • Avoiding walking alone at night.
  • Keeping valuable items secure in a hotel safe.
  • Using caution when crossing streets or walking in busy areas.

Health in Shilin

Visitors should also take precautions to protect their health during their trip to Shilin, such as:

  • Drinking bottled water rather than tap water.
  • Using sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against the sun and bugs.
  • Checking with a doctor before visiting if they have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Shilin: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to get to Shilin, including:

Airports Near Shilin

  • Kunming Changshui International Airport: approximately 85 km from Shilin County.

Bus Stations Near Shilin

  • Kunming East Bus Station: approximately 85 km from Shilin County.
  • Kunming South Bus Station: approximately 95 km from Shilin County.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: visitors can hire a private car or taxi to transport them directly to Shilin County.

How to Get Around in Shilin

Visitors can get around Shilin via several modes of transportation, including:

  • Taxis: readily available throughout the city, but be sure to negotiate a price before getting in.
  • Buses: an inexpensive option for getting around the area, with several routes available.
  • Private Cars or Scooters: visitors can rent a car or scooter to explore the area on their own.

Where to Go Next from Shilin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Shilin, including:

  • Kunming: the capital city of Yunnan Province, known for its natural beauty and cultural attractions.
  • Dali: a historic town that is home to several temples and scenic landscapes.
  • Lijiang: a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is known for its traditional Naxi culture and ancient city center.

Conclusion

Shilin is a beautiful and unique destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural wonders and cultural heritage of Yunnan Province. From the towering limestone formations of the Stone Forest to the traditional food and handicrafts of the local Yi minority, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Shilin.
