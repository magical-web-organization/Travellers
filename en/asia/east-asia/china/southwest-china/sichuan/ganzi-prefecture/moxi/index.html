Introduction to Moxi

Moxi is a small town located in Ganzi (prefecture), in Sichuan, in Southwest China. It is an off-the-beaten-path destination that has managed to preserve its traditional culture and natural beauty despite the influx of tourism. Moxi is the gateway to the Siguniang Mountains, also known as the Four Sisters Mountains, which offer some of the most scenic hiking trails in China.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Moxi, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip. From the top attractions to the best outdoor adventures, from local cuisine to accommodations, and logistics, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Moxi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moxi Ancient Town: This charming old town is a great place to explore the local architecture and handicrafts. It is home to several temples and ancient buildings, including the impressive Guanmiao Temple.

  • Wenchuan Earthquake Memorial Museum: This museum pays tribute to the victims and survivors of the devastating 2008 Wenchuan earthquake that shook the region. It is a moving experience that provides insight into the resilience and bravery of the local people.

  • Qingchengshan Taoist Temple: This temple, located at the foot of the Qingcheng Mountain, is one of the most important Taoist sites in China. It has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site and is a must-visit for history buffs and spiritual seekers.

Natural Attractions of Moxi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Siguniang Mountains: These mountains are a hiker’s paradise, featuring an extensive network of trails that wind through subalpine forests, grassy meadows, and snow-capped peaks. The most popular route is the Changping Valley Trail, which takes about six hours to complete and offers breathtaking views of the Four Sisters Mountains.

  • Huanglong National Park: This park is home to a stunning series of travertine pools that cascade down the mountainside, creating a surreal landscape of turquoise water and limestone terraces. The best time to visit is in the fall, when the foliage adds a burst of color to the scenery.

  • Balang Mountain Pass: This mountain pass offers some of the most spectacular views of the surrounding peaks and valleys. It is often shrouded in mist, creating an ethereal atmosphere that is a photographer’s dream.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moxi

Moxi and the surrounding areas are renowned for their hiking and biking trails, which offer some of the most scenic and challenging outdoor adventures in China. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Changping Valley Trail: This 14-kilometer trail is the most popular route in the Siguniang Mountains, offering stunning views of the Four Sisters Peaks and the Changping Valley.

  • Shuangqiao Valley Trail: This trail is renowned for its biodiversity, featuring a diverse array of flora and fauna including golden monkeys, pandas, and rare orchids.

  • Moxi Ancient Town to Shuangqiao Valley Trail: This relatively easy trail connects Moxi’s ancient town with the Shuangqiao Valley, passing through picturesque villages, terraced fields, and peaceful forests along the way.

Best Things to Do in Moxi: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Daduhe River: The Daduhe River is one of the wildest and most picturesque rivers in China, offering adrenaline-pumping rafting adventures that are not for the faint of heart.

  • Hot Spring Bathing: Moxi is famous for its hot springs, which offer a relaxing respite after a day of hiking or biking in the mountains. Some of the most popular hot springs include Banbianhu and Longchi.

  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a great way to explore the scenic countryside around Moxi. Several tour operators offer guided horseback riding tours that take you through lush forests, rolling hills, and quaint villages.

Food and Drink Scene of Moxi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maoxuewang: This spicy Sichuan dish is made with cow or pig blood, chili pepper, and Sichuan peppercorn. It may sound unappealing, but it is a local delicacy that is worth trying.

  • Sichuan Hot Pot: This is a classic Sichuan dish that features a spicy and numbing broth in which diners cook various meats, seafood, and vegetables.

  • Tibetan Yak Meat: Yak meat is a staple in Tibetan cuisine, and it is often served as a stew with potatoes and carrots.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: Butter tea is a traditional Tibetan beverage that is made by churning tea, salt, and butter together. It is a rich and hearty drink that is perfect for chilly mountain evenings.

  • Chang: Chang is a fermented barley beverage that is popular in Tibetan and Nepalese communities. It has a slightly sour and nutty flavor and is usually served in a wooden bowl.

Where to Stay in Moxi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Siguniangshan Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the heart of Moxi and offers comfortable rooms and great views of the surrounding mountains.

  • ZhuoMa’s Garden Hostel: This cozy hostel is a great option for budget travelers, offering clean and comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

  • Herui Holiday Inn: This modern hotel is located in a quiet area just outside of Moxi and offers spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Moxi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tibetan Carpets: These handcrafted carpets are a popular souvenir in Moxi, featuring intricate designs and bold colors.

  • Yak Wool Clothing: Yak wool is a warm and durable material that is used to make a variety of clothing items, including hats, scarves, and sweaters.

  • Tibetan Silver Jewelry: Tibetan silver is known for its intricate designs and unique motifs, making it a great souvenir for jewelry lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Moxi

Moxi is a predominantly Tibetan region, and the local culture is heavily influenced by Tibetan traditions and beliefs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and etiquette, which includes taking off your shoes before entering a home or temple, avoiding pointing with your finger, and refraining from touching someone’s head.

Best Time to Visit Moxi

The best time to visit Moxi is from April to October, when the weather is mild and the mountains are lush and green. However, the peak season is from July to August, so expect larger crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moxi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting temples and holy sites.
  • Pack warm clothing and suitable outdoor gear for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without permission.
  • Engage in political discussions or debate sensitive topics.
  • Purchase or use illegal drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moxi

Moxi is a safe and welcoming destination, but travelers should take standard precautions to guard against pickpocketing and petty theft. Visitors are also advised to pay attention to weather conditions, especially during the rainy season from May to September, which can cause landslides and flooding.

Health in Moxi

Moxi is located at a high altitude, which can cause altitude sickness in some travelers. Symptoms include headaches, dizziness, and nausea, and can be mitigated by drinking plenty of water, resting, and avoiding alcohol and caffeine for the first few days of your trip. Visitors should also be cautious with the local food and water, and consider bringing their own water purification tablets or filters.

Getting to Moxi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Moxi

  • Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU), located about 350 kilometers from Moxi.

Bus Stations Near Moxi

  • Chengdu Chadianzi Bus Station, located about 350 kilometers from Moxi.
  • Chengdu Xinnanmen Bus Station, located about 350 kilometers from Moxi.

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi: Visitors can hire a private car or taxi from Chengdu to Moxi, which takes about six hours and costs around 2000 RMB.
  • Shared van: There are several tour operators that offer shared van transfers from Chengdu to Moxi, which is a more affordable option than a private car.

How to Get Around in Moxi

The best way to get around Moxi is on foot or by bike, as the town is small and easily walkable. Most of the hiking trails and scenic areas are accessible by car or shuttle bus, which can be arranged through local tour operators or your accommodation.

Where to Go Next from Moxi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chengdu: This vibrant city is the capital of Sichuan Province and is known for its panda breeding center, spicy cuisine, and lively nightlife.

  • Maerkang: This historic town is located about 200 kilometers from Moxi and is home to several ancient monasteries and temples, including the Maerkang Monastery and the Dajin Monastery.

  • Kangding: This charming town is located about 270 kilometers from Moxi and is famous for its hot springs, Tibetan architecture, and vibrant music scene.

Conclusion

Moxi is a hidden gem in the heart of Southwest China, offering an authentic window into Tibetan culture and some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in the country. Whether you’re an avid hiker, a culture buff, or just looking for some peace and quiet in the mountains, Moxi has something for everyone. With our comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan an unforgettable trip to this enchanting destination.
