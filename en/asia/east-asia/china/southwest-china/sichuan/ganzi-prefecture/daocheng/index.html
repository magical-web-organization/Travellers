Introduction to Daocheng

Daocheng is a remote and beautiful district located in the Garzê Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture in Sichuan, Southwest China. It is known for its natural beauty, including the Yading Nature Reserve, which boasts towering peaks, crystal-clear lakes, and gorgeous meadows. Daocheng is also home to an ancient Tibetan culture that you can explore through the many local festivals and monasteries.

In this guide, we will take a closer look at what Daocheng has to offer, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Daocheng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yangmaiyong Glacier Park: Home to one of the largest glaciers in China, this park offers breathtaking views of the glacier and the surrounding area.
  • Litang Monastery: One of the largest and most important monasteries in the region, Litang Monastery is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.
  • Daocheng Yading Airport: While not technically a historical site, this airport offers one of the most breathtaking views of the Himalayas you will ever see.

Natural Attractions of Daocheng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yading Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to three spectacular peaks - Chenrezig, Jambeyang, and Chanadorje - as well as many stunning lakes and meadows.
  • Gaoyuan Scenic Area: With its steep cliffs, deep canyons, and lush forests, the Gaoyuan Scenic Area is a feast for the eyes.
  • Aden Scenic Area: Known for its crystal-clear waters and towering mountains, the Aden Scenic Area is a must-see for any nature lover.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Daocheng

  • Chonggu Temple Hike: This 8-kilometer (5-mile) hike takes you through breathtaking scenery to arrive at Chonggu Temple, one of the most famous and beautiful monasteries in the region.
  • Yading Nature Reserve Hike: A 21-kilometer (13-mile) hike that takes you through three stunning peaks and several breathtaking lakes.
  • Kasi Horse Trek: This week-long trek takes you through some of the most beautiful, remote regions of the Garze region on horseback.

Best Things to Do in Daocheng: Activities and Tours

  • Attend a Local Festival: Daocheng is home to many local festivals throughout the year celebrating everything from Tibetan New Year to the annual Horse Racing Festival.
  • Horseback Riding: Rent a horse and explore the stunning countryside of Daocheng on horseback.
  • Photography Tours: With its stunning natural beauty and unique cultural heritage, Daocheng is a photographer’s dream come true.

Food and Drink Scene of Daocheng

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tibetan Butter Tea: A unique tea made with butter and salt, this is a traditional Tibetan drink that you can find all over Daocheng.
  • Yak Meat: Rich and hearty, yak meat is a staple of Tibetan cuisine and is especially delicious when cooked in a hotpot.
  • Momos: Similar to dumplings, momos are stuffed with a variety of fillings including vegetables, meat, or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: A staple of Tibetan cuisine, this savory tea with its unique flavor should not be missed.
  • Chang: A traditional fermented barley drink that is enjoyed in many parts of Tibet.

Where to Stay in Daocheng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Daocheng Yading International Youth Hostel: This hostel is a great option for budget travelers looking for a comfortable place to stay.
  • Daocheng Paradise Boutique Hotel: This hotel offers luxury accommodation in the heart of Daocheng.
  • Rainbow International Hotel: Another great option for those seeking a comfortable and affordable place to stay in Daocheng.

What to Buy in Daocheng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tibetan Artwork: From traditional thangkas to modern pieces inspired by Tibetan art and culture, Daocheng is a great place to buy unique and beautiful artwork.
  • Tibetan Jewelry: Many local shops in Daocheng sell handmade jewelry made from silver and other precious metals.
  • Tibetan Carpets and Rugs: Beautifully woven carpets and rugs are a staple of Tibetan culture and can be found in many shops throughout Daocheng.

Local Culture and Customs of Daocheng

  • Tibetan Buddhism: The majority of people in Daocheng are followers of Tibetan Buddhism, so be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Language: While Mandarin is spoken widely throughout China, many locals in Daocheng speak Tibetan.
  • Hospitality: Tibetan people are known for their warm hospitality, so don’t be surprised if you are invited into someone’s home for tea or a meal.

Best Time to Visit Daocheng

The best time to visit Daocheng is from May to October. During this time, the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. However, be prepared for crowds during peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Daocheng

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting monasteries and other cultural landmarks.
  • Try the local food and drink, including traditional Tibetan dishes and drinks.
  • Explore the stunning natural beauty of Daocheng by embarking on a hike or scenic drive.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or during local festivals.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission, as this can be considered disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Daocheng

Daocheng is a safe destination for travelers. However, keep in mind that the region is remote, so be prepared for limited access to medical care and other services.

Health in Daocheng

When traveling in Daocheng, be sure to drink only bottled or treated water and wash your hands frequently. Altitude sickness can also be a concern in this region, so take it easy and acclimate slowly to the higher elevations.

Getting to Daocheng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Daocheng

  • Daocheng Yading Airport: The best way to get to Daocheng is to fly into the Daocheng Yading Airport, which offers daily flights to and from Chengdu.

Bus Stations Near Daocheng

  • Daocheng Bus Station: This station offers daily bus service to and from several neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Private Car: While more expensive than public transportation, hiring a private car is a good option for those looking to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Daocheng

The best way to get around Daocheng is by foot, bike, or hired car. While public transportation is available, it can be infrequent and unreliable, especially in more remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Daocheng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Litang: Known for its stunning architecture and rich cultural heritage, Litang is a great destination for those interested in exploring Tibetan culture further.
  • Chengdu: As the capital of Sichuan province, Chengdu offers a bustling city vibe with plenty of cultural landmarks and tourist attractions.
  • Kangding: Known as the gateway to Tibet, Kangding offers stunning natural beauty, unique cultural traditions, and plenty of outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Daocheng is a destination that should not be missed by anyone looking to explore the stunning natural beauty and unique cultural heritage of Southwest China. From the breathtaking landscapes to the rich history and culture, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Daocheng.
