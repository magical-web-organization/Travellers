Introduction to Dêgê

Located in the Ganzi Prefecture of Sichuan Province, Dêgê is a hidden gem in Southwest China. Nicknamed “the book town of China,” Dêgê is a melting pot of culture, history, natural landscapes, and spiritual retreats. The town is famous for the Dêgê Parkhang, founded in 1729, which is one of the oldest and finest printing houses in the world. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to explore the cultural and natural attractions of Dêgê, recommend the best activities and tours to try, and offer tips on where to stay and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Dêgê

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dêgê Parkhang

The Dêgê Parkhang is a must-visit attraction for book lovers and history buffs. Founded in 1729, the printing house is a UNESCO World Heritage site, and houses an extensive collection of sacred Tibetan texts, including the Kangyur and Tengyur. Visitors can witness the traditional wooden block printing process and observe the preservation and restoration of ancient manuscripts.

Dêgê Monastery

Situated on a hilltop overlooking the town, the Dêgê Monastery is a prominent center of the Nyingma school of Tibetan Buddhism. The monastery is renowned for its stunning architecture, murals, and thangka paintings. Visitors can witness the daily life of monks, attend meditation and prayer sessions, and learn about the history and philosophy of Tibetan Buddhism.

Barkham Road

The Barkham Road is a 50-kilometer stretch of road that connects Dêgê to Barkham County. The road offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped mountains, deep valleys, and grasslands. Along the way, visitors can stop at traditional Tibetan villages, visit local markets, and witness the nomadic way of life.

Natural Attractions of Dêgê: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yading Nature Reserve

Located 200 kilometers northwest of Dêgê, the Yading Nature Reserve is a paradise for trekkers and nature lovers. The reserve is home to three sacred peaks – Chenrezig, Jambeyang, and Chenadorje – which are revered as the embodiment of compassion, wisdom, and power. Visitors can explore the alpine forests, crystal-clear lakes, and cascading waterfalls, and witness the local wildlife such as yaks, Himalayan bears, and golden monkeys.

Hailuogou Glacier Park

Located 80 kilometers southwest of Dêgê, the Hailuogou Glacier Park is a unique natural wonder. The park features a massive glacier that descends from the eastern slope of Mt. Gongga, the highest peak in Sichuan Province. Visitors can walk on the glacier, take a cable car ride, and relax in the natural hot springs.

Mugecuo Scenic Area

Located 50 kilometers southeast of Dêgê, the Mugecuo Scenic Area is a picturesque landscape of rocky cliffs, waterfalls, and blue-green lakes. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake, hike through the forests, and enjoy the tranquility of the natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dêgê

Hailuogou Glacier Park Hike

This 10-kilometer hike takes visitors through the alpine forests, across suspension bridges, and along the glacier. The hike starts from the park entrance and ends at the glacier viewing area.

Yading Nature Reserve Trek

This multi-day trek takes visitors through the high-altitude landscapes of the Yading Nature Reserve. The trek covers a distance of 34 kilometers and includes an ascent to the Chonggu Monastery, a visit to the Pearl Lake, and a walk along the Milk River.

Barkham Road Cycling Route

This 50-kilometer cycling route takes visitors from Dêgê to Barkham County through traditional Tibetan villages and scenic landscapes. The route has several uphill sections and is suitable for experienced cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Dêgê: Activities and Tours

Book Printing Experience at Dêgê Parkhang

Visitors can participate in the traditional book printing process at the Dêgê Parkhang, from carving the wooden blocks to printing the pages. This unique experience offers a glimpse into the ancient art of bookmaking.

Cultural Tour of Dêgê Town

Visitors can take a guided tour of Dêgê Town to explore the traditional Tibetan architecture, visit local markets, and learn about the local customs and traditions.

Horseback Riding in Barkham County

Visitors can rent a horse or join a guided tour to explore the grasslands, hills, and valleys of Barkham County.

Food and Drink Scene of Dêgê

Must-Try Dishes

Yak Butter Tea

A staple in Tibetan cuisine, yak butter tea is a warming and energizing drink made from tea leaves, yak butter, and salt.

Gyurma

Gyurma, also known as Tibetan sausage, is a spicy and flavorful sausage made from minced yak meat and spices.

Yak Meat BBQ

Yak meat BBQ is a popular street food in Dêgê, made from marinated and grilled yak meat served on skewers.

Local Beverages

Chang

Chang is a brewed alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet or barley. It has a slightly sour taste and is a common drink at Tibetan social gatherings.

Butter Tea

Butter tea is a filling and nourishing drink made from tea leaves, yak butter, and salt. It’s a staple beverage in Tibetan households and is served with every meal.

Where to Stay in Dêgê: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lotus Hostel

Located in the heart of Dêgê Town, Lotus Hostel offers comfortable and affordable accommodation for backpackers and budget travelers. The hostel features shared dormitories and private rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the town.

Dêgê Baoshan Hotel

Located near the Dêgê Parkhang, Dêgê Baoshan Hotel is a luxury hotel that offers elegant and spacious rooms and suites, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant serving Chinese and Tibetan cuisine.

Dêgê Gonchen Hotel

Located near the Dêgê Monastery, Dêgê Gonchen Hotel is a traditional Tibetan style hotel that offers comfortable and cozy rooms, a courtyard garden, and an on-site restaurant serving local dishes.

What to Buy in Dêgê: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tibetan Buddhist Artifacts

Dêgê is home to some of the finest Tibetan Buddhist artifacts, including thangkas, prayer flags, and statues. Visitors can purchase these items from local shops and markets.

Traditional Tibetan Clothing and Jewelry

Visitors can purchase traditional Tibetan clothing and jewelry, such as chubas (long robes), boots, and beads, from local shops and markets in Dêgê.

Local Culture and Customs of Dêgê

The local culture of Dêgê is deeply influenced by Tibetan Buddhism and spiritual practices. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering temples, refraining from taking photos during prayer sessions, and asking permission before taking photos of locals.

Best Time to Visit Dêgê

The best time to visit Dêgê is from May to October when the weather is mild and dry, and the natural landscapes are in full bloom. The peak season is from July to August, so visitors should book their accommodations and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dêgê

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Tibetan to communicate with locals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and monasteries.
  • Try local Tibetan cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their permission.
  • Disrupt prayer sessions or religious rituals.
  • Engage in loud or disrespectful behavior in public places.
  • Damage or litter the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dêgê

Dêgê is a relatively safe place for travelers, with low levels of crime and theft. However, visitors should be cautious when hiking or trekking in remote areas and should always inform their hotel or tour operator of their itinerary.

Health in Dêgê

Visitors should take precautions to prevent altitude sickness when traveling to Dêgê, as the town sits at an altitude of 3,600 meters above sea level. Visitors should also carry necessary medication and supplies, such as insect repellent, sunscreen, and first aid kits.

Getting to Dêgê: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dêgê

The nearest airports to Dêgê are Kangding Airport and Daocheng Yading Airport, both of which are located approximately 200 kilometers from Dêgê. Visitors can take a shuttle bus or hire a taxi to reach Dêgê from these airports.

Bus Stations Near Dêgê

Dêgê Bus Station is located in the town center and offers regular bus services to major cities in Sichuan Province, such as Chengdu, Kangding, and Litang.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or join a guided tour to reach Dêgê from neighboring towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Dêgê

Visitors can explore Dêgê Town on foot, or rent a bicycle or motorbike to visit nearby attractions. Visitors can also hire taxis or join guided tours to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Dêgê: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Litang

Located 170 kilometers south of Dêgê, Litang is a charming town known for its traditional Tibetan architecture and vibrant cultural scene. Visitors can explore the Litang Monastery, visit local markets, and attend cultural festivals.

Danba

Located 150 kilometers east of Dêgê, Danba is a picturesque town known for its ancient watchtowers, hillside villages, and traditional Tibetan culture. Visitors can explore the Jiaju Tibetan Village, hike to the Suopo watchtower, and relax in the natural hot springs.

Conclusion

Dêgê is a hidden gem in Southwest China, offering a fascinating blend of culture, history, natural landscapes, and spiritual experiences.
