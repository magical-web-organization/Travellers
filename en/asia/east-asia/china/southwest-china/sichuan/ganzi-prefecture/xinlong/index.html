Introduction to Xinlong

Located in Ganzi (prefecture), Sichuan, Xinlong is a remote, yet charming town in Southwest China that offers a blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and unique ethnic customs, Xinlong has plenty to offer visitors seeking an authentic Chinese experience.

List of Places to Visit in Xinlong

Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient museums, hiking scenic trails, or sampling local cuisine, Xinlong has a range of attractions to keep you entertained during your visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of Xinlong’s highlights is its wealth of museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s deep cultural roots. Head to the Xinlong Museum to learn about Sichuan’s history and artistic heritage. The museum offers a wide range of exhibits showcasing artifacts, sculptures, and traditional costumes from local ethnic groups. Another must-visit attraction is the Xinlong Maoniu Monastery with its impressive rock carvings, intricate architecture, and serene ambiance.

Natural Attractions of Xinlong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will be in paradise in Xinlong. The region is surrounded by gorgeous mountains, lush forests, and vibrant flora and fauna. Head to Tagong Grassland for a magnificent view of the countryside, where you can walk, bike or horseback ride along scenic trails, or camp under the starry skies. You can also visit the Shougan Scenic Area to see impressive rock formations, valleys, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xinlong

Xinlong’s rugged terrain offers endless opportunities for nature lovers and adventure seekers. From the Hailuo Valley to the Zhonglu Tibetan Village, there are plenty of hiking and biking routes to choose from. For those who want to explore the town center, a walking tour of Xinlong Old Town is a must-do activity.

Best Things to Do in Xinlong: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor adventures and cultural experiences, Xinlong has plenty of fun activities and tours to suit all travelers’ tastes. Experience the region’s unique customs firsthand by attending the Xinlong Mango Festival, which takes place each autumn, and features all things mango, from food to handicrafts. If you’re in the mood for some relaxation, treat yourself to a traditional Chinese massage at Xinlong Hot Spring Resort.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xinlong

For those planning a week-long trip to Xinlong, you can follow this itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Xinlong and check in at your hotel
2 Visit the Xinlong Museum and explore the town center
3 Take a guided tour of the Maoniu Monastery
4 Explore the scenic Tagong Grassland
5 Hike the Zhonglu Tibetan Village Trail
6 Relax at Xinlong Hot Spring Resort
7 Depart from Xinlong

Food and Drink Scene of Xinlong

No trip to Xinlong is complete without sampling its delicious cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Xinlong’s unique cooking style is an infusion of traditional Tibetan and Sichuanese flavors, which results in mouth-watering dishes like Yak Butter Tea, Momo, and Spicy Pot.

Local Beverages

Xinlong is known for producing some of China’s best mangoes. Visitors can also indulge in a range of alcoholic beverages, including Chang, a fermented barley beer.

Where to Stay in Xinlong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xinlong has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. For those looking for an all-inclusive stay, the Xinlong Hot Spring Resort offers excellent amenities, including hot spring baths, restaurants, and scenic views. Budget-conscious travelers can opt for a stay at the Tashi Khang Hostel.

What to Buy in Xinlong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Xinlong is an excellent destination for souvenir shopping. Some of the most popular items to take home include traditional Tibetan apparel, prayer flags, yak butter sculptures, and Thangka paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Xinlong

Xinlong is home to a diverse population of ethnic minorities. Visitors are advised to respect the local customs and traditions, such as avoiding public display of affection and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Xinlong

The best time to visit Xinlong is from May to October when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities. However, keep in mind that due to the high altitude and mountainous terrain, temperatures can fluctuate widely throughout the day.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xinlong

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Make time to explore the mountains and natural scenery

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Hike alone, especially in unfamiliar areas
  • Litter or leave waste behind

Safety and Health

Safety in Xinlong

Xinlong is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of the high altitude and mountainous terrain, which can pose risks for those with pre-existing health conditions. Travelers are advised to avoid hiking or outdoor activities alone and to wear suitable gear to protect themselves from the elements.

Health in Xinlong

Xinlong has limited medical facilities, and visitors are advised to bring their medications and first-aid kits. Discuss any pre-existing health conditions with your doctor before traveling to the region. Remember to drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and avoid drinking unfiltered water.

Getting to Xinlong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Xinlong is located in a remote part of Southwest China, and getting there can be a challenge. However, visitors can choose from several transportation options, including:

  • Flight: The nearest airport is the Kangding Airport, located approximately 150 km from Xinlong.
  • Bus: Several long-distance buses connect Xinlong with other major cities in Sichuan Province.
  • Private Car: Visitors can hire private cars or taxis from nearby cities like Kangding or Chengdu.

How to Get Around in Xinlong

The best way to explore Xinlong is on foot or by bicycle. The town center is small and easy to navigate, with several walking trails and bike rental shops. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Xinlong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Xinlong can extend their trip and explore other destinations nearby. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Kangding: Known as the “Gateway to Tibet,” Kangding is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes.
  • Danba: Known as the “Beauty Valley,” Danba is renowned for its pristine natural scenery, ancient villages, and Tibetan culture.
  • Yading National Park: An awe-inspiring natural wonder that features snow-capped mountains, crystal-clear lakes, and lush forests.

Conclusion

Xinlong is a hidden gem in Southwest China that offers a unique blend of rich culture, stunning landscapes, and authentic cuisine. Visitors can explore ancient museums, hike scenic trails, and indulge in traditional food and beverages. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip to Xinlong and make the most of your stay.
