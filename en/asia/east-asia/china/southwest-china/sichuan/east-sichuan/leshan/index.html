Introduction to Leshan

Leshan is a hidden gem in the East Sichuan region of China known for its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts some of the most stunning natural attractions in China and is home to several significant cultural and historical landmarks. Leshan provides visitors with an excellent opportunity to explore the region while experiencing authentic Chinese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Leshan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Leshan is home to several museums that offer an in-depth look at the region’s history and culture. Start your journey by visiting the Leshan Museum, where you can find exhibits on the region’s dynastic past, including ceramics, bronze and iron ware, and ancient coins. Other important historical places to visit include the Leshan Giant Buddha and the Lingyun Temple.

Natural Attractions of Leshan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Leshan is home to breathtaking natural scenery that will leave you awe-struck. The most famous attraction is undoubtedly the Leshan Giant Buddha, the largest stone-carved Buddha statue globally, and one of China’s most visited sites. The Mt. Emei Scenic Area and the Jiayang Steam Train are other must-visit natural attractions that offer stunning scenic views that can be enjoyed by hiking, biking, or simply taking a tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leshan

Leshan is the perfect place for outdoor lovers who enjoy hiking and biking. There are several hiking and biking routes that offer magnificent scenery and breathtaking views. Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes in Leshan include the Mt. Emei-Sendai Trail, the Leshan-Lingyun Mountain Trail, and the Moxi Valley Trail.

Best Things to Do in Leshan: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Leshan. You can explore local culture and history by visiting museums, temples, and other attractions. You can also try your hand at various outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, or even skiing in the winter months. Leshan also has several guided tours available that cater to different interests.

Emei-Sendai Trail Day 3: Take a day trip to the Jiayang Steam Train Day 4: Visit the Emeishan Scenic Area and hike the Moxi Valley Trail Day 5: Experience local culture and customs Day 6: Try local cuisine and shopping Day 7: Explore nearby destinations and excursions

Food and Drink Scene of Leshan

Must-Try Dishes

Leshan cuisine is known for its spicy and bold flavors, and there are several dishes that you must try while visiting. These include Mapo tofu, Leshan roast duck, and hot pot.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking for a drink to pair with your Leshan meal, be sure to try the region’s famous tea, such as the Wuyi rock tea, Furong tea, or the En Shi Yulu tea.

Where to Stay in Leshan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Leshan has several accommodation options to suit different budget ranges, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the most popular hotels include the Grand Buddha Hotel, the Leshan Hilton Hotel, and the Jin Jiang International Hotel.

What to Buy in Leshan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Leshan has plenty of unique souvenirs and products that make excellent gifts or keepsakes to bring home. Some popular items to purchase include tea, silk, and porcelain.

Local Culture and Customs of Leshan

Leshan is known for its vibrant culture and traditions. It’s best to familiarize yourself with some local customs before your trip, such as greeting people with “Ni hao” and bringing a small gift if invited to someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Leshan

The best time to visit Leshan is usually from March to June and September to November as the weather is mild, and tourist crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leshan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking
  • Try local cuisine and tea

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or religious sites
  • Litter or damage delicate natural surroundings
  • Take photos of people without their consent

Safety and Health

Safety in Leshan

Leshan is considered a safe destination, but it’s always good to remain vigilant and aware of your surroundings while traveling. Be mindful of your belongings and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Leshan

Be sure to take necessary precautions to avoid getting sick while traveling, such as washing your hands frequently and sticking to bottled water for drinking. It’s also a good idea to carry basic first aid supplies and any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Leshan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Leshan

Leshan is easily accessible by air, with the nearest major airport being Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport, which is roughly 120km away from Leshan.

Bus Stations Near Leshan

Leshan has several bus stations with frequent routes to nearby destinations. The Leshan Coach Terminal is the main bus station in the city, with connections to various parts of Sichuan and neighboring provinces.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Leshan by train, with the Leshan Railway Station serving as the primary train station in the area.

How to Get Around in Leshan

Getting around in Leshan is relatively easy, with several transportation options available. Taxi and bus services are both readily accessible and affordable, while renting a bike is a popular option for exploring the city’s natural scenery and countryside.

Where to Go Next from Leshan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Leshan, visitors can explore several nearby destinations, including Chengdu, the capital city of Sichuan, and the Leshan Grand Buddha Scenic Area. The Dujiangyan Irrigation System and the Qingcheng Mountain are other popular nearby attractions worth adding to your itinerary.

Conclusion

Leshan offers visitors an unmatched experience full of history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re equipped to plan your ultimate trip to this gem in the East Sichuan region of China. Remember to respect the local culture and customs, and most importantly, enjoy your adventure in Leshan.
