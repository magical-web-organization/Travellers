Introduction to Guangyuan

Guangyuan is a beautiful city located in East Sichuan, in the Southwest region of China. Known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning natural scenery, Guangyuan offers a unique experience to travelers looking to explore the less-touristy parts of China. The city is home to several historical sites, including the ancient Sichuan province, and is surrounded by beautiful mountains, such as Mount Qingcheng.

In this travel guide, we will take you through some of the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs in Guangyuan.

List of Places to Visit in Guangyuan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Guangyuan Museum

The Guangyuan Museum is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in learning about the rich cultural heritage of Guangyuan. The museum has an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history and traditions of the region.

Sichuan Cultural Park

Located in the heart of Guangyuan, the Sichuan Cultural Park is a beautiful place to visit with friends and family. The park is dotted with several pavilions, gardens, and streams that offer a serene and tranquil atmosphere. The park also hosts several cultural events throughout the year, giving visitors a chance to experience the local customs and traditions.

Shaanxi-Gansu-Ningxia Border Region Red Army Long March Memorial Hall

For history buffs, the Shaanxi-Gansu-Ningxia Border Region Red Army Long March Memorial Hall is a must-visit attraction. Located in the Dabashan Mountain area, the memorial hall tells the story of the Red Army’s epic journey during the Long March. Visitors can explore the exhibits showcasing the life of the soldiers and their heroic deeds during this period.

Natural Attractions of Guangyuan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Qingcheng

Mount Qingcheng is one of the most popular attractions in Guangyuan. The mountain is located close to the city and is known for its stunning natural scenery, hiking trails, and temples. Visitors can take a cable car to the top of the mountain and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.

Jialing River

The Jialing River is one of the most scenic spots in Guangyuan. The river runs through the city, and visitors can enjoy a leisurely boat ride or take a stroll along the riverbanks. The Jialing River is also a popular spot for fishing and other water activities.

Qianfo Shan Scenic Area

The Qianfo Shan Scenic Area is a must-visit attraction for nature lovers. The area is home to several peaks, waterfalls, and forests that offer a serene and tranquil atmosphere. Visitors can take a hike through the forests and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guangyuan

Guangyuan has several hiking and biking trails that offer a fun and adventurous way to explore the city and its surroundings. Some of the best routes include:

  • Mount Qingcheng Trail
  • Jialing River Trail
  • Jinyan Mountain Trail
  • Qianfo Shan Trail

Best Things to Do in Guangyuan: Activities and Tours

Bamboo Rafting on Jialing River

Bamboo Rafting on the Jialing River is a fun and unique activity that visitors to Guangyuan should not miss. The rafting trip takes visitors through some of the most stunning scenery in the area, offering a unique perspective of the landscape.

Tea Picking Tour

For tea lovers, a tea picking tour is a perfect way to learn more about the local tea culture. Visitors can learn how tea is grown and processed and even take part in the tea-picking process themselves.

Food and Drink Scene of Guangyuan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Guanguan Beef Noodle: A popular local dish made with spicy beef broth and topped with cooked beef, vegetables, and noodles.
  • Stinky Tofu: A unique and pungent tofu dish that is fried and served with spicy sauce.
  • Liangfen: A cold jelly made from green beans, potatoes, and chili oil.

Local Beverages

  • Maotai: A strong and aromatic local liquor made from fermented sorghum.
  • Tea: Guangyuan is famous for its tea culture, and visitors can try several local tea varieties, such as green tea and black tea.

Where to Stay in Guangyuan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guangyuan has several accommodation options for travelers, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Wanda Realm Guangyuan: A luxurious hotel offering stunning views of the city and several amenities, such as a fitness center and spa.
  • Motel 168: A budget-friendly option for travelers, located near several attractions in the city.
  • Xiushan Guesthouse: A cozy and comfortable guesthouse located close to Mount Qingcheng.

What to Buy in Guangyuan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bamboo Products: Guangyuan is famous for its bamboo products, such as baskets, hats, and fans.
  • Tea: Visitors can buy several varieties of local tea, such as green tea and black tea, as well as tea sets and accessories.
  • Spices: Guangyuan is known for its spicy cuisine, and visitors can buy several local spices and sauces to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Guangyuan

The locals in Guangyuan are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are expected to reciprocate the same level of respect and kindness. One of the most important cultural customs in Guangyuan is eating together, and visitors should be prepared to share meals and drinks with their hosts.

Best Time to Visit Guangyuan

The best time to visit Guangyuan is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guangyuan

Do:

  • Try local cuisine, such as Guanguan Beef Noodle and Liangfen
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Practice basic Mandarin phrases

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission
  • Disrespect religious sites and temples
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Guangyuan

Guangyuan is a relatively safe city for travelers, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and scams in crowded areas. It is advisable to keep valuables safe and secure and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Guangyuan

The tap water in Guangyuan is not safe for drinking, and visitors should stick to bottled water. It is also recommended to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Guangyuan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Guangyuan is well-connected by air, rail, and road, making it easily accessible to visitors from different parts of China.

Airports Near Guangyuan

  • Guangyuan Panlong Airport: Located about 12 km from the city center, the airport offers flights to several major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Guangyuan

  • Guangyuan Bus Station: Located in the city center, the station offers regular bus services to several nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Train: Guangyuan has a train station that offers regular services to several major cities in China.

How to Get Around in Guangyuan

Guangyuan has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent a bike or a car to explore the city and its surroundings at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Guangyuan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guangyuan is located in a beautiful part of China, and visitors can explore several nearby attractions and destinations, such as:

  • Leshan Giant Buddha: Located about a 3-hour drive from Guangyuan, the Leshan Giant Buddha is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular attractions in the region.
  • Dujiangyan: Located about a 2-hour drive from Guangyuan, Dujiangyan is known for its ancient irrigation system and stunning natural scenery.

Conclusion

Guangyuan is a hidden gem in China, offering a unique experience to travelers looking to explore the less-touristy parts of the country. With its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning natural scenery, Guangyuan is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning more about the local culture and customs, trying local cuisine, and enjoying outdoor adventures.
