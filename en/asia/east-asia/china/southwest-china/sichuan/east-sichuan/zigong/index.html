Introduction to Zigong

Located in East Sichuan, in Southwest China, Zigong is a historic city known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delectable cuisine. The city offers a fascinating blend of ancient traditions, modern landmarks, and breathtaking landscapes that are sure to captivate any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Zigong

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventurer, Zigong has something to offer. Here’s a rundown of the top places to visit in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zigong Salt History Museum: This museum showcases Zigong’s long-standing history of salt production and its impact on the city’s economy and culture.
  • Dinosaur Museum of Zigong: Home to over 18,000 dinosaur fossils, this museum is a fascinating destination for anyone interested in paleontology.
  • Zigong Lantern Museum: The museum features a vast collection of traditional lanterns and provides a glimpse into the city’s heritage.
  • Qingbian Pass (Jiangjun Pass) and Tuo River Ancient Town: This ancient town, located on the banks of the Tuo River, is known for its impressive architecture, quaint streets, and beautiful river views.

Natural Attractions of Zigong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fuxi River Scenic Area: This scenic area is a dream destination for nature enthusiasts, boasting picturesque landscapes, serene waterfalls, and verdant forests.
  • Ziliujing Scenic Area: With its unique geological formations, crystal-clear water, and refreshing spring, Ziliujing Scenic Area is a haven for adventure-seekers.
  • Bi Shan Park: Located in the heart of Zigong, Bi Shan Park offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. The park is adorned with winding paths, manicured lawns, and lush trees that provide shade on a hot summer day.
  • Xinpu Park: This park is famous for its stunning lotus flowers and is a popular spot for photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zigong

While exploring Zigong, hiking or biking is an excellent way to experience the beauty of the city. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Xiniu Mountain Scenic Area: The scenic area is popular for its stunning mountain views and tranquil surroundings, making it a perfect destination for hiking enthusiasts.
  • Chonghushi Scenic Area: This area provides a spectacular hiking or biking experience, featuring scenic hills, clear streams, and panoramic views of the Tuo River.
  • Zigong City Moat: Zigong City Moat is a popular destination for cycling, perfect for those who want to sneak some exercise while enjoying the city’s scenery.

Best Things to Do in Zigong: Activities and Tours

With numerous activities and tours, Zigong is never out of things to do. Some of the best things to do in the city include:

  • Experience Zigong Salt: Zigong has a long history of salt production, and there are plenty of opportunities to learn about the production process.
  • Try Traditional Lantern Making: At the Zigong Lantern Museum, visitors can try their hand at traditional lantern making and create their own unique lanterns.
  • Visit a Hot Spring: Tuojiang Hot Spring Resort offers a relaxing experience after a day of exploring the city.
  • Attend Zigong International Dinosaur Lantern Festival: The festival showcases impressive dinosaur-themed lanterns and offers a unique cultural experience.
  • Explore Local Markets: Zigong offers a variety of local markets, featuring fresh produce, local crafts, and traditional cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Zigong

Zigong is renowned for its unique and delectable cuisine, which is a blend of Sichuan and Yunnan flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lao Gan Ma (Spicy chili sauce): A spicy Sichuan sauce that is popular throughout China.
  • Zigong Roasted Duck: A local specialty, Zigong roasted duck is seasoned with honey and garlic and has a crispy texture.
  • Malaren Shufan: A traditional Zigong dish made with pork, bamboo shoots, and preserved vegetables.
  • Tuojiang River Fish: The fish is served with ginger, garlic, and peppers and named after its place of origin.

Local Beverages

  • Tuojiang River Beer: A local beer brand that is refreshing and delicious.
  • Tuojiang River Wine: Zigong’s local wine, with a sweet and fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Zigong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zigong offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets, ranging from luxury hotels to budget guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  • Haitian Hotel: A modern four-star hotel located in the city center, offering excellent services and facilities.
  • Zigong Grand Hotel: A five-star hotel, known for its premium amenities and spacious rooms.
  • Home Inn: A budget-friendly option, offering clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Zigong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zigong is known for its traditional handicrafts and local souvenirs, making it an ideal place to shop for unique gifts. The following are some popular souvenirs:

  • Bamboo Baskets: Zigong is famous for its unique bamboo baskets, which serve as a household decoration or a functional storage container.
  • Zigong Embroidery: A traditional handicraft, Zigong embroidery showcases unique styles and patterns.
  • Golden Lanterns: Zigong’s golden lanterns are a unique souvenir and serve as a beautiful and practical decoration.

Local Culture and Customs of Zigong

Zigong has a rich cultural heritage, with its residents maintaining many traditional customs and festivals. Visitors should respect the local customs and abide by the following etiquette:

  • Greet people with a slight bow or a handshake.
  • Avoid discussing sensitive topics, such as politics or religion.
  • Be courteous and respectful when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Zigong

Zigong has a subtropical monsoon climate, with mild winters and hot summers. The best time to visit the city is from March to November, when the weather is warm and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zigong

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Zigong:

Do:

  • Always carry your passport when traveling.
  • Try the local cuisine, and explore local markets.
  • Learn about the city’s culture and history.
  • Respect local customs and etiquettes.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water in the city.
  • Disrespect sacred places or cultural norms.
  • Litter or damage public facilities.
  • Photograph people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zigong

Zigong is a safe city, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to:

  • Keep valuables in a safe place, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Be cautious when traveling alone at night.
  • Follow cautionary signs and guidelines while hiking or exploring nature reserves.

Health in Zigong

Visitors should take the following health precautions when visiting Zigong:

  • Drink bottled water, and avoid street food.
  • Seek medical attention if they experience any symptoms of illness.
  • Apply mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Zigong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zigong

The nearest airport to Zigong is Yibin Caiba Airport, which is approximately 70 km away from the city centre. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to reach Zigong.

Bus Stations Near Zigong

Zigong has bus stations that connect the city to major cities in China. The Zigong Bus Station and the Zigong North Bus Station are the primary bus stations in the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Zigong via train or by hiring a taxi from other cities in China.

How to Get Around in Zigong

Zigong has an efficient transportation system, including buses, taxis, and motorbikes. Buses are the most affordable option, while taxis are convenient for short-distance travel. Visitors can also rent bicycles at various locations in the city.

Where to Go Next from Zigong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zigong is an excellent stop for travelers exploring Sichuan and the surrounding areas. Some popular destinations include:

  • Leshan Giant Buddha: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, which is the largest Buddha statue in the world and located 80 km from Zigong.
  • Yibin: A picturesque riverside city, famous for its bamboo forest and tea plantations, just 70 km from Zigong.
  • Chongqing: A well-known gastronomic city, and popular for Hot Pot, about 190 km from Zigong.

Conclusion
