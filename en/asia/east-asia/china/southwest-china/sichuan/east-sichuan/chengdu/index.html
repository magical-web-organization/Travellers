Introduction to Chengdu

Chengdu is the capital city of Sichuan province, located in Southwest China. It is a place with a rich cultural history, natural beauty, and an impressive culinary scene. Chengdu is also known for its panda breeding center, which attracts visitors from all over the world. The city has a population of over 16 million people and is the economic and cultural hub of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Chengdu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jinsha Site Museum: This museum exhibits the artifacts excavated from the ancient Jinsha Ruins, dating back to the Shang and Zhou dynasties.
  • Wenshu Monastery: A peaceful Buddhist temple that dates back to the Tang dynasty, with a beautiful garden and several halls displaying historical relics.
  • Du Fu Thatched Cottage: A tranquil garden complex dedicated to one of China’s most famous poets, where visitors can learn more about his life and work.

Natural Attractions of Chengdu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Qingcheng: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that is home to several Taoist temples, hiking trails, and waterfalls.
  • Jiuzhaigou National Park: A nature reserve with stunning natural landscapes, including striking blue lakes, forests, and mountains.
  • Leshan Giant Buddha: The largest carved stone Buddha in the world, located on the east bank of the Dadu River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chengdu

  • Leshan to Emei Mountain Hiking Trail: A multi-day trek through the mountains, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Chengdu to Dujiangyan Biking Trail: A scenic route that passes through rural villages, ancient bridges, and temples.

Best Things to Do in Chengdu: Activities and Tours

  • Panda Breeding Center: See giant pandas up close and learn more about the conservation efforts being made to protect this endangered species.
  • Hot Pot Dinner: Chengdu is famous for its spicy hot pot, which is a must-try experience for foodies visiting the city.
  • Sichuan Opera: A traditional performance art that showcases local music, dance, and drama.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chengdu

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Jinsha Site Museum in the morning, and Wenshu Monastery in the afternoon.
Day 2 Take a day trip to Leshan to visit the Giant Buddha, and stop by Emei Mountain for a hike.
Day 3 Visit the Sichuan Museum to learn more about the area’s culture and history, and enjoy a hot pot dinner in the evening.
Day 4 Spend the day at the Panda Breeding Center, and visit a local tea house in the evening.

Food and Drink Scene of Chengdu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spicy Hot Pot: A simmering pot of broth filled with meat, vegetables, and noodles, with a fiery kick of Sichuan peppercorns.
  • Mapo Tofu: A silky tofu dish with ground pork and spicy chili bean paste, served over rice.
  • Dan Dan Noodles: A bowl of chewy noodles with minced pork, chili oil, and Sichuan peppercorns.

Local Beverages

  • Baijiu: A strong distilled liquor made from grains, commonly served at Chinese banquets.
  • Tea: Chengdu is known for its tea culture, with several teahouses serving up traditional brews.

Where to Stay in Chengdu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shangri-La Chengdu: A luxury hotel located in the city center, with spacious rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Nova Traveller’s Lodge: A trendy hostel in the Jinli area, with comfortable dorms and private rooms.
  • Chengdu Flipflop Hostel: A budget-friendly option in the Kuanzhai alley area, with a cozy atmosphere and helpful staff.

What to Buy in Chengdu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sichuan Pepper: The region’s famous spice can be bought in whole or ground form, and is perfect for adding a fiery kick to any dish.
  • Tea: Chengdu is renowned for its tea culture, so be sure to pick up some local blends to bring home.
  • Shu Embroidery: A traditional form of embroidery that originated in Chengdu, with intricate designs and vibrant colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Chengdu

Chengdu locals are known for their relaxed and laid-back attitude. They value family, food, and community, and enjoy spending time at teahouses, parks, and public spaces. It’s common to see groups of people practicing tai chi, playing mahjong, or enjoying a hot pot dinner together.

Best Time to Visit Chengdu

The best time to visit Chengdu is during the spring and autumn months, from March to May and September to November. The weather is mild, and the skies are clear. It’s also the best time to see the famous cherry blossoms and fall foliage in the surrounding parks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chengdu

Do:

  • Try the local spicy cuisine, like hot pot, Mapo Tofu, and Dan Dan Noodles.
  • Visit the Giant Panda Breeding Center to learn more about these adorable creatures.
  • Take a stroll in People’s Park or Huanhuaxi Park to experience the local lifestyle.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect the local culture by littering, smoking in public places, or being rude to others.
  • Do not touch or tease the pandas, as they are still wild animals.
  • Do not go out too late at night or walk in isolated areas alone for safety reasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chengdu

Chengdu is generally a safe city, but tourists should still take normal safety precautions, like keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding dark, isolated areas at night. Scams can be common in busy tourist areas, so always be cautious when approached by strangers offering services or goods.

Health in Chengdu

Chengdu has high levels of air pollution, so it’s recommended to wear a mask if you have respiratory issues. The tap water is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water or boiled water instead.

Getting to Chengdu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chengdu

  • Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU)

Bus Stations Near Chengdu

  • Xinnanmen Bus Station
  • Chengdu Chadianzi Bus Station

Other Options

  • High-speed train: Chengdu is connected to other major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in the city and are a convenient mode of transportation for short trips.

How to Get Around in Chengdu

  • Metro: The Chengdu Metro system is cheap, efficient, and easy to use.
  • Bus: The city has an extensive bus network, with tickets priced at ¥2-3 for most routes.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance using a ride-hailing app.

Where to Go Next from Chengdu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Huanglongxi Ancient Town: A well-preserved ancient town located about 40 kilometers southeast of Chengdu.
  • Emei Shan: A sacred mountain with several temples, hiking trails, and stunning views.
  • Mount Qingcheng: A UNESCO World Heritage site, with several Taoist temples and scenic hiking trails.

Conclusion

Chengdu is a fascinating destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. With its relaxed atmosphere and friendly locals, it’s a wonderful place to explore and discover the unique aspects of Sichuan province’s rich heritage. Whether you’re a foodie, nature lover, or history buff, Chengdu has something for everyone.
