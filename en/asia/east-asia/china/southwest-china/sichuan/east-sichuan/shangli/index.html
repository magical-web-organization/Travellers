Introduction to Shangli

Shangli is a picturesque ancient town located in East Sichuan, Sichuan Province, in Southwest China. It is surrounded by verdant mountains and winding rivers, making it a popular destination for local and international tourists. Shangli boasts over 2000 years of history and charm that can be seen through its ancient temples, traditional architecture, and vibrant cultural scene.

List of Places to Visit in Shangli

Visitors to Shangli will have no shortage of things to see and do. From culturally significant museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there is something for everyone in this charming town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Liu Manor Museum: Situated in the center of the town, this museum exhibits artifacts and stories that showcase the life of a wealthy family from the Ming dynasty.
  • Zhonglu Folk Museum: This museum captures the essence of traditional Shangli customs and routines, housed in a restored traditional courtyard.
  • Baoguo Temple: Located on the outskirts of the town, this temple dates back to the Tang dynasty and is a great place to learn more about Buddhism.

Natural Attractions of Shangli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jiajiang Qianfo Mountain National Forest Park: This park is located about 20 minutes away from Shangli and is filled with nature trails, waterfalls and scenic viewpoints.
  • Leshan Giant Buddha: This attraction is about an hour away from Shangli and features a 71-meter Buddha carved into a cliff face.
  • Qingyi River Scenic Area: This area is perfect for a leisurely walk and combines natural views with ancient architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shangli

Shangli is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery and is a great place for hiking or biking enthusiasts. Visitors can rent bikes from local vendors or join guided tours through the mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Shangli: Activities and Tours

  • Stroll through the ancient streets of the town and admire traditional Chinese architecture.
  • Take a pottery-making class and learn about the ancient art of ceramics.
  • Visit local tea plantations and enjoy a traditional Chinese tea ceremony.
  • Attend the bullfighting festival held annually in Shangli.

Where to Stay in Shangli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pandanli Inn: This guesthouse is located in the heart of the town and offers traditional accommodations with modern amenities.
  • Mountainside Homestay: A cozy and comfortable homestay that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Shangli Taohuayuan Inn: Located in the neighboring Pingle county, this inn offers traditional-style guest rooms and is perfect for those who want to explore nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Shangli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics and pottery
  • Traditional Chinese silk
  • Tea leaves and tea sets
  • Local snacks such as preserved fruits and sesame cakes

Local Culture and Customs of Shangli

  • Shangli is known for its rich history and ancient architecture, evidenced by the many temples and museums in the town.
  • Bullfighting festivals are an important tradition in Shangli and are often held throughout the year.
  • The town is also famous for its ceramics, and visitors can take pottery-making classes to learn more about this ancient art form.

Best Time to Visit Shangli

The best time to visit Shangli is during the spring or autumn when the weather is mild and comfortable. During these seasons, visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding mountains and explore the ancient town without worrying about the hot and humid summers or the chilly winters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shangli

Do:

  • Try local delicacies such as Shunan bamboo shoots and Shangli Tofu Pudding.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Protect the ancient architecture and sites in the town by not climbing structures or littering.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Disrespect traditional rituals or ceremonies.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shangli

Overall, Shangli is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take the usual precautions and avoid walking alone late at night. The mountainous terrain around Shangli can be dangerous, so hikers and bikers should ensure they have appropriate gear and check weather warnings before setting out.

Health in Shangli

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude and take appropriate measures to prevent altitude sickness. Additionally, tap water in Shangli is not drinkable, so visitors should drink bottled or boiled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Shangli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shangli

The closest major airport to Shangli is Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport, which is approximately a three-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Shangli

There are several bus stations near Shangli including Qianwei Bus Station, Leshan Bus Station, and Meishan Bus Station. Visitors can take a bus from these stations to Shangli for a reasonable price.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to get to Shangli from nearby towns or cities.

How to Get Around in Shangli

The best way to get around Shangli is on foot or by bike. The town is small and easy to navigate, and the many streets are best explored on foot or by bike to take in the ancient architecture and surrounding nature.

Where to Go Next from Shangli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Emei Mountain: The famous mountain is about 2 hours away from Shangli and offers stunning scenery and various hiking routes.
  • Leshan: A cultural city known for the Leshan Giant Buddha and delicious local culinary.
  • Jiuzhaigou: This National park is known for its picturesque lakes, waterfalls and forests.

Conclusion

Shangli is a unique and charming destination that offers a glimpse into China’s rich history and culture. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or immersing yourself in ancient traditions, Shangli has something for everyone. By following this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip and enjoy all that Shangli has to offer.
