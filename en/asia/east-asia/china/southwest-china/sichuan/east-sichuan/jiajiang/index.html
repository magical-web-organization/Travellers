Introduction to Jiajiang

Welcome to Jiajiang! Situated in the eastern part of Sichuan Province in China, Jiajiang is known for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, and unique local cuisine. Jiajiang is a hidden gem in the southwestern region of China. This off-the-beaten-track destination is a must-visit for travelers who want to experience traditional China away from the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Jiajiang

Jiajiang is a small but vibrant city that offers unique attractions, historic sites, scenic landscapes, and cultural experiences. Here are some places you shouldn’t miss during your visit to Jiajiang.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jiajiang Confucian Temple - This temple is a well-preserved example of classical Chinese architecture and provides a glimpse into Chinese Confucianism.
  • Qintong Ancient Town - This historic town is known for its well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.
  • Jiulongshan Han Dynasty Tomb - This tomb site dates back to the Han Dynasty and is one of the most significant archaeological finds in Jiajiang.

Natural Attractions of Jiajiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hailuogou Glacier Park - This national park boasts the largest glacier forest in China and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Qinglong River Scenic Area - This scenic area is prized for its hiking trails, waterfalls, and dramatic limestone cliffs.
  • Heishui Wan Hot Springs - Relax in the natural hot springs and take in stunning mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiajiang

Jiajiang is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts who love to hike and bike. Here are some popular routes you can explore.

  • Mount Emei Hiking Trail - This is a challenging but rewarding trail from Jiajiang to Mount Emei, one of the four sacred mountains of Buddhism.
  • Hailuogou Glacier Forest Hiking Trail - This moderate trail takes you through the spectacular glacier forest of Hailuogou National Park.
  • Qinglong Gorge Biking Trail - This scenic trail offers a great workout while taking in picturesque views of the Qinglong River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Jiajiang: Activities and Tours

Jiajiang has many unique activities and tours that offer insight into local culture and traditions.

  • Tea-Picking Experience at Shuilian Tea Garden - Learn about the process of making tea and pick your own tea leaves at the Shuilian Tea Garden.
  • Wine Tasting at Sichuan Yingshi Winery - Savor a glass of Sichuan wine and learn about the wine-making process at the Sichuan Yingshi Winery.
  • Jiajiang Puppet Theatre Performance - Experience Chinese traditional culture with an entertaining performance by trained puppeteers at the Jiajiang Puppet Theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jiajiang

Here is an itinerary to make the most of your week in Jiajiang.

1 Explore Jiajiang historical and cultural attractions

Food and Drink Scene of Jiajiang

Jiajiang’s unique cuisine is influenced by its location in Sichuan Province, known for its spicy and flavorful dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiajiang Steamed Pork Ribs - This dish is slow-cooked with soy sauce and a special blend of local spices.
  • Sichuan Hotpot - This spicy dish should not be missed during your visit to Jiajiang.
  • Jiajiang Bean Jelly - A cold dish made with peanuts, sesame, and bean jelly.

Local Beverages

  • Yak Butter Tea - This salty, creamy tea is a staple of traditional Tibetan culture.
  • Sichuan Baijiu - This clear liquor is distilled from sorghum and wheat and is often shared during meals with friends and family.

Where to Stay in Jiajiang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jiajiang offers various accommodation options for travelers, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some suggested options:

  • Jiajiang Zhongdian Hotel - This hotel is centrally located and provides comfortable, clean rooms and good service.
  • Jiuzhaigou Xiaohong House Hostel - This stylish hostel is perfect for solo travelers or groups and offers bike rental services.
  • Bashan Guesthouse - This cozy guesthouse has a great location and a friendly staff that makes your stay comfortable and memorable.

What to Buy in Jiajiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jiajiang is known for its traditional handicrafts, specialty foods, and unique souvenirs. Consider bringing home some of these local products:

  • Jiajiang Chopsticks - These beautifully crafted chopsticks are perfect for dining or as gifts for family and friends.
  • Jiajiang Bean Jelly - Take home the flavors of Jiajiang with a jar of local bean jelly.
  • Tibetan Prayer Flags - These colorful flags are an essential symbol of Tibetan culture and are available at local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiajiang

Jiajiang has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in Chinese traditions. Here are some customs and traditions you should be aware during your visit to Jiajiang:

  • Respectful dress-code - Cover your shoulders and knees when visiting temples or other historic sites.
  • Courtesy manners - Practice courteous greetings and use proper forms of address when meeting locals.
  • Tea culture - Tea is an important part of Jiajiang’s culture, and visitors can enjoy tea ceremonies and learn about tea culture in local tea shops.

Best Time to Visit Jiajiang

The best time to visit Jiajiang is during the fall and spring seasons, from March to May and September to November. These seasons offer cooler temperatures and clear skies. Winter is ideal for skiing, and summer is the high season for tourism in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiajiang

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine; it’s spicy and flavorful!
  • Take part in a tea ceremony to learn about Jiajiang’s tea culture.
  • Bring a coat for cooler temperatures during the fall and winter months.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Speak loudly or use informal language in public places.
  • Wear shorts or tank tops when visiting temples or other sacred places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jiajiang

Jiajiang is a relatively safe city, but it’s always good to practice common sense when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and be cautious around tourist attractions.

Health in Jiajiang

Jiajiang has a clean environment and healthy food. However, visitors should drink bottled water and take precautions against altitude sickness when visiting higher-altitude destinations.

Getting to Jiajiang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can easily get to Jiajiang by air or by road. Here are some options:

Airports Near Jiajiang

  • Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport - Located 164 km from Jiajiang.

Bus Stations Near Jiajiang

  • Jiajiang Bus Station - This station offers local and regional bus services.
  • Chengdu Xinnanmen Bus Station - This station offers long-distance bus services to Jiajiang.

Other Options

  • Private Car Service - Visitors can arrange a private car with a local travel agency or hire a taxi from Chengdu.

How to Get Around in Jiajiang

Jiajiang is a small city, and many attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use public buses, taxis, or hire bicycles to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Jiajiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jiajiang is an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience Sichuan’s natural beauty and rich culture. After exploring Jiajiang, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Chengdu - The capital city of Sichuan is famous for pandas, spicy food, and rich history.
  • Yibin - This city is known for its stunning scenery, great food, and famous Chinese liquor, Wuliangye.
  • Leshan - Home to the Leshan Giant Buddha, one of the tallest Buddha statues in the world.

Conclusion

We hope you found this guide helpful and informative when planning your trip to Jiajiang. This city offers unique cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and natural beauty that you won’t find anywhere else in China. Come and explore the hidden gem of Sichuan!
