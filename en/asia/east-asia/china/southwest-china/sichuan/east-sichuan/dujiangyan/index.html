Introduction to Dujiangyan

Dujiangyan is a small city located in East Sichuan, in Sichuan province, in Southwest China. It is an ancient city with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. The city is best known for the Dujiangyan Irrigation System, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest water conservation projects in the world. The irrigation system is still used today to irrigate the fertile land in the Dujiangyan area.

Local Culture and Customs of Dujiangyan

Dujiangyan has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should show respect for local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Use both hands when giving or receiving items, such as when accepting a gift or paying for merchandise.

Best Time to Visit Dujiangyan

The best time to visit Dujiangyan is from March to November. The weather is mild and pleasant during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dujiangyan

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Dujiangyan:

Do:

  • Try the local food, including spicy Sichuan cuisine.
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Pack comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city’s natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of other people without their consent.
  • Hike or bike alone in remote areas, as it can be dangerous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dujiangyan

Dujiangyan is a safe city for visitors, with the usual precautions taken in any large city. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas, such as markets and train stations.
  • Be careful when walking or driving on narrow roads in the mountainous areas.

Health in Dujiangyan

The water quality in Dujiangyan is generally good, but visitors should still take precautions to stay healthy. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink bottled water, as tap water can be unsafe.
  • Be careful with street food in order to avoid foodborne illness.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun during outdoor activities.
