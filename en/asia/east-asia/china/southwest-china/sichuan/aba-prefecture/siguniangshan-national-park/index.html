Introduction to Siguniangshan National Park

Siguniangshan National Park, located in Aba (prefecture), in Sichuan, in Southwest China, is a majestic mountain range with soaring peaks, forested slopes, and cascading waterfalls. The name “Siguniangshan” means “Four Girls Mountain,” which refers to the four peaks within the range, all over 6,000 meters in height.

Siguniangshan National Park is a year-round destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and culture seekers. The park is home to numerous culturally significant sites, such as ancient temples, shrines, and historic villages. The natural attractions of Siguniangshan National Park are equally impressive, with hiking routes, biking trails, and fantastic panoramic views of the snow-capped mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Siguniangshan National Park

Siguniangshan National Park is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of well-marked routes and trails for all levels of fitness and experience. The park has three main valleys, namely Changping, Haizi, and Shuangqiao, each with its unique features and attractions.

Here are some popular hiking trails that you can take:

  • Changping Valley Loop Trek
  • Haizi Valley Hiking Trail
  • Shuangqiao Valley Loop Trek
  • Mt. Siguniang Summit Trek

Local Culture and Customs of Siguniangshan National Park

The region surrounding Siguniangshan National Park is home to several ethnic groups, including the Qiang and Tibetan people. The local culture is rich in traditions, festivals, and ceremonies. Respect for local customs and traditions is essential when visiting Siguniangshan National Park.

Best Time to Visit Siguniangshan National Park

The best time to visit Siguniangshan National Park is between April and October, during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and comfortable. However, the scenery is most impressive during the winter months when the mountain peaks are covered in a blanket of snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Siguniangshan National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and cultures.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines.
  • Carry warm clothing, especially during winter.
  • Stay on designated paths and trails.
  • Leave no trace when hiking.

Don’t:

  • Enter restricted areas.
  • Harm or disturb wildlife.
  • Pick flowers or plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Siguniangshan National Park

The trails and paths in Siguniangshan National Park are well maintained and safe for hikers of all levels. However, it’s essential to carry suitable clothing and equipment, such as sturdy hiking shoes, warm clothing, and a first-aid kit. Make sure to check the weather forecast before starting any hike or outdoor activity.

Health in Siguniangshan National Park

The altitude of Siguniangshan National Park ranges from 3,000 meters to over 7,000 meters, so visitors may experience altitude sickness. It’s essential to acclimatize gradually and to stay hydrated. Use sunscreen to prevent sunburn, and pack insect repellent to avoid bug bites.
