Introduction to Maoxian

Maoxian is a picturesque region located in the Aba (prefecture), in Sichuan, Southwest China. The region is known for its rich cultural heritage sites, natural wonders, and vibrant food and drink scene. If you are planning a trip to China, Maoxian is a must-visit destination that offers a perfect mix of adventure, culture, and relaxation.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and much more. Whether you are a nature lover or a culture enthusiast, you will find something to enjoy in Maoxian. Let us explore the beauty of this region together.

List of Places to Visit in Maoxian

Maoxian is home to numerous cultural and natural attractions. Here, we have compiled a list of must-visit places in Maoxian that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mao County Museum: The Mao County Museum is one of the most iconic museums in Maoxian. It contains a vast collection of cultural relics that showcase the region’s history and culture. The museum’s main attraction is the Han Prince’s Tomb, a cultural relic site of the Western Han Dynasty.

  • Sanxingdui Museum: Sanxingdui is an ancient city in Maoxian that dates back to over 3,000 years ago. The Sanxingdui Museum showcases the archeological findings of the ancient city, including bronze statues, jade carvings, gold masks, and pottery.

  • Ancient City Wall and East Gate Tower: The ancient City Wall and East Gate Tower of Maoxian are an integral part of the region’s cultural heritage. Built during the Ming Dynasty, these structures offer a glimpse into the region’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of Maoxian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jiuzhaigou Valley National Park: Jiuzhaigou Valley National Park is a natural wonder located in the northern part of Maoxian County. The national park is famous for its stunning lakes, waterfalls, and scenic landscapes.

  • Hailuogou Glacier Park: Hailuogou Glacier Park is located on the eastern slope of Mount Gongga and offers a host of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and skiing. The main attraction of the park is the Hailuogou Glacier, which is the lowest altitude glacier in China.

  • Moxi Town: Moxi Town is a charming town located at the entrance of Mount Gongga. The town is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes and is a great starting point for many outdoor adventures, including hiking, camping, and rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maoxian

Maoxian offers a host of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural and cultural attractions. Here are some popular routes that you can try:

  • Gongga Mountain Trek: The Gongga Mountain Trek is one of the most challenging and rewarding hiking routes in Maoxian. The trail covers a distance of 120 kilometers and takes around nine days to complete. Along the way, you will come across mesmerizing landscapes, including alpine meadows, glaciers, and mountain peaks.

  • Ruoergai Grasslands Trek: The Ruoergai Grasslands Trek takes you through the rolling hills, vast grasslands, and sparkling rivers of Maoxian. The trek covers a distance of 30 kilometers and takes around two days to complete.

  • Maoxian City Cycling Tour: The Maoxian City Cycling Tour is a great way to explore the city’s cultural heritage sites, including ancient temples, museums, and city walls. The cycling tour covers a distance of 20 kilometers and takes around two hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Maoxian: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Maoxian offers a host of other activities and tours that you can try during your visit. Here are some popular options:

  • Hot Springs: Maoxian is home to several natural hot springs that are perfect for relaxation and rejuvenation. Some popular hot springs include Hailuogou Glacier Hot Springs, Guokeji Hot Springs, and Waitong Hot Springs.

  • Rafting: The Daduhe River in Maoxian offers a host of rafting opportunities for adventure enthusiasts. The river’s rapids range from class III to class V, making it a challenging and exciting adventure.

  • Local Market Tour: Explore Maoxian’s vibrant food and drink scene with a local market tour. Taste local delicacies like spicy skewers, potato pancakes, and corn cakes while learning about the region’s culinary culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Maoxian

Maoxian is home to several local dishes and beverages that are worth trying during your visit. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maoxian Stuffed Pancakes: Maoxian Stuffed Pancakes are a local specialty made with flour, eggs, meat, and vegetable fillings. These pancakes are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them a perfect breakfast or snack option.

  • Spicy Skewers: Spicy Skewers are a popular street food in Maoxian made with meat, vegetables, and spices. These skewers are grilled over open flames, giving them a delicious smoky flavor.

  • Corn Cakes: Corn Cakes are a traditional dish made with cornmeal, eggs, and vegetables. These cakes are steamed and served with soy sauce or chili oil, making them a perfect snack option.

Local Beverages

  • Rice Wine: Rice Wine is a popular local beverage in Maoxian made from fermented rice. The wine has a strong aroma and a slightly sweet taste, making it perfect to pair with local dishes.

  • Butter Tea: Butter Tea is a traditional beverage in Maoxian made with tea leaves, yak butter, salt, and milk. The tea has a creamy texture and a unique flavor that takes some getting used to.

Where to Stay in Maoxian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maoxian offers a host of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Jiuzhaigou Sheraton Hotel: Jiuzhaigou Sheraton Hotel is a luxury hotel in Maoxian that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain ranges. The hotel features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.

  • Maoxian Charming Inn: Maoxian Charming Inn is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Maoxian. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a cozy dining area.

  • Hailuogou Tibetan Homestay: Hailuogou Tibetan Homestay is a traditional homestay located in the Hailuogou Glacier Park. The homestay offers comfortable rooms and home-cooked meals, giving visitors an opportunity to experience the local culture firsthand.

What to Buy in Maoxian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maoxian is known for its handicrafts, snacks, and tea. Here are some popular souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Maoxian:

  • Sanxingdui Bronze Artifacts: Sanxingdui Bronze Artifacts are reproductions of the ancient bronze statues found in the Sanxingdui Museum. These artifacts are beautifully crafted and make for great souvenirs.

  • Maoxian Tea: Maoxian Tea is a famous tea brand in China that offers a range of high-quality teas, including black tea, green tea, and oolong tea. These teas make for great gifts for tea lovers.

  • Spicy Skewers Seasoning: Spicy Skewers Seasoning is a popular seasoning used to add flavor to grilled meats and vegetables. If you have tried the spicy skewers in Maoxian and want to recreate the taste at home, this seasoning is a must-buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Maoxian

Maoxian is home to several minority groups, including the Tibetan and Qiang people. These groups have their own unique culture and customs, which are reflected in the region’s cuisine, clothing, and architecture. Visitors to Maoxian should be respectful of these cultures and customs and seek to learn more about them during their visit.

Best Time to Visit Maoxian

The best time to visit Maoxian is from April to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and explore the region’s natural and cultural attractions. However, the peak tourist season is from July to August, so expect larger crowds during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maoxian

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Carry warm clothes, as the temperature drops rapidly in the evenings.
  • Be prepared for outdoor activities with appropriate gear.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Speak loudly in public places or disturb the peace.
  • Hike or camp in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maoxian

Maoxian is a safe region to visit, but it is always essential to stay alert and cautious, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of any travel advisories issued by their consulate or embassy.

Health in Maoxian

Maoxian has good healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, visitors are advised to carry their medication and first aid kit, especially if they have any pre-existing medical conditions. It is also essential to stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Maoxian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Maoxian is well-connected to other parts of China, making it easy to reach. Here are some transportation options that you can use to reach Maoxian:

Airports Near Maoxian

  • Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport: Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport is the nearest international airport to Maoxian, located around 220 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Maoxian

  • Chengdu Chadianzi Bus Station: Chengdu Chadianzi Bus Station offers regular bus services to Maoxian.

  • Jiuzhaigou Bus Station: Jiuzhaigou Bus Station offers regular bus services to Maoxian.

Other Options

  • Private Car: You can also hire a private car or taxi from Chengdu to Maoxian.

How to Get Around in Maoxian

While Maoxian is a small region that can be explored on foot, it is advisable to use public transportation or taxis to reach more remote locations. Buses and taxis are readily available and offer a convenient and affordable way to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Maoxian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maoxian is located in the heart of Southwest China, surrounded by several other destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some popular nearby destinations that you can visit from Maoxian:

  • Jiuzhaigou Valley National Park: Jiuzhaigou Valley National Park is located around 150 kilometers north of Maoxian and is famous for its stunning lakes and waterfalls.

  • Chengdu: Chengdu is the capital of Sichuan Province and is located around 220 kilometers east of Maoxian. It is known for its spicy cuisine, Giant Panda Breeding Research Base, and historic sites.

  • Kangding: Kangding is a charming Tibetan town located around 200 kilometers west of Maoxian. The town is known for its stunning mountain scenery, hot springs, and local culture.

Conclusion

Maoxian is a beautiful region in Southwest China, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you are a nature lover, culture enthusiast, or adventure junkie, Maoxian has something for everyone. Follow our comprehensive guide to explore the best of Maoxian and make the most of your trip to this stunning region.
