Introduction to Ruoergai

Located in Aba (prefecture), in Sichuan, in Southwest China, Ruoergai is a hidden gem that is slowly gaining popularity among tourists. The town is situated on the eastern edge of the Tibetan Plateau, and it boasts a unique blend of Tibetan and Han Chinese cultures. Ruoergai is known for its vast grasslands, scenic lakes, and stunning landscapes that attract nature lovers and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Ruoergai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ruoergai Monastery: This Tibetan monastery is one of the oldest and most important religious sites in Ruoergai. The monastery is home to several buildings, including temples and halls, and it houses a collection of precious Buddhist relics.
  • Ruoergai Folk Culture Museum: This museum showcases the customs and traditions of the Tibetan and Han Chinese people who reside in Ruoergai. The museum includes exhibits on clothing, crafts, music, and dance.

Natural Attractions of Ruoergai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hongyuan Grassland: One of the most beautiful grasslands in China, Hongyuan is a vast expanse of pristine grassland that stretches for miles. Visitors can hike, bike, or horseback ride in the grassland and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and lakes.
  • Yagu Lake: This crystal-clear lake is situated in the middle of a forest and is surrounded by snow-capped mountains. Visitors can rent a boat and explore the lake or take a leisurely walk along its shores.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ruoergai

  • Gexi Grassland: The Gexi Grassland is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking in Ruoergai. The grassland is surrounded by hills and is home to several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Sanke Grassland: The Sanke Grassland is located at an altitude of 4,200 meters and is home to several hiking trails that offer spectacular views of the grassland and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ruoergai: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Tibetan Homestay: Visitors can stay in a traditional Tibetan guesthouse with a local family and immerse themselves in the culture and customs of the Tibetan people.
  • Take a Photography Tour: Ruoergai is known for its stunning landscapes and natural beauty, making it a popular destination for photography enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Ruoergai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yak Hot Pot: A traditional Tibetan dish made with yak meat and served in a hot pot. The dish is typically seasoned with garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppers.
  • Ruoergai Mushroom Soup: A hearty soup made with local mushrooms, beef, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Yak Butter Tea: A traditional Tibetan drink that is made from tea leaves, yak butter, and salt.
  • Chang: A local Tibetan beer made from fermented barley.

Where to Stay in Ruoergai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tibet Ruoergai Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and is located in the center of town.
  • Ruoergai Grassland Hostel: This hostel is situated on the edge of the Hongyuan Grassland and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Ruoergai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tibetan Carpets: These colorful carpets are hand-woven by local artisans and make for a beautiful souvenir.
  • Qingke Barley Wine: A local alcoholic beverage made from fermented barley and served in a traditional Tibetan horn cup.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruoergai

Ruoergai is home to a rich culture and unique customs that are heavily influenced by Tibetan and Han Chinese cultures. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering a Tibetan home and avoiding touching or pointing at religious artifacts.

Best Time to Visit Ruoergai

The best time to visit Ruoergai is during the summer months of June to August when the grasslands are lush and green, and the weather is mild. The autumn months of September to November are also a good time to visit when the foliage changes color, and the landscapes are even more stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruoergai

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately when visiting temples and monasteries.
  • Try local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Touch or point at religious artifacts.
  • Litter or damage the natural landscape.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ruoergai

Ruoergai is a relatively safe destination for visitors, but it is important to take precautions when traveling alone or at night. It is also important to be aware of the potential for altitude sickness, as Ruoergai is situated at a high altitude.

Health in Ruoergai

Visitors to Ruoergai should be aware of the potential for altitude sickness and take precautions such as drinking plenty of water and avoiding alcohol. It is also important to bring any necessary medications and visit a medical professional if necessary.

Getting to Ruoergai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ruoergai

  • Jiuzhai Huanglong Airport: Located in Jiuzhaigou, this airport is approximately three hours away from Ruoergai.

Bus Stations Near Ruoergai

  • Ruoergai Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Ruoergai and offers bus services to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Private transfers and car rentals are also available for visitors who prefer a more personalized mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Ruoergai

The best way to get around Ruoergai is by hiring a driver or taking a taxi. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or a scooter to explore the town and its surroundings on their own.

Where to Go Next from Ruoergai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jiuzhaigou National Park: A stunning National Park located approximately three hours away from Ruoergai and known for its crystal-clear lakes and waterfalls.
  • Huanglong National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the same region as Jiuzhaigou that is known for its natural scenery and colorful pools.

Conclusion

Ruoergai is a hidden gem in Southwest China that offers a unique blend of Tibetan and Han Chinese cultures and stunning natural beauty. Visitors to Ruoergai can explore grasslands, lakes, and forests, and immerse themselves in the local customs and traditions of the Tibetan people. With its rich culture and diverse landscapes, Ruoergai is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those looking to discover a unique side of China.
