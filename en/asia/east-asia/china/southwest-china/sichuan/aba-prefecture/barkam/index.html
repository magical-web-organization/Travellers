Introduction to Barkam

Barkam is a county city located in the Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture in the province of Sichuan, China. It is a visually stunning area that offers some of the best natural attractions in the country. With an altitude of 2,700 meters, Barkam is a popular destination for hiking, mountain biking, and outdoor recreation.

List of Places to Visit in Barkam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Barkam Museum: This museum preserves the history, culture, and traditions of Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture, including many artifacts from the region’s ancient civilizations.

  • Dazang Temple: This ancient temple has been around for more than 1,000 years and is famous for its unique architecture, which reflects the fusion of Tibetan and Chinese cultures.

  • Hongge National Forest Park: A massive park that is home to a large variety of flora and fauna, and is a popular spot for nature walks and hikes.

Natural Attractions of Barkam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bayankala Mountain: A picturesque mountain that is part of the Bayankala Range, known for its stunning views and hiking trails.

  • Jinzi Mountain: Another popular mountain range, offering scenic views and challenging hikes for avid hikers and mountaineers.

  • Taoping Qiang Village: A serene and beautiful village that is home to the Qiang people, a group with a unique architecture, culture, and traditions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barkam

  • Bayankala Mountain Hiking Trail: A challenging trail that offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Jiajin Mountain Bike Trail: This bike trail winds its way through the stunning countryside, offering breathtaking views and a chance to explore the unique flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Barkam: Activities and Tours

  • White-water Rafting: The area has several white-water rafting options on offer, catering to all levels of water enthusiasts.

  • Horse-riding: Horse riding is a popular activity in Barkam that allows you to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Barkam

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sichuan Spicy Hotpot: One of the most popular dishes in Sichuan cuisine, this hotpot is a perfect blend of spice, flavor, and texture.

  • Ma Po Tofu: Another classic Sichuan dish that features silky tofu in a spicy sauce that is sure to leave you wanting more.

  • Zanba: A staple dish of the Qiang people, it is made of roasted barley flour, yak butter, and saltwater, and is often served with other side dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: Made from yak butter and tea leaves, it is a popular beverage in the region, known for its rich and savory taste.

  • Chang: A traditional Tibetan fermented alcoholic beverage, similar in taste to beer and made from barley and other grains.

Where to Stay in Barkam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express Aba Hongge Plaza: A three-star hotel located in the heart of Barkam that offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  • Jinchuan Hotel: A four-star hotel located near the Barkam Museum that provides standard rooms and suites with free breakfast and Wi-Fi.

  • Gyalthang Dzong Hotel: A luxury hotel that offers a unique cultural experience, located near Jiajin Mountain with fantastic views of the mountain range.

What to Buy in Barkam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yak Wool Products: Barkam is known for its woolen products, and you can find an array of items ranging from blankets, hats, and Ponchos made from yak wool.

  • Pu’er Tea: A famous tea variety that is produced in the region, it is known for its unique taste and health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Barkam

Barkam is home to several ethnic groups, including the Qiang people, and is known for its rich cultural heritage. The area is famous for its unique architecture, arts and crafts, traditional festivals, and food culture.

Best Time to Visit Barkam

The best time to visit Barkam is from April to October. During this time, the weather is warm and pleasant, with a comfortable temperature ideal for outdoor activities. However, Barkam is a year-round destination, and each season offers its unique charm and beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barkam

Do:

  • Respect the local people, their customs, and traditions.
  • Try local food and beverages.
  • Carry enough warm clothes, as the weather can be unpredictable.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Disrupt the natural environment.
  • Try to bargain too hard, as it can be seen as disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barkam

Barkam, like many other remote places in China, is relatively safe, but some precautions should be taken, such as:

  • Make sure to carry enough warm clothes as the weather gets chilly at night.
  • Carefully plan your hikes, making sure you have enough food, water and inform someone where you are going.
  • Keep valuables out of sight, especially in crowded or tourist areas.

Health in Barkam

It is always advisable to carry your medicines as medical facilities in Barkam are limited. It’s important to drink only bottled water, and occasionally boiled water as tap water is not safe.

Getting to Barkam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barkam

  • Kangding Airport: The nearest airport to Barkam is the Kangding Airport, which is located approximately 200 kilometers away. However, it’s not well-connected to other parts of the country.

  • Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport: This airport is located 400 kilometers from Barkam and is well-connected with other parts of the country.

Bus Stations Near Barkam

  • Barkam Bus Station: The main bus station in Barkam which operates buses to the surrounding areas and other cities in China.

Other Options

There are several other options available for traveling to Barkam, such as taking private cabs or hiring a ride share. It’s essential to be cautious while taking such options and make sure to choose reputable service providers.

How to Get Around in Barkam

The best way to get around Barkam is by private transport, such as taxis or rental cars, or by hiring a local tour guide or driver. Bikes are also a popular option for those who want to explore the area on their own.

Where to Go Next from Barkam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kangding: Known as the gateway to Tibet, Kangding is a picturesque mountain town that offers stunning views, amazing hikes, and a unique cultural experience.

  • Xinduqiao: Known for its beautiful natural scenery, it’s often named as the most photogenic village in China.

Conclusion

Barkam is a must-visit destination in China, offering an authentic cultural experience that includes stunning natural scenery, delicious food, and a unique blend of Tibetan and Qiang cultures. With this comprehensive travel guide, plan your trip to Barkam and enjoy all that this amazing destination has to offer.
