A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Aba (Prefecture), China

Aba (prefecture) is located in Sichuan, Southwest China. The prefecture is blessed with natural beauty, including snow-capped mountains, pristine lakes, and ancient monasteries. The region is also rich in cultural heritage and offers visitors a chance to explore traditional Tibetan and Qiang culture. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore top attractions and activities in Aba (prefecture) and provide insider tips to help you make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Aba (prefecture)

Aba (prefecture) is a region rich in natural beauty and cultural heritage. The region is home to several ethnic groups, including Tibetans, Qiangs, and Hans. The rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and ancient monasteries offer a breathtaking backdrop for visitors. Aba (prefecture) is also known for its mouth-watering cuisine, traditional handicrafts, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Aba (prefecture)

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Aba (prefecture) that will give you a glimpse of the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kirti Monastery: Located in the town of Ngawa, Kirti Monastery is one of the most famous Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in China. The monastery has a rich history and is home to thousands of monks.
  • Langmusi Monastery: Langmusi Monastery is a Tibetan Buddhist monastery located in the town of Langmusi. The monastery is famous for its stunning architecture and beautiful surroundings.
  • Songpan Ancient Town: Songpan Ancient Town is located in the northern part of Aba (prefecture). The ancient town dates back to the Tang Dynasty (618-907) and is home to several historical sites, including temples, gates, and towers.

Natural Attractions of Aba (prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Huanglong National Park: Huanglong National Park is located in the southern part of Aba (prefecture). The park is known for its stunning limestone formations, colorful pools, and hot springs.
  • Zecha Stone Forest: Zecha Stone Forest is located in the town of Xiaojin. It is a beautiful natural wonder formed over millions of years.
  • Mount Siguniang: Mount Siguniang is one of the most beautiful natural wonders in Aba (prefecture). It is known for its snow-capped peaks and beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aba (prefecture)

Aba (prefecture) is a haven for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes that will take you through the region’s stunning natural beauty:

Food and Drink Scene of Aba (prefecture)

Aba (prefecture) is known for its mouth-watering cuisine and traditional beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tibetan Yak Meat: This is a popular meat dish in the region. It is usually slow-cooked and served with traditional noodles.
  • Qiang Sausage: Qiang sausage is made by stuffing pork into a sheep’s intestine. It is a spicy delicacy that is usually served as an appetizer.
  • Butter Tea: Butter tea is a traditional Tibetan drink made with tea leaves, butter, and salt.

Local Beverages

  • Chang: Chang is a traditional Tibetan beverage made from fermented barley. It is usually served in a wooden cup and has a slightly sour taste.
  • Baijiu: Baijiu is a strong liquor made from sorghum. It is usually served during traditional celebrations and ceremonies.

What to Buy in Aba (prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aba (prefecture) is known for its traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some of the top items to buy in the region:

  • Thangka Paintings: Thangka paintings are traditional Tibetan paintings that are usually painted on canvas or silk. They make an excellent souvenir to take back home.
  • Hand-Woven Carpets: Hand-woven carpets are traditional Qiang handicrafts. They come in various colors and designs and make for a unique and beautiful souvenir.
  • Yak Wool Products: Yak wool products, including sweaters and hats, are warm and cozy. They make for a perfect winter souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Aba (prefecture)

Aba (prefecture) is a culturally rich region with unique customs and traditions. Here are some things you should know about local culture and customs:

  • Tibetan New Year: Tibetan New Year, also called Losar, is the most important festival in Aba (prefecture). It usually falls in February or March and is celebrated with feasts, dances, and other traditional activities.
  • Prayer Flags: Prayer flags are common in Aba (prefecture). They are hung on the rooftops or in high places, and are believed to spread good luck and blessings.
  • Respect Locals: Aba (prefecture) is home to several ethnic groups. You should respect their culture and customs, including dress codes and behavior in temples and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Aba (prefecture)

The best time to visit Aba (prefecture) is from May to October. During this time, the weather is mild, and the region’s natural beauty is at its peak. However, if you plan to visit during the winter months, make sure to bring warm clothing, as the temperature can drop below freezing.
