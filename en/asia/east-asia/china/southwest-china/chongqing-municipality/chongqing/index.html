Introduction to Chongqing

Located in southwest China, Chongqing is a sprawling metropolis with a population of over 30 million people. Known as the “Mountain City”, it is situated on the Yangtze River and surrounded by towering hills and mountains. This city is a fusion of ancient traditions and modernity, offering a unique blend of cultures and experiences to its visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Chongqing

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Three Gorges Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Three Gorges area, including artifacts from ancient civilizations and modern times.
  2. Chongqing China Three Gorges Museum of Fine Arts: With a collection of over 50,000 pieces of Chinese art, this museum is a must-visit for art lovers.
  3. Ciqikou Ancient Town: Located in the Shapingba District, this historic town is over 1,000 years old and features traditional Chinese architecture.
  4. Hongya Cave: A multi-story shopping and entertainment complex built into the side of a cliff, this attraction offers stunning views of the Yangtze River.

Natural Attractions of Chongqing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dazu Rock Carvings: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Dazu Rock Carvings feature over 50,000 statues and carvings of Buddha and other religious figures.
  2. Wulong National Park: Famous for its karst landforms and deep valleys, Wulong offers a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking and exploring caves.
  3. Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street: As the busiest shopping street in Chongqing, Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street is a lively area with street performers, retail shops, and restaurants.
  4. Nanshan Scenic Area: This area offers stunning views of the cityscape and the Yangtze River from atop a mountain, along with a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chongqing

  1. Jinyun Mountain National Nature Reserve: This reserve features a variety of trails for hikers and bikers, ranging from easy to difficult.
  2. Eling Park: Located in the Yuzhong District, this park offers scenic views of the city and a tranquil environment for hiking and picnicking.
  3. Chongqing Grand Canyon National Park: This park features a challenging, 36-kilometer trail with stunning views of waterfalls, cliffs, and the Yangtze River.

Best Things to Do in Chongqing: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a Yangtze River Cruise: A cruise along the Yangtze River offers the chance to see some of the most breathtaking scenery in China.
  2. See the Pandas: The Chongqing Zoo is home to giant pandas and offers the opportunity to get up close and personal with these beloved creatures.
  3. Hot Pot Meal: Chongqing is known for its spicy hot pot, a dish made with a variety of ingredients dipped in a flavorful broth.
  4. Experience Chongqing Nightlife: Chongqing offers a vibrant nightlife scene with a variety of bars, clubs, and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chongqing

Food and Drink Scene of Chongqing

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hot Pot: A spicy dish featuring a variety of ingredients such as meat, vegetables, and tofu cooked in a simmering broth.
  2. Chongqing Noodles: A local specialty made with hand-pulled noodles and a spicy, savory sauce.
  3. Kao Ping (Roasted Duck): A dish that originated in Beijing but has become a popular staple in Chongqing restaurants.
  4. Shancheng Laoyao (Old Hot Pot Restaurant): A local favorite hot pot restaurant known for its rich and flavorful broth.

Local Beverages

  1. Baijiu: A traditional Chinese liquor made from distilled sorghum, wheat, or rice.
  2. Chongqing Craft Beer: The local craft beer scene in Chongqing is rapidly growing, with a variety of breweries and taprooms offering locally brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Chongqing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hilton Chongqing: A five-star hotel located in the city center with stunning views of the Yangtze River.
  2. Chengyuanye Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the Shapingba District with a traditional Chinese design.
  3. Wanyou Conifer Hotel Chongqing: A four-star hotel located in Yuzhong District with a scenic mountain view.

What to Buy in Chongqing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Peppercorns: Chongqing is known for its spicy cuisine, and peppercorns are an essential ingredient in many local dishes.
  2. Mala Tang Seasoning: Mala Tang is a spicy soup popular in Chongqing, and the seasoning used in the dish can be bought as a souvenir.
  3. Ciqikou Porcelain: The ancient town of Ciqikou is known for its handmade porcelain crafts, such as teapots and bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Chongqing

Chongqing is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are proud of their city’s unique culture and heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as removing one’s shoes before entering a home or temple and avoiding discussions of politics or religion.

Best Time to Visit Chongqing

The best time to visit Chongqing is during the spring and autumn seasons, from March to May and September to November. The climate is mild during these times, with comfortable temperatures and less rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chongqing

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the spicy hot pot.
  • Explore the historic sites and natural attractions.
  • Observe local customs and respect the culture.

Don’t:

  • Discuss politics or religion.
  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chongqing

Chongqing is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions against petty theft and pickpocketing. It is also important to be aware of traffic when crossing streets, as drivers in Chongqing can be aggressive.

Health in Chongqing

Visitors should take precautions against the heat and humidity during the summer months, as well as air pollution in certain areas. It is also recommended to drink bottled water and practice good hygiene.

Getting to Chongqing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chongqing

  1. Chongqing Jiangbei International Airport: The main airport serving Chongqing.
  2. Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport: Located approximately 300 kilometers from Chongqing, this airport is a good alternative for international flights.

Bus Stations Near Chongqing

  1. Chongqing North Bus Station: The main bus station in Chongqing with a variety of domestic and international destinations.
  2. Chongqing West Bus Station: Located in the Shapingba District, this station offers bus routes to nearby provinces.

Other Options

  • High-Speed Train: Chongqing North Railway Station is the main high-speed train station with connections to other major cities in China.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Chongqing, but it is important to negotiate the fare before getting in.
  • Ferry: Ferry services are available on the Yangtze River for travel to nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Chongqing

Public transportation, such as buses and the metro, is widely available in Chongqing. Taxis and ridesharing services, such as Didi, are also convenient options for getting around the city. For those looking for a unique experience, taking the cable car across the Yangtze River offers stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.

Where to Go Next from Chongqing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Zhangjiajie National Forest Park: Known for its stunning natural beauty and unique rock formations, this park is located approximately 400 kilometers from Chongqing.
  2. Chengdu: Home to the famous Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding and spicy Sichuan cuisine, Chengdu is located approximately 300 kilometers from Chongqing.

Conclusion

Chongqing is a city that should not be missed on any trip to China. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural history, and delicious food, there is something for everyone in this bustling metropolis. Plan your trip to Chongqing today and experience all that this vibrant city has to offer.
