Introduction to Xijiang

Xijiang, located in Guizhou, Southwest China, is a culturally rich destination, well-known for its Miao ethnic minority village. It is an ideal destination for tourists looking for a unique cultural experience, scenic views, outdoor adventures, and tasty local cuisine. The picturesque village boasts of wooden houses built on stilts, traditional costumes, and intricate handicrafts.

List of Places to Visit in Xijiang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Xijiang Qianhu Miao Village - is a living museum showcasing the life and culture of the Miao people. Visitors can watch traditional dances, listen to Miao music, and explore the village’s intricate architecture and handicrafts.

  • Basha Miao Village - is located in the mountains adjacent to Xijiang. It is one of the most well-preserved Miao villages in China, famous for its unique customs and beliefs.

  • Xijiang Miao Embroidery Museum - is a must-visit attraction for art lovers. It displays intricate embroidery works by the Miao women, featuring ancient stories, legends, and myths.

Natural Attractions of Xijiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Leigong Mountain - is a must-visit destination for hiking enthusiasts. It offers breathtaking views of Xijiang and its surrounding areas.

  • Zhouxi Miao Village - is located near the Leigong mountain and offers spectacular views of the surrounding waterfall and terrace fields.

  • Qiandongnan National Park - is located 45 minutes away from Xijiang and offers a range of activities like hiking, camping, and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xijiang

Xijiang offers several hiking and biking routes, and here are some of the best ones:

  • Leigong Mountain Hiking Trail - is a challenging yet rewarding trail that takes you through lush forests, hidden waterfalls, and panoramic views of the village and its surroundings.

  • Xijiang to Zhouxi Miao Village Biking Route - is a scenic route that takes you through rice fields, charming old houses, and lush green forests.

Best Things to Do in Xijiang: Activities and Tours

  • Miao Cultural Show - is a fascinating experience that showcases the unique customs and traditions of the Miao people, featuring traditional dances, music, and costumes.

  • Rice Wine Tasting Tour - is a must-try for food and wine lovers. The tour takes you on a journey exploring the history and culture of rice-wine making in Xijiang, culminating in a wine-tasting session.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xijiang

Here is a perfect itinerary for a week-long trip to Xijiang:

Day Activity
1 Arrival and exploration of Xijiang Qianhu Miao Village
2 Day trip to Leigong Mountain for hiking and scenic views
3 Visit to Xijiang Miao Embroidery Museum and Basha Miao Village
4 Rice wine-making tour and tasting
5 Cycling trip to Zhouxi Miao Village
6 Qiandongnan National Park for a day of outdoor activities
7 Departure from Xijiang

Food and Drink Scene of Xijiang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sour Soup Fish - is a signature dish from Guizhou, a spicy and sour soup topped with tender fish fillets and fresh vegetables.

  • Miao Sausages - are made using a mixture of pork, chili, and various spices, smoked until they are tender and juicy, perfect for snacking.

Local Beverages

  • Rice Wine - made from fermenting glutinous rice, is an essential part of Miao culture and is available in various flavors like green tea, rose, and ginger.

  • Butterfly Pea Flower Tea - is a refreshing herbal tea that changes color based on the pH level, and is usually served with honey and lemon for added flavor.

Where to Stay in Xijiang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Xijiang Yinjie Resort Hotel - is a luxury resort with modern amenities and breathtaking views of the village and its surroundings.

  • Yueliangwan Boutique Hotel - is a cozy hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent customer service.

  • Xijiang Qilou Colorful Hostel - is a budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a friendly staff, ideal for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Xijiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Miao Embroidered Products - are an ideal souvenir to bring home, featuring intricate designs and patterns.

  • Silver Jewelry - is another must-buy, showcasing the Miao’s unique craftsmanship and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Xijiang

The Miao people are friendly, and their culture is deeply rooted in traditions and customs. Visitors are expected to be respectful of their beliefs and customs while visiting the village.

Best Time to Visit Xijiang

The best time to visit Xijiang is from March to November when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best. The village is particularly beautiful during the Miao New Year festival in November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xijiang

Do:

  • Do explore the traditional architecture and handicrafts of Xijiang Qianhu Miao Village.
  • Do respect the local customs and beliefs of the Miao people.
  • Do try the local cuisine and traditional rice wine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothes while visiting the village.
  • Don’t take photographs without permission.
  • Don’t litter in the village or surrounding areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xijiang

Xijiang is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is recommended to be cautious while hiking and follow the designated trails.

Health in Xijiang

It is advisable to carry necessary medication while visiting Xijiang. Visitors are also recommended to drink bottled water to avoid any gastric impacts during the trip.

Getting to Xijiang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Xijiang is well-connected to major cities in China, and here are some of the best ways to get there:

Airports Near Xijiang

  • Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport - is located 175 km away from Xijiang and is the nearest airport to the village.
  • Changsha Huanghua International Airport - is located 385 km away from Xijiang and is another option for reaching the village.

Bus Stations Near Xijiang

  • Kaili Bus Station - is the nearest bus station to Xijiang, and buses are available from major cities like Guiyang, Chengdu, and Changsha.

Other Options

  • Taxis are also available from Kaili city to Xijiang, and the fare should be negotiated with the driver before getting in.

How to Get Around in Xijiang

The best way to explore Xijiang is on foot, as the village is pedestrian-friendly, and many of the attractions are easily accessible within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Xijiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kaili

Kaili, located just 42 km away from Xijiang, is the capital of the Miao and Dong ethnic minority prefecture, and is an excellent destination for tourists interested in exploring the region’s unique culture and history.

Huangguoshu Waterfall

The beautiful Huangguoshu waterfall is located around 200 km from Xijiang and is a must-visit destination known for its natural beauty and scenery.

Conclusion

Xijiang is a culturally-rich destination, ideal for tourists looking for a unique experience. It boasts of beautiful scenery, outdoor adventures, traditional handicrafts, and delicious local cuisine. This comprehensive guide is perfect for planning a memorable trip to Xijiang, China.
