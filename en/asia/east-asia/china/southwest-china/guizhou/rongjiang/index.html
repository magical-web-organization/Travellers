Introduction to Rongjiang

Rongjiang is a stunning city located in the southeastern part of Guizhou province in China. This city is known for its picturesque natural landscapes, historical sites, and rich culture. Rongjiang is surrounded by hills and is home to several ethnic minority groups, including the Dong, Miao, and Shui. These groups have their own unique traditions, language, and customs, which makes Rongjiang an attractive destination for cultural tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Rongjiang

There are numerous attractions to visit in Rongjiang which will be an interesting and unique experience for travelers. Some of the popular tourist destinations are:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge: This beautiful wooden bridge was built by the Dong people in 1916 and has become an iconic symbol of their culture. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside from the bridge.
  • Jitang Old Street: Jitang Old Street is an ancient alleyway lined with traditional shops, restaurants serving local delicacies, and architectural relics. It is a great place to immerse yourself in the local culture and history of the city.
  • Sanbao Dong Village: One of the largest Dong villages in the region, Sanbao Dong has been preserved to protect and showcase their traditional architecture, arts and crafts, and daily routines.

Natural Attractions of Rongjiang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zhouxi Scenic Area: The Zhouxi Scenic Area is located in the Dong Autonomous County of Rongjiang. This area has unique karst terrain, waterfalls, and is abundant in flora and fauna.
  • Mengdong River Rafting: Rafting on the Mengdong River is a popular activity in Rongjiang. The river flows through steep and rugged terrain and is known for its white-water rapids.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rongjiang

Rongjiang offers several hiking trails that are perfect for nature lovers who enjoy exploring the great outdoors. Some of the best routes are:

  • Longsheng Trail: A scenic and moderately challenging route that will take you through the beautiful Longsheng moountain range.
  • Linhai Trail: The Linhai Trail offers a mix of natural and cultural scenery with a visit to Tang’an Dong village.

Best Things to Do in Rongjiang: Activities and Tours

Rongjiang offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best things to do in Rongjiang are:

  • Attend a Dong Opera Performance: Traditional Dong Opera performances, a form of folk art, are held at Rongjiang People’s Theatre. The performances showcase the Dong people’s culture and art.
  • Visit the Local Markets: The local markets are vibrant and colorful, selling fresh produce, local handicrafts, and traditional clothing.
  • Explore the Gulong Miaozhai Scenic Area: The Gulong Miaozhai Scenic Area is a beautiful attraction that features rich Miao culture, exotic customs, and unique customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rongjiang

For a week-long itinerary in Rongjiang, we suggest the following experiences:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the old town and visit Jitang Old Street and Sanbao Dong Village
Day 2 Visit Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge, then hike around the bridge
Day 3 Spend a day rafting on the Mengdong River
Day 4 Go on a bike tour to Tang’an Dong Village
Day 5 Visit Gulong Miaozhai and learn about the Miao culture

Food and Drink Scene of Rongjiang

Rongjiang offers some unique and exquisite dishes that are worth trying. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sour Soup Noodles: This is a popular dish found throughout Guizhou. The dish includes thin noodles in a sour soup broth, with pork, potato, and pickled vegetables.
  • Dong Fish: This is a local specialty from the Dong minority, and is cooked using traditional methods.

Local Beverages

  • Maotai: Maotai is a type of Chinese liquor made from fermented sorghum. It is the national drink of China.
  • Rongjiang Honey: This local honey is a specialty of Rongjiang, and is made from the nectar of flowers from the local area.

Where to Stay in Rongjiang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rongjiang has a mix of accommodations, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the top places to stay in Rongjiang are:

  • Rongjiang Dongda Hotel: A well-appointed hotel in the heart of the city, offering comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Dongxiang Hotel: A budget hotel with clean rooms and helpful staff.
  • Sanbao International Youth Hostel: A popular hostel offering cozy rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Rongjiang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rongjiang has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take the opportunity to buy traditional Dong and Miao handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. Some of the best products to bring home are:

  • Batik Cloth: Handcrafted by the Dong people using wax-resistant dyeing techniques. This cloth is popular for its delicate patterns and bright colors.
  • Wooden Crafts: The Dong people are known for their exquisite wooden craftsmanship. Visitors can buy wooden items such as combs, flower vases, and frames.

Local Culture and Customs of Rongjiang

Rongjiang is home to many ethnic minority groups that have their own unique cultures and traditions. Visitors should experience the local culture by participating in traditional festivals and attending performances of Dong opera.

Best Time to Visit Rongjiang

The best time to visit Rongjiang is between March and November when the weather is mild and pleasant. The peak season is from April to October when the scenery is lusher and more vibrant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rongjiang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Rongjiang:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Negotiate prices at the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Touch any religious statues or monuments without permission.
  • Take pictures of the locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rongjiang

Rongjiang is a safe destination for tourists, and violent crimes are rare. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions like keeping their valuables secure at all times.

Health in Rongjiang

The tap water in Rongjiang is not safe to drink. Visitors should drink bottled water or boil water before drinking it.

Getting to Rongjiang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rongjiang

The nearest airport to Rongjiang is the Kaili Huangping Airport (KJH), which is approximately 2 hours away by road. Another option is the Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport (KWE), which is about 5 hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Rongjiang

Rongjiang Bus Station is the main bus station in the city, providing connections to various cities in Guizhou.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Rongjiang, but this is more expensive than taking a bus.

How to Get Around in Rongjiang

The best way to get around Rongjiang is by using public transportation such as buses, taxis, and tuk-tuks. Bicycles are also available for rent, which is a great way to explore the city at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Rongjiang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations that are worth visiting from Rongjiang are:

  • Kaili: Kaili is 2 hours away from Rongjiang and is home to several ethnic minority groups, museums, and natural attractions.
  • Leishan: Leishan is a small town located east of Rongjiang that is known for its beautiful Huangdu Dong Village.

Conclusion

Rongjiang is a spectacular destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and history. It is an excellent choice for travelers who seek an authentic and unforgettable experience. With its friendly locals, mouth-watering local cuisine, scenic natural landscapes, and fascinating culture, Rongjiang is a city that should be on every traveler’s list.
