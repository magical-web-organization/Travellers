Discover Tongren: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Tongren is a small city located in the eastern part of Guizhou province, in Southwest China. The city has a rich history and culture and is known for its natural beauty, traditional architecture, and unique art forms. The city is also home to several ethnic minorities, including the Miao and Dong people. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know to plan your trip to Tongren, including where to stay and eat, what to see and do, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Tongren

Tongren has several culturally significant attractions, natural wonders and scenic views, and outdoor adventure activities to explore. Here is a list of must-visit places in Tongren:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wanshou Palace: This ancient palace was built during the Ming Dynasty and is home to several historical artifacts and traditional architecture.
  • Drum Tower: The Drum Tower is the tallest building in Tongren and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding countryside.
  • Guizhou Folk Art Museum: The museum showcases the unique art and culture of the Miao and Dong people.

Natural Attractions of Tongren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Fanjing: The highest peak in the Wuling Mountains, Mt. Fanjing is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to several rare and endangered species.
  • Zhangjiang River Scenic Area: The picturesque river offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Wanfeng Lake: A scenic spot with clear waters and lush greenery, Wanfeng Lake is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and relaxing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tongren

Tongren offers several hiking and biking trails for adventure enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Mt. Fanjing Scenic Area: The trek to the summit of Mt. Fanjing offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.
  • Wanfeng Lake Scenic Area: The hiking and biking trails around Wanfeng Lake offer beautiful views of the lake and the surrounding forests.
  • Zhangjiang River Scenic Area: The river offers several hiking trails that take you through lush green forests and along the riverbank.

Best Things to Do in Tongren: Activities and Tours

  • Visit traditional villages: Tongren is home to several traditional villages with unique architecture and cultural practices such as Shiwan and Tang’an villages.
  • Attend local festivals: Tongren hosts several annual festivals, including the Miao New Year and the Dong Music Festival.
  • Take a cooking class: Learn how to make traditional Guizhou dishes with a local chef.
  • Take a cultural tour: Book a guided cultural tour to learn about the unique customs and practices of the Miao and Dong people.

Fanjing Day 3: Visit the traditional village of Tang’an Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to make local Guizhou dishes Day 5: Attend the Miao New Year festival Day 6: Explore the Zhangjiang River Scenic Area Day 7: Depart Tongren and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Tongren

Guizhou cuisine is known for its spicy and sour flavors and is influenced by local ingredients and cooking techniques. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Tongren:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sour fish hotpot: A spicy and sour hotpot dish with freshwater fish and local vegetables.
  • Siwawa: Steamed sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves and filled with pork, mushrooms, and other local ingredients.
  • Miao-style rice noodles: Thin rice noodles served with spicy broth and topped with minced meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Maotai: A strong distilled spirit and a Chinese national drink.
  • Wu Jue: A local wine made from fermented glutinous rice and barley.

Where to Stay in Tongren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tongren offers a range of accommodations for every budget. Here are some recommendations:

  • Tongren Fanjing Mountain Yijun Hotel: A modern hotel located in the scenic area of Mt. Fanjing.
  • Tongren Tang’an Shiwan Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse located in the Tang’an village.
  • Tongren Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the city center.

What to Buy in Tongren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Embroidery: Tongren is known for its intricate embroidery, especially by the Miao people.
  • Paper-cuttings: A traditional craft in China, paper-cuttings are intricate and colorful.
  • Batik cloth: A traditional textile method where wax is applied to cloth to create a unique pattern.

Local Culture and Customs of Tongren

Tongren is home to several ethnic minorities, and their unique customs and traditions are an important part of the city’s culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as respecting local dress codes, and asking permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Tongren

The best time to visit Tongren is fromMarch to October when the weather is mild and comfortable. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and attend local festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tongren

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting traditional villages or religious places.
  • Try local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Touch or damage local cultural artifacts.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tongren

Tongren is generally a safe city, but visitors should be cautious when exploring the city at night and keep their valuables safe. Visitors should also be careful when hiking, especially in the mountainous areas.

Health in Tongren

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude when hiking in Tongren and take the necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness. Visitors should also stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as the sun can be strong at high altitudes.

Getting to Tongren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tongren has several transportation options, including flights, trains, and buses.

Airports Near Tongren

  • Tongren Fenghuang Airport: Located about 15 km from the city center, the airport offers domestic flights to major Chinese cities.

Bus Stations Near Tongren

  • Tongren Bus Station: Located in the city center, the bus station offers both long-distance and local buses.

Other Options

  • Trains: The nearest train station is in Huaihua, located about 3 hours from Tongren by bus.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available throughout the city and are a convenient way to get around.

How to Get Around in Tongren

Tongren is a relatively small city, and walking is the best way to explore the city center. Buses and taxis are also readily available, and rental bikes are available at some hotels.

Where to Go Next from Tongren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tongren is located near several other popular destinations in Guizhou province. Here are some nearby places visitors can explore:

  • Fenghuang Ancient Town: A picturesque town famous for its traditional architecture and stunning river views.
  • Guiyang: The capital city of Guizhou province, Guiyang is known for its unique cuisine and vibrant nightlife.
  • Zhangjiajie: One of China’s most famous natural wonders, Zhangjiajie is home to stunning sandstone pillars and a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Tongren is a hidden gem in Guizhou province, offering a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure activities. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Tongren with ease, and make the most of their time in this beautiful city.
