Introduction to Weining

Weining is a small town in the Guizhou province in Southwest China. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and biodiversity. Situated in the northern part of the province, Weining is home to several ethnic groups, including the Miao, Yi, and Gelao. Visitors to the town will be impressed by the unique blend of traditional and modern culture that exists here. In this guide, we will explore some of the places to visit, things to do, and experiences to have in Weining.

List of Places to Visit in Weining

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, there are several museums and historical sites to explore in Weining.

1. Weining Confucian Temple

Built in 1658, the Weining Confucian Temple is a must-visit destination for those interested in ancient Chinese culture. The temple contains many artifacts and exhibits that showcase Chinese poetry, calligraphy, and literature.

2. Weining White Pagoda

Also known as the “White Dragon Pagoda” or “Bai Long Ta,” this ancient Buddhist temple dates back to the Ming dynasty (1368-1644). It is a unique blend of Chinese and Tibetan architecture and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

3. Suoluo Village

A traditional Miao village located about 15 kilometers outside of Weining. The village is known for its unique architecture and cultural customs, including its famous bullfighting festivals.

Natural Attractions of Weining: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Weining is a paradise for nature lovers with its rolling hills, lush forests, and rushing rivers. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Weining.

1. Zuoqi Black River Scenic Area

Located in the southwestern part of Weining, the Black River is a scenic area boasting natural wonders such as waterfalls, mountains, and unique rock formations. The area is perfect for hiking, camping, and fishing.

2. Zangke River

One of the largest tributaries of the Chishui River, the Zangke River is known for its crystal-clear waters, unique rock formations, and dense forests. Visitors can enjoy rafting, fishing, and hiking along the riverbank.

3. Heihe Wetlands National Nature Reserve

Located in Weining County, the Heihe Wetlands National Nature Reserve is home to a variety of plant and animal species. Visitors can explore the wetlands on foot or by boat and watch for rare and endangered species such as the Chinese Merganser and the White-eared Night Heron.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Weining

Weining is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top trails to explore in and around Weining.

  1. Zhijin Cave Scenic Area: This area offers more than 20 kilometers of hiking trails through a spectacular landscape of karst formations, underground rivers, and dense forests.

  2. Qianxi Forest Park: This park offers a variety of trails that meander through the stunning forests of Weining. From easy walks to challenging hikes, there is something for everyone here.

  3. Zangke River Scenic Area: This area offers a challenging, 48-kilometer cycling route that follows the Zangke River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Weining: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Weining, whether you prefer exploring nature or immersing yourself in the local culture.

  1. Attend a Bullfight Festival: In Suoluo Village, visitors can experience one of the most unique cultural events in the region – the bullfighting festival. The festival is held in August and is a fantastic way to immerse yourself in local traditions.

  2. Visit the Guiyang Zoo: Located just an hour’s drive from Weining, the Guiyang Zoo is home to over 5,000 animals, including rare and endangered species like the giant panda and South China Tiger.

  3. Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the beauty of Weining from above with a hot air balloon ride. Glide over the rolling hills and forests and take in the stunning scenery below.

Food and Drink Scene of Weining

Weining is known for its unique cuisine, which is strongly influenced by the local Miao and Gelao cultures. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sour Fish Soup (Suan Tang Yu)

  2. Roast Whole Sheep (Kao Quan Yang)

  3. Miao Bacon (Miao La Rou)

Local Beverages

  1. Dian Hong Tea

  2. Shan Lao Hong Wine

Where to Stay in Weining: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Weining, including budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts.

  2. Hongfeng Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

  3. Weining Youth Hostel: A popular hostel that offers affordable dorm rooms and private rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and lounge area.

Local Culture and Customs of Weining

Weining has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience local customs and traditions through festivals, local food, and unique architecture.

Best Time to Visit Weining

The best time to visit Weining is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Weining

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

  2. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  3. Try the local cuisine and drink.

Don’t:

  1. Take photographs of locals without their permission.

  2. Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public areas.

  3. Engage in behavior that may be offensive to local people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Weining

Weining is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions to protect their personal belongings.

Health in Weining

Visitors should ensure that they have current vaccinations and take precautions to protect themselves from mosquitoes and other insects.

Getting to Weining: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Weining

The nearest airports to Weining are Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport (GYN) and Kunming Changshui International Airport (KMG), which are both about three hours’ drive from Weining.

Bus Stations Near Weining

There are several bus stations in Weining, including the Weining Bus Station and the Weining North Bus Station. Buses run regularly to other towns and cities in the region, including Guiyang and Kunming.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire in Weining.

How to Get Around in Weining

The best way to get around Weining is by taxi, bike or on foot, although there are also regular buses that connect the town with nearby destinations.
