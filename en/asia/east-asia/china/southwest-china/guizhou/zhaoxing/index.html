Introduction to Zhaoxing

Nestled in the mountains of Guizhou in Southwest China, Zhaoxing is a charming town known for its rich culture, natural beauty, and historic architecture. Home to the Dong ethnic minority, Zhaoxing offers travelers an authentic glimpse into the daily life, traditions, and customs of this ancient people. With its idyllic landscapes, quaint streets, and friendly locals, Zhaoxing is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to China.

List of Places to Visit in Zhaoxing

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As a town steeped in history and culture, Zhaoxing boasts several significant museums and historical sites, including:

  • Zhaoxing Drum Tower: Arguably the most recognizable landmark in Zhaoxing, the drum tower stands at the center of town and serves as a gathering place for the community. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Dong Heritage Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look into the traditional architecture, clothing, music, and customs of the Dong people. With multiple exhibits and a wealth of information, the museum is a must-see for anyone interested in Dong culture.
  • Zhaoxing Wind and Rain Bridge: One of several wind and rain bridges in the region, this beautiful wooden bridge spans the river that runs through Zhaoxing. It’s a popular spot for taking photos and enjoying the peaceful sounds of the water.
  • Tang’an Dong Village: About an hour’s hike from Zhaoxing, Tang’an is a picturesque Dong village that’s been designated as a protected cultural site. Its well-preserved architecture and traditional way of life offer a fascinating glimpse into the history and traditions of the Dong people.

Natural Attractions of Zhaoxing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond its rich cultural heritage, Zhaoxing boasts some of the most stunning natural landscapes in all of China, including:

  • Jitang Rice Terraces: Located just outside of Zhaoxing, the Jitang Rice Terraces are a breathtaking sight to behold. Visitors can hike through the terraces, take in the stunning views, and learn about the traditional farming practices of the Dong people.
  • Zhaoxing Dong Village Park: This park offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of town life. With its immaculate gardens, tranquil ponds, and serene walking paths, it’s a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.
  • Huanggang Mountain: For more adventurous visitors, a hike up Huanggang Mountain offers unparalleled views of the surrounding countryside. The trek is challenging but well worth it for the stunning panorama at the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhaoxing

With its stunning natural landscape, Zhaoxing is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Some of the best routes in the area include:

  • Jitang Rice Terraces Hike: This moderate hike takes visitors through the stunning Jitang Rice Terraces, where they can learn about traditional farming practices and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Huanggang Mountain Hike: This challenging hike takes visitors to the top of Huanggang Mountain, where they can take in stunning panoramic views that stretch for miles.
  • Zhaoxing to Tang’an Village Hike: This scenic hike takes visitors from Zhaoxing to the picturesque Tang’an Dong Village, with its well-preserved architecture and traditional way of life.

Best Things to Do in Zhaoxing: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and sightseeing, there are plenty of other fun things to do in Zhaoxing, including:

  • Dong Minority Singing and Dancing Performance: Don’t miss the opportunity to witness a traditional Dong singing and dancing performance, which is filled with lively music, colorful costumes, and intricate choreography.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to make traditional Dong dishes like sour fish soup and glutinous rice cakes by taking a cooking class with a local instructor.
  • Biking Tours: Explore the scenic countryside around Zhaoxing on a guided bicycle tour, which will take you through rice paddies, past waterfalls, and into remote villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zhaoxing

Day 1: Arrive in Zhaoxing, check into your hotel, and take a leisurely stroll around town to get your bearings.

Day 2: Visit the Dong Heritage Museum to get a deeper understanding of Dong culture, then explore the town’s historic Drum Tower and Wind and Rain Bridge.

Day 3: Take a hike through the Jitang Rice Terraces, stopping to learn about traditional farming practices along the way.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Huanggang Mountain for a challenging but rewarding hike with stunning panoramic views.

Day 5: Visit Tang’an Dong Village to witness its well-preserved architecture and learn about traditional Dong life.

Day 6: Participate in a traditional Dong cooking class before taking in a lively singing and dancing performance.

Day 7: Spend the day biking through the scenic countryside around Zhaoxing, then reflect on your trip over dinner at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhaoxing

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Zhaoxing is a feast for the senses, with dishes featuring fresh, local ingredients and bold Dong flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Sour Fish Soup: This tangy, fragrant soup is made with fresh fish and sour bamboo shoots, and is a favorite among Dong people.
  • Glutinous Rice Cakes: These sweet, sticky cakes are a Dong specialty, made with glutinous rice flour and stuffed with sweet or savory fillings.
  • Pickled Vegetables: Dong cuisine features plenty of pickled vegetables, which add a tangy, flavorful punch to any meal.

Local Beverages

Zhaoxing is also known for its unique and flavorful beverages, including:

  • Rice Wine: This traditional Dong drink is made from fermented rice, and has a slightly sweet, slightly sour taste.
  • Herbal Tea: With the abundance of medicinal herbs found in the mountains around Zhaoxing, it’s no surprise that the Dong people have a long history of brewing herbal teas for their health benefits.

Where to Stay in Zhaoxing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For a true taste of traditional Dong hospitality, consider staying in one of the town’s guesthouses, which offer comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere. Some top picks include:

  • Zhaoxing Dong Village Inn: This charming inn is housed in a traditional Dong building, with wooden beams, sloping roofs, and a courtyard garden. Rooms are clean and comfortable, and the staff is friendly and helpful.
  • Zhaoxing Chunxiang Guesthouse: With its rustic wooden interiors and scenic mountain views, this guesthouse has a cozy, homelike feel. Rooms are spacious and well-appointed, and the restaurant serves up delicious homemade Dong dishes.

What to Buy in Zhaoxing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some top souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Zhaoxing include:

  • Embroidered Cloth: The Dong people are known for their intricate embroidery work, which can be found on everything from clothing to table runners.
  • Bamboo Weavings: Bamboo weavings, such as baskets and mats, are a typical handicraft of the Dong people and make great decorative items for the home.
  • Rice Wine: If you’ve developed a taste for Zhaoxing’s unique rice wine, be sure to bring a bottle or two home to share with family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Zhaoxing

As a town steeped in tradition, you’ll want to be sure to respect the local culture and customs during your visit to Zhaoxing:

  • Clothing: Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Food and Drink: When dining with locals, it’s customary to try every dish and not waste food. It’s also polite to offer a toast before drinking.
  • Language: Though many locals speak Mandarin Chinese, the Dong people have their own unique language, which you may hear being spoken on the streets of Zhaoxing.

Best Time to Visit Zhaoxing

The best time to visit Zhaoxing is from May to October, when the weather is mild and precipitation is low. Peak tourist season runs from July to August, so if you want to avoid the crowds, plan to visit in the shoulder seasons of May to June or September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhaoxing

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be sure to dress modestly, try the local food and drink, and learn about local customs and traditions.
  • Take Your Time: Zhaoxing moves at a slower pace than many other tourist destinations in China, so take your time and enjoy the relaxed atmosphere.
  • Get Off the Beaten Path: Explore the countryside around Zhaoxing to get a deeper understanding of the natural beauty and traditional way of life in this region.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Local Culture: Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and avoid behavior that could be considered disrespectful or inappropriate.
  • Litter: Help keep Zhaoxing clean by disposing of your trash in the proper receptacles.
  • Overlook the Natural Beauty: While the cultural attractions in Zhaoxing are worth visiting, don’t forget to take in the breathtaking natural beauty of the region as well.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhaoxing

Zhaoxing is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, it’s always wise to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking in unfamiliar areas or traveling alone at night.

Health in Zhaoxing

Before traveling to Zhaoxing, be sure to check with your doctor to make sure you’re up-to-date on all necessary vaccinations. Additionally, be sure to bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Zhaoxing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhaoxing

The closest airport to Zhaoxing is Huangping Airport, which offers domestic flights to cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or hire a private driver to drive the 100km to Zhaoxing.

Bus Stations Near Zhaoxing

There are several bus stations in the region that connect to Zhaoxing, including the Congjiang North Bus Station and the Sanjiang Bus Station. Buses run regularly from major cities like Guiyang and Guangzhou.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange for private transportation to Zhaoxing, either by hiring a driver or renting a car. However, be aware that driving in China can be challenging for those who aren’t used to the traffic and road conditions.

How to Get Around in Zhaoxing

The best way to get around Zhaoxing is on foot, as the town is small and easy to navigate. For longer journeys, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Zhaoxing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sanjiang: This is another town famous for its Wind and Rain Bridges and Dong architecture, located about two hours’ drive from Zhaoxing.
  • Fanjingshan National Nature Reserve: This beautiful nature reserve is home to Fanjing Mountain, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that boasts stunning views and unique flora and fauna.
  • Liping: This ancient town is home to a wealth of historic architecture, including preserved city walls and temples.

Conclusion

Zhaoxing is a hidden gem in the heart of China, with its rich culture, natural beauty, and friendly locals offering an unforgettable travel experience. From exploring historic sites and museums to hiking through rice terraces and mountain trails, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Zhaoxing – making it a must-visit destination for travelers of all types.
