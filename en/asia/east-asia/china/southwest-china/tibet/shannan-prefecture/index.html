Introduction to Shannan (Prefecture)

Shannan (Prefecture), located in Tibet, in Southwest China, is a hidden gem worth visiting. It is rich in natural scenery, cultural significance, and a long and rich history dating back to the Tang Dynasty. The prefecture is divided into ten counties and cities, including Gonggar County, Lhunze County, and Quxu County.

Shannan (Prefecture) is approximately 290 kilometers from the city of Lhasa, and it is accessible by air, train, and road. The best way to get there is by flying to Lhasa Gonggar Airport and then taking a taxi or bus to the prefecture.

This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive tour of Shannan (Prefecture), including the best places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink, accommodations, and local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Shannan (Prefecture)

Shannan (Prefecture) has a wide range of attractions for tourists to explore, from historical sites and museums to natural wonders and scenic areas.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Samye Monastery: Founded in the 8th Century, Samye Monastery is the oldest monastery in Tibet and a site of immense cultural significance.
  • Yungbulakang Palace: Built in the 2nd Century BC, Yungbulakang Palace is considered to be the first palace in Tibet and the birthplace of Tibetan civilization.
  • Tombs of 25 Tibetan Kings: Located in Chongye County, these tombs are the final resting place of 25 Tibetan Kings, and the site is a testament to the region’s rich history.
  • Mindroling Monastery: Located in Nêdong County, Mindroling Monastery is one of the six largest monasteries of Nyingma.

Natural Attractions of Shannan (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yarlung Tsangpo River: Known as the “mother river” of Tibet, the Yarlung Tsangpo River offers breathtaking views of the region’s natural wonders.
  • Gongga Mountain: With an elevation of over 7,000 meters, Gongga Mountain offers unparalleled views and is a popular destination for experienced hikers.
  • Yamalung Valley: The Yamalung Valley is one of the most beautiful valleys in Tibet, with stunning views and a rich history of religious significance.
  • Yumbulagang Palace: The Yumbulagang Palace is located on a hill and offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shannan (Prefecture)

Shannan (Prefecture) offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for adventurers and nature enthusiasts.

  • Yarlung Tsangpo Grand Canyon: The Grand Canyon is an excellent destination for hiking and biking, with its beautiful natural scenery and challenging terrain.
  • Lake Yamdrok: Lake Yamdrok is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Tibet and offers stunning scenery, as well as hiking and biking routes for visitors.
  • Karola Glacier: The Karola Glacier is a beautiful destination for hiking and biking, with its stunning views and unique natural characteristics.

Best Things to Do in Shannan (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Explore the rich history of Tibet by visiting cultural sites and landmarks.
  • Embark on a hiking or biking expedition through the natural wonders of the region.
  • Experience the local culture by attending traditional festivals and events.

Food and Drink Scene of Shannan (Prefecture)

Shannan (Prefecture) is known for its unique cuisine, which is a blend of Tibetan and Chinese flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tibetan butter tea: This staple drink of Tibetan cuisine is a blend of tea, salt, and butter, and is a must-try for visitors to the region.
  • Tsampa: Tsampa is a Tibetan staple food made from roasted barley flour and is often consumed with butter tea.
  • Yak meat: Yak meat is a common protein source in Tibetan cuisine and can be prepared in a variety of ways, including stir-fry and soup.

Local Beverages

  • Chang: Chang is a traditional Tibetan alcoholic beverage made from fermented barley and is often consumed during celebrations and festivals.
  • Tibetan beer: Tibetan beer is a local favorite and is made from barley and hops.

Local Culture and Customs of Shannan (Prefecture)

Tibetan culture is a unique blend of Buddhism, traditional customs, and practices.

Best Time to Visit Shannan (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Shannan (Prefecture) is from April to October when the weather is mild and stable, and visitors can enjoy the stunning natural scenery.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shannan (Prefecture)

Shannan (Prefecture) is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be cautious when hiking, biking, or participating in outdoor activities and should seek the advice of local experts to ensure their safety.

Health in Shannan (Prefecture)

Visitors to Shannan (Prefecture) should be aware of the high altitude and take the necessary precautions, including acclimating slowly, staying hydrated, and avoiding strenuous activities during the first few days.
