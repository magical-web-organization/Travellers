Introduction to Nagqu (Prefecture)

Located in the northern part of Tibet Autonomous Region in China, Nagqu (Prefecture) is a strikingly beautiful area that offers a glimpse of untouched wilderness and traditional Tibetan culture. The prefecture covers an area of 450,537 square kilometers with a total population of over 430,000 people. Nagqu is known for its grasslands, pristine lakes, and mountain ranges that are perfect for a range of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Nagqu (Prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nagqu Museum

Nagqu Museum is an excellent place to learn about the history and culture of Nagqu and the surrounding area. The museum exhibits include historic documents, textiles, and ancient artifacts such as pottery, coins, and bronze ware.

Lhagang Monastery

One of the most significant monasteries in Nagqu, Lhagang Monastery, was built during the Yuan Dynasty (1271-1368). It is located on the left bank of the Nujiang River, and the building complex contains several halls and courtyards that are typical of Tibetan monastery architecture.

Natural Attractions of Nagqu (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Siling Lake

Siling Lake, also known as the “Mysterious Lake,” is the largest saltwater lake in the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau with an area of around 1,700 square kilometers. Surrounded by vast grasslands and snow-capped mountains, the lake has a unique landscape that changes with the seasons.

Nyenchen Tanglha Mountain Range

Nyenchen Tanglha Mountain Range features soaring snow-clad peaks, clear rivers, and vast glaciers. The highest peak is Nyenchen Tanglha Peak (7,162 m), which is sacred to Tibetans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nagqu (Prefecture)

Lhagang Grassland Trek

The trek takes 4-5 days on average and covers over 50 kilometers. During the trek, you can explore the natural beauty of Lhagang Grassland as you cross vast grasslands, walk through gorges and valleys, and climb hills to get stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Qinghai-Tibet Plateau Mountain Biking Route

The Qinghai-Tibet Plateau Mountain Biking Route is one of the most popular and challenging cycling routes in China. The route extends through Nagqu and covers a total distance of over 2,000 kilometers.

Best Things to Do in Nagqu (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

Horse Riding

Horse riding is one of the most popular activities in Nagqu. Explore the vast grasslands and stunning natural beauty of Nagqu and immerse yourself in the Tibetan culture as you ride on horseback.

Camping

Camping is an excellent way to enjoy the beautiful natural scenery, fresh air, and the stunning night sky in Nagqu. There are several campsites to choose from, and visitors can rent a tent, camping gear, and other necessary equipment.

Food and Drink Scene of Nagqu (Prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

Tibetan Butter Tea

Tibetan Butter Tea, known as Po Cha, is a traditional beverage made from tea leaves, yak butter, and salt. It is considered the national drink of Tibet and has a unique taste.

Tibetan Tsampa

Tsampa is a staple food in Tibetan cuisine made from roasted barley flour. It is commonly served as a soup or mixed with butter tea.

Local Beverages

Tibetan Chang

Tibetan Chang, also known as Raksi, is a popular alcoholic beverage made from barley or rice. It has a unique taste and is served hot or cold.

Tibetan Milk Tea

Tibetan Milk Tea, known as Po Cha, is made from black tea leaves, yak butter, and salt. It is a tasty way to stay warm during Tibet’s cold winter months.

Where to Stay in Nagqu (Prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nagqu Hotel

Located in the city center of Nagqu, Nagqu Hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities such as free Wi-Fi, satellite TV, and private bathroom facilities.

Friend House

Friend House is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in Lhagang town. It offers simple rooms with shared bathroom facilities.

What to Buy in Nagqu (Prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tibetan Jewelry

Tibetan Jewelry, typically made of silver, is one of the most popular souvenirs in Nagqu. The intricate designs and traditional techniques used to make them reflect the rich Tibetan culture.

Tibetan Carpet

Tibetan Carpet, commonly known as the “Dragon Carpet,” is known for its unique design and high-quality craftsmanship. It is a popular souvenir that visitors can buy in Nagqu.

Local Culture and Customs of Nagqu (Prefecture)

Tibetan culture is rich and unique, and visitors to Nagqu can experience it in many ways. A few aspects of Tibetan culture in Nagqu:

  • Tibetan Buddhism is the dominant religion in Nagqu
  • Tibetan opera is an important part of the local culture
  • Traditional Tibetan dress is still widely worn in Nagqu

Best Time to Visit Nagqu (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Nagqu is from May to October when the weather is mild, and the scenic surroundings offer good visibility. In winter, Nagqu is severely cold, which can make it difficult to explore the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nagqu (Prefecture)

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry enough cash while traveling in rural areas
  • Dress modestly as Nagqu is predominantly a religious area

Don’t:

  • Do not take photographs without permission
  • Do not walk on the wetland, so as not to damage the fragile ecosystem
  • Do not touch or remove any religious items in monasteries

Safety and Health

Safety in Nagqu (Prefecture)

Nagqu is generally a safe area to travel to, but visitors should be aware of the risk of altitude sickness. It is essential to stay hydrated, rest well, and avoid strenuous activity when first arriving in Nagqu.

Health in Nagqu (Prefecture)

Visitors should also take precautions to prevent colds, respiratory infections, and other illnesses that can be prevalent in high-altitude areas. It is a good idea to pack appropriate clothing and a first aid kit.

Getting to Nagqu (Prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nagqu (Prefecture)

The closest airport to Nagqu is Lhasa Gonggar Airport, which is around 600 kilometers away from Nagqu. Visitors can catch flights to Lhasa from several major cities in China.

Bus Stations Near Nagqu (Prefecture)

Several bus stations operate in Nagqu, with buses connecting to other destinations in Tibet and China.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a car or taxi to travel around Nagqu.

How to Get Around in Nagqu (Prefecture)

Tourists can rent motorcycles or bicycles to explore Nagqu, or a private car with a driver can be hired to travel around the area.

Where to Go Next from Nagqu (Prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lhasa

Lhasa, the capital of Tibet Autonomous Region, is just a few hundred kilometers away from Nagqu. The city is known for its rich history, stunning landmarks, and ancient religious sites.

Namtso Lake

Located in Damxung County, Namtso Lake is one of the most sacred lakes in Tibet and has stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Nagqu (Prefecture) is a unique travel destination that combines natural beauty, traditional culture, and a range of outdoor activities. With so many attractions and activities to choose from, visitors are sure to enjoy their experience in Nagqu.
