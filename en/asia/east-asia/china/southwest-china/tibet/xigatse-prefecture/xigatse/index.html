Introduction to Xigatse

Located in the southwest region of Tibet, Xigatse is one of the most scenically beautiful destinations in China. It’s a popular choice for travelers looking for a mix of culture and natural beauty. With its sprawling monasteries, mountain passes, and vast lakes, Xigatse is a great place to experience the rich history and stunning landscapes of Tibet. This guide will help you make the most of your visit here.

List of Places to Visit in Xigatse

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tashilhunpo Monastery: The seat of the Panchen Lama, Tashilhunpo is a sprawling complex that combines religious devotion with architectural grandeur. The 144-foot-tall Maitreya Buddha statue is one of the highlights.

  • Sakya Monastery: Known for its unique architecture, Sakya Monastery has great historical and religious significance.

  • Shalu Monastery: A beautiful example of Tibetan architecture, Shalu Monastery is known for its frescos.

  • Jangtang Lhakhang: A shrine and temple with stunning views of the Himalayas.

Natural Attractions of Xigatse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Everest Base Camp: One of the highest points on earth, it is a must-visit spot in Tibet for mountaineers and adventurers.

  • Gyantse: A beautiful town surrounded by mountains and lakes, Gyantse is a great place to appreciate Tibet’s natural beauty.

  • Yamdrok Lake: A breathtakingly-beautiful lake, Yamdrok Lake is a truly awe-inspiring sight.

  • Karo La Glacier: Located near Nam Tso Lake, it’s a breathtaking spot to visit for fans of nature and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xigatse

  • Tashilhunpo Monastery trek: It’s a beautiful trek that you can complete in 1-2 days, starting from the Tashilhunpo Monastery.

  • Mount Everest Base Camp trek: A 14-day trek that requires physical fitness, but it’s a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

  • Kora around Mt. Kailash: This 32-mile trail passes through beautiful landscapes and offers a chance to experience local customs and rituals.

Best Things to Do in Xigatse: Activities and Tours

  • Local tours: A great way to experience Xigatse’s culture and history, local tours offer insights and information about the region.

  • Cultural events: Check out the calendar for traditional festivals like the Saga Dawa Festival or the Shoton Festival to experience local culture.

  • Yak racing: A prime example of traditional Tibetan entertainment, Yak racing is a must-see event.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xigatse

Day 1-2: Tashilhunpo Monastery and Jangtang Lhakhang

Spend two days exploring one of Tibet’s most famous monasteries and its surroundings. Enjoy scenic views and the peaceful atmosphere of the temple.

Day 3-4: Mount Everest Base Camp

Visit the base camp and make the most of the natural scenery.

Day 5-6: Gyantse and Karo La Glacier

Head to Gyantse, a beautiful town with stunning views of the mountains and surrounded by lakes. Visit Karo La Glacier to see the natural beauty of the region.

Day 7: Cultural Tour

Participate in a cultural tour to learn more about Tibet’s rich culture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Xigatse

Must-Try Dishes

  • Momos: A popular Tibetan dumpling filled with veggies, meat, or cheese.

  • Tibetan yogurt: A creamy yogurt with a unique texture and taste.

  • Kham pa: A local staple food that is made of barley flour and is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Butter tea: A salty and rich traditional Tibetan tea made with yak butter and tea leaves.

  • Chang: A traditional Tibetan alcoholic beverage made of barley, rice, or corn.

Where to Stay in Xigatse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Xigatse Hotel: A luxurious option with stunning views of the mountains.

  • TashiChoeta Hotel: A great value and convenient location in central Xigatse.

  • Youth Hostel: A popular option for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Xigatse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thangka: A traditional Tibetan painting that makes a great souvenir.

  • Tibetan jewelry: Handmade by local craftsmen, Tibetan jewelry is unique and meaningful.

  • Tibetan incense: A traditional scent that is used for purification and meditation.

Local Culture and Customs of Xigatse

  • Respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes, photography policies, and religious rituals.

  • Engage with locals to learn about the rich culture and history of Tibet.

Best Time to Visit Xigatse

The best time to visit Xigatse is from May to October, when the weather is mild and clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xigatse

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and respectfully in religious places and public areas.

  • Learn a few Tibetan phrases to connect with locals.

  • Try the local food and drinks to experience the culture.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Take pictures without permission in religious places or with locals.

  • Damage the environment by littering or leaving trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xigatse

  • Be cautious when trekking mountains and glaciers, as weather can be unpredictable.

  • Secure your valuables and do not trust strangers with your belongings.

Health in Xigatse

  • Altitude sickness is a common concern, so take enough time to acclimate to the altitude.

  • Drink bottled water and avoid raw or uncooked food to prevent foodborne illnesses.

Getting to Xigatse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xigatse

  • Lhasa Gonggar Airport: Around 195 km from Xigatse, it’s the nearest airport to Xigatse.

  • Shigatse Peace Airport: Xigatse has its own airport that connects to some major Chinese cities.

Bus Stations Near Xigatse

  • Xigatse Bus Station: The main bus station in the city, connecting to various destinations in Tibet and China.

Other Options

  • Private car: Hiring a private car is a comfortable and convenient way to travel around Xigatse.

  • Train: The Lhasa-Everest Base Camp railway connects Xigatse with Lhasa, Shigatse, and other destinations in Tibet.

How to Get Around in Xigatse

  • Public transportation: Xigatse has cheap and efficient public transportation options like buses and taxis.

  • Private car: You can hire a private car to explore Xigatse at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Xigatse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lhasa: The capital of Tibet and a hub of culture and spiritual activities.

  • Namtso Lake: One of the highest lakes in the world, Namtso Lake is located at over 4,700 meters above sea level.

  • Shigatse: Another beautiful city in Tibet and home to the Tashilhunpo Monastery.

Conclusion

Xigatse is a must-visit destination in Tibet for those looking for a mix of culture and natural beauty. With its stunning monasteries, scenic trekking routes, and delicious food, Xigatse has something for everyone. Follow this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this one-of-a-kind city.
