Introduction to Qamdo

Located in the eastern part of Tibet in China, Qamdo (also known as Chamdo or Changdu) prefecture is one of the largest prefectures in Tibet. Qamdo is famed for its beautiful natural landscapes, diverse cultural experiences, and unique local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Qamdo

Qamdo is filled with a number of tourist attractions that offer an experience like no other. Here are some of the must-visit places in Qamdo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Galden Jampaling Monastery
  • Baiyu Monastery
  • Museum of Chamdo Prefecture
  • Riwoche Monastery
  • Lhunze County

Natural Attractions of Qamdo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rawok Lake
  • Basum Lake
  • Lulang Forest
  • Lancang River Valley
  • Mount Namjagbarwa

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qamdo

For nature lovers, there are a number of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the beautiful valley and mountainous terrain. Here are some of the popular hiking/biking routes in Qamdo:

  • Peikucuo Glacier Trek (Three-day trek)
  • Yanjingdong Trek (Half-day trek)
  • Shenzha to Lhegu Trek (Two-day trek)

Best Things to Do in Qamdo: Activities and Tours

Apart from scenic views and hiking trails, there are plenty of activities and tours to participate in Qamdo. Here are some of the best things to do in Qamdo:

  • Visit the local markets and shops for souvenirs and local handicrafts
  • Attend traditional dances and music performances
  • Participate in the various festivals celebrated in Qamdo

Week-Long Itinerary for Qamdo

For travelers who plan to stay in Qamdo for a week or more, here’s an itinerary that covers all the important attractions and activities of Qamdo:

Day Itinerary
1 Visit Galden Jampaling Monastery and Museum of Chamdo Prefecture
2 Drive to Lulang Forest for a scenic view of the forest
3 Trek to Peikucuo Glacier
4 Visit Rawok Lake
5 Attend the local festival (if scheduled)
6 Trek Shenzha to Lhegu Trek
7 Visit Baiyu Monastery and farewell to Qamdo

Food and Drink Scene of Qamdo

The food and drink scene in Qamdo is dominated by typical Tibetan cuisine that includes momos, tsampa, and butter tea. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Qamdo:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yak meat dishes
  • Tingmo (steamed bread)
  • Sha Phaley (fried bread with meat filling)

Local Beverages

  • Yak butter tea
  • Chang (Tibetan beer)
  • Highland barley wine

Where to Stay in Qamdo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a wide range of accommodation options in Qamdo ranging from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Qamdo:

  • Qomolangma Hotel
  • Chamdo Hotspring Hotel
  • Yalong International Hotel
  • Zhuomalai Hostel

What to Buy in Qamdo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Qamdo offers various unique and interesting souvenirs for tourists, including local handicrafts, Tibetan-style jewelry, and traditional clothes. Here are some of the top souvenirs to buy in Qamdo:

  • Tibetan knives
  • Tibetan carpets
  • Thangka Paintings
  • Hand-woven baskets

Local Culture and Customs of Qamdo

The local culture and customs of Qamdo are heavily influenced by Tibetan Buddhism. Tourists are advised to respect the local customs and avoid offending any religious sentiments of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Qamdo

The ideal time to visit Qamdo is from May to October as the weather is mild during this time, and the trekking trails are more accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qamdo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry enough cash as credit/debit cards are not widely accepted
  • Dress modestly while visiting monasteries and other religious sites

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions
  • Take photographs without seeking permission
  • Get involved in political discussions with the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Qamdo

Qamdo is generally a safe place for tourists to visit. However, travelers are advised to be cautious, especially while trekking or hiking.

Health in Qamdo

Travelers should carry medication for altitude sickness while visiting Qamdo as the altitude is higher than other regions in Tibet.

How to Get Around in Qamdo

The best way to travel around Qamdo is by hiring a local guide or renting a private car.
