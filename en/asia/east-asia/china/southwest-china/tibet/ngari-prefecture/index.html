Introduction to Ngari (Prefecture)

Ngari (prefecture), also known as Ali, is a prefecture in the northwest of the Tibet Autonomous Region, China. Ngari has a unique combination of natural beauty, ancient history, and spiritual significance. It is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a rare blend of adventure, mystique, and cultural immersion.

The rugged yet stunning terrain of Ngari boasts numerous monasteries, lakes, and mountains that are of significant religious and cultural importance. Many of these locations and landmarks are considered sacred by the local people and attract thousands of visitors each year.

In this travel guide, we will explore Ngari’s top attractions, best places to stay, local customs and traditions, and much more. Read on to find out everything you need to know before embarking on a journey to this beautiful part of the world.

List of Places to Visit in Ngari (Prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tholing Monastery: Tholing Monastery is a majestic 11th-century monastery that features impressive frescoes, prayer halls, and sacred relics. It was one of the earliest monasteries built in Tibet and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Guge Kingdom Ruins: The Guge Kingdom Ruins are located in a remote area of Tibet and are an impressive archaeological site that attests to the glory of the Guge Kingdom from the 10th to the 17th century. The ruins feature well-preserved frescoes and sculptures, and visitors can learn about the daily life and culture of the former kingdom.

Natural Attractions of Ngari (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kailash: Mount Kailash is considered one of the most sacred mountains in the world by several religions, including Buddhism, Hinduism, Jainism, and Bon. It is renowned for its natural beauty and spiritual significance and offers various trekking routes and hiking trails.

  • Manasarovar Lake: Manasarovar Lake is considered one of the most sacred lakes in the world by several religions. It is a beautiful high-altitude lake that offers stunning views of its crystal-clear blue waters, ancient temples, and snow-capped mountains. Visitors can take a dip in the lake’s holy waters, go for a boat ride, or simply sit back and soak up the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ngari (Prefecture)

  • Mount Kailash Trek: The Mount Kailash Trek is considered one of the most challenging and rewarding treks in the world. The trek takes you around the sacred Mount Kailash and offers stunning views of the Himalayas, glaciers, and turquoise lakes.

  • Manasarovar Kora: The Manasarovar Kora is a popular trek around the holy Manasarovar Lake. The trek offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, ancient temples, and local culture.

Best Things to Do in Ngari (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Visit Local Villages: Ngari is home to several small, traditional villages where visitors can learn about the local culture, cuisine, and way of life. Some of the most popular villages include Purang, Darchen, and Zanda.

  • Attend a Festival: Ngari has several festivals throughout the year that offer visitors a unique opportunity to experience local customs and traditions. Some of the most popular festivals include the Saga Dawa Festival, the Tibetan New Year, and the Guge Kingdom Cultural Tourism Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Ngari (Prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thenthuk: Thenthuk is a traditional Tibetan noodle soup made with hand-pulled noodles, vegetables, and meat.

  • Chang: Chang is a traditional Tibetan beer made from fermented barley. It has a slightly sweet and malty taste and is a popular beverage during festivals and celebrations.

Local Beverages

  • Butter Tea: Butter tea is a staple beverage in Tibet and is made by mixing salt, butter, and tea leaves. It is a rich and filling drink that is perfect for keeping warm in the cold mountain climate.

  • Yak Milk: Yak milk is a nutritious and tasty beverage that is enjoyed by locals in Ngari. It has a slightly sweet and creamy taste and is often used to make butter and cheese.

Where to Stay in Ngari (Prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tholing Guesthouse: Tholing Guesthouse is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a central location in Zanda.

  • Shangrila Hotel: Shangrila Hotel is a mid-range hotel that offers modern amenities, delicious food, and a convenient location near Mount Kailash.

  • Sheraton Tibet Hotel: Sheraton Tibet Hotel is a luxurious hotel that offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped mountains and is equipped with top-notch facilities, including a spa and restaurant.

What to Buy in Ngari (Prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tibetan Carpets: Tibetan carpets are renowned for their intricate designs, vibrant colors, and exceptional quality. They are available in several markets and stores in Ngari and make for a unique and valuable souvenir.

  • Prayer Flags: Prayer flags are colorful flags that feature prayers and mantras of Tibetan Buddhism. They are used to promote peace, compassion, and wisdom and make for a lovely souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Ngari (Prefecture)

Ngari has a rich and unique cultural heritage that is deeply influenced by Tibetan Buddhism. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes, temple rules, and religious practices. It is also important to be mindful of the environment and avoid littering or damaging protected areas and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Ngari (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Ngari is during the months of May to October when the weather is mild, and the skies are clear. The temperature can be cool, so visitors are advised to bring warm clothing and dress in layers. It is also recommended to avoid traveling during peak tourist season to avoid crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ngari (Prefecture)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Try local food and beverages
  • Be mindful of your impact on the environment

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Disrespect religious practices or temples
  • Litter or damage natural landmarks or protected areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Ngari (Prefecture)

Ngari is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors are advised to take precautions, especially when trekking or hiking in remote areas. Altitude sickness can be a health risk, so it is important to acclimate slowly and drink plenty of water. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for earthquakes and landslides in the area.

Health in Ngari (Prefecture)

Visitors to Ngari should consult their doctor or travel health clinic before departing and ensure they are up to date on all standard vaccinations. Altitude sickness is a common health risk due to the high altitude, so it is important to acclimate slowly and avoid overexertion.

Getting to Ngari (Prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ngari (Prefecture)

The nearest airport to Ngari is the Ngari Gunsa Airport, located in the town of Shiquanhe. The airport has regular flights to several cities in China, including Beijing, Chengdu, and Lhasa.

Bus Stations Near Ngari (Prefecture)

There are several bus stations in Ngari that offer services to neighboring towns and cities. The most popular bus station is in Shiquanhe and offers regular services to Lhasa, Ali, and other destinations.

Other Options

Private tours and drivers are also available in Ngari and can offer a comfortable and convenient way to explore the area. Visitors should ensure they book a reputable and licensed tour operator to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

How to Get Around in Ngari (Prefecture)

The most common way to get around Ngari is by bus, which offers services to neighboring towns and cities. Taxis and private drivers are also available, although they can be expensive. Many visitors choose to hire bicycles or trek to explore the area and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.

Where to Go Next from Ngari (Prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lhasa: Lhasa is the capital of Tibet and is home to several iconic landmarks, including the Potala Palace and Jokhang Temple.

  • Nepal: Nepal is a neighboring country to Tibet and offers a unique cultural and scenic experience, including trekking to Mount Everest Base Camp.

Conclusion

Ngari is a unique and mystical destination that offers visitors an opportunity to explore some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes and cultural landmarks in the world. From its majestic monasteries and ruins to its crystal-clear blue lakes and snow-capped mountains, Ngari has something to offer everyone.
