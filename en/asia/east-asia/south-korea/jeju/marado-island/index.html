Introduction to Marado Island

Marado Island is a small, secluded island located in the southernmost part of South Korea. The island is known for its natural beauty, unique culture, and outdoor adventures. It is a perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful getaway that allows them to recharge and connect with nature.

List of Places to Visit in Marado Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marado Traditional Hall – a museum that showcases the island’s cultural heritage, offering an insight into the lifestyle, traditions, and customs of the islanders.
  • Dolhareubang Park – a park that features more than 40 dolhareubangs, the iconic stone statues found all over Jeju Island.

Natural Attractions of Marado Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Marado Lighthouse – this lighthouse, situated at the island’s southernmost point, provides stunning views of the surrounding sea.
  • Haenyeo Museum – this museum pays homage to the island’s haenyeos, female divers who collect seafood and harvest seaweed without using any breathing apparatus.
  • Marado Forest – nature lovers can explore the unique flora and fauna of the island.
  • Marado Beach – this secluded beach offers a serene escape from the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marado Island

Marado Island has excellent hiking and biking trails, which offer an excellent opportunity to explore the island’s rugged terrain and unique flora and fauna. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Marado Coastal Trail – this trail follows the island’s southern coastline, offering stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.
  • Marado Forest Trail – this trail winds through the lush forest, taking hikers through the island’s diverse flora and fauna.
  • Marado Ridge Trail – this trail takes hikers to the island’s highest point, providing unparalleled panoramic views of Jeju Island’s southern coast and the sea beyond.

Best Things to Do in Marado Island: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving – Marado Island’s clear waters are perfect for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts.
  • Camping – nature lovers will adore the island’s campsites that offer stunning views of the sea.
  • Sea Kayaking – explore the island’s rugged coastline on a sea kayaking tour.
  • Fishing – try your hand at fishing like the locals do.
  • Jeju Marado Island Massage – relax with a traditional Jeju massage, famous for its healing properties.

Food and Drink Scene of Marado Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Abalones – Marado Island’s waters are home to some of the best abalones in South Korea, making it a must-try dish for seafood lovers.
  • Black Pork – one of the island’s specialties, this pork has a unique flavor due to the pigs’ diet of chestnuts and acorns.
  • Grilled Fish – freshly caught fish on the island grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Jeju Mandarins – Marado Island is famous for its mandarins, which are known for their sweetness and juiciness.
  • Jeju Soju – a traditional Korean alcoholic beverage made from rice, barley, or wheat. Jeju Island has its variety with a distinct flavor because of its volcanic soil.

Where to Stay in Marado Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marado Island has several accommodation options that cater to different budget needs. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Dongwon Guesthouse – located near the beach, this guesthouse offers stunning sea views and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Marado Hostel – a budget-friendly hostel with private rooms and dorms.
  • Ocean Hill Hotel – a luxury hotel with all the modern amenities, offering stunning views of the sea and the island.

What to Buy in Marado Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Haenyeo Figurines – these figurines depict the haenyeos, female divers who collect seafood without using any breathing apparatus.
  • Marado Tangerines – take home a bag of sweet and juicy tangerines, a perfect souvenir.
  • Jeju Black Pork Jerky – this unique snack features thinly sliced black pork that is seasoned and dried.

Local Culture and Customs of Marado Island

The island has its unique culture and customs. Some of the noteworthy ones include:

  • Haenyeos – the female divers are an essential part of the island’s culture and economy.
  • Black Pork – this unique pork variety reflects the islanders’ respect for nature and tradition.
  • Dolhareubangs – these iconic stone statues found all over Jeju Island are considered gods of protection and fertility, and a symbol of the island’s cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Marado Island

The best time to visit Marado Island is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, allowing visitors to enjoy the island’s outdoor activities fully.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marado Island

Do:

  • Respect the island’s culture and customs, including the haenyeos’ way of life.
  • Take care of the island’s pristine natural environment by disposing of garbage correctly.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing while enjoying the island’s outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Take any natural souvenirs, such as plants or stones, from the island.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Consume marine animals or plants that you have caught without proper knowledge of marine biology, and if it is allowed in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marado Island

Marado Island is considered a safe destination. However, visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking. It is always best to seek local guidance or go with a local guide.

Health in Marado Island

The island has good health infrastructure. However, visitors must carry their medications and travel insurance for emergencies.

Getting to Marado Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marado Island can only be reached by boat. The island is serviced by regular ferry services from the mainland’s Jeju Island, with a journey time of about an hour.

Airports Near Marado Island

Jeju International Airport (CJU) is the closest airport to Marado Island. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Seogwipo Port, where they can catch a ferry to Marado Island.

Bus Stations Near Marado Island

Seogwipo Bus Terminal is the nearest bus station to Marado Island. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a local bus to Seogwipo Port.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car or hire a taxi from Jeju Island to Seogwipo Port, making it convenient for those traveling in large groups.

How to Get Around in Marado Island

The island is small, and it is easy to explore on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take taxis or local buses.

Where to Go Next from Marado Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jeju Island has many nearby destinations worth exploring after visiting Marado Island. Some of the popular options include:

  • Hallasan National Park – a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its diverse flora and fauna and impressive volcano trails.
  • Jeju Teddy Bear Museum – a museum dedicated to teddy bears, offering an insight into the culture and history of South Korea.
  • Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak – a volcanic peak and famous sunrise spot.

Conclusion

Marado Island offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience South Korea’s natural beauty, culture, and outdoor adventures. From hiking the island’s scenic trails to exploring its cultural heritage, visitors are spoilt for choice. With delicious local cuisine, friendly locals, and a peaceful atmosphere, Marado Island is the perfect place to unwind and connect with nature.
