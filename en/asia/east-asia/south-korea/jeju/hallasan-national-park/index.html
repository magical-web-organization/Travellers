A Comprehensive Guide to Hallasan National Park

Located in the heart of Jeju Island, South Korea, Hallasan National Park is a natural haven that offers visitors breathtaking views of mountain landscapes, rare flora and fauna, and shimmering waterfalls. This UNESCO World Heritage site is a must-see destination for nature lovers, hikers, and tourists alike. Discover the beauty and adventure of Hallasan National Park with our detailed travel guide. Explore the best hikes, cultural attractions, food and drink scene, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Hallasan National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jeju Folk Village Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and folk traditions of Jeju Island. Visitors can explore 117 traditional houses, farmhouses, and more.
  • Seongeup Folklore Village: This is a living museum where visitors can experience the life of Jeju people in the olden days. Highlights include ancient homes, blacksmithing, and pottery making.
  • Yongduam Rock: This iconic rock formation looks like a dragon’s head stretching out to sea. It’s one of the most famous landmarks in Jeju Island and attracts millions of visitors every year.

Natural Attractions of Hallasan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hallasan Mountain: The centerpiece of the park, Hallasan Mountain is the highest peak in South Korea. Hikers can choose from several trails, ranging from easy to challenging, that offer panoramic views of Jeju Island.
  • Eorimok Trail: This trail is ideal for families and beginners. It’s a leisurely walk through lush forests and winding streams.
  • Cheonjiyeon Waterfall: This magnificent waterfall features three tiers and plunges 22 meters into a pool below. It’s especially stunning at night when it’s lit up.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hallasan National Park

Hallasan National Park has a variety of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Yeongsil Trail: A 3.7-kilometer-long (2.3-mile-long) trail that passes through dense forests and rocky terrain. It’s a moderate hike that takes around 2.5 hours.
  • Gwaneumsa Trail: A challenging 8.7-kilometer-long (5.4-mile-long) trail that takes around 5 hours to complete. Hikers are rewarded with breathtaking views of Hallasan Mountain.
  • Ole 8 Hiking Trail: This trail is part of the larger Olle Trail network and is a 7.6-kilometer-long (4.7-mile-long) hike that takes around 3 hours. It’s a great option for those who want to soak up the natural beauty of Jeju Island.

Best Things to Do in Hallasan National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Canyoning: Explore the rocky canyons of Jeju Island and rappel down thundering waterfalls with professional guides.
  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy a leisurely horseback ride through the lush forests surrounding Hallasan Mountain.
  • Jeju Island East Course Bus Tour: This eight-hour bus tour takes visitors to the east coast of Jeju Island, where they can see stunning landscapes, traditional villages, and pristine beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hallasan National Park

Day 1: Jeju Folk Village Museum, Seongeup Folklore Village, and Yongduam Rock

Day 2: Eorimok Trail, Cheonjiyeon Waterfall, and Gwaneumsa Trail

Day 3: Eco Land Theme Park, Halla Mountain and National Park, and Jeju Stone Park

Day 4: Udo Island, Woljeongri Beach, and Jeju Kimnyoung Maze Park

Day 5: Underground Cave, Geomunoreum Lava Tube System, and Hyeopjae Beach

Day 6: Manjanggul Cave, Camellia Hill, and Hangpaduri Hangmong Historical Site

Day 7: Jeju Island East Course Bus Tour, Arang Joeun Community, and Seogwipo Sunday Flea Market

Food and Drink Scene of Hallasan National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jeonbokjuk: A rice porridge made with abalone and vegetables.
  • Heukdwaeji: Black pork that’s known for its tender and juicy flavor.
  • Gogi-guksu: A spicy noodle soup made with beef broth, chili flakes, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Jeju Soju: A rice-based liquor that’s smooth and easy to drink.
  • Samdasoo Water: A refreshing and natural mineral water that’s bottled in Jeju Island.
  • O’Sulloc Green Tea: Jeju Island is famous for green tea, and O’Sulloc is one of the most popular brands.

Where to Stay in Hallasan National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Brown Suite Hotel: This stylish hotel is located in the heart of Jeju City and offers guests a relaxing and comfortable stay.

  • J2 Family Guesthouse: This family-run guesthouse is located near Hallasan Mountain and offers clean and cozy rooms.

  • Ecos Nature Park: This nature-themed resort is situated near Eongtto Falls and offers guests a unique and eco-friendly experience.

What to Buy in Hallasan National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jeju Island Black Tea: Jeju Island is famous for its tea, and black tea is one of the most popular varieties.

  • Jeju Island Tangerines: These sweet and juicy tangerines are a popular snack and gift item.

  • Jeju Island Hyeopjae Beach Sand: This unique souvenir features sand from the beautiful Hyeopjae Beach and is a great keepsake for visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Hallasan National Park

Jeju Island has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its music, dance, and art. Visitors can explore local museums, attend traditional festivals and ceremonies, and learn about the island’s unique history and customs.

Best Time to Visit Hallasan National Park

The best time to visit Hallasan National Park is from March to May and from September to November. The weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most beautiful during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hallasan National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites.

  • Take care of the environment by avoiding littering and following designated hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission from locals or traditional performances.

  • Touch or climb on cultural artifacts and statues.

  • Ignore safety signs and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hallasan National Park

  • Always stay on designated hiking trails.

  • Carry enough water and food for your hiking trip.

  • Check weather conditions before your visit and be prepared for changing weather.

Health in Hallasan National Park

  • Bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito bites.

  • Bring sunscreen to protect your skin from harmful UV rays.

  • Be wary of eating raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Hallasan National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hallasan National Park

  • Jeju International Airport: This is the closest airport to Hallasan National Park and offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Hallasan National Park

  • Jeju Intercity Bus Terminal: This is the main bus station in Jeju Island and offers regular bus services to Hallasan National Park.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Jeju Island, and it’s an easy and convenient way to get to Hallasan National Park.

How to Get Around in Hallasan National Park

  • Hiking and Walking: The best way to explore Hallasan National Park is on foot. There are several well-marked hiking trails that offer stunning views of the park.

  • Public Transportation: Buses are available to take visitors to various parts of the park.

  • Bicycle: Visitors can rent bicycles and explore the park at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Hallasan National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Seogwipo City: This charming seaside town is a popular destination for its beaches, waterfalls, and cultural attractions.

  • Udo Island: This small island is located off the coast of Jeju Island and is known for its beautiful beaches and traditional villages.

  • Jeju City: The largest city on Jeju Island, Jeju City is home to several museums, shopping districts, and other tourist attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, adventurer, or culture enthusiast, Hallasan National Park has something for everyone.
