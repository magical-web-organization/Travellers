Introduction to Jeju City

Jeju City is a scenic coastal town located in the southern part of Jeju Island, South Korea. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, cultural attractions, and impressive geological features like volcanic craters, caves, and mountains, Jeju City is a must-visit destination for those who want to experience the natural beauty of South Korea.

List of Places to Visit in Jeju City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history and culture enthusiasts, Jeju City has a lot to offer. Here are some of the top museums and historical places to visit:

  • Jeju National Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Jeju Island, with exhibits on art, architecture, folklore, and more.
  • Jeju Folklore and Natural History Museum: Learn about the unique flora and fauna of Jeju Island at this natural history museum, which also features a collection of traditional Korean artifacts and folklore.
  • Haenyeo Museum: Here, visitors can learn about the livelihood and culture of the Haenyeo, the female divers who collect seafood from the ocean floor.

Natural Attractions of Jeju City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jeju City is known for its stunning natural landscapes, from the serene beaches to the rugged volcanic hills. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Hallasan National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site includes Hallasan, the highest peak in South Korea, and offers breathtaking views of the island’s lush forests and volcanic terrain.
  • Jeju Olle Trail: The Jeju Olle Trail is a network of 26 hiking routes that traverse the island’s coastlines, forests, and mountains for a total of 424 kilometers of trails.
  • Seongsan Ilchulbong: This volcanic crater is a must-visit destination in Jeju City, with a scenic view of the surrounding sea and countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jeju City

With its stunning landscapes and numerous trails, Jeju City is a fantastic place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Jeju Olle Trail Route 1: This trail follows the coastline from the charming village of Sehwa-ri to the famous Seongsan Ilchulbong volcanic crater.
  • Hallasan Traverse: Experienced hikers will enjoy this strenuous 9-hour hike that takes visitors to the summit of Hallasan, the highest peak in South Korea.
  • Geumneung Mountain Trail: This easy 3 km hike takes visitors through the forested hills of Geumneung Mountain, offering stunning views of the countryside and coastline.

Best Things to Do in Jeju City: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and sightseeing, Jeju City has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Jeju Glass Castle: This artistic theme park showcases stunning glass sculptures and mosaics, making for a unique and colorful experience.
  • Horse-Riding on the Beach: Experience the beauty of Jeju Island’s beaches on horseback, with guided tours offered near the city.
  • Jeju Teseum Aquarium: This modern, state-of-the-art aquarium features exhibits on marine life from Korea and around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jeju City

Jeju City is a fantastic destination for a week-long vacation, with plenty of natural and cultural attractions to explore. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Haenyeo Museum and the Jeju Folklore and Natural History Museum, then hike the Geumneung Mountain Trail.
  • Day 2: Explore the Seongsan Ilchulbong volcanic crater, then visit the Jeju Glass Castle in the afternoon.
  • Day 3: Take a guided horseback tour of the beaches of Jeju Island, then spend the evening at the Jeju Teseum Aquarium.
  • Day 4: Hike the Jeju Olle Trail Route 1 to Sehwa-ri and explore the village in the afternoon.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby Udo Island for more hiking and sightseeing.
  • Day 6: Spend the day at Hallasan National Park, taking in the sweeping views and scenic mountain trails.
  • Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the Jeju Traditional 5-Day Market and relax on the beach before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Jeju City

Must-Try Dishes

Jeju Island is known for its fresh seafood and unique cuisine, influenced by the island’s geography and history. Here are some dishes to try:

  • Haejangguk: This spicy soup made with beef, vegetables, and blood sausage is a traditional hangover cure in South Korea.
  • Jjajangmyeon: A popular Korean-Chinese dish, this noodle dish features a savory black bean sauce.
  • Fresh Seafood: Try Jeju Island’s famous octopus, sashimi, and abalone, served fresh and cooked in a variety of delicious styles.

Local Beverages

In addition to its cuisine, Jeju Island is also known for its local beverages. Here are some to try:

  • Hallasan Soju: Produced using water from Hallasan, this locally made soju is a popular drink on Jeju Island.
  • Makgeolli: This traditional Korean rice wine is brewed on the island and is known for its sweet, milky flavor.
  • Citrus-Flavored Tea: Made with fresh citrus fruit grown on Jeju Island, this tea is a refreshing and healthy drink.

Where to Stay in Jeju City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jeju City has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Hotel Astar: This luxury hotel features spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Jeju Tongyeong Hotel: This mid-range hotel offers comfortable rooms and great amenities like an on-site restaurant, fitness center, and sauna.
  • Youngsan Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse has a family atmosphere and offers affordable rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Jeju City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jeju Island is known for its unique products and souvenirs, many of which can only be found on the island. Here are some to bring home:

  • Jeju Tangerines: These juicy and sweet citrus fruits are a must-try on the island, and are available to bring home in all forms, from fresh to dried.
  • Jeju Black Pork: This unique breed of pork is only found on Jeju Island and is known for its high quality and delicious flavor.
  • Jeju Artisanal Crafts: From pottery to textiles, Jeju Island’s artisans produce a variety of high-quality handicrafts that make great gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Jeju City

Jeju Island has its unique cultural traditions and customs, many of which have been passed down for generations. Here are some to keep in mind:

  • Haenyeo: The tradition of female divers, called Haenyeo, is still practiced on the island today.
  • Jeju Folk Songs: Jeju Island has a rich tradition of folk songs, which are often accompanied by the traditional instrument called the gayageum.
  • Jeju Olle Trail: Walking the Jeju Olle Trail is an important cultural activity on the island, with many locals viewing it as a way to connect with the island’s natural beauty and history.

Best Time to Visit Jeju City

The best time to visit Jeju City depends on your preferences and interests. Spring and fall are mild and pleasant, while summer brings warm temperatures and more crowds. Winter can be snowy and cold, but is also a great time for winter sports and hot springs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jeju City

Do:

  • Experience the Natural Beauty of Jeju Island: From hiking to beach-hopping, make sure to take advantage of all the stunning scenery the island has to offer.
  • Try the Local Foods: Jeju Island has a unique cuisine influenced by its geography and history, so be sure to try some of the island’s dishes and drinks.
  • Learn about the Culture: Jeju Island has a rich history and cultural traditions that are fascinating to learn about and experience.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Local Customs: Be aware of local customs and traditions, and avoid actions that could be seen as disrespectful or offensive.
  • Damage the Natural Environment: Be respectful of the island’s natural environment by practicing Leave No Trace principles and avoiding littering or damaging plant life.
  • Cause Disturbances: Avoid loud or disruptive behavior, especially in residential areas, and be mindful of others while hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jeju City

Jeju City is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and a welcoming atmosphere. However, visitors should still take basic precautions like securing valuables, being aware of surroundings, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Jeju City

Jeju Island has modern medical facilities and a good standard of healthcare. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and take necessary precautions like staying hydrated and using sunscreen in the summer months.

Getting to Jeju City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jeju City

Jeju International Airport is located in Jeju City and is the primary airport for the island. It offers domestic and international flights from cities like Seoul, Busan, Tokyo, and Taipei.

Bus Stations Near Jeju City

Jeju City has several intercity bus terminals that connect to other parts of the island, as well as to major cities on the Korean peninsula.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the ferry from several ports on the mainland to the island of Jeju, although this option is less convenient and takes longer than flying or taking a bus.

How to Get Around in Jeju City

The best way to get around Jeju City is by renting a car or using public transportation. Jeju Island has an extensive bus network that connects most major towns and attractions. Taxis and rental cars are also widely available.

Where to Go Next from Jeju City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jeju Island has many other destinations and attractions to explore near Jeju City. Here are some worth considering:

  • Seogwipo City: This coastal city is the second-largest on Jeju Island and is home to many natural attractions, parks, and scenic coastal drives.
  • Udo Island: This small island just off the coast of Jeju City is a popular day trip destination, featuring pristine beaches and rugged hiking trails.
  • Manjanggul Cave: This impressive lava tube system is located near Seongsan Ilchulbong and is another must-visit natural attraction on Jeju Island.

Conclusion

Jeju City is a fascinating and scenic destination that offers visitors a unique look at the natural beauty and cultural traditions of South Korea. Whether you’re interested in hiking, sightseeing, or trying local foods and drinks, Jeju City has something for everyone.
