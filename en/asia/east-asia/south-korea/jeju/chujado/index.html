Introduction to Chujado

Chujado is a picturesque island located off the southwestern coast of Jeju, South Korea. A small island measuring only 11.8 square kilometers, Chujado is known for its unspoiled natural beauty, stunning beaches, and coastal rock formations. Visitors are drawn to the island’s laid-back vibe, unique culture, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Chujado

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chujado Gwangjeongsa Temple: One of the island’s most important cultural landmarks, this ancient temple is believed to date back to the 9th century. Visitors can explore the temple’s grounds, take part in a traditional tea ceremony, or attend one of the temple’s famed music concerts.
  • Chujado Traditional Culture Experience Hall: A fascinating museum that showcases the island’s unique culture and customs, including its traditional agriculture and fishing practices. Visitors can try their hand at weaving or pottery-making, or sample some of Chujado’s famous black pork.
  • Chujado Natural History Museum: A small but informative museum that delves into the island’s unique geology and ecology. Visitors can learn about Chujado’s endangered species, or take part in a guided nature walk around the island.

Natural Attractions of Chujado: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jangsanbong Peak: At 78 meters high, this peak is one of Chujado’s most popular hiking spots. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the island and its surrounding ocean, as well as take part in a wide range of outdoor activities, such as rock climbing or bird-watching.
  • Gyehangri Beach: A beautiful, unspoiled beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or simply relaxing. Visitors can also hike along the nearby coastal trails or go fishing in the island’s clear waters.
  • Jeungdo Rock: One of Chujado’s most famous landmarks, this unique rock formation is said to resemble the shape of a dragon. Visitors can climb to the top of the rock for incredible views of the island and the surrounding sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chujado

  • Jeju Olle Trail Route 18: A beautiful coastal trail that leads visitors past some of Chujado’s most stunning natural landmarks, such as the Oedolgae Rock and Jeungdo Rock.
  • Jangsanbong Peak Trail: A challenging hiking trail that takes visitors to the top of Jangsanbong Peak, offering breathtaking views of the island and the sea below.
  • Chujado Coastal Bike Trail: A leisurely bike ride that takes visitors along the island’s beautiful coastline, passing through quaint fishing villages and picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in Chujado: Activities and Tours

  • Black Pork BBQ: No trip to Chujado would be complete without sampling the island’s famous black pork. Visitors can try it grilled or braised, and can even learn to cook it themselves at one of the island’s many cooking classes.
  • Snorkeling/Scuba Diving: With its crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life, Chujado is a paradise for underwater enthusiasts. Visitors can book a guided snorkeling or scuba diving tour to explore the island’s colorful reef systems and see some of its unique marine creatures up close.
  • Sea Kayaking: For a more relaxed adventure, visitors can rent a sea kayak and explore the island’s stunning coastline at their own pace. With its calm waters and secluded coves, Chujado is the perfect place for a peaceful day on the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chujado

Day 1: Arrive on the island and settle into your accommodations. Day 2: Begin exploring the island’s cultural attractions, such as the Chujado Traditional Culture Experience Hall and Gwangjeongsa Temple. Day 3: Take part in some outdoor adventures, such as hiking to the top of Jangsanbong Peak or exploring the island’s beautiful beaches. Day 4: Continue your explorations with a guided sea kayaking tour or a snorkeling/scuba diving excursion. Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Jeju Island to explore its famous attractions, such as the Jeju Stone Park or Mount Hallasan. Day 6: Sample some of Chujado’s local cuisine and relax on the beach. Day 7: Depart the island and head back to the mainland.

Food and Drink Scene of Chujado

Must-Try Dishes

  • Black Pork BBQ: As mentioned earlier, Chujado’s black pork is a must-try for any foodie visiting the island.
  • Haemul Pajeon: A savory seafood pancake that is a popular snack or appetizer on the island.
  • Heuk Dweji: A hearty stew made with black pork and a variety of vegetables, often served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Jeju Tangerine Makgeolli: A traditional rice wine that is flavored with Jeju tangerines, giving it a sweet, fruity taste.
  • Bijoori: A refreshing, non-alcoholic drink made from Jeju’s famous Hallabong oranges.

Where to Stay in Chujado: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chujado Guesthouse: A charming and affordable option that offers comfortable accommodations and stunning ocean views.
  • Chujado Benikea Hotel: A more upscale option that features luxurious rooms, on-site dining, and a rooftop pool with panoramic views of the island.
  • Chujado Camping Ground: For a more adventurous option, visitors can choose to camp at one of the island’s many camping grounds, such as the Chujado Camping Ground, which offers stunning seaside views and convenient amenities.

What to Buy in Chujado: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Black Pork Products: Chujado’s black pork is not only delicious, but is also smoked and cured into a variety of unique products, such as ham or bacon, which make for great gifts to bring home.
  • Traditional Crafts: Visitors can purchase handmade pottery, weaving, or woodwork items that showcase the island’s traditional crafts and techniques.
  • Jeju Tangerines: A perfect souvenir for foodies, Jeju tangerines are a sweet and juicy fruit that is grown only on the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Chujado

Chujado has a unique culture that is heavily influenced by its maritime history and traditional way of life. Visitors can experience this culture through various cultural experiences, such as attending a tea ceremony or participating in a local festival. Additionally, visitors should be respectful of the island’s customs, such as removing shoes before entering someone’s home, and be mindful of the environment by practicing responsible tourism.

Best Time to Visit Chujado

The best time to visit Chujado is during the spring or fall months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The island can become very crowded during the summer months, which may impact the overall experience. The winter months, while colder, can also offer a unique experience for visitors looking for a quieter, more serene island atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chujado

Do:

  • Respect the island’s unique culture and customs.
  • Take part in the island’s outdoor activities and try the local cuisine.
  • Be mindful of the environment and practice responsible tourism.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home.
  • Leave trash or litter behind when exploring the island’s outdoor attractions.
  • Disrupt the local wildlife or natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chujado

Chujado is generally a safe place to visit, with low levels of crime and petty theft. Visitors should, however, exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or swimming, and follow safety guidelines provided by local authorities.

Health in Chujado

Medical facilities on Chujado are limited, though there are clinics and pharmacies available for basic healthcare needs. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with swimming or snorkeling in the island’s waters, and take necessary precautions to avoid injury or illness.

Getting to Chujado: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chujado

Jeju International Airport is the nearest airport to Chujado, located on the mainland of Jeju. From there, visitors can take a ferry or flight to the island.

Bus Stations Near Chujado

There are no bus stations on Chujado itself, though visitors can take a bus from Jeju City to Moseulpo, where they can catch a ferry to Chujado.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to take them to Chujado, which can provide flexibility and convenience for exploring the island.

How to Get Around in Chujado

The best way to get around Chujado is on foot, as the island is compact and easily navigable. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or motorbike to explore the island at their own pace. Alternatively, visitors can take a taxi or hire a private driver to get around the island.

Where to Go Next from Chujado: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can easily take a ferry or flight from Chujado back to Jeju City, where they can explore the many attractions of Jeju Island. Additionally, visitors can take a day trip to nearby islands such as Marado, Hyeongjeseom, or Gapado, for more outdoor adventures and cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Chujado is a hidden gem in Jeju, South Korea, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re looking to escape the crowds of the mainland or want to explore the rich history and traditions of Korea, Chujado is the perfect destination for a memorable and unforgettable vacation.
