Introduction to North Jeolla

Nestled in the southwest corner of South Korea, North Jeolla is a province filled with historical sites, natural attractions, and delicious cuisine. Home to the city of Jeonju, known for its traditional hanok villages and mouth-watering bibimbap, North Jeolla offers visitors a unique experience with its stunning scenery, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in North Jeolla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jeonju Hanok Village: Explore the traditional Korean houses and enjoy authentic cuisine.
  • Jeonju National Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Jeolla Province.
  • Naejangsan National Park: Hike through the fall foliage and witness the natural beauty of South Korea.
  • Gunsan Modern History Museum: Experience the evolution of Gunsan as an international port city.
  • Gyeonggijeon Shrine: Pay respects to renowned historical figures and learn about their legacy.

Natural Attractions of North Jeolla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baegyangsa Temple: Venture on a scenic hike to enjoy views of the temple and its surroundings.
  • Byeonsanbando National Park: Experience rocky cliffs, white sand beaches, and great hiking trails.
  • Wolchulsan National Park: Hike through the beautiful landscapes and take in the natural wonders of South Korea.
  • Tapsa Temple: Witness the unique stone pagodas and soak in the stunning panoramas.
  • Jangsuja, the Pine forest for K-dramas lovers : Visit the picturesque forest and see the place where scenes in Korean dramas have been filmed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Jeolla

  • Naejangsan National Park: With a number of different trails, visitors can explore the stunning autumn foliage through a variety of hikes.
  • Byeonsanbando National Park: Hike, bike, or stroll to enjoy breathtaking views of the sea, cliffs, and countryside.
  • Jogyesan Mountain: This hike offers a unique chance to explore a 1000-year-old temple and take in the stunning mountain scenery.
  • Geumsusan Mountain: This scenic hike offers views of unique rock formations, waterfalls, and beautiful forests.

Best Things to Do in North Jeolla: Activities and Tours

  • Make your own bibimbap at a traditional Jeonju restaurant. Not only will you enjoy a delicious meal, but you’ll also learn about Korean cuisine and culture.
  • Take a tour of the traditional hanok village in Jeonju and learn about the history of these homes.
  • Attend the Jeonju International Film Festival, which attracts movie lovers from around the world.
  • Visit the North Jeolla area in the spring and enjoy the Cherry Blossom festivals.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Jeolla

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to North Jeolla:

  • Day 1: Explore Jeonju Hanok Village
  • Day 2: Visit the Jeonju National Museum and Gyeonggijeon Shrine
  • Day 3: Hike in Wolchulsan National Park and visit Tapsa Temple
  • Day 4: Visit Gunsan Modern History Museum and explore the port city of Gunsan
  • Day 5: Trek in Naejangsan National Park and enjoy the local cuisine
  • Day 6: Bike or hike through Byeonsanbando National Park and relax at the beach
  • Day 7: Visit Baegyangsa Temple before departing

Food and Drink Scene of North Jeolla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jeonju Bibimbap: A signature dish from the city of Jeonju made with rice, vegetables and chili paste, all mixed together presented in a beautifully-shaped earthenware.
  • Kongnamul Gukbap: A comforting soup made from mung bean sprouts and rice.
  • Cheongpodo Halmeoni Gomtang: A rich beef bone soup with chewy beef slices and white puffy rice.
  • Makgeolli : A traditional Korean alcoholic beverage made from rice and yeast.

Local Beverages

  • Makgeolli: a traditional Korean rice wine with a unique and refreshing taste.
  • Sikhye: A sweet and refreshing rice beverage.
  • Beopju: A smooth, traditional Korean wine with a hint of citrus.

Where to Stay in North Jeolla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hanok-style guesthouses are available in Jeonju, offering traditional Korean accommodation and an immersive cultural experience.
  • Budget-conscious travellers can look for rooms at local guesthouses or homestays.
  • Modern hotels and luxury resorts are available throughout the province.

What to Buy in North Jeolla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hanji Paper: A type of handmade paper that is well-known for its durability and texture.
  • Hanbok: A traditional Korean dress, which can be found in a variety of colors and styles.
  • Roasted sesame oil or Yellow rice pickles: These local food specialities make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of North Jeolla

North Jeolla is steeped in Korean tradition and culture. Etiquette is important in South Korea, and it is important to be respectful of cultural norms when visiting. For example, removing shoes when entering homes or traditional establishments, such as temples or palaces.

Best Time to Visit North Jeolla

The best time to visit North Jeolla is during the fall months (from September to November), when the autumn foliage is at its peak. Spring (from March to May) is also a popular time to visit due to the beautiful cherry blossoms.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Jeolla

Do:

  • Dress respectfully when visiting temples or religious sites.
  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Korean homes, temples or establishments.
  • Respect the quiet and peaceful atmosphere at religious sites and historical places.

Don’t:

  • Bring up sensitive or controversial topics.
  • Smoke in non-smoking areas or throw away cigarette butts in public spaces.
  • Disrespect the national or cultural symbols of South Korea.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Jeolla

North Jeolla is generally a safe place for visitors. It is important to take normal precautions, such as securing your valuables and being aware of your surroundings. Visitors should avoid areas close to the North-South Korea border, as this area is heavily militarized.

Health in North Jeolla

South Korea has one of the best healthcare systems in the world, with modern medical facilities and highly trained doctors. There are hospitals and medical clinics throughout the province in case of emergency.

Getting to North Jeolla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Jeolla

  • Jeonju International Airport: Located in the city of Jeonju, it provides easy access to many of the region’s tourist areas.
  • Gunsan Airport: Located to the west and provides access to more remote attractions in the region.

Bus Stations Near North Jeolla

  • Jeonju Bus Terminal: The main intercity bus station with frequent connections to all major cities of Korea.
  • Gunsan Express Bus Terminal : Provide connections to surrounding provinces

Other Options

  • Train Services: There are regular train services from Seoul to Iksan and from there, you can hop on the local bus or train to reach the destinations in North Jeolla.
  • Car rental is also an option, as road conditions are good throughout the province.

How to Get Around in North Jeolla

North Jeolla is served by an extensive public transportation network. Buses and trains are the easiest options to explore the province. Taxis are also available in most tourist areas.

Where to Go Next from North Jeolla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jeolla Province is a gateway to the city of Busan, which is only a three-hour train ride away.
  • Travel north to Seoul or visit the city of Daejeon to see a different side of South Korea.

Conclusion

North Jeolla is a must-see destination for anyone visiting South Korea. With its rich history, stunning scenery, and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming province. By following this guide, you can explore all the wonders that North Jeolla has to offer.
