Introduction to Cheongdo County

Cheongdo County is a beautiful countryside area located in North Gyeongsang, South Korea. Known as the land of artists and culture, the county is famous for its traditional Korean crafts, cultural heritage, and natural resources. It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to explore rural Korea and experience traditional Korean culture.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best attractions, activities, food, and accommodations in Cheongdo County. Whether you are coming for a day trip or plan to stay longer, this guide will assist you in planning a memorable trip to this beautiful part of South Korea.

List of Places to Visit in Cheongdo County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cheongdo Bullfighting Arena: Bullfighting is a traditional sport in Korea, and Cheongdo County is one of the few places in the country where it is still practiced. The arena hosts numerous bullfighting tournaments throughout the year, attracting visitors from all over Asia.

  • Cheongdo Rail Bike: Visitors can enjoy beautiful sceneries while riding a rail bike on a disused railway track. It is a unique way to explore the beautiful countryside of Cheongdo County.

  • Mt. Palgongsan Natural Recreational Forest: Offering beautiful hiking trails, hot springs, and a beautiful waterfall, Mt. Palgongsan Natural Recreational Forest is a perfect destination for a family day trip.

  • Seongbo Museum: The Seongbo Museum is an archaeological museum that showcases the ancient history and cultural heritage of Korea. It is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

  • Mabijeong Mural Village: A vibrant mix of art and culture, Mabijeong Mural Village exhibit colorful murals, street art, and different exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Cheongdo County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cheongdo Wine Tunnel: Wine lovers cannot leave Cheongdo without visiting the Cheongdo Wine Tunnel. The 1.2 km tunnel houses a variety of wines and offers unique themes, such as the pyramids in the Egyptian wine model creating an ambience of a historical place.

  • Baegundae Peak: This is the highest peak in Cheongdo County, sitting 1,429 meters above sea level. Visitors can hike up to the top of the peak and enjoy Korea’s beautiful sceneries of blooming flowers and rivers.

  • Unmunsa Temple: Unmunsa Temple comprises beautiful architecture with waterfall and embracing nature. Visitors can participate in meditation classes and take a break from the busy city life to become one with nature.

  • Cheongdo Light Festival: The Cheongdo Light Festival is an annual event that takes place in winter. The festival offers beautiful illuminated projections, entertainment, and interactive activities for families and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cheongdo County

Cheongdo County has amazing outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking trails, that provide opportunities for engaging in physical activities and exploring the natural sceneries. Here are a few of the best hiking/biking trails in Cheongdo County:

  • Baekun Mountain Hike: A stunning natural panorama with locals and guests can enjoy an easy hike up to the summit of the Baekun Mountain.

  • Cheongdo Wine Marathon: A wine marathon is conducted in September every year in Cheongdo County. Participants have the opportunity to run a unique race while exploring the county’s scenic beauty.

  • Cheongdo Rail Bike: This activity offers a unique experience of riding a rail bike on a disused railway track. It is a perfect activity to explore the beautiful countryside of Cheongdo while exercising.

Best Things to Do in Cheongdo County: Activities and Tours

Cheongdo County has many activities and tours to offer for visitors. Here are some of the best activities and tour options that you can do during your visit.

  • Cheongdo Wine Tunnel Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour through the Cheongdo Wine Tunnel and learn about the history of Korean wine, winemaking, and the different types of wines available.

  • Cheongdo Bullfighting Tournaments: Visitors can experience the traditional sport of bullfighting in Cheongdo County. Tournaments are held throughout the year, but the most significant one is held during the Cheongdo Bullfighting Festival in March.

  • Cheongdo Light Festival: Visitors can experience the beauty of light installations and projections during the Cheongdo Light Festival, held annually in winter.

Food and Drink Scene of Cheongdo County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Dakgalbi: It is a Korean-style stir-fried chicken topped with melted cheese. A popular local dish that is mouthwatering.

  • Beef Bulgogi: Bulgogi is a famous Korean food, and Cheongdo County prepares it uniquely, rich with all the necessary ingredients and aromas.

  • Sunae Rice: Cheongdo Sunae Rice is famous for its chewy texture and unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Cheongdo Wine: Cheongdo wine is famous throughout Korea and made with fruits such as pear, persimmon, and grapes.

  • Imjong Traditional Punch: It is a traditional Korean punch made with ginger, cinnamon, jujube, and a Korean sweetener, honeyed Jeju hallabong.

Where to Stay in Cheongdo County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cheongdo Tourist Hotel offers comfortable rooms with great amenities, including free Wi-Fi and breakfast, and it is close to all the popular attractions in Cheongdo County.

  • Cheongdo Jukjeon Guest House is a budget-friendly option that offers excellent food and unique guestroom decor.

  • Lavender Pension offers well-furnished apartments with a view of the beautiful lavender garden on the property.

What to Buy in Cheongdo County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheongdo Wine: Cheongdo wine makes an excellent souvenir, and visitors can buy it at many local stores or from the Cheongdo Wine Tunnel gift shop.

  • Traditional Korean Handicrafts: Local markets in Cheongdo County sell a variety of traditional Korean handicrafts such as pottery, wooden dolls, and traditional Korean dress.

Local Culture and Customs of Cheongdo County

Cheongdo County residents are proud of their historical heritage and traditions. Bowing and respecting the elderly are an integral part of Korean culture, and visitors are advised to learn about local practices and norms.

Best Time to Visit Cheongdo County

Cheongdo County has four distinctive seasons. Spring and autumn (March to May and September to November) are the best times to visit as the weather is mild, and the county’s natural beauty is spectacular. Summers (June to August) can be hot and humid, while winters (December to February) can be very cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cheongdo County

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Carry cash as many rural areas do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Do not wear revealing clothing in cultural and religious places.
  • Do not disrespect Korean culture and traditions.
  • Smoking is prohibited in public areas such as cafes, restaurants, and parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cheongdo County

Cheongdo County is generally safe for visitors, and the crime rate is low. Visitors are advised to take basic precautions and safeguard their personal belongings, particularly in crowded areas such as markets and festivals.

Health in Cheongdo County

Cheongdo County has good health facilities, including hospitals and health clinics operated by the Korean government. The county is also COVID-19 safe, keeping all the precautionary measures at each attraction.

Getting to Cheongdo County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cheongdo County

The nearest international airport is Incheon International Airport, which is approximately 3 hours away by KTX (Korean train) from Cheongdo County.

Bus Stations Near Cheongdo County

Cheongdo Bus Terminal is in the central part of the county, and there are frequent buses from and to South Korean cities such as Seoul, Busan, Daegu, and Gyeongju.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train (KTX or Mugunghwa) to Goryeong Station or Cheongdo Station, which are easily accessible. Taxis and buses are available from both stations to different places in Cheongdo County.

How to Get Around in Cheongdo County

The best way to get around in Cheongdo County is by bus or taxi. The county’s bus system is extensive and covers most of the significant attractions. Taxis are also readily available and offer a convenient way to travel around without having to worry about navigating the county’s public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Cheongdo County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Daegu City: Daegu is the 4th largest city in South Korea and is famous for its traditional markets, historical sites, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Ulsan City: Ulsan is the industrial hub of Korea, located on the southeastern coast and is known for its beautiful coastline, hiking, and seafood.

Conclusion

Cheongdo County is a hidden gem in South Korea, with a mix of rural charm, culture, and natural beauty. It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience traditional Korean culture and explore the beautiful countryside. This comprehensive guide provides helpful information about the must-visit attractions, hiking/biking trails, food, accommodations, and transportation options in Cheongdo County, making it easy for visitors to plan their trip to this beautiful part of Korea.
