Introduction to Ulleungdo

Ulleungdo, a small island off the eastern coast of South Korea, is a hidden gem that boasts pristine natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique local customs. This volcanic island, often called the “Mystery Island,” offers a range of activities for adventure-seekers, nature lovers, and history enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Ulleungdo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dokdo Museum: Dedicated to the Dokdo Islets, the museum showcases the history of the island and its surrounding waters.
  • Haengnam Coastal Trail: A walking trail along the coast that features stunning views of the sea and cliffs.
  • Ulleung Folk Village: A traditional village featuring hanok buildings and cultural exhibits.
  • Jeodong Port: A historic port where visitors can learn about the island’s fishing industry and sea trade.

Natural Attractions of Ulleungdo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seonginbong Peak: The highest peak on Ulleungdo offers a challenging hike and panoramic views of the island.
  • Bongnae Falls: A picturesque waterfall surrounded by lush vegetation.
  • Nari Basin: A caldera formed by a volcanic eruption that features a beautiful lake and hiking trails.
  • Jeodong Forest Path: A scenic forest path that leads to Jeodong Port.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ulleungdo

  • Seonginbong Peak Trail: A challenging hiking trail that leads to the highest peak on Ulleungdo.
  • Dodong Coastal Trail: A coastal trail that passes through fishing villages and offers beautiful views of the sea.
  • Dongjin Forest Trail: A forest trail that leads to the Nari Basin and the Bongnae Falls.
  • Ulleung Island Cycling Course: A 60-km cycling course that circles the island and passes through scenic spots.

Best Things to Do in Ulleungdo: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the island’s dramatic coastlines and sea caves with a guided sea kayaking tour.
  • Jeodong Fish Market Tour: Join a local fisherman for a tour of the Jeodong fish market and experience the island’s seafood culture.
  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Discover the island’s underwater wonders with a guided snorkeling or scuba diving tour.
  • Cultural Experience: Try making traditional pottery, woodcrafts, or tea at a local cultural center.

Food and Drink Scene of Ulleungdo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gomchigi: A traditional dish made with fresh squid and vegetables, seasoned with chili pepper paste.
  • Jeotgal: A traditional Korean side dish made with fermented seafood, usually served with rice or drinks.
  • Ojingeo Sundae: A squid sausage served with a variety of spices, usually eaten as a snack.
  • Myeolchi Bokkeum: A spicy stir-fried anchovy dish, often enjoyed with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Makgeolli: A traditional Korean rice wine, usually served cold and sweet.
  • Soju: A popular distilled drink in South Korea, usually served with meals or as a cocktail mixer.
  • Hallasan Water: A premium bottled water from Ulleungdo, known for its high quality and mineral content.

Where to Stay in Ulleungdo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ulleung Marina Tourist Hotel: A luxury hotel located near the Jeodong Port, with stunning sea views and modern amenities.
  • Ulleung Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of the Dodong Village, offering comfortable rooms and local hospitality.
  • Jeodong Seaside Pension: A cozy pension located near the Jeodong Port, with easy access to local restaurants and attractions.

What to Buy in Ulleungdo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ulleungdo Island Black Garlic: A unique garlic variety grown on the island, known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
  • Ulleungdo Sea Salt: A premium sea salt harvested from the clean waters surrounding the island, often used in Korean cuisine.
  • Ulleungdo Tangerines: A sweet and juicy citrus fruit grown on the island, often used in desserts and drinks.
  • Ulleungdo Ginseng: A high-quality ginseng variety grown on the island, often used for its medicinal properties.

Local Culture and Customs of Ulleungdo

  • Dolhareubang: Stone statues of mythical creatures that are often featured in local traditional ceremonies and festivals.
  • Seolnal: The lunar new year celebration, usually celebrated with special food, drinks, and customs.
  • Dokdo Day: An annual festival celebrating the Dokdo Islets and their historical and cultural significance.

Best Time to Visit Ulleungdo

The best time to visit Ulleungdo is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and sunny. Summer is the busiest season, with more tourists and higher lodging rates. Fall is a great time to visit for fall foliage and seafood harvest festivals, while winter is the least crowded season and offers a chance to experience local winter sports and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ulleungdo

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Learn about local customs and manners, and abide by them during your visit.
  • Try local cuisine: Sample local dishes and drinks, and support local restaurants and cafes.
  • Conserve natural resources: Follow the rules for responsible tourism, and help protect the island’s natural landscapes and resources.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources: Do not leave trash or any damage in natural areas, and respect protected habitats.
  • Disrespect cultural heritage: Do not damage or disturb cultural heritage sites or artifacts, and follow guidelines for respectful behavior.
  • Ignore safety guidelines: Follow safety guidelines for outdoor activities and transportation, and wear appropriate gear and clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ulleungdo

Ulleungdo is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should take precautions for outdoor activities, such as hiking or sea kayaking, and follow safety guidelines for transportation.

Health in Ulleungdo

Ulleungdo has a modern healthcare system and good medical services but use travel insurance for peace of mind while traveling. Bring necessary medications and mosquito repellent.

Getting to Ulleungdo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ulleungdo

The nearest airports to Ulleungdo are:

  • Pohang Airport: A domestic airport served by several airlines, including Asiana Airlines and Korean Air.
  • Ulsan Airport: A domestic airport served by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Ulleungdo

There are bus stations in major cities close to Ulleungdo, including:

  • Pohang Intercity Bus Terminal: A bus terminal located in central Pohang, offering regular bus services to Ulleungdo.
  • Ulsan Intercity Bus Terminal: A bus terminal located in central Ulsan, offering regular bus services to Pohang.

Other Options

  • Ferry Services: Daily ferry services are available from Pohang to Ulleungdo, with a travel time of around 3 hours.

How to Get Around in Ulleungdo

The most convenient and efficient way to get around Ulleungdo is by rental car or scooter, available at the main port and local rental shops. There are also regular bus services to major tourist sites and villages, and taxi services in urban areas.

Where to Go Next from Ulleungdo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dokdo Island: The Dokdo Islets, located in the East Sea, are a pair of islets under South Korean control, often visited by boat tours from Ulleungdo.
  • Gyeongju: A nearby city famous for its UNESCO-listed historical sites and cultural heritage, accessible by bus or car.

Conclusion

Ulleungdo, a hidden gem in South Korea, offers a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and local hospitality that is sure to fascinate visitors from all over the world. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experience, or just a relaxing getaway, Ulleungdo has something for you.
