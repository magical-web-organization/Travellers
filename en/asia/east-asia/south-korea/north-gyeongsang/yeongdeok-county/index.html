A Comprehensive Guide to Yeongdeok County, South Korea

Yeongdeok County is located in North Gyeongsang Province, East Asia, South Korea. It boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique local customs that draw visitors from around the world. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Yeongdeok County so that you can explore all the highlights and experience the best that this region has to offer.

Introduction to Yeongdeok County

Yeongdeok County is known for its seafood culture, with crab and squid being the most commonly featured ingredients in local cuisine. The county has a deep history and played an active role in many historical events. Yeongdeok is also known for its natural beauty, with stunning coastlines, mountains, and countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Yeongdeok County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pyeongyang Myiki Memorial Hall: This museum showcases the life and works of Pyeongyang Myiki, a renowned Korean poet and prose writer. The museum has a vast collection of artworks, photographs, and manuscripts.
  • Yeongdeok Traditional Craft Museum: This museum displays various crafts and artisanal products from the county, including embroidery, pottery, and calligraphy.

Natural Attractions of Yeongdeok County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sangju Beach: This beach is famous for its crystal clear waters, golden sand, and coral reefs. Visitors can enjoy swimming, snorkeling or diving in the water.
  • Bongsudae Rock: It is a famous cliff in Yeongdeok County where visitors can enjoy scenic views of the East Sea.
  • Jeongja Beach: A white sandy beach with mesmerizing views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yeongdeok County

  • Jeolmul Natural Forest Trail: This peaceful trail runs across the one of the Yeongdeok county’s largest forests, Jeolmul. Visitors can feel serene and closely interact with nature.
  • Sobaeksan Mountain National Park Hiking Trail: This is one of the most popular hiking routes in the county, the trail stretches over 20 kilometers and offers some of the most impressive views of the county.

Best Things to Do in Yeongdeok County: Activities and Tours

  • Seafood Tour: Visitors can take an immersive tour of the county’s seafood culture, including tastings of local crab, squid, and other seafood specialties.
  • Water Sports: Yeongdeok County is known for its numerous water activities, including swimming, scuba diving, kayaking, fishing, and surfing.

Food and Drink Scene of Yeongdeok County

Must-Try Dishes

  • King Crab: Yeongdeok is renowned for its King Crab, and visitors should try the dish in various forms, including steamed, grilled, or baked.
  • Squid: The county’s most consumed seafood, and visitors can try squid in different cuisines and styles, including fried squid with spicy sauce.
  • Pine Mushroom: This is a must for those who love vegetarian cuisine, known for its characteristic cinnamon scent and fine taste.

Local Beverages

  • Nokcha (Green Tea): Visitors can enjoy fresh, locally produced green tea that is known for its high-quality taste and aroma.
  • Makgeolli (Rice Wine): A traditional Korean wine that is widely consumed in Yeongdeok county with a unique and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Yeongdeok County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yeongdeok County offers a range of accommodations, including budget guesthouses, mid-range hotels, and high-end resorts. Recommended places to stay include:

  • Sangju Abbey Resort: A luxury resort located in the heart of Sangju Beach.
  • Sleeping Green Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guest house located near Jeongja Beach.
  • Peaceful Pension: A mid-range hotel that offers a peaceful and tranquil stay for visitors.

What to Buy in Yeongdeok County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Embroidery: Yeongdeok has a rich embroidery craft culture, and visitors can buy unique traditional embroidery from local markets and souvenir shops.
  • Pine Mushrooms: Visitors can buy packets of fresh or dried pine mushrooms, which are grown locally and renowned for their aromatic flavor.
  • Traditional Korean pottery: Yeongdeok County has a long and rich history of pottery, and visitors can buy traditional Korean pottery from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Yeongdeok County

Yeongdeok County has a rich culture and unique customs. Visitors are highly recommended to participate in the following customary activities:

  • Seafood Harvesting: Yeongdeok county offers local tours and activities that allow visitors to experience harvesting seafood directly from the sea.
  • Pottery Making: Visitors can learn about traditional Korean pottery and try their hand on a pottery class tour at one of the local pottery workshops.

Best Time to Visit Yeongdeok County

Yeongdeok County has a moderate climate with four distinct seasons. The best time to visit Yeongdeok County is during the spring and autumn seasons (March - May; September - November), where the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy the county’s outdoor activities and natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yeongdeok County

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Keep the environment clean and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside temple halls.
  • Communicate offensive slang.
  • Show public affection, as it may be considered inappropriate.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yeongdeok County

Yeongdeok county is a safe place to visit. However, visitors are advised to take the usual safety precautions, such as safeguarding valuables and not carrying excessive cash.

Health in Yeongdeok County

The county has a high-quality healthcare system, and there are multiple hospitals and medical facilities. Visitors are recommended to have travel healthcare insurance and have their vaccinations updated.

Getting to Yeongdeok County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yeongdeok County

  • Gimhae International Airport: The nearest major airport to Yeongdeok county. The airport is approximately 90 km from the county.

Bus Stations Near Yeongdeok County

  • Seoul Express Terminal: The terminal has regular buses to Yeongdeok County from Seoul and other major cities.
  • Dong Seoul Bus Terminal: It connects Yeongdeok County to different cities within South Korea.

Other Options

  • Travelers can also get to Yeongdeok County through car rentals and private taxis.

How to Get Around in Yeongdeok County

Public transportation is readily available in Yeongdeok county, including buses, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Yeongdeok County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gyeongju: Located only a short drive away, it is known for its rich history and ancient Silla Kingdom architecture.
  • Busan: The second-largest metropolitan city of South Korea, known for unique architecture, vibrant nightlife, and its famous Haeundae beach.

Conclusion

Yeongdeok County is a treasure trove of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique local customs. This comprehensive guide helps visitors plan and enjoy an unforgettable trip to the county. From delicious local food and drink to breathtaking scenery and immersive cultural activities, Yeongdeok County has something for everyone.
