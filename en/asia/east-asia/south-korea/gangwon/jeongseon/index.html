Introduction to Jeongseon

Jeongseon is a beautiful county located in Gangwon province in South Korea. Known for its unspoiled natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse outdoor activities, Jeongseon is quickly becoming a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists.

Nestled in the heart of the Taebaek Mountains, Jeongseon offers breathtaking scenery, ranging from majestic peaks to pristine rivers and forests. Visitors can explore ancient temples, museums, and traditional Korean villages, or enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and fishing. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Jeongseon is the perfect destination for your next vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Jeongseon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jeongseon Arirang Museum - Located in the heart of Jeongseon-gun, this museum is dedicated to the Arirang folk song, which is recognized as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. Visitors can learn about the history and significance of the song and enjoy live performances.
  • Sogeumsan Mountain Fortress - This ancient fortress, which dates back to the 14th century, offers a glimpse into Korea’s military and cultural history. Visitors can explore the ruins and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Gangwon Provincial Museum - This modern museum features exhibits on the history, culture, and natural environment of Gangwon province. Highlights include the Goguryeo pottery collection and the dinosaur exhibition.

Natural Attractions of Jeongseon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jeongseon Rail Bike - This unique attraction allows visitors to pedal along an old railway track while enjoying stunning views of the mountains and rivers. A perfect daytime activity for families and groups of friends.
  • Taebaek National Park - With its lush forests, waterfalls, and breathtaking peaks, Taebaek National Park offers a wealth of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and fishing. Visitors can explore the park’s pristine natural beauty and immerse themselves in Korea’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Jeongseon Aurora Valley - This picturesque valley offers visitors the chance to see the Northern Lights, a rare natural phenomenon that occurs in this part of Korea. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, including camping, fishing, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jeongseon

For hiking enthusiasts, Jeongseon has a range of scenic trails to explore, including:

  • Geumgangsan Mountain - At over 1,500 meters high, this mountain offers a challenging but rewarding hike, with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, including the nearby Pyeongchang Olympic Village.
  • Jeongseon Arirang Trail - This 7 km long trail takes visitors along the scenic valleys of Jeongseon and is an excellent option for a scenic hike. The trail is also popular with mountain bikers, who can rent bikes at the starting point.

Best Things to Do in Jeongseon: Activities and Tours

  • Jeongseon Sky Walk - This unique attraction allows visitors to walk along a glass bridge that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. A perfect activity for adrenaline junkies and photography enthusiasts.
  • Jeongseon Paragliding - Visitors can experience the thrill of paragliding over Jeongseon’s beautiful landscapes with experienced guides.
  • Jeongseon Jazz Festival - Held annually in August, the Jeongseon Jazz Festival is a must-attend event for music lovers. Visitors can enjoy live performances by local and international artists and sample local food and drinks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jeongseon

Day 1: Arrival in Jeongseon; check-in at hotel; visit Jeongseon Arirang Museum and Sogeumsan Mountain Fortress.

Day 2: Hike the Jeongseon Arirang Trail; visit Jeongseon Rail Bike.

Day 3: Visit Gangwon Provincial Museum; explore the nearby Yulgok Historical Site.

Day 4: Go skiing or snowboarding at High1 Ski Resort in winter or hiking in Taebaek National Park in summer.

Day 5: Visit Jeongseon Aurora Valley; try fishing, camping, or hiking.

Day 6: Take the Jeongseon Sky Walk; try paragliding.

Day 7: Attend the Jeongseon Jazz Festival; visit local craft markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Jeongseon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jeongseon Arirang Makguksu (Buckwheat Noodles) - A local specialty made with buckwheat noodles, spicy red pepper sauce, and fresh vegetables.
  • Deodeok Gui (Grilled Deodeok) - Deodeok is a type of Korean mountain herb that is grilled and served with soy sauce, garlic, and sesame oil.
  • Heotjesabap (Soybean Rice) - A nutritious and delicious dish made with soybean paste, rice, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Makgeolli - A traditional Korean rice wine that is mildly sweet and slightly carbonated.
  • Soju - A clear distilled liquor made from rice, wheat, or barley that is typically consumed with Korean barbeque.

Where to Stay in Jeongseon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jeongseon offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some recommended options include:

  • High1 Resort - A large, modern resort offering skiing, golf, and a range of other activities. Ideal for families and couples.
  • Jeongseon Prince Hotel - A stylish, boutique hotel located in the heart of the city. Perfect for couples and solo travelers.
  • Jeongseon Youth Hostel - A budget-friendly option for backpackers and groups of friends.

What to Buy in Jeongseon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jeongseon Arirang Embroidery - The traditional embroidery of Jeongseon is an intricate and beautiful handicraft that makes for a unique souvenir.
  • Pyeongchang Trout - The cold waters of Jeongseon are home to some of the tastiest and freshest trout in Korea. Visitors can buy fresh or smoked trout to take home.
  • Korean Ginseng - Jeongseon is famous for its high-quality Korean ginseng, which is believed to have a range of health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Jeongseon

Jeongseon has a rich cultural heritage, rooted in ancient Korean traditions and beliefs. Visitors should take care to respect local customs and beliefs, including bowing to elders, removing shoes before entering homes, and avoiding displays of public affection.

Best Time to Visit Jeongseon

Jeongseon can be visited year-round, but the best time to go depends on your interests and preferences. Spring and fall offer mild temperatures and stunning landscapes, while summer is ideal for outdoor activities and festivals. Winter is the best season for skiing and snowboarding, but temperatures can be very cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jeongseon

Do:

  • Respect local customs and beliefs.
  • Be mindful of the environment and take care not to damage natural habitats.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive.
  • Disrespect elders or local traditions.
  • Litter or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jeongseon

Jeongseon is generally a safe destination, with low levels of crime and a friendly local population. Visitors should still take standard precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and not leaving belongings unattended.

Health in Jeongseon

Jeongseon has high-quality healthcare facilities and services, including hospitals and clinics. Most medical professionals speak English, and many accept international insurance policies.

Getting to Jeongseon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jeongseon

The closest airport to Jeongseon is Yangyang International Airport, which is located about 60 km away. Other nearby airports include Incheon International Airport and Gimpo International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Jeongseon

Jeongseon has a bus terminal that connects with major cities in Korea, including Seoul and Busan. The terminal is located in the center of town and offers frequent services throughout the day.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Jeongseon from nearby cities. Trains are also available, and the nearest station is on the Gangneung Line.

How to Get Around in Jeongseon

Jeongseon is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot. Taxis are also available and are a convenient option for longer distances. Buses operate throughout the city and surrounding areas, and fares are very affordable.

Where to Go Next from Jeongseon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pyeongchang - Site of the 2018 Winter Olympics, Pyeongchang is a beautiful city known for its skiing, snowboarding, and hot springs.
  • Sokcho - Located on the east coast of Korea, Sokcho is a popular destination for beach lovers, hikers, and seafood lovers.
  • Gangneung - This historic city is known for its rich culture and architecture, as well as its beautiful beaches, lakes, and mountains.

Conclusion

Jeongseon is a hidden gem of Korea, full of natural beauty, history, and culture. With its range of outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, and local cuisine, it’s the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique, off-the-beaten-path adventure. Whether you’re a history buff, an adrenaline junkie, or just looking for some peace and quiet, Jeongseon has something for everyone.
