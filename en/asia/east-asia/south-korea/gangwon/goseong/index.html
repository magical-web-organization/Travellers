Introduction to Goseong

Goseong is a picturesque county in the eastern part of Gangwon Province, South Korea. Bordered by the East Sea to the east and North Korea to the north, it offers a unique blend of scenic beauty, cultural attractions and outdoor adventures. The county is particularly known for its stunning coastline, rich history and breathtaking mountain ranges. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, activities to do, cultural customs, accommodation options, food and drink scene, and much more in Goseong.

List of Places to Visit in Goseong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Goseong Unification Observatory: Located near the DMZ, the observatory offers panoramic views of the North Korean town of Gaeseong and its surrounding areas. You can also learn about the history of the Korean War and the ongoing conflict between North and South Korea.

  • Goseong Folk Village: This traditional Korean village offers a glimpse into the country’s rural past. You can see various types of thatched-roof houses, agricultural tools and everyday items from the Joseon Dynasty era.

  • Goseong Castle: The castle dates back to the early Joseon Dynasty and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can explore the castle walls, watchtowers and gates, and learn about its role in Korean history.

Natural Attractions of Goseong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Simpson Harbor: Simpson Harbor is a beautiful natural harbor located on the east coast of Goseong. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, rocky outcroppings, and beautiful vistas of the East Sea.

  • Geobok Mountain: This mountain has several hiking trails that offer a variety of challenges, from easy walks to more strenuous climbs. The summit offers unparalleled views of the surrounding valleys and sea.

  • Gangguhang Port: This beautiful port is a popular spot for fishing and boating. Visitors can enjoy fresh seafood at local restaurants and snack vendors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goseong

Goseong is a hiker and biker’s paradise. The following are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Goseong:

  • Geobok Mountain Trail: This beautiful trail takes you through lush green forests, past waterfalls, and up to the summit of Geobok Mountain.

  • Goseong Coastal Trail: This 6-kilometer-long trail takes you along the stunning coastline of Goseong. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the East Sea, rocky cliffs, and scenic fishing villages.

  • Seokdaeseom Island Trail: This small island is located just off the coast of Goseong and can only be reached by boat. The trail takes you through a beautiful forest of pine trees and offers spectacular views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Goseong: Activities and Tours

  • DMZ Tour: The Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) is a buffer zone that separates North and South Korea. Goseong is one of the closest cities to the DMZ and offers various tours and experiences.

  • Fishing Tour: Goseong is known for its fresh seafood, and visitors can enjoy fishing tours where they catch their own fish and have it cooked for them.

  • Beach Day: Goseong has some of the most beautiful beaches in South Korea. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Goseong

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goseong Crab: Goseong is famous for its fresh crab. There are many restaurants that offer this delicacy, which is usually served steamed or grilled.

  • Squid and Octopus: Goseong’s coastal location means that there is an abundance of squid and octopus. Try it raw or grilled.

  • Makgeolli: Makgeolli is a traditional Korean rice wine. There are many local breweries in Goseong that produce fresh and delicious makgeolli.

Local Beverages

  • Omija Water: Omija is a type of berry that is grown in Goseong. Omija water is a refreshing and healthy drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

  • Hwachae: Hwachae is a traditional Korean fruit punch. In Goseong, it is usually made with locally grown fruits such as strawberries and raspberries.

Where to Stay in Goseong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Goseong Coastal Hotel: This hotel is located near the beach and offers stunning views of the East Sea. The rooms are comfortable and clean, and the staff is friendly and welcoming.

  • Goseong Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the heart of Goseong and offers a traditional Korean experience. The rooms are cozy and clean, and the breakfast is amazing.

What to Buy in Goseong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Omija Products: Omija is a local berry that is used to make tea, snacks, and other products. Visitors can buy omija products such as tea, syrup, and candies.

  • Goseong Crab: Take home some fresh Goseong crab to impress your foodie friends or family.

  • Hanji Products: Hanji is a traditional Korean paper that is handmade from mulberry trees. Visitors can buy hanji products such as notebooks, fans, and lamps.

Local Culture and Customs of Goseong

  • Respectful behaviour: South Koreans are very polite and respectful, so try to bow when you greet or thank them.

  • Etiquette at meals: Don’t start eating until the oldest person at the table has started, and don’t pour your own drink. Instead, let someone else pour for you and return the favour.

Best Time to Visit Goseong

The best time to visit Goseong is from April to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. In the summer, the beaches are ideal for swimming and water sports. In the fall, the forests are ablaze with colour, making it a perfect time to go hiking or biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goseong

Do:

  • Try the fresh seafood.
  • Go hiking or biking in the mountains or on the coastal trails.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home.
  • Take photographs of military installations or soldiers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goseong

Goseong is a relatively safe place to visit. However, visitors should be aware of the risks associated with hiking and water sports. It’s also important to be aware of the ongoing conflict between North and South Korea and avoid taking photographs of military installations or soldiers.

Health in Goseong

There are no major health concerns in Goseong. However, visitors should stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and take precautions against mosquito bites in the summer months.

Getting to Goseong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Goseong

  • Yangyang International Airport: This airport is the closest to Goseong and offers flights from Seoul and other major Korean cities.

  • Incheon International Airport: This airport is located near Seoul and offers international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Goseong

  • Goseong Bus Terminal: Located in the center of Goseong, this bus terminal offers frequent connections to Seoul and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Goseong from Seoul or other nearby cities.

  • Train: Visitors can take a train to Gangneung Station and then take a bus or taxi to Goseong.

How to Get Around in Goseong

The best way to get around Goseong is by car or taxi. However, visitors can also take buses to get to different attractions.

Where to Go Next from Goseong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sokcho: Sokcho is a popular tourist destination located to the north of Goseong. It’s known for its beautiful beaches, national parks, and fresh seafood.

  • Seoraksan National Park: This national park is located near Sokcho and offers some of the most stunning landscapes in Korea, including dramatic cliffs, waterfalls, and mountain peaks.

Conclusion

Goseong is a hidden gem in South Korea that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to hike in the mountains, relax on the beach, or learn about Korean history and culture, Goseong has something for everyone. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore this beautiful county.
