Introduction to Seoraksan National Park

Located in the northernmost region of Gangwon Province, Seoraksan National Park is one of the most spectacular natural wonders of South Korea, attracting millions of visitors every year. Seoraksan, which means “snowy ridges mountain” in Korean, features towering peaks, crystal-clear streams, dense forests, and breathtaking waterfalls. The park covers an area of 163.6 square kilometers, and it was designated as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 1982. Whether you’re an avid hiker or a nature lover, Seoraksan National Park has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Seoraksan National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sinheungsa Temple: This ancient Buddhist temple was built in the 7th century and is one of the most famous temples in South Korea. The temple houses several important cultural relics, including a bronze Buddha statue and a wooden pagoda.
  • Gwongeumseong Fortress: This historical fortress was built during the Goguryeo period and is located on the top of a steep cliff. The fortress offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Sokcho Traditional Market: This bustling market is a great place to experience the local culture and cuisine of Sokcho. The market features a wide variety of traditional Korean foods, including seafood, kimchi, and street food.

Natural Attractions of Seoraksan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seorak Cable Car: This cable car is one of the most popular attractions in Seoraksan National Park and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The cable car takes visitors to Gwongeumseong Fortress and is a great way to experience the park’s natural beauty.
  • Biseondae Rock: This rock formation is located along the Ulsanbawi Trail and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail to Biseondae Rock is one of the most popular hiking routes in Seoraksan National Park.
  • Cheonbuldong Valley: This pristine valley features crystal-clear streams, towering peaks, and dense forests. The valley is a great place for hiking, fishing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seoraksan National Park

Seoraksan National Park is a hiker’s paradise, with over 30 hiking trails to explore. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced hiker, Seoraksan National Park has something to offer for everyone. Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Ulsanbawi Rock Course: This challenging hiking trail takes visitors to the summit of Ulsanbawi Rock, one of the most iconic landmarks in Seoraksan National Park. The trail is steep and requires a good level of fitness, but the views from the top are worth the effort.
  • Biseondae Course: This moderate hiking trail takes visitors to Biseondae Rock, a natural rock formation located along the Ulsanbawi Trail. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Sokcho Coastal Bike Path: This scenic bike path follows the coastline of Sokcho and offers stunning views of the East Sea. The bike path is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Sokcho and is suitable for all ages and skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Seoraksan National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Trekking tours: Trekking tours are a great way to explore the natural beauty of Seoraksan National Park. The tours are led by experienced guides and cover some of the most popular hiking trails in the park.
  • ATV tours: ATV tours are a great way to explore the rugged terrain of Seoraksan National Park. The tours are suitable for all ages and skill levels.
  • Temple-stay program: The temple-stay program at Sinheungsa Temple offers visitors a unique cultural experience. The program includes meditation, temple tours, and traditional Korean meals.

Food and Drink Scene of Seoraksan National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spicy Seafood Noodle Soup: This spicy noodle soup is a popular dish in Sokcho and features fresh seafood and a spicy broth.
  • Grilled Fish: Grilled fish is a staple dish in Sokcho and can be found at many local restaurants and cafes.
  • Makguksu: Makguksu is a traditional Korean wheat noodle dish that is commonly found in Sokcho.

Local Beverages

  • Soju: Soju is a popular Korean alcoholic beverage that is made from rice, wheat, or barley. It is usually consumed with a meal or as a social drink.
  • Makgeolli: Makgeolli is a traditional Korean rice wine that is often served with Korean barbecue or spicy food. It has a slightly sweet taste and a low alcohol content.

  • Goodstay Smile Resort and Guesthouse: This budget-friendly guesthouse features comfortable rooms and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Seoraksan National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Korean tea: Korean tea is a popular beverage in South Korea, and Seoraksan National Park is home to many tea plantations and shops that sell a wide variety of teas.

Local Culture and Customs of Seoraksan National Park

South Korea is a country with rich cultural traditions and customs, and Seoraksan National Park is no exception. Some of the most important cultural customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Bowing: Bowing is a common greeting in South Korea and is a sign of respect and politeness.
  • Removing shoes: It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a Korean home or place of business.
  • Korean manners: Korean manners are important to observe when visiting Seoraksan National Park. These include speaking quietly, using chopsticks properly, and refraining from public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Seoraksan National Park

The best time to visit Seoraksan National Park is from September to November, when the fall foliage is at its peak. The weather is mild and pleasant during this time, and the crowds are smaller than during the summer months. Spring (April to June) is also a great time to visit, when the cherry blossoms are in bloom and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seoraksan National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow the designated hiking trails to avoid damaging the natural vegetation.
  • Carry out any trash and litter to keep the park clean.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural plants and animals.
  • Smoke or light fires in the park.
  • Enter restricted areas or areas that are closed for conservation purposes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Seoraksan National Park

Seoraksan National Park is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is important to be aware of potential hazards, such as:

  • Slippery trails: The trails can be slippery, especially during rainy or snowy weather. Wear appropriate footwear and use caution on steep or uneven terrain.
  • Wildlife encounters: The park is home to many wild animals, including bears and mountain goats. Always keep a safe distance and do not approach or feed the animals.
  • Weather conditions: The weather can change quickly in the park, and it is important to be prepared for cold or wet weather. Bring appropriate clothing and gear for the conditions.

Health in Seoraksan National Park

Seoraksan National Park is located in a mountainous area, and visitors should be prepared for the physical demands of hiking and outdoor activities. It is important to bring appropriate clothing, footwear, and gear for the conditions, as well as any necessary medications or first aid supplies.

Getting to Seoraksan National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Seoraksan National Park

The closest airport to Seoraksan National Park is Yangyang International Airport, located about 30 minutes away by car. Other nearby airports include Incheon International Airport and Gimpo International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Seoraksan National Park

There are several bus stations near Seoraksan National Park, including Sokcho Intercity Bus Terminal and Gangneung Intercity Bus Terminal. Buses run regularly from major cities in South Korea, including Seoul.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Seoraksan National Park is Sokcho Station. Trains run regularly from Seoul and other major cities in South Korea.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available from Sokcho and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Seoraksan National Park

The best way to get around Seoraksan National Park is by hiking or biking. There are also shuttle buses and taxis available for visitors who prefer not to hike or bike.

Where to Go Next from Seoraksan National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
