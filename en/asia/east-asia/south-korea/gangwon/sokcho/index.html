Introduction to Sokcho

Located in Gangwon province, Sokcho is a small and charming city in South Korea that attracts visitors with its stunning natural beauty, dramatic mountain landscapes, and abundance of outdoor activities. Its coastal location and mountainous terrain offer a unique blend of opportunities for adventure seekers and those who want to take a relaxing break.

Whether you’re looking for some time to unwind, explore South Korea’s rich cultural heritage, or live an exciting experience, this charming little city is sure to exceed your expectations.

List of Places to Visit in Sokcho

Sokcho has a range of attractions and sites that can cater to everyone’s interests. Here’s the list of places to visit while you’re in Sokcho:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seorak Cultural Center: An arts and culture museum that showcases the history and culture of Sokcho in diverse artwork with exhibitions and events held regularly.
  • Sokcho Traditional Market: An open-air marketplace that features fresh seafood and delicious street foods that represents the local taste of Sokcho.
  • Abai Village: A small community near the sea built by a group of North Korean refugees who escaped during the Korean War.
  • Sokcho Trout Museum: An indoor-outdoor modern museum that educates visitors about trout and fishing history in Sokcho.

Natural Attractions of Sokcho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seoraksan National Park: Sokcho’s biggest attraction, the tallest mountain in Korea offers fantastic hiking trails and breathtaking views.
  • Naksan Beach: A popular beach destination, it is well known for its crystal-clear water, white sand, and panoramic views of the coast.
  • Seorak Cable Car: Take a ride with view of scenery providing a panoramic view of the ocean and Mt. Seorak.
  • Biryong Falls: A beautiful waterfall with picturesque scenery, a must-visit spot for nature lovers.
  • Silkrodaebong Peak: Located at Seoraksan National Park, a popular mountain summit destination for tourists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sokcho

Being a mountainous area, Sokcho offers exhilarating hiking and mountain biking paths for adventure lovers. Here’s a list of Sokcho’s best hiking and biking routes:

  • Ulsanbawi Rock - Known for its unique rock formations, and winding trails that offer a panoramic view of the sea and mountains.
  • Osaek Mineral Spring - A popular hiking trail that provides views of the forest and Mount Seorak
  • Beombawi Rock - A challenging hike but with breathtaking scenery, it offers views of the beach and surrounding cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Sokcho: Activities and Tours

Sokcho offers a diverse range of activities catering to different tastes and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do while in Sokcho:

  • Fishing Trips: Sokcho is known for its fishing industry, so visitors can enjoy a fishing trip or even visi Sokcho’s fish market directly.
  • Korean Traditional Culture Experience Learn how to make traditional Korean dishes or pottery. Visitors can experience interactive traditional Korean workshops.
  • Seafood Tours Visit the fish markets and indulge in a delicious seafood lunch with some traditional side dishes.
  • Temples Visits Historically significant in Buddhism, there are several temples open to visitors who want to experience a bit of the local culture first-hand.
  • Chill Out on Naksan Beach During winter, the beach is recognized to be the perfect spot to take a stroll along and catch some sun while lounging.

Food and Drink Scene of Sokcho

South Korean cuisine is both varied and flavorsome, and Sokcho has an impressive selection of dishes that can satisfy your taste buds. Here are the must-try foods and beverages in Sokcho:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Fish: As Sokcho is a coastal fishing destination, it offers some of the best grilled fish that will melt in your mouth.
  • Ojingeo: Popular Korean seafood, squid, is served in many styles from spicy stir-fries, to soups or sliced raw.
  • Icy Naengmyeon: the famous Korean cold noodle soup made with buckwheat or sweet potato starch.

Local Beverages

  • Makgeolli: A sweet and sour traditional Korean rice wine with relatively low alcohol content.
  • Soju: A distilled beverage that has a neutral flavor and higher alcohol content.
  • Yuja tea: A hot Korean beverage, that is made with yuja marmalade or citrus its fresh taste giving you a refreshing feeling.

Where to Stay in Sokcho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Sokcho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sokcho is known for handicrafts made from natural materials, such as wood and stone, as well some exciting Korean variations that make great souvenirs. Make sure to pick up some of these products when you visit:

  • Traditional knives and cutlery
  • Gemstones and accessories
  • Pottery and ceramics
  • Traditional instruments

Local Culture and Customs of Sokcho

South Korea has a unique lifestyle and culture that has been influenced by its deep traditions, beliefs and a mix of both Buddhist and Christian cultures. Here are some of the customs you should know before heading to Sokcho:

  • Bowing: A common way of greeting someone, respect is shown to the elders by bowing;
  • Shoes Off: South Korea follows an ancient tradition of removing shoes before entering a house;
  • Use Both Hands: Two hands are used for most gestures, including exchange of currency in markets;
  • Socializing over Food: The tradition of sharing meals is important in Korean culture.

Best Time to Visit Sokcho

South Korea has a temperate climate, but Sokcho’s temperature differs with changes in seasons. The best time to visit is between April to October. It is a great time to enjoy outdoor activities and take in Sokcho’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sokcho

Here are some general do’s and don’ts you should follow during your Sokcho visit:

Do:

  • Cover your tattoos - Tattoos can be considered taboo
  • Carry Cash - Most small/local markets only accept cash
  • Learn Respectful Language – Basic Korean language skills can come in handy when socializing with locals.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a home

Don’t:

  • Sip Alcohol Before Elderly People Start Drinking - Drinking in South Korea shows appreciation and respects.
  • Use Chopsticks as a Poking Tool - It is considered impolite and rude to use chopsticks in a non appropriate way.
  • Don’t Forget to Bow: A sign of appreciation, Bowing shows manners towards elders and shows due respect.

Safety and Health

Sokcho is a safe city with a low crime rate. Although it’s advisable to follow the general precautions when visiting. Here is essential health and safety information:

Safety in Sokcho

  • Keep Sounds Low after Dark - As a sign of respect for others, it is advised to refrain from loud conversations after dark.
  • Wear Appropriate Clothing on Hikes - For your safety, do not wear hiking gear that can reveal your skin during hikes.
  • COVID-19 Rules - Keep your mask on, sanitize your hands regularly, and follow general guidelines when outside.

Health in Sokcho

  • Carry Sunscreen and Mosquito Repellant - Sunburn and mosquito bites can become a severe issue in summer, carry some repellant and sunscreen when outside.
  • Take Sufficient rest - Fatigue and exhaustion during a visit can occur, try to give yourself time to rest between hikes and other physically draining activities.

Getting to Sokcho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sokcho is just a day away from Seoul, offering visitors many options. Here’s all you need to know about getting there:

Airports Near Sokcho

  • Yangyang Airport (YNY) - Located around 30 minutes from Sokcho provides some international airlines like Eastar Jet and China Eastern Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Sokcho

  • Seoul Express Bus Terminal: There are regular direct buses to Sokcho with the journey taking around three hours.
  • Dong Seoul Bus Terminal: Offers regular buses that take roughly two and a half hours to Sokcho.
  • Incheon Airport Bus Terminal: It provides direct buses to Sokcho with an entire distance taking four hours roughly.

Other Options

  • KTX train - While there’s no direct rail link from Seoul to Sokcho, visitors can use the express train from Seoul to Gangneung, and from there, take a local bus or taxi to Sokcho.

How to Get Around in Sokcho

A bus, taxi or car are the easiest ways to get around while touring Sokcho. Buses and taxis are easily available in the city, while renting a car requires an international driver’s license.
