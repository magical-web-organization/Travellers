Introduction to Yangyang

Nestled in the mountains of Gangwon, South Korea, Yangyang is a hidden gem for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path destination. Known for its stunning natural beauty, numerous hiking trails, and cultural landmarks, Yangyang offers a unique and authentic Korean experience for visitors.

This comprehensive guide provides everything you need to plan the perfect trip to Yangyang, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, must-try dishes, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Yangyang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Naksansa Temple: Built over 1,300 years ago, Naksansa Temple offers stunning views of the East Sea and is home to numerous cultural landmarks such as the Amita Buddha statue and the Bell of Unification.

  2. Osan-ri Prehistoric Site: This archaeological site dates back to the late Neolithic period and offers a glimpse into Korea’s prehistoric era.

  3. Yangyang Folk Museum: This museum showcases traditional Korean culture and artifacts, from pottery to clothing.

Natural Attractions of Yangyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Seoraksan National Park: A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, Seoraksan National Park boasts stunning mountain views, waterfalls, and hiking trails for all skill levels.

  2. Sokcho Beach: A popular summer destination, Sokcho Beach offers clear water and white sand, making it a perfect spot for swimming or sunbathing.

  3. Yangyang Beach: Known for its clear blue water and clean sand, Yangyang Beach is perfect for those who prefer less crowded beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yangyang

  1. Baekdamsa Temple Trail: This hiking trail leads to Baekdamsa Temple, a scenic site nestled in the mountains of Seoraksan National Park.

  2. Seoraksan Mountain: This popular hiking spot offers stunning panoramic views of the national park and its surroundings.

  3. Naksansa Temple Hike: A challenging hiking trail leads to Naksansa Temple, famous for its stunning views of the East Sea.

Best Things to Do in Yangyang: Activities and Tours

  1. Sea Fishing Tour: A unique experience for visitors, sea fishing tours offer an opportunity to catch fish in the East Sea.

  2. Traditional Korean Cooking Class: Visitors can learn how to cook traditional Korean dishes and experience the country’s culinary culture.

  3. River Rafting: Yangyang’s rivers offer exciting rafting experiences for all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Yangyang

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Gangwon-style Naengmyeon (Cold Noodles): Made with noodles, sliced beef, and seasonal vegetables, this refreshing dish is perfect for the summer.

  2. Hwangtae (Dried Pollack): A regional specialty, Hwangtae is whitefish that has been freeze-dried and is often served grilled or stewed.

  3. Makguksu (Buckwheat Noodles): Made with buckwheat noodles, sliced beef, and a spicy sauce, this dish is perfect for those who love spicy food.

Local Beverages

  1. Makgeolli: a traditional Korean rice wine that is slightly sweet and has a cloudy appearance.

  2. Soju: Korea’s most popular alcoholic drink, a clear and potent spirit made from rice or wheat.

  3. Hwaeomsa Temple Tea: A specialty tea made with flowers from Hwaeomsa Temple, famous for its sweet aroma and flavor.

Where to Stay in Yangyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Ocean Hotel: Located near Yangyang Beach, this hotel offers stunning sea views, comfortable rooms, and modern amenities.

  2. Pine Beach Condo: A self-catered accommodation option, Pine Beach Condo offers spacious apartments with stunning sea views.

  3. The House Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers or solo travelers, The House Hostel offers comfortable dorm rooms and easy access to Yangyang’s attractions.

What to Buy in Yangyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hwangtae: Dried pollack makes for a unique and tasty souvenir to bring home.

  2. Honey: Yangyang is known for its high-quality honey, made with natural ingredients and distinct floral flavors.

  3. Korean Traditional Ceramics: Visitors can purchase handmade ceramics, such as teapots and bowls, at local pottery workshops.

Local Culture and Customs of Yangyang

Yangyang is known for its distinct culture and customs, including the Naksansa Temple’s bell-ringing ceremony, a traditional practice believed to bring good luck.

Best Time to Visit Yangyang

The best time to visit Yangyang is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. However, Yangyang is still an enjoyable destination throughout the year, each season bringing its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yangyang

Do:

  1. Remove your shoes before entering traditional Korean homes or temples.
  2. Try the local cuisine and experience Korean culture.
  3. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural landmarks or temples.
  2. Be loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  3. Litter or leave trash in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yangyang

Yangyang is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take standard safety precautions and avoid venturing alone to remote areas at night.

Health in Yangyang

Medical facilities in Yangyang are of a high standard, with numerous clinics and hospitals available for any health issues.

Getting to Yangyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yangyang

  1. Yangyang International Airport: The closest airport to Yangyang, with domestic flights to Seoul, Gwangju, and Jeju.

  2. Incheon International Airport: This airport serves numerous international destinations and offers a direct bus connection to Yangyang.

Bus Stations Near Yangyang

  1. Yangyang Express Bus Terminal: this terminal offers connections to major cities in Korea, including Seoul and Busan.

  2. Sokcho Express Bus Terminal: another option for visitors, offering connections to Seoul, Gyeonggi-do, and other cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby cities or airports.

How to Get Around in Yangyang

The best way to explore Yangyang is by car, as public transportation can be limited outside of the city center. Car rental options are available at Yangyang International Airport or in nearby cities.

Where to Go Next from Yangyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Sokcho: A nearby coastal city known for its beaches, seafood, and Seoraksan National Park.

  2. Gangneung: A popular tourist destination famous for its traditional architecture, beaches, and coffee shops.

  3. Daegwallyeong: A mountainous area known for its beautiful scenery and alpine farm village.

Conclusion

Yangyang may not be as well-known as other Korean destinations, but it has numerous cultural landmarks, stunning natural scenery, and exciting outdoor adventures. This comprehensive tour guide provides everything you need to plan the perfect trip to Yangyang and experience South Korea’s unique and authentic culture.
