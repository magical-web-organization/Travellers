A Comprehensive Travel Guide to North Chungcheong, South Korea

North Chungcheong is a province situated in the central region of South Korea, known for its picture-perfect mountains, breathtaking landscapes, and rich cultural and culinary heritage. In this travel guide, we will explore the best of North Chungcheong and give you insights into the places to visit, adventure activities to indulge in, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong is one of eight provinces in South Korea and has a population of around 1.5 million people, with its capital being Cheongju. The province lies to the south of the Demilitarized Zone and shares boundaries with Gyeongsangbuk-do, Gyeonggi Province, and Chungcheongbuk-do. The province features a diverse landscape, including high mountains, deep valleys, and scenic riverside plains. The region experiences a temperate climate, which makes it an excellent destination for outdoor activities, especially hiking and biking.

List of Places to Visit in North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong province boasts an array of attractions for travelers, comprising both natural and cultural landmarks.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historic Places

  • Chungju Museum: Located in downtown Chungju city, this museum showcases a wide range of Korean art, featuring ancient pottery, sculptures, and calligraphy. It is a must-visit for art enthusiasts and history buffs.
  • Suanbo Hot Springs: Suanbo is a picturesque valley known for its natural hot springs that were used by the royal family during the Korean Joseon Dynasty. The area boasts many spas and resorts, making it an ideal place to relax and rejuvenate in style.
  • Cheonan Baekje Museum: Located in Cheonan city, this museum showcases the history and culture of the Baekje Dynasty, one of the three dynasties that ruled Korea. Visitors can see several artifacts, including pottery, royal tombs, and murals, depicting how the dynasty lived.
  • Gyerim Forest: Situated in Cheongju city, this forest is said to be the birthplace of King Tangun, a legendary figure in Korean mythology. Visitors can explore several hiking trails and see the monument dedicated to King Tangun.
  • Jeongbangsa Temple: This historical temple dates back to the early Goryeo Dynasty and is located in Nonsan city. It boasts Buddha statues, murals, and other artifacts, reflecting the deep Buddhist traditions of Korea.

Natural Attractions of North Chungcheong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Danyang Gosu Cave: Located in Danyang city, this cave is considered one of the most beautiful limestone caves in Korea. Visitors can take a tour and see an underground river and various rock formations, including stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Songni Mountain National Park: This park boasts a vast area of breathtaking scenery and exquisite natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and bird-watching while enjoying the picturesque views. One of the highlights of the park is the Songgwangsa Temple, which dates back to the early Joseon Dynasty.
  • Seonyu Island: This scenic island off the western coast of North Chungcheong is known for its pristine beaches, beautiful natural surroundings, and unique culture. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, canoeing, and swimming in the clear waters of the sea.
  • Gumsan Ginseng Market: Known as the ‘Ginseng Town’ of Korea, Gumsan city is famous for its ginseng cultivation. Visitors can explore the local markets and learn about the various types of ginseng available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong features a plethora of hiking and biking trails, catering to the needs of travelers of all ages and skill levels. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Daejeon-Yecheon Tumuli Trail: This 10 km trail is located in Daejeon city and is dotted with over 300 tombs from the Baekje and Silla dynasties. The route is great for hiking or biking.
  • Namsan Mountain Trail: This trail is located near Jecheon city and is ideal for camping and hiking trips. The course passes several temples and waterfalls, offering picturesque views of the mountain range.
  • Seonyu Island Cycling Course: This 6.3-km-long cycling route on Seonyu Island features flat terrain and takes travelers through the island’s picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in North Chungcheong: Activities and Tours

In North Chungcheong, visitors can indulge in a wide range of activities and tours, providing them with unforgettable experiences. Some of the must-do activities include:

  • Cheongpung Lake Cruise: One of the most exciting things to do in North Chungcheong is taking a relaxing boat ride on the scenic Cheongpung Lake, which is shaped like a heart. The ride gives travelers a peaceful retreat into the serene ambiance of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Suanbo Hot Springs Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the Suanbo hot springs, where they can indulge in a hot bath, sauna, and aromatic oils treatment. The tour also features a visit to the nearby Korean traditional village and Korean martial arts center.
  • Nonsan Strawberry Picking Tour: Nonsan city is known for its sweet strawberries, and visitors can pick their own strawberries during the harvest season. The tour also includes a visit to the nearby cherry blossom avenue and tomato farm, making it a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the cities.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Chungcheong

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring North Chungcheong’s best attractions:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Cheongju and visit Gyerim Forest
  • Day 2: Explore Chungju Museum and go to Suanbo Hot Springs
  • Day 3: Go hiking to Songni Mountain National Park and visit Songgwangsa Temple
  • Day 4: Visit Danyang Gosu Cave and go kayaking in Seonyu Island
  • Day 5: Go to the Cheonan Baekje Museum and the Gumsan Ginseng Market
  • Day 6: Take a boat ride on Cheongpung Lake and explore Jeongbangsa Temple
  • Day 7: Enjoy Strawberry picking in Nonsan and return to Cheongju for departure

Food and Drink Scene of North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong is known for its diverse culinary culture, featuring a variety of dishes using local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to have while in North Chungcheong:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chungcheong-style bibimbap (Jeonju bibimbap): This popular Korean dish features rice topped with vegetables, egg yolk, meat, and chili pepper paste.
  • Daejeon-style galbi-jjim: This dish is made by simmering beef or pork ribs in a sweet soy sauce and other spices until tender.
  • Ssambap: This dish features lettuce leaves filled with rice and spicy meat, seafood, or vegetable toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Cheongju: This traditional Korean rice wine is made from fermented rice and is known for its sweet taste and floral aroma.
  • Ginseng tea: Made by boiling ginseng roots, this tea is widely known for its medicinal properties and is great for boosting immunity and energy levels.
  • Matitsool: This Korean traditional liquor is made by fermenting various grains, including barley and rice, and is known for its smooth taste and unique flavor.

Where to Stay in North Chungcheong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in North Chungcheong, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the popular hotels in the province include:

  • Hotel Miranda Cheongju: This premium hotel features spacious rooms, a fitness center, a spa, and a classic restaurant serving Korean and international cuisine.
  • Danyang Herren House Pension: This guesthouse is located in Danyang city and offers a fantastic view of the surroundings. The rooms are equipped with all modern amenities, and the staff is friendly and courteous.

What to Buy in North Chungcheong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Chungcheong is known for its traditional arts and crafts, including ceramics, pottery, and textiles. Some of the popular souvenirs to buy in North Chungcheong include:

  • Seonyudo Island Artifacts: Visitors can buy traditional Korean clothes, accessories, and household items made by artisans on Seonyudo Island.
  • Baekje-style Pottery: Visitors can buy pottery made in the Baekje Dynasty style at the Cheonan Baekje Archaeology Museum.
  • Ginseng Products: Gumsan city is known for its high-quality ginseng products, including ginseng tea, candy, and supplements.

Local Culture and Customs of North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong has a rich cultural heritage that reflects in its customs and traditions. The locals are friendly and hospitable and take pride in their tradition and culture. Visitors should show respect for the local customs, which include:

  • Take off your shoes when entering homes or temples.
  • Bow when meeting someone for the first time.
  • Learn a few basic Korean phrases, including ‘hello,’ ‘thank you,’ and ‘goodbye.’

Best Time to Visit North Chungcheong

The best time to visit North Chungcheong is in the spring and fall when temperatures are mild, and the landscape is picturesque with cherry blossoms and fall foliage. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are frigid, with snow and low temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Chungcheong

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting North Chungcheong:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the natural surroundings and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Smoke or drink in public places.
  • Ignore the rules of temples or museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong has a low crime rate, and the province is relatively safe for travelers. Visitors should take general precautions, including keeping valuables in safe places and locking the doors of accommodation. Travelers are advised to check for updates on the COVID-19 pandemic before visiting.

Health in North Chungcheong

Medical facilities in North Chungcheong are adequate, and visitors can access both public and private hospitals. Visitors can also buy over-the-counter medications from drug or convenience stores. Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to North Chungcheong: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Chungcheong province is well-connected to the rest of South Korea by various modes of transportation, including buses, trains, and flights.

Airports Near North Chungcheong

The nearest airport to North Chungcheong is Incheon International Airport in Seoul, which is about 3 hours and 30 minutes away by car. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to North Chungcheong.

Bus Stations Near North Chungcheong

North Chungcheong has several bus stations that connect to major South Korean cities, including Seoul, Busan, and Daegu. Visitors can take a bus from the Central City Terminal in Seoul or other cities and arrive at the province’s central bus terminal in Cheongju city.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to explore the province. However, this option can be expensive, and drivers should have a valid international driving license.

How to Get Around in North Chungcheong

Visitors can use public transportation, including buses and trains, to get around North Chungcheong. Taxis are also readily available, but they can be more expensive. Visitors can also rent a car to go to more remote places, but it is important to note that driving in Korea can be challenging for foreigners due to different road signs and driving standards.

Where to Go Next from North Chungcheong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Chungcheong is situated in the central region of South Korea, making it an excellent base to explore other nearby destinations. Visitors can visit:

  • Daejeon city: Located south of North Chungcheong, Daejeon city boasts an array of museums, parks, and cultural landmarks.
  • Jeonju city: Situated west of North Chungcheong, Jeonju city is known for its traditional hanok village and delicious Korean food.
  • Gyeonggi Province: Located north of North Chungcheong, Gyeonggi Province is home to several cultural heritage sites and natural landmarks.

Conclusion

North Chungcheong province is a charming destination for travelers of all ages interested in exploring South Korea’s culture, nature, traditions, and cuisine. With its plethora of attractions, outdoor adventures, welcoming locals, and convenient transportation options, North Chungcheong is sure to provide visitors with an unforgettable travel experience.
