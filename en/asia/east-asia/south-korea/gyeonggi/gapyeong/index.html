Introduction to Gapyeong

Gapyeong is a picturesque county located in the Gyeonggi province of South Korea, renowned for its natural attractions and culturally significant sites. The region is nestled amidst the breathtaking natural wonders of the Taebaek Mountains, surrounding lakes, and verdant forests that are scattered across the county. The area offers a tranquil getaway to those looking for a natural escape from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Gapyeong

Gapyeong is known for its pristine scenery and offers a plethora of tourist destinations that cater to all types of travelers. The following are the most popular tourist attractions that Gapyeong has to offer.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gapyeong offers some of the most remarkable cultural attractions in South Korea. The following museums and historical sites provide insight into the rich cultural history of the region:

  • Korean Folk Village - This village showcases the traditional way of life of Koreans during the Joseon dynasty period.

  • The Garden of Morning Calm - This beautifully landscaped garden features exotic plants and flowers, and was initially created by a professor.

  • Namsong Art Museum - This museum houses an impressive collection of fine artworks and is a must-visit attraction for art enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Gapyeong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural attractions of Gapyeong are some of the most captivating in South Korea. The lush forests, bubbling rivers, and serene lakes that can be found in Gapyeong offer an idyllic environment for outdoor activities. Here are some of the natural tourist attractions in Gapyeong:

  • Cheongpyeong Lake - The lake offers water sports activities such as fishing, kayaking, and motorboating.

  • Yongso Pond - A beautiful pond located in the heart of the forest. A perfect destination for a picnic.

  • The Rail Bike - This activity allows you to pedal your way through the scenic countryside of Gapyeong on a rail track.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gapyeong

Gapyeong provides many hiking and biking trails that offer unparalleled views of the natural beauty of the region. Below are some of the famous hiking and biking routes:

  • Namhansanseong Fortress Hiking Trail - This trail is a UNESCO World Heritage site and features a 9.8-kilometer-long hike over the Namhansanseong fortress

  • The Bicycle Path - This 17.7-kilometer-long bicycle path offers stunning views of the Namhangang River and the surrounding farmland

Best Things to Do in Gapyeong: Activities and Tours

Gapyeong offers visitors a variety of cultural, outdoor, and leisurely activities that cater to all ages. Below are the popular things to do in Gapyeong:

  • Paddleboarding and water sports at Cheongpyeong Lake - A perfect place for adventure lovers

  • Strawberry Picking - During the winter season, you can pick your strawberries at local farms in Gapyeong

  • Swiss Sheep Farm - This farm features various animals, including sheep, alpaca, and goats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gapyeong

Below is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Gapyeong:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Nami Island, the iconic island which has been featured in K-dramas
Day 2 Visit Petite France, a small French-style village in Korea
Day 3 Hike the Namhansanseong Fortress trail or the Seoraksan National Park
Day 4 Visit the Korean Folk Village and The Garden of Morning Calm
Day 5 Rail Bike cycling through the countryside
Day 6 Visit the Cheongpyeong Lake and enjoy water sports
Day 7 Visit the frozen waterfalls in winter

Food and Drink Scene of Gapyeong

Gapyeong is famous for its delicious local cuisine and traditional beverages. Below are some of the most popular dishes and beverages that you must try while visiting the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sundubu Jjigae - Spicy tofu stew with seafood and vegetables

  • Dakgalbi - A popular Korean dish made with spicy marinated chicken and vegetables

  • Naengmyeon - Cold buckwheat noodles served in a broth with slices of beef

Local Beverages

  • Makgeolli - A Korean traditional alcoholic beverage made of rice.

  • Bosing-Tang and Samgyetang - Herbal soups that are known to boost the immune system

Where to Stay in Gapyeong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gapyeong houses several accommodations catering to different budgets, preferences, and styles. Below are some of the most popular places to stay in Gapyeong:

  • The Garden of Morning Calm Pension - A serene pension located within the beautiful garden grounds

  • Gapyeong Co-op Residence - A budget-friendly residence while still being comfortable and clean

  • The Crystal Boicemarine Resort - A luxurious resort that boasts of its beautiful rooms with a magnificent view of the lake.

What to Buy in Gapyeong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can purchase several unique and charming souvenirs in Gapyeong that are perfect for your family and friends. Below are some of the things you can buy:

  • Strawberry jam - Local farms make fresh strawberries and turn them into jam.

  • Makgeolli and local herbs - These make for an excellent gift and are readily available in the markets

Local Culture and Customs of Gapyeong

Gapyeong has a rich cultural heritage that reflects in its traditional architecture, cuisine, and festivals. Here are some notable cultural customs and traditions that you may want to learn about:

  • Bungeo-ppang - A fish-shaped pastry filled with an array of delectable fillings

  • Daepo and Chilbo festivals - The festivals celebrate Korean ancestry with traditional dances, music, food, and art.

Best Time to Visit Gapyeong

The best time to visit Gapyeong is during the spring season (April to June) and autumn season (September to November) due to the mild weather, beautiful natural scenes, and seasonal events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gapyeong

Do:

  • Respect the locals - Koreans are friendly people, so it’s important to respect their customs and traditions.

  • Travel lightly - Avoid bringing unnecessary or bulky items since space is usually limited on public transportation.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind - There are proper waste management practices in South Korea, and locals expect visitors to follow them.

  • Enter into temples with shoes on - In Korea, it’s customary to remove your shoes before entering any religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gapyeong

Gapyeong is considered to be a safe place to visit. However, it’s advisable to take general safety measures like you would anywhere else.

Health in Gapyeong

The medical facilities in Gapyeong are top-notch, and you can find clinics and hospitals in the vicinity quickly. Local pharmacies are easily accessible, making it convenient to purchase over-the-counter medication when in need.

Getting to Gapyeong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gapyeong

  • Incheon International Airport - The airport is located 80km away from Gapyeong.

  • Gimpo Airport - This airport is situated 60km away from Gapyeong.

Bus Stations Near Gapyeong

  • Dong Seoul Bus Terminal - This is the central bus terminal in Seoul, which has frequent buses leaving for Gapyeong.

Other Options

  • Train - You can take a train from the Cheongnyangni Station in Seoul (Jungang Line), and it’ll take roughly an hour to reach Gapyeong Station.

How to Get Around in Gapyeong

There are several modes of transportation for sightseeing in Gapyeong:

  • Bus - Gapyeong has a vast network of local buses serving the region.

  • Taxi - Taxis are plentiful in the area, but it’s essential to negotiate the prices if needed.

Where to Go Next from Gapyeong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gapyeong is well-connected to several other popular destinations in South Korea, with frequent transport connections.

  • Seoul - The nation’s capital, which offers a host of cultural and historical sites for tourists to explore.

  • Pyeongchang - A winter sports haven, home to the 2018 Winter Olympics.

Conclusion

A visit to Gapyeong, South Korea, offers tourists an opportunity to experience the stunning natural beauty of the Taebaek Mountains, coupled with the region’s rich cultural heritage. The area boasts of a variety of activities and attractions that cater to all types of visitors, making it an ideal holiday destination for both adventure seekers and those looking for a tranquil retreat.
