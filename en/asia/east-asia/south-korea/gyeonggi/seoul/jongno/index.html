Introduction to Jongno

Welcome to Jongno, the heart and soul of Seoul. Located in Gyeonggi province, this district is renowned for its cultural significance, historic landmarks, and natural beauty. From ancient palaces and temples to bustling markets and scenic parks, Jongno is a popular tourist destination that offers something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a comprehensive tour of Jongno – from its famous landmarks and must-visit attractions to its food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, local customs and culture, and more. So, let’s pack our bags and get ready to explore the beauty of Jongno.

List of Places to Visit in Jongno

Jongno has a plethora of attractions to offer travelers of all ages and interests. Here are some of the top places you must visit during your trip to Jongno:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gyeongbokgung Palace: The grandest of all the palaces in Seoul, Gyeongbokgung Palace was built in the 14th century and is a must-visit attraction in Jongno.
  • Changdeokgung Palace: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Changdeokgung Palace is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • Jongmyo Shrine: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Jongmyo Shrine is dedicated to the spirits of the Joseon Dynasty’s ancestors and is a prime example of Confucian architecture.
  • Bukchon Hanok Village: This traditional Korean village is home to over 900 hanoks (traditional Korean houses) and offers a glimpse into the country’s rich history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Jongno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Namsan Park: Located in the heart of Seoul, Namsan Park offers breathtaking views of the city from the top of its mountain peak. You can take a cable car or hike up to the top.
  • Cheonggyecheon Stream: A man-made waterway that runs through the heart of Seoul, Cheonggyecheon offers a tranquil escape from the bustling city and is a beautiful spot for a peaceful stroll.
  • Mt. Inwangsan: A popular hiking spot in Seoul, Mt. Inwangsan offers incredible panoramic views of the city and is a great outdoor adventure for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jongno

Jongno offers some great hiking and biking routes that take you through beautiful natural landscapes and historic landmarks. Here are some popular routes to try:

  • Namsan Circular Road: This 15-kilometer route takes you around the base of Namsan Mountain and offers stunning views of the city skyline.
  • Cheonggyecheon Stream Trail: A 10-kilometer trail that follows the Cheonggyecheon Stream, this route takes you through the heart of Seoul’s bustling downtown area.
  • Bugak Skyway: This scenic route takes you through the beautiful Bugaksan Mountain area and is a popular spot for recreational cyclists and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Jongno: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the top attractions and hiking/biking trails, there are plenty of other things to do in Jongno. Here are some popular activities and tours:

  • Cooking classes: Experience authentic Korean cuisine by taking a cooking class and learning how to cook traditional Korean dishes.
  • Korean traditional music and dance shows: Experience the vibrant and colorful culture of Korea by enjoying a traditional music and dance performance.
  • Hanbok rental and photo shoot: Immerse yourself in Korean culture by renting out a traditional Korean dress (Hanbok) and taking a photo shoot in Seoul’s famous tourist destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Jongno

Korean cuisine is world-famous for its delicious flavors and unique cooking techniques. Jongno offers a diverse range of dining options that cater to all budgets and tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Samgyetang: A traditional Korean soup made with chicken, ginseng, and rice.
  • Kimchi: A spicy and tangy fermented vegetable side dish that is a staple in Korean cuisine.
  • Bibimbap: A rice-based dish mixed with vegetables, meat, and a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Soju: A clear, distilled spirit made from rice or wheat and is often referred to as Korean vodka.
  • Makgeolli: A traditional Korean rice wine that is sweet and tangy.
  • Korean Green Tea: A healthy and refreshing beverage that is widely consumed in Korea.

Where to Stay in Jongno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jongno has plenty of accommodation options, including luxurious hotels, affordable hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Skypark Kingstown Dongdaemun: This luxurious hotel offers modern amenities and is conveniently located near popular tourist attractions.
  • Kimchee Guesthouse Jongno: This affordable guesthouse is located in the heart of Jongno and offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Namsan Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is located near Namsan Mountain and offers beautiful views of the city skyline.

What to Buy in Jongno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jongno is a great place to shop for souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hanboks: Traditional Korean clothing that comes in various designs and colors.
  • Korean Ginseng: Known for its medicinal properties, Korean ginseng can be found in various forms, including capsules, tea, and candies.
  • K-beauty products: Korean skincare and makeup products are world-famous for their quality and effectiveness.

Local Culture and Customs of Jongno

Korea has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs that you should be aware of before visiting. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Remove shoes before entering homes and certain businesses.
  • Address older people with the appropriate honorific titles (a sign of respect for seniors).
  • Cover your shoulders and knees when visiting palaces and temples.

Best Time to Visit Jongno

The best time to visit Jongno is during spring (April-June) and autumn (September-November), when the weather is pleasant and the scenery is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jongno

Do:

  • Learn some basic Korean phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Try Korean cuisine and local beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Get too drunk in public.
  • Take photos without permission in palaces and temples.
  • Disrespect older people or authority figures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jongno

Jongno is a safe district, but it’s important to stay cautious and aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid going out alone late at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be careful of traffic when crossing the road.

Health in Jongno

South Korea has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can avail of affordable medical services. It’s important to have travel insurance before your trip to Jongno.

Getting to Jongno: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options and logistics to help you get to Jongno:

Airports Near Jongno

  • Incheon International Airport (ICN): This is the largest airport in South Korea and is located approximately 50 kilometers from Jongno.

Bus Stations Near Jongno

  • Seoul Central Bus Terminal: This is the largest bus terminal in Seoul and is located approximately 6 kilometers from Jongno.

Other Options

  • Subway: The Seoul Metropolitan Subway network covers most of the city, including Jongno.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout Jongno.

How to Get Around in Jongno

Jongno has an efficient transportation system that includes buses, subways, and taxis. Here are some tips on getting around Jongno:

  • Use public transportation for budget-friendly travel.
  • Taxis are convenient for late-night travel or for carrying heavy luggage.
  • You can rent a bike for an eco-friendly way of getting around Jongno.

Where to Go Next from Jongno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jongno is located in the heart of Seoul and is close to many popular tourist destinations. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Myeong-dong: A trendy shopping district in Seoul.
  • Hongdae: A popular area for nightlife, shopping, and entertainment.
  • Gangnam: A district in Seoul known for its modern and upscale amenities.

Conclusion

Jongno is a culturally rich and vibrant district that offers a unique blend of history, nature, and modernity. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, Jongno is sure to captivate you with its many attractions and activities. Use this guide as your roadmap and discover the beauty of Jongno on your next trip to Seoul.
