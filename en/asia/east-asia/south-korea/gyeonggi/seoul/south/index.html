South Korea Travel Guide: Exploring the Best of South, Gyeonggi

South, a district in Gyeonggi, South Korea, is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for an authentic Korean experience. This guide aims to provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to South, from where to go and what to see, to how to get around and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South is a region steeped in history, with several important cultural attractions to explore. Some must-see spots include:

  • Namhansanseong Fortress: A well-preserved mountain fortress built during the Joseon Dynasty, offering beautiful views of the surrounding area.
  • Hwaseong Fortress: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that was also built during the Joseon Dynasty, featuring beautiful architecture and a commanding presence.
  • Korean Folk Village: A living museum featuring traditional Korean houses and cultural performances.
  • Seoksu Art Project: An outdoor art museum featuring large-scale sculptures and installations amidst beautiful natural scenery.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South is also home to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in South Korea. Here are some top natural attractions to explore:

  • Cheonan Baekseok Arts University Park: A scenic park with beautiful gardens, sculptures, and architecture, perfect for a peaceful stroll.
  • Acha Mountain: A popular hiking destination with trails of various difficulty levels and stunning views from the top.
  • Gwanggyosan Mountain: Another great hiking spot, offering scenic views and beautiful temples along the way.
  • Lake Seokchon: A serene lake surrounded by lush greenery and featuring beautiful walking paths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

South is a hiker and biker’s paradise, offering several beautiful trails that wind through picturesque scenery. Some top routes include:

  • Namhansanseong Fortress Trail: A scenic 12-kilometer trail that winds through the mountains around the Namhansanseong Fortress, offering beautiful views of the fortress and the surrounding area.
  • Acha Mountain Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike that takes you to the top of Acha Mountain and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Songsan-ri Bicycle Path: A picturesque 12-kilometer bike trail that winds through the countryside, past rice paddies, and ancient temples.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, South offers plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy, such as:

  • Templestay Experience: Spend a night in a traditional Korean temple, take part in Buddhist rituals, and experience the peaceful way of life of monks.
  • Gongju and Buyeo Day Trip: A day trip to two neighboring cities that offer beautiful scenery, ancient temples, and historic sites.
  • Kimchi Making Experience: Learn how to make the iconic Korean dish of kimchi from scratch, using traditional methods.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

If you’re planning on spending a week in South, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the museums and historical sites of Hwaseong Fortress and Namhansanseong Fortress Day 2: Hike to the top of Acha Mountain and enjoy the breathtaking views Day 3: Take a day trip to the neighboring cities of Gongju and Buyeo and explore the temples and historic sites Day 4: Visit the Korean Folk Village and experience traditional Korean culture Day 5: Go on a biking tour of the scenic countryside Day 6: Learn how to make kimchi and enjoy a stay in a traditional Korean temple Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural scenery at Lake Seokchon or Cheonan Baekseok Arts University Park

Food and Drink Scene of South

Korean cuisine is world-famous for its bold, spicy flavors and unique ingredients, and South is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bibimbap: A popular Korean dish consisting of rice, vegetables, meat, and a spicy sauce
  • Japchae: A noodle dish made from sweet potato starch noodles
  • Kimchi: A side dish made of pickled vegetables and spices, a staple in Korean cuisine
  • Bulgogi: A grilled Korean beef dish marinated in a sweet and savory sauce

Local Beverages

  • Soju: A clear, distilled liquor that is the most popular alcoholic beverage in South Korea
  • Makgeolli: A traditional Korean fermented rice wine, slightly sweet and cloudy in appearance

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South offers plenty of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. Some top choices include:

  • Ritz Carlton: A luxury hotel with stunning views and top-notch amenities
  • Sheraton: A great mid-range option with spacious rooms and a central location
  • Hostel Haru: A budget-friendly hostel with clean rooms and a friendly atmosphere

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in South, make sure to bring home some of these local products and souvenirs:

  • Hanji paper: Handmade paper that is used for traditional Korean arts and crafts
  • Ginseng: A herb used in traditional Korean medicine and renowned for its health benefits
  • Traditional Korean Clothing: Colorful and intricately designed hanboks are a great souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Koreans place a strong emphasis on respect and politeness, and this is reflected in the local customs and culture. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Bow when greeting: A polite bow is the common way to greet someone in South Korea
  • Remove shoes when entering homes: It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Cover shoulders and legs when visiting temples: When visiting a temple or religious site, it is respectful to cover your shoulders and legs.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is during the spring and fall months, from March to May and September to November. The weather is comfortable during these times, and the scenery is especially beautiful during the fall when the leaves change colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture: Take the time to learn about the local customs and practices and show respect towards them.
  • Try the local cuisine: Korean food is delicious and varied, so don’t be afraid to try new dishes.
  • Stay in traditional accommodations: Staying in a traditional Korean guesthouse or temple is a unique and cultural experience.

Don’t:

  • Be loud in public areas: Koreans tend to be reserved and polite, so avoid making loud noises or causing a disturbance in public spaces.
  • Forget to tip: Tipping is not customary in South Korea, so don’t feel obligated to tip at restaurants or other service establishments.
  • Use red ink: In Korea, using red ink is associated with negativity and bad luck, so avoid using it for any formal documents or invitations.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South Korea is generally a safe country, with relatively low crime rates. However, it is always a good idea to take necessary precautions, such as locking your valuables in your hotel room and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in South

South Korea has a very high standard of healthcare, with both public and private hospitals offering top-notch medical care. However, it is always a good idea to have travel insurance, just in case.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Korea has a well-developed transportation system, making it easy to get to South from other parts of the country

Airports Near South

The closest airport to South is Incheon International Airport, which is located approximately 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near South

The main bus station in South is the Suwon Bus Terminal, which offers connections to other major cities in South Korea.

Other Options

Trains are also a reliable and affordable way to get to South, with several stops in the area.

How to Get Around in South

There are several ways to get around South, including:

  • Bus: South has a comprehensive bus system that runs frequently and is easy to use.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in South and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • Bicycle: South is a great place to explore on two wheels, with several bike rental options available.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Korea has plenty of other destinations worth exploring. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Seoul: The bustling capital city of South Korea, located just a short distance from South.
  • Busan: A scenic coastal city with beautiful beaches and a bustling nightlife.
  • Jeju Island: A volcanic island located off the southern coast of South Korea, known for its stunning landscapes and natural beauty.

Conclusion

South is a truly unique and beautiful destination that offers something for every traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient temples, hiking breathtaking trails, or indulging in mouth-watering cuisine, South has it all. With this guide, you’re ready to plan your trip to South and discover everything this wonderful region has to offer.
