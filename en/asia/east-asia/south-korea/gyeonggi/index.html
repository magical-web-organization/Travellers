Introduction to Gyeonggi

Gyeonggi Province is a beautiful region situated in the heart of South Korea. Covering an area of 10,171 square kilometers, it is the most populous province in the country, with over 12 million inhabitants. Gyeonggi is easily accessible from Seoul, the capital city of South Korea, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike. Boasting a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and modern amenities, Gyeonggi has a lot to offer to visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Gyeonggi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gyeongbokgung Palace - the largest and most important palace of the Joseon Dynasty
  • Korean Folk Village - an open-air museum showcasing the traditional Korean way of life
  • Suwon Fortress - a UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to the late 18th century
  • Imjingak Peace Park - a memorial to the Korean War and a symbol of peace and unity
  • DMZ (Demilitarized Zone) - a 155-mile-long buffer zone that divides the Korean Peninsula

Natural Attractions of Gyeonggi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nami Island - a scenic island known for its beautiful tree-lined roads, riverside walks, and natural landscapes
  • Bukhansan National Park - a stunning park with granite peaks, diverse flora and fauna, and hiking trails
  • Pocheon Art Valley - a former quarry transformed into an eco-friendly park with sculptures, gardens, and art installations
  • Yongin Everland - a world-class theme park with thrilling rides, animal exhibits, and seasonal events
  • Hangang River - a popular destination for water sports, cycling, and scenic walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gyeonggi

  • Bukhansan Dulle-gil - an advanced 70-km trail that takes you through Bukhansan National Park
  • Seoul Trail - a network of walking trails (157 km) that interlace through the city of Seoul and neighboring communities
  • Jinhae Cherry Blossom Marathon Trail - a 4.8 km trail that offers a stunning view of cherry blossom trees in spring
  • Gyeonggi Tamna-gil - a 342-km circular route that includes scenic coastal views, historic landmarks and towns.

Best Things to Do in Gyeonggi: Activities and Tours

  • Temple Stay - a chance to experience traditional Buddhist culture while staying in a serene, mountaintop temple
  • Hanbok Experience - try on traditional Korean clothing and learn about its history, culture and significance
  • Korean BBQ Dining Experience - a feast of grilled meat and traditional side dishes, enjoyed with friends or family
  • K-pop Dance Class - learn the coolest K-pop dance moves from talented dance instructors
  • Kimchi Making Class - a cooking class that teaches you how to make Korea’s most famous dish

Food and Drink Scene of Gyeonggi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galbi-jjim (Korean Braised Beef Short Ribs)
  • Bibimbap (Mixed Rice Bowl)
  • Jajangmyeon (Black Bean Noodles)
  • Dak-galbi (Stir-Fried Chicken with Vegetables)
  • Gimbap (Seaweed Rice Rolls)

Local Beverages

  • Soju - distilled alcoholic beverage similar to vodka
  • Makgeolli - fermented rice wine with a sweet-tart flavor
  • Binsul - a traditional medicinal liquor made with herbs and other ingredients.
  • Maesilju - a plum wine that is sweet, fragrant, and refreshing
  • Fruit Soju - a fruity twist on the classic soju drink

  • Q Hotel - a 4-star hotel located in the heart of Suwon
  • Stay & Home Residence Suite Hotel - a comfortable and modern hotel with spacious rooms and great service
  • Banana Backpackers - a cozy and affordable hostel in Seoul
  • Han-ok Guesthouse - a traditional Korean-style guesthouse located in Jeonju

What to Buy in Gyeonggi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hanji (traditional Korean paper)
  • Ginseng Products (supplements, teas, and skincare)
  • Traditional Korean Pottery (vases, teapots, plates)
  • Crispy Seaweed Snacks (delicious and healthy snack)

Local Culture and Customs of Gyeonggi

  • Respect the elders, and use honorifics while addressing them
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home, a temple, or a traditional restaurant.
  • Bowing is a way of showing respect for others, especially for your elders and superiors.
  • Do not leave chopsticks upright in a bowl of rice, as this is considered offensive.
  • Do not blow your nose in public, as it is considered impolite.

Best Time to Visit Gyeonggi

The best time to visit Gyeonggi is during the autumn (September to November) and spring (April and May) when the weather is mild and pleasant. During these seasons, you can witness the changing colors of the leaves and the blooming flowers in parks and gardens. Also, many festivals and events take place during these periods, such as the Seoul Lantern Festival and the Hi! Seoul Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gyeonggi

Do:

  • Learn basic Korean phrases.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.
  • Use public transportation whenever possible.
  • Take off your shoes before entering traditional restaurants or temples.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Hesitate to ask for help when needed.
  • Use your phone loudly on public transport or in public places.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Underestimate the traffic in Seoul and Gyeonggi.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gyeonggi

Overall, Gyeonggi is a safe place for travelers. However, you should take the usual precautions to avoid pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas. Also, be aware of traffic, especially when crossing the streets, and follow the traffic rules.

Health in Gyeonggi

South Korea has an excellent healthcare system, and most hospitals offer high-quality medical services. It is advisable to have travel insurance before departing for Gyeonggi.

How to Get Around in Gyeonggi

The best way to get around Gyeonggi is by public transportation since it is affordable, efficient, and widely available. You can use the subway, bus, or train to move around the region.

Where to Go Next from Gyeonggi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Seoul - The capital city of South Korea and the country’s political and economic center
  • Jeju Island - A popular tourist destination with a stunning natural landscape
  • Busan - A vibrant port city located in the southeast of South Korea
  • Sokcho - A popular beach destination and gateway to Seoraksan National Park
  • Andong - A traditional city famous for its traditional villages, museums, and festivals.

Conclusion

Gyeonggi Province is a must-see destination for every traveler visiting South Korea. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural landscapes, there is so much to discover and explore in this region. Whether you’re looking for cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, or delicious cuisine, Gyeonggi has something for everyone. Plan your trip carefully using our comprehensive travel guide, and make unforgettable memories in Gyeonggi.
