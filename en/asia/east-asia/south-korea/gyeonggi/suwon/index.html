Suwon is a charming city located in the Gyeonggi province of South Korea. Widely recognized for its UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Hwaseong Fortress, Suwon is a destination worth exploring. The city boasts a mix of modernity and tradition, preserving its cultural heritage and offering tourists a glimpse into the past. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at Suwon, from the best things to do to the must-try local delicacies.

Introduction to Suwon

Suwon’s history dates back to the 18th century when King Jeongjo ordered the construction of the Hwaseong Fortress. Since then, the city has been a hub for culture, education, and tourism. Suwon is not only popular for its historical background but also for its natural wonders.

The city enjoys a temperate climate for most of the year, with pleasant spring and fall seasons. In the summer, temperatures can spike and get humid, while winter sees snowfall throughout the city.

Suwon caters to all types of travelers, offering cultural expeditions, outdoor adventures, and an excellent culinary scene. Let’s dive deeper into the top attractions of Suwon.

List of Places to Visit in Suwon

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Suwon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hwaseong Fortress
  • Suwon Hwaseong Museum
  • Korean Traditional Village
  • Haenggung Palace

Each of these attractions offers tourists a glimpse into Suwon’s history, culture, and traditions.

The Hwaseong Fortress is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that spans over five miles, including traditional gates, watchtowers, stonework, and the palace. The Suwon Hwaseong Museum is dedicated to exhibiting the fortress’s history and significance.

The Korean Traditional Village is an open-air museum where visitors can experience traditional Korean architecture, crafts, and performances. Haenggung Palace was built for the royalties to stay during visits to Suwon, and visitors can explore its grounds and witness historical reenactments.

Natural Attractions of Suwon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paldalmun Gate
  • Gwanggyo Lake Park
  • Suwon Olympic Parklands
  • Gyeonggi Arts Center Pond

Nature lovers can enjoy breathtaking views and outdoor activities at the above attractions. Paldalmun Gate, situated at the entrance to Hwaseong Fortress, offers panoramic views of the city. Gwanggyo Lake and Olympic Parklands are perfect spots for walking, cycling, and outdoor picnics. Gyeonggi Arts Center Pond is a calming oasis in the city center, surrounded by nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suwon

Hikers and bikers can indulge in the following routes:

  • Suwon Hwaseong Fortress Trail
  • Seoho-dong Bunker Hill Trail
  • Yeonmudae Hill Trail

These trails offer different levels of difficulty, scenic views, and historical significance. The Hwaseong Fortress Trail is a 5.7-mile-long circular route encompassing Hwaseong Fortress’s circumference. The Seoho-dong Bunker Hill Trail is a shorter, 1.6-mile-long path leading to an old bunker on top of a hill. Yeonmudae Hill Trail is a challenging hike up steep terrain, offering a spectacular view at the top.

Best Things to Do in Suwon: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best experiences you should not miss while in Suwon:

  • Suwon Firefly Festival
  • Suwon Hwaseong Cultural Festival
  • Korean Drum Performance
  • Night markets

The Suwon Firefly Festival is held in late spring/early summer, where visitors can witness the spectacle of thousands of glowing fireflies. The Hwaseong Cultural Festival is a celebration of the fortress, with performances, exhibitions, and parades. Korean drum performances and night markets are also widespread throughout the city and offer a taste of local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Suwon

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Suwon:

Day 1: Arrive in Suwon, check into a hotel, and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit the Hwaseong Fortress and hike along the fortress trail. Day 3: Explore the museums, including Suwon Hwaseong Museum and Korean Traditional Village. Day 4: Take a cultural tour of the city and attend a drumming performance at night. Day 5: Cycle or walk around Gwanggyo Lake Park and picnic in Olympic Parklands. Day 6: Take a day-trip to Yongin, home to the Korean Folk Village and Everland Theme Park. Day 7: Visit the historical Haenggung Palace and shop for local souvenirs at night markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Suwon

Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Suwon:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suwon Galbi
  • Suwon Naengmyeon
  • Suwon Fried Chicken

Suwon Galbi or grilled beef ribs is a signature dish, marinated in soy sauce, sugar, and Korean spices. Suwon Naengmyeon is a refreshing cold noodle dish eaten in summer. Suwon Fried Chicken is a popular snack that locals enjoy with beer.

Local Beverages

  • Makgeolli
  • Soju
  • Bokbunja

Makgeolli or rice wine, Soju, a Korean spirit, and Bokbunja, a type of Korean raspberry wine, are all traditional drinks that Suwon produces.

What to Buy in Suwon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique Souvenirs and local products that visitors should take home from Suwon:

  • Suwon Galbi Marinade
  • Suwon Naengmyeon paste
  • Korean Laver
  • Suwon traditional crafts

Suwon Galbi Marinade and Naengmyeon paste make great gifts for food lovers. Korean Laver, aka Seaweed, is a popular snack that visitors can take home. Suwon is also famous for its traditional crafts, so consider purchasing some to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Suwon

The people of Suwon are friendly and welcoming towards visitors. It is customary in Suwon to remove shoes before entering someone’s home or certain buildings. Respect for elders is highly valued in Korean culture, so show your respect to older people.

Best Time to Visit Suwon

The best time to visit Suwon is during spring and fall, when temperatures are mild and nature is at its best. Summer sees heavy rain and humidity, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suwon

Do:

  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home or temple.
  • Bow down as a form of greeting and thanks.
  • Show respect to Korea’s elders.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be excessively loud in public spaces.
  • Don’t disrespect Korean national symbols like the flag.
  • Don’t point your feet in someone’s direction.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suwon

Suwon is considered a safe city. However, like any other city, petty crime may occur in crowded places, so maintain awareness and vigilance.

Health in Suwon

It’s always recommended to take necessary precautions while traveling. Tap water is drinkable in Suwon. However, it’s always advisable to drink bottled water. Be sure to carry clean drinking water while hiking or traveling outdoors.

Getting to Suwon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Suwon is easily accessible from Seoul by train, bus, or car. The following options can be considered while traveling to Suwon:

Airports Near Suwon

The nearest airport to Suwon is Incheon International Airport, located 50 km away. From there, visitors can take an airport bus or train to Suwon.

Bus Stations Near Suwon

Suwon has two primary bus stations, Suwon Bus Terminal and Suwon City Hall Bus Station, both providing intercity and intracity transportation options.

Other Options

The Seoul Metro Line 1 connects Suwon to Seoul, offering a comfortable and affordable mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Suwon

Suwon has an extensive public transportation system, including buses and metro lines. Taxis are also widely available and reasonably priced. Visitors can also rent bikes or use shared bikes to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Suwon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yongin: Korean Folk Village and Everland Theme Park.
  • Seoul: Capital of South Korea, home to more than 600 years of history, culture, and modern-day adventures.
  • Jeju Island: A fascinating destination long favored by Koreans for its natural beauty and volcanic landscapes.

Conclusion

Suwon is a charming and vibrant city that offers visitors a taste of Korean culture, history, and nature. With some of the country’s best museums, outdoor activities, and unique culinary experiences, Suwon is an excellent destination for all types of travelers. Whether you plan to visit for a short stay or an extended trip, Suwon is a must-visit destination and should be added to your travel bucket list.
