Introduction to Goyang

Located in the Gyeonggi Province of South Korea, Goyang is a bustling city that offers the perfect blend of traditional culture and modern entertainment. Home to the world-famous Hwaseong Fortress, Goyang is a city steeped in history, but it’s not all about the past. The city boasts an impressive shopping district, beautifully landscaped parks, and a thriving nightlife scene, making it a perfect destination for travelers of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Goyang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Goyang is a city with a rich history and culture, and it shows in the many museums and historical places dotted throughout the area. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical sites:

  • Hwaseong Fortress - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Hwaseong Fortress is a stunning example of Korean architecture and engineering. Built in the 18th century, the fortress is an impressive display of the country’s strength and determination.
  • Korean Folk Village - A glimpse into the past, the Korean Folk Village is a living museum that showcases traditional Korean life. Visitors can immerse themselves in the culture, learn about ancient crafts, and watch live performances.
  • Goyang Aram Nuri Arts Center - An impressive cultural complex that includes a theater, concert hall, art museum, and more. The center hosts a variety of performances throughout the year, including traditional Korean arts, dance, music, and theater.

Natural Attractions of Goyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Goyang is not just about the history and culture, but also about the stunning natural beauty of the area. With many outdoor spaces to explore, here are some of the top natural attractions in Goyang:

  • Jeongbalsan Mountain Park - Offering spectacular views of the city, Jeongbalsan Mountain Park is a popular hiking destination for locals and tourists alike. The park has several trails of varying difficulty levels, making it accessible to all.
  • Goyang Lake Park - Located in the heart of the city, Goyang Lake Park is a serene oasis that offers a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. The park has walking trails, bike rentals, and even a small zoo.
  • Onemount Snow Park - A winter wonderland in the heart of Goyang, Onemount Snow Park is an attraction that offers skiing, snowboarding, and sledding in an artificial snow park. The park operates year-round, making it a must-visit attraction no matter what the season.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goyang

Goyang is a hiker’s paradise, with several stunning trails of varying difficulty levels for visitors to explore. There are also several bike trails and rental options available for those looking to explore the city on two wheels. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Jeongbalsan Mountain Trail - A moderately challenging trail that takes about two hours to complete, this trail takes hikers through the beautiful Jeongbalsan Mountain Park.
  • Paju Bukhan Mountain Trail - A challenging hike that takes about seven hours to complete, the Paju Bukhan Mountain Trail is a must-do for experienced hikers. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside from the summit.
  • Imjingak Bike Trail - A 30-kilometer bike trail that runs from Imjingak Park to Ilsan Lake Park, the Imjingak Bike Trail offers beautiful views of the city and is the perfect way to explore Goyang at a leisurely pace.

Best Things to Do in Goyang: Activities and Tours

Goyang is a city that offers something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in Goyang:

  • Catch a Show at the Aram Nuri Arts Center - The culture hub of Goyang, the Aram Nuri Arts Center hosts a variety of performances and shows throughout the year. From traditional Korean theater to modern dance performances, there is something for everyone.
  • Spend a Day at Onemount Waterpark - A perfect place to cool off in the hot summer months, Onemount Waterpark is an attraction that offers a variety of water activities for visitors of all ages.
  • Shop at the Ilsan Lake Park Street - A popular shopping district in Goyang, the Ilsan Lake Park Street offers visitors a chance to indulge in some retail therapy. The street is dotted with shops, restaurants, and cafes, making it a perfect place to spend an afternoon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Goyang

Day 1: Visit the Hwaseong Fortress and explore the surrounding area. Day 2: Take a day trip to the Korean Folk Village. Day 3: Spend the day at Jeongbalsan Mountain Park, hiking or biking. Day 4: Catch a show at the Aram Nuri Arts Center. Day 5: Spend the day at Onemount Snow Park. Day 6: Take a relaxing stroll through Goyang Lake Park. Day 7: Explore the Ilsan Lake Park Street and indulge in some retail therapy.

Food and Drink Scene of Goyang

Must-Try Dishes

Goyang offers a variety of traditional Korean dishes that are a must-try for visitors. Here are some of the top dishes to look out for:

  • Lake Sashimi - A local delicacy of Goyang, Lake Sashimi is made from raw fish that is caught in the nearby lake.
  • Bibimbap - A traditional Korean dish that is made with rice, vegetables, and beef, Bibimbap is a must-try for visitors.
  • Pajeon - A savory pancake made with green onions, Pajeon is a popular snack in Goyang.

Local Beverages

Goyang has a rich drinking culture, with several local beverages that are a must-try for visitors. Here are some of the top local beverages to look out for:

  • Makgeolli - A traditional Korean rice wine, Makgeolli is a must-try for visitors looking to experience the local drinking culture.
  • Soju - Korea’s most popular alcoholic beverage, Soju is a clear spirit that is similar in taste to vodka.
  • Bokbunja Wine - Made from black raspberries, Bokbunja Wine is a sweet and fruity wine that is a popular local beverage in Goyang.

Where to Stay in Goyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Goyang offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • Narisawa Hotel - A luxury hotel located in the heart of Goyang, Narisawa Hotel offers guests stunning views of the city and top-notch amenities.
  • Cube Guesthouse - A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable private rooms and shared spaces for visitors on a budget.
  • Goodstay Hotel H - A mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and all the necessary amenities at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Goyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Goyang is filled with unique and traditional Korean products that are perfect for souvenirs. Here are some of the top things to buy in Goyang:

  • Traditional Korean Crafts - Goyang is home to several traditional Korean craft shops that offer a variety of handmade products, ranging from pottery to traditional Korean clothing.
  • Kimchi - A popular Korean side dish, Kimchi is a must-try for visitors to Goyang. Many shops in the area sell packaged Kimchi that can be taken home as a souvenir.
  • Korean Beauty Products - Korean beauty products are renowned for their quality and effectiveness, and several shops in Goyang offer a variety of skincare and makeup products.

Local Culture and Customs of Goyang

Goyang has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the customs and traditions of the locals. Visitors should be respectful of these customs, which include:

  • Removing Shoes - It is customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s home in Korea, and visitors should follow suit when visiting a local’s home or temple.
  • Bow and Greet Respectfully - A polite bow and greeting are customary when meeting locals or entering a place of business.
  • Respectful Dress - Visitors to Goyang should dress conservatively, keeping shoulders and knees covered when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Goyang

The best time to visit Goyang is in the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. However, visitors can enjoy the city year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goyang

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including traditional Korean dishes and local beverages.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the area, including hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Do be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
  • Don’t be loud or disrespectful in public places.
  • Don’t leave trash or litter in natural areas, such as parks and hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goyang

Goyang is a safe city, with low crime rates and a generally friendly and welcoming local population. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings at night.

Health in Goyang

The healthcare system in Goyang is modern and efficient, with several hospitals and clinics throughout the area. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance to ensure they are covered in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Goyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Goyang is easily accessible from Seoul, with several transportation options available for visitors.

Airports Near Goyang

  • Incheon International Airport - Located approximately 40 kilometers from Goyang, Incheon International Airport is the nearest airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Goyang

  • Dong Seoul Bus Terminal - Located in central Seoul, Dong Seoul Bus Terminal offers several bus routes to Goyang.
  • Jangam Station - Located in Goyang, Jangam Station offers several bus routes to other cities in South Korea.

Other Options

  • Train - Goyang is accessible by train, with several train stations located throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Goyang

Goyang has an extensive public transportation system that makes it easy to get around the city. Visitors can use buses, taxis, and trains to get around Goyang and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Goyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Goyang can enjoy. Here are some of the top options:

  • Seoul - The bustling capital city of Seoul is just a short train ride away from Goyang and offers visitors a chance to explore the culture, history, and modernity of Korea.
  • Paju - A small city located just north of Goyang, Paju is home to several historical sites, including the DMZ.
  • Incheon - A bustling port city, Incheon offers visitors beautiful beaches, bustling markets, and a variety of cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Goyang is a city that offers visitors a chance to experience the history, culture, and natural beauty of Korea. With a variety of attractions, accommodation options, and transportation options available, visitors are sure to have a memorable trip to Goyang.
