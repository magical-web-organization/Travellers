Introduction to Haenam

Located in South Jeolla province of South Korea, Haenam is a picturesque town famous for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, scenic natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Haenam is a popular destination among both locals and tourists, who come here to relax among stunning landscapes, explore the town’s colorful culture and enjoy exciting outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Haenam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haenam Folk Museum

The Haenam Folk Museum is an excellent place to learn about the history and culture of the region. The museum features a vast collection of artifacts, including traditional clothing, farming tools, and household items, providing visitors with a glimpse into the daily life of local residents.

Naganeupseong Folk Village

Naganeupseong is a well-preserved 18th-century fortress village that offers a unique glimpse into the architecture and lifestyle of the Joseon dynasty. Visitors can explore the narrow streets and alleyways lined with traditional houses, shops, and even learn to make traditional handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Haenam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dadohae National Marine Park

Dadohae National Marine Park is the largest marine park in South Korea and offers stunning views of the surrounding islands, including Namhae and Wando. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the park’s diverse marine ecosystem, including coral reefs, rare species of fish, and other marine life.

Yeosu Sunrise Park

Yeosu Sunrise Park is a perfect place to catch the sunrise and enjoy stunning views of the sea. The park also offers various hiking trails of varying difficulty, making it an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haenam

Haenam is home to many scenic hiking and biking trails, including the Chilyeon Falls trail and the Borimsa Temple trail. These trails run through spectacular landscapes, providing opportunities for wildlife watching and bird spotting.

Best Things to Do in Haenam: Activities and Tours

Kayaking at Songjiho Lake

Songjiho Lake is among the most popular destinations in South Korea for kayaking enthusiasts. The serene lake surrounded by wooded mountains offers peaceful and relaxing kayaking experiences.

Haenam Bamboo Forest

Haenam Bamboo Forest provides a unique experience for visitors to walk through the lush green bamboo groves and take in the fresh air. The forest is quite popular among instagrammers because of its unique beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Haenam

Must-Try Dishes

Haenam Kalguksu

Haenam Kalguksu is a local specialty made with handmade noodles and a rich broth of beef or chicken. The dish is served with various vegetables like mushrooms, carrots, and green onions and is a must-try for anyone visiting Haenam.

Dakgukbap

Dakgukbap is a soup made with chicken, rice, vegetables, and ginseng. The dish is filling and perfect for a chilly night.

Local Beverages

Dongdongju

Dongdongju is a traditional rice wine made with fermented rice, water, and nuruk (a natural fermentation starter). The drink has a unique taste of sweet and sour, making it a popular drink amongst locals.

Makgeolli

Makgeolli is another traditional Korean drink made by fermenting rice with yeast and water. The drink has a slightly sweet and sour taste and is well-liked by visitors.

Where to Stay in Haenam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haenam offers various accommodation options to suit all types of visitors. From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, Haenam has plenty to offer.

Splurge

Haenam Eastern Tourist Hotel

Haenam Eastern Tourist Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city. The hotel offers stunning views of the sea and provides world-class amenities and services like a swimming pool, fitness center, spa, and restaurant.

Budget

Haenam Business Hotel

Haenam Business Hotel is an affordable hotel located in the city center. The hotel offers basic amenities like free Wi-Fi, TV, and breakfast.

What to Buy in Haenam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Haenam is known for its bamboo products, traditional handicrafts, and delicious Korean snacks. Visitors can buy gifts and souvenirs for their loved ones and carry a little bit of Haenam back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Haenam

Haenam is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly, take off their shoes before entering homes and temples, and show respect towards locals.

Best Time to Visit Haenam

The best time to visit Haenam is during the spring season from March to May and autumn from September to November. The weather during these seasons is mild and pleasant with beautiful cherry blossoms and fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haenam

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the scenic hiking trails of Haenam.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing to temples and palaces.
  • Touch historical artifacts.
  • Litter or leave trash around.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haenam

Haenam is a relatively safe destination. Visitors should take the necessary precautions to avoid accidents, including following traffic rules, monitoring weather conditions, and following strictly marked hiking trails.

Health in Haenam

Haenam has well-maintained healthcare facilities and hospitals. However, visitors should keep a first aid kit handy and take precautions against insect bites, sunburn, and overexertion during outdoor activities.

Getting to Haenam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Haenam is well-connected to all major cities in South Korea via various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Haenam

Muan International Airport is the closest airport to Haenam, located about 45 miles away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Haenam

The Haenam Bus Terminal offers various bus services to all major South Korean destinations, including Seoul, Busan, and Gwangju.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and intercity trains are also available for transportation.

How to Get Around in Haenam

Haenam is a small town, and the best way to get around is by walking or renting a bicycle to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Haenam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boseong Green Tea Plantation

Boseong Green Tea Plantation offers beautiful views of green tea fields sprawling across the mountains. Visitors can taste some delicious green teas and spend a day amidst lush greenery.

Yeosu

Yeosu, located about 30 miles from Haenam, is a vibrant coastal city famous for its stunning beaches, historic temples, and delicious seafood specialties.

Conclusion

Haenam is a picturesque town that offers the perfect blend of rich cultural heritage, scenic natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. With this travel guide, visitors can explore the best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Haenam, helping them to plan a memorable trip that will be cherished forever.
