Introduction to Jindo

Jindo is a beautiful small island located in South Jeolla, South Korea’s southwestern province. It is famous for its beautiful white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning natural landscapes, perfect for outdoor adventures.

But Jindo is not only about nature, but it also has a rich cultural heritage, as seen in its many historical sites and museums, showcasing the island’s history and traditions.

This comprehensive travel guide will give you a list of places to visit, things to do in Jindo, where to stay, what to buy, and local culture and customs to make your trip to Jindo a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Jindo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jindo Folk Custom Museum – This museum exhibits traditional clothing, tools, and household items of Jindo’s ancestors, showcasing Jindo’s history and culture.
  2. Haenyeo Museum – Haenyoe, also known as Jeju Island’s “women of the sea,” are female divers who collect marine products such as abalone, seaweed, and sea urchin. The Haenyeo Museum highlights the lives of these women and their contributions to the economic development of Jindo’s coastal communities.
  3. Jindo Bridge – Known to be the longest naval bridge in South Korea, the Jindo Bridge is a 2.5 km-long masterpiece that connects Jindo to the mainland.

Natural Attractions of Jindo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ssanggyesa Temple – a serene and peaceful Buddhist temple with an impressive 14 meter-high Buddha statue.
  2. Modo Island – Modo Island is located south of Jindo and is connected by a causeway bridge. It is famous for its beautiful beaches, stunning sunsets, and unspoiled natural landscapes, perfect for a day trip.
  3. Mihwangsa Temple – A magnificent temple that dates back to the Silla Dynasty, Mihwangsa is the perfect place to relax amidst the tranquil surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jindo

  1. Jindo Olle Trail – This 23.2 km trail is divided into two parts, offering spectacular views of the sea, mountains, and traditional villages of Jindo.
  2. Yeongsil Trail – Located within the vicinity of Ssanggyesa Temple, this trail is ideal for those who want a shorter hiking experience.

Best Things to Do in Jindo: Activities and Tours

  1. Jindo Sea parting – Every year, during the full moon, Jindo’s sea parts for a few hours, creating a 2.8 km pathway for visitors. It is truly an awe-inspiring natural phenomenon that draws people from all over the world.
  2. Jindo Dog Show – The Jindo Dog Show is famous for the breed of loyal and affectionate dogs unique to Jindo. It is a must-see event for animal-lovers.
  3. Kayaking – Discover the wondrous beauty of Jindo’s coastline and hidden coves by kayaking. You can also enjoy snorkeling and fishing during the trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Jindo

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Jindogae – A soup made of dog meat that is believed to boost stamina and energy, and is a local delicacy in Jindo.
  2. Ggomak Juk – A porridge made from bamboo sprouts, served as a breakfast dish.
  3. Seafood – Jindo seafood is among the freshest in South Korea, with sailfish, octopus, clams, and squid being the most popular.

Local Beverages

  1. Jindo andongju – Jindo’s own distilled liquor with a smooth, rich flavor.
  2. Shiboru – A fizzy, carbonated drink made from kudzu root, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Jindo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Jamboree Pension, located in the center of town, offers a comfortable and cozy stay with friendly service.
  2. Je-un Yeogwan is famous for its traditional Korean rooms with heated floors and excellent views of the sea.

What to Buy in Jindo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Jindo Blanket – The traditional woven blanket made entirely by hand is a perfect souvenir to take home.
  2. Jindo Black Garlic – Known to have numerous health benefits, Jindo Black Garlic is excellent for gifting friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Jindo

  1. Fishermen’s Dancing – Every year in May, fishermen perform a unique local dance on board their boats to celebrate the good harvest of the season.
  2. Shamanism – Shamanism worship is deeply rooted in Jindo’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Jindo

The best time to visit Jindo is between April and October when the climate is mild, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable. However, the unique natural phenomenon of Jindo sea parting takes place in May and June, making it the best time to witness the awe-inspiring sight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jindo

Do:

  1. Take your shoes off – It is customary to take off your shoes when entering homes, temples or guesthouses.
  2. Learn a few Korean words – Even learning basic phrases such as “hello,” “thank you,” and “goodbye” can be a delightful and practical way to show respect for the local culture.

Don’t:

  1. Do not cause disruption at night – Jindo is a quiet place at night, so it is best to keep noise levels to a minimum.
  2. Do not take unnecessary risks – Be careful and mindful when exploring natural sites and following the rules and guidelines set out by local authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jindo

Jindo is a relatively safe place with low crime rates. However, it is essential to be aware of basic safety precautions when exploring natural sites and taking part in outdoor activities.

Health in Jindo

It is recommended to ensure that you have up-to-date vaccinations and to have travel health insurance to cover potential medical emergencies while on your trip. It is also essential to carry sunscreen, insect repellent, and a first aid kit.

Getting to Jindo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jindo

Muan International Airport is the closest airport to Jindo, located 56 km from the island. From Muan Airport, you can take a taxi or bus to Jeolla-do Province’s Buan Bus Terminal, where you can catch a bus to Jindo.

Bus Stations Near Jindo

The easiest way to get to Jindo is by bus from Seoul, Busan, or other major South Korean cities. The most convenient bus terminal is the Jindo Bus Terminal, connected to other major destinations in South Korea.

Other Options

You can also take a ferry from Wando Port to Jindo Island, which takes approximately three hours.

How to Get Around in Jindo

The best way to explore Jindo Island is by car or bike. You can rent a car or bike at Jindo Bus Terminal or any other places near the terminal.

Where to Go Next from Jindo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Jeju Island – A popular destination known for its incredible natural landscapes, beaches, and iconic landmarks, such as the Hallasan Mountain and Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak. It is a perfect place for a day trip from Jindo, and you can take a flight, ferry, or bus to Jeju Island.
  2. Boseong Green Tea Fields – Located in Jeollanam-do Province, Boseong Green Tea Fields is a beautiful and serene landscape of tea fields and plantations, making it an ideal place for a day trip from Jindo.

Conclusion

Jindo is a unique and diverse place filled with natural wonders, cultural treasures, and delicious cuisine. It is an excellent destination for those seeking a new adventure that is off the beaten path. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now well-equipped to plan the ultimate Jindo itinerary, and it won’t disappoint!
