Naju is a charming city located in South Jeolla province, in the southwestern part of South Korea. Its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and local traditions attract visitors from around the world. If you’re planning a trip to Naju, here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Naju

Naju is a historic city that dates back to the Baekje Dynasty (18 BC-660 AD), and has played an important role in the political, cultural, and economic development of Korea. Today, Naju is known for its temples, museums, natural parks, and local festivals. The city has a population of around 100,000 people, and is located 219 kilometers southwest of Seoul.

List of Places to Visit in Naju

When it comes to attractions, Naju has something for everyone. From culturally significant sites to natural landscapes, here are some of the best places to visit in Naju.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Naju National Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Korea, including pottery, bronze ware, and Buddhist art.
  • Naju Image Theme Park: Explore traditional Korean art and culture through exhibitions, performances, and workshops.
  • Baekje Cultural Land: Experience the ancient Baekje Dynasty through a reconstructed palace, tombs, and fortress.
  • Mireuksaji Temple: Visit one of the oldest Buddhist temples in Korea, and see its beautiful architecture and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Naju: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baekyangsa Temple: Hike through the serene forest to reach this scenic temple, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Seomjingang River: Take a leisurely walk along the river, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere and natural beauty.
  • Wolchulsan National Park: Explore the diverse flora and fauna of this national park, which boasts waterfalls, peaks, and valleys.
  • Jangheung Reservoir: Enjoy fishing, camping, and watersports at this picturesque reservoir, which is surrounded by mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naju

Naju is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of hiking and biking routes for different skill levels. Some of the best ones include:

  • Naejangsan Course: Follow the trail through dense forests and rocky cliffs, and see the colorful autumn foliage.
  • Wolchulsan Course: Trek through rugged terrain and waterfalls, and enjoy panoramic views of the valley below.
  • Amnamdong Course: Cycle through the countryside, passing by traditional houses, rice paddies, and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Naju: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Naju, such as:

  • Join a Temple Stay program: Experience the life of a Buddhist monk, and learn about Korean culture and spirituality.
  • Watch the Naju Guksaeje Festival: Witness the traditional Korean martial arts performance, which showcases the skills and strength of the participants.
  • Visit the Naju Pear Festival: Taste the juicy and sweet pears that Naju is famous for, and enjoy the lively atmosphere and entertainment.
  • Take a pottery class: Learn the techniques and history of Korean pottery, and create your own masterpiece.
  • Try Hanjeongsik: Indulge in the Korean-style set meal, which consists of rice, soup, and a variety of side dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Naju

Korean cuisine is delicious and healthy, and Naju has its own specialties that you should try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Naju Pears: This juicy and sweet fruit is the most famous product of Naju, and is exported around the world.
  • Baekseju: This strong and sweet rice wine has medicinal properties, and is a popular local drink.
  • Jawoori: This spicy and savory soup is made with vegetables, meat, and seafood, and is a staple of Naju cuisine.
  • Gamgol Tteokgalbi: This grilled beef and pork patty is seasoned with sweet and spicy sauce, and is a popular comfort food.

Local Beverages

  • Korean Tea: Green tea, black tea, herbal tea, and fruit tea are popular drinks in Korea, and are served hot or cold.
  • Chungju: This clear liquor is made from rice, and has a mild and crisp flavor.
  • Makgeolli: This cloudy rice wine is slightly sweet and fizzy, and is often accompanied by spicy snacks.

  • Luxury: Ramada Plaza Gwangju, Savoy Hotel Naju
  • Mid-range: Naju Nara Hotel, Naju Apple Hotel, Hotel Cleopatra
  • Budget: Ssanggyesa Templestay, Backpackers in Naju, Pension Raja

What to Buy in Naju: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to bring some authentic Korean products back home? Here are some ideas.

  • Naju Pears: Pack some fresh pears or pear products, such as jams and candies, as a healthy and delicious present.
  • Traditional Crafts: Buy pottery, lacquerware, or textiles, which are handmade by local artisans and reflect the beauty and elegance of Korean culture.
  • Baekseju: Buy a bottle of this traditional rice wine, and enjoy its robust flavor and warming effect.

Local Culture and Customs of Naju

Korean people are known for their hospitality, politeness, and respect for traditions. Therefore, here are some tips to follow when visiting Naju.

  • Remove your shoes: When entering a temple, a house, or a traditional restaurant, take off your shoes as a sign of respect and cleanliness.
  • Bow and say hello: Greet the locals with a slight bow and a friendly “annyeonghaseyo”, which means “hello” in Korean.
  • Use both hands: When giving or receiving something, use both hands to show sincerity and attentiveness.
  • Try the local customs: Join in the local festivals, wear a hanbok (Korean traditional dress), and learn some Korean phrases to show your appreciation and openness.

Best Time to Visit Naju

Naju can be visited all year round, but the best time depends on your preferences and activities.

  • Spring (March-May): The weather is mild and pleasant, and the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.
  • Summer (June-August): The weather is hot and humid, but there are plenty of outdoor festivals and activities.
  • Autumn (September-November): The weather is cool and dry, and the foliage turns into vibrant colors.
  • Winter (December-February): The weather is cold and snowy, but the ski resorts and hot springs are open for business.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naju

Do:

  • Respect the elders: Show deference and politeness to the seniors, and don’t interrupt or contradict them.
  • Practice good hygiene: Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing, and wash your hands frequently to prevent the spread of germs.
  • Try the Korean etiquette: Use chopsticks and spoons correctly, pour drinks for others, and respect the dining hierarchy.
  • Be punctual: Arrive on time for meetings, tours, or appointments, and apologize if you’re late.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head: Avoid patting or touching someone’s head, as it’s considered disrespectful and invasive.
  • Point with your finger: Use your hand or chin to indicate a direction, as pointing with your finger is impolite.
  • Blow your nose in public: Use a tissue or go to the restroom instead of blowing your nose in front of others.
  • Refuse a drink or food: It’s customary to accept an offer of hospitality, even if you don’t like the drink or food.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naju

Naju is generally a safe city, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, you should be aware of some potential risks.

  • Pedestrian safety: Be careful when crossing the street, as some drivers may not respect pedestrian signals.
  • Extreme weather: Naju can experience typhoons, floods, and landslides, especially in the summer and autumn seasons.
  • Traffic accidents: Traffic can be heavy and chaotic, especially during rush hours, and accidents can happen.

Health in Naju

Naju has a good healthcare system, with modern facilities and competent doctors. However, you should take some precautions to maintain your health.

  • Water quality: Drink boiled or bottled water, and avoid tap water or food that may have been rinsed or washed in tap water.
  • Sun protection: Use sunscreen or wear a hat, sunglasses, and light clothing to avoid sunburn or heat exhaustion.
  • Mosquito prevention: Use insect repellent or mosquito nets, especially during the summer season, to prevent mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Naju: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naju

  • Gwangju Airport: Located 30 kilometers south of Naju, Gwangju Airport has domestic flights to Seoul and Jeju Island.

Bus Stations Near Naju

  • Naju Bus Terminal: Located downtown, Naju Bus Terminal has buses to major cities in Korea, such as Seoul, Busan, Gwangju, and Jeonju.

Other Options

  • Train: Although there’s no train station in Naju, you can take a train from Gwangju Station or Mokpo Station, and then transfer to a bus or taxi to Naju.
  • Car rental: If you prefer driving, you can rent a car at the airport or in major cities, and then follow the highway signs to Naju.

How to Get Around in Naju

Naju has a convenient and affordable public transportation system, which includes:

  • City buses: There are several bus routes that cover the main areas of Naju, and the fare ranges from 1,000 to 2,000 won.
  • Taxi: Taxis are easy to find in Naju, and the fare starts at 2,800 won.
  • Private van: If you’re in a group, you can hire a private van or car for a customized tour, and negotiate the price with the driver.

Where to Go Next from Naju: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Naju is a good base for exploring other destinations in South Jeolla and beyond. Some of the nearby attractions include:

  • Mokpo: This coastal city is known for its seafood, beaches, and maritime culture, and has a ferry to Jeju Island.
  • Suncheon: This eco-city is famous for its bay wetlands, bamboo forest, and film set of the Korean drama “Love in the Moonlight”.
  • Jeonju: This Hanok (traditional house) village is a UNESCO World Heritage site, and has a diverse food scene, ranging from bibimbap to street snacks.

Conclusion

Naju is a hidden gem in South Korea, offering a unique blend of history, nature, and local traditions. Whether you’re a culture buff, a foodie, or an adventurer, Naju has something to offer. Follow this comprehensive guide, and make your trip to Naju a memorable one.
