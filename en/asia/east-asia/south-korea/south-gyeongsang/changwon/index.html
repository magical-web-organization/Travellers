Introduction to Changwon

Changwon, located in South Gyeongsang province, is a hidden gem of South Korea. It is a perfect blend of the old and the new, giving travelers the chance to experience the distinct Korean culture alongside modern amenities.

The city is known for its rich history, scenic surroundings, and welcoming locals. It is also home to the Changwon National University, which makes it a youthful and vibrant city. Whether you are a history buff, adventure-seeker, or just looking for a relaxing and culturally enriching getaway, Changwon has something for you.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Changwon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Masan Fish Market

The Masan Fish Market, located in the southern part of Changwon, is an exciting and unique place to visit. It is South Korea’s largest fish market and one of the most popular attractions in the city. You can experience the hustle and bustle of the market and sample fresh seafood at its many restaurants.

2. Changwon Exhibition Convention Center (CECO)

The Changwon Exhibition Convention Center is a cultural hub in Changwon that hosts a variety of events throughout the year. It also features an art museum that showcases contemporary art from local and international artists.

3. Changwon National Museum

If you love history, the Changwon National Museum should be on your must-visit list. It has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that narrate the history and culture of South Gyeongsang province.

Natural Attractions of Changwon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Yongji Park

Yongji Park, located in the city center, is a charming green space with a serene lake, hiking trails, and scenic views of Changwon. It is ideal for a relaxing picnic or a morning workout.

2. Gyeongnam Goseong Dinosaur Museum

The Gyeongnam Goseong Dinosaur Museum is a fascinating place to visit for families and anyone interested in prehistoric creatures. It has a vast collection of dinosaur fossils and reconstructs and provides interactive exhibits.

3. Jinhae Cherry Blossom Festival

The Jinhae Cherry Blossom Festival in early April draws visitors from around the world who come to see the pink cherry blossoms that bloom across the city. It is also an opportunity to experience Korean culture and food.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changwon

If you love outdoor adventures, Changwon has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the most popular options include:

  • The Mt. Bulmo-Sanbong trail that leads to the top of Mt. Bulmo for panoramic views of the city and the sea
  • The Mt. Sangdangsan-seonghyeon Trail that winds through a historical fortress and offers stunning views of the natural surroundings
  • The Yongji Lake Trail that provides a leisurely stroll or bike ride around the lake in Yongji Park

Best Things to Do in Changwon: Activities and Tours

1. Join a Korean Cooking Class

Korean cuisine is famous worldwide for its unique flavors and variety. If you are a foodie, joining a Korean cooking class is a great way to learn about the country’s traditional dishes and techniques.

2. Take a Temple Stay

Changwon is home to several Buddhist temples, and taking a temple stay is an excellent way to experience and learn more about the Buddhist culture in South Korea. It also allows you to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and find peace in a serene setting.

3. Visit the Jinhae Naval Port

The Jinhae Naval Port is a popular destination for history buffs and anyone interested in military history. You can see and explore the old warships, submarines, and other naval artifacts.

Food and Drink Scene of Changwon

Must-Try Dishes

1. Eoriguljeot

Eoriguljeot is a fermented seafood dish that is a staple of the Masan Fish Market. It is made from baby octopus, shrimps, and squid, which is left to ferment for several months to produce a unique and pungent flavor.

2. Hoe

Hoe is raw fish that is thinly sliced and served with a variety of herbs and sauces. It is a popular Korean dish that is enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

3. Tteokgalbi

Tteokgalbi is a Korean beef short rib that is marinated in soy sauce, garlic, and other herbs and grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

1. Makgeolli

Makgeolli is a Korean rice wine that is mildly sweet and tangy. It is often served in traditional Korean bowls and is a refreshing drink to accompany Korean meals.

2. Soju

Soju is a clear, distilled Korean spirit that has an alcohol content of around 20%. It is commonly consumed with Korean barbeque and is available in many flavors, including green grape and peach.

What to Buy in Changwon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Changwon Bamboo Salt

Changwon Bamboo Salt is an all-natural and organic salt that is made by roasting salt crystals inside bamboo stalks, giving it a distinct smoky flavor. It is a popular souvenir that is often given as a gift to family and friends.

2. Jukryeojiri

Jukryeojiri is a traditional Korean tea that is made from Korean Thistle. It is believed to have many health benefits, including improved digestion and a stronger immune system.

Local Culture and Customs of Changwon

Koreans are very proud of their culture, and they have a rich and unique history that is reflected in their customs and traditions. Here are some pointers to keep in mind when visiting Changwon:

  • Bowing is a common gesture of respect in Korean culture
  • Removing shoes before entering a home or temple is a custom followed by Koreans
  • Korean elders are highly respected and should be addressed politely
  • Korean dining etiquette is different from western cultures; it is respectful to wait for everyone to be seated before eating

Best Time to Visit Changwon

The best time to visit Changwon is during the spring (March-May) when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. The weather is mild, and the colorful blossoms make for stunning photographs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Changwon

Do:

  • Learn some basic Korean phrases before traveling to Changwon
  • Try the local cuisine, including Korean barbeque and seafood dishes
  • Respect local customs and etiquette

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or cause a disturbance in public places
  • Wear shoes inside temples, homes, or traditional Korean restaurants
  • Ignore elders or talk back to them

Safety and Health

Safety in Changwon

Changwon is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, it is always advisable to exercise caution, especially when traveling alone at night or carrying valuables in crowded areas.

Health in Changwon

Changwon has excellent healthcare facilities, including public and private hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Most healthcare professionals speak English and are trained to treat foreign patients.

Getting to Changwon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Changwon

The closest airport to Changwon is Busan’s Gimhae International Airport, which is around a 40-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Changwon

Changwon has two major bus terminals: the Changwon-Pungam Express Bus Terminal and the Changwon Intercity Bus Terminal. They offer bus services to major cities in South Korea, including Seoul, Busan, and Daegu.

Other Options

Changwon is also accessible by train, with the Changwon station being the main railway hub. The city also has an efficient local bus system, and taxis are readily available.

How to Get Around in Changwon

Changwon has an efficient public transportation system, including buses and subways. Taxis are also readily available and relatively inexpensive compared to other major cities in South Korea. It’s best to use a transportation map or ask locals for help when getting around.

Where to Go Next from Changwon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Changwon is well-connected to many major cities and towns in South Korea, making it a great base for exploring nearby destinations. Some of the most popular excursions include:

  • Haeundae Beach in Busan
  • Tongyeong City and the Geoje Island
  • Gyeongju’s ancient ruins and historical landmarks

Conclusion

Changwon is a lesser-known but culturally rich and naturally stunning city in South Korea. It has something to offer every traveler, from historical landmarks and museums to outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine. Next time you’re planning a trip to South Korea, make sure to include Changwon in your itinerary.
