Introduction to South Gyeongsang

South Gyeongsang, located in the southeastern part of South Korea, is a region steeped in history and culture. This province, also known as “Gyeongnam,” is famous for its gorgeous beaches, mountains, and temples. It is home to several UNESCO World Heritage sites, including the ancient capital city of Gyeongju.

List of Places to Visit in South Gyeongsang

Whether you are looking for cultural experiences or outdoor adventures, South Gyeongsang has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in this beautiful province:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gyeongju Historic Areas: The ancient capital city of Gyeongju is a must-visit destination for those interested in Korean history and culture. This UNESCO World Heritage site boasts an impressive collection of temples, tombs, and artifacts from the Silla Dynasty.

  2. Tongyeong: This coastal city is known as the “Naples of Korea” and is home to several cultural attractions, including the Yi Sun-sin Park and the Dongpirang Wall Painting Village.

  3. Jinju Fortress: This impressive fortress dates back to the Joseon Dynasty and is located in the city of Jinju. Visitors can explore the fortress walls, gates, and pavilions while taking in the stunning views of the Namgang River.

Natural Attractions of South Gyeongsang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hallyeo Maritime National Park: This park is located along the southern coast of the province and offers visitors stunning views of the sea and islands. It is home to several hiking trails, beaches, and fishing villages.

  2. Jiri Mountain: This iconic mountain range is a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is home to several waterfalls and temples.

  3. Namhae Island: This picturesque island is known for its beautiful beaches, cliffs, and forests. It is a popular destination for camping, hiking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Gyeongsang

  1. Saryangdo Island: This island located off the coast of Tongyeong offers visitors a scenic hiking trail that takes them through a bamboo forest and up to the summit of the island.

  2. Jeongseon Recreational Forest: This forest is located in the city of Changwon and offers visitors several hiking trails through the scenic woodland.

  3. Gyeongju National Park: This park is home to several hiking trails that take visitors through the historic city of Gyeongju and offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in South Gyeongsang: Activities and Tours

  1. Learn about the history of the Silla Dynasty with a visit to the Gyeongju National Museum.

  2. Take a walk through the picturesque Dongpirang Wall Painting Village in Tongyeong.

  3. Admire the stunning views of the sea and islands with a scenic drive along the coastal roads.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Gyeongsang

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to South Gyeongsang:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historic city of Gyeongju, including the Gyeongju National Museum and Bulguksa Temple.
Day 2 Visit the rocky cliffs and beautiful beaches of Namhae Island.
Day 3 Enjoy a hike in the picturesque Jiri Mountain range.
Day 4 Take a walk through the Dongpirang Wall Painting Village in Tongyeong.
Day 5 Relax on the beaches of Hallyeo Maritime National Park.
Day 6 Take a tour of the Jinju Fortress and enjoy the views of the Namgang River.
Day 7 Explore the charming city of Changwon and enjoy a hike in the Jeongseon Recreational Forest.

Food and Drink Scene of South Gyeongsang

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ssambap: This traditional Korean dish consists of steamed rice wrapped in lettuce leaves and topped with various sautéed vegetables.

  2. Guamegi: This dish is a local specialty of Tongyeong and consists of fresh raw fish marinated in soy sauce and served with vegetables.

  3. Jokbal: This dish features boiled pig trotters seasoned with soy sauce, sugar, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  1. Makgeolli: This traditional Korean rice wine is made by fermenting rice, wheat, and water. It has a slightly sweet flavor and is often served with savory dishes.

  2. Soju: This popular Korean liquor is similar to vodka and is often served with meals or used as a base for cocktails.

Where to Stay in South Gyeongsang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hilton Gyeongju: This luxury hotel offers guests fantastic views of the Bomun Lake and is located close to several cultural attractions.

  2. Khaosan Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the heart of Changwon and offers dormitory-style rooms as well as private rooms.

What to Buy in South Gyeongsang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hanji paper: This traditional paper is made from the inner bark of the mulberry tree and is used for calligraphy, bookbinding, and other crafts.

  2. Tea: South Gyeongsang is famous for its green tea, which is grown in the scenic Jirisan Mountain region.

Local Culture and Customs of South Gyeongsang

South Korea is a country steeped in tradition and has many customs and etiquette rules. It is important to be respectful of these customs and follow them while visiting South Gyeongsang.

Best Time to Visit South Gyeongsang

The best time to visit South Gyeongsang is in the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November). The weather is mild and pleasant during these seasons, and the scenery is especially beautiful due to the blooming flowers and autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Gyeongsang

Do:

  1. Bow when greeting someone.

  2. Take off your shoes when entering a Korean home or temple.

  3. Cover your mouth when eating or drinking.

Don’t:

  1. Touch someone’s head or shoulder without permission.

  2. Talk loudly in public places.

  3. Point with your feet or use your fingers to beckon someone.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Gyeongsang

South Korea is a very safe country with a low crime rate. However, it is still important to take precautions to stay safe while traveling.

Health in South Gyeongsang

South Korea has a modern healthcare system with many clinics and hospitals located throughout the country. However, it is still important to purchase travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to South Gyeongsang: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Gyeongsang is easily accessible by train, bus, and airplane.

Airports Near South Gyeongsang

  1. Gimhae International Airport: This airport is located in Busan and is the closest airport to most destinations in South Gyeongsang.

  2. Jinju Airport: This small regional airport is located in the city of Jinju and offers domestic flights within South Korea.

Bus Stations Near South Gyeongsang

  1. Changwon Express Bus Terminal: This terminal is located in the heart of Changwon and offers frequent buses to destinations throughout South Korea.

  2. Gyeongju Express Bus Terminal: This terminal is located in the historic city of Gyeongju and offers buses to nearby cities and attractions.

Other Options

There are several high-speed train stations located throughout South Gyeongsang, including the Dongdaegu Station and Gyeongju Station.

How to Get Around in South Gyeongsang

The best way to get around South Gyeongsang is by public transportation, including buses and trains. Taxis are also readily available in most cities.

Where to Go Next from South Gyeongsang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Busan: This vibrant city is located just north of South Gyeongsang and offers visitors fantastic shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

  2. Jeju Island: This stunning island is located off the southern coast of South Korea and is known for its scenic beauty and unique culture.

Conclusion

South Gyeongsang is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean history, culture, and natural beauty. From the stunning temples and ruins of the ancient Silla Dynasty to the picturesque beaches and mountains, there is something for everyone in this beautiful province.
