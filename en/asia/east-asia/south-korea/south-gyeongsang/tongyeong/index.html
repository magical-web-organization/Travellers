Introduction to Tongyeong

Tongyeong is a charming coastal city located in South Gyeongsang, South Korea. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious seafood cuisine, Tongyeong is a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience a unique side of South Korea.

The city sits on a peninsula and has over 200 islands around it. With its mild climate and beautiful coastal scenery, Tongyeong is a popular vacation spot for locals as well as tourists from around the world. You won’t be disappointed with what Tongyeong has to offer, from culturally historic sites and engaging outdoor activities to delicious seafood and beautiful scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Tongyeong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in South Korean history and culture, you will find plenty of fascinating museums and historical sites in Tongyeong. Below are some of the top attractions you can visit to learn more about the city’s past:

  • Tongyeong Jungang Market: Known as the center of traditional Korean culture, Jungang Market is the oldest market in Tongyeong. You’ll get a true glimpse of Korean culture and history by visiting the market, as it not only offers an opportunity to shop but showcases traditional food, art, and crafts.
  • Dongpirang Village: You’ll love the colorful murals and narrow alleys of Dongpirang Village, which is situated on the hillside overlooking Tongyeong Harbor. A vibrant and lively place, the village emerged out of a community-led initiative to revitalize the area and preserve its cultural heritage.
  • Tongyeong City Bus Tour: Take a guided bus tour around the city to get a comprehensive understanding of Tongyeong’s history and way of life. The tour includes stops at the major historical sites and museums throughout the city.

Natural Attractions of Tongyeong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For adventure seekers and nature lovers, Tongyeong offers a diverse outdoor playground that is hard to resist. From kayaking in the crystal-clear water to hiking the hillsides for panoramic views, Tongyeong caters well to those who want to be immersed in natural environments. Below are the top attractions for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Tongyeong Cable Car: An aerial tramway that takes you over the channels and islands of Tongyeong, the cable car is a must-do activity when visiting. You’ll enjoy sweeping views of the surrounding natural beauty and panoramic vistas of the city below from the top.
  • Hallyeo Waterway Observation Cable Car: A 1.5km-long aerial tramway that sails over the sea, offering an unbeatable view of the surrounding islands and the city, and potential sightings of dolphins.
  • Saryangdo Island: With its unique geological formations, hiking trails dotted with wildflowers, and stunning views of the ocean and sky, Saryangdo is a nature lover’s paradise. If you’re feeling adventurous, try the Saryangdo Sky Walk which is shaped like a boat and offers the thrill of walking across the deep blue sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tongyeong

Tongyeong boasts of hikes made for all levels. The city offers serene paths for beginners and children or steep and challenging ones with incredible rewards for experienced hikers. Below are some trails to consider:

  • Mt. Mireuksan: A 670m-high mountain in the heart of Tongyeong, Mt. Mireuksan is a great spot for mountain climbing enthusiasts. The mountain has a challenging trail, fantastic views and unfolds an opportunity to take in the mountain’s rich history and significance seen at the Tombs of King Soji and King Gyeonghae historical sites.
  • Dongbok Namsan Mountain: A quiet, less crowded mountain that offers incredible views of the city and the entire harbor. Perfect for an early morning trek to greet the sunrise, you can begin from downtown Tongyeong or along the Hallyeosudo Viewing Ropeway trail.
  • Bieumdo Island Trail: Nestled between the ocean and the mountains, Bieumdo Island is a tranquil paradise for hikers with a 9.9km trail. The peak stands at 282m and takes about three hours to reach the top, making it a moderately easy trail for visitors.

Best Things to Do in Tongyeong: Activities and Tours

Tongyeong is filled with exciting activities that can keep visitors occupied for weeks. Below are some of the top things to do while visiting this lovely coastal town.

  • Boat Tours to Nearby Islands: With around 200 islands surrounding the town, boat tours are the perfect means of exploring the sea, with a host of boat tours available. Haegeumgang Island, Gwanmaedo Island, and Somaemuldo Island are popular spots for visitors, and usually, organized tour operators include stops at nearby attractions too.
  • Local Seafood Tasting: Famous for its wide range of seafood delicacies, Tongyeong offers visitors a chance to indulge in delicious meals of squid, octopus, fish, eel, and shrimp cuisines. The Jungang Market is a popular seafood market; visitors can watch fishermen unload their catches boats, buy fresh seafood, and try the raw squid rice bowl or grilled king prawns.
  • Tongyeong City Festival: Held annually, the Tongyeong City Festival showcases the city’s tradition and culture, with live performances, music and street food. Visitors can enjoy a traditional costume experience, wine tasting, paintings by local artists, and fireworks over the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Tongyeong

Tongyeong is typically famous for its seafood, with a wide range of dishes available that are fresh, delicious and worth indulging. Visitors to Tongyeong can dive into a long list of seafood delicacies, including grilled or fried fish, shrimp, octopus, raw squid, and clam soups.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gaejang-guk: A soup with blue crabs, soy sauce, garlic, and other seasonings.
  • Dombe-gi: A fried fish that’s typically seasoned with garlic and salt.
  • Eoriguljeot: A side dish made from freshly fermented squid.
  • Squid Anjeon-ghui: A raw squid dish that seasoned with sesame oil and chili paste.

Local Beverages

Tongyeong also offers up delicious beverages to enjoy with food, local restaurants, or convenience stores quickly accessible. One popular drink that visitors should try is the yachae-jeongol (a broth with vegetables, rice cakes, and fish) and plum extract cans that are readily available throughout the city.

Where to Stay in Tongyeong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tongyeong has a variety of lodging options to suit various budgets; whether you’re looking for top-rated for design or good value for cost, discover the best places to rest below:

  • Vin Hotel Tongyeong: A modern, 4-star hotel that’s famous for its deluxe rooms, spa, and comfortable beddings. It’s situated near the beach.
  • Naneun Nara Hotel: A prestigious hotel that’s loved for its Korean ondol rooms and emphasizes traditional architecture. There’s a massive landscaped garden and sea views, perfect for a sunrise or sunset experience.
  • Old Harbor Guesthouse: A delightful and inviting guesthouse tucked away in the alleyway of Yongho-dong, this property serves up cozy traditional Korean-style accommodations, affordable rates, and scenic water views.

What to Buy in Tongyeong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home different traditional souvenirs from Tongyeong, ranging from seafood-based treats to ceramics, textiles, and other local handicrafts. Here are some popular souvenirs:

  • Squid: Packets of seasoned and dried squid are popular as they last long and make a great gift for foodies.
  • Ceramics: Celebrities praise the ceramic arts of Tongyeong, including Yeoju ceramics, which are a souvenir favorite.
  • Traditional Fabrics: They are locally made and include a variety of traditional designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Tongyeong

Tongyeong has its traditional customs and culture, which date back to ancient times. The city prides itself on its traditional Korean heritage, preserved through the generations. Visitors should look out for points of local customs, including clothing styles, particular foods, religion, and more.

Best Time to Visit Tongyeong

Tongyeong has different colors for all seasons, offering the best nature scenery per weather conditions. The city’s peak tourist time is from May to September during the warmer months, while winter months offer unforgettable snow-covered hiking routes for hikers. However, some of the city’s museums and other indoor attractions are only open during the peak tourist-season, so it’s advisable to check availability accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tongyeong

Do:

  • Research before a trip so you can become more familiar with the local customs and traditions.
  • Pack accordingly, with comfortable shoes and warm clothes if you’re traveling in winter.
  • Try all the delicious seafood dishes.
  • Enjoy an aerial tour of the area via the Hallyeo Waterway Observation Cable Car, which offers breathtaking views of the area.

Don’t:

  • Try not to wear anything overboard and flashy, keeping to a modest dress approach.
  • Don’t talk loudly, disturb others or litter.
  • Avoid disrupting natural reserves or trails, especially during nature walks.
  • Don’t eat or drink while walking around.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tongyeong

Tongyeong is a safe and friendly place to visit, with low crime rates and highly visible and responsive local security measures. Visitors should adhere to general safety practices, such as locking their accommodations and avoiding leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Tongyeong

Tongyeong has modern medical facilities and doctors, making it relatively safe and convenient for travelers. Visitors should still stock up with necessary medication and insurance, as well as plan for avoiding mosquito bites if traveling in warmer months.

Getting to Tongyeong: Transportation Options and Logistics

To get to Tongyeong, visitors will generally land in one of the major South Korean airports, either Seoul/Incheon Airport or Busan Airport, which offers further transportation options.

Airports Near Tongyeong

  • Seoul/Incheon Airport: The airport is approximately 400km away from the city center of Tongyeong, and it takes around four to five hours via bus or train. Taxis and private transfers are also available.
  • Busan Airport: The airport is 115km from Tongyeong, and the trip takes about two hours by bus, making it the most convenient option. Visitors can also choose to take a taxi or a private transfer from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Tongyeong

Tongyeong is easily accessible via intercity bus routes from major centers throughout South Korea, including:

  • Seoul: Visitors can take an express bus from Seoul Central Bus Terminal or other Seoul bus stations, with a journey time of approximately 4-5 hours.
  • Busan: Visitors can catch direct buses from Busan’s Central Bus Terminal, with a travel time of approximately 2-3 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider taking the KTX (Korea Train Express), South Korea’s high-speed rail, to Tongyeong. Alternatively, ferry services provide direct access to Tongyeong from Busan’s international cruise terminal or from southern islands like Jeju.

How to Get Around in Tongyeong

Getting around Tongyeong is easy, with several modes of public and private transportation. Visitors can choose from buses, taxis, scooters, and rental cars to move around the city depending on their budget and preference.

Where to Go Next from Tongyeong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

A beautiful thing with Tongyeong’s prime location is the brevity of travel time to neighboring regions such as Geoje, where visitors can relax on beautiful beaches or engage in activities like hiking.

Visitors to Tongyeong would enjoy the wide variety of world-class seafood, outdoor activities, and local culture, the perfect combination for a relaxing vacation.
