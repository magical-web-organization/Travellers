Introduction to Hyangsan County

Hyangsan County, located in Pyongan in North Korea, offers a unique perspective into the country’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Hyangsan County is home to the famous Mount Myohyang, a symbol of North Korea, and offers breathtaking views, diverse wildlife, and historical landmarks.

This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with the best places to visit, hike/bike routes, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Hyangsan County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pohyonsa Temple: A 1,000-year-old Buddhist temple that houses several cultural relics, including rare paintings and sculptures.
  • International Friendship Exhibition: A massive museum dedicated to showcasing gifts given to the North Korean leaders over the years.
  • Manpukuji Temple: A unique Japanese-style temple that was built in the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Hyangsan County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Myohyang: The most famous attraction in Hyangsan County, Mount Myohyang is over 2,000 meters high and offers stunning views and diverse wildlife.
  • Ryongmun Caves: A stunning cave system that is popular with tourists for its natural beauty and impressive stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Kuryong Falls: A beautiful waterfall that is easily accessible from Mount Myohyang and offers a peaceful escape from the bustling city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hyangsan County

  • Mount Myohyang Trail: This trail is over 14 km long and takes hikers to the peak of Mount Myohyang. It’s a challenging hike that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Chilsong Gate Trail: This trail is 6 km long and takes you through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Hyangsan County. It’s a great option for leisurely hiking and offers scenic views of the region.
  • Bike Trail to Botanist’s Peak: This 12 km long trail takes you to the top of Botanist’s Peak, a popular destination among botanists and hikers alike. The route goes past vibrant fields of wildflowers and offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys.

Best Things to Do in Hyangsan County: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the River: The scenic river in Hyangsan County is perfect for an adrenaline-filled day of rafting. Several tour operators offer rafting trips that take you through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the region.
  • Skiing in Mount Myohyang: Mount Myohyang provides the perfect terrain for skiing, and several ski resorts operate around the mountain. The season typically runs from December to February.
  • Visit Traditional Villages: Hyangsan County is home to several traditional Korean villages that offer a glimpse into the local culture and customs, including Chilbosan, Dongmu, and Happy Mountain Village.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hyangsan County

Day 1-2: Visit Mount Myohyang and Pohyonsa Temple Day 3-4: Hiking/Biking Trails to Botanist’s Peak and Chilsong Gate Day 5: Visit International Friendship Exhibition and Manpukuji Temple Day 6: Enjoy skiing at Mount Myohyang or rafting on the river Day 7: Explore local villages and Ryongmun Caves

Food and Drink Scene of Hyangsan County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jjigae: A thick soup made from a variety of meats, vegetables, and tofu.
  • Pyongyang Cold Noodles: A refreshing dish of cold noodles in a tangy broth.
  • Naengmyeon: A dish of chewy noodles in a savory broth with vegetables and a boiled egg.

Local Beverages

  • Soju: A Korean distilled spirit that is present in almost all social gatherings
  • Beer: North Korea has two domestically produced beers – Taedonggang and Ryongsong, which are available in most restaurants.
  • Insam-cha: A traditional Korean tea made from ginseng, with medicinal properties and an earthy flavor.

Where to Stay in Hyangsan County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hyangsan Hotel: A luxury hotel that offers spectacular views of Mount Myohyang and comfortable rooms.
  • Chongchon Hotel: A government-owned hotel known for its spacious rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Jangsusan Hotel: A traditional Korean-style guesthouse that offers a unique cultural experience.

What to Buy in Hyangsan County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ginseng: Known as one of the healthiest herbs in the world, North Korea is famed for producing some of the best ginseng in the world.
  • Korean stamps: North Korea has a very unique and beautiful stamp collection which is popular amongst collectors.
  • Kimchi: The fermented dish enjoyed across Korea is considered a must-bring item from the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Hyangsan County

  • Hospitality: North Koreans are known for being hospitable and welcoming, and visitors should be ready to receive a warm welcome throughout their stay.
  • Bowing: When greeting someone, the traditional Korean bow is used instead of shaking hands, women typically do it with hands stayed near their side.

Best Time to Visit Hyangsan County

The best time to visit Hyangsan County is during spring and autumn, from April to June, and September to November. The weather is mild, and the natural landscapes are gorgeous during these months. Summer is peak season, so it can get crowded, and winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hyangsan County

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn some basic Korean phrases before your visit, locals appreciate it when visitors make an effort to communicate.
  • Respect the government regulations and travel with an authorized guide.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their explicit consent or photograph military personnel, installations, or equipment.
  • Express a negative opinion on politics or religion.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hyangsan County

Hyangsan County is relatively safe for tourists. You should always exercise caution and take the necessary precautions when traveling to any foreign country. Avoid all travel to areas near the border, as they are known to be heavily militarized.

Health in Hyangsan County

Before traveling to Hyangsan County, you should be up to date on routine vaccinations. Visitors should avoid tap water and bottled water should only be consumed if it’s from a reputable brand. You should note that medical care in North Korea is limited, and visitors should bring proper medications in case of emergencies.

Getting to Hyangsan County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hyangsan County

The closest airport to Hyangsan County is Pyongyang International Airport (FNJ). From here, you can take a taxi or a bus to reach the county.

Bus Stations Near Hyangsan County

Several buses run between Pyongyang and Hyangsan County. The journey takes around four hours and costs approximately $10-$15 per person.

Other Options

Private cars are available for rent in Pyongyang, and you can hire a driver to take you around Hyangsan County. Alternatively, several tours and travel agencies specialize in organizing tours within the region.

How to Get Around in Hyangsan County

The best way to get around Hyangsan County is on foot, especially when visiting the natural attractions and hiking trails. Taxis are available for hire, but prices can be high, and a local guide is always recommended.

Where to Go Next from Hyangsan County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pyongyang: The capital city of North Korea, it has several cultural, historical, and political landmarks, notably the Arch of Triumph, the Juche Tower, and the grand Kim Il-sung Square.
  • Sariwon: A perfect destination for foodies to visit, Sariwon is renowned for its traditional Korean dishes like bulgogi, kalguksu, and naengmyeon.

Conclusion

Hyangsan County is a fascinating destination that offers travelers a unique perspective into North Korean history, culture, and natural beauty. With plenty of activities, food, and outdoor excursions to explore, Hyangsan County is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an extraordinary travel experience. Plan your trip in advance to make the most of your stay and enjoy all that Hyangsan County has to offer!
