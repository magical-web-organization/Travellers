Introduction to Pyongyang

Pyongyang is the capital city of North Korea and is known for its monumental architecture, grand statues, and well-maintained gardens. The city has a rich cultural history and offers tourists a fascinating glimpse into life in North Korea. With its unique traditions, awe-inspiring natural beauty, and charming street scenes, Pyongyang is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience something truly different.

List of Places to Visit in Pyongyang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pyongyang has a wealth of cultural attractions including museums, galleries, and historical buildings. Here are some of the most popular:

  • The Korean Central History Museum: This museum is a showcase of the country’s history and culture. It features ancient artifacts, medieval weapons, and displays on the birth of the Korean people.
  • The Grand People’s Study House: This is North Korea’s largest library, and it is open to the public. Visitors can enjoy a stunning view of the city from the rooftop terrace, and check out the impressive collection of books and original North Korean literature.
  • The Tower of the Juche Idea: This tower celebrates the ideology of Juche, which is central to North Korea’s national identity. The tower stands at 170 meters tall and offers stunning views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Pyongyang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond its museums and historical places, Pyongyang also has an abundance of natural beauty, outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best options:

  • Mount Taesong: This mountain in the heart of Pyongyang offers stunning views of the city below. It’s popular with hikers and nature lovers, and there are a number of trails to explore.
  • Mangyongdae Native House: This is the birthplace of Kim Il-sung, the founder of North Korea. The house is located in the scenic countryside and offers a glimpse into the leader’s early life.
  • Ryongwang Pavilion: This pavilion is located on Moran Hill in central Pyongyang and offers classic Korean views. It has been restored many times over the years, and the current version dates back to the 17th century.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pyongyang

Pyongyang has a number of hiking and biking routes to choose from. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Ryongak-san Trail: This trail starts on the southern edge of the city and follows the river along the way. It’s a great option for beginners, and offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding area.
  • Pyongyang-Taesong Trail: This trail is a more demanding option, and takes you through some of the most scenic parts of Pyongyang. It’s a great way to explore the city and adjacent mountains.
  • Tongil Street Route: This route is a popular cycling option, with stunning views of the city and lot of places to stop and take photos.

Best Things to Do in Pyongyang: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to see and do in Pyongyang. Here are some suggestions:

  • Watch a Mass Games Show: This is one of North Korea’s most famous cultural events. It features thousands of performers showcasing the country’s history and culture.
  • Visit the Mansudae Grand Monument: This monument is dedicated to the country’s two former leaders, Kim Il-sung and Kim Jong-il.
  • Explore Moranbong Park: This park is located in central Pyongyang and features a large lake, pavilions, and scenic paths.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pyongyang

Here is an example of a week-long itinerary that you can follow for your trip to Pyongyang:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Pyongyang, visit the Korean Central History Museum
  • Day 2: Explore the Moranbong Park and the Grand People’s Study House
  • Day 3: Visit Mount Taesong and take a hike up the mountain
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Mansudae Grand Monument and the Ryongwang Pavilion
  • Day 5: Explore the city’s markets and souvenir shops
  • Day 6: Enjoy a Mass Games show or other cultural events, such as a North Korean opera
  • Day 7: Departure from Pyongyang

Food and Drink Scene of Pyongyang

Must-Try Dishes

North Korean cuisine is quite unique, with Korean, Chinese, and Russian influences. Here are some dishes you must try:

  • Kimchi: This spicy pickled cabbage dish is a staple in North Korean cuisine.
  • Ginseng Chicken Soup: Soup featuring chicken stuffed with ginseng, and is a unique North Korean twist on a classic Korean dish.
  • Naengmyeon: This is a cold noodle dish which is perfect to try in the summer months.

Local Beverages

North Korea has its own selection of beverages for the thirsty traveler, such as:

  • Taedonggang Beer: This locally brewed beer is considered one of the best in the country.
  • Soju: This is a local spirit similar to vodka, and is often consumed after meals.

Where to Stay in Pyongyang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pyongyang has a number of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to choose from. Here are some options to consider:

  • Ryugyong Hotel: This iconic hotel offers a unique experience, with stunning views of the city and luxurious amenities.
  • Koryo Hotel: This hotel is centrally located and offers a range of room types to choose from.
  • Chongnyon Hotel: This hotel offers a more budget-friendly option for travelers looking for simple accommodations.

What to Buy in Pyongyang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Pyongyang, be sure to bring home some souvenirs to remember your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • North Korean postage stamps: These stamps are a unique way to remember your trip, featuring images of North Korean leaders, monuments, and other iconic landmarks.
  • Traditional North Korean outfits: These outfits are colorful and made from high-quality materials. They make a great addition to any wardrobe.
  • North Korean ceramics and pottery: These handmade items are unique to North Korea and make great gifts for family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Pyongyang

Respectful behavior is key when visiting Pyongyang. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress conservatively: Dress in a way that shows respect to the local culture, such as covering the shoulders and knees.
  • Don’t take photographs without permission: Always ask before taking photos of people, monuments, or historical sites.
  • Carry cash: North Korea is largely a cash-only economy, so make sure to have enough cash on hand for purchases.

Best Time to Visit Pyongyang

The best time to visit Pyongyang is during the spring (April and May) and autumn (October and November) months. The weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists, which means smaller crowds at popular attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pyongyang

Here are some things to remember when visiting Pyongyang:

Do:

  • Follow the rules: Obey the laws and customs of North Korea.
  • Be respectful: Show respect to local residents, culture, and traditions.
  • Try the local food: North Korean food is delicious, and there are many unique dishes to try.

Don’t:

  • Speak negatively about the government or leaders: Avoid political discussions with locals, especially if they relate to the government or political leaders.
  • Take photos of military installations: Photography of military sites is not allowed and can result in serious consequences.
  • Bring in foreign money: Use the North Korean won for all transactions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pyongyang

Pyongyang is a safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should take care to follow local laws and customs and avoid political discussions or actions.

Health in Pyongyang

Visitors to Pyongyang should drink only bottled water and ensure that all food is cooked properly. Medical facilities are limited, so travelers should pack any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Pyongyang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pyongyang

Pyongyang Sunan International Airport is the main airport serving the city, located around 25 kilometers north of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Pyongyang

Pyongyang has a number of bus stations serving both domestic and international destinations.

Other Options

Flights arrive from Beijing, China, and Moscow, Russia. Visitors should obtain a visa beforehand and usually travel as part of an organized tour.

How to Get Around in Pyongyang

Getting around Pyongyang is fairly easy, as there are taxis, buses, and bikes available for hire. Visitors should keep in mind that communication with taxi drivers may be difficult, so it’s a good idea to have the name of your destination written down in Korean before getting into a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Pyongyang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are a number of nearby destinations that you can explore from Pyongyang:

  • Kaesong: This historic city is located just south of Pyongyang and is home to several UNESCO World Heritage sites.
  • Mount Myohyang: This mountainous region features scenic views, waterfalls, and several historic temples.
  • Wonsan: This coastal city offers beautiful beaches and relaxing spa experiences.

Conclusion

Pyongyang is a unique destination, and a must-see for anyone interested in experiencing a different way of life. With its mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in North Korea’s capital city. Plan your trip today and embark on a journey of a lifetime.
