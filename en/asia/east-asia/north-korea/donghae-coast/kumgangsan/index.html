Introduction to Kumgangsan

Kumgangsan, also known as Diamond Mountains, is located in the Donghae Coast region of North Korea. This mountain range is a natural wonder with breathtaking scenery and unique rock formations. It is popular among Koreans and tourists for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Kumgangsan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bulgogi - a Korean grilled beef dish
  2. Naengmyeon - cold buckwheat noodles in a savory broth
  3. Pajeon - a savory pancake made from green onions and vegetables

Local Beverages

  1. Soju - a traditional Korean alcoholic drink
  2. Makgeolli - a rice wine brewed from wheat and barley
  3. North Korean beer - a popular light beer in North Korea

Local Culture and Customs of Kumgangsan

  1. Cover your shoulders and knees while visiting culturally significant sites.
  2. Always bow to elders as a sign of respect.
  3. Tipping is not customary in North Korea.

Best Time to Visit Kumgangsan

The best time to visit Kumgangsan is during the fall season from September to November, when the leaves change color and the air is crisp and cool, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumgangsan

Do:

  1. Be respectful of the North Korean culture and customs.
  2. Carry your passport with you at all times.
  3. Adhere to the instructions of your guide at all times.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of military buildings or personnel without permission.
  2. Bring large amounts of foreign currency or electronics.
  3. Smoke in non-smoking areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumgangsan

North Korea is a very safe country. It is important, however, to follow the instructions of your guide and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Kumgangsan

It is recommended to get vaccinated and carry necessary medication for common illnesses such as diarrhea, cold and cough.

Getting to Kumgangsan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bus Stations Near Kumgangsan

Other Options

You can also take a train ride to Tumen or Rason and then a bus ride to Kumgangsan.

How to Get Around in Kumgangsan

The best way to get around Kumgangsan is on foot or by bicycle. Your tour guide should be able to arrange transportation for you.

Where to Go Next from Kumgangsan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Kumgangsan is a hidden gem in East Asia, with its stunning natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Whether you are looking to hike and explore the mountains or relax and enjoy the local culture, Kumgangsan is sure to leave you with unforgettable experiences.
