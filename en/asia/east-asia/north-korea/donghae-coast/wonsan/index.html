Introduction to Wonsan

Wonsan is a beautiful city located in Donghae Coast, in North Korea. This port city boasts a rich history dating back to the 10th century, as well as striking natural landscapes and outdoor activities, making it a popular destination for local and international travelers alike.

Wonsan is known for its picturesque beaches, hiking trails, and stunning coastal scenery. The city is also home to an array of cultural and historical attractions, including museums, temples, and ancient ruins.

Whether you are traveling solo, with friends or family, or in a group, Wonsan has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will cover all the essential information you need to plan a memorable trip to Wonsan, from where to stay and what to do to how to stay safe while exploring this beautiful city. Keep reading to discover everything you need to know before embarking on your Wonsan adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Wonsan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wonsan is home to many culturally significant museums and historical places that should not be missed.

The Wonsan Revolutionary Site

The Wonsan Revolutionary Site is dedicated to the Korean people’s struggle against Japanese colonial rule. It is also famous for its beautiful gardens and stunning views of the city.

The Songdowon International Children’s Camp

Songdowon International Children’s Camp is not only a perfect place for children to learn and grow, it is also a fantastic attraction for visitors who want to learn more about the fascinating history of the DPRK.

Kangwon Province Museum

The Kangwon Province Museum was built in honor of the founding of the DPRK. Here, you can learn about Wonsan’s long history and the various influences that have shaped the city and the country.

Natural Attractions of Wonsan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Wonsan has plenty of natural attractions to explore. From beautiful beaches to breathtaking valleys and mountains, there is something for everyone.

Wonsan Kalma Beach

Wonsan Kalma Beach stretches over 11 kilometers and is a must-visit for beach lovers. Whether you are looking to soak up some sun or take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of the East Sea, this beach is perfect for a day of relaxation and fun.

Mount Kumgang

Mount Kumgang is one of the most stunning natural landmarks in the region, famous for its picturesque waterfalls, forests, and hiking trails. This mountain offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wonsan

Wonsan offers a wide range of hiking and biking routes, perfect for exploring the area’s natural beauty. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or prefer a leisurely stroll, Wonsan has something for you.

Munsu Waterfall Hiking Trail

The Munsu Waterfall Hiking Trail is one of the most popular routes in Wonsan, taking visitors on a challenging yet rewarding journey through the beautiful valleys and mountains near Wonsan.

Masikryong Pass Cycling Route

The Masikryong Pass Cycling Route is a fantastic way to explore the scenic countryside around Mount Masik. This route takes you through picturesque villages, forests, and scenic landscapes, with plenty of opportunities for photos along the way.

Best Things to Do in Wonsan: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the city’s natural and cultural attractions, Wonsan offers a wide range of activities and tours, suitable for travelers of all interests.

Tour of the DMZ

The DMZ Tour is a must-do excursion for anyone interested in North Korea’s history and politics. This tour takes visitors on a memorable journey through the Demilitarized Zone, providing insight into North Korea’s relationship with the south and the world at large.

Snorkeling and Diving

Wonsan offers excellent opportunities for water sports enthusiasts, with several diving and snorkeling sites in the area. The clear waters of the East Sea offer a unique opportunity to explore the underwater world and see a range of marine life up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Wonsan

No trip to Wonsan is complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in the area.

Must-Try Dishes

Raengmyeon (Cold Noodle Soup)

Raengmyeon, a refreshing noodle soup made with cold beef broth, potato starch noodles, and plenty of fresh vegetables, is a popular dish in the area and perfect for hot summer days.

Hand-Pulled Noodles

Hand-pulled noodles, made by stretching dough into long, thin strands, is another must-try dish in Wonsan. These noodles are usually combined with meat, vegetables, and a variety of sauces to create delicious and hearty meals that are perfect for any time of day.

Local Beverages

Soju

Soju is a popular Korean alcoholic beverage made from rice, wheat, or barley. This light and refreshing drink is perfect for hot summer days and can be enjoyed with spicy or savory snacks.

Makgeolli

Makgeolli is a traditional Korean rice wine that is brewed in the area. This sweet and tangy drink is made from rice, water, and a fermentation starter that gives it its distinct flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Wonsan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wonsan offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

Here are some of the best places to stay in Wonsan:

Maebongsan International Hotel

Maebongsan International Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. This hotel offers a range of amenities, including a spa, sauna, and restaurant.

Wonsan Golden Beach Hotel

Wonsan Golden Beach Hotel is a budget-friendly option located close to the beach. This hotel offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a restaurant serving delicious local and international cuisine.

What to Buy in Wonsan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Wonsan is complete without bringing home a few souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in the area:

Korean Ginseng

Korean ginseng is a popular traditional medicine that is believed to have numerous health benefits. This herb is widely available in Wonsan, with many shops selling high-quality ginseng products.

Jangdokdae Pickles

Jangdokdae pickles are a type of fermented vegetable that is popular in the area. These pickles are made with a combination of cabbage, radish, and other vegetables, giving them a unique and delicious flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Wonsan

Before embarking on your trip to Wonsan, it’s important to understand the local culture and customs. Here are some of the essential things to know:

Respect for Authority

The North Korean government demands respect and obedience from its citizens and visitors. It’s important to avoid any critical comments about the government or its policies, as this can lead to serious consequences.

Traditional Dress

Traditional Korean dress, known as hanbok, is still commonly worn in the area, especially during special occasions and festivals. Visitors are encouraged to try on traditional clothing and take photos for a memorable experience.

Best Time to Visit Wonsan

The best time to visit Wonsan is during the late spring and early fall months, when the weather is pleasant and the scenery is beautiful. Avoid visiting during the summer months, as temperatures can be uncomfortably high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wonsan

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Wonsan:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Take part in traditional activities and festivals

Don’t:

  • Criticize the government or its policies
  • Take unauthorized photos or video footage
  • Smoke in public areas

Safety and Health

Wonsan is a safe and healthy city for travelers, with a low crime rate and access to quality healthcare. However, visitors should still take precautions to stay safe and healthy during their stay.

Safety in Wonsan

It’s important to follow North Korean laws and regulations while in Wonsan, as the penalties for breaking the law can be severe. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid any areas that are off-limits to tourists.

Health in Wonsan

Visitors should also take steps to protect their health while in Wonsan, including using insect repellent, wearing sunscreen, and drinking bottled water. It is also recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to North Korea.

Getting to Wonsan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Wonsan is relatively straightforward, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Wonsan

The nearest airport to Wonsan is the Kalma International Airport, which offers flights to several international destinations, including China and Russia.

Bus Stations Near Wonsan

Visitors can also travel to Wonsan by bus, with several bus stations located throughout the city. Buses run regularly from major cities across North Korea, making it easy to reach Wonsan from other parts of the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Wonsan by train, with several major train stations located throughout the city. Trains run regularly from major cities across North Korea, offering a convenient and affordable way to explore the country.

How to Get Around in Wonsan

Getting around Wonsan is easy, with several transportation options available for visitors. Taxis are widely available, while buses and trains offer affordable and convenient options for traveling between different parts of the city and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Wonsan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wonsan is located close to several other popular destinations in the region, making it a perfect base for exploring the area. Some of the nearby attractions and excursions include:

Mount Kumgang

Mount Kumgang is located just a few hours’ drive from Wonsan and is a popular natural landmark in the area.

Pyongyang

Pyongyang, the capital city of North Korea, is located just a few hours’ drive from Wonsan and offers a range of attractions and activities for visitors.

Conclusion

Wonsan is a beautiful and fascinating city that offers plenty of natural and cultural attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you are interested in hiking, biking, or learning about the history and culture of the DPRK, Wonsan has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to help plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this incredible city.
