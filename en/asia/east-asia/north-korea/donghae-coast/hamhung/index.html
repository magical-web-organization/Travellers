Introduction to Hamhung

Hamhung, located on the east coast of North Korea, is a city of cultural importance and natural beauty. With its historical sites, stunning mountain views, and sandy beaches, Hamhung is truly a hidden gem and a must-see for any traveler.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Hamhung. From a list of must-see attractions to the best places to stay and where to go next, you’ll have all the tools you need to enjoy this fascinating city to its fullest.

List of Places to Visit in Hamhung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hamhung Grand Theatre

Built in the 1980s, the Hamhung Grand Theatre is known for its stunning architecture and impressive size. It seats up to 2,000 people and is home to theatrical performances and concerts.

Kwangbok Street

Known as the “Street of Blessed Reunification”, Kwangbok Street is a popular shopping street in Hamhung. Here you can find all kinds of goods, from clothing and shoes to electronics and souvenirs.

Jangguhwa Revolutionary Site

The Jangguhwa Revolutionary Site is a historical attraction honoring North Korea’s revolutionary past. It commemorates the battle that took place during the anti-Japanese struggle in North Korea.

Natural Attractions of Hamhung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mt. Tonghung

At a height of 1074 meters, Mt. Tonghung offers stunning panoramic views of Hamhung City and the surrounding coastline. Many visitors enjoy hiking to the summit, and there are several trails to choose from.

Tokbuk Beach

Located on the outskirts of Hamhung, Tokbuk Beach is a beautiful sandy beach that’s popular with locals and tourists alike. It’s a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.

Taehwa Peak

Taehwa Peak is another popular spot for hiking and offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and coastline. In the spring, the mountain is covered in cherry blossoms, making it an especially romantic spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hamhung

Mt. Tonghung Hiking Route

Starting from the base of Mt. Tonghung, this route takes you through rugged terrain and rocky outcrops. The hike can last up to six hours, but the stunning views from the summit are well worth the effort.

Hamhung Coastal Bike Route

This scenic route runs along the coastline of Hamhung, offering stunning views of the sea and the mountains beyond. It’s a great way to explore the city and see all its beauty at a leisurely pace.

Best Things to Do in Hamhung: Activities and Tours

Hamhung City Tour

This guided tour will take you to all the must-see cultural and natural attractions in the city. You’ll learn about the city’s history and see some of its most stunning sights.

Taehwa Peak Sunrise Tour

Get up early and join this tour to see the sunrise from the top of Taehwa Peak. You’ll be rewarded with stunning views and a unique experience that you’ll never forget.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hamhung

Food and Drink Scene of Hamhung

Must-Try Dishes

Hamhung Cold Noodles

This dish is a local favorite and a great way to beat the heat on a hot summer day. The noodles are made from potato starch and are served in a tangy sauce with slices of beef and vegetables.

grilled clams

Served on the beach, these clams are grilled over an open flame and served with garlic and soy sauce. They’re a delicious and authentic local delicacy.

Local Beverages

Soju

A popular alcoholic beverage in North Korea, Soju is made from rice or sweet potato starch and has a mild taste. It’s often enjoyed with a meal or on its own at social gatherings.

Aroumsaekju

This beer is brewed locally and has a distinctive bitter taste that many visitors enjoy. It’s a great way to cool down after a day of exploring Hamhung.

Where to Stay in Hamhung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haedanghwa Hotel

One of the most popular hotels in Hamhung, the Haedanghwa Hotel offers comfortable, modern rooms and stunning views of the sea. It’s located in the heart of the city, making it a great base for exploring.

Hamhung Youth Hostel

For budget travelers, the Hamhung Youth Hostel is a great option. It offers dormitory-style accommodation with shared facilities, making it a great place to meet other travelers.

What to Buy in Hamhung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Fabrics

Hamhung is known for its high-quality handmade fabrics, including traditional Korean-style clothing and home furnishings. Be sure to pick up some of these beautiful items to bring home as a souvenir.

Koryo Insam

Also known as Korean ginseng, Koryo Insam is a popular herbal supplement that’s believed to have medicinal properties. You can find it at local markets and shops throughout Hamhung.

Local Culture and Customs of Hamhung

North Korea is a country with a rich cultural heritage, and Hamhung is no exception. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including bowing to elders and dressing modestly.

Best Time to Visit Hamhung

The best time to visit Hamhung is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, be aware that this is also peak tourist season, so prices may be higher and attractions may be more crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hamhung

Do:

  • Do dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Do bow to elders as a sign of respect.
  • Do try the local food and drink.
  • Do be aware of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs without permission.
  • Don’t bring religious materials into the country.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t engage in political discussions or debates.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hamhung

Overall, Hamhung is a safe city for visitors, and crime rates are very low. However, visitors are advised to exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Hamhung

Visitors to Hamhung should be aware that medical facilities in North Korea are basic, and emergency medical services are limited. Visitors should take out comprehensive travel insurance and familiarize themselves with local health risks before traveling.

Getting to Hamhung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hamhung

The nearest airport to Hamhung is the Wonsan Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Hamhung

Hamhung has several bus stations, including the Hamhung bus terminal and the Hamhung Intercity Bus Station.

Other Options

It’s also possible to reach Hamhung by train, but be aware that tickets can be difficult to obtain and trains can be slow and overcrowded.

How to Get Around in Hamhung

Taxis and buses are the most common forms of public transportation in Hamhung. Taxis are widely available and affordable, while buses run regularly throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Hamhung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wonsan

Just an hour’s drive from Hamhung, Wonsan is a beautiful port city that’s home to a large number of historic monuments and exciting attractions.

Pyongyang

The capital city of North Korea, Pyongyang, is just a few hours’ drive from Hamhung. It’s a fascinating blend of modernity and tradition, and visitors can enjoy everything from shopping to sightseeing to exploring the local food scene.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Hamhung has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan your ideal trip to this beautiful and fascinating city. Don’t wait – book your trip to Hamhung today!
