Introduction to Haeju

Haeju, located in the Hwanghae province of North Korea, is a vibrant coastal city that boasts a rich history and culture. It is surrounded by picturesque mountain ranges, dense forests, and pristine beaches, making it an ideal destination for those looking for a unique travel experience. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the must-see places in Haeju, the hiking and biking routes, the local food and culture, and everything else you need to know before visiting this hidden gem of North Korea.

List of Places to Visit in Haeju

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haeju has a rich history, and a visit to the city is incomplete without exploring the numerous museums and historical sites, including:

  1. Haeju Folk Museum: This museum houses a comprehensive collection of artefacts that bear witness to the rich cultural history of Haeju.

  2. Haeju Castle: A trip to Haeju would be incomplete without visiting the Haeju Castle, a 14th-century fortress built by King Kongmin of the Goryeo Dynasty.

  3. Sonjuk Bridge: This old wooden bridge, built in 1216, is considered a historic masterpiece and a representation of the architectural glory of the Goryeo Dynasty.

  4. Jayeonri Cultural Site: A picturesque village that dates back to the Koryo Dynasty, Jayeonri Cultural Site is home to well-preserved ancient temples, palaces, and tombs.

Natural Attractions of Haeju: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking an outdoor adventure and the best scenic views, Haeju has something to offer:

  1. Mt. Suyangsan: One of the most popular hiking destinations in Haeju, Mt. Suyangsan offers a breathtaking view of the Lagoon and the Sea of Korea.

  2. Haeju Beach: A long stretch of pristine golden sand and clear blue waters make the Haeju Beach one of the most stunning beaches in North Korea.

  3. Ryesonggang Bamboo Forest: This bamboo forest offers a unique experience to visitors as it features a wooden suspension bridge and a small waterfall.

  4. Sangwon Hermitage: This tranquil temple, located atop a mountain, provides a stunning panoramic view of Haeju and is an ideal spot for relaxation and meditation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haeju

Haeju is blessed with picturesque mountain ranges and scenic trails, making it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking/biking routes include:

  1. Hooam Mountain Trail: The trail starts at the entrance of the Hooam Temple and provides a stunning view of the city and the surrounding mountains.

  2. Songi Mountain Trail: This trail is ideal for hikers of all skill levels and takes visitors to some of the best viewpoints in Haeju.

  3. Haeju – Suyangsan Mountain Cycling Trail: A 20-kilometre long cycling trail that offers a unique experience of exploring the city and the Suyangsan mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Haeju: Activities and Tours

Haeju offers several activities and tours for visitors:

  1. Visit a Local Farm: Visitors can tour a local farm and experience a typical rural lifestyle in North Korea.

  2. Haeju Seafood Market: The Haeju Seafood Market offers a distinctly North Korean experience where visitors can sample local seafood caught fresh from the Sea of Korea.

  3. Haeju Film Studio: The Haeju Film Studio is one of the largest film studios in North Korea and gives visitors a chance to see the behind-the-scenes of Korean cinema making.

  4. Visit Chunghwa Hot Spring: This natural hot spring located on the slopes of Mt. Songgak is well-known for its healing properties and offers a truly relaxing experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Haeju

Must-Try Dishes

Haeju is known for its unique cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the coastal region. Here are some dishes that should be on your must-try list:

  1. Nangmyeon: Cold buckwheat noodles served with cucumber, pear, boiled egg, and a spicy sauce.

  2. Haeju-style Chicken Soup: A flavourful soup made with chicken, mushrooms, spinach, and green onions.

  3. Pheasant Soup: A hearty soup made with pheasant meat, vegetables, and rice.

Local Beverages

Haeju is also famous for its local drinks, including:

  1. Liquor: Haeju Makkoli, a local rice wine, has a sour taste and is a popular alcoholic beverage in the city.

  2. Tea: Haeju Tea, made with leaves grown in the plantation of the Mt. Unpa, is well-known for its unique taste and aroma.

Where to Stay in Haeju: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haeju has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  1. Haeju Yaksu Hotel: A luxury hotel with an on-site restaurant, spa, and wellness centre.

  2. Haeju Homestay: A budget-friendly option where visitors can experience local culture and hospitality.

  3. Haeju Youth Capsule Hostel: A backpacker-friendly hostel with a shared lounge and kitchen.

What to Buy in Haeju: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Haeju is home to several markets where visitors can find unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  1. Haeju Koryo Ginseng: Known for its medicinal properties, Koryo Ginseng is a popular purchase among visitors.

  2. Haeju Tea: The locally grown tea is well-known for its unique aroma and flavour.

  3. Haeju Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase locally made souvenirs such as traditional Korean pottery, silk products, and wooden crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Haeju

Haeju follows traditional Korean customs, including:

  1. Bowing: Greetings are often accompanied by a slight bow, and it is considered rude not to return the gesture.

  2. Shoes: It is customary to remove shoes before entering homes or temples.

  3. Dining: Meals are often served in communal dishes, and it is impolite to finish all the food on your plate.

Best Time to Visit Haeju

The best time to visit Haeju is during the spring (April to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons, which are considered the most pleasant times of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haeju

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and culture.
  2. Follow the strict travel guidelines and code of conduct.
  3. Be mindful of taking photos, especially in sensitive areas.

Don’t:

  1. Criticize the political regime or the country.
  2. Bring in any banned items or engage in any illegal activities.
  3. Exchange currency on the black market.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haeju

Haeju is considered a safe destination for tourists, and crime rates are minimal. However, visitors should be aware of the strict travel guidelines and should avoid taking any risks.

Health in Haeju

Visitors should be cautious of the different strains of viruses in North Korea and should take necessary vaccinations and maintain hygiene to avoid any health issues. It is advisable to drink bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Haeju: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haeju

The nearest airport to Haeju is Pyongyang Sunan International Airport, which is approximately 110 kilometres away. Visitors can take a connecting bus from Pyongyang to Haeju.

Bus Stations Near Haeju

Haeju has a well-connected bus network, with daily services from several cities in North Korea.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Haeju by train or ferry from different cities in North Korea.

How to Get Around in Haeju

Visitors can use taxis, buses, and trains to travel within the city.

Where to Go Next from Haeju: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations such as Sinchon, Kaesong, or Pyongyang to experience other facets of Korean culture.

Conclusion

Haeju is a hidden gem of North Korea that offers a unique travel experience to visitors. From cultural and natural attractions to food and local culture, Haeju has everything to offer. Follow the strict travel guidelines, and explore this less-known city in North Korea.
