As one of the most beautiful provinces in North Korea, Hwanghae is an amazing tourist destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike. Known for its rich culture and breathtaking landscapes, Hwanghae is a must-visit place in East Asia. From visiting the famous museums and historical places to exploring outdoor adventures and scenic views, there is something for everyone in Hwanghae.

Introduction to Hwanghae

Hwanghae Province is located in the westernmost part of North Korea. It has two administrative divisions, North Hwanghae and South Hwanghae. The province got its name from the Hwanghae Mountains that stretch along its eastern border. The province is surrounded by large bodies of water, making it a perfect destination for fishing and water activities. Its capital city is Haeju, located on the shore of the Yellow Sea.

List of Places to Visit in Hwanghae

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haeju Folk Museum: This museum showcases the traditional Korean way of life, including customs, clothing, and tools.

  • Haeju Revolutionary Museum: The museum displays documents, photographs, and artifacts related to the anti-colonial struggle led by Kim Il-sung.

  • Haeju Old Town: This historical town is one of the best-preserved parts of Hwanghae Province.

  • Kangso Mineral Water Spa: Known for its medicinal and healing properties, Kangso Mineral Water Spa is a perfect place to relax and rejuvenate.

Natural Attractions of Hwanghae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kuwol: Mount Kuwol is a majestic mountain range that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Lake Sijung: Lake Sijung is a beautiful body of water surrounded by mountains and forests.

  • Sinpyong Lake: Sinpyong Lake is a popular destination for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hwanghae

  • Mount Kuwol Trail: The Mount Kuwol Trail is a challenging 2-day hike that takes visitors through the heart of the Kuwol Mountains.

  • Haeju City Biking Route: This 12 km biking route takes visitors through the historic streets of Haeju City.

Best Things to Do in Hwanghae: Activities and Tours

  • Local Market Tour: Visitors can explore the vibrant local markets of Hwanghae and taste the local cuisine.

  • Kim Il-sung Square: The largest square in the city hosts parades, events, and concerts.

Food and Drink Scene of Hwanghae

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haeju Naengmyeon: A popular cold noodle dish made with buckwheat noodles and seasoned broth.

  • Baekseju: A sweet traditional Korean rice wine made from steamed rice and fermented herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Sokryonju: A traditional Korean alcoholic beverage made from fermented corn and medicinal herbs.

  • Taedonggang Beer: A popular North Korean beer that is brewed in Pyongyang.

Where to Stay in Hwanghae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Moranbong Hotel: A comfortable and modern hotel in the heart of the city.

  • Sinuiju Hotel: A budget-friendly option located in the outskirts of the city.

What to Buy in Hwanghae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Korean clothing: Visitors can buy traditional Korean clothing, such as hanbok, for themselves or as gifts.

  • Handmade crafts: There are various local shops selling handmade handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hwanghae

Visitors to Hwanghae should be respectful of the local culture and customs, including dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering homes and temples.

Best Time to Visit Hwanghae

The best time to visit Hwanghae is during the spring and autumn seasons, from April to May and September to November, respectively. During this time, the temperatures are pleasant, and the landscapes are vibrant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hwanghae

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and location.

  • Take off your shoes before entering homes and temples.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of military personnel or sensitive political sites.

  • Bring unauthorized items into the country.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hwanghae

Hwanghae is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. Visitors should be cautious when traveling outside of the city, especially at night.

Health in Hwanghae

Visitors to Hwanghae should consult their doctor before traveling and ensure that they have all necessary vaccinations. Visitors should also be cautious when drinking tap water and only consume bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Hwanghae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hwanghae

  • Pyongyang International Airport: The largest airport in North Korea, located in Pyongyang.

Bus Stations Near Hwanghae

  • Haeju Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Haeju City, with daily buses to and from other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Train: Visitors can take a train from Pyongyang to Haeju City.

How to Get Around in Hwanghae

Visitors can get around Hwanghae by bus or taxi. Rental cars are also available, but visitors should be cautious when driving due to North Korea’s strict driving laws.

Where to Go Next from Hwanghae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pyongsong: A city located in South Pyongan Province, known for its famous university and beautiful parks.

  • Sinuiju: A city located on the border with China, famous for its bustling markets and street food.

Conclusion

Hwanghae is an awe-inspiring destination for nature lovers and history buffs. With its rich culture, breathtaking landscapes, and numerous attractions, visitors to Hwanghae are sure to have an unforgettable experience. Plan your next trip to Hwanghae today!
