Introduction to Hovd

Nestled in western Mongolia, Hovd is a gem of a city that offers visitors a unique combination of cultural heritage and natural wonders. It is located at the foot of the Mongol Altai Mountains, making it the perfect starting point for those adventure seekers looking for outdoor activities. In this comprehensive guide, we will discuss the best places to visit, where to eat and stay, and what to bring back home when exploring the city of Hovd.

List of Places to Visit in Hovd

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hovd offers a rich history and culture that is preserved in the various museums and historical landmarks that grace the city. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Hovd Museum: A museum dedicated to the local history and culture of the province.
  • Dörvöljin Mountains: A picturesque range that offers visitors stunning views of the landscape and a chance to learn more about the local Kazakh culture.
  • Khar Us Lake: Home to a variety of birds and fish, this lake is also the site of the ancient Turkic inscriptions and petroglyphs carved into the rocks.
  • Khar-Uul Art Gallery: Established by a Mongolian sculptor Bat-Uul Baldandorj, this gallery exhibits a variety of artwork from local and national Mongolian artists.

Natural Attractions of Hovd: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hovd is famous for its stunning natural landscapes, from the towering peaks of the Mongol Altai Mountains to the serene beauty of Khar Us Lake. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Hoton and Hurgan Lakes: Located in the heart of the Mongol Altai Mountains, these two lakes are ideal for fishing, hiking, and camping.
  • Tavan Bogd National Park: Home to the country’s highest peaks and glaciers, this national park offers unparalleled opportunities for hiking and mountaineering.
  • Tsambagarav Uul National Park: Located in the country’s far west, the park is an ideal spot for trekking and wildlife watching, especially for snow leopards and marmots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hovd

The breathtaking natural landscapes of Hovd are also home to several scenic hiking and biking routes:

  • Hoton and Hurgan Lakes Trek: A two to five-day trek that takes visitors through the region’s valleys, mountains, and deserts.
  • Mount Khüiten Trek: A three to four-day trek up to the summit of the highest peak in the Mongol Altai mountains.
  • Khar Us Lake and Sharga River Trek: A two-day trek or biking route through the picturesque landscapes of the Sharga River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Hovd: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and outdoor activities, Hovd has several other activities and tours to offer:

  • Attend a local Naadam festival: Experience the Mongolian way of life and enjoy cultural activities such as horse racing, archery, and wrestling.
  • Visit local nomads: Learn about the traditional way of life of the nomads, chat with them and share traditional meals.
  • Visit the local handicraft market: Buy souvenirs, such as handmade carpets, cashmere, and yak woolen products directly from the makers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hovd

Day 1: Visit the Hovd Museum and explore the city center.

Day 2: Head up to the Mongol Altai Mountains to see Hoton and Hurgan lakes.

Day 3: Trek the Khuiten peak and soak in the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Visit the Khar-Uul Art Gallery and learn about Mongolian art.

Day 5: Travel west to Tsambagarav Uul national park for a trek or wildlife observation.

Day 6: Stop at the Khar Us Lake and enjoy fishing and camping.

Day 7: Attend a local Naadam festival or visit the local handicraft market.

Food and Drink Scene of Hovd

Must-Try Dishes

Mongolian cuisine is unique, and Hovd’s culinary scene is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes in the city:

  • Khuushuur: A fried beef or mutton pastry similar to a dumpling.
  • Buuz: A steamed meat dumpling similar to a Chinese dim sum.
  • Khorkhog: A traditional Mongolian dish, where meat is cooked with hot pebbles and herbs in a pot.

Local Beverages

Mares’ milk, fermented milk, and spirits such as vodka and beer are popular beverages in Hovd. Airag, a fermented drink made from mare’s milk, is a must-try traditional drink.

What to Buy in Hovd: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hovd is rich in handicrafts and souvenirs that reflect the rich culture and tradition of the Mongolian people. Handmade carpets and cashmere products, such as shawls and scarfs, are popular purchases. Yak woolen products such as socks, gloves, and hats are also popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hovd

In Hovd, respect for the culture and traditions within the community is crucial. Visitors should follow these basic customs:

  • Greetings: It’s customary to greet people with a handshake followed by a nod of the head.
  • Shoes: It’s disrespectful to wear shoes inside somebody’s home.
  • Punctuality: Mongolians value punctuality, and it’s important to make every effort to arrive on time for appointments.

Best Time to Visit Hovd

The best time to visit Hovd is from June to August when the weather is warm and pleasant for outdoor activities. The winter season is from November to February, and the temperature can drop below -40°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hovd

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions of the Mongolian people.
  • Try local food and beverages, such as khuushuur and airag.
  • Follow the dress code and cover shoulders and legs when visiting religious sites and temples.

Don’t:

  • Touch people on the head or pass things over someone’s head as this is considered disrespectful.
  • Speak loudly or act brash in public spaces.
  • Dispose of litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hovd

Hovd is relatively safe to travel to. It’s best to take regular precautions to stay safe, such as always keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Hovd

Visitors should take the necessary precautions when traveling to Hovd, such as getting all the required vaccinations before the trip. The city has medical centers, clinics, and hospitals to cater to visitors with medical needs.

Getting to Hovd: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hovd

The nearest airport to Hovd is Hovd Airport, which serves domestic routes from Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia’s capital. Flights operate only a few times a week.

Bus Stations Near Hovd

Regular bus services are available to and from Ulaanbaatar, which is about 1400 km away from the region. The travel time from Ulaanbaatar to Hovd via road is approximately 24 - 30 hours, depending on the weather and road conditions.

Other Options

Traveling by train is also a viable option. The nearest railway station is in Zamyn Uud and is approximately 1300 km away.

How to Get Around in Hovd

Taxis and car rentals are readily available, but the best way to explore Hovd is by foot or bicycle. Tours, guided or self-guided, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Hovd: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Hovd include:

  • Altai Tavan Bogd National Park: A stunning national park located in the far west of Mongolia.
  • Bayan-Ulgii: Home to the Kazakh people and their culture.
  • Uvs province: A region that offers visitors a chance to see the ancient petroglyphs.

Conclusion

Hovd’s combination of cultural heritage and natural landscapes makes it a must-visit destination in Mongolia. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their adventure to Hovd and explore all that the region has to offer. Whether trekking the Mongol Altai Mountains, trying local cuisine, or shopping for souvenirs, Hovd has something for everyone.
