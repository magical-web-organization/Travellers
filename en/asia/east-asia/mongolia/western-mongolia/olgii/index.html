Introduction to Ölgii

Ölgii is a small city located in the western part of Mongolia, near the borders of China and Russia. The city is the capital of the Bayan-Ölgii province, which is the only province in Mongolia where the majority of the population is Kazakh. Ölgii is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Ölgii, Mongolia.

List of Places to Visit in Ölgii

Ölgii offers a variety of places to visit, including culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Museum of Bayan-Ölgii: The museum offers a fascinating insight into the history and culture of the Bayan-Ölgii province, including traditional Kazakh customs, handicrafts, and costumes.
  2. The Kazakh Eagle Hunter’s Association: Here, you can learn about the Kazakh tradition of eagle hunting, which has been passed down from generation to generation.
  3. The Tavan Bogd National Park: This park offers spectacular views of the mountains, glaciers, and lakes.

Natural Attractions of Ölgii: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Altai Tavan Bogd National Park: This is a UNESCO World Heritage site and home to several amazing natural wonders like the five peaks of Tavan Bogd Mountain and Potanin Glacier, which is the largest glacier in Mongolia.
  2. Achit Lake: This lake is located just outside of the city and is a great spot for swimming, fishing, and hiking.
  3. Khoton Lake: This lake is known for its crystal-clear water, stunning views, and being a great spot for camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ölgii

Ölgii is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several great routes to explore.

  1. Tavan Bogd Mountain: This is the tallest peak in Mongolia and offers a challenging hike with breathtaking views.
  2. Sogoog Mountain: This is an easy hike suitable for beginners, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. Altai Tavan Bogd National Park: This park offers several hiking and biking routes that vary in difficulty level.

Best Things to Do in Ölgii: Activities and Tours

  1. Eagle hunting: Attend an eagle hunting demonstration and see the Kazakh tradition up close.
  2. Camping: There are several great camping spots in and around Ölgii, perfect for a peaceful night under the stars.
  3. Horseback riding: Experience the sweeping landscapes of Mongolia on horseback tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ölgii

Day 1: Arrive in Ölgii and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the city and visiting the Museum of Bayan-Ölgii. Day 2: Visit the Kazakh Eagle Hunter’s Association and witness a traditional eagle hunting demonstration. Day 3: Hike to the top of Sogoog Mountain and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Day 4: Visit Achit Lake and hike around the lake. Day 5: Take a horseback riding tour in Altai Tavan Bogd National Park and visit the Potanin Glacier. Day 6: Explore Khoton Lake and spend the night camping in the wilderness. Day 7: Depart from Ölgii and return home.

Food and Drink Scene of Ölgii

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beshbarmak: A traditional Kazakh dish of boiled meat served over noodles.
  2. Shashlik: Grilled skewered meat, commonly mutton or beef.
  3. Khuushuur: Fried dumplings filled with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Airag: A traditional Mongolian beverage made from fermented mare’s milk.
  2. Tea: A staple of Mongolian hospitality, tea is often served hot and strong with milk and sugar.

Where to Stay in Ölgii: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Altiar Hotel: A 4-star hotel in the city center with comfortable rooms and traditional Mongolian decor.
  2. Bayan-Olgii Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse located in the city center, owned by a local family.
  3. Golden Eagle Hotel: A modern hotel with spacious rooms and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Ölgii: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kazakh Traditional Clothing: These bright and colorful clothes reflect the rich and vibrant culture of the Kazakh people.
  2. Felt crafts: Mongolian artisans are known for their intricate felt crafts, including hand-made slippers, bags, and hats.
  3. Jewelry: The unique designs of the local silver and gold jewelry make it a great souvenir for any traveler.

Local Culture and Customs of Ölgii

  1. Hospitality: Mongolians are famously hospitable, and visitors to Ölgii will be welcomed with open arms.
  2. Eagle Hunting: This ancient tradition is a vital part of the Kazakh culture and is still practiced today.
  3. Nomadic Lifestyle: Many Mongolians still live a nomadic lifestyle, moving with their herds throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Ölgii

The best time to visit Ölgii is from June to August, when the weather is warm and dry. The winter months, from December to March, can be harsh and extremely cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ölgii

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Seek permission before taking photographs of people or their homes.
  3. Try the traditional Mongolian and Kazakh cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing, as it is considered disrespectful.
  2. Touch or disturb animals in the wild.
  3. Ignore warnings from locals about potentially dangerous routes or areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ölgii

Ölgii is generally a safe place to travel, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take normal precautions such as keeping an eye on personal belongings and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ölgii

Visitors to Ölgii should take normal precautions when it comes to health and hygiene. It is recommended to drink bottled water, avoid raw or undercooked foods, and ensure that all vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Ölgii: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ölgii

  1. Ölgii Airport: The nearest airport to Ölgii, with flights from Ulaanbaatar and other major cities.

Bus Stations Near Ölgii

  1. Ölgii Bus Station: Located in the city center, this is the main bus station in town.

Other Options

  1. Private Car: Many visitors choose to hire a private car for transportation, which offers flexibility and convenience.
  2. Hitchhiking: Hitchhiking is a popular mode of transportation in Mongolia, and many locals are happy to give visitors a ride.

How to Get Around in Ölgii

The best way to get around Ölgii is by foot or by car. Taxis are also available but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Ölgii: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ulaanbaatar: The capital city of Mongolia, offering a great contrast to the remote and rural Ölgii.
  2. Gobi Desert: One of the world’s most famous deserts, offering stunning scenery, wildlife, and adventure.
  3. Khuvsgul Lake: The largest freshwater lake in Mongolia, located in the north of the country.

Conclusion

Ölgii is a unique and beautiful destination in Mongolia, offering visitors a chance to experience traditional Kazakh culture, stunning natural beauty, and outdoor adventure. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan your perfect trip to Ölgii, Mongolia.
