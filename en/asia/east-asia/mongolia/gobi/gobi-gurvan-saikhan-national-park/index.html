Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park Travel Guide

Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is one of the most stunning and diverse natural landscapes in Mongolia, located in the Gobi Desert. It is a vast area of desert, mountains, valleys, and grasslands that create a unique ecosystem that is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna. Whether you are a nature lover, adventure seeker, or history buff, Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is the perfect destination. This guide covers everything you need to know to plan your trip.

Introduction to Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park encompasses an area of 27,000 square kilometers in southern Mongolia. It was established in 1993 and is home to some of the most beautiful and pristine natural landscapes in the world. The park is divided into three major regions:

  • The eastern part, known as the Gurvan Saikhan Mountains
  • The middle part, which includes the Yol Valley
  • The western part, which includes the Altai Mountains

The park is home to many rare and endangered species of birds, animals, and plants, including the Mongolian wild ass, Gobi bear, snow leopard, saiga antelope, and many more. It is a place of exceptional natural beauty where you can immerse yourself in the culture, history, and traditions of Mongolia.

List of Places to Visit in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

There are many attractions to see and things to do in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park. Here are some of the places you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Flaming Cliffs: The Flaming Cliffs are located in the western part of the park and are known for their deep red color. They are the site where the first ever dinosaur eggs were discovered in the 1920s.
  • Bayanzag: Bayanzag, also known as the “land of the dinosaurs,” is a paleontological site where many fossils have been found. It is located in the western part of the park and is a must-visit for anyone interested in paleontology.
  • Khongor Sand Dunes: The Khongor Sand Dunes are the largest sand dunes in the park, reaching a height of up to 80 meters. They are located in the south and are a popular place for camel riding, hiking, and camping.
  • Yol Valley: The Yol Valley is a protected area in the middle of the park that is known for its stunning scenery and unique wildlife. You can hike through the valley and see the ice formations that remain even in the hottest summer months.
  • Tsenkher Cave: The Tsenkher Cave is a large limestone cave located in the eastern part of the park. It is known for its stunning natural formations and is also a site where many ancient artifacts have been discovered.

Natural Attractions of Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tsagaan Suvarga: Tsagaan Suvarga is a geological formation that is located in the southeastern part of the park. It is made up of huge white limestone formations that rise up to 30 meters high and is a popular place for hiking and rock climbing.
  • Altai Mountains: The Altai Mountains are located in the western part of the park and are a popular place for hiking and camping. They are home to many rare and endangered species of animals and plants, including the Argali sheep, ibex, and snow leopard.
  • Eagle Valley: Eagle Valley, also known as Yolyn Am, is a deep canyon that is located in the middle of the park. It is home to many rare species of birds and animals, including the lammergeier eagle.
  • Bayan Mountain: Bayan Mountain is located in the eastern part of the park and is known for its unique rock formations and stunning views. It is a popular place for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Yol Valley to Gobi Glacier: This 7-kilometer hike takes you through the stunning Yol Valley and ends at the Gobi Glacier, where you can see the ice formations that remain throughout the year.
  • Khongor Sand Dunes Loop: This 20-kilometer loop takes you through the Khongor Sand Dunes and is a great way to experience the landscape of the park. You can also opt to ride camels instead of hiking.
  • Bayan Mountain Trek: This trek takes you to the top of Bayan Mountain, where you can see stunning views of the park. It is a 12-kilometer round trip and takes around 6 hours to complete.
  • Tsagaan Suvarga Loop: This 15-kilometer loop takes you through the stunning white limestone formations of Tsagaan Suvarga.

Best Things to Do in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Camel Riding: Riding camels is a popular activity in the park and allows you to explore the vast landscapes in a unique way.
  • Bird Watching: Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is home to many rare species of birds, making it a great place for bird watching.
  • Jeep Tours: Jeep tours are a great way to explore the park and can take you to some of the more remote areas of the park.
  • Cultural Tours: There are many cultural tours available in the park that allow you to learn more about the history and traditions of Mongolia.

If you have a week to explore Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park, here is a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

Mongolian cuisine is unique and flavorful, and Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khuushuur: Khuushuur is a traditional Mongolian dish made with meat (usually lamb) that is fried in a dough pocket.
  • Buuz: Buuz is a steamed dumpling that is filled with meat and vegetables. It is a staple food in Mongolia.
  • Milk Tea: Milk tea is a popular drink in Mongolia and is made with black tea and milk.

Local Beverages

  • Airag: Airag is a traditional Mongolian drink made from fermented horse milk.
  • Vodka: Vodka is the most popular alcoholic drink in Mongolia and is often made from fermented milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

Mongolian culture and traditions are deeply rooted in the natural environment, and this is especially true in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park. The park is home to many nomadic families who have maintained their traditional way of life for centuries. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Dress Code: Mongolian culture is conservative, and it is recommended that visitors dress modestly while in the park.
  • Respect for the Environment: Mongolians have a deep respect for the environment, and it is important to not litter or damage the natural landscape while visiting the park.
  • Hospitality: Mongolian hospitality is famous for its warmth and generosity, and it is common for visitors to be invited into a nomadic family’s ger (traditional dwelling) for a meal or a cup of tea.

Best Time to Visit Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

The best time to visit Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to November) seasons when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are vibrant. The winter months (December to March) can be harsh, with temperatures dropping to below freezing, and the summer months (July to August) can be hot and dry.

Safety and Health

Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it is important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health while visiting the park.

Safety in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

  • Weather: The weather can be unpredictable in the park, and it is important to check weather forecasts and bring appropriate clothing and gear for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Wildlife: The park is home to many rare and endangered species of animals, and it is important to keep a safe distance from wildlife and not approach them.
  • Emergency Services: Emergency services are limited in the park, and it is important to have a fully charged phone and emergency supplies before heading into the park.

Health in Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park

  • Altitude: The park is at high altitude, and it is important to take precautions to avoid altitude sickness, such as drinking plenty of water, avoiding alcohol, and taking regular breaks.
  • Food and Water: It is important to be cautious when consuming food and water in the park and to only drink bottled or boiled water and eat thoroughly cooked food.
