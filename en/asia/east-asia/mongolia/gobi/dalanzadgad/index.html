Introduction to Dalanzadgad

Dalanzadgad is a picturesque city located in the South Gobi Desert in Mongolia. It is the capital of the Umnugobi province, and its name means “broad flatland.” It has a population of around 20,000 people, and it is a remote city that can only be visited by road or air.

Being in the heart of Gobi, it is a perfect place to explore the great outdoors, meet the nomads, and learn about their culture. But that’s not all it has to offer. With its rich collection of natural wonders and cultural experiences, there is something for everyone in Dalanzadgad.

This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the must-see attractions, things to do, where to stay, and the best food and drink options that Dalanzadgad has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Dalanzadgad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gurvantes Museum: The museum introduces visitors to Gobi’s history, nature, and culture.
  • Dinosaur museum: If you are fascinated by dinosaurs, don’t forget to visit this museum that displays a collection of dinosaur fossils.
  • Teeth of the Devil: It is a natural rock formation located near the city; legend has it that it was formed by a devil who bit the mountain peak.

Natural Attractions of Dalanzadgad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Flaming Cliffs: This famous tourist spot offers striking views of the desert’s red sandstone formations.
  • Yolyn Am: A picturesque valley located in the Gobi desert that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding glaciers.
  • Gobi Gurvan Saikhan National Park: Explore the picturesque lake, Khongoryn Els, and the stunning dunes of Gobi.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dalanzadgad

  • Khongor sand dunes: With a possible length of over 180 km, the sand dunes are the perfect spot for ultra running, mountain biking, and hiking in the area.
  • Bayanzag: The area is perfect for exploring on foot, with spectacular rocky terrain and views of natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Dalanzadgad: Activities and Tours

  • Gobi Desert homestay: Experience the real desert lifestyle by spending a night in a traditional yurt, eating traditional food, and meeting with locals.
  • Camel Ride: Ride a gentle, two-humped bactrian camel and explore the Gobi Desert’s stunning landscapes.
  • Explore Local Culture: Meet local nomads, participate in traditional cultural activities such as milking goats, and learning about their lifestyle.

Food and Drink Scene of Dalanzadgad

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khorkhog: This is lamb or goat meat cooked over hot stones or metal plates
  • Buuz: This is a Mongolian version of dumplings, filled with meat and vegetables
  • Aaruul: A popular snack made from dried curd that can last for months, popular among nomads

Local Beverages

  • Airag / Khoormog: Fermented horse milk that Mongolians love which has a sour and fermented flavor.
  • Chinggis Khaan Vodka: Named after Mongolia’s famous leader Chinggis Khaan, this vodka is made in Mongolia and often served with traditional Mongolian foods.

Where to Stay in Dalanzadgad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Three Camel Lodge: A luxurious accommodation option that provides guests with a traditional Mongolian experience.
  • Gobi tourist camp: A unique camping experience in the heart of the Gobi Desert.
  • Khongoryn Els tourist camp: A perfect and stunning camping spot for nature lovers.

What to Buy in Dalanzadgad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cashmere: Mongolia is renowned for producing high-quality cashmere products perfect to warm you up in your home country.
  • Khuushuur: These are pastry pockets filled with meat and vegetables, perfect to bring home and reheat later.
  • Camel wool socks: Made from one of the most durable and insulating fibers of the world; perfect for winter conditions

Local Culture and Customs of Dalanzadgad

  • Respect local customs and beliefs, including the Tsagaan Sar (Lunar New Year) celebrations.
  • Dress appropriately according to the local conservative culture.
  • Greet older people by touching your wrist with your right hand and offering them a handshake.

Best Time to Visit Dalanzadgad

The best time to visit is between May and September. The weather is dry and warm, and visitors can enjoy the desert’s stunning landscapes without experiencing the harsh cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dalanzadgad

Do:

  • Takeout travel insurance before coming to Mongolia.
  • Respect the environment and avoid leaving trash in the desert.
  • Carry enough local currency to cover your costs; cash is preferred in remote areas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t insult their traditions or customs.
  • Don’t take photographs without asking first.
  • Don’t trust strangers who try to sell you cheap tours or souvenirs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dalanzadgad

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching can occur in urban areas.
  • Visitors should stay within marked areas in the desert to avoid getting lost or stranded.
  • Take the necessary safety precautions when hiking, camping, or on any adventure activity.

Health in Dalanzadgad

  • Visitors should carry adequate medications and travel insurance coverage before coming to Mongolia.
  • Drink bottled water for safety purposes.
  • Seek medical attention immediately in case of any illness, with a hospital located in the city center.

Getting to Dalanzadgad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dalanzadgad

  • Dalanzadgad airport is the primary airport, with flights from Mongolia’s capital, Ulaanbaatar, twice or thrice a week.

Bus Stations Near Dalanzadgad

  • Buses run between Ulaanbaatar and Dalanzadgad daily, with a travel time of around 12 hours.

Other Options

  • Hiring a driver or renting a car is a popular transportation option for visitors.
  • A non-stop or direct shared taxi from Ulaanbaatar provides an affordable option.

How to Get Around in Dalanzadgad

  • Private vehicles or taxis are the most convenient mode of transport in the city. However, walking is the easiest option for getting around the town center.

Where to Go Next from Dalanzadgad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Bayanzag cliffs, famous for their red sandstone formations.
  • The nearby small and charming tourist town of Kharkhorin, famous for its Erdene Zuu Monastery.
  • The famous Genghis Khan Mausoleum, located close to Ulaanbaatar.

Conclusion

Dalanzadgad is a beautiful and remote city that serves as the perfect base for exploring the majestic desert vistas of the Gobi. With rich in natural landscapes, local culture, and delectable cuisine, this is undoubtedly a destination not to be missed when visiting Mongolia. Take full advantage of this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and create unforgettable memories from your trip.
