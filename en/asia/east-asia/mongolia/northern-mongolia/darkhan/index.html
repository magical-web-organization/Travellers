Darkhan is a city in northern Mongolia, located in the Selenge Province. Although often overlooked by tourists in favor of more commonly visited Mongolian destinations, Darkhan has much to offer for those looking to delve deeper into the country’s rich culture and natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will highlight the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and accommodations to consider during your trip to Darkhan.

Introduction to Darkhan

Founded in 1961, Darkhan was established as an industrial center during Mongolia’s socialist period. Today, it is a city of over 83,000 people, known for its quiet streets and clean air. Despite its relatively recent founding, Darkhan is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Darkhan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Darkhan has several museums and historical places that offer insight into Mongolia’s past and present:

  • Darkhan Museum of History and Ethnography: This museum offers a comprehensive look at the region’s history and culture, including exhibits on traditional Mongolian clothing, household items, and musical instruments.
  • Darkhan Cultural Center: This center features performances by local musicians, dancers, and actors.
  • Lenin Monument: A symbol of Mongolia’s communist past, this monument is located in the central square of Darkhan.

Natural Attractions of Darkhan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Darkhan is surrounded by majestic natural scenery, including:

  • Selenge River: This river is the second-longest river in Mongolia, stretching over 1,000 kilometers.
  • Ikh Mongol Uul: This mountain is located to the east of Darkhan and provides panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscape.
  • Amarbayasgalant Monastery: Located about 60 kilometers south of Darkhan, this 18th-century monastery is situated in a lush valley surrounded by mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Darkhan

Darkhan is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Ikh Mongol Uul Hike: This hike up Ikh Mongol Uul takes approximately three hours and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Selenge River Bike Trail: This trail follows the Selenge River and is perfect for those looking for a leisurely ride through the countryside.
  • Khar Yamaat Hike: This hike takes you up to the top of Khar Yamaat Hill, which offers panoramic views of Darkhan and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Darkhan: Activities and Tours

Darkhan has a variety of activities and tours that allow visitors to experience the city and surrounding area:

  • Horse Trekking Tours: Darkhan is an excellent starting point for horse trekking tours into the surrounding countryside.
  • Yak Riding Tours: Visitors can take a yak riding tour through the lush hills and valleys surrounding the city.
  • Hot Springs: Janchivlan Hot Springs, located about 15 kilometers outside of Darkhan, is a popular spot for relaxing and rejuvenating in natural hot springs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Darkhan

Day 1: Arrival and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit the Darkhan Museum of History and Ethnography and the Darkhan Cultural Center. Day 3: Hike up Ikh Mongol Uul or take a horse trekking tour into the surrounding countryside. Day 4: Drive south to visit Amarbayasgalant Monastery. Day 5: Take a yak riding tour through the hills and valleys surrounding Darkhan. Day 6: Relax at Janchivlan Hot Springs. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Darkhan

Mongolian cuisine is hearty and meat-heavy, with a variety of dishes that reflect the country’s nomadic past. In Darkhan, travelers can try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khuushuur: Fried meat dumplings, often filled with lamb, beef, or horse meat.
  • Buuz: Steamed meat dumplings, often filled with beef or mutton.
  • Khorkhog: A traditional Mongolian dish made by cooking meat, usually lamb or mutton, and vegetables together in a sealed pot with hot stones.

Local Beverages

  • Airag: A traditional Mongolian drink made from fermented mare’s milk.
  • Mongolian Tea: A type of tea made with milk, salt, and butter. It is a staple drink in Mongolian culture and is often served during meals.

Where to Stay in Darkhan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Darkhan has a variety of accommodation options for travelers, including:

  • Hotel Darkhan: A modern hotel located in the city center.
  • Darkhan Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers both private and shared rooms.
  • Ger Guesthouse: A traditional Mongolian yurt that provides a unique lodging experience.

What to Buy in Darkhan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Darkhan is known for its handmade crafts and artisanal products, including:

  • Felted Wool Products: Mongolian nomads often use felted wool to make clothing and household items, including blankets, slippers, and hats.
  • Horsehair Products: Horsehair is a versatile material often used to create ropes, belts, and other decorative items.
  • Leather Products: Mongolian leather is known for its quality and durability and is used to create a variety of products, including bags, wallets, and belts.

Local Culture and Customs of Darkhan

Mongolian culture is deeply influenced by its nomadic past and close relationship with nature. Visitors to Darkhan should be aware of the following customs:

  • Greetings: It is customary to greet others with a handshake and a smile.
  • Removing Shoes: It is customary to remove shoes before entering a Mongolian home or temple.
  • Respect for Elders: Mongolian culture places great emphasis on respecting elders, and they should be addressed with proper titles and gestures of respect.

Best Time to Visit Darkhan

The best time to visit Darkhan is during the warmer months of June through August, when temperatures range from 10°C to 20°C. However, travelers should be aware that the weather in Mongolia can be unpredictable and prepare for sudden temperature drops and changes in weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Darkhan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress in layers to prepare for sudden changes in weather.
  • Try traditional Mongolian dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head or pass objects over their head, as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Wear shoes inside a Mongolian home or temple.
  • Take photos of individuals or religious items without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Darkhan

Darkhan is generally a safe destination for travelers, but they should take common-sense precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep belongings secure and watch out for pickpockets.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Darkhan

Travelers to Darkhan should take precautions to protect against altitude sickness, as the city is located at an altitude of 640 meters. Visitors should also be aware of the potential risk of tick-borne illnesses, such as tick-borne encephalitis and lyme disease, and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Darkhan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Darkhan

The nearest airport to Darkhan is the Amarbayasgalant Airport, located about 10 kilometers outside of the city. However, this airport has limited service, and most travelers to Darkhan fly into the Chinggis Khaan International Airport in Ulaanbaatar, about 240 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Darkhan

Darkhan has several bus stations that offer service to Ulaanbaatar and other destinations throughout Mongolia.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Darkhan by train from Ulaanbaatar or Russia.

How to Get Around in Darkhan

Darkhan is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by using local transportation, such as buses or taxis. Bicycles and motorcycles are also available for rent, and visitors can arrange for private transportation for longer excursions outside the city.

Where to Go Next from Darkhan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Several destinations near Darkhan offer opportunities for further exploration of Mongolia, including:

  • Ulaanbaatar: Mongolia’s capital city offers further insights into the country’s history and culture.
  • Lake Khövsgöl: Located about 400 kilometers north of Darkhan, this pristine lake is considered one of Mongolia’s most beautiful natural attractions.
  • Khustain Nuruu National Park: Located about 100 kilometers southwest of Ulaanbaatar, this park is home to Mongolia’s wild horse population and other wildlife.

Conclusion

Darkhan has much to offer for travelers seeking a deeper understanding of Mongolia’s history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether hiking through the surrounding hills and mountains, trying traditional Mongolian dishes and drinks, or exploring the city’s museums and cultural sites, visitors to Darkhan are sure to have a memorable and enriching experience.
