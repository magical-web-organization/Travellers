Introduction to Sühbaatar

Sühbaatar is a picturesque city situated in the northernmost region of Mongolia. It is known for its rich historical and cultural heritage, outstanding natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor activities. The city is named after Damdin Sükhbaatar, a national hero, who played a vital role in the country’s independence and revolution.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we have curated a list of the top things to do, see, and experience in Sühbaatar, Mongolia. From exploring culturally significant attractions to indulging in local cuisine, hiking and biking, and venturing into the stunning surrounding countryside, this guide has everything you need to plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Sühbaatar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sükhbaatar Museum - One of the best places to learn about Sühbaatar’s history and culture. The museum displays various artifacts, documents, and pictures related to the national hero Damdin Sükhbaatar and country’s revolution.
  • Monument of Damdin Sükhbaatar - Situated in the city center, the monument is a must-visit attraction. The statue of the national hero on horseback symbolizes Mongolia’s independence and revolution.
  • Sühbaatar Square - Another important landmark of the city, the square is surrounded by government buildings and features a prominent statue of Genghis Khan.
  • Zavkhan River - The river cuts through the city, offering picturesque views and recreational activities like fishing, camping, and rafting.

Natural Attractions of Sühbaatar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Selenge River - One of the most important rivers in Mongolia, Selenge River is situated close to Sühbaatar. Enjoy rafting, kayaking, and boating into the stunning countryside along the river.
  • Amarbayasgalant Monastery - Visit the picturesque Buddhist monastery situated in the Selenge River valley. The architecture and style of the monastery are well worth a visit and recognized on the UNESCO World Heritage List.
  • Numrug National Park - Only a few kilometers away from the city, the national park offers scenic hikes, camping, and horseback riding adventures. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna including moose, musk oxen, and wild boar.
  • Khövsgöl Lake - The deepest and freshest lake in Mongolia. Take a scenic drive to Khövsgöl to explore natural beauty, wildlife, and water sports in the clear waters of the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sühbaatar

  • Mount Munkhkhairkhan - A prominent peak in the northern region of Mongolia, the mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Hiking is the most popular way to explore the natural beauty of the mountain.
  • Zulbaatar mountain - The easy day trip near the city of Sükhbaatar includes hiking, picnicking, and a visit to the historic Zulbaatar Monastery.
  • Selenge River Trail - Best for mountain biking enthusiasts, cycling along the pristine river through the forest trail, gives you an up-close and personal with the surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Sühbaatar: Activities and Tours

  • Nomad Eagle Festival - Every September in the land of the blue sky, participate in one of the most unique festivals in Mongolia. The two-day event includes eagle-hunting competition, traditional dances, and games.
  • Horseback riding – Mongolia is known for its horsemanship skills, and it is a great opportunity to join experienced Mongolian horsemen exploring the natural beauty of the countryside on horseback
  • Visit a ger camp – A unique and authentic Mongolian cultural experience is to stay in a ger camp in the countryside, learn about the nomadic lifestyle, and join the locals in various outdoor activities.
  • Attend a Naadam Festival - Enjoy the three traditional games of wrestling, archery, and horse racing, organised once a year during July.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sühbaatar

Here’s an example itinerary for exploring Sühbaatar over a week-long trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Sühbaatar, rest and acclimate Day 2: Visit the Sükhbaatar Square and the Monument of Damdin Sükhbaatar Day 3: Explore the Selenge River and Amarbayasgalant Monastery Day 4: Hike Mount Munkhkhairkhan or visit the traditional Zulbaatar Monastery Day 5: Take a scenic drive or tour to Khövsgöl Lake Day 6: Visit local families in a ger camp, learn about nomadic life and indulge in local cuisine Day 7: Participate in outdoor activities like horseback riding, rafting or fishing

Food and Drink Scene of Sühbaatar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buuz - Mongolia’s traditional steamed dumplings, filled with minced meat (beef or mutton) and onion.
  • Khorkhog - A delightful delicacy consisting of meat (beef, goat, or lamb) cooked inside the skin of a sheep.
  • Aaruul - Fermented yoghurt that is dried and shaped into small pieces.

Local Beverages

  • Airag- Fermented mare’s milk, a popular summer drink in Mongolia.
  • Vodka- A traditional alcoholic beverage distilled from fermented grains and potatoes.

Where to Stay in Sühbaatar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Khan of Sühbaatar - A 4-star hotel located in the center of the city, that offers comfortable rooms and excellent facilities.
  • Ödval Bileg Guesthouse - The guesthouse is well known for its friendly environment and family-like atmosphere, located near the stunning Zulbaatar Monastery.
  • Khövsgöl Lodge - situated near Khövsgöl Lake, this lodge offers peaceful accommodation in a remote and idyllic location.

What to Buy in Sühbaatar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Mongolian clothing and accessories, such as a a “del” (a long traditional Mongolian robe), and “Deel -chir” (traditional shoes).
  • Handicrafts, including traditional jewelry, embroidered belts, cashmere woolen goods, and felt items.
  • Local food products like airag, aaruul, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Sühbaatar

Mongolian culture is deeply influenced by their nomadic heritage, and hospitality is an essential aspect of Mongolian life. The locals are known for their friendly nature, and traditional customs such as respect for the elderly, nature, and the spirits of the land.

Best Time to Visit Sühbaatar

The best time to visit Sühbaatar is from June to August when the weather is pleasant and the landscape is green, with long sunlit days. Travelers can indulge in outdoor activities, attend local festivals, and explore the stunning countryside.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sühbaatar

Do:

  • Dress modestly, avoiding revealing clothes.
  • Remove shoes before entering a Mongolian yurt (ger) or a temple
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions
  • Try to learn some Mongolian phrases or greetings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect any religious or cultural beliefs.
  • Touch or damage any historical relics
  • Litter or harm any wildlife or nature during your trip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sühbaatar

Sühbaatar is a safe city to visit, and the locals are welcoming to tourists. However, it is always necessary to take precautions like locking hotel room doors and leaving valuables in hotel room safes or locker.

Health in Sühbaatar

Make sure to drink only bottled or boiled water, especially during summer when airag (fermented mare’s milk) is more commonly served. Learn about the food and the ingredients used during your trip as some local dishes may contain specific spices or herbs that may not suit your appetite.

Getting to Sühbaatar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sühbaatar

The nearest international airport to Sühbaatar is Chinggis Khaan International Airport in Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia. From there, you can take a domestic flight to Sühbaatar Airport, which is only 7 kilometers away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Sühbaatar

Sühbaatar Bus Station is situated in the city center, and buses run regularly between Sühbaatar and Ulaanbaatar. Buses are an affordable and comfortable means of transportation.

Other Options

You can rent a car or hire a private driver to explore the region on your own schedule. Several well-known car companies have operations in Mongolia.

How to Get Around in Sühbaatar

The best way to get around the city is by hiring a taxi – either pre-arranged with your hotel or on the street. However, rental cars are also available, including a growing number of SUV rental options.

Where to Go Next from Sühbaatar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several other fascinating destinations worth exploring near Sühbaatar. Here are some examples of nearby destinations:

  • Ulaanbaatar - The Mongolian capital is a vibrant city with a thriving cultural scene and numerous historical landmarks.
  • Bulgan city - A small town located in the Northern Province. It has several interesting Buddhist temples and natural wonders.
  • Khuvsgul Province - The province is home to stunning landscapes, including the famous Khövsgöl Lake, and is perfect for outdoor exploration.

Conclusion

Sühbaatar, located in the northernmost region of Mongolia, offers a fascinating combination of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. With its stunning historical landmarks, picturesque landscapes, authentic Mongolian culture, and friendly locals, Sühbaatar is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for an immersive experience in Mongolia.
