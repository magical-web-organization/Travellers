Introduction to Ulaanbaatar

Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia’s capital city, is a fascinating mix of the ancient and the modern. This vibrant metropolis is home to over 1.4 million people and is the country’s economic, cultural, and political center. It provides a unique opportunity to experience traditional Mongolian culture while also enjoying modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Ulaanbaatar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of Mongolia

The National Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in Mongolian history and culture. It houses over 60,000 artifacts, including ancient costumes, weapons, and tools, that showcase the country’s rich history.

Gandantegchenlin Monastery

Gandantegchenlin Monastery is a serene Buddhist monastery located in the heart of Ulaanbaatar. The monastery is home to over 150 monks and is the center of Mongolian Buddhism.

Chinggis Khaan Statue Complex

The Chinggis Khaan Statue Complex is an impressive 131-foot tall statue of Chinggis Khaan, the founder of the Mongol Empire. It is located just outside the city and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Ulaanbaatar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Located just 50 km from Ulaanbaatar, Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is a stunning natural wonderland. Visitors can enjoy hiking, horseback riding, and camping while surrounded by breathtaking scenery.

Bogd Khan Mountain National Park

Bogd Khan Mountain National Park is a beautiful protected area situated on the outskirts of Ulaanbaatar. It offers some of the best hiking and skiing opportunities in Mongolia and is an ideal place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ulaanbaatar

Zaisan Memorial Hill

Zaisan Memorial Hill is a popular hiking spot located on the outskirts of the city. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill for panoramic views of Ulaanbaatar and the surrounding countryside.

Khustain Nuruu National Park

Khustain Nuruu National Park is a stunning natural oasis that offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the country. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the rolling hills and grasslands while experiencing the unique flora and fauna of Mongolia.

Best Things to Do in Ulaanbaatar: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding Tour

Horseback riding is an essential part of Mongolian culture, and visitors can experience it firsthand with a horseback riding tour. These tours take visitors on scenic routes through the countryside, and some include camping or staying with nomadic Mongolian families.

Nomadic Life Experience Tour

Nomadic Life Experience Tours allow visitors to experience traditional Mongolian culture firsthand. They typically involve staying with nomadic families, trying local food, and learning traditional crafts and skills.

Food and Drink Scene of Ulaanbaatar

Must-Try Dishes

Mongolian Barbecue

Mongolian Barbecue is a staple dish in Mongolia. It typically consists of thinly sliced meat, vegetables, and noodles cooked on a large metal grill.

Khorkhog

Khorkhog is a traditional Mongolian dish that involves cooking meat and vegetables in a pot with hot stones. The result is tender, flavorful meat with a slight smoky taste.

Local Beverages

Airag

Airag is a type of fermented horse milk that is a staple beverage in Mongolia. It has a slightly sour taste and is traditionally served cold.

Mongolian Beer

Mongolian beer is gaining popularity worldwide. They have a unique flavor and are brewed with traditional ingredients, such as barley and wheat.

Where to Stay in Ulaanbaatar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Blue Sky Hotel and Tower

The Blue Sky Hotel and Tower is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Ulaanbaatar. It offers modern amenities, such as a swimming pool and spa, and stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside.

UB Guesthouse

UB Guesthouse is a budget-friendly option located near the city center. It offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ulaanbaatar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cashmere Products

Mongolia is known for its high-quality cashmere products, such as sweaters, scarves, and blankets. Visitors can find a wide variety of cashmere products in Ulaanbaatar.

Traditional Crafts

Traditional Mongolian crafts, such as embroidered textiles and handmade boots, make unique souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Ulaanbaatar

Mongolian culture is deeply rooted in traditions and customs that date back centuries. Visitors should be respectful of these traditions, such as removing shoes when entering a home and being mindful of personal space.

Best Time to Visit Ulaanbaatar

The best time to visit Ulaanbaatar is between May and September when the weather is warm and dry. Winters in Mongolia can be harsh and frigid, making travel difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ulaanbaatar

Do:

  • Do research Mongolian culture and customs before you visit.
  • Do try traditional Mongolian cuisine, such as Mongolian barbecue.
  • Do dress appropriately for the weather and local customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect Mongolian traditions and customs.
  • Don’t take photos or videos of people without their consent.
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ulaanbaatar

While Ulaanbaatar is generally safe for visitors, petty crime can occur. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash on them.

Health in Ulaanbaatar

Visitors should check with their doctor before traveling to Mongolia to ensure they are up to date on necessary vaccinations. It is also important to drink bottled water and take precautions to avoid foodborne illnesses.

Getting to Ulaanbaatar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ulaanbaatar

Chinggis Khaan International Airport is the main airport in Ulaanbaatar.

Bus Stations Near Ulaanbaatar

The Central Bus Station is the main bus station in Ulaanbaatar and provides routes to various destinations across Mongolia.

Other Options

There are also train routes available to Ulaanbaatar from neighboring countries, such as Russia and China.

How to Get Around in Ulaanbaatar

Taxis and public transportation are available in Ulaanbaatar. Visitors can also opt to rent a car or bicycle to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Ulaanbaatar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kharkhorin

Kharkhorin, also known as Karakorum, was once the capital of the Mongol Empire. Today, the city is a popular tourist destination with several historic attractions, including the Erdene Zuu Monastery.

Lake Khovsgol

Lake Khovsgol, located in northern Mongolia, is one of the country’s most beautiful natural wonders. Visitors can enjoy hiking, kayaking, and horseback riding while taking in the stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Ulaanbaatar is a unique and exciting destination, offering a blend of traditional Mongolian culture and modern amenities. With its stunning natural beauty, historic landmarks, and vibrant city life, Ulaanbaatar has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan an unforgettable trip to Ulaanbaatar.
