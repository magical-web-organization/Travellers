Karakorum, situated in Central Mongolia, is a timeless destination that has managed to preserve its ancient, cultural essence. Being the ancient capital of the Mongol empire and a key hub of the ancient Silk Road, Karakorum features spectacular historical monuments, museums, and rich natural attractions that appeal to people from all around the world.

In this travel guide, we’ll help you explore the best of Karakorum, plan your itinerary, and discover the hidden gems of this fascinating tourist destination.

Introduction to Karakorum

Karakorum is located in central Mongolia and is considered one of the must-visit destinations for travel enthusiasts. The region is known for its vast plains, lush forests, and rolling hills. Visitors are transported to a bygone era, to when the city was considered the epicenter of Mongolian culture and the capital of the Mongol empire.

Local Culture and Customs of Karakorum

Mongolian culture is one of the oldest in the world and is deeply embedded in Karakorum. Visitors can discover many aspects of traditional Mongolian life by visiting the local monasteries, nomadic camps, and museums. Learning about and respecting the traditions and customs of the locals is an essential aspect of traveling to Karakorum.

Best Time to Visit Karakorum

Karakorum experiences a continental climate; therefore, it can be visited all year round. However, the best time to visit Karakorum is in the summer months, between June and early September, when the weather is mild, and the scenery is lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Karakorum

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture.
  2. Try the traditional Mongolian dishes and drinks.
  3. Engage and interact with the locals.
  4. Visit the local museums and monasteries.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the local culture.
  2. Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  3. Use or consume illegal drugs.
  4. Be loud or disruptive, especially in public places.

Safety and Health

Visitors must remain cautious while traveling to Karakorum. Here are some tips to ensure that you remain safe and healthy:

Safety in Karakorum

  1. Avoid walking alone in the dark.
  2. Keep your valuables with you at all times.
  3. Respect the laws and customs of the local culture.

Health in Karakorum

  1. Ensure you have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling.
  2. Always drink bottled or boiled water.
  3. Apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing in sunny weather.
