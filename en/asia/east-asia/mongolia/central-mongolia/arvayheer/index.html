Introduction to Arvayheer

Arvayheer is a small town located in the central province of Mongolia. It is situated in an area surrounded by mountains, and the valley of the Arhangai River runs through the town. Arvayheer is a popular destination for travelers who are looking for a laid back, off-the-beaten-path experience while exploring Mongolia. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to Arvayheer.

List of Places to Visit in Arvayheer

Arvayheer may be small compared to other cities in Mongolia, but it still has a lot to offer. Here are some must-visit places when in Arvayheer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arvayheer History Museum

Arvayheer History Museum is located in the heart of the town, and it’s considered as one of the best museums in the region. The museum showcases the history and culture of the Arhangai province, including the local flora and fauna, traditional clothing, and traditional crafts.

Tuvkhun Monastery

Tuvkhun Monastery is a hidden gem located in the outskirts of Arvayheer. This Buddhist temple was built in the 17th century, and it’s considered one of the most sacred places in Mongolia. The monastery is perched on top of a mountain, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding valley.

Natural Attractions of Arvayheer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Kharvaa

Mount Kharvaa is a popular destination for hikers and mountaineers. It’s located just a few kilometers away from Arvayheer, and it offers stunning panoramic views of the valley. The hike to the top of the mountain takes around 4-5 hours, and it can be quite challenging, so make sure to bring sturdy hiking shoes and plenty of water.

Terkh White Lake

Terkh White Lake is a beautiful lake surrounded by mountains and pine trees. It’s located around 60 kilometers south of Arvayheer, and it’s a popular destination for camping, fishing, and hiking. The lake gets its name from the white limestone deposits that line its shores, giving the water an opaque appearance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arvayheer

Arvayheer and the surrounding areas are a hiker’s paradise. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the region:

Mount Kharvaa Hike

The Mount Kharvaa hike is a challenging but rewarding hike that takes you to the summit of the mountain. The trail is well-marked, and it offers stunning views of the valley.

Tuvkhun Monastery Hike

The Tuvkhun Monastery hike is a moderate hike that takes you to the sacred monastery. The trail leads you through dense pine forests, meadows, and rocky terrain, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding valley.

Best Things to Do in Arvayheer: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding

Horseback riding is a popular activity in Mongolia, and it’s the perfect way to explore the countryside around Arvayheer. There are plenty of local operators who offer horseback riding tours, ranging from a few hours to a few days.

Hot Springs

There are several natural hot springs located around Arvayheer, and they’re the perfect way to relax after a long day of hiking or exploring. The most popular hot springs are located at Tsenkher Jiguur and Orkhon Waterfall.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arvayheer

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Arvayheer:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Arvayheer, visit Arvayheer History Museum.
  • Day 2: Hike to the top of Mount Kharvaa.
  • Day 3: Visit Tuvkhun Monastery, take a horseback riding tour.
  • Day 4: Explore the Orkhon Valley, visit the Orkhon Waterfall.
  • Day 5: Visit Tsenkher Jiguur Hot Springs.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Terkh White Lake.
  • Day 7: Depart from Arvayheer.

Food and Drink Scene of Arvayheer

Arvayheer may be a small town, but it still has some great food and drink options. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khorkhog: A traditional Mongolian dish that consists of goat or lamb meat cooked with vegetables in a cast iron pot.
  • Buuz: Mongolian dumplings filled with meat and vegetables.
  • Khuushuur: Deep-fried meat pies.

Local Beverages

  • Airag: Fermented mare’s milk, a popular drink in Mongolia.
  • Suutei Tsai: A salty milk tea that’s a staple in Mongolian cuisine.

What to Buy in Arvayheer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arvayheer is the perfect place to shop for traditional Mongolian souvenirs and handicrafts. Some of the best things to buy in Arvayheer are:

  • Traditional Mongolian clothing, such as the deel.
  • Handmade woolen carpets and blankets.
  • Leather crafts, such as belts and wallets.

Local Culture and Customs of Arvayheer

Mongolian culture is deeply rooted in nomadic traditions, and Arvayheer is no exception. If you’re planning to visit Arvayheer, here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Always ask for permission before taking pictures of people and their belongings.
  • Avoid pointing your feet at people or objects, which is considered disrespectful in Mongolian culture.

Best Time to Visit Arvayheer

The best time to visit Arvayheer is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and dry. However, keep in mind that this is also the busiest time of the year, so expect higher prices and more crowds. Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are also good times to visit Arvayheer, as the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arvayheer

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food.
  • Bring warm clothing, even in the summer, as temperatures can get cold at night.

Don’t:

  • Point your feet at people or objects.
  • Take pictures of people without asking for permission.
  • Speak loudly in public places, as this is considered impolite in Mongolian culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arvayheer

Arvayheer is a safe place to visit, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your belongings with you at all times.
  • Be cautious when taking taxis, and make sure to agree on a price beforehand.

Health in Arvayheer

The health care system in Mongolia is basic, so it’s important to take precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink only bottled water, and avoid ice cubes and tap water.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Pack a basic first aid kit with essentials such as band-aids, pain relievers, and antihistamines.

Getting to Arvayheer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arvayheer

The nearest airport to Arvayheer is in Ulaanbaatar, the capital of Mongolia, which is around 380 kilometers away. From there, you can take a bus or a private car to Arvayheer.

Bus Stations Near Arvayheer

Arvayheer has a bus station that connects it to other parts of Mongolia. Some popular destinations include Ulaanbaatar, Karakorum, and Kharkhorin.

Other Options

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also travel to Arvayheer by bike or on horseback.

How to Get Around in Arvayheer

Arvayheer is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can rent a car, hire a taxi or take a local bus.

Where to Go Next from Arvayheer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you still have time to explore after you’ve seen everything there is to see in Arvayheer, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Karakorum: The ancient capital of Mongolia, located around 200 kilometers east of Arvayheer.
  • Kharkhorin: A small town located in the Orkhon Valley, known for its historical sites.
  • Terelj National Park: A popular national park located near Ulaanbaatar, known for its stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Arvayheer may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of natural and cultural attractions that are sure to delight any traveler. From hiking to hot springs, there’s something for everyone in Arvayheer. So why wait? Start planning your journey to Arvayheer today!
