Introduction to Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Located in Central Mongolia, Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is a stunning destination offering a mix of natural beauty and cultural treasures. Covering an area of over 290,000 hectares, the park is known for its picturesque landscapes, including rock formations, rivers, and rolling hills. Visitors to Gorkhi-Terelj National Park can also explore historical and cultural attractions, including museums, ancient temples, and traditional Mongolian ger camps.

List of Places to Visit in Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Turtle Rock - a natural rock formation resembling a turtle. Visitors can climb on the rock and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Aryabal Meditation Temple - a peaceful temple surrounded by beautiful gardens and offering stunning views of the park. Visitors can participate in meditation classes or enjoy a traditional tea ceremony.
  • Genghis Khan Statue Complex - featuring a large statue of Genghis Khan and a museum documenting Mongolian history and culture.
  • Mongol Nomadic Ger Camp - a traditional Mongolian ger camp offering cultural experiences, such as horseback riding and traditional food.

Natural Attractions of Gorkhi-Terelj National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chinggis Khaan’s horse statue - a larger-than-life statue of Genghis Khan riding a horse. Visitors can climb to the top for panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Terelj River - a stunning, crystal-clear river perfect for fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • The Aryabal Hill - a scenic hill overlooking the park with a Buddhist temple and small Buddhist statues adorning it, visitors can also enjoy zipline adventures
  • Tergel Lodge - a beautiful lodge surrounded by nature offering cultural experiences, outdoor activities, and serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

  • Turtle Rock Circuit - a moderately easy hike that takes visitors past the iconic Turtle Rock formation and through scenic valleys and hills.
  • Terelj River Circuit - a multi-day hike along the Terelj River, featuring stunning views and opportunities for wildlife spotting.
  • Gorkhi-Terelj Transverse Trail - a challenging hike covering over 60km of diverse terrain and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Gorkhi-Terelj National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding - horse riding tours are available throughout the park, offering visitors a unique perspective and the opportunity to interact with local nomads and their herds.
  • Meditation Classes - visitors can participate in meditation classes at the Aryabal Meditation Temple, learning techniques used by Buddhist monks for centuries.
  • Traditional Ger Camping - stay in a traditional Mongolian ger camp and experience the nomadic lifestyle, including local meals and games.
  • Adventure and Sports Activities - adventurous visitors can enjoy zipline and horseback riding experiences, rock climbing, archery, and river rafting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Day 1: Arrive at Gorkhi-Terelj National Park Day 2: Explore cultural attractions, including the Genghis Khan Statue Complex and Mongol Nomadic Ger Camp Day 3: Hiking day, try the Turtle Rock Circuit Day 4: Cultural day, visit the Aryabal Meditation Temple and do meditation classes Day 5: Adventure and sports day, visit the Terelj River for water-based activities Day 6: Relaxing day, stay at the Tergel Lodge Day 7: Depart from Gorkhi-Terelj National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buuz - steamed dumplings filled with beef or mutton and served with a dipping sauce.
  • Khorkhog - a traditional Mongolian dish consisting of lamb or goat meat and vegetables cooked inside a sealed container with heated stones.
  • Airag - a traditional fermented drink made from mare’s milk, known for its tangy flavor and mild alcoholic content.

Local Beverages

  • Arkhi - a traditional Mongolian distilled spirit made from fermented milk, grain, and water.
  • Suutei Tsai - a traditional Mongolian salty tea made with milk, tea leaves, and salt, a staple drink in Mongolia.

Where to Stay in Gorkhi-Terelj National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tergel Lodge - an all-inclusive lodge surrounded by nature, offering cultural experiences, outdoor activities, and serene surroundings.
  • Nomads Ger Camp - a traditional Mongolian ger camp offering cultural experiences, horseback riding, and traditional food and drink.
  • Munkh Tenger Ger Resort - a family-friendly resort with a beautiful garden, bicycle rental, and traditional Mongolian ger accommodations.
  • Eco Ger Camp - a rustic camp offering nature experiences, including hikes, bird watching, and horseback riding.

What to Buy in Gorki-Terelj National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cashmere Products - Mongolia is known for its high-quality cashmere wool, and visitors can purchase items such as scarves, gloves, and clothing made from this luxurious material.
  • Traditional Mongolian Clothing - visitors can purchase traditional Mongolian clothing, including deel (a traditional coat-like garment) and hats made from lamb’s wool.
  • Crafts and Souvenirs - local markets and shops offer a variety of handmade crafts and souvenirs, including jewelry, sculptures, and traditional musical instruments.

Local Culture and Customs of Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Mongolian culture is deeply rooted in nomadic traditions and Buddhism. Visitors are expected to respect local customs, such as removing shoes before entering a ger or temple and asking permission before taking photos of locals. It is also important to dress modestly and appropriately when visiting cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

The best time to visit Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is mild and dry, and the park is at its most vibrant. However, spring and autumn can also be a beautiful time to visit, with colorful foliage and wildflowers adding to the already stunning scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural attractions.
  • Ask permission before taking photos of locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural or cultural attractions.
  • Ride horses or participate in outdoor activities if you are not confident or experienced.
  • Disrespect local beliefs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is a safe destination for visitors. However, it is important to stay alert and cautious, especially when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking, horseback riding or rafting. Visitors should also stay on marked trails and respect local wildlife to avoid any accidents, and follow safety advice given by tour providers.

Health in Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

The park has no major health risks for visitors. However, it is important to be aware of altitude sickness if you plan on hiking or climbing to higher elevations. Visitors should also be prepared for changes in weather, which can be unpredictable, and bring appropriate clothing and gear.

Getting to Gorkhi-Terelj National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Chinggis Khaan International Airport is the closest airport to Gorkhi-Terelj National Park, situated approximately 70 km to the west of the park.

Bus Stations Near Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

There are regular bus services running between Ulaanbaatar, the capital city of Mongolia, and Gorkhi-Terelj National Park. Buses depart from the Dragon Center shopping mall in Ulaanbaatar and take approximately two hours to reach the park.

Other Options

Private tours and transfers are also available from Ulaanbaatar, and many tour providers offer pick-up and drop-off services from other locations in Mongolia.

How to Get Around in Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

The best way to get around Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is by car, either by renting your own vehicle or by hiring a driver or private guide. Alternatively, visitors can participate in organized tours that offer transportation to and from the park.

Where to Go Next from Gorkhi-Terelj National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hustai National Park - a beautiful park known for its wild horses and diverse wildlife.
  • Khustain Nuruu National Park - a stunning park featuring valleys, forests, and rivers, perfect for hiking and camping.
  • Bayanzag - a unique geological formation consisting of red cliffs and rock formations, known as the “Flaming Cliffs”.
  • Ulaanbaatar - Mongolia’s bustling capital city, offering a mix of traditional culture and modern amenities.

Conclusion

Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is a beautiful destination that offers something for everyone, from cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to serene surroundings and delicious local cuisine. Follow this comprehensive guide to make the most of your visit to this stunning part of Mongolia.
