Introduction to Zuunmod

Zuunmod is a peaceful and scenic town located in the Töv Province of Mongolia, approximately 30 kilometers south of the capital city of Ulaanbaatar. The name “Zuunmod” translates to “East Town” in Mongolian, and the town is nestled between the Bogd Khan Mountain range to the west and the Khentii Mountains to the east, offering breathtaking views and outdoor activities. Zuunmod is rich in cultural history and heritage, with ancient temples and ruins dating back to the era of Genghis Khan, making it a must-visit destination in Central Mongolia.

List of Places to Visit in Zuunmod

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Töv Province Museum: The museum showcases the history and culture of the Töv Province, including exhibits on the life of the nomadic people, traditional costumes, crafts, and household items.
  • Dinosaurs’ Remains: The surrounding area of Zuunmod is known for its dinosaur remains, including a Megalosaurus skeleton discovered in the Khukhrov Formation.
  • Sükhbaatar’s Monument: Located in the town center, the monument pays tribute to the national hero Damdin Sükhbaatar, who led the Mongolian Revolution and declared Mongolia’s independence in 1921.
  • Equestrian Statue of Genghis Khan: Located near the town square, the bronze statue of the famous conqueror and warrior is 40 meters high and is one of the largest equestrian statues in the world.

Natural Attractions of Zuunmod: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khustai National Park: Located 30 km east of Zuunmod, the park is home to the endangered Przewalski’s horse, a species native to Mongolia, along with other wildlife such as wolves, deer, and eagles.
  • Bogd Khan Mountain: Located west of the town, Bogd Khan Mountain is a sacred place for Mongolians, with several temples and monasteries scattered along the mountain.
  • Terelj National Park: Located a few hours’ drive away from Zuunmod, Terelj National Park is known for its unique rock formations, stunning scenery, and outdoor activities such as hiking, horse riding, and rock climbing.
  • Tuul River: The Tuul River runs through Zuunmod, providing opportunities for fishing, swimming, and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zuunmod

  • Bogd Khan Mountain Circuit Trail: A 50km trail that leads through the Bogd Khan Mountain range, offering views of the surrounding valleys, rivers, and peaks.
  • Terelj National Park Trail: A 15km trail that leads through the park’s scenic landscapes, including rock formations and forests.
  • Tuul River Trail: A 5km trail that follows the Tuul River, providing the opportunity to enjoy the river’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Zuunmod: Activities and Tours

  • Horse riding: Explore the Mongolian steppe on horseback, experiencing the same way of life as the nomadic people.
  • Traditional Ger stay: Stay overnight in a traditional Mongolian Ger, a portable dwelling used by nomadic people, to fully experience the Mongolian culture and hospitality.
  • Visit a local family: Visit a local family in a nearby village and learn about their daily life, traditions, and customs.
  • Mongolian Folk Performance: Enjoy a traditional Mongolian performance of throat singing, horse fiddle, and dance.

Food and Drink Scene of Zuunmod

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khorkhog: A traditional Mongolian dish made by cooking meat, usually mutton, with hot stones in a sealed container, creating a unique cooking method and flavor.
  • Buuz: Steamed dumplings filled with meat, onions, and spices.
  • Khuushuur: Deep-fried pastry filled with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Airag: Fermented mare’s milk, a popular beverage among Mongolian nomads.
  • Suutei tsai: A salty tea made with milk, tea leaves, and salt.

Where to Stay in Zuunmod: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Zuunmod Guesthouse: A local guesthouse offering traditional Mongolian hospitality and accommodations.
  • Golden Sun Hotel: A mid-range hotel, offering clean rooms and amenities.
  • Steppe Nomads Hotel & Resort: A luxury hotel with breathtaking views of the countryside and a traditional Mongolian Spa.

What to Buy in Zuunmod: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cashmere products: Mongolia is known for its high-quality cashmere products, including sweaters, scarves, and blankets.
  • Morin khuur: Also known as the horse fiddle, it is a traditional Mongolian musical instrument made from horsehair and wood.
  • Deel: A traditional Mongolian garment worn by men and women, often embroidered with intricate patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Zuunmod

  • Hospitality: Mongolians are known for their exceptional hospitality, often inviting guests to their homes and providing food and drink.
  • Nomadic Lifestyle: Many Mongolian families still live a nomadic lifestyle, herding animals and moving from one place to another.
  • Respect for Elders: Mongolians have a strong sense of respect for older family members and community leaders.

Best Time to Visit Zuunmod

The best time to visit Zuunmod is during the summer months from June to August, where the weather is warm and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zuunmod

Do:

  • Learn some basic Mongolian phrases: Mongolians will appreciate if you make an effort to communicate in their language.
  • Respect the local culture: Mongolians have a proud and rich cultural heritage, so be respectful when visiting cultural sites and engaging with locals.
  • Dress appropriately: Mongolia is a conservative country, so dressing modestly is recommended.

Don’t:

  • Touch people’s heads: In Mongolian culture, the head is considered sacred, and touching it is seen as disrespectful.
  • Point with your feet: Mongolians believe that the feet are the lowest part of the body and pointing with them is ill-mannered.
  • Take photos without asking permission: Mongolians are generally friendly, but respect their privacy and ask for permission before taking their photo.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zuunmod

Zuunmod is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is always recommended to take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and staying away from unfamiliar areas after dark.

Health in Zuunmod

Mongolia has a well-established healthcare system, but medical facilities in rural areas might be limited. It is recommended to have comprehensive travel insurance, including medical coverage, before traveling to Zuunmod.

Getting to Zuunmod: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zuunmod

The closest airport to Zuunmod is Chinggis Khaan International Airport in Ulaanbaatar, approximately a one-hour drive to the town.

Bus Stations Near Zuunmod

Buses and minibusses depart frequently from the Dragon Center in Ulaanbaatar, reaching Zuunmod in approximately 45 minutes.

Other Options

Private taxis are also available, and it is recommended to arrange transportation in advance.

How to Get Around in Zuunmod

Zuunmod is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and car rentals are also available for longer distances and outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Zuunmod: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ulaanbaatar: The capital city of Mongolia, offering a vibrant city scene and cultural sites.
  • Kharkhorin: A historical city, once the capital of the Mongol Empire, offering ancient ruins and religious sites.
  • Gorkhi-Terelj National Park: A stunning national park, offering outdoor activities such as horseback riding, hiking, and camping.

Conclusion

Zuunmod is a hidden gem in Central Mongolia, offering a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With its friendly locals, welcoming hospitality, and rich history, Zuunmod is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Mongolian experience.
