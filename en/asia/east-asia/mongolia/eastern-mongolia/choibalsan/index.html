Introduction to Choibalsan

Choibalsan is a city located in the eastern part of Mongolia, near the border with China and Russia. It serves as the capital of the Dornod province and stands as one of the major transportation hubs in this region.

This city is a great tourist destination because of its rich natural setting, vibrant cultural background, and strategic location that offers a gateway to several remote Mongolian destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Choibalsan

Whether you’re a nature lover or culture buff, Choibalsan has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Choibalsan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Choibalsan Museum of Local Lore and History: Showcasing the history, art, and culture of the region, this museum is the ideal place for history buffs.
  • Ondor Gegeen Zanabazar Museum: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the life and works of Zanabazar, a renowned Buddhist monk, sculptor, and painter from the 17th century.
  • Dornod Mongol Museum: Learn about the customs and traditions of the Dornod Province, the only region in Mongolia where the Khalkha Mongols and Buryats live in harmony.

Natural Attractions of Choibalsan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buir Lake: This is one of the largest freshwater lakes in the region and is home to various species of birds.
  • Buir Nuur Strictly Protected Area: This area is a protected habitat for rare birds such as the Great Bustard, Swan Goose, and the Dalmatian Pelican.
  • Hatanbulag Hill: Situated on the outskirts of the city, this is an ideal place to hike and take in the beautiful scenery of Choibalsan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Choibalsan

Choibalsan offers various hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. Here are some popular routes:

  • Huulit Trail: This 7km trail offers a beautiful view of the city, mountains, and the Hatanbulag Hill.
  • Bayartai Trail: A 14km trail that winds through the forest and offers stunning views of the Buir River.
  • Galanbulag Trail: A 12km trail that runs along the Buir Lake and offers a beautiful view of the bird species that live near its banks.

Best Things to Do in Choibalsan: Activities and Tours

Choibalsan offers a plethora of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some popular things to do:

  • Jeep Safari: Explore the beautiful countryside of Choibalsan and learn about the nomadic lifestyle of Mongolia.
  • Horseback Riding: Mongolia is famous for its horseback riding culture, and Choibalsan is no exception. Go on a horseback ride through the beautiful landscape!
  • Visit a Nomad Family: A unique cultural experience where you can learn about the customs and traditions of the nomadic people of Mongolia.

Food and Drink Scene of Choibalsan

The food and drink scene of Choibalsan is influenced by their nomadic culture, with many dishes comprising of meat and dairy that are abundant in the region. Some must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boodog: A traditional Mongolian dish, where mutton is grilled over open flame with its skin still on.
  • Khuushuur: Fried dumplings stuffed with mutton, onions, and spices.
  • Airag: A type of fermented milk that is a staple drink in Mongolia.

Local Beverages

  • Mongolian Vodka: Made from fermented grains or potatoes, this tasty beverage packs a punch, and is present in almost every social gathering.
  • Chinggis Beer: A popular beer in Mongolia, named after the legendary Mongol warrior, Genghis Khan.

Where to Stay in Choibalsan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Choibalsan has a diverse range of accommodations to choose from, catering to all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Dornod Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Lode Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel offering clean and comfortable rooms
  • Mongoliin ger: Traditional nomadic tents that offer an authentic experience of living like a Mongolian nomad.

What to Buy in Choibalsan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home:

  • Cashmere Clothing: Mongolia is known for its soft and high-quality cashmere.
  • Falconry Accessories: Falconry is an ancient Mongolian tradition, and you can find various accessories like hoods and gloves used in falconry.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: Mongolia is home to many talented artisans who produce beautiful textiles, ceramics, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Choibalsan

Mongolia’s culture and customs are heavily influenced by nomadism, Buddhism, and Shamanism. It’s essential to respect the local customs and etiquette while visiting Choibalsan. Some tips include:

  • Shake hands: Mongolians prefer to greet people with a handshake.
  • Remove shoes: It’s customary to remove shoes upon entering homes or buildings.
  • Respect elders: Mongolians hold great respect for their elders, and it’s considered rude to point with feet, raise voice or make direct eye-contact with them.

Best Time to Visit Choibalsan

The best time to visit Choibalsan is between June and August when the weather is warm and sunny, making it the ideal time for outdoor activities. However, it’s also peak tourist seasons, so expect the prices to be higher than usual.

Do’s and Don’ts of Choibalsan

Do:

  • Carry some cash with you: Cards are not commonly accepted, so carrying some cash can come in handy.
  • Learn some phrases in Mongolian: Learning a few words in Mongolian can help you in your interactions with locals.
  • Carry warm clothing: Mongolia is a cold country, and even in summers, night temperatures can drop significantly.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their consent: Always ask for the person’s permission before taking their picture.
  • Wander off alone at night: Like any other city, it’s advisable to take precautions while travelling solo at night.
  • Litter: Mongolia is a beautiful country, and it’s vital to keep it clean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Choibalsan

Choibalsan is generally considered safe, and incidences of violent crimes are rare. However, it’s always advisable to keep your valuables safe and take precautions when venturing out at night.

Health in Choibalsan

Mongolia has a low rate of infectious diseases, but it’s advisable to vaccinate for tetanus and hepatitis A and B before travelling. Food and water-borne illnesses like diarrhoea can be common, so it’s essential to maintain good hygiene and avoid street food.

Getting to Choibalsan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Choibalsan

Choibalsan airport is located 6km from the city, and daily flights operate from Ulaanbaatar to Choibalsan.

Bus Stations Near Choibalsan

Buses run regularly between Ulaanbaatar and Choibalsan, and it takes around 15-20 hours to cover the distance.

Other Options

Shared taxis are an option, but it can be uncomfortable and time-consuming.

How to Get Around in Choibalsan

Taxis and public buses are available in the city, but the best way to explore Choibalsan’s scenic beauty is by renting a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from Choibalsan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Uvs Province: A vast province located in the northwest of Mongolia and famous for its beautiful scenery.
  • Khukh Burd Temple: A small temple located a few kilometres southeast of Choibalsan.
  • Dadal: A small village located on the bank of the Onon River, a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Choibalsan is an excellent destination for those seeking adventure, culture, and a glimpse into the Mongolian way of life. With a rich history, scenic landscape and plenty of outdoor activities, it offers something for everyone. Plan your trip to Choibalsan today and experience the unique beauty of Mongolia.
