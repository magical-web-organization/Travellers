The New Territories in Hong Kong is a treasure trove of cultural and natural wonders waiting to be discovered. Often overlooked in favor of the more popular tourist destinations like Hong Kong Island and Kowloon, the New Territories is a place where the old and the new mingle seamlessly. From traditional ancestral halls to modern architecture, from serene hiking trails to exhilarating mountain biking routes, the New Territories has something for every kind of traveler.

Introduction to New Territories

Located in the northeastern part of Hong Kong, the New Territories is the largest and least populated region of the city. It covers an area of approximately 977 square kilometers and is home to more than 3.8 million people. Despite its modern development, the New Territories still holds on to a rich cultural heritage that can be seen in its architecture, festivals, and cuisine.

Best Things to Do in New Territories: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there are plenty of things to do in the New Territories:

  1. Visit Tin Shui Wai Sports Ground and enjoy a variety of activities including cycling, jogging, and football.
  2. Experience rural life at Yuen Long Old Market.
  3. Take a boat ride to explore the wetlands of Mai Po Nature Reserve.
  4. Join a dragon boat race at Sai Kung.
  5. Surf at Big Wave Bay beach.
  6. Visit Disneyland Hong Kong, located in Tsuen Wan in the New Territories.

Local Culture and Customs of New Territories

The New Territories is deeply influenced by Chinese culture and customs. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  1. Respect for the elderly and ancestors is highly valued.
  2. Tipping is not expected in restaurants, but it is appreciated.
  3. Taking off shoes before entering a home is a common practice.
  4. Chinese New Year is the most important festival in the area, celebrated for 15 days.

Best Time to Visit New Territories

The best time to visit the New Territories is during the autumn season (October to November) when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Territories

Do:

  1. Try the local food and drinks.
  2. Carry a map and a charged, updated copy of offline GPS on your phone.
  3. Get an Octopus card for travel convenience.
  4. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious or cultural sights.
  5. Book cultural experiences in advance.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage public property.
  2. Talk loudly in public places.
  3. Point with your finger.
  4. Gift clocks or handkerchiefs, which are considered bad luck.
  5. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious or cultural sights.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Territories

The New Territories is a safe place to travel to. However, it is advisable to exercise caution and take necessary precautions like:

  1. Keeping valuables and important documents in a safe place.
  2. Avoiding isolated areas or dimly lit streets, especially at night.
  3. Staying aware of your surroundings and not leaving your belongings unattended.

Health in New Territories

Hong Kong has a world-class healthcare system with excellent medical facilities. It is advisable to carry adequate travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.
