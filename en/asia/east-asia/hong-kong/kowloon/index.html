Introduction to Kowloon

As one of the two main regions of Hong Kong, Kowloon is a bustling urban landscape with a rich blend of Chinese and Western influences. Located on the Kowloon Peninsula overlooking Victoria Harbour, Kowloon is home to some of Hong Kong’s most iconic skyscrapers, bustling street markets, and mouth-watering street food.

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Kowloon offers something for everyone with its mix of old-world charm and modern amenities. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Kowloon, from its cultural attractions and natural wonders to local cuisine and logistics.

List of Places to Visit in Kowloon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kowloon is home to many museums and historical sites that showcase the region’s rich cultural heritage. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • Hong Kong Museum of History: This exceptional museum will take you through the history of Hong Kong from prehistoric times to contemporary culture through immersive exhibits, live demonstrations and more.
  • Chi Lin Nunnery and Nan Lian Garden: It’s an oasis of tranquility amid the city’s skyscraper jungle. Traditional blue-green pagodas and pavilions interspersed with Zen fountains and waterfalls are set in immaculate gardens lined with trees.
  • Kowloon Walled City Park: Once a densely packed neighborhood, all that’s left now are the remains of this former bastion. The entrance gate and a small exhibit at the museum offer a glimpse into its history.

Natural Attractions of Kowloon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Kowloon offers a range of natural attractions that provide some breathtaking views of the city’s skyline. Check out these popular outdoor activities:

  • Lion Rock: A hiking trail that offers panoramic views of Kowloon and Hong Kong island.
  • Kowloon Peak: Hiking up to the peak of this mountain offers spectacular views of Hong Kong Island to the south and the New Territories to the north.
  • Victoria Harbour: Take in the city’s glittering skyline at one of the many vantage points along the harbor, including Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade and Kowloon Public Pier.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kowloon

Kowloon offers a scenic and exciting environment for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Below are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the city:

  • MacLehose Trail: A 100km-long trail that winds through the New Territories and ends in Sai Kung. You can take a section of it into the mountains of Kowloon.
  • Wilson Trail: A 78 km-long trail running through eight-country parks between west Hong Kong island and the New Territories. The mountainous section of the Kowloon ridges includes a short section of this trail.
  • Shing Mun River Cycling Path: This 11km cycling path and hiking trail follows the Shing Mun River and offers views of the surrounding mountains and forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Kowloon: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to shopping and dining, check out some of the best things to do in Kowloon:

  • A Symphony of Lights: Every night, 44 buildings on Hong Kong Island and in Kowloon come alive with a spectacular light show synchronized to music and narration.
  • Temple Street Night Market: One of the most popular street markets in Hong Kong, the Temple Street Night Market is a must-visit for shopping, street food, and local entertainment.
  • Nathan Road: A bustling street that’s home to some of the city’s most iconic landmarks, from the Clock Tower to the Avenue of Stars.
  • Foodie Tour of Kowloon: Take a foodie tour of Kowloon to sample some of the region’s most delicious street food and local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kowloon

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kowloon, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Check in to your hotel and start exploring Tsim Sha Tsui.
  • Day 2: Visit the Hong Kong Museum of History, and the Chi Lin Nunnery and Nan Lian Garden.
  • Day 3: Take a hike up Lion Rock, followed by shopping and dining in Mong Kok.
  • Day 4: Visit the Kowloon Walled City Park, and shop at the Temple Street Night Market.
  • Day 5: Take a foodie tour of Kowloon and enjoy some of the region’s most delicious street food and local cuisine.
  • Day 6: Visit the Shing Mun River Cycling Path and outdoor activities, followed by a relaxing evening at Victoria Harbour.
  • Day 7: End your trip by taking in the city’s glittering skyline from one of the many vantage points along Victoria Harbour.

Food and Drink Scene of Kowloon

Must-Try Dishes

Kowloon’s food scene is famous around the world for its unique blend of local and regional cuisines. Here are some dishes you must try:

  • Dim Sum: A traditional Hong Kong breakfast or lunch of twirl-cooked small bites of Chinese cuisine.
  • Roast meatThey are Cantonese style BBQ meats including roast duck, roast port and roast chicken.
  • Wonton Noodle Soup: A bowl of noodles in wonton soup that features a tender broth, perfectly cooked noodles, and savory pork and shrimp dumplings.
  • Egg Tarts: A Cantonese favorite, egg tarts are small pastries with a flaky, buttery crust and a silky smooth egg custard filling.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, there are a few popular and delicious drinks that you should try:

  • Yuen Yeung: A popular drink made from a mix of coffee and tea, served hot or cold.
  • Tong Cha: It is a sweet drink made of flowers, herbs, and rock sugar boiled over time to allow the flavor of each ingredient to come through.
  • Chinese Liquor: There are different types of Chinese liquors or baijiu for you to choose from.

Where to Stay in Kowloon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kowloon offers a range of accommodation options to fit your budget, from luxury hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the recommended places to stay in the city:

  • The Peninsula Hong Kong: A legendary hotel that’s been an icon in Kowloon since its opening in 1928.
  • Sheraton Hong Kong Hotel and Towers: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Tsim Sha Tsui.
  • Casa Del Rio: A boutique guesthouse with cozy and tastefully decorated rooms.

What to Buy in Kowloon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kowloon is a shopper’s paradise, with plenty of unique and cultural souvenirs to bring home. Here are some items that you should consider buying:

  • Jade jewelry: Kowloon is well-known for its jade market with many unique pieces to buy.
  • Silk clothing and scarves: The shops in Kowloon offer a variety of luxurious silk clothing and scarves, perfect for souvenirs.
  • Chinese tea: As one of the major producers of tea, China offers different types of tea, and you cannot miss purchasing high-quality of tea bags in Kowloon.

Local Culture and Customs of Kowloon

Like the rest of China, Kowloon has its own unique customs and culture. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs: It’s important to respect local customs and traditions, such as taking off your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Language: In Kowloon, Cantonese and English are the two most commonly used languages.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not a common practice in Kowloon, but it’s appreciated for good service.

Best Time to Visit Kowloon

The best time to visit Kowloon is between October and December, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are thinner. Avoid visiting in the peak summer months of July and August, as the weather can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kowloon

Do:

  • Explore the food scene: Kowloon is famous for its street food and local cuisine, so make sure you explore the culinary scene.
  • Visit the markets: Kowloon has many bustling street markets where you can shop for souvenirs and experience the vibrant local culture.
  • Take in the view of the city skyline: Kowloon offers some of the best views of the city’s glittering skyline.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs: It’s important to respect local customs and traditions, so avoid doing anything that could be considered disrespectful.
  • Litter: Keep the city clean by disposing of your trash in bins.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kowloon

Kowloon is generally a safe city, with low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions.

  • Pickpocketing and Theft: Watch your belongings when in crowded areas, especially on public transport, and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Protests: Protests may occur in Kowloon or nearby areas, so always be aware of the situation, keep up to date with the news and follow official advice.

Health in Kowloon

Kowloon has good healthcare facilities and medical services that can provide basic care to treating serious illnesses or injuries.

  • Vaccinations: It’s important to check with your doctor and have the recommended vaccinations before traveling to Kowloon.
  • Water: Drinking tap water is not safe in Kowloon, but bottled water is readily available.

Getting to Kowloon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kowloon is easily accessible by air or land. Here are some transportation options to get around Kowloon:

Airports Near Kowloon

The closest airport to Kowloon is the Hong Kong International Airport, which is located about 30 minutes away by Taxi or through the MTR Line, also you can find cabs available 24/7.

Bus Stations Near Kowloon

A popular way to get around Kowloon is by bus or minibus. Some major bus stations in Kowloon include:

  • Tsim Sha Tsui Ferry Terminal: Connects to Hong Kong Island and the rest of the city by ferry.
  • Star Ferry Terminal: Offers ferry services connecting TST with Central, Wan Chai and Hung Hom.

Other Options

  • MTR and buses: The Mass Transit Railway (MTR) is a convenient and efficient way to get around Kowloon and the rest of Hong Kong, coupled with the bus network.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout Kowloon, making it easy to get around the city day or night.

How to Get Around in Kowloon

Kowloon offers various transportation options for getting around:

  • MTR: The Mass Transit Railway (MTR) is a fast and convenient way to get around Kowloon and the rest of Hong Kong.
  • Buses: Buses run all over the Kowloon Peninsula and surrounding areas, connecting major landmarks and points of interest.
  • Taxis: Taxis are a convenient option for getting around Kowloon, but make sure you have enough cash as many taxis do not accept credit cards.

Where to Go Next from Kowloon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kowloon is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding areas. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Hong Kong Island: A short ferry ride from Kowloon, Hong Kong Island offers a range of attractions, from the Peak Tram to the nightlife of Lan Kwai Fong.
  • Lantau Island: Home to the Ngong Ping 360 cable car and the famous Big Buddha.
  • Macau: A former Portuguese colony, Macau is now a gambling mecca with a unique blend of Portuguese and Chinese culture.

Conclusion

Kowloon is a city that’s rich in history, culture, and natural wonders. From the bustling street markets to the scenic hiking trails, Kowloon offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern amenities. With this comprehensive guide, we hope you’ll have all the information you need to make the most of your visit to Kowloon, Hong Kong.
