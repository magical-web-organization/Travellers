Introduction to Seto Inland Sea

Seto Inland Sea (also known as Setonaikai) is a tranquil body of water located in Japan, in East Asia, in Asia. It stretches roughly 450 kilometers from Hiroshima to Kyoto and includes more than 700 islands of all sizes. Seto Inland Sea is not only an important transportation hub but also a scenic tourist destination. It is surrounded by beautiful landscapes, traditional Japanese architecture, and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Seto Inland Sea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those who are interested in Japanese history and culture, Seto Inland Sea has a wealth of historical sites and cultural institutions to visit. The following are some of the most famous museums and historical places in the region:

  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum
  • Miyajima Island and Itsukushima Shrine
  • Okayama Castle and Korakuen Garden
  • Himeji Castle
  • Kurashiki Bikan Historic Area
  • Naoshima Art Island
  • Matsuyama Castle

Natural Attractions of Seto Inland Sea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Seto Inland Sea’s scenic beauty is undoubtedly one of its main attractions. Indeed, many visitors come to the region to admire its incredibly beautiful vistas, vibrant sunsets, and the rugged coastline. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Seto Inland Sea:

  • Shimanami Kaido Cycling Route
  • Tomonoura (quaint fishing village)
  • Oboke Gorge
  • Seto Ohashi Bridge
  • Kurushima Strait Whirlpool

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seto Inland Sea

Seto Inland Sea offers fantastic opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the region on foot or by bike. The following are some of the most popular hiking and biking trails in Seto Inland Sea:

  • Shimanami Kaido Cycling Route (70 km)
  • Mt. Misen (Miyajima Island)
  • Mt. Washuzan and Kotohira-gu Shrine
  • Takamatsu Castle and Gardens
  • Shodoshima Olive Park

Best Things to Do in Seto Inland Sea: Activities and Tours

Seto Inland Sea offers many exciting activities and tours to suit everyone’s interests. Some of the most popular things to do in the region include:

  • Visit the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum
  • Climb to the top of Miyajima Island’s Mt. Misen
  • Admire the breathtaking views from Mt. Washuzan
  • Take a leisurely bike ride along the Shimanami Kaido Cycling Route
  • Relax and soak in a traditional Japanese hot spring bath

Week-Long Itinerary for Seto Inland Sea

A week in Seto Inland Sea will provide enough time to see many of the region’s top spots. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum and Miyajima Island
  • Day 2: Okayama Castle and Korakuen Garden
  • Day 3: Kurashiki Bikan Historic Area and Naoshima Art Island
  • Day 4: Tomonoura and Oboke Gorge
  • Day 5: Matsuyama Castle and Shikoku Karst National Park
  • Day 6: Shimonoseki and the strait of Kanmon
  • Day 7: Takamatsu Castle and Gardens

Food and Drink Scene of Seto Inland Sea

The food scene in Seto Inland Sea is as diverse as its cultural heritage. With fresh seafood, udon, and green tea, Seto Inland Sea’s cuisine is a reflection of its historical and geographical diversity.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki (savory Japanese pancake)
  • Iwagaki Oyster (locally caught oyster)
  • Udon (Japanese thick noodle)
  • Sanuki Udon (Kagawa specialty)
  • Sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • Shochu (Japanese distilled spirit)

Local Beverages

  • Green Tea
  • Josenji-yaki (Japanese ceramic sake cup)
  • Nambu Tekki (traditional ironware made in Iwate)

Local Culture and Customs of Seto Inland Sea

Seto Inland Sea has a unique blend of cultures that are influenced by the region’s history, geography, and religion. The following are some of the most important customs and traditions in Seto Inland Sea:

  • Wearing slippers inside Japanese homes and guesthouses
  • Taking shoes off when entering a shrine or temple
  • Eating with chopsticks
  • Bowing in greeting or respect

Best Time to Visit Seto Inland Sea

The best time to visit Seto Inland Sea is from May to September when the weather is mild and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities. The peak season is from June to August, so expect crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seto Inland Sea

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry cash (many smaller businesses do not accept credit cards)
  • Take off your shoes when entering a Japanese home or guesthouse

Don’t:

  • Eat, drink, or smoke while walking in public
  • Hug or kiss in public
  • Wear shoes inside a home or a Japanese-style inn

Safety and Health

Safety in Seto Inland Sea

Seto Inland Sea is generally a safe region to visit, and crime rates are low. However, it is always advisable to take standard safety precautions and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Seto Inland Sea

Japan has an excellent healthcare system, and medical facilities in Seto Inland Sea are no exception. Pharmacies and hospitals are readily available, and staff speaks both English and Japanese.

Bus Stations Near Seto Inland Sea

How to Get Around in Seto Inland Sea

Seto Inland Sea is well-connected by road, rail, and ferry. Buses and trains are the most convenient option for short distances, while ferries are the best alternative for island hopping.

Where to Go Next from Seto Inland Sea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Seto Inland Sea is a unique region that offers something for everyone, from ancient history and beautiful landscapes to delicious food and exciting outdoor activities. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to explore the best that Seto Inland Sea has to offer.
