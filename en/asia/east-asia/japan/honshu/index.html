Introduction to Honshu

Located in Japan, in East Asia, Honshu is the largest island of Japan’s four major islands. It is known for its historical places, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Honshu’s major cities include Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and Hiroshima.

List of Places to Visit in Honshu

Honshu is full of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tokyo National Museum, Tokyo
  2. Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum, Hiroshima
  3. Kyoto Imperial Palace, Kyoto
  4. Himeji Castle, Hyogo Prefecture
  5. Matsumoto Castle, Nagano Prefecture

Natural Attractions of Honshu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hakone, Kanagawa Prefecture
  2. Mount Fuji, Shizuoka Prefecture
  3. Shirakami-Sanchi, Aomori and Akita Prefecture
  4. Nachi Falls, Wakayama Prefecture
  5. Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route, Toyama and Nagano Prefecture

Hiking/Biking Routes of Honshu

Honshu offers amazing hiking and biking opportunities. Some noteworthy routes are:

  1. Kumano Kodo, Wakayama Prefecture
  2. Nakasendo Trail, Nagano and Gifu Prefecture
  3. Kamikochi, Nagano Prefecture
  4. Shimanami Kaido, Ehime and Hiroshima Prefecture
  5. Daisetsuzan National Park, Hokkaido Prefecture

Best Things to Do in Honshu: Activities and Tours

  1. Experience a traditional tea ceremony in Kyoto
  2. Visit the Tsukiji Fish Market in Tokyo
  3. Attend a sumo wrestling tournament in Tokyo or Osaka
  4. Take a trip to Hiroshima’s Miyajima Island
  5. Explore the night markets in Osaka

Week-Long Itinerary for Honshu

For a week-long itinerary in Honshu, consider these locations:

  1. Day 1-2: Tokyo (Tokyo Tower, Senso-ji Temple, Ueno Park)
  2. Day 3: Hakone (Hakone Shrine, Mount Fuji, Lake Ashi)
  3. Day 4-5: Kyoto (Fushimi Inari Shrine, Kiyomizu-dera Temple, Gion District)
  4. Day 6-7: Hiroshima (Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum, Miyajima Island)

Local Culture and Customs of Honshu

Honshu’s locals have their unique customs and traditions. Here are some cultural aspects to consider:

  1. Remove your shoes before entering a Japanese home.
  2. Bowing is a common mode of greeting.
  3. Be aware of other people around you and avoid making noise.
  4. Public displays of affection are not customary in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Honshu

The best time to visit Honshu is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Honshu

Do:

  1. Dress modestly while visiting temples and shrines.
  2. Carry cash as some places do not accept credit cards.
  3. Take off your shoes before entering a temple, shrine or traditional restaurant.
  4. Try local cuisine and participate in cultural activities.

Don’t:

  1. Use your phone on public transport.
  2. Smoke while walking or in non-designated areas.
  3. Tip wait staff or taxi drivers.
  4. Be excessively loud in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Honshu

Honshu is safe for travelers, but it’s always best to take precautions, such as:

  1. Keep track of your belongings, especially in crowded public areas.
  2. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dark or deserted areas.
  3. Always carry sufficient cash for emergencies.

Health in Honshu

Japan is generally a healthy country, and any medical needs can be met with the following:

  1. Pharmacies are widely available throughout Honshu.
  2. Medical treatment in Japan can be expensive without travel insurance.
