Introduction to Tottori

Tottori is a hidden gem of Japan that is definitely worth exploring. Located in the Chugoku region of Japan, Tottori offers a unique blend of incredible natural beauty, rich history, and cultural experiences. You can enjoy a range of outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and skiing while also exploring museums, historical landmarks, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Tottori

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tottori Castle Ruins: This picturesque castle ruin dates back to the 16th century and offers stunning views of the city.
  • Watanabe Museum: This museum showcases the history of Tottori and showcases local art and pottery.
  • The Sand Museum: If you love sand art, then this is a must-visit attraction. Every year, this museum features sand sculptures created by artists from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Tottori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tottori Sand Dunes: These sand dunes are the only large sand dune system in Japan and are a sight to behold.
  • Mizuki Shigeru Road: If you are a fan of manga, you must visit this road which features bronze statues of iconic characters.
  • Mount Daisen: This beautiful mountain offers great hikes, skiing, and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tottori

  • Kerakuen Garden: A beautiful garden with hiking trails and cherry blossoms.
  • Yubara Onsen: A quiet hot spring town with plenty of hiking and biking routes for beginners and experts alike.
  • Hiruzen Plateau: A vast plateau offering hiking and skiing opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Tottori: Activities and Tours

  • Sake Brewery Tour: Tottori is known for its exceptional sake, and you can visit local breweries to learn more about the production process.
  • Yonago Water Lantern Festival: This summer festival celebrates the beauty of traditional Japanese lanterns that float in the water.
  • Tottori Hanakairo Flower Park: This park is a must-visit for flower lovers with over 500,000 flowers in bloom throughout the year.

Food and Drink Scene of Tottori

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tottori Gyukotsu Ramen: A hearty beef bone broth ramen that is perfect for cold weather.
  • Matsuba Crab: This regional delicacy is sweet and succulent, and a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Tottori Wagyu: This premium beef is famous for its beautiful marbling and melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Tottori is known for its exceptional sake that is brewed with local water and rice.
  • Yonago Beer: This locally brewed beer is made with spring water from Mt. Daisen and is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Yuzu Juice: This refreshing citrus juice is made with locally grown yuzu and is a great way to cool down in summer.

Where to Stay in Tottori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tottori Washington Hotel Plaza: A centrally located hotel with affordable rates and modern amenities.
  • Yonago New Urban Hotel Annex: A great option for families with spacious rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • Yubara Onsen Yubara no Yu: A traditional Japanese ryokan with private hot spring baths.

What to Buy in Tottori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tottori Sand: Tiny bags of sand that make great souvenirs and gifts.
  • Matsuba Crab: You can buy fresh and dried Matsuba crabs to take home.
  • Local Sake: You can pick up a bottle of local sake to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tottori

Tottori is known for its traditional way of life, and it’s important to respect local customs when visiting. Bowing is the traditional greeting, and it’s also common to remove your shoes when entering a home or temple. It’s important to be respectful of the countryside and to avoid littering or damaging the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Tottori

The best time to visit Tottori is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the climate is mild, and you can enjoy flowering trees and autumn foliage. Summer (June to August) can be quite hot and humid, while winter (December to February) can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tottori

Do:

  • Try local food and beverages like Tottori Gyukotsu Ramen and Tottori Wagyu.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Keep the natural environment clean and litter-free.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in prohibited areas.
  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.
  • Damage or litter the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tottori

Tottori is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should be careful when hiking and skiing, and make sure to follow local rules and recommendations.

Health in Tottori

The tap water in Tottori is safe to drink, but it’s always a good idea to carry bottled water when exploring. If you have any health concerns, it’s best to consult a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Tottori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tottori

The closest airport to Tottori is Tottori Airport, which has flights to and from Tokyo and Osaka. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tottori

Tottori Bus Center is the main bus station in Tottori and connects to nearby cities like Osaka and Hiroshima.

Other Options

You can also reach Tottori by train as it’s well-connected to Osaka, Hiroshima, and other cities in Japan via the JR train network.

How to Get Around in Tottori

The best way to explore Tottori is by rental car as public transportation can be limited in some areas. If you don’t have a car, you can use local buses or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Tottori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Okayama: This nearby city is known for its beautiful gardens and historic castle.
  • Hiroshima: A must-visit destination with a rich history, delicious food, and plenty to see and do.
  • Miyajima: This picturesque island is known for its floating torii gate and beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

A visit to Tottori is a great way to experience the hidden gems of Japan. From the Tottori Sand Dunes to Matsuba Crab, there are plenty of attractions, food, and activities to explore. Whether you’re hiking, skiing, or soaking in a hot spring, Tottori has something for everyone.
