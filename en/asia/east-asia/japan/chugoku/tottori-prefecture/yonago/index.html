Yonago Travel Guide - Discover the Hidden Gem of Tottori, Japan

Welcome to Yonago, a small city located in Tottori Prefecture, in the Chugoku region of Japan. Although lesser known than its neighboring cities, Yonago offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that will captivate any traveler. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Yonago.

Introduction to Yonago

Yonago is a peaceful, laid-back city located on the shore of the Sea of Japan. It has a population of approximately 150,000 people and serves as a hub for transportation and commerce in the region. Yonago is also known for its clean air and water, which makes it an ideal place for outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and beachcombing. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the city has a rich historical and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Yonago

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Yonago City Museum of Art: This museum features various artworks by local artists as well as international artists.

  • Yasugi-bushi Dance Museum: This museum showcases traditional Japanese dance and music, which is an integral part of the culture in Yonago.

  • Kawara Museum of Takahashi Setsuro: One of the famous ceramic artists was born in Yonago. The museum displays his artworks and the history of the Kawara family.

Natural Attractions of Yonago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Daisen: One of the tallest peaks in Japan, the mountain is known for its stunning views and hiking trails.

  • Kaike Onsen: Kaike Onsen is a hot spring known for its therapeutic properties.

  • Mihonoseki Lighthouse: The lighthouse is situated at the entrance to the Sea of Japan, and offers a great view of the sea and coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yonago

  • Daisen-Oki National Park: This park covers an area of 342 square kilometers and offers a wide range of hiking trails that suit hikers of any skill level.

  • Mihonoseki Cliff: The cliff is approximately 1km long and offers a great view of the coast and the sea.

  • Benkei Gorge: The gorge is known for its clear water and the hiking trails that lead to the famous Benkei Bridge.

Best Things To Do in Yonago: Activities and Tours

  • Go Swimming at Kaike Beach: The beach has a long sandy shore and crystal clear waters which are perfect to relax and swim.

  • Experience Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony: At Wakasa-Kan, you can enjoy the traditional tea ceremony in a serene Japanese style room.

  • Take a Sightseeing Train Tour on the San’in Line: This scenic train ride offers stunning views of the Sea of Japan and the surrounding scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Yonago

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sansonjiru: A miso soup with vegetables, mushroom, and wild boar meat.

  • Kaisendon: A mix of raw seafood served over rice, especially fresh tuna.

  • Shamoji Yakitori: Chicken skewers that are a regional specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: There are several breweries in the Yonago region for sake lovers to explore

  • Beer: Shimane Prefecture borders Yonago, and they own a large brewery

  • Tea: The Yonago region is well-known for producing some of the best green teas in Japan

Where to Stay in Yonago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • ANA Yonago Hotel: A luxurious option for those who want to stay in the heart of the city.

  • Daisen Royal Hotel: A spacious and comfortable resort located on the foot of Mt.Daisen, perfect for nature lovers.

  • Yonago Guesthouse T&G: Budget guesthouse in the city center that offers cozy and clean accommodation.

What to Buy in Yonago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sake: Sake, a traditional Japanese alcoholic drink, is readily available in Yonago.

  • Mino-yaki Pottery: Yonago is known for this beautiful and rustic pottery that is made from the local clay.

  • Washi Paper Products: Yonago produces some of the best quality washi paper in Japan.

Local Culture and Customs of Yonago

Yonago has a rich and unique cultural heritage. Folk dancing, music, and festivals are prevalent in the region. Yonago also has a deep connection with nature and environmental conservation.

Best Time to Visit Yonago

The best time to visit Yonago is from April to May and September to November when the weather is mild, and the foliage is breath-taking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yonago

Do:

  • Try out different traditional foods found in Yonago.

  • Respect the natural surroundings in Yonago.

  • Participate in a tea ceremony to experience local customs.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas

  • Drink in the open air

  • Wear shoes on tatami mats; socks, barefoot or special slippers called toubi are acceptable

Safety and Health

Safety in Yonago

Yonago is a peaceful and safe city with low crime rates. However, the usual caution is necessary to be taken while traveling in a foreign place.

Health in Yonago

There are several hospitals and clinics that provide medical care in Yonago. However, it is advisable to have travel insurance and take precautionary measures for health issues.

Where to Go Next from Yonago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Matsue City: Explore the old city that still has traditions like samurai district or the castle.

  • Izumo City: A trip to see the grand shrine and learn about the history of Japan’s ancient mythology.

  • Hiroshima City: Hiroshima city is located approximately 2 hours from Yonago, and it is worth visiting to see the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and the museum that depicts the atomic attack in 1945.

Conclusion

Yonago is a hidden gem and is often overlooked by the popular tourist destinations in Japan. But, as you have learned through this guide, Yonago has a lot to offer, from museums to natural attractions, from traditional cuisine to outdoor activities. By visiting Yonago, you will experience a unique blend of Japanese culture and make memories for a lifetime.
