Are you planning to visit Hiroshima? Then get ready to explore one of the most popular and historic destinations in Japan. Located in Chugoku, Hiroshima is a popular city known for its attractive tourist destinations, delicious food, rich history, and cultural significance. This comprehensive travel guide for Hiroshima covers everything you need to know to plan your visit, such as where to go, what to see, what to eat, and more.

Introduction to Hiroshima

Hiroshima is a city located in the western part of Honshu Island, Japan. Known as the birthplace of Mazda and for the tragic events that occurred during World War II, it is an important city with significant cultural and historical significance. Nowadays it is a vibrant, friendly and international city. Hiroshima has over 1.1 million residents and is the capital of the Hiroshima Prefecture.

List of Places to Visit in Hiroshima

Here are some of the must-visit places to explore while in Hiroshima.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial (Genbaku Dome): Also known as the Atomic Bomb Dome, this building is one of the only structures that survived the atomic bombing of Hiroshima. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and an important symbol of peace.
  • Hiroshima Castle: This historic castle is known for its beautiful stonework and lush gardens.
  • Hiroshima Museum of Art: This museum is home to both Japanese and Western art, including works by Renoir, Cezanne, and Matisse.
  • Hiroshima Prefectural Art Museum: This museum is dedicated to the collection, preservation, and exhibition of modern and contemporary art.

Natural Attractions of Hiroshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Miyajima Island: Located in the Seto Island Sea, Miyajima Island is often referred to as one of the most beautiful places in Japan. The Itsukushima Shrine, one of the most significant cultural heritage sites in the country, is located here.
  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Garden: This park spans over 120,000 square meters and is home to many monuments and memorials dedicated to the victims of the atomic bombing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hiroshima

Hiroshima has great hiking and biking trails, scenic gardens, and parks that offer ideal opportunities for outdoor adventure lovers. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Mt. Misen: Hikers can enjoy an awe-inspiring view of the city of Hiroshima and Miyajima Island from the summit of this mountain.
  • Shukkeien Garden: This beautiful garden has a traditional Japanese landscape design and is ideal for nature walks and picnics.

Best Things to Do in Hiroshima: Activities and Tours

Take a look at some of the best things to do in Hiroshima:

  • Visit the Mazda Museum: Car lovers will enjoy exploring the history of the iconic car brand and seeing some of its unique models.
  • Hiroshima Okonomiyaki Cooking Class: Okonomiyaki is the most famous street food in Hiroshima, and visitors can learn how to cook it themselves in a traditional Hiroshima-style cooking class.
  • Hiroshima Food and Drink Crawl: Foodies can explore the best local food and drink spots with a guided tour of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hiroshima

Here’s an example of a week-long itinerary to help you explore Hiroshima:

Food and Drink Scene of Hiroshima

Hiroshima is known for its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hiroshima-Style Okonomiyaki: This savory pancake is made with cabbage, noodles, and egg and is topped with a variety of ingredients, including meats, seafood, and vegetables.
  • Hiroshima-Style Tsukemen Noodles: These thick noodles are served with a dipping sauce made from pork broth, soy sauce, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Hiroshima Sake: The region is known for its delicious sake with almost 80 breweries located in the surrounding areas.
  • Hiroshima Beer: This locally produced beer comes in several delicious flavors and is perfect for sightseeing.

Where to Stay in Hiroshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Hiroshima between budget-friendly hostels and luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Sheraton Grand Hiroshima: This five-star hotel is located in the city center and provides a luxurious stay for visitors.
  • K’s Hostel Hiroshima: This budget-friendly hostel offers travelers a comfortable and affordable stay.

What to Buy in Hiroshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and products to purchase in Hiroshima include:

  • Oysters: Hiroshima is known for its fresh oysters, available in many markets and stores.
  • Ceramic Goods: The area is famous for its ceramics with notable pottery towns such as Arita and Hasami.

Local Culture and Customs of Hiroshima

Hiroshima is a town of friendly locals, good food, and rich history. Some local customs to follow while visiting include:

  • Remember Hiroshima Atomic Bombing: Hiroshima is known for its history and cultural significance in the Second World War, so visitors should show respect for the tragedies of the past.
  • Greetings: Bowing is a common form of greeting, though a nod of the head is acceptable.

Best Time to Visit Hiroshima

The best time to visit Hiroshima is from March to May and from September to November when the climate is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hiroshima

Do:

  • Public Transport: It’s easy to get around via public transportation in Hiroshima, so use it to explore the city.
  • Respect the Culture: Take time to learn and understand Japanese culture during your stay.
  • Try Local Food: Hiroshima has a diverse food scene and boasts some of the best street food in Japan, so don’t miss out.

Don’t:

  • Littering: Japan has a strict policy on littering. Always dispose of your waste in the proper receptacles.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Japan, as the prices include service charges.
  • No E-Cigarettes: Public smoking is prohibited, and E-cigarettes are also illegal in some areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hiroshima

Hiroshima is a safe city to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, such as not traveling alone late at night.

Health in Hiroshima

Hiroshima has a good medical infrastructure, and visitors can quickly get access to health services if needed.

Getting to Hiroshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some options to consider when getting to Hiroshima:

Airports Near Hiroshima

  • Hiroshima Airport: Hiroshima Airport is the closest major airport to the city centre, serviced by both domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Hiroshima

  • Hiroshima Bus Center: Located in the city center and serves most of the long-distance bus lines and some local buses.

Other Options

  • Shinkansen Train: The bullet train connects Hiroshima with other major cities in Japan, such as Tokyo and Osaka.

How to Get Around in Hiroshima

Hiroshima has excellent public transportation systems including buses, trains, and trams, making it easy to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Hiroshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some popular destinations to visit from Hiroshima:

  • Miyajima Island: Take a day trip to the gorgeous Miyajima Island, which is just a ferry ride away from Hiroshima.
  • Himeji Castle: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a beautiful historic castle just a short train ride from Hiroshima.

Conclusion

Hiroshima is an incredible city with a rich history, exquisite food, and culture that is worth exploring. We hope our comprehensive guide has helped you plan your visit and make the most of your time in this memorable destination.
