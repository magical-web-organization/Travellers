Introduction to Mihara

Located in Hiroshima Prefecture, Mihara is a charming city that offers visitors a unique blend of historical intrigue and natural beauty. With a fascinating history, plentiful outdoor activities, and incredible food and drink options, there’s something for every traveler in Mihara.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at what makes Mihara such a special destination. From must-visit cultural attractions and natural wonders to the best hiking and biking routes and local dishes, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Mihara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mihara History and Folklore Museum: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into Mihara’s past, with exhibits focused on history, religion, and folklore.
  • Hiroshima Castle: While not technically located in Mihara, the iconic Hiroshima Castle is only a short train ride away and is well worth a visit.
  • Mibu-dera: Nestled in the hills outside of Mihara, this beautiful Buddhist temple is an excellent place to relax and learn about local spirituality.
  • Tomonoura: This picturesque fishing village, also known as Sensui-jima, is said to have inspired Hayao Miyazaki’s film Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea.

Natural Attractions of Mihara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Fudekage: This scenic mountain hike offers incredible views of the surrounding landscape and is particularly popular during autumn when the leaves change color.
  • Onomichi U2: While not technically a natural attraction, this renovated warehouse district-turned-cultural center is a must-visit for its unique blend of art, architecture, and nature.
  • Tomonoura: In addition to its cultural attractions, this fishing village also offers plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors, including fishing, kayaking, and cycling.
  • Kurahashi-jima: This island, accessible by ferry, offers visitors a chance to explore historic temples and shrines and enjoy beautiful views of the Seto Inland Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mihara

  • Shimanami Kaido: This scenic bike route spans several islands and offers stunning views of the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Mt. Fudekage: As mentioned earlier, this mountain hike is a great way to experience Mihara’s natural beauty.
  • Kurahashi-jima: Cycling around this island is a great way to take in the scenic views and immerse yourself in local culture.
  • Miyajima: While technically not located in Mihara, this iconic island offers plenty of hiking and cycling opportunities for visitors.

Best Things to Do in Mihara: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Sake Brewery: Hiroshima is known for its delicious sake, and there are plenty of local breweries in Mihara to choose from.
  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn to cook local specialties like okonomiyaki and oysters from a friendly local chef.
  • Try Sea Kayaking: Navigate the beautiful coastal waters around Mihara on a guided sea kayaking tour.
  • Visit a Local Market: Get a taste of local life by visiting one of Mihara’s bustling markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Mihara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mihara Oysters: These delicious oysters are renowned across Japan for their rich, briny flavor.
  • Yakitori: This juicy grilled chicken is a staple in many Japanese izakayas and is especially delicious in Mihara.
  • Okonomiyaki: A savory pancake filled with cabbage, meat, and other ingredients, this Hiroshima specialty is a must-try in Mihara.
  • Hiroshima-style tsukemen: A type of ramen where the noodles and broth are served separately, allowing the noodles to be dipped in the broth for maximum flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hiroshima is known for its high-quality sake, and Mihara has its fair share of breweries to choose from.
  • Iced Lemon Tea: A refreshing summer drink made with locally grown lemons.
  • Matcha: A Japanese green tea that’s a perfect complement to any meal.

Where to Stay in Mihara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel AreaOne Hiroshima Wing: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Miharaso: Located in the charming Tomonoura fishing village, this traditional Japanese inn offers a unique cultural experience.
  • Guesthouse Mihara: This cozy guesthouse offers affordable dormitory-style accommodation and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Mihara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mihara Oysters: Take some of the city’s delicious oysters home with you for a taste of Mihara.
  • Hiroshima-style Tsukemen: Stock up on some instant tsukemen noodles to enjoy at home.
  • Sake: Bring home a bottle or two of Hiroshima’s famous sake as a memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mihara

Mihara is a relatively laid-back city, but it’s always important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. When visiting temples and other cultural attractions, remember to remove your shoes and dress modestly. It’s also considered good manners to bow as a greeting.

Best Time to Visit Mihara

The best time to visit Mihara is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mihara

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering temples or traditional Japanese inns.
  • Try the local cuisine and sake.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other cultural attractions.
  • Be loud or rowdy in public areas.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mihara

Mihara is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always important to stay alert and take standard safety precautions. Ensure you have travel insurance before visiting Japan.

Health in Mihara

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Mihara. However, visitors with sensitive stomachs may want to stick to bottled water. Medical facilities are readily available in the city.

Getting to Mihara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mihara

The nearest airport to Mihara is Hiroshima Airport, which is served by several domestic and international airlines. From there, it’s easy to reach Mihara by train or bus.

Bus Stations Near Mihara

Mihara Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers several bus routes to nearby destinations.

Other Options

The fastest way to reach Mihara is by shinkansen (bullet train), which stops at Mihara Station. From there, it’s easy to navigate the city by train, bus, or taxi.

How to Get Around in Mihara

The best way to get around Mihara is by train, which offers convenient connections to other cities and towns in the region. Buses and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Mihara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hiroshima: Located only a short train ride away, Hiroshima is a must-visit destination for its history, culture, and food.
  • Onomichi: This charming port town is famous for its beautiful scenery, cycling routes, and art.
  • Miyajima: While technically located a bit further away, this iconic island is well worth a visit for its beautiful torii gate and friendly deer.

Conclusion

Mihara is a hidden gem in Japan that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or soaking up local culture, there’s something for everyone in Mihara. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to visit this beautiful city and make the most of your time there!
