Introduction to Miyajima

Miyajima is a small island located in the Hiroshima Bay in the Chugoku region of Japan. It is famous for Itsukushima Shrine, which is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is said to be one of the three most beautiful views in Japan, together with Matsushima and Amanohashidate. Miyajima is also known for its stunning fall foliage and cherry blossom seasons.

List of Places to Visit in Miyajima

Miyajima is a place of rich cultural history and natural beauty. Here are the must-visit places in Miyajima:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Itsukushima Shrine: A UNESCO World Heritage site, Itsukushima Shrine is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Miyajima. It is famous for its “floating” Torii gate, which appears to be floating on the water during high tide.
  2. Daisho-in Temple: A Buddhist temple with a history dating back more than 1,200 years. It is famous for its 500 Rakan statues, which are said to resemble the disciples of Buddha.
  3. Miyajima History and Folklore Museum: A museum that showcases the cultural history and folklore of Miyajima.

Natural Attractions of Miyajima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Misen: The highest peak in Miyajima, Mount Misen offers stunning views of the surrounding islands and the Seto Inland Sea. It is also a popular hiking destination.
  2. Momijidani Park: A park famous for its fall foliage. It is a popular spot for picnics during autumn.
  3. Miyajima Aquarium: A popular aquarium that showcases the marine life of the Seto Inland Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyajima

Miyajima is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Miyajima:

  1. Mount Misen Hiking Trail: A popular hiking trail that leads to the summit of Mount Misen. It offers stunning views of the Seto Inland Sea and the surrounding islands.
  2. Miyajima Cycling Road: A cycling route that goes around the island of Miyajima. It is a great way to explore the island’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Miyajima: Activities and Tours

  1. Torii Gate Sunset Tour: A tour that takes you on a boat ride to see the Itsukushima Shrine Torii gate during sunset.
  2. Oyster Tasting Tour: Miyajima is famous for its oysters. A tour that takes you to taste the fresh and delicious oysters.
  3. Kimono Rental and Photoshoot: Experience Japanese culture by renting a kimono and taking photos in scenic locations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Miyajima

Day 1: Arrival in Miyajima Day 2: Explore Itsukushima Shrine and Daisho-in Temple Day 3: Hike to the summit of Mount Misen Day 4: Visit Miyajima Aquarium and Momijidani Park Day 5: Cycling tour around the island Day 6: Torii gate sunset tour Day 7: Departure from Miyajima

Food and Drink Scene of Miyajima

Miyajima is famous for its fresh seafood, especially oysters. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages in Miyajima:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Grilled Oysters: Fresh and grilled to perfection, Miyajima’s oysters are a must-try for seafood lovers.
  2. Anago Meshi: A dish of grilled sea eel on top of rice.
  3. Momiji Manju: A sweet treat made from the island’s famous maple-shaped cakes.

Local Beverages

  1. Sake: Miyajima is home to many local sake breweries. Try out some of the brewery’s sake offerings.
  2. Mikan Juice: A refreshing juice made from Miyajima’s citrus fruit.

Where to Stay in Miyajima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Miyajima Grand Hotel Arimoto: A luxurious hotel located near the Itsukushima Shrine. It provides stunning views of the Torii gate during high tide.
  2. Backpackers Miyajima: A budget-friendly hostel located near the Miyajima ferry terminal.
  3. Traditional Ryokan Kinsuikan: A traditional Japanese inn that provides a unique cultural experience.

What to Buy in Miyajima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Miyajima Oysters: Bring home some fresh and delicious oysters to share with your loved ones.
  2. Maple Leaf-shaped Manju: A sweet treat made from the island’s famous maple-shaped cakes.
  3. Yosegi Zaiku Crafts: Wooden boxes and items with intricate geometric patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyajima

  1. Respect the nature and the environment: Miyajima is a place of natural beauty. To protect it, it is important to not litter and to be mindful of the environment.
  2. Respect the local customs and traditions: Miyajima is a sacred place for the locals. Be respectful of the customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Miyajima

The best time to visit Miyajima is during the fall foliage season from November to early December or during the cherry blossom season in late March to early April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyajima

Do:

  1. Explore the island: Miyajima is best explored on foot or by bicycle. Take some time to explore the island’s natural beauty.
  2. Try the local food and drinks: Miyajima is famous for its fresh seafood and local sake. Don’t miss out on the opportunity to try it.
  3. Take part in local customs: Miyajima is a place of rich cultural history. Take part in the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Litter: Miyajima is a place of natural beauty. Be sure to throw trash in designated areas to keep the island clean.
  2. Touch the wild deer: Miyajima is home to wild deer that roam the island. While they may be friendly, it is important to not touch or feed them.
  3. Wear shoes inside the temples: When visiting the temples and shrines, it is customary to take off your shoes before entering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Miyajima

Miyajima is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is important to always be aware of your surroundings and to take precautions to secure your personal belongings.

Health in Miyajima

There are no major health risks in Miyajima. It is important to practice good hygiene and to always stay hydrated, especially during hot and humid weather.

Getting to Miyajima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Miyajima can be reached by ferry from Hiroshima. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Miyajima

Hiroshima Airport is the nearest airport to Miyajima.

Bus Stations Near Miyajima

Hiroshima Bus Center offers regular bus services to Miyajima.

Other Options

There are also train services available that connect Hiroshima to Miyajima.

How to Get Around in Miyajima

Miyajima is best explored on foot or by bicycle. Bicycles are readily available for rent near the ferry terminal.

Where to Go Next from Miyajima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Hiroshima: A city with a rich history and culture, Hiroshima is located near Miyajima.
  2. Okayama: Located near Hiroshima, Okayama is home to the Korakuen Garden, one of the three great gardens of Japan.

Conclusion

Miyajima offers a great opportunity to explore Japan’s rich cultural history and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, travelers can make the most out of their trip to Miyajima. Whether it is trying local food, exploring the island’s natural beauty, or learning about the local culture and customs, Miyajima has something for everyone to enjoy.
