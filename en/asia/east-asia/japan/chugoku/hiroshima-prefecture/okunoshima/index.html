Introduction to Ōkunoshima

Ōkunoshima is a small island located in Hiroshima Prefecture, Japan. It is also known as Rabbit Island due to its large population of wild rabbits that roam freely around the island. Despite its small size, Ōkunoshima has a fascinating history, stunning natural beauty, and unique cultural attractions that make it a must-visit destination in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Ōkunoshima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ōkunoshima Poison Gas Museum

The Ōkunoshima Poison Gas Museum is a significant historical site that takes visitors back to the horrific events of World War II. In 1929, the Japanese army built a secret chemical weapons factory on Ōkunoshima, and the museum tells the story of the thousands of workers who were subjected to painful experiments and the effects of exposure to poison gas.

Umihotaru Exhibition Hall

The Umihotaru Exhibition Hall is a small museum that educates visitors about the ecology and environmental issues affecting Ōkunoshima. The exhibition is designed to raise awareness about the importance of preserving the island’s natural beauty and wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Ōkunoshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beaches

One of the biggest draws of Ōkunoshima is its stunning beaches. The crystal-clear waters are perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and diving. Guests can relax on the soft sand, surrounded by the island’s breathtaking scenery.

Hiking and Biking

For those who enjoy exploring the outdoors, Ōkunoshima is the perfect destination. The island has a range of hiking and biking trails that wind through the stunning landscape, offering visitors the chance to discover hidden coves, stunning viewpoints, and historic landmarks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ōkunoshima

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore Ōkunoshima’s stunning scenery.

Ankokuji Temple Course

The Ankokuji Temple Course is a 1.5km trail that leads visitors to the island’s only temple, Ankokuji Temple. The trail winds through the lush forest, passing by stunning waterfalls and offering spectacular views of the surrounding mountains.

Miharashidai Observation Deck Course

The Miharashidai Observation Deck Course is a 5km trail that leads visitors to the island’s most famous viewpoint, Miharashidai Observation Deck. The trail passes through stunning forests, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Ōkunoshima: Activities and Tours

Rabbit Feeding

One of the most popular activities on Ōkunoshima is feeding the wild rabbits that roam the island. Visitors can purchase rabbit food from the gift shop and spend an enjoyable afternoon playing with the adorable animals.

Kayaking

For those seeking adventure, kayaking is a popular activity on Ōkunoshima. Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the island’s stunning coastline at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ōkunoshima

Here is a seven-day itinerary that covers all the must-see attractions on Ōkunoshima:

  • Day 1: Visit the Ōkunoshima Poison Gas Museum
  • Day 2: Relax on the beach and feed the rabbits
  • Day 3: Take a bike tour of the island
  • Day 4: Explore the hiking trails
  • Day 5: Try kayaking along the shoreline
  • Day 6: Visit the Umihotaru Exhibition Hall
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the island’s natural beauty

Food and Drink Scene of Ōkunoshima

Must-Try Dishes

One of the island’s delicacies is the Setoda-Style Soba Noodle, which is made from buckwheat flour and served in a rich broth.

Local Beverages

Ōkunoshima is also famous for its Shimanami Wine, which is made from grapes grown on the island. Visitors can learn about the wine-making process and taste the delicious wine at the local winery.

Where to Stay in Ōkunoshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several cozy guesthouses, hotels, and hostels on Ōkunoshima that offer accommodation for visitors.

What to Buy in Ōkunoshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rabbit Themed Products

Visitors can bring home rabbit-themed products such as stuffed animals, key chains, and t-shirts.

Local Culture and Customs of Ōkunoshima

Ōkunoshima has a rich history and many cultural traditions that visitors can learn about, such as the island’s role in the production of poison gas during World War II.

Best Time to Visit Ōkunoshima

The best time to visit Ōkunoshima is during the spring and autumn when the weather is mild and the island’s natural scenery is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ōkunoshima

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the Setoda-Style Soba Noodles and Shimanami Wine
  • Enjoy the island’s natural beauty and wildlife

Don’t:

  • Feed the rabbits with anything other than designated rabbit food
  • Leave litter on the island

Safety and Health

Safety in Ōkunoshima

Ōkunoshima is a relatively safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their bikes and keeping their valuables secure.

Health in Ōkunoshima

Visitors should take care to protect themselves against insect bites and sunburn, and be sure to stay hydrated while exploring the island.

Getting to Ōkunoshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ōkunoshima

The closest airport to Ōkunoshima is Hiroshima Airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Ōkunoshima

The nearest bus station to Ōkunoshima is Tadanoumi Bus Station. From there, visitors can take a ferry to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the Shinkansen (bullet train) to Mihara Station and then transfer to a local train to Tadanoumi.

How to Get Around in Ōkunoshima

The island is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors can also rent bikes to make getting around easier.

Where to Go Next from Ōkunoshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hiroshima

After visiting Ōkunoshima, visitors can explore the nearby city of Hiroshima, which is known for its fascinating history and beautiful landmarks.

Conclusion

Ōkunoshima is an incredible destination that offers visitors a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in learning about the island’s fascinating history or simply relaxing on the beach with the wild rabbits, Ōkunoshima is sure to be a highlight of any trip to Japan.
