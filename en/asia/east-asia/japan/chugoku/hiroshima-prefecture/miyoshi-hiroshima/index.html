Introduction to Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Located in the eastern part of Hiroshima prefecture in the Chugoku region, Miyoshi is a small town known for its breathtaking natural scenery and cultural heritage. This hidden gem of Japan offers various activities for outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, culture seekers, history buffs, and travelers seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of big cities. In this comprehensive guide to Miyoshi, you will discover the best places to visit, hike, bike, eat, and stay in this charming town, as well as learn about its unique culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Miyoshi is home to several culturally significant attractions that offer visitors a glimpse into its history and traditions. Among them, the following places are worth exploring:

Miyao Castle Ruins

Miyao Castle Ruins is a significant historical site of Miyoshi, representing the region’s feudal past. The castle was built in the early 17th century and served as the residence of the Miyao clan. Today, the site is a popular attraction for history buffs and offers stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Miyoshi City Museum

The Miyoshi City Museum offers an insight into the town’s heritage and culture. The museum’s exhibits showcase the town’s history, art, and crafts, including traditional pottery and textiles.

Natural Attractions of Miyoshi (Hiroshima): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miyoshi is also home to several natural attractions that offer visitors a chance to experience Japan’s natural beauty. From stunning waterfalls to scenic hiking trails, there’s plenty to explore in Miyoshi.

Taishakukyo Gorge

Taishakukyo Gorge is a picturesque valley located in the northern part of Miyoshi. The gorge is known for its stunning rock formations, clear streams, and waterfalls. Visitors can enjoy hiking along the walking paths, and the gorge is especially beautiful during the autumn foliage season.

Shishiiwa Observatory

Shishiiwa Observatory is located at the top of Mount Shishiiwa and offers stunning panoramic views of Miyoshi and the surrounding mountains. The observatory is accessible by hiking or taking a cable car up the mountain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Miyoshi is a hiker’s paradise, offering fantastic trails that lead to stunning views of the town and the surrounding area. Some of the best hiking routes in Miyoshi are:

Miyoshi Nature Trail

The Miyoshi Nature Trail is a 12-kilometer trail that winds through the woods and along the Miyata River. The trail features several scenic points, such as waterfalls and rock formations, and is an excellent spot for bird watching and nature photography.

Shimanami Kaido (Cycling Road)

The Shimanami Kaido cycling road is a popular biking route that connects the islands of the Seto Inland Sea with a series of bridges. The route is 60 kilometers long and offers stunning views of the sea and the mountains. Cycling enthusiasts can rent bikes in Miyoshi or nearby towns and enjoy a leisurely ride.

Best Things to Do in Miyoshi (Hiroshima): Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking through the beautiful nature and scenic views offered by Miyoshi, there are plenty of other activities and tours to try out. Here are some of the best things to do:

Miyoshi Onsen (Hot Springs)

Miyoshi is home to several hot springs, offering visitors a chance to relax and unwind. The waters of these hot springs are rich in minerals and are believed to have therapeutic properties. Try visiting the Fureai no Yu Miyoshi, a modern hot spring facility that is popular among locals and visitors alike.

Rafting and Canyoning Adventure

Miyoshi is also known for its thrilling rafting and canyoning adventures. Visitors can book the trips and explore the beautiful river and valley while enjoying the exhilarating rapids and waterfalls.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Must-Try Dishes

Miyoshi’s cuisine features fresh seafood, wild mountain vegetables, and locally grown rice. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Iwakuni Oysters

Iwakuni oysters are a local delicacy in Miyoshi. These succulent and plump oysters are harvested in the nearby town of Iwakuni and are known for their rich and briny taste.

Kakiage Udon

Kakiage udon is a popular noodle dish in Miyoshi. The dish consists of thick udon noodles and mixed vegetable tempura. The noodles are served in a hot broth and topped with a special blend of soy sauce.

Local Beverages

Miyoshi is known for producing sake, Japan’s traditional rice wine. There are several breweries in the town, offering visitors a chance to taste this local specialty. Another popular alcoholic beverage in Miyoshi is Nihonshu, a type of Japanese whiskey.

Where to Stay in Miyoshi (Hiroshima): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Miyoshi, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

Miyoshi Grand Hotel

Miyoshi Grand Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Miyoshi. The hotel features a hot spring and traditional Japanese-style rooms.

Sora Blue Miyoshi

Sora Blue Miyoshi is a hostel located in the Miyoshi countryside, offering breathtaking views of the natural surroundings. The hostel features shared dormitories and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and living room.

What to Buy in Miyoshi (Hiroshima): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Miyoshi is known for its traditional crafts and pottery, as well as its local food products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

Iwakuni Konjac

Iwakuni konjac is a type of Japanese yam that is grown in the nearby town of Iwakuni. The konjac is processed into a variety of snacks, such as chips and crackers, that are sold throughout Miyoshi.

Miyoshi Pottery

Miyoshi pottery is a traditional craft that has been passed down through generations. The pottery features unique patterns and designs and is known for its durability and quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Miyoshi’s culture is deeply rooted in nature and traditional customs. Visitors should be aware of the following cultural aspects while visiting Miyoshi:

  • Respect for nature and the environment is highly valued in Miyoshi
  • Traditionally, Miyoshi residents practice “Ho-o-ryu,” a form of Japanese classical dance with historical roots originating from Miyoshi

Best Time to Visit Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

The best time to visit Miyoshi is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most breathtaking. The autumn foliage season, which typically runs from late October to early December, is particularly stunning in Miyoshi.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Miyoshi:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take the time to explore Miyoshi’s natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disturb the wildlife or natural habitats

Safety and Health

Safety in Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Miyoshi is generally a safe town, with a low crime rate. Visitors should be aware of the natural hazards of the area, such as slippery trails and dangerous wildlife.

Health in Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Japan has an excellent healthcare system, and Miyoshi has several hospitals and clinics. Visitors should bring travel insurance to ensure they are covered in case of injury or illness.

Getting to Miyoshi (Hiroshima): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

The closest major airport to Miyoshi is Hiroshima Airport, which is located approximately 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Miyoshi is accessible by bus from major cities in the region, including Hiroshima and Matsuyama.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Miyoshi by train from Hiroshima or Matsuyama.

How to Get Around Miyoshi (Hiroshima)

Miyoshi is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking or cycling distance. Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Miyoshi (Hiroshima): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miyoshi is the perfect base for exploring other scenic spots in the Chugoku region. Among the nearby destinations are:

Onomichi

Onomichi is a charming coastal town located approximately 50 kilometers from Miyoshi. The town is known for its famous Temple Walk, featuring several temples and shrines along the scenic mountain path.

Hiroshima

Hiroshima is the capital of Hiroshima prefecture and is known for its historical significance and rich cultural heritage. Among the attractions in Hiroshima are the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and the Miyajima Island.

Conclusion

Miyoshi is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to experience Japan’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. With its stunning scenery and outdoor adventures, combined with its unique cuisine and traditional crafts, Miyoshi is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking to explore off-the-beaten-path Japan.
