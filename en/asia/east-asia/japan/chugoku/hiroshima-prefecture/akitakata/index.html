Introduction to Akitakata

Situated in the Hiroshima prefecture in the Chugoku region of Japan, Akitakata is a beautiful town surrounded by mountains and forests. Despite being a relatively small town, Akitakata offers a plethora of attractions ranging from historical landmarks to natural wonders, making it a popular destination among tourists. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the hidden gems of Akitakata.

List of Places to Visit in Akitakata

Akitakata offers a variety of attractions for tourists. Here are some of the must-visit places in Akitakata:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jissho-ji Temple
  • Eishoji Temple
  • Akitakata City Museum of Art
  • Oyama Shrine
  • Miyoshi Winery

Natural Attractions of Akitakata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Osorakan Snow Park
  • Hiba-Dogo-Taishaku Quasi-National Park
  • Akitsumikawa Nature Park
  • Sera Kogen Plateau
  • Momiji no Yu hot spring

Best Things to Do in Akitakata: Activities and Tours

Akitakata offers a variety of activities and tours that will keep tourists engaged throughout their stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Akitakata:

Food and Drink Scene of Akitakata

Akitakata has a diverse food and drink scene, offering a range of local specialties and seasonal dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Akitakata:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hōfun: a flat and wide noodle dish often served with broth and chicken or vegetables
  • Oyaki: a filled bun made with fermented dough and stuffed with savory and sweet fillings
  • Momiji Manju: a maple leaf-shaped cake filled with red bean paste, a specialty of the area
  • Kaki: oysters that are farmed along the coast of Hiroshima

Local Beverages

  • Saijo sake: one of the most famous types of sake brewed in Hiroshima
  • Kishu Nanko plum wine: a fruity and fragrant wine made from Kishu Nanko plums

Local Culture and Customs of Akitakata

As a small town with a rich cultural history, Akitakata has its own unique customs and traditions. One of the most significant events in Akitakata is the Kuroso Festival, a traditional festival held in January that features a parade of dancers and drummers.

Best Time to Visit Akitakata

The best time to visit Akitakata is during the autumn months of September to November when the foliage is at its peak. Spring is also a good time to visit when the cherry blossoms are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akitakata

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take part in cultural festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Speak loudly or be disruptive in public
  • Take photographs of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Akitakata

Akitakata is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, tourists should always take precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Akitakata

Akitakata has a good healthcare system, and tourists can easily find hospitals and clinics in the area. It is always recommended to purchase travel insurance before visiting a foreign country.

How to Get Around in Akitakata

Akitakata is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available for hire, and there is a local bus network that covers the town and its surrounding areas.
