Introduction to Higashihiroshima

Nestled in the Chugoku region of Japan’s main island, Honshu, Higashihiroshima is a hidden gem that deserves more attention from travellers. The city is situated on the eastern parts of the Hiroshima prefecture and offers a blend of Japanese history, culture, and natural beauty. It is an ideal destination for those who want to explore less-visited parts of Japan and experience authentic rural life.

List of Places to Visit in Higashihiroshima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Higashihiroshima’s historical attractions provide an insight into the region’s past and its rich cultural heritage. Some popular sites include:

  • Kurahashi-Yahata-kan Museum: This museum displays various artifacts that depict the life of people in the Hiroshima region.
  • Osukame Shrine: A serene shrine that is believed to have existed for over 1,000 years.
  • Takehara City: A historic town that is popular for its merchant district, which dates back to the Edo period.

Natural Attractions of Higashihiroshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Higashihiroshima’s natural beauty is another highlight of the city that tourists cannot afford to miss. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Shimanami Kaido Cycling Route: This picturesque route allows visitors to cycle or walk between six islands, offering some of the most scenic views of the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Mount Fudekage: A breathtaking hiking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Mominoki Park: A hidden gem that offers a tranquil and relaxing atmosphere, ideal for picnics and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Higashihiroshima

Higashihiroshima is an excellent location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following are some popular trails that offer unique experiences to visitors:

  • Tomo-no-ura to Onomichi Hiking Trail: A 14 km trail that offers stunning views of the sea and mountains as you hike between historic towns.
  • **Makimine to Yu-kake-Iwa!: A hiking trail that takes approximately 4 hours, passing through beautiful Japanese forests and leading to a magnificent view of the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Shimanami Kaido Cycling Route: A 70 km cycling route connecting Honshu and Shikoku islands, allowing you to explore six islands that form the Shimanami Kaido archipelago.

Best Things to Do in Higashihiroshima: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the city’s natural and cultural heritage, there are several fun activities and tours that visitors can enjoy.

  • Momo-no-kai Cherry Blossom Viewing Party: A spring festival that celebrates the beauty of cherry blossoms; visitors can enjoy traditional music, food and drink, and watch fireworks.
  • Setouchi Sea Safari: A sea tour that allows visitors to explore the Seto Inland Sea, its islands, marine life, and unique rock formations.
  • Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Factory Tour: A guided tour that provides an exciting opportunity to see the production process at one of the world’s largest corporations that specializes in aerospace engineering and manufacturing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Higashihiroshima

Here is a suggested itinerary for exploring the best of Higashihiroshima in one week:

1 Visit Kurahashi-Yahata-kan Museum and Osukame Shrine

Food and Drink Scene of Higashihiroshima

Must-Try Dishes

Higashihiroshima’s local cuisine is delicious and unique, and visitors should try the following dishes:

  • Kaki Ryori: A dish made from oysters that are grown in the Seto Inland Sea and cooked in various ways, including grilled, deep-fried, and in a hotpot.
  • Sera-udon: A noodle dish made with thin noodles, vegetables, and pork that is slow-cooked before serving.
  • Izumiootsu Wagyu: A premium Japanese beef that is popular for its marbling and tender texture.

Local Beverages

Higashihiroshima is also famous for its local beverages, and visitors should try:

  • Sake: A Japanese rice wine that is produced in the Hiroshima prefecture and enjoyed throughout Japan.
  • Izumibashi beer: A locally brewed craft beer that offers unique flavors and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Higashihiroshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Higashihiroshima offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Granvia Hiroshima: Located close to the Higashihiroshima station and offers comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and free internet access.
  • Guest House Sukeroku: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms, a shared lounge, and a garden.
  • Japanese-style Ryokan: A traditional Japanese guesthouse that offers a unique cultural experience, with traditional Japanese rooms, hot springs, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Higashihiroshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Higashihiroshima has several local products that make great souvenirs:

  • Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki: A savory pancake made with cabbage and various meats and vegetables, cooked on a griddle, and topped with sauce, mayonnaise, and green onions.
  • Hiroshima-style tsukemen noodles: Thick and chewy noodles dipped in a rich and flavorful broth with various toppings.
  • Izumibashi Sake: Sake produced using traditional methods that are unique to the Hiroshima prefecture.

Local Culture and Customs of Higashihiroshima

Higashihiroshima has a rich culture and customs that reflect the region’s history and traditions. Visitors should respect the following customs:

  • Removing shoes: It is customary to remove shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Respect for elders: The Japanese culture values respect for elders, and visitors should show deference to older people in social situations.
  • Bow when greeting: Bowing is a way of showing respect in Japan, and visitors should bow when greeting people.

Best Time to Visit Higashihiroshima

The best time to visit Higashihiroshima is during spring and autumn when the weather is mild, and the natural surroundings offer stunning views. Spring (March - May) is perfect for cherry blossom viewing and hiking, and autumn (September - November) is ideal for sightseeing and cultural festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Higashihiroshima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Take off your shoes before entering homes or temples.
  • Carry cash, as some places may not accept credit cards.
  • Take care of the environment and dispose of waste in designated bins.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes indoors.
  • Speak loudly or disturb the peace.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Tip in restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Higashihiroshima

Higashihiroshima is a safe destination, and visitors need not worry about crime. However, it is essential to observe the following safety tips:

  • Pay attention to weather forecasts, especially during typhoon and rainy seasons.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and shoes for hiking or other outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated and use sunscreen to avoid heatstroke.
  • Stay alert when using public transport and be mindful of your belongings.

Health in Higashihiroshima

Higashihiroshima has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical care if needed. However, visitors should take basic health precautions, including:

  • Wash hands regularly.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Take appropriate medication for any pre-existing conditions and allergies.

Getting to Higashihiroshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Higashihiroshima is accessible by different means of transport, and visitors can choose the most suitable option depending on their budget and preferences.

Airports Near Higashihiroshima

The closest airport to Higashihiroshima is Hiroshima Airport (HIJ), which operates domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Higashihiroshima

Central Higashihiroshima Station is the main bus terminal in the city, connecting visitors to different parts of the Hiroshima prefecture.

Other Options

Visitors can also use the Shinkansen from Tokyo or Osaka to Hiroshima, and then take a local train to Higashihiroshima.

How to Get Around in Higashihiroshima

Higashihiroshima has an efficient public transport system, including buses and trains, making it easy for visitors to get around the city and its surrounding areas. Taxis are also available, but they are more expensive than buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Higashihiroshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Higashihiroshima is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations, including:

  • Hiroshima city: A must-visit destination that offers historical sites, museums, and a vibrant nightlife.
  • Onomichi: A picturesque town that is famous for its temple walk and fascinating museums.
  • Iwakuni: A historic town that is ideal for day trips, offering stunning views of the Kintaikyo Bridge and castle.

Conclusion

Higashihiroshima is an underrated destination that offers a blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy hiking, sightseeing, and authentic Japanese cuisine, while also experiencing local customs and traditions. This guide has provided useful information on the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to get around Higashihiroshima, making it easier for visitors to plan their trip and make the most of their time in this hidden gem of Japan.
