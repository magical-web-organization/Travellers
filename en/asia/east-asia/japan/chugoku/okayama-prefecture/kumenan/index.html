Introduction to Kumenan

Nestled deep in the heart of Okayama Prefecture, Kumenan is a quaint rural town that is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. The town is situated in the Chugoku region of Japan, in East Asia, and is the perfect destination for anyone who wants to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in the serene natural beauty of the Japanese countryside.

The town of Kumenan is steeped in history and boasts a plethora of cultural and historical attractions that are waiting to be explored. Visitors can also enjoy a wide range of outdoor pursuits, such as hiking, cycling, and nature walks, while soaking in the breathtaking natural landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Kumenan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kumenan Town Museum: This museum was established to preserve the rich cultural heritage of Kumenan and showcases a wide range of exhibits related to the town’s history, natural beauty, and culture. Featuring artifacts, photographs, and other memorabilia, the museum offers visitors a chance to explore the town’s past and learn more about its present.

  • Tatsuo Shrine: This beautiful Shinto shrine is located at the foot of Mt. Tatsuo and is dedicated to the deity of agriculture. The shrine features a beautiful wooden torii gate, surrounded by stunning cherry blossoms that are in full bloom during spring.

  • Tatsuo-ji Temple: This famous Buddhist temple is situated next to Tatsuo Shrine and is renowned for its beautiful cherry blossoms and picturesque scenery. The temple grounds are a tranquil oasis amidst the town, with beautiful gardens and an observation deck that offers a panoramic view of the entire area.

Natural Attractions of Kumenan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Tatsuo: At 767 meters high, Mt. Tatsuo provides visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding natural beauty. The mountain’s hiking trail is a popular spot for visitors who enjoy nature and want to experience the rich natural landscapes of the region.

  • Sohara Lake: This lake is a hidden gem of Kumenan, popular for its mesmerizing clear waters, picturesque landscape, and tranquil surroundings. Visitors can enjoy boat rides or simply relax in the surrounding areas to witness the beauty of nature.

  • Kumenan Gorge: This magnificent gorge offers a mix of natural beauty and scenic views, with its stunning waterfalls and crystal-clear streams. The gorge features several hiking trails that range from easy to moderately difficult, perfect for nature lovers of all experience levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumenan

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Kumenan, thanks to its natural beauty and picturesque scenery. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Mt. Tarou Hike: This hiking trail leads visitors to the summit of Mt. Tarou, providing stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, including nearby peaks and the scenic valleys.

  • Tatsuoji Temple and Shrine Cycling Route: Starting from Kumenan Station, this cycling route takes visitors through the countryside, towards the beautiful Tatsuoji Temple and Shrine. It is an easy trail making it the perfect route for day-trippers.

Best Things to Do in Kumenan: Activities and Tours

  • Kumenan Grape Picking: Kumenan is known for its succulent grapes, which are typically harvested from late-August to September. Visitors can pick their own grapes in the surrounding vineyards and taste the freshest fruit available.

  • Sohara Lake Canoeing: Visitors can enjoy the tranquil beauty of Sohara Lake with a canoeing tour. It’s a great opportunity for visitors to witness the natural beauty of the lake, with clear waters and the surrounding greenery in full bloom.

Food and Drink Scene of Kumenan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kawara Soba: This dish includes soba noodles served on a heated tile of Kawara or roof tiles. The tiles are heated before serving to cook the noodles and add a crispy texture to them.

  • Kumenan Grilled Venison: This is a unique dish of Kumenan where venison meat is grilled to perfection with Kumenan sauce. It has a unique taste that’s unlike anywhere else in the country.

Local Beverages

  • Tatsuo Temple’s Bishamon Daiginjo Sake: This sake is made from water from the Tatsuo temple in spring and is made using traditional methods.

  • White Peach Juice: Kumenan is famous for its fresh and succulent white peaches, which are used to make refreshing juice.

Where to Stay in Kumenan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kumenan Ryokan: This traditional Japanese-style inn offers visitors comfortable and luxurious accommodation while immersing them in the rich cultural heritage of Kumenan.

  • Purari Kumenan: This modern guesthouse offers affordable accommodation while providing visitors with a comfortable stay and beautiful scenic views.

  • Kumenan Oyama Sanso: This guesthouse offers beautiful natural landscapes with plenty of outdoor activities such as hiking, canoeing, and cycling.

What to Buy in Kumenan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kumenan’s Soba Noodles: You can bring home some soba noodles that are made using traditional methods of grinding and stone milling for a unique taste and texture.

  • Kumenan Grilled Venison sauce: The Kumenan sauce is used to make grilled venison and is a unique souvenir that visitors can take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumenan

Kumenan is home to several unique cultural customs that reflect the town’s rich heritage. The town is famous for its traditional festivals that are held throughout the year, such as the Kumenan Festival and the Tatsuo Shrine Festival. Visitors can also witness the natural beauty of Kumenan by participating in other Japanese cultural activities, such as tea ceremonies and calligraphy classes.

Best Time to Visit Kumenan

The best time to visit Kumenan is during the autumn months, from September to November, when the weather is dry and comfortable. During this time, the area is awash with vibrant fall colors, which make the natural beauty of Kumenan even more gorgeous. Cherry blossom season in the spring is also a beautiful time to explore Kumenan.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumenan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local dishes and explore the local cuisine
  • Carry cash, as many places in Kumenan do not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Litter or spoil the natural beauty of Kumenan
  • Take photographs of locals or their homes without their permission
  • Use loud voices or behave rudely and aggressively

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumenan

Kumenan is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take precautions against natural elements and keep themselves updated on local weather warnings.

Health in Kumenan

Kumenan has adequate medical facilities, with a local hospital being located not far from the town. Visitors should carry any required medications with them and take reasonable health precautions.

Getting to Kumenan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kumenan is accessible via train or bus from major cities such as Osaka and Tokyo.

Airports Near Kumenan

The nearest airport is Okayama Airport, and visitors can take a train from there to reach Kumenan.

Bus Stations Near Kumenan

There are several bus stations throughout Kumenan that provide visitors with easy access to all parts of the town and its surroundings.

Other Options

Taxis are available throughout the town, but visitors are advised to keep in mind that they can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Kumenan

The easiest way to explore Kumenan is by bicycle or on foot, thanks to the town’s well-maintained roads and numerous walking trails. Visitors can rent bicycles from the train station or hotels.

Where to Go Next from Kumenan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Okayama City: Just over an hour’s drive from Kumenan, Okayama City is home to a castle, beautiful gardens and many cultural landmarks.

  • Himeji Castle: One of the most well-preserved castles in Japan, located in the heart of Himeji City.

Conclusion

Kumenan is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. From hiking and biking routes to delicious local cuisine and souvenirs, Kumenan has something for everyone. With our comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the town’s hidden treasures and immerse themselves in its rich cultural heritage.
