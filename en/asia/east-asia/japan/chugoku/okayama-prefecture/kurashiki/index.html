A Comprehensive Guide to Kurashiki, Japan

Nestled in Okayama prefecture, Kurashiki is a picturesque city rich in history and culture. Known for its well-preserved, traditional architecture and serene canals, the city attracts both locals and tourists alike. Kurashiki has many things to offer, including museums, natural attractions, and a thriving food and drink scene. Follow this comprehensive guide for an unforgettable trip to Kurashiki.

List of Places to Visit in Kurashiki

Kurashiki has many interesting places to visit, categorized into culturally important attractions and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kurashiki is steeped in history, and there are countless museums and historical places to visit. Some of them include:

  • Ohara Museum of Art: Founded in 1930, this is the first museum in Japan to specialize in Western art. The museum showcases paintings and sculptures by renowned artists such as Monet, Renoir, and Gauguin.
  • Kurabo Memorial Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and development of the textile industry in Kurashiki. The museum exhibits a range of textiles, including clothes and traditional Japanese fabrics.
  • Kurashiki Folkcraft Museum: This museum showcases traditional Japanese crafts, with a focus on ceramics, textiles, and lacquerware.

Natural Attractions of Kurashiki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kurashiki is also home to many natural attractions that offer scenic views and opportunities for outdoor activities:

  • Mt. Washuzan: This mountain, standing at 133 meters, offers a stunning panoramic view of the city and the surrounding Seto Inland Sea.
  • Kurashiki Ivy Square: A garden complex with a variety of plants and flowers, including ivy, roses, and hydrangeas.
  • Kojima Bay: This bay is a peaceful and scenic spot that offers a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurashiki

Kurashiki has many hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the city and the surrounding nature:

  • Kurashiki Bike Route: This route runs along the canal and offers a scenic view of the historic district.
  • Shinjoji Temple Hike: This hike takes you up to Shinjoji Temple, which offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Kurashiki: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours that you can join in Kurashiki, such as:

  • Kimono Rental: Rent a traditional Japanese kimono and stroll through the historic district of Kurashiki.
  • Canal Boat Tour: Take a leisurely boat ride along the canal and admire the traditional architecture of the houses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kurashiki

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Kurashiki:

Day Activity
1 Explore the historic district of Kurashiki and visit the Ohara Museum of Art.
2 Visit the Kurashiki Textile Industry Museum and Kurashiki Folkcraft Museum.
3 Hike up Mt. Washuzan and enjoy the panoramic views.
4 Take a canal boat tour and visit Kojima Bay.
5 Rent a kimono and spend the day exploring the city.
6 Visit the Kurabo Memorial Museum and take a bike ride along the Kurashiki Bike Route.
7 Visit Shinjoji Temple and end the trip with a relaxing day at Kurashiki Ivy Square.

Food and Drink Scene of Kurashiki

Kurashiki has a thriving food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kibi Dango: These sweet rice dumplings are a local specialty and are often served with green tea.
  • Okayama Soba: These handmade noodles are a regional favorite and are often served with a light broth and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Doburoku: This is a type of unfiltered sake that is brewed in the region.
  • Momiji Manju: A traditional Japanese cake filled with sweet bean paste and flavored with maple leaves.

Where to Stay in Kurashiki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kurashiki offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets:

  • Kurashiki Kokusai Hotel: This hotel is located in the historic district and offers rooms with a traditional Japanese ambiance.
  • Toyoko Inn Kurashiki-eki Minamiguchi: This hotel is conveniently located near the train station and offers modern amenities at an affordable price.
  • Guest House Kikusui-Ro: This guesthouse offers private rooms and dormitory-style accommodations for those on a budget.

What to Buy in Kurashiki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kurashiki has many unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home:

  • Bizen-yaki Pottery: This is a type of traditional Japanese pottery that originated in Okayama prefecture.
  • Kurashiki Canvas: This is a type of heavy-duty canvas that is produced in Kurashiki and is used to make bags and other goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Kurashiki

When visiting Kurashiki, it is important to be mindful of the local customs and culture:

  • Shoes Off Indoors: It is customary to remove your shoes before entering homes and some restaurants in Japan.
  • Respect for Elders: Respect for elders is deeply ingrained in Japanese culture.

Best Time to Visit Kurashiki

The best time to visit Kurashiki is from late March to early May and from September to November. These periods offer comfortable weather and beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurashiki

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kurashiki:

Do:

  • Bow: Bowing is a traditional way to greet people in Japan.
  • Carry Cash: Many small businesses in Japan do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Wear Shoes Inside: As mentioned, it is customary to remove shoes before entering homes and some restaurants in Japan.
  • Tip: Tipping is not customary in Japan and may even be considered rude.

Safety and Health

Kurashiki is a safe city to visit. However, it’s always important to take precautions:

Safety in Kurashiki

  • Watch for Bicycles: Bicycles are a popular mode of transportation in Japan, and visitors should be mindful of them when walking on sidewalks and crossing streets.
  • Take Caution in Mountains: If you plan to hike, always be prepared with proper equipment and be cautious of weather conditions.

Health in Kurashiki

  • Stay Hydrated: Summers in Kurashiki can be hot and humid, so it’s important to stay hydrated.
  • Be Mindful of Allergies: Many traditional Japanese dishes contain soy, wheat, and other allergens, so be sure to inform your waiter of any allergies or dietary restrictions.

Getting to Kurashiki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kurashiki is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options:

Airports Near Kurashiki

  • Okayama Airport: This airport is located approximately 43 kilometers from Kurashiki and offers domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Kurashiki

  • Kurashiki Station: This is the main bus hub in Kurashiki and offers frequent bus services to neighboring cities.

Other Options

  • Train: Kurashiki is on the JR Sanyo Shinkansen line, making it easily accessible by train.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a good option for those who want to explore the surrounding towns and villages.

How to Get Around in Kurashiki

Kurashiki is a small city that is easily explored on foot or by bike. Buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kurashiki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Kurashiki:

  • Bitchu-Takahashi: This small town is located approximately 36 kilometers from Kurashiki and is known for its historic castle and traditional Japanese houses.
  • Kurashiki Station City: This is a shopping and entertainment complex located near Kurashiki Station.

Conclusion

Kurashiki is a culturally rich and beautiful city that has something to offer everyone. Whether you’re interested in museums, outdoor activities, or food and drink, Kurashiki will not disappoint. Use this comprehensive guide to make the most of your time in Kurashiki and create unforgettable memories.
