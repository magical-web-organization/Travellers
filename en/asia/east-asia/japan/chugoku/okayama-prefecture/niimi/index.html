Introduction to Niimi

Niimi is a small city located in Okayama prefecture, in Chugoku region of Japan. With its beautiful natural landscapes and rich history, Niimi makes for a perfect off-the-beaten-path destination for travelers seeking to explore the less visited parts of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Niimi

Niimi offers a wide range of activities and attractions for tourists. Below is a list of places to visit in Niimi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Niimi City Museum: Established in 1987, the museum showcases the history of Niimi with a collection of artifacts and exhibits.

  • Iwahara Castle Ruins: Built in the 15th century, this castle was once an important stronghold during the Sengoku period.

  • Shugendo Shrine Honden: This shrine is a significant religious site for the Shugendo sect of Buddhism, which has its roots in mountain worship.

Natural Attractions of Niimi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kamisugi Falls: This waterfall is a popular spot for hiking and nature enthusiasts, with a 45-meter drop and beautiful views.

  • Mount Daisen: With an elevation of 1,729 meters, Mount Daisen is a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Nacchan Kogen Park: The park features beautiful flowers, hiking trails, and camping facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Niimi

Niimi is a paradise for hikers and cyclists with plenty of trails to explore. Here are some popular routes:

  • Iwahara Castle Ruins Hiking Trail: This trail leads you through the ruins of Iwahara Castle, providing beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Daisen Skyline Road: This scenic route is a popular biking spot, taking you through the mountains with stunning views.

  • Kamisugi Falls Hiking Trail: This moderate hike leads you to the breathtaking Kamisugi Falls.

Best Things to Do in Niimi: Activities and Tours

  • Shugendo Experience: Visitors can participate in an authentic Shugendo experience, led by a local guide.

  • Niimi City Museum: Take a deep dive into the city’s rich history and culture at the Niimi City Museum.

  • Mountain Hiking and Biking: Explore the beautiful landscapes of Niimi by hiking or biking up the mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Niimi

Niimi offers a range of local delicacies and beverages. Below are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iwahara Soba: A delicious local soba dish made from a blend of buckwheat and local wheat flour.

  • Hiruzen Yakisoba: A unique yakisoba dish made with handmade noodles and pork belly.

  • Momiji Manju: A popular Niimi snack that consists of maple leaf-shaped steamed buns filled with sweet bean paste.

Local Beverages

  • Hiruzen Beer: A locally brewed craft beer made using natural mountain water and local hops.

  • Umeshu: A traditional Japanese plum wine that is popular in Niimi.

  • Hiruzen Cider: A refreshing sparkling cider, perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Niimi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Niimi has several options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels.

Here are some recommended options:

  • Hiruzen Hotel: A luxurious hotel with hot springs, tennis courts, and an indoor pool.

  • Nacchan Kogen Park Campgrounds: A fun and affordable camping option for nature lovers.

  • Niimi Guesthouse Yumeya: A cozy guesthouse that gives visitors a taste of traditional Japanese hospitality.

What to Buy in Niimi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Niimi is famous for its traditional arts and crafts, and local delicacies. Below are some recommended souvenirs and local products:

  • Sake: Niimi produces some of the finest sake in Japan, including the famous Dassai sake brand.

  • Hiruzen Lavender Products: Hiruzen is famous for its lavender fields, and visitors can buy lavender-themed products like soap, oil, and sachets.

  • Bizen Pottery: Bizen pottery is a traditional Japanese craft, and Niimi is a great place to buy authentic Bizen pottery pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Niimi

Niimi has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Some customs and practices unique to Niimi include:

  • Shugendo: Niimi is home to the Shugendo sect of Buddhism, which places a strong emphasis on mountain worship and ascetic practices.

  • Fudo Waterfall Festival: The Fudo Waterfall Festival is held annually at the Fudo Waterfall and celebrates the Shugendo religion.

  • Hiruzen Amidaho Temple: This ancient temple features a distinctive thatched roof and is considered a cultural treasure of Niimi.

Best Time to Visit Niimi

The best time to visit Niimi is during the spring and autumn months, from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The cherry blossoms in spring and the fall foliage are particularly beautiful in Niimi.

Do’s and Don’ts of Niimi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Be mindful of your surroundings when hiking or biking.

  • Try the local delicacies and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

  • Damage local natural landscapes.

  • Leave trash or waste behind when hiking or camping.

Safety and Health

Safety in Niimi

Niimi is generally a safe place to travel, with a very low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take normal precautions, such as securing valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Niimi

Japan has a high standard of healthcare, and Niimi has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics that cater to both locals and tourists.

Getting to Niimi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Niimi can be reached by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Niimi

The closest airport is Okayama Airport, which is serviced by both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Niimi

Niimi Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers regular bus services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Niimi is also accessible by train, with several JR train stations located within the city.

How to Get Around in Niimi

Niimi is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. Biking and cab services are also available for visitors who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Niimi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Niimi is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations, including:

  • Okayama: A lively city in Chugoku region, known for its beautiful gardens and historic castle.

  • Hiroshima: A major city in Japan, with a tragic history and numerous historical and cultural attractions.

  • Izumo: Located in nearby Shimane prefecture, Izumo is home to the famous Izumo Taisha, one of Japan’s oldest and most revered Shinto shrines.

Conclusion

Niimi offers a unique blend of natural scenery, cultural heritage, and authentic Japanese experiences. With its friendly locals, delicious cuisine, and rich history, Niimi is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to explore the less-touristy parts of Japan.
