Introduction to Okayama (Prefecture)

Located in the Chugoku region of Japan, Okayama (Prefecture) boasts of its natural and cultural wonders. This charming and picturesque prefecture is often overlooked by tourists, but it is actually a hidden gem with so much to offer. Known as the “Land of Sunshine,” Okayama has mild weather all year round, making it the perfect destination any time of the year. In this travel guide, discover the best things to do, where to stay and eat, how to get there, and more in Okayama (Prefecture), Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Okayama (Prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Okayama Castle: One of the most famous castles in Japan, featuring unique architectural designs and stunning views of the city.

  • Korakuen Garden: One of Japan’s top three gardens, featuring beautiful landscapes, scenic spots, and traditional tea houses.

  • Okayama Prefectural Museum of Art: One of Japan’s finest art museums, housing a collection of contemporary and modern art, including works from Monet, Picasso, and Renoir.

  • Bitchu Matsuyama Castle: One of the few remaining mountain castles in Japan, featuring a breathtaking view of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Okayama (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kibiji Cycling Road: A 20km cycling road lined with cherry blossoms, making it a perfect spot for a scenic bike ride.

  • Washuzan Hill: One of the best places to view the Seto Inland Sea, featuring a cable car ride and a panoramic view of the surrounding area.

  • Tama River: A popular spot for kayak and rafting enthusiasts, boasting of its crystal-clear waters and picturesque scenery.

  • Mount Tsuneyama: A 450m high mountain that offers a scenic hiking trail, featuring a panoramic view of the Seto Inland Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okayama (Prefecture)

  • Mount Washu: A moderate hiking trail that offers an excellent view of the city of Okayama.

  • Okutsu Valley: A popular spot for hiking and camping, featuring a scenic trail along the river.

  • Nature Walk in Bitchu-Takahashi: A relaxing walking tour that offers a glimpse of the natural wonder of Okayama.

Best Things to Do in Okayama (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Okayama-Kurashiki Canal Trip: A unique canal tour that offers a view of the traditional houses and buildings along the canal.

  • Okayama Prefectural Museum of Art: Experience the beauty of art and culture in this world-renowned museum.

  • Visit Local Sake Breweries: Discover the traditional way of making Sake and taste the delicious local Sake.

  • Bizen Pottery Experience: Learn about the history and culture of Bizen pottery, one of Japan’s oldest pottery traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Okayama (Prefecture)

Day 1 Visit Okayama Castle and Korakuen Garden
Day 2 Explore Bitchu Matsuyama Castle and Washuzan Hill
Day 3 Visit Kibiji Cycling Road and kayak/raft at Tama River
Day 4 Hike Mount Tsuneyama and Nature Walk in Bitchu-Takahashi
Day 5 Okayama-Kurashiki Canal Trip and Visit to Local Sake Breweries
Day 6 Visit the Okayama Prefectural Museum of Art
Day 7 Bizen Pottery Experience and shopping at Okayama’s local markets

Food and Drink Scene of Okayama (Prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barazushi: A type of sushi that features cooked and marinated fish on top of sushi rice.

  • Okayama Soba: Thin noodles made from buckwheat flour, served in hot soup.

  • Hiruzen Yakisoba: Fried noodles made with local ingredients, including pork and cabbage.

  • Kibi Dango: A type of mochi balls made with wheat flour, served with local honey or sweet soy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Local Sake: Made from locally grown rice and water, Okayama is known for its delicious Sake.

  • Momoshu: A sweet liquor made from fermented peaches.

  • Tayuzake: A type of Sake that is traditionally brewed by workers in the rice fields.

Where to Stay in Okayama (Prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Granvia Okayama: Located in the heart of Okayama Station, this hotel offers modern amenities and an excellent view of the city.

  • Okayama Guesthouse Moko: A budget-friendly accommodation option, featuring a cozy and relaxing vibe.

  • Ryokan Kurashiki: A traditional Japanese inn located in the historical district of Kurashiki, offering a glimpse of Japanese culture and hospitality.

What to Buy in Okayama (Prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bizen Pottery: Known as one of Japan’s oldest pottery traditions, Bizen pottery is a popular souvenir item.

  • Okayama Sake: Bring home a bottle of locally brewed Sake.

  • Kibi Dango: A type of mochi ball made with wheat flour, served with local honey or sweet soy sauce.

Local Culture and Customs of Okayama (Prefecture)

  • Okayama is known for its culture of respect for nature and tradition.

  • Bowing when greeting is a common practice in Okayama, showing respect and courtesy to others.

  • Omotenashi, the Japanese hospitality culture, is deeply embedded in the way of life in Okayama.

Best Time to Visit Okayama (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Okayama is in spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November), when the weather is mild and the flora and fauna are at their most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okayama (Prefecture)

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Be kind and courteous, especially to the elderly.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

  • Litter, as Okayama is known for its respect for nature.

  • Speak too loudly or disturb the peace and tranquility of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Okayama (Prefecture)

Okayama is generally a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, it is still important to take the necessary precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Okayama (Prefecture)

Okayama has good medical facilities, but it is still important to have adequate travel insurance and to take the necessary precautions, such as receiving the proper vaccinations before traveling to Japan.

Getting to Okayama (Prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okayama (Prefecture)

  • Okayama Airport: Located approximately 20 minutes from the city center, this airport offers both domestic and international flights.

  • Hiroshima Airport: Located approximately 90 minutes from Okayama by train, this airport offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Okayama (Prefecture)

  • Okayama Station: A major transportation hub that offers bus routes to various destinations in Okayama and beyond.

Other Options

  • Train: Okayama is well-connected by train, with a Shinkansen and local train lines.

  • Car rental: Renting a car is another option for traveling around Okayama.

How to Get Around in Okayama (Prefecture)

  • Train: Okayama is well-connected by train, making it an easy and convenient way to get around the prefecture.

  • Bus: Buses are also available for traveling around Okayama, and the bus routes are well-connected.

  • Taxi: Taxis are available but can be expensive, so it is recommended to take the train or bus whenever possible.

Where to Go Next from Okayama (Prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hiroshima: Explore the vibrant city and learn about its history and culture, including the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum.

  • Osaka: Known for its food and nightlife, Osaka is a must-visit destination offering an abundance of things to do and see.

  • Kurashiki: Explore the beautiful historical district and the Ohara Museum of Art, one of the oldest private museums of Western art in Japan.

Conclusion

Okayama (Prefecture) may be a hidden gem, but it is definitely worth a visit. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, Okayama has so much to offer any traveler. Whether you want to experience the traditional culture, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply relax and enjoy the beauty of nature, Okayama is the perfect destination. Plan your trip today and discover what makes Okayama (Prefecture) so special.
