Introduction to Okayama

Located in the Chugoku region of Japan, Okayama is a charming city known for its traditional Japanese gardens, historic sites, and cultural landmarks. Often referred to as the “Land of Sunshine,” Okayama has a warm climate, mild winters, and plenty of sunshine throughout the year. Its central location makes it a convenient starting point for exploring nearby cities including Hiroshima and Kobe.

List of Places to Visit in Okayama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Okayama Castle

Okayama Castle, also known as “Crow Castle” due to its black exterior, sits atop a hill overlooking Okayama City. Built in the 16th century, the castle was destroyed during World War II and later rebuilt in 1966. Its impressive design and historical significance make it a popular destination for tourists.

Korakuen Garden

Korakuen Garden is one of the three most beautiful gardens in Japan and is considered a masterpiece of traditional Japanese landscaping. The garden dates back to the 18th century and features a large pond, walking trails, and stunning views of Okayama Castle.

Okayama Prefectural Museum

The Okayama Prefectural Museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the history and culture of Okayama. Highlights include ancient pottery, samurai armor, and traditional artwork.

Natural Attractions of Okayama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tsuyama Castle

Tsuyama Castle is a well-preserved hilltop castle that offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and countryside. The castle was built in the 16th century and is located in the city of Tsuyama, approximately 40 kilometers northeast of Okayama.

Kibi Plain

The Kibi Plain is a scenic area located in the eastern part of Okayama. The area is known for its vast cycling routes, which are lined with beautiful cherry blossom trees in the spring and offer stunning views of the surrounding hills.

Mt. Washu

Mt. Washu is a 1,375-meter mountain located in the northern part of Okayama. The mountain offers hiking opportunities and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okayama

Okayama is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and visitors have plenty of hiking and cycling opportunities to choose from. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Kibi Plain Cycling Route
  • Utazu Seaside Cycling Road
  • Washuzan Hill Mountain Road
  • Mitsuigawa Cycling Road

Best Things to Do in Okayama: Activities and Tours

Okayama Castle Samurai Experience

Visitors can experience what life was like as a samurai during the Edo period (1603-1868) with a guided tour of Okayama Castle. The tour includes dressing up in samurai armor, learning traditional sword-fighting techniques, and experiencing the daily life of a samurai.

Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter

The Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter is a beautifully preserved area of Kurashiki, located approximately 20 kilometers southwest of Okayama. Visitors can explore the quaint streets lined with traditional houses, shops, and restaurants.

Okayama Momotaro Karakuri Museum

The Okayama Momotaro Karakuri Museum is a unique museum featuring mechanical dolls, or karakuri, that perform various plays and acts. Visitors can watch the dolls in action and learn about their history and significance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Okayama

Day 1: Visit Okayama Castle, Korakuen Garden, and Okayama Prefectural Museum.

Day 2: Visit Tsuyama Castle and spend the afternoon cycling on the Kibi Plain.

Day 3: Hike Mt. Washu and take in the stunning views of the countryside.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter.

Day 5: Experience life as a samurai with the Okayama Castle Samurai Experience.

Day 6: Visit the Okayama Momotaro Karakuri Museum and explore the city.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some local food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Okayama

Must-Try Dishes

Okayama-style Barazushi

Barazushi is a type of sushi unique to Okayama Prefecture. It features a variety of ingredients, including vinegared rice, seafood, and vegetables, and is served in a bowl.

Okayama-style Kurumi Pork

Kurumi pork is a type of locally raised pork that is known for its tender and juicy taste. It is often served in traditional Japanese dishes, including hot pots and grilled dishes.

Local Beverages

Okayama Sake

Okayama is known for producing high-quality sake, and there are several breweries in the area that offer tours and tastings. Some popular brands include Dassai and Kamoshibito Kuheiji.

Bizen Beer

Bizen Beer is a local craft beer that is brewed using Bizen’s famous water. The brewery offers tours where visitors can learn about the brewing process and sample the beer.

Where to Stay in Okayama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

ANA Crowne Plaza Okayama

The ANA Crowne Plaza Okayama is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Okayama. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a range of dining options, and easy access to the city’s attractions.

Guest House Kikusui Ryokan

Guest House Kikusui Ryokan is a traditional Japanese guesthouse located in the city of Kurashiki. The guesthouse features tatami rooms, a hot spring bath, and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Okayama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bizen-yaki Pottery

Bizen-yaki Pottery is a traditional style of pottery that has been produced in Okayama for over 1,000 years. The pottery is known for its natural and simple design and is often used in traditional Japanese tea ceremonies.

Okayama Peach

Okayama is known for producing high-quality peaches that are juicy, sweet, and fragrant. Visitors can purchase fresh peaches in local markets and shops throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Okayama

Okayama has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Bowing is the most common form of greeting, and it is considered polite to remove shoes before entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Okayama

The best time to visit Okayama is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. The cherry blossoms bloom in the spring, and the autumn leaves are stunning in the fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okayama

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local food and drinks
  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home

Don’t:

  • Be loud in public places
  • Tip at restaurants and hotels (it is not customary in Japan)
  • Touch someone without permission (personal space is important in Japan)

Safety and Health

Safety in Okayama

Okayama is generally a safe city, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major safety concerns. However, it is always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Okayama

There are no major health concerns in Okayama. Tap water is safe to drink, and hospitals and clinics are widely available throughout the city.

Getting to Okayama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okayama

Okayama Airport is the main airport serving the city, and it is located approximately 20 kilometers northeast of the city center. There are several international and domestic flights available.

Bus Stations Near Okayama

Okayama Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the city, and it offers bus services to other cities in Japan.

Other Options

Okayama is connected to the rest of Japan via the Shinkansen and local train lines, making it easy to travel around the city and explore other parts of Japan.

How to Get Around in Okayama

Okayama is a compact city, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. Cycling is also a popular way to get around, and visitors can rent bicycles from several shops and rental services throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Okayama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hiroshima

Hiroshima is located approximately one hour by train from Okayama and is known for its historic landmarks, including the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and the Hiroshima Castle.

Kobe

Kobe is located approximately two hours by train from Okayama and is known for its stunning harbor views, delicious food, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Okayama is a charming and picturesque city that offers a range of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. From traditional Japanese gardens and historic castles to outdoor adventures and local culinary delights, Okayama is a destination that should not be missed.
