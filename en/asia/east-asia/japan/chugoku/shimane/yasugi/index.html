Introduction to Yasugi

Yasugi is a picturesque city located in Shimane, one of the six prefectures of Japan’s Chugoku region. Known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic natural beauty, and warm hospitality, Yasugi is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Japanese experience.

To make the most of your stay in Yasugi, we have put together a comprehensive travel guide that covers everything from top attractions and activities, to dining and accommodation options, and practical travel tips. Read on to discover why Yasugi should be on your travel bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Yasugi

Yasugi offers a plethora of attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers. From culturally significant landmarks to natural wonders and thrilling outdoor adventures, here are the top places to visit in Yasugi.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Adachi Museum of Art: This renowned museum houses a vast collection of contemporary Japanese art and boasts a stunning garden that has been voted as Japan’s best garden for the past 17 years.
  • Yasugi-bushi Hall: This traditional Japanese dance hall showcases the famous Yasugi-bushi dance, which is believed to have originated in Yasugi during the Edo period.
  • Yakumo Koizumi Memorial Hall: Dedicated to the famous Japanese author Yakumo Koizumi (Lafcadio Hearn), this museum has an extensive collection of his works and personal belongings.

Natural Attractions of Yasugi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Sanbe: This is an active volcano that offers a scenic hiking trail with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Yaegaki Shrine: This Shinto shrine is known for its beautiful garden, which is particularly stunning during cherry blossom season.
  • Hii River: This picturesque river offers a variety of water and outdoor activities, such as kayaking, rafting, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yasugi

Yasugi is a hiker’s paradise, with several scenic trails that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Here are the best hiking/biking routes in Yasugi.

  • Mt. Sanbe Trail: This is a challenging hiking trail that takes you to the summit of Mt. Sanbe for breathtaking panoramic views of the city and the Sea of Japan.
  • Yasugi Cycling Course: This 27-km cycling course takes you through scenic countryside landscapes and past historic landmarks such as the Adachi Museum of Art.

Best Things to Do in Yasugi: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the top attractions, there are plenty of exciting activities and tours to engage in. Here are some of the best things to do in Yasugi.

  • Admire the Cherry Blossom: Yasugi is famous for its stunning cherry blossom displays, with several parks and gardens offering a picturesque setting for hanami (flower viewing) during spring.
  • Try Traditional Arts and Crafts: Yasugi has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its craft traditions, such as sword making, pottery, and paper crafts. Take a workshop to learn more about these art forms.
  • Relax in a Hot Spring: Yasugi is well-known for its hot springs (onsen) with therapeutic properties. Soak in the natural mineral waters and rejuvenate your body and soul.

To make the most of your time in Yasugi, we have put together a week-long itinerary that covers the top attractions and activities in the city and its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Yasugi

Yasugi offers a diverse culinary scene, with seafood being a staple of the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages to indulge in during your trip.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Izumo soba: This is a type of soba noodle that is thicker and heartier compared to traditional soba.
  • Shimane wagyu beef: This high-quality beef is known for its tender texture and rich flavor.
  • Matsuba crab: This seasonal delicacy is a type of snow crab found in the Sea of Japan and is often featured in regional cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Shimane is home to several breweries that produce premium-quality sake, made from locally grown rice.
  • Amazake: This sweet and nutritious drink is made from fermented rice that is rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Doppo Beer: This locally brewed beer is a popular choice among beer lovers for its distinctive flavor and high-quality ingredients.

Where to Stay in Yasugi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yasugi offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in Yasugi.

  • Adachi Museum of Art Hotel: This hotel is adjacent to the museum and offers stunning views of the garden and surrounding landscapes.
  • Tsunagi Guesthouse: This traditional Japanese guesthouse is located in the heart of the city and offers a unique cultural experience.
  • Hotel Harvest In Yasugi: This modern hotel offers luxurious amenities and is conveniently located close to the Adachi Museum of Art.

What to Buy in Yasugi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yasugi is famous for its traditional crafts and locally made products. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home.

  • Japanese swords and knives: Yasugi is known for its sword-making heritage, and visitors can buy high-quality swords and knives made by skilled craftsmen.
  • Pottery and ceramics: Yasugi has a rich history of pottery making, and visitors can buy beautiful handmade ceramics from local artisans.
  • Lacquerware: Yasugi is home to several lacquer craftsmen who create stunning pieces of lacquerware using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Yasugi

Yasugi has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take pride in preserving their traditions and customs. Here are a few customs and etiquettes to keep in mind during your trip.

  • Shoes off indoors: It is customary to take off your shoes before entering a Japanese home or a public building.
  • Bowing: Bowing is a common form of greeting in Japan, and you should return the gesture when someone bows to you.
  • Dining etiquette: It is considered impolite to blow your nose or talk with your mouth full while dining in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Yasugi

The best time to visit Yasugi is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild, and nature is at its best. Cherry blossom season (late March to early April) is a popular time to visit Yasugi, as several parks and gardens offer a picturesque setting for hanami. Fall foliage season (late October to early November) is equally beautiful, with vibrant hues of red, orange, and yellow painting the landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yasugi

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Yasugi.

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Learn a few basic Japanese phrases

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes indoors
  • Touch food with your bare hands
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places

Safety and Health

Yasugi is a safe and welcoming destination, with a very low crime rate. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind.

Safety in Yasugi

  • Always carry a photocopy of your passport and visa, and store the original in a hotel safe.
  • Be cautious when walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.
  • Always use a designated crosswalk when crossing the road and be mindful of cyclists and motorbikes.

Health in Yasugi

  • Japan has a high standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and clinics located in Yasugi.
  • Tap water is safe to drink in Yasugi, but if you prefer bottled water, it is readily available at convenience stores and vending machines.
  • Mosquitoes can be a nuisance during the summer months, so bring insect repellent with you.

Getting to Yasugi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yasugi is easily accessible by air, train, and bus. Here are the different transportation options and logistics to consider.

Airports Near Yasugi

  • Izumo Airport: This is the closest airport to Yasugi, located approximately 30 km away. Regular flights connect the airport to Tokyo, Osaka, and other major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Yasugi

  • Yasugi Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Yasugi, with connections to major cities in Shimane and neighboring prefectures.

Other Options

  • Train: Yasugi is accessible by train, with the JR Sanin Main Line running through the city. The journey from Tokyo to Yasugi takes approximately 8-9 hours.
  • Car: Yasugi is easily accessible by car, with several car rental services available in the city and nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Yasugi

Yasugi is a compact city that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. There are also several public transportation options available, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Yasugi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yasugi is located in the heart of Shimane, which is known for its stunning natural beauty and cultural landmarks. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider.

  • Izumo Taisha Shrine: This is one of Japan’s oldest and most important shrines, located approximately 50 km from Yasugi.
  • Matsue Castle: Also known as the “black castle,” this historic fortress is located approximately 25 km from Yasugi.
  • Oki Islands: This group of islands located off the coast of Shimane is known for its natural beauty and unique cultural traditions.

Conclusion

Yasugi is a hidden gem in Japan, offering a perfect blend of culture, nature, and adventure. With our comprehensive travel guide, you are now armed with all the information to plan your perfect trip to Yasugi. Pack your bags and get ready to explore the best of Shimane’s hidden gem.
