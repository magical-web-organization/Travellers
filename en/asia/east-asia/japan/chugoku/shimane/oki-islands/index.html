Introduction to Oki Islands

Located in the Sea of Japan, the Oki Islands are a hidden gem that offer the perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. Consisting of four main islands and numerous smaller ones, Oki Islands are an ideal destination for those seeking an off-the-beaten-path escape.

List of Places to Visit in Oki Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oki Islands UNESCO Global Geopark: A unique site that takes visitors on a journey through time, showcasing the island’s geological and cultural heritage.
  • Saigoku Pilgrimage: The Oki Islands’ version of the famous 33-temple Kannon pilgrimage.
  • Kuniga Coast: A scenic coastline that highlights the rock formations that make the Oki Islands unique.

Natural Attractions of Oki Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dangyo Waterfall: A stunning waterfall located in the heart of the Oki Islands, surrounded by lush greenery and serene landscapes.
  • Mt. Daimanji: A picturesque mountain hike that leads you to jaw-dropping panoramic views of the archipelago.
  • Nishinoshima Island: A pristine paradise that boasts one of the most beautiful beaches in Japan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oki Islands

Oki Islands offer incredible biking and hiking trails that take you through breathtaking vistas and hidden gems, such as:

  • The Oki Nature Trail
  • The Kuniga Coast Hiking Trail
  • The Momojima Island Cycling Course

Best Things to Do in Oki Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking: A unique way to explore the seas surrounding the islands, witness the rich marine life, and discover remote bays and hidden coves.
  • Oki Seal Encounter: One of the best places to encounter and learn about the majestic seals that inhabit the sea surrounding the islands.
  • Scuba Diving: The Oki Islands offer excellent diving sites that showcase the diverse underwater world of Japan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oki Islands

Day 1-2: Explore the cultural attractions and historical sites of the islands, including the Oki Islands UNESCO Global Geopark and Saigoku Pilgrimage.

Day 3-4: Experience the scenic views and outdoor adventures of Oki Islands, from hiking and biking to kayaking and diving.

Day 5-6: Relax on the pristine beaches of Nishinoshima Island and enjoy the local cuisine and culture.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with souvenir shopping and a farewell meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Oki Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oki Sushi: A local dish that features fresh and tasty seafood, wrapped in vinegared rice, and served with miso soup.
  • Kiritanpo Nabe: A hotpot dish made with chicken and a unique Oki Island specialty, kiritanpo, which is grilled rice cake.
  • Red Squid: A delicacy in Oki Islands, this squid variety is caught fresh and served in different ways.

Local Beverages

  • Oki Sake: A rice wine made from locally grown rice, Oki Sake is a must-try for every visitor to the islands.
  • Oki Craft Beer: A refreshing way to beat the heat, the Oki Craft Beer is made locally and with unique ingredients.

Where to Stay in Oki Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Oki Grand Hotel: A luxurious hotel with breathtaking views of the Sea of Japan and a range of amenities for a comfortable stay.
  • Oki Seaside Hotel: A beachfront hotel with spacious rooms, excellent views, and a range of activities.
  • Oki Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option that offers a cozy and authentic experience of local hospitality.

What to Buy in Oki Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oki Tori-shio-ko: A special seasoning made from local chicken stock and salt that is used to flavor many Oki Islands dishes.
  • Ryukyu Glass Crafts: Unique, colorful glasswares that are handmade by local artisans using traditional methods.
  • Oki Beef: A tender, juicy, and high-quality variety of beef, Oki Beef is a must-try for meat lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Oki Islands

The locals in Oki Islands have a unique culture and customs that are worth exploring, such as:

  • The Oki Islands’ traditional dance and music, called Dojima Kagura, which highlights the island’s cultural richness.
  • The Ryukyu Culture that blends elements of Chinese, Japanese, and Okinawan culture and can be seen in traditional crafts and dances.

Best Time to Visit Oki Islands

The best time to visit Oki Islands is from late spring to early summer (May-June) when the weather is pleasant, and the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. The fall season (September-November) is also an ideal time to visit, with mild temperatures, colorful foliage, and a range of local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oki Islands

Do:

  • Keep the island clean and respect nature.
  • Try the local cuisine and learn about the culture and history of the islands.
  • Follow safety procedures when engaging in outdoor activities and water sports.

Don’t:

  • Disturb nature or harm any wildlife.
  • Engage in activities that are harmful to the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oki Islands

Overall, Oki Islands is a safe place to visit with low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take safety measures when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Oki Islands

Basic medical facilities are available in Oki Islands, but visitors with specific health concerns should bring the necessary medication and consult their physician before traveling.

Getting to Oki Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oki Islands

  • Izumo Airport: The nearest airport to Oki Islands, located in Izumo city, Shimane Prefecture.

Bus Stations Near Oki Islands

  • Matsue Bus Station: Buses depart from Matsue to Sakaiminato Port, where you can catch a ferry to Oki Islands.

Other Options

  • Ferry from Sakaiminato Port: One can take the ferry from Sakaiminato Port to Oki Islands, with the journey ranging from 3-5 hours.

How to Get Around in Oki Islands

The best way to get around Oki Islands is by car, rented bikes or public transportation, such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Oki Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Izumo: Located on the mainland, Izumo is famous for its 8th century Shinto Shrine, Izumo Taisha.
  • Matsue: Known for its samurai history, Matsue is a quaint city that offers a range of cultural attractions and natural sights.
  • Hiroshima: Known as the “City of Peace”, Hiroshima is a must-visit destination for history buffs and those interested in World War II history.

Conclusion

Oki Islands offer a unique cultural and natural experience, where visitors can discover the rich history and unique traditions of Japan’s hidden gem. From breathtaking landscapes to authentic cuisine and hospitality, there’s something for everyone in these picturesque islands.
