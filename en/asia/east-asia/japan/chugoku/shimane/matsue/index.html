Introduction to Matsue

Nestled in the beautiful region of Chugoku, Japan, Matsue is a city that is steeped in history and culture. The city boasts of numerous historically important sites, beautiful gardens, and natural landscapes that will leave you mesmerized. Whether you are a history buff, nature enthusiast, or a simple traveler looking for novel experiences, Matsue is the perfect destination for you. Let’s take you on an exciting journey of discovering this fascinating city.

List of Places to Visit in Matsue

Matsue is home to a plethora of attractions that are sure to impress any visitor. Some of the must-visit places include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Matsue Castle, one of the twelve surviving feudal castles in Japan
  • Adachi Museum of Art, a famous garden museum with stunning views and traditional Japanese arts exhibitions
  • Lafcadio Hearn Residence, a museum dedicated to the life and works of Lafcadio Hearn, a famous author and scholar of Japan
  • Shimane Art Museum, a contemporary art museum in a serene forested location
  • Matsue History Museum, displaying local history, culture and archaeology

Natural Attractions of Matsue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Shinji, a picturesque lake with surrounding marshland and parks
  • Mount Daisen, a popular hiking spot with scenic views
  • Matsue Vogel Park, a bird park with exotic and local species
  • Numazu-Kaneyama Castle Remains Park, a tranquil Japanese garden with castle ruins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matsue

Matsue is a hotspot for outdoor enthusiasts. From scenic hikes to breathtaking bicycle trails, Matsue has plenty to offer. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Matsue Castle Town Course, a cycling route through Matsue city to Matsue Castle
  • Mount Daisen Asahi Course, a hiking trail that leads to the highest peak in Chugoku, Mt. Daisen
  • Gozenzawa Hiking Trail, a scenic hiking trail through the mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Matsue: Activities and Tours

Matsue is a city that is full of exciting activities and tours, such as:

  • Boat Tours on the Horikawa River, for an up-close experience of the city’s picturesque scenery
  • Kimono rental and walking tour, for a unique experience of Japanese culture
  • Visit the Horan-enya Memorial Hall, a facility that hosts traditional Japanese tea ceremonies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matsue

To ensure you have the best experience possible in Matsue, we have created a week-long itinerary for you to follow:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Arrive in Matsue, explore the city, and visit Matsue Castle
Day 2 Hike Mount Daisen, one of the best scenic spots in Matsue
Day 3 Visit Adachi Museum of Art and Lafcadio Hearn Residence
Day 4 Take a boat tour on the Horikawa River and explore the city's nature
Day 5 Visit Matsue History Museum, Shimane Art Museum, and Lake Shinji
Day 6 Try on a kimono and take a walking tour, end the day with an evening stargazing tour

Food and Drink Scene of Matsue

Matsue has a rich culinary tradition that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Some of the local dishes and beverages that you must try while you’re in Matsue are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Izumo soba, a traditional buckwheat noodle dish that is famous for its flavor
  • Horan-enya, a local specialty that is a mix of egg and sugar paste, rolled in the leaves of the yamaimo mountain potato.
  • Shimane Wagyu, a high-quality beef sourced from the Shimane Prefecture

Local Beverages

  • Matsue Craft Beer, with multiple breweries offering a wide range of flavors
  • Sake, with multiple breweries in the region offering traditional Japanese sake.

Where to Stay in Matsue: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matsue has plenty of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Matsue Urban Hotel, a mid-range hotel located in the heart of the city
  • Tamatsukuri Grand Hotel Choseikaku, a luxury ryokan located in a peaceful area in the city
  • Zen Hostel Japan, a hostel located near Matsue Castle, offering budget-friendly options with clean amenities

What to Buy in Matsue: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matsue is known for its unique souvenirs and local products that make for excellent gifts. Some of the best buys include:

  • Matsue Castle inspired souvenirs, including keychains, magnets, and postcards.
  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics from Izumo City
  • Matsue Wagyu beef, available at local markets

Local Culture and Customs of Matsue

Matsue has a rich cultural heritage that is evident in its many festivals and traditions. Some of the most notable include:

  • The Matsue Castle Festival, held annually in May, showcases traditional Japanese entertainment and arts.
  • The Shimane Tea Ceremony Festival, held in August or September, is a must-see event for tea lovers.
  • The Tatara Festival, held in October, is a celebration of Matsue’s ironmaking history.

Best Time to Visit Matsue

The best time to visit Matsue is from March to May when the cherry blossom trees are in full bloom, or from September to November when the autumn leaves are changing, creating stunning scenery. During winters, Matsue Castle is illuminated, and the winter landscape is a magical sight to behold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matsue

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks, from Izumo soba to Matsue Wagyu beef
  • Respect the local culture and customs, for an enjoyable experience
  • Take part in Festivals and cultural events

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas not intended for smoking
  • Litter in public areas
  • Ignore signage and instructions at tourist sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Matsue

Matsue is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, it is advisable to take normal precautions such as not leaving valuables lying around and being aware of your surroundings while exploring.

Health in Matsue

Medical facilities are well equipped, and pharmacies are readily available within the city. Remember to carry any prescriptions with you.

Getting to Matsue: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matsue is well-connected to other parts of Japan, with various transport options available.

Airports Near Matsue

  • Izumo Airport is the closest airport to Matsue, with direct flights from Tokyo and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Matsue

  • Matsue Bus Terminal serves as the main bus hub for the city and regional buses.

Other Options

  • Matsue is accessible by train on the San’in Main Line that runs from Kyoto in the east and Tottori in the west.
  • Local taxis are widely available for hire throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Matsue

Matsue is an easy city to navigate around. The public transport system is efficient, and most attractions can be reached on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Matsue: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Matsue is an excellent starting point for exploring the Chugoku region of Japan. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Izumo, famous for its grand shrine and close proximity to sand dunes
  • Tottori, known for its sand dunes and stone kyoekigama
  • Hiroshima, with many iconic World War II and atomic bomb memorials to visit.

Conclusion

Matsue is a city that offers travelers an authentic and culturally rich experience. With its fascinating history, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Matsue is an ideal destination for anyone looking for unique and enriching travel experiences. Plan your trip to Matsue with our comprehensive travel guide and discover this hidden gem for yourself.
