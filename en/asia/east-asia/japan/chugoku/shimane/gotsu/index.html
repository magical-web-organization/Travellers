Introduction to Gotsu

Located in Shimane prefecture in Japan, Gotsu is a small town rich in culture and natural beauty. This hidden gem has gained popularity in recent years for its well-preserved historical landmarks, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious local cuisine.

With its tranquil atmosphere and stunning surroundings, Gotsu provides a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of Japan’s city life. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best things to do, the must-visit places, the food and drink scene, local culture and customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Gotsu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gotsu Castle Ruins: A must-see for history enthusiasts, these ruins offer a glimpse into Gotsu’s past as a castle town. The ruins are located on a hill overlooking the town and offer stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Gotsu City Museum: This museum offers insight into Gotsu’s history, culture, and traditions. From exhibits on local handicrafts to ancient artifacts and samurai swords, there is something for every visitor here.

  • Oda Nobunaga House: Oda Nobunaga was a famous Japanese warlord who had connections to Gotsu. His former house has been preserved as a historical landmark and now serves as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Gotsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Gotsu hiking trail: The hiking trail offers a scenic route through Gotsu’s picturesque mountains, with breathtaking views of the town and the sea below.

  • Lake Shinji: Lake Shinji provides a tranquil atmosphere and is a great place for a leisurely stroll. Visitors can enjoy the view of the lake from the boardwalk or take a boat ride to get a closer look at the water and the wildlife.

  • Souma Dam: Souma Dam is a beautiful man-made lake with a serene atmosphere, offering visitors the perfect spot for swimming or enjoying a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gotsu

Gotsu is a perfect destination for hikers and bikers, with its numerous hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Among the most popular trails are the Mt. Gotsu hiking trail, the Mt. Sanbe National Park trail, and the Shimane mountain range trail.

Best Things to Do in Gotsu: Activities and Tours

  • Gotsu Sake Brewery Tour: Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice. The Gotsu Sake Brewery is renowned for its high-quality sake and offers visitors a tour of their facilities, including tasting sessions.

  • Traditional Japanese Crafts Workshops: Gotsu is well-known for its traditional handicrafts such as pottery, weaving, and dyeing. Visitors can experience these unique crafts firsthand at various workshops in the town.

  • Gotsu Hot Springs: Gotsu has several natural hot springs, which offer the perfect opportunity for relaxation and rejuvenation. Among the most popular hot springs are Tamatsukuri Onsen and Yunotsu Onsen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gotsu

Day 1: Explore the Gotsu Castle Ruins and Gotsu City Museum

Day 2: Hike the Mt. Gotsu trail in the morning, have lunch at a local restaurant, and take a relaxing boat ride on Lake Shinji in the afternoon.

Day 3: Visit the Oda Nobunaga House in the morning and enjoy a traditional Japanese lunch. Spend the afternoon exploring the traditional handicrafts workshops.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Izumo Shrine, one of Japan’s most famous shrines, and experience the local culture and traditions.

Day 5-7: Spend the rest of the week relaxing and rejuvenating at a local hot spring, such as Tamatsukuri Onsen or Yunotsu Onsen.

Food and Drink Scene of Gotsu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Izumo Soba: This is a type of buckwheat noodle that is a specialty of the region. It is served cold or hot, and is often accompanied by various toppings such as green onions and grated wasabi.

  • Gotsu Beef: Gotsu beef is a high-quality beef that is raised in the area. It is served in various dishes such as steak, beef bowl, and sukiyaki.

  • Seafood: Gotsu is located near the sea, so fresh seafood is abundant. Popular seafood dishes include sashimi, grilled fish, and seafood hotpot.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Sake is a traditional Japanese rice wine that is made in various regions of Japan. The Gotsu Sake Brewery produces high-quality sake that is renowned for its smoothness and rich flavor.

  • Green Tea: Shimane prefecture is known for its high-quality green tea. Visitors can enjoy various types of green tea at local cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Gotsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gotsu has various accommodations that cater to different budgets and preferences. Among the most popular options are:

  • Ryokans (Traditional Japanese Inns): Ryokans offer a unique experience, with traditional Japanese-style rooms, Japanese cuisine, and hot springs.

  • Hotels: Gotsu has various hotels that cater to different budgets and preferences, ranging from luxury hotels to budget ones.

  • Hostels: Hostels offer affordable accommodations for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Gotsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Gotsu is famous for its pottery, with various shops and workshops selling unique and beautiful pottery pieces.

  • Handicrafts and Weaving: Gotsu is also famous for its traditional handicrafts and weaving, with local shops selling unique and high-quality handicrafts and fabrics.

  • Green Tea and Sake: Visitors can bring home high-quality green tea and sake from the local shops and supermarkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Gotsu

Gotsu has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs that are worth exploring. Among the most notable customs are:

  • Festivals: Gotsu has various festivals throughout the year, such as the Gotsu Tenjin Matsuri, which is a traditional Japanese festival featuring parades, fireworks, and street food.

  • Tea Ceremony: Shimane prefecture is known for its tea ceremony, a traditional Japanese art of preparing and serving green tea.

  • Onsen Etiquette: When visiting a hot spring, visitors are expected to follow certain etiquette, such as taking a shower before entering the hot spring, covering tattoos, and not bringing food or drinks.

Best Time to Visit Gotsu

The best time to visit Gotsu is from late spring to early autumn (April to October), when the weather is mild and pleasant. Autumn is especially beautiful, with the trees changing colors and offering breathtaking views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gotsu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Take off your shoes when entering a traditional Japanese house or building.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese houses or buildings.

  • Eat or drink while walking on the street.

  • Talk loudly or make noise in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gotsu

Gotsu is a safe town for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Gotsu

Gotsu has various medical facilities such as clinics and hospitals. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance before visiting and to bring any necessary medications.

Getting to Gotsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gotsu

  • Izumo Airport: Izumo Airport is the nearest airport to Gotsu, located about 70 km away. It has regular domestic flights to major cities in Japan.

  • Hiroshima Airport: Hiroshima Airport is located about 200km away from Gotsu and has regular international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Gotsu

  • Gotsu Bus Station: Gotsu Bus Station is located in the town center and has regular bus services to nearby towns and cities.

  • Hiroshima Bus Station: Hiroshima Bus Station is located in the city center and has regular bus services to major cities in Japan.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Gotsu is Gotsu Station, which is located on the San’in Main Line. The station has regular trains to major cities in the region.

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airports or train stations to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Gotsu

Gotsu is a small town that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Buses and taxis are also available for visitors who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Gotsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Izumo Shrine: Izumo Shrine is one of Japan’s most famous shrines and is located just 30 km from Gotsu.

  • Matsue castle: Matsue castle is a beautiful castle that is located about 40 km from Gotsu and is renowned for its stunning architecture and cherry blossoms.

  • Matsue City: Matsue City is the capital of Shimane prefecture and is located about 50 km from Gotsu. It has various attractions such as historical landmarks, beautiful gardens, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

With its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Gotsu is a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the authentic side of Japan. Whether you are interested in historical landmarks or outdoor adventures, Gotsu has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the wonders of Gotsu!
