Introduction to Shunan

Shunan, located in Yamaguchi Prefecture in the Chugoku region, is a hidden gem that offers unique cultural experiences, breathtaking natural landscapes, and fantastic food and drink. Despite its relatively unknown status, Shunan is a destination that is worth venturing to for those looking for an authentic Japanese experience.

List of Places to Visit in Shunan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hagi Uragami Museum

Located in Hagi, a town neighboring Shunan, Hagi Uragami Museum gives visitors an insight into the history and culture of the local region. The museum features a vast range of artifacts, including ceramics, pottery, and traditional handicrafts that reflect local life in years gone by.

Kozan Castle Ruins

Perched atop a hill and surrounded by beautiful forested scenery, Kozan Castle Ruins is a historical site that was first built in the 14th century. Visitors can stroll the castle grounds and imagine the castle as it once was, while taking in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and hills.

Natural Attractions of Shunan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ruriko Temple

Nestled in the heart of the city, Ruriko Temple is a beautiful historic structure that is known for its three-story pagoda, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding cityscape. Visitors can wander through the temple complex and gardens, taking in the tranquil atmosphere and natural beauty of the area.

Hikinoyama Conservation Area

Hikinoyama Conservation Area is a great destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. The area offers trails for both novice and experienced hikers, allowing visitors to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shunan

Mt. Shunan

Mt. Shunan, located outside the city and surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, offers visitors an excellent hiking experience with incredible views of the surrounding area. The climb requires moderate physical fitness, but it is well worth it for the breathtaking 360-degree views from the summit.

Best Things to Do in Shunan: Activities and Tours

Sake Brewery Tours

Shunan is famous for its sake, and a visit to one of the many breweries in the area is a must-do experience. These tours offer visitors the opportunity to learn about the history and process of sake-making and, of course, sample some of the delicious sake produced in the region.

Cycling tours

Shunan is also a great destination for cycling enthusiasts, with several companies offering guided tours around the region. Visitors can explore the beautiful countryside and stop off at local attractions along the way, all while getting some exercise and fresh air.

Food and Drink Scene of Shunan

Must-Try Dishes

Kawara Soba

Kawara Soba is a traditional local dish that consists of soba noodles cooked on a hot stone. The dish is typically accompanied by a variety of condiments, such as freshly grated wasabi and negi (Japanese green onion), that add a spicy kick.

Anago Meshi

Anago Meshi is a Japanese-style rice bowl that features tender eel, which is slow-cooked with a sweet and savory sauce. The dish is a true delicacy and is typically enjoyed as a lunchtime treat.

Local Beverages

Shunan Sake

Shunan sake is one of the finest in Japan, and a visit to one of the many local breweries is a must-do experience. Visitors can sample various types of sake and learn about the traditional sake-making process.

Where to Stay in Shunan: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

Shunan City Hotel

Located in the heart of the city, the Shunan City Hotel offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price. The hotel has an on-site restaurant and is conveniently located near many local attractions.

Zekkei no Yakata Miyataya Ryokan

For a more traditional stay in Shunan, Zekkei no Yakata Miyataya Ryokan is a great option. The ryokan features beautifully decorated Japanese-style rooms, an onsen (hot spring), and traditional cuisine.

What to Buy in Shunan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Shunan should not leave before stocking up on the many local products and souvenirs available in the area. Shunan is known for its pottery, sake, and traditional handicrafts, all of which make for great gifts and mementos.

Local Culture and Customs of Shunan

Visitors to Shunan should be aware of the local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes when entering homes and temples. Visitors should also be respectful of local culture and be mindful of waste management practices, as the area is committed to sustainable tourism.

Best Time to Visit Shunan

The best time to visit Shunan is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the landscapes are beautiful. Visitors should be mindful of the rainy season, which occurs in June and July.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shunan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Clean up after yourself, and dispose of waste responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside homes and temples.
  • Litter or contribute to unsustainable tourism practices.
  • Express overly loud or aggressive behavior in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shunan

Shunan is a safe area, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should be mindful of their belongings and avoid leaving valuables unattended in public places.

Health in Shunan

Medical facilities in Shunan are generally good, but visitors should purchase health insurance before traveling to the area. Visitors should also be mindful of food safety, ensuring they eat only from reputable vendors.

Getting to Shunan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shunan

Yamaguchi Ube Airport is the closest airport to Shunan, located about 45 minutes away by train.

Bus Stations Near Shunan

Several bus companies run services from the main cities in the area to Shunan.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Hikari or Shunan Station, both of which are located in the city and offer convenient access to local attractions.

How to Get Around in Shunan

Despite being a small town, Shunan is well-connected and easy to navigate. Train and bus services are reliable and provide access to most local attractions. Taxis are also readily available, but visitors should expect to pay more for this service.

Where to Go Next from Shunan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hagi

Hagi is a nearby town known for its historic sites, such as Hagi Castle and Shoka Sonjuku, as well as its high-quality pottery.

Yamaguchi

Yamaguchi is a bustling city that is home to several temples, museums, and parks. Visitors can also take day trips to nearby natural attractions, such as Akiyoshidai Quasi-National Park and the Tsunoshima Lighthouse.

Conclusion

Shunan may be a lesser-known destination, but this charming town in the heart of Japan’s Chugoku region offers a unique travel experience for those willing to venture out of the usual tourist hotspots. From stunning natural scenery to delicious local dishes, Shunan is a destination that promises a memorable trip.
