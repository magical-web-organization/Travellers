Introduction to Ube

Nestled in the Yamaguchi prefecture of Japan, Ube is a beautiful and charming city that is gradually gaining popularity among tourists. Its rich culture, natural beauty and impressive urban setting make Ube a perfect destination for explorers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Ube

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the things that you must do when you’re in Ube is to visit its top attractions, which include museums and historical sites. Some of the must-see cultural attractions include:

  • Tokiwakai Memorial Museum: This museum houses a collection of ancient relics and artifacts that speak to the city’s past. It also contains several indoor and outdoor exhibits that are both educational and entertaining.
  • Kotozaki Hachiman-gu Shrine: This shrine is one of the most popular and revered sites in Ube. It offers visitors a chance to explore the city’s rich spiritual, cultural and architectural heritage.
  • Ube City Folk Museum: This museum offers an immersive experience of Ube’s cultural legacy. It contains a vast collection of local artifacts, ranging from traditional attire, pottery, paintings, and folk crafts.

Natural Attractions of Ube: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for more outdoor adventure, there are a variety of options to explore in Ube. The following natural attractions are worth your time:

  • Kikugawa Wetland: The Kikugawa Wetland is a beautiful and pristine natural site that offers visitors a rare and unique experience of Ube’s diverse wildlife. The wetland is home to several animal and plant species, making it a naturalist’s paradise.
  • Izumi no Mori: This forested area is an ideal location to enjoy some tranquility and enjoy nature. Visitors can take long walks and hikes and appreciate the breathtaking scenery and lush foliage.
  • Mt. Atago: If you are up to a challenging but rewarding hiking experience, then head over to Mount Atago, which is the highest peak in the Chugoku region. The peak offers visitors stunning panoramic views of the city of Ube and is a must-visit spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ube

Ube city has a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to people of all levels of experience. The following are some of the most popular routes:

  • Ube-Shiroyama Course: This trail covers around eight kilometers and takes hikers past the famous Shiroyama Castle and some of the city’s other historic landmarks. Head up to the castle to enjoy panoramic views of the city of Ube.
  • Jifukuji Temple Area Hiking Trail: This trail takes visitors on a scenic journey through the Jifukuji Temple area. It is a tranquil and peaceful environment away from the hustle and bustle of city life and great for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Kikushi-Hachiman Hiking Trail: This trail is a more advanced option, taking visitors through some challenging terrains but offering striking views of the city’s serene landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Ube: Activities, and Tours

Ube offers visitors a range of activities to try, and tours to participate in. Some of the best activities and tours include;

  • City Sightseeing Tours: Participating in city tours is a great way to get a comprehensive understanding of the city’s culture, history and get to see its top attractions. The tour may include visiting top tourist sites, sampling local food and drinks, shopping for souvenirs, and more.
  • Craft Workshops: Ube is known for its excellent traditional handicrafts. Visitors can enroll in workshops and learn unique skills like pottery making, weaving, and dyeing with natural pigments.
  • Ube Tsuruhashi Festival: If you are visiting Ube in August, you must take part in one of the most popular local festivals- the Ube Tsuruhashi Festival. The festival features decorated floats, parades, traditional Japanese music, and fireworks.

Food and Drink Scene of Ube

Must-Try Dishes

Ube’s food scene is a unique blend of traditional Japanese cuisine and local specialties. The following are must-try dishes when you’re in Ube:

  • Fugu Nabe: This is a local delicacy made from pufferfish served in a single hotpot with vegetables, tofu, and mushroom.
  • Nukazuke: Nukazuke is a fermented vegetable dish that is both delicious and healthy. It’s made from soaking vegetables in fermented rice bran and served alongside rice or as a side dish.
  • Kendama Soba: This dish comprises handcrafted soba noodles served with a side of sesame on top, providing a great mix of textures and flavors.

Local Beverages

Ube has a thriving drinking scene with a collection of local beverages worth a try. Some of the popular beverages include:

  • Hashiramatsu Sake: This locally produced sake has a robust and sweet flavor profile that locals adore.
  • Hagi Cider: Hagi cider is a refreshing and fruity drink that can help quench your thirst during the hot summer days in Ube.

What to Buy in Ube: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ube has an impressive collection of local products and craftwork that visitors can purchase as souvenirs. Some of the popular items include:

  • Cherry Blossom Pins: These ornamental accessories are delicately crafted to resemble the city’s symbol, cherry blossom flowers.
  • Ube Indigo Dyeing: Indigo dyeing is a traditional handicraft that locals have been practicing for generations. Visitors can purchase items made from indigo-dyed fabric, like coasters, bandanas, and t-shirts.
  • Sake: Visitors can purchase a bottle or two of locally distilled sake as a souvenir or a gift for loved ones back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ube

Ube is a friendly and warm city that values its sense of community and hospitality. It’s customary to bow and say ‘arigatou gozaimasu’ or thank you to show appreciation for assistance. Remove shoes and bow when entering homes, and always ask for permission to photograph locals or anything of significance.

Best Time to Visit Ube

The best time to explore Ube is between April and November, during the mild weather when visitors can explore the city’s top attractions without any issues. If you plan to attend major festivals or events, it’s essential to consult the official schedules beforehand.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ube

Do:

  • Learn some basic Japanese words and phrases, including greetings, numbers, and gestures.
  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Explore public transport options like the buses and trains to move around the city.

Don’t:

  • Carry prohibited substances like drugs or arms.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Disrespect locals or their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ube

Ube has a reputation for being safe for both locals and visitors. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions, like avoiding dark or secluded alleys at night and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Ube

Ube is a relatively healthy city, but visitors should still take basic precautions like staying hydrated and wearing sunscreen, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Ube: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ube

If you are coming from abroad, the easiest way to get to Ube is by flying. The nearest airport is Yamaguchi Ube Airport, which offers domestic flights from major cities within Japan.

Bus Stations Near Ube

Alternatively, visitors can opt for bus transportation, with the nearest bus station being Ube Bus Center. From there, visitors can catch a bus to their preferred destination within Ube.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Ube from nearby cities like Hiroshima or Yamaguchi.

How to Get Around in Ube

Ube is a relatively small city, and getting around is easy. Visitors can choose from several modes of transportation, including buses, trains, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Ube: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of the Yamaguchi prefecture, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to add to your list.

  • Yamaguchi: This prefecture capital is famous for its historical sites, vibrant culture, and impressive landscapes.
  • Hagi: Hagi is a charming port town with a vibrant culture and rich heritage.
  • Miyajima Island: Explore the tiny island’s untouched nature, visit the ancient Itsukushima Shrine, and sample delicious local cuisine.

Conclusion

Ube is a hidden gem in Japan that is well worth a visit. From its natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and impressive food and drink scene, there’s plenty to do for visitors of all ages and interests. Make sure to add Ube to your bucket list when planning your next trip to Japan.
