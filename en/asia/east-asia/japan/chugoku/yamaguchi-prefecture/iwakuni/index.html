Introduction to Iwakuni

Nestled in the southern part of Japan’s Honshu Island, Iwakuni is a quaint little city that offers a perfect blend of traditional and modern Japan. With a vibrant culture, rich history, stunning landscapes, and delicious cuisine, Iwakuni is a must-visit destination for every traveler exploring Japan. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or just looking for a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of the city, Iwakuni has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Iwakuni

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kintaikyo Bridge - Built in the 17th century, this iconic bridge over the Nishiki River is one of the most picturesque spots in Iwakuni. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll across the bridge while enjoying stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Iwakuni Castle - Situated on top of a hill, Iwakuni Castle is a famous historical landmark that offers breathtaking views of the city. The castle’s grounds provide an excellent opportunity to learn about the history of Iwakuni.
  • Iwakuni Art Museum - This modern museum showcases the works of local and international artists in a beautiful, contemporary space.

Natural Attractions of Iwakuni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Shiroyama - This lush nature park offers visitors numerous hiking trails and scenic overlooks, as well as a tranquil zen garden and a Shinto shrine.
  • Kikko Park - Home to cherry blossom trees, Kikko Park is a popular spot for picnics, jogging or just strolling around. The nearby Kintai Bridge offers incredible views of the river and the park.
  • Momijidani Park - One of the best places to see the autumn foliage in Japan, Momijidani Park is a beautiful place to stroll around and enjoy the natural beauty of Iwakuni.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iwakuni

Iwakuni boasts of numerous hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the city and its environs. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Iwakuni Sanroku Nature Trail - This 4-km trail leads to the top of Mount Shiroyama and offers stunning views of the city and its surrounding mountains.
  • Kintaikyo Bridge Cycling Road - This cycling route takes you across the iconic Kintai Bridge, along the Nishiki River and through scenic countryside roads.

Best Things to Do in Iwakuni: Activities and Tours

  • Cycling Tour of Iwakuni - Explore the city’s hidden gems and countryside on a cycling tour.
  • Kayaking on the Nishiki River - Kayak down the gentle rapids of the Nishiki River and take in the beauty of the surrounding nature.
  • Iwakuni Sake Brewery Tour - Go on a sake brewery tour to learn about the local brewing process and sample some delicious sake.
  • Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony - Experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony in a serene garden setting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iwakuni

Food and Drink Scene of Iwakuni

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iwakuni Sushi - A local style of sushi, made with mackerel, tuna, or eel, served with a sweet, vinegary flavor.
  • Hagi Beef - Locally raised beef served with a side of delicious sauces, a must-try dish for any meat lover.
  • Kawara Soba - A variation on traditional soba noodles, these noodles are served on heated roof tiles, and are said to have a distinctive, smoky flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sake - Iwakuni has a long history of sake brewing, and visitors can enjoy a variety of different types ranging from dry to sweet.
  • Shochu - A distilled liquor made from rice or barley, frequently served with ice or mixed with hot water in the winter.
  • Green tea - Known for its delicate flavor and health benefits, green tea is a popular beverage in Iwakuni.

Where to Stay in Iwakuni: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Kikko Park Hotel Iwakuni - This hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms with all modern amenities.
  • Iwakuni Kokusai Kanko Hotel - A great choice for budget travelers, this hotel is centrally located and has simple, comfortable rooms.
  • Miyajima Guest House Mikuniya - This traditional guesthouse is just a short ferry ride from Iwakuni, and is an excellent choice for travelers looking for a more authentic, off-beat experience.

What to Buy in Iwakuni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Iwakuni Sake - A perfect souvenir for anyone who loves sake.
  • Iwakuni Bento Boxes - Beautifully designed lunch boxes, perfect for packing a picnic or enjoying a meal on the go.
  • Iwakuni Kokeshi Dolls - These charming wooden dolls are a popular Japanese souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Iwakuni

Iwakuni has a rich cultural heritage and a few customs and traditions that every visitor should be aware of:

  • Respect for elders and authority figures is highly valued.
  • Removing shoes is customary at most homes and traditional establishments.
  • Be mindful of the traditional dress code when visiting religious sites.
  • Tipping is not customary in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Iwakuni

The best time to visit Iwakuni is from March to May during the spring and September to November during the fall. During these seasons, the weather is mild, and the natural beauty of the city is breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iwakuni

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or traditional establishment.
  • Carry a face mask when visiting during flu season.
  • Bow when meeting someone, it is a sign of respect.
  • Carry a small towel when dining out since Japanese dining can be a messy experience.

Don’t:

  • Waste food, it’s considered disrespectful.
  • Ignore the rules and customs when visiting religious shrines and temples.
  • Be loud in public areas, as it is considered impolite.
  • Tip at restaurants, as it is not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iwakuni

Iwakuni is generally a safe city for tourists. However, visitors should remain cautious and avoid unlit areas and dark alleys, particularly during nighttime.

Health in Iwakuni

The tap water in Iwakuni is safe to drink, and there are no major health concerns for visitors to the city.

Getting to Iwakuni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iwakuni

  • Hiroshima Airport - The nearest airport to Iwakuni, located approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Iwakuni

  • Iwakuni Bus Station - Located in the city center and provides bus transportation to other areas in the region.

Other Options

  • Train - Visitors can take the Sanyo Shinkansen from Hiroshima to Iwakuni Station.
  • Car Rental - Rental cars are available at Hiroshima Airport for visitors who wish to drive their own vehicle.

How to Get Around in Iwakuni

  • Bus - An affordable and convenient way to get around the city.
  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available in Iwakuni, but be sure to have the appropriate address written down in Japanese.
  • Bicycle Rental - Bicycles can be rented at multiple locations throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Iwakuni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hiroshima - A short train ride away, Hiroshima is home to the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and numerous other historical sites.
  • Miyajima - Just a short ferry ride away from Iwakuni, Miyajima is a small, picturesque island, known for its floating Torii gate.

Conclusion

Iwakuni is much more than just a small, quaint city in Japan – it is a blend of natural beauty, rich cultural traditions, and lively modern city life. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can fully explore Iwakuni’s must-visit attractions, indulge in the delicious local cuisine, and experience the customs and traditions of the city.
