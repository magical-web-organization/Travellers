Introduction to Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima is a beautiful island located in Yamaguchi (prefecture), in Chugoku, in Japan, in East Asia, in Asia. With a quaint charm and a rich cultural heritage, Suo-Oshima is an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore the lesser-known parts of Japan. The island is known for its beautiful beaches, lush green forests, and historic sites. With an array of activities and experiences, Suo-Oshima will undoubtedly leave you with unforgettable memories. In this travel guide, we will take a detailed look at the must-visit places, best things to do, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Suo-Oshima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Suo-Oshima has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the island’s past. Some of the most popular places to visit are:

  • Oshima Museum of Picture Book Art: This museum showcases the works of internationally acclaimed picture book artists from around the world.
  • Suo-Oshima Historical Museum: This museum has a collection of items related to the history and culture of Suo-Oshima.
  • Nagahama Beach: This beach is famous for its beautiful sunset and is perfect for a relaxing evening stroll.

Natural Attractions of Suo-Oshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Suo-Oshima is a place where you can connect with nature and indulge in outdoor adventures. Some of the popular natural attractions of Suo-Oshima are:

  • Kaminoseki Beach: This beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing. It is also a great spot to watch the beautiful sunrise.
  • Unoshima Island: This uninhabited island is famous for its rugged coastline, perfect for hiking and nature walks.
  • Mt. Kanpuzan: This mountain is perfect for hiking and offers breathtaking views of the island and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers, with several well-marked trails and routes. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes are:

  • The Oshima Nature Trail: This trail takes you through the forests and along the coast of Suo-Oshima, offering stunning views of the island’s natural beauty.
  • Shonai-Machi Biking Course: This is a popular biking route that takes you through the rural landscape of Suo-Oshima and offers an insight into the island’s agricultural traditions.

Best Things to Do in Suo-Oshima: Activities and Tours

Suo-Oshima offers an array of activities and tours that cater to travelers of all interests. Some of the must-do activities and tours are:

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the waters around Suo-Oshima in a sea kayak and discover the island’s hidden coves and bays.
  • Tasting Local Sake: Suo-Oshima is famous for its sake, and taking a sake tour is a great way to explore the local brewing traditions.
  • Stay at a Traditional Ryokan: A traditional Japanese inn that offers an authentic Japanese experience with tatami mats, futon bedding, and regional cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Suo-Oshima

Day 1: Visit the Nagahama Beach and Suo-Oshima Historical Museum.

Day 2: Take a Sea Kayaking Tour and visit the Kaminoseki Beach.

Day 3: Take a Cycling Tour of the Shonai-Machi Biking Course and visit the Mt. Kanpuzan for a scenic hike.

Day 4: Explore the Unoshima Island and discover its rugged coastline.

Day 5: Take a Sake Tasting Tour and explore the local brewing traditions.

Day 6: Stay at a Traditional Ryokan and indulge in regional cuisine.

Day 7: Visit the Oshima Museum of Picture Book Art and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Suo-Oshima

Must-Try Dishes

Suo-Oshima is known for its delicious cuisine, which primarily features fresh seafood and vegetables. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Fugu Sashimi: Thinly sliced pufferfish that’s a delicacy in Japan.
  • Ankake Ramen: Ramen noodles served in a thick, starchy soup made with seafood broth.
  • Kake Soba: Soba noodles served in a hot soup made with soy sauce and dashi broth.

Local Beverages

Suo-Oshima is famous for its sake, which is made from locally grown rice. Some of the popular sake breweries are:

  • Sakai Sake Brewery: One of the oldest sake breweries on the island.
  • Yoshinogawa Sake Brewery: This brewery offers tours and tastings and is one of the most popular sake breweries in Japan.

Where to Stay in Suo-Oshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Suo-Oshima has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the popular places to stay are:

  • Suo-Oshima Onsen Hotel: A traditional Japanese hotel that’s perfect for travelers looking for an authentic Japanese experience.
  • Minshuku Komeya: A guesthouse that offers affordable accommodation and a homely atmosphere.
  • Suo-Oshima Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that’s perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Suo-Oshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Suo-Oshima is known for its local handicrafts and souvenirs that make for great gifts and keepsakes. Some of the popular things to buy are:

  • Suo-Oshima Sake: This sake makes for a great gift and is available in different varieties and packaging.
  • Oshima Tsumugi Silk: A type of silk that’s made using traditional methods and is known for its beautiful color and texture.
  • Handmade Pottery: Suo-Oshima is known for its pottery, including traditional tea sets and decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several traditional festivals and customs. Some of the popular festivals and customs are:

  • Oshima Tsubaki Matsuri: A festival held in February to celebrate camellias, which bloom profusely on the island.
  • Henro: A pilgrimage that takes place on the island and is dedicated to the Buddhist deity Kobo Daishi.

Best Time to Visit Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima is beautiful throughout the year, but the best time to visit is from April to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the island is covered in lush greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suo-Oshima

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering a house or temple.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Use your mobile phone in public places like trains and restaurants.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima is a safe place to visit, with negligible crime rates. However, it’s still advisable to take basic precautions like keeping your belongings safe and being cautious while walking alone at night.

Health in Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima has good medical facilities and hospitals in case of any emergency. It’s advisable to carry a first-aid kit and necessary medications while traveling.

Getting to Suo-Oshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Suo-Oshima

The nearest airports to Suo-Oshima are Yamaguchi Ube Airport and Hiroshima Airport. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to the island.

Bus Stations Near Suo-Oshima

The nearest bus station to Suo-Oshima is the Yanai Bus Terminal, which has regular bus services to the island.

Other Options

You can also reach Suo-Oshima by ferry from Kudamatsu or Yanai. The ferry journey takes around an hour and offers breathtaking views of the island’s coastline.

How to Get Around in Suo-Oshima

Suo-Oshima is a small island, and the best way to get around is by renting a bicycle or a car. Taxis and buses are also available for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Suo-Oshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Suo-Oshima is a great starting point for exploring the other parts of Yamaguchi (prefecture). Some of the popular nearby destinations are:

  • Yamaguchi City: The capital city of Yamaguchi (prefecture), known for its historic sites.
  • Hagi City: A historic city known for its samurai district and traditional pottery.

Conclusion

Suo-Oshima is a hidden gem in Japan that’s waiting to be discovered by travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience. With its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Suo-Oshima offers an array of experiences that cater to different interests and preferences. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or a culture enthusiast, Suo-Oshima has something special for you. We hope that this travel guide has provided you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Suo-Oshima and make the most of your visit.
