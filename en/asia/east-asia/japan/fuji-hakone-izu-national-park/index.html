Introduction to Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park

Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park is located in Japan, in East Asia, in Asia. It is a stunning destination that showcases Japan’s natural beauty, culture, and history. The park consists of three regions: Mount Fuji and the surrounding Five Lakes, the Hakone hot spring resort area, and the Izu Peninsula.

Local Culture and Customs of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park

  1. Remove shoes before entering a tatami (straw mats) room
  2. Respect local customs and manners, such as bowing when meeting someone
  3. Do not talk loudly in public spaces or while on public transportation
  4. Follow proper etiquette when visiting shrines and temples
  5. Try to speak basic Japanese phrases, such as “hello” and “thank you.”

Best Time to Visit Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park

The best time to visit Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park is from mid-April to mid-June and from mid-September to mid-November. These months provide the best weather conditions and allow for comfortable outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and culture
  2. Follow proper etiquette when visiting shrines and temples
  3. Try new foods and drinks
  4. Take advantage of outdoor activities in the park
  5. Learn basic Japanese phrases to help communicate with locals

Don’t:

  1. Take photos of people without their permission
  2. Wear shoes inside temples and shrines
  3. Talk loudly in public spaces or while on public transportation
  4. Litter or damage natural landmarks
  5. Ignore safety warnings and precautions

Safety and Health

Safety in Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park

  1. Follow all safety guidelines and instructions while participating in outdoor activities
  2. Do not swim alone in natural bodies of water
  3. Pay attention to weather forecasts and be aware of potential typhoons and earthquakes
  4. Carry a map or GPS device while hiking or biking
  5. Do not hike or climb outside of designated trails and paths.

Health in Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park

  1. Make sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated
  2. Use sunscreen and wear appropriate clothing to avoid sunburn and heatstroke
  3. If you have allergies, bring necessary medication with you
  4. Be aware of poisonous plants, insects, and animals in the park
  5. Seek medical attention if you become ill or injured during your trip.
