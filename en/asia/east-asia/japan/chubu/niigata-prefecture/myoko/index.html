Myoko is a serene town located in Niigata Prefecture, Chubu Region, Japan. Nestled in the Northern Japanese Alps, this quaint town offers breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, pristine lakes, and lush vegetation. Myoko has something to offer for every kind of traveler - from adventurous hikers to culture buffs, foodies to shopaholics. This article will guide you on the best places to visit, where to stay, and what to eat and drink in Myoko.

Introduction to Myoko

Myoko is a small town in the Niigata Prefecture of Japan, situated in the Chubu region’s eastern part. Although it is not very popular among international tourists, it attracts thousands of Japanese tourists every year. The town’s charm lies in its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Myoko

Myoko has plenty of exciting places to visit, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders. Here, we list some must-visit places in Myoko:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Myoko Folk Museum: This museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of Myoko town. Visitors can see traditional Japanese farmhouses, antique farming tools, and handicrafts.
  2. Imori Pond: This beautiful pond surrounded by tall trees is located in the heart of Myoko town. It is famous for its cherry blossom trees in spring and colorful foliage in autumn.
  3. Myokosan Kanko-ji Temple: This temple is one of the oldest and largest Buddhist temples in the Chubu region. Visitors can see the stunning pagoda, beautiful gardens, and intricate artworks.
  4. Myoko Kogen Sky Cable Car: Experience Myoko’s stunning beauty from above on this sky cable car ride. The cable car ride takes visitors to the top of Mt. Myoko, offering panoramic views of the entire town and the Northern Japanese Alps.

Natural Attractions of Myoko: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Myoko: One of Japan’s 100 Famous Mountains, Mount Myoko is a paradise for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery, hot springs, and wildlife.
  2. Lotte Arai Resort Ski Area: The ski area boasts over 20 km of ski slopes, catering to all levels of skiers and snowboarders.
  3. Sasagamine Plateau: The plateau beautifully adorned with wildflowers during the summer and fall, making it a great place to hike and experience breathtaking views of Mt. Myoko.
  4. Lake Nojiri: This beautiful lake is a popular spot for water sports, fishing, barbecues, and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myoko

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Myoko offers several routes that take visitors through lush forests and scenic mountain trails.

Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Myoko are:

  1. Myoko Akakura Onsen Course: This course takes visitors through beautiful forest scenery, and visitors can experience traditional Japanese Onsen after the hike.
  2. Mount Gondola Course: This trail takes visitors to the peak, where they can enjoy sweeping views of the Myoko area.
  3. Ikenotaira Hashikura Course: A great course for beginner and intermediate hikers, it takes visitors through beautiful forest and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Myoko: Activities and Tours

Myoko offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors looking for unique experiences.

Some of the best things to do in Myoko are:

  1. Snowshoeing: Visitors can experience Myoko’s beautiful winter scenery on snowshoes.
  2. Rafting: Brave the rapids of Myoko’s rivers on a thrilling rafting adventure.
  3. Sake Brewery Tours: Myoko is known for its delicious sake. Visitors can take a brewery tour, learn about sake making and taste their local brews.
  4. Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Japanese dishes using locally sourced ingredients.

Week-Long Itinerary for Myoko

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Myoko:

Day 1: Visit Myoko Folk Museum, Imori Pond, and Myoko Kogen Sky Cable Car. Day 2: Hike Mt. Myoko. Day 3: Ski or snowboard at Lotte Arai Resort Ski Area. Day 4: Take a brewery tour and go snowshoeing in the countryside. Day 5: Take a cooking class and explore Sasagamine Plateau. Day 6: Relax in an Onsen and explore Lake Nojiri. Day 7: Visit Myokosan Kanko-ji Temple and do some shopping at local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Myoko

Myoko is known for its mouth-watering local cuisine and sake. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  1. Niigata Soba Noodles: Myoko is known for its thin, chewy soba noodles served piping hot with a flavorful broth, crispy tempura, and savory toppings.
  2. Shamo Chicken: This free-range chicken bred in Niigata is so tender and flavorful, it’s a delight to eat in any form.
  3. Ochazuke: This rice dish topped with grilled fish, vegetables, and green tea broth is light, refreshing, and delicious.

Local Beverages:

  1. Sake: Myoko is famous for its robust, flavorful sake brewed using high-quality rice and pristine water.
  2. Joka Cider: This locally brewed cider is made from fresh apples and is a perfect drink for hot summer days.
  3. Koshihikari Rice Tea: Made from the best quality rice, this tea is fragrant, light, and is perfect either hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Myoko: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Myoko offers several accommodation options, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses.

Some of the best places to stay in Myoko are:

  1. Myoko Kogen Hotel: A luxurious hotel offering beautiful mountain views and world-class amenities.
  2. Akakura Hotel: A family-friendly hotel located close to the slopes of Akakura Ski Resort.
  3. Guesthouse Bunk: A budget-friendly guesthouse with cozy rooms and warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Myoko: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Myoko is famous for its local products, which make wonderful souvenirs to remember your trip.

Here are some of the best local products you can buy in Myoko:

  1. Myoko Kogen Beer: This locally brewed beer is made from pure Myoko water and premium malt.
  2. Myoko Lacquerware: Beautifully crafted wooden bowls, dishes, and chopsticks are skillfully decorated with intricate lacquer designs.
  3. Tsunan Silk: This high-quality silk is produced in Tsunan town, Niigata, and is known for its luster and softness.

Local Culture and Customs of Myoko

Myoko has a rich cultural heritage, and it is important to understand and respect local customs and practices.

Some important customs to keep in mind when in Myoko are:

  1. Shoes off: It is customary to remove shoes at the entrance of houses, hotels, and some shops.
  2. Bowing: Bowing is a traditional way of greeting in Japan, and it is important to follow the appropriate bowing angle and duration.
  3. Respect for elders: Japanese culture values respect for elders, so it is important to show proper respect and deference.

Best Time to Visit Myoko

The best time to visit Myoko depends on what you want to do and see. Winter (December to February) is the best time to enjoy skiing and snowboarding, while spring (March to May) is perfect for cherry blossom viewing. Summer (June to August) is ideal for hiking and outdoor activities, while autumn (September to November) is perfect for foliage viewing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myoko

When visiting Myoko, it is important to be respectful and mindful of local customs and culture. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  1. Learn some basic Japanese phrases and etiquette.
  2. Follow rules and regulations in public places.
  3. Try local cuisine and take part in cultural activities.

Don’t:

  1. Speak loudly or make noise in public places.
  2. Smoke in public places or while walking.
  3. Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese buildings.

Safety and Health

Myoko is generally a safe town to visit. However, as with any travel destination, it is important to take some precautions.

Safety in Myoko

  1. Dress appropriately for outdoor activities and changing weather conditions.
  2. Be mindful of wildlife in the mountains and forests.
  3. Use caution and follow safety guidelines when skiing or snowboarding.

Health in Myoko

  1. Be aware of any allergies or dietary restrictions when trying new foods.
  2. Drink plenty of water, especially during summer months.
  3. Be prepared for the high altitude when hiking.

Getting to Myoko: Transportation Options and Logistics

Myoko is easily accessible by plane, train, or bus.

Airports Near Myoko

  1. Niigata Airport: The closest airport to Myoko, approximately a 90-minute drive away.
  2. Tokyo Narita Airport: Approximately a 4.5-hour train ride to Myoko.

Bus Stations Near Myoko

  1. Joetsu Myoko Station: Located approximately 20 minutes from Myoko town.
  2. Nagano Station: Approximately a 1.5-hour bus ride from Myoko.

Other Options

  1. Train: The Hokuriku Shinkansen from Tokyo takes about 2.5 hours to reach Joetsu Myoko Station.
  2. Car: Myoko is located along the Joshinetsu Expressway, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Myoko

Myoko is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by public transportation.

Public Transportation

  1. Myoko Bus: A bus service that covers the major attractions and ski resorts in Myoko.
  2. Myoko Kogen Local Bus: A bus service that connects the ski resorts in Myoko.

Taxis

Taxis are readily available in Myoko and can be hailed on the street or booked over the phone.

Where to Go Next from Myoko: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Myoko is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas and attractions.

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Kanazawa: A picturesque town famous for its traditional architecture, gardens, and museums. Approximately a 2-hour drive or train ride from Myoko.
  2. Yuzawa: A popular ski town famous for its hot springs and local sake. Approximately a 45-minute train ride from Joetsu Myoko Station.
  3. Snow Monkey Park: A park where visitors can see wild Japanese macaques enjoying the hot springs. Approximately a 1.5-hour drive from Myoko.

Conclusion

Myoko is a hidden gem of Japan, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and exquisite cuisine. Visitors can explore the snow-capped mountains, hike through serene bamboo forests, and sample delicious local delicacies. With an abundance of cultural attractions and outdoor activities, Myoko is an ideal destination for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.
