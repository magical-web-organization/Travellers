Introduction to Sakai (Fukui)

Sakai (Fukui) is a small town located in the Fukui prefecture of Japan. This charming town is nestled in the heart of Chubu, which is known for its majestic natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Despite its small size, Sakai (Fukui) has a lot to offer visitors, including beautiful natural sceneries, rich historical and cultural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know about Sakai (Fukui) so that you can plan and enjoy a memorable trip to this hidden gem of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Sakai (Fukui)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eiheiji Temple: This temple is one of the most important Zen Buddhism temples in Japan. It was founded in 1244 and is famous for its peaceful atmosphere and beautiful architecture.

Fukui Castle: Also known as “Wakasa-Tsuruga Castle”, it is a reconstruction of a castle built in the 17th century. Visitors can explore the castle grounds and enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding area.

Fujino Genkuro Memorial Museum: This museum showcases the works of Fujino Genkuro, a famous Japanese artist known for his unique style and works in ceramics, lacquerware, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Sakai (Fukui): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Maruoka Castle: One of the oldest preserved castles in Japan, Maruoka Castle is famous for its unique architecture and beautiful cherry blossom viewing during the spring season.

Katsuyama Dinosaur Quarry: Located just outside of Sakai (Fukui), this site is the largest dinosaur excavation site in Japan. Visitors can see the remains of ancient dinosaurs and learn about their history.

Tojinbo Cliffs: This natural wonder has been created by the rough waves of the sea, which have carved out some of the most unique natural rock formations in Japan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sakai (Fukui)

Mt. Hatsuzuka: This mountain is a popular spot for hiking and offers beautiful views of the surrounding area.

Junrei Park: With its beautiful walking paths and natural surroundings, Junrei park is a perfect spot for biking.

Best Things to Do in Sakai (Fukui): Activities and Tours

Sakai (Fukui) Industrial Tour: This tour takes visitors through the heart of Sakai (Fukui) and shows them the town’s unique industrial and agricultural practices, including its famous dinosaur excavation site.

Eiheiji Temple Meditation Experience: Visitors can participate in a meditation experience at Eiheiji temple and learn about the ancient Zen Buddhism practices.

Sakai (Fukui) Hot Spring Tour: Visitors can relax and unwind in one of the many hot springs located in and around Sakai (Fukui).

Food and Drink Scene of Sakai (Fukui)

Must-Try Dishes

Saba Sushi: A type of sushi made from mackerel, a local cuisine of Fukui.

Koshihikari Rice: A type of rice grown in the Fukui prefecture, known for its high quality and unique flavor.

Sakai Curry Udon: A dish made with udon noodles and a spicy curry sauce, a popular local dish of Sakai (Fukui).

Local Beverages

Echizen Sake: A local sake made in the Echizen area, known for its smooth and rich flavor.

Fukui Whiskey: An award-winning whiskey made in the Fukui prefecture.

Where to Stay in Sakai (Fukui): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eiheiji Kaikan: A traditional ryokan located near Eiheiji temple, featuring tatami-matted rooms and hot springs.

Sakai (Fukui) Onsen Hotel: A hotel located in the heart of Sakai (Fukui), featuring hot springs and traditional Japanese-style rooms.

What to Buy in Sakai (Fukui): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Echizen Lacquerware: A traditional craft of the Fukui prefecture, known for its intricate designs and detailed workmanship.

Bodaisenbei: A type of rice cracker made in the Fukui prefecture, known for its unique texture and flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Sakai (Fukui)

Sakai (Fukui) has a unique culture and customs. Visitors are expected to remove their shoes before entering most buildings, including Ryokans and temples. The locals are also known for their hospitality and welcoming attitude towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Sakai (Fukui)

The best time to visit Sakai (Fukui) is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms and fall foliage are at their peak. However, the town is beautiful year-round, so visitors can plan their trip according to their preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sakai (Fukui)

Do:

  • Take your shoes off before entering temples and ryokans.

  • Try the local cuisine, including Saba Sushi and Sakai Curry Udon.

  • Be respectful of the locals and their culture.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside most buildings, including temples and ryokans.

  • Litter or disrespect the town’s natural attractions.

  • Be loud and disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sakai (Fukui)

Sakai (Fukui) is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should be cautious when hiking or trekking in unfamiliar areas and should always be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Sakai (Fukui)

Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance before traveling to Sakai (Fukui) as medical treatment can be expensive in Japan. It is also important to carry any necessary medication with you and to check with your doctor for any recommendations before traveling.

Getting to Sakai (Fukui): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sakai (Fukui)

The nearest airport to Sakai (Fukui) is Komatsu Airport, located around 60 kilometers away. Visitors can take a bus or taxi from the airport to get to Sakai (Fukui).

Bus Stations Near Sakai (Fukui)

Sakai (Fukui) is well connected by buses, and visitors can take buses from nearby towns such as Fukui and Tsuruga to get to Sakai (Fukui).

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a train to get to Sakai (Fukui).

How to Get Around in Sakai (Fukui)

Visitors can easily get around Sakai (Fukui) by renting a car, taking a bus, or by taxi.

Where to Go Next from Sakai (Fukui): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fukui: The capital city of the Fukui prefecture, Fukui boasts rich cultural and historical attractions and is known for its stunning natural beauty.

Kanazawa: Known for its beautiful gardens and historic buildings, Kanazawa is an hour’s drive from Sakai (Fukui).

Conclusion

Sakai (Fukui) is a hidden gem of Japan. It offers visitors breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural and historical attractions, and a unique food and drink scene. Whether you are a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Sakai (Fukui) has something for everyone.
