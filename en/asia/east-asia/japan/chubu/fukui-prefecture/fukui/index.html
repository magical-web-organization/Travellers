Introduction to Fukui

Fukui is a city located in the Chubu region of Japan, known for its rich culture and natural beauty. This once castle town is now a bustling city that attracts tourists with its beautiful scenery, ancient temples and shrines, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Fukui

Fukui City boasts a variety of attractions that cater to all interests. Here is a list of places to visit in Fukui:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fukui Castle - This castle was built in the 16th century and it is considered one of the most beautiful castles in Japan.
  • Eiheiji Temple - This Zen Buddhist temple is known for its simple yet breathtaking architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • Maruoka Castle - A well-preserved castle that dates back to 1576, surrounded by beautiful cherry blossoms in the spring.
  • Fukui Dinosaur Museum - Home to the largest dinosaur skeleton in Japan, this museum educates visitors on the history and evolution of dinosaurs.
  • Ichijodani Asakura Clan Historic Ruins - Dating back to the 16th century, this site showcases the rich history of the region with reconstructed buildings, exhibits and artefacts.

Natural Attractions of Fukui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tojinbo Cliffs - These beautiful and unique cliffs are made out of hexagonal basalt rock columns carved by the waves of the sea.
  • Echizen Coastline - This beautiful coastal area boasts beautiful views and a variety of outdoor activities such as skiing, snowboarding, and rafting.
  • Tsuruga Bay - A beautiful bay that is perfect for a relaxing day spent swimming, kayaking, and sunbathing on the beach.
  • Echizen-Kaga Kaigan Quasi-National Park - Beautiful scenery, gorgeous beaches and beautiful rock formations.
  • Echizen Ono Castle - A must-see attraction for history buffs, this beautifully preserved castle offers stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fukui

Fukui is a dream destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking routes perfect for outdoor activities.

  • Kuzuryu River Cycling Road - A beautiful 27km cycling route that offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding landscape.
  • Hakusan White Road Drive - Drive up stunning mountain passes to find beautiful views of the mountains of Fukui.
  • Echizen Ono Castle Hill - Hike this hill to the top of the castle and get beautiful views of the area.

Best Things to Do in Fukui: Activities and Tours

  • Sake Brewery Tours - Relax and experience the taste of Fukui by taking a tour of the local sake breweries.
  • Kimono Experience - Immerse yourself in Japanese culture by trying on a traditional Japanese Kimono and have your picture taken in the beautiful scenery of Fukui.
  • Wara Art Festival - An annual festival where locals create sculptures out of straw, an amazing display of local creativity.
  • Fukui Traditional Crafts Village - A great opportunity to learn about traditional Japanese crafts and watch artisans work in front of your eyes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fukui

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Fukui:

Day Plan
Day 1 Visit Fukui Castle, Ichijodani Asakura Clan Historic Ruins, and Fukui Dinosaur Museum.
Day 2 Take a walking tour of Eiheiji Temple, Maruoka Castle and Zuiryū-ji Temple in the afternoon
Day 3 Hike up Echizen Ono Castle Hill or spend the morning cycling on the Kuzuryu River Cycling Road.
Day 4 Visit Tojinbo Cliffs and stop for a swim at Tsuruga Bay in the afternoon.
Day 5 Drive up Hakusan White Road, stopping for beautiful views and a picnic.
Day 6 Participate in the Wara Art Festival and take a Kimono Experience.
Day 7 Visit Fukui Traditional Crafts Village and enjoy some shopping for local souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Fukui

Fukui is known for its delicious cuisine, take a look at the must-try dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Echizen Crab - A local favourite, Echizen crab is known for its sweet and succulent flesh.
  • Sobameshi - A unique local dish consisting of stir-fried noodles and rice.
  • Wakasa Blowfish - Famous in Japan, Fugu (blowfish) is known for its delectable flesh that melts in your mouth.
  • Kamo-namban - A sour and spicy soup made with duck meat and vegetables.
  • Mamezushi - A type of sushi that features small beans as a topping.

Local Beverages

  • Sake - Try some of the elegant and refined sake made with the pure spring water of Fukui.
  • Echizen Yuzu - A refreshing citrus drink that is perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Fukui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fukui has several accommodation options to choose from, here are some recommended options:

  • Hotel Fujita Fukui - A luxurious option located near Fukui station.
  • Guesthouse O Habitat - A budget-friendly option located in Fukui city.
  • Hotel Riverge Akebono - A ryokan-style hotel featuring beautiful Japanese-style rooms.

What to Buy in Fukui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Echizen Lacquerware - Handcrafted lacquerware products made in the Echizen region, known for its delicate designs and beautiful finishes.
  • Echizen Washi - A traditional Japanese paper made in Fukui, prized for its strength and texture.
  • Baked Echizen Crab - A local delicacy, baked Echizen crab tastes like heaven and a must-buy for seafood lovers.
  • Fukui Wine - Produced with local grapes, Fukui wine is perfect for wine enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Fukui

  • Respect the local customs such as bowing when you meet someone and taking off your shoes before entering a temple.
  • Learn about the traditional craftsmanship of Fukui, it is a part of the rich culture of the region.
  • Try to learn some basic Japanese, the locals will appreciate it.

Best Time to Visit Fukui

The best time to visit Fukui is from April to June and September to November, when the weather is mild and the tourist crowds are less.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fukui

Do:

  • Carry cash, as some places in Fukui may not accept credit cards.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a temple

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission in temples where photography is prohibited.
  • Eat while walking on the streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fukui

Fukui is a relatively safe city, however, tourists should always be aware of their surroundings and take basic precautions like any other city.

Health in Fukui

Fukui has excellent healthcare facilities that cater to tourists, it is important to carry travel insurance and stay informed about the facilities.

Getting to Fukui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are your transportation options for getting to Fukui:

Airports Near Fukui

The nearest airport to Fukui is Komatsu Airport. From there, take a one-hour train ride to Fukui.

Bus Stations Near Fukui

Fukui Bus Terminal is conveniently located near Fukui station and it serves long-distance bus routes to major cities in Japan.

Other Options

  • JR Fukui station is located in the heart of the city and serves train lines to major cities in Japan.
  • Taxis and car rentals are also available at Fukui station.

How to Get Around in Fukui

Fukui’s public transportation system is well connected and easy to use, here are your options:

  • Trains: Traveling by train is highly recommended as it is the most efficient way to explore the city.
  • Buses: Fukui has a well-developed bus system that is easy to navigate and covers most of the city’s attractions.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available at the stations and major tourist spots.

Where to Go Next from Fukui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fukui is conveniently located near several destinations that are perfect for day trips, here are some suggestions:

  • Kanazawa - A city known for its rich history and stunning gardens. It is only a 1-hour train ride from Fukui.
  • Kyoto - A former capital of Japan known for its ancient temples, shrines, and beautiful gardens. It is only a 2-hour train ride from Fukui.
  • Wakasa Bay - A beautiful bay known for its stunning views and fresh seafood. It is only a 1-hour drive from Fukui.

Conclusion

Fukui is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the beauty, culture, and nature of Japan. With a rich history, delicious cuisine, and plenty of outdoor activities, Fukui has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Fukui today and discover the hidden gem of Japan!
