Introduction to Eiheiji

Eiheiji is a picturesque town located in Fukui prefecture, in the Chubu region of Japan. The town is nestled amidst the serene mountains, giving visitors a glimpse of the traditional Japanese way of living, as well as an opportunity to explore their spirituality.

Eiheiji is home to the famous Eiheiji Temple, which was founded in 1244 by Zen master Dogen. The temple is one of the most important Zen training centers in Japan, and it attracts thousands of tourists every year. Apart from the temple, there are also several historical sites, hiking and biking trails, natural attractions, and authentic local experiences to be had in Eiheiji.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, the food and drink scene, where to stay, and local customs in Eiheiji.

List of Places to Visit in Eiheiji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Eiheiji Temple - The most renowned attraction in Eiheiji is this Buddhist temple, founded by Zen master Dogen. Visitors can witness Zen meditation performed by the monks, enjoy a peaceful and serene walk through the temple complex, and learn about the history and philosophy of Zen Buddhism.
  2. Eiheiji Museum - A museum adjacent to the Eiheiji temple complex, which displays historical artifacts, texts, and artwork on the life and teachings of Dogen and the history of Eiheiji Temple.
  3. Daimonji Temple - A peaceful and secluded temple with a serene Zen garden, a popular spot for meditation and reflection.
  4. Daikuin Temple - A lesser-known temple famous for its intricate wooden sculptures, including a “dragon head” carving on the main gate.
  5. Konjo-In Temple - A temple located at the top of a hill, which offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. A perfect spot for nature and spirituality enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Eiheiji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kisekitei Observation Deck - A scenic viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the Echizen Mountains and the surrounding villages.
  2. Wakasa Lake - A picturesque lake surrounded by hills and forests, where visitors enjoy boating, fishing, and camping.
  3. Ontake Valley - A ravine with clear streams and lush forests, famous for its hiking trails and scenic waterfalls.
  4. Nata Gorge - A scenic gorge with crystal-clear waterfalls, where visitors can also enjoy hiking, rock climbing, and river-rafting.
  5. Maruoka Castle - A traditional Japanese castle located in the nearby town of Sakai, known for its unique “musha-gaeshi” design and scenic surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eiheiji

Eiheiji is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, especially hikers and bikers, who can explore the scenic natural beauty of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Some of the popular hiking trails in Eiheiji are:

  1. Eiheiji Temple Hiking Trail - A 5-kilometer trail that winds through the hills and forest, connecting the Eiheiji temple with nearby temples and scenic spots.
  2. Mt. Kinpou Hiking Trail - A scenic hiking trail that takes you to the summit of Mt. Kinpou, which offers a panoramic view of the Echizen Mountains and the Sea of Japan.
  3. Shojin Hiking Trail - A peaceful trail that takes you to the Konjo-in temple, through scenic bamboo groves, and along the flowing river.
  4. Tour de Echizen - A popular biking trail that starts from Eiheiji and circles around the Echizen coast, offering picturesque views of the sea and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Eiheiji: Activities and Tours

  1. Zen Meditation - Eiheiji is a popular destination for Zen meditation, and visitors can join the monks in their daily meditation sessions.
  2. Local Workshops - Visitors can join local workshops and learn traditional crafts such as pottery, calligraphy, and tea ceremony.
  3. Cultural Festivals - Eiheiji hosts several cultural festivals throughout the year, including the “Eiheiji Goma Festival,” where Buddhist monks perform a sacred fire ritual.
  4. Winter Sports - Eiheiji is also an excellent winter sports destination, as visitors can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing in the nearby ski resorts.
  5. Guided Tours - Visitors can join guided tours of the Eiheiji temple and nearby attractions, which provide an in-depth understanding of the history, culture, and spirituality of Eiheiji.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eiheiji

Day 1: Arrive in Eiheiji, visit Eiheiji Temple and adjacent Museum. Day 2: Hike the Eiheiji Temple Hiking Trail, visit Daimonji Temple and Konjo-In Temple. Day 3: Spend a day exploring the Ontake Valley or the Nata Gorge, including waterfalls and hiking trails. Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Maruoka Castle, visit the nearby town of Sakai. Day 5: Explore the Kisekitei Observation Deck, visit the local workshops and learn traditional crafts. Day 6: Try winter sports such as skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing in the nearby ski resorts. Day 7: Join the Zen Meditation experience and attend the Eiheiji Goma Festival (if available).

Food and Drink Scene of Eiheiji

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Echizen Crab - A delicacy of the region, the sweet and succulent meat of Echizen crab is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  2. Soba Noodles - Eiheiji is also known for its traditional soba noodles made from buckwheat flour, served with dipping sauces and various toppings.
  3. Sekihira - A local snack made from rice flour and syrup, shaped like a peach, and filled with sweet bean paste.
  4. Oden - A local winter dish made from daikon radish, eggs, tofu, and meat, served in a hot pot with a simmering broth.

Local Beverages

  1. Echizen Sake - A locally brewed sake made from pure mountain water and high-quality rice.
  2. Sake-lees Tea - A unique tea made from leftover sake lees, which is said to aid digestion and has a rich, fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Eiheiji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Eiheiji Grand Hotel - A luxurious hotel located near the Eiheiji temple, with comfortable rooms, hot springs, and a traditional Japanese restaurant.
  2. Eiheiji Guesthouse Goo - A cozy guesthouse with affordable dorm beds, private rooms, and a communal kitchen.
  3. Maruoka Palace Hotel - A charming hotel located in the nearby town of Sakai, with traditional Japanese-style rooms, gardens, and hot springs.
  4. Ontake Golf & Resort - A modern resort situated in the Ontake Valley, with stunning views, golf courses, and sports facilities.

What to Buy in Eiheiji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Echizen Lacquerware - A traditional craft of the region, Echizen lacquerware has a glossy finish and features intricate designs and patterns.
  2. Echizen Soy Sauce - A high-quality soy sauce made from locally grown soybeans and wheat, with a rich flavor and aroma.
  3. Echizen Washi Paper - A Japanese-style paper made from mulberry fiber, with unique textures and patterns not found in regular paper.
  4. Echizen Crab - Visitors can buy fresh or frozen Echizen crab to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Eiheiji

Eiheiji is a deeply spiritual and traditional place, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and way of life. Some of the customs to keep in mind are:

  1. Dress Code - Visitors are expected to dress modestly, covering their shoulders and knees, when entering temples and other religious sites.
  2. Etiquette - Visitors are expected to be quiet and respectful when inside temples, and avoid any disruptive behavior, such as taking pictures or talking loudly.
  3. Greeting - Visitors are expected to bow when meeting monks or locals, as a sign of respect.
  4. Shoes - Visitors are expected to remove their shoes when entering Japanese-style houses, temples, or other indoor spaces.

Best Time to Visit Eiheiji

The best time to visit Eiheiji is from spring to fall (March to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant, and nature is in full bloom. The peak season is during the cherry blossom season (late March to early April), when the temple and surrounding areas are covered in a beautiful pink hue. Fall (late September to early November) is also a great time to visit, as the autumn colors paint the hills and valleys with vibrant hues.

Winter (December to February) is also a great time to visit Eiheiji, as visitors can enjoy winter sports and festivals, but the weather can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eiheiji

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately when entering religious sites or temples.
  3. Try local food and drinks.
  4. Join a Zen meditation session for a true Eiheiji experience.
  5. Take off your shoes when entering Japanese-style houses or temples.
  6. Learn simple Japanese phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  1. Be loud or disruptive in temples or public areas.
  2. Take photos or make noise during meditation sessions or ceremonies.
  3. Touch or remove any historical artifacts or sculptures in the temples or museums.
  4. Smoke or litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eiheiji

Eiheiji is a safe and peaceful town with low crime rates, and visitors need not worry about their safety. However, visitors are advised to be cautious and follow common-sense measures, such as:

  1. Secure their belongings, especially in crowded areas, and avoid carrying valuables.
  2. Look both ways before crossing the road, as Japanese cars may drive on the left-hand side.
  3. Follow the instructions and warnings of local authorities during natural disasters, such as typhoons or earthquakes.

Health in Eiheiji

Eiheiji is a generally healthy town, and visitors need not worry about any major health hazards. However, visitors are advised to take basic health precautions, such as:

  1. Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid any stomach infections.
  2. Carry a first-aid kit to address minor injuries or illnesses.
  3. Check the weather forecast, especially during hot or cold seasons, to avoid any weather-related illnesses.

Getting to Eiheiji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eiheiji is well-connected to major Japanese cities by train, bus, or car. Visitors can take the following transportation options to reach Eiheiji:

Airports Near Eiheiji

  1. Komatsu Airport - The nearest airport to Eiheiji, located roughly 80 kilometers away, which is connected to major Japanese cities by domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Eiheiji

  1. Fukui Bus Terminal - The main bus terminal in Fukui city, where visitors can take a direct bus to Eiheiji.

Other Options

  1. Train - Visitors can take the Hokuriku Shinkansen from Tokyo, Osaka, or Kyoto to Fukui station, and then take a local bus or taxi to Eiheiji.
  2. Car - Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Eiheiji, as it is well-connected by highways and major roads.

How to Get Around in Eiheiji

The best way to explore Eiheiji is on foot or by bicycle, as the town is a walkable and bike-friendly destination, with dedicated pedestrian lanes and bike paths. Visitors can also take local buses or taxis to reach the nearby attractions or towns. Renting a car or bike is also an excellent option for those who want to explore more remote areas or go on day trips.

Where to Go Next from Eiheiji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eiheiji is surrounded by several nearby towns and attractions, which are worth visiting. Some of the popular destinations are:

  1. Fukui - The capital city of Fukui Prefecture, famous for its dinosaur museum, Echizen crabs, and scenic views of Wakasa Bay.
  2. Kanazawa - A historic castle town famous for its traditional architecture, art museums, and scenic gardens.
  3. Kaga Onsen - A spa town known for its natural hot springs, traditional inns, and beautiful architecture.
  4. Takayama - A picturesque town located in the Japanese Alps, famous for its old-fashioned streets, sake breweries, and folk art museum.

Conclusion

Eiheiji is a unique destination that offers a perfect blend of spirituality, nature, and culture. Visitors can explore the historical temples, scenic hikes, local food, and crafts, and participate in the traditions and customs of this charming town. With a week-long itinerary, our travel guide provides everything you need to know to plan your next trip to Eiheiji with ease and comfort.
