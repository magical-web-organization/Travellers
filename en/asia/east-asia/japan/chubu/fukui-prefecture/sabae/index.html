Introduction to Sabae

Sabae is a charming city situated in the Fukui Prefecture of Japan, which is located in the Chubu region of central Japan. Known for its production of eyeglasses, Sabae is also a cultural hub with several museums and historical attractions. The city has plenty of outdoor adventures, scenic views, and delicious local cuisine, making it a must-visit destination in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Sabae

Whether you like to explore museums, go hiking or biking, or simply want to relax and enjoy the scenic views, Sabae has something for every traveler.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sabae has a rich history that is reflected in its museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Sabae:

  • Sabae City Museum of Glass: A museum dedicated to showcasing the history of Sabae’s eyeglasses production and the works of local artisans.
  • Sabae Contemporary Art Museum: A contemporary art museum featuring modern installations from local and international artists.
  • Ono Castle Ruins: A historical site that is home to the ruins of the Ono Castle, a former castle from the Edo period.

Natural Attractions of Sabae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From stunning waterfalls and hiking trails to lush forests and scenic lakes, Sabae has plenty of natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-visit outdoor adventures and scenic views in Sabae:

  • Mount Hoonji: A popular hiking spot that offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Tojinbo Cliffs: The Tojinbo Cliffs are a series of steep cliffs that offer an impressive view of the Sea of Japan.
  • Fukubu Valley Waterfall: A stunning waterfall that cascades down a rocky cliff and surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sabae

If you enjoy hiking or cycling, Sabae has plenty of trails that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Sabae include:

  • Hoon-ji Temple Trail: This trail takes you through a peaceful forest to the Hoon-ji Temple, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of Sabae City.
  • Sabae Green Road: A scenic cycling route that takes you through the lush countryside and past historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Sabae: Activities and Tours

Sabae has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Sabae:

  • Take a glass-making workshop: Learn the art of making eyeglasses at Sabae’s Glass Studio.
  • Join a local cultural experience: Sabae is known for its traditional Japanese culture. Join a tea ceremony or a flower arrangement class to learn more about this culture.
  • Try local cuisine: Sabae is home to some delicious local dishes, like Fukui soba noodles and Echizen crab.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sabae

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Sabae:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Sabae City Museum of Glass and the Sabae Contemporary Art Museum
Day 2 Hike Mount Hoonji in the morning and visit the Ono Castle Ruins in the afternoon
Day 3 Take a glass-making workshop at Sabae’s Glass Studio in the morning and explore the Fukubu Valley Waterfall in the afternoon
Day 4 Go cycling along the Sabae Green Road
Day 5 Join a local cultural experience like a tea ceremony or flower arrangement class
Day 6 Try local cuisine like Fukui soba noodles or Echizen crab
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Sabae

Food and Drink Scene of Sabae

Sabae is known for its delicious local cuisine and vibrant drinking scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Sabae:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fukui soba noodles: Thin, soba noodles served in a hot broth.
  • Echizen Crab: A type of crab known for its sweet and succulent meat.
  • Kani Nabe: A hot pot dish made with Echizen crab and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Hakkaisan Sake: An award-winning sake produced in the nearby prefecture of Niigata.
  • Asahi Super Dry: A popular Japanese beer.

Where to Stay in Sabae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sabae has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sabae:

  • Hotel Route-Inn Sabae Ekimae: A modern hotel located near Sabae Station.
  • Guesthouse Mido: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Sabae.
  • Hostel Tomar: A budget-friendly hostel located near Sabae Station.

What to Buy in Sabae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sabae is famous for its eyeglasses, and there are several shops in the city where you can buy a pair of locally made glasses. Other popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Sabae include:

  • Kutani ware: Beautiful porcelain dishes made in the nearby Ishikawa Prefecture.
  • Echizen Lacquerware: Handcrafted dishes and bowls made with a unique lacquer technique.

Local Culture and Customs of Sabae

Sabae has a rich culture that is reflected in its festivals and traditions. Some of the most popular festivals in Sabae include the Sabae Hikiyama Festival and the Ikkoku Mairi festival.

Best Time to Visit Sabae

The best time to visit Sabae is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months are cold with occasional snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sabae

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Sabae:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Use your mobile phone on public transportation.
  • Tip at restaurants or hotels.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sabae

Sabae is a safe city with low crime rates. However, like in any other city, it is important to take precautions to ensure your safety, especially at night.

Health in Sabae

Sabae has high-quality medical facilities, and there are several clinics and hospitals in the city. It is advisable to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies during your stay in Sabae.

Getting to Sabae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sabae

The closest airport to Sabae is the Komatsu Airport, which is located approximately 50 kilometers away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Sabae

The Sabae Bus Terminal is located near Sabae Station and is a major hub for long-distance buses.

Other Options

Sabae is well-connected by train, and the Sabae Station serves as a major transportation hub in the city. There are also several taxis and rental car services available for transportation within the city.

How to Get Around in Sabae

The best way to get around in Sabae is on foot or by bicycle. The city is relatively small and easy to navigate, and there are plenty of bicycle rental services available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Sabae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sabae is located near several other popular destinations in Japan, including:

  • Kanazawa: A beautiful city known for its traditional Japanese architecture and gardens.
  • Kyoto: A historic city with stunning temples and cultural landmarks.
  • Osaka: A vibrant city known for its delicious food and nightlife.

Conclusion

Sabae is a beautiful city with plenty of natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re interested in hiking, cycling or exploring museums, Sabae has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and enjoy your stay in this charming Japanese city.
