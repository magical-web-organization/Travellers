Introduction to Fukui (prefecture)

Fukui prefecture is located in the Chubu region of Japan and boasts a rich cultural and natural heritage. The region is known for its exquisite cuisine, historic landmarks, and breathtaking landscapes. Visitors to Fukui prefecture can explore the city’s historical sites and museums, hike in the numerous nature preserves, and experience local festivals and cultural celebrations.

List of Places to Visit in Fukui (prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eiheiji Temple: A Zen temple founded in 1244, Eiheiji is considered one of the top three Zen temples in the country.
  • Fukui Castle Ruins: The castle was built in the 1600s by the lord of Fukui Castle, and visitors can explore the ruins of the castle’s walls and moats.
  • Museum of Dinosaur Prefectural: The museum features one of Japan’s largest museum dinosaur collections, including a life-sized model of Fukuiraptor, which was discovered in the prefecture.

Natural Attractions of Fukui (prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tojimbo Cliff: A beautiful geological formation on the coast, the cliffs rise up to 25 meters above the sea, offering spectacular views.
  • Echizen-Kaga Kaigan Quasi-National Park: The park features beautiful coastlines, stunning vistas, and abundant wildlife.
  • Tsuruga Bay: A beautiful bay known for its clear waters and rocky shores.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fukui (prefecture)

  • Echizen Ono Castle Course: This 12 km trail leads hikers through the beautiful scenery surrounding Echizen Ono Castle.
  • Sabae Nature Trail: This trail takes hikers through forests and along streams and is perfect for birdwatching and wildlife spotting.
  • Mt. Sakauchi Hike: A challenging hike, the trek up Mt. Sakauchi rewards hikers with beautiful views of the mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Fukui (prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Attend a local festival: Fukui Prefecture has a rich tradition of local festivals that take place throughout the year.
  • Visit an onsen: There are numerous onsen resorts in Fukui prefecture that offer unique bathing experiences and relaxation.
  • Try traditional cuisine: Fukui is famous for its cuisine, including freshwater fish, crab, and traditional dishes such as Echizen soba and Fukui rice.

Food and Drink Scene of Fukui (prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Echizen crab: This type of crab is caught off the coast of Fukui prefecture and is known for its sweet, delicate flavor.
  • Echizen soba: This type of soba noodle is made with locally-grown buckwheat and is known for its unique texture.
  • Fukui rice: Fukui prefecture is known for its high-quality rice, which is used in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Fukui prefecture is home to many high-quality sake breweries that produce a variety of delicious and unique sake.
  • Kuroube: This traditional drink is made from fermented brown sugar and is a unique local beverage.

Where to Stay in Fukui (prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Beniya Mukayu: This high-end ryokan offers luxurious accommodations and an onsen.
  • Hotel Route-Inn Fukui Ekimae: This affordable hotel is conveniently located near Fukui Station.
  • Guesthouse Urameshiya: This charming guesthouse features traditional Japanese-style rooms and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Fukui (prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Echizen washi: This traditional Japanese paper is made by hand using locally-grown mulberry trees.
  • Sake: Fukui prefecture produces some of Japan’s best sake, which makes a great souvenir to bring home.
  • Fukui lacquerware: Fukui is known for its high-quality lacquerware, which comes in a variety of colors and styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Fukui (prefecture)

  • Danjiri Matsuri: One of Fukui’s most popular festivals, the Danjiri Matsuri features large wheeled floats that are pulled through the streets.
  • Ishidori Matsuri: This festival features traditional dance performances and is held to celebrate the harvest season.
  • Wadaiko drumming: Fukui is known for its unique style of taiko drumming, which is often performed at local festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Fukui (prefecture)

The best time to visit Fukui prefecture is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fukui (prefecture)

Do:

  • Respect local customs: Take your shoes off when entering a Japanese-style room or house.
  • Try traditional food: Fukui is known for its delicious cuisine, so be sure to try some of the local dishes.
  • Follow festival etiquette: If attending a local festival, be sure to follow any rules or regulations set forth by the event organizers.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly on trains: It is considered impolite to speak loudly or use your phone on trains or public transportation in Japan.
  • Skip the onsen: Onsen are an integral part of Japanese culture, so be sure to visit at least one during your stay.
  • Litter: Japan has strict laws against littering, so be sure to dispose of your trash properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fukui (prefecture)

Fukui prefecture is generally safe for travelers. However, like in any other destination, it is important to take basic precautions, such as keeping a close eye on your belongings and avoiding poorly lit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Fukui (prefecture)

Fukui prefecture does not pose any major health risks to travelers. However, it is important to have comprehensive travel insurance and to take any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Fukui (prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fukui (prefecture)

The closest airports to Fukui prefecture are Komatsu Airport, located approximately 60 km away, and Osaka Kansai International Airport, located approximately 180 km away.

Bus Stations Near Fukui (prefecture)

The Fukui bus terminal is located in the city center and offers connections to various destinations in the prefecture and wider region.

Other Options

The JR Hokuriku Main Line runs through Fukui prefecture, providing access to other cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Fukui (prefecture)

Fukui prefecture has an extensive transportation network, including buses, trains, and taxis. Rental cars are also available for those who prefer the flexibility of driving themselves.

Where to Go Next from Fukui (prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kanazawa: This beautiful city is known for its well-preserved Edo-era architecture and traditional crafts.
  • Kyoto: A short train ride away, Kyoto is home to ancient temples, shrines, and beautiful gardens.
  • Osaka: Japan’s third-largest city, Osaka is known for its delicious food, vibrant culture, and bustling nightlife.

Conclusion

Fukui prefecture is a hidden gem that offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions for visitors. From its historic landmarks and museums to its stunning natural landscapes and delicious cuisine, Fukui has something for everyone. Plan your trip using this guide, and discover all that this wonderful prefecture has to offer.
