Introduction to Tsuruga

Tsuruga is a beautiful port city located on the coast of the Sea of Japan in Fukui Prefecture, Chubu region. Surrounded by mountains and Lake Biwa, Japan’s largest freshwater lake, Tsuruga boasts a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. This hidden gem is known for its breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and delicious seafood.

List of Places to Visit in Tsuruga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tsuruga’s the rich cultural heritage can be seen in its museums and historical places.

1. Kanegasaki Shrine

Kanegasaki Shrine, one of the oldest shrines in Japan, was founded over 1,300 years ago. The shrine has a unique history and is situated on a hill overlooking the city. Here, you can admire the ancient architecture and learn about the rituals and traditions of Japanese Shintoism.

2. Tsuruga Red Brick Warehouse

Tsuruga Red Brick Warehouse is an impressive red brick building that has now been designated as a historic site. Built in 1905, it houses the Tsuruga Museum, showcasing the town’s long history and culture. Be amazed by the architecture and then learn about the deep cultural roots of the people of Tsuruga.

Natural Attractions of Tsuruga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tsuruga boasts some of the most stunning natural scenery that Japan has to offer.

1. Mikata Five Lakes

The Mikata Five Lakes are a collection of crater lakes that have formed over thousands of years. Located on a mountain range in Wakasa, these lakes offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. With hiking trails around the area, you can have the opportunity to explore each lake and enjoy the beauty of nature.

2. Wakasa Bay

Wakasa Bay is the perfect place for visitors to enjoy the outdoors, with amazing water activities such as swimming, diving, and even sea kayaking. You can also take a boat tour of the Bay to explore the stunning scenery and surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsuruga

Tsuruga has plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer spectacular views of the city.

1. Tsuruga-jo Peak

Tsuruga-jo Peak is a popular hike that starts at the foot of Tsuruga Castle. The trail takes you through lush green forests with stunning views and scenic pathways. Once you reach the summit, you will be rewarded with a stunning panoramic view of Tsuruga.

2. Lake Biwa Cycling Road

The Lake Biwa Cycling Road is a popular biking route that covers a 240-kilometer path around Lake Biwa. You can take in the stunning scenery of the lake, forests, and mountains. Cycling enthusiasts will enjoy the challenge, making several stops along the way to enjoy the beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Tsuruga: Activities and Tours

Tsuruga offers various activities that travelers can indulge in, from taking in the stunning scenery to enjoying the delicious Japanese cuisine.

1. Taste the Fresh Seafood

Tsuruga is known for its fresh seafood delicacies. Try the local specialty, Kaisendon (Fresh Seafood Bowl), which can be found at local seafood restaurants around the city. Enjoy flavorful and succulent seafood that’s unique to this area.

2. Take a Yachting Tour

Take a yachting tour of the Sea of Japan, starting from Tsuruga Port. Enjoy the view of the clear blue sea as you sail around the coastal areas of Tsuruga. Seeing the natural beauty of Tsuruga in this way is a unique experience that you will not forget.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tsuruga

Day 1: Visit the Tsuruga Red Brick Warehouse and Tsuruga Museum

Day 2: Experience kayaking at Wakasa Bay

Day 3: Hike the Mikata Five Lakes

Day 4: Take a yachting tour of the sea of Japan

Day 5: Try the fresh seafood at the local seafood restaurants

Day 6: Take a cycling tour of Lake Biwa

Day 7: Visit Kanegasaki Shrine

Food and Drink Scene of Tsuruga

Tsuruga’s cuisine is unforgettable, with its fresh seafood delicacies and local dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Kaisendon

Kaisendon, a bowl of fresh seafood, is Tsuruga’s specialty dish. Savor different types of seafood including clams, octopus, squid, sea urchin, and much more at local seafood restaurants.

2. Tsuruga Oden

This slow-cooked stew is a popular winter dish in Tsuruga. Tsuruga Oden is a soulful dish made with eggs, vegetables, and seafood in a hot broth.

Local Beverages

1. Sake

Sake, a Japanese fermented drink made from rice, is a must-try in Tsuruga. The drink has a long history in the country and is enjoyed at many local restaurants and bars around the city.

2. Fugeshi

Fugeshi is a traditional Japanese snack originating from Tsuruga made of mochi (rice cake) that’s grilled until it’s crispy. It’s perfect to enjoy it with warm sake while taking in the beautiful scenery.

Where to Stay in Tsuruga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tsuruga has a range of accommodation options for all types of travelers.

Hotels:

1. Hotel Sunrise Tsuruga

This hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable and spacious rooms at a reasonable price.

2. Tsuruga Manten Hotel

Tsuruga Manten Hotel is located near the Tsuruga Red Brick Warehouse and offers cozy rooms, spacious bathrooms, and a relaxing hot spring bath.

Hostels:

1. Tsuruga Guesthouse Nostalgia

This cozy guesthouse is located near the city center and offers dormitory rooms or private rooms for solo and group travelers.

Guesthouses:

1. Guesthouse Origami

This comfortable guesthouse offers Japanese-style tatami rooms in a tranquil setting for a relaxing stay.

What to Buy in Tsuruga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tsuruga offers a variety of unique and interesting souvenirs that you can take home with you.

1. Wakasa Lacquerware

Wakasa lacquerware is a local craft from Tsuruga that has been practiced for over 450 years. The intricate designs of the lacquerware can be found throughout the city in gift shops and souvenir stores.

2. Echizen Crabs

Echizen crab is a type of crab that’s unique to the area. It’s a popular gift for tourists who want a taste of the local seafood delicacies.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsuruga

Tsuruga has a deep cultural history, and visitors must understand some of the customs, traditions, and etiquette while there.

Best Time to Visit Tsuruga

The best time to visit Tsuruga is between March and June and from September to November. The weather is ideal during these months, and travelers can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and boating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsuruga

Do:

  • Try the local seafood delicacies.
  • Learn about the cultural heritage of the city.
  • Take a boat tour of Wakasa Bay.
  • Visit the Mikata Five Lakes for stunning scenery.

Don’t:

  • Leave any trash behind while hiking or exploring nature.
  • Speak loudly or disturb peaceful surroundings at temples and shrines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsuruga

Tsuruga is generally a safe city to travel in, though visitors should stay vigilant and take the necessary precautions when exploring the outdoors.

Health in Tsuruga

Tsuruga has top-class medical facilities, and travelers do not have to worry about health concerns.

Getting to Tsuruga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsuruga

Osaka International Airport or Kansai International Airport are the closest airports to Tsuruga, and travelers can take the express train or bus from either airport to reach the city.

Bus Stations Near Tsuruga

Tsuruga is well connected by road, and visitors can find several bus stations throughout the city, making transportation convenient.

Other Options

The express train is the best way to travel to Tsuruga from cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto.

How to Get Around Tsuruga

Tsuruga is well connected and has an extensive public transportation network. Visitors can take buses to travel around the city or use the local train system that connects various places around the prefecture.

Where to Go Next from Tsuruga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Kyoto

Kyoto is one of Japan’s most visited tourist destinations and is only two hours away from Tsuruga by express train. Visitors can enjoy a range of exciting activities here, from visiting ancient temples and shrines to exploring local markets.

2. Kanazawa

Kanazawa is located on the coast of the Sea of Japan and is famous for its rich cultural heritage and impressive gardens. It is situated approximately two hours from Tsuruga by express train.

Conclusion

Tsuruga is a hidden gem that offers visitors an opportunity to explore some of the most stunning natural scenery and rich cultural heritage of Japan. With a range of unique foods, top-class accommodation, and exciting outdoor activities, the city provides an exciting and memorable travel experience.
