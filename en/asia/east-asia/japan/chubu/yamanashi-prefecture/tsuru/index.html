Introduction to Tsuru

Nestled in the Yamanashi Prefecture of Chubu on the main island of Honshu, Tsuru is a hidden gem in Japan waiting to be discovered. Tsuru is a small town, but it offers plenty of sightseeing and entertainment options to satisfy any traveler. Whether you’re interested in culture and history, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Tsuru has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tsuru

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tsuru offers a glimpse into Japan’s rich and complex history through its museums and historical landmarks. Here are some places that every culture vulture should visit:

  • Tsuru City Local Museum: This museum provides a comprehensive historical overview of the city’s traditions, arts, and crafts. The building itself is a converted school, and it has a beautiful garden.
  • Gotemba Peace Park: This park is a tribute to the victims of the Atomic bomb. Located at the base of the iconic Mt. Fuji, the park has an inspiring peace museum and a Japanese garden.
  • Akatsuka Fujio Memorial Museum: Japan is known for its anime and manga, and Tsuru is part of that legacy. The Akatsuka Fujio Memorial Museum showcases the works of Fujio Akatsuka, the creator of the famous manga “Osomatsu-kun.”

Natural Attractions of Tsuru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tsuru is blessed with some of Japan’s most breathtaking natural scenery, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures to enjoy. Here are a few must-visit outdoor attractions:

  • Mt. Fuji Viewpoints: With its close proximity to Japan’s iconic mountain, Tsuru offers some fantastic viewpoints of Mt. Fuji. Make sure to visit the Fuji viewing platform in Funatsu park for a perfect backdrop for your travel memories.
  • Yamanashi Prefectural Forest Park: For nature lovers, the Yamanashi Prefectural Forest Park is a heaven. This 900-acre park has hiking trails, picnic spots, and scenic views of surrounding mountains like Mt. Fuji, the Southern Alps and Yatsugatake Mountains.
  • Yamanashi Agricultural Park: This agricultural park, located on the eastern side of Tsuru, offers a charming insight into Japan’s farming activities. Here visitors can learn about, hands-on activities of rice planting, fruit picking and other farming activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsuru

Tsuru is a perfect location for hiking or biking enthusiasts. If you’re visiting, make sure to check out these routes:

  • Tsuru-Miyota Road: This scenic route is excellent for bikers and car enthusiasts since the road runs between Tsuru and Miyota over the mountain pass giving fantastic views.
  • Mikuriya Pass Hiking Trail: This 6.8 km is a moderately difficult trail that connects with the Fujimi Pass trail. Peak foliage season is between late October and mid-November.
  • Shimokamo Hiking Trail: This hiking trail is a hidden gem in Tsuru for those who love nature. The trail is 5.5 km long, and it runs between Takami and Nobeyama Station and features amazing views of forests and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Tsuru: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours in Tsuru to keep you busy throughout your trip.

  • Hot Springs and Onsens: Tsuru has many Onsens (Japanese hot springs), and it is the perfect way to relax after a day of hiking and exploring the town.
  • Blackwater Rafting: Blackwater rafting inTsuru River is another popular activity. This is a unique Japanese river experience at night, where visitors can enjoy floating down a river illuminated by blue LED lights.
  • Agricultural Experience: Visitors can participate in various agricultural experiences in and around Tsuru, including picking fruits or vegetables on local farms, making soba noodles, or even trying making Japanese noodles from Buckwheat in the area.

Based on the above recommendations, here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Tsuru:

Food and Drink Scene of Tsuru

Must-Try Dishes

Tsuru has a vibrant food scene, with cuisine that is unique to the region. Here are some dishes you must try during your visit:

  • Houtou: Houtou is a popular noodle dish served in a thick miso soup with vegetables and meat.
  • Shingen Mochi: This is a traditional dessert that is a rice cake with a center of sweet chestnut paste and coated with kinako bean powder.
  • Local Wine and Sake: Yamanashi Prefecture is famous for its fruits production, including peaches, grapes, and blueberries. Hence Yamanashi sake and wines are famous across Japan.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, the area is famous for its Yamanashi wines and sake. Seemingly every town in Yamanashi has its winery, brewery or even a distillery. Some to try are:

  • Ishihara Brewery: Ishihara Brewery is situated in Tsuru and is known for producing fine traditional plum wines and Japanese sake.
  • Akigawa Winery: Akigawa winery produces various brandy and whiskey using Japanese Method, which is different from the traditional methods used in Europe.

Where to Stay in Tsuru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Tsuru offers various options for every budget. Some top hotels and guesthouses in Tsuru are:

  • Yumoto Fujiya Hotel: Yumoto Fujiya Hotel has a natural hot spring and a lush garden that offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Hotel Route Inn Court Tsuruoka Inter: This hotel has comfortable rooms with modern amenities and ideal for those planning to stay in tsuru for a longer duration.
  • Guest House Fujinoya: Guest House Fujinoya has budget-friendly guestrooms with access to a shared kitchen and a lovely courtyard garden.

What to Buy in Tsuru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tsuru is an excellent place to shop for souvenirs and unique gifts to bring home. Here are some recommendations:

  • Koshu Inden: Koshu Inden is a traditional craft project that makes wallets, handbags, and other high-quality accessories using deer skin and lacquerware techniques.
  • Yamanashi Wine and Sake: As mentioned earlier, Yamanashi Prefecture is known for its wine and sake production, and there are many opportunities to buy local wines and sake in Tsuru.
  • Soba Noodles: Soba noodles are a staple of Japanese cuisine, and visitors can purchase pre-made soba or even soba-making kits as souvenirs from local shops in Tsuru.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsuru

When visiting Tsuru, it’s important to respect Japanese customs and traditions. It’s customary to take off your shoes before entering someone’s home, temple or even some hotels. Visitors should also observe proper manners while using chopsticks, and should never stand chopsticks upwards in a bowl or share large dishes. When sitting in a Japanese styled restaurant, cross-legged sitting posture is appreciated. Lastly, please always respect the environment while camping, hiking or involve in other outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Tsuru

The best time to visit Tsuru is from March to May or September - November which has milder temperature making outdoor activities more comfortable. But autumn provides a further bonus of Peak Foliage towards the end of October and early November. Though summer also has its reward with Matsuri (festivals) in mid-august that run for two days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsuru

Do:

  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or temple
  • Respect the environment and dispose of waste responsibly

Don’t:

  • Avoid loud, obnoxious behavior
  • Don’t tip in restaurants. Tipping in Japan is not common.
  • Don’t smoke in non-smoking areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsuru

Tsuru is a safe destination, with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution while hiking and mountain climbing, especially during the winter season. It is always advisable to follow basic mountain rules and guidelines mentioned around hiking trails.

Health in Tsuru

It’s always advisable to have comprehensive travel health insurance when traveling to any foreign country. Japan has an excellent medical infrastructure, including quality hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. However, it’s always better to have a basic first aid kit in case you face minor injuries or allergies.

Getting to Tsuru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsuru

The nearest airports to Tsuru are Tokyo Haneda Airport and Narita International Airport. Both airports are well connected to Tsuru by bus, train, or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Tsuru

Tsuru has excellent transportation options for getting around town, including:

  • Tsuru Station: Tsuru Station the central railway station in southern Tsuru located on the Tokaido Main Line and the Minobu Line.
  • Gansu Bus terminal: This bus terminal serves routes to Shinjuku and Umeda amongst other major hubs.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Tsuru by rental car from various parts of Japan, including Takao and Hakone. Understand to have an international driver’s permit before driving anywhere in Japan.

How to Get Around in Tsuru

Tsuru has excellent public transport options and affordable taxis to help you get around the town. The Tsuru station serves as the central point for train travel in the southern part of Tsuru, which connects to additional train lines and bus networks.

Where to Go Next from Tsuru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tsuru is an excellent destination on its own. However, there are several attractive towns that visitors can explore in the surrounding region. A few notable destinations include:

  • Kawaguchiko: Visitors can take a bus from Tsuru to Kawaguchiko, known for its stunning lake and scenic views of Mt. Fuji.
  • Hakone: Hakone is another popular destination known for its hot springs, traditional inns, and a viewing platform of Mt. Fuji.
  • Yamanakako: Yamanakako is a popular location for water sports and boating and is an excellent spot to catch magnificent views of Mount Fuji from different angles.

Conclusion

Tsuru is a fantastic off-the-beaten-track destination in Japan. This town has a rich culture and history, amazing natural attractions, outdoor adventures, delicious food, and drinks, and is the ideal spot for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city. By following this comprehensive guide, you are sure to have an unforgettable experience.
