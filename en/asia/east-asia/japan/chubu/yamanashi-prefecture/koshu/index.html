Introduction to Koshu

Koshu is a beautiful city located in the Yamanashi Prefecture of Japan, known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a plethora of outdoor activities. The city is surrounded by mountains and is famous for its scenic vineyards, hot springs, and historic sites.

In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, the delicious food to eat, the must-do activities, and even provide you with some travel tips to help you plan your trip to Koshu.

List of Places to Visit in Koshu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  1. Katsunuma Winery - One of the oldest wineries in Japan.
  2. Yamanashi Prefectural Museum of Art - A must-visit place for art enthusiasts.
  3. Maizuru Castle Park - A beautiful park that is surrounded by stately Yamanashi castles, with a great view of the city from the top.
  4. Nakamura Shrine - A beautiful, traditional shrine that preserves the cultural heritage of Koshu.
  5. Kyosan Shojin Ryori House - A unique experience that allows you to enjoy authentic Japanese food inside a traditional house.

Natural Attractions of Koshu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Daibosatsu - One of the most beautiful mountains to climb in Japan, offering stunning views of the surrounding area.
  2. Lake Nakatsuna - A freshwater lake known for its beautiful scenery, fishing, and boating.
  3. Shosenkyo Gorge - A beautiful gorge with clear streams, beautiful waterfalls, hiking trails, and a cable car.
  4. Fuefukigawa Fruit Park - A beautiful park where you can pick and eat fresh fruit while enjoying the beautiful natural scenery of Koshu.
  5. Hottarakashi Onsen - A hot spring that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains while you soak and relax.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Koshu

  1. Katsunuma Trail - A beautiful trail that winds through Katsunuma’s vineyards and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Daibosatsu Pass Trail - A beautiful trail that takes you to the summit of Mount Daibosatsu, offering stunning views of the surrounding area.
  3. Tsuru-Notaki Falls - A beautiful waterfall located in Shosenkyo Gorge.
  4. Horai Bridge Trail - A beautiful trail that leads to Horai Bridge, with stunning views of Lake Suwa and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Koshu: Activities and Tours

  1. Koshu Wine Tasting Tour - A unique experience that allows you to sample some of the best Japanese wines, produced by local wineries.
  2. Cave Exploration Tour - A unique experience that takes you through the beautiful caves and limestone deposits of Koshu.
  3. Fruit Picking Tour - A fun family-friendly activity that allows you to pick and taste fresh fruit at Fuefukigawa Fruit Park.
  4. Sake Brewery Tour - Learn about the traditional methods of sake brewing and taste some of the finest Japanese sake at local breweries.

Food and Drink Scene of Koshu

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hoto Noodles - A hot pot meal made with hand-rolled udon noodles, vegetables, and meat in a miso broth.
  2. Basashi - Thin slices of raw horse meat are served with soy sauce and grated ginger.
  3. Houtou - Thick udon noodles with seasonal vegetables, meat, and a miso or soy sauce broth.
  4. Tsuke-soba - A cold noodle soup that comes with a dipping sauce of soy sauce and wasabi.
  5. Soba Noodles - Buckwheat noodles cooked in a savory broth with vegetables and meat, usually served hot.

Local Beverages

  1. Koshu Wine - A type of Japanese wine made from locally grown grapes.
  2. Sake - A traditional Japanese rice wine made from fermented rice.
  3. Ume-shu - A sweet liqueur made of Japanese plum and sugar soaked in Shochu or sake.

Where to Stay in Koshu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Yumura Onsen Hotel - A hot spring resort that offers traditional tatami rooms and Japanese-style dining.
  2. Izumiso - A traditional Japanese ryokan with hot springs, beautiful gardens, and traditional Japanese dining.
  3. Agriturismo Illugini - A unique farm stay that offers a peaceful retreat in the beautiful countryside and delicious Italian food.
  4. Villa Yatsugaoka - A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a great location, and a delicious breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Koshu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Yamanashi Wine - A variety of locally produced wines.
  2. Shingen-mochi - A type of traditional Japanese sweet that is made of mochi and filled with sweet red bean paste.
  3. Houtou Noodles - Dry packaged houtou noodles that you can take home and cook, as a souvenir.
  4. Japanese Plum Liqueur - Bottles of Ume-shu, a sweet liqueur made of Japanese plum and sugar soaked in sake or Shochu.

Local Culture and Customs of Koshu

The people of Koshu take great pride in their traditions and customs, and it is important to respect them when visiting. Remove your shoes when entering homes, temples, and shrines, and be sure to bow when greeting people. Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites. And, never eat or drink on public transportation.

Best Time to Visit Koshu

The best time to visit Koshu is in spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and comfortable. Spring offers the spectacle of Cherry blossoms, while autumn offers beautiful fall foliage. The winter season (December to February) is also a great time to visit if you are interested in winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Koshu

Do:

  1. Explore the vineyards and wineries in Koshu.
  2. Take a dip in the hot springs.
  3. Try the delicious local cuisine.
  4. Visit the traditional temples and shrines.
  5. Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Wear shoes inside people’s homes or religious sites.
  2. Eat or drink on public transportation.
  3. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  4. Make loud noises in public places.
  5. Touch the koi fish in the ponds and lakes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Koshu

Koshu is a very safe place to visit, and you can walk around the city at night without any concerns. However, you should always be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items with you.

Health in Koshu

Koshu has some of the cleanest water quality in Japan and is known for its fresh fruits and vegetables. It is recommended to drink bottled water if you have a sensitive stomach. You should also be careful while hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, and always have proper attire and equipment.

Getting to Koshu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Koshu

The nearest airport is Tokyo Haneda Airport, which is approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Koshu

Koshu has several bus stations offering transportation to all major cities in Japan.

Other Options

Koshu is easily accessible by train with a station in the center of the city. You can also rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Koshu

Public transportation, such as buses and trains are reliable and efficient, making it easy and convenient to get around in Koshu. Taxis are also available but can be a bit pricey. You can also rent a car and explore the surrounding areas at your leisure.

Where to Go Next from Koshu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Matsumoto - A beautiful Alpine city famous for its historic castle and beautiful gardens.
  2. Hakone - A popular hot spring resort town that offers stunning views of Mount Fuji.
  3. Kamikochi - A beautiful hiking area with stunning mountain views and waterfalls.
  4. Takayama - A historic city famous for its traditional Japanese houses, food, and sake.

Conclusion

Koshu is a beautiful and exciting city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a variety of fun and unique experiences for travelers to enjoy. We hope this guide has provided you with all the necessary information to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Koshu.
