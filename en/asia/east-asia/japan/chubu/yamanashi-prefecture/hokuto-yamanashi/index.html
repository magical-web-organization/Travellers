Introduction to Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Hokuto is a city located in Yamanashi, a prefecture in Chubu, Japan. It is surrounded by beautiful mountains and offers breathtaking views all year round. Hokuto’s natural beauty coupled with its rich cultural heritage make it a destination that should not be missed during your visit to Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Daizaifu Tenmangu Shrine: Built in the 9th century, Daizaifu Tenmangu Shrine is dedicated to the god of learning and attracts students from all over Japan who come to pray for success in their studies.
  • Enzan City Museum of History: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Enzan area. It also has a collection of artifacts dating back to the Jomon period.
  • Katsunuma-budokyo: Katsunuma-budokyo is a place known for producing grapes and wine. The area has several vineyards where visitors can take part in wine tastings and learn about wine-making.

Natural Attractions of Hokuto (Yamanashi): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Yatsugatake: Mount Yatsugatake is a beautiful mountain range that offers stunning views of the city below. Visitors can hike the trails to the summit or take the cable car for a more scenic route.
  • Shosenkyo Gorge: Shosenkyo Gorge is a popular hiking spot with a beautiful waterfall and scenery.
  • Lake Suwa: Located near Hokuto, Lake Suwa is the largest lake in Nagano prefecture. It is a popular spot for fishing and boating during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hokuto (Yamanashi)

  • Yatsugatake Nature Hiking Trail: This trail is a 4km hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Sawada Pass: This is a popular route for mountain bikers and hikers.
  • Ogasawara Pass: The Ogasawara Pass trail takes visitors through a forest with beautiful views of the city and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Hokuto (Yamanashi): Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting at Katsunuma-budokyo: Take a tour of the vineyards and learn about wine-making, then enjoy a tasting of some of the best local wines.
  • Hot Springs: Hokuto has several outdoor hot springs perfect for relaxing after a long day of exploring.
  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and get a bird’s eye view of Hokuto’s natural beauty with a paragliding tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Houtou: A dish made from flat udon noodles and vegetables in a soup broth.
  • Salmon: Hokuto is well known for its salmon, which you can find in many local restaurants.
  • Fruit: Hokuto is famous for its grapes, peaches and other fruits, which are grown in the area and are available at local markets.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Hokuto is known for its wine, which is produced in the Katsunuma-budokyo area.
  • Sake: Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic beverage made from rice. It is brewed in several places in Hokuto and is available at local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Hokuto (Yamanashi): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hokuto Kogen Hotel: A hotel located in the mountains with stunning views of the city.
  • Ryokan Fujitomita: A traditional Japanese-style inn with hot springs.
  • Hotel Route-Inn Court Hokuto: A budget-friendly hotel located close to the city center.

What to Buy in Hokuto (Yamanashi): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: As mentioned earlier, Hokuto is famous for its wine, which makes for a perfect souvenir or gift.
  • Fruit: Freshly-picked grapes and peaches make for delicious and healthy souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: Hokuto has several shops that sell traditional Japanese crafts, such as pottery and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Hokuto is a traditional Japanese city, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Bowing when greeting others, offering gifts and removing shoes when entering homes are some common customs in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Hokuto (Yamanashi)

The best time to visit Hokuto is during the autumn months when the leaves change color, and the views of the surrounding mountains become more stunning. Spring is also a great time to visit when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Take in the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind
  • disrespect local customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Hokuto is a safe city, and violent crimes are rare. However, visitors should be mindful of their belongings and avoid walking alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, as obtaining certain medications in Japan can be difficult. The water in Hokuto is safe to drink, so visitors do not need to worry about dehydration.

Getting to Hokuto (Yamanashi): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hokuto (Yamanashi)

The nearest airport to Hokuto is Tokyo International Airport (Haneda), which is approximately 2 hours away by train.

Bus Stations Near Hokuto (Yamanashi)

Hokuto Bus Terminal is located in the city center and is the main bus station in Hokuto, offering connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Hokuto, with several train stations located throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Hokuto (Yamanashi)

The best way to explore Hokuto is by car, as it offers the most flexibility and allows visitors to see more of the surrounding areas. Alternatively, visitors can take public transport, such as the local bus or train, to travel around the city and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Hokuto (Yamanashi): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Matsumoto: A city located in Nagano prefecture, known for its stunning medieval castle.
  • Lake Kawaguchi: A beautiful lake located in Yamanashi prefecture, surrounded by stunning natural scenery.
  • Mount Fuji: An iconic symbol of Japan, Mount Fuji is located in Yamanashi prefecture and is a popular destination for car enthusiasts and hikers.

Conclusion

Hokuto is a destination that offers something for everyone, from its stunning natural beauty to its rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Hokuto will leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
