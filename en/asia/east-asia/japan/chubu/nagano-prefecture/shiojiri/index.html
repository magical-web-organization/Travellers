Introduction to Shiojiri

Located in the heart of Nagano Prefecture, Shiojiri is a small city filled with history, culture, and scenic beauty. Surrounded by the Japanese Alps, it offers visitors a chance to explore the mountains, hike in the valleys, and experience Japanese culture in a unique and authentic way. It’s also home to a variety of museums, shrines, and local shops, making it one of the most interesting destinations in the area. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll introduce you to the best things to do, see, and taste in Shiojiri.

List of Places to Visit in Shiojiri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shiojiri Station – This station is known for its beautiful architecture and is a famous spot for photographers who come to take pictures of the unique roof design.
  • Shiki no Yakata – This building is home to the Shiojiri City Historical Museum where visitors can learn about the history and culture of the region.
  • Nakaide-Machi Stonemason Museum – This museum showcases the local tradition of stonemasonry, originating from a time when the town was known as “the town of stone.”
  • Takada Shingen Historical Museum – This museum is located in the Takada family’s former residence and exhibits historical materials related to the city’s history.
  • Shiojiri Nature Museum – This museum showcases the natural environment of Shiojiri, including its unique flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Shiojiri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kiso River – This river is a great spot for fishing or taking a stroll along its banks.
  • Daio Wasabi Farm – Known as the largest wasabi farm in Japan, it’s a unique experience to see how wasabi is grown and visit the charming shops, restaurants, and museums on the grounds.
  • Shiota Onsen – This hot spring resort is located in the heart of nature and offers visitors a chance to relax and unwind.
  • Mt. Jonen – This mountain boasts one of the highest peaks in the region and offers hikers a challenging but rewarding climb, with a magnificent view over the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shiojiri

  • Matsumoto-Kanazawa Cycle Route – This route stretches from Matsumoto in Nagano to Kanazawa in Ishikawa and is a popular destination for biking enthusiasts.
  • Nanakura Mountain Trail – This trail is a great opportunity to explore the scenic beauty of the Japanese Alps and offers stunning view points of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Mt. Hoken Traverse – This trail is only for experienced hikers and offers stunning views of the Japanese Alps and the city of Shiojiri.

Best Things to Do in Shiojiri: Activities and Tours

  • Soba Making Workshop – Learn how to make soba, one of the most popular Japanese noodles, and taste your creations.
  • Shiojiri Bike Tour – Join a guided bike tour through Shiojiri and discover the city’s hidden gems.
  • Japanese Calligraphy Lesson – Learn the art of Japanese calligraphy and create your own unique piece of art.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shiojiri

Food and Drink Scene of Shiojiri

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shinshu Salmon – A local specialty, this salmon is grilled to perfection and served with a side of wasabi.
  • Soba Noodles – Shiojiri is famous for its soba noodles, which are made from local buckwheat and served with a variety of toppings.
  • Hoba Miso – A traditional dish from the region made with miso paste, mushrooms, and other local ingredients, all grilled on a magnolia leaf.

Local Beverages

  • Shinshu Sake – A famous local sake, known for its unique flavor and high quality.
  • Jaju – A traditional Japanese beverage made from local fruits and vegetables, often mixed with local spirits.

Where to Stay in Shiojiri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shiojiri Station Hotel – A convenient hotel located next to the train station, making it easy to explore the surrounding area.
  • Shiojiri City Hotel – A budget-friendly option located in the city center, close to many attractions and restaurants.
  • Seiryu no Umi – A traditional Japanese inn with excellent hot spring facilities and beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Shiojiri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shiojiri Stone Products – Take home a unique stone memento from the city known as “the town of stone.”
  • Shinshu Sake – Grab a bottle of the famous local sake and enjoy it with friends back home.
  • Kiso Lacquerware – Handcrafted lacquerware made in the neighboring city of Kiso is a popular souvenir choice.

Local Culture and Customs of Shiojiri

  • Yamato Shiki Festival – An annual festival in early February to honor the historic samurai leader, Takada Shingen.
  • Obon Festival – An annual festival in August to honor ancestors and loved ones who have passed away, with traditional cultural events and dances.

Best Time to Visit Shiojiri

The best time to visit Shiojiri is in the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and the colors of nature are at their best. From late September to early November, is an especially beautiful time to visit for the autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shiojiri

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as taking off shoes before entering someone’s home or a sacred space.
  • Try the local food and drinks, which are unique to the region.
  • Follow the rules and regulations when hiking in the mountains.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud or boisterous in public places, as it’s frowned upon in Japanese culture.
  • Take photos of people without permission, especially during local festivals or ceremonies.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment when hiking or exploring the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shiojiri

Shiojiri is a very safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Shiojiri

There are no specific health concerns in Shiojiri. Visitors are advised to take standard precautions such as drinking bottled water, washing hands regularly, and seeking medical treatment if any medical issue arises.

Getting to Shiojiri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shiojiri

The nearest airport to Shiojiri is Matsumoto Airport, which is about 30 minutes away by car. Narita International Airport in Tokyo is another option that offers international flights with a direct bus service to Shiojiri.

Bus Stations Near Shiojiri

Shiojiri Station is a major bus terminal that connects to several major cities in Japan, including Nagano, Tokyo, and Osaka.

Other Options

For those who prefer driving, Shiojiri is easily accessible from the main highways that run through the region. Car rental is available at both Matsumoto and Nagoya airports.

How to Get Around in Shiojiri

The city is small enough to explore on foot or by bike. Alternatively, public transportation such as trains and buses are available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Shiojiri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Matsumoto – A city famous for its castle, museums, and stunning views of the Japanese Alps.
  • Kiso Valley – A scenic valley known for its historic post towns, hiking trails, and local hospitality.
  • Nagano – A popular destination known for its winter sports, hot springs, and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Shiojiri is a city filled with natural and cultural attractions that make it a great destination for travelers seeking to explore the heart of Japan. From hiking to hot springs to traditional festivals and local cuisine, there’s plenty to see, taste, and experience in Shiojiri. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your itinerary and make the most of your trip to this unique destination.
