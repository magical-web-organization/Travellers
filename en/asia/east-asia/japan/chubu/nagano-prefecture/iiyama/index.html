Introduction to Iiyama

If you’re planning a trip to Japan and looking for something off the beaten path, Iiyama is the perfect destination. Located in Nagano prefecture, this charming city offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and practical tips for a memorable trip to Iiyama.

List of Places to Visit in Iiyama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Iiyama has a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. Visit the Iiyama Castle Ruins, which dates back to the 16th century and offers breathtaking views of the city. Learn about local folklore and traditions at the Takahashi Korekiyo Memorial Museum, which is dedicated to the life of the famous politician who was born in Iiyama. Another must-visit place is the Iiyama City Museum, which showcases the region’s history, art, and culture.

Natural Attractions of Iiyama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts will find plenty to see and do in Iiyama. Take a stroll through the Nanohana Park, which is famous for its beautiful flowers in spring. The Togakushi Forest Botanical Garden is another stunning natural attraction that is worth a visit. For stunning panoramic views of the city and its surroundings, head to the top of Mt. Madarao or Mt. Takahara.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iiyama

There are many scenic hiking and biking routes in and around Iiyama. The Madarao Kogen Trail is a popular hiking route that offers breathtaking views of the city and beyond. Another hike worth exploring is the Togakushi Shrine Hike, which takes you through forests, shrines, and stunning mountain vistas. For a more leisurely activity, rent a bike and explore the scenic countryside around Iiyama.

Best Things to Do in Iiyama: Activities and Tours

Iiyama has a wide array of activities and tours to offer. During winter, visitors can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing at the Madarao Kogen Ski Resort, one of the largest ski resorts in the area. In the summer, visitors can try their hand at river rafting on the Chikuma River, or take a hot air balloon ride over the city. For a more unique experience, visit the Iiyama Snow Hut Village, where you can dine in a traditional Japanese snow hut.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iiyama

Day 1: Explore the Iiyama Castle Ruins and Takahashi Korekiyo Memorial Museum Day 2: Hike to the top of Mt. Madarao or Mt. Takahara Day 3: Visit the Iiyama City Museum and Nanohana Park Day 4: Ski or snowshoe at the Madarao Kogen Ski Resort Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby Togakushi Shrine Hike or Zenko-ji Temple in Nagano City Day 6: Go river rafting on the Chikuma River Day 7: Relax and explore local cafes and shops in Iiyama City

Food and Drink Scene of Iiyama

Must-Try Dishes

Iiyama is famous for its soba noodles, which are made from locally grown buckwheat. Don’t miss the chance to try the Ni-hachi soba, which is made from a mixture of buckwheat and wheat flour. Another local specialty is oyaki, which is a steamed bun filled with various fillings such as mushroom, pumpkin, and red bean paste. For a unique dining experience, visit the Daio Wasabi Farm, where you can try wasabi ice cream and other wasabi-infused dishes.

Local Beverages

The Niigata and Nagano regions are famous for their sake, and Iiyama is no exception. Don’t miss the chance to try the local sake brands such as Miyama Nishiki and Iiyama Bijin. Another popular local beverage is apple juice, which is made from locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Iiyama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Iiyama has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. For a traditional Japanese experience, try staying in a ryokan, which is a Japanese-style inn that offers tatami-matted rooms, futons, and communal baths. Some of the popular ryokans in Iiyama include Sekizenkan and Genjikoh. For budget travelers, there are many guesthouses and hostels available.

What to Buy in Iiyama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to take back home, there are plenty of options in Iiyama. Some of the popular items include locally crafted pottery, handmade woodwork, and traditional Japanese textiles such as tsumugi and kasuri. Don’t forget to stock up on locally grown produce such as apples and buckwheat noodles.

Local Culture and Customs of Iiyama

Iiyama has a rich cultural heritage that is worth experiencing. Respect for elders and community harmony are important values in Japanese culture, and visitors are expected to follow these customs. If you’re visiting during a festival or event, don’t miss the chance to witness local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Iiyama

Iiyama can be visited all year round, but the best time depends on your preferences. Winter is the peak season for skiing and snowboarding, while spring is the best time to see cherry blossoms and other flowers in bloom. Summer is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking and river rafting, while fall offers stunning autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iiyama

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be polite and courteous towards locals
  • Try local cuisine and specialties

Don’t:

  • Ignore local etiquette and customs
  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces
  • Forget to take off your shoes before entering a Japanese-style room

Safety and Health

Safety in Iiyama

Iiyama is a safe and welcoming city, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take normal precautions such as avoiding dark alleys at night and being aware of their surroundings. In case of an emergency, dial 110 for the police and 119 for medical assistance.

Health in Iiyama

There are no major health risks in Iiyama, but visitors are advised to take basic precautions such as drinking bottled water and using sunscreen. In case of a medical emergency, there are hospitals and clinics available in the city.

Getting to Iiyama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iiyama

The closest airport to Iiyama is Narita International Airport, which is located approximately 250 kilometers away. From Narita, visitors can take a train or bus to reach Iiyama.

Bus Stations Near Iiyama

Iiyama is well-connected to other cities in the region via bus. The Iiyama Bus Terminal is located near the train station and offers convenient connections to Nagano, Tokyo, and other destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Iiyama by train. The Hokuriku Shinkansen connects Iiyama with major cities such as Tokyo, Nagano, and Kanazawa. Once you arrive in Iiyama, getting around the city is best done by rental car or public transportation.

How to Get Around in Iiyama

Getting around Iiyama is easy and convenient. Visitors can rent a car from one of the many rental agencies in the city or use public transportation such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Iiyama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions from Iiyama that are worth exploring. The nearby city of Nagano offers stunning temples, shrines, and museums, as well as fantastic food and shopping. The Kurobe Gorge Railway offers a scenic train ride through stunning mountain scenery, while the Onsen town of Yamanouchi offers a relaxing hot spring experience.

Conclusion

Iiyama offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now ready to plan your trip to this charming city and experience everything it has to offer.
