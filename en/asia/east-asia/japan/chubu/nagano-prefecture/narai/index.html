Narai is a charming village located in the heart of Japan’s Nagano prefecture. Situated amidst the lush landscape of central Japan, Narai is a picturesque location that attracts tourists with its serene beauty, cultural richness and adventurous spirit. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, a history buff or just seeking some peace, Narai has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll walk you through the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, local culture, safety, getting there and nearby destinations in Narai.

Introduction to Narai

Narai is a picturesque village nestled in the mountains of Nagano prefecture in Japan. Known for its many traditional Japanese-style houses, Narai provides a glimpse into the country’s ancient past. The village’s narrow streets, charming wooden buildings, historic inns, and hamlets give visitors a real sense of Japan’s traditional way of life. Narai attracts tourists throughout the year but is especially beautiful during autumn when the landscape explodes into a riot of colors.

List of Places to Visit in Narai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Narai’s rich cultural heritage is evident from the many museums and historical places dotted around the village. The following are some of the top attractions:

  • Narai-juku Honjin Museum: This museum, housed in a 19th-century lodging house, presents exhibits detailing the daily life of travelers along the Nakasendo highway during the Edo period (1603-1868).
  • Naraijuku Nakasendo Museum: This museum showcases the famed Nakasendo highway that once connected Kyoto and Edo (Tokyo). Visitors can learn about the history of the ancient route and enjoy the many exhibitions related to the Edo period.

Natural Attractions of Narai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Narai is a paradise for nature lovers, with its many beautiful parks and outdoor attractions. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Senjojiki Cirque: This breathtaking gorge is located just a few kilometers outside the village and is widely regarded as one of the most spectacular sights in the region.
  • Magome Pass: One of the most popular hiking trails in the area, the Magome Pass connects Narai with nearby Magome, a beautiful village that’s been preserved in its Edo period (1603-1868) layout.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narai

Apart from Magome Pass, which is suitable for both hiking and biking, Narai has several other scenic routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. These include:

  • Yabuhara Kogen Nature Trail: This hiking trail is an excellent way to explore the area’s plant and animal life, as well as enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Narai Pass: This ancient pass once led travelers to Narai from the neighboring provinces. Today, it makes for a beautiful hike, especially during autumn when the panorama of fall leaves is at its best.

Best Things to Do in Narai: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions and museums, Narai offers tourists a chance to experience the village’s unique culture and way of life. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony: Visitors can experience the traditional Japanese tea ceremony in an authentic setting, complete with tea houses and tatami mats.
  • Paper Lamp Making: Narai is famous for its beautiful paper lamps, and tourists can create their paper lamps in a workshop run by local residents.

Week-Long Itinerary for Narai

To explore the best of what Narai has to offer, we recommend a week-long itinerary that includes:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the village’s architecture and museums.
  • Day 3: Hike the Magome Pass.
  • Day 4: Take a nature walk along the Yabuhara Kogen Nature Trail.
  • Day 5-6: Experience the local culture through tea ceremonies and paper lamp making.
  • Day 7: Relax and take in the beautiful scenery of Narai.

Food and Drink Scene of Narai

No visit to Narai is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. The village offers visitors a chance to sample traditional Japanese dishes made from fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shinshu soba: A popular noodle soup that’s a staple of Nagano prefecture. The dish is made using locally sourced buckwheat noodles and is served with a variety of side dishes.
  • Hoba miso: A local dish consisting of miso paste served with grilled vegetables, mushrooms, and meat. It’s served on a popular local leaf called “hoba.”

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Made using locally produced rice, the sake in Narai is famous for its unique taste and aroma.
  • Hiraya-Shuppan: This locally produced wine is made using grapes grown on the hillsides around the village.

Where to Stay in Narai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

To make the most of your visit, we recommend staying in one of the traditional Japanese inns in the village. Here are some of the best options:

  • Naraijuku Oyado Senjukaku: A traditional Japanese inn, offering rooms with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Yuyado Chobo-so: A traditional Japanese inn that provides guests with a chance to experience authentic Japanese life.

What to Buy in Narai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Narai is famous for its traditional wooden crafts, paper lamps, and local foods. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  • Narai wooden crafts: These beautiful wooden crafts made by local artisans are a must-buy for anyone interested in traditional Japanese crafts.
  • Paper lamps: These beautiful paper lamps, which are made using traditional Japanese techniques, are another popular souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Narai

Narai is a village that’s proud of its unique culture and heritage. Visitors are expected to respect the traditional way of life and follow local customs, such as removing shoes before entering a house or temple.

Best Time to Visit Narai

The best time to visit Narai is during autumn when the leaves on the trees turn into a riot of colors, creating a picturesque backdrop for the village’s historic buildings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narai

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the villagers.
  • Enjoy the beautiful scenery and nature walks.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Enter private homes or temples without permission.
  • Litter or damage the village’s historic buildings and structures.
  • Disturb the peace and tranquility of the village.

Safety and Health

Safety in Narai

Narai is a very safe village, with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take basic precautions and be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Narai

Narai is a clean and healthy village, with no major health risks. However, visitors should take precautions against mosquito bites and wear protective clothing to avoid heatstroke during summer.

Getting to Narai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Narai

The closest airport to Narai is Matsumoto Airport, which is located about 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Narai

Visitors can take a bus to Narai from Matsumoto station or Nagano station.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or drive to Narai.

How to Get Around in Narai

The best way to explore Narai is on foot. The village is compact and easily navigable, and walking allows visitors to take in the stunning scenery and charming architecture of the traditional Japanese houses.

Where to Go Next from Narai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Narai is the perfect base for exploring other parts of Nagano. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Matsumoto City: Famous for its castle and art museum, Matsumoto City is an excellent destination for a day trip from Narai.
  • Kiso Valley: A beautiful valley that offers visitors a chance to experience traditional Japanese life in the countryside.

Conclusion

Narai is a charming and picturesque village that captures the essence of traditional Japanese life. From its historic buildings and museums to its beautiful natural landscapes, Narai offers visitors a chance to experience Japan’s rich cultural heritage and stunning beauty. We hope this travel guide helps you make the most of your visit to this enchanting part of Japan.
