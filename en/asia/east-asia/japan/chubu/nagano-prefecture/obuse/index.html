Introduction to Obuse

Located in Nagano prefecture, Obuse is a small town that’s rich in art, culture, and natural beauty. It’s a popular tourist destination for both international and domestic visitors. Obuse is known for its traditional Japanese architecture, world-class museums, fascinating history, and numerous hiking trails.

List of Places to Visit in Obuse

Here are some of Obuse’s most popular attractions, divided into two categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hokusaikan Museum: a museum dedicated to the works of the famous Japanese painter, Hokusai.
  • Nakajima Chinami Hall: a restored 19th-century traditional Japanese house.
  • Obuse Museum and Nakajima Chinami Gallery: showcases works of locally born artists and craftsmen.
  • Gansho-in Temple: an elegant temple with delicate Japanese-style gardens.
  • Takai Kozan Memorial Museum: displays the works of Takai Kozan, a renowned calligrapher, and painter.

Natural Attractions of Obuse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hokusai Forest Walk: a forested trail that features nine carved Hokusai wood panels.
  • Obuse Town Bike Path: a scenic bike path that crosses the Chikuma River.
  • Obuse Gorge: a stunning natural formation that can be reached via a short walking trail.
  • Mt. Kurohime: a picturesque mountain that offers a challenging hike and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Obuse

Obuse is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Besides the biking path already mentioned, there are several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the town and surrounding mountains. Some popular routes include Obuse Gorge, Hokusai Forest Walk, and the hiking trails on Mt. Kurohime.

Best Things to Do in Obuse: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Museums: Hokusaikan Museum, Obuse Museum and Nakajima Chinami Gallery, and Takai Kozan Memorial Museum.
  • Explore the Town’s Historical Sites: Nakajima Chinami Hall, Gansho-in Temple, and Zenshoji Temple.
  • Experience Nature: Obuse Gorge and Hokusai Forest Walk.
  • Attend Local Festivals: Obuse City Wine Festival and Obuse Yakishime Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Obuse

Obuse is known for its food, particularly its soba (buckwheat noodles) and chestnuts. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Obuse Soba: a famous soba dish that’s a local specialty.
  • Kuri Kinton: a traditional Japanese dessert made of mashed chestnuts and sugar.
  • Okowa: a sticky rice dish that’s steamed with different flavors and toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Obuse Brewery: a local brewery that produces craft beers and sake.
  • Shinshu Obuse Winery: a winery that produces quality wines using locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Obuse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Obuse offers various affordable and comfortable accommodations for a great stay. Some popular options include

  • Toboso: a cozy, traditional-style ryokan (Japanese inn).
  • Hotel Kokusai 21: a modern hotel that offers comfortable Western-style rooms.
  • Obuse-no-Kaze Youth Hostel: a budget-friendly option that offers wholesome meals and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Obuse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Obuse is famous for its high-quality handicrafts, particularly its pottery and soba noodles. Some popular souvenirs include

  • Hokusai woodblock prints
  • Local sake and wine
  • Chestnut sweets and desserts
  • Ceramic pottery and porcelain

Local Culture and Customs of Obuse

Obuse is a conservative town, so it’s important to dress modestly and respectfully when visiting shrines or temples. Smoking in public spaces and eating while walking is generally frowned upon. It’s common courtesy to bow when greeting people or showing respect.

Best Time to Visit Obuse

Obuse is beautiful in every season, but the best time to visit is from spring to fall. The cherry blossoms in spring, the foliage in autumn, and the comfortable weather make it ideal for visitors to take hikes and bike tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Obuse

Do:

  • Try the local food, especially soba noodles and chestnut sweets.
  • Explore the numerous temples and museums.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public spaces or eat while walking.
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing when visiting shrines or temples.
  • Be loud and unruly in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Obuse

Obuse is a generally safe town. However, travelers should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing, can happen in crowded tourist areas. It’s essential to follow safety protocols when hiking, biking, or exploring natural areas.

Health in Obuse

Obuse and Japan have a world-class healthcare system. Visitors with pre-existing conditions should ensure to bring sufficient medication and bring the appropriate health insurance.

Getting to Obuse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Obuse is easily accessible via different transportation modes.

Airports Near Obuse

The nearest airport is Matsumoto Airport, which is about 25 kilometers from Obuse. From there, visitors can take a taxi or shuttle bus, which takes about 30 to 40 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Obuse

Obuse Bus Terminal is conveniently located near the town center, offering local and regional bus services under the Nagaden bus company.

Other Options

Visitors coming from Tokyo or Nagoya can take the shinkansen (bullet train) to Nagano station, then take a local train or bus to get to Obuse.

How to Get Around in Obuse

Obuse is small enough to explore on foot or by bicycle. Renting a bike at a local bike shop is a popular way to get around. Taxis are also available around the town, and the train and bus services run regularly.

Where to Go Next from Obuse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yamanouchi: home to the famous snow monkeys and hot springs
  • Matsumoto: a scenic castle town that’s a gateway to the Northern Japan Alps
  • Takayama: a preserved town with a distinctive traditional Japanese style

Conclusion

Obuse is a hidden gem in the Nagano prefecture that offers unique and unforgettable experiences to visitors. With its rich cultural heritage, picturesque surroundings, and welcoming locals, it’s no wonder why both locals and foreigners alike keep coming back to this lovely town.
