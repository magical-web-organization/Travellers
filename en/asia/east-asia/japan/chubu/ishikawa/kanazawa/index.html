Introduction to Kanazawa

Kanazawa is a hidden gem in Ishikawa, located in the Chubu region of Japan. Known as “Little Kyoto,” it’s one of the best-preserved castle towns in Japan. Kanazawa is home to many cultural and historical attractions, which make it a popular destination for both local and international tourists. The city’s food scene is also well-regarded, with many local specialties that you won’t find anywhere else in Japan. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, activities to do, food to eat, accommodations, and transportation options in Kanazawa.

List of Places to Visit in Kanazawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Sites

Kanazawa Castle

Kanazawa Castle, also known as Kanazawa-jō, was originally built in the 16th century and has undergone various renovations throughout the years. While much of the original castle was destroyed, there are still a few structures, such as the Ishikawamon Gate and Sanjikken Nagaya, that offer a glimpse into the past. The castle also boasts a beautiful garden that is worth visiting.

Kenrokuen Garden

Kenrokuen Garden is one of Japan’s best-known gardens and is a must-visit when in Kanazawa. The garden is beautifully landscaped, featuring a pond, streams, teahouses, and many rare plants and trees. It’s especially stunning during the spring (when the cherry blossoms bloom) and the fall (when the leaves change color).

21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art

This museum is home to many contemporary art exhibits and installations. It’s a great place to visit if you’re interested in contemporary art or just want to see something different from the city’s traditional attractions.

Higashi Chaya District

The Higashi Chaya district is a well-preserved geisha district in Kanazawa. It’s a great place to stroll around, with many traditional teahouses and shops lining the narrow streets.

Omicho Market

Omicho Market is a busy and colorful market that has been around for over 300 years. You’ll find fresh seafood, produce, and local specialties here. It’s a great place to visit if you want to sample some of Kanazawa’s famous seafood, such as crab.

Natural Attractions of Kanazawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mt. Utatsu

Located just outside of the city, Mt. Utatsu is a popular hiking spot with stunning views of the city below. It takes about an hour to hike to the summit, but the views are well worth it.

Noto Peninsula

The Noto Peninsula is a scenic region just north of Kanazawa, known for its rugged coastline and beautiful beaches. It’s a great place to visit if you want to enjoy some outdoor activities, such as hiking, swimming, or cycling.

Myoryu-ji Temple (Ninja Temple)

While not technically a natural attraction, Myoryu-ji Temple (also known as the Ninja Temple) is worth a visit for its unique architecture and secret passages. The temple was built in the 16th century and has many hidden doors, trapdoors, and hidden rooms.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kanazawa

Hiking Routes

In addition to Mt. Utatsu, there are many other hiking trails in and around Kanazawa that offer great views of the city and its surrounding countryside. Some of the most popular hiking routes include the Kazue-machi Chaya District Course and the Kanazawajo Course. Both of these routes offer a mix of urban and natural scenery.

Biking Routes

Kanazawa is a very bike-friendly city, with many bike rental shops and designated bike lanes. There are many scenic bike routes in the city, such as the Asanogawa Cycling Road and the Kanazawa Bay Area Cycling Road. Biking is a great way to explore the city and its surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Kanazawa: Activities and Tours

Gold Leaf Experience

Kanazawa is one of Japan’s top gold leaf producers, and you can experience firsthand how it’s made by taking a gold leaf workshop at one of the city’s many studios.

Sake Brewery Tours

Kanazawa is also known for its sake production. You can take a tour of a local brewery, learn about the brewing process, and sample different types of sake.

Kanazawa Samurai District Tour

The Kanazawa Samurai District is a well-preserved neighborhood that is home to many samurai residences. You can take a guided tour of the district and learn about the life and culture of these famous warriors.

Nighttime Food Tour

Kanazawa’s food scene is worth exploring, and what better way to do it than by taking a nighttime food tour? You’ll get to try many local specialties and learn about the city’s culinary history.

Food and Drink Scene of Kanazawa

Must-Try Dishes

Kanazawa Curry

Kanazawa curry is a local specialty that is similar to Japanese curry but has a unique taste. It’s made with high-quality beef, vegetables, and spices, and is often served with rice or udon noodles.

Kaga Vegetables

Kaga vegetables are a type of specialty vegetable grown in Kanazawa, known for their unique taste and texture. They’re often served in salads or as a side dish in local restaurants.

Jibuni

Jibuni is a traditional dish made with chicken, vegetables, and wheat gluten. It’s often served in a hot pot and is perfect for cold winter nights.

Local Beverages

Kanazawa Sake

Kanazawa is famous for its sake production, and you can find many local varieties in restaurants and bars throughout the city.

Kaga Green Tea

Kaga green tea is a type of high-quality green tea that is grown in the Kaga region of Kanazawa. It has a delicate flavor and is often served with traditional Japanese sweets.

Where to Stay in Kanazawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kanazawa Tokyu Hotel

The Kanazawa Tokyu Hotel is a modern and comfortable hotel located in the heart of the city. It’s just a few minutes’ walk from many of the city’s top attractions.

Good Neighbors Hostel

Good Neighbors Hostel is a popular budget option located in the Higashi Chaya district. It’s a great place to meet other travelers and has a cozy and friendly atmosphere.

Kanazawa Guesthouse Pongyi

Kanazawa Guesthouse Pongyi is a traditional Japanese guesthouse located in the city center. It has tatami rooms and a communal kitchen, making it a great choice for travelers looking for an authentic Japanese experience.

What to Buy in Kanazawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gold Leaf Products

Kanazawa is one of Japan’s top gold leaf producers, and you can find many products made with gold leaf in the city’s shops and markets. Some popular items include gold leaf-covered pottery, jewelry, and stationery.

Kaga Yuzen Silk Products

Kaga Yuzen is a traditional dyeing technique used to create beautiful silk fabrics. You can find many products made with Kaga Yuzen silk in Kanazawa, such as scarves, kimonos, and wall hangings.

Kutani Pottery

Kutani pottery is a type of ceramics that originated in Ishikawa prefecture. You can find many Kutani pottery shops in Kanazawa, selling everything from teacups to vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanazawa

Kanazawa has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions when visiting. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Bowing is a common greeting in Japan, and it’s customary to bow when meeting someone for the first time.
  • Shoes should be removed before entering someone’s home, a temple, or a traditional restaurant.
  • Tipping is not customary in Japan, and can sometimes be seen as rude.
  • It’s important to be respectful when visiting temples and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kanazawa

The best time to visit Kanazawa is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and the cherry blossoms or fall foliage are in full bloom. However, Kanazawa is beautiful year-round, and there’s something to do and see no matter what time of year you visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanazawa

Do:

  • Take your shoes off before entering a temple or restaurant.
  • Bow when meeting someone for the first time.
  • Explore the city on foot or by bike.
  • Try the local food and sake.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Skimp on tipping - it’s not customary in Japan.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanazawa

Kanazawa is a safe city, with low levels of crime. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Kanazawa

Japan has a very high standard of healthcare, and there are many hospitals and clinics in Kanazawa. It’s a good idea to have travel insurance in case you need medical treatment while visiting.

Getting to Kanazawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kanazawa

The nearest airport to Kanazawa is Komatsu Airport, which is about a 40-minute bus or train ride from the city center. There are several daily flights from Tokyo and other major Japanese cities.

Bus Stations Near Kanazawa

Kanazawa is well-connected by bus to other cities in Japan, with several bus companies offering regular services to and from the city. The Kanazawa Bus Terminal is located in the city center, making it easy to get around.

Other Options

You can also reach Kanazawa from Tokyo by train. The journey takes about 2.5 hours on the Hokuriku Shinkansen line. There are also limited express trains that run from other major cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Kanazawa

Kanazawa is a very walkable city, with many of the city’s top attractions within walking distance of each other. Biking is also a great way to get around, with many bike rental shops available. There are also buses and taxis available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Kanazawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Takayama

Takayama is a historic city located in the mountains of Gifu prefecture. It’s known for its well-preserved old town and traditional festivals. It’s about a 2.5-hour train ride from Kanazawa.

Shirakawa-go

Shirakawa-go is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its traditional thatched-roof houses. It’s located in the mountains of Gifu prefecture, about a 1.5-hour bus ride from Kanazawa.

Kyoto

Kyoto is one of Japan’s top tourist destinations, known for its many temples, shrines, and traditional gardens. It’s about a 2.5-hour train ride from Kanazawa.

Conclusion

Kanazawa is a beautiful and charming city with a lot to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, food, or the outdoors, you’re sure to find something that interests you here. We hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Kanazawa, and that you have a wonderful time exploring this gem of a city.
