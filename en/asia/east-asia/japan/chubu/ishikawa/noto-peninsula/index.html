Introduction to Noto Peninsula

Located on the northwestern coast of Honshu Island, Noto Peninsula is a scenic region in the Ishikawa Prefecture of Japan. Known for its charming coastal towns, pristine beaches, rugged mountains, and authentic rural life, Noto Peninsula offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural richness.

The peninsula stretches over 100 kilometers from the city of Kanazawa to the tip of the Noto Peninsula, and is surrounded by the Japan Sea on three sides. The region has a unique climate that results in lush green forests, clear blue skies, and mild temperatures throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Noto Peninsula

There’s a wealth of attractions to explore in Noto Peninsula, from culturally significant museums and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wajima Lacquerware Center: Learn about the art of Japanese lacquerware and shop for unique souvenirs.
  • Notojima Glass Art Museum: Admire contemporary glass art exhibitions and participate in glass-blowing workshops.
  • Noto Satoyama Airport: Experience the history of Japanese aviation and explore vintage aircraft displays.
  • Noto Peninsula History Museum: Discover the rich history and traditional culture of the region through exhibits and interactive activities.

Natural Attractions of Noto Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Senmaida Rice Terraces: Marvel at the stunning scenery of over a thousand shining rice paddies.
  • Ganmon Sea Cave: Visit the iconic sea arch that was formed by crashing waves and volcanic activity.
  • Nanao Bay: Take a refreshing swim, go fishing, or simply relax on the sandy beach.
  • Mount Dainichi: Hike up to the summit for breathtaking views of the entire peninsula.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noto Peninsula

Noto Peninsula is ideal for outdoor enthusiasts who love to explore nature on foot or on a bike. Some of the most popular routes and trails include:

  • Notojima Cycling Road: Enjoy a scenic ride along the coast and stop at fishing villages along the way.
  • Hakusan Super Forest Road: Travel through dense forests, rivers, and hills, and see the stunning views of Mount Hakusan.
  • Shirayama Hime Shrine Walking Course: Visit the historic and sacred shrines of Shirayama and enjoy a peaceful walk in the woods.

Best Things to Do in Noto Peninsula: Activities and Tours

  • Yamato Shokudo: Savor the local gourmet, including fresh seafood and vegetables, at this must-try restaurant in Nanao.
  • Noto Experience: Participate in the local cultural activities, such as traditional fishing, rice planting, and crafting.
  • Noto Marine Park: Take a boat tour and spot the playful dolphins, seals, and seabirds of the region.
  • Kanazawa-Noto Peninsula Sightseeing Bus Tour: Take a day trip from Kanazawa and see the scenic highlights of Noto Peninsula.

Week-Long Itinerary for Noto Peninsula

Day 1: Arrive in Nanao and explore the neighborhood of Wakura Onsen.

Day 2: Visit the historic Wajima City and see the famous morning market.

Day 3: Explore the natural wonders of Notojima Island, including the Glass Art Museum and the Marine Park.

Day 4: Take a hiking excursion in the beautiful Hakusan National Park.

Day 5: Relax on the beautiful beaches of Suzu City and enjoy water sports activities.

Day 6: Discover the ancient history and traditional crafts of the town of Kawakita.

Day 7: Departure from Noto Peninsula.

Food and Drink Scene of Noto Peninsula

Noto Peninsula is renowned for its fresh and delicious seafood, rice, and sake. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaisendon (seafood rice bowl)
  • Noto Beef
  • Wakura Onsen Egg Custard

Local Beverages

  • Noto Sake
  • Hakusan Shirakawago Kogen Beer

Where to Stay in Noto Peninsula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There’s a range of accommodation options in Noto Peninsula to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Kagaya: A luxurious hotel with hot spring baths, Japanese-style tatami rooms, and ocean views.
  • Noto Royal Hotel: A modern hotel with Western-style rooms, a swimming pool, and a seafood restaurant.
  • Minshuku Iwamoto: A traditional Japanese guesthouse with tatami rooms, shared baths, and home-cooked meals.
  • Noto Guesthouse: A budget-friendly hostel with dorms and private rooms, a shared kitchen, and a cozy lounge area.

What to Buy in Noto Peninsula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wajima Lacquerware: Beautiful and intricate handcrafted lacquerware items.
  • Noto Beef: High-quality local beef from free-range cattle.
  • Kutani Pottery: Elegant and colorful ceramics that reflect the region’s history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Noto Peninsula

Noto Peninsula has a rich and vibrant cultural heritage that has been preserved through generations. Here are some customs and traditions of the region:

  • Noto Gagaku: A traditional Japanese court music and dance performance.
  • Wajima Kiriko Festival: A lively procession of giant paper lanterns carried by locals through the streets during August.
  • Hanamiyama Park: A scenic park where visitors can admire the cherry blossom trees in the springtime.

Best Time to Visit Noto Peninsula

The best time to visit Noto Peninsula is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons when the temperature is pleasant, and the scenery is breathtaking. The cherry blossoms and autumn leaves are particularly stunning during these times of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noto Peninsula

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take your shoes off when entering a traditional Japanese house.
  • Be courteous and polite to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter around.
  • Smoke or drink while walking on the street.
  • Take pictures of people or their property without permission.
  • Point with your fingers, as it’s considered rude.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noto Peninsula

Noto Peninsula is a safe and peaceful destination for tourists. However, visitors should always take precautions and follow the local guidelines, such as wearing a seatbelt while driving and avoiding swimming in restricted areas.

Health in Noto Peninsula

Travelers to Noto Peninsula should ensure they are up to date with their routine vaccinations and take necessary precautions against mosquito bites, which may transmit diseases such as dengue fever or Japanese encephalitis.

Getting to Noto Peninsula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Noto Peninsula

  • Komatsu Airport: Located in Ishikawa Prefecture, about an hour’s drive from Noto Peninsula.

Bus Stations Near Noto Peninsula

  • Nanao Bus Terminal: The main bus station in Noto Peninsula that connects with major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for travelers who prefer to travel at their own pace and explore remote areas.
  • Train: Traveling by train is easy and convenient, with several direct routes from Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka to Kanazawa.

How to Get Around in Noto Peninsula

The best way to get around Noto Peninsula is by car or by bus. Rental cars are available at major tourist destinations for those who have an international driver’s license. The local buses also provide frequent services at reasonable prices and connect most of the main tourist attractions.

Where to Go Next from Noto Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kanazawa: The capital of Ishikawa Prefecture and home to the Kenrokuen Garden, one of Japan’s most beautiful gardens.
  • Takayama: A picturesque city in Gifu Prefecture that showcases the traditional Japanese architecture and local crafts.
  • Kyoto: A former Japanese capital that boasts numerous temples, shrines, and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Noto Peninsula is a hidden gem in Japan that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and authenticity. Whether you’re interested in exploring the outdoors, the history and traditions, or the exquisite cuisine, Noto Peninsula has something to offer for everyone. Visit this remarkable destination and experience the true essence of Japan.
