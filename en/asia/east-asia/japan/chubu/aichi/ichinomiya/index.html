Introduction to Ichinomiya

Ichinomiya is a city located in Aichi Prefecture, in Japan’s Chubu region. Known for its rich history and traditional culture, Ichinomiya is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The city boasts of numerous shrines and temples, scenic landscapes, and delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will dive into what Ichinomiya has to offer and help you plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Ichinomiya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aichi Prefecture Ceramic Museum

The Aichi Prefecture Ceramic Museum showcases a variety of ceramic art, including both Japanese and Western styles. Visitors can witness demonstrations of ceramic-making techniques and participate in workshops to create their own pieces.

Suwa Shrine

Suwa Shrine is a beautiful shrine known for its unique architecture and breathtaking scenery. It is said to be one of the oldest shrines in Japan, dating back to 200 AD. Upon arrival, visitors are greeted by a massive torii gate and can enjoy exploring the picturesque grounds and numerous prayer halls.

Natural Attractions of Ichinomiya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gozaisho-dake Mountain

Gozaisho-dake is a scenic mountain that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can take the cable car up the mountain and enjoy various hiking trails with varying difficulty levels. At the top, there is also an observation deck that offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Okumikawa River

The Okumikawa River flows through Ichinomiya and offers various watersports and activities, including kayaking, rafting, and fishing. The river also boasts of stunning views of the surrounding trees and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ichinomiya

Mt. Kongosan Trail

The Mt. Kongosan Trail is a challenging hike that offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. The trail starts from Kongobu-ji Temple and takes you through scenic forests and steep inclines. It takes around five hours to complete the roundtrip hike but is definitely worth it for the view at the top.

Mikawa Bay Cycling Route

The Mikawa Bay Cycling Route stretches for around 31 kilometers along the coastline and offers stunning ocean views. The route is mostly flat and is great for both beginner and experienced cyclists. Along the way, you can also encounter charming fishing villages and sample fresh seafood.

Best Things to Do in Ichinomiya: Activities and Tours

Tea Ceremony Experience

The tea ceremony is an important aspect of Japanese culture, and visitors can experience it firsthand in Ichinomiya. A tea ceremony typically involves the preparation and presentation of matcha, a type of powdered green tea. Visitors can learn about the meticulous process of preparing tea and enjoy the tea in a serene tea room.

Edo-era Farmhouse Visit

The Edo-era Farmhouse Visit offers visitors a glimpse into Japan’s traditional farming lifestyle. The farmhouses are restored to their original condition and contain various artifacts of daily life during Japan’s Edo period. Visitors can also participate in activities, including harvesting vegetables and preparing traditional foods.

Food and Drink Scene of Ichinomiya

Must-Try Dishes

Hitsumabushi

Hitsumabushi is a famous dish from the Nagoya region that features grilled unagi (eel) served over rice. The dish is typically served with condiments, including scallions, wasabi, and nori (seaweed), and can be enjoyed in various ways.

Tebasaki

Tebasaki are Nagoya-style chicken wings that are deep-fried and then coated in a sweet and spicy sauce. They’re a popular snack or appetizer to enjoy with a cold beer.

Local Beverages

Miso Sake

Miso Sake is a unique sake that’s made by fermenting sake with miso, a paste made from fermented soybeans. The result is a savory and earthy flavor that pairs well with a wide range of foods.

Hatcho Miso

Hatcho miso is a dark, earthy, and salty miso paste that’s another local specialty. It’s made from soybeans that have been fermented for two to three years in large wooden barrels. Hatcho miso is a key ingredient in many local dishes and is often used in soups and stews.

Where to Stay in Ichinomiya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hanamizuki Ryokan

Hanamizuki Ryokan is a charming traditional Japanese inn that features tatami-matted rooms and hot springs. The inn is located in a picturesque setting and offers guests an authentic Japanese experience.

Hotel Route-Inn Ichinomiya Ekimae

Hotel Route-Inn Ichinomiya Ekimae is a great option for those looking for a more modern hotel experience. Located adjacent to Ichinomiya Station, the hotel offers comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a public bath.

What to Buy in Ichinomiya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sekishu Paper

Sekishu paper is a traditional Japanese paper that has been produced in Ichinomiya for over 1,300 years. The paper is made from mulberry bark and has a smooth texture that is perfect for calligraphy and painting.

Oyatsu Senbei

Oyatsu Senbei are traditional Japanese rice crackers that come in a variety of flavors, including soy sauce, miso, and shrimp. They make for a tasty and portable snack to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ichinomiya

Ichinomiya is known for its Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and etiquette when visiting these places. Visitors should also remove their shoes when entering traditional Japanese homes or inns and use a separate pair of slippers provided by the host.

Best Time to Visit Ichinomiya

The best time to visit Ichinomiya is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is pleasant, and the foliage is at its best. During the spring, visitors can witness cherry blossom season, and during the fall, the leaves change color, creating a stunning and picturesque landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ichinomiya

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Japanese buildings.
  • Participate in a tea ceremony or other traditional Japanese experiences.
  • Try local food and beverages, including miso sake and tebasaki chicken wings.

Don’t:

  • Talk loudly or disturb the peace at shrines and temples.
  • Enter hot springs or public baths without washing yourself first.
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing when visiting traditional Japanese sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ichinomiya

Ichinomiya is a safe city with relatively low crime rates. Visitors should still exercise caution and be mindful of their belongings, especially when in crowded areas.

Health in Ichinomiya

It is recommended that visitors to Ichinomiya have up-to-date vaccinations, including those for Japanese encephalitis and measles. Visitors should also be mindful of the heat and humidity during the summer months and stay hydrated.

Getting to Ichinomiya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ichinomiya

The nearest airport to Ichinomiya is Chubu Centrair International Airport, located approximately 51 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a train, taxi, or bus to Ichinomiya.

Bus Stations Near Ichinomiya

There are various bus stations in Ichinomiya that connect to nearby cities and towns, including Nagoya and Gifu. The Meitetsu Bus Station is the main bus terminal in Ichinomiya and is located near the train station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Ichinomiya, with the city being easily accessible via the Meitetsu Line, which connects to Nagoya and other cities.

How to Get Around in Ichinomiya

Ichinomiya is a relatively small city that is easily walkable, with most main attractions within walking distance of each other. There are also various rental options available, including bicycles and cars.

Where to Go Next from Ichinomiya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nagoya

Nagoya is the largest city in the Chubu region and is easily accessible via train from Ichinomiya. The city is known for its modern architecture, delicious cuisine, and a wide range of cultural attractions, including the Nagoya Castle and the Atsuta Shrine.

Inuyama

Inuyama is a small city located near Ichinomiya and is known for its historic castle and charming streets. The Inuyama Castle is one of the twelve original castles in Japan and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Conclusion

Ichinomiya is a city that is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. From its traditional shrines and temples to its stunning mountain hikes and scenic cycling routes, there is something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to explore this hidden gem and plan your perfect trip to Ichinomiya, Japan.
