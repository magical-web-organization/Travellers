Introduction to Toyohashi

Toyohashi, located in the eastern part of Aichi Prefecture, is a beautiful coastal city, rich in history, culture, and natural resources. With its idyllic surroundings, visitors can enjoy a variety of activities including hiking, cycling, and sightseeing. Despite being a relatively small city, Toyohashi is home to a range of cultural and historical attractions as well as culinary delights, making it a popular destination in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Toyohashi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Toyohashi Museum of Natural History: This museum features a wide variety of exhibits and displays that provide visitors with insight into Toyohashi’s history and natural wonders. The exhibits focus on marine life, geology, and ecology.
  • Toyohashi City Museum of Art and History: This museum showcases the history, culture, and art of Toyohashi through its vast collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures.
  • Toyohashi Ceramics Museum: This museum is dedicated solely to Japanese ceramics and showcases a large collection of works from various regions of Japan.

Natural Attractions of Toyohashi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Adzuma Falls: Located in the northeast of Toyohashi, Adzuma Falls is a natural wonder that offers a picturesque landscape. The waterfall is approximately 60 meters high, and the surrounding landscape is particularly stunning during the autumn months.
  • Kougaike Lake: This man-made lake is a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts, and visitors can enjoy activities such as kayaking and canoeing.
  • Takeshima Island: Takeshima Island, located off the coast of Toyohashi, is a small island that is only accessible by a short ferry ride. The island is best known for its scenic views, traditional shrines, and unique rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toyohashi

  • Toyohashi Nature Trail: This nature trail is approximately 10 kilometers long and runs from the Toyohashi City Hall to Yatsu Jizo. Along the way, visitors can admire the stunning scenery that comprises lush greenery, clear streams, and various flora and fauna.
  • Iwayado Park: This park is best known for its hiking trails, which provide visitors with a stunning view of Toyohashi and its surrounding areas.
  • Mikawa Bay Cycling Course: This 14-kilometer cycling course runs along Mikawa Bay and offers breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Toyohashi: Activities and Tours

  • Tea Ceremony Experience: Visitors can experience the traditional Japanese tea ceremony in Toyohashi, where they will have the opportunity to learn about the ritual behind the preparation and presentation of matcha (powdered green tea).
  • Day Tour to Nagoya: Nagoya, the fourth-largest city in Japan, is just a short train ride from Toyohashi. Visitors can take a day trip to Nagoya and explore its many attractions, including the Nagoya Castle, the Toyota Commemorative Museum of Industry and Technology, and the Tokugawa Art Museum.
  • Sado Island Tour: Sado Island lies off the coast of Niigata Prefecture and is known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and history. Visitors can join a guided tour from Toyohashi to explore the island’s attractions, which include cultural festivals, ninja experiences, and sake breweries.

Food and Drink Scene of Toyohashi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oden: Oden is a traditional Japanese dish made from boiled eggs, daikon radish, konjac, and other ingredients. The dish is popular in Toyohashi and is usually served during the winter months.
  • Eel: Toyohashi is renowned for its eel, which is served grilled or steamed and is known for its tender texture and savory taste.
  • Miso Katsu: Miso Katsu is a traditional Aichi dish consisting of deep-fried pork cutlet covered in a thick miso sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Toyohashi is home to several sake breweries that offer a variety of sake types, including junmai (pure rice) sake and nigori (unfiltered) sake.
  • Green Tea: Toyohashi is known for its green tea, which is grown locally and is known for its delicate flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Toyohashi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Toyohashi Hotel: This hotel is situated in the city center and offers a range of facilities, including hot springs and spa services.
  • Daiwa Roynet Hotel Toyohashi: This hotel is conveniently located near major attractions such as the Toyohashi Station and offers modern facilities and comfortable rooms.
  • Toyohashi Station Hotel: This is a budget-friendly option that provides guests with a comfortable stay and is conveniently located near the Toyohashi Station.

What to Buy in Toyohashi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Eel: Eel is a popular souvenir, and visitors can purchase dried eel or eel sauce from local shops.
  • Ceramics: Toyohashi is home to several ceramics factories, and visitors can purchase exquisitely crafted ceramics.
  • Green Tea: Visitors can purchase locally grown green tea, which is known for its delicate flavor and aroma.

Local Culture and Customs of Toyohashi

  • Respect for Elders: In Toyohashi, there is a strong emphasis on respect for elders. Visitors should be mindful of this and show proper respect to the elderly.
  • Etiquette and Formality: Japanese culture places a high value on etiquette and formality. Visitors should be mindful of this and maintain a respectful demeanor when interacting with locals.
  • Onsen Etiquette: Onsen (hot springs) are a popular attraction in Toyohashi, and visitors should be mindful of the proper etiquette when using them, including not wearing swimsuits.

Best Time to Visit Toyohashi

The best time to visit Toyohashi is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. During these months, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, cultural festivals, and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toyohashi

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Visitors should respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try Local Food: Visitors should try the local food and beverages.
  • Carry Cash: Cash is still widely used in Toyohashi, so visitors should carry enough to cover their expenses.

Don’t:

  • Wear Shoes Inside: Visitors should remove their shoes when entering traditional Japanese houses, temples, and restaurants.
  • Litter: Visitors should dispose of their trash properly.
  • Be Loud in Public Spaces: Visitors should be mindful of the noise they make in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toyohashi

Toyohashi is a relatively safe city, and crime rates are low. Visitors should exercise the same caution they would in any other city and take measures to protect their belongings.

Health in Toyohashi

Healthcare in Toyohashi is excellent, and visitors can expect high-quality medical care in case of illness or injury. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Toyohashi: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • By Air: The nearest airport to Toyohashi is Chubu Centrair International Airport, which is approximately 60 kilometers away. Visitors can take a train or bus from the airport to Toyohashi.
  • By Train: Toyohashi is well-connected by train, and visitors can take a Shinkansen or local train from major cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto.
  • By Bus: Toyohashi is also accessible by bus, and visitors can take a bus from major cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Toyohashi

Toyohashi has a well-developed transportation system, and visitors can easily get around using trains, buses, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Toyohashi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nagoya: Nagoya is a short train ride from Toyohashi and offers a range of attractions, including the Nagoya Castle and Toyota Commemorative Museum of Industry and Technology.
  • Toba: Toba is a popular destination in Mie Prefecture, known for its beautiful scenery, hot springs, and seafood.
  • Gifu: Gifu is a prefecture in central Japan known for its beautiful natural scenery, including the iconic Inuyama Castle.

Conclusion

Toyohashi is a charming destination that offers visitors a mix of natural beauty, rich culture and history, delicious cuisine, and outdoor adventures. By following this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore this hidden gem in Japan and create unforgettable memories.
