Introduction to Inuyama

Inuyama is a beautiful and culturally rich city located in Aichi Prefecture, Japan. It is situated about 30 kilometers north of Nagoya and is easily accessible by train or bus. The city is famous for its stunning Inuyama Castle, which is one of only twelve original castles in Japan.

Inuyama is surrounded by beautiful nature, including the Kiso River, which is considered one of the most beautiful rivers in Japan. The city is also famous for its traditional festivals, including the famous Inuyama Matsuri held every April.

List of Places to Visit in Inuyama

If you’re planning a trip to Inuyama, there are plenty of places to visit and things to do. Here is a comprehensive list of the must-see attractions in Inuyama:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Inuyama Castle: This magnificent castle was built in 1537 and is considered one of the national treasures of Japan. It offers stunning views of the city and the Kiso River.
  • Urakuen Garden: This beautiful garden was built in the Edo period and is known for its stunning cherry blossoms during spring.
  • Meiji Mura: A historic open-air museum that showcases Japanese buildings from the Meiji era, including a restored lighthouse, a Western-style house, and a steam locomotive.
  • Karakuri Exhibition Room: A museum showcasing Karakuri dolls, which are mechanical puppets that were developed during the Edo period.

Natural Attractions of Inuyama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kiso River: A beautiful river that runs through Inuyama and offers stunning views and outdoor activities such as fishing, kayaking, and rafting.
  • Mount Inuyama: A popular hiking destination offering panoramic views of the city and the surrounding nature.
  • Tsushima Shrine: A beautiful shrine located in a forested area on the outskirts of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Inuyama

  • Mount Inuyama Hiking Course: A challenging hiking trail that takes you up to the top of Mount Inuyama.
  • Kiso River Cycling Course: A beautiful cycling trail that follows the Kiso River through Inuyama and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Inuyama: Activities and Tours

  • Inuyama Matsuri: A famous festival held every April, featuring traditional floats and performances.
  • Soba Making Experience: Learn how to make traditional Japanese soba noodles with a local chef.
  • Tea Ceremony Experience: Experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony in a beautiful Japanese tea house.
  • Samurai Exhibition: Learn about the history of samurai in Japan at the Inuyama City Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inuyama

Day 1: Explore Inuyama Castle and Urakuen Garden.

Day 2: Visit Meiji Mura and the Karakuri Exhibition Room.

Day 3: Hike Mount Inuyama in the morning and bike along the Kiso River in the afternoon.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Nagoya to explore its many attractions, including the Nagoya Castle and the Tokugawa Art Museum.

Day 5: Experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony and enjoy the beautiful surroundings of Tsushima Shrine.

Day 6: Learn how to make traditional Japanese soba noodles and enjoy a delicious soba lunch.

Day 7: Experience the famous Inuyama Matsuri festival and witness the traditional floats and performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Inuyama

Must-Try Dishes

  • Inuyama-Yaki: A local snack made with grilled wheat gluten and sweet miso sauce.
  • Hitsumabushi: A famous dish of Nagoya, featuring grilled eel served with rice and green tea.
  • Miso Katsu: A dish of Tonkatsu (deep-fried pork cutlet) served with miso sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Inuyama is famous for its sake, which is made from local rice and water.
  • Green Tea: Inuyama is also known for its high-quality green tea, which is grown in the surrounding hills.

Where to Stay in Inuyama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Inuyama offers a range of accommodation options, from traditional Japanese guesthouses to modern hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Inuyama:

  • Meitetsu Inuyama Hotel: A modern hotel located near the train station.
  • Inuyama Guesthouse Hare: A traditional Japanese guesthouse located near Inuyama Castle.
  • Inuyama Miyako Hotel: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of Inuyama Castle.

What to Buy in Inuyama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Karakuri Dolls: Buy a traditional Karakuri doll as a souvenir of your trip to Inuyama.
  • Sake: Bring home a bottle of local sake as a gift for friends or family.
  • Green Tea: Inuyama’s high-quality green tea makes for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Inuyama

Inuyama is a city rich in culture and tradition. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Wearing yukata (light cotton kimono) during the summer months.
  • Celebrating the Inuyama Matsuri festival every April.
  • Enjoying the traditional arts of tea ceremonies and calligraphy.

Best Time to Visit Inuyama

The best time to visit Inuyama is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. However, Inuyama is a great destination year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inuyama

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Japanese guesthouses and tea houses.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drink.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Inuyama

Inuyama is a safe city with very low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take normal precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Inuyama

Inuyama has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Make sure to have adequate travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Inuyama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Inuyama

The nearest airport to Inuyama is Chubu Centrair International Airport, located about 55 kilometers away. From the airport, you can take a train or bus to Inuyama.

Bus Stations Near Inuyama

Inuyama has a bus station located near the train station. From there, you can take buses to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Inuyama is easily accessible by train, with regular services from Nagoya and other major cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Inuyama

Inuyama is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, there are also bus and taxi services available for those who prefer.

Where to Go Next from Inuyama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Inuyama is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Nagoya: Japan’s fourth-largest city, known for its castle, museums, and shopping districts.
  • Gifu: Famous for its cormorant fishing, and home to the Gifu Castle and the Mount Kinka Ropeway.
  • Ise-Shima: A beautiful coastal region known for its hot springs, beaches, and seafood.

Conclusion

Inuyama is a beautiful and culturally rich city that offers plenty to see and do. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Inuyama has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover this hidden gem in the heart of Japan.
