Introduction to Nagakute

Nagakute is a picturesque city located in Aichi Prefecture, in the Chubu region of Japan. Nestled in the heart of Japan, it is a city that is rich in history and culture, and is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery. Nagakute may be considered small compared to other cities in Japan, but it is full of surprises and wonderful things to discover.

List of Places to Visit in Nagakute

Nagakute has a variety of attractions that cater to all interests. Below are some of the must-visit places that you should consider adding to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tokugawa Art Museum
  • Nagoya Castle
  • Atsuta Shrine
  • Toyota Commemorative Museum of Industry and Technology
  • The Palace of the Hidden Dragon

Natural Attractions of Nagakute: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sangane Mountain Range
  • Yahagi no Oka Park
  • Nagara River
  • Shiroyama Lagoon
  • Nagakute Plateau

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nagakute

For those who love outdoor adventures, Nagakute offers several hiking and biking routes that vary in difficulty. Some popular trails are:

  • Sangane Mountain Range Trail
  • Yahagi no Oka Park Trail
  • Nagara River Cycling Course

Best Things to Do in Nagakute: Activities and Tours

If you want to explore the city beyond the usual tourist destinations, here are some unique things to do in Nagakute:

  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Nagakute dishes.
  • Visit a local sake brewery and try some of the best sake in the region.
  • Experience Nagakute’s tea ceremony and learn about Japanese tea culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Nagakute

Nagakute has a rich culture and customs that reflect its history and traditions. Here are some customs and etiquettes you should know:

  • Shoes should be removed before entering someone’s home.
  • Bowing is a common way of showing respect.
  • When dining, it is customary to say “itadakimasu” before starting the meal.
  • Tipping is not customary in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Nagakute

The best time to visit Nagakute is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and there are many festivals and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nagakute

Do:

  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking and hiking
  • Try the local food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Talk loudly in public places
  • Tip at restaurants or hotels
  • Smoke in non-smoking areas
  • Take photos or videos of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Nagakute

Japan is generally a safe country, and Nagakute is no exception. However, it is still important to take precautions, such as keeping your belongings safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Nagakute

Japan has excellent healthcare facilities, and Nagakute has several clinics and hospitals that provide quality medical care. It is recommended to have travel insurance before your trip.
