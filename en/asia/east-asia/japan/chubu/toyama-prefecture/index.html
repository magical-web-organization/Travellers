Introduction to Toyama (prefecture)

Toyama Prefecture is located in the Chubu region of Japan, known for its beautiful nature and rich cultural heritage. With its mix of cityscapes, historical sites, and unparalleled natural wonders, Toyama is definitely worth a visit.

List of Places to Visit in Toyama (prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route: The Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route is one of the must-see destinations in Toyama. It is a scenic mountain pass that offers panoramic views of the alpine landscape of the Northern Japan Alps.

  • Gokayama: Gokayama is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a picturesque mountain village that is famous for its traditional gassho-zukuri houses.

  • Zuiryu-ji Temple: This is one of three temples that make up the Hokuriku Sanjusan Gosen temple circuit, a famous Japanese pilgrimage route. Its standout feature is the stunning ceiling paintings done by the famous Edo-period painter Iwasa Matabei.

  • Toyama Castle: Toyama Castle is situated in the city of Toyama. It offers great views of the city and its surroundings, as well as a glimpse into the feudal history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Toyama (prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kurobe Gorge: The Kurobe Gorge is a deep ravine surrounded by high mountains and beautiful forests. Visitors can enjoy the scenery while taking a relaxing train ride.

  • Tateyama Mountain Range: The Tateyama Mountain Range offers a stunning panoramic view of the scenery around Mount Tateyama.

  • Shomyo Falls: Shomyo Falls is one of the highest waterfalls in Japan and is located in the Chubusangaku National Park. It is also quite accessible, making it a popular tourist destination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toyama (prefecture)

  • Trekking in the Northern Japan Alps: The Northern Japan Alps offer excellent trekking opportunities. There are several routes that lead to the summit of Mount Tateyama, including the famous Daikiretto ridge line.

  • Cycling along the Sea of Japan: Cycling enthusiasts can enjoy a scenic ride along the Sea of Japan coastline. There are several routes, including the Toyama Bay cycling road and the Noto Peninsula cycling road.

Best Things to Do in Toyama (prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Sightseeing Boat Tour in Chijimatera Gorge: Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery of Chijimatera Gorge while taking a relaxing boat ride.

  • Glass Art Workshop in Toyama City: Toyama is famous for its glass art. Visitors can try their hand at glassblowing and make their own unique glass creations.

  • Sake Brewery Tour: Toyama is known for its high-quality sake. Visitors can take a guided tour of a local sake brewery and learn more about the brewing process.

Food and Drink Scene of Toyama (prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Masuzushi: Masuzushi is a local specialty that consists of sushi rice and trout stuffed in a bamboo leaf.

  • Shiroebi: Shiroebi is a local shrimp that is enjoyed boiled or in tempura form.

  • Himi Udon: Himi Udon is a type of udon noodle that is made using a unique method that gives it its distinct texture.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Toyama is known for its high-quality sake. Be sure to try some of the local brews while you’re there.

  • Shinshu Apple Juice: Toyama is also famous for its apples. The Shinshu Apple Juice is a refreshing way to enjoy the flavor of Toyama’s apples.

Where to Stay in Toyama (prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Toyama Excel Hotel Tokyu: Located in the heart of Toyama City, this hotel is a good choice for those who want to be close to the city’s attractions.

  • Gokan-no-Yu Dormy Inn: This hotel is located near the Kurobe Gorge train station and is a good choice for those who want to enjoy the scenery of the Gorge.

  • Gokayama Terrace: This hostel is a great option for those looking for a unique stay in Gokayama.

What to Buy in Toyama (prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Glass Art: Toyama is famous for its glass art, so be sure to pick up some unique creations to take home.

  • Shiroebi: Shiroebi is a popular souvenir item. Be sure to pick up some dried shiroebi or shiroebi-flavored snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Toyama (prefecture)

  • Gassho-zukuri Houses: The gassho-zukuri houses are a unique feature of the region. These thatched-roof houses are built to withstand heavy snowfall and have become one of the symbols of Toyama Prefecture.

  • Kokiriko: Kokiriko is a traditional musical instrument made from bamboo. It is a symbol of the region’s fishing culture and is often used in local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Toyama (prefecture)

The best time to visit Toyama is from spring to autumn when the weather is mild and the flowers and foliage are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toyama (prefecture)

Do:

  • Respect Traditional Customs: Toyama has a rich cultural heritage, so be sure to respect traditional customs and etiquette.

  • Try Local Food and Drink: Toyama is known for its delicious cuisine, so be sure to try some of the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Waste Food: In Japan, wasting food is frowned upon, so be sure to only order what you can eat.

  • Litter: Japan is known for its cleanliness, so be sure to dispose of your trash properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toyama (prefecture)

Toyama is generally a safe place to visit, with very low crime rates.

Health in Toyama (prefecture)

Japan has a high-quality healthcare system, so visitors can rest easy knowing that they have access to good medical care if needed.

Getting to Toyama (prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Toyama (prefecture)

  • Toyama Airport: The main airport in Toyama.

  • Komatsu Airport: This airport is located in Ishikawa Prefecture but is also a good option for those visiting Toyama.

Bus Stations Near Toyama (prefecture)

  • Toyama Station Bus Terminal: Located in the heart of Toyama City, this is the main bus terminal in the region.

Other Options

  • Shinkansen: The Shinkansen (bullet train) stops at Toyama Station, making it a convenient option for those coming from Tokyo or Osaka.

How to Get Around in Toyama (prefecture)

Train

  • There are several train lines that run through Toyama Prefecture, including the Hokuriku Shinkansen, the Toyama Chiho Railway, and the Ainokaze Toyama Railway.

Bus

  • There are several bus lines that operate in the region, including the Hokuriku Expressway Bus and the Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route.

Where to Go Next from Toyama (prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kanazawa: Kanazawa is a city in Ishikawa Prefecture known for its well-preserved Edo-period districts and world-class museums.

  • Matsumoto: Matsumoto is a city in Nagano Prefecture known for its beautiful castle and stunning natural scenery.

Conclusion

Toyama Prefecture is a hidden gem in Japan that offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and high-quality cuisine. With its numerous attractions and activities, there’s something for everyone in Toyama. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, Toyama is definitely worth a visit.
