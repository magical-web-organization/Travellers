Introduction to Gokayama

If you are planning a trip to Japan, Gokayama is a must-visit destination. It is located in Toyama prefecture in the Chubu region of Japan, which is known for its natural beauty, hot springs, and traditional culture. Gokayama is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is famous for its gassho-zukuri (thatched-roof) houses, which are symbols of Japanese traditional architecture.

Gokayama is surrounded by mountains and forests, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. It is also a culturally rich destination with many historical sites, museums and festivals. This guide will help you make the most of your trip to Gokayama, with a comprehensive list of the top attractions, hiking trails, local food and drink, and cultural customs.

List of Places to Visit in Gokayama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gokayama Folklore Museum

The Gokayama Folklore Museum is a must-visit attraction if you want to learn more about the local culture and history of Gokayama. It is located in a restored gassho-zukuri house, and showcases artifacts from the local community, such as clothing, household items, and tools.

Ainokura Gassho-style Village

Ainokura is the largest village in Gokayama, with over 20 restored gassho-zukuri houses. It is surrounded by rice fields and mountains, making it a picturesque site to visit. The village has a few restaurants and souvenir shops that sell local products.

Suganuma Gassho-style Village

Suganuma is a smaller village in Gokayama with around nine gassho-style houses. It is also nestled between rice fields and mountains, so it is another idyllic location to visit. The village has an observation deck that offers a stunning view of the village and the surrounding scenery.

Natural Attractions of Gokayama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Takaoka Kojo Park

Takaoka Kojo Park is a beautiful park located in Takaoka City, about an hour’s drive from Gokayama. The park has a stunning Japanese garden, which is popular among locals and tourists. It is also home to a castle that was built in the 17th century.

Ainokura Cherry Blossom Trees

When visiting Ainokura, you’ll want to check out the blooming cherry blossom trees in the spring. The contrast of the pink flowers against the traditional gassho-style houses is simply stunning.

Mount Tsurugi

Mount Tsurugi is the highest peak in the Toyama Prefecture, standing at 2,999 meters tall. It is located in the Northern Alps, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests. You can hike to the summit to enjoy the scenic view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gokayama

Gokayama has several hiking and biking trails that offer a chance to explore the natural surroundings. Here are some must-visit trails:

Ainokura to Suganuma Trail

This trail leads from Ainokura to Suganuma, with a distance of about 2.2 kilometers. You will pass by rice fields and traditional houses, and you’ll also have opportunities to take photos from observation decks.

Toyama Chubu Sangaku National Park

The Toyama Chubu Sangaku National Park is a natural reserve located near Gokayama. The park has several hiking trails that lead to stunning waterfalls, deep gorges, and alpine lakes. The trails range from easy to challenging, so you can choose one that suits your level of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Gokayama: Activities and Tours

Gokayama Sightseeing Bus

The Gokayama Sightseeing Bus is a great way to explore the area, especially if you don’t have a car. The bus will take you to the top attractions such as the Ainokura and Suganuma villages, the Folklore Museum, and observation decks.

Visit a Sake Brewery

Toyama Prefecture is famous for its sake (Japanese rice wine), and Gokayama has several breweries that offer tours and tastings. You can learn about the brewing process, sample some sake, and even purchase some to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Gokayama

Must-Try Dishes

Ayu no Shioyaki (Grilled Sweetfish)

Ayu no Shioyaki is a popular dish in Gokayama, especially in the summer. It is grilled over an open flame, and seasoned with salt and soy sauce.

Shoyu Katsu Don (Pork Cutlet Bowl with Soy Sauce)

This dish is made with crispy pork cutlet, onion, and egg over rice. It is a filling and hearty dish that is perfect for lunch or dinner.

Local Beverages

Sake

As mentioned earlier, Toyama Prefecture is famous for its sake, and Gokayama has several breweries that offer tastings and tours. You can sample different types of sake, such as junmai (pure rice sake) and ginjo (premium sake).

Matcha

Matcha is a powdered green tea that is used in traditional Japanese tea ceremonies. You can find many shops in Gokayama that sell matcha products, such as sweets and beverages.

Where to Stay in Gokayama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Gokayama, ranging from traditional guesthouses to modern hotels.

Gassho Zukuri Stay Wanosato

Gassho Zukuri Stay Wanosato is a guesthouse that offers a traditional Japanese experience. The guesthouse has a garden, a hot spring bath, and serves meals made with local ingredients.

Gokayama Onsen Ryokan

Gokayama Onsen Ryokan is a hot spring inn that offers a relaxing experience. The inn has indoor and outdoor hot spring baths, and serves traditional Japanese meals.

What to Buy in Gokayama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kutani-yaki Pottery

Kutani-yaki is a type of Japanese pottery that is produced in the nearby Ishikawa Prefecture. You can find many shops in Gokayama that sell Kutani-yaki products, such as plates, cups, and vases.

Local Sake

If you enjoyed the sake tastings during your trip, you can purchase some bottles to take home with you. Many of the sake breweries offer shipping services, so you don’t have to worry about carrying it around.

Local Culture and Customs of Gokayama

Gassho-Zukuri Houses

The gassho-zukuri houses are the main attraction in Gokayama, and they are considered national treasures. The steep roofs are designed to withstand the heavy snowfall in the region. Some of the gassho-zukuri houses are open to visitors.

Local Festivals

Gokayama has several festivals throughout the year that showcase the local culture and traditions. The Gokayama Doburoku Festival, held in October, is a festival that celebrates the brewing of sake. The Ainokura Winter Festival, held in January, features snow sculptures and traditional performances.

Best Time to Visit Gokayama

The best time to visit Gokayama is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and the cherry blossom trees in Ainokura are blooming in the spring, while the autumn leaves in the fall create a beautiful contrast against the gassho-zukuri houses.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gokayama

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering a gassho-zukuri house.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be mindful of your volume at night, as it can be disruptive to the peaceful environment.

Don’t:

  • Take photos inside some of the gassho-zukuri houses, as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Litter in the nearby forests and rice fields.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gokayama

Gokayama is a safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, it is still advisable to take precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and locking your accommodation.

Health in Gokayama

Gokayama has a few medical facilities, including clinics and hospitals. However, it is still advisable to pack any necessary medications or contact lenses, as they may not be readily available.

Getting to Gokayama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gokayama

The nearest airport to Gokayama is Toyama Airport, which is approximately 60 kilometers away. There are direct flights from Tokyo, Osaka, and Sapporo.

Bus Stations Near Gokayama

There are several bus stations near Gokayama, including Johana Bus Terminal and Nanto Bus Terminal. There are also local buses that operate within Gokayama.

Other Options

Driving is also a viable option, as Gokayama is easily accessible from the major highways in Japan.

How to Get Around in Gokayama

The best way to get around in Gokayama is by car. There are several car rental companies in neighboring cities, such as Toyama City.

Where to Go Next from Gokayama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shirakawa-go

Shirakawa-go is another UNESCO World Heritage Site that is located about 30 kilometers from Gokayama. It is famous for its gassho-zukuri houses and is an excellent destination to explore more traditional Japanese architecture.

Kanazawa

Kanazawa is a beautiful city that is located about 80 kilometers from Gokayama. The city has several attractions, such as the Kenrokuen Garden and the Kanazawa Castle.

Conclusion

Gokayama is an idyllic destination that offers a perfect balance of nature, culture, and tradition. It is a must-visit destination that should be on every traveler’s list when visiting Japan. With this travel guide, you will be able to make the most of your trip to Gokayama and explore its many cultural and natural attractions.
