Introduction to Mount Tate

Mount Tate or Tateyama is a picturesque mountain located in the Toyama Prefecture of Japan. Its snowy peak and beautiful landscapes make it a must-visit destination for hikers, outdoor enthusiasts, and nature lovers. Mount Tate is also an important spiritual site for pilgrims and has been considered one of Japan’s three sacred mountains for centuries. This guide provides everything you need to know to plan your trip to this beautiful mountain.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Tate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route: This attraction is a must-visit for visitors to Mount Tate. The route is a spectacular mountain drive crossing the Japanese Alps with great views and sights.
  • Tateyama Ropeway: The highest cable car in Japan takes you to the summit of Mount Tate. There, you can enjoy fantastic views of the surrounding scenery and take the time to appreciate the peace this sacred site offers.
  • Tateyama Murodo: Enjoy the amazing views of the snow-capped peak of Mount Tate, the largest snow wall, and take a ride on the Kanden Tunnel Trolleybus to reach the highest visitor center.

Natural Attractions of Mount Tate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hiking Trails: There are a variety of hiking trails available to hikers of all skill levels. One of the most popular trails is the Tateyama Tanigawa Traverse, which leads hikers through a beautiful alpine landscape.
  • Shomyo Falls: Located near the base of Mount Tate, Shomyo Falls is one of Japan’s largest waterfalls. You can take a short hike to the viewing platform to see and hear the roaring sounds of the waterfall.
  • Kurobe Dam: This is the highest dam in Japan with a height of 186 meters is one of the major tourist attractions of Toyama prefecture. Visitors can go on guided tours and learn about the history of the dam.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Tate

Mount Tate offers a variety of routes for both hikers and bikers. The vastness of the mountain makes it possible to get lost, so it is recommended to hire a guide. Some of the most remarkable hiking and biking routes in Mount Tate include:

  • Murodo to Daikanbo Hike: This 6-hour hike offers incredible views of the Alpine range and the surrounding scenery.
  • Bike Hut to Murodo Trek: This 4-hour trek is an intermediate level hike that goes through a beautiful alpine landscape.
  • Hotaka Shrine: Mount Tate’s Hotaka Shrine is an easy and enjoyable walk that requires about an hour hike from the main viewpoint.

Best Things to Do in Mount Tate: Activities and Tours

  • Mountain Climbing: If you are an experienced mountaineer, then climbing Mount Tate will be an unforgettable adventure.
  • Kyoboku Valley: Explore this valley on horseback, one of the most beautiful scenic routes in the mountain.
  • Snow Walls: Visiting Tateyama Murodo between May and June is a unique opportunity to see the massive snow walls of the Northern Japan Alps. Opt for a guided tour to visit the snow corridor.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Tate

Day 1: Arrive in Toyama - Check-in to Hotel Day 2: Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route Day 3: Hiking at Mount Tate and Shomyo Falls Adventure Day 4: Explore the Kyoboku Valley on horseback and visit the Tateyama Kurobe Dam Day 5: Visit the Historic Village of Gokayama and experience Traditional Japanese Culture Day 6: Visit nearby Kanazawa to explore its rich history and art scene. Day 7: Final day of your visit to Mount Tate.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Tate

Must-Try Dishes

  • Masuzushi: Sushi molded by hands and wrapped in bamboo leaves with trout soaked in vinegar.
  • Shiro-ebi Rice: A tasty rice dish served with white shrimps, which are small, translucent and sweet.
  • Toyama Black Ramen: A type of ramen soup where the broth is made using pork and soy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kokutou Shochu: A type of distilled spirit made from potato or black sugar, often consumed by the locals.
  • Sake of Toyama: Tour the Tateyama Sake Brewery and discover the rice wine-making process that has been developed for over a century.

Where to Stay in Mount Tate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse, a boutique hotel, or a luxurious resort, there are plenty of options to choose from around Mount Tate. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Tateyama Kokusai Hotel: This hotel is famous for its hot spring baths, and its location near the Tateyama Ropeway makes it an ideal choice for exploring Mount Tate.
  • Goyomon: A beautifully designed traditional Ryokan with an outdoor hot tub.
  • Kiyosato no Mori Hotel: Located in the middle of the forest, this hotel is a comfortable and peaceful place to stay.

What to Buy in Mount Tate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shiro-ebi Rice: This item is well wrapped in clear packaging, and it’s a must-try food souvenir of Toyama. It is the perfect souvenir for those who love a quick and easy meal at home.
  • Masu Sake Cup: These sake cups are made of square-shaped wooden boxes known as masu, commonly used for serving traditional Japanese rice wine.
  • Wagashi Treats: These Japanese confectioneries are made with a blend of rice flour and plant-based ingredients in traditional Japanese flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Tate

Mount Tate is considered one of Japan’s sacred mountains, and its cultural impact is significant. It’s also home to the Shugendo religion, which focuses on achieving spiritual enlightenment through the practice of both ritual and the connection with the natural environment. Visitors should be mindful of temple etiquette, such as removing shoes and making proper offerings.

Best Time to Visit Mount Tate

Spring and summer are the best times to visit Mount Tate, specifically from mid-April to mid-November, when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and sightseeing are accessible. The winter season at Mount Tate is often marked by heavy snowfall, making it ideal for skiing and other snow sports. The views are also amazing during the winter when the mountain is covered in a thick layer of snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Tate

Do:

  • Respect the holy shrines and temples
  • Check the weather and dress appropriately for outdoor activities
  • Hire a guide if hiking or biking alone
  • Try the local food and drinks

Don’t:

  • Litter in the mountain
  • Leave trash on the hiking trails
  • Touch or damage the natural sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Tate

Mount Tate can be challenging for hikers of all levels, especially during the winter season. Be mindful of the weather conditions and make sure to wear appropriate footwear and clothing. Also, it’s always advisable to take a buddy with you while hiking along the mountain trails.

Health in Mount Tate

The cold weather at higher altitudes can affect your health if you’re not adequately prepared. Make sure to stay adequately hydrated and take necessary supplements or medication in case of any health issues.

Getting to Mount Tate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Tate

Depending on your point of origin, several airports in and around Toyama can serve as your gateway to Mount Tate, including:

  • Toyama Airport: A domestic airport with regular flights from Narita, Haneda, Sapporo, and Fukuoka.
  • Komatsu Airport: A domestic airport with regular flights from Haneda, Sapporo, and Okinawa.

Bus Stations Near Mount Tate

Several bus services depart from Toyama Station or Shinano-Omachi Station and take visitors to Mount Tate’s base.

Other Options

Visitors can opt to rent a private car, take a taxi or hire a local guide to explore the mountain at their pace.

How to Get Around in Mount Tate

The mountain is best explored on foot, although visitors can also explore it by bike or RV touring. There are also local buses that travel to the attractions nearby.

Where to Go Next from Mount Tate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations to Mount Tate include:

  • Kanazawa: This old town is known for the famous Kenroku-en Garden and traditional crafts that originated from the Edo period.
  • Kurobe Gorge Railway: A scenic train ride that traverses the Kurobe Gorge and provides a breathtaking view of the regional landscape.

Conclusion

Mount Tate is a stunning mountain that offers visitors a unique blend of Japanese culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure. Whether you are a hiker, a skier, or someone seeking spiritual enlightenment, this mountain has something for everyone. Plan your next trip to Mount Tate with this comprehensive guide, and discover all that this sacred mountain has to offer.
