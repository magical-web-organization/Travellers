    • Introduction to Shimada

    Shimada is a charming city in Shizuoka Prefecture, located in the Chubu region of Japan. This laid-back destination is famous for its beautiful natural attractions, including stunning mountains, forests, and rivers that offer endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. But Shimada is also rich in history and culture, with many fascinating museums and historical sites that showcase the region’s past.

    In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit in Shimada, including must-see attractions, exciting activities, delicious food and drink, and much more. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an unforgettable adventure, you’ll find everything you need to plan your dream trip to Shimada right here!

    Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

    Shimada has a rich cultural history, and there are several fascinating museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s past. Some of the top attractions to visit include:

    • Sumpu Castle: This reconstructed castle was a former stronghold of Tokugawa Ieyasu, one of the most powerful feudal lords in Japan’s history. The castle features a museum with exhibits on the political and cultural history of the Tokugawa shogunate.
    • Shimada City Museum: This museum has a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Shimada and its surrounding areas.
    • Ieyasu and Mikawa Bushi Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of Tokugawa Ieyasu and the history of the Mikawa Province. It features a variety of exhibits on samurai culture, weapons, and armor.

    Natural Attractions of Shimada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

    Shimada is known for its beautiful natural attractions, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Some of the top natural attractions include:

    • Mount Fuji: Shimada is located just south of Mount Fuji, one of Japan’s most iconic landmarks. Visitors can take a day trip to explore the mountain’s trails and enjoy breathtaking views.
    • Makai no Sakura: This 800-year-old cherry tree is located in the Oi River basin and is one of the largest cherry trees in Japan. During the cherry blossom season, the tree is surrounded by a sea of pink flowers.
    • Tenryu River: This scenic river is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and rafting.
    • Shimada City Park: This beautiful park features a variety of walking paths, gardens, and a pond. Visitors can also enjoy a picnic or visit the park’s museum.

    Hiking/Biking Routes of Shimada

    Shimada offers several scenic hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the region’s natural beauty. Some of the top routes to explore include:

    • Nihondaira Plateau: This hiking route offers breathtaking views of Mount Fuji and the surrounding mountains.
    • Oigawa Railway Cycling Road: This 13-kilometer cycling route runs alongside the Oigawa River and offers beautiful views of the landscape.
    • Kumomi Pass: This mountain pass is a popular hiking spot and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

    Best Things to Do in Shimada: Activities and Tours

    There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Shimada, no matter what your interests are. Some of the top things to do include:

    • Traditional Tea Ceremony: Visitors can participate in a traditional Japanese tea ceremony and learn about the history and customs of this ancient practice.
    • River Rafting: The Tenryu River is a popular spot for river rafting and offers thrilling adventures for visitors of all skill levels.
    • Oigawa Railway: This scenic railway winds through the beautiful countryside of Shimada and offers breathtaking views of the mountains and rivers.
    • Shimada City Bike Tour: This guided bike tour takes visitors on a tour of the city’s top attractions, including museums, temples, and natural sites.

    Week-Long Itinerary for Shimada

    Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Shimada:

    • Day 1: Explore the museums and historical sites of Shimada, including Sumpu Castle, Shimada City Museum, and Ieyasu and Mikawa Bushi Museum.
    • Day 2: Take a scenic train ride on the Oigawa Railway and enjoy the stunning views of the countryside.
    • Day 3: Go hiking on the Nihondaira Plateau and enjoy breathtaking views of Mount Fuji.
    • Day 4: Participate in a traditional tea ceremony and learn about the history and customs of this ancient practice.
    • Day 5: Go river rafting on the Tenryu River and experience the thrill of adventure in the beautiful natural surroundings.
    • Day 6: Explore the local food and drink scene of Shimada, including must-try dishes and local beverages.
    • Day 7: Visit nearby destinations and excursions, such as the hot springs town of Shuzenji or the beautiful beach town of Shimoda.

    Food and Drink Scene of Shimada

    Shimada is a great destination for foodies, with a variety of delicious local dishes and drinks to try. Some of the must-try dishes include:

    • Unagi: This eel dish is a local specialty and is typically grilled and served over rice.
    • Green Tea: Shimada is known for its high-quality green tea, which is grown in the surrounding mountains and hills.
    • Sakura Shrimp: These tiny shrimp are a local delicacy and are often used to flavor rice dishes and soups.
    • Wasabi: Shimada is famous for its high-quality wasabi, which is often served with sushi and sashimi.

    Where to Stay in Shimada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

    Shimada offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay include:

    • Sanco Inn Shimada Station Front: This modern hotel is located just steps from the train station and offers comfortable rooms and convenient amenities.
    • Guesthouse Kabuki: This cozy guesthouse offers traditional Japanese-style rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
    • Toyoko Inn Shizuoka Shimada Ekimae: This affordable hotel chain offers clean and comfortable rooms at a great price.

    What to Buy in Shimada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

    There are plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Shimada. Some of the top options include:

    • Green Tea: Shimada is known for its high-quality green tea, which is a great souvenir to bring back for tea lovers.
    • Shimada Tsumugi Silk: This traditional silk fabric is woven by hand and is a popular souvenir for those interested in Japanese textiles.
    • Wasabi: Shimada is famous for its high-quality wasabi, which is often sold in paste form or as fresh roots.
    • Sakura Shrimp: These tiny shrimp make a unique and delicious souvenir to bring back home.

    Local Culture and Customs of Shimada

    Shimada has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Some of the key customs and traditions include:

    • Respect for elders: Japanese culture places a strong emphasis on showing respect to elders, so visitors should be mindful of this when interacting with locals.
    • Shinto Shrines: Shinto shrines are an important part of Japanese culture, and visitors should be respectful when visiting these religious sites.
    • Removing Shoes: It is customary to remove shoes when entering certain buildings, such as temples, shrines, and traditional Japanese homes.

    Best Time to Visit Shimada

    The best time to visit Shimada is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and comfortable. The cherry blossom season in April is a particularly beautiful time to visit, as the cherry blossoms bloom throughout the city and surrounding countryside. The fall season in November is also a great time to visit, as the autumn leaves turn into a beautiful array of colors.

    Do’s and Don’ts of Shimada

    Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Shimada:

    • Do: Bow when greeting people, remove your shoes when visiting certain buildings, and be respectful of local customs and culture.

    • Don’t: Talk loudly in public spaces, eat while walking, or be disrespectful to elders.

    Safety and Health

    Safety in Shimada

    Shimada is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should be aware of basic safety precautions, such as staying alert in crowded areas and keeping an eye on personal belongings.

    Health in Shimada

    Shimada has a modern healthcare system and visitors should have no problem accessing medical care if needed. It is also important to stay aware of any potential health risks, such as insect bites or exposure to extreme weather conditions.

    Getting to Shimada: Transportation Options and Logistics

    Airports Near Shimada

    The closest airport to Shimada is Shizuoka Airport, which is about a 45-minute drive away. Alternatively, visitors can also fly into Tokyo Haneda Airport or Tokyo Narita Airport and take a train or bus to Shimada from there.

    Bus Stations Near Shimada

    Shimada has several bus stations that offer connections to other destinations in the region. The Shimada Station Bus Terminal and the Shimada City Bus Terminal are two of the top options.

    Other Options

    Shimada is also accessible by train, with the JR Tokaido Line and Oigawa Railway connecting the city to other destinations in the region.

    How to Get Around in Shimada

    Shimada is a relatively small city, so it is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Public transportation options include buses and trains, with the Shimada Station serving as a main hub for transportation in the city.

    Where to Go Next from Shimada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

    Shimada is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in the surrounding area. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

    • Shuzenji: This hot springs town is located about an hour away from Shimada and offers relaxing onsens, beautiful temples, and traditional restaurants.
    • Shimoda: This beach town is located about two hours away from Shimada and offers scenic coastal views, historic sites, and delicious seafood.
    • Hamamatsu: This larger city is located about an hour away from Shimada and offers a variety of attractions, including museums, parks, and shopping malls.

    Conclusion

    Whether you’re looking for natural beauty, history and culture, delicious food and drink, or exciting outdoor adventures, Shimada has something for everyone. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your dream trip to this charming city and surrounding area. Safe travels!
