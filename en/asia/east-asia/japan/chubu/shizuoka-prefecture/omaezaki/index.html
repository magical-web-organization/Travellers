Introduction to Omaezaki

Located in Shizuoka prefecture, in Chubu, Japan, Omaezaki is renowned for its scenic views, outdoor activities, and local culture. The city lies on the Pacific coast, and is known for its warm weather, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking to escape the bustle of Japan’s major cities. In this guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Omaezaki, as well as where to stay, eat, and shop.

List of Places to Visit in Omaezaki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Omaezaki has a rich cultural heritage, with many historical sites and museums worth visiting. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Omaezaki Lighthouse:　One of the best places to catch scenic views of the Pacific Ocean, the lighthouse is situated on a hill, surrounded by beautiful gardens.
  • Omaezaki City Museum of History and Culture: A museum that chronicles the city’s history, from prehistoric times to the present day. Exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  • Mishima Taisha Shrine: A 1,400-year-old Shinto shrine situated near Mt. Fuji, known for its beautiful gardens and historic architecture.
  • Omaezaki Fishing Port: An ideal destination for seafood lovers, the port offers fresh seafood caught daily by local fishermen.

Natural Attractions of Omaezaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Omaezaki boasts some of the most scenic views in Japan, with a variety of outdoor activities available for tourists. Some of the top natural attractions in the city include:

  • Cape Irozaki: A cape renowned for its stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, with a lighthouse, observation deck, and hiking trail.
  • Omaezaki Beach: A popular destination for swimming and sunbathing, the beach offers stunning views of Mt. Fuji on a clear day.
  • Mt. Fuji: An iconic symbol of Japan, Mt. Fuji is situated just a short drive from Omaezaki, offering incredible views and hiking opportunities.
  • Nakatajima Sand Dunes:　Known as the largest sand dune in Japan, the Nakatajima Sand Dunes offer a unique desert-like landscape to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Omaezaki

Omaezaki offers a range of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the natural beauty of the city. Some popular routes include:

  • Isonoura Beach Walking Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and takes visitors through a local fishing village and forest.
  • Omaezaki Cycling Road: This 22-kilometer road spans from the coast to the mountains, offering breathtaking views and challenging terrain.
  • Omaezaki Forest Park: A 70-hectare park with several hiking trails, a bird sanctuary and a large playground for children.

Best Things to Do in Omaezaki: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking routes, Omaezaki has plenty of activities to offer travelers looking for unique experiences. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Fishing: Omaezaki’s fishing industry is well-known throughout Japan, so visitors can charter a boat and try their hand at catching local fish.
  • Surfing: The beaches in Omaezaki offer some of the best surf spots in Japan, attracting many surfers from around the world.
  • Whale Watching: Omaezaki is an ideal destination for whale watching, with tours offered from February to April.
  • Sake Tasting: Omaezaki’s sake is renowned throughout Japan, so visitors can take a tour of a local brewery and taste the city’s special sake.

Food and Drink Scene of Omaezaki

Must-Try Dishes

Omaezaki’s cuisine revolves around its fishing industry, so seafood dishes are the most popular. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Kakiage: A deep-fried dish made of shrimp, squid, and vegetables, served with soy sauce and grated daikon.
  • Shirasu Don: A rice bowl topped with raw whitebait, a specialty of Omaezaki.
  • Sakana no Kama: Grilled fish head, a delicacy in Omaezaki.
  • Omaezaki Sake: This local sake is characterized by its dry, crisp flavor, due to the high quality of water from Mt. Fuji.

Local Beverages

Apart from sake, Omaezaki offers a variety of local beverages, including:

  • Green Tea: Omaezaki’s tea is grown in the city’s hills, with rich and fragrant flavors.
  • Coffee: Omaezaki offers a variety of specialty coffee shops, including those that roast their own beans.
  • Craft Beer: Omaezaki has a thriving craft beer scene, with many breweries located throughout the city.

Where to Stay in Omaezaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Omaezaki offers a variety of accommodations to suit travelers of all budgets. Some of the best options include:

  • Omaezaki Royal Hotel: A luxurious hotel with ocean views, hot springs and swimming pools.
  • Omaezaki Sun Beach Hotel: A more affordable option, located near Omaezaki Beach, with ocean views and hot springs.
  • Guesthouse Sumire: A charming guesthouse located near the city center, with affordable rates and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Omaezaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Omaezaki is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best options include:

  • Whitebait: Omaezaki’s shirasu (baby sardine) is a popular souvenir, available in various forms, such as dried, canned, or pickled.
  • Sake: Omaezaki’s sake is renowned throughout Japan, with many local breweries offering their premium products for purchase.
  • Tea: Omaezaki’s tea is grown in the city’s hills and is available for purchase in various forms, such as loose tea or tea bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Omaezaki

Omaezaki has a rich history and culture, with various traditional customs still practiced today. In order to show respect for the local culture, visitors should:

  • Remove shoes before entering temples or traditional houses.
  • Use a small towel to wipe hands and face when dining out.
  • Bow when greeting locals or saying goodbye.
  • Avoid talking loudly on public transportation or in public places.

Best Time to Visit Omaezaki

The best time to visit Omaezaki is from March to May when the weather is mild and pleasant, and cherry blossoms are in full bloom. The summer months of June to August are the busiest time of year as tourists flock to the beach to soak up the sun and surf. Fall, from September to November, is also a great time to visit, as the leaves change color, creating a stunning landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Omaezaki

Do:

  • Try local seafood dishes such as Shirasu Don.
  • Visit the city’s historical sites and museums to learn about its rich cultural heritage.
  • Take a whale watching tour during the winter months.
  • Respect local customs when visiting temples and traditional houses.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas, as it is prohibited by law.
  • Speak in a loud voice in public areas or on public transportation.
  • Leave trash in public places or on beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Omaezaki

Omaezaki is generally a safe city to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and locking valuables in a safe place.

Health in Omaezaki

Omaezaki has an excellent healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics located throughout the city. Travelers should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before embarking on their trip.

Getting to Omaezaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Omaezaki

The closest airport to Omaezaki is Shizuoka Airport, located approximately 55 kilometers away. Alternately, visitors can use Tokyo’s Narita or Haneda airports, which are both located approximately 250 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Omaezaki

The fastest and most convenient way to travel to Omaezaki is via bullet train (Shinkansen) to Atami Station, followed by a transfer to the JR Ito Line to Omaezaki. Another option is to take a bus from Shinjuku or Tokyo Station, which takes approximately 4 hours.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can drive from Tokyo to Omaezaki, which takes approximately 3 hours, or rent a car from Shizuoka Airport.

How to Get Around in Omaezaki

Omaezaki is a walkable city, with many attractions located within walking distance of each other. However, visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses or taxis, to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Omaezaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Omaezaki is located in Shizuoka prefecture, which offers numerous attractions and scenic views. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Shimoda: A historic town located on the Izu Peninsula, known for its beautiful beaches and hot springs.
  • Shizuoka City: The capital city of Shizuoka prefecture, known for its historic castle and tea plantations.
  • Gotemba Premium Outlets: A large shopping center with many international and Japanese brands, located near Mt. Fuji.

Conclusion

Omaezaki is an ideal destination for tourists looking for a change of pace from Japan’s major cities. With its scenic views, outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage, the city offers an unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages. So pack your bags, book your flight, and get ready for an adventure of a lifetime in Omaezaki, Japan!
