Introduction to Mori

Mori is a small town located in the Shizuoka Prefecture of Japan. Despite its size, it boasts of some outstanding natural beauty and cultural significance that make it a popular destination for tourists. Mori is also home to some of the most stunning hiking and biking routes in the region, which attract visitors with a passion for outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive guide, we take you through the best places to visit, activities to do, food and drink scene, local culture, safety and health information, and transportation options in Mori, Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Mori

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mori Castle Ruins: This is a must-visit attraction in Mori. It used to be the residence of the famous Yamanouchi family and is now preserved as a historical site.
  • Fureai-kan Museum: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of Mori, including its traditional handicrafts and festivals.
  • Shuzenji Nature Park: A beautiful park that has a hot spring and a temple. The temple dates back to the 9th century and is a place where visitors can relax and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Mori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Eboshi: This is the highest mountain in Mori, and it is one of the most popular hiking trails in the area. It is also known for its stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Matsuzaki Cherry Blossom Festival: This festival takes place in early April when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. It is a great place to enjoy the beautiful scenery and take part in traditional Japanese celebrations.
  • Mito Beach: An excellent spot for swimming during the summers, Mito Beach is also an ideal location for picnics, stargazing, and sunset watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mori

  • The Izu Pilgrimage Route: This 800 km long path is the perfect way to explore Mori’s beautiful natural landscapes. The trail involves rugged terrain and is suitable for experienced hikers.
  • The Shizuoka Cycling Road: This 50 km long cycling track is perfect for visitors who want to explore the beautiful Izu Peninsula scenery. It is an easy ride that includes stops along the way for sightseeing and refreshments.

Best Things to Do in Mori: Activities and Tours

  • Shuzenji Niji-no-Sato: This is a great place to experience Mori’s traditional culture. Visitors can try their hands at various crafts, such as pottery and Japanese calligraphy.
  • River rafting: Mori has some of the best river rafting spots in Japan, thanks to its stunning natural landscapes, including beautiful cascading waterfalls, scenic forests, and mountain tunnels.
  • Shuzenji Spa: This spa complex is one of the most popular destinations in Mori. Visitors can relax in a traditional hot spring, which is fed by natural mountain springs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mori

Day 1: Visit the Mori Castle Ruins and Fureai-kan Museum. Day 2: Hike Mount Eboshi and take in the beautiful scenery. Day 3: Explore the Izu Pilgrimage Route and visit Shuzenji Niji-no-Sato. Day 4: Visit the Mito Beach and indulge in water sports activities. Day 5: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Shizuoka Cycling Road. Day 6: Enjoy rafting on the Izu Peninsula. Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate at Shuzenji Spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Mori

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wasabi: Mori is known for its high-quality wasabi, which is a must-try for food enthusiasts.
  • Sashimi: This is a Japanese delicacy that comprises raw fish, and Mori has some of the finest sashimi dishes.
  • Mochi: This is a sweet rice cake that is commonly eaten in Japan. Its chewy texture and sweet flavor make it a perfect dessert option.

Local Beverages

  • Green tea: Mori produces some of the best green teas in Japan, making it the perfect place to enjoy a traditional cup of tea.
  • Sake: This Japanese rice wine is brewed in Mori and is a great way to experience the local culture.

Where to Stay in Mori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mori no Terasu Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Mori and offers comfortable rooms with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • NIPPONIA MorinoSumika: This is a unique Japanese-style accommodation option that offers traditional tatami rooms and hot spring baths.
  • Shuzenji Onsen Ryokan Yamanoshou: This is a luxurious ryokan that boasts of traditional Japanese architecture and soothing hot spring baths.

What to Buy in Mori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wasabi products: Visitors can purchase various wasabi products like wasabi seasoning, wasabi pickles, and wasabi-flavored snacks.
  • Yamanouchi family crafts: These include items like traditional handicrafts, paintings, and calligraphy.

Local Culture and Customs of Mori

  • Respect for elders: Visitors are encouraged always to show respect to older people in Mori, as this is an essential part of the local culture.
  • Removal of shoes: Visitors are expected to remove their shoes when indoors, especially in houses, restaurants, and temples.

Best Time to Visit Mori

The best time to visit Mori is during spring (March to May) when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. This makes for a beautiful experience that visitors should not miss.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mori

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the Japanese people.
  • Take your shoes off before entering restaurants and homes.

Don’t:

  • Tip your servers as it is not a part of Japanese culture.
  • Speak loudly and disturb others as Japanese people are known for their reserved nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mori

Mori is generally safe for visitors as it has a low crime rate. However, visitors should always take care of their belongings and avoid walking alone in the dark.

Health in Mori

Visitors should remain hydrated and protect themselves against the sun’s rays during the summer months. Visitors should also be careful when consuming raw seafood like sashimi.

Getting to Mori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mori

The nearest airport to Mori is Shizuoka Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Mori

The best way to reach Mori by bus is through the Shizuoka Bus Station located in the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Mori by train, using the Tokaido Line or Shinkansen.

How to Get Around in Mori

Visitors can get around Mori by bus, taxi, or rental car.

Where to Go Next from Mori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tokyo: Visitors can take the Shinkansen train from Mori to Tokyo, which is just a few hours away.
  • Kyoto: Kyoto is a popular tourist attraction in Japan and can easily be reached by train from Mori.

Conclusion

Mori is a hidden gem in the Shizuoka Prefecture that offers visitors an opportunity to explore Japan’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes. Whether it’s enjoying the local cuisine, exploring museums, taking part in adventurous outdoor activities, or relaxing at a hot spring, Mori has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now well-equipped to plan your next visit to Mori and create some unforgettable memories.
