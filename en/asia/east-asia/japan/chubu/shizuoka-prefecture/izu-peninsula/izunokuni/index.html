Introduction to Izunokuni

Izunokuni is a beautiful city in the Izu Peninsula of Shizuoka Prefecture, located in the Chubu region of Japan. The city boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for travelers.

Izunokuni was once a hub for hot spring resorts, which attracted many visitors to the city. Today, the city has expanded to offer much more than just hot springs. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, Izunokuni has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Izunokuni

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Izunokuni has a rich history, and visitors should explore the city’s cultural attractions, such as museums and historical sites, to understand its past. Some of the must-visit culturally important attractions include:

  • Izunokuni Panorama Park - This park offers stunning views of Mount Fuji, Izu Peninsula, and Suruga Bay. It also has a museum that displays the history of the city from the Edo period.
  • Izu-Kogen Sogo Park - This park has several attractions, including a botanical garden, a zoo, and an art museum, making it an excellent destination for a family outing.
  • Shimoda History Museum - This museum showcases the history of Shimoda, a nearby town that played a significant role in Japan’s opening to the West in the mid-19th century.

Natural Attractions of Izunokuni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Izunokuni has several natural attractions that visitors can explore to experience the beauty of the city. Some of the natural attractions include:

  • Mt. Omuro - This mountain is an extinct volcano that offers a stunning panoramic view of the city and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can reach the top of the mountain by taking a chairlift or hiking the trail.
  • Hakone-Izu National Park - This park spans across several cities, including Izunokuni. The park has numerous natural attractions, including Owaku-dani, a volcanic valley, and Lake Ashi, a beautiful crater lake.
  • Izunuma-Kannon Lake - This lake is famous for its beautiful scenery and the Kanzanji Temple on its shore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Izunokuni

Izunokuni offers numerous trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts to have an exhilarating outdoor adventure. The following are some of the popular routes in the city:

  • Izu Peninsula Geo Trail - This trail spans across several cities, including Izunokuni, and offers a picturesque view of the Izu Peninsula.
  • Amagi-toge Pass - This pass connects the cities of Izunokuni and Izu and offers a challenging hike to the top of the mountain.
  • Izu Skyline - This pathway runs through the Amagi Mountain range and offers a breathtaking view of the city and the surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Izunokuni: Activities and Tours

Besides visiting the cultural and natural attractions, there’s plenty to do in Izunokuni. Visitors can engage in several activities and tours, such as:

  • Hot Spring Experience - Izunokuni is renowned for its hot spring resorts, and visitors can enjoy the experience of the traditional Japanese hot springs.
  • Tea Picking Experience - Green tea is an integral part of Japanese culture, and visitors can participate in the tea picking experience in the Amagi Mountain area.
  • Sake Brewery Tour - The city’s sake breweries offer visitors the chance to learn about the traditional way of making sake and to sample the different varieties of sake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Izunokuni

If you plan to visit Izunokuni for a week, here’s a proposed itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Izunokuni Panorama Park and explore the views of Mount Fuji
  • Day 2: Visit Izu-Kogen Sogo Park and experience the flora, fauna, and art museum
  • Day 3: Go hiking/biking on the Izu Peninsula Geo Trail
  • Day 4: Explore the Amagi-toge Pass and relax in a hot spring in the evening
  • Day 5: Visit Shimoda History Museum and learn about Japan’s opening to the West
  • Day 6: Participate in the tea picking experience and tour the sake brewery
  • Day 7: Relax in a hot spring or visit a local attraction that you missed earlier

Food and Drink Scene of Izunokuni

Izunokuni offers several mouth-watering dishes and local beverages that visitors must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wasabi - Izunokuni is famous for its fresh wasabi, which is distinct from the usual wasabi paste offered in other parts of the world.
  • Shirasu-don - A typical dish in the Izu Peninsula area that consists of small, freshly caught whitefish called shirasu over a bed of rice.
  • Sakura Shrimp - A local delicacy, served in a variety of dishes, made from tiny, pinkish shrimp caught in Suruga Bay.

Local Beverages

  • Shizuoka Sake - Sake enthusiasts should try the local sake in Izunokuni, which is famous for its crisp and smooth taste.
  • Green Tea - The city is surrounded by tea fields, and visitors should try the green tea at the local teahouses.

Where to Stay in Izunokuni: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Izunokuni offers several options for accommodation that cater to various budgets and preferences.

  • Hotels - There are several luxury and mid-range hotels in Izunokuni that offer hot spring facilities and excellent views of the city and its surroundings.
  • Guesthouses and Hostels - Budget-conscious travelers can opt for guesthouses and hostels that offer affordable accommodation with essential amenities.
  • Ryokans - Japanese-style inns that offer traditional hot spring experiences, Japanese cuisine, and tatami-matted rooms.

What to Buy in Izunokuni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visit these local souvenir shops that sell items that reflect Izunokuni’s culture and heritage:

  • Izu no Kuni Mingei-kan - A gift shop that sells traditional handicrafts and souvenirs, including pottery, woodwork, and textiles.
  • Izunagaoka Onsen Chaya - A unique souvenir shop that sells hot spring eggs made of wasabi and green tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Izunokuni

  • Etiquette - Visitors should follow the Japanese customs of bowing, removing shoes before entering homes or some restaurants, and using chopsticks.
  • Festivals - The city hosts several festivals throughout the year, such as the Izunagaoka Hachimangu Shrine Festival and the Izunokuni Sakura Festival, that showcase local culture, traditions, and performances.

Best Time to Visit Izunokuni

Izunokuni is an all-year-round destination, with each season offering unique experiences. However, the best time to visit the city is from March to May and from September to November when the climate is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Izunokuni

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the hot springs and enjoy the traditional Japanese experience.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural attractions.
  • Enter hot springs if you have open wounds or contagious diseases.

Safety and Health

Safety in Izunokuni

The city is generally safe, but visiting some natural attractions or hiking trails requires caution, especially in adverse weather conditions. Visitors should also adhere to the warning signs and instructions at the hot springs.

Health in Izunokuni

The city has excellent medical facilities and pharmacies, and visitors can access them if needed. However, it’s advisable to have travel and health insurance before visiting.

Getting to Izunokuni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Izunokuni is accessible via different modes of transportation, including:

  • Airports Near Izunokuni - The nearest airport to Izunokuni is Shizuoka Airport, which is about 70 km away. Visitors can take a train, taxi or bus from the airport to the city.
  • Bus Stations Near Izunokuni - Izunokuni has several bus stations, and visitors can take a bus from other cities, such as Tokyo, Osaka, or Kyoto.
  • Other Options - Visitors can also take a train or drive to Izunokuni, depending on their preference.

How to Get Around in Izunokuni

The city has an efficient public transportation system that comprises buses and trains, making it easy to explore the city and its surrounding areas. Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars from rental agencies in the city.

Where to Go Next from Izunokuni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Izunokuni is an excellent starting point for exploring other cities in the Izu Peninsula, such as:

  • Shimoda - A nearby city that played a significant role in Japan’s opening to the West in the mid-19th century.
  • Atami - A popular hot spring town with numerous historical and cultural attractions.
  • Hakone - A resort town that offers breathtaking views of Mount Fuji, hot springs, and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Izunokuni is a charming city that offers a diverse range of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions that cater to different interests and preferences. Visitors can explore the city’s rich history, indulge in the traditional hot spring experience, and engage in outdoor adventures to experience the beauty of the city and its surroundings.
