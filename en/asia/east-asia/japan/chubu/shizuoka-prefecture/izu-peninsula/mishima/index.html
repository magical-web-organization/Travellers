Introduction to Mishima

Mishima is a small city situated in Izu Peninsula, Shizuoka Prefecture, in the Chubu region of Japan. It lies at the foot of Mount Fuji, one of Japan’s most celebrated natural wonders. Mishima is a quiet and quaint city that offers a perfect blend of rich traditional culture and modern attractions.

Mishima is well-known as a popular tourist destination, and it has many historical landmarks that showcase the city’s rich cultural heritage. The city is also home to several natural attractions that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

In this comprehensive guide, we will highlight some of the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Mishima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mishima Taisha Shrine is one of the most important cultural landmarks in Mishima. It is a Shinto shrine that was built in the 1st century and is dedicated to the deity of Mishima. The shrine has a rich history and features several buildings, including a museum that displays artifacts and relics from the past.

Another historical attraction is the Mishima Station, which is considered a national historical monument. It is an exception design example of the blend of modern architecture and traditional Japanese aesthetics.

Mishima Skywalk suspension bridge is Japan’s longest pedestrian suspension bridge, offering an exciting experience to travelers seeking adventure. It is 400 meters long and connects two peaks, providing a stunning view of the surrounding mountainside.

Natural Attractions of Mishima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mishima is home to several natural attractions that provide opportunities for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Here are some of our top picks:

The Shiraito Falls is a popular tourist attraction in Mishima. It is a stunning waterfall that is over 20 meters high and surrounded by lush vegetation. Visitors can take a relaxing hike through a nature trail leading to the waterfall, and enjoy the breathtaking view.

Kakitagawa Park is an ideal place for nature lovers. This park is famous for cherry blossom trees that bloom in March and offer a spectacular view. During autumn, the park is an explosion of color as the trees change color.

Sento-ga-yama hill is a popular spot for hiking activities. With a height of about 228 meters, visitors can enjoy a panoramic view of the Mishima city and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mishima

Mishima’s natural landscape provides plenty of opportunities for hikers and bikers to explore the area. Here are some popular routes for hiking and biking activities:

Takinomiya Park is a popular place for bike rides. It features a 10-kilometer cycling route that meanders through the park, which is known for its scenic beauty.

Mt. Izu is a popular spot for hiking activities. Visitors can take a trek to the mountain’s peak, which offers a stunning view of the surrounding landscape during clear weather.

Best Things to Do in Mishima: Activities and Tours

There’s so much to do in Mishima that it would be hard to list everything. Here’s a rundown of exciting things to do in the city:

Visit Hakone Open Air Museum - this museum is a unique blend of art and nature. It features breathtaking outdoor sculptures and exhibits, making it a must-visit destination for art lovers.

Take a hot spring bath - Mishima is rich in hot spring baths, offering you an opportunity to relax and unwind in the natural hot water.

Try Sake tasting - Mishima is famous for its high-quality Sake. Visitors can experience the city’s culture by taking a guided tour of a local sake brewery and learn about the process of making this traditional Japanese spirit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mishima

Planning a week-long itinerary in Mishima? Here’s one option that you might consider:

Day 1: Visit Mishima Taisha Shrine and explore the city’s historic district

Day 2: Take a bike ride at Takinomiya Park, followed by a relaxing dip in a hot spring bath

Day 3: Visit Shiraito Falls and enjoy a picnic lunch in the tranquil setting

Day 4: Take a trek to Mt. Izu and enjoy a majestic view of the city from the peak

Day 5: Explore neighboring towns such as Hakone and Atami for exciting cultural experiences

Day 6: Embark on a sake tasting tour and learn about the traditional sake brewing process

Day 7: Take a stroll on the Mishima Skywalk suspension bridge for a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape

Food and Drink Scene of Mishima

Mishima has a rich culinary culture, offering a variety of traditional Japanese dishes that are unique to the region.

Must-Try Dishes

Saba-kaido Sushi is a specialty dish in Mishima. It includes sushi made with the local Mackerel fish that is grilled and served with soy sauce.

Tororo soba is another delicacy in the city, that consists of noodles topped with grated yam and served with soy sauce.

Local Beverages

Mishima is known for its sake and is home to several breweries. Visitors can try the Myokosan, a traditional sake made of pure spring water and local rice.

Where to Stay in Mishima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mishima offers plenty of accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are three recommended hotels:

The Gora Hanaougi hotel provides a relaxing escape to travelers seeking luxurious accommodations. The hotel features hot spring baths, spa treatments, and an on-site restaurant offering traditional Japanese cuisine.

Mishima Royal Hotel is a highly rated hotel that provides visitors with a relaxing stay. It features an outdoor pool, restaurant, and bar.

Mishima Tokyu Hotel is a luxury hotel located only a few meters from Mishima Station. It is ideal for visitors seeking accessibility to transportation.

What to Buy in Mishima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mishima offers several souvenir shops where visitors can purchase traditional Japanese souvenirs. The following items make great souvenir gifts:

  • Shizuoka green tea
  • Traditional Japanese crafts such as ceramics and pottery
  • Sake

Local Culture and Customs of Mishima

Mishima’s traditional customs are deeply rooted in the city’s history.

The city has several annual festivals that showcase traditional Japanese performances such as Taiko drumming, dance, and music. The most notable festival is the Mishima Tanabata festival, which takes place in July and involves colorful decorations and parades.

Best Time to Visit Mishima

Mishima is an all-year-round destination, and visitors can travel any time of the year. However, the best time to visit is during spring and autumn when the weather is mild, and the scenery is breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mishima

Do:

  • Do bow when greeting someone
  • Do take your shoes off before entering a traditional Japanese home or restaurant
  • Do try the local cuisine and participate in traditional activities

Don’t:

  • Don’t tip; it is not a part of Japanese culture
  • Don’t talk loudly or be rowdy in public
  • Don’t touch the display items in stores unless you’re intended to buy it

Safety and Health

Safety in Mishima

Mishima is one of the safest cities in Japan. Visitors can explore the city with minimal concerns about the petty crime rate.

Health in Mishima

Mishima is generally a healthy city. However, visitors must ensure to have their comprehensive travel insurance to avoid any unwanted car accidents in the unfamiliar city streets. Visitors having any pre-existing condition must consult their physician or medical professional before traveling to Mishima.

Getting to Mishima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mishima is easily accessible by various transportation modes.

Airports Near Mishima

The Haneda Airport in Tokyo is the closest airport to Mishima. Travelers can take a train ride to Mishima Station from Haneda Airport.

Bus Stations Near Mishima

Mishima Bus Terminal is the primary bus terminal in the city. It offers several scheduled bus services to neighboring cities and destinations.

Other Options

Mishima Station is the primary train station in the city, and it offers high-speed rail services to Tokyo and other parts of Japan.

How to Get Around in Mishima

Mishima has an efficient transportation system that provides easy access to different parts of the city. Visitors can move around by train or bus, and it’s recommended to get a Pasmo, a pre-paid transportation card that can be used on trains, buses, and subways.

Where to Go Next from Mishima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mishima is located at the heart of Izu Peninsula, offering travelers with another exciting place in Japan.

Hakone is another scenic city located near Mishima. It is well-known for its hot springs, art museums, and parks, making it a must-visit destination.

Atami is another popular destination. It is famous for its hot springs, beaches, and traditional architecture.

Conclusion

Mishima is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a blend of authentic traditional Japanese cultural experiences and scenic natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan an exciting itinerary and explore the city’s attractions to the fullest.
