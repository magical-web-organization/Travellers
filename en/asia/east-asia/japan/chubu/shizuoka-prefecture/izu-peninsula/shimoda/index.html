Introduction to Shimoda

Shimoda is a coastal city nestled in the southern part of Izu Peninsula in Shizuoka prefecture, Japan. This city boasts a rich history and culture, stunning natural attractions, delicious local cuisine, and exciting outdoor activities that make it a top tourist destination.

Shimoda has played a significant role in Japan’s history as the first port opened to the world in the Edo period. The city is dotted with historical sites, museums, and traditional attractions to explore. It is also famous for its hot springs, beaches, and scenic hiking trails.

List of Places to Visit in Shimoda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shimoda History Museum: This museum showcases the city’s history, including the arrival of Commodore Perry, the opening of Japan to the world, and the development of Shimoda over the years.
  • Ryosenji Temple: A beautiful temple in the center of the city that was founded in the early 9th century.
  • Perry Road: A historical street with traditional Japanese architecture and shops that lead to the Commodore Perry Memorial Museum.

Natural Attractions of Shimoda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shirahama Beach: A beautiful white sandy beach with crystal clear waters that offer excellent swimming, surfing, and sunbathing.
  • Mount Nesugata: A scenic mountain with a hiking trail that offers stunning views of the city and the ocean.
  • Kawazu Seven Waterfalls: A series of seven waterfalls set amidst a beautiful natural environment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shimoda

Shimoda is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with its picturesque mountains and seaside. Some of the best hiking/biking routes include:

  • Jogasaki Coast: A scenic 9 km hiking trail along the rugged coastline that offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Amagi Forest Trail: A 16 km hiking trail through a lush green forest that is perfect for nature lovers and bird watchers.
  • Cycling around Izu Peninsula: This is an ideal route that allows travelers to explore the city’s picturesque scenery while cycling around the Izu Peninsula

Best Things to Do in Shimoda: Activities and Tours

  • Geisha Experience: Travelers can dress up as a geisha and experience the art of traditional Japanese entertainment.
  • Onsen Hot Spring Experience: Shimoda has many hot springs where travelers can relax and soak in therapeutic waters.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Shimoda’s clear waters make it an ideal spot for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shimoda

Day 1: Visit the Shimoda History Museum and Ryosenji Temple

Day 2: Take a scenic hike on the Jogasaki Coast followed by seafood dinner at the nearby restaurants.

Day 3: Experience Geisha Entertainment in the evening and attend the Yosakoi Festival

Day 4: Go Snorkeling or Diving and enjoy lunch at any of the seaside restaurants

Day 5: Relax and rejuvenate in one of Shimoda’s Hot Springs and experience the underground cave baths

Day 6: Explore Kawazu Seven Waterfalls and take in the serene and calm environment

Day 7: Stroll along Perry Road, visit the Commodore Perry Memorial Museum, and enjoy Shimoda’s delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Shimoda

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shirasu-bowl: A rice bowl filled with whitebait or baby sardines that are caught locally and served fresh.
  • Wasabi: Shimoda is famous for its high-quality wasabi that is served with sushi, sashimi, and other local dishes.
  • Ise ebi: A type of lobster that is caught in the nearby sea and served grilled or raw.

Local Beverages

  • Shizuoka sake: A high-quality sake brewed in Shizuoka that is famous for its rich flavor and aroma.
  • Green tea: Shimoda is also known for its high-quality green tea that is grown locally

Where to Stay in Shimoda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shimoda has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Shimoda Prince Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on the beachfront with a panoramic view of the sea.
  • Ryokan Tokaikan: A traditional Japanese ryokan with hot spring baths and Japanese-style rooms.
  • Backpackers Shimoda: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of the city that offers shared dormitories and private rooms.

What to Buy in Shimoda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wasabi-related products: Wasabi is a specialty of Shimoda, and travelers can find it in different forms, including pickles, dressing, and sweets.
  • Shizuoka tea: Green tea is another local product that travelers can take back home.
  • Handmade pottery: Shimoda is known for its handmade pottery, and many shops sell beautiful pottery pieces that make excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Shimoda

Shimoda is a traditional city that values its history and culture. It is essential to respect the local customs and traditions to have a pleasant trip. A few things to keep in mind include:

  • Take off shoes when entering a traditional Japanese building
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Avoid being too loud or disruptive in public places

Best Time to Visit Shimoda

The best time to visit Shimoda is from April to November when the weather is mild and warm. May and October are the peak seasons for tourism, and travelers should book accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shimoda

Here are some do’s and don’ts to bear in mind:

Do:

  • Take off shoes when entering a traditional Japanese building.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Follow the rules while hiking and biking.
  • Try local delicacies such as wasabi and seafood dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Be disruptive or loud in public places.
  • Take pictures of geishas without their permission.
  • Visit hot springs with tattoos.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shimoda

Shimoda is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, travelers should be cautious while hiking as the trails could be steep and rugged, and the weather could change quickly.

Health in Shimoda

Shimoda has excellent medical facilities, and travelers can access emergency medical care if needed. It is essential to carry necessary medications and health insurance while traveling.

Getting to Shimoda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shimoda is accessible by different modes of transportation, including air, rail, and bus.

Airports Near Shimoda

The closest airport near Shimoda is Shizuoka Airport, located about 2 hours away from Shimoda. Haneda Airport in Tokyo is another option, and travelers can take a direct bus or train to Shimoda from there.

Bus Stations Near Shimoda

Shimoda has a bus terminal that offers regular bus services to major cities like Tokyo and Osaka.

Other Options

Travelers can also opt for trains to reach Shimoda. The city has its train station, and travelers can catch a direct train from Tokyo, Osaka, or Nagoya.

How to Get Around in Shimoda

Travelers can get around Shimoda by bus, taxi or renting bicycles. The city is highly accessible by buses, and there is a comprehensive bus network that covers all major attractions and destinations.

Where to Go Next from Shimoda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shimoda is a gateway to the Izu Peninsula, and there are many nearby destinations that travelers can explore, including:

  • Atami: A seaside town famous for its hot springs and culinary delights.
  • Izu Nagaoka: A small town known for its scenic beauty and hot spring baths.
  • Hakone: A popular tourist destination famous for its hot springs, museums, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Shimoda is a city that offers something for everyone, from nature lovers to history enthusiasts. Travelers can enjoy the city’s warm hospitality, delicious cuisine, and exciting outdoor activities for a memorable trip. With our comprehensive guide, travelers can plan their trip to Shimoda with ease and enjoy everything this city has to offer.
