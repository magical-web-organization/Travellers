Introduction to Atami

Located in the Izu Peninsula of Shizuoka Prefecture, Atami is a charming coastal town in Japan that has been a popular destination for tourists for decades. Atami’s name literally means “hot ocean,” a nod to the town’s proximity to volcanic activity in the region – hot springs and thermal vents are common sights in Atami. The town is a popular getaway for city-dwellers from Tokyo, given its proximity to the capital city.

List of Places to Visit in Atami

If you’re planning to visit Atami, here’s a list of places that you absolutely must-see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. MOA Museum of Art

The MOA Museum of Art is a private museum that houses some of the most notable works of Japanese and East Asian art in the region. Situated in a beautiful park, the museum’s exhibits change regularly and include everything from ancient calligraphy to contemporary sculpture.

2. Atami Castle

Built in the 16th century, Atami Castle is a must-visit historical site in Atami. Situated on a hilltop overlooking the city, the castle provides stunning views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Atami: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Atami Sun Beach

If you’re looking to soak in some Japanese sun, Atami Sun Beach is the perfect spot. A perfect place to take a dip in the ocean or relax on the beach, Atami Sun Beach is a popular destination for beach-goers thanks to its clear waters and long stretch of sand.

2. Izu Granpal Park

Just a few kilometers outside of the town proper, Izu Granpal Park is a popular theme park in the region. The park features everything from roller coasters to water rides, and is a favorite among families visiting the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Atami

Atami is an ideal location for both hikers and bikers, given its stunning natural landscape.

1. Mount Kogashi Hiking Route

One of the most popular hiking routes in the area is the Mount Kogashi hiking route. The trail is, at times, steep and challenging but rewards hikers with sweeping views of the Atami Bay and the surrounding mountains.

2. Izu Skyline

For bikers looking for an epic ride, the Izu Skyline is a must-ride route. The twisting mountain road provides stunning views of the surrounding forests and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Atami: Activities and Tours

Atami is filled with opportunities to explore the ocean, experience Japanese culture, and indulge in thermal relaxation.

1. Take a Dip in a Japanese Onsen

Given Atami’s proximity to volcanic activity, the region is full of natural hot springs and thermal baths. Visitors can enjoy the various onsens throughout the region, which promise some of the most relaxing experiences.

2. Attend a Tea Ceremony

Atami has a rich cultural history, and attending a tea ceremony is a fun and immersive way to explore that history. The traditional ceremony centers around the preparation and presentation of matcha tea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Atami

If you’re planning on spending a week in Atami, here’s what your itinerary could look like:

Day Activities
1 Relax at Atami Sun Beach
2 Visit Atami Castle, followed by a scenic hike on Mount Kogashi
3 Take a day trip to Izu Granpal Park, followed by dinner at one of Atami’s many seafood restaurants
4 Visit the MOA Museum of Art in the morning, followed by an afternoon exploring the local onsen culture
5 Experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony in the morning before exploring the beautiful Izu Peninsula beaches in the afternoon
6 Spend the day at the beautiful and relaxing Atami Bay resort, taking in the beautiful views and relaxing by the ocean
7 Rent a bike and ride the Izu Skyline, taking in the beautiful scenery along the way

Food and Drink Scene of Atami

No visit to Atami is complete without indulging in the local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Kaisendon

A staple dish of the region, Kaisendon is a dish of steamed rice topped with a mix of raw seafood such as tuna, squid, and salmon.

2. Shizuoka Oden

Oden is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of boiled eggs, daikon radish, and other ingredients that make for a warm and satisfying meal. Shizuoka Oden is a regional variation of the dish and is made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

No visit to Atami is complete without trying some Japanese sake or shochu.

Where to Stay in Atami: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Atami has a wide variety of accommodations to suit any budget whether it is hotels, hostels, or guesthouses.

What to Buy in Atami: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Atami is full of unique products to buy as souvenirs that will transport you back to Japan whenever they are discovered in your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Atami

From traditional tea ceremonies to going to onsens, Atami is home to many unique cultural customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Atami

The best time to visit Atami is between March and May during the spring, when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Atami

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Atami:

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood dishes.
  • Do visit the local onsens.
  • Do attend a tea ceremony.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear shoes inside of someone’s home.
  • Don’t take photos in an onsen.

Safety and Health

Safety in Atami

Atami is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, as with any travel to a new place, travelers should always exercise caution and common sense.

Health in Atami

The town is known for its onsens and hot springs; however, visitors should always consult their doctors if they have any pre-existing health conditions.

Getting to Atami: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Atami

The closest airport to Atami is Tokyo’s Narita airport. From there, visitors can take a train to Atami.

Bus Stations Near Atami

There are bus stations located throughout the town of Atami, making it easy to travel by bus within the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train directly to Atami from Tokyo or another nearby city.

How to Get Around in Atami

In Atami, visitors can use the town’s extensive bus system or walk from place to place within the town center. Taxis are also readily available throughout the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Atami: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Atami is part of the larger Izu Peninsula region, which is home to many other beautiful and interesting towns worth exploring, such as Shuzenji and Shimoda.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in experiencing Japan’s vibrant culture, taking in the beautiful scenery, or indulging in the local cuisine, Atami has something for everyone. From picturesque beaches to lively theme parks, and cultural experiences, it offers a variety of experiences that will make any trip unforgettable.
