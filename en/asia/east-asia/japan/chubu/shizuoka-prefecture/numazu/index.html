Introduction to Numazu

Numazu is a gorgeous port city in Shizuoka prefecture, Chubu region, Japan. It is situated at the northeast coast of Suruga bay and has an area of 186 square kilometers. It features a vibrant city life, rich history, natural beauty, and excellent cuisine. This makes it a popular destination for tourists who want to explore a less touristy and authentic Japanese city.

List of Places to Visit in Numazu

Here are the top places to visit in Numazu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Numazu Imperial Villa Memorial Park: It was built as a summer retreat for the Imperial family, and now it is open to the public. The park has a beautiful garden and a museum showcasing the history of Numazu.
  • Numazu Castle Park: The Numazu Castle was destroyed in the past, but the castle park still preserves the castle ruins, moats, and stone walls. The park has a nice view of the city and Suruga bay.
  • Numazu Deep Sea Aquarium: It is a unique aquarium situated on a cliff overlooking Suruga bay. It showcases various deep-sea creatures, such as giant isopods, spider crabs, and lanternfish.

Natural Attractions of Numazu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Fuji: Although not in Numazu, it is visible from many places in Numazu, including Numazu station, the Numazu Castle Park, and the Numazu Imperial Villa Memorial Park. You can take a bus to Mount Fuji from Numazu Bus Terminal Station.
  • Senbonhama Park: It is a beautiful beach park with a great view of Mount Fuji, Suruga bay, and clean sandy beaches.
  • Jogasaki Coast: It is a picturesque coastline with a walking trail that offers scenic views of the ocean and cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Numazu

  • Numazu Alps Hiking Trail: It is a hiking trail located in the middle of the city, near Numazu station. It offers a beautiful view of the city and a chance to see local flora and fauna.
  • Numazu Forest Cycling Road: It is a cycling route that goes through the beautiful forests in Numazu. It is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Numazu while cycling.

Best Things to Do in Numazu: Activities and Tours

  • Fish Market Tour: Take a guided tour to the Numazu Fish Market to learn about local seafood and its auctioning system.
  • Hot Springs: Numazu is famous for its hot springs. Try one of the many onsens or hot springs in the city for a relaxing experience.
  • Japanese Tea Ceremony: Experience the traditional Japanese tea ceremony at one of the many tea rooms in Numazu.

Week-Long Itinerary for Numazu

Here is a week-long itinerary for Numazu:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Numazu Maritime Museum and the Numazu Deep Sea Aquarium
2 Hike the Numazu Alps Hiking Trail and enjoy the city view from the Numazu Castle Park
3 Explore the Numazu Fish Market and Senbonhama Park
4 Visit the Numazu Imperial Villa Memorial Park and experience the Japanese tea ceremony
5 Take a day trip to Mount Fuji or the Jogasaki Coast
6 Relax at a hot spring and enjoy traditional Japanese cuisine
7 Learn about the history of Numazu at the Numazu History Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Numazu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hayashi Rice: A thick beef and mushroom sauce served over rice.
  • Numazu Gyoza: Dumplings filled with pork and cabbage, served with dipping sauce.
  • Shirasu-don: A rice bowl topped with fresh baby sardines, a local delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Numazu has many local breweries producing excellent sake. Visit one of the many sake breweries for a sake tasting experience.
  • Green Tea: Numazu is also famous for its green tea. Visit a local tea room to taste the different types of green tea.

Where to Stay in Numazu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Numazu:

  • Numazu Grand Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near Numazu station.
  • K’s House: A budget-friendly hostel located in the city center.
  • Benten guesthouse: A guesthouse located near the Numazu Fish Market.

What to Buy in Numazu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shirasu: Numazu is famous for its baby sardines, which are preserved and sold as a snack or ingredient.
  • Green Tea: Numazu’s tea is packaged beautifully and is an excellent souvenir to take home.
  • Sake: Take home a bottle of local sake as a gift or for yourself.

Local Culture and Customs of Numazu

  • Shirasu: It is the most important local ingredient in Numazu. You will find it in almost every dish in Numazu.
  • Numazu Fish Market: The Numazu Fish Market auction system is one of the most organized and efficient in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Numazu

The best time to visit Numazu is during spring (March to May), when cherry blossoms are in bloom, or autumn (September to November), when the weather is mild, and foliage turns into beautiful colors. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (June to July) or summer (August), as they can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Numazu

Do:

  • Explore the local seafood scene
  • Take a traditional tea ceremony lesson
  • Try the hot springs or onsen

Don’t:

  • Take photos in the Numazu Fish Market auction area
  • Litter in public places
  • Enter private property without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Numazu

Numazu is generally safe, and crime rates are low. However, take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping your valuables safe and avoiding isolated areas.

Health in Numazu

Numazu has excellent health facilities, and tap water is safe to drink.

Getting to Numazu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Numazu

The nearest airport to Numazu is Tokyo Haneda Airport. From there, take a direct train or bus to Numazu.

Bus Stations Near Numazu

Numazu is well-connected to nearby cities like Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. You can take a bus from cities like Tokyo and Nagoya directly to Numazu.

Other Options

You can also take a train or rent a car to reach Numazu. The train journey from Tokyo to Numazu takes around 90 minutes via the JR Tokaido Main Line.

How to Get Around in Numazu

The best way to explore Numazu is by walking or cycling, as most attractions are situated in the city center. You can also take a bus or a local train for convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Numazu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hakone: A hot spring resort town with stunning views of Mount Fuji.
  • Izu Peninsula: A beautiful coastline with cliffs, hot springs, and botanical gardens.
  • Fuji Five Lakes: A group of five lakes located at the northern foot of Mount Fuji.

Conclusion

Numazu is an excellent destination for travelers who want to explore authentic Japanese culture, natural beauty, and a unique food scene. From hiking to hot springs to cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in Numazu. So pack your bags, and plan a trip to Numazu now!
