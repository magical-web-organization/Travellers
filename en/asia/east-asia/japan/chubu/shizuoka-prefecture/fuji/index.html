Introduction to Fuji

Fuji, located in Shizuoka prefecture in Chubu, Japan, is one of the most iconic symbols of Japan. This majestic volcano, standing at 3,776 meters tall, is Japan’s highest mountain and attracts millions of visitors each year. Our comprehensive guide will provide all the information you need to plan your trip to Fuji and make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Fuji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fuji-san Hongu Sengen Taisha Shrine: This beautiful Shinto shrine is located at the foot of Mount Fuji and is dedicated to Princess Konohanasakuya, the goddess of Mount Fuji. Visitors can explore the shrine’s many buildings, which are filled with beautiful paintings and sculptures.

  • Iwamotoyama Park: This beautiful park is located on the southeastern slope of Mount Fuji and offers stunning views of the mountain. The park is also home to the Iwamotoyama Shrine, which was founded over 1,300 years ago.

  • Fuji Visitor Center: Located on the northern side of Mount Fuji, this center provides visitors with information on the mountain’s geology and history.

Natural Attractions of Fuji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kawaguchiko Lake: This picturesque lake offers stunning views of Mount Fuji and is one of the most popular spots for viewing the mountain. Visitors can rent boats or walk along the lakeside promenade.

  • Fujinomiya Trail: This trail is one of the most popular hiking routes up Mount Fuji. It is also the shortest and steepest route, making it a challenging climb. The trail is closed during the winter months due to heavy snowfall.

  • Fuji Subaru Line 5th Station: This is the point from where most hikers start their climb up Mount Fuji. The 5th Station offers breathtaking views and visitors can also find shops selling souvenirs, hiking gear, and food.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fuji

  • Subashiri Trail: This trail starts at the Subashiri 5th Station and is known for its spectacular views of the mountain. The trail is popular among hikers and bikers alike.

  • Oshino Course: This biking trail takes visitors through scenic villages and offers stunning views of Mount Fuji. It is a great way to explore the area.

  • Yoshida Trail: This is the most popular and well-maintained hiking trail up Mount Fuji. The trail starts at the Yoshida 5th Station and takes about 6-7 hours to reach the summit.

Best Things to Do in Fuji: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Springs: The Fuji region has an abundance of natural hot springs, also known as onsen. Visitors can relax and soak in these hot springs after a day of hiking and exploring.

  • Stargazing: The Fujisan World Heritage Center offers stargazing tours with professional astronomers. Visitors can watch the stars from a nearby observatory and learn about the constellations.

  • Kimonos and Samurai: Visitors can experience the traditional culture of Japan by trying on a kimono or participating in a samurai experience. These activities are offered in many places around Mount Fuji.

Food and Drink Scene of Fuji

Must-Try Dishes

  • Houtou: This noodle dish is a local specialty of the Fuji region. It is made with udon noodles, vegetables, and miso soup.

  • Wasabi: The Fuji region is known for its wasabi, which is a type of Japanese horseradish. Visitors can try wasabi in a variety of dishes, such as wasabi ice cream or wasabi tempura.

Local Beverages

  • Shizuoka Tea: This region is known for its high-quality green tea. Visitors can experience a tea ceremony and taste different types of tea.

  • Sake: Japan’s famous rice wine is also produced in the Fuji region. Visitors can take a sake tasting tour and learn about the brewing process.

Where to Stay in Fuji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Capsule Hotel Fuji View: This unique hotel offers capsule-style rooms with stunning views of Mount Fuji.

  • Fuji Premium Resort: This resort features hot springs, restaurants, and spacious rooms with great views of the mountain.

  • Camping: Visitors can also camp at one of the many campsites in the area.

What to Buy in Fuji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yoshida-gama Pottery: This pottery is made in the nearby town of Fujiyoshida and is known for its unique design.

  • Mount Fuji-shaped Cookies: These cookies are a popular souvenir and can be found in many shops in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Fuji

Visitors to Fuji should be aware of the customs and traditions of the area. It is important to be respectful of local customs, such as removing shoes when entering certain buildings, bowing when greeting someone, and following proper etiquette during tea ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Fuji

The best time to visit Fuji is during the summer months (July-September) when the weather is warm and the mountain is open for climbing. However, this is also the busiest time of year, and accommodations may be harder to find. Spring and autumn are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fuji

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting shrines and temples.
  • Bring appropriate hiking gear and clothing for the mountain.
  • Have cash on hand as some shops and restaurants do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Throw away trash improperly.
  • Speak loudly or disturb others when visiting temples or shrines.
  • Climb the mountain outside of the climbing season (July-September).

Safety and Health

Safety in Fuji

  • Always be aware of the weather conditions before hiking or climbing the mountain.
  • Follow the guidelines and rules established by the authorities when climbing the mountain.
  • Be prepared for earthquakes, which can occur in the area.

Health in Fuji

  • Be aware of altitude sickness when climbing the mountain.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites, as there have been cases of Japanese encephalitis in the area.

Getting to Fuji: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Tokyo to Fuji: Visitors can take the bullet train from Tokyo to Shizuoka and then take a bus or car to Mount Fuji. The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours.

  • Osaka to Fuji: Visitors can take the bullet train from Osaka to Shizuoka and then take a bus or car to Mount Fuji. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours.

Airports Near Fuji

  • Shizuoka Airport: This regional airport is located approximately 65 kilometers from Mount Fuji.

  • Haneda Airport: Tokyo’s Haneda airport is approximately 150 kilometers from Mount Fuji. There are bus and train options available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Fuji

  • Kawaguchiko Station: This station is located near Kawaguchiko Lake and is a popular starting point for exploring the area.

  • Fujikyu Highland Station: This station is located near the Fuji-Q Highland amusement park.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car and explore the area independently.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in the area but can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Fuji

The best way to get around the Fuji area is by car, as public transportation can be limited in some areas. Visitors can also rent bikes or take guided tours to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Fuji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shimizu: This port city is located approximately 40 kilometers from Mount Fuji and is famous for its seafood and views of the Pacific Ocean.

  • Shizuoka City: This vibrant city is home to many museums, temples, and shrines, as well as an array of shopping and dining options.

  • Hakone: This hot spring town is located approximately 50 kilometers from Mount Fuji and is a popular destination for its natural beauty and hot springs.

Conclusion

Mount Fuji is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan. With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone to enjoy. We hope that this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Fuji.
