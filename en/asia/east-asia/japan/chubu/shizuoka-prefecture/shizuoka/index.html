Introduction to Shizuoka

Shizuoka, the capital of Shizuoka Prefecture, is a coastal city located on the Pacific coast of Honshu Island in Japan. It is surrounded by breathtaking mountains on one side and the sea on the other side. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures.

Shizuoka is home to several ancient temples, museums, and historical sites that showcase its unique culture and traditions. The city is also known for its delicious food, including fresh seafood, tea, and other local delicacies.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Shizuoka, with information on the top attractions, food, accommodations, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Shizuoka

If you’re wondering what to do in Shizuoka, there’s a plethora of attractions to check out. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sunpu Castle: This castle was built in the 16th century and is located on the site of the former residence of the Shogun. The castle is now a museum that showcases the history of the city and the surrounding region.

  • Sumpujo Park: This park is located in the heart of the city and includes several historical sites, including the ruins of the former residence of Tokugawa Ieyasu, the first Shogun of the Tokugawa shogunate. The park also features beautiful gardens, tea houses, and a traditional Japanese restaurant.

  • Shizuoka Sengen Shrine: This shrine is one of the most important historic and cultural sites in Shizuoka. It was built in the 6th century and is dedicated to the three goddesses of Mt. Fuji.

Natural Attractions of Shizuoka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Fuji: The iconic snow-capped peak of Mount Fuji can be seen from many locations throughout Shizuoka. Several hiking trails lead to the summit of the mountain, which is the highest point in Japan.

  • Miho no Matsubara: This stunning pine tree forest is located on the coast of Shizuoka and offers breathtaking views of Mount Fuji and the Pacific Ocean. The area is also known for its black sand beach.

  • Nihondaira Plateau: Located on the slopes of Mt. Kuno, this scenic plateau offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains. It can be reached by a scenic ropeway ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shizuoka

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, there are several hiking and biking routes in Shizuoka that offer stunning views of the mountains and the sea. Here are some popular routes:

  • Nihondaira Plateau to Miho no Matsubara: This 8-mile hiking trail offers breathtaking views of the coastal pine tree forest and Mount Fuji.

  • Makaino Farm Hiking Trail: This hiking trail leads to a farm where you can pick your own strawberries, apples, and other fruits.

  • Shizuoka Cycle Sports Center: This center offers several mountain bike trails that range from beginner to advanced levels.

Best Things to Do in Shizuoka: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours to enjoy in Shizuoka. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Tea Picking: Shizuoka is famous for its tea production, and many tea farms offer tea-picking experiences. You can pluck your own tea leaves and then enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea.

  • Sea Kayaking: This activity allows you to explore the rugged coastline of Shizuoka and is a great way to get up close and personal with sea life.

  • Tokaido Shinkansen Train Ride: This high-speed train ride takes you from Tokyo to Shizuoka in just 90 minutes and offers stunning views of the countryside and Mount Fuji.

Food and Drink Scene of Shizuoka

Shizuoka is a culinary paradise, known for its fresh seafood, tea, and other local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Unagi: Grilled eel is a mainstay on many Shizuoka menus, and there are several restaurants throughout the city that specialize in this dish.

  • Sushi: Shizuoka is located near the sea, which means that there is an abundance of fresh seafood available. Sushi is a must-try dish and can be found at many local restaurants.

  • Mochi: This traditional Japanese dessert is made from glutinous rice and comes in a variety of flavors, including green tea and red bean.

Local Beverages

  • Shizuoka Green Tea: This type of tea is famous for its unique flavor and aroma, and there are several tea farms throughout the city that offer tastings and tours.

  • Shizuoka Sake: This traditional rice wine is brewed in the mountains surrounding Shizuoka and is known for its smooth taste and high quality.

Where to Stay in Shizuoka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mid-Range: Shizuoka Daiichi Hotel or Smile Hotel Shizuoka

  • Luxury: Arai Ryokan or Hotel Associa Shizuoka

What to Buy in Shizuoka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Shizuoka:

  • Green Tea: Shizuoka green tea is famous all over the world and can be bought at several tea shops throughout the city.

  • Shizuoka Sake: This traditional rice wine makes for an excellent souvenir and can be found at several local liquor stores.

  • Hina Dolls: These traditional Japanese dolls are made from ceramic or cloth and are often used to celebrate Hinamatsuri, a festival that takes place in March.

Local Culture and Customs of Shizuoka

Shizuoka has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its temples, museums, and historical sites. The city is also known for its festivals and events, including the annual Daidogei World Cup, which showcases street performers from around the world.

When visiting Shizuoka, it is important to be mindful of local customs and etiquette. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering homes and temples, and to bow as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Shizuoka

The best time to visit Shizuoka is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild and there are several festivals and events taking place throughout the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shizuoka

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Shizuoka:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette: Be mindful of local customs and etiquette, including removing your shoes before entering buildings and bowing as a sign of respect.

  • Try the local food: Shizuoka is known for its delicious food, so be sure to try some of the local specialties.

  • Explore the outdoors: Shizuoka is home to several stunning natural attractions, including Mount Fuji and Miho no Matsubara.

Don’t:

  • Forget to take off your shoes: It is considered impolite to enter buildings with your shoes on.

  • Be disrespectful of local traditions: Respect local customs and traditions, including those related to religion and festivals.

  • Litter: Keep Shizuoka beautiful by disposing of trash properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shizuoka

Shizuoka is generally a safe city, but it is still important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your doors at night and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Shizuoka

Shizuoka has clean tap water, and there are several hospitals and medical facilities throughout the city in case of emergency. It is also a good idea to have travel insurance in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Shizuoka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shizuoka can be easily accessed from Tokyo and other major cities in Japan. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Air: Shizuoka Airport is located about 25 miles from the city center and has domestic flights to several cities in Japan.

  • Train: Shizuoka is located on the Tokaido Shinkansen line, which runs from Tokyo to Osaka. The journey from Tokyo takes about 90 minutes.

  • Bus: Shizuoka can also be accessed by bus from Tokyo and other cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Shizuoka

Shizuoka has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses and trains. The city can also be easily navigated on foot or by bicycle, with several bike rental shops available.

Where to Go Next from Shizuoka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Japan, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Hakone: This mountain town is famous for its hot springs, museums, and scenic views of Mount Fuji.
  • Kyoto: This historic city is home to several ancient temples, shrines, and traditional teahouses.
  • Tokyo: Japan’s bustling capital city is famous for its shopping, nightlife, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Shizuoka is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions, and delicious food, there is something for everyone in this vibrant city. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide and get ready to fall in love with all that Shizuoka has to offer.
