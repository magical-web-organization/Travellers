Introduction to Gotemba

Gotemba is a small city located in Shizuoka prefecture, Chubu region of Japan. It is a popular tourist destination due to its natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures. The city is known for being home to Mount Fuji, the tallest mountain in Japan, and also for its outlet shopping malls. Gotemba is the perfect place to visit if you want to explore Japan’s rich cultural heritage while being surrounded by stunning natural landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Gotemba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gotemba is rich in history and culture, and there are numerous museums and historical sites that are worth visiting. One of the most famous attractions is the Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park, which is home to the iconic Mount Fuji, Japan’s highest mountain.

Other places of cultural significance that you can visit in Gotemba include the Hakone Shrine, which dates back to the 8th century, the Odawara Castle, which was built in the 15th century, and the Yunessun Spa Resort, which is famous for its hot springs and themed baths.

Natural Attractions of Gotemba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gotemba’s natural beauty is unrivaled, from breathtaking lakes to majestic mountains. There are many outdoor adventures to enjoy, including hiking, biking, and camping. Some of the most popular natural attractions in Gotemba include:

  • Lake Yamanaka
  • Mount Fuji
  • Hakone Mountains
  • Komagatake Ropeway
  • Amagi mountain range

You can also enjoy scenic views of nature, such as the breathtaking sunrise at Mount Fuji or the beautiful cherry blossom trees in springtime.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gotemba

The hiking and biking routes in Gotemba are some of the best in Japan. The city is surrounded by mountains, making it the perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the popular hiking routes in Gotemba include:

  • Gotemba Trail
  • Subashiri Trail
  • Fujinomiya Trail

For biking enthusiasts, there are many scenic routes to choose from, such as the Fujigoko Road, which offers stunning views of Mount Fuji.

Best Things to Do in Gotemba: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Gotemba, whether you’re traveling with family or friends. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit the Gotemba Premium Outlets for some shopping
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over Mount Fuji
  • Experience traditional Japanese tea ceremony at the Kubo Tea Ceremony
  • Try Japanese sake tasting at the Gotemba Kogen Brewery
  • Enjoy the stunning views of Mount Fuji on the Hakone Ropeway

Week-Long Itinerary for Gotemba

If you’re planning to spend a week in Gotemba, here’s a recommended itinerary to help you make the most out of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Gotemba

Must-Try Dishes

Gotemba is known for its delicious and unique cuisine, which includes:

  • Shizuoka Oden: A local dish made of fishcakes and vegetables boiled in a light dashi broth.
  • Sakasa Fuji: A sweet and creamy tofu dish served with a sweet miso sauce.
  • Shingen-mochi: A type of mochi made with a sweet paste of red beans and covered with a layer of pounded rice.
  • Wasabi: Gotemba is famous for its spicy wasabi, which is used in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

Gotemba is home to several breweries and wineries, which produce some of the best craft beers and wines in Japan. The Gotemba Kogen Brewery is a popular destination for sake and beer tasting, and it offers stunning views of Mount Fuji from its terrace.

Where to Stay in Gotemba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gotemba offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • The Prince Hakone Lake Ashinoko: A luxury hotel with stunning views of Lake Ashinoko.
  • Hakone Hotel Kowaki-en: A traditional Japanese-style hotel with hot springs.
  • K’s House Hakone: A budget-friendly hostel with clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Gotemba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home souvenirs and local products from Gotemba, here are some of the best options:

  • Wasabi: You can bring home some locally grown wasabi, which is used in many local dishes.
  • Gotemba Premium Outlets: This is the perfect place to buy designer clothes and accessories at discounted prices.
  • Shingen-mochi: This sweet and chewy treat is a must-try, and you can buy it from local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Gotemba

Etiquette and manners are highly valued in Japan, and it’s important to be respectful towards the local culture and customs. Some of the customs to keep in mind when in Gotemba include:

  • Removing your shoes when entering someone’s house or a temple.
  • Bowing instead of shaking hands as a sign of respect.
  • Eating and drinking should be done quietly and with reverence.

Best Time to Visit Gotemba

The best time to visit Gotemba is in the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the weather is cool and the scenery is beautiful. During the summer months of June to August, the temperature can be hot and humid, but this is also a great time to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. The winter months of December to February can be cold and snowy, but this is a great time to enjoy winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gotemba

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes as there is a lot of walking involved.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash as many places do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Be late for appointments or meetings as punctuality is highly valued in Japan.
  • Talk loudly or make unnecessary noise in public spaces.
  • Leave tips as it is considered impolite in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gotemba

Gotemba is generally a safe place to visit, and crime rates are low. However, it’s important to take precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Gotemba

Gotemba offers excellent healthcare facilities, and medical care is easily accessible. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical costs.

Getting to Gotemba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gotemba is easily accessible from Tokyo or other major cities in Japan. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Gotemba

  • Tokyo Haneda Airport (80 km)
  • Narita International Airport (150 km)

Bus Stations Near Gotemba

  • Gotemba Station
  • Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Train: The Gotemba Line, part of the Odakyu Electric Railway system, connects Gotemba to Tokyo and other major cities.
  • Car: Gotemba is easily accessible by car via the Tomei Expressway.

How to Get Around in Gotemba

Getting around Gotemba is easy, with several transportation options available:

  • Bus: Local buses run regularly around Gotemba, and it’s an affordable way to get around.
  • Train: The Gotemba Line connects to other cities in the area, and it’s a convenient way to travel.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available and affordable in Gotemba, and they can be hailed on the street or at designated taxi stands.

Where to Go Next from Gotemba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gotemba is located in a great position for exploring the Chubu region of Japan. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Hakone: A popular tourist destination with hot springs, museums, and beautiful scenery.
  • Yokohama: A vibrant city with a rich history and cultural attractions.
  • Tokyo: The bustling capital of Japan, with endless options for shopping, dining, and sightseeing.

Conclusion

Gotemba is a hidden gem in the Chubu region of Japan, with a rich cultural heritage, stunning nature, and outdoor adventures. From hiking and biking to shopping and dining, there are plenty of things to do and see in Gotemba. Whether you’re traveling with family or friends, Gotemba is a destination that should not be missed.
