Introduction to Kakegawa

Kakegawa is located in Shizuoka prefecture, in Japan’s central region of Chubu. The city is famous for its beautiful parks, hot springs, beautiful landscapes, and historic landmarks. Kakegawa is a great destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and get in touch with the beauty of nature.

List of Places to Visit in Kakegawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kakegawa Castle

Kakegawa Castle, also known as the Shizuoka Kenritsu Kakegawa Koen Castle, is a beautiful landmark that dates back to 1469. It has played an important role in Japanese history, and visitors can explore the castle’s fascinating history while enjoying the beautiful views of the city.

Tokoha Museum of Art

The Tokoha Museum of Art is a must-visit for art lovers. It features a wide variety of artwork from different periods, including traditional Japanese art as well as modern and contemporary art.

Natural Attractions of Kakegawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kakegawa Kachouen Park

The Kakegawa Kachouen Park is a beautiful garden located in the heart of Kakegawa. The park is famous for its beautiful flowers and its picturesque Japanese garden. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the park, admire the beauty of nature, and relax in the peaceful atmosphere.

Nihondaira

Nihondaira is a picturesque mountain range located near Kakegawa. It’s a great place for hiking, with plenty of trails and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kakegawa

Kakegawa Kachouen Park

The Kakegawa Kachouen Park is a perfect destination for hikers and bikers. The park has several hiking trails and paved roads that are perfect for cycling.

Nihondaira

Nihondaira is another popular destination for hikers and bikers. There are several trails that offer different levels of difficulty, and visitors can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Kakegawa: Activities and Tours

Hot Springs

Kakegawa is famous for its natural hot springs. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing soak in one of the city’s many hot springs, which are known for their healing properties.

Tea Plantations

Kakegawa is renowned for its tea plantations, which produce some of the best tea in Japan. Visitors can take a tour of the plantations, learn about the tea-making process, and sample some of the best tea in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Kakegawa

Kakegawa has a rich cultural heritage and has preserved its traditions over the centuries. Visitors can learn about the city’s history and culture by visiting its museums, castles, and temples. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors can get a taste of traditional Japanese culture by taking part in local festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Kakegawa

The best time to visit Kakegawa is in the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November), when the weather is temperate, and the foliage is at its most beautiful. The city is also famous for its cherry blossom season, which takes place in late March to early April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kakegawa

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting temples and shrines
  • Try the local cuisine, including the city’s famous green tea

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public areas
  • Tip in restaurants, as it is not a common practice in Japan
  • Touch the food with your hands, especially at restaurants

Safety and Health

Safety in Kakegawa

Kakegawa is a safe city, with low crime rates. Visitors should take basic precautions, such as not leaving their belongings unattended and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Kakegawa

Kakegawa is a relatively clean city, with good sanitation standards. Visitors should be aware of the risks of foodborne illnesses and take precautions such as washing their hands frequently and drinking bottled water.

Getting to Kakegawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kakegawa

The nearest major airport is Shizuoka Airport, which is located about 48 kilometers from Kakegawa.

Bus Stations Near Kakegawa

Kakegawa Bus Station is serviced by major bus companies, including JR Bus and Willer Express.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kakegawa by train. The city is served by the JR Tokaido Shinkansen and the Tokaido Main Line.

How to Get Around in Kakegawa

Kakegawa is a relatively small city, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bicycle. There are also local buses and taxis available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Kakegawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kakegawa is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations in Shizuoka prefecture, including the cities of Hamamatsu and Shizuoka. Visitors can also take a day trip to Mount Fuji, which is just a short drive away.

Conclusion

Kakegawa is a beautiful city with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s cultural landmarks, enjoy its natural beauty, or simply relax and unwind, Kakegawa is the perfect destination. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to this amazing city.
