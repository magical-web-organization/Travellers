Introduction to Hida

A small town located in the Gifu prefecture, Hida is a hidden gem in Japan’s Chubu region. With a population of approximately 27,000 people, it’s a peaceful and tranquil getaway for tourists that want to experience Japan’s rural landscapes. The town provides a combination of natural beauty, rich history, and local culture that makes it an ideal destination for tourists that want an authentic Japanese experience.

List of Places to Visit in Hida

Here are the must-visit attractions and points of interest for anyone looking to explore Hida.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or just interested in exploring Japan’s rich cultural heritage, make sure you visit these museums and historical places:

  • Takayama Jinya
  • Hida Folk Village (also known as Hida-no-Sato)
  • Yoshijima Heritage House
  • Tsugawa Family Residence
  • Hida Takayama Museum of Art

Natural Attractions of Hida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hida is known for its stunning natural beauty, so make sure you take time to enjoy its outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Shirakawago
  • Kamikochi
  • Okuhida Hot Springs
  • Norikura Plateau

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hida

Hida’s mountainous terrain makes it an ideal location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are the best trails to explore for breathtaking scenery and an adventurous experience:

  • Kamikochi Nature Trail
  • Norikura Skyline
  • Daijokura Nature Trail
  • Shirakawago Hiking Course

Best Things to Do in Hida: Activities and Tours

Here are some unique activities and tours to engage in to make the most of your visit to Hida:

  • Hida beef tasting tour
  • Sake brewery tour
  • Traditional Japanese paper making class
  • Hida cooking class

Week-Long Itinerary for Hida

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Hida:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Takayama Jinya and explore Hida Folk Village
Day 2 Hike Kamikochi Nature Trail and soak in Okuhida Hot Springs
Day 3 Take a day trip to Shirakawago and explore the village
Day 4 Visit Tsugawa Family Residence and Yoshijima Heritage House
Day 5 Take a cooking class and learn how to make a traditional Hida meal
Day 6 Explore the Norikura Skyline and Daijokura Nature Trail
Day 7 Take a sake brewery tour and taste some of the local sake

Local Culture and Customs of Hida

Hida has a unique and interesting culture that’s worth experiencing. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect for nature and the environment
  • Onsen etiquette
  • Traditional Japanese hospitality
  • Importance of ceremony and ritual

Best Time to Visit Hida

Hida is beautiful all year round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re interested in hiking, biking, and outdoor adventures, the best time to visit is during the summer months of June to September. If you’re interested in winter sports or enjoying the hot springs, then the winter months of December to February are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hida

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Hida:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Treat nature and the environment with respect.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Disrespect cultural and religious sites.
  • Speak loudly or disrupt the peaceful atmosphere in public areas.

Safety and Health

Hida is a safe and healthy destination to explore. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Hida

  • Keep important documents, money and other valuables safe.
  • Follow traffic rules and be careful when crossing the road.
  • Be aware of wildlife when hiking

Health in Hida

  • Pack a basic first aid kit.
  • Stay hydrated and bring enough water when hiking.
  • Be mindful of food-borne illnesses while eating out.

Getting to Hida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hida is easily accessible via several modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Hida

  • Chubu Centrair International Airport
  • Nagoya Airfield

Bus Stations Near Hida

  • Takayama Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Shinkansen (Tokaido Shinkansen to Nagoya and then change to Limited Express Wide View Hida)
  • JR train (JR Takayama line)

How to Get Around in Hida

Hida is a small town, and most places of interest are within walking distance. However, for longer trips or more remote locations, renting a bike or a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Hida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hida’s location makes it a convenient starting point for visiting nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some recommended options:

  • Nagoya City
  • Kyoto City
  • Kanazawa City
  • Toyama City

Conclusion

Hida is an idyllic destination that provides a combination of natural beauty, rich history, and local culture that can’t be found anywhere else. With plenty of attractions, outdoor adventures, and culinary experiences to choose from, it’s an ideal destination for travelers that want an authentic Japanese experience. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or culture seeker, Hida has something to offer everyone.
