Ena Travel Guide: Discover the Hidden Gems of Gifu, Japan

Ena is a picturesque town located in Gifu, a prefecture in the Chubu region of Japan. Often overlooked by tourists, Ena offers a perfect blend of traditional and modern Japan and is a fantastic destination for those seeking an authentic cultural experience.

This comprehensive travel guide for Ena will help you plan your trip to this hidden gem of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Ena

Ena is a perfect blend of natural and cultural attractions. Here are some of the best places to add to your Ena itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ena City Museum of Art: Featuring a collection of Japanese and international contemporary art, this museum is a must-visit for all art lovers.
  • Ena Gorge Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Ena Gorge and its surrounding area, including fossils and flora and fauna found in the region.
  • Kokubun-ji Temple: This temple, dating back to the Nara period, features a five-story pagoda and a beautiful garden.
  • Iwamura Castle: This 16th-century castle is located on top of a hill and provides visitors with a spectacular view of Iwamura town and the surrounding valley.

Natural Attractions of Ena: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ena Valley: A beautiful valley famous for its autumn foliage, Ena Valley is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride.
  • Mt. Ena: At 1,924 meters high, Mt. Ena is one of the most famous peaks in Japan and offers an unforgettable hiking experience.
  • Iwamura Yanagi-ga-take Natural Park: Situated near the Iwamura Castle, this park offers a tranquil setting for a peaceful walk or picnic.
  • Ena Gorge: With its unique rock formations and stunning views, Ena Gorge is a must-visit for all nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ena

Ena is known for its stunning nature and is home to several hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Mt. Ena Hiking Trail: This 18 km trail takes visitors to the summit of Mt. Ena and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Ena Valley Biking Trail: This 9 km trail follows the Ena River and takes visitors through small towns and picturesque countryside.
  • Iwamura Historical Town Walking Trail: This 4 km trail takes visitors through the nostalgic streets of Iwamura town, offering a glimpse into the town’s rich history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Ena: Activities and Tours

Ena has something to offer for every kind of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Ena:

  • Take a Sake Brewery Tour: Ena is known for its delicious sake, and visitors can take a brewery tour and taste the different varieties of sake.
  • Participate in a Traditional Tea Ceremony: Experience Japanese culture at its finest by participating in a traditional tea ceremony.
  • Take a Scenic Train Ride: The JR Takayama Line, which passes through Ena, offers a scenic train ride through the Japanese Alps.

Food and Drink Scene of Ena

Ena’s food and drink scene is a must-try for all visitors. Here are some of the best dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hida Beef: This regional specialty is a must-try for all meat lovers.
  • Hiyajiru: A refreshing summer dish made with miso and cucumbers, perfect for hot days.
  • Soba Noodles: Made with locally sourced buckwheat, Ena’s soba noodles are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Ena Sake: Ena is known for its exceptional sake, made with locally sourced water.
  • Green tea: Ena tea is known for its strong flavor and unique aroma.

Where to Stay in Ena: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ena has several accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Ena Hotel: A modern hotel in the heart of Ena city, offering comfortable rooms and a range of amenities.
  • Ena Ryokan: A traditional Japanese inn, complete with tatami rooms and onsen baths.
  • Minshuku Murasugi: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the Ena Gorge, offering clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Ena: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ena is famous for its traditional crafts and local products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to take home:

  • Ena Pottery: Ena is known for its traditional pottery, which is made using locally sourced clay.
  • Ena Textiles: Ena textiles are hand-woven using traditional methods and patterns.
  • Ena Sake: Take a bottle or two of Ena’s famous sake home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ena

Ena has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Remove Shoes in Traditional Japanese settings: Many traditional Japanese inns and restaurants require visitors to remove their shoes when entering. Be sure to wear socks and keep your feet clean.
  • Bowing: Bowing is a common sign of respect in Japan. When greeting someone or expressing thanks, it’s customary to bow slightly.
  • Onsen Etiquette: When visiting an onsen (hot spring), it’s important to wash before entering and to enter the water without a swimsuit. Tattoos are often not allowed in onsen, so be sure to check before entering.

Best Time to Visit Ena

Ena is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here’s a breakdown of the best time to visit:

  • Cherry Blossom Season: Late March to early April is the best time to visit for cherry blossom season.
  • Autumn Foliage: Late October to early November is the best time to visit for autumn foliage.
  • Hiking and Biking: Spring and fall (April to June and September to November) are the best times to visit for hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ena

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ena:

Do:

  • Try Local Food: Ena has a rich food culture, so be sure to try the local specialties.
  • Respect Local Customs: Bowing and removing shoes in traditional Japanese settings are signs of respect.
  • Be Polite: Japanese culture values politeness, so be sure to say “please” and “thank you” often.

Don’t:

  • Be Loud in Public: Japanese culture values quietness and being mindful of others, so avoid speaking loudly in public spaces.
  • Tip: Tipping is not customary in Japan, so don’t feel obligated to tip at restaurants or hotels.
  • Skip the Onsen: Visiting an onsen is a quintessential part of Japanese culture, so don’t skip this experience.

Safety and Health

Ena is a safe destination, but it’s important to take precautions when travelling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Ena

  • Be Aware of Traffic: Japanese roads can be narrow, so be cautious when walking or biking on the road.
  • Be Careful on Hiking Trails: Be sure to stay on designated hiking trails and be cautious of loose rocks and slippery surfaces.
  • Watch Out for Wildlife: Be cautious of wild animals when hiking or walking in natural areas.

Health in Ena

  • Stay Hydrated: Ena can get hot in the summer months, so be sure to stay hydrated.
  • Watch Out for Allergies: Pollen counts can be high in Ena, so be sure to bring appropriate medication if you suffer from allergies.
  • Be Prepared for Earthquakes: Japan is prone to earthquakes, so be sure to familiarize yourself with safety procedures if an earthquake occurs.

Getting to Ena: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ena is easily accessible from major Japanese cities. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Ena

  • Chubu Centrair International Airport: Located in Nagoya, is the closest international airport to Ena.

Bus Stations Near Ena

  • Meitetsu Bus Terminal: Located in Nagoya, offers direct buses to Ena.

Other Options

  • Train: Ena is accessible by train via the JR Takayama Line.
  • Car: Ena is accessible by car via the Chuo Expressway.

How to Get Around in Ena

Ena is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Biking is also a popular way to get around, and rental bikes are available.

Where to Go Next from Ena: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ena is located in the heart of the Japanese Alps and is a great base for exploring nearby destinations such as Takayama, Gero Onsen, and Shirakawa-go.

Conclusion

Ena is a hidden gem in Japan, and this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your trip. With its unique blend of natural and cultural attractions, authentic food scene, and welcoming locals, Ena is a perfect destination for those seeking a deeper understanding of Japanese culture.
