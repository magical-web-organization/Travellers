Introduction to Mino (Gifu)

Mino (Gifu) is a small city located in the Gifu prefecture of Japan. It is known for its traditional paper-making industry, which was added to UNESCO’s Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2014. Mino (Gifu) boasts a rich culture, beautiful natural scenery, and authentic Japanese experiences that will make any traveler fall in love with this quaint city. In this travel guide, we will share with you everything you need to know before you plan your trip to Mino (Gifu).

Best Time to Visit Mino (Gifu)

The best time to visit Mino (Gifu) is between September and mid-November when the weather is mild and dry, and the autumn leaves are at their peak. Spring is also a lovely time to visit Mino (Gifu) with the blooming of cherry blossoms, but the crowds in touristic places can be overwhelming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mino (Gifu)

Do:

  • Practice good manners when visiting the temples and shrines.
  • Try the city’s famous ramen noodles and sake.
  • Take off your shoes when entering guesthouses, a home of a Japanese family, and temples and shrines.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or play music on public transportation.
  • Touch or step over sacred stones in a shrine or temple.
  • Smoke in designated non-smoking areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mino (Gifu)

Mino (Gifu) is a safe city, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any other city, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Mino (Gifu)

The city has excellent health facilities; however, travelers should ensure they have health insurance before visiting Mino (Gifu), and water is safe to drink from the tap.

Getting to Mino (Gifu): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mino (Gifu)

The nearest airport is Chubu Centrair International Airport, which is approximately 90 kilometers from Mino City.

Bus Stations Near Mino (Gifu)

Gifu Bus Company connects travelers from Nagoya, Takayama, and Gifu to the city of Mino (Gifu) by bus.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Mino (Gifu) by Japan Rail (JR) or Shinkansen, but there is no direct train line to Mino (Gifu). The nearest train station, Gifu Station, is approximately 30 minutes from Mino City, and then visitors can take a local bus to the city’s center.

How to Get Around in Mino (Gifu)

Mino (Gifu) City is relatively small, and many areas of the city are accessible on foot. Visitors can also take local city buses in Mino (Gifu) to reach areas of the city that are further out, such as Tono Nature Park.
