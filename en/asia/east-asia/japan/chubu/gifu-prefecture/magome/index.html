Magome, located in Gifu prefecture in the Chubu region of Japan, is a picturesque village that offers visitors a glimpse into Japan’s rich history and culture. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Magome, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs.

Introduction to Magome

Magome is a historic village that was once an important post town on the Nakasendo highway, one of the five routes that connected Tokyo to the rest of Japan during the Edo period. Today, Magome is a popular tourist destination known for its well-preserved traditional architecture and picturesque streets that wind their way through the hills.

List of Places to Visit in Magome

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Magome Tourist Information Center: This is a great place to start your visit to Magome. The center offers maps and brochures in multiple languages and provides information about the town’s history and culture.

  • Magome Wakihonjin Museum: Located in the heart of the village, this museum is housed in a restored honjin, or inn, that once served travelers on the Nakasendo highway. The museum features exhibits on the history of Magome and the Nakasendo highway.

  • Toson Memorial Hall: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of the famous Japanese writer Shimazaki Toson, who was born in Magome.

Natural Attractions of Magome: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Magome Pass: This historic walking trail connects Magome to the nearby village of Tsumago and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Magome Observatory: Located at the top of a hill overlooking the village, this observation deck offers breathtaking views of the Kiso Valley and the Southern Alps.

  • Kayano Dam: This scenic spot is located just outside of Magome and offers visitors the opportunity to paddle a boat or enjoy a picnic while taking in the beautiful mountain scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Magome

Magome is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails that wind their way through the hills and forests surrounding the village. Some popular routes include:

  • Magome to Tsumago Trail: This historic trail is a moderate, two-hour hike that connects the two villages and offers stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Magome to Odaki-no-Taki Falls Trail: This is a challenging trail that takes hikers through dense forests and past several scenic waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Magome: Activities and Tours

  • Sake Brewery Visit: Magome is known for its delicious locally brewed sake, and visitors can take a tour of the local brewery to learn about the brewing process and sample some of the town’s famous rice wine.

  • Traditional Crafts Workshops: Visitors can take part in workshops that teach traditional Japanese crafts such as pottery, calligraphy, and weaving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Magome

Day 1: Arrive in Magome, explore the village, and visit the Magome Tourist Information Center, the Magome Wakihonjin Museum, and the Toson Memorial Hall.

Day 2: Hike the Magome to Tsumago trail and explore Tsumago.

Day 3: Take a sake brewery tour and visit the Kayano Dam.

Day 4: Participate in traditional crafts workshops and explore the village’s shops and restaurants.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Mt. Ontake, a sacred mountain that offers scenic hiking trails.

Day 6: Explore the forests and waterfalls of the Magome to Odaki-no-Taki Falls trail.

Day 7: Depart from Magome.

Food and Drink Scene of Magome

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hida beef: This local delicacy is a type of wagyu beef that is known for its tender texture and rich flavor.

  • Soba noodles: Magome is famous for its delicious handmade soba noodles, which are made from locally grown buckwheat.

  • Fish dishes: As Magome is located near rivers and lakes, it’s easy to find fresh seafood dishes like grilled ayu (sweetfish) and trout.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Magome is known for its delicious locally brewed sake, which can be sampled at the local brewery.

  • Japanese green tea: Visitors can enjoy freshly brewed Japanese green tea, which is grown locally in the surrounding hills.

Where to Stay in Magome: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Magome offers a variety of accommodations, including traditional Japanese inns (ryokans), guesthouses, and Western-style hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Magomechaya: This ryokan is located in the heart of the village and offers traditional Japanese-style rooms and meals.

  • Magome Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers private and shared rooms, a communal kitchen, and a cozy living room where guests can relax and meet other travelers.

  • Magome Seiryu-so: This Western-style hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and a restaurant that serves local cuisine.

What to Buy in Magome: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Magome is known for its traditional Japanese crafts, locally produced sake and tea, and delicious Hida beef. Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Lacquerware: Magome is known for its high-quality lacquerware, which is crafted using traditional techniques.

  • Japanese green tea: Visitors can purchase locally grown Japanese green tea to take home as a souvenir.

  • Hida beef: Visitors can purchase Hida beef to take home and prepare for themselves or to give as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Magome

Magome is a traditional Japanese village, and visitors should take care to respect local customs and traditions. Some important things to keep in mind include:

  • Remove shoes when entering homes and traditional Japanese inns.

  • Be mindful of noise levels, especially late at night and early in the morning.

  • Respect local shrines and temples by following posted rules and guidelines.

Best Time to Visit Magome

The best time to visit Magome is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the village is at its most beautiful. In the spring, visitors can enjoy the cherry blossoms that bloom throughout the village, while in the fall, the trees turn vibrant shades of red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Magome

Do:

  • Take your shoes off before entering traditional Japanese inns and homes.

  • Try the local soba noodles and sake.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local shrines and temples.

  • Make excessive noise, especially late at night and early in the morning.

  • Vandalize or litter in the village or surrounding nature areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Magome

Magome is a safe and friendly village, but visitors should take the usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. It’s important to watch out for traffic while walking around the village, and to be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking in remote areas.

Health in Magome

Magome is a clean and healthy village with modern medical facilities. Visitors should take care to drink bottled water, especially when hiking or traveling in rural areas, and to get any necessary vaccinations before traveling to Japan.

Getting to Magome: Transportation Options and Logistics

Magome is not accessible by car, but there are several transportation options available to visitors:

Airports Near Magome

  • Chubu Centrair International Airport (NGO) is the closest airport to Magome, located about 90 minutes away by train and bus.

  • Tokyo Narita International Airport (NRT) and Tokyo Haneda International Airport (HND) are both located about 4 hours away by train and bus.

Bus Stations Near Magome

  • Magome Bus Terminal is located in the center of the village and is the main point of entry for visitors arriving by bus.

Other Options

  • Visitors can take the shinkansen (bullet train) to nearby Nagoya and then transfer to a local train and bus to reach Magome.

How to Get Around in Magome

Magome is a small village that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire taxis to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Magome: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tsumago: This nearby village is also located on the Nakasendo highway and offers visitors a similar glimpse into Japan’s rich history and culture.

  • Matsumoto: This nearby city is home to the famous Matsumoto Castle, one of Japan’s most beautiful and historic castles.

Conclusion

Magome is a beautiful and historic village that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Japan’s rich culture and history. Whether you’re interested in hiking, traditional crafts, or simply relaxing in a picturesque setting, Magome is a must-visit destination that should be on your Japan travel itinerary.
