Introduction to Ogaki

Ogaki is a beautiful city located in Gifu Prefecture, in the Chubu region of Japan. It is a surprisingly large city, with a population of over 160,000. The city boasts of natural beauty and a strong cultural heritage, as evidenced by its museums and historical sites. Tourists can enjoy hiking and biking, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. With its tranquil surroundings, friendly people, and a perfect mix of culture and nature, Ogaki has become a popular destination for domestic and international visitors.

In this travel guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, food to eat, and where to stay in Ogaki. We will provide you with useful tips about the local culture, customs, and traditions, and we will also recommend nearby destinations and excursions. This comprehensive guide will help make your trip to Ogaki an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Ogaki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ogaki has a rich history and culture that is evident through its numerous museums and historical places. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

Ogaki Castle

Ogaki Castle, also known as Sunomata Castle, is a reconstructed castle located in Ogaki city. The original castle was built in 1537 but was destroyed in 1600. The castle was rebuilt in 1989, and today, it stands as a symbol of the city’s rich history and culture. You can explore the castle’s interior, which houses a museum featuring samurai armor, swords, and other historical artifacts.

Akiba Shrine

The Akiba Shrine is an ancient and beautiful shrine that is said to have been established in the 3rd century. The shrine is dedicated to the deity of health, and many visitors come here to pray for good health. The shrine has a serene atmosphere, and visitors can enjoy a peaceful stroll along the beautiful gardens and the pond.

Natural Attractions of Ogaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ogaki is blessed with natural beauty, and many outdoor activities await tourists who want to enjoy the beauty of nature. Here are some of the natural attractions that should be on your itinerary:

Mount Kinka

Mount Kinka is a 329-meter-high mountain located in Gifu Prefecture. You can take a cable car ride to the top of the mountain and enjoy the breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding mountains. You can also hike to the summit and explore the beautiful forested areas along the way.

Ogaki Park

Ogaki Park is a beautiful park located in the heart of the city. The park has a large pond, a Japanese garden, a tea house, and many other attractions. There is also a zoo inside the park, where you can see various animals such as lions, tigers, and bears.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ogaki

Ogaki has many hiking and biking trails that are perfect for nature lovers. Here are some of the best routes:

Mount Yoro

Mount Yoro is a 624-meter-high mountain located in the Yoro Mountains. The mountain is famous for its beautiful cherry blossoms and autumn leaves. You can hike to the summit and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and the city.

Ogaki Bike Path

The Ogaki Bike Path is a 10-km-long path that runs alongside the Yahagi River. The path is perfect for biking or walking, and you can enjoy the beautiful scenery along the way.

Best Things to Do in Ogaki: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting museums, historical sites, and enjoying outdoor activities, there are many other fun things to do in Ogaki. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

Sake Brewery Tour

Ogaki is famous for its sake, and you can take a tour of the local breweries to learn more about the sake-making process. You can also taste some of the best sake produced in the region.

Tea Ceremony

You can experience traditional Japanese culture by attending a tea ceremony in Ogaki. The ceremony involves the preparation and presentation of matcha tea, which is a finely ground green tea.

Food and Drink Scene of Ogaki

Ogaki offers a diverse range of food and drinks that will delight your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Tenmusu

Tenmusu is a popular dish in Ogaki that consists of tempura shrimp wrapped in rice and seaweed. It is a delicious snack or light meal that you can enjoy at any time of the day.

Miso Katsu

Miso Katsu is a local dish that consists of deep-fried pork cutlets served with a thick miso sauce. It is a hearty and filling meal that is perfect for lunch or dinner.

Local Beverages

Sake

Ogaki is famous for its sake, and you can find various types of sake produced in the region. Sake is a rice wine that is brewed using traditional methods.

Mugi-cha

Mugi-cha is a refreshing beverage made from roasted barley. It is a popular drink in Ogaki, especially during the summer months when it is served cold.

Where to Stay in Ogaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options in Ogaki that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended places to stay:

Hotel Route-Inn Ogaki

Hotel Route-Inn Ogaki is a modern hotel located in the city center. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and an on-site restaurant.

Yumotokan

Yumotokan is a traditional Japanese inn located near the Yoro Mountains. The inn offers comfortable rooms with tatami flooring and futon beds. Guests can enjoy a hot spring bath and a traditional Japanese breakfast.

What to Buy in Ogaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ogaki offers various souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a reminder of their trip to the city. Here are some recommended items:

Sake

Take home some of the best sake produced in Ogaki as a souvenir for your friends or family back home.

Japanese Pottery

Ogaki is famous for its pottery, and you can find various types of ceramic dishes, cups, and bowls that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ogaki

Ogaki has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some essential things to keep in mind:

  • Remove your shoes when entering a private residence, temple, or shrine.
  • Bow when meeting someone, as it is a sign of respect.
  • Do not speak loudly in public places or on public transportation.
  • Avoid using your mobile phone in public places.

Best Time to Visit Ogaki

The best time to visit Ogaki is during spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms or autumn leaves are in full bloom. Summer (June to August) can be hot and humid, and winter (December to February) can be cold, but both seasons offer unique experiences, such as snow activities in winter or summer festivals in summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ogaki

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Ogaki:

Do:

  • Carry cash as many small shops and restaurants do not accept credit cards.
  • Follow local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes at the entrance of a house, temple, or shrine.
  • Respect the privacy of others and avoid taking photographs of people without their permission.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places, including streets, parks, and train stations.
  • Eat or drink while walking in public places.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places or on public transportation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ogaki

Ogaki is a safe city with a low crime rate, so visitors need not worry about safety issues. However, visitors should be cautious when crossing the streets, especially during rush hour.

Health in Ogaki

Ogaki has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where visitors can receive medical attention if needed. However, visitors are advised to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Ogaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ogaki

The nearest airport to Ogaki is Chubu Centrair International Airport, which is approximately 80 km away. From the airport, you can take a train to Ogaki Station.

Bus Stations Near Ogaki

Ogaki has a central bus station called Ogaki Station Bus Terminal, where you can catch buses to various destinations in and around the city.

Other Options

You can also reach Ogaki by train from Tokyo or Osaka. There are frequent trains that operate between these two cities and Ogaki.

How to Get Around in Ogaki

Ogaki has an excellent public transportation system, including buses and trains. Visitors can purchase a day-pass that allows unlimited travel on buses and trains within the city. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Ogaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ogaki is well-connected to other popular tourist destinations in Japan. Here are some recommended nearby destinations and excursions:

Nagoya

Nagoya is a major city located approximately 40 km away from Ogaki. The city is famous for its castle, museums, and traditional food.

Inuyama

Inuyama is a small historic city located approximately 35 km away from Ogaki. The city is home to one of the oldest and smallest castles in Japan.

Conclusion

Ogaki is a beautiful city that offers a perfect blend of nature and culture. With its numerous museums, historical sites, and outdoor activities, Ogaki has something to offer every visitor. By following this comprehensive guide, you can make the most of your trip to Ogaki and have an unforgettable experience.
