Introduction to Tarui

Hidden in the heart of Gifu, Tarui is a town that is truly worth experiencing. Unlike the bustling cities of Japan, Tarui offers visitors an opportunity to experience the traditional, laid-back and peaceful lifestyle of the rural countryside. Rich in history and culture, this town offers a wide range of exciting outdoor experiences, incredible food, and a chance to immerse yourself in local traditions. From exploring natural wonders to engaging in local festivals, Tarui offers a unique chance to explore a lesser-known gem in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Tarui

There are many places to visit in Tarui, from cultural landmarks to breathtaking natural beauty. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yazoriki Park

Located in the heart of Tarui, Yazoriki Park is a must-visit spot for history lovers. The park is home to centuries-old ruins of a castle, which provides visitors an insight into the rich and complex history of Tarui. You can stroll through the grounds, enjoy the picturesque views of the traditional Japanese gardens, and learn more about the cultural significance of the region.

Tarui-juku Honjin

For a unique historical experience, head to Tarui-juku Honjin, which is a stunning wooden building that functioned as a lodge for government officials during Japan’s feudal era. Today, it has been converted into a museum showcasing different exhibits such as traditional clothing, samurai armor, and various tools used in ancient Japan.

Natural Attractions of Tarui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tarui River

The Tarui River proudly winds its way through the town, and its beauty draws numerous photographers, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers. This spot is famous for canoeing, fishing, and, most importantly, the amazing natural scenery that surrounds it.

Hyonosen-Ushiroyama-Nagisan Quasi-National Park

The park is an absolute highlight for nature enthusiasts as it boasts stunning mountain views, hiking trails, and peaceful picnic spots. It is an ideal destination for anyone looking to unwind in a serenely peaceful location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarui

Tarui is a town that offers an extraordinary opportunity to experience the stunning Japanese countryside. To explore the city to its fullest, hiking or biking is the ideal way to go about it.

  • Daikakujiyama Route
  • Mount Hyonosen

Best Things to Do in Tarui: Activities and Tours

Tarui offers a unique set of activities to try, regardless of your interests. Here are some recommendations to experience the best of the town:

  • Attend a local festival such as the Tarui Hagoita Festival
  • Enjoy a Tarui River Cruise
  • Join a cultural workshop to learn a traditional Japanese art form like calligraphy or ikebana
  • Visit geta workshop and make your own Japanese wooden sandals.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarui

Food is an essential part of Japanese culture, and it holds true for Tarui as well. The town offers an array of unique food choices that are worth trying.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karashi Renkon: A must-have dish, this delicacy is composed of lotus root stuffed with miso and mustard paste and deep-fried to golden brown perfection.
  • Soba Noodles: Soba noodles, made from buckwheat flour, are famous all over Japan, and Tarui is no exception. Enjoy this popular dish in its authentic local flavor in Tarui.
  • Tarui-Inaniwa Udon: Udon noodles made from the finest wheat flour and yeast are another must-try dish. This incredible noodle recipe goes back centuries and is still prepared using traditional methods today.

Local Beverages

Throughout time, Japan has been synonymous with fabulous tea culture. Here are some unique drinks to try during your Tarui trip:

  • Sake: Known as “rice wine,” Sake has been used in Japanese culture for centuries. Try the Tarui Sake, made from specially prepared rice and pristine water sources.
  • Green Tea: This local specialty not only has a unique flavor but also contains numerous health benefits.

Where to Stay in Tarui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarui has a range of accommodation options, from hotels to traditional Japanese inns. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Tarui: Surrounded by lush greenery, Hotel Tarui is an excellent choice for a comfortable overnight stay. The hotel offers both Western-style and Japanese-style rooms.
  • Minshuku Takei: A traditional guesthouse with clean rooms, Japanese decor, and friendly staff. This place provides the unique opportunity to experience cultural immersion during your stay.
  • Ryu Resort & Spa: A well-appointed property with spacious rooms and top-notch amenities, Ryu Resort & Spa also boasts of a picturesque location amidst the mountains.

What to Buy in Tarui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here is a list of souvenirs and local goods that one can buy in Tarui:

  • Inden-yaki wristbands: Inden-yaki is a traditional Japanese craft that makes leather products like wallets and wristbands colored with Urushi lacquer.
  • Owariya Honten: Owariya Honten is famous for its soba noodles, which you can carry back home.
  • Sake: For those interested in taking some of Tarui’s finest sake back home, visit the local brewery known for its refined sake.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarui

Tarui is a town that appreciates its cultural heritage, and visitors should expect to experience Japanese customs and traditions during their visit. Visitors should consider dressing modestly while visiting any religious or historic cultural sites, participate in traditional Japanese activities, and be mindful of littering and noise pollution.

Best Time to Visit Tarui

The best time to visit Tarui is between September and November when comfortable temperatures make it ideal for outdoor activities. Additionally, July and August are perfect for exploring other water activities, such as canoeing activities, along the river in Tarui.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarui

Do:

  • Respect local laws, traditions, and customs.
  • Be mindful of others in public spaces.
  • Carry cups for drinks that allows refilling, rather than plastic bottled water.

Don’t:

  • Eat and walk on streets.
  • Use chopsticks inconsiderately.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarui

Tarui is a safe town with little to no concern about crime. However, visitors should always exercise standard precautions, such as keeping their wallets and purses safe, always have their valuable goods with them, and locking the doors of accommodations.

Health in Tarui

Tarui is a relatively safe place to visit when it comes to health matters. The availability of medical facilities is relatively good in Tarui. It is essential to check the weather updates before planning to visit the surrounding natural areas to avoid any adverse weather conditions.

Getting to Tarui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarui

The nearest airports to Tarui are:

  • Centrair International Airport (NGO)
  • Osaka International Airport (ITM)

From the airport, visitors can rent a car or take a train/bus to reach Tarui.

Bus Stations Near Tarui

The Tarui bus station is located on the south side of the town, with frequent buses available for neighboring towns, including Mino City, Gifu City, and Seki City.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to explore the town and its surroundings, as public transport options in Tarui can be limited.

How to Get Around in Tarui

Biking and hiking are the most recommended options to explore Tarui. However, for travelers who prefer to use public transport, buses are the main modes of transport for intra-city travel. Alternatively, travelers can also rent a car or take a taxi for travel convenience.

Where to Go Next from Tarui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the exciting day trips or nearby towns to visit from Tarui:

  • Gero Onsen: Experience the amazing hot springs at Gero Onsen, a nearby town famous for its natural hot springs.
  • Seki City: Known for its sword making, Seki City is a great destination to get a glimpse into Japanese history and traditions.
  • Mino City: Mino City is famous for its traditional Japanese paper-making and provides a deeply satisfying cultural experience.

Conclusion

Tarui is one of the hidden gems of Japan that deserves more attention from tourists. This small town offers a unique blend of scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and traditional experiences. From exploring the natural beauty and sampling delicious food to learning about the local customs, Tarui provides something unique, authentic, and suitable for a wide range of travelers. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you in planning your trip to Tarui with ease!
