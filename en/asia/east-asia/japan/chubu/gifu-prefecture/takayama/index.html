Introduction to Takayama

Takayama, a quaint city nestled in the heart of the Japanese Alps, is a must-visit destination for tourists seeking to immerse themselves in Japan’s rich cultural heritage. Located in Gifu Prefecture, in the Chubu region of Japan, Takayama attracts thousands of visitors every year who come to experience the city’s well-preserved Edo-era streets, fascinating museums, serene temples, and natural wonders. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Takayama by providing a comprehensive overview of what to expect, where to go, and what to do while you’re there.

List of Places to Visit in Takayama

No visit to Takayama is complete without exploring its many attractions, which range from museums and historical landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor activities. Here are some places you should visit when in Takayama:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hida Furukawa Edo-style Streets: These streets are reminiscent of the Edo period and provide a glimpse into what life was like during the 17th and 18th centuries in Takayama.
  • Takayama Jinya: This former administrative center was a crucial hub of power during the Edo period.
  • Sakurayama Hachimangu Shrine: A sacred shrine located at the foot of Mount Hakusan, a place of worship since the Heian period.
  • Takayama Festival Float Exhibition Hall: A museum dedicated to showcasing the magnificent festival floats of Takayama.

Natural Attractions of Takayama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Norikura: This majestic mountain with an elevation of 3,026 meters offers breathtaking views of Takayama and is popular for hiking.
  • Kamikochi: A stunning valley located in the Northern Japan Alps, famous for its pristine rivers and hiking trails.
  • Shirakawa-go and Gokayama: These picturesque villages are UNESCO World Heritage Sites, famous for their traditional gassho-style farmhouses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Takayama

Takayama is surrounded by some of the most picturesque mountains in Japan, and this makes it a perfect place for outdoor activities. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Mt. Norikura: There are various hiking routes that take you to the summit of Mt. Norikura, ranging from beginner-friendly to more intense.
  • Kamikochi: Several trails of varying lengths and difficulty levels start from Kamikochi, and they offer some of the most spectacular views of the Northern Japan Alps.
  • Hida Great Limestone Cave: This cave system provides an exhilarating underground hiking adventure.

Best Things to Do in Takayama: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums and natural attractions, Takayama boasts a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests.

  • Sake Brewery Tour: Takayama is known for its high-quality sake, so why not take a tour of one of its many breweries and try some sake for yourself?
  • Old Town Walking Tour: This guided tour takes you through the streets of Takayama’s charming old town, and gives you an insight into its rich cultural heritage.
  • Takayama Festival: One of Japan’s biggest and most famous autumn festivals, Takayama Matsumoto Festival is an unforgettable experience, taking place every year on April 14-15 and October 9-10.

Week-Long Itinerary for Takayama

If you’re planning to spend a week in Takayama, here’s a sample itinerary we recommend:

Day Activity
1 Explore Takayama’s Old Town
2 Visit the Takayama Jinya and Hida Folk Museum
3 Go on a hike at Mt. Norikura
4 Take a day trip to Shirakawa-go and Gokayama
5 Check out Kamikochi’s stunning natural beauty
6 Take a sake brewery tour and learn about how the beverage is made

Food and Drink Scene of Takayama

Takayama’s food scene is a unique blend of traditional Japanese and local Hida cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hida beef: One of the most sought-after meats in Japan, Hida beef is known for its tenderness and flavor.
  • Sakana-zushi: This traditional sushi dish is made with freshwater fish that have been pickled in vinegar and rice.
  • Miso Nabe: A hot-pot dish made with vegetables, meats, and seafood cooked in miso soup.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Takayama is home to several breweries that offer high-quality sake.
  • Hida no sake: This sake is made with local spring water and is known for its mild and refreshing flavor.

Where to Stay in Takayama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Takayama has a range of accommodations, from high-end hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • Hoshokaku: A luxurious ryokan located near Takayama’s old town.
  • Ryokan Asunaro: A more affordable ryokan option in the heart of Takayama.
  • J-Hoppers Hida Takayama Guest House: A popular hostel that offers both dormitory and private room options.

What to Buy in Takayama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Takayama can find a wide range of souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some top picks:

  • Sarubobo Dolls: These small, colorful dolls are a symbol of good luck in the Hida region.
  • Sake: Takayama’s high-quality sake makes for a great gift.
  • Hida wood crafts: Hida, the region surrounding Takayama, is renowned for its woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Takayama

Takayama is known for its rich cultural heritage, with traditions that date back centuries. Some customs to keep in mind when visiting Takayama include:

  • The Takayama Festival: Takayama’s most famous festival is a two-day event held annually in April and October, featuring elaborate floats and costumes.
  • Gassho-zukuri: Takayama is home to several traditional thatched-roof houses, known as gassho-zukuri, which are an iconic feature of the Hida region’s architecture.

Best Time to Visit Takayama

The best time to visit Takayama is in the spring (March-April) or autumn (September-November), when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most spectacular. Be aware that during peak tourist seasons, the city can get crowded, so book your accommodations and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Takayama

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Takayama:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes: It’s customary to remove shoes when entering temples, ryokans, and some restaurants.
  • Respect local customs: Visitors should be mindful of the local customs and etiquette, such as bowing when greeting someone.
  • Try the local cuisine: Takayama offers a unique blend of traditional Japanese and local Hida cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Tip: Tipping is not customary in Japan and can be considered rude.
  • Be loud in public places: It’s considered impolite to speak loudly or to be disruptive in public areas.
  • J-Walk: Jaywalking is illegal in Japan, and offenders can face a hefty fine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Takayama

Takayama is generally a safe city, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions when traveling, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and securing valuables.

Health in Takayama

The tap water in Takayama is safe to drink, and medical facilities are readily available. Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Takayama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Takayama

The nearest airport to Takayama is the Chubu Centrair International Airport (NGO) in Nagoya. Alternatively, visitors can arrive at Narita Airport in Tokyo or Kansai Airport in Osaka and join the rest of the travel by bus and train.

Bus Stations Near Takayama

Takayama has its bus terminal, from which buses depart to nearby destinations such as Shirakawa-go and Gokayama.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to reach Takayama from nearby cities such as Nagoya or Kanazawa. Additionally, driving is an option, but it is not recommended for visitors unfamiliar with driving in Japan.

How to Get Around in Takayama

Takayama is a compact city and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters if they prefer. Taxis and local buses are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Takayama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Takayama is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Shirakawa-go and Gokayama: These picturesque villages are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Kamikochi: A stunning valley located in the Northern Japan Alps, famous for its scenic views.
  • Hida-Takayama Onsen: A hot spring resort located just outside of Takayama, offering a relaxing break after a day of sightseeing.

Conclusion

Takayama is a city unlike any other, a place where old meets new in the most delightful of ways. Hopefully, this comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming city in the heart of Japan.
