Introduction to Onuma Quasi-National Park

Onuma Quasi-National Park is a natural park situated in the Southern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan. It is a popular destination among locals and tourists alike, known for its unique natural features and activities. The park covers an area of approximately 9,350 hectares, composed of volcanic mountains, lakes, forests, and marshes. Onuma Quasi-National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventurers, as it offers a wide range of activities throughout the year.

Onuma Quasi-National Park is home to several culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Onuma Quasi-National Park:

  • Onuma Quasi-National Park Nature Center
  • Hokkaido Centennial Memorial Tower
  • Goryokaku Tower

Best Things to Do in Onuma Quasi-National Park: Activities and Tours

Onuma Quasi-National Park offers a wide range of activities and tours throughout the year. Here are some popular things to do in Onuma Quasi-National Park:

  • Go hiking and biking
  • Take a boat ride on Onuma Lake
  • Enjoy the scenic views from the Komagatake Ropeway
  • Try skiing and snowboarding in winter

Week-Long Itinerary for Onuma Quasi-National Park

Day 1: Visit the Hokkaido Konbukan Museum and explore the Onuma Quasi-National Park Nature Center.

Day 2: Take a boat ride on Onuma Lake and visit the Hokkaido Centennial Memorial Tower.

Day 3: Go hiking or biking on the Onuma Lake Trail and take the Komagatake Ropeway to enjoy scenic views.

Day 4: Visit Mount Showa Shinzan and Mount Usu and try skiing or snowboarding in winter.

Day 5: Go hiking or biking on the Mount Komagatake Trail and explore the Goryokaku Tower.

Day 6: Visit the Onuma Pond and take a relaxing walk through the park.

Day 7: Go shopping for souvenirs and local products before leaving Onuma Quasi-National Park.

Best Time to Visit Onuma Quasi-National Park

Onuma Quasi-National Park can be enjoyed all year round, as it offers different activities and scenery throughout the seasons. However, the best times to visit Onuma Quasi-National Park are:

  • Spring (April to June): Ideal for hiking and enjoying the cherry blossoms.
  • Summer (July to August): Perfect for boating and enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Autumn (September to November): Best time to view fall foliage.
  • Winter (December to March): Great for skiing and snowboarding.

Safety and Health

Safety in Onuma Quasi-National Park

Onuma Quasi-National Park is generally safe, but visitors should be mindful of the following:

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities
  • Be aware of trail conditions and weather conditions
  • Avoid going off-trail or hiking alone

Health in Onuma Quasi-National Park

The park has a first-aid station for minor injuries. For serious medical emergencies or health concerns, visitors can go to the nearest hospital or clinic.

Getting to Onuma Quasi-National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Onuma Quasi-National Park

  • Shin-Chitose Airport: The nearest international airport, approximately 120 km away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Onuma Quasi-National Park

  • JR Hokkaido Bus: Operates regular services to Onuma Quasi-National Park from Sapporo, Hakodate, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Renting a car: Visitors can rent a car in Sapporo and drive to Onuma Quasi-National Park.

How to Get Around in Onuma Quasi-National Park

Visitors can get around Onuma Quasi-National Park by walking, hiking, biking, or taking a bus or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Onuma Quasi-National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hakodate: A historical port city known for its seafood and panoramic views of the bay.
  • Sapporo: The capital city of Hokkaido, famous for its beer, ramen, and snow festival.
  • Lake Toya: A volcanic lake famous for its hot springs and scenic views.
