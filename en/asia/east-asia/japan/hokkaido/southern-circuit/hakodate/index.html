Introduction to Hakodate

Hakodate is a beautiful port town located in the Southern Circuit of Hokkaido, in Japan. It is known for its picturesque views, historical landmarks, and delicious seafood. This city is a perfect blend of traditional and modern Japan, with a range of activities to offer for all kinds of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Hakodate

Hakodate has a lot of culturally important attractions, natural beauty, and landmarks that shouldn’t be missed when exploring the city. Here are some of the must-visit places in Hakodate:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Goryokaku Tower: With its unique star-shaped fort, Goryokaku Tower offers breathtaking views and insights into Japan’s history.
  • Hakodate Museum of Art: This museum showcases both modern and traditional art, including exhibitions of local artists.
  • Hakodate Orthodox Church: This church is known for its Russian influence in its design and is also one of the oldest churches in Japan.
  • Old Public Hall of Hakodate Ward: This classic European-style building was used as a governmental building in the past, now serves as a museum.
  • Kanemori Red Brick Warehouse: A restored warehouse from the 19th century that now houses various shops and restaurants, making it an excellent place to shop and eat.

Natural Attractions of Hakodate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hakodate: At night, Mount Hakodate offers a breathtaking view of the city, making it one of the most popular places for travelers.
  • Onuma Quasi-National Park: With its calm lakes and beautiful nature trails, this park is a great destination for a day trip.
  • Yunokawa Onsen: This hot spring resort offers a relaxing retreat in natural surroundings.
  • Motomachi: This historic district of the city is known for its romantic streets, with great views of the waterfront.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hakodate

Hakodate is known for its natural beauty, and exploring the city’s scenic trails by bike or foot is the perfect way to experience it. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Hakodate:

  • Mount Hakodate Ropeway: A scenic cable car that takes you to the top of Mount Hakodate for unforgettable views.
  • Hakodate City Central Park: This park has great walking trails and is also an excellent place to have a picnic.
  • Fukuura Island: A scenic island that can be reached by bicycle. The island has great views of Mount Hakodate and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Hakodate: Activities and Tours

  • Try some fresh seafood: Hakodate is known for its delicious seafood, especially the squid.
  • Take a Sake tour: Hakodate has a rich history of sake-making, and taking a sake tour will give you an insight into the local culture.
  • Visit a traditional Japanese tea house: The city has a few traditional Japanese tea houses, offering a great opportunity to experience the tea ceremony.
  • Take a boat ride: A boat ride around the city’s harbor will give you a different perspective of the city and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hakodate

If you plan to visit Hakodate for a week, here’s what you can do:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Mount Hakodate, take a cable car ride, see the view of the city at night.
Day 2 Visit Fort Goryokaku, and take a walk in its park, visit the Hakodate Museum of Art.
Day 3 Visit the Kanemori Red Brick Warehouse, explore the Motomachi area, and see the Hakodate Orthodox Church.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Onuma Quasi-National Park, hike, and explore the park.
Day 5 Visit Yunokawa Onsen, relax in an outdoor hot spring.
Day 6 Rent a bike and see Fukuura Island, visit the Old Public Hall of Hakodate Ward.
Day 7 Take a boat ride around the harbor, explore local markets, and try some fresh sushi.

Food and Drink Scene of Hakodate

Hakodate is famous for its delicious seafood and traditional Japanese dishes. Here are some of the best food and drink options to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hakodate-style ramen: Served in a rich soup made of chicken and seafood broth.
  • Kaisendon: A seafood bowl topped with fresh sashimi, rice, and vegetables.
  • Ikameshi: A baked squid dish stuffed with glutinous rice, flavored with soy sauce, and sake.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hakodate has a long history of sake-making and is known for having excellent sake breweries.
  • Shochu: A distilled alcohol made from barley, sweet potatoes, or rice.

Where to Stay in Hakodate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hakodate offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Hakodate:

  • La Cachette: A Hakodate hotel with views of the bay, and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Hakodate Danshaku Club Hotel & Resorts: A beautiful traditional-style hotel with hot springs and excellent service.
  • Guest House Tomo: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Hakodate: Souvenirs and Local Products

Hakodate has plenty of local products to buy, including traditional Japanese crafts, sweets, and seafood. Here are some of the best things to buy in Hakodate:

  • Hakodate Shio Ramen: A pack of instant noodles, famous for their rich seafood flavor that you can bring home.
  • Hakodate Morning Market: A market where you can buy fresh seafood, vegetables, and locally-produced products.
  • Glassware made in Hakodate: The city is known for its beautiful glassware, and you can find fantastic souvenirs at many stores in the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Hakodate

Hakodate is a unique blend of Japanese, Western, and Russian culture. The people here are known for their hospitality and politeness, and it is essential to follow the local customs to enjoy your stay in Hakodate.

Best Time to Visit Hakodate

The best time to visit Hakodate is from June to September when the weather is mild, and the city experiences little rainfall. This is also the time for festivals and events, like the Hakodate Port Festival, which takes place in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hakodate

While in Hakodate, here are some things to do and not do:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before you enter traditional Japanese buildings like temples or tea houses.
  • Bow when greeting people as it is considered polite.
  • Try local seafood, some of the best in the world.

Don’t:

  • Don’t tip in restaurants, as it is considered rude in Japan.
  • Don’t talk loudly in public places or disturb others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hakodate

Hakodate is generally a safe city, but it is important to remain vigilant and take necessary precautions, just like in any other city. Petty theft and pickpocketing are rare, but it is still essential to keep your belongings safe.

Health in Hakodate

Hakodate has excellent healthcare facilities and hospitals, with well-trained medical staff. It is essential to carry health insurance when traveling to avoid any emergencies.

Getting to Hakodate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hakodate

Hakodate has a small airport that operates domestic flights, and the closest international airport is in Sapporo. From Sapporo, you can take a train or bus to Hakodate City.

Bus Stations Near Hakodate

Hakodate Bus Terminal is in the center of the city, and it operates frequent bus services to other cities in Hokkaido, such as Sapporo or Asahikawa.

Other Options

The city also has excellent railway connectivity, with the Hakodate station connecting to the JR Hokkaido network. You can take a Shinkansen train to Hakodate from Tokyo or take a local train from Sapporo, Asahikawa, or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Hakodate

The city has an excellent public transportation system and is easy to navigate. The streetcars and buses connect major tourist attractions, and taxis are also readily available. Renting a bike is a great way to explore the city’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Hakodate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hakodate is surrounded by beautiful destinations to explore, including the following:

  • Sapporo: The largest city in Hokkaido, known for its beer and skiing.
  • Otaru: A historic port town known for its glassware and seafood.
  • Asahikawa: The hometown for Asahikawa zoo, also has stunning parks and gardens to relax in.

Conclusion

A visit to Hakodate is an experience you won’t forget. With its natural beauty, culture, and delicious food, the city has something to offer to every traveler. Follow this comprehensive travel guide and start exploring the unique blend of Japan in Hakodate.
