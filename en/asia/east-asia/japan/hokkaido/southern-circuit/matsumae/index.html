Introduction to Matsumae

Matsumae is a quaint town located in the Southern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan’s northernmost island. The town is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and picturesque landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Matsumae

There are plenty of places to visit in Matsumae, ranging from culturally significant attractions to natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Matsumae Castle is undoubtedly the most famous attraction in the town. The castle was completed in the late 16th century and has an intriguing history. It now serves as a museum showcasing the town’s history and culture. Other museums worth visiting include the Matsumae Folk History Museum and the Cape Shirakami Historical Museum.

Natural Attractions of Matsumae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Matsumae is surrounded by enchanting natural beauty. Cape Shirakami is a haven for nature enthusiasts, offering stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding mountains. Other natural attractions include the Matsumae Park, Matsumae Hot Springs, and the Matsumae Coastal National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matsumae

Matsumae has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that traverse through mountainous regions and scenic landscapes. Popular routes include the Matsumae-Soma Mountain Trail, the Cape Shirakami Trail, and the Matsumae Coastal Trail.

Best Things to Do in Matsumae: Activities and Tours

Visitors can engage in various activities in Matsumae, ranging from water sports to cultural tours. Some of the best things to do in the town include sea kayaking, fishery tours, and a visit to the Matsumae Sake Brewery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matsumae

Day 1: Arrival in Matsumae and visit to Matsumae Castle. Day 2: Hiking/biking trip to Cape Shirakami. Day 3: Tour of local fishery and lunch at a seafood restaurant. Day 4: Visit to Matsumae Folk History Museum and Matsumae Hot Springs. Day 5: A day trip to Goryokaku, a star-shaped fortification in Hakodate. Day 6: Sea kayaking tour in Matsumae Bay. Day 7: Departure from Matsumae.

Food and Drink Scene of Matsumae

The food and drink scene in Matsumae is exceptional, offering an array of local delicacies and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Matsumae is renowned for its fresh seafood, particularly its octopus, scallops, and crabs. Visitors should try the local delicacy, tako-zen (octopus skewers), and hotate-meshi (scallops served on a bed of rice).

Local Beverages

Sake is the most popular local beverage in Matsumae. The town has several sake breweries, including the Matsumae Sake Brewery, which offers tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Matsumae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matsumae has a range of accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets. The town has several traditional ryokans, guesthouses, and modern hotels.

What to Buy in Matsumae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy various souvenirs and local products in Matsumae, including sake, locally made crafts, and seafood products.

Local Culture and Customs of Matsumae

Matsumae has a unique culture and customs. The town cherishes its traditional way of life, with several cultural festivals and ceremonies throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Matsumae

The best time to visit Matsumae is between May and September when the weather is mild, and the town’s natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matsumae

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Japanese homes, temples, and shrines.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try local delicacies and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disrespectful in public places, including restaurants and hotels.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter, smoke, or vandalize public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matsumae

Matsumae is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should, however, take necessary precautions and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Matsumae

The health facilities in Matsumae are reliable, with several clinics and hospitals in the town.

Getting to Matsumae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matsumae is accessible by various modes of transport, including air, train, and bus.

Airports Near Matsumae

The closest airport to Matsumae is Hakodate Airport, which is approximately 80 km away.

Bus Stations Near Matsumae

Matsumae Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the town. Buses operate regularly from Hakodate and Sapporo to Matsumae.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Matsumae, or take a train to Kikonai Station and continue the journey by bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Matsumae

The best ways to get around Matsumae are on foot, by bicycle or car rental. The town has an excellent network of cycling and walking trails, allowing visitors to explore the town’s natural beauty at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Matsumae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hakodate, known for its stunning night views, star-shaped fortifications, and fresh seafood, is approximately 80 km away from Matsumae. Visitors can also explore other nearby towns, including Akkeshi, Esashi, and Yoichi.

Conclusion

Matsumae is a picturesque town in Hokkaido, Japan, offering a unique blend of cultural attractions and natural wonders. Visitors can engage in various activities, indulge in local cuisine and beverages, and immerse themselves in the town’s rich culture and heritage.
