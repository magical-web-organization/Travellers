Introduction to Southern Circuit

Southern Circuit is a scenic region located in Hokkaido, Japan. With untouched natural landscapes, wildflowers, and waterfalls to be explored, Southern Circuit provides a tranquil getaway from the bustling cities of Japan. This is a place where visitors can immerse themselves in Japanese culture, indulge in local cuisine, and discover the traditional lifestyle of the towns and cities here. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best that Southern Circuit has to offer, including the must-visit attractions to the best hiking and biking routes, and the best accommodations to stay in.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Circuit

Southern Circuit has a rich cultural heritage. Here, visitors can immerse themselves in the traditions of the Ainu people by discovering the local crafts, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Southern Circuit

The best time to visit Southern Circuit is from June to October. This is when the region offers the most pleasant climate and the scenery is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Circuit

Do:

  • (1) Respect the local culture and customs
  • (2) Try the local food and drink
  • (3) Visit the local museums and historical attractions

Don’t:

  • (1) Litter in the natural surroundings
  • (2) Speak loudly in public places
  • (3) Overstay in the hot springs

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Circuit

Southern Circuit is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions when participating in outdoor activities and follow the guidelines provided by the local authorities.

Health in Southern Circuit

The health facilities in Southern Circuit are generally of high quality. However, visitors are advised to take additional precautions during the cold season.
