Introduction to Central Circuit

Located in the northernmost island of Japan, Hokkaido, Central Circuit is a hidden gem in East Asia. This region boasts a rich cultural heritage, awe-inspiring natural beauty, and fascinating traditions. Central Circuit is home to numerous attractions, including museums, historical sites, natural wonders, outdoor activities, and culinary delights. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the top places to visit and things to do in Central Circuit.

List of Places to Visit in Central Circuit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Asahikawa Museum of Sculpture: This museum showcases the works of internationally renowned artists and offers a glimpse into the world of sculpture.

  • Biei-cho Shirogane Blue Pond: This stunning blue pond is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Hokkaido. The blue pond’s ethereal beauty is a must-see sight that will leave you in awe.

  • Asahiyama-zoo: One of the most innovative zoos in Japan, the Asahiyama-zoo offers a unique experience that allows visitors to get up-close and personal with the animals.

  • Otokoyama Higashi Hongo Brewery: Otokoyama Higashi Hongo Brewery is one of the oldest breweries in Japan, producing some of the tastiest sake in the country. Visitors can sample different varieties and learn about the brewing process.

Natural Attractions of Central Circuit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Daisetsuzan National Park: Daisetsuzan National Park is a breathtaking natural reserve which offers a wide range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, camping and skiing. It features some of the tallest peaks in Japan, dense forests, and natural hot springs.

  • Shikisai no Oka: Known as the “Hill of Seasonal Colors,” Shikisai no Oka is a mesmerizing garden that offers stunning views of colorful fields and flowers throughout the year.

  • Sounkyo Gorge: The Sounkyo Gorge is an epic natural site and one of the most popular attractions in Hokkaido. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing dip in the natural hot springs, go hiking in the lush forests, or take a scenic cable car ride.

  • Furano Ski Resort: The Furano Ski Resort is a world-class ski destination, famous for its powder snow and beautiful scenery. There are various slopes for all levels of skiers, and during the summer, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like mountain biking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Circuit

  • Asahidake: Asahidake, located in Daisetsuzan National Park, is a must-visit hiking spot in Central Circuit. The ascent to the peak takes around 5 hours, but the view from the top is worth every effort.

  • Furano Ropeway: The Furano Ropeway offers a beautiful route for biking and hiking, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and lush fields.

  • Sounkyo Gorge: The Sounkyo Gorge offers various hiking routes with different levels of difficulty, from easy strolls to challenging treks. Visitors can enjoy beautiful waterfalls, hot springs, and impressive rock formations along the way.

Best Things to Do in Central Circuit: Activities and Tours

  • Furano Wine Factory: Visiting the Furano Wine Factory is a perfect opportunity to taste some of the finest wines in Hokkaido, learn about the wine-making process, and explore the vineyards.

  • Asahiyama Snow Village: The Asahiyama Snow Village is a unique attraction where visitors can witness stunning ice sculptures, try ice fishing, and enjoy snowmobiling.

  • Farm Tomita: Farm Tomita is a picturesque lavender farm that bloom colours during the season. It’s one of Hokkaido’s most famous landmarks and the perfect site for admiring the natural beauty and taking pictures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Circuit

Day 1: Visit the Asahikawa Museum of Sculpture and explore the city of Asahikawa.

Day 2: Head to the stunning Blue Pond, followed by lunch in Biei.

Day 3: Explore the Asahiyama-zoo, one of the most innovative zoos in the world.

Day 4: Travel to Daisetsuzan National Park for hiking, camping, and exploring the scenic beauty.

Day 5: Visit the Furano Ski Resort for skiing and other outdoor activities.

Day 6: Explore the Sounkyo Gorge and take a relaxing dip in natural hot springs.

Day 7: Visit the Furano Wine Factory and explore the vineyards and taste some of the finest wines in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Circuit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Genghis Khan: A traditional Hokkaido dish made from lamb or mutton, Genghis Khan is grilled over an open flame and served with vegetables and soy sauce.

  • Soba Noodles: Soba noodles, made from buckwheat flour and served cold or hot, are a well-loved dish throughout Japan, and Central Circuit features some of the best soba restaurants in the country.

  • Kaisendon: Kaisendon is an assorted bowl of freshly caught seafood over rice and a popular dish in Hokkaido.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hokkaido is famous for its sake, and there’s nothing better than enjoying a cup of sake fresh from the brewery.

  • Yubari King: Yubari King melon is a famous local delicacy in Hokkaido, and its distinct sweetness and juiciness make it a popular ingredient in cocktails and juices.

Where to Stay in Central Circuit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Asahikawa City Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Asahikawa and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  • Aoi Ike Lodge: This cozy guesthouse is located in the Furano area, and, with a stunning view of Mount Tokachi, offers a relaxing stay.

  • Sounkaku Grand Hotel: This traditional Japanese inn boasts natural hot springs and is nestled amidst scenic mountain forests.

What to Buy in Central Circuit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hokkaido Milk: Hokkaido Milk is rich, creamy, and famous throughout Japan, and drinking it is a must when visiting Central Circuit.

  • Sarubobo Dolls: These cute and unique dolls originate from the Aomori prefecture, but they can be found in Central Circuit and make excellent gifts or souvenirs.

  • Shiroi Koibito: Shiroi Koibito is a famous Japanese biscuit, originating from Hokkaido, and is a perfect snack to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Circuit

The people of Central Circuit are a friendly and welcoming lot, and visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and traditions. Some of the most prominent customs include:

  • Onsen: Onsen, or hot springs, are an essential part of Japanese culture, and visitors are encouraged to try them.

  • Shinto Shrines: Visitors are encouraged to visit Shinto shrines and observe traditional customs, such as washing their hands before entering.

Best Time to Visit Central Circuit

The best time to visit Central Circuit depends on what you want to do. The summer months, from June to August, are perfect for hiking, exploring the natural beauty, and enjoying the local festivals. The winter months, from December to February, are ideal for skiing and snowboarding in the region’s world-class resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Circuit

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Hokkaido boasts some of the best food in Japan, and visitors are encouraged to try new dishes.

  • Experience Traditional Onsen: Onsen is an essential part of Japanese culture, and visitors are encouraged to experience it.

Don’t:

  • Leave Trash or Litter Behind: Japan is a clean country, and leaving litter behind is considered disrespectful.

  • Ignore Customs and Traditions: Visitors are encouraged to respect Japanese customs and observe the traditional practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Circuit

Central Circuit is generally very safe, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions, such as locking their doors and being aware of pickpockets.

Health in Central Circuit

Medical facilities are easily accessible in Hokkaido’s major cities. Visitors are encouraged to purchase travel insurance before visiting Central Circuit.

Getting to Central Circuit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central Circuit

The closest airports to Central Circuit are Asahikawa Airport and New Chitose Airport, located approximately 30 and 90 minutes away, respectively.

Bus Stations Near Central Circuit

Buses are an affordable and convenient way to travel around Central Circuit, and the major bus stations are located in Asahikawa, Furano, and Biei.

Other Options

Trains are available from Sapporo to Asahikawa and Furano. Taxis are also available, but they tend to be more expensive.

How to Get Around in Central Circuit

Getting around Central Circuit is easy, with various transportation options such as buses, trains, and taxis. Visitors are encouraged to explore the region on bicycles, which can be rented from many hotels and shops.

Where to Go Next from Central Circuit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sapporo: Sapporo is the capital of Hokkaido and a vibrant city with a rich culture, delicious food, and scenic beauty.

  • Otaru: Otaru is a beautiful port city with a well-preserved historic district and stunning sea views.

  • Hakodate: Hakodate is a charming city with a unique blend of Japanese and Western influences, making it a popular tourist destination.

Conclusion

Central Circuit is a beautiful and diverse region in Hokkaido, Japan, with something to offer every visitor. From its scenic beauty, rich culture, culinary delights, and outdoor adventures, Central Circuit is a destination that should not be missed.
