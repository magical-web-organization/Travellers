Introduction to Sapporo

Sapporo is the largest city and capital of Hokkaido, Japan’s northernmost prefecture. It is a vibrant and cosmopolitan destination, known for its stunning natural surroundings, fascinating history, delicious food, and welcoming locals.

List of Places to Visit in Sapporo

If you’re planning your trip to Sapporo, here are the top attractions you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sapporo Beer Museum - learn about the history of Sapporo’s famous beer and taste some samples.
  • Hokkaido Museum of Modern Art - explore the works of Hokkaido’s artists and designers.
  • Former Hokkaido Government Office Building - a beautiful building that now functions as a museum filled with art and history exhibits.
  • Clock Tower - an iconic landmark of Sapporo, the clock tower was built in 1878 and is a popular photo spot.

Natural Attractions of Sapporo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Odori Park - a large park stretching across the center of Sapporo, perfect for a leisurely stroll or picnic.
  • Sapporo TV Tower - an observation deck offering panoramic views of the city.
  • Mt. Moiwa - a popular hiking spot that offers breathtaking views of the city below.
  • Maruyama Park - a beautiful park with various trails, gardens, and a zoo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sapporo

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Sapporo offers various hiking/biking routes that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Discover these routes:

  • Mt. Moiwa - a popular hiking destination with trails for all levels, from beginner to advanced.
  • Nopporo Forest Park - a large park with hiking and bike trails, as well as camping facilities.
  • Moerenuma Park - a stunning park designed by renowned artist Isamu Noguchi, filled with bike trails and unique sculptures.

Best Things to Do in Sapporo: Activities and Tours

Sapporo offers various activities and tours that cater to all types of interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Sapporo:

  • Sapporo Snow Festival - held annually every February, the festival features magnificent ice sculptures and attracts millions of visitors from all around the world.
  • Sapporo Beer Garden Tour - explore the history of Sapporo beer and learn how it’s made.
  • Sapporo Ramen Alley Tour - indulge in some of the best ramen in the city while exploring its fascinating history.
  • Sapporo City Tour - discover the city’s most iconic landmarks and hidden gems with a local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sapporo

If you’re spending a week in Sapporo, here’s a possible itinerary you can follow:

Day 1 Explore the city center and enjoy a stroll in Odori Park.
Day 2 Visit the Sapporo Beer Museum and enjoy some delicious beer samples.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Otaru and marvel at its stunning glassworks and canals.
Day 4 Hike to the summit of Mt. Moiwa for panoramic views of the city.
Day 5 Enjoy a day in Nopporo Forest Park, hiking and exploring its nature.
Day 6 Indulge in some delicious ramen at the famous Sapporo Ramen Alley.
Day 7 Visit the Hokkaido Museum of Modern Art and experience its art and design.

Food and Drink Scene of Sapporo

Sapporo is known for its delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sapporo Ramen - savory soup noodles served with various toppings such as pork, egg, and vegetables.
  • Jingisukan - lamb or mutton grilled with vegetables on a hot plate.
  • Genghis Khan - a hot pot dish made with fresh lamb or mutton.

Local Beverages

  • Sapporo Beer - a famous local beer brand, brewed in Sapporo.
  • Shiroi Koibito Park - a theme park with a chocolate factory producing the famous Shiroi Koibito cookies.

Where to Stay in Sapporo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sapporo offers various accommodation options to suit all types of budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sapporo:

  • Sapporo Grand Hotel - a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Guesthouse Waya - a cozy guesthouse near the city center.
  • Asadoya - a traditional Japanese inn located in the tranquil suburbs of Sapporo.

What to Buy in Sapporo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some options:

  • Shiroi Koibito Cookies - a famous local chocolate cookie brand, available in various sizes and flavors.
  • Hokkaido Milk Candies - a tasty candy made from Hokkaido’s fresh milk.
  • Otaru Glassware - beautiful glassware made in Otaru, a small town near Sapporo.

Local Culture and Customs of Sapporo

Sapporo has a rich and unique culture and customs. Here are some things you should know:

  • Respectful behavior - in Japan, it’s important to be respectful and considerate towards locals and their customs.
  • Bowing - it’s customary to bow when meeting or greeting someone in Japan, and the depth of the bow depends on the situation and the individual’s status.
  • Slurping noodles - it’s acceptable to slurp noodles in Japan as it’s believed to enhance the flavor of the dish.

Best Time to Visit Sapporo

The best time to visit Sapporo is from late December to early March, during the Sapporo Snow Festival, when the city is transformed into a winter wonderland with magnificent ice sculptures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sapporo

To make the most of your trip to Sapporo, here are some do’s and don’ts you should keep in mind:

Do:

  • Bow when meeting or greeting someone.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking.
  • Talk loudly or disruptively in public areas.
  • Tip in restaurants or other services, as tipping is not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sapporo

Sapporo is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions while traveling.

Health in Sapporo

Japan has a good healthcare system, and hospitals in Sapporo offer excellent medical care. It’s important to have health insurance before traveling to Japan.

Getting to Sapporo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sapporo

  • New Chitose Airport - located 40 km south of Sapporo, it’s the main airport serving Hokkaido.

Bus Stations Near Sapporo

  • Sapporo Bus Terminal - located in the city center, it’s the main bus terminal for long-distance buses.

Other Options

  • Train - there are various trains connecting Sapporo with other cities in Hokkaido and other parts of Japan.

How to Get Around in Sapporo

Sapporo has an extensive public transportation network, including subway, bus, and tram lines. Taxis are also widely available but can be quite expensive.

Where to Go Next from Sapporo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sapporo is a gateway to the beautiful Hokkaido region of Japan. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Otaru - a charming port town famous for its glassware and seafood.
  • Niseko - a popular ski resort with some of the best powder snow in the world.
  • Sounkyo Onsen - a hot spring resort located in the beautiful Daisetsuzan National Park.

Conclusion

Sapporo is a fascinating and beautiful city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in nature, culture, food, or adventure, Sapporo has it all. Plan your trip today and experience the best of Hokkaido.
