Introduction to Shinhidaka

Shinhidaka is a small town located in Hokkaido, the northernmost island of Japan. The town is situated in the Central Circuit and is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural heritage. Shinhidaka is an ideal destination for those who want to experience the natural beauty of Hokkaido without the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Shinhidaka

Whether you want to explore the cultural heritage of the town or indulge in outdoor activities, Shinhidaka has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Shinhidaka.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several museums and historical places. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Shinhidaka:

  • Shinhidaka Historical Museum
  • Daisetsu Museum
  • Gomotsu Memorial Museum

Natural Attractions of Shinhidaka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shinhidaka is blessed with natural beauty and offers a range of outdoor activities. Whether you want to hike in the mountains or enjoy scenic views, Shinhidaka has something for everyone. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Shinhidaka:

  • Mount Mokoto
  • Shikaribetsu Lake
  • Kisennuma Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shinhidaka

Shinhidaka is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The town offers a range of hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of difficulty. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Shinhidaka:

  • Mount Mokoto Hiking Trail

  • Hakkinsan Hiking Trail

Best Things to Do in Shinhidaka: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor activities, there are plenty of things to do in Shinhidaka. Here are some of the top things to do in Shinhidaka:

Local Culture and Customs of Shinhidaka

Shinhidaka has a rich culture and customs that are unique to the town. Here are some of the customs and etiquette to keep in mind while visiting Shinhidaka:

  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a traditional restaurant.
  • Tipping is not a common practice in Japan.
  • Bowing is a sign of respect and is a common greeting in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Shinhidaka

The best time to visit Shinhidaka is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The fall months, from September to November, are also a great time to visit as the foliage turns to a beautiful shade of red and orange.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shinhidaka

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Shinhidaka:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Bow as a sign of respect.
  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Be loud in public places.
  • Tip in restaurants.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shinhidaka

Shinhidaka is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take basic precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Shinhidaka

Shinhidaka has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical facilities in case of any emergency.
