Shikotsu-Toya National Park Travel Guide

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is a stunning natural wonder located in Central Circuit, Hokkaido, Japan. With its breathtaking scenery, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a fun and memorable travel experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Shikotsu-Toya National Park. From the best things to do to where to stay and how to get there, we’ve got you covered.

Introduction to Shikotsu-Toya National Park

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its breathtaking natural scenery, outdoor activities, hot springs, and volcanic landscapes. It’s located in Central Circuit, Hokkaido, Japan, and covers an area of over 99,000 hectares. Some of the most popular attractions in the park include the Lake Toya, Mount Usu, and Mount Tarumae.

List of Places to Visit in Shikotsu-Toya National Park

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is home to a wide range of attractions, including culturally important museums and historical places, as well as natural wonders such as scenic views and outdoor adventure opportunities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the rich cultural heritage of Shikotsu-Toya National Park, here are some must-visit museums and historical places to put on your itinerary:

  • Lake Toya Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the Ainu people, Hokkaido’s indigenous people, at this museum located near the lake’s southern shore.
  • Usu Zenkoji Temple: This historic temple, located at the foot of Mount Usu, is one of the most important Buddhist temples in the region.
  • Shikotsu-Kohan Onsen Preservation Area: This area preserves the traditional architecture of the Shikotsu-Kohan Onsen hot springs resort.

Natural Attractions of Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is packed with natural wonders, from volcanic landscapes to scenic views and opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are the top things to see and do in the park:

  • Lake Toya: One of the most popular natural attractions in the park, Lake Toya is a stunning caldera lake that offers plenty of opportunities for boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • Mount Usu: This active volcano provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape from its summit. Visitors can take a cable car to the top or hike up the mountain.
  • Bear Ranch: The park is home to a bear sanctuary where visitors can see different species of bears, including the famous Hokkaido brown bear.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shikotsu-Toya National Park

Hiking and biking are two of the most popular ways to explore Shikotsu-Toya National Park. Here are some of the best trails to try out:

  • Lake Toya Trail: This trail circles the entire lake and offers hikers stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Mount Usu Trail: This trail takes hikers to the summit of Mount Usu, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the park.
  • Shikotsu-Kohan Trail: This trail follows the picturesque coastline of Lake Shikotsu and offers hikers stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Shikotsu-Toya National Park offers something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in the park:

  • Enjoy the hot springs: Don’t miss the chance to soak in the hot springs at Shikotsu-Kohan or Toyako Onsen.
  • Take a boat tour: Hop on a boat to explore the scenic Lake Toya and enjoy the surrounding landscapes.
  • Try skiing: In winter, the park offers some of the best skiing opportunities in Japan. Take advantage of the amazing slopes and enjoy a day in the snow!

Week-Long Itinerary for Shikotsu-Toya National Park

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Shikotsu-Toya National Park, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Lake Toya and the surrounding area.
  • Day 3: Visit Mount Usu and take a cable car to the summit for stunning panoramic views.
  • Day 4: Visit the Bear Ranch and explore the nearby hot springs.
  • Day 5: Hike the Shikotsu-Kohan Trail and enjoy the scenic views of Lake Shikotsu.
  • Day 6-7: Ski or snowboard in one of the many resorts in the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Shikotsu-Toya National Park

The food and drink scene in Shikotsu-Toya National Park is rich and diverse, offering visitors a chance to taste some of the region’s local delicacies.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the must-try dishes in the park:

  • Hokkaido crab: This local specialty is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Ishikari nabe: This delicious hot pot dish is a Hokkaido classic made with salmon and vegetables.
  • Jingisukan: This grilled lamb dish is a staple of Hokkaido cuisine.

Local Beverages

Sake and beer are two of the most popular local beverages in Hokkaido. Don’t miss the chance to try some of the region’s finest sake and craft beer while you’re in the park.

Where to Stay in Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shikotsu-Toya National Park offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses and hostels. Here are some of the top places to stay in the park:

  • Lake Toya Onsen: This onsen resort offers luxurious accommodations and stunning views of Lake Toya.
  • Hotel Hanayura: This traditional Japanese ryokan offers guests a chance to experience authentic Japanese hospitality and cuisine.
  • Guest House Shikotsu Kamui: This cozy guesthouse is located near Lake Shikotsu and offers affordable accommodations for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is known for its local crafts and products, including pottery, woodcarvings, and handmade textiles. Don’t miss the chance to pick up a unique souvenir to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Shikotsu-Toya National Park

The people of Hokkaido are known for their warm hospitality and traditional way of life. Visitors to Shikotsu-Toya National Park can enjoy a variety of cultural experiences, from traditional festivals to local craft workshops.

Best Time to Visit Shikotsu-Toya National Park

The best time to visit Shikotsu-Toya National Park is in the spring and summer, when the weather is mild and the parks’ famous cherry blossoms bloom. Autumn is also a great time to visit, thanks to the spectacular fall foliage. Winter is ideal for skiing and snowboarding, but be prepared for cold weather and heavy snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shikotsu-Toya National Park

Before you visit Shikotsu-Toya National Park, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the region.
  • Pack appropriate clothing for the season, including warm layers in winter.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural wonders the park has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings or venture off designated trails.
  • Disrespect local customs.

Safety and Health

The safety and health of visitors to Shikotsu-Toya National Park are of utmost importance. Here are some tips to ensure a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in Shikotsu-Toya National Park

  • Always follow safety guidelines at the park’s attractions and activities.
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks when hiking or exploring the park.
  • Be aware of the risks associated with natural disasters, such as volcanic eruptions, and follow instructions from authorities in the event of an emergency.

Health in Shikotsu-Toya National Park

  • Be sure to drink plenty of fluids and take breaks when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of the risk of tick bites and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing protective clothing and using insect repellent.

Getting to Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including air, bus, and train.

Airports Near Shikotsu-Toya National Park

New Chitose Airport is the closest airport to the park and is served by domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Shikotsu-Toya National Park

The park is also easily accessible by bus. From Sapporo, take a bus to Toya or Shikotsu-Kohan.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a train to one of the nearby train stations.

How to Get Around in Shikotsu-Toya National Park

The best way to get around Shikotsu-Toya National Park is by car or bus. Buses run regularly between the park’s main attractions, and car rental is available near the airport and train stations.

Where to Go Next from Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip outside of Shikotsu-Toya National Park, there are plenty of exciting destinations and excursions to explore in the surrounding areas. Here are some of the top recommendations:

  • Sapporo: Hokkaido’s largest city is just over an hour’s drive from the park and offers a range of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Otaru: This charming port town is known for its historic architecture and delicious seafood.
  • Furano and Biei: These two picturesque towns are known for their stunning lavender fields and offer visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty of Hokkaido.

Conclusion

Shikotsu-Toya National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a fun and memorable travel experience in Japan. With its stunning natural scenery, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Start planning your trip today and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!
