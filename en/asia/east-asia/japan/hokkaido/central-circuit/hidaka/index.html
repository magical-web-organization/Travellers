Introduction to Hidaka

Hidaka is a charming rural town located in the Central Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan’s northernmost island. The town is surrounded by the breathtaking Hidaka mountain range, and boasts stunning natural scenery, lush forests, and crystal-clear rivers. Hidaka is a popular destination for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and those looking to explore the traditional Japanese way of life. In this guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, activities, accommodation options, dining experiences, and transportation logistics for your perfect Hidaka vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Hidaka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hidaka Museum of Art: This museum showcases contemporary and traditional art and crafts produced by local artists.
  • Hidaka Folk Museum: This museum provides insight into the traditional way of life in Hidaka through exhibits of local crafts, tools, and clothing.
  • Obira Grange: This 19th-century farmhouse has been preserved to showcase the traditional Japanese-style architecture of the area.

Natural Attractions of Hidaka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hidaka Mountain Range: This range boasts some of the highest peaks in Hokkaido and is perfect for hiking, climbing, and skiing.
  • Kofuku Natural Park: This park offers stunning panoramic views of the Hidaka mountain range and a variety of hiking trails.
  • Obira River: This pristine river is ideal for fishing, rafting, kayaking, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hidaka

  • Mt. Kuma Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Shizunai Nijukken Road: This route is perfect for cycling, with gentle inclines and scenic views of the Hidaka mountain range.
  • Hidaka-cho Nature Trail: This easy trail is perfect for families and nature enthusiasts, offering peaceful nature walks through lush forests and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Hidaka: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Hidaka’s mountain range is ideal for winter sports, with several ski resorts in the area.
  • Rafting and Kayaking: The Obira River is perfect for adventure seekers looking for a thrilling experience.
  • Hidaka Cheese Factory Tour: This tour provides a hands-on experience of cheesemaking and tasting the local cheese.

Food and Drink Scene of Hidaka

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hidaka Beef: This grilled or Sukiyaki-style beef is famous for its high-quality meat.
  • Seafood: The town’s location on the Pacific coast ensures that the seafood is always fresh and delicious, with specialties such as scallops, crab, and sea urchin.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hidaka produces several locally brewed sake varieties, each offering a unique taste from different rice cultivation techniques.
  • Shochu: A distilled, high-alcohol Japanese beverage made from sweet potato, barley, or rice.

Where to Stay in Hidaka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hidaka Prince Hotel: A luxurious resort-style hotel that offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valley.
  • Tomamu The Tower: A cost-effective yet comfortable stay option that boasts stunning ski resort views in a convenient location.
  • Pension Lilla Huset: A budget-friendly guesthouse offering traditional Japanese-style rooms, perfect for immersing yourself in the local culture.

What to Buy in Hidaka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hidaka Beef: Buy some of the world-famous beef to take home and cook for yourself.
  • Local Sake and Shochu: Bring home a bottle of the locally brewed sake or shochu to share with friends and family.
  • Hidaka Woodcrafts: Hidaka is famous for its traditional woodwork, with local artisans producing intricate wooden bowls, chopsticks, and other items.

Local Culture and Customs of Hidaka

Hidaka is a rural town that prides itself on preserving traditional Japanese culture and customs. Visitors should show respect for the local traditions and customs, including bowing to greet people, taking off shoes before entering homes and some tourist sites, and following etiquette when using public baths.

Best Time to Visit Hidaka

The best time to visit Hidaka is from late May to early October. This period offers perfect weather conditions for outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling, as well as a chance to enjoy the lush greenery and mountain views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hidaka

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take off shoes before entering homes and public baths.
  • Try local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local temples and monuments.
  • Ignore the etiquette of public baths.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hidaka

Hidaka is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and skiing, and always adhere to safety guidelines and advice from local guides.

Health in Hidaka

Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive international travel insurance and consider getting vaccinations before travel. Water in Hidaka is safe to drink, but a precautionary measure, visitors may choose to drink bottled water instead.

Getting to Hidaka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hidaka

The nearest airport to Hidaka is New Chitose Airport, which is well-connected to several international and domestic destinations in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Hidaka

Hidaka Bus Terminal offers regular bus services to and from all major destinations in and around Hidaka.

Other Options

Renting a car is also an excellent option for those who want to explore the town and surrounding areas at their convenience.

How to Get Around in Hidaka

Hidaka is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or bicycle. Taxis are also readily available for those who need them.

Where to Go Next from Hidaka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asahikawa: A popular destination in Hokkaido, known for its stunning natural landscapes and hiking trails.
  • Sapporo: The capital city of Hokkaido renowned for its vibrant nightlife, exciting food scene, and beautiful parks.

Conclusion

Hidaka is a beautiful town that offers a perfect blend of nature, adventure, culture, and tradition. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your perfect vacation in Hidaka, Japan.
