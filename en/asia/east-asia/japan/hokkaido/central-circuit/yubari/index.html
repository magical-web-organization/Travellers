Introduction to Yubari

Yubari is a small city located in the Central Circuit region of Hokkaido, Japan’s northernmost island. Known for its coal mining history, Yubari has transformed into a popular tourist destination with its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and unique local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Yubari

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yubari Coal Mine Museum

Experience the city’s mining history at the Yubari Coal Mine Museum, featuring exhibits on the lives of coal miners and coal mining technology.

Yubari City Museum

Discover the history and culture of Yubari at this local museum that houses artifacts from the early 1900s to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Yubari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Racey Ski Area

This ski area is known for its beautiful mountain views, groomed trails, and various ski courses suitable for all levels.

Shibatoge Pass

Drive through the beautiful landscape of Shibatoge Pass and take in the stunning views of Yubari’s mountain scenery.

Horoka Onsen

Relax and rejuvenate in natural hot springs while admiring the surrounding scenery at Horoka Onsen.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yubari

Mikuni Pass

Traverse the beautiful landscape on this hiking route with stunning views of scenic Mikuni Pass.

Best Things to Do in Yubari: Activities and Tours

Yubari Horseback Riding Tour

Experience the beautiful Yubari landscape on horseback and enjoy a unique adventure.

Coal Mine Nighttime Illumination

Take a tour of the Yubari Coal Mine Museum when it is lit up at night, creating a beautiful and unique experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yubari

Day Activity
1 Visit Yubari Coal Mine Museum and drive by Shibatoge Pass
2 Go skiing or snowboarding at Mount Racey Ski Area
3 Relax at Horoka Onsen
4 Take a horseback riding tour
5 Explore Yubari City Museum
6 Go hiking on Mikuni Pass
7 Experience the Coal Mine Nighttime Illumination

Food and Drink Scene of Yubari

Must-Try Dishes

Yubari Melon

This sweet, juicy melon is a local specialty and is considered one of Japan’s most expensive fruits.

Genghis Khan

Grilled mutton, a traditional Hokkaido dish.

Local Beverages

Sapporo Beer

Sapporo, Hokkaido’s capital, is the birthplace of one of Japan’s most famous beers.

Hokkaido Milk

Thick, creamy, and delicious, Hokkaido milk is renowned across Japan for its exquisite taste.

Where to Stay in Yubari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Emisia Sapporo

A short drive from Yubari, this beautiful hotel offers a luxurious and peaceful stay.

Yubari Hotel 135

Located conveniently in the centre of the city, this traditional ryokan offers a comfortable and authentic Japanese experience.

What to Buy in Yubari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yubari Melon

Take home one of the famous Yubari melons for yourself or as an edible souvenir to share with others.

Yubari Coal Mine Museum Souvenirs

Memorabilia related to the Yubari coal mining industry, such as miniature coal carts and hats worn by coal miners.

Local Culture and Customs of Yubari

Yubari is known for its strong agricultural heritage and is home to many traditional agricultural festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Yubari

The best time to visit Yubari is during the summer months between June and August when the city is at its liveliest, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yubari

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, particularly the Yubari melon and Genghis Khan.
  • Take in the beautiful natural scenery of Yubari, particularly Mount Racey and Shibatoge Pass.
  • Attend local agricultural festivals and celebrations.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese accommodations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yubari

Yubari is generally a safe city with low crime rates, but as a precaution, it is advisable to take the same safety measures as you would in any Japanese city.

Health in Yubari

Hospitals and healthcare facilities are available in the nearby city of Sapporo, and adequate medical facilities are also available in Yubari.

Getting to Yubari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yubari

New Chitose Airport is the closest international airport to Yubari, located approximately 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Yubari

You can take a bus to Yubari from Sapporo Bus Terminal, with services running regularly throughout the day.

Other Options

The fastest and most efficient mode of transportation between Yubari and Sapporo is by car, which takes around 40 minutes.

How to Get Around in Yubari

The best way to get around Yubari is by car, but public transportation options include buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Yubari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby cities of Sapporo and Otaru offer a variety of cultural and natural attractions, including museums, ski resorts, and hot springs.

Conclusion

Yubari is a must-visit destination in Japan, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, fascinating culture, and tradition. Plan your itinerary with this comprehensive travel guide to make the most of your trip to Yubari.
