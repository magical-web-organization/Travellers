Noboribetsu, located in the Central Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan, is a picturesque city surrounded by natural wonders. Famous for its geothermal hot springs and stunning landscapes, the city offers an unforgettable experience to all travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the top things to do, places to visit, and local culture to explore in Noboribetsu.

Introduction to Noboribetsu

Located on the southern coast of Hokkaido, Noboribetsu is a city renowned for its beautiful natural scenery, geothermal hot springs, and captivating culture. With a population of approximately 13,000, the city attracts thousands of tourists every year.

List of Places to Visit in Noboribetsu

From culturally important attractions to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Noboribetsu has something for everyone. Here’s a rundown of the top places to visit while in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Noboribetsu Date Jidaimura - a historical theme park where you can experience life in the Edo period.
  2. Noboribetsu Bear Park - home to over 150 bears, this park offers a unique opportunity to observe them up close.
  3. Jigokudani (Hell Valley) - a popular tourist destination featuring geysers, hot springs, and boiling mud ponds.
  4. Shiraoi Ainu Museum - dedicated to preserving the culture and heritage of the Ainu people, indigenous to Hokkaido.
  5. Noboribetsu Marine Park Nixe - an aquarium showcasing a variety of marine life native to the Okhotsk Sea.

Natural Attractions of Noboribetsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Kuttara - a volcanic mountain offering breathtaking views of Lake Kuttara and the surrounding landscape.
  2. Noboribetsu Onsen - a hot spring resort town famous for its therapeutic geothermal waters.
  3. Lake Toya - a serene volcanic lake with stunning views of Mount Usu.
  4. Lake Shikotsu - a beautiful caldera lake surrounded by lush forests and hiking trails.
  5. Noboribetsu Bear Mountain Ski Resort - a popular ski resort located in the scenic mountains surrounding the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts as well. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  1. Hell Valley Nature Trail - a 45-minute hike that takes you through the heart of Jigokudani (Hell Valley).
  2. Mt. Tarumae Trail - a challenging hike up the active volcano, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Kojohama Coast Trail - a scenic biking route that takes you along the coastal road, offering stunning views of the Sea of Japan and the mountains.
  4. Lake Toya Hiking Trail - a scenic trail that takes you around the circumference of Lake Toya, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Noboribetsu: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a dip in a hot spring - Noboribetsu is famous for its geothermal hot springs, so take some time to relax and soak in the therapeutic waters.
  2. Attend a cultural festival - the city hosts several cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Noboribetsu Jigoku festival and the Noboribetsu Onsen Yukake Matsuri.
  3. Sample local delicacies - experience the unique culinary traditions of Noboribetsu by trying dishes like seafood rice bowls, Hokkaido ramen, and local sweets.
  4. Take a scenic cruise - explore the beautiful caldera lakes like Toya or Shikotsu by taking a scenic cruise.
  5. Visit the hot spring theme parks - Noboribetsu boasts several hot spring theme parks, including Daiichi Takimotokan and Noboribetsu Manseikaku.

Week-Long Itinerary for Noboribetsu

If you’re planning to spend a week in Noboribetsu, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu is home to a lively food and drink scene that proudly features local ingredients and traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kaisen-don - a bowl of rice topped with fresh seafood like salmon, sea urchin, and squid.
  2. Genghis Khan - a Hokkaido specialty dish made of grilled lamb and vegetables.
  3. Ramen - Hokkaido ramen is known for its rich and flavorful broth and chewy noodles.
  4. San Pei Bento - a lunch box featuring three dishes, including sashimi, tempura, and grilled fish or meat.
  5. Zangi - deep-fried chicken marinated in soy sauce, ginger, sake, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Sapporo Beer - brewed locally in Hokkaido, Sapporo beer is a popular beverage option in Noboribetsu.
  2. Otaru wine - Otaru is a neighboring city known for its wine production, and you can try some of their delicious wines in Noboribetsu.
  3. Yubari King melon juice - Yubari King melons are a premium variety of melons grown in Hokkaido, and their juice is a popular beverage in Noboribetsu.

Where to Stay in Noboribetsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Noboribetsu offers a wide range of accommodation options, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some recommendations based on varying budgets:

  1. Daiichi Takimotokan - one of the most famous hot spring resorts in Noboribetsu, it features high-quality services, rooms with hot springs, exceptional culinary experiences, and meticulously crafted architecture.
  2. Noboribetsu Grand Hotel - another premium option that’s been a stalwart in the city since 1934, with 490 guest rooms, numerous dining options, a souvenir store, spa, swimming pools, gym, and hot springs.
  3. Bourou Noguchi Noboribetsu - a modern hotel located in Noboribetsu provides 25 guest rooms with hot springs, open-air baths, and private hot springs.
  4. Takimoto Inn - a budget option with clean rooms and a shared bath, perfect for travelers on a tight budget.
  5. Kojohama Spa Hotel - a mid-range option with a variety of hot springs and a stunning view of the ocean.

What to Buy in Noboribetsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Noboribetsu is the perfect place to pick up souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some of the most popular options:

  1. Shichiku Garden tea - a specialty tea produced in Noboribetsu that’s known for its unique aroma and taste.
  2. Hokkaido chocolates - Hokkaido is famous for its chocolates, and you can find a wide variety of options in Noboribetsu.
  3. Ainu crafts - the Ainu people, indigenous to Hokkaido, create beautiful crafts like wood carvings, embroidered clothes, and jewelry.
  4. Locally made bath salts and beauty products - with so many natural hot springs in the area, it’s no surprise that there’s a thriving industry of bath salts and beauty products in Noboribetsu.

Local Culture and Customs of Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu has a unique culture and customs that you can explore during your visit. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  1. Onsen etiquette - when visiting the hot springs, be sure to follow the proper etiquette, such as rinsing off before getting in the water, not using soap or shampoo in the hot springs, and covering any tattoos.
  2. Respect cultural heritage - Noboribetsu is home to several museums and cultural landmarks, so be sure to respect their history and significance.
  3. Learn about the Ainu culture - the Ainu people, indigenous to Hokkaido, have a rich and unique culture that’s worth exploring during your trip.

Best Time to Visit Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. For outdoor enthusiasts, the best time to visit is between May and October when the weather is mild and perfect for hiking and exploring. Winter months from December to March are ideal for snow sports activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noboribetsu

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Noboribetsu:

Do:

  1. Try the local food and drinks.
  2. Respect cultural heritage and local customs.
  3. Take advantage of the natural hot springs.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in overt displays of public affection, particularly in hot spring areas.
  2. Smoke in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu is a very safe city with low levels of crime. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary safety precautions while traveling.

Health in Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu is home to several hot springs that are famous for their therapeutic properties. However, it’s important to take proper precautions while using them, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions. Drinking bottled water is also recommended to avoid potential water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Noboribetsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Noboribetsu

New Chitose Airport is the closest airport to Noboribetsu, located approximately 60 km away. It’s easily accessible with bus and train services that run directly to Noboribetsu.

Bus Stations Near Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu is well-connected through a network of buses that operate within the city and connecting to neighboring cities as well.

Other Options

Driving or hiring a taxi is also a convenient option for getting around the city and exploring the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Noboribetsu

Noboribetsu is a compact city that’s easy to explore on foot, but there are also several transportation options available, including rental bicycles, taxis, and buses.

Where to Go Next from Noboribetsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Noboribetsu is the perfect base to explore the rest of Hokkaido. Some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider are:

  1. Sapporo - the largest city in Hokkaido, known for its beer and ramen.
  2. Asahikawa - a city famous for its zoo and winter sports.
  3. Lake Akan - a serene volcanic lake famous for its unique “marimo” algae.
  4. Furano - a picturesque city known for its lavender fields and vineyards.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, a foodie, or looking for a relaxing spa experience, Noboribetsu is the perfect destination for you. From hiking on active volcanoes to relaxing in natural hot springs, this city has something for everyone. We hope our comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your next trip to Noboribetsu and makes it an unforgettable one.
