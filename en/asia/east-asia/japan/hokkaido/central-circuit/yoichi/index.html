Introduction to Yoichi

Yoichi is a town located in the Central Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan. Known for its picturesque landscapes and natural beauty, this charming town attracts tourists from all over the world. Its location on the Sea of Japan makes it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts who want to explore its rugged coastline, steep mountains, and dense forests. Apart from its natural beauty, Yoichi also boasts a deep cultural heritage and is home to many museums, historical sites, and local festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Yoichi

Here are some of the best places to visit in Yoichi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yoichi Nikka Whisky Distillery: Founded in 1934, Yoichi Nikka Whisky Distillery is one of the oldest whisky distilleries in Japan. Visitors can take a tour of the distillery and learn about the history and process of whisky making. They can also sample different varieties of whiskies and buy souvenirs from the gift shop.
  • Yoichi Historical Museum: This museum showcases the local history, culture, and arts of Yoichi. It has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits, including traditional costumes, farming tools, and artworks.
  • Mount Yoichi Observation Deck: Located on the top of Mount Yoichi, this observation deck offers a panoramic view of the town, the Sea of Japan, and the surrounding mountains. On a clear day, visitors can see as far as Sapporo, the capital city of Hokkaido.

Natural Attractions of Yoichi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Kamui: Also known as Kamui Misaki, Cape Kamui is a stunning rocky peninsula on the coast of Shakotan Peninsula. Visitors can hike on the trails that lead to the cape and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Sea of Japan. The cape is also a popular spot for photography.
  • Yoichi Fruit Orchard: Located in the outskirts of Yoichi, this fruit orchard offers visitors the opportunity to pick fresh berries, cherries, and other seasonal fruits. The orchard also has a cafe where visitors can enjoy freshly made fruit desserts and drinks.
  • Yoichi Forest Park: Spread over 55 hectares, Yoichi Forest Park is a great place for nature lovers. It has several walking trails that weave through the forest, a children’s playground, and a campsite.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yoichi

Yoichi has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to explore its stunning landscapes up-close. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Mount Yoichi Trail: This trail leads to the top of Mount Yoichi and offers stunning views of the town and the sea. It is a moderately difficult trail and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.
  • Shakotan Peninsula Trail: This trail is a long-distance hiking route that spans the entire Shakotan Peninsula. It takes around 2-3 days to complete and passes through several scenic spots, including Cape Kamui, Shakotan Blue-tinted Cave, and Hokkaido’s largest active offshore reef.
  • Yoichi Cycling Road: This road is a popular cycling route that runs along the coast of Yoichi. It is a relatively easy route and offers beautiful views of the Sea of Japan.

Best Things to Do in Yoichi: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Yoichi offers visitors many other activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Yoichi:

  • Whisky Tasting Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the Yoichi Nikka Whisky Distillery and sample different varieties of whiskies. They can also buy whisky bottles and souvenirs from the gift shop.
  • Sea Kayaking: Sea kayaking is a popular activity in Yoichi, as the town is located on the coast of the Sea of Japan. Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the rugged coastline and nearby islands.
  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity in Yoichi, and visitors can join local fishing tours that take them to the best fishing spots.

Food and Drink Scene of Yoichi

Yoichi has a diverse food and drink scene, including local specialties and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaisen-don: a seafood rice bowl topped with fresh sashimi, shrimp, and other seafood.
  • Jingisukan: a grilled lamb dish that is a local specialty in Hokkaido.
  • Soup Curry: a spicy Japanese curry served with vegetables and other ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Nikka Whisky: Yoichi is home to the famous Yoichi Nikka Whisky Distillery, where visitors can sample different varieties of whiskies.
  • Sapporo Beer: Sapporo is a nearby city and is known for its famous Sapporo Beer, which is brewed with pure water from the nearby mountains.

Where to Stay in Yoichi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yoichi has a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Yoichi:

  • Yoichi Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the heart of Yoichi and offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices. It is a great option for budget travelers.
  • Yoichi Nikka Whisky Hostel: This hostel is located adjacent to the Yoichi Nikka Whisky Distillery and offers guests a unique whisky-themed experience. The rooms are comfortable and well-equipped, and guests can enjoy sampling different varieties of whiskies at the distillery.
  • Yoichi Onsen Ryokan: This ryokan is located near the Yoichi Onsen (hot spring) and offers guests a traditional Japanese experience. The rooms are decorated in a Japanese style and come with private hot spring baths.

What to Buy in Yoichi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yoichi is known for its local products and handmade crafts. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy in Yoichi:

  • Whisky Bottles: Visitors can buy different varieties of whiskies from the Yoichi Nikka Whisky Distillery and other local shops.
  • Shakotan Blue-tinted Glass: This unique glassware is made using a traditional technique that gives it a blue tint. Visitors can buy these glasses as souvenirs or gifts.
  • Yoichi Apple Products: Yoichi is known for its delicious apples, and visitors can buy apple juice, jam, and other apple products in local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Yoichi

Yoichi has a deep cultural heritage and is home to many festivals and events throughout the year. Some of the most popular festivals include the Yoichi Summer Festival, the Shakotan Fire Festival, and the Yoichi Apple Festival. Visitors are advised to follow local customs and traditions while visiting Yoichi, such as removing their shoes before entering homes or temples and respecting the local culture and practices.

Best Time to Visit Yoichi

The best time to visit Yoichi is from May to October, as the weather is pleasant and sunny during this time. The peak tourist season is from July to September, when the town is crowded with tourists and prices can be higher than usual. Winter is also a good time to visit Yoichi, especially for skiing and snowboarding in nearby resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yoichi

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Yoichi:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Follow local rules and regulations.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Damage or litter the environment.
  • Be loud or disturb the peace and tranquility of the town.
  • Disrespect local culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yoichi

Yoichi is a safe town for tourists, and crime rates are relatively low. However, visitors are advised to use common sense and take precautions to ensure their safety, such as not leaving their belongings unattended and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Yoichi

Yoichi has good healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical care in case of emergencies. It is recommended that travelers have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and check with their doctor before traveling to ensure that they are up-to-date with their vaccinations.

Getting to Yoichi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yoichi is accessible by various modes of transportation, including air, train, and bus.

Airports Near Yoichi

The closest airport to Yoichi is New Chitose Airport in Sapporo. From there, visitors can take a bus or train to Yoichi.

Bus Stations Near Yoichi

Yoichi is well-connected by bus services, and visitors can take a bus from Sapporo or other nearby cities to Yoichi.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Yoichi.

How to Get Around in Yoichi

Yoichi is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take local buses to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Yoichi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yoichi is located in the Central Circuit of Hokkaido, and there are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore. Here are some of the best options:

  • Otaru: A charming port town known for its historic buildings, glassware, and seafood.
  • Niseko: A popular ski resort town that offers excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities.
  • Sapporo: The capital city of Hokkaido and a vibrant cultural hub that offers many attractions, including shopping, food, and museums.

Conclusion

Yoichi is a beautiful town that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Whether you are interested in hiking, skiing, or exploring local culture, Yoichi has something to offer for everyone. Plan your visit today to experience the charm and beauty of this stunning town.
