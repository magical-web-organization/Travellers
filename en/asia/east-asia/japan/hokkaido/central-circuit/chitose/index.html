Introduction to Chitose

Chitose is a charming city located in Central Circuit, Hokkaido, in Japan’s northernmost main island, known for its natural beauty, hot springs, and delicious local cuisine. The city is easily accessible from Sapporo, Hokkaido’s capital, making it a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Chitose

If you’re planning a trip to Chitose, you won’t be short of things to see and explore. Here’s a list of some of the top attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chitose Salmon Aquarium: Learn about the lifecycle of salmon and enjoy interactive exhibits at this unique museum.
  • Chitose City History Museum: Discover the rich history of Chitose through displays of artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  • Lake Shikotsuko: This pristine lake is surrounded by mountains and is a popular spot for water sports, hiking, and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Chitose: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sapporo Beer Garden: Visit the iconic Sapporo Brewery’s beer garden for a tasting of their famous beers, and relax in their beautiful garden area.
  • Shikotsu-Toya National Park: Take a hike through stunning natural landscapes, and relax in hot springs, such as the Jozankei Onsen and the Takino Suzuran Hillside National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chitose

Chitose has a variety of hiking and biking routes for visitors who enjoy the outdoors. Some of the most popular include:

  • Chitose River Cycling Road: A 45km route that takes you along the picturesque Chitose River.
  • Lake Shikotsuko Loop Line: This 22km route takes you through the scenic mountains around Lake Shikotsuko.

Best Things to Do in Chitose: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, Chitose has something for everyone. Here are some popular activities and tours:

  • Chitose Factory Tour: Take a tour of the city’s famous Sapporo Beer Factory, learning about its brewing process, and tasting a variety of beers.
  • Rafting and Canoeing: Enjoy the cool and refreshing water of Chitose River while paddling down the river.
  • Winter Activities: Experience a winter wonderland by joining various activities, including snowmobiling, snowshoeing, skiing, and snowboarding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chitose

If you have a week to explore Chitose, we suggest the following itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrival and visit the Chitose Salmon Aquarium
2 Explore the Chitose city center and shop at the Rera Outlet Mall
3 Hike the Lake Shikotsuko Loop Line
4 Visit the Sapporo Beer Garden and Factory
5 Take a hot spring bath in Jozankei Onsen or Takino Suzuran Hillside National Park
6 Explore the Lake Akan and Ainu Kotan, where you can learn about the indigenous Ainu people’s culture
7 Departure and Transfer to the airport

Food and Drink Scene of Chitose

Chitose is known for its delicious local cuisine, including seafood, ramen, and Sapporo beer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon: Chitose is known for salmon, which is served in many forms, including sashimi, grilled, or in a hot pot.
  • Hokkaido Ramen: Try the hearty miso or soy sauce-based ramen at one of Chitose’s popular local ramen shops.
  • Sapporo Beer: Sapporo Beer is one of Japan’s most iconic brands, and you’ll find it on tap at many bars and restaurants throughout Chitose.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hokkaido is famous for its sake, and you can taste the delicious local flavors at the many sake breweries in the city.

Where to Stay in Chitose: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chitose has a variety of accommodations for all types of travelers, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Chitose: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and close to the airport.
  • La’gent Stay Sapporo Odori: A stylish hotel in Sapporo, a short train ride from Chitose.
  • Hoshino Resorts Tomamu: A luxury hotel set in a beautiful natural environment, offering a unique Japanese experience.

What to Buy in Chitose: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, we recommend:

  • Salmon Products: As Chitose is famous for salmon, look for unique salmon products and souvenirs, such as salmon jerky, canned salmon, and salmon furikake.
  • Hokkaido Sweets: Hokkaido is famous for its milk and dairy products, so try some delicious local sweets, such as Kitakaro’s mochi, Kinotoya’s cheesecake, and Royce’s chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Chitose

When visiting Chitose, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Etiquette in hot springs: When visiting a hot spring, be respectful of others and follow the etiquette, including washing your body before entering the hot spring.
  • Cover your tattoos: Many public places, including hot springs and swimming pools, require you to cover your tattoos.

Best Time to Visit Chitose

The best time to visit Chitose depends on your preferences. If you’re looking for winter sports, visit from December to March; to see the colorful autumn leaves, visit in October and November; and to enjoy warm weather and outdoor activities, visit from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chitose

When visiting Chitose, here are some things to remember:

Do:

  • Get a Japan Rail Pass: If you’re planning to travel around Japan, consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass before arriving to save money on transportation.
  • Visit Lake Shikotsuko: This beautiful lake is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts, offering fantastic views and activities.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: In Japan, tipping is not customary, and could even be considered rude in some situations.
  • Ignore etiquette in hot springs: As mentioned earlier, be respectful of others and follow the etiquette when visiting hot springs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chitose

Chitose is a safe city, with low crime rates. As with any travel, be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as securing valuables and avoiding unlit and deserted areas at night.

Health in Chitose

Chitose is generally a healthy place to visit, with no major health concerns. However, if you’re planning to hike or engage in outdoor activities, be sure to take proper safety precautions, such as wearing appropriate clothing and carrying enough water and food.

Getting to Chitose: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chitose

Chitose is serviced by the New Chitose Airport, located just a 10-minute train ride from the city center. The airport offers domestic and international flights to various destinations.

Bus Stations Near Chitose

Chitose has several bus stations that offer routes to destinations within Hokkaido and beyond, including Sapporo and Noboribetsu.

Other Options

Chitose is connected to the rest of Hokkaido by train and road, making it easy to access from other parts of the island.

How to Get Around in Chitose

Chitose is a compact city, and most of the main attractions are easily accessible on foot. However, if you’re planning to travel further afield, renting a car or taking public transportation is recommended. The city also has a bike rental service, making it easy to explore on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Chitose: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding areas of Hokkaido, some popular destinations and excursions include:

  • Sapporo: The capital of Hokkaido, home to many unique attractions, such as the Sapporo Beer Museum and the Tanukikoji Shopping Street.
  • Noboribetsu: Known for its hot springs and Jigokudani, a valley of volcanic activity.
  • Otaru: A charming seaside city, known for its canal and glassworks.

Conclusion

Chitose is a beautiful and unique destination, offering a range of exciting activities for travelers of all ages and interests. From exploring local culture and customs to hiking and enjoying the natural beauty, Chitose is a must-visit destination in Hokkaido.
