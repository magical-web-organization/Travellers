Introduction to Nakasatsunai

Nestled in the Eastern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan, Nakasatsunai is a charming traditional town that offers visitors a unique perspective into rural Japanese life. Surrounded by stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Nakasatsunai is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Nakasatsunai

Whether you’re a culture vulture looking to explore historical sites or an outdoor enthusiast seeking adventure in the great outdoors, Nakasatsunai has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the top attractions in Nakasatsunai is the Nakasatsunai Art Village, which boasts beautiful gardens and exhibitions of modern art in traditional Japanese houses. The town is also home to the Nakasatsunai Folklore Museum, a great place to learn about the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Nakasatsunai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, a hike up Mount Akaiwa offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area. The area is also known for its hot springs, with Ashoro Onsen being a popular spot to relax and soak in the mineral-rich waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakasatsunai

One of the best ways to experience the stunning natural scenery in Nakasatsunai is through hiking or biking. The town is home to several scenic routes, including the Nakasatsunai Forest Trail and the Shirataki Nature Trail.

Best Things to Do in Nakasatsunai: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to keep visitors entertained. The Nakasatsunai Art Village offers workshops and classes in traditional Japanese arts and crafts, while the Kita no Kuni Komadori Sansou offers horseback riding tours of the local area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nakasatsunai

For those looking to experience everything that Nakasatsunai has to offer, a week-long itinerary might look something like this:

Day 1-2: Explore the cultural attractions of Nakasatsunai, including the Nakasatsunai Art Village and Nakasatsunai Folklore Museum. Day 3-4: Hike or bike the scenic trails around the town, taking in the stunning natural landscapes. Day 5-6: Relax at a local hot spring, such as Ashoro Onsen, and enjoy the mineral-rich waters. Day 7: Take a horseback riding tour of the local area with Kita no Kuni Komadori Sansou.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakasatsunai

No trip to Japan is complete without indulging in the local cuisine, and Nakasatsunai is no exception.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Nakasatsunai include:

  • Hiyajiru, a cold soup made with miso and vegetables.
  • Genghis Khan, a popular Hokkaido dish made with grilled lamb and vegetables.
  • Imo Mochi, a sweet potato dessert that is steamed and mashed before being formed into small bite-sized cakes.

Local Beverages

Be sure to try some of the local brews during your trip, including Sapporo Beer, a brand that originated in Hokkaido, and local sake such as RAPA.

Where to Stay in Nakasatsunai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Nakasatsunai have a range of accommodation options to choose from, depending on their budget and preferences. Some popular choices include the Nakasatsunai Guest House and the Kita no Kuni Komadori Sansou.

What to Buy in Nakasatsunai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a souvenir of their trip to Nakasatsunai, some popular items include locally made pottery, handcrafted traditional Japanese clothing and accessories, and local food products such as Hokkaido cheese and sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakasatsunai

One of the unique aspects of Nakasatsunai is its traditional way of life, with many residents still adhering to the local customs and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of these traditions, and learn more about the local customs by participating in traditional activities such as tea ceremonies or Japanese calligraphy workshops.

Best Time to Visit Nakasatsunai

The best time to visit Nakasatsunai is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, and traditional festivals such as the Nakasatsunai Yakata Matsuri.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakasatsunai

Do:

  • Learn about local customs and traditions, and participate in traditional activities.
  • Embrace the natural beauty of the area by going on hikes and taking in scenic views.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks, including Sapporo beer and RAPA sake.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Leave trash or litter in the natural areas surrounding the town.
  • Speak loudly or disrupt the peaceful nature of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakasatsunai

Nakasatsunai is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should still take precautions such as keeping valuables safe and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Nakasatsunai

Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them, as access to medical facilities can be limited in the area. It is also important to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Nakasatsunai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nakasatsunai

The closest airport to Nakasatsunai is the Obihiro Airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Nakasatsunai.

Bus Stations Near Nakasatsunai

There are several bus stations in Hokkaido that connect to Nakasatsunai, including Obihiro Bus Terminal and Asahikawa Station. Visitors can take a local bus from these stations to Nakasatsunai.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a popular option for visitors, as it allows for greater flexibility in exploring the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Nakasatsunai

The best way to get around Nakasatsunai is by car or bike. Many of the outdoor attractions are located outside of the town, such as Mount Akaiwa, and having your own transportation can allow for easier access.

Where to Go Next from Nakasatsunai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore more of Hokkaido, nearby destinations such as Obihiro and Asahikawa offer a range of activities and attractions. The popular ski resort of Niseko is also located nearby, offering visitors a chance to hit the slopes during the winter months.

Conclusion

Nakasatsunai, Japan offers visitors a unique and off-the-beaten-path travel experience, with stunning natural scenery and rich cultural heritage to explore. From hiking and biking to soaking in local hot springs, Nakasatsunai has something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination for any Japan itinerary.
