Daisetsuzan National Park is a nature lover’s paradise and a must-see destination for anyone visiting Hokkaido, Japan. Home to stunning vistas, lush forests, towering mountains, and a plethora of outdoor activities, this national park offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best things to do and see in Daisetsuzan National Park, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine.

Introduction to Daisetsuzan National Park

Located in the eastern circuit of Hokkaido, Daisetsuzan National Park is the largest national park in Japan, covering more than 2,000 square kilometers. The park is home to numerous hot springs, a diverse range of wildlife, and many hiking trails that lead to the summit of Mount Asahi, the highest mountain on Hokkaido.

List of Places to Visit in Daisetsuzan National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sounkyo Onsen: A traditional hot spring resort town that offers magnificent views of waterfalls, gorges, and mountains.
  • Asahi-dake Observatory: A panoramic observation deck that provides breathtaking views of the Daisetsuzan mountains.
  • Daisetsu Mori-no Garden: A beautiful garden that features a range of plants and flowers, including cherry blossoms and autumn colors.

Natural Attractions of Daisetsuzan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Asahi: This majestic mountain is the highest peak in Hokkaido and offers a challenging hiking trail that rewards climbers with spectacular views of the park.
  • Kurodake Ropeway: A cable car that takes visitors to the top of Mount Kurodake, where they can enjoy stunning views of the Daisetsuzan mountains and explore hiking trails.
  • Ginsendai Parking Area: A scenic spot that offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and access to several hiking trails and natural hot springs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Daisetsuzan National Park

The park offers numerous hiking trails that range from easy to challenging, offering a range of scenic views and natural wonders. Some of the most popular hiking trails include:

  • Mount Asahidake: The hike up Mount Asahi is rigorous but very rewarding. The trail leads to the summit of the mountain and offers magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Tokachidake Hot Springs trail: The hike to the hot springs offers an opportunity to see the dramatic landscape of the park, with pristine forests, sparkling streams, and soothing hot springs.
  • Kamikawa Forest Park: This park offers several hiking trails that lead to the top of the surrounding mountains, providing breathtaking panoramic views of the park.

Best Things to Do in Daisetsuzan National Park: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities and tours available for visitors to enjoy in Daisetsuzan National Park, including:

  • hot spring tours
  • wildlife watching tours
  • guided hiking tours
  • scenic flights
  • outdoor photography tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Daisetsuzan National Park

This itinerary is a perfect guide for a week-long visit to Daisetsuzan National Park.

Day 1 - Arrival and check into a local hotel Day 2 - Visit Sounkyo Onsen for a hot spring experience and take a scenic walk Day 3 - Visit Ginsendai Parking Area, relax in the hot springs and explore Day 4 - Hike to the top of Mount Asahi Day 5 - Take a scenic flight over the park Day 6 - Visit cultural attractions, such as Daisetsu Mori-no Garden or Asahi-dake Observatory Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Daisetsuzan National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ramen: Hokkaido is famous for its delicious ramen, which is a must-try dish when visiting the region.
  • Genghis Khan: A famous Hokkaido dish that consists of grilled lamb and vegetables, perfect for cold weather.
  • Ice cream: Hokkaido produces some of the best ice cream in Japan, with a range of unique flavors such as lavender and melon.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hokkaido produces some of the best sake in Japan, with a range of flavors, including fruit-infused or unpasteurized sake.
  • Beer: Hokkaido is famous for its beer, with some of the best breweries found in the region.
  • Whiskey: Hokkaido has some of the best whiskey distilleries in Japan, with smooth and unique flavors.

Where to Stay in Daisetsuzan National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Daisetsuzan National Park, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the recommended places to stay include:

  • Sounkyo Kankou Hotel: A 4-star hotel located in the heart of Sounkyo Onsen that offers comfortable rooms with hot springs.
  • Tenninkyo Onsen: A hot spring retreat that offers traditional Japanese-style accommodation and relaxing hot springs.
  • Aizan-so: A guesthouse located in the center of the park that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Daisetsuzan National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Daisetsuzan National Park include:

  • Hokkaido ice cream
  • Local honey

Local Culture and Customs in Daisetsuzan National Park

A central part of Daisetsuzan National Park’s culture is hot spring bathing, which has been practiced in the region for centuries. It is important to remember that hot springs are sacred places and should be treated with respect. Visitors should remove all clothing before entering the hot springs and not disturb the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Daisetsuzan National Park

The best time to visit Daisetsuzan National Park is from June to September, when the temperature is mild, and the scenery is vibrant. During this period, visitors can enjoy hiking, rafting, and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Daisetsuzan National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and terrain
  • Bring plenty of water and snacks on hikes
  • Use designated campsites and facilities

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park
  • Leave designated hiking trails
  • Touch or disturb wildlife
  • Use hot springs if you have any open cuts or sores

Safety and Health

Safety in Daisetsuzan National Park

It is essential to be aware of the potential hazards of hiking in Daisetsuzan National Park, including steep terrain, sudden weather changes, and wildlife encounters. Visitors should always carry a map, a compass, and a communication device in case of an emergency.

Health in Daisetsuzan National Park

Visitors to the park should be aware of the risk of insect bites, especially in the summer months. It is also essential to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated while hiking, as the altitude can cause dehydration.

Getting to Daisetsuzan National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Daisetsuzan National Park

The closest airport to Daisetsuzan National Park is the Asahikawa Airport, which is approximately 100 kilometers from the park.

Bus Stations Near Daisetsuzan National Park

There are several bus stations near Daisetsuzan National Park, including the Asahikawa Bus Station, which has direct buses to the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to the park or take a train to the nearby towns and then take a bus to the park. It is essential to check the bus schedule in advance to plan your trip.

How to Get Around in Daisetsuzan National Park

To explore the park, visitors can rent a car, take a tour bus, or walk. Hiking is the best way to see the park’s natural beauty and offers a range of scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Daisetsuzan National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations to visit after exploring Daisetsuzan National Park include:

  • Shikaribetsu Lake: A beautiful lake surrounded by untouched forests and mountains.
  • Lake Akan: A crater lake with clear water and beautiful scenery.
  • Furano: A picturesque town famous for its beautiful lavender fields and delicious local wine.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive guide to Daisetsuzan National Park has inspired you to explore this beautiful natural wonder. From steep mountain trails and hot springs to delicious local cuisine and cultural attractions, Daisetsuzan National Park offers something for everyone. Be sure to plan your trip in advance and enjoy all the beautiful sights and experiences this park has to offer!
