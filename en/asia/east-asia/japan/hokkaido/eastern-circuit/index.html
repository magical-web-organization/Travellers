Introduction to Eastern Circuit

Eastern Circuit is one of Hokkaido’s best-kept secrets, tucked away in the eastern part of Japan’s northernmost island. With its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural history, and vibrant food scene, this region is a treat for any traveler seeking off-the-beaten-path destinations. From natural hot springs to mountain trails, Eastern Circuit offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Circuit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Kushiro City Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Ainu indigenous people, as well as the overall history of the city.

2. Akkeshi Museum of History and Folklore: This museum provides a glimpse into the lives of the early settlers of Akkeshi through its exhibits of antique household items and crafts.

3. Obihiro Centennial City Museum: Discover the history and culture of this century-old city at this museum, which offers a range of exhibits from the Ainu period to modern-day Hokkaido.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Circuit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Lake Akan: Famous for its clear blue waters and marimo (fluffy green algae) sightings, this lake offers boat tours and picturesque hikes around its shores.

2. Shiretoko National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its towering cliffs, breathtaking waterfalls, and diverse wildlife, including brown bears.

3. Nemuro Peninsula: Take a hiking tour around the rugged coastline of the Nemuro Peninsula and spot various bird species, including the endangered Blakiston’s fish owl.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Circuit

1. Akan Mashu National Park: One of the best places to hike in Eastern Circuit, Akan Mashu National Park offers scenic routes for all levels of hikers. The park features numerous lakes, most notably Mashu Lake, one of the clearest lakes in the world.

2. Mount Iou and Lake Kussharo: Hike to the top of Mt. Iou, an active volcanic mountain, and enjoy the stunning view of Lake Kussharo, the largest crater lake in Japan.

3. Tokachi Nature Trail: This trail offers an easy hike through lush forests, waterfalls, and hot springs of the Tokachi region.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Circuit: Activities and Tours

1. Off-Road Adventure: Get off the beaten path and explore Eastern Circuit’s scenic views and landscapes on an off-road adventure tour.

2. Ice Fishing: Try traditional mollusk ice fishing on Lake Akan or Lake Kussharo during winter months.

3. Sea Kayaking: Paddle along the Shiretoko coast and explore its rugged coastline and abundant wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Circuit

Must-Try Dishes

1. Kaisendon: A seafood lover’s delight, this dish features a bowl of fresh sashimi served on a bed of rice.

2. Butadon: Hokkaido’s famous pork bowl is a tasty treat made with local pork simmered in savory broth and served over rice.

3. Yubari melon: This juicy, sweet melon is one of Hokkaido’s most famous products and is a must-try when visiting Eastern Circuit.

Local Beverages

1. Sake: Eastern Circuit produces some of Hokkaido’s finest sake, including the light and refreshing Tokachi Sake.

2. Japanese Whisky: Hokkaido is known for producing delicious whisky, with Nikka Whiskey being one of the most popular brands.

3. Shochu: A distilled spirit made from sweet potato, Hokkaido’s shochu is a flavorful drink that locals love.

Where to Stay in Eastern Circuit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Onsen Ryokan Yamanashi: A traditional ryokan with its natural hot springs, charming outdoor baths, and comfortable tatami rooms.

2. La Vista Akangawa Hotel: This modern hotel sits on the banks of the Akanko Onsen hot spring resort, offering stunning lake views and an outdoor bath.

3. Guesthouse Okamotoya: This budget-friendly guesthouse offers cozy rooms with futon beds and traditional Japanese meals in a communal setting.

What to Buy in Eastern Circuit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Marimo: Buy a small, fluffy Marimo as a souvenir of your visit to Eastern Circuit.

2. Shiretoko Honey: Get a taste of Hokkaido’s nature bounty with Shiretoko honey, harvested from bees feeding on wildflowers in the Shiretoko region.

3. Tokachi Wine: Eastern Circuit boasts one of the finest wine regions in Hokkaido, with Tokachi Wine producing some of the best bottles.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Circuit

Eastern Circuit’s locals are incredibly proud of their history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn as much as possible about their language, arts, and crafts. Take time to learn a few phrases of the Ainu language and try your hand at traditional embroidery and woodcarving.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Circuit

Eastern Circuit is a year-round destination, but its peak seasons are during the spring and summer months. Early summer brings warmer temperatures, while fall ushers in beautiful autumn foliage. Winter is the prime time to experience traditional winter sports, including skiing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Circuit

Do:

  • Learn basic Japanese phrases.

  • Take your shoes off upon entering homes and temples.

  • Respect local customs, such as bowing when greeting people.

Don’t:

  • Eat on the go or while walking.

  • Enter an onsen (hot spring) without washing your body first.

  • Talk loudly in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Circuit

Eastern Circuit is a relatively safe place to visit, but travelers are advised to take basic precautions, such as securing personal belongings and keeping valuables in hotel safes. Be mindful of weather conditions, especially during the winter months when roads can be icy and difficult to navigate.

Health in Eastern Circuit

Travelers to Eastern Circuit should consider purchasing travel insurance and seeking medical attention immediately if they experience any health concerns. It’s also a good idea to stock up on any necessary medications before leaving home as they may not be easily available in the region.

Getting to Eastern Circuit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Circuit

The nearest airport to Eastern Circuit is Kushiro Airport (KUH), which offers domestic flights from major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Circuit

Buses to Eastern Circuit are available from major cities in Hokkaido, including Sapporo, Asahikawa, and Abashiri. The region is well-connected by a network of bus routes and is an affordable way to travel around Eastern Circuit.

Other Options

Car rental companies are available in Kushiro and Obihiro, allowing travelers to explore Eastern Circuit at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Eastern Circuit

Travelers can get around Eastern Circuit using a combination of public transportation, such as buses and trains, and by renting cars.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Circuit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Furano and Biei

About a four-hour drive from Eastern Circuit, Furano and Biei are known for their stunning lavender fields and rolling hills.

Sapporo

Japan’s fifth-largest city, Sapporo is a must-visit destination, offering plenty of sights and activities for travelers.

Noboribetsu

Known for its hot springs and geothermal activity, Noboribetsu is a prime location for travelers looking to relax and rejuvenate after a long trip.

Conclusion

Eastern Circuit offers travelers a unique view of Japanese culture and is a great destination for those looking to experience Hokkaido’s natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and fascinating history. Whether you’re interested in hiking, soaking in a hot spring, or simply admiring the scenery, this region has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and get ready to discover the best-kept secret of Hokkaido, Japan.
