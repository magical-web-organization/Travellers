Introduction to Nakashibetsu

Nakashibetsu is a charming town located in the Eastern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan. This peaceful town is known for its rich culture, beautiful scenery, and warm hospitality. If you are a nature lover, you will be delighted by the natural beauty of the area, including scenic mountains and rivers. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through all the best things to do, places to visit, local food and drink, and much more in Nakashibetsu, Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Nakashibetsu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nakashibetsu is home to many cultural and historical attractions. Here are some must-visit destinations for those interested in the history and culture of the town:

Nakashibetsu Folklore Museum

The Nakashibetsu Folklore Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the history and culture of the town. It showcases the town’s rich cultural heritage with exhibits on traditional clothing, homes, and folk art.

Nakashibetsu Shrine

The Nakashibetsu Shrine is a beautiful shrine that dates back to the 1600s. It is one of the most visited attractions in the town and is known for its stunning architecture and serene environment.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers rejoice! Here are some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic vistas in Nakashibetsu:

Mount Shari

Mount Shari is a must-visit destination for hikers and nature lovers. It is located in the Shiretoko National Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Nakashibetsu River

If you’re in the mood for a leisurely stroll or a relaxing picnic, the Nakashibetsu River is the place to be. The river is surrounded by beautiful foliage in the fall, making it a great place to take a scenic walk or enjoy a pleasant kayaking trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakashibetsu

If you’re looking for some adventure, Nakashibetsu has got you covered. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the town:

  • Mount Shari Hike
  • Nakashibetsu River Trail
  • Rausu-Mount Io Hiking Trail

Best Things to Do in Nakashibetsu: Activities and Tours

Are you looking for things to do in Nakashibetsu? Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Nakashibetsu River Kayaking Tour
  • Horseback Riding Tour
  • Traditional Fishing Tour

Week-Long Itinerary for Nakashibetsu

If you have a week to explore the town, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Nakashibetsu and visit the Nakashibetsu Folklore Museum.

Day 2: Take a tour of the Nakashibetsu Shrine and enjoy a hike around Mount Shari.

Day 3: Go on a Nakashibetsu River Kayaking Tour and enjoy a picnic by the river.

Day 4: Take a traditional fishing tour.

Day 5: Spend the day horseback riding around the town.

Day 6: Visit the Shiretoko National Park and enjoy a hike around the park.

Day 7: Enjoy some of the local food and culture of Nakashibetsu before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakashibetsu

Must-Try Dishes

Nakashibetsu offers a unique culinary scene that you won’t want to miss. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Sanmanmai: a traditional Japanese dish made with salmon, salmon roe, and dashi broth.
  • Zangi: a deep-fried chicken dish that originated in Hokkaido.
  • Ikameshi: a popular local dish made with squid stuffed with glutinous rice and served with a sweet sauce.

Local Beverages

Don’t forget to try the local beverages while in Nakashibetsu:

  • Nihonshu: a popular local rice wine.
  • Japanese tea: Japan is known for its tea culture, so be sure to try the local blends.
  • Sake: Japan’s most famous alcoholic drink, made from fermented rice.

Where to Stay in Nakashibetsu

Nakashibetsu has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some fantastic options for your stay:

  • Nakashibetsu Grand Hotel
  • Kussharo-Genya Youth Guest House
  • Miharo Lodge

What to Buy in Nakashibetsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Be sure to bring back some unforgettable souvenirs from Nakashibetsu:

  • Dyed textiles: locally made fabrics using traditional techniques.
  • Fresh seafood: take home some of the local seafood, such as salmon or squid.
  • Wooden crafts: the town is famous for its woodcarvings, so pick up a unique piece to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakashibetsu

In Nakashibetsu, it is essential to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Always be respectful at shrines and temples.
  • Be sure to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • It’s customary to bow your head when greeting someone.

Best Time to Visit Nakashibetsu

The best time to visit Nakashibetsu is in the summer and autumn when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are available. Moreover, the autumn foliage is particularly stunning during October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakashibetsu

Do:

  • Be respectful of local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take in the stunning natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Be disrespectful to local people or culture.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakashibetsu

Nakashibetsu is generally a safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as securing their valuables and staying aware of their surroundings, when traveling.

Health in Nakashibetsu

There are no significant health risks when traveling to Nakashibetsu. It is always essential to ensure you are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Nakashibetsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nakashibetsu

The nearest airport to Nakashibetsu is Nakashibetsu Airport, which is about 20 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Nakashibetsu

Buses serve the town from the nearby Obihiro and Kushiro airports.

Other Options

Trains are also an option for traveling to Nakashibetsu.

How to Get Around in Nakashibetsu

Nakashibetsu is a small town with limited public transportation. Renting a car or a bike is the best way to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Nakashibetsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to take from Nakashibetsu, including:

  • Kushiro, Hokkaido’s largest marshland, where you can see the endangered Japanese cranes.
  • Obihiro, known for its famous hot springs and tasty-food.

Conclusion

Nakashibetsu is a beautiful and tranquil town that offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. With its stunning scenery, rich culture, and friendly people, Nakashibetsu is sure to capture your heart. This guide will help you make the most of your stay in this charming town.
