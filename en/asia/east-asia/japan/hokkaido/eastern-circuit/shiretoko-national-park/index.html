Introduction to Shiretoko National Park

Located on the eastern coast of Hokkaido in Japan, Shiretoko National Park (知床国立公園) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unspoiled natural beauty and diverse wildlife. Covering an area of over 1,300 square kilometers, the park is home to pristine forests, towering mountains, and a dramatic coastline that offers breathtaking views of the Sea of Okhotsk.

The park is also home to a rich cultural history, with evidence of human settlement dating back to the Jomon period (14,000-300 BC). The indigenous Ainu people have lived in the region for thousands of years and have a deep connection to the land and its wildlife. Today, visitors to Shiretoko National Park can engage with the park’s natural and cultural heritage through a variety of activities and experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Shiretoko National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shiretoko Nature Center: A visitor center featuring exhibits on the region’s flora, fauna, and geology.
  • Shari Museum: A museum dedicated to the history and culture of the Ainu people, who are indigenous to Hokkaido.
  • Rausu Museum: A museum showcasing the history and culture of the fishing village of Rausu, located on the eastern coast of the park.

Natural Attractions of Shiretoko National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shiretoko Five Lakes: A series of five interconnected lakes located at the foot of Mount Shiretoko. The lakes offer stunning views of the mountain and are great for hiking and birdwatching.
  • Kamuiwakka Falls: A beautiful waterfall located in the heart of the park, known for its thermal waters and natural hot spring.
  • Cape Kamui: A scenic lookout overlooking the rocky coastline and the Sea of Okhotsk, located in the northern part of the park.
  • Mount Rausu: The highest peak in the park, standing at 1,661 meters. The mountain offers challenging hikes for experienced adventurers, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shiretoko National Park

Shiretoko National Park offers a variety of hiking trails and biking routes that cater to different levels of experience and fitness. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Shiretoko Five Lakes Trail: A 9-kilometer trail that passes by all five of the park’s interconnected lakes. The trail is rated as easy to moderate and takes approximately 3-4 hours to complete.
  • Furepe Waterfall Trail: A 3.5-kilometer trail that leads to the beautiful Furepe Waterfall, located near the park’s western entrance. The trail is rated as easy and takes approximately 1-2 hours to complete.
  • Mount Rausu Trail: A challenging 18-kilometer trail that leads to the summit of Mount Rausu. The trail is rated as difficult and takes approximately 10-12 hours to complete round trip.

Best Things to Do in Shiretoko National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Watching: The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including brown bears, deer, foxes, and eagles. Visitors can take guided tours to observe these animals in their natural habitat.
  • Fishing: The Sea of Okhotsk is home to a variety of fish species, including salmon, trout, and cod. Visitors can participate in fishing tours or try their luck at shore fishing.
  • Kayaking: The park’s coastline offers opportunities for sea kayaking, providing a unique perspective on the park’s rugged coastline and wildlife.
  • Onsen: Visitors can relax in one of the park’s natural hot springs, such as the Kamuiwakka Falls hot spring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shiretoko National Park

Day 1-2: Explore the western side of the park, visiting the Furepe Waterfall, Kaminoko Pond, and Oshinkoshin Falls. Day 3-4: Take a guided wildlife tour and go fishing in the Sea of Okhotsk. Day 5-6: Hike the Shiretoko Five Lakes Trail and Mount Rausu Trail. Day 7: Relax in an onsen and visit the Shiretoko Nature Center or Shari Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Shiretoko National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Uni (Sea Urchin): Shiretoko National Park is known for its high-quality uni, which is harvested from the Sea of Okhotsk. Visitors can try uni in various forms, such as raw or grilled.
  • Kani (Crab): Hokkaido is famous for its snow crabs, which are caught in the Sea of Okhotsk. Visitors can enjoy kani in various forms, such as sashimi, hot pot, or grilled.
  • Ikura (Salmon Roe): The park’s rivers and streams are home to salmon, and their roe is prized as a delicacy. Visitors can try ikura in various forms, such as sushi or rice bowls.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hokkaido is known for its high-quality sake, which is made using local rice and water. Visitors can sample different varieties at local breweries.
  • Shochu: A distilled beverage made from rice, barley, or sweet potatoes. Shochu is a popular drink in Hokkaido and can be enjoyed in different flavors.

Where to Stay in Shiretoko National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options in Shiretoko National Park to fit different budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Shiretoko Daiichi Hotel: A luxury hotel located near the park’s entrance, with hot springs and a restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • Shiretoko Iwaobetsu Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located near the park’s western entrance, with dormitory-style and private rooms.
  • Kita-no-Daichi Hotel: A traditional Japanese-style hotel located near the park’s eastern entrance, with hot springs and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

What to Buy in Shiretoko National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ainu Crafts: Visitors can purchase traditional Ainu crafts, such as carved wooden masks and woven baskets, at local shops and markets.
  • Seafood: Visitors can purchase fresh seafood caught in the Sea of Okhotsk, such as crab and salmon, to bring home.
  • Local Sake and Shochu: Visitors can bring home bottles of Hokkaido’s famous sake and shochu as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Shiretoko National Park

The Ainu people have a strong cultural presence in Shiretoko National Park, with many traditional practices and customs still observed today. Visitors should respect local customs, such as removing shoes when entering homes or sacred places, and avoid disturbing wildlife or natural habitats.

Best Time to Visit Shiretoko National Park

The best time to visit Shiretoko National Park is during the summer months from June to September, when the weather is mild and the park’s hiking trails are open. During this time, visitors can also observe wildlife and participate in outdoor activities such as fishing and kayaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shiretoko National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes when entering homes or sacred places.
  • Properly dispose of trash and waste, and avoid disturbing wildlife or natural habitats.
  • Follow park rules and regulations, such as staying on designated hiking trails and not feeding wild animals.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harm wildlife, such as feeding or approaching bears or other animals.
  • Leave trash or waste on hiking trails or in natural areas.
  • Ignore park rules or regulations, as they are in place to protect visitors and the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shiretoko National Park

Shiretoko National Park is a wild and rugged area, and visitors should take appropriate safety precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. Some safety tips to follow include:

  • Bring appropriate gear and clothing for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Carry a map and compass or GPS device, and stay on designated hiking trails.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and pack accordingly.
  • Carry bear spray and know how to use it in case of a bear encounter.

Health in Shiretoko National Park

Visitors to Shiretoko National Park should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities and take appropriate precautions to protect their health. Some health tips to follow include:

  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and bug bites.
  • Stay hydrated and bring plenty of water on hikes and outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of food safety and hygiene when consuming local cuisine.

Getting to Shiretoko National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shiretoko National Park

  • Memanbetsu Airport: The closest airport to Shiretoko National Park, located approximately 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Shiretoko National Park

  • Utoro Bus Terminal: The main bus station located near the park’s western entrance.
  • Rausu Bus Station: The bus station located near the park’s eastern entrance.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car at Memanbetsu Airport or in nearby towns and cities to explore the park at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Shiretoko National Park

The best way to get around Shiretoko National Park is by car or bus. Visitors can take the park’s public bus system to access hiking trails and scenic viewpoints. Rental cars are also available in nearby towns and cities for those who prefer to explore the park independently.

Where to Go Next from Shiretoko National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abashiri: A historic city located on the eastern coast of Hokkaido known for its prison museum and icebreaker ship.
  • Lake Akan: A volcanic lake with crystal-clear waters located in the eastern part of Hokkaido, known for its hot springs and Ainu cultural experiences.
  • Kushiro: A port city located on the eastern coast of Hokkaido known for its marshlands and wildlife, as well as its fresh seafood.

Conclusion

Shiretoko National Park is a nature-lover’s paradise, offering a diverse range of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and stunning natural scenery. Visitors to the park can immerse themselves in the rich history and culture of the Ainu people while exploring the rugged coastline, pristine forests, and towering mountains. With its unique wildlife and diverse range of activities, Shiretoko National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan.
