Introduction to Obihiro

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Circuit, in the beautiful island of Hokkaido, the city of Obihiro is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Often overlooked by tourists in favour of better-known Hokkaido destinations, Obihiro boasts a unique and charming character that is both authentic and inviting.

List of Places to Visit in Obihiro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Obihiro is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the city’s rich cultural heritage. We recommend visiting the following:

  • Obihiro Shrine: a beautiful shrine located in the heart of the city that dates back to the Edo period.
  • Obihiro City Museum: dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of Obihiro and its surrounding areas.
  • Tokachi Agricultural Museum: featuring exhibitions on the region’s farming practices and history.

Natural Attractions of Obihiro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, Obihiro has plenty of options to explore. From hiking to scenic drives, here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Tokachi Millennium Forest: a sprawling park with beautiful gardens, forests, and walking trails.
  • Obihiro Zoo: home to more than 350 animals, including the popular red pandas.
  • Mount Hakuun: a scenic hiking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Obihiro

Obihiro is a paradise for nature lovers, with several hiking and biking trails in and around the city. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Hakuunzan Nature Trail: an easy hiking trail that starts at the foot of Mount Hakuun and offers stunning views of the Tokachi Plains.
  • Obihiro City Walk: a lovely walking route that takes you through the heart of the city, showcasing its unique architecture and cultural heritage.
  • Taisetsuzan National Park: a breathtaking park that offers various hiking trails through mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Obihiro: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural attractions, Obihiro offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • Tokachi Wine: visit a local winery and sample some of the region’s delicious wines.
  • Horseback Riding: take a guided horseback ride through the beautiful Tokachi Plains.
  • Tokachi Millennium Forest canopy tour: experience the park’s beautiful forests from a unique perspective on a canopy tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Obihiro

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Obihiro:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Obihiro Zoo and nearby parks
Day 2 Experience local culture at Obihiro City Museum and Obihiro Shrine
Day 3 Hike Mount Hakuun
Day 4 Take a guided horseback tour
Day 5 Visit Tokachi Wine winery and enjoy a wine tasting
Day 6 Explore Taisetsuzan National Park
Day 7 Relax and indulge in a visit to an onsen or spa

Food and Drink Scene of Obihiro

Must-Try Dishes

Obihiro is known for its delicious cuisine, particularly its Hokkaido-style dishes. Here are some must-try dishes when visiting the city:

  • Butadon: a local specialty of grilled pork over rice.
  • Motsunabe: a hotpot dish with beef offal and vegetables.
  • Tokachi Gyu: a type of local beef known for its outstanding quality.

Local Beverages

Hokkaido’s cool, humid climate is ideal for producing some of Japan’s finest drinks, particularly beer and sake. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Asahi Hokkaido: a popular beer brewed in Hokkaido.
  • Otokoyama Sake: a high-quality sake that has won numerous awards.
  • Tokachi Wine: a local winery that produces delicious wine using grapes grown in the Tokachi area.

Where to Stay in Obihiro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Obihiro offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Obihiro Grand Hotel: a luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Fukeiso: a traditional Japanese inn located near Obihiro Zoo.
  • Apa Hotel Obihiro Ekimae: a budget-friendly hotel located near the train station.

What to Buy in Obihiro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs to consider bringing back home:

  • Tokachi Wine: a bottle or two of the region’s delicious wine.
  • Horse Oil: a popular skincare product made from horse fat.
  • Tokachi Beef: a package of local beef to cook at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Obihiro

Obihiro has a unique culture that is both traditional and modern. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect for elders: Japan has a strong tradition of valuing and respecting elders, and this extends to Obihiro.
  • Shoes off: it’s customary to remove your shoes when entering homes, temples, and other traditional buildings.
  • Etiquette in onsen: when visiting an onsen, it’s important to shower before entering the hot spring and to cover your private parts.

Best Time to Visit Obihiro

The best time to visit Obihiro depends on personal preferences. If you enjoy winter sports, the city is perfect for skiing and snowboarding in the winter months. However, if you prefer milder temperatures and hiking, spring and fall are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Obihiro

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes before entering traditional buildings.

Don’t:

  • Talk loudly in public places.
  • Litter or smoke in public areas.
  • Refuse food or drink offered to you by a local.

Safety and Health

Safety in Obihiro

Obihiro is a safe city that sees very little crime. However, it’s always essential to take necessary safety precautions, such as looking both ways before crossing the street or securing your belongings during travel.

Health in Obihiro

Japan has a high standard of healthcare, and Obihiro is no exception. Visitors can find many hospitals and clinics in the city if needed, and medical staff is generally fluent in English.

Getting to Obihiro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Obihiro

  • Obihiro Airport: located just 30 minutes from the city centre, Obihiro Airport is the most convenient option for those travelling to Obihiro.

Bus Stations Near Obihiro

  • Obihiro Bus Terminal: located just a few minutes walk from the train station, the bus terminal offers convenient connections to other Hokkaido destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Obihiro is well connected to other Japanese cities by train, with frequent services to and from Tokyo, Kyoto, and other major cities.

How to Get Around in Obihiro

Obihiro is a compact city that is easily navigable on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and buses are also widely available for those who prefer not to walk or cycle.

Where to Go Next from Obihiro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other parts of Hokkaido, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Sapporo: the vibrant capital city of Hokkaido, known for its historic parks and museums, bustling nightlife, and world-renowned seafood.
  • Furano: a charming town known for its stunning lavender fields and winter sports.
  • Noboribetsu: a hot spring resort town that offers relaxing onsens, picturesque scenery, and delicious local cuisine.

Conclusion

Obihiro is a charming city that offers visitors the perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and modern convenience. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Obihiro has something for everyone. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your trip to this fascinating part of Hokkaido.
