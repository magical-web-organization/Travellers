Introduction to Akan National Park

Located in the Eastern Circuit region of Hokkaido, Japan, Akan National Park is a natural wonderland that is famous for its hot springs, volcanoes, and picturesque lakes. Established in 1934, the park covers an area of 90,481 hectares and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna. The park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and those interested in Japanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Akan National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Akan Ainu Kotan: A traditional Ainu village where you can learn about the culture and history of the Ainu people who have lived in Hokkaido for centuries.
  • Akankohan Eco-Museum Center: An educational center that showcases the natural history and geology of Akan National Park.

Natural Attractions of Akan National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Akan: This picturesque lake is one of the most beautiful and popular tourist destinations in the park. Take a boat tour, enjoy the hot springs, or hike to the top of Mt. Oakan for breathtaking views.
  • Mt. Meakan: A popular hiking destination, Mt. Meakan is an active volcano that last erupted in 2008. The hike to the summit is challenging but rewarding, offering stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Kawayu Onsen: A natural hot spring that is located by the riverside and has plenty of outdoor baths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akan National Park

There are many hiking and biking routes in Akan National Park that offer stunning views and an opportunity to explore the beautiful natural surroundings. Some popular routes include:

  • Mt. Oakan: A challenging hike that takes around 8 hours to complete, but the breathtaking views from the summit are well worth the effort.
  • Lake Mashu: A scenic hiking trail that winds around the beautiful Lake Mashu, known for its clear water and stunning sunrises.
  • Biking Route 44: This scenic highway is a popular route for cyclists, offering beautiful views of the Akan Mountains and Lake Akan.

Best Things to Do in Akan National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Winter activities: Skiing, snowshoeing, and ice fishing are popular winter activities in the park.
  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour of Lake Akan or Lake Mashu for stunning views and an opportunity to see local wildlife and hot springs.
  • Hot springs: Akan National Park is home to many natural hot springs, including Kawayu Onsen and Akanko Onsen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akan National Park

Day 1: Explore Lake Akan and take a boat tour. Day 2: Hike Mt. Oakan or Mt. Meakan. Day 3: Visit the Akan Ainu Kotan. Day 4: Visit Kawayu Onsen and enjoy the hot springs. Day 5: Take a scenic drive along Biking Route 44. Day 6: Visit Lake Mashu and hike the surrounding trails. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Akan National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marimo: a type of algae that grows in Lake Akan and is harvested for its unique texture and flavor.
  • Hokkaido milk: Known for its rich and creamy taste, Hokkaido milk is used in many local dishes and desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Made from local rice, Hokkaido sake is known for its smooth and mellow flavor.
  • Otaru beer: Brewed in the nearby town of Otaru, this beer is made using fresh Hokkaido water and local hops.

Where to Stay in Akan National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation in Akan National Park, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Akan Tsuruga Besso Hinanoza: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of Lake Akan and has its own hot springs.
  • Akan Yuku no Sato Tsuruga: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a variety of dining options.
  • Akan Yuku no Sato DAN: This cozy guesthouse is located in a traditional Ainu village and offers an opportunity to experience local culture and hospitality.

What to Buy in Akan National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ainu handicrafts: Handmade crafts such as wood carvings and embroidery are popular souvenirs that are sold in many local shops.
  • Marimo products: Marimo is used to create a variety of unique products such as marimo-shaped soap and keychains.

Local Culture and Customs of Akan National Park

The Ainu people have a rich history and culture that is heavily influenced by the natural surroundings of Akan National Park. Visitors can learn about their traditions, music, and dance at the Akan Ainu Kotan and other cultural centers in the area. Respect for nature and harmony with the environment are key values in Ainu culture.

Best Time to Visit Akan National Park

The best time to visit Akan National Park depends on your interests and preferences. The park is stunning in all seasons, but each season offers unique activities and experiences. Winter is a popular time for skiing and snow sports, while spring and summer are great for hiking and outdoor activities. Autumn is a beautiful time to visit, with colorful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akan National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take your shoes off at the entrance of temples, museums, and homes.
  • Dispose of trash properly.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local rules and regulations.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akan National Park

Akan National Park is generally safe, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Always check the weather forecast before heading out, bring plenty of water and snacks, and wear appropriate clothing and footwear. In case of emergency, dial 110 for police, 119 for ambulance or fire department.

Health in Akan National Park

There are no major health risks in Akan National Park, but visitors should be aware of the high altitude and changeable weather. Proper hydration and sun protection are recommended, and it’s always a good idea to carry a basic first aid kit with you.

Getting to Akan National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akan National Park

The nearest airport to Akan National Park is Kushiro Airport, which is served by domestic flights from various cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Akan National Park

Buses run from Kushiro and other nearby cities to Akan National Park. There are also several bus routes within the park that connect different attractions.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for exploring Akan National Park, and there are several rental car companies located in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Akan National Park

Walking, hiking, and biking are popular ways to get around Akan National Park. There are also bus routes and boat tours that connect different attractions within the park.

Where to Go Next from Akan National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Shikotsu: A stunning lake surrounded by mountains and forests, located about 2 hours away from Akan National Park.
  • Sapporo: The largest city in Hokkaido, known for its food, culture, and festivals. About 4 hours away from Akan National Park by car.

Conclusion

With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and outdoor activities, Akan National Park is a must-visit destination in Hokkaido, Japan. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip and given you inspiration for things to see and do in the park. Enjoy your visit!
