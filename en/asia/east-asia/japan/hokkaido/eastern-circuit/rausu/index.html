Travel Guide to Rausu, Hokkaido, Japan

Rausu is a small fishing village located in the eastern circuit of Hokkaido, Japan. It is known for its stunning natural beauty and diverse marine wildlife, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with everything you need to plan a memorable trip to Rausu.

List of Places to Visit in Rausu

Rausu offers an array of tourist attractions that cater to both the adventurous and the cultural traveler. Here are the top places to visit in Rausu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rausu Historical Museum: This museum displays local artifacts, photographs, and documents showcasing the history and culture of the Ainu people and the fishing industry in Rausu.
  • Rausu Folk History Museum: This museum highlights the daily life and traditions of the locals through exhibits of their tools, arts and crafts, and household items.

Natural Attractions of Rausu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shiretoko National Park: The park is located on the Shiretoko Peninsula and is known for its breathtaking scenery, diverse flora, and fauna. It also offers various trails for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching.
  • Rausu Mount Iou: The active volcano offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and sea. The hike to the top is steep but rewarding.
  • Rausu River: The river runs through the village and is an excellent spot for fishing salmon and trout during summer and fall.
  • Whale watching: Rausu is one of the best places for whale watching in Japan, with various whale-watching tours available from the harbor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rausu

Rausu is a fantastic place for trekking and cycling. Here are some popular routes:

  • Shiretoko Five Lakes: A walking trail that leads through five picturesque lakes surrounded by the Shiretoko mountain range.
  • Rausu Mountain: A challenging hike to the summit of Rausu Mountain, offering a panoramic view of the Sea of Okhotsk, Kunashir Island, and the Nemuro Peninsula.
  • Cycling trails: Rent a bike and explore the scenic countryside of Rausu village.

Best Things to Do in Rausu: Activities and Tours

Rausu is full of exciting activities, making it a perfect place to spend a memorable vacation. Here are some popular things to do in Rausu:

  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour and explore the Sea of Okhotsk or watch whales and dolphins.
  • Fishing: Rausu is known for its fishing industry, so indulge in fishing salmon or trout.
  • Birdwatching: Rausu is home to many migratory birds, and birdwatching is a popular activity among bird lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rausu

Day 1: Arrive in Rausu and relax after a long journey. Day 2: Explore the Rausu village, take a stroll along the beach and visit the Rausu Folk History Museum. Day 3: Hike the Shiretoko Five Lakes Trail. Day 4: Take a boat tour to explore the Sea of Okhotsk and watch whales and dolphins. Day 5: Soak in the natural hot springs at Rausu Onsen. Day 6: Go fishing on the Rausu River and enjoy a freshly caught meal. Day 7: Take a cycling tour of the village.

Food and Drink Scene of Rausu

Hokkaido is known for its fresh seafood and dairy products, and Rausu is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaisen Don: A bowl of rice topped with fresh sashimi from the Sea of Okhotsk.
  • Taraba Crab: A species of snow crab that’s a local delight in Hokkaido.
  • Uni Don: A bowl of rice topped with sea urchin.

Local Beverages

  • Hokkaido Milk: Hokkaido is famous for its high-quality dairy products, including milk, cheese, and ice cream.
  • Sake: Sake is a traditional Japanese fermented rice wine that pairs well with food.

Where to Stay in Rausu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rausu offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended options:

  • Rausu Daiichi Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near the harbor, offering stunning views of the sea and mountains.
  • Guest House Tomoya: A cozy guest house with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Kunashiri Green Hotel: A modern hotel located near the Rausu Onsen, offering comfortable rooms and hot springs.

What to Buy in Rausu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rausu offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that are unique to Hokkaido. Here are some popular items to bring home:

  • Rausu Ainu Crafts: Unique handcrafted items made by the Ainu people, such as wooden carvings and embroidery.
  • Seafood Products: Fish products like salmon, trout, and taraba crab can be bought fresh or preserved.
  • Shiretoko National Park Souvenirs: T-shirts, keychains and other items featuring the park’s emblem can make excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Rausu

Rausu has a significant Ainu population, and the local community still follows their unique customs and traditions. The Ainu people are known for their craftsmanship, music, and dance. Visitors can learn about their daily life and attend traditional festivals that showcase their culture.

Best Time to Visit Rausu

The best time to visit Rausu is from May to October when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities. In winter, the village is covered in snow, making it an excellent destination for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rausu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Ainu people.
  • Try the local seafood delicacies.
  • Be mindful of wildlife and marine animals while on tours.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage natural landmarks.
  • Do not disturb wildlife or marine animals.
  • Do not break any fishing laws or regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rausu

Rausu is generally a safe place to travel, but visitors should follow general safety precautions, such as wearing appropriate clothing and carrying necessary gear while hiking, and following instructions while on tours or boat rides.

Health in Rausu

Hokkaido has an excellent healthcare system, and Rausu has a local clinic that can treat minor ailments. However, it’s recommended to carry travel insurance that includes emergency medical coverage.

Getting to Rausu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rausu is located at the eastern end of Hokkaido, and transportation options are limited. Here are some ways to reach Rausu:

Airports Near Rausu

  • Kushiro Airport: The nearest airport to Rausu is Kushiro airport, which is around a 3-hour drive from the village.

Bus Stations Near Rausu

  • JR Shiretoko-Rausu Station: A railway station located near Rausu offers frequent bus services to the village.

Other Options

  • Rent a Car: Renting a car is an excellent option for travelers who want to explore the adjacent areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Rausu

Rausu village is small, and you can travel on foot or rent a bicycle to explore the town. For nearby attractions or tours, hiring a car or taking a local bus is a suitable option.

Where to Go Next from Rausu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have a few extra days, you can extend your trip and explore nearby destinations or excursions from Rausu, such as:

  • Nemuro: A small town located on the Nemuro Peninsula, known for its excellent seafood and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Lake Mashu: The lake is located in Akan Mashu National Park and is known for its crystal-clear waters.
  • Abashiri: A city located on the northeast coast of Hokkaido, known for its prison museum and the drift ice that forms off the coast in winter.

Conclusion

Rausu is a perfect summer destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and cultural travelers. Whether you want to hike, fish, or explore local culture and cuisine, Rausu has something for everyone. With the help of this comprehensive guide, plan your Rausu itinerary, and make unforgettable memories on your trip to Japan.
