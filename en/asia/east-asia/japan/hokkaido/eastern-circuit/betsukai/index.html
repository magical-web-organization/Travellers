Introduction to Betsukai

Located in the Eastern Circuit of Hokkaido, Betsukai is a charming town known for its scenic views, outdoor activities, and cultural landmarks. The town has a rich history that dates back to the Jomon period, and it is home to many cultural and historical sites. Visitors can explore the town’s natural beauty by hiking or biking through the lush forests, and they can also enjoy local dishes and beverages that are unique to the region. This travel guide will provide an overview of the town’s must-see attractions, recommended activities and tours, dining options, accommodations, and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in Betsukai

Betsukai is a quaint town that is filled with numerous tourist attractions. Here are some of the must-visit sites in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Betsukai History Museum: This is a small museum that showcases the history and culture of Betsukai through various artifacts and exhibits. It is a great place to learn about the town’s history, particularly during the Jomon period.

  • Betsukai Shrine: This is a sacred Shinto shrine that holds great cultural significance. The shrine was established in the 19th century, and it is dedicated to the deity of agriculture.

  • Heiwa no Mori Park: This park is a tribute to the victims of the Second World War. It has a peaceful atmosphere and is an excellent place to unwind and appreciate the beauty of the surroundings.

  • Katsurazawa Dam: This dam was built in the 1960s and provides hydroelectric power to the surrounding areas. The dam is known for its incredible views of the vast Betsukai countryside.

Natural Attractions of Betsukai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nemuro Karafuto Park: This park is a bird watcher’s paradise. Visitors will be able to watch various migratory birds from different regions around the world, as well as see the stunning views of the Sea of Okhotsk.

  • Cape Kimuaneppu: This cape is well-known for its stunning views of the surrounding sea and coast. It is an excellent place to watch the sunrise or sunset.

  • Menashidai Marsh: This marsh is a protected wildlife sanctuary that is home to various birds, animals, and plants. It is an excellent place to hike or bike, and visitors can enjoy the serene beauty of the marsh.

  • Notsuke Peninsula: This peninsula is a nature reserve that features a diverse landscape of sand dunes, forests, and wetlands. Visitors can walk along the shore, watch various animals in their natural habitats, and take in the unique scenery of the peninsula.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Betsukai

Betsukai is a town that is best explored by foot or bike. Here are some of the recommended routes for hiking or biking:

  • Betsukai Nature Trail: This trail is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Betsukai. It is a well-marked path that takes visitors through various forests and marshes.

  • Cape Kimuaneppu Trail: This is a relatively easy trail that takes visitors around the cape. It is an excellent place to watch the sunrise or sunset.

  • Menashidai Marsh Trail: This trail takes visitors through the Menashidai Marsh and is a great way to see the vast assortment of local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Betsukai: Activities and Tours

Betsukai is a town that has many unique activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Bird Watching Tour: Betsukai is known to be a great place for bird watchers, with various migratory birds visiting the town every year. Visitors can go on a guided tour to watch these birds up close.

  • Horseback Riding Tour: This is a great way to explore the town’s natural beauty. Visitors can hire a guide and take a horseback riding tour around the lush forests of Betsukai.

  • Sea Fishing Tour: Visitors who love fishing can take a guided tour to the Sea of Okhotsk to catch local species of fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Betsukai

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Betsukai:

Day 1: Visit Betsukai History Museum and Betsukai Shrine.

Day 2: Go on a bird watching tour in Nemuro Karafuto Park.

Day 3: Explore Menashidai Marsh Trail by bike or foot.

Day 4: Take a horseback riding tour through the forests of Betsukai.

Day 5: Visit Katsurazawa Dam and Cape Kimuaneppu.

Day 6: Go on a sea fishing tour in the Sea of Okhotsk.

Day 7: Relax in Heiwa no Mori Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Betsukai

Betsukai has a unique food and drink scene that is influenced by its location and history. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Katsuo no Tataki: This is a dish that features lightly seared bonito. It is served with soy sauce, ginger, and garlic.

  • Hokke no Shioyaki: This is a simple dish that features grilled Atka mackerel with salt. It is usually served with rice and miso soup.

  • Iburigakko: This is a pickled vegetable that is unique to Hokkaido. It is made by smoking and pickling daikon radish.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hokkaido is known for its sake. Visitors should try the local varieties, such as Tokachi and Yoichi sake.

  • Kombucha: This fermented tea is known for its numerous health benefits. Visitors can find local variations of this tea in Betsukai.

Where to Stay in Betsukai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Betsukai has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Sun Hills Kousen: This is a well-appointed hotel with stunning views of the countryside. It has an on-site restaurant and is within walking distance of many local attractions.

  • Betsukai Guest House: This is an affordable option for travelers on a budget. The guest house has clean and comfortable rooms and is close to many hiking and biking trails.

  • Minshuku Terakawa: This is a traditional Japanese guesthouse that offers visitors a unique cultural experience. The guesthouse has beautiful tatami rooms and Japanese-style baths.

What to Buy in Betsukai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Betsukai is known for its unique products that make for great souvenirs. Visitors should consider purchasing the following:

  • Iburigakko: This pickled vegetable is unique to Hokkaido and makes for a great souvenir.

  • Pottery: Betsukai is known for its unique pottery, featuring local designs and patterns.

  • Hokkaido Sake: Visitors can bring home a bottle of local sake as a gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Betsukai

Here are some of the local customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of when visiting Betsukai:

  • Respect: It is customary to be respectful towards others and to be mindful of local customs and traditions.

  • Gift Giving: When visiting a local’s home or attending a social gathering, it is customary to bring a small gift.

  • Onsen Etiquette: When using the local hot springs (onsen), visitors should be aware of the proper etiquette. Men and women usually bathe separately, and it is customary to wash oneself before entering the onsen.

Best Time to Visit Betsukai

The best time to visit Betsukai is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, visitors can also enjoy the stunning autumn foliage in September and October, and the winter snowscapes in December to February. Spring (April to May) is also a great time to visit when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Betsukai

When visiting Betsukai, visitors should keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do be respectful towards locals and their customs.

  • Do bring a small gift when visiting someone’s home.

  • Do try local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your trash in public places.

  • Don’t litter in the mountains or forests.

  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Betsukai

Betsukai is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also be mindful of safety measures when hiking or biking in the forests or mountains.

Health in Betsukai

Betsukai has a modern healthcare system that provides excellent medical care. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before visiting the town.

Getting to Betsukai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Betsukai

The closest airport to Betsukai is Nakashibetsu Airport, which is about 50 km away. The airport has several connecting flights to major cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Sapporo.

Bus Stations Near Betsukai

Betsukai has a bus station that connects to other nearby towns and cities. Visitors can take a bus from Sapporo, Asahikawa, and Obihiro.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a rental car or hire a taxi to travel to Betsukai. However, it is recommended that visitors have an international driving license if they plan on driving in Japan.

How to Get Around in Betsukai

Betsukai is a small town that is best explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Betsukai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Betsukai is located in a region that has many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the recommended places to visit:

  • Nemuro: This town is known for its stunning views of the Sea of Okhotsk and its fresh seafood.

  • Shiretoko: This national park is a UNESCO World Heritage site that features stunning landscapes and wildlife.

  • Obihiro: This city is known for its local delicacies, such as butadon (pork rice bowl) and sweets made from locally grown potatoes.

Conclusion

Betsukai is a charming town that is filled with many unique attractions, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the town by hiking, biking, or walking around the various trails and paths. To make the most of your visit, be sure to try the local cuisine, stay in one of the charming accommodations, and learn about the local customs and traditions.
