Monbetsu Travel Guide: Everything You Need to Know Before You Go

Monbetsu, a scenic city on the northeastern coast of Hokkaido, is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. Offering a blend of natural and cultural experiences, Monbetsu is perfect for travelers who want to experience the authentic beauty of Japan outside the typical tourist sites. If you’re planning a trip to Monbetsu, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most out of your visit.

Introduction to Monbetsu

Monbetsu is a city in the Eastern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan, known for its scenic beauty and rich culture. The city is located in the Okhotsk region, which is known for its abundant seafood, rugged coastline, and stunning natural scenery. Monbetsu is home to approximately 24,000 people and is located 585 kilometers northeast of Sapporo, the capital of Hokkaido.

List of Places to Visit in Monbetsu

Monbetsu is home to many attractions, ranging from cultural landmarks and museums to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best places to visit in Monbetsu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Okhotsk Ryu-hyo Museum: This museum showcases the ice floes that drift southward along the Okhotsk Sea coast every winter. Visitors can learn about the natural environment of the Okhotsk region and see up close the different species of marine life that inhabit the area.

  • Monbetsu City Museum: This museum displays various artifacts and photographs that showcase the history and culture of Monbetsu. The museum also hosts exhibitions and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Monbetsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Saroma: This is the largest lake in Hokkaido and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including fishing, camping, hiking, and kayaking.

  • Cape Monbetsu: This cape offers incredible views of the Okhotsk Sea, especially during the winter months when ice floes can be seen drifting by. At the top of the cape, you can find the Monbetsu Okhotsk Airport Observation Tower, which offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monbetsu

Monbetsu has several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the area’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Monbetsu Nature Trail: This trail offers a scenic hike through the forested hills of Monbetsu. The round trip is approximately 8 kilometers and takes around 3-4 hours to complete.

  • Lake Saroma Cycling Road: This road stretches for 22 kilometers along the shore of Lake Saroma and offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscape. The road is well-maintained and is suitable for cyclists of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Monbetsu: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Monbetsu. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Icebreaker Cruise: During the winter months, visitors can take a cruise on an icebreaker ship and experience the thrill of breaking through the thick ice floes of the Okhotsk Sea.

  • Seafood Tasting: Monbetsu is known for its fresh and delicious seafood. Visitors can sample a variety of seafood dishes, including crab, salmon, and scallops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monbetsu

If you’re planning to spend a week in Monbetsu, here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the best attractions and activities in the area:

Day 1: Arrive in Monbetsu and check into your hotel. Visit the Monbetsu City Museum to learn about the history and culture of the area.

Day 2: Take a hike on the Monbetsu Nature Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the forested hills.

Day 3: Go on an icebreaker cruise and experience the thrill of breaking through the ice floes of the Okhotsk Sea.

Day 4: Visit Cape Monbetsu and see the ice floes up close. Go to the Monbetsu Okhotsk Airport Observation Tower for a panoramic view of the area.

Day 5: Rent a bike and ride along the Lake Saroma Cycling Road. Stop at a local restaurant to sample some of the area’s delicious seafood dishes.

Day 6: Take a trip to Lake Saroma and enjoy a day of fishing, camping, or kayaking.

Day 7: Depart Monbetsu and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Monbetsu

Monbetsu is famous for its fresh seafood, which is caught daily by local fishermen. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Okhotsk king crab: This is the priciest yet the most sought-after seafood of Monbetsu. It is known for its sweet, tender meat and is usually served boiled or grilled.

  • Hokkai Shiretoko oyster: These oysters are plentiful in the region, and offer a unique and delicate flavor that will leave you wanting for more.

Local Beverages

  • Shirorun sake: This locally brewed sake is famous for its mild flavor and aroma. It is made from pure, natural water from the Shirorun River.

Where to Stay in Monbetsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monbetsu offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Monbetsu include:

  • Hokkaido Hotel: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the Okhotsk Sea and is known for its excellent service and amenities.

  • Monbetsu Prince Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Monbetsu and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.

  • Guesthouse Monbetsu: This budget-friendly guesthouse is perfect for travelers who want to save money on accommodation without sacrificing comfort and convenience.

What to Buy in Monbetsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Monbetsu, don’t forget to bring home some souvenirs and local products. Some of the best items to buy in Monbetsu include:

  • Okhotsk Monbetsu Ice: This is a souvenir item that is unique to Monbetsu. It is a cylindrical piece of ice that is harvested from the sea and can be enjoyed as is or used to make drinks.

  • Monbetsu Kobo biscuits: These biscuits are made with local ingredients and are known for their crispy texture and rich flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Monbetsu

Monbetsu has a rich culture and history that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Politeness and respect for others are highly valued in Japanese culture. Be sure to follow the rules and customs of each place you visit.

  • When entering someone’s home or a public place, be sure to take off your shoes and put on slippers provided.

Best Time to Visit Monbetsu

The best time to visit Monbetsu depends on what you want to do and see. If you want to experience the ice floes and winter sports, the best time to visit is from January to March. If you prefer hiking and outdoor activities, the best time to visit is from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monbetsu

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Monbetsu:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a public place.

  • Follow the rules and customs of each place you visit.

Don’t:

  • Use your phone or talk loudly in public places or on public transportation.

  • Smoke in non-smoking areas.

Safety and Health

Monbetsu is a safe and healthy place to visit, but it’s always important to take necessary precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Monbetsu

  • Always carry a map and a mobile phone with you.

  • Watch out for traffic when walking or cycling.

Health in Monbetsu

  • Make sure you have travel insurance and carry any necessary medications with you.

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Monbetsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Monbetsu is easy, thanks to its convenient location and excellent transportation options. Here are some ways to get to Monbetsu:

Airports Near Monbetsu

  • Monbetsu Airport: This airport offers domestic flights to and from Tokyo, Sapporo, and other major cities in Japan.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station to Monbetsu is Abashiri Station, which is served by the JR East and Hokkaido Railway Company.

  • Bus: There are several daily bus services that connect Monbetsu to major cities in Hokkaido.

How to Get Around in Monbetsu

Getting around Monbetsu is easy, thanks to its well-maintained transportation network. Here are some of the best ways to get around in Monbetsu:

  • Rent a car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the area, especially if you plan to visit attractions that are not easily accessible by public transportation.

  • Public transportation: Monbetsu has a reliable bus network that connects major attractions and points of interest.

Where to Go Next from Monbetsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Hokkaido after your visit to Monbetsu, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Abashiri: This is a coastal city that is known for its seafood, hot springs, and historical landmarks.

  • Sapporo: Hokkaido’s capital city is known for its vibrant culture, shopping, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Monbetsu is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to experience the authentic beauty of Japan. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural landmarks, and delicious seafood, Monbetsu has something to offer for every type of traveler. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to plan an unforgettable trip to Monbetsu.
