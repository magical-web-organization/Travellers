Comprehensive Guide to Hokkaido

If you’re planning a trip to Japan, Hokkaido, Japan’s northernmost island, should definitely be on your list. Hokkaido is well-known for its breathtaking natural beauty, unique culture, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Hokkaido by providing you with all the necessary information.

Introduction to Hokkaido

Hokkaido is Japan’s second-largest island and is located in the northern part of the country. With its rugged mountains, unspoiled forests, hot springs, and vibrant cities, the island is a delightful mix of modern and traditional culture. Hokkaido is rich in history and culture, and it’s fascinating to learn about the indigenous Ainu people, who have lived on the island for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Hokkaido

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hokkaido is home to several museums and historical sites that are a testament to the region’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Sapporo Beer Museum
  • Hokkaido Museum of Northern Peoples
  • Asahiyama Memorial Park

Natural Attractions of Hokkaido: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hokkaido is blessed with bountiful nature, and there’s no shortage of scenic views and outdoor adventures to indulge in. Some of the top-rated attractions include:

  • Shiretoko National Park
  • Daisetsuzan National Park
  • Lake Toya

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hokkaido

Hokkaido is an outdoor lover’s paradise, and the island has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some recommendations:

  • Asahidake Onsen Hot Springs
  • Noboribetsu Onsen Hot Springs
  • Mt. Yotei

Best Things to Do in Hokkaido: Activities and Tours

Hokkaido offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to take part in. There’s something for everyone, from skiing and snowboarding to hot air ballooning and whale watching. Here are some of the best things to do in Hokkaido:

  • Winter Festival in Sapporo
  • Otaru Canal
  • Shiraoi Ainu Museum

Week-Long Itinerary for Hokkaido

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Hokkaido:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Sapporo and explore the city
Day 2 Visit Lake Toya and Noboribetsu Onsen Hot Springs
Day 3 Explore Asahikawa and Asahiyama Memorial Park
Day 4 Discover Shiretoko National Park
Day 5 Visit Furano and Biei for picturesque views
Day 6 Explore Otaru and the famous Otaru Canal
Day 7 Head back to Sapporo and explore the city

Food and Drink Scene of Hokkaido

Must-Try Dishes

Hokkaido is famous for its fresh seafood and dairy products. Some must-try dishes while in Hokkaido include:

  • Jingisukan (Genghis Khan)
  • Sapporo Ramen
  • Zangi (Fried Chicken)

Local Beverages

Hokkaido is home to several breweries and wineries that produce delicious local beverages. Here are some must-try drinks in Hokkaido:

  • Sapporo Beer
  • Shikaribetsu Lake Blue Water
  • Yubari Melon Liquor

Where to Stay in Hokkaido: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hokkaido offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some suggestions:

  • Sapporo Prince Hotel
  • Nachi no Taki Lodge
  • Sapporo International Youth Hostel

What to Buy in Hokkaido: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hokkaido is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Some popular items to take home from Hokkaido include:

  • Hokkaido Milk Cookies
  • Shiroi Koibito Chocolate
  • Otaru Glassware

Local Culture and Customs of Hokkaido

Hokkaido has its own unique culture and customs that are different from the rest of Japan. Some things to keep in mind while visiting Hokkaido include:

  • Respect the Ainu culture and traditions
  • Take your shoes off when entering a traditional Japanese inn or home
  • Be punctual and on time for appointments and meetings

Best Time to Visit Hokkaido

The best time to visit Hokkaido is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The winter season from December to February is also a great time to visit Hokkaido if you enjoy winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hokkaido

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Learn about the Ainu culture
  • Practice proper etiquette when visiting religious sites

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking in public
  • Show up late for appointments or meetings
  • Disrespect the Ainu culture and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Hokkaido

Hokkaido is generally considered a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of natural hazards, such as snow and ice, which can be present during the winter months. Make sure to dress appropriately and be cautious while participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Hokkaido

Hokkaido has excellent medical facilities, and visitors should have no difficulty finding medical attention if necessary. However, it’s recommended that travelers have health insurance that covers them for any medical emergencies.

Getting to Hokkaido: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hokkaido

Hokkaido has two main airports: New Chitose Airport and Asahikawa Airport. Both have direct flights to major cities in Asia, including Seoul, Shanghai, Taipei, and Bangkok.

Bus Stations Near Hokkaido

Hokkaido has an extensive bus network that connects cities and towns on the island. Major bus stations in Hokkaido include Sapporo Bus Terminal and Asahikawa Station North Exit.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Hokkaido is by ferry from cities such as Niigata or Otaru.

How to Get Around in Hokkaido

Hokkaido has an excellent transportation system, with trains, buses, and taxis readily available. However, the best way to get around and explore the island is by renting a car or a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Hokkaido: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have a few extra days in Japan, there are several exciting destinations to explore from Hokkaido, including Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. One great excursion to consider is a trip to the nearby island of Rishiri, known for its stunning scenery and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, a foodie, or a culture enthusiast, Hokkaido has something to offer everyone. This comprehensive guide should provide you with all the necessary information to plan a successful trip to this beautiful and unique destination.
