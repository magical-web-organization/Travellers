Introduction to Rebun Island

Rebun Island is a hidden gem located in the northern part of Japan’s Hokkaido island. It is known for its unique flora, rugged coastline, and unspoiled natural beauty. Despite being relatively isolated, the island attracts thousands of visitors each year who come to hike, bike, or simply relax in its tranquil atmosphere. In this ultimate travel guide to Rebun Island, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Rebun Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Rebun Island is primarily known for its natural wonders, it also has a rich cultural history worth exploring. Some of the top cultural attractions on the island include:

  • Rebun Visitor Center: This museum provides an excellent introduction to the island’s unique flora and fauna. Interactive exhibits and audiovisual displays make it a great stop for families and nature enthusiasts.

  • Naganuma Arctic Museum: This museum showcases the life and culture of the indigenous Ainu people, who have lived on Hokkaido for thousands of years. Exhibits include traditional costumes, artifacts, and tools, as well as Ainu folklore and customs.

Natural Attractions of Rebun Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rebun Island is home to some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in Japan. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions on the island:

  • Mount Rebun: This mountain is the highest point on the island and offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape. Hiking to the top takes about two hours, but the scenery is worth the effort.

  • Hiking Trails: With over 300 kilometers of hiking trails, Rebun Island is a paradise for nature lovers. The most popular trail is the Rebun-dake Course, which takes you through fields of alpine flowers and past scenic overlooks.

  • Cape Sukoton: This rugged cape on the northern tip of the island is known for its dramatic rock formations and sweeping sea views. It’s a popular spot for watching the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rebun Island

Rebun Island is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, thanks to its extensive network of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Rebun-dake Course: This 9.2-kilometer hiking trail takes you to the summit of Mount Rebun, the island’s highest point. Along the way, you’ll pass through fields of alpine flowers and be rewarded with stunning views of the Sea of Japan.

  • Hama-Ike Course: This 6.5-kilometer hiking trail takes you through the forest to the scenic Hama-Ike pond. The trail is relatively easy, making it a great option for families with children.

  • Cycling Course: If you prefer cycling to hiking, Rebun Island has a 21-kilometer cycling course that takes you through picturesque villages and past stunning sea views.

Best Things to Do in Rebun Island: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy on Rebun Island. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the island’s rugged coastline by sea kayak. Guided tours are available for all skill levels.

  • Bird Watching: Rebun Island is home to over 200 species of birds, making it a paradise for bird watchers.

  • Flower Viewing: In the spring and summer, the island comes alive with fields of colorful alpine flowers. Take a stroll through the meadows and marvel at the natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rebun Island

If you’re planning to spend a week on Rebun Island, here’s a sample itinerary to get you started:

Day Activity
1 Arrive on Rebun Island and settle into your accommodations. Take a stroll around the town of Kafuka and visit the Rebun Visitor Center.
2 Hike the Rebun-dake Course and enjoy the alpine scenery.
3 Take a sea kayaking tour around the island’s coastline.
4 Rent a bike and explore the island’s quaint villages and scenic views.
5 Visit the Naganuma Arctic Museum to learn more about the Ainu culture.
6 Take a guided bird watching tour and spot some of the island’s rare bird species.

Food and Drink Scene of Rebun Island

Must-Try Dishes

Rebun Island is known for its fresh seafood and local produce. Here are some of the must-try dishes on the island:

  • Kombu:* This type of seaweed is a staple in the local cuisine and is used in everything from miso soup to sushi.

  • Uni:* Sea urchin is a delicacy in Japan and is particularly popular on Rebun Island. It can be enjoyed raw or cooked in a variety of dishes.

  • Hokke:* This local fish is popular for its firm texture and mild flavor. It’s often grilled or served as sashimi.

Local Beverages

Rebun Island is also home to some unique local beverages. Here are some of the best:

  • Sake: The island is home to several small sake breweries, where you can sample the locally made rice wine.

  • Haskap Berry Juice: Haskap berries are a type of tart berry that grows well in the island’s cold climate. The juice is a refreshing treat on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Rebun Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rebun Island has a range of accommodations to suit any budget or travel style. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hana Rebun: This traditional Japanese inn is located in the town of Kafuka and offers comfortable rooms with tatami mats and futon bedding. Guests can enjoy a hot spring bath and traditional kaiseki meals.

  • Rebunso: This family-run hotel is located in the town of Funadomari and offers cozy rooms with sea views. Guests can enjoy bicycle rentals and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

  • Yuyado Sakamoto: This ryokan-style inn is located in the town of Kafuka and offers Japanese-style rooms with traditional futon bedding. Guests can enjoy hot spring baths and a restaurant serving seasonal dishes.

What to Buy in Rebun Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Rebun Island, consider these local products:

  • Kombu: You can find packets of locally harvested kombu at most gift shops and markets on the island.

  • Haskap Berry Jam: This tart fruit makes great jam and is perfect for spreading on toast or biscuits.

Local Culture and Customs of Rebun Island

Rebun Island has a unique culture and history that is worth exploring during your visit. Some key customs and traditions include:

  • Ainu Culture: The indigenous Ainu people have lived on Hokkaido and its surrounding islands for thousands of years. Their distinct language, culture, and traditions form an important part of the island’s heritage.

  • Seafood Cuisine: Rebun Island is known for its fresh seafood, which is a staple in the local cuisine. Some of the most popular dishes include grilled fish, sea urchin, and crab.

Best Time to Visit Rebun Island

The best time to visit Rebun Island is in the late spring or summer, when the island’s fields of alpine flowers are in full bloom. The peak bloom period is from late June to early July. However, fall is also a beautiful time to visit, when the leaves turn golden and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rebun Island

Do:

  • Respect the Environment: Rebun Island is a pristine wilderness that needs to be preserved for future generations. Please do your part to keep the island clean and free of litter.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Rebun Island is known for its fresh seafood and unique local cuisine. Don’t be afraid to try something new!

Don’t:

  • Disturb the Wildlife: Rebun Island is home to many rare and endangered species of plants and animals. Please don’t disturb their habitats or feed the animals.

  • Ignore Local Customs: Rebun Island has a rich cultural heritage that should be respected by visitors. Learn about the local customs and traditions before you arrive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rebun Island

Rebun Island is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Rebun Island

Rebun Island is home to a small hospital and a few clinics. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, as the island may not have all medications available.

Getting to Rebun Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Rebun Island can be a bit of a challenge, but the journey is well worth it. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Rebun Island

The closest airport to Rebun Island is Wakkanai Airport, which has direct flights to Tokyo, Osaka, and Sapporo.

Bus Stations Near Rebun Island

From Wakkanai, visitors can take a bus to Kafuka Port, where they can catch a ferry to Rebun Island.

Other Options

If you’re traveling by car, you can take the Hokkaido Expressway to Wakkanai and then follow the signs to the ferry terminal. There is also a train that runs from Sapporo to Wakkanai.

How to Get Around in Rebun Island

The best way to get around Rebun Island is by bus or by bike. There is a local bus service that runs throughout the island, and bike rentals are available in most towns.

Where to Go Next from Rebun Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to continue your travels after Rebun Island, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Rishiri Island: This neighboring island is known for its rugged coastline and alpine scenery.

  • Wakkanai: This northern city is the gateway to Rebun Island and has a rich culture and history worth exploring.

Conclusion

Rebun Island is a hidden gem in Japan’s Hokkaido region, offering stunning natural beauty and unique cultural experiences. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply relax on the beach, Rebun Island has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of this unspoiled destination!
