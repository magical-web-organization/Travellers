Introduction to Sounkyo Onsen

Sounkyo Onsen is a hot spring resort located in the Northern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Sounkyo Onsen is nestled between the Daisetsuzan National Park and the Ishikari River. The region is home to stunning waterfalls, canyon views, and hiking trails. Tourists travel from all over the world to soak in the hot springs and take in the breathtaking views.

List of Places to Visit in Sounkyo Onsen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

A must-visit site in Sounkyo Onsen is the Kurodake Ropeway, the highest gondola lift in Hokkaido. The ropeway provides stunning views and access to several hiking trails. Another popular destination is the Daisetsu Mori-no Garden, a beautifully landscaped Japanese garden surrounded by the natural beauty of the Daisetsuzan National Park.

Natural Attractions of Sounkyo Onsen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sounkyo Gorge offers jaw-dropping scenery, waterfalls, and hiking trails for all levels of hikers. Ginsendai is a great spot for nature lovers, offering access to hiking trails and stunning lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sounkyo Onsen

The Daisetsuzan National Park offers an array of hiking routes, ranging from beginner to experienced level hikers. Hikers can climb Mount Kurodake and Mount Asahidake, the highest peaks in Hokkaido. Mountain biking enthusiasts can explore the rugged terrain of Mount Meakan.

Best Things to Do in Sounkyo Onsen: Activities and Tours

For a unique experience, take a tour of the Otokoyama Sake Brewery or Kita no Daichi Winery. For a relaxing outing, soak in the hot springs of Sounkyo Onsen’s hotels and resorts, or pamper yourself with a spa treatment. During the winter, visitors can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, or snowshoeing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sounkyo Onsen

Day 1-2: Explore the natural beauty of Sounkyo Gorge, hike the trails, and admire the waterfalls.

Day 3-4: Go sightseeing at the Daisetsu Mori-no Garden and enjoy a sake tasting at the Otokoyama Sake Brewery.

Day 5-6: Soak in the hot springs and pamper yourself with a massage or spa treatment. Enjoy winter activities like skiing, snowboarding or snowshoeing during the winter season.

Day 7: Visit the Ginsendai Forest Park for a scenic hike, followed by a wine tasting at the Kita no Daichi Winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Sounkyo Onsen

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Sounkyo Onsen include the Genghis Khan Mongolian BBQ, a specialty in Hokkaido made with lamb or pork, and Shika-meshi, a Japanese-style rice dish made with venison.

Local Beverages

Sake and wine tastings are popular activities in Sounkyo Onsen. Be sure to try the local Otokoyama Sake and Kita no Daichi Winery’s wines during your trip.

Where to Stay in Sounkyo Onsen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sounkyo Onsen has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. From luxury hotels to traditional ryokan, visitors have many options to choose from. Some popular accommodation options include Sounkaku Grand Hotel, Choyo Resort Hotel, and Hotel Taisetsu.

What to Buy in Sounkyo Onsen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase souvenirs such as Hokkaido themed apparel, local snacks, or Onsen-related merchandise. Hokkaido milk caramel and Shiroi Koibito chocolate are popular souvenirs among tourists.

Local Culture and Customs of Sounkyo Onsen

In Sounkyo Onsen, it is customary to remove your shoes when entering an Onsen or Ryokan. Visitors should also note that tattoos are often associated with the Yakuza, a Japanese criminal organization, and may not be allowed in some Onsen or public places.

Best Time to Visit Sounkyo Onsen

The best time to visit Sounkyo Onsen is during the winter season, from December to March for winter sports and the hot springs. During the summer months, from June to August, visitors can enjoy hiking and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sounkyo Onsen

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Adhere to Onsen etiquette
  • Respect local customs and culture

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes in Onsen or Ryokan
  • Have tattoos visible in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Sounkyo Onsen

Sounkyo Onsen is generally a safe area. Visitors should be cautious during the winter season as heavy snowfall and cold temperatures can make it difficult to travel.

Health in Sounkyo Onsen

The quality of the Onsen water is monitored and tested daily to ensure safety for visitors. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent altitude sickness when hiking in the mountains.

Getting to Sounkyo Onsen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sounkyo Onsen

The nearest airport to Sounkyo Onsen is Asahikawa Airport, which is approximately 70 km away. New Chitose Airport, located approximately 200 km away, is another option.

Bus Stations Near Sounkyo Onsen

A direct express bus service runs from Sapporo to Sounkyo Onsen. Visitors can also take a local bus from Asahikawa or Kamikawa to reach the Onsen.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to explore Hokkaido, and several car rental companies are available at the Asahikawa Airport.

How to Get Around in Sounkyo Onsen

Visitors can explore Sounkyo Onsen on foot, bicycle or car. Getting around by car is the most convenient option for accessing nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Sounkyo Onsen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Asahikawa is a popular destination near Sounkyo Onsen, known for its zoo and attractions. Other nearby destinations include the Furano and Biei region, which features stunning landscapes and nature trails.

Conclusion

Sounkyo Onsen is a must-visit destination in Hokkaido, Japan. Its stunning natural beauty and rich culture make it a unique experience for travelers of all ages. Whether you’re looking to soak in the hot springs, hike the trails in the mountains, or explore local culture and customs, Sounkyo Onsen has something for everyone to enjoy.
