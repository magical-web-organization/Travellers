Introduction to Wakkanai

Located in the Northern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan, Wakkanai is the northernmost city of Japan and offers visitors an unforgettable travel experience. The city is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, cultural and historical landmarks, and local cuisine. Whether you enjoy hiking, biking, skiing or just want to relax in a serene environment, Wakkanai is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Wakkanai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wakkanai City Museum - exhibits on the history and culture of Wakkanai and the region

  2. Michi-no-Eki Cape Noshappu - a roadside station that features a historical museum, local products, and a spectacular view of the sea

  3. Wakkanai Park - home to Japan’s northernmost lighthouse, a World War II monument, and a museum

  4. Sarobetsu Wetland Center - a nature center that showcases the rich flora and fauna of the Sarobetsu wetlands

Natural Attractions of Wakkanai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cape Soya - the northernmost tip of Japan, offers a panoramic view of the sea and a monument that marks the endpoint of Japan’s main islands

  2. Rishiri-Rebun-Sarobetsu National Park - a stunning natural park that is home to a diverse range of wildlife, such as brown bears and Japanese cranes, and offers hiking and skiing opportunities

  3. Monument of Sakhalin - a memorial of the forced migration of Sakhalin Koreans and other ethnic groups after World War II

  4. Cape Nosappu - offers a spectacular view of the sunset over the sea and the lighthouse of the cape

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wakkanai

  1. Cape Soya to Wakkanai Park - a 7-km route that offers a stunning panoramic view of the sea and the park’s museum

  2. Rishiri Island Trail - a 20-km trail that offers a scenic view of Mount Rishiri

  3. Sarobetsu Plain Trail - a 5-km route that offers a gentle hike through the Sarobetsu wetlands

Best Things to Do in Wakkanai: Activities and Tours

  1. Whale-watching Tour - experience the thrill of watching whales in their natural habitat in the sea around Wakkanai

  2. Skiing in Rishiri Island - enjoy skiing on the slopes of Mount Rishiri in winter months

  3. Wakkanai Guided Tour - explore the city’s landmarks and museums with the help of English-speaking guides

Week-Long Itinerary for Wakkanai

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Wakkanai; visit Wakkanai City Museum and eat at a local restaurant
Day 2 Hike from Cape Soya to Wakkanai Park; visit the park’s museum
Day 3 Take a whale-watching tour in the morning; visit Michi-no-Eki Cape Noshappu during the afternoon
Day 4 Visit the Sarobetsu Wetland Center to learn about the region’s nature and wildlife
Day 5 Take a trip to Rishiri Island to enjoy skiing or hiking
Day 6 Visit Cape Nosappu to see the sunset, then take a guided tour of Wakkanai city
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and local products before leaving Wakkanai

Food and Drink Scene of Wakkanai

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Seafood: try the local seafood, such as crab, sea urchin, and salmon

  2. Memuro Pork: a local breed of pork that is specially raised in the open fields of Memuro

  3. Seaweed: try the local seaweed, such as wakame, konbu, and nori

Local Beverages

  1. Sake: try the local sake, such as Menkoina, Takasago, and Koikawa

  2. Shochu: a Japanese distilled beverage that comes in various flavors, such as sweet potato, barley, and rice

  3. Wine: try the local wine made from grapes grown in Wakkanai’s cold climate.

Where to Stay in Wakkanai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Rishiri Greenhill Hotel - located in Rishiri Island, this hotel offers great views of Mount Rishiri and has spacious rooms with modern amenities

  2. Hotel Okabe Shiosaitei - located in Wakkanai, this hotel offers simple yet comfortable rooms and a traditional hot spring bath

  3. Guest House Kusanagi - a budget-friendly option for travelers, this guesthouse provides clean and cozy dorm rooms with shared facilities

What to Buy in Wakkanai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Seafood and Seaweed Products: bring home some fresh and dried seafood and seaweed products

  2. Sake and Shochu: buy some locally brewed sake and shochu for yourself or as a gift for others

  3. Art and Crafts: take home some locally made art and crafts, such as pottery, weaving, and woodcarving

Local Culture and Customs of Wakkanai

Wakkanai is home to the Ainu, an indigenous people of Japan, who have a rich culture and heritage. Visitors can learn about the Ainu culture and customs at the Ainu Museum in Shiraoi, a 2-hour drive from Wakkanai. The Ainu people believe in living in harmony with nature and respect the environment.

Best Time to Visit Wakkanai

The best time to visit Wakkanai is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities. However, winter months can also be a great time to visit as the city is covered in snow, making it a winter wonderland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wakkanai

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions, such as bowing and taking off shoes before entering houses and temples

  2. Try local cuisine and products, such as seafood, sake, and shochu

  3. Dress in layers in winter months as the temperature can drop to -20°C

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or smoke in public places

  2. Don’t touch or disturb wildlife, such as brown bears, on hiking trails

  3. Don’t be loud or disrespectful in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Wakkanai

Wakkanai is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be cautious when hiking or skiing in mountainous regions, and follow safety guidelines such as wearing appropriate clothing and carrying emergency supplies.

Health in Wakkanai

Visitors to Wakkanai should take precautions against the cold weather in winter months by wearing warm clothing and protecting their skin and eyes from frostbite and UV radiation. The city has modern medical facilities in case of emergencies.

Getting to Wakkanai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wakkanai

The nearest airport to Wakkanai is Wakkanai Airport, which operates domestic flights to and from major Japanese cities, such as Tokyo, Sapporo, and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Wakkanai

Wakkanai Bus Terminal provides bus services to major cities and towns in Hokkaido, such as Sapporo and Asahikawa.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the ferry from Wakkanai Port to Rishiri and Rebun islands, or drive on the scenic Hokkaido Expressway to reach Wakkanai.

How to Get Around in Wakkanai

Travelers can get around Wakkanai by taking local buses or rental cars. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Wakkanai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Rishiri Island: a picturesque island known for its beautiful scenery and skiing/hiking opportunities

  2. Rebun Island: an island famous for its alpine flowers and scenic hiking trails

  3. Shiraoi: a town known for its Ainu museum and cultural center

Conclusion

Wakkanai is a city of contrasts, with its natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and delicious cuisine. Visitors to this northernmost city in Japan can experience the unique culture and traditions of the Ainu people, enjoy outdoor adventures, or just relax in a serene environment. Whether you are a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, Wakkanai has something to offer for everyone. Plan your next adventure to Wakkanai with our comprehensive travel guide!
