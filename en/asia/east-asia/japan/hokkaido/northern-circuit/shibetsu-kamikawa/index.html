Introduction to Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Shibetsu (Kamikawa) is a hidden gem located in the Northern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan. With a population of only around 10,000 people, this charming town boasts abundant natural beauty, rich culture and heritage, as well as plenty of outdoor activities and attractions. The town is accessible through public transportation, making it a perfect location for a day trip or a peaceful retreat from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Higashimokoto Shibazakura Park - A stunning flower park featuring over 100,000 shibazakura (pink moss phlox) flowers blooming from late April to early June.
  • Nukibetsu Onsen Village - A small hotspring village in the countryside, where you can relax and recharge surrounded by nature.
  • Kamikawa Michi no Eki - A roadside station featuring local produce, crafts, and restaurants.
  • Shibetsu Central Market - A bustling indoor market offering seafood, produce, and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Shibetsu (Kamikawa): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nukibetsu Nature Park - A natural park featuring a hiking trail through dense forests, waterfalls, and scenic views.
  • Higashimokoto Nyuraku-en - A peaceful Japanese garden that displays various flowers throughout the year.
  • Lake Kussharo - Hokkaido’s largest caldera lake, offering stunning panoramic views and activities such as boating and fishing.
  • Mt. Meakan - An active volcano located in Akan National Park, offering hiking trails and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

  • Nukibetsu Nature Park Trail - A 4.5km hiking trail through dense forests, waterfalls, and scenic views.
  • Shibetsu to Nukibetsu Cycling Route - A 40km cycling route through scenic countryside roads.

Best Things to Do in Shibetsu (Kamikawa): Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding - Horseback riding lessons and excursions are available at various stables around Shibetsu.
  • Fishing - Enjoy fishing for salmon, trouts and other species in the Teshio River or local ponds.
  • Clay Shooting - Try your marksmanship skills at a clay shooting range in Shibetsu.
  • Snow Sports - Downhill skiing and snowboarding can be enjoyed at Kamui Ski Links, approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes drive from Shibetsu.

Food and Drink Scene of Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kamikawa Beef - A local specialty beef from Shibetsu, known for its tender meat and rich flavor.
  • Hokke (Arctic Char) - A popular local fish known for its sweet taste and soft texture.
  • Ikameshi (Squid Rice) - Squid stuffed with rice and other ingredients, a beloved local dish.

Local Beverages

  • Sake - Hokkaido has a thriving sake industry, and Shibetsu is no exception, producing unique local sake flavors.
  • Wine - The cool climate in Hokkaido is perfect for grape growing and wine production with various vineyards around the area.

Where to Stay in Shibetsu (Kamikawa): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Yogai - A luxurious hotel with onsens and restaurants located in the center of Shibetsu
  • Nukibetsu Onsen Village Yumotokan - A quaint ryokan and onsen with private hot spring baths located in Nukibetsu village.
  • Mashu Lake Youth Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel located within Akan National Park.
  • Guesthouse Matabee - A cozy guest house located in the countryside, perfect for retreats.

What to Buy in Shibetsu (Kamikawa): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Higashimokoto Shibazakura Artisanal Goods - Locally made products inspired by the pink moss phlox at Higashimokoto Shibazakura Park such as crafts, fabrics and sweets.
  • Shibetsu Sake - Shibetsu produces distinctive variations of sake that make great souvenirs.
  • Hokke Fish Products - Hokke is not only delicious, but it is also processed into many products such as canned food, chowder, and jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Shibetsu is known for its traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Higashimokoto Shibazakura Festival in May, and the Nukibetsu Onsen Summer Festival in August. The town also has strong Ainu cultural influences, and visitors can learn more about these indigenous people at the Ainu Museum in nearby Shiraoi.

Best Time to Visit Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

The best time to visit Shibetsu is during the spring and summer months (late May to early October) when the weather is mild and outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and manners, including removing shoes when entering houses, shops and restaurants.
  • Try to speak a few Japanese words, this will be appreciated by the locals.
  • Be mindful of the rules and instructions when enjoying outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or smoke in public places.
  • Be offensive to local customs or beliefs.
  • Ignore the rules or guidelines when participating in outdoor activities or visiting museums and attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Shibetsu is a relatively safe place to visit, but be cautious of wildlife and bears when hiking in secluded areas. Visitors should also be mindful of traffic when cycling or driving around town.

Health in Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

While no specific vaccination or precaution is required to visit Shibetsu, visitors are advised to take extra care of hygiene, especially during the Covid-19 pandemic.

Getting to Shibetsu (Kamikawa): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

The closest airport to Shibetsu is Asahikawa Airport, which is serviced by domestic flights from Narita, Haneda, and other major airports in Japan. From Asahikawa Airport, visitors can take a bus or a train to Shibetsu.

Bus Stations Near Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Buses from Asahikawa and other nearby cities arrive at Shibetsu Bus Terminal, which is located near the center of town.

Other Options

Driving and cycling are also viable options to explore Shibetsu, particularly for those who wish to enjoy the scenic countryside roads.

How to Get Around in Shibetsu (Kamikawa)

Visitors can explore Shibetsu on foot, particularly in the central area. Buses and taxis are available for longer journeys, while cycling and driving are also popular options for exploring the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Shibetsu (Kamikawa): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shiraoi Ainu Museum - A museum dedicated to the Ainu people and culture, located approximately 40 minutes from Shibetsu.
  • Sounkyo Gorge - A scenic valley and hot spring resort town located approximately 2.5 hours from Shibetsu.
  • Asahikawa - The second-largest city in Hokkaido, known for its zoo, ramen, and winter festival, located approximately 1.5 hours from Shibetsu.

Conclusion

Shibetsu (Kamikawa) is a beautiful and unique destination that offers visitors an abundance of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a quiet escape or an adventure-packed holiday, Shibetsu has plenty to offer. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their time in this wonderful town and explore nearby destinations as well.
