Introduction to Shimukappu

Located in the Northern Circuit of Hokkaido, Shimukappu is a picturesque town that boasts of natural beauty, rich history and culture, and a wide range of exciting activities that caters to the adventurous and the laid-back alike. Known for its scenic views, hot springs, and famous puma sightings, this region is a nature lover’s paradise. Shimukappu is also famous for being home to the Ainu, an indigenous population of Hokkaido, who have preserved their culture and traditions for thousands of years.

List of Places to Visit in Shimukappu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shimukappu offers a range of museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s past and present. Here are some of the most interesting places to visit:

  • The Ainu Museum: The Ainu Museum offers visitors an insight into the culture and history of the Ainu people, with exhibits displaying traditional clothing, tools, and religious artifacts. Visitors can also participate in Ainu crafts and cultural events.
  • Ginga no Yu: A hot spring with a spectacular view of the Milky Way, Ginga no Yu is a must-visit spot for travelers looking to relax and unwind.
  • Hokkaido Historical Village: A living history museum that showcases life in different periods of Hokkaido’s history, Hokkaido Historical Village is a fascinating place to visit for anyone interested in Japanese history.

Natural Attractions of Shimukappu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shimukappu is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Daisetsuzan National Park: Known for its mountainous landscape and wide array of flora and fauna, Daisetsuzan National Park offers visitors a chance to hike and explore some of the best natural scenery in the region.
  • Sukai no Yu: Another hot spring with spectacular views, Sukai no Yu is known for its unique sulfuric mineral content, which is said to be great for the skin.
  • Lake Shikotsu: One of Japan’s deepest and clearest caldera lakes, Lake Shikotsu offers visitors a variety of water activities, such as kayaking and fishing. There are also hiking trails around the lake that offer scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shimukappu

Shimukappu is the perfect place for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Shimukappu to Kurodake Trail: This 15-kilometer trail offers hikers stunning views of Mount Kurodake, one of the highest peaks in Daisetsuzan National Park.
  • Kanchu-tei Trail: This easy 5-kilometer trail takes hikers through a beautiful forest of birch trees, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Bikenba-uri Trail: A popular mountain biking trail, Bikenba-uri offers stunning scenic views and a challenging ride through the mountains and forests of Shimukappu.

Best Things to Do in Shimukappu: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Shimukappu offers a variety of other exciting activities and tours that will leave visitors with unforgettable experiences of their trip. Here are some recommendations:

  • Ainu Cultural Experience Tour: Experience the rich history and culture of the Ainu people through this guided tour, which includes a visit to the Ainu village, traditional dancing and crafts, and a delicious meal of traditional Ainu cuisine.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Shimukappu is known for its fantastic powder snow, making it a great destination for skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts.
  • Guided Nature Walks: Take a guided nature walk through the forests and mountains of Shimukappu to learn about the flora and fauna of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shimukappu

Shimukappu offers so much to see and do that visitors can easily spend several days exploring the region. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a one-week trip:

Day 7 Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Shimukappu

In addition to its natural beauty and cultural attractions, Shimukappu is also known for its gastronomic delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Katsuo Tataki: Freshly seared bonito fish served with soy sauce and ginger.
  • Jingisukan: Grilled lamb served with vegetables.
  • Cream Puffs: A local dessert made with fresh Hokkaido cream.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hokkaido is known for producing high-quality sake, which is made from locally grown rice.
  • Shochu: A distilled alcohol made from rice, barley, or sweet potatoes, shochu is a popular drink in Hokkaido.

Where to Stay in Shimukappu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shimukappu offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Hoshino Resorts RISONARE Tomamu: A luxurious resort with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Pension CHINO: A cozy guesthouse with clean and affordable rooms.
  • Zenkoji Temple Stay: Experience the serene and peaceful lifestyle of a Buddhist monk at the Zenkoji Temple Stay.

What to Buy in Shimukappu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shimukappu is known for producing high-quality local products that make great souvenirs for visitors. Here are some recommended items to bring home with you:

  • Ainu Crafts: Unique and traditional Ainu crafts such as carvings, baskets, and embroidery make great souvenirs.
  • Hokkaido Sake: High-quality sake brewed locally in Hokkaido.
  • Shimukappu Onsen Bath Salts: Take a little piece of Shimukappu home with you by purchasing some of the region’s famous hot spring bath salts.

Local Culture and Customs of Shimukappu

Shimukappu is home to the Ainu people, an indigenous population of Hokkaido that has lived in the region for thousands of years. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions of the Ainu, such as not touching the artifacts in the Ainu Museum, and dressing modestly when visiting sacred sites.

Best Time to Visit Shimukappu

The best time to visit Shimukappu is during the summer (June-August), when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. The fall (September-November) is also a beautiful time to visit, with stunning fall foliage adding to the scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shimukappu

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the Ainu people.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting sacred sites.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Touch or damage the exhibits in the Ainu Museum.
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shimukappu

Shimukappu is a relatively safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take common safety precautions, such as locking their valuables in a safe and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Shimukappu

Shimukappu has modern medical facilities and pharmacies, so visitors should not have any trouble accessing medical care if needed. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and skiing, and should take appropriate safety measures such as wearing appropriate footwear and clothing, and carrying an emergency communication device.

Getting to Shimukappu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shimukappu is located in a relatively remote part of Hokkaido, but there are several transportation options for visitors. Here are some recommended ways to get to Shimukappu:

Airports Near Shimukappu

The closest major airports to Shimukappu are:

  • New Chitose Airport - approximately 100km away

Bus Stations Near Shimukappu

There are several bus stations near Shimukappu that offer daily bus services to and from major cities in Hokkaido:

  • Obihiro Bus Terminal
  • Sapporo Terminal

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Shimukappu, or take a train to JR Shimukappu Station, which is located a few kilometers away from the town.

How to Get Around in Shimukappu

The best way to get around Shimukappu is by car. However, visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and trains, or rent bicycles to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Shimukappu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shimukappu is located in a prime location for exploring other parts of Hokkaido. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Furano: A beautiful town known for its lavender fields and delicious wine.
  • Asahikawa: A vibrant city with a zoo, museums, and a delicious food scene.
  • Sapporo: The capital city of Hokkaido, Sapporo offers a wide range of cultural attractions, shopping and dining options, and exciting nightlife.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture and history, and exciting activities and attractions, Shimukappu is a must-visit destination in Hokkaido. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind, or seeking adventure and outdoor activities, Shimukappu has something for everyone.
