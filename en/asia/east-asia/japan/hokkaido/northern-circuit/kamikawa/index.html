Introduction to Kamikawa

Kamikawa is a mountainous region located in the Northern Circuit of the Hokkaido island of Japan. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, including rugged mountains, deep valleys, and crystal-clear rivers. Kamikawa is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, who come to hike, bike, fish, and ski in the region. Kamikawa’s heritage is also rich in history and culture, with a range of fascinating museums, temples, shrines, and historical sites to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Kamikawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Asahikawa Museum of Art

Asahikawa Museum of Art is a modern art museum in Asahikawa City, Hokkaido, Japan. The museum’s unique architecture is shaped like a conch shell and its exhibition spaces focus on contemporary art and design.

Kamikawa Shrine

Kamikawa Shrine is a Shinto shrine located in the town of Kamikawa. This shrine is said to have been established over 1,400 years ago, and it is a must-visit spot to experience traditional Japanese culture.

Ginzan Yuhodo (Silver Mine Museum)

Ginzan Yuhodo is a museum that tells the story of the silver mines that once operated in Kamikawa. Here, visitors can learn about the lives of the miners who worked here, and see examples of the silver that was mined.

Natural Attractions of Kamikawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Daisetsuzan National Park

Daisetsuzan National Park is the largest national park in Japan and is located in Kamikawa. Here, visitors can hike and explore the rugged mountain terrain, forests, and pristine lakes.

Sounkyo Gorge

Sounkyo Gorge is a spectacular natural gorge located in the town of Kamikawa. Visitors can enjoy scenic walks, hot springs, and sensational views of the mountains and waterfalls.

Kurodake Ropeway

Kurodake Ropeway is a cable car system that takes visitors to the top of Kurodake Mountain, the highest peak in Daisetsuzan. This is an excellent place to appreciate the scenery and admire the panoramic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kamikawa

Daisetsuzan Grand Traverse

Daisetsuzan Grand Traverse is a multi-day hiking trail that takes visitors across the Daisetsuzan mountain range. Along the way, hikers can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, including waterfalls, forests, and wildflowers.

Kamikawa Cycling Route

Kamikawa Cycling Route is a scenic bike trail that takes visitors through the picturesque countryside of Kamikawa. Cyclists can enjoy the fresh air and beautiful views of the countryside while experiencing the rich heritage of Japan.

Best Things to Do in Kamikawa: Activities and Tours

Visit a Hot Spring

Kamikawa is home to numerous hot springs, and visiting one is a must-do activity. Relax in the soothing warm waters while taking in the spectacular views of the mountains and valleys.

Snow Sports in Winter

During winter, Kamikawa becomes a winter wonderland, and snow sports enthusiasts can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports in the pristine snow-covered landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Kamikawa

Must-Try Dishes

Ramen

Kamikawa is well-known for its delicious ramen, with various styles and flavors to suit everyone’s taste buds.

Ishikari-nabe

Ishikari-nabe is a famous Hokkaido dish, consisting of salmon and vegetables cooked in a hot pot.

Local Beverages

Sapporo Beer

Sapporo Beer is the most famous beer brand in Japan and originates from Hokkaido. Enjoy a cold glass along with your meal.

Shochu

Shochu is a local spirit made from sweet potato, barley, or rice. It is a must-try for those who like stronger alcoholic drinks.

Where to Stay in Kamikawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Kamikawa will find a range of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

Asahidake Onsen Hotel Deer Valley

Asahidake Onsen Hotel Deer Valley is a fabulous ryokan-style hotel located in the heart of Kamikawa. This hotel is perfect for those seeking an authentic Japanese experience while enjoying amenities such as hot springs, a traditional Japanese-style pub, and delicious meals.

Kamikawa Pension Cedar

Kamikawa Pension Cedar is a comfortable and cozy guesthouse situated in a quiet area surrounded by nature. Rooms are spacious and equipped with all the necessary facilities, making your stay comfortable and convenient.

What to Buy in Kamikawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Kamikawa will find a wide range of local products and souvenirs to take home.

Sasa-Kamaboko

Sasa-Kamaboko is a fish paste made from fish and starch, wrapped in bamboo leaves, and grilled. It is a popular local delicacy and is considered a must-try for all visitors to Kamikawa.

Kuma Geta

Kuma Geta is a traditional wooden sandal made from high-quality wood found in Kamikawa. They are both durable and comfortable.

Local Culture and Customs of Kamikawa

Kamikawa has a rich cultural heritage, with unique customs and practices that visitors can experience.

Ainu Culture

The indigenous Ainu people of Hokkaido have a rich culture and history, and visitors to Kamikawa can learn about Ainu history and traditions through museums and cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Kamikawa

The best time to visit Kamikawa depends on the visitor’s interests.

Summer

Summer is the best time to enjoy hiking, mountain biking, and water sports in Kamikawa. The weather is pleasant, and the scenery is in full bloom.

Winter

Winter is the peak season for snow sports enthusiasts, who will find fantastic ski resorts and frozen landscapes to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kamikawa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes when entering houses or temples.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disrespectful in public.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Leave trash around in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kamikawa

Kamikawa is a safe destination. However, visitors should take regular precautions, such as locking doors and securing valuables.

Health in Kamikawa

Japan has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can have peace of mind knowing they are covered if needed.

Getting to Kamikawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kamikawa is best accessed via air or bus.

Airports Near Kamikawa

The nearest airport is Asahikawa Airport, which has regular domestic flights to and from Tokyo, Osaka, and other major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Kamikawa

Visitors can take a direct bus from Asahikawa City to Kamikawa.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Kamikawa or take a train, although this may require additional travel arrangements.

How to Get Around in Kamikawa

Visitors can rent bicycles, cars or hire a taxi to explore Kamikawa. The public transportation system in Kamikawa is well developed, with buses and local trains readily available.

Where to Go Next from Kamikawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Kamikawa can take day trips to a range of nearby destinations, including Sapporo, Otaru, and Furano.

Conclusion

Kamikawa offers a unique travel experience that is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan. Visitors can enjoy fascinating history and culture, stunning natural scenery, and a range of outdoor activities. With this guide, visitors can plan their trip to Kamikawa with confidence and enjoy their stay to the fullest.
