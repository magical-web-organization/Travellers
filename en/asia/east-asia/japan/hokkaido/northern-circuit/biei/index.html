Introduction to Biei

Nestled in the Northern Circuit of Hokkaido, Japan, Biei is a picturesque town famed for its breathtaking landscapes, endless fields of flowers, and rolling pastures that seem to stretch out forever. Biei is known for its natural wonders and outdoor adventures, as well as its world-famous food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Biei

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in Biei’s cultural history, there are a variety of museums and historical places throughout the town. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Biei Folk Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Biei through interactive exhibits and displays.

Natural Attractions of Biei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will rejoice in the endless outdoor adventure opportunities in Biei. Here are just a few natural attractions to explore:

  • Biei Blue River: Enjoy a peaceful walk along the river and take in the breathtaking scenery.
  • Shikisai Hill: Visit one of the most popular tourist sites in Biei and enjoy the vast array of flowers and plants.
  • Patchwork Road: Take a scenic drive or bike ride along this picturesque road through the Biei countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biei

For those looking to get out and explore the beauty of Biei on foot or by bike, there are a variety of options to choose from. Here are some popular routes:

  • Biei Nature Trail: Take a leisurely walk along this scenic trail that winds through the countryside.
  • Panoramic Cycling Road: Rent a bike and ride along this picturesque road, taking in the amazing views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Biei: Activities and Tours

When it comes to activities and tours, Biei has something for everyone.

Some popular options include:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the panoramic views of Biei’s beautiful landscapes from a hot air balloon.
  • Farm Stay: Experience the rural life in Biei by staying on a local farm and enjoying the fresh produce and local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biei

Here’s what a week in Biei could look like:

Day Plan
1 Arrive and check into your accommodation. Explore the town center and enjoy a local dinner.
2 Visit Biei Folk Museum and then spend the afternoon at Blue River.
3 Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning and then explore the Shikisai Hill in the afternoon.
4 Enjoy a farm stay and learn about the local agriculture.
5 Take a bike ride along the Panoramic Cycling Road.
6 Hike along the Biei Nature Trail and then relax in one of the town’s hot springs.
7 Visit the local markets and stores to buy souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Biei

Biei is known for its tasty local cuisine and delicious drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kamifurano pork: Sample the delicious, locally raised pork in various dishes.
  • Potato Croquette: Try this local favorite made with Hokkaido potatoes, often served at local bistros and eateries.

Local Beverages

  • Nikka Whiskey: This internationally acclaimed whiskey distillery is located just a few miles from Biei.
  • Sake: Try some locally brewed sake, made from Hokkaido’s fresh water and unique rice varieties.

Where to Stay in Biei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses, Biei has a variety of lodging options to suit any preference or budget. Some of the top choices include:

  • Pension Ken & Mary: Enjoy a cozy and welcoming stay at this guesthouse, with options for dorm rooms, private rooms, and family-style living.

What to Buy in Biei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenirs to bring back from your trip to Biei? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Lavender Products: Biei is famous for its lavender fields, and there are many shops in town that sell lavender products like soaps, oils, and sachets.

Local Culture and Customs of Biei

Biei is a welcoming town with a rich cultural history. Visitors should be mindful of local customs, such as removing shoes before entering homes and temples, and being respectful of the local religion and culture.

Best Time to Visit Biei

The best time to visit Biei is from June to September, when the lavender fields are in full bloom and the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biei

Do:

  • Take in the peaceful and picturesque surroundings by hiking or biking along one of the many trails.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks, such as Nikka Whiskey and Kamifurano pork.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs or religion.
  • Litter or damage local natural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biei

Visitors to Biei should exercise caution when hiking and exploring the natural surroundings. Be sure to bring proper equipment and clothing, and avoid trespassing on private property.

Health in Biei

Biei is a safe and healthy destination for visitors. However, travelers should be sure to bring any necessary medications or prescriptions with them, as there are limited medical facilities in town.

Getting to Biei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biei

The closest major airport to Biei is Asahikawa airport, located about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Biei

Biei bus station is located in the town center, with connections to other major cities in Hokkaido.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Biei, taking in the stunning scenery along the way.

How to Get Around in Biei

The best way to explore Biei is by bike, car, or foot. Many of the town’s natural attractions are within walking or biking distance, and there are several bike rental companies available in town.

Where to Go Next from Biei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and day trips to take from Biei, including:

  • Furano: Visit this nearby town and explore its many natural and cultural attractions.
  • Sapporo: Take a day trip to Hokkaido’s largest city and enjoy its vibrant culture and food scene.

Conclusion

Biei is truly a wonderful destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With its diverse array of natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and delicious food and drink offerings, there is something for everyone in this charming town.
