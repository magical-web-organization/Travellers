Introduction to Daito Islands

Daito Islands is a small archipelago located in Okinawa, Japan, consisting of three islands - Okidaitōjima, Kitadaitōjima, and Minamidaitōjima. It is a hidden gem that is still relatively unknown to most tourists, offering beautiful beaches, stunning natural wonders, and a vibrant local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Daito Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Daito Islands Museum of History and Culture

The Daito Islands Museum of History and Culture is a must-visit attraction that houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the local culture and history of the islands.

Okidaito Shiroyama Observatory

Okidaito Shiroyama Observatory is located on the highest point of Okidaitojima and offers breathtaking views of the island and its surroundings. The observation deck is accessible via a short hike and is a great spot to watch the sunset.

Natural Attractions of Daito Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whale Watching

Daito Islands is an excellent place to go whale watching, especially during the winter months when humpback whales migrate to the area. Several tour operators offer whale watching excursions, providing an opportunity to observe these majestic creatures up close.

Okidaito Beach

Okidaito Beach is a beautiful white sand beach located on the northeast coast of Okidaitojima. The crystal clear water and pristine environment make it a perfect spot for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Daito Islands

Minamidaito Island Hiking Trail

The Minamidaito Island Hiking Trail is a popular 4.4-kilometer hike that takes visitors through the lush forest and volcanic landscape of Minamidaitojima. The trail leads to a scenic viewpoint overlooking the ocean.

Kitadaito Island Biking Route

The Kitadaito Island Biking Route is a 14-kilometer bike ride through beautiful farmland and small villages. The flat terrain and quiet roads make it an ideal route for beginners and experienced cyclists alike.

Best Things to Do in Daito Islands: Activities and Tours

Island Hopping Tour

An island hopping tour is an excellent way to explore all three islands of Daito Islands. Several tour operators offer boat tours that allow visitors to visit multiple islands in one day, providing an opportunity to discover the local culture, beautiful beaches, and scenic spots.

Scuba Diving

Daito Islands is a scuba diving paradise, offering crystal clear water, beautiful coral reefs, exciting drop-offs, and abundant marine life. Several dive centers on Okidaitojima offer courses and guided dives for beginners and experienced divers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Daito Islands

Day 1: Explore Okidaito Island - visit Okidaito Shiroyama Observatory, Daito Islands Museum of History and Culture, and Okidaito Beach.

Day 2: Go Whale Watching - book a whale watching excursion to witness humpback whales migrating during the winter months.

Day 3: Discover Kitadaito Island - visit Kitadaito Island Biking Route and the local villages to experience the island’s charm.

Day 4: Enjoy Minamidaito Island - Hike the Minamidaito Island Hiking Trail, visit the local market, and enjoy a relaxing day on the beach.

Day 5-6: Try Scuba Diving - book a dive course or guided dive with a local dive center.

Day 7: Depart from Daito Islands

Food and Drink Scene of Daito Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Daito Island Soba

Daito Island Soba is a traditional noodle dish made from locally grown buckwheat flour, served cold with a dipping sauce or hot in a soup.

Sea Grapes

Sea grapes are a type of seaweed found in the waters around Daito Islands. The seaweed is harvested fresh and served as a popular local delicacy.

Local Beverages

Awamori

Awamori is a distilled alcoholic beverage, made from rice and unique to Okinawa. It is a must-try drink when visiting Daito Islands.

Where to Stay in Daito Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Daito Islands offer various accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some of the highly recommended properties include Hotel Daito Beach, Daito Pearl Sea Resort, and Cottage Daitosendo.

What to Buy in Daito Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Daito Island Salt

Daito Island salt is a natural sea salt harvested locally using traditional methods. It is a great souvenir or gift to bring home.

Ryukyu Glass

Ryukyu glass is a unique type of glassware that originated from Okinawa, featuring beautiful and intricate designs. There are several glassware shops in Daito Islands where visitors can buy authentic Ryukyu glass products.

Local Culture and Customs of Daito Islands

Daito Islands have a unique and vibrant culture, strongly influenced by the Ryukyu Kingdom and Japanese traditions. Visitors should respect local customs and etiquette, such as removing shoes before entering a private home or temple, and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Daito Islands

The best time to visit Daito Islands is from late December to early April, when the weather is dry, and the waters are crystal clear. The summer months from June to August can be hot and humid with occasional typhoons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Daito Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove any marine life while snorkeling or scuba diving
  • Enter restricted areas without permission
  • Be disrespectful to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Daito Islands

Daito Islands is generally safe for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions against sunburn, dehydration, and insect bites during outdoor activities.

Health in Daito Islands

There are medical facilities on Daito Islands, but visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should bring their necessary medication. Also, visitors should be aware of the risks of food and waterborne illnesses and take precautionary measures such as drinking bottled water and avoiding raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Daito Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Daito Islands are accessible via plane or ferry. Several airlines fly to Okinawa, and once in Okinawa, visitors can take a domestic flight or ferry to Daito Islands. Naha Airport in Okinawa is the nearest international airport to Daito Islands.

Airports Near Daito Islands

  • Naha Airport (OKA)

Bus Stations Near Daito Islands

  • None

Other Options

  • Ferry: Daito Islands can be reached by ferry from Itoman Port in Okinawa.

How to Get Around in Daito Islands

The best way to get around the islands is by rental car or motorbike. Visitors can rent a car at Okinawa Airport or in Naha and take it to the islands via ferry.

Where to Go Next from Daito Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby islands of Miyako and Yaeyama after visiting Daito Islands. Miyako islands are known for their beautiful beaches and coral reefs, while Yaeyama islands offer a more laid-back island experience.

Conclusion

Daito Islands offer an unforgettable island experience, a mix of cultural immersion, outdoor adventures, and breathtaking natural wonders. Plan your trip well and enjoy the best of Daito Islands.
