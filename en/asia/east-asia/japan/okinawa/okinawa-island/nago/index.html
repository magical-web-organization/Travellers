Introduction to Nago

If you’re looking for a place that provides both the buzz of modern city life and the relaxation of natural beauty, Nago in Okinawa Island is the perfect destination. Located at the northwest of Okinawa Island, Nago is home to fascinating cultural attractions, stunning natural landscapes, and exciting activities. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Nago has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Nago

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nago is steeped in history and culture, and it shows in its many museums and historical sites. The best ones include:

  • Nago Castle Ruins: The ruins of what once was an immense castle that dates back to the Ryukyu Kingdom era.
  • Nago Museum: A must-see if you want to learn about Okinawa’s history and culture, with exhibits on the Ryukyu Kingdom and the Battle of Okinawa.
  • Okinawa Fruits Land: A farm where visitors can see, taste, and learn about various tropical fruits.
  • Pineapple Park: A park dedicated to pineapples, where visitors can enjoy pineapple products, take part in workshops, and enjoy garden tours.

Natural Attractions of Nago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nago’s natural beauty is unparalleled, and there is no shortage of outdoor activities to enjoy. Some of the best things to see and do include:

  • Cape Manzamo: A cliff overlooking the East China Sea, famous for its stunning views and whale-like rock formation.
  • Ocean Expo Park: A park featuring Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium, one of the world’s biggest aquariums, and the Okinawa Science Museum.
  • Sesoko Beach: A picturesque sandy beach with crystal-clear water and a perfect destination for sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling.
  • Mount Yae: A popular hiking spot in Nago, where adventurous visitors can tackle the challenging trails to reach the summit’s breathtaking panoramic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nago

Nago’s lush green forests and mountains make the destination perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include:

  • Yanbaru Forest Trail: A moderate three-hour trek inside the dense forests surrounding Nago.
  • Mount Yae Trail: A challenging hike with some parts that require ropes to climb.
  • Nago Castle Historical Nature Trail: A flat trail that takes visitors through Nago’s history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Nago: Activities and Tours

Apart from culture, history, and natural scenery, Nago offers plenty of activities and tours worth adding to your itinerary. Some great options include:

  • Scuba Diving: Take a dip in the beautiful coral reefs of Okinawa with Kokusaidori Diving
  • Whale Watching: Spot humpback whales in their natural habitat with Nago Whale Watching Association
  • Helicopter Tours: Explore Nago from above and enjoy scenic views with Marine Club Berry
  • Seabreacher Adventure: Take on an adrenaline-filled ride in a submersible watercraft with Seabreacher Okinawa

Food and Drink Scene of Nago

Must-Try Dishes

Nago’s rich culture is also reflected in its food scene, and no visit is complete without trying some of the local dishes, such as:

  • Soki Soba: A noodle dish topped with tender pork ribs, perfect for warming the soul on a chilly day.
  • Jushi: A traditional local dish, similar to sushi, made with a mixture of local ingredients, including rice, meat, seafood, and vegetables.
  • Okinawa-style Miso Soup: A miso soup that uses local ingredients such as goya, mirin, and awamori which is often enjoyed at breakfast.

Local Beverages

Okinawa is also known for its unique alcoholic beverages like Awamori, a rice-distilled liquor that can be enjoyed straight, and Orion Beer, a local lager perfect for a hot sunny afternoon.

Where to Stay in Nago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels, Nago has plenty of options for every traveler’s preferences and budget. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Kanehide Kise Country Club: A golf and spa resort rated as one of Tripadvisor’s Traveler’s Choice 2021 Award Winners.
  • The Crest Hotel: A modern hotel with stunning panoramic views of the sea and city.
  • GuestHouse Yun-Tan: A cozy hostel perfect for a laid-back stay with a friendly atmosphere and affordable prices.

What to Buy in Nago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nago’s markets are filled with unique souvenirs that you can bring back home, including:

  • Chinsukou: A traditional Okinawan pastry that is often given as a souvenir.
  • Awamori: A local liquor that makes for a perfect gift for friends and family.
  • Ryukyu Glass: A type of glasswork that was developed by the Okinawan people after WW II, characterized by its beautiful blue-green color.

Local Culture and Customs of Nago

Okinawan culture is heavily influenced by Ryukyu Kingdom, and the traditional values of respect and hospitality can still be seen today. Visitors should be aware of Okinawa’s customs, including removing your shoes before entering a house or temple, and when giving or receiving anything, use both hands.

Best Time to Visit Nago

The best time to visit Nago is between April-June and September-November when temperatures are mild, and the rainfall is minimal. Visitors should note that typhoon season is between July and October, so prepare accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nago

Do:

  • Do try the local food, and try several different dishes. Okinawan cuisine is famous around the world, thanks to its unique blend of cultural influences.
  • Be respectful of Okinawan customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples, castles, or other cultural sites.
  • Always wear sunscreen, even if the weather is cloudy, and wear comfortable shoes as the terrain can be rough in some outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave your trash behind, as Okinawa’s natural beauty depends on keeping pollution to a minimum.
  • Don’t ignore the typhoon warnings during the rainy season.
  • Don’t immediately assume that everyone speaks English; try to learn some useful Japanese phrases to foster better communication.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nago

Overall, Nago is a safe destination with a low crime rate. Visitors should take normal precautions such as not leaving their valuables unattended in public areas.

Health in Nago

Okinawa has excellent medical facilities and a high standard of health care. Visitors should take precautions to prevent sunburn and insect bites, as well as staying hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Nago: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nago

The nearest airport to Nago is Naha Airport, located about 60 miles away in Naha City. Visitors can take a bus or rent a car to reach Nago.

Bus Stations Near Nago

Visitors can also reach Nago by taking an express bus from Naha or other cities in Okinawa Island. The Nago Bus Terminal is located downtown, and visitors can easily walk to various points of interest.

Other Options

For visitors who prefer traveling on their own time, renting a car is a fantastic option. Nago is connected to Okinawa’s major highways, making it easy to explore the island at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Nago

Getting around in Nago is relatively easy, thanks to its extensive public transportation system. Visitors can take the local bus, rent a car or take a taxi to explore the city’s sights and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Nago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Nago’s tourist attractions, visitors can plan to visit other nearby destinations such as:

  • Motobu Peninsula: Home to Ocean Expo Park and the Churaumi Aquarium, motobu peninsula lies just north of Nago City
  • Okinawa City: Experience the vibrant nightlife of Okinawa City, the home of the island’s largest supermarket San-A
  • Nakijin-Jo Castle: This is an ancient castle just northwest of Nago City, with stunning views of the East China Sea, and a must-see for history buffs.

Conclusion

With its unique blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure, Nago is a hidden gem that should be on everyone’s Okinawa travel itinerary. Whether it’s exploring the city’s historical sites, hiking through its lush forest trails or relaxing at its beautiful beaches, there’s something for every traveler in Nago. So pack your bags and get ready to discover this stunning destination for yourself.
