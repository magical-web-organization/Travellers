Introduction to Naha

Naha is the capital and largest city in Okinawa Prefecture, Japan. Located on the southern part of Okinawa Island, Naha is a vibrant and bustling city that offers a fascinating mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. With its subtropical climate, sandy beaches, and turquoise waters, Naha is also a popular destination for tourists looking to soak up some sunshine and relax.

List of Places to Visit in Naha

If you’re planning to visit Naha soon, here’s a list of top attractions and things to do:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1) Shuri Castle - a UNESCO World Heritage site and the former royal palace of the Ryukyu Kingdom 2) Tamaudun Mausoleum – a royal tomb complex 3) Okinawa Prefectural Museum & Art Museum - a museum dedicated to the heritage and culture of Okinawa 4) Shikinaen Royal Garden – an ornate garden with a pond and unique architecture

Natural Attractions of Naha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

5) Naminoue Shrine - a picturesque shrine perched on a cliff overlooking the ocean
6) Naha Beach - a great spot for sunbathing and swimming 7) Tomari Port - take a ferry to other neighboring islands like Zamami or Tokashiki and explore their natural beauty 8) Okinawa World - an amusement park showcasing limestone caves and local cultural events

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naha

9) Todoroki Waterfall – a popular hiking trail leading to a beautiful waterfall 10) Hiji Falls – a challenging hike with a stunning waterfall 11) Yogi Park – a park with a cycling course where you can rent bicycles and explore the park’s nature trails

Best Things to Do in Naha: Activities and Tours

12) Ryukyu Glass Village – an artistic workshop where you can learn about traditional Okinawan glass 13) Naha Traditional Craft Center - a place to learn about and buy Okinawan crafts and artifacts 14) Makishi Public Market - a bustling marketplace that offers a huge variety of fresh seafood, vegetables, and other local delicacies 15) Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium – one of the largest aquariums in East Asia, famous for its whale sharks and manta rays

Week-Long Itinerary for Naha

Day 1: Visit Shuri Castle and walk around the nearby Shuri neighborhood to see the traditional Okinawan architecture. Day 2: Take a day trip to nearby islands such as Tokashiki or Zamami. Day 3: Learn about Okinawan history at the Okinawa Prefectural Museum and Art Museum Day 4: Go shopping for souvenirs and try local foods at Makishi Public Market Day 5: Enjoy a day of relaxation on one of Naha’s famous beaches like Naha Beach Day 6: Explore the unique Ryukyu Glass Village and Traditional Craft Center Day 7: Visit the Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium and see its diverse and rare marine life

Food and Drink Scene of Naha

### Must-Try Dishes

Okinawan cuisine is different from Japanese cuisine which is quite obvious when you come to Okinawa. Here are some must-try dishes you should seek out:

1) Goya Champuru - a stir-fried dish with goya, tofu, and pork 2) Okinawan soba – a noodle soup usually served with pork or chicken bone broth 3) Rafute - braised pork belly simmered with Awamori and soy sauce 4) Sata Andagi - deep-fried and doughnut-like dessert

Local Beverages

Okinawa is known for its unique alcoholic drinks. You should definitely try these local drinks:

1) Awamori - a rice wine made in Okinawa 2) Orion Beer - Okinawa’s most famous beer 3) Shikuwasa juice - a citrus fruit juice from Okinawa

Where to Stay in Naha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Naha has a wide range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some suggestions:

Luxury: 1) The Naha Terrace - a hotel with a pool, spa, and ocean view rooms 2) La’gent Hotel Okinawa Naha - a luxurious hotel with a rooftop bar and jacuzzi 3) Hotel Mahaina Wellness Resorts Okinawa – a wellness hotel with an ocean view and spa amenities

Mid-range: 1) Mercure Okinawa Naha - a modern hotel with a great location 2) Loisir Hotel Naha - a cozy hotel with karaoke rooms 3) Hotel JAL City Naha - a comfortable hotel with a rooftop bar

Budget: 1) Hostel Hakuseiso - a budget hostel with capsule-like dorm beds 2) Okinawa Guest House KERAMA - a budget guest house with Japanese-style rooms and a shared kitchen 3) Guest House Umikaji - a budget guest house with a shared kitchen

What to Buy in Naha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Okinawa is famous for its artisans and handicrafts. You should visit Kokusai Street in Naha to find many traditional and colorful local products that you can bring home. Here are some items to look for:

1) Ryukyu glassware 2) Shisa figurines – a pair of lion-like creatures that serve as guardians 3) Bingata textiles - traditional Okinawan dyed clothes 4) Okinawan salt

Local Culture and Customs of Naha

Okinawan culture is unique and distinct from the rest of Japan. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting Naha:

1) Shisa - Anywhere you go in Okinawa, you’re likely to come across shisa, which are often found guarding the entrances to buildings or homes. It is customary to pet the shisa for good luck before entering a building. 2) Dialect - Although Japanese is the official language, people in Okinawa also speak Uchinaaguchi, a local language. 3) Manners - Okinawans are known for their politeness, respectfulness, and hospitality towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Naha

The best time to visit Naha is during spring (April to May) or fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and the city is less crowded. Summer (June to August) is the peak season for tourists and can be hot and humid. Winter (December to February) can be cold and windy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naha

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Naha:

Do:

1) Respect the traditions and manners. 2) Try the local cuisine and drinks. 3) Learn a few basic Japanese phrases, such as “hello” and “thank you.”

Don’t:

1) Don’t litter. 2) Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places. 3) Don’t take photos or videos without asking for permission first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naha

Naha is generally considered safe, but it’s always a good idea to take usual precautions when traveling abroad. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid carrying large sums of money.

Health in Naha

Okinawa has one of the highest life expectancies in the world, and the quality of medical care is excellent. However, it is always a good idea to have travel insurance that includes medical coverage when traveling to a foreign country.

Getting to Naha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naha

1) Naha Airport - the main airport in Okinawa that receives domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Naha

1) Naha Bus Terminal - the main bus terminal connecting Naha to other parts of Okinawa.

Other Options

1) Ferry - you can take a ferry from Naha to nearby islands like Kerama or Miyako. 2) Taxi - Taxis are widely available in Naha, but they can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Naha

Public Transportation

Naha has a well-connected public transportation system consisting of buses and monorails. The monorail system runs through the city and connects to the airport.

Car Rental

Renting a car is also a good option, especially if you want to explore the island. However, drivers should be aware that driving is on the left side of the road in Japan.

Where to Go Next from Naha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations you can visit from Naha:

1) Kerama Islands – famous for snorkeling and diving 2) Ishigaki Island - famous for beaches and stunning coral reefs 3) Miyako Island - known for its beautiful coastlines and beaches

Conclusion

Naha is a beautiful city with lots of attractions and things to do. With its rich history, unique culture, and stunning natural beauty, it’s a must-visit destination in Japan. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your Naha experience.
