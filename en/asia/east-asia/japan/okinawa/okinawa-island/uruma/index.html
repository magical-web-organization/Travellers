Introduction to Uruma

Uruma is a city located in the central part of Okinawa Island, in Okinawa prefecture, Japan. It is the third-largest city in the prefecture, with a population of over 120,000. Uruma is known for its rich culture, pristine beaches, and scenic mountains, making it a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, activities, food, accommodation, and transport options in Uruma, as well as the local culture and customs, safety and health, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Uruma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Uruma is home to several museums and historical sites that provide insights into the city’s culture and history. These include:

  • (1) Yonagusuku Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 14th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • (2) Okinawa Prefectural Museum & Art Museum, which displays artifacts and exhibits related to Okinawa’s natural history, culture, and art.
  • (3) Uruma Historical Museum, which showcases the history of Uruma through a collection of artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Uruma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Uruma is blessed with abundant natural beauty, from picturesque beaches and scenic mountains to lush forests and mangrove swamps. Here are some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views Uruma has to offer:

  • (1) Miyagi Island, a small island off the coast of Uruma that boasts crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches, as well as opportunities for snorkeling, kayaking, and diving.
  • (2) Mount Ishikawa, the tallest mountain in Uruma, with several hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • (3) Hiji Waterfall, a stunning waterfall that plunges into a deep pool of clear water, offering a refreshing respite from the tropical heat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uruma

Uruma is also a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with numerous trails and routes that offer a chance to explore the city’s natural beauty up close. Some of the best routes include:

  • (1) The Yonaguni Course, a 12km hiking route that takes you through lush forests and stunning landscapes.
  • (2) The Higashi Course, a 17km biking route that offers panoramic views of the sea and mountain ranges of Okinawa.
  • (3) The Katsuren Course, a 10km route that takes you through historical sites and offers glimpses into Uruma’s rich cultural heritage.

Best Things to Do in Uruma: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the city’s cultural and natural attractions, Uruma offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • (1) Go diving, snorkeling, or kayaking in the crystal-clear waters of Miyagi Island.
  • (2) Take a sunset cruise along Uruma’s coastline and enjoy stunning views of the sea and sky.
  • (3) Participate in a traditional Okinawan tea ceremony and learn about the history and culture of this fascinating art.
  • (4) Attend a cultural festival, such as the Uruma City Summer Festival or the Katsuren Castle Festival, and experience the vibrant traditions of Uruma’s local communities.

Food and Drink Scene of Uruma

Must-Try Dishes

Uruma is known for its unique cuisine, which features a blend of Japanese, Chinese, and Southeast Asian flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • (1) Goya Champuru: a stir-fry dish made with bitter melon, tofu, and pork.
  • (2) Rafute: a pork belly dish served in a sweet soy-based sauce.
  • (3) Soki Soba: a traditional Okinawan noodle dish served with pork spare ribs.

Local Beverages

Uruma is also known for its local beverages, including:

  • (1) Awamori: a distilled rice liquor that is a popular drink in Okinawa.
  • (2) Orion Beer: a locally brewed beer that is a popular drink in Uruma and the rest of Okinawa.

Where to Stay in Uruma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Uruma to suit every budget and travel style. Some of the top choices include:

  • (1) Crowne Plaza ANA Okinawa Harborview: a five-star hotel with stunning views of Uruma Bay.
  • (2) Guesthouse Iza: a cozy and affordable guesthouse with a rooftop terrace and ocean views.
  • (3) Naha West Inn: a budget-friendly hotel located within easy access to major attractions in Uruma.

What to Buy in Uruma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, Uruma has plenty to offer. Some of the top products include:

  • (1) Shisa Statues: traditional Okinawan statues that are often placed on rooftops to ward off evil spirits.
  • (2) Awamori: a locally distilled rice liquor that makes an excellent gift or souvenir.
  • (3) Okinawan Glass: unique glassware made with traditional Okinawan techniques, perfect for adding a touch of Uruma to your home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Uruma

Uruma is steeped in cultural traditions that are still very much alive today. Some of the customs and cultural practices that visitors should be aware of include:

  • (1) Respect for elders and authority figures is a central value in Uruma, so it’s important to show deference to people in positions of power or influence.
  • (2) Uruma is home to several unique festivals and cultural events throughout the year, such as the Shikinaisha Festival, which dates back over 300 years.
  • (3) Drinking awamori is a common social activity in Uruma, and visitors should be prepared to participate in this traditional form of hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Uruma

The best time to visit Uruma is between November and April when the weather is cooler and drier. However, Uruma is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy plenty of outdoor activities and cultural festivals during the summer months as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uruma

Do:

  • (1) Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • (2) Try local dishes and beverages, such as goya champuru and awamori.
  • (3) Take advantage of the city’s natural beauty by exploring hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • (1) Ignore signs or warnings indicating danger, such as those near beaches during typhoon season.
  • (2) Wear shoes or hats inside some cultural or religious venues.
  • (3) Be too loud or boisterous in public places, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uruma

Uruma is generally a safe place to visit, although visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities or exploring remote areas. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • (1) Wear appropriate footwear when hiking, and be aware of local wildlife such as snakes and spiders.
  • (2) Avoid swimming alone, especially during typhoon season, when currents can be strong.
  • (3) Keep valuables secure and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Uruma

Uruma is generally a healthy place to visit, with no major health concerns or epidemics reported in recent years. Visitors should take common-sense precautions such as drinking bottled water and using sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Uruma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uruma

The nearest airport to Uruma is Naha Airport, which offers both domestic and international flights. From Naha Airport, visitors can either take a bus or a taxi to Uruma, which takes approximately one hour.

Bus Stations Near Uruma

Uruma has several bus stations that connect to other parts of Okinawa, such as Nago and Naha. The most convenient bus station for visitors is Uruma Bus Terminal, which is located in the city center and offers multiple bus routes.

Other Options

Other transportation options for visitors to Uruma include renting a car or taking a private shuttle service from Naha Airport. Renting a car is a convenient option for visitors who want to explore the city and its surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Uruma

Uruma is a relatively small city, and most of its major attractions are within easy walking distance or accessible via public transport. Visitors can take buses or taxis to get around the city or rent bicycles to explore the area on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Uruma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Uruma can easily explore other parts of Okinawa prefecture, including nearby destinations such as:

  • (1) Naha, the capital of Okinawa, which is known for its historical sites and vibrant nightlife.
  • (2) Churaumi Aquarium, one of the largest and most impressive aquariums in the world, located in Motobu.
  • (3) Ishigaki Island, a tropical paradise with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a laid-back atmosphere.

Conclusion

Uruma is a city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring towering mountains, pristine beaches, or vibrant cultural festivals, Uruma has something to offer every traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Uruma with confidence and make the most of your time in this fascinating and beautiful part of Japan.
