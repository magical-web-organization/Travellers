Yonaguni Travel Guide

Located in Japan’s Yaeyama Islands, Yonaguni is a great destination for travelers looking to immerse themselves in the rich culture and natural beauty of Japan. With its picturesque landscapes, fascinating history, and vibrant local culture, Yonaguni offers something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll dive into everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Yonaguni.

Introduction to Yonaguni

Yonaguni is known for being the westernmost inhabited island of Japan, sitting very close to Taiwan. It’s a small Japanese island but carries significant historical and cultural importance. The island is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse marine life, and ancient ruins.

List of Places to Visit in Yonaguni

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Yonaguni Monument - The monument, which resembles a pyramid, is a mysterious formation that has raised many questions among archaeologists and geologists. Is it a man-made structure or a natural rock formation? Visitors can dive around the monument for a unique view of its underwater structure.
  2. Yonaguni Castle Ruins - This historic site dates back to the 15th century, and visitors can explore the ruins of the castle while learning about their importance to the island’s history.
  3. Yonaguni Shrine - A charming shrine dedicated to the god of the sea that sits cliffside, providing stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Yonaguni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ibigawa Fishing Port - This is the main harbor on the island, where visitors can see the fishermen bringing in their catch for the day.
  2. Tombs/Tsumibaka - There are many ancient sites on the island that have significant historical and archeological importance, and visitors can explore them to learn more about the island’s past.
  3. Yonaguni Horse Riding Center - Visitors can take a beautiful guided tour of the island, including riding horses along beautiful beaches and through forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yonaguni

  1. Kinsei Path - This 1 km path will take you to a stunning cliffside overlooking the sea. Along the way, you’ll pass through beautiful forests and fields of flowers.
  2. Ryuguu Terrace - This trail leads you to the beautiful Ryugu Cave and offers incredible views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Kuguru Cave Trail - Visitors can explore the stunning Kuguru Cave while hiking through the beautiful tropical jungle.

Best Things to Do in Yonaguni: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving - Yonaguni offers visitors some of the best snorkeling and diving spots in Japan, with various schools and diving companies available.
  2. Nightlife at Izakayas - Visitors can experience Japanese nightlife with local food and drinks, in the many izakayas scattered across the island.
  3. Glass Bottom Boat Tour - A fantastic way to see the island’s beauty, visitors can take a ride on a glass-bottom boat to see the underwater treasures around the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yonaguni

Day 1: Arrival and relaxation at the Island. Day 2: Cultural attractions: visit Yonaguni’s Monuments, Castle Ruins, and Garden. Day 3-4: Outdoor Adventures: go horseback riding, hiking, snorkeling, and diving. Day 5: Explore the island’s food scene and local products Day 6: Visit Tsumibaka, tombs, and shrines of the island for a glimpse of the local history. Day 7: A final day of relaxation, enjoying the island’s beaches, spas, and onsen.

Food and Drink Scene of Yonaguni

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kakuni - A traditional pork belly dish that has been simmered in soy sauce, sugar and mirin, served with a variety of condiments.
  2. Yaeyama soba - This popular local noodle dish is made with purple-colored soba that served in hot soup or cold with a dipping sauce.
  3. Umibudo - A strange-looking but delicious local seaweed harvested directly from the ocean.

Local Beverages

  1. Awamori - This is Okinawan rice liquor and is the local alcohol in the Yonaguni region.
  2. Yukishu - A type of aged rice wine, served as a digestif for post-meal in many onsen hot springs.

Where to Stay in Yonaguni: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hikawa Ryokan - A traditional Japanese inn with spacious rooms, fantastic views of the sea, and a natural hot spring.
  2. Tsuki no Yado Sadayoshi - A local-style guest house within walking distance of the beaches and other attractions, with comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  3. Guest House Minsa - An affordable option with excellent amenities, including a shared kitchen and ample space for families or groups of friends.

What to Buy in Yonaguni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Yonaguni Donuts - These unique donuts are a must-try food souvenir to bring back home, representing the shape of Yonaguni Monument.
  2. Yonaguni Salt - Harvested from nearby seawater pools, this pure, unrefined salt is a great ingredient to use in cooking and a perfect souvenir for presents.
  3. Earthenware Pottery - Yonaguni Island is known for its fine ceramics, and at the Takimotokan workshop, visitors can learn how to make their pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Yonaguni

The island’s culture is part of the Ryukyu Kingdom legacy with a vibrant and distinct culture embodies the friendliness of the people of this island paradise. One unique cultural aspect of Yonaguni is its traditional music performed on the sanshin, an Okinawan stringed instrument.

Best Time to Visit Yonaguni

The best time to visit Yonaguni is from October to April when the weather is cooler and less humid. The cherry blossom season starts in early February, and March is the nesting season for sea turtles on the beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yonaguni

Do:

  • Respect the traditional customs and culture of the locals.
  • Visit the island with an open mind and embrace the unknown.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases before visiting.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take stones, shells, or other natural items from the beaches.
  • Don’t Touch the sea turtles, stick to a safe distance without disturbing their nests.
  • Don’t litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yonaguni

Yonaguni is a safe place to travel, and crime rates are low. However, be wary of ocean safety, especially on popular beaches, and always follow the local weather advisories to ensure safe conditions.

Health in Yonaguni

Visitors to Yonaguni should be up to date with their routine vaccinations, but there are no special requirements or specific health concerns for the island.

Getting to Yonaguni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yonaguni

Yonaguni Airport is the main airport on the island, served by a small fleet of runways.

Bus Stations Near Yonaguni

Public transportation is not available on the island, so visitors will have to rent a car or bike or hire a private taxi to navigate the island’s many attractions.

Other Options

There are several ferry services available that connect Yonaguni to neighboring islands.

How to Get Around in Yonaguni

Renting a car, scooter or bicycle are the most convenient ways to move around the island as public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Yonaguni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Yonaguni is a captivating destination on its own, there are other Yaeyama Islands worth exploring, including Ishigaki Island, Taketomi Island, and Iriomote Island, all accessible via ferry or short flight from Yonaguni.

Conclusion

Yonaguni island is an ideal destination for travelers seeking serene natural beauty, rich history, and unique Japanese culture. With a wide range of attractions and activities available, the island is an all-in-one holiday destination that people can explore to their heart’s content.
