Ultimate Travel Guide to Tarama, Japan

Tarama is a small island located in Okinawa, Japan, a mesmerizing archipelago in the East China Sea. Though it’s not one of the highly popular tourist destinations in Japan, Tarama Island has a lot to offer. From its pristine beaches and coral reefs to its rich history and unique culture, Tarama is a must-visit location.

Introduction to Tarama

Tarama is a traditional island known for its beautiful beaches, crystal blue waters, mangrove forests, coral reefs, and exotic fauna. The village of Tarama is home to about 1,200 residents, many of whom are traditional artisans specializing in weaving baskets and making pottery. The best time to visit Tarama is between October and May, as it is the driest period of the year.

List of Places to Visit in Tarama

Tarama offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness that tourists will undoubtedly appreciate. Here is a list of places to visit while in Tarama:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tarama Museum: It showcases exhibits of ancient pottery and folkcraft, including traditional agricultural tools used by locals.
  • Tarama Island Culture Center: It promotes the history, culture, and traditions of Tarama Island with an emphasis on the distinct Ryukyu culture.

Natural Attractions of Tarama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kirakira Beach: It offers clean and warm seawaters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.
  • Nagannu Island: It’s a deserted island that has hiking trails leading to secluded beaches, cliffs and offers stunning views of the Okinawa Islands.
  • Gusuku Site: It’s an archaeological site that is believed to be a former residence of the Ryukyu Kingdom’s elite class, thus offering a back-in-time experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarama

Hiking and biking are popular activities on the island. Visitors can explore the island’s attractions by renting a bicycle or joining a guided tour from locals. The island also has well-marked hiking trails that lead to scenic locations such as the Sakiyama Observation Deck, which offers panoramic views of the island.

Best Things to Do in Tarama: Activities and Tours

  • Island Tour: Hire a local guide and explore the island on foot, discovering hidden gems and learning about the island’s culture.
  • Kayaking and Snorkeling: Rent kayaks or hire a guide and explore the island’s coral reefs and marine life.
  • Pottery Class: Take a pottery-making class with the locals and learn about the artistry behind traditional pottery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarama

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long tour of Tarama:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Kirakira Beach and Gusuku Site
Day 2 Explore Nagannu Island
Day 3 Take a pottery class
Day 4 Go on an island tour with a local guide
Day 5 Cycling tour of the island
Day 6 Go kayaking and snorkeling
Day 7 Relax and unwind at Sakiyama Observation Deck

Food and Drink Scene of Tarama

Tarama boasts a unique culinary tradition that’s heavily influenced by the island’s Ryukyu culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goya Champuru: It’s a stir-fried dish composed of bitter melon and tofu, flavored with miso, and egg.
  • Rafute: It’s a traditional pork dish slow-cooked in soy sauce and Okinawan brown sugar until it’s tender and juicy.
  • Soba: It’s a dish consisting of Buckwheat noodles served in hot broth and accompanied by slices of pork, seaweed, and boiled egg.

Local Beverages

  • Awamori: Tarama is known for the production of Awamori, which is a distilled beverage made from Thai rice and is aged for three years.

Where to Stay in Tarama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarama offers a variety of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from luxurious resorts to affordable guesthouses.

  • Tarama Island Resort: This resort is located near Kirakira Beach, offering scenic views of the Okinawa Islands.
  • Tarama Pension: It’s a traditional guesthouse that offers a warm and homely ambiance, giving visitors a taste of the island’s hospitality.

What to Buy in Tarama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase unique souvenirs such as baskets, pottery, and other traditional crafts made by local artisans. Additionally, they can purchase Awamori and other local delicacies like Soba noodles and Okinawan sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarama

Tarama Island has a unique culture that is influenced by its Ryukyu heritage. The people of Tarama are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and visitors are expected to reciprocate. Visitors should respect the local customs and social norms, such as removing their shoes before entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Tarama

The best time to visit Tarama is between October and May. During these months, the weather is dry, and the sea is calm, allowing visitors to experience a fantastic beach and underwater experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarama

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Be polite and courteous to the locals.
  • Explore the island on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Enter a home with shoes on.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore the traditional etiquette of the Ryukyu culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarama

Tarama is a safe and peaceful island, with a low crime rate. Visitors should follow standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding dark and secluded areas at night.

Health in Tarama

Tarama has a small hospital, and medical facilities are limited. Visitors are advised to bring their prescription medication and take preventive measures to avoid health issues.

Getting to Tarama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarama is accessible by air and sea.

Airports Near Tarama

There are two airports near Tarama, the Miyako Airport and Ishigaki Airport; both offering domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Tarama

Visitors can take a bus from Miyako Airport to Hirara Port, where they can catch a ferry to Tarama.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from Miyako or Ishigaki airports to Hirara Port.

How to Get Around in Tarama

Visitors can explore Tarama by bicycle, car, or taxi. It’s recommended to rent bicycles as it’s a great way to explore the island and enjoy the natural scenery at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Tarama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tarama is close to many other Okinawan islands, and visitors can take boats from Hirara Port to explore the region’s natural beauty further.

Conclusion

Tarama is a hidden gem that is waiting to be uncovered. From its pristine beaches and coral reefs to its rich history and unique culture, the island has something for everyone. Whether it’s hiking, biking, pottery-making or just enjoying the natural beauty and warm hospitality of the locals, Tarama Island is a must-visit location for any traveler interested in exploring the beauty of Japan.
