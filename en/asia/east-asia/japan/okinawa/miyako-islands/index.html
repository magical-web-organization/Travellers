Introduction to Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands is a group of picturesque islands located in Okinawa, Japan. It is an off-the-beaten-path destination that remains untouched by mass tourism. The islands are known for its crystal-clear waters, breathtaking views, and unspoiled natural beauty. The archipelago comprises eight main islands, including Miyako-jima, the largest and most developed island. The friendly locals, unique culture, and warm climate make Miyako Islands a must-visit destination for travelers visiting Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands is a paradise for those who love exploring the outdoors, immersing in local culture, and enjoying stunning views. Here are some of the must-visit places in Miyako Islands.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Miyakojima City Museum: This museum features exhibits showcasing the history and culture of Miyako Islands. Visitors can learn about the traditional lifestyle, architecture, and customs of the locals.
  • Agarihama Site: It is an archaeological site that provides insights into the ancient history of Okinawa. The site contains remains from the Old Stone Age and Jomon period.
  • Higashi Hennazaki Lighthouse: Located at the southernmost point of Miyako-jima, this lighthouse offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can also visit the nearby Tuyumya Tomb, which is a historical burial site.

Natural Attractions of Miyako Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yabiji: This uninhabited island is located off the coast of Miyako-jima and offers visitors a pristine beach, clear waters, and lush greenery. It is an excellent place for snorkeling, diving, and hiking.
  • Ikema Ohashi Bridge: This bridge connects Miyako-jima with Ikema-jima and offers stunning views of the ocean and the islands. It is a popular spot for photography and watching the sunrise/sunset.
  • Aragusuku Island: It is a small, idyllic island located off the coast of Miyako-jima. Visitors can enjoy swimming, kayaking, and snorkeling in the clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes that visitors can explore.

  • Yonaha Maehama Beach to Sunayama Beach: This route is a scenic 6 km trail that offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape. It also passes through some local farms and villages.
  • Ueno German Culture Village to Kuura Island: This 5 km trail passes through the Ueno German Culture Village, which contains replicas of German buildings and a beer garden. It then leads to a small island called Kuura, famous for its crystal-clear waters and coral reefs.

Best Things to Do in Miyako Islands: Activities and Tours

Miyako Islands is a paradise for adventure and nature lovers. Here are some of the best activities and tours that visitors can experience in Miyako Islands.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Miyako Islands is famous for its clear waters and diverse marine life. Visitors can book snorkeling and diving tours to explore the coral reefs and encounter sea turtles, colorful fish, and other marine creatures.
  • Sea Kayaking and Stand-up Paddleboarding: Visitors can rent kayaks and paddleboards and explore the calm, turquoise waters of Miyako Islands. It is a perfect way to enjoy the natural beauty of the islands.
  • Mangrove Kayaking Tour: This tour takes visitors to the mangrove forests located on Miyako-jima. Visitors can explore the serene environment, see unique flora and fauna, and spot wildlife such as egret, kingfisher, and mudskipper.

Miyako Islands is a small archipelago, but it offers plenty of opportunities to explore and have fun. Here is a week-long itinerary that visitors can follow to experience the best of Miyako Islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands offer a unique cuisine that is a mix of Japanese, Chinese, and Taiwanese flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and traditional drinks that visitors can enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ishigaki Beef: It is a type of Wagyu beef that is raised on Ishigaki Island, a nearby island to Miyako-jima. The beef is known for its rich flavor and tender texture.
  • Sea Grapes: It is a type of seaweed that resembles caviar and is a delicacy in Okinawa. It is usually served as a side dish or a topping on sushi.
  • Sata Andagi: It is a type of fried doughnut that originated in Okinawa. It is made by mixing flour, sugar, and eggs and deep-frying the mixture. It is a popular comfort food in Miyako Islands.

Local Beverages

  • Awamori: It is a distilled rice liquor that is popular in Okinawa. It is an essential drink during festivals and celebrations.
  • Mango Juice: Miyako Islands is known for its sweet and juicy mangoes. Visitors can enjoy freshly squeezed mango juice at local cafes and juice bars.

Where to Stay in Miyako Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Miyako Islands offer plenty of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are some recommendations for where to stay in Miyako Islands.

Budget-Friendly Accommodations:

  • Little Mermaid: It is a cozy hostel located in Miyako-jima, offering private and shared rooms. The hostel is in a convenient location, close to Maehama Beach and local restaurants.
  • Pension SeaFriend: It is a guesthouse located in the heart of Miyako-jima, offering basic amenities and a warm welcome from the hospitable hosts.

Mid-Range Accommodations:

  • Hotel Maple: It is a modern hotel located near Irabu Bridge, offering comfortable rooms and excellent facilities such as an outdoor pool and spa.
  • Hotel Risingsun Miyakojima: It is a beachfront hotel located on Miyako-jima, offering spacious rooms and stunning ocean views.

Luxury Accommodations:

  • The Shigira: It is a luxurious resort located on Irabu Island, offering refined accommodations and world-class facilities such as an infinity pool, spa, and private beach.
  • Hoshinoya Resorts Miyakojima: It is a high-end resort located on Miyako-jima, offering stylish villas and exceptional services such as private dining and personal concierge.

What to Buy in Miyako Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Miyako Islands offers plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home as a memory of their trip. Here are some of the popular items to buy in Miyako Islands.

  • Miyako-jima Salt: This sea salt is harvested in Miyako-jima and is known for its pure taste and mineral-rich content. It is an excellent gift for foodies and culinary enthusiasts.
  • Mango Products: Miyako Islands is known for its sweet and juicy mangoes. Visitors can buy mango jam, mango juice, and other mango-based products that make for a delicious gift.
  • Coral Jewelry: Miyako Islands is known for its diverse coral reefs. Visitors can buy beautiful coral jewelry, such as necklaces, bracelets, and earrings, from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands has a unique culture that has been shaped by its history and location. Here are some insights into the local culture and customs of Miyako Islands.

  • Hirara-Odori: It is a traditional dance form that originated in Miyako Islands. It is performed during festivals and celebrations and is a reflection of local culture and heritage.
  • Island Hopping: Island hopping is a popular activity in Miyako Islands, and it reflects the proximity of the islands. Visitors can explore the distinct cultures and customs of each island in the archipelago.

Best Time to Visit Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands has a warm and humid subtropical climate, making it an ideal destination for year-round travel. The best time to visit Miyako Islands is from March to May and September to November when the climate is mild, and the tourist crowds are low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyako Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and traditional drinks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the islands.
  • Engage with the friendly locals and learn about their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the water.
  • Enter the coral reefs or disturb the marine life.
  • Disrespect the cultural and religious sites.
  • Ignore the safety warnings and advice.

Safety and Health

Miyako Islands is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions and follow the safety guidelines. Here are some insights into safety and health in Miyako Islands.

Safety in Miyako Islands

  • The waters around Miyako Islands can be rough and dangerous at times. Visitors should pay attention to the weather conditions and follow the safety guidelines.
  • Visitors should apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn and dehydration.
  • Visitors should be aware of the presence of venomous creatures such as jellyfish and stonefish.

Health in Miyako Islands

  • Visitors should drink bottled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.
  • Visitors should follow basic hygiene practices such as washing hands and avoiding contact with sick individuals.
  • Visitors should carry necessary medication and consult a doctor before traveling to Miyako Islands.

Getting to Miyako Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Miyako Islands is accessible by air and sea. Visitors can choose from various transportation options to reach the islands.

Airports Near Miyako Islands

  • Miyako Airport: It is the main airport serving Miyako Islands and is located on Miyako-jima. It is well-connected to major cities in Japan and other Asian countries.

Bus Stations Near Miyako Islands

  • Miyako-jima Bus Terminal: It is the main bus terminal located on Miyako-jima, offering bus services to various destinations on the island.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Visitors can also reach Miyako Islands by ferry from Okinawa.

How to Get Around in Miyako Islands

Miyako Islands is a small archipelago, and visitors can easily explore the islands by public transportation or rental vehicles such as cars or bicycles. Here are some transportation options in Miyako Islands.

  • Local Bus: Miyako Bus is the main public transportation system on Miyako-jima, offering bus services to various destinations on the island.
  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent cars and drive around the islands at their own pace.
  • Rental Bicycle: Visitors can rent bicycles and explore the islands leisurely.

Where to Go Next from Miyako Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miyako Islands is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in Okinawa. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can consider.

  • Ishigaki Island: It is a nearby island famous for its pristine beaches, coral reefs, and sea turtles.
  • Okinawa Island: The largest island in Okinawa, offering a mix of urban and natural attractions such as the Shurijo Castle and the Churaumi Aquarium.
  • Yaeyama Islands: It is a group of islands known for its unique culture and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the traditional villages, coral reefs, and hot springs.

Conclusion

Miyako Islands is a hidden gem in Okinawa, Japan, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the best places to visit, try local cuisine, and immerse in the local culture. Whether traveling solo or with family and friends, Miyako Islands is a destination that will leave visitors with unforgettable memories.
