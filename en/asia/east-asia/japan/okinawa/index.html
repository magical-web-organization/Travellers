Introduction to Okinawa

Okinawa, a small cluster of islands located in the southernmost part of Japan, offers its visitors an abundance of natural beauty, rich cultural traditions, historical sites, and delicious cuisine. Famous for its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and lush green forests, Okinawa attracts tourists from all over the world.

The subtropical climate of the islands makes it an all-year-round destination, with temperatures ranging from 20-32°C (68-89°F). The main island is Okinawa Honto, and the other popular islands are Ishigaki, Miyako, and Taketomi.

List of Places to Visit in Okinawa

Here are some places you must visit while in Okinawa.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Shurijo Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that served as the residence of the Ryukyu Kingdom.
  2. Okinawa Prefectural Museum & Art Museum: Learn about the history and traditional culture of Okinawa.
  3. Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium: One of the largest aquariums in the world featuring a variety of marine life.

Natural Attractions of Okinawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Manzamo Cliff: A spectacular rock formation with an unobstructed view of the sea.
  2. Kouri Island: A heart-shaped island with a picturesque bridge and beach.
  3. Hiji Waterfall: A beautiful waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okinawa

Okinawa boasts of numerous hiking and biking trails, including:

  1. Yanbaru Forest Trail: Explore the dense subtropical forests and diverse wildlife of the northern part of Okinawa.
  2. Nakagusuku Castle Ruins Trail: A scenic hike leading to impressive castle ruins.
  3. Bise Fukugi Tree Road: A picturesque bike trip through tunnels of beautiful trees on the west coast.

Best Things to Do in Okinawa: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the beautiful coral reefs and diverse marine life.
  2. Parasailing: Enjoy the stunning views of Okinawa from the sky.
  3. Ryukyu Mura Village: Experience the traditional Ryukyu lifestyle with cultural performances and artisan workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Okinawa

Here’s the perfect way to experience Okinawa in a week.

Day Activity
Day 1-3 Okinawa Honto Island: Explore Shuri Castle, Kokusaidori Street, and enjoy a traditional Ryukyu show.
Day 4-5 Miyako Island: Relax on the beaches and visit Irabu Bridge, and Miyakojima City Museum.
Day 6-7 Ishigaki Island: Enjoy hiking to Kabira Bay and visiting Taketomi Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Okinawa

Okinawa offers a unique blend of Japanese, Chinese, and Southeast Asian cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Okinawa Soba: Wheat noodles served in a rich broth with pork.
  2. Goya Champuru: A stir-fry of goya (bitter melon), tofu, and pork.
  3. Beni Imo Tart: A purple sweet potato tart.

Local Beverages

  1. Awamori: A spirit made from rice unique to Okinawa.
  2. Orion Beer: The popular and refreshing local beer.

Where to Stay in Okinawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Okinawa offers a wide range of accommodation options such as:

  1. Resorts: Luxury stays with beautiful beachfront views.
  2. Guesthouses and Hostels: Affordable stays with a communal atmosphere and opportunities to meet other travelers.
  3. Ryokans: Traditional Japanese-style inns.

What to Buy in Okinawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Shisa: A popular Okinawan talisman.
  2. Ryukyu Glass: Handcrafted glassware and ornaments.
  3. Hanaori Textiles: Handwoven textiles with unique Okinawan designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Okinawa

Okinawa has a rich cultural heritage which includes:

  1. Okinawan Dance: Traditional dance performances showcasing the history and culture of the islands.
  2. Uchinaaguchi: A unique dialect spoken only in Okinawa.
  3. Festivals: Celebrations with traditional performances and food.

Best Time to Visit Okinawa

The best time to visit Okinawa is between March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild, and the beaches are not too crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okinawa

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine.
  3. Take off your shoes when entering a home or temple.

Don’t:

  1. Swim in restricted areas.
  2. Take photographs of locals without permission.
  3. Visit sacred places wearing revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Okinawa

Okinawa is a safe place for tourists, but it’s best to take extra precautions like not leaving your valuables unattended on the beach.

Health in Okinawa

Okinawa has modern healthcare facilities, including international clinics. However, it’s advisable to carry health insurance.

Getting to Okinawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okinawa

Naha Airport is the busiest airport located on Okinawa Honto Island, and Ishigaki Airport is another popular airport.

Bus Stations Near Okinawa

Okinawa has an extensive bus network that connects major cities and towns.

Other Options

Ferries and taxis are also available for getting around the islands.

How to Get Around in Okinawa

The most convenient way to explore Okinawa is by renting a car or scooter. Buses are also available but may not cover all destinations on the islands.

Where to Go Next from Okinawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Tokyo: Experience the modern and cultural hub of Japan.
  2. Kyoto: An epicenter of traditional Japanese culture and ancient temples.
  3. Taiwan: A neighboring country with beautiful natural scenery and delicious cuisine.

Conclusion

Okinawa offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience, with an abundance of natural beauty, cultural richness, and fun activities. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your perfect Okinawan holiday.
