Introduction to Zamami

Zamami Island is a small, picturesque island that is part of the Kerama Islands in Okinawa, Japan. It is a tropical paradise with crystal-clear waters and white-sand beaches that beckon visitors from around the world. Zamami is particularly famous for its coral reefs and incredible marine life, making it a popular destination for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

But there is more to Zamami than its beaches and underwater attractions. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and learning about local culture and customs, Zamami has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore all the top things to do in Zamami, as well as practical information on transportation, accommodation, food, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Zamami

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zamami may be a small island, but it is rich in culture and history. Here are a few places to visit that offer a glimpse into the island’s past and present:

  • The Zamami Island History and Culture Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning more about the history and culture of Zamami. The museum displays traditional tools, clothing, and other artifacts that were once used by the island’s inhabitants, as well as exhibits on the island’s history and way of life.

  • The Shibumi Observatory offers stunning views of the island and the ocean surrounding it. Visitors can also learn about astronomy and the constellations at the observatory.

  • The Akainikumaya Memorial Museum is a tribute to the late Akainikumaya, a famous local storyteller and musician. The museum displays his unique musical instruments and other artifacts related to his life and work. Visitors can also enjoy live performances and events at the museum.

Natural Attractions of Zamami: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zamami is well-known for its natural beauty, from its stunning beaches to its lush jungle interior. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions that Zamami has to offer:

  • Furuzamami Beach is considered by many to be the most beautiful beach in Zamami. The water here is clear and turquoise, making it ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

  • Aka Beach is another must-visit for beach lovers. The water here is a bright blue, and there are plenty of opportunities for water sports such as kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding.

  • Mount Takatsuki is a popular hiking spot on the island. From the top, hikers can enjoy panoramic views of Zamami and the surrounding ocean.

  • The Kerama Islands are home to an incredible array of marine life, such as sea turtles, dolphins, and whales. One of the best ways to explore this underwater paradise is by snorkeling or scuba diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zamami

Zamami is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • The Takatsuki Hiking Trail is a popular route that takes visitors to the top of Mount Takatsuki. The trail is relatively easy, making it suitable for hikers of all levels.

  • The Zamami Village Cycling Road is a scenic route that takes visitors through the heart of the island. The road is well-maintained and relatively flat, making it ideal for leisurely bike rides.

Best Things to Do in Zamami: Activities and Tours

Zamami offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Whether you’re interested in exploring the island’s natural beauty, learning about local culture, or experiencing some adrenaline-pumping adventure, there is something for everyone on Zamami. Here are some of the top activities and tours to try:

  • Dolphin and whale watching tours are a popular activity on Zamami. These tours take visitors out on boats to see these majestic creatures up close in their natural habitat.

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving are must-do activities for anyone visiting Zamami. There are plenty of opportunities to explore the island’s coral reefs and see an incredible variety of marine life.

  • Culture and history tours are also available on Zamami. These tours offer visitors the chance to learn about the island’s history, culture, and way of life.

  • Guided hiking tours are a great way to explore the island’s natural beauty. Experienced guides can take visitors on routes that are suitable for their fitness level and provide information about the flora and fauna along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zamami

If you’re planning to spend a week on Zamami, here is a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time on the island:

Day 1: Arrive on Zamami and settle into your accommodation. Spend the day relaxing on the beach and exploring Zamami Village.

Day 2: Take a snorkeling or scuba diving tour to explore the island’s coral reefs and see some of the amazing marine life that calls the Kerama Islands home.

Day 3: Explore the island’s natural beauty on a guided hiking tour. Visit Mount Takatsuki and enjoy the panoramic views from the top.

Day 4: Take a culture and history tour of the island. Visit the Zamami Island History and Culture Museum and the Akainikumaya Memorial Museum to learn about the island’s past and present.

Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the island at your own pace. Visit Furuzamami Beach and Aka Beach, two of the most beautiful beaches on the island.

Day 6: Take a dolphin and whale watching tour to see these incredible creatures up close in their natural habitat.

Day 7: Spend your last day on Zamami relaxing on the beach and enjoying the island’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Zamami

Zamami is famous for its fresh seafood and unique local dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Okinawan Soba: A unique twist on traditional Japanese soba, this dish is made with a mixture of wheat and tapioca flour, giving it a chewy texture.

  • Goya Champuru: A stir-fry made with goya, a bitter melon that is a staple of Okinawan cuisine. The dish is typically made with tofu, eggs, and pork.

  • Taco Rice: A fusion dish that combines Tex-Mex style tacos with Japanese rice. The dish is topped with seasoned ground beef, shredded cheese, lettuce, and tomato.

Local Beverages

  • Awamori: A traditional Okinawan distilled spirit made from long-grain rice. It is typically served straight or with water or ice.

  • Orion Beer: A popular beer brand in Okinawa, Orion Beer is a light and refreshing lager that pairs well with local seafood.

Where to Stay in Zamami: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zamami offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious beachfront resorts. Here are some top choices:

  • Pension Oukoku: A cozy guesthouse located just a few minutes’ walk from Zamami Beach. The guesthouse offers both private rooms and dormitory-style accommodation.

  • Kerama Terrace: A luxurious beachfront resort that offers stunning views of the ocean. The resort has a private beach, restaurants, and a swimming pool.

  • Guesthouse Iyonchi: A budget-friendly guesthouse located close to the port. The guesthouse offers dormitory-style accommodation and has a communal kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Zamami: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Zamami, here are a few ideas:

  • Shisa: These traditional Okinawan statues are often placed on rooftops to ward off evil spirits. They come in various sizes and designs, making them a great souvenir or gift.

  • Ryukyu Glass: This colorful glassware is made in Okinawa using traditional glassblowing techniques. Visitors can watch master glassblowers at work and purchase unique pieces to take home.

  • Awamori: A bottle of locally distilled awamori makes for a great souvenir or gift for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Zamami

Zamami has a unique local culture that is influenced by its location in Okinawa. Here are a few customs to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.

  • Bow to show respect when meeting someone for the first time.

  • Be polite and refrain from speaking loudly or causing a disturbance in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Zamami

The best time to visit Zamami is between March and November when the weather is warm and sunny, and the waters are calm and clear for water activities. However, keep in mind that peak tourism season is between July and August, so expect crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zamami

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Be mindful of your impact on the environment.

  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt public spaces or noise levels.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Ignore local customs and cultural norms.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zamami

Zamami is a very safe place for visitors, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take normal precautions when traveling in any new place, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unsafe areas at night.

Health in Zamami

Visitors to Zamami should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella, as well as Hepatitis A and B. They should also take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should consult with their doctor before travelling and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Zamami: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zamami

The nearest airport to Zamami is Naha Airport in Okinawa, which is the main gateway to the Kerama Islands. There are multiple flights per day from Naha to Zamami on a variety of airlines, including Japan Airlines and ANA.

Bus Stations Near Zamami

Zamami is accessible by bus from Naha, Okinawa. The bus takes approximately 2 hours and 30 minutes and drops off passengers at the Zamami Port.

Other Options

Visitors to Okinawa can also access Zamami via ferry from Naha Port. The ferry takes approximately 2 hours and runs multiple times per day.

How to Get Around in Zamami

The best way to get around Zamami is on foot or by bicycle. Most of the island’s top attractions are within walking or cycling distance from Zamami Village. Visitors can also take a taxi to reach more remote areas of the island.

Where to Go Next from Zamami: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zamami is part of the beautiful Kerama Islands, which are home to several other stunning destinations, including:

  • Tokashiki Island: Known for its beautiful white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters, Tokashiki Island is a great day-trip destination from Zamami.

  • Aka Island: Aka Island is another beautiful island in the Kerama Islands chain. It is known for its excellent snorkeling and diving spots.

Conclusion

Zamami Island is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan, particularly those who love the great outdoors and want to experience a unique local culture. With its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse range of activities, Zamami has something for everyone.
