Introduction to Kumejima

Kumejima is a picturesque island located in the Okinawa Prefecture of Japan. It is considered as one of the hidden gems of Okinawa, thanks to its pristine beaches, lush greenery, and unspoiled nature. The island is also home to some cultural attractions, such as museums and historical places, that offer a glimpse into the ancient Ryukyuan culture.

Tourists can enjoy various outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, and water sports. You can also explore the unique local culture, try authentic Okinawan dishes, and shop for souvenirs.

List of Places to Visit in Kumejima

Kumejima has a wide range of attractions that cater to all types of travelers. From picturesque beaches to hiking trails and historical ruins, here’s a list of top places to visit in Kumejima:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kume Island Cultural History Museum: This museum showcases the rich Ryukyuan history of Kumejima, with exhibits on local traditions, crafts, and festivals.

  2. Hatenohama Reef: This area is home to the famous “sandbar” - a stretch of pure white sand that emerges during low tide.

  3. Nakadomari Site: Explore the historical ruins of Nakadomari, an ancient Ryukyuan port that dates back to the 14th century.

  4. Torinji Temple: Admire the unique architecture of this temple, which features a blend of Chinese and Japanese elements.

Natural Attractions of Kumejima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tatami Rocks: These natural basalt formations are a popular spot for photography and offer stunning views of the ocean.

  2. Kumejima Island Beach: This beach is a perfect spot for sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling, thanks to its crystal clear waters and gorgeous coral reefs.

  3. Mifuga Rock: Hike to the summit of this rock to enjoy panoramic views of the island and the surrounding sea.

  4. Kumejima Sea Turtle Park: This park is home to various species of sea turtles and offers opportunities for snorkeling and guided tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumejima

Kumejima offers several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the island’s unique landscape and natural beauty. Some popular routes include:

  1. Hiking trail to Mifuga Rock: This trail offers a moderate-level trek that culminates in breathtaking views of the island.

  2. Bike tour around the island: Rent a bike and explore the island at your own pace, visiting beaches, rock formations, and other natural attractions along the way.

Best Things to Do in Kumejima: Activities and Tours

Kumejima has plenty of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. From water sports to cultural tours, here are some of the best things to do in Kumejima:

  1. Snorkeling and scuba diving: Explore the vibrant coral reefs and marine life of Kumejima’s clear waters.

  2. Glass-bottom boat tours: Take a tour in a glass-bottom boat to see the underwater world without getting wet.

  3. Cultural tours: Learn about the Ryukyuan culture and history of Kumejima by taking a tour of historical sites and museums.

  4. Food tours: Taste authentic Okinawan dishes and local specialties, such as Orion beer, soki soba, and sea grapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kumejima

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Kumejima that covers the best places to visit and things to do:

Day 1: Visit the Cultural History Museum and Hatenohama Reef

Day 2: Explore the Tatami Rocks and Kume Island Beach

Day 3: Take a bike tour around the island, stopping at Mifuga Rock and other scenic spots

Day 4: Visit the Nakadomari Site and Torinji Temple

Day 5: Go snorkeling or scuba diving at Kumejima Sea Turtle Park

Day 6: Take a glass-bottom boat tour and visit other natural attractions, such as Kanegusuku Beach and Tamatorizaki Observatory

Day 7: Experience the local culture through a food tour and traditional Ryukyuan music and dance performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Kumejima

Kumejima has a diverse food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties and traditional dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Soki Soba: A signature Okinawan dish that consists of wheat noodles in pork broth, served with tender pork ribs and green onion

  2. Taco-rice: A fusion dish that combines Tex-Mex flavors with traditional Okinawan rice and vegetables, topped with seasoned ground beef and cheese

  3. Okinawan-style tempura: Fried seafood and vegetables, coated in a light batter made of wheat flour and Okinawan black sugar

Local Beverages

  1. Awamori: A distilled liquor made from Thai rice and unique to Okinawa

  2. Orion Beer: A popular local beer that pairs well with Okinawan cuisine

  3. Goya juice: A refreshing drink made from the juice of bitter melons, which are grown in Okinawa and known for their health benefits

Where to Stay in Kumejima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kumejima has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  1. Kumejima Eef Beach Hotel: A beachside resort that offers stunning ocean views and private balconies

  2. Kumejima Guest House: A budget-friendly option that provides simple, clean rooms and communal spaces for socializing with other travelers

  3. Y’s Cabin & Hotel Kumejima: A stylish hotel that features modern amenities, such as a fitness center and rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Kumejima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kumejima is known for its traditional crafts and local specialties, such as:

  1. Ryukyuan pottery: Unique ceramic art pieces that reflect the island’s vibrant culture and traditions

  2. Bingata fabric: A distinctive textile that is dyed and woven using traditional methods and natural materials

  3. Okinawan black sugar: A type of brown sugar that is known for its rich flavor and health benefits

Local Culture and Customs of Kumejima

Kumejima has a distinct Ryukyuan culture that is characterized by its history, traditions, and local customs. Some unique aspects of Kumejima’s culture include:

  1. Karate-do: A traditional martial art that originated in Okinawa and is still practiced on the island today

  2. Eisa dance: A colorful and lively dance performance that features taiko drums, bamboo flutes, and traditional costumes

  3. Shishi-mai: A traditional lion dance, which is performed during festivals and other special occasions

Best Time to Visit Kumejima

The best time to visit Kumejima is from March to June or from September to December, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The island can get crowded during peak season from July to August, so it’s recommended to book in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumejima

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Kumejima:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions

  2. Learn basic Japanese phrases, such as “hello” and “thank you”

  3. Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  1. Take photos of people or sacred places without permission

  2. Wear shoes indoors

  3. Litter or damage the environment

Safety and Health

Kumejima is a safe and peaceful island, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. However, it’s always important to take general safety precautions, such as securing your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Kumejima

  1. Keep valuables in a secure place or hotel safe

  2. Be aware of traffic and follow traffic rules while cycling or driving

  3. Check weather forecasts and avoid swimming in rough seas

Health in Kumejima

  1. Take measures to avoid sunburn and heat stroke, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated

  2. Check with your doctor prior to leaving the country to see if any specific vaccinations or medical precautions are necessary

Getting to Kumejima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kumejima is accessible by air or sea, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Kumejima

  1. Naha Airport: The closest international airport, located on Okinawa mainland.

  2. Kumejima Airport: Small regional airport, serves only domestic flights.

Boat Services

  1. Ferry: A car ferry that travels from Naha to Kumejima in around 4 hours.

  2. High-speed boat: A quicker option that runs between Naha and Kumejima, taking only 55 minutes.

How to Get Around in Kumejima

The best way to explore Kumejima is by renting a car, bike or scooter. Alternatively, visitors can also use local buses or take taxis to get around the island.

Where to Go Next from Kumejima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Kumejima, visitors can consider the following destinations for a rewarding side trip:

  1. Zamami Island: A nearby island renowned for its clear waters, coral reefs, and sea turtles.

  2. Okinawa Main Island: This island offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, as well as shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Kumejima is a hidden gem in Okinawa that offers a plethora of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local cuisine. With its lush landscape, stunning beaches, and unique traditions, Kumejima offers a rich travel experience that is steeped in Ryukyuan culture and history. So pack your bags and explore the scenic charm of this beautiful island.
