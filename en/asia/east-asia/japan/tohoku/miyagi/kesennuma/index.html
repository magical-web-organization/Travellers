Introduction to Kesennuma

Kesennuma is a coastal city located in Miyagi Prefecture, in the Tohoku region of Japan. Known for its stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, Kesennuma is a popular destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and tourists looking to experience Japan off the beaten path. The city is renowned for its seafood, particularly its fresh tuna, and is home to numerous cultural attractions and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Kesennuma

Here are some of the top places to visit in Kesennuma:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kesennuma Municipal Museum
  • Kesennuma Fishing Museum
  • Shakado Museum of Jomon Culture
  • Kesennuma Castle Ruins

Natural Attractions of Kesennuma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okama Crater
  • Mount Hiyoriyama
  • Kinkasan Island
  • Karakuwa Peninsula

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kesennuma

Kesennuma is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include the Karakuwa Hiking Trail, Hamaori to Karakuwa Cycling Road, and Mount Hiyoriyama Trail.

Best Things to Do in Kesennuma: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Kesennuma, including:

  • Visit Kesennuma Port
  • Go on a tuna-fishing tour
  • Take a scenic drive along the Sanriku Coast
  • Attend a local festival or event

Week-Long Itinerary for Kesennuma

For those planning to spend a week in Kesennuma, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit Kesennuma Port and Municipal Museum
2 Go on a tuna-fishing tour and visit Himekami Shrine
3 Explore the Karakuwa Peninsula and go hiking
4 Take a day trip to Mount Hiyoriyama
5 Visit the Shakado Museum of Jomon Culture and Kesennuma Castle Ruins
6 Go on a scenic drive along the Sanriku Coast
7 Relax at Jodogahama Beach and explore Oshika Peninsula

Food and Drink Scene of Kesennuma

Must-Try Dishes

Kesennuma is known for its fresh seafood, and some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Maguro (tuna)
  • Kaki (oysters)
  • Uni (sea urchin)
  • Hotate (scallops)

Local Beverages

Some of the local beverages to try in Kesennuma include:

  • Sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • Shochu (Japanese distilled alcohol)
  • Beer from Kesennuma Brewery

Where to Stay in Kesennuma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Kesennuma, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top hotels in Kesennuma include the Hotel Oshohei, Kyukamura Kesennuma-Ohshima, and Miyako Hotel Kesennuma.

What to Buy in Kesennuma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Kesennuma include:

  • Maguro no shioyaki (grilled salted tuna)
  • Kesennuma lacquerware
  • Kesennuma miso
  • Kesennuma beer

Local Culture and Customs of Kesennuma

Kesennuma has a rich cultural history, and visitors can explore local customs and traditions by attending a local festival or event, such as the Sanriku Fukko Festival or the Kesennuma Matsuri.

Best Time to Visit Kesennuma

The best time to visit Kesennuma is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. The cherry blossoms in spring and the colorful foliage in fall provide stunning backdrops for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kesennuma

Do:

  • Try the local seafood
  • Take in the stunning coastal views
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or hiking trails
  • Ignore safety warnings during natural disasters
  • Touch or disturb wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Kesennuma

Kesennuma is generally considered a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of potential natural disasters, such as earthquakes, tsunamis, and typhoons.

Health in Kesennuma

There are several hospitals and clinics in Kesennuma, and medical care is generally of a high standard. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance in case of unexpected illness or injury.

Getting to Kesennuma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kesennuma

The closest airport to Kesennuma is Sendai Airport, which is approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kesennuma

Kesennuma is served by several bus routes, including the Sendai-Kesennuma Line and the Tokyo-Kesennuma Line.

Other Options

There are several train stations near Kesennuma, including Nobiru Station and Kesennuma Station. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Kesennuma

The best way to get around Kesennuma is by car, but visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and trains. Taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Kesennuma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Kesennuma include the Matsushima Bay area, Akiu Onsen hot springs, and the city of Sendai.

Conclusion

Kesennuma offers a unique combination of stunning natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious seafood. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, Kesennuma has something for everyone. Plan your next trip to this hidden gem in Japan and experience everything that it has to offer.
