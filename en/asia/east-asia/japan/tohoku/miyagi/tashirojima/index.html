Introduction to Tashirojima

Tashirojima Island, also known as “Cat Island”, is a small and serene island in the Miyagi Prefecture of Japan’s Tohoku region. The island is home to a population of around 1000 people and the number of cats is more than that of the residents. The cats have become a popular attraction and a symbol of the island, which has drawn many tourists and cat lovers in recent years. The island is also known for its scenic landscapes, numerous fishing villages, and traditional Japanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Tashirojima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tashirojima Island offers an array of cultural attractions and landmarks to explore for visitors. Among them, the Odomari Fishing Village is one of the most popular places that provides a glimpse into the historic fishing industry on the island. Visitors can learn about the traditional methods of fishing and the way of life of the fishermen at the Odomari Fishing Museum.

Another notable attraction is the Tashirojima Island History and Folklore Museum that showcases the history of Tashirojima Island and its traditions. The museum has a rich collection of artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays that highlight the local history and customs.

Natural Attractions of Tashirojima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The pristine natural beauty of Tashirojima Island is its key attraction that draws visitors from around the world. The island is surrounded by crystal clear waters, scenic coastlines, and lush green forests. The best way to explore the island is by walking or cycling along the hiking trails that run through the lush landscapes.

The Nitoda Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches on the island, which offers stunning views and perfect relaxation. The beach water is shallow and warm and ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. Besides, the island offers numerous other activities such as fishing, kayaking, and boat tours to explore the surrounding pristine waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tashirojima

Tashirojima Island is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering an array of hiking and biking trails that take visitors through the island’s stunning natural landscapes. The most popular hike is the 5-kilometer trail that runs from the Kashima Port to the Nitoda Beach, passing through several picturesque viewpoints and scenic spots.

Another popular route is the cycling path that takes visitors to the southern part of the island, where they can enjoy stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, quaint fishing villages, and lush greenery. Visitors can rent bicycles from several rental shops available near the ports.

Best Things to Do in Tashirojima: Activities and Tours

Tashirojima Island has a range of fun activities and tours for visitors to explore and experience Japanese culture. One of the best activities that visitors can do is cat-watching, as the island is famous for the numerous felines wandering throughout. The best time to spot them is early in the morning or evening.

Visitors can also take a boat tour around the island, which offers stunning views of the coastline and nature. There are several tour operators providing such services at affordable prices. Additionally, visitors can enjoy fresh seafood, especially catfish dishes, and a variety of traditional Japanese cuisine at the island’s restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Tashirojima

Must-Try Dishes

Tashirojima Island is known for its fresh seafood and delicious traditional Japanese cuisine. Visitors must try some of the local specialties, such as catfish dishes, which are a must-try for seafood lovers. The island’s small restaurants and cafes offer a mix of traditional and modern dishes, like rice bowls with freshly caught seafood, tempura dishes, and more.

For those looking for snacks, the island’s specialty is the juwari-soba, a type of soba noodle made with 100% buckwheat and local soy sauce, which has a unique and distinct flavor.

Local Beverages

Visitors should try the local beverages when exploring Tashirojima Island. The most popular drink on the island is sake, a traditional Japanese rice wine produced locally. Visitors can sample a variety of flavors at the local bars and shops throughout the island.

Where to Stay in Tashirojima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tashirojima Island offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, offering comfortable and affordable stays. Visitors can choose from traditional Japanese-style ryokans and guesthouses to modern hotels and hostels. Most of the accommodations are located near the ports, offering easy access to nearby attractions and activities.

What to Buy in Tashirojima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find several shops and stores throughout Tashirojima Island that offer a mix of traditional and modern craft items, souvenirs, and local products. Visitors can purchase cat-themed merchandise, such as keychains, magnets, and plush toys, which are popular souvenirs. The island’s specialty products include soy sauce, wakame seaweed, and other seafood-related products that make excellent gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tashirojima

Tashirojima Island has a rich and unique culture that has been preserved for centuries, providing visitors with a glimpse into the local traditions and customs. One of the notable customs on the island is the cat worshipping culture, which dates back to ancient times. The local residents have a deep love and respect for the feline population, which they consider protectors of the island and bring good luck to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Tashirojima

The best time to visit Tashirojima Island is during the spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) months when the weather is mild, and the island is at its best scenery. Visitors can enjoy comfortable temperatures, clear skies, and a beautiful landscape as the leaves change colors. June to August is the summer season, which is the busiest time on the island and has higher temperatures and humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tashirojima

Do:

  • Respect the cats and their spaces.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Follow the local customs and traditions.
  • Take time to explore the scenic beauty of the island.

Don’t:

  • Feed the cats anything toxic or harmful.
  • Leave any litter or trash anywhere on the island.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Disturb the peaceful environment of the island by making loud noises.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tashirojima

Tashirojima Island is a safe destination for visitors, and there are no major safety concerns related to crimes or theft. However, visitors are advised to be cautious when exploring the island’s natural landscapes and hiking trails, especially during rainy or windy conditions, to avoid any accidents.

Health in Tashirojima

Visitors are advised to carry necessary medications and consult a doctor before visiting Tashirojima Island. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with consuming raw or undercooked seafood, and stick to reliable restaurants and eateries to avoid any health concerns.

Getting to Tashirojima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tashirojima

The nearest airport to Tashirojima Island is the Sendai Airport, which is located approximately 60 km away from the island. Visitors can take a taxi or a shuttle bus to catch a ferry from Ishinomaki Port, which takes approximately 45 minutes to reach the island.

Bus Stations Near Tashirojima

The Miyagi Kotsu Bus Station is located at Ishinomaki Port, which is the hub for bus services accessing Tashirojima Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also take advantage of the ferry services that operate between Tashirojima Island and other surrounding islands or coastal cities. The ferries run frequently during the day and offer affordable transportation options for visitors.

How to Get Around in Tashirojima

Walking and cycling are the best ways to explore Tashirojima Island’s scenic beauty and natural landscapes. Visitors can rent bicycles from several shops available near the ports, and also take advantage of the local taxi services to access more remote parts of the island.

Where to Go Next from Tashirojima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tashirojima Island is surrounded by numerous islands and coastal cities in Miyagi Prefecture, which offer unique cultural and scenic attractions. Visitors can explore nearby attractions such as Ishinomaki City, Matsushima Bay, and Osaki Hachiman Shrine for an off-island adventure.

Conclusion

Tashirojima Island is a hidden gem located in the heart of Tohoku, offering unique cultural and natural attractions that visitors can explore and enjoy. Whether you are interested in cat watching, outdoor adventures, traditional Japanese culture, or local cuisine, Tashirojima Island has something for everyone. So plan your visit today and experience the unique charm of Cat Island!
