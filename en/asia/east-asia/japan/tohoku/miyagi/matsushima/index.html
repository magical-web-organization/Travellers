Introduction to Matsushima

Matsushima is a picturesque town located in Miyagi Prefecture, Japan, and is famous for its breathtakingly beautiful bay, scattered with pine-clad islets. Matsushima is known to be one of the top three most scenic spots in Japan, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Matsushima

Matsushima has something to offer for everyone - from its historical landmarks to its natural attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zuiganji Temple

Located in the heart of Matsushima, Zuiganji Temple is one of the town’s most important cultural landmarks. The temple was founded in 828 and has since become one of the most visited temples in Japan. Inside the temple, visitors can see beautiful paintings and carvings that are considered national treasures.

Godaido Temple

Godaido Temple is a small, picturesque temple located on a small island in Matsushima Bay. Visitors can reach the temple via a short bridge and can soak up stunning views of the bay and the town.

Entsuin Temple

Entsuin Temple is known for its beautiful garden and intricate wooden carvings. The temple was originally built in 1646 and has since been restored to its former glory. Visitors can walk through the temple and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere while admiring the beautiful scenery.

Natural Attractions of Matsushima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Matsushima Bay Cruise

One of the best ways to enjoy the beauty of Matsushima is by taking a relaxing boat trip around the bay. The cruise takes visitors around the numerous islets that dot the bay, offering stunning views of the pine-clad rocks.

Fukuura Island

Fukuura Island is a small island located in the middle of Matsushima Bay. Visitors can reach the island by boat and then can explore the island’s walking trails, scenic overlooks, and small shrine.

Takagiyama Observation Deck

Located on top of a hill close to Zuiganji Temple, Takagiyama Observation Deck offers panoramic views of Matsushima Bay and its surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matsushima

Matsushima has plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking and biking.

Tanesashi Coastline Trail

Tanesashi Coastline Trail is a beautiful hiking trail that offers stunning views of the ocean and coastline. The trail is 16 kilometers long and takes hikers through scenic forests and along the coast.

Matsushima Bay Cycling Road

The cycling road runs for around 12 kilometers and offers scenic views of the bay and its surroundings. Visitors can rent bicycles from local rental shops and explore the area at a leisurely pace.

Best Things to Do in Matsushima: Activities and Tours

Matsushima Bay Oyster Farm Tour

Matsushima is known for its delicious oysters. Visitors can take a tour of one of the local oyster farms and learn about the history of oyster farming in the area.

Tea Ceremony Experience

Visitors can participate in a traditional Japanese tea ceremony at one of the local tea houses in Matsushima. The ceremony is a great way to experience Japanese culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matsushima

Day 1-2: Visit temples and shrines, including Zuiganji Temple, Godaido Temple, and Entsuin Temple. Take a Matsushima Bay cruise to see the beauty of the bay and its surroundings.

Day 3-4: Take a hike or bike ride along one of the scenic trails in Matsushima. Visit the Tanesashi Coastline Trail or Matsushima Bay Cycling Road.

Day 5-6: Take a tour of one of the local oyster farms and try some of the local delicacies. Experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony at a local tea house in Matsushima.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing and exploring the town. Visit local shops to buy souvenirs and local products to bring back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Matsushima

Matsushima is known for its delicious seafood, especially oysters. Visitors should try these local dishes during their stay.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: Matsushima is famous for its oysters, which can be enjoyed raw, grilled, or in a variety of other dishes.
  • Sashimi: Fresh seafood sashimi is a popular dish in Matsushima, and visitors can enjoy a variety of seafood options.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Matsushima is known for its delicious sake, which is made using the pure waters of Matsushima Bay.

Where to Stay in Matsushima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matsushima Guesthouse

Matsushima Guesthouse is a cozy and affordable option for visitors looking to stay in Matsushima. The guesthouse offers traditional Japanese-style rooms and a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

Hotel Matsushima Taikanso

Hotel Matsushima Taikanso is a luxurious hotel located in a scenic area of Matsushima. The hotel offers breathtaking views of Matsushima Bay, and guests can enjoy the hotel’s public hot springs.

What to Buy in Matsushima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matsushima Bay Area Souvenirs

Visitors can find a variety of souvenirs in Matsushima, including local products such as oysters, sake, and tea. Other popular souvenirs include traditional Japanese clothing and accessories, like kimonos and yukatas.

Local Culture and Customs of Matsushima

Matsushima has a rich history and culture, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Visitors should remove their shoes when entering temples and other cultural landmarks and should be mindful of their behavior in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Matsushima

The best time to visit Matsushima is during the fall or spring, when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. The fall foliage in Matsushima is especially stunning and should not be missed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matsushima

Do:

  • Remove shoes before entering temples or traditional buildings.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try local specialties like oysters and sake.

Don’t:

  • Bring food or drink into temples or other cultural sites.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Take photographs of people without asking for permission first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matsushima

Matsushima is a safe town, and crime rates are low. Visitors should still take normal precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Matsushima

Matsushima has excellent medical facilities and hospitals, and visitors who require medical assistance should not have any issues in getting the necessary care.

Getting to Matsushima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matsushima

The closest airport to Matsushima is Sendai Airport, which is located approximately 50 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Matsushima

There are several local bus stations in Matsushima, including Matsushima Station and Matsushima-Kaigan Station. Visitors can take local buses to explore the area and reach nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Matsushima, as there is a train station located in the town.

How to Get Around in Matsushima

Visitors can explore Matsushima by foot or by bike. Bicycles can be rented from local shops, and there are plenty of walking trails and pathways throughout the town. Taxis are also available for visitors who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Matsushima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sendai

Sendai is the largest city in Miyagi Prefecture and is famous for its cosmopolitan atmosphere and its beautiful parks and gardens.

Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi is a small town located approximately 50 kilometers away from Matsushima. The town is home to several important historical landmarks, including the Chuson-ji Temple and the Motsu-ji Temple.

Conclusion

Matsushima is a beautiful and unique town in Japan that offers visitors a wealth of cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and outdoor activities. From its historical landmarks to its delicious cuisine, Matsushima has something to offer for everyone.
