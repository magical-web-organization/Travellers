Introduction to Yurihonjo

Located in the mountainous region of Tohoku, Yurihonjo is a small city that offers a serene atmosphere and rich cultural heritage. The city is situated in Akita Prefecture, Japan, and is home to picturesque landscapes, historical landmarks, and friendly locals.

If you’re planning a trip to Japan and looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination, Yurihonjo is definitely worth adding to your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Yurihonjo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yurihonjo City Museum of History and Folklore: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the city’s cultural history, including artifacts, traditional clothing, and art.
  • Yurihonjo City Cultural Center: This is a popular attraction in the city, housing many cultural centers such as art, history, library and cinema.
  • Aoyagi Samurai Manor Museum: This samurai estate has been well-preserved and is one of the best samurai houses in Japan. It provides an inside look into the life of a samurai.

Natural Attractions of Yurihonjo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shinminato Kitaura: This beautiful spot offers amazing views of the Sea of Japan and is a great place for swimming, surfing, and fishing.
  • Yuriho Beach: Head to this secluded beach for a peaceful day out. Visitors can enjoy swimming and sunbathing, or simply relax on the sandy shore.
  • Chokai Blue Line: This mountain road offers stunning views of Chokai Mountain, one of the most famous mountain ranges in Japan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yurihonjo

  • Mt. Chokai: This mountain is the biggest attraction in Yurihonjo and is great for hiking. There are various trails leading up to the summit, offering magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kugami Road: This picturesque road is great for cycling or a scenic drive. The road passes through beautiful rice fields, forests, and quaint villages.

Best Things to Do in Yurihonjo: Activities and Tours

  • Yurihonjo Kiku Matsuri: This is a popular festival held in Yurihonjo every October. The festival features beautiful chrysanthemum displays, parades, and food vendors.
  • Rural Life Experience: Get a taste of the local rural life by visiting local farms and trying your hand at planting rice, harvesting crops, and milking cows.
  • Onsen: Yurihonjo is famous for its hot springs, with many ryokans (traditional Japanese inns) and hotels offering access to them.

Food and Drink Scene of Yurihonjo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiritanpo: This is a local dish made of mashed rice, wrapped around a stick and grilled over charcoal. It is typically served with a miso-based soup and grilled meat or fish.
  • Iburigakko: This is a popular snack in Yurihonjo, made by pickling Japanese radish and then smoking it with cherry wood chips.
  • Hatahata-zushi: This is a type of sushi made with sandfish, a type of fish found in the Sea of Japan, and pickled vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Akita Prefecture is famous for its sake. You can visit a sake brewery in Yurihonjo to learn about the brewing process and taste different types of sake.
  • Kawatsuru: This is a locally-produced brand of shochu, a distilled liquor made from rice, barley, or sweet potatoes.

Where to Stay in Yurihonjo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Yachi Onsen: This is a popular hot spring resort with ryokans offering traditional Japanese-style rooms and meals.
  • Hotel Route-Inn Yurihonjo: This is a modern hotel with comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • Kugami Onsen Hotel: This onsen hotel is located in a peaceful area and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Yurihonjo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Akita Cedar Products: This is a specialty product of Akita Prefecture, made from locally-produced cedar wood. You can find various items made of cedar, such as furniture, tableware, and accessories.
  • Hatahata-zushi: This pickled fish is a popular local specialty and is a perfect gift to bring back home.
  • Sake: As mentioned before, Akita Prefecture is known for its sake. You can find different types of sake to bring back home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Yurihonjo

  • Onsen Etiquette: When visiting an onsen, it is important to follow proper etiquette. This includes washing yourself thoroughly before entering the hot spring and not wearing any clothing or swimwear while in the bath.
  • Respect for Nature: Yurihonjo is known for its beautiful natural scenery, so it is important to respect the environment and follow the rules for outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Yurihonjo

The best time to visit Yurihonjo is during the autumn months (September-November), when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is mild. Additionally, the Yurihonjo Kiku Matsuri takes place in October which is a highlight of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yurihonjo

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the environment and follow outdoor rules
  • Attend festivals and cultural events

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese-style accommodations.
  • Speak loudly in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Yurihonjo

Yurihonjo is a generally safe city with a low crime rate. However, always take basic safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings when exploring.

Health in Yurihonjo

Yurihonjo is known for having clean air and water. Additionally, the medical facilities in the city are well-equipped and provide quality care.

Getting to Yurihonjo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yurihonjo

  • Akita Airport
  • Odate-Noshiro Airport

Bus Stations Near Yurihonjo

  • Yurihonjo Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is JR Oga Line, Yurihonjo Station.
  • Car: You can rent a car from the nearby airports or cities and drive to Yurihonjo.

How to Get Around in Yurihonjo

The best way to get around in Yurihonjo is by car or taxi as the city is spread out and the public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Yurihonjo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Akita: This prefectural capital is only a short train ride away from Yurihonjo and is known for its beautiful nature, onsen, and festivals.
  • Matsushima: This is a coastal town known for its beautiful scenery, with various islands, pine forests and temples.
  • Hirosaki: Located a few hours away by train, Hirosaki is famous for its cherry blossoms and ancient samurai district.

Conclusion

Yurihonjo offers a unique travel experience for those looking to explore Japan’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you’re interested in hiking, soaking in hot springs, or sampling local cuisine, Yurihonjo has something to offer for everyone.
