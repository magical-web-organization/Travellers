Misato (Akita) is a picturesque small town located in the northwestern part of Akita prefecture, in the Tohoku region of Japan. With a population of less than 10,000 people, Misato is a hidden gem that offers visitors unique cultural experiences, stunning natural scenery, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at all the places to visit, things to do, and local customs and traditions that make Misato (Akita) a must-see destination in Japan.

Introduction to Misato (Akita)

Misato is known for its rich history and traditional way of life. The town was founded in 1889 during the Meiji period, and many ancient temples and shrines still remain as a testament to its cultural past.

One of the reasons why Misato is so special is its unique location surrounded by mountains and forests. Outdoor enthusiasts will find a plethora of hiking and biking trails, as well as scenic spots such as lakes, hot springs, and waterfalls.

With friendly locals, great food, and plenty of cultural and natural attractions, Misato (Akita) is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list.

List of Places to Visit in Misato (Akita)

Whether you’re interested in learning about the town’s history, immersing yourself in nature, or simply relaxing in a soothing onsen (hot spring), Misato has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions and places to visit in Misato (Akita).

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Misato Machinaka History Museum - Learn about the history and culture of Misato through various artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits.

  • Yabasekihachiman Shrine - This ancient Shinto shrine was built over 1,000 years ago and is a popular spot for worship and relaxation.

  • Ritsuin Temple - This Zen Buddhist temple, built in the early 1600s, features a beautiful garden and several historic buildings.

  • Kakunodate Samurai District - Just a short drive from Misato, this historic district features preserved samurai houses and museums that offer a glimpse into Japan’s feudal past.

Natural Attractions of Misato (Akita): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sugata-kyo Gorge - This scenic gorge, located in the nearby Akita prefecture, boasts crystal clear water, steep cliffs, and spectacular seasonal foliage.

  • Mount Moriyoshi - Hiking to the top of this mountain provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Oyasukyo Onsen - These natural hot springs are said to have healing properties and offer a relaxing break amidst beautiful mountain scenery.

  • Kankodai Observation Deck - This observation deck provides panoramic views of the Sea of Japan and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Misato (Akita)

Misato (Akita) is a great location for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Here are some of the best trails and routes to explore.

  • Mount Moriyoshi Trail - This moderate trail takes about 3 hours to complete and offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

  • Hoshino Bairin Nature Trail - This easy trail takes about an hour to complete and leads visitors through a grove of cherry trees that bloom in the spring.

  • Akita Inariyama Trail - This challenging trail takes about 6 hours to complete and leads hikers to the summit of Mount Inariyama, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Misato (Akita): Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Misato. Here are some of our top recommendations.

  • Attend a traditional Matsuri festival - These lively festivals, held throughout the year, feature music, dancing, and local food and drinks.

  • Take a cooking class - Learn how to make traditional Akita cuisine, such as kiritampo (grilled rice cakes wrapped in cedar leaves) and Hatahata-zushi (pickled sandfish sushi).

  • Visit a sake brewery - Akita is known for its high-quality sake, and several breweries in Misato offer tours and tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Misato (Akita)

If you have a week to spend in Misato, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Misato and explore the town’s historic sites, such as the Misato Machinaka History Museum and Yabasekihachiman Shrine.

Day 2: Take a short drive to Kakunodate Samurai District and spend the day exploring its historic samurai houses and museums.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Mount Moriyoshi and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 4: Visit Oyasukyo Onsen for a relaxing soak in the natural hot springs.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Akita cuisine.

Day 6: Attend a Matsuri festival and experience the local culture and traditions.

Day 7: Visit a sake brewery and try some of Akita’s finest sake before departing from Misato.

Food and Drink Scene of Misato (Akita)

Akita cuisine is known for its simple yet flavorful dishes that make use of local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Misato.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hinai Jidori chicken - This locally-raised chicken is known for its tender meat and rich flavor.

  • Inaniwa Udon - These thin, chewy noodles are a specialty of Akita prefecture and can be served hot or cold.

  • Hatahata-zushi - Pickled sandfish sushi that is a regional specialty of Akita.

Local Beverages

  • Akita sake - Akita has a long tradition of sake brewing, and there are many different varieties to try.

  • Tazawako beer - This local beer is brewed using natural water from the Tazawako Lake in Akita.

Where to Stay in Misato (Akita): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Misato has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some options to consider.

  • Hotel Green Plaza Misato - This hotel features comfortable rooms, a hot spring bath, and traditional Japanese meals.

  • Misato Guesthouse Warabi - This cozy guesthouse offers private and dormitory-style rooms, a shared kitchen, and free bike rentals.

What to Buy in Misato (Akita): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some suggestions.

  • Traditional Akita crafts - Misato is known for its lacquerware, pottery, and woven textiles.

  • Akita cedar products - These wooden products, such as chopsticks and tea cups, are made from the aromatic Akita cedar tree.

Local Culture and Customs of Misato (Akita)

Misato residents are known for their warm hospitality and strong connection to their local culture and traditions. Here are some customs and etiquette tips to be aware of.

  • Bowing - In Japan, bowing is a sign of respect, and it is customary to bow when greeting someone.

  • Shoes - Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a traditional Japanese-style guesthouse.

  • Onsen etiquette - When visiting a hot spring, it’s important to wash thoroughly before entering the water and to keep quiet and respectful while in the baths.

Best Time to Visit Misato (Akita)

The best time to visit Misato is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. The town also holds several Matsuri festivals throughout the year, which are a great way to experience the local culture and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Misato (Akita)

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Misato.

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Embrace the traditional Japanese way of life.

  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.

  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese guesthouses, temples, or shrines.

  • Leave a tip at restaurants.

Safety and Health

Misato is a safe destination, with low levels of crime and a friendly local community. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit.

Safety in Misato (Akita)

  • Take care when hiking or biking in the mountains, and be aware of the weather forecast.

  • Look both ways before crossing the street, as cars may drive on the left or right side of the road depending on the area.

Health in Misato (Akita)

  • Japan has a high standard of healthcare, with many hospitals and clinics throughout the country.

  • It’s important to stay hydrated during the hot summer months and to wear sunscreen to protect against the sun’s rays.

Getting to Misato (Akita): Transportation Options and Logistics

Misato is located in the northwestern part of Akita prefecture, and there are several ways to get there.

Airports Near Misato (Akita)

  • Akita Airport is the closest airport to Misato, located about 1 hour and 30 minutes away by car.

  • Hanamaki Airport is another option, located about 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Misato (Akita)

  • Akita Chuo Bus Station is a major bus terminal in Akita city, with buses that run to Misato.

  • Nishimeya Bus Station is another option, with buses that run to Misato and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • You can also rent a car and drive to Misato from cities such as Akita, Morioka, or Sendai.

  • Alternatively, you can take a train to Misato station, which is located on the JR Hanawa Line.

How to Get Around in Misato (Akita)

Misato is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Public transportation is also available, with buses that run to nearby attractions and towns.

Where to Go Next from Misato (Akita): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you might enjoy.

  • Akita City - The capital city of Akita prefecture is known for its lively street food scene and colorful festivals.

  • Lake Tazawa - This stunning lake is the deepest in Japan and is surrounded by lush forests and mountains.

  • Hirosaki Castle - Located in Aomori prefecture, this beautiful castle is famous for its cherry blossom festival in the spring.

Conclusion

Misato (Akita) is a unique destination that offers visitors a combination of beautiful scenery, rich culture and history, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re seeking relaxation or adventure, Misato has something to offer for everyone. Plan your visit today and experience the best of this hidden gem in Japan’s Tohoku region.
