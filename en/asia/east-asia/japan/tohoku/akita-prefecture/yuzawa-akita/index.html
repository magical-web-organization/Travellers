Ultimate Yuzawa (Akita) Travel Guide

Located in Tohoku, Japan, Yuzawa (Akita) is a hidden gem that offers a mix of natural wonders and cultural experiences. With its stunning mountains, hot springs, and historic sites, this destination has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, activities, and travel tips to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Yuzawa (Akita).

Introduction to Yuzawa (Akita)

Yuzawa (Akita) is a town located in the Akita prefecture of Japan’s Tohoku region. The town is famous for its Tamasudare, a traditional bamboo craft that is now a designated Intangible Folk Cultural Property of Japan. Yuzawa (Akita) is also known for its beautiful mountains, such as Mount Moriyoshi, and its hot springs, including the Hachimantai Onsen area. The town has a rich history and culture, making it an ideal destination for travelers who are interested in learning about Japan’s past.

List of Places to Visit in Yuzawa (Akita)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tamasudare-no-Sato: A museum that showcases the traditional bamboo craft of Tamasudare, which is made by weaving bamboo strips together to create a beautiful curtain-like object. Visitors can try making their tamasudare and learn about the history of this ancient craft.
  • Kakunodate Samurai District: A well-preserved samurai town that dates back to the Edo period. Visitors can explore the old samurai houses, walk along the beautiful cherry blossom-lined street, and enjoy traditional performances.

Natural Attractions of Yuzawa (Akita): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hachimantai Aspite Line: A beautiful driving route that takes visitors through the Hachimantai Mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding nature.
  • Mount Moriyoshi: A mountain that offers hiking trails with beautiful views of the Yuzawa (Akita) area. Visitors can also enjoy skiing during the winter months.
  • Nyuto Onsen: A hot spring area that is famous for its milky-white waters and beautiful natural surroundings. Visitors can relax in the hot springs and enjoy the traditional Japanese-style inn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yuzawa (Akita)

  • Mount Moriyoshi Hiking Trail: A 7km-long hiking trail that takes visitors to the summit of Mount Moriyoshi, offering beautiful views of the Yuzawa (Akita) area.
  • Hachimantai Aspite Line Cycling Route: A 32km-long cycling route that takes visitors through the Hachimantai Mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Yuzawa (Akita): Activities and Tours

  • Hachimantai Aspite Line Bus Tour: A bus tour that takes visitors through the Hachimantai Mountains, stopping at scenic spots along the way.
  • Nyuto Onsen Village Tour: A tour that takes visitors to the various hot springs in the Nyuto Onsen area.

Food and Drink Scene of Yuzawa (Akita)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Inaniwa Udon: A type of udon that is famous in the Akita prefecture. The noodles are thin and smooth, and the dish is usually served cold with a dipping sauce.
  • Kiritanpo: A dish made by shaping mashed rice around a stick and roasting it over an open flame. Kiritanpo is often served in a hot pot with vegetables and chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: The Akita prefecture is known for its sake, which is made using high-quality rice and water from local springs.
  • Shochu: Another popular alcoholic beverage in the Akita prefecture, shochu is made by distilling various grains or potatoes.

Where to Stay in Yuzawa (Akita): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nyuto Onsen Tsurunoyu: A traditional Japanese-style inn that offers hot springs and beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Kashoen: A grand ryokan that was built in the early 1900s and has been designated as a National Important Cultural Property of Japan.

What to Buy in Yuzawa (Akita): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tamasudare: The bamboo craft of Tamasudare makes for a unique and beautiful souvenir that is synonymous with Yuzawa (Akita).
  • Akita Sugi Wood Products: The Akita prefecture is known for the quality of its Sugi (Japanese cedar) wood, which is used to make various products such as furniture and kitchenware.

Local Culture and Customs of Yuzawa (Akita)

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a traditional Japanese-style inn.
  • Yuzawa (Akita) is one of the coldest places in Japan during the winter months, so be sure to dress warmly and take precautions against the cold.

Best Time to Visit Yuzawa (Akita)

The best time to visit Yuzawa (Akita) is during the autumn months, from September to November. This is when the fall foliage is at its peak, offering beautiful views of the mountains around Yuzawa (Akita). The winter months, from December to February, are also popular for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yuzawa (Akita)

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress warmly during the winter months.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese-style inns.
  • Leave trash in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yuzawa (Akita)

Yuzawa (Akita) is generally a safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take precautions against the cold during the winter months.

Health in Yuzawa (Akita)

The tap water in Yuzawa (Akita) is safe to drink. However, visitors should take precautions against the cold during the winter months.

Getting to Yuzawa (Akita): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yuzawa (Akita)

  • Akita Airport: The closest airport to Yuzawa (Akita), which is about 60km away.

Bus Stations Near Yuzawa (Akita)

  • Yuzawa Bus Station: The main bus terminal in Yuzawa (Akita), which has connections to various cities in the Akita prefecture.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Yuzawa (Akita) is the JR Yuzawa Station, which has connections to cities such as Akita and Morioka.

How to Get Around in Yuzawa (Akita)

The best way to get around Yuzawa (Akita) is by car or by bus. Car rentals are available at the Akita Airport and other locations throughout the prefecture. Local buses connect various cities in the Akita prefecture, including Yuzawa (Akita).

Where to Go Next from Yuzawa (Akita): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kakunodate: A well-preserved samurai town that is about 50km away from Yuzawa (Akita).
  • Akita City: The capital city of the Akita prefecture, which is about 70km away.

Conclusion

Yuzawa (Akita) is a beautiful destination that offers a mix of natural beauty and cultural experiences. From hiking and biking to relaxing in hot springs and exploring historic sites, there is something for everyone in Yuzawa (Akita). We hope that this guide has helped you plan your perfect trip and discover all that Yuzawa (Akita) has to offer.
