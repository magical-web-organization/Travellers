Introduction to Dewa Sanzan

Nestled in Yamagata prefecture, in the northern Tohoku region of Japan, Dewa Sanzan is a sacred mountainous complex with a rich cultural and spiritual history. Comprising three mountains - Mt. Haguro, Mt. Gassan, and Mt. Yudono - Dewa Sanzan has been a revered pilgrimage site for over a thousand years. It is a must-visit destination for those seeking profound cultural and spiritual experiences, as well as outdoor adventurers seeking to explore the beauty of Japan’s mountain landscape.

List of Places to Visit in Dewa Sanzan

Here’s a comprehensive list of the must-visit places in Dewa Sanzan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sanjin Gosaiden Shrine: A shrine dedicated to the three deities of Mount Haguro, also known as Sanjin or “Three Shrines”. Accessible by a 2446-step staircase, this shrine is known for its impressive architecture and beautiful surroundings, particularly in autumn when the leaves turn red.
  • Buddha Hall at Mt. Haguro: A stunning five-story wooden pagoda-style building, it is considered a masterpiece of Japanese architecture. The hall houses a 14-foot statue of Yakushi Nyorai, the Buddha of healing, and is surrounded by a tranquil garden.
  • Gassan Shrine: The main shrine at the summit of Mount Gassan, Gassan Shrine is a sacred site with a long history of worship.
  • Dewa Sanzan Historical Museum: A museum dedicated to the history and culture of Dewa Sanzan, with a diverse collection of artifacts, including Buddhist statues, traditional instruments, and ancient manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Dewa Sanzan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zuishinmon Gateway: A grand entrance to the Mount Haguro area, the gateway itself is a beautiful and impressive structure, and from here, visitors can take a stroll along the picturesque cedar-lined path.
  • Mt. Yudono: Regarded as the most sacred of the three mountains, visitors must perform a special purification ritual before ascending to the top.
  • Goshado: A collection of five shrines, located at the summit of Mount Gassan, with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Lake Jinnai: A serene lake known for its crystal clear waters and vibrant autumn foliage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dewa Sanzan

Dewa Sanzan is a paradise for hikers, cyclists, and nature lovers. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  • Mount Haguro: The most accessible of the three mountains, with a well-maintained hiking trail and a 2446-step staircase leading to the Sanjin Gosaiden Shrine.
  • Mount Gassan: The tallest of the three mountains, a challenging but rewarding hike to the summit, taking around 8 hours to complete.
  • Yudono-Sanrojo: A 14-kilometer hiking trail that connects to Mount Haguro and Mount Gassan, through stunning natural scenery and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Dewa Sanzan: Activities and Tours

  • Forest Therapy: Take a guided tour of the serene forests of Dewa Sanzan and experience the healing powers of nature.
  • Temple Stays: A unique opportunity to experience the spiritual practices of the mountains, by staying at one of the traditional temples on Mount Haguro.
  • Nighttime Visit to Yudono Shrine: Experience the mystical atmosphere of Yudono Shrine by taking a guided tour after dark.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dewa Sanzan

Day 1: Arrive in Yamagata and take a bus to Tsuruoka City.

Day 2: Explore the historical and cultural attractions of Mount Haguro, including the Sanjin Gosaiden Shrine, Zuishinmon Gateway, and the Five-Story Pagoda.

Day 3: Hike up Mount Gassan, take in the stunning views, and visit the Gassan Shrine at the summit.

Day 4-5: Take part in forest therapy and temple stays, experiencing the tranquility of the mountains.

Day 6: Visit Yudono Shrine, the most sacred of the three mountains, and perform the purification ritual.

Day 7: Explore the local food and drink scene and shop for souvenirs before departing from Yamagata.

Food and Drink Scene of Dewa Sanzan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dewa Sanzan Soba: Buckwheat noodles served in a hot dashi broth, accompanied by mushrooms, ginger, and wasabi.
  • Kaisendon: A bowl of rice topped with a variety of fresh sashimi seafood, usually served with miso soup.
  • Shojin Ryori: A type of Buddhist vegetarian cuisine, often served at the temples on Mount Haguro.

Local Beverages

  • Dewa Sanzan Sake: Sake brewed using the pure spring water from the mountains, with a smooth, delicate taste.
  • Fruit Juice: Made from locally grown fruits such as apples, cherries, and pears, these juices are refreshing and nourishing.

Where to Stay in Dewa Sanzan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hagurokan: A traditional Japanese-style inn located at the foot of Mount Haguro, offering tatami rooms and an onsen (hot spring) bath.
  • Shukubo: Temple lodgings located around Mount Haguro and Mount Gassan, offering guests an authentic cultural and spiritual experience.
  • Ryokan Yamanobe: A cozy and welcoming guesthouse located in Tsuruoka City, with Western-style rooms and easy access to the city’s attractions.

What to Buy in Dewa Sanzan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Koma-inu: These lion-like statues with fierce expressions are traditional guardians of Japanese temples and shrines. You can find various sizes and styles in souvenir shops around Dewa Sanzan.
  • Dewa Sanzan Sake: Bring home a bottle of the locally brewed sake, made using the pure water from the mountains.
  • Traditional Crafts: You can find various traditional crafts, such as wooden crafts, ceramic wares, and textiles, made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Dewa Sanzan

  • Respect for the Mountains: Dewa Sanzan is a sacred site, and visitors are expected to show respect for the natural environment and the buildings and artifacts on the mountains.
  • Pilgrimage: Dewa Sanzan has been a pilgrimage site for over a thousand years, and visitors are welcome to participate in the unique cultural and spiritual practices of the area.
  • Special Events: Dewa Sanzan hosts various cultural and spiritual events throughout the year, such as the Dewa Sanzan Grand Festival and the Yakushido Festival.

Best Time to Visit Dewa Sanzan

The best time to visit Dewa Sanzan is during the autumn months of September to November when the leaves turn vibrant shades of orange, red, and yellow. Spring and summer are also great times to visit, with cooler temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities on offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dewa Sanzan

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and for temple visits.
  • Respect the natural environment and the cultural and spiritual sites of the mountains.
  • Participate in the unique cultural and spiritual practices of Dewa Sanzan.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on the mountains.
  • Engage in loud or disrespectful behavior.
  • Ignore the cultural and spiritual significance of Dewa Sanzan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dewa Sanzan

  • Proper Footwear: Dewa Sanzan is a mountainous area, and visitors should wear sturdy and comfortable shoes suitable for hiking.
  • Trail Conditions: Visitors should check the condition of the hiking trails before embarking on any hikes and be aware of any weather changes that may affect the trails.
  • Respect Wildlife: Dewa Sanzan is home to a variety of wildlife, such as monkeys, bears, and serows. Visitors should keep a safe distance and avoid interacting with them.

Health in Dewa Sanzan

  • Hydration: Visitors should stay hydrated, especially during hot weather and strenuous activities.
  • Altitude Sickness: Visitors ascending to the summits of the mountains may experience altitude sickness. Symptoms include headaches, nausea, and dizziness.

Getting to Dewa Sanzan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dewa Sanzan

  • Yamagata Airport: The nearest airport to Dewa Sanzan, with domestic flights to and from Tokyo, Osaka, and other major cities.

Bus Stations Near Dewa Sanzan

  • Tsuruoka Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Tsuruoka city, offering regular bus services to Dewa Sanzan.
  • Haguro-sen-ni Station: A bus stop near the entrance of Haguro Mountain.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from Yamagata or Tsuruoka and drive to Dewa Sanzan, with free parking available at each mountain.

How to Get Around in Dewa Sanzan

  • Hiking: The most common mode of transportation in Dewa Sanzan is hiking, with well-maintained trails connecting the three mountains and the surrounding areas.
  • Cycling: Visitors can rent bicycles in Tsuruoka and explore the city and the surrounding areas.
  • Bus: Regular bus services connect Tsuruoka city to the entrance of each mountain.

Where to Go Next from Dewa Sanzan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yamadera: A famous temple complex located in the nearby city of Yamagata, known for its stunning views and peaceful atmosphere.
  • Shonai Plains: A vast and picturesque area of rice fields and countryside, located south of Tsuruoka city.
  • Matsushima: A coastal town, located northeast of Yamagata, known for its scenic views of pine-clad islands.

Conclusion

Dewa Sanzan is a truly unique and enchanting destination, offering visitors a glimpse into Japan’s rich cultural and spiritual heritage, as well as awe-inspiring natural scenery. Whether you’re seeking adventure, tranquility, or cultural immersion, Dewa Sanzan has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of this sacred mountainous complex.
