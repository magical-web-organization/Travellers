Introduction to Obanazawa

Nestled in the foothills of the Yamagata Mountains on the northern coast of Japan, Obanazawa is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This charming town offers visitors a unique blend of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Obanazawa boasts a distinct local charm coupled with an authentic Japanese experience, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Obanazawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Obanazawa is home to several cultural attractions, including museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the town’s rich heritage. The following are some of the top must-visit cultural attractions in Obanazawa:

  • Obanazawa City Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the town through the museum’s collection of art and artifacts.
  • Obanazawa Yuki no Sato: A unique museum dedicated to snow and ice where visitors can learn about the history of winter culture in Japan.
  • Hatago Kawakami: A well-preserved traditional Japanese inn from the Edo period that has been converted into a museum.

Natural Attractions of Obanazawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Obanazawa is known for its stunning natural landscapes, offering visitors a range of outdoor activities and scenic views. If you are looking for some outdoor adventures and breathtaking natural beauty, check out the following places:

  • Mount Zao: A majestic mountain range that straddles the border between Yamagata and Miyagi prefectures. The area is known for its hot springs and ski resorts.
  • Okama Crater: A stunning volcanic crater located at the summit of Mount Zao. It is a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Kurikoma Quasi-National Park: A beautiful park that features a range of natural wonders, including waterfalls, lakes, and stunning views of Mount Kurikoma.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Obanazawa

Obanazawa offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the town and its surroundings. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Ajikura Toge Pass Trail: A hike that takes you through the stunning countryside and over the Ajikura Toge Pass, offering stunning views of Mount Zao.
  • Tsukioka Onsen Cycling Road: A relaxing bike ride through the scenic countryside that ends at the Tsukioka Onsen hot spring resort.

Best Things to Do in Obanazawa: Activities and Tours

Obanazawa offers visitors a range of activities and tours that allow them to experience the town’s unique culture and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Obanazawa:

  • Basho no Yakata: A cultural center dedicated to the famous haiku poet Basho Matsuo, offering visitors a chance to learn about his life and work.
  • Katakuri no Sato: A popular springtime attraction featuring the beautiful and rare katakuri flowers.
  • Sankyo Soko: A restored merchant house from the Edo period that has been converted into a museum, offering visitors a glimpse into the town’s past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Obanazawa

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Obanazawa, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Obanazawa City Museum and Obanazawa Yuki no Sato. Day 2: Take a hike over the Ajikura Toge Pass Trail and visit the Hatago Kawakami museum. Day 3: Visit Mount Zao and relax in one of the hot springs. Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Tsukioka Onsen Cycling Road and visit the Kurikoma Quasi-National Park. Day 5: Check out the rare and beautiful katakuri flowers at Katakuri no Sato. Day 6: Take a tour of the Sankyo Soko museum and explore the town’s historic buildings. Day 7: Visit Basho no Yakata and attend a haiku workshop.

Food and Drink Scene of Obanazawa

Must-Try Dishes

Obanazawa is known for its delicious local cuisine, which includes the following dishes:

  • Sasa Kamaboko: A type of fish cake made with bamboo leaves that is a local favorite.
  • Oshokuji-dokoro: A traditional Japanese meal that includes a range of local dishes.
  • Yonezawa beef: A high-quality beef from nearby Yonezawa City that is known for its rich flavor.

Local Beverages

Obanazawa is known for its sakes and other alcoholic beverages, including:

  • Dewazakura Sake: A well-known sake brewery that produces a range of high-quality sakes.
  • Takanabe Shochu: A local shochu made from sweet potatoes.

Where to Stay in Obanazawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Obanazawa offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference, from traditional Japanese inns to modern hotels. Some of the top options include:

  • Okujoen: A stunning ryokan located on the slopes of Mount Zao, offering hot springs and traditional Japanese rooms.
  • Obanazawa Guesthouse: A friendly and affordable guesthouse located in the heart of the town center.
  • Akiu Grand Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in nearby Akiu Onsen with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Obanazawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Obanazawa offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and keepsakes. Some of the top items include:

  • Bamboo Crafts: Locally made bamboo crafts that include baskets, mats, and other items.
  • Sasa Kamaboko: A local favorite fish cake made with bamboo leaves.
  • Obanazawa Yakitori Sauce: A special local sauce used to flavor grilled chicken skewers.

Local Culture and Customs of Obanazawa

Obanazawa has a rich cultural heritage that includes many local customs and traditions. Some of the most notable include:

  • Yosakoi Festival: A lively festival held in August featuring dance and music performances.
  • Shishi Dance: A traditional dance performed in the town center during the New Year’s holiday.
  • Chacha Dance: A dance performed during the Obanazawa winter festival that features a large wooden mask and traditional clothing.

Best Time to Visit Obanazawa

The best time to visit Obanazawa is in the spring and winter months when the town comes alive with blooms and winter sports enthusiasts. The cherry blossoms around town begin blooming in early April, while winter sports like skiing and snowboarding in the nearby mountains are popular from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Obanazawa

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks to fully experience the local culture.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as taking off your shoes when entering a building.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural wonders that Obanazawa offers.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink in public places.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Touch or disturb wild animals and plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Obanazawa

Obanazawa is a relatively safe town for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to exercise caution and take basic safety precautions, such as securing your valuables and keeping an eye on your surroundings.

Health in Obanazawa

Obanazawa has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and check with your doctor about any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Obanazawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Obanazawa

The nearest airport to Obanazawa is Yamagata Airport, which is located about 50 km away from the town center. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Obanazawa.

Bus Stations Near Obanazawa

There are several bus stations in Obanazawa, including the Obanazawa Station Bus Terminal and the Obanazawa Bus Center. Buses run regularly to and from nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Obanazawa can also be reached by car or train. The nearest train station is the JR Obanazawa Station, which is located in the town center.

How to Get Around in Obanazawa

Obanazawa is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. There are also some buses that run throughout the town, as well as taxis for hire.

Where to Go Next from Obanazawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can easily explore from Obanazawa, including:

  • Yamagata: A nearby city known for its cherry blossoms and delicious local cuisine.
  • Sendai: The largest city in the Tohoku region, known for its modern cityscape and historic sites.
  • Zao Onsen: A popular hot spring resort located in the nearby mountains.

Conclusion

Obanazawa may not be the most well-known destination in Japan, but it certainly has plenty to offer visitors looking for a unique travel experience. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures, Obanazawa is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
