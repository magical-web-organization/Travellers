Introduction to Goshogawara

Nestled in the northernmost region of Honshu Island, Goshogawara is a hidden gem in the Aomori Prefecture of Japan. The city offers stunning scenery with the Izumi Mountain Range in the backdrop, and the city’s rich history, charming architecture, and vibrant cultural festivals attract many visitors each year.

List of Places to Visit in Goshogawara

Goshogawara has a plethora of tourist attractions, including culturally rich museums, historical places, outdoor adventures, and scenic views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Take a trip through Goshogawara’s rich history by visiting the following museums and historical places:

Tachineputa No Yakata Museum

This museum showcases the elaborate floats used in the Tachi Neputa festival, a traditional Japanese summer festival. Visitors can enjoy the intricate designs and learn about the festival’s history.

Takashima Coal Mine Museum

This museum showcases the history of the Takashima Coal Mine, one of the largest coal mines in Japan. Visitors can explore the mine’s atmosphere and learn about its significance to the local economy.

Natural Attractions of Goshogawara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Goshogawara offers breathtaking scenic views and adventurous outdoor activities, including:

Mount Iwaki

At 1625m, Mount Iwaki is the highest mountain in the Tsugaru Peninsula. Visitors can hike the mountain to enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding scenery.

Hirosaki Castle

One of the most beloved castles in Japan, Hirosaki Castle is a popular destination for visitors seeking peace and tranquility in nature. Visitors can enjoy the park’s cherry blossom trees in the spring and colorful foliage during autumn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goshogawara

For avid hikers and bikers, Goshogawara offers a host of challenging mountain trails and cycling routes that wind through the Tsugaru Plains.

Best Things to Do in Goshogawara: Activities and Tours

Explore the following things to do and tours to make the most of your visit to Goshogawara:

Tachi Neputa Festival

Held in August, this vibrant festival features a parade of colorful floats with traditional Japanese designs. Join the locals in celebrating this lively festival.

Tsugaru Railway

Hop on the Tsugaru Railway to explore the picturesque countryside and explore the region’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Goshogawara

Day 1: Visit Tachineputa No Yakata Museum and Hirosaki Castle. Day 2-3: Hike Mount Iwaki and explore the Tsugaru Plains. Day 4-5: Attend the Tachi Neputa Festival and enjoy a Tsugaru Railway excursion. Day 6-7: Visit the Takashima Coal Mine Museum and explore the city’s local markets and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Goshogawara

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Goshogawara is complete without sampling the local cuisine, including the following dishes:

Kattchoufu

This traditional dish is a type of fishcake that is cooked with miso and local ingredients.

Mow

Mow is a regional dish made from boiled taro roots and served with soy sauce and green onions.

Local Beverages

Goshogawara has a rich brewing culture where visitors can enjoy a range of local sake, cider, and craft beer.

Where to Stay in Goshogawara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Goshogawara offers a range of accommodation options, from guest houses to ryokans.

What to Buy in Goshogawara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy traditional handicrafts and souvenirs, including pottery and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Goshogawara

Goshogawara is renowned for its local festivals, including the Tachi Neputa and Tsugaru Shamisen festivals. Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully to adhere to cultural norms.

Best Time to Visit Goshogawara

The best time to visit Goshogawara is from late May to early October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goshogawara

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the natural wonders of the region.
  • Attend the local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in public spaces.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.
