Introduction to Aomori (prefecture)

Aomori (prefecture) is located in the Tohoku region of Japan, the northernmost part of Honshu, the main island of Japan. Aomori is known for its beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and delicious cuisine. The prefecture is surrounded by the Tsugaru Strait to the north, the Pacific Ocean to the east, and the Sea of Japan to the west, making it a unique and diverse region to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Aomori (prefecture)

Aomori has a lot to offer visitors, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Here are the top places to visit in Aomori:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aomori Museum of Art: This contemporary museum showcases a variety of artwork, including pieces by local artists.
  • Nebuta Museum Wa Rasse: This museum is dedicated to the famous Aomori Nebuta Festival, one of the largest festivals in Japan.
  • Hirosaki Castle: This castle is among the few original castles remaining in Japan, and it is especially beautiful during the cherry blossom season.

Natural Attractions of Aomori (prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hakkoda: This beautiful mountain range is perfect for hiking and skiing, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Towada-Hachimantai National Park: This park is home to beautiful forests, lakes, and mountains, and it is an excellent place to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and camping.
  • Oirase Gorge: This picturesque gorge is known for its spectacular waterfalls and colorful autumn foliage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aomori (prefecture)

  • Tsutanuma Hiking Course: This course takes hikers through the beautiful Tsuta-numa wetland area, which is home to a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Tanesashi Coast Nature Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the coastline and allows hikers to explore the unique ecosystems of the area.
  • Shimokita Peninsula Rinko Line: This 70-kilometer biking route takes visitors through the beautiful landscapes of the Shimokita Peninsula, including forests, mountains, and coastlines.

Best Things to Do in Aomori (prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Aomori Nebuta Festival: This annual festival is held in August and is a must-see for visitors to the region. The festival features giant illuminated floats, music, and dancing.
  • Sea kayaking: Visitors can explore the coastline of Aomori by sea kayak, allowing them to get a unique view of the surrounding landscapes and wildlife.
  • Sake tasting: Aomori is famous for its delicious sake, and visitors can sample a variety of locally-produced varieties at breweries throughout the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aomori (prefecture)

Day 1: Explore the Aomori Museum of Art and visit the beautiful Hirosaki Castle. Day 2: Take a day trip to the Oirase Gorge and enjoy hiking and nature. Day 3: Visit the Nebuta Museum Wa Rasse and learn about the famous Aomori Nebuta Festival. Day 4: Travel to the Tsutanuma Hiking Course and enjoy a day of hiking and wildlife viewing. Day 5: Visit the Towada-Hachimantai National Park and explore the beautiful lakes and mountains. Day 6: Enjoy sea kayaking along the Aomori coastline and sample some of the region’s delicious sake. Day 7: Take a relaxing day to explore the local markets and souvenir shops, and enjoy some of the region’s delicious cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Aomori (prefecture)

Aomori is known for its delicious seafood, including fresh fish and crab, as well as its locally-produced sake. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hachinohe Senbei-jiru: This soup is made with crispy rice crackers and a variety of seafood, including squid, scallops, and clams.
  • Hirosaki Neputa-yaki: This is a type of grilled chicken dish that is marinated in a sweet soy sauce.
  • Aomori Guroriesu: This is a type of seafood dish that includes crab, squid, and scallops, all cooked in a delicious sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Aomori Sake: Aomori is known for its sake, which is made using local ingredients, including the famous Aomori apples.
  • Apple Juice: Aomori is known for its delicious apples, and visitors can enjoy fresh apple juice throughout the region.

What to Buy in Aomori (prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aomori has a variety of unique products and souvenirs that visitors can take home as a reminder of their trip. Here are some of the best items to buy in the region:

Local Culture and Customs of Aomori (prefecture)

Aomori has a rich cultural heritage, including traditional festivals, crafts, and cuisine. Here are some of the most important customs and traditions in the region:

  • Aomori Nebuta Festival: This famous festival is held annually in August and features large illuminated floats, music, and dancing.
  • Tsugaru Shamisen: This traditional stringed instrument is popular in the region and is often played during local festivals and events.
  • Local Cuisine: Aomori is known for its delicious seafood, including fresh fish and crab, as well as its locally-produced sake.

Best Time to Visit Aomori (prefecture)

The best time to visit Aomori depends on your interests and preferences. If you want to experience the famous Nebuta Festival, then August is the best time to visit. If you want to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and skiing, then the winter months are the best time to visit. Autumn is also a beautiful time to visit, as the foliage is at its peak and there are many seasonal festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aomori (prefecture)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and events.
  • Try local cuisine and sake, which are some of the best in Japan.
  • Be friendly and open-minded, as the people of Aomori are known for their hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and events.
  • Waste food or drink, as this is considered rude in Japanese culture.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places, as this is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aomori (prefecture)

Aomori is a relatively safe region to visit, with low crime rates and a generally welcoming and friendly atmosphere. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking their valuables and being aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Aomori (prefecture)

Aomori has a well-developed healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics throughout the region. Visitors should have travel insurance and bring any necessary medications with them, as some items may not be available in Japan.
