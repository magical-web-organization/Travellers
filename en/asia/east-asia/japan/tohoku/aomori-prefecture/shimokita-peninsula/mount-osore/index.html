Introduction to Mount Osore

Located in Shimokita Peninsula in Aomori Prefecture, Japan, Mount Osore is a dormant volcano that is considered one of the most sacred places in the country. It is believed to be the gateway to the afterlife and is a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists alike. The name “Osore” translates to “fear” in Japanese, but despite its ominous name, the scenic beauty and cultural importance of the area make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Osore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Osorezan Bodaiji Temple: The temple is the main attraction of Mount Osore and encompasses the entire mountain. It is believed that the temple was founded over a thousand years ago and is the largest and most important temple in the area.

  • Osorezan Chokokuji Temple: The temple is located at the foot of the mountain and is known for its statues and carvings of Buddhist deities.

  • Usoriyama Observation Deck: Located on the southern side of the mountain, this observation deck provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Osorezan Reijo Sacred Ground: A place of great religious importance, this sacred ground is believed to be the gateway to the afterlife and the place where souls cross from the living world to the world beyond.

Natural Attractions of Mount Osore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Osore Crater: The main attraction of Mount Osore, the crater is a must-visit for anyone traveling to the area. The view from the top is breathtaking, and visitors can see all the way to the Pacific Ocean on a clear day.

  • Goshogake Hot Springs: Located at the foot of Mount Osore, these hot springs are rich in minerals and offer a relaxing and therapeutic experience.

  • Lake Usori: A beautiful and serene lake located near the summit of Mount Osore, Lake Usori is a popular spot for fishing and boating.

  • Osore Forest: A dense forest located at the foot of the mountain, Osore Forest is a great place for hiking and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Osore

Mount Osore offers several hiking and biking routes, each offering a unique perspective on the area’s natural beauty and cultural significance. Some of the most popular routes are:

  • Mount Osore Crater Hike: This moderate hike takes visitors to the summit of Mount Osore, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Osorezan Chokokuji Temple Trail: This easy trail takes visitors through the forest to the Osorezan Chokokuji Temple, where they can admire the intricate carvings and statues.

  • Lake Usori Loop Trail: This easy trail circles Lake Usori, offering stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Mount Osore: Activities and Tours

  • Pilgrimage: Mount Osore is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Japan, and visitors can participate in a guided pilgrimage to learn more about the cultural and religious significance of the area.

  • Hiking and Biking: Mount Osore offers several hiking and biking routes, each with its own unique beauty and cultural significance.

  • Hot Springs: The hot springs at the foot of Mount Osore are a great place to relax and unwind after a long day of exploring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Osore

Day 1: Arrival in Aomori, transfer to Mount Osore.

Day 2: Visit Osorezan Bodaiji Temple and Osorezan Chokokuji Temple, hike to the summit of Mount Osore.

Day 3: Explore the hot springs at Goshogake, visit the Usoriyama Observation Deck.

Day 4: Take a guided pilgrimage to Osorezan Reijo Sacred Ground, visit the Osore Forest.

Day 5: Bike the Lake Usori Loop Trail, enjoy a picnic by the lake.

Day 6: Take a day trip to neighboring Hirosaki and visit the Hirosaki Castle and Park.

Day 7: Departure from Aomori.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Osore

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hatahata Nabe: A winter specialty in Aomori, this hot pot dish includes hatahata fish, vegetables, and tofu.

  • Nokke Don: A rice bowl dish topped with salmon roe, scallions, and soy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Aomori is known for its production of high-quality sake. Some of the most popular brands include Dassai and Dewazakura.

  • Apple Juice: Aomori is Japan’s largest producer of apples, and the local apple juice is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Mount Osore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Osorezan Hotel: Located at the foot of Mount Osore, this hotel offers traditional Japanese-style rooms and a hot spring bath.

  • Osorezan Youth Hostel: This hostel offers dormitory-style accommodations and is a great option for budget travelers.

  • Osorezan Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers private rooms and shared facilities, and is located in a beautiful and tranquil setting.

What to Buy in Mount Osore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Osorezan Gohyaku Rakan: These small wooden statues, representing the five hundred Buddhist arhats, are popular souvenirs from Mount Osore.

  • Aomori Apples: Aomori is Japan’s largest producer of apples, and the local apples are a popular gift and souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Osore

  • Pilgrimage: Mount Osore is a sacred place in Japanese culture, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions when visiting the area.

  • Etiquette: Visitors should remove their shoes when visiting temples and other sacred places, and should dress modestly and respectfully.

Best Time to Visit Mount Osore

The best time to visit Mount Osore is from April to November, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. However, the area is also popular in the winter months for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Osore

Do:

  • Take part in a guided pilgrimage to learn more about the cultural and religious significance of the area.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions when visiting the area.
  • Explore the hiking and biking routes to fully appreciate the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other sacred places.
  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Osore

Mount Osore is a safe area to visit, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or exploring the area. It is important to stay on marked trails and to be aware of the weather conditions.

Health in Mount Osore

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude and thin air when hiking or exploring the area. It is important to stay hydrated and to avoid strenuous activities if feeling unwell.

Getting to Mount Osore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Osore

The closest airport to Mount Osore is Aomori Airport, which is approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Mount Osore

Buses from Aomori Station serve the Mount Osore area, with several departures each day.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available for hire in the area.

How to Get Around in Mount Osore

The best way to get around Mount Osore is by car or taxi, as public transportation is limited in the area.

Where to Go Next from Mount Osore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hirosaki: A castle town located approximately 2 hours by car from Mount Osore, Hirosaki is known for its cherry blossom festival and historic castle.

  • Hachinohe: A coastal city approximately 2 hours by car from Mount Osore, Hachinohe is known for its fresh seafood and local festivals.

Conclusion

Mount Osore is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan, offering stunning natural beauty and cultural significance. From hiking and biking to exploring the local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this unique and special place.
