Introduction to Tsugaru Peninsula

Located in Aomori prefecture in the Tohoku region of Japan, Tsugaru Peninsula is blessed with natural beauty, cultural heritage, and a warm, welcoming atmosphere that makes it a great destination for all types of travelers, from nature enthusiasts to history buffs to foodies. Home to stunning vistas, scenic coastal drives, delicious local cuisine, and unique festivals, Tsugaru Peninsula offers a unique travel experience that immerses visitors in the heart and soul of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Tsugaru Peninsula

Whether you want to explore the region’s cultural heritage, indulge in adventure sports, or simply soak up the natural beauty of the surroundings, there are plenty of things to see and do in Tsugaru Peninsula. Here are some of the top places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hirosaki Castle: One of the most iconic landmarks in Tsugaru Peninsula, Hirosaki Castle is a beautifully preserved castle that offers a glimpse into Japan’s feudal past. Visitors can explore the castle grounds, which include cherry blossoms, gardens, and a moat.
  • Nebuta Museum Wa Rasse: Nebuta is a popular summer festival in which gigantic illuminated floats featuring historical figures and mythical creatures are paraded through the streets. The Nebuta Museum Wa Rasse showcases the history and artistry behind the festival, and is a must-visit for anyone interested in traditional Japanese festivals.
  • Nambu Traditional Houses: These traditional farmhouses are a symbol of the region’s agricultural heritage. Visitors can explore these houses, see the tools and techniques used by farmers in the past, and even get a taste of local produce.

Natural Attractions of Tsugaru Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oirase Gorge: This picturesque gorge features some of the most stunning natural scenery in all of Japan. Visitors can hike through the gorge to see cascading waterfalls, emerald forests, and crystal-clear streams.
  • Mt. Iwaki: Located in the heart of Tsugaru Peninsula, Mt. Iwaki offers some of the best panoramic views of the region. Visitors can hike to the summit or take a cable car for a stunning view from the top.
  • Osorezan: Known as the “Mountain of Dread,” Osorezan is a sacred place in Japanese Buddhism and Shintoism. This active volcanic crater boasts a stark, otherworldly landscape that is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsugaru Peninsula

Tsugaru Peninsula is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Oirase Stream Trail: This 14-kilometer trail winds alongside the Oirase Stream, offering stunning views of waterfalls, rapids, and lush forests along the way.
  • Asamushi Onsenkyo Nature Trail: This 5-kilometer trail takes visitors through a peaceful forest, alongside a tranquil river, and past an old shrine.
  • Tsugaru-Shinjo Station Cycling Road: This 20-kilometer road is a popular cycling route that goes through scenic countryside, along the coast, and through charming villages.

Best Things to Do in Tsugaru Peninsula: Activities and Tours

  • Tsugaru-jamisen Performance: Tsugaru-jamisen is a traditional Japanese musical instrument, and visitors can enjoy live performances by local musicians.
  • Tsugaru Painting Workshop: Visitors can try their hand at Tsugaru-nuri, a traditional lacquerware craft that is unique to the region.
  • Cycling and Kayaking Tours: For visitors who want to explore the natural beauty of Tsugaru Peninsula, cycling and kayaking tours are available that take visitors to the top natural attractions in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsugaru Peninsula

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tsugaru-ramen: A local specialty ramen dish made with pork bone broth, soy sauce, and a unique blend of spices that give it a distinct flavor.
  • Nanbu Bijin Sake: This premium sake is brewed using water sourced from the Shirakami-Sanchi Mountain Range, and is known for its fruity aroma and elegant, dry flavor.
  • Kogin Mochi: A sweet and chewy mochi (rice cake) that is colored with vegetable dyes and embroidered with traditional patterns.

Local Beverages

  • Apple juice: Tsugaru Peninsula is famous for its juicy, sweet apples, and visitors can enjoy fresh-pressed apple juice at many local cafes and restaurants.
  • Hayachine Soba-cha: A tea made from roasted buckwheat that is known for its nutty flavor and medicinal properties.
  • Kirin Beer: The Kirin Brewery has a longstanding tradition in Aomori, and visitors can enjoy a cold glass of Kirin beer at many local bars and pubs.

Where to Stay in Tsugaru Peninsula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations in Tsugaru Peninsula that cater to all types of travelers and budgets. Here are some top options:

  • Aomori Royal Hotel: This modern hotel offers spacious rooms, a hot spring spa, and excellent dining options.
  • Towadako Lake View Hotel: This lakeside hotel features comfortable rooms with stunning views of Lake Towada.
  • Guesthouse Hoshinosuna: This cozy guesthouse offers a homely atmosphere and is a great budget option for backpackers.

What to Buy in Tsugaru Peninsula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tsugaru-nuri Lacquerware: This traditional craft is unique to Tsugaru Peninsula and is known for its intricate patterns and designs.
  • Tsugaru Jamisen CDs: Take home a memento of the region’s musical heritage by purchasing a CD of traditional Tsugaru-jamisen performances.
  • Tsugaru Apples: No visit to Tsugaru Peninsula is complete without trying the region’s famous apples!

Local Culture and Customs of Tsugaru Peninsula

  • Tsugaru-ben Dialect: Tsugaru-ben is the local dialect of the region, and visitors may hear unique pronunciations and vocabulary not typically heard in standard Japanese.
  • Hayachine Kagura: This traditional dance and musical performance is performed at local festivals and is rooted in Shinto mythology.
  • Kikko-no-mori: This traditional artform involves making intricate designs and patterns using natural materials such as leaves, branches, and flowers.

Best Time to Visit Tsugaru Peninsula

The best time to visit Tsugaru Peninsula is during the spring (April to May) and fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable, the scenery is at its most beautiful (with cherry blossoms and autumn leaves), and there are several festivals and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsugaru Peninsula

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks, especially the Tsugaru-ramen and Nanbu Bijin Sake.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Take your time to explore the region’s natural beauty, such as the Oirase Stream Trail and Mt. Iwaki.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside homes, temples, or other traditional buildings.
  • Refuse an offer of sake or food, as it is considered impolite.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsugaru Peninsula

Tsugaru Peninsula is a generally safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take normal precautions against pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Health in Tsugaru Peninsula

There are no major health risks associated with Tsugaru Peninsula. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and take care to stay hydrated during hot weather.

Getting to Tsugaru Peninsula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsugaru Peninsula

  • Aomori Airport (AOJ): This airport is the main gateway to Tsugaru Peninsula and offers daily flights from major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Tsugaru Peninsula

  • JR Aomori Station: This station serves as a hub for local buses that connect to various destinations throughout Tsugaru Peninsula.

Other Options

  • Train: The JR Ou Line connects Aomori with major cities in the Tohoku region, such as Sendai and Morioka.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the region at your own pace and visit more remote destinations.

How to Get Around in Tsugaru Peninsula

There are several options for getting around Tsugaru Peninsula:

  • Bus: Local buses connect major cities and tourist destinations throughout Tsugaru Peninsula.
  • Train: The JR Ou Line runs from Aomori to Akita and offers scenic views of the coastline and countryside.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available throughout the region and can be hired for shorter journeys and day trips.
  • Rental car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the region at your own pace and visit more remote destinations.

Where to Go Next from Tsugaru Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shirakami-Sanchi: This UNESCO World Heritage Site features lush, virgin beech forests that are home to several rare and endangered species.
  • Hachinohe City: The largest city in southern Aomori, Hachinohe is known for its fresh seafood, friendly locals, and lively festivals.
  • Hakodate City: Located just across the sea in Hokkaido, Hakodate is a historic port city that offers stunning views of the night skyline.

Conclusion

Tsugaru Peninsula offers a unique travel experience that combines natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm, welcoming locals. From exploring the region’s museums and historical sites to hiking through its forests and gorges, Tsugaru Peninsula has something to offer for all types of travelers. So why not plan your next trip to this hidden gem of northern Japan and discover its many wonders for yourself?
