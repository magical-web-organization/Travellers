Introduction to Yomogita

Located in the Tsugaru Peninsula, in the Aomori prefecture of Tohoku, Yomogita is a small village that’s often overlooked by tourists. This peaceful town is surrounded by mountains and the sea, which makes it a perfect destination for those who love nature and outdoor activities. The region is known for its rural charm, local traditions, and stunning landscapes, making it an ideal destination for those who want to explore the authentic side of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Yomogita

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yomogita Folklore Museum: The museum showcases the local culture and history of the Tsugaru region. It offers a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle and customs of the people who lived here for generations.
  • Yomogita Shrine: The Shinto shrine is believed to have been established in the 8th century and is dedicated to three gods who protect the village. It’s a serene place with well-preserved architecture and beautiful natural surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Yomogita: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Iwaki: Also known as Tsugaru-Fuji, Mount Iwaki is a stratovolcano that offers stunning views of the Tsugaru Peninsula. It’s a popular hiking spot and is beautiful during the autumn season when the surrounding forests turn into a mosaic of colors.
  • Yomogita Beach: Located just a short walk from Yomogita Village, this secluded beach is perfect for those who want to relax and enjoy the stunning coastal views. The beach is known for its clear water and is a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling.
  • Osorezan: Translated as “Fear Mountain,” Osorezan is a volcanic mountain located in the neighboring town of Mutsu. It’s considered one of the holiest sites in Japan and is believed to be the gateway to the afterlife. The site is home to various temples and shrines and is a unique place to visit for those interested in spiritual tourism.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yomogita

  • Yomogita Forest Trail: This is a popular hiking and biking trail that takes you through the dense forests of Yomogita. The trail is well-marked, and there are several rest areas along the way.
  • Tsugaru Coastal Cycling Route: This scenic route runs along the coast of the Tsugaru Peninsula and is ideal for those who want to explore the region on two wheels. The route stretches for over 80 kilometers and offers stunning panoramic views of the sea and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Yomogita: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the calm waters of Mutsu Bay on a kayaking tour. The tour takes you through the beautiful coastline, passing by secluded coves and cliffs.
  • Hotspring Bathing: Indulge in some relaxation time by visiting one of the many hotspring baths in the region. Yomogita boasts several hot springs with stunning views of the sea and the mountains.
  • Sake Tasting: Visit one of the local breweries and sample the famous Tsugaru Sake. The drink is made with locally sourced rice and is known for its rich and distinct flavor.

Food and Drink Scene of Yomogita

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tsugaru Specialty: Nokkedon: This is a local dish that’s made with fresh seafood and rice. The ingredients are served in a bowl, creating a delicious and filling meal.
  • Kiritampo: This is a local delicacy made with grounded rice that’s grilled on a stick. It’s usually served with a miso-based sauce and is a popular snack among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  • Tsugaru Sake: Made with locally sourced rice, this sake has a distinct and rich flavor. It’s a perfect drink to accompany a meal or to enjoy on its own.
  • Apple Juice: Aomori prefecture is known for its delicious apples, and Yomogita is no exception. The apple juice is sweet and refreshing and is a must-try beverage in the region.

Where to Stay in Yomogita: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Yomogita Onsen Ryokan: This traditional Japanese inn is located in the heart of Yomogita Village and offers an authentic Japanese experience. The inn features a hot spring bath and serves delicious local cuisine.
  • Yomogita Guesthouse Akane-yado: This charming guesthouse is perfect for budget travelers. The rooms are simple but comfortable, and the guesthouse features a communal kitchen and lounge area.
  • Yomogita Midorinokaze Resort: This modern resort is located just a short distance from Yomogita Beach. The resort boasts a hot spring bath, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant that serves delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in Yomogita: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tsugaru Nuri: This is a traditional Japanese lacquerware that’s made in the Tsugaru region. The items are usually decorated with colorful and intricate designs and make for a unique and beautiful souvenir.
  • Iwayado Tansu: The Iwayado Tansu is a type of traditional Japanese chest that’s made in the neighboring town of Noheji. The chest is made with local wood and is decorated with delicate carvings and metal fittings.

Local Culture and Customs of Yomogita

Yomogita is a small village that’s known for its rural charm and traditions. The village is home to several festivals throughout the year, including the Yomogita Taisai festival, which takes place in July. During the festival, locals dress up in traditional clothing and parade through the streets while carrying portable shrines.

Best Time to Visit Yomogita

The best time to visit Yomogita is during the autumn season (September to November) when the surrounding forests turn into a gorgeous mosaic of red, orange, and yellow. Summer (June to August) is also a great time to visit, as the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yomogita

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the region’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese inns and temples.
  • Be loud and disrespectful in public spaces.
  • Take pictures without asking for permission in religious sites and private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yomogita

Yomogita is a relatively safe place to travel, violent crime is rare, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. Keep in mind that the region is prone to natural disasters such as earthquakes and typhoons, so it’s important to follow local news and weather updates.

Health in Yomogita

The tap water in Yomogita is safe to drink, and the region has good medical services. It’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and to bring any necessary medication.

Getting to Yomogita: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yomogita

The closest airport to Yomogita is Aomori Airport, which is about a 2-hour drive from the village. The airport offers domestic flights to several major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Yomogita

There are several bus stations near Yomogita, including the Noheji Bus Terminal and the Mutsu Bus Terminal. Buses connect the region to major cities in the Aomori prefecture and beyond.

Other Options

Those traveling from Tokyo can take a train to Hachinohe Station and then transfer to a local train that goes to Noheji Station. From there, a bus or taxi can take you to Yomogita.

How to Get Around in Yomogita

Yomogita is a small village that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and buses are also available for those who want to visit neighboring towns.

Where to Go Next from Yomogita: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Towada: Located in the neighboring Aomori prefecture, Lake Towada is a stunning caldera lake that’s surrounded by forests and mountains. The lake offers various outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Hirosaki Castle: This historical castle is located in the city of Hirosaki and is known for its beautiful cherry blossom trees. The castle is open to visitors throughout the year and offers stunning views of the surrounding gardens.
  • Sannai-Maruyama Site: This well-preserved archaeological site is located in the city of Aomori and is believed to be the remains of an ancient Japanese settlement. The site offers a glimpse into the daily life and customs of the Jomon period.

Conclusion

Yomogita may be a small village, but it’s packed with unique experiences and breathtaking natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, cycling, or exploring the local culture and traditions, Yomogita has plenty to offer. Plan your trip today and discover the authentic side of Japan!
