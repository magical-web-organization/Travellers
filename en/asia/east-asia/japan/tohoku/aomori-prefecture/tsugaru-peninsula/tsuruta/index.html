Introduction to Tsuruta

Tsuruta is a charming little town located in Tsugaru Peninsula, in Aomori prefecture, Tohoku region of Japan. Here, you’ll find a wealth of natural and cultural attractions, each one unique and breathtaking. Tsuruta is known for its scenic views, delicious cuisine, and outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. In this guide, we’ll introduce you to this hidden gem of Japan and help you plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Tsuruta

Tsuruta has a lot to offer, from cultural and historical attractions to natural wonders. Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places in Tsuruta:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tsuruta City Museum of History: Learn about the history and culture of Tsuruta through interactive displays and exhibits.

  • Tsugaru Traditional Crafts Museum: See how the traditional crafts of Tsugaru Peninsula such as lacquer, pottery, and woodcarvings are made.

  • Iwakiyama Shrine: This ancient shrine is dedicated to the god of watersports and is a must-visit spot for all mythology enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Tsuruta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Iwaki: Hiking, camping, and biking are very popular here, with the sprawling Mount Iwaki as the main attraction.

  • Tsuruta Park: Enjoy a picnic or a relaxing stroll around this beautiful park that is particularly stunning in autumn when the leaves turn golden.

  • Tsurutayama Observatory: Take in the stunning nighttime views of the city from this observation deck.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsuruta

Tsuruta has many hiking and biking trails, and all levels of expertise are catered to. Some of the must-explore trails are:

  • The Mt. Iwaki Climbing Trail: a 7 km trail to the summit which takes about 4-6 hours.

  • Lake Toyoda Cycling Road: This 7-kilometre cycling road will give you a chance to enjoy the beautiful natural scenery of Tsuruta.

  • Tsuruta Park to Iwayama Shrine Hiking Trail: This trail starts at Tsuruta Park and ends at the ancient Iwayama Shrine, taking you through beautiful woods and forests.

Best Things to Do in Tsuruta: Activities and Tours

Tsuruta has many activities that travelers can engage in. Here are some of the best things to do in Tsuruta:

  • Take a Soba Making Class: Learn how to make the popular Japanese buckwheat noodle.

  • Tsugaru Shamisen Concert: Attend a concert of Tsugaru-jamisen which is a traditional Japanese instrument from Tsugaru Peninsula.

  • Fruit Picking: From strawberry to apple, Tsuruta has several farms where visitors can go fruit picking.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsuruta

Tsuruta is famous for its delicious local dishes and beverages. Here are some things to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iwaki Soba: This buckwheat noodle dish has a unique flavor perfect for a cold winter day.

  • Hatahata: This local fish is served grilled, as sushi, or in a hot pot, and is a must-try for all seafood lovers.

  • Nanbu Kohrogi Chicken: This dish features Nanbu Kohrogi chicken, a varietal known for its chewy texture and delicate taste.

Local Beverages

  • Tsuruta Sake: Made from locally grown rice, Tsuruta sake is known for its complex and refreshing flavor.

  • Tsugaru Apple Juice: Tsugaru Peninsula is known for its delicious apples, and Tsugaru Apple Juice is a refreshing way to enjoy this local specialty.

Where to Stay in Tsuruta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Tsuruta catering to various budgets. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Tsuruta Central Hotel: Located in the heart of town, Tsuruta Central Hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • La Vista Tsuruta: This modern hotel offers stunning views of Mount Iwaki and is perfect for those seeking a luxurious experience.

  • Hachinohe Grand Hotel: Situated just a short drive from Tsuruta, Hachinohe Grand Hotel offers affordable accommodation with great amenities.

What to Buy in Tsuruta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products that are worth taking home from Tsuruta:

  • Tsugaru Nuri Lacquerware: These beautifully crafted lacquer wares have been made in Tsuruta for centuries and make great souvenirs.

  • Tsuruta Sake: Bring home a bottle of Tsuruta sake to enjoy the delicious flavor of Tsuruta at home.

  • Apple Pie: Made from Tsugaru’s famous apples, this pie is a great souvenir for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsuruta

Tsuruta has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Greet with a bow: Bowing is customary in Japan, and it’s respectful to bow when meeting someone.

  • Remove shoes when entering a home: It’s customary to remove your shoes when entering a Japanese home or temple.

  • Respect personal space: Japanese people like their personal space, so avoid standing too close or touching people without permission.

Best Time to Visit Tsuruta

The best time to visit Tsuruta is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the scenery is stunning. Cherry blossom season in April and the autumn foliage from late October to early November are particularly popular among visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsuruta

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Dress modestly when visiting shrines and temples.

Don’t:

  • Interfere with wildlife or damage natural surroundings.

  • Smoke in non-designated areas.

  • Make too much noise, particularly at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsuruta

Tsuruta is a very safe area with low crime rates. However, as with any travel destination, tourists should take precautions against typical risks like pickpocketing and suchlike.

Health in Tsuruta

Tap water in urban areas is safe to drink. Medical facilities are modern and efficient with well-trained staff.

Getting to Tsuruta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tsuruta is easily accessible from other Japanese cities, with several modes of transportation available.

Airports Near Tsuruta

The nearest airport to Tsuruta is Aomori Airport, which has direct flights from several major Japanese cities. You can hire a taxi or take a bus from here to Tsuruta.

Bus Stations Near Tsuruta

Several bus companies operate regular services to Tsuruta from major Japanese cities like Tokyo, Sendai, and Sapporo.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can also take the train to the nearby Hachinohe Station and then take a bus to Tsuruta.

How to Get Around in Tsuruta

Tsuruta is a small town, and most of the popular attractions can be easily reached on foot. If you plan to visit Mount Iwaki, it’s better to rent a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Tsuruta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to spare, consider visiting other nearby destinations such as:

  • Hirosaki: Famous for its cherry blossoms and Hirosaki Castle.

  • Aomori: Discover Japanese mythology lore and history, enjoy fresh seafood, and take in the scenic views of the ocean.

  • Lake Towada: Enjoy hiking, boating, and other outdoor activities, surrounded by stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Tsuruta is a small, charming town but packed with surprises. From history and culture to natural beauty, outdoor activities, and delicious cuisine, there’s something to suit every traveler here. Plan your trip to Tsuruta carefully, and you’ll be mesmerized by the town’s unique beauty and charm.
