Introduction to Aomori

Located in the northern part of Honshu, Japan’s main island, Aomori is a city and a prefecture blessed with natural wonders and cultural heritage. The city has a vibrant food and drink scene, and it is famous for its winter festivals, where visitors can marvel at snow sculptures and enjoy local delicacies. Aomori is also the perfect base for exploring the spectacular Shirakami Sanchi Mountains, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

List of Places to Visit in Aomori

Aomori has an abundance of attractions, including historical landmarks, museums, natural wonders, and scenic views. Here is a list of must-visit places in Aomori.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aomori Museum of Art: This contemporary art museum has a vast collection of Japanese and Western art.
  • Nebuta Museum WA RASSE: This museum showcases the history and craftsmanship behind the Nebuta festival, a summer event featuring large paper lanterns in different shapes and designs.
  • Sannai-Maruyama Site: This archaeological site displays the ruins of a Jomon Period village and highlights the unique culture of the ancient inhabitants of Aomori.

Natural Attractions of Aomori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hakkoda: This mountain offers breathtaking views and is a popular destination for hiking and skiing.
  • Oirase Stream: This scenic stream is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a perfect spot for a peaceful hike.
  • Hirosaki Castle Park: This park is famous for its impressive cherry blossom trees and is a great place to visit during spring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aomori

Aomori’s mountains and forests make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Aomori.

  • Tsugaru Fujimi Lake Trail: This 12-kilometer trail offers stunning views of Lake Juniko.
  • Jogakura Bridge Cross-Country Bike Trail: This challenging 20-kilometer trail offers breathtaking views of the Hakkoda Mountains.
  • Towadako Forest Hiking Trail: This trail is a 10-kilometer loop around the picturesque Towada Lake.

Best Things to Do in Aomori: Activities and Tours

From exploring cultural heritage to outdoor adventures, Aomori has a plethora of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Aomori.

  • Visit the Hirosaki Castle Cherry Blossom Festival.
  • Take a dip in one of the many onsen (hot springs) in Aomori.
  • Watch the Nebuta festival and participate in haneto dancing.
  • Snowshoe through the Hakkoda Mountains.
  • Sample local cuisine on a food tour of Aomori.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aomori

Here is a week-long itinerary for experiencing the best of Aomori.

Food and Drink Scene of Aomori

Aomori’s cuisine boasts fresh seafood and local produce, such as apples and garlic. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Aomori.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nokke-don: A rice bowl topped with fresh seafood.
  • Kuroishi Neputa Miso Curry: A curry made with local vegetables and miso paste.
  • Sumibiyaki-Char: Charcoal-grilled seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Aomori is known for its high-quality sake, such as the popular Seishu.
  • Apple Juice: Aomori produces 50% of all apples in Japan, and the fresh apple juice is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Aomori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aomori has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Aomori.

  • Hotel JAL City Aomori: A luxury hotel located near Aomori Station.
  • Aomori Kokusai Hotel: A mid-range hotel with an onsen and a restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • Aomori Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse with shared facilities.

What to Buy in Aomori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aomori is famous for its local produce, such as apples, garlic, and fresh seafood. Here are some recommended souvenirs to bring home from Aomori.

  • Nebuta and Neputa festival souvenirs, such as lanterns and miniature floats.
  • Tsugaru-nuri lacquerware: This traditional craft is made by coating wood with layers of lacquer.
  • Local sake and apple wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Aomori

Aomori has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs. Here are some interesting facts about Aomori’s local culture.

  • Aomori’s dialect, called Tsugaru-ben, is known for its distinct intonation and vocabulary.
  • Haneto dancing is a summer festival tradition where participants wear colourful costumes and dance along with the Nebuta float.
  • In winter, locals dig snow huts called kamakura and enjoy hot sake inside.

Best Time to Visit Aomori

Aomori can be visited throughout the year. However, the best time to visit depends on the activities you plan to do.

  • Spring (March to May): Best time to visit for cherry blossom season.
  • Summer (June to August): Best time to visit for outdoor festivals and activities.
  • Autumn (September to November): Best time to visit for autumn foliage.
  • Winter (December to February): Best time to visit for winter festivals and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aomori

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Aomori.

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the cultural landmarks and participate in the festivals.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Leave a tip at restaurants or other service providers.
  • Wear shoes indoors.
  • Enter a hot spring bath without washing beforehand.

Safety and Health

Aomori is generally a safe destination, but it’s always better to be prepared. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind.

Safety in Aomori

  • Follow traffic laws and pedestrian signals when walking or biking.
  • Be aware of natural hazards, such as slippery trails or sudden changes in weather conditions.
  • Take extra precaution when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking and skiing.

Health in Aomori

  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outside.
  • Keep an eye out for insects, such as ticks, during hikes.

Getting to Aomori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aomori is easily accessible by train, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options to get you to Aomori.

Airports Near Aomori

  • Aomori Airport: The airport is located in Aomori city and offers domestic flights to major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Aomori

  • Aomori Bus Terminal: The bus terminal is located near Aomori Station and offers bus services to different parts of Aomori and neighboring prefectures.

Other Options

  • Train: Aomori is served by the Tohoku Shinkansen line, with direct connections to Tokyo and other major cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Aomori

Aomori is a relatively compact city, and most attractions are within walking distance from the city center. However, for destinations outside the city, public transportation is the best option.

  • Bus: Aomori has an extensive bus network that connects different parts of the city and neighboring towns.
  • Train: The train network in Aomori connects the city to other parts of the prefecture, such as the Shirakami Sanchi Mountains.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available but can be expensive. It’s best to check the fare beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Aomori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aomori is a great starting point for exploring the many wonders of Tohoku region. Here are some recommended destinations and excursions from Aomori.

  • Hirosaki: A charming town famous for its castle and cherry blossoms.
  • Shirakami Sanchi Mountains: A UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its pristine forests and hiking trails.
  • Hachinohe: A coastal city known for its fresh seafood and local festivals.

Conclusion

Aomori is a city that offers something for every type of traveler, whether you’re interested in cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine. With our travel guide, you can easily plan your itinerary and make the most of your visit to Aomori.
