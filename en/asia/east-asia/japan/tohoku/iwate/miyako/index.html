Introduction to Miyako

Miyako is a charming city located in the Iwate prefecture of Japan. It is famous for its stunning natural scenery and fascinating cultural heritage. Miyako is a hidden gem in Japan, which has recently gained popularity as a vacation destination for its breathtaking landscapes and the warm hospitality of its people. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Miyako effectively.

List of Places to Visit in Miyako

Miyako has a lot to offer, from historical landmarks to natural wonders. Here’s a comprehensive list of the top places to visit in Miyako:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Miyako Folk Museum: This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the traditional way of life in Miyako. Visitors can learn about the region’s history, culture, and customs through a series of interactive displays and exhibitions.

  • Kokusekishudou Memorial Hall: This museum pays tribute to the Kokusekishudou, a group of poets and writers who lived in Miyako and made significant contributions to Japanese literature.

  • Shyusse Inari Shrine: Located on the top of a hill, the Shyusse Inari Shrine is believed to bring good luck to those who visit it. The shrine is dedicated to Inari, the god of fertility, agriculture, and foxes.

Natural Attractions of Miyako: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jodogahama Beach: Jodogahama Beach is a stunning beach that is famous for its crystal clear waters and panoramic views. The beach is surrounded by towering cliffs and lush pine forests, creating a surreal backdrop.

  • Ryusendo Cave: Ryusendo Cave is a unique limestone cave system that is known for its underground lakes and rivers. Visitors can take a boat or go on foot to admire the awe-inspiring rock formations and the glittering waters.

  • Mount Hayachine: Mount Hayachine is the highest peak in the Kitakami range and is known for its spectacular views. Visitors can hike to the summit and enjoy breathtaking views of Miyako and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyako

Miyako is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. It has miles of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the natural scenery. Here are some of the best routes in Miyako:

  • Kodomari Coast Trail: This route is a scenic trail that follows the Kodomari Coastline. The trail takes you through the dense pine forest, along cliffs overlooking the sea, and sandy beaches.

  • Ishikawa Gorge Trail: This is a popular route that takes you through the beautiful Ishikawa Gorge. The trail is surrounded by tall cliffs, waterfalls, and colorful foliage.

  • Ryusendo Cave Trail: This route takes you through the Ryusendo Cave system. It is a challenging hike that involves trekking through underground tunnels and crossing underground lakes.

Best Things to Do in Miyako: Activities and Tours

Miyako offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Whether you want to explore the city’s history, culture, or natural beauty, you will find something that suits your taste. Here are some of the best things to do in Miyako:

  • Kayaking: There are plenty of opportunities to kayak along the Miyako coastline. You can rent a kayak and explore the natural beauty of the area at your own pace.

  • Onsen Bathing: There are plenty of Onsens or hot springs in Miyako where visitors can relax and soak in the thermal waters.

  • Fishing Trips: Miyako is home to several fishing villages, and visitors can take part in fishing tours and learn about the traditional techniques used in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyako

Miyako is well-known for its fresh seafood, and visitors should try the local cuisine during their visit. Here are some must-try dishes in Miyako:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Morioka Reimen: This dish is made up of cold Korean noodles served in a chilled broth, served with meat and vegetables.

  • Sasakamaboko: Sasakamaboko is a type of fish cake that is usually served in broths and soups.

  • Nanjawan: Nanjawan is a local specialty that is made of steamed beans and potatoes, seasoned with soy sauce and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Iwate Sake: Miyako’s sake is known for its bold flavors and is made with water from the nearby rivers.

  • Miyako Craft Beer: The city has several craft beer breweries that serve a range of local brews.

Where to Stay in Miyako: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Miyako has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Visitors can choose from hotels and guesthouses, ryokans or traditional Japanese inns, and even camping grounds. Here are some top accommodation options in Miyako:

  • Hotel Route Inn Miyako: This hotel is located near the Miyako Train Station and offers modern amenities, comfortable rooms, and free Wi-Fi.

  • Iwate-ken Miyoung: This guesthouse offers clean rooms, comfortable beds, and a shared kitchen area for cooking. The guesthouse also offers a free shuttle service to and from the train station.

What to Buy in Miyako: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Miyako is rich in crafts and produces unique souvenirs that are perfect for bringing home. Visitors can buy local products such as pottery, traditional crafts, and locally-grown produce. Here are some popular souvenirs to buy in Miyako:

  • Miyako-yaki Pottery: Miyako-yaki Pottery is a type of Japanese earthenware that is produced in Miyako. The pottery is decorated with traditional Japanese designs and is perfect for displaying or using for cooking and serving.

  • Nanbu Tetsubin: Nanbu Tetsubin is a type of Japanese teapot that is made of cast-iron. The teapot is decorated with intricate designs and is considered a prized possession in Japan.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyako

Miyako has its own unique customs and traditions, which are deeply rooted in its history and culture. Visitors are expected to follow certain etiquette to show respect for local culture. Here are some things you should know before visiting Miyako:

  • Remove your shoes: Visitors are expected to remove their shoes when entering homes, temples, and other buildings.

  • Respect the elderly: The Japanese have a deep respect for their elders, and visitors should show the same courtesy when interacting with them.

Best Time to Visit Miyako

The best time to visit Miyako is during the months of April to October. The weather during this time is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyako

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Miyako:

Do:

  • Be polite: The Japanese value politeness and good manners, and visitors should show the same courtesy when interacting with locals.

  • Be on time: Japanese people are punctual, and visitors should make sure to be on time for any appointments or activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside: Visitors should not wear shoes inside homes or other buildings, as it is considered impolite.

  • Be loud: Japanese people value silence and understated behavior, and visitors should avoid being loud or boisterous in public places.

Safety and Health

Miyako is a safe place to visit, and visitors should not be concerned about their safety during their stay. However, visitors should be aware of possible health risks and take precautions accordingly.

Safety in Miyako

Miyako is a safe place, and visitors can travel around the city without fear of crime or violence. However, visitors should be cautious when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking or kayaking and should adhere to safety guidelines.

Health in Miyako

Visitors should be aware of the possible health risks when traveling to Miyako. The city is prone to earthquakes and typhoons, and visitors should be prepared for possible emergencies. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito bites as the region is prone to mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever.

Getting to Miyako: Transportation Options and Logistics

Miyako is located in the Iwate prefecture of Japan and can be reached by plane, train, or bus. Here are some transportation options for getting to Miyako:

Airports Near Miyako

The nearest airport to Miyako is the Iwate Hanamaki Airport, which is located about 90mins away. Visitors can take a train or bus from the airport to Miyako.

Bus Stations Near Miyako

Miyako has a bus station that serves several major cities in Japan, including Tokyo and Sendai. Visitors can take a bus to Miyako from any of these cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Miyako from any major city in Japan. The Miyako Station is served by several train lines, making it easy to get to Miyako.

How to Get Around in Miyako

Miyako is a small city, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bike. However, visitors can also use public transport to get around the city. Buses and taxis are available, and visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Miyako: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miyako is surrounded by several charming towns and villages that are worth visiting. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Miyako:

  • Hachimantai: Hachimantai is a town known for its hot springs and scenic beauty. Visitors can explore the Hachimantai National Park and relax in one of the town’s many hot springs.

  • Tono: Tono is a charming town known for its folktales and traditional crafts. Visitors can explore the Tono Folk Village and learn about the region’s history and culture.

Conclusion

Miyako is a wonderful destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Visitors to Miyako can enjoy hiking, kayaking, and exploring the city’s many museums and historical landmarks. With its warm hospitality, delicious food, and stunning scenery, Miyako is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Japan.
