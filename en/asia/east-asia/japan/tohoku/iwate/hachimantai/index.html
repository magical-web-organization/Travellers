Introduction to Hachimantai

Located in Iwate, in the Tohoku region of Japan, Hachimantai is a serene mountainous area surrounded by natural beauty. It is known for its vast expanse of hot springs, scenic hiking trails, and ski resorts that attract tourists from around the world. Hachimantai’s rich cultural heritage is punctuated by museums and historical places, displaying the harmony between the past and the present. This guide will provide useful insights for exploring the highlights of this tranquil town, including where to go, what to eat, and the must-try activities.

List of Places to Visit in Hachimantai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in delving into Hachimantai’s rich history and culture, make sure to include its museums and historical sites on your itinerary:

  • Hachimantai Folk Museum – showcases the town’s agrarian lifestyle and its ancient history dating back to the Jomon Period (14,000 B.C – 300 B.C)
  • Jodo-ji Temple – belongs to the Tendai sect and has a history of over 1300 years
  • Motsu-ji Temple – a UNESCO World Heritage site with beautifully preserved ancient gardens and buildings
  • Tenshochi Park – a popular spot for cherry blossom viewing during spring and known for its historically significant Samurai Bridge
  • Tsutanuma Pond – a symbolic reflection of Hachimantai’s natural beauty and a sacred place in the region’s folklore

Natural Attractions of Hachimantai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hachimantai offers a range of outdoor activities for nature lovers and adventurers. Here are some noteworthy natural attractions that you should not miss:

  • Appi Kogen Ski Resort – a world-class ski resort that offers a variety of terrain for skiers and snowboarders alike.
  • Hachimantai Hot Springs – 15 unique hot springs in Hachimantai offer a refreshing and tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of busy life.
  • Mt. Iwate – Hachimantai’s tallest mountain offers a breathtaking view of the entire town.
  • Hachimantai Aspite Line – an unmissable scenic drive that offers stunning panoramic views of the Hachimantai mountain range and surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hachimantai

Hachimantai offers a plethora of exploration options for hikers and bikers. Grab your hiking or biking gear and check out these trails:

  • Panorama Line – a cycling and walking trail that offers panoramic views of the Hachimantai mountain range.
  • Goshoishi Pond Trail – a 4.4 km hiking trail that leads to Goshoishi Pond and showcases the area’s natural beauty.
  • Mount Iwate Trail – a 6.2 km summit trail that offers a challenging but rewarding experience for seasoned hikers.

Best Things to Do in Hachimantai: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural and cultural attractions, Hachimantai provides a multitude of stimulating activities and tours for visitors.

  • Snowshoeing Tours – enjoy the winter wonderland of Hachimantai with a guided snowshoeing tour.
  • Rafting and Canoeing – navigate Hachimantai’s rivers and lakes with a guided canoe tour.
  • Soba Making Classes – Hachimantai is famous for its buckwheat soba noodles, so why not take a class and learn how to make them?

Week-Long Itinerary for Hachimantai

If you’re visiting Hachimantai for a week, here’s an itinerary with some recommended activities.

Day Activities
1 Explore the Hachimantai Folk Museum, Temple Trail, and historical monuments
2 Go skiing or snowboarding at Appi Kogen Ski Resort
3 Visit the Hachimantai Hot Springs and surrounding onsens
4 Explore the Mount Iwate area and hike Goshoishi Pond Trail
5 Take a cycling tour of the Panorama Line
6 Try your hand at making soba noodles, then relax at a nearby park
7 Take a guided canoe tour, visit Aspite Line or do some souvenir shopping

Food and Drink Scene of Hachimantai

Beyond the region’s stunning natural wonders, Hachimantai also offers an exquisite culinary experience. Make sure to sample the following dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wanko Soba – Wanko soba is a Hachimantai specialty, which is a unique style of soba noodles served in small dishes.
  • Morioka Reimen – a refreshing cold noodle dish, perfect for summers in Hachimantai.
  • Jajamen – a local take on Chinese sesame noodles with a sweet flavor and a spicy kick.

Local Beverages

  • Iwate Sake – Hachimantai is known for its high-quality sake, brewed by traditional methods.
  • Mugi Shochu – a traditional Japanese beverage, made from barley and wheat that is grown in the Hachimantai region.

Where to Stay in Hachimantai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hachimantai offers an excellent selection of accommodation options, catering to every type of traveler’s preferences.

  • Hachimantai Royal Hotel – A luxurious option with an onsen and traditional Japanese decor.
  • Atarashiya Ryokan – traditional style guesthouse perfect for relaxing and indulging in local cuisine.
  • Sanshichi-so – an affordable and comfortable option with Western-style rooms.

What to Buy in Hachimantai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenir shopping, Hachimantai has plenty to offer.

  • Local Produce – Buy fresh local fruits, vegetables, and dairy products from Hachimantai’s farmers’ markets.
  • Iwate Sake – Hachimantai’s locally brewed sake is a must-try, and visitors can purchase it directly from the breweries.
  • Wanko Soba Set – Bring home a souvenir set of Hachimantai’s unique wanko soba noodles and bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Hachimantai

Hachimantai’s people are incredibly proud of their traditional culture and welcome visitors to share their passion. Here are a few cultural customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect for Elders – Hachimantai has a long history of honoring and respecting elders, and visitors should always follow suit.
  • Onsen Etiquette – Pay attention to onsen rules and customs of not wearing clothes inside the hot springs and using a towel for modesty or to dry off.
  • Respectful Greetings – It is customary to say “Ohisashiburi desu” when re-encountering someone you haven’t seen in a while, which means “long time, no see.”

Best Time to Visit Hachimantai

The best time to visit Hachimantai depends on what you’re looking for.

  • Spring (March to May) – Spring weather provides perfect conditions for cherry blossom viewing, hiking, and outdoor activities.
  • Summer (June to August) – Summer is the best time for outdoor adventures such as cycling or canoeing and enjoying the scenic beauty around.
  • Fall (September to November) – The fall season is a photographer’s dream come true, where visitors can enjoy the beautiful autumn colors and hot springs in warm and welcoming temperatures.
  • Winter (December to February) – If winter sports are your thing, this is the best time to visit the ski resorts and enjoy the snow activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hachimantai

Do:

  • Do try Wanko Soba. It is Hachimantai’s signature dish.
  • Do be respectful of the nature and culture.
  • Do try the traditional onsen experience. It is a quintessential Japanese experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t enter a shrine or any traditional venue without removing shoes.
  • Don’t try eating while walking or on public transport. It is considered inappropriate in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hachimantai

Hachimantai is generally a safe place, and you need not worry about significant crime or theft. However, you should keep in mind regular safety protocols and be careful while engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Hachimantai

Hachimantai is known for its hot springs, which are said to have a variety of health benefits, including reducing stress and fatigue, helping blood circulation, and providing pain relief. The area also has modern medical facilities and emergency services to cater to visitors’ needs.

Getting to Hachimantai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hachimantai

  • Iwate Hanamaki Airport – is the closest airport to Hachimantai, located around 1 hour and 30 minutes by bus.

Bus Stations Near Hachimantai

  • Morioka Station – Buses to Hachimantai leave regularly from the West Exit bus stop.
  • Appi Kogen Station – Enjoy a direct bus to Hachimantai from Appi Kogen Station

Other Options

  • Taxi – Taxis are available from Iwate Hanamaki Airport and Morioka Station to Hachimantai.
  • Car Hire – Renting a car in Hachimantai is also an option for easy access to places of interest.

How to Get Around in Hachimantai

Hachimantai is an extensively connected and easy-to-navigate town, and there are several options to get around:

  • Bus – The most popular and affordable option for navigating within Hachimantai.
  • Car Hire/Taxi – Taxis and rental cars are available but can be expensive.
  • Cycling – Renting bicycles is a great option for those who want to explore the region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Hachimantai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Hachimantai, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Morioka – A beautiful city, rich in history and culture, located 1.5 hours south of Hachimantai.
  • Kitakami – Also rich in history and culture, Kitakami is just an hour’s drive from Hachimantai.
  • Akita – A beautiful prefecture, famous for its pristine nature and traditional festivals, Akita is just under two hours west of Hachimantai.

Conclusion

Hachimantai is truly an unmissable destination, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural wonders, and endless activities. Make sure to plan your trip with this guide in mind to make your visit to Hachimantai a memorable one.
