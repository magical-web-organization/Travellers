Introduction to Tono

Tono is a quaint Japanese town located in Iwate, in Tohoku, in East Asia. Famous for its traditional folklore, breathtaking natural scenery, and delicious cuisine, Tono is a perfect destination for travelers yearning for an authentic Japanese experience. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will be exploring the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, and transportation options in Tono, Japan. Read on to discover the hidden gems of Tono.

List of Places to Visit in Tono

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tono is steeped in rich history and folklore, and there are several museums and historical places that are worth visiting to understand the town’s cultural significance. Here are the top places to visit:

1. Tono Folklore Museum

The Tono Folklore Museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the town’s folklore traditions. It features exhibits on local festivals, traditional costumes, and ancient myths that have inspired generations of Japanese people.

2. Kyodo Shiryo-kan

The Kyodo Shiryo-kan is an impressive cultural center that celebrates the art of weaving. It has a vast collection of traditional Japanese textiles and tools used in the weaving process.

3. Kappabuchi Pool

Kappabuchi Pool is a small pond located in the center of Tono, and it is one of the most iconic locations in the town’s folklore. According to ancient myths, the pond is said to be the dwelling place of a mischievous water sprite called “kappa.”

Natural Attractions of Tono: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tono is situated in a region of Japan that is well-known for its unbelievable natural scenery. If you are a nature lover or a thrill-seeker, here are three must-visit places for you:

1. Takasagoyama Natural Park

Takasagoyama Natural Park is a stunning park featuring lush green forests and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. It is an excellent spot for hiking, picnicking, and just enjoying the serene beauty of nature.

2. Kamoshika Falls

Kamoshika Falls is one of the most scenic waterfalls in Japan, located in the heart of Tono. The waterfall is surrounded by verdant forests and is a perfect spot for a picnic with family and friends.

3. Koiwai Farm

Koiwai Farm is a massive park located on the outskirts of Tono that offers a range of outdoor activities such as horseback riding, bike tours, and nature walks. There is also a farm on the property where you can learn about Japanese agriculture and indulge in delicious dairy products.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tono

Tono is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers who love immersing themselves in nature. Here are the top hiking/biking routes you should not miss:

1. Tono Valley Trail

The Tono Valley Trail is a popular hiking path that runs along the scenic Tono Valley. The trail is an exciting combination of natural beauty and historical significance, and it offers hikers an opportunity to explore the town’s folklore through several shrines, temples, and ancient ruins.

2. Iwaiwa Trail

The Iwaiwa Trail is a mountain path that leads to the top of Mount Iwate. It is a challenging hike, but it rewards visitors with exceptional views of the surrounding mountains and a unique experience of Japanese wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Tono: Activities and Tours

There are numerous exciting things to do in Tono, from cultural experiences, outdoor adventures to culinary delights. Here are the top recommendations:

1. Experience Traditional Japanese Crafts

Tono is renowned for its traditional Japanese crafts, such as pottery, weaving, and cutlery. Visitors can enroll in workshops at local craft centers and learn how to create their unique handmade souvenirs.

2. Tono Koutsuu An

Tono Koutsuu An is a unique experience that allows tourists to ride in traditional Japanese rickshaws and explore the town’s scenic beauty.

3. Savor the Local Cuisine

Tono’s culinary scene is not to be missed, and there are several traditional dishes that visitors must try. The most popular dishes are Wanko Soba, Iwate Wagyu, and Mochi Rice Cake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tono

If you are planning to spend a week in Tono, here is an itinerary that you can follow to make the most out of your visit:

Day 1 Arrival in Tono, Visit Kappabuchi Pool, and Tono Folklore Museum
Day 2 Hike the Tono Valley Trail
Day 3 Visit Kyodo Shiryo-kan and Kamoshika Falls
Day 4 Outdoor activities at Koiwai Farm
Day 5 Take a Rickshaw tour of Tono
Day 6 Visit Iwate Museum of Art in Morioka City
Day 7 Departure from Tono

Where to Stay in Tono: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tono offers tourists a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are the top recommendations:

1. Iwate Hotel

Iwate Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in Morioka City. It offers spacious rooms, exceptional amenities, and an ideal location just minutes away from Tono’s top attractions.

2. Guesthouse Tono

Guesthouse Tono is a cozy accommodation option located in the heart of Tono. It offers private rooms, dormitory-style accommodation, and a shared kitchen that guests can utilize to cook their meals.

What to Buy in Tono: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tono is renowned for its traditional crafts and unique souvenirs that tourists can purchase to take a piece of Japan back home. Here are the top items you must buy:

1. Nanbu Tekki Ironware

Nanbu Tekki Ironware is a type of traditional steel pottery that is famous in Japan. It is the perfect souvenir to take back home and comes in a range of styles and sizes.

2. Local Sake

Tono is home to several local breweries that offer a range of exquisite Japanese sake. The sake is a quintessential Japanese souvenir that you can gift to your friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Tono

Tono is renowned for its traditional Japanese folklore, which plays an essential role in shaping the town’s cultural identity. Visitors must familiarize themselves with the local customs to understand the town’s cultural significance.

Best Time to Visit Tono

The best time to visit Tono is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is breathtaking. The summer season is also a great time to visit as it is the peak season for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tono

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Be courteous and polite to the locals
  • Take off your shoes inside temples and other sacred places

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink on the streets
  • Litter in public spaces
  • Touch the exhibits in museums and other cultural centers

Safety and Health

Tono is a safe destination for tourists, and visitors must take basic precautions to ensure their safety. Here are the safety and health recommendations:

Safety in Tono

  • Avoid walking alone during late hours
  • Keep an eye on your belongings
  • Wear appropriate hiking gear and check weather conditions before embarking on outdoor activities

Health in Tono

  • It is advisable to carry mosquito repellent as the town is near forests where mosquitoes can be found
  • Always carry water and avoid dehydration

Getting to Tono: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tono

The nearest international airport to Tono is Narita International Airport, located approximately 400 km away. However, travelers can also use Iwate Hanamaki Airport, which is local and only approximately 60 km away.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Tono by bullet train or local trains. A simple train ride from Tokyo to the city of Morioka, which is only 13 km away from Tono, takes an estimated 2.5 hours.

How to Get Around in Tono

Tono has several transportation options, including buses and taxis. Renting a bicycle is another affordable and fun way to explore the town’s beauty.

Where to Go Next from Tono: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions from Tono include:

  • Hiraizumi: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located approximately 50 km away
  • Geibi Gorge: A beautiful natural gorge located just north of Tono
  • Morioka City: The Prefectural capital of Iwate and a cultural hub located only a short distance away from Tono

Conclusion

Tono is a stunning traditional Japanese town with a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. It is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic experience of Japan. We hope this tour guide has been helpful in planning your visit to Tono and exploring the town’s hidden gems. Happy travels!
