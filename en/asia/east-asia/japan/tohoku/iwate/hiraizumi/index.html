Introduction to Hiraizumi

Located in Iwate, in Tohoku, in Japan, Hiraizumi is a historic town that is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The town boasts a rich cultural heritage and ancient history from the Heian Period, making it a must-visit destination for history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi is home to several historical sites and attractions that are worth exploring. Here are some of the top places to visit in the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chuson-ji Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Chuson-ji Temple is a Buddhist temple that was founded in 850AD. It is home to several national treasures, including the golden Konjikido Hall.

  • Motsu-ji Temple: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, Motsu-ji Temple was built in 850AD and is home to the famous Pure Land Garden.

  • Takkoku no Iwaya Bishamon-do: This temple is carved into a cliff and boasts stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Genbikei Gorge: A scenic gorge that is famous for its crystal clear water and natural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Hiraizumi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Geibikei Gorge: A scenic gorge that is famous for its stunning waterfalls and rock formations.

  • Mt. Iwate: A majestic mountain that is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking and camping.

  • Lake Tazawa: Japan’s deepest lake, Lake Tazawa is a popular spot for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi has several trails and routes that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the top routes include:

  • Hiraizumi Walking Trail: This trail runs through the town, passing by many of the top attractions.

  • Hiraizumi Cycling Road: A popular route for cycling enthusiasts, this road passes by several scenic spots.

  • Mt. Iwate Trail: A challenging trail that leads to the top of Mt. Iwate, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Hiraizumi: Activities and Tours

Hiraizumi offers several activities and tours that are perfect for visitors who want to experience the town’s culture and history. Here are some of the top things to do in Hiraizumi:

  • Samurai Experience: Visitors can experience what it was like to be a samurai by participating in a samurai training program.

  • Sake Brewery Tour: Hiraizumi is known for its sake, and visitors can take a tour of a local sake brewery and learn about the production process.

  • Zen Meditation: Visitors can take part in a traditional Zen meditation session at one of the town’s temples.

Food and Drink Scene of Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi is known for its traditional Japanese cuisine and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Hiraizumi.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wanko Soba: A local specialty, Wanko Soba is a type of soba noodle served in small bowls.

  • Mochi: A traditional Japanese dessert made from glutinous rice.

  • Nanbu Bijin Sake: Hiraizumi is famous for its sake, and one of the best is Nanbu Bijin Sake.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: As mentioned earlier, Hiraizumi is famous for its sake, and visitors can try several varieties throughout the town.

  • Green Tea: Hiraizumi is also known for its green tea, which is grown in the surrounding mountains.

Where to Stay in Hiraizumi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hiraizumi offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top options:

  • Kura Hotel Hiraizumi: A luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of Chuson-ji Temple.

  • Hiraizumi Hot Springs Ryokan: A traditional Japanese inn that is perfect for travelers who want to experience Japanese-style accommodation.

  • Fujiya Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Hiraizumi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hiraizumi is famous for its local crafts and souvenirs, many of which make for great gifts or keepsakes. Here are some top products to look out for:

  • Nanbu Ironware: A type of traditional Japanese ironware that is produced in the surrounding mountains.

  • Hiraizumi Kokeshi: A type of wooden doll that is unique to Hiraizumi.

  • Wanko Soba Keychains: For a fun and unique souvenir, look for keychains that feature the local specialty, Wanko Soba.

Local Culture and Customs of Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi has a rich cultural heritage and traditions that are still alive today. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Hiraizumi

The best time to visit Hiraizumi is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild and the landscapes are stunning. However, the town is also beautiful in the winter, when it is covered in snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hiraizumi

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Hiraizumi:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering a temple or Japanese-style accommodation.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Take part in Zen meditation or other cultural activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural sites.

  • Touch or damage any artifacts or historic structures.

  • Speak loudly or disturb the peace.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi is a safe place for visitors, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Hiraizumi

Japan has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect to receive quality care in the event of an emergency. However, visitors should still take basic health precautions, such as washing their hands frequently and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Hiraizumi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hiraizumi is located approximately 500km north of Tokyo, making it easily accessible by train, bus or car.

Airports Near Hiraizumi

The nearest airport to Hiraizumi is Hanamaki Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi has several bus stations that connect it with other cities and towns in the region, including Morioka Station and Ichinoseki Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Hiraizumi, which is approximately 5 hours from Tokyo.

How to Get Around in Hiraizumi

Hiraizumi is a small town, making it easy to get around on foot or by bike. However, visitors can also take the local bus or hire a taxi to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Hiraizumi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hiraizumi is located in the Tohoku region of Japan, which has several other destinations and attractions that are worth exploring. Here are some top options:

  • Matsushima Bay: A scenic bay that is famous for its picturesque islands and pine trees.

  • Sendai: The largest city in the Tohoku region, Sendai is known for its historic castle and famous ramen.

  • Aomori: A city located on the northern tip of Honshu, Aomori is famous for its Nebuta Festival and apples.

Conclusion

Hiraizumi is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors a glimpse into Japan’s rich cultural heritage and history. With its stunning temples, scenic natural landscapes, and delicious local cuisine, it’s no wonder that the town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
