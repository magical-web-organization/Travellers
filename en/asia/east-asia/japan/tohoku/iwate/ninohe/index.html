Introduction to Ninohe

Ninohe is a city located in Iwate Prefecture, in the Tohoku region of Japan. Known for its stunning natural scenery and rich cultural heritage, Ninohe offers visitors a unique and authentic Japanese experience. The city is easily accessible by bus or train from various parts of Japan, making it an ideal destination for both solo travelers and families.

List of Places to Visit in Ninohe

Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor adventurer, Ninohe boasts a diverse range of attractions to explore. Here’s a list of the top places to visit during your trip to Ninohe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ninohe City Museum: This museum houses a collection of ancient artifacts and cultural items from Ninohe’s past. Visitors can learn about the city’s history and culture through interactive exhibits and displays.

  • Komonjo Park: Located just outside the city center, Komonjo Park is home to several historical buildings and museums. The former residence of the Narita family, Komonjo is a beautifully preserved example of traditional Japanese architecture.

  • Iwate-Aomori Tategamori Sakura Park: Located on the border of Iwate and Aomori prefectures, this park is home to a beautiful cherry blossom grove and several historical landmarks. Visitors can explore the park’s walking trails and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Natural Attractions of Ninohe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Himekami: This mountain is a popular hiking destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and takes hikers past several waterfalls and scenic spots.

  • Kagami Lake: This lake is a popular spot for fishing and boating, and offers some of the most stunning views in the area. Visitors can rent a boat or take a leisurely stroll around the shoreline.

  • Waga Highlands: Located just outside Ninohe, the Waga Highlands are a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding in the winter. During the summer months, visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ninohe

Ninohe is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts, offering a variety of trails and routes for all skill levels. Here are a few of the best routes to explore during your visit:

  • Hachimantai Aspite Line: This scenic route takes visitors through the heart of the Hachimantai Mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Ninohe Youth Hostel to Kogen Station: This route takes visitors through some of Ninohe’s most picturesque scenery, including rice paddies, forests, and streams.

  • Mount Himekami Trail: Hiking to the top of Mount Himekami rewards visitors with glorious views of the area. The trail takes hikers past stunning waterfalls and other scenic spots.

Best Things to Do in Ninohe: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Ninohe:

  • Ninohe Samurai Residences Tour: This guided tour takes visitors through some of Ninohe’s most historic neighborhoods, showcasing the city’s samurai legacy.

  • Ninohe Sake Brewery Tour: Ninohe is famous for its high-quality sake, and visitors can take a tour of the city’s breweries to learn about the traditional methods and taste a variety of sake varieties.

  • Fukada Farming Experience: Join a local farming family for a hands-on experience on their farm. Visitors get to plant and harvest seasonal crops and learn about traditional Japanese farming techniques.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ninohe

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a weeklong trip to Ninohe:

Day 1: Arrive in Ninohe and explore the city center.

Day 2: Visit the Ninohe City Museum and Komonjo Park.

Day 3: Hike the Mount Himekami Trail or bike the Ninohe Youth Hostel to Kogen Station route.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Waga Highlands for skiing or snowboarding in winter or hiking and camping in summer.

Day 5: Explore Kagami Lake and nearby attractions.

Day 6: Take a tour of the Ninohe Sake breweries or visit a local farm.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the city center before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Ninohe

Ninohe is known for its traditional cuisine, which features fresh seafood, high-quality rice, and locally grown vegetables. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiritanpo: A rice dish made by pounding rice into a stick shape and roasting it over an open flame. It’s often served with a variety of side dishes.

  • Sake Sashimi: Fresh slices of raw fish marinated in sake and served with soy sauce and wasabi.

  • Sasakama: Grilled rice cake wrapped with char, a freshwater fish that is unique to the region.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Ninohe is famous for its high-quality sake, which is made using traditional methods and locally grown rice.

  • Green Tea: Ninohe is also known for its high-quality green tea, which has a delicate flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Ninohe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ninohe offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Hotel Route Inn Ninohe Ekimae: This hotel is conveniently located near the train station and offers comfortable, modern rooms.

  • Kokumin Shukusha Hakkoda Sanso: Located in the Waga Highlands, this cozy guesthouse is the perfect place to relax and enjoy the surrounding scenery.

  • Ninohe Youth Hostel: A great option for budget travelers, this youth hostel offers clean, comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ninohe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring home, Ninohe has plenty of options. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Kiritanpo Mochi: A delicious treat made from kiritanpo and mochi.

  • Nanbu Hishikuri Lacquerware: Handcrafted lacquerware made using traditional methods passed down through generations of artisans.

  • Iwaya Kobo Crafts: A local crafts shop selling a variety of handmade pottery, traditional Japanese cloth, and other unique items.

Local Culture and Customs of Ninohe

Ninohe has a rich cultural heritage, with a legacy that dates back over 1,000 years. Visitors can learn about the city’s history and traditions by exploring its museums, temples, and other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Ninohe

The best time to visit Ninohe depends on your interests. If you’re interested in winter sports, the best time to visit is during the winter months when skiing and snowboarding are popular activities. If you prefer hiking and outdoor adventures, the spring and fall months offer mild weather and beautiful scenery. The summer months can be hot and humid, but they’re also a great time to enjoy water sports and other outdoor activities.

  • Do be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

  • Do take advantage of the city’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.

  • Don’t disrespect local culture and customs.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help or directions if you need them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ninohe

Ninohe is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. As with any travel destination, visitors should take basic precautions to stay safe, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Ninohe

Ninohe has good healthcare facilities, with several clinics and hospitals located in the city center. Visitors are advised to take out travel insurance before their trip to ensure they’re covered in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Ninohe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ninohe is easily accessible by bus or train from various parts of Japan. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Ninohe

  • Hanamaki Airport (Iwate)

Bus Stations Near Ninohe

  • Ninohe Station Bus Terminal
  • Ninohe Ekimae Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • JR East Rail Pass: This pass allows unlimited travel on JR East Rail lines, including the Tohoku Shinkansen.

How to Get Around in Ninohe

Ninohe has a reliable public transportation system, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Ninohe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ninohe is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations in the Tohoku region. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Hachimantai National Park: A beautiful national park known for its stunning scenery and hot springs.

  • Lake Towada: A large crater lake located on the border of Aomori and Akita prefectures, known for its scenic beauty and cherry blossoms.

  • Morioka City: The capital city of Iwate Prefecture, known for its historic buildings, museums, and teahouses.

Conclusion

Ninohe offers visitors a unique and authentic Japanese experience, with its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor adventurer, Ninohe has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and start exploring this beautiful corner of Japan.
