## Introduction to Towada-Hachimantai National Park

Towada-Hachimantai National Park is a popular destination in Tohoku, Japan. The park is located in the northern part of Honshu, the largest of Japan’s islands. It stretches across the Aomori, Akita, and Iwate Prefectures and is known for its stunning natural beauty, volcanic landscapes, hot springs, and vibrant local culture. With its diverse range of attractions and activities, Towada-Hachimantai National Park has something for everyone, from families with young children to adventurous trekkers and seasoned travelers.

## List of Places to Visit in Towada-Hachimantai National Park

### Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Towada-Hachimantai National Park is not only known for its natural beauty but also for its historical and cultural significance. One of the most iconic cultural attractions of the park is the Towada Art Center, an art museum that hosts various exhibitions throughout the year. Pictured above, it’s located in Aomori prefecture and boasts a breathtaking modern design nestled in a forest.

For history buffs, the Tsuta Onsen History Museum is a must-visit place. This museum showcases the history of the Tsuta Onsen, a hot spring that has been around for over 800 years. You can see how the hot spring water was used for bathing, cooking, and brewing sake in the past.

### Natural Attractions of Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Towada-Hachimantai National Park offers a wide range of natural attractions that are sure to leave you in awe. From crystal clear lakes and waterfalls to dense forests and mountain trails, the park presents a diverse range of outdoor adventures that are perfect for nature lovers.

Oirase Stream, a river that flows for 14 km along the eastern edge of Lake Towada, is a popular attraction for visitors to Towada-Hachimantai National Park. You can rent bikes and cycle along the riverbank or take a leisurely walk that will take you through a beautiful forest with cascading waterfalls of every shape and size.

For those who prefer a more rugged outdoor experience, the Oyu Stone Mountain Trail is an excellent option. This trail runs along the volcanic range and offers a stunning bird’s eye view of the park.

## Hiking/Biking Routes of Towada-Hachimantai National Park

One of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of Towada-Hachimantai National Park is on foot or on a bicycle. The park offers various hiking and biking routes, depending on your fitness level and interests. Your effort will surely be rewarded with stunning views and unforgettable memories.

The Hachimantai Aspite Line is a popular biking route that connects the Hachimantai plateau to the Towada area. Along the way, you’ll be able to enjoy the stunning beauty of the plateau, including the changing seasons’ breathtaking colors and the peaks of the Hachimantai Mountains in the distance.

For hiking enthusiasts, the trail to the summit of Mt. Hachimantai is a must-do. This trail will take you through a beautiful forest and past colorful wildflowers to the top of the Hachimantai Mountains, where you’ll be rewarded with a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and forests.

## Best Things to Do in Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Towada-Hachimantai National Park offers a wide range of activities that are not to be missed. One of these is to have an authentic hot spring experience. There are many hot springs (and hot spring hotels) in the area that you can visit, so it is simply up to you to choose one of your favorite hot spring experiences.

Another activity that you can experience is to join a local tour. Experience authentic Japanese culture by taking a guided tour of the Hachimantai Shirakami Observatory. You’ll get to see the observatory’s excellent views and learn about the history of the natural preservation of the park.

## Week-Long Itinerary for Towada-Hachimantai National Park

If you have a week to spare in Towada-Hachimantai National Park, it will give you ample time to experience the park’s highlights. A suggested itinerary for your week-long visit is as follows:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the Lake Towada Oirase Stream area by bike or on foot.
  • Day 3: Hike in the Hachimantai Aspite Line, then visit the nearby Tsuta Onsen History Museum.
  • Day 4: Take a hot spring bath and explore the local cuisine.
  • Day 5-6: Admire the views of the Hachimantai Mountains from a bird’s-eye view while hiking.
  • Day 7: Take part in a tour to learn more about the park’s history and the culture of the local communities.

## Food and Drink Scene of Towada-Hachimantai National Park

### Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine of Towada-Hachimantai National Park includes a variety of dishes made with fresh and locally sourced ingredients. One of the must-try dishes is the infamous Inaniwa udon, a type of udon noodle that’s known for its unique texture and flavor.

Nanbu Tekki, a type of traditional ironware, is also produced in the area. So grab a traditional ironware teapot and have a hot cup of authentic Japanese tea for yourself or share it with your travel companions.

### Local Beverages

While in Towada-Hachimantai National Park, you shouldn’t miss the chance to try local sake. Produced with clear water from the park, the local sake is considered as one of the best in Northern Japan. Another popular beverage that is popular and can be found all over Japan is calpis, one of Japan’s original soft drinks.

## Where to Stay in Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels or cozy guesthouses, Towada-Hachimantai National Park offers a variety of accommodation options that will suit any style of traveler. There are many types of accommodations available throughout the park, ranging from traditional Japanese ryokans to modern hotels.

## What to Buy in Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home Towada-Hachimantai National Park’s flavors and culture with you! The perfect souvenir for yourself or your loved ones includes Nanbu Tekki ironware, local sake, or Inaniwa udon.

## Local Culture and Customs of Towada-Hachimantai National Park

Respect for nature and traditional Japanese culture is deeply embedded in the local community’s customs and practices. For example, at hot springs, you’ll be asked to scrub yourself before entering the bath area - this is because Japanese people consider it rude to enter a bath with dirty skin. Therefore, it is essential to be familiar with Japanese customs and practices before going to the park.

## Best Time to Visit Towada-Hachimantai National Park

The best time to visit Towada-Hachimantai National Park is from the end of May to the beginning of June or from late September to early October. The park’s weather is most pleasant during these periods as there are fewer crowds, and the autumn colors add to the park’s natural beauty.

## Do’s and Don’ts of Towada-Hachimantai National Park

### Do:

  • Respect the customs and practices of the local communities.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting shrines, temples, or museums.
  • Try local cuisine and experience the beauty of nature.

### Don’t:

  • Leave your trash behind.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Disturb wildlife.

## Safety and Health

### Safety in Towada-Hachimantai National Park

Towada-Hachimantai National Park is relatively safe, and Japan has one of the lowest crime rates in the world. However, be careful when hiking as the trails can be challenging and may have rough terrain. Always check the weather and make sure to bring enough water with you when hiking to prevent dehydration.

### Health in Towada-Hachimantai National Park

Towada-Hachimantai National Park is isolated, and medical facilities are not accessible in some areas. For this reason, it is essential to take precautions when traveling in the park. Cover yourself adequately when outside, as the sun can be intense during summer.

## Getting to Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Towada-Hachimantai National Park is accessible by both train and bus. It takes around 3-4 hours to get from Tokyo to the park by train. If you’re coming internationally, you can board a flight to Tokyo and take a train to Tohoku.

### Airports Near Towada-Hachimantai National Park

The nearest airport to Towada-Hachimantai National Park is the Aomori Airport. From there, you can make your way to the park by bus or rental car (approx. 100-120 minutes).

### Bus Stations Near Towada-Hachimantai National Park

Buses from Morioka Station and Aomori Station are available to Towada-Hachimantai National Park. Make sure to check the schedules beforehand to avoid any inconvenience to your travel plans.

### Other Options

Alternatively, you can rent a car and explore the park at your own pace. There are car rental agencies at Aomori Airport, and most offer English navigation systems.

## How to Get Around in Towada-Hachimantai National Park

The best way to get around Towada-Hachimantai National Park is by car. Car rental services are available in Aomori airport and can be used for the duration of your trip. Alternatively, you can rely on the public transportation system which offers links to the main tourist attractions.

## Where to Go Next from Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After your visit to Towada-Hachimantai National Park, you may also want to explore some of the nearby destinations. These include:

  • Hirosaki City: A beautiful city famous for its cherry blossoms, which bloom in springtime.
  • Kakunodate: A small town that’s known for its Samurai residences and classic Samurai culture.
  • Matsushima: A coastal town known for its beautiful bay and many islands.

## Conclusion

Towada-Hachimantai National Park is an incredible destination that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. From hiking to hot springs, the park has something for everyone. So, plan your trip today and experience everything that this beautiful park has to offer!
