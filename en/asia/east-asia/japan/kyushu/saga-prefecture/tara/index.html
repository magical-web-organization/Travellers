Introduction to Tara

Located in the Saga prefecture on the island of Kyushu in Japan, Tara is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Known for its historic significance and unspoiled landscapes, Tara offers something for everyone. With this travel guide, you will discover the top attractions, activities, and cultural experiences to make your visit to Tara unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Tara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nabeshima-Kan Historical Museum: Learn about the rich history of the Nabeshima clan in this beautifully restored Edo-era home.
  2. Shoko Shuseikan Museum: Discover the craftsmanship and technological advancements of the ancient Japanese in this museum located in the historic Shoko-shuseikan Complex.
  3. Kakurezato-no-Yakata: Experience life as a hidden Christian in this museum that recreates the secret hiding places and daily life of Japanese Christians during the Edo period.

Natural Attractions of Tara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Tara: Hike to the summit of this spectacular mountain for breathtaking views of the valley below.
  2. Yoshinogari Historical Park: Explore the ruins of an ancient city that dates back more than 2,000 years.
  3. Nanatsugama Caves: Admire the rugged cliffs and deep caves, carved out by the sea, along the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tara

  1. Tara Mountain Hike: This scenic 5-mile hike takes you to the summit of Tara Mountain, passes through lush forests and stunning vistas.
  2. Ureshino Hot Spring Trail: Enjoy a leisurely hike through the forests followed by a relaxing dip in the natural hot springs.
  3. Tara Cycling Tour: Explore Tara’s countryside and coastline by bike on this 15-mile guided tour.

Best Things to Do in Tara: Activities and Tours

  1. Sake Tasting Tour: Visit the famous brewery of the Nabeshima clan and sample some of the best Sake in Japan.
  2. Hanami (Cherry Blossom Viewing): Witness the beauty of the blooming sakura trees, known for their pink and white petals, in the spring.
  3. Kimono Dressing Experience: Immerse yourself in the traditional Japanese culture by wearing a Kimono and learn about the art of dressing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tara

Food and Drink Scene of Tara

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sashimi: Taste the fresh and delicious seafood from Tara’s coastline.
  2. Yoshinogari rice: Savor the distinct flavor of rice grown in the Yoshinogari Historical Park area.
  3. Ureshino tea: Enjoy the refreshing and aromatic tea from the Ureshino Hot Spring Trail region.

Local Beverages

  1. Nabeshima Sake: Sample some of the best Sake in Japan made by the Nabeshima clan brewery.
  2. Lager Beer: Drink the famous Tara Lager Beer brewed locally.

Where to Stay in Tara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Ryokan Nagominoyado: Stay in a traditional Japanese-style inn with hot springs and experience the local culture up close.
  2. Hotel Tasaki: Located in the city center, this hotel offers easy access to the city’s attractions.
  3. Guesthouse Tsukino: This budget-friendly guesthouse is perfect for travelers looking for a comfortable yet affordable stay.

What to Buy in Tara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tara-Yaki Pottery: Take home some exquisite pottery made from the local clay of Tara.
  2. Japanese paper: Buy some hand-crafted Japanese paper that is known for its durability and beauty.
  3. Nabeshima Sake: Bring a bottle of Nabeshima Sake to savor the unique taste after returning home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tara

Tara has a rich history and culture that is deeply rooted in the daily life of its people. When visiting Tara, it is important to be aware of and respect the local customs and traditions. Some practices to keep in mind include removing shoes before entering homes and temples, using chopsticks respectfully, and being mindful of noise levels in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Tara

The best time to visit Tara is during the spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) seasons when the weather is mild and the foliage is stunning. The cherry blossom season in spring is a particularly beautiful time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tara

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Japanese words and phrases.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Wear shoes inside homes and temples.
  • Litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tara

Tara is generally a safe place to visit. Visitors should be aware of the risks of natural hazards such as typhoons and earthquakes. It is also important to exercise caution when swimming in the sea or hiking on the mountain trails.

Health in Tara

There are no major health concerns in Tara. However, it is recommended that visitors be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling. Tap water is safe to drink in Tara.

Getting to Tara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tara

The closest airport to Tara is Nagasaki Airport, which is approximately 70 kilometers away. From Nagasaki, visitors can take a taxi, bus or rental car to Tara.

Bus Stations Near Tara

The main bus station in Tara is located in the city center, offering daily services to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

The easiest way to get to Tara is by renting a car and driving from the neighboring cities. Visitors can also take the train and get off at Saga station or Takeo-Onsen station, and then take a taxi or bus to Tara.

How to Get Around in Tara

The best way to get around Tara is by renting a car. Its scenic countryside and coastline is best explored at your own pace. Public buses are also available for those without a rental car.

Where to Go Next from Tara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Nagasaki: Visit the historic port city of Nagasaki with its rich cultural heritage.
  2. Kagoshima: Explore the city of Kagoshima, famous for its hot springs, scenic views, and active volcano, Sakurajima.
  3. Fukuoka: Immerse yourself in the vibrant city of Fukuoka, known for its lively food and nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Tara is a charming destination offering a unique glimpse into the cultural heritage and natural beauty of Japan. Whether you are a nature lover, food enthusiast, or art aficionado, Tara has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan a trip to Tara that will be unforgettable.
