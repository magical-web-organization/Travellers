Introduction to Imari

Imari is a charming town located in Saga Prefecture, in the Kyushu region of Japan. This picturesque town, also known as Imari Toshi, is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. It is situated on the western coast of Kyushu and is surrounded by mountains and the sea, making it an excellent destination for nature lovers.

  • Imari Okawachiyama Traditional Crafts Center
  • Imari Nabeshima Shokokai

Best Things to Do in Imari: Activities and Tours

  • Join a pottery-making class

Week-Long Itinerary for Imari

Day 1: Arrive in Imari, explore Maeyama Park, and enjoy the natural beauty of the city. Day 2: Visit the Imari Okawachiyama Traditional Crafts Center and learn about traditional pottery making techniques. Day 3: Take a trip to the Hirayama Onsen and relax in the hot springs. Day 4: Visit the Nanatsugama Caves and explore the beautiful rock formations. Day 5: Rent a bike and cycle along the Arita Hizen Yume Kaido trail. Day 6: Indulge in local cuisine and shop for some souvenirs. Day 7: Depart Imari for your next destination.

Local Culture and Customs of Imari

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering a house or temple.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases, which can help you communicate better with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Imari

The best time to visit Imari is from April to June, and September to November. The weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy the blooming of cherry blossoms in spring or the autumn foliage in fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Imari

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and indulge in cultural experiences like pottery-making.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Ignore the rules of a shrine or temple.

Safety and Health

Safety in Imari

Imari is a safe place to visit. However, be cautious while trekking or hiking, especially if you’re traveling solo.

Health in Imari

The healthcare system in Japan is efficient and of high-quality. However, it is advisable to invest in travel insurance before traveling to Japan.

Getting to Imari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Imari

The nearest airport to Imari is Saga International Airport, which is about 44 km away. The airport is well-connected to major cities in Japan, including Tokyo and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Imari

Imari Bus Terminal is situated in the city center and is easily accessible. Buses run regularly from Fukuoka, Saga, and Nagasaki.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Imari. The nearest train station is Imari Station, which is well-connected to major cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Imari

The best way to explore Imari is by renting a car or bicycle. Trains and buses are also available, but the frequency and schedules may be limited.

Conclusion

Imari is an excellent destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover or a foodie, this charming town in Japan has something for everyone.
