Introduction to Kashima

Located in Saga prefecture on the island of Kyushu in Japan, Kashima is a charming town that attracts visitors with its rich cultural history and natural beauty. The town is home to several ancient shrines and temples, as well as stunning mountain trails and hot springs. If you’re looking for a tranquil getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life, Kashima is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kashima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kashima Jingu Shrine - One of the most significant Shinto shrines in Japan, this shrine is believed to be over 1,500 years old and is dedicated to the god of martial arts.
  • Museum of Japanese Emigration to Hawaii - This museum showcases the history of Japanese emigration to Hawaii and features exhibits about the lives of Japanese immigrants.
  • Kashima Castle - Built in the 17th century, this castle is an important historical site in Kashima and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kashima City Museum of Art - Featuring works by both local and international artists, this museum is a must-visit for art lovers.

Natural Attractions of Kashima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nabeyama Road - This scenic road offers stunning views of the countryside and is a popular spot for hikers and bikers.
  • Ureshino River - Known for its crystal-clear waters and picturesque views, this river is a great spot for fishing, kayaking, and other water activities.
  • Tenzan Hot Springs - Relax and unwind at this traditional hot spring resort surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Nabeyama Kogen Trailhead - Explore the stunning mountain trails at this trailhead, which offers a variety of hiking options for all skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kashima

Kashima offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Nabeyama Kogen Trail - This is a challenging, 5.5-mile hike that takes you to the top of Mount Nabeyama, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kashima Coastline Cycling Route - This 12-mile route takes you along the scenic coastline of Kashima, showcasing its natural beauty.
  • Koze River Cycling Road - A gentle, 7-mile path that runs alongside the Koze River, offering picturesque views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kashima: Activities and Tours

  • Take a traditional Japanese tea ceremony class - Learn about the history and significance of the Japanese tea ceremony and try your hand at making your own tea.
  • Visit a local sake brewery - Kashima is home to several sake breweries, and a tour and tasting is a great way to experience the local culture.
  • Join a calligraphy class - Learn the art of Japanese calligraphy and create your own unique piece of artwork.
  • Take a pottery class - Create your own pottery masterpiece at one of the many pottery studios in Kashima.

Food and Drink Scene of Kashima

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karage - Fried chicken is a popular dish in Kyushu, and Kashima is no exception. Try some of the delicious karaage here, which is known for its crispy exterior and juicy interior.
  • Tonkotsu Ramen - This noodle dish is made with a rich and savory pork bone broth and is a staple of the Kyushu region.
  • Ureshino Tea - Famous for its high-quality green tea, Ureshino Tea is a local favorite that is often enjoyed with sweet snacks or desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Sake - Kashima is home to several sake breweries, and a tasting at one of these breweries is a must-do.
  • Shochu - This traditional Japanese spirit is made from sweet potatoes, rice, or barley and is produced in many different flavors and varieties.
  • Green tea - The Ureshino Tea is also used to make a refreshing and healthy green tea.

Where to Stay in Kashima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Koyaji Ryokan - This traditional Japanese inn offers a relaxing and authentic experience, featuring hot springs, Japanese-style rooms, and home-cooked meals.
  • Wakaba Guesthouse - A budget-friendly option, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Aqua Hotel Kashima - Located in the heart of the city, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to all of Kashima’s attractions.

What to Buy in Kashima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ureshino Tea - This high-quality green tea is a popular souvenir from Kashima.
  • Pottery and ceramics - Kashima is known for its pottery, and there are several studios and shops where you can purchase unique and beautiful ceramics.
  • Sake and shochu - Bring home a bottle of local sake or shochu as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kashima

  • Respect for elders - In Japan, there is a strong emphasis on respecting one’s elders, and this is especially true in Kashima, where traditional values remain strong.
  • Removing shoes inside - It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a traditional Japanese guesthouse.
  • Bowing as a sign of respect - Bowing is a common way of showing respect in Japan, and is frequently used in greetings and other social situations.

Best Time to Visit Kashima

The best time to visit Kashima is in the spring (March - May) or fall (September - November), when the weather is mild and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. The summer months can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kashima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions - Japan has a rich cultural heritage, and it is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try local foods and drinks - The food and drink scene in Kashima is not to be missed, so be sure to try some of the local specialties during your visit.
  • Be polite and courteous - Japan is known for its politeness and courtesy, so be sure to be respectful and polite during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside - It is considered impolite to wear shoes inside someone’s home or a traditional Japanese guesthouse, so be sure to remove your shoes before entering.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces - Japanese culture values tranquility and peace, so avoid being loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Tip at restaurants or other establishments - Tipping is not customary in Japan, so there is no need to leave a tip at restaurants or other establishments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kashima

Kashima is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is still important to be vigilant and take basic safety precautions while traveling.

Health in Kashima

Japan has a high standard of healthcare, and Kashima has several clinics and hospitals that offer quality medical care. It is always a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses in case of emergency.

Getting to Kashima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kashima is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Kashima

  • Fukuoka Airport - Located about 70 miles away, Fukuoka Airport is the closest airport to Kashima.

Bus Stations Near Kashima

  • Kashima Bus Station - From here you can take buses to other towns in Saga, as well as to Fukuoka and Nagasaki.

Other Options

  • Train - You can take the train to neighboring cities and then take a bus to Kashima.
  • Rent a Car - Renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Kashima

Kashima is a relatively small town, and many of the main attractions are within walking distance of each other. For longer distances or for exploring the surrounding areas, renting a car or taking a bus is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Kashima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sasebo - Explore the beautiful harbor town of Sasebo, located about 30 miles away from Kashima.
  • Nagasaki - Discover the fascinating history and culture of Nagasaki, located about 70 miles away.
  • Fukuoka - Visit the vibrant city of Fukuoka, known for its delicious food and exciting nightlife.

Conclusion

Kashima is a hidden gem in Kyushu, Japan, offering visitors the opportunity to experience traditional Japanese culture and natural beauty in a tranquil setting. From temples and shrines to natural hot springs and hiking trails, there is something for everyone in this charming town. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the ultimate trip to Kashima and create unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.
