Introduction to Tosu

Located in the Saga prefecture of Kyushu, Tosu is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Known for its cultural and historical significance, the city offers an immersive experience that is hard to find anywhere else in Japan. From mesmerizing natural landscapes to a rich history and culture, Tosu has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tosu

If you are planning to visit Tosu, here are some of the top places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tosu Premium Outlets: The Tosu Premium Outlets is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves shopping. The mall features a wide range of local and international brands and offers incredible discounts.

  • Tosu City Museum of History and Folklore: Located in the heart of Tosu, the museum offers visitors an insight into the city’s rich cultural heritage. With exhibits that cover everything from folklore to ancient history, there is plenty to explore at this museum.

  • Nakao Castle Ruins: The Nakao Castle Ruins is a historical site that is a must-visit for history buffs. The castle was once the residence of the Tsushima clan, and visitors can explore the ruins and learn about the city’s fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Tosu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kiyomizu: A popular destination for hikers and nature lovers, Mount Kiyomizu offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing hike and take in the beauty of the mountain’s natural scenery.

  • Tosu City Park: The Tosu City Park is a great place to relax and unwind. The park features a beautiful lake, lawns, and walking trails, making it an ideal destination for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

  • Tosu Agricultural Park: The Tosu Agricultural Park is a popular destination for families with children. The park offers plenty of activities, including fruit picking, nature walks, and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tosu

Tosu is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking routes in Kyushu. Here are some of the best routes that you can explore:

  • Kinryu Onsen Course: The Kinryu Onsen Course is a 4-kilometer route that takes visitors through lush forests and scenic trails. The route ends at a natural hot spring where hikers can take a relaxing dip.

  • Tosu City Park to Higashi Tosu Station: This 3-kilometer route is perfect for nature lovers and those who enjoy cycling. The route takes you through some of the most scenic landscapes in Tosu, and there are plenty of spots to stop for a picnic or take in the sights.

  • Tosu to Yoshinogari: This 20-kilometer route is ideal for experienced hikers and bikers. The route takes visitors through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Kyushu and features plenty of opportunities to interact with the local community.

Best Things to Do in Tosu: Activities and Tours

From exploring the city’s rich cultural heritage to sampling local delicacies, there are plenty of things to do in Tosu. Here are some of the must-try activities:

  • Try Yakitori: Yakitori is a popular dish in Tosu, and there are plenty of restaurants that serve this local delicacy. Be sure to try the Tsukune, a type of chicken meatball that is a local favorite.

  • Visit the Tosu Premium Outlets: As mentioned earlier, the Tosu Premium Outlets is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves shopping. Be sure to leave enough time to explore this vast mall.

  • Participate in the Saga Balloon Festival: The Saga Balloon Festival is an annual event that attracts visitors from all over the world. The festival features hot air balloons, food stalls, and cultural performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Tosu

Tosu is known for its delicious cuisine, and there are plenty of options for foodies to explore. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tosu Ramen: The Tosu Ramen is a local specialty that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. The soup is made using pork bone broth and features chewy noodles and succulent meat.

  • Mentai Kamaboko: Mentai Kamaboko is a type of fish cake that is a popular snack in Tosu. The cake is infused with spicy cod roe and has a unique flavor that is hard to resist.

  • Tosu Yakitori: The Tosu Yakitori is a type of chicken skewer that is grilled over charcoal. The skewers are marinated in a special sauce, which gives them a unique flavor that is hard to replicate.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Sake is a type of Japanese rice wine that is popular in Tosu. There are plenty of local breweries that offer sake tastings and tours, allowing visitors to discover the history and culture behind this ancient beverage.

  • Shochu: Shochu is a distilled beverage that is made from rice, barley, or sweet potatoes. It is a popular drink in Tosu, and visitors can enjoy tastings at local distilleries.

Where to Stay in Tosu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tosu offers a wide range of accommodations for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Route-Inn Tosu Ekimae: This hotel is located in the heart of Tosu and offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

  • Tosu Guest House: The Tosu Guest House is a great option for budget travelers. The guesthouse offers clean and comfortable rooms at low prices.

  • Tosu Royal Hotel: The Tosu Royal Hotel is a luxury hotel that offers spacious rooms and top-notch amenities. It is located a short distance from some of Tosu’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Tosu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tosu is home to a range of local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best options:

  • Tosu Ceramic Ware: The Tosu Ceramic Ware is a type of traditional pottery that is made by local artisans. Visitors can pick up ceramics in a range of colors and designs at local markets and shops.

  • Tosu Tomatoes: Tosu is known for its delicious tomatoes, which are prized for their sweetness and juiciness. Visitors can buy fresh tomatoes at local markets and shops.

  • Cloth and Textiles: Tosu is home to a range of textile artisans who produce unique fabrics using traditional techniques. Visitors can buy cloth and textiles at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Tosu

Tosu has a rich and vibrant culture that is deeply rooted in the city’s history. Here are some of the unique customs and traditions that you may encounter during your stay in Tosu:

  • Ondo Dancing: Ondo Dancing is a type of dance that is performed during traditional festivals and events. Visitors can join in the fun and learn the moves at local dance workshops.

  • Tea Ceremonies: Tea ceremonies are a traditional Japanese ritual that is practiced in Tosu. Visitors can participate in a tea ceremony and learn about the history and customs behind this ancient practice.

  • Shishimai Dancing: Shishimai Dancing is a type of dance that is performed during New Year’s celebrations. The dance features a lion’s mask and is steeped in symbolism and tradition.

Best Time to Visit Tosu

Tosu is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit the city is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the city is less crowded with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tosu

Here are some of the things to keep in mind during your stay in Tosu:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases, as English is not widely spoken in the city.

  • Explore the city’s history and culture by visiting museums and historical sites.

  • Try local delicacies, including Tosu Ramen and Mentai Kamaboko.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a traditional restaurant.

  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.

  • Don’t tip at restaurants, as it is not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Tosu is a safe city, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major health or safety issues during their stay. Here are some basic guidelines to keep in mind:

Safety in Tosu

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure.

  • Follow basic traffic rules and be cautious when crossing the street.

  • Don’t engage in any activities that may endanger your safety, such as hiking alone or swimming in unguarded waters.

Health in Tosu

  • Tosu has a robust healthcare system, and visitors can seek medical help at local clinics and hospitals.

  • Make sure to get any necessary vaccinations before traveling to Japan.

  • Be cautious with your food and water intake, as waterborne illnesses are a common risk.

Getting to Tosu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tosu is well-connected to other parts of Japan, making it easy to reach via various modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Tosu

  • Fukuoka Airport: Located around 40 kilometers from Tosu, the Fukuoka Airport is the closest airport to the city.

  • Saga Airport: Saga Airport is located around 15 kilometers from Tosu but offers limited flight options.

Bus Stations Near Tosu

  • Tosu Bus Terminal: The Tosu Bus Terminal is located in the heart of the city and offers frequent bus services to other parts of Japan.

Other Options

  • JR Tosu Station: The JR Tosu Station offers frequent train services to other parts of Japan, including Tokyo and Osaka.

How to Get Around in Tosu

Tosu is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot or by public transport. Here are some of the options:

  • Train: The JR Tosu Station is the main train station in the city and offers frequent services to other parts of Japan.

  • Bus: Tosu has a well-connected bus network that makes it easy to get around the city and its suburbs.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in the city, but they can be expensive compared to public transport options.

Where to Go Next from Tosu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tosu is an excellent base for exploring other parts of the Kyushu region. Here are some top destinations you can explore:

  • Fukuoka: Located just 30 minutes from Tosu, Fukuoka is a vibrant city known for its food and nightlife.

  • Karatsu: Karatsu is a beautiful coastal city located around an hour from Tosu. The city is known for its stunning beaches and traditional ceramics.

  • Nagasaki: Nagasaki is a historic city located around two hours from Tosu. The city is known for its unique blend of Japanese and European culture and its rich history.

Conclusion

Tosu is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by travelers who want to experience the rich culture and history of Japan. From exploring the city’s museums and historical sites to hiking in the mountains and trying local delicacies, there is plenty to see and do in Tosu. This comprehensive guide is the perfect resource to help you plan your trip and make the most of your stay.
