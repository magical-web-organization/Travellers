Introduction to Arita

Nestled in the heart of Saga Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu in Japan, Arita has a rich history that dates back to the early 17th century. Known for its link to the famed blue-and-white Japanese porcelain (imari), this small town has become a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. Arita offers a multitude of attractions that allow visitors to immerse themselves in Japanese culture and tradition.

List of Places to Visit in Arita

Arita is a town with a long history and a rich culture. The town has a lot of places to visit. Here are some of the destinations that visitors should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arita Porcelain Park
  2. Kyushu Ceramic Museum
  3. Izumiyama Quarry Museum
  4. Arita Ceramic Art Museum
  5. Touzan Shrine

Natural Attractions of Arita: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mt. Oshidori
  2. Uchiyama Dam Lake
  3. Yokomineji Temple
  4. Minamishibata Onsen

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arita

Arita is an excellent destination for those who love nature activities such as hiking and biking. Below are some of the recommended routes for adventurous travelers visiting Arita:

  1. Mt. Oshidori Hiking Trail
  2. Uchiyama Dam Biking Trail
  3. Sefuri Kaido

Best Things to Do in Arita: Activities and Tours

Arita offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Some of the must-try experiences include:

  1. Pottery-making experience
  2. Visiting a kiln and witnessing the firing process
  3. Visiting an old Japanese-style house
  4. Arita Togeikan - Touring the museum and shopping

Week-Long Itinerary for Arita

Here is an itinerary that visitors can follow for a week-long stay in Arita:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Tour of Arita Ceramic Art Museum, Kyushu Ceramic Museum and Touzan Shrine
Day 2 Visit the Izumimaya quarry museum then hiking in Mt. Oshidori
Day 3 Arita Porcelain Park followed by pottery-making at Kyushu artisans studio
Day 4 Spend a day in Minamishibata Onsen
Day 5 Explore Uchiyama Dam Lake
Day 6 Sefuri Kaido Biking Route
Day 7 Visit Yokominej Temple and shopping at Arita Togeikan

Where to Stay in Arita: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arita has various accommodation options that cater to both budget and luxury travelers. Here are some recommended options:

  1. Guesthouse Tanya, a hot spring guesthouse with friendly staff and comfy rooms.
  2. Yoshida Ryokan, a traditional Japanese-style inn, perfect for those who want an authentic Japanese experience.
  3. Arita Grand Hotel, a great option for luxury travelers, with beautiful rooms and high-class amenities.

What to Buy in Arita: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors should not leave Arita empty-handed. Below are some of the souvenirs and local products that travelers can bring home:

  1. Imari Porcelain
  2. Pottery-making tools and books
  3. Arita-yaki Japanese porcelain, tea cups, and tea utensils

Local Culture and Customs of Arita

Arita people are known for their hospitality, and visitors are expected to respect their culture and customs. Here are some of the things to remember when visiting Arita:

  • Be aware of appropriate dress codes when visiting religious sites
  • Refrain from littering
  • Respect the customary bow when greeting locals

Best Time to Visit Arita

Arita can be visited year-round. However, to experience the beauty of Arita’s nature, it is best to visit during the cherry blossom season (late March to early April) or the fall colors season (late November to early December).

Do’s and Don’ts of Arita

Do:

  • Try the local food
  • Embrace Arita culture and traditions
  • Respect the local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Be loud and rowdy in public areas
  • Disrespect religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Arita

Arita is a very safe place to visit with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Arita

Arita does not have any major health concerns, but visitors should watch out for the following:

  1. Sunburn during summer
  2. Dehydration during summer
  3. Seasonal allergies during spring
