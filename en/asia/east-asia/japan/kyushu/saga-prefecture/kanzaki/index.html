Introduction to Kanzaki

Kanzaki is a small town located in the Saga Prefecture of Japan’s Kyushu island. Known for its lush greenery, gorgeous countryside, and rich history and culture, it is a must-visit destination for those looking to explore Japan beyond its typical tourist hubs.

List of Places to Visit in Kanzaki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kanzaki City Traditional Industrial Museum: It displays the town’s traditional craft techniques, including pottery and weaving.
  • Sekkuji Temple: Featuring a five-storied pagoda and the oldest stone lantern in Saga prefecture, it is a charming place to visit, especially during autumn when the leaves change colors.
  • Josenji Temple: A thousand-year-old temple surrounded by Japanese cedar trees.
  • Kanzaki Castle Ruins: Home to an ancient samurai lord, the castle ruins are a great spot to catch the sunset.

Natural Attractions of Kanzaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Kiyomizu Hiking Trail: A moderately difficult hiking trail that takes you to the top of the Kiyomizu mountain, offering stunning views of the Saga valley.
  • Natsuigahara Falls: A beautiful waterfall that is surrounded by lush greenery, providing the perfect spot for a picnic.
  • Hoshizaki Green Park: A vast park filled with flowers and greenery, featuring a playground, BBQ area, and an outdoor stage where local events are held.
  • Izumi Park: A 58-acre park with several walking trails, fishing ponds, and an observation deck to view the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kanzaki

Kanzaki is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails. Some popular ones are:

  • Kiyomizu Hiking Trail
  • Yamakasa Cycling Road: A 15-km cycling road that passes through the town’s scenic countryside and historic landmarks.
  • Sasayama Mountains Hiking Trail: A challenging 6-km hike with breathtaking views of the waterfall, gorge, and mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in Kanzaki: Activities and Tours

  • Furusato no Sato Kanzaki: A tourist farm where visitors can enjoy harvesting crops, trying out traditional crafts, and learning about local culture.
  • Kanzaki Cycling Tour: Join a cycling tour and explore the town’s hidden gems.
  • Takatora Summit Get Together: A local festival where visitors can meet up and enjoy traditional music and dance performances.
  • Kanzaki Cherry Blossom Festival: Held in March, visitors can enjoy a stroll under the cherry blossom trees and try local foods.

Kiyomizu and visit Sekkuji Temple Day 3 - Take a cycling tour of the town’s historic landmarks and countryside Day 4 - Visit Natsuigahara Falls and Hoshizaki Green Park Day 5 - Learn about local crafts and try pottery at Furusato no Sato Kanzaki Day 6 - Enjoy the cherry blossom festival and local food Day 7 - Relax near the Izumi Park fishing ponds and go for a picnic

Food and Drink Scene of Kanzaki

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kanzaki Ramen: A local delicacy consisting of chicken and pork broth, soy sauce, and salt, served with boiled egg, noodles, and chashu pork.
  • Komekome Rice: Highly polished, sweet-tasting rice that is locally grown in Kanzaki.
  • Toriten: A dish of deep-fried chicken served with dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Miyazaki Beer: A delicious local beer brewed in the neighboring Miyazaki Prefecture.
  • Kanzaki Shuzo Sake: A high-quality sake made from the pristine natural waters of Mt. Kiyomizu in Kanzaki.

Where to Stay in Kanzaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kyomizu Sanso: A traditional ryokan located near Mt. Kiyomizu, featuring a hot spring and beautiful garden.
  • Ryokan Hamafuku: A comfortable and cozy ryokan located near the famous Natsuigahara Falls.
  • Auotaki no Mezonette: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Kanzaki town.

What to Buy in Kanzaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kanzaki Pottery: Local pottery, including teacups, vases, plates, and sake cups, handmade using traditional techniques.
  • Kanzaki Sake: Take home locally distilled Kanzaki Shuzo Sake as a souvenir.
  • Kanzaki Rice: Buy locally grown rice and take home a taste of Kanzaki’s agriculture.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanzaki

  • Respect nature: Kanzaki is known for its beautiful scenery, and locals believe in respecting and preserving nature.
  • Politeness: Like other parts of Japan, locals in Kanzaki place great importance on politeness and respectfulness.
  • Appreciation of tradition: Kanzaki is proud of its history and culture, and locals celebrate their heritage at various festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Kanzaki

The best time to visit Kanzaki is from late March to early April when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. However, the town is beautiful year-round, and each season has its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanzaki

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and products
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take your shoes off before entering someone’s home

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage nature
  • Be loud or disrespectful in public spaces
  • Smoke in non-designated areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanzaki

Kanzaki is a small, safe town with low crime rates. However, travelers should still be aware of their surroundings and take common precautions.

Health in Kanzaki

Kanzaki has good medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. There are also English-speaking doctors available in larger cities nearby.

Getting to Kanzaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kanzaki

The closest international airport to Kanzaki is the Fukuoka International Airport, located about 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kanzaki

Kanzaki Bus Terminal is located in the town center and offers direct connections to neighboring cities.

Other Options

Taxis and trains are also available, though it is recommended to rent a car due to Kanzaki’s rural location.

How to Get Around in Kanzaki

The best way to explore Kanzaki and its surroundings is by car or bicycle. Several rental car and bicycle shops are available around the town.

Where to Go Next from Kanzaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Karatsu: A nearby city known for its beautiful beaches and historic castle.
  • Takeo Onsen: A town famous for its hot springs and beautiful nature.
  • Sasebo: A bustling city with a deep history and delicious food scene.

Conclusion

Kanzaki is a hidden gem in Saga Prefecture, Japan, filled with natural beauty, rich history, and unique cultural experiences. Whether you come for the hiking trails, local food, or to learn about traditional crafts, Kanzaki is a fantastic destination for any traveler looking to explore beyond Japan’s more well-known cities.
