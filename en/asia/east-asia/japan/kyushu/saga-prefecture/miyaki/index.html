Introduction to Miyaki

Miyaki is a beautiful district located in Saga prefecture on the island of Kyushu, in Japan. It is a hidden gem, often overlooked by tourists visiting the country. Miyaki is known for its historical landmarks, stunning landscapes, and cultural experiences that have survived the test of time. The district is surrounded by rolling hills, vast rice paddies, and crystal-clear rivers, making it a nature lover’s paradise.

List of Places to Visit in Miyaki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re looking to learn about Miyaki’s history and culture, there are several historical landmarks that you must visit. The Tashima Jingu Shrine is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the area. It is a Shinto shrine dedicated to Emperor Ojin, and it is said to have been built over 1400 years ago. The Yobuko Torpedo Squadron Museum is another must-visit place for history enthusiasts. It is a museum that showcases the history of the Japanese Navy and the Yobuko torpedo squadron during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Miyaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miyaki is famous for its natural beauty, and there are several breathtaking views and outdoor activities that you can do while in the district. The Yoshinogari Historical Park is one of the most famous landmarks in the area. It is an archaeological site where you can see ruins of ancient Japanese settlements dating back to the Yayoi period. The Arita Ceramic Park is another outdoor attraction that you should not miss. It is a beautiful park that showcases the traditional Japanese ceramic art, and it allows visitors to make their ceramic art pieces.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyaki

Miyaki is surrounded by lush hills and beautiful landscapes, making it a perfect location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Takasuka Nature Park is one of the most popular hiking trails in the area. The trail is approximately 6km long and is surrounded by scenic views of the Miyakigawa River. Another popular hiking destination is the Mt. Tenzan Trail, which is a 3km trail that takes you to the top of the mountain, where you can enjoy stunning views of the district. The Miyaki Cycling Road is a popular destination for bikers, and it offers a beautiful view of the countryside as you ride along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Miyaki: Activities and Tours

Miyaki offers various activities and tours that allow you to experience the district’s culture and beautiful scenery. Hado Experience Miyaki is a unique experience that allows visitors to try out the traditional Japanese martial art of “Hado,” which combines martial arts and technology. The Teuchi Soba Workshop allows visitors to learn how to make handmade soba noodles, a traditional Japanese delicacy. There are also several guided tours of the district, such as the Tea and Ceramic Tour, which allows you to experience Japanese tea culture and ceramic art.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyaki

Must-Try Dishes

Miyaki’s cuisine is known for its fresh seafood and locally grown veggies, and here’s a list of must-try dishes:

  • Mackerel Sushi: Freshly caught Mackerel served on top of sushi rice.
  • Imari Beef: A popular dish made from the local Japanese Black beef breed.
  • Tori Meshi: Rice with chicken cooked with Miyaki’s own soy sauce.
  • Onsen Tamago: Soft-boiled eggs cooked in hot springs.

Local Beverages

Miyaki offers several delicious local beverages, including:

  • Sake: Miyaki has several Sake breweries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Shochu: Shochu is a traditional Japanese spirit made from barley, sweet potato, or rice.
  • Tea: Miyaki is known for producing high-quality green tea.
  • Ramune: Ramune is a Japanese soda that has been popular in the country since 1872.

Where to Stay in Miyaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Miyaki has several accommodations options that caters to different preferences and budgets. Some of the top options are:

  • Hotel Morino Kumamoto: A luxurious hotel that offers a relaxing onsen experience.
  • Aso Plaza Hotel: A hotel that provides sweeping views of Mt. Aso and is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Country House Jubanso: A guesthouse that offers a comfortable stay in a traditional Japanese house.

What to Buy in Miyaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Miyaki is known for several local products and souvenirs that you can bring home, including:

  • Imari porcelain: Beautifully designed ceramic art made in Miyaki.
  • Wagasa: Traditional Japanese handmade umbrellas.
  • Nishikisugi: A traditional Japanese fabric dyeing method that produces beautiful designs.
  • Yabusame Arrow: Handmade arrows used in the traditional Japanese martial art of Yabusame.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyaki

Miyaki is known for its traditional Japanese customs and culture. Some of the local customs that visitors should be aware of are bowing as a sign of respect and being punctual as lateness is considered rude in Miyaki. Visitors are also asked to take their shoes off before entering someone’s house or certain facilities.

Best Time to Visit Miyaki

The best time to visit Miyaki is between March and May when the weather is pleasant, and the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. The fall season from September to November is another great season to visit Miyaki.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyaki

Do:

  • Bow as a sign of respect.
  • Be punctual.
  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s house or specific facilities.
  • Try local cuisines.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Eat or drink while walking in the streets.

Safety and Heath

Safety in Miyaki

Miyaki is generally a safe district to visit. Visitors should still take precautions and follow standard safety tips while exploring the area.

Health in Miyaki

Miyaki has several medical facilities available to visitors, including hospitals and clinics.

Getting to Miyaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miyaki

Saga Airport is the closest airport to Miyaki, and it is approximately a 50-minute drive from Miyaki.

Bus Stations Near Miyaki

The nearest bus station to Miyaki is the Imari Bus Terminal, which is approximately 10-minutes from Miyaki by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Miyaki by train or by car.

How to Get Around in Miyaki

The best way to explore Miyaki is by renting a car or taking a taxi. However, cycling or hiking is also a popular way to explore the natural beauty of Miyaki.

Where to Go Next from Miyaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miyaki is surrounded by several popular tourist destinations. Some of the best places to visit nearby are:

  • Nagasaki: Located approximately a two-hour drive from Miyaki, Nagasaki is a popular tourist destination known for its cultural landmarks and natural beauty.
  • Fukuoka: Fukuoka is a must-visit tourist destination in Kyushu that is rich in history, culture, and tradition.
  • Kumamoto: Kumamoto is a beautiful city that is home to several historical landmarks, including the Kumamoto Castle and Suizenji Garden.

Conclusion

Miyaki is a beautiful district that offers visitors several opportunities to explore Japan’s culture, history, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Miyaki with ease and discover the best places to visit, activities to do, and local experiences to try.
