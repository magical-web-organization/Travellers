Introduction to Miyazaki

Miyazaki prefecture is located on the eastern coast of Kyushu island and is home to scenic beaches, famous surfing spots, hot spring towns, historic places, and more. The area offers a mix of both modern and traditional Japanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Miyazaki

Let’s explore what Miyazaki offers to tourists.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Miyazaki is home to a rich history and many exceptional museums dedicated to it. These historical places are definitely worth exploring on your tour.

Miyazaki Prefectural Art Museum

The Miyazaki Prefectural Art Museum features various exhibitions of art, including fine art paintings, prints, and sculptures by prominent artists. It is a great place to experience art, Japanese culture, and meet new people.

Takachiho Shrine

Takachiho Shrine is a historical site situated amongst the beautiful greenery of Takachiho Gorge. It was first built in the eighth century and is closely linked to Japanese mythology, making it an exciting place for those interested in Japanese history and mythology.

Natural Attractions of Miyazaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miyazaki’s natural attractions offer some of the most scenic views in Japan. The prefecture is mostly mountainous and surrounded by the ocean which makes this destination perfect for outdoor adventurers.

Takachiho Gorge

Takachiho Gorge, located in the Takachiho region, is a must-visit attraction for anyone traveling to Miyazaki. The colorful cliffs, waterfalls, and the clear waters of the gorge provide a unique and breathtaking setting. Take a boat ride and bask in the beauty of the surrounding nature.

Aoshima Island

A small island located south of Miyazaki is Aoshima Island. The island is known for its unique rock formations along the shore, the serene beaches, and its legendary history. The small and picturesque island is a tranquil getaway spot, and it makes for an excellent day trip from Miyazaki.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyazaki

Miyazaki offers some challenging routes for hikers and bikers, making it an ideal destination for those looking for adventure.

Kirishima Mountains

The Kirishima National Park, situated in the prefecture of Miyazaki, offers an excellent hiking experience through the Kirishima Mountains. The trails traverse through a wooded pathway lined with wildflowers with an abundance of plant species that create a picturesque landscape.

Nichinan Coastline

The Nichinan coastline, located in the south of Miyazaki prefecture, is a cycling route that covers a 60-kilometer distance. This breathtaking coastal route is perfect for anyone who loves cycling, consisting of a mix of mountainous terrain and scenic oceanfront views.

Best Things to Do in Miyazaki: Activities and Tours

Miyazaki offers various activities and tours showcasing its unique cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Surfing

Miyazaki is known for its fantastic waves, making it a popular surfing destination. The beaches of Miyazaki offer an unforgettable experience that attracts surfers from all over the world.

Reimei-no-Sato Seagaia

Reimei-no-Sato Seagaia is an enormous facility that offers various activities from snorkeling to kayaking. The facility also highlights Miyazaki’s culture with various Japanese traditional events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Miyazaki

Spending a week in Miyazaki prefecture allows you to discover the prefecture’s true essence.

Day 1: Visit the Miyazaki Prefectural Art Museum
Day 2: Explore the surroundings of Takachiho Gorge
Day 3: Take a day trip to Aoshima Island
Day 4: Walk through Kirishima National Park
Day 5: Cycle along the Nichinan coastline
Day 6: Experience surfing at the beaches of Miyazaki 
Day 7: Explore the Reimei-no-Sato Seagaia facility

Food and Drink Scene of Miyazaki

Must-Try Dishes

Miyazaki is known as the land of fire and is famous for its spicy and savory dishes. Don’t miss out and try:

  • Chicken Nanban
  • Miyazaki Beef
  • Mango

Local Beverages

Miyazaki is also known for its distilleries and breweries that offer a broad range of local beverages such as:

  • Takachiho Beer
  • Awamori

Where to Stay in Miyazaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Miyazaki Prefecture has accommodations for all kinds of travelers. Choose from:

  • Luxurious resorts like the Sheraton Grande Ocean Resort or the Aoshima Grand Hotel
  • Mid-range hotels like Saiki Wakamatsu Kan and Marix Lagoon Onsen
  • Budget hostels like the Mappa Guesthouse

What to Buy in Miyazaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring back some souvenirs from Miyazaki:

  • Miyazaki Mango
  • Japanese pottery and ceramics
  • Local sake and beer

Local Culture and Customs of Miyazaki

Miyazaki Prefecture is rich in traditions and cultural heritage. In Miyazaki, people often greet each other with the phrase ‘Ohisashiburi,’ which means ‘It’s been a long time since we met.’ Politeness is highly valued in Japanese culture and especially in Miyazaki.

Best Time to Visit Miyazaki

Miyazaki is suitable for travel any time of the year; however, the best time to visit is from March-June and September- December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyazaki

Do:

  • Bow when greeting someone.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a Japanese home.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking.
  • Tip servers in a restaurant or hotel.

Safety and Health

Safety in Miyazaki

Miyazaki is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are low, but always take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Miyazaki

Make sure to drink a sufficient amount of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months. Also, bring proper protection against the sun and wear sunscreen.

Getting to Miyazaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miyazaki

  • Miyazaki Airport: Domestic flights depart to cities including Tokyo, Osaka, and Fukuoka.

Bus Stations Near Miyazaki

  • Miyazaki Station: JR Kyushu provides local and regional bus services.

Other Options

  • Car rental

How to Get Around in Miyazaki

Miyazaki’s public transportation system is efficient and user-friendly. You can travel via train, bus, or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Miyazaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Miyazaki, you can visit the neighboring prefectures of Kumamoto and Kagoshima.

Conclusion

Miyazaki Prefecture is an ideal destination for travelers looking to tour a distinctive part of Japan that highlights its natural beauty as well as its rich cultural heritage. With its stunning landscapes and cultural sites, Miyazaki offers unforgettable experiences for everyone.
