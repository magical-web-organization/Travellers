Introduction to Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Nestled on the southeastern coast of Kyushu, Miyazaki Prefecture boasts a stunning coastline, lush forests, and a unique culture that combines the traditions of old Japan with modern innovations. Known as the “Land of Fire” due to its history of volcanic activity and hot springs, Miyazaki is a popular destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and relaxing getaways.

List of Places to Visit in Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Miyazaki Prefecture is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a fascinating glimpse into Japan’s past and present. The highlights include:

  • Miyazaki Prefectural Museum of Nature and History: This museum chronicles the natural history of Miyazaki, showcasing its diverse flora, fauna, and geological formations. Visitors can also learn about the region’s human history, including its ancient Jomon culture and the influence of Buddhism.
  • Udo Jingu Shrine: This Shinto shrine is located in a cave on the edge of a cliff overlooking the ocean, making it one of the most scenic and unique shrines in Japan. It is dedicated to the god of marriage and matchmaking, and many couples visit here to pray for happiness and love.
  • Aya Castle Ruins: This historical site was once a fortress built in the 14th century to defend against invading Mongol forces. Today, visitors can explore the castle’s ruins and learn about its role in Miyazaki Prefecture’s history.
  • Saitobaru Burial Mounds: These ancient burial mounds date back to the Kofun period (250-538 AD) and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. They provide a fascinating glimpse into the ceremonial and spiritual practices of ancient Japan.

Natural Attractions of Miyazaki (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miyazaki Prefecture boasts several natural wonders that are worth exploring. These include:

  • Kirishima-Yaku National Park: Known for its volcanic landscape and hot springs, this national park is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and outdoor adventures. It is home to several mountains, including Mount Kirishima, which rises to a height of 1700 meters.
  • Takachiho Gorge: This gorge is famous for its stunning waterfalls and dramatic beauty. Visitors can take a boat tour down the river that runs through the gorge or hike along its trails.
  • Ebino Kogen Plateau: This highland is home to a rare ecosystem of subalpine flora and fauna, including Yaku Sugi cedar trees and alpine flowers. Visitors can hike along its trails and admire the panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Nichinan Coastline: This scenic coastline is dotted with stunning beaches, rocky cliffs, and charming fishing villages. Visitors can take a scenic drive along the coast or go for a swim in the crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Miyazaki Prefecture offers several hiking and biking routes that provide a unique way to explore its natural beauty. Some of the best options include:

  • Mount Takachiho Hiking Trail: This trail leads to the summit of Mount Takachiho, offering stunning views of the Takachiho Gorge and surrounding landscape along the way.
  • Kirishima Hiking Trail: This trail leads through the volcanic landscape of Kirishima-Yaku National Park, with several peaks to choose from, including Mount Karakuni and Mount Kirishima.
  • Nango Road Cycling Course: This 60-kilometer course takes cyclists through picturesque rural landscapes and charming small towns, with plenty of opportunities to stop for local food and snacks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Miyazaki (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

Miyazaki Prefecture offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to choose from, including:

  • Surfing Lessons: Miyazaki is known for its excellent surfing conditions, with several surf schools and rental shops offering lessons and equipment.
  • Sake Brewery Tours: Miyazaki is home to several sake breweries, and visitors can take tours to learn about the sake-making process and sample some of the region’s best brews.
  • Horseback Riding: Miyazaki is famous for its horse-breeding industry, and visitors can take horseback riding tours through the scenic countryside.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take to the skies and admire the panoramic views of Miyazaki from above with a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Miyazaki (Prefecture)

For visitors with a week to spare, we recommend the following itinerary for exploring Miyazaki Prefecture:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Miyazaki City and explore the city’s cultural attractions, including the Miyazaki Prefectural Museum of Nature and History and the Aoshima Shrine.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to the Takachiho Gorge and explore its stunning waterfalls and hiking trails.
  • Day 3: Explore the Kirishima-Yaku National Park and hike to the summit of Mount Karakuni.
  • Day 4: Spend the day relaxing on one of Miyazaki’s beautiful beaches, such as Aoshima or Sunmesse Nichinan.
  • Day 5: Take a sake brewery tour and learn about the region’s traditional brewing techniques and history.
  • Day 6: Go horseback riding through the scenic countryside of Miyazaki.
  • Day 7: Take a hot air balloon ride and admire the panoramic views of Miyazaki from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Miyazaki Prefecture is known for its delicious local cuisine and traditional dishes. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Nanban: A popular dish featuring fried chicken topped with tartar sauce.
  • Hyuga-natsu Orange: A sweet and juicy variety of orange that is grown locally in Miyazaki.
  • Kurobuta Pork: A high-quality breed of pork that is raised in Miyazaki and served in a variety of dishes.
  • Sashimi: Fresh seafood is abundant in Miyazaki, and sashimi (sliced raw fish) is a popular way to enjoy it.

Local Beverages

  • Hyuganatsu Sour: A refreshing cocktail made with Hyuga-natsu orange juice and shochu (a type of Japanese liquor).
  • Miyazaki Shochu: A high-quality shochu that is made in Miyazaki using local spring water.

Where to Stay in Miyazaki (Prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Miyazaki Prefecture offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some top picks include:

  • Sheraton Grande Ocean Resort: A luxurious beachfront resort with multiple restaurants, a spa, and other amenities.
  • Hostel Seawall: A budget-friendly hostel located near the beach and Miyazaki City’s main attractions.
  • Yoshinoya Ryokan: A traditional Japanese inn that offers tatami rooms and onsen (hot springs) baths.

What to Buy in Miyazaki (Prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Miyazaki Prefecture is known for its high-quality agricultural products and handicrafts. Some popular souvenirs and local products include:

  • Hyuga-natsu oranges: Bring back a box of sweet and juicy oranges as a delicious souvenir.
  • Miyazaki beef: A high-quality variety of beef that is known for its marbled texture and rich flavor.
  • Aoshima Katagami: Handmade paper stencils that are used to print traditional patterns on fabric and other materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Miyazaki has a unique culture that is influenced by its history and geography. Some interesting customs and traditions include:

  • Kagura: A traditional Shinto dance that is performed at festivals and shrines throughout Miyazaki.
  • Hinokami Kagura: A type of Kagura that is performed in Miyazaki City, featuring elaborate costumes and music.
  • Horse Racing: A popular sport in Miyazaki, with races held throughout the year at the Miyazaki Horse Racing Association.
  • Onsen: The region is known for its hot springs, and visiting them is a popular way to relax and rejuvenate.

Best Time to Visit Miyazaki (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Miyazaki Prefecture depends on your preferences and interests. Generally, the most popular time to visit is during the summer (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are also great times to visit, with mild temperatures and beautiful foliage. Winter (December-February) is the least popular time to visit, but it is a great time for skiing and hot springs visits.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and visit local markets to experience the region’s vibrant food culture.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Do be polite and greet people with a smile and a bow when meeting them.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, as Japan has strict laws regarding waste disposal.
  • Don’t talk loudly or make noise in public places, as this is considered impolite in Japanese culture.
  • Don’t tip waitstaff or service providers, as tipping is not a common practice in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Miyazaki is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should still exercise common sense and take precautions to stay safe, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Japan has a high standard of healthcare, with modern facilities and highly trained medical professionals. Miyazaki Prefecture has several hospitals and clinics that can provide medical care in case of an emergency. However, it is recommended that visitors take out travel insurance before their trip to ensure they are covered in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Miyazaki (Prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Miyazaki Airport is the main airport serving Miyazaki Prefecture, with direct flights to major cities in Japan such as Tokyo and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Miyazaki (Prefecture)

There are several bus stations in Miyazaki City that provide transportation to other cities in Kyushu and beyond.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Miyazaki Prefecture by train, with several options available including the Kyushu Shinkansen and local trains. Driving is also an option, with several rental car companies offering services in Miyazaki.

How to Get Around in Miyazaki (Prefecture)

Miyazaki Prefecture has an extensive public transportation system, including buses and trains, that can take visitors to most major attractions and cities. Rental cars and bicycles are also available for visitors who prefer to explore on their own.

Where to Go Next from Miyazaki (Prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miyazaki Prefecture is just one part of the Kyushu region, which has many other attractions and destinations to explore. Some popular options include:

  • Kumamoto Prefecture: Known for its historic castle and beautiful parks.
  • Nagasaki Prefecture: Famous for its connections to World War II and the atomic bomb, with several museums and memorials.
  • Fukuoka City: The largest city in Kyushu, with a bustling food and entertainment scene.

Conclusion

Miyazaki Prefecture is a unique and beautiful destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing beach getaway, Miyazaki has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this fascinating region of Japan.
