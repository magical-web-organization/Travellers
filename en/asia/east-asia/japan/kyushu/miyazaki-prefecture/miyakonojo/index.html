Introduction to Miyakonojo

Miyakonojo is a beautiful city located in Miyazaki Prefecture, Kyushu, Japan. The city is known for its natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality of its people. With a population of around 162,000 people, Miyakonojo is the second-largest city in the prefecture.

List of Places to Visit in Miyakonojo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Miyakonojo Castle: This castle was built in the early 1600s by the Tokugawa shogunate and is now a museum showcasing local history and culture.
  • Kiyomizu Temple: This temple was founded in the late 8th century and is considered one of the most beautiful places in Miyakonojo.
  • Miyazaki Prefectural Art Museum: This museum features a large collection of art, including works by famous Japanese artists.

Natural Attractions of Miyakonojo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Takachiho Gorge: Located about an hour away from Miyakonojo, this beautiful gorge is famous for its stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Aya Teruha Suspension Bridge: This bridge is over 250 meters long and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest.
  • Mount Kirishima: This volcanic mountain range is located about an hour away from Miyakonojo and is a popular hiking destination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyakonojo

  • Kanoya Southern Cross Route: This 70-kilometer route is perfect for experienced hikers and bikers, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Miyakonojo to Takanabe Route: This 25-kilometer route is perfect for beginners, offering beautiful views of the countryside and small villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Miyakonojo: Activities and Tours

  • Sake Brewery Tour: Miyakonojo is home to several sake breweries, where you can take a tour and sample some of the best sake in Japan.
  • Takachiho Rafting: This exciting activity takes you down the rapids of the beautiful Takachiho Gorge.
  • Miyakonojo Udon Noodle Making: Learn how to make local udon noodles from scratch and enjoy a delicious meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyakonojo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Miyazaki Beef: This high-quality beef is bred locally in Miyazaki and is famous for its delicious flavor and tenderness.
  • Chicken Nanban: This dish originated in Miyazaki and features fried chicken topped with tartar sauce.
  • Shochu: This distilled spirit is famous all over Japan and is made from local ingredients like sweet potato or barley.

Local Beverages

  • Miyazaki Sake: Miyakonojo is home to several sake breweries, producing some of the best sake in the country.
  • Miyazaki Shochu: This distilled spirit is made from local ingredients and is famous for its unique taste and aroma.

Where to Stay in Miyakonojo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Miyakonojo Grand Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of the city, offering luxurious amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Miyakonojo Green Hotel: This hotel is perfect for budget-conscious travelers, offering affordable rates and comfortable rooms.
  • Miyakonojo Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is perfect for solo travelers, offering a comfortable and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Miyakonojo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Miyazaki Mango: Miyakonojo is known for its delicious mangoes, which are a perfect gift for friends and family back home.
  • Shochu: This distilled spirit is a popular souvenir from Miyakonojo, with many different flavors and varieties to choose from.
  • Pottery: Miyakonojo is known for its pottery, which makes for a unique and beautiful gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyakonojo

Miyakonojo has a rich culture and history, with several traditional festivals and customs. One of the most famous is the Miyakonojo Autumn Festival, a three-day event featuring traditional music, dance, and food. The people of Miyakonojo are also known for their warm hospitality and friendly demeanor.

Best Time to Visit Miyakonojo

The best time to visit Miyakonojo is in the spring and autumn, when the weather is mild and the scenery is beautiful. The cherry blossoms in the spring and the colorful foliage in the autumn are a must-see.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyakonojo

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try new foods and drinks.
  • Be friendly and polite to locals.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local culture and customs.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Leave your trash in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Miyakonojo

Miyakonojo is a safe city, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always important to take precautions when traveling, like keeping your valuables safe and avoiding dangerous areas.

Health in Miyakonojo

Miyakonojo has excellent medical facilities, with several hospitals and clinics available for emergency and routine care. It’s always important to have travel insurance when traveling to Japan, to ensure that you’re covered in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Miyakonojo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miyakonojo

The nearest airport to Miyakonojo is Miyazaki Airport, located about 30 kilometers away. From there, you can take a bus or train to Miyakonojo.

Bus Stations Near Miyakonojo

Miyakonojo has several bus stations located throughout the city, making it easy to get around by public transportation. The most popular bus station is Miyakonojo Station, which is also a major train station.

Other Options

There are several rental car companies available in Miyakonojo, which is a great option if you plan on exploring the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Miyakonojo

The best way to get around Miyakonojo is by using public transportation, like buses and trains. You can also rent a bicycle or a car if you prefer to explore on your own.

Where to Go Next from Miyakonojo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kagoshima: This city is located about two hours away from Miyakonojo and is home to several famous attractions like Mount Sakurajima and Japan’s first European-style castle.
  • Kumamoto: This city is located about 1.5 hours away from Miyakonojo and is famous for its beautiful castle and stunning natural beauty.
  • Fukuoka: This city is located about three hours away from Miyakonojo and is known for its delicious food and rich history.

Conclusion

Miyakonojo is a beautiful city with lots to offer, from its natural beauty and historical attractions to its delicious food and warm hospitality. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip to Miyakonojo and make the most of your time in this stunning location.
