Introduction to Saito

Saito is a hidden gem nestled in the picturesque Miyazaki prefecture, in Kyushu Island in Japan. The city boasts of rich culture and history, lush natural landscapes, and unique food and drink scene. Saito offers a perfect blend of modern trends and traditional customs that make it an ideal destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Saito

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saito Folklore Museum: Discover the rich cultural heritage of Saito through exhibits showcasing traditional crafts, festivals, and customs.
  • Saito Castle Ruins: Explore the ruins of the historic Saito Castle, built during the Edo period.
  • Sobo-ji Temple: Visit this ancient temple known for its beautiful gardens, intricate architecture, and stunning statues.

Natural Attractions of Saito: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kurokami: Embark on a challenging hike up to the Mount Kurokami peak for stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Fukushima Dam: Relax and enjoy picturesque views of the dam and its stunning surroundings.
  • Yajirimatsu Coast: Explore this hidden gem, a rugged and picturesque coastline with beautiful rock formations and stunning views of the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saito

  • Kurokami Mountain Trail: This scenic hiking trail is a must-do for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Saito Cycling Course: The cycling course is an excellent way to explore the beautiful landscapes of Saito.

Best Things to Do in Saito: Activities and Tours

  • Saito Art Walk: Take a stroll through Saito to discover its beautiful artworks created by local artists.
  • Saito City Sumo Tournament: Experience the thrill of Sumo Wrestling at this exciting annual event.

Food and Drink Scene of Saito

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Nanban: This traditional dish consists of deep-fried chicken served with tartar sauce and lemon wedges.
  • Kurokichi Tea: Try this unique tea made from roasted soybeans for a distinctively nutty flavor.
  • Saito Soba: Satisfy your cravings with Saito’s famous soba noodles, made from buckwheat and served with a flavorful broth.

Local Beverages

  • Saito Whiskey: Indulge in some locally brewed whiskey; Saito is home to several distilleries.
  • Kanmurishu: Taste Japan’s oldest alcoholic drink, made from sweet potato.

Where to Stay in Saito: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Wing International Miyazaki Saito: This elegant hotel boasts of comfortable rooms with stunning views of the city.
  • Sakaeya Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable traditional-style rooms and a warm atmosphere.

What to Buy in Saito: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saito Pottery: Take home some unique pottery, specially crafted using traditional techniques.
  • Saito Lacquerware: Buy beautifully crafted lacquerware to decorate your home.
  • Saito Tea: You can’t leave Saito without having a taste of their locally sourced tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Saito

Saito is known for its rich and unique cultural heritage, which is preserved through various festivals, traditions, and ceremonies. The city takes pride in its traditional crafts, such as pottery and lacquerware, which have been passed down from generations.

Best Time to Visit Saito

The best time to visit Saito is during autumn (October-November) when the weather is mild, and the autumn leaves make the landscapes even more beautiful. Spring is also a fantastic time to visit Saito when the cherry blossoms bloom and the air is scented with their delicate fragrance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saito

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Talk loudly in public spaces.
  • Litter the streets or public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saito

Saito is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, it is advisable to take basic precautions, such as staying vigilant of pickpockets and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Saito

Saito has excellent healthcare facilities, and the chances of catching illnesses are relatively low. However, it is always advisable to carry health insurance and take necessary precautions, such as wearing repellents for insect bites.

Getting to Saito: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saito

The closest airport to Saito is Miyazaki Airport, which is situated 57 kilometers (35 miles) away.

Bus Stations Near Saito

Saito Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers frequent services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

The train is an excellent option for travel to Saito. You can take the JR Nippo Main Line to Miyakonojo station and then take the bus or train to reach Saito.

How to Get Around in Saito

Saito has well-developed transport systems such as buses and taxis, making it easy to move around the city. You could also try cycling, which is a popular way to explore Saito’s scenic landscapes.

Where to Go Next from Saito: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Miyazaki: Explore the stunning city of Miyazaki with its pristine beaches, ancient shrines, and breathtaking mountain sceneries.
  • Takachiho: Visit Takachiho, known for its stunning gorge, ancient temples and shrines, and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Saito is a perfect destination for travelers looking to explore traditional Japanese culture and scenic landscapes. With its cultural and historical attractions, outdoor adventures, unique food and drink scene, and warm hospitality, Saito has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip to Saito today and create some unforgettable memories.
