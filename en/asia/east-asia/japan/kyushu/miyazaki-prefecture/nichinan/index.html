Introduction to Nichinan

Nestled in the southern part of Kyushu island of Japan, Nichinan is a picturesque coastal city in Miyazaki prefecture. This lively city is known for its stunning natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and sumptuous cuisine. Nichinan is a perfect destination for those looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Nichinan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Udo Jingu Shrine: Surrounded by cliffs and offering breathtaking ocean views, the shrine is known as the “power spot” of Nichinan. It is said that bouncing a stone off the shrine’s torii gate will bring good luck.
  • Obi Castle Town: A beautifully preserved castle town from the Edo period (1603-1867). Walk the cobblestone streets and explore the many traditional Japanese houses that have been converted into museums, shops, and restaurants.
  • Sun Messe Nichinan: Modeled after the sun temple in Peru, this unique museum and amusement park features an exhibition of pre-Columbian art, replica of Machu Picchu, and a 360-degree cinema.
  • Nichinan Kaigan Quasi-National Park: A scenic coastline featuring cliffs, bays, and natural rock formations. The park has several walking trails and observation decks with stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Ryugu Sea Cave: An enormous hexagonal cave formed by volcanic activity along the Nichinan Coast. The cave’s interior can only be accessed by guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Nichinan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aoshima Island: A small, forested island connected to the mainland by a bridge. The island is known for its stunning landscapes, including the famous “Devil’s Washboard” rock formations, and a shrine dedicated to the Shinto sea goddess.
  • Kirishima-Yaku National Park: A sprawling national park located on the border between Miyazaki and Kagoshima prefectures. The park features several active volcanoes, hot springs, hiking trails, and scenic views.
  • Horikiri Pass: A mountain pass located in the north of Nichinan offering scenic views of the surrounding countryside. The pass has a well-maintained cycling path that makes it popular among cyclists.
  • Udo Coast: A dramatic coastline with towering cliffs, sea stacks, and secluded sandy beaches. The area is popular for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing.
  • Takachiho Gorge: Located about 2 hours drive from Nichinan, this breathtaking gorge is known for its high cliffs, waterfalls, and clear blue water. Visitors can rent boats and row up the gorge to experience its beauty up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nichinan

  • Nichinan-kaigan Trail: A scenic 2.8 km walking trail along the Nichinan coastline with numerous viewpoints and rest areas along the way.
  • Tobiume Valley Trail: A short and easy trail through a scenic valley with a small waterfall and a suspension bridge.
  • Horikiri Pass Cycling Path: A 10-km gently undulating path along the Horikiri Pass offering scenic mountain views.
  • Ishinamiya Trail: A 7.6 km hiking trail through scenic forests and along a river.

Best Things to Do in Nichinan: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and snorkeling: Explore the beautiful Nichinan coast by kayak and discover the marine life of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Surfing: Ride the waves at some of the best surfing spots in Kyushu.
  • Cycling: Rent a bike and explore Nichinan’s scenic countryside on a peaceful cycling route.
  • Japanese Cooking Class: Learn to make traditional Japanese dishes from local chefs, using local ingredients.
  • Sake Tasting Tour: Visit Nichinan’s top sake breweries and taste some of the best local sake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nichinan

Day Activities
1 Visit Udo Jingu Shrine and the nearby caves.
2 Explore Obi Castle Town and its many museums and shops.
3 Take a cycling trip to Horikiri Pass or enjoy a kayaking and snorkeling tour.
4 Visit Sun Messe Nichinan and enjoy the exhibitions, amusement park rides, and cinema.
5 Take a day trip to Takachiho Gorge and enjoy hiking or boating.
6 Visit Aoshima Island and its famous rock formations and shrine.
7 Enjoy a leisurely day exploring the Nichinan Kaigan Quasi-National Park or relaxing at Udo Coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Nichinan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Namban: Fried chicken topped with tartar sauce and soy sauce.
  • Hyuga-natsu: A juicy citrus fruit only found on the Nichinan Coast.
  • Nebuta: A type of sushi roll made with raw fish, cucumber, and shiso leaves.
  • Dango-jiru: A hearty soup made with local vegetables, dumplings, and miso broth.
  • Miyazaki beef: A premium beef known for its rich flavor and tender texture.

Local Beverages

  • Shochu: A type of distilled liquor made from rice, barley, or sweet potatoes. It is a popular drink in Kyushu.
  • Umeshu: A sweet plum wine made with local Hyuga plums.
  • Sake: Nichinan produces a variety of high-quality sake using local rice and pristine water sources.

Where to Stay in Nichinan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nango Prince Hotel: A luxury hotel with an outdoor pool and beach access.
  • Solaria Nishitetsu Hotel Nichinan: A modern hotel near the harbor with an onsen (hot spring).
  • Nichinan Kaigan Super Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with a great location near the Nichinan Coast.
  • Cul-Port Guest House: A cozy guesthouse in Obi Castle Town offering traditional Japanese-style rooms.

What to Buy in Nichinan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hyuga-natsu Products: Buy a variety of products made with Hyuga-natsu, such as juice, jam, and sweets.
  • Nichi-ban Shochu: Bring home a bottle of locally distilled shochu as a souvenir.
  • Obi Pottery: A traditional Japanese pottery style produced in Obi Castle Town.
  • Sun Messe Nichinan Souvenirs: Bring home a miniature replica of Machu Picchu or other Peruvian artifacts as a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Nichinan

  • Shinto: Shinto is the indigenous religion of Japan and is still widely practiced in Nichinan. Visitors should be aware of local customs, such as removing shoes before entering a shrine or temple.
  • Respect for Elders: Nichinan is known for its large elderly population, and visitors should be aware of local customs, such as using respectful language when addressing older people.
  • Eating and Drinking in Public: It is uncommon to eat or drink in public in Japan, and visitors should be aware of local customs and avoid doing so.

Best Time to Visit Nichinan

The best time to visit Nichinan is between October and May when the weather is mild and dry. However, Nichinan is beautiful all year round, and visitors can enjoy summer activities like swimming and surfing, and autumn activities like hiking and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nichinan

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Learn basic Japanese phrases
  • Observe proper etiquette when visiting temples and shrines

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas
  • Tip at restaurants or hotels
  • Talk loudly on public transportation
  • Ignore safety warnings at hiking trails or beaches

Safety and Health

Safety in Nichinan

Nichinan is a safe city, and visitors can feel secure walking around at night. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of typhoons and earthquakes, which are common in Japan.

Health in Nichinan

There are several medical facilities in Nichinan, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should have travel health insurance and keep in mind any necessary vaccinations before traveling to Japan.

Getting to Nichinan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nichinan

  • Miyazaki Airport: Located about 45 minutes by car from Nichinan, Miyazaki Airport has regular domestic flights to major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Nichinan

  • Nichinan Bus Terminal: Located in the center of Nichinan, the bus terminal has regular buses to nearby cities and destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Nichinan to explore the surrounding area.
  • Train: The closest train station to Nichinan is Aburatsu Station, which is about 30 minutes by car from the city center.

How to Get Around in Nichinan

The easiest way to get around Nichinan is by car, as public transportation can be limited. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or join organized tours to explore the city and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Nichinan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kagoshima

Located about 3 hours by car or train from Nichinan, Kagoshima is a vibrant city known for its active volcano, cultural landmarks, and delicious food.

Miyazaki

Located about 1 hour by car from Nichinan, Miyazaki City is the capital of Miyazaki Prefecture and is known for its beautiful beaches, hot springs, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Nichinan is a hidden gem in Japan, offering visitors a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and delicious cuisine. By following this guide, travelers can experience the best of Nichinan and create unforgettable memories.
