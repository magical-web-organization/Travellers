Introduction to Takachiho

Takachiho, located in Miyazaki prefecture of Kyushu Island in Japan, is one of the most alluring destinations in the country. Known for its scenic beauty, Takachiho is surrounded by lush green mountains, pristine rivers, and stunning waterfalls. Moreover, it is also a place of rich cultural heritage and history that dates back to the mythical age of Japan. This charming town attracts visitors seeking unique experiences, outdoor adventures, and a glimpse into Japan’s ancient past.

List of Places to Visit in Takachiho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Takachiho has a rich cultural heritage and history that is preserved in museums and historical sites. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Takachiho Historical Museum: This museum showcases the town’s local history, including its unique mythological legacy, ancient customs, costumes, and utensils.
  • Amano Iwato Shrine: This shrine is a significant cultural and spiritual site. It is said to be the place where the Sun Goddess Amaterasu hid in a cave that resulted in darkness all over Japan until lured out again.
  • Takachiho Shrine: The Takachiho Shrine is one of the most significant Shinto shrines in Japan. It is nestled in a valley surrounded by tall mountains and lush forests. The shrine is known for its traditional architecture and sacred dance performances.

Natural Attractions of Takachiho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Takachiho is known for its scenic views and natural outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit attractions for nature enthusiasts:

  • Takachiho Gorge: A beautiful gorge with turquoise blue water surrounded by steep cliffs of basalt columns. You can rent a boat and row under the fall or enjoy rafting down the river.
  • Manai Falls: A serene waterfall located in a forested area, ideal for hiking and picnicking.
  • Mt. Aso: An active volcano located near Takachiho offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kyusuikei: A scenic gorge and waterfall located in the nearby town of Hyuga. It is a popular spot for hiking and river rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Takachiho

Cycling and hiking are great ways to explore Takachiho’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Takachiho Gorge Loop Trail: A one-mile-long scenic trail that surrounds the gorge and offers spectacular views.
  • Takachiho Nature Trail: A nine-mile-long picturesque trail that takes you through the lush forests and ancient cemeteries.
  • Takachiho to Amano Iwato Shrine: A two-mile-long trail that leads to the mysterious cave where the sun goddess Amaterasu hid.

Best Things to Do in Takachiho: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural beauty and cultural attractions, Takachiho offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Takachiho:

  • Takachiho Fireworks Show: An annual summer event taking place on the Takachiho riverbank, offering a spectacular display of fireworks.
  • Takachiho Kagura Dance: A traditional dance performance held at the Takachiho Shrine, showcasing the town’s cultural and mythological heritage.
  • Kamishikimi Kumano-Ima Shrine: A hidden gem located deep in the forest, surrounded by an impressive mountain range.
  • Takachiho Beer: A local brewery where you can taste a variety of craft beers made with pure mineral-rich water from the mountains

Week-Long Itinerary for Takachiho

If you have a week to spend in Takachiho, you can immerse yourself in its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Takachiho

Takachiho has a rich food and drink culture with many delicious local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Takachiho Torinabe: A hotpot dish made with chicken and vegetables cooked in a rich miso-based broth.
  • Takachiho Soba Noodles: Buckwheat noodles served either hot or cold with a dipping sauce.
  • Local Tempura: Takoyaki, Squid Hoods, and other seasonal ingredients are battered and fried to a crisp.

Local Beverages

  • Takachiho Sake: Sake made from pure mineral-rich water from the mountains.
  • Takachiho Beer: Local beer made with pure mountain water and unique flavors, such as Green tea, Sakura, or Ginger.

Where to Stay in Takachiho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Takachiho offers a range of accommodations for all budgets. Here are some of the most comfortable options:

  • Ryokans: Traditional Japanese inns that offer a unique cultural experience, including Tatami mats, Futon bedding, and communal baths.
  • Minshuku: Small guesthouses owned by locals that offer a homey atmosphere and a chance to get to know the local culture and lifestyle.
  • Hotels: Several modern Western-style hotels with good amenities.

What to Buy in Takachiho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Takachiho is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best:

  • Takachiho Kagura Masks: Traditional masks used in the Kagura Dance performances that make for unique souvenirs.
  • Takachiho Sake: Takachiho’s sake makes for an excellent gift or souvenir that captures the local flavor.
  • Takachiho Handicrafts: Local handicrafts include pottery, textiles, and other handmade items.

Local Culture and Customs of Takachiho

Takachiho is known for its unique cultural heritage, ancient customs, and mythological significance. Here are some things to know about the local culture and customs:

  • Kagura Dance: a sacred Shinto dance of Takachiho that tells Japan’s mythological stories.
  • Shinto Shrine: Takchiho’s shrine is a spiritual center and hosts many traditional festivals throughout the year.
  • Respect for Nature: The town’s residents have a strong connection to nature and are committed to preserving it.

Best Time to Visit Takachiho

Takachiho can be visited throughout the year, each season offering a unique experience. Spring and autumn seasons are especially lovely due to mild weather, blossoms, and colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Takachiho

To make the most of your trip, it is best to keep in mind a few things:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take your shoes off when visiting shrines, temples, and people’s houses.
  • Try local dishes, drinks, and products.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb shrine and temple artifacts.
  • Litter in public areas.
  • Be late for appointments or tours.

Safety and Health

Takachiho is generally a safe place to visit. However, as with any travel destination, it is essential to take necessary precautions and be aware of local conditions.

Safety in Takachiho

  • Respect the local natural environment and follow the defined paths.
  • Stay aware of the weather and natural hazards such as landslides, strong winds, and earthquakes.
  • Be careful when walking on narrow roads and when driving a car.

Health in Takachiho

  • Check the weather conditions before going out, take precautions, and dress appropriately for the weather.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated.
  • Inform your hotel/guesthouse of any medical conditions before you arrive.

Getting to Takachiho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here is how to get to Takachiho:

Airports Near Takachiho

  • Kumamoto Airport: The nearest major airport, located about 100 km away from Takachiho.
  • Fukuoka Airport: Located about 160 km from Takachiho.

Bus Stations Near Takachiho

  • Miyazaki Terminal: The nearest transport hub located about 63 km away from Takachiho, taking around an hour by road.
  • Nobeoka Terminal: Located about 83 km from Takachiho and requiring one or two transfers.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Easily rented at the airport or in central Kumamoto.
  • Taxis: Accessible from the Miyazaki or Kumamoto airports and notable intercity stations.

How to Get Around in Takachiho

The easiest way to get around Takachiho is by rental car or taxi. Public transport options in the area are limited, and a car is the most flexible and reliable way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Takachiho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Takachiho is surrounded by many notable destinations worth exploring. Some excellent nearby destinations include:

  • Mt. Aso: A visit to the active volcano Mt. Aso, located about 35 km from Takachiho and next to the town of Minamiaso.
  • Udo Jingu Shrine: A scenic shrine located near Nichinan City, about 88 km from Takachiho
  • Kusasenri: A scenic pasture near Mt. Aso, around 38 km from Takachiho

Conclusion

Takachiho’s mesmerizing natural beauty and rich cultural heritage make it one of Japan’s most alluring destinations. From hiking and outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and local delicacies, Takachiho has something to offer for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to Takachiho, and get ready to embark on a once-in-a-lifetime adventure in Japan.
