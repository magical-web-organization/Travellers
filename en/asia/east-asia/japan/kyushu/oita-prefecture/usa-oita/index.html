Introduction to Usa (Oita)

Usa (Oita) is a small town located in Oita Prefecture on the island of Kyushu, in Japan. The town is known for its rich cultural history, stunning natural scenery, and delicious local cuisine.

Despite its small size, Usa (Oita) has a lot to offer visitors, from historic temples and shrines, to beautiful hiking and biking trails, and unique souvenirs to bring home.

This comprehensive guide will provide you with everything you need to plan your trip to Usa (Oita) and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Usa (Oita)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Usa (Oita) is home to several culturally significant attractions, including historic temples and museums that offer a glimpse into the town’s rich past.

  • Usa Jingu Shrine: One of the oldest and most important Shinto shrines in Japan, Usa Jingu Shrine is home to over 40 distinct buildings and structures. The shrine dates back to the 8th century and is considered a national treasure.
  • Oita Prefectural Museum: Located in Usa (Oita), this museum is dedicated to preserving the region’s history and culture, including exhibits on local pottery, traditional crafts, and archaeological artifacts.
  • Futago-ji Temple: A Buddhist temple located in Usa (Oita), Futago-ji Temple dates back to the 8th century and is known for its vibrant autumn foliage.

Natural Attractions of Usa (Oita): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to explore the great outdoors, Usa (Oita) offers many natural attractions, including hiking trails, scenic vistas, and even natural hot springs.

  • Mt. Yufu: A popular hiking destination, Mt. Yufu offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The mountain is also a popular spot for autumn foliage and cherry blossom viewing.
  • Hinatayama Hot Springs: Located on the outskirts of Usa (Oita), Hinatayama Hot Springs are known for their therapeutic properties and scenic vistas.
  • Makiodo Cave: A popular spot for spelunking, this limestone cave system in Usa (Oita) is home to intricate, naturally-formed stalactites and stalagmites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Usa (Oita)

Usa (Oita) has a variety of hiking and biking trails throughout the town and surrounding mountains.

  • Yufudake Trail: This hiking trail leads to the summit of Mt. Yufu and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Makiodo Trail: This trail leads to the Makiodo Cave and is known for its scenic views of the Oita River.
  • Ozeki Road: This scenic road winds its way through the mountains surrounding Usa (Oita) and is popular with bikers and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Usa (Oita): Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Usa (Oita). Whether you are interested in cultural activities, outdoor adventures, or unique experiences, there is something for everyone.

  • Sake Tasting: Usa (Oita) is home to several sake breweries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Tea Ceremony: For a unique cultural experience, consider participating in a traditional Japanese tea ceremony at one of the local tea houses.
  • Kayaking: Paddle down the Oita River and take in the stunning mountain scenery on a kayak tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Usa (Oita)

Day 1: Explore the historic Usa Jingu Shrine and nearby Futago-ji Temple.

Day 2: Take a leisurely bike ride or hike on the Ozeki Road trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 3: Visit the Oita Prefectural Museum to learn about the history and culture of the region.

Day 4: Relax in the natural hot springs at Hinatayama.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Beppu, a nearby town known for its hot springs and unique sand baths.

Day 6: Participate in a traditional Japanese tea ceremony at one of the local tea houses.

Day 7: Visit one of the local sake breweries for a tour and tasting.

Food and Drink Scene of Usa (Oita)

Must-Try Dishes

Usa (Oita) is known for its unique and delicious cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Toriten: A local specialty consisting of chicken tempura marinated in soy sauce and ginger.
  • Oita Beef: This premium beef is raised in nearby Oita Prefecture and is known for its marbled texture and rich flavor.
  • Jigoku Mushi: Translated as “Hell Steam,” this dish is a steamed dish using the natural heat from hot springs in Beppu.

Local Beverages

In addition to its delicious food, Usa (Oita) is also home to several unique local beverages.

  • Mugi Shochu: A type of Japanese distilled spirit made from barley, Mugi Shochu is a local favorite in Usa (Oita).
  • Oita Sake: Usa (Oita) is home to several sake breweries that produce unique and high-quality sake using locally-sourced rice.
  • Yufuin No Mori Beer: A craft beer brewed in nearby Yufuin, this beer is known for its crisp and refreshing flavor.

Where to Stay in Usa (Oita): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Usa (Oita) has a variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Yufuin Onsen Ryokan Hotel: This traditional Japanese inn offers comfortable rooms, stunning views of Mt. Yufu, and access to nearby hot springs.
  • Usa Hotel Annex: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Usa (Oita) and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • Hostel Yufuin: For those on a budget, Hostel Yufuin offers affordable dorm-style accommodation with a shared kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Usa (Oita): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Usa (Oita) offers a variety of unique and locally-made souvenirs and products, including:

  • Usa Mino-yaki Pottery: This local style of pottery is known for its distinct patterns and designs and can be found at several shops and galleries in Usa (Oita).
  • Bamboo Crafts: Usa (Oita) is home to several artisans who specialize in crafting beautiful and functional objects out of bamboo.
  • Local Sake: Bring home a bottle of delicious and unique Oita sake as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Usa (Oita)

Usa (Oita) has a rich cultural history and several unique customs and traditions. Visitors to the town should be respectful of these traditions and follow local customs.

  • Shinto Shrines: Shintoism is a prominent religion in Japan and visitors to Usa should respect the customs and practices of local Shinto shrines, including removing shoes before entering.
  • Bow and Greet: It is customary to bow and greet others when entering and leaving shops, restaurants, and other public spaces.
  • Etiquette: Japanese culture places a strong emphasis on politeness and respect. Visitors should be mindful of their behavior and speech and avoid causing offense.

Best Time to Visit Usa (Oita)

The best time to visit Usa (Oita) is during the autumn months (September to November) when the leaves on the trees begin to change colors, offering stunning vistas throughout the town.

Spring (March to May) is also a beautiful time to visit, when the cherry blossoms are in bloom and the weather is mild and pleasant.
