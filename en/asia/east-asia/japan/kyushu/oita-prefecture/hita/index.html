Introduction to Hita

Hita is a charming city located in the Oita Prefecture of Japan’s Kyushu region. The city is known for its tranquil and scenic atmosphere, welcoming locals, and rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Edo period. Hita’s traditional architecture, beautifully preserved temples, and picturesque landscapes offer an immersive experience into Japanese culture and lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Hita

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hita City Art Museum: This is a serene and modern art museum that showcases contemporary Japanese art.
  • Hita Gion Yamaboko Kaikan: The museum is dedicated to the city’s famous Gion Festival, which is held annually in July. The museum features various floats, costumes, and other artifacts from the festival.
  • Kuma no Yu: It’s a cultural center, a bathhouse, and a hot spring all rolled into one, rejuvenating experience.

Natural Attractions of Hita: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mamedamachi Traditional Village: This village is known for its preserved Edo period architecture and scenic views.
  • Mt. Hiko: It’s located on the border between Oita and Fukuoka prefecture and offers a beautiful hiking and climbing experience.
  • Oyamakuni Shrine: It’s an ancient shrine built more than 2,000 years ago in a sacred forest, offering a serene and peaceful atmosphere amidst nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hita

Hita is an outdoor adventure enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of hiking, biking, climbing and canoeing opportunities. Here are some of the famous routes:

  • Daikazashiki Hiking Trail: A hiking trail that leads to the top of Mt. Ryozen. The trail is challenging but rewarding, with scenic views of the city and surrounding landscapes.
  • Yabe River Canoeing: A canoeing experience down the scenic Yabe River is the perfect way to explore Hita and take in the stunning natural beauty of the region.
  • Hitayama Nature Trail: This trail winds through Hita’s verdant countryside and offers walkers an immersive experience into the Japanese countryside.

Best Things to Do in Hita: Activities and Tours

  • Enjoy Hot Spring Bathing: Hita has a variety of hot springs that offer a rejuvenating and relaxing experience.
  • Participate in the Gion Festival: If you’re in town in July, don’t miss the Hita Gion festival, a colorful parade of floats and traditional Japanese performances.
  • Cycling: With its picturesque landscapes, Hita is perfect to explore on bicycle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hita

Food and Drink Scene of Hita

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hita Yakisoba: Hita Yakisoba is a type of stir-fried noodle dish made with locally sourced ingredients, such as miso paste, shiitake mushrooms, and cabbage.
  • Hita Tori Dango: These meatballs of chicken are a local specialty and a perfect snack while roaming the streets.
  • Sake: Hita is home to various sake breweries, which produce some of the best sake in Japan.

Local Beverages

  • Brown Sugar Shochu: This alcoholic beverage is made from brown sugar and is often enjoyed with hot water.
  • Matcha Latte: Perfect for those who want a refreshing drink that is also healthy and tasty.

Where to Stay in Hita: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hita River Hotel: This hotel is located in a prime spot along the Hita River and offers scenic views and comfortable rooms.
  • Hita Onsen Ryokan Sanyokan: This Ryokan combines luxury and traditional Japanese hospitality, offering a relaxing experience in a beautiful location.
  • KKR Hotel Hita-ekimae: This hotel is located near Hita station and offers spacious rooms and modern facilities.

What to Buy in Hita: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hita Pottery: This pottery is a local specialty that comes in various shapes, sizes, and colors, making it an excellent souvenir to take home with you.
  • Miso Paste: Hita is known for high-quality, delicious miso paste that is made using traditional methods.
  • Yabe Fruit: Hita’s agricultural region, Yabe, is renowned for its high-quality fruit, which includes peaches, grapes, and melons.

Local Culture and Customs of Hita

Hita is famous for its cultural heritage and traditional Japanese customs that are celebrated and practiced in the city, including:

  • Gion Festival: The festival is held annually in July and is a time when locals gather to celebrate with music, dance performances, and traditional Japanese cuisine.
  • Tea Ceremony: Hita is known for its love of tea and its traditional Japanese tea ceremonies, which are held in various tea houses throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Hita

The best time to visit Hita is between March and May when the cherry blossom trees in the city are in bloom, creating a spectacular, picturesque scene.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hita

Do:

  • Respect customs and traditions in Hita, such as performing a small bow when entering a traditional Japanese restaurant.
  • Make an effort to learn some basic Japanese phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Explore the city with a bicycle to experience Hita’s picturesque landscapes and traditional architecture to the fullest.

Don’t:

  • Do not wear shoes inside traditional Japanese restaurants or homes.
  • Do not litter or smoke in public areas, as it’s against Japanese customs.
  • Do not speak loudly in public or play loud music, as it is considered impolite and disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hita

Hita is generally safe for visitors. The city has a low crime rate, and locals are welcoming and friendly. However, like any city, it’s essential to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Health in Hita

Japan has excellent healthcare facilities and advanced technology, making it one of the safest destinations to travel to. Visitors shouldn’t have any major health concerns while travelling to Hita.

Getting to Hita: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hita

  • Oita Airport (OIT): Located around 50 minutes away from Hita.

Bus Stations Near Hita

  • Hita Bus Terminal: Located near Hita Station, the main bus terminal connects Hita with other cities in Japan.

Other Options

  • Train: The easiest way to reach Hita is by taking the JR Kyushu Line, which runs from both Fukuoka and Oita.

How to Get Around in Hita

Hita is a compact city and is best explored on foot or by bicycle. Buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer them.

Where to Go Next from Hita: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beppu: Beppu is famous for its hot springs, sand baths, and boiling hot mud pools. It’s located between Hita and the Oita airport and can easily be visited in a day.
  • Usuki: This city is known for its Usuki Stone Buddhas, a group of over 60 Buddhist statues carved into the rock, which date back to the Heian period.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Hita is a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to explore and experience the traditional Japanese way of life. From its ancient shrines and temples, picturesque landscapes, and traditional festivals, Hita offers visitors an immersive experience into Japanese culture and lifestyle. Visitors can enjoy hot springs, climb mountains, ride bicycles, and explore the city’s local food and drink scene. Hita is an ideal destination for anyone looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in Japan.
