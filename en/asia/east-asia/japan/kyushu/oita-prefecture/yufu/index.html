Introduction to Yufu

Yufu is a small but charming city located in Oita Prefecture, part of the Kyushu region in Japan. It is a popular destination for both local and international tourists, thanks to its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and relaxing hot springs.

The city is surrounded by mountains, and its landscape is dotted with lush greenery, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. Yufu also has a rich cultural history, with many museums, historic buildings, and festivals to explore.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover all the must-visit attractions, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and everything else you need to know about visiting Yufu.

List of Places to Visit in Yufu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yufuin Showa Museum: This museum showcases life in Japan during the Showa era (1926-1989). Visitors can step back in time and see vintage toys, household items, and traditional crafts.

  • Yunohira Onsen History Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of Yunohira Onsen, one of the oldest hot springs in Yufu. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of hot springs in the region.

  • Lake Kinrin: Located in the center of Yufuin, Lake Kinrin is a beautiful lagoon surrounded by trees and flowers. Visitors can take a leisurely walk around the lake or enjoy a boat ride.

Natural Attractions of Yufu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Yufu: This is a popular hiking destination for visitors who want to enjoy panoramic views of the city.

  • Oita Fragrance Museum: This botanical museum is home to over 1,000 plant species, including aromatic plants such as lavender and rosemary.

  • Hebin-no-ie: This walking trail leads to a beautiful waterfall located in a peaceful forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yufu

Yufu is known for its beautiful hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the city. Here are some of the best routes to experience in Yufu:

  • Mount Yufu: As mentioned earlier, Mount Yufu is a popular hiking destination that offers stunning views of the city. The trail is relatively easy, and hikers can take their time to enjoy the scenery.

  • Yufu Road: This biking route takes visitors through the beautiful countryside of Yufu, passing by farms, hot springs, and scenic views.

  • Yunohira Onsen: This trail leads visitors through the Yunohira hot springs area, showcasing the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Yufu: Activities and Tours

Yufu offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • Onsen (Hot spring) Experience: Yufu is famous for its hot springs, and visitors can have a relaxing hot spring experience in one of the many onsen facilities in the city.

  • Bamboo Crafts Workshop: Visitors can learn about the traditional bamboo crafts of Yufu and try their hand at making their own bamboo crafts.

  • Food Tour: Visitors can enjoy the diverse food scene in Yufu, such as restaurants, markets, and street vendors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yufu

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors who want to explore all that Yufu has to offer:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Yufu and relax at an onsen facility.
  • Day 2: Visit the Yufuin Showa Museum and Lake Kinrin.
  • Day 3: Hike or bike on the Yufu Road trail.
  • Day 4: Explore the Yunohira Onsen area.
  • Day 5: Go on a food tour and dine at a local restaurant.
  • Day 6: Visit the Oita Fragrance Museum and Hebin-no-ie.
  • Day 7: Relax at another onsen facility before departing Yufu.

Food and Drink Scene of Yufu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Nanban: This dish features deep-fried chicken served with tartar sauce and vegetables.

  • Oita Wagyu Beef Sushi: This dish features locally sourced, high-quality beef paired with sushi rice.

  • Onsen Tamago: This dish features eggs boiled in a hot spring, resulting in a unique, soft texture.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Yufu is known for its sake, which is brewed using pure spring water and high-quality rice.

  • Shochu: This is a distilled Japanese beverage that is popular in Yufu. It is made using various ingredients, such as sweet potatoes, barley, and rice.

  • Yufuin tea: This locally produced tea is known for its unique aroma and subtle taste.

Where to Stay in Yufu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yufu offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, with something to suit every budget. Here are a few recommended options:

  • Yufuin Onsen Yufuin Gettoan: This onsen hotel offers luxurious accommodation with beautiful views of Mount Yufu.

  • Ryokan Yufuin Kaze no Mori: This traditional Japanese inn offers a comfortable stay in a peaceful countryside setting.

  • Yufuin Sansuikan: This modern hotel features traditional Japanese design elements and is located within walking distance of Yufuin Station.

What to Buy in Yufu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yufuin candles: These candles are made using a unique process that results in beautiful, multicolored wax designs.

  • Bamboo crafts: Visitors can purchase a variety of bamboo crafts, such as baskets and trays, as souvenirs.

  • Yufuin tea: Visitors can bring home locally produced tea as a reminder of their trip to Yufu.

Local Culture and Customs of Yufu

Yufu has a rich cultural heritage that visitors should be aware of during their stay. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Removing shoes: Visitors should remove their shoes before entering a traditional Japanese home or inn.

  • Respectful behavior: Visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices, such as bowing when greeting someone.

  • Etiquette in onsens: Visitors should be respectful of others in hot springs and follow the proper etiquette, such as washing before entering the water.

Best Time to Visit Yufu

The best time to visit Yufu is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most beautiful. However, Yufu is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy the hot springs and other activities regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yufu

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Yufu:

Do:

  • Do wear comfortable clothing and shoes for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local food and drinks.
  • Do explore Yufu’s natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to remove your shoes before entering a traditional Japanese home or inn.
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals.
  • Don’t ignore proper etiquette in hot springs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yufu

Yufu is a safe city, with low crime rates, but visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Yufu

Yufu is known for its hot springs, which are believed to have therapeutic benefits for a variety of ailments. However, visitors should still take care of their health while traveling, such as staying hydrated and avoiding overexertion during outdoor activities.

Getting to Yufu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yufu

The nearest major airport to Yufu is Oita Airport, which is approximately 60 minutes by bus or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Yufu

Yufuin Station is the main bus station in Yufu, offering connections to nearby cities and attractions. The city also has several local bus routes that provide access to various tourist spots in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Yufu by train or car, although a car is not necessary once in the city, as most attractions are within walking distance of each other.

How to Get Around in Yufu

Yufu is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take local buses to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Yufu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few suggestions for nearby destinations and excursions from Yufu:

  • Beppu: This neighboring city is known for its hot springs and offers a variety of tourist attractions, such as a bamboo forest and a monkey park.

  • Mount Aso: This active volcano has spectacular views and offers hiking and outdoor activities.

  • Oita City: Oita City is the capital of Oita Prefecture and offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions, such as museums and temples.

Conclusion

Yufu is a gem of a city, offering natural beauty, relaxing hot springs, and rich cultural heritage. With so much to see and do, visitors are sure to have a memorable experience in this charming city.
