Introduction to Gotō Islands

Gotō Islands is a charming archipelago in Nagasaki Prefecture, Japan. Comprised of approximately 140 islands, the region offers stunning natural scenery and cultural heritage. The islands have been a significant center for Christianity in Japan since the 16th century and are known for their unique churches and shrines.

Whether you’re interested in exploring Japan’s cultural heritage or you seek to experience nature at its best, Gotō Islands should be on your destination list. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip, including the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, local customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Gotō Islands

Gotō Islands offer a wealth of attractions to explore, ranging from historically significant sites, scenic views, to outdoor adventures. Here is our pick of the top places to visit on Gotō Islands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gotō Islands is rich in cultural heritage, and it’s evident in the many museums, churches, and shrines on the island. Here are a few places that highlight the unique and fascinating history and culture of the region:

  • Fukue Island Museum of History and Folklore: This museum showcases the history of the island and the unique culture of the region. Exhibits include traditional costumes, crafts, and historical artifacts from the islands.

  • Kazusaya Residence: This is Gotō Island’s oldest private residence that has been preserved for public viewing. The residence was built in the 18th century and now houses an extensive collection of antique ceramics and furniture.

  • Tadehara Church: A landmark of the islands, the Tadehara Church stands tall as an icon of Christian faith on the island. Visitors can marvel at the unique architecture of the Gothic style church and the beautiful stained-glass windows.

  • The Hidden Christian Sites in the Nagasaki Region: The hidden Christian Sites on Gotō islands include Akasaki Ura and Hisaka Island, which are registered as UNESCO World Heritage Sites. These places are significant in the history of Christianity in Japan and are essential for anyone interested in religious history.

Natural Attractions of Gotō Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The stunning natural beauty of the islands is evident from the many outdoor attractions on the islands. Here are some of the breathtaking natural attractions to visit in Gotō Islands:

  • Kofuji: This is Gotō’s highest peak and offers stunning views of the surrounding islands. The area is popular among hikers, and there are several trails available to explore.

  • Narao: Narao is known for its beautiful white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters. The area has an excellent fishing and marine sports scene and is perfect for a relaxing day at the beach.

  • Fukue Island Observation Deck: The Observation Deck is an excellent spot to get a panoramic view of Fukue Island.

  • Islands of Tamanoura: The Tamanoura National Park is made up of several islands, including Kuroshima and Kamigoshima, offering stunning scenery and vast natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gotō Islands

Gotō Islands offer several trails and routes to explore for hikers and bikers. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a beginner more interested in leisurely bike rides, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to explore in Gotō Islands:

  • Nakadake Onsen Road: This is a popular route with hikers who want to explore the natural beauty of the region. The trail is approximately 12km long and takes visitors through gorgeous landscapes to the hot springs.

  • Gotō Cycling Road: This bike route is a popular way to see the enchanting landscapes of Gotō. The road is approximately 33km long and takes visitors through the heart of the islands, stunning coastal scenery, and rural villages.

Best Things to Do in Gotō Islands: Activities and Tours

Gotō Islands offer a host of activities and tours to participate in during your visit. Here are some of the best activities to try:

  • Crab Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity in Gotō Islands, and some visitors come here specifically to enjoy this. Crab fishing is a fun and unique way to experience the local marine life while enjoying a delicious seafood meal after.

  • Scuba Diving: Another fantastic way to see the marine life of Gotō Islands is through scuba diving. The crystal-clear waters of Gotō Islands offer excellent visibility, and visitors can expect to come face to face with sea turtles, colorful fish, and coral reefs.

  • Cruising: A popular way to see the islands from a different angle is through cruising. Visitors can take a glass-bottom boat tour or try island hopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Gotō Islands

Gotō Islands offer a unique and delicious food scene with a focus on seafood cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gotō Island Sashimi: This sashimi combines the freshest seafood from the local waters, including yellowtail, tuna, squid, and more.

  • Teuchi Udon: These handmade noodles are a local specialty that is served with various toppings such as tempura, grated daikon, and soy sauce.

  • Gotō Island Oysters: The oysters from Gotō Islands are some of the best in Japan; they are large, plump, and juicy, and can be enjoyed grilled, raw, or fried.

Local Beverages

  • Hishiwa: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from shochu and water, it is a local specialty that is enjoyed with meals.

  • Gotō Island Sake: This sake is made from local rice and water and has a smooth and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Gotō Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gotō Islands offer a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel New Nagasaki: This hotel is located in the heart of Nagasaki and offers comfortable and modern rooms with excellent amenities.

  • Hotel Southern Cross Goto: This hotel is located on Fukue Island and offers comfortable rooms with excellent views of the surrounding sea.

  • Goto Guesthouse: This guesthouse is a cozy and charming place to stay and offers comfortable dormitory-style rooms and private rooms with a shared bathroom.

What to Buy in Gotō Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gotō Islands offer a range of local products and souvenirs to bring home. Here are some of the best items to buy:

  • Gotō Island Pottery: Gotō’s pottery is renowned for its unique designs and quality craftsmanship.

  • Gotō Island Sweets: The islands produce a range of sweets, including Momoyama Cookies, which are a local favorite.

  • Gotō Island Sofuma: Gotō’s unique style of clothing has a simple, elegant design and is perfect for any occasion.

Local Culture and Customs of Gotō Islands

Gotō Islands have a unique culture and customs that visitors should respect when visiting. Here are some of the customs to keep in mind:

  • Shoes should be removed when entering a private home or temple.

  • Public bathing is an important tradition on the island, and visitors should follow the local protocols.

  • Gotō Islands have a strong Christian heritage, and visitors should respect the religious sites and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Gotō Islands

The best time to visit Gotō Islands is between April and November when temperatures are mild, and there is less rainfall. Spring and fall are particularly beautiful on the island, with cherry blossoms and autumn leaves providing breathtaking views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gotō Islands

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to follow when visiting Gotō Islands:

Do:

  • Try local seafood cuisine and traditional drinks.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Explore the outdoors and natural beauty of the islands.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or traditions.

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Ignore rules or protocols for public bathing.

Safety and Health

Gotō Islands are generally safe for visitors, but it’s essential to keep in mind a few safety and health tips.

Safety in Gotō Islands

  • Visitors should be wary of the strong ocean currents in the area when swimming.

  • When hiking, it is essential to stay on the designated trails and bring proper gear and snacks.

  • It’s recommended to get travel insurance and keep a copy of the emergency contact information with you.

Health in Gotō Islands

  • Visitors should be aware of the risk of sunburn; it’s critical to bring sunscreen and wear a hat.

  • The islands have a range of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies.

  • Visitors should be aware of the Japanese style of bathing and follow the local protocols.

Getting to Gotō Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gotō Islands are accessible by plane or ferry from Nagasaki City. Here are some of the transportation options to consider:

  • Plane: Flights are available from Nagasaki Airport to Fukue Airport, which takes approximately 35 minutes.

  • Ferry: Direct ferries are available from Nagasaki Port to Fukue Port, which takes approximately 2 hours and 45 minutes.

Airports Near Gotō Islands

  • Nagasaki Airport: This airport is the nearest airport to Gotō Islands, and it offers flights to major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Gotō Islands

  • Nagasaki Bus Terminal: The Nagasaki Bus Terminal is the hub for bus travel to the Nagasaki region.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are available on the islands, but they can be expensive.

  • Rental Car: Rental cars are available on Fukue Island.

How to Get Around in Gotō Islands

The best way to get around Gotō Islands is by rental car or bus. Boats and ferries are also available to explore the surrounding islands. Taxis are expensive but can be convenient for short trips.

Where to Go Next from Gotō Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gotō Islands are an excellent starting point for exploring Nagasaki Prefecture and the surrounding areas. Here are some of the nearby destinations to explore:

  • Nagasaki City: Known for its rich cultural heritage, including significant sites related to World War II and traditional Japanese architecture.

  • Shimabara Peninsula: An area famous for its hot springs and stunning natural scenery.

  • Unzen Onsen: A popular Hot spring resort town famous for its Jigoku, a steamy area with sulfurous steam vents.

Conclusion

Gotō Islands offer a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty, and are an ideal destination for anyone seeking an off-the-beaten-track experience in Japan. With this comprehensive guide, we hope to inspire you to discover all that these enchanting islands have to offer. From exploring historic sites, enjoying outdoor adventures, savoring local cuisine, to experiencing local customs, there’s something for everyone on Gotō Islands.
