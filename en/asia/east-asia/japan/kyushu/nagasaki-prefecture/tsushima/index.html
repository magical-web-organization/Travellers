Introduction to Tsushima

Tsushima is a small island located in the Tsushima Strait, situated between South Korea and Japan’s Kyushu Island. The island is part of Nagasaki Prefecture, Japan. Tsushima is known for its historical significance, beautiful nature, and mouth-watering cuisine that has been influenced by Japanese and Korean cultures. Despite being a small island, Tsushima has an array of attractions to offer, from fascinatingly unique museums to waterfalls and hiking trails.

List of Places to Visit in Tsushima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tsushima Folk Museum

The Tsushima Folk Museum is a historical museum that showcases traditional Tsushima culture, folk tales, and everyday life. The museum exhibits items like old tools and traditional costumes and has a section dedicated to the island’s relationship with Korea. It is a recommended place to visit if you want to learn more about Tsushima’s culture and history.

Shimauchi Shrine

Built-in 719, the Shimauchi Shrine is one of the oldest and most respected shrines in Tsushima. The shrine has been rebuilt several times, with its current design dating back to 1682. A must-visit spot when you want to learn more about the local beliefs and religious practices.

Tsushima Wildlife Conservation Center

The Tsushima Wildlife Conservation Centre offers visitors the chance to get acquainted with Japanese Tsushima black rats, and learn more about the island’s efforts to repopulate the species. This museum is expected to fascinate animal lovers and curious travelers alike.

Natural Attractions of Tsushima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sotaro Falls

Sotaro Falls is located on the western side of the island and is a must-visit place for nature enthusiasts. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greens and offers a serene environment to relax and take in the beauty of the area. If you’re lucky, you might even spot the local wildlife here.

Komoda Lighthouse

Komoda Lighthouse is situated on the southern coast of Tsushima Island, Japan. The view from the lighthouse is stunning, offering a panoramic view of Tsushima Island and the surrounding sea. Visitors can hike to the lighthouse and enjoy nature’s beauty along the way while being rewarded with a magnificent view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsushima

Mt. Izanagi

The trek up to Mt. Izanagi is a challenging and rewarding experience. Stunning views of Tsushima can be enjoyed by those who hike up the mountain, and the hike takes about four hours (round trip). Visitors can opt to bike up to the trek starting point and hike from there or hike the entire trail. It is a favored activity by both locals and tourists.

Shirataki Falls

Shirataki Falls is located within a hike from Sotaro Falls, around 90 minutes in total to get to the falls. As you trek to the falls, hikers will be greeted with stunning views of Tsushima Island’s greenery and the sound of flowing waterfalls. It is a perfect location for those who want a little adventure, a little hike, and a peaceful waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Tsushima: Activities and Tours

Sea Kayaking Tours

Tsushima is surrounded by water, so sea kayaking is a fun way to explore the nearby islands and appreciate the natural beauty of Tsushima from another viewpoint. There are various kayaking tours to choose from, ranging from a few hours to a full day, and some tours come with snorkeling and other activities.

Tsushima Island Trip

A Tsushima island trip is a must-do tour since it takes you around the island to visit several famous spots by vehicle, such as the famous Shimauchi Shrine and Komoda Lighthouse. A local tour guide will show you around and provide you with historical and cultural facts while showcasing the natural beauty of the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsushima

Must-Try Dishes

Kuruma Ebi

Kuruma Ebi, commonly known as Japanese tiger prawns, are one of Tsushima’s local specialties. These prawns are impeccably well-prepared, a must-try dish for seafood lovers.

Kalguksu

Tsushima was a crucial stop for Korean traders, who left an influence in the local food scene. Kalguksu is a Korean dish composed of hand-made noodles and seafood in a flavorful broth with a spicy kick.

Local Beverages

Tsushima is known for its local rice wine, which has a crisp and refreshing taste. You can experience a factory tour and learn how the rice wine is made, as well as sample some local flavors. It’s a perfect match for the local dishes, especially fresh seafood.

Where to Stay in Tsushima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation options on the island are limited, but they’re perfect for those looking for comfort and convenience. You have a range of options to pick from depending on your budget and preference. There are numerous traditional Japanese inns, ryokans, with Tatami rooms, futons, and onsen. Those looking for a more relaxed stay can opt for a western-style hotel offering modern amenities and convenience.

What to Buy in Tsushima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Various souvenirs are available for visitors to take home, showcasing the local culture of Tsushima. Some of the must-buy souvenirs include:

  • Local sake and/or rice wine
  • Traditional pottery
  • Handmade glassworks
  • Local textiles

Local Culture and Customs of Tsushima

Tsushima has a mix of local Korean and Japanese cultures, influencing the island’s customs significantly. Visitors appreciate respecting the local culture by learning and observing the customs while visiting. For example, before entering a shrine, visitors must bow and cleanse themselves at temizuya by washing their hands and rinsing their mouths.

Best Time to Visit Tsushima

The best time to travel to Tsushima is between February and May, when the weather is comfortable and the temperatures are mild: ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. June to September can be considered for those who do not mind the summer heat; Autumn is also a popular season for those who wish to explore the beautiful foliage at their finest. Tsushima’s height is 680m above sea level, so it tends to be chilly during the winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsushima

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Get to know the locals and interact with them.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Explore outdoor activities and natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter, the island is strict about garbage disposal.
  • Ignore rules and regulations, especially when visiting shrines and temples.
  • Capture photos where photography is restricted.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsushima

Tsushima is considered safe, but visitors should be careful while indulging in outdoor activities like hiking, and swimming. Visitors should always follow the rules and regulations, especially when entering natural reserves and protected wildlife areas.

Health in Tsushima

It is recommended that visitors drink filtered water, as tap water is not advisable for drinking. There are medical facilities located around the island, but it is always advised to have travel insurance before traveling.

Getting to Tsushima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsushima

Tsushima Airport is accessible from other major airports across Japan, with regular flights from Tokyo, Nagoya, and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Tsushima

JR Kyushu Bus terminal in Nagasaki city or directly from Hakata bus terminal in Fukuoka has a bus service available to Tsushima island.

Other Options

It is also possible to get to Tsushima by boat from Fukuoka, Japan, to Busan, South Korea. Tsushima is generally accessible only by boat or airplane, making it a unique destination for visitors.

How to Get Around in Tsushima

The easiest way to travel around Tsushima is by hiring a car with an English GPS navigation system, but there are bus and taxi options available for those who don’t fancy driving themselves.

Where to Go Next from Tsushima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kyushu region along with Nagasaki and Fukuoka city in particular, offer a lot of attractions to visitors all year round. Travelers wanting to explore more of Japan can hop on a plane from Tsushima Airport, or choose to take a ferry to different destinations from the port of Tsushima.

Conclusion

Tsushima is one of Japan’s less known travel gems, but it is surrounded by natural beauty, fascinating culture, and is a perfect getaway from Japan’s more crowded cities. Visitors will be welcomed with open arms as they explore everything Tsushima has to offer, from outdoor activities to indulging in the local delicacies. Plan a trip to Tsushima, and you will not be disappointed.
