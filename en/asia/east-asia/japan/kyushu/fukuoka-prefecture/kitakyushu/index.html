Introduction to Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu, located in Fukuoka Prefecture, in the island of Kyushu, is a vibrant city that offers both modern and traditional Japan. Known for its famous landmarks such as the Kokura Castle, the city of Kitakyushu is also popular for its outdoor attractions such as the stunning Kawachi Fuji Garden and Mount Sarakura. This city is a great destination for tourists who want to experience Japanese culture and nature.

List of Places to Visit in Kitakyushu

There are many exciting places to visit in Kitakyushu that cater to different interests. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kokura Castle: A beautifully restored castle that showcases architectural brilliance and historical significance.
  • Kitakyushu Museum of Natural History & Human History: An informative museum featuring exhibits that showcase the rich history and natural beauty of the region.
  • Mojiko Retro: A preserved merchant district that encapsulates the charm of old Japan.
  • Space World: An amusement park with a space theme that is perfect for families.

Natural Attractions of Kitakyushu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kawachi Fuji Garden: A breathtaking floral wonderland featuring wisteria tunnels and gardens.
  • Mount Sarakura: A scenic spot that lets you take in panoramic views of the city and the surrounding landscape.
  • Kokura Bay: A picturesque place to relax and enjoy the sea breeze while savoring fresh seafood.
  • Kawachi Reservoir: A popular spot for fishing and enjoying the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu also offers some fantastic hiking and biking routes, allowing visitors to explore the city’s natural beauty at their leisure. Some popular options include:

  • Mt. Adachi Nature Park: A scenic spot with a popular hiking trail that rewards visitors with stunning views at the summit.
  • Kitakyushu Cycling Road: A 37-kilometer long cycling road that is perfect for biking enthusiasts and offers stunning scenic views
  • Kawachi Fujien: A serene location that has a network of trails that allows visitors to explore the beautiful wisteria gardens.

Best Things to Do in Kitakyushu: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to food tours, there is something for everyone in Kitakyushu. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Take a tea ceremony class and learn about the art of tea-making.
  • Visit Motoyasu no yu Onsen and soak in the natural hot springs while taking in the beautiful scenery around you.
  • Take a food tour of Mojiko Retro, where you can sample local delicacies and learn about the history of the town.
  • Explore the city on a rental bike and take in the stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kitakyushu

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Kitakyushu:

Day Activity
1 Visit Kokura Castle and the surrounding park, and take a guided tour of the castle.
2 Explore the Mojiko Retro district and take in the charm of this historic town.
3 Take a hike up Mount Sarakura, and enjoy the panoramic views of the city from the summit.
4 Visit Kawachi Fuji Garden and marvel at the stunning wisteria gardens and tunnels.
5 Go on a food tour and sample local delicacies in the Mojiko Retro district.
6 Take a bike ride along the Kitakyushu Cycling Road and enjoy the beautiful scenery along the way.
7 Visit the Kitakyushu Museum of Natural History & Human History and learn about the rich history of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu has a diverse food and drink scene that boasts a variety of local specialties and regional dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokura beef: Savor the rich flavors of “Japanese Wagyu” or “Japanese Black Cattle” breed known for its delicious marbling.
  • Ika-no-tataki: A dish made from fresh squid that is lightly grilled, sliced, and seasoned with soy sauce and wasabi.
  • Mentaiko: A popular dish made from marinated pollock roe that is enjoyed as a spicy snack or a condiment.

Local Beverages

  • Fukuoka’s Craft Beers: Try the locally brewed beers that have gained popularity all over Japan - they are available in many bars and restaurants.
  • Tsuharu Green Tea: One of the tea variations from Fukuoka prefecture, Tsuharu Green Tea, is known for its smooth taste and fragrance.

Where to Stay in Kitakyushu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kitakyushu offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different tastes and budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Nest Hotel Kokura: A centrally located hotel near Kokura station that offers comfortable rooms at reasonable prices.
  • Riverside Hotel Kokura: A luxurious hotel overlooking the river that offers high-end amenities and services.
  • Koyokan Wan Sabi: A traditional Japanese-style guesthouse that offers tatami rooms, onsen, and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Kitakyushu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kitakyushu is known for its local products and souvenirs, ranging from traditional crafts to contemporary items. Here are some recommendations:

  • Kokura Wagyu Beef: A popular souvenir that can be purchased in various food and souvenir shops in the city.
  • Japanese Whiskey: Some of the country’s finest whiskey brands are produced in Kitakyushu, and they are readily available to purchase.
  • Kokura-yaki Pottery: A traditional craft that has been produced in the region for more than 300 years, known for its sophisticated and elegant designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu boasts a rich cultural heritage that reflects the traditional and modern aspects of Japan. Some unique customs and festivals to experience in Kitakyushu include:

  • Ohara Festival: A lively festival that takes place in early July, featuring parades and street performances.
  • Nagoshi no Harae: An annual festival that takes place in August, celebrating the arrival of autumn and featuring various rituals for purifying the soul.
  • Kyushu traditional performing arts festival: An event held every August that showcases a range of traditional performing arts, including dance and music performances.

Best Time to Visit Kitakyushu

The best time to visit Kitakyushu is in the spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. During the spring season, visitors can also enjoy the stunning cherry blossoms, while in the autumn the colorful autumn leaves provide a picturesque beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kitakyushu

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering a house or a traditional establishment.
  • Say “Itadakimasu” before starting a meal, and “Gochisousama deshita” after finishing.
  • Be punctual and respectful of people’s time and schedules.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking in public.
  • Wear shoes on tatami mats.
  • Talk on the phone while riding on public transport.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate, but standard precautions should be taken.

  • Take care of your belongings when in crowded areas.
  • Use designated pedestrian crossings when crossing the street.
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency numbers and procedures.

Health in Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu has good healthcare facilities, including modern hospitals and clinics.

  • Take precautions against mosquito bites during the summer months.
  • Drink enough water during outdoor activities to avoid dehydration.
  • Consult a doctor before traveling if you have any underlying medical conditions.

Getting to Kitakyushu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kitakyushu is well connected to other cities in Japan, making it easy to access. There are several transportation options available to reach Kitakyushu, including:

Airports Near Kitakyushu

  • Kitakyushu Airport: The closest airport that offers regular flights from Tokyo and other major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Kitakyushu

  • Kokura Bus Center: The primary bus terminal that offers regular bus services to other cities in Kyushu and Honshu regions.

Other Options

  • Bullet Train: The JR Sanyo Shinkansen runs between Shin-Osaka and Hakata Stations, with a stop at Kokura Station in Kitakyushu.

How to Get Around in Kitakyushu

Kitakyushu has a reliable and efficient network of public transport, which includes buses and trains. Taxis are also readily available throughout the city, but fares can be expensive. It is also recommended to rent a bike to explore the city, as some attractions are pedestrian-friendly.

Where to Go Next from Kitakyushu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after visiting Kitakyushu. Some popular options include:

  • Fukuoka: The capital city of Kyushu, known for its beautiful cityscape, famous temples, and shrines.
  • Beppu: Located in Oita Prefecture, it is famous for its natural hot springs and beautiful scenery.
  • Nagasaki: Known for its many attractions including beautiful temples, gardens, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Kitakyushu is a fantastic destination that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and adventure. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and delicious cuisine it is sure to become a memorable experience for all visitors. Plan your trip using this guide and explore all that this beautiful city has to offer.
