Introduction to Kurume

Kurume is a city located in the Fukuoka Prefecture of Kyushu, Japan. Known for its thriving textile industry and beautiful natural landscapes, Kurume offers a unique blend of city amenities and small-town charm. The city is home to many cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and bike trails that provide visitors with a wide range of experiences to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Kurume

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kurume Castle

Arguably the most famous landmark in Kurume, Kurume Castle was established in the 17th century and is now a historical museum. The castle’s beautiful architecture and gorgeous gardens make it a must-visit for anyone interested in Japanese history and culture.

Kokura Gion Daiko Festival

One of the most extraordinary festivals in Japan, Kokura Gion Daiko is a stunning display of drumming from all over the country. The event, held in late July, attracts thousands of visitors who come to watch the skillful performers in a parade that weaves through the streets of the city.

Ishibashi Museum of Art

Three beautiful gardens surround this museum, which displays traditional Japanese art and ceramics, as well as traveling exhibitions.

Shofu-en Garden

This garden is a prefecture-designated scenic spot, set up in honor of philosopher Shigenori Soejima. The garden is a peaceful place to take in the natural beauty of Japan while taking in the meditative Japanese Tea Ceremony.

Natural Attractions of Kurume: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Natural Forest Kurume

One of the best places to experience nature in Kurume is Natural Forest Kurume. This massive 1,000-acre park has many walking trails, play areas for children, an outdoor recreation area with BBQ facilities, and natural hot springs.

Kasuya Gyoen Park

This park has a pond with a walking path, a Japanese garden, a rose garden and a Western-style garden. There are also open spaces to relax, and a big playground of over 100 meters wide. Enjoy the seasonal samplings of chrysanthemums, roses and hydrangeas.

Forest Therapy Road

The Forest Therapy Road is a peaceful way to explore the natural beauty of Kurume with several pleasant, shaded walking trails through the trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kurume

Yanagawa River Cycling Road

The Yanagawa River Cycling Road is a popular destination for both tourists and locals. The bike trail is over 60 km long, making it perfect for an all-day adventure. Visitors can take in some incredible views of the riverbank and enjoy the serene countryside scenery.

Yamamiyame Cycling Road

The Yamamiyame Cycling Road is a 50 km road that connects Kurume and Yamaguchi prefectures. It is surrounded by the stunning natural beauty of the region and is a popular destination for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Kurume: Activities and Tours

Shopping at Kurume’s Hachiman-dori Street

If you are looking for some local souvenirs, consider shopping at Hachiman-dori, a street that is lined with locally owned shops selling arts, crafts, and traditional Japanese clothing.

Hot spring experience at Hotaru no Yu

The Hotaru no Yu is a natural hot spring with an indoor and outdoor area that provides a relaxing, refreshing experience. A place to relax after a long day of sightseeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Kurume

Must-Try Dishes

Mizutaki

A local specialty in Kurume that consists of boiling chicken with vegetables and tofu then dipping them in a ponzu sauce.

Tonkotsu Ramen

Kurume is famous for its Tonkotsu Ramen, a variation of noodle soup made with pork bones to give it its unique flavor.

Umazura Soba

This local noodle dish is made with straight-cut soba noodles, a meat porridge sauce, bean sprouts, sliced beef, and onions.

Local Beverages

Mugi Shochu

Made from barley, Mugi Shochu is a popular drink in Kurume, especially during the chilly winter months.

Kurume sake

Kurume sake is brewed using the underground water from the Chikugo river that flows into the Kurume scene, making for a pure and original local sake that can only be tasted here.

  • Japanese pottery and ceramics
  • Kurume Kasuri

Local Culture and Customs of Kurume

Kurume is known for its friendly and hospitable locals, who take pride in their city’s unique culture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs, such as removing their shoes before entering homes or temples and showing appreciation towards the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Kurume

The best time to visit Kurume is during the autumn months, from September to November, when the weather is mild, and the autumn leaves bring beautiful hues of yellow, orange, and red to the city’s natural landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kurume

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take the time to explore the city’s impressive natural landmarks
  • Learn about the city’s unique history and culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or make loud noises in public areas
  • Touch any sacred artifacts or belongings
  • Ignore the advice of local park rangers or tour guides

Safety and Health

Safety in Kurume

Kurume is a relatively tranquil and safe city. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around day and night. However, as with any city, it’s essential to stay alert in crowded places and avoid carrying large amounts of cash to avoid opportunistic crime.

Health in Kurume

Kurume has a reliable healthcare system, and hospitals in the city are fully equipped to handle most medical issues.

Getting to Kurume: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kurume

  • Fukuoka Airport
  • Kumamoto Airport

Both Fukuoka and Kumamoto airports offer convenient access to Kurume via train or bus.

Bus Stations Near Kurume

  • Kurume Bus Center
  • Yoshizuka Bus Terminal

Kurume has two main bus stations where visitors can take local or long-distance buses to get around the city or other nearby destinations.

Other Options

Many visitors opt to travel to Kurume by train, with many stations providing easy access to the city and its many attractions.

How to Get Around in Kurume

Some of the most popular ways to get around Kurume include:

  • Walking
  • Biking
  • Public transportation

Kurume has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, and trains that can take you anywhere in the city and beyond.

Where to Go Next from Kurume: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations and excursions to consider after your visit to Kurume include:

  • Yanagawa Riverboat Tour

Conclusion

Kurume is a city that blends tradition and modernity, with something to offer every visitor. Its natural landscapes, cultural landmarks, and unique local cuisine make it a must-visit destination in Japan. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Kurume with ease and confidence, knowing they will enjoy an unforgettable experience that will stay with them for a lifetime.
