Introduction to Fukuoka (Prefecture)

Fukuoka (Prefecture) is located on the northern shore of Kyushu, Japan’s southernmost main island. It is a vibrant, modern city, renowned for its technological advancements, historical landmarks, and delectable cuisine. It boasts an array of scenic sights that will appeal to nature lovers, and exotic cultural sites for lovers of history.

Best Time to Visit Fukuoka (Prefecture)

The best times to visit Fukuoka (Prefecture) are spring (March through May) and autumn (September through November). During these times, the climate is mild, and the city is at its most picturesque, with blooming cherry blossoms in spring, and colorful foliage in autumn.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fukuoka (Prefecture)

Do:

  1. Try local dishes
  2. Respect cultural customs and traditions
  3. Appreciate the beauty of nature
  4. Wear comfortable footwear for walking and hiking
  5. Explore Fukuoka’s scenic areas on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places
  2. Ignore local customs and traditions
  3. Damage or remove historical artifacts
  4. Take pictures of people without their permission
  5. Smoke in non-smoking areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Fukuoka (Prefecture)

Fukuoka (Prefecture) is generally safe for visitors, with low crime rates. However, travelers should be vigilant when using public transport or crowded areas, especially in the evenings.

Health in Fukuoka (Prefecture)

The standard of healthcare in Fukuoka (Prefecture) is high, with many hospitals and clinics available. Visitors should carry their medical insurance details with them at all times.
