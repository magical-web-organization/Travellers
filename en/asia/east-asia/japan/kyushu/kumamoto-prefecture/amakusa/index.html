The Ultimate Guide to Amakusa, Japan

Situated in the Kumamoto Prefecture of Japan’s Kyushu region, Amakusa is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by tourists. The archipelago is made up of 120 small islands, of which only a handful are inhabited. Amakusa is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture.

Introduction to Amakusa

Amakusa is a scenic and tranquil destination that attracts tourists who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of Japan’s larger cities. Its rich history dates back to the 16th century when Portuguese missionaries first arrived in Japan. The islands were also the site of a rebellion by Japanese Christians in the 17th century.

Today, Amakusa is home to a small population of around 100,000 residents who are proud of their unique culture and heritage. Visitors can explore scenic vistas, pristine beaches, and quaint fishing villages. The islands’ natural beauty has also attracted many outdoor enthusiasts to come for the hiking, biking, and water sports.

List of Places to Visit in Amakusa

Amakusa is a small archipelago, but it has a wealth of natural and cultural attractions that are worth exploring. Some of the top destinations to visit include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Amakusa Christian Museum: This museum showcases the history and legacy of Christianity in Japan, including the Amakusa Rebellion.
  • Sakitsu Village: This historic village was one of the first places where Catholicism was introduced in Japan.
  • Hondo Castle Ruins: This castle was once a stronghold of the Amakusa Rebellion and is now a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Amakusa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Shimojoshi: This peak offers stunning views of the surrounding islands and is a popular spot for hiking and camping.
  • Tsuji Beach: This picturesque beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and is perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Oyano Dam: This dam offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and is popular for fishing and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amakusa

Amakusa is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Some of the top hiking and biking trails in Amakusa include:

  • Amakusa Shichiraizaki Trail: This 22-kilometer trail offers stunning views of the coast and is known for its wildflowers and scenic landscapes.
  • Tsukushi-Hayatsue Trail: This 10-kilometer trail offers panoramic views of the ocean and the surrounding mountains and is perfect for hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Amakusa: Activities and Tours

Amakusa offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Some of the best things to do in Amakusa include:

  • Island Hopping: Take a ferry or boat tour to explore the different islands of Amakusa and experience the unique culture and landscape of each one.
  • Dolphin Watching: Take a tour to see the playful dolphins that call the waters around Amakusa home.
  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the crystal-clear waters around Amakusa on a sea kayaking tour and see some of the stunning landscapes up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amakusa

A week-long trip to Amakusa will give visitors ample time to explore the islands’ natural and cultural attractions. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore the Historical Sites of Amakusa

  • Visit the Amakusa Christian Museum, Sakitsu Village, and Hondo Castle Ruins.
  • Explore the historic town of Reihoku, which was once a center of Christianity in Japan.

Day 3-4: Enjoy the Natural Beauty of Amakusa

  • Visit Mount Shimojoshi and Tsuji Beach for hiking, swimming, and sunbathing.
  • Take a boat trip to Goshoura Island to see the unique rock formations and stunning sea views.

Day 5-6: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hike the Amakusa Shichiraizaki Trail or the Tsukushi-Hayatsue Trail.
  • Take a sea kayaking tour to explore the stunning coasts of Amakusa.

Day 7: Relax and Unwind

  • Spend a day relaxing on the beach, soaking up the sun, and enjoying the local cuisine.
  • Check out some of the local shops and markets to buy souvenirs and learn more about the culture of Amakusa.

Food and Drink Scene of Amakusa

Amakusa is known for its fresh seafood and unique cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ruri Saba: Horse mackerel marinated in vinegar and soy sauce.
  • Kamishime Squid: Grilled squid with a sweet and savory sauce.
  • Cucumber and Shijimi Clam Salad: A refreshing salad made with fresh cucumbers and clams.

Local Beverages

  • Awabi Shochu: This local spirit is made from the abalone shell and has a smooth, mellow flavor.
  • Amakusa Umeshu: This plum wine is made using local plums and has a sweet and tangy flavor.

Where to Stay in Amakusa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amakusa has a variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay in Amakusa include:

  • Amakusa Prince Hotel: A luxurious hotel with stunning sea views and premium amenities.
  • Amakusa Tenku no Fugaku: A boutique hotel that offers stunning views of Mount Shimojoshi and the surrounding landscape.
  • Guesthouse Amakusa Santopia: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Amakusa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amakusa has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can buy a variety of souvenirs and local products to remember their trip. Some of the top items to buy include:

  • Amakusa Pottery: Unique ceramics that are colorful and intricate in their design.
  • Shimabara Hana Candle: Handcrafted candles that are made using local materials and natural dyes.
  • Abalone Shell Accessories: Jewelry and other accessories made using abalone shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Amakusa

Amakusa has a unique culture and heritage that is deeply rooted in Christianity and Japanese traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and abide by the following:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and temples.
  • Remove shoes when entering a home or traditional Japanese inn (ryokan).
  • Bow when greeting people, especially elders and those in positions of authority.

Best Time to Visit Amakusa

The best time to visit Amakusa is from April to November when the weather is mild and sunny. The peak tourist season is from July to August when many Japanese tourists flock to the islands for water sports and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amakusa

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s home or temple.
  • Try the fresh seafood and local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or temples.
  • Litter or pollute the beaches and natural areas.
  • Ignore social etiquette and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amakusa

Amakusa is a safe destination for visitors, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean, as the currents can be strong.
  • Watch out for slippery surfaces when hiking and biking.
  • Follow all safety instructions when participating in water sports.

Health in Amakusa

Medical facilities are available in Amakusa, but visitors should bring any necessary medication and follow the following guidelines:

  • Stay hydrated when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and other insect-borne illnesses.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to Amakusa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amakusa is accessible by air, train, bus, and car. Visitors can choose from the following transportation options:

Airports Near Amakusa

  • Kumamoto Airport: The closest airport to Amakusa, located about 70 kilometers away.
  • Fukuoka Airport: Another option, located about 180 kilometers from Amakusa.

Bus Stations Near Amakusa

  • Kumamoto Bus Terminal: Buses run from Kumamoto to Amakusa regularly.
  • Shin-Tosu Station: Buses run from this station to Amakusa a few times a day.

Other Options

  • Train: Visitors can take the Kyushu Shinkansen from Hakata Station in Fukuoka to Kumamoto, and then take a local train to Hondo Station in Amakusa.
  • Car: Rental cars are available at the airports and train stations, and visitors can take the Kyushu Expressway to reach Amakusa.

How to Get Around in Amakusa

Visitors can get around Amakusa by rental car, bus, or bicycle. Rental cars are the most convenient option for exploring the islands’ scenic routes, but buses also offer a reliable and affordable way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Amakusa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Amakusa is a great starting point for exploring the rest of Kyushu and the surrounding islands. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Mount Aso: One of the largest active volcanoes in the world, located in the Kumamoto Prefecture.
  • Nagasaki: A historic city that is known for its role in World War II and its unique cultural heritage.
  • Gunkanjima: Also known as Battleship Island, this abandoned mining island is a popular tourist attraction.

Conclusion

Amakusa is a hidden gem that is perfect for travelers looking to get off the beaten path and experience Japan’s natural beauty and unique culture. Visitors can explore scenic vistas, pristine beaches, and quaint fishing villages, and have a truly unforgettable trip.
