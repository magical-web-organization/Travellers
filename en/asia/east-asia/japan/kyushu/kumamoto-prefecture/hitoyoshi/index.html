Introduction to Hitoyoshi

Nestled in the Kumamoto prefecture in the southern Japanese island of Kyushu, Hitoyoshi offers a glimpse into traditional Japan. This picturesque town is well-known for cultural and natural attractions, and outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs will find plenty of things to do. It is an ideal destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the big cities and immerse themselves in Japanese history and culture. In this travel guide, we will share with you the best things to do, local food, where to stay and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Hitoyoshi

If you are visiting Hitoyoshi, then you must not miss these amazing attractions that offer a glimpse into Japanese history and culture.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hitoyoshi Castle: One of the most significant historical sites in Hitoyoshi, the castle offers an opportunity to witness the beauty of a medieval castle.
  • Aoi Aso Shrine: Aoi Aso Shrine is a must-visit, as it connects the spiritual and historical aspects of the town.
  • Hitoyoshi City Museum: This museum contains invaluable information about the history of the town, and it gives an excellent overview of Kumamoto prefecture’s folk craft culture and nature.

Natural Attractions of Hitoyoshi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kuma River Rafting: The Kuma River is one of the best places to raft in Japan, offering thrilling rapids and white water rafting experiences.
  • Mount Aso: The mountain is a unique site of natural beauty, and visitors can see the volcano’s breath taking view and experience breathtaking walks.
  • Hirayama Hot Spring: You can relax your body and soul at this hot spring place, surrounded by beautiful natural scenery that will leave you in awe.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hitoyoshi

Hiking and biking are the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Hitoyoshi. Here are some of the top hiking and bike routes to explore:

  • Hitoyoshi Riverside Path: This paved path follows the Kuma River, passing through scenic landmarks such as the 12 bridges.
  • Miyama Route: This 10 kilometres trail takes you through the beautiful natural landscape of Hitoyoshi, ending at the top of a hill that offers a spectacular panoramic view of the town.
  • Tsuetate Gorge Course: This trail is one of the most scenic trails in Japan and is an excellent place for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Hitoyoshi: Activities and Tours

Here are some other activities and tours worth trying out in Hitoyoshi:

  • Explore the Local Market: Taking a tour or heading on a solo exploration tour of the local market and trying some local food in Hitoyoshi is a good starting point to get familiar with the local cuisine and culture.
  • Take a Yoga Class: Beyond hiking and biking, visitors can stretch their muscles and experience yogic postures under the guidance of a professional yoga instructor.
  • Fishing: Visitors can also undertake fly fishing in the Kuma River and catch some sweetfish and trout, that are common to the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Hitoyoshi

Must-Try Dishes

Hitoyoshi’s cuisine is unique and diverse, representing the best of local food. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • ** Hitoyoshi Ramen **: This dish is a must-try for any visitors. This dish is a unique twist on the traditional Japanese Ramen, it is made with soy sauce broth and is topped with local vegetables, gyoza dumplings and juicy fried chicken.
  • ** Kumagawa Beef **: Hitoyoshi is known for locally bred Kumagawa beef, which is prized for its high-quality meat and flavourful fat. Patients can try it in a BBQ restaurant or in a hot pot.
  • Kori Tofu: A type of tofu, made using water from Mount Aso has a unique flavour and texture.

Local Beverages

Hitoyoshi is also famous for its unique local drinks. Be sure to try the following drinks:

  • Hitoyoshi Sake: This Sake is known for its high quality and is brewed using clean water from Mount Aso.
  • Shochu: A distilled beverage made from rice or sweet potatoes that is popular in the Kyushu region.
  • Japanese Green Tea: Hitoyoshi has a long history of tea cultivation, and it’s green tea is known for its delicate flavor and fragrance.

Where to Stay in Hitoyoshi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hitoyoshi offers a variety of accommodation options from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some top recommendations for where to stay in Hitoyoshi:

  • Hitoyoshi Onsen Ryokan: A traditional Japanese hotel that offers mineral springs and traditional Japanese-style rooms.
  • Hotel New Kawachiya Hitoyoshi: This hotel is a great option for visitors who want to stay at the heart of the town but not too far from the nature and scenic landscape..
  • Mizu no Uta Guest House: This guesthouse will allow you to experience a different kind of stay in Japan. The guesthouse is within walking distance to the Hitoyoshi train station and is perfect for those on a budget.

What to Buy in Hitoyoshi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home from Hitoyoshi, and here are some best options:

  • Tenjinmon Banpeiyu: Known as the “Citrus Grand Slam” of Japan, the citruses grown in Hitoyoshi are some of the best in the country.
  • Hitoyoshi Yaki: These are traditional, handcrafted pottery items that feature a unique design and are perfect for souvenirs.
  • Hitoyoshi Wagyu: Visiting Kumamoto prefecture won’t be complete without bringing home its world-renowned Wagyu beef.

Local Culture and Customs of Hitoyoshi

Visitors to Hitoyoshi are expected to abide by the customs and traditions of the town. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be courteous: Politeness and respect is highly valued in the Japanese culture so always be mindful of your language, tone of voice and body language.
  • Remove your shoes: It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or some other establishments, such as restaurants and temples.
  • Tipping is not necessary: Tipping in Japan is not common and may even be seen as an offence so avoid doing it.

Best Time to Visit Hitoyoshi

The best time to visit Hitoyoshi is during the autumn season (September to November), when the weather is mild, and the trees are turning golden, adding a natural radiance to the landscape. Spring (March to May) is also an excellent time to visit when the numerous cherry blossoms are in full bloom, painting the town with a vibrant pink hue.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hitoyoshi

Do:

  • Eat local delicacies: Explore the town’s intriguing food scene and try some of the unique cuisines.
  • Experience the local culture: Visit local temples and museums to learn more about the town’s rich culture and history.
  • Wear comfortable shoes: Lots of walking and hiking are part of the fun, so wear comfortable shoes.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud: Loud speaking or yelling can be considered rude in Japanese culture. Keep it calm and respectful.
  • Eat while walking: Eating in public places is frowned upon, so it’s best to find a designated area before enjoying your meal.
  • Refuse hospitality: If a local invites you for a drink, dinner or to visit their home, don’t refuse it. Being hospitable is an essential part of Japanese culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hitoyoshi

Hitoyoshi is considered a safe place to visit, and crime rates are minimal. However, visitors should keep an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded areas such as train stations and markets.

Health in Hitoyoshi

Visitors should be aware of Japan’s COVID-19 guidelines and safety protocols that are unique to towns and prefectures. For those with pre-existing medical conditions or allergies, it’s essential to bring adequate medication and inform medical practitioners of any relevant medical history.

Getting to Hitoyoshi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hitoyoshi is accessible through various transport options, and below are three of the most common ways to get to the town.

Airports Near Hitoyoshi

The nearest airport to Hitoyoshi is Kumamoto airport, located about 72 km away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Hitoyoshi

Buses run frequently between Kumamoto and Hitoyoshi, and it takes around 2 hours from Kumamoto to reach Hitoyoshi by bus.

Other Options

Hitoyoshi is situated on the southern part of Kyushu, which is well-known for its train network. Hitoyoshi also has a train station and visitors can take a train from Kumamoto station to reach the town.

How to Get Around in Hitoyoshi

The best way to move around in Hitoyoshi is on foot or on a bike. For places that are slightly out of town, buses and taxis are available at the train station.

Where to Go Next from Hitoyoshi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Due to Hitoyoshi’s central location, it acts as an excellent base point for visitors who want to explore other nearby destinations and excursions in the Kumamoto prefecture after their visit. Here are a few best places to visit after Hitoyoshi:

  • Kumamoto City: The capital city of Kumamoto, home to the famed Kumamoto Castle.
  • Mount Aso: The largest active volcano in Japan.
  • Yufuin: A hot spring resort town known for its scenic views and unique local crafts.

Conclusion

Hitoyoshi offers visitors an authentic Japanese cultural experience, combining nature and local attractions. Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Hitoyoshi is a destination worth visiting. This town is full of hidden gems waiting to be explored, and we hope that by reading through this Hitoyoshi travel guide, you can have a fulfilling and memorable visit, creating a once in a lifetime experience.
