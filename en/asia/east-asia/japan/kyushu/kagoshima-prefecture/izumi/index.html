Introduction to Izumi

Nestled in the scenic hills of Kagoshima prefecture in Kyushu, Izumi is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its unique blend of cultural and natural attractions, this small town offers travelers an authentic taste of Japan that is hard to find elsewhere.

Izumi is known for its hot springs, hiking trails, and scenic views, as well as its many historical landmarks and cultural festivals that date back over a thousand years. Whether you are looking to immerse yourself in nature, or get a glimpse into the rich history and traditions of the region, Izumi has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Izumi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When in Izumi, you can explore its unique culture and history by visiting the following places:

  • Izumi City Historical Museum: Learn about the city’s history and culture through its art, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • Izumi Shrine: One of the oldest and most revered shrines in Japan, this site is a popular pilgrimage destination for those seeking blessings and good luck.
  • Izumi Samurai House: Experience the lifestyle of Izumi’s samurai- the traditional Japanese warriors who served the ruling class.
  • Izumi Fumoto Samurai Castle: This reconstructed castle showcases the architectural style of the Edo period (1603-1867), a crucial period in Japan’s history.
  • Izumi Green Tourism Farm: This organic farm offers various activities, including vegetable harvesting, DIY pizza-making, and feeding the farm animals.

Natural Attractions of Izumi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Izumi has plenty for you to explore, including:

  • Izumi Crane Observation Center: Known for the rare Japanese Crane- an endangered bird, this center is a popular destination for birdwatchers.
  • Mt. Izumi-Katsuragi: An active volcano- one of the “New 100 Famous Japanese Mountains,” offering picturesque views of the town.
  • Izumi no Mori Natural Park: Peaceful surroundings, tranquil ponds, and hiking areas with river views to relish.
  • Izumi Hot Springs: With rich, mineral-infused water and multiple hot spring spots, tourists can soak in after a long day of sightseeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Izumi

Izumi offers an outstanding experience of hiking and biking routes that’s perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Hike to Mount Izumi-Katsuragi: Take in breathtaking views of the countryside and mountains on this challenging hike to Mt. Izumi-Katsuragi.
  • Izumi Cycling Road: A level cycling path extending for about 13 kilometres from Izumi-Matsubara to Fumoto-Midai, taking in the beautiful scenery of Izumi Bay.
  • Takahae Promenade: A well-maintained walking path following the shoreline with fantastic views of the surrounding mountains that can be accessed from Takahae Port.

Best Things to Do in Izumi: Activities and Tours

Here are some activities that you can do and tours you must join in Izumi:

  • Shirokita Park: A unique place that’s perfect for families, adventure-seekers, and sports enthusiasts looking for outdoor activities, including water sporting events, hiking, and a rope course adventure park.
  • Izumi Cycling Tour: A dedicated cycling tour company that provides a fantastic experience of exploring Izumi within a limited touring time frame.
  • Izumi Crane Observation Tour: Known for the rare Japanese Crane, several companies offer observation tours with off-the-beaten-path hiking routes and picturesque views.
  • Izumi Samurai Experience: Experience the true Samurai legacy that includes Samurai dress-up, weapons handling, and Samurai swordsmanship training.

Week-Long Itinerary for Izumi

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Izumi:

Food and Drink Scene of Izumi

No trip to Izumi is complete without tasting its delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Izumi Eel: Sweet, juicy, and delicate, Izumi Eel is a local delicacy that’s simply divine.
  • Somen Noodles: Cold wheat noodles served with ice, requiring a unique method of eating that involves slurping.
  • Izumi Beef: This high-quality beef is famous for its marbling and flavor that melts in your mouth.
  • Izumi Tofu: Made from locally produced soybean, Izumi Tofu is perfect for vegetarians looking for a healthy, delicious option.

Local Beverages

  • Shochu: A distilled beverage that’s popular in Kyushu, typically made from rice, barley, or sweet potatoes.
  • Miyazaki Beer: Locally made beer that showcases the brewing art of the region’s pure natural water.
  • Green Tea: With tea plantations located near Izumi, you’ll find various green tea products, including tea leaves, tea sets, and tea-flavored drinks.

Where to Stay in Izumi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Izumi offers a variety of accommodations suitable for all budgets, including:

  • Ryokans: Traditional Japanese-style inns that offer luxury amenities, including hot spring baths and exquisite dinners in a tatami-matted room.
  • Hotels: Izumi has several international hotels, offering Western-style accommodation categories, including deluxe rooms with scenic views.
  • Guesthouses: These budget-friendly stays are perfect for travelers looking for a clean stay and a chance to experience local culture.

What to Buy in Izumi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the top local souvenirs and products to bring home with you:

  • Izumi’s Local Sake: Made from rice and Izumi’s pure mineral water, this delicious rice wine is perfect as a gift.
  • Higo-Musubi: A traditional Japanese tie used for decorating sake bottles, designed by samurai during the Edo period.
  • Izumi-yaki: Pottery that is exclusive to Izumi and is characterized by traditional Shino-style crackling and natural color glaze.

Local Culture and Customs of Izumi

Izumi’s unique characteristics, festive events, and historical landmarks provide an insight into its culture and customs. Some exciting facts about Izumi’s culture and customs include:

  • Izumi Tsurugi Jinku: A traditional festival featuring the “Tsugura Dancers,” dressed in a special outfit called Keppaku.
  • Izumi Eel Festival: A food festival dedicated to the local delicacy, Izumi Eel, where vendors sell various eel dishes, sweet eel rice crackers, and other eel products.

Best Time to Visit Izumi

The best time to visit Izumi is during spring, summer, and autumn, from April to November. During this time, the town is blessed with mild weather, perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Additionally, visitors in April and May can enjoy the spectacular sakura (cherry blossom) season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Izumi

Do:

  • Respect Japanese cultural and religious practices.
  • Try traditional Izumi delicacies, including eel dishes and Izumi beef.
  • Take off shoes or wear slippers in temples, shrines, and Japanese-style inns.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in the streets or other public places.
  • Tip at restaurants or in other local businesses.
  • Speak loudly or cause disturbances in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Izumi

Izumi is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors to the hot springs and other natural attractions must follow safety protocols to avoid accidents or incidents.

Health in Izumi

Japan has excellent health care facilities, and Izumi has hospitals and clinics that cater to all types of medical emergencies. In addition, visitors should ensure they have medical insurance before traveling to Japan.

Getting to Izumi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Izumi

  • Kagoshima Airport, located about 50 miles or 80 km from Izumi, offers numerous domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Izumi

  • Izumi Bus Terminal offers local bus services that connect Izumi to nearby towns and cities within Kagoshima prefecture.

Other Options

  • Train: Kagoshima Chuo Station is the closest rail station, which is located 60 km north of Izumi.
  • Taxi: Taxis are an expensive transportation option, though numbers of taxi companies offer various touring options.

How to Get Around in Izumi

Public transportation is limited in Izumi. The best way to get around is by renting a car or cycling, as the city is small enough to explore within a day.

Where to Go Next from Izumi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some suggestions for where to visit next after exploring Izumi:

  • Kagoshima city (70 km northwest): Explore history, modern culture, and natural attractions, including the Sakurajima (active volcano), Sengan-en Garden, and Satsuma Kiriko Glassware shops.
  • Kirishima (60 km northeast): Home to numerous spectacular hot springs, the Kirishima Mountains, and Kirishima-jingu Temple.

Conclusion

Izumi offers a unique and distinct travel experience that is a must-do for every traveler visiting Kyushu, Japan. From exploring its dynamic culture, hiking trails with breathtaking views, indulging in delicious food and drinks, to experiencing a slice of Samurai history, Izumi is an ultimate destination for an unforgettable vacation.
