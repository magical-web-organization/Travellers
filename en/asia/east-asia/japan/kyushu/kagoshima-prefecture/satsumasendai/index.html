Introduction to Satsumasendai

Satsumasendai is located in Kagoshima prefecture in Kyushu, Japan. The city is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. Satsumasendai was once known as Satsuma, the birthplace of the Shimazu clan, a prominent political family during the Edo period. Today, Satsumasendai boasts a vibrant culture and a wide range of attractions that cater to all interests.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the top places to visit in Satsumasendai, the best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Satsumasendai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shimazu Residence Kagoshima Satsuma Kiriko Glass Museum: This museum is dedicated to Satsuma Kiriko, a style of glassware that originated in Satsumasendai during the Edo period. Visitors can admire intricate glasswork and make their own pieces in a glassblowing workshop.

  • Iso Residence: This traditional Japanese house was once the residence of the Iso family, feudal lords of Satsuma. The house has a beautiful garden and is the perfect place to learn about the history and culture of Satsumasendai.

  • Shoko Shuseikan Museum: This museum showcases the history of Satsumasendai and the Shimazu clan through artifacts and exhibits. The museum is adjacent to the ruins of Kagoshima Castle, which was destroyed during the Satsuma Rebellion.

Natural Attractions of Satsumasendai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kirishima-Yaku National Park: This national park is located on the border of Kagoshima and Miyazaki prefectures and is home to a number of active volcanoes, hot springs, and hiking trails. Visitors can take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Sakura no Sato: This park is known for its stunning cherry blossom trees and is one of the best places to view cherry blossoms in Satsumasendai. The park also has walking paths and a small lake.

  • Yoshino River: The Yoshino River is one of the longest rivers in Japan and is known for its scenic views and opportunities for outdoor activities such as kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Satsumasendai

  • Kirishima hiking trail: This hiking trail takes visitors through the Kirishima-Yaku National Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests. The trail is approximately 8 kilometers long.

  • Satsumasendai cycling course: This cycling course takes visitors through Satsumasendai and offers views of the city’s historical and cultural landmarks. The course is approximately 25 kilometers long.

Best Things to Do in Satsumasendai: Activities and Tours

  • Kagoshima Sightseeing Tour: This tour takes visitors around Satsumasendai and Kagoshima prefecture and includes stops at cultural and historical landmarks such as Yakushima Island, Sengan-en Garden, and Chiran Samurai Residence.

  • Satsuma Kiriko Glassmaking Experience: Visitors can make their own Satsuma Kiriko glassware at the Shimazu Residence Kagoshima Satsuma Kiriko Glass Museum.

  • Yoshino River Rafting: Visitors can go on a guided rafting trip down the Yoshino River and take in the beautiful scenery of Satsumasendai.

Food and Drink Scene of Satsumasendai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Satsuma-age: This is a type of deep-fried fish cake made with minced fish and flour. It is a popular snack in Satsumasendai.

  • Kurozu: This is a type of black vinegar that is made using traditional methods in Kagoshima. It is often used as a condiment or marinade.

  • Satsuma-imo: This is a type of sweet potato that is cultivated in Satsumasendai. It is often used in desserts and is known for its soft and creamy texture.

Local Beverages

  • Shochu: This is a traditional Japanese spirit that is made from sweet potatoes, barley, or rice. The shochu produced in Satsumasendai is particularly renowned for its quality.

  • Kagoshima Tea: This is a type of green tea that is grown in the Kagoshima prefecture. It has a mild and sweet flavor and is often served in traditional tea ceremonies.

Where to Stay in Satsumasendai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sankan-nosho: This traditional Japanese inn is located near the Iso Residence and offers guests a traditional Japanese experience, including a tatami room and onsen hot spring baths.

  • Royal Hotel Satsuma Sendai: This hotel is located in the heart of Satsumasendai and offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a restaurant and spa.

  • Yutorelo Sendai: This modern guesthouse is located near the Yoshino River and offers guests a comfortable and affordable stay, including a range of outdoor activities such as rafting and hiking.

What to Buy in Satsumasendai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Satsuma-yaki: This is a type of pottery that has been produced in Satsumasendai since the 17th century. Visitors can purchase a range of Satsuma-yaki products, including teapots, vases, and plates.

  • Kurozu: Visitors can purchase bottles of black vinegar to take home as a souvenir.

  • Satsuma-imo: Visitors can purchase Satsuma-imo sweets and other foods to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Satsumasendai

Satsumasendai has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its traditional arts, crafts, and festivals. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including taking off shoes before entering traditional Japanese houses and following proper etiquette in public places.

Best Time to Visit Satsumasendai

The best time to visit Satsumasendai is during the spring (March - May) or fall (September - November) when the weather is mild and the scenery is beautiful. However, visitors can enjoy Satsumasendai’s attractions year-round.

Safety and Health

Safety in Satsumasendai

Satsumasendai is a safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Satsumasendai

Visitors should ensure they have proper health insurance and are up to date with their vaccinations before visiting Satsumasendai. In case of a medical emergency, visitors can go to the nearest hospital or clinic for treatment.

Getting to Satsumasendai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Satsumasendai

  • Kagoshima Airport: This airport is located approximately 45 kilometers from Satsumasendai and is the nearest major airport. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Satsumasendai.

  • Miyazaki Airport: This airport is located approximately 110 kilometers from Satsumasendai and is another option for visitors.

Bus Stations Near Satsumasendai

  • Kagoshima Chuo Station: This station is located in central Kagoshima and is the nearest major bus station. Visitors can take a bus from Kagoshima Chuo Station to Satsumasendai.

  • Satsumasendai Station: This station is located in the center of Satsumasendai and is a convenient starting point for visitors exploring the city.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Satsumasendai from other parts of Kagoshima prefecture.

How to Get Around in Satsumasendai

Visitors can get around Satsumasendai by bus, taxi, rental car, or bicycle. Buses are the most convenient and affordable option for most visitors, but taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Satsumasendai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yakushima Island: This beautiful island is known for its pristine forests, mountain trails, and hot springs. Visitors can take a ferry from Kagoshima to Yakushima Island.

  • Kagoshima City: This bustling city is known for its historic landmarks, hot springs, and outdoor activities.

  • Ibusuki: This coastal town is known for its sandy beaches and natural hot springs.

Conclusion

Satsumasendai offers visitors a unique and authentic Japanese experience, with a rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local culture, Satsumasendai has something for everyone.
