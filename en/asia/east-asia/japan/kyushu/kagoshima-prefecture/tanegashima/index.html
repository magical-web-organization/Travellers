Introduction to Tanegashima

Tanegashima, located in the Kagoshima prefecture of Japan, is a small island that offers a unique and breathtaking travel experience. The island is known for its rich history, amazing outdoor activities, friendly locals, and delicious food. Whether you are a history enthusiast or an adventure seeker, Tanegashima has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tanegashima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tanegashima Space Center: One of the most popular museums on the island, the Tanegashima Space Center is the largest space development and test site in Japan. The museum is an interesting place for both children and adults to visit and learn about the history of space exploration.

  2. Yakushima Island: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is an ancient forest that is famous for its diverse flora and fauna. It is home to some of the oldest trees in Japan, some of which date back more than 1,000 years.

  3. Chikura-ji Temple: This temple is a great place to explore the history and culture of Tanegashima. The temple is surrounded by beautiful gardens and features traditional Japanese architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tanegashima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Nagata Inakahama Beach: This is one of the most relaxing and beautiful beaches in Tanegashima. The beach features fantastic views and crystal clear waters, making it a great spot for swimming and sunbathing.

  2. Taniyama Observatory: This observatory is located on a hilltop and offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape. The observatory also has a planetarium where visitors can learn about the stars and other celestial bodies.

  3. Ambo Harbour: This harbour is a great spot for fishing and boating. Visitors can rent a boat and explore the waters around Tanegashima.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanegashima

  1. Araki Forest Road: This 14-kilometer road is an excellent hiking spot, offering beautiful views of the forest and mountains. The road is also popular among cyclists and offers some challenging routes.

  2. Nishinoomote Coastline: This picturesque coastal area is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. The area offers stunning views and is also home to some of the best seafood restaurants on the island.

Best Things to Do in Tanegashima: Activities and Tours

  1. Space Launch Viewing: Visitors can watch a space launch from the Tanegashima Space Center. It’s a fantastic experience to watch a rocket launch into space.

  2. Horse Riding: Horseback riding is a unique experience in Tanegashima. Visitors can explore the island’s forests and mountains on horseback.

  3. Dolphin Watching: Tanegashima is home to many dolphins. Visitors can take a boat tour to see these beautiful creatures up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tanegashima

Day 1: Visit the Tanegashima Space Center and learn about space exploration.

Day 2: Explore Yakushima Island and see the ancient forest.

Day 3: Visit Chikura-ji Temple and immerse yourself in the history and culture of Tanegashima.

Day 4: Relax on Nagata Inakahama Beach or take a boat tour to see dolphins.

Day 5: Go horseback riding in the Araki Forest Road.

Day 6: Hike the Nishinoomote Coastline and enjoy the beautiful views.

Day 7: Enjoy fresh seafood at one of the local restaurants or go fishing in Ambo Harbour.

Food and Drink Scene of Tanegashima

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kurobuta Pork: Tanegashima’s famous black pig that is known for its rich and tender meat.

  2. Kibinago Sashimi: A local specialty, Kibinago is a small silver fish that is served raw with soy sauce and grated ginger.

  3. Onsen Tamago: A traditional Japanese soft-boiled egg that is cooked in the hot springs of Tanegashima.

Local Beverages

  1. Shochu: A distilled spirit made from sweet potatoes, barley, or rice.

  2. Awamori: A distilled spirit made from rice that is aged in clay jars.

  3. Green Tea: Tanegashima is known for its high-quality green tea, which has a unique flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Tanegashima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Yakushima Pension Mugishirazu: A cozy and rustic guesthouse located in Yakushima Island with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.

  2. Jomon No Yado Manten: A fantastic hot spring resort that features traditional Japanese architecture and a peaceful atmosphere.

  3. APA Hotel Kagoshima Chuo-Ekimae: A budget-friendly hotel that provides comfortable rooms and amenities for its guests.

What to Buy in Tanegashima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kurobuta Pork: Tanegashima’s famous black pig is a great souvenir and also an excellent ingredient for cooking.

  2. Tanegashima Green Tea: The high-quality green tea of Tanegashima is a fantastic souvenir to bring home to your family and friends.

  3. Tanegashima Salt: Produced from the saltwater around Tanegashima, this natural salt is a perfect gift for food lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanegashima

  1. Tanegashima is home to some unique cultural festivals, including the Tanegashima Rocket Matsuri and the Tanegashima Minato Matsuri.

  2. The locals of Tanegashima are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and they take pride in their island’s culture and history.

Best Time to Visit Tanegashima

The best time to visit Tanegashima is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. The temperatures during these seasons are comfortable, and the island is less crowded than during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanegashima

Do:

  1. Practice common courtesy and respect the locals’ customs and traditions.

  2. Try the local food and beverages, which are unique to the island.

  3. Explore the island’s natural attractions and enjoy the outdoor activities available.

Don’t:

  1. Discriminate against the locals or other tourists.

  2. Litter or damage the island’s natural environment.

  3. Ignore safety guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanegashima

Tanegashima is a safe island with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Tanegashima

The island has several medical facilities that can provide basic medical care and treatment. However, visitors with pre-existing conditions should bring their medication and ensure they have proper health insurance coverage.

Getting to Tanegashima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tanegashima

Tanegashima Airport is a small airport that is accessible by domestic flights from either Kagoshima or Fukuoka.

Bus Stations Near Tanegashima

There are regular bus services that connect Tanegashima with Kagoshima and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take ferries from Kagoshima or other nearby cities to reach Tanegashima.

How to Get Around in Tanegashima

The island has regular bus services that connect the major attractions and cities on the island, making it easy for visitors to get around. Alternatively, visitors can rent bicycles or cars to access the less accessible areas of the island.

Where to Go Next from Tanegashima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kagoshima City: The city is famous for its hot springs, history, and beautiful gardens.

  2. Yakushima Island: Explore more of this beautiful island, which is known for its ancient forest and diverse flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Tanegashima is a unique and beautiful island that offers a fantastic travel experience for history enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike. With a rich history, stunning natural attractions, welcoming locals, and delicious food, Tanegashima is a must-visit destination for travelers to Japan. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide and explore all the island has to offer.
