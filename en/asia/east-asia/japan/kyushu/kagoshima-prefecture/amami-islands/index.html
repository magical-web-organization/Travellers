Introduction to Amami Islands

Amami Islands, located in the Kagoshima prefecture of Japan, is a group of small islands popular for their natural beauty, rich culture, and history. These islands are situated in the East China Sea, between the Kyushu and Okinawa islands. The islands are known for their tropical climate, white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and coral reefs teeming with marine life.

List of Places to Visit in Amami Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The islands have a rich history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that provide an insight into the island’s past. Some must-visit sites include:

  • Amami Museum: Learn about the culture, history, and nature of the Amami islands through exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Takegawara Onsen: A traditional Japanese hot spring that has been in use for over a hundred years.
  • Kasari Shrine: One of the oldest shrines on the islands, dedicated to the god of hunting.

Natural Attractions of Amami Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Amami Islands are a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Cape Ayamaru: A scenic viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the ocean and surrounding islands.
  • Tomori Imugya Marine Park: A protected marine park that offers snorkeling and diving opportunities.
  • Mount Yudake: The highest peak on Amami Oshima that offers a stunning view of the surrounding islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amami Islands

The Amami Islands are a great destination for hiking and cycling. Here are some scenic routes:

  • Yakushima Island Circuit: A 5-day trek that takes you through lush forests, waterfalls, and the island’s highest peak, Mount Miyanoura.
  • Amami Oshima Coastal Road: A 40km route that runs along the coast and offers scenic views of the ocean and surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Amami Islands: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural and cultural attractions, there are several activities and tours that you can enjoy on the islands:

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Visit the Tomori Imugya Marine Park and explore the underwater world.
  • Sea Kayaking: Enjoy the scenic views of the islands while paddling through the clear waters.
  • Whale Watching: Visit the islands during the winter months and go on a whale watching tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amami Islands

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most out of your trip to Amami Islands:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the natural attractions of Amami Oshima Island.
  • Day 3-4: Go on a hiking or cycling tour on Yakushima Island.
  • Day 5-6: Enjoy water sports and island hopping in Kakeromajima Island.
  • Day 7: Relax at the beach or visit the local markets and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Amami Islands

Must-Try Dishes

The Amami Islands have a unique cuisine that reflects the local culture and history. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Keihan: A rice bowl topped with chicken and egg, served with soup and pickles.
  • Okinawa Soba: A soup dish made with wheat noodles, pork, and vegetables.
  • Mozuku: A type of seaweed that is served in salads, soups, and as a side dish.

Local Beverages

The islands are known for their traditional alcoholic beverage called Awamori, a distilled rice wine. Other popular drinks include local fruit juices and teas.

Where to Stay in Amami Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options on the islands, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some popular choices include:

  • Amami Sunplaza Hotel: A beachfront hotel with comfortable rooms and stunning views.
  • Guesthouse Amami: A budget-friendly guesthouse with a communal kitchen and lounge area.
  • Amami Jungle Trail: A unique eco-lodge that offers glamping accommodation in the middle of the jungle.

What to Buy in Amami Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The islands have a rich culture and history, and there are several souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home. Some popular choices include:

  • Shisa: A traditional Okinawan decorative item that symbolizes protection and brings good luck.
  • Awamori: A bottle of locally made Awamori will make for a unique and authentic gift.
  • Ryukyu Glass: Beautifully crafted glassware made using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Amami Islands

The islands have a unique culture and customs that reflect their history and geographical location. Some notable cultural aspects include:

  • Traditional Folk Music: The islands are known for their unique folk music called Amami Oshima Folk Songs.
  • Textile Crafts: The islands have a rich tradition of textile crafts, such as weaving, dyeing, and embroidery.
  • Tea Ceremony: The Amami Islands have their own traditional tea ceremony called Amami Ryukyu Chado.

Best Time to Visit Amami Islands

The best time to visit the Amami Islands is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amami Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take off your shoes before entering a traditional Japanese house or temple.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious or cultural sites.
  • Swim outside designated areas or during prohibited times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amami Islands

The Amami Islands are generally safe and crime-free, but it’s always advisable to take precautions and keep your belongings safe when traveling. The islands can also be prone to typhoons during certain times of the year, so it’s important to check the weather forecast before planning your trip.

Health in Amami Islands

The islands have access to modern medical facilities and services, but it’s important to take precautions and carry necessary medication and health insurance when traveling. The islands also have a unique ecosystem, and visitors should be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions when exploring the natural surroundings.

Getting to Amami Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amami Islands

There are two airports that serve the Amami Islands: Amami Airport on Amami Oshima and Kikai Airport on Kikai Island. Both airports receive flights from major cities in Japan, such as Tokyo, Osaka, and Fukuoka.

Bus Stations Near Amami Islands

There are several bus services that operate within and between the islands, offering convenient and affordable transportation.

Other Options

Ferry services are available between the islands and other cities in Japan, such as Kagoshima and Okinawa.

How to Get Around in Amami Islands

The islands have several transportation options for getting around, including rental cars and bicycles. There are also several bus services that operate within and between the islands.

Where to Go Next from Amami Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the Amami Islands, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Okinawa: An archipelago that is known for its rich culture and history, as well as beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Yakushima Island: A UNESCO World Heritage site that is known for its dense forests and unique wildlife.
  • Kagoshima: A city located on the mainland of Japan that is known for its active volcano, Mount Sakurajima, and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

The Amami Islands offer a unique and authentic travel experience, with a combination of stunning natural beauty, rich culture and history, and adventure opportunities. Plan your next vacation to Amami Islands and make memories that will last a lifetime.
