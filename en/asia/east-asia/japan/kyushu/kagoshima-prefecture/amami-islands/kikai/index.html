Introduction to Kikai

Kikai Island is a small but stunning island located in the Amami archipelago, in Kagoshima Prefecture, Kyushu, Japan. Once an important center for agriculture and commerce, Kikai is now best known for its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and vibrant culture. Nestled in the heart of East Asia, Kikai Island attracts visitors from around the world with its stunning beaches, lush forests, cultural heritage, and unique local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Kikai

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or outdoor adventures, Kikai Island has something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some must-visit places on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kikai Folk Museum: Learn about the rich cultural heritage of Kikai Island through exhibits of traditional crafts, clothing, and artifacts.

  2. Hikino-ura Shell Mound: Explore the remains of ancient shell mounds dating back to the Jomon period (approx. 14,000-300 BCE).

  3. Kikai Shrine: Pay your respects to the local deity and admire the intricate Shinto-style architecture.

  4. Kikai Castle Ruins: Discover the remnants of an ancient castle that once stood on Mount Kikai.

Natural Attractions of Kikai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Uegusuki Mountain: Hike up to the summit of this mountain and enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view of the island.

  2. Yoro Falls: Take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear water of this stunning waterfall.

  3. Shinkai Bay: Snorkel or scuba dive in the calm waters of this picturesque bay and discover an underwater world filled with colorful marine life.

  4. Kikaijima Tropical Botanical Garden: Explore the lush vegetation of this tropical garden, which is home to a wide array of plants and flowers native to the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kikai

Kikai Island is a hiker’s paradise, featuring a network of trails that lead through lush forests, across mountain peaks, and along scenic coastlines. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes:

  1. Kikai Island Ridge Trail: Traverse the entire length of the island on this 30-kilometer trail, passing through dense forests and over steep mountain peaks.

  2. Yorotsu Trail: Enjoy stunning views of the ocean and coastline on this easy 4-kilometer hike from Yorotsu Village to Nishizaki Lighthouse.

  3. Geographical Features Trail: Learn about the unique geological features of the island on this 4-kilometer loop trail, which takes you through various natural landmarks, including Yoro Falls and Uegusuki Mountain.

Best Things to Do in Kikai: Activities and Tours

Kikai Island offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the top things to do on the island include:

  1. Snorkeling and scuba diving: Explore the vibrant world beneath the waves in Shinkai Bay.

  2. Beach hopping: Relax on the pristine white-sand beaches of Sakibaru and Hama, or discover the colorful pebble beaches of Uchinoura and Nanai.

  3. Local food and drink tour: Sample the island’s famous seafood, such as Ishinabe (fish stew) and Okinawa Soba (noodle soup), along with local spirits like Awamori and Imo Shochu.

  4. Traditional “mud ball” making: Join a local community in making traditional “dorodango” or mud balls, a unique and fascinating cultural experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kikai

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kikai Island, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive on the island and explore its scenic beauty.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour around the island.

Day 3: Visit some cultural landmarks, such as the Hikino-ura Shell Mound and Kikai Shrine.

Day 4: Spend a relaxing day on the beach.

Day 5: Take a tour of local food and drink establishments.

Day 6: Experience traditional cultural activities, such as dorodango-making.

Day 7: Depart from Kikai Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Kikai

Kikai Island is renowned for its fresh seafood and unique local cuisine, which blends Japanese and Okinawan flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ishinabe: A hearty fish stew that features locally caught fish, vegetables, and tofu cooked in a clay pot.

  2. Okinawa Soba: A savory noodle soup made with buckwheat noodles, pork, and various vegetables.

  3. Shima Tofu: A type of tofu made with tropical soybeans that gives it a distinct flavor and texture.

  4. Sata andagi: A deep-fried doughnut-like treat made with local sweet potatoes and eggs.

Local Beverages

  1. Awamori: A distilled spirit made from Thai rice that is unique to Okinawa and the surrounding islands.

  2. Imo Shochu: A distillate made from sweet potatoes, which is popular throughout Kyushu.

  3. Yuzu-cha: A refreshing beverage made from Japanese citrus, which is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Kikai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

On Kikai Island, you’ll find a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some recommended options:

  1. Nanseirou Guesthouse: A traditional-style guesthouse located in the heart of the island, offering cozy rooms and home-cooked meals.

  2. Hotel Grateful Kikai: A modern hotel featuring spacious guest rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop hot spring bath with stunning ocean views.

  3. Kikai Camellia Pension: A family-run pension located near the beach, which offers a comfortable and affordable option for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Kikai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kikai Island is known for its unique crafts and souvenirs, which make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some popular items to consider:

  1. Kikai-gama Pottery: Handmade pottery made from local clays, which features unique colors and designs.

  2. Kikai-tei Weaving: Traditional woven fabrics that are made from natural fibers using age-old techniques.

  3. Kikai Vinegar: Made from sugarcane, this vinegar has a sweet and mild flavor and is perfect for salad dressings and marinades.

Local Culture and Customs of Kikai

Kikai Island has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its traditions, festivals, and daily life. Here are some key aspects of local culture and customs:

  1. Shima-Umi: The term “Shima-Umi” refers to the rich maritime culture of the island, which has roots in the fishing and trading industries.

  2. Kikai-Odori: A traditional dance performed during local festivals, which features colorful costumes and lively music.

  3. Satoumi Conservation: The island is actively involved in the Satoumi conservation movement, which seeks to protect and sustainably manage coastal ecosystems.

Best Time to Visit Kikai

Kikai Island can be visited year-round, but the best time to go depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months (June-August) are the busiest and hottest, with temperatures reaching up to 30°C. If you prefer cooler weather, consider visiting in spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November), when the temperatures are milder and the island is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kikai

When visiting Kikai Island, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  1. Respect nature and the environment.

  2. Try local foods and drinks.

  3. Learn about and appreciate the local culture and customs.

  4. Take your shoes off when entering traditional-style buildings.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or pollute the environment.

  2. Wear shoes inside traditional-style buildings.

  3. Ignore signage or warnings related to safety and conservation.

Safety and Health

Kikai Island is generally safe and well-maintained, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their health and safety during their stay.

Safety in Kikai

  1. Be aware of your surroundings and stay on designated trails when hiking.

  2. Know the location of emergency facilities, such as hospitals and police stations.

  3. Follow all posted warnings related to natural hazards, such as typhoons or tsunamis.

Health in Kikai

  1. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated in the island’s hot and humid climate.

  2. Protect yourself from the sun with sunscreen and appropriate clothing.

  3. Be aware of any health risks or advisories related to food or water quality.

Getting to Kikai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kikai Island is only accessible by boat or plane, but there are several options to get you there.

Airports Near Kikai

  1. Kagoshima Airport: This is the nearest major airport to Kikai Island, located approximately 150 kilometers away. From Kagoshima, you can take a domestic flight to Amami Airport.

  2. Amami Airport: This airport is located on neighboring Amami Island and offers connecting flights to Kikai.

Bus Stations Near Kikai

  1. Nishinoomote Station: Located on mainland Kagoshima, this station offers bus service to Kikai Port.

  2. Amami Station: This station is located on Amami Island and offers bus service to Kikai Port.

Other Options

  1. Ferry: There are several ferry operators that offer daily service between mainland Kagoshima and Kikai Island.

  2. Private Charter: For a more exclusive experience, consider chartering a private boat or plane to take you directly to Kikai Island.

How to Get Around in Kikai

Kikai Island is relatively small, so getting around is easy and convenient. Rental bikes, cars, and scooters are available on the island, or you can use the local bus service to reach your desired destination.

Where to Go Next from Kikai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the surrounding area, there are several nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  1. Amami Oshima: The largest and most populated island in the Amami archipelago, Amami Oshima is known for its stunning beaches and unique culture.

  2. Yakushima: A UNESCO World Heritage site, Yakushima is a nature lover’s paradise, featuring ancient forests and diverse wildlife.

  3. Kagoshima City: The capital city of Kagoshima Prefecture, this bustling city is known for its hot springs, history, and delicious cuisine.

Conclusion

Kikai Island is a hidden gem in the Amami archipelago, offering visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty, rich culture, and unique flavor of Kagoshima, Japan. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or culinary delights, Kikai Island has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and discover why Kikai is one of Japan’s best-kept secrets.
