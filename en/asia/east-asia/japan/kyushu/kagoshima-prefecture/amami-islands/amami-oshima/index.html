Amami Oshima is a stunning island surrounded by crystal-clear waters, located in the Amami Islands of Kagoshima Prefecture in Kyushu, Japan. Known for its rich culture and breathtaking natural landscapes, Amami Oshima is the perfect destination for anyone seeking adventure, relaxation, or an opportunity to explore a new culture. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll explore everything that Amami Oshima has to offer, including where to go, what to do, what to eat, and where to stay.

Introduction to Amami Oshima

Amami Oshima is the largest island of the Amami archipelago and one of the most popular tourist destinations in Japan. The island is located 380 kilometers south of the city of Kagoshima and 250 kilometers north of Okinawa. With a land area of 712.35 square kilometers and a population of approximately 54,000 people, Amami Oshima has a unique blend of Japanese and Okinawan cultures.

List of Places to Visit in Amami Oshima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amami Oshima has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its numerous museums and historical sites:

  • Amami Museum: This museum displays the island’s weaving and dyeing traditions, as well as its rich natural environment.
  • Ueno German Culture Village: This historic village was built during the early 20th century by German prisoners of war and is now a unique tourist attraction.
  • Ohama Seaside Park: This park is home to a beautiful beach and numerous historical sites, including an old castle and former military installation.

Natural Attractions of Amami Oshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Amami Oshima is truly awe-inspiring, and there are plenty of outdoors activities and scenic views to enjoy:

  • Tomori Imugya Marine Biological Station: This research center showcases the island’s diverse marine life, including coral reefs and sea turtles.
  • Amami Park: This park is home to numerous hiking trails, stunning waterfalls, and a natural hot spring.
  • Kinsakubaru Forest: This forest is home to a wide range of endemic plant and animal species and is a popular destination for birdwatching and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amami Oshima

Amami Oshima has numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels:

  • Yakushima Island National Park: This 1,400-sq.-km park has numerous trails that wind through ancient forests and past waterfalls.
  • Yoron-to Island: This small island is known for its stunning coastline and numerous hiking and biking trails.
  • Ohama Seaside Park: This park is home to numerous hiking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s coastline and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Amami Oshima: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to see and do in Amami Oshima:

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving: The island’s crystal-clear waters are perfect for exploring the local marine life.
  • Whale watching: The island is home to numerous species of whales, and numerous tour operators offer whale watching tours.
  • Cultural Experience: Visitors can experience the local culture through traditional dance performances, handicraft workshops, and cultural tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Amami Oshima

Must-Try Dishes

Amami Oshima is known for its unique cuisine, which combines Japanese and Okinawan influences:

  • Tonkatsu: This dish consists of breaded and fried pork cutlets and is a local favorite.
  • Sata andagi: These sweet deep-fried doughnuts are another popular local dish.
  • Umaibou: A local specialty made from dried fish, vegetables, and koji.

Local Beverages

  • Awamori: This alcoholic drink is made from Thai rice and is similar to sake.
  • Amami Kokutou Shochu: This drink is made from brown sugar and has a sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Amami Oshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amami Oshima offers a wide range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Recommended accommodations include:

  • Hotel Lexton Amami: This hotel offers stunning ocean views and a central location.
  • Guesthouse Raicho-no-Mori: This guesthouse is located in a tranquil forest and offers a unique, peaceful atmosphere.
  • Amami Resort Bashayamamura: This resort offers a spa, outdoor pool, and stylish rooms.

What to Buy in Amami Oshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tsumugi Silk: This local silk fabric is used to make clothing and accessories.
  • Amami Oshima Tsumugi Dyeing: Traditional dyed fabrics made from the local silk.
  • Basho-fu (banana cloth): Made from the fibers of the banana tree, Basho-fu cloth is a unique and durable fabric.

Local Culture and Customs of Amami Oshima

Amami Oshima has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its language, music, dance, and cuisine. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Amami Oshima

The best time to visit Amami Oshima is between March and May or from October to December when the weather is mild and the island is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amami Oshima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Try local cuisine and attend cultural events.
  • Be respectful of the island’s natural environment.
  • Be prepared for the island’s humid climate.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Damage the environment or local wildlife.
  • Speak loudly or make excessive noise.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amami Oshima

Amami Oshima is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and following local traffic laws.

Health in Amami Oshima

Amami Oshima has good medical facilities, but visitors should be aware of the island’s humid climate and take precautions to prevent heatstroke and dehydration.

Getting to Amami Oshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Amami Oshima is relatively easy, with multiple transportation options available:

Airports Near Amami Oshima

  • Amami Airport: Located on the island’s northeast coast and is served by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Amami Oshima

  • Kagoshima Central Bus Terminal: Buses run from here to Amami Oshima, with several daily departures.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Ferries run regularly between Kagoshima and Amami Oshima.

How to Get Around in Amami Oshima

Renting a car or a scooter is the best way to get around the island, as public transportation is limited. Taxis are also available, but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Amami Oshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Okinawa: This nearby island chain is a popular destination for its beaches and unique culture.
  • Yakushima Island: This island is known for its ancient forests and diverse wildlife.
  • Kagoshima City: This city is located on the mainland of Kyushu and is famous for its stunning coastline and numerous hot springs.

Conclusion

Amami Oshima is a unique and beautiful destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the island’s rich history and culture, enjoying its natural beauty, or simply relaxing on the beach, Amami Oshima is a must-visit destination. Plan your trip today and get ready to discover the best of Japan’s southern islands!
