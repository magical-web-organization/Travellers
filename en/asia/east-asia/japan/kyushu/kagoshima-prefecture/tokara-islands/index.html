Introduction to Tokara Islands

Located in Kagoshima Prefecture, in Kyushu Island, Japan, Tokara Islands is a hidden gem of East Asia. Composed of twelve small volcanic islands, this archipelago boasts beautiful natural landscapes that include pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush vegetation. The islands have a rich history that dates back to ancient times, and this culture can be seen through their traditional lifestyles and daily customs.

List of Places to Visit in Tokara Islands

There are several culturally important attractions, museums, historical places, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and other places to see in the Tokara Islands. Below is a list of some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tokara Folkcraft Museum: This museum displays traditional tools, furniture, and crafts used by the residents of the islands.
  • Takarajima Island Museum: Located on Takarajima Island, this museum showcases the island’s history through artifacts, images, and videos.
  • Tomioka Castle Ruins: These castle ruins were built during the Edo Period (1603-1868) and offer stunning views of the surrounding sea.

Natural Attractions of Tokara Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kuchierabu Island: This island has an active volcano and is home to various hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the ocean.
  • Akusekijima Island: A small island that has a volcanic lake, several waterfalls, and different outdoor sports activities like diving, snorkeling, and fishing.
  • Teuri Island: Known for its bird population, Teuri Island boasts different types of seabirds that can be seen near its shores.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tokara Islands

Tokara Islands have different routes ideal for hiking and biking, which offer adventurers the opportunity to explore the diverse landscapes of the islands.

Hiking Routes

  • Mt. Onodake: A challenging trek that leads to the highest peak of the islands.
  • Mt. Akuseki : A moderate hike that provides stunning views of the Akuseki Island’s volcano.
  • Mt. Tenpouzan: An easy hike that provides spectacular views of the Pacific Ocean.

Biking Routes

  • Kuchierabu Island: This island offers scenic routes that run along its coastlines and offer great views of the sea and the island’s flora and fauna.
  • Takarajima Island and Akusekijima Island: Both islands have bikes available for rent, making it easy for visitors to explore them on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Tokara Islands: Activities and Tours

There are various activities and tours to enjoy while in the Tokara Islands. Below are some of the best offers:

  • Fishing excursions: Visitors can participate in fishing tours that will take them to the areas around the islands where they can catch different types of fish that are typical of the region.
  • Snorkeling: The clear waters surrounding the Tokara Islands are perfect for snorkeling, where visitors can encounter various marine life species like sea turtles, dolphins, and colorful fish.
  • Visit the hotsprings: Tokara Islands has several hot springs on different islands that are open to visitors.
  • Sunset tours: The sunset tours offer a great opportunity to explore the islands’ natural beauty during the magical hours of dawn or dusk.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tokara Islands

If you have a week to explore the islands, you can follow this itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive and settle in Tokara Islands
Day 2 Visit the Takarajima Island Museum and Tomioka Castle Ruins
Day 3 Hike to Mt. Tenpouzan and kayak in the afternoon
Day 4 Participate in a fishing excursion in the morning, followed by snorkeling and relaxing at the hot springs in the afternoon
Day 5 Go bike riding in Kuchierabu Island and have a picnic on the beach
Day 6 Take a sunset tour and learn about the different folklore and legends of Tokara Islands
Day 7 Depart from the islands

Food and Drink Scene of Tokara Islands

The food scene of the Tokara Islands is influenced by its location and maritime industries, making it a seafood paradise.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes)
  • Kibinago (silver-stripe round herring)
  • Otoro and Chutoro (tuna belly)
  • Ikinari Dango (sweet potato doughnuts)

Local Beverages

  • Sake: This Japanese rice wine has a strong, smooth taste that is typical of the area surrounding the islands.
  • Shochu: Tokara Islands produce a type of shochu that has a distinct aroma and flavor due to the unique local way of brewing.

Where to Stay in Tokara Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are different types of accommodations in the Tokara Islands, from hotels to guesthouses and hostels, where visitors can relax after a day’s activity.

Hotels

  • Tokara Hotel: This hotel offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and has comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • Akuseki Resort Hotel: This hotel is located on a cliff that overlooks the sea and has Japanese-style rooms.
  • Minshuku on the Go: This hotel has cozy guest rooms and is famous for its fresh, locally-sourced seafood.

Hostels

  • Kuchierabu Hostel: A simple but comfortable hostel located in Kuchierabu Island, ideal for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Takarajima Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in Takarajima Island, has comfortable rooms and rates that are affordable.

What to Buy in Tokara Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are various souvenirs and local products to bring home as a memory of the islands. Below is a list of some of them:

  • Katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes)
  • Shochu (local distilled spirit)
  • Takarajima island cotton cloth
  • Ikinari Dango (sweet potato doughnuts)

Local Culture and Customs of Tokara Islands

The locals of Tokara Islands have their unique culture and customs that visitors should note while on the islands, including:

  • Residents of the islands observe a traditional lifestyle that is heavily influenced by fishing and maritime cultures.
  • Visitors should learn some basic Japanese customs, such as bowing and removing shoes in certain areas.
  • Support the local economy by buying local products and services.

Best Time to Visit Tokara Islands

The best time to visit the Tokara Islands is from late April to early June, and from late September to early November. The weather during these months is mild and pleasant, with fewer crowds and an excellent opportunity to experience the beauty of the island at a leisurely pace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tokara Islands

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Stay environmentally conscious and do not litter.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Disrespect the natural habitats and parks on the islands.
  • Take pictures of locals without asking for their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tokara Islands

Tokara Islands are relatively safe for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and mosquito repellent, carrying a flashlight at night, and following guidelines from tour guides.

Health in Tokara Islands

There are no major health risks associated with visiting the Tokara Islands. However, visitors should make sure they have adequate medical insurance and any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Tokara Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are different ways to get to the Tokara Islands, depending on your location and budget.

Airports Near Tokara Islands

Kagoshima Airport is the nearest airport to the Tokara Islands. From there, visitors can take a direct flight to Tokara Islands Airport, which is situated in Toshima Island.

Bus Stations Near Tokara Islands

From Kagoshima City, visitors can take a ferry or a high-speed boat to reach the Tokara Islands. The ferries are available at the Kagoshima Port and operate daily.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a chartered sea plane or a helicopter from Kagoshima City to Tokara Islands.

How to Get Around in Tokara Islands

Visitors can explore the islands on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available on the islands, and buses run between the different islands.

Where to Go Next from Tokara Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the other regions of Kagoshima Prefecture after visiting the Tokara Islands. Some of the places to see in Kagoshima include:

  • Mt. Sakurajima: An active volcano that offers stunning views of Kagoshima.
  • Yakushima Island: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that features various hiking trails and lush forests.
  • Satsuma Peninsula: Known for its hot springs and beautiful natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Tokara Islands are a beautiful destination to enjoy the culture, nature, and adventure of Japan. From the picturesque coastal landscapes to the traditional local culture, visitors are in for an unforgettable experience. Plan your trip, explore the different places to see on the islands, and immerse yourself in the unique way of life of the islanders.
