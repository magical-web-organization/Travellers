Introduction to Kagoshima

Located in Kyushu, Kagoshima is a beautiful city in Japan’s southwestern region. Kagoshima has a rich history and is well-known for its stunning landscapes, hot springs, and active volcano, Sakurajima. The city is also famous for its unique culture, including the Satsuma culture, which is an amalgamation of local traditions and customs. Kagoshima is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore Japan’s rich history and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Kagoshima

Kagoshima has a variety of exciting places to visit. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kagoshima City Museum of Art - This museum, located in Tenmonkan, houses a vast collection of paintings, ceramics, and sculptures from prominent Japanese artists.

  2. Reimeikan Museum - This museum provides insight into Kagoshima’s rich history and culture, including Satsuma and Meiji-era artifacts.

  3. Kagoshima Castle - This beautiful castle, located on a hill in the city center, dates back to 1602 and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Kagoshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sakurajima - This active volcano is Kagoshima’s most famous landmark. You can experience the volcano’s majesty by taking a ferry to the island and exploring the landscape on foot.

  2. Kirishima Mountains - The Kirishima Mountains offer a variety of hiking and trail options, including the famous Yama-no-Eki Trail, which affords spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Sengan-en Garden - This beautiful garden is a designated national treasure and was built in the Edo period. The garden provides an excellent opportunity to see traditional Japanese architecture and landscaping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kagoshima

Kagoshima is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, particularly hikers and bikers. Here are some popular routes:

  1. Kirishima-Kinkowan National Park - This park is well-known for its hiking and offers a variety of trails, ranging from easy to challenging.

  2. Sakurajima-Yunohira Lookout - This lookout offers stunning views of Sakurajima and Kagoshima Bay. You can get there by bike via a cycling route along the coast.

  3. Hirakawa-Zoo Cycling Road - This 27km cycling route offers beautiful views of Kagoshima’s countryside and is an excellent option for cyclists of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Kagoshima: Activities and Tours

Kagoshima offers a range of activities and tours that provide insight into its culture, traditions, and natural beauty. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Shochu Distillery Tours - Shochu is a traditional Japanese distilled beverage that is popular in Kagoshima. You can take a tour of one of the city’s famous distilleries and learn about the production process and history of the drink.

  2. Kagoshima Food Tours - Kagoshima is famous for its cuisine, which features unique local ingredients, including Kurobuta pork and sweet potatoes. Take a food tour and sample the city’s many culinary delights.

  3. Kagoshima City View Bus - This tour takes you around the city’s famous landmarks, including Kagoshima Castle, the city center, and Sakurajima.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kagoshima

Day 1: Explore the city center and visit Kagoshima Castle and Reimeikan Museum.

Day 2: Take a ferry to Sakurajima and hike up the volcano.

Day 3: Explore the Kirishima Mountains and take a guided hiking or biking tour.

Day 4: Relax at one of the city’s famous hot springs, such as Ibusuki or Yunohira.

Day 5: Take a food tour and explore Kagoshima’s culinary scene.

Day 6: Visit Sengan-en Garden and take a tour of the traditional Japanese architecture and landscaping.

Day 7: Explore the city’s shopping districts, including Tenmonkan and Taniyama Shotengai.

Food and Drink Scene of Kagoshima

Kagoshima is a food lover’s paradise. The city is famous for its unique local ingredients, including Kurobuta pork and sweet potatoes. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kurobuta Pork - This is a locally-raised breed of pig that is considered one of the best-tasting pork varieties in the world.

  2. Imo-jochu - This distilled beverage is made from sweet potatoes and is a popular local drink in Kagoshima.

  3. Satsuma-age - This is a regional specialty made from deep-fried fish paste.

Local Beverages

  1. Shochu - This is a traditional Japanese distilled beverage that is popular in Kagoshima.

  2. Sake - Kagoshima is also known for producing high-quality sake, with several breweries in the area.

Where to Stay in Kagoshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kagoshima has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Here are some popular options:

  1. Hotel Granvia Kagoshima - This stylish hotel is located in the city center and offers excellent amenities.

  2. JR Kyushu Hotel Kagoshima - This hotel is conveniently located near Kagoshima Chuo Station and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  3. Hostel Green Guesthouse - This cozy hostel is a popular choice for budget travelers and offers clean, comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Kagoshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kagoshima is home to a variety of unique local products and souvenirs. Here are some popular options:

  1. Satsuma Pottery - This is a type of ceramics that is produced in Kagoshima and dates back to the 16th century.

  2. Shochu - Kagoshima is famous for its shochu, and you can buy bottles of the distilled beverage as souvenirs.

  3. Kurobuta Pork - You can buy locally-raised Kurobuta pork as a unique souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Kagoshima

Kagoshima has a unique and rich culture that is influenced by its history and geographic location. Here are some customs and practices to be aware of:

  • Chindon-ya - This is a traditional street performance that originated in Kagoshima and features a marching band playing lively music while parading through the streets.

  • Satsuma Kiriko - This is a type of cut glass that is made using traditional techniques and is a popular souvenir in Kagoshima.

Best Time to Visit Kagoshima

The best time to visit Kagoshima is during the spring (March-May) and fall (September-November), when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid, while winter (December-February) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kagoshima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions. Kagoshima has a unique culture, and it’s essential to respect the customs and practices of the locals.

  • Try local food and drink. Kagoshima is famous for its unique cuisine and drinks, so make sure to sample some of the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive. The people of Kagoshima are generally courteous and reserved, and it’s important to respect their space and privacy.

  • Be disrespectful towards Sakurajima. The active volcano is a local landmark and a source of pride for the people of Kagoshima, so it’s important to treat it with respect.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kagoshima

Kagoshima is generally a safe city, and crime rates are low. However, you should take standard precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kagoshima

Kagoshima has excellent medical facilities, and travelers can access quality healthcare if needed. However, it’s essential to purchase travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies that may arise.

Getting to Kagoshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kagoshima is well-connected by air, rail, and road. Here are some transportation options:

  • Air: Kagoshima Airport offers domestic and international flights, with connections to major cities in Japan and Asia.

  • Rail: Kagoshima Chuo Station is a major rail hub, with connections to Tokyo, Osaka, and other major cities in Japan.

  • Road: Kagoshima is connected to the rest of Japan via highways, and buses are a convenient and affordable option for travel within the city.

How to Get Around in Kagoshima

Kagoshima is a compact city and is easy to navigate. Here are some transportation options:

  • Bus: The city has an extensive bus network, with routes that cover most of the city’s major attractions.

  • Tram: The city has two tram lines that cover the city center and surrounding areas.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Kagoshima, and they are a convenient and affordable option for travel within the city.

Where to Go Next from Kagoshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kagoshima is an excellent base for exploring other destinations in Kyushu. Here are some popular options:

  • Fukuoka: This vibrant city is known for its food and shopping and is easily accessible by train from Kagoshima.

  • Nagasaki: This historic city is famous for its role in World War II and is a fantastic destination for history buffs.

  • Yakushima: This small island off the coast of Kagoshima is a designated UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers stunning hiking trails and natural scenery.

Conclusion

Kagoshima is an exciting and diverse destination that offers something for everyone. With its stunning natural landscapes, unique culture and traditions, and delicious cuisine, it’s no wonder that Kagoshima is becoming an increasingly popular destination among travelers to Japan. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Kagoshima and experience all that this beautiful city has to offer!
