Introduction to Kanto

Home to Tokyo, one of the largest and most populated cities in the world, Kanto is a region located in Japan’s east-central area. Kanto is composed of seven prefectures, including Tokyo, Chiba, Saitama, Gunma, Tochigi, Ibaraki, and Kanagawa. Known for its bustling cities, rich history, and stunning natural landscapes, Kanto has plenty of attractions to offer for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Kanto

Whether you are interested in experiencing Japan’s vibrant culture, exploring its natural wonders, or enjoying outdoor activities, Kanto has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Kanto:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tokyo National Museum
  • Nikko Toshogu Shrine
  • Tokyo Imperial Palace
  • Kamakura Great Buddha

Natural Attractions of Kanto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Fuji
  • Lake Kawaguchi
  • Hitachi Seaside Park
  • Oze National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kanto

Kanto is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. With its stunning natural landscapes, the region has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views. Here are some of the must-try hiking and biking routes in Kanto:

  • Mount Takao
  • Mount Tsukuba
  • Tama River Cycling Road
  • Mount Nokogiri

Best Things to Do in Kanto: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling solo, with family, or friends, Kanto has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Kanto:

  • Visit Tokyo Disneyland or Disneysea
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Japanese dishes
  • Explore the markets and nightlife in Shibuya and Shinjuku
  • Take a guided tour to Mount Fuji

Local Culture and Customs of Kanto

Kanto is known for its rich cultural heritage and customs. Understanding the local culture is essential for travelers who want to get the most out of their visit. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Bowing is a common form of greeting in Japan
  • Remove your shoes when entering traditional Japanese houses or temples
  • Tipping is not customary in Japan
  • Respect the local customs and traditions

Best Time to Visit Kanto

The best time to visit Kanto depends on what you want to experience. Spring (March to May) is a great time to explore the region’s natural beauty when cherry blossoms bloom. Summer (June to August) brings hot and humid weather, but also offers exciting outdoor activities. Autumn (September to November) brings cool temperatures and stunning fall foliage. Winter (December to February) is ideal for skiing and enjoying the winter festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanto

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kanto:

Do:

  • Try local Japanese food and drinks
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be polite and courteous to locals
  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Japanese houses or temples

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas
  • Talk loudly on public transportation
  • Use your mobile phone in quiet public spaces
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanto

Kanto is a safe region for travelers. However, like any large city, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep your belongings secure in crowded areas
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas
  • Take caution when crossing roads
  • Avoid walking alone in unlit or unfamiliar areas at night

Health in Kanto

Kanto has a high standard of health care. However, it’s always important to take the necessary health precautions when traveling. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Check with your doctor regarding any necessary vaccinations before your trip
  • Use insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the hot and humid summer months
  • Carry a small first aid kit with you when traveling

Getting to Kanto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kanto

Kanto is easily accessible by air with two major international airports:

  • Narita International Airport (NRT)
  • Haneda International Airport (HND)

Bus Stations Near Kanto

Kanto has a vast network of bus services connecting cities throughout the region. Major bus terminals in Tokyo include:

  • Shinjuku Bus Terminal
  • Tokyo Station Bus Terminal
  • Tokyo Disneyland Bus Terminal

Other Transportation Options

Kanto has an extensive railway network which includes Tokyo’s famous Yamanote Line and Shinkansen bullet trains, connecting Tokyo to other regions of Japan. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

How to Get Around in Kanto

Kanto has excellent public transportation, including buses, subways, and trains. The Tokyo Metropolitan Area has an extensive network of public transportation that allows for easy navigation. Trains are the fastest and most convenient way to travel around the region, while buses are ideal for local travel.

Where to Go Next from Kanto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kanto has plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best places to visit outside of Kanto:

  • Kyoto
  • Osaka
  • Hiroshima
  • Nagano

Conclusion

Kanto is a dynamic, fascinating region that has something for every traveler. With its bustling cities, rich history, and stunning natural landscapes, Kanto is an ideal destination for those who want to experience the best of Japan.
