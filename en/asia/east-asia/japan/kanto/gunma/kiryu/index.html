Introduction to Kiryu

Kiryu is a city located in Gunma Prefecture, in the Kanto region of Japan. It is known for its history as a textile town, with the Kiryu Ori textile being a well-known product of the area. Today, Kiryu is a mix of traditional and modern, with cultural attractions and outdoor activities that draw in visitors from around the world. This travel guide provides a comprehensive overview of all things Kiryu, including its attractions, food scene, local customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Kiryu

There are plenty of things to see and do in Kiryu, from its culturally significant museums and historical sites to its natural attractions and hiking trails. Here are some of the must-visit destinations in Kiryu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kiryu Yagibushi Festival Museum

This museum showcases the Kiryu Yagibushi Festival, a traditional festival that has been held in Kiryu for over 400 years. Visitors can learn about the history of the festival, as well as its significance to the local community.

Kiryu Textile Museum

Kiryu’s history as a textile town is on full display at this museum, which features exhibits on Kiryu Ori textile weaving and dyeing techniques.

Kiryu Machinaka Historical Walking Tour

This walking tour takes you through the historic Machinaka district of Kiryu, highlighting its traditional architecture and its significance to the town’s textile industry.

Natural Attractions of Kiryu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Hotaka

Mount Hotaka is a popular hiking destination, offering stunning views of the surrounding Gunma landscape.

Shiraiwa Falls

This 20-meter waterfall is located in the foothills of Mount Hotaka, and offers a refreshing break from the summer heat.

Lake Okushima

Lake Okushima offers a variety of outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kiryu

Kiryu is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some popular routes include:

  • The Kiryu Shichikashuku Hiking Trail, a 6-kilometer route that takes you through the scenic countryside of Kiryu.
  • The Ashio Copper Mine Cycling Road, a 25-kilometer cycling route that takes you through the historic Ashio Copper Mine area.
  • The Mount Akagi Hiking Trail, a challenging 15-kilometer trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Kiryu: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Kiryu, from traditional crafts workshops to hot spring experiences. Here are a few must-try activities and tours:

  • Kiryu Textile Weaving Workshop, where you can learn the traditional art of Kiryu Ori textile weaving.
  • Kiryu Yagibushi Dance Class, where you can learn the traditional dance performed during the Kiryu Yagibushi Festival.
  • Onsen Hot Spring Experience, a relaxing way to unwind after a long day of sightseeing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kiryu

If you’re planning to visit Kiryu for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Kiryu

Kiryu has a rich food and drink culture, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiryu Ramen, a local spin on the popular Japanese dish that includes thin noodles and a soy sauce-based broth.
  • Tebasaki, or chicken wings, a popular local snack.
  • Kiryu Manju, a sweet pastry filled with red bean paste.

Local Beverages

  • Gunma Sake, a traditional Japanese rice wine made in Gunma Prefecture.
  • Nikko Beer, a craft beer produced in nearby Nikko using fresh local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Kiryu

Kiryu has a rich cultural heritage and a unique set of customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Shoes should be removed when entering houses and certain buildings.
  • Be mindful of etiquette when visiting shrines and temples, including respecting shrines and not taking photos in certain areas.
  • Try to learn a few Japanese phrases to show respect to locals.

Best Time to Visit Kiryu

The best time to visit Kiryu depends on your interests. Spring and fall are great times to visit for mild weather and beautiful foliage, while summer is ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kiryu

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages.
  • Respect local customs and cultures.
  • Learn a few basic Japanese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside homes or certain buildings.
  • Disrespect shrines and temples by taking photographs in certain areas.
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kiryu

Kiryu is generally a safe city for visitors. Be sure to keep an eye on your belongings and be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Kiryu

There are no major health concerns in Kiryu, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and be aware of emergency care facilities in the area you are visiting.

Where to Go Next from Kiryu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Kanto region, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Nikko, a mountainous town known for its traditional shrines and temples.
  • Karuizawa, a resort town known for its scenic beauty and shopping.
  • Maebashi, the capital city of Gunma Prefecture, known for its museums and parks.

Conclusion

Kiryu is a unique destination that offers a mix of traditional and modern Japan. From its cultural attractions to its outdoor activities and local cuisine, there’s something for everyone to experience in Kiryu. Plan your trip today, and get ready to explore all that this vibrant city has to offer!
