Introduction to Numata

Numata is a picturesque town located in Gunma prefecture, Kanto, Japan. It is situated at the foothills of the scenic Mount Akagi and surrounded by natural beauty. Numata is a perfect destination for those looking for peace and solitude amidst nature.

List of Places to Visit in Numata

Numata has a lot to offer to its visitors. Here are some must-visit places in Numata:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Numata Castle Ruins: You can visit the ruined castle of Numata, which was built in the 16th century. Not much of the castle remains today, but you can still see the impressive stone walls.
  • Numata City Museum of Art: This museum exhibits contemporary, traditional, and international art forms from different regions of Japan.
  • Numata Matsuri Kaikan: It is a museum that showcases the history and cultural significance of the Numata Festival, which takes place in August.

Natural Attractions of Numata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Akagi: This mountain is a great hiking destination and is popular for providing panoramic views of the surrounding Numata valley.
  • Lake Akagi: This lake provides a scenic view of Mount Akagi and is popular among visitors to enjoy boating and fishing.
  • Fukiware Falls: Located in Oigami Onsen area, the Fukiware Falls is a must-visit place for nature lovers and photographers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Numata

Numata is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes:

  • Tenjō Massif Ridge Trail: This trail is perfect for experienced hikers as it is an 11 km route and takes about six hours to complete.
  • Mt. Hotaka and Mt. Myogi Circuitous Trail: This trail is about 12 km long and takes around six hours to finish. This trail is perfect for nature lovers as it provides scenic views of mountains and lakes.
  • Mt. Akagi Trail Loop: This trail around Mount Akagi is considered relatively easy and takes around three hours to complete. Hikers can enjoy the natural beauty of the Akagi Mountains, including traditional villages and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Numata: Activities and Tours

Numata offers a variety of activities for all kinds of visitors. These are some of the best things to do in Numata:

  • Numata Hot Springs: You can relax and rejuvenate your body and soul while soaking in the mineral-rich hot springs around Numata.
  • Numata Matsuri: The Numata Matsuri is an annual festival held in August. Visitors can enjoy the festivities and experience the local culture, customs and traditions.
  • Numata Cycling Tour: Join a guided cycling tour and explore the scenic beauty around Numata on a bicycle.

Food and Drink Scene of Numata

Numata offers a variety of local dishes and beverages that visitors should try. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kusa mochi: Sweet dessert made of mugwort and rice flour
  • Oshibori soba: Buckwheat noodles served with hot noodles and tsuyu sauce
  • Yaki manju: Sweet cake filled with red bean paste, grilled over an open flame

Local Beverages

  • Numata Apple Cider: Refreshing and flavorful cider made from locally grown apples
  • Kokeshi Sake: Traditional Japanese sake made from local rice and water

Where to Stay in Numata: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Numata has a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some popular hotels, hostels and guesthouses:

  • Numata City Hotel: A contemporary hotel in the heart of Numata city.
  • Auberge Bonheur: A cozy guesthouse near Mount Akagi.
  • Hatcho-no-yu Ryokan: A traditional Japanese ryokan offering hot springs and scenic views of the mountains.

What to Buy in Numata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Numata offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home. Some popular souvenirs are:

  • Stone Carvings: Traditional stone carvings showcasing local designs and motifs.
  • Akagi Art Pottery: Handcrafted pottery created in the Akagi area.
  • Apple Products: Locally grown apples, apple juices, and apple pies.

Local Culture and Customs of Numata

Visitors should take note of some local customs and etiquettes while in Numata. It is customary to bow slightly upon meeting and departing and to remove shoes before entering a house or temple. Also, it is polite to say “Arigatou gozaimashita” (Thank you very much) after receiving any item or after completing a transaction.

Best Time to Visit Numata

The best time to visit Numata is during the spring and autumn seasons. The weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy the picturesque natural scenery of the town.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health considerations for visitors to Numata:

Safety in Numata

Numata is a safe and tranquil town, but visitors should follow some basic safety guidelines:

  • Keep an eye on personal belongings.
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of your surroundings at night.

Health in Numata

Numata has an adequate healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics. It is advisable to bring local currency or a credit card for any medical services required.

Getting to Numata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Numata can be accessed by various modes of transportation. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Numata

The nearest airports to Numata are:

  • Tokyo Haneda Airport (HND), around 140 km away.
  • Tokyo Narita Airport (NRT), around 175 km away.

Bus Stations Near Numata

Numata can be accessed by bus from Tokyo:

  • From Tokyo Station, take the Kanetsu Kotsu Bus to Numata Bus Terminal (2.5 hours)

Other Options

  • By car: Numata is approximately 2.5 hours from Tokyo by car.
  • By train: From Tokyo, take the Joetsu Shinkansen to Jomo-Kogen Station, then take a bus to Numata.

How to Get Around in Numata

The best way to get around in Numata is by rental car, bike, or walking. There are no train stations in the town, but there are buses available for some routes.

Where to Go Next from Numata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Numata:

  • Kusatsu Onsen: Famous hot springs and spa town, around 40 km away.
  • Karuizawa: Scenic town and popular resort area, around 50 km away.
  • Takasaki: Historical city and gateway to the Joshinetsu region, around 50 km away.

Conclusion

Numata is a scenic town that offers a perfect mix of cultural and natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy the local cuisine, relax in hot springs, explore the scenic beauty, and take part in cultural events. Numata is a must-visit destination for those looking for peace and solitude amidst nature.
