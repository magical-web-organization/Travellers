Introduction to Tsumagoi

Tsumagoi is a picturesque mountainous town located in the Gunma Prefecture of Japan’s Kanto Region. This charming destination is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking, biking, skiing, and soaking in hot springs. Tsumagoi is also steeped in history and culture, with numerous museums and historical landmarks to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Tsumagoi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tomizawa Site Museum: This archaeological museum showcases the history of the Tomizawa Site, which dates back to the Jomon Period over 10,000 years ago.

  • Tsumagoi Folk Museum: Learn about the daily life, traditions, and crafts of Tsumagoi’s residents during the Edo Period (1603-1868) in this museum.

  • Kogiai History Museum: Discover the history of the Kogiai region through exhibits, artifacts, and audio-visual displays.

Natural Attractions of Tsumagoi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shima Onsen Hot Springs: Relax and rejuvenate in these natural hot springs, which have been known for their healing properties since the Edo Period.

  • Mount Kusatsu-Shirane: Challenge yourself with a hike to the top of this active volcano, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Lake Nozori: This tranquil lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsumagoi

  • Minenohara course: This beginner-friendly hiking trail takes you through scenic forests and fields, and ends at a panoramic viewpoint.

  • Tashiro Marsh course: This moderate hiking trail takes you through marshlands and forests, and offers picturesque views of Mount Kusatsu-Shirane.

  • Karuizawa course: This advanced biking trail takes you through the scenic mountains of Karuizawa and can be enjoyed all year round.

Best Things to Do in Tsumagoi: Activities and Tours

  • Snow Monkey Tour: Experience a snow monkey hot spring bath and explore the surrounding area with professional guides.

  • Rafting Adventure: Get your adrenaline pumping with a thrilling whitewater rafting experience down the Tone River.

  • Golfing: Spend a leisurely day golfing on Tsumagoi’s lush greens.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tsumagoi

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Tsumagoi and check-in at hotel. Visit Tomizawa Site Museum and Kogiai History Museum.
2 Go hiking on the Minenohara course and relax in Shima Onsen Hot Springs.
3 Visit Lake Nozori for fishing and boating.
4 Take a Snow Monkey Tour and experience a hot spring bath.

7 Depart Tsumagoi.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsumagoi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Onsen Tamago: These soft-boiled eggs are cooked in hot springs and served with soy sauce.

  • Tsumagoi Soba: This buckwheat noodle dish is a local specialty and can be enjoyed hot or cold.

  • Yuba: This tofu skin dish is a staple in Japanese cuisine and is often served with soy sauce and wasabi.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: This fermented rice wine is a popular alcoholic beverage in Japan and can be enjoyed in Tsumagoi’s many restaurants and bars.

  • Green Tea: This healthy beverage is a local favorite and can be found in most cafes and tea houses in Tsumagoi.

  • Shochu: This distilled beverage is made from sweet potatoes, rice, or barley and is often served with hot water or mixed with fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Tsumagoi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Manza Prince Hotel: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of Mount Shirane and easy access to skiing, hot springs, and golfing.

  • Kokuya Hotel: This traditional Japanese inn features tatami-floored rooms, kaiseki cuisine, and natural hot springs.

  • J-Hoppers Tsumagoi Nature Retreat: This hostel offers affordable accommodations in a natural setting and is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Tsumagoi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tsumagoi Soba: Bring home some of Tsumagoi’s famous buckwheat noodles to enjoy later.

  • Shima Onsen Cosmetics: These natural skincare and body products are made with hot spring water from Tsumagoi and are perfect for pampering yourself or as gifts.

  • Karuizawa Wine: This high-quality wine is produced in nearby Karuizawa and can be found in Tsumagoi’s liquor stores and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsumagoi

  • Tsumagoi is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, lacquerware, and woodcraft.

  • Respect for elders and nature is an important aspect of Japanese culture, and visitors are expected to show appreciation for Tsumagoi’s natural beauty and historic landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Tsumagoi

The best time to visit Tsumagoi is from April to November when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. For winter sports enthusiasts, the ski season lasts from December to March when the town is covered in a blanket of snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsumagoi

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothing and shoes suitable for outdoor activities.

  • Observe proper etiquette when visiting temples and other cultural landmarks.

  • Try the local cuisine, including Tsumagoi Soba and Onsen Tamago.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas. There are designated smoking areas in Tsumagoi.

  • Use your phone in quiet areas or on public transportation.

  • Touch or disturb wildlife in Tsumagoi’s natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsumagoi

Tsumagoi is generally a safe destination. However, visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, especially during the winter months, when heavy snowfall can cause dangerous conditions on roads and hiking trails.

Health in Tsumagoi

Tsumagoi has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors can access medical facilities in nearby towns like Kusatsu and Karuizawa. It is recommended to have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Japan.

Getting to Tsumagoi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsumagoi

The nearest airport to Tsumagoi is Haneda Airport in Tokyo, which offers domestic and international flights. From Haneda Airport, visitors can take a train or bus to Tsumagoi, which takes approximately 3 hours.

Bus Stations Near Tsumagoi

JR Bus Kanto operates several bus lines that travel to and from Tsumagoi, including the Tokyo-Tsumagoi line, the Nagoya-Tsumagoi line, and the Osaka-Tsumagoi line.

Other Options

Tsumagoi is easily accessible by train, with several lines running through nearby stations like Karuizawa and Takasaki. Rental cars and taxis are also available for those who prefer to travel on their own schedule.

How to Get Around in Tsumagoi

The best way to get around Tsumagoi is by car or bicycle. Rental cars and bikes are available at the train station and other rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Tsumagoi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Karuizawa: This upscale resort town is located nearby and is famous for its shopping, dining, and skiing.

  • Kusatsu: This hot spring town is a popular destination for relaxation and wellness and is home to the famous Yubatake hot spring source.

  • Matsumoto: This historic castle town is located in Nagano Prefecture and is known for its beautiful castle and scenic surroundings.

Conclusion

Tsumagoi is a hidden gem in Japan’s Kanto region that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history and culture, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you are exploring museums and temples, relaxing in hot springs, or hiking through forests, Tsumagoi has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the many wonders of this charming mountain town.
