Introduction to Gunma

Nestled in the Kanto region of Japan, Gunma is a prefecture that boasts of a rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural landscapes, and mouth-watering local cuisines. On top of that, it is situated just a short train ride away from Tokyo, making it an ideal destination for both locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Gunma

Take a look at this curated list of must-visit spots in Gunma:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ikaho Onsen - This charming hot spring town has been known for its health benefits since the Edo period. The town center is full of traditional Japanese inns, cafes, and shops that will take you back in time.
  • Tomifussa Roman Museum - Discover the history of Gunma with a visit to this museum located in the former Tomioka Silk Mill site. You’ll learn about the area’s silk production and the impact it had on Gunma’s economy.
  • Haruna Shrine - This stunning shrine is situated on Mount Haruna and offers picturesque views of the surrounding area. It is also a popular spot for autumn foliage viewing.

Natural Attractions of Gunma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Tanigawa - This mountain range offers a variety of hiking and trekking options for outdoor enthusiasts. If you’re up for a challenge, try scaling the 1,964-meter tall peak for unforgettable views.
  • Kusatsu Onsen - Known for its effective healing properties, this hot spring town is a popular destination for locals and travelers alike. The town center has plenty of ryokans (traditional Japanese inns), restaurants, and souvenir shops.
  • Fukiware Falls - Located in Oze National Park, this waterfall is a must-visit for nature lovers. The cascading falls create a stunning view that makes it one of the most popular destinations for tourists in Gunma.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gunma

Gunma offers several scenic hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Tanigawa-dake Ropeway Course - This hiking course takes you up the Mount Tanigawa ropeway and provides stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Tomioka -> Minakami Trail - This beginner-friendly biking and hiking trail offers breathtaking views of the Gunma countryside. It is also a great way to experience the cultural heritage of the region.
  • Mount Akagi Hike - This moderate hiking trail takes you up one of the most popular mountains in Gunma. The trail is dotted with several temples and shrines, making it a great way to experience the local culture.

Best Things to Do in Gunma: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to experience in Gunma:

  • Soba Making Class - Soba is a type of Japanese noodle made from buckwheat flour, and Gunma is known for it. Take a soba-making class to learn how to make this local delicacy from scratch.
  • Fishing at Lake Haruna - Lake Haruna is a popular spot for fishing enthusiasts. Rent a boat and try your hand at catching some of the lake’s famous rainbow trout.
  • Tatebayashi Hot-Air Balloon Festival - Every November, the skies over Tatebayashi are filled with colorful hot-air balloons. Join the festivities and witness this beautiful sight for yourself.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gunma

Day 1: Explore Ikaho Onsen and Haruna Shrine Day 2: Take the Mount Tanigawa Ropeway and hike up to the peak Day 3-4: Relax at Kusatsu Onsen Day 5: Visit Tomifussa Roman Museum and Oze National Park Day 6-7: Conquer Mount Akagi and learn how to make soba noodles

Food and Drink Scene of Gunma

Sampling local food and drink is a highlight of any trip to Gunma:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gunma Beef - This local beef is known for its high quality and flavor. Head to a yakiniku (Japanese barbeque) restaurant to try it out.
  • Okkirikomi - This hearty noodle soup is made with local vegetables and is the perfect comfort food on a cold day.
  • Naganuma Ice Cream - Made with fresh milk and local ingredients, this ice cream is a popular dessert in Gunma.

Local Beverages

  • Shimizu Sake - Gunma is known for producing high-quality sake, and Shimizu Sake is one of the best. Take a tour of the brewery to learn about the sake-making process and sample some of their products.
  • Azuma Tea - This premium green tea is grown in the mountainous areas of Gunma and is known for its refreshing taste and health benefits.

Where to Stay in Gunma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gunma offers a variety of accommodation options, including:

  • Ryokans - These traditional Japanese inns offer a unique cultural experience. They typically include tatami mat floors, futon beds, and communal hot spring baths.
  • Business Hotels - These hotels are designed for travelers on a budget who are looking for a comfortable and convenient place to stay.
  • Guesthouses - Perfect for solo travelers and backpackers, these accommodations offer a more social and communal experience.

What to Buy in Gunma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back from Gunma include:

  • Gunma Craft Beer - This local brew is made with Gunma’s famous water and is the perfect way to remember your trip.
  • Gunma Ceramic Ware - This region is known for producing high-quality pottery that makes for great gifts.
  • Gunma Silk Products - The silk industry is an important part of Gunma’s history and culture. Take home a silk scarf or handkerchief as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Gunma

Gunma is a region with a rich cultural heritage. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect for hot springs - Many of Gunma’s towns and cities are known for their hot springs. Remember to follow proper etiquette, such as rinsing yourself before entering the bath and not using soap in the water.
  • Remove your shoes - It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a traditional Japanese inn.
  • Use chopsticks - In Japan, it is customary to use chopsticks when eating. Remember to respect local customs and use them properly.

Best Time to Visit Gunma

The best time to visit Gunma is during the spring (March-May) and the fall (September-November). The weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gunma

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect local customs and etiquette
  • Explore the region’s natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Disrespect hot springs etiquette
  • Forget to remove your shoes in traditional Japanese inns
  • Litter in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Gunma

Gunma is a relatively safe region. However, travelers should still take basic precautions, such as:

  • Keeping an eye on your belongings
  • Sticking to well-lit and populated areas at night
  • Paying attention to weather warnings in case of natural disasters

Health in Gunma

Health is not a major concern when traveling to Gunma. However, it is always a good idea to take basic precautions, such as drinking bottled water and washing your hands frequently.

Getting to Gunma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gunma

  • Tokyo Haneda Airport - This is the closest major airport to Gunma, with direct flights from many countries and cities.
  • Narita International Airport - Located about two hours away by train or bus, this airport also offers direct flights from many countries and cities.

Bus Stations Near Gunma

  • Takasaki Bus Terminal - This terminal is located in the heart of Gunma and is served by several bus companies.
  • Kusatsu Bus Terminal - This terminal is located in one of Gunma’s most popular hot spring destinations and is served by several local and long-distance bus companies.

Other Options

  • Bullet Train (Shinkansen) - Several bullet train lines run through Gunma, with stops at Takasaki and other major cities.
  • Rental Car - Renting a car is a great way to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Gunma

Gunma is well-connected by public transportation, including trains and buses. Getting around is generally easy and affordable. Taxis and rental cars are also available, but these can be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Gunma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tokyo - Just a short train ride away, Tokyo offers endless possibilities for sightseeing, shopping, and dining.
  • Nikko - This city is known for its stunning shrines and temples, and is located less than two hours away by train.
  • Karuizawa - This peaceful resort town is located in Nagano prefecture and is a popular destination for those looking to escape the city.

Conclusion

With its rich culture, stunning natural landscapes, and mouth-watering local cuisine, Gunma is an ideal destination for any traveler. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply relaxing in hot springs, there’s something for everyone in Gunma.
