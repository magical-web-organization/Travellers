Introduction to Choshi

Welcome to Choshi, a charming coastal city located in Chiba Prefecture, in the Kanto region of Japan. Choshi boasts a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities, making it an ideal destination for any traveler looking to immerse themselves in the authentic Japanese experience.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, must-try food and drink recommendations, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations to explore during your stay in Choshi.

List of Places to Visit in Choshi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Choshi has a rich history, and you can experience it by visiting some of its museums and historical sites, including:

  • Ukita-ya: This traditional Japanese house dates back to the Edo-era and was once home to a wealthy merchant. The Ukita-ya Museum offers an insight into the life of a prosperous Choshi family.
  • Choshi Museum of History: Discover the city’s past through its extensive collection of artifacts and documents, including displays on the local fishing industry, World War II air raids, and traditional festivals.
  • Choshi Port Tower: This tower offers a panoramic view of Choshi Port, Choshi Bay, and even Mt. Fuji on clear days.

Natural Attractions of Choshi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Choshi is known for its natural beauty, with plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic spots to explore. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in Choshi:

  • Choshi Otaki Waterfall: This impressive waterfall is one of Japan’s top 100 famous waterfalls, with a height of 20 meters. Located in the middle of a forest, the Choshi Otaki Waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Inubosaki Lighthouse: Located at the easternmost point of the Boso Peninsula, the Inubosaki Lighthouse offers breathtaking panoramic views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape. The lighthouse also has a small museum that displays maritime artifacts.
  • Kujukuri Beach: A 66-kilometer stretch of sandy beach, Kujukuri Beach is perfect for swimming, surfing, or just taking a peaceful walk along the shore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Choshi

Choshi has several hiking and biking trails that offer a unique perspective on the city’s natural beauty. Some recommended routes include:

  • Choshi Nature Trail: This 10-kilometer trail leads hikers through the city’s scenic countryside, with stunning views of the ocean and Mt. Fuji. The trail begins at Cape Nojima and ends at Inubosaki Lighthouse.
  • Awa Shrine Walking Course: This 3-kilometer course leads hikers through Awa Shrine, famous for its cherry blossoms in the spring, and to Choshi Otaki Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Choshi: Activities and Tours

Choshi offers plenty of fun activities and tours for all ages, including:

  • Grampus Football Club: Catch a soccer game at Fukuda Denshi Arena, where the Grampus Football Club plays its home games.
  • Choshi Electric Railway: Ride on Japan’s shortest railway line, the Choshi Electric Railway, which runs for only 6.4 kilometers and takes passengers through the scenic countryside and coast.
  • Choshi Brewery Tours: The Kasumi Shuzo Brewery offers tours of its artisanal sake-making facilities, where visitors can learn about the brewing process and sample some of their award-winning sake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Choshi

If you’re planning to spend a week in Choshi, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Explore Choshi Port Tower, Ukita-ya Museum, and Choshi Bay
2 Hike the Choshi Nature Trail and visit Inubosaki Lighthouse
3 Relax on Kujukuri Beach
4 Visit Choshi Museum of History and Awa Shrine Walking Course
5 Take a tour of Kasumi Shuzo Brewery

Food and Drink Scene of Choshi

Must-Try Dishes

Choshi is famous for its seafood, so don’t miss out on trying these local dishes:

  • Sardines: Grilled or fried, sardines are a local specialty and a must-try for any seafood lover.
  • Namero: A traditional Choshi dish made with raw fish, ginger, and green onion, namero is a refreshing and flavorful dish.
  • Choshi Soba: Made with locally grown buckwheat, Choshi soba is a simple but delicious noodle dish.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Choshi has some fantastic options, including:

  • Sake: The city is home to several excellent sake breweries, and their products can be found in many restaurants and bars around the city.

Where to Stay in Choshi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Choshi has plenty of options for accommodation, including:

  • Inubosaki Kanko Hotel: Located on the eastern cape of Choshi, this hotel offers panoramic views of the ocean and Mt. Fuji.
  • Makino Bekkan: A traditional Japanese-style hotel with hot-spring baths and on-site barbecues.
  • Choshi Guest House: This budget-friendly guest house offers a comfortable stay in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Choshi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Choshi is known for several local products, including:

  • Sake: Bring home a bottle of Choshi sake as a souvenir or gift for friends and family.
  • Seafood: Any seafood lover will appreciate a gift of dried squid or sardines from Choshi.

Local Culture and Customs of Choshi

Choshi has several unique customs and traditions, including:

  • The Choshi Ameyoko Festival: Held every year in February, this traditional festival features parades and performances by locals dressed in colorful and elaborate costumes.
  • The Choshi Sanja Festival: This festival is held every third weekend of July and features traditional mikoshi (portable shrines) being carried through the streets of Choshi.

Best Time to Visit Choshi

The best time to visit Choshi is during the spring and autumn. The cherry blossoms in the spring and the fall foliage make for breathtaking views, and the temperatures are mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Choshi

Do:

  • Try the local seafood dishes and sake.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s scenic countryside and coast on foot or by bicycle.

Don’t:

  • litter or leave trash behind.
  • Wear shoes inside traditional Japanese-style accommodation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Choshi

Choshi is a safe city, and violent crimes are rare. However, as with any destination, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to avoid becoming the victim of petty crimes such as pickpocketing.

Health in Choshi

Choshi is a clean and hygienic city, and there are no specific health concerns for travelers. Tap water is safe to drink, and medical facilities are available in case of an emergency.

Getting to Choshi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Choshi

The nearest airport to Choshi is Narita International Airport, which is approximately 50 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Choshi

Choshi is well-connected by bus to other major cities in Japan. The Choshi City Bus offers convenient access to many tourist attractions in and around the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Choshi by train. The nearest station is Choshi Station, which is serviced by the JR Sobu Line.

How to Get Around in Choshi

Choshi is a small city, and walking or cycling are the most convenient ways to get around. In addition, the Choshi Electric Railway runs for 6.4 kilometers and connects the city’s major tourist attractions.

Where to Go Next from Choshi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Choshi, visitors can explore several nearby destinations, including:

  • Mount Nokogiri: A beautiful mountain located in Chiba’s Boso Peninsula, Mount Nokogiri offers spectacular views of Tokyo Bay and the surrounding landscape.
  • Kamogawa: A small coastal town located on the southern tip of the Boso Peninsula, Kamogawa is known for its beautiful beaches and traditional Japanese inns.
  • Narita: Located approximately 70 kilometers from Choshi, Narita is a quaint Japanese town famous for Narita International Airport and the beautiful Naritasan Shinshoji Temple.

Conclusion

Choshi is one of Japan’s hidden gems, with its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to visit Choshi and experience everything this charming coastal city has to offer.
