Introduction to Chiba (Prefecture)

Chiba (Prefecture) is located in the Kanto region of Japan, on the east coast of Honshu island. It is a popular destination for tourists who are looking for a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and natural beauty. With easy access from Tokyo, Chiba (Prefecture) is a great addition to any itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Chiba (Prefecture)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chiba Castle: Located in the heart of Chiba City, this castle is a famous symbol of the prefecture’s history.
  • Sakura Castle: This castle was built in the 16th century and is known for its beautiful cherry blossom trees.
  • Katori Jingu Shrine: A popular shrine with a rich history, the Katori Jingu Shrine is said to have been established over 2,000 years ago.

Natural Attractions of Chiba (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Nokogiri: A mountain with a beautiful view of Tokyo Bay, Mount Nokogiri is a great spot for hiking and sightseeing.
  • Kujukuri Beach: One of Japan’s longest beaches, Kujukuri Beach is a great place for swimming and water sports.
  • Boso no Mura: This open-air museum showcases traditional Japanese life and architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chiba (Prefecture)

  • Kamogawa Sea World Road: A scenic cycling road that follows the coast of Kamogawa City.
  • Kashiwa Sports Park: A popular spot for hiking, Kashiwa Sports Park has several trails of varying difficulty.
  • Yoro Valley: A beautiful valley in the mountains of Chiba (Prefecture) that is perfect for hiking.

Best Things to Do in Chiba (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

  • Tokyo Disney Resort: A popular theme park that is part of the Disney franchise, Tokyo Disney Resort is located in Urayasu City.
  • Narita Airport Transit & Stay Program: A program that offers layover tours of Narita City for those who are passing through Narita Airport.
  • Oyama Senmaida Terrace: A beautiful rice terrace that offers scenic views and traditional Japanese culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chiba (Prefecture)

Day Activities
1 Explore Chiba City
2 Visit local museums and historical sites
3 Go hiking or biking in one of Chiba (Prefecture)’s scenic areas
4 Relax on the beach or participate in water sports at Kujukuri Beach

7 Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Narita City

Food and Drink Scene of Chiba (Prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Katsu-don: A rice bowl topped with deep-fried pork and eggs, and served with a sweet sauce.
  • Unagi: Freshwater eel that is grilled and served over rice.
  • Chiba Mochi: A type of rice cake that is made with local ingredients and flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Saki: An alcoholic drink that is brewed from rice and water.
  • Shochu: A distilled drink that is made from sweet potatoes or barley.
  • Hojicha: A traditional Japanese tea that is roasted to give it a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Chiba (Prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel New Otani Makuhari: A luxurious hotel that is located near Makuhari Messe convention center.
  • Toyoko Inn Chiba Ekimae: A budget-friendly hotel that is located near Chiba Station.
  • Amor Farm Stay: A guesthouse that offers a unique farm-stay experience in the countryside of Chiba (Prefecture).

What to Buy in Chiba (Prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ginbei-san Dolls: A traditional doll that is dressed like a samurai warrior.
  • Narita-yaki Pottery: Handcrafted pottery that is made in Narita City.
  • Boso Towels: Traditional towels that are made locally in Chiba (Prefecture).

Local Culture and Customs of Chiba (Prefecture)

  • Chiba Dance Festival: A traditional dance festival that is held in Chiba City in August.
  • Narita Taiko Festival: A traditional drum festival that is held in Narita City in July.
  • Sakura Matsuri: A cherry blossom festival that is held in various locations throughout Chiba (Prefecture) in spring.

Best Time to Visit Chiba (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Chiba (Prefecture) is from late March to early May, when the cherry blossom trees are in full bloom. Autumn is also a great time to visit, as the leaves change color and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chiba (Prefecture)

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine, such as unagi and katsu-don.
  • Do respect local culture and customs.
  • Do take your shoes off when entering someone’s home or a traditional Japanese inn (ryokan).

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in public areas.
  • Don’t talk loudly on public transportation.
  • Don’t tip servers or taxi drivers, as it is not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chiba (Prefecture)

Chiba (Prefecture) is generally safe, with low crime rates. However, it is always best to take common sense precautions, such as locking your valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Chiba (Prefecture)

Chiba (Prefecture) has an excellent healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics that cater to tourists. It is recommended that you have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Japan.

Getting to Chiba (Prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chiba (Prefecture)

  • Narita International Airport: Located in Narita City, this airport is the main international gateway to Japan.
  • Haneda Airport: Located in Tokyo, Haneda Airport is a smaller airport that primarily services domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Chiba (Prefecture)

  • Makuhari Messe: A large convention center that is located in Chiba City.
  • Tokyo Disney Resort: A popular theme park that is located in Urayasu City.

Other Options

  • Train: Chiba (Prefecture) is well-connected to other parts of Japan by train, with several major train stations located throughout the prefecture.
  • Car: It is possible to rent a car and drive around Chiba (Prefecture), but be aware of the rules of the road and be prepared for heavy traffic in some areas.

How to Get Around in Chiba (Prefecture)

  • Train: Chiba (Prefecture) has an extensive train network that is operated by several different companies.
  • Bus: There are many local and express buses that run throughout Chiba (Prefecture).
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Chiba (Prefecture), but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Chiba (Prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tokyo: Japan’s capital city is just a short train ride away.
  • Mount Fuji: Japan’s famous mountain is accessible from Chiba (Prefecture) by bus or train.
  • Nikko: A historic town that is known for its temples and natural beauty, Nikko is a great destination for a day trip.

Conclusion

Chiba (Prefecture) is a diverse and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring historic sites, hiking in scenic areas, or experiencing local cuisine and culture, Chiba (Prefecture) is a must-visit destination in Japan.
