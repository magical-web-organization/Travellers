Introduction to Kashiwa

Located in Chiba prefecture, Kashiwa is a city in the Kanto region of Japan. The city is known for its rich history and culture, as well as its abundance of natural beauty. From scenic parks to cultural museums, Kashiwa has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kashiwa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kashiwa-no-ha Campus Station - A futuristic station that has won design awards for its uniqueness, Kashiwa-no-ha Campus Station is a must-see attraction for architecture enthusiasts.

  • Kashiwa City Museum of Literature - This museum is dedicated to the works of local authors and provides visitors with a glimpse into the literary history of Kashiwa.

  • Kashiwanoha Park - With its beautiful cherry blossom trees and seasonal flowers, Kashiwanoha Park is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

  • Kashiwa Shrine - A Shinto shrine that dates back to 725 AD, Kashiwa Shrine is steeped in history and is a must-visit destination for those interested in Japan’s rich religious traditions.

Natural Attractions of Kashiwa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Teganuma Shinsui Park - This park is home to Teganuma, one of the largest and most beautiful lakes in Chiba. Visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, and birdwatching, as well as take in scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Kashiwa Nature Park - With its expansive network of trails and scenic vistas, Kashiwa Nature Park is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and cycling to picnicking and birdwatching, there are endless activities to enjoy in this beautiful park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kashiwa

  • Teganuma Nature Trail - A scenic 9-kilometer trail that follows the perimeter of Teganuma Lake, the Teganuma Nature Trail is a popular destination for nature lovers.

  • Mitsuike Park Hiking Course - A 4-kilometer circular trail that takes visitors through Mitsuike Park, a beautiful green space that is home to a variety of plants and animals.

Best Things to Do in Kashiwa: Activities and Tours

  • Kashiwa Fireworks Festival - Held annually on the first Saturday of August, the Kashiwa Fireworks Festival is a must-see spectacle that features over 10,000 fireworks.

  • Kashiwa Sports Park - With its diverse range of sports facilities, including a baseball field, soccer field, and tennis courts, Kashiwa Sports Park is a great destination for sports enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Kashiwa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kashiwa-mochi - A traditional Japanese sweet made from mochi and red bean paste, Kashiwa-mochi is a specialty of Kashiwa.

  • Kashiwa-yaki - A traditional Japanese snack that is similar to takoyaki, Kashiwa-yaki is a must-try for foodies visiting Kashiwa.

Local Beverages

  • Sake - Kashiwa is home to several sake breweries, and visitors can enjoy local varieties of this traditional Japanese drink.

  • Green tea - Kashiwa is known for its high-quality green tea, and visitors can enjoy a cup at one of the city’s many tea houses.

Where to Stay in Kashiwa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • APA Hotel Kashiwa-Ekimae - A modern hotel located near Kashiwa Station, the APA Hotel Kashiwa-Ekimae is a great option for those looking for convenient and affordable accommodations.

  • Kashiwa Plaza Hotel - With its spacious rooms and convenient location, the Kashiwa Plaza Hotel is a great option for both business and leisure travelers.

What to Buy in Kashiwa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kashiwa-mochi - This traditional Japanese sweet is a great souvenir to bring back home.

  • Green tea - Kashiwa is known for its high-quality green tea, and visitors can purchase locally grown tea to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kashiwa

Kashiwa is known for its rich history and culture, and visitors should take the time to learn about the city’s traditions and customs. One of the most important cultural events in Kashiwa is the Kashiwa Festival, which takes place in early May and features traditional music, dance, and food.

Best Time to Visit Kashiwa

The best time to visit Kashiwa is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and the city’s many parks and gardens are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kashiwa

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering homes and many other buildings

  • Take your trash with you and dispose of it properly

  • Bow to show gratitude and respect

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside homes and many other buildings

  • Speak too loudly, as Japanese culture values tranquility and privacy

  • Tip, as tipping is not customary in Japan

Safety and Health

Safety in Kashiwa

Kashiwa is a safe city with low crime rates, but visitors should take the usual precautions to stay safe, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Kashiwa

Kashiwa has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can rest assured that they will receive high-quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Kashiwa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kashiwa

  • Narita International Airport (NRT) - Located approximately 30 kilometers away, Narita International Airport is the closest airport to Kashiwa.

  • Haneda Airport (HND) - Located approximately 50 kilometers away, Haneda Airport is another option for those traveling to Kashiwa.

Bus Stations Near Kashiwa

  • Kashiwa Station - The main bus hub in Kashiwa, Kashiwa Station is served by several bus lines.

Other Options

  • Train - Kashiwa Station is served by several train lines, making it easy to reach the city by rail.

  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available in Kashiwa and are a good option for those traveling in groups or with heavy luggage.

How to Get Around in Kashiwa

Kashiwa is a easy city to get around on foot, but visitors can also use the city’s public transportation system, which includes buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Kashiwa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tokyo - Located approximately 30 kilometers away, Tokyo is a must-visit destination for all travelers to Japan.

  • Chiba City - Located approximately 20 kilometers away, Chiba City is known for its rich history and culture.

Conclusion

With its rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty, Kashiwa is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Japan. From hiking and cycling to sampling traditional Japanese cuisine, there is something for everyone in this vibrant and exciting city. Plan your trip today and discover all that Kashiwa has to offer.
