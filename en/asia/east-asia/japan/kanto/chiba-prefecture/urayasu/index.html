Introduction to Urayasu

Urayasu is a city located in Chiba prefecture, Japan. Known for its amusement parks and close proximity to Tokyo, the city is a popular travel destination for tourists who want to experience the best of Japanese culture, entertainment, and sightseeing. Whether you’re interested in natural beauty, cultural experiences, food, or shopping, Urayasu has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll provide you with all the essential information you need to make the most of your trip to Urayasu.

List of Places to Visit in Urayasu

Urayasu is home to many cultural and natural attractions that are worth visiting. Below are some of the must-visit places in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tokyo Disneyland: The renowned theme park offers a magical experience to its visitors with its fun rides, Disney characters, and parades.
  • Tokyo DisneySea: Another popular theme park in the city, Tokyo DisneySea offers a unique experience with its water rides and attractions.
  • Kasai Rinkai Park: Located near Tokyo Bay, the park offers a stunning view of Tokyo city, along with Aquarium facility.
  • Tokyo Sea Life Park: Discover the wonders of the marine world at this aquarium which houses over 600 different species of sea creatures.
  • Samurai Museum: Learn about samurai history, weaponry, and culture at this museum.

Natural Attractions of Urayasu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Edogawa River: Enjoy a leisurely stroll or cycle the banks of the Edogawa river, which offers a serene and peaceful atmosphere.
  • Urayasu Local Nature Park: Experience the beauty of nature at this tranquil location, which features walking paths, ponds, and greenery.
  • Araha Beach: Located within a 30-minutes ride from Urayasu, Araha Beach is an ideal place to relax and soak up the sun.
  • Kasai Seaside Park: This park offers splendid panoramic views of Tokyo Bay and Tokyo Disneyland.
  • Yatsu Alter: A hilltop location offering a bird’s-eye view of Urayasu and the surrounding area.

Food and Drink Scene of Urayasu

Urayasu has some delicious local dishes and beverages that you must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yaki-niku: Popular dish among Japanese BBQ grilled meat usually finely sliced, grilled and seasoned to perfection.
  • Udon: A serving of thick white noodles in soup which is warm and comforting
  • Tempura: Seafood and vegetables that are deep-fried and deliciously crispy - a Japanese favourite.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Japanese rice wine typically served with sushi, tempura or sashimi.
  • Chu–Hai: A refreshing alcoholic drink made from grapefruit, lemon or lime juice.

Local Culture and Customs of Urayasu

Japanese culture and customs are unique and distinct. While visiting the city, it’s important to be respectful of these customs. Some of the customs include not wearing shoes inside houses or temples, bowing instead of shaking hands when greeting, and not blowing noses in public.

Best Time to Visit Urayasu

The best time to visit Urayasu is from March to May or September to November when the climate is mild, and the temperature is comfortable. The cherry blossom season during March/April is also highly recommended.

Do’s and Don’ts of Urayasu

Do:

  • Do take your shoes off when entering a private home, traditional restaurant or temple.
  • Do bow when greeting someone especially elders or business associates.
  • Do take a ride on a Tokyo taxi, it’s highly recommended.

Don’t:

  • Don’t tip in restaurants, bars or taxis in Japan.
  • Don’t eat or drink in public places.
  • Don’t engage in loud or disruptive behaviour on the train or bus systems.

Safety and Health

Safety is always a top priority when travelling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting Urayasu.

Safety in Urayasu

Urayasu is a safe city, but it’s always important to be mindful of your surroundings and exercise precautions. To stay safe, avoid unlit areas at night and don’t leave your belongings unattended. In case of emergencies, dial 110.

Health in Urayasu

Japan places an emphasis on personal hygiene, and this extends to its food and water practices. You can safely drink water from the tap, and it’s safe to eat food from street vendors or traditional restaurants.

Getting to Urayasu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Urayasu is easily accessible from Tokyo by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Urayasu

Narita International Airport is the nearest airport to Urayasu, located about 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Urayasu

The City buses and private bus service also provide reliable transportation to travel easily within Urayasu and other neighbouring cities. Regular buses serves Tokyo’s raillways and bus stations.

Other Options

You can also travel to Urayasu by train or taxi.

How to Get Around in Urayasu

Urayasu is easy to navigate by local buses, ferry boats, and trains. Taxis services are also available, but they tend to be more expensive.
