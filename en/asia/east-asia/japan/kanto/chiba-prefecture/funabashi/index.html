Introduction to Funabashi

Funabashi is a city in Chiba prefecture, located on the eastern coast of Tokyo Bay, in the Kanto region of Japan. It has a population of approximately 630,000 people and covers an area of 85 square kilometers. The city has a rich history with cultural attractions, natural beauty, hiking and biking routes, and local food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Funabashi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Funabashi Castle Ruins

Funabashi Castle (Funabashi-jo) used to stand here, but only its ruins remain today. The site houses an important historical structure, including the tenshu (main keep). The castle was originally built in 1537 and suffered significant damage due to fires and natural disasters before being destroyed by government order in 1873.

Nishiki Shrine

The Nishiki Shrine is a shrine that has been worshipped since ancient times with a history of 1,300 years. The shrine is characterized by the “Fureai no Mori” that spreads over an area of about 3,000 square meters which is densely covered with trees that rise high, and it is also a popular spot for cherry blossom viewing.

Kura-no-Netsuryo Museum

Kura-no-Netsuryo Museum is a museum that introduces the history and culture of Funabashi through the display of precious materials related to local industries such as sake breweries, lumberyards, and merchants from the Edo period (1603-1868). You can experience the temperature control technology used in the past for storing goods in the warehouse carefully restored from the old town’s sake brewery that unfortunately burned down.

Natural Attractions of Funabashi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wakasu Seaside Park

Wakasu Seaside Park is a spacious park located on the reclaimed land on Tokyo Bay. It sports a beautiful landscape and various leisure facilities, including cycling paths, camping grounds, and picnic areas.

Funabashi Cycling Road

Funabashi Cycling Road is a cycling path paved near the coast of the Tokyo Bay. It runs across Funabashi and connects to other cycling paths in the neighboring cities. You can enjoy the beautiful scenery of the sea and trails along the way.

Inage Seaside Park

Located about six kilometers from the city center, Inage Seaside Park is a beautiful park blessed with rich nature, such as a natural beach, woodlands, and Emerald Park. It also houses the Chiba Zoological Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Funabashi

Ara River Cycling Road

The Ara River Cycling Road is a beautiful path that runs parallel to the river and provides beautiful views of the surrounding countryside. It also connects to the Funabashi Cycling Road, stretching a total of 28 kilometers.

Arakawa Nature Park

Located along the Arakawa River, the park provides visitors with opportunities to enjoy beautiful scenery in a natural setting. It also features walking trails and cycling paths, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Funabashi: Activities and Tours

River Rafting at Nature Activity Park

River rafting at Nature Activity Park is a perfect adventure for thrill-seekers for outdoor enthusiasts. This activity is an exciting way to explore the natural scenery of Funabashi while challenging yourself.

Funabashi Festivals

Funabashi has numerous traditional festivals throughout the year, featuring local dances, food stalls, and fireworks displays. Some notable festivals include the Funabashi Hina Matsuri (Doll Festival), Funabashi Sakura Matsuri (Cherry Blossom Festival), and Funabashi Summer Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Funabashi

Must-Try Dishes

Funabashi Yakisoba

Funabashi Yakisoba is a regional specialty that features thick noodles stir-fried with pork and cabbage. The dish is usually served with mayonnaise and spice mix, which enhances its flavor.

Funabashi Udon

Funabashi Udon is thick and soft noodle in a soy sauce broth topped with tempura shrimp and other vegetables. This dish is another popular local specialty.

Local Beverages

Funabashi Sake

Funabashi is home to several sake breweries, making it an ideal place to sample and purchase locally brewed sake. The city’s particular mix of water and temperature influences the taste of its sake, making it different from other regions.

Funabashi Craft Beer

The city has several craft beer breweries, offering a wide range of locally brewed beer in many flavors.

Where to Stay in Funabashi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Family Lodge Hatagoya

Family Lodge Hatagoya is a simple and affordable hotel located near the Funabashi Station. Amenities include free Wi-Fi, vending machines, and laundry facilities.

Hotel Brussel

Hotel Brussel is a modern and affordable hotel located near the JR Funabashi Station. Amenities include free Wi-Fi, a 24-hour front desk, and air conditioning.

What to Buy in Funabashi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Funabashi Peanuts

Funabashi is known for its peanuts, which are locally harvested and sold in numerous stores throughout the city. The peanuts are available in raw or roasted form and are an ideal souvenir to bring home.

Funabashi Sake

Funabashi produces some of the finest sake in Japan, and it is available in several stores and breweries throughout the city. Visitors can purchase it as a souvenir to take home or even to drink while in their hotel room.

Local Culture and Customs of Funabashi

Funabashi has a deep cultural history, and the locals pride themselves on their traditions. Visitors should respect their customs and avoid loud and disruptive behavior, especially during traditional festivals.

Best Time to Visit Funabashi

The best time to visit Funabashi is from March to May, when the cherry blossoms are blooming, and the weather is mild. Alternatively, September to November is an excellent time to visit with the fall foliage and lower humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Funabashi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines.
  • Try the local specialties, such as Funabashi Yakisoba and Udon.
  • Follow proper etiquette when you are cycling on the Funabashi Cycling Road.

Don’t:

  • Litter or smoke, especially in public areas.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public spaces.
  • Touch or climb on historical buildings or artifacts.
  • Ignore safety signs or warnings when engaging in outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Funabashi

Funabashi is a safe city, even for solo travelers. However, visitors should follow common safety practices, such as avoiding walking in secluded areas at night or carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Funabashi

The city has excellent medical facilities, including several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have proper health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Funabashi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Funabashi

The nearest airport to Funabashi is Narita International Airport, which is approximately 45 kilometers away. The airport has shuttle buses and trains that connect it to the city.

Bus Stations Near Funabashi

Funabashi has several bus terminals offering services to neighboring cities and other parts of Japan. The central bus terminal located at the JR Funabashi Station has the most routes and services.

Other Options

Funabashi is accessible by train, with several train stations located throughout the city. The JR Funabashi Station serves as a central hub for train services.

How to Get Around in Funabashi

Funabashi is a large city, and the best way to explore it is by train, bus or bicycle. The city has an extensive network of train and bus services that connect it to neighboring cities and tourist attractions. Cycling is also a popular option, with many cycling paths throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Funabashi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tokyo Disneyland

Located just a few kilometers from Funabashi, Tokyo Disneyland is a popular destination for families and theme park enthusiasts. The park features various attractions, shows, and restaurants.

Narita City

Narita City is a small city located 20 kilometers from Funabashi. It provides visitors with opportunities to experience traditional Japanese culture and visit famous landmarks such as the Naritasan Shinshoji Temple.

Conclusion

Funabashi is an attractive destination for anyone seeking to explore the rich cultural diversity of Japan. It combines natural beauty, history, and adventure, offering something for everyone. Its local food, sake, and festivals make it a perfect place to discover the local culture comfortably. Visitors should follow common safety practices and respect the customs and traditions of the locals. Funabashi is easily accessible through various transportation options, making it an ideal place to stay and explore during your Japanese adventure.
