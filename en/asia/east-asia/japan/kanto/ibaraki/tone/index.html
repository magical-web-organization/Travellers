Introduction to Tone

Tucked away in the outskirts of Kanto in Japan, Tone is a city that boasts a rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes. It is home to the largest river in the Kanto region, the Tone River, and is surrounded by vast farmlands and lush mountains. It’s also known for its hiking and biking trails, local cuisine, and historical landmarks.

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or a glimpse into Japan’s history and culture, Tone is the perfect destination. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Tone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kasama Inari Shrine: Dedicated to the god of harvest, this shrine is one of the oldest and most revered landmarks in Tone.

  • Ryujin Big Suspension Bridge: This pedestrian bridge is 375 meters long and offers breathtaking views of the Tone River and surrounding mountains.

  • Gijui Shrine: A small but beautiful shrine located on the banks of the Tone River that’s believed to be more than 800 years old.

  • Tone Art Museum: Showcases an incredible collection of contemporary Japanese art and sculpture.

Natural Attractions of Tone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fukuroda Falls: One of Japan’s famous waterfalls, with a height of 120 meters and a width of 73 meters. This is a must-visit landmark in Tone.

  • Kasama-yaki Pottery Village: This village is surrounded by mountains and is home to some of the finest pottery in Japan.

  • Mount Tsukuba: A majestic twin-peaked mountain that’s popular among hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Lake Kasumigaura: The second-largest lake in Japan and famous for its fishing and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tone

Tone is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Trek to Mount Tsukuba: The trail to Mount Tsukuba is about 9 kilometers long and takes around 4 hours to complete. The summit offers a panoramic view of the city and surrounding mountains.

  • Tone River Cycling Route: This 180-kilometer route runs along the Tone River and is suitable for cyclists of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Tone: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Local Cuisine: Tone is known for its fresh seafood, locally grown vegetables, and Kasama-yaki pottery. Don’t miss trying the Tonkatsu, a breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet, and soba noodles.

  • Take a Boat Tour of the Tone River: See the city from a different perspective while enjoying a relaxing boat ride along the Tone River.

  • Visit the Kasama Inari Shrine Festival: This annual festival, held every November, features traditional Japanese performances and a variety of food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Tone

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tonkatsu: A breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet served with a side of rice and miso soup.

  • Soba Noodles: Thin, brown noodles made from buckwheat flour and served in a hot broth.

  • Kasama-yaki Pottery Dishes: Featuring high-quality ceramic dishes, often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: A rice-based alcoholic drink, with several local breweries producing high-quality versions.

  • Green Tea: A range of green teas, with many shops selling a variety of flavors.

Where to Stay in Tone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Route-Inn Oyama: Located in the heart of Oyama, this hotel offers affordable rooms and is well-placed for visitors to Tone.

  • Grand Park Oyama: Situated in Oyama, this hotel has a traditional Japanese vibe, with a tatami-mat room and hot springs available.

  • Kasama Inari Onsen: This ryokan-style accommodation is located near Kasama Inari Shrine and offers comfortable rooms and hot springs.

What to Buy in Tone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kasama-yaki Pottery: High-quality pottery, often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

  • Sake: High-quality rice-based alcoholic drinks produced by local breweries.

  • Green Tea: A range of green teas, with many shops selling a variety of flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Tone

Tone’s culture is heavily influenced by its history and geography. The city is known for its traditional pottery, festivals, and love for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

Best Time to Visit Tone

The best time to visit Tone is between March and May or September and November, with mild temperatures and plenty of sunshine. The spring season is great for cherry blossom viewing, and the fall season is perfect for enjoying the colorful autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tone

Do:

  • Bow when meeting someone for the first time.

  • Remove shoes before entering homes, temples, and shrines.

  • Use chopsticks correctly.

  • Be respectful of Japan’s unique customs and cultural norms.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking on the streets.

  • Speak loudly or make excessive noise in public places.

  • Blowing your nose in public is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tone

Tone is a relatively safe city, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as being aware of their surroundings and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Tone

Tone has good medical facilities, with several hospitals and clinics available. Visitors should carry copies of their medical history and prescription medication, just in case.

Getting to Tone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tone

  • Narita International Airport: Japan’s busiest airport is located approximately 110 kilometers from Tone.

  • Haneda Airport: Tokyo’s second airport is located approximately 93 kilometers from Tone.

Bus Stations Near Tone

  • Oyama Station: The largest train station in Tone, with several bus routes departing from here.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car and drive from either Narita or Haneda airports to Tone.

  • Train: There are several train options available, with the Shinkansen being the fastest and preferred choice for many.

How to Get Around in Tone

Tone has an excellent public transportation system, with buses and trains running frequently. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Tone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tone is located in the Kanto region of Japan, making it an ideal base for exploring more of Japan. Some nearby destinations include:

  • Tokyo: Japan’s bustling capital city, located approximately 80 kilometers from Tone.

  • Nikko: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, located approximately 70 kilometers from Tone, famous for its stunning shrines and temples.

  • Fukushima: A scenic region that’s home to several hot springs and ski resorts, located approximately 100 kilometers from Tone.

Conclusion

Tone is a beautiful city that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural landscapes. From hiking to biking, from traditional pottery to local cuisine, there is something for everyone. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide, and you’re sure to have a fantastic time exploring everything that Tone has to offer.
