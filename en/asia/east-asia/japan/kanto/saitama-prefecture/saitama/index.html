Introduction to Saitama

Saitama is a prefecture in the Kanto region of Japan, located in East Asia. It borders Tokyo to the south and Gunma Prefecture to the north. The prefecture is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Saitama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saitama is rich in history and culture, and visitors can explore its many museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit attractions in this category include:

  • The Railway Museum: This museum showcases the history of railways in Japan, from the steam locomotives of the Meiji era to the bullet trains of today. It’s a fun and educational experience for all ages.
  • Kawagoe Old Town: Also known as “Little Edo,” Kawagoe is a historic town that has preserved the atmosphere of the Edo period. Visitors can wander around the town’s many traditional shops and admire its old-fashioned architecture.
  • Yoshimi Hyakuana: This historical site features a complex network of underground tunnels that were dug during the Second World War. Visitors can explore the tunnels and learn about their history.
  • Musashi Ichinomiya Hikawa Shrine: This ancient shrine is said to have been founded over 2,000 years ago and is dedicated to the god of war. It’s a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.

Natural Attractions of Saitama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Saitama is blessed with natural beauty, and there are many outdoor adventures to be had here. Some of the top attractions in this category include:

  • Chichibu-Tama-Kai National Park: This national park is located in the western part of Saitama and offers stunning views of mountains, rivers, and forests. Visitors can go hiking, camping, and fishing here.
  • Nagatoro River: This scenic river is famous for its crystal-clear waters and vibrant foliage. Visitors can go canoeing, rafting, and hiking along its banks.
  • Mount Buko: This mountain is a popular destination for hikers and climbers, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Kinchakuda Red Spider Lily Field: This flower field is famous for its stunning red spider lilies that bloom in September and October. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll around the field and take in the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saitama

Saitama is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the top routes include:

  • Mount Mitsumine: This mountain offers a challenging hike with rewarding views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Mount Hiwada: This mountain is famous for its stunning autumn foliage and is a popular destination for hikers.
  • Toda River Cycling Road: This flat and easy cycling trail runs along the Toda River and is perfect for families and beginners.

Best Things to Do in Saitama: Activities and Tours

Saitama offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups. Here are some of the best things to do in the prefecture:

  • Saitama Seibu Lions Baseball Game: This popular baseball team plays at the Seibu Dome in Tokorozawa and offers a fun and exciting experience for sports fans.
  • Kawagoe Kimono Experience: Visitors can rent traditional Japanese kimono and stroll around Kawagoe’s historic streets for a truly authentic experience.
  • Tango-no-Sekku Koinobori Festival: This annual festival takes place in May and features thousands of colorful carp-shaped windsocks flying in the wind.
  • Mitsumine Shrine Autumn Festival: This traditional festival takes place in October and features a procession of mikoshi (portable shrines) and taiko drumming.

Food and Drink Scene of Saitama

Must-Try Dishes

Saitama is known for its delicious food, and there are some local dishes that visitors shouldn’t miss out on. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Miso-Nikomi Udon: This hearty dish consists of thick udon noodles cooked in a savory miso broth with chicken and vegetables.
  • Hanyu Steamed Dumplings: These steamed dumplings are stuffed with juicy pork and vegetables and are a popular snack in Saitama.
  • Kawagoe-style Sweet Potato: This local specialty features sweet potatoes coated in a caramelized sugar glaze and is perfect for those with a sweet tooth.
  • Ogawa Strawberry: These juicy and sweet strawberries are harvested in Ogawa, Saitama and are a must-try for fruit lovers.

Local Beverages

Saitama also has some unique beverages worth trying. Here are some popular choices:

  • Chichibu Whisky: This award-winning whisky is distilled in the city of Chichibu and is known for its smooth and fruity flavor.
  • Kawagoe Beer: This local brewery produces a range of craft beers, including a refreshing wheat beer and a rich stout.
  • Saitama Sake: Saitama is home to several sake breweries, offering a variety of flavors and styles.

Where to Stay in Saitama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saitama has plenty of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some popular choices:

  • Kawagoe Tobu Hotel: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Kawagoe and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Old-fashioned Guesthouse Wada: This guesthouse is located in a traditional Japanese house and offers a unique and authentic experience.
  • Candeo Hotels Omiya: This stylish hotel is located near the Omiya Railway Museum and offers great views of the city.

What to Buy in Saitama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saitama has some unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or personal mementos. Here are some suggestions:

  • Kawagoe Candy: This colorful candy is shaped like a roof tile, a symbol of Kawagoe’s traditional architecture.
  • Chichibu Meisen Silk: This high-quality silk is woven in Chichibu and features beautiful patterns and colors.
  • Omiya Bonsai: Omiya is known for its bonsai trees and visitors can buy their own miniature bonsai trees to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Saitama

Saitama has a rich history and culture, and visitors should take some time to learn about its customs and traditions. Here are some important aspects to keep in mind:

  • Respectful Behavior: Japanese culture places a high value on respectful behavior, so visitors should be mindful of their actions and language.
  • Etiquette at Shrines: When visiting shrines and temples, visitors should follow proper etiquette, including bowing and cleansing their hands and mouth at the purification fountain.
  • Seasonal Festivals: Saitama has many traditional festivals throughout the year, and visitors should take the opportunity to experience these colorful and lively events.

Best Time to Visit Saitama

The best time to visit Saitama depends on personal preferences, but generally, the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) months offer the most pleasant weather and beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saitama

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Take your shoes off when entering a home or temple.
  • Carry cash, as some smaller shops and restaurants may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking.
  • Talk loudly on public transportation.
  • Forget to tip, as it’s not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saitama

Saitama is a generally safe prefecture, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings in busy areas like train stations and tourist attractions.

Health in Saitama

Saitama has modern medical facilities and hospitals, and visitors can easily access healthcare if needed. However, it’s recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Saitama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saitama is easily accessible from Tokyo and other parts of Japan, with a range of transportation options. Here are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Saitama

  • Narita International Airport: This is the closest international airport to Saitama, located about 80 km away.
  • Haneda Airport: This airport is located in Tokyo and offers domestic flights as well as some international flights.

Bus Stations Near Saitama

  • Omiya Station: This is a major transportation hub in Saitama, with several bus routes connecting to other parts of the prefecture.
  • Kawagoe Station: This station also offers several bus routes to nearby attractions and destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Saitama is well-connected by train, with several major train lines passing through the prefecture.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore Saitama and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Saitama

Saitama has an excellent public transportation system, including trains, buses, and taxis. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the cities and countryside at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Saitama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saitama is located in the Kanto region of Japan, making it a great base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are some popular options:

  • Tokyo: Japan’s capital city is just a short train ride away from Saitama and offers endless attractions and activities.
  • Nikko: This historic town is located in Tochigi Prefecture and is famous for its stunning temples and shrines.
  • Kusatsu Onsen: This hot spring resort town is located in Gunma Prefecture and offers relaxing and therapeutic baths.

Conclusion

Saitama is a prefecture that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and delicious food, there’s something for everyone here. Whether you’re a first-time visitor to Japan or a seasoned traveler, Saitama is definitely worth adding to your itinerary.
