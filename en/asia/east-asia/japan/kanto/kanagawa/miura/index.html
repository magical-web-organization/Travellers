Introduction to Miura

Nestled in the heart of Kanagawa, on Japan’s southern coast, lies the hidden gem of Miura. This charming seaside city offers breathtaking natural scenery, exciting outdoor activities, and a rich history and culture that’s yours to explore. From the iconic Misaki fishing port to the beautiful parks and trails, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Miura. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do in Miura and provide tips on where to stay, transportation options, and safety considerations.

List of Places to Visit in Miura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Miura boasts a rich history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore. Some of the must-visit spots include:

  • Military Museum of Miura: This museum features displays and artifacts from Japan’s military history, from ancient times to the present day.
  • Hayama Shiosai Park: Enjoy a stroll through this beautiful waterfront park, which features a lighthouse and stunning views of Sagami Bay.
  • Enkyoji Temple: This Buddhist temple was founded in the 1300s and is home to a number of historic structures and art pieces.

Natural Attractions of Miura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miura’s natural beauty is truly breathtaking, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in this seaside city. Here are some of the natural attractions that are definitely worth visiting:

  • Jogashima Island: This scenic island offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, as well as a lighthouse, hiking trails, and relaxing hot springs.
  • Mikasa Park: This beautiful park offers a peaceful respite from the city, with walking trails, a rose garden, and plenty of greenery.
  • Mount Ogusu: This mountain offers challenging hiking trails and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miura

Miura is a great place for hiking and biking, with plenty of scenic trails and routes to explore. Some of the must-visit routes include:

  • Miura Kaigan: This seaside path offers stunning views of Sagami Bay and the surrounding coastline, and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.
  • Sarushima Island: Sarushima Island is a nature reserve and the only natural island in Tokyo Bay, offering hiking trails, sandy beaches, and incredible views of the bay.
  • Cape Tsurugi: This cape offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean, as well as challenging hiking trails and opportunities for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Miura: Activities and Tours

Miura offers a wide range of activities and tours, from guided sightseeing tours to cooking classes and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do in Miura:

  • Visit the Misaki Fish Market: This famous market is a must-visit for seafood lovers, and offers a wide range of fresh fish and other seafood.
  • Take a sake brewery tour: Miura is known for its sake, and there are plenty of breweries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Learn about samurai culture: Miura was once home to many samurai, and there are a number of cultural experiences that allow visitors to learn about this fascinating history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Miura

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Miura, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you get the most out of your stay:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Miura and explore the city on foot.
  • Day 2: Visit Mikasa Park and Enkyoji Temple.
  • Day 3: Take a hiking or biking tour and explore the natural beauty of Miura.
  • Day 4: Take a sake brewery tour and learn about the history and production of this traditional Japanese drink.
  • Day 5: Visit the Misaki Fish Market and enjoy some fresh seafood.
  • Day 6: Take a cultural experience tour and learn about samurai culture.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beach and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Miura

Must-Try Dishes

Miura is known for its seafood, and there are plenty of delicious dishes to try during your visit. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Maguro Zuke Don: This dish features marinated tuna served over rice and is a local specialty.
  • Kaki Furai: These deep-fried oysters are a popular local snack and make a great appetizer.
  • Kamaboko: This fishcake is a popular snack and makes a great souvenir to bring home.

Local Beverages

Miura is known for its sake, and there are plenty of breweries that offer tours and tastings. Other popular local drinks include:

  • Green Tea: This healthy beverage is a local favorite and can be found in many cafes and restaurants throughout the city.
  • Shochu: This distilled spirit is similar to sake and is often served with meals.

Where to Stay in Miura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodations in Miura, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Guest House Misaki-sou: This cozy guesthouse offers affordable accommodation and is located near the Misaki Fish Market.
  • Hotel Nishi Kamakura: This upscale hotel offers luxurious amenities and stunning views of Kamakura Bay.

What to Buy in Miura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Miura is known for its seafood and sake, and there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Kamaboko: This traditional fishcake makes a great souvenir or gift to bring home.
  • Sake: Miura is known for its sake, and there are plenty of breweries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Local crafts: Miura is home to many talented artisans, and there are plenty of locally-made crafts and souvenirs to choose from.

Local Culture and Customs of Miura

Miura has a rich culture and history, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions during your visit. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Removing shoes: In traditional Japanese homes and many businesses, it is customary to remove your shoes upon entering.
  • Bowing: Bowing is a common show of respect in Japanese culture, and is often used when greeting someone or expressing gratitude.
  • Eating etiquette: It’s important to use chopsticks correctly and to not play with your food or leave food uneaten.

Best Time to Visit Miura

The best time to visit Miura is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miura

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of Miura.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Make loud noises in public places.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Miura

Miura is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take safety precautions as you would in any other city. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Lock up your valuables and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Follow local safety guidelines and laws.

Health in Miura

Miura has good healthcare facilities and services available, and potential health risks are relatively low. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic health precautions during your trip, such as washing your hands regularly and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Miura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miura

The closest airport to Miura is Haneda Airport, which is located in Tokyo and offers a variety of international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Miura

Miura is easily accessible by bus, with many buses running from neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

Miura is also accessible by train, with several train stations located throughout the city. Additionally, rental cars are available for those who prefer to drive.

How to Get Around in Miura

Miura is easily navigable on foot or by bike, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Buses and trains are also available for those who prefer public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Miura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miura is located in close proximity to a number of other popular destinations in Kanagawa and beyond. Some nearby destinations worth visiting include:

  • Kamakura: This historic city is home to many temples and shrines, including the famous Great Buddha of Kamakura.
  • Yokosuka: This city is located nearby and is home to the famous Yokosuka Naval Base, as well as many scenic parks and beaches.
  • Tokyo: Japan’s capital city is easily accessible by train from Miura, and offers endless attractions and activities to explore.

Conclusion

Miura is a hidden gem in Japan’s Kanagawa prefecture, offering breathtaking natural scenery, exciting outdoor activities, and a rich history and culture. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, exploring museums and historic sites, or trying local cuisine and drinks, Miura has something for everyone. By following the tips and suggestions in this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Miura.
