Introduction to Fujisawa

Located in Kanagawa Prefecture, Fujisawa is a charming seaside city in Japan’s Kanto region. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and delicious local cuisine, Fujisawa is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Japan. Home to a number of historical landmarks, breathtaking beaches, and scenic hiking trails, Fujisawa promises a delightful holiday experience to every type of traveler.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the top attractions and activities in Fujisawa:

List of Places to Visit in Fujisawa

From ancient temples to modern museums, Fujisawa has no shortage of attractions for all ages.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Buddhism has long been an important part of Japanese heritage, and Fujisawa is home to a number of stunning temples and shrines. Some of the most notable landmarks in the city include:

  • Enoshima Shrine: A beautiful Shinto shrine located on a small offshore island of the same name.
  • Jojuin Temple: A peaceful Buddhist temple surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Shinbayashi Park: A serene and tranquil park located near the Jojuin Temple.
  • Shonan Museum of Art: One of the top art museums in the region, with a collection of modern and contemporary art.
  • Fujisawa City Historical Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the city’s long and rich history.

Natural Attractions of Fujisawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fujisawa is blessed with natural beauty and has plenty of outdoor adventures for nature lovers. Some of the most breathtaking natural attractions in the city include:

  • Enoshima Island: A picturesque island offering panoramic views of the ocean and Mount Fuji.
  • Southern Beach Chigasaki: A popular beach with a laid-back atmosphere and excellent surfing conditions.
  • Tsujido Beach: Another stunning beach in Fujisawa, famous for its beautiful sunsets.
  • Kamakura Hiking Trails: With plenty of hiking trails, Kamakura is a hiking and trekking paradise. One can trek to the top of Mount Koya to experience the scenic views of the surrounding lush green hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fujisawa

Fujisawa is surrounded by lush green hills, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, here are some of the most popular hiking and biking trails in Fujisawa:

  • Enoshima Island: Take a leisurely walk around the island and soak up the stunning ocean views.
  • The Sakai River: Ride along the river banks and enjoy the peaceful scenery.
  • Oyama Nature Park: Hike through the evergreen forest trails and take in the fresh air.

Best Things to Do in Fujisawa: Activities and Tours

There is an abundance of things to do in Fujisawa, and here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Surfing Lessons: Join the surfing lessons in Chigasaki Beach and catch some waves.
  • Enoshima Aquarium: Experience the marine life upto the depths of the ocean.
  • Kamakura Hiking Tours: These tours are guided expeditions through the mountains of Kamakura.
  • Traditional Japanese Teahouse Experience: Enjoy a traditional tea ceremony in a Japanese teahouse.
  • Samurai Experience: Travel back in time to the age of samurais and participate in a sword-fighting class.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fujisawa

If you want to get the most out of your trip to Fujisawa, here is an ideal itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day Plan
1 Visit the Enoshima Island and Enoshima Shrine
2 Take a surfing lesson at Southern Beach
3 Explore Kamakura’s hike trails
4 Visit local parks and museums
5 Enjoy a traditional Japanese tea ceremony
6 Experience a Samurai class
7 Enjoy a relaxing day at Tsujido Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Fujisawa

Fujisawa is a paradise for food lovers, and its local cuisine is something that every tourist should try during their visit. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Fujisawa:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shonan Pizza: A delicious pizza with a unique blend of herbs and spices.
  • Kamakura Vegetables: These locally grown vegetables for a highly nutritious meal.
  • Shoyu Ramen: A local favorite, this dish is well known for its rich and hearty flavors.
  • Shirasu: Young sardines that are served raw or cooked.
  • Tako-yaki: Balls of fried batter with bits of octopus inside.

Local Beverages

  • Shonan Beer: A locally brewed beer with a unique taste.
  • Sake: A traditional Japanese rice wine that pairs well with a wide variety of dishes.
  • Matcha Tea: A powdered green tea that is an essential part of Japanese tradition.

Where to Stay in Fujisawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fujisawa offers a variety of accommodation options to cater to all budgets and styles. Some of the best places to stay in Fujisawa include:

  • Enoshima Island Spa: A luxurious spa resort with beautiful ocean views.
  • Kamakura Park Hotel: A popular choice for visitors who want to stay close to the local landmarks.
  • Fujisawa Hotel EN: A modern and stylish hotel that offers excellent value for money.

What to Buy in Fujisawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fujisawa offers plenty of traditional Japanese souvenirs that make for the perfect gift to take back home. Some of the most popular products to bring home include:

  • Shibori-Dye Cloth: Hand-dyed fabrics that are uniquely Japanese.
  • Kanagawa Pine Beer: A unique and refreshing beer that is made with local pine.
  • Kamakura Bori: Wooden souvenirs and items of art that are beautifully crafted with intricate details.
  • Daruma Dolls: Traditional Japanese dolls that are believed to bring good luck.

Local Culture and Customs of Fujisawa

Fujisawa is deeply rooted in Japanese traditions and customs, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture during their stay. Some of the customs to keep in mind include bowing when greeting someone, taking shoes off before entering a house or temple, and tipping service staff is not customary in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Fujisawa

The best time to visit Fujisawa is during the spring season (March to May) when the weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. The autumn season (September to November) is also a great time to visit Fujisawa as the temperatures are cool and pleasant, and the autumn foliage is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fujisawa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Use public transportation to get around the city as much as possible.
  • Try the local cuisine and drink.
  • Learn basic Japanese phrases and etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside the temples and traditional Japanese establishments.
  • Smoke in public spaces, such as the beach or park.
  • Tip service staff; it is not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fujisawa

Fujisawa is a safe city with low levels of crime. However, tourists should be aware of their surroundings and take general safety precautions such as locking doors and windows at night, avoiding unlit areas, and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Fujisawa

Japan has one of the best healthcare systems globally, with good quality medical facilities and trained medical practitioners. However, tourists should have comprehensive travel insurance to cover them in case of any emergency.

Getting to Fujisawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fujisawa

Fujisawa is served by two major airports: Narita International Airport (NRT) and Haneda International Airport (HND). From the airport, tourists can take public transportation such as buses, trains, or taxis to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near Fujisawa

There are several bus stations near Fujisawa, including the Fujisawa Bus Terminal, the Enoshima Bus Terminal, and the Kamakura Bus Station. Buses are convenient for shorter trips, but trains are generally the best mode of transportation for getting around the city.

Other Options

Tourists can also travel to Fujisawa by train or rent a car if they prefer to explore the city independently.

How to Get Around in Fujisawa

For internal transportation, tourists can take trains, buses, or taxis to reach their destination. Trains, known as the Enoden, is a popular and affordable way to explore Fujisawa, and it also offers scenic routes to some of the top tourist spots.

Where to Go Next from Fujisawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fujisawa is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas, with a variety of nearby destinations that offer unique cultural experiences and scenic beauty. Some of the best places to visit near Fujisawa include:

  • Kamakura: Famous for its ancient temples and shrines.
  • Yokohama: A bustling city famous for its night markets and shopping.
  • Hakone: A mountainous retreat famous for its hot springs and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Fujisawa is a hidden gem of Japan that will delight any traveler seeking a unique cultural experience, natural beauty, and mouth-watering food. With its historical landmarks, stunning beaches, and scenic hiking trails, Fujisawa is definitely worth adding to your Japan travel itinerary. We hope this guide helped you plan your visit to Fujisawa and assisted in exploring the beauty of Japan to the fullest.
