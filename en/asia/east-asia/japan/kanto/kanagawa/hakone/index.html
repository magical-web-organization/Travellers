Introduction to Hakone

Nestled in the heart of Kanagawa Prefecture, Hakone is the perfect place for a relaxing and rejuvenating getaway. This charming town is located just two hours away from Tokyo, making it a popular destination among Japanese and international travelers alike. Known for its stunning natural scenery, hot springs, and cultural attractions, Hakone has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hakone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hakone has a rich cultural heritage that can be witnessed through its numerous museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Hakone Open-Air Museum: This sprawling museum is home to over 100 sculptures, including works by Salvador Dali, Auguste Rodin, and Pablo Picasso. The museum also has a footbath and hot spring, perfect for soaking sore muscles after walking around.

  • Hakone Shrine: This historic Shinto shrine was founded in the 8th century and is dedicated to the god of war. The shrine’s iconic red torii gate is situated on the shores of Lake Ashi and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Hakone Checkpoint: This reconstructed checkpoint was once used during the Edo period to prevent the spread of Christianity and control the movement of people and goods between Tokyo and Kyoto. The checkpoint provides an interesting glimpse into Japan’s feudal past.

Natural Attractions of Hakone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hakone’s natural beauty is simply breathtaking, with an abundance of opportunities for outdoor activities, such as:

  • Lake Ashi: Also known as Ashinoko, Lake Ashi is a crater lake formed in the caldera of Mount Hakone. Visitors can take a boat tour of the lake or hike along its scenic trails.

  • Hakone Ropeway: This aerial tramway offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valley. The ride takes visitors over sulfurous fumes, hot springs, and the magnificent Owakudani Valley.

  • Mount Fuji: Located just an hour away from Hakone, Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can take a day trip to Mount Fuji from Hakone to marvel at the stunning natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hakone

Hakone is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with a variety of routes to choose from. Some popular trails include:

  • Old Tokaido Road: This ancient road once connected Tokyo and Kyoto and offers visitors a glimpse into Japan’s past. The trail is lined with traditional houses, shrines, and temples.

  • Hakone Hachiri: This 10km trail runs along the historic Hakone Hachiri highway, which linked Tokyo and Kyoto during the Edo period. The trail offers stunning views of Mount Fuji and Lake Ashi.

Best Things to Do in Hakone: Activities and Tours

In addition to its cultural and natural attractions, Hakone is home to a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the highlights include:

  • Onsen baths: Hakone is famous for its natural hot springs, or onsen. Visitors can soak in the therapeutic waters of these baths while taking in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Sake tasting: Kanagawa Prefecture is known for its delicious sake, and visitors can indulge in a variety of tastings and tours across Hakone.

  • Owakudani Valley: This volcanic valley is home to bubbling sulfur springs, hot streams, and unique rock formations. Visitors can enjoy a scenic cable car ride to the valley, followed by a hike to the summit for panoramic views of the landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hakone

For travelers looking to spend a week in Hakone, here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a hotel or guesthouse in Hakone.

  • Day 2: Visit the Hakone Open-Air Museum and explore the town.

  • Day 3: Take a boat tour of Lake Ashi and enjoy the scenic views.

  • Day 4: Hike the Old Tokaido Road and visit the Hakone Checkpoint.

  • Day 5: Relax in one of Hakone’s many hot springs.

  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Mount Fuji.

  • Day 7: Departure from Hakone.

Food and Drink Scene of Hakone

Visitors to Hakone will not be disappointed by its culinary offerings, with an array of delicious dishes and local beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaiseki cuisine: A traditional Japanese multi-course meal made up of seasonal ingredients and presented in a stunning display.

  • Black eggs: Hard-boiled eggs cooked in the hot springs of Owakudani Valley, which turn black due to the sulfur.

  • Hoto noodles: A thick, flat noodle soup made with miso and vegetables, a regional specialty of the nearby Yamanashi Prefecture.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Kanagawa Prefecture is known for its high-quality sake, with many breweries dotted throughout the region.

  • Shochu: A distilled spirit made from sweet potatoes or other grains, often served with ice or mixed with soda or water.

Where to Stay in Hakone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hakone offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from traditional ryokans to modern hotels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Gora Kadan: A luxurious ryokan situated in the heart of Hakone’s hot springs district, offering private hot springs baths and traditional kaiseki cuisine.

  • Hakone Ginyu: Another upscale ryokan with panoramic views of Mount Fuji, featuring private hot springs and a selection of sake and shochu for guests to try.

  • K’s House Hakone: A popular backpacker hostel with dormitory and private rooms, offering a communal kitchen and lounge area for guests.

What to Buy in Hakone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local specialties and souvenirs to pick up during your stay in Hakone:

  • Wooden sculptures: The region is known for its intricate woodcarvings, such as the popular “Netsuke” miniatures.

  • Yosegi Zaiku: A type of marquetry that creates intricate geometric patterns from different pieces of wood, often used to create jewelry boxes, coasters, or other decorative items.

  • Sake and shochu: Bring home a bottle of the locally produced drinks to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hakone

Hakone’s residents are proud of their rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life. Visitors to the region are expected to respect local customs, such as removing shoes before entering a tatami-matted room, and try to learn a few basic Japanese phrases.

Best Time to Visit Hakone

The best time to visit Hakone is during the autumn months of September to November when the foliage is at its most beautiful. The winter months of December to February are also a popular time to visit, with the soothing hot springs providing a warm respite from the cold weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hakone

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take advantage of Hakone’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Litter or leave trash in public spaces.
  • Speak loudly in public places or on public transportation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hakone

Hakone is a relatively safe destination, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from natural hazards such as weather and wildlife.

Health in Hakone

Hakone’s hot springs and natural scenery provide a relaxing and rejuvenating experience for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of sunburn and dehydration during outdoor activities.

Getting to Hakone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hakone

The closest airport to Hakone is Tokyo’s Haneda Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus or train to Hakone.

Bus Stations Near Hakone

There are several bus stations in Hakone, including the Hakone-Yumoto Bus Terminal, which serves as a hub for local and regional bus routes.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Hakone-Yumoto Station and then transfer to a local bus or taxi to their final destination.

How to Get Around in Hakone

Hakone is easily navigated by bus, train, and taxi. The Hakone Free Pass provides unlimited access to local transportation for a set period and is an excellent option for visitors looking to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Hakone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hakone is ideally located for day trips to nearby destinations such as:

  • Kamakura: A historic coastal town with numerous temples and shrines, located just an hour away from Hakone by train.

  • Yokohama: A bustling port city with an international flavor, featuring attractions such as Chinatown and the Yokohama Landmark Tower.

  • Tokyo: The capital city of Japan is just a short train ride away, offering endless possibilities for sightseeing and exploration.

Conclusion

Hakone is a destination that truly offers something for everyone, whether you’re interested in natural beauty, cultural attractions, or outdoor adventures. With its rich history, delicious cuisine, and stunning landscapes, Hakone is a must-visit destination during any trip to Japan.
