Introduction to Futamatagawa

Futamatagawa is a charming town nestled in the Kanagawa prefecture of Japan. This hidden gem is often overlooked by tourists, but it has a lot to offer visitors who want to explore a more authentic side of Japan.

Futamatagawa boasts a rich history and culture, as well as beautiful natural landscapes that make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers. This town is perfect for those who want to escape the crowded cities and immerse themselves in the tranquil beauty of Japan.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you a list of places to visit, must-try dishes, transportation options, and more to help you plan your perfect trip to Futamatagawa.

List of Places to Visit in Futamatagawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Futamatagawa Temple

Futamatagawa Temple is a must-visit spot for those who want to learn more about Buddhist culture. This temple is known for its beautiful architecture and tranquil atmosphere.

Kawamura Art Museum

The Kawamura Art Museum is a museum that combines both traditional and modern art, and is a great example of how art can be used to bridge the gap between cultures.

The Great Buddha of Kamakura

The Great Buddha of Kamakura is a historical site that is one of the icons of Japan. This statue is over 40 feet tall and was constructed in the early 13th century.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sagami River

Sagami River is a beautiful spot for those who love nature. In the spring, cherry blossoms line the river banks, while autumn brings beautiful foliage.

Hiking in the Mountains

Futamatagawa is home to beautiful mountains that are perfect for hiking. Mount Takao is a popular destination for those who want to enjoy the beauty of the natural landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Futamatagawa

Futamatagawa has a variety of hiking and biking trails to choose from. The Mount Takao hiking trail is popular for those who want a more challenging hike, while the Sagamiko cycling trail is perfect for those who prefer biking.

Best Things to Do in Futamatagawa: Activities and Tours

Tea Ceremony

A tea ceremony is a traditional Japanese cultural activity and is a great way to learn about Japanese culture.

Sake Brewery Tour

Futamatagawa is home to some of the best sake breweries in Japan. Take a tour to learn about the process of making sake and sample some of the best varieties.

Week-Long Itinerary for Futamatagawa

Day 1: Visit Futamatagawa Temple and enjoy a traditional tea ceremony. Day 2: Take a hike in Mount Takao and enjoy the beautiful scenery. Day 3: Visit the Kawamura Art Museum to learn about traditional and modern art. Day 4: Explore the Sagami River and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 5: Take a sake brewery tour. Day 6: Visit the Great Buddha of Kamakura. Day 7: Take a break and relax at a local onsen.

Food and Drink Scene of Futamatagawa

Must-Try Dishes

Ramen

Ramen is a popular Japanese dish and is a must-try in Futamatagawa.

Sushi

Sushi is a Japanese delicacy and Futamatagawa is known for its fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

Sake

Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic drink and Futamatagawa is home to some of the best breweries in the country.

Green Tea

Green tea is a staple in Japanese culture and is a must-try when visiting Futamatagawa.

Where to Stay in Futamatagawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Futamatagawa has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels and guesthouses. Some of the popular choices include the Futamatagawa Onsen Hotel, the Kamakura Prince Hotel, and the Guesthouse Futamatagawa.

What to Buy in Futamatagawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sake

Sake is a popular souvenir and is the perfect way to bring a taste of Futamatagawa home with you.

Locally-Made Crafts

Futamatagawa is home to many talented artisans who create beautiful crafts that make great gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Futamatagawa

Futamatagawa has a rich history and culture, and there are many traditions and customs that are still practiced today. Some of these include the tea ceremony, calligraphy, and flower arrangement.

Best Time to Visit Futamatagawa

The best time to visit Futamatagawa is during the spring or autumn when the weather is mild and the scenery is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Futamatagawa

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local dishes and beverages.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Talk loudly or make noise in public places.
  • Leave your chopsticks sticking up in your food.
  • Wear shoes inside temples and other sacred places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Futamatagawa

Futamatagawa is a safe place to visit, but it’s always important to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Pickpocketing can happen in crowded areas and it’s recommended to keep your belongings secured.

Health in Futamatagawa

Futamatagawa has excellent healthcare facilities in case of emergency. Make sure to have adequate travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Futamatagawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Futamatagawa

The closest airport to Futamatagawa is Haneda Airport in Tokyo. From there, take the train to the nearest station.

Bus Stations Near Futamatagawa

There are several bus stations that connect Futamatagawa to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Futamatagawa is well-connected by train and taxi services.

How to Get Around in Futamatagawa

The best way to get around Futamatagawa is on foot, bike, or public transport.

Where to Go Next from Futamatagawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Several nearby destinations include Kamakura, Yokohama, and Tokyo – all of which are accessible by train or bus.

Conclusion

Futamatagawa is a hidden gem in Japan, waiting to be discovered. With its rich history and culture, beautiful natural landscapes, and delicious food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
