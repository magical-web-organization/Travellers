Kawasaki, located in Kanagawa, Kanto, Japan is a city known for its industrial and historical importance. The fourth most populous city in Japan, Kawasaki offers an unforgettable experience to its visitors. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a food enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in Kawasaki.

Introduction to Kawasaki

Founded in 1924, Kawasaki has a rich history and culture. The city boasts a blend of historical architecture, modern skyscrapers, breathtaking gardens, and trendy shopping destinations. Kawasaki is located near Tokyo and is a significant stop for travelers visiting the Tokyo area.

List of Places to Visit in Kawasaki

Kawasaki is an attractive and diverse city with numerous sights and attractions. Here’s a list of the must-visit sights in Kawasaki.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kawasaki Daishi: Kawasaki Daishi is a beautiful temple and pilgrimage site famous for its magnificent architecture and beautiful gardens.

  • Kawasaki City Museum: Kawasaki City Museum displays the history and culture of the city. The museum showcases a collection of art, pottery, and ancient relics showcasing the history and culture of the area.

  • Taro Okamoto Museum of Art: The Taro Okamoto Museum of Art showcases works of the well-known artist Taro Okamoto. Visitors can enjoy art exhibitions, movies, and other cultural experiences.

Natural Attractions of Kawasaki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nihon Minkaen: The Nihon Minkaen presents an extraordinary opportunity to experience Japanese traditional architecture. The museum displays two dozen historical Japanese houses and buildings open to visitors using real-life scenarios.

  • Kawasaki Rinko Park: Kawasaki Rinko Park is a beautiful park and the perfect place to enjoy a picnic or a stroll by the riverside. The park has several recreational activities available and favorable for all ages.

  • Ikuta Ryokuchi Park: Surrounded by lush greenery, Ikuta Ryokuchi Park is a perfect place to relax and take a break from the hustle and bustle of the city. The park has an amphitheater, picnic areas and is a venue for many concerts and events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kawasaki

  • Kawasaki Daishi Kaido: Kawasaki Daishi Kaido is a popular and scenic biking and hiking trail. The trail covers almost 10 kilometers and is surrounded by natural beauty along the way.

  • Tamagawa River Cycling Road: Tamagawa River Cycling Road goes along the Tamagawa river and is a popular biking and hiking trail surrounded by beautiful greenery.

Best Things to Do in Kawasaki: Activities and Tours

  • Take a cruise along the Keihin Canal: Enjoy a relaxing boat ride along the Keihin Canal and enjoy the beautiful views of the city.

  • Visit Fujiko F. Fujio Museum: Fujiko F. Fujio Museum is a museum dedicated to the renowned cartoon artist and his famous cartoon characters. The museum has many exhibitions, and visitors can enjoy interactive experiences, photo opportunities as well as purchasing souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kawasaki

For travelers who have a week to spend in Kawasaki, here is a sample itinerary.

Day Activity
1 Visit Kawasaki Daishi
2 Explore Kawasaki City Museum
3 Take a hike on the Kawasaki Daishi Kaido or Tamagawa River Cycling Road
4 Visit Taro Okamoto Museum of Art
5 Take a cruise along the Keihin Canal
6 Visit Nihon Minkaen
7 Spend the day at Ikuta Ryokuchi Park

Food and Drink Scene of Kawasaki

Kawasaki offers a delicious food scene, and here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Unagi: Unagi is the most famous dish in Kawasaki. Marinated grilled eel is served with rice and is available in many restaurants in the city.

  • Miso Soup: Kawasaki’s miso soup is famous for its rich flavor and is served in many places.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Kawasaki produces its sake, and the city is home to several breweries that visitors can visit.

  • Green Tea: Green tea is available in many cafes and restaurants across Kawasaki, Japan.

Where to Stay in Kawasaki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kawasaki has a diverse range of accommodations from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. Here are some of the most popular options.

  • Hotel Metropolitan Kawasaki: Located in the heart of the city, the Hotel Metropolitan Kawasaki is a luxurious 4-star hotel with modern amenities and excellent service.

  • Kawasaki King Skyfront Tokyu REI Hotel: This 3-star hotel is located near Kawasaki station and offers comfortable rooms and various amenities for an unforgettable stay.

  • Kawasaki Central Apartment Hotel: The Kawasaki Central Apartment Hotel is a budget-friendly accommodation option offering clean rooms, basic facilities, and an excellent location.

What to Buy in Kawasaki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Eel Products: Eel-based products are popular souvenirs to buy in Kawasaki. Visitors can bring home canned eels, dried eels, or eel sauce.

  • Japanese Sake: Kawasaki is known for producing some of the finest sake, and visitors can bring home bottles of Japanese sake as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kawasaki

Kawasaki has a unique culture and customs that visitors should learn and respect. The city is home to a mix of Japanese and other Asian cultures, and visitors can witness cultural events and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Kawasaki

The best time to visit Kawasaki is during the spring season from March to May or autumn from October to November. The weather is pleasant and mild, and it’s the perfect time to enjoy the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kawasaki

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering a Japanese home or temple.
  • Respect the privacy and personal space of the Japanese people.
  • Be polite and courteous when speaking with locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t use your cell phone when using public transport.
  • Don’t tip in restaurants in japan as it is not a common practice.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kawasaki

Kawasaki is a generally safe place, but visitors should adhere to cautionary measures like keeping an eye on personal belongings and avoiding dark alleys, especially at night.

Health in Kawasaki

Kawasaki has excellent medical facilities and hospitals providing exceptional healthcare for residents and tourists.

Getting to Kawasaki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kawasaki is easily accessible from Tokyo and other major cities in Japan. Here are some of the transportation options available.

Airports Near Kawasaki

The nearest international airports to Kawasaki include:

  • Tokyo Haneda Airport
  • Narita International Airport

Bus Stations Near Kawasaki

  • Kawasaki Bus Terminal is conveniently located near the train station

Other Options

  • Trains: One of the easiest ways to reach Kawasaki is by train, with regular train services available from Tokyo and other major cities in Japan.

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Kawasaki and can be found at train stations, airports, and other major transportation hubs.

How to Get Around in Kawasaki

Getting around in Kawasaki is easy, with many transportation options available, including trains, buses, and taxis. Visitors can also choose to rent bicycles to explore the city’s sights and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kawasaki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kawasaki is an excellent base to explore other destinations in the Kanto region and surrounding areas. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Yokohama: The second-largest city in Japan, Yokohama has plenty to offer visitors, including historic buildings, museums, and excellent seafood.

  • Tokyo: The bustling capital city, Tokyo, is just a short train ride away from Kawasaki, and visitors can explore famous landmarks like the Tokyo Tower and the Imperial Palace.

Conclusion

Kawasaki is a must-visit destination for travelers to Japan. Whether you’re looking for history, culture, or adventure, Kawasaki has it all. With an excellent transportation infrastructure, plenty of attractions and activities, delicious food, and a unique local culture, visitors are sure to enjoy their time in the city.
