Sagamihara is a charming city located in the Kanagawa Prefecture of Japan, just 50 km southwest of Tokyo. It offers a perfect blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for unique experiences. This guide will provide a comprehensive view of Sagamihara, featuring must-visit attractions, hiking routes, activities, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local products, and cultural insights.

Introduction to Sagamihara

Sagamihara is renowned for its unspoiled nature, rich culture, and unique history. The city is home to many museums, historical sites, and natural attractions. Sagamihara is a scenic destination to explore, with lovely parks, lakes, and mountains.

The city has excellent transportation options. It’s well-connected to Tokyo and other nearby cities via trains, buses, and highways. Sagamihara also features many accommodation options ranging from traditional Ryokans to modern hotels and hostels.

List of Places to Visit in Sagamihara

Sagamihara has a diverse range of attractions to offer visitors. From its historical and cultural landmarks to its natural beauty, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Sagamihara.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sagamihara City Museum: This museum features exhibits on Sagamihara’s history, culture, and nature. It’s a great place to learn about the city’s past.

  • Sagamigawa Fureai Science Museum: This museum offers interactive exhibits on science and technology. It’s an ideal place for kids and adults alike.

  • Sagamiko Pleasure Forest: It’s an amusement park that offers various attractions, like roller coasters, zip lines, and water rides, and is perfect for the whole family.

  • Hasedera Temple: The temple features a beautiful garden, a five-storied pagoda, and a large Buddhist statue. It’s a peaceful spot to visit and learn about Buddhism.

  • Zama Castle Ruins: The castle once belonged to Lord Odawara and played a significant role in Japan’s history. Today, it’s a popular spot for history buffs to explore.

Natural Attractions of Sagamihara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Takao: This mountain features many hiking trails, with breathtaking panoramic views of the city. It’s a popular spot for hiking enthusiasts.

  • Sagamiko Lake: The lake is surrounded by mountains, making it a beautiful spot for a leisurely stroll, boat ride, or BBQ.

  • Shiroyama Park: A stunning park with beautiful cherry blossom trees and a great view of the city.

  • Sagamiko Forest Resort: The resort is surrounded by nature and one can enjoy many activities like fishing, zip-lining, mountain biking, and camping.

  • Lake Tsukui: This lake features beautiful trails around it and an opportunity to canoe or kayak. It’s a great place to visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sagamihara

Sagamihara is a hiker’s paradise, offering some of the best scenic trails in Japan. From easy to difficult, Sagamihara features trails for every hiking enthusiast. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Sagamihara.

  • Mount Takao: This mountain offers plenty of hiking trails to suit all levels, with beautiful views of the city.

  • Takao-san Yakuo-in: A beautiful route with steep inclines and gorgeous views of nature.

  • Arukayama Trail: A long-range trail through the forested mountains on the outskirts of the town.

  • Sagamiko Forest Trail: A beautiful trail that leads through Sagamiko Forest Resort and offers beautiful views and adventure sports.

Best Things to Do in Sagamihara: Activities and Tours

Sagamihara has many activities and tours to offer visitors, from boat rides to museums to hot spring baths. Here are some of the best things to do in Sagamihara:

  • Visit Hasedera Temple
  • Explore Sagamihara City Museum
  • Take a boat ride on Sagamiko Lake
  • Visit Sagamiko Pleasure Forest amusement park
  • Experience traditional hot springs at the Tsukui Susono Hot Spring
  • Rent a bicycle and explore the city

Week-Long Itinerary for Sagamihara

If you’re planning to stay in Sagamihara for a week, you can follow this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Sagamihara’s historical landmarks, museums, and temples.
  • Day 2: Visit Mount Takao and hike its scenic trails.
  • Day 3: Take a boat ride on Sagamiko Lake and visit Sagamiko Pleasure Forest.
  • Day 4: Visit Shiroyama Park for a leisurely walk and enjoy the beautiful cherry blossom trees.
  • Day 5: Visit the Tsukui Susono Hot Spring for a traditional Japanese hot spring experience.
  • Day 6: Explore Sagamiko Forest Resort and try adventure sports like fishing, zip-lining, and camping.
  • Day 7: Rent a bike and explore the city’s hidden gems, like old streets or small parks.

Food and Drink Scene of Sagamihara

Sagamihara offers a diverse range of local delicacies and beverages that one must try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shojin Ryori: A vegetarian meal often served in Buddhist temples. It’s a multi-course meal with delicious vegetable dishes.

  • Unagi-no-kabayaki: Grilled eel glazed with sweet soy sauce, a popular Japanese dish.

  • Machida Mochi: Rice cakes made with sweet black soybeans and wrapped in an oak leaf.

  • Tsuke-soba: A cold noodle dish served with dipping sauces and toppings like wasabi, green onion, and seaweed.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Traditional Japanese rice wine, available in many varieties.
  • Shochu: A Japanese distilled spirit, matured in an earthenware pot or oak barrel.
  • Green tea: A healthy drink and a popular Japanese beverage served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Sagamihara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sagamihara offers many accommodation options ranging from traditional Ryokans to modern hotels and hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sagamihara.

  • LaLa Resort Sagamihara: A modern and stylish hotel located in the middle of nature. It offers spacious rooms and beautiful views.

  • Odakyu Hotel: A luxurious hotel located next to Sagamihara Station. It features comfortable rooms, restaurants, and a spa.

  • V Motel Sagamihara: A modern and comfortable hotel with reasonable rates, located in the city center.

  • Ryotei Ryokan Miyasaka: A traditional Japanese Ryokan with lovely rooms and private hot spring baths.

What to Buy in Sagamihara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sagamihara offers a diverse range of local products, from traditional crafts to unique snacks. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back home.

  • Sagamihara Tenugui: Traditional Japanese hand towels made of thin cotton, with beautiful designs.

  • Machida Mochi: Rice cakes made with sweet black soybeans and wrapped in oak leaves.

  • Sagami Green Tea: High-quality green tea grown in the Sagami area.

  • Iwamoto Boiled Fish Paste: A popular Sagamihara snack made with white fish.

Local Culture and Customs of Sagamihara

Sagamihara is a friendly and welcoming place with unique customs and traditions. Here are some of the local customs and etiquette tips to keep in mind:

  • Remove your shoes when entering homes, temples, and Ryokans.

  • Bow when greeting people.

  • Respect the elders and authorities.

  • Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing.

  • Be quiet on public transport.

Best Time to Visit Sagamihara

The best time to visit Sagamihara is during spring (March to May) when the cherry blossom trees bloom and in autumn (September to November) when the leaves change colors. The weather is also mild during these seasons making ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sagamihara

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Explore Sagamihara’s museums, temples, and parks.
  • Follow local customs and etiquette.
  • Learn some basic words/phrases in Japanese.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public.
  • Don’t smoke in public areas.
  • Don’t be loud and disruptive in public transport.
  • Don’t touch the produce at the market without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sagamihara

Sagamihara is a safe destination with low crime rates, and visitors can explore the city without much concern. Still, take your regular precautions like being aware of your surroundings and keeping your valuables safe.

Health in Sagamihara

Sagamihara has excellent medical facilities, and visitors can receive medical attention if needed. Visitors should carry adequate health insurance or prepare for any medical emergencies.

Getting to Sagamihara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sagamihara is well-connected to Tokyo and other nearby cities via trains, buses, and highways. Here are some transport options to consider:

Airports Near Sagamihara

  • Narita International Airport: Located 110 km away from Sagamihara.

  • Haneda Airport: Located 63 km away from Sagamihara.

Bus Stations Near Sagamihara

  • Sagamihara Station Bus Terminal: Located in the city center.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Available throughout the city.

  • Car rental: Car rental options are available at the airports and Sagamihara Station.

How to Get Around Sagamihara

Sagamihara is a walkable city and visitors can easily explore it on foot. Trains and buses are also available to get around the city. Taxis and car rentals are also an option for a more convenient option to travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from Sagamihara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sagamihara is conveniently located near many other popular tourist sites in Japan. Here are some destinations to check out after visiting Sagamihara:

  • Tokyo: The vibrant capital city, located just an hour’s train ride away.

  • Kamakura: A charming coastal town famous for its historical temples and shrines.

  • Yokohama: A modern port city known for its vibrant cultural scene and delicious food.

  • Hakone: A mountainous town famous for its hot springs and scenic views of Mount Fuji.

Conclusion

Sagamihara offers a unique blend of nature, culture, history, and adventure, making it an excellent destination for travelers looking for a diverse experience. From its stunning natural beauty to its delicious food scene and rich cultural heritage, Sagamihara has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Sagamihara, and you won’t be disappointed!
