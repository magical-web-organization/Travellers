Introduction to Kamakura

Kamakura is a coastal city located in the Kanagawa prefecture, Japan. It has a rich history and was once the political center of Japan during the Kamakura period from 1185 to 1333. Today, it is a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and traditional culture. Its proximity to Tokyo also makes it a convenient day trip destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kamakura

Kamakura has a lot to offer in terms of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some of the most popular ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kanazawa Bunko Museum: A museum dedicated to Japanese culture and art, featuring a collection of over 20,000 items.
  • Hokokuji Temple: A Zen temple famous for its bamboo grove and tea ceremony.
  • Kamakura Museum of National Treasures: A museum featuring Japanese arts and crafts, including pottery, textiles, and lacquerware.
  • Tsurugaoka Hachimangu Shrine: The most important Shinto shrine in Kamakura, with a long history dating back to 1063.

Natural Attractions of Kamakura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kamakura Beaches: The city has several beautiful beaches, including Yuigahama Beach and Zaimokuza Beach, that are perfect for swimming and surfing.
  • Enoshima Island: A small island off the coast of Kamakura that is perfect for hiking and enjoying stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Kamakura Daibutsu: A massive bronze statue of Buddha that dates back to the 13th century, located in Kotokuin Temple.
  • Meigetsuin Temple: A temple known for its beautiful hydrangea garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kamakura

Kamakura is surrounded by mountains and has many hiking and biking trails. Some popular routes include:

  • Daibutsu Hiking Course: A 3.5km trail that takes you from Kita-Kamakura Station to Kotokuin Temple, passing by the Kamakura Daibutsu.
  • Tenen Hiking Course: A 4km trail that takes you from Kenchoji Temple to Zuisenji Temple, passing by beautiful bamboo groves and ancient cemeteries.
  • Kamakura Biking Route: A 10km route that takes you along the coast and through Kamakura’s countryside, passing by some of the city’s most famous landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Kamakura: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting the city’s famous landmarks and natural attractions, there are many other things to do in Kamakura. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Kamakura Rickshaw Tour: Take a traditional rickshaw ride through the city to explore its most famous landmarks.
  • Kamakura Sake Tasting Tour: Visit local sake breweries and enjoy tasting some of Japan’s best sake.
  • Kamakura Samurai Tour: Learn about the city’s rich samurai history and see some of the weapons and armor used by the warriors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kamakura

If you have a week to spend in Kamakura, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit Tsurugaoka Hachimangu Shrine and discover the city’s samurai history at the Kamakura Samurai Museum.
2 Spend the day hiking the Daibutsu Hiking Course and visiting Kotokuin Temple and the Kamakura Daibutsu.
3 Explore Kamakura’s natural attractions, including the bamboo grove at Hokokuji Temple and the hydrangea garden at Meigetsuin Temple.
4 Take a day trip to Enoshima Island to hike and enjoy the island’s beautiful views.
5 Learn about Japanese art and culture at the Kamakura Museum of National Treasures and Kanazawa Bunko Museum.
6 Relax at one of Kamakura’s beautiful beaches, such as Yuigahama Beach or Zaimokuza Beach.
7 Join a Kamakura Sake Tasting Tour and discover the city’s sake brewing traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Kamakura

Kamakura has a diverse food and drink scene, featuring traditional Japanese cuisine as well as international options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kamakura Shirasu-don: A rice bowl topped with fresh whitebait, a local specialty.
  • Kamakura Vegetables: The city is known for its fresh and delicious vegetables, including sweet potatoes, spinach, and green beans.
  • Kamakura Curry: A unique Japanese-style curry made with local spices and ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Kamakura Beer: A local craft beer made with natural spring water from the nearby mountains.
  • Amazake: A traditional Japanese sweet rice drink that is a perfect treat on a hot day.
  • Sake: Kamakura is home to several sake breweries that produce some of Japan’s best sake.

Where to Stay in Kamakura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kamakura has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular choices include:

  • Kamakura Prince Hotel: A luxury hotel located directly on the beach, offering stunning ocean views.
  • Iza Kamakura Guesthouse & Bar: A cozy guesthouse with a bar on the first floor, featuring Japanese-style rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Kamakura Guesthouse: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Kamakura, with easy access to all the city’s famous landmarks.

What to Buy in Kamakura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kamakura is a great place to pick up unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Kamakura T-shirts: Show off your love for the city with a trendy Kamakura-themed T-shirt.
  • Kamakura Pottery: The city is known for its beautiful handmade pottery, featuring intricate designs and unique shapes.
  • Kamakura Shichirin: A small charcoal grill that is perfect for barbecuing at home or on a picnic.

Local Culture and Customs of Kamakura

Kamakura has a rich history and traditional culture that is still evident today. Some important customs and etiquette include:

  • Be respectful when visiting shrines and temples, including removing your shoes and covering your shoulders and knees if necessary.
  • Try to learn some basic Japanese phrases to communicate with locals, such as “arigatou gozaimasu” (thank you) and “sumimasen” (excuse me).
  • Avoid talking on your phone or using electronics loudly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Kamakura

The best time to visit Kamakura is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November). During these seasons, the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller than during the summer months. However, Kamakura is a year-round destination and can be enjoyed at any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kamakura

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kamakura:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and etiquette.
  • Try the local food and drink specialties.
  • Explore the city by foot or by bike to fully appreciate its beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Ignore signs and warnings at beaches or hiking trails.
  • Disrespect other visitors or locals.

Safety and Health

Kamakura is a safe city to visit, with a low crime rate and excellent healthcare facilities. However, it is important to be aware of any potential health or safety risks. Here are some important tips:

Safety in Kamakura

  • Be aware of the risks of natural disasters, including typhoons and earthquakes.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas to avoid pickpocketing.
  • Follow beach safety guidelines and obey warning flags and signs.

Health in Kamakura

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated in hot and humid weather.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Seek medical care if you experience any health problems or symptoms.

Getting to Kamakura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kamakura is easily accessible from Tokyo and other nearby cities. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Kamakura

  • Tokyo Haneda Airport: The closest airport to Kamakura, with direct train and bus connections to the city.
  • Narita International Airport: A larger airport located further away from Kamakura, with direct train and bus connections available.

Bus Stations Near Kamakura

  • Kamakura Station: The city’s main transport hub, with bus connections to nearby cities and attractions.
  • Yuigahama Bus Stop: A bus stop located near Yuigahama Beach, with connections to Kamakura Station and other nearby attractions.

Other Options

  • Train: Kamakura is easily accessible by train from Tokyo and other nearby cities, with frequent services available.
  • Car: Driving is not recommended in Kamakura due to limited parking space and heavy traffic during peak seasons.

How to Get Around in Kamakura

One of the best ways to get around Kamakura is by foot or by bike. Many of the city’s famous landmarks and attractions are located within walking or biking distance of each other. Public transportation options, including buses and trains, are also available for longer journeys or when traveling to more remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Kamakura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kamakura is located in the Kanagawa prefecture, which has many other interesting cities and towns to explore. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Yokohama: A modern port city with a rich history and cultural attractions, including Chinatown and the Yokohama Museum of Art.
  • Hakone: A mountainous town known for its hot springs and stunning views of Mount Fuji.
  • Fujisawa: A city located on the coast that is famous for its beaches and surfing.

Conclusion

Kamakura is a unique and fascinating destination that has something to offer for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in Japanese history and culture, outdoor adventures, or great food and drinks, this city has it all. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of Kamakura for yourself!
