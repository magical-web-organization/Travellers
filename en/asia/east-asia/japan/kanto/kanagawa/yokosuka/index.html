Introduction to Yokosuka

Yokosuka is a city located in Kanagawa, Japan, on the east coast of the Miura Peninsula. The city is known for its naval base, which is home to the United States Fleet Activities Yokosuka and the Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force. The city is also famous for being the birthplace of the Japanese battleship, the Yashima, in 1896. With its rich history, natural beauty, cultural attractions, and diverse food scene, Yokosuka is a must-visit destination in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Yokosuka

When visiting Yokosuka, there are plenty of places to explore. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, natural scenery, or outdoor adventures, Yokosuka has something to offer for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of Yokosuka is its many museums and historical sites, showcasing the city’s rich history and culture. Here are some must-visit attractions in Yokosuka to include in your itinerary:

Yokosuka Museum of Art

The Yokosuka Museum of Art is a contemporary art museum featuring various exhibitions of Japanese and international artists. The museum also has a collection of modern and contemporary art from Japan, Europe, and America. The highlight of the museum is its large glass ceiling that spans the entire building, providing plenty of natural light throughout the gallery.

Verny Park

Verny Park is a beautiful park situated on the waterfront, named after French engineer Louis-Émile Bertin Verny, who helped modernize the Japanese navy during the Meiji period. The park offers a panoramic view of the bay and is an excellent spot for picnics, walks, and relaxation. The park also houses the Japanese battleship Mikasa, which played a significant role in Japan’s victory in the Russo-Japanese War.

Yokosuka City Museum

The Yokosuka City Museum is a local museum that focuses on Yokosuka’s history, culture, and daily life. The museum has over 15,000 items, including art, artifacts, documents, and photographs. You can explore exhibits ranging from Yokosuka’s earliest inhabitants to the present day, with a focus on the city’s naval history.

Natural Attractions of Yokosuka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yokosuka is also known for its natural beauty, with plenty of outdoor activities and scenic views to enjoy. Here are some of the top natural attractions to include in your Yokosuka itinerary:

Sarushima Island

Sarushima Island is a beautiful island located in the Tokyo Bay, just a few kilometers from Yokosuka. The island is a popular spot for outdoor activities, such as hiking, kayaking, and swimming. You can also relax on the secluded beach and enjoy the scenic views of the bay.

Kannonzaki Lighthouse

The Kannonzaki Lighthouse is a historic lighthouse located on the Kannonzaki Peninsula, offering breathtaking views of Tokyo Bay and Mt. Fuji on a clear day. The lighthouse also has a museum that exhibits the history and construction of the lighthouse.

Hakkeijima Sea Paradise

Hakkeijima Sea Paradise is a leisure complex featuring an aquarium, amusement park, and shopping mall. The aquarium houses over 100,000 marine creatures, including dolphins, sea lions, and penguins. Visitors can also enjoy the rides, restaurants, and shops in the complex.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yokosuka

Yokosuka has several hiking and biking trails that offer a unique way to explore the city’s scenic nature and stunning views. Here are some of the best hiking and cycling routes in Yokosuka:

Umikaze Park

Umikaze Park is a beautiful park located on the hill overlooking Yokosuka City. The park features several trails, ranging from easy to challenging, that provide spectacular views of the city and the Tokyo Bay.

Tateishi Park

Tateishi Park is a scenic green space situated in the heart of Yokosuka, offering a beautiful view of the city and the bay. The park has several trails that are perfect for cycling and hiking, providing a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Best Things to Do in Yokosuka: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting Yokosuka’s cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of activities and tours to experience in the city. Here are some unique things to do in Yokosuka:

Yokosuka Navy Base Tour

The Yokosuka Navy Base is the largest U.S. naval installation in Japan, housing over 25,000 personnel. Visitors can take a guided tour of the base, including historic ships and landmarks, to learn more about the naval operations in Yokosuka.

Yokosuka Kanazawa Brewery Tour

The Yokosuka Kanazawa Brewery is a local brewery that produces a variety of craft beers and sake. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery, learn about the brewing process, and sample the drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Yokosuka

Yokosuka has a diverse food scene with many local restaurants and bars serving traditional Japanese cuisine and international dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Yokosuka:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Curry Rice: Yokosuka is famous for its curry rice, a popular dish among navy personnel. The dish features Japanese curry sauce served over rice and can be found in many local restaurants and cafes.
  • Tempura: Tempura is a classic Japanese dish made of seafood or vegetables dipped in batter and fried. Yokosuka has many excellent tempura restaurants serving fresh seafood from the local bay.
  • Donburi: Donburi is a rice bowl topped with various meats, vegetables, and sauces. There are many delicious donburi options in Yokosuka, such as katsudon (pork cutlet), gyudon (beef bowl), and unadon (grilled eel).

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Yokosuka has several local breweries producing high-quality sake, such as Yokosuka Kanazawa Brewery and Kitaya Brewery. The sake is known for its quality and smooth taste.
  • Shochu: Shochu is a distilled spirit made from sweet potatoes, barley, or rice. Yokosuka has many bars and izakayas (Japanese pubs) serving various types of shochu, including local brands.

  • Hotel Harbour Yokosuka: A waterfront hotel with stunning views of the bay and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Yokosuka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yokosuka has many local souvenirs and products that make perfect gifts for friends and family. Here are some recommended items to buy in Yokosuka:

  • USS George Washington Memorabilia: The USS George Washington is a U.S. aircraft carrier that is stationed in the Yokosuka Navy Base. Visitors can buy memorabilia and souvenirs related to the ship, such as t-shirts, hats, and keychains.
  • Japanese Sweets: Yokosuka has many local sweets and snacks that make perfect gifts, such as mochi (rice cakes), senbei (rice crackers), and yuzu candies.
  • Yokosuka Kanazawa Beer and Sake: Yokosuka has several local breweries producing high-quality beer and sake, such as Yokosuka Kanazawa Brewery and Kitaya Brewery. Visitors can buy their products at local shops and breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Yokosuka

Yokosuka has a rich culture and customs that reflect its history and regional identity. Here are some cultural highlights of Yokosuka:

  • Navy Culture: Yokosuka has a strong navy culture, with many naval bases and personnel stationed in the city. Visitors can learn about the history and operations of the navy through various attractions and tours.
  • Yokosuka Matsuri: The Yokosuka Matsuri is an annual festival held in August, featuring parades, street performances, and fireworks. The festival celebrates the city’s history and culture and is a festive event for locals and visitors alike.
  • Onsen Culture: Yokosuka has several hot springs (onsen) that provide a relaxing escape from the city. Onsen culture is an essential part of Japanese culture and is an excellent opportunity to experience Japanese hospitality and relaxation.

Best Time to Visit Yokosuka

The best time to visit Yokosuka is from March to May or from September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers in Yokosuka can be hot and humid, with rain showers and typhoons, while winters can be chilly with occasional snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yokosuka

When visiting Yokosuka, it’s essential to be aware of the local customs and etiquette. Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow in Yokosuka:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, such as removing your shoes in houses and temples and bowing as a sign of respect.
  • Carry cash: Many restaurants and shops only accept cash, so it’s advisable to have some yen on hand.
  • Be mindful of noise levels, especially at night, as many residential areas are quiet and peaceful.

Don’t:

  • Tip: Tipping is not common in Japan and can be considered rude. Service charges are usually included in the bill.
  • Eat while walking: In Japan, it’s impolite to eat while walking on the street. Instead, find a designated area, such as a park or restaurant, to enjoy your food.
  • Take photos of people without permission: It’s essential to respect people’s privacy and ask for permission before taking their photo.

Safety and Health

Yokosuka is generally a safe city with low crime rates, but it’s always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety and health tips for visiting Yokosuka:

Safety in Yokosuka

  • Be cautious when crossing the street, as traffic in Japan drives on the left side of the road.
  • Be aware of pickpockets and be careful with your belongings in crowded places, such as trains and buses.
  • Follow local customs and etiquette, such as queuing and not smoking in non-designated areas.

Health in Yokosuka

  • Make sure to stay hydrated, especially during hot and humid weather. It’s also advisable to wear sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Be mindful of food and water hygiene, especially when eating street food or drinking tap water.
  • Make sure to have comprehensive travel insurance, including coverage for medical emergencies.

Getting to Yokosuka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yokosuka is easy to access from Tokyo and other major cities in Japan. Here are some transportation options to get to Yokosuka:

Airports Near Yokosuka

  • Narita International Airport (NRT): The largest international airport in Japan is located about 105 km from Yokosuka.
  • Haneda Airport (HND): A smaller international airport is located about 55 km from Yokosuka.

From both airports, visitors can take a taxi or public transportation to Yokosuka, such as trains or buses.

Bus Stations Near Yokosuka

  • Keikyu Bus operates many routes to and from Yokosuka Bus Station, which is located near the Yokosuka-Chuo train station.

Other Options

  • Trains: Yokosuka is easy to access from Tokyo and other major cities in Japan through the Yokosuka Line and Keihin Kyuko Line.
  • Taxis: Taxis are also available in Yokosuka but can be expensive compared to other transportation options.

How to Get Around in Yokosuka

Yokosuka is a relatively small city, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot or by public transportation. Here are some transportation options for getting around in Yokosuka:

  • Trains: Yokosuka has several train stations, such as Yokosuka-Chuo and Shioiri, that connect to other cities in the region.
  • Buses: Yokosuka has several bus routes operated by Keikyu Bus that connect to major attractions and train stations.
  • Taxis: Taxis are also available in Yokosuka but can be expensive compared to other transportation options.

Where to Go Next from Yokosuka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yokosuka is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some nearby excursions from Yokosuka:

  • Kamakura: Kamakura is a historic city located about 15 km from Yokosuka, featuring many temples, shrines, and ancient ruins.
  • Enoshima: Enoshima is a small island located about 25 km from Yokosuka, famous for its beaches, aquarium, and scenic views of Mt. Fuji.
  • Zushi: Zushi is a coastal town located about 10 km from Yokosuka, known for its beautiful beaches and traditional Japanese architecture.

Conclusion

Yokosuka is a diverse and exciting city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, nature, and food. From its naval culture to its natural attractions and delicious cuisine, Yokosuka has something to offer for every traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical sites, experiencing its outdoor adventures, or trying its local delicacies, Yokosuka is a must-visit destination in Japan.
