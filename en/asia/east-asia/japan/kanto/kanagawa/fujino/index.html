Introduction to Fujino

Fujino is a charming mountain town in Kanagawa, located in the Kanto region of Japan. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and laid-back way of life. The town offers a perfect retreat from the bustling cities of Japan, providing visitors with a serene and relaxing atmosphere where they can unwind and rejuvenate.

List of Places to Visit in Fujino

Fujino has something to offer for everyone, from history buffs to nature lovers, adventure seekers to foodies. Here are some of the top places to visit in Fujino:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fujino Art House: This contemporary art museum features a collection of Japanese and international artworks, including paintings, sculptures, installations, and performances. It hosts various events and workshops throughout the year, providing visitors with a unique cultural experience.

  • Fujino Folk Craft Museum: This museum showcases the traditional handicrafts of Fujino, such as weaving, pottery, woodwork, and lacquerware. Visitors can learn about the history and techniques of these crafts and even try their hand at making them.

  • Fujino Castle Ruins: This historical site dates back to the Edo period and offers a glimpse into the feudal era of Japan. Visitors can explore the ruins of the castle and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Fujino: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Takao: This sacred mountain is located near Fujino and is a popular destination for hiking, with its diverse trails offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. The top of the mountain is also home to a temple where visitors can take part in traditional rituals.

  • Fujino Sakura Park: This park is famous for its cherry blossom trees, which bloom in spring, creating a stunning pink canopy. It is an ideal spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

  • Fujino Nature Trail: This trail winds through the mountains of Fujino and offers visitors a chance to connect with nature while enjoying the beautiful scenery. The trail is suitable for hikers and bikers of all levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fujino

Fujino is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with its many mountains and trails offering different levels of difficulty and stunning views. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Fujino:

  • Takao Route: This challenging hiking trail takes visitors through the forests and mountains of Mount Takao, offering stunning vistas of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Fujino Nature Trail: This peaceful hiking and biking trail winds through the mountains of Fujino, providing visitors with a serene and picturesque environment.

  • Tanzawa-Oyama Nature Trail: This challenging trail takes visitors through the Alpine terrain of Tanzawa-Oyama, with its rugged peaks and breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Fujino: Activities and Tours

Fujino offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Fujino:

  • Japanese Tea Ceremony: Visitors can experience the traditional Japanese tea ceremony, which involves the preparation and serving of matcha (powdered green tea) in a serene and peaceful setting.

  • Fishing: Visitors can try their hand at fishing in one of the many streams and rivers that flow through Fujino, where they can catch trout, salmon, and other species.

  • Hot Springs: Fujino is home to many hot springs where visitors can relax and rejuvenate in the thermal waters. Some of the notable hot springs include Fujino Onsen and Kibune Onsen.

Food and Drink Scene of Fujino

Fujino offers a unique gastronomical experience, with its traditional dishes and local beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages of Fujino:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiritanpo: This dish is made of mashed rice that is molded onto a stick and grilled over an open flame. It is often served with miso soup and is a popular dish in the Akita prefecture.

  • Houtou: This dish is a thick noodle soup made with vegetables and meat. It is a specialty of the Yamanashi prefecture and is a comforting and hearty meal.

  • Soba Noodles: Fujino is famous for its buckwheat soba noodles, which are handmade and served with a variety of toppings and dipping sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Fujino is home to many small breweries that produce high-quality sake, which is a traditional Japanese rice wine.

  • Green Tea: Fujino is known for its green tea production, which is made from the finest tea leaves and has a delicate and refreshing flavor.

Where to Stay in Fujino: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fujino offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Fujino:

  • Fujino Kirameki: This elegant hotel offers spacious rooms with Japanese-style décor and modern amenities. It also features a hot spring bath, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Fujino Akane: This cozy guesthouse provides visitors with a traditional Japanese experience, with its tatami-floored rooms and views of the mountains. It also offers a communal bath and a garden.

  • Fujino Station Inn: This budget-friendly hostel is located near the train station and provides visitors with a comfortable and convenient stay. It offers free Wi-Fi and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Fujino: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fujino is known for its traditional handicrafts and local products, which make perfect souvenirs to bring home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Fujino:

  • Pottery: Fujino is famous for its pottery, which is often made with locally sourced clay and has a rustic and earthy look.

  • Soba Noodles: Visitors can buy packs of Fujino’s famous soba noodles, which make a delicious and authentic Japanese meal.

  • Green Tea: Fujino’s green tea is high-quality and flavorful, making it a perfect gift for tea lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Fujino

Fujino has a rich cultural heritage, with its traditional crafts, festivals, and customs still thriving today. Visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture and learn about the customs and traditions of Fujino.

Best Time to Visit Fujino

The best time to visit Fujino is from spring to autumn, when the weather is mild and the natural scenery is at its best. Visitors can enjoy the cherry blossoms in spring, the hiking trails in summer, and the stunning autumn foliage in fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fujino

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try local foods and drinks.

  • Take off your shoes when entering homes and temples.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.

  • Eat or drink while walking on the street.

  • Disrespect the natural environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fujino

Fujino is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors are advised to take precautions, such as keeping their belongings secure and avoiding dark or isolated places at night.

Health in Fujino

Fujino has adequate healthcare facilities, including clinics and hospitals. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive medical insurance and to bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Fujino: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fujino is easily accessible by public transportation and car. Here are some of the ways to get to Fujino:

Airports Near Fujino

  • Haneda Airport: This airport is located in Tokyo and is a 90-minute drive to Fujino.

  • Narita International Airport: This airport is located in Chiba and is a two-hour drive to Fujino.

Bus Stations Near Fujino

  • Odakyu Fujino Station: This station is located in the center of Fujino and is accessible by bus and train.

  • Tokyu Highway Bus Yamanakako Line: This bus line connects Fujino with other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from Tokyo and drive to Fujino, which takes around 2 hours depending on traffic.

  • Taxi: Visitors can take a taxi from nearby train stations or airports to Fujino.

How to Get Around in Fujino

Fujino is a small town that is best explored on foot, with its many walking trails and scenic spots. Visitors can also rent bikes or use local transportation to get around.

Where to Go Next from Fujino: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fujino is an ideal base for exploring other nearby towns and attractions in Kanagawa and beyond. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Hakone: This popular resort town is known for its hot springs, scenic views of Mount Fuji, and traditional museums and galleries.

  • Yokohama: This bustling port city is known for its cosmopolitan vibe, historic landmarks, and vibrant food and drink scene.

  • Tokyo: The capital city of Japan is a vibrant and dynamic metropolis that offers a diverse range of attractions, from cultural landmarks to shopping districts and entertainment venues.

Conclusion

Fujino is a hidden gem that combines natural beauty, rich culture, and a laid-back atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy hiking and biking trails, traditional crafts and cuisine, hot springs, and cultural landmarks, making it an ideal destination for anyone seeking an authentic Japanese experience.
